to barricado

1. VERB

/ ˌbærɪˈkeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbærɪˈkeɪdəʊ / (BrE)
to block or defend a place by setting up barricades
block with barricades
archaic

Examples

The rebels hurried to barricado the streets before dawn.
They barricadoed the entrance against the advancing soldiers.
During the siege, citizens tried barricadoing their homes for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - to block or defend with a physical barrier

Etymology

From Spanish 'barricada' (barricade), influenced by French 'barricader'.
