halberdier

1. NOUN

/ ˌhælbərˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌhæl.bəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a guard or soldier armed with a halberd, especially in European armies from the 15th to 17th centuries
soldier armed with a halberd

Examples

The royal palace was guarded by a group of halberdiers in colorful uniforms.
During the ceremony, a halberdier stood at each entrance to welcome the guests.
Historical reenactments often include a halberdier to represent 16th-century soldiers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halberd - a combined spear and battle-axe weapon

Etymology

From French 'hallebardier', from 'hallebarde' (halberd), of Germanic origin.
