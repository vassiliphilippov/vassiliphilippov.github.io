Eira

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrə / (AmE), / ˈɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the weasel family, Mustelidae, consisting of the tayra and its relatives, native to Central and South America.
genus of weasel family mammals

Examples

The Eira genus is well-known among zoologists for its adaptability to diverse habitats.
Members of the Eira genus are native to forests in Central and South America.
Research on the Eira genus has contributed to our understanding of mustelid evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Eira, the scientific genus name, from Tupí eirá (tayra).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.rə / (AmE), / ˈɪə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous mammals in the weasel family (Mustelidae), containing the tayra as its only species.
genus of mustelids (tayra)

Examples

The Eira genus comprises only a single living species, the tayra.
Scientists observed the tracks of an animal belonging to the Eira genus in the rainforest.
The classification of the Eira genus has been debated among mammalogists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tayra - a carnivorous mammal in the genus Eira
  • Degree Higher: Mustelidae - the family of carnivorous mammals including weasels, otters, and badgers

Etymology

From New Latin Eira, from Portuguese eira (tayra), itself from Tupi eirá.
