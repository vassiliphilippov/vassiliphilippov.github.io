jerry-built

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪlt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪlt / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) badly or hastily built, often of poor-quality materials and workmanship.
badly built; poorly constructed

Examples

The apartment was a jerry-built affair that started falling apart within months.
They quickly erected a jerry-built shed at the back of the house.
Critics described the new roads as jerry-built and unsafe for heavy traffic.

Synset Examples

mean little jerry-built houses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-built - solidly or strongly constructed

Etymology

Probably from the name Jerry, of uncertain origin, possibly from 19th-century British slang.
