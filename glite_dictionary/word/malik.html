malik

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˈmælɪk / (BrE)
a leader or headman of a town or community, especially in parts of Asia Minor and the Indian subcontinent
local leader in South or Central Asia

Examples

The village malik was responsible for settling disputes among the residents.
Many maliks played an important role in negotiating peace between rival clans.
After the former malik resigned, the community elected a new leader.

Synset Examples

maliks rule the hinterland of Afghanistan under the protection of warlords

Etymology

From Arabic مَالِك‎ (mālik, 'owner, master, king').
