tauntingly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɔntɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈtɔːntɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that mocks, ridicules, or provokes, often in a playful or teasing way.
in a mocking or teasing way

Examples

He smiled tauntingly at his opponent before the game began.
She waved the trophy tauntingly in front of the other team.
The children called out tauntingly to their friends from across the street.

Synset Examples

‘You hate things to be out of order, don't you?’ she said teasingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasingly - in a playful or mocking way
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect

Etymology

From 'taunt' (to mock or provoke), with -ingly forming an adverb.
