Austrotaxus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔstroʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəʊˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of gymnosperm plants represented by a single species, the New Caledonian yew, native to New Caledonia.
genus of New Caledonian yew

Examples

The rare Austrotaxus is found only in the forests of New Caledonia.
Botanists are studying Austrotaxus to better understand gymnosperm evolution.
Austrotaxus differs from other yew genera by its unique leaf arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxus - Genus of yews

Etymology

From Latin 'austro-' meaning 'southern' and 'Taxus' (yew genus), referencing its southern distribution.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəʊˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of gymnosperm trees in the yew family, native to New Caledonia and includes only one species, the New Caledonian yew.
genus of gymnosperm trees (New Caledonian yew)

Examples

The Austrotaxus genus is found only in the forests of New Caledonia.
Scientists study Austrotaxus to understand the diversity of ancient gymnosperms.
The New Caledonian yew, classified as Austrotaxus, is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxus - genus of yew trees

Etymology

From Latin 'auster' (south) and 'Taxus' (yew), referring to its southern distribution.
