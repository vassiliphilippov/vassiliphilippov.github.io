Delphinus

1. PROPN

/ dɛlˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / dɛlˈfɪnəs / (BrE)
a small constellation in the northern sky, located near Pegasus and Aquila
a northern sky constellation

Examples

Delphinus is easily spotted in the summer sky due to its distinctive shape.
Ancient astronomers named Delphinus because its stars form the outline of a dolphin.
The star chart showed the position of Delphinus near the constellations Pegasus and Aquila.

Etymology

From Latin 'Delphinus' meaning 'dolphin', from Greek 'delphís'.

2. NOUN

/ dɛlˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / dɛlˈfɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals in the family Delphinidae, commonly known as the common dolphin.
genus of dolphins

Examples

The genus Delphinus includes species commonly known as common dolphins.
Researchers discovered new behavioral patterns in Delphinus populations off the Mediterranean coast.
Fossil evidence suggests that Delphinus has existed for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a small, intelligent marine mammal within the family Delphinidae

Etymology

From Latin 'delphinus' meaning 'dolphin'.
