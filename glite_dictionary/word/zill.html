zill

1. NOUN

/ zɪl / (AmE), / zɪl / (BrE)
one of a pair of small metallic cymbals worn on the thumb and middle finger and played in rhythm, especially in belly dancing
small cymbal used in belly dancing

Examples

The dancer skillfully played the zill as she moved across the stage.
She wore a pair of zills on her fingers to accentuate her performance.
Learning to use the zill requires precise timing and coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cymbal - a percussion instrument consisting of a thin, round metal plate

Etymology

From Turkish 'zil' meaning 'cymbal'.
