enchantress

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntrɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃæntrəs / (BrE)
a woman who uses magic or sorcery, especially in myths and stories
female magician or sorceress

Examples

In the ancient tale, the enchantress bewitched the prince with a single glance.
The villagers whispered about the mysterious enchantress who lived deep in the forest.
Legends told of a powerful enchantress who could control the winds and the seas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorceress - a woman who practices magic or sorcery

Etymology

From Old French *enchanteresse*, based on Latin *incantare* (to chant a magic spell).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntrəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃæntrɪs / (BrE)
a woman who is irresistibly attractive or charming, often in a way that is considered dangerous or seductive
seductively charming woman

Examples

The mysterious enchantress bewitched everyone at the masquerade ball.
Many legends tell of an enchantress who lures sailors to their doom.
He couldn't resist the charm of the enchantress who entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seductress - a woman who seduces

Etymology

From Old French *enchanteresse*, from *enchanter* (to enchant), based on Latin *incantāre* (to chant a spell).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
