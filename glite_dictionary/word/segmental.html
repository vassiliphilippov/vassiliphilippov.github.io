segmental

1. ADJ

/ sɛɡˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / sɛɡˈmɛn.təl / (BrE)
(biology) having the body divided into successive segments or metameres, as in earthworms or lobsters.
having the body divided into segments (biology)

Examples

Earthworms are segmental animals, with bodies made up of repeating units.
The segmental organization of the lobster's body allows for flexible movement.
Some annelids show clear segmental division along their length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segmented - having the body or a structure divided into segments

Etymology

From Latin segmentum (a piece cut off).

2. ADJ

/ sɛɡˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / sɛɡˈmɛn.tl / (BrE)
relating to or divided into segments, especially referring to separate speech sounds or units in linguistics.
relating to segments in speech or linguistics

Examples

In phonology, vowels and consonants are known as segmental elements of speech.
The segmental features of a language are studied separately from suprasegmental aspects like intonation.
Linguists often analyze words into their segmental components to understand sound patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suprasegmental - relating to properties of speech (like intonation, stress) that extend over more than one sound segment

Etymology

From Latin segmentum (a piece cut off) + -al (forming adjectives).
