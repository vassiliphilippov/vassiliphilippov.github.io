entranced

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtrænst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɑːnst / (BrE)
filled with wonder and delight; so interested or amazed that you cannot look away or think of anything else
filled with wonder and delight

Examples

The audience sat entranced by the brilliant performance on stage.
She was entranced by the beauty of the night sky.
The children listened entranced as their grandmother told them stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spellbound - held by fascination or amazement
  • Antonym: bored - feeling uninterested or unengaged

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'entrance,' from Old French entrer (to put into a trance).
