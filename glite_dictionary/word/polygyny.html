polygyny

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdʒəni / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪdʒəni / (BrE)
the practice or custom of having more than one wife at the same time
the practice of a man having multiple wives

Examples

In some traditional societies, polygyny is practiced as a common form of marriage.
The anthropologist studied cases of polygyny in rural communities across Africa.
Debates about polygyny often raise questions about gender equality and family structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyandry - the practice of a woman having multiple husbands at the same time
  • Synonym: polygamy - the practice of having more than one spouse at the same time

Etymology

From Greek polygynia, from poly- (many) + gynē (woman, wife).
