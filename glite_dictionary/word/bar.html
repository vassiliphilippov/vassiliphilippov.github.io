BAR

1. NOUN

/ biː eɪ ɑːr / (AmE), / biː ɑː ɑː / (BrE)
a portable .30 caliber automatic rifle operated by gas pressure and fed by a magazine, used by U.S. troops in World War I, World War II, and the Korean War
U.S. military automatic rifle (historical)

Examples

The infantry squad relied on the BAR for providing suppressive fire during the attack.
He inherited a vintage BAR from his grandfather, a World War II veteran.
During the demonstration, the instructor explained how to operate a BAR safely.

Etymology

Acronym for Browning Automatic Rifle, named after its designer John M. Browning.
