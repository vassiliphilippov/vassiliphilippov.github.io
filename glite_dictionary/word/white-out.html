to white-out

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌaʊt / (BrE)
(of weather conditions) to become so obscured by fog, snow, or rain that daylight visibility is lost.
lose daylight visibility in heavy fog, snow, or rain

Examples

As the blizzard intensified, the entire road seemed to white-out in a matter of minutes.
If the weather white-outs again tomorrow, we’ll have to cancel our hike.
The storm white-outed the mountain pass, leaving the skiers stranded.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clear up - of weather, to become clear

Etymology

Derived from 'whiteout', originally a noun describing blizzard conditions in which visibility is lost.
