sunburst

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɜːst / (BrE)
a sudden appearance or emergence of the sun from behind clouds, often producing radiant beams of light
sudden emergence of sun from clouds

Examples

As the storm passed, a brilliant sunburst lit up the countryside.
We watched a beautiful sunburst break through the dark clouds after the rain.
The artist painted a sunburst in the sky to capture the moment the sun emerged.

Etymology

From 'sun' + 'burst', reflecting the sudden bursting forth of sunlight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɜːst / (BrE)
A decorative design or pattern consisting of a central disk resembling the sun with rays radiating outward.
radiating sun-like decorative design

Examples

The stained-glass window featured a beautiful sunburst above the main entrance.
She wore a necklace with a gold sunburst pendant that caught the light.
The ceiling was painted with a large sunburst motif radiating from the central chandelier.

Etymology

From 'sun' + 'burst', referring to the appearance of rays bursting from a central point.
