misanthropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
Disliking or distrusting humankind and avoiding human society; believing the worst of human nature and motives.
disliking or distrusting humankind

Examples

His misanthropic outlook made him avoid all social gatherings.
The novel's main character is a misanthropic recluse who distrusts everyone.
Some critics accused the artist of holding misanthropic views about society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humanitarian - concerned with or seeking to promote human welfare
  • Synonym: cynical - believing people are motivated purely by self-interest

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpos (hating mankind), from misein (to hate) + anthrōpos (man).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsənˈθrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪsænˈθrɒpɪk / (BrE)
Disliking or distrusting humankind and avoiding human society.
disliking or avoiding humankind

Examples

Her misanthropic outlook made it difficult for her to form close relationships.
The misanthropic writer preferred the solitude of his cabin in the woods.
His misanthropic remarks often alienated him from the rest of the group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philanthropic - showing a desire to help others, especially by giving money to good causes
  • Synonym: misanthropy - the general hatred, distrust, or dislike of humankind

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpos (hating mankind), from misein (to hate) + anthrōpos (man).
