vale

1. NOUN

/ veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
a long, low area of land between hills or mountains, usually with a river running through it; a valley, especially in poetic or literary contexts
a valley, especially a broad one

Examples

They walked through the green vale, listening to the gentle flow of the river.
The poet described a peaceful vale hidden between towering mountains.
Early morning mist hung low over the vale, giving it a mysterious appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley - a low area of land between hills, often with a river

Etymology

From Latin vallis (valley) via Old French and Middle English.
