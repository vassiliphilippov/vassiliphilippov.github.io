Torah

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrə / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
the first five books of the Hebrew Bible, regarded as a unit and forming the foundation of Jewish law and teachings
the first five books of the Hebrew Bible

Examples

The rabbi explained a passage from the Torah to the congregation.
Every year, Jewish communities read the entire Torah in weekly portions.
He studied the laws and stories found in the Torah for his religious education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentateuch - the first five books of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew *tōrāh* (instruction, law).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrə / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
The scroll containing the first five books of the Hebrew Scriptures, used in Jewish religious services.
Jewish sacred scroll; first five books of Hebrew Bible

Examples

The rabbi carefully unrolled the Torah during the service.
Every synagogue has at least one Torah that is read on special occasions.
He studied the Torah to deepen his understanding of Jewish law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentateuch - the first five books of the Hebrew Bible

Etymology

From Hebrew תּוֹרָה (tōrāh), meaning 'instruction' or 'law'.
