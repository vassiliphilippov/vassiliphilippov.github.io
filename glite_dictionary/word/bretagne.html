Bretagne

1. PROPN

/ brəˈtɑn / (AmE), / brəˈtan / (BrE)
a former province in northwestern France, located on a peninsula between the English Channel and the Bay of Biscay, known in English as Brittany.
former province in northwestern France

Examples

Many ancient megaliths can be found throughout Bretagne in northwestern France.
She spent her summer holiday exploring the rugged coastline of Bretagne.
The traditional music of Bretagne has a unique Celtic influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brittany - the English name for the region of Bretagne

Etymology

French form of 'Brittany', from Latin 'Britannia'.
