surcharge

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to fill or load with something to an excessive or overwhelming degree
to fill or load excessively

Examples

The room was surcharged with excitement before the results were announced.
After hours of debate, the atmosphere became surcharged with emotion.
He surcharged the container with more water than it could hold.

Synset Examples

The air was surcharged with tension

Etymology

From Old French 'surcharger' (to overload), from sur- (over) + charger (to load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01491343-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an extra charge added to the usual cost, often as a penalty or for additional services
an additional charge or fee

Examples

Customers will be required to pay a surcharge for late payment of their bills.
A fuel surcharge is added to the cost of airline tickets during times of high oil prices.
There was a surcharge for requesting expedited shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee - a payment made for a service
  • Synonym: penalty - a punishment or consequence for breaking a rule or law

Etymology

From Old French surcharge (excess, overload), from sur- (over) + charger (to load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13346527-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ sərˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / səˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to charge an additional fee to the usual cost, especially for a special service or circumstance
charge an extra fee

Examples

Some airlines surcharge customers for checked baggage.
The hotel surcharged us for early check-in and late checkout.
If you pay by credit card, they may surcharge an extra 3% fee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcharge - charge someone more than the correct or fair amount

Etymology

From Middle French *surcharger*, from Old French *surcharger* (to overcharge).

6. VERB

/ ˈsɜrˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to charge someone an additional or excessive amount of money, especially more than what is considered reasonable or fair
to charge in excess, overcharge

Examples

The taxi driver tried to surcharge tourists by doubling the normal fare.
Many companies surcharge customers for paying their bills late.
Be wary of restaurants that surcharge diners for splitting the check.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcharge - to charge someone too much; synonym for 'surcharge' in this sense
  • Antonym: undercharge - to charge less than the correct or appropriate amount; antonym for 'surcharge' (verb)

Etymology

From Old French *surcharger*, from Late Latin *supercarricāre* (to overload).
