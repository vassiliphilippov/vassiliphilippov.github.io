idle

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪd(ə)l / (BrE)
(of talk or activity) silly, trivial, or having no real purpose or value.
silly or trivial (of talk or activity)

Examples

She dismissed the rumors as idle gossip that should not be taken seriously.
Their conversation was little more than idle small talk to pass the time.
He was annoyed by the idle speculation about his personal life.

Synset Examples

idle pleasure
light banter
light idle chatter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: meaningful - having a serious, useful, or important purpose
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdl̩ / (BrE)
(of an engine) to run at a low speed when disconnected from a load or not in gear.
(of an engine) run when not in gear

Examples

It's bad for the environment to let your car idle for long periods.
She listened to the boat motor gently idling at the dock.
