Catherine the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæθərɪn ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌkæθərɪn ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(1729–1796) The empress of Russia who ruled from 1762 to 1796 and greatly expanded the territory and influence of the Russian Empire.
empress of Russia, 18th century

Examples

Catherine the Great is remembered for bringing Enlightenment ideas to Russia.
Historians often debate the political savvy and reforms enacted by Catherine the Great.
Many territorial gains were made during the reign of Catherine the Great.
