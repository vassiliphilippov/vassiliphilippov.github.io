orally

1. ADV

/ ˈɔːrəli / (AmE), / ˈɒrəli / (BrE)
By means of spoken words rather than in writing.
by spoken, not written, means

Examples

The professor asked the students to present their research orally instead of submitting a paper.
The contract was made orally and not put in writing.
Some medications should be taken orally, not injected.

Synset Examples

these stories were transmitted by word of mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbally - by spoken word rather than in writing

Etymology

From Latin 'oralis' (of the mouth) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɔr.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ə.li / (BrE)
By mouth; in such a way that something is taken through the mouth, especially medicine.
by mouth; via the mouth

Examples

The medication should be taken orally once daily with water.
Certain antibiotics can be administered orally or by injection, depending on the severity of the infection.
After surgery, she was unable to take anything orally for several days.

Synset Examples

he was administered the drug orally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intravenously - by injection directly into a vein

Etymology

From Latin oral(is) + -ly, relating to the mouth.
