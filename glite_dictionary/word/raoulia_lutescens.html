Raoulia lutescens

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈuːli.ə luːˈtɛs.ənz / (AmE), / raʊˈuːlɪ.ə luːˈtɛs.enz / (BrE)
A perennial, mat-forming herb native to New Zealand, known for its hoary, woolly foliage.
New Zealand mat-forming herb species

Examples

Raoulia lutescens thrives in the alpine regions of New Zealand.
The foliage of Raoulia lutescens forms dense mats across rocky ground.
Botanists appreciate Raoulia lutescens for its distinctive woolly appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Raoulia' honors French botanist Étienne Raoul; species epithet 'lutescens' means 'yellowish'.
