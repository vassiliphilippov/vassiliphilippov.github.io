swelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
an abnormal area of raised skin or tissue, often due to injury, infection, or disease
an abnormal raised area on the body

Examples

She noticed a painful swelling on her ankle after the fall.
The doctor examined the swelling and recommended an ice pack to reduce it.
Allergic reactions can cause a swelling in the throat, which may be dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bump - a raised area on a surface or body
  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass, often felt under the skin
  • Synonym: bruise - an injury causing discoloration but not breaking the skin

Etymology

From Old English 'swelling', from 'swellan' (to swell).

/ ˈswɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɛlɪŋ / (BrE)

He noticed a swelling in the pipe, indicating a possible blockage.
