Chimonanthus praecox

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmoʊnænθəs ˈpriːkɒks / (AmE), / kɪˈmɒnænθəs ˈpriːkɒks / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to Japan and China, cultivated for its fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in winter.
deciduous shrub with fragrant yellow flowers

Examples

The Chimonanthus praecox in our garden blooms with fragrant yellow flowers every winter.
Botanists recommend planting Chimonanthus praecox in well-drained soil for optimal growth.
During the cold months, the Chimonanthus praecox adds a splash of color and scent to the landscape.

Etymology

From Greek kheimon (winter) + anthos (flower), and Latin praecox (early).
