diagonal

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæɡənəl / (AmE), / daɪˈaɡən(ə)l / (BrE)
an oblique line of squares of the same color on a checkerboard, along which certain chess or checkers pieces move
oblique checkerboard line

Examples

The bishop can only move along a diagonal during the game.
She counted the number of red squares on the diagonal of the board.
Checkers are often captured by jumping over opponents on the diagonal.

Synset Examples

the bishop moves on the diagonals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - line without angle, not oblique

Etymology

From Latin 'diagonalis', from Greek 'diagonios' (from angle to angle).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈæɡənl / (AmE), / daɪˈaɡən(ə)l / (BrE)
(geometry) A straight line joining any two non-adjacent vertices of a polygon.
line between non-adjacent polygon vertices

Examples

In a square, the diagonal divides it into two congruent triangles.
The diagonal of the rectangle measures five centimeters.
To solve the problem, draw a diagonal connecting opposite corners of the hexagon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: side - a straight edge of a polygon

Etymology

From Late Latin 'diagonalis', from Greek 'diagonios' (from angle to angle).

6. NOUN

/ daɪˈæɡənl̩ / (AmE), / daɪˈaɡənl̩ / (BrE)
(mathematics) A line of entries in a square matrix that runs from one corner to the opposite corner, either from upper left to lower right (main diagonal) or from upper right to lower left (secondary diagonal).
line of entries in a matrix from corner to corner

Examples

When calculating the trace of a matrix, you sum the entries on its diagonal.
The diagonal of the matrix contains all the elements where the row and column indices are the same.
In some matrices, only the diagonal contains nonzero values; the rest are zero.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: main diagonal - the diagonal in a matrix from the upper left to the lower right
  • Degree Lower: secondary diagonal - the diagonal in a matrix from the upper right to the lower left

Etymology

From Latin diagonalis, from Greek diagōnios (from angle to angle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

Pin the pattern to the diagonal for a bias-cut skirt.

Related Terms
