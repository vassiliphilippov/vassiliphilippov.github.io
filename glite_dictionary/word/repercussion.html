repercussion

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
a movement back caused by an impact; a rebound or reflection
movement back after an impact

Examples

The repercussion of the dropped ball sent it bouncing across the floor.
The scientist measured the repercussion of the shock wave on the metal plate.
The sudden repercussion from the collision damaged the fragile equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - a movement back from a surface after hitting it
  • Synonym: reflection - the return of something after striking a surface, as with light or sound

Etymology

From Latin repercussio (a striking back), from repercutere (to strike back).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
An unintended or indirect consequence of an action, often one that occurs later or at some remove.
indirect consequence or effect

Examples

The policy change had significant repercussions throughout the industry.
He was unaware of the possible repercussions of his decision.
New environmental laws often bring repercussions for businesses and consumers alike.

Synset Examples

his declaration had unforeseen repercussions
reverberations of the market crash were felt years later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: effect - a change that is a result or consequence of an action
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From Latin repercussio (a striking back), from 're-' (again) + 'percutere' (to strike).
