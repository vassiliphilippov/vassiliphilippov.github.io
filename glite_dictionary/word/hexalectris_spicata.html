Hexalectris spicata

1. NOUN

/ hɛksəˈlɛktrɪs spaɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / hɛksəˈlɛktrɪs spɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of orchid with yellowish-brown flowers marked with dark veins, native to southeastern Arizona and the eastern United States.
a North American orchid species

Examples

The rare Hexalectris spicata blooms in shaded woodlands throughout the southeastern United States.
Botanists were excited to find a cluster of Hexalectris spicata growing in an unexpected location.
The distinctive yellowish-brown flowers of Hexalectris spicata make it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Hexalectris' as the genus (from Greek hexa- 'six' and lektris 'couch' or 'bed') and 'spicata' meaning 'spiked'.
