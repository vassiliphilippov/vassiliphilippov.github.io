knocked-out

1. ADJ

/ nɑktˈaʊt / (AmE), / nɒktˈaʊt / (BrE)
(informal) Damaged, worn out, or no longer functioning properly, typically as a result of heavy use or impact.
damaged; worn out
informal american

Examples

The old truck is so knocked-out that it can barely run anymore.
He replaced the knocked-out fence panels after the storm.
Most of the machines in that workshop are pretty knocked-out from years of hard use.

Synset Examples

the gym has some of the most knocked-out equipment since Vic Tanny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - damaged and not working correctly
  • Antonym: intact - undamaged; in perfect condition

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'knock out,' meaning to render something unusable or incapacitated.
