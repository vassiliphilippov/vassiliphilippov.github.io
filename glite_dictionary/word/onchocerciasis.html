onchocerciasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑŋkoʊsərˌsaɪˈəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒŋkəsɜːˌsaɪˈæsɪs / (BrE)
A tropical disease caused by infestation with threadlike parasitic roundworms (filaria) transmitted by blackflies, often resulting in skin issues and, if the eyes are affected, possible blindness.
parasitic disease caused by roundworms
medical

Examples

Onchocerciasis is a major concern in many regions of sub-Saharan Africa, affecting millions of people.
Treatment programs have reduced the prevalence of onchocerciasis in some river valleys.
Because onchocerciasis can cause severe itching and blindness, it is also known as river blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: river blindness - Another term for onchocerciasis, emphasizing the potential for blindness

Etymology

From New Latin Onchocerca (the genus of the worm) + -iasis (disease condition).
