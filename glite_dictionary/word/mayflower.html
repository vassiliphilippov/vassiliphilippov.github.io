Mayflower

1. PROPN

/ ˈmeɪˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌflaʊə / (BrE)
the ship that carried the Pilgrim Fathers from England to Massachusetts in 1620
ship that carried Pilgrims to America in 1620

Examples

The Mayflower set sail from Plymouth, England, in September 1620.
Many Americans can trace their ancestry back to passengers on the Mayflower.
The story of the Mayflower voyage is taught in schools across the United States.

Etymology

Named for the mayflower, a common English wildflower; used as the ship's name.
