to enthral

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθrɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrɔːl / (BrE)
to capture and hold the complete attention of someone; to fascinate or enchant deeply
hold spellbound; captivate

Examples

The magician's incredible illusions enthralled the entire audience for over an hour.
She was enthralled by the beauty of the snowy mountains at sunrise.
His storytelling ability enthrals listeners of all ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest of someone
  • Antonym: bore - to cause someone to feel uninterested

Etymology

From Old English *thrall* (slave), influenced by Old Norse *þræll* (slave), originally meaning to enslave or captivate.
