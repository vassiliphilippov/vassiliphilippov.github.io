bleb

1. NOUN

/ blɛb / (AmE), / blɛb / (BrE)
A small blister or elevation of the skin filled with serous fluid.
small fluid-filled blister
medical

Examples

The patient developed a bleb on his arm after the burn.
A small bleb formed at the injection site, which later disappeared.
Doctors examined the fluid inside the bleb to check for infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister - A small bubble on the skin filled with serum and caused by friction, burning, or other damage.

Etymology

From Late Latin 'blebă' (bubble, blister), of imitative origin.
