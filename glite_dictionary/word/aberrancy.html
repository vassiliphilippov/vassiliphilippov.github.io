aberrancy

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɛrənsi / (AmE), / əˈbɛrənsi / (BrE)
A state or condition that is markedly different from what is typical or expected.
marked deviation from the norm

Examples

Genetic aberrancy in the cells can lead to unexpected traits.
His behavior displayed a level of aberrancy that concerned his friends.
Scientists studied the aberrancy in the experiment’s results to find the cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aberration - a deviation from what is normal or expected
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal

Etymology

From Latin 'aberrare' (to wander away, to stray) and the suffix '-ancy' indicating a state or quality.
