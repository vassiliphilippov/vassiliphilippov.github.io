terreplein

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛə.pleɪn / (BrE)
A level area on the top of a rampart or fortification, behind the parapet, where artillery or heavy guns may be mounted.
level area behind parapet on rampart

Examples

Soldiers positioned the cannons on the terreplein to defend the fortress from naval attacks.
The wide terreplein provided enough space for artillery crews to maneuver during battle.
Much of the fort's original terreplein remains intact, offering views over the surrounding landscape.

Etymology

From French 'terre-plein' (solid ground), from 'terre' (earth) + 'plein' (full).
