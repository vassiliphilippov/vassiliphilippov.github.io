unique

1. ADJ

/ juˈnik / (AmE), / juːˈniːk / (BrE)
being the only one of its kind; unlike anything else
the only one of its kind

Examples

Each snowflake is considered unique because no two are exactly alike.
The museum displayed a unique artifact from ancient Egypt.
Her approach to solving the problem was truly unique and impressed everyone.

Synset Examples

a singular example
the unique existing example of Donne's handwriting
a unique copy of an ancient manuscript
certain types of problems have unique solutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singular - exceptionally good or great; remarkable, or referring to a single instance
  • Antonym: common - occurring or found frequently; not rare or unique

Etymology

From French 'unique', from Latin 'unicus' (only, single).
