/ hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)
a single entity made up of connected parts considered together; an assemblage that forms a complete unit
something complete formed by parts

Examples

Each chapter fits together to form the whole of the book.
You can't judge the whole by looking at just one part.
The whole is often greater than the sum of its parts.

Synset Examples

the team is a unit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: part - a piece or segment of something considered in relation to a whole

Etymology

From Old English 'hāl' (unhurt, entire).
