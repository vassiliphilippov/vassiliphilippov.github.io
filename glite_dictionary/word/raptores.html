Raptores

1. NOUN

/ ræpˈtɔːriˌiz / (AmE), / ræpˈtɔːriːz / (BrE)
A former taxonomic group comprising birds of prey, once including both falcons and owls, now considered obsolete in modern classification.
obsolete group of birds of prey

Examples

The term Raptores is found in many early ornithological texts.
Modern ornithologists no longer use Raptores as a valid taxonomic group.
In the past, falcons and owls were both classified under Raptores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird of prey - a bird that hunts and feeds on other animals

Etymology

From Latin raptor (plunderer, robber), used in taxonomy for predatory birds.
