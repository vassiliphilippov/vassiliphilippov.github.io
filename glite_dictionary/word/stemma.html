stemma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˈstɛmə / (BrE)
a simple eye with a single lens, found especially in certain insects and arthropods
simple eye with a single lens

Examples

The larval stages of many insects possess a stemma on each side of the head.
Each stemma is capable of detecting light but not detailed images.
Unlike compound eyes, a stemma has only a single simple lens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocellus - a simple eye found in many invertebrates

Etymology

From Greek stemma (wreath, garland, offspring), applied in biology to denote simple visual organs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə, ˈstɛmə / (BrE)
A diagram, resembling a tree, that illustrates the hypothesized relationships and transmission of manuscripts of a literary work.
tree diagram of manuscript transmission

Examples

Scholars constructed a stemma to trace the history of the medieval texts.
The stemma clarified relationships among several corrupted manuscript copies.
Building a stemma requires analyzing similarities and differences in the manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stemma codicum - A detailed stemma specific to manuscript traditions

Etymology

From Greek 'stemma' meaning 'wreath' or 'pedigree'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13935555-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɛm.ə / (AmE), / ˈstiː.mə / (BrE)
a person's line of descent, ancestry, or hereditary lineage
ancestry; line of descent

Examples

The ancient manuscript traces the stemma of the royal family back several centuries.
Geneticists can reconstruct a person's stemma using DNA analysis and historical records.
Many noble families have elaborate charts depicting their stemma for future generations.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent

Etymology

From Latin 'stemma' (wreath, pedigree), from Greek 'stemma' (wreath, garland, pedigree).
