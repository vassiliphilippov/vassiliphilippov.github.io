cheewink

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiˌwɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːwɪŋk / (BrE)
A North American songbird, the common towhee (Pipilo erythrophthalmus), found in eastern parts of the continent.
eastern North American songbird (towhee)

Examples

Several cheewinks were singing in the brush near the edge of the forest.
The distinctive call of the cheewink is often heard in springtime.
Birdwatchers hoped to spot a cheewink during their morning walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern towhee - a North American songbird, Pipilo erythrophthalmus

Etymology

Imitative, from the bird's call, 'cheewink'.
