swindle

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈswɪn.dl / (BrE)
A fraudulent scheme or action intended to cheat someone out of money or possessions.
fraudulent scheme; cheating act

Examples

The investment turned out to be a swindle that cost people their life savings.
Authorities uncovered a major swindle targeting elderly homeowners.
He was arrested for organizing a swindle involving fake lottery tickets.

Synset Examples

that book is a fraud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - the crime of cheating somebody to get money or goods illegally
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme; a fraud

Etymology

From German 'schwindeln' (to be giddy, to cheat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00781784-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈswɪn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈswɪn.dl̩ / (BrE)
to cheat someone out of money or property by deceit or fraud
cheat by deceit or fraud

Examples

He tried to swindle investors by selling shares in a fake company.
The couple was arrested for attempting to swindle elderly homeowners out of their savings.
She warned her friends not to swindle others, no matter how tempting the opportunity.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

From German 'schwindeln' (to be giddy, to cheat).

WordNet ID: oewn-02578034-v
Synset POS: v
