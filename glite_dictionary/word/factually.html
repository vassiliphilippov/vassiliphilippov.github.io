factually

1. ADV

/ ˈfæktʃuəli / (AmE), / ˈfæktʃuəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on facts; actually or objectively
in a way based on facts

Examples

The statement is factually correct but may be misleading in context.
She argued factually, supporting her claims with reliable data.
News reports should be factually accurate and unbiased.

Synset Examples

they learn much, factually, about the problems of retirement and provision for old age, and, psychologically, in the sharing of their thoughts on retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings; based on facts
  • Antonym: subjectively - in a way that is influenced by personal feelings; not based on facts

Etymology

From 'factual' + '-ly', forming the adverbial form.
