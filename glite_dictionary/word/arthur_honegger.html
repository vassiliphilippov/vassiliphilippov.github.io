Arthur Honegger

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrθɚ hɒˈnɛɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːθə ˈɒnɛɡə / (BrE)
(1892–1955) A Swiss composer, born in France, and founding member of the influential Paris-based composer group Les Six.
Swiss-French composer, member of Les Six

Examples

The compositions of Arthur Honegger are celebrated for their innovation and emotional depth.
During the 1920s, Arthur Honegger was closely associated with the artistic scene in Paris.
One of Arthur Honegger's most famous works is the orchestral piece 'Pacific 231'.
