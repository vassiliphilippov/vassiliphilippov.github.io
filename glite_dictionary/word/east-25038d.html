east

1. NOUN

/ ist / (AmE), / iːst / (BrE)
the eastern part of a country, region, or city
the eastern area of a place

Examples

Many artists have moved to the east of the city, where rents are cheaper.
He grew up in the east and still visits his childhood neighborhood.
The east of the country is known for its beautiful coastline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: west - the western part of a country, region, or city
  • Antonym: north - the northern part of a country, region, or city
  • Antonym: south - the southern part of a country, region, or city

Etymology

From Old English *ēast*, from Proto-Germanic *austan* (east).

Situated in, facing, or moving toward the east.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: west - situated in or facing or moving toward the west
  • Synonym: eastern - relating to or situated in the east

Etymology

From Old English 'ēast', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'oost' and German 'Ost'.

4. NOUN

/ ist / (AmE), / iːst / (BrE)
the direction toward which the sun rises, one of the four main compass points, usually to the right on a standard map.
one of the four cardinal directions

Examples

The sun rises in the east and sets in the west.
If you keep walking to the east, you will eventually reach the coast.
Their house faces the east, so it gets a lot of morning sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: west - the direction opposite to east, another cardinal point
  • Degree Higher: north - the direction to the left of east on a map
  • Degree Lower: south - the direction to the right of east on a map

Etymology

From Old English 'ēast', related to German 'Ost' and Latin 'aurora' (dawn).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ist / (AmE), / iːst / (BrE)
the direction toward the point of the horizon where the sun rises, one of the four main compass points, situated at 90 degrees clockwise from north
the compass direction at 90 degrees

Examples

The sun rises in the east every morning, creating a beautiful glow.
They drove steadily toward the east, watching the landscape gradually change.
Our house faces east, so we get plenty of morning light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: west - the direction opposite to east, at 270 degrees
  • Antonym: north - the direction at 0 degrees on the compass
  • Antonym: south - the direction at 180 degrees on the compass

Etymology

Old English *ēast*, from Proto-Germanic *aust-*, related to the dawn.
