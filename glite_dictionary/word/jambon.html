jambon

1. NOUN

/ ʒæmˈbɒn / (AmE), / ʒæ̃ˈbɒ̃ / (BrE)
a cut of meat from the thigh of a pig, especially when cured or smoked; ham, often used in reference to French-style preparations
ham (esp. French-style cured pork)

Examples

The charcuterie board featured a generous portion of jambon alongside cheeses and olives.
He ordered a sandwich filled with jambon and Gruyère at the Paris café.
French recipes often call for jambon as a key ingredient in savory pastries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ham - meat from the upper part of a pig's leg, usually cured

Etymology

From French 'jambon', from Old French, from 'jambe' (leg), from Late Latin 'gamba'.
