cavalcade

1. NOUN

/ ˌkævəlˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ˌkævəlˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a formal procession or parade of people on horseback, often as part of a ceremonial event
procession of people on horseback

Examples

The royal cavalcade passed through the cheering crowds lining the main street.
A cavalcade of riders paraded proudly during the festival.
We watched the colorful cavalcade disappear into the distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group or line of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly way

Etymology

From Italian cavalcata (a ride, procession), from cavalcare (to ride), from cavallo (horse), from Latin caballus (horse).
