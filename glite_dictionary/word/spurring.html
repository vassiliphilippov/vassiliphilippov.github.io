spurring

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɜr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɜː.rɪŋ / (BrE)
verbal encouragement or urging someone to attempt something
verbal encouragement or urging

Examples

Her constant spurring gave him the courage to apply for the job.
Despite the team's poor performance, the coach's spurring kept them motivated.
Without his friend's spurring, he might never have finished his novel.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless prodding got on his nerves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support, confidence, or hope
  • Synonym: prodding - persistent encouragement or urging
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of dissuading or attempting to prevent someone from doing something

Etymology

From the verb 'spur', originally referring to urging a horse onward with spurs, later extended to urging people verbally.
