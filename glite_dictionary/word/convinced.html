convinced

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɪnst / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnst / (BrE)
completely certain or persuaded about something; having a firm belief or conviction
completely certain or persuaded

Examples

She seemed absolutely convinced that her plan would succeed.
I am not convinced by his explanation.
After reading all the evidence, the jury was finally convinced of his innocence.

Synset Examples

were convinced that it would be to their advantage to join
I am positive he is lying
was confident he would win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty about something
  • Antonym: uncertain - not sure or not convinced

Etymology

Past participle of 'convince', from Latin 'convincere' (to overcome, convict).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈvɪnst / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnst / (BrE)
Completely certain or having a strong belief about something.
completely certain; having a firm belief

Examples

She is a convinced supporter of environmental causes.
After years of research, he became convinced of the theory's truth.
The convinced atheist refused to participate in religious ceremonies.

Synset Examples

a convinced and fanatical pacifist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certain - sure, with no doubt
  • Antonym: doubtful - uncertain or not sure

Etymology

From the past participle of 'convince', which comes from Latin convincere 'to overcome, refute'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00339319-a
Synset POS: a
