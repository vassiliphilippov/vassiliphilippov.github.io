woad

1. NOUN

/ woʊd / (AmE), / wəʊd / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered plant of the genus Isatis, especially Isatis tinctoria, formerly cultivated for the blue dye obtained from its leaves
a dye-yielding plant, Isatis tinctoria

Examples

The ancient Britons used woad to dye their clothes and bodies.
Fields of woad once thrived across medieval Europe for the blue pigment they produced.
Researchers are studying woad as a sustainable source of natural dye.

Etymology

Old English 'wād', from Proto-Germanic *waida- (dye plant).

2. NOUN

/ woʊd / (AmE), / wəʊd / (BrE)
a blue dye obtained from the leaves of the woad plant (Isatis tinctoria), formerly used for coloring fabrics
blue dye from the woad plant

Examples

Historically, the Celts used woad to dye their clothing and even their bodies.
The artisan demonstrated how to extract blue dye from woad during the workshop.
Before indigo became common, European textiles were often colored with woad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - a deep blue dye obtained from certain plants, used as a modern alternative to woad

Etymology

From Old English wād, related to Proto-Germanic *waidą (woad).
