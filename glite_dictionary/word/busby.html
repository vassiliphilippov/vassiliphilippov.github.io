busby

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌz.bi / (AmE), / ˈbʌz.bi / (BrE)
a tall fur hat worn by some British soldiers on ceremonial occasions, especially by hussars and some guards regiments
tall ceremonial military hat
british

Examples

The guard marched proudly in his busby and scarlet uniform.
Tourists often photograph the soldiers in their impressive busbies outside Buckingham Palace.
He adjusted his busby before the ceremonial parade began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearskin - tall fur cap worn by guards regiments

Etymology

Perhaps from the name of a London hatter, Busby, in the 18th century.
