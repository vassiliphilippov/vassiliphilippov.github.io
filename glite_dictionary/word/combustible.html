combustible

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbʌstəbəl / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstɪbəl / (BrE)
a substance that can burn easily and is used as a source of heat or power
a burnable substance

Examples

The warehouse stores large quantities of combustible for industrial use.
Proper storage of combustible is essential to prevent fire hazards.
Certain chemicals are considered highly combustible and must be handled with care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - a material burned to produce heat or power

Etymology

From Latin 'combustibilis' (capable of burning), from 'comburere' (to burn up).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈbʌstəbəl / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstəbəl / (BrE)
able to catch fire and burn easily; flammable.
able to catch fire easily

Examples

You should store combustible materials away from heat sources to prevent accidents.
Many cleaning products are highly combustible and should be handled with care.
The fire spread quickly because the warehouse was full of combustible chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflammable - easily set on fire; flammable
  • Synonym: flammable - easily set on fire; capable of burning quickly
  • Antonym: noncombustible - not capable of catching fire or burning

Etymology

From Late Latin combustibilis (that can be burned), from Latin comburere (to burn up).
