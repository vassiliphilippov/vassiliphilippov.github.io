Lorisidae

1. NOUN

/ lɔːˈrɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɒˈrɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of slow-moving, nocturnal primates found in tropical Asia, usually tailless and omnivorous
a family of tailless, nocturnal Asian primates

Examples

The Lorisidae are recognized by their round heads and large forward-facing eyes.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Lorisidae throughout Southeast Asia.
Unlike monkeys, members of the Lorisidae family lack tails and move slowly among the branches.

Etymology

From modern Latin Lorisidae, from the genus name Loris and the suffix -idae (denoting a zoological family).
