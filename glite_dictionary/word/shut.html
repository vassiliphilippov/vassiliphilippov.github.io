shut

1. ADJ

/ ʃʌt / (AmE), / ʃʌt / (BrE)
(of the eyes, mouth, or another opening) closed so as to prevent access, passage, or exposure, especially temporarily.
closed (esp. eyes or mouth)

Examples

She sat quietly with her shut eyes, listening to the music.
He kept his mouth shut during the entire argument.
The baby fell asleep with her eyes tightly shut.

Synset Examples

he sat quietly with closed eyes
his eyes were shut against the sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closed - not open; shut
  • Antonym: open - not closed or shut; allowing access

Etymology

From Middle English shuten, related to Old English scyttan (to shut, bolt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01659588-a
Synset POS: a

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt / (AmE), / ʃʌt / (BrE)
(of a door, window, etc.) to become closed, often moving from an open position to a closed one.
(of a door/window) become closed

Examples

The door began to shut slowly when the wind picked up.
As the bell rang, the elevator doors shut automatically.
If you don't hold the gate, it will shut on its own.

Synset Examples

The windows closed with a loud bang

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - (of a door, etc.) to become unfastened or no longer closed

Etymology

From Old English scyttan (to shut, bolt), related to German schütten (to shut, close).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01349660-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʃʌt / (AmE), / ʃʌt / (BrE)
to move (a door, window, lid, etc.) so that it covers or blocks an opening; to close something so that passage is obstructed
to close something so as to block an opening

Examples

Please remember to shut the door quietly when you leave.
She shut the window because it was starting to rain.
He shut his eyes for a moment to rest.

Synset Examples

Close the door
shut the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close - to move so as to block an opening or passage
  • Antonym: open - to move (something) so that it is no longer covering or blocking an opening

Etymology

Old English 'scyttan' (to bolt, shut), related to Middle Dutch 'schutten'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01347791-v
Synset POS: v

4. ADJ

/ ʃʌt / (AmE), / ʃʌt / (BrE)
(of a door, window, or container) not open; closed or fastened so as not to allow entry or passage.
not open; closed (adj.)

Examples

The door was firmly shut during the meeting.
Please keep the window shut to prevent the cold air from coming in.
After midnight, all the stores in town are shut.

Synset Examples

the door slammed shut

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - allowing access; not closed
  • Synonym: closed - not open; shut

Etymology

From Middle English shutte, from Old English scyttan (to shut, bolt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01657224-a
Synset POS: a
