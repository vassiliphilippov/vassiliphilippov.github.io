critique

1. NOUN

/ krɪˈtik / (AmE), / krɪˈtiːk / (BrE)
an essay or article that provides a detailed analysis and evaluation of a work, such as a book or play
critical evaluation essay or article

Examples

She wrote a thoughtful critique of the latest novel.
The film received a harsh critique in the newspaper.
After reading the critique, the playwright made changes to the script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an assessment or appraisal of a work, often less thorough than a critique

Etymology

From French 'critique', from Greek 'kritikē' (the art of criticism).

2. NOUN

/ krɪˈtik / (AmE), / krɪˈtiːk / (BrE)
A detailed analysis and assessment of something, especially a literary, philosophical, or political theory.
detailed analysis or assessment

Examples

The editor wrote a thorough critique of the new novel for the magazine.
After reading his film critique, I decided not to watch the movie.
Her critique of the proposal highlighted several important weaknesses.

Synset Examples

constructive criticism is always appreciated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an evaluation or assessment of something
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From French 'critique', from Greek 'kritikē' (the art of judgment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05741963-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ krəˈtik / (AmE), / krɪˈtiːk / (BrE)
to evaluate or analyze something in a detailed and critical way, especially with regard to its merits and faults
evaluate or analyze critically

Examples

The professor asked us to critique the research article in detail.
She was invited to critique the latest performance at the local theater.
Can you critique my essay and suggest improvements?

Synset Examples

She reviews books for the New York Times
Please critique this performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to examine or assess something critically
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of something

Etymology

From French *critique* (criticism), from Greek *kritikē* (art of criticism).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00857194-v
Synset POS: v
