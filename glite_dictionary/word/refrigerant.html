refrigerant

1. NOUN

/ rəˈfrɪdʒɚənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɪdʒərənt / (BrE)
a substance used for cooling, especially in refrigeration systems by absorbing and releasing heat
substance used for cooling in refrigeration

Examples

The air conditioner's refrigerant needs to be replaced regularly for efficient cooling.
Some older refrigerators contain a refrigerant that can harm the environment if not disposed of properly.
Technicians are trained to handle refrigerant safely when repairing cooling systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coolant - a fluid used to cool an engine or other device

Etymology

From Latin refrigerare (to make cool) via French réfrigérant.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈfrɪdʒɚənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɪdʒərənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) causing cooling or freezing; capable of reducing temperature.
causing cooling or freezing

Examples

The scientist used a refrigerant solution to quickly cool the sample.
Liquid nitrogen is often employed as a refrigerant in laboratory settings.
The machine failed to operate without an adequate refrigerant component.

Synset Examples

a refrigerant substance such as ice or solid carbon dioxide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooling - causing a lower temperature

Etymology

From Latin 'refrigerare' (to make cool), from 're-' (again) + 'frigere' (to be cold).
