reception

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
In American football, the act of catching a forward pass.
catching a pass (American football)
american

Examples

He set a new record with twelve receptions in a single game.
The wide receiver made an impressive reception in the end zone.
Each reception gained valuable yards for the team.

Synset Examples

the tight end made a great reception on the 20 yard line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - the act of catching a thrown object

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of receiving or being received, especially a welcome or acceptance
the act of receiving

Examples

The reception of the new policy by the community was overwhelmingly positive.
We were delighted by the warm reception we received at the conference.
Her work met with a mixed reception from the critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptance - the act of accepting something offered
  • Antonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept

Etymology

From Latin 'receptionem', from 'recipere' meaning 'to receive'.

a formal social gathering, especially held after a ceremony such as a wedding, to welcome guests

Examples

  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion, especially one celebrating an event

Clear weather usually improves radio reception in this area.
