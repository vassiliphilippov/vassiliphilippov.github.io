principalship

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪn.sə.pəlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of being a principal, especially of a school
position of a school principal

Examples

She accepted the principalship of the school with enthusiasm and new ideas.
After many years of teaching, his goal was to attain a principalship.
During her principalship, the school’s academic performance improved significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal - the head of a school or other institution

Etymology

From 'principal' + '-ship', a suffix denoting office or state.
