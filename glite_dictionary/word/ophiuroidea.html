Ophiuroidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.fi.jʊˈrɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.fɪ.jʊˈrɔɪ.dɪ.ə / (BrE)
A class of echinoderms that includes brittle stars and basket stars, characterized by slender, flexible arms radiating from a central disk.
class of echinoderms: brittle stars, basket stars

Examples

The Ophiuroidea are easily distinguished from sea stars by their slender, flexible arms.
Marine biologists discovered several new species within the class Ophiuroidea during the deep-sea expedition.
Unlike sea urchins, members of Ophiuroidea have a distinct central disk and long, whip-like arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittle star - a type of marine animal with slender, flexible arms (Ophiuroidea)

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'oura' (tail), with Neo-Latin suffix '-oidea' (like, form).

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˌjʊəˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌəʊfɪjʊəˈrɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A class of echinoderms comprising brittle stars and basket stars, characterized by a distinct central disc and long, flexible arms.
class of brittle stars and basket stars

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Ophiuroidea during their deep-sea expedition.
Brittle stars, classified under Ophiuroidea, display remarkable regenerative abilities.
The Ophiuroidea are easily distinguished from other echinoderms by their slender, flexible arms.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + 'oura' (tail) + 'eidos' (form), referring to their snake-like arms.
