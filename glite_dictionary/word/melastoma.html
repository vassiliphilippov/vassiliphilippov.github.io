Melastoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic shrubs in the family Melastomataceae, known for their leathery leaves, large purple flowers, and edible fleshy black berries.
genus of Asiatic shrubs with purple flowers

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Melastoma shrub with vibrant purple flowers.
Wild Melastoma berries are sometimes collected and eaten in parts of Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Melastoma to better classify the Melastomataceae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the dark berries.
