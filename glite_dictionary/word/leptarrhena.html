A small genus of flowering plants in the saxifrage family, containing a single species, the leatherleaf saxifrage.
a plant genus (leatherleaf saxifrage)

Examples

The Leptarrhena can be found in moist alpine habitats in northwestern North America.
Botanists have long studied the unique adaptations of Leptarrhena to cold climates.
The leatherleaf saxifrage, the only species within Leptarrhena, is notable for its hardy leaves.

Etymology

Modern Latin, genus name formed from Greek 'leptos' (thin, slender) and 'arrhēn' (male, likely referring to botanical characteristics).
