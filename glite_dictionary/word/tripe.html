tripe

1. NOUN

/ traɪp / (AmE), / traɪp / (BrE)
nonsensical or worthless talk or writing; rubbish
nonsense; worthless talk or text

Examples

I don't believe a word of that tripe he wrote in his blog.
The article was full of tripe and wild speculation.
She dismissed his explanation as utter tripe and laughed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense
  • Synonym: drivel - silly or senseless talk
  • Antonym: sense - words that are reasonable and make sense

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪp / (AmE), / ˈtraɪp / (BrE)
the edible lining of the stomach of a ruminant animal, such as a cow, often used in cooking
edible animal stomach lining

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional stew using tripe as the main ingredient.
Some people enjoy eating tripe in spicy soups or broths.
He bought fresh tripe from the butcher for his grandmother's recipe.

Etymology

From Old French *tripe*, meaning 'guts' or 'entrails'.
