Ursidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈɜːsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, heavy mammals including all bears and their extinct relatives.
bear family in zoology

Examples

The family Ursidae includes species such as the brown bear, polar bear, and panda.
Paleontologists discovered fossils belonging to extinct members of Ursidae.
All modern bears are classified in the family Ursidae within the order Carnivora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - a large heavy mammal with thick fur and a short tail

Etymology

From Latin ursus (bear) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.sɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of large mammals that includes bears and their extinct relatives.
the bear family (mammals)

Examples

The Ursidae family contains eight living bear species found across the world.
Fossils of the Ursidae have provided insights into the evolution of bears.
Members of the Ursidae family are known for their strength and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - a large, heavy mammal of the family Ursidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'ursus' (bear) with the suffix '-idae' used in zoological family names.
