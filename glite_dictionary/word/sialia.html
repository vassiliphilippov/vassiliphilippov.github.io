Sialia

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈeɪliə / (AmE), / sɪˈeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds known as bluebirds, recognized for their blue or blue-and-red plumage.
genus of North American bluebirds

Examples

The Sialia genus includes the eastern, western, and mountain bluebirds.
Researchers observed several Sialia nesting in the park during the spring migration.
Birdwatchers are often excited to spot a Sialia due to its vibrant coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebird - a North American songbird with blue plumage

Etymology

From New Latin Sialia, originally coined in scientific classification.
