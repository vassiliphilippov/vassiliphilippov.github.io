governance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnəns / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənəns / (BrE)
The act or process of governing or exercising authority over an organization, state, or people.
the act of governing; management

Examples

The university's policies emphasize good governance and transparency.
Corporate governance has become a major topic in business ethics.
Strong governance helps ensure the fair use of public resources.

Synset Examples

regulations for the governing of state prisons
he had considerable experience of government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country or state
  • Antonym: misrule - bad or improper governance or management

Etymology

From Old French gouvernance, from governer (to govern).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnəns / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənəns / (BrE)
the people or groups who make up the body responsible for managing or administering an organization, institution, or country
people or groups responsible for administration

Examples

The university's new governance includes representatives from both faculty and students.
Effective governance is essential for the stability and growth of large organizations.
Questions were raised about the transparency of the city's governance during the crisis.

Synset Examples

he claims that the present administration is corrupt
the governance of an association is responsible to its members
he quickly became recognized as a member of the establishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the group of people responsible for running an organization or government
  • Synonym: management - the group who direct or administer an organization
  • Synonym: government - the body with the authority to make and enforce laws in a country or organization

Etymology

From Old French 'governer' via Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, to govern).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
