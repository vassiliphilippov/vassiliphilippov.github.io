absolute

1. ADJ

/ ˈæbsəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
(of a right or rule) not capable of being violated or infringed; unconditional and unchallengeable.
not capable of being violated or infringed

Examples

Some argue that human dignity is an absolute right and should never be compromised.
The constitution guarantees certain absolute freedoms for all citizens.
An absolute ban on discrimination is necessary to ensure equality.

Synset Examples

infrangible human rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inviolable - never to be broken, infringed, or dishonored

Etymology

From Latin absolutus (completed, absolute, perfect), past participle of absolvere (to set free, make complete).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00094922-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈæb.səˌlut / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.ljuːt / (BrE)

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ ˈæbsəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
(philosophy) That which exists independently and is not relative to anything else; an ultimate reality or principle that is complete and beyond limitation.
philosophical ultimate reality

Examples

Many philosophers have debated the existence of the absolute throughout history.
He devoted his life to seeking the absolute in both art and science.
Some claim that moral laws are based on an absolute rather than human convention.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relative - that which is dependent on or related to something else

Etymology

From Latin absolutus (complete, unrestricted, perfect).
