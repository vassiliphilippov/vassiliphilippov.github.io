Musaceae

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈseɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈseɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical, treelike herbs including bananas and plantains, native to Asia and Africa
family of tropical plants (bananas, plantains)

Examples

The Musaceae family includes both edible bananas and plantains.
Many species in Musaceae are cultivated for their fruit worldwide.
Botanists classify bananas under the family Musaceae.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Musaceae', based on 'Musa', the genus of bananas.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈseɪsiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈseɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical, treelike herbs that includes bananas and plantains, native mainly to Asia.
family of tropical herbs (bananas, plantains)

Examples

The Musaceae are distinguished by their large, broad leaves and fleshy fruit clusters.
Scientists are studying the evolution of Musaceae to improve banana crop resilience.
The Musaceae family plays a vital role in tropical agriculture worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - the edible fruit of plants in the genus Musa, part of Musaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Musaceae', based on the genus 'Musa' (banana genus) plus the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
