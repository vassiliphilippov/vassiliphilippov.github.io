Hamsun

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑːm.sʊn / (AmE), / ˈhæm.sʊn / (BrE)
(1859–1952) Norwegian novelist and writer, known for influential works such as 'Hunger' and 'Growth of the Soil'.
Norwegian novelist (1859–1952)

Examples

Hamsun won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1920 for his novel 'Growth of the Soil'.
Many critics regard Hamsun as the father of modern Norwegian literature.
During his lifetime, Hamsun was both celebrated and controversial for his unique literary style.
