Walapai

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləˌpaɪ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Walapai people of northwestern Arizona.
the Yuman language of the Walapai people

Examples

She is learning to speak Walapai to better connect with her heritage.
Researchers have published a grammar of the Walapai language.
Several elders teach children their stories in Walapai each week.

Etymology

From the name 'Walapai', an Indigenous people of Arizona.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwæləˌpaɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people native to the Colorado River valley in northwestern Arizona.
member of a Native American people of Arizona

Examples

The Walapai have preserved many of their cultural traditions despite modern influences.
Several Walapai elders spoke about the tribe's history at the community event.
He studied the language and customs of the Walapai during his research in Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hualapai - Alternate spelling of Walapai, referring to the same people.
