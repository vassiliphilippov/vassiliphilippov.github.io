passer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsə / (BrE)
(in American football) a player who attempts to advance the ball by throwing a forward pass.
football player who throws forward passes

Examples

The team's starting passer broke the school record for touchdown throws this season.
A skilled passer can change the outcome of a football game.
The coach praised the young passer for his quick decision-making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - player who usually serves as the primary passer in football

Etymology

From 'pass' + '-er', denoting someone who passes.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæsər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsə / (BrE)
a person who passes by, especially casually or by chance
person passing by casually

Examples

The old man sat on the bench, greeting each passer with a gentle smile.
A sudden shout startled the passer walking along the quiet street.
The artist sketched a passer who paused briefly to admire his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bystander - a person who is present at an event but does not take part
  • Synonym: onlooker - a person who watches an event without participating

Etymology

From the verb 'pass' + agent noun suffix '-er'.
