Passer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsər / (AmE), / ˈpæsə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Passeridae, comprising the true sparrows
genus of true sparrows

Examples

The Passer genus includes the well-known house sparrow.
Biologists study Passer species to understand adaptation to urban environments.
One of the most widespread birds in the world belongs to the genus Passer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Passeridae - the family of true sparrows

Etymology

From Latin 'passer' meaning 'sparrow'.
