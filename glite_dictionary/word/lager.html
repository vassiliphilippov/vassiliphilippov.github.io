lager

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːɡər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːɡə / (BrE)
a type of beer that is fermented and conditioned at low temperatures, typically using bottom-fermenting yeast, and is often pale and light in flavor
a light, bottom-fermented beer

Examples

He ordered a cold lager to go with his meal.
Many people prefer the crisp taste of lager on a hot summer day.
The brewery specializes in several award-winning lagers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ale - a type of beer brewed using top-fermenting yeast, typically with a stronger flavor

Etymology

From German 'Lagerbier', literally ‘storage beer’, referring to the beer being stored for maturation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːɡər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːɡə / (BrE)
a camp defended by arranging wagons in a circle, especially as used by South African pioneers for protection against attack
camp of wagons formed into a defensive circle

Examples

During the trek, the pioneers formed a lager each night for safety.
The Boers protected themselves by creating a lager from their wagons.
History books describe how families slept within the lager to guard against attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kraal - an enclosure for cattle or a defensive enclosure made of thornbushes or stakes, especially in southern Africa

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'laer', from Dutch 'leger' (camp, army).
