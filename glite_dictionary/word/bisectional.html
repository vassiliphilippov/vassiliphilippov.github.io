bisectional

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈsɛk.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈsɛk.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
relating to or involving bisection, especially the act or process of dividing something into two equal parts
relating to division into two equal parts

Examples

The engineer analyzed the bisectional properties of the support beam for maximum strength.
Mathematicians often study bisectional methods to find roots of equations numerically.
The artist's design features a bisectional line that perfectly divides the canvas in two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bisection - the division of something into two equal parts

Etymology

Formed from 'bisection' (from Latin 'bis-' meaning twice + 'sectio' meaning cutting) and the suffix '-al'.
