Lactuca sativa capitata

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtjuːkə səˈtaɪvə kæpɪˈteɪtə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə səˈtaɪvə kæpɪˈteɪtə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of lettuce distinguished by leaves arranged in a dense rosette that form a compact head.
variety of lettuce with a compact head

Examples

The crisp texture of Lactuca sativa capitata makes it a favorite in salads.
Many gardeners prefer to grow Lactuca sativa capitata for its compact, easy-to-harvest heads.
Supermarkets often stock several varieties of Lactuca sativa capitata throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head lettuce - another term for lettuce with a compact head

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lactuca' meaning lettuce, 'sativa' meaning cultivated, and 'capitata' meaning headed.
