Pyrrosia lingua

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈroʊsiə ˈlɪŋɡwə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒsɪə ˈlɪŋɡwə / (BrE)
An East Asian fern with fronds shaped like tongues, sometimes classified in the genus Cyclophorus.
East Asian fern with tongue-shaped fronds

Examples

The Pyrrosia lingua is popular among gardeners for its unusual, tongue-shaped leaves.
In Japan, Pyrrosia lingua is sometimes grown as an ornamental houseplant.
Researchers examined how Pyrrosia lingua adapts to dry conditions in rocky habitats.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pyrrosia' and species epithet 'lingua' (meaning 'tongue', referring to the shape of the frond).
