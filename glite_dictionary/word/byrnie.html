byrnie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːni / (AmE), / ˈbɜːni / (BrE)
a long, usually sleeveless, tunic made of chain mail, formerly worn as defensive armor
a chain mail tunic; old armor
archaic

Examples

The knight donned his byrnie before heading into battle.
Archaeologists found a rusted byrnie among the ancient burial goods.
In old sagas, heroes are often described wearing a shimmering byrnie into combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hauberk - a long coat of mail; a type of chain mail armor

Etymology

From Old English 'byrne' (mailcoat), of Germanic origin.
