Thiobacillus

1. NOUN

/ θaɪ.oʊ.bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / θaɪ.əʊ.bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
A genus of motile, rod-shaped, gram-negative bacteria that obtain energy by oxidizing sulfur compounds.
genus of sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
medical

Examples

Thiobacillus species are commonly found in soils rich in sulfur compounds.
The laboratory identified the bacteria as Thiobacillus through genetic analysis.
Researchers study Thiobacillus to better understand sulfur cycling in the environment.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'thio-' (sulfur) + 'bacillus' (rod-shaped bacterium).

2. NOUN

/ θaɪ.oʊ.bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / θaɪ.əʊ.bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small rod-shaped bacteria found in sewage or soil, known for oxidizing sulfur compounds.
genus of sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
medical

Examples

Some species of Thiobacillus play a crucial role in the sulfur cycle.
Researchers isolated Thiobacillus from samples taken at the sewage treatment plant.
The presence of Thiobacillus in the soil can aid in natural remediation processes.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining thio- (sulfur) and Bacillus (rod-shaped bacteria).
