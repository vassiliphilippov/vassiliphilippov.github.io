Polygonaceae

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪɡəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, chiefly found in temperate regions, including buckwheats and knotweeds
plant family including buckwheats and knotweeds
medical

Examples

Buckwheat belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many important food crops.
Certain invasive species in the Polygonaceae family are known for spreading rapidly along riverbanks.
Plant scientists have classified knotweed as part of the Polygonaceae due to its characteristic flower structure.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'polygonon' (knotgrass) + '-aceae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪgəˈneɪsi.i / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪgəˈneɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, chiefly from temperate regions, that includes buckwheats and docks
family of flowering plants, including buckwheat

Examples

Many species in the Polygonaceae are known for their jointed stems and sheathing stipules.
Buckwheat, a member of the Polygonaceae, is cultivated for its grain-like seeds.
Botanists often study the Polygonaceae to understand plant diversity in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonon' (knotweed) + New Latin '-aceae' (denoting plant families).
