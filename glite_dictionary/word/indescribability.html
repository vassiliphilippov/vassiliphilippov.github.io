indescribability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌskraɪbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌskraɪbəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible or extremely difficult to describe in words.
impossibility of being described

Examples

The indescribability of the landscape left the travelers lost for words.
Some experiences possess a certain indescribability that transcends what language can capture.
She spoke of the pain’s indescribability, emphasizing that no words could ever do it justice.

Synset Examples

Here, humility and prayer are connected directly to Evagrius' apophatic reflection and are subsumed in the indescribability of God's being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe; beyond words
  • Antonym: expressibility - the quality of being able to be expressed or described

Etymology

From 'indescribable' (Latin prefix in- ‘not’ + describere ‘to describe’) + -ity.
