to butylate

1. VERB

/ ˈbjuːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to introduce a butyl group into a chemical compound
add a butyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The researchers decided to butylate the compound to increase its solubility.
When you butylate the molecule, its reactivity may change significantly.
The process of butylating certain chemicals is common in pharmaceutical synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkylate - to introduce an alkyl group into a compound

Etymology

From 'butyl', denoting a four-carbon alkyl group, and the suffix '-ate' denoting a chemical process.
