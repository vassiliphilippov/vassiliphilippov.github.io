harlequin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrlɪkwɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlɪkwɪn / (BrE)
A comic character typically dressed in multicolored, diamond-patterned costume, originating from the commedia dell'arte, or any clownish or buffoonish person.
comic character or clown

Examples

The harlequin danced across the stage, delighting the audience with his antics.
At the festival, a harlequin in a patchwork costume handed out balloons to children.
Her colorful outfit made her look like a modern harlequin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a comic entertainer who wears funny clothes and makeup
  • Synonym: buffoon - a ridiculous but amusing person; a clown

Etymology

From Italian *Arlecchino*, the name of the comic servant character in commedia dell’arte, itself from Old French *Hellequin*.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑrləˌkwɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlɪkwɪn / (BrE)
to variegate or decorate with patches, spots, or marks of different colors
variegate with spots or marks

Examples

Large clouds harlequined the autumn sky with shifting patterns of light and shadow.
The artist harlequined the wall with bold, irregular patches of color.
After playing in the mud, the puppy's fur was harlequined with brown spots.

Synset Examples

His face was harlequined with patches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mottle - to mark with spots or smears of color
  • Synonym: spotted - marked with spots of color or light

Etymology

From Harlequin, a comic character in Italian commedia dell'arte known for a multicolored costume; by extension to objects marked in a similar way.
