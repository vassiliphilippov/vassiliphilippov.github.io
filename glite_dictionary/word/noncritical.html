noncritical

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
not involving or relating to a crisis, emergency, or severe situation; not critical.
not in a state of crisis or emergency

Examples

The patient was moved to a noncritical care unit after showing improvement.
Only noncritical systems will be shut down during the routine maintenance.
Please reserve this line for emergencies, not for noncritical matters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: critical - involving a crisis or severe situation; essential or urgent

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'critical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00654719-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkrɪdɪkl̩ / (AmE), / nɒnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
not involving a crucial or decisive point; not essential or not at risk of abrupt change
not crucial or not at risk

Examples

The hospital reserved the intensive care beds for the most serious cases, moving noncritical patients to other wards.
The system can continue functioning even if some noncritical components fail.
Most of the repairs required are noncritical and can be scheduled for later.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: critical - involving a decisive or crucial point

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'critical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00655555-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
Not involving or adhering to established standards of criticism or evaluation; not critical.
not complying with critical standards

Examples

The report was rejected for its noncritical analysis of the data.
Her review was considered noncritical, as it lacked any substantive feedback.
Many noncritical readers accept information without questioning its accuracy.

Synset Examples

news sources reflected uncritical estimates of the number of juvenile addicts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: critical - involving careful judgment or evaluation
