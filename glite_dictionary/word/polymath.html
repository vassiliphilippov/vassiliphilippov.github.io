polymath

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːliˌmæθ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌmæθ / (BrE)
a person who has wide-ranging knowledge or learning across multiple fields
person with extensive knowledge in many areas

Examples

Leonardo da Vinci is often described as a true polymath for his achievements in art, science, and engineering.
The conference speaker was a polymath with expertise ranging from mathematics to music.
In today’s world, being a polymath is rare due to the vast amount of specialized knowledge in each field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalist - a person with knowledge in many areas rather than specialization
  • Antonym: specialist - a person highly skilled in a specific area

Etymology

From Greek polymathēs, from poly- (many) + manthanein (to learn).
