billhook

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈbɪlhʊk / (BrE)
A tool with a hooked blade used for cutting wood or pruning shrubs and branches.
hooked pruning or cutting tool

Examples

The gardener trimmed the hedges using a billhook to cut through thick branches.
He always carries his old billhook when he works in the woods.
A billhook is essential for clearing brambles and small trees.

Synset Examples

he used a bill to prune branches off of the tree

Etymology

From Middle English 'bille' (hooked blade) + 'hook.'
