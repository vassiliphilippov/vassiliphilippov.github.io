torpid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔr.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.pɪd / (BrE)
In a state of physical or biological inactivity or dormancy; having slowed functions or movements, especially as a natural response to cold or lack of activity.
inactive or dormant state

Examples

The lizard remained torpid throughout the chilly morning until the sun warmed the rocks.
After eating a large meal, she felt unusually torpid and unable to concentrate on her work.
During winter, certain fish become torpid and conserve energy by greatly reducing their activity.

Synset Examples

dormant buds
a hibernating bear
torpid frogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormant - in a state of rest or inactivity
  • Antonym: active - engaged in action or movement

Etymology

From Latin torpidus (numb, sluggish), from torpere (to be numb or sluggish).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɔr.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.pɪd / (BrE)
lacking energy or movement; inactive, sluggish, or showing little interest or feeling
inactive or sluggish; apathetic

Examples

After the long meeting, I felt so torpid that I could barely keep my eyes open.
During winter, some animals become torpid to conserve energy.
His torpid response to the news surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

she was fat and inert
a sluggish worker
a mind grown torpid in old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluggish - moving or reacting slowly; lacking energy
  • Synonym: lethargic - drowsy and lacking energy
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or requiring a lot of energy or activity

Etymology

From Latin torpidus (numb, sluggish), from torpere (to be numb or inactive).
