unintelligible

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈtɛlɪdʒɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible to understand because of poor articulation, loud noise, or other interference
impossible to understand (speech, writing, etc.)

Examples

The announcement was so distorted by static that it became unintelligible to everyone in the room.
His accent was so thick that his words were almost unintelligible to outsiders.
She tried to call for help, but her voice was unintelligible over the sound of the crashing waves.

Synset Examples

unintelligible speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible to understand
  • Antonym: intelligible - able to be understood; clear enough to be understood

Etymology

From Latin 'intelligibilis' (that can be understood) with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈtɛlədʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
impossible or extremely difficult to understand; not able to be comprehended
impossible or very hard to understand

Examples

His accent was so thick that his speech became unintelligible to most of the audience.
The tape recording was old and unintelligible after years of damage.
The note was written in such a hurry that it was almost unintelligible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible to understand; not able to be comprehended
  • Antonym: intelligible - able to be understood; clear enough to be comprehended

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'intelligibilis' (able to be understood).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00537516-s
Synset POS: s
