dysosmia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑzmiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒzmɪə / (BrE)
A medical disorder in which the sense of smell is impaired or distorted.
disorder of the sense of smell
medical

Examples

The patient reported dysosmia following a head injury.
Chronic sinus infections can sometimes lead to dysosmia.
Researchers are studying treatments for dysosmia caused by viral infections.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + osme (smell).
