pleasant

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈplɛzənt / (BrE)
giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment; agreeable or enjoyable.
agreeable; enjoyable; pleasing

Examples

We spent a pleasant afternoon relaxing by the lake.
Her voice has a pleasant tone that puts people at ease.
It was a pleasant surprise to see my old friend again.

Synset Examples

we had a pleasant evening together
a pleasant scene
pleasant sensations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or agreeable; causing discomfort or displeasure
  • Synonym: enjoyable - giving joy or pleasure; pleasurable

Etymology

From Old French 'pleasant', from 'plaisant' (pleasing), present participle of 'plaisir' (to please), from Latin 'placere' (to please).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈplɛzənt / (BrE)
(of a person) friendly and good-tempered; giving a sense of enjoyment by their manners or behavior.
friendly and agreeable (person)

Examples

Despite the stressful situation, she remained a pleasant companion throughout the evening.
It's always easier to work with someone who is genuinely pleasant and considerate.
He tried to be as pleasant as possible to the new colleagues.

Synset Examples

I didn't enjoy it and probably wasn't a pleasant person to be around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeable - friendly and easy to get along with
  • Antonym: unpleasant - not friendly or enjoyable

Etymology

From Old French plaisant (pleasing), from plaisir (to please), ultimately from Latin placēre (to please).

WordNet ID: oewn-01591274-s
Synset POS: s
