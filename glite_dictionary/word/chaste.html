chaste

1. ADJ

/ tʃeɪst / (AmE), / tʃeɪst / (BrE)
abstaining from sexual activity considered immoral or unlawful; maintaining sexual purity or modesty.
abstaining from immoral sexual activity

Examples

The monks vowed to remain chaste for the rest of their lives.
She lived a chaste life, devoted to spiritual pursuits instead of romance.
Many ancient cultures praised those who remained chaste until marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: impure - not morally pure or chaste

Etymology

From Latin castus (pure, morally clean).

2. ADJ

/ tʃeɪst / (AmE), / tʃeɪst / (BrE)
not having engaged in sexual intercourse; morally pure, especially in regard to sexual conduct
morally pure; sexually abstinent

Examples

The knight took a vow to remain chaste until marriage.
She lived a chaste life dedicated to her faith and service to others.
Despite many temptations, he chose to stay chaste and true to his values.

Synset Examples

a holy woman innocent and chaste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virginal - not having had sexual experience; pure
  • Antonym: impure - lacking purity, especially in moral or sexual respects

Etymology

From Latin 'castus' (pure, morally clean).

3. ADJ

/ tʃeɪst / (AmE), / tʃeɪst / (BrE)
simple and restrained in style or design; not ornate
simple and restrained in style or design

Examples

Her wedding dress had a chaste elegance, with only minimal decorative details.
The architect favored a chaste style, avoiding excessive ornamentation in the building's facade.
The chaste lines of the vase made it a classic example of minimalist design.

Synset Examples

a chaste border of conventionalized flowers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated; opposite of 'chaste' in design

Etymology

From Latin castus (pure, clean, morally pure).
