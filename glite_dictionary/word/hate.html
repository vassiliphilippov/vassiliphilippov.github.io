hate

1. NOUN

/ heɪt / (AmE), / heɪt / (BrE)
an intense feeling of strong dislike or hostility, often leading to a desire to act against someone or something
intense feeling of strong dislike

Examples

The politician's speech stirred up hate among the crowd.
He was motivated by hate, not by justice.
There is no place for hate in a tolerant society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: love - an intense feeling of deep affection; opposite of 'hate'
  • Synonym: loathing - a strong feeling of disgust or intense dislike

Etymology

Old English *hatian* (to hate), related to Old High German *hazan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07561835-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ heɪt / (AmE), / heɪt / (BrE)
to dislike someone or something very strongly; to feel intense aversion or hostility
to dislike intensely

Examples

Many people hate being stuck in traffic for hours every day.
I absolutely hate lying to my friends, even when the truth is difficult.
She hates broccoli so much that she won't even touch it.

Synset Examples

I hate Mexican food
She detests politicians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detest - to dislike intensely, often with a sense of moral disapproval
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong positive feeling for someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *hatan* (to hate), related to Old High German *hazzan* and Old Norse *hata*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01778057-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ heɪt / (AmE), / heɪt / (BrE)
to feel and openly express strong dislike or hostility toward someone
express strong dislike or hostility

Examples

She openly hates her former boss and never speaks kindly of him.
He hated being the center of attention at social events.
They hate anyone who betrays their trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detest - to dislike intensely
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection for
  • Degree Lower: dislike - to not like
  • Degree Higher: to loathe - to feel intense disgust or hatred for

Etymology

From Old English 'hatan' (to persecute, treat as an enemy), related to Old High German 'hazan'.
