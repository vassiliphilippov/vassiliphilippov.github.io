Testudo

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstjuːdoʊ / (AmE), / tɛˈstjuːdəʊ / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Testudinidae, comprising certain true tortoises
genus of true tortoises

Examples

The Galápagos giant tortoise belongs to the genus Testudo.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Testudo genus during their expedition.
Many pet owners keep species from the Testudo genus due to their manageable size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoise - land-dwelling reptile with a domed shell

Etymology

From Latin *testūdō* meaning 'tortoise'.

2. NOUN

/ tɛˈstuːdoʊ / (AmE), / tɛˈstjuːdəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of tortoises in the family Testudinidae, including several species of Mediterranean and Eurasian tortoises.
genus of tortoises

Examples

The Testudo genus includes some of the most popular pet tortoises.
Researchers studied the shell formation in several Testudo species.
Many Mediterranean tortoises belong to the Testudo group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoise - a land-dwelling reptile with a hard shell

Etymology

From Latin *testūdo* (tortoise).
