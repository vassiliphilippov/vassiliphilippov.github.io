tilter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪltər / (AmE), / ˈtɪltə / (BrE)
a device used to tip or tilt a cask, barrel, or similar vessel to empty its contents without disturbing the sediment or dregs at the bottom.
device for tilting casks to empty them

Examples

They used a tilter to pour the wine carefully without stirring up the sediment.
The brewery invested in a new tilter to make bottling more efficient and clean.
A skilled worker operates the tilter to ensure only the clear liquid is drawn from the cask.

Etymology

From 'tilt' (to cause to lean or tip) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪltər / (AmE), / ˈtɪltə / (BrE)
a person who participates in a tilt or joust, especially in medieval tournaments
person who jousts in a tournament

Examples

The medieval tilter prepared his lance before riding into the arena.
Each tilter wore a suit of armor designed for the tournament.
As a skilled tilter, he was celebrated throughout the kingdom for his bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joust - a combat between two mounted knights with lances

Etymology

From Middle English 'tilten' (to joust) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
