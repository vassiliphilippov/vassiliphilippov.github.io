Cimicifuga americana

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
a species of bugbane native to eastern North America, known for its erect racemes of white flowers
a North American species of bugbane

Examples

Cimicifuga americana is commonly found in shady woodlands throughout the eastern United States.
Gardeners prize Cimicifuga americana for its tall spikes of delicate white flowers.
The leaves of Cimicifuga americana are divided and deeply toothed, adding interest to woodland gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbane - a common name for plants of the genus Actaea, including Cimicifuga

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cimicifuga' (a former genus name for bugbane plants) and 'americana' (referring to America).
