Onondaga

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnənˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˌɒnənˈdɑːɡə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Onondaga people, native to the area of present-day New York.
Iroquoian language of the Onondaga people

Examples

Only a handful of elders still speak Onondaga fluently in the community.
Linguists are working to preserve Onondaga as part of broader indigenous language revitalization efforts.
He studied both Onondaga and Mohawk to understand their linguistic similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - a family of languages to which Onondaga belongs

Etymology

From the name of the Onondaga people, itself likely from their autonym meaning 'people of the hills' in the Onondaga language.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnənˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˌɒnənˈdɑːɡə / (BrE)
a member of an Iroquoian Indigenous people originally from central New York State, United States
a member of an Iroquoian Indigenous people

Examples

The Onondaga played an important role in the Iroquois Confederacy.
Several Onondaga traditions are still practiced today by their descendants.
An Onondaga leader spoke at the cultural heritage event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois - a member of a group of related Indigenous peoples in northeastern North America
  • Synonym: Mohawk - a member of another nation within the Iroquois Confederacy

Etymology

From the Onondaga word *Onöñda’gega’* (people of the hills).
