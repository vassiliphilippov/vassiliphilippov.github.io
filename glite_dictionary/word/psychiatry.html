psychiatry

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkaɪətri / (AmE), / saɪˈkaɪətri / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental disorders.
medical field for mental disorders
medical

Examples

After years of medical training, she decided to specialize in psychiatry.
Advancements in psychiatry have improved treatment for many mental illnesses.
The conference brought together experts in psychiatry from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior

Etymology

From French 'psychiatrie', from Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and 'iatreia' (healing).
