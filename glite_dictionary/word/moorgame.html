moorgame

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡeɪm / (BrE)
a reddish-brown grouse found on the upland moors of Great Britain, especially known as the red grouse
reddish-brown grouse of British moors
british

Examples

The moorgame is prized by hunters during the shooting season on Scottish estates.
We spotted several moorgame feeding among the heather.
Conservation efforts aim to protect the natural habitat of the moorgame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red grouse - a species of grouse native to the British Isles, also called the moorgame

Etymology

From 'moor' (open uncultivated land) + 'game' (wild animals hunted for sport or food).
