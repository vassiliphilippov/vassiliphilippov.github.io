Rommel

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑməl / (AmE), / ˈrɒməl / (BrE)
Erwin Rommel (1891–1944), a German field marshal celebrated for his military leadership during the North African campaign in World War II.
German field marshal, WWII North Africa

Examples

Erwin Rommel was known as the 'Desert Fox' for his tactics in North Africa.
The Allied forces respected Rommel for his strategic brilliance and chivalry.
Many military historians have written biographies about Rommel's campaigns and leadership style.

Etymology

Surname of Erwin Rommel, German origin.
