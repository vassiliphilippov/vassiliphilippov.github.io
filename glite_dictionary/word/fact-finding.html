fact-finding

1. ADJ

/ ˈfækt ˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfækt ˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a mission, committee, or activity) intended to discover information or ascertain the facts about a situation.
intended to discover facts

Examples

The organization sent a fact-finding team to gather evidence about the incident.
A fact-finding mission can help uncover the truth behind complex issues.
The judge requested a fact-finding report before making any final decisions.

Synset Examples

a fact-finding committee
investigative reporting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative - involving investigation; intended to investigate

Etymology

From English 'fact' + 'finding', referring to the act of seeking facts.
