siltstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪltˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪltstəʊn / (BrE)
A sedimentary rock composed mainly of consolidated silt-sized particles, finer than sandstone but coarser than claystone.
fine-grained sedimentary rock

Examples

The geologist studied a sample of siltstone taken from the valley floor.
Layers of siltstone were visible in the cliff, revealing ancient river deposits.
Unlike sandstone, siltstone feels smooth and sometimes breaks into thin sheets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sandstone - sedimentary rock made mainly of sand-sized particles
  • Degree Lower: claystone - sedimentary rock composed mainly of clay-sized particles

Etymology

From 'silt' (fine soil or earth) + 'stone'.
