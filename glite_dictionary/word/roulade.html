roulade

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈlɑd / (AmE), / ruːˈlɑːd / (BrE)
A dish consisting of a thin slice of meat rolled around a filling and cooked.
meat dish rolled around filling

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious roulade stuffed with spinach and cheese for dinner.
She served a chicken roulade at the holiday party, impressing all the guests.
Making a beef roulade requires some skill in rolling the meat around the flavorful filling.

Etymology

From French 'roulade', from 'rouler' meaning 'to roll'.

2. NOUN

/ ruˈlɑd / (AmE), / ruːˈlɑːd / (BrE)
(music) An elaborate run or rapid succession of several notes sung to one syllable in vocal music.
musical ornament: several notes sung to one syllable

Examples

The soprano dazzled the audience with a breathtaking roulade at the end of the aria.
Mastering the roulade requires excellent breath control and vocal agility.
Early Baroque music often features long roulades to showcase the singer's skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - A rapid succession of musical notes sung or played smoothly
  • Synonym: melisma - A group of notes sung to a single syllable

Etymology

From French 'roulade', from 'rouler' (to roll).
