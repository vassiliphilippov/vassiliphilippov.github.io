child

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a young human being below the age of puberty or below the legal age of majority
young person; boy or girl

Examples

The little child clung to her mother's hand as they crossed the street.
Each child in the class received a book for the reading challenge.
As a child, he spent hours exploring the woods behind his house.

Synset Examples

she writes books for children
they're just kids
‘tiddler’ is a British term for youngster

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown person; opposite of child
  • Synonym: kid - informal synonym for child

Etymology

Old English cild (infant, young person).

2. NOUN

/ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / tʃaɪld / (BrE)
A person who behaves in an immature or childish way, regardless of age.
a person behaving childishly

Examples

Stop acting like a child and take responsibility for your actions.
Although he is thirty, he can be a child when things don't go his way.
Don't be such a child; try to handle criticism more maturely.

Synset Examples

he remained a child in practical matters as long as he lived
stop being a baby!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby - a person who behaves in an overly childish or immature manner
  • Antonym: adult - a person who behaves with maturity

Etymology

From Old English 'cild' (fetus, infant, unborn or newly born person).

4. NOUN

/ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / tʃaɪld / (BrE)
A member of a particular clan, tribe, or people, often used in religious or literary contexts.
member of a clan or tribe

Examples

In the ancient texts, the children of Israel wandered through the desert for forty years.
He spoke with pride of his ancestors, calling himself a child of the Lakota tribe.
The children of Abraham are mentioned frequently in the scriptures.

Synset Examples

the children of Israel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old English cild (fetus, infant, unborn or newly born person; child), related to German Kind (child).
