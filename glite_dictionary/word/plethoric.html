plethoric

1. ADJ

/ pləˈθɔrɪk / (AmE), / plɪˈθɒrɪk / (BrE)
excessively abundant; overfull or overflowing with something, especially to a degree that is excessive or undesirable
excessively abundant or overflowing

Examples

The market was plethoric with imported goods, resulting in lower prices for consumers.
His plethoric speech included far more details than were necessary for the meeting.
The garden was plethoric with flowers in spring, bursting with color and fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities; plentiful
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; in short supply

Etymology

From Greek 'plethōrikos' (fullness), from 'plēthōrē' meaning fullness.
