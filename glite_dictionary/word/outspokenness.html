outspokenness

1. NOUN

/ aʊtˈspoʊkənnəs / (AmE), / aʊtˈspəʊkənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of expressing opinions or feelings freely and openly, often bluntly
quality of being outspoken or blunt

Examples

Her outspokenness sometimes surprised her colleagues during meetings.
Many people admire his outspokenness on controversial social issues.
While some value outspokenness, others find it uncomfortable in formal settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open, honest, and straightforward in speech or expression
  • Antonym: reticence - the quality of being reserved or not revealing one's thoughts easily

Etymology

Derived from 'outspoken' (past participle of 'outspoke'), meaning to speak out, with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun of quality.
