torture

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
Extreme mental anguish or suffering; intense psychological distress.
extreme mental anguish

Examples

The memory of his failure was a constant torture to him.
Waiting for the test results was pure torture for the whole family.
She found the long silence between them to be an emotional torture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - severe mental or physical pain
  • Antonym: comfort - state of physical or mental ease, free from pain

Etymology

From Latin tortura (act of twisting, torment), from torquere (to twist).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.tʃə / (BrE)
The deliberate and systematic infliction of severe physical or mental pain by one person on another, often to obtain information, a confession, or as punishment.
deliberate infliction of pain for information or punishment

Examples

The prisoners were subjected to torture in order to force a confession.
He described the psychological torture he endured while in captivity.
International law prohibits the use of torture under any circumstances.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical or mental ease and freedom from pain

Etymology

From Latin 'torquere' (to twist).
