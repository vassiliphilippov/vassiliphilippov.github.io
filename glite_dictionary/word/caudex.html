caudex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːdɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɛks / (BrE)
the thickened, often woody, persistent stem base of a herbaceous perennial plant, usually found underground or at ground level, from which new growth emerges each season
woody stem base of certain perennial plants

Examples

The caudex of this desert plant stores water to survive long periods of drought.
Each spring, new shoots sprout from the ancient caudex buried just beneath the soil.
Botanists measured the diameter of the caudex to estimate the plant's age.

Etymology

From Latin caudex (tree trunk, stem).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːdɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɛks / (BrE)
the thickened, woody stem found in some plants, such as palms and tree ferns, typically at or near ground level
woody stem of certain plants (palms, ferns)
medical

Examples

The caudex of many palms helps support their tall, slender trunks.
Some tree ferns develop a thick caudex as they mature.
Botanists studied the caudex to determine the plant's age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trunk - the main stem of a tree

Etymology

From Latin 'caudex' (tree trunk, stem).
