jubilant

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːbɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːbɪlənt / (BrE)
feeling or expressing great joy, especially because of a triumph or success
feeling or showing great joy

Examples

The jubilant fans cheered wildly after their team won the championship.
She had a jubilant smile when she heard the good news.
The city erupted in jubilant celebration following the end of the conflict.

Synset Examples

rejoicing crowds filled the streets on VJ Day
a triumphal success
a triumphant shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elated - ecstatically happy or proud
  • Degree Higher: ecstatic - overwhelmed with intense joy
  • Degree Lower: glad - pleased or happy
  • Antonym: miserable - extremely unhappy

Etymology

From Latin jubilare (to shout for joy) via French jubilant.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːbɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːbɪlənt / (BrE)
feeling or expressing great joy, satisfaction, or triumph
showing great joy or triumph

Examples

The fans were jubilant after their team won the championship.
She had a jubilant expression as she opened her acceptance letter.
The city erupted in jubilant celebrations on New Year's Eve.

Synset Examples

a joyful heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - full of joy, happy
  • Synonym: elated - very happy or proud; jubilant
  • Antonym: miserable - extremely unhappy or uncomfortable

Etymology

From Latin 'iubilare' (to shout for joy), via French 'jubilant'.
