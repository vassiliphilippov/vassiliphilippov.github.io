caruncle

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌŋkəl / (AmE), / kəˈrʌŋkl / (BrE)
A small, fleshy outgrowth, such as a fowl's wattle or a protuberance near the hilum of certain seeds.
small fleshy outgrowth in animals/plants

Examples

The turkey's distinctive caruncle turns bright red during courtship.
The botanist identified the seed by its unusual caruncle near the hilum.
Some birds have a brightly colored caruncle on their heads which helps attract mates.

Etymology

From Latin caruncula (a little piece of flesh), diminutive of carō (flesh).
