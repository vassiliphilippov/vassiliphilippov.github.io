fretfully

1. ADV

/ ˈfrɛtfəli / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtfəli / (BrE)
In a fretful or irritable manner, showing discomfort or restlessness.
in an irritable or restless way

Examples

The patient sighed fretfully as she waited for the doctor to arrive.
Children may react fretfully when they are overtired or hungry.
He shifted fretfully in his seat, unable to concentrate on the lecture.

Synset Examples

fretfully, the baby tossed in his crib

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritably - in an irritable manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm manner

Etymology

From 'fretful' + '-ly'. 'Fretful' from Middle English 'freten' (to fret, gnaw).
