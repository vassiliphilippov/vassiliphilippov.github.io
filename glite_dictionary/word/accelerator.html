accelerator

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛləreɪtə / (BrE)
A pedal in a motor vehicle that controls the engine's power and speed by regulating the throttle; used to increase speed.
pedal controlling vehicle speed

Examples

She pressed the accelerator to merge onto the highway quickly.
When traffic cleared, he tapped the accelerator and sped ahead of the other cars.
A sudden push on the accelerator made the car lurch forward.

Synset Examples

he stepped on the gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas pedal - pedal that controls engine speed in a car
  • Antonym: brake - pedal used to slow down or stop a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten), via French 'accélérateur'.

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ər.eɪ.tər / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A substance that increases the speed of a chemical reaction without itself being changed.
substance that speeds up a reaction
medical

Examples

The chemist added an accelerator to the mixture to speed up the reaction.
In rubber production, an accelerator is used to reduce curing time.
Some reactions are so slow that they require an accelerator to occur efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change

Etymology

From Latin accelerare (to hasten, quicken) + English suffix -or.

3. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tər / (BrE)
a device or scientific instrument that increases the speed and energy of charged particles, typically for research in physics
device that speeds up charged particles

Examples

The new accelerator has enabled scientists to explore fundamental particles at higher energies.
A team of physicists operated the accelerator to study proton collisions.
Upgrades to the accelerator were completed last year to boost its performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle accelerator - machine that propels charged particles to high speeds

Etymology

From Latin accēlerāre (to hasten).

4. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛl.ɚˌeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
A device, such as a pedal or valve, that controls the supply of fuel to an engine and regulates its speed.
device regulating engine speed

Examples

She pressed the accelerator to increase the car's speed.
The mechanic found a fault in the accelerator valve.
Modern cars use electronic sensors instead of a traditional accelerator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas pedal - a pedal that controls the accelerator in a vehicle
  • Antonym: brake - a device for slowing or stopping a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten, speed up), via 'accelerate'.
