Homarus americanus

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɛɹ.əs əˌmɛɹ.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / həʊˈmɑː.rəs əˌmɛə.rɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A large lobster native to the Atlantic coast of North America, valued as a seafood delicacy.
American lobster species

Examples

The Homarus americanus is commonly harvested along the coasts of Maine and Nova Scotia.
Marine biologists are studying Homarus americanus to understand the impact of climate change on its habitat.
Lobster rolls are often made from fresh Homarus americanus caught that same morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and claws, valued as food

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus Homarus and species epithet americanus (Latin for 'American').
