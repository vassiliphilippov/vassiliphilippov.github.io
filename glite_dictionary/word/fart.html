fart

1. NOUN

/ fɑrt / (AmE), / fɑːt / (BrE)
an escape of intestinal gas from the anus, often producing a sound and a smell
expulsion of intestinal gas
informal vulgar

Examples

He apologized after letting out a loud fart during the meeting.
The joke was about a dog that couldn't stop farts.
She tried not to laugh when she heard the unmistakable sound of a fart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatulence - the accumulation of gas in the alimentary canal

Etymology

From Old English *feortan* (to break wind).

2. VERB

/ fɑrt / (AmE), / fɑːt / (BrE)
to expel intestinal gas through the anus, usually with a sound and sometimes an odor
expel gas through the anus
informal vulgar

Examples

He tried not to fart during the quiet meeting.
Some people feel embarrassed when they fart in public.
The dog farted loudly and then looked surprised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatulate - to expel intestinal gas through the anus

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, attested since Middle English.
