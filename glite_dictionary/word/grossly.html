grossly

1. ADV

/ ˈɡroʊsli / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsli / (BrE)
In a way that is extreme, flagrant, or obviously wrong; to a very great or serious degree.
to an extreme or flagrant degree

Examples

The judge found the company's actions to be grossly negligent.
Her behavior was grossly inappropriate at the meeting.
That estimate is grossly exaggerated and not supported by the evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagrantly - in a conspicuously offensive manner
  • Antonym: mildly - in a moderate or gentle way

Etymology

From 'gross' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Gross' from Old French *gros* (large, thick), from Late Latin *grossus* (thick, coarse).
