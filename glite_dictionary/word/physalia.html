Physalia

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / fɪˈzeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of marine hydrozoans known as the Portuguese man-of-war, characterized by a gas-filled bladder and long stinging tentacles.
genus of jellyfish-like marine animals

Examples

The stings from a Physalia can cause intense pain and should be treated promptly.
Boaters spotted several Physalia floating near the shore after the storm.
Researchers are studying the venom of the Physalia to develop better medical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese man-of-war - a marine hydrozoan with a float and long stinging tentacles

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek physalis (bladder).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / fɪˈzeɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of marine hydrozoans, including the Portuguese man-of-war, noted for its gas-filled bladder and long stinging tentacles.
genus of marine organisms (Portuguese man-of-war)

Examples

The most well-known species in the genus Physalia is the Portuguese man-of-war.
Scientists studied the stinging cells of Physalia to better understand their venom.
A school of Physalia drifted near the surface, warning swimmers to stay away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese man-of-war - a marine hydrozoan with a gas-filled float and venomous tentacles

Etymology

From New Latin, named after a type of air-bubble float, from Greek *physalis* (bladder, bubble).
