Mikmaq

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪgˌmɑː / (AmE), / ˈmɪgˌmɑː / (BrE)
a member of the First Nations people inhabiting the Maritime Provinces of Canada and parts of the northeastern United States
member of a First Nations group in Canada

Examples

Many Mikmaq continue to preserve their language and cultural traditions.
The Mikmaq have lived in the region for thousands of years.
A local museum features artifacts from the Mikmaq people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mi'kmaq - the same Indigenous group using standard orthography with diacritic
  • Antonym: Inuit - a distinct Indigenous people of northern Canada

Etymology

From Mi’kmaq, the self-designation in their own language, meaning 'my friends' or 'allies'.
