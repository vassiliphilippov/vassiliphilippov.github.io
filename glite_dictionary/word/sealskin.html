sealskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːlˌskɪn / (BrE)
the pelt or fur of a seal, especially the soft underfur, used as material for making garments and other items
the pelt or fur of a seal

Examples

Her coat was made from luxurious sealskin and felt incredibly warm.
Traditional Inuit boots are often crafted with sealskin for insulation.
The trader displayed high-quality sealskin at the coastal market.

Synset Examples

a coat of seal

Etymology

From 'seal' (the marine mammal) + 'skin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːl.skɪn / (BrE)
a garment, such as a coat or jacket, made from the skin or pelt of a seal
garment made from seal pelt

Examples

She wore a luxurious sealskin to the gala on a cold winter night.
Vintage sealskin coats are now rare and often considered collectibles.
The explorer kept warm in his sealskin jacket during the Arctic expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur coat - a coat made from the fur of an animal
