Copernicia cerifera

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌpɜrˈnɪʃiə sərˈɪfərə / (AmE), / kəʊˌpɜːˈnɪsɪə sɛˈrɪfərə / (BrE)
A Brazilian fan palm with edible roots, used as a source of fiber and a hard, yellowish wax.
Brazilian fan palm, source of carnauba wax

Examples

Copernicia cerifera is cultivated in Brazil for its valuable carnauba wax.
The leaves of Copernicia cerifera supply strong natural fibers used in handicrafts.
Researchers are studying the edible root of Copernicia cerifera for its nutritional benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba wax - A hard yellowish wax obtained from the leaves of Copernicia cerifera

Etymology

From New Latin Copernicia (genus named after Copernicus) + Latin cerifera (wax-bearing).
