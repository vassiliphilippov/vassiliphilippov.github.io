Argo

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a former large constellation in the southern sky, now divided into Carina, Puppis, Pyxis, and Vela
former constellation (now divided)

Examples

The ancient Greeks identified the stars of Argo as the ship sailed by Jason and the Argonauts.
With modern astronomy, the constellation Argo has been divided into several smaller constellations.
Star charts from the 18th century still show Argo as a prominent feature of the southern sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Carina - a southern constellation, formerly part of Argo
  • Degree Lower: Puppis - a southern constellation, formerly part of Argo
  • Degree Lower: Pyxis - a southern constellation, formerly part of Argo
  • Degree Lower: Vela - a southern constellation, formerly part of Argo

Etymology

From Greek Argō, the name of the ship in the myth of Jason and the Argonauts.
