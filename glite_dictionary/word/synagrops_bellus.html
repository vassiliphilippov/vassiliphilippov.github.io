Synagrops bellus

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɪnəˈɡrɑps ˈbɛləs / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈɡræps ˈbɛləs / (BrE)
A small species of marine fish noted for its black mouth and gill cavity, found in deep waters.
species of small marine fish

Examples

The scientist collected specimens of Synagrops bellus during the deep-sea expedition.
Synagrops bellus is easily recognized by its distinctive black mouth and gill cavity.
Many marine biologists study Synagrops bellus to understand deep-sea fish adaptations.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Synagrops' and the Latin 'bellus' meaning 'pretty' or 'beautiful'.
