leaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪd / (BrE)
(of windowpanes) made or held together with narrow strips of lead.
windowpane held by strips of lead

Examples

The cottage had beautiful leaded windows that let in colorful light.
Restoring the old church required replacing several leaded glass panels.
They admired the intricate designs in the leaded windowpanes of the Victorian house.

Synset Examples

leaded windowpanes

Etymology

From 'lead' (the metal) + '-ed', referring to the use of lead strips in window construction.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01064003-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪd / (BrE)
Containing or treated with lead, especially as an additive or admixture.
containing or treated with lead

Examples

The use of leaded gasoline has been banned in many countries due to health concerns.
Some old water pipes are made of leaded materials, which can pose a risk of contamination.
He discovered that the compound was a leaded alloy used in industrial processes.

Synset Examples

leaded gasoline
leaded zinc

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unleaded - not containing lead, especially as an additive

Etymology

From 'lead' (the metal) + '-ed' (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01399805-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪd / (BrE)
(of text or printing) having thin strips of lead inserted between lines of type to increase spacing in printed material.
having extra space between lines of type

Examples

The old books were printed with leaded text to make reading easier.
Designers sometimes prefer a leaded layout for better readability.
The publisher requested all poetry to be leaded to give the lines more space.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unleaded - having no extra lead strips between lines of type

Etymology

From 'lead', the metal formerly used as thin strips in typesetting; derives from Old English 'lǣd'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01660555-s
Synset POS: s
