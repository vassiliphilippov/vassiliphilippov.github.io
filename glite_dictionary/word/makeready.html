makeready

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌrɛdi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌrɛdi / (BrE)
the final preparations and adjustments made to equipment or materials before beginning a production process, especially in printing or manufacturing
final preparation before production

Examples

The press operator inspected the makeready before starting the run of brochures.
It took several hours to complete the makeready for the new job.
Good makeready is essential to achieve high-quality prints in the workshop.

Etymology

From 'make' + 'ready', used as a compound noun in printing and manufacturing since the 19th century.
