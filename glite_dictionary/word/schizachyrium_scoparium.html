Schizachyrium scoparium

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪzəˈkɪriəm skoʊˈpɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌskɪzəˈkɪəriəm skəʊˈpɛəriəm / (BrE)
A species of handsome, hardy North American grass known for its foliage turning pale bronze in autumn; commonly called little bluestem.
North American grass species (little bluestem)

Examples

Prairie ecologists often seek out Schizachyrium scoparium because of its importance to native grasslands.
The garden featured Schizachyrium scoparium for its striking autumn color and resilience.
Many restoration projects use Schizachyrium scoparium to reestablish healthy prairie ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little bluestem - Common name for Schizachyrium scoparium, a North American prairie grass

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) and 'achyron' (chaff), alluding to the separated chaff, and Latin 'scoparium' (broom-like).
