truncated

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌŋˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Shortened by having a part cut off or by being reduced in duration or extent.
cut short; shortened in duration or extent

Examples

The meeting was truncated due to technical difficulties.
Their vacation plans were truncated by an unexpected emergency.
He gave a truncated version of his speech because of time constraints.

Synset Examples

the abbreviated speech
her shortened life was clearly the result of smoking
an unsatisfactory truncated conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviated - shortened; made briefer than usual or necessary
  • Synonym: shortened - made shorter in length or duration

Etymology

From Latin truncatus, past participle of truncare (to lop, cut off).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌŋ.keɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having an end or part cut off; shortened by having a part removed or missing, especially in reference to a shape or form.
having an end cut off; shortened

Examples

The top of the ancient tower had been truncated by a powerful storm.
He designed a truncated cone for the new sculpture exhibit.
Many geological features, such as truncated hills, are formed by erosion or collapse.

Synset Examples

a truncate leaf
truncated volcanic mountains
a truncated pyramid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortened - made shorter in length or duration
  • Antonym: elongated - lengthened or extended in length

Etymology

From Latin truncatus, past participle of truncare (to lop off, cut off).
