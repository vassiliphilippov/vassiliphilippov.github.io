preordination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˌɔːrdɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Theology) The act of deciding or determining something in advance, especially the doctrine that God has foreordained all events throughout eternity.
theological doctrine of predetermination

Examples

The doctrine of preordination has been debated for centuries among theologians.
He questioned whether human free will was compatible with the idea of preordination.
In Calvinist belief, preordination determines every aspect of existence, including salvation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that God has already determined all events, especially salvation

Etymology

From Latin praeordinatio (determination in advance), from prae- (before) + ordinatio (arrangement).
