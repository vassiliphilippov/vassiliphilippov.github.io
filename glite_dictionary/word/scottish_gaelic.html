Scottish Gaelic

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɑːtɪʃ ˈɡeɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌskɒtɪʃ ˈɡaːlɪk / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken, especially in the Highlands and Islands of Scotland
Celtic language of Scotland

Examples

He is learning to speak Scottish Gaelic to connect with his heritage.
Many road signs in the Highlands are written in Scottish Gaelic and English.
The preservation of Scottish Gaelic is important to the culture of Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish Gaelic - Celtic language spoken in Ireland

Etymology

From Scottish + Gaelic, based on the native name Gàidhlig.
