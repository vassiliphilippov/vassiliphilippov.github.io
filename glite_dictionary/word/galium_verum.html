Galium verum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪliəm ˈvɛrəm / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪliəm ˈvɪərəm / (BrE)
A species of perennial herb with small yellow flowers, commonly known as lady's bedstraw, native to Europe, Asia, and northern Africa.
species of yellow-flowered perennial herb

Examples

Galium verum can often be found growing wild along meadows and roadsides in Europe.
In herbal medicine, Galium verum was traditionally used for its supposed healing properties.
The vibrant yellow flowers of Galium verum attract many species of pollinating insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's bedstraw - common name for Galium verum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Galium' meaning 'milk' and 'verum' meaning 'true', referring to the plant's use in curdling milk.
