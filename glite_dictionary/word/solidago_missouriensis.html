Solidago missouriensis

1. NOUN

/ səˌlɪdəɡoʊ mɪˌzuːriˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / sɒlɪˌdeɪɡəʊ mɪˌzʊərɪˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod native to North America, similar to meadow goldenrod but usually smaller in size.
a species of goldenrod plant

Examples

Solidago missouriensis thrives in prairies and open grasslands across central North America.
Gardeners often plant Solidago missouriensis for its bright yellow flowers and its ability to attract pollinators.
Unlike other goldenrods, Solidago missouriensis usually grows to a more modest height.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solidago canadensis - common or Canada goldenrod, a related but larger species

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Solidago' and specific epithet 'missouriensis', meaning 'from Missouri'.
