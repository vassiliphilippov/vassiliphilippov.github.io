abstinence

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.stɪ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈæb.stɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the practice of restraining oneself from indulging in something, especially alcohol or other pleasures or habits
self-restraint from indulgence, especially alcohol

Examples

His doctor recommended complete abstinence from alcohol to improve his health.
She has practiced abstinence from smoking for over a year now.
During the recovery program, abstinence is considered essential for success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sobriety - the state of not being affected by alcohol
  • Antonym: intemperance - lack of moderation, especially in drinking alcohol

Etymology

From Latin 'abstinentia', from 'abstinere' (to refrain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbstɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈæbstɪnəns / (BrE)
the practice of deliberately avoiding something, especially alcoholic drink or sexual activity
the practice of avoiding indulgence

Examples

Some religions encourage abstinence from alcohol and other intoxicating substances.
He practiced abstinence for several months while training for the marathon.
Total abstinence from sugar can be difficult for people with a sweet tooth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indulgence - an act of allowing oneself to enjoy something, especially something considered unhealthy
  • Synonym: self-control - the ability to control one’s own emotions and desires

Etymology

From Latin abstinentia (act of abstaining), from abstinēre (to withhold).
