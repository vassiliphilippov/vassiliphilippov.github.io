tholepin

1. NOUN

/ ˈθoʊlˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈθəʊl.pɪn / (BrE)
a pin set in the gunwale of a boat to hold an oar in place while rowing, serving as a pivot point
oar holder on a boat

Examples

The wooden tholepin creaked each time he pulled on the oar.
One of the tholepins snapped during the storm, making it difficult to steer the boat.
She secured the oar beneath the tholepin before beginning to row across the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarlock - a hinged ring or clamp holding an oar in place on a boat

Etymology

From Old English 'thol', meaning a pin or peg.
