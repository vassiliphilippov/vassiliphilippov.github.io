unfeigned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪnd / (BrE)
(of feelings, expressions, or actions) genuine; not pretended or insincere.
genuine, not pretended

Examples

She spoke with unfeigned delight about her new job.
His unfeigned apology convinced everyone of his sincerity.
The child's unfeigned curiosity made the museum tour especially enjoyable.

Synset Examples

genuine emotion
her interest in people was unfeigned
true grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic or sincere
  • Synonym: sincere - free from pretense or deceit; genuine feelings
  • Antonym: feigned - pretended or insincere

Etymology

From un- (not) + feigned (pretended), from Old French 'feindre' and Latin 'fingere' (to shape, invent).
