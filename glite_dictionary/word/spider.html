spider

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
a type of frying pan, typically made of cast iron, with legs and often used over an open fire
cast-iron frying pan with legs

Examples

The cook placed the heavy spider over the coals to fry the potatoes.
In colonial kitchens, a spider was an essential tool for preparing meals over the hearth.
The antique shop displayed an old cast-iron spider with three sturdy legs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
A small arthropod animal with eight legs, belonging to the order Araneae, which spins silk to make webs or cocoons and often has venomous fangs.
eight-legged arachnid that spins webs

Examples

A spider spun an intricate web in the corner of the window.
Some people are frightened of even the smallest spider they see indoors.
The garden was full of dewdrops clinging to spider webs after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arachnid - a class of joint-legged invertebrate animals including spiders, scorpions, and mites

Etymology

Old English *spīthra*, related to the verb 'spin' because spiders spin webs.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
a computer program that automatically searches and indexes websites across the internet, typically for use by search engines
web-crawling computer program

Examples

A search engine uses a spider to crawl the internet and index website content.
The spider identified hundreds of new pages overnight.
Web developers sometimes block a spider from accessing certain parts of their sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web crawler - a program that systematically browses the internet for data collection
  • Synonym: bot - an automated computer program performing tasks over the internet

Etymology

Extension of the original sense of 'spider' (the animal), referring to the program's ability to 'crawl' across the web.
