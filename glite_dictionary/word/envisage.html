to envisage

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɪzɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzɪdʒ / (BrE)
to form a mental picture of something not present or to imagine as a possibility
to imagine or visualize something

Examples

It is difficult to envisage a world without electricity.
The architects envisage a new city park where the old factory stands.
She could easily envisage herself living in the countryside.

Synset Examples

Can you conceive of him as the president?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form an idea or plan in the mind

Etymology

From French 'envisager' (to look in the face), from 'en-' (in) + 'visage' (face).
