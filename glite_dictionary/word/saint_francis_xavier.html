Saint Francis Xavier

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs ɪgˈzeɪviər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs ɪɡˈzeɪvɪə / (BrE)
(1506–1552) Spanish Catholic missionary and Jesuit priest, known for establishing Christian missions in Asia, including Japan, Ceylon (Sri Lanka), and the East Indies.
Spanish Jesuit missionary (1506–1552)

Examples

Many schools and churches around the world are named after Saint Francis Xavier.
Saint Francis Xavier is remembered for his extensive missionary work in Asia.
The feast day of Saint Francis Xavier is celebrated on December 3rd.

Etymology

From his given name Francis and surname Xavier, after the castle of Xavier (Javier) in Navarre, Spain.
