pepperidge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɛpərɪdʒ / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its columnar shape, horizontal limbs, small leaves that appear late in spring, and brilliant autumn foliage; also called the black gum or tupelo.
a North American deciduous tree with striking fall color

Examples

The pepperidge in our backyard turns a stunning red in early autumn.
Many wildlife species rely on the fruit produced by the pepperidge.
We identified the tree by its unique branching pattern and learned it was a pepperidge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tupelo - another common name for this tree (Nyssa sylvatica)
  • Synonym: black gum - another common name for this tree (Nyssa sylvatica)

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; recorded in American English since the 17th century, possibly from an Algonquian language.
