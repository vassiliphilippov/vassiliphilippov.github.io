drowsing

1. ADJ

/ ˈdraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
being half asleep or in a light sleep, especially in a relaxed state.
half asleep; lightly sleeping

Examples

She sat drowsing in the warm afternoon sun, barely awake.
The lecture left several students drowsing at their desks.
After dinner, I found my grandfather drowsing in his favorite chair.

Synset Examples

made drowsy by the long ride
it seemed a pity to disturb the drowsing (or dozing) professor
a tired dozy child
the nodding (or napping) grandmother in her rocking chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dozing - being half asleep; lightly sleeping
  • Antonym: awake - fully alert and not sleeping

Etymology

From the verb 'drowse,' which developed from Middle English 'drousen' (become sluggish or doze), possibly related to Old Norse 'drosa' (to doze).
