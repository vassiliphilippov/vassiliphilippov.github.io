felicity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪsɪti / (BrE)
a pleasing and appropriate manner or style, especially in the expression of thoughts or feelings
pleasing appropriateness in style or expression

Examples

The critic admired the felicity of her language in the novel's opening chapter.
His speech was distinguished by its felicity of expression and elegant phrasing.
She envied his felicity in finding the perfect words for every occasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace - elegant and pleasing quality of movement or style
  • Antonym: awkwardness - lack of grace or appropriateness in manner or style

Etymology

From Latin felicitas (happiness, fertility), from felix (happy, fortunate).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪsɪti / (BrE)
A state of great happiness, contentment, or intense joy.
intense happiness or joy

Examples

She accepted the award with a sense of felicity and gratitude.
Their wedding day was filled with pure felicity.
He spoke with felicity about his experiences living abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering or unhappiness

Etymology

From Latin felicitas (happiness, fertility), from felix (happy, fortunate).
