hypnotist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪpnətɪst / (AmE), / ˈhɪpnətɪst / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at inducing hypnosis in others, often for therapeutic or entertainment purposes
person who induces hypnosis

Examples

The hypnotist asked the volunteer to focus on the swinging pendant.
At the party, a skilled hypnotist mesmerized the audience with his act.
She became a certified hypnotist after completing her professional training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnotizer - a person who hypnotizes others
  • Antonym: subject - a person who is hypnotized

Etymology

From Greek 'hypnos' (sleep) + English suffix '-ist'.
