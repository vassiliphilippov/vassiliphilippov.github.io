divider

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a vertical structure or object that separates areas or spaces, such as a wall or partition between rooms
vertical structure that separates spaces

Examples

The office uses a divider to create separate workspaces for staff.
A bookshelf can serve as a stylish divider between the dining and living areas.
Temporary dividers were installed in the hall for the exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partition - a structure dividing a space into parts
  • Synonym: wall - a continuous vertical brick or stone structure dividing a room, building, or area of land

Etymology

From Latin 'dividere' (to divide) via Middle English 'divide' and the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a drafting instrument with two legs, used for dividing lines into equal parts or transferring measurements
instrument for dividing lines or measuring

Examples

The architect used a pair of dividers to transfer measurements from one drawing to another.
Before the age of digital tools, dividers were essential for engineers and draftsmen.
She carefully adjusted the dividers to mark equal intervals along the baseline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compass - drawing tool with two legs used for drawing circles and arcs

Etymology

From the verb 'divide' + -er, meaning one who divides.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03220452-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a person who separates something into parts or groups
a person who separates things

Examples

The divider in the debate split the group into two opposing teams.
She acted as a divider, organizing the students into different categories based on skill level.
In ancient societies, a divider would allocate land among families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separator - a person or thing that separates

Etymology

From 'divide' (Latin dividere, to force apart) + -er.
