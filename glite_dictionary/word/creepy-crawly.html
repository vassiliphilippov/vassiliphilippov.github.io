creepy-crawly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripi ˌkrɔli / (AmE), / ˈkriːpi ˌkrɔːli / (BrE)
An informal term for a small creature that creeps or crawls, such as an insect, worm, or spider.
small crawling creature (informal)
informal

Examples

My daughter screamed when she saw a creepy-crawly under her bed.
Some people love collecting creepy-crawlies from the backyard to observe them.
I brushed a creepy-crawly off my arm while hiking through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - small arthropod animal
  • Synonym: arachnid - animal such as a spider or scorpion

2. ADJ

/ ˈkripiˌkrɔli / (AmE), / ˈkriːpiˌkrɔːli / (BrE)
making you feel as if something unpleasant or frightening is crawling over your skin, often provoking unease or fear
causing a crawling sensation; inducing unease

Examples

The old, abandoned house gave me a creepy-crawly feeling as soon as I entered.
She shuddered with a creepy-crawly sensation after walking through the cobweb-covered basement.
Reading horror novels sometimes gives me that unmistakable creepy-crawly feeling late at night.

Synset Examples

a creepy story
I had a creepy-crawly feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creepy - causing an unpleasant feeling of fear or unease
  • Synonym: spine-chilling - causing a shiver or a feeling of fear or excitement
  • Antonym: comforting - making you feel more comfortable or less worried

Etymology

Formed by reduplication of 'creepy' with 'crawly', echoing the movement of small insects or creatures.
