elm

1. NOUN

/ ɛlm / (AmE), / ɛlm / (BrE)
the hard, tough wood from an elm tree, often used for implements and furniture
wood from an elm tree

Examples

The chair was crafted from polished elm to highlight the natural grain.
They used elm for the boat's hull because of its strength and durability.
Antique desks made of elm are highly prized by collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - the wood of an oak tree

Etymology

From Old English *elm*, from Proto-Germanic *elmaz* (elm tree).

2. NOUN

/ ɛlm / (AmE), / ɛlm / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus, with broad leaves and often grown for timber or shade
type of deciduous tree (Ulmus)

Examples

The old elm in our backyard provides shade during the summer.
Many city streets are lined with elm trees because of their dense foliage.
Dutch elm disease has devastated many populations of these trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a large deciduous tree with lobed leaves and acorns

Etymology

Old English 'elm', from Proto-Germanic *elmaz.
