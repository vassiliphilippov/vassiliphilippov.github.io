setline

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a long fishing line with multiple shorter lines and hooks attached, typically suspended between buoys
fishing line with attached hooks and buoys

Examples

The fishermen checked each setline for a fresh catch at dawn.
Modern regulations limit the number of hooks allowed on a single setline.
A setline can stretch for hundreds of meters across open water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longline - a long fishing line with baited hooks, similar fishing equipment

Etymology

From 'set' (to place) + 'line' (cord for fishing), reflecting its method of deployment in fishing.
