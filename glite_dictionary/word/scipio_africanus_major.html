Scipio Africanus Major

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪp.i.oʊ ˌæf.rɪˈkeɪ.nəs ˈmeɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈsɪp.i.əʊ ˌæf.rɪˈkeɪ.nəs ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
(circa 237–183 BC) A prominent Roman general who led the invasion of Carthage during the Second Punic War and defeated Hannibal at the Battle of Zama.
notable Roman general, defeated Hannibal at Zama

Examples

Scipio Africanus Major is regarded as one of Rome's greatest military commanders.
The victory of Scipio Africanus Major at Zama ended the Second Punic War.
Many historians credit Scipio Africanus Major with changing the course of Roman history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hannibal - Carthaginian general defeated by Scipio Africanus at Zama

Etymology

From Latin 'Scipio Africanus', meaning 'Scipio the African', an honorific given after his victory in Africa.
