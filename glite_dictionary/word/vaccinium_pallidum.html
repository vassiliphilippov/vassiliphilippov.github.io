Vaccinium pallidum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm pəˈlɪdəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm pəˈlɪdəm / (BrE)
A low, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, bearing dark blue, sweet berries commonly known as early lowbush blueberries.
a shrub of the eastern US with edible blue berries

Examples

Many wild animals feed on the berries of Vaccinium pallidum in summer.
Researchers study Vaccinium pallidum for its antioxidant properties.
You can find Vaccinium pallidum growing in dry, rocky woodlands in the eastern US.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue or purple berry from shrubs of the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vaccinium' (a genus of shrubs including blueberries), 'pallidum' meaning 'pale' or 'light-colored'.
