branchiate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræn.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈbræn.sɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
(of animals) having gills for breathing; provided with gills.
having gills; provided with gills

Examples

The branchiate larval stage allows amphibians to survive in aquatic environments.
Certain aquatic insects remain branchiate throughout their life cycles.
The fossil shows evidence of a branchiate structure, suggesting it lived underwater.

Synset Examples

a gilled tadpole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gilled - having gills for breathing; provided with gills

Etymology

From Latin 'branchia' (gill), via Greek 'bránkhia'.
