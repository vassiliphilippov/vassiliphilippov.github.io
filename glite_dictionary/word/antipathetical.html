antipathetical

1. ADJ

/ ænˌtɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ænˌtɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
having or showing strong aversion, hostility, or opposition to something or someone; characterized by antipathy.
strongly opposed or hostile

Examples

His antipathetical attitude towards modern art was well known among his friends.
The two departments remained antipathetical despite corporate efforts to encourage collaboration.
Surrounded by antipathetical colleagues, he felt isolated at work.

Synset Examples

slaves antagonistic to their masters
antipathetic factions within the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antagonistic - showing or feeling active opposition or hostility
  • Antonym: sympathetic - feeling, showing, or expressing sympathy

Etymology

From Greek 'antipatheia' (opposition in feeling) + '-ical'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
strongly opposed or having a strong dislike (usually followed by 'to')
strongly opposed or averse

Examples

Many voters remain antipathetical to the proposed tax reforms despite numerous campaigns.
He is antipathetical to modern art and prefers classical paintings instead.
The CEO was openly antipathetical to changing the company’s long-standing policies.

Synset Examples

antipathetic to new ideas
averse to taking risks
loath to go on such short notice
clearly indisposed to grant their request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipathetic - having or showing a strong aversion
  • Antonym: sympathetic - showing approval or favor; sharing feelings

Etymology

From Greek antipatheia (opposition in feeling) via late Latin and English alteration.
