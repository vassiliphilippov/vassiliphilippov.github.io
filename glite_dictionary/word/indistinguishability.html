indistinguishability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌstɪŋɡwɪʃəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪsˌtɪŋɡwɪʃəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to differentiate or tell apart; exact sameness
impossibility of being distinguished

Examples

The indistinguishability of the two twins made it hard for even their parents to tell them apart.
Quantum particles exhibit indistinguishability, which is crucial in understanding their behavior.
Modern forgeries can sometimes approach a level of indistinguishability from genuine artworks.

Synset Examples

they shared an identity of interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identity - the quality of being identical or the same
  • Antonym: difference - the quality of being not the same

Etymology

From 'indistinguishable' (not able to be distinguished) + '-ity' (forming nouns denoting a quality or condition).
