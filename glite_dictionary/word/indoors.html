indoors

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnˈdɔrz / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈdɔːz / (BrE)
in or into a building; inside, rather than outside
inside a building

Examples

We decided to stay indoors because it was raining heavily outside.
During the summer heatwave, many people preferred to work indoors.
You should bring the plants indoors before the first frost arrives.

Synset Examples

in winter we play inside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outdoors - outside, not inside a building

Etymology

From 'in' + plural of 'door', originally meaning 'within the doors'.
