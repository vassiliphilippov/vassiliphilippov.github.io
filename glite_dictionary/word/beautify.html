to beautify

1. VERB

/ ˈbjuːtəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something beautiful or more attractive
make beautiful or more attractive

Examples

The city planted new trees to beautify the streets.
She hired a landscape designer to beautify her backyard.
Murals and sculptures were used to beautify the old community center.

Synset Examples

Flowers adorned the tables everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • Antonym: to uglify - to make ugly or less attractive

Etymology

From Old French *beauté* (beauty), from Latin *bellus* (pretty, handsome), plus the suffix -fy (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈbjuːt̬əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbjuː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something more beautiful or attractive
make more beautiful

Examples

The city plans to beautify the riverfront with new parks and gardens.
She spent hours trying to beautify her home for the holiday season.
Artists volunteered to beautify the old building by painting a colorful mural.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decoration or detail
  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate or add beauty to something
  • Antonym: to uglify - to make ugly or less attractive

Etymology

From beauty + -fy (from Latin -ficare, meaning 'to make').

3. VERB

/ ˈbjuː.tɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈbjuː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something or someone more attractive by adding decorative elements or improving its appearance
make more attractive; decorate

Examples

Volunteers worked together to beautify the city park with flowers and murals.
She tried to beautify her handwriting by practicing every evening.
New laws encouraged homeowners to beautify their front yards with native plants.

Synset Examples

Decorate the room for the party
beautify yourself for the special day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to add ornaments or features to make something more attractive
  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate or add beauty to
  • Antonym: to disfigure - to spoil the appearance of something or someone

Etymology

From beauty + -fy, from Old French beauté and Latin bellus (beautiful) + Latin -ficare (to make).
