tragic

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of tragedy, especially in literature, drama, or events involving great suffering or destruction.
relating to tragedy or tragic events

Examples

The death of the young artist was a truly tragic event for the entire community.
Shakespeare's plays often feature a tragic hero who is doomed by fate or a personal flaw.
The film had a tragic ending that left the audience in tears.

Synset Examples

tragic hero

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comic - relating to comedy

Etymology

From Greek tragikos, from tragōidia (tragedy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪk / (BrE)
Causing or characterized by extreme sadness, especially due to death, suffering, or disaster.
causing deep sadness or disaster

Examples

The accident was a tragic event that affected the entire community.
She gave a tragic performance as the doomed queen in the play.
Losing his family in the fire was the most tragic thing that ever happened to him.

Synset Examples

a tragic face
a tragic plight
a tragic accident

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comic - causing laughter; humorous

Etymology

From Greek tragikos, from tragōidia (tragedy).
