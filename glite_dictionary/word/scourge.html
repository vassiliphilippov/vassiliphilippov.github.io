scourge

1. NOUN

/ skɝdʒ / (AmE), / skɜːdʒ / (BrE)
A whip used as an instrument of punishment.
whip used for punishment

Examples

The prison guard used a scourge to discipline the inmates.
In ancient Rome, a scourge was often employed as a form of punishment.
He feared the cruel scourge more than the harsh words of his captors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - an instrument for striking or flogging, consisting of a lash attached to a handle

Etymology

From Old French *escorgier* (to whip), from Vulgar Latin *excorrigiare* (to flog with a whip).
