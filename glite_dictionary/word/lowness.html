lowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnəs / (BrE)
a condition or position of being low in rank, status, or estimation
inferior rank or status

Examples

He struggled for years to overcome the lowness of his social position.
The novel explores the lowness and humiliation felt by those at the bottom of society.
Despite the lowness of her rank, she was admired for her integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in status or quality
  • Antonym: superiority - the state of being higher in status or quality

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāw' (low) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
