Trogoniformes

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌgoʊnɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / trəʊˌɡɒnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of brightly colored tropical birds known as trogons, found mainly in Central and South America, Africa, and Asia.
an order of tropical birds (trogons)

Examples

The Trogoniformes are known for their vibrant plumage and unique nesting habits.
Scientists have identified several new species within the Trogoniformes in the rainforests of South America.
The quetzal, belonging to the Trogoniformes order, is famous for its colorful feathers and cultural significance.

Etymology

From Greek trōgōn (gnawing, trogon, a bird genus) + Latin -formes (having the form of).
