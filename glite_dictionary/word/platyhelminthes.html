Platyhelminthes

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætiˌhɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (AmE), / ˌplætiˌhɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (BrE)
A phylum of simple, soft-bodied, unsegmented invertebrate animals commonly known as flatworms.
phylum of flatworms
medical

Examples

Platyhelminthes are studied for their unique regenerative abilities in biological research.
The class Trematoda belongs to the phylum Platyhelminthes.
Many Platyhelminthes are parasites that can infect humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatworm - a simple invertebrate animal of the phylum Platyhelminthes

Etymology

From Greek platus (flat) + helmins (worm).
