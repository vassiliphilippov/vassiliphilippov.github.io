cylinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪndər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure with straight parallel sides and a circular or oval cross section, bounded by two parallel planes at the ends (the bases).
solid figure with circular bases

Examples

The soup can was shaped like a cylinder with two flat circular bases.
In geometry class, we learned to calculate the volume of a cylinder.
A cylinder is different from a sphere because of its parallel sides and flat ends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cone - solid figure with a circular base and a single vertex
  • Degree Higher: sphere - perfectly round solid geometric figure

Etymology

From Greek *kylindros* (roller, cylinder), via Latin *cylindrus*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ɪn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ɪn.də / (BrE)
a metal container in the shape of a cylinder, used especially to hold gases such as oxygen or compressed air under pressure
container for compressed gases

Examples

The hospital replaced the empty oxygen cylinder in the emergency ward.
Always check that the cylinder is full before taking it on a diving trip.
Compressed air cylinders must be stored upright and secured to prevent accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for holding liquid or gas

Etymology

From Greek *kylindros*, meaning 'roller', via Latin *cylindrus* and Old French *cylindre*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ɪn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ɪn.də / (BrE)
A chamber in an engine or other machine in which a piston moves back and forth.
engine chamber for a piston

Examples

The car has a four-cylinder engine that offers efficient performance.
When the cylinder is worn out, the piston may not move smoothly.
The mechanic checked inside the cylinder for signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piston - A cylindrical engine component that moves up and down inside a cylinder

Etymology

From Greek 'kulindros' (roller), through Latin 'cylindrus'.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
A surface generated by rotating a straight line, called a generatrix, parallel to a fixed straight line, called the axis.
surface formed by rotating a straight line

Examples

The equation describes the surface of a cylinder in three-dimensional space.
A right circular cylinder has its axis perpendicular to its base.
In mathematics, a cylinder is defined as all points at a fixed distance from a given line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cone - a surface generated by rotating a line around another that meets at a fixed point

Etymology

From Greek 'kylindros' (roller, cylinder), from 'kylindein' (to roll).
