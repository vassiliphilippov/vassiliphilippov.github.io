bicolour

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌkʌlə / (BrE)
having two colours; composed of or marked with two distinct colours.
having two colours

Examples

The team's new jerseys are bicolour, featuring blue and white stripes.
She admired the bicolour petals of the exotic orchid.
The artist painted a bicolour background to make the subject stand out.

Synset Examples

a bicolor flower
a bicolored postage stamp

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricolour - having three colours
  • Degree Lower: monochrome - having only one colour

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'color' (colour).
