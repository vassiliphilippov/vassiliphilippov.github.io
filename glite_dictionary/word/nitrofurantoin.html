nitrofurantoin

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊfjʊˈræntɔɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊfjʊˈræntɔɪn / (BrE)
An antibacterial drug used especially to treat urinary tract infections, effective against a wide range of bacteria.
antibacterial drug for urinary tract infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nitrofurantoin to treat her urinary tract infection.
Some people may experience mild side effects when taking nitrofurantoin.
If you are allergic to nitrofurantoin, let your healthcare provider know immediately.

Etymology

From 'nitro-' (referring to the nitro group), 'furan' (a chemical ring structure), and '-toin' (from hydantoin, a chemical compound).
