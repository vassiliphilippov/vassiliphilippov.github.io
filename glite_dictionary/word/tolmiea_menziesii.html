Tolmiea menziesii

1. NOUN

/ toʊlˈmiːə mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (AmE), / tɒlˈmiːə mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to western North America, known for its erect cluster of small flowers and the unique ability to produce new plantlets at the base of its leaves.
a North American perennial herb (pickaback plant)

Examples

The Tolmiea menziesii thrives in shady, moist woodland gardens.
You can propagate Tolmiea menziesii easily due to the plantlets that grow on its leaves.
My grandmother kept a pot of Tolmiea menziesii on her kitchen windowsill for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickaback plant - Another common name for Tolmiea menziesii, a plant growing new plantlets on its leaves

Etymology

Named for Scottish-Canadian botanist William Fraser Tolmie and Archibald Menzies, Scottish naturalist.
