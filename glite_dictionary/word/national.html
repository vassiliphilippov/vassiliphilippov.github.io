national

1. ADJ

/ ˈnæʃənl / (AmE), / ˈnæʃ(ə)n(ə)l / (BrE)
limited to or concerning only a particular nation or country; relating to the interests or affairs of a specific nation.
relating to a particular nation or country

Examples

The meeting focused on national security concerns.
Environmental regulations often vary according to national policies.
Her research explores how national identity influences cultural attitudes.

Synset Examples

national interests
isolationism is a strictly national policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: international - involving two or more nations
  • Synonym: domestic - relating to policies within a single country

Etymology

From French *national*, from Latin *natio* (nation, people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01572255-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈnæʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈnaʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the people of a particular nation; typical of a nation's people.
characteristic of a nation's people

Examples

Pride in one's history is often considered a national trait.
The dish became a national specialty over generations.
That sense of humor is almost national among the people here.

Synset Examples

a national trait

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: regional - relating to a specific region; characteristic of a region
  • Antonym: international - involving two or more countries or nations

Etymology

From French national, from nation (nation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02999870-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈnæʃənl / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a nation or country as a whole, rather than to local or regional divisions.
relating to a nation or country

Examples

The president delivered a televised address on national security.
It is a national holiday celebrated throughout the entire country.
The company is expanding its national presence with new offices in several states.

Synset Examples

national hero
national anthem
a national landmark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: international - between or involving two or more nations
  • Antonym: local - belonging or relating to a particular area or neighborhood

Etymology

From French *national*, from Latin *natio* (nation, birth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02999528-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃ.ən.əl / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ.ən.əl / (BrE)
a person who is a citizen or subject of a particular nation, especially in relation to legal or immigration status
citizen or subject of a nation

Examples

Foreign nationals must have a valid visa to work in this country.
He was arrested along with several other French nationals.
The embassy provides services to its nationals who are living abroad.

Synset Examples

a monarch has a duty to his subjects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citizen - a legally recognized subject or member of a state or nation
  • Antonym: alien - a person who is not a citizen or national of a country

Etymology

From French national (adjective), from nation (nation) + -al (adjectival suffix). Noun use developed from the adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09648571-n
Synset POS: n

7. ADJ

/ ˈnæʃ(ə)nəl / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting a whole nation or country rather than just a part of it.
relating to a whole nation

Examples

The president addressed issues of national importance in his speech.
The museum is a national treasure, attracting visitors from across the country.
They watched the results of the national elections together.

Synset Examples

the national government
national elections
of national concern
the national highway system
national forests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local - relating to a particular area, not the whole nation
  • Synonym: domestic - relating to internal affairs of a country, especially in contrast with international

Etymology

From Latin 'natio' (nation, people) via Old French 'nacional'.
