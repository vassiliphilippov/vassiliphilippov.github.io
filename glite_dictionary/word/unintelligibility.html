unintelligibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪnˌtɛlədʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˌtɛlɪdʒəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to understand; lack of intelligibility
incomprehensibility; inability to be understood

Examples

The speaker's heavy accent contributed to the unintelligibility of his words.
The document was filled with such unintelligibility that even experts struggled to decipher it.
The film's poor audio quality resulted in the unintelligibility of much of the dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intelligibility - the quality of being understandable
  • Synonym: incomprehensibility - the quality of being impossible to understand

Etymology

From un- (not) + intelligibility, modeled on intelligible from Latin intelligibilis (that can be understood).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪnˌtɛlədʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˌtɛlɪdʒəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible or very difficult to understand.
state of being not understandable

Examples

The unintelligibility of his speech made it hard for the audience to follow his argument.
Some dialects can verge on unintelligibility for speakers of standard English.
The document's legal jargon contributed greatly to its unintelligibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensibility - the quality of being impossible to understand
  • Antonym: intelligibility - the quality of being clear and understandable

Etymology

From un- (not) + intelligibility, after intelligible, from Latin intelligibilis (that can be understood).

Synset POS: n
