vena thoracoepigastrica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə θɔːˌroʊˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌveɪnə θɔːˌrəʊˌepɪˈɡæstrɪkə / (BrE)
a vein that originates near the superficial epigastric vein and drains into either the axillary vein or thoracic vein
a vein connecting epigastric to axillary/thoracic vein
medical

Examples

The vena thoracoepigastrica provides an alternative pathway for venous blood return in cases of blockage.
In some surgical procedures, care must be taken not to damage the vena thoracoepigastrica.
The presence of a prominent vena thoracoepigastrica can indicate increased venous pressure.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'thoraco-' (relating to thorax), 'epigastrica' (relating to the upper abdomen).
