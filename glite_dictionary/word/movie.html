movie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvi / (AmE), / ˈmuːvi / (BrE)
A film; a story or event recorded by a camera as a set of moving images, usually shown in a theater or on television.
motion picture; film for entertainment
american

Examples

We watched a movie together at the cinema last night.
The movie received excellent reviews from critics.
She dreams of directing her own movie one day.

Synset Examples

they went to a movie every Saturday night
the film was shot on location

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a story or event recorded as moving images; British equivalent of 'movie'

Etymology

Originally from 'moving picture', shortened in American English in the early 20th century.
