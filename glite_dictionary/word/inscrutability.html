inscrutability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌskruːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌskruːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to understand or interpret
impossibility of being understood

Examples

Despite her friendly demeanor, there was an inscrutability about her that made others wary.
Philosophers have long debated the inscrutability of the universe and its laws.
The inscrutability of his motives left everyone puzzled.

Synset Examples

the inscrutability of the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something difficult or impossible to understand
  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being difficult to understand
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of being easy to perceive or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'inscrutabilis' (impossible to search or examine).
