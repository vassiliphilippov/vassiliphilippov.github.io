falcate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæɫ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈfal.keɪt / (BrE)
curved like a sickle; having a hooked or curved shape
curved like a sickle

Examples

The eagle's falcate talons help it grasp its prey securely.
Many tropical plants feature falcate leaves to shed rainwater efficiently.
Biologists noted the shark's falcate dorsal fin as a distinguishing feature.

a falcate leaf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle-shaped - having the shape of a sickle; curved
  • Antonym: straight - not curved or bent

Etymology

From Latin falcatus (sickle-shaped), from falx (sickle).
