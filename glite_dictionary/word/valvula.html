valvula

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælvjələ / (AmE), / ˈvalvjʊlə / (BrE)
A small valve, especially as found in anatomical or zoological structures.
small valve (especially in anatomy/zoology)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the valvula in the patient's heart was functioning properly.
Insects have a valvula that regulates the flow of air through their respiratory system.
Damage to the valvula can disrupt normal blood circulation in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valve - a device or structure that opens and closes to control the flow of fluids

Etymology

From Latin 'valvula', diminutive of 'valva' (leaf of a door, valve).
