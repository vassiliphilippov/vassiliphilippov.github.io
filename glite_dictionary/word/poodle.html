poodle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpuː.dl̩ / (BrE)
a dog breed known for its intelligence, dense curly coat, and often used as a companion or show dog
breed of dog with curly coat

Examples

The poodle trotted proudly beside its owner, its fur styled into neat pom-poms.
Many people are surprised by how intelligent a poodle can be when trained properly.
She entered her white poodle in the national dog show and took home first prize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - domesticated mammal often kept as a pet or for work

Etymology

From German 'Pudel', meaning 'puddle dog' or 'to splash in water'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpuːdəl / (AmE), / ˈpuːdl̩ / (BrE)
A breed of intelligent dog with a heavy, curly coat, often kept as a pet and sometimes trained for performances or as sporting dogs.
a breed of curly-haired dog

Examples

The poodle performed tricks for the audience at the circus.
Many people admire the distinctive coat of the poodle.
Her family adopted a miniature poodle from the animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal, Canis familiaris

Etymology

From German 'Pudel', short for 'Pudelhund', from 'pudeln' (to splash) and 'Hund' (dog).
