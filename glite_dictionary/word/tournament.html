tournament

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtʊənəmənt / (BrE)
a sports competition in which players or teams compete in a series of games to determine the overall winner
sports competition with multiple games

Examples

Our team entered an international tournament and made it to the semi-finals.
The chess tournament will be held over three days next month.
She won the tennis tournament after defeating the defending champion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - an event in which people compete to win

Etymology

From Old French 'torneiement', from 'tournoier' (to joust), related to medieval jousting events.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʊrnəmənt, ˈtɜrnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtʊənəmənt, ˈtɔːnəmənt / (BrE)
In the Middle Ages, a series of jousts or contests between knights competing for a prize.
medieval series of jousts
archaic

Examples

The king sponsored a grand tournament to celebrate the princess's birthday.
Knights from all over Europe gathered for the annual tournament in the royal city.
The tournament provided an opportunity for young noblemen to demonstrate their skill and bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joust - A combat between two knights on horseback with lances.
  • Degree Lower: duel - A fight between two people, usually with weapons, to settle a matter of honor.

Etymology

From Old French *torneiement* and *tornei* (a joust), from *tornoier* (to joust, to turn about).
