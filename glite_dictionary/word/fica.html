FICA

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.kə / (BrE)
a tax levied on employees and employers in the United States to fund the Social Security and Medicare programs
US payroll tax for Social Security and Medicare
american

Examples

Your paycheck shows how much was deducted for FICA each pay period.
Employers are legally required to match the FICA contributions of their employees.
Understanding FICA taxes is important for anyone who works in the United States.

Etymology

Acronym of Federal Insurance Contributions Act.
