Acocanthera

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈkænθərə / (AmE), / ˌakəˈkanθərə / (BrE)
a small genus of trees and shrubs native to Arabia and Africa, noted for containing highly toxic cardiac glycosides
genus of toxic shrubs and trees (Arabia, Africa)

Examples

Acocanthera species are known for their toxic properties and have been used in arrow poisons.
Botanists studied the distribution of Acocanthera in eastern Africa.
Extracts from Acocanthera must be handled with care due to their potent toxins.

Etymology

From Greek 'akokantha' (thorny point), referring to the plant's spiny nature.

2. NOUN

/ əˌkoʊkænˈθɛrə / (AmE), / ˌakəʊˈkanθərə / (BrE)
a genus of small trees and shrubs native to Africa and Arabia, known for containing toxic cardiac glycosides
toxic plant genus native to Africa and Arabia

Examples

The Acocanthera genus includes species that are highly poisonous to mammals.
Researchers extracted cardiac glycosides from Acocanthera plants for pharmaceutical studies.
Local communities have used Acocanthera for traditional medicine despite its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - a living organism of the kind exemplified by trees, shrubs, herbs, grasses, ferns, and mosses

Etymology

From New Latin Acocanthera, named using Greek roots for plant taxonomy.
