Megaptera novaeangliae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡ.æpˈtɪr.ə ˌnoʊ.vi.æŋˈɡli.i / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡ.æpˈtɪə.rə ˌnəʊ.vi.æŋˈɡliː.iː / (BrE)
the scientific name for the humpback whale, a large baleen whale with long flippers, known for arching its back when diving
humpback whale species (scientific name)

Examples

Researchers have observed Megaptera novaeangliae migrating thousands of miles every year.
The song of Megaptera novaeangliae can be heard great distances underwater.
Many conservation efforts focus on protecting Megaptera novaeangliae from threats like ship strikes and entanglement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humpback whale - large whalebone whale with a distinctive humped back

Etymology

From Greek 'megás' (great) + 'pterón' (wing), referring to long flippers, and neo-Latin, meaning 'New England'.
