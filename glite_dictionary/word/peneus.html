Peneus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈniːəs / (AmE), / pɪˈniːəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Peneidae, comprising certain prawns and shrimps.
genus of prawns/shrimps (Peneidae)

Examples

The genus Peneus includes several species of prawns found in marine environments worldwide.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of Peneus in the Gulf of Mexico.
Commercial fishing often targets species within the Peneus genus due to their economic importance.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek mythology (the river god Peneus).
