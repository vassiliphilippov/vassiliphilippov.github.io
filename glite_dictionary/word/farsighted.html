farsighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.saɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
able to plan wisely for the future; showing thought for what may happen later.
able to plan wisely for the future

Examples

The company's farsighted investments helped it weather the economic downturn.
Her farsighted approach to education reform was praised by experts.
Only a farsighted leader would implement policies that benefit future generations.

Synset Examples

large goals that required farsighted policies
took a long view of the geopolitical issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foresighted - having or showing foresight; planning for the future
  • Antonym: shortsighted - lacking consideration for future consequences

Etymology

From 'far' + 'sighted', originally relating to vision, later used figuratively for prudence.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrˌsaɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːˌsaɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
able to see distant objects more clearly than those that are near; affected by hyperopia.
able to see distant objects clearly

Examples

She has been farsighted since childhood and wears glasses for reading.
The optometrist explained that being farsighted means you have trouble seeing things up close.
Because he was farsighted, he could easily spot the sign on the distant building.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nearsighted - able to see near objects more clearly than distant ones; affected by myopia
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02164814-a
Synset POS: a
