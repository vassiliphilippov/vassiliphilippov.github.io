pyrene

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːn / (AmE), / paɪˈriːn / (BrE)
a pale yellow crystalline hydrocarbon (C16H10) found in coal tar and used in chemical research
a hydrocarbon found in coal tar

Examples

Pyrene is often used as a standard compound in fluorescence spectroscopy experiments.
Some polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, like pyrene, are studied for their environmental impact.
The researcher isolated pure pyrene from coal tar for further chemical analysis.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyrēn' (pit, stone), related to 'pyr' (fire), due to its isolation from coal tar.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːn / (AmE), / paɪˈriːni / (BrE)
the small hard stone or nutlet found within a drupe or drupelet, consisting of the seed surrounded by the hard endocarp
the stone or seed inside a drupe

Examples

The pyrene of a cherry is commonly referred to as the pit or stone.
Botanists study the structure of the pyrene to learn more about fruit evolution.
Inside the fleshy fruit, the pyrene protects the seed until it is ready to germinate.

Etymology

From Greek purēn (pit of a fruit, stone, kernel).
