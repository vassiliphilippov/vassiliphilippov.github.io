workhorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌhɔːs / (BrE)
A machine or device that operates reliably under heavy or continuous use, often performing the main or most demanding tasks.
dependable machine under heavy use

Examples

The diesel generator has become a workhorse for many remote construction sites.
This server is the workhorse of our computer lab, running day and night without failure.
For decades, the Boeing 747 was the workhorse of international air travel.

Synset Examples

the IBM main frame computers have been the workhorse of the business world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main element
  • Synonym: workhorse - a person who works hard and reliably

Etymology

From literal sense 'horse used for hard work', applied figuratively to machines from the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrkˌhɔːrs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌhɔːs / (BrE)
a horse used for pulling heavy loads, plowing, and other hard labor, especially on a farm
horse used for heavy labor

Examples

The farmer relied on his workhorse to plow the fields each spring.
Clydesdales are often considered one of the strongest breeds of workhorse.
Before machinery, every farm needed at least one workhorse for daily chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft horse - a large horse bred for hard, heavy tasks such as plowing and farm labor
