sottishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːtɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒtɪʃnəs / (BrE)
The state of being stupefied or dull from excessive drinking; drunkenness.
stupefaction from drinking alcohol

Examples

His sottishness embarrassed his family at every gathering.
Years of sottishness had dulled his once sharp mind.
The novel portrays the tragic consequences of sottishness and neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkenness - the state of being drunk
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, not affected by alcohol

Etymology

From 'sottish' (drunken, foolish), from Old French 'sot' (foolish, foolish person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːtɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒtɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of lacking restraint in the consumption of alcohol; drunkenness or habitual drunken behavior
lack of restraint in drinking alcohol

Examples

His sottishness led to frequent absences from work and damaged relationships.
The play depicted the severe consequences of sottishness in society.
Years of sottishness eventually took a toll on his health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intemperance - lack of moderation or self-restraint; especially with alcohol
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober; temperance in the use of alcohol

Etymology

From Middle English 'sottish', from Old French 'sot' (foolish, silly).
