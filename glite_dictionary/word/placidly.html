placidly

1. ADV

/ ˈplæs.ɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈplæs.ɪd.li / (BrE)
In a calm and good-natured manner; without excitement or disturbance.
in a calm or peaceful manner

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, she continued to read her book placidly.
The dog lay placidly at his owner's feet, unfazed by the noise.
He placidly accepted the news, showing no sign of distress.

Synset Examples

I put the questions, and she answered them placidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmly - in a calm manner
  • Synonym: peacefully - in a peaceful way
  • Antonym: agitatedly - in an agitated or disturbed manner

Etymology

From 'placid' + '-ly'. 'Placid' from Latin 'placidus' (pleasing, gentle).

2. ADV

/ ˈplæs.ɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈplæs.ɪd.li / (BrE)
In a calm and peaceful manner, without disturbance or excitement.
in a calm and peaceful manner

Examples

The lake reflected the mountains placidly in the early morning light.
She listened placidly to the music, lost in thought.
Despite the chaos around him, he continued to work placidly at his desk.

Synset Examples

the sea now shimmered placidly before our eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmly - in a calm or composed manner
  • Synonym: peacefully - in a peaceful manner; without disturbance

Etymology

From Latin 'placidus' (pleasant, gentle, calm) via 'placid' + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
