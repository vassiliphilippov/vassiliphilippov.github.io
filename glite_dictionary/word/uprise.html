to uprise

1. VERB

/ əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / ʌpˈraɪz / (BrE)
to rise to one's feet, especially after sitting or lying down.
rise to one's feet

Examples

The whole assembly uprise in silent protest at the announcement.
As the king entered, everyone in the hall uprise to show their respect.
He would uprise from his chair, stretching after a long conversation.

Synset Examples

The audience got up and applauded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position

Etymology

From Middle English uprisen, from Old English uprīsan (up + rise).
