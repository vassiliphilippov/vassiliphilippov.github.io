Annelida

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈlɪdə / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈlɪdə / (BrE)
A phylum of invertebrate animals that includes segmented worms such as earthworms, lugworms, and leeches.
phylum of segmented worms

Examples

Earthworms and leeches both belong to the phylum Annelida.
Scientists study the diverse habitats of Annelida around the world.
Most Annelida have bodies divided into ring-like segments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - any individual worm of the phylum Annelida

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'anellus' (little ring), referring to their ring-like segments.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈlɪdə / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈlɪdə / (BrE)
a phylum of segmented worms, including earthworms, leeches, and related species, characterized by ring-like segments along their bodies
phylum of segmented worms
medical

Examples

Earthworms, leeches, and polychaetes are all classified within the phylum Annelida.
Biology students studied the unique features of Annelida in their invertebrate zoology course.
Segmented bodies are one of the most distinctive traits of organisms within Annelida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - any member of the phylum Annelida; segmented worm

Etymology

From Latin 'anellus' (little ring), referring to the ringed segments of the worms.
