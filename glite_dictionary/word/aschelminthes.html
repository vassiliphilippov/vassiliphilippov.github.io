Aschelminthes

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (AmE), / ˌaskɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (BrE)
a former phylum of unsegmented worms, including roundworms, threadworms, and eelworms, now considered obsolete in current classifications
former worm phylum (obsolete)

Examples

The textbook described Aschelminthes as a group of simple, unsegmented worms.
Although Aschelminthes is no longer a valid phylum, the term appears in older biological literature.
Scientists now classify worms previously grouped under Aschelminthes into several distinct phyla.

Etymology

From Greek askos (bag) + helmins (worm), referring to the worm's body shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæskɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (AmE), / ˌæskɛlˈmɪnθiːz / (BrE)
a former phylum of unsegmented, pseudocoelomate worms including roundworms, threadworms, and eelworms, now considered to encompass several separate phyla
group of unsegmented, pseudocoelomate worms
medical

Examples

The Aschelminthes were once considered a single phylum, but are now divided into several groups.
Textbooks from the 20th century frequently discussed Aschelminthes as a major category of invertebrates.
Scientists no longer use Aschelminthes as a formal classification due to advances in molecular phylogeny.

Etymology

From Greek askos (sac, bag) + helmins (worm), originally coined for worms with a saclike body shape.
