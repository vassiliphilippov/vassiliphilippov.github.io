button

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən / (BrE)
a small area or icon on a computer screen that you click or tap to perform a particular function or command
on-screen control (computing)

Examples

Click the button at the bottom of the screen to submit your application.
If you hold down the power button, the computer will shut down.
She accidentally pressed the delete button and lost her work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icon - a small graphic symbol on a screen representing a program, file, or function

4. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬.ən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən / (BrE)
a small, typically round object sewn onto clothing and pushed through a slit (buttonhole) to fasten or close it
round clothing fastener

Examples

She sewed a new button onto her coat this morning.
The shirt was missing a button, so he couldn't fasten it properly.
I chose a jacket with brass buttons for a more formal look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - a fastener used to join fabric by pressing together two interlocking pieces
  • Antonym: zipper - a fastening device used in place of buttons, consisting of two strips of fabric with interlocking metal or plastic teeth

Etymology

From Old French *bouton* (bud, knob, button), from *bouter* (to push).
