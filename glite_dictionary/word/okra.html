okra

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkɹə / (AmE), / ˈəʊkɹə / (BrE)
a tall annual plant of the mallow family, cultivated for its edible green pods, which are mucilaginous when cooked and commonly used in soups and stews
edible green seed pod (vegetable)

Examples

She added sliced okra to the gumbo for extra thickness.
Many Southern dishes feature okra as a main ingredient.
I bought fresh okra at the farmer's market to make a vegetable stew.

Etymology

From Igbo *ọ́kwùrù* (the plant), through American English via West African speakers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.krə / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.krə / (BrE)
a tall annual plant of the mallow family, widely cultivated for its long green edible pods, which are used especially in soups and stews
edible plant with green pods

Examples

She sliced fresh okra for the gumbo she was preparing for dinner.
The okra plants in the garden grew impressively tall by midsummer.
Many Southern dishes feature okra as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gumbo - a stew or soup containing okra, especially in Southern US cuisine

Etymology

From Igbo *ọ́kụ̀rụ̀* (a West African language), via American English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkrə / (AmE), / ˈəʊkrə / (BrE)
A long, green, edible pod used as a vegetable in cooking, especially in soups and stews.
long green edible vegetable pod

Examples

She added sliced okra to the gumbo for extra flavor and texture.
Fried okra is a popular side dish in Southern cuisine.
The market had fresh okra alongside other summer vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's finger - another common name for okra

Etymology

From Igbo *ọ́kụ̀rụ̀*, referring to the plant and its pods.
