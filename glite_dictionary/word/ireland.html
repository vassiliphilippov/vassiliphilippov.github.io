Ireland

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪərlənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪələnd / (BrE)
An island in the North Atlantic, comprising the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland.
island including Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland

Examples

The island of Ireland is divided into two political entities.
Many people visit Ireland for its beautiful landscapes and rich history.
Ireland is the third largest island in Europe.

Etymology

From Old English Irland, from Old Norse Írland, meaning 'land of the Irish'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈaɪərlənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪələnd / (BrE)
A country occupying most of the island of Ireland in the North Atlantic, west of Great Britain; officially known as the Republic of Ireland, it gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1921.
country in western Europe; Republic of Ireland

Examples

She traveled across Ireland to visit her family in Cork and Dublin.
The government of Ireland introduced new environmental policies last year.
Many famous poets and writers have hailed from Ireland throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Northern Ireland - the region on the island of Ireland that remains part of the United Kingdom

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Ériu' (the goddess of the land) + 'land'.
