Desmodium gyrans

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdiəm ˈdʒaɪrænz / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdiəm ˈdʒaɪrænz / (BrE)
An erect tropical Asian shrub, also known as the telegraph plant, whose small lateral leaflets rotate and move up and down in response to sunlight.
tropical Asian shrub with moving leaflets

Examples

The unique movement of the Desmodium gyrans leaflets has fascinated botanists for centuries.
In the sunlight, Desmodium gyrans displays its characteristic jerky motions of the small lateral leaves.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Desmodium gyrans as an ornamental curiosity due to its remarkable leaf movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegraph plant - common name for Desmodium gyrans, known for leaf movement

Etymology

From Latin genus name Desmodium; 'gyrans' from Latin 'gyrāns' (turning, whirling).
