Algol

1. PROPN

/ ˈælˌɡɑl / (AmE), / ˈælɡɒl / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the constellation Perseus, notable as the first known eclipsing binary star system.
a bright star in Perseus, eclipsing binary

Examples

Ancient astronomers closely observed Algol for its mysterious variations in brightness.
With a telescope, you can spot Algol in the constellation Perseus on a clear night.
The periodic dimming of Algol revealed its nature as an eclipsing binary star.

Etymology

From Arabic رأس الغول (raʾs al-ghūl) meaning 'the demon's head.'
