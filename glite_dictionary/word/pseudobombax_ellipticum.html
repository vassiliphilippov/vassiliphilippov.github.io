Pseudobombax ellipticum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsjuːdoʊˈbɒmbæks ɛˈlɪptɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈbɒmbaks ɛˈlɪptɪkəm / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to Mexico and Central America, known for its dense, hairy flowers with long narrow petals clustered at the ends of branches before the leaves appear
a tropical tree with dense, hairy flowers

Examples

The Pseudobombax ellipticum produces spectacular bursts of pink flowers before any leaves grow in spring.
Botanists observed a mature Pseudobombax ellipticum thriving in the dry forests of southern Mexico.
Gardeners value Pseudobombax ellipticum for its showy flowers and unique growth habits.

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + Latin Bombax (a genus of trees), referencing its resemblance to Bombax; ellipticum from Latin (elliptical) describing the shape of its leaves.
