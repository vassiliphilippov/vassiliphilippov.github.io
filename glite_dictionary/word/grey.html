Grey

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
Lady Jane Grey (1536–1554), often referred to as the 'Nine Days' Queen,' was Queen of England for a brief period in 1553 before being deposed and executed for treason.
Lady Jane Grey, brief Queen of England

Examples

Lady Grey is remembered in history as England's 'Nine Days' Queen'.
The tragic fate of Grey has inspired numerous books and plays.
Few monarchs ruled as briefly as Grey, yet her legacy endures.
