prickliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪk.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪk.li.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being covered in prickly thorns or spines; the condition of being prickly
the state of being prickly or spiny

Examples

The prickliness of the cactus deterred the animals from coming too close.
She avoided touching the rose bush because of its prickliness.
The prickliness of the hedgehog's spines was obvious when it curled up into a ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly - covered with or full of prickles or sharp points

Etymology

From 'prickly' + '-ness', after Middle English 'prik' (point, puncture).
