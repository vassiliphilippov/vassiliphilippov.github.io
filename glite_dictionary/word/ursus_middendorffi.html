Ursus middendorffi

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrsəs ˌmɪdənˈdɔrfi / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs ˌmɪdənˈdɔːfi / (BrE)
a large subspecies of brown bear native to coastal Alaska and British Columbia, commonly known as the Kodiak bear
Kodiak bear subspecies

Examples

The Ursus middendorffi is known for its impressive size and strength.
Scientists studied Ursus middendorffi populations to understand bear conservation.
Coastal forests provide an ideal habitat for Ursus middendorffi in Alaska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - large bear species found across Eurasia and North America
  • Synonym: Ursus arctos - the scientific name for the brown bear species

Etymology

Named after Aleksandr Theodor von Middendorff, a Baltic German naturalist, with genus from Latin 'ursus' (bear).
