Lamentations

1. PROPN

/ ˌlæmənˈteɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˌlæmənˈteɪʃənz / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament, traditionally attributed to the prophet Jeremiah, expressing sorrow over the destruction of Jerusalem in 586 BC.
Old Testament book of sorrowful poems

Examples

Many scholars believe that Lamentations was written by the prophet Jeremiah after the fall of Jerusalem.
During the Bible study, we read passages from Lamentations to reflect on loss and hope.
The poetry in Lamentations vividly portrays the grief of a devastated people.

Etymology

From Latin Lamentationes, from Latin lamentari (to lament).
