raddled

1. ADJ

/ ˈræd.əld / (AmE), / ˈræd.əld / (BrE)
Looking worn out or disheveled as a result of fatigue, hardship, or suffering.
worn out in appearance

Examples

His raddled face revealed years of hardship on the road.
She felt raddled after weeks of caring for the sick.
The actor portrayed a raddled detective worn down by endless cases.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue or worry
  • Synonym: careworn - showing the effects of worry or anxiety
  • Antonym: fresh - looking healthy, rested, or not tired

Etymology

From the verb 'raddle' (to mark with lines or color), later extended to mean 'showing signs of being worn out.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈræd.əld / (AmE), / ˈræd.əld / (BrE)
(of an object) worn out or in poor condition from long or hard use; battered or shabby.
worn out or battered (object)
archaic

Examples

The raddled armchair in the corner looked ready to collapse at any moment.
He played an old, raddled guitar that had seen better days.
The attic was filled with raddled boxes and forgotten relics.

Synset Examples

battered trumpets and raddled radios
worn-out shoes with flapping soles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn-out - too old or damaged to be used
  • Synonym: battered - damaged by repeated use or blows
  • Antonym: new - recently made or obtained; not worn

Etymology

Related to the verb 'raddle' (to make worn or untidy), possibly from the dialect noun 'raddle' (a type of marking red ochre). Often literary or archaic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593311-s
Synset POS: s
