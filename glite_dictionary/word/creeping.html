creeping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪŋ / (BrE)
a slow type of movement, typically on hands and knees or by dragging the body along the ground
slow movement on hands and knees or by dragging body

Examples

The wounded soldier made his way to safety by creeping through the underbrush.
Progress was slowed to a near creeping as the hikers crossed the swampy terrain.
After the lights went out, there was only the sound of creeping in the hallway.

Synset Examples

a crawl was all that the injured man could manage
the traffic moved at a creep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crawl - a slow mode of locomotion, especially on hands and knees
