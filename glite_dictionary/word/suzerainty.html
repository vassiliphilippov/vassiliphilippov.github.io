suzerainty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːzərənti / (AmE), / ˈsjuːzərənti / (BrE)
The right of a state or ruler to exercise control over another state that retains its own government but is subordinate in certain matters, especially in foreign policy.
the control of one state over another
legal

Examples

In the 19th century, the Ottoman Empire claimed suzerainty over several Balkan states.
The treaty established British suzerainty while allowing internal autonomy.
Tibet was nominally under the suzerainty of the Chinese emperor during that period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority of a state to govern itself

Etymology

From French suzeraineté, from suzerain (feudal overlord).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːzərənti, ˈsjuːzərənti / (AmE), / ˈsjuːzərənti / (BrE)
the right or position of a sovereign or dominant state to control the foreign affairs of a vassal state while allowing it internal autonomy.
dominant control over another state

Examples

For much of the 19th century, the country remained under the suzerainty of a foreign empire.
The treaty acknowledged the suzerainty of the king while granting autonomy to the region.
Historians debate the nature of Ottoman suzerainty over its subject provinces.

Synset Examples

under the suzerainty of …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority, especially of a state

Etymology

From French suzeraineté, from suzerain (feudal overlord), ultimately from Old French suserain.
