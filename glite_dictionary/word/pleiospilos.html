Pleiospilos

1. NOUN

/ plaɪˈɒspɪlɒs / (AmE), / plaɪˈɒspɪlɒs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial succulent plants native to South Africa, known for their stone-like appearance and thick leaves.
genus of South African succulents

Examples

The Pleiospilos is often called 'split rock' because of its unique, rock-like shape.
Gardeners appreciate Pleiospilos species for their low water needs and unusual appearance.
She has a collection of Pleiospilos growing on her sunny windowsill.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleios' (more) and 'spilos' (spot), referring to the plants' spotted appearance.
