argyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrˌɡaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡaɪl / (BrE)
a pattern of interlocking, colored diamonds, often with an overlay of crossed lines, traditionally used in knitted clothing such as socks and sweaters
diamond-shaped knit pattern

Examples

He pulled up his argyle socks and prepared for golf practice.
The sweater featured a bold argyle pattern in shades of green and navy.
Classic argyle designs remain popular in preppy fashion circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tartan - woolen cloth with a pattern of intersecting stripes, associated with Scottish clans

Etymology

Named after the Argyll region in western Scotland, associated with the tartan of Clan Campbell.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.ɡaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ɡaɪl / (BrE)
a covered metal vessel for serving gravy, with a detachable insert for hot water to keep the contents warm
covered metal vessel for hot gravy

Examples

The silver argyle on the table kept the gravy piping hot throughout dinner.
He polished the antique argyle before the guests arrived for the formal meal.
Victorian households often used an argyle to serve sauces and gravies in style.

Etymology

Named after the Duke of Argyll, Scotland, who originated its use in the 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑrɡaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡaɪl / (BrE)
A sock knitted or woven with a pattern of colored diamonds, typically used for a distinctive, traditional look.
sock with diamond pattern

Examples

He wore a pair of bright argyle socks to the party.
Her favorite argyle matched the colors of her sweater perfectly.
Classic argyle is popular among golfers and preppy fashions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argyle - a pattern of diamonds or lozenges, often with overlapping motifs

Etymology

From Argyle (now Argyll), a region of western Scotland, where this pattern originated.
