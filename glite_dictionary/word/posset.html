posset

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑsɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒsɪt / (BrE)
a hot drink made of sweetened and spiced milk curdled with ale or beer, formerly popular as a remedy or dessert
sweet spiced hot milk curdled with ale or beer
archaic

Examples

In medieval England, a posset was often given to the sick as a comforting remedy.
She served a traditional posset flavoured with nutmeg and ginger after the evening meal.
The recipe calls for mixing hot milk with ale to make a sweet posset.

Etymology

From Middle English *posset*, of uncertain origin.
