otiose

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊtiˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈəʊtɪəʊs / (BrE)
disinclined to work or exert effort; lazy or idle
lazy; unwilling to work

Examples

His otiose attitude made it difficult for the team to complete the project on time.
The report criticized the board members for their otiose approach to urgent problems.
She grew frustrated with her otiose colleague, who rarely contributed to the workload.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolent - habitually lazy or avoiding activity
  • Antonym: industrious - diligent and hardworking
  • Synonym: slothful - lazy and unwilling to make an effort

Etymology

From Latin otiosus (at leisure, idle), from otium (leisure).

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊtioʊs / (AmE), / ˈəʊtɪəʊs / (BrE)
serving no practical purpose; producing no effect or result; futile or useless
producing no effect; futile

Examples

Their criticism was deemed otiose as the decision had already been made.
Launching another investigation would be otiose at this point.
He considered the warning otiose since the danger had already passed.

Synset Examples

a futile effort
the therapy was ineffectual
an otiose undertaking
an unavailing attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futile - incapable of producing any useful result; pointless
  • Synonym: ineffectual - not producing any significant or desired effect
  • Antonym: useful - able to be used for a practical purpose or in several ways

Etymology

From Latin otiosus (at leisure, idle, inactive, useless).

serving no useful purpose; pointless or unnecessary
serving no useful purpose; unnecessary

Examples

Much of the document consisted of otiose details that could have been omitted.
Continuing the argument seemed otiose once everyone had made up their minds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: useless - having no use or purpose
