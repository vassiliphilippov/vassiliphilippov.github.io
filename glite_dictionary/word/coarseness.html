coarseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being rude, lacking taste, or showing little refinement.
lack of taste or refinement

Examples

Many criticized the coarseness of his remarks during the debate.
The coarseness of the comedy offended more sensitive audience members.
Some felt that modern trends introduced a coarseness to culture that was once more refined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - rudeness or lack of refinement in manners or language
  • Antonym: refinement - cultivation or elegance in taste, manners, or style

Etymology

From coarse + -ness, originally from Old French cors (harsh) and Middle English –ness (forming nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rough or loose in texture, especially referring to materials like cloth or fabric
roughness or looseness in texture

Examples

The coarseness of the wool made the sweater uncomfortable against her skin.
He disliked the coarseness of the fabric and preferred something much softer.
The carpet's coarseness was apparent as soon as you touched it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of being rough to the touch or not smooth

Etymology

From coarse (rough, crude) + -ness (forming nouns denoting quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr·snəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having large, rough particles or a rough texture; the condition of not being fine or smooth
quality of having large, rough particles

Examples

The coarseness of the sand made it uncomfortable to walk on barefoot.
You can adjust the coarseness of the grind for your coffee beans depending on your preference.
The coarseness of the fabric was obvious when compared to the silk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fineness - the quality of being very small or fine in size or texture

Etymology

From coarse + -ness, modeled after Middle English forms.

/ ˈkɔrsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsnɪs / (BrE)

lack of refinement in language or behavior

Examples

Some people found the coarseness of his jokes offensive, while others thought they were hilarious.
Her writing is admired for its honesty and occasional coarseness when dealing with taboo subjects.
The stand-up comedian's coarseness made his performance feel both authentic and edgy.
