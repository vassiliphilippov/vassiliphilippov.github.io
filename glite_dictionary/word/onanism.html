onanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒnənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A method of birth control in which sexual intercourse is started but the penis is withdrawn before ejaculation to prevent pregnancy.
birth control by withdrawal method
medical

Examples

Some religious groups strictly prohibit onanism as a method of contraception.
The couple practiced onanism in an attempt to avoid having children.
Medical professionals generally do not recommend onanism as a reliable form of birth control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coitus interruptus - intentional withdrawal of the penis before ejaculation during intercourse

Etymology

From Late Latin Onanismus, after Onan, a biblical figure (Genesis 38:9) associated with withdrawal before ejaculation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.nəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.nə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
Manual stimulation of the genitals for sexual pleasure; masturbation.
manual genital stimulation; masturbation

Examples

Medical literature often uses the term onanism when discussing the effects of self-stimulation.
Debates about onanism and its morality have occurred throughout history.
He researched the history of beliefs regarding onanism in different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masturbation - self-stimulation of the genitals for sexual pleasure

Etymology

From Late Latin onanismus, from Onan, a Biblical figure (Genesis 38:9), originally referring to coitus interruptus.
