long-stalked

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈstɔkt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈstɔːkt / (BrE)
(of plants) having relatively long stalks.
having relatively long stalks (of plants)

Examples

The long-stalked leaves give the plant a graceful, arching appearance.
This species is distinguished by its long-stalked flowers that tower over the rest of the foliage.
Gardeners prefer long-stalked varieties for their ornamental value in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-stalked - (of plants) having relatively short stalks
