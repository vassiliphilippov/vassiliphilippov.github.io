Cervus elaphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvəs ɪˈlæfəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs ɪˈlæfəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the red deer, a large deer species native to Europe and Asia.
scientific name for red deer

Examples

The population of Cervus elaphus has rebounded in several European countries due to conservation efforts.
Biologists observed a herd of Cervus elaphus grazing at the edge of the forest.
The antlers of Cervus elaphus are prized by collectors for their impressive size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red deer - a large deer species native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin: ‘cervus’ meaning ‘deer’ and ‘elaphus’ meaning ‘stag’.
