pallid

1. ADJ

/ ˈpælɪd / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's face) pale, typically because of poor health, shock, or emotional distress.
pale or sickly in appearance

Examples

She noticed his pallid complexion as soon as he entered the room.
The patient's pallid face worried the attending nurse.
After the fright, her cheeks turned pallid with shock.

Synset Examples

the pallid face of the invalid
her wan face suddenly flushed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wan - pale and giving the impression of illness or exhaustion

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' (pale), from 'pallere' (to be pale).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpælɪd / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd / (BrE)
(of light) lacking in intensity or brightness; dim or feeble.
dim or feeble (of light)

Examples

The pallid glow from the lamp barely illuminated the corner of the room.
Early morning brought a pallid light through the misty window.
A pallid moon hung in the sky above the quiet city.

Synset Examples

the pale light of a half moon
a pale sun
the late afternoon light coming through the el tracks fell in pale oblongs on the street
a pallid sky
the pale (or wan) stars
the wan light of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dim - not bright or clear (of light)
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting a lot of light

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' (wan, pale), from 'pallere' (to be pale).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02333753-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpælɪd / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd / (BrE)
lacking in vitality, interest, or effectiveness; dull or uninspired.
dull or lacking vitality

Examples

The novel was well-researched, but the plot and characters remained disappointingly pallid.
Her speech was criticized for its pallid enthusiasm and lack of conviction.
The exhibit's pallid colors failed to inspire the visitors.

Synset Examples

a pale rendition of the aria
pale prose with the faint sweetness of lavender
a pallid performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images
  • Synonym: lifeless - lacking vitality or spirit

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' (pale), from 'pallēre' (to be pale).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
