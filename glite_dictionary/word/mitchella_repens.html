Mitchella repens

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈtʃɛlə rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / mɪˈtʃɛlə rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
A creeping woody plant native to eastern North America, distinguished by shiny evergreen leaves and scarlet berries.
creeping North American plant with red berries

Examples

Mitchella repens is often found carpeting the forest floor in shady woodlands.
The bright red berries of Mitchella repens are a favorite food for wildlife in the region.
Gardeners appreciate Mitchella repens for its attractive groundcover and year-round greenery.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name honoring John Mitchell, an American physician and botanist; 'repens' meaning 'creeping'.
