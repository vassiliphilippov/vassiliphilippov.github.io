positivist

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːz.ɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of positivism, a philosophical theory asserting that only factual knowledge gained through observation and experience is meaningful.
relating to positivism (philosophy)

Examples

Many positivist thinkers argue that only observable phenomena should be studied in science.
Her views on law are firmly rooted in the positivist tradition.
The university course explores the influence of positivist ideology on social research.

Synset Examples

positivist thinkers
positivist doctrine
positive philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positivism - philosophical theory that knowledge is exclusively derived from empirical and observable evidence

Etymology

From French positiviste, from positif (positive) + -iste (ist), related to the philosophical movement of positivism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətəvɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who emphasizes observable facts and excludes metaphysical speculation, especially as an adherent of the philosophy of positivism
adherent of the philosophy of positivism

Examples

As a committed positivist, he argued that scientific knowledge must be based solely on observable evidence.
The positivist rejected any explanation that could not be verified through empirical observation.
Many critics accused the positivist school of ignoring deeper philosophical questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricist - someone who believes knowledge comes only from experience or evidence
  • Antonym: metaphysician - someone who studies metaphysics; opposite to empirical approach

Etymology

From 'positivism', which derives from French 'positivisme', coined by Auguste Comte.
