Sami

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous nomadic people inhabiting the northern parts of Norway, Sweden, and Finland, and parts of Russia, known especially for reindeer herding
indigenous people of northern Scandinavia

Examples

The Sami have a rich tradition of music and storytelling.
Many Sami continue the practice of reindeer herding in Lapland today.
The museum features artifacts from Sami culture and history.

Etymology

From the North Sami word sámi, the people’s own name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.mi / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.mi / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Sami people of northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula
language of the Sami people

Examples

Linguists study the Sami language to preserve its rich oral traditions.
She learned Sami while traveling through northern Norway.
Several dialects exist within Sami, each with unique vocabulary and pronunciation.

Etymology

From Finnish and North Sami 'sápmi' or 'sámi', referring to the Sami people.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people inhabiting northern Scandinavia and parts of Russia, traditionally known for reindeer herding and a nomadic lifestyle
indigenous people of northern Scandinavia and Russia

Examples

The Sami have a rich tradition of storytelling and music.
Many Sami continue to practice reindeer herding as their ancestors did.
She studied the culture and languages of the Sami during her trip to Norway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lapp - another term for Sami people; considered outdated and sometimes offensive

Etymology

From the Sami people's own word for themselves, 'Sámi,' related to Finnish 'saamelainen.'
