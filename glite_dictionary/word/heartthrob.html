heartthrob

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌθrɑb / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌθrɒb / (BrE)
a person, especially a famous man, who is the object of intense romantic admiration or infatuation
person who is a romantic idol

Examples

In the 1990s, Leonardo DiCaprio became a teenage heartthrob after starring in 'Titanic'.
That actor is a major heartthrob among young fans around the world.
Every magazine featured the new pop heartthrob on its cover this month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - a person admired or revered, especially excessively
  • Synonym: crush - a person one has feelings of infatuation for

Etymology

Formed from 'heart' (the seat of emotion) and 'throb' (to beat strongly); recorded from the early 20th century.
