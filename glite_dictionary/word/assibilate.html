to assibilate

1. VERB

/ əˈsɪbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪbɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a consonant) to change into or be pronounced as a sibilant sound.
change into a sibilant sound

Examples

Some Romance languages assibilate the /t/ before certain vowels.
The sound /t/ may assibilate into /ts/ in some dialects.
Linguists note that the /d/ can assibilate to /dz/ depending on the phonetic environment.

Synset Examples

In the syllable /si/, the /s/ sibilates in Japanese

Etymology

From Latin assibilare, from ad- (to) + sibilare (to hiss).

2. VERB

/ əˈsɪbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪbɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) to insert or change into a sibilant, or to add a sibilant sound before or after another sound.
change or add a sibilant sound

Examples

Some dialects assibilate the 't' sound at the end of words.
The process will assibilate the original consonant.
Linguists study how certain phonemes assibilate in different language families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to palatalize - (in phonetics) to pronounce a sound with the tongue close to the palate

Etymology

From Latin 'assibilare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'sibilare' (to hiss).
