demiurge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmiˌɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛmiˌɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(in certain philosophical and religious systems) a being responsible for the creation of the universe, often viewed as subordinate to the supreme deity.
creator deity in some philosophies

Examples

According to Plato, the demiurge shaped the material world using eternal forms as his guide.
In Gnostic beliefs, the demiurge is often portrayed as an ignorant creator distinct from the true, supreme God.
Some philosophers argued that the demiurge acted as a craftsman, imposing order on pre-existing chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being

Etymology

From Greek dēmiourgos (public worker, craftsman, maker), from dēmos (people) + ergon (work).
