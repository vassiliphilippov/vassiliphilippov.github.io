sclerotomy

1. NOUN

/ sklɪˈrɑtəmi / (AmE), / sklɪəˈrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the sclera, the white outer layer of the eyeball
surgical incision of the eye's sclera
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist performed a sclerotomy to relieve intraocular pressure.
Complications following a sclerotomy include infection and delayed wound healing.
A sclerotomy is sometimes necessary during retinal surgery to access the back of the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sclera - the white outer layer of the eyeball

Etymology

From Greek sklēros (hard) and -tomy (cutting).
