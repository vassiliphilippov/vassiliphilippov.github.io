Rhinotermitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊtərˈmɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌrʌɪnəʊtəˈmɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A large, widely distributed family of termites found in temperate and tropical regions.
family of termites

Examples

Members of the Rhinotermitidae family are commonly known as subterranean termites.
The Rhinotermitidae are responsible for much of the termite-related wood damage in homes worldwide.
Scientists study the behavior of Rhinotermitidae to improve pest control strategies.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'rhino-' (meaning nose) and 'Termitidae' (the termite family name).
