badly

1. ADV

/ ˈbædli / (AmE), / ˈbadli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great distress, resentment, regret, or an emotional reaction.
with distress or emotional upset

Examples

After hearing the news, she cried badly for hours.
He took the criticism badly and refused to speak to anyone.
The children reacted badly when their pet disappeared.

Synset Examples

they took their defeat badly
took her father's death badly
conducted himself very badly at the time of the earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poorly - in a way that is not good; with poor quality or effectiveness
  • Antonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way

Etymology

From bad + -ly, forming an adverb from the adjective.
