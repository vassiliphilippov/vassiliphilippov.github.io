query

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪri / (AmE), / ˈkwɪəri / (BrE)
A question, especially one addressed to an official or organization, or an expression of doubt about something.
a question or inquiry

Examples

She had a query about the new company policy that needed clarification.
If you have any queries, please don't hesitate to contact customer support.
The student submitted his query to the professor after the lecture.

Synset Examples

there was a question about my training
we made inquiries of all those who were present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence or phrase used to find out information
  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information

Etymology

From Latin *quaerere* (to ask, inquire).

2. VERB

/ ˈkwɛri / (AmE), / ˈkwɪəri / (BrE)
to ask a question, especially in order to express doubt or to seek information
to ask a question or express doubt

Examples

She decided to query the manager about the missing files.
If you have any concerns, feel free to query the results.
The journalist queried the spokesperson on the accuracy of the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to put a question to someone
  • Synonym: question - to ask about something in order to get information or express doubt
  • Antonym: answer - to respond to a question or query

Etymology

From Latin 'quaerere' meaning 'to ask, seek'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00786670-v
Synset POS: v
