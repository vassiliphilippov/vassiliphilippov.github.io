metarule

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəˌruːl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌruːl / (BrE)
a rule that defines or governs the use of other rules, especially in fields such as artificial intelligence or logic
rule about how other rules are used

Examples

In this system, a metarule determines how new inference rules can be introduced.
Researchers discussed the importance of each metarule in shaping the behavior of the AI model.
Without a guiding metarule, the software could not adapt its logic effectively.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rule - a statement that tells you what is or is not allowed in a particular situation

Etymology

From Greek meta- (beyond) + rule, indicating a rule about rules.
