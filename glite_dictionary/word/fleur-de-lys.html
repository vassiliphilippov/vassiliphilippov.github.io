fleur-de-lys

1. NOUN

/ flɜr də ˈliːs / (AmE), / flɜː də ˈliː / (BrE)
a stylized representation of an iris flower, especially used as a decorative design or symbol in heraldry and art
stylized iris flower symbol; heraldic emblem

Examples

The royal coat of arms featured a prominent fleur-de-lys in gold.
Each gate was adorned with an elegant iron fleur-de-lys finial.
Historians often debate the origins of the fleur-de-lys in French heraldry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a type of flower often associated with the fleur-de-lys symbol

Etymology

From French 'fleur de lys' (flower of the lily/iris), referring to the stylized flower design.
