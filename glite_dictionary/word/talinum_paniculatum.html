Talinum paniculatum

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪnəm ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətəm / (AmE), / təˈlaɪnəm ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətəm / (BrE)
A tropical plant with tuberous roots and clusters of red to yellow flowers, native to southwestern North America through Central America and widely introduced elsewhere.
tropical flowering plant with tuberous roots

Examples

The garden featured a striking display of Talinum paniculatum in full bloom.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Talinum paniculatum roots.
Talinum paniculatum has become naturalized in many tropical regions around the world.

Etymology

From Latin, with Talinum as the genus name and paniculatum referring to a panicle (branched cluster of flowers).
