growler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊlər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊlə / (BrE)
a person who speaks with a voice that has a deep, harsh, or growling quality
person with a growling voice

Examples

The old actor was known as a growler due to his deep, rumbling voice.
Her father could be quite the growler when he was angry, his voice thunderous and rough.
Some radio announcers are memorable growlers, captivating audiences with their gravelly tones.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊlə / (BrE)
a small iceberg or ice floe, just large enough to pose a hazard to ships
small hazardous iceberg or ice floe

Examples

The ship carefully navigated around a growler lurking just below the surface.
Spotting a growler can be difficult because of its small size and low profile.
Despite its harmless appearance, a growler can severely damage a vessel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: iceberg - a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier
  • Degree Higher: bergy bit - a medium-sized fragment of glacier ice smaller than an iceberg and larger than a growler

Etymology

Probably from the low, growling noise such small icebergs make when scraping against a ship's hull or sea surface.
