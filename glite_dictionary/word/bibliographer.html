bibliographer

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪbliˈɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who is trained in or specializes in compiling bibliographies
person who compiles bibliographies

Examples

As a bibliographer, her work involves compiling extensive lists of academic publications.
The library hired a bibliographer to organize its growing research collection.
He became a renowned bibliographer known for his meticulous attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: librarian - person responsible for a library or its organization

Etymology

From Greek 'biblion' (book) + 'graphos' (writer).
