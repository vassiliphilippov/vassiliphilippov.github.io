Victor Frankenstein

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪktər ˌfræŋkənˈstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɪktə ˌfræŋkənˈstaɪn / (BrE)
the fictional Swiss scientist who is the central character in Mary Shelley's novel 'Frankenstein', known for creating a living being from assembled body parts
fictional scientist in Mary Shelley's Frankenstein

Examples

In the novel, Victor Frankenstein is haunted by the consequences of his scientific ambition.
Some people mistakenly call the creature itself Victor Frankenstein, but that is actually the name of the scientist.
The tragic story of Victor Frankenstein has influenced countless adaptations in literature and film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frankenstein's monster - the creature created by Victor Frankenstein in the novel
