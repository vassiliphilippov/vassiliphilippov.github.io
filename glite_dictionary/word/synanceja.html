Synanceja

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnænsiə / (AmE), / sɪˈnænsiə / (BrE)
A genus of venomous marine fishes, known as stonefishes, found in the Indo-Pacific region.
genus of venomous stonefishes

Examples

The genus Synanceja includes some of the most venomous fish in the world.
Marine biologists study Synanceja to understand its potent toxins.
A Synanceja can be mistaken for a rock due to its remarkable camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonefish - A venomous fish of the genus Synanceja, camouflaged to resemble a stone

Etymology

From Greek syn (with) + angeion (vessel, jar), referring to body features.

A genus of venomous marine fishes commonly known as stonefishes, noted for their ability to camouflage among rocks and coral.
genus of venomous stonefishes

Examples

The Synanceja genus contains some of the most venomous fish in the world.
A diver must beware of Synanceja, as their sting can be dangerous and even fatal.

Related Terms
