to deploy

1. VERB

/ dɪˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to move, arrange, or distribute people or resources systematically or strategically, especially in a military or organizational context
to position or distribute strategically

Examples

The tech company will deploy its new software across all offices next week.
During the crisis, emergency services were deployed to affected areas.
The general decided to deploy additional troops along the border.

Synset Examples

The U.S. deploys its weapons in the Middle East
The general deploys troops in a battle formation
The government rolled out the vaccine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - to assign to a specific place, especially for work or duty
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove or draw back troops or resources from a position

Etymology

From French 'déployer' (to unfold), from Latin 'displicitare', based on 'plicare' (to fold).
