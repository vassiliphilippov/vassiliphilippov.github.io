samba

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.bə / (AmE), / ˈsæm.bə / (BrE)
A lively, rhythmical dance and style of music originating in Brazil, characterized by African influences and commonly performed at festivals and social events.
Brazilian lively rhythmic dance and music

Examples

The energetic samba filled the streets during the festival.
She learned to dance the samba while visiting Brazil.
The band played a fast samba that got everyone moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - A lively dance of Cuban origin with Afro-Cuban rhythms

Etymology

From Portuguese 'samba', probably of Bantu origin, referring to a traditional Brazilian dance of African roots.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæmbə / (AmE), / ˈsæmbə / (BrE)
a large West African tree with palmately lobed leaves, axillary cymose clusters of small white flowers, and one-winged seeds; its soft white to pale yellow wood is often used for lightweight timber.
a large West African tree and its pale soft wood

Examples

The lightweight wood of the samba is popular for making furniture and musical instruments.
Carpenters often choose samba for interior construction due to its workability.
The forests of West Africa are home to many samba trees used in local industries.
