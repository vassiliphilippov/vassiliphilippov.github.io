South Vietnam

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ viˈɛtnæm / (AmE), / saʊθ viˈɛtnam / (BrE)
a former country in Southeast Asia that existed from 1954 to 1975, until its annexation by North Vietnam
former country in Southeast Asia (1954–1975)

Examples

After the fall of South Vietnam in 1975, the country was reunified under communist rule.
Many refugees fled South Vietnam during the final days of the Vietnam War.
The political situation in South Vietnam was unstable throughout much of its existence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Vietnam - former country in northern Vietnam (1945–1976)
