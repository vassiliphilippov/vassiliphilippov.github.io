rube

1. NOUN

/ ruːb / (AmE), / ruːb / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as unsophisticated, gullible, or lacking in culture, especially from a rural area
unsophisticated or gullible person
informal american

Examples

The city folk laughed at the rube who had never seen an escalator before.
Don't take me for a rube; I know what you're trying to pull.
He felt like a complete rube at the elegant dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumpkin - an unsophisticated or socially awkward person from the countryside
  • Antonym: sophisticate - a person with much worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture

Etymology

Shortened form of the given name Reuben, which became a stereotypical name for an unsophisticated country person in 19th-century America.

2. NOUN

/ ruːb / (AmE), / ruːb / (BrE)
a person who is unsophisticated or naïve, especially someone from a rural area regarded as lacking in urban polish or sophistication
unsophisticated or naïve person
informal

Examples

Only a rube would be impressed by those cheap tricks.
The city lawyer thought he could take advantage of a rube like me.
She felt like a rube in the fancy restaurant, unsure of which fork to use.

Synset Examples

He's such a rube, falling for that obvious scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumpkin - an awkward or unsophisticated person, typically from the countryside
  • Antonym: sophisticate - a person with much worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture

Etymology

Probably from the given name 'Reuben,' used as a generic name for a country person in the US.
