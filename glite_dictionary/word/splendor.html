splendor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɛndər / (AmE), / ˈsplendə / (BrE)
brilliant or impressive beauty, magnificence, or grandeur; a quality that outshines what is usual or ordinary
impressive beauty or grandeur

Examples

The palace was filled with the splendor of golden chandeliers and ornate decorations.
She was awestruck by the natural splendor of the Grand Canyon at sunrise.
The ceremony displayed all the splendor of royal tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificence - impressive beauty or grandeur
  • Antonym: humbleness - the quality of being modest or lacking grandeur

Etymology

From Latin 'splendor' (brightness, lustre), from 'splendere' (to shine).
