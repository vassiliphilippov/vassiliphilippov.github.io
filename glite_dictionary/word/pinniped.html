pinniped

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnɪˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈpɪnɪˌpɛd / (BrE)
an aquatic carnivorous mammal with a streamlined body and limbs modified as flippers, such as seals, sea lions, and walruses
aquatic mammal with flippers, e.g. seal

Examples

The polar region is home to several pinnipeds, including seals and walruses.
Researchers observed a group of pinnipeds basking on the rocky shore.
All pinnipeds have flippers that allow them to move swiftly through the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - common aquatic mammal, member of the pinniped group
  • Synonym: walrus - large marine mammal with tusks, member of the pinniped group

Etymology

From Latin 'pinna' (fin) + 'pes, pedis' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.əˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.ɪˌpɛd / (BrE)
an aquatic, carnivorous mammal with a streamlined body and flipper-like limbs, such as a seal, sea lion, or walrus
aquatic flippered mammal (seal, sea lion, walrus)

Examples

The harbor seal is a common pinniped found along many coastlines.
Researchers study pinnipeds to learn about their adaptation to marine life.
The sea lion is a pinniped known for its intelligence and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - a common pinniped with a sleek body and flippers
  • Synonym: sea lion - a type of pinniped with external ears and long flippers
  • Synonym: walrus - a large pinniped with tusks and whiskers

Etymology

From Latin 'pinna' (fin) + 'pedis' (foot), meaning 'fin-footed'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnəˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈpɪnɪˌpɛd / (BrE)
a large aquatic carnivorous mammal with a streamlined body and limbs modified as flippers, belonging to a group that includes seals, sea lions, and walruses
aquatic mammal with flippers (seals, sea lions, walruses)
medical

Examples

The walrus is a well-known pinniped found in Arctic waters.
Scientists study the diving abilities of various pinnipeds to understand their adaptations.
Unlike most mammals, a pinniped can close its nostrils completely while swimming.

Etymology

From Latin *pinna* (feather, fin) + *pes* (foot)
