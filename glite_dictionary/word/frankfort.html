Frankfort

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋkfərt / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkfət / (BrE)
a major city in Germany, known for its industrial, commercial, and financial importance
German industrial and financial city

Examples

Frankfort is home to one of Europe's largest stock exchanges.
The skyline of Frankfort features many modern skyscrapers.
International flights often connect through Frankfort due to its central location.

Etymology

From German 'Frankfurt', meaning 'ford of the Franks'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋkfɚt / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkfət / (BrE)
the capital city of Kentucky, located in the northern part of the state, on the Kentucky River
capital city of Kentucky, USA

Examples

Frankfort was bustling with activity during the annual Derby Festival.
Many government offices are headquartered in Frankfort, Kentucky's state capital.
We stopped in Frankfort on our road trip through the southern United States.

Etymology

Named after pioneer Stephen Frank; 'Frank's ford' eventually became Frankfort.
