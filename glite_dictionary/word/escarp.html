escarp

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskɑrp / (AmE), / ɪˈskɑːp / (BrE)
a steep artificial slope built in front of the walls of a fortification, usually to make assault more difficult
steep artificial slope before a fortress

Examples

The defenders used the escarp to hinder the progress of attackers.
The engineer explained how the escarp was constructed to strengthen the fortress's defenses.
Remnants of the old escarp can still be seen surrounding the ancient walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarp - a steep slope or artificial embankment, especially in fortification

Etymology

From French 'escarpe' (cutting, slope), from Italian 'scarpa' (slope), likely related to Latin 'scarpa' (scraped).
