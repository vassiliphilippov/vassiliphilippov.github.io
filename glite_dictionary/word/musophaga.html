Musophaga

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːsoʊˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌmjuːsəˈfɑːɡə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Musophagidae, a group of colorful African birds known as turacos.
genus of African turacos (birds)

Examples

The genus Musophaga includes several species of strikingly colored turacos.
Members of Musophaga are often found in West and Central African forests.
Researchers observed a rare Musophaga feeding on ripe bananas in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turaco - a colorful African bird of the Musophagidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'mus' (banana) and 'phagos' (eater), referring to the birds' diet.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈsɑfəɡə / (AmE), / mjuːˈsɒfəɡə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Musophagidae, commonly known as turacos, native to sub-Saharan Africa.
genus of turaco birds
medical

Examples

The Musophaga genus includes several colorful species of African turacos.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Musophaga in the wild.
The bright plumage of Musophaga makes them popular among birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turaco - any bird of the family Musophagidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Musophaga, from Greek 'mus' (banana) and 'phagein' (to eat), referring to their supposed fondness for bananas.
