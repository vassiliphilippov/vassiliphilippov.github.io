drippiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpinɪs / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively or insincerely sentimental; mawkishness
excessive or insincere sentimentality

Examples

The soap opera's drippiness made it hard for serious viewers to take it seriously.
His letter was filled with such drippiness that it seemed insincere rather than heartfelt.
Some critics complained about the drippiness of the film's final scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mawkishness - excessive or sickly sentimentality

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpinəs / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being drippy, especially in being soft and allowing liquid to drip
quality of being drippy

Examples

The drippiness of the ice cream made it difficult to eat on a hot day.
She complained about the drippiness of the paint, which ruined the clean finish.
After washing the lettuce, its drippiness left water spots all over the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drippy - tending to drip; soft and moist to the point of releasing liquid

Etymology

From 'drippy' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
