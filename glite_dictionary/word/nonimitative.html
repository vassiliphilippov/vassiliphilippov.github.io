nonimitative

1. ADJ

/ nɑːnˈɪmɪteɪtɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈɪmɪtətɪv / (BrE)
not marked by or given to imitation; original or not imitative.
not imitative; original

Examples

Her nonimitative approach to painting set her work apart from her contemporaries.
The composer is known for his highly nonimitative musical style.
Critics praised the author's nonimitative voice in her debut novel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imitative - copying or following a model or example

Etymology

From non- (not) + imitative (from Latin imitari, to copy).
