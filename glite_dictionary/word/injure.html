to injure

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dʒə / (BrE)
to hurt or damage someone's feelings; to offend or wound emotionally
hurt someone's feelings; offend emotionally

Examples

Her comments injured me deeply, though she probably didn't intend to.
He felt injured by the way his friends ignored his suggestion.
It is easy to injure someone's pride with careless words.

Synset Examples

She hurt me when she did not include me among her guests
This remark really bruised my ego

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel upset or hurt
  • Synonym: hurt - to cause emotional pain or distress

Etymology

From Latin 'injuriare' (to wrong), from 'injuria' (wrong, injustice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01797206-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒə / (BrE)
to cause harm, damage, or loss to someone or something
cause harm or damage

Examples

Be careful not to injure your back while lifting those heavy boxes.
Negative publicity can injure a company's reputation for years.
The new tax policy could injure many small businesses.

Synset Examples

Our business was hurt by the new competition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harm - to physically or otherwise hurt or damage
  • Synonym: damage - to cause physical harm to something, making it less usable or valuable
  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy again; to make better after harm

Etymology

From Latin 'injuriare' (to wrong), from 'injuria' (a wrong, injury).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00261356-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒə / (BrE)
to harm or damage someone's body or part of it, often as a result of an accident or attack
to cause bodily harm or damage

Examples

The soccer player was careful not to injure his ankle again before the big game.
Improper lifting techniques can injure your back over time.
She accidentally injured her finger while chopping vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wound - to inflict an injury, especially one involving a break in the skin
  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy again; to make something healthy again after injury or damage

Etymology

From Latin 'injuriare', from 'injuria' meaning 'a wrong, injury'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069650-v
Synset POS: v
