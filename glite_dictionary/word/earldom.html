earldom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrldəm / (AmE), / ˈɜːldəm / (BrE)
the rank, title, or domain of an earl; the territory or jurisdiction governed by an earl
the territory or rank of an earl

Examples

After his father's death, he inherited the earldom and its estates.
The history museum displayed a map of the ancient earldom as it looked in the 12th century.
She was given the title and responsibilities of the earldom after her husband's passing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: county - the domain of a count or countess

Etymology

From Old English 'eorldom' (the jurisdiction or dignity of an earl).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrl.dəm / (AmE), / ˈɜːl.dəm / (BrE)
the rank, title, or office of an earl, or the territory governed by an earl
rank or office of an earl
british

Examples

After his father's death, he inherited the earldom and became a powerful noble.
The earldom was granted by the king in recognition of his service.
She became countess upon her marriage into the earldom.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duchy - the territory or rank of a duke
  • Degree Lower: barony - the rank or dignity of a baron

Etymology

From Old English 'eorldom', from 'eorl' (earl) + '-dom' (domain, jurisdiction).
