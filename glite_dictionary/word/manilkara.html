Manilkara

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪlˈkɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌmanɪlˈkɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of large evergreen tropical trees with milky latex, found across tropical regions.
genus of large evergreen tropical trees

Examples

The sap of Manilkara trees is used in making chicle for gum.
Several species of Manilkara produce edible fruit enjoyed in many tropical countries.
Botanists recently discovered a new Manilkara species in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From the genus name in Latin, derived from Malayalam 'manil-kara', a local tree name.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪlˈkɑɹə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪlˈkɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of large evergreen trees with milky latex, found in tropical regions worldwide.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

The Manilkara genus includes several species known for their hardwood.
Many fruits from Manilkara trees are edible and enjoyed in tropical regions.
Some Manilkara trees produce latex that can be used similarly to rubber.

Etymology

From the Malayalam 'manil-kara', a local name for one of the trees (Manilkara kauki).
