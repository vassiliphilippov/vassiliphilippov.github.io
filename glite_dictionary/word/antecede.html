to antecede

1. VERB

/ ˌæn.təˈsid / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiːd / (BrE)
to come before something in time; to precede
to come before in time

Examples

Written language antecedes the printing press by many centuries.
These traditions anteceded the formation of the modern state.
In scientific development, certain discoveries antecede major technological changes.

Synset Examples

Stone tools precede bronze tools

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to precede - to come before in time or order
  • Antonym: to follow - to come after in time or order

Etymology

From Latin antecedere, from ante (before) + cedere (to go).
