Crixivan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪk.siˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪk.siˌvan / (BrE)
A trademarked medication (indinavir) used as a protease inhibitor in the treatment of HIV infection.
HIV medication (protease inhibitor)
medical

Examples

His doctor prescribed Crixivan as part of his antiretroviral therapy for HIV.
Many patients have experienced side effects while taking Crixivan for an extended period.
Access to medications like Crixivan has significantly improved the life expectancy of people living with HIV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indinavir - the generic name for the medication marketed as Crixivan
  • Synonym: antiretroviral - a drug used to treat retrovirus infections, mainly HIV
