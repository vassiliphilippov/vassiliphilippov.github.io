mollycoddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑliˌkɑd(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈmɒliˌkɒd(ə)l / (BrE)
a person, especially a man or boy, who is pampered and seen as weak or overly effeminate
a pampered or effeminate man
archaic

Examples

He was treated like a mollycoddle by his overprotective parents.
Some critics dismissed the gentle poet as a mere mollycoddle, unfit for real hardship.
The coach warned that he had no patience for a mollycoddle on his team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sissy - a boy or man regarded as effeminate or cowardly
  • Antonym: tough - a strong and courageous person

Etymology

From 'molly' (mild diminutive for Mary) and 'coddle' (to treat tenderly); dates to early 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑliˌkɑdəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒliˌkɒd.əl / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive care or indulgence, often to their detriment
treat with excessive indulgence

Examples

Parents should be careful not to mollycoddle their children, as it can hinder their independence.
The manager refused to mollycoddle his team and instead encouraged self-reliance.
Some believe that modern society tends to mollycoddle young adults too much.

Synset Examples

grandparents often pamper the children
Let's not mollycoddle our students!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pamper - treat with too much care or indulgence
  • Synonym: spoil - give too much to or indulge someone, especially a child

Etymology

Probably from 'molly' (a pet form of 'Mary') + 'coddle'.
