Xylariaceae

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌlɛəriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / zaɪˌlɑːrɪˈiːsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi distinguished by dark brown to black spores, including many wood-decaying species
family of fungi with dark spores
medical

Examples

Members of the Xylariaceae are often found on decaying wood in forests.
The study focused on the ecological roles of fungi in the Xylariaceae family.
Several species within Xylariaceae are important to wood decomposition processes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Xylaria.
