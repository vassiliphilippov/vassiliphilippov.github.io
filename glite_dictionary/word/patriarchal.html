patriarchal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪtriˈɑrkəl / (AmE), / ˌpeɪtrɪˈɑːkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a society, family, or system controlled by men, especially older men or those of higher rank.
controlled or dominated by men

Examples

The company culture was strongly patriarchal, with most leadership positions held by older men.
Many traditional societies operate under patriarchal systems that give priority to male authority.
She criticized the patriarchal values that limited women's opportunities in her community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchal - relating to or characteristic of a society controlled by women

Etymology

From Late Latin patriarchalis, from Greek patriarchēs (chief or father of a family).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪtriˈɑrkəl / (AmE), / ˌpeɪtriˈɑːkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a social system in which men hold primary power and predominate in roles of leadership, moral authority, and control of property
relating to male-dominated society

Examples

The community was strictly patriarchal, with men making all major decisions.
Historically, many societies around the world have been organized on patriarchal lines.
She challenged the patriarchal norms that limited women's opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchal - relating to a social system in which women hold primary power
  • Synonym: patriarchy - a system of society or government dominated by men

Etymology

From late Latin patriarcālis, from patriarch, originally from Greek patriarkhēs (chief or ruler of a family).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01742074-a
Synset POS: a
