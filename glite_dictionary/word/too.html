too

1. ADV

/ tuː / (AmE), / tuː / (BrE)
to a degree exceeding what is desirable, necessary, or acceptable; more than is suitable or required
to an excessive degree

Examples

The coffee was too hot for me to drink immediately.
She found the instructions too complicated to follow without help.
It's too late to catch the last train tonight.

Synset Examples

too big

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: very - to a great degree (but not exceeding a desirable limit)
  • Synonym: also - in addition; as well

Etymology

From Old English 'tō', related to German 'zu' (to) and Dutch 'te'.

2. ADV

/ tu / (AmE), / tuː / (BrE)
used to show that a statement applies to someone or something in addition to another person or thing; also; as well.
in addition; also; as well

Examples

She likes chocolate, too.
I want to come too, if that’s okay.
Not only is he smart, but he's funny too.

Synset Examples

he has a Mercedes, too

Related Terms

  • Synonym: also - used to indicate addition or similarity
  • Synonym: as well - in addition; also

Etymology

From Old English tō (to, towards), later strengthened and specialized in sense.
