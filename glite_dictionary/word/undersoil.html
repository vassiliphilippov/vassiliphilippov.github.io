undersoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌndəsɔɪl / (BrE)
the layer of soil that lies beneath the topsoil and above the bedrock
layer of soil below topsoil

Examples

Plants with deep roots may reach into the undersoil in search of nutrients.
The construction team analyzed both the topsoil and the undersoil before beginning the project.
Heavy rainfall can cause nutrients to wash down from the topsoil into the undersoil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsoil - the soil lying immediately under the surface soil
