asynchronous

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (BrE)
not happening, existing, or moving at the same time or rate; not synchronous
not occurring at the same time

Examples

The server can handle multiple asynchronous requests simultaneously.
Unlike traditional classes, asynchronous online courses let students complete work on their own schedule.
Their clocks are asynchronous, which causes coordination problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronous - occurring or existing at the same time or rate

Etymology

From Greek 'asynkhronos', from 'a-' (not) + 'syn-' (together) + 'khronos' (time).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02388163-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋkrənəs / (BrE)
(of digital communication) not requiring a common clock between communicating devices, with timing signals derived from special characters within the data stream.
not requiring a common clock in digital communication

Examples

In an asynchronous transmission, data is sent without the need for both devices to share a clock signal.
Many modern network protocols use asynchronous communication to increase flexibility and reduce complexity.
The company upgraded its equipment to support asynchronous data transfer, allowing for more efficient device communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronous - requiring a common timing signal or clock between communicating devices

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'synchronos' (synchronous, occurring at the same time).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02389387-a
Synset POS: a
