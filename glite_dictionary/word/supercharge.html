to supercharge

1. VERB

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to increase the pressure of a gas or liquid, especially in an engine or mechanical system
increase pressure of a gas or liquid

Examples

The engineers decided to supercharge the engine to increase its performance.
Compressed air was supercharged into the chamber for testing purposes.
They are currently supercharging the system to meet the new standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compress - reduce in volume by increasing pressure

Etymology

From 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'charge', referring to filling or loading with pressure.

2. VERB

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to increase the power, intensity, or effectiveness of something, especially beyond the normal level
increase power or intensity

Examples

The company wants to supercharge its growth by entering new markets.
Adding spices can supercharge the flavor of a simple dish.
The latest update will supercharge your smartphone's performance.

Synset Examples

boost the voltage in an electrical circuit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boost - to increase or raise; make something better or more powerful
  • Synonym: to amplify - to make something stronger, especially sound or intensity
  • Antonym: decrease - to make or become smaller or less

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above, beyond) + 'charge' (to fill with power or energy), originally from mechanical engineering.

Source: WordNet
