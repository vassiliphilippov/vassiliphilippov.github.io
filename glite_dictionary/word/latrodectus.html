Latrodectus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs / (AmE), / ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs / (BrE)
a genus of venomous spiders, commonly known as widow spiders, found worldwide
genus of venomous widow spiders

Examples

The Latrodectus genus includes several notorious species, such as the black widow spider.
Scientists captured a specimen of Latrodectus to study its venom.
Most bites from Latrodectus spiders are not fatal but can cause severe symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widow spider - common name for spiders of the genus Latrodectus

Etymology

From Greek latros (physician) + dektēs (biter), referring to the spider's bite.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs / (AmE), / ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous spiders, including the black widow, known for their potent neurotoxic venom.
genus of venomous spiders
medical

Examples

The bite of a Latrodectus spider can cause severe pain and muscle cramps in humans.
Scientists are studying the venom of Latrodectus to develop new painkillers.
Several species within the Latrodectus genus are commonly called black widows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black widow - a venomous spider of the genus Latrodectus, known for the female's red hourglass marking

Etymology

From New Latin, coined from Greek latros (physician) + dektēs (biter).
