atmometer

1. NOUN

/ ætˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ætˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the rate at which water evaporates into the atmosphere
instrument for measuring water evaporation rate

Examples

The researcher placed the atmometer in the field to track evaporation rates during the summer.
Farmers sometimes use an atmometer to optimize their irrigation schedules.
The lab demonstrated how a simple atmometer collects data on water loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'atmos' (vapor) and 'metron' (measure).
