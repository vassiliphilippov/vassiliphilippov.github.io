antiaircraft

1. ADJ

/ ˌæntiˈɛrkræft / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈeəkrɑːft / (BrE)
(of weapons or defenses) designed for use against aircraft from the ground or a surface position.
defensive against aircraft attacks

Examples

The base was surrounded by antiaircraft guns to protect it from enemy bombers.
They installed new antiaircraft missile systems along the coastline.
An antiaircraft battery opened fire when enemy planes appeared overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-aircraft - alternative spelling for defense against aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) + 'aircraft'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈɛrkræft / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈeəkraːft / (BrE)
artillery or weapons designed to attack or defend against aircraft in flight
weapons against aircraft

Examples

The ship was equipped with powerful antiaircraft guns to defend against enemy planes.
During the war, antiaircraft fire filled the sky as bombers approached the city.
Modern antiaircraft systems use radar to track and destroy hostile aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-aircraft gun - a gun designed to shoot at aircraft

Etymology

From anti- (against) + aircraft (flying vehicle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02718623-n
Synset POS: n
