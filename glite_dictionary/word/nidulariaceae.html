Nidulariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪdʒʊˌlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌnɪdjʊˌlɑːriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as bird's-nest fungi, characterized by fruiting bodies resembling tiny nests containing egg-like structures
family of bird's-nest fungi

Examples

The Nidulariaceae are easily recognized by their unique nest-like fruiting bodies in forest habitats.
Scientists have studied the reproductive strategies of Nidulariaceae in various ecosystems.
Several genera within the Nidulariaceae display remarkable adaptations for spore dispersal.

Etymology

From Latin nidulus (little nest) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant and fungal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪdjuˌlɑːriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌnɪdjʊləˌraɪəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as bird's-nest fungi, recognized for their nest-like fruiting bodies containing peridioles resembling eggs.
family of bird's-nest fungi

Examples

Some species in the Nidulariaceae family are commonly called bird's-nest fungi because of their unique fruiting bodies.
The Nidulariaceae can be found on decaying wood in damp forests worldwide.
Researchers studied the dispersal mechanism of peridioles in members of the Nidulariaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird's-nest fungus - a fungus with a nest-like fruiting body containing egg-like structures

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'nidularius' (of a nest), referring to nest-like appearance.
