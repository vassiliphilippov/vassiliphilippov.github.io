havelock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæv.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈhæv.lɒk / (BrE)
A cloth covering for a service cap, with a flap extending over the back of the neck to protect against the sun.
neck-protecting cap cover

Examples

The soldiers wore a havelock to shield their necks from the desert sun.
During the expedition, he adjusted his havelock to provide better protection from sunburn.
The museum displayed an old military cap complete with a white havelock.

Etymology

Named after Sir Henry Havelock (1795–1857), British general.
