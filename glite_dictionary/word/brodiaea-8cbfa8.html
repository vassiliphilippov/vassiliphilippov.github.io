Brodiaea

1. NOUN

/ broʊˈdiːə / (AmE), / brəʊˈdiːə / (BrE)
A genus of bulbous plants native to the western United States, having basal leaves and colorful flowers, sometimes classified in the family Alliaceae.
genus of bulbous plants native to western US

Examples

Several species in the Brodiaea genus are prized for their vibrant blue-purple flowers in gardens.
The Brodiaea blooms in late spring across many regions of California and Oregon.
Some botanists argue about whether Brodiaea should be placed in the lily family or the Alliaceae.

Etymology

Named after James Brodie (1744–1824), Scottish botanist.
