aileron

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.ləˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.lərɒn / (BrE)
A hinged surface on the wing of an aircraft used to control lateral balance by regulating roll.
hinged control surface on aircraft wing

Examples

The pilot adjusted the aileron to help the plane turn smoothly to the left.
During turbulent weather, damaged ailerons can make an aircraft difficult to control.
Learning how the aileron affects flight is crucial for any student pilot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flap - a movable part on an aircraft wing used to increase lift or drag
  • Antonym: rudder - a control surface used to steer an aircraft by controlling yaw

Etymology

From French *aile* (wing) with the diminutive suffix *-eron*, meaning "little wing."
