cryptobiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊbaɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊbaɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a reversible state in which an organism's metabolic processes nearly stop, allowing survival under extreme conditions
reversible suspended metabolic state
medical

Examples

Certain microorganisms enter cryptobiosis to survive long periods of drought or extreme heat.
The eggs of some aquatic species undergo cryptobiosis when their environment dries out.
Scientists study cryptobiosis in tardigrades to understand how life persists under harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibernation - a state of inactivity and metabolic depression in animals
  • Synonym: anabiosis - a temporary return to life after apparent death or dormancy

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'bios' (life) + '-osis' (process).
