Sanicula

1. PROPN

/ səˈnɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / səˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American and Eurasian herbs in the family Apiaceae, commonly known as sanicle.
a genus of herbs (sanicle)

Examples

Botanists discovered a rare species of Sanicula growing in the forest understory.
She has several Sanicula plants in her native wildflower garden.
The Sanicula genus is valued for its medicinal uses in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanicle - any plant of the genus Sanicula

Etymology

From Latin 'sanicula', a kind of plant, diminutive of 'sanus' meaning 'healthy'.
