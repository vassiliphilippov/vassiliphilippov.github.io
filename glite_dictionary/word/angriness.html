angriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋ.ɡri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋ.ɡri.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being angry.
state of being angry

Examples

His angriness became clear when he started raising his voice during the meeting.
She tried to hide her angriness, but her clenched fists gave her away.
It is important to address children's angriness in a healthy and constructive manner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - strong feeling of displeasure or hostility

Etymology

From 'angry' + '-ness', using the productive English noun-forming suffix.
