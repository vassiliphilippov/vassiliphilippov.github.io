cyclone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.kloʊn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.kləʊn / (BrE)
a violent windstorm characterized by a rotating system of winds, often causing damage, especially in tropical or subtropical regions
violent rotating windstorm

Examples

The cyclone destroyed several homes along the coast, leaving widespread devastation in its wake.
Meteorologists issued a warning as a powerful cyclone approached the region.
Indian farmers often fear the loss of crops during the cyclone season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurricane - a type of intense tropical cyclone in the Atlantic
  • Synonym: typhoon - a tropical cyclone in the western Pacific

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklōn', present participle of 'kykloun' (move in a circle), from 'kyklos' (circle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkloʊn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkləʊn / (BrE)
A system of winds rotating around a center of low atmospheric pressure, characterized by inward spiraling air and often bringing stormy weather; rotates counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere.
rotating storm system with low pressure center

Examples

The coastal city was evacuated when a powerful cyclone approached from the ocean.
Meteorologists are tracking the movement of a cyclone forming off the southern coast.
Damage from the cyclone included flooding, downed trees, and widespread power outages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticyclone - a weather system with high pressure at its center, winds circulate opposite to a cyclone
  • Synonym: hurricane - a type of severe tropical cyclone in the Atlantic and Northeast Pacific
  • Synonym: typhoon - a mature tropical cyclone in the Northwest Pacific

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklōn', present participle of 'kykloun' (to move in a circle), from 'kyklos' (circle).
