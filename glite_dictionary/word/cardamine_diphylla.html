Cardamine diphylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːr.dəˈmiːn ˌdaɪˈfɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.dəˈmiːn ˌdaɪˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the mustard family, with pungent scaly or toothed roots, also known as two-leaved toothwort.
North American herb with pungent roots

Examples

Botanists are studying the unique root structure of Cardamine diphylla in eastern forests.
Early settlers sometimes used Cardamine diphylla as a medicinal herb.
You can identify Cardamine diphylla by its two distinctive leaves and white spring flowers.

Etymology

From genus name Cardamine, from Greek 'kardamon' (cress), and Latin 'diphylla', meaning 'two-leaved'.
