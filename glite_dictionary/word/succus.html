succus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌk.əs / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.əs / (BrE)
A liquid or juice secreted by the body, especially in a medical context.
body fluid or juice (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the succus of the stomach is essential for digestion.
Researchers collected samples of intestinal succus to analyze for digestive enzymes.
An increase in gastric succus production can lead to discomfort or ulcers.

Synset Examples

digestive juices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juice - liquid that comes from or is present in fruits, vegetables, or living organisms

Etymology

From Latin *succus* (juice).
