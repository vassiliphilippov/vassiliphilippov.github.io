Pachyrhizus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækɪˈraɪzəs / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈrɪzəs / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical vines with tuberous roots, some species of which are cultivated for their edible tubers.
genus of tropical vines with tuberous roots

Examples

The tubers of Pachyrhizus are popular in many tropical cuisines.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Pachyrhizus in the wild.
Some farmers plant Pachyrhizus as a drought-resistant food crop.

Etymology

From Greek 'pachys' (thick) and 'rhiza' (root).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈraɪzəs / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈrʌɪzəs / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical vines in the pea family, known for their edible tuberous roots, including the jicama plant.
genus of tropical vines with tuberous roots

Examples

The Pachyrhizus genus is best known for producing the edible root vegetable jicama.
Farmers in Central America often cultivate Pachyrhizus for its nutritious tubers.
Botanists classify jicama as a member of the Pachyrhizus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'pachys' (thick) and 'rhiza' (root).
