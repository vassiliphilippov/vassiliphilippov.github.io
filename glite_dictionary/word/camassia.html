Camassia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæsiə / (AmE), / kəˈmæsiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to North and South America, having large edible bulbs, commonly known as camas or quamash.
genus of edible-bulbed plants (camas)

Examples

Camassia species were historically important as a food source for indigenous peoples.
The meadows bloomed with distinctive blue flowers of Camassia during spring.
Botanists often study Camassia to understand the ecology of North American prairies.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the Native American Nez Perce word 'quamás' (referring to the plant's bulb).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmæsiə / (AmE), / kəˈmasɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North and South American herbaceous plants with large edible bulbs, also known as camas.
genus of edible-bulb plants (camas)

Examples

Camassia plants were once an important food source for Native Americans.
Many gardeners grow Camassia for its attractive blue flowers.
Botanists classified the wild camas under the genus Camassia.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, named after 'camas', a Native American word for the plant's edible bulbs.
