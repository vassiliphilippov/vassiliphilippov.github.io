solidification

1. NOUN

/ səˌlɪdəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌlɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming solid or hard, especially by cooling, drying, or crystallization
the process of becoming solid or hard

Examples

The solidification of magma leads to the formation of igneous rocks.
Rapid solidification of the metal is essential in casting processes.
The chef waited for the solidification of the chocolate coating before serving the dessert.

Synset Examples

the hardening of concrete
he tested the set of the glue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardening - the process of becoming hard or harder
  • Antonym: melting - the process of becoming liquid by heating

Etymology

From French solidification, from solidifier (to solidify), ultimately from Latin solidus (solid).
