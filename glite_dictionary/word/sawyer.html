sawyer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔː.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.jə / (BrE)
a type of beetle whose larvae bore holes in dead or dying trees, especially conifers
wood-boring beetle (larva in trees)

Examples

The larvae of the sawyer can severely damage timber by tunneling through the wood.
A forester identified several sawyers infesting the dead pines in the forest.
The distinctive markings of a sawyer make it easy to recognize among wood-boring beetles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔjər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːjə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to saw wood, especially as a profession
a person who saws wood

Examples

The sawyer spent hours cutting timber for the construction project.
Each sawyer wore safety goggles and ear protection in the busy mill.
After years as a sawyer, he became an expert in handling all types of saws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcutter - a person who cuts down trees or cuts wood

Etymology

From Middle English 'sawyer', from 'saw' + '-yer', denoting an agent noun.
