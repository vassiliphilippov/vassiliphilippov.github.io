cupule

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (BrE)
a small sucker-like structure on the feet of certain flies, used for attachment or locomotion
sucker on a fly's foot

Examples

The housefly uses its cupules to cling to smooth vertical surfaces.
Scientists observed that the cupule allows stable attachment even on glass.
The structure of the cupule is critical for the insect's movement and grip.

Etymology

From Latin 'cupula', meaning small cup.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (BrE)
A cup-shaped structure of hardened bracts at the base of an acorn or similar fruit, enclosing or supporting it.
cup-shaped structure at the base of an acorn

Examples

The cupule protects the developing acorn until it is mature.
Each acorn is held in place by a woody cupule at its base.
Botanists examined the cupule structure to identify the species of oak.

Etymology

From Latin 'cupula' (small cup), diminutive of 'cupa' (cup).
