sleek

1. ADJ

/ sliːk / (AmE), / sliːk / (BrE)
well-groomed and stylish in appearance, sometimes suggesting excessive neatness or refinement
well-groomed and stylish in appearance

Examples

Clara always looked sleek in her perfectly tailored suits.
He admired her sleek hairstyle and flawless appearance at the gala.
The model's sleek look turned heads on the runway.

Synset Examples

sleek figures in expensive clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smart - neat and stylish in appearance
  • Antonym: sloppy - careless and untidy in appearance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02436793-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ sliːk / (AmE), / sliːk / (BrE)
having a smooth, shiny, and often lustrous surface or texture; resembling silk in softness and appearance
smooth, glossy, and shiny

Examples

The cat's sleek black fur shone in the sunlight.
She ran her fingers over the sleek surface of the polished table.
His hair looked especially sleek after he used the new conditioner.

Synset Examples

satiny gardenia petals
sleek black fur
silken eyelashes
silky skin
a silklike fabric
slick seals and otters
silky auburn hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - having a smooth, soft, and lustrous surface like silk
  • Synonym: satiny - having a smooth, glossy texture like satin
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or irregular surface; not smooth or soft

Etymology

Perhaps related to Middle Low German *slick* (smooth, soft).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00283541-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ slik / (AmE), / sliːk / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or design) having a smooth shape designed to minimize resistance to fluid flow; streamlined.
smooth and streamlined in design

Examples

The sleek sports car glided down the highway, its design cutting through the air effortlessly.
Engineers worked to make the new aircraft as sleek and aerodynamic as possible.
Their latest yacht features a sleek hull, helping it move quickly through the water.

Synset Examples

a streamlined convertible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamlined - (of a vehicle or object) designed to offer little resistance to flow of fluid; aerodynamic
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02245872-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ sliːk / (AmE), / sliːk / (BrE)
to make (something) smooth, glossy, or neat, typically by brushing, stroking, or pressing
make smooth and glossy

Examples

She carefully sleeked her hair back before the meeting.
The cat sleeked its fur with graceful licks.
He used wax to sleek the surface of the wooden table.

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse sljókr (smooth, sleek).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
