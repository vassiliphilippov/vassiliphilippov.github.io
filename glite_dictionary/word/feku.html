Feku

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪkuː / (AmE), / ˈfeɪkuː / (BrE)
(Indian slang) a person who is dishonest or deceptive, especially by exaggerating or making false claims.
dishonest or deceptive person (Indian slang)
slang

Examples

During the campaign, his critics called him a Feku for making unrealistic promises.
No one believed his story because he had a reputation as a Feku.
She accused her opponent of being a Feku in front of the entire audience.

Synset Examples

The politician was accused of being a Feku during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • Antonym: truthful - someone who tells the truth

Etymology

Hindi and Urdu फेकू/فیکو‎ (fekū), meaning 'one who bluffs or lies'.
