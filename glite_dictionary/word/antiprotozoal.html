antiprotozoal

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌproʊtəˈzoʊəl / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌprəʊtəˈzəʊəl / (BrE)
A medicinal drug used to treat or prevent infections caused by protozoa, such as malaria.
drug for protozoal infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiprotozoal to treat her amoebic infection.
Malaria can often be managed with effective antiprotozoal medications.
Some antiprotozoal drugs also have side effects that must be monitored carefully.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: antibacterial - a drug used to treat bacterial infections
  • Degree Lower: antiviral - a drug used to treat viral infections

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'protozoa' (single-celled organisms), reflecting its use against protozoan diseases.
