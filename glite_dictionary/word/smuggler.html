smuggler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmʌɡlər / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡlə / (BrE)
a person who illegally imports or exports goods without paying the required duties or taxes
person who illegally imports/exports goods

Examples

The police caught the smuggler at the border with hundreds of illegal goods.
A smuggler tried to hide rare artifacts in his luggage.
During the prohibition era, every smuggler risked years in prison if discovered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trafficker - a person involved in the illegal trade of goods or people
  • Antonym: customs officer - an official responsible for enforcing import/export regulations

Etymology

From the verb 'smuggle', probably from Low German 'smuggeln', meaning to sneak in or steal.
