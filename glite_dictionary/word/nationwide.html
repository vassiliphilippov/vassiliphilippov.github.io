nationwide

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪʃənˌwaɪd / (AmE), / ˌneɪʃənˈwaɪd / (BrE)
existing or happening throughout an entire country; involving the whole nation.
happening across the whole country

Examples

The new law will have a nationwide impact when it comes into force.
There was a nationwide search for the missing child.
The company launched a nationwide advertising campaign to promote its latest product.

Synset Examples

the event aroused nationwide interest
a countrywide fund-raising campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countrywide - happening across the whole country

Etymology

From 'nation' + 'wide', first recorded in the 19th century.

2. ADV

/ ˈneɪʃənˌwaɪd / (AmE), / ˈneɪʃ(ə)nˌwaɪd / (BrE)
Extending or occurring throughout an entire nation.
across the whole country

Examples

The results of the survey were reported nationwide in all major newspapers.
The new policy will be implemented nationwide next year.
A nationwide network of volunteers responded quickly to the disaster.

Synset Examples

nationally advertised
it was broadcast nationwide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationally - adverb meaning 'in a way that concerns the whole nation'

Etymology

Formed from 'nation' + 'wide', modeled after similar adjectives and adverbs like 'citywide'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00409125-r
Synset POS: r
