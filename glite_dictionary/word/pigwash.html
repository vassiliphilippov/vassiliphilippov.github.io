pigwash

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
Wet food waste, such as kitchen scraps mixed with water or milk, fed chiefly to pigs.
wet kitchen waste used to feed pigs

Examples

The farmer poured the pigwash into the trough for the hungry pigs.
Household pigwash was collected daily to supplement the animals' diet.
Some people found the smell of pigwash unpleasant, but the pigs loved it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swill - kitchen refuse or scraps used as feed for pigs

Etymology

Pig + wash, referring to kitchen scraps washed or mixed with liquid and fed to pigs.
