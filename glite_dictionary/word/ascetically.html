ascetically

1. ADV

/ əˈsɛtɪkli / (AmE), / əˈsetɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons.
in a strict, self-denying manner

Examples

Despite her wealth, she chose to live ascetically, giving away most of her possessions.
The monks ate and dressed ascetically as part of their spiritual discipline.
He approached his training ascetically, avoiding all distractions and luxuries.

Synset Examples

she lived ascetically in a small house all by herself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austerely - in a severely simple or strict manner; closely related in meaning

Etymology

From 'ascetic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek askētikos (rigorously self-disciplined).
