Hemipteronatus

1. NOUN

/ hɛmɪpˌtɛroʊˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / hɛˌmɪptəˈrɒnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Centriscidae, commonly known as razor fish, characterized by their elongated bodies and sharp-edged appearance.
genus of razor fish

Examples

The Hemipteronatus is easily recognized by its knife-like body shape.
Marine biologists observed a school of Hemipteronatus near the coral reef.
The aquarium's newest exhibit features the elusive Hemipteronatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor fish - common name for fish in the genus Hemipteronatus

Etymology

From Greek 'hēmi-' (half) + 'pteron' (wing) + Latinized taxonomy suffix '-atus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmpɪtəroʊˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmpɪtəˈrɒnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of razor fish belonging to the family of wrasses, known for their slender bodies and sharp dorsal fins.
a genus of razor fish

Examples

The genus Hemipteronatus contains several species known for their elongated bodies and sharp dorsal fins.
Marine biologists discovered a new member of the Hemipteronatus genus along the coral reef.
Children visiting the aquarium were fascinated by the unique appearance of the Hemipteronatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor fish - a slender fish with a sharp dorsal fin, sometimes called a razorfish
