anaglyphical

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of anaglyphs or anaglyphy, especially the technique used to create stereoscopic 3D images by superimposing two images of different colors.
relating to anaglyphs or 3D image creation

Examples

The anaglyphical process allows viewers to see three-dimensional images using red and blue glasses.
Her thesis explored the history and technology behind anaglyphical 3D imaging in cinema.
We analyzed the anaglyphical representation of landscapes to compare depth perception among participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaglyph - a stereoscopic 3D image created by superimposing two colored images

Etymology

From anaglyph (from Greek 'anagluphos', carved in relief) + -ical.
