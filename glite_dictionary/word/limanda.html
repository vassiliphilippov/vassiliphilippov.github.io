Limanda

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmændə / (AmE), / lɪˈmandə / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Pleuronectidae, known as righteye flounders, with a humped nose and small, often brightly colored underside.
genus of righteye flounders

Examples

The genus Limanda includes several species of flatfish found in northern oceans.
Ichthyologists have studied the unique coloration patterns seen in Limanda.
The Limanda genus is important for both commercial fishing and marine research.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Limanda', originally from French 'limande' (dab or sole).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈmændə / (AmE), / lɪˈmændə / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish (family Pleuronectidae) known as righteye flounders, distinguished by a humped nose, small scales, and often brightly colored undersides.
a genus of righteye flounders

Examples

The genus Limanda includes several species of commercially important flatfish.
A scientist collected specimens from the Limanda genus to study their brightly colored undersides.
Fishermen in northern waters often catch members of the Limanda genus.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Limanda, based on a Latinized fish name.
