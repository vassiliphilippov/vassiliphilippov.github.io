paederasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːdərˌæsti / (AmE), / ˈpiːdəˌræsti / (BrE)
sexual relations between a man and a boy, especially referring to anal intercourse with the boy as a passive partner, historically associated with certain ancient societies
sexual relations between a man and a boy

Examples

The ancient Greeks often discussed paederasty within the context of philosophy and education.
Debates around paederasty appear in studies of classical literature and history.
The law strictly prohibits paederasty in all its forms in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pederasty - sexual relations between a man and a boy (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Greek paiderastia, from pais (boy) + erastes (lover).
