streptokinase

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptoʊˈkaɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəʊˈkɪneɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme produced by certain streptococci, used to dissolve blood clots in the treatment of conditions such as myocardial infarction and pulmonary embolism.
enzyme used to dissolve blood clots
medical

Examples

The doctor administered streptokinase to break up the patient's blood clot.
Treatment with streptokinase is common after certain types of heart attacks.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of streptokinase in various clinical settings.

Etymology

From modern Latin strepto- (from Greek streptos ‘twisted’) + kinase (from ‘enzyme catalyzing transfer of a phosphate group’).
