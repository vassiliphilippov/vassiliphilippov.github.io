Lactuca sativa longifolia

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtuːkə səˈtaɪvə ˌlɒŋɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə səˈtaɪvə ˌlɒŋɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with long, dark-green, spoon-shaped leaves, commonly known as romaine lettuce.
variety of lettuce (romaine) with long leaves

Examples

She added chopped Lactuca sativa longifolia to the Caesar salad for extra crunch.
Farmers in the region are known for growing high-quality Lactuca sativa longifolia.
The nutritionist recommended including more Lactuca sativa longifolia in your diet for its vitamin content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romaine - a common name for Lactuca sativa longifolia
  • Synonym: cos lettuce - another common name for Lactuca sativa longifolia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lactuca' (lettuce), 'sativa' (cultivated), and 'longifolia' (long-leaved).
