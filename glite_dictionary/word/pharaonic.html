Pharaonic

1. ADJ

/ fɛrˈɑːnɪk / (AmE), / fəˈrɑːnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the ancient Egyptian pharaohs or their period of rule
of or relating to ancient Egyptian pharaohs

Examples

The pyramids are among the most impressive Pharaonic monuments ever built.
Her research focuses on Pharaonic art and its religious significance.
Visitors to Egypt are often amazed by the scale of Pharaonic architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharaoh - an ancient Egyptian king

Etymology

From French 'pharaonique', from 'pharaon' (pharaoh), ultimately from Egyptian 'per-aa' (great house).
