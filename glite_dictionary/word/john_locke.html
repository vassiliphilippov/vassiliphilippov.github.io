John Locke

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn lɑk / (AmE), / dʒɒn lɒk / (BrE)
(1632–1704) An English philosopher and physician, regarded as one of the most influential Enlightenment thinkers, best known for his work on empiricism and political philosophy.
English philosopher (1632–1704), influential empiricist

Examples

Many modern theories of government are rooted in the political philosophy of John Locke.
John Locke's ideas on empiricism dramatically shaped Western thought in the 17th and 18th centuries.
The social contract theory was developed in part by John Locke and other Enlightenment thinkers.
