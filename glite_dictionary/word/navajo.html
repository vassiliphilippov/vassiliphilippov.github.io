Navajo

1. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnavəhəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people traditionally living in the southwestern United States, especially in Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah
a member of a Native American people of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

Her grandmother is a proud Navajo who grew up on a reservation in Arizona.
Many Navajo served as code talkers during World War II, helping to secure Allied communications.
The festival celebrated the art and traditions of the Navajo people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo - the language spoken by the Navajo people

Etymology

From Tewa Navahu, meaning 'farm fields in the valley'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnavəhəʊ / (BrE)
the native Athabaskan language spoken by the Navajo people, primarily in the southwestern United States
Athabaskan language of the Navajo

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Navajo.
The documentary included interviews in Navajo with English subtitles.
Efforts are underway to preserve the Navajo language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apache - Athabaskan language spoken by the Apache people

Etymology

From Spanish 'Navajo', originally from Tewa (a Pueblo language) 'Navahuʼu' (planted fields).
