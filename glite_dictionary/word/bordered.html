bordered

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔrdɚd / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəd / (BrE)
having a border, especially one of a specified kind
having a border or edging

Examples

The garden was beautifully bordered with bright marigolds.
She wore a red scarf bordered with white lace.
The official document was bordered in gold, giving it an elegant touch.

Synset Examples

black-bordered handkerchief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: borderless - without a border or edge

Etymology

From the verb 'border' + -ed suffix.
