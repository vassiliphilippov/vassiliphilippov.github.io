Praxiteles

1. PROPN

/ prækˈsɪtəˌliz / (AmE), / prakˈsɪtəliːz / (BrE)
an ancient Greek sculptor active around 370–330 BC, renowned for his graceful and naturalistic statues, such as the Aphrodite of Knidos.
ancient Greek sculptor (c. 370–330 BC)

Examples

Many art historians consider Praxiteles to be one of the greatest sculptors of classical Greece.
The statue of Hermes and the Infant Dionysus is attributed to Praxiteles.
Students studied the works of Praxiteles to learn about ancient Greek art.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πραξιτέλης (Praxitélēs), a personal name.
