forsaking

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈseɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈseɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of abandoning or giving something up.
the act of abandoning something

Examples

His forsaking of old friends disappointed everyone in his community.
The forsaking of unhealthy habits is never easy but often necessary.
There was a sense of relief in her forsaking of city life for the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving or deserting someone or something

Etymology

Gerund and verbal noun from the verb 'forsake', from Old English 'forsacan' (to deny, renounce).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˈseɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈseɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of abandoning or renouncing someone or something.
the act of abandoning

Examples

His forsaking of his old friends left many people feeling hurt and confused.
The novel centers around her forsaking of her family in pursuit of freedom.
Religious texts often warn against the forsaking of one's duties and principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of giving up completely or deserting someone or something
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithfulness or allegiance to someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'forsake', which comes from Old English 'forsacan' (deny, renounce).
