Ploceus

1. NOUN

/ ˈploʊsiːəs / (AmE), / ˈpləʊsiːəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the weaver family (Ploceidae), known for their intricate woven nests.
genus of weaver birds

Examples

The Ploceus genus includes several species of brightly colored weaver birds.
Researchers studied the nest-building behavior of the Ploceus in African wetlands.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a rare member of the Ploceus family during their expedition.

Etymology

From Greek *plokeus* (weaver), referring to the birds' nest-building habits.
