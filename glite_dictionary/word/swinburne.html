Swinburne

1. PROPN

/ ˈswɪnbɝn / (AmE), / ˈswɪnbɜːn / (BrE)
(1837–1909) English poet and playwright known for his lyrical verse and controversial themes.
English poet (1837–1909)

Examples

Many critics regard Swinburne as one of the great poets of the Victorian era.
Swinburne was known for both his innovative meter and radical subject matter.
Literature students often study the works of Swinburne alongside his contemporaries.
