to organise

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
(of workers) to form or join a labor union.
form or join a union
british

Examples

The workers planned to organise in order to negotiate better pay.
Many employees are reluctant to organise for fear of retaliation from management.
Efforts to organise the warehouse staff met with strong resistance from the company.

Synset Examples

The auto workers decided to unionize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - to form or join a labor union

2. VERB

/ ˈɔr.ɡə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or systematize things or activities in an orderly way
arrange or systematize
british

Examples

She spent the afternoon trying to organise the documents on her desk.
We need to organise a team meeting for next week.
He always finds time to organise his workspace before starting work.

Synset Examples

Can you help me organize my files?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - put things in a neat or required order
  • Antonym: to disorganise - to make things disordered or chaotic

Etymology

From French 'organiser', from Medieval Latin 'organizare', from Latin 'organum' (instrument, tool).
