Carpobrotus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrpəˈbroʊtəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpəˈbrəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of succulent plants in the family Aizoaceae, known for their thick, fleshy leaves and brightly colored flowers, commonly called ice plants.
genus of succulent plants (ice plants)

Examples

The coastal gardens featured several varieties of Carpobrotus with striking purple flowers.
Carpobrotus is often planted to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.
Some species of Carpobrotus produce edible fruits enjoyed in local cuisines.

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + brotos (edible), referring to the edible fruit of some species.

2. NOUN

/ kɑr-pə-ˈbroʊ-təs / (AmE), / kɑː-pə-ˈbrɒ-təs / (BrE)
A genus of succulent plants in the family Aizoaceae, native to southern Africa, known for their fleshy leaves and large, daisy-like flowers.
genus of succulent plants (Aizoaceae)

Examples

The Carpobrotus genus includes species commonly known as ice plants.
Gardeners often choose Carpobrotus for coastal landscaping due to its hardiness.
The bright pink flowers of Carpobrotus attract many pollinators in spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'karpos' (fruit) and 'brotos' (edible), referring to the edible fruit of some species.
