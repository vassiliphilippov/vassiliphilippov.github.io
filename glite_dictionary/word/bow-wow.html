bow-wow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊ ˌwaʊ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ ˌwaʊ / (BrE)
an informal or childish term for a dog
informal term for a dog
informal

Examples

Whenever she saw a bow-wow, the toddler giggled and clapped her hands.
The cartoon featured a talking bow-wow as its main character.
He promised his children a bow-wow if they kept their rooms tidy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal with a barking or howling voice

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a dog's bark, first recorded in English in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊ ˌwaʊ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ ˌwaʊ / (BrE)
a barking sound made by a dog; the vocalization that sounds like 'bow-wow'
the bark of a dog

Examples

The little puppy's playful bow-wow echoed through the garden as it chased its tail.
We were awakened by the loud bow-wow of dogs barking in the street.
Children laughed every time they heard the dog's happy bow-wow near the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woof - the bark or vocal sound of a dog
  • Synonym: bark - the sharp explosive cry of a dog

Etymology

Imitative of a dog's bark, first used in English in the late 16th century.
