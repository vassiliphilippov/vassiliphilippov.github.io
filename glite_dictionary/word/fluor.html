fluor

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊər / (AmE), / ˈflʊə / (BrE)
A soft mineral composed of calcium fluoride, known for its fluorescence under ultraviolet light and as the main source of fluorine.
a fluorescent mineral (calcium fluoride)

Examples

The mineral fluor is commonly used as the main source of industrial fluorine.
Under ultraviolet light, fluor glows with a distinctive blue or violet color.
Geologists discovered a rich vein of fluor near the old mining town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorite - the mineral calcium fluoride, another name for fluor

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow), from the mineral's use as a flux; related to its property of lowering melting points in metallurgy.
