Schizophragma

1. NOUN

/ skɪˌzoʊˈfræɡmə / (AmE), / skɪˌzɒˈfræɡmə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous climbing and creeping shrubs with white flowers in flat clusters, sometimes classified in the family Saxifragaceae.
genus of climbing shrubs with white flowers

Examples

Schizophragma is prized in gardens for its attractive foliage and clusters of white flowers.
Several species in the Schizophragma genus are native to East Asia.
The climbing habit of Schizophragma makes it ideal for covering walls and pergolas.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) and 'phragma' (fence or partition), referring to characteristics of the plant's fruit.

2. NOUN

/ skɪˌzɒfəˈræɡmə / (AmE), / skɪˌzɒfəˈræɡmə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous climbing and creeping shrubs with white flowers in flat clusters, sometimes considered part of the Saxifragaceae family.
genus of climbing shrubs with white flowers

Examples

Schizophragma is often found growing on trees in Japanese forests.
Gardeners appreciate the ornamental clusters of white flowers produced by Schizophragma.
Unlike hydrangeas, Schizophragma blooms slightly later in the summer.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) and 'phragma' (fence, partition), referring to a feature of the plant's fruit.
