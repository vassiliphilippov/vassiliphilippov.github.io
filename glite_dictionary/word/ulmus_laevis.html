Ulmus laevis

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌlməs ˈlaɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs ˈliːvɪs / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian elm tree that closely resembles the American elm and thrives in moist environments.
species of Eurasian elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus laevis is commonly found along riverbanks where the soil remains moist.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Ulmus laevis to compare it with other elm species.
An ancient Ulmus laevis stands in the park, admired for its towering branches and smooth bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree in the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' meaning 'elm' and 'laevis' meaning 'smooth'.
