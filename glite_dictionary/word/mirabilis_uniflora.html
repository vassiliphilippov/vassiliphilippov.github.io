Mirabilis uniflora

1. NOUN

/ məˈræbɪlɪs ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A garden plant native to North America with fragrant red, purple, yellow, or white flowers that open in late afternoon.
North American garden plant with fragrant late-afternoon flowers

Examples

The Mirabilis uniflora in our garden blooms with vibrant purple flowers every evening.
Gardeners appreciate Mirabilis uniflora for its pleasant fragrance and striking late-afternoon blossoms.
You can find Mirabilis uniflora growing wild in certain regions of North America.

Etymology

From Latin 'mirabilis' (wonderful) and 'uniflora' (one-flowered).
