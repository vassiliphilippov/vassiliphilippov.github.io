obsequiousness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsi:kwiəsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈsiːkwɪəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively eager to please or obey someone, often in a servile or ingratiating way
excessive eagerness to please; servility

Examples

His obsequiousness toward his boss made his colleagues uncomfortable.
She used her obsequiousness as a strategy to gain favor with influential people.
The obsequiousness of the staff was obvious every time the celebrity visited the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servility - excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: insolence - rude or disrespectful behavior (opposite attitude)

Etymology

From Latin 'obsequiosus' (compliant, obedient).
