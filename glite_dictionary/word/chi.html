2. NOUN

/ tʃiː / (AmE), / tʃiː / (BrE)
the vital life force believed in Chinese philosophy and medicine to flow through all living things, essential for good health and balance
vital life force in Chinese philosophy

Examples

In tai chi, practitioners focus on harnessing their chi to improve strength and flexibility.
Acupuncture is said to help restore the flow of chi throughout the body.
Many people meditate to balance their chi and promote wellbeing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: qi - another spelling of 'chi' denoting the same concept of vital energy in Chinese philosophy
  • Synonym: prana - Sanskrit term for life force, similar concept in Indian philosophy

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 氣 (qì), meaning 'air' or 'breath'.
