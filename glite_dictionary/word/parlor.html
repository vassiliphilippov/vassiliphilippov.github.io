parlor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə / (BrE)
A room in an inn, club, or large house where visitors may be received or entertained.
reception room in an inn or club

Examples

Guests waited in the parlor while their rooms were being prepared.
The club's parlor was elegantly decorated with portraits and antique furniture.
She greeted her friends in the front parlor of the old inn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salon - a room for receiving guests or holding a social gathering, especially in a large house

Etymology

From Old French *parloir*, from parler 'to speak'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə / (BrE)
a room in a private house or establishment where people can sit, talk, and relax, especially one formerly used for entertaining guests.
room for sitting and conversation

Examples

They spent the afternoon chatting in the parlor with tea and biscuits.
The old mansion's parlor was elegantly furnished for entertaining visitors.
She led her guests into the parlor, where a fire was already burning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing room - a formal room for entertaining guests
  • Synonym: living room - an informal room in a house for general social activities

Etymology

From Old French 'parloir,' from parler (to speak).
