tollgatherer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlˌɡæðərər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlˌɡæðərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect tolls, especially on a road, bridge, or ferry
person who collects tolls

Examples

The tollgatherer sat in his booth, waiting for the next car to approach.
In historical novels, you often encounter a tollgatherer stationed at the city gates.
She worked as a tollgatherer on the old stone bridge during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toll collector - person who collects tolls on roads or bridges

Etymology

From 'toll' (a fee for use of a road or bridge) + 'gatherer' (one who collects).
