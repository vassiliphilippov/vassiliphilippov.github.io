paresis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / pəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
a condition of slight or partial paralysis, resulting in weakness or impaired movement of a part of the body
slight or partial paralysis
medical

Examples

The patient experienced paresis of the left arm following the stroke.
Improvement in paresis was noted after weeks of physical therapy.
Certain infections can cause facial paresis in adults.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: paralysis - loss of the ability to move (and sometimes to feel) part or most of the body
  • Degree Lower: weakness - lack of physical strength or energy in a part of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'paresis' (relaxation, paralysis), from 'pairein' (to loosen, disable).
