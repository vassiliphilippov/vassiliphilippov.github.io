swaybacked

1. ADJ

/ ˈsweɪˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈsweɪˌbækt / (BrE)
having an abnormal downward curvature or sagging of the spine, especially in horses or animals
having a sagging spine (esp. in animals)

Examples

The old mare was swaybacked after years of hard work in the fields.
The veterinarian examined the swaybacked horse to assess its overall health.
A swaybacked cat strolled across the barnyard, her spine curving noticeably.

Etymology

From 'swayback', referring to a sagging back, plus the adjective suffix '-ed'.
