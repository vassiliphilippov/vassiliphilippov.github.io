fractiously

1. ADV

/ ˈfræk.ʃəs.li / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows irritability, quarrelsomeness, or a tendency to be difficult or unruly.
in an irritable or unruly manner

Examples

The child stomped fractiously around the room, refusing every suggestion from his parents.
Debate members interrupted fractiously, making it difficult to reach any agreement.
The puppy barked fractiously when it didn't get its favorite toy.

Synset Examples

the horse was behaving fractiously and refused to jump

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fractious - irritable and difficult to control
  • Antonym: peacefully - in a peaceful manner

2. ADV

/ ˈfræk.ʃəs.li / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃəs.li / (BrE)
In an irritable or quarrelsome manner; behaving in a peevish way.
in an irritable or peevish manner

Examples

The child answered fractiously, unhappy with the restrictions placed upon her.
She fractiously demanded to know why she was being kept waiting so long.
When criticized, he often responds fractiously instead of calmly discussing the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritably - in an irritable manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm and placid manner

Etymology

From 'fractious' + '-ly'; 'fractious' comes from Latin 'frangere' (to break).
