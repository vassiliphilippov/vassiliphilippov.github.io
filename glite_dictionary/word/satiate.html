satiate

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.ʃɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
(formal) Supplied to full or excessive satisfaction; having had enough or more than enough, especially of food or pleasure.
fully satisfied; filled to excess

Examples

After the elaborate banquet, he felt completely satiate and unable to eat another bite.
The guests left the table satiate, having enjoyed every course of the meal.
Her curiosity was not yet satiate by the professor’s explanation and she pressed for more details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sated - having had more than enough; fully satisfied
  • Antonym: hungry - feeling the need or desire for food
  • Synonym: full - having eaten to satisfaction

Etymology

From Latin satiatus, past participle of satiare (to fill, satisfy), from satis (enough).

2. VERB

/ ˈseɪ.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.ʃɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to satisfy (a desire, appetite, or need) fully or to excess
satisfy fully; fill to excess

Examples

A large meal can easily satiate even the hungriest appetite.
Her curiosity was finally satiated after hearing the entire story.
The lavish dessert buffet will satiate anyone with a sweet tooth.

Synset Examples

I am sated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satisfy - to fulfill a need or desire completely
  • Synonym: surfeit - to feed or supply to excess or disgust

Etymology

From Latin satiatus, past participle of 'satiare' (to fill, satisfy), from 'satis' (enough).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈseɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈseɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to eat or drink so much that you are fully or excessively satisfied; to overindulge in food or drink
to overeat or fully satisfy hunger

Examples

After three generous helpings of dinner, he felt completely satiated.
The buffet was so abundant that even the hungriest guest could satiate themselves.
She satiated her sweet tooth by indulging in rich chocolate cake.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfeit - to cause someone to desire no more of something because they have had too much
  • Synonym: to overeat - to eat more food than your body needs
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from extreme hunger

Etymology

From Latin *satiare* (to fill, satisfy), from *satis* (enough).
