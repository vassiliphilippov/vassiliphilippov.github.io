to overdramatise

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈdræməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdræmətaɪz / (BrE)
to present or describe something as more dramatic or serious than it really is
exaggerate the drama or seriousness of something
british

Examples

She tends to overdramatise minor setbacks at work, making them seem like disasters.
The journalist was accused of trying to overdramatise the events to sell more papers.
It's important not to overdramatise the situation before knowing all the facts.

Synset Examples

She is overdramatizing her child's failure in the physics class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as greater, better, or worse than it really is
  • Antonym: to understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + dramatise (from Greek drama, through French and English).
