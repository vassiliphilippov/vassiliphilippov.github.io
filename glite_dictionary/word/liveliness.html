liveliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvlinəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being full of energy, enthusiasm, or excitement; animation in actions or expressions
energy or animation; spiritedness

Examples

The liveliness of the conversation made the evening truly enjoyable.
Her liveliness on stage captivated the entire audience.
A sense of liveliness fills the park during the summer festivals.

Synset Examples

it was a heavy play and the actors tried in vain to give life to it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animation - the quality of being lively or spirited
  • Synonym: energy - vitality and enthusiasm in action or expression
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of energy or excitement; the opposite of liveliness

Etymology

From 'lively' + '-ness', modelled after Old English formation of abstract nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvli.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvli.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being full of energy, enthusiasm, or activity
energy; spirited activity

Examples

The liveliness of the crowd made the festival truly memorable.
Her liveliness on stage captivated everyone in the audience.
Despite the rain, there was a surprising liveliness in the city square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity; vigour
  • Antonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or excitement
  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated

Etymology

From 'lively' + '-ness', with 'lively' from Old English 'līflīc' (living, lifelike).
