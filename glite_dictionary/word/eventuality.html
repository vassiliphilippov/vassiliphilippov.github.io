eventuality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvɛntʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌvɛntʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
a possible event or outcome, especially one that may happen in the future
possible future event or result

Examples

We must prepare for every eventuality, no matter how unlikely it seems.
The insurance policy covers all possible eventualities.
In the eventuality that the meeting is canceled, you will be notified by email.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possibility - something that may happen or be the case
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen or be the case

Etymology

From French éventualité, from Late Latin eventualitas, from Latin eventus (event).
