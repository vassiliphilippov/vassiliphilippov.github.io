Chechnya

1. PROPN

/ tʃɛtʃˈniə / (AmE), / tʃɛtʃˈniːə / (BrE)
An autonomous republic in southwestern Russia in the northern Caucasus Mountains, known for its history of declaring independence from Russia and ongoing conflict.
autonomous republic in southwestern Russia, Caucasus region

Examples

Conflict has plagued Chechnya for decades, drawing international attention to the region.
The mountainous terrain of Chechnya has shaped its culture and history.
Many civilians were displaced by the wars in Chechnya in the late 20th century.

Etymology

From Russian 'Чечня' (Chechnya), via Chechen 'Noxçiyn' (Chechen land).
