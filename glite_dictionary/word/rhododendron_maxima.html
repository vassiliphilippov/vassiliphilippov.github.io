Rhododendron maxima

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈdɛndrən ˈmæksɪmə / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈdɛndrən ˈmæksɪmə / (BrE)
A species of rhododendron native to eastern North America, blooming in late spring with rosy to pink-purple flowers.
a rhododendron species with pink-purple blooms

Examples

The Rhododendron maxima in our garden burst into vibrant pink bloom every May.
Botanists have documented the widespread presence of Rhododendron maxima in the Appalachian region.
The showy flowers of Rhododendron maxima attract many visitors to local parks during late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhododendron - any shrub or small tree of the genus Rhododendron

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Rhododendron (from Greek 'rhodon' rose + 'dendron' tree) and Latin 'maxima' meaning 'greatest'.
