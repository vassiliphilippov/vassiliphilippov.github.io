wittiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪt̬i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing quick and inventive verbal humor; cleverness combined with humor
clever and humorous quality

Examples

Her wittiness always brightens up our meetings and keeps everyone laughing.
The comedian’s wittiness captivated the entire audience last night.
His speech was admired for its wittiness and originality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to use words in a clever and humorous way

Etymology

From 'witty' + '-ness'; 'witty' from Old English 'witig', related to 'wit'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever, amusing, and quick with words
quality of being clever and funny

Examples

Her wittiness always lightens up the mood during meetings.
The novel was praised for the wittiness of its dialogue.
He admired her wittiness, which made every conversation more interesting.

Synset Examples

And the result would be that he would kill the wittiness of the lines by burlesque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to use words in a clever and humorous way
  • Synonym: humor - the quality of being amusing or entertaining
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of interest or excitement, especially lacking cleverness or humor

Etymology

From English 'witty' + '-ness' (forming the noun of a quality).
