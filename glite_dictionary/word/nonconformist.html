Nonconformist

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnkənˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
(in England) A member of a Protestant church that does not belong to the Church of England.
Protestant not part of Church of England
british

Examples

His grandfather was a strict Nonconformist who refused to attend Church of England services.
Many English Nonconformists suffered persecution in the seventeenth century.
The village chapel was built by Nonconformists seeking a place to worship freely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissenter - A person who does not belong to the established church, especially in England
  • Antonym: Anglican - A member of the Church of England

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'conformist' (one who conforms), originally referring to those who refused to conform to the Church of England after the Act of Uniformity (1662).
