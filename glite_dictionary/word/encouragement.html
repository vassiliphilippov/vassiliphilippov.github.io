encouragement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɜrɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
The feeling of being given support, confidence, or hope.
feeling of being supported or given hope

Examples

Her kind words gave me a real sense of encouragement before the exam.
He drew encouragement from his friends' support during a difficult time.
The team's early success was a great source of encouragement to the new coach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discouragement - the feeling of losing confidence or hope

Etymology

From Old French ‘encouragement’, from ‘encourager’ (to make strong, hearten).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɝːɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving someone support, confidence, or hope
expression of support or approval

Examples

Her words of encouragement helped me get through a difficult time.
The team received a lot of encouragement from their coach before the match.
A little encouragement can make a big difference in someone's life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of making someone less confident or hopeful
  • Synonym: support - help or approval that someone gives to others

Etymology

From Old French encouragier (to make strong) + -ment (noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɝː.ɪdʒ.mənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌr.ɪdʒ.mənt / (BrE)
the act of giving support, confidence, or hope to someone
support or hope given to someone

Examples

Her words of encouragement helped me get through a difficult time.
Teachers offer encouragement to their students to help them succeed.
A little encouragement can make a big difference in someone's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or backing given to someone
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of making someone less confident or enthusiastic

Etymology

From Old French 'encoragier', from 'en-' (make, put in) + 'corage' (courage).
