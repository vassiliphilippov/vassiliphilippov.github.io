oriental

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːr.iˈɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɔː.riˈen.təl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the countries and cultures of East Asia and the Far East, especially in terms of art, design, or traditions (now often considered dated or potentially offensive when referring to people)
of or relating to East Asian countries or culture

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful oriental rug in the main hall.
He has always been fascinated by oriental art and ceramics.
They sampled several oriental dishes at the new restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occidental - relating to the Western world or Europe

Etymology

From Latin orientalis, from oriens (east, rising sun).

2. ADJ

/ ɔːriˈɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈɛnt(ə)l / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the countries of the Far East, especially East Asia.
of or relating to the Far East

Examples

The museum features an impressive collection of oriental art and ceramics.
They decorated the room with oriental rugs and lanterns to create an exotic ambiance.
His studies focused on oriental history and languages.

Synset Examples

oriental civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern - relating to the east or to the eastern part of a region

Etymology

From Latin orientalis (eastern), from orient- (rising, from oriens, east, sunrise).
