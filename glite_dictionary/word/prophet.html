prophet

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪt / (BrE)
a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God; someone who speaks by divine inspiration
inspired speaker or teacher of God's will

Examples

The ancient Israelites believed Moses was a prophet who led them out of Egypt.
Many religions regard their founders as great prophets who received divine messages.
The prophet spoke to the crowd, urging them to live with compassion and justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seer - a person who claims to see or predict future events, often with supernatural insight
  • Synonym: oracle - a person through whom a deity is believed to speak
  • Antonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox religious doctrine

Etymology

From Greek prophētēs (spokesman, predictor), through Latin propheta.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the future, often considered divinely inspired or authoritative
person who predicts or proclaims the future

Examples

Many people considered her a prophet after her warnings proved true.
The prophet predicted years of peace and prosperity for the kingdom.
Throughout history, prophets have played crucial roles in guiding communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - a person through whom advice or prophecy is sought
  • Synonym: seer - a person who is supposed to be able to see what the future holds
  • Synonym: fortune-teller - a person who predicts future events

Etymology

From Greek prophētēs (spokesman, interpreter), from pro- (before) and phēnai (to speak).
