to swob

1. VERB

/ swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
to clean or wash by using a swab or mop
clean with a swab or mop
archaic

Examples

The deckhands swobbed the old wooden planks until they shone in the morning sun.
Every day, the sailors are expected to swob the decks as part of their duties.
He swobbed the spill from the floor before anyone noticed the mess.

Synset Examples

swab the ship's decks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swab - to clean or wipe with a swab or mop

Etymology

Variant of 'swab', likely from Dutch or Low German 'zwabber', meaning mop or swab.
