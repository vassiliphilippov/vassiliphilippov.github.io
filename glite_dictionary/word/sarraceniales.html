Sarraceniales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsærəˌsiːniˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌsærəˌsiːniˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of insectivorous plants that includes the pitcher plants and sundews, noted for their insect-trapping modifications.
order of insectivorous plants

Examples

The order Sarraceniales includes some of the most fascinating carnivorous plants.
Sarraceniales are known for their leaves, which have evolved into complex insect traps.
Botanists study Sarraceniales to understand the evolution of carnivorous adaptations in plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sarraceniaceae - family of North American pitcher plants
  • Synonym: Droseraceae - family of sundews and related insectivorous plants
  • Synonym: Nepenthaceae - family of tropical pitcher plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Sarracenia plus the order suffix -ales.
