Essene

1. ADJ

/ əˈsin / (AmE), / ɛˈsiːn / (BrE)
of or relating to the Essenes, a member of an ancient Jewish ascetic sect active from the 2nd century BCE to the 1st century CE.
relating to the Essenes (Jewish sect)

Examples

Many ancient texts offer an Essene interpretation of Jewish law.
The Essene community at Qumran is often associated with the Dead Sea Scrolls.
Some scholars believe Jesus was influenced by Essene teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Essenes - members of an ancient Jewish sect known for asceticism

Etymology

From Greek 'Essēnoi' or 'Essaioi', the name of the sect, as found in ancient sources.

2. NOUN

/ ɛˈsin / (AmE), / ɛˈsiːn / (BrE)
a member of an ascetic Jewish sect that existed around the time of Jesus, known for strict religious practices and communal living
member of an ancient Jewish sect

Examples

Some scholars believe the Dead Sea Scrolls were written by the Essene community at Qumran.
The Essene lifestyle emphasized purity, prayer, and communal living.
During ancient times, the Essene sect separated itself from mainstream Jewish society.

Etymology

From Greek Essēnoi (Ἐσσηνοί), possibly from Aramaic or Hebrew root.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09675059-n
Synset POS: n
