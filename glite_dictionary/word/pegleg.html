pegleg

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛɡˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
a wooden leg or a simple artificial limb, especially one replacing a missing leg; traditionally made from a single piece of wood
wooden artificial leg

Examples

The old sailor walked with a distinctive pegleg after losing his limb at sea.
Pirates in stories are often depicted with a pegleg and an eye patch.
He fashioned his own pegleg from sturdy oak after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthesis - an artificial body part, such as a limb

Etymology

Formed from 'peg' (a rod or pin) and 'leg', referring to the shape and construction of early wooden prosthetic legs.
