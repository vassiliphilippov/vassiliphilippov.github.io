sinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of something going down below the surface of liquid, especially water
descent through liquid, especially water

Examples

The news reported the sinking of a cargo ship during the storm.
The sinking of Atlantis is a legend that has fascinated people for centuries.
After the sinking of the fishing boat, the crew was rescued by a nearby vessel.

Synset Examples

they still talk about the sinking of the Titanic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submersion - the act of being submerged or put under liquid
  • Antonym: floating - the act of remaining on the surface of a liquid

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
a feeling of sudden anxiety or dread, especially in anticipation of something unpleasant
feeling of sudden anxiety or dread

Examples

She felt a sinking in her stomach as she opened the letter.
A sinking feeling washed over him when he realized he had forgotten the meeting.
With a sinking heart, he watched the train pull away without him.

Synset Examples

with a sinking heart
a sinking feeling in the pit of my stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprehension - fear or anxiety about the future
  • Antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual decline, fall, or loss of strength, position, or value
gradual decline or fall

Examples

The sinking of the company's profits worried the shareholders.
He felt a sinking in his energy as the marathon progressed.
There was a steady sinking of morale during the long crisis.

Synset Examples

after several hours of sinking, an unexpected rally rescued the market
he could not control the sinking of his legs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falling - the act of moving downward
  • Antonym: rising - a process of increasing or going upward

Etymology

From the verb 'sink,' Old English 'sincan' (to become submerged).
