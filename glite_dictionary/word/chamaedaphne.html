Chamaedaphne

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪmiːˈdæfniː / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈdæfniː / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, containing a single species known as leatherleaf, native to northern boggy regions.
genus of evergreen shrubs (leatherleaf)

Examples

Chamaedaphne is commonly found in acidic bogs throughout the northern hemisphere.
The genus Chamaedaphne includes just one species, known as leatherleaf.
Botanists identified the thick, leathery leaves as belonging to Chamaedaphne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherleaf - A low-growing evergreen shrub of bogs (Chamaedaphne calyculata)

Etymology

From Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) and 'daphne' (laurel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪmiˈdæfni / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈdafni / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, consisting of a single species, the leatherleaf.
genus of evergreen shrub (leatherleaf)

Examples

The Chamaedaphne genus is represented solely by the leatherleaf shrub.
Botanists studied the adaptations of Chamaedaphne to nutrient-poor, acidic soils.
Chamaedaphne grows abundantly in northern bogs and wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherleaf - common plant in boggy areas, sole member of Chamaedaphne

Etymology

From Greek khamaidaphne, meaning 'ground laurel'.
