prescient

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛʃiənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛsiənt / (BrE)
Having or showing knowledge of events before they take place.
able to foresee events

Examples

Her prescient warning about the risks proved true just months later.
The scientist's prescient insights led to groundbreaking discoveries.
Critics now see his early essays as remarkably prescient in their predictions.

Synset Examples

extraordinarily prescient memoranda on the probable course of postwar relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - accurately predicting or describing what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin praescient-, stem of praescire (to know beforehand), from prae- (before) + scire (to know).
