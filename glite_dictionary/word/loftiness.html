loftiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔftinəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒftɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being high in position, rank, or character; the state of being lofty
quality of being lofty or elevated

Examples

The loftiness of the mountain peaks took our breath away.
She admired the loftiness of his ideals and aspirations.
His speech was marked by a certain loftiness that inspired the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: height - the quality or state of being high or tall
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance

Etymology

From 'lofty' + '-ness'; 'lofty' from Late Middle English 'lofte' (air, sky).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔf.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒf.ti.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being impressive in scale, height, or proportion
impressiveness in height or scale

Examples

The loftiness of the cathedral's vaulted ceilings left visitors in awe.
Architects admired the loftiness of the new skyscraper's design.
There was a certain loftiness to the mountain range that inspired many artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - magnificent and impressive quality or appearance
  • Antonym: humbleness - the quality of being modest or unassuming

Etymology

From 'lofty' + '-ness', with 'lofty' from Old Norse 'lopt' (air, sky) via Middle English.
