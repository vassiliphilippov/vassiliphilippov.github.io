empowerment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpaʊ.ɚ.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊə.mənt / (BrE)
the act of giving someone official authority or legal power to do something
act of granting legal authority

Examples

The new legislation provided the necessary empowerment for the agency to enforce regulations.
Her empowerment as executor of the will was confirmed by the court.
The council issued a formal empowerment for the police to act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - the act of giving official permission or approval
  • Antonym: disempowerment - the act of removing power or authority

Etymology

From verb 'empower' + suffix '-ment', ultimately from Old French 'em-', Latin 'in-' + 'power'.
