Hyla crucifer

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪlə kruˈsɪfər / (AmE), / ˌhɪlə kruˈsɪfə / (BrE)
A small brown tree frog of eastern North America, known for its high-pitched call heard near wetlands in early spring.
small North American tree frog

Examples

The distinctive call of the Hyla crucifer signals the arrival of spring in wetland areas.
Biologists often track populations of Hyla crucifer to monitor wetland health.
At dusk, the chorus of Hyla crucifer can be heard throughout the marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring peeper - common name for Hyla crucifer, a small North American tree frog

Etymology

From Greek 'hylē' (wood, forest) and Latin 'crucifer' (cross-bearer), referring to the X-shaped mark on its back.
