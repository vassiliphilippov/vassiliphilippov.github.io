intentionality

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛnʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being deliberate or having intentions, especially the capacity of the mind to direct itself toward objects, actions, or goals
the capacity to form intentions

Examples

Many philosophers debate the nature of intentionality as it relates to consciousness.
The artist's intentionality is evident in every brushstroke of the painting.
His actions lacked intentionality, seeming more spontaneous than planned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intention - a thing intended; an aim or plan
  • Synonym: purposefulness - the quality of having a definite purpose
  • Antonym: accidentality - the quality of happening by chance or unintentionally

Etymology

From Medieval Latin intentionalitas, based on Latin intentio (intention, purpose).
