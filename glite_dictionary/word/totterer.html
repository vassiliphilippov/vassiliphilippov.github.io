totterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːtərər / (AmE), / ˈtɒtərə / (BrE)
a person who walks unsteadily as if about to fall
someone who walks unsteadily

Examples

The old totterer made his way slowly down the sidewalk, leaning on his cane.
After the illness, she became a totterer and needed support to move safely.
The nurses watched the totterer carefully, ready to assist if he lost his balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to totter - to walk unsteadily as if about to fall

Etymology

From the verb 'totter' + -er, denoting someone who totters.
