Mephitis

1. NOUN

/ məˈfaɪtɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈfɪtɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the subfamily Mephitinae, comprising skunks and related animals known for their ability to emit a strong-smelling defensive spray.
genus of skunks

Examples

The genus Mephitis includes several species of skunks native to North and Central America.
Scientists studied the defense mechanisms of Mephitis during the field expedition.
Members of the genus Mephitis are easily recognized by their distinctive black and white coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small mammal known for its unpleasant-smelling spray

Etymology

From Latin *mephītis* (noxious vapor or foul smell).

2. NOUN

/ məˈfaɪtɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈfɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of skunks, including North American species, and the type genus of the subfamily Mephitinae.
genus of skunks

Examples

The striped skunk belongs to the genus Mephitis, which is known for its pungent defense spray.
Researchers studied several members of the Mephitis genus in their natural habitats.
Most skunks native to North America are classified within Mephitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small mammal known for its strong, unpleasant odor

Etymology

From Latin *mephitis* (noxious vapor), referring to the skunk's strong odor.
