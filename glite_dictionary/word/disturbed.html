disturbed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
feeling or showing anxiety, uneasiness, or emotional trouble; afflicted by worry or distress
afflicted by anxiety or emotional trouble

Examples

She looked disturbed after hearing the bad news.
His disturbed mind kept him awake for hours every night.
The disturbed expression on her face worried her friends.

Synset Examples

too upset to say anything
spent many disquieted moments
distressed about her son's leaving home
lapsed into disturbed sleep
worried parents
a worried frown
one last worried check of the sleeping children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upset - feeling unhappy or worried
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling anxious or troubled

Etymology

From Latin 'disturbare' (to throw into disorder, to upset).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
suffering from mental or emotional problems, especially severe ones involving lost touch with reality or sanity.
mentally or emotionally troubled

Examples

His family became concerned when he began acting disturbed and withdrawn.
The patient was so disturbed that he required constant supervision in the hospital.
Many characters in classic literature have been portrayed as deeply disturbed individuals.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - seriously mentally ill
  • Synonym: mad - suffering from serious mental disorder
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02082451-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
experiencing emotional instability and difficulty in coping with personal relationships or daily life; mentally troubled
emotionally unstable; mentally troubled

Examples

The counselor worked extensively with several disturbed adolescents who struggled to form healthy relationships.
After the traumatic event, he became increasingly disturbed and withdrawn from his friends and family.
The film offers an insightful portrayal of a disturbed character trying to reconnect with society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - prone to emotional or mental disturbance
  • Antonym: balanced - emotionally stable and well-adjusted

Etymology

From the verb 'disturb', from Latin 'disturbare' (throw into disorder), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'turba' (disorder).

4. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
(of an object or place) altered in position, order, or arrangement; having been moved from its original state.
having been moved or altered in position

Examples

The disturbed sand revealed where the animal had buried something.
After the meeting, the disturbed files still littered the conference table.
You could tell by the disturbed tracks that someone had recently walked through the snow.

Synset Examples

the disturbed books and papers on her desk
disturbed grass showed where the horse had passed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undisturbed - not altered or moved, left in its original place or state

Etymology

From the verb 'disturb', from Latin 'disturbare' (to throw into disorder).
