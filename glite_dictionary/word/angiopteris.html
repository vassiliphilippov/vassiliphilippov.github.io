angiopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / ˌandʒiˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of very large primitive ferns native to tropical regions of the Pacific with high rainfall.
genus of large tropical ferns

Examples

The lush valley was dominated by towering angiopteris ferns, creating a prehistoric atmosphere.
Botanists discovered a rare species of angiopteris thriving in the humid, shaded understory of the forest.
The museum's exhibit features a fossil of angiopteris, illustrating its ancient origins.

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + pteris (fern), referring to the sori (spore vessels) of the plant.
