Angiopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / ˌandʒɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical ferns with massive fronds, including large tree ferns found mainly in the Old World tropics.
genus of large tropical ferns

Examples

The Angiopteris genus is notable for its massive, spreading fronds.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of Angiopteris in tropical rainforests.
Some species in the Angiopteris genus resemble miniature palm trees due to their size.

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + pteris (fern).
