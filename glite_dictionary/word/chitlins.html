chitlins

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtlɪnz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtlɪnz / (BrE)
The small intestines of hogs cooked and eaten as food, especially in Southern U.S. cuisine.
hog intestines cooked as food

Examples

My grandmother makes the best chitlins every Thanksgiving.
The smell of chitlins cooking filled the house.
Many people serve chitlins with vinegar and hot sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chitterlings - the small intestines of a pig cooked as food

Etymology

Alteration of 'chitterlings,' from Middle English 'cheterling.'
