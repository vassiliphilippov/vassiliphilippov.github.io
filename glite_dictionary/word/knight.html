knight

1. NOUN

/ naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
(historical) A man of noble birth who was trained to arms and chivalry and served as a mounted soldier in the Middle Ages; (in modern use) a man who has been honored by the sovereign in the United Kingdom for personal merit and given the title 'Sir'.
medieval mounted nobleman or modern UK honoree

Examples

In medieval times, a knight was expected to uphold the code of chivalry.
He was made a knight by the Queen for his services to charity.
The museum displayed armor once worn by a fifteenth-century knight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dame - female equivalent of a modern knight in the British honors system
  • Synonym: sir - title used for knights in the UK

Etymology

From Old English 'cniht' (boy, servant), later meaning a military follower and mounted nobleman.

2. VERB

/ naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
to make someone a knight, especially in a formal ceremony by a monarch or other important figure
to confer knighthood upon

Examples

Sir Paul McCartney was knighted for his contributions to music.
It was a great honor when she watched her father knighted by the Queen.
Each year, a few individuals are knighted in a ceremony at Buckingham Palace.

Synset Examples

The Beatles were knighted

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dame - to make a woman a dame as an honorific title, especially in the British honours system

Etymology

From Old English 'cniht' (boy, servant), later specialized in Middle English as the title for a mounted warrior, from Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
A chess piece shaped like a horse's head that moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular.
chess piece shaped like a horse's head

Examples

The knight captured the opponent's bishop with a clever move.
You need to move your knight to protect your king.
Many beginners forget how the knight moves in chess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishop - A chess piece that moves diagonally
  • Synonym: rook - A chess piece that moves in straight lines vertically or horizontally
  • Synonym: pawn - A chess piece that moves forward one square, with special rules

Etymology

From Old English cniht (boy, servant), later 'knight' used for the chess piece based on its movement and status.
