clitoridectomy

1. NOUN

/ klɪˌtɔrəˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / klɪˌtɒrɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which all or part of the clitoris is removed, typically for cultural or medical reasons
surgical removal of the clitoris
medical

Examples

The practice of clitoridectomy has been widely condemned by international health organizations.
Medical professionals no longer recommend clitoridectomy as a treatment for any condition.
She spoke about the physical and psychological impact of undergoing a clitoridectomy in her youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: female genital mutilation - the partial or total removal of external female genitalia for non-medical reasons

Etymology

From clitoris + -ectomy (Greek ektemnein, to cut out).
