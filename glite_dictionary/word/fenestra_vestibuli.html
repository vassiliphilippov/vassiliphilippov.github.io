fenestra vestibuli

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛstrə vɛˈstɪbjʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə vɛˈstɪbjʊˌliː / (BrE)
the oval opening in the inner wall of the middle ear to which the base of the stapes is attached; also called the oval window.
oval opening in the middle ear (oval window)
medical

Examples

The fenestra vestibuli plays a crucial role in transmitting sound vibrations to the inner ear.
Damage to the fenestra vestibuli can lead to hearing loss or balance problems.
The base of the stapes footplate fits snugly into the fenestra vestibuli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval window - another name for the fenestra vestibuli in the ear

Etymology

From Latin: fenestra (window) + vestibuli (of the vestibule).
