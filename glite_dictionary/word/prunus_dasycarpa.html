Prunus dasycarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌdæsɪˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌdæsɪˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A small hybrid apricot tree native to Asia and Asia Minor, noted for its purplish twigs, white flowers, and inferior purple fruit.
hybrid apricot tree of Asia with purple fruit

Examples

The Prunus dasycarpa is often grown in botanical gardens for its unusual purple twigs and white blossoms.
Despite its attractive appearance, the fruit of Prunus dasycarpa is considered inferior compared to other apricots.
Horticulturists study Prunus dasycarpa to understand hybridization between apricot species.

Etymology

From Latin 'prunus' (plum tree genus) and Greek 'dasy-' (hairy) + 'carpos' (fruit), referring to the plant's features.
