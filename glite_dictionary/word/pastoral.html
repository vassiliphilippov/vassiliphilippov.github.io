pastoral

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæs.tɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.stər.əl / (BrE)
relating to the duties or office of a pastor, especially in giving spiritual guidance to a congregation.
relating to a pastor or spiritual guidance

Examples

The new priest quickly became engaged in pastoral work within the community.
She wrote a pastoral letter addressing her congregation’s concerns.
His pastoral responsibilities include visiting the sick and offering spiritual counsel.

Synset Examples

pastoral work
a pastoral letter

Etymology

From Latin pastoralis, from pastor (shepherd, pastor).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæstərəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstərəl / (BrE)
A literary work that idealizes rural life, especially that of shepherds, depicting it as peaceful and untroubled.
literary work idealizing rural life

Examples

The poet wrote a pastoral celebrating the simple joys of country life.
Many Renaissance writers were inspired to compose pastorals set among shepherds and green meadows.
Her novel reads almost like a modern pastoral, filled with romanticized descriptions of the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idyll - a short poem describing a peaceful, idealized rural scene

Etymology

From Latin 'pastoralis' (relating to a shepherd), from 'pastor' (shepherd).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
