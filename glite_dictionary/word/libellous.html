libellous

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪbələs / (AmE), / ˈlɪbələs / (BrE)
(of a statement) containing or constituting a false published statement that damages a person's reputation.
containing defamatory falsehoods

Examples

The article was so libellous that the newspaper was sued for defamation.
She denied making any libellous statements about her competitor.
To publish libellous material can have serious legal consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanderous - (of a statement) false and damaging to reputation, usually spoken
  • Synonym: defamatory - containing false statements that harm someone's reputation
  • Antonym: praiseworthy - deserving approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'libellus' (small book), diminutive of 'liber' (book); reflected in 'libel' (defamatory writing).
