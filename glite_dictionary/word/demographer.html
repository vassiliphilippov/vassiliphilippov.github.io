demographer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the structure, growth, and distribution of human populations and their vital statistics
population scientist

Examples

The demographer presented new data on global urbanization trends at the conference.
A skilled demographer can help governments plan for future changes in population size.
Several demographers have warned about the implications of an aging population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demography - the scientific study of human populations

Etymology

From Greek dēmos (people) and -grapher (one who writes or studies).
