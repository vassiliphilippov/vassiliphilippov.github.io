splashy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsplæʃi / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃi / (BrE)
Attracting attention by being bright, flashy, or showy, often in a way that lacks good taste.
showy and attention-grabbing, often tasteless

Examples

The magazine cover featured a splashy photo of the latest celebrity couple.
They threw a splashy party to impress their neighbors, complete with fireworks and a live band.
His splashy tie was the first thing everyone noticed when he walked into the room.

Synset Examples

a cheap showy rhinestone bracelet
a splashy half-page ad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - drawing attention by being visually striking
  • Antonym: understated - presented in a subtle and restrained manner

2. ADJ

/ ˈsplæʃi / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃi / (BrE)
characterized by splashes of liquid, especially water, flying about in a disorderly or vigorous way
marked by splashing liquid

Examples

The children had a splashy good time playing in the fountain.
She wore a raincoat to protect herself from the splashy waves at the shore.
After a splashy water balloon fight, the backyard was soaked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watery - full of or resembling water

Etymology

Formed from 'splash' + '-y', a common English adjective suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02811860-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈsplæʃi / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃi / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) covered with patches of bright color, often in a striking or irregular manner.
having bright, irregularly colored patches

Examples

The artist created a splashy mural with bold, irregular swaths of color.
She chose a splashy dress decorated in bright, contrasting patches.
Their new living room features splashy cushions that brighten the entire space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorful - having much or varied color; vividly bright or striking

Etymology

From 'splash', imitating the action or effect of splashed liquid, plus '-y' forming an adjective.

Source: WordNet
