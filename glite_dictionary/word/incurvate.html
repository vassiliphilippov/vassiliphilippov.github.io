incurvate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɜrˌveɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːveɪt / (BrE)
to bend or curve inward
bend or curve inward

Examples

The petals slowly incurvate as the flower closes at night.
An expert can incurvate the metal sheet without breaking it.
With time, the branches of the tree incurvate under the weight of the snow.

Synset Examples

gravity incurvates the rays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - to form or cause to form a curve
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Latin incurvatus, past participle of incurvare ‘to bend in’.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɝːveɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːveɪt / (BrE)

to bend or curve inward

Examples

The delicate petals incurvate slightly toward the center of the flower.
As the metal cools, it may incurvate due to uneven contraction.
The old wooden beam had started to incurvate under the weight of the roof.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - to bend or cause to bend

Etymology

From Latin incurvatus, past participle of incurvare (‘to bend inward’), from in- ‘in, into’ + curvare ‘to bend’.
