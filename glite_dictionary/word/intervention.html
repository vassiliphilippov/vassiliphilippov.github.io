intervention

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
A policy or practice of becoming involved in the affairs of other countries, often to influence events or outcomes.
involvement in foreign affairs or countries

Examples

The government announced a policy of military intervention in the region.
Many experts argue that foreign intervention can make local conflicts worse.
Economic intervention by powerful nations often affects developing countries' sovereignty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - unwanted or inappropriate involvement in others' affairs
  • Antonym: nonintervention - the policy of not becoming involved in the affairs of other countries

Etymology

From Latin 'interventio' (a coming between).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of putting something in between or among other things; interposing.
the act of inserting or interposing

Examples

The sudden intervention of clouds blocked the sunlight from reaching the valley.
Her intervention in the conversation shifted the topic to safer ground.
The intervention of new evidence changed the outcome of the deliberations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interposition - the act of placing or inserting between

Etymology

From Latin intervenire (to come between), from inter- (between) + venire (to come).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈvɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈvɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of becoming involved in a situation in order to change what is happening, especially to help settle a dispute or solve a problem.
the act of becoming involved to alter a situation

Examples

Government intervention was needed to resolve the economic crisis.
The negotiations proceeded without direct intervention from either party.
Parental intervention in school matters is sometimes necessary for a child's well-being.

Synset Examples

it occurs without human intervention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediation - the process of intervening to resolve a dispute
  • Antonym: nonintervention - the practice of not becoming involved in a situation

Etymology

From Latin 'interventio(n-)' from 'intervenire' meaning 'come between'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
(Law) A legal proceeding that allows a person who is not an original party to a lawsuit to become involved in order to protect a right or interest affected by the case.
legal procedure of joining a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The judge granted the company's intervention in the ongoing litigation.
Environmental groups filed for intervention to protect their interests in the court case.
His lawyer explained the procedure for requesting intervention in the suit.

Synset Examples

the purpose of intervention is to prevent unnecessary duplication of lawsuits

Etymology

From Latin 'interventio' (a coming between), from 'intervenire' (to intervene).

5. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈvɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈvɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of intervening, especially treatment or actions taken to improve a situation or relieve illness or injury
action taken to help a situation, especially medically

Examples

Early intervention can significantly improve outcomes for children with developmental delays.
The committee discussed possible interventions to address the rising number of infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatment - medical care given to a patient for an illness or injury
  • Synonym: procedure - a medical operation or process

Etymology

From Latin 'interventio', from 'intervenire' meaning 'to come between'.
