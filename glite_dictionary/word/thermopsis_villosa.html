Thermopsis villosa

1. NOUN

/ θɜːrˈmɑp.sɪs vɪˈloʊ.sə / (AmE), / θɜːˈmɒp.sɪs vɪˈləʊ.sə / (BrE)
A species of bush pea native to the eastern United States, known for its yellow flowers and used as an ornamental plant.
species of bush pea native to eastern US

Examples

The garden featured several Thermopsis villosa plants blooming with bright yellow flowers in the spring.
Native to the Appalachian region, Thermopsis villosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
Botanists often remark on the distinctive appearance of Thermopsis villosa among other native pea plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' meaning 'heat' and Latin 'villosa' meaning 'hairy', referring to the plant's warm habitat and hairy leaves.
