dipterous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪptərəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪptərəs / (BrE)
of, relating to, or belonging to the order Diptera (an order of insects that includes flies, mosquitoes, and gnats)
of the insect order Diptera

Examples

The scientist collected several dipterous insects from the wetland area.
Mosquitoes, which are dipterous, can transmit diseases to humans and animals.
In the lab, students analyzed dipterous specimens under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Diptera - an order of insects encompassing flies, mosquitoes, and gnats

Etymology

From Greek 'dipteros' (having two wings), from 'di-' (two) + 'pteron' (wing).
