cortical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving a cortex, especially the outer layer of the brain or other organs
relating to a cortex (outer layer of an organ)
medical

Examples

The cortical regions of the brain are responsible for higher cognitive functions.
Damage to the cortical tissue can affect movement and sensation.
The doctor explained the results showed cortical thinning in the elderly patient's MRI scan.

Etymology

From Latin 'cortex' (bark, rind, shell), with the suffix '-al' forming the adjective.
