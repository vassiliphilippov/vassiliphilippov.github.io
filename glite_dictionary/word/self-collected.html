self-collected

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf.kəˈlɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kəˈlɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
calm and composed; fully in control of one's emotions and faculties, especially in difficult situations
calm and fully in control

Examples

Despite the intense scrutiny, she remained self-collected during the interview.
His self-collected manner reassured everyone in the room after the accident.
A leader must be self-collected even when facing unexpected crises.

Synset Examples

the witness remained collected throughout the cross-examination
perfectly poised and sure of himself
more self-contained and more dependable than many of the early frontiersmen
strong and self-possessed in the face of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composed - calm and in control of oneself
  • Synonym: poised - having a composed and self-assured manner
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'collected', meaning collected or composed in oneself.
