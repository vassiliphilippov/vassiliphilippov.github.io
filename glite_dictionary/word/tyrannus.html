Tyrannus

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈræn.əs / (AmE), / tɪˈran.əs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Tyrannidae, known as the tyrant flycatchers, found chiefly in the Americas.
genus of tyrant flycatcher birds

Examples

The Tyrannus genus includes the well-known eastern kingbird and the scissor-tailed flycatcher.
Many species in Tyrannus are recognized for their bold defense of their nests against larger birds.
Researchers observed a flock of Tyrannus feeding in the open grasslands during migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tyrannidae - family of birds known as tyrant flycatchers

Etymology

From Latin 'tyrannus', meaning 'tyrant', referring to the birds' aggressive behavior.

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈrænəs / (AmE), / tɪˈranəs / (BrE)
A genus of American passerine birds known as tyrant flycatchers, which are large, aggressive insect-eating birds.
a genus of tyrant flycatchers (birds)

Examples

The Tyrannus genus includes several well-known kingbirds found throughout the Americas.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Tyrannus species in southern Texas.
Members of Tyrannus are recognized for their bold defense of territory against larger birds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tyrannidae - the family of tyrant flycatchers

Etymology

From Latin 'tyrannus' meaning 'tyrant', referring to their aggressive behavior.
