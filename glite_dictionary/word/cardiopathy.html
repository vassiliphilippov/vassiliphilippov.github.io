cardiopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈpæθi / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
any disease of the heart.
disease of the heart
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient suffered from a rare form of cardiopathy.
Early detection of cardiopathy can greatly improve a person's quality of life.
Researchers are investigating new treatments for congenital cardiopathy in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart disease - any disorder affecting the heart
  • Degree Lower: carditis - inflammation of the heart

Etymology

From Greek kardio- (heart) + -pathy (disease, suffering).
