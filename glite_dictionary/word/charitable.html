charitable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃær.ɪ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
showing kindness, sympathy, and generosity toward others, especially in judging or helping them
showing kindness and generosity

Examples

She was always charitable in her judgment of other people's mistakes.
He offered a charitable interpretation of her late arrival.
It is important to be charitable when listening to alternative opinions.

Synset Examples

was charitable in his opinions of others
kindly criticism
a kindly act
sympathetic words
a large-hearted mentor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Synonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature
  • Antonym: harsh - cruel or severe in attitude or judgment

Etymology

From Old French *charitable*, from Latin *caritabilis* (dear, costly, esteemed)

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛ.rə.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
relating to the giving of help, especially money, to those in need; generous in giving to charity.
generous; relating to giving to charity

Examples

The company made a charitable donation to support local schools.
Her charitable nature inspired others to volunteer at the shelter.
Many charitable organizations rely on public support to carry out their work.

Synset Examples

a charitable foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philanthropic - showing a desire to promote the welfare of others, especially by generous donation
  • Antonym: selfish - lacking consideration for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure

Etymology

From Old French 'charitable', from Latin 'caritabilis', from 'caritas' (dearness, charity).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtʃær.ɪ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
Showing kindness, generosity, and willingness to help others, especially by giving money or aid to those in need.
generous; giving to those in need

Examples

She made a charitable donation to help families affected by the disaster.
The foundation supports many charitable causes throughout the year.
Despite his wealth, he is known for being charitable to the less fortunate.

Synset Examples

charitable to the poor
a charitable trust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give or share unselfishly
  • Antonym: selfish - lacking consideration for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure

Etymology

From Old French 'charitable', from Latin 'caritabilis' (dear, costly, charitable), from 'caritas' (dearness, love).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360539-a
Synset POS: a
