miscreation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that is abnormal, anomalous, or mistakenly created; a monstrosity or malformed object.
something abnormal or anomalous

Examples

Some critics described the controversial sculpture as a miscreation unworthy of public display.
His unchecked ambition led to the miscreation of a plan that quickly fell apart.
In the darkness, they stumbled upon a miscreation of nature unlike anything they'd ever seen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monstrosity - something that is very abnormal and grotesque
  • Antonym: creation - something that has been created, often implying normalcy

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'creation', ultimately from Latin 'creare' (to create).
