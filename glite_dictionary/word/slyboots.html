slyboots

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪˌbuːts / (AmE), / ˈslaɪˌbuːts / (BrE)
A person who is cunning or sly, often using deception or trickery in a playful or secretive way.
cunning or sly person

Examples

Everyone knows Jeremy is a bit of a slyboots—always finding ways to get what he wants.
She smiled knowingly, suspecting the slyboots had rearranged the files to hide his mistake.
You have to watch out for slyboots like her in these negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickster - a person who deceives or cheats people
  • Antonym: innocent - a person free from guilt or cunning

Etymology

From 'sly' (cunning) + 'boots' (archaic term for person added for emphasis, as in 'lazyboots').
