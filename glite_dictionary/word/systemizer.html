systemizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəməˌzaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəməˌzaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or organizes things systematically or puts things in order
person who organizes things systematically

Examples

As a systemizer, she enjoyed creating detailed plans and frameworks for the new project.
Many scientists are natural systemizers, thriving on patterns and organization.
His reputation as a systemizer made him the ideal candidate to oversee the restructuring.

Synset Examples

Aristotle was a great orderer of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges or puts things into order
  • Antonym: disorganizer - a person who causes disorder or disarray

Etymology

From 'system' (from Greek 'systēma', organized whole) + '-izer' (agent noun suffix).
