Melolontha

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈlɒnθə / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈlɒnθə / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Melolonthidae, commonly known as European cockchafers.
genus of beetles: cockchafers

Examples

The larvae of Melolontha can cause extensive damage to meadow grasses and crops.
Researchers collected several adult Melolontha from the forest for their entomological study.
In spring, large numbers of Melolontha often emerge at dusk, flying noisily around trees.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek 'mēlolonthē' (a kind of beetle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈlɒnθə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈlɒnθə / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Melolonthidae, including the European cockchafers.
beetle genus (European cockchafers)

Examples

Several species within the genus Melolontha are known as agricultural pests in Europe.
The larvae of Melolontha can cause significant damage to plant roots.
Entomologists collected specimens of Melolontha during their field study in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - a common name for beetles of the genus Melolontha

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mēlolonthē' (beetle).
