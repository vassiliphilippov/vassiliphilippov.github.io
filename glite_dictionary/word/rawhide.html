rawhide

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɔːhaɪd / (BrE)
untanned animal hide, especially from cattle, used for making whips, ropes, and similar items
untanned animal hide, esp. from cattle

Examples

The cowboy cracked his rawhide whip to startle the herd.
She bought a rawhide dog chew for her puppy.
Ropes made from rawhide are incredibly strong and durable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leather - tanned animal hide

Etymology

From 'raw' + 'hide', referring to the untreated skin of an animal.
