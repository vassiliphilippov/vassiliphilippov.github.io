scow

1. NOUN

/ skaʊ / (AmE), / skaʊ / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed boat or barge, typically with sloping ends, used chiefly for transporting bulk materials in an open hold
flat-bottomed barge for bulk materials

Examples

The workers loaded gravel into the scow for transport down the river.
A rusty old scow sat moored at the edge of the harbor.
The company rented a fleet of scows to move sand for the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barge - a long, flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight

Etymology

From Dutch 'schouw', meaning a kind of boat.

2. NOUN

/ skaʊ / (AmE), / skaʊ / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed boat with sloping ends, used especially for transporting goods in shallow waters
flat-bottomed boat with sloping ends

Examples

The old scow was anchored near the riverbank, loaded with timber.
They used a scow to carry supplies across the shallow lake.
A rusty scow drifted slowly down the canal under the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barge - a long, flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *schouw* (a type of boat).
