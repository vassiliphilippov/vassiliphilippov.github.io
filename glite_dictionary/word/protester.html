protester

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtɛstər / (AmE), / prəˈtɛstə / (BrE)
a person who publicly demonstrates strong objection or support for something, especially as part of a group.
person taking part in a public demonstration

Examples

Thousands of protesters gathered outside the parliament building demanding political reform.
The police arrested several protesters during the march.
A lone protester stood in front of the factory holding a sign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstrator - a person who participates in a public protest or demonstration
  • Degree Lower: supporter - a person who supports a cause or party
  • Degree Higher: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change

Etymology

From 'protest' (verb) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈtɛstər / (AmE), / prəˈtɛstə / (BrE)
a person who publicly expresses strong objection or disapproval of an official policy or action
person who objects publicly to policies

Examples

Hundreds of protesters gathered outside the government building demanding change.
The police tried to disperse the protesters after the march became unruly.
As a passionate protester, she regularly attends rallies for environmental justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstrator - a person who takes part in a public protest meeting or march
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports or endorses a policy or authority

Etymology

From the verb 'protest' + '-er' denoting someone who protests; originally from Latin 'protestari' (to declare publicly).
