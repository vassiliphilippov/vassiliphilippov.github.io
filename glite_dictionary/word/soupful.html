soupful

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːp.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈsuːp.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount of soup that fills a spoon, bowl, or other container; a portion of soup.
amount of soup (spoon or similar portion)

Examples

He handed me a steaming soupful to warm me up.
With every soupful, she felt better and stronger.
The recipe calls for just one soupful of broth.

Synset Examples

She ladled a soupful of broth into the bowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonful - quantity that fills a spoon
  • Synonym: cupful - quantity that fills a cup

Etymology

Formed by combining 'soup' with the suffix '-ful' (meaning 'full of').
