Botox

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.tɑks / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.tɒks / (BrE)
a trademarked preparation of botulinum toxin used in small doses for medical and cosmetic treatments, especially to reduce facial wrinkles by temporarily paralyzing muscles
preparation of botulinum toxin used medically and cosmetically

Examples

She scheduled an appointment to get Botox injections to smooth her forehead lines.
Some patients use Botox to treat chronic migraines under a doctor's supervision.
Botox is commonly administered by dermatologists for cosmetic purposes.

Etymology

From the trademark based on 'botulinum toxin'.
