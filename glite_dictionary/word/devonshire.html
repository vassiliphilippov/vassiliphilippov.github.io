Devonshire

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛvənˌʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈdɛvənʃə / (BrE)
A county in southwestern England, now commonly known as Devon, historically named for the Dumnonii, a Celtic tribe.
county in southwestern England

Examples

She grew up in Devonshire and has fond memories of its coastline.
The rolling hills of Devonshire attract tourists from all over the world.
Their family owns a farm in Devonshire that has been passed down for generations.

Etymology

From 'Devon', after the Dumnonii tribe, with the suffix '-shire' meaning county.
