Tulostomaceae

1. NOUN

/ tuːˌlɒstəˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / tjuːˌlɒstəˈmiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as stalked puffballs, characterized by their round spore sacs borne on stalks
a family of stalked puffball fungi

Examples

Many species within the Tulostomaceae family can be found in arid regions around the world.
Members of the Tulostomaceae are distinguished by their stalked fruiting bodies and spore dispersal method.
The mycologist presented a lecture about the ecology of Tulostomaceae during the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a globular fungus that releases spores in a cloud when mature

Etymology

From genus name Tulostoma (from Greek 'tulos' meaning knob and 'stoma' meaning mouth) + scientific family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ tuːˌlɒstoʊˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / tjuːˌlɒstəˈmiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi known as stalked puffballs, characterized by their round, spore-producing fruiting bodies on a stalk.
family of fungi (stalked puffballs)

Examples

Several species in the Tulostomaceae family are commonly known as stalked puffballs.
Mycology students studied the unique spores produced by members of the Tulostomaceae.
The Tulostomaceae are found in arid and semi-arid environments across different continents.

Etymology

From the genus name Tulostoma, combined with the botanical family suffix -aceae.
