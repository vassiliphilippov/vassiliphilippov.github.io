to overlard

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈlɑrd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlɑːd / (BrE)
to excessively burden or embellish something with content, details, or ingredients, often to the point of detriment
to burden or embellish excessively

Examples

She tried not to overlard her essay with too many quotations.
Some television commercials overlard their message with dramatic music and flashy graphics.
If you overlard your soup with rich ingredients, the flavors may become overwhelming.

Synset Examples

It’s easy to overlard a presentation with too many slides.
Don’t overlard your writing with unnecessary jargon.
The chef warned against overlording the dish with too much butter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdo - to do something to an excessive degree
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make more attractive by adding details

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'lard' (to cover with fat or embellish).
