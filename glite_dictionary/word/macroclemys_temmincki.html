Macroclemys temmincki

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈklɛmɪs tɛmˈmɪnki / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈklɛmɪs tɛmˈmɪŋki / (BrE)
A large freshwater turtle species with three ridges on its back, native to the southeastern United States; also known as the alligator snapping turtle.
large snapping turtle species (alligator snapping turtle)

Examples

The Macroclemys temmincki is renowned for its powerful jaws and prehistoric appearance.
Researchers observed a Macroclemys temmincki basking on a log near the riverbank.
Many wildlife experts consider Macroclemys temmincki to be the largest freshwater turtle in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator snapping turtle - Large turtle species native to southeastern U.S.; common name for Macroclemys temmincki

Etymology

Named after the Dutch zoologist Coenraad Jacob Temminck; Macroclemys from Greek makros (large) + klemmys (turtle).
