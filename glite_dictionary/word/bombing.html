bombing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
an attack in which bombs are dropped, or explosives are detonated, usually to cause damage or destruction
an attack using bombs

Examples

The city was devastated by a bombing during the war.
News of the bombing shocked the entire nation.
Several people were injured in yesterday's bombing at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - the act of something bursting with a loud noise
  • Synonym: assault - a physical attack

Etymology

From 'bomb' (from French *bombe*, from Italian *bomba*) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
an attack or act of sabotage using bombs, often with the intention of causing destruction or terror
attack or sabotage with bombs

Examples

The city endured a series of bombings during the war.
The bombing caused extensive damage to the government building.
Police are investigating the recent bombing that occurred downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent burst, often from a bomb
  • Antonym: shooting - an attack using firearms rather than bombs

Etymology

From the verb 'bomb' + -ing suffix, first used in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
