fritter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪtə / (BrE)
A piece of food, such as fruit, meat, or vegetables, coated in batter and deep-fried.
food fried in batter with filling

Examples

She ordered an apple fritter and a cup of coffee for breakfast.
Vegetable fritters make a delicious appetizer or side dish.
The menu featured several types of fritter, including ones filled with cheese and ham.

Etymology

From Old French *friture*, from Latin *frictura* (frying).

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɪt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪt.ə / (BrE)
to waste time, money, or resources frivolously and unwisely, especially little by little (usually used as 'fritter away')
waste money or time carelessly

Examples

He managed to fritter away most of his savings on unnecessary gadgets.
She didn't want to fritter her time on trivial matters.
Over the years, they had frittered away the fortune their grandparents had left them.

Synset Examples

Fritter away one's inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner
  • Antonym: conserve - to protect something from harm or destruction; to use carefully

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal fritter 'fragment, shred', from Old French 'friture' (something fried), used figuratively for fragmenting something.
