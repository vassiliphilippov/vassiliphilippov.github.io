fictitious

1. ADJ

/ fɪkˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
Not real or true; invented or imagined rather than actual.
invented or imaginary; not real

Examples

The lawyer presented a fictitious account of what happened that night.
Many people use fictitious names when checking into hotels.
The story was entirely fictitious but captured everyone's imagination.

Synset Examples

a fabricated excuse for his absence
a fancied wrong
a fictional character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fictional - Existing only in fiction; invented for a story or book
  • Antonym: real - Actually existing as a fact; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From Latin ficticius, from fingere (to form, invent).

2. ADJ

/ fɪkˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
invented with the intention to deceive; not real or true but made up to mislead.
invented to deceive; false

Examples

He provided a fictitious address to avoid being contacted by the authorities.
The report was filled with fictitious figures designed to mislead investors.
They used fictitious names when making reservations at the hotel.

Synset Examples

an assumed name
an assumed cheerfulness
a fictitious address
fictive sympathy
a pretended interest
a put-on childish voice
sham modesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false - not true; incorrect or made to deceive
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring in fact

Etymology

From Latin ficticius (artificial, counterfeit), from fingere (to form, invent).
