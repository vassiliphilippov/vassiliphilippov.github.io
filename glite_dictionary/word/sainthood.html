sainthood

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪnthʊd / (AmE), / ˈseɪnthʊd / (BrE)
All saints collectively, regarded as a group or community.
saints collectively; community of saints

Examples

Throughout history, the sainthood has played a crucial role in the development of many religious traditions.
Medieval artwork often depicted the sainthood as a gathering in paradise.
Many believers pray for guidance and intercession from the sainthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saint - a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous

Etymology

Formed from 'saint' + 'hood', where '-hood' denotes a condition or collective state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪnthʊd / (AmE), / ˈseɪnthʊd / (BrE)
the status or condition of being recognized as a saint, especially in a religious context
condition of being a saint

Examples

The process of achieving sainthood can take many years within the Catholic Church.
Her acts of compassion led some people to speak of her eventual sainthood.
He was posthumously awarded sainthood for his lifelong dedication to others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonization - the official act of declaring someone a saint

Etymology

Formed from 'saint' + '-hood' (state or condition), both from Old French and ultimately Latin roots.
