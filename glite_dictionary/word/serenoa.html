Serenoa

1. NOUN

/ sɛrɪˈnoʊə / (AmE), / sɛrɪˈnəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of palm, chiefly known for the saw palmetto (Serenoa repens), a plant native to the southeastern United States.
genus of palm; saw palmetto

Examples

The fruit of Serenoa is often used in herbal remedies for prostate health.
Botanists classify the saw palmetto under the genus Serenoa.
Researchers are studying extracts from Serenoa for potential medicinal benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saw palmetto - a palm (Serenoa repens) with fan-shaped leaves, native to the southeastern U.S.

Etymology

Named after Sereno Watson (1826–1892), an American botanist.

2. NOUN

/ sɛrɪˈnoʊə / (AmE), / sɛrɪˈnəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of palm trees in the family Arecaceae, consisting of a single species, the saw palmetto (Serenoa repens).
genus of palms, saw palmetto

Examples

The Serenoa genus includes only one species, commonly known as saw palmetto.
Botanists studied Serenoa to better understand the medicinal properties of its fruit.
Many herbal supplements contain extracts from the Serenoa plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saw palmetto - a palm species native to the southeastern United States, from the genus Serenoa

Etymology

Named after the American botanist Sereno Watson (1826–1892).
