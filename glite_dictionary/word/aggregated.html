aggregated

1. ADJ

/ ˈæɡ.rəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Formed or calculated by combining several separate elements into a whole.
combined into a whole; amassed

Examples

The company's aggregated sales figures showed significant growth compared to last year.
All the aggregated data from the survey was analyzed for trends.
The aggregated total required several separate calculations before reaching the final figure.

Synset Examples

aggregate expenses include expenses of all divisions combined for the entire year
the aggregated amount of indebtedness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregate - form or group into a class or cluster (verb); formed by the collection of units or particles into a body, mass, or amount (adjective)

Etymology

From Latin aggregatus, past participle of aggregare (to add to a flock, to gather).
