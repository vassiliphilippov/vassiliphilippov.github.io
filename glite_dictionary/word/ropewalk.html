ropewalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊp.wɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊp.wɔːk / (BrE)
A long narrow building or covered path where ropes are made by twisting fibers together.
place where ropes are made

Examples

The old ropewalk in the dockyard is now a museum dedicated to maritime trades.
Ropes of great length were manufactured in the ropewalk to supply the navy's ships.
She visited a historic ropewalk where artisans demonstrated traditional rope-making techniques.

Etymology

From 'rope' + 'walk', referring to the walking required to twist and lay ropes by hand.
