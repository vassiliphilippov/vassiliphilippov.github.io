Attila

1. PROPN

/ əˈtɪlə / (AmE), / əˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A king of the Huns (c. 406–453), known for leading the most successful invasions of the Roman Empire and often called 'Attila the Hun.'
king of the Huns, 5th century

Examples

Historians often refer to Attila as one of Rome's most formidable enemies.
The campaigns of Attila brought fear across much of Europe in the 5th century.
Some legends depict Attila as a ruthless leader, while others highlight his strategic brilliance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Attila the Hun - another name for Attila, king of the Huns (406–453)

Etymology

From Gothic or possibly East Germanic roots; Latin Attila, probably from a diminutive of atta (father).
