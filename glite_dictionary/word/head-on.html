head-on

1. ADV

/ ˌhɛdˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌhɛdˈɒn / (BrE)
With the front part or head leading; in a direct or frontal manner, especially in reference to collisions or confrontations.
directly in front; with the front foremost

Examples

A truck hit the barrier head-on during the storm.
They tackled the problem head-on rather than avoiding it.
Two trains crashed head-on in the tunnel.

Synset Examples

the cars collided head-on

Etymology

From 'head' + 'on', signifying movement or contact with the front part leading.

/ ˌhɛdˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌhɛdˈɒn / (BrE)

Examples
