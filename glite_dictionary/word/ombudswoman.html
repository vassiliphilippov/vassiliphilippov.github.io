ombudswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmbʊdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɒmbʊdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female official appointed to investigate individuals' complaints against the government or public organizations
female government complaints investigator

Examples

The university appointed an ombudswoman to handle student grievances.
As ombudswoman, she mediated several disputes between citizens and local authorities.
The role of the ombudswoman is crucial in ensuring government accountability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ombudsman - an official appointed to investigate individuals' complaints against public authorities
  • Degree Lower: ombudsman - the gender-neutral or male form for the same office

Etymology

Modelled on 'ombudsman,' with 'woman' replacing 'man.' From Swedish 'ombud' (representative) and English 'woman.'
