grandmother

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn(d)ˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)ˌmʌðə / (BrE)
the mother of one’s father or mother
the mother of a parent

Examples

My grandmother told us stories about her childhood in the old country.
Every Sunday, we visit my grandmother for dinner with the whole family.
Her grandmother taught her how to knit when she was a child.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grandparent - the parent of one’s father or mother
  • Antonym: grandfather - the father of one’s father or mother

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'grand' (meaning large or important) and 'mother'.
