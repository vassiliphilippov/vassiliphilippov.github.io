heptasyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛptəˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˌhɛptəˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A word or line of verse consisting of seven syllables.
a word or line with seven syllables

Examples

The poet chose a heptasyllable structure for each line to create a rhythmic effect.
In classical poetry, the heptasyllable is less common than the octosyllable.
A heptasyllable can sometimes sound awkward in English verse due to its uneven rhythm.

Synset Examples

On the other hand, this heptasyllable is not merely common in the Saturnian but obviously the ‘regular’ or ‘normal’ form of the longer cola.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: octosyllable - a word or line of verse with eight syllables
  • Degree Lower: hexasyllable - a word or line of verse with six syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'hepta' (seven) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
