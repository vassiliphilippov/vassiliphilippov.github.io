Dahlia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪliə, ˈdɑliə / (AmE), / ˈdɑːliə, ˈdeɪliə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial tuberous plants native to Mexico and Central America, widely cultivated for their brightly colored flowers
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The garden was filled with colorful Dahlia blooms in late summer.
She planted several varieties of Dahlia in her flower beds this year.
Many gardeners prize the Dahlia for its vibrant and diverse flower shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a genus of flowering plants related to dahlias

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Anders Dahl (1751–1789).
