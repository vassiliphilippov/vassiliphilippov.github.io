clink

1. NOUN

/ klɪŋk / (AmE), / klɪŋk / (BrE)
a short, light, metallic sound, such as that made by two glasses or metal objects striking each other
a short, light metallic sound

Examples

The clink of coins in his pocket was unmistakable as he walked into the room.
We heard the clink of glasses from the dining table as the toast began.
The clink of keys on the metal hook echoed in the empty hallway.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clank - a loud, sharp, metallic sound
  • Degree Higher: clang - a resonant, prolonged, metallic sound

2. VERB

/ klɪŋk / (AmE), / klɪŋk / (BrE)
to make or emit a short, sharp, high-pitched sound like that of small pieces of metal or glass striking together
make a high, sharp sound

Examples

The glasses clink as we toast to the newlyweds.
Her keys clinked in her pocket with every step she took.
Coins clink against each other as I drop them into the jar.

Synset Examples

tinkling bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinkle - to make a light, high, ringing sound
  • Degree Higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant, metallic sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound produced.

3. VERB

/ klɪŋk / (AmE), / klɪŋk / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a light, sharp ringing sound like glass or metal striking
make a light, sharp ringing sound

Examples

They raised their glasses and clinked them in celebration.
The keys clinked together in his pocket as he walked.
She clinked her spoon against the cup while stirring her tea.

Synset Examples

champagne glasses clinked to make a toast

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clank - make a loud, metallic sound
  • Degree Higher: clang - make a loud, resonant, metallic sound
  • Degree Lower: tinkle - make a light, clear, ringing sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by light striking metal or glass; related to Middle English 'clinken'.

4. NOUN

/ klɪŋk / (AmE), / klɪŋk / (BrE)
(informal) A prison or jail, especially as a place where people are held in custody by the authorities.
informal term for prison or jail
informal

Examples

After getting caught, he ended up spending a night in the clink.
They threatened to throw him in the clink if he broke the law again.
Her brother had a reputation for landing himself in the clink every few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial
  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime

Etymology

Originally from 'The Clink', a historic London prison.
