vena vesicalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nə vəˈsaɪ.kəl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə vɛˈsɪ.kə.lɪs / (BrE)
Any of the veins that drain blood from the vesical plexus into the internal iliac veins.
veins draining the vesical plexus
medical

Examples

The vena vesicalis are important for draining blood from the urinary bladder.
During pelvic surgery, care must be taken not to damage the vena vesicalis.
Blockage of the vena vesicalis can contribute to urinary tract complications.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'vesicalis' (of the bladder).
