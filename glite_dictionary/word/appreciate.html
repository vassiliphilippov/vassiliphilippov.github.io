to appreciate

1. VERB

/ əˈpriʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to value something highly or hold it dear
value highly; hold dear

Examples

I really appreciate the time you spent helping me move.
She appreciates the beauty of the old architecture in the city.
He has always appreciated his grandmother's advice and love.

Synset Examples

I prize these old photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to consider as important or worth a lot
  • Synonym: to cherish - to hold something dear and care for it lovingly

Etymology

From Latin 'appretiare' (to set a price to), from 'ad' (to) + 'pretium' (price).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02260917-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈpriʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
(of a currency, asset, etc.) to increase in value over time.
increase in value (finance)

Examples

The company's shares appreciated significantly after the merger announcement.
If the euro continues to appreciate, European exports may become less competitive.
Her investment in the property appreciated over a period of five years.

Synset Examples

The yen appreciated again!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to depreciate - to decrease in value over time

Etymology

From Late Latin *appretiatus* (valued), from Latin *appretiare* (to set a price to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00316996-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈpriːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to be fully aware of or understand the full importance or meaning of something
be fully aware; realize fully

Examples

It took her years to appreciate the complexity of the project.
He didn't appreciate the risks involved until it was too late.
Do you appreciate what this decision means for our future?

Synset Examples

Do you appreciate the full meaning of this letter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to realize - be fully aware of something; understand clearly
  • Synonym: to understand - grasp the meaning, significance, or nature of something

Etymology

From Latin 'appretiare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'pretium' (price, value).

5. VERB

/ əˈpriːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to recognize the value or significance of something, especially with gratitude; to be thankful for
to be thankful for something

Examples

I really appreciate all the help you have given me with my project.
She didn't appreciate how much work went into organizing the event until she tried it herself.
We appreciate your patience while we resolve the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to consider something to be important or beneficial; to have a high opinion of
  • Synonym: to thank - to express gratitude to someone
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; to ignore the value or significance of something

Etymology

From Late Latin 'appretiare' (to value), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'pretium' (price, value).
