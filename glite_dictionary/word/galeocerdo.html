Galeocerdo

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪliəˈsɜːrdoʊ / (AmE), / ɡæliəˈsɜːdəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory sharks commonly known as tiger sharks, found in warm seas worldwide.
genus of tiger sharks

Examples

Scientists have observed Galeocerdo in tropical and subtropical oceans around the world.
The Galeocerdo genus includes the well-known tiger shark, famous for its distinct striped pattern.
Researchers are studying the behavior of Galeocerdo to learn more about their role in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger shark - a species of large predatory shark, Galeocerdo cuvier

Etymology

From Greek galeos (shark) and cerdo (the fox), referring to the perceived cunning nature of the shark.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡeɪliəˈsɜːrdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡeɪlɪəˈsɜːdəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory tiger sharks found in warm and temperate seas, best known for the tiger shark (Galeocerdo cuvier).
genus of tiger sharks

Examples

The Galeocerdo genus is known for its distinctive striped tiger sharks.
Scientists have studied the feeding habits of Galeocerdo in shallow coastal regions.
The only living species in the genus Galeocerdo is the tiger shark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger shark - a large predatory shark with dark stripes, member of this genus

Etymology

From Greek galeos (a kind of shark) + kerdo (fox).
