razing

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of completely demolishing a building or structure to ground level
complete demolition of a structure

Examples

The razing of the historic theater sparked fierce debate in the community.
After the razing of the old warehouse, developers began preparing the site for new apartments.
The city approved the razing of several abandoned buildings to improve public safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolition - the act of knocking down or destroying a building
  • Antonym: construction - the process of building something, especially structures

Etymology

From the verb 'raze,' which comes from Old French raser (to scrape, erase) and Latin radere (to scrape, shave).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the complete destruction or demolition of a building or structure
complete demolition of a building

Examples

The razing of the historic theater sparked protests throughout the community.
After the razing of the abandoned factory, the land was cleared for redevelopment.
The city council approved the razing of several old warehouses to make way for new apartments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolition - the act of intentionally knocking down a building
  • Antonym: construction - the building of something, typically a structure

Etymology

From the verb 'raze,' from Old French 'raser' (to scrape, erase), ultimately from Latin 'rasare,' frequentative of 'radere' (to scrape).
