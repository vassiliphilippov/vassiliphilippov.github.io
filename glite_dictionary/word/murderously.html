murderously

1. ADV

/ ˈmɝː.dɚ.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.dər.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows a strong intention to seriously harm or kill; with violent or deadly intent.
in a violently deadly manner

Examples

He glared murderously at his opponent across the table.
The villain laughed murderously as he revealed his plan.
Lightning flashed as the storm raged murderously overhead.

Synset Examples

the huge dog bore down on them with bared fangs and barking murderously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viciously - in a cruel or violent manner

Etymology

From 'murderous' (from 'murder' + suffix '-ous') + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈmɝːdɚəsli / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdərəsli / (BrE)
In a way that suggests a strong desire to kill or cause serious harm; with extreme violence or aggression.
in a violent, murderous manner

Examples

He glared murderously at his opponent after losing the match.
The mob surged murderously through the streets, breaking windows and overturning cars.
She spoke murderously of her adversary, her voice trembling with suppressed rage.

Synset Examples

rioters running amuck and throwing sticks and bottles and stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - in a violent manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a gentle manner, without violence

Etymology

From 'murderous' + '-ly'. 'Murderous' derives from 'murder'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00274737-r
Synset POS: r
