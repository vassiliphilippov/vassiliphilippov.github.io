hydrocolloid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈkɑːlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈkɒlɔɪd / (BrE)
a substance that forms a gel when mixed with water
substance forming a gel with water

Examples

Several food thickeners are classified as hydrocolloids, including agar and pectin.
The wound dressing contains a hydrocolloid to keep the area moist and promote healing.
Scientists are studying the behavior of hydrocolloid systems in pharmaceutical formulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloid - a mixture in which one substance is dispersed evenly throughout another

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) + colloid (from Greek 'kolla' meaning glue).
