Ge'ez

1. NOUN

/ ɡɛˈʔɛz / (AmE), / ɡɛˈʔɛz / (BrE)
an ancient Semitic language of Ethiopia, now used only as the liturgical language of the Ethiopian Church
ancient Ethiopian liturgical language

Examples

The priest chanted the prayer in Ge'ez during the morning service.
Many ancient Ethiopian manuscripts are written in Ge'ez.
Scholars often study Ge'ez to better understand the history and theology of the Ethiopian Church.

Synset Examples

Some linguists do not believe that Geʻez constitutes the common ancestor of modern Ethiopian languages, but that Geʻez became a separate language early on from some hypothetical, completely unattested language, and can thus be seen as an extinct sister language of Tigre and Tigrinya.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Amharic - a modern Semitic language of Ethiopia descended from Ge'ez
  • Degree Lower: Tigrinya - another modern Ethiopian Semitic language related to Ge'ez

Etymology

From Ge'ez ግዕዝ (Gəʿəz), meaning 'free, unbound'.
