razzmatazz

1. NOUN

/ ˌræz.məˈtæz / (AmE), / ˌræz.məˈtaz / (BrE)
exciting or elaborate action, display, or activity, especially intended to impress or confuse
exciting or elaborate action or display

Examples

The team's offense relied on razzmatazz to keep their opponents guessing throughout the game.
Some critics say the new movie has more razzmatazz than substance.
Amid all the razzmatazz of the show, the audience was left dazzled and entertained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razzle-dazzle - elaborate or showy action intended to impress or confuse

Etymology

Originated in American English in the early 20th century, possibly an elaboration of 'razzle-dazzle.'
