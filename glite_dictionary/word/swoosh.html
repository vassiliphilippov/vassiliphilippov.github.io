swoosh

1. VERB

/ swuʃ / (AmE), / swuːʃ / (BrE)
to move quickly with or cause to move with a rushing or whistling sound
move quickly with a whistling sound

Examples

The wind swooshed through the trees as the storm began to intensify.
She swooshed the mop across the floor, leaving a trail of suds behind.
The train doors swooshed open as passengers waited on the platform.

Synset Examples

The bubbles swoshed around in the glass
The curtain swooshed open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - to move swiftly with a rushing sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02192644-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ swuːʃ / (AmE), / swuːʃ / (BrE)
a rushing sound produced by the sudden movement of air, water, or another fluid
a rushing sound (e.g. of air or water)

Examples

I heard a swoosh as the wind rushed past the open window.
The swoosh of water filled the room as the waves crashed against the shore.
With a swoosh, the basketball zipped through the net.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - a soft sound made by something moving quickly through the air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07411130-n
Synset POS: n
