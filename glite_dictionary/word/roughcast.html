roughcast

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to shape or cut (stone, timber, etc.) in a rough form, without finishing the surface
shape or cut roughly

Examples

The workers roughcast the beams before smoothing them for the final structure.
He roughcasted the stone to get the basic shape before the detailed carving began.
They roughcasting the timber to prepare it for the carpenters.

Synset Examples

rough-hew stone or timber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hew - to chop or cut (something, especially wood or stone) with an axe or other tool

Etymology

From 'rough' + 'cast'; 'cast' here meaning to shape or form.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01263414-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A coarse plaster made of lime, cement, gravel, and small stones, applied to the surface of external walls.
coarse wall plaster

Examples

The old cottage was covered in roughcast, giving it a rustic appearance.
Builders applied a layer of roughcast to protect the exterior from harsh weather.
Some homeowners choose roughcast as a decorative finish for garden walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stucco - fine plaster used for coating wall surfaces
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to give a rough or preliminary shape to something, without finishing or smoothing it
give a rough initial shape

Examples

The sculptor began to roughcast the block of marble before carving the finer details.
They roughcasted the model from clay to establish its general form.
Before adding detail, it's helpful to roughcast your design so you can see the proportions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - to form or mould something
  • Antonym: finish - to complete or smooth by working on it fully

Etymology

From Middle English, from rough + cast (to throw, shape).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a preliminary model or version of something, made without fine detail, to serve as a basis for further development
rough preliminary model or version

Examples

The sculptor made a roughcast before starting on the final marble statue.
This report is only a roughcast and will require significant refinement.
Architects often create a roughcast to visualize their initial ideas.

Etymology

From 'rough' + 'cast', where 'cast' refers to a form or model.

to coat a wall or surface with roughcast, a coarse plaster mixture of lime, cement, gravel, and water, giving a textured finish
apply textured plaster to a surface

Examples
