sulphide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.faɪd / (BrE)
a compound of sulphur and another, more electropositive element
compound of sulphur with another element

Examples

Iron reacts with sulphur to produce iron sulphide.
The engineer explained that hydrogen sulphide is a highly toxic gas.
Many minerals contain sulphide compounds essential for industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfide - American English spelling of 'sulphide'

Etymology

From French 'sulfure' and 'sulfide', based on Latin 'sulphur' (sulphur).
