Dairen

1. PROPN

/ daɪˈrɛn / (AmE), / daɪˈrɛn / (BrE)
a port and shipbuilding center in northeastern China on the Liaodong Peninsula; now part of the city of Dalian
port city in northeastern China

Examples

During the early 20th century, Dairen was an important base for international trade in the region.
The port facilities at Dairen made it a strategic target during the Russo-Japanese War.
Historical maps often use the name Dairen for what is now known as Dalian.

Etymology

From Japanese *Dairen*, the former name under Japanese administration; now Dalian in Chinese.
