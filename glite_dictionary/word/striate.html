striate

1. VERB

/ ˈstraɪ.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to mark or groove with striae or thin lines
to mark with thin lines or grooves

Examples

The glacier had striated the rocks as it moved across the landscape.
Biologists observed that the muscle fibers were clearly striated under the microscope.
Artists sometimes striate their canvases for a textured visual effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groove - to make a long, narrow cut or depression
  • Synonym: stripe - to mark with stripes or long bands

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus', past participle of 'striare' (to furrow, to stripe), from 'stria' (furrow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstraɪ.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
marked with striae or striations; having parallel grooves or lines.
marked with grooves or stripes

Examples

The surface of the mineral was striate with delicate, parallel lines.
Researchers observed striate patterns in the muscle tissue under the microscope.
The shell's striate appearance made it easy to identify among other specimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striated - having striae or linear marks
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without grooves or lines

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus', past participle of 'striare' (to furrow, to make streaks).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03148523-a
Synset POS: a
