Chrysobalanus icaco

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsoʊbəˌleɪnəs aɪˈkɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / krɪsəbəˌleɪnəs aɪˈkɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree bearing edible, plumlike fruit, commonly known as cocoplum.
tropical tree with edible fruit (cocoplum)

Examples

The Chrysobalanus icaco thrives in coastal areas and produces fruit that is both edible and nutritious.
Botanists often study Chrysobalanus icaco for its resilience to salty environments.
Local markets in the Caribbean sometimes sell the fruit of Chrysobalanus icaco as a sweet treat.

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *balanos* (acorn), and Latinized scientific nomenclature.
