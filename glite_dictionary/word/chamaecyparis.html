Chamaecyparis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmiːˈsaɪpərɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæmiːˈsɪpərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family, native to eastern Asia and North America, commonly known as false cypresses
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

The Chamaecyparis genus includes several important species used in landscaping and timber production.
Gardeners often plant Chamaecyparis for its attractive foliage and columnar growth.
The wood of Chamaecyparis is highly valued for its durability and pleasant aroma.

Etymology

From Greek *khamai* (on the ground) + *kyparissos* (cypress).
