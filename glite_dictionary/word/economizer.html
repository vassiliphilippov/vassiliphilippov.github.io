economizer

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪzər / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is careful with money and resources, avoiding waste and unnecessary spending
a frugal, thrifty person

Examples

My grandfather was a devoted economizer, always looking for ways to save on household expenses.
Being an economizer helped her build substantial savings over the years.
He is such an economizer that he never buys anything unless it’s absolutely necessary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money carelessly or wastefully
  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible
  • Synonym: thrifty - using money and resources carefully and not wastefully

Etymology

From 'economize' + '-er', patterned on other agent nouns.
