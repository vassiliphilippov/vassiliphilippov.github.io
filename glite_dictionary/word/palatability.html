palatability

1. NOUN

/ ˌpælətəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌpalətəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant or acceptable to the taste
pleasantness to the taste

Examples

The chef improved the palatability of the dish with fresh herbs and spices.
Certain medications are coated to enhance their palatability and make them easier to swallow.
The palatability of school lunches is a frequent topic of debate among students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste - the sensation of flavor perceived in the mouth
  • Antonym: unpalatability - the quality of being unpleasant to the taste

Etymology

From palate (the roof of the mouth, sense of taste) + -ability.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæl.ə.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌpæl.ə.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being acceptable or agreeable to the mind or feelings
quality of being acceptable or agreeable

Examples

The palatability of the proposal helped it win broad support among the committee members.
Politicians often try to increase the palatability of their policies to appeal to a wider audience.
The film's palatability to mainstream viewers was a key factor in its commercial success.

Synset Examples

the pursuit of electoral palatability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptability - the quality of being acceptable or satisfactory
  • Antonym: unacceptability - the quality of being unacceptable or not satisfactory

Etymology

From palatable + -ity. Ultimately from Latin 'palatum' (palate).
