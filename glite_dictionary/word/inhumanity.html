inhumanity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.hjuˈmæn.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hjuːˈmæn.ə.ti / (BrE)
Cruel behaviour or a lack of compassion and consideration for others.
cruelty; lack of compassion

Examples

The inhumanity of their actions shocked everyone who heard the story.
History is filled with examples of inhumanity during times of war.
Many activists work tirelessly to fight against inhumanity in its various forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - callousness and causing pain to others
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathy and concern for others' suffering

Etymology

From Latin inhumanitas, from in- (not) + humanitas (human nature, kindness).

2. NOUN

/ ɪn.hjuˈmæn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪn.hjuːˈmæn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an extremely cruel and brutal act; great cruelty or lack of compassion.
extreme cruelty; a cruel act

Examples

The reports documented the inhumanity inflicted upon prisoners during the war.
He was shocked by the sheer inhumanity of the attack on civilians.
Throughout history, acts of inhumanity have been condemned by society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - callous indifference to suffering; the quality of being cruel
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From Latin inhumanitas (inhumaneness, cruelty), from in- (not) + humanitas (human nature, kindness).
