farthest

1. ADV

/ ˈfɑrðɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðɪst / (BrE)
To the greatest degree or extent; most advanced or far in degree, distance, or progress.
greatest degree or extent

Examples

Of all the candidates, she has gone farthest in her research on the subject.
He pushed the boundaries farthest with his bold theories.
The new policy takes environmental concerns farthest among all those proposed.

Synset Examples

went the furthest of all the children in her education
furthest removed from reality
she goes farthest in helping us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthest - To the greatest degree or extent; most advanced (alternative form, often preferred in abstract senses)

Etymology

Superlative of 'far', formed by adding the superlative suffix '-est'.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɑrðəst / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðɪst / (BrE)
To or at the greatest distance in space or time.
at/to the greatest distance

Examples

He ran the farthest of anyone in the race.
We sat at the farthest table from the stage.
This telescope can see farthest into space, revealing distant galaxies.

Synset Examples

see who could jump the farthest
chose the farthest seat from the door
he swam the furthest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthest - to or at the greatest distance in space or time
  • Antonym: nearest - at the shortest distance away

Etymology

Superlative of 'far'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrðɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðɪst / (BrE)
at the greatest distance in space, time, or order; most remote
most distant in space, time, or order

Examples

Of all the planets, Pluto is the farthest from the sun.
She settled in the farthest corner of the room to read her book.
This is the farthest I've ever traveled from my hometown.

Synset Examples

had traveled to the farthest frontier
don't go beyond the farthermost (or furthermost) tree
explored the furthest reaches of space
the utmost tip of the peninsula

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthermost - most distant, especially in position
  • Antonym: nearest - closest or shortest in distance

Etymology

Superlative form of "far"; -est is a common English superlative suffix.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
