Arminius

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈmɪniəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪniəs / (BrE)
A German chieftain who led an alliance of Germanic tribes to victory against the Romans at the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest in AD 9.
German hero, leader vs Romans in AD 9

Examples

Arminius is celebrated in German history for his victory over the Roman legions at the Teutoburg Forest.
Many historians consider Arminius a key figure in halting Roman expansion into northern Europe.
The legacy of Arminius is commemorated by a large monument in Germany known as the Hermannsdenkmal.

Etymology

Latinized form of a Germanic name; known as Hermann in German historiography.
