to admire

1. VERB

/ ədˈmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with respect, approval, or pleasure
regard with respect or approval

Examples

I really admire her dedication to her work.
Many people admire the architecture of the ancient temple.
As a child, he admired his older brother and wanted to be just like him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - to show regard or esteem for someone or something
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'admirari' (to wonder at), from 'ad-' (at) + 'mirari' (to wonder).

2. VERB

/ ədˈmaɪr / (AmE), / ədˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with pleasure, approval, or respect
look at with pleasure or respect

Examples

He paused to admire the beautiful sunset over the mountains.
Many people admire her dedication to helping others.
The tourists stopped to admire the architecture of the old cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - to admire someone for their abilities or qualities
  • Antonym: to despise - to dislike and have no respect for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin admīrārī (to wonder at, admire).
