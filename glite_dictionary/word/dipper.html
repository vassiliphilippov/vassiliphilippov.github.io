dipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a small North American diving duck, especially the male, which has bushy head plumage
a small North American diving duck

Examples

We spotted a dipper swimming quickly underwater in the lake.
The male dipper is easy to recognize by its bushy head plumage.
Several dippers nested near the riverbank this spring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
A ladle or utensil with a cup-shaped bowl and a long handle, used for dipping liquid from a container.
long-handled ladle or utensil

Examples

She used a dipper to pour soup into the bowls.
The old well had a wooden dipper hanging beside it.
After washing the vegetables, he scooped water with a dipper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladle - a large long-handled spoon with a cup-shaped bowl

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a small, stout bird that frequents fast-moving streams, diving and feeding underwater; species of the family Cinclidae.
small bird that dives in streams

Examples

We spotted a dipper darting in and out of the rushing stream.
The dipper is known for its ability to walk underwater in search of insects.
On our hike, a dipper paused on a mossy rock before plunging beneath the water.
