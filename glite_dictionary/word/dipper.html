Dipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a group of seven stars in the constellation Ursa Minor, the last of which is Polaris, commonly known as the Little Dipper
group of seven stars in Ursa Minor

Examples

Many people use the Dipper to find the North Star, Polaris.
The Dipper is easy to spot in the northern night sky during summer.
Children learned to trace the shape of the Dipper among the stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Little Dipper - the asterism formed by seven stars in Ursa Minor, including Polaris
  • Antonym: Big Dipper - a group of seven bright stars in Ursa Major, forming another dipper-shaped asterism

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a member of a Baptist denomination founded in 1708 by Americans of German descent, known for opposing military service and legal oaths and practicing trine immersion
member of a specific Baptist denomination

Examples

The Dippers were known for their strict interpretation of nonviolence and refusal to take legal oaths.
Many historical records describe how the Dippers practiced trine immersion during baptism.
Members of the Dippers refused military service on religious grounds.

Etymology

From the practice of trine immersion ('dipping') in baptism.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a group of seven bright stars in the constellation Ursa Major, forming a shape resembling a ladle or dipper; commonly known as the Big Dipper.
group of stars in Ursa Major

Examples

As the sky darkened, the Dipper became visible above the northern horizon.
She pointed out the Dipper to her friends and explained its importance in navigation.
The seven stars of the Dipper are among the most recognizable patterns in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Big Dipper - the seven-star asterism in Ursa Major
  • Synonym: Ursa Major - the constellation containing the Big Dipper
  • Synonym: Plough - British name for the Big Dipper asterism

Etymology

From the resemblance of the star pattern to a dipper (ladle).
