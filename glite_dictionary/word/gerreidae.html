Gerreidae

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈriːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / dʒɛˈriːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes commonly known as mojarras, found in warm coastal waters and estuaries, characterized by their compressed bodies and protrusible mouths.
family of fishes (mojarras)

Examples

The Gerreidae family includes several species important to coastal fisheries.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Gerreidae in estuarine environments.
Many aquarists appreciate the silvery appearance of Gerreidae species in their tanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mojarra - A common name for species of Gerreidae; small silvery fish found in warm waters

Etymology

From New Latin *Gerreidae*, based on *Gerres* (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈriːɪdiː / (AmE), / dʒɛˈriːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly tropical and subtropical marine fish known as mojarras, characterized by their laterally compressed bodies and protractile mouths.
family of fish known as mojarras

Examples

The Gerreidae family includes various species adapted to brackish and coastal waters.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Gerreidae in Caribbean estuaries.
Mojarras from the Gerreidae family are popular among inshore anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mojarra - a common name for fishes in the family Gerreidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Gerres.
