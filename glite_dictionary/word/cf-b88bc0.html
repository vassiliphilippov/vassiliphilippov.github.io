cf.

1. ADV

/ si ɛf / (AmE), / siː ɛf / (BrE)
Used to refer a reader to other material for comparison; compare.
abbreviation meaning 'compare'

Examples

In support of this argument, see also Smith (1998); cf. Jones (2001) for a contrary view.
The temperature readings this year are unusually high (cf. last year's data).
For a more detailed explanation, cf. the appendix in section 3.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare - to examine similarities and/or differences

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'confer' (compare).

2. ADV

/ siː ˈɛf / (AmE), / siː ˈɛf / (BrE)
(used to direct the reader to another place for comparison) compare.
used to direct for comparison (compare)

Examples

In the notes section, the author writes cf. Smith (1999) for a different interpretation.
For more details on the procedure, cf. the appendix at the end of the book.
The use of this term may vary by region; cf. its meaning in European texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare - to examine the similarities and/or differences

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'confer', meaning 'compare'.
