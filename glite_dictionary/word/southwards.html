southwards

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊθwərdz / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθwədz / (BrE)
toward the south; in the direction of the south.
toward the south

Examples

The birds migrate southwards every autumn to escape the cold.
After crossing the river, the hikers continued southwards through the valley.
The storm is moving southwards and is expected to reach the coast by morning.

Synset Examples

the ship turned southerly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northwards - toward the north
  • Degree Lower: eastwards - toward the east
  • Degree Lower: westwards - toward the west

Etymology

Formed from 'south' + suffix '-wards', indicating direction.
