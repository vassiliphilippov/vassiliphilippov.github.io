sobriquet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊbrɪˌkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbrɪˌkeɪ / (BrE)
a name or nickname given to a person, often describing a trait or characteristic, sometimes used in place of their real name
descriptive nickname or alias

Examples

He was known throughout the city by his sobriquet, 'The Fixer.'
The writer adopted the sobriquet 'Mark Twain' for his literary works.
Her friends gave her the affectionate sobriquet 'Sunny' because of her cheerful disposition.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - a familiar or humorous name given to a person instead of or as well as their real name

Etymology

From French sobriquet (nickname).
