Manitoba

1. PROPN

/ ˌmænɪˈtoʊbə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈtəʊbə / (BrE)
A province in central Canada, one of the three Prairie Provinces.
province in central Canada

Examples

Many immigrants settled in Manitoba during the late nineteenth century.
The vast prairies of Manitoba are known for their breathtaking sunsets.
Winnipeg, the capital of Manitoba, experiences very cold winters.

Etymology

Named after Lake Manitoba. From Cree or Ojibwa Manitou-wapow, meaning 'strait of the Manitou (spirit)'.
