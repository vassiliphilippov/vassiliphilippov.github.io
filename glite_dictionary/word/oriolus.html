Oriolus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːriˈoʊləs / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Oriolidae, comprising Old World orioles.
type genus of Old World orioles

Examples

The genus Oriolus includes several brightly colored species native to Asia and Africa.
Researchers have classified the black-naped oriole under the genus Oriolus.
Field guides often list the Eurasian golden oriole as Oriolus oriolus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful Old World or New World songbird

Etymology

From Latin *oriolus*, meaning oriole.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːriˈoʊləs / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Oriolidae, consisting of various species of orioles, which are passerine birds found mainly in the Old World.
type genus of orioles (Old World birds)

Examples

The genus Oriolus includes some of the most brightly colored orioles in Asia and Africa.
Researchers have discovered a new species within the Oriolus genus in Southeast Asia.
Birdwatchers are fascinated by the melodic songs of birds in the Oriolus genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'oriolus', referring to the golden oriole.
