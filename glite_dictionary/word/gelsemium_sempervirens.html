Gelsemium sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛlˈsiːmi.əm sɛmpərˈvaɪrɛnz / (AmE), / dʒɛlˈsiːmɪəm sɛmpəˈvaɪərɛnz / (BrE)
A poisonous evergreen vine native to the southeastern United States, known for its fragrant yellow funnel-shaped flowers.
poisonous evergreen vine with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Gelsemium sempervirens in early spring.
Children should be warned not to ingest any part of the Gelsemium sempervirens vine because it is highly poisonous.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Gelsemium sempervirens in southeastern woodlands.

Etymology

From Latin, genus name Gelsemium and species epithet sempervirens, meaning 'always green.'
