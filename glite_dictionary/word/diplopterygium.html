Diplopterygium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləpˈtɛrɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌdɪplɒpˈtɛrɪdʒɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of scandent, thicket-forming ferns native to Asia and the western Pacific regions.
genus of Asian and Pacific ferns

Examples

The dense thickets of Diplopterygium ferns provided shelter for many small forest animals.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Diplopterygium genus in the Malaysian rainforest.
Diplopterygium ferns are commonly found climbing in subtropical Asian forests, creating lush green undergrowth.

Etymology

From Greek 'diplo-' (double) and 'pterygion' (small wing), referring to features of the fern's fronds.
