Ancylus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈan.sɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, freshwater limpets in the family Ancylidae, often found in rivers and streams.
genus of freshwater river limpets

Examples

The Ancylus genus includes several species found in European rivers.
Researchers studied the distribution of Ancylus in different freshwater habitats.
A specimen of Ancylus was collected from the riverbank for analysis.

Etymology

From Greek ankýlos (curved, bent), referring to the limpet's shell shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈaŋ.sɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater snails, commonly known as river limpets, belonging to the family Ancylidae.
genus of freshwater snails (river limpets)

Examples

The Ancylus genus is characterized by its distinctive, limpet-shaped shells.
Scientists discovered a new species of Ancylus in the mountain stream.
The ecological role of Ancylus includes grazing on algae in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (crooked, curved), referring to the shape of the shell.
