reverting

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɝːtɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a thing, process, or tendency) tending to return to an earlier or previous state.
returning to an earlier state

Examples

After years of reform, the country is now facing reverting social policies.
The patient's symptoms showed a reverting pattern, returning to previous levels of severity.
Many old buildings exhibit reverting features when modern updates are neglected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regressive - tending to revert to an earlier or less advanced state
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or causing progress toward better conditions or new ideas
