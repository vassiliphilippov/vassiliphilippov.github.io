epiglottitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪɡlɑˈtaɪt̬ɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪɡlɒˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the epiglottis becomes inflamed, often causing fever, severe sore throat, and difficulty in swallowing.
inflammation of the epiglottis
medical

Examples

Immediate medical attention is necessary when epiglottitis is suspected in children.
The patient was diagnosed with epiglottitis after complaining of a high fever and trouble swallowing.
Doctors treated the rare case of adult epiglottitis with antibiotics and airway management.

Etymology

From epiglottis (Greek epi- 'upon' + glottis 'mouth of the windpipe') + -itis (Greek, indicating inflammation).
