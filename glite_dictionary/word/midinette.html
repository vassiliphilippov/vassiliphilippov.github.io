midinette

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːdiˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmiːdɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
A young female sales assistant or shopgirl in Paris, especially in the early 20th century.
Parisian shopgirl or sales assistant

Examples

The fashion designer was inspired by the lively energy of the midinettes who strolled along the Boulevard Haussmann.
During the early 1900s, many midinettes became symbols of Parisian style and elegance.
She dreamed of working as a midinette in a chic Paris boutique.

Etymology

From French 'midinette', originally referring to a shopgirl who had only a short break for lunch ('midi' = noon, 'nette' = diminutive).
