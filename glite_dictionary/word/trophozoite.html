trophozoite

1. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊ.fəˈzoʊ.aɪt / (AmE), / ˌtrɒf.əˈzəʊ.aɪt / (BrE)
A stage in the life cycle of certain protozoa, such as sporozoans, during which the organism is actively feeding and growing.
active feeding stage of a protozoan
medical

Examples

The trophozoite stage is when the parasite is most actively growing and dividing within its host.
Under the microscope, the trophozoite of Plasmodium falciparum is distinguished by its ring shape.
Treatment is most effective when the protozoan is in its trophozoite stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cyst - the dormant, non-feeding protective stage of a protozoan

Etymology

From Greek trophos (nourisher) + zōion (animal).
