Eleocharis

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛl.iˈɑː.kə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛl.iˈɒ.kə.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sedges with dense spikes of flowers and leaves reduced to basal sheaths, commonly known as spikerushes.
a genus of sedges (spikerushes)

Examples

Eleocharis species can be found in wetlands around the world.
Botanists identified the plant as belonging to the genus Eleocharis.
Many waterfowl feed on the seeds of Eleocharis in marshy habitats.

Etymology

From Greek *helos* (marsh) and *charis* (grace), referring to its habitat and appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛliˈɑkərɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛliˈɒkərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sedges characterized by dense spikes of flowers and leaves reduced to basal sheaths, commonly found in wetlands.
a genus of wetland sedges

Examples

Many wetlands are dominated by Eleocharis species, which are important for soil stabilization.
The botanist identified the plant as belonging to the Eleocharis genus based on its distinctive flower spikes.
Restoration plans included the reintroduction of native Eleocharis to improve habitat diversity.

Etymology

From Greek *heleos* (marsh) + *charis* (grace).
