bestowal

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈstoʊ.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ.əl / (BrE)
the act of formally giving or conferring something, especially an honor or gift
the act of giving or conferring

Examples

The bestowal of the award was the highlight of the ceremony.
The bestowal of such a title carries great responsibility.
Many traditions include the bestowal of gifts during important festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - the act of giving something formally or legally
  • Synonym: presentation - the act of formally presenting something to someone

Etymology

From the verb 'bestow', originally from Middle English 'bestowen', meaning to place or put.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈstoʊ.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ.əl / (BrE)
a gift or conferment; the act of giving something as an honor or present
a gift or act of giving

Examples

The bestowal of the award was a highlight of the evening ceremony.
Her generous bestowal of books inspired the entire community to read.
Each scholarship bestowal helps a student achieve their educational dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - a thing given willingly to someone without payment; a present
  • Synonym: grant - something granted, especially as a right or privilege
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of taking something away

Etymology

From the verb 'bestow' (Middle English, from be- + stowe 'to place, put') + -al.
