anise

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs / (AmE), / ˈænɪs / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to Egypt, widely cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used as a flavoring in cooking and to produce an essential oil with medicinal uses.
aromatic plant used as flavoring

Examples

The chef added anise to the sauce for a subtle licorice flavor.
Some herbal teas contain anise seeds as a digestive aid.
In Mediterranean cuisine, anise is used to enhance the taste of breads and pastries.

Etymology

From Latin 'anisum', from Greek 'anison', referring to the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs / (AmE), / ˈænɪs / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant whose seeds are used to flavor foods and drinks with a sweet, liquorice-like taste.
aromatic plant with liquorice-flavored seeds

Examples

The chef added a pinch of anise to the bread dough for extra flavor.
I enjoy sipping a liqueur made from anise after dinner.
Anise seeds have long been used to soothe digestive problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - aromatic plant with a liquorice-like flavor, related to anise

Etymology

From Latin anisum, from Greek anison, anēthon (dill or anise).
