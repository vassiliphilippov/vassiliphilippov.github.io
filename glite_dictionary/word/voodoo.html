voodoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈvuːduː / (AmE), / ˈvuːduː / (BrE)
A religious practice of West African origin, developed chiefly in Haiti and other Caribbean regions, combining elements of Roman Catholic ritual with traditional African rites and involving belief in magic and spiritual possession.
Caribbean religious practice with magic and spirits

Examples

Many people travel to Haiti to learn more about voodoo and its rituals.
Some believe that voodoo ceremonies can heal illnesses or cast spells.
The film portrayed voodoo in a sensationalized and inaccurate way.

Etymology

From Louisiana French 'voudou,' from Ewe 'vodu' (spirit, deity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvuːduː / (AmE), / ˈvuːduː / (BrE)
A charm or object believed, especially superstitiously, to possess magical powers.
magical charm

Examples

She carried a small voodoo in her pocket, hoping it would bring her good luck.
The merchant sold handmade voodoo charms to curious tourists.
Some believed the mysterious illness was caused by a voodoo placed on the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talisman - an object thought to have magical powers and bring good luck
  • Synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry believed to protect against evil

Etymology

From Louisiana Creole French vaudou, from West African Vodun (spirit, deity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvuˌdu / (AmE), / ˈvuː.duː / (BrE)
A religion practiced chiefly in Haiti, involving a mix of West African, Catholic, and indigenous beliefs, with rituals involving spirits and deities.
religion with origins in Haiti involving rituals, spirits, and deities

Examples

Many people in Haiti practice voodoo as a central part of their cultural and spiritual life.
She studied the rituals of voodoo for her anthropology research project.
Some Westerners misunderstand voodoo, confusing its beliefs and traditions with stereotypes about witchcraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vodou - alternate spelling of voodoo, especially in Haitian context
  • Synonym: witchcraft - practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities

Etymology

From Louisiana French 'vaudou', from Haitian Creole 'vodou', ultimately from West African Vodun ('spirit' or 'deity').

4. VERB

/ ˈvuːduː / (AmE), / ˈvuːduː / (BrE)
to bewitch, enchant, or affect someone or something as if by voodoo magic
bewitch or affect by voodoo magic

Examples

Some superstitious locals believe the old house was voodooed years ago.
He claimed his unlucky streak started after someone voodooed him.
Rumors spread that the opposing team had been voodooed before the playoffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewitch - to cast a spell over; enchant

Etymology

From Louisiana Creole French 'voudou', from West African Vodun, meaning 'spirit'.
