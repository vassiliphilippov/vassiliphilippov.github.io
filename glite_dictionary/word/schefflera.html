Schefflera

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈflɛrə / (AmE), / ʃɛˈflɛərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants, often with shrub-like or climbing forms and showy, finger-like compound leaves.
genus of tropical plants with compound leaves

Examples

The Schefflera in the corner of her living room gives the space a lush, tropical feel.
Gardeners often choose Schefflera for its attractive, glossy leaves and easy care requirements.
Some species of Schefflera can grow quite tall, making them popular as indoor trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella plant - a common name for several species in the genus Schefflera

Etymology

Named after German botanist J.C. Scheffler (fl. 18th century).

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈflɛr.ə / (AmE), / ʃɛˈflɛl.ə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical shrubs and climbing plants characterized by showy palmately compound leaves, widely grown as ornamentals
genus of tropical ornamental plants

Examples

The popular houseplant Schefflera is valued for its attractive umbrella-like leaves.
Botanists recently reclassified many species previously belonging to the genus Schefflera.
The Schefflera thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect sunlight.

Etymology

Named after J. C. Scheffler, an 18th-century German botanist.
