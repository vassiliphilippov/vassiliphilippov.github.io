capon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpɑn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒn / (BrE)
a castrated male chicken, especially one raised for eating
castrated male chicken

Examples

The chef recommended using a capon for the holiday roast because of its tender meat.
On the farm, they kept several capons specifically for festive meals.
Historically, capon was considered a delicacy served at medieval banquets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rooster - an adult male chicken
  • Antonym: hen - an adult female chicken

Etymology

From Old French capon, from Latin capo, caponem (capon, castrated cock).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpɑn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒn / (BrE)
the flesh of a castrated male chicken, valued for its tenderness and flavor, used as food
flesh of a castrated male chicken

Examples

Many chefs prefer to roast capon for its tender and juicy meat.
We served slices of capon with seasonal vegetables at the holiday feast.
The recipe called for a whole capon, seasoned with herbs and garlic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - the flesh of a domestic fowl as food

Etymology

From Old French *chapun*, from Latin *capo, caponem* (castrated cock).
