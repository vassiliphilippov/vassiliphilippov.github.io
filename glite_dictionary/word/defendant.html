defendant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛndənt / (BrE)
a person or institution against whom a legal action is brought in a court of law, especially one accused or sued
person or institution being sued or accused in court
legal

Examples

The defendant pleaded not guilty to all charges during the trial.
If the defendant is found liable, they must pay damages to the plaintiff.
The judge instructed the defendant to remain silent while witnesses were testifying.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in a court of law
  • Synonym: accused - person charged with a crime, especially in criminal proceedings

Etymology

From Latin 'defendere' (to defend) via Old French 'defendant' (defending).
