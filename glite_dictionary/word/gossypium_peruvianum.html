Gossypium peruvianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːˈsɪpiəm pəˌruːviˈɑːnəm / (AmE), / ˌɡɒˈsɪpɪəm pəˌruːvɪˈɑːnəm / (BrE)
A species of cotton plant native to South America, noted for its long, rough, hairy fibers.
South American cotton species

Examples

Textile researchers are examining the qualities of Gossypium peruvianum to develop stronger fabrics.
The long, rough fibers of Gossypium peruvianum make it valuable for certain industrial uses.
Weavers in South America traditionally used Gossypium peruvianum for its unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - A soft, white, fibrous substance that surrounds the seeds of the cotton plant; used in textiles.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gossypium' (cotton) and 'peruvianum' (of Peru, Peruvian).
