to sympathize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪz / (BrE)
to share or understand the feelings or suffering of another person
share or understand another's feelings

Examples

I sympathize with your frustration over the delay, as I have experienced similar situations myself.
Many people sympathized with the victims of the flood and offered help.
Although she could not fix the problem, she listened and sympathized with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Degree Lower: pity - to feel sorrow for someone's misfortune

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), via Late Latin and Old French.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪz / (BrE)
to feel or express sympathy or compassion for someone else's situation or feelings
feel or show sympathy or compassion

Examples

I sympathize with your frustration over the delayed project.
Many people sympathize with victims of natural disasters around the world.
She sympathized with her friend during his difficult time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling) via French sympathiser and Medieval Latin sympathizare.

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪz / (BrE)
to feel or express understanding or compassion for someone else's situation or feelings
express or feel understanding and compassion

Examples

Many people sympathize with victims of natural disasters.
I sympathized with her when she described her difficult childhood.
Although he doesn't agree, he can sympathize with your concerns.

Synset Examples

You don't need to explain — I understand!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
  • Synonym: to understand - to grasp the meaning, significance, or nature of something
  • Antonym: to antagonize - to cause someone to become hostile

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), via Medieval Latin sympathizare and French sympathiser.
