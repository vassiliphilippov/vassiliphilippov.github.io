meticulous

1. ADJ

/ məˈtɪkjələs / (AmE), / mɪˈtɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise.
very careful and precise

Examples

She is meticulous in her research, always checking every source thoroughly.
The artist worked with meticulous precision on every detail of the painting.
His meticulous notes made it easy for others to follow the complex procedure.

Synset Examples

meticulous research
punctilious in his attention to rules of etiquette

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - taking care or paying close attention to avoid mistakes
  • Antonym: sloppy - showing a lack of care or attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin *meticulosus* (fearful), from *metus* (fear).

2. ADJ

/ məˈtɪkjələs / (AmE), / məˈtɪkjələs / (BrE)
showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
very careful and precise

Examples

She maintained a meticulous record of every expense during her travels.
The scientist was meticulous about documenting each step in the experiment.
His meticulous planning ensured that nothing was overlooked at the event.

Synset Examples

a meticulous craftsman
almost worryingly meticulous in his business formalities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - exercising care or caution
  • Antonym: sloppy - careless and lacking attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin 'meticulosus' meaning 'fearful' or 'timid'.
