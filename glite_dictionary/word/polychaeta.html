Polychaeta

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈkiːtə / (BrE)
A class of chiefly marine annelid worms with paired, bristle-bearing appendages on most segments.
class of marine bristle worms

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Polychaeta living near hydrothermal vents.
Some Polychaeta exhibit bright colors and elaborate appendages.
The diet of Polychaeta varies greatly depending on habitat and species.

Etymology

From Greek polu- (many) + khaitē (hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a class of annelid worms that are mostly marine, characterized by segmented bodies with paired, bristled appendages on most segments
class of marine bristle worms

Examples

Many species in the Polychaeta class play a vital role in marine ecosystems as detritivores.
The bristled bodies of Polychaeta can be observed under a microscope by studying preserved specimens.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Polychaeta class near the hydrothermal vents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - a segmented worm of the phylum Annelida

Etymology

From Greek poly- (many) + chaite (hair), referring to their many bristles.
