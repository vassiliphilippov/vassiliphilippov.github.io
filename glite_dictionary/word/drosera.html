Drosera

1. NOUN

/ droʊˈsɪərə / (AmE), / drəʊˈsɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of insectivorous plants known as sundews, typically found in bogs and noted for sticky glandular leaves used to trap insects
genus of insectivorous sundew plants

Examples

The Drosera is remarkable for its ability to capture and digest insects.
Botanists discovered a new species of Drosera in the remote wetlands.
Many gardeners cultivate Drosera for its unique appearance and insect-trapping abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sundew - common name for plants of the genus Drosera

Etymology

From Greek 'drosos' meaning 'dew', referring to the dew-like drops on the plant's leaves.

2. NOUN

/ droʊˈsɪrə / (AmE), / drɒˈsɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing, bog-inhabiting insectivorous plants, commonly known as sundews, in the family Droseraceae
a genus of insect-eating plants (sundews)

Examples

The Drosera species are known for their sticky leaves that trap insects.
Many bogs in Australia and South Africa are home to unique Drosera plants.
Some gardeners cultivate Drosera for their unusual insect-eating abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sundew - any plant of the genus Drosera, with sticky glandular hairs for trapping insects

Etymology

From Greek 'drosos' (dew), referring to the dew-like droplets on the plant.
