Simhath Torah

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˌhɑt ˈtoʊrə / (AmE), / sɪmˌhɑt ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
a Jewish festival celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri to mark the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings
Jewish festival marking Torah cycle completion

Examples

Every year, the congregation comes together to celebrate Simhath Torah with singing and dancing.
During Simhath Torah, the Torah scrolls are carried around the synagogue in joyous procession.
Many families attend special services for Simhath Torah to mark the end and beginning of the Torah readings.

Etymology

From Hebrew *simḥat tôrāh*, meaning 'rejoicing of the Torah'.
