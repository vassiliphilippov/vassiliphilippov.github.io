/ məˌkɛr.ənˈθɪr.ə / (AmE), / məˌkɪə.rænˈθɪə.rə / (BrE)
a genus of wildflowers native to western North America, belonging to the family Asteraceae
genus of wildflowers in North America

Examples

Many species of Machaeranthera thrive in the arid regions of the American Southwest.
The purple petals of Machaeranthera add color to the desert landscape each spring.
Botanists studied Machaeranthera to understand its adaptation to drought conditions.

Etymology

From Greek makhaira (a knife or sword) and anthera (anther), referring to the shape of the anther.
