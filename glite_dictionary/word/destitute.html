destitute

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛstəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
Extremely poor and lacking the means to provide for oneself; without the basic necessities of life.
extremely poor; lacking necessities

Examples

After losing his job and home, he was left completely destitute.
Charities work hard to support families who are destitute and struggling to survive.
Many refugees arrive in the country destitute and without any belongings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient money; very poor
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets

Etymology

From Latin destitutus, past participle of destituere (to abandon, forsake).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛstəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
completely lacking or without something that is needed or wanted.
lacking or without something needed

Examples

The report was destitute of any solid evidence to support its claims.
He felt completely destitute of hope after the failed experiment.
Her stories were destitute of emotion, making them hard to relate to.

Synset Examples

writing barren of insight
young recruits destitute of experience
innocent of literary merit
the sentence was devoid of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devoid - entirely lacking or free from
  • Synonym: bereft - deprived of or lacking something

Etymology

From Latin 'destitutus', past participle of 'destituere' (to abandon, forsake).
