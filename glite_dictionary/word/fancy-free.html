fancy-free

1. ADJ

/ ˈfænsi fri / (AmE), / ˈfænsi friː / (BrE)
having no commitments, responsibilities, or attachments; carefree and untroubled
carefree, without commitments

Examples

After quitting his job, he spent a year traveling the world, feeling completely fancy-free.
Many envy the fancy-free lifestyle of young artists living in big cities.
He remembered his college days as fancy-free and full of adventure.

Synset Examples

a fancy-free bachelor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibilities; unconcerned
  • Antonym: responsible - having an obligation to do something, or having control over or care for someone

Etymology

From 'fancy' (imagination/desire) + 'free'; popularized in 16th-century English poetry.
