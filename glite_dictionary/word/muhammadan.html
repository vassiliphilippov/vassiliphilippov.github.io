Muhammadan

1. ADJ

/ məˈhɑː.mə.dən / (AmE), / məˈhæm.ə.dən / (BrE)
Relating to Muhammad, the Arabian prophet, or to the religion of Islam, especially as used in historical or older contexts.
relating to Muhammad or Islam (historical)
archaic offensive

Examples

The historian wrote about Muhammadan architecture in medieval India.
Many older European texts refer to Muslims as Muhammadan people, though this is now considered inaccurate.
The British colonial administration sometimes used Muhammadan law in their legal codes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic - relating to Islam or its teachings
  • Antonym: Christian - relating to Christianity or its adherents

Etymology

From Muhammad, the founder of Islam, + -an.

2. NOUN

/ məˈhæmədən / (AmE), / mʊˈhæmədən / (BrE)
an archaic term for a follower of the prophet Muhammad; a Muslim (now considered outdated and sometimes offensive)
archaic term for a Muslim
archaic offensive

Examples

Many 18th-century writers referred to Muslims as Muhammadans, though this term is no longer preferred.
The British colonial administration classified various communities as Muhammadans in their census records.
She studied the beliefs and practices of Muhammadans in the context of South Asian history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - a follower of the religion of Islam

Etymology

From the name Muhammad + -an (‘follower of’), modeled after older English formations like 'Christian'.
