indiscretion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈskrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɛʃən / (BrE)
lack of good judgment or care in speech or action, especially resulting in a mistake or misjudgment.
lack of good judgment; mistake

Examples

Her single indiscretion almost cost her the trust of her colleagues.
The politician tried to downplay his earlier indiscretions during the campaign.
A moment of indiscretion led to years of regret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprudence - lack of caution or wisdom
  • Antonym: discretion - the quality of being careful and judicious

Etymology

From Latin indiscretio (want of discernment), from in- (not) + discretio (separation, discernment).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɛʃən / (BrE)
a minor act of bad judgment or behavior that is not considered serious or criminal
a minor misdeed or lapse in judgment

Examples

His political career was almost ruined by a youthful indiscretion.
She apologized for her indiscretion at the company dinner.
One little indiscretion doesn't define a person's character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misdemeanor - a minor wrongdoing or legal offense
  • Degree Higher: crime - an act against the law, often more serious than a misdeed
  • Degree Lower: error - a mistake, often accidental or less serious than indiscretion

Etymology

From Latin 'indiscretio' (lack of discernment).
