salsify

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælsəfi / (AmE), / ˈsalsɪfi / (BrE)
The long, edible root of a Mediterranean plant, often cooked as a vegetable and known for its mild flavor resembling that of oysters.
edible root vegetable

Examples

The chef roasted salsify with olive oil and herbs for a delicious side dish.
Have you ever tried mashed salsify instead of potatoes?
In some markets, salsify is also known as the oyster plant due to its ocean-like flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorzonera - a related root vegetable sometimes called black salsify

Etymology

From French *salsifis*, from Italian *salsefica*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsælsɪfi / (AmE), / ˈsælsɪfi / (BrE)
Either of two plants with long edible roots, especially the root of the plant cultivated as a vegetable.
edible root vegetable

Examples

The chef roasted salsify to bring out its sweet, nutty flavor.
You can substitute parsnips or salsify in this recipe for a similar texture.
At the farmers' market, I bought fresh salsify along with carrots and turnips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorzonera - A related root vegetable often called black salsify

Etymology

From French *salsifis*, which likely comes from Italian *salsefica* or Spanish *salsifí*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsælsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsælsɪfi / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant with long edible white roots and purple flowers, cultivated as a vegetable.
a Mediterranean plant with edible root

Examples

She pulled a salsify root from the garden to use in her soup.
The restaurant served roasted salsify alongside potatoes and carrots.
In the spring, purple flowers appear atop the salsify plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorzonera - another root vegetable plant sometimes called black salsify

Etymology

From French salsifis, probably from Italian salsefica or obsolete Spanish salsifí.
