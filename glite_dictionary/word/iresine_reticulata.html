Iresine reticulata

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈriːsiːn ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / aɪˈriːsiːn ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A South American plant with green to purple or red branches, ornamental green to purple foliage, and spikes of small woolly flowers with dry membranous bracts, often grown for its decorative leaves.
ornamental South American plant species

Examples

The vibrant leaves of Iresine reticulata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Many gardeners admire Iresine reticulata for its colorful foliage and easy care.
Botanists have studied Iresine reticulata to understand its adaptation to tropical climates.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Iresine' and species epithet 'reticulata', meaning 'netted' or 'net-like' in reference to the leaf venation.
