East

1. NOUN

/ ist / (AmE), / iːst / (BrE)
the countries of Asia, especially those of eastern and southeastern Asia, considered as a region distinct from the West culturally and geographically
the countries of Asia as a region

Examples

Many ancient philosophies originated in the East and continue to influence global thought today.
She has traveled extensively throughout the East, studying its diverse cultures and religions.
Trade between the East and the West has shaped history for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West - the countries of Europe and the Americas, considered as a region especially in contrast to the East

2. PROPN

/ ist / (AmE), / iːst / (BrE)
the region of the United States lying north of the Ohio River and east of the Mississippi River; often considered as a distinct cultural and political area
region of the eastern United States
american

Examples

Many of the country's oldest cities are located in the East of the United States.
He moved from California to the East in search of new job opportunities.
The weather in the East can be very different from that in the West.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Midwest - central north region of the United States
  • Antonym: West - the region of the United States to the west of the Mississippi River
  • Synonym: Northeast - the northeastern region of the United States
