Procaviidae

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌkæviˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / prəʊˌkævɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals in the order Hyracoidea that includes all modern hyraxes
family of mammals including hyraxes

Examples

All modern hyrax species are members of the family Procaviidae.
Scientists study the evolutionary traits of Procaviidae to understand mammalian ancestry.
The Procaviidae are found mainly in Africa and parts of the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small, thickset, herbivorous mammal of Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From New Latin 'Procavia', the type genus, plus '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
