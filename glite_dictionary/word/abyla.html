Abyla

1. PROPN

/ əˈbaɪlə / (AmE), / əˈbaɪlə / (BrE)
a promontory in northern Morocco, situated opposite the Rock of Gibraltar, known as one of the Pillars of Hercules in classical mythology.
a promontory in northern Morocco; classical Pillar of Hercules

Examples

According to ancient sources, Abyla was considered one of the fabled Pillars of Hercules.
The view from Abyla offers a striking panorama across the Strait of Gibraltar.
Historians have debated the exact location of Abyla for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Rock of Gibraltar - the towering limestone promontory on the southern coast of Spain, also a Pillar of Hercules

Etymology

From Latin 'Abyla,' from Greek 'Abýla,' name of the promontory.
