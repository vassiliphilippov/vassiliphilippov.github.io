economiser

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪzər / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəˌmaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is careful with money, limiting spending and avoiding waste
a person who avoids waste and limits spending

Examples

She has always been an economiser, making her clothes last longer than anyone else.
An economiser will compare prices and look for sales before making a purchase.
As an economiser, he prefers to save his money rather than spend it on luxuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spender - a person who spends money freely or extravagantly
  • Synonym: economist - an expert in economics

Etymology

From the verb 'economise', derived from French 'économiser', from 'économie' (economy).
