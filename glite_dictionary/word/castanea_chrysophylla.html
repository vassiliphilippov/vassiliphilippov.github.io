Castanea chrysophylla

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈteɪniə ˌkraɪsəˈfɪlə / (AmE), / kæsˈtiːniə ˌkrɪsəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of the Pacific Coast of North America, with glossy yellow-green leaves and edible nuts, also known as the golden chinquapin
evergreen tree of the Pacific Coast; golden chinquapin

Examples

The Castanea chrysophylla is native to the woodlands of Oregon and California.
Gardeners appreciate Castanea chrysophylla for its attractive foliage and edible nuts.
Wildlife often feed on the seeds produced by Castanea chrysophylla trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden chinquapin - common name for Castanea chrysophylla

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Castanea* (chestnut) + specific epithet *chrysophylla* from Greek *chryso-* (golden) + *phyllon* (leaf).
