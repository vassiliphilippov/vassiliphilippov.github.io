resettled

1. ADJ

/ riˈsɛtld / (AmE), / riˈsɛtld / (BrE)
having moved or been moved to a new place to live, especially after being displaced.
having been moved to a new place

Examples

After the war, thousands of refugees were resettled in different countries around the world.
The family was resettled in a quiet village far from their original home.
Several communities have welcomed resettled immigrants and helped them adjust to their new lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resettle - to move or be moved to a new place to live
  • Synonym: displaced - forced to leave one's home, usually as a result of war or disaster

Etymology

From re- (again) + settle, meaning to settle again or anew.
