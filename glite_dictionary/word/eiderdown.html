eiderdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdərˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈaɪdədaʊn / (BrE)
The soft feathers or down of the eider duck, valued for use in bedding, such as quilts and pillows.
soft feathers of the eider duck

Examples

The luxurious quilt was filled with eiderdown collected from northern shores.
Her grandmother cherished an old eiderdown that kept her warm through cold winters.
Many bedding companies advertise the warmth and softness of eiderdown products.

Etymology

From 'eider' (the duck species), ultimately from Icelandic 'æður', and 'down' meaning soft feathers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdərˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈaɪdədaʊn / (BrE)
A soft quilt filled with the down feathers of the eider duck, often used as a warm bed covering.
soft quilt filled with eider duck down

Examples

She snuggled under the eiderdown to keep warm on the cold winter night.
The antique eiderdown was beautifully embroidered and very soft to the touch.
We replaced our old blanket with a luxurious eiderdown for extra comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duvet - A soft quilt filled with feathers, down, or a synthetic alternative
  • Synonym: comforter - A thick, quilted bed covering filled with down or synthetic fibers

Etymology

From German Eiderdaune, from Eider (the eider duck) + Daune (down).
