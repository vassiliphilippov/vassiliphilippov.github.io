flaccid

1. ADJ

/ ˈflæs.ɪd, ˈflæk.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈflæs.ɪd, ˈflæk.sɪd / (BrE)
soft and lacking firmness or stiffness; hanging limply, especially due to lack of elasticity or muscle tone
soft, limp, lacking firmness

Examples

After weeks in a cast, his leg muscles were noticeably flaccid and weak.
The leaves became flaccid due to a lack of water.
The balloon hung flaccid after most of the air escaped.

Synset Examples

a flaccid penis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - not firm or stiff; lacking strength
  • Antonym: firm - solid, hard, or rigid; not soft or yielding

Etymology

From Latin 'flaccidus' (flabby, drooping), from 'flaccus' (flabby).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈflæs.ɪd / (BrE)
soft and lacking firmness; weak or not strong and robust, especially as a result of being out of condition
soft, weak, and lacking firmness

Examples

After weeks without exercise, his muscles became noticeably flaccid.
The lettuce leaves were flaccid after sitting in the fridge for too long.
Her argument was flaccid, lacking any real evidence or strength.

Synset Examples

he was too soft for the army
flabby around the middle
flaccid cheeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flabby - soft and loose, lacking firmness
  • Antonym: firm - strong, solid, and not soft

Etymology

From Latin flaccidus (flabby), from flaccus (flabby, drooping).
