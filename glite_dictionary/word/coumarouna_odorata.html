Coumarouna odorata

1. NOUN

/ kuːməˈruːnə ɒdəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / kuːməˈruːnə ɒdəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A tall tropical South American tree that produces pulpy egg-shaped pods containing fragrant black almond-shaped seeds used for flavoring, commonly known as the source of tonka beans.
tropical South American tree; source of tonka beans

Examples

The Coumarouna odorata tree is cultivated for its valuable seeds, known as tonka beans.
Perfume makers often use extracts from Coumarouna odorata to add a sweet, vanilla-like scent.
Harvesting Coumarouna odorata helps support local economies in parts of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonka bean - fragrant seed from Coumarouna odorata, used for flavoring

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: genus 'Coumarouna', species 'odorata', meaning fragrant.
