margin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the blank space around the text on a page, especially at its sides, top, or bottom
blank space around text on a page

Examples

He wrote a question in the margin of his notebook.
Please leave a wide margin when formatting your essay.
Small notes were scribbled in the margins of the textbook pages.

Synset Examples

he jotted a note in the margin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something

Etymology

From Latin 'margo, marginis' (edge, border).

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(finance) The difference between the selling price of goods or services and the cost of producing them, often expressed as a percentage of sales.

Examples
