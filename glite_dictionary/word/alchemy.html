alchemy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.mi / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.mi / (BrE)
a mysterious or powerful quality that makes a relationship, especially between people, work well or seem extraordinary
mysterious quality in a relationship

Examples

There was a unique alchemy between the lead actors that captivated the audience.
Sometimes, the alchemy of a good team can't be explained—it's just there.
Their friendship is held together by a certain alchemy that neither of them fully understands.

Synset Examples

their chemistry was wrong from the beginning — they hated each other
a mysterious alchemy brought them together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - a mutual attraction or rapport between people

Etymology

From Old French alquemie, from Medieval Latin alchymia, from Arabic al-kīmiyā’ (the art), from Greek khymeia (pouring together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.mi / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.mi / (BrE)
a medieval philosophical and proto-scientific tradition concerned with the transmutation of materials, especially the attempt to transform base metals into gold, and the search for the elixir of life; regarded as a forerunner of modern chemistry
medieval precursor to chemistry, transformation of materials

Examples

Medieval scholars devoted much of their lives to the study of alchemy in hopes of discovering the philosopher's stone.
Modern science sees alchemy as a blend of mysticism and early chemistry.
The legend of turning lead into gold has its roots in alchemy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chemistry - the science of the composition and properties of substances

Etymology

From Old French *alquimie*, from Medieval Latin *alchimia*, from Arabic *al-kīmiyā’* (the art of transformation).
