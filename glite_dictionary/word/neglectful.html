neglectful

1. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡlɛktfəl / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktfəl / (BrE)
not giving proper care or attention to someone or something; failing to fulfill one's duties or responsibilities.
failing to give proper care or attention

Examples

The teacher was criticized for being neglectful of her students’ emotional needs.
He became neglectful of his health after starting his new job.
It is neglectful to leave your dog outside in extreme weather.

Synset Examples

derelict (or delinquent) in his duty
neglectful of his duties
remiss of you not to pay your bills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remiss - lacking care or attention to duty
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; taking care

Etymology

From 'neglect' + '-ful', ultimately from Latin 'neglegere' (to disregard, not heed).

2. ADJ

/ nəˈɡlɛktfəl / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktfəl / (BrE)
Not giving enough care or attention to someone or something; careless or inattentive.
not giving enough care or attention

Examples

The teacher noticed that John was being neglectful of his homework assignments.
Poor conditions in the shelter were blamed on a neglectful management team.
If you are neglectful of your health, minor problems can quickly become serious.

Synset Examples

inattentive students
an inattentive babysitter
neglectful parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; alert and observant

Etymology

From 'neglect' + suffix '-ful'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00759397-s
Synset POS: s
