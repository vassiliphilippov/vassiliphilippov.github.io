Gracilariidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˌsɪləˈraɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ɡrəˌsɪləˈraɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small moths whose larvae feed as leaf miners, creating tunnels within leaves
family of small moths (leaf miners)

Examples

Larvae of the Gracilariidae family are known for the distinctive tunnels they create inside leaves.
The Gracilariidae can cause significant damage to ornamental plants and crops through leaf mining.
Entomologists often study Gracilariidae to understand the ecological impacts of leaf miners.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Gracilaria, with the standard zoological family suffix -idae.
