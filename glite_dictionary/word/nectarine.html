nectarine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛktəriːn / (AmE), / ˈnɛktəriːn / (BrE)
A variety of peach with smooth, thin skin and (usually) yellow flesh, differing from the typical fuzzy peach.
peach with smooth skin and yellow flesh

Examples

He packed a ripe nectarine for lunch, preferring its smooth skin to that of a peach.
Her favorite summer snack is a cold nectarine, juicy and sweet.
The fruit market was overflowing with fresh peaches and nectarines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a round, soft, juicy fruit with fuzzy skin and a stone

Etymology

From early 17th century, via obsolete French 'nectarine', from Latin 'nectar' (drink of the gods).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛktəriːn / (AmE), / ˈnɛktəriːn / (BrE)
A variety of peach with smooth, hairless skin and a sweet, juicy flesh.
smooth-skinned peach variety

Examples

She picked a ripe nectarine from the fruit bowl for her breakfast.
The market was stocked with peaches, nectarines, and plums.
This nectarine has a particularly sweet and juicy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a soft, round, sweet fruit with fuzzy skin

Etymology

From French 'nectarine', probably from 'nectar' for its sweetness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.təˌrin / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.təriːn / (BrE)
a variety of peach with smooth skin and (usually) yellow flesh, distinct from the common fuzzier peach
smooth-skinned variety of peach

Examples

She sliced a ripe nectarine for her breakfast cereal.
We bought a bag of nectarines at the farmers' market.
Unlike a peach, the nectarine has smooth, thin skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a round, juicy fruit with fuzzy skin

Etymology

From early modern Latin 'nectarina', from 'nectar' (as the fruit was considered sweet).
