shrinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming smaller in size, amount, or value
process of becoming smaller

Examples

The shrinking of the ice caps is a serious environmental concern.
There has been a noticeable shrinking of the company's profits this quarter.
The tailor warned me about the shrinking of this fabric after washing.

Synset Examples

the material lost 2 inches per yard in shrinkage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrinkage - the act or process of shrinking; the amount by which something shrinks

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or act of becoming smaller in size, amount, or value
process of becoming smaller

Examples

The shrinking of the ice caps is a major concern for scientists.
Many companies are worried about the shrinking of their customer base.
She noticed the shrinking of resources available for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the process of making something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: growth - the process of increasing in size, number, or importance

Etymology

From verb 'shrink' (Old English scrincan, to contract).
