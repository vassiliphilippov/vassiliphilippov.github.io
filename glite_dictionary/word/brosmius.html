Brosmius

1. NOUN

/ broʊsˈmiːəs / (AmE), / brɒsˈmiːəs / (BrE)
A cusk; a North Atlantic marine fish of the cod family (Brosme brosme), known for its elongated body and single dorsal fin.
a North Atlantic marine fish (cusk)

Examples

The Brosmius is commonly found along rocky bottoms in the cold waters of the North Atlantic.
Fishermen reported a record catch of Brosmius this season due to favorable conditions.
Marine biologists study the Brosmius to better understand its role in Atlantic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cusk - a North Atlantic marine fish, also known as Brosmius

Etymology

From scientific Latin Brosmius, based on Norwegian 'brosme' (cusk).
