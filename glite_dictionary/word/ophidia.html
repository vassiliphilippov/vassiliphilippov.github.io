Ophidia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈfɪdiə / (AmE), / əʊˈfɪdiə / (BrE)
a zoological group comprising snakes; formerly used as an order in some classifications, now equivalent to Serpentes
zoological group of snakes

Examples

The suborder Ophidia consists of all true snakes.
Early zoologists debated whether lizards and Ophidia belonged to the same order.
Most herpetology texts now use 'Serpentes' instead of the classical term Ophidia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Serpentes - current zoological order comprising snakes
  • Synonym: snake - long, legless reptile; common term for members of Ophidia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ophidion' (little snake), from 'ophis' (snake).
