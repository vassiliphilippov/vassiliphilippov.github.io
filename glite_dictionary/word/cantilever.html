cantilever

1. VERB

/ ˈkæntəˌlivɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæntɪˌliːvə / (BrE)
(of a structure) to project or extend outward, supported at only one end, like a cantilever.
to extend outward supported at one end

Examples

The architect designed a balcony to cantilever over the river below.
The modern art gallery's top floor cantilevers dramatically beyond the lower stories.
A series of steel beams cantilevered from the wall, creating extra space without columns.

Etymology

From 'cantilever' (noun, a projecting beam), formed from 'canti-' (possibly from cant, meaning edge or corner) and 'lever'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.təˌli.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɪˌliː.və / (BrE)
a long projecting horizontal beam or structure that is fixed at only one end and carries a load at the other end or along its length.
projecting beam fixed at one end

Examples

The balcony was supported by a steel cantilever extending from the building's main frame.
Engineers tested the strength of the cantilever under heavy loads.
The new bridge design uses a massive cantilever to span the river without supporting pillars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girder - a large iron or steel beam used for building bridges and large buildings

Etymology

From cant (a projecting part) + lever, modeled after French 'cantilever' or earlier Italian 'cantalévero'.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈkæntɪˌliːvər / (AmE), / ˈkæntɪˌliːvə / (BrE)
to construct or project (something) so that it is supported at only one end, typically forming an overhang
project so that only one end is supported

Examples

The architect decided to cantilever the balcony to create a dramatic visual effect.
They cantilevered the roof so it extended several meters beyond the main structure.
Modern bridges often cantilever sections to span wide rivers without intermediate supports.

Synset Examples

Frank Lloyd Wright liked to cantilever his buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: project - extend outward beyond a surface

Etymology

From 'cantilever', originally a noun, probably from 'cantaloup' (a projecting stone in architecture) + 'lever'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
