morality

1. NOUN

/ məˈræləti / (AmE), / məˈralɪti / (BrE)
principles or motivation concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
principles of right and wrong

Examples

The debate questioned whether technology can exist without morality.
Teaching children a sense of morality is essential for developing a good society.
Some novels explore complex issues of morality and human nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - philosophical principles relating to right and wrong conduct
  • Antonym: immorality - lack of moral principles or standards; wickedness
  • Antonym: amorality - lack of concern for morality; absence of moral sense

Etymology

From Latin 'moralitas' (manner, character, proper behavior), from 'moralis'.

2. NOUN

/ məˈræləti / (AmE), / məˈralɪti / (BrE)
principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
principles of right and wrong behavior

Examples

Debates on morality often divide people along religious and cultural lines.
Some philosophers argue that morality is not absolute but changes over time and place.
The novel explores the morality of its main characters through their difficult choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - moral principles that govern behavior
  • Antonym: immorality - the state of being wicked or lacking morals

Etymology

From Latin moralitas (manner, character, proper behavior), from moralis (moral).
