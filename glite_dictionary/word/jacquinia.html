Jacquinia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈkwɪniə / (AmE), / dʒəˈkwɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs and small trees, sometimes placed in the family Myrsinaceae.
genus of tropical American shrubs/trees

Examples

The rare Jacquinia shrubs thrive in the subtropical forests of the Caribbean.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several species within the Jacquinia genus.
Botanists debated whether Jacquinia should be classified under the Primulaceae or Myrsinaceae family.

Etymology

Named after Nikolaus Joseph von Jacquin, Austrian botanist (1727–1817).

2. NOUN

/ ʒæˈkwiːniə / (AmE), / dʒæˈkwɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs and small trees, sometimes placed in the family Myrsinaceae, known for their evergreen leaves and clusters of flowers.
a genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

Several species in the genus Jacquinia are prized for their ornamental value.
Botanists debated whether Jacquinia should be placed in Myrsinaceae or its own family.
The evergreen leaves of Jacquinia plants make them popular for tropical landscaping.

Etymology

Named after the Austrian botanist Nikolaus Joseph von Jacquin (1727–1817).
