guitarfish

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈtɑːrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ɡɪˈtɑːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A primitive tropical bottom-dwelling ray with a guitar-shaped body, found in warm coastal waters.
a ray with a guitar-shaped body

Examples

The guitarfish can often be found in shallow coastal waters, camouflaged against the sandy sea floor.
Unlike most rays, the guitarfish has a body shape that resembles a guitar, making it easy to identify.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of the endangered guitarfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a cartilaginous fish with flattened body and enlarged pectoral fins
  • Synonym: shovelnose ray - another name for certain species of guitarfish

Etymology

Named for its guitar-like body shape.
