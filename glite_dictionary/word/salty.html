salty

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔlti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlti / (BrE)
tasting of or containing a lot of salt.
containing or tasting of salt

Examples

The soup was too salty for my taste, so I added more water.
After hours in the ocean, his skin felt rough and salty.
She prefers salty snacks like pretzels and chips over sweets.

Synset Examples

salt water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsalted - not containing salt
  • Synonym: saline - containing or resembling salt; salty

Etymology

From Old English 'sealt' (salt) + -y (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔlti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlti / (BrE)
Tasting of or containing salt; one of the four basic taste sensations, like sea water.
having a taste of salt; basic taste sensation

Examples

The soup was too salty, so I added more water to dilute it.
He prefers salty snacks, such as pretzels and potato chips, to sweet ones.
Sea water has a distinctly salty flavor that is hard to miss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet - having the pleasant taste characteristic of sugar
  • Antonym: bitter - having a sharp, pungent taste or smell; not sweet
  • Antonym: sour - having an acid taste like lemon or vinegar

Etymology

From Old English *sealt* (salt) + *-y*.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɔlti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlti / (BrE)
(of language, humor, or style) engagingly stimulating or provocative, often in a slightly coarse or irreverent way.
provocative or stimulating in language or style

Examples

Her salty remarks kept everyone amused during the meeting.
The comedian's show was filled with salty jokes that drew big laughs from the audience.
He is known for his salty sense of humor, which can be both sharp and entertaining.

Synset Examples

a piquant wit
salty language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquant - interesting and stimulating, especially of remarks or style
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong features; uninteresting or dull
