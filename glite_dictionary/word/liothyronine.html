liothyronine

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.oʊˈθaɪ.rəˌnin / (AmE), / laɪ.əʊˈθaɪ.rəˌniːn / (BrE)
a thyroid hormone similar to thyroxine but with one less iodine atom per molecule, more potent and shorter-acting
potent thyroid hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed liothyronine to manage the patient's hypothyroidism.
Recent studies compare liothyronine with levothyroxine for treating thyroid disorders.
Liothyronine is known for its rapid effect on metabolism compared to other thyroid hormones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroxine - main hormone produced by the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'thyreos' (shield, referring to thyroid) + 'ine' (chemical suffix).
