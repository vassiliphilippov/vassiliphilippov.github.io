vasiform

1. ADJ

/ ˈveɪsəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈveɪsɪfɔːm / (BrE)
having the form or function of a tube, especially for the passage of fluids.
having a tube-like form

Examples

The plant's vasiform vessels efficiently transport water throughout its tissues.
Certain worms possess a vasiform body structure for the movement of nutrients.
A vasiform canal allows fluids to flow between different organs in this species.

Etymology

From Latin vas (vessel) + -form (having the form of).
