Cenchrus ciliaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.krəs sɪˈlɪə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛŋ.krʊs sɪˈlɪə.rɪs / (BrE)
A perennial grass forming dense clumps, used mainly in South Africa and Australia as pasture and forage; also known as buffel grass.
a perennial grass used for pasture (buffel grass)

Examples

Many farmers in arid regions rely on Cenchrus ciliaris for grazing livestock.
Recent studies suggest that Cenchrus ciliaris can help restore degraded rangelands.
The rapid spread of Cenchrus ciliaris has impacted native plant biodiversity in Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffel grass - common name for Cenchrus ciliaris, a perennial forage grass

Etymology

From Latin; Cenchrus from Greek *kenkhros* (millet-grass), ciliaris meaning 'having fine hairs'.
