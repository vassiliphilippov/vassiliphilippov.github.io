schoolhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a building where children are taught; a place where a school is held, especially a traditional or rural one-room building
building for education; traditional school building

Examples

The old schoolhouse was renovated into a local museum.
Children gathered every morning in the small village schoolhouse.
A bell rang from the schoolhouse to signal the start of classes.

Synset Examples

the school was built in 1932
he walked to school every morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - an institution for educating children

Etymology

Formed from 'school' + 'house', first recorded in Middle English.
