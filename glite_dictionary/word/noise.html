noise

1. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
a loud public expression of protest, complaint, or opposition
outcry or protest

Examples

The new law stirred up a lot of noise from angry citizens.
Despite all the noise surrounding the decision, the policy did not change.
There was plenty of noise in the media after the controversial announcement.

Synset Examples

the announcement of the election recount caused a lot of noise
whatever it was, he didn't like it and he was going to let them know by making as loud a noise as he could

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcry - a strong expression of public disapproval or anger
  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially of people shouting or protesting
  • Antonym: approval - the act of approving something, opposite of protest or complaint

2. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
Irrelevant or meaningless information that obscures or distorts the main message.
irrelevant or distracting information

Examples

All the noise in his speech made it hard to focus on his main argument.
When analyzing data, it's important to filter out the noise to find real patterns.
There's a lot of noise in the news these days, making it difficult to identify reliable information.

Synset Examples

all the noise in his speech concealed the fact that he didn't have anything to say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: static - crackling or hissing on radio or television; meaningless interference

Etymology

From Old French *noise* (quarrel, noise), from Latin *nausea* (seasickness).

3. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
Unwanted electrical or acoustic signals that interfere with communication or the transmission of information.
unwanted signal interfering with communication

Examples

The radio reception was poor because of excessive noise in the signal.
Audio engineers work to reduce noise in recording equipment for clear sound quality.
The scientist analyzed the noise generated by thermal fluctuations in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - disturbance that alters or disrupts a signal in communication
  • Antonym: signal - transmitted electrical or electromagnetic information in communication systems

Etymology

From Old French noise (a quarrel, noise), from Latin nausea (disgust, nausea), but technical meaning developed in 20th century.

4. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
a sound, especially a loud or unpleasant one, that lacks musical quality or is considered disagreeable
unpleasant or disagreeable sound

Examples

The construction outside produced so much noise that I could not concentrate on my work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - that which is perceived by the sense of hearing
  • Antonym: music - organized and harmonious sound with structure and aesthetic value

Etymology

From Old French 'noise' (quarrel, noise), probably from Latin 'nausea' (seasickness, discomfort).

Examples
