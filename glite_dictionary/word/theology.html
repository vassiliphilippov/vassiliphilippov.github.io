theology

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / θiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A particular system or school of religious beliefs and teachings.
system of religious beliefs

Examples

Catholic theology differs in significant ways from Protestant traditions.
He spent years studying Hindu theology at university.
Contemporary Jewish theology addresses modern ethical questions.

Synset Examples

Jewish theology
Roman Catholic theology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a Church or other group

Etymology

From Greek theologia (discourse on the gods), from theos (god) + -logia (study).

2. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / θiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the academic study of the nature of God and religious belief, often as a professional discipline taught at universities or seminaries
academic study of religion and God

Examples

She decided to pursue theology as her field of study at university.
His interest in theology led him to attend a seminary after graduation.
Many scholars debate the influence of culture on theology and religious practice.

Synset Examples

he studied theology at Oxford

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divinity - the study of religion, especially as an academic discipline

Etymology

From Greek *theologia* (discourse on the gods), from *theos* (god) + *-logia* (study, discourse).

3. NOUN

/ θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / θiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the nature of God, religious beliefs, and the influence of religion on society, often pursued as an academic discipline
study of religion and religious beliefs

Examples

She decided to study theology at university to better understand world religions.
The professor gave a fascinating lecture on medieval theology and its impact on Western thought.
Many debates in theology revolve around questions of faith versus reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divinity - the quality or study of being divine; the formal academic study of religion
  • Antonym: philosophy - the study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, and ethics; sometimes contrasted with theology

Etymology

From Greek *theologia* (discourse on God), from *theos* (god) + *-logia* (study of).
