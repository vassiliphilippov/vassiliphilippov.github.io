hydrostaticity

1. NOUN

/ haɪdroʊstæˈtɪsɪti / (AmE), / haɪdrəʊstæˈtɪsɪti / (BrE)
the condition or quality of relating to fluids, especially concerning the pressure they exert or transmit
condition related to fluid pressure

Examples

The hydrostaticity of the experiment was monitored to ensure accurate pressure measurements.
Variations in hydrostaticity can dramatically affect the physical properties of materials under pressure.
Maintaining perfect hydrostaticity is crucial for high-pressure crystal growth studies.

Synset Examples

The structure of the high-pressure phase differs completely, depending on the hydrostaticity of applied pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrostatics - the branch of physics dealing with fluids at rest and the forces on them

Etymology

From 'hydrostatic', which is from Greek 'hydōr' (water) + 'statikos' (causing to stand), plus the suffix '-ity' (quality or state).
