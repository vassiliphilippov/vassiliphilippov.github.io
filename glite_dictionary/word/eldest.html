eldest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛl.dɪst / (BrE)
the oldest child or offspring among a group of siblings
the oldest sibling or child

Examples

She is the eldest of three daughters in her family.
The eldest often takes on more responsibility for younger siblings.
As the eldest, he set a good example for his brothers and sisters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: youngest - the child or offspring who is the last born

Etymology

From Old English 'eldest', superlative of 'eald' (old).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛldɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛldɪst / (BrE)
(of a person) being the oldest or firstborn among a group of siblings or relatives.
oldest sibling; first in birth order

Examples

She is the eldest daughter in the family.
As the eldest son, he inherited the family business.
Their eldest child just graduated from university.

Synset Examples

the firstborn child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oldest - having lived or existed the longest; oldest in age
  • Antonym: youngest - being the last or most recently born among a group of siblings

Etymology

From Old English 'yldesta', superlative of 'eald' (old).
