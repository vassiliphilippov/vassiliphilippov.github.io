myxoedema

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊˈdimə / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊˈdiːmə / (BrE)
A condition caused by severe hypothyroidism, characterized by dry skin, swelling of tissues, especially around the face, and mental deterioration.
condition caused by severe hypothyroidism
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with myxoedema after observing the characteristic swelling and lethargy.
If left untreated, myxoedema can result in serious complications, including heart problems and mental decline.
She presented with dry skin and facial puffiness, both classic symptoms of myxoedema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothyroidism - underactivity of the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Greek 'myxa' (mucus) + 'oidēma' (swelling).
