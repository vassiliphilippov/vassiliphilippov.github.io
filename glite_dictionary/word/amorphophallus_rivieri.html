Amorphophallus rivieri

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔːr.foʊˈfæl.əs rɪˈvɪr.i / (AmE), / əˌmɒ.fəˈfæl.əs rɪˈvɪə.ri / (BrE)
A foul-smelling, somewhat fleshy tropical plant from Southeast Asia, cultivated for its edible corms or for its large leaves and striking dark red spathe surrounding a large spadix.
tropical plant with edible corms and showy spathe

Examples

The Amorphophallus rivieri is often grown in greenhouses for its unusual flowers.
In Southeast Asia, the corms of Amorphophallus rivieri are considered a delicacy.
Gardeners are attracted to Amorphophallus rivieri because of its striking dark red spathe and spadix.

Etymology

From genus name Amorphophallus (Greek: 'amorphos' meaning shapeless, 'phallos' meaning penis, referring to the plant's shape) and species named after French botanist Rivière.
