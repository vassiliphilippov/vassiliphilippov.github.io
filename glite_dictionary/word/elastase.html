elastase

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæsteɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈlæsteɪz / (BrE)
a pancreatic enzyme that breaks down elastin and other proteins by catalyzing their hydrolysis
an enzyme that digests elastin
medical

Examples

The pancreas secretes elastase to help digest proteins in the small intestine.
A deficiency of elastase can lead to complications in breaking down certain proteins.
Researchers measured the activity of elastase in various tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trypsin - a pancreatic enzyme that breaks down proteins
  • Synonym: chymotrypsin - a digestive enzyme similar in function to elastase

Etymology

From 'elastin', the protein substrate, and '-ase', a suffix used for enzymes.
