triteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtraɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unoriginal, dull, or lacking freshness due to overuse
the state of being dull or unoriginal

Examples

The triteness of his jokes made the audience sigh rather than laugh.
Critics were disappointed by the triteness in the plot of the new film.
She avoided triteness by expressing her ideas in an original way.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unique
  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse

Etymology

From trite + -ness; trite from Latin tritus (worn, rubbed).
