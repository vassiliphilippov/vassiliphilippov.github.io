blame

1. VERB

/ bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
to say or think that someone or something is responsible for something bad or wrong
to hold responsible for something bad

Examples

He always tries to blame others for his own mistakes.
It's easy to blame the weather when things go wrong.
Don't blame me if you arrive late to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
  • Synonym: to accuse - to say someone has done something wrong

Etymology

From Old French *blamer* (to reproach), from Latin *blasphemare* (to reproach, revile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844217-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
to attribute responsibility for a fault or wrong to someone or something
attribute responsibility for a fault or mistake

Examples

She tried to blame her lateness on the traffic, but no one believed her.
It’s unfair to blame others for mistakes you made yourself.
The manager blamed the poor results on lack of preparation.

Synset Examples

We blamed the accident on her
The tragedy was charged to her inexperience

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or commendation, especially as the opposite of blame
  • Synonym: to accuse - to claim someone is responsible for a fault, crime, or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French 'blamer', from Latin 'blasphemare' (to reproach, revile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00729953-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
Responsibility for a fault or wrong; the act of saying or thinking that someone is responsible for something bad that has happened.
responsibility for a fault or wrong

Examples

The manager placed the blame for the failure on the entire team.
No one wanted to accept blame for the mistake that cost them the contract.
After the accident, the driver faced much public blame.

Synset Examples

his incrimination was based on my testimony
the police laid the blame on the driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - responsibility for a mistake or wrong
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval; the opposite of blame

Etymology

From Old French 'blamer' (to criticize), from Latin 'blasphemare' (to reproach, to speak evil of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
Responsibility for a fault, wrong, or bad situation; censure or reproach for something considered to be a failure or misdeed.
responsibility or reproach for a fault or misdeed

Examples

He accepted the blame for the mistake to protect his colleague.
There was much blame assigned after the project failed.
She refused to take the blame for something she didn't do.

Synset Examples

he took the blame for it
it was a bum rap

Related Terms

  • Antonym: credit - praise or approval, especially for an achievement
  • Synonym: fault - responsibility for a bad situation or mistake

Etymology

From Old French *blamer*, from Latin *blasphemare* (to reproach, to speak evil of).
