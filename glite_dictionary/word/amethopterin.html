amethopterin

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛθˈɑptəˌrɪn / (AmE), / ˌæmɛθˈɒptərɪn / (BrE)
A toxic drug that inhibits cell reproduction by blocking folic acid action, used to treat certain cancers, psoriasis, and rheumatoid arthritis.
drug used to treat cancer and autoimmune diseases
medical

Examples

High doses of amethopterin are often used in chemotherapy for certain cancers.
The patient was prescribed amethopterin to manage her rheumatoid arthritis symptoms.
Careful monitoring is required during amethopterin therapy due to its potential side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methotrexate - a drug used to inhibit cell growth, especially in cancer and autoimmune diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'amethys' (not drunk) and '-pterin', a chemical suffix referring to its molecular structure.
