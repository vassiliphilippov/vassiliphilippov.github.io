Epacris impressa

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpækrɪs ɪmˈprɛsə / (AmE), / ɪˈpækrɪs ɪmˈprɛsə / (BrE)
A spindly upright shrub native to southern Australia and Tasmania, having white, rose, or purple-red tubular flowers.
Australian shrub with tubular flowers

Examples

The Epacris impressa is commonly found in the heathlands of southern Australia.
Gardeners admire Epacris impressa for its delicate, colorful blooms during the winter.
Botanists identified the shrub as Epacris impressa by its tubular, pinkish flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Epacris, meaning 'upon summit'; specific epithet 'impressa' meaning 'impressed' or 'indented'.
