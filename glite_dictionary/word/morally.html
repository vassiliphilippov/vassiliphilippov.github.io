morally

1. ADV

/ ˈmɔrəli / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to principles of right and wrong behavior.
in a way relating to moral principles

Examples

She believed it would be morally wrong to lie about the results.
The company was criticized for acting morally irresponsibly in its overseas operations.
Many people consider helping others to be morally commendable.

Synset Examples

morally unjustified

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immorally - in a way that violates moral principles

Etymology

From Latin moralis (moral) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈmɔrəli / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to principles of right and wrong behavior; in a moral manner.
in a moral manner

Examples

She always tries to act morally even when no one is watching.
The company claims to behave morally towards its employees and customers.
Many people believe it is important to live morally upright lives.

Synset Examples

he acted morally under the circumstances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immorally - in a way that is not moral
  • Synonym: ethically - in a manner relating to ethics or moral principles

Etymology

From Latin moralis (moral) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

Synset POS: r
