pilot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
a small auxiliary gas burner that produces a flame to ignite a larger gas burner
small flame that ignites a larger gas burner

Examples

If the pilot goes out, the stove won't light.
You may need to relight the pilot on your water heater after a power outage.
The technician checked the pilot before turning on the main burner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot light - a small gas flame kept burning to ignite larger burners

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
a person with specialized knowledge who guides ships through hazardous or congested waters, especially in or out of ports or harbors
person who guides ships in/out of harbors

Examples

The pilot boarded the vessel to guide it safely into the port.
Without a skilled pilot, the ship could have run aground on the rocks.
Every large ship entering the harbor must be accompanied by a pilot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor master - an official in charge of a harbor

Etymology

From Middle French pilote and Italian pilota (helmsman, guide), ultimately from Greek pēdon (rudder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10453216-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
a person who is licensed to operate and navigate an aircraft in flight
person licensed to fly aircraft

Examples

The pilot made a smooth landing despite poor weather conditions.
After many years as a military pilot, she joined a commercial airline.
Each pilot must pass rigorous training before being allowed to fly alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aviator - a person who flies an aircraft; synonym for 'pilot'
  • Antonym: passenger - a person who is not operating the vehicle but is being transported; antonym for 'pilot' in aviation context

Etymology

From French 'pilote', from Italian 'pilota', likely of Greek origin 'pēdon' (rudder, blade).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

an inclined metal frame attached to the front of a locomotive to clear obstacles from the tracks, also known as a cowcatcher
metal frame in front of a locomotive

Examples

The old steam engine's pilot was dented after hitting fallen branches on the tracks.
Railway workers inspected the pilot to ensure it would safely clear debris ahead of the train.
Some collectors restore antique locomotives with a polished pilot at the front.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowcatcher - a metal frame at the front of a locomotive for clearing the track

5. VERB

/ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
to act as the navigator of a vehicle such as an aircraft, ship, or car, directing and plotting its course and position
to navigate and direct a vehicle's course

Examples

She volunteered to pilot the boat through the narrow channel.
He learned to pilot the aircraft before becoming a commercial airline captain.
The scientist carefully piloted the vessel through treacherous waters during the expedition.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to navigate - to plan and direct the route or course of a ship or other form of transportation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'pilota', probably of Greek origin, meaning 'steersman'.
