coast

1. VERB

/ koʊst / (AmE), / kəʊst / (BrE)
to move easily and without using power, especially by the help of gravity or momentum
move effortlessly, often by gravity

Examples

The cyclist decided to coast down the hill instead of pedaling.
After reaching the peak, the car could coast all the way to the bottom.
He let go of the brakes and began to coast along the smooth road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ koʊst / (AmE), / kəʊst / (BrE)
an act of moving smoothly and effortlessly along a surface while remaining in contact with it, especially downhill or due to momentum
a smooth, effortless movement along a surface

Examples

The children enjoyed a long coast down the hill after the first snowfall.
He took a quick coast on his bike before starting to pedal again.
The sled picked up speed for a thrilling coast across the icy field.

Synset Examples

his slide didn't stop until the bottom of the hill
the children lined up for a coast down the snowy slope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a smooth movement along a surface while remaining in contact with it
  • Synonym: glide - a smooth continuous movement, typically through air or on a surface

3. NOUN

/ koʊst / (AmE), / kəʊst / (BrE)
a slope or hill down which sleds or toboggans may glide, especially in snowy conditions
slope for sledding

Examples

After the snowfall, the children created a coast on the playground hillside.
Every winter, we looked forward to building a coast behind the school for our sleds.
The golf course became a perfect coast once it was blanketed in snow.

Synset Examples

when it snowed they made a coast on the golf course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sledding hill - a hill used for sledding
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ koʊst / (AmE), / kəʊst / (BrE)
the land beside or near the sea or ocean; the shore
shore of the sea or ocean

Examples

After driving for hours, we finally reached the coast and saw the ocean stretching out before us.
Many vacationers prefer to spend their holidays at the coast, where they can enjoy the beach and sea air.
The city lies on the Atlantic coast, making shipping and fishing important industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shore - the land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water
  • Antonym: inland - the interior of a country or region away from the coast or shore

Etymology

From Old French coste (side, rib), from Latin costa (rib, side).
