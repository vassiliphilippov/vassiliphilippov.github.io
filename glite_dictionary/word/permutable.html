permutable

1. ADJ

/ pərˈmjuː.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / pəˈmjuː.tə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be rearranged or have its order changed; capable of being permuted.
capable of being rearranged

Examples

The elements of the set are permutable, so their arrangement is not fixed.
The permutable nature of these tasks allows for greater flexibility in scheduling.
In some puzzles, only permutable pieces can be moved around to form the correct solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permutable - able to be permuted
  • Antonym: fixed - unable to be changed or rearranged

Etymology

From Latin 'permutare' (to change thoroughly, interchange) + '-able'.
