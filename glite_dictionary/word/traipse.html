to traipse

1. VERB

/ ˈtreɪps / (AmE), / ˈtreɪps / (BrE)
to walk or move about casually or aimlessly, often with little energy or enthusiasm
walk or wander aimlessly

Examples

We spent the afternoon traipsing around the city in search of the perfect café.
After dinner, the children traipsed through the muddy fields, getting their shoes dirty.
She didn't mind traipsing from one store to another while looking for bargains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
  • Antonym: march - to walk with regular steps in a specified direction

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Old French 'trepasser' (to trespass).
