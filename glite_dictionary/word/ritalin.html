Ritalin

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtəˌlɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪtəlɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used as a central nervous system stimulant, commonly prescribed to treat attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and narcolepsy.
central nervous system stimulant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Ritalin to help manage his ADHD symptoms.
Many parents have concerns about their children taking Ritalin for hyperactivity.
He noticed an improvement in his concentration after starting Ritalin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methylphenidate - the generic name for Ritalin, a stimulant drug

Etymology

Trademark, proprietary name for methylphenidate.
