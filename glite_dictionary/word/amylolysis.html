amylolysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.ɪˈlɑː.lɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæm.ɪˈlɒ.lɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the process by which starch is converted into sugar, especially by the action of enzymes.
conversion of starch to sugar
medical

Examples

Certain enzymes speed up amylolysis during the digestion of starchy foods.
Researchers study amylolysis to better understand how sugars are produced from grains.
The rate of amylolysis can be affected by temperature and pH levels in the solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrolysis - chemical breakdown of a compound due to reaction with water
  • Degree Higher: fermentation - conversion of carbohydrates to alcohols or acids using microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek amylon (starch) + -lysis (loosening, dissolution).
