assent

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the act of agreeing to a statement, proposal, or idea, typically in a formal or official way
agreement or approval

Examples

Parliament's assent is required before the law can take effect.
She nodded in assent when asked if she agreed with the proposal.
Without their assent, we cannot move forward with the project.

Synset Examples

he gave his assent eagerly
a murmur of acquiescence from the assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consent - permission for something to happen or agreement to do something
  • Antonym: dissent - the holding or expression of opinions that differ with those previously, commonly, or officially held
  • Synonym: acquiescence - reluctant acceptance without protest

Etymology

From Latin 'assensus' (agreement, approval), from 'assentire' (to agree to).

2. VERB

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
to agree or give approval, especially formally or after careful consideration
to agree or give approval

Examples

After a moment of consideration, the judge assented to the proposed settlement.
She assented to the new regulations set by the committee.
The shareholders unanimously assented during the annual general meeting.

Synset Examples

The Maestro assented to the request for an encore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion or to approve
  • Antonym: dissent - to disagree or refuse to approve

Etymology

From Latin assentīrī (to agree, to consent), from ad- (to) + sentīre (to feel, think).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
