to denitrify

1. VERB

/ diˈnaɪtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈnaɪtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove nitrogen or nitrogen compounds from (a substance, especially soil or water), often through a chemical or biological process
remove nitrogen or nitrogen compounds
medical

Examples

Modern wastewater treatment plants are designed to denitrify the effluent before discharge into rivers.
Some bacteria naturally denitrify soil, improving its health for certain crops.
If you want to prevent algal blooms in your aquarium, you must regularly denitrify the water.

Synset Examples

Denitrify the soil

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nitrate - a compound containing nitrogen and oxygen, often found in soil or water
  • Antonym: to nitrify - to convert into a nitrate, or undergo nitrification

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (removal) and nitro- (nitrogen), analogous to related scientific terms.
