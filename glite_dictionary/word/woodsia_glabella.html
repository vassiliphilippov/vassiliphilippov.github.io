Woodsia glabella

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.zi.ə ɡləˈbɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.zi.ə ɡləˈbɛl.ə / (BrE)
A species of rock-inhabiting fern found in Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and eastern Asia.
species of rock-inhabiting fern

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered in Woodsia glabella, thriving in the harsh Arctic climate.
Researchers documented the distribution of Woodsia glabella throughout northern Europe and Asia.
Gardeners appreciate Woodsia glabella for its delicate fronds and adaptation to rocky habitats.

Etymology

Genus name 'Woodsia' honors English botanist Joseph Woods; 'glabella' is Latin for 'smooth'.
