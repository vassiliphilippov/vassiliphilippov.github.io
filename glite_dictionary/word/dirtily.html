dirtily

1. ADV

/ ˈdɝːtɪli / (AmE), / ˈdɜːtɪli / (BrE)
In a dirty, sordid, or unclean manner.
in a dirty or sordid way

Examples

The politician played dirtily to win the campaign.
She looked dirtily at her rival, betraying her contempt.
His hands were stained and dirtily marked after hours of digging.

Synset Examples

as dirtily drunk as usual

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanly - in a clean or neat manner
  • Synonym: sordidly - in a filthy or morally degraded manner

Etymology

Formed from "dirty" (adjective) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).

in a dirty or unclean manner
