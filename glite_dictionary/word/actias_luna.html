Actias luna

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.ti.əs ˈluː.nə / (AmE), / ˈæk.ti.as ˈluː.nə / (BrE)
a large, pale-green moth native to North America, known for its long-tailed hind wings and yellow crescent marks on its forewings
large pale-green North American moth

Examples

We spotted an Actias luna resting on the tree trunk during our night walk.
The wings of the Actias luna shimmer a delicate green in the moonlight.
Researchers studied the lifecycle of the Actias luna for the conservation project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luna moth - common name for Actias luna, a large, pale-green North American moth

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Actias' and species name 'luna' (moon), referring to its pale color and crescent markings.
