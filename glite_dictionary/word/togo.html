Togo

1. PROPN

/ ˈtoʊɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɡəʊ / (BrE)
a republic on the western coast of Africa, bordering the Gulf of Guinea; formerly a French colony
country in West Africa

Examples

She traveled to Togo to conduct research on West African cultures.
Togo gained its independence from France in 1960.
Tourists are drawn to Togo's vibrant markets and coastal scenery.

Etymology

From the name of Lake Togo; adopted in French and English during the colonial period.
