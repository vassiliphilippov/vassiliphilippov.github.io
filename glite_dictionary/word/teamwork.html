teamwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːmˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈtiːmwɜːk / (BrE)
the combined action of a group, especially when effective and efficient, in working towards a common goal
the cooperative work of a group

Examples

Good teamwork is essential for the success of any large project.
The coach praised the players for their excellent teamwork during the game.
Our company's culture strongly encourages teamwork over individual achievement.

Synset Examples

it will take money, good planning and, above all, teamwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperation - the process of working together toward the same end
  • Synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce something
  • Antonym: competition - the activity of striving to gain or win something by defeating others

Etymology

Formed from 'team' + 'work', first recorded in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːmˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈtiːmwɜːk / (BrE)
the combined effort and cooperation of a group of people working together to achieve a common goal
cooperation in a group to achieve a goal

Examples

Strong teamwork helped the company overcome many obstacles during the project.
Effective teamwork requires clear communication between all members.
Athletes often say that teamwork is more important than individual skill.

Synset Examples

Finding a solution is always possible with teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperation - the process of working together for the same end

Etymology

Formed from 'team' + 'work', referring to working as a group.
