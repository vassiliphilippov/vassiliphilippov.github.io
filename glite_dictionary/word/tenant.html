tenant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord, or holds buildings or land by any kind of title
person who occupies land or property

Examples

After signing the new lease, the tenant moved into the apartment the following week.
The tenant is responsible for informing the landlord about any repairs needed in the flat.
In that building, each tenant has access to the shared garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landlord - a person who rents land, a building, or an apartment to a tenant
  • Synonym: leaseholder - a person who holds a lease on a property

Etymology

From Old French 'tenant', present participle of 'tenir' (to hold), from Latin 'tenere' (to hold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
person renting a place to live or work

Examples

The new tenant moved into the apartment last weekend.
As a tenant, you are responsible for paying rent on time.
The landlord visited to discuss repairs with his tenant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landlord - a person who rents out land, a building, or accommodation to a tenant
  • Synonym: lessee - a person who holds the lease of a property; another term for tenant
  • Synonym: occupant - a person who resides in or uses a premises

Etymology

From Old French *tenant* (holding) and Latin *tenere* (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10720166-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a person who pays rent to use land, a building, or other property belonging to someone else
person who rents property

Examples

The tenant called the property manager to report a broken window in the apartment.
Each tenant must sign the lease agreement before moving in.
The building had several long-term tenants who enjoyed living there.

Synset Examples

the landlord can evict a tenant who doesn't pay the rent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landlord - a person who owns and rents out property

Etymology

From Old French tenant (holding), from tenir (to hold), from Latin tenere (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10719850-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
To occupy or live in (a property or land) as a tenant.
occupy as a tenant

Examples

The old farmhouse was tenanted by a family for over fifty years.
Several artists tenant the studios in that converted warehouse.
Many historical buildings are no longer tenanted and have fallen into disrepair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to pay money to use property, especially as a tenant

Etymology

From Old French 'tenant', present participle of 'tenir' (to hold), from Latin 'tenere' (to hold).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
