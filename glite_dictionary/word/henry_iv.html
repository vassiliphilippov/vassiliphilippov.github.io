Henry IV

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ðə fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ðə fɔːθ / (BrE)
King of France from 1589 to 1610, founder of the Bourbon dynasty, notable for granting religious freedom to Protestants.
King of France (1589–1610), Bourbon founder

Examples

As king, Henry IV brought stability to France after years of turmoil.
The Edict of Nantes was issued by Henry IV to promote religious tolerance.
Many historians regard Henry IV as one of France's most effective rulers.
