laudatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔːdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdətəri / (BrE)
expressing praise or approval; giving commendation.
expressing praise or approval

Examples

The critic wrote a laudatory review of the novel, praising its brilliant storytelling.
She received a laudatory letter from management for her outstanding performance.
Most of his comments were laudatory, focusing on the positive aspects of the proposal.

Synset Examples

a laudatory remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praiseful - expressing praise
  • Antonym: derogatory - showing a critical or disrespectful attitude

Etymology

From Latin laudatorius, from laudare (to praise).
