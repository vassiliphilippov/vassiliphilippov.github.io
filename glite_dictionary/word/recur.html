to recur

1. VERB

/ rəˈkɜr / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜː / (BrE)
to return to a subject or idea in thought or in conversation
return to a subject in thought or speech

Examples

No matter how hard she tried, her mind would recur to the accident.
During discussions, he tended to recur to the same topic repeatedly.
Memories of childhood often recur when I'm feeling nostalgic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revisit - to return to a topic or subject again, especially in discussion or thought

Etymology

From Latin recurrere (to run back, return).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00961746-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rəˈkɝː / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜː / (BrE)
to happen or occur again, especially at intervals or repeatedly
happen again; repeat

Examples

If the pain recurs, please contact your doctor immediately.
Certain dreams tend to recur throughout my life.
The same issue recurred several times over the next year.

Synset Examples

This is a recurring story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to do or occur again; to happen more than once
  • Synonym: to occur - to happen; to take place

Etymology

From Latin 'recurrere' meaning 'to run back'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00343988-v
Synset POS: v
