Cornus

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly deciduous shrubs or small trees (dogwoods) native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including dogwoods and cornels.
genus of shrubs and small trees; dogwoods

Examples

Many gardens feature Cornus for their attractive spring blooms and vivid autumn foliage.
The Cornus genus includes popular ornamental trees such as the flowering dogwood.
Botanists have classified dozens of species within the Cornus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - any tree or shrub of the genus Cornus

Etymology

From Latin 'cornus' (cornel, dogwood).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly deciduous shrubs or small trees, commonly known as dogwoods, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of shrubs and small trees (dogwoods)

Examples

The Cornus genus includes the popular flowering dogwood tree found in many gardens.
Several species within Cornus are valued for their colorful branches in winter.
Botanists often study Cornus for its unique flower structure and ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - common name for shrubs and trees of the genus Cornus

Etymology

From Latin *cornus* (dogwood tree).
