to immaterialise

1. VERB

/ ɪˌmæt̬iˈɹiːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˌmatɪˈrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to become immaterial or incorporeal; to render without physical substance.
make immaterial or incorporeal

Examples

The wizard whispered a spell to immaterialise his form and pass unnoticed through the wall.
In some stories, ghosts can immaterialise at will to move between realms.
The character seemed to immaterialise before our eyes, dissolving into thin air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dematerialize - to lose or cause to lose physical substance; become immaterial

Etymology

From 'immaterial' + '-ise', modeled after 'materialise'.
