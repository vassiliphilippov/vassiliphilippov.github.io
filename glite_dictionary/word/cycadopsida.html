Cycadopsida

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / ˌsɪkəˈdɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of palmlike gymnospermous plants, including both living cycads and extinct relatives; sometimes treated as a division (Cycadophyta) or subdivision (Cycadophytina) in classification systems
class of palmlike gymnosperm plants

Examples

The fossils suggest that Cycadopsida were once much more widespread than today.
Modern cycads are the only surviving members of Cycadopsida.
Botanists debate whether Cycadopsida is a true natural group or an artificial assemblage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a plant of the order Cycadales, a surviving member of Cycadopsida

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kykas' (cycad) with '-opsida' (appearance/class).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌkædˈɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˌkædˈɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of palmlike gymnosperms, including the extant order Cycadales and several extinct orders, sometimes considered a natural group in botanical classification.
a class of palmlike gymnosperms

Examples

Fossils of Cycadopsida reveal their prevalence during the Mesozoic era.
Modern Cycadopsida are represented only by the order Cycadales.
Scientists debate whether Cycadopsida should be regarded as a natural group or not.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cycadales - order of gymnosperms comprising cycad species

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek kykas (type of palm) + -opsida (class suffix).
