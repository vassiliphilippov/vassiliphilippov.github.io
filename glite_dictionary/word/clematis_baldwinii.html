Clematis baldwinii

1. PROPN

/ klɪˈmætɪs bælˈdwɪni / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs bɔːlˈdwɪni / (BrE)
A species of erect clematis native to Florida, characterized by pink to purple flowers.
Florida clematis with pink or purple flowers

Examples

Clematis baldwinii is often found in the wetlands and open woods of central and southern Florida.
Gardeners are drawn to Clematis baldwinii for its showy, purple flowers and its ability to attract butterflies.
The conservation status of Clematis baldwinii is of interest due to its limited range in Florida.

Etymology

From the genus Clematis and the species name honoring botanist William Baldwin.
