cantata

1. NOUN

/ kænˈtɑtə / (AmE), / kænˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
a musical composition for voices and orchestra, often based on a religious text and performed without stage action
musical composition for voices and orchestra

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful cantata during the Easter service.
Bach's most famous cantatas are still widely performed today.
The newly composed cantata was inspired by ancient liturgical chants.

Etymology

From Italian 'cantata', feminine of 'cantato' (sung), from Latin 'cantare' (to sing).
