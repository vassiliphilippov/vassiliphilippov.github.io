Hippotragus niger

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈtreɪɡəs ˈnaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈtrɑːɡəs ˈnaɪɡə / (BrE)
a large black antelope of East and Southern Africa, known for its sharp backward-curving horns
large black African antelope with curved horns

Examples

The Hippotragus niger is admired for its striking black coat and impressive horns.
Wildlife photographers travel far to capture images of the elusive Hippotragus niger in its natural habitat.
The park ranger gave a lecture on the conservation status of Hippotragus niger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sable antelope - common name for Hippotragus niger, a large black antelope of Africa

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'tragos' (goat) and Latin 'niger' (black), referring to its appearance.
