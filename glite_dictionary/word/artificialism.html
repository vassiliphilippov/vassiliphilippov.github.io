artificialism

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪfɪʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪfɪʃəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A style of surrealist art developed in Czechoslovakia in 1928 by Jindřich Štyrský and Toyen, characterized by abstract compositions suggestive of dreamlike or subconscious imagery.
Czech surrealist art style, 1928

Examples

Her paintings from the 1930s are marked by the influence of artificialism.
The exhibition focused on the history of artificialism in Central European art.
Jindřich Štyrský is celebrated as a founder of artificialism, a movement blending abstraction and surreal imagery.

Etymology

From French 'artificiel' (artificial) + English suffix '-ism', named by Jindřich Štyrský and Toyen.
