expletive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksplətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛksplɪtɪv / (BrE)
A word or phrase inserted into a sentence that does not add independent meaning but serves to fill out its structure or rhythm.
filler word in sentences

Examples

In the sentence 'There are three books on the table,' the word there is considered an expletive.
The poet used an expletive to maintain the meter of the line.
Sometimes, an expletive can help a sentence flow more naturally, even if it adds no meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filler - a sound or word used to fill a pause or grammatical slot

Etymology

From Latin 'expletivus' (serving to fill out), from 'explere' (to fill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.splɪ.tɪv, ɪkˈspli.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛk.splɪ.tɪv, ɪkˈspliː.tɪv / (BrE)
a word or phrase, often profane or obscene, used to express strong emotion such as anger or surprise
profane or obscene expression

Examples

He muttered an expletive under his breath when he dropped the box.
The movie was edited to remove any expletive language.
She let out a string of expletives when she stubbed her toe.

Synset Examples

expletives were deleted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse word - offensive word used to express anger or pain
  • Synonym: oath - profane or blasphemous word or phrase

Etymology

From Latin expletivus (serving to fill out), from explere (to fill).
