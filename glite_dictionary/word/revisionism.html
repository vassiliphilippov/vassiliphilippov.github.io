revisionism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪʒ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒ.ən.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A policy or theory advocating modifications or moderate changes to established Marxist doctrine, especially by favoring evolutionary and gradual reforms over revolutionary methods.
moderate or evolutionary form of Marxism

Examples

Some critics accused the party of revisionism for abandoning its commitment to revolution.
Lenin was strongly opposed to any form of revisionism within the socialist movement.
The debate between orthodox Marxists and supporters of revisionism shaped early twentieth-century socialist politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - conventional or traditional Marxist belief

Etymology

From 'revisionist' + '-ism'; 'revisionist' from Latin 'revidēre' (to look back, revise).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
(in Marxist contexts) A departure from or re-interpretation of orthodox Marxist theory, often regarded as a dangerous deviation by traditionalists.
re-interpretation of Marxist theory

Examples

The party leadership denounced the speech as revisionism that threatened the movement's foundations.
He was expelled for promoting revisionism rather than adhering strictly to orthodox principles.
Many debates within leftist circles center on accusations of revisionism and betrayal of the revolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - the state of conforming to established doctrine, especially in religion or ideology

Etymology

From English 'revision' + '-ism'.
