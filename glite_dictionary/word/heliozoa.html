Heliozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a group of mostly freshwater protozoa characterized by radial symmetry and long, thin pseudopodia
a group of freshwater protozoa
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, Heliozoa appear as tiny creatures radiating slender arms in all directions.
Many biology students are fascinated by the unique structure of Heliozoa found in freshwater ponds.
Researchers use Heliozoa to study movement and feeding behaviors in protozoa.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēlios' (sun) + 'zōion' (animal), referring to their sun-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːli.oʊˈzoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪəˈzəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of mostly freshwater single-celled protozoa characterized by radiating axopodia, commonly known as 'sun animalcules'.
order of freshwater protozoa

Examples

Heliozoa are known for their spherical bodies and radiating axopodia.
Some scientists studied the feeding habits of Heliozoa in local ponds.
The slow movement of Heliozoa is facilitated by their slender projections.

Etymology

From Greek helios (sun) + zoon (animal), referring to their sun-like appearance.
