skip

1. VERB

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to move by hopping lightly from one foot to the other, often in a playful or lively manner
move with light hops

Examples

The children skipped across the playground, laughing as they went.
She loves to skip down the street on sunny days.
They skipped lightly over the puddles to avoid getting their shoes wet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - to move by jumping on one foot
  • Synonym: jump - to push oneself off a surface and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'skippa' (to skip, leap).

5. NOUN

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
A light, bouncing movement in which steps and hops alternate, typically used by children.
a light gait of alternating steps and hops

Examples

The little girl crossed the playground with a joyful skip.
His skip showed his excitement as he approached the stage.
She moves with a light skip when she's happy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hop - a small jump off one foot
  • Degree Higher: run - move at a speed faster than a walk
  • Degree Higher: jump - propel oneself off the ground with both feet
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00290750-n
Synset POS: n

6. VERB

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to move quickly and lightly by bounding or leaping from one point to another
move by bounding or leaping lightly

Examples

The little girl loves to skip down the path on her way to school.
He skipped across the puddles, barely getting his shoes wet.
Children were skipping and laughing in the sunny park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - to move by jumping on one foot or in a short bouncing way
  • Synonym: jump - to push oneself off a surface and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet
  • Antonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'skopa' (to run, leap).

7. VERB

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to pass over or omit something, especially intentionally, when going through a series or sequence
to pass over or omit

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to include - to make something part of a group or set; the opposite of omitting
