numeration

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of counting or listing numbers in ascending order
act of counting; listing numbers

Examples

The teacher introduced numeration by having the children count aloud together.
Early numeration systems were much simpler than what we use today.
Understanding numeration is fundamental to learning mathematics.

Synset Examples

the counting continued for several hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enumeration - the action of mentioning things one by one; the process of listing
  • Synonym: counting - the act of determining the total number of items

Etymology

From Latin *numeratio* (a counting, numbering), from *numerare* (to count).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnuːməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of naming or designating numbers, often as part of a system
the act of naming numbers

Examples

The teacher explained the concept of numeration to her students using counting blocks.
Proper numeration is essential in early mathematics education.
Different cultures have developed unique systems of numeration throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enumeration - the action of mentioning items one by one; listing

Etymology

From Latin numeratio (a counting, numbering), from numerare (to count, number).
