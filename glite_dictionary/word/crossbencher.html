crossbencher

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbentʃə / (BrE)
a member of a legislature, especially the UK House of Commons or House of Lords, who is not aligned with either the government or the opposition parties
independent legislator, UK parliament
british

Examples

Lord Smith sat as a crossbencher in the House of Lords for many years.
As a crossbencher, she voted on each issue according to her beliefs rather than party lines.
Several crossbenchers played a crucial role in passing the new legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not belonging to or controlled by a political party

Etymology

From 'cross-bench', referring to the benches set across the chamber in the UK parliament.
