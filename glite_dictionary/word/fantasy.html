fantasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
a belief or idea that many people hold but that is not true; an illusion or misconception
false belief or misconception

Examples

Many people have the fantasy that money will bring them happiness.
The idea that fame guarantees success is just a fantasy.
It's a common fantasy that life will be perfect after retirement.

Synset Examples

they have the illusion that I am very wealthy
He fancies himself to be a local celebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief
  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination), via Latin and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
the faculty or activity of imagining things, especially things that are impossible or unlikely in reality
imagination divorced from reality

Examples

As a child, her fantasy worlds were full of dragons and magic.
He escapes the stresses of daily life through fantasy and storytelling.
The novel blends elements of fantasy with harsh realities.

Synset Examples

a schoolgirl fantasy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the ability to form mental images or concepts not present to the senses
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination), via Latin and Old French.

3. VERB

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
to imagine or indulge in fanciful ideas, especially things that are unlikely or impossible
imagine or indulge in fantasies

Examples

She would often fantasy about living on a tropical island.
He tends to fantasy unrealistic scenarios when faced with difficult problems.
Instead of studying, she spent the afternoon fantasying about meeting her favorite actor.

Synset Examples

he is fantasizing when he says he plans to start his own company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
  • Synonym: daydream - to indulge in a series of pleasant thoughts that distract one from the present
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination) via Latin and Old French.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
A genre of fiction characterized by the use of magic, supernatural elements, or imaginative worlds that do not exist in reality.
imaginative fiction genre

Examples

She enjoys reading fantasy novels set in magical kingdoms.
The movie blended elements of fantasy and adventure to captivate audiences.
His first book is a classic work of fantasy filled with dragons and wizards.

Synset Examples

she made a lot of money writing romantic fantasies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
