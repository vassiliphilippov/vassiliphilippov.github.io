dockworker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑkˌwɝːkɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒkˌwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to load and unload ships at a dock or port
laborer at a dock loading/unloading ships

Examples

The dockworker skillfully operated the crane to unload heavy containers from the ship.
During the busy season, every dockworker works overtime to ensure cargo moves efficiently.
A strike by dockworkers delayed shipments at the port for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed at a dock to load and unload ships

Etymology

From 'dock' (a structure for ships) + 'worker'.
