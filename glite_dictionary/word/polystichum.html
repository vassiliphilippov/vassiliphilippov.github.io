Polystichum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈstɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized terrestrial ferns, commonly known as holly ferns, found in diverse regions worldwide.
genus of holly ferns

Examples

Many gardeners favor Polystichum for its resilience and attractive evergreen foliage.
The shaded woodland was carpeted with various species of Polystichum.
Researchers discovered a new Polystichum species in the remote mountain region.

Etymology

From Greek polystichos, meaning 'many rows,' referring to the arrangement of leaflets.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈstɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized terrestrial ferns, especially known for holly ferns, formerly sometimes placed in the family Polypodiaceae.
a genus of terrestrial ferns (holly ferns)

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species within the Polystichum genus.
Many gardeners cultivate Polystichum for its attractive evergreen fronds.
The Polystichum ferns thrive in shaded woodland environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'stichos' (row, line), referring to the arrangement of sori.
