Haemosporidia

1. NOUN

/ hiːmoʊspɔːˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / hiːməʊspɔːˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
A subclass of parasitic protozoa within the phylum Apicomplexa, including organisms that infect the blood of various animals, such as the malaria parasites.
subclass of blood parasites (Apicomplexa)
medical

Examples

The Haemosporidia include the organisms responsible for malaria in humans and animals.
Researchers discovered a new species of Haemosporidia in migratory birds.
Knowledge of Haemosporidia biology is essential for understanding the transmission of blood-borne protozoan diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apicomplexa - phylum of protozoan parasites including Haemosporidia

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + spora (seed) + -idia (diminutive suffix).

2. NOUN

/ hiːməˌspɔːˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / hiːməʊˌspɒˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
A subclass of apicomplexan protozoans, including parasites that infect the blood of vertebrates, such as the organisms causing malaria.
parasitic protozoan subclass causing malaria
medical

Examples

Haemosporidia are responsible for several important diseases in humans and animals, including malaria.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of Haemosporidia to develop better treatments for malaria.
Many birds are infected by Haemosporidia, which are transmitted by blood-sucking insects.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + sporos (seed, spore), referring to blood-borne spores.
