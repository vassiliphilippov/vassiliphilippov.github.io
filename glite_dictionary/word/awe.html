awe

1. NOUN

/ ɑː / (AmE), / ɔː / (BrE)
a feeling of great respect mixed with fear or wonder
great respect or wonder

Examples

The children gazed in awe at the fireworks lighting up the night sky.
She listened to the concert in silent awe of the musicians' talent.
His first glimpse of the mountains filled him with awe.

Synset Examples

he stared over the edge with a feeling of awe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admiration - a feeling of respect and approval
  • Degree Lower: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by threat or danger

Etymology

From Old English *ege* (fear, terror), from Proto-Germanic *agiz*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07525423-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɔ / (AmE), / ɔː / (BrE)
A feeling of deep respect mixed with wonder or fear, often inspired by greatness or beauty.
profound respect mixed with wonder

Examples

The children gazed at the towering mountains in awe.
Her voice filled the audience with awe as she sang the anthem.
He listened in awe to the scientist’s fascinating explanation of the universe.

Synset Examples

the fear of God
the Chinese reverence for the dead
the French treat food with gentle reverence
his respect for the law bordered on veneration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverence - a deep respect for someone or something
  • Synonym: admiration - a feeling of respect and approval
  • Antonym: contempt - lack of respect or reverence

Etymology

From Old English *ege* (fear, terror), related to Old Norse *agi* (fear, dread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07536278-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɔː / (AmE), / ɔː / (BrE)
to fill someone with a feeling of wonder, respect, or fear; to inspire awe in someone
to inspire awe in someone

Examples

The breathtaking view awed everyone who reached the mountain summit.
The magician's talent never failed to awe his audience.
Even as an experienced scientist, she was awed by the complexity of the universe.

Synset Examples

The famous professor awed the undergraduates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amaze - to surprise greatly; fill with wonder
  • Synonym: impress - to make someone feel admiration and respect

Etymology

From Old English *ege* (fear, dread), via Middle English *awe*.
