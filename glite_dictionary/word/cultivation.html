cultivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of preparing and using land for growing crops, especially on a large scale
preparing land for growing crops

Examples

The cultivation of rice requires careful management of water resources.
Advances in cultivation methods have increased food production worldwide.
Many rural communities depend on the cultivation of wheat for their livelihoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farming - the practice of growing crops or raising animals for food
  • Degree Higher: harvesting - collecting mature crops from the fields

Etymology

From Latin 'cultivatio', from 'cultivare' (to till, cultivate).
