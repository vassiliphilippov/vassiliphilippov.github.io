pre-emption

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛm(p)ʃən / (AmE), / priːˈɛmpʃən / (BrE)
The right of a government to seize or appropriate property before others may do so.
government right to appropriate property
legal

Examples

The government exercised its pre-emption rights to acquire the land for highway construction.
By law, pre-emption allows authorities to claim property before private buyers.
Legislation detailing the scope of pre-emption is often debated in parliament.

Etymology

From Latin 'praeemptio', from 'praeemere' (to buy before).

2. NOUN

/ priˈɛm(p)ʃ(ə)n / (AmE), / priˈɛm(p)ʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(law) The judicial principle that federal law overrides or takes precedence over state laws on the same subject.
supremacy of federal law over state law
legal

Examples

The doctrine of pre-emption was cited when the state law conflicted with federal regulations.
Under the principle of pre-emption, state governments cannot pass laws that undermine federal statutes.
The Supreme Court's ruling on pre-emption clarified the balance of power between federal and state authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supremacy clause - the clause in the U.S. Constitution establishing federal law as supreme

Etymology

From Latin 'praeemere' (to buy before); sense of legal precedence developed in U.S. law.

3. NOUN

/ priˈɛmpʃən / (AmE), / priˈɛmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the right to purchase something before it is offered to others, often by law or agreement
right to buy before others
legal

Examples

The company exercised its pre-emption rights to acquire the land before it was put on the market.
Shareholders were given pre-emption rights when the new shares became available.
Many countries have laws granting government pre-emption in cases involving national security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right of first refusal - right to buy something before it is sold to others

Etymology

From Latin praeemptio (buying in advance), from prae- (before) + emere (to buy).

4. NOUN

/ priˈɛm(p)ʃən / (AmE), / priˈɛmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of acquiring or appropriating something before others; the right or action of taking priority over others in claiming something
prior appropriation; right of priority

Examples

The company's pre-emption of new technology gave it a strong competitive advantage.
Government pre-emption of the land prevented any private development in the area.
The pre-emption of tickets by sponsors left little for the general public.

Synset Examples

the preemption of bandwidth by commercial interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - the state or right of coming before others
  • Synonym: reservation - the act of keeping something back or setting it aside for future use

Etymology

From Latin praeemere, 'to buy before' (prae- 'before' + emere 'to buy'), via French préemption.
