John Browning

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈbraʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈbraʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
(1855–1926) A United States inventor renowned for developing firearms such as automatic pistols, repeating rifles, and machine guns, including the Peacemaker.
U.S. firearms inventor (1855–1926)

Examples

The designs of John Browning revolutionized the development of modern firearms.
Collectors highly value rifles modeled after John Browning's original blueprints.
Many military weapons today still bear the imprint of John Browning's engineering.
