Ivan IV Vasilievich

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈvɑn ðə ˈfɔrθ væˌsɪlˈjɛvɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvan ðə ˈfɔːθ væˌsɪlˈjɛvɪtʃ / (BrE)
the first czar of Russia, ruling from 1547 to 1584, known as Ivan the Terrible
the first czar of Russia (Ivan the Terrible)

Examples

Ivan IV Vasilievich established a centralized Russian state and expanded its territory significantly.
Many historians still debate the legacy of Ivan IV Vasilievich, whose rule was marked by both innovation and brutality.
The reign of Ivan IV Vasilievich is often remembered for his introduction of autocracy to Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ivan the Terrible - nickname for Ivan IV, the first czar of Russia

Etymology

Ivan from Russian *Иван* (Ivan), Vasilievich from patronymic meaning 'son of Vasilii'.
