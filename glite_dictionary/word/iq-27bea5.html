I.Q.

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ˈkju / (AmE), / aɪ ˈkjuː / (BrE)
a measure of a person's intelligence, typically expressed as a number based on standardized tests
measure of intelligence (score)

Examples

Her I.Q. was tested as part of the university's entrance process.
Many people mistakenly believe that I.Q. scores alone determine future success.
The psychologist explained how a child's I.Q. can change as they grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence quotient - formal or full name for I.Q.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'intelligence quotient', from Latin 'intelligentia' (understanding) and Medieval Latin 'quotientem' (dividing).
