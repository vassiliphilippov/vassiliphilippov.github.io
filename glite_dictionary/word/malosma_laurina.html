Malosma laurina

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɒzmə lɔːˈriːnə / (AmE), / məˈlɒzmə lɔːˈriːnə / (BrE)
An aromatic evergreen shrub native to California, with paniculate leaves and whitish berries; also known as laurel sumac.
aromatic California shrub (laurel sumac)

Examples

Malosma laurina thrives in the chaparral regions of Southern California.
The aromatic leaves of Malosma laurina are easily recognizable along hiking trails.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Malosma laurina in wildfire recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laurel sumac - common name for Malosma laurina, a Californian shrub

Etymology

Malosma: from Greek 'malos' (apple) and 'osma' (smell); laurina: referring to laurel-like leaves.
