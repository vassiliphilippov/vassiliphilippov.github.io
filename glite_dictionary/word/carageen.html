carageen

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈɡin / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈɡiːn / (BrE)
a dark purple edible seaweed found on the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, often used in food and medicine
edible Atlantic seaweed

Examples

Traditional Irish desserts sometimes use carageen to create a jelly-like texture.
The chef simmered the carageen with milk to thicken the pudding.
Harvesting carageen from the rocky shore requires careful attention to tides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish moss - another name for carageen, edible Atlantic seaweed
  • Degree Higher: seaweed - general term for marine algae like carageen

Etymology

From Irish 'carraigín' (little rock), referring to the rocky habitat of the seaweed.
