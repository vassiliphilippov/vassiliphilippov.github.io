Congo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a major river in central Africa, flowing through the Democratic Republic of the Congo into the Atlantic Ocean; one of the world's longest rivers
major river in central Africa

Examples

The Congo is essential for transportation and trade in central Africa.
Wildlife flourishes along the banks of the Congo.
The explorers navigated the treacherous rapids of the Congo.

Etymology

Named after the Kongo people, an ethnic group living near its mouth.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɑŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A republic in west-central Africa, officially known as the Republic of the Congo, which gained independence from France in 1960.
country in west-central Africa

Examples

The president of Congo delivered a speech at the United Nations yesterday.
Many international organizations provide aid to rural areas in Congo.
After achieving independence in 1960, Congo has experienced significant political changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic Republic of the Congo - a neighboring, larger country in central Africa, also sometimes referred to as 'Congo'

Etymology

Named after the Congo River, which derives from the Kongo people, an ethnic group in the region.

3. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A country in central Africa, officially known as the Democratic Republic of the Congo, which gained independence from Belgium in 1960.
country in central Africa

Examples

The Congo is rich in natural resources but has faced decades of political instability.
Jane traveled to the Congo to work with a humanitarian organization.
Many rare species of wildlife can be found in the forests of the Congo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Democratic Republic of the Congo - the full official name of the country known as Congo
  • Degree Lower: Republic of the Congo - neighboring country to the west, also known as Congo-Brazzaville

Etymology

From Kongo, the name of the local ethnic group and kingdom; adopted by European colonizers for the river and region.
