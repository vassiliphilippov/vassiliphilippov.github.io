solidarity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑː.ləˈdɛr.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɒl.ɪˈdær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
Unity or agreement of feeling or action, especially among individuals with a common interest; mutual support within a group.
unity or mutual support within a group

Examples

The workers showed strong solidarity during the strike, supporting each other's demands.
International solidarity is crucial when responding to humanitarian crises.
A feeling of solidarity developed among the volunteers as they worked together toward a common goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
  • Antonym: division - the state of being divided; disunity

Etymology

From French 'solidarité', from Latin 'solidus' (solid, whole).
