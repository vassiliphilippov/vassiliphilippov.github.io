nimbus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪm.bəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪm.bəs / (BrE)
A ring or halo of radiant light drawn around the head of a holy figure, especially in art.
halo or radiant light around a saint's head

Examples

In the painting, the saint was shown with a bright nimbus around his head.
Medieval artists often depicted holy figures with a golden nimbus.
The nimbus in the mosaic glowed, setting the saint apart from other figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halo - A circle of light appearing to surround a luminous body, especially depicted around a saint's head

Etymology

From Latin 'nimbus' meaning 'cloud' or 'halo.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪm.bəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪm.bəs / (BrE)
A dark grey cloud that brings rain; a type of rain cloud.
a rain-bearing dark cloud

Examples

A dark nimbus hung over the city, threatening a heavy downpour by evening.
Artists often depict gods with a glowing nimbus, but here it refers to a rain cloud.
As the nimbus swept in, the temperature dropped and rain began to fall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cumulonimbus - a dense, towering, vertical cloud associated with thunderstorms and heavy precipitation
  • Synonym: stratus - a low-level cloud forming a uniform, grey layer, often bringing light rain or drizzle

Etymology

From Latin 'nimbus' meaning 'cloud' or 'rainstorm'.
