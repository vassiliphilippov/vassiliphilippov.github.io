to pollinate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːləˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪneɪt / (BrE)
to fertilize (a plant or flower) by transferring pollen to it, especially to the stigma of a flower
fertilize by transferring pollen

Examples

Bees help to pollinate many of the crops that humans depend on for food.
Gardeners often use small brushes to pollinate flowers in greenhouses.
Wind can also pollinate certain plants by carrying pollen from one flower to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to cause to bear fruit or young by introducing male reproductive material

Etymology

From Latin 'pollen' (fine flour, dust) + '-ate' (verbal suffix), meaning to supply with pollen.
