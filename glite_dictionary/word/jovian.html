Jovian

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊ.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to the planet Jupiter; resembling or characteristic of Jupiter.
relating to Jupiter (planet)

Examples

The atmosphere of a Jovian planet is mostly composed of hydrogen and helium.
Astronomers discovered new Jovian moons orbiting Jupiter last year.
The Jovian system contains several large and fascinating satellites.

Synset Examples

Jovian satellites

Etymology

From Latin Iovius, from Iovis (Jupiter).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊ.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.ən / (BrE)
Of or relating to the Roman god Jupiter, especially in characteristics considered grand or majestic.
relating to the Roman god Jupiter

Examples

The play described the king's Jovian presence as awe-inspiring and powerful.
Legends tell of Jovian wrath destroying entire cities with thunderbolts.
Her Jovian demeanor commanded respect from everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

Jovian thunderbolts
Jovian wrath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jovial - friendly and cheerful; originally derived from Jupiter's characteristics

Etymology

From Latin Iovianus, from Iovis (Jupiter).
