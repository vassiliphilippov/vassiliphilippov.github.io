basswood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæswʊd / (AmE), / ˈbæswʊd / (BrE)
the soft, light-colored wood from linden trees, commonly used in making crates, boxes, carvings, and millwork
soft, light wood from linden trees

Examples

They often use basswood for carving because it is easy to shape and holds fine details.
The crates were made from basswood due to its lightweight and sturdy qualities.
Many musical instrument makers prefer basswood for producing electric guitar bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linden - a type of tree that produces basswood

Etymology

From 'bass', an alteration of 'bast' (the inner bark of the linden tree) + 'wood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæswʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɑːswʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the genus Tilia, having heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant yellowish flowers, or its soft, light timber often used in carving and woodworking.
deciduous tree of genus Tilia; its wood

Examples

The carver chose basswood for his intricate sculpture because of its soft texture.
In early summer, the basswood blooms fill the air with a sweet fragrance.
Many musical instrument makers prefer basswood due to its light weight and even grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linden - alternative name for trees of the genus Tilia

Etymology

From 'bass' (an alteration of 'bast', referring to the fibrous inner bark) + 'wood'.
