naif

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈiːf, ˈneɪf / (AmE), / naˈiːf, ˈneɪf / (BrE)
A person who is naive or inexperienced, especially in matters of the world or society.
a naive or inexperienced person

Examples

Despite his education, John remained a naif in the ways of business.
The shrewd politician easily manipulated the naif newcomer in parliament.
She played the role of a naif discovering the world for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience or sophistication
  • Synonym: innocent - lacking worldly experience or guile

Etymology

From French naïf (naive), originally from Latin nativus (native, natural).

2. ADJ

/ naɪˈiːf / (AmE), / naɪˈiːf / (BrE)
Showing unaffected simplicity, innocence, or lack of worldly experience; unsophisticated.
innocent or unsophisticated

Examples

He was a naif in the business world, trusting everyone he met.
Her naif charm made her instantly likable to the group.
As a naif artist, she approached her work with a fresh and untrained perspective.

Synset Examples

a teenager's naive ignorance of life
the naive assumption that things can only get better
this naive simple creature with wide friendly eyes so eager to believe appearances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - showing lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture

Etymology

From French naïf (naive), from Latin nativus (native, natural).
