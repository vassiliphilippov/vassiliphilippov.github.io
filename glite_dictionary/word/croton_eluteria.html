Croton eluteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊtən ɪˈluːtəriə / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊtən ɪˈluːtəriə / (BrE)
A West Indian shrub with aromatic bark, used sometimes in medicine and flavoring.
West Indian shrub with aromatic bark

Examples

Traditional medicine sometimes uses Croton eluteria bark for its aromatic qualities.
Scientists collected samples of Croton eluteria during their fieldwork in the Caribbean.
The essential oils derived from Croton eluteria are prized for their unique fragrance.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Croton with specific epithet from Greek eluteros (free), often given to plants of uncertain affinity.
