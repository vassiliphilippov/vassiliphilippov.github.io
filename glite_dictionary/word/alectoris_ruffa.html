Alectoris ruffa

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktərɪs ˈrʌfə / (AmE), / əˈlɛktərɪs ˈrʌfə / (BrE)
a species of partridge native to western Europe, noted for its red legs and distinctive plumage
European partridge with red legs

Examples

The Alectoris ruffa is a popular game bird in Spain and France.
Birdwatchers often seek to spot the distinctive Alectoris ruffa in rural European landscapes.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Alectoris ruffa in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-legged partridge - another common name for Alectoris ruffa

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Alectoris and species epithet ruffa (Latin for 'red' or 'reddish').
