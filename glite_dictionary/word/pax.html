pax

1. NOUN

/ pæks / (AmE), / pæks / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a greeting or object signifying Christian love and peace, exchanged among worshippers during the Eucharist.
Eucharistic sign of peace in Catholic ritual

Examples

During Mass, the congregation exchanged the pax as a symbol of unity.
The ornate silver pax was passed around during the service.
He explained the history of the pax used in the medieval Catholic Church.

Etymology

From Latin 'pax' meaning 'peace'.
