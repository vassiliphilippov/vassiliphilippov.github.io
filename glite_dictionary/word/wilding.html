wilding

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
A wild, uncultivated plant, especially a wild apple or crabapple tree.
wild, uncultivated plant or tree

Examples

The old orchard was surrounded by wildings that had grown from fallen seeds.
She picked a basket of tart apples from the wilding near the stream.
Many wildings flourish in abandoned fields, untouched by human cultivation.

Etymology

From Middle English 'wilding', meaning a wild apple or fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
a violent rampage, especially one involving group attacks and sexual assaults, typically by young men
violent group rampage, often with sexual assaults

Examples

Reports of wilding in the city park spread panic among local residents.
The film depicts a group of teenagers going on a wilding that terrorizes the neighborhood.
The media coverage of the alleged wilding case sparked heated debate across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampage - a period of violent and uncontrollable behavior
  • Synonym: riot - a violent public disorder

Etymology

Derived from 'wild' + '-ing'; usage popularized in late 20th-century American media.
