Truncocolumella

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrʌŋkoʊkəˈluːmələ / (AmE), / ˌtrʌŋkəʊkəˈluːmɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Rhizopogonaceae, consisting of truffle-like species found in certain regions.
genus of fungi (Rhizopogonaceae)

Examples

The species Truncocolumella citrina is known for its subterranean, truffle-like fruiting bodies.
Some mycologists specialize in studying rare genera such as Truncocolumella.
Fungi in the genus Truncocolumella are associated with certain tree roots in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin truncus (trunk, stem) and columella (little column), referring to features of the fruiting body.
