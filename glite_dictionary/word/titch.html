titch

1. NOUN

/ tɪtʃ / (AmE), / tɪtʃ / (BrE)
a small or tiny person or creature, especially a child
small or tiny person or creature
informal british

Examples

Although he's a titch, his personality fills the whole room.
My little sister used to be such a titch when she started school.
Compared to the other players, Sam looked like a real titch on the field.

Synset Examples

The puppy is a titch, but full of energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - a very small person or creature
  • Antonym: giant - an extremely large person or creature

Etymology

Probably from dialectal English titch, a pet form of 'Tiny' or nickname, first recorded in early 20th century Britain.

2. NOUN

/ tɪtʃ / (AmE), / tɪtʃ / (BrE)
an informal term for a very small person, especially a child or someone of short stature.
a very small person (informal)
informal british

Examples

Even as a titch, she was full of confidence and energy.
You don’t have to be a titch to play on the children’s team.
He’s always been a bit of a titch compared to his older brothers.

Synset Examples

He's such a titch; he barely reaches my shoulder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runt - the smallest or weakest of a group, especially an animal litter
  • Antonym: giant - a person of very great size or stature

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin; popularized in early 20th-century British English.
