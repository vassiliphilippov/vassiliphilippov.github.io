Piedmont

1. PROPN

/ ˈpidˌmɑnt / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt / (BrE)
a region of northwestern Italy, including the Po Valley, known for its rich history, wine production, and Alpine landscapes
region of northwestern Italy

Examples

The wines of Piedmont are famous worldwide, especially Barolo and Barbaresco.
We traveled to Piedmont to explore its picturesque villages and enjoy the Alpine scenery.
Truffles from Piedmont are highly prized in gourmet cuisine.

Etymology

From Italian 'Piemonte', from Latin 'pedemontium' meaning 'at the foot of the mountains'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpiˌɑdmɑnt / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt / (BrE)
a plateau region in the eastern United States, situated between the Atlantic coastal plain and the Appalachian Mountains, extending from New Jersey to Alabama
plateau region between the coastal plain and Appalachians

Examples

The fertile soil of the Piedmont region makes it ideal for agriculture.
Many major cities, including Atlanta, are located within the Piedmont.
After leaving the Appalachian foothills, the river flows through the Piedmont before reaching the coast.

Etymology

From Italian 'piemonte,' meaning 'foot of the mountain.'
