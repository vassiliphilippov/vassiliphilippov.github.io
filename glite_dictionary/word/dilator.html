dilator

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈleɪtər / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a surgical instrument used to widen or expand an opening or organ in the body
surgical instrument for widening openings
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a dilator to gently stretch the narrowed blood vessel.
Nasal dilators are sometimes used to improve airflow in patients with breathing difficulties.
After the incision, the doctor inserted a dilator to widen the cervical canal.

Etymology

From Latin 'dilatator' (that which spreads out), from 'dilatare' meaning 'to dilate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.leɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a muscle, nerve, or device that causes a body part to become wider or more open
muscle, nerve, or device that widens a body part
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist explained how the dilator muscles in the eye control pupil size.
Certain drugs affect the dilator nerves, making blood vessels wider.
After the surgery, the doctor used a dilator to gradually open the passageway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constrictor - a muscle that narrows a body part

Etymology

From Latin 'dilator' (that which widens), from 'dilatare' (to spread out, widen).

3. NOUN

/ daɪˈleɪtər / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes dilation of a part of the body, especially blood vessels or the pupils.
drug causing dilation
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a dilator to help open the patient's airways.
Eye drops containing a dilator were used before the eye examination.
Some medications act as a dilator to improve blood flow in certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasodilator - a drug that causes dilation of blood vessels
  • Antonym: constrictor - a drug or agent that causes narrowing (constriction) of a part of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'dilatare' (to spread out, enlarge) via Medieval Latin 'dilator'.
