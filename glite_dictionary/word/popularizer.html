popularizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who makes a subject, idea, or activity appealing or accessible to the general public
someone who makes ideas accessible to the public

Examples

Carl Sagan was a renowned popularizer of science, making complex topics understandable to everyone.
The museum hired a skilled popularizer to help attract more visitors with engaging exhibits.
Many credit her as the popularizer of yoga in the Western world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promoter - a person who actively supports or encourages something
  • Antonym: specialist - a person concentrating primarily on a particular subject or activity

Etymology

From 'popularize' (to make popular) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
