Benzedrine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbenzɪˌdriːn / (AmE), / ˈbenzɪˌdriːn / (BrE)
A brand name for a form of amphetamine, formerly used as a stimulant and in treating certain medical conditions.
brand of amphetamine; stimulant drug

Examples

After taking Benzedrine, he felt unusually energetic and alert for hours.
Benzedrine was once commonly prescribed to treat narcolepsy and depression.
The novel's protagonist becomes addicted to Benzedrine as he tries to keep up with his demanding job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphetamine - a synthetic stimulant drug related to Benzedrine

Etymology

From 'benz-' (referring to benzene) and 'edrine', as in amphetamine.
