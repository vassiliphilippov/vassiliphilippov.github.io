Hypericum pyramidatum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌpɪrɪəˈdeɪtəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌpɪrɪəˈdeɪtəm / (BrE)
A perennial shrub of the St. John's wort family, characterized by large star-shaped yellow flowers arranged in narrowly pyramidal cymes.
perennial shrub with yellow star-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with vibrant Hypericum pyramidatum bushes, their yellow flowers standing out among the greenery.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Hypericum pyramidatum due to its similarity to other St. John's wort species.
I planted Hypericum pyramidatum along the fence because its star-shaped blooms attract butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. John's wort - common name for various plants of the genus Hypericum

Etymology

From the genus name Hypericum (from Greek hyperikon), and the Latin 'pyramidatum' meaning 'pyramidal'.
