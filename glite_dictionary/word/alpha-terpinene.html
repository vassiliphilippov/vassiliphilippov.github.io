alpha-Terpinene

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə tərˈpɪniːn / (AmE), / ˌal.fə tɜːˈpɪniːn / (BrE)
an isomeric hydrocarbon found in cardamon, marjoram oils, and other natural sources; used in perfumes and flavorings.
isomeric hydrocarbon from natural oils
medical

Examples

alpha-Terpinene is commonly extracted from essential oils in marjoram and cardamom plants.
Cosmetic companies use alpha-Terpinene as a fragrance ingredient in soaps and lotions.
The presence of alpha-Terpinene adds a distinctive aroma to some herbal teas.

Synset Examples

α-Terpinene is a perfume and flavoring chemical used in the cosmetics and food industries.

Etymology

From 'alpha-' meaning the first variant and 'terpinene', from 'terpene', denoting a class of hydrocarbons.
