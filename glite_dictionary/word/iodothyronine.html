iodothyronine

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.oʊdəʊˈθaɪrəˌniːn / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊdəʊˈθaɪ.rəˌniːn / (BrE)
Any of a group of compounds derived from thyronine by the addition of iodine atoms, especially those forming the core structure of thyroid hormones.
iodine-containing thyroid hormone compound
medical

Examples

Triiodothyronine is a biologically active iodothyronine produced by the thyroid gland.
Researchers studied the effects of different iodothyronines on metabolic processes.
Levels of each iodothyronine must be precisely regulated for normal development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyronine - a non-iodinated amino acid forming the basis of thyroid hormones

Etymology

From 'iodine' and 'thyronine', the latter from Greek 'thyra' (door) referring to the thyroid gland.
