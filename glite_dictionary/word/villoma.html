villoma

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈloʊmə / (AmE), / vɪˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor formed from epithelial tissue, typically appearing as a rounded mass.
benign epithelial tumor
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a villoma discovered during the patient's examination.
A villoma is often diagnosed through imaging or biopsy.
Most villomas are benign and rarely become cancerous.

Etymology

From Latin 'villus' (tuft of hair) and '-oma' (tumor, swelling).
