engraver

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡreɪvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪvə / (BrE)
a skilled worker who carves or etches designs, writing, or images onto surfaces such as metal, wood, or glass
person who carves or etches designs

Examples

The engraver meticulously carved the intricate design into the silver plate.
She hired an experienced engraver to personalize the trophy with the winner’s name.
Many antique books have beautiful illustrations created by a master engraver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etcher - a person who uses acid to engrave a design onto a surface
  • Synonym: sculptor - an artist who carves or shapes materials

Etymology

From the verb 'engrave', which comes from Middle French 'engraver', from 'en-' (in) + 'graver' (to grave, carve, engrave).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡreɪvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪvə / (BrE)
a person who makes prints from engraved plates or blocks, especially as an artist or craftsperson
person who prints from engraved plates

Examples

The museum featured works by a renowned engraver from the 18th century.
As an engraver, she specialized in recreating landscapes on metal plates.
Many famous prints were produced by a master engraver in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: printmaker - an artist who makes prints by various processes

Etymology

From English 'engrave' + '-er', denoting one who engraves or prints from engravings.
