marker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkə / (BrE)
an object that is conspicuous and used to indicate a position, boundary, or route
conspicuous object used to mark something

Examples

We placed a marker at the trailhead so hikers would know where to start.
The red flag acted as a marker for the finish line of the race.
Buoys serve as markers to guide ships through dangerous waters.

Synset Examples

the buoys were markers for the channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - an object that gives information or guidance
  • Synonym: indicator - a thing that indicates the state or level of something

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkə / (BrE)
a pen with a broad, often felt tip, used for making bold marks or writing on various surfaces
pen with a broad tip for writing or drawing

Examples

She labeled each box using a black marker.
The teacher asked the students to use a marker to highlight the main points.
I accidentally left the cap off my marker and it dried out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felt-tip pen - a pen with a tip made of felt, used for writing or drawing

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkə / (BrE)
a symbol or sign that distinguishes something or shows ownership or identity
distinguishing symbol or sign

Examples

The cattle had a unique marker on their ears to distinguish them from neighboring herds.
The old building still bears the marker left by its original owners over a century ago.
His initials served as a marker on all his personal belongings.

Synset Examples

the owner's mark was on all the sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a visible impression or symbol used to identify or distinguish
  • Synonym: label - a piece of paper or other material attached to an object to identify or describe it
  • Synonym: identifier - a thing that identifies something or someone

Etymology

From mark + -er, meaning one that marks or identifies.
