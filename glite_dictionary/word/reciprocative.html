reciprocative

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəkətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkətɪv / (BrE)
moving alternately backward and forward; characterized by recurring movement in opposite directions
alternately moving backward and forward

Examples

The engine uses a reciprocative pump to move fuel efficiently.
Many old sewing machines rely on a reciprocative motion to stitch fabric.
The mechanic explained how the reciprocative parts converted rotary movement into linear action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocating - moving alternately backward and forward
  • Synonym: oscillating - moving or swinging back and forth at a regular speed

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way) + -ive (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkətɪv / (BrE)
given, done, or owed mutually between two or more parties; involving reciprocal action or response
mutual; involving reciprocal action

Examples

The reciprocative agreement between the companies benefited both parties equally.
Their friendship was marked by reciprocative acts of kindness and support.
A reciprocative arrangement ensures that all members share both responsibilities and rewards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - mutual; done or felt by each toward the other

Etymology

From Latin reciprocativus, from reciprocatus (past participle of reciprocāre 'to move back and forth').

Synset POS: s
