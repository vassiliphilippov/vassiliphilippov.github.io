parr

1. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
a young salmon, typically up to two years old, before it becomes a smolt
young salmon up to two years old

Examples

The river is an important habitat for parr before they migrate to the sea.
Scientists studied the diet of parr in different streams across the region.
Fisheries managers monitor the number of parr each spring to predict future salmon runs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: smolt - a young salmon or trout migrating to the sea for the first time

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from Scottish Gaelic or Old Norse.

2. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
a young fish, especially a salmon or trout, at a stage when it has distinctive markings and before it becomes a smolt
young salmon or trout with distinctive markings

Examples

The river is full of parr during the spring months.
Fishermen monitor the growth of parr to study salmon populations.
The distinct markings help identify the parr before they mature into smolts.

Etymology

Of unknown origin; possibly from northern English dialect.
