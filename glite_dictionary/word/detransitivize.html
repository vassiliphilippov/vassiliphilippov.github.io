to detransitivize

1. VERB

/ diːˈtrænzɪˌtɪvaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈtrænsɪˌtɪvaɪz / (BrE)
(Linguistics) To make a verb intransitive, or to become intransitive, usually by removing its direct object.
make or become intransitive (linguistics)

Examples

Linguists sometimes detransitivize verbs to analyze their syntactic properties.
In this example, you can detransitivize the verb by deleting its direct object.
The process to detransitivize a verb is common in language change and grammar studies.

Synset Examples

removing the object will intransitivize the verbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intransitivize - to make (a verb) intransitive

Etymology

Formed from de- (expressing removal) + transitivize (to make transitive).
