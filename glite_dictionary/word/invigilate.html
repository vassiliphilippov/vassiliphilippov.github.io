to invigilate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɪdʒɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪdʒɪleɪt / (BrE)
to supervise students during an examination to prevent cheating or misconduct
supervise exams to prevent cheating
british

Examples

It is the teacher’s responsibility to invigilate during the final examinations.
Three staff members invigilated the room to ensure there was no cheating.
She will be invigilating for the mathematics test next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proctor - to supervise or monitor exams, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin vigilare (to watch), via Latin invigilare (stay awake, keep watch).
