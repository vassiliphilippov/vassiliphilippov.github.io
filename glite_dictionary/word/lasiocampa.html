Lasiocampa

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪzi.oʊˈkæmpə / (AmE), / ˌleɪsi.əˈkæmpə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Lasiocampidae, comprising moths commonly known as eggars.
genus of moths (type of Lasiocampidae)

Examples

The larvae of Lasiocampa species are known for their distinctive hairy appearance.
Scientists discovered a new Lasiocampa moth in the northern forests.
The Lasiocampa genus is widespread across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Greek lasios (hairy) + kampē (caterpillar).
