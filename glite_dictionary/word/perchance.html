perchance

1. ADV

/ pərˈtʃæns / (AmE), / pəˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) by chance; perhaps; possibly.
(archaic) perhaps; by chance
archaic

Examples

Perchance he will come to the feast, though I doubt it.
Wander through the city streets, perchance you'll discover something wonderful.
She read the letter aloud, hoping perchance to find a clue within its lines.

Synset Examples

perhaps she will call tomorrow
we may possibly run into them at the concert
it may peradventure be thought that there never was such a time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perhaps - possibly; maybe
  • Synonym: possibly - by chance; perhaps
  • Antonym: certainly - definitely; surely

Etymology

From Old French 'par cheance' (by chance), from Latin 'per' (by) + 'cadentia' (falling, chance).

2. ADV

/ pərˈtʃæns / (AmE), / pəˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
(archaic) By chance; perhaps.
by chance; perhaps (archaic)
archaic

Examples

Do you think, perchance, we might see her at the ball tonight?
He wandered the gardens, perchance to find inspiration for his latest poem.
I shall wait here, perchance you change your mind.

Synset Examples

To sleep, perchance to dream …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perhaps - used to express uncertainty or possibility
  • Synonym: maybe - used to express possibility

Etymology

From Middle English perchaunce, from Old French par chance (by chance).
