glitz

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪts / (AmE), / ɡlɪts / (BrE)
attractive but often superficial or tasteless display; flashy showiness that emphasizes style over substance
tasteless showiness or flashy display

Examples

The hotel lobby was full of glitz but lacked real charm.
He was drawn to the glitz and glamour of Hollywood.
All that glitz can't hide the poor quality of the product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamour - an exciting and often illusory attractiveness
  • Synonym: gaudiness - showiness or tastelessness in decoration
  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish

Etymology

Probably from Yiddish *glits* (brightness, sparkle), related to German *glitzern* (to glitter).
