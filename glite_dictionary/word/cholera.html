cholera

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɚə / (AmE), / ˈkɒlərə / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease of the small intestine, typically caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae and characterized by severe diarrhea and dehydration, usually spread through contaminated water or food
infectious disease causing severe diarrhea
medical

Examples

An outbreak of cholera swept through the village after the flood contaminated the water supply.
Doctors rushed to treat hundreds of cholera patients in the emergency clinic.
Good sanitation is essential to prevent cholera epidemics in affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysentery - infection causing severe diarrhea with blood, often due to unsanitary water or food

Etymology

From Latin 'cholera', from Greek 'kholera' ('bilious disease'), related to 'kholē' (bile).
