mocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑkər / (AmE), / ˈmɒkə / (BrE)
a person who makes fun of someone or something, often in a contemptuous or derisive manner
a person who mocks or jeers

Examples

The mocker in the back row kept interrupting the speaker with sarcastic comments.
She refused to let the mocker's laughter undermine her confidence.
No one likes to be around a constant mocker who belittles every idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeerer - a person who jeers or ridicules
  • Synonym: taunter - a person who taunts others
  • Antonym: admirer - a person who has respect or fondness for someone

Etymology

From mock + -er, denoting an agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈmɒkə / (BrE)
A long-tailed grey-and-white songbird of the southern United States, known for its ability to mimic the songs of other birds.
songbird that mimics other birds

Examples

We saw a mocker perched on the fence, echoing the calls of other birds nearby.
The mocker's remarkable mimicry makes it easy to mistake for several different species.
A mocker is often heard before it is seen, thanks to its variety of songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingbird - a songbird known for imitating other birds' songs
