Tammany

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæməni / (AmE), / ˈtæməni / (BrE)
a powerful political organization within the Democratic Party in New York City during the late 1800s and early 1900s, notorious for corruption and political control
notorious New York political organization

Examples

Tammany was known for its widespread influence in New York City politics during the 19th century.
He accused his opponent of employing tactics reminiscent of Tammany corruption.
Many reforms were introduced to curb the power once wielded by Tammany in the city.

Etymology

Named after Tammany Hall, itself named for Tamanend, a Native American leader.
