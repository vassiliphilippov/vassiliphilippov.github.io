unarticulated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of sounds) uttered without the use of normal words or syllables; not clearly pronounced or expressed in speech.
not spoken in clear words or syllables

Examples

His response was an unarticulated grunt of frustration.
The child's cry was unarticulated, filled with emotion but no real words.
She made an unarticulated sound, unable to express how she felt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: articulate - able to express oneself clearly or to pronounce words clearly

Etymology

From un- (not) + articulate (clearly spoken or expressed).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
not composed of segments joined by joints; lacking articulations between the parts.
not having joints between segments

Examples

The worm's body is unarticulated, lacking any obvious divisions or segments.
Unlike insects, certain soft-bodied animals have an unarticulated structure.
Some early fossils show unarticulated limbs, indicating primitive anatomical organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: articulated - having joints between segments

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and articulated (joined by joints).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00154919-a
Synset POS: a
