bimli

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪmli / (AmE), / ˈbɪmli / (BrE)
A valuable fiber plant of the East Indies, now widely cultivated, known for its strong bast fibers.
fiber plant of East Indies

Examples

The strong fibers extracted from the bimli are often used in rope-making.
Many farmers in tropical regions have started cultivating bimli for its economic value.
Textiles produced from bimli are known for their durability and strength.

Etymology

From Hindi 'बिमली' (bimlī).
