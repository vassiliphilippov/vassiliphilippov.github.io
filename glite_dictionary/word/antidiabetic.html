antidiabetic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.daɪˈəbɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.daɪˈiː.bɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
a drug or agent used to treat diabetes mellitus by lowering blood sugar levels
drug for treating diabetes mellitus
medical

Examples

Metformin is a common antidiabetic prescribed to control blood sugar in type 2 diabetes.
Many patients require a combination of antidiabetic medications to achieve optimal glycemic control.
The doctor adjusted her dosage of antidiabetic after reviewing the latest blood tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoglycemic - a drug that lowers blood glucose levels

Etymology

From anti- (against) + diabetic, relating to diabetes.
