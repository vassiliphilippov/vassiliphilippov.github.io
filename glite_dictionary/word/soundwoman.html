soundwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to amplify sound or produce sound effects, especially for television or radio broadcasts
female sound technician

Examples

The soundwoman adjusted the microphones before the band started playing.
At the TV studio, a talented soundwoman ensures every broadcast is crystal clear.
She takes pride in her work as a soundwoman for live sporting events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundman - a technician in charge of sound amplification or effects

Etymology

Formed from 'sound' + 'woman', patterned after 'soundman'.
