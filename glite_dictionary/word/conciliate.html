to conciliate

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to stop someone from being angry or hostile, or to bring disputing parties to agreement; to reconcile.
bring to agreement; make peace

Examples

The company tried to conciliate the workers after the strike by offering better benefits.
Her sincere apology helped to conciliate the offended parties.
Efforts to conciliate the two rival teams proved successful in the end.

Synset Examples

After some discussion we finally made up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - to restore friendly relations between
  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify someone, especially by giving in to their demands
  • Antonym: to provoke - to make someone angry or hostile

Etymology

From Latin conciliatus, past participle of conciliare (to unite, bring together), from concilium (assembly, council).

2. VERB

/ kənˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to make (different ideas, facts, or situations) compatible or bring them into agreement
make compatible; bring into agreement

Examples

The committee tried to conciliate the conflicting proposals into a single plan.
It is difficult to conciliate these two opposing theories within one framework.
The manager worked hard to conciliate the interests of both parties during the negotiation.

Synset Examples

The scientists had to accommodate the new results with the existing theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - restore friendly relations or make compatible

Etymology

From Latin conciliare (to bring together, unite), from concilium (assembly, council).

3. VERB

/ kənˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to make someone more friendly or less angry by gaining their good will or by trying to end a disagreement
make someone more friendly or less angry

Examples

The manager tried to conciliate staff concerns by offering flexible work hours.
He hoped to conciliate the opposing sides in the negotiation.
Her efforts to conciliate the board members were ultimately successful.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify someone, especially by giving in to their demands
  • Synonym: to mollify - to calm or soothe someone, especially someone who is angry
  • Antonym: to aggravate - to make someone more angry or hostile

Etymology

From Latin conciliare (to bring together, unite), from concilium (assembly).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
