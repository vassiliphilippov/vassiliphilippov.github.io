Cheremiss

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛrɪˈmɪs / (AmE), / tʃɛrɪˈmɪs / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Cheremis (now more commonly referred to as the Mari people) of Russia
the Finnic language of the Cheremis people

Examples

The Cheremiss language is spoken by the Mari people of central Russia.
Linguists have studied the Cheremiss language for its distinctive features among Finnic tongues.
She is fluent in both Russian and Cheremiss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mari - the modern name for the language of the Cheremis people

Etymology

From Russian 'черемисы' (cheremisy), referring to the Mari people.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛrəˈmɪs / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛrəˈmɪs / (BrE)
a member of a rural Finnish people living in eastern Russia, also known as Mari
member of a Finnish ethnic group in eastern Russia

Examples

The Cheremiss have retained many ancient traditions in their rural communities.
A group of Cheremiss musicians performed traditional songs at the festival.
The history of the Cheremiss people is closely linked to the Mari El region of Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mari - a member of the Mari people; the self-designation of the Cheremiss

Etymology

From Russian Чере́мис (Cheremis); possibly of Turkic origin.
