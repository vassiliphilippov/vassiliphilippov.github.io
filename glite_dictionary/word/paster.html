paster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to apply paste, especially for attaching items such as labels or posters
person who applies paste, often as a job

Examples

The paster carefully applied labels to thousands of bottles each day.
Before automation, a paster would manually mount posters on billboards.
In the workshop, the pasters ensured that every box was neatly sealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluer - a person who applies glue

Etymology

From paste + -er, indicating a person who performs an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæstər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstə / (BrE)
a label with an adhesive backing that can be stuck to a surface
an adhesive label

Examples

She placed a paster on each folder to label them clearly.
The envelope had a bright red paster in the corner saying 'URGENT'.
Make sure every product has the correct paster before shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticker - an adhesive label or decorative label
  • Synonym: label - a piece of paper, fabric, or similar material attached to an object and giving information about it

Etymology

From 'paste' + '-er'; originally referring to something that is pasted.
