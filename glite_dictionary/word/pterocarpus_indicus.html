Pterocarpus indicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛɹoʊˈkɑːɹpəs ˈɪndɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəʊˈkɑːpəs ˈɪndɪkəs / (BrE)
A tree native to Southeast Asia, valued for its reddish wood with a mottled or striped black grain.
Southeast Asian tree with reddish wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from the richly colored wood of the Pterocarpus indicus.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Pterocarpus indicus in its native habitats.
The distinctive grain of Pterocarpus indicus makes it popular among woodworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narra - Common name for Pterocarpus indicus, a tropical hardwood tree

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'pteron' (wing) and 'karpos' (fruit), with 'indicus' meaning 'of India'.
