aspirant

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspərənt, əˈspaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈæspɪrənt, əˈspaɪərənt / (BrE)
a person who has ambitions to achieve a particular goal or position, especially someone young or just starting out
ambitious person with goals

Examples

The company is seeking an aspirant who is eager to take on new challenges.
She was an aspirant to the national chess championship, practicing every day after school.
Among the group of aspirants, only two were selected for the scholarship.

Synset Examples

a lofty aspirant
two executive hopefuls joined the firm
the audience was full of Madonna wannabes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidate - a person who applies for a job or is nominated for election
  • Synonym: contender - a person competing for a chance to win something
  • Degree Higher: winner - a person who wins something

Etymology

From Latin aspirans, aspirant- ‘aspiring’, from the verb aspirare.

2. ADJ

/ ˈæspɚrənt / (AmE), / əˈspɪər(ə)nt / (BrE)
having ambitions to achieve recognition, advancement, or a particular goal
having ambitions for advancement or achievement

Examples

Many aspirant politicians attend leadership training programs to hone their skills.
The competition attracted aspirant writers from all over the country, each hoping to win the prize.
As an aspirant actor, she struggled for years before finally landing a prominent role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambitious - having a strong desire for success or achievement
  • Antonym: unambitious - having little or no desire for success or advancement

Etymology

From Latin 'aspirant-', present participle of 'aspirare' (to aspire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105332-s
Synset POS: s
