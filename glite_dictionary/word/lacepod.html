lacepod

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈleɪsˌpɒd / (BrE)
An annual herb with deeply divided basal leaves and slender racemes of small white flowers, producing single-seeded, winged fruits.
an annual herb with divided leaves and white flowers

Examples

The delicate flowers of the lacepod made it a favorite among gardeners.
Researchers studied how lacepod seeds are dispersed by the wind.
We found a cluster of wild lacepods growing near the stream.
