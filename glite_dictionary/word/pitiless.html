pitiless

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪt̬i.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪlɪs / (BrE)
Showing no pity or compassion for others; merciless or unfeeling.
lacking pity or compassion

Examples

The dictator's pitiless regime caused widespread suffering and despair.
Despite her pleas for mercy, the judge remained pitiless in her sentencing.
The winds were pitiless, whipping across the plain without respite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy
  • Synonym: ruthless - having no compassion or pity
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

From 'pity' + '-less', first recorded in late Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪt̬ɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪlɪs / (BrE)
showing no sympathy or mercy for others; cruel or merciless.
showing no mercy

Examples

The dictator ruled with a pitiless disregard for human life.
She delivered a pitiless critique of his work, sparing no feelings.
The desert sun beat down with pitiless intensity on the travelers.

Synset Examples

an act of ruthless ferocity
a monster of remorseless cruelty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy
  • Synonym: ruthless - having no compassion or pity
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

Formed from 'pity' + '-less' (without); 'pity' from Old French pitié, from Latin pietas (piety, compassion).
