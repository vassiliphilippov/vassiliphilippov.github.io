allotropy

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑː.trə.pi / (AmE), / əˈlɒ.trə.pi / (BrE)
The property of an element to exist in two or more different physical forms, with distinct structures and characteristics.
existence of an element in different forms

Examples

Diamond and graphite are examples of allotropy in carbon.
The discovery of allotropy in oxygen changed our understanding of atmospheric chemistry.
Students learned about allotropy while studying the different forms of sulfur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorphism - The occurrence of something in several distinct forms, especially in chemistry and biology

Etymology

From Greek allos (other) + tropos (way, manner).
