Pyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit trees native to the Old World, including pears.
genus of pear trees

Examples

The genus Pyrus includes many species of pear trees cultivated worldwide.
Botanists have classified several hybrid species under Pyrus.
Pyrus is native to Europe and Asia, with some species adapted to cold climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - the sweet fruit of trees in the genus Pyrus

Etymology

From Latin Pyrus (pear tree), from earlier Greek pēros (pear).
