to deplore

1. VERB

/ dɪˈplɔr / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔː / (BrE)
to strongly disapprove of or express deep regret for something, especially on moral grounds
express strong disapproval or regret

Examples

Many citizens deplore the lack of affordable housing in the city.
The committee deplored the corruption found within the organization.
She deplores any form of discrimination in the workplace.

Synset Examples

I deplore this hostile action
we lamented the loss of benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret about something
  • Synonym: to condemn - to express strong disapproval of something
  • Antonym: to approve - to have a positive opinion or support something

Etymology

From French 'déplorer', from Latin 'deplorare' (to bewail, lament).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈplɔr / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔː / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval of something, especially in a formal or official way
express strong condemnation or disapproval

Examples

Many international leaders deplore the use of violence in the conflict.
She deplored the lack of funding for education in her speech.
Environmental groups deplore reckless waste of natural resources.

Synset Examples

We deplore the government's treatment of political prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condemn - to express complete disapproval of; censure
  • Antonym: to approve - to have a positive opinion of; to accept as satisfactory

Etymology

From French *déplorer*, from Latin *deplorare* ('to weep bitterly, lament').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00827994-v
Synset POS: v
