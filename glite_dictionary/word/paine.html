Paine

1. PROPN

/ peɪn / (AmE), / peɪn / (BrE)
Thomas Paine (1737–1809), English-born writer and pamphleteer, influential in the American and French Revolutions.
influential Revolutionary writer

Examples

The writings of Paine inspired many to support the cause of American independence.
Paine's pamphlet 'Common Sense' played a pivotal role in shaping revolutionary attitudes.
Historians often discuss Paine's influence on both the American and French Revolutions.
