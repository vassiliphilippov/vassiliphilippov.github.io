Myrsinaceae

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˌsɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / mɪˌsɪnˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of Old World tropical trees and shrubs, some of which are found in Florida
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Myrsinaceae family includes a wide range of tropical plants.
Several species within the Myrsinaceae are native to Southeast Asia and Africa.
Botanists have reclassified some Myrsinaceae members based on new genetic evidence.

Etymology

From modern Latin *Myrsinaceae*, based on the genus name *Myrsine*.

2. NOUN

/ mɪrˌsaɪnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / mɪˌsɪnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of mainly Old World tropical trees and shrubs, including some species found in Florida.
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Many members of the Myrsinaceae family are used for ornamental purposes in gardens.
Botanists recently reclassified certain genera previously belonging to Myrsinaceae.
Some plants of the Myrsinaceae family are native to Florida's wetlands.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Myrsine (a genus in the family) and the suffix -aceae.
