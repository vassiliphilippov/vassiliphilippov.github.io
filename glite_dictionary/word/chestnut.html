chestnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
a small, hard, horny growth on the inner surface of a horse's leg, near the knee or hock, believed to be a vestige of a toe pad
a horny growth on a horse's leg

Examples

The veterinarian examined the chestnut on the horse's front leg for signs of infection.
Grooming involved cleaning around the chestnut to prevent buildup of dirt.
Each horse has a unique pattern and size to its chestnut.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
the edible nut of any of several trees of the genus Castanea, with a sweet taste and a spiny husk
edible nut of the chestnut tree

Examples

She roasted chestnuts over the open fire during the holidays.
A bowl of fresh chestnuts was placed on the kitchen table.
Street vendors often sell hot chestnuts in winter markets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conker - the seed of the horse chestnut tree, not edible

Etymology

From Old English 'cisten', ultimately from Latin 'castanea' (chestnut).

(of hair, feathers, or animals) having a golden brown to reddish brown color.
golden to reddish brown (hair, fur, etc.)

Examples

She admired the horse's shiny chestnut coat under the sunlight.
His chestnut hair curled softly around his face.
The bird had a distinctive chestnut patch on its wings.

Synset Examples

a chestnut horse
chestnut hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auburn - of a reddish-brown color, especially hair
  • Degree Higher: mahogany - a reddish-brown color (often of wood or hair)
  • Degree Lower: tan - a light brown to brownish orange color

Etymology

From Old French chastaigne, from Latin castanea, referring to the chestnut tree and its nut, whose color inspired the term.

7. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
A deciduous tree with yellow-brown leaves in autumn, producing a hard wood and edible nuts in a prickly husk.
deciduous tree with edible nuts

Examples

The ancient chestnut in the park provides shade throughout summer.
Every autumn, we gather chestnuts from the ground to roast over the fire.
A row of chestnut trees lines the village street, turning golden in October.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a common deciduous tree with acorns and lobed leaves
  • Synonym: beech - another type of deciduous tree with edible nuts

Etymology

From Old English 'cisten', via Latin 'castanea', from Ancient Greek 'kastanea'.
