Mary Wollstonecraft

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈwʊl.stənˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈmɛəri ˈwʊlstənˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
(1759–1797) An English writer, philosopher, and early advocate of women's rights, known for challenging male supremacy and promoting equal education for women; mother of Mary Shelley.
English writer and early feminist (1759–1797)

Examples

Mary Wollstonecraft is best known for her groundbreaking work, "A Vindication of the Rights of Woman."
Feminist scholars often reference Mary Wollstonecraft as a foundational figure in the movement for women's rights.
The life and writings of Mary Wollstonecraft continue to inspire debates about gender equality today.
