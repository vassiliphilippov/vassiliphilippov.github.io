columbarium

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌləmˈbɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒləmˈbɑːrɪəm / (BrE)
A building or vault with niches in the walls for holding cinerary urns containing cremated remains.
vault for urns containing cremated remains

Examples

The family placed her ashes in a columbarium at the local cemetery.
Many modern cemeteries feature a columbarium for those who choose cremation.
The marble walls of the columbarium are lined with nameplates and flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mausoleum - a large stately tomb or building housing tombs

Etymology

From Latin 'columbarium' (dovecote), from 'columba' (dove), due to the resemblance of the niches to pigeonholes.

pigeon house or niche for urns

Examples

The old manor once had a beautifully carved columbarium for its pigeons.
Archaeologists discovered a Roman columbarium used for housing funeral urns.
The garden featured a columbarium where doves nested peacefully.

Related Terms

From Latin 'columbarium', from 'columba' (dove, pigeon).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkə.ləmˈbɛr.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.əmˈbeə.ri.əm / (BrE)
A structure or chamber with niches for storing funeral urns holding the ashes of cremated people.
niche for funeral urns with ashes

Examples

The family placed the urn in the columbarium at the cemetery after the memorial service.
Many modern churches include a columbarium for parishioners who have chosen cremation.
The architect designed a peaceful garden around the columbarium to provide a place for reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mausoleum - a building housing tombs or burial chambers for the dead

Etymology

From Latin 'columbarium' (dovecote), from 'columba' (dove), due to resemblance to pigeonholes.
