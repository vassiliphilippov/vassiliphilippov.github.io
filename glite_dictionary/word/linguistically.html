linguistically

1. ADV

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkli / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to language or linguistics.
in relation to language or linguistics

Examples

The region is linguistically diverse, with several languages spoken by its inhabitants.
He is linguistically gifted, able to speak and write in five different languages.
The textbook examines various communities linguistically rather than culturally.

Synset Examples

linguistically impaired children
a lingually diverse population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics

Etymology

From 'linguistic' + '-ally', ultimately from Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language).

2. ADV

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkli / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to linguistics or the study of language.
in terms of linguistics

Examples

Some languages are linguistically related despite having different writing systems.
The project is linguistically challenging because it involves translating rare dialects.
She analyzed the poem linguistically to understand its structure and meaning.

Synset Examples

linguistically interesting data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics

Etymology

From 'linguistic' + '-ally'. 'Linguistic' from French linguistique, from Latin lingua (tongue, language).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00131928-r
Synset POS: r
