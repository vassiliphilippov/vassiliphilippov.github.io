blotchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɑːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈblɒtʃi / (BrE)
having irregular or discolored patches or spots, often referring to skin or surfaces.
covered with irregular spots or patches

Examples

The rash left his arms looking blotchy and irritated.
She avoided wearing shorts because of her blotchy skin.
The old wallpaper had become blotchy over the years.

Synset Examples

blotchy skin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - marked with spots
  • Antonym: clear - free from marks or blemishes

Etymology

From 'blotch' (spot, blot) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɑːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈblɒtʃi / (BrE)
covered with irregular spots, marks, or patches of color or shade.
marked with irregular spots or patches

Examples

Her face became blotchy after she spent too much time in the sun.
The old painting's surface had turned blotchy with age and humidity.
The allergic reaction left his arms red and blotchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having spots
  • Antonym: clear - free from marks or spots

Etymology

From "blotch" (a spot or blot) + "-y".
