ZnCO3

1. NOUN

/ ziː en ˈsiː oʊ ˈθriː / (AmE), / ziː en ˈsiː əʊ ˈθriː / (BrE)
the chemical formula for zinc carbonate, a mild astringent used in topical powders and skin treatments
zinc carbonate (chemical compound)
medical

Examples

Many dusting powders contain ZnCO3 as a gentle astringent for irritated skin.
Pharmaceutical companies use ZnCO3 in certain topical formulations.
The safety of ZnCO3 as a skin treatment is well established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc oxide - a common topical zinc compound used in ointments and powders

Etymology

Chemical symbol: Zn (zinc) from German 'Zink', CO3 indicates one carbonate ion (from Latin 'carbo' for coal plus '-ate' for salts).
