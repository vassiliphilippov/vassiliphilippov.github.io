hyaline

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəliːn / (BrE)
A glassy, translucent substance found in certain tissues, especially in hyaline cartilage and in some skin conditions.
glassy translucent biological substance
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the affected tissue showed deposits of hyaline.
The pathologist explained that hyaline in the cartilage helps provide its smooth texture.
In some skin disorders, hyaline can accumulate within the affected areas.

Etymology

From Greek *hualinos* (of glass), from *hualos* (glass).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ.əˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.əˌliːn / (BrE)
having a glass-like transparency or translucency; clear and transparent.
glass-like in transparency; clear

Examples

The coral’s skeleton had a hyaline appearance under the microscope.
Morning dew covered the leaves in hyaline droplets that sparkled in the sunlight.
The artist chose hyaline glass for its incredible clarity and delicate beauty.

Synset Examples

the morning is as clear as diamond or as hyaline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen
  • Antonym: opaque - not allowing light to pass through; not transparent

Etymology

From Greek 'hualinos', from 'hualos' meaning 'glass'.
