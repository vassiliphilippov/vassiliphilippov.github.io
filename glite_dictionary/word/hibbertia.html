Hibbertia

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈbɜːrʃiə / (AmE), / hɪˈbɜːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs, often heath-like or climbing, native to Madagascar, Australasia, and Polynesia.
genus of evergreen shrubs

Examples

Many species of Hibbertia are valued for their attractive yellow flowers in gardens.
The Hibbertia genus is widespread across Australia, thriving in a variety of habitats.
Botanists have discovered a new Hibbertia species in the forests of Madagascar.

Etymology

Named after George Hibbert (1757–1837), an English merchant and amateur botanist.

Examples
