Fatimah

1. PROPN

/ ˈfæ.tɪ.mə / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.tɪ.mə / (BrE)
the youngest daughter of the Prophet Muhammad and wife of Ali, revered especially by Shiite Muslims
daughter of Muhammad; wife of Ali in Islam

Examples

Many Shiite Muslims hold Fatimah in the highest regard as a role model of piety.
The story of Fatimah is often recounted in Islamic teachings and sermons.
Some parents choose the name Fatimah for their daughters in homage to her legacy.

Etymology

From Arabic فاطمة (Fāṭima), meaning 'she who weans an infant'.
