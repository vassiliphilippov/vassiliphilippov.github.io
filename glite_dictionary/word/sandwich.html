sandwich

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsænwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Two or more slices of bread with a filling such as meat, cheese, or vegetables between them, eaten as a snack or meal.
bread slices with a filling

Examples

He made a delicious sandwich with turkey and cheese for lunch.
Would you like a sandwich or a salad for your meal?
She quickly packed a ham sandwich for her picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - long sandwich with a variety of fillings
  • Degree Lower: wrap - food wrapped in a soft flatbread
  • Synonym: burger - cooked patty in a bun, similar to a sandwich

Etymology

Named after John Montagu, 4th Earl of Sandwich (1718–1792), an English nobleman.

2. VERB

/ ˈsæn.wɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsæn.wɪdʒ / (BrE)
to put (food) between two slices of bread to make a sandwich
put food between bread slices

Examples

She likes to sandwich slices of cheese and turkey between hearty whole-grain bread.
I usually sandwich ham and lettuce for my lunch.
The chef sandwiched grilled vegetables with melted cheese for a delicious meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - put together; combine parts or components

Etymology

From the title of the 4th Earl of Sandwich (18th c.), reputed inventor of the sandwich as a food item.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01668976-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsænˌwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsæn(w)ɪdʒ / (BrE)
to insert or squeeze (someone or something) tightly between two other people or things
insert or squeeze tightly between two things

Examples

Sarah was sandwiched between her two brothers on the crowded bench.
He sandwiched the confidential file between two old magazines for secrecy.
Commuters often find themselves sandwiched into packed subway cars during rush hour.

Synset Examples

She was sandwiched in her airplane seat between two fat men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeeze - to press something firmly, especially from opposite sides
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not together or not touching

Etymology

From the noun 'sandwich', named after the 4th Earl of Sandwich, from the town Sandwich in Kent, England.
