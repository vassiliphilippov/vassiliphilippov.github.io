fingerprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
a distinctive or identifying characteristic that reveals the involvement or influence of a person or thing
identifying characteristic or trace

Examples

The new legislation bears the fingerprint of the prime minister's economic philosophy.
That theory has the fingerprint of classic Newtonian physics.
The design carries the unique fingerprint of her artistic style.

Synset Examples

that tax bill had the senator's fingerprints all over it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - distinctive feature or typical characteristic
  • Synonym: trace - a sign or indication of something's presence
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06899538-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
A mark made by the pattern of ridges on the skin of a finger, often used for identification purposes.
mark/impression of finger ridges

Examples

The detective carefully lifted a fingerprint from the broken glass at the crime scene.
Each person has a unique fingerprint that can be used for identification.
The new phone unlocks automatically when it recognizes your fingerprint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thumbprint - an impression of a thumb's skin, similar to a fingerprint
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06657156-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
A visible mark or smudge left on a surface by a finger, often due to dirt, grease, or moisture.
a visible finger mark or smudge

Examples

There was a fingerprint on the clean window that I had just washed.
The detective noticed a greasy fingerprint on the doorknob.
She wiped away the fingerprint from the glass before serving the drink.

Etymology

From finger + print, originally referring to the unique impression left by a finger, later generalized to any mark left by a finger.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04702328-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
to take an impression of a person's fingerprints, usually for identification purposes
take an impression of fingerprints

Examples

All new employees must be fingerprinted before starting work at the bank.
The police fingerprinted the suspect as part of the investigation.
She was surprised when airport security asked to fingerprint her upon arrival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to identify - to establish the identity of somebody or something

Etymology

From 'finger' + 'print', originally used as a noun in the late 19th century, later as a verb.
