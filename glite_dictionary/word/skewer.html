skewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskjuːər / (AmE), / ˈskjʊə / (BrE)
A long, thin metal or wooden pin used for holding pieces of food together while they are being cooked, especially over a fire or in an oven.
long pin for holding food while cooking

Examples

She threaded pieces of chicken and vegetables onto a skewer before grilling them.
After the meat was cooked on the skewer, it was served with fresh bread.
He used a metal skewer to hold the lamb steady while it roasted over the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - a long rod for roasting meat over a fire

Etymology

From Old French 'esquier', from Old Norse 'skifa' (a stick or slice).

2. VERB

/ ˈskjuːər / (AmE), / ˈskjʊə / (BrE)
to fasten or pierce with a skewer or similar pointed object, especially for cooking
pierce with a skewer, especially for cooking

Examples

Before grilling, carefully skewer the vegetables and brush them with olive oil.
The chef skewered chunks of chicken to prepare the kebabs.
She skewered the marshmallows to toast them over the fire.

Synset Examples

skewer the meat for the BBQ

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impale - to pierce with a sharp object

Etymology

From Middle English 'skuer', from Old Northern French 'eskiouer' (to spit meat), ultimately of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01447182-v
Synset POS: v
