to sync

1. VERB

/ sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
to cause two or more things to happen at the same time or to match exactly, especially by adjusting their timing or manner
make things happen at the same time

Examples

I need to sync my phone with my computer before transferring the files.
Let's sync our schedules so we can meet next week.
The band members synced their instruments to play perfectly together.

Synset Examples

Let's synchronize our efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to synchronize - to cause to occur at the same time or to operate in unison

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'synchronize', from Greek 'synchronos' (happening at the same time).
