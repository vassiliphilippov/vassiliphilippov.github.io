linuron

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪn.jʊˌrɒn / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.jʊ.rɒn / (BrE)
A synthetic herbicide used to control weeds in crops without damaging the vegetables.
a herbicide for vegetables

Examples

Farmers often apply linuron to their fields to keep weeds under control without harming the main crops.
The effectiveness of linuron makes it a popular choice among vegetable growers.
Environmental regulations limit the use of linuron due to potential risks to water quality.

Etymology

Formed from chemical naming conventions, possibly from 'lin-' (as in similar compounds) and 'urea' (a chemical group).
