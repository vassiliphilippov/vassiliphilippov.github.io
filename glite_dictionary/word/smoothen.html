to smoothen

1. VERB

/ ˈsmuːðən / (AmE), / ˈsmuːð(ə)n / (BrE)
to make or become smooth or smoother, especially by rubbing or polishing
make or become smooth

Examples

She used sandpaper to smoothen the edges of the wooden table.
The lotion helps to smoothen rough skin over time.
Engineers worked to smoothen the road before reopening it to traffic.

Synset Examples

smooth the surface of the wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - to make or become smooth
  • Antonym: to roughen - to make or become rough

2. VERB

/ ˈsmuːðən / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðən / (BrE)
to become smooth or make something smooth
become or make smooth

Examples

After many years, the stone walls began to smoothen from wind and rain.
The cream will help smoothen your skin if used regularly.
Over time, the sharp edges of the table started to smoothen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - to make or become free from roughness or unevenness

3. VERB

/ ˈsmuːðən / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðən / (BrE)
to make (a surface) smooth or shiny, often by polishing
make smooth or shiny by polishing

Examples

The jeweler carefully smoothened the ring until it gleamed in the light.
She used a special cloth to smoothen the surface of the antique table.
Please smoothen your shoes before the event to make them look presentable.

Synset Examples

shine the silver, please
polish my shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make something smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • Synonym: buff - to polish a surface to make it shine

Etymology

From the adjective 'smooth' with the verbal suffix '-en'.
