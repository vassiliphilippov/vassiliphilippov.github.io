JEM

1. NOUN

/ dʒi ɛm / (AmE), / dʒiː ɛm / (BrE)
An acronym for a militant Islamic group, Jaish-e-Mohammed, founded in 2000 and active mainly in Kashmir, known for acts of terrorism and seeking the release of imprisoned members through kidnappings and other violent means.
militant Islamic group; acronym for Jaish-e-Mohammed

Examples

The government increased security after JEM claimed responsibility for the attack.
International authorities have designated JEM as a terrorist group.
Reports indicated that JEM was involved in the kidnapping of several journalists.

Etymology

Acronym from Urdu, meaning 'Army of Muhammad' (Jaish-e-Mohammed).
