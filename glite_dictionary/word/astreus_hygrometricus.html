Astreus hygrometricus

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrius haɪˌɡroʊməˈtrɪkəs / (AmE), / əˈstriːəs haɪˌɡrəʊməˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of earthstar fungus widely found in sandy soils, recognized by its pale tan spore mass (gleba) and star-shaped fruiting body.
species of earthstar fungus

Examples

We found several Astreus hygrometricus growing in the sandy field after heavy rain.
The distinctive star-shaped fruiting body of Astreus hygrometricus makes it easy to identify.
Scientists study Astreus hygrometricus for its unique response to changes in moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthstar - any of several fungi that open into star-like shapes when mature

Etymology

From Greek 'Astreus' (mythological name) and 'hygrometricus' meaning related to moisture measurement.
