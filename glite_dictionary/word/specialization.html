specialization

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which a body part or structure becomes adapted for a specific function.
biological adaptation of a body part
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, specialization allows stem cells to become muscle or nerve cells.
The specialization of bird beaks enables them to access different food sources.
Nerve cells exhibit a high degree of specialization for transmitting electrical signals.

Synset Examples

cell differentiation in the developing embryo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differentiation - process by which cells or tissues become specialized

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (particular, special) + '-ation', influenced by French 'spécialisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular area or field of work or study that someone has concentrated on, especially as a career
specific field of work or study

Examples

After finishing medical school, she chose oncology as her specialization.
His specialization in environmental law made him a sought-after consultant.
The university offers a wide range of specialization options for graduate students.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialty - a person's field of specialization or expertise

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specialisatio, from Latin specialis (special).

3. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of concentrating on and becoming expert in a particular subject or skill, or adapting something for a special purpose
the act or process of specializing

Examples

Technological advances often lead to increased specialization in the workforce.
Her specialization in cardiology helped her provide expert care to her patients.
The company underwent specialization to focus exclusively on renewable energy sources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalization - the process of making something less specialized or more common; the opposite process

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (of a species or kind) + '-ation'.
