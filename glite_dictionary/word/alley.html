alley

1. NOUN

/ ˈæli / (AmE), / ˈæli / (BrE)
a long, narrow lane in a bowling center where the ball is rolled toward the pins
bowling lane for rolling the ball

Examples

We spent the afternoon playing at the alley downtown.
The polished surface of the alley helps the ball roll smoothly toward the pins.
Each alley was occupied by enthusiastic bowlers competing in a local tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowling lane - the surface in a bowling alley on which the ball is bowled

Etymology

From Middle French *alee* (a walk or passage), from *aller* (to go).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæli / (AmE), / ˈæli / (BrE)
A narrow passage or street, often between or behind buildings.
narrow street or passageway

Examples

We walked down a dark alley behind the restaurant.
The thief slipped quietly into the narrow alley and disappeared from sight.
Cats like to roam the alley in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lane - a narrow road, often in the countryside or in a town

Etymology

From Old French 'alee' (walk, passage), from 'aler' (to go), from Latin 'ambulare' (to walk).
