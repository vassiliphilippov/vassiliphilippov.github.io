Oder

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊdər / (AmE), / ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
a major river in Central Europe, rising in the Czech Republic and flowing through western Poland into the Baltic Sea
a major Central European river

Examples

The town is situated on the banks of the Oder river in western Poland.
Ships travel up and down the Oder between Poland and Germany.
Flooding along the Oder has occasionally caused widespread damage to nearby villages.

Etymology

From German 'Oder', derived from Latin 'Viadua' or 'Oddera', ultimately of pre-Slavic origin.
