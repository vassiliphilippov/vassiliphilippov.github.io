to carjack

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑrˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdʒæk / (BrE)
to steal a car by force, especially while the owner is present
steal a car by force

Examples

The police arrested two men who tried to carjack a woman downtown.
He was terrified when someone attempted to carjack him at the traffic light.
Incidents where criminals carjack vehicles often occur in big cities.

Synset Examples

My car was carjacked last night!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijack - to unlawfully seize a vehicle in transit
  • Synonym: steal - to take property without permission or legal right

Etymology

Blend of 'car' and 'hijack', first recorded in the early 1990s in the US.
