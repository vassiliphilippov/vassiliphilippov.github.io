Merginae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrdʒɪniː / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdʒɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of waterfowl including mergansers and closely related diving birds.
subfamily of mergansers and diving ducks

Examples

The Merginae include species that are specially adapted for diving and fishing in cold waters.
Many ornithologists study the differences between Merginae and other duck subfamilies.
Various members of the Merginae can be found inhabiting northern lakes and rivers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Merginae*, from genus *Mergus* (merganser) with the taxonomic suffix *-inae* for subfamilies.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɜrɡɪˈnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɜːɡɪˈniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in the family Anatidae that includes mergansers and closely related diving ducks.
duck subfamily with mergansers

Examples

The subfamily Merginae is characterized by ducks that are specially adapted for diving.
Several North American species in the Merginae can be seen in coastal waters during winter.
Mergansers, members of the Merginae, have serrated bills for catching fish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anatidae - the biological family of ducks, geese, and swans
  • Synonym: merganser - fish-eating diving duck of the genus Mergus

Etymology

From scientific Latin Merginae, based on genus Mergus (the type genus of this subfamily).
