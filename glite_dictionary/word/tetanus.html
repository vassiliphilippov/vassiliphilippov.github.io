tetanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛt̬.ən.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛt.ə.nəs / (BrE)
A sustained muscular contraction caused by a rapid series of nerve impulses, without relaxation between stimuli.
sustained muscle contraction
medical

Examples

In cases of severe poisoning, patients may experience tetanus of the jaw muscles.
Laboratory tests demonstrated repeated stimulation of the nerve could induce a tetanus in the isolated muscle.
A rapid train of electrical impulses produced tetanus rather than individual twitches in the frog's leg muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary contraction of a muscle

Etymology

From Greek *tetanos* (rigid, tense), via Latin *tetanus*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛt̬.ən.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛt.ə.nəs / (BrE)
A serious bacterial disease marked by muscle stiffness and spasms, often caused by infection of a wound.
serious bacterial disease causing muscle spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that tetanus can be prevented through vaccination.
Without immediate treatment, tetanus can lead to severe muscle spasms and even death.
He needed a tetanus shot after stepping on a rusty nail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lockjaw - old term for tetanus, marked by jaw muscle rigidity

Etymology

From Greek *tetanos* (rigid) via Latin *tetanus*.
