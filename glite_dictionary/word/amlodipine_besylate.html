amlodipine besylate

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.loʊˈdɪ.piːn bɛsˈɪ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˌam.ləʊˈdɪ.piːn bɛsˈɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
a vasodilator drug used to treat high blood pressure and angina pectoris, often sold under the trade name Norvasc
vasodilator drug for hypertension and angina
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amlodipine besylate to help control her high blood pressure.
Some patients may experience swelling in the ankles after starting amlodipine besylate.
He takes amlodipine besylate every morning as part of his heart medication regimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amlodipine - a calcium channel blocker used to treat high blood pressure and angina
  • Synonym: Norvasc - trade name for amlodipine besylate

Etymology

From 'amlodipine' (the active pharmaceutical ingredient) plus 'besylate' (the besylate salt form).
