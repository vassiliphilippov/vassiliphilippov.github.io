retrograde

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of amnesia) affecting memory for events that occurred immediately before a trauma.
amnesia affecting pre-trauma memories
medical

Examples

After the accident, she suffered from retrograde amnesia and could not recall the hours before the crash.
The patient’s retrograde amnesia made it difficult to remember recent conversations.
Doctors explained that retrograde memory loss is common among head trauma victims.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterograde - amnesia affecting memory for events after trauma

Etymology

From Latin retrogradus, from retro ('backwards') + gradus ('step').

5. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of motion or orbit) moving in the opposite direction to that considered normal, especially from east to west on the celestial sphere or, for planets, in a direction opposite to Earth's.
moving in the opposite direction, especially in astronomy

Examples

Mars appears to be in retrograde motion when it seems to move backward across the sky.
Some moons have retrograde orbits around their planets, moving in the opposite direction to the planet's rotation.
During retrograde periods, astrologers often associate the apparent backward movement of planets with delays or confusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prograde - (of motion or orbit) moving in the normal direction, especially from west to east on the celestial sphere

Etymology

From Latin retrogradus, from retro (back) + gradus (step).
