chug

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌg / (AmE), / tʃʌg / (BrE)
a dull, explosive sound, especially that produced by a motor or engine running steadily
a dull, explosive engine sound

Examples

The steady chug of the tractor could be heard across the field.
I listened to the chug of the old train as it pulled out of the station.
The boat's engine started with a loud chug before settling into a constant rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throb - a rhythmic pulsating sound or vibration, especially from an engine
  • Degree Higher: roar - a loud, deep, continuous sound, often of an engine
  • Degree Lower: purr - a low, continuous, soft sound, often of an engine running smoothly

2. VERB

/ tʃʌɡ / (AmE), / tʃʌɡ / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to make a series of dull, explosive, or rhythmic noises, especially while moving
make dull, rhythmic, explosive noises

Examples

The old tractor chugged across the field, leaving a trail of smoke behind.
As the boat chugged along the river, we admired the peaceful scenery.
In the early morning, the delivery truck chugged up the steep hill toward the bakery.

Synset Examples

the engine chugged down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throb - make a regular pulsating sound
  • Synonym: hum - produce a continuous low sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound produced.
