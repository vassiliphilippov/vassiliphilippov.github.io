telescope

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument that makes distant objects appear closer and larger, using lenses or mirrors to magnify their images
optical instrument for seeing distant objects

Examples

He used his telescope to observe the craters on the moon.
The astronomer adjusted the telescope to focus on a distant galaxy.
With a powerful telescope, you can see the rings of Saturn from your backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binoculars - an instrument with two eyepieces for viewing distant objects
  • Antonym: microscope - an instrument for viewing very small objects

Etymology

From Italian 'telescopio', from Greek 'tēle' (far) + 'skopein' (to look at).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
to collapse or slide parts together so that they fit inside one another, or to become crushed together by such a movement
collapse or slide together (like sections)

Examples

The metal pole can telescope down to half its original length for easy storage.
Several train cars telescoped during the crash, causing severe damage.
The designer created a table that can telescope to fit smaller spaces.

Synset Examples

In the accident, the cars telescoped
my hiking sticks telescope and can be put into the backpack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size, range, or amount; opposite of collapse

Etymology

Derived from the noun, originally from Italian 'telescopio', from Greek 'tēle' (far) + 'skopein' (to look). Verb sense based on mechanical function of shortening or collapsing.

3. VERB

/ ˈtɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
to make something shorter by compressing or condensing it, especially in length, duration, or content
compress or condense something

Examples

The director decided to telescope the two acts into a single performance.
Our company plans to telescope the project timeline by eliminating unnecessary steps.
Historical events were telescoped to fit the hour-long documentary.

Synset Examples

the novel was telescoped into a short play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condense - to make something denser or more concise; shorten text or speech
  • Antonym: to expand - to make something larger or longer; the opposite of shorten or compress

Etymology

From Italian *telescopio* (an instrument for viewing at a distance), from Greek *tēle-* (far) + *skopein* (to look). The verb use arises by figurative extension, as in collapsing a telescope.
