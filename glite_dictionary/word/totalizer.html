totalizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊtəlˌaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊt(ə)lˌaɪzə / (BrE)
a computer, often at a racetrack, that records bets and calculates winnings by dividing the total amount wagered among those who win
betting computer for dividing winnings

Examples

All bets are processed by the totalizer before the race starts.
The totalizer quickly calculated the payouts for the winning tickets.
Due to a malfunction in the totalizer, some bettors had to wait longer for their winnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tote - a machine for registering bets and displaying odds

Etymology

From 'total' + '-izer', referring to the device's function of totaling bets.

WordNet ID: oewn-03896211-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊt̬əlˌaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a device or calculator that performs simple arithmetic functions, especially addition and summing totals
device for performing simple arithmetic

Examples

The totalizer was used to quickly add up the day's sales receipts.
Early mechanical totalizers were essential in accounting departments before the advent of computers.
Each betting booth was equipped with an electronic totalizer to manage wagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - a device used for mathematical calculations

Etymology

From 'total' + '-izer', an agent noun suffix; originally referring to devices that totaled values mechanically.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02682111-n
Synset POS: n
