dreadful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəl / (BrE)
Extremely bad; causing fear, shock, or disgust.
very bad; causing fear or shock

Examples

The weather has been dreadful all week, with heavy rain and cold winds.
Her handwriting is dreadful; I can hardly read a single word she writes.
We had a dreadful experience at that restaurant, and we will never go back.

Synset Examples

atrocious taste
abominable workmanship
an awful voice
dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or serious
  • Antonym: delightful - very pleasant or enjoyable
  • Degree Lower: bad - of poor quality or low standard
  • Degree Higher: horrific - causing horror or shock; extremely bad

Etymology

From Old English 'drēd' (fear) + '-ful' (full of).

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəl / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant, shocking, or bad; causing fear, suffering, or unhappiness.
extremely unpleasant or bad

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - very bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: delightful - extremely pleasant or charming

Etymology

3. ADJ

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəl / (BrE)
Causing fear, shock, or suffering; extremely bad or distressing.
causing fear, shock, or distress

Examples

The hurricane left behind a dreadful scene of destruction and chaos.
She had a dreadful feeling that something terrible was about to happen.
The film depicted the dreadful realities of life during the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - causing great fear or suffering
  • Synonym: horrible - causing horror; very unpleasant
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good; marvelous (opposite)

Etymology

From English 'dread' (fear) + '-ful' (full of), meaning 'full of dread'.
