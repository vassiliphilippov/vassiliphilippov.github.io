to gallivant

1. VERB

/ ˈɡælɪˌvænt / (AmE), / ˈɡælɪˌvant / (BrE)
to travel, roam, or move about for pleasure, often without a fixed purpose
wander or travel for pleasure

Examples

After finishing her exams, she decided to gallivant across Europe for the summer.
They spent the afternoon gallivanting around the city, visiting shops and cafes.
He can't be trusted to finish the project; he's always gallivanting somewhere without telling anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to roam - to move about aimlessly or without a fixed course
  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual way

Etymology

Perhaps from French 'galer' (to enjoy oneself, have fun) + 'vaguer' (to wander).
