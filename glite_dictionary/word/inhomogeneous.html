inhomogeneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌhoʊməˈdʒiniəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌhəʊməˈdʒiːniəs / (BrE)
not uniform or consistent in composition or structure; not homogeneous.
not uniform; not homogeneous

Examples

The distribution of minerals in the rock is highly inhomogeneous.
An inhomogeneous mixture contains substances unevenly distributed throughout.
The patient’s symptoms were caused by an inhomogeneous tissue structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homogeneous - of the same kind or nature throughout

Etymology

From in- (not) + homogeneous, from Greek homogeneēs (of the same kind).
