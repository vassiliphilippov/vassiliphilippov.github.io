bellpull

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌpʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.pʊl / (BrE)
a handle or cord that is pulled to ring a bell, especially one used to summon servants or announce visitors
handle or cord for ringing a bell

Examples

She pulled the bellpull to announce her arrival at the manor.
The vintage bellpull in the hallway is decorated with tassels and embroidery.
A servant would respond whenever the bellpull in the guest room was used.

Etymology

Compound of 'bell' and 'pull'.
