clog

1. NOUN

/ klɑɡ / (AmE), / klɒɡ / (BrE)
a type of shoe with a thick, often wooden sole, sometimes with an open heel, traditionally worn in parts of Europe
shoe with a thick wooden sole

Examples

She slipped off her clogs and walked barefoot through the garden.
Traditional Dutch dancers often perform in wooden clogs.
The museum displays a collection of antique clogs from different regions.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'clogge' (thick piece of wood, wooden shoe).
