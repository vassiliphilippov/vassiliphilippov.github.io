buffalofish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish found in North America, especially in the Mississippi valley, belonging to the sucker family (genus Ictiobus)
large North American freshwater sucker fish

Examples

The buffalofish is commonly found in the rivers and lakes of the Mississippi valley region.
Local anglers often prize the buffalofish for its size and challenging catch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, carp-like freshwater fish native to North America, belonging to the genus Ictiobus.
large North American freshwater fish

Examples

The river is home to several species of buffalofish prized for their size.
Fishermen often catch buffalofish during the spring spawning season.
Local recipes sometimes include smoked buffalofish as a delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a freshwater fish often found in rivers and lakes, similar in appearance to the buffalofish

Etymology

From 'buffalo' (the animal, referring to its size/appearance) + 'fish'.
