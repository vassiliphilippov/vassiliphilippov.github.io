Lysiloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪsɪˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌlɪsɪˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American trees and shrubs with pinnate leaves and flat, straight pods.
genus of tropical American trees and shrubs

Examples

The wood of the Lysiloma tree is valued for its durability and is used in furniture making.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Lysiloma genus in Central America.
The leaves of Lysiloma are pinnate, resembling those of other leguminous trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'loma' (border, fringe).

2. NOUN

genus of tropical American trees and shrubs

Examples

The desert is home to several species of Lysiloma, which provide shade for wildlife.
Botanists classified the peculiar tree as a member of the genus Lysiloma.
The flat pods of Lysiloma trees make them easy to distinguish from other local flora.

Etymology

From Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'loma' (fringe), referring to features of the plant's pods.
