leanness

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈliːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being meager, scant, or insufficient in amount or extent.
the state of being meager or insufficient

Examples

The leanness of the winter months made food a daily concern for the villagers.
The poem describes the leanness of opportunity in the dusty, forgotten town.
Despite the leanness of his resources, he managed to finish the project.

Synset Examples

an exiguity of cloth that would only allow of miniature capes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From 'lean' + '-ness', with 'lean' from Old English 'hlǣne' (thin, poor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈliːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having little body fat; thinness or lack of excess flesh
the state of having little body fat

Examples

His leanness made him an ideal candidate for the long-distance running team.
The model's leanness was often praised by fashion designers.
A diet focused on reducing body fat can help achieve leanness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the state of being thin, especially related to the body
  • Antonym: fatness - the state of having much body fat; obesity

Etymology

From lean + -ness, where 'lean' comes from Old English hlēne (thin, meager).
