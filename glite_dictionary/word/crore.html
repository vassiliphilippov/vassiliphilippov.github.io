crore

1. NOUN

/ krɔr / (AmE), / krɔː / (BrE)
A unit in the Indian numbering system equal to ten million (10,000,000).
ten million (Indian numbering unit)

Examples

India's population is over 130 crore, making it the second most populous country in the world.
The project was funded with a total of 5 crore rupees.
Every year, millions of people in India buy lottery tickets hoping to win a crore or more.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lakh - a unit in the Indian numbering system equal to one hundred thousand
  • Degree Lower: million - the number one million (1,000,000)
  • Degree Higher: billion - the number one billion (1,000,000,000)

Etymology

From Hindi करोड़ (karor), from Sanskrit कोटि (koṭi), meaning 'ten million'.
