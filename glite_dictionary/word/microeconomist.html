microeconomist

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊɪˈkɑnəmɪst / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊɪˈkɒnəmɪst / (BrE)
an economist who specializes in the study of microeconomics, focusing on the behavior of individuals and firms in making economic decisions.
economist specializing in microeconomics

Examples

The conference featured a renowned microeconomist who discussed market behavior among small businesses.
As a microeconomist, she researches how households respond to changes in price.
Many microeconomists analyze the effects of government regulation on individual industries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macroeconomist - an economist who specializes in macroeconomics

Etymology

From 'micro-' (meaning small) and 'economist'.
