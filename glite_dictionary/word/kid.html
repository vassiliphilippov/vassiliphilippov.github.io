kid

1. NOUN

/ kɪd / (AmE), / kɪd / (BrE)
a child or young person
child; young person

Examples

The park was filled with kids playing soccer and riding bikes.
She still acts like a kid even though she’s in college now.
As a kid, he dreamed of traveling around the world.

Synset Examples

she writes books for children
they're just kids
‘tiddler’ is a British term for youngster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown or mature person

Etymology

Originally from Old Norse 'kith' meaning 'young goat'; transferred to mean 'child' in English around the late 16th century.
