pestered

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛstɚd / (AmE), / ˈpɛstəd / (BrE)
repeatedly annoyed or troubled, especially with frequent petty disturbances
repeatedly annoyed or troubled

Examples

After hours of questions, the pestered teacher finally asked for a short break.
The politician appeared pestered by reporters as he left the building.
Her pestered expression made it clear she needed some peace and quiet.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harassed - persistently troubled or distressed
  • Synonym: bothered - mildly annoyed or disturbed

Etymology

From the verb 'pester', which comes from French *pester* (to plague), from Italian *pestare* (to crush), related to 'pest'.
