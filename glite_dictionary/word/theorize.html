to theorize

1. VERB

/ ˈθiəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈθɪəraɪz / (BrE)
to form an idea or set of ideas intended to explain something; to create a theory about something
form or construct a theory

Examples

Many scientists theorize about the existence of life on other planets.
She likes to theorize about why people behave the way they do.
The students theorized possible outcomes before testing their experiment.

Synset Examples

he thinks and theorizes all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speculate - to form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence

Etymology

From Greek 'theōria' (contemplation, speculation), via Medieval Latin 'theorizare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00634593-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈθiːəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈθɪəraɪz / (BrE)
to form an explanation or set of ideas about something based on limited information or evidence
form a theory or explanation

Examples

Many historians theorize that the city was destroyed by an earthquake.
Scientists continue to theorize about the exact cause of the sudden climate change.
She theorized that the missing letter had been accidentally thrown away.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hypothesize - to suggest a possible explanation or theory
  • Synonym: to speculate - to form ideas without firm evidence
  • Antonym: to prove - to demonstrate the truth of something by evidence

Etymology

From Greek *theōria* (contemplation, theory) via Medieval Latin *theorizare*.

WordNet ID: oewn-00635105-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈθɪəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈθɪə.raɪz / (BrE)
To form a theory or set of ideas about something, especially based on limited evidence or speculation.
form a theory based on ideas or speculation

Examples

Scientists often theorize about the origins of the universe.
Some experts theorize that climate change will accelerate migration patterns.
She theorized that the missing painting had been stolen years ago.

Synset Examples

Galileo theorized the motion of the stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hypothesize - to suggest a possible explanation that can be tested
  • Synonym: to speculate - to guess possible answers based on incomplete evidence

Etymology

From Greek 'theōria' (contemplation, speculation) via late Latin 'theoria.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00634756-v
Synset POS: v
