handshaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhandˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of grasping and shaking another person's hand, typically as a greeting, introduction, or to signify an agreement
act of shaking hands

Examples

The staff welcomed each guest with a warm handshaking at the entrance.
A firm handshaking is often seen as a sign of confidence in business meetings.
In many cultures, handshaking is an important gesture of respect and agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handshake - an act of shaking hands with someone

Etymology

Formed from 'handshake' (n.) + '-ing' (suffix forming nouns).
