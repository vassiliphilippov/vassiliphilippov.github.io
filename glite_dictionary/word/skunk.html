2. NOUN

/ skʌŋk / (AmE), / skʌŋk / (BrE)
A small North American mammal known for its ability to spray a foul-smelling liquid as a defense mechanism.
small mammal that sprays a foul odor

Examples

A skunk crossed the road in front of our car last night.
The dog got sprayed by a skunk and smelled terrible for days.
Skunks are known for their distinctive black and white stripes.

Etymology

From Algonquian (Massachusett) *squnck* (urinating fox).

5. VERB

/ skʌŋk / (AmE), / skʌŋk / (BrE)
to defeat someone by a large margin, especially to prevent them from scoring any points
defeat by a large margin (esp. shutout)

Examples

The home team skunked their rivals, winning 10–0 in the final game.
If you skunk your opponent in this round, you move on to the finals.
She was skunked in chess and didn't win a single game this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut out - to prevent an opponent from scoring
  • Synonym: thrash - to defeat someone easily or decisively
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated in a competition or struggle

Etymology

From the noun 'skunk,' possibly alluding to the strong, overwhelming odor as a metaphor for overwhelming defeat, 19th century U.S. origin.
