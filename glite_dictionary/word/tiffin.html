tiffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪfɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɪfɪn / (BrE)
a light meal eaten at midday, especially in South Asia or among British people living abroad
midday meal, often light (esp. South Asian)

Examples

We stopped for tiffin at a roadside café during our journey.
She packed a tiffin of sandwiches and fruit for her lunch.
Many office workers in Mumbai enjoy their tiffin delivered by dabbawalas every day.

Etymology

From Anglo-Indian English, probably a variant of 'tiffing' (taking a little drink or sip).
