mend

1. NOUN

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
a piece of sewing that repairs a hole or worn area, especially in a garment
repair in fabric by sewing

Examples

The mend on her sweater was hardly noticeable.
He made a careful mend to his jeans after they ripped.
Several mends on the dress told stories of its long life.

Synset Examples

her stockings had several mends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing something that is broken or damaged
  • Antonym: tear - a hole or split in something, caused by being pulled apart
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03750776-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
to repair something that is broken or damaged by fixing what is torn or replacing a part
to repair something damaged or broken

Examples

He managed to mend the torn sleeve on his shirt in just a few minutes.
After dropping her necklace, she took it to a jeweler to mend the broken clasp.
Can you mend this old chair, or is it beyond repair?

Synset Examples

She repaired her TV set
Repair my shoes please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - to restore something to a good condition after damage
  • Synonym: fix - to restore something that is broken to working order
  • Antonym: break - to cause something to separate into pieces or stop working

Etymology

From Middle English 'menden', a contraction of 'amenden', from Old French 'amender', from Latin 'emendare' (to correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00261534-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
(of an injury, illness, or person) to heal or recover; to become healthy again after damage or sickness.
heal or recover from injury or sickness

Examples

It took several weeks for his ankle to mend after the accident.
Doctors say her wounds are starting to mend rapidly.
After the operation, he stayed at home to mend before returning to work.

Synset Examples

My broken leg is mending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heal - to make or become healthy again

Etymology

From Old French *amender*, from Latin *emendare* (to correct, improve).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
the act of repairing or restoring something to working order
the act of repairing or restoring

Examples

After a few hours on the mend, the watch was ticking perfectly again.
The streets are finally on the mend after last month's flooding.
He was released from the hospital and is now definitely on the mend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of restoring something that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From Middle English menden, from Old French amender, ultimately from Latin emendare (to correct).
