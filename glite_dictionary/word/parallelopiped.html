parallelopiped

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌlɛləˈpaɪpɪd / (AmE), / ˌparəlɛləˈpʌɪpɛd / (BrE)
A three-dimensional solid figure whose six faces are all parallelograms.
three-dimensional figure with parallelogram faces

Examples

The architect designed the building in the shape of a parallelopiped.
In geometry class, we calculated the volume of a parallelopiped.
A rectangle is the base for a right parallelopiped called a rectangular prism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - a solid geometric figure with two equal and parallel faces

Etymology

From Greek 'parallēlopipedon', from 'parallēlos' (parallel) + 'epipedon' (plane surface).
