Sporobolus cryptandrus

1. NOUN

/ spɔːˈrɑbələs krɪpˈtændrəs / (AmE), / spɒˈrɒbələs krɪpˈtændrəs / (BrE)
A species of erect, smooth grass commonly found in sandy areas of eastern North America, also known as sand dropseed.
a grass species native to North America

Examples

Sporobolus cryptandrus is often found stabilizing sandy soils along riverbanks.
Botanists have studied the drought resistance of Sporobolus cryptandrus in prairie restoration projects.
Sporobolus cryptandrus is also called sand dropseed because of its characteristic seed dispersal.

Etymology

From Greek 'sporos' (seed), 'bolos' (throwing), and 'cryptandrus', meaning hidden stamens.
