Schinus molle

1. NOUN

/ ˈskiːnəs ˈmoʊli / (AmE), / ˈskiːnəs ˈmɒleɪ / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to Peru, with a broad, rounded crown, slender, drooping branches, clusters of greenish flowers, and rose-pink fruit; also known as the Peruvian pepper tree.
Peruvian evergreen tree with pink fruit

Examples

The garden's old Schinus molle provided a shaded spot to rest on hot afternoons.
Some people use the berries of Schinus molle as a substitute for pink peppercorns.
The Schinus molle along the avenue bloomed with clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peruvian pepper tree - another common name for Schinus molle

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Schinus' and species name 'molle', meaning 'soft' in Spanish, referring to the soft leaves.
