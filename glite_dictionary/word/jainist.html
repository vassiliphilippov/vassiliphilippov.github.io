Jainist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪnɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪnɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or follows Jainism, an ancient Indian religion that emphasizes non-violence and asceticism
follower of Jainism

Examples

As a Jainist, she strictly follows the practice of non-violence in her daily life.
Many Jainists celebrate Mahavir Jayanti as a major religious festival.
He became interested in the teachings of a Jainist monk while traveling in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jain - a follower of Jainism

Etymology

From Jain + -ist, modeled on similar formations like Buddhist and Hinduist.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒaɪnɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪnɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Jainism or its adherents.
relating to Jainism

Examples

The museum features several Jainist sculptures from the 12th century.
Her family follows Jainist dietary practices, which are strictly vegetarian.
The Jainist philosophy emphasizes non-violence toward all living beings.

Synset Examples

Jain gods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jain - a follower of Jainism

Etymology

From Jain + -ist, formed after Jainism (from Sanskrit jaina, relating to Jina, a spiritual conqueror).
