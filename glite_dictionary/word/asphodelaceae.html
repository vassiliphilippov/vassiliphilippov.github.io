Asphodelaceae

1. NOUN

/ æsfəˌdɛləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / æsfəˌdɛlɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in the order Asparagales, including asphodels, aloe, and related genera, formerly classified as a subfamily within Liliaceae or Xanthorrhoeaceae
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Asphodelaceae family includes well-known plants such as aloe and asphodel.
Recent botanical studies have reclassified certain genera under Asphodelaceae.
Gardeners often favor members of the Asphodelaceae for their drought resistance.

Etymology

From genus name Asphodelus (asphodel), from Greek asphodelos, with the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæsfəˌdɛləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ˌæsfədɒˈliːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in the order Asparagales, formerly considered a subfamily of Liliaceae, including asphodels, aloes, and related species
family of flowering plants, including asphodels and aloes

Examples

The Asphodelaceae family includes many well-known plants such as aloes and daylilies.
Recent changes in plant taxonomy have reclassified Asphodelaceae as a distinct family, separate from Liliaceae.
Several members of the Asphodelaceae are cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens worldwide.

Etymology

From the genus name Asphodelus plus the standard botanical family ending -aceae, ultimately Greek ἀσφόδελος (asphodelos, 'asphodel').
