blistery

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈblɪstəri / (BrE)
(of weather or a surface) very hot, as if capable of causing blisters.
extremely hot, as if raising blisters

Examples

We walked for miles under the blistery afternoon sun.
The desert's blistery winds made travel almost unbearable.
On that blistery day, the sand burned our feet through our shoes.

Synset Examples

blistering sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistering - extremely hot; intense as if causing blisters

Etymology

From 'blister' + '-y', denoting possessing the nature of blisters.

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈblɪstəri / (BrE)
Covered with small blisters.
covered with small blisters

Examples

Her hands were blistery after working all afternoon in the garden without gloves.
The patient presented with blistery skin due to a severe allergic reaction.
Walking in tight shoes for hours left his feet sore and blistery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistered - having one or more blisters

Etymology

From 'blister' + '-y', denoting covered with or having a specified quality.
