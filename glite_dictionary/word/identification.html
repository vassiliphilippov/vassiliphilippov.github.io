identification

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of recognizing or establishing who or what someone or something is, especially by memory or observation
process of recognizing or establishing identity

Examples

Her quick identification of the suspect amazed the police officers during the lineup.
Early identification of symptoms can lead to more effective treatment of diseases.
The student's identification of historical events relied on her excellent memory.

Synset Examples

a politician whose recall of names was as remarkable as his recognition of faces
experimental psychologists measure the elapsed time from the onset of the stimulus to its recognition by the observer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of identifying someone or something from previous encounters or knowledge

Etymology

From Medieval Latin identificatio (the act of identifying).

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of establishing the identity of a person or object
establishing identity of someone or something

Examples

The police announced the identification of the suspect after reviewing security footage.
Accurate identification of the species was essential for the study's success.
The identification of the stolen car led to the recovery of other stolen property.

Synset Examples

the thief's identification was followed quickly by his arrest
identification of the gun was an important clue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of identifying someone or something as already known
  • Antonym: misidentification - incorrect identification of a person or object

Etymology

From Medieval Latin identificatio (identifying), from Latin idem (same) + facere (to make).

evidence or documents that show who a person is; something that identifies a person or thing.

Examples

The police officer asked for some form of identification.
He forgot his identification, so he couldn't board the plane.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin identificatio (becoming the same), from Late Latin identificare (to make the same).

5. NOUN

/ aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person attributes the characteristics or traits of another person or group to themselves, either consciously or unconsciously, often as a psychological or social mechanism
attributing traits of others to oneself

Examples

His identification with the sports team was so strong that their victories felt like his own.
Through identification with her mentor, she adopted many of his professional habits.
The concept of identification is important in understanding how children develop their sense of self.

Etymology

From Latin identificare (to make the same) and -tion, indicating action or process.

Synset POS: n
