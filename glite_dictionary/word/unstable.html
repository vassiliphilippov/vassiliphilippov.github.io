unstable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
prone to change, fail, or fall; not steady or firmly fixed
not stable; prone to change or fall

Examples

The old bridge has become unstable and must be repaired before it can be used again.
Her mood is unstable, shifting rapidly from happiness to anger.
After the earthquake, the ground remained unstable for several days.

Synset Examples

unstable political conditions
the tower proved to be unstable in the high wind
an unstable world economy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to change or fail
  • Synonym: insecure - not firmly fixed; liable to give way

Etymology

From Latin 'stabilis' (stable) with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02299575-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a person) suffering from severe mental illness or liable to sudden, unpredictable changes in behavior or mood.
suffering from severe mental illness

Examples

After the tragedy, he became increasingly unstable and withdrawn from his friends.
The hospital specializes in treating unstable patients who need constant supervision.
Her unstable mental state worried her family and colleagues.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: mentally ill - having a mental illness

Etymology

/ ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a substance or compound) highly or violently reactive and likely to undergo chemical change, often dangerously so.

Examples

Some unstable isotopes can decay suddenly, releasing dangerous levels of radiation.
Handling unstable compounds requires special laboratory equipment and strict safety procedures.
