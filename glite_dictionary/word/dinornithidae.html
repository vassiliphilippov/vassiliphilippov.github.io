Dinornithidae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌnɔːrˈnɪθɪdiː / (AmE), / daɪˌnɔːˈnɪθɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, extinct, flightless birds native to New Zealand, commonly known as moas.
family of extinct flightless birds (moas)

Examples

The Dinornithidae thrived in New Zealand until their extinction several centuries ago.
Researchers have uncovered fossilized remains of Dinornithidae in various parts of the country.
The extinction of Dinornithidae was largely caused by human activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - common name for the extinct flightless birds of the family Dinornithidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'deinos' (terrible) + 'ornis' (bird) + 'idae' (family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌnɔːrˈnɪθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / dɪˌnɔːnɪˈθɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless, extinct birds known as moas, native to New Zealand.
family of extinct flightless birds (moas)

Examples

The extinct Dinornithidae once roamed the forests of New Zealand before the arrival of humans.
Scientists studied fossilized eggs attributed to the Dinornithidae for clues about their life cycle.
Members of the Dinornithidae were among the largest birds that ever lived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - any of several extinct flightless birds native to New Zealand

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'deinos' (terrible) + 'ornis' (bird) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
