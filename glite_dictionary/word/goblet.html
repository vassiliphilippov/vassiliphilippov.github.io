goblet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːblət / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblɪt / (BrE)
a drinking glass with a stem and a base, often used for wine or ceremonial purposes
drinking glass with a stem and base

Examples

He raised his goblet in a toast to the guests.
The antique goblet was decorated with intricate designs.
She filled her goblet with red wine and smiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalice - a large cup or goblet, typically used for ceremonial purposes

Etymology

From Old French *gobelet* (drinking cup), diminutive of *gobel* (cup), related to *gober* (to gulp).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːblət / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblɪt / (BrE)
a drinking vessel with a stem and a bowl-shaped cup, often used in ceremonies or religious contexts
bowl-shaped drinking cup with a stem

Examples

She raised the ornate goblet and took a small sip of wine.
During the ceremony, the priest lifted the silver goblet high above the altar.
The medieval goblet was decorated with precious stones and intricate engravings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalice - a large cup or goblet, often used in religious ceremonies

Etymology

From Old French *gobelet* (cup), diminutive of *gobe* (mouthful, cup), from *gober* (to swallow).
