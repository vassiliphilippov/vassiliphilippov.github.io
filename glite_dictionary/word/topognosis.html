topognosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊpəgˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɒpəgˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the ability to recognize or identify the specific place on the skin where a stimulus has been applied
recognition of location of skin stimulus
medical

Examples

Damage to the parietal lobe can impair topognosis, making it difficult to localize touch on the skin.
Doctors tested the patient's topognosis by asking her to identify spots touched on her arm and hand.
In neurological exams, assessing topognosis helps reveal sensory pathway integrity.

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) and 'gnōsis' (knowledge).
