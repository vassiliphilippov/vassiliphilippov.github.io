waddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒd.lə / (BrE)
a person or animal that walks with a waddling gait
one who walks with a waddling gait

Examples

The toddler was a cute little waddler as she learned to walk across the living room.
Penguins are often called natural waddlers because of their charming way of moving.
After his injury, he became a slow waddler rather than the runner he once was.

Synset Examples

fat waddlers who walk like pigeons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waddle - to walk with short steps, swaying from side to side

Etymology

From 'waddle' (to walk with short steps, swaying side to side) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
