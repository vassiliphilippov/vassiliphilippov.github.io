to analyze

1. VERB

/ ˈænəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something in detail in order to understand it better or discover its essential features or meaning
to examine in detail for understanding

Examples

The scientist will analyze the data to gain new insights into the disease.
He spent hours trying to analyze her response for hidden meanings.
Before making a decision, it's important to analyze all available options.

Synset Examples

analyze a sonnet by Shakespeare
analyze the evidence in a criminal trial
analyze your real motives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to look at something carefully and thoroughly
  • Antonym: to synthesize - to combine different elements to form a coherent whole

Etymology

From Greek 'analyein' (to loosen up), from 'ana-' (up) + 'lyein' (to loosen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646245-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæn.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæn.əl.aɪz / (BrE)
to subject (a person) to psychoanalytic treatment in order to explore their unconscious mind or diagnose mental disorders
to subject to psychoanalytic treatment

Examples

After her traumatic experience, she decided to be analyzed by a specialist.
Many patients were analyzed at Freud's clinic in the early twentieth century.
He spent two years being analyzed to better understand his recurring nightmares.

Synset Examples

I was analyzed in Vienna by a famous psychiatrist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to psychoanalyze - to treat using psychoanalytic methods
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈænəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something in detail by breaking it into its components to understand it better or discover its essential features
examine in detail; break down

Examples

The scientist will analyze the samples to identify their chemical composition.
Experts often analyze data trends to predict future market movements.
She spent hours analyzing the text for underlying themes.

Synset Examples

analyze today's financial market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - inspect or study something carefully
  • Antonym: to synthesize - combine parts to form a whole

Etymology

From Greek analyein (to break up), from ana- (up) + lyein (loosen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00738221-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈænəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something in detail by breaking it down into its basic parts or essential features, often in order to understand or interpret it
examine by breaking into parts

Examples

The scientist will analyze the results of the experiment before drawing any conclusions.
Linguists often analyze sentences to show how their parts fit together.
The lab will analyze the blood sample for signs of infection.

Synset Examples

analyze a specimen
analyze a sentence
analyze a chemical compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or study carefully
  • Antonym: to synthesize - to combine parts into a whole, especially after analysis

Etymology

From French 'analyser', based on Greek 'analusis' (a breaking up, loosening).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
