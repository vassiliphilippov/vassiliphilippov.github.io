Felis

1. PROPN

/ ˈfiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals that includes the true cats and most wildcats, in the family Felidae.
genus of true cats and wildcats

Examples

The domestic cat belongs to the genus Felis.
Researchers discovered a new wild species within Felis.
The characteristics of Felis distinguish it from other genera in the family Felidae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Felidae - the biological family including all cats
  • Synonym: cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal, Felis catus

Etymology

From Latin 'felis' meaning 'cat'.
