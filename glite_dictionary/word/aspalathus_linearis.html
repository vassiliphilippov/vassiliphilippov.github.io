Aspalathus linearis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspəˈleɪθəs laɪˈnɪərɪs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈleɪθəs laɪˈnɪərɪs / (BrE)
A South African shrub with flat, pointed leaves and yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used to make the herbal tea known as rooibos.
South African shrub used for rooibos tea

Examples

The leaves of Aspalathus linearis are harvested to produce the popular herbal drink rooibos tea.
Botanists have long studied Aspalathus linearis for its medicinal properties and unique characteristics.
In South Africa, vast fields of Aspalathus linearis can be seen growing in the Cederberg region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rooibos - herbal tea made from Aspalathus linearis leaves

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Aspalathus (genus name) + linearis (Latin for 'linear', referring to the leaf shape).
