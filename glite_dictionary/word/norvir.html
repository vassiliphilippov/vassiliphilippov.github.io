Norvir

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːrˌvɪr / (AmE), / ˈnɔːvɪə / (BrE)
a trademarked drug (ritonavir) used as a protease inhibitor in the treatment of HIV infection
HIV medication (ritonavir)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Norvir as part of the patient's new antiretroviral regimen.
Some patients experience side effects such as nausea when taking Norvir.
The introduction of Norvir was a significant advancement in HIV therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritonavir - antiviral drug used to treat HIV/AIDS
