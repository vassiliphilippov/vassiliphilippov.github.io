xerox

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪəˌrɑks / (AmE), / ˈzɪə.rɒks / (BrE)
a copy of a document or image made using a xerographic copying machine
copy made by a xerographic copier

Examples

He handed me a xerox of the original contract for my records.
Please make three xeroxes of this report before the meeting.
The xerox was almost as clear as the handwritten letter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopy - a copy made using a photographic or electronic process

Etymology

From Xerox, the trademarked name of the company that developed xerographic copying technology.

2. VERB

/ ˈzɪrəks / (AmE), / ˈzɪərɒks / (BrE)
to make a copy of a document or image using a xerographic photocopier; to photocopy
to photocopy using a xerographic machine

Examples

Could you xerox these documents for the meeting?
She quickly xeroxed the forms before heading to the client’s office.
He was seen xeroxing pages from the textbook in the library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopy - to make a photographic copy of a document

Etymology

From Xerox, the trademark name of a prominent company that developed early xerographic copiers, based on Greek 'xēros' (dry) + '-graphy' (writing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01740180-v
Synset POS: v
