epistle

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsl̩ / (BrE)
A book of the New Testament written as a letter by an Apostle to a person or group, containing teachings or guidance.
New Testament letter from an Apostle

Examples

Many Christians study the Epistles to understand early church teachings.
The Epistle to the Romans is one of Paul's most influential letters.
Each epistle in the New Testament provides specific guidance for believers.

Etymology

From Latin epistola, from Greek epistolē (letter, message).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪsəl / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsəl / (BrE)
a formal or lengthy letter, often written in a serious or literary style
formal or lengthy letter

Examples

He wrote a lengthy epistle explaining his reasons for leaving the company.
In the eighteenth century, poets often composed epistles to friends and patrons.
She received an epistle from her aunt that detailed family history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter - a written or printed message addressed to a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'epistola', from Greek 'epistolē' (letter, message).
