Harakat ul-Jihad-I-Islami

1. PROPN

/ hɑːˈrɑːkət ʊl dʒɪˈhɑːd i ˌɪsˈlɑːmi / (AmE), / hɑːˈrɑːkət ʊl dʒɪˈhɑːd i ˌɪsˈlɑːmi / (BrE)
an extremist militant group based in Pakistan-administered Kashmir, seeking to establish an Islamic government and known for its connections with the Taliban in Afghanistan.
Pakistani militant Islamist group

Examples

Harakat ul-Jihad-I-Islami was founded in the 1980s with the aim of establishing an Islamic state in Kashmir.
International reports have linked members of Harakat ul-Jihad-I-Islami to several cross-border attacks.
The Pakistani authorities have at times denied any official ties to Harakat ul-Jihad-I-Islami.

Etymology

From Arabic, meaning 'Movement of Islamic Jihad.'
