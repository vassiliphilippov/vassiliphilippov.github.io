dacryocystitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækri.oʊˌsɪsˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪəʊsɪsˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the lacrimal sac, often causing pain, swelling, and obstruction of the tear drainage into the nose
inflammation of the lacrimal sac
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with dacryocystitis after presenting with swelling near the inner corner of her eye.
Antibiotics are commonly prescribed to treat acute cases of dacryocystitis.
If dacryocystitis recurs frequently, surgical intervention may be necessary.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear) + kystis (sac) + -itis (inflammation).
