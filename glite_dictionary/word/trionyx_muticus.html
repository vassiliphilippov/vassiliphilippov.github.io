Trionyx muticus

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑːnɪks mjuːˈtiːkəs / (AmE), / traɪˈɒnɪks mjuːˈtiːkəs / (BrE)
A species of softshell river turtle found in the Mississippi basin, preferring habitats with running water.
river turtle of the Mississippi basin

Examples

Trionyx muticus is most commonly found along the sandy banks of major rivers in the Midwest.
Unlike other turtles, Trionyx muticus prefers swift-moving water to still ponds.
Because of habitat loss, populations of Trionyx muticus have decreased in recent decades.

Etymology

From Greek 'trionyx' meaning 'three-clawed', and Latin 'muticus' meaning 'docked' or 'blunt'.
