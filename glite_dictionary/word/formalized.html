formalized

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrməˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlaɪzd / (BrE)
Made official, legitimate, or recognized, especially through a defined process or by following required procedures.
made official or recognized

Examples

Her temporary appointment was finally formalized after months of review.
The company formalized its policies on remote work this year.
Our partnership became much stronger once it was formalized through a contract.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: informal - not officially recognized or made official

Etymology

From 'formalize' + -ed, based on Latin 'formalis' meaning 'of form'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrməˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlaɪzd / (BrE)
characterized by strict or systematic adherence to established forms or conventions, especially in religion or art
characterized by adherence to established forms

Examples

The museum's display of formalized rituals explores the roots of religious ceremonies.
Her artwork became increasingly formalized, drawing on strict aesthetic principles.
Classical ballet is a highly formalized form of dance with precise movements and traditions.

Synset Examples

highly formalized plays like ‘Waiting for Godot’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - following traditional forms or standards
  • Antonym: informal - not following established or official forms or conventions

Etymology

From 'formal' + '-ize' + '-ed', indicating something made to conform to formality.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrməˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlaɪzd / (BrE)
concerned with or characterized by strict adherence to established forms or conventions, especially in art or religion
strictly following recognized forms

Examples

The ritual was highly formalized, with each step precisely choreographed.
Their arguments often became formalized debates instead of open discussions.
Many traditional art forms are formalized, requiring artists to follow established methods.

Synset Examples

highly formalized plays like ‘Waiting for Godot’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - following accepted customs and standards
  • Antonym: informal - not following established conventions

Etymology

From Latin 'formalis' (relating to form) via French 'formaliser'; in English, formed from 'formalize' + '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02741040-a
Synset POS: a
