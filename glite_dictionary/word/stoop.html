stoop

1. NOUN

/ stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
a small porch or set of steps at the front entrance of a house, especially in American contexts
small porch/steps at a house entrance
american

Examples

She sat on the stoop to watch the children play in the street.
Every morning, he swept the leaves from the front stoop.
Neighbors often gather on the stoop to chat during the summer evenings.

Etymology

From Dutch 'stoep' meaning 'step' or 'sidewalk.'

2. NOUN

/ stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
a basin, usually at the entrance of a church, containing holy water for ritual use
holy water basin in a church

Examples

The old cathedral still has its original stoop near the doorway for worshippers to use.
She dipped her fingers into the stoop and made the sign of the cross.
The marble stoop was ornately carved and filled with fresh holy water.

Etymology

From Middle English ‘stoup’ or ‘stoupe’, meaning a container or vessel, of Germanic origin.
