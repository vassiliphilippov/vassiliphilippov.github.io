Cyrus the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪrəs ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪrəs ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
The king of Persia who founded the Persian Empire and ruled from about 600 to 529 BC.
founder of the Persian Empire

Examples

Many historians regard Cyrus the Great as one of the most remarkable leaders of the ancient world.
Cyrus the Great is credited with establishing principles of tolerance and human rights in his empire.
The tomb of Cyrus the Great remains a significant monument in Iran.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'Kŷros ho Mégas', Latinized as 'Cyrus Magnus', from Old Persian 'Kūruš'.
