poisonous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪzənəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənəs / (BrE)
Having the qualities or effects of a poison; capable of causing illness or death if swallowed, inhaled, or absorbed.
containing poison; harmful if taken in

Examples

Some mushrooms are poisonous and should never be eaten.
The snake's bite was poisonous and required immediate medical attention.
They discovered a poisonous chemical in the contaminated water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxic - containing or being poisonous material; harmful or deadly
  • Synonym: noxious - harmful, poisonous, or very unpleasant
  • Antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From Old French *poisonneux*, from *poison* (poison).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪzənəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənəs / (BrE)
Containing or producing poison; harmful or unsafe to eat or touch.
containing poison; unsafe to eat

Examples

You should never eat those mushrooms because they are poisonous.
The scientist warned us that the liquid in the container is highly poisonous.
Some plants are poisonous to pets and should be kept out of reach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxic - containing or being poisonous material
  • Antonym: edible - safe to eat

Etymology

From Old French 'poisonneux', from 'poison' (poison).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪzənəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənəs / (BrE)
Full of deep ill will and intended to harm or wound emotionally; extremely spiteful or malicious.
full of harmful ill will; spiteful

Examples

She ignored his poisonous remarks and tried to stay calm.
There was a poisonous atmosphere in the room after the argument.
His poisonous gossip damaged several people's reputations.

Synset Examples

poisonous hate
venomous criticism
vicious gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venomous - showing strong hostility or spite; deeply hateful
  • Synonym: vicious - deliberately cruel or malicious

Etymology

From Old French 'poisonneux', from 'poison' (poison), from Latin 'potio' (drink, potion).
