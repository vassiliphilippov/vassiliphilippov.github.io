Hyacinthoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪəˌsɪnˈθɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪəˌsɪnˈθɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial bulbous plants native to western Europe and North Africa, often known as bluebells, sometimes placed in the family Hyacinthaceae.
genus of perennial bulbous plants

Examples

The woodland was carpeted with blooming Hyacinthoides during the spring.
Botanists recently reclassified several species under the genus Hyacinthoides.
Hyacinthoides is sometimes confused with members of the Hyacinthaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - common name for species in Hyacinthoides, especially Hyacinthoides non-scripta

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining 'Hyacinthus' (a related genus) and Greek '-oides' (resembling).
