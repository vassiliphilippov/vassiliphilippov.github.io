imponderable

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑndɚəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒndərəbəl / (BrE)
something whose impact or outcome cannot be precisely measured or assessed
factor impossible to measure or assess accurately

Examples

The project was affected by several imponderables that no one had anticipated.
When investing in stocks, you must always account for imponderables like market sentiment.
There are too many imponderables to guarantee the plan's success.

Synset Examples

human behavior depends on many imponderables

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being uncertain or unknown
  • Antonym: measurable - able to be measured

Etymology

From Latin 'imponderabilis', from 'im-' (not) + 'ponderabilis' (able to be weighed), from 'pondus' (weight).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑːnd(ə)rəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒnd(ə)rəbl̩ / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to estimate, assess, or evaluate with precision due to being unknown or indeterminate.
difficult to measure or estimate precisely

Examples

The outcome of the experiment depended on several imponderable variables.
Investors must account for imponderable risks that cannot be precisely calculated.
There are always imponderable factors that make predictions uncertain in such situations.

Synset Examples

such imponderable human factors as aesthetic sensibility

Related Terms

  • Antonym: measurable - able to be measured or precisely assessed

Etymology

From Latin imponderabilis, from in- (not) + ponderabilis (able to be weighed), from pondus (weight).
