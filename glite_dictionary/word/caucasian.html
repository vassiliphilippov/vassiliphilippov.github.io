Caucasian

1. ADJ

/ kɔːˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
Of or relating to the Caucasus region, its people, or languages.
relating to the Caucasus region

Examples

The region is home to many unique Caucasian languages.
She studied the history of several Caucasian ethnic groups.
The museum had an exhibition on Caucasian culture and art.

Synset Examples

Caucasian languages

Etymology

From the Caucasus, a mountain region between Europe and Asia; influenced by Latin 'Caucasus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02854844-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ kɔˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
a person belonging to a race traditionally identified as having light or pale skin, originally from Europe, North Africa, or parts of Western Asia
person of European or light-skinned descent

Examples

The survey showed that most participants identified as Caucasian.
She is a Caucasian who grew up in Eastern Europe.
The film featured a diverse cast, including both Caucasian and non-Caucasian actors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: White - a person with light skin associated with European ancestry

Etymology

From Caucasus (a region in Eurasia), referring to people thought to originate from there, plus -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09660255-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ kɔˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
relating to white people of European descent, especially in reference to physical characteristics, ethnicity, or ancestry
of white European descent

Examples

He identified himself as Caucasian on the application form.
The study compared dietary habits among Caucasian and Asian populations.
Both Caucasian and African American students participated in the survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: White - relating to people of light skin color, especially of European origin

Etymology

From Caucasus, a region between the Black and Caspian seas, regarded in the 18th–19th centuries as the origin of European peoples + English -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00244343-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ kɔˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪzɪən / (BrE)
any of a group of languages spoken in the Caucasus region, which are not related to languages spoken elsewhere.
languages spoken in the Caucasus region

Examples

The Caucasian languages include Georgian, Chechen, and Abkhaz.
Linguists study Caucasian languages to understand their unique grammar systems.
Some Caucasian languages have sounds that are rare in other parts of the world.

Etymology

From the Caucasus, the mountainous region between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06990840-n
Synset POS: n
