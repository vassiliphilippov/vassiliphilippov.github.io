pyrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.roʊp / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪ.rəʊp / (BrE)
a deep red variety of garnet used as a gemstone
deep red garnet gemstone

Examples

The jeweler admired the brilliant red color of the pyrope in the ring.
A flawless pyrope can fetch a high price at auction.
She wore a necklace studded with sparkling pyropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals often used as gemstones

Etymology

From Greek 'pyropēs' (fiery-eyed), from 'pyr' (fire) + 'ōps' (eye).
