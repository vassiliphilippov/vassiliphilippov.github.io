candle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkændl̩ / (BrE)
a former unit of luminous intensity equal to the luminous intensity emitted by a standard candle; replaced by the candela in the International System of Units (SI)
former unit of luminous intensity

Examples

The lamp's brightness was once measured in candles before the adoption of the modern SI unit.
Scientists now prefer the candela instead of the candle for measuring luminous intensity.
Old photometric tables often list values in candle units, which can be confusing to readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candela - the SI base unit of luminous intensity

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkændl / (BrE)
A cylinder or block of wax or tallow with a central wick that is burned to provide light.
stick of wax with a wick; for light

Examples

She lit a candle when the power went out during the storm.
The room was filled with the soft glow of a single candle.
He made a wish and blew out all the candles on his birthday cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamp - device for giving light, commonly using oil, electricity, or gas

Etymology

From Latin 'candela' (a light, torch, candle).

3. VERB

/ ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkændl / (BrE)
to examine eggs for freshness or quality by holding them up to a light source
inspect eggs with a light

Examples

The farmer carefully candled each egg before packing them for sale.
Modern hatcheries often use machines to candle hundreds of eggs at once.
Before incubation, it is important to candle the eggs to check for cracks or defects.

Etymology

From the noun 'candle', used as a light source for this inspection method.
