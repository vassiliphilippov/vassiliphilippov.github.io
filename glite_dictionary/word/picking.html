picking

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gathering or harvesting crops, fruit, or similar produce by hand
the act of gathering crops or fruit

Examples

Berry picking is a popular activity in this region during summer.
The apple picking season usually begins in September.
After hours of picking, their baskets were full of ripe strawberries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvesting - the process of gathering a crop from the fields

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the amount of a crop gathered during a single harvest or at one time
amount of crop gathered in one harvest

Examples

The first picking of strawberries always fetches the best prices at market.
Yesterday's picking yielded nearly 50 crates of apples.
Each picking from the vineyard is kept separate for quality control.

Synset Examples

he sent the first picking of berries to the market
it was the biggest peach pick in years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - the process or period of gathering in crops
  • Synonym: crop - the total yield of agricultural produce gathered in a season

Etymology

From 'pick' + -ing, meaning the act or result of picking.
