drake

1. NOUN

/ dreɪk / (AmE), / dreɪk / (BrE)
an adult male duck, especially one of wild or domestic species
adult male duck

Examples

The drake led the flock of ducks across the pond with brightly colored feathers.
You can often distinguish a drake from a female duck by its vibrant plumage.
During the spring, the drake becomes more territorial as it protects its mate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

From Middle English drake, from Old English draca (male duck), distinct from dragon-related meanings.
