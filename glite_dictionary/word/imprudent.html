imprudent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprudənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːdnt / (BrE)
not showing care or good judgment; unwise
not wise; lacking good judgment

Examples

It would be imprudent to invest all your savings in one company.
Her imprudent remarks embarrassed everyone at the dinner table.
Driving without insurance is extremely imprudent and risky.

Synset Examples

very imprudent of her mother to encourage her in such silly romantic ideas
would be imprudent for a noneconomist to talk about the details of economic policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prudent - showing care and good judgment; wise
  • Synonym: reckless - without thought of danger or consequences; rash

Etymology

From Latin 'imprudent-', from 'im-' (not) + 'prudent-' (foreseeing, wise).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruːdənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːdənt / (BrE)
not showing care for the consequences of an action; lacking good judgment or self-restraint.
not showing good judgment

Examples

It would be imprudent to invest all your money in a single stock.
Her imprudent comments caused unnecessary confusion during the meeting.
He was criticized for making an imprudent decision without consulting his team.

Synset Examples

an imprudent remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prudent - showing good judgment; careful and wise
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard to danger or consequences; rash

Etymology

From Latin imprudens, imprudentis, meaning 'not foreseeing, unaware, rash'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00777014-s
Synset POS: s
