rani

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːni / (AmE), / ˈrɑːni / (BrE)
A Hindu princess or the wife of a raja (king) in South Asia.
Hindu princess or wife of a raja

Examples

The rani was admired for her bravery and wisdom during the uprising.
Visitors to the palace often glimpsed the rani participating in public ceremonies.
She was crowned rani after her marriage to the local raja.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raja - a king or prince in India
  • Synonym: queen - female ruler or wife of a king

Etymology

From Hindi rānī, from Sanskrit rājñī meaning 'queen', feminine of rāja (king).
