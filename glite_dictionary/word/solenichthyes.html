Solenichthyes

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌlɛnɪkˈθaɪiːz / (AmE), / səʊˌlɛnɪkˈθiːz / (BrE)
A small order of chiefly tropical marine fishes of varied and unusual forms, all characterized by having a small mouth at the end of an elongated tubular snout.
order of tropical marine fishes with tubular snouts

Examples

Marine biologists recently discovered a new species within the Solenichthyes in the South Pacific.
The Solenichthyes are known for their unusual tubular snouts and small mouths.
Among the Solenichthyes, the shrimpfishes display some of the most bizarre body shapes.

Etymology

From Greek 'solen' (pipe) and 'ichthys' (fish).
