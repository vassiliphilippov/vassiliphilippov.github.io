heaviness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛvinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being heavy; great weight compared to other things
quality of being heavy; great weight

Examples

The heaviness of the box made it difficult to lift on his own.
She was surprised by the heaviness of the winter coat when she picked it up.
Engineers must account for the heaviness of materials used in construction.

Synset Examples

the heaviness of lead

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being light in weight
  • Synonym: weight - the amount that something weighs

Etymology

From Old English 'hefig' (heavy) + '-ness' (state, quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛv.i.nɪs / (BrE)
A feeling of deep and lingering sadness or emotional burden.
persistent emotional sadness

Examples

She couldn't shake the heaviness that settled over her after the breakup.
A sense of heaviness filled the room during the memorial service.
Writing about her feelings helped ease the heaviness in her heart.

Synset Examples

nothing lifted the heaviness of her heart after her loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadness - The state of being unhappy or sorrowful
  • Antonym: lightness - The quality of having little weight; also relief or lack of emotional burden

Etymology

Formed from 'heavy' + '-ness'; 'heavy' from Old English 'hefig' (having weight).

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛvinɪs / (BrE)
A feeling of unwelcome difficulty or burden; the quality of weighing down emotionally or mentally.
feeling of burden or difficulty

Examples

The heaviness of her responsibilities made it difficult for her to enjoy the holiday.
There's a certain heaviness in the air whenever bad news is about to be delivered.
He spoke of the heaviness he felt after losing his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or duty; a cause of hardship
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being easy or not burdensome (emotionally or physically)

Etymology

From Middle English hevenes, from Old English hefignes (heaviness, weight, sorrow), from hefig (heavy).

5. NOUN

/ ˈhev.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhev.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a quality of being laborious, solemn, or oppressive, especially in style, manner, or atmosphere, lacking grace or fluency
oppressive or ponderous quality

Examples

The heaviness of the novel's prose made it challenging to read for long periods.
There was a certain heaviness in his speech that left the audience disengaged.
Critics commented on the heaviness of the film, noting its solemn tone and slow pace.

Synset Examples

a book so serious that it sometimes subsided into ponderousness
his lectures tend to heaviness and repetition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponderousness - the quality of being heavy, dull, or lacking lightness, especially in manner or style
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being airy, graceful, or not oppressive in style or manner

Etymology

From Old English 'hefig' (heavy) + '-ness' (state or quality).
