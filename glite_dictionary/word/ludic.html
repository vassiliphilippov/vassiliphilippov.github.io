ludic

1. ADJ

/ ˈluː.dɪk / (AmE), / ˈluː.dɪk / (BrE)
relating to play or playfulness, especially in behavior, activities, or attitude
relating to play or playfulness

Examples

Her ludic approach to teaching made the classroom a joyful place to learn.
Modern art often includes ludic elements that encourage audience interaction.
The children's ludic laughter echoed through the playground all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playful - full of fun and high spirits

Etymology

From Latin ludicus (of play), from ludus (play, sport, game).
