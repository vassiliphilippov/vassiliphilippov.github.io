euphonious

1. ADJ

/ juːˈfoʊniəs / (AmE), / juːˈfəʊniəs / (BrE)
(of sound) pleasing to the ear; having a pleasant and harmonious sound.
pleasant-sounding; harmonious

Examples

The poem was praised for its euphonious language and graceful rhythm.
She chose a euphonious name for her new company because it was easy to say and remember.
Birdsongs at dawn create a euphonious backdrop to the quiet morning.

Synset Examples

a euphonious trill of silver laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - having a pleasant melody; tuneful
  • Antonym: cacophonous - having a harsh or discordant sound

Etymology

From Greek euphōnos (sweet-voiced), from eu- (good) + phōnē (sound, voice).

2. ADJ

/ juˈfoʊniəs / (AmE), / juːˈfəʊniəs / (BrE)
(of sound, speech, or language) pleasing to the ear; harmonious and smooth-sounding.
pleasing or harmonious in sound

Examples

Her euphonious Southern accent charmed everyone at the gathering.
The euphonious melody soothed the listeners after a long day.
Writers often choose euphonious words to create a more pleasant effect in their poetry.

Synset Examples

her euphonious Southern speech

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cacophonous - harsh or discordant in sound
  • Synonym: melodious - having a pleasant musical sound

Etymology

From Greek euphōnos (sweet-voiced), from eu- (well) + phōnē (sound, voice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01458000-s
Synset POS: s
