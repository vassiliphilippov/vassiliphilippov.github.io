distance

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
A point in time far from the present moment; a remote or extended period in the past or future.
remote point in time

Examples

From a distance of several decades, the events of his youth seemed almost unreal.
She hoped to look back, from the distance of years, with pride on her achievements.
At the distance of twenty years, their friendship had endured all obstacles.

Synset Examples

if that happens, it will be at some distance in the future
at a distance of ten years he had forgotten many of the details

Etymology

From Latin 'distantia' (a standing apart).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
The amount of space between two points, places, or objects, measured in units such as meters or miles.
space between two points

Examples

We could barely see the mountains in the distance.
The distance between the two towns is roughly 50 miles.
He shouted across the distance, hoping she would hear him.

Synset Examples

the distance from New York to Chicago
he determined the length of the shortest line segment joining the two points

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proximity - closeness in space or relationship
  • Synonym: remoteness - being far away in space

Etymology

From Latin 'distantia', from 'distant-', meaning 'standing apart'.
