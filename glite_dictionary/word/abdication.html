abdication

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The formal act of giving up a position of power or responsibility, especially by a monarch or leader.
formal renunciation of power

Examples

The king's unexpected abdication shocked the entire nation.
After years of controversy, her abdication from the throne was finally announced.
The history book details the abdication of several famous monarchs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the act of voluntarily leaving an office or position

Etymology

From Latin abdicatio(n-), from abdicare (to renounce, reject).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæb.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of formally renouncing or giving up a throne, office, or responsibility.
the act of giving up a throne or office

Examples

The king's unexpected abdication shocked the entire nation.
After years of unrest, the leader's abdication was seen as inevitable.
Her abdication of responsibility led to confusion among her team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the act of giving up a job or position voluntarily
  • Antonym: coronation - the act of crowning a sovereign

Etymology

From Latin abdicatio (renunciation), from abdicare (to disown, renounce).
