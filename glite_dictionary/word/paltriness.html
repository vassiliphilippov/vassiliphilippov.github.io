paltriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔltrinəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːltrinɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being paltry; lack of value or importance because of insignificance or meanness.
insignificant worth or quality

Examples

The paltriness of the reward did not motivate anyone to participate in the contest.
She complained about the paltriness of his apology, finding it insincere and insufficient.
Many were disappointed by the paltriness of the government’s response to the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthlessness - the state of having no value or use
  • Antonym: significance - the quality of being worthy of attention; importance

Etymology

From 'paltry' + '-ness', where 'paltry' is probably from dialectal English 'poultry' meaning trivial things.
