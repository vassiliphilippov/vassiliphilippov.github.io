Abramis brama

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪmɪs ˈbrɑːmə / (AmE), / əˈbreɪmɪs ˈbrɑːmə / (BrE)
A European freshwater fish, also known as the common bream, with a flattened body and silvery scales, considered to have little value as food.
a European freshwater fish (common bream)

Examples

The river was filled with Abramis brama, shimmering in the afternoon sun.
Anglers often catch Abramis brama, although it is not highly prized for eating.
Biologists conducted a survey of Abramis brama populations in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bream - a common name for various species of freshwater and marine fish

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name 'Abramis brama', where 'Abramis' is the genus and 'brama' is the species.
