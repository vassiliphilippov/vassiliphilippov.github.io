Pulsatilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the buttercup family, commonly known as pasqueflowers, native to meadows and prairies in Eurasia and North America.
a genus of plants; pasqueflowers

Examples

The garden's rocky border was dotted with blooming Pulsatilla in early spring.
Many herbalists value extracts from Pulsatilla for their traditional medicinal uses.
The distinctive feathery seed heads make Pulsatilla an attractive choice for ornamental gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anemone - a related genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family

Etymology

From Latin *pulsatilla*, diminutive of *pulsatus* (beaten, quivered), referring to the nodding flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), commonly known as pasqueflowers, native to temperate and cold regions of Europe, Asia, and North America
a genus of pasqueflowers (plants)

Examples

The meadow was full of blooming Pulsatilla in early spring.
Gardeners value Pulsatilla for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers and feathery seed heads.
Some botanists classify Pulsatilla within the larger genus Anemone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anemone - a related genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family

Etymology

From Medieval Latin, from Latin 'pulsare' (to beat or strike), perhaps referring to the motion of the flowers in the wind.
