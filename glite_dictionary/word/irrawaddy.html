Irrawaddy

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪrəˈwɑːdi / (AmE), / ˌɪrəˈwædi / (BrE)
the principal river of Myanmar, flowing from the north to the south of the country and emptying into the Andaman Sea
main river of Myanmar

Examples

The Irrawaddy is vital to transportation and trade throughout Myanmar.
Numerous villages line the banks of the Irrawaddy River.
Flooding of the Irrawaddy can disrupt life in the delta regions.

Etymology

From Burmese 'Ayeyarwady.'
