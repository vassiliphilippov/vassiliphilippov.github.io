affected

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / əˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
influenced or changed by an external factor; acted upon by something
influenced by something

Examples

The crops were severely affected by the drought this year.
Several employees felt affected by the recent changes in company policy.
Her performance was deeply affected by the loss of her mentor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaffected - not influenced or changed by something

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'affect', from Latin 'afficere' (to do to, act upon).

2. ADJ

/ əˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / əˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) artificial, pretentious, or designed to impress others rather than genuine.
artificial or pretentious (person/behavior)

Examples

Her affected accent made it clear she was trying to impress the group.
He gave an affected laugh that didn't sound sincere at all.
Many critics found the actor's performance too affected to be believable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - not natural, made to seem real
  • Antonym: natural - genuine, not artificial or pretentious
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00073656-a
Synset POS: a
