overacting

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈæktɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of performing with excessive or exaggerated emotion, especially in a theatrical or film context
exaggerated or excessive acting

Examples

Her overacting in the movie made the dramatic scenes hard to take seriously.
Critics accused him of overacting during the final confrontation.
Some find overacting entertaining, especially in comedies or melodramas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underacting - acting with less emotion or restraint than appropriate

Etymology

From 'over-' meaning 'excessive' + 'acting', formed in English.
