Alstroemeriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ælˌstroʊməriˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ælˌstrəʊmɛriˈeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, often recognized separately from Liliaceae in some botanical classifications, including species like Alstroemeria.
family of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The Alstroemeriaceae family is known for its showy, colorful flowers often used in bouquets.
Botanists sometimes include Alstroemeriaceae within the larger family Amaryllidaceae.
Members of the Alstroemeriaceae are mostly native to South America.

Etymology

From the genus name Alstroemeria, named after Swedish botanist Claus von Alstroemer, plus the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌælstroʊˌmɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌalstrəʊˌmɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, sometimes included within Liliaceae or Amaryllidaceae, known for species such as the Peruvian lily.
family of flowering plants (Peruvian lily, etc.)

Examples

The Alstroemeriaceae family is known for its ornamental plants such as the Peruvian lily.
Many species in the Alstroemeriaceae are native to South America.
Some botanists classify Alstroemeriaceae as a separate group from the Liliaceae.

Etymology

From the genus Alstroemeria, named after Swedish botanist Baron Claus von Alstroemer.
