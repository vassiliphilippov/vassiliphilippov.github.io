to reelect

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to elect someone to a position again, especially after the end of a previous term in office
elect again, especially to office

Examples

Voters decided to reelect the incumbent mayor for another term.
The board may choose to reelect its current chairperson at the annual meeting.
Despite controversies, the governor was reelected by a comfortable margin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elect - to choose someone for a position by voting

Etymology

Prefix 're-' (again) + 'elect', from Latin 'eligere' (to choose).
