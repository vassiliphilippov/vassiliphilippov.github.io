Shortia galacifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtiə ˌɡæləsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtɪə ˌɡæləsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a plant native to the southeastern United States, known for its solitary white, pink-tinged funnel-shaped flowers and large glossy green leaves that turn bronze-red in fall
plant with pink-tinged white flowers and glossy green leaves

Examples

The rare Shortia galacifolia blooms with delicate white flowers in the early spring forests.
Gardeners prize Shortia galacifolia for its striking foliage that turns red in the autumn.
Many botanists travel to the Appalachian region to see wild populations of Shortia galacifolia.

Etymology

Genus name 'Shortia' honors American botanist Charles Wilkins Short; 'galacifolia' from Latin 'galax' (milky) and 'folia' (leaves), referring to its leaves.
