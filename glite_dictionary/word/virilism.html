virilism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪrɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.ɪ.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the development of male secondary sexual characteristics in a female, or prematurely in a young boy
development of male traits in females or boys
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited signs of virilism, including a deepened voice and increased body hair.
Doctors are investigating the underlying causes of virilism in adolescents.
Prolonged exposure to certain medications can sometimes lead to virilism in women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculinization - the process of developing male characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'virilis' (of a man) + '-ism'.
