rhomboid

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːm.bɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.bɔɪd / (BrE)
A parallelogram with oblique angles and only opposite sides equal in length; a quadrilateral with adjacent sides of unequal length.
type of parallelogram with oblique angles

Examples

The textbook illustration showed a bright blue rhomboid next to a square.
If the adjacent sides of the parallelogram are unequal, the shape is called a rhomboid.
Architects sometimes incorporate the shape of a rhomboid into their building designs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: parallelogram - a quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel
  • Degree Higher: rhombus - a parallelogram with all sides equal in length

Etymology

From Latin 'rhomboides', from Greek 'rhomboeidēs', meaning rhombus-like.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːm.bɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.bɔɪd / (BrE)
either of two muscles in the upper back, beneath the trapezius, that connect the spine to the shoulder blade and help move the shoulder blade
muscle of the upper back
medical

Examples

The physical therapist recommended exercises to strengthen the rhomboid muscles.
An injury to the rhomboid can cause pain between the shoulder blades.
Proper posture helps engage the rhomboid for better back support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezius - large muscle in the upper back and neck

Etymology

From Greek *rhomboeidēs* (shaped like a rhombus), from *rhombos* (rhombus).
