cress

1. NOUN

/ krɛs / (AmE), / krɛs / (BrE)
a plant of the cabbage family with small, pungent leaves, often eaten raw in salads; especially watercress and related species
edible pungent-leafed plant

Examples

She sprinkled chopped cress on her sandwich for extra flavor.
Fresh cress grows quickly and is often used as a garnish.
Gardeners often grow cress on the windowsill for use in salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - an edible aquatic plant with pungent leaves

Etymology

From Old English 'cresse', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ krɛs / (AmE), / krɛs / (BrE)
any of several plants with small, edible, pungent leaves, commonly used in salads or as a garnish
edible plant with pungent leaves

Examples

She sprinkled fresh cress on top of the egg salad.
A sandwich filled with cress and cucumber makes a refreshing snack.
Gardeners often grow cress on the windowsill for its quick and easy harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - a type of cress that grows in water, with spicy leaves

Etymology

Old English 'cresse', related to Dutch 'kers' and German 'Kresse'.
