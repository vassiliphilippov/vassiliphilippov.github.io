nationalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnaʃnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring a business, industry, or asset from private to state ownership or control
transfer of private assets to state ownership

Examples

The nationalization of the railway system was a major political decision.
After the nationalization of the banks, all financial transactions came under government control.
Many workers supported the nationalization of the coal industry in the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: privatization - the transfer of ownership from state to private hands, opposite of nationalization

Etymology

From French 'nationalisation', based on 'nation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming or becoming a nation, especially as a distinct political entity
the forming or becoming a nation

Examples

The nationalization of previously fragmented territories shaped modern states in Europe.
Historians often debate the nationalization of identity during the 19th century.
The nationalization of the region was a gradual process involving language, culture, and governance.

Etymology

From nation + -alization, modeled after 'nationalize'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnaʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something national in character or scope
making something national in character

Examples

His policies led to the nationalization of local traditions, promoting a unified cultural identity.
The nationalization of the language helped foster communication throughout the vast country.
Efforts at nationalization sought to give the holidays a distinctly national character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalization - the action of bringing under government control

Etymology

From 'national' + '-ization', modeled after 'nationalize'.
