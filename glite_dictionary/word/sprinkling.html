sprinkling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
a light, brief fall of rain that only affects some spots, not widespread
a light, patchy rain shower

Examples

There was just a sprinkling of rain last night, enough to wet the ground but not much more.
A light sprinkling fell as we walked to the station, but the sun was shining a block away.
Farmers hoped for more than a sprinkling, as their fields were still too dry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drizzle - very light rain, finer than a sprinkling
  • Degree Higher: downpour - a very heavy, sudden rain shower

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, frequentative of sprengen, from Old English sprengan, related to 'sprinkle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
(Rare) The act or method of baptizing by scattering a small amount of water on a person's head instead of immersing.
baptism by scattering water

Examples

The church practiced sprinkling as the preferred form of baptism for infants.
Some denominations accept sprinkling while others insist on full immersion during baptism.
Historical records indicate that sprinkling became more common in Europe after the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immersion - baptism by fully submerging in water
  • Synonym: baptism - Christian rite of admission and adoption often by water

3. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of scattering drops of liquid, especially water, over a surface or object
the act of scattering drops of liquid

Examples

The priest performed a sprinkling of holy water during the ceremony.
A gentle sprinkling of rain refreshed the dry earth.
There was a sprinkling of water on the leaves after the morning dew.

Synset Examples

baptized with a sprinkling of holy water
a sparge of warm water over the malt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - a fine stream of liquid dispersed in small drops
  • Degree Higher: dousing - the act of wetting thoroughly

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, related to 'sprinkle', of Germanic origin.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
a small number or thin layer of things spread or scattered over an area
small amount scattered about

Examples

There was a sprinkling of snow on the ground this morning.
A sprinkling of applause followed her announcement, polite but not enthusiastic.
He added a sprinkling of herbs to the soup before serving.

Synset Examples

the first scatterings of green
a sprinkling of grey at his temples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scattering - a small number of things spread or scattered over an area
  • Degree Lower: handful - a small number or quantity
  • Degree Higher: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From the verb 'sprinkle' + -ing; 'sprinkle' from Middle English 'sprenklen', of Germanic origin.
