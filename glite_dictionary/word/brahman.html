Brahman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmən / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmən / (BrE)
a breed of large, usually greyish, humped cattle originally developed in the United States from Indian breeds, valued for its resistance to heat and ticks and widely used for crossbreeding
a heat- and tick-resistant cattle breed

Examples

The rancher imported several Brahman calves to improve his herd's resistance to local parasites.
Brahman cattle are well suited to hot, humid climates thanks to their distinctive hump and tough skin.
By crossbreeding Brahman bulls with local cows, farmers have produced livestock that thrive in the Southern US.

Etymology

From Hindi *brāhman*, relating to the sacred or high caste; applied to cattle imported from India.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmən / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmən / (BrE)
a member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally priests or scholars
a member of the highest Hindu caste

Examples

Her family identifies as Brahman and follows many sacred rituals.
Many ancient texts were preserved and interpreted by learned Brahmans.
As a Brahman, he took part in religious ceremonies from a young age.

Synset Examples

originally all brahmans were priests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform sacred rituals
  • Synonym: caste - one of the hereditary classes in Hindu society
  • Antonym: Shudra - member of the lowest Hindu caste

Etymology

From Sanskrit brāhmaṇaḥ (member of the priestly class).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑː.mən / (AmE), / ˈbrɑː.mən / (BrE)
A member of the highest of the four Hindu varnas, traditionally priests or scholars.
member of the Hindu priestly caste

Examples

As a Brahman, he was responsible for performing religious rituals and ceremonies.
Many Brahmans have traditionally devoted themselves to studying sacred texts.
The family was proud of its long Brahman lineage and scholarly achievements.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Kshatriya - member of the second-highest Hindu caste (warriors, rulers)
  • Degree Lower: Vaishya - member of the third Hindu caste (merchants, traders)
  • Degree Lower: Shudra - member of the lowest Hindu caste (laborers, servants)

Etymology

From Sanskrit *brāhmaṇaḥ* (priest, member of the priestly caste).
