to claver

1. VERB

/ ˈkleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkleɪvə / (BrE)
to engage in light, informal conversation; to chat or gossip casually without serious purpose
talk casually or chat
archaic

Examples

We sat on the porch and clavered away the afternoon, not saying anything important.
Every Sunday after church, the neighbors would claver for hours by the gate.
They clavered over coffee, discussing the weather and local news.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people and their private lives

Etymology

From Scots, probably from Middle English 'claver', related to 'claver' (clover), possibly from the idea of idle talk being as common as clover.
