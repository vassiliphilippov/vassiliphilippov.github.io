scaly

1. ADJ

/ ˈskeɪli / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli / (BrE)
having a surface covered with or resembling scales; rough or flaky to the touch.
covered with or resembling scales

Examples

The scaly skin of the lizard helps protect it from predators and harsh weather.
After swimming in the chlorinated pool every day, her hands became dry and scaly.
The old tree trunk was covered in scaly patches of lichen and moss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or scaly

Etymology

From scale (referring to the plates on the skin of animals) + -y (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskeɪli / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli / (BrE)
covered or partly covered with thin, flat, hard plates called scales, as on the skin of some fish and reptiles
covered with scales (like fish or reptiles)

Examples

The scaly skin of the lizard helps protect it from predators and environmental hazards.
After weeks at sea, the fishermen returned with buckets full of scaly fish.
His hands felt rough and scaly from working in the garden without gloves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scaleless - lacking scales
  • Synonym: scalelike - resembling a scale in form or appearance

Etymology

From scale (NOUN) + -y, meaning 'having scales'.
