Stenopterygius quadrisicissus

1. NOUN

/ stɛ-nɒp-təˈrɪdʒ-i-əs ˌkwɒ-drɪ-sɪˈsɪs / (AmE), / stɛ-nɒp-təˈrɪdʒ-ɪ-əs ˌkwɒ-drɪ-sɪˈsɪs / (BrE)
A species of extinct marine reptile (ichthyosaur) of the genus Stenopterygius, known from the Jurassic period.
extinct Jurassic ichthyosaur species

Examples

The fossil of Stenopterygius quadrisicissus was discovered in the Jurassic strata of Germany.
Scientists often study Stenopterygius quadrisicissus to better understand marine reptiles of the Jurassic period.
Unlike some other ichthyosaurs, Stenopterygius quadrisicissus had distinct features in its fin structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - extinct marine reptiles resembling fish and dolphins

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'pteryx' (wing or fin), and Latinized specific epithet 'quadrisicissus' meaning 'four-parted'.
