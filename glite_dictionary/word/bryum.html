Bryum

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪəm / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of mosses in the family Bryaceae, characterized by mostly erect, tufted gametophytes and symmetrical, short-necked capsules
genus of mosses (Bryaceae family)

Examples

Bryum species are commonly found in damp or shaded habitats worldwide.
The study focused on the reproductive structures of Bryum mosses.
Several varieties of Bryum are used as indicator species in ecological monitoring.

Etymology

From Greek bryon (moss).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪəm / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of mosses in the family Bryaceae, distinguished by mostly erect and tufted gametophytes and symmetrical short-necked capsules.
genus of mosses (Bryaceae)

Examples

Several species in the Bryum genus are common in temperate regions worldwide.
The botanist identified the moss as a member of the Bryum genus due to its distinctive capsules.
Bryum moss often grows in tufts on damp, shaded rocks and soil.

Etymology

From Greek bryon (moss).
