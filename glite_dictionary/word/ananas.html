Ananas

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ə.næs / (AmE), / ˈan.ə.nas / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American plants known for sword-shaped leaves and fleshy compound fruits, including the pineapple.
genus of tropical plants; includes the pineapple

Examples

The most famous species in the genus Ananas is the pineapple, widely cultivated for its fruit.
Botanists have classified more than one species within the Ananas genus.
Many tropical gardens feature various kinds of Ananas due to their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineapple - the edible fruit produced by Ananas comosus

Etymology

From Tupi (Brazilian indigenous language) *nanas* (pineapple), adopted into Portuguese as *ananas*.
