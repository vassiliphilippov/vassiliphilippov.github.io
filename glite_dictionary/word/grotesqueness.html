grotesqueness

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsknəs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being comically or repulsively ugly or distorted; the state of being grotesque
quality of being grotesque or distorted

Examples

The grotesqueness of the sculpture drew both fascination and discomfort from viewers.
She was struck by the grotesqueness of the carnival costumes, which seemed exaggerated and surreal.
Literature often uses grotesqueness to highlight deeper truths about society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotesque - strangely or comically distorted; repulsively ugly or incongruous

Etymology

From 'grotesque' + '-ness', where 'grotesque' comes from Italian 'grottesca' (in the style of a cave).
