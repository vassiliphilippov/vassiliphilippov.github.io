Occident

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdənt / (BrE)
the western part of the world, originally meaning Europe and later including North and South America
the Western world; Europe and the Americas

Examples

Many philosophers in the Occident were influenced by Greek thought.
Trade between the Occident and the Orient increased during the Middle Ages.
The foods of the Occident differ greatly from those traditionally found in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Orient - the eastern part of the world, especially East Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'occidens' (the west, the setting sun), from 'occidere' (to fall, set).
