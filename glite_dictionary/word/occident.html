occident

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdənt / (BrE)
the western part of the world, especially North and South America; the West
the western part of the world

Examples

Many explorers set out from the occident in search of new trade routes to the East.
The occident and the orient have long been compared in literature and philosophy.
Art from the occident differs greatly from ancient Asian styles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to orient - the eastern part of the world; the East

Etymology

From Latin 'occidens' (west, sunset), from 'occidere' (to fall, set).
