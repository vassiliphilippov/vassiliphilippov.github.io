congratulation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌɡrætʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡrætjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an expression of pleasure or praise for someone's success or good fortune, often used in the plural
expression of praise for success or good fortune

Examples

Please accept my congratulation on your well-deserved promotion.
They sent their congratulations to the newlyweds after the ceremony.
He received many congratulations when he announced his graduation.

Synset Examples

I sent them my sincere congratulations on their marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felicitation - formal expression of good wishes, especially on a special occasion

Etymology

From Latin congratulationem (act of rejoicing together), from 'congratulatus', past participle of 'congratulare'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌɡrætʃ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡratjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing praise or good wishes to someone on a special occasion or for an achievement
act of expressing praise or good wishes

Examples

Please accept my congratulation on your promotion to manager.
The ceremony began with a formal congratulation of the graduates.
Her smile was a silent congratulation for his outstanding performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felicitation - the act of offering congratulations
  • Antonym: condolence - expression of sympathy, especially after a loss

Etymology

From Latin 'congratulationem' (expression of joy), from 'congratulatus' (joyfully wished).
