Assyria

1. PROPN

/ əˈsɪriə / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom and empire in northern Mesopotamia, located largely in present-day Iraq, which flourished from the 25th century BCE until its fall in the 7th century BCE.
ancient Mesopotamian kingdom

Examples

Historians have uncovered many artifacts from Assyria dating back thousands of years.
The military campaigns of Assyria greatly expanded its territory across the ancient Near East.
Many ancient texts from Assyria reveal a sophisticated legal and administrative system.

Etymology

From Latin Assyria, from Ancient Greek Assyria (Ἀσσυρία), from Akkadian Aššur, the city-name and god.
