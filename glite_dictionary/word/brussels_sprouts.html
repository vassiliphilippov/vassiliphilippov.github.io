Brussels sprouts

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌsəlz ˌspraʊts / (AmE), / ˈbrʌsəlz ˌspraʊts / (BrE)
Small green vegetables consisting of edible buds that resemble miniature cabbages, grown on tall stalks.
small cabbage-like vegetable buds

Examples

My mother roasted Brussels sprouts with garlic for dinner last night.
Some people dislike Brussels sprouts, but they are rich in vitamins and fiber.
We harvested several stalks of Brussels sprouts from our garden this autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - large-leaved edible plant, related vegetable

Etymology

Named after Brussels, the capital city of Belgium, where they were first cultivated widely.
