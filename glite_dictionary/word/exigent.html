exigent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛksɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪdʒənt / (BrE)
requiring immediate action or attention; pressing or urgent.
requiring urgent attention

Examples

The patient was admitted due to an exigent medical condition that required immediate treatment.
Environmental concerns are becoming increasingly exigent in global policy discussions.
The chairman called an exigent meeting to address the financial crisis.

Synset Examples

clamant needs
a crying need
regarded literary questions as exigent and momentous
insistent hunger
an instant need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
  • Synonym: pressing - demanding quick attention; urgent
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance; not urgent

Etymology

From Latin exigent-, exigens, present participle of exigere (to demand, require).
