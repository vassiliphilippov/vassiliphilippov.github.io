spigot

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈspɪɡət / (BrE)
A plug used to seal the bunghole of a cask.
plug for a cask's bunghole

Examples

The cooper fitted a spigot into the bunghole to keep the wine from spilling.
After draining the cask, the spigot was removed and cleaned thoroughly.
He carefully inserted the spigot to preserve the contents of the barrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bung - a stopper for closing the hole in a barrel or cask

Etymology

From Old French *espigot*, perhaps from Provençal *espigou* (small ear of grain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈspɪɡət / (BrE)
a device used to control the flow of liquid from a container, such as a tap or faucet
device for controlling flow of liquid

Examples

The rain barrel has a spigot at the bottom for easy water access.
He turned the spigot and fresh juice flowed into his glass.
Before refilling the tank, make sure the spigot is tightly closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap - a device for controlling liquid flow, especially in British English
  • Synonym: faucet - a device for drawing liquid, especially in American English

Etymology

From Old French 'espigot', of uncertain origin, possibly from Spanish 'espita' meaning 'tap' or Latin 'spica', meaning 'ear of grain'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
