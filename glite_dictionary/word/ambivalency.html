ambivalency

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪvələntsi / (AmE), / æmˈbɪvələntsi / (BrE)
the state of having mixed or conflicting feelings about someone or something
state of mixed feelings

Examples

Her ambivalency toward the new job made her hesitant to accept the offer.
The politician's ambivalency on climate policy frustrated many of his supporters.
He struggled with ambivalency about moving to another city for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambivalence - state of having mixed or contradictory feelings

Etymology

Variant of 'ambivalence,' from German 'Ambivalenz,' from Latin 'ambi-' (both) and 'valentia' (strength).
