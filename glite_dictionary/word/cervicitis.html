cervicitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜr.vɪˈsaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.vɪˈsaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the cervix of the uterus, often caused by infection
inflammation of the uterine cervix
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with cervicitis following her symptoms of pain and unusual discharge.
Common causes of cervicitis include bacterial and viral infections, such as chlamydia or HPV.
Untreated cervicitis can lead to complications such as pelvic inflammatory disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endometritis - inflammation of the endometrium (lining of the uterus)
  • Synonym: vaginitis - inflammation of the vagina

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' (neck, cervix) + '-itis' (inflammation).
