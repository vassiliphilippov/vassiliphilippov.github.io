Trapa bicornis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪpə baɪˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪpə bɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
An Asian aquatic plant species known as water chestnut, whose fruit is spiny with two prongs rather than four.
species of water chestnut with two-pronged fruit

Examples

Trapa bicornis is cultivated in parts of Asia for its edible seeds.
The distinctive two-pronged fruits are the hallmark of Trapa bicornis.
Botanists distinguish Trapa bicornis from related species by examining the fruit's shape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Trapa natans - species of water chestnut with four-pronged fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'trapa' (the plant's genus) and 'bicornis' meaning 'two-horned'.
