to negotiate

1. VERB

/ nɪˈɡoʊʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
to successfully find a way through, around, or over an obstacle or difficult path
succeed in passing through or over something

Examples

He had to negotiate a steep and slippery slope to reach the campsite.
The driver carefully negotiated the sharp bend in the road despite the heavy rain.
It took them hours to negotiate the dense forest and reach the village.

Synset Examples

The hiker negociated the high mountain pass

Etymology

From Latin negotiari (to carry on business), from negotium (business, difficulty).

2. VERB

/ nɪˈɡoʊʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
(finance) To sell, transfer, or discount (a security or similar financial instrument).
sell or transfer a financial instrument

Examples

The firm decided to negotiate its bonds before the end of the quarter.
Banks often negotiate securities on behalf of their clients.
She helped the company negotiate several promissory notes for immediate cash.
