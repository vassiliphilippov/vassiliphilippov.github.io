apperception

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the mental process by which new perceptions are interpreted and integrated based on previous experiences and knowledge
integration of perceptions with prior experience

Examples

The child's apperception of the new situation was influenced by similar experiences in the past.
In cognitive psychology, apperception explains how prior knowledge shapes what we notice or interpret.
Her apperception allowed her to connect the artist’s new work with earlier movements she had studied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the process of becoming aware of something through the senses

Etymology

From Latin 'apperceptio' (perception toward), from ad- (to) + percipere (to perceive).
