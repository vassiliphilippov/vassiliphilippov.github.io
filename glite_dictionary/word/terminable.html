terminable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːrmɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being ended or brought to an end after a set period or under certain conditions
able to be ended after a time

Examples

The contract is terminable after one year with proper notice.
Many rental agreements are terminable at the discretion of either party.
Some pensions are terminable upon the death of the annuitant.

Synset Examples

terminable employees
a terminable annuity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perpetual - continuing forever, not able to be terminated
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for a limited time

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'terminabilis' (that can be limited), from Latin 'terminare' (to end, limit).
