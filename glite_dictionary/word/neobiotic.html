Neobiotic

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊbaɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic obtained from an actinomycete, used as a sulfate form as an intestinal antiseptic during surgery.
antibiotic drug; intestinal antiseptic
medical

Examples

The surgeon prescribed Neobiotic to prevent postoperative infections after the intestinal procedure.
Use of Neobiotic as an intestinal antiseptic is common in certain types of surgery.
Some patients experienced mild side effects after receiving Neobiotic during their hospital stay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug that destroys or inhibits the growth of bacteria

Etymology

Formed from neo- (new) + antibiotic, trademarked as Neobiotic.
