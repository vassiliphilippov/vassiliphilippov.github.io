fingerboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A narrow strip of wood on the neck of some stringed instruments, against which the strings are pressed with the fingers to vary the pitch.
part of a stringed musical instrument's neck

Examples

The guitarist placed his fingers precisely on the fingerboard to play each note.
Over time, the fingerboard of the old violin had worn smooth from years of use.
She wiped rosin dust off the fingerboard before starting her cello practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fretboard - the fingerboard of a guitar or similar instrument with frets

Etymology

From 'finger' + 'board', referring to the fingered use and board-like shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəbɔːd / (BrE)
a flat strip or surface on a musical instrument on which the fingers press to produce different notes, especially on the neck of stringed instruments like violins or guitars; sometimes refers to the bank of keys on a keyboard instrument.
part of a musical instrument played with the fingers

Examples

The violinist moved her hand swiftly along the fingerboard to play the high notes.
Guitar players spend years developing precision on the fingerboard for complex chords and solos.
The piano’s fingerboard must be kept clean to maintain smooth playability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyboard - the set of keys on a piano or similar musical instrument

Etymology

From 'finger' + 'board', indicating a board played with the fingers.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəbɔːd / (BrE)
a signpost or guidepost shaped like a hand with an extended index finger, used to indicate direction
signpost shaped like a pointing hand

Examples

The old country road had a wooden fingerboard pointing the way to the next village.
A weathered fingerboard stood at the crossroads, its painted hand faded with age.
Travelers relied on the fingerboard to guide them through the winding lanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signpost - a post with signs giving information or directions
