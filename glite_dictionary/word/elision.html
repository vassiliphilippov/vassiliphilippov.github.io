elision

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪʒən / (BrE)
the deliberate omission of something, especially in speech, writing, or a process
deliberate omission

Examples

The report's elision of key facts sparked public debate.
Historians criticized the elision of important events in the official narrative.
Her elision of minor details made the story simpler to follow.

Synset Examples

with the exception of the children, everyone was told the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omission - the act of leaving something out or failing to include it
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included

Etymology

From Latin 'elidere' (to strike out), via French 'élision'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the omission of a sound or syllable when speaking, especially the omission of a vowel at the end of one word when the next word begins with a vowel
omission of a sound in speech

Examples

In casual speech, elision often leads to sounds being dropped from words.
The phrase 'I'm' is an example of elision because 'am' loses its initial vowel.
French uses elision frequently when a word ending in a vowel is followed by another starting with a vowel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elide - to omit a sound or syllable when speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'elisio', from 'elisio' meaning 'a striking out', related to 'elidere' (to strike out).
