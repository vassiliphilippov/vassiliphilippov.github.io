Utopian

1. ADJ

/ juːˈtoʊ.pi.ən / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊ.pi.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to an ideal or perfect society; resembling a utopia.
relating to a perfect or ideal society

Examples

He outlined a Utopian vision for the future of education in his speech.
Many critics argue that her proposals are too Utopian to be practical.
The novel describes a Utopian society where everyone lives in harmony.

Synset Examples

a Utopian novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealistic - believing in or aiming for perfection
  • Antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; pragmatic

Etymology

From Thomas More's 1516 book 'Utopia', describing an ideal society.

2. NOUN

/ juːˈtoʊpiən / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊpiən / (BrE)
a person who advocates or believes in the creation of a perfect or ideal society, often impractical or unrealistic
person who believes in a perfect society

Examples

Many Utopians dream of a society free from poverty and injustice.
As a young man, he was dismissed as a Utopian by more cynical politicians.
The debate featured a Utopian who argued that radical change was not only possible but necessary.

Synset Examples

a Utopian believes in the ultimate perfectibility of man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealist - someone guided more by ideals than practical considerations
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and is prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From 'Utopia', the fictional perfect society in Thomas More's 1516 book, from Greek 'ou' (not) + 'topos' (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10763013-n
Synset POS: n
