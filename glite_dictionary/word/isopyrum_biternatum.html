Isopyrum biternatum

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪsəˈpaɪrəm bɪˈtɜrnətəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈpɪərəm bɪˈtɜːnətəm / (BrE)
A slender, erect perennial plant native to eastern North America, with tuberous roots and pink-tinged white flowers, resembling meadow rue.
North American perennial plant with white flowers

Examples

The woodland was dotted with blooming Isopyrum biternatum in early spring.
Botanists studied the distinctive tuberous roots of Isopyrum biternatum.
Isopyrum biternatum is sometimes confused with meadow rue due to its similar flowers.

Etymology

From Modern Latin; genus name Isopyrum from Greek isos (equal) + pŷr (wheat), species name biternatum referring to its twice-ternate leaves.
