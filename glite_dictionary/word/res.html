RES

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.iːˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɑːr.iːˈɛs / (BrE)
a system in the body composed of cells that can ingest bacteria or particles, excluding certain white blood cells; also known as the reticuloendothelial system
reticuloendothelial system in medicine
medical

Examples

Doctors study the RES to understand how the body fights infections.
Certain diseases can affect the RES, leading to problems with immune response.
The RES is involved in removing old red blood cells from circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticuloendothelial system - system of cells capable of ingesting bacteria and particles

Etymology

Abbreviation of reticuloendothelial system.
