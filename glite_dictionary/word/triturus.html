Triturus

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈtjuːrəs / (AmE), / trɪˈtjuːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly aquatic salamanders found in Europe and western Asia, commonly known as newts.
genus of aquatic salamanders (newts)

Examples

Species in the genus Triturus are commonly referred to as newts.
The smooth newt, a member of Triturus, is widespread across Europe.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Triturus populations in various ponds.

Etymology

From Latin triturus, the name of a kind of lizard or salamander.

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈtʊr.əs / (AmE), / trɪˈtjuː.rəs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly aquatic newts found in Eurasia, known for their smooth skin and aquatic adult stage
a genus of aquatic newts

Examples

The Triturus genus includes several species of newts native to Europe and Asia.
Scientists studied the life cycle of a Triturus in its natural environment.
Members of the Triturus genus are known for their elaborate courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small semi-aquatic amphibian of the family Salamandridae
  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

From Latin *triturus* (a type of salamander), used in zoological naming.
