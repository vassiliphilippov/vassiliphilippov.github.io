rhagades

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡəˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˈræɡədiːz / (BrE)
cracks or fissures in the skin, especially around the mouth or anus, often associated with certain diseases
skin cracks or fissures (medical term)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited several rhagades at the corners of his mouth.
Repeated episodes of chapped lips led to the formation of painful rhagades.
Doctors noted rhagades during the examination, which suggested a possible underlying infection.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhagas' (a crack, fissure).
