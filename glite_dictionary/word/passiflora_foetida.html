Passiflora foetida

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˈflɔːrə foʊˈɛtɪdə / (AmE), / ˌpasɪˈflɔːrə fəʊˈɛtɪdə / (BrE)
A tropical American passion flower with finely divided bracts, known for the unpleasant odor of its crushed stems.
tropical passion flower species

Examples

The garden features a rare specimen of Passiflora foetida blooming among the other passionflowers.
When crushed, the stems of Passiflora foetida release a distinctly unpleasant smell.
Botanists studied the intricate bracts of Passiflora foetida for their unique adaptation.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Passiflora' (passion flower) and 'foetida' (fetid, stinking).
