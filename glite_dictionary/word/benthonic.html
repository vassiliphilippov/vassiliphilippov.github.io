benthonic

1. ADJ

/ bɛnˈθɑnɪk / (AmE), / bɛnˈθɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to, or occurring on, the bottom of a sea, lake, or other body of water
relating to the bottom of a body of water

Examples

The benthonic zone supports a wide variety of unique marine organisms.
Researchers collected benthonic samples to study the sediment's composition.
Some fish are adapted to life in the benthonic region of lakes and rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a sea or lake

Etymology

From Greek *benthos* (depth of the sea), with the suffix -ic forming adjectives.
