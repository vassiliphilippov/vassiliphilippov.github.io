Cassiope mertensiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiˈoʊpi mɜːrˈtɛnsiənə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiˈəʊpiː mɜːˈtɛnsɪənə / (BrE)
A species of heath found in the mountains of western North America, known for its bell-shaped white flowers.
mountain heath with bell-shaped white flowers

Examples

The Cassiope mertensiana thrives in the subalpine meadows of the Pacific Northwest.
We spotted patches of Cassiope mertensiana along the rocky mountain trail.
Hikers admired the delicate, drooping blossoms of Cassiope mertensiana blooming in early summer.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek mythology (Cassiopeia); species named after German naturalist Franz Carl Mertens.
