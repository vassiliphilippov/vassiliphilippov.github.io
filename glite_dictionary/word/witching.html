witching

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the use or practice of witchcraft
practice of witchcraft

Examples

The old legends spoke of witching in the dark woods outside the village.
Some believed the strange events were caused by witching and spells.
The authorities passed strict laws to suppress witching in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills and abilities

Etymology

From 'witch' + '-ing', denoting action or process.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Having qualities or powers characteristic of witchcraft or the supernatural; enchanting or magical.
relating to witchcraft or magic

Examples

The novel describes the witching atmosphere of the old, abandoned house.
It was said to be the witching hour, when spells and enchantments were most powerful.
Under the witching moonlight, the forest seemed alive with mysterious magic.

Synset Examples

charming incantations
magic signs that protect against adverse influence
a magical spell
'tis now the very witching time of night
wizard wands
wizardly powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magical - relating to magic or having supernatural qualities
  • Synonym: charming - delightfully enchanting, sometimes with a magical quality

Etymology

From Middle English 'wicche', from Old English 'wicce' (witch) plus '-ing' (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
