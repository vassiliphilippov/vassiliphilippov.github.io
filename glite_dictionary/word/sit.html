to sit

1. VERB

/ sɪt / (AmE), / sɪt / (BrE)
(of a building or object) to be located or situated in a particular place.
be located; be situated

Examples

The famous cathedral sits at the center of the old city.
Our cabin sits on a hill overlooking the lake.
A statue of the founder sits in the main courtyard.

Synset Examples

The White House sits on Pennsylvania Avenue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand - (of a building or object) to be situated in a particular place

Etymology

Old English *sittan*, from Proto-Germanic *sitjaną*, related to German *sitzen* and Dutch *zitten*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sɪt / (AmE), / sɪt / (BrE)
to rest with the body supported by the buttocks or thighs, typically on a chair or other support; to take a seat
to take a seat; to be in a seated position

Examples

Please sit here while you wait for your appointment.
After a long walk, we decided to sit on the bench and rest.
She asked her students to sit quietly before the lesson began.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stand - be in an upright position on the feet
  • Synonym: perch - to sit, especially in a high or precarious position

Etymology

Old English 'sittan', from Proto-Germanic 'sitjan' and related to Latin 'sedere' (to sit).
