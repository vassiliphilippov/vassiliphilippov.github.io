soursop

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊrˌsɑp / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəsɒp / (BrE)
A large, spiny tropical fruit with sour, juicy pulp, related to custard apples, or the tree that bears this fruit.
tropical fruit with tart pulp

Examples

The soursop is prized for its deliciously tangy flavor in juices and desserts.
We bought a ripe soursop at the market while traveling in the Caribbean.
The leaves of the soursop tree are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custard apple - a fruit closely related to soursop with sweet, creamy pulp

Etymology

Probably a blend of ‘sour’ and ‘sop’ (something soaked or steeped).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊrˌsɑp / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəsɒp / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree (Annona muricata) bearing large, green, prickly fruit with soft, white, slightly acidic flesh, also called the fruit itself.
tropical American fruit tree and its fruit

Examples

The soursop tree produces large, spiky fruits with creamy, tangy pulp inside.
She made a refreshing juice from ripe soursop for the party.
In many tropical countries, soursop is used in desserts and beverages for its unique flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guanabana - another name for soursop (Annona muricata)

Etymology

From Spanish 'zoursop', perhaps via Dutch 'zuursap' (sour juice), alluding to the fruit's tart flavor.
