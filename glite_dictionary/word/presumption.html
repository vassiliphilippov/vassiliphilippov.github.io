presumption

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
An idea or assumption that is accepted as true without proof, often as the basis for further reasoning or action.
assumption taken as true without proof

Examples

The judge operated under the presumption that the defendant was innocent until proven guilty.
There is a widespread presumption that taxes will rise next year.
Her theory relies on the presumption that people act rationally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof
  • Synonym: supposition - an uncertain belief or guess

Etymology

From Latin praesumptio (anticipation, assumption), from praesumere (to take beforehand).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
Audacious or arrogant behavior, especially when displayed by someone who has no right to it.
arrogant or bold behavior

Examples

She had the presumption to ask for a raise after only a week on the job.
He was shocked by the presumption of the newcomer, who spoke as if he owned the place.
Their presumption in making such demands was met with disapproval from the group.

Synset Examples

he despised them for their presumptuousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudence - rude or disrespectful behavior
  • Antonym: humility - modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin praesumptio (assumption, audacity), from praesumere (to take in advance).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Law) An inference that a fact is true based on other established or admitted facts, unless disproved.
legal inference based on established facts
legal

Examples

In criminal cases, there is a presumption of innocence until proven guilty.
The court relied on the legal presumption that the missing person was deceased.
A presumption arises when certain facts are admitted without challenge in the proceedings.

Etymology

From Latin praesumptio (anticipation, assumption), from praesumere (to take beforehand).

4. NOUN

/ prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
behaviour that is too confident or shows a lack of proper respect; an act of going beyond what is proper or allowed
overconfident or disrespectful behaviour

Examples

Her presumption in interrupting the meeting annoyed everyone in the room.
I was shocked by the presumption of his request, considering how little we knew each other.
There is a difference between confidence and presumption when dealing with authority figures.

Synset Examples

his presumption was intolerable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinence - lack of respect; rudeness
  • Synonym: assumption - something taken for granted; also, presumptuousness
  • Antonym: politeness - respectful or considerate behaviour
