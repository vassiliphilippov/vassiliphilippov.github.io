doodle

1. VERB

/ ˈduːdəl / (AmE), / ˈduːdəl / (BrE)
to draw or scribble absent-mindedly, without a specific purpose
draw aimlessly or absent-mindedly

Examples

She likes to doodle in her notebook during long meetings.
As a child, I would often doodle on the margins of my textbooks.
He doodled little stars and faces while talking on the phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - to write or draw something quickly or carelessly

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly of imitative origin or from earlier 'doodle' meaning fool or simpleton, 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdudl̩ / (AmE), / ˈduːdl̩ / (BrE)
a drawing made without any particular purpose, often while a person's attention is elsewhere
an aimless or casual drawing

Examples

She covered her notebook margins with a doodle of flowers and stars.
When he's bored in meetings, he often creates a little doodle on his notepad.
A simple doodle can be surprisingly expressive and fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a rough or unfinished drawing or painting

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps imitative or from early 17th-century English 'doodle' meaning 'fool, simpleton'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04162371-n
Synset POS: n
