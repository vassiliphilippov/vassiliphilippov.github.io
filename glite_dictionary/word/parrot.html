parrot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹət / (AmE), / ˈpærət / (BrE)
a usually brightly colored tropical bird with a short, hooked beak, often able to imitate human speech
brightly colored tropical bird, often imitates sounds

Examples

The brightly feathered parrot perched on my shoulder and squawked loudly.
Many species of parrot are known for their ability to mimic human speech.
We saw a green parrot flying through the rainforest during our trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaw - a large, long-tailed parrot of Central and South America
  • Synonym: cockatoo - a parrot with an erectile crest, native to Australia and nearby islands

Etymology

From Portuguese 'papagaio', perhaps via Spanish, from Arabic 'babaghāʔ', of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærət / (AmE), / ˈparət / (BrE)
a person who imitates the words or actions of others without understanding them
person who copies without understanding

Examples

She was just a parrot, repeating what the professor said without grasping the concepts.
Don't be a parrot; try to form your own opinions instead of blindly imitating others.
His arguments sounded like those of a parrot, simply echoing the views of his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copycat - a person who imitates others

Etymology

From the literal sense, referring to the bird known for repeating sounds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10421030-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpærət / (AmE), / ˈpærət / (BrE)
to repeat or imitate something mechanically, without understanding or thought
repeat or imitate mechanically

Examples

The young employee would parrot whatever his boss said without thinking for himself.
She didn't understand the theory but could parrot all the textbook definitions.
Children sometimes parrot phrases they hear from adults, not knowing what they mean.

Synset Examples

The students parroted the teacher's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to repeat someone's words or ideas
  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy or reproduce someone or something

Etymology

From the noun 'parrot', referring to the bird's ability to mimic speech.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
