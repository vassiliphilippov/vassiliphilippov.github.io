mayhem

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (BrE)
the crime of willfully and unlawfully crippling or mutilating another person, especially by depriving them of a body part
unlawful mutilation or crippling (legal term)
legal

Examples

The defendant was charged with mayhem after the violent altercation left the victim permanently disfigured.
In some jurisdictions, mayhem is considered a felony offense, separate from assault or battery.
Historic cases of mayhem often involved the intentional disabling of an opponent to prevent them from fighting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiming - the act of injuring or wounding seriously and permanently

Etymology

From Old French mahaigne (physical injury), possibly from mahainier (to injure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (BrE)
violent or extreme disorder and chaos, especially causing unnecessary disruption or damage.
violent disorder; chaos

Examples

The protest quickly turned into mayhem when the crowd stormed the building.
There was mayhem in the kitchen after the shelf collapsed.
Fans caused mayhem outside the stadium after their team won.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: order - a situation in which everything is arranged or organized

Etymology

From Old French 'mahaignier' (to maim), influenced by Anglo-Norman 'maihem'.

Synset POS: n
