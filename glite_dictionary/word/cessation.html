cessation

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛˈseɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the fact or process of ending or being brought to an end; a stopping
the act of stopping or ceasing

Examples

There was a sudden cessation of noise as the engine shut down.
Both countries agreed to a cessation of hostilities.
The doctor recommended a cessation of smoking for better health.

Synset Examples

a cessation of the thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - the final point or termination of something
  • Antonym: beginning - the point at which something starts

Etymology

From Latin 'cessatio' (a ceasing), from 'cessare' (to cease).
