Gronland

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrinˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd / (BrE)
the largest island in the world, located between the North Atlantic and Arctic Oceans, and a self-governing province of Denmark
the world's largest island; Danish autonomous territory

Examples

Scientists travel to Gronland to study the effects of climate change on the polar environment.
Many native Inuit communities call Gronland their home.
The autonomous province of Gronland maintains strong cultural ties with Denmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greenland - the English name for Gronland, the largest island in the world

Etymology

From Danish 'Grønland' meaning 'Greenland'.
