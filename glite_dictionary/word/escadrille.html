escadrille

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛskəˈdrɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈdrɪl / (BrE)
A unit of an air force consisting typically of six aircraft, used especially in France during World War I.
small air force squadron, esp. in France WWI

Examples

The famous Lafayette escadrille attracted American volunteer pilots during World War I.
Each escadrille was comprised of a small number of fighter planes.
He researched the history of a French escadrille that fought on the Western Front.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squadron - A military unit of aircraft, ships, or vehicles grouped for tactical operations

Etymology

From French 'escadrille', diminutive of 'escadre' (squadron), from Spanish 'escuadra'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛskəˈdrɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈdrɪl / (BrE)
a small unit of aircraft and their crew, especially in the air force, typically smaller than a squadron
air force unit smaller than a squadron

Examples

The museum displays a restored fighter plane from a famous World War I escadrille.
She joined the air force and was assigned to an elite escadrille based near Paris.
Several pilots in the escadrille received medals for their bravery during the battle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: squadron - a larger military unit of aircraft or ships

Etymology

From French 'escadrille', diminutive of 'escadre' (squadron), from Spanish 'escuadra'.
