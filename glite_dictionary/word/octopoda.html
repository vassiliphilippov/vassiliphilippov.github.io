Octopoda

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtɑpədə / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɒpədə / (BrE)
an order of cephalopod mollusks that includes octopuses and the paper nautilus
order of cephalopods (octopuses, paper nautilus)
medical

Examples

The order Octopoda encompasses more than 300 species, including the common octopus.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Octopoda to better understand cephalopod intelligence.
Unlike squid, members of Octopoda generally lack internal shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - marine animal with eight arms, member of Octopoda
  • Degree Higher: Cephalopoda - the class of mollusks that includes squid, cuttlefish, and octopuses

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek ‘oktōpous’ (eight-footed).
