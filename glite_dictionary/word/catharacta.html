Catharacta

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈræktə / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈræktə / (BrE)
A former genus of large predatory seabirds known as skuas, found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of large predatory seabirds (skuas)

Examples

The Catharacta species are known for their aggressive behavior towards other seabirds.
Researchers observed a Catharacta nest along the remote coastline of South Georgia.
Several taxonomists have debated the classification of Catharacta within the skua family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - A predatory seabird known for stealing food from other birds

Etymology

From Greek katharaktēs (torrent, waterfall), alluding to the bird's plunging flight.
