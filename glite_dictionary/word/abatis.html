abatis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.ə.ti, əˈbɑː.ti / (AmE), / ˈab.ə.ti / (BrE)
A defensive barrier made of felled trees with sharpened branches or barbed wire facing the enemy.
defensive barrier of felled trees

Examples

The engineers constructed an abatis to strengthen the fort's defenses.
Soldiers used an abatis to slow the enemy's advance through the forest.
During the battle, the abatis proved effective in delaying the attack.

Etymology

From French abatis (heap of material thrown down), from abattre (to fell).
