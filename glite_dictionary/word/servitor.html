servitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪtə / (BrE)
a person who serves or acts as an attendant, especially in a formal or ceremonial capacity
attendant; someone who serves another

Examples

The elderly lord was always accompanied by his loyal servitor.
At the ancient university, a servitor would assist students with various tasks.
The wedding ceremony included a servitor who attended to the distinguished guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially as a job

Etymology

From Old French 'serviteur' (servant), from Latin 'servitor' (a servant), from 'servire' (to serve).
