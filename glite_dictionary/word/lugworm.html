lugworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌɡˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈlʌɡˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a marine worm with a row of tufted gills along each side of its back, often used as fishing bait
marine worm used for bait

Examples

The fisherman dug in the sand to find a fresh lugworm for his hook.
Beachcombers can sometimes see the coiled sand casts left by lugworms.
Using a live lugworm as bait greatly improves your chances of catching flatfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragworm - a similar marine worm also used as fishing bait
  • Degree Lower: earthworm - common type of terrestrial worm; different habitat and use
