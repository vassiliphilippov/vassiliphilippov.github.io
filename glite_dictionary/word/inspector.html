inspector

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈspɛktɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛktə / (BrE)
a high-ranking officer in the police force, typically above a sergeant and below a chief inspector or superintendent
police officer of high rank

Examples

The inspector interviewed all the witnesses to gather more evidence.
After years of hard work, she was promoted to inspector in the police department.
The inspector supervised a team of officers during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sergeant - a police officer ranking below inspector
  • Degree Higher: chief inspector - a police officer ranking above inspector

Etymology

From Latin 'inspector', from 'inspicere' meaning 'to look into'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛktə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to examine things carefully in order to check quality, safety, or compliance with rules.
person who examines or investigates

Examples

The inspector visited the factory to ensure all safety protocols were being followed.
A building inspector found several code violations during the routine check.
Detective Lee works as an inspector in the city’s police department.

Synset Examples

the examiner searched for clues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examiner - a person who inspects or investigates closely
  • Synonym: auditor - a person who officially examines records, especially financial accounts

Etymology

From Latin 'inspectare' (to look into), from 'inspicere' (to inspect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10087775-n
Synset POS: n
