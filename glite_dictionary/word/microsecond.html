microsecond

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌsɛkənd / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to one millionth (10⁻⁶) of a second.
one millionth of a second

Examples

The computer can complete the calculation in less than a microsecond.
It took only a microsecond for the light to travel across the fiber.
Scientists measure reaction times down to the microsecond in high-speed experiments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: millisecond - one thousandth (10⁻³) of a second
  • Degree Lower: nanosecond - one billionth (10⁻⁹) of a second

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + second (unit of time).
