intransitivity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtræn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtræn.sɪˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of a verb not requiring or being able to take a direct object; the state of being intransitive.
property of lacking a direct object (grammar)

Examples

The intransitivity of the verb 'sleep' means it cannot take a direct object.
Grammarians discuss intransitivity in relation to how verbs interact with their arguments.
Understanding intransitivity can help learners construct correct English sentences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transitivity - the quality of a verb that requires a direct object
  • Synonym: intransitive - (of a verb) not taking a direct object

Etymology

From intransitive + -ity, ultimately from Latin transire (to go across).
