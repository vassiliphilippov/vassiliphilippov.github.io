Apidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈæpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of bees that includes honeybees, bumblebees, and carpenter bees, characterized by their social behavior and important role in pollination.
family of bees including honeybees and bumblebees

Examples

The family Apidae includes some of the most important pollinators in agriculture.
Scientists study the anatomy of Apidae to learn more about bee social structures.
Many wildflowers depend on the bees in the Apidae family for reproduction.

Etymology

From Latin 'Apidae', from 'Apis' (bee).
