driftfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪftˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, tropical butterfish found worldwide, typically growing up to six inches long
small tropical butterfish

Examples

A single driftfish was swimming near the floating debris in the open ocean.
Many driftfishes are known to associate with jellyfish for protection and food.
The marine biologist collected a rare driftfish specimen during the survey expedition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪftˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large butterfish from the western Atlantic, found from the New York area to the northern Gulf of Mexico.
large western Atlantic butterfish

Examples

The Atlantic driftfish is often caught by accident in fishing nets.
Researchers observed several driftfish swimming near the ocean's surface.
The driftfish is an important species for the region's marine ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfish - small, oily fish of the family Stromateidae
