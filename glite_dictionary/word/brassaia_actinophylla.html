Brassaia actinophylla

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsaɪ.ə ækˌtɪn.əˈfaɪ.lə / (AmE), / brəˈsaɪ.ə ækˌtɪn.əˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
An erect evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia and northern New Guinea, with palmately compound leaves; commonly known as the umbrella tree.
Australian evergreen shrub or small tree

Examples

The Brassaia actinophylla adds a tropical touch to many gardens and landscapes.
You can recognize Brassaia actinophylla by its distinctive umbrella-like leaves.
In some regions, Brassaia actinophylla is grown as a popular indoor plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella tree - Common name for Brassaia actinophylla, an Australian shrub or tree with compound leaves

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of French botanist Brassai; species name from Greek 'aktis' (ray) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to its leaf arrangement.
