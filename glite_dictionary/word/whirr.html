whirr

1. VERB

/ wɜr / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
(of a machine or object) to make a soft, continuous, low, vibrating or humming sound.
make a soft, continuous sound

Examples

The old fan began to whirr softly as soon as it was switched on.
Dozens of insects whirred in the warm summer air at dusk.
As the drone took off, its blades started whirring overhead.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - to make a low, steady, continuous sound
  • Synonym: buzz - to make a continuous, vibrating sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced; related to 'whir' (16th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02193033-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɜr / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
(of an object or creature) to make a continuous, vibrant, buzzing or humming sound, often with a rapid motion.
make a continuous buzzing or humming sound

Examples

The hummingbird's wings began to whirr as it hovered over the flower.
I woke up to the sound of a fan whirring gently in the background.
As she spun the toy, its blades started to whirr loudly across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - make a continuous humming sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, first recorded in the early 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01054434-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ wɝː / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
a continuous buzzing or humming sound produced by something moving rapidly
buzzing or humming sound from rapid movement

Examples

The whirr of the ceiling fan was soothing as she drifted off to sleep.
We listened to the constant whirr of machinery in the background.
There was a sudden whirr as the drone lifted off the ground.

Synset Examples

whir of a bird's wings
the whir of the propellers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - a low, continuous humming or murmuring sound
  • Synonym: hum - a low, steady, continuous sound

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound itself, similar to 'whir'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414517-n
Synset POS: n
