admiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌædməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadmɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of great approval and pleasure towards someone or something
feeling of approval and pleasure

Examples

She looked at her teacher with admiration after the inspiring lesson.
His achievements earned him the admiration of his colleagues and friends.
The artist's work attracted widespread admiration at the exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone due to their abilities, qualities, or achievements
  • Antonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless or beneath consideration

Etymology

From Latin admiratio (wonder, astonishment), from admirari (to admire).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæd.məˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæd.mɪˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of wonder and amazement aroused by something strange or surprising.
feeling of wonder or amazement

Examples

She gazed at the magician's performance in utter admiration at the unexpected tricks.
There was a sense of admiration in the crowd when the ancient ruins were revealed.
The child's eyes opened wide in admiration at the sight of the enormous, glowing jellyfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishment - a feeling of great surprise
  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of amazement and admiration
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin admiratio (wonder, amazement), from admirari (to wonder at).
