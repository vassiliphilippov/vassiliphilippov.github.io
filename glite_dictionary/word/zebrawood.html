zebrawood

1. NOUN

/ ˈziː.bɹəˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈziː.brəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A decorative wood with dark and light stripes, obtained from certain tropical trees, especially used in fine furniture and cabinetry.
striped decorative wood from a tropical tree

Examples

The table was made from rare zebrawood, showcasing beautiful contrasting stripes.
Collectors prize furniture crafted with zebrawood for its unique appearance and durability.
He selected a piece of zebrawood to create an elegant veneer for the cabinet doors.

2. NOUN

/ ˈziː.brəˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈziː.brəˌwʊd / (BrE)
The wood of several trees or shrubs, typically characterized by a striped or mottled appearance resembling the stripes of a zebra, used especially for decorative purposes.
wood with striped pattern from certain trees

Examples

The cabinet was made of rare zebrawood, which gave it a striking appearance.
Many luxury cars feature zebrawood trim because of its unique striping.
Craftsmen prize zebrawood for decorative veneers and fine furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingwood - a type of decorative wood with a striped pattern

Etymology

From 'zebra' + 'wood', referring to its striped appearance.
