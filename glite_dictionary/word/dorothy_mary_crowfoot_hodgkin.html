Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɔːrəθi ˈmɛri ˈkraʊfʊt ˈhɒdʒkɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒrəθi ˈmɛri ˈkrəʊfʊt ˈhɒdʒkɪn / (BrE)
(1910–1994) English chemist, Nobel laureate, known for pioneering the use of X-ray crystallography in determining the structure of organic compounds.
English chemist and Nobel laureate

Examples

Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1964 for her work on the structure of important biochemical substances.
The pioneering research of Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin advanced our understanding of insulin and vitamin B12.
Many scientists were inspired by the achievements of Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin in the field of crystallography.
