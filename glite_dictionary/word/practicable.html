practicable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəbəl / (BrE)
able to be done or put into practice successfully with the available means; feasible.
able to be done; feasible

Examples

The proposed plan is not practicable without additional funding.
After reviewing the options, they chose the most practicable solution.
It may sound ideal in theory, but is it really practicable in everyday life?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • Antonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out or put into practice
  • Antonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done

Etymology

From Late Latin practicabilis (that can be put into practice), from Greek praktikos (practical).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəbl / (BrE)
able to be done, used, or put into practice successfully; feasible or workable in practice
able to be done or used; feasible

Examples

The committee considered whether the proposal was practicable given the available resources.
It may not be practicable to finish the project by Friday, but we will try.
Installing solar panels on the roof was found to be a practicable solution to their energy needs.

Synset Examples

an operable plan
a practicable solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out or put into practice
  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently

Etymology

From Latin practicabilis (that may be done), from practicare (to practice).
