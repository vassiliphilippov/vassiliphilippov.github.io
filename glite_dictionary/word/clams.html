clams

1. NOUN

/ klæmz / (AmE), / klæmz / (BrE)
(informal) Money, especially in small amounts.
(informal) money
informal slang american

Examples

He spent all his clams on the new video game.
It only costs a few clams to get a ticket to the show.
I'm a little short on clams until payday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bucks - (informal) dollars, money
  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of bills or coins

Etymology

US slang, attested from late 19th century, possibly from clamshells used as barter among Native Americans.
