protectorate

1. NOUN

/ prəˈtɛktɚət / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktərət / (BrE)
A country or territory that is partly controlled and protected by a stronger state but remains autonomous in its internal affairs, usually established by treaty.
state partly controlled by another

Examples

India was once a protectorate of the British Empire before gaining independence.
The status of the territory was changed to a protectorate following the agreement between the two nations.
France established a protectorate over Tunisia in the late nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial political control of another country and occupied by settlers from that country
  • Synonym: dependency - a territory that is not fully independent but governed by another country

Etymology

From French 'protectorat', from Late Latin 'protector' (guardian).
