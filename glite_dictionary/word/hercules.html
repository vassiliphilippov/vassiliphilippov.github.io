Hercules

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɝːkjəˌliz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːkjʊˌliːz / (BrE)
a hero in classical mythology renowned for his extraordinary strength and for performing twelve challenging labors to attain immortality
mythical hero known for strength and labors

Examples

The legendary Hercules was famous for his incredible feats of strength.
Many stories recount how Hercules completed the twelve labors assigned to him.
In modern culture, someone with great strength is often compared to Hercules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Heracles - Greek name for the same mythological hero as Hercules

Etymology

From Latin Hercules, from Greek Hēraklēs, the name of the mythological hero.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhɝːkjəliːz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːkjʊliːz / (BrE)
a large constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located between Lyra and Corona Borealis
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The Milky Way passes through the constellation Hercules, making it a popular target for stargazers.
Hercules contains one of the brightest globular clusters in the night sky, M13.
From our location, Hercules is most visible during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin *Hercules*, from Greek *Hēraklēs*, the name of a mythological hero.
