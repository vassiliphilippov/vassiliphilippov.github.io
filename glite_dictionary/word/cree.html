Cree

1. NOUN

/ kri / (AmE), / kri / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Cree people of Canada and the northern United States
the native language of the Cree people

Examples

Many elders still speak Cree as their first language.
She is taking courses to learn the Cree language.
The children's story was translated into Cree for the school project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cree - a member of the Cree people

Etymology

From French 'Cri', from Canadian Algonquian (Montagnais) 'kiristino'.

2. NOUN

/ kriː / (AmE), / kriː / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian Indigenous people living mainly in central Canada
member of an Indigenous people of Canada

Examples

A group of Cree gathered to discuss how to preserve their language and traditions.
She is proud of her Cree heritage and often visits her family's community in Manitoba.
The Cree have a rich oral history that has been passed down for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwe - a member of another Algonquian-speaking Indigenous people in Canada and the northern US
  • Antonym: Inuit - a member of the Indigenous people inhabiting the Arctic regions of Canada, Greenland, and Alaska

Etymology

From the French 'Cris', a contraction of 'Kristinaux', a French adaptation of the Ojibwe word 'kiristino'.
