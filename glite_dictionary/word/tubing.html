tubing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbɪŋ / (BrE)
a long, hollow pipe or tube, often cylindrical, used to carry objects, liquids, or gases from one place to another
long hollow pipe or tube for carrying substances

Examples

The medical staff used clear tubing to deliver oxygen to the patient.
We need several meters of copper tubing for the plumbing project.
Plastic tubing is commonly used in laboratories to transport chemicals safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube used to convey substances such as liquids or gases
  • Synonym: hose - a flexible tube conveying water or other fluid
  • Synonym: duct - a tube or passage for air, liquid, cables, etc.

Etymology

From 'tube' + '-ing', denoting material made into tubes.
