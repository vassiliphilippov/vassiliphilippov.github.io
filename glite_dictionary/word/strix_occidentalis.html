Strix occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪks ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈstrɪks ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
A large owl of North America, known as the spotted owl, with dark brown plumage and a heavily spotted chest, found in forests from British Columbia to central Mexico.
species of North American spotted owl

Examples

The conservation status of the Strix occidentalis has become a major concern for environmentalists.
Researchers tracked the habitat range of Strix occidentalis throughout the Pacific Northwest.
Efforts to protect forests are essential for the survival of Strix occidentalis in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted owl - common name for Strix occidentalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'strix' meaning owl, 'occidentalis' meaning western.
