Blechnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛknəm / (AmE), / ˈblɛknəm / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere, commonly known as hard ferns, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae.
a genus of ferns (hard ferns)

Examples

The Blechnum species thrive in shaded, moist woodland environments.
Gardeners often plant Blechnum for its attractive, leathery fronds.
He specializes in studying the biology and distribution of Blechnum ferns.

Etymology

From Greek 'blechnon', meaning 'fern'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɛk.nəm / (AmE), / ˈblɛk.nəm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly Southern Hemisphere terrestrial ferns, commonly called hard ferns, found chiefly in the family Blechnaceae.
genus of mainly Southern Hemisphere hard ferns

Examples

Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Blechnum genus in South America.
The distinctive fronds of Blechnum make it a popular choice for shaded gardens.
Many Blechnum species thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a flowerless plant of the group Pteridophyta

Etymology

From Greek *blechnon* (a type of fern).
