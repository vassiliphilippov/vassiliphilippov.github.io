casualty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuˌælti / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjʊəlti / (BrE)
a person who is injured, killed, captured, or missing as a result of an accident or especially a military action
a person injured, killed, or missing in an event

Examples

The battle resulted in heavy casualty numbers on both sides.
After the explosion, the medic rushed to help every casualty on the field.
Reports confirmed that there was at least one casualty during the night raid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of a crime, accident, or other event

Etymology

From Middle French 'casualté', from Late Latin 'casualitas', from Latin 'casus' (fall, chance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuˌælti / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjʊəlti / (BrE)
a reduction in number of military personnel or equipment as a result of loss, injury, or destruction
military loss of people or equipment

Examples

The battalion suffered a significant casualty in equipment after the attack.
Naval reports indicated a severe casualty rate among the fleet’s destroyers.
The exercise simulated a casualty situation to prepare soldiers for combat losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loss - the state of no longer having something or as much of something

Etymology

From Middle French 'casualté', from Late Latin 'casualitas', from Latin 'casualis' (by chance).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuˌælti / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjuəlti / (BrE)
a person who is injured or killed in an accident, disaster, or war
person injured or killed in an accident or war

Examples

The storm last night caused several casualties in the small village.
There were no casualties reported after the explosion in the factory.
The first responders worked quickly to help the casualty at the scene of the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of an event or action

Etymology

From Middle French *casualté*, from Late Latin *casualitas* (chance, accident), from *casualis* (by chance).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒjəlti / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjuəlti / (BrE)
An accident or event that results in a person's death.
fatal accident; accident causing death

Examples

The motorway was closed for hours after a casualty involving a lorry and two cars.
There was only one casualty reported in the otherwise minor train derailment.
The company was fined following a workplace casualty last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatality - a death resulting from an accident or disaster

Etymology

From Middle French *casualté*, from Late Latin *casualitas* (chance, misfortune).
