OWLT

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ ˈdʌbəl.ju ˈɛl ˈti / (AmE), / ˈəʊ ˈdʌbəl.juː ˈɛl ˈtiː / (BrE)
the elapsed time it takes for light or radio signals to travel between the Earth and a celestial object
one-way light time (signal travel time)

Examples

The OWLT to Mars varies depending on the planets' positions in their orbits.
Engineers must account for the OWLT when sending commands to distant spacecraft.
Because of the long OWLT, communication with the probe is not instantaneous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: RTLT - the round trip light time between two objects

Etymology

Acronym from 'one-way light time'.
