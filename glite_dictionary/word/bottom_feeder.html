bottom feeder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəm ˌfidər / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌfiːdə / (BrE)
a person who seeks personal gain by exploiting situations or taking advantage of others, often in an unscrupulous or opportunistic manner
an exploitative or opportunistic person
informal

Examples

Many consider corporate lobbyists to be bottom feeders who manipulate the system for profit.
After the scandal, only a few bottom feeders tried to take advantage of the chaos left behind.
Some tabloids are run by bottom feeders who don't hesitate to invade people's privacy.

Synset Examples

Those bottom feeders only show up when there's free food.
He called the opportunists bottom feeders, taking advantage of others.
In the corporate world, some folks are just bottom feeders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities with little regard for principles
  • Synonym: parasite - a person who exploits others for personal gain
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, often with no expectation of personal gain

Etymology

Originally referred to fish or aquatic creatures that feed on or near the bottom of a body of water; figurative use for people arose in the late 20th century.
