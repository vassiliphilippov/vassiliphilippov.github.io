DDC

1. NOUN

/ di di siː / (AmE), / diː diː siː / (BrE)
An antiviral drug used in the treatment of HIV infection; a nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NARTI).
antiviral drug for HIV, nucleoside analog
medical

Examples

DDC was once widely used as part of combination therapy for HIV.
The patient experienced side effects while taking DDC as part of their medication regimen.
Clinical studies have shown that DDC can inhibit HIV replication by blocking reverse transcriptase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zalcitabine - An alternative name for DDC, used as an antiviral medication for HIV

Etymology

Acronym of dideoxycytidine, also known as zalcitabine.
