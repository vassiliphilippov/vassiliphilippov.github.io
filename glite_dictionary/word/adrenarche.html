adrenarche

1. NOUN

/ əˈdrɛnˌɑːrki / (AmE), / əˈdriːˌnɑːki / (BrE)
an increase in the activity of the adrenal glands that occurs just before puberty, resulting in changes such as body odor and growth of pubic hair
increase in adrenal gland activity before puberty
medical

Examples

The onset of adrenarche often precedes visible signs of puberty.
Researchers study adrenarche to better understand early hormonal changes in children.
Symptoms such as body odor and oily skin are associated with adrenarche.

Etymology

From Greek 'adēnal' (adrenal) + 'arkhē' (beginning, origin).
