pabulum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæb.jəl.əm / (AmE), / ˈpab.jʊ.ləm / (BrE)
A substance that provides nourishment and can be used as food, especially in a scientific or technical context.
substance providing nourishment

Examples

The scientist added a pabulum to the culture to promote bacterial growth.
Some novels offer little more than intellectual pabulum for their readers.
Proper pabulum is essential for the healthy development of young animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutriment - a substance that provides nourishment; food
  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance that people or animals eat or drink

Etymology

From Latin 'pabulum' (fodder, food), from 'pasci' (to feed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæbjələm / (AmE), / ˈpabjʊləm / (BrE)
Bland or insipid intellectual material that offers little stimulation or challenge.
bland or dull intellectual material

Examples

The novel was dismissed as mere pabulum for mass consumption, lacking real depth.
Some television shows offer little more than intellectual pabulum to their viewers.
The speech was criticized for being pabulum that failed to inspire or provoke thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fodder - material that is considered readily available and of little value, often used for dull or mediocre intellectual material
  • Degree Higher: grist - something that can be used for a particular purpose, especially as material for thinking or discussion

Etymology

From Latin 'pābulum' meaning 'food, nourishment'.
