to alienate

1. VERB

/ ˈeɪliəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈeɪliəneɪt / (BrE)
(Law) To transfer ownership of property or rights to another party.
transfer ownership or property (legal)
legal

Examples

Under the terms of the trust, the grantor cannot alienate the property.
He decided to alienate his share of the land to his children before retiring.
Once the contract is signed, she will formally alienate her interest in the estate.

Synset Examples

The will aliened the property to the heirs

Etymology

From Latin alienare (to make another's, transfer), from alienus (belonging to another).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02225792-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈeɪliəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈeɪlɪəneɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel isolated or estranged; to cause someone to become withdrawn or emotionally detached
cause to become isolated or estranged

Examples

His harsh criticism began to alienate his closest friends.
Rigid company policies can easily alienate new employees.
She felt alienated from her peers after moving to a new school.

Synset Examples

the boring work alienated his employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to estrange - to cause someone to stop feeling friendly or close; to alienate

Etymology

From Latin 'alienatus', past participle of 'alienare' (to make another's, estrange), from 'alienus' (belonging to another).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01827830-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈeɪ.li.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.li.ə.neɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose friends or support, or to make them feel isolated or unfriendly, especially where there was previously affection or goodwill
make someone hostile or distant

Examples

His harsh words began to alienate his colleagues at the office.
The new policy may alienate many long-time supporters of the company.
She worried that her criticism would alienate her closest friends.

Synset Examples

She alienated her friends when she became fanatically religious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to estrange - to make someone unfriendly or distant, especially in a relationship
  • Antonym: to reconcile - to restore friendly relations

Etymology

From Latin 'alienare' (to make another's, estrange), from 'alienus' (belonging to another).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01827478-v
Synset POS: v
