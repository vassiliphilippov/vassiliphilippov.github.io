kilogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈkɪləɡræm / (BrE)
The basic unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI), equal to one thousand grams.
SI unit of mass; 1,000 grams

Examples

The sack of rice weighs five kilograms.
She lost three kilograms after changing her diet.
A newborn panda typically weighs only about 0.1 kilogram.

Synset Examples

a kilogram is approximately 2.2 pounds

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gram - a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram

Etymology

From French *kilogramme*, from Greek *khilioi* (thousand) + French *gramme* (gram).
