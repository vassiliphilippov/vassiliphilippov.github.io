divide

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪd / (BrE)
a serious disagreement or difference between groups of people, often resulting in tension or hostility
serious disagreement between groups

Examples

The political divide between the parties has deepened in recent years.
Social media can sometimes widen the divide between different communities.
Efforts are being made to bridge the divide that exists within the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rift - a serious break in friendly relations
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'dividere' (to separate, divide).
