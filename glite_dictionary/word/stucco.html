stucco

1. VERB

/ ˈstʌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstʌkəʊ / (BrE)
to cover or coat a surface, especially a wall or ceiling, with stucco
to coat with stucco

Examples

The workers will stucco the exterior of the new house next week.
After repairing the cracks, the contractor stuccoed the wall to give it a smooth finish.
They spent hours stuccoing the ceiling to achieve the perfect texture.

Synset Examples

stucco the ceiling

Etymology

From Italian 'stucco', from Latin 'stucum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstʌkəʊ / (BrE)
a type of plaster, typically made from cement, sand, and lime, applied to exterior walls or surfaces while still soft, to create a hard, decorative coating when dry
plaster for covering surfaces

Examples

The house was finished with a layer of stucco that gave it a Mediterranean look.
Cracks formed in the stucco after several years of rain and sun exposure.
Many southwestern homes use stucco for both aesthetic appeal and weather resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - a paste applied to walls or ceilings that hardens to a smooth surface

Etymology

From Italian 'stucco', from Latin 'stuccum', meaning 'plaster'.

3. VERB

/ ˈstʌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstʌkəʊ / (BrE)
to cover or decorate a surface, especially a wall or ceiling, with stucco plaster.
to cover with stucco plaster

Examples

The builders stuccoed the exterior walls of the house to protect them from weather damage.
After renovating the living room, they stuccoed the ceiling to give it a decorative finish.
He spent the afternoon stuccoing the garden wall to match the new extension.

Synset Examples

stuccoed ceilings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - to cover a surface with plaster

Etymology

From Italian 'stucco', from Old High German 'stucchi' (crust, piece).
