to alkalinize

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.kə.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.lɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become alkaline; to increase the alkalinity of a substance or solution
make or become alkaline

Examples

Doctors may use medications to alkalinize the urine in patients with certain types of poisoning.
Adding sodium bicarbonate will alkalinize the solution and help prevent further corrosion.
If the blood becomes too acidic, treatments can help alkalinize it to restore normal balance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to make or become acidic
  • Antonym: to neutralize - to make chemically neutral; counteract acidity or alkalinity

Etymology

From alkaline + -ize, patterned after similar chemical verbs.

2. VERB

/ ˈælkəˌlaɪnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈalkəlɪnʌɪz / (BrE)
to make (a substance or solution) alkaline; to increase the pH above 7
make alkaline; raise pH above 7
medical

Examples

Doctors may alkalinize the urine to help patients excrete certain toxins more efficiently.
You can alkalinize your pool water by adding sodium carbonate.
The process will alkalinize the solution, reducing its corrosive effects.

Synset Examples

applying lime will alkalinise soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to make or become acidic

Etymology

From 'alkaline' + '-ize' (to make or become).
