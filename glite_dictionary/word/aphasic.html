aphasic

1. NOUN

/ əˈfeɪsɪk / (AmE), / əˈfeɪsɪk / (BrE)
a person affected by aphasia, the impairment or loss of the ability to understand or use language
person with aphasia
medical

Examples

The therapist worked closely with the aphasic to improve his communication skills.
Many aphasics experience frustration because they struggle to find the right words.
After her stroke, she became aphasic, unable to express her thoughts clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphasia - impairment of language ability

Etymology

From Greek 'aphatos' (speechless), via medical Latin 'aphasia' and English suffix '-ic'.

3. ADJ

/ əˈfeɪsɪk / (AmE), / əˈfeɪsɪk / (BrE)
Unable to speak or communicate effectively due to damage to the brain, especially from injury or disease.
unable to speak due to brain damage
medical

Examples

After the stroke, her doctors described her as aphasic and began intensive speech therapy.
The aphasic patient struggled to find the right words during the neurological assessment.
Many aphasic individuals can regain some language abilities with proper rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphasia - loss of ability to understand or express speech

Etymology

From Greek 'aphatos' (speechless), from 'a-' (not) + 'phanai' (to speak), via medical Latin 'aphasia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00152798-s
Synset POS: s
