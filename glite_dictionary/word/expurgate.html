to expurgate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.spɚˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to remove or alter parts of a book, play, or other text that are considered offensive or inappropriate
remove objectionable content from text

Examples

The publisher decided to expurgate the controversial scenes before releasing the book to schools.
Older editions of the novel were expurgated to remove any language deemed offensive at the time.
He was criticized for attempting to expurgate history to make it more palatable.

Synset Examples

bowdlerize a novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bowdlerize - to remove material considered improper or offensive from a text

Etymology

From Latin expurgare, from ex- (out) + purgare (to cleanse).
