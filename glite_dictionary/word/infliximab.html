infliximab

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (BrE)
a monoclonal antibody drug used to treat autoimmune diseases by inhibiting tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNFα) activity
monoclonal antibody medication for autoimmune diseases
medical

Examples

The patient began receiving infliximab injections to control her Crohn's disease symptoms.
Doctors often prescribe infliximab when other treatments for rheumatoid arthritis are ineffective.
After three months on infliximab, his joint inflammation markedly improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adalimumab - a monoclonal antibody that also neutralizes TNFα
  • Synonym: etanercept - another anti-TNF medication used for autoimmune diseases

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (BrE)
a monoclonal antibody used as a drug to treat autoimmune diseases such as Crohn's disease and rheumatoid arthritis, administered by infusion under medical supervision
monoclonal antibody drug for autoimmune diseases
medical

Examples

The patient received an infusion of infliximab to manage her Crohn's disease symptoms.
Doctors often prescribe infliximab when other treatments for rheumatoid arthritis are ineffective.
After starting infliximab, his joint pain and inflammation decreased significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adalimumab - another monoclonal antibody for autoimmune diseases
  • Synonym: etanercept - a biologic drug for rheumatoid arthritis and related autoimmune diseases

Etymology

Blend of 'inflix-' (possibly from 'inflixare', Latin root suggesting 'to fasten in') and '-imab', a common antibody drug suffix.
