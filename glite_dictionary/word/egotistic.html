egotistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəʊˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing an exaggerated sense of self-importance; excessively self-centered or conceited.
excessively self-centered; conceited

Examples

Her egotistic attitude made it difficult for her to work as part of a team.
He was so egotistic that he never considered anyone else’s opinion.
The egotistic celebrity was constantly demanding attention from everyone around him.

Synset Examples

a conceited fool
an attitude of self-conceited arrogance
an egotistical disregard of others
so swollen by victory that he was unfit for normal duty
growing ever more swollen-headed and arbitrary
vain about her clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfish - concerned excessively or exclusively with oneself
  • Antonym: altruistic - showing a disinterested and selfless concern for others
  • Synonym: egotistical - having an exaggerated sense of self-importance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From ego + -istic; based on Latin ego (I, self).

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
having or showing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities; self-centered.
self-centered; conceited

Examples

She found his egotistic attitude difficult to tolerate in group projects.
An egotistic person often neglects the needs of others.
His egotistic remarks made him unpopular with his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotistical - having an exaggerated sense of self-importance; synonym for 'egotistic'
  • Antonym: selfless - concerned more with others than oneself; opposite of 'egotistic'

Etymology

From 'ego' (Latin, 'I') + '-istic' (forming adjectives).
