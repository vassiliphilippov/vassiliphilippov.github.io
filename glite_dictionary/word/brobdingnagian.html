Brobdingnagian

1. ADJ

/ ˌbrɑb.dɪŋˈnæɡ.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌbrɒb.dɪŋˈnæɡ.i.ən / (BrE)
Extremely large; gigantic; of tremendous size.
gigantic; extremely large

Examples

Their new house dwarfs the neighbors with its Brobdingnagian proportions.
The museum displayed a Brobdingnagian sculpture that dominated the main hall.
His ambitions are as Brobdingnagian as his ideas.

Synset Examples

immense numbers of birds
at vast (or immense) expense
the vast reaches of outer space
the vast accumulation of knowledge … which we call civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - very large or enormous in size
  • Synonym: colossal - extremely large
  • Antonym: tiny - extremely small in size

Etymology

From Brobdingnag, the land of giants in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver’s Travels', plus the suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˌbrɑːb.dɪŋˈnæɡ.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌbrɒb.dɪŋˈnæɡ.i.ən / (BrE)
Extremely large; gigantic. Also, relating to or characteristic of Brobdingnag, the land of giants in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels'.
extremely large; gigantic

Examples

The new shopping mall is a Brobdingnagian structure that dominates the city skyline.
Her appetite for knowledge was as Brobdingnagian as her collection of books.
Standing next to the Brobdingnagian statue, I felt incredibly small.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large in size or extent
  • Antonym: Lilliputian - very small, or relating to Lilliput (also from Gulliver's Travels)

Etymology

From Brobdingnag, the fictional land of giants in Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).

Synset POS: a
