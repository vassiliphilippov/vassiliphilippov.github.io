Anaphalis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæfəlɪs / (AmE), / əˈnafəlɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs with woolly or hoary leaves, native to north temperate regions, commonly called pearly everlasting
genus of perennial herbs (pearly everlasting)

Examples

The dried flowers of Anaphalis are popular in floral arrangements for their longevity.
In the wild, Anaphalis often grows on sunny hillsides and open meadows.
Gardeners admire the silvery foliage that gives Anaphalis its distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearly everlasting - a common name for plants in the genus Anaphalis

Etymology

From Greek 'anaphalis', the name of the plant in classical authors.

2. PROPN

/ əˈnæfəlɪs / (AmE), / əˈnafəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the family Asteraceae, native to northern temperate regions, characterized by woolly leaves and small white flowers; commonly known as 'pearly everlasting.'
genus of woolly-leaved herbs (pearly everlasting)

Examples

The Anaphalis genus is popular among gardeners for its long-lasting flowers.
Many species of Anaphalis are commonly called pearly everlasting.
Botanists have discovered new varieties within the Anaphalis genus in recent years.

Etymology

From Greek anaphalis, the classical name for a kind of plant.
