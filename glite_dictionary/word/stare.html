stare

1. NOUN

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
A long and fixed look with eyes wide open, often showing surprise, curiosity, or hostility.
a fixed, prolonged look

Examples

He gave me a cold stare when I walked into the room.
After the embarrassing incident, she felt everyone's stare upon her.
The stranger's intense stare made him feel uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glance - a quick look
  • Synonym: gaze - a steady and intent look

Etymology

From Old English *starian* (to gaze), related to Old High German *staran*.

2. VERB

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with eyes fixed wide open, often in surprise, curiosity, or thought
look with fixed, wide-open eyes

Examples

She couldn't help but stare at the breathtaking view from the mountain top.
The child stared at the magician, mesmerized by the trick.
People often stare when something unusual happens in public.

Synset Examples

The students stared at the teacher with amazement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - look steadily and intently
  • Synonym: glare - stare in an angry or fierce way
  • Antonym: glance - take a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Old English 'starian' (to look fixedly); related to Dutch 'staren' and German 'starren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02137210-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛː / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with a fixed or steady gaze, often for a long time or with intense focus
look fixedly with eyes

Examples

He couldn't help but stare at the unusual sculpture in the gallery.
She stared out the window, lost in thought for several minutes.
Please stop staring; it's making everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

The ancestor in the painting is staring down menacingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - look steadily and intently
  • Degree Higher: glare - look in an angry or fierce way
  • Degree Lower: glance - look quickly or briefly

Etymology

From Old English *starian* (to gaze, look fixedly), related to Dutch *staren* and German *starren*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
