hydrargyrum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑrˌdʒɪrəm / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɑːdʒɪrəm / (BrE)
A heavy, silvery metallic element (symbol Hg), also known as mercury, which is the only metal that is liquid at ordinary temperatures and is toxic in nature.
scientific term for mercury (chemical element)

Examples

The element hydrargyrum is more commonly known as mercury in modern chemistry.
Laboratory thermometers once used hydrargyrum because of its liquid state at room temperature.
Exposure to hydrargyrum can pose serious health risks due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury - a heavy silvery toxic metallic element, also known as quicksilver

Etymology

From Latin 'hydrargyrum', from Greek 'hydrargyros' (hydor 'water' + argyros 'silver').
