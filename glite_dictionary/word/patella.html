Patella

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛl.ə / (AmE), / pəˈtel.ə / (BrE)
A genus of marine molluscs in the family Patellidae, comprising common European limpets.
genus of European limpets

Examples

The Patella genus includes several species commonly found on rocky European shores.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Patella to understand limpet development.
The shell of Patella is often used in studies of marine adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - a marine mollusc with a conical shell

Etymology

From Latin 'patella' (small pan), referring to the shell's shape.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛlə / (AmE), / pəˈtɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of marine gastropod mollusks known as limpets, found commonly in European coastal waters
genus of European limpets

Examples

The Patella genus is easily recognized by its distinctive conical shell.
Several species within the Patella genus inhabit the rocky shores of Western Europe.
Biologists studied how Patella adapts to changing tidal environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - marine mollusk with a conical shell

Etymology

From Latin patella, meaning 'small pan' or 'dish', referring to the shell's shape.
