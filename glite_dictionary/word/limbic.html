limbic

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmbɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or forming a limbus, especially in anatomy; often used to denote the limbic system of the brain or a border area in anatomical structures.
relating to a limbus or border (anatomy)

Examples

The limbic system plays a vital role in regulating emotions and memory.
Damage to limbic structures can affect a person’s mood and behavior.
Researchers are studying how the limbic regions interact with other parts of the brain.

Etymology

From Latin limbus (border, edge) + -ic.
