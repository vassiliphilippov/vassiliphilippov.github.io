to advise

1. VERB

/ ədˈvaɪz / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to inform someone formally about something
inform someone formally

Examples

She advised me that the meeting had been postponed until next week.
Passengers were advised of changes to their flight schedule via email.
You should advise your manager as soon as the problem is resolved.

Synset Examples

I advised him that the rent was due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to notify - to inform someone officially
  • Synonym: to inform - to give information to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'aviser', from Latin 'advisare', from 'ad' (to) + 'visare' (to see, look at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00875364-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ədˈvaɪz / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to offer suggestions or guidance about what someone should do; to give advice to someone
to give advice or guidance

Examples

The doctor advised him to get more rest after his surgery.
I strongly advise you to double-check your work before submitting it.
She often advises her friends on financial matters.

Synset Examples

The teacher counsels troubled students
The lawyer counselled me when I was accused of tax fraud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recommend - to suggest that something is good or suitable for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: counsel - to give professional advice to someone
  • Antonym: to discourage - to advise someone against doing something

Etymology

From Old French 'aviser', from Latin 'advisare', frequentative of 'advisere', from 'ad-' (to) + 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00874568-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ədˈvaɪz / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to formally propose or announce a plan or course of action
formally propose or announce something

Examples

The committee will advise major changes to the current policy tomorrow.
She plans to advise the board on a new direction for the company.
The government may advise stricter regulations next year.

Synset Examples

the senator proposed to abolish the sales tax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propose - to put forward a plan or suggestion for consideration
  • Synonym: to recommend - to suggest as suitable or advisable; to endorse a course of action

Etymology

From Old French 'aviser', from Latin 'advisare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'visare' (to see, consider).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00876925-v
Synset POS: v
