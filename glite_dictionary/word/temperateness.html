temperateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərətnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being moderate in degree, especially with regard to climate or weather; mildness.
moderation, especially of climate or weather

Examples

The temperateness of the region makes it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts year-round.
Tourists appreciate the city's temperateness compared to the sweltering summers of nearby areas.
Due to its temperateness, the valley rarely experiences extreme temperature changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the quality of being moderate, especially in behavior or weather

Etymology

From Latin 'temperatus' (moderate), via Old French 'temprer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərətnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of showing moderation or self-restraint; avoidance of excess
moderation or self-restraint

Examples

Her temperateness in making decisions made her a trusted leader.
The debate was marked by an unusual temperateness in tone and approach.
Practicing temperateness can help maintain good health and balanced relationships.

Synset Examples

an effective temperateness in debate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes
  • Antonym: intemperance - lack of moderation; excess

Etymology

From Latin 'temperatus' (moderate, restrained) via the adjective 'temperate' and the suffix '-ness'.
