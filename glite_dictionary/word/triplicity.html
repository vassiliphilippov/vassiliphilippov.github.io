triplicity

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / trɪˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
(in astrology) one of the four groups of zodiac signs, each comprising three signs that are 120 degrees apart.
astrological group of three zodiac signs

Examples

In traditional astrology, each triplicity is associated with one of the four classical elements.
The fire triplicity consists of Aries, Leo, and Sagittarius.
Many astrologers believe that planets in the same triplicity tend to work in harmony.

Etymology

From Middle French *triplicité*, from Latin *triplicitas* (threefoldness), from *triplex* (threefold).

2. NOUN

/ trɪˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / trɪˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being triple; a group or set of three
state of being triple; group of three

Examples

The triplicity of the design gives it a sense of balance and harmony.
In astrology, each element is assigned to a triplicity composed of three signs.
The artist explored the concept of triplicity in her latest sculpture, arranging the forms in groups of three.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trio - a group of three people or things
  • Synonym: triad - a group of three closely related things or people

Etymology

From Latin triplicitas (threefoldness), from triplex (triple).
