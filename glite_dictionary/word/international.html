international

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving two or more countries
between or among nations

Examples

The United Nations is an international organization that promotes peace and cooperation among countries.
They signed an international trade agreement to encourage business between the two nations.
Many students attend international schools to learn about different cultures and languages.

Synset Examples

international affairs
an international agreement
international waters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - relating to one country or home affairs
  • Degree Higher: global - relating to the whole world

Etymology

From inter- (between) + national (relating to a nation).

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃnəl / (BrE)
existing, occurring, or carried on between two or more countries.
between or involving countries

Examples

She hopes to pursue an international career in finance after graduation.
The international conference attracted experts from over 50 countries.
Many companies are expanding their international operations to reach new markets.

Synset Examples

external commerce
international trade
developing nations need outside help

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - relating to one country; not international
  • Degree Higher: global - relating to the whole world

Etymology

From inter- (between) + national.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01041567-s
Synset POS: s
