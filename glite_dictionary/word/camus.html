Camus

1. PROPN

/ kæˈmu / (AmE), / kaˈmyː / (BrE)
(1913–1960) French author and philosopher noted for works exploring themes of existentialism and the absurd, including 'The Stranger' and 'The Myth of Sisyphus.'
French writer and philosopher (1913–1960)

Examples

Many students read Camus in philosophy and literature courses.
The existential themes in Camus's novels have influenced generations of thinkers.
Critics often compare Sartre and Camus when discussing twentieth-century philosophy.

Etymology

French surname, Albert Camus.
