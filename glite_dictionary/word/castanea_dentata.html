Castanea dentata

1. NOUN

/ kæˈsteɪniə dɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / kasˈtiːnɪə dɛnˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, commonly known as the American chestnut, once abundant from Maine to Alabama
American chestnut tree species

Examples

The blight nearly wiped out the majestic Castanea dentata forests in the early twentieth century.
Botanists are working to restore populations of Castanea dentata throughout its native range.
Leaves from Castanea dentata are easily recognized by their long, serrated edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American chestnut - common name for Castanea dentata

Etymology

From Latin: 'castanea' meaning 'chestnut' and 'dentata' meaning 'toothed', referring to the leaf margins.
