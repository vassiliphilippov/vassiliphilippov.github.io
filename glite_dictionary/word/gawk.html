gawk

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔk / (AmE), / ɡɔːk / (BrE)
a person who is awkward or clumsy, and often appears unintelligent
an awkward or clumsy person

Examples

He always felt like a gawk at social gatherings, tripping over his own feet.
The boy turned out to be more than just a gawk; he was actually very clever.
She didn't want to be seen as a gawk among her more graceful friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clod - a dull or stupid person
  • Synonym: lout - an uncouth or aggressive person
  • Synonym: oaf - a stupid, uncultured, or clumsy person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10294336-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡɑk / (AmE), / ɡɔːk / (BrE)
to stare openly and stupidly; to look at someone or something with awkward amazement or curiosity
to stare open-mouthed or stupidly

Examples

Tourists would often gawk at the towering skyscrapers in the city center.
Please don't gawk—it's impolite to stare at people like that.
She couldn't help but gawk in amazement at the celebrity walking by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ogle - to stare at someone in a way that shows sexual attraction
  • Synonym: stare - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something
  • Antonym: glance - to take a brief or hurried look

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a variant of Scottish 'gowk' (fool, cuckoo).
