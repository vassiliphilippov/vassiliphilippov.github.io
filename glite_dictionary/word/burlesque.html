burlesque

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
a type of theatrical entertainment featuring comic skits, parody, and occasionally striptease, often characterized by broad and earthy humor
theatrical comedy show, often with parody or striptease

Examples

The theater downtown puts on a burlesque show every Friday night featuring comedians and dancers.
Historically, burlesque was known for its playful parodies of serious drama.
The troupe revived the art of burlesque with clever skits and dazzling costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a particular writer or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to expose or criticize

Etymology

From Italian 'burlesco' (mockery, ridicule), from 'burla' (joke, fun).

2. NOUN

/ bɝːˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
a literary, dramatic, or musical work that imitates and exaggerates the style of someone or something in a humorous or satirical way
comic imitation or parody of a style

Examples

The play is a clever burlesque of Victorian melodrama.
He wrote a burlesque that pokes fun at the conventions of opera.
Their performance was more burlesque than sincere tribute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - a humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature or writing
  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize
  • Antonym: homage - a respectful imitation or tribute

Etymology

From Italian 'burlesco', from 'burla' meaning joke or mockery.

Examples

4. VERB

/ bɝːˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
to imitate or represent someone or something in a way that exaggerates or mocks them, often for comic effect
parody or mock through exaggerated imitation

Examples

The comedian burlesqued famous politicians in his new stand-up routine.
Their skit burlesqued the overly dramatic scenes in classic soap operas.
In the play, several characters were cleverly burlesqued for comic relief.

Synset Examples

The students spoofed the teachers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - to imitate the style of someone or something for comic effect
  • Synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful or contemptuous manner

Etymology

From Italian *burlesco* (mockery), from *burla* (joke, mockery).
