caparison

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpærɪsən / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
a decorated cloth covering, often richly ornamented, placed over a horse, especially in ceremonial or historical contexts
decorated covering for a horse

Examples

The knight's horse wore an elaborate caparison embroidered with the family's crest.
During the parade, each stallion was adorned with a brightly colored caparison.
Museum exhibits often display the caparison alongside medieval armor and weapons.

Etymology

From Old French 'caparasson', from Spanish 'caparazón' (armor, covering), ultimately from late Latin 'cappa' (cape).

2. VERB

/ kəˈpærɪsən / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
to dress or cover (a horse or other animal) with an ornamental or decorative covering
dress a horse with decorative covering

Examples

The knights caparison their steeds before the grand parade.
She caparisoned the horse in velvet and gold for the royal procession.
Grooms spend hours caparisoning each horse for the festival.

Synset Examples

caparison the horses for the festive occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes or coverings on

Etymology

From Old French caparasson, from Spanish caparazón (armor, covering).
