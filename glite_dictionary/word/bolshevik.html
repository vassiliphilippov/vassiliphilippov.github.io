Bolshevik

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəvɪk / (BrE)
a member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, later the Communist Party, which seized power in Russia in 1917 under the leadership of Lenin
member of early Russian Communist Party

Examples

The Bolsheviks played a pivotal role in the Russian Revolution of 1917.
After the overthrow of the provisional government, the Bolsheviks established a socialist state.
Many Bolsheviks faced opposition from both internal and external enemies during the civil war.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Menshevik - member of the minority faction opposed to the Bolsheviks in the Russian Social Democratic Party
  • Synonym: Communist - advocate or supporter of communism, especially in Russia

Etymology

From Russian bolʹshevik, from bolʹshoy (majority), referring to the majority faction.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəvɪk / (BrE)
A member of the radical faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party that seized power in the Russian Revolution of 1917; more broadly, an extremist revolutionary.
member of radical Russian revolutionary faction; extreme revolutionary

Examples

He was accused of being a Bolshevik after expressing his radical views at the meeting.
During the early 20th century, the rise of the Bolsheviks changed the course of Russian history.
Some critics called the reforms Bolshevik in their extremity and opposition to tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - a person with extreme political or social views
  • Antonym: moderate - a person with moderate views, not extreme

Etymology

From Russian 'bol'shevik' (member of the majority), from 'bol'she' meaning 'more'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəvɪk / (BrE)
relating to Bolshevism or its supporters, especially to the Russian Bolshevik movement or its principles
relating to Bolshevism or the Bolsheviks

Examples

The Bolshevik regime implemented sweeping social and economic reforms after 1917.
She wrote a thesis on Bolshevik ideology during the early Soviet period.
Many exiles fled abroad to escape Bolshevik persecution after the revolution.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Menshevik - relating to the Russian socialist group opposing the Bolsheviks
  • Synonym: communist - relating to communism or a member of a Communist party

Etymology

From Russian 'bol'shevik', meaning 'member of the majority', from 'bol'shoy' (big, great).
