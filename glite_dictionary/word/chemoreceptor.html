chemoreceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌkemoʊrɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊrɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory receptor that detects and responds to chemical substances, especially as part of the nervous system
receptor detecting chemical stimuli
medical

Examples

Some animals rely heavily on their chemoreceptors to detect food and danger in their environment.
The human tongue contains chemoreceptors that allow us to taste different flavors.
Researchers are studying how certain drugs affect chemoreceptor activity in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptor - a cell or group of cells that receives stimuli

Etymology

From chemo- (relating to chemicals) + receptor.
