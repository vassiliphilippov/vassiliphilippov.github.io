vanity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænəti / (AmE), / ˈvænɪti / (BrE)
Excessive pride in or admiration of one's own appearance, abilities, or achievements; conceit.
excessive pride or conceit

Examples

She could not hide her vanity when she received the award and praised her own talents.
His vanity made it difficult for him to accept any criticism, no matter how constructive.
The actress’s vanity was evident in the way she constantly checked her appearance in the mirror.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a high opinion of one's own worth or abilities
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance; the opposite of vanity

Etymology

From Old French vanité, from Latin vanitas (emptiness, futility, pride).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈvæn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
excessive pride or admiration in one's own appearance, abilities, or achievements
excessive pride in oneself

Examples

His vanity prevented him from admitting he was wrong.
The celebrity was known for her vanity and love of attention.
Critics accused him of vanity after he boasted about his achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction from one's own achievements
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness

Etymology

From Latin vanitas (emptiness, futility), from vanus (empty).

4. NOUN

/ ˈvænəti / (AmE), / ˈvænɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being worthless, futile, or lacking real value or substance.
worthlessness or futility

Examples

He came to realize the vanity of his endless arguments.
Much of the work was exposed as mere vanity after the results were published.
She spoke about the vanity of worldly pursuits in her speech.

Synset Examples

he rejected the vanities of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futility - the quality of being pointless or useless
  • Synonym: worthlessness - the quality of lacking value or usefulness

Etymology

From Latin vanitas (emptiness, futility), from vanus (empty, vain).
