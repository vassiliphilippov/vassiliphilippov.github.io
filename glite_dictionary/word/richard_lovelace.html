Richard Lovelace

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ˈlʌvˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ˈlʌvlɪs / (BrE)
(1618–1657) English poet known for his lyrical poems and as a supporter of the Royalist cause during the English Civil War.
English poet and Royalist (1618–1657)

Examples

Richard Lovelace is best remembered for his poem 'To Althea, from Prison'.
The works of Richard Lovelace reflect the tensions of the English Civil War era.
Many literature students study Richard Lovelace for his contributions to Cavalier poetry.
