turbojet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːboʊˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
A jet engine in which a turbine-driven fan provides air to the burner and produces additional thrust.
jet engine type with turbine-driven fan

Examples

The fighter was powered by a single turbojet capable of supersonic speeds.
Early commercial airliners often used turbojet engines before the introduction of turbofans.
He explained how a turbojet works during his presentation on aircraft propulsion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet engine - type of engine that generates thrust by expelling jet of gas

Etymology

From 'turbo-' (relating to a turbine) + 'jet' (a stream of gas).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.boʊˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bəʊˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
A jet engine in which a turbine-driven compressor draws in and compresses air for combustion, producing a powerful thrust; also, an aircraft powered by such an engine.
jet engine with turbine compressor

Examples

The fighter was powered by a single turbojet that allowed it to reach supersonic speeds.
Early turbojet aircraft revolutionized military aviation in the 1940s.
A turbojet is less efficient at low speeds compared to a turbofan engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet engine - an engine that propels an aircraft by ejecting a high-speed jet of gas

Etymology

Formed from 'turbo-' (relating to a turbine) and 'jet' (as in jet engine); first used in the 20th century in aviation.
