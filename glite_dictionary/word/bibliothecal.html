bibliothecal

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪbliəˈθikəl / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliəˈθiːkəl / (BrE)
Relating to a library, bibliotheca, or librarian.
pertaining to libraries or librarians

Examples

The rare volumes were kept in a bibliothecal archive accessible only to researchers.
Her bibliothecal duties required cataloguing thousands of new books each year.
The report described changes in bibliothecal administration across the country's academic institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a building or room containing a collection of books

Etymology

From Late Latin bibliotheca (library) + -al.
