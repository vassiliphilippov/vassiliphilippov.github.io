Apium graveolens rapaceum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpiəm ɡrəˈviːələnz rəˈpeɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪpiəm ɡrəˈviːələnz rəˈpæsiəm / (BrE)
a variety of celery cultivated for its thick, edible, aromatic root, commonly known as celeriac.
variety of celery (celeriac) with edible root

Examples

Apium graveolens rapaceum is often used in soups and stews for its nutty flavor.
Chefs prize Apium graveolens rapaceum for its unique texture in winter dishes.
Farmers harvest Apium graveolens rapaceum in autumn when its root is at its largest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celeriac - the common name for Apium graveolens rapaceum, used in cooking
  • Degree Lower: celery - a related variety of Apium graveolens with edible stalks

Etymology

From Latin: Apium (celery) + graveolens (strong-smelling) + rapaceum (turnip-like).
