canalis inguinalis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌneɪlɪs ɪŋˌɡwɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / kaˌnaːlɪs ɪŋˌɡwɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
the oblique passage through the lower abdominal wall, through which, in males, the testes descend and the spermatic cord passes, and in females, the round ligament of the uterus transmits
anatomical passage in lower abdominal wall
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the canalis inguinalis for signs of a hernia.
During fetal development, the testes descend through the canalis inguinalis into the scrotum.
In females, the round ligament passes through the canalis inguinalis to reach the labia majora.

Etymology

From Latin: 'canalis' (canal, channel) + 'inguinalis' (of the groin).
