to irradiate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to expose something to radiation, especially for preservation, sterilization, or therapeutic purposes
expose to radiation

Examples

Modern food processing plants often irradiate products to eliminate bacteria and extend shelf life.
Doctors may irradiate cancerous tumors as part of radiation therapy.
The laboratory irradiated the samples to test their resistance to radiation exposure.

Synset Examples

irradiate food

Etymology

From Latin irradiatus, past participle of 'irradiare' (to shine upon).

4. VERB

/ ɪˈreɪdieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪdieɪt / (BrE)
to expose something, especially a sample, to radiation, often for analysis, sterilization, or treatment
expose to radiation (for analysis or treatment)

Examples

Before analyzing the minerals, the technician will irradiate the sample in a controlled environment.
Some laboratories irradiate samples to eliminate any biological contaminants before testing.
You should always wear protective gear when you irradiate the sample in the lab.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irradiation - the process of exposing to radiation
