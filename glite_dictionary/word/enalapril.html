enalapril

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæləˌprɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈnaləprɪl / (BrE)
A medication that acts as an ACE inhibitor, used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure by relaxing blood vessels.
medication; ACE inhibitor for blood pressure, heart failure
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed enalapril to help manage his high blood pressure.
After her heart attack, she started taking enalapril as part of her treatment regimen.
Some patients may experience a cough as a side effect of enalapril.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lisinopril - an ACE inhibitor used to treat high blood pressure

Etymology

From English 'en-' (a chemical prefix) and '-alapril' (a coined stem based on related ACE inhibitors).
