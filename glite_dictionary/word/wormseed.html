wormseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrmˌsid / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmˌsiːd / (BrE)
a strong-smelling tropical American plant of the genus Dysphania, formerly used as a medicinal herb, especially for expelling parasitic worms
rank-smelling tropical American pigweed

Examples

Farmers used wormseed to control intestinal parasites in livestock before modern medications were available.
The fields were overrun with the pungent odor of wormseed during the summer months.
Some traditional remedies involve brewing tea from wormseed leaves to treat digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigweed - any of various coarse weedy plants of the genus Amaranthus and related genera

Etymology

Named for its traditional use as a remedy against intestinal worms; from 'worm' + 'seed'.
