Janus-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪ.nəs feɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪ.nəs feɪst / (BrE)
Having two contrasting aspects, often used to describe someone or something that is deceitful or hypocritical, or that looks both to the past and the future.
having two contrasting faces or aspects

Examples

The politician was criticized for being Janus-faced in his public and private statements.
Her Janus-faced approach to friendship left many unsure of where they stood with her.
The company’s Janus-faced policies seemed to support both environmental initiatives and increased industrial output.

Synset Examples

Janus the two-faced god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-faced - insincere or deceitful; having two different sides

Etymology

From Janus, the Roman god depicted with two faces, and 'faced'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪnəs feɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪnəs feɪst / (BrE)
deliberately deceitful or hypocritical, particularly by pretending one set of feelings and acting under the influence of another; two-faced
deceitful, two-faced

Examples

The company's Janus-faced public statements hid its true environmental practices from the public.
A Janus-faced politician will say one thing to the press and another in private meetings.
Her Janus-faced demeanor made it difficult to know her true motives.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-faced - insincere or deceitful; pretending to have one set of feelings while acting on another
  • Synonym: hypocritical - behaving in a way that suggests one has higher standards or more noble beliefs than is the case
  • Antonym: honest - truthful and sincere, not deceitful

Etymology

From Janus, the Roman god depicted with two faces, symbolizing duality or duplicity.
