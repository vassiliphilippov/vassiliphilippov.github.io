Ramphomicron

1. NOUN

/ ræm.foʊˈmaɪ.krən / (AmE), / ræm.fəˈmɪ.krɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small birds in the hummingbird family, including the black-backed and purple-backed thornbills.
genus of thornbill hummingbirds

Examples

The Ramphomicron genus is known for its distinctive, brightly colored plumage.
Scientists observed a rare species of Ramphomicron in the Andes mountains.
Both the black-backed and purple-backed thornbills belong to the Ramphomicron genus.

Etymology

From Greek rhamphe (beak) + mikron (small), referring to the bird’s small beak.

2. NOUN

/ ræm.foʊˈmaɪ.krən / (AmE), / ræm.fəʊˈmɪ.krɒn / (BrE)
A genus of hummingbirds known as thornbills, including the black-backed thornbill and the purple-backed thornbill, found in parts of South America.
a genus of thornbill hummingbirds

Examples

The Ramphomicron genus includes some of the smallest and most colorful thornbills.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Ramphomicron species in the Andes.
The distinctive bill shape sets Ramphomicron apart from other hummingbird genera.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhamphos' (beak) + 'micron' (small), referring to the bird's small beak.
