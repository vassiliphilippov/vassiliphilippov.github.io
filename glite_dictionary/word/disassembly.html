disassembly

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bə.li / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəli / (BrE)
the act of taking something apart, especially a piece of machinery or equipment
the act of taking something apart

Examples

The disassembly of the engine revealed several worn-out parts.
Proper disassembly is required before any repairs can be made.
He documented each step during the disassembly process to ensure nothing was lost.

Synset Examples

Russia and the United States discussed the dismantling of their nuclear weapons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assembly - the act of putting parts together to make a whole
  • Synonym: dismantling - the act of taking something apart, especially machinery or structures

Etymology

From dis- (a prefix meaning 'apart') + assembly (from Latin 'assimulare', to bring together).
