orifice

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ə.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ɪ.fɪs / (BrE)
an opening or hole, especially one that leads into a bodily cavity or passage
an opening or hole, especially in the body

Examples

During the surgery, the doctor examined the nasal orifice for signs of obstruction.
The plumber cleared the orifice of the pipe to restore water flow.
An artificial valve controls the orifice between the heart's chambers.

Synset Examples

the orifice into the aorta from the lower left chamber of the heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - an act or space of making open
  • Synonym: mouth - the opening through which an organism takes in food

Etymology

From Latin 'orificium', from 'os' (mouth) + 'facere' (to make).
