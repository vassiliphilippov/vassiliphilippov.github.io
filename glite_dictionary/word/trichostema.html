Trichostema

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌkoʊˈstiːmə / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒˈstiːmə / (BrE)
a genus of North American aromatic herbs or subshrubs, commonly known as blue curls
genus of aromatic North American herbs (blue curls)

Examples

Botanists studied the Trichostema species growing in the California chaparral.
The aromatic leaves of Trichostema have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
Blue curls, officially known as Trichostema, attract many pollinators to the garden.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'stēma' (thread, stem), referring to the hairy stems of the plants in this genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪkoʊˈstiːmə / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈstiːmə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs or subshrubs native to North America, commonly known as blue curls.
genus of North American aromatic herbs

Examples

The Trichostema genus is notable for its distinctly aromatic leaves and striking blue flowers.
Many gardeners plant Trichostema for its resilience and pleasant scent.
Several species within Trichostema are commonly referred to as blue curls.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'stema' (stamen), referring to the hair-like stamens of the flowers.
