Madagascan

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæd.əˈɡæs.kən / (AmE), / ˌmæd.əˈɡæs.kən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Madagascar
person from Madagascar

Examples

Many Madagascans speak both Malagasy and French fluently.
The Madagascans are known for their unique culture and biodiversity.
Several Madagascans attended the international conference in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malagasy - a native or inhabitant of Madagascar

Etymology

From Madagascar + -an, a suffix forming demonyms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09741043-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌmædəˈɡæskən / (AmE), / ˌmadəˈɡaskən / (BrE)
of or relating to Madagascar or its people.
relating to Madagascar or its people

Examples

The Madagascan rainforests are home to many unique species found nowhere else.
She is conducting research on Madagascan culture and traditions.
They enjoyed a meal flavored with authentic Madagascan vanilla beans.

Synset Examples

Madagascan pepper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malagasy - of or relating to Madagascar, its people, or its language

Etymology

From Madagascar + -an, forming an adjective meaning 'of Madagascar.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03094017-a
Synset POS: a
