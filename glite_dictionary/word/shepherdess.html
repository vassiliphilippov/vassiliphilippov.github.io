shepherdess

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛpərdɛs / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpədɪs / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to look after sheep
woman who tends sheep

Examples

The young shepherdess led her flock to graze in the green meadows.
Dressed in a simple cloak, the shepherdess watched over her sheep at dawn.
In the painting, a shepherdess is shown resting beneath an old oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who takes care of sheep
  • Synonym: sheepdog - a dog trained to help with herding sheep

Etymology

Formed from "shepherd" + "-ess" (a suffix denoting female).
