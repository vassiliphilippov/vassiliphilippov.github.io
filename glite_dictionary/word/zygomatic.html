zygomatic

1. NOUN

/ ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌzaɪɡəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
the bone that forms the prominence of the cheek below and to the side of the eye, also known as the cheekbone
cheekbone; bone below the eye
medical

Examples

She fractured her zygomatic in the car accident, requiring surgery to repair the bone.
The zygomatic gives the human face its distinctive cheek contour.
In some animals, the size of the zygomatic is related to the strength of their jaw muscles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zygomatic bone - the bone forming the prominence of the cheek

Etymology

From Greek zygōmatikos, from zygōma (yoke, bone under the eye).

2. ADJ

/ ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌzɪɡəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the cheek region of the face, especially the zygomatic bone
of the cheek or zygomatic bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the zygomatic region for any signs of fracture.
Bruising over the zygomatic bone can indicate facial trauma.
The artist emphasized the prominent zygomatic arches in her sculpted portrait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malar - relating to the cheek or cheekbone
  • Degree Lower: temporal - relating to the temples or sides of the skull

Etymology

From Greek zygōmat- (yoke) via Latin zygomaticus, referring to the yoke-shaped bone of the cheek.
