Saturn

1. PROPN

/ ˈsætərn / (AmE), / ˈsætən / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of agriculture and vegetation, equivalent to the Greek Cronus.
Roman god of agriculture

Examples

The festival of Saturnalia was held in honor of Saturn each winter.
In Roman mythology, Saturn was overthrown by his son Jupiter.
Many ancient temples in Rome were dedicated to Saturn.

Synset Examples

Saturday is Saturn's Day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cronus - Greek god of time and harvest; equivalent to Saturn

Etymology

From Latin Saturnus, name of the Roman god.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsæ.tɚn / (AmE), / ˈsæ.tən / (BrE)
the sixth planet from the Sun in the solar system, known for its prominent rings made of ice particles
the sixth planet from the Sun, with rings

Examples

The rings of Saturn can be seen with a small telescope.
Scientists have discovered many moons orbiting Saturn.
In Roman mythology, Saturn was the god of time and agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the solar system
  • Synonym: Uranus - the seventh planet from the Sun, an ice giant

Etymology

From Latin 'Saturnus', the Roman god of agriculture; name adopted for the planet in antiquity.
