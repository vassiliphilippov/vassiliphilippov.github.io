Leucocytozoon

1. NOUN

/ luːkoʊˌsaɪtəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / luːkəˌsɪtəzəʊˈɒn / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic protozoa that use blackflies as their definitive hosts and birds as intermediate hosts, often causing disease in birds
genus of protozoan parasites in birds
medical

Examples

Scientists found Leucocytozoon parasites in several species of migratory birds.
The presence of Leucocytozoon can cause anemia and other health issues in infected birds.
Veterinarians studied how Leucocytozoon infections spread through blackfly populations.

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white), 'kytos' (cell), and 'zoon' (animal).

2. NOUN

/ luːˌkoʊsaɪtəˈzoʊ.ɒn / (AmE), / luːˌkəʊsaɪtəˈzəʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic protozoa that infect birds, transmitted mainly by blackflies.
genus of bird-infecting protozoa
medical

Examples

Several bird species were found to be infected with Leucocytozoon during the field study.
Blackflies are responsible for transmitting Leucocytozoon parasites among wild waterfowl.
Veterinarians identified Leucocytozoon organisms in the blood samples taken from sick ducks.

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white), kytos (cell), and zoon (animal).
