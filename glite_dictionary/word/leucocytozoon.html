leucocytozoon

1. NOUN

/ luˌkoʊsaɪtəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / luːˌkəʊsaɪtəˈzəʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic protozoa that infects the blood of birds, often causing disease.
genus of parasitic protozoa in birds
medical

Examples

Leucocytozoon infections can cause anemia and weakness in wild birds.
The veterinarian identified leucocytozoon in the blood sample of the sick duck.
Researchers study leucocytozoon to understand avian diseases better.

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white), 'kytos' (cell), and 'zoon' (animal).
