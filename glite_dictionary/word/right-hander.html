right-hander

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtˌhændər / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhændə / (BrE)
A person who uses their right hand more skillfully or frequently than their left.
person who primarily uses right hand

Examples

Despite being a right-hander, she learned to write with her left hand after the injury.
Most right-handers find it easier to use scissors designed for their dominant hand.
The teacher noticed that nearly all the students in the class were right-handers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-hander - person who uses their left hand more skillfully

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪtˈhændər / (AmE), / ˌraɪtˈhændə / (BrE)
A person who uses their right hand more skillfully or predominantly than their left.
person who mainly uses right hand

Examples

Most people are right-handers and feel more comfortable writing with their right hand.
As a natural right-hander, she struggled to use the scissors in her left hand.
The coach asked the right-hander to demonstrate the proper grip for throwing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-hander - a person who uses their left hand more skillfully than the right

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌhændər / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌhændə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitcher who throws with the right hand.
baseball pitcher who throws with right hand

Examples

The team decided to start their best right-hander in the first game of the series.
Last year, the rookie right-hander quickly became one of the league’s top pitchers.
Switch-hitters can adjust their stance when facing a right-hander on the mound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-hander - (in baseball) a pitcher who throws with the left hand
