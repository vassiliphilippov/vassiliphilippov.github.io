sou'-sou'-east

1. NOUN

/ saʊ su saʊ iːst / (AmE), / saʊ suː saʊ iːst / (BrE)
an old-fashioned term for the compass point midway between south and southeast (i.e., south-southeast)
old form of south-southeast (compass point)
archaic

Examples

The wind shifted to sou'-sou'-east just before dawn.
Sailors used terms like sou'-sou'-east in navigation charts of previous centuries.
In his journal, he recorded the ship's heading as sou'-sou'-east all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: south-southeast - the compass point midway between south and southeast

Etymology

Formed by shortening 'south-south-east'; 'sou'' is an archaic or dialect contraction for 'south'.
