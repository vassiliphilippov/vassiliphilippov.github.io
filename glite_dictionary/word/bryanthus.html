Bryanthus

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈænθəs / (AmE), / braɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing, mat-forming evergreen shrubs with racemes of pinkish-white flowers, native to the Old World.
genus of mat-forming evergreen shrubs

Examples

The alpine garden displayed a rare Bryanthus in full bloom near the rocks.
Botanists are studying Bryanthus for its adaptation to cold environments.
During the spring, the Bryanthus covers the ground with small, pinkish-white flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *bryon* (moss) + *anthos* (flower), referring to the plant's moss-like appearance and its flowers.

2. NOUN

/ braɪˈænθəs / (AmE), / braɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen shrubs in the heather family, closely related to and formerly included in the genus Phyllodoce.
a genus of evergreen shrubs (botany)

Examples

The Bryanthus genus is native to cold mountain regions of eastern Asia.
Botanists debated whether certain species belong to Bryanthus or Phyllodoce.
The delicate flowers of Bryanthus are popular among alpine garden enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phyllodoce - a related genus of shrubs in the same family as Bryanthus

Etymology

From Greek 'bryon' (moss) + 'anthos' (flower), referring to the moss-like habit and flowering nature.
