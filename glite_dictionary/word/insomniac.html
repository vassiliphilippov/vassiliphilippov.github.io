insomniac

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɑːmniˌæk / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒmniˌæk / (BrE)
a person who finds it difficult or impossible to sleep, especially as a result of insomnia
person unable to sleep

Examples

As an insomniac, he often reads late into the night.
Many insomniacs struggle to function during the day due to lack of sleep.
She joined a support group for insomniac adults looking for advice and community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insomnia - inability to sleep

Etymology

From Latin 'insomnia' (sleeplessness) and '-ac' (forming nouns and adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɑmniˌæk / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒmniˌæk / (BrE)
Experiencing, suffering from, or relating to sleeplessness; unable to sleep.
Unable to sleep; suffering from sleeplessness

Examples

After several insomniac nights, she finally visited her doctor for advice.
The insomniac hours before dawn felt endless to him.
He read a book to pass the time during his insomniac night.

Synset Examples

insomniac old people
insomniac nights
lay sleepless all night
twenty watchful, weary, tedious nights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepless - Unable to sleep; experiencing lack of sleep

Etymology

Formed from insomnia + -ac (patterned after words like maniac), early 20th century.
