Agrimonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrɪˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌaɡrɪˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs found mainly in northern temperate regions, characterized by pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and bristly fruit
genus of northern temperate herbs

Examples

The medicinal properties of Agrimonia have been recognized since ancient times.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Agrimonia in northern Europe.
The leaves of Agrimonia are often used in traditional remedies.

Etymology

From Latin *Agrimonia*, the name of the plant in classical Latin.
