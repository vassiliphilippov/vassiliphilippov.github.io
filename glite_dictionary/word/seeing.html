seeing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of perceiving with the eyes; visual perception.
visual perception; act of seeing

Examples

Seeing is essential for most people to navigate the world around them.
The seeing of the distant mountains filled her with awe.
Good seeing is important for astronomers observing the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vision - the faculty or state of being able to see; sight
  • Antonym: blindness - the condition of lacking sight

Etymology

From the verb 'see', Old English 'seon' (to see).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or condition of using the faculty of vision normally; the ability to see.
the act or power of seeing

Examples

Good seeing is important for driving safely at night.
After the surgery, her seeing improved dramatically.
He is grateful for having perfect seeing throughout his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sight - the faculty or power of seeing
  • Antonym: blindness - the state of being unable to see

3. ADJ

/ ˈsiɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
Having the ability to see; not blind.
able to see, not blind

Examples

She is one of the few seeing members in her family, as most relatives are blind.
The museum offers special tours for both seeing and visually impaired visitors.
Dogs are often trained to assist seeing people as well as those who are blind.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blind - unable to see
