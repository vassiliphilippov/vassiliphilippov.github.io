to delete

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlit / (AmE), / dɪˈliːt / (BrE)
to remove something, especially written or stored information, so that it no longer exists or is visible
remove or erase information

Examples

Please delete my name from your list if I no longer qualify.
He accidentally deleted an important file from his computer.
You can delete old messages to free up space on your phone.

Synset Examples

Please delete my name from your list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written or recorded information completely
  • Antonym: insert - to put something into something else, especially text or data

Etymology

From Latin deletus, past participle of delere (to wipe out, destroy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01551969-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dəˈlit / (AmE), / dɪˈliːt / (BrE)
to remove or erase digital or electronic information, such as files or data, from a storage device or system
remove digital information

Examples

I accidentally deleted all my photos from the computer yesterday.
Please make sure to delete any sensitive information before sending the file.
After you delete a message, it cannot be recovered from your inbox.

Synset Examples

Who erased the files from my hard disk?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove recorded or written information

Etymology

From Latin deletus, past participle of delere (to wipe out, destroy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01001860-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dəˈlit / (AmE), / dɪˈliːt / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate something, especially written, printed, or digital material, by cutting it out
remove or eliminate something

Examples

She accidentally deleted an important file from her computer.
He asked the editor to delete the final paragraph from the article.
You can delete individual items from your shopping cart by clicking the 'remove' button.

Synset Examples

she edited the juiciest scenes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove something completely, especially information or marks, from a surface or record
  • Antonym: insert - to put something into something else, especially written or digital text

Etymology

From Latin 'delēre' (to destroy, to wipe out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00201227-v
Synset POS: v
