maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.pəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.pəl / (BrE)
a tree or shrub of the genus Acer, native to the north temperate regions, known for its lobed leaves and winged seeds, and often valued for its wood and sap
a deciduous tree of the genus Acer

Examples

The leaves of the maple turn brilliant red and orange in autumn.
Canadian flags often feature the distinctive maple leaf as a national symbol.
A large maple tree provided welcome shade for the children playing below.

Etymology

From Old English 'mapul', influenced by related Germanic forms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.pəl / (BrE)
The hard, close-grained wood of the maple tree, especially the sugar maple, used particularly for furniture and flooring.
wood from the maple tree

Examples

This table is made from solid maple and has a beautiful grain.
The chef preferred a maple cutting board for its durability.
They installed maple flooring throughout the living room for a warm look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - hardwood from the oak tree, often used in furniture
  • Antonym: pine - softwood from the pine tree

Etymology

From Old English mapul, related to Old Norse mǫpurr (maple tree).
