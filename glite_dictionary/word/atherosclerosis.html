atherosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθɚoʊskləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæθərəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease in which fatty deposits build up on the inner walls of arteries, causing them to narrow and restrict blood flow.
disease involving fatty deposits in arteries
medical

Examples

Doctors often warn that atherosclerosis increases the risk of heart attacks and strokes.
A diet high in saturated fat can contribute to the development of atherosclerosis.
Regular exercise and a healthy lifestyle help prevent atherosclerosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - general term for hardening and thickening of the arteries

Etymology

From Greek 'athērē' (gruel, porridge) and 'sklērōsis' (hardening).
