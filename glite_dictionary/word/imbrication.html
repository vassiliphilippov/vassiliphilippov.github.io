imbrication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.brɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.brɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement of overlapping elements, such as tiles or shingles, so that each one covers part of the one beneath it
overlapping arrangement of elements

Examples

The imbrication of the roof tiles helped prevent rain from seeping into the house.
In art, the imbrication of scales gives the illusion of texture on the painted fish.
Biologists noted the imbrication of feathers on the bird's wing, making it more aerodynamic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlap - extend over and cover a part of something else

Etymology

From Latin imbricatio, from imbricare (to cover with tiles), from imbric-, imbrex (a roof tile).
