to instigate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnstɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnstɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to cause an event or situation to happen, often by making someone do something or encouraging an action, especially something bad or undesirable
cause or provoke (often something bad)

Examples

The journalist's article helped instigate a wave of protests across the city.
Rumors can easily instigate unnecessary fear among the public.
The manager tried not to instigate conflict between team members.

Synset Examples

She prompted me to call my relatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a reaction or emotion, often anger
  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up violent or unlawful behavior
  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin instigare (to urge on, incite).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪn.stɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to happen, especially something bad; to provoke or stir up (an action or event, often negative)
provoke or cause (especially something bad)

Examples

The politician's speech helped to instigate widespread protests throughout the city.
Rumors began to instigate panic among the villagers after the storm warnings.
Some believe that social media can instigate negative behaviors among teenagers.

Synset Examples

incite a riot
set off great unrest among the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior)
  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or give rise to (a reaction or emotion)

Etymology

From Latin instigāre (to incite, stimulate).

Synset POS: v
