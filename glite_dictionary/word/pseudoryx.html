Pseudoryx

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈɾaɪɪks / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of large, cow-like mammals native to Vietnam and Laos, first discovered by scientists in 1992; commonly known as the saola.
genus of large cow-like mammals (saola)

Examples

The discovery of the Pseudoryx in the forests of Vietnam surprised zoologists worldwide.
Researchers are working to prevent the extinction of the rare Pseudoryx.
A new population of Pseudoryx was spotted in Laos after decades of elusive sightings.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false) + 'oryx' (antelope, gazelle), indicating it is not a true oryx.

2. NOUN

/ suːˈdɔːrɪks / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɒrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of large, cow-like mammals, native to Vietnam and Laos, first identified by scientists in 1992, including the saola.
genus of large mammals from Vietnam

Examples

Scientists were excited when a new species in the genus Pseudoryx was discovered in Southeast Asia.
The saola, belonging to the Pseudoryx genus, is one of the rarest large mammals in the world.
Conservation efforts for Pseudoryx are critical due to the species' endangered status.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + 'oryx' (antelope, gazelle), referencing its superficial resemblance to oryx antelopes.
