berkelium

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈkiliəm / (AmE), / bɜːˈkiːliəm / (BrE)
a radioactive chemical element (symbol Bk) of the actinide series, produced by bombarding americium with helium ions.
a radioactive chemical element (Bk)

Examples

Researchers produced berkelium by bombarding americium with alpha particles in the lab.
Because berkelium is highly radioactive, it must be handled with extreme care.
Only minute amounts of berkelium have ever been made due to its rarity and complexity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actinium - a radioactive element of the actinide series

Etymology

Named after Berkeley, California, where it was first synthesized.
