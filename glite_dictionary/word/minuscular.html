minuscular

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈnʌskjələr / (AmE), / mɪˈnʌskjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or written in a small, cursive script developed from uncial, used chiefly from the 7th to 9th centuries.
relating to early medieval small script

Examples

Scholars deciphered the ancient manuscript, which was written in a distinctive minuscular hand.
The evolution of minuscular scripts marked a significant development in early medieval European writing.
The minuscular style made texts easier to copy and read than their earlier, uncial counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minuscule - a small, lowercase letter or script form of medieval writing

Etymology

From Latin 'minusculus' (rather small), a diminutive of 'minor' (less).
