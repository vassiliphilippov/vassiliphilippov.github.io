subsiding

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / səbˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of gradually sinking or settling to a lower level, especially of the ground or a surface
gradual sinking or settling

Examples

The subsiding of the old building's foundations led to visible cracks in the walls.
Geologists monitored the subsiding of the riverbank after the heavy rains.
After the earthquake, the subsiding of the land posed a risk to nearby homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidence - the process of sinking or settling of the ground's surface

Etymology

From the verb 'subside', which originates from Latin 'subsīdere' (to settle, sink down).
