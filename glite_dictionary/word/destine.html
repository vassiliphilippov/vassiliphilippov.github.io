to destine

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛstɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪn / (BrE)
to determine or intend something for a particular purpose, fate, or future; to preordain
to determine or preordain for a purpose

Examples

His parents seemed to destine him for a career in medicine.
This land was destined to become a national park one day.
The company destined the new building for research and development purposes.

Synset Examples

She was intended to become the director

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intend - to have something in mind as a plan or purpose

Etymology

From Latin destinare (to fasten down, determine, appoint).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛstɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪn / (BrE)
to decide or determine beforehand, especially by fate, divine will, or external circumstances; to designate for a particular purpose or fate
to determine or designate beforehand

Examples

Many believed she was destined for greatness from an early age.
The treaty destined the island to remain neutral in future conflicts.
His talent seemed to destine him for a career on the stage.

Synset Examples

She was destined to become a great pianist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doom - to condemn to a terrible fate or outcome
  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign to a position or role

Etymology

From Latin destinare (to make firm, establish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
