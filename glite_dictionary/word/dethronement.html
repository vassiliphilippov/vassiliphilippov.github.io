dethronement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈθroʊnmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈθrəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of removing a person, especially a ruler or leader, from power or office
removal from power or office

Examples

The sudden dethronement of the king shocked the entire nation.
Following his dethronement, the dictator spent years in exile.
The dethronement of the CEO led to major changes within the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposition - the act of removing someone, especially a monarch, from office
  • Antonym: enthronement - the act of installing someone on a throne or in power

Etymology

From English 'dethrone' + '-ment'. 'Dethrone' from Old French 'destroner', from 'des-' (de-) + 'trone' (throne).
