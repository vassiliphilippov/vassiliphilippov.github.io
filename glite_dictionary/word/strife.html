strife

1. NOUN

/ straɪf / (AmE), / straɪf / (BrE)
angry or violent disagreement; conflict or struggle between opposing groups or individuals
bitter conflict or struggle

Examples

The political strife in the country led to years of unrest.
Decades of religious strife have left deep scars in the region.
Sibling strife often disrupts the harmony of a household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • Antonym: peace - a state or period in which there is no war or conflict

Etymology

From Old French 'estrif', probably of Frankish origin.

2. NOUN

/ straɪf / (AmE), / straɪf / (BrE)
angry or violent disagreement or conflict between people or groups
conflict or disagreement

Examples

The country has been torn apart by political strife for decades.
Their marriage suffered from constant strife over finances and priorities.
Religious strife in the region led to thousands fleeing their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument
  • Synonym: discord - lack of harmony or agreement
  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord

Etymology

From Old French 'estrif' (quarrel, dispute).
