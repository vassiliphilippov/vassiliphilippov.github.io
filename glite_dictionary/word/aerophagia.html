aerophagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈfeɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌeərəˈfeɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person repeatedly swallows air, leading to belching, discomfort, and sometimes flatulence.
swallowing air (medical condition)
medical

Examples

After eating too quickly, she experienced aerophagia and felt bloated for hours.
Some patients with anxiety may develop aerophagia as a nervous habit.
Treatment for aerophagia often focuses on changes in eating and breathing patterns.

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'phagein' (to eat).
