disparager

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspærədʒər / (AmE), / dɪˈspærɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person who criticizes or belittles the worth or value of someone or something
someone who belittles or criticizes

Examples

The disparager at the meeting found fault with every proposal presented.
Despite being a talented artist, she was deeply affected by the words of a persistent disparager.
Ignoring his inner disparager, he continued to pursue his ambitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds fault
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who approves of and encourages something

Etymology

From the verb 'disparage,' which comes from Old French 'desparagier' (to degrade), from 'des-' (apart) + 'parage' (rank, lineage).
