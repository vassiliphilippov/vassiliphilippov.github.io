cypress

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪprəs / (BrE)
An evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Cupressus, native to temperate regions, with dark, scale-like leaves and rounded cones.
an evergreen coniferous tree (Cupressus)

Examples

The garden was lined with tall cypress trees that provided both shade and privacy.
A solitary cypress stood at the edge of the cemetery, its dark leaves contrasting with the bright sky.
Mediterranean landscapes are often dotted with slender cypress rising above olive groves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - an evergreen coniferous shrub or tree, genus Juniperus

Etymology

From Old French cipres, from Latin cyparissus, from Greek kuparissos.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
The wood of any of several trees of the genus Cupressus or related species, valued for its durability and fragrance.
wood from cypress trees

Examples

The furniture was crafted from cypress, giving the room a pleasant, earthy scent.
They chose cypress for the patio, as its wood resists moisture and decay.
The old barn was rebuilt using reclaimed cypress beams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - fragrant, durable wood from cedar trees

Etymology

From Old French cipres, from Latin cupressus, from Greek kuparissos (cypress tree).
