Trafalgar

1. PROPN

/ trəˈfælɡər / (AmE), / trəˈfælɡə / (BrE)
the site of the decisive 1805 naval battle in which the British fleet defeated the combined French and Spanish fleets off the southwest coast of Spain
site of 1805 naval battle won by Britain

Examples

The victory at Trafalgar secured British naval supremacy for over a century.
Admiral Nelson became a national hero after his triumph at Trafalgar.
Many ships in the British Navy proudly bear the names of those who fought at Trafalgar.

Etymology

From Cape Trafalgar, southwest Spain; the site of the battle.
