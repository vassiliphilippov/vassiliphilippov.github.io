whirlwind

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝlˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlˌwɪnd / (BrE)
A column of air moving rapidly around and over itself in a vertical movement over the Earth's surface, often causing swirling debris.
a fast, spinning column of air

Examples

A sudden whirlwind swept leaves and dust high into the air.
The farm was damaged when a powerful whirlwind passed through the area.
As we hiked, we saw a small whirlwind dancing across the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tornado - a violent rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground
  • Synonym: cyclone - a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Old English 'hwirflind', from 'hwirfian' (to turn) + 'wind'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝlˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlˌwɪnd / (BrE)
A situation or period of intense activity, rapid change, or confusion.
period of intense activity or rapid change

Examples

The company's growth has been a whirlwind over the past year.
After the announcement, her life became a whirlwind of interviews and events.
Their romance was a whirlwind, moving from first date to marriage in a few months.

Synset Examples

The company's growth has been a whirlwind over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - a violent disturbance such as a tempest; used metaphorically for chaos
  • Antonym: calm - a period or state of tranquility, opposite of chaos or rapid change

Etymology

From Old English hwirflind, from 'hwirf' (whirl) + 'wind'. Originally referred to the meteorological phenomenon.
