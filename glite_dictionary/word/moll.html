moll

1. NOUN

/ mɑl / (AmE), / mɒl / (BrE)
the female companion or girlfriend of a gangster; a gangster's woman
gangster's girlfriend
slang

Examples

The notorious gangster was always seen with his moll at his side.
In classic crime films, the moll often played a crucial role in the heist.
Detectives interviewed the moll to gather information about the suspect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun moll - female companion of a gangster, sometimes involved in crime

Etymology

From early 20th-century American slang; derived from the given name Molly, a diminutive of Mary, often used generically for women.

2. NOUN

/ mɑl / (AmE), / mɒl / (BrE)
the female companion or associate of a gangster or criminal
female companion of a gangster

Examples

In old movies, the moll often stood by the gangster's side despite the danger.
She quickly grew tired of her life as a moll and left the city behind.
The police were suspicious of the glamorous moll who accompanied the notorious crime boss.

Synset Examples

She was known as the moll of the local gang.

Etymology

From British slang, derived from the given name Molly, a pet form of Mary; later applied to women associated with criminals.
