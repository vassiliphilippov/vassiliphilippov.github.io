Brindisi

1. PROPN

/ brɪnˈdiːzi / (AmE), / brɪnˈdiːzi / (BrE)
a port city in southeastern Apulia, Italy, known as a center for the Crusades in the Middle Ages
port city in southeastern Italy

Examples

The ferry from Greece docked at Brindisi early in the morning.
During the Crusades, Brindisi was a crucial embarkation point for knights and pilgrims.
Many tourists visit Brindisi to explore its Roman and medieval landmarks.
