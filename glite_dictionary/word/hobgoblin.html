hobgoblin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑbˌɡɑblɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒbˌɡɒblɪn / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, grotesque, and mischievous supernatural creature believed to cause trouble for humans.
mischievous supernatural creature in folklore

Examples

Children believed that a hobgoblin lived in the woods behind the old house.
In the story, a mischievous hobgoblin played tricks on the villagers every night.
Many superstitions warn travelers to beware of the forest hobgoblin after dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goblin - a mischievous, grotesque sprite or elf in folklore

Etymology

From Middle English hobgobelin, influenced by 'Hob' (a common nickname for 'Robert') and 'goblin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑbˌɡɑblɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒbˌɡɒblɪn / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes fear or worry, especially in a fanciful or exaggerated way.
object or source of dread or apprehension

Examples

For many politicians, inflation has become a hobgoblin used to justify drastic economic policies.
The threat of automation is often presented as a hobgoblin for workers in traditional industries.
In her mind, failure was the constant hobgoblin she struggled to silence.

Synset Examples

Germany was always a bugbear for France
A foolish consistency is the hobgoblin of little minds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbear - something that causes persistent fear, anxiety, or irritation

Etymology

From hob (a rustic, clown) + goblin (a mischievous sprite); first recorded in late Middle English.
