heavier-than-air

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛviər ðæn ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌhɛviə ðæn ˈɛə / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) having a weight greater than the air it displaces, requiring aerodynamic lift for flight.
(of aircraft) requiring lift to fly

Examples

The first successful heavier-than-air flight was achieved by the Wright brothers in 1903.
Unlike balloons, heavier-than-air craft generate lift using wings or rotors.
Most commercial aircraft today are classified as heavier-than-air machines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lighter-than-air - (of aircraft) having a weight equal to or less than the air it displaces, such as balloons or airships
