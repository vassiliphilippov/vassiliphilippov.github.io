Zoroastrian

1. NOUN

/ ˌzɔːroʊˈæstriən / (AmE), / ˌzɒrəʊˈæstriən / (BrE)
a follower of Zoroaster and the religion of Zoroastrianism, an ancient Persian faith
follower of Zoroastrianism

Examples

Many Zoroastrians celebrate Nowruz as their new year festival.
The Zoroastrian temple was built centuries ago.
He grew up in a family of devout Zoroastrians in Iran.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parsee - a member of the Zoroastrian community in India

Etymology

From Zoroaster, the founder of the religion, plus -ian (denoting a follower or supporter).

2. ADJ

/ ˌzɔroʊˈæstriən / (AmE), / ˌzɒrəʊˈæstriən / (BrE)
relating to Zoroaster or the religion of Zoroastrianism, which he founded
of or relating to Zoroastrianism

Examples

Many ancient texts reveal the influence of Zoroastrian beliefs on other world religions.
The city is home to a small but active Zoroastrian community.
His research focuses on Zoroastrian rituals and their historical development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoroastrianism - the ancient monotheistic religion founded by Zoroaster

Etymology

From Zoroaster (the founder of Zoroastrianism) + -ian (forming adjectives meaning 'relating to')

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
