doubtfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtfəlnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being uncertain or lacking conviction about the truth, reality, or existence of something.
state of being doubtful or uncertain

Examples

There was considerable doubtfulness in her voice when she answered the question.
His report was met with doubtfulness by the investigation committee.
The doubtfulness surrounding the result led to a call for a recount.

Synset Examples

the dubiousness of his claim
there is no question about the validity of the enterprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not knowing
  • Antonym: confidence - the state of being certain or trusting

Etymology

From 'doubtful' + '-ness', with 'doubtful' from Old French 'douteux' and Latin 'dubiosus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtfəlnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being uncertain or not fully convinced about something
state of being unsure or uncertain

Examples

There was a clear doubtfulness in her voice when she answered the question.
His doubtfulness about the plan made others hesitate to proceed.
Despite initial doubtfulness, the team managed to complete the project successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - lack of certainty or sureness
  • Antonym: confidence - the state of being certain or self-assured

Etymology

From 'doubtful' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating state or quality.
