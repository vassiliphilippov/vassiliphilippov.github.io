irridentism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛdəntɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈred.ən.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The political principle or policy that a country should reclaim and control territories inhabited by people who share its ethnic or historical ties.
belief in reclaiming related territories

Examples

The rise of irridentism caused tensions between neighboring countries.
Many conflicts in the region stem from irridentism and disputed borders.
She studied the impact of irridentism on modern European history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalism - advocacy of political independence for a country
  • Synonym: separatism - advocacy of a state of separation for a group or region

Etymology

From Italian *irredentismo*, from *Italia irredenta* (unredeemed Italy), a 19th-century nationalist movement.
