fairground

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈfeəɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an open area where fairs, exhibitions, or circuses are held, often equipped with temporary stalls, amusements, and rides
open area for fairs or exhibitions

Examples

We spent the afternoon at the fairground enjoying the rides and games.
Vendors set up food stalls all along the fairground during the summer festival.
The circus came to town and performed on the fairground for a week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnival - a traveling amusement show or fair
  • Synonym: amusement park - a large outdoor area with rides and entertainment

Etymology

From fair + ground, referring to the land used for fairs.
