tremolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛməˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛm.ə.laɪt / (BrE)
a white or pale green mineral of the amphibole group, consisting mainly of calcium and magnesium silicate, sometimes used as a form of asbestos
a white or pale green amphibole mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the white crystals as tremolite during the rock analysis.
Exposure to tremolite fibers can pose health risks similar to other forms of asbestos.
Some deposits of tremolite have been found in old serpentinite quarries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asbestos - a group of minerals used commercially for their heat resistance
  • Degree Higher: amphibole - a group of inosilicate minerals including tremolite

Etymology

From Tremola Valley in Switzerland, where the mineral was first identified.
