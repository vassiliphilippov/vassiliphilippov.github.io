unconstricted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
not constricted or narrowed; free from tightness or restriction, either physically or psychologically
not constricted; free from tightness

Examples

The doctor explained that unconstricted blood vessels are essential for healthy circulation.
She spoke with an unconstricted confidence that put everyone at ease.
Proper breathing requires keeping the airway unconstricted during physical activity.

Synset Examples

healthy unconstricted arteries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constricted - narrowed or made smaller; restricted

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'constricted' (from Latin 'constringere', to draw together).
