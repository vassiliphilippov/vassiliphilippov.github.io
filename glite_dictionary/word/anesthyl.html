anesthyl

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛsθɪl / (AmE), / əˈnɛsθɪl / (BrE)
A mixture of methyl and ethyl chloride, used as a local anesthetic and typically sprayed onto the skin.
local anesthetic mixture (methyl and ethyl chloride)
medical

Examples

The doctor sprayed anesthyl on the patient's skin before performing the minor procedure.
Using anesthyl provided effective pain relief for the localized area.
Nurses applied anesthyl to numb the wound site before cleaning it.
