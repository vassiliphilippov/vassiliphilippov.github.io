Augean

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈdʒiən / (AmE), / ɔːˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
extremely filthy or difficult to clean because of long neglect; relating to an enormous task of cleaning up corruption or accumulated filth.
extremely filthy from long neglect

Examples

The investigators faced an Augean task sorting through years of neglected records.
The landlord finally tackled the Augean conditions in the abandoned apartment building.
Cleaning the old warehouse was an Augean effort that required dozens of workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthy - very dirty
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt

Etymology

From Augeas, a king in Greek mythology whose stables had not been cleaned for thirty years.
