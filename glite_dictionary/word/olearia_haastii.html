Olearia haastii

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈlɪəriə hɑːˈstiː / (AmE), / əʊˈlɪəriə hɑːˈstiː / (BrE)
A bushy shrub native to New Zealand, cultivated for its fragrant white flower heads.
bushy New Zealand shrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden features several Olearia haastii plants blooming in late spring.
Botanists admired the dense foliage and flowers of Olearia haastii during their field study.
Many gardeners prefer Olearia haastii for its hardiness and attractive scent.

Etymology

Named after the genus Olearia and the explorer Julius von Haast, commemorated in the species epithet.
