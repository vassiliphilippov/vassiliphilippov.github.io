centerfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.təˌfiːld / (BrE)
the area of the baseball outfield directly behind second base and in front of the catcher
area of a baseball outfield

Examples

The batter hit the ball deep into centerfield, giving the runners time to advance.
He played centerfield for his college baseball team.
A spectacular catch was made in centerfield during the championship game.

Synset Examples

he hit the ball to deep center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center fielder - the player who defends centerfield
  • Degree Lower: outfield - the outer part of a baseball field

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚˌfild / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌfiːld / (BrE)
the position in the outfield of a baseball field that is roughly in the center, or the player who defends this area
central outfield position in baseball

Examples

He played a brilliant game in centerfield, making several difficult catches.
The coach assigned her to centerfield because of her speed and strong arm.
A long fly ball sailed over centerfield and landed just short of the fence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: outfield - the part of a baseball field beyond the infield
  • Synonym: center fielder - the player who occupies centerfield
