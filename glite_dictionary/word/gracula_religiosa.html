Gracula religiosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈkjuːlə rɪˈlɪdʒioʊsə / (AmE), / ɡrəˈkjuːlə rɪˈlɪdʒɪəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of large, glossy black starling native to South and Southeast Asia, commonly known as the hill myna, noted for its ability to mimic human speech.
a starling species, the hill myna, known for mimicry

Examples

The Gracula religiosa is prized by bird enthusiasts for its exceptional ability to imitate sounds and speech.
Many people keep Gracula religiosa as pets because of their sociable nature and mimicking talent.
In the forests of Southeast Asia, the calls of Gracula religiosa can often be heard echoing through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hill myna - a common name for Gracula religiosa, the mimicking starling

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gracula' (a genus of Asian starlings), 'religiosa' (religious).
