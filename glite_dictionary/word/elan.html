elan

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈlɑn / (AmE), / eɪˈlɑːn / (BrE)
enthusiastic and confident energy or style; spirited self-assurance and liveliness
spirited self-assurance; lively energy

Examples

The orchestra played the lively piece with admirable elan, filling the hall with excitement.
She completed the project with such elan that her colleagues were truly impressed.
His speech was delivered with elan and captivated everyone in the audience.

Synset Examples

a performance of great elan and sophistication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - physical or mental energy and strength
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From French 'élan' (ardor, dash, impulse), ultimately from Old French 'eslan' (rush, dash).

2. NOUN

/ eɪˈlɑn / (AmE), / eɪˈlɑ̃ː / (BrE)
enthusiastic and energetic style or eagerness, especially suggesting flair or spirited self-confidence
enthusiastic style or eagerness

Examples

She performed her duties with remarkable elan, impressing everyone in the office.
The artist's work was characterized by a unique elan that set him apart from his contemporaries.
The team approached the final challenge with elan, determined to give it their all.

Synset Examples

they were imbued with a revolutionary ardor
he felt a kind of religious zeal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeal - great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause
  • Synonym: ardor - intense enthusiasm or passion

Etymology

From French élan (impetus, ardor), from élancer (to dart forth).

3. NOUN

/ eɪˈlɑn / (AmE), / eɪˈlɑ̃ː / (BrE)
energy, style, and enthusiastic self-assurance; distinctive and stylish elegance.
stylish elegance; spirited confidence

Examples

She spoke with such elan that the entire audience was captivated.
The chef added a touch of elan to every dish he created.
Despite the challenges, she tackled her duties with remarkable elan.

Synset Examples

he wooed her with the confident dash of a cavalry officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flair - natural ability or stylishness
  • Synonym: panache - distinctive and stylish manner or flair

Etymology

From French élan (dash, ardor, enthusiasm), from élancer (to throw, dash).
