colourlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚlɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə.lɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being without color; lacking chromatic color
lack of color
british

Examples

The colourlessness of the walls made the room feel cold and sterile.
The artist deliberately used colourlessness to evoke a sense of emptiness in his paintings.
The fog had a peculiar colourlessness, making it hard to distinguish any objects through it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorlessness - the quality or state of being without color; mainly American spelling
