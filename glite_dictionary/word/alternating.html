alternating

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an electric current) repeatedly reversing direction at regular intervals.
reversing direction (of electric current)

Examples

The device is designed to operate with an alternating current supply.
Power lines typically carry alternating current over long distances.
An inverter can convert direct current into alternating current for household use.

Synset Examples

alternating current

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - (of electric current) flowing in one direction only
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of things or actions) occurring by turns; happening first one, then the other, in succession.
occurring by turns; in succession

Examples

The music was played at alternating intervals of loud and soft passages.
She had alternating shifts at work: one week in the day, the next at night.
Rain and sunshine came in alternating periods throughout the afternoon.

Synset Examples

alternating feelings of love and hate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to occur or cause to occur by turns

Etymology

From Latin alternare (to do by turns), via the verb alternate.
