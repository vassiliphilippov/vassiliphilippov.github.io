tidal

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdəl / (BrE)
Relating to, affected by, or caused by the rise and fall of the sea tides.
of or caused by tides

Examples

The tidal currents in this area are particularly strong at full moon.
A tidal power plant generates electricity using the movement of the sea.
Some coastal areas are subject to regular tidal flooding during stormy weather.

Synset Examples

tidal wave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tideless - not affected by tides

Etymology

From tide + -al, formed from Old English 'tīd' (time, tide) and the suffix '-al'.
