patchily

1. ADV

/ ˈpætʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈpatʃɪli / (BrE)
In an irregular or uneven manner; in spots or patches rather than uniformly.
in an uneven or irregular way

Examples

The grass grew patchily on the lawn, with bare spots scattered throughout.
Paint covered the old wall patchily, leaving some bricks exposed.
Attendance at the event was patchily distributed, with clusters of people in some sections and empty seats in others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniformly - in a consistent or even manner

Etymology

From 'patchy' + '-ly', where 'patchy' comes from 'patch' (a small area differing from its surroundings).
