twelfth

1. NOUN

/ twɛlfθ / (AmE), / twɛlfθ / (BrE)
the position in a series that comes after the eleventh and before the thirteenth; the ordinal number for twelve
ordinal number for twelve

Examples

Her apartment is on the twelfth floor of the building.
The twelfth in line received a special prize.
He finished in twelfth place in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eleventh - the position 11 in a series; ordinal for eleven
  • Degree Higher: thirteenth - the position 13 in a series; ordinal for thirteen

Etymology

From Middle English twelfthe, from Old English twelfta, meaning 'twelfth'.

2. NOUN

/ twɛlfθ / (AmE), / twɛlfθ / (BrE)
Each of the twelve equal parts into which something can be divided.
one of twelve equal parts

Examples

Each student received a twelfth of the total prize money.
A twelfth of the cake was left after the party.
He owns a twelfth share in the company.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-fourth - one of twenty-four equal parts
  • Degree Higher: sixth - one of six equal parts

Etymology

From Old English 'twelfta', related to 'twelve'.

3. ADJ

/ twɛlfθ / (AmE), / twɛlfθ / (BrE)
coming after the eleventh and before the thirteenth in a sequence; 12th
ordinal number: 12th

Examples

She finished in twelfth place at the competition.
Today is the twelfth of January; don't forget the appointment.
The twelfth page of the book contains the table of contents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eleventh - coming after the tenth and before the twelfth; 11th
  • Degree Higher: thirteenth - coming after the twelfth and before the fourteenth; 13th

Etymology

From Old English 'twelfta', from 'twelf' (twelve).
