whitlavia

1. NOUN

/ wɪtˈleɪviə / (AmE), / wɪtˈleɪvɪə / (BrE)
A desert plant native to southern California, noted for its blue or violet tubular flowers arranged in terminal racemes.
desert plant with blue or violet tubular flowers

We spotted a beautiful whitlavia blooming along the dry hillside trail.
The whitlavia thrives in arid regions with well-drained soil.
