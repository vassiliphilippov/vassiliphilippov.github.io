asbestosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæzˌbɛsˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæsbɛsˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic lung disease caused by inhaling asbestos fibers, leading to inflammation and scarring of the lung tissue.
lung disease from asbestos exposure
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that his shortness of breath was due to asbestosis caused by years of exposure at work.
Many former shipyard workers developed asbestosis after inhaling asbestos fibers for decades.
Doctors warn that the symptoms of asbestosis can take years to appear after initial exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicosis - a lung disease caused by inhaling silica dust

Etymology

From 'asbestos' + '-osis' (disease condition).
