purpura

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpjərə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpjʊərə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by purple spots on the skin or mucous membranes caused by subcutaneous bleeding as a result of various blood diseases.
subcutaneous bleeding causing purple skin spots
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with purpura after developing multiple purple spots on her arms.
Certain medications may trigger purpura in susceptible individuals.
Doctors examined the child for underlying causes of purpura following unexplained bruising.

Etymology

From Latin 'purpura' (purple), referring to the purple coloration of the spots.
