Harold Harefoot

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrəld ˈhɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈharəld ˈhɛəfʊt / (BrE)
King of England from 1035 to 1040, known as the son of Canute who gained the throne despite questions of legitimacy.
King of England (1035–1040)

Examples

Harold Harefoot ruled England for a brief period after King Canute's death.
Historians debate whether Harold Harefoot was the rightful heir to the English throne.
The reign of Harold Harefoot was marked by political instability and succession disputes.

Etymology

'Harefoot' refers to his reputed speed and agility; 'Harold' from Old English 'Hereweald.'
