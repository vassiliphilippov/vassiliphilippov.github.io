segregationist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛɡ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the separation of different racial groups
supporter of racial segregation

Examples

The segregationist argued against racial integration in the 1950s.
As a known segregationist, he openly opposed the civil rights movement.
Laws enacted by segregationist politicians enforced separation in schools and public spaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrationist - a person who supports the integration of racial groups

Etymology

From 'segregation' + '-ist' (patterned after similar English agent nouns).
