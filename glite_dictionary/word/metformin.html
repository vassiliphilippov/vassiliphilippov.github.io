metformin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtˈfɔrmɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtˈfɔːmɪn / (BrE)
an oral drug used to manage type 2 diabetes by lowering blood sugar levels
oral antidiabetic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed metformin to help control his blood sugar levels.
Many patients with type 2 diabetes are treated with metformin.
Taking metformin can improve insulin sensitivity and reduce the risk of diabetes complications.

Etymology

From methyl + guanidine + formic acid, referencing chemical structure.
