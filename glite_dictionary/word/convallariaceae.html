Convallariaceae

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvælər.iˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / kɒnˌvæl.ər.iˈeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
A former plant family, including lily-of-the-valley and related plants, sometimes separated from the Liliaceae in older botanical classification systems.
former plant family (lily-of-the-valley, etc.)

Examples

Some botanists have treated Convallariaceae as a separate family from Liliaceae.
Recent classification systems no longer recognize Convallariaceae as a valid family.
Lily-of-the-valley was once placed in the Convallariaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a large family of flowering plants known as the lily family

Etymology

From the genus Convallaria (from Latin) + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌvælərɪˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kɒnˌvælərɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that has sometimes been classified as a subfamily within the Liliaceae, but is not widely recognized as a distinct family in modern taxonomy
a plant family sometimes separated from Liliaceae

Examples

Botanists once treated Convallariaceae as a separate family, but recent systems usually include it in Liliaceae.
Some references mention Convallariaceae as the family for lily of the valley.
The classification of Convallariaceae remains debated among plant taxonomists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a large family of flowering plants commonly known as the lily family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Convallaria (lily of the valley) and the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
