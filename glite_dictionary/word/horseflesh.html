horseflesh

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌflɛʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌflɛʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of a horse, especially when used as food.
meat from a horse used as food

Examples

In some European countries, horseflesh is considered a delicacy.
The market sold a variety of meats, including horseflesh and venison.
Many people are unfamiliar with the taste of horseflesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsemeat - the meat of a horse used as food

Etymology

Formed from 'horse' + 'flesh', meaning the flesh of a horse.
