Polistes

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɪstiːz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of social wasps in the family Vespidae, commonly known as paper wasps, found in many parts of the world.
genus of paper wasps

Examples

The Polistes construct their nests from chewed plant material molded into paper-like combs.
Unlike hornets, Polistes are generally less aggressive and easier to observe.
Several species within the genus Polistes are important models for studying social behavior in insects.

Etymology

From Greek polistēs, meaning builder or founder of a city, referencing their nest-building behavior.

2. NOUN

a genus of social wasps in the family Vespidae, known commonly as paper wasps.
genus of paper wasps

Examples

Polistes nests are often found hanging from eaves and window frames in urban areas.
Unlike hornets, Polistes wasps tend to be less aggressive when disturbed.
Many species in the Polistes genus build open, umbrella-shaped nests from chewed plant fibers.

Etymology

From Greek 'polistēs' (founder of cities), used in taxonomy for these social wasps.
