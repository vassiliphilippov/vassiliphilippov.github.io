Alectoris graeca

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛktəˌrɪs ˈɡreɪkə / (AmE), / əˌlɛktəˌrɪs ˈɡriːkə / (BrE)
a species of partridge native to mountainous areas of southern Europe, also called the rock partridge
a partridge species found in southern Europe

Examples

The Alectoris graeca is well adapted to the rocky slopes of the Mediterranean region.
Researchers observed several Alectoris graeca nesting near the summit of the mountain.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Alectoris graeca in its native range.

Etymology

From New Latin: Alectoris from Greek 'alektor' (a cock), graeca meaning 'Greek'.
