Artemisia pontica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɛmɪˈziə pɒnˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɛmɪˈziːə pɒnˈtɪkə / (BrE)
A species of wormwood native to southeastern Europe, known as Roman wormwood, used as a minor source of absinthe.
a species of wormwood used in absinthe

Examples

Artemisia pontica is cultivated in gardens for its aromatic, silvery leaves.
The traditional recipe for absinthe sometimes includes Artemisia pontica to add a subtle flavor.
Herbalists have valued Artemisia pontica for its medicinal properties since ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a group of plants of the genus Artemisia, known for their bitter taste

Etymology

From the genus name 'Artemisia' (named after the Greek goddess Artemis) and Latin 'pontica' meaning 'of Pontus', a region near the Black Sea.
