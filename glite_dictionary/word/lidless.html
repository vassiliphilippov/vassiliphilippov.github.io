lidless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪd.ləs / (AmE), / ˈlɪd.ləs / (BrE)
not having a lid or cover; uncovered or open at the top
without a lid or cover

Examples

The scientist poured the solution into a lidless beaker.
She peered into the old, lidless chest in the attic.
Rainwater collected in the lidless barrel left outside.

Synset Examples

a lidless container

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lidded - having a lid or cover

Etymology

Formed from 'lid' + '-less', where '-less' means 'without'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01449523-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪdləs / (AmE), / ˈlɪdləs / (BrE)
not having a lid; lacking a cover or closure at the top.
without a lid or cover

Examples

Be careful with that lidless jar; the contents might spill easily.
He stared with wide, lidless eyes that never seemed to blink.
The old well was left lidless after the storm damaged its cover.

Synset Examples

a lidless container

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covered - having a lid or cover
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02451388-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪdləs / (AmE), / ˈlɪdləs / (BrE)
(literary) always watchful or unblinking, as if unable to close the eyes; constantly observing.
always watchful or unblinking

Examples

The fortress was guarded by a lidless camera that recorded every movement.
Some described his lidless gaze as piercing and uncanny.
In the story, the mountain was said to have a lidless eye, always awake and watching.

Synset Examples

to an eye like mine, a lidless watcher of the public weal

Etymology

Formed from 'lid' + '-less', meaning 'without a lid', often used figuratively for 'without eyelids'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00093245-s
Synset POS: s
