diminuendo

1. NOUN

/ dəˌmɪn.juˈen.doʊ / (AmE), / dɪˌmɪn.juˈen.dəʊ / (BrE)
In music, a gradual decrease in loudness or intensity.
gradual decrease in loudness (music)

Examples

The orchestra played a beautiful diminuendo at the end of the piece.
She marked a diminuendo in the score to indicate the volume should gradually decrease.
The choir's diminuendo added a delicate touch to the final bars.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crescendo - a gradual increase in loudness (music)

Etymology

From Italian 'diminuendo', present participle of 'diminuire' (to diminish), from Latin 'diminuere'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˌmɪnjuˈɛndoʊ / (AmE), / dɪˌmɪnjuˈɛndəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a gradual decrease in loudness or intensity of sound.
gradual decrease in volume (music)

Examples

The orchestra played the passage with a delicate diminuendo that faded to silence.
As the music moved towards the end, there was a noticeable diminuendo in the strings section.
The conductor signaled for a gradual diminuendo, lowering the energy of the piece.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crescendo - a gradual increase in loudness in music

Etymology

From Italian 'diminuendo', gerund of 'diminuire' (to diminish), from Latin 'dīminuendus'.
