befuddled

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈfʌdəld / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌdəld / (BrE)
unable to think clearly, especially as a result of drinking alcohol; confused or stupefied.
confused or stupefied, often due to alcohol

Examples

After several drinks, he became befuddled and could barely speak coherently.
The befuddled driver struggled to remember where he had parked his car.
She looked befuddled when asked complicated questions, as if her mind was clouded.

Synset Examples

the wino's poor befuddled mind
a mind befogged with drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or understand
  • Antonym: clear-headed - thinking in a calm, sensible, and logical way

Etymology

From 'befuddle' (to confuse), based on the prefix be- and 'fuddle' (to confuse with drink; from dialect).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈfʌd.əld / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌd.əld / (BrE)
unable to think clearly; confused or perplexed, often by many conflicting situations or statements
confused or perplexed

Examples

He looked befuddled after reading the complex instructions.
She gave me a befuddled stare when I mentioned the changes to the schedule.
The befuddled manager forgot where he had put the important documents.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening
  • Synonym: perplexed - completely baffled or puzzled
  • Antonym: clearheaded - able to think clearly; not confused

Etymology

From 'befuddle' (to confuse), formed from 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'fuddle' (to confuse or stupefy), originally 17th century.

3. ADJ

/ bɪˈfʌdəld / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌdəld / (BrE)
confused and unable to think clearly; muddled, often as a result of tiredness or bewilderment
confused; muddled in thinking

Examples

After staying up all night, he felt completely befuddled during the exam.
The speaker's befuddled explanation left the audience even more confused.
Alcohol had left him befuddled and unable to remember what happened.

Synset Examples

muddleheaded ideas
your addled little brain
woolly thinking
woolly-headed ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or to understand something
  • Antonym: clear-headed - able to think in a clear and sensible way

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' + 'fuddle' (to confuse or stupefy), early 19th century.
