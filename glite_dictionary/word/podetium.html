podetium

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈdiːʃiəm / (AmE), / pɒˈdiːtɪəm / (BrE)
a stalk-like outgrowth from the thallus of certain lichens, bearing the ascocarp
stalk in some lichens bearing fruiting body
medical

Examples

The podetium of the lichen extended upward, supporting the fruiting body at its tip.
Under the microscope, you can see the podetium emerging from the thallus.
Different species of lichens feature podetia of various shapes and lengths.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot), referencing its stalk-like form.
