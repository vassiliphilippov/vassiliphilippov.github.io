cytomembrane

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
the thin, double-layered membrane surrounding the cytoplasm of a cell, controlling the movement of substances in and out of the cell
cell's enclosing membrane
medical

Examples

The cytomembrane plays a crucial role in regulating what enters and leaves the cell.
Damage to the cytomembrane can disrupt vital cellular processes.
Researchers studied how certain drugs interact with the cytomembrane of human cells.

Synset Examples

all cells have a cell membrane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell membrane - the semipermeable membrane surrounding the cytoplasm of a cell
  • Synonym: plasma membrane - another term for the cell membrane

Etymology

From Greek kytos (cell) + English membrane.
