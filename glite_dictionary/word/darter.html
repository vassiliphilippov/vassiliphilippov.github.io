darter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
a person or animal that moves quickly and suddenly in short bursts
one who moves quickly and suddenly

Examples

Squirrels are darters, often zigzagging across the lawn to evade predators.
The little boy was a notorious darter, making it hard for his parents to keep up with him in the park.
At dusk, the rabbits became darters, dashing from bush to bush.

Synset Examples

squirrels are darters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinter - a person who runs at full speed over a short distance

Etymology

From the verb 'dart' (to move swiftly), with agentive suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
A fish-eating bird of warm inland waters, having a long flexible neck and a slender, sharp-pointed bill; also called anhinga or snakebird.
fish-eating bird with a long neck

Examples

The darter spread its wings to dry after diving for fish.
We spotted a darter gliding gracefully across the lake.
A darter catches its prey with its sharp, pointed bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anhinga - another name for darter, a fish-eating bird with a long neck
  • Synonym: snakebird - common name for darter (bird) due to snake-like neck
