St. Edward the Martyr

1. PROPN

/ sənt ˈɛdwɚd ðə ˈmɑrtɪr / (AmE), / sənt ˈɛdwəd ðə ˈmɑːtɪə / (BrE)
(c. 962–978) King of England from 975 to 978, regarded as a saint and martyr after his murder, and remembered for his short reign and untimely death at a young age.
King of England and Christian saint (c. 962–978)

Examples

Many English churches are dedicated to St. Edward the Martyr in memory of his piety and tragic fate.
Historians debate the circumstances of St. Edward the Martyr's death and its impact on the succession.
Pilgrims continue to visit sites associated with St. Edward the Martyr, especially in Dorset.
