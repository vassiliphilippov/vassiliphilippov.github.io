to overbalance

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to outweigh or be more significant than something else; to have greater importance or effect
to outweigh in importance or effect

Examples

The risks of the investment overbalance the potential rewards, making it too dangerous.
His desire for revenge overbalanced his sense of justice.
Sometimes, practical concerns overbalance our emotional responses in difficult decisions.

Synset Examples

these considerations outweigh our wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outweigh - to be more important or significant than something else
  • Antonym: counterbalance - to act against something with equal force or importance

Etymology

From over- (above, beyond) + balance (from Old French, from Late Latin bilancia).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæləns / (BrE)
to cause to lose balance or to become unsteady; to upset the equilibrium of something, physically or metaphorically
cause to lose balance or become unsteady

Examples

The sudden weight shift threatened to overbalance the ladder.
Careless spending can easily overbalance the household budget.
He leaned too far forward and began to overbalance on the stool.

Synset Examples

It is not desirable to overbalance the budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbalance - to make something unstable or cause it to lose balance

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + balance (to keep steady); recorded since the early 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02269898-v
