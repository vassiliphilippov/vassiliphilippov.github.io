dried-up

1. ADJ

/ draɪd ˈʌp / (AmE), / draɪd ˈʌp / (BrE)
Having lost all or most of its water; no longer containing moisture.
depleted of water or moisture

Examples

After months without rain, the riverbed was completely dried-up.
The plants in the pot were dried-up and brown from neglect.
By the end of summer, the pond had become just a dried-up patch of mud.

Synset Examples

a dried-up water hole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry, especially due to lack of rain or moisture
  • Antonym: moist - slightly wet; not dry

2. ADJ

/ draɪd ˈʌp / (AmE), / draɪd ˈʌp / (BrE)
(especially of plants or vegetation) having lost all moisture; no longer fresh or green due to drying.
having lost all moisture (plants/vegetation)

Examples

The garden was filled with dried-up grass after weeks without rain.
We walked past the dried-up plants that once bloomed so brightly in spring.
A pile of dried-up leaves crackled beneath our feet in the autumn breeze.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dried up, shriveled, or faded (plants or parts of plants)
  • Antonym: fresh - (of plants) recently picked or still alive, not dry or wilted
