dire

1. ADJ

/ daɪɚ / (AmE), / daɪə / (BrE)
causing great fear, shock, or worry; extremely serious or urgent.
causing fear or extreme seriousness

Examples

The company is in dire financial trouble after several bad investments.
The villagers were warned of the dire consequences if the river flooded.
Her face showed dire fear as she received the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

the awful war
an awful risk

a career or vengeance so direful that London was shocked
the dread presence of the headmaster
polio is no longer the dreaded disease it once was
a dreadful storm
a fearful howling
horrendous explosions shook the city
a terrible curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - causing fear or distress
  • Synonym: dreadful - causing great fear or suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'dirus' (fearful, ominous).

2. ADJ

/ daɪɚ / (AmE), / daɪə / (BrE)
(of a situation or event) extremely serious or urgent; involving great danger or disaster.
extremely serious or dangerous

Examples

The company is facing a dire financial crisis that threatens its survival.
After the earthquake, the town was left in dire need of food and shelter.
Ignoring the warning signs could lead to dire consequences for our health.

Synset Examples

a desperate illness
on all fronts the Allies were in a desperate situation due to lack of materiel
a dire emergency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desperate - (of a situation) involving extreme danger or hopelessness
  • Synonym: direful - extremely dreadful or disastrous

Etymology

From Latin 'dirus' (fearful, dreadful).
