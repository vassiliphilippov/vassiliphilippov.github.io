McGuffin

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡʌfɪn / (AmE), / məˈɡʌfɪn / (BrE)
an object or event in a film or story that serves to trigger the plot but is of little intrinsic importance
plot-driving device in film or story

Examples

The stolen briefcase was the McGuffin that set the entire spy thriller in motion.
In many movies, the search for a rare artifact is just a McGuffin to bring characters together.
Hitchcock often used a McGuffin to propel his storylines, regardless of its actual value to the audience.

Synset Examples

the McGuffin was a key element of Alfred Hitchcock's films

Etymology

Coined by filmmaker Alfred Hitchcock; popularized in the 20th century to describe a plot device.
