trek

1. NOUN

/ tɹɛk / (AmE), / tɹɛk / (BrE)
a long and difficult journey, especially one made on foot
long and difficult journey

Examples

After hours of hiking through the mountains, the trek finally ended at sunset.
Their trek across the desert was exhausting but rewarding.
The documentary follows a family's trek through the remote jungles of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose, often difficult or challenging

Etymology

From Afrikaans *trek* (pull, travel), from Dutch *trekken* (to pull, travel).

2. NOUN

/ trɛk / (AmE), / trɛk / (BrE)
a long and difficult journey by ox wagon, especially a migration undertaken by a group of settlers
migration by ox wagon

Examples

Many Boers undertook the trek from the Cape Colony into the interior of South Africa in the 1830s.
The family's trek across the plains took several arduous months.
Historians have studied the patterns of settlement formed by the great trek in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migration - movement from one place to another, especially by a group of people
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken by a group with a particular purpose

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'trek' (pull, travel), from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull, travel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311342-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ trɛk / (AmE), / trɛk / (BrE)
to make a long and difficult journey, especially on foot
make a long and difficult journey

Examples

They plan to trek through the mountains for ten days, carrying all their supplies.
We trekked across the desert, facing tough conditions and little rest.
She enjoys trekking in remote areas where few people go.

Synset Examples

They trekked towards the North Pole with sleds and skis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hike - to walk a long distance, especially for pleasure or exercise
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with effort, especially over a difficult surface or while carrying something heavy
  • Antonym: sprint - to run at full speed over a short distance

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'trek' (migration, journey), from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull, travel).

4. VERB

/ trɛk / (AmE), / trɛk / (BrE)
to make a long or difficult journey on foot, especially through mountainous or challenging terrain
to journey on foot, especially over rough terrain

Examples

They trekked across the Andes for two weeks in search of adventure.
Every spring, tourists trek through the lush valleys of Nepal.
We spent the afternoon trekking along a rocky mountain trail.

Synset Examples

We spent the summer trekking in the foothills of the Himalayas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hike - to walk a long distance for pleasure or exercise
  • Degree Lower: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically due to exhaustion or difficult conditions

Etymology

From South African Dutch 'trek' (pull, travel), from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull, travel).
