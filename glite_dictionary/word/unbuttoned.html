unbuttoned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbʌtnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbʌtənd / (BrE)
(of clothing) not fastened with buttons; with buttons left undone.
not fastened with buttons

Examples

He walked in with his unbuttoned shirt flapping in the breeze.
She noticed his coat was unbuttoned despite the cold weather.
Leaving the jacket unbuttoned gave his outfit a relaxed look.

Synset Examples

the wind picked up the hem of her unbuttoned coat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buttoned - with buttons fastened
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297963-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbʌtənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbʌtənd / (BrE)
(of behavior or style) free from constraint, reserve, or conventional propriety; open or uninhibited.
uninhibited; lacking constraint

Examples

The author's unbuttoned style makes the memoir both entertaining and honest.
During the party, his unbuttoned laughter and stories put everyone at ease.
The film's unbuttoned humor was refreshing compared to more traditional comedies.

Synset Examples

this unbuttoned and disrespectful age
unlaced behavior in the neighborhood pub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninhibited - not restrained or held back; expressing oneself freely
  • Antonym: buttoned-up - restrained or very reserved in expression or behavior
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02008872-s
Synset POS: s
