luxuriantly

1. ADV

/ lʌɡˈʒʊriəntli / (AmE), / lʌɡˈzjʊəriəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is characterized by rich and abundant growth or production.
abundantly or richly (esp. growth)

Examples

The garden was covered with vines that grew luxuriantly over the walls.
Her hair tumbled luxuriantly over her shoulders after the rain.
Ferns and mosses thrived luxuriantly in the humid, shaded forest.

Synset Examples

whiskers grew luxuriantly from his ample jowls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundantly - in large quantities, plentifully
  • Antonym: sparsely - in a thinly dispersed or distributed way

Etymology

From 'luxuriant' + '-ly'; 'luxuriant' from Latin luxurians, luxuriantis (overgrown, excessive).

2. ADV

/ lʌgˈʒʊriəntli / (AmE), / lʌgˈzjʊəriəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is rich, elaborate, or abundant, often marked by complexity or wealth of detail.
in a rich or elaborate manner

Examples

The garden was luxuriantly green after the spring rains.
Her hair grew luxuriantly, curling in soft, thick waves down her back.
The palace was decorated luxuriantly, with gold accents and intricate carvings everywhere.

Synset Examples

this suave, culture-loving and luxuriantly good-looking M.P. represents the car-workers of Coventry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: richly - in a wealthy or sumptuous manner
  • Antonym: simply - in a plain, uncomplicated way

Etymology

From 'luxuriant' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'luxurians', present participle of 'luxuriare' (to be excessive, to abound).

Source: WordNet
