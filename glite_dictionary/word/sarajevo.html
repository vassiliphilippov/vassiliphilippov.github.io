Sarajevo

1. PROPN

/ sɑːrəˈjeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsærəˈjeɪvəʊ / (BrE)
the capital city and largest city of Bosnia and Herzegovina, known for its historical significance, including the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914 that precipitated World War I
capital city of Bosnia and Herzegovina

Examples

Many tourists visit Sarajevo to explore its rich blend of Eastern and Western cultures.
The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo was a pivotal event in world history.
During the 1990s, Sarajevo endured a long and devastating siege.

Etymology

From Bosnian Sarajevo, possibly derived from Turkish saray (palace) and Slavic suffix -evo.
