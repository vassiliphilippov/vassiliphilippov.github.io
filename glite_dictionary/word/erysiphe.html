Erysiphe

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪsɪfi / (AmE), / ɛˈrɪsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that cause powdery mildew disease in plants.
genus of powdery mildew fungi

Examples

Erysiphe species are responsible for many cases of powdery mildew affecting crops worldwide.
Scientists isolated Erysiphe from the leaves of several infected rose bushes.
Controlling Erysiphe outbreaks is crucial for maintaining healthy vineyards.

Etymology

From Greek 'erysiphe' meaning 'mildew' or 'blight'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɛr.ɪˈsaɪ.fi / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈsɪ.fi / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Erysiphaceae, known as powdery mildews, which are plant pathogens causing powdery mildew disease.
genus of powdery mildew fungi
medical

Examples

Many crops are susceptible to infection by Erysiphe species, which cause powdery mildew.
The biologist identified the fungus as Erysiphe during her study of grapevines.
Controlling Erysiphe can be challenging in damp, temperate climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powdery mildew - a fungal disease causing a white powdery coating on plants

Etymology

From Greek 'erysiphe' (red skin, blight), originally used for plant diseases.
