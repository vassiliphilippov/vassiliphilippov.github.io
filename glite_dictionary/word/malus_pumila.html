Malus pumila

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.ləs ˈpuː.mɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.ləs ˈpjuː.mɪ.lə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the apple tree, a native Eurasian tree cultivated for its edible rounded fruit.
scientific name for the apple tree

Examples

Many commercial orchards grow Malus pumila for its high-quality apples.
The blossoms of Malus pumila are a sign of spring in many regions.
Botanists often study Malus pumila to understand fruit development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple tree - tree that produces apples, cultivated for its edible fruit
  • Synonym: Malus domestica - another scientific name commonly used for the domesticated apple

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' (apple tree) and Latinized form 'pumila' (dwarf, small).
