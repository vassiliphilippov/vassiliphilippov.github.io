to ossify

1. VERB

/ ˈɑː.sə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue or cartilage) to turn into bone or become bony; to undergo or cause ossification.
to turn into bone; become bony

Examples

The cartilage will gradually ossify during the teenage years.
After the injury, some muscle tissue began to ossify, leading to restricted movement.
As we age, certain soft tissues may ossify and lose their flexibility.

Synset Examples

The tissue ossified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calcify - to become hardened by deposition of calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin 'ossificare', from 'os' (bone) + '-ficare' (make).

WordNet ID: oewn-00268135-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɑs.ə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to become or make something rigid, fixed, and unwilling to change, especially in habits, ideas, or institutions
become rigid or fixed in attitude or practice

Examples

After years of the same routine, his thinking began to ossify and he resisted any new ideas.
If an organization fails to adapt, its methods can ossify and hinder progress.
The school board's refusal to consider new teaching techniques has ossified the curriculum.

Synset Examples

rigidify the training schedule
ossified teaching methods
slogans petrify our thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rigidify - to make rigid or inflexible
  • Synonym: to petrify - to make rigid, inflexible, or stone-like

Etymology

From Latin 'ossificare', from 'os' (bone) + 'facere' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419035-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɑː.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to cause (something, especially tissue) to become hard and bony; to undergo or cause to undergo a transformation into bone or a bone-like substance.
to turn into bone or harden
medical

Examples

The disease can ossify soft tissue, making movement painful and difficult.
After years in the ground, the remains began to ossify into fossilized bone.
Scientists observed how certain chemicals could ossify cartilage in the experimental animals.

Synset Examples

The disease ossified the tissue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calcify - to become or cause to become hard by deposition of calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + -fy (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00268251-v
Synset POS: v
