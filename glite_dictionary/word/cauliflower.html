cauliflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.ləˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.lɪˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A cultivated plant of the cabbage family with a dense white head of undeveloped flowers, eaten as a vegetable.
white vegetable with dense edible head

Examples

She picked a fresh cauliflower from the garden to cook for dinner.
Roasted cauliflower makes a delicious and healthy side dish.
He bought a head of cauliflower and some carrots at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broccoli - green vegetable with edible flowering head

Etymology

From Italian cavolfiore, from cavolo (cabbage) + fiore (flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.ləˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ɪˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a vegetable with a large, compact head of white flower buds, eaten as food
vegetable with white flower head

Examples

She steamed some cauliflower to serve with dinner.
The recipe calls for one head of cauliflower and a handful of spices.
You can roast cauliflower with olive oil for a tasty side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broccoli - a similar vegetable with green flower buds

Etymology

From Italian 'cavolfiore', from Latin 'caulis' (cabbage) + 'flos, floris' (flower).
