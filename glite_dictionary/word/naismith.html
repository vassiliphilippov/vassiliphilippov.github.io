Naismith

1. PROPN

/ ˈneɪˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈneɪˌsmɪθ / (BrE)
James Naismith (1861–1939), Canadian-born educator and inventor of the game of basketball.
inventor of basketball

Examples

James Naismith invented basketball in 1891 while teaching in Springfield, Massachusetts.
The NBA championship trophy is named after Naismith in honor of his lasting impact on the sport.
Many historians credit Naismith with shaping the early development of modern basketball.
