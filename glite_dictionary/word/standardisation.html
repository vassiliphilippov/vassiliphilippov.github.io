standardisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændərdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstændədaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of making something conform to a standard or set of standards
making things conform to a standard
british

Examples

The standardisation of safety procedures has improved workplace conditions across the company.
International standardisation is essential for seamless global trade and collaboration.
The standardisation of test scores allows for fairer comparisons between students.

Synset Examples

standardization of nuts and bolts had saved industry millions of dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardization - the American spelling of the same word

Etymology

From standard + -isation, after French 'standardisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstændərdɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstændədaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of establishing or enforcing standards or regulations to ensure consistency or uniformity
the act of establishing standards

Examples

The standardisation of safety procedures helped reduce workplace accidents.
International standardisation allows products to be sold across multiple countries more easily.
Many industries benefit from the standardisation of technical specifications.

Synset Examples

a committee was appointed to recommend terminological standardization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardization - the US spelling of standardisation
  • Antonym: diversification - the process of making something more varied or diverse

Etymology

From 'standard' + '-isation', patterned after French 'standardisation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01161177-n
Synset POS: n
