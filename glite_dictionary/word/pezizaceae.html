Pezizaceae

1. NOUN

/ pɛzɪˈzeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / pɛzɪˈzeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a large family of fungi comprising many typical cup fungi, found in various habitats worldwide
family of cup fungi
medical

Examples

Many members of the Pezizaceae are commonly found on decaying wood in forests.
The classification of Pezizaceae has changed as scientists learn more about fungal genetics.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Pezizaceae family in a remote cave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup fungus - fungus with a cup-shaped fruiting body

Etymology

From New Latin Peziza (a genus of fungi) + -aceae (family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ pɛzɪˈzeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɛzɪˈzæsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of fungi comprising many typical cup fungi, belonging to the order Pezizales.
family of cup fungi (fungi)

Examples

The Pezizaceae include several edible and inedible species of cup fungi.
Members of the Pezizaceae can be found in both temperate and tropical regions.
Mycologists frequently study Pezizaceae for their complex reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup fungus - a type of fungus with cup-shaped fruiting body

Etymology

From the genus name Peziza, which is the type genus of the family, plus the standard taxonomic suffix '-aceae' for families.
