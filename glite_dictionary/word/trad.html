trad

1. NOUN

/ træd / (AmE), / træd / (BrE)
A style of traditional jazz, especially as revived in Britain and the US in the 1950s, characterized by simple harmonies and collective improvisation.
traditional jazz genre of the 1950s

Examples

The band played classic trad at the festival to an enthusiastic crowd.
He developed a love for trad after listening to recordings from the 1950s.
Many young musicians are rediscovering trad in modern jazz clubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a style of music with syncopated rhythms and improvisation
  • Synonym: Dixieland - an early form of jazz, also called traditional jazz

Etymology

Shortened form of 'traditional jazz.'
