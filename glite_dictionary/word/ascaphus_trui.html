Ascaphus trui

1. NOUN

/ əˈskæfəs ˈtruː.aɪ / (AmE), / əˈskæfəs ˈtruː.iː / (BrE)
A species of frog native to western North America, notable for its taillike copulatory organ used in reproduction.
species of tailed frog from North America

Examples

The Ascaphus trui is adapted to cool, fast-flowing streams in western forests.
Scientists observed the unique mating behavior of Ascaphus trui during the breeding season.
Conservation efforts have been put in place to protect the habitat of Ascaphus trui.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascaphus truei - closely related species of tailed frog; sometimes previously considered the same species

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'askaphos' (tailed frog); species named in honor of herpetologist Charles M. True.
