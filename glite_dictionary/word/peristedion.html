Peristedion

1. NOUN

/ pɛr.ɪˈstiː.di.ɑn / (AmE), / pɛr.ɪˈstiː.di.ən / (BrE)
A genus of armored sea robins found in marine environments, often classified as the type genus of the subfamily Peristediinae.
a genus of armored sea robins

Examples

Several species in the genus Peristedion are known for their bony armor and spiny fins.
The waters near the continental shelf are home to Peristedion and other sea robins.
Scientists discovered a new Peristedion species during their marine expedition.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'stedion', diminutive of 'stēs', a kind of fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈstɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈstɪdiən / (BrE)
A genus of armored sea robins, constituting the type genus of the subfamily Peristediinae in some classifications.
genus of armored sea robins

Examples

The species within the genus Peristedion are known for their distinctive armored plates.
Marine biologists often study Peristedion to understand evolutionary adaptations in sea robins.
The Peristedion genus includes several species found in deep-sea environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots: peri- (around) + stedion (derived from stethos, breast), referring to features of the fish.
