2. NOUN

/ ˈtizɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of making fun of or provoking someone in a playful or malicious way, often with persistent jokes or ridicule.
making fun of or provoking someone

Examples

The constant teasing from his classmates made him feel uncomfortable.
She enjoyed lighthearted teasing with her friends after school.
His teasing sometimes crossed the line from playful to hurtful.

Synset Examples

he ignored their teases
his ribbing was gentle but persistent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mocking - The act of making fun of someone, often in a scornful way
  • Synonym: taunting - The act of provoking or challenging with insulting remarks
  • Degree Higher: bullying - The act of intimidating or mistreating someone persistently

Etymology

From the verb 'tease', which originates from Old English 'tǣsan' (to pluck, pull apart).
