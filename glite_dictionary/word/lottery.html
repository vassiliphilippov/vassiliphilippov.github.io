lottery

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːt̬əri / (AmE), / ˈlɒtəri / (BrE)
an event, process, or situation whose outcome is determined by chance rather than by planning or control
event decided by chance

Examples

Many people feel that getting a good job these days is a lottery.
Admission to the program is a lottery, with places assigned randomly.
Life can sometimes seem like a lottery, where luck plays a huge part.

Synset Examples

the election was just a lottery to them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - an undertaking with an uncertain outcome

Etymology

From Italian *lotteria*, from *lotto* (lot, portion, fate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈlɒt.ər.i / (BrE)
A game or method of raising money in which people buy numbered tickets and prizes are given to the holders of numbers drawn at random.
game of chance with random prizes

Examples

He bought a lottery ticket hoping to win the million-dollar jackpot.
The school raised money for charity by organizing a lottery.
Many people view life as a kind of lottery, full of unpredictable events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffle - a lottery in which the prizes are goods rather than money
  • Synonym: sweepstake - a type of lottery where each ticket holder wins the whole prize

Etymology

From Italian 'lotteria', from 'lotto' (a drawing of lots), from Old French 'lote' (lot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00509102-n
Synset POS: n
