chronically

1. ADV

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that persists for a long time or recurs constantly, often in reference to illness or problems.
in a persistent or long-lasting way

Examples

Many patients remain chronically ill despite long-term treatment.
The building has been chronically understaffed for years, causing constant delays.
Some regions are chronically short of clean drinking water.

Synset Examples

chronically ill persons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acutely - in a way that is severe but short in duration

Etymology

From Greek 'khronikos' (of time), from 'khronos' (time).

2. ADV

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a habitual, long-lasting, or recurring manner, often referring to illness or negative behavior.
persistently, over a long period

Examples

She has suffered chronically from migraines for most of her adult life.
Many patients with asthma cough chronically, especially in the winter.
Some people are chronically late despite their best efforts.

Synset Examples

smoking chronically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acutely - In a sudden, severe, or brief manner (often of illness)

Etymology

From Greek 'khronikos' (of time) via Late Latin 'chronicus'; related to 'chronos' (time).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00142067-r
Synset POS: r
