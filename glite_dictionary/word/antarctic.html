Antarctic

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɑːrktɪk / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːktɪk / (BrE)
the region surrounding the South Pole, including Antarctica and the surrounding southern ocean waters
region around the South Pole

Examples

Scientists study climate change in the Antarctic to better understand global warming.
The unique wildlife of the Antarctic includes penguins and seals.
The harsh conditions of the Antarctic make exploration extremely challenging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Arctic - the region around the North Pole

Etymology

From Greek 'antarktikos' meaning 'opposite to the Arctic'.

2. ADJ

/ ænˈtɑrktɪk / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or situated at or near the South Pole or the region around it
relating to the South Pole or its region

Examples

The Antarctic climate is among the harshest on Earth.
Scientists set up a research station in Antarctic waters.
Penguins are well adapted to the Antarctic environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Arctic - relating to the region around the North Pole

Etymology

From Greek antarktikos (opposite to the Arctic), from anti- (opposite) + arktikos (of the Bear, northern).
