isobutylene

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈbjuːtɪˌliːn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈbjuːtiːliːn / (BrE)
a flammable gaseous hydrocarbon (C4H8) used primarily in the manufacture of synthetic rubber, plastics, and gasoline additives
a gaseous hydrocarbon used in industry

Examples

Isobutylene is commonly used as a starting material in the production of synthetic rubber.
The chemical plant increased its output of isobutylene to meet the growing demand for high-octane gasoline.
Workers must monitor all storage areas because isobutylene is highly flammable and volatile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butylene - any of several isomeric hydrocarbons C4H8

Etymology

From iso- (indicating isomerism) + butylene (from butyl + -ene).
