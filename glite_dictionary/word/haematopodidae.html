Haematopodidae

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmætəˈpɒdəˌdi / (AmE), / hiːˌmætəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of shorebirds known as oystercatchers, characterized by their strong bills and coastal habitats.
family of shorebirds (oystercatchers)

Examples

There are several species within the Haematopodidae family found along rocky coastlines.
The striking orange beak is a distinctive feature of birds in the Haematopodidae.
Researchers studied nesting behaviors across various members of the Haematopodidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oystercatcher - a type of shorebird in the Haematopodidae family

Etymology

From scientific Latin Haematopodidae, formed from Greek haima (blood) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to the type genus Haematopus.
