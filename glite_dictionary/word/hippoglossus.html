Hippoglossus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈɡlɑːsəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of large flatfish known as halibuts, found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
genus of flatfish (halibuts)

Examples

The Atlantic Hippoglossus is prized by commercial fisheries for its massive size.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Hippoglossus in the North Pacific Ocean.
A single Hippoglossus can weigh hundreds of pounds, making it one of the largest flatfish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halibut - a large flatfish of the genus Hippoglossus

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the shape of the fish's tongue.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of large flatfish, commonly known as halibuts, found in northern oceans.
genus of halibuts (flatfish)

Examples

The Atlantic Hippoglossus is highly prized by commercial fisheries for its mild flavor and large size.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Hippoglossus to monitor population health in northern waters.
Most Hippoglossus specimens can be identified by their diamond-shaped bodies and pale undersides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halibut - a large flatfish belonging to the genus Hippoglossus

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to the fish's shape.
