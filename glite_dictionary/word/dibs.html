dibs

1. NOUN

/ dɪbz / (AmE), / dɪbz / (BrE)
the right to have or choose something before others, usually declared informally by stating one's claim
informal claim to rights or precedence
informal

Examples

She shouted dibs on the front seat as soon as she saw the car.
If you see cookies in the break room, I call dibs on the chocolate chip ones.
He always tries to get dibs on the newest video games before anyone else.

Synset Examples

I have dibs on that last slice of pizza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - an assertion of the right to something

Etymology

Originated as children's slang in the 19th century; possibly from 'dibstones', a game similar to jacks.
