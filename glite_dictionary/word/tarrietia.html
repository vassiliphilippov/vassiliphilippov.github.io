Tarrietia

1. NOUN

/ təˈraɪ.ɪ.ti.ə / (AmE), / təˈraɪ.ɪ.ti.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of timber trees native to East Asia and Australia, belonging to the family Malvaceae.
genus of East Asian and Australian timber trees

Examples

The forests of Queensland are home to several species of Tarrietia.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Tarrietia trees in East Asia.
The wood from Tarrietia is highly valued in the timber industry.
