microflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈflɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
The community of microscopic plants and often bacteria living in a particular environment, such as part of the body or an ecosystem.
community of microscopic plants and bacteria
medical

Examples

The intestinal microflora plays a crucial role in digestion and immune function.
Antibiotics can disrupt the natural balance of your gut microflora.
Scientists are studying the microflora found in soil to improve crop health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microbiota - the community of microorganisms inhabiting a particular environment

Etymology

From micro- (small) + Latin flora (plants).
