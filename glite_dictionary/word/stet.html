to stet

1. VERB

/ stɛt / (AmE), / stɛt / (BrE)
(in editing or proofreading) to instruct that something marked for correction or deletion should remain as originally written.
to let stand (in editing)

Examples

When the editor suggested changes to the manuscript, the author asked to stet several original phrases.
Please stet the previous sentence—the revision is unnecessary.
After reviewing the changes, she decided to stet most of the text as it was.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to delete - to remove marked text (in editing)

Etymology

From Latin *stet* ('let it stand'), third person singular present subjunctive of *stāre* ('to stand').

2. VERB

/ stɛt / (AmE), / stɛt / (BrE)
to cancel a previous correction or deletion, used especially in editing to instruct that the original text should stand
cancel a previous correction in editing

Examples

The editor decided to stet the previous change, keeping the author's original sentence.
When you see a mistake that should remain, you can stet the correction in the margin.
After some discussion, they chose to stet the deleted paragraph from the draft.

Etymology

From Latin 'stet' meaning 'let it stand', third person singular present subjunctive of 'stāre' (to stand).
