misfeasance

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈfiːzəns / (AmE), / mɪsˈfiːzəns / (BrE)
The wrongful or improper performance of a lawful act, especially by a public official or authority.
improper performance of a lawful act
legal

Examples

The town council was investigated for misfeasance after improperly awarding contracts to certain companies.
Lawsuits were filed against the agency for misfeasance in carrying out environmental inspections.
The court found him guilty of misfeasance rather than outright fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malfeasance - wrongdoing or misconduct, especially by a public official
  • Antonym: nonfeasance - failure to act where action is required

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *mesfesaunce* (wrongdoing), from Old French *mesfaire* (to do wrong).
