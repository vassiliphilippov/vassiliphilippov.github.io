oaf

1. NOUN

/ eɪf / (AmE), / əʊf / (BrE)
a person who is clumsy, unintelligent, or lacks social grace
a clumsy or stupid person

Examples

He acted like an oaf at the dinner party, spilling his drink and making awkward jokes.
Don't be such an oaf; pay attention to what you're doing.
Despite his size, Tom is no oaf—he's actually quite thoughtful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clod - a stupid or awkward person
  • Synonym: lout - an uncouth or aggressive man or boy
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability

Etymology

From obsolete English 'auf' (elf’s child, changeling), of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'ufe' (fool, simpleton).
