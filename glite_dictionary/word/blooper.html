blooper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːpər / (AmE), / ˈbluːpə / (BrE)
an embarrassing mistake, especially one made in public or during the recording of a television program, film, or live performance
an embarrassing mistake

Examples

The news anchor couldn't help but laugh at his own blooper during the live broadcast.
Every year, they release a hilarious reel of movie bloopers after filming wraps up.
She apologized for the blooper she made during her presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act or remark causing embarrassment
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

Probably from 'blooper' (1920s U.S. baseball slang for a weakly hit ball), later applied to errors caught on tape.
