finial

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪəl / (BrE)
A decorative ornament, often foliated, that forms the top of a spire, gable, or other architectural element.
ornamental top of a spire or gable

Examples

The cathedral's spire was crowned with a large bronze finial.
A stone finial adorned the peak of the old mansion's roof.
Restorers carefully repaired the damaged finial to preserve the building's historical character.

Etymology

From Old French *fini(al)*, from Latin *finis* meaning 'end'.
