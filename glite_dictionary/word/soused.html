soused

1. ADJ

/ saʊst / (AmE), / saʊst / (BrE)
Very drunk; intoxicated to a great degree.
very drunk; intoxicated

Examples

He was so soused last night that he couldn't remember getting home.
The party guests left, some soused and others just tipsy.
After several rounds of whiskey, she became completely soused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriated - drunk; intoxicated
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol
  • Degree Lower: tipsy - slightly drunk
  • Synonym: plastered - extremely drunk

Etymology

From Middle English *sousen* (to pickle, soak in liquid), later figuratively extended to being drunk as if soaked with alcohol.
