tectonics

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtɑnɪks / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
the branch of geology that studies the structure and movement of the Earth's crust, especially the processes of folding and faulting
geology of Earth's structural features

Examples

Research in tectonics has revealed much about the forces shaping mountain ranges.
The course covers both earthquakes and the fundamentals of tectonics.
Advancements in tectonics have helped explain the distribution of continents and oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate tectonics - the theory explaining the movement of Earth's lithospheric plates
  • Synonym: geology - the scientific study of the Earth's physical structure and substances

Etymology

From Greek tektonikos (pertaining to building), from tektōn (builder).

2. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtɑnɪks / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
the science or study of building or construction, especially as it relates to the design and structure of buildings
the science of building design and structure

Examples

Modern tectonics explores how structural elements define the character of a building.
Her research interests include both sustainable design and tectonics in contemporary architecture.
The professor gave a lecture on the history of architectural tectonics and its influence on urban landscapes.

Etymology

From Greek tectonikos (pertaining to building), from tektōn (builder, carpenter).
