sapropel

1. NOUN

/ səˈprəʊpəl / (AmE), / səˈprəʊpɛl / (BrE)
A muddy deposit rich in organic matter that accumulates at the bottom of lakes or oceans.
organic-rich lake or oceanic mud

Examples

Scientists analyzed the sapropel to learn more about ancient climate changes.
Thick layers of sapropel are often found in the Black Sea seabed.
The sediment core contains a distinct band of sapropel indicating a period of high organic productivity.

Etymology

From Greek 'sapros' (rotten) and 'pelos' (mud, clay).
