Junco hyemalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkoʊ haɪˈɛməlɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəʊ hʌɪˈɛməlɪs / (BrE)
a common North American bird of the sparrow family with mainly grey plumage and dark brown eyes, known as the dark-eyed junco
common North American junco species (dark-eyed junco)

Examples

The Junco hyemalis is often seen foraging on the ground during winter months.
Birdwatchers can identify the Junco hyemalis by its distinctive grey and white coloring.
A flock of Junco hyemalis gathered beneath the bird feeder after the snowstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark-eyed junco - another common name for Junco hyemalis

Etymology

From Latin; junco (reed bird) and hyemalis (of winter).
