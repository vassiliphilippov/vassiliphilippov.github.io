to morph

1. VERB

/ mɔrf / (AmE), / mɔːf / (BrE)
to change or cause something to change smoothly from one image or shape to another, especially using computer animation
change shape (esp. in computer animation)

Examples

The designer used software to morph the company logo into a variety of shapes.
In the film, one character morphs into another right before our eyes.
We watched as the animation slowly morphed a cat into a lion.

Synset Examples

The computer programmer morphed the image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change completely the appearance or character of something
  • Degree Lower: to distort - to change something so that it is no longer true or accurate, especially the shape

Etymology

Shortened from 'metamorphose', influenced by the technical process of animation morphing in the late 20th century.

2. VERB

/ mɔrf / (AmE), / mɔːf / (BrE)
to change smoothly from one image or shape into another, often using computer animation
to change smoothly from one shape to another

Examples

The character was designed to morph into different creatures throughout the movie.
Advanced software can morph two faces into one seamless image.
During the music video, the lead singer morphed into an eagle and flew away.

Synset Examples

In the video, Michael Jackson morphed into a panther

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Synonym: to metamorphose - to change completely in form or nature

Etymology

From 'metamorphose,' shortened and popularized in computer graphics and animation in the 1980s–1990s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469943-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɔrf / (AmE), / mɔːf / (BrE)
to change, transform, or undergo a significant alteration, especially in form or character
change or transform significantly

Examples

Her mood seemed to morph with the weather.
The caterpillar will eventually morph into a butterfly.
Over the years, his hobby began to morph into a successful business.

Synset Examples

Her mood seemed to morph with the weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Synonym: change - to make or become different
  • Antonym: to remain - to stay the same; not change

Etymology

From Greek 'morphē' (form, shape), popularized in the late 20th century by computer graphics terminology.
