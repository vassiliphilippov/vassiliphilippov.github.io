merry-go-round

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeri ɡoʊ ˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɛri ɡəʊ ˌraʊnd / (BrE)
a situation or process that keeps repeating itself in a continuous, often pointless or confusing cycle
cycle of repetitive or pointless events

Examples

After years on the corporate merry-go-round, he decided to take a break and change careers.
The legal dispute became a merry-go-round of appeals and counterclaims with no end in sight.
She grew frustrated with the merry-go-round of daily meetings that accomplished nothing.

Synset Examples

if we lose the election, the whole legislative merry-go-round will have to start over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order

Etymology

Figurative use based on the amusement ride 'merry-go-round', describing a repetitive circular motion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛri ɡoʊ ˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɛri ɡəʊ ˌraʊnd / (BrE)
a large, rotating amusement ride with seats, often in the form of animals, on which children or adults can sit and be spun around
rotating amusement ride with seats

Examples

The children laughed as they rode the merry-go-round at the fair.
We spent the afternoon riding the merry-go-round and eating cotton candy.
A brightly painted merry-go-round stood in the center of the amusement park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - a rotating amusement ride; synonym for merry-go-round

Etymology

From the phrase 'merry-go-round', possibly a calque from French 'carrousel' meaning 'a tilting match'.

Source: WordNet
