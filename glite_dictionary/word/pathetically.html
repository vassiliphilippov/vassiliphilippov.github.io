pathetically

1. ADV

/ pəˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that arouses scornful pity or contempt due to being weak, inadequate, or pitiful.
in a way that arouses scornful pity

Examples

He apologized pathetically, hoping for some sympathy from the committee.
The old sign was pathetically faded and broken, barely legible after years of neglect.
She clung pathetically to her unrealistic hopes, refusing to accept reality.

Synset Examples

they had pathetically little money
it was pathetically bad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitifully - in a way that evokes pity or compassion

Etymology

From 'pathetic' + '-ally', formed on the model of adverbs from adjectives.

2. ADV

/ pəˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that arouses pity or sadness; so as to cause sympathy or compassion.
in a way that arouses pity or compassion

Examples

The abandoned puppy whimpered pathetically in the cold alley.
She looked at him pathetically, hoping he would help.
The actor sobbed pathetically on stage, moving the audience to tears.

Synset Examples

the sick child cried pathetically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitifully - in a way that evokes pity; similar meaning

Etymology

From 'pathetic' + '-ally', formed on the pattern of adverbs.
