gaseousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪsiəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being in the form of a gas; the condition of having the properties of a gas
state of being a gas

Examples

The gaseousness of the substance made it suitable for use in balloons.
Scientists measured the gaseousness of the material under different temperatures.
Some drugs are stored in a pressurized form to maintain their gaseousness until use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaseity - state of being gaseous
  • Antonym: solidity - state or quality of being solid
  • Antonym: liquidity - state or quality of being a liquid

Etymology

From 'gaseous' + '-ness', with 'gaseous' from 'gas' (from Dutch 'gas', possibly related to Greek 'khaos').
