authoritarianism

1. NOUN

/ əˌθɔr.əˈtɛr.i.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒr.ɪˈteə.ri.əˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
a form of government or political system in which a single authority or small group holds absolute power, typically not limited by laws, constitutions, or opposition.
absolute or dictatorial government

Examples

Many historians have written about the dangers of authoritarianism arising in times of crisis.
The country's shift toward authoritarianism has alarmed human rights advocates worldwide.
Teaching students about authoritarianism helps them understand important political concepts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: democracy - a form of government in which power is vested in the people, who rule either directly or through freely elected representatives
  • Degree Higher: totalitarianism - a political system in which the state holds total authority over society and seeks to control all aspects of public and private life

Etymology

From 'authoritarian' + '-ism', modeled after French 'autoritarisme'.
