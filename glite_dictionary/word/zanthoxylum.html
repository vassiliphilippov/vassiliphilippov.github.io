Zanthoxylum

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɒksɪləm / (AmE), / zanˈθɒksɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous or evergreen trees and shrubs, some species of which are known as prickly ash and are used for culinary or medicinal purposes
a plant genus including prickly ash

Examples

The bark of Zanthoxylum species is often used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners sometimes plant Zanthoxylum for its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
Sichuan pepper, obtained from Zanthoxylum, is a key ingredient in Chinese cuisine.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + xylon (wood), referring to the yellow wood of some species.
