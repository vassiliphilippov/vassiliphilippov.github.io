bullfrog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌfrɔg / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌfrɒg / (BrE)
A large, highly aquatic frog native to North America, known for its loud, deep croaking call.
large North American aquatic frog

Examples

The loud croak of a bullfrog echoed across the pond at dusk.
Children tried to spot the bullfrog hiding among the lily pads.
Many wetlands are home to the American bullfrog, which is known for its impressive size.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: frog - a tailless amphibian with a short squat body, moist skin, and long hind legs for leaping
