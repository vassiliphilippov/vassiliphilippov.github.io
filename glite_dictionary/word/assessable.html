assessable

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɛsəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈsɛsəbl̩ / (BrE)
Capable of being assessed, especially for taxation or evaluation purposes.
capable of being assessed

Examples

Only assessable income should be included in the tax calculation.
The property is not assessable under current regulations.
Certain goods are assessable for customs duties at the port of entry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxable - subject to being taxed
  • Antonym: non-assessable - not capable of being assessed

Etymology

From 'assess' + '-able', modeled after Latin roots.

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɛs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛs.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being evaluated or considered carefully; able to be assessed.
capable of being assessed or evaluated

Examples

The judge focused on the assessable aspects of the applicant's experience.
Many artworks are not easily assessable using objective criteria.
The project offered clearly assessable outcomes for quality assurance.

Synset Examples

the assessable qualities of art

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unassessable - not able to be assessed or evaluated

Etymology

From 'assess' + '-able', following the pattern of Latin-based adjectives.
