redolent

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdələnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdələnt / (BrE)
strongly suggestive or reminiscent of something; serving to bring something to mind
suggestive or reminiscent of something

Examples

The old house was redolent of childhood memories.
Her speech was redolent of hope and resilience.
That film is redolent of classic Hollywood glamour.

Synset Examples

cannot forbear to close on this redolent literary note
a campaign redolent of machine politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evocative - bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind
  • Synonym: reminiscent - tending to remind one of something
  • Antonym: forgetful - not likely to recall something; failing to bring something to mind

Etymology

From Latin redolēre (to emit a smell), originally referring to scent but later extended figuratively.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdələnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdələnt / (BrE)
Having a strong, pleasant, or suggestive smell.
having a strong, pleasant smell

Examples

The garden was redolent with the scent of blooming roses on summer evenings.
Her kitchen was always redolent of fresh baking and spices.
The air inside the cabin was redolent with the smell of pinewood and smoke.

Synset Examples

the pine woods were more redolent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Synonym: aromatic - having a distinctive and pleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'redolēre' (to emit a smell), from 're-' (again) + 'olēre' (to smell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01056127-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdələnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdəl(ə)nt / (BrE)
Having a strong, pleasant, or distinctive smell; often used with 'of' or 'with' to specify the source of the odor.
having a noticeable smell, often of something

Examples

The old library was redolent of leather-bound books and polished wood.
Her kitchen was always redolent with the aroma of freshly baked bread.
The summer air became redolent of blooming jasmine as the evening approached.

Synset Examples

the hall was redolent of floor wax
air redolent with the fumes of beer and whiskey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Synonym: aromatic - having a noticeable and usually pleasant smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell

Etymology

From Latin 'redolent-' (giving out a scent), from 'redolēre' (to smell of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
