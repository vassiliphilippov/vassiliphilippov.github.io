analecta

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈlɛktə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
A collection of selected passages or excerpts from one or more literary works, often compiled for scholarly study.
collection of literary excerpts

Examples

The professor assigned readings from a analecta of classical Greek poetry.
His research relied heavily on a analecta of 19th-century essays.
For her thesis, she compiled a analecta of feminist literary criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Greek 'analekta' (things gathered up), from 'analegein' (to gather up).
