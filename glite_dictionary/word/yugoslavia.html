Yugoslavia

1. PROPN

/ juːɡəˈslɑːviə / (AmE), / juːɡəˈslɑːviə / (BrE)
a former country in southeastern Europe, which existed in various forms from 1918 to 2003, originally composed of several Balkan states, and later split into independent nations such as Serbia, Croatia, Slovenia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, and North Macedonia.
former country in southeastern Europe

Examples

During the 1990s, the breakup of Yugoslavia led to a series of conflicts in the Balkans.
Her family emigrated from Yugoslavia years before the country dissolved.
Athletes from Yugoslavia competed under one flag until the early 1990s.

Etymology

From Serbo-Croatian *Jugoslavija*, from *jug* (south) + *Slavija* (land of Slavs).

2. PROPN

/ juˌgoʊˈslɑːviə / (AmE), / juːˌɡɒˈslɑːviə / (BrE)
a former country in southeastern Europe that existed from 1918 until the early 1990s, comprising the present-day territories of several Balkan states.
former country in southeastern Europe

Examples

The country of Yugoslavia dissolved into several independent nations during the 1990s.
My grandfather was born in Yugoslavia before it broke up into separate states.
Athletes from Yugoslavia competed together in the Olympics until the early 1990s.

Synset Examples

Tito's Yugoslavia included Slovenia, Croatia, Macedonia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Serbia, and Montenegro

Etymology

From South Slavic *Jugoslavija*, meaning 'Land of the South Slavs'.
