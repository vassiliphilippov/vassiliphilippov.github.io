exilic

1. ADJ

/ ɛgˈzɪlɪk / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to exile or a period of exile, especially the exile of the Jews during the Babylonian Captivity
relating to exile

Examples

The poet's exilic writings reveal his longing for his homeland.
Several psalms are believed to have originated during the exilic period of Jewish history.
Her research focuses on exilic experiences throughout ancient and modern history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country

Etymology

From Latin exilium (exile) + English -ic (adjectival suffix).
