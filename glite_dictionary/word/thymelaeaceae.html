Thymelaeaceae

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌmiːliˈeɪsiːi / (AmE), / θɪˌmiːlɪˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and herbs with tough bark, found especially in Australia and tropical Africa
a family of trees, shrubs, and herbs

Examples

The Thymelaeaceae include many species with medicinal properties.
Some members of the Thymelaeaceae produce fibers used for making paper.
Researchers found several new Thymelaeaceae species in tropical Africa.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Thymelaea.

A family of trees, shrubs, and herbs with tough bark, found primarily in Australia and tropical Africa.
family of plants with tough bark
medical

Examples

The Thymelaeaceae are known for their fibrous bark, used in local crafts.
Many species in the Thymelaeaceae family produce toxic compounds.
Researchers discovered several new plants belonging to the Thymelaeaceae in western Australia.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Thymelaea + -aceae, the standard ending for plant family names.
