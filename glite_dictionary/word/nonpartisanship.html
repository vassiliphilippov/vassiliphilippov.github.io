nonpartisanship

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˌpɑr.tɪˈzæn.ʃɪp / (AmE), / nɒnˌpɑː.tɪˈzæn.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the quality of not supporting or being influenced by any political party, group, or side; impartiality
impartiality or neutrality in opinions

Examples

The committee valued nonpartisanship in all of its decisions.
Journalists are expected to demonstrate nonpartisanship when reporting on political issues.
Her nonpartisanship helped build trust among all members of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: partisanship - the strong support of one side or party

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'partisan' + '-ship' (state or condition).
