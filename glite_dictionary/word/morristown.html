Morristown

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔːrɪsˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒrɪsˌtaʊn / (BrE)
A town in northern New Jersey, USA, known as the site where the Continental Army spent two winters during the American Revolutionary War.
town in northern New Jersey, USA
american

Examples

The Continental Army endured a harsh winter in Morristown during the Revolution.
Tourists visit Morristown to see historical sites related to George Washington.
Morristown has preserved its heritage through museums and colonial architecture.

Etymology

Named after Lewis Morris, a colonial governor of New Jersey.
