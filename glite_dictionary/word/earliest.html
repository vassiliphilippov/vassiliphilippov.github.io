earliest

1. ADV

/ ˈɜrliɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːliɪst / (BrE)
At the soonest possible time; with the least delay.
most immediate; soonest

Examples

The earliest I can meet you is at 9 a.m. tomorrow.
If you order now, the earliest delivery date is next Monday.
Please reply at your earliest convenience to confirm your availability.

Synset Examples

the soonest I can arrive is 3 P.M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soonest - at the soonest possible time; with the least delay
  • Antonym: latest - at the greatest delay or at the last possible time

2. ADJ

/ ˈɜːrliɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːliɪst / (BrE)
superlative form of 'early': occurring or done before all others in time; first in order or appearance.
superlative of 'early'; first in time

Examples

The earliest known fossils date back over three billion years.
She took the earliest train to avoid the morning rush.
Of all the entries, his was the earliest to arrive.

Synset Examples

a fashion popular in earlier times
his earlier work reflects the influence of his teacher
Verdi's earliest and most raucous opera

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: early - occurring before the expected time; near the beginning
  • Degree Lower: earlier - comparative form of 'early'; more early than another
