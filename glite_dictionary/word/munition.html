munition

1. NOUN

/ mjuːˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuːˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
military weapons, ammunition, and equipment used in war.
military weapons and supplies

Examples

The army transported large quantities of munition to the front lines.
They discovered stockpiles of munition hidden in underground bunkers.
Strict controls were placed on the export of munition to conflict zones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammunition - Projectiles and explosives fired from weapons

Etymology

From French 'munition', from Latin 'munitio' (fortification).

2. NOUN

/ mjuˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
military weapons and equipment used in fighting or warfare, considered collectively
military weapons and equipment

Examples

The government increased its spending on munitions to prepare for possible conflict.
Factories were converted to produce munitions during the war effort.
Strict controls were imposed on the export of munitions to unstable regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armament - military weapons and equipment
  • Synonym: ammunition - supply of bullets, shells, and other explosive items for weapons

Etymology

From Middle French *munition* (fortification, military supplies), from Latin *munire* (to fortify).
