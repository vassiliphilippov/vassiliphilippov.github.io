hurricane

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜːr.ɪ.keɪn / (AmE), / ˈhʌr.ɪ.kən / (BrE)
A violent tropical storm with very strong winds and heavy rain, typically occurring in the Atlantic Ocean and northeastern Pacific.
violent tropical storm

Examples

The coastal city was devastated by a hurricane last year.
Many residents evacuated as the hurricane approached the shoreline.
The hurricane caused widespread flooding and power outages across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhoon - a tropical cyclone occurring in the northwestern Pacific Ocean
  • Synonym: cyclone - a strong wind system with a spiral arrangement; general term for such storms

Etymology

From Spanish huracán, from Taino hurakán (god of the storm).
