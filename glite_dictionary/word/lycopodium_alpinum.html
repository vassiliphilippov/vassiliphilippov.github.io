Lycopodium alpinum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpoʊ.di.əm ælˈpaɪ.nəm / (AmE), / laɪ.kəˈpəʊ.di.əm ælˈpiː.nəm / (BrE)
a species of club moss found throughout northern latitudes in the Northern Hemisphere
species of club moss

Examples

Lycopodium alpinum is commonly found in cold, mountainous regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
Botanists collected samples of Lycopodium alpinum during their arctic expedition.
Some traditional medicines use Lycopodium alpinum for its purported healing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club moss - a group of primitive, spore-producing plants

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Lycopodium' from Greek 'lycos' (wolf) + 'podion' (little foot); 'alpinum' from Latin 'alpinus' (alpine).
