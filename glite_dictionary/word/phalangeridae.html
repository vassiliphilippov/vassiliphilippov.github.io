Phalangeridae

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlændʒəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˌlandʒəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marsupial mammals including the phalangers, cuscuses, and related species native to Australasia.
family of marsupial mammals (phalangers and cuscuses)

Examples

The family Phalangeridae includes a wide variety of arboreal marsupials found in Australasia.
Researchers study Phalangeridae to understand the evolution of marsupial mammals.
Some members of the Phalangeridae family are commonly known as cuscuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalanger - a marsupial mammal of the family Phalangeridae

Etymology

From modern Latin Phalangeridae, from 'phalanger' (from Greek phalangion 'spider's web' or 'knitted toe').

2. NOUN

/ fəˌlændʒəˈraɪdi / (AmE), / fəˌlandʒəˈrɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of marsupials including phalangers and cuscuses, native to Australia and surrounding regions
family of marsupials (phalangers, cuscuses)

Examples

The Phalangeridae family includes a variety of arboreal marsupials found in Australasia.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Phalangeridae during their expedition to Papua New Guinea.
Some members of the Phalangeridae are commonly known as cuscuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalanger - arboreal marsupial of the Phalangeridae family

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from genus name Phalanger + family suffix -idae.
