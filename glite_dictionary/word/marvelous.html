marvelous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrvələs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvələs / (BrE)
Very wonderful, astonishing, or resembling a miracle; causing great wonder or surprise.
astonishing, like a miracle

Examples

The recovery of the patient was marvelous and amazed all the doctors.
She gave a marvelous performance that seemed almost magical.
It was a marvelous coincidence that saved the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miraculous - having the nature of a miracle; extraordinary
  • Antonym: ordinary - normal; lacking anything unusual or remarkable

Etymology

From Middle English *merveillous*, from Old French *merveillos*, from *merveille* (marvel) + *-ous*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrvələs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvələs / (BrE)
Extremely good, excellent, or causing great wonder; extraordinary.
extremely good or extraordinary

Examples

The view from the mountain was absolutely marvelous, taking her breath away.
He gave a marvelous performance that captivated the entire audience.
They had a marvelous time on their vacation in Greece.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonderful - Extremely good or delightful; marvelous.
  • Antonym: terrible - Extremely bad or unpleasant; the opposite of marvelous.
  • Synonym: amazing - Causing great surprise or wonder; astonishing.

Etymology

From Middle English marvelous, from Old French merveillos, from merveille (wonder).
