Francois Rabelais

1. PROPN

/ fʀɑ̃ˈswa ɹæbəˌleɪ / (AmE), / fʀɑ̃ˈswa ˈræbələ / (BrE)
(1494–1553) French writer and physician known for his satirical works criticizing medieval scholasticism, especially the series 'Gargantua and Pantagruel'.
French satirical writer (1494–1553)

Examples

Scholars still study the works of Francois Rabelais for their rich humor and biting social commentary.
Francois Rabelais was influential in shaping the course of Renaissance literature with his daring satires.
The imaginative tales written by Francois Rabelais continue to captivate readers centuries after his death.

Etymology

From French François Rabelais, the author's given name and surname.
