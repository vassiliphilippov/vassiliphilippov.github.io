serene

1. ADJ

/ səˈrin / (AmE), / sɪˈriːn / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) completely clear and calm; free from clouds, storms, or disturbance.
completely clear and calm (sky, weather)

Examples

After the storm, the serene afternoon sky dazzled everyone with its pale blue hue.
We enjoyed a picnic under serene skies at the lakeside park.
The pilot appreciated the serene weather, making takeoff smooth and pleasant.

Synset Examples

serene skies and a bright blue sea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cloudy - (of the sky or weather) covered with clouds; not clear

Etymology

From Latin 'serenus' (clear, calm, unclouded).

2. ADJ

/ səˈrin / (AmE), / sɪˈriːn / (BrE)
calm, peaceful, and untroubled; not agitated or disturbed by emotions or events.
calm or peaceful; untroubled

Examples

She always remained serene in stressful situations, never raising her voice.
The serene waters of the lake reflected the early morning sky.
His face was serene, betraying none of the worries he felt inside.

Synset Examples

spoke in a calm voice
remained calm throughout the uproar
he remained serene in the midst of turbulence
a serene expression on her face
she became more tranquil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Synonym: tranquil - free from disturbance; calm
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From Latin serenus (clear, calm).
