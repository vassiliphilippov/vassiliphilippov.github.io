prying

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) showing too much interest in other people's private affairs, especially in a way that is annoying or offensive.
offensively curious; intrusive

Examples

She avoided her prying relatives who always asked personal questions about her life.
Journalists can sometimes be prying, digging into details best left private.
I caught a prying glance from the man across the street as I entered my house.

Synset Examples

curious about the neighbor's doings
he flipped through my letters in his nosy way
prying eyes
the snoopy neighbor watched us all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nosy - showing too much interest in other people's affairs

Etymology

Present participle of 'pry', from Middle English 'pryen', probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Offensively curious or nosy behavior; the act of inquiring into matters that do not concern one.
offensively nosy behavior

Examples

She resented his constant prying into her personal affairs.
The letter was carefully hidden to avoid the prying of nosy neighbors.
Such blatant prying is not welcome in this office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusiveness - the quality or state of interrupting or intruding where one is not wanted
  • Degree Lower: curiosity - a desire to know or learn about anything, often related to inquisitiveness

Etymology

From the verb 'pry', meaning 'to inquire impertinently'; origin uncertain, possibly related to Middle English 'pryen'.
