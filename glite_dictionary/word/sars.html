SARS

1. NOUN

/ sɑrz / (AmE), / sɑːz / (BrE)
a severe acute respiratory disease marked by fever, coughing, and breathing difficulties, first identified in China in 2003 and potentially fatal
severe acute respiratory syndrome; contagious lung disease
medical

Examples

Several countries implemented strict measures to contain the spread of SARS after the initial outbreak in 2003.
Doctors were on high alert when new cases of SARS appeared in the hospital.
The rapid transmission of SARS prompted urgent international cooperation among health agencies.

Etymology

Acronym for Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome.
