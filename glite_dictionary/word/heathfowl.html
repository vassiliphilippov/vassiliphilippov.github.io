heathfowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiθˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ˈhiːθˌfaʊl / (BrE)
A large northern European grouse with black plumage and a lyre-shaped tail, also known as the black grouse.
large northern European grouse (black grouse)

Examples

The male heathfowl is known for its striking black plumage and distinctive tail feathers.
During spring, the heathfowl can be seen performing elaborate courtship displays on the moors.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the threatened habitats of the heathfowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large Eurasian grouse, also called blackcock or heathfowl

Etymology

From 'heath' (open uncultivated land) and 'fowl' (bird).
