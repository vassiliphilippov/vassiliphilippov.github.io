Eriocaulon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈkoʊlɒn / (AmE), / ˌɛri.əˈkɔːlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of rushlike aquatic or marginal perennial plants, usually found in shallow waters of acidic lakes, pools, and bogs.
genus of aquatic or bog plants

Examples

The Eriocaulon species thrive in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and shallow lakes.
Botanists conducted a survey of Eriocaulon in the national park to assess their diversity.
Certain Eriocaulon are cultivated for use in aquatic gardens due to their unique appearance.

Etymology

From Greek erion (wool) + kaulos (stem), referring to some species' woolly stems.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈkoʊlɒn / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪ.əˈkɔːlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of rushlike aquatic or marginal perennial plants, usually found in shallow waters of acidic lakes, pools, and bogs.
genus of aquatic or bog perennials

Examples

The Eriocaulon species thrive in the acidic waters of northern bogs.
Botanists discovered a rare Eriocaulon growing in the wetland reserve.
Many aquatic gardens feature Eriocaulon for its unique tufted appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'kaulos' (stalk), referring to the woolly heads of some species.
