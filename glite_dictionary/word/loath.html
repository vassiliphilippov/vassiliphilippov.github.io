loath

1. ADJ

/ loʊθ / (AmE), / ləʊθ / (BrE)
reluctant or unwilling to do something; strongly opposed (usually followed by 'to').
reluctant or unwilling

Examples

She was loath to admit her mistake in front of everyone.
Many employees are loath to embrace sudden changes in the workplace.
He seemed loath to leave the comfort of his home on a rainy day.

Synset Examples

antipathetic to new ideas
averse to taking risks
loath to go on such short notice
clearly indisposed to grant their request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: averse - having a strong dislike of or opposition to something
  • Antonym: eager - strongly wanting to do or have something

Etymology

Old English 'lāth' (hostile, hateful); related to Old Norse 'leiðr' (hateful).

2. ADJ

/ loʊθ / (AmE), / l ə ʊ θ / (BrE)
reluctant or unwilling to do something; disinclined
reluctant; unwilling

Examples

She was loath to share personal details with strangers.
Politicians are often loath to admit their mistakes in public.
He is loath to leave his hometown, even for a promising job.

Synset Examples

a reluctant smile
loath to admit a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling and hesitant; disinclined
  • Antonym: eager - strongly wanting to do or achieve something; keen

Etymology

From Old English *lǣþ* (hostile, hateful).
