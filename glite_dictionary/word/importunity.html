importunity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɔːrˈtuː.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈtjuː.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being persistent to the point of annoyance or being unreasonably demanding.
persistent or unreasonable demanding

Examples

The importunity of the salespeople made it impossible to shop in peace.
She eventually gave in to the importunity of the reporters, reluctantly answering their questions.
His importunity began to test the patience of everyone in the office.

Synset Examples

This situation might explain why Lilburne, in spite of his harsh language and challenges to the court's authority, not to mention his importunateness, was not cited for contempt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistence - the act of demanding something forcefully
  • Antonym: forbearance - patient self-control; restraint and tolerance

Etymology

From Old French importunité, from Latin importunitas (unfitness, unreasonableness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɔːrˈtuː.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈtjuː.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
persistent and sometimes annoying requests or demands; insistent solicitation or entreaty.
persistent and insistent requesting

Examples

Despite her friend's importunity, she refused to change her mind.
The importunity of his phone calls eventually wore me down.
He was annoyed by the importunity of the street vendors.

Synset Examples

his importunity left me no alternative but to agree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plea - an earnest request or appeal
  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request, typically signed by many people
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination

Etymology

From Old French importunité, from Latin importunitas (inconvenience), from importunus (inconvenient, unsuitable).
