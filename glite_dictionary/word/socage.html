socage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.kɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɒ.kɪdʒ / (BrE)
A type of land tenure in which a tenant held land in return for agricultural service or payment of rent, rather than for military duty.
land tenure by agricultural service or rent
archaic legal

Examples

Under the system of socage, peasants paid rent to the lord instead of performing military service.
The manor lands were mostly held in socage during the late Middle Ages.
His ancestors acquired their estate by socage, making them free from military obligations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyhold - a form of customary land tenure under the manor system
  • Degree Higher: feudalism - the medieval European social system based on holding land in exchange for service

Etymology

From Old French 'soca' (ploughshare), of Germanic origin, referring to the agricultural nature of the service.
