to testify

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛstɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to serve as evidence for something; to show or indicate the truth or existence of something
serve as evidence; indicate or show

Examples

The sudden drop in temperature testifies to the arrival of winter.
The endurance of ancient monuments testifies to the skill of past civilizations.
Her generous acts testify to her compassion and kindness.

Synset Examples

The blood test showed that he was the father
Her behavior testified to her incompetence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prove - to demonstrate the truth of something by evidence or argument
  • Antonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true or incorrect

Etymology

From Latin 'testificari', from 'testis' (witness) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make a formal statement of what is true, especially in a court of law
give evidence in court

Examples

The witness was asked to testify about what he saw on the night of the accident.
Several experts will testify for the prosecution in tomorrow's session.
She testified under oath that she had never seen the defendant before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bear witness - to give or serve as evidence; to testify
  • Antonym: to recant - to withdraw a statement or belief formally

Etymology

From Latin testificari, from testis (witness) + facere (to make).

Synset POS: v
