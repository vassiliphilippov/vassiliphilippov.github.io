Massorete

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsəˌrit / (AmE), / ˈmasəˌriːt / (BrE)
a Jewish scholar, especially a scribe, who contributed to the Masorah, the body of tradition relating to the correct textual reading of the Hebrew scriptures
Jewish scholar of the Masorah

Examples

The Massorete diligently annotated the margins of the Hebrew Bible.
Many Massoretes lived during the early medieval period in the Middle East.
Without the work of a Massorete, much of the textual tradition would have been lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masorah - the body of Jewish tradition relating to the correct textual reading of the Hebrew scriptures

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Massoreta, from Hebrew masoreth (tradition).
