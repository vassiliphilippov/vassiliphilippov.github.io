sluiceway

1. NOUN

/ ˈsluːsˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsluːs.weɪ / (BrE)
A channel or passage for carrying a rapid flow of water, especially one controlled by a sluicegate.
water channel controlled by a sluicegate

Examples

The engineers inspected the sluiceway for signs of damage after the heavy rains.
Water gushed through the sluiceway as the dam operators raised the gate.
Sediment build-up in the sluiceway can impede the efficient flow of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - A sliding gate or other device for controlling the flow of water.
  • Synonym: spillway - A passage for surplus water to flow over or around a dam.

Etymology

From 'sluice' (from Old French escluse, from Latin exclusa) + 'way'.
