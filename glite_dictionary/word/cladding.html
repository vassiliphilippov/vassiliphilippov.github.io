cladding

1. NOUN

/ ˈklædɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklædɪŋ / (BrE)
a protective layer of material that is fixed to the outside of a building to protect it or improve its appearance
protective exterior covering for buildings

Examples

The building's cladding was designed to withstand extreme weather conditions.
Concerns were raised about the safety of the cladding used on high-rise towers.
Architects selected a metal cladding to give the structure a modern look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siding - material attached to the exterior side of a wall

Etymology

From the verb 'clad', meaning to cover or dress, + -ing suffix.
