to hot-dog

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑt dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɒt dɒɡ / (BrE)
to perform showy or intricate maneuvers, especially while skiing or in sports, to impress others
perform showy maneuvers in sports
informal american

Examples

The skier loves to hot-dog down the slopes, twisting and turning for the crowd.
He was hot-dogging so much during the competition that he almost fell.
Some athletes prefer not to hot-dog and focus on clean technique instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'hot dog' (the food), later used figuratively in American English for showy athletic display, especially from the 1960s-70s.
