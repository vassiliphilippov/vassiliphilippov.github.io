E.S.P.

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː ˌɛs ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌɛs ˈpiː / (BrE)
the supposed ability to gain information about an object, person, or event through means other than the known senses; extrasensory perception
supposed ability to perceive beyond normal senses

Examples

Many people claim to have E.S.P. and believe they can sense things others cannot.
The psychic was famous for her alleged E.S.P. abilities during investigations.
Scientific studies have tried to prove the existence of E.S.P., but with little success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clairvoyance - supposed ability to gain information about an object or event beyond normal sensory contact
  • Synonym: telepathy - supposed communication of thoughts or ideas without using the known senses

Etymology

Abbreviation of extrasensory perception.
