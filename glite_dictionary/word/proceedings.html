proceedings

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsiːdɪŋz / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdɪŋz / (BrE)
an official written record of what took place at a meeting or conference
official meeting record

Examples

The proceedings from last week's board meeting were distributed to all the members.
She referred to the proceedings to confirm the committee's decision on the project.
A copy of the proceedings is kept on file for future reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minutes - the written record of what was said and decided at a meeting

Etymology

From Latin 'procedere' (to go forward), via Old French 'proceder'.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈsiːdɪŋz / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdɪŋz / (BrE)
(law) The formal steps or actions taken as part of a legal process; the process of conducting judicial business, especially in court.
formal steps in a legal process
legal

Examples

The court proceedings are scheduled to begin next Monday.
All proceedings were recorded and reviewed by the judge.
Her lawyer explained every stage of the proceedings to her in detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: action - a legal process started by one party against another
  • Synonym: lawsuit - a claim or dispute brought to a law court for adjudication
  • Degree Lower: investigation - the action of investigating something, especially as part of a legal process

Etymology

From 'proceed', from Latin 'procedere' (to go forward).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
