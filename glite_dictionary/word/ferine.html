ferine

1. ADJ

/ fəˈraɪn / (AmE), / fəˈriːn / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) wild, savage, or untamed, especially in a menacing way.
wild or savage; menacing

Examples

The explorers stumbled upon a ferine tribe deep in the jungle.
Her ferine gaze made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
Stories often depict wolves as ferine creatures roaming the night.

Synset Examples

a pack of feral dogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feral - in a wild state, especially after escape from captivity
  • Antonym: tame - domesticated; not wild or savage

Etymology

From Latin 'ferinus' (of a wild animal), from 'ferus' (wild).
