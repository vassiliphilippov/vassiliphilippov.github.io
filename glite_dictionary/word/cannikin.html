cannikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈkænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a small can or drinking vessel, especially an old-fashioned or decorative one
small can or drinking vessel
archaic

Examples

The archaeologist found a delicate cannikin among the ruins of the old tavern.
She sipped her wine from a silver cannikin engraved with a family crest.
In his tale, the hero received a magic cannikin as a reward for his bravery.

Etymology

Diminutive of 'can', with the suffix '-kin' (indicating smallness) from Middle Dutch or Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈkænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a small wooden bucket or drinking vessel, especially one made of staves and hoops
small wooden bucket or vessel
archaic

Examples

The old sailor drank rum from his cannikin each evening.
She found a dusty cannikin on the shelf of the ancient kitchen.
A cannikin of water was passed around among the workers at noon.

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'canni' (variant of 'can' meaning container), with the diminutive suffix '-kin.'
