to cohere

1. VERB

/ koʊˈhɪr / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪə / (BrE)
to stick or hold together as a unified whole; to remain closely connected
stick or hold together; be united

Examples

The grains of rice cohere to form a solid block in sushi.
Without clear instructions, the team failed to cohere as a group.
The notes in the melody cohere beautifully, creating a harmonious effect.

Synset Examples

The dress clings to her body
The label stuck to the box
The sushi rice grains cohere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick firmly to a surface or substance

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerēre' (to stick together), from 'co-' (together) + 'haerēre' (to stick).

2. VERB

/ koʊˈhɪr / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪə / (BrE)
to cause the parts of something to form a united, orderly, and consistent whole
form a unified whole

Examples

The museum exhibition was carefully arranged to cohere diverse artworks into a single theme.
Policies that cohere the different departments are crucial for a successful company.
He struggled to cohere his various notes into a coherent report.

Synset Examples

Religion can cohere social groups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together for a common purpose or action
  • Antonym: fragment - to break or cause something to break into pieces

Etymology

From Latin cohaerēre (to stick together), from co- (together) + haerēre (to stick).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02759517-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ koʊˈhɪr / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪə / (BrE)
to be logically or aesthetically consistent, with all parts or elements fitting together in a unified way
be logically or aesthetically consistent

Examples

His arguments did not cohere into a single convincing theory.
The different sections of the novel cohere beautifully to create a unified story.
A successful design must cohere both aesthetically and functionally.

Synset Examples

the principles by which societies cohere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick fast to a surface or substance
  • Antonym: fragment - to break or separate into pieces, losing unity

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerere' (to stick together), from 'co-' (together) + 'haerere' (to stick).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
