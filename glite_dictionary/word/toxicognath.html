toxicognath

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪkoʊˌnæθ / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪkəʊˌnaθ / (BrE)
either of a pair of poison fangs formed by the modified front pair of legs in a centipede
poison fang of a centipede

Examples

The centipede injects venom into its prey using its toxicognaths.
Scientists studied the structure of the toxicognath to understand how centipedes hunt.
Damage to the toxicognath can affect the centipede's ability to defend itself.

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poison) + gnathos (jaw).
