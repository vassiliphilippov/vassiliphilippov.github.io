landswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlændzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who lives and works on land, especially as distinct from those who work at sea.
woman who lives and works on land

Examples

As a proud landswoman, she took great care of her family farm.
The documentary followed a landswoman balancing rural work and community life.
Unlike her seafaring relatives, she considered herself a true landswoman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landsman - a person who lives and works on land, not at sea
  • Antonym: seawoman - a female sailor or woman who works at sea

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'woman', modeled on 'landsman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlændzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is inexperienced as a sailor, especially one on her first voyage.
inexperienced female sailor

Examples

In the crew, she was the only landswoman and faced many new challenges at sea.
The captain gave the landswoman extra instruction since it was her first voyage.
Though a landswoman now, she quickly learned the ropes and gained the crew's respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landsman - an inexperienced sailor, especially a man on his first voyage

Etymology

Formed from 'land' (as in 'landsman') + 'woman'.
