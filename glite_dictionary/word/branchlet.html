branchlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræntʃˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːntʃ.lɪt / (BrE)
a small branch or subdivision of a branch, typically referring to a terminal division produced in the current or previous year
small subdivision of a branch

Examples

Several branchlets sprouted from the main stem of the young tree.
The botanist examined the delicate branchlets for signs of new growth.
In spring, tiny buds appear at the ends of each branchlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twig - a small, thin terminal branch of a tree or shrub

Etymology

From 'branch' + diminutive suffix '-let'.
