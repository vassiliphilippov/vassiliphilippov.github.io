necrophagy

1. NOUN

/ nəˈkrɑːfədʒi / (AmE), / nɛˈkrɒfədʒi / (BrE)
the act of feeding on dead bodies or carrion, especially by animals or insects
feeding on dead bodies or carrion

Examples

Many beetles and flies practice necrophagy as part of the natural decomposition process.
The study of necrophagy is important in understanding nutrient cycles in ecosystems.
Vultures are known for their specialized adaptation to necrophagy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scavenging - eating discarded or dead organic matter

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body) + -phagy, from phagein (to eat).
