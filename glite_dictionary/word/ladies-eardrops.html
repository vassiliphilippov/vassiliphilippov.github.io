ladies'-eardrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˈɪrˌdrɑps / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˈɪəˌdrɒps / (BrE)
An erect or climbing shrub of Brazil, known for its deep pink to red, pendulous flowers; often refers to species of the genus Fuchsia.
Brazilian shrub with pink or red flowers (Fuchsia)

Examples

The vibrant ladies'-eardrops in her garden bloomed all summer long.
Botanists collected specimens of ladies'-eardrops during their expedition to Brazil.
Hummingbirds are attracted to the bright red flowers of ladies'-eardrops.

Etymology

Refers to the flower's resemblance to ornate ear pendants ('eardrops') traditionally worn by women ('ladies').
