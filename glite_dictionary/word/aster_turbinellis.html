Aster turbinellis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər tɜr.bɪˈnɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˈæstər tɜː.bɪˈnɛlɪs / (BrE)
A violet-flowered perennial aster native to the central United States, characterized by solitary flower heads.
violet-flowered perennial aster of central USA

Examples

The prairie was dotted with blooming Aster turbinellis in late summer.
Gardeners value Aster turbinellis for its attractive violet flowers and resilience.
Researchers conducted a study on the growth habits of Aster turbinellis in different soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin: *Aster* (star) and *turbinellis* (little whirl or top), referring to the flower's shape.
