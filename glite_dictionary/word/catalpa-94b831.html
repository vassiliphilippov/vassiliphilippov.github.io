Catalpa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtæl.pə / (AmE), / kəˈtæl.pə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the family Bignoniaceae, known for their large, showy flowers and long, slender seed pods.
genus of flowering trees with large flowers and long pods

Examples

The Catalpa tree is admired for its beautiful white blossoms in the spring.
Botanists studied several species within the Catalpa genus to understand their growth habits.
A large Catalpa was planted in the park for its striking flowers and shade.

Etymology

From the Muscogee (Creek) word *kutálpa* (winged head, referring to the seeds).
