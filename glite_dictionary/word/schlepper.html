schlepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃlɛpər / (AmE), / ˈʃlɛpə / (BrE)
an awkward, clumsy, or foolish person; someone who moves or carries things with difficulty
awkward or clumsy person

Examples

Don’t be such a schlepper—you’re always dropping things and tripping over your own feet.
He’s a real schlepper, always bumbling around and making a mess of things.
Nobody wants to hire a schlepper when there’s real work to be done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clutz - a clumsy or awkward person

Etymology

From Yiddish *shleper* (one who drags), from German *schleppen* (to drag or haul).
