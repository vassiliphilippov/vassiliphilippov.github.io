Pan troglodytes troglodytes

1. NOUN

/ pæn troʊˈɡlɒdaɪtiːz troʊˈɡlɒdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / pan trəˈɡlɒdɪtiːz trəˈɡlɒdɪtiːz / (BrE)
A subspecies of the common chimpanzee native to central Africa, characterized by its black face and closely related to eastern chimpanzees.
central African chimpanzee subspecies

Examples

The Pan troglodytes troglodytes population has declined due to habitat loss and poaching.
Researchers observed social behaviors unique to Pan troglodytes troglodytes in Gabon's forests.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the Pan troglodytes troglodytes in central Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - large African ape, Pan troglodytes

Etymology

From Latin: genus Pan (the chimpanzees); species and subspecies troglodytes, meaning 'cave-dweller.'
