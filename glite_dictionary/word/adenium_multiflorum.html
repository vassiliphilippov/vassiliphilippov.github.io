Adenium multiflorum

1. NOUN

/ əˈdiːniəm ˌmʌl.tɪˈflɔːr.əm / (AmE), / əˈdiːni.əm ˌmʌl.tɪˈflɔː.rəm / (BrE)
A species of South African shrub with a swollen, succulent stem, producing showy pink and white flowers after shedding its leaves; widely grown as an ornamental plant in tropical regions.
flowering South African ornamental shrub

Examples

The Adenium multiflorum brightened the garden with its vibrant pink and white blossoms.
Many gardeners prize Adenium multiflorum for its striking flowers and distinctive, swollen stem.
After losing its leaves, Adenium multiflorum produces a dramatic display of blooms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Adenium' genus name, and 'multiflorum' meaning 'many-flowered'.
