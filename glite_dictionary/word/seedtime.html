seedtime

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌdtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈsiːdtaɪm / (BrE)
the period in the year when seeds should be sown or planted
time for sowing seeds

Examples

The farmer carefully planned for seedtime in order to maximize his harvest.
Spring is traditionally considered seedtime for cereals and vegetables in temperate regions.
Missing seedtime can lead to poor crop yields later in the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harvest - the time when crops are gathered
  • Synonym: planting season - the period suitable for planting crops

Etymology

From Old English sædtīma, combining 'seed' and 'time'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːd.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈsiːd.taɪm / (BrE)
A period when something new begins to develop or is initiated; a time of new growth or opportunity.
period of new development

Examples

The reforms introduced during the country's seedtime shaped its future for decades.
College can be a seedtime for developing one's passions and interests.
The turn of the century was a seedtime for technological innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring - the season of new growth; often used metaphorically for new beginnings

Etymology

From seed + time, patterned after the agricultural period when seeds are sown.
