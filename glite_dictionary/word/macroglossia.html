macroglossia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈɡlɑsiə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈɡlɒsɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has an abnormally large tongue, often present from birth and associated with certain medical syndromes
abnormally large tongue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with macroglossia after exhibiting difficulty with speech and swallowing.
Several genetic conditions, including Down’s syndrome, may present with macroglossia.
Surgery may be considered in severe cases of macroglossia to improve function and appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'glōssa' (tongue).
