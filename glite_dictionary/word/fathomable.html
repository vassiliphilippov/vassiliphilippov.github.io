fathomable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæð.ə.mə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfað.ə.mə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a concept or situation) able to be understood or comprehended; intelligible.
able to be understood; comprehensible

Examples

The reason behind his sudden departure was completely fathomable to everyone in the office.
She explained the complex theory in such a way that it became fathomable even to those unfamiliar with the subject.
While some poems are mysterious, this one is quite fathomable and open to interpretation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensible - able to be understood; intelligible
  • Antonym: unfathomable - impossible to understand; incomprehensible

Etymology

From 'fathom' (to measure the depth of water) + '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of water or depths) capable of being measured or sounded to determine its depth.
able to be measured for depth

Examples

The river was surprisingly fathomable despite its dark, murky waters.
Only the harbor's fathomable channels allowed large ships to anchor safely.
Early explorers sought fathomable passages through the dangerous reefs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfathomable - impossible to measure the depth of; impossible to understand

Etymology

From 'fathom' (to measure depth) + '-able'. Originates from Old English 'fæthm' (embrace, a measure of length).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00998091-a
Synset POS: a
