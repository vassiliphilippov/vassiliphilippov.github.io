crystallisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a solid forms in which the atoms or molecules are highly structured in a crystal lattice
formation of crystals

Examples

The slow crystallisation of salt from seawater can be observed under the right conditions.
The process of crystallisation is crucial in the manufacture of high-quality sugar.
Impurities in the solution can interfere with the crystallisation of minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crystallization - American spelling of 'crystallisation'

Etymology

From French 'cristallisation', from 'cristal' (crystal) + '-isation'.
