retraction

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the act of pulling or drawing something back, especially a part of the body or a mechanical component
the act of drawing something back

Examples

The retraction of the landing gear is essential before takeoff.
The surgeon performed a careful retraction of the tissue during the operation.
Automatic retraction of the cable prevents it from tangling.

Synset Examples

the retraction of the landing gear
retraction of the foreskin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the act of withdrawing or pulling back

Etymology

From Latin 'retractio', from 'retrahere' meaning 'to draw back'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
an official statement saying that something you previously said or wrote is not true or correct; the act of withdrawing a previous statement or claim
withdrawal of a previous statement

Examples

The journalist issued a retraction after realizing her report contained several errors.
After his mistake was exposed, the scientist published a formal retraction in the journal.
The company demanded a public retraction of the false allegations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recantation - the public withdrawal of a former opinion or belief
  • Synonym: denial - the act of declaring something to be untrue
  • Antonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief

Etymology

From Latin 'retractio(n-)' meaning 'a drawing back', from 'retractare' (to draw back).
