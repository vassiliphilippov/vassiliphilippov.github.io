mineshaft

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnʃɑːft / (BrE)
a deep, narrow, vertical or sloping passageway underground, used for accessing or ventilating a mine
vertical or sloping mine passage

Examples

The old mineshaft was sealed off for safety reasons.
Rescuers worked tirelessly to reach the trapped miners deep in the mineshaft.
A ventilation system was installed to ensure fresh air reached every part of the mineshaft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adit - a nearly horizontal passage from the surface into a mine

Etymology

Formed from 'mine' (excavation for minerals) + 'shaft' (long, narrow passage or structure).
