panther

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænθər / (AmE), / ˈpænθə / (BrE)
a large wild cat with a spotted coat, found in tropical America, similar to a leopard; sometimes considered a member of the genus Felis
large spotted wild cat of tropical America

Examples

The South American panther is an elusive predator of the rainforest.
Scientists are working to protect the dwindling population of the panther in Central America.
He spotted a panther resting on a tree branch during his expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leopard - a large, spotted wild cat native to Africa and parts of Asia

Etymology

From Latin panthera, from Greek panthēr (spotted panther/leopard).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpænθər / (AmE), / ˈpænθə / (BrE)
a large wild cat found mainly in the Americas and Asia, often referring to a cougar, jaguar, or leopard, typically with a dark coat
large wild cat (cougar, jaguar, or leopard)

Examples

The panther moved silently through the dense jungle, hidden from sight.
Many people believe the black panther is a separate species, but it is just a color variant.
The wildlife sanctuary became famous after rescuing an injured panther from the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cougar - a large American wild cat (Puma concolor)
  • Synonym: jaguar - a large spotted cat (Panthera onca) of the Americas
  • Synonym: leopard - a large spotted wild cat (Panthera pardus) found in Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Greek 'panthēr' (leopard, panther), via Latin 'panthera'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpænθər / (AmE), / ˈpænθə / (BrE)
a leopard or jaguar, especially one with black fur due to a genetic variation; commonly refers to a black leopard in Asia and Africa, or a black jaguar in the Americas
large wild cat, often black

Examples

The panther moved silently through the dense jungle at dusk.
Scientists observed a rare panther in the mountains of southern India.
Legends about the mysterious panther have fascinated people for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leopard - large spotted wild cat found in Africa and Asia
  • Synonym: jaguar - large wild cat native to the Americas; sometimes called panther when black

Etymology

From Latin 'panthera', from Greek 'pánthēr', meaning 'leopard'.
