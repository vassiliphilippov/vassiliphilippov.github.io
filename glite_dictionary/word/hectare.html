hectare

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛkˌtɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɛktɛə / (BrE)
a unit of surface area equal to 10,000 square meters or 2.471 acres, used especially for measuring land.
unit of surface area (10,000 square meters)

Examples

The farm covers nearly fifty hectares of fertile land.
One hectare is equivalent to about 2.47 acres.
The forest reserve was expanded by 200 hectares last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acre - a unit of area equal to 4,047 square meters, used in the US and UK

Etymology

From French 'hectare', formed from Greek 'hekaton' (hundred) and 'are' (a metric unit of area).
