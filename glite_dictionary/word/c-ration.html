C-ration

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː ˌræʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌræʃ.ən / (BrE)
a type of canned food ration issued to United States military personnel, especially during field operations from World War II to the Vietnam War
canned U.S. military food ration

Examples

During the war, soldiers often complained about the taste of C-ration meals.
My grandfather still remembers opening a can of C-ration stew in the trenches.
We found an old C-ration tin while exploring the abandoned army base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: K-ration - an individual daily combat food ration supplied to U.S. troops
  • Synonym: field ration - a generic term for food supplied to military personnel in the field

Etymology

From 'C' for 'combat', indicating a type of ration; used by the U.S. Army.
