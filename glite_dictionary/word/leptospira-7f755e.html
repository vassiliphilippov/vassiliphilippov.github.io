leptospira

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛptəˈspaɪrə / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəˈspaɪrə / (BrE)
a genus of spiral-shaped bacteria, some species of which cause diseases such as Weil's disease and canicola fever in humans and animals
genus of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Leptospira can cause serious infections in both humans and animals.
The laboratory tests confirmed the presence of leptospira in the contaminated water sample.
Public health campaigns aim to raise awareness about diseases spread by leptospira.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'leptos' (thin) + Greek 'speira' (coil, spiral).
