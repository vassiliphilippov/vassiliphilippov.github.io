to transform

1. VERB

/ trænˈsfɔrm / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to change something significantly in form, appearance, or character
change in form, appearance, or nature

Examples

The new policies will transform the way we approach education.
Exposure to different cultures can transform your worldview.
They hope to transform the abandoned warehouse into a community center.

Synset Examples

transubstantiate one element into another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or use
  • Synonym: to alter - to make small changes to something
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state

Etymology

From Latin transformare (to change in shape) via Old French transformer.
