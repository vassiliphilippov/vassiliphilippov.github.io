Halocarpus bidwilli

1. PROPN

/ ˌhæloʊˈkɑrpəs bɪdˈwɪli / (AmE), / ˌhæl.əʊˈkɑː.pəs bɪdˈwɪli / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, known for its needle-like leaves and coniferous characteristics.
New Zealand coniferous shrub or tree

Examples

The Halocarpus bidwilli is found in alpine and subalpine regions of New Zealand.
Botanists studied the distribution of Halocarpus bidwilli in native forests.
The foliage of Halocarpus bidwilli is valued for its unusual texture and appearance.

Etymology

From genus name Halocarpus and species epithet honoring British botanist John Carne Bidwill.
