Kemal Ataturk

1. PROPN

/ kəˌmɑl əˈtɑːtɜrk / (AmE), / kɛˌmæl əˈtætɜːk / (BrE)
(1881–1938) The founder and first president of modern Turkey, who established a secular and nationalist state after abolishing the Ottoman caliphate.
founder and first president of modern Turkey

Examples

Kemal Ataturk introduced sweeping reforms to modernize Turkish society in the early twentieth century.
Many students learn about Kemal Ataturk as a key figure in world history classes.
As a result of Kemal Ataturk's efforts, Turkey adopted a secular system of government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mustafa Kemal - birth name of Kemal Ataturk

Etymology

From Turkish 'Atatürk' meaning 'Father of the Turks', a surname granted by the Turkish parliament.
