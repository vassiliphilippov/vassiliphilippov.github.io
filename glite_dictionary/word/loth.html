loth

1. ADJ

/ loʊθ / (AmE), / ləʊθ / (BrE)
reluctant or unwilling to do something; strongly opposed (usually followed by 'to')
reluctant; unwilling

Examples

She was loth to reveal the details of her private life.
Many people are loth to accept new technologies at first.
Though he was invited, John was loth to attend the party after the argument.

Synset Examples

antipathetic to new ideas
averse to taking risks
loath to go on such short notice
clearly indisposed to grant their request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loath - reluctant; unwilling (alternate spelling)
  • Antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something

Etymology

Old English lāð (hostile, hateful)

2. ADJ

/ loʊθ / (AmE), / ləʊθ / (BrE)
reluctant; unwilling to do something contrary to one's habits or preferences
reluctant; unwilling
archaic

Examples

He was loth to reveal the details of his plan to anyone.
Though loth to admit it, she knew he was right.
My grandfather is loth to try new foods, preferring traditional dishes.

Synset Examples

a reluctant smile
loath to admit a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling or hesitant to do something
  • Antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something

Etymology

Old English lāth (hostile, hateful, reluctant), related to Old Norse leiðr (hateful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02577165-s
Synset POS: s
