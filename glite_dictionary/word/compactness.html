compactness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpæktnəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpæktnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being closely and neatly packed together in space; the state of being crowded together
the state of being closely packed

Examples

The compactness of the city center makes it easy to walk everywhere.
Engineers admired the compactness of the machine's design, which saves space.
The compactness of the furniture allowed more people to fit into the small apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance
  • Antonym: spaciousness - the property of occupying a lot of space; roominess

Etymology

From 'compact', from Latin 'compactus' (joined, connected), plus the suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpækt.nəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpaktnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dense and closely packed together, especially within a solid material
density; closely packed quality of a solid

Examples

The compactness of the material makes it suitable for building heavy-duty structures.
Scientists measured the compactness of the rock to determine its porosity.
High compactness in soil often indicates low water penetration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'compactus', past participle of 'compingere' meaning 'to put together'.
