Tuatha De Danann

1. PROPN

/ ˌtuːəˌhɑː deɪ ˈdænən / (AmE), / ˌtuːəˌhɑː deɪ ˈdænən / (BrE)
A group of deities in Irish mythology, considered gods or demigods who ruled Ireland during the legendary Golden Age.
mythical Irish gods or demigods

Examples

The Tuatha De Danann were said to possess great magical abilities and wisdom.
Many legends recount the battles between the Tuatha De Danann and the invading Milesians.
Art and literature often depict the Tuatha De Danann as powerful figures in Irish mythology.

Etymology

From Old Irish Tuatha Dé Danann, meaning ‘the peoples of the goddess Danu’.
