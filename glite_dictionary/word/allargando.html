allargando

1. ADJ

/ ɑːˌlɑːrˈgɑːndoʊ / (AmE), / alˌlɑːˈɡændəʊ / (BrE)
(music) Gradually slowing down in tempo and broadening in style or manner.
gradually slowing and broadening (music)

Examples

The orchestra played the final bars allargando, giving the piece a grand and spacious conclusion.
The conductor motioned for the choir to sing the last line allargando to emphasize its emotional weight.
In this passage, notice how the tempo becomes allargando as the dynamics increase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritardando - (music) gradually slowing down in tempo
  • Antonym: accelerando - (music) gradually speeding up in tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'allargando', present participle of 'allargare' meaning 'to widen' or 'to broaden'.
