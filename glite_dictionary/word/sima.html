sima

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪmə / (AmE), / ˈsiːmə / (BrE)
the layer of the Earth's crust that forms the lower continuous part, rich in silicon and magnesium
lower layer of Earth's crust (rich in silicon and magnesium)

Examples

Geologists believe the sima underlies the continents as well as the ocean basins.
The sima has a higher density than the upper crustal layers.
In some regions, the sima is exposed due to tectonic shifts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sial - uppermost layer of the Earth's crust, rich in silicon and aluminum

Etymology

From 'silicon' and 'magnesium'; coined in geology for its elemental composition.
