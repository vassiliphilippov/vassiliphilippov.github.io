alcaptonuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.kæp.toʊˈnʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌalkæptəˈnjʊərɪə / (BrE)
A rare inherited metabolic disorder characterized by the accumulation of homogentisic acid, causing darkening of urine and pigmentation of connective tissues (ochronosis).
rare inherited metabolic disorder
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of alcaptonuria was confirmed after detecting dark-colored urine and joint pain in the patient.
Individuals with alcaptonuria often develop pigmentation of connective tissues over time.
Genetic testing is essential for confirming suspected cases of alcaptonuria.

Etymology

From alkapton (obsolete name for homogentisic acid) + -uria (urine condition).
