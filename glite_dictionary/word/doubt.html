doubt

1. NOUN

/ daʊt / (AmE), / daʊt / (BrE)
A feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction about something.
a feeling of uncertainty

Examples

She had a nagging doubt about the accuracy of the information.
Despite their reassurance, a doubt still lingered in his mind.
There is little doubt that the company will succeed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being completely sure or confident
  • Synonym: suspicion - a feeling that something may not be true or right

Etymology

From Old French 'doute', from Latin 'dubium', meaning 'doubt, hesitation'.

/ daʊt / (AmE), / daʊt / (BrE)
to feel uncertain about the truth, quality, or reliability of something; to lack confidence in
