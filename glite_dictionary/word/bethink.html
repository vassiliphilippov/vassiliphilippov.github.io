to bethink

1. VERB

/ bɪˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / bɪˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to think about or consider something carefully; to recall or remind oneself of something
consider or remember carefully
archaic

Examples

Before answering, he bethought himself of his earlier promise.
She bethought herself of a solution to the problem.
As he wandered the halls, he bethinking memories of his childhood.

Synset Examples

She bethought her of their predicament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remember - to bring to mind or think of again
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Old English 'beþencan', meaning 'to consider' or 'to think about'.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / bɪˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to cause oneself to consider or remember something; to recall or reflect upon (oneself).
cause oneself to consider or remember
archaic

Examples

She bethought herself of the promise she had made long ago.
He suddenly bethought himself that he had left the door unlocked.
Before replying, she bethought herself and chose her words carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remember - to recall something to mind
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Old English 'biþencan' (to consider, reflect, call to mind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02171205-v
Synset POS: v
