to cavort

1. VERB

/ kəˈvɔrt / (AmE), / kəˈvɔːt / (BrE)
to jump or move around in a lively, playful, or excited way
move or jump about excitedly

Examples

The puppies cavorted around the yard, chasing each other through the grass.
Children cavort in the fountain every summer afternoon in the city square.
Excited by the news, he cavorted across the living room with joy.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'curvet', from French 'courber' (to curve, bend).
