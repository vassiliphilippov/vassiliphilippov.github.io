Orthopristis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθəˈprɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈprɪstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grunts in the family Haemulidae, found along the Atlantic and Pacific coasts of the Americas.
a genus of grunts (fish) in Haemulidae

Examples

The genus Orthopristis includes several species of grunts found in both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
Researchers discovered a new member of Orthopristis near the coast of Peru.
Juvenile fish in the Orthopristis genus are often observed in shallow tropical waters.

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'pristis' (sawfish), used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrθəˈprɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈprɪstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Haemulidae, commonly known as grunts, found along the Atlantic and Pacific coasts of the Americas.
genus of marine grunts (fish)

Examples

The genus Orthopristis includes several species commonly referred to as grunts.
Fishermen along both coasts often encounter members of the Orthopristis genus.
In marine biology, Orthopristis is studied for its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'pristis' (saw, referring to a type of fish).
