Viola tricolor

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˌoʊlə ˈtraɪkələr / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə ˈtrɪkəlɔː / (BrE)
a small European wildflower, commonly called wild pansy or heartsease, from which most garden pansies are derived
a European wild pansy species

Examples

The delicate petals of the Viola tricolor add vibrant color to spring gardens.
Many cultivated pansies were originally bred from Viola tricolor.
Researchers study the medicinal properties of Viola tricolor in treating skin conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pansy - garden plant with variously colored flowers, derived from Viola tricolor

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'three-colored violet'.
