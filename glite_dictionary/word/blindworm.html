blindworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪndˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A small, slender, limbless burrowing amphibian of the order Gymnophiona, found in moist tropical soil.
slender limbless burrowing amphibian

Examples

The scientist carefully unearthed a blindworm while studying animals in the rainforest.
Blindworms are often mistaken for worms or snakes because of their appearance and burrowing habits.
Because a blindworm has no limbs, it moves through wet soil with ease.

Etymology

From Old English 'blindwyrm', meaning 'blind worm'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪndˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndwɜːm / (BrE)
a small, burrowing legless European lizard (Anguis fragilis) with tiny eyes, popularly believed to be blind
a small legless European lizard

Examples

The gardener spotted a blindworm moving slowly under a pile of leaves.
Contrary to its name, the blindworm is not actually blind but has very small eyes.
Many people mistake the blindworm for a snake, but it is actually a legless lizard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowworm - a legless lizard of the same species as the blindworm

Etymology

Old English *blindwyrm* (blind snake or worm), from 'blind' + 'worm'.
