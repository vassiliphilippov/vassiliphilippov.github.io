numerical

1. ADJ

/ nuˈmɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / njuˈmɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to numbers, or to the ability to work with numbers; expressed or measured in numbers.
relating to numbers or numeric ability

Examples

The course requires strong numerical skills to solve complex problems.
Her job involves analyzing large amounts of numerical data every day.
Applicants must demonstrate numerical aptitude through an online assessment.

Synset Examples

tests for rating numerical aptitude
a mathematical whiz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematical - relating to mathematics; involving numbers or calculations

Etymology

From Latin numerus (number) + -ical (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02238540-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ nuˈmɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / njuˈmɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or expressed as numbers; involving or denoting numbers.
relating to numbers

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ nuˈmɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / njuːˈmɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
expressed, measured, or involving numbers rather than words or qualitative descriptions
relating to numbers

Examples

The report provided a detailed numerical analysis of the company's profits.
They recorded the survey results in both graphical and numerical formats.
A numerical value was assigned to each response in the experiment.

Synset Examples

numerical value
the numerical superiority of the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantitative - relating to the quantity or amount of something
  • Antonym: qualitative - relating to, measuring, or measured by the quality of something rather than its quantity

Etymology

From Latin numerus (number) + English -ical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01921967-s
Synset POS: s
