yam

1. NOUN

/ jæm / (AmE), / jæm / (BrE)
A starchy edible tuber of various climbing plants of the genus Dioscorea, widely grown in tropical and subtropical regions.
starchy edible tuber

Examples

She cooked a delicious curry using fresh yam and spices.
At the market, I bought several yams to make a hearty stew.
After harvesting the yam, the farmers prepared it for drying.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet potato - sweet-tasting root vegetable, often confused with yam

Etymology

From Portuguese 'inhame', from West African languages (e.g., Fulani 'nyami' meaning 'to eat').

2. NOUN

/ jæm / (AmE), / jæm / (BrE)
A starchy edible tuber, or any of several tropical climbing plants of the genus Dioscorea that produce such tubers, widely grown in warm regions.
tropical plant with edible tuber

Examples

The farmer dug up several yams for tonight's dinner.
In many cultures, yam is a staple food enjoyed in a variety of dishes.
African yams can grow to impressive sizes, sometimes reaching up to five feet in length.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet potato - an edible tuber sometimes confused with yam, from the Convolvulaceae family

Etymology

From Portuguese inhame, likely from West African languages (cf. Fulani nyami, meaning 'to eat').

3. NOUN

/ jæm / (AmE), / jæm / (BrE)
A type of sweet potato with deep orange flesh that remains moist when baked, commonly found in North American supermarkets.
a sweet potato with orange flesh, moist when baked

Examples

She baked a yam for dinner and served it with butter and cinnamon.
At the grocery store, I can never tell the difference between a yam and a sweet potato.
The recipe calls for one large yam, peeled and cut into cubes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet potato - a root vegetable with orange flesh
  • Antonym: yam (true yam) - the starchy tuber from plants of the genus Dioscorea

Etymology

From Portuguese 'inhame', from West African languages (e.g., Fulani 'nyami', meaning 'to eat'), though this sense refers especially to usage in North America for a sweet potato variety.

4. NOUN

/ jæm / (AmE), / jam / (BrE)
a starchy tuberous root of tropical climbing plants of the genus Dioscorea, widely grown for food in many parts of the world
starchy tropical tuberous root

Examples

I bought a yam from the market to cook for dinner.
In West Africa, the yam is a staple food and features in many traditional dishes.
The yam harvest festival is celebrated every year with music and dancing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet potato - a similar but botanically distinct starchy root vegetable, often confused with yam
  • Degree Lower: potato - common edible starchy tuber, Solanum tuberosum

Etymology

From Portuguese inhame, from West African languages (cf. Fulani nyami 'to eat', Twi anyinam 'yam').
