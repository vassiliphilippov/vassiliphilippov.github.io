Langley

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæŋli / (AmE), / ˈlæŋli / (BrE)
(1834–1906) United States astronomer and aviation pioneer, inventor of the bolometer and contributor to early aircraft design.
U.S. astronomer and aviation pioneer (1834–1906)

Examples

Langley's research advanced the field of infrared astronomy significantly.
The Smithsonian Institution supported Langley's experiments with early flying machines.
The bolometer, invented by Langley, revolutionized the measurement of radiant energy.
