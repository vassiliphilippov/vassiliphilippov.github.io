tsetse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtsi / (AmE), / ˈtsɛtsi / (BrE)
A large African bloodsucking fly that transmits sleeping sickness and other diseases to humans and animals.
African fly transmitting sleeping sickness

Examples

The tsetse fly is notorious for spreading sleeping sickness across sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers are studying ways to control tsetse populations to protect livestock.
A bite from a tsetse can transmit dangerous parasites to both humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - a small flying insect with two wings

Etymology

From Tswana *tsétsé*, meaning 'fly'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtsɛtˌsi, ˈtsɛtsi / (AmE), / ˈtsɛtsi, ˈtsetsi / (BrE)
a large African biting fly of the genus Glossina that feeds on the blood of vertebrates and transmits sleeping sickness to humans and animals
African bloodsucking fly; transmits sleeping sickness

Examples

The bite of a tsetse can transmit trypanosomiasis, also known as sleeping sickness.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the tsetse in sub-Saharan Africa.
Farmers are trying to control tsetse populations to protect their cattle from disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - a small flying insect with two wings

Etymology

From Tswana *tsetse*, the word for the insect.
