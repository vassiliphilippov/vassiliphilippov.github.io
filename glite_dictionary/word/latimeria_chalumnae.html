Latimeria chalumnae

1. PROPN

/ ləˌtɪməriə kəˈlʌmni.i / (AmE), / ləˌtɪməriə kəˈlʌmni.i / (BrE)
a species of coelacanth fish thought extinct since the Cretaceous period, discovered alive off the coast of Africa in 1938
species of coelacanth fish

Examples

The discovery of the Latimeria chalumnae amazed scientists who believed the species was long extinct.
A living Latimeria chalumnae was first identified off the coast of South Africa in 1938.
Researchers study Latimeria chalumnae to understand more about the evolution of vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - a rare order of fish to which Latimeria chalumnae belongs

Etymology

Genus name from Marjorie Courtenay-Latimer, the discoverer; species name from the Chalumna River, where it was found.
