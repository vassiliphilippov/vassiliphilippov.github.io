to readapt

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈdæpt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈdæpt / (BrE)
to adapt again or anew, especially after a change in circumstances
adapt again or anew

Examples

After moving to a new country, he had to readapt to a different culture.
The species may readapt over generations to survive environmental changes.
She quickly readapted to her old job after returning from her sabbatical.

Synset Examples

He readapted himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to adjust to new conditions

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'adapt' (from Latin 'adaptare', to fit to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00151394-v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈdæpt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈdæpt / (BrE)
to adapt or adjust again; to become accustomed to new conditions after having previously adapted
adapt again; adjust anew

Examples

After spending years abroad, she had to readapt to her home culture.
Wild animals often readapt to their environment after being released from captivity.
The old factory was readapted for use as an art gallery.

Synset Examples

After moving back to America, he had to readjust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - make something suitable for a new use or situation
  • Synonym: to readjust - adjust or adapt again

Etymology

From re- (again) + adapt, from Latin adaptare (to fit to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00150934-v
Synset POS: v
