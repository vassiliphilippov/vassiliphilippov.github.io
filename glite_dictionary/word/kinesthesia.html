kinesthesia

1. NOUN

/ kɪnəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / kɪnɛsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
the sense that detects bodily position, weight, or movement of the muscles, tendons, and joints
sense of body movement and position
medical

Examples

After his injury, his kinesthesia was impaired, making it difficult for him to sense the position of his arm.
Athletes often have highly developed kinesthesia, allowing them to coordinate complex movements.
The study examined how vision and kinesthesia interact during tasks requiring balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of body parts

Etymology

From Greek kinein (to move) + aisthēsis (sensation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɪnəˈsθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
the sense that allows a person to perceive the position, movement, and tension of their own muscles and joints
awareness of body movement and position
medical

Examples

After his injury, he lost his kinesthesia and could not sense where his limbs were.
Dancers and athletes often have highly developed kinesthesia to control their precise movements.
The patient reported difficulty with kinesthesia following nerve damage in his arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the position and movement of one's own body parts

Etymology

From Greek kinein (to move) + aisthēsis (sensation, perception).
