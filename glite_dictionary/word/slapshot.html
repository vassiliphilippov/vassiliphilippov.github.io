slapshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæpˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈslæpˌʃɒt / (BrE)
a fast, powerful shot in ice hockey made by swinging the stick quickly and forcefully
powerful ice hockey shot

Examples

He scored from the blue line with a powerful slapshot that the goalie couldn't stop.
Her slapshot flew past the defenders and straight into the net.
Coaches often teach young players how to improve their slapshot technique.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wrist shot - shot in ice hockey made by flicking the wrist
  • Degree Lower: snap shot - quick shot in ice hockey using a short swinging motion

Etymology

From 'slap' (to strike with a quick blow) + 'shot' (attempt to score).
