maisonette

1. NOUN

/ meɪzəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmeɪzəˈnɛt / (BrE)
a self-contained apartment, usually on two floors, in a larger building and with its own private entrance from the outside
two-floor apartment with private entrance
british

Examples

They bought a maisonette in a quiet part of the city.
Her maisonette has a small garden and a separate entrance.
The newly built maisonette offers two bedrooms and plenty of light.

Etymology

From French 'maisonnette', diminutive of 'maison' (house).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪzəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmeɪzəˈnɛt / (BrE)
a small, self-contained apartment or house, often part of a larger building, with its own private entrance; especially common in British English
small self-contained apartment/house, often in a larger building
british

Examples

They recently bought a maisonette in the center of town.
The ground-floor maisonette has its own private garden.
She lives in a cozy maisonette above the bakery.

Etymology

From French 'maisonnette', diminutive of 'maison' (house).
