portmanteau

1. NOUN

/ pɔrtˈmæntoʊ / (AmE), / pɔːtˈmæntəʊ / (BrE)
a large suitcase or travelling bag made of stiff leather, especially one with two compartments opening outwards
large stiff leather travelling bag
archaic

Examples

He packed his clothes into a sturdy portmanteau before embarking on the long journey.
A battered portmanteau stood in the corner, covered in travel stickers from around the world.
She struggled to lift the heavy portmanteau onto the train's luggage rack.

Etymology

From French 'portemanteau', from 'porter' (to carry) + 'manteau' (cloak).

2. NOUN

/ pɔrtˈmæntoʊ / (AmE), / pɔːtˈmæntəʊ / (BrE)
a word formed by blending parts of two other words and combining their meanings, such as 'brunch' from 'breakfast' and 'lunch'
word formed by blending two words

Examples

The word portmanteau combines two existing words to express a new idea.
'Blog' is a portmanteau of 'web' and 'log', now widely used online.
She explained that 'brunch' is a classic portmanteau blending 'breakfast' and 'lunch'.

Synset Examples

‘smog’ is a blend of ‘smoke’ and ‘fog’
‘motel’ is a portmanteau word made by combining ‘motor’ and ‘hotel’
‘brunch’ is a well-known portmanteau

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - a word created by combining parts of two words

Etymology

From French 'portemanteau', originally meaning a suitcase; term adopted by Lewis Carroll in linguistics.
