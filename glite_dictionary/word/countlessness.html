countlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntləsnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being too numerous to be counted
the state of being too numerous to count

Examples

The countlessness of stars in the night sky inspired generations of poets and astronomers.
She was overwhelmed by the countlessness of tasks awaiting her at work.
They marveled at the countlessness of grains of sand on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innumerability - the state of being innumerable, impossible to count
  • Antonym: finiteness - the state of having limits or bounds

Etymology

From 'countless' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state of being countless.
