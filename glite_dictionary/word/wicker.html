wicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
Slender, flexible twigs or branches, especially of willow or cane, used for making baskets or furniture.
material: flexible twigs for weaving

Examples

The artisan carefully selected the best wicker for weaving the basket.
Chairs made from wicker are popular for outdoor use because they are both strong and lightweight.
The craftsman soaked the wicker to make it more pliable before starting his project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a type of tree whose branches are often used for wicker

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse víkja (to bend, turn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
material made by weaving slender branches, especially willow, used for furniture and baskets.
material made of woven branches

Examples

The old chair was made of wicker and had a beautifully woven backrest.
She bought a wicker basket at the market to carry her vegetables.
We sat on wicker furniture out on the sunny porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickerwork - objects made using woven slender branches

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse 'vikja' (to bend).
