Sticherus

1. PROPN

/ ˈstɪkərəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪkərəs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns known as umbrella ferns, native to warm regions of Australia and New Zealand.
a genus of ferns (umbrella ferns)

Examples

The Sticherus genus is distinguished by its umbrella-shaped fronds.
Many species within Sticherus thrive in the moist forests of New Zealand.
Botanists recently discovered a new Sticherus specimen in Australia.

Etymology

From Greek stichēros, meaning 'in rows' or 'arranged in rows', referring to the frond arrangement.
