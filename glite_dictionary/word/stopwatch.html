stopwatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
a small timepiece designed to be started and stopped for measuring the exact duration of an event, such as a race
a device for measuring elapsed time

Examples

The referee clicked the stopwatch and signaled the start of the race.
She used a stopwatch to time each lap around the track.
Please reset the stopwatch before timing the next event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timer - a device used for measuring time intervals

Etymology

Formed from 'stop' + 'watch', denoting a watch that can be stopped and started.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
a small clock designed to be started and stopped quickly, used to measure the exact time taken to do something, especially in races
a handheld timing device

Examples

The coach used a stopwatch to time each runner's performance.
When the experiment began, she pressed the stopwatch to record the reaction time.
He glanced at the stopwatch as he crossed the finish line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timer - a device that measures time intervals

Etymology

Formed from 'stop' + 'watch' (timepiece), first used in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
