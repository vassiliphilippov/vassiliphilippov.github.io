caricature

1. VERB

/ ˈkæɹɪkətʃʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪkətʃə / (BrE)
to represent or imitate someone or something in a way that exaggerates their distinctive features, often for comic or satirical effect
to exaggerate features for comic effect

Examples

The artist caricatured her boss in the company newsletter.
Many political cartoons caricature public figures to make a point.
He caricatured the celebrity's mannerisms during the comedy routine.

Synset Examples

The drawing caricatured the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satirize - to mock or criticize using satire
  • Synonym: lampoon - to publicly criticize by using ridicule or sarcasm

Etymology

From Italian 'caricatura', from 'caricare' (to load, exaggerate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪkəˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈkærɪkətjʊə / (BrE)
A drawing or description of someone that exaggerates certain features or characteristics in order to create a comic or grotesque effect.
exaggerated comic representation of a person

Examples

The newspaper published a caricature of the mayor, highlighting his prominent chin and bushy eyebrows.
She laughed at the caricature drawn by the street artist on her holiday in Paris.
The film's villain was more of a caricature than a realistic character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor or exaggeration to criticize
  • Antonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, or photograph of a person

Etymology

From Italian 'caricatura', from 'caricare' (to load or exaggerate).
