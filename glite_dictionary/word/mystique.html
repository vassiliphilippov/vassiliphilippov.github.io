mystique

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈstik / (AmE), / mɪˈstiːk / (BrE)
a quality of mystery, glamour, or fascination that makes someone or something seem special or attractive
an aura of mystery or fascination

Examples

The mystique of the ancient ruins attracts thousands of tourists every year.
She has a certain mystique that makes her performances captivating.
Much of the mystique surrounding the artist fades once you learn more about his personal life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something difficult or impossible to understand or explain

Etymology

From French 'mystique' (mystical), from Greek 'mystikos' (secret, mystical).
