harried

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛrid / (AmE), / ˈhærid / (BrE)
Feeling strained, troubled, or stressed due to persistent demands or repeated small problems.
persistently troubled or stressed

Examples

After a week of deadlines and meetings, she looked harried and exhausted.
The harried assistant barely had time to eat lunch between her many tasks.
Despite his harried appearance, the teacher remained calm with his students.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harassed - feeling distressed or troubled by repeated attacks or demands
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety

Etymology

Past participle of 'harry', from Old English 'hergian' (to harry, ravage).
