Cyclopterus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklɑptərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈklɒptərəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Cyclopteridae family, including lumpfishes, notable for their rounded bodies and sucking discs.
type genus of lumpfishes

Examples

The genus Cyclopterus includes the common lumpfish found in northern waters.
Scientists studied Cyclopterus to better understand the adhesive organs on their underside.
Several species within Cyclopterus display unique parental care behavior among fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a type of fish in the Cyclopteridae family, known for a lumpy body and pelvic sucking disc

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle, ring) + 'pteron' (fin, wing).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈklɑptərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈklɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of lumpfishes in the family Cyclopteridae, known for species with a globular body and sucking disc.
genus of lumpfishes

Examples

The Cyclopterus genus includes unique lumpfishes found in cold northern waters.
Scientists studied the adhesive abilities of Cyclopterus to design new underwater technology.
A member of the Cyclopterus genus, the lumpfish is often found clinging to rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a fish in the family Cyclopteridae with a rounded body and adhesive disc

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'pteron' (fin), referring to the circular fins.
