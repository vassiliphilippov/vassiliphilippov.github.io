inga

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡə / (BrE)
Any tree or shrub of the genus Inga, native to tropical regions, with pinnate leaves and showy white flowers; often grown as an ornamental.
genus of tropical trees or shrubs

Examples

The inga tree is prized in tropical gardens for its attractive flowers and rapid growth.
Many farmers plant inga as a shade tree in coffee plantations.
The delicate, feathery leaves of the inga provide shelter for a variety of wildlife.

Etymology

From Latin *Inga*, from the Tupi word *in-gá* meaning 'soaked'.
