Lophophora

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈfɑfəˌrɑ / (AmE), / ləˈfɒfəˌrə / (BrE)
A genus of small, spineless cacti found in northeastern Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for their rounded stems covered with jointed tubercles; notable species include peyote.
genus of small North American cacti

Examples

The Lophophora cactus is well-known for its psychoactive properties in traditional rituals.
Conservationists are concerned about the overharvesting of Lophophora for use in ceremonies.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of Lophophora in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - A small, spineless cactus species (Lophophora williamsii) known for its psychoactive properties

Etymology

From Greek lophos (crest) + phoros (bearing), referring to the tufted appearance of the plant.
