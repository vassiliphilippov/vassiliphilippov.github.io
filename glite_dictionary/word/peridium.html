peridium

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / pəˈrɪdɪəm / (BrE)
the outer layer of the spore-bearing structure in many fungi
outer layer of spore case in fungi
medical

Examples

The peridium protects the developing spores until they are ready for release.
When the peridium breaks open, spores are dispersed into the environment.
Some puffballs have a tough peridium that splits at maturity.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'peridion' (small skin), diminutive of 'pera' (pouch, bag).
