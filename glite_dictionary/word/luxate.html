to luxate

1. VERB

/ ˈlʌk.seɪt / (AmE), / ˈlʌk.seɪt / (BrE)
to dislocate a bone or joint; to move out of its normal position
dislocate a bone or joint
medical

Examples

The force of the accident caused her shoulder to luxate, requiring immediate medical attention.
Surgeons had to carefully manipulate the joint after it luxated during the procedure.
If you luxate your finger, it's important to seek treatment to prevent further injury.

Synset Examples

dislocate joints
the artificial hip joint luxated and had to be put back surgically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dislocate - to put (a bone or joint) out of its normal position

Etymology

From Latin 'luxatus', past participle of 'luxare', meaning 'to dislocate'.
