Pythia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪθiə / (AmE), / ˈpɪθɪə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the priestess of Apollo at Delphi who delivered his oracles.
priestess of Apollo at Delphi; oracle

Examples

According to legend, the Pythia spoke in riddles that priests would interpret for visitors.
In ancient times, people traveled great distances to consult the Pythia at Delphi.
The Pythia played a central role in Greek religious life, acting as the mouthpiece of Apollo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - a priest or priestess through whom advice or prophecy was sought from the gods in ancient times

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πυθία (Pythía), the title of the priestess at Delphi.
