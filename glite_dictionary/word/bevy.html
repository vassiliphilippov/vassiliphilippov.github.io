bevy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvi / (AmE), / ˈbɛvi / (BrE)
a large group of birds, especially when gathered closely together on the ground
a group of birds gathered together

Examples

A bevy of geese gathered at the edge of the pond early in the morning.
The children watched as a bevy of sparrows pecked at the ground in the park.
We spotted a bevy of doves resting together under the old oak tree.

Synset Examples

we were visited at breakfast by a bevy of excited ducks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds, especially when flying or feeding together

Etymology

Probably from Anglo-Norman *bevee* (drinking bout, company).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvi / (AmE), / ˈbɛvi / (BrE)
a large group of people, especially of a particular kind
large group of similar people

Examples

A bevy of excited fans waited outside the theater for the celebrities to arrive.
He found himself the center of attention among a bevy of journalists at the press conference.
A bevy of children rushed into the playground as soon as the bell rang.

Synset Examples

he was surrounded by a bevy of beauties in bathing attire
a bevy of young beach boys swarmed around him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - a number of people or things that are located, gathered, or classed together
  • Synonym: gathering - an assembly or meeting, especially a social or festive one

Etymology

From Middle English bevey, of uncertain origin, possibly from Anglo-Norman.
