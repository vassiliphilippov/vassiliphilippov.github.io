primality

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / praɪˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being a prime number
the property of being a prime number

Examples

Testing the primality of large numbers can be quite challenging for mathematicians.
The algorithm is designed to determine the primality of any given integer.
In number theory, primality plays a crucial role in many theorems and proofs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compositeness - the property of being a composite number (not prime)

Etymology

From prime + -ality, patterned after 'primacy'.
