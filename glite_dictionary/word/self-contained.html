self-contained

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf kənˈteɪnd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf kənˈteɪnd / (BrE)
complete and independent; not needing or relying on anything else to function or exist
complete and independent unit

Examples

The new apartment is self-contained, with its own kitchen and bathroom.
She prefers to work in a self-contained office space.
The community was designed to be self-contained, including schools, shops, and parks.

Synset Examples

the university is like a self-contained city with shops and all amenities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not relying on others; self-sufficient
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on something or someone else

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'contained'; influenced by contain meaning 'to hold within'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf kənˈteɪnd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf kənˈteɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person) calm, composed, and in control of one's emotions or faculties; self-possessed.
composed and self-possessed (of a person)

Examples

Despite the chaotic situation, she remained self-contained and handled every challenge calmly.
He is known for being self-contained, rarely letting his feelings show in public.
The manager's self-contained manner reassured everyone during the emergency.

Synset Examples

the witness remained collected throughout the cross-examination
perfectly poised and sure of himself
more self-contained and more dependable than many of the early frontiersmen
strong and self-possessed in the face of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composed - calm and in control of emotions
  • Synonym: self-possessed - in control of one's feelings and actions
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'contained', indicating control over oneself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00532559-s
Synset POS: s
