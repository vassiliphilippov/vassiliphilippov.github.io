tyrothricin

1. NOUN

/ taɪ.rəˈθraɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌtɪərəˈθraɪsɪn / (BrE)
a mixture of antibiotics used topically to treat infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria
mixture of antibiotics for bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tyrothricin ointment to treat the localized skin infection.
Tyrothricin is effective mainly against Gram-positive bacteria.
Many over-the-counter throat lozenges contain tyrothricin to help prevent bacterial infections.

Etymology

From Greek 'tyros' (cheese) and 'thrix, trichos' (hair), referring to its isolation from Bacillus brevis found in cheese.
