generalship

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, authority, or office of a general in the military
the office or authority of a military general

Examples

He was promoted to generalship after many years of distinguished service.
The responsibilities associated with generalship require both strategic insight and leadership skills.
Her rise to generalship marked a significant moment in the country's military history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - the authority to lead and direct military forces

Etymology

Formed from 'general' + suffix '-ship', denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚ(ə)lˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill or ability of a military general in planning and directing military operations; leadership and strategy in warfare
leadership and skill of a military general

Examples

Her brilliant generalship turned the tide of the war in their favor.
The troops admired his generalship and trusted his decisions unconditionally.
Napoleon’s generalship is still studied in military academies around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - the skill of planning military operations or maneuvers
  • Synonym: command - the act of leading or controlling a military unit

Etymology

From 'general' (military rank) + '-ship' (state or skill), first attested in English in the 17th century.
