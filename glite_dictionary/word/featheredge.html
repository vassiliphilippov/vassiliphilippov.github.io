featheredge

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðɚˌɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəˌɛdʒ / (BrE)
a thin, tapering edge, especially on a piece of material such as wood, stone, or metal
a thin, tapering edge

Examples

Carefully grind the metal until you create a featheredge for a precise fit.
The carpenter shaped a featheredge on the board to allow for a smoother joint.
Always check that the featheredge is even and straight before final installation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel - an edge of a structure that is not perpendicular to the faces of the piece

Etymology

From 'feather' + 'edge,' describing the narrow, slender shape resembling a feather's edge.
