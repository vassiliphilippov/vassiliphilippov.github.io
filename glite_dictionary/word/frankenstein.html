Frankenstein

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkənˌstaɪn / (BrE)
A creation or agency that escapes the control of its creator and causes harm to them.
a creation that harms its creator

Examples

The new technology became a Frankenstein, spiraling out of control and threatening its inventors.
His once-promising invention turned into a Frankenstein that ruined his career.
Some fear that artificial intelligence could become a modern Frankenstein if left unchecked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a large, frightening imaginary creature; used figuratively for anything powerful and uncontrollable

Etymology

From the title character in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel, where Dr. Frankenstein creates a destructive monster.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkənˌstaɪn / (BrE)
the monster created by Victor Frankenstein in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel; popularly, the name of the creature itself
the monster in Mary Shelley's novel Frankenstein

Examples

In popular culture, Frankenstein is often depicted as a green-skinned giant with bolts in his neck.
Many people mistakenly call the monster Frankenstein, though that was actually the name of its creator.
After reading the novel, she understood why people sympathize with Frankenstein despite his terrifying appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - an imaginary creature that is typically large, ugly, and frightening

Etymology

From Victor Frankenstein, the scientist in Mary Shelley's novel (1818). The name is often applied to the monster he created.

3. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkənstaɪn / (BrE)
The fictional Swiss scientist who is the protagonist of Mary Shelley's 1818 novel, known for creating a living creature from parts of corpses.
fictional scientist in Shelley's novel

Examples

Many people mistakenly think that Frankenstein is the name of the monster, not the scientist.
The story of Frankenstein has inspired countless adaptations and movies over the years.
Mary Shelley's novel explores the consequences of Frankenstein's ambition and scientific curiosity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Frankenstein's monster - the creature created by Frankenstein in Shelley's novel

Etymology

From the title of Mary Shelley's 1818 novel 'Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus'.
