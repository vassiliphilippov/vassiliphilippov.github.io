Isatis tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsætɪs tɪŋˈtɔriə / (AmE), / aɪˈsætɪs tɪŋˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A European biennial plant, also known as woad, formerly cultivated for the blue dye extracted from its leaves.
European plant, source of blue dye

Examples

The leaves of Isatis tinctoria were once an important source of blue dye in medieval Europe.
Botanists studied the characteristics of Isatis tinctoria to understand its natural pigment production.
Farmers no longer cultivate Isatis tinctoria for dye, but it remains of interest to historians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woad - Common name for Isatis tinctoria, plant used as a source of blue dye

Etymology

From Latin 'Isatis' (the plant name) and 'tinctoria' (related to dyeing or coloring).
