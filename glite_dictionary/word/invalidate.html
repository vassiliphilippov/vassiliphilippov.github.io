to invalidate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvæləˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvælɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to prove or show that something is not valid, legally acceptable, or correct
to make something invalid or prove untrue

Examples

The discovery of new evidence could invalidate the previous court ruling.
If the contract is signed under duress, it may invalidate the agreement.
One error in the calculations could invalidate the entire experiment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to validate - to confirm or prove the validity of something
  • Synonym: to nullify - to make something legally null and void

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidare' (to make invalid), from 'in-' (not) + 'validare' (to strengthen, from 'validus' strong).
