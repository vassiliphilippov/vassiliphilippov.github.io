counselor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl.ə / (BrE)
a person trained to give advice and guidance about personal, social, or psychological problems.
person who gives advice or guidance

Examples

After her parents' divorce, she spoke to a counselor at school to help her cope.
The company provides access to a professional counselor for employees seeking support.
As a counselor, he listens patiently and offers practical solutions to his clients' concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisor - a person who gives advice in a particular field
  • Synonym: therapist - a person trained to treat mental or psychological disorders

Etymology

From Old French 'conseiller', from Latin 'consiliarius' (advisor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.sə.lə / (BrE)
a person who supervises youngsters and leads activities at a summer camp
person supervising at a summer camp
american

Examples

My older sister worked as a counselor at Lakeview Summer Camp last year.
The counselor organized games and activities for the campers each afternoon.
Becoming a counselor helped him develop leadership and communication skills.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: camp director - person in charge of a camp

Etymology

From Latin 'consiliarius' (advisor), via Old French 'conseillour.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.sələ / (BrE)
A lawyer who gives legal advice or represents clients in court; an attorney or advocate.
lawyer; legal adviser or advocate
legal

Examples

The counselor argued persuasively on behalf of her client before the judge.
Each side in the trial was represented by an experienced counselor.
He worked as a defense counselor in the district court for over ten years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate, particularly in higher courts
  • Synonym: solicitor - a type of lawyer who advises clients and prepares legal documents (esp. in the UK)

Etymology

From Latin 'consiliarius' (advisor), from 'consilium' (counsel, advice).
