krummhorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊmˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a Renaissance woodwind instrument with a double reed and a curved tube, producing a buzzing, nasal tone
curved Renaissance woodwind instrument

Examples

The music ensemble featured a krummhorn alongside recorders and viols.
She learned to play the krummhorn to perform authentic Renaissance music.
The distinctive sound of the krummhorn is both reedy and somewhat nasal.

Etymology

From German 'Krummhorn', meaning 'curved horn' (krumm ‘crooked’ + Horn ‘horn’).
