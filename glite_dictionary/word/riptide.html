riptide

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪp.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪp.taɪd / (BrE)
a strong, fast, and often dangerous current of water, especially where two currents meet or flow in different directions, typically in the sea or a river
strong, turbulent water current

Examples

Swimmers are advised to avoid this area because of a dangerous riptide that sometimes forms.
He was carried far out to sea by a sudden riptide while surfing.
The warning flags were raised due to reports of a strong riptide near the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertow - a subsurface current moving offshore when waves approach the shore
  • Degree Lower: current - a body of water or air moving in a definite direction

Etymology

Formed from 'rip' (to tear quickly) + 'tide'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪp.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪp.taɪd / (BrE)
a strong current of water flowing away from the shore, often making swimming dangerous
strong current flowing away from shore

Examples

Swimmers should beware of the riptide that can pull them out to sea.
A sudden riptide made it difficult for the surfers to get back to shore.
He explained how to escape a riptide by swimming parallel to the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertow - a subsurface current of water moving offshore after waves break on a beach

Etymology

From 'rip' (to tear) + 'tide' (sea's rise and fall), originally US English.
