Calochortus albus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈkɔrtəs ˈælbəs / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈkɔːtəs ˈalbus / (BrE)
a species of globe lily native to southern California, with open, branched clusters of egg-shaped white flowers
a species of white-flowered California globe lily

Examples

The delicate petals of the Calochortus albus shimmered in the morning sun.
Many gardeners in California cultivate Calochortus albus for its elegant white blooms.
During spring hikes, we often spot wild Calochortus albus growing on the hillsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe lily - a type of lily with globe-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) + chortos (grass), and Latin albus (white), referring to the plant's appearance.
