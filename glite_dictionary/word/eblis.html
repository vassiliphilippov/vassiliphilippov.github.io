Eblis

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːblɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːblɪs / (BrE)
In Islamic mythology, the principal evil jinni who defied God and was cast from heaven; often regarded as the Islamic equivalent of Satan.
evil spirit in Islamic mythology

Examples

According to legend, Eblis refused to bow to Adam and was cast out from paradise.
In some stories, Eblis tempts humans to stray from the righteous path.
The scholars debated the role of Eblis in the moral development of humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satan - the devil; chief evil spirit in Christianity and Judaism

Etymology

From Arabic Iblīs, possibly from Greek diábolos (devil).
