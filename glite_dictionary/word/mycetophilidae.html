Mycetophilidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌsiːtəˈfɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / maɪˌsiːtəˈfɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small flies commonly known as fungus gnats, whose larvae feed on fungi
family of small flies (fungus gnats)

Examples

The Mycetophilidae are commonly encountered in damp woodlands where fungi abound.
Entomologists study the Mycetophilidae because of their ecological role in decomposing fungi.
Many species within the Mycetophilidae family are important in the life cycle of forest mushrooms.

Etymology

From Greek mykēs (fungus) + phileo (to love), referring to the flies' association with fungi.
