sedum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.dəm / (AmE), / ˈsiː.dəm / (BrE)
a plant of a large genus (Sedum) of fleshy-leaved flowering plants, often grown as ornamentals or ground cover
a fleshy-leaved flowering plant

Examples

We planted sedum along the edge of the rock garden where little else would grow.
The thick, succulent leaves of sedum help it survive in dry conditions.
Each autumn, the pink flowers of the sedum attract bees and butterflies to the backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonecrop - another name for sedum or a related plant with fleshy leaves

Etymology

From Latin sedum, meaning 'houseleek' or ‘stonecrop’.
