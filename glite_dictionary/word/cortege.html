cortege

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɛʒ / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɛʒ / (BrE)
A solemn procession, especially one for a funeral.
funeral procession

Examples

The mourners walked in a solemn cortege behind the hearse.
The cortege made its way slowly through the village streets.
A long cortege of cars followed the family to the cemetery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group moving forward in an orderly way, especially as part of a ceremony

Etymology

From French cortège, from Italian cortege (retinue, procession), from corte (court).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɹˈteɪʒ / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɛːʒ / (BrE)
a solemn procession or group of attendants, especially one following an important person, often as part of a funeral or ceremonial event
formal procession or group of attendants

Examples

The royal cortege made its way slowly through the cheering crowds.
A solemn cortege followed the hearse to the cemetery.
Dignitaries walked behind the president in a formal cortege during the state event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group moving forward as part of a ceremony

Etymology

From French cortège (procession), from Italian corteggio, from corte (court).
