Huang Hai

1. PROPN

/ ˈhwæŋ ˈhaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhwæŋ ˈhaɪ / (BrE)
the Yellow Sea, a shallow part of the Pacific Ocean off the east coast of Asia, between China and Korea
the Yellow Sea; sea between China and Korea

Examples

Many migratory birds rest along the shores of the Huang Hai during their annual journey.
The pollution levels in the Huang Hai have become a growing concern for environmentalists in China and Korea.
Fishermen have relied on the rich waters of the Huang Hai for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yellow Sea - another name for Huang Hai, body of water between China and Korea

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 'Huáng Hǎi' (黃海), literally 'Yellow Sea'.
