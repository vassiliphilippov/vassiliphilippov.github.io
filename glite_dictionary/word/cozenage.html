cozenage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌz.ən.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.ən.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
Deceit or trickery, especially in financial or business dealings; fraud.
fraud; trickery in business
archaic

Examples

The merchant was accused of cozenage after several customers complained of being tricked.
His schemes relied on subtle cozenage rather than open theft.
Victims of cozenage often found it difficult to reclaim their losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception for financial or personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit

Etymology

From Middle English 'cosinage', from 'cozen', itself possibly from Italian 'cozzonare' (to cheat).
