paleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪl.nɪs / (BrE)
An unnatural lack of color in the skin, often due to illness, bruising, or emotional distress.
unnatural lack of skin color

Examples

The doctor noticed the paleness of the patient's face and became concerned.
Her sudden paleness revealed how nervous she was before giving the speech.
After several days of illness, his paleness was hard to ignore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance of the skin

Etymology

From 'pale' + '-ness', formed in Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a light or pale complexion, especially of the skin
the quality of being pale

Examples

Her paleness stood out in the crowd, drawing concerned glances from her friends.
The paleness of his cheeks suggested he hadn't been out in the sun for months.
Doctors noticed an unusual paleness on the boy's face, which prompted further medical tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance
  • Antonym: redness - the quality of being red, especially of the skin

Etymology

From Middle English 'palnesse', from 'pale' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪl.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being pale in color, especially of the skin, often suggesting ill health, fear, or lack of exposure to sunlight
the state of being pale in color

Examples

A sudden paleness spread across her face as she heard the news.
Doctors became concerned by the paleness of the boy's skin.
The paleness of the morning light made the room look cold and uninviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - unnatural paleness, especially of the face

Etymology

From 'pale' (Middle English, from Old French 'pale', from Latin 'pallidus') + '-ness', a noun-forming suffix.
