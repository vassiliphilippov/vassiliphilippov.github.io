brass

1. NOUN

/ bræs / (AmE), / brɑːs / (BrE)
the section of a band or orchestra that plays brass instruments, such as trumpets, trombones, and tubas
section in a band/orchestra playing brass instruments

Examples

The brass played a triumphant fanfare at the end of the symphony.
During rehearsals, the conductor asked the brass to play more softly.
The school's brass section is known for its impressive sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woodwind - the section of a band or orchestra that plays woodwind instruments

Etymology

From Old English 'bræs' (brass, the metal); musical sense dates from the 19th century, referring to instruments made from brass.
