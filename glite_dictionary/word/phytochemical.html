phytochemical

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪtoʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a biologically active chemical substance naturally found in plants, other than nutrients, often studied for its effects on health
biologically active substance from plants

Examples

The study focused on the effects of a specific phytochemical found in green tea.
Researchers are interested in how phytochemicals may help prevent chronic diseases.
Many fruits and vegetables are rich in phytochemicals with potential health benefits.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyto-' (plant) + 'chemical'.
