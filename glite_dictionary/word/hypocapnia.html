hypocapnia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈkæp.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌhɪ.pəʊˈkæp.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the level of carbon dioxide in the blood is lower than normal, often caused by rapid or deep breathing
low carbon dioxide levels in blood
medical

Examples

Anxiety can sometimes lead to hypocapnia due to rapid breathing.
Doctors measured the patient’s blood gases and confirmed hypocapnia.
Prolonged hyperventilation may result in hypocapnia and associated symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypercapnia - high level of carbon dioxide in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under, less) + 'kapnos' (smoke/vapour, here meaning carbon dioxide) + medical suffix '-ia'.
