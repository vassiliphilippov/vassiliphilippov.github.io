detector

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
an electronic device that discovers the presence of radio signals or radioactivity
device that detects signals or radioactivity

Examples

The scientist used a detector to measure the level of radioactivity in the laboratory.
Modern metal detectors can distinguish between different types of metals and objects.
The technician adjusted the detector to pick up weak radio signals from space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor - device that detects physical properties or changes

Etymology

From Latin 'detegere' meaning 'to uncover'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
a device that receives a physical signal or stimulus and responds in a distinctive way, often to indicate the presence of something
device that senses and responds to signals

Examples

The smoke detector went off when it sensed a small fire in the kitchen.
Scientists use a particle detector to observe high-energy collisions in the laboratory.
Motion detectors are commonly used in security systems to alert owners of unusual activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor - device that detects or measures physical properties and records, indicates, or responds to them

Etymology

From Latin 'detectus', past participle of 'detegere' (to uncover).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
An electronic component that extracts modulation signals from a radio carrier wave, used in radio receivers.
device extracting modulation from radio signal

Examples

The radio's detector converts the signals into audible sound.
Modern receivers typically use a semiconductor diode as the detector.
A malfunctioning detector can cause loss of audio in AM radios.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demodulator - a device that separates the original information-bearing signal from a modulated carrier wave

Etymology

From Latin 'detegere' (to uncover), via detect + -or (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
