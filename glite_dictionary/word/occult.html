occult

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌlt / (AmE), / əˈkʌlt / (BrE)
(of a process, condition, or object) hidden from view or not easily detected, especially by ordinary means.
hidden; not easily detected
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a test to check for occult blood in the patient's stool.
An X-ray revealed an occult fracture that was not visible during the initial examination.
Certain infections can present with occult symptoms, making diagnosis more challenging.

Synset Examples

occult blood in the stool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed

Etymology

From Latin occultus (hidden, concealed), past participle of occulere (to hide from view).

5. VERB

/ əˈkʌlt / (AmE), / əˈkʌlt / (BrE)
(of a celestial body) to block from view or obscure by passing in front of it, causing an eclipse.

Examples

Astronomers observed as the moon occulted the bright star during last night's event.
Jupiter's moon is expected to occult the distant planet, making it invisible for several minutes.
