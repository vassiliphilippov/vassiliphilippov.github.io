Jehovah

1. PROPN

/ dʒɪˈhoʊvə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈhəʊvə / (BrE)
a name for God, especially in the Old Testament, used as a transliteration of the Hebrew name YHVH
a name for God (YHVH) in the Old Testament

Examples

Many hymns begin by praising Jehovah as the creator of all things.
The translation of the Old Testament most commonly uses Jehovah for God's name.
Some religious groups refer to God specifically as Jehovah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yahweh - the Hebrew name of God used in the Bible

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Jehovah', a transliteration of the Hebrew YHVH (Yahweh).

Source: WordNet

2. PROPN

/ dʒɪˈhoʊvə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈhəʊvə / (BrE)
A name used to refer to God, especially in the Old Testament, in the context of Judaism and Christianity.
name for the Judeo-Christian God

Examples

Many translations of the Bible use the name Jehovah for God.
He prayed to Jehovah for guidance during difficult times.
Some hymns address the Lord as Jehovah in their lyrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: God - the supreme being worshipped in monotheistic religions

Etymology

From Hebrew 'YHWH' (the tetragrammaton), via Latin and English transliterations.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09559474-n
Synset POS: n
