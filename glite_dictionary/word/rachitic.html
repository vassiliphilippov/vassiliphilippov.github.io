rachitic

1. ADJ

/ rəˈkɪtɪk / (AmE), / rəˈkɪtɪk / (BrE)
affected by or characteristic of rickets; showing symptoms similar to rickets, such as weak or deformed bones
relating to or affected by rickets
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with rachitic changes in his bones.
A rachitic skeleton appeared in the medical textbook to show the effects of rickets.
The patient's rachitic condition required immediate nutritional intervention.

Synset Examples

rickety limbs and joints
a rachitic patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rickety - weak or feeble, especially in relation to bones or physical structure, often due to rickets

Etymology

From Greek rhachitis (inflammation of the spine), related to rickets.
