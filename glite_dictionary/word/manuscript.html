manuscript

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.jəˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.juˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
a handwritten book, document, or piece of writing, especially one from before the invention of printing; also, an author's text that is submitted for publication
handwritten book or document

Examples

The ancient manuscript was carefully preserved in the monastery's library.
She submitted her novel's manuscript to several publishing houses for consideration.
Scholars spent years deciphering a medieval manuscript written in Latin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typescript - a document typed on a typewriter or computer, not handwritten

Etymology

From Latin 'manuscriptum', from 'manu' (by hand) + 'scriptum' (written).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.jəˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈman.jə.skrɪpt / (BrE)
the original text of an author's work submitted for publication, typically before it is printed or published
original text submitted for publication

Examples

The author submitted her manuscript to several publishers in hopes of finding a deal.
The editor sent the manuscript back with detailed feedback and suggestions for revision.
Before printing, every manuscript is carefully reviewed for errors by the editorial team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typescript - a typed version of a manuscript

Etymology

From Latin 'manuscriptus', meaning 'written by hand'.
