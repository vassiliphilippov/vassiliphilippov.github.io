nonsubmersible

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.səbˈmɜr.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.səbˈmɜː.sə.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be submerged or placed under water
not capable of being submerged

Examples

The life jackets are designed to be nonsubmersible for added safety in rough waters.
This floating dock is completely nonsubmersible even when supporting heavy loads.
All equipment labeled as nonsubmersible must be kept above water at all times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: submersible - able to be submerged

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'submersible' (able to be submerged).
