Polygonum

1. PROPN

/ pəˈlɑːɡənəm / (AmE), / pəˈlɒɡənəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbs or woody subshrubs found chiefly in north temperate regions, including knotweeds and related plants.
plant genus including knotweeds

Examples

The invasive Polygonum species crowded out native plants along the riverbank.
Several members of the Polygonum genus are known for their medicinal properties.
Botanists collected samples of Polygonum for genetic analysis.

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonon' (knot-grass), from 'poly-' (many) and 'gony' (knee, joint), referring to the plant's many jointed stems.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlɑːɡəˌnəm / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡənəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and woody subshrubs in the buckwheat family, found mainly in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of temperate-region herbs and subshrubs

Examples

Several species within the genus Polygonum are valued for their medicinal properties.
The botanist spent years studying the complex taxonomy of Polygonum species.
Invasive plants in the Polygonum genus can dominate wetland areas and displace native flora.

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonon', meaning 'many knees', referring to the jointed stems.
