Pedionomus

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛdiəˈnoʊməs / (AmE), / ˌpiːdiəˈnəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of birds consisting of the plain wanderer, native to Australia.
genus of Australian birds (plain wanderer)

Examples

The Pedionomus is unique among Australian birds for its solitary habits.
Ornithologists are especially interested in the conservation of Pedionomus in its native grasslands.
The genus Pedionomus consists of only one extant species, the plain wanderer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain wanderer - a small, ground-dwelling bird native to Australia, only species in the genus Pedionomus

Etymology

From Greek 'pedion' (plain) + 'nomos' (dweller, wanderer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdiəˈnoʊməs / (AmE), / ˌpɛdɪəˈnəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Pedionomidae, consisting of the plains-wanderer, native to Australia.
a genus of Australian birds (plains-wanderer)

Examples

The Pedionomus is considered a living fossil among Australian birds.
Scientists are conducting research on the habitat of Pedionomus in the grasslands.
Unlike other ground-dwelling birds, Pedionomus has a unique foraging style.

Etymology

From Greek 'pedion' (plain) and 'nomos' (dweller).
