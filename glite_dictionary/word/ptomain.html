ptomain

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌmeɪn / (BrE)
any of various toxic amines, such as putrescine or cadaverine, formed by the action of bacteria during the decomposition of organic matter.
toxic amine from decaying matter
medical

Examples

Bacterial action on protein leads to the release of ptomain compounds with a strong odor.
The presence of ptomain in spoiled meat can pose a serious health risk.
Scientists detected ptomain as a byproduct of advanced tissue decomposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putrescine - a toxic diamine produced during the putrefaction of animal tissue
  • Synonym: cadaverine - a foul-smelling diamine compound produced by protein hydrolysis during putrefaction of animal tissue

Etymology

From Italian 'ptomaina', from Greek 'ptōma' meaning 'fallen body, corpse'.
