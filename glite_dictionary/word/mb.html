MB

1. NOUN

/ ɛm bi / (AmE), / ɛm biː / (BrE)
A bachelor’s degree in medicine awarded by British universities, standing for 'Medicinae Baccalaureus' (Bachelor of Medicine).
bachelor's degree in medicine (Britain)
british

Examples

After five years of study, she finally received her MB from Cambridge.
Many doctors in the UK hold an MB as their primary medical qualification.
He went on to practice medicine after earning his MB degree.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: MD - doctorate in medicine, typically used in the US

Etymology

From Latin 'Medicinae Baccalaureus' (Bachelor of Medicine).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈbiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for megabyte, a unit of information equal to 1,048,576 bytes (2^20 bytes) in computer science contexts
abbreviation for megabyte (1,048,576 bytes)

Examples

The file is too large to email at 25 MB.
My phone has only 100 MB of free space left.
The video will cost you about 500 MB of data to download.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: byte - a unit of digital information consisting of eight bits
  • Degree Higher: GB - abbreviation for gigabyte, a larger unit of digital information (1,073,741,824 bytes)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'megabyte', from Greek 'mega-' (large) and 'byte' (unit of digital information).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13649517-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈbiː / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000 bytes (one million bytes), commonly used to measure data storage and file sizes.
unit of digital information (one million bytes)

Examples

The file was too large, exceeding 20 MB of storage.
You should have at least 500 MB of free space to install the updates.
My camera's photos are about 3 MB each in size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megabyte - a unit of information equal to one million bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of megabyte, from mega- (million) + byte.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13649707-n
Synset POS: n
