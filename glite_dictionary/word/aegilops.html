Aegilops

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdʒɪˌlɑps / (AmE), / ˈiːdʒɪˌlɒps / (BrE)
A genus of grasses commonly known as goat grass, some species of which are closely related to wheat and considered agricultural weeds.
genus of goat grasses

Examples

Several species within the genus Aegilops are notorious for contaminating wheat fields.
Botanists study Aegilops to better understand the evolution of cultivated wheat.
Wild populations of Aegilops can be found throughout the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Greek aigílōps, meaning 'goat grass'.
