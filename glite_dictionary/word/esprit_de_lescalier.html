esprit de l'escalier

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˌpri dɛ lɛsˈkɑːliˌeɪ / (AmE), / ɛsˌpriː də lɛsˈkælɪeɪ / (BrE)
the occurrence of a clever or witty remark or comeback too late to use it
witty remark thought of too late

Examples

As she left the meeting, the perfect esprit de l'escalier came to her mind.
He was frustrated by his constant esprit de l'escalier whenever arguments got heated.
The best jokes often arrive as esprit de l'escalier, well after the moment has passed.

Etymology

From French, literally 'staircase wit'; refers to thinking of a witty reply while leaving a social gathering, as if having it on the stairs.
