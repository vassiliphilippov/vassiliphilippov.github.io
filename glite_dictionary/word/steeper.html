steeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːpər / (AmE), / ˈstiːpə / (BrE)
a container, such as a pot or vat, used for soaking something in liquid to extract flavor or soften it
vessel used for steeping

Examples

The barley was left in the steeper overnight to absorb water.
Please clean the steeper after each use to prevent residue buildup.
A large wooden steeper stood in the corner of the brewery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaker - a person or thing that soaks; container for soaking items

Etymology

From the verb 'steep' (to soak in liquid), with agentive suffix '-er'.
