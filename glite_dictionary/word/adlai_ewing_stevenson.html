Adlai Ewing Stevenson

1. PROPN

/ ˈæd.leɪ ˈjuː.ɪŋ ˈstiː.vən.sən / (AmE), / ˈæd.laɪ ˈjuː.ɪŋ ˈstiː.vən.sən / (BrE)
(1900–1968) An American politician and diplomat, two-time Democratic presidential candidate, and U.S. ambassador to the United Nations.
American politician and UN ambassador (1900–1968)

Examples

Adlai Ewing Stevenson was twice the Democratic nominee for President of the United States in the 1950s.
As U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations, Adlai Ewing Stevenson played a key role during the Cuban Missile Crisis.
Many historians regard Adlai Ewing Stevenson as one of the most eloquent American political leaders of his era.
