Catha edulis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːθə ɪˈdjuːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæθə ɪˈdjuːlɪs / (BrE)
A tropical shrub native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, whose leaves are chewed or brewed as a stimulant.
shrub chewed/brewed for its stimulant leaves

Examples

In some cultures, people gather daily to chew Catha edulis leaves for their stimulating effects.
The export of Catha edulis is regulated in many countries due to its psychoactive properties.
Recent studies have examined the health risks associated with consuming Catha edulis tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khat - another name for Catha edulis, especially for the prepared leaves used as a stimulant

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining Catha (the genus name) and edulis (Latin for ‘edible’).
