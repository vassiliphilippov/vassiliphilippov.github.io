camera

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / ˈkam(ə)rə / (BrE)
A device for recording visual images, in this sense especially referring to television equipment that uses a lens to focus an image onto a photosensitive surface which is scanned electronically.
television device for recording visual images

Examples

The director adjusted the camera to get a clearer shot of the actor.
Modern television studios use high-definition cameras for live broadcasts.
The technician repaired the malfunctioning camera before the evening news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: television camera - a camera specifically designed for broadcasting or recording television images

Etymology

From Latin camera (chamber, vault, room), referring to the 'camera obscura' or 'dark chamber' used in early image projection.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
a device for taking photographs or recording moving images, typically consisting of a lightproof box with a lens at one end and a light-sensitive surface at the other
device for taking photographs

Examples

She bought a new camera before her trip to capture beautiful landscapes.
Security footage from the camera helped the police identify the suspect.
The camera on his phone takes surprisingly sharp pictures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camcorder - a device combining a video camera and video recorder
  • Synonym: video camera - a camera for recording moving images especially for television or film

Etymology

From Latin 'camera' (chamber, vaulted room), referring to the chamber of the device.
