merchandise

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃən.daɪz / (BrE)
branded goods produced to promote or advertise something, especially a film, band, or other entertainment product
branded goods for promotion (e.g. films, bands)

Examples

The band sold merchandise such as T-shirts and posters at the concert.
Movie studios often release exclusive merchandise to generate excitement for a new film.
Fans lined up early to buy limited edition merchandise at the pop star's tour stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-80664327-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃənˌdaɪz / (BrE)
to engage in the buying and selling of goods; to promote or market a product for sale.
to trade or market goods

Examples

The company plans to merchandise its new line of eco-friendly products worldwide.
He has spent years learning how to effectively merchandise various consumer electronics.
Retailers often merchandise goods differently during the holiday season to attract more customers.

Synset Examples

he is merchandising telephone sets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell - to exchange something for money
  • Antonym: buy - to acquire in exchange for payment

Etymology

From Old French 'marchandiser', from 'marchand' (merchant), from Late Latin 'mercatans' (trading).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02265169-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːrtʃənˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃənˌdaɪz / (BrE)
goods that are bought and sold, especially items for sale to the general public
items for sale; goods

Examples

The new shop displays its merchandise in attractive windows to draw in customers.
Holiday sales boosted the amount of merchandise sold by the department store last year.
Please keep your receipt if you wish to exchange any merchandise after purchase.

Synset Examples

good business depends on having good merchandise
that store offers a variety of products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goods - things produced for sale
  • Synonym: products - items created to be sold in the market

Etymology

From Old French 'marchandise', from 'marchand' (merchant), from Late Latin 'mercatans' (trader).
