Gazania

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzeɪniə / (AmE), / ɡəˈzeɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to southern Africa, known for their bright, showy flowers
genus of African flowering plants

Examples

The garden was vibrant with blooming Gazania flowers in shades of orange and yellow.
Many landscapers choose Gazania for its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
She planted several Gazania in her front yard to add color throughout the summer.

Etymology

Named after Theodorus Gaza, a 15th-century Greek scholar.
