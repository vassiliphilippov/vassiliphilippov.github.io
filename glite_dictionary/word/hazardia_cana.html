Hazardia cana

1. NOUN

/ həˈzɑːrdi.ə ˈkænə / (AmE), / həˈzɑːdɪə ˈkænə / (BrE)
A species of western American shrub with white felted leaves and yellow flowers that turn red-purple.
western American shrub species

Examples

Hazardia cana is native to California, where it grows on rocky slopes.
The distinctive silver leaves of Hazardia cana help it survive in dry environments.
Botanists study Hazardia cana for its unique adaptation to arid climates.

Etymology

Hazardia: named after American botanist Barclay Hazard; cana: from Latin 'canus' (grey, hoary), referring to the foliage.
