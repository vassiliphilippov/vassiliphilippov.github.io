to collateralize

1. VERB

/ kəˈlæt.ə.rəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈlat.ər.əˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to pledge an asset as collateral to secure a loan or obligation
pledge an asset as collateral

Examples

The company decided to collateralize its loan by using real estate assets.
Banks often collateralize loans with accounts receivable or inventory.
The mortgage was collateralized by the property itself.

Synset Examples

The loan was collateralized by government bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to guarantee a loan with collateral

Etymology

From 'collateral', meaning an asset pledged as security, plus the suffix '-ize'.
