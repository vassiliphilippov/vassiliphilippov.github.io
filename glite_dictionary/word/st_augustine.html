St. Augustine

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn / (AmE), / seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstiːn / (BrE)
A resort city in northeastern Florida, recognized as the oldest city in the United States, founded by the Spanish in 1565.
historic city in Florida, USA
american

Examples

Many visitors are drawn to St. Augustine for its rich colonial architecture and vibrant cultural scene.
The historic fort in St. Augustine is a popular tourist attraction.
On our Florida road trip, we stopped at St. Augustine to explore its ancient city streets.

Etymology

Named after Saint Augustine of Hippo by Spanish explorers, reflecting the feast day of his namesake when the city was sighted.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɔˈɡʌstɪn / (AmE), / sənt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn / (BrE)
(354–430) An early Christian theologian and philosopher, bishop of Hippo, whose writings greatly influenced Western Christianity and philosophy.
early Christian theologian and bishop

Examples

Many consider St. Augustine to be one of the most influential figures in Western Christianity.
The teachings of St. Augustine significantly shaped medieval philosophical thought.
After his conversion, St. Augustine devoted his life to theology and pastoral work.

Etymology

From Latin 'Augustinus', meaning 'of Augustus'.
