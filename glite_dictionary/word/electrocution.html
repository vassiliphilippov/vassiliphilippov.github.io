electrocution

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛk.trəˈkju.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəˈkjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of executing a person by passing electric current through their body as a legal punishment
execution by electrical current

Examples

The prisoner was sentenced to electrocution after being found guilty of murder.
Public debate about the use of electrocution as a method of capital punishment has continued for decades.
The state no longer carries out electrocution and has switched to lethal injection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of putting a person to death, especially as a legal penalty

Etymology

From 'electro-' (electricity) and 'execution'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛk.trəˈkjuː.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəˈkjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of killing someone by passing a strong electric current through their body
killing by electric shock

Examples

The accidental electrocution of the worker prompted an investigation into safety standards.
He faced electrocution after being convicted of murder in a state that still used the electric chair.
The report detailed several cases of electrocution caused by faulty household wiring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of carrying out a death sentence

Etymology

Formed from 'electro-' (electric) + 'execution', first used in the late 19th century.
