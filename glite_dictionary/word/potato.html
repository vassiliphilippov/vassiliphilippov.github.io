potato

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
An annual plant native to South America, grown for its edible starchy tubers, which are widely cultivated as a vegetable; the rest of the plant is poisonous.
starchy tuberous plant, vegetable

Examples

We planted potatoes in the garden this spring and harvested them in the fall.
The potato plant has green leaves above ground but is valued for its tubers below ground.
After the blight destroyed the potato crop, farmers faced a difficult season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet potato - a tuberous root vegetable with a sweet taste

Etymology

From Spanish *patata*, from Taino *batata* (sweet potato) and Quechua *papa* (potato).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪdoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A round or oval edible tuber with brown, yellow, or red skin and white, yellow, or purple flesh, originally from South America, commonly used as a staple food.
edible starchy tuber vegetable

Examples

She bought a bag of russet potatoes at the market for dinner.
The Irish famine in the 1840s was partly caused by a potato blight.
He prefers his potatoes mashed with a little butter and cream.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet potato - a different edible tuber with orange flesh

Etymology

From Spanish 'patata', originally from Taíno 'batata' (sweet potato) and Quechua 'papa' (potato).
