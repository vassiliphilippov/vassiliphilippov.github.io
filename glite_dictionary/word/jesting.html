jesting

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
characterized by jokes and good humor; joking or playful in manner.
joking; playful in manner

Examples

His jesting remarks lightened the mood during the tense meeting.
She adopted a jesting tone when discussing the mistakes, so no one felt offended.
Despite his jesting manner, he was deeply committed to his work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serious - not joking or playful in manner
  • Synonym: jocular - fond of or characterized by joking

Etymology

From Middle English 'jeste' (a narrative or a joke) and '-ing', denoting the act or characteristic.
