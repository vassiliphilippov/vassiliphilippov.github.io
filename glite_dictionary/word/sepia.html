Sepia

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːpiə / (AmE), / ˈsiːpiə / (BrE)
The type genus of the Sepiidae, comprising cuttlefish, especially those found in the Mediterranean and adjacent seas.
type genus of cuttlefish family

Examples

The Mediterranean is home to several species in the genus Sepia.
Marine biologists often study Sepia for its remarkable camouflage abilities.
The ink produced by Sepia was historically used in drawing and painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuttlefish - a marine mollusk of the order Sepiida, known for its ability to eject ink

Etymology

From Latin *sepia*, from Greek *sēpia* (cuttlefish, ink of cuttlefish).
