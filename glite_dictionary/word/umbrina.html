Umbrina

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbriːnə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbriːnə / (BrE)
a genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, commonly known as drums, comprising about 16 species found in coastal and estuarine waters
genus of fish in the drum family

Examples

The genus Umbrina includes species commonly found in subtropical marine environments.
Researchers recently described a new member of the Umbrina genus in Southeast Asian waters.
Fishermen occasionally catch Umbrina species for local consumption and the aquarium trade.

Etymology

From Latin *umbrina* (a type of fish, possibly derived from *umbra*, meaning shadow).
