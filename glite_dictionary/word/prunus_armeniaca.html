Prunus armeniaca

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ɑːrˌmiːniˈeɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ɑː(ˌ)miːnɪˈeɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to temperate regions, cultivated for its edible downy yellow to rosy apricot fruits.
apricot tree species (scientific name)

Examples

The orchard was full of Prunus armeniaca trees heavy with ripe fruit.
Botanists classify the apricot under the scientific name Prunus armeniaca.
A specimen of Prunus armeniaca was planted in the botanical garden for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apricot - the fruit or tree produced by Prunus armeniaca

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Armenian plum', referring to its supposed origins in Armenia.
