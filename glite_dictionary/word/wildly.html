wildly

1. ADV

/ ˈwaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldli / (BrE)
in a way that is uncontrolled, unrestrained, or lacking discipline
in an uncontrolled or unrestrained way

Examples

The crowd cheered wildly when the team scored the winning goal.
He waved his arms wildly to attract the driver's attention.
The storm caused the trees to sway wildly in the wind.

Synset Examples

He gesticulated wildly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessly - without thinking about danger or consequences
  • Antonym: calmly - in a controlled, composed way

Etymology

Formed from "wild" + suffix "-ly" making an adverb.
