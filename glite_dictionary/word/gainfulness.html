gainfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪnfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of affording gain, benefit, or profit
quality of being profitable or beneficial

Examples

The gainfulness of the investment was the main reason we decided to proceed.
She evaluated the gainfulness of each project before allocating resources.
Some jobs may offer satisfaction in other ways, but their gainfulness is minimal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitability - the quality of being profitable
  • Antonym: unprofitability - the quality of being unprofitable

Etymology

From 'gainful' (profitable) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
