pasty

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪsti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsti / (BrE)
(of a person's face or skin) pale and unhealthy in appearance; resembling paste in color.
pale, unhealthy-looking (of skin)

Examples

After the illness, his pasty complexion was a cause for concern among his friends.
She looked pasty and tired after staying up all night to study.
The dim light made his face appear even more pasty than usual.

Synset Examples

he looked pasty and red-eyed
a complexion that had been pastelike was now chalky white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - pale, typically because of poor health
  • Antonym: ruddy - having a healthy, reddish color

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæsti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsti / (BrE)
A small pastry case filled with meat, vegetables, or other ingredients, baked or fried; especially one typical of Cornwall, England.
small meat pie or turnover
british

Examples

We bought a hot pasty from the bakery for lunch.
The traditional Cornish pasty is filled with beef, potatoes, and onions.
She made a batch of homemade pasties for the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnover - a type of pastry made by folding dough over a filling
  • Synonym: pie - a baked dish of fruit, meat, or vegetables covered with pastry

Etymology

From Cornish *pasti* (pie, pastry), related to Middle French *paste* (pastry).
