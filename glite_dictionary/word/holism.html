holism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊ.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The theory that the parts of a whole cannot exist or be understood except in relation to the whole.
philosophical theory about parts and wholes

Examples

Holism suggests that we must study the entire ecosystem rather than isolated parts.
Many psychologists now consider holism essential to understanding human behavior.
Some critics argue that holism can overlook the importance of individual components.

Synset Examples

holism holds that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts
holistic theory has been applied to ecology and language and mental states

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reductionism - the theory that complex systems can be understood by reducing them to their parts

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' meaning 'whole', with '-ism' denoting a system or theory; coined in early 20th century.
