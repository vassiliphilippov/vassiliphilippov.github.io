vendor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛndər / (AmE), / ˈvɛndə / (BrE)
a person or company that sells goods or services, especially in a market or as a supplier
person or company selling goods or services

Examples

The food vendor on the corner sells the best hot dogs in the city.
We negotiated the price directly with the software vendor before signing the contract.
Each vendor at the craft fair decorated their stall to attract more customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seller - a person who sells something
  • Antonym: buyer - a person who purchases goods or services

Etymology

From Latin 'venditor' (seller), derived from 'vendere' (to sell).
