Mesua

1. NOUN

/ məˈsuːə / (AmE), / mɛˈzuːə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asiatic trees known for their large, solitary flowers.
genus of tropical trees with large flowers

Examples

The Mesua trees in the garden bloom with beautiful white flowers every spring.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Mesua species growing in Southeast Asia.
A rare Mesua specimen was planted in the botanical conservatory for research purposes.

Etymology

From Sanskrit मेष (meṣa), the name for the tree, adopted into botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ məˈsuːə / (AmE), / mɪˈsuːə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asian trees in the family Calophyllaceae, known for their large solitary flowers.
genus of tropical Asian trees

Examples

The Mesua genus is notable for its fragrant white flowers and glossy leaves.
Many species in Mesua are cultivated for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
Scientists recently identified a new species within the Mesua genus in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after Jean Mesue, a medieval Arabic physician and scholar (via Latin scientific naming).
