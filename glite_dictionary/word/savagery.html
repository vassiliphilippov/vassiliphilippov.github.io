savagery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæv.ɪdʒ.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsæv.ɪdʒ.ər.i / (BrE)
cruel and violent behavior; the quality of being extremely cruel or brutal.
extreme cruelty or brutality

Examples

The savagery of the attack shocked the entire community.
Historical records describe the savagery of ancient battles in vivid detail.
Her speech condemned the savagery of war and called for peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutality - cruel and violent treatment of people or animals
  • Synonym: barbarity - extreme cruelty or brutality
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From French 'sauvagerie', from 'sauvage' (wild), from Latin 'silvaticus' (of the woods, wild).
