upstream

1. ADV

/ ˌʌpˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction toward the source of a river or stream, or against the flow of a current
toward the source of a river or stream

Examples

The canoeists paddled upstream against the strong current.
Pollution can be traced to factories located upstream from the city.
The salmon swim upstream each year to spawn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstream - in the direction away from the source of a river or stream; with the current
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00097658-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ʌpˈstriːm / (AmE), / ʌpˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction opposite to the flow of a stream or river; towards the source
against a stream's current

Examples

The salmon swam upstream to reach their breeding grounds.
Pollution from factories located upstream can affect water quality.
They paddled upstream for several miles before stopping for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstream - in the direction of a stream's current; towards where it flows

Etymology

From up + stream, meaning towards the higher part of a river.
