confusedness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfjuːzɪdnəs / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪdnɪs / (BrE)
a state of being confused, characterized by a lack of clear and logical thought or behavior
state of being confused

Examples

Her confusedness during the meeting was obvious to everyone in the room.
Periods of confusedness often occur when someone is under intense stress.
The sudden news brought a sense of confusedness among the team members.

Synset Examples

a confusion of impressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a lack of clarity or order; bewilderment
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From 'confused' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the quality or state of being confused.
