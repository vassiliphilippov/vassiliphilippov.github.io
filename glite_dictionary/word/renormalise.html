to renormalise

1. VERB

/ riːˈnɔːrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make normal again or adjust something once more to conform to a standard, especially after an earlier normalization; commonly used in mathematics and physics.
adjust again to a standard; especially in science

Examples

After the data set was distorted, scientists decided to renormalise the results to allow proper comparison.
Quantum field theory often requires physicists to renormalise certain values to avoid infinite results.
To account for changes in experimental conditions, the team had to renormalise the measurements.

Synset Examples

normalize relations with China
normalize the temperature
normalize the spelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to normalize - make or become normal or standard
  • Antonym: deviate - depart from an established standard or norm

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + normalise, from Latin normalis (according to a rule or standard).
