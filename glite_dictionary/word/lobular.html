lobular

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːbjələr / (AmE), / ˈlɒbjʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a lobule, which is a small lobe or subdivision of a lobe in an organ or tissue.
relating to a small lobe (lobule)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the tumor had a lobular structure under the microscope.
Certain types of breast cancer are classified as lobular carcinoma.
This disease leads to lobular inflammation within the liver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobule - a small lobe or subdivision of a lobe in an organ or tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'lobulus', diminutive of 'lobus' (lobe).
