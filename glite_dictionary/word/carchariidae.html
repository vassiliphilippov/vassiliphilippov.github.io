Carchariidae

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈkɛəriɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɑːˈkɛərɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of sand sharks, sometimes considered to overlap with or be coextensive with the family Carcharhinidae
family of sand sharks

Examples

Fossils of the Carchariidae family have been found in sediments dating back millions of years.
The sand tiger shark belongs to the Carchariidae, commonly found in temperate and tropical waters.
Some taxonomists classify certain shark species into Carchariidae rather than Carcharhinidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carcharhinidae - the family of requiem sharks, sometimes considered to overlap with Carchariidae

Etymology

From New Latin Carchariidae, based on the type genus Carcharias.
