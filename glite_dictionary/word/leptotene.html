leptotene

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛp.təˌtin / (AmE), / ˈlɛp.təˌtiːn / (BrE)
the first stage of the prophase in meiosis, during which chromosomes begin to condense and become visible as thin threads
first stage of prophase in meiosis
medical

Examples

During the leptotene stage, chromosomes start to condense and are visible under a microscope.
Researchers observed defects in meiotic division during the leptotene phase of cell development.
The transition from leptotene to zygotene is marked by the pairing of homologous chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zygotene - second stage of prophase in meiosis

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (thin) + 'tainia' (band or ribbon), referring to the threadlike appearance of chromosomes.
