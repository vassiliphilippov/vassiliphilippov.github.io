bedsore

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.sɔː / (BrE)
A sore or ulcer on the skin caused by prolonged pressure on a part of the body, especially in people who are confined to bed for long periods.
ulcer from prolonged skin pressure
medical

Examples

The nurse checked the patient's back for any signs of a bedsore.
He developed a painful bedsore after being bedridden for several weeks.
Proper repositioning can help prevent bedsores in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure ulcer - ulcer caused by prolonged pressure on the skin
  • Synonym: decubitus ulcer - medical term for a bedsore

Etymology

Formed from 'bed' + 'sore', first attested in the 19th century.
