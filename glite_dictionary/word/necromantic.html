necromantic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɛkrəˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɛkrəˈmantɪk / (BrE)
relating to or connected with necromancy, the supposed practice of communicating with the dead to predict the future or perform magic
relating to necromancy or the supernatural

Examples

The ancient text was filled with necromantic incantations and spells.
She was fascinated by the necromantic rituals described in folklore.
The movie's plot centers on a necromantic cult attempting to raise the dead.

Synset Examples

mysterious necromantic rites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necromancy - the supposed practice of communicating with the dead, especially to predict the future

Etymology

From Late Latin necromanticus, from necromantia (necromancy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɛkrəˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɛkrəˈmantɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of necromancy, especially involving the supposed art of communicating with or conjuring up the dead.
relating to necromancy or conjuring the dead

Examples

The necromantic rituals described in the ancient book were said to raise spirits from their graves.
Her fascination with necromantic powers led her to collect old, mysterious artifacts.
In the play, a necromantic sorcerer attempts to summon the ghost of a fallen king.

Synset Examples

a necromantic sorcerer
necromantic delusions
necromantic powders and other weird objects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necromancy - the supposed practice of communicating with the dead
  • Synonym: magical - relating to magic or enchantment

Etymology

From necromancy, ultimately from Greek 'nekromanteia' (divination by the dead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01581254-s
Synset POS: s
