paralytic

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌparəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person who is suffering from paralysis
person suffering from paralysis
archaic medical

Examples

The hospital cared for several paralytics who needed constant assistance.
In the 19th century, facilities for paralytics were limited and often inadequate.
She dedicated her life to improving the lives of paralytics through her charity work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move

Etymology

From Late Latin paralyticus, from Ancient Greek paralytikos (relating to paralysis).
