Melanerpes erythrocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnɜrpiz ˌɛrɪˌθroʊsɪˈfæləs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənˈɜːpiːz ˌɛrɪθrəʊsɪˈfæləs / (BrE)
A black-and-white North American woodpecker with a distinctive red head and neck, commonly known as the red-headed woodpecker.
species of North American woodpecker

Examples

The Melanerpes erythrocephalus is noted for its vibrant red head and striking black-and-white plumage.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the Melanerpes erythrocephalus population in some regions.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Melanerpes erythrocephalus during their morning walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-headed woodpecker - Common name for Melanerpes erythrocephalus, a North American woodpecker

Etymology

From Greek melan- (black) + herpēs (creeper) and erythros (red) + kephalē (head).
