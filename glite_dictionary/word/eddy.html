eddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdi / (AmE), / ˈɛdi / (BrE)
a small whirlpool or whirlwind formed when a fluid current doubles back on itself
a small whirlpool or whirlwind

Examples

Leaves spun in a tiny eddy at the edge of the pond.
The canoe drifted dangerously close to an eddy beneath the bridge.
Dust rose in an eddy as the wind whipped through the alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirlpool - a rapidly rotating mass of water
  • Synonym: vortex - a mass of whirling fluid or air

Etymology

From Middle English *edy*, from Old English *edy*, from Proto-Germanic *edwō* (whirlpool).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛdi / (AmE), / ˈɛdi / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) to move or flow in a circular motion or current.
move in a circular current

Examples

Mist began to eddy around the trees in the early morning light.
Leaves eddied in the wake of the passing car.
Tiny fish eddy in small pools formed by the stream's uneven flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swirl - to move in a twisting or spiraling pattern

Etymology

From Middle English 'edy', from Old English 'edēa' (whirlpool), related to 'ed-' (again, against) + 'ēa' (stream, river).

Synset POS: v
