Betula pendula

1. NOUN

/ bɛˈtʃulə ˈpɛndjələ / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːlə ˈpɛndjʊlə / (BrE)
A species of birch native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its silvery white peeling bark and drooping branches.
European birch tree species

Examples

The Betula pendula is prized for its striking white bark and elegant silhouette in landscape gardens.
Botanists planted several Betula pendula to restore the native woodland habitat.
The drooping branches of the Betula pendula create a graceful canopy in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver birch - Common name for Betula pendula, a birch tree with white bark

Etymology

From Latin: 'betula' meaning 'birch' and 'pendula' meaning 'hanging' or 'drooping'.
