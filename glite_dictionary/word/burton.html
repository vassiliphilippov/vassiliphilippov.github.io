Burton

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrtən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːtən / (BrE)
A strong dark English ale, historically brewed in the town of Burton upon Trent.
a strong dark English ale
british

Examples

He ordered a pint of Burton at the traditional English pub.
The dark color and rich flavor set Burton apart from lighter ales.
Connoisseurs praise the complex maltiness of a well-brewed Burton.

Etymology

Named after Burton upon Trent, an English brewing town known for its ales.
