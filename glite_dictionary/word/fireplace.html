fireplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌpleɪs / (BrE)
an open space built into a wall at the base of a chimney, used for building a fire to heat a room or for decoration
open space in a wall for a fire

Examples

We gathered around the fireplace to keep warm during the winter storm.
She decorated the mantle above the fireplace with family photos and candles.
The old house still had its original stone fireplace in the living room.

Synset Examples

the fireplace was so large you could walk inside it
he laid a fire in the hearth and lit it
the hearth was black with the charcoal of many fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace, often extending into a room

Etymology

Formed from 'fire' + 'place'; attested since the 17th century.
