3. NUM

/ ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (BrE)
the number equivalent to seventy plus eight; 78
the number 78

Examples

There are seventy-eight students enrolled in the course this semester.
My grandmother just turned seventy-eight years old last week.
The collection contains seventy-eight unique postcards from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seventy-seven - the number 77, one less than seventy-eight
  • Degree Higher: seventy-nine - the number 79, one more than seventy-eight

Etymology

From 'seventy' (Old English 'seofontig') + 'eight' (Old English 'eahta').

4. NOUN

/ ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a shellac phonograph record designed to be played at 78 revolutions per minute
78 rpm phonograph record

Examples

My grandfather still keeps a collection of rare seventy-eights in his attic.
The seventy-eight was known for its durability but limited playing time.
Even modern collectors value a pristine seventy-eight for its nostalgic sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a flat disc for storing sound, played on a phonograph
  • Degree Lower: forty-five - a vinyl phonograph record playing at 45 rpm
  • Degree Lower: long play - a vinyl phonograph record playing at 33⅓ rpm, typically called 'LP'

Etymology

Refers to the rotational speed (78 rpm) of these records.
