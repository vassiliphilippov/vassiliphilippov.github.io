footwall

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌwɔːl / (BrE)
the lower side of a fault or vein in the earth's crust, especially the lower wall of an inclined fault.
lower wall of an inclined fault

Examples

The mineral deposit was found along the footwall of the fault.
Miners often distinguish between the footwall and the hanging wall in underground operations.
The earthquake caused a shift along the footwall, impacting the surrounding landscape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hanging wall - the upper side of a fault or vein in the earth's crust

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'wall', referring to the side on which one could stand if the fault surface were exposed and inclined.
