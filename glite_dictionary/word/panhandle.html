panhandle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (BrE)
a narrow strip of territory projecting from the main part of a larger area, often likened to the handle of a pan
narrow projecting strip of land

Examples

Oklahoma's panhandle is known for its wide-open spaces and flat terrain.
The Florida panhandle suffered heavy damage during the hurricane.
Alaska's southeastern panhandle includes many islands and coastal towns.

Synset Examples

Wheeling is located in the northern panhandle of West Virginia

Etymology

From the resemblance to the handle of a cooking pan.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (BrE)
the handle attached to a pan, used for holding or lifting it
handle of a pan

Examples

Be careful, the panhandle is very hot right after cooking.
She gripped the panhandle firmly as she moved the skillet to the table.
The panhandle broke off, so we had to throw the saucepan away.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03888657-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpanˌhandl̩ / (BrE)
to beg for money by approaching people in public places, especially on the street
ask strangers for money in the street
american informal

Examples

He lost his job and had to panhandle on the streets to survive.
Tourists were often approached by people who would panhandle near the busy subway station.
It's illegal to panhandle in certain parts of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask people for money or food, usually because you are poor

Etymology

Originally American slang, likely referring to the image of a pan handle being held out for coins.
