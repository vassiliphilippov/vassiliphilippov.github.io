belittled

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈlɪt.əld / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.əld / (BrE)
made to seem less important, valuable, or worthy; disparaged or diminished in status or reputation
made to seem less important or valued

Examples

Her achievements were unfairly belittled by her critics in the media.
He often feels belittled when his ideas are ignored in meetings.
The director's harsh words left the actor feeling belittled and unappreciated.

Synset Examples

her comments made me feel small

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparaged - regarded or represented as being of little worth
  • Antonym: praised - expressed warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

From 'belittle', formed by prefix 'be-' + 'little' (early 19th century, first recorded 1781).
