Paipai

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪˌpaɪ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people living in Baja California, Mexico, who speak the Paipai language, a Hokan language.
Native American people of Baja California

Examples

The Paipai have preserved many aspects of their traditional culture.
Researchers have studied the Paipai language to understand its roots within the Hokan family.
Several families in Baja California still identify as Paipai.

Etymology

Name of the group in their own language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪˌpaɪ / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous community, also called Akwa'ala, living in Baja California, Mexico.
a member of an indigenous Baja California community

Examples

The Paipai have preserved much of their traditional language and customs.
Anthropologists spent several months living with the Paipai to learn about their culture.
There are only a few hundred Paipai living in Baja California today.
