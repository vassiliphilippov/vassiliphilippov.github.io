obstinance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.stɪ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being stubborn and difficult to persuade or overcome
stubbornness; difficult to persuade

Examples

Her obstinance made it nearly impossible to reach a compromise during the negotiation.
Despite the evidence, his obstinance kept him from admitting he was wrong.
The team's obstinance frustrated their coach, who wanted them to try new tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being adaptable or willing to change

Etymology

From Latin obstinantia (firmness, stubbornness), related to obstinatus (resolute, stubborn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑbstɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɒbstɪnəns / (BrE)
the quality of stubbornly or unreasonably sticking to one’s own ideas, opinions, or desires
stubborn adherence to own views

Examples

His obstinance made it impossible to reach a compromise during the meeting.
She admired his obstinance in standing up for what he believed was right.
Despite the advice of his friends, Tom’s obstinance prevented him from changing his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or unyielding; alternative form
  • Synonym: stubbornness - unwillingness to change one's attitude or position
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or adapt; the opposite quality

Etymology

From Latin 'obstinātia', via Old French 'obstinance', meaning persistent stubbornness.
