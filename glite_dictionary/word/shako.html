shako

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
a tall, usually cylindrical military hat with a visor, often adorned with a plume, worn especially on ceremonial occasions by members of certain military units
tall military ceremonial hat

Examples

The soldier adjusted his shako before marching in the ceremonial parade.
On special occasions, some British regiments still wear the traditional shako.
The museum displayed a beautifully decorated shako from the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busby - military fur hat worn by hussars and certain British regiments
  • Synonym: bearskin - tall fur cap worn by guards regiments

Etymology

From French *shako*, originally from Hungarian *csákó* (a type of cap), related to *csák* (peak, projecting point).
