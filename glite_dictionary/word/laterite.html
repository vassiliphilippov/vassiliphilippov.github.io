laterite

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.t̬əˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.təˌraɪt / (BrE)
A reddish soil formed by the decomposition of rocks, rich in iron and aluminum oxides, commonly found in tropical regions.
red tropical soil rich in iron and aluminum oxides

Examples

Extensive areas of India are covered with laterite, which supports only sparse vegetation.
Some tropical countries use laterite blocks as a building material due to their hardness when dry.
The road construction project was delayed because heavy rain made the laterite soil too soft to support vehicles.

Etymology

From Latin 'later' (brick), referring to its brick-red color and use.
