undisciplined

1. ADJ

/ ˌəndɪˈsɪplɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
lacking self-control or the ability to follow rules or order; not disciplined.
lacking discipline or control

Examples

The classroom descended into chaos due to the undisciplined behavior of the students.
She is talented but her undisciplined approach often leads to mistakes.
An undisciplined athlete may struggle to reach their full potential.

Synset Examples

undisciplined behavior
ungoverned youth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disciplined - showing control or obedience to rules; opposite of undisciplined

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'disciplined' (from Latin disciplina, instruction, training).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈdɪsəplɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdɪsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
lacking in discipline or self-control; not subjected to rules or correction.
lacking discipline or control

Examples

The children's undisciplined behavior made it difficult for the teacher to maintain order.
She found the undisciplined team hard to coach and often struggled to keep them focused.
His undisciplined financial habits eventually led him into debt.

Synset Examples

let her children grow up uncorrected

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disciplined - showing or possessing discipline or self-control

Etymology

From un- (not) + disciplined, based on Latin disciplina (instruction, training).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01908425-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈsɪplɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
not showing control, self-restraint, or obedience to rules; lacking discipline.
lacking discipline or control

Examples

His undisciplined behavior caused problems in class.
The team lost because their play was too undisciplined and chaotic.
She has incredible talent, but it's still quite undisciplined.

Synset Examples

undisciplined talent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disciplined - showing self-control or obedience to rules
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or manage; disorderly
