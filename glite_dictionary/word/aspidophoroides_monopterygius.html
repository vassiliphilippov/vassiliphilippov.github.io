Aspidophoroides monopterygius

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspɪdəˌfɔːrɔɪˈdiːz ˌmɒnpɪˈtrɪdʒiəs / (AmE), / ˌæspɪdəˌfɔːrəˈdiːz ˌmɒnpɪˈtrɪdʒɪəs / (BrE)
A small, very elongate species of sea poacher found in marine waters, belonging to the family Agonidae.
a species of elongate sea poacher fish
medical

Examples

The Aspidophoroides monopterygius is primarily found in cold North Atlantic waters.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of Aspidophoroides monopterygius along the rocky sea floor.
Due to its distinctive shape, Aspidophoroides monopterygius is easy to distinguish from other poacher species.

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'aspis' (shield), 'phoros' (bearing), and 'monopterygius' (single-finned).
