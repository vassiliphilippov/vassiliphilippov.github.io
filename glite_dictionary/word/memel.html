Memel

1. PROPN

/ ˈmeɪmɛl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪmɛl / (BrE)
a city in western Lithuania on the Baltic Sea, historically a significant trading port of the Hanseatic League, now called Klaipėda.
historical city in Lithuania (now Klaipėda)

Examples

The port of Memel was once a vital center of trade in the Baltic region.
After World War I, Memel became a contentious area among European powers.
Today, the city formerly known as Memel is called Klaipėda.

Etymology

Name of the city in German, from Old Prussian *memelis* ('quiet', referring to the River Nemunas).
