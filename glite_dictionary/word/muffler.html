muffler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌflər / (AmE), / ˈmʌflə / (BrE)
A device used to reduce noise or vibrations, particularly from engines or machinery.
device reducing noise or vibration

Examples

The muffler on my car needs to be replaced because it's making too much noise.
Modern recording studios often use a muffler to limit unwanted acoustical oscillations.
Some musicians place a muffler inside their drum to reduce the volume and resonance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silencer - device for reducing noise produced by machinery or firearms

Etymology

From English 'muffle' (to deaden sound) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌflɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌflə / (BrE)
A device in a vehicle's exhaust system that reduces engine noise.
device reducing vehicle engine noise

Examples

The mechanic replaced the muffler because it was making a loud rattling noise.
Driving with a damaged muffler can result in a hefty fine due to noise regulations.
The car's new muffler made the ride much quieter and more comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silencer - device that reduces engine noise, especially in exhaust systems
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04225631-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌf.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.lə / (BrE)
A scarf or wrap worn around the neck to keep it warm.
scarf worn around the neck

Examples

He wrapped his woolen muffler snugly around his neck before stepping outside.
She wore a blue muffler to protect herself from the chilly wind.
During winter, a thick muffler is essential to stay warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarf - a length or square of fabric worn around the neck or head

Etymology

From English 'muffle' (to wrap up), influenced by the idea of covering or wrapping.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
