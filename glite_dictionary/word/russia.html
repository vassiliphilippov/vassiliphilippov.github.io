Russia

1. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
a former empire in eastern Europe and northern Asia, ruled from the 14th century with Moscow as its capital, reaching its height under Peter the Great and Catherine the Great, and overthrown by the 1917 revolution
former empire in eastern Europe and northern Asia

Examples

The borders of Russia expanded significantly during the reign of Peter the Great.
In 1917, the revolution brought an end to imperial Russia.
Saint Petersburg served as the capital of Russia for much of the 18th and 19th centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Soviet Union - a former federal socialist state in Eurasia (1922–1991) that succeeded the Russian Empire

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Russia, from Rus, the medieval people and state in the region.

2. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
A country in northeastern Europe and northern Asia, officially the Russian Federation, formed after the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991.
country in northeastern Europe and northern Asia

Examples

The president of Russia addressed the nation on television.
Tourists often visit Moscow and Saint Petersburg when traveling to Russia.
After 1991, Russia became an independent country distinct from the Soviet Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian Federation - official name of Russia
  • Antonym: Soviet Union - federation that preceded modern Russia

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Russia', from Old East Slavic 'Rusĭ', referring to the people and lands of the Kievan Rus'.
