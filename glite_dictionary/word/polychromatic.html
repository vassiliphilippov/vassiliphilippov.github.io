polychromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊliˈkroʊmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪkrəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of light or other electromagnetic radiation) consisting of several wavelengths or colors.
having multiple wavelengths or colors

Examples

A prism can separate polychromatic light into its individual colors.
Unlike monochromatic lasers, polychromatic sources emit a broad spectrum of light.
The vivid patterns on the butterfly's wings appear almost polychromatic in sunlight.

Synset Examples

polychromatic light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochromatic - (of light) consisting of one wavelength or color

Etymology

From Greek polychromatos, from poly- (many) + chroma (color).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːlikroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪkrəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Having or exhibiting many different colors; multicolored.
having many colors; multicolored

Examples

The artist’s polychromatic paintings brightened the entire gallery with vivid hues.
Sunlight filtered through the stained glass, casting polychromatic patterns across the floor.
The butterfly's polychromatic wings shimmered in the afternoon light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochromatic - having only one color
  • Synonym: multicolored - having many different colors

Etymology

From Greek polychromatos, from poly- (many) + chrōma (color).

Synset POS: s
