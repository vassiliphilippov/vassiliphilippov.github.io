promisee

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːməˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌprɒmɪˈsiː / (BrE)
the person to whom a promise is made, especially in a legal context
person to whom a promise is made
legal

Examples

As the promisee, she was entitled to claim damages if the agreement was broken.
The contract specified the rights and obligations of both the promisee and the promisor.
If the promisee waives the promise, the promisor may be released from their obligation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: promisor - person who makes a promise

Etymology

Formed from 'promise' + '-ee', modelled on legal terminology (as in 'payee', 'lessee') from French '-é', from Latin '-atus'.
