Grozny

1. PROPN

/ ˈgroʊzni / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒzni / (BrE)
the capital city of Chechnya in southwestern Russia, known as a center of extensive oil fields
capital of Chechnya, Russia

Examples

After years of conflict, Grozny has undergone significant rebuilding and transformation.
Many people travel to Grozny to witness its unique blend of modern architecture and history.
The oil industry plays a central role in the economy of Grozny.

Etymology

From Russian Грозный (Grozny), meaning 'terrible' or 'formidable'.
