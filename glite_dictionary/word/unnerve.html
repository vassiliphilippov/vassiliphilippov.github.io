to unnerve

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈnɜrv / (AmE), / ʌnˈnɜːv / (BrE)
to make someone feel less confident or nervous; to disturb the composure of someone
make someone feel nervous or insecure

Examples

The unexpected question seemed to unnerve the speaker during the interview.
Loud noises at night can easily unnerve even the bravest of people.
Her constant criticism started to unnerve him after a while.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt the normal state of something or someone
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone feel relaxed or tranquil

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'nerve' (courage, composure), originally meaning to deprive of nerve or strength.
