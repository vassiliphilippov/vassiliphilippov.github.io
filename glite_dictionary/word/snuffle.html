snuffle

1. VERB

/ ˈsnʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌf.əl / (BrE)
to breathe noisily through a blocked or congested nose, often by snuffing up mucus
breathe noisily through nose

Examples

He began to snuffle as the cold took hold of him.
Allergy season makes her snuffle constantly at her desk.
The child snuffled noisily while looking for a tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - to sniff repeatedly, typically because of a cold or crying
  • Synonym: snort - to force air noisily through the nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, related to 'sniff' and 'snuff'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101533-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsnʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfəl / (BrE)
to sniff or smell by breathing noisily through the nose, often in an inquisitive or searching manner
to sniff inquiringly or noisily

Examples

The dog began to snuffle around the base of the tree, searching for hidden treats.
She could hear him snuffling at the door, curious about what was inside.
Bears often snuffle the ground for food as they wander through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniff - to draw air audibly through the nose to detect a smell

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of sniffing; related to 'sniff' and 'snuff'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02130617-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsnʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfəl / (BrE)
to cry or whine in a way that involves making snuffling sounds, especially when one's nose is blocked or one is sniffing repeatedly
cry or whine with snuffling sounds

Examples

The child began to snuffle quietly after being told off.
She couldn't help but snuffle as she explained what had upset her.
He snuffled through his words, tears running down his cheeks.

Synset Examples

Stop snivelling — you got yourself into this mess!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snivel - to cry and sniffle, complain in a tearful way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067045-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfəl / (BrE)
the act or sound of breathing noisily through the nose, especially when it is blocked or congested
noisy nasal breathing

Examples

He had a snuffle from his cold all through the meeting.
The child's constant snuffle worried his mother.
You could hear her snuffle as she tried to breathe through a blocked nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - an act of sniffing repeatedly due to a runny nose

Etymology

Imitative of the act of sniffing or sniffling; related to 'sniff' and 'snivel'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837810-n
Synset POS: n
