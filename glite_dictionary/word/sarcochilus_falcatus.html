Sarcochilus falcatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑrkoʊˈkaɪləs fælˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəˈkaɪləs falˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A small Australian orchid with loosely arranged racemes of fragrant white flowers marked with purple and orange on the lip.
Australian orchid species

Examples

The delicate Sarcochilus falcatus can be found clinging to tree branches in eastern Australia.
Many orchid enthusiasts prize Sarcochilus falcatus for its beautifully marked white flowers.
We spotted a rare Sarcochilus falcatus blooming during our rainforest hike.

Etymology

From the genus name Sarcochilus (from Greek 'sarx' meaning 'flesh' and 'cheilos' meaning 'lip') and the species Latin 'falcatus' meaning 'sickle-shaped'.
