chattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a high-pitched, continuous noise, especially that made by birds or monkeys
high-pitched animal noise

Examples

The constant chattering of the monkeys echoed through the rainforest canopy.
We woke up to the chattering of sparrows outside our window at dawn.
The zoo was filled with the excited chattering of parrots and other tropical birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirping - short, sharp, high-pitched sounds made by small birds or insects
  • Synonym: twittering - continuous light, high-pitched sounds made by birds

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬ərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætərɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid, repeated series of noises, especially made by the moving parts of a machine
rapid repeated noise from a machine

Examples

The constant chattering of the gears made it clear that the engine needed maintenance.
He was concerned by the chattering from the computer's fan every time it was turned on.
Reducing chattering in the machine will improve both its performance and its lifespan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattling - a series of short, sharp noises, often from something shaking or vibrating

Etymology

From the verb 'chatter', from Middle English 'chateren'; of imitative origin.
