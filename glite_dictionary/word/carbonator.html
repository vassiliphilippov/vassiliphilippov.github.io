carbonator

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a device or machine designed to add carbon dioxide to a liquid, especially to create carbonated beverages.
device for adding carbon dioxide to liquids

Examples

The new carbonator in our kitchen lets us make sparkling water whenever we want.
The technician repaired the faulty carbonator in the soda dispenser at the restaurant.
Compact carbonators are now available for home use, making it easy to create custom sodas.

Synset Examples

Detailed and simple plans to build small in-home carbonator for under $100 in parts are freely available on the internet and can produce a liter of carbonated water for less than 2 US cents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonate - to dissolve carbon dioxide in a liquid under pressure

Etymology

From 'carbonate' + '-or', following the pattern of agent nouns denoting devices or people that perform an action.
