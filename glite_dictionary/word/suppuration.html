suppuration

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌpjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) the formation or discharge of pus, typically as a result of infection or inflammation.
formation or discharge of pus
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted significant suppuration at the site of the wound.
Proper cleaning of the injury can prevent suppuration and further complications.
Chronic suppuration may be a sign of an underlying infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pus - thick, yellowish fluid produced by infected tissue

Etymology

From Latin suppuratio(n-), from suppurare 'to form pus', from sub- (under) + pus, pur- (pus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌpjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of forming or discharging pus as a result of inflammation.
formation of pus by inflammation
medical

Examples

The wound showed signs of suppuration and required additional medical attention.
Chronic suppuration often indicates an ongoing infection that needs to be treated.
After a few days, suppuration began at the site of the injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pus - thick, yellowish-white fluid produced by infected tissue

Etymology

From Latin suppuratio (the act of making pus), from suppurare (to form pus).
