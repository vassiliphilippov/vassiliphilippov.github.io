torchlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the light produced by a torch or torches, especially in darkness
light from a torch

Examples

The castle corridor was dimly lit by torchlight flickering on the stone walls.
They explored the ancient cave using only torchlight to guide their way.
The parade moved through the streets, illuminated by torchlight and cheers from the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torch - a portable light typically consisting of a flaming stick

Etymology

From 'torch' + 'light'; 'torch' from Old French *torche* (twisted thing, torch), from Vulgar Latin *torca*.
