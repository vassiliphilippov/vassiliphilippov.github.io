Vladimir Ilich Lenin

1. PROPN

/ vlɑːˈdɪmɪr iˈlitʃ ˈlɛnɪn / (AmE), / vləˈdɪmɪə iˈlɪtʃ ˈlɛnɪn / (BrE)
(1870–1924) Russian revolutionary leader and founder of the Bolsheviks; the first head of the Soviet Union.
Russian revolutionary leader and first Soviet head

Examples

Vladimir Ilich Lenin played a central role in the October Revolution of 1917.
Many biographies have been written about Vladimir Ilich Lenin and his political theories.
After his death, Vladimir Ilich Lenin became a symbol of Soviet communism.

Etymology

Lenin adopted the surname to conceal his identity; 'Lenin' is thought to derive from the Lena River in Siberia. His birth name was Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov.
