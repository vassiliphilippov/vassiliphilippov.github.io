bedlam

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛd.ləm / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.ləm / (BrE)
A scene or situation of chaotic noise and confusion.
a state of chaos or confusion

Examples

When the fire alarm rang, the office instantly descended into bedlam.
After the referee's controversial decision, the stadium was a scene of bedlam.
The classroom became pure bedlam when the teacher stepped out for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: order - a state of being orderly and organized

Etymology

From 'Bedlam', a nickname for the Hospital of St. Mary of Bethlehem in London, which became an insane asylum.
