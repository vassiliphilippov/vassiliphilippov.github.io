Alamo

1. PROPN

/ ˈæləmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈaləməʊ / (BrE)
A mission in San Antonio, Texas, where a famous siege and massacre took place in 1836 during the Texas Revolution, when Mexican forces defeated American rebels.
site of 1836 Texas Revolution siege

Examples

The defenders of the Alamo became symbols of courage and resistance.
Many tourists visit the Alamo each year to learn about Texas history.
The phrase 'Remember the Alamo' became a rallying cry for Texan independence.

Etymology

From Spanish 'álamo' meaning 'poplar tree'; named after the mission's full Spanish name 'Misión San Antonio de Padua del Álamo'.
