to titivate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪt̬əˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make neat, smart, or attractive; to spruce up or tidy, especially in appearance
make neat or attractive

Examples

She spent an hour trying to titivate herself before the party.
He likes to titivate his desk every Monday morning to start the week fresh.
Just titivate the garden a bit and it will look much better for the guests.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make neat or smart in appearance
  • Synonym: smarten - to make or become smart or neat

Etymology

Possibly from earlier 'tidivate', an alteration of 'tidy'.
