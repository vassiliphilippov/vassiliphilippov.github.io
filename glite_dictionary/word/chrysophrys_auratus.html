Chrysophrys auratus

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsɒfrɪs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / krɪˌsɒfrɪs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
an Australian food fish with a pinkish body and blue spots, commonly known as the snapper
species of Australian food fish (snapper)

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh Chrysophrys auratus caught off the Australian coast.
Many anglers seek out Chrysophrys auratus for its flavor and fighting ability.
The pinkish body and blue spots make Chrysophrys auratus easy to identify among other fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a common name for Chrysophrys auratus, an edible marine fish

Etymology

From Greek chrysos (gold) + ophrys (eyebrow), and Latin auratus (golden).
