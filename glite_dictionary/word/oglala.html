Oglala

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈɡlɑːlə / (AmE), / əʊˈɡlɑːlə / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Oglala people, a subgroup of the Lakota
Siouan language of the Oglala people

Examples

The Oglala language is still spoken by many members of the tribe today.
Linguists are working to preserve Oglala for future generations.
She learned to introduce herself in Oglala during her cultural studies class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lakota - the Siouan language spoken by the Lakota people

Etymology

From the name of the Oglala people, a subgroup of the Lakota Sioux.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈɡlɑːlə / (AmE), / əʊˈɡlɑːlə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people who are a division of the Teton Sioux, traditionally inhabiting the Black Hills of western South Dakota.
member of a Teton Sioux Native American group

Examples

The Oglala played an important role in the preservation of the Lakota language and traditions.
She visited the Oglala community on the Pine Ridge Reservation to learn about their history.
Many Oglala leaders have contributed to efforts for Native sovereignty and cultural revitalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples including the Oglala, Dakota, and Nakota

Etymology

From the Lakota word meaning 'to scatter one's own.'
