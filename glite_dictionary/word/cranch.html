to cranch

1. VERB

/ kræntʃ / (AmE), / kræntʃ / (BrE)
to press or grind with a crushing noise
press or grind noisily

Examples

She could hear the snow cranch under his heavy boots as he approached the door.
The old wooden floor cranched every time someone stepped into the hallway.
He liked to cranch through autumn leaves on his morning walks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch - to crush or chew with a noisy crackling sound

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, related to 'crunch'.
