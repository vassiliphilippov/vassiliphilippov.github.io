ultranationalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈnæʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Extreme or fanatical nationalism, especially marked by a feeling of superiority over other countries.
extreme or fanatical nationalism

Examples

Rising ultranationalism has sparked concern among minority communities in the region.
The leader's speeches were filled with ultranationalism and aggressive rhetoric.
Historians debate the role of ultranationalism in the outbreak of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nationalism - identification with and support for one's own nation

Etymology

From ultra- (beyond) + nationalism, originally from Latin 'ultra' (beyond) and 'natio' (nation).
