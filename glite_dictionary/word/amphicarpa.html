Amphicarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A small genus of twining vines native to North America and Asia, commonly known as hog peanut.
genus of twining vines (hog peanut)

Examples

The Amphicarpa genus includes several twining vine species found in temperate forests.
Botanists recently discovered a new Amphicarpa specimen in eastern China.
Hog peanut, a member of Amphicarpa, is often used as ground cover in native gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the plant's production of different types of fruit.
