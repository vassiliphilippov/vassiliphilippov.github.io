to politicize

1. VERB

/ pəˈlɪtəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to give a political character to something or make it political in nature.
make political in nature

Examples

Some commentators tried to politicize the health crisis for their own advantage.
The organization aims to avoid politicizing scientific research.
He accused the media of politicizing the issue unnecessarily.

Synset Examples

politicize the discussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depoliticize - to remove a political character from something

Etymology

From French 'politiser', ultimately from Greek 'politikós' (of citizens, political).
