conjunctive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
serving to connect, especially words or clauses in a sentence; relating to conjunctions
serving to connect (esp. in grammar)

Examples

The word 'and' is a conjunctive that links two clauses together.
In grammar lessons, we learned about conjunctive adverbs and their functions.
Their efforts were conjunctive, working together towards the same goal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disjunctive - serving to separate; expressing contrast or alternative

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctivus', from 'conjungere' (to join together).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
involving or characterized by joint action or cooperation of two or more elements
involving joint action or cooperation

Examples

The hurricane's destruction was caused by the conjunctive force of wind and rain.
Successful negotiation relies on the conjunctive efforts of both parties involved.
In the study, researchers examined the conjunctive impact of diet and exercise on overall health.

Synset Examples

concerted action
the conjunct influence of fire and strong wind
the conjunctive focus of political opposition
a cooperative effort
a united effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concerted - done in cooperation with others
  • Synonym: cooperative - involving mutual assistance in working towards a common goal
  • Synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctivus', from 'conjungere' meaning 'to join together'.
