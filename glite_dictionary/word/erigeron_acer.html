Erigeron acer

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪdʒəˌrɒn ˈeɪsər / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪərɒn ˈeɪsə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the daisy family, commonly known as blue fleabane, with pale purple-blue flowers and widespread as a weed.
a species of blue-flowered weed

Examples

Erigeron acer grows abundantly in dry grasslands across Europe and Asia.
Gardeners often remove Erigeron acer due to its tendency to spread quickly as a weed.
The pale blue flowers of Erigeron acer can be seen blooming from midsummer to early autumn.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Erigeron, from Greek 'ērigerōn' (early old man), and specific epithet 'acer' meaning sharp.
