unsympathetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
not showing or expressing sympathy, concern, or understanding; indifferent or unfeeling toward others' problems or situations.
not showing sympathy or understanding

Examples

The manager was unsympathetic to our concerns about the new policy.
Her unsympathetic attitude made the difficult situation worse for everyone involved.
The judge appeared unsympathetic, refusing to listen to the defendant's explanation.

Synset Examples

unsympathetic officialdom
people unsympathetic to the revolution
his dignity made him seem aloof and unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic - showing sympathy or understanding
  • Synonym: indifferent - showing no interest or concern

Etymology

From un- (not) + sympathetic, from Greek sympathetikos (having sympathy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
Not evoking feelings of sympathy or approval; lacking in likable or appealing qualities, especially when referring to characters in literature or drama.
not likable; not evoking sympathy

Examples

The novel's main character was deeply unsympathetic, making it hard for readers to relate to him.
Despite their hardships, the film portrayed the family in an unsympathetic light.
Critics found the villain compelling but ultimately too unsympathetic to generate much interest.

Synset Examples

all the characters were peculiarly unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic - evoking sympathy or compassion; likable

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'sympathetic', from Greek 'sympatheia' (fellow feeling).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
