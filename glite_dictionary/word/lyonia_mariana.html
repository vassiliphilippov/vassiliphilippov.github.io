Lyonia mariana

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈoʊniə məˈriːənə / (AmE), / laɪˈəʊniə məˈriːənə / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to the coastal plains of the eastern United States, known for its nodding pinkish-white flowers and toxicity to livestock
poisonous shrub of eastern US with pinkish-white flowers

Examples

The Lyonia mariana in the wetland attracts attention with its delicate pinkish-white blooms.
Farmers must be cautious, as Lyonia mariana can be poisonous to grazing livestock.
During our hike, we identified several stands of Lyonia mariana along the sandy trail.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *lyon* (to loosen) referring to the bark; species epithet from Latin 'Mariana', meaning 'of Mary'.
