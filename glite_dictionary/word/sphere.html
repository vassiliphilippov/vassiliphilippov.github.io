sphere

1. NOUN

/ sfɪr / (AmE), / sfɪə / (BrE)
the imaginary surface around the earth on which celestial bodies appear to be projected
imaginary surface for celestial bodies

Examples

Medieval astronomers believed the stars were fixed on a crystal sphere surrounding the Earth.
The concept of the celestial sphere helps astronomers describe the positions of stars and planets.
To the naked eye, the planets seem to move across the sphere of the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial sphere - the imaginary sphere of the sky to which stars and planets are attached

Etymology

From Greek sphaira (globe, ball), via Latin sphaera.

3. NOUN

/ sfɪr / (AmE), / sfɪə / (BrE)
A round three-dimensional object whose surface is made up of all points that are equally distant from its center.
round three-dimensional object

Examples

The Earth is not a perfect sphere, but slightly flattened at the poles.
A soap bubble forms a nearly perfect sphere in the air.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a sphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ball - a round object used in games and sports; can also refer to any spherical object

Etymology

From Greek sphaira (ball, globe) via Latin sphaera.
