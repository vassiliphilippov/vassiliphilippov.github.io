Ephedra sinica

1. NOUN

/ əˈfiːdrə ˈsɪnɪkə / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːdrə ˈsɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of ephedra native to China, used in traditional medicine and as a source of ephedrine.
Chinese plant used for ephedrine

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine often uses Ephedra sinica to treat asthma and bronchitis.
The plant Ephedra sinica is valued for its natural content of ephedrine alkaloids.
Researchers are studying the safety of supplements containing Ephedra sinica extracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephedrine - a medication and stimulant derived from Ephedra plants

Etymology

From genus name Ephedra (from Greek *ephedra*, horsetail plant) and Latin *sinica*, meaning 'Chinese'.
