to miscast

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈkæst / (AmE), / mɪsˈkɑːst / (BrE)
to assign an actor, singer, or dancer a role that is inappropriate or unsuitable for them
assign a performer to an unsuitable role

Examples

Many critics agreed that he was miscast as the romantic lead in the film.
The director admitted she miscast the role of Hamlet and regretted her decision.
It's risky to miscast a talented dancer in a singing role that doesn't suit them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cast - to assign a role to an actor

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'cast' (assign a role).
