Mucoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkɒˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly saprophytic fungi that includes many common molds destructive to food products
family of molds (fungi) affecting food

Examples

Members of the Mucoraceae family can cause spoilage in bread and other food products.
Researchers studied several species within the Mucoraceae to understand their effects on stored crops.
The presence of Mucoraceae in food-processing environments requires careful monitoring and control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a furry growth of fungus on damp or decaying food
  • Synonym: fungus - any member of a large group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Mucor + -aceae (family suffix).
