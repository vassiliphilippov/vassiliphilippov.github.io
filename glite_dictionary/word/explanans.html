explanans

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈsplænænz / (AmE), / ɛkˈsplananz / (BrE)
(in logic and philosophy) The statement or set of statements that provides the explanation for a phenomenon, especially as the premises in an argument that accounts for the explicandum.
explanatory statement(s) in logic or philosophy

Examples

The explanans must provide sufficient information to account for the observed facts.
In scientific theories, the explanans are the principles or laws used to explain phenomena.
He distinguished carefully between the explicandum and the explanans in his argument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicandum - statement or proposition which is explained by the explanans

Etymology

From Latin explanans, present participle of explanare (to explain).
