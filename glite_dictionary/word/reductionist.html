reductionist

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of reductionism, especially the practice of analyzing and describing a complex phenomenon in terms of its simple or fundamental components.
relating to reductionism

Examples

His reductionist perspective on biology emphasizes genes over environmental factors.
Critics say the report takes a reductionist approach to social issues.
The documentary challenges reductionist explanations of human behavior.

Synset Examples

reductionist arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reductionism - the practice of analyzing complex phenomena by reducing them to their basic components
  • Antonym: holistic - relating to or concerned with wholes rather than analysis or separation into parts

Etymology

From 'reduction' plus '-ist', indicating an adherent or characteristic of reductionism.
