tiger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a large wild cat found in the forests of Asia, recognized by its orange-brown coat with black stripes; now considered endangered
large striped Asian wild cat

Examples

The tiger moved silently through the dense jungle, its stripes blending with the shadows.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the remaining wild tigers from extinction.
At the zoo, the children were fascinated by the sight of a sleeping tiger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lion - large tawny wild cat of Africa and India
  • Synonym: leopard - spotted large wild cat native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Old French 'tigre', from Latin 'tigris', from Ancient Greek 'tígris' (tiger), of Iranian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a person who is very energetic, fierce, or audacious
energetic or fierce person (figurative)

Examples

Don't underestimate her; she's a tiger when it comes to negotiations.
The coach told him to unleash the tiger within if he wanted to win the race.
Everyone knew Mark was a tiger in the courtroom, never backing down from a fight.

Synset Examples

he's a tiger on the tennis court
it aroused the tiger in me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go-getter - a very energetic and ambitious person
  • Antonym: pushover - someone who is easily influenced or defeated

Etymology

From Persian *tigra* (sharp, pointed), via Latin *tigris* and Greek *tigris* (the tiger animal); the figurative use derives from the fierce nature of the animal.
