absolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˈljuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of forgiving someone for their sins, especially in the Christian Church, as pronounced by a priest
formal forgiveness of sins

Examples

The priest granted absolution to the penitent after hearing his confession.
She sought absolution for her mistakes at the church on Sunday.
Receiving absolution brought him a deep sense of relief and peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven
  • Antonym: condemnation - the act of declaring someone to be reprehensible, wrong, or evil

Etymology

From Latin 'absolutio' (a setting free, acquittal), from 'absolvere' (to set free, acquit, complete).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the formal act of forgiving someone’s sins, given by a priest, especially as part of the sacrament of penance.
formal forgiveness of sins by a priest

Examples

After confessing his sins, he received absolution from the priest.
Many people seek absolution for their wrongdoings through prayer and reflection.
The bishop granted absolution to the congregation during the special service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving someone

Etymology

From Latin absolutio (acquittal, release), from absolvere (to set free, acquit).
