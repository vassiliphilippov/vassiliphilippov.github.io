to displume

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈplum / (AmE), / dɪsˈpluːm / (BrE)
to strip someone or something of honors, possessions, or distinguishing attributes, often in a figurative sense
strip of honors, possessions, or attributes

Examples

The scandal threatened to displume him of his reputation and dignity.
After the defeat, the general was displumed of his command and titles.
They sought to displume their rivals by exposing their secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deprive - to take something away from someone
  • Synonym: strip - to remove all coverings or possessions from

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (removal) + 'pluma' (feather, plume).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈplum / (AmE), / dɪsˈpluːm / (BrE)
to strip the feathers from a bird; to pluck.
remove feathers from a bird
archaic

Examples

The cook began to displume the pheasants for the evening meal.
He carefully displumed the birds before preparing them for roasting.
In the old tales, noblemen would displume their conquests as a sign of victory.

Synset Examples

pull a chicken
pluck the capon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to remove feathers from a bird

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart, away) + pluma (feather).
