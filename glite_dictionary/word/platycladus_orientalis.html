Platycladus orientalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈklædəs ɔːrɪˈentəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈklædəs ɔːrɪˈentəliːs / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub or small tree native to Asia, widely cultivated in the United States and Europe as an ornamental plant.
evergreen Asian shrub or small tree

Examples

The Platycladus orientalis in our garden grew much taller than we expected.
The landscape architect recommended Platycladus orientalis for its year-round greenery and low maintenance.
Many parks in Europe feature mature specimens of Platycladus orientalis along their walking paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - an evergreen coniferous tree or shrub in the cypress family, sometimes considered related

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) + 'klados' (branch); 'orientalis' Latin for 'eastern', referring to its Asian origin.
