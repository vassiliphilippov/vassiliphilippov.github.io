cleansing

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛnzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɛnzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something clean, especially by removing dirt or impurities
the act of making something clean

Examples

The cleansing of the historic building took several weeks to complete.
Daily cleansing is important for maintaining healthy skin.
After a long winter, a thorough cleansing of the garden was necessary.

Synset Examples

he gave his shoes a good cleaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the act of making something clean

Etymology

From Middle English 'clensinge', from 'clensen' (to cleanse) + '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00251880-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (BrE)
having the effect of cleaning or acting like an antiseptic, especially by removing dirt or harmful microorganisms.
having an antiseptic or cleaning effect

Examples

She used a cleansing lotion to remove makeup and bacteria from her face.
The doctor recommended a cleansing solution for the wound to prevent infection.
Many soaps have cleansing properties that help eliminate germs effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiseptic - preventing the growth of disease-causing microorganisms
  • Synonym: disinfectant - a chemical that destroys bacteria

Etymology

From the verb 'cleanse', derived from Old English 'clænsian' (to purify), related to 'clean'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02123584-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɛnzɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to the washing or purification of the body, especially in a ritual or religious context.
related to ritual or bodily washing

Examples

The temple required participation in cleansing rites before entering the sacred area.
Many cultures practice cleansing ceremonies as a symbol of spiritual renewal.
Handwashing as a cleansing ritual is common in various religions.

Synset Examples

ablutionary rituals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ablutionary - relating to washing or cleansing, especially for religious ritual
