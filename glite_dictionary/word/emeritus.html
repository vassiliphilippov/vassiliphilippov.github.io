emeritus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (BrE)
A retired professor or minister who retains their title as an honor.
retired professor or minister (still with title)

Examples

After forty years of teaching, Dr. McCarthy is now an emeritus at the university.
The church invited Reverend Harris, an emeritus, to speak at the anniversary.
Several emeriti attended the annual faculty dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retired - having left one's job and stopped working

Etymology

From Latin 'emeritus' (having earned by service).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (BrE)
(of a retired person, especially a professor or cleric) having retired but allowed to retain their title as an honor, often used after the title (e.g., 'professor emeritus').
retired but retaining honorary title

Examples

After decades of teaching, he is now a professor emeritus at the university.
The board invited the emeritus director to attend the annual meeting.
As emeritus dean, she continues to contribute to academic affairs.

Etymology

From Latin 'emeritus' (having served one's time), from 'emereri' (to serve out, earn by service).
