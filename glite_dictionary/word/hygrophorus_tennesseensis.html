Hygrophorus tennesseensis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːfəˌrʊs tɪˌnɛsiˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfəˌrʊs tɪˌnɛsiˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a brownish sticky cap with a white margin, white gills, and an odor of raw potatoes.
species of fungus with sticky brown cap

Examples

The field guide described Hygrophorus tennesseensis as having a sticky brown cap and a distinctive potato-like odor.
While hiking, she spotted several specimens of Hygrophorus tennesseensis growing near the riverbank.
Mycologists were excited to discover Hygrophorus tennesseensis in a region where it had not been previously recorded.

Etymology

From genus name Hygrophorus (Greek hygros, 'moist' + phoros, 'bearing') and Latinized form of Tennessee, U.S. state, with -ensis indicating origin.
