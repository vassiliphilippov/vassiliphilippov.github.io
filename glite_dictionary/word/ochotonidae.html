Ochotonidae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌkoʊtəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / əʊˌkɒtəˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small mammals within the order Lagomorpha, including the pikas and their extinct relatives.
family of small mammals (pikas)

Examples

The Ochotonidae family includes both living pikas and several extinct species.
Researchers study the Ochotonidae to understand the evolution of lagomorphs.
Climate change poses a threat to many members of the Ochotonidae in mountainous regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small mammal of the genus Ochotona, related to rabbits and hares

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Ochotona plus the suffix -idae indicating a zoological family.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˌkoʊtəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / əʊˌkəʊtəˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small mammals comprising the pikas and related extinct forms within the order Lagomorpha.
family of mammals including pikas

Examples

The Ochotonidae family includes small mammals commonly known as pikas.
Recent studies have revealed new extinct species within the Ochotonidae lineage.
Scientists are interested in how Ochotonidae adapted to cold mountainous habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small mountain-dwelling mammal of Eurasia and North America, member of Ochotonidae

Etymology

From the type genus Ochotona + Latin '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
