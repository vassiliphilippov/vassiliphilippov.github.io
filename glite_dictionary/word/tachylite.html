tachylite

1. NOUN

/ ˈtækɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtækɪlʌɪt / (BrE)
a dark, glassy volcanic rock formed by the rapid cooling of basaltic lava
dark volcanic glass (basalt)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a layer of tachylite beneath the volcano's surface.
The thin slice of tachylite revealed its glassy structure under the microscope.
Volcanologists collected tachylite samples to study rapid lava cooling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsidian - a naturally occurring volcanic glass formed from felsic lava
  • Degree Lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock

Etymology

From Greek 'takhys' (swift) + 'lithos' (stone), referring to rapid cooling.
