news

1. NOUN

/ nuːz / (AmE), / njuːz / (BrE)
A program on radio or television that reports and discusses current events, often including interviews and commentary.
current events program on TV or radio

Examples

We watch the 7 o'clock news every night to stay informed.
The news often features interviews with experts on important topics.
She reported the story live on the evening news.

Synset Examples

we watch the 7 o'clock news every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newscast - a broadcast of news reports
  • Synonym: broadcast - a program that is transmitted on radio or television

Etymology

From Middle English newes (new things), plural of new.

2. NOUN

/ nuːz / (AmE), / njuːz / (BrE)
information that is new or previously unknown to a person, especially when received informally.
informal information not previously known

Examples

Her marriage was news to me, as I hadn't heard anything about it before.
If you got a promotion, that's news I want to hear!
It was certainly news when Mark announced he was moving to Australia.

Synset Examples

it was news to me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: information - facts provided or learned about something

Etymology

From Middle English *newes*, plural of *new* (new thing or event).

3. NOUN

/ nuz / (AmE), / njuːz / (BrE)
the quality of being sufficiently interesting, important, or unusual to be reported by the media
quality of being newsworthy

Examples

The recent scandal has enough news to make headlines nationwide.
Once the case went to trial, it instantly became news all over the country.
His retirement was hardly news to anyone familiar with the team.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newsworthiness - the quality of being worthy to be reported as news

recent and important information or reports

Examples

5. NOUN

/ nuz / (AmE), / njuːz / (BrE)
information about recent events or happenings, especially as reported in newspapers, magazines, or broadcasts.
information about recent events

Examples

I read the news in the local newspaper every morning.
The news of their engagement spread quickly through town.
Breaking news interrupted the television program with an urgent announcement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: report - an account describing events or situations

Etymology

From late Middle English, plural of 'new', influenced by Old French 'nouvelles' (news).
