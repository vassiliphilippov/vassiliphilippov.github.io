HS2

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ɛs ˈtu / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ɛs ˈtjuː / (BrE)
Herpes simplex virus type 2, a virus that commonly causes genital herpes in humans.
herpes simplex virus type 2 (causes genital herpes)
medical

Examples

Doctors often test for HS2 in patients with recurring genital sores.
Proper use of protection reduces the risk of contracting HS2.
Many people infected with HS2 are unaware due to mild or absent symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: HSV-2 - herpes simplex virus type 2, causes genital herpes
  • Degree Higher: herpes simplex virus - either of two viruses known as HSV-1 and HSV-2
  • Synonym: genital herpes - sexually transmitted disease caused by HSV-2

Etymology

Abbreviation of Herpes Simplex Virus type 2.
