wisecrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪzˌkræk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪz.kræk / (BrE)
a clever or witty remark, often intended to be humorous or sarcastic
clever or witty remark

Examples

She couldn't resist making a wisecrack during the boring meeting.
His constant wisecracks made him popular among his friends.
Instead of taking offense, the teacher laughed at the student's wisecrack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quip - a witty or clever remark
  • Synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration (opposite)

Etymology

Formed from 'wise' (slang for clever) + 'crack' (remark or joke), early 20th century American English.

2. VERB

/ ˈwaɪsˌkræk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪz.kræk / (BrE)
to make a witty or ironic remark, often in a joking or sarcastic manner
make a witty or sarcastic remark
informal american

Examples

He couldn't help but wisecrack during the serious meeting, making everyone chuckle.
She always wisecracks when things get tense, trying to lighten the mood.
Stop wisecracking and focus on the task at hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - say something to cause amusement or laughter
  • Synonym: quip - make a witty remark

Etymology

From 'wisecrack' (noun), originally American slang (late 19th century): 'wise' (meaning 'clever') + 'crack' (remark).
