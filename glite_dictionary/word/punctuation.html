punctuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌŋktʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌŋktjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the marks used in writing, such as periods, commas, and question marks, that help to separate sentences and clarify meaning
marks used in writing for clarity and separation

Examples

Always check your punctuation when editing your essays for clarity and correctness.
The teacher marked errors in spelling and punctuation on the students' assignments.
Learning how to use proper punctuation can greatly improve your writing skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comma - a punctuation mark (,) used to indicate a pause or separation in sentences
  • Synonym: period - a punctuation mark (.) used to indicate the end of a sentence

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'punctuatio' (the marking of a text with points or stops).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpʌŋk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌŋk.tjʊˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the use of standard marks such as periods, commas, and question marks in writing to separate sentences, clauses, and phrases, and to clarify meaning
system of marks in writing

Examples

Good punctuation makes your writing clearer and easier to understand.
Learning proper punctuation is essential for effective communication.
The essay lost marks due to incorrect punctuation.

Etymology

From Latin punctuatio (a marking with points), from punctus (point).
