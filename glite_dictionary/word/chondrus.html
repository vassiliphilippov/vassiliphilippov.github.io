Chondrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːndrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒndrəs / (BrE)
A genus of red algae, including species such as Chondrus crispus (Irish moss), found in the North Atlantic.
genus of red algae

Examples

The species Chondrus crispus is commonly known as Irish moss.
Researchers study Chondrus for its valuable carrageenan content.
Chondrus is often harvested along the coasts of the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alga - simple, typically aquatic, plant-like organism

Etymology

From Greek 'khondros' meaning 'cartilage,' referring to the gelatinous texture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.drəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.drəs / (BrE)
A genus of red algae in the family Gigartinaceae, commonly known as Irish moss.
genus of red algae (Irish moss)

Examples

Chondrus is widely harvested for use as a food additive in the preparation of carrageenan.
Scientists are studying the ecological role of Chondrus in coastal marine habitats.
The gelatinous texture of Chondrus makes it valuable in the food and pharmaceutical industries.

Etymology

From Greek khóndros (cartilage or grain), referring to the texture.
