axerophthol

1. NOUN

/ ˌæksəˈrɑfθɑl / (AmE), / ˌæksərəˈfɒl / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin essential for normal vision and healthy skin, commonly known as vitamin A
fat-soluble vitamin, vitamin A
medical

Examples

A deficiency in axerophthol can lead to night blindness and dry eyes.
The chemical structure of axerophthol classifies it as a fat-soluble vitamin.
Doctors recommend sources rich in axerophthol for maintaining healthy eyesight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin A - a fat-soluble vitamin essential for vision, immunity, and skin health

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'xēros' (dry) + 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to its role in preventing dry eye conditions.
