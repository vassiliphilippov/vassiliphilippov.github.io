pillion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪliən / (AmE), / ˈpɪliən / (BrE)
a seat or place for a passenger situated directly behind the rider of a motorcycle, bicycle, or horse
seat behind motorcycle or horse rider

Examples

She sat on the pillion as they rode through the city on his motorcycle.
He offered me a ride, so I climbed onto the pillion behind him.
The motorbike was built with a comfortable pillion for passengers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle - a seat fastened on the back of a horse or bicycle

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic *pillean* (little rug), originally referring to a cushion for a passenger on horseback.
