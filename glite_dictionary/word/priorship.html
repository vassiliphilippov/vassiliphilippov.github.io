priorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpraɪəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of being a prior, especially in a religious community
office of a prior

Examples

After his election to the priorship, he introduced many reforms to the monastery.
The history of his priorship is recorded in several ancient manuscripts.
Her research focuses on the role of women during the medieval priorship at Wroxall Abbey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prior - the head of a priory or a religious community, subordinate to an abbot

Etymology

From prior + -ship, following patterns of office nouns such as stewardship or leadership.
