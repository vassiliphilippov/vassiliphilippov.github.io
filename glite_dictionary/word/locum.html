locum

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəm / (BrE)
A person, especially a doctor or clergyman, who temporarily substitutes for another member of the same profession.
substitute doctor or clergyman

Examples

Dr. Green is working as a locum while the regular physician is on maternity leave.
The church hired a locum to cover services during the vicar's absence.
Many young doctors gain experience by taking locum positions in rural hospitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another

Etymology

From Latin 'locum tenens', meaning 'holding the place'.
