Tectona

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtoʊnə / (AmE), / tɛkˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical trees native to southeastern Asia, including the teak tree, known for its durable hardwood.
genus of tropical trees (teak family)

Examples

The wood of Tectona is highly valued for its durability and resistance to water.
Many commercial teak plantations cultivate trees belonging to the genus Tectona.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Tectona in varying soil types to improve timber yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teak - a tropical hardwood tree of the genus Tectona, valued for its wood

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tektōn' (carpenter).
