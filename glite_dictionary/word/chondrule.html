chondrule

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌdrul / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.druːl / (BrE)
a small, rounded granule of mineral found in some stony meteorites, typically composed of silicate and formed by rapid cooling from molten or partially molten droplets in space
small mineral granule in meteorites
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered several chondrules within the meteorite sample.
The texture of the stone meteorite is defined by the presence of rounded chondrules.
The formation of a chondrule involves rapid cooling in the early solar system.

Etymology

From Greek 'chondros' (grain, cartilage) + '-ule' (diminutive suffix).
