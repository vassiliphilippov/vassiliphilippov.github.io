magnesia

1. NOUN

/ mægˈniːʒə / (AmE), / mæɡˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
a white solid mineral, magnesium oxide (MgO), occurring naturally as periclase and used as a source of magnesium and in various industrial and medical applications
white mineral; magnesium oxide

Examples

The laboratory ordered a new supply of magnesia for their chemical experiments.
As an antacid, magnesia helps to relieve symptoms of indigestion.
Periclase is the natural mineral form of magnesia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magnesium - a chemical element, symbol Mg, essential for life and industry

Etymology

From Latin 'magnesia', from Greek 'Magnesia', a district in Thessaly where the mineral was first found.
