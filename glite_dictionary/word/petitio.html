petitio

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪtioʊ / (AmE), / pɛˈtɪtiəʊ / (BrE)
The logical fallacy of assuming the truth of what one is supposed to prove; begging the question.
logical fallacy of assuming the conclusion

Examples

The philosopher accused his opponent of committing petitio in their argument.
A circular argument is a classic example of petitio.
Understanding petitio is essential for critical thinking and sound reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging the question - the logical fallacy of assuming the conclusion in the premises

Etymology

From Latin 'petitio' (petition, assumption), especially in the phrase 'petitio principii'.
