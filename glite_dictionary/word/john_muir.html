John Muir

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn mjʊr / (AmE), / dʒɒn mjʊə / (BrE)
(1838–1914) United States naturalist and writer, born in England, known for advocating the preservation of wilderness and the establishment of national parks.
U.S. naturalist; national park advocate

Examples

Many parks in the United States honor John Muir for his contributions to conservation.
John Muir played a crucial role in the creation of Yosemite National Park.
The writings of John Muir continue to inspire environmentalists today.
