bloodline

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the ancestors or line of descent from which a person comes, regarded in terms of heredity or genetics.
ancestral line; hereditary lineage

Examples

The royal family maintained a pure bloodline for generations.
Many dog breeders are interested in tracing the bloodline of their animals.
She takes pride in her noble bloodline and family traditions.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.laɪn / (BrE)
the direct line of descent or ancestry, especially in purebred animals; lineage
direct ancestry or lineage, esp. of purebred animals

Examples

The breeder carefully traced the bloodline of each dog to ensure purity.
Many racehorses can trace their bloodline back to famous stallions.
A champion's bloodline is often considered valuable in the world of show dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct line of descent from an ancestor
  • Synonym: pedigree - the recorded ancestry, especially of purebred animals

Etymology

From 'blood' (ancestry) + 'line' (lineage); metaphorical use referring to inherited qualities.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the direct line of descent from an ancestor; a sequence of hereditary lineage within a family, breed, or species
family line of descent

Examples

Their family bloodline can be traced back to ancient royalty.
Many racehorses are valued for their pure bloodline.
The tradition has been passed down through generations of the same bloodline.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor, ancestry
  • Synonym: ancestry - one’s family or ethnic descent
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From 'blood' referring to ancestry, and 'line', meaning sequence of descent.
