blastospheric

1. ADJ

/ blæstəˈsfɪrɪk / (AmE), / blɑːstəˈsfɪərɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a blastula, an early stage of embryonic development
relating to a blastula in embryology
medical

Examples

During early embryonic development, the blastospheric cavity forms, enabling cell migration.
Researchers observed changes in the blastospheric structure under different laboratory conditions.
The blastospheric wall is a key feature used to identify the blastula stage in embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastular - relating to the blastula stage

Etymology

From 'blastula', itself from Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout), and the suffix '-ic'.
