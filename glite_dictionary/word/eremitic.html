eremitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or relating to the solitary, ascetic lifestyle of a hermit
relating to hermit-like solitude

Examples

The monk led an eremitic existence in the mountains, far from all human contact.
Her fascination with eremitic traditions inspired her to spend a summer in a remote monastery.
The eremitic lifestyle has appealed to spiritual seekers throughout history.

Synset Examples

the eremitic element in the life of a religious colony
his hermitic existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermitic - characterized by living as a hermit or recluse

Etymology

From Greek erēmitikos, from erēmitēs (hermit, recluse).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of eremites (hermits) or their way of life; hermit-like.
relating to the lifestyle of hermits

Examples

She chose an eremitic life in the mountains, far from society.
The monk was admired for his eremitic devotion and simplicity.
The novel explores the eremitic existence of a solitary wanderer.

Synset Examples

eremitic austerities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermitic - relating to or characteristic of hermits or their way of life
  • Antonym: communal - relating to or shared by a community, contrasting with solitary life

Etymology

From Greek 'erēmitēs' (hermit), from 'erēmos' (desert, solitary).

WordNet ID: oewn-02697687-a
Synset POS: a
