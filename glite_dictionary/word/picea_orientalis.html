Picea orientalis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiː.ə ɔːr.iˈæn.tə.lɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈsiː.ə ɒr.ɪˈæn.təl.ɪs / (BrE)
an evergreen coniferous tree native to the Caucasus and Asia Minor, with pendulous branchlets, often grown as an ornamental
a spruce tree native to the Caucasus region

Examples

The Picea orientalis is prized in gardens for its graceful, drooping branches.
We planted a Picea orientalis along the northern edge of the park for its year-round greenery.
Many landscapers admire Picea orientalis for its dense foliage and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen tree with needlelike leaves

Etymology

From Latin; 'Picea' meaning spruce, and 'orientalis' meaning eastern.
