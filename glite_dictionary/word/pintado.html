pintado

1. NOUN

/ pɪnˈtɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / pɪnˈtɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a large edible mackerel found in the temperate Atlantic coastal waters of the United States
large edible Atlantic mackerel

Examples

The chef prepared the freshly caught pintado with lemon and herbs for dinner.
Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often prize the pintado for its firm, flavorful flesh.
The pintado is known for its distinctive spotted pattern and size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king mackerel - a large mackerel species found in the western Atlantic Ocean

Etymology

From Spanish 'pintado' meaning 'painted', referring to the fish's spotted appearance.
