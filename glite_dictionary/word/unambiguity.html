unambiguity

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear and not open to more than one interpretation; lack of ambiguity
clarity; absence of ambiguity

Examples

Legal documents are written with unambiguity to avoid misinterpretation.
Her instructions were marked by unambiguity, making the process easy to follow.
The unambiguity of his answer left no room for doubt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguity - the quality of being open to more than one interpretation
  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to understand

Etymology

From un- (not) + ambiguity, based on Latin ambiguus (uncertain).
