omelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmlət / (AmE), / ˈɒmlət / (BrE)
A dish made by beating eggs and cooking them until set, often folded around a filling such as cheese, ham, or vegetables.
beaten eggs cooked and folded, often with filling

Examples

She made a delicious cheese omelet for breakfast this morning.
The menu offers a vegetable omelet served with toast and coffee.
He prefers his omelet with mushrooms and spinach.

Etymology

From French 'omelette', alteration of earlier 'amelette', from 'lamelle', meaning 'thin strip'.
