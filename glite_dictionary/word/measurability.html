measurability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛʒərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌmiːʒərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be measured.
quality of being measurable

Examples

The measurability of the results made the scientific findings more credible.
Before investing, we should consider the measurability of projected returns.
Some aspects of happiness lack clear measurability in psychological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measurable - able to be measured
  • Antonym: immeasurability - the quality of being impossible to measure

Etymology

From 'measurable' + '-ity', based on Latin roots 'mensurabilis' (measurable) and '-itas' (forming nouns).
