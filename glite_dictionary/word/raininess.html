raininess

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪni.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being rainy; the extent to which there is frequent or heavy rain
the condition of being rainy

Examples

The constant raininess of the season made outdoor activities nearly impossible.
We were unprepared for the raininess that lasted all week during our holiday.
Due to the raininess of the climate, the area is lush and green throughout the year.

Synset Examples

they were wearied with the foulness of the weather

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being free from rain
  • Synonym: wetness - the quality of being wet

Etymology

From 'rainy' + '-ness', the suffix forming nouns denoting a state or condition.
