Pennatula

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈnætʃələ / (AmE), / pɛˈnatjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of marine colonial animals known as sea pens, belonging to the family Pennatulidae
genus of marine sea pens

Examples

The marine biologists collected specimens of Pennatula during their deep-sea expedition.
Certain species within the genus Pennatula glow faintly when disturbed by predators.
The distinctive structure of Pennatula makes it easily recognizable among sea pens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea pen - a colonial marine animal resembling a feather, related to Pennatula

Etymology

From Latin 'penna' (feather), referencing the feather-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ pɛˈnætʃʊlə / (AmE), / pɛˈnatjʊlə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Pennatulidae, comprising certain sea pens (colonial marine cnidarians)
type genus of sea pens (Pennatulidae)

Examples

The coral structure of Pennatula is often likened to a quill pen.
Marine biologists discovered several Pennatula colonies in the deep waters off the coast.
In their research, they identified a new species belonging to the genus Pennatula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea pen - a colonial marine cnidarian resembling a quill pen, member of Pennatulidae

Etymology

From Latin 'penna' meaning 'feather', referring to the feather-like appearance of the organism.
