Tuscan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌskən / (AmE), / ˈtʌskən / (BrE)
the dialect of Italian spoken in Tuscany, especially in and around Florence
dialect of Italian spoken in Tuscany

Examples

Many consider Tuscan to be the basis for standard Italian.
She studied the influence of Tuscan on Italian literature.
The poet wrote in Tuscan rather than using a regional dialect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Florentine - the variety of Italian spoken in Florence

Etymology

From Latin 'Tuscānus' (of Etruria or Tuscany).

/ ˈtʌskən / (AmE), / ˈtʌskən / (BrE)
relating to Tuscany, its people, language, or culture

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʌskən / (AmE), / ˈtʌskən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or resident of Tuscany, a region in central Italy
resident or native of Tuscany, Italy

Examples

As a proud Tuscan, he often shares stories of Florence and the surrounding countryside.
The Tuscans are known for their rich culinary traditions and beautiful Renaissance art.
She became friends with a local Tuscan who taught her how to cook traditional dishes.

Etymology

From Latin 'Tuscānus', relating to Etruria (Tuscany).
