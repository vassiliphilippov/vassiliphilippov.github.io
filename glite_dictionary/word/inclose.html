to inclose

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkloʊz / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊz / (BrE)
to surround or close off on all sides; to shut in completely
to surround on all sides
archaic

Examples

The old stone walls inclose the ancient garden, protecting it from the wind.
A high wooden fence was built to inclose the area for privacy.
At night, a sense of fear seemed to inclose the deserted village.

Synset Examples

Darkness enclosed him
They closed in the porch with a fence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround or close off on all sides

Etymology

From Middle English enclosen, inclosen; variant influenced by Latin 'in-' and 'claudere' (to close).
