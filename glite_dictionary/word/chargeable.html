chargeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑrdʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
liable to be accused of an offense or held legally responsible; subject to being charged in court
liable to be accused; legally responsible
legal

Examples

The suspect was found chargeable with several counts of fraud.
Only certain actions are chargeable under the new criminal code.
She denied being chargeable for her colleague's misconduct.

Synset Examples

the suspect was chargeable
an indictable offense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indictable - (of an offense) able to be charged formally in a court
  • Antonym: blameless - not deserving blame or accusation

Etymology

From Old French *chargier* (to load, accuse), from Late Latin *carricare* (to load).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑrdʒə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.dʒə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being charged, especially in terms of payment, liability, or electrical energy.
able to be charged (payable, liable, or able to store electrical charge)

Examples

The repair costs are chargeable to the customer if the warranty has expired.
In many hotels, calls from the room phone are chargeable services.
This battery is chargeable and can be reused many times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payable - required to be paid or able to be paid
  • Synonym: liable - legally responsible or subject to a charge
  • Antonym: nonchargeable - not capable of being charged

Etymology

From 'charge' + '-able', modeled on similar adjectival formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90000780-s
Synset POS: s
