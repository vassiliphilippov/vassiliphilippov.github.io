judo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəʊ / (BrE)
A Japanese martial art and sport based on using an opponent's strength against them, involving throws and grappling.
Japanese martial art and sport

Examples

He has practiced judo since he was a child, earning his black belt at age sixteen.
Many Olympic athletes compete in judo tournaments on the world stage.
The local sports center offers beginner judo classes for children and adults alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jujitsu - a Japanese martial art focusing on hand-to-hand combat and self-defense
  • Synonym: wrestling - a sport or activity involving grappling and throwing opponents

Etymology

From Japanese, literally 'the gentle way', from 'jū' (gentle) + 'dō' (way, path).
