Kordofanian

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrdəˈfeɪniən / (AmE), / ˌkɔːdəˈfeɪniən / (BrE)
a group of languages spoken in the Kordofan region of southern Sudan, classified within the Niger–Congo language family
language group of the Kordofan region, Sudan

Examples

The Kordofanian languages are spoken by a number of ethnic groups in central Sudan.
Researchers have studied the grammar and vocabulary of Kordofanian in efforts to preserve endangered languages.
Linguists classify Kordofanian as a subgroup of the Niger–Congo language family.

Etymology

From Kordofan, a region in Sudan, plus '-ian' meaning 'relating to'.
