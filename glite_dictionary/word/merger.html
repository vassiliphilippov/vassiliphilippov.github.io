merger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdʒə / (BrE)
the joining together of two or more companies, organizations, or businesses to form a single entity
joining of companies into one

Examples

The merger of the two banks created one of the largest financial institutions in the country.
Shareholders voted to approve the proposed merger between the companies.
After the merger, many departments were consolidated to reduce costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquisition - the taking over of one company by another

Etymology

From Latin 'mergere' (to dip, plunge); adopted in business use from the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdʒə / (BrE)
the joining of two or more organizations, businesses, or entities into one; the act or process of merging
the joining of two or more entities into one

Examples

The companies completed their merger after months of negotiation.
The merger resulted in a larger, more competitive business.
Many employees were anxious about the changes brought by the merger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amalgamation - the combination of two or more things into one
  • Synonym: acquisition - the act of acquiring control of another company
  • Antonym: division - the act of separating into parts or groups

Etymology

From merge + -er, modelled after earlier legal/business terminology.

Synset POS: n
