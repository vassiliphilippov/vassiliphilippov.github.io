pom-pom

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑm pɑm / (AmE), / ˈpɒm pɒm / (BrE)
a type of automatic anti-aircraft gun, especially one used during the early 20th century, designed to fire rapidly at aircraft
automatic anti-aircraft gun

Examples

The gun crew quickly fired the pom-pom at the approaching enemy planes.
During the war, ships were often equipped with a pom-pom to defend against air raids.
The distinctive sound of the pom-pom could be heard across the harbor during the attack.

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by the gun when firing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑm pɑm / (AmE), / ˈpɒm pɒm / (BrE)
a small ball made of tufted wool, silk, or other fabric, used as a decorative ornament, especially on hats or clothing
decorative ball of tufted material

Examples

The child's winter hat was topped with a bright red pom-pom.
Many cheerleaders wave large, colorful pom-poms during their routines.
She sewed a blue pom-pom onto her scarf for a playful touch.

Etymology

From French *pompon*, meaning a tufted ornament.
