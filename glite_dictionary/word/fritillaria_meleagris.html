Fritillaria meleagris

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪləriə ˌmɛliˈæɡrɪs / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlɪˈeəriə ˌmɛliˈæɡrɪs / (BrE)
a Eurasian flowering plant of the lily family, known for its checkered, bell-shaped purple or maroon flowers; often grown as an ornamental garden plant
Eurasian lily with checkered bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The Fritillaria meleagris is prized by gardeners for its distinctive checkered flowers.
In spring, the meadow blooms with Fritillaria meleagris, adding a touch of wild beauty.
Botanists admire Fritillaria meleagris for its unusual coloration and historical associations.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Fritillaria* and species name *meleagris* (meaning 'guinea fowl', referring to the flower's speckled pattern).
