gouache

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑʃ / (AmE), / ɡwɑːʃ / (BrE)
a type of opaque watercolor paint made with gum, used by artists for its vibrant, matte finish
opaque watercolor paint for artists

Examples

The artist chose gouache for its rich, solid colors and quick drying time.
She layered gouache over the sketch to create a bold, expressive effect.
Many illustrators prefer gouache because it can be easily reworked with water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: watercolor - transparent paint made with pigments suspended in water

Etymology

From French 'gouache', from Italian 'guazzo' (a puddle, splashing), referring to a painting technique using opaque watercolors.

2. NOUN

/ ɡwɑʃ / (AmE), / ɡwɑːʃ / (BrE)
A method of painting using opaque watercolors mixed with gum, or the paint used in this technique.
opaque watercolor paint or technique

Examples

Her illustration was created entirely with gouache for a rich, vibrant effect.
Many artists prefer gouache to achieve bold colors that stand out on the paper.
He mixed water and gouache carefully to get the right opacity for the sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: watercolor - A painting method using transparent water-based paints

Etymology

From French 'gouache', from Italian 'guazzo' (puddle; mud), referring to a technique of painting.
