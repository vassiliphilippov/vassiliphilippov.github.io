porousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːrəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being porous; the ability to let fluids or air pass through.
quality of being porous

Examples

The porousness of the sponge allows it to soak up water quickly.
Scientists measured the porousness of soil samples to predict water drainage patterns.
The porousness of the material made it ideal for use as a filter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porosity - the quality or state of being porous, especially as measured in materials science

Etymology

From 'porous' + '-ness', where 'porous' is from Latin 'porosus', from 'porus' (pore).
