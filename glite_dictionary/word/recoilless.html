recoilless

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɔɪləs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔɪlɪs / (BrE)
(of a weapon) designed to minimize or eliminate the backward force (recoil) when fired.
(of a weapon) minimizing recoil

Examples

The recoilless rifle was favored for its lightweight design and lack of heavy kickback.
Many anti-tank weapons use a recoilless system to make them easier to handle.
The engineers developed a recoilless launcher for use by infantry units.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recoiling - experiencing recoil; moving backward when fired

Etymology

Formed from 'recoil' + the suffix '-less', meaning 'without recoil'.
