mortuary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔr.tʃuˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tjʊə.ri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of death; funerary or associated with the dead.
relating to death or the dead

Examples

The house had a mortuary atmosphere after the tragic accident.
He spoke in a mortuary tone that unsettled the audience.
The mortuary silence in the church was almost overwhelming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funereal - suggestive of a funeral or mourning

Etymology

From Latin 'mortuarius' (pertaining to the dead), from 'mortuus' (dead).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːrtʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtjʊəri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of funerals or burial of the dead
of funerals or burial of the dead

Examples

The family gathered for the mortuary services held at the chapel.
A mortuary silence fell over the room as the casket was brought in.
He specialized in mortuary customs and rituals around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funereal - having the mournful, somber character appropriate to a funeral

Etymology

From Latin mortuarius (pertaining to the dead), from mortuus (dead).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrtʃueri / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtjʊəri / (BrE)
a building or room where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation
place for keeping dead bodies before burial

Examples

The body was taken to the mortuary for identification.
She worked as an assistant in the hospital mortuary.
Flowers lined the entrance to the mortuary on the day of the funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morgue - a place where bodies are kept, especially to be identified or claimed

Etymology

From Latin mortuarius (pertaining to the dead), from mortuus (dead).
