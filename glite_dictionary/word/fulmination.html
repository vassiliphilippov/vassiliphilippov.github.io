fulmination

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌlmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfʌlmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a forceful or thunderous verbal denunciation or attack
forceful verbal attack

Examples

The politician's fulmination against the media shocked even his supporters.
Her sudden fulmination during the meeting left everyone speechless.
Despite his fulminations, the proposal moved forward without changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
  • Synonym: diatribe - a bitter, forceful verbal attack
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin fulminatio (striking with lightning), from fulminare (to strike with lightning).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfʌlmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an outburst marked by noise or violence; a vehement expression of protest or condemnation
a noisy outburst or explosion

Examples

The general's fulmination against the enemy was heard across the battlefield.
Her angry fulminations during the meeting shocked even her closest friends.
The novel is filled with fulminations about the injustices of society.

Synset Examples

his fulminations frightened the horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outburst - a sudden release of strong emotion
  • Synonym: explosion - a violent burst producing noise and energy
  • Synonym: diatribe - a forceful and bitter verbal attack

Etymology

From Latin fulminatio (a striking with lightning), from fulminare (to strike with lightning).
