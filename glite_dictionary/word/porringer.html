porringer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːrɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈpɒrɪndʒə / (BrE)
a small, shallow bowl, typically with a handle, used for serving porridge or soup
shallow handled bowl for porridge

Examples

She ladled hot oatmeal into a porringer for her son every morning.
The museum displayed a silver porringer from the eighteenth century.
He ate the thick stew directly from the porringer, savoring each spoonful.

Synset Examples

the child was eating pottage from a porringer

Etymology

From Old French 'potager', from 'potage' (pottage), referring to a vessel for soup or porridge.
