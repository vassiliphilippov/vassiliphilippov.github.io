shoemaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌmeɪ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌmeɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or trade of making shoes by hand or by machine
the trade of making shoes

Examples

He learned the art of shoemaking from his father, who was a skilled craftsman.
The town was once famous for its shoemaking industry, which provided jobs to many residents.
Modern shoemaking often combines traditional techniques with advanced technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobbling - the trade of repairing shoes

Etymology

From 'shoe' + 'making', referring to the craft of making shoes.
