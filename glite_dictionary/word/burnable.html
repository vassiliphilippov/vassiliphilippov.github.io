burnable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɝːnəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to catch fire and burn; capable of being burned.
able to catch fire; flammable

Examples

Do not store burnable materials near open flames.
The council collected all burnable waste separately from recyclables.
Some fabrics are more burnable than others and require special handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flammable - easily set on fire; quick to burn
  • Antonym: nonflammable - not able to catch fire or burn easily

Etymology

Formed from 'burn' + '-able', following Latin-derived pattern for adjectives meaning 'able to be burned'.
