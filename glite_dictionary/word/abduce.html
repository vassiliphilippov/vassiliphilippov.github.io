to abduce

1. VERB

/ æbˈdus / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to advance evidence in support of a conclusion or inference
advance evidence for a conclusion

Examples

The lawyer attempted to abduce several pieces of evidence in support of her argument.
Philosophers often abduce the best explanation for observed phenomena.
He failed to abduce any convincing support for his theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning; to infer logically
  • Synonym: to induce - to lead to a conclusion by generalizing or inferring from specific cases

Etymology

From Latin abducere (to lead away), related to the process of reasoning by abduction.
