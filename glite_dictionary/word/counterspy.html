counterspy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌspaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌspaɪ / (BrE)
a spy employed to detect and thwart enemy spies
spy who counters enemy espionage

Examples

The agency hired a skilled counterspy to infiltrate the enemy's intelligence network.
During the war, every counterspy played a crucial role in national security.
It was difficult to know whom to trust, as even a counterspy could be deceived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects and reports information on the activities, movements, and plans of an enemy or competitor

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'spy'.
