tang

1. NOUN

/ tæŋ / (AmE), / tæŋ / (BrE)
a type of brown seaweed with serrated edges, found mostly along northern Atlantic coasts
brown seaweed with serrated edges

Examples

The rocky shore was covered in tang washed up by the tide.
Locals harvest tang in the early summer for use as fertilizer.
You could smell the salt air and the drying tang along the waterfront.

2. NOUN

/ tæŋ / (AmE), / tæŋ / (BrE)
a type of seaweed, especially common rockweed, used in preparing kelp and as manure
type of seaweed used for kelp/manure

Examples

The beach was covered in tang after the last storm.
Local farmers collect tang to use as fertilizer for their fields.
As a child, she helped gather tang for making kelp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - marine algae used for food or fertilizer

Etymology

From Old Norse *tangr* (seaweed, tongue, spit of land).

3. NOUN

/ tæŋ / (AmE), / tæŋ / (BrE)
any of various kelps, especially those of the genus Laminaria, found in coastal waters and sometimes used as food or fertilizer
a type of kelp or seaweed

Examples

The rocky shore was covered in slippery tang after the storm.
Locals harvest tang to use as a natural fertilizer for their gardens.
You could see piles of brown tang washed up along the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - a large brown seaweed often found in coastal waters

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *þang* (seaweed), reflecting its northern European use.
