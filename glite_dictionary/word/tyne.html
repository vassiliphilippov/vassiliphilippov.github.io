Tyne

1. PROPN

/ taɪn / (AmE), / taɪn / (BrE)
a river in northern England that flows eastward to the North Sea, noted for passing through Newcastle upon Tyne.
river in northern England

Examples

The Tyne is famous for its bridges and the city life along its banks.
Shipping once brought great prosperity to the Tyne region.
Salmon have returned to the Tyne thanks to successful conservation efforts.

Etymology

From Old English Tīn, probably of Celtic origin.
