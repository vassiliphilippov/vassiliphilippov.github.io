flirt

1. VERB

/ flɝːt / (AmE), / flɜːt / (BrE)
to behave or talk in a playful or teasing way that suggests a romantic or sexual attraction, without serious intent
behave amorously without serious intent

Examples

She likes to flirt with the bartender whenever she goes out with friends.
He tends to flirt a lot but rarely means anything by it.
Emily noticed Jake flirting with her at the party last night.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Antonym: court - to try to attract someone romantically, often with serious intentions
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke playfully

Etymology

Probably from earlier sense 'to flick or dart', of uncertain origin; meaning 'behave amorously' dates from the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ flɝːt / (AmE), / flɜːt / (BrE)
to behave carelessly or irresponsibly with something, especially with ideas, feelings, or danger, often without serious intention
act carelessly or lightly with something

Examples

He liked to flirt with danger by taking unnecessary risks.
She often flirted with the idea of quitting her job, but never did it.
The politician was accused of flirting with extremist views to gain attention.

Synset Examples

Play about with a young girl's affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toy - to act without seriousness or play with something lightly
  • Synonym: trifle - to treat something as unimportant or act with little seriousness

Etymology

From 16th-century English, originally meaning 'to move briskly'; later specialized to playful or careless behavior.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02442312-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ flɝt / (AmE), / flɜːt / (BrE)
a person who behaves playfully with others, often to attract sexual interest or affection
a person who behaves playfully to attract others

Examples

He was known as a flirt at every party he attended.
Some people thought she was just friendly, but others called her a flirt.
The office flirt always had a witty comment and a charming smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - a person who makes fun of or provokes someone, sometimes playfully

Etymology

Originally from the verb 'flirt,' which is likely of imitative origin, first recorded in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00513533-n
Synset POS: n
