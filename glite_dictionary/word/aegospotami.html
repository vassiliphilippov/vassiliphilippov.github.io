Aegospotami

1. PROPN

/ iːˌɡɑːspəˈteɪmaɪ / (AmE), / iːˌɡɒspəˈteɪmaɪ / (BrE)
a creek in ancient Thrace, emptying into the Hellespont in present-day Turkey, known as the site where the Spartan fleet defeated the Athenians in 405 BC, ending the Peloponnesian War
ancient creek and battle site in Thrace

Examples

The Battle of Aegospotami marked the end of the Peloponnesian War.
Historians still debate the exact location of Aegospotami on the shores of the Hellespont.
The defeat at Aegospotami left Athens without a fleet and at the mercy of Sparta.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰγὸς ποταμοί (Aigos Potamoi), meaning 'goat rivers.'

2. PROPN

/ iːˌɡoʊspəˈteɪmaɪ / (AmE), / iːˌɡɒspəˈtæmɪ / (BrE)
a river in ancient Thrace, now in Turkey, notable as the site where the Spartan fleet defeated the Athenians in the final battle of the Peloponnesian War in 404 BC
ancient Thracian river, Peloponnesian War battle site

Examples

The Battle of Aegospotami marked the end of Athenian naval supremacy.
Many historians consider Aegospotami a turning point in the Peloponnesian War.
A guided tour visited the site where Aegospotami once flowed.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰγὸς Ποταμοί (Aigos Potamoi), meaning 'Goat Rivers'.
