squama

1. NOUN

/ ˈskweɪmə / (AmE), / ˈskwɑːmə / (BrE)
a scale or scale-like protective structure, especially in animals or plants
scale-like protective structure

Examples

The fish's body was covered in overlapping squamae that protected it from predators.
Certain plants develop a squama to shield their reproductive organs.
In reptiles, each squama has a distinctive pattern that aids in identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - a small rigid plate forming part of the body covering of certain animals or plants

Etymology

From Latin 'squama' meaning 'scale'.
