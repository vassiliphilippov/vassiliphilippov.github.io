dolabrate

1. ADJ

/ doʊˈlæb.rət / (AmE), / dəʊˈlæb.rət / (BrE)
having the shape of the head of an ax or cleaver, especially used of certain leaves or crystals
shaped like an ax head

Examples

The fossil features a dolabrate leaf that is distinct from its relatives.
Under the microscope, the mineral exhibited a striking dolabrate form.
Some ferns have dolabrate fronds, making them easy to identify.

Etymology

From Latin 'dolabra' (a mattock, pickaxe).
