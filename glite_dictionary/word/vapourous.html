vapourous

1. ADJ

/ ˈveɪpərəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs / (BrE)
full of vapor; consisting of or resembling vapor; misty or foggy.
full of vapor; misty or foggy
british

Examples

The air in the greenhouse became vapourous after watering the plants.
She wandered through the vapourous morning mist along the lakeside path.
The artist captured the vapourous sunlight filtering through the clouds in her painting.

Synset Examples

miasmic jungles
a vaporous bog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - full of or covered with mist
  • Synonym: foggy - full of or resembling fog
  • Antonym: clear - free from mist, fog, or vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, vapor) + English '-ous' (having the qualities of).

/ ˈveɪpɚəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of vapor; having a misty, insubstantial, or ethereal quality.
resembling or characteristic of vapor
british

Examples

Early in the morning, vapourous clouds drifted slowly across the hills.
The artist captured the vapourous atmosphere of the misty forest in her painting.
Her thoughts seemed vapourous, slipping away before she could hold on to them.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaporous - resembling or characteristic of vapor (American spelling)
  • Synonym: misty - full of, or resembling, mist
  • Antonym: solid - having a definite shape and volume, not like vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, mist), with the suffix '-ous'. British spelling of 'vaporous'.

Examples

Mist drifted over the lake in vapourous wisps, almost hiding the surface from view.
They admired the vapourous curtains that allowed morning light to gently fill the room.
