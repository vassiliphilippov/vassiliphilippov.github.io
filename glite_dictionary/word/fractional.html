fractional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrækʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl / (BrE)
(of money) constituting a division or an aliquot part of the basic monetary unit; relating to coins or currency worth less than the standard unit.
of coins or currency: less than basic unit

Examples

The U.S. government once issued fractional currency to make change during coin shortages.
In many countries, fractional coins like nickels and dimes are used for small transactions.
Collectors often seek rare fractional notes issued during times of financial crisis.

Synset Examples

American divisional (fractional) coins include the dime and the nickel
fractional currency is currency in denominations less than the basic monetary unit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divisional - relating to coins that are fractions of a monetary unit

Etymology

From Latin 'frāctiōnālis', from 'frāctiō' (a breaking), from 'frangere' (to break).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrækʃənl / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl / (BrE)
Constituting or comprising only a part or fraction of a whole; relating to or expressed as a fraction.
constituting a part or fraction of a whole

Examples

He received only a fractional share of the inheritance compared to his siblings.
There was a fractional difference in the prices between the two stores.
The patient experienced only a fractional improvement in her symptoms after the medication.

Synset Examples

a fractional share of the vote
a partial dose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partial - existing only in part; incomplete
  • Antonym: whole - entire; complete, not divided into parts

Etymology

From fraction + -al, modeled after Latin *fractionalis* (pertaining to a fraction).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00518464-a
Synset POS: a
