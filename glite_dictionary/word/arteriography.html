arteriography

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪriˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the medical imaging of arteries using X-rays after injection of a contrast medium
imaging of arteries using X-rays
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an arteriography to check for blockages in the patient's arteries.
Advances in arteriography have improved the diagnosis of vascular diseases.
Preparation for arteriography includes fasting for several hours before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angiography - medical imaging of blood vessels using X-rays and contrast medium

Etymology

From Greek 'arteria' (artery) + 'graphia' (writing, recording).
