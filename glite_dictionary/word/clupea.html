Clupea

1. NOUN

/ kluˈpiːə / (AmE), / klʊˈpiːə / (BrE)
A genus of fish that includes the typical herrings, important as food fishes in many parts of the world.
genus of typical herrings

Examples

The Atlantic herring is classified under the genus Clupea.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Clupea in the North Sea.
Many commercial fisheries depend on species belonging to Clupea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - A small, silvery fish of the Clupea genus, widely caught for food

Etymology

From Latin 'clupea' (a kind of fish, herring).
