luminescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːməˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌluːmɪˈnɛs(ə)ns / (BrE)
the emission of light by a substance not resulting from heat; light produced by nonthermal means
light emission without heat

Examples

The deep-sea creatures exhibited luminescence to attract prey in the darkness.
Scientists are studying luminescence in certain minerals to develop new types of lighting.
A faint luminescence glowed from the algae in the nighttime waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorescence - persistent luminescence after the source is removed
  • Synonym: fluorescence - luminescence caused by absorption of radiation, typically ultraviolet light

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) and the suffix '-escence' denoting a process.

2. NOUN

/ luː.mɪˈnɛs.əns / (AmE), / luː.mɪˈnes.əns / (BrE)
the emission of light by a substance not resulting from heat, often occurring at low temperatures
light emission not caused by heat

Examples

The deep-sea creatures produced a faint luminescence to navigate in the darkness.
Scientists studied the luminescence of the mineral under ultraviolet light.
The watch face glowed with a soft green luminescence in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incandescence - light emission caused by high temperature
  • Degree Lower: phosphorescence - persistent luminescence after the energizing source is removed
  • Degree Higher: fluorescence - luminescence occurring when a substance emits light during exposure to radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) + '-escence' (process or state), patterned after 'incandescence'.
