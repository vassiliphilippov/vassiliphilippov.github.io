moderationism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdəˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or practice of advocating moderation and avoiding extremes, especially in political or ideological contexts
policy of advocating moderation

Examples

Many political leaders appeal to moderationism as a way to unite divided societies.
Her commitment to moderationism is evident in her balanced approach to controversial issues.
Critics argue that moderationism sometimes fails to address urgent problems decisively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extremism - the holding of extreme political or religious views; fanaticism
  • Synonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes, especially in behavior or political opinions

Etymology

From 'moderation' + '-ism', denoting a doctrine or system.
