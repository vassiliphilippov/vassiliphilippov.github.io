womb

1. NOUN

/ wum / (AmE), / wuÀêm / (BrE)
the organ in female mammals where offspring develop before birth; the uterus
organ where offspring develop; uterus

Examples

The baby grows inside the mother's womb until it is ready to be born.
Medical scans showed the fetus developing healthily in the womb.
After surgery, she was told that her womb was healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uterus - the medical and scientific term for womb

Etymology

Old English 'wamb' or 'womb' (belly, uterus); related to Old High German 'wamba' (belly).
