Procrustes

1. PROPN

/ proʊˈkrʌstiːz / (AmE), / prəʊˈkrʌstiːz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a mythical giant and bandit who forced his victims to fit an iron bed, stretching or amputating their limbs; he was eventually slain by Theseus.
mythical Greek giant who tortured victims to fit a bed

Examples

The story of Procrustes warns against forcing conformity through cruel means.
In the myth, Procrustes made his victims fit the bed by stretching them or cutting off their limbs.
The term 'Procrustean' is derived from Procrustes and his brutal methods.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Προκρούστης (Prokroústēs), meaning 'the stretcher'.
