neoplasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊˈpleɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌniː.əˈpleɪ.zɪə / (BrE)
the process by which abnormal and uncontrolled cell growth leads to the formation of a tumor
abnormal process leading to tumor formation
medical

Examples

The physician explained that neoplasia can be benign or malignant, depending on the characteristics of the tumor.
Researchers are studying genetic mutations that contribute to the development of neoplasia in various organs.
Early detection of neoplasia is vital for successful cancer treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue resulting from neoplasia

Etymology

From Greek neos (new) + plasis (formation, molding).
