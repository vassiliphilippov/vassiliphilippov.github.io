patricide

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
a person who murders their father
a person who kills their father

Examples

The court sentenced the patricide to life imprisonment for his shocking crime.
Throughout history, literature has explored the motives that drive a patricide.
He was haunted by the stigma of being labeled a patricide in his community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matricide - a person who murders their mother
  • Synonym: parricide - a person who kills a close relative or parent

Etymology

From Latin 'patricida', from 'pater' meaning 'father' and '-cida' meaning 'killer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪsaɪd / (BrE)
the act of killing one's father
killing of one's father

Examples

The ancient legend tells of a prince accused of patricide after his father's mysterious death.
In law, patricide is considered one of the most serious crimes.
His motives for committing patricide were debated at length during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matricide - the killing of one's mother
  • Synonym: parricide - the killing of a parent or close relative

Etymology

From Latin patricida, from pater (father) + -cida (killer).
