dégagé

1. ADJ

/ deɪˈɡɑːʒeɪ / (AmE), / deɪˈɡɑːʒeɪ / (BrE)
relaxed, unconcerned, and casual in manner or appearance; free and easy.
relaxed and unconcerned in manner

Examples

She appeared dégagé despite the stressful situation.
His dégagé attitude helped put everyone at ease.
During the interview, he remained remarkably dégagé and self-assured.

Synset Examples

rather degage after the nervousness he had shown at dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casual - relaxed and unconcerned
  • Synonym: nonchalant - calm and seeming not to care
  • Antonym: tense - nervous or anxious

Etymology

From French dégagé (free, unconstrained).

2. ADJ

/ deɪˈɡɑʒeɪ / (AmE), / deɪˈɡɑːʒeɪ / (BrE)
calm and unconcerned; showing lack of emotional involvement or restraint, often in a relaxed or carefree way.
calm, unconcerned, relaxed

Examples

She walked into the interview with a dégagé air, seemingly untroubled by the high stakes.
His dégagé manner at the party impressed everyone with its effortless charm.
Despite the tension in the room, Anna appeared entirely dégagé and unaffected.

Synset Examples

an uninvolved bystander

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effusive - expressing feelings of gratitude, pleasure, or approval in an unrestrained or heartfelt manner

Etymology
