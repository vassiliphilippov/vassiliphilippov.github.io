lambda

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.də / (AmE), / ˈlæm.də / (BrE)
the 11th letter of the Greek alphabet, written as Λ (uppercase) or λ (lowercase), used in mathematics, physics, and other fields.
11th Greek letter (Λ, λ)

Examples

The symbol lambda is commonly used to represent wavelength in physics.
In the Greek alphabet, lambda comes after kappa and before mu.
Many mathematicians use lambda to denote an eigenvalue in linear algebra.

Etymology

From Greek 'λάμβδα' (lambda), the letter's name in Greek.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.də / (AmE), / ˈlæm.də / (BrE)
the point on the skull where the sagittal and lambdoid sutures meet, used as a reference in craniometry
craniometric point on the skull
medical

Examples

The lambda is an important landmark for neurosurgeons during certain procedures.
The position of the lambda is measured to assess cranial development in infants.
The technician identified the lambda while examining the patient's skull X-ray.

Etymology

From Greek 'lambda', referring to the Greek letter λ, which the suture resembles at this point.
