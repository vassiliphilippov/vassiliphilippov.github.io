request

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkwɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
to politely or formally ask someone to do something or to provide something
to politely ask someone to do something

Examples

She requested a meeting with her supervisor to discuss her progress.
You are kindly requested to turn off your mobile phones during the performance.
The teacher requested that all assignments be submitted by Friday.

Synset Examples

I requested that she type the entire manuscript

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for something forcefully or insistently
  • Synonym: to ask - to make a request or inquiry

Etymology

From Old French 'requeste', from Latin 'requisita', past participle of 'requirere' (to seek, ask for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00755473-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
an act of asking for something to be given or done, especially politely or formally
an act of asking for something

Examples

At her request, the meeting was postponed until next week.
The waiter responded quickly to our request for more water.
He made a formal request for additional funding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: demand - an insistent and forceful request
  • Degree Higher: plea - an emotional or urgent request
  • Antonym: offer - an act of presenting something for acceptance or rejection

Etymology

From Old French 'requeste', from Latin 'requaerere' (to seek, ask).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07199985-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
a formal message or petition asking someone in authority for something
formal message or petition for something

Examples

The committee denied his request for more funding.
You must put the request in writing to the managing director.
She submitted an official request to access the documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request, typically one signed by many people
  • Degree Higher: demand - an insistent or authoritative request
  • Degree Lower: suggestion - an act of suggesting rather than formally requesting

Etymology

From Old French *requeste*, from Latin *requaerere* (to seek, ask for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06525357-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ rɪˈkwɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
to ask formally or politely for something, such as information, help, or an action
to ask for something politely or formally

Examples

She requested a copy of the report from the manager.
If you need more details, you can request them by email.
They requested permission to enter the restricted area.

Synset Examples

I requested information from the secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to say something in order to get an answer or information
  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for something forcefully or firmly

Etymology

From Old French 'requeste' (request, demand), from Latin 'requaerere' (to seek, require).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01071831-v
Synset POS: v
