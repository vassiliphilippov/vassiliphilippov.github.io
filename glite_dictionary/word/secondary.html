secondary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənˌdɛr.i / (AmE), / sɪˈkɒn.dəri / (BrE)
The group of defensive football players who line up behind the linemen, primarily responsible for defending against passing plays.
defensive football players behind linemen
american

Examples

The team's secondary intercepted the ball twice in the first half.
Injuries to the secondary left the defense vulnerable to deep passes.
The coach praised the secondary for their aggressive coverage on the receivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive back - player in the secondary; covers pass receivers or defends the backfield

6. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.əndˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ənd.ər.i / (BrE)
(in electrical engineering) a coil in which current is induced by a current passing through a primary coil, as in a transformer.
the induced coil in a transformer

Examples

The voltage is induced in the secondary when an alternating current flows through the primary coil.
Engineers measured the current generated in the secondary during the transformer test.
Each transformer consists of a primary and a secondary for efficient energy transfer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: primary - the coil that induces current in the secondary coil of a transformer

Etymology

From Latin secundarius, meaning 'of the second rank'; technical use evolved in relation to electrical circuits.
