hardheaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑrdˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
practical and realistic; guided by experience and facts rather than by theory or emotion
practical, unsentimental, realistic

Examples

Her hardheaded approach to negotiations helped the company reach a fair agreement.
You need to be hardheaded when making difficult financial decisions.
Despite criticism, she remained hardheaded and focused on tangible results.

Synset Examples

a hardheaded appraisal of our position
a hard-nosed labor leader
completely practical in his approach to business
not ideology but pragmatic politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things realistically and sensibly
  • Synonym: hard-nosed - practical and tough-minded
  • Antonym: idealistic - guided more by ideals or theory than practicality

Etymology

Formed from 'hard' + 'headed', meaning having a hard head (figuratively, being tough-minded or practical).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːrdˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
unwilling to change one's mind or be influenced by others, especially in an unreasonable or stubborn way
stubborn; unwilling to change mind

Examples

Despite everyone's advice, she remained hardheaded and refused to reconsider her decision.
His hardheaded approach made it difficult to negotiate any compromises.
Even when the evidence was clear, Mark was too hardheaded to admit he was wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
  • Synonym: obstinate - refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Etymology

From 'hard' + 'headed', indicating a figuratively tough or unyielding character.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02336289-s
Synset POS: s
