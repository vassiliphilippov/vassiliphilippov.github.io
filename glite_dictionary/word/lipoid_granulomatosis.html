lipoid granulomatosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪpɔɪd ˌɡræn.jʊ.loʊˈmə.toʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪpɔɪd ˌɡræn.jʊ.ləˈməʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A rare medical condition characterized by widespread xanthomas, especially on the elbows and knees, often linked to disorders of lipid metabolism.
rare condition with widespread xanthomas
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of lipoid granulomatosis was confirmed after the appearance of yellowish plaques on her elbows and knees.
Patients with lipoid granulomatosis often require management of underlying lipid metabolism disorders.
Due to its rarity, few clinicians have encountered a case of lipoid granulomatosis in their practice.

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) and 'granuloma' (small granular tumor), with '-osis' denoting a pathological condition.
