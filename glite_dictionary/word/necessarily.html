necessarily

1. ADV

/ ˌnɛsəˈsɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌnɛsɪˈsɛrɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is required or unavoidable; as a necessary result.
inevitably; as a necessary result

Examples

Not all rich people are necessarily happy or satisfied with their lives.
If we increase production, it does not necessarily mean that profits will rise.
Being the oldest does not necessarily make you the wisest.

Synset Examples

such expenses are necessarily incurred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - as is certain to happen; unavoidably
  • Antonym: accidentally - by chance, not planned or intended

Etymology

From Latin necessarius (necessary) + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˌnɛsəˈsɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌnɛsɪˈsɛrəli / (BrE)
in a way that could not be otherwise; unavoidably or inevitably
unavoidably or inevitably

Examples

A higher price does not necessarily mean better quality.
Not every minor delay will necessarily cause major problems.
Being honest does not necessarily lead to success.

Synset Examples

it is necessarily so
we must needs be objective

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - in a way that cannot be avoided or prevented
  • Antonym: optionally - in a way that is not required; by choice

Etymology

From Latin necessarius (necessary) via Old French necessaire.

3. ADV

/ ˌnɛsəˈsɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌnɛsɪˈsɛrəli / (BrE)
as a result that cannot be avoided; inevitably or unavoidably.
inevitably; unavoidably

Examples

The changes will necessarily affect all employees in the company.
Success does not necessarily come from working the longest hours.
Just because it is expensive does not necessarily mean it is better.

Synset Examples

we are necessarily bound for federalism in Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - in a way that cannot be avoided or prevented
  • Antonym: possibly - in a way that might happen or be true, but is not certain

Etymology

From Latin necessariō (by necessity), from necesse (necessary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410285-r
Synset POS: r
