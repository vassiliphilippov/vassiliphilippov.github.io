Pyrrosia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈroʊsiə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns found in the tropical Old World, which may grow as epiphytes, lithophytes, or terrestrially.
genus of tropical Old World ferns

Examples

The Pyrrosia species thrive on the trunks of large trees in rainforests.
Botanists discovered a new Pyrrosia growing on limestone cliffs in Southeast Asia.
Many gardeners appreciate Pyrrosia for its attractive, leathery fronds.

Etymology

From Greek pyrros (flame-colored), referring to the color of the spores in some species.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈroʊsiə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒsiə / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic, lithophytic, or terrestrial ferns native to the tropical Old World, belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.
a genus of ferns in the tropics

Examples

Several species of Pyrrosia are commonly cultivated as ornamental ferns.
The sori on the underside of Pyrrosia leaves are usually reddish or brown.
Pyrrosia can grow on rocks, tree trunks, or soil in shaded tropical areas.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyrrhos' meaning 'flame-colored', likely referring to the coloration of the sori or fronds in some species.
