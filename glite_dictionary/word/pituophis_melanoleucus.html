Pituophis melanoleucus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtuˈɑfɪs ˌmɛlənoʊˈlukəs / (AmE), / ˌpɪtjʊˈɒfɪs ˌmɛlənəˈljuːkəs / (BrE)
A large non-venomous snake, commonly known as the bull snake, native to western North America and known for inhabiting rodent burrows.
a large North American bull snake

Examples

The Pituophis melanoleucus is often found in grasslands and prairies across the western United States.
Farmers appreciate the Pituophis melanoleucus for its appetite for rodents in their fields.
A Pituophis melanoleucus can make a loud hissing sound when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull snake - a common name for Pituophis melanoleucus, a large North American snake

Etymology

From Greek 'pitys' (pine) + 'ophis' (snake) and 'melano-' (black) + 'leukos' (white), referring to the coloration and habitat.
