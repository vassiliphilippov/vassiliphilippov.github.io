Huckleberry Finn

1. PROPN

/ ˌhʌkəlˌbɛri ˈfɪn / (AmE), / ˌhʌkəlˌbɛri ˈfɪn / (BrE)
a mischievous boy who is the main character in the novel of the same name by Mark Twain, known for his adventures along the Mississippi River
fictional boy in Mark Twain's novel

Examples

Many readers admire Huckleberry Finn for his adventurous spirit and cleverness.
The novel featuring Huckleberry Finn explores themes of freedom and friendship.
Mark Twain created Huckleberry Finn as a symbol of youthful independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tom Sawyer - Huckleberry Finn's friend, protagonist of another Mark Twain novel

Etymology

The character first appeared in Mark Twain’s novel 'The Adventures of Tom Sawyer' (1876) and is the protagonist of 'Adventures of Huckleberry Finn' (1884).
