Pecten magellanicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛk.tən məˌɡɛ.ləˈnɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.tɛn məˌɡɛ.ləˈnɪ.kəs / (BrE)
a large species of scallop inhabiting deep waters along the Atlantic coast of North America, valued for its edible muscle
large Atlantic scallop species

Examples

Fishermen harvest Pecten magellanicus from the cold, deep waters off the New England coast.
The edible muscle of Pecten magellanicus is highly prized by chefs and seafood lovers alike.
Demand for Pecten magellanicus has led to sustainable fishing regulations in parts of the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - an edible marine bivalve mollusk with a ribbed fan-shaped shell

Etymology

From Latin: 'pecten' meaning 'comb' and 'magellanicus' referring to the Magellan region.
