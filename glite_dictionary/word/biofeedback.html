biofeedback

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪoʊˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
A technique for training people to control physiological processes such as heart rate or blood pressure by providing information about those processes.
technique using feedback to control bodily processes
medical

Examples

Through biofeedback, patients can learn to manage stress by monitoring their heart rates.
Many therapists recommend biofeedback to help with chronic pain management.
The doctor explained how biofeedback could be used to control blood pressure.

Etymology

From Greek bio- (life) and English feedback.
