unworthy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːði / (BrE)
not deserving respect, help, or attention; lacking value or merit.
not deserving; lacking merit

Examples

She felt unworthy of the praise she received after the project succeeded.
His actions were considered unworthy of someone in his position.
They believed he was unworthy of their trust after breaking his promise.

Synset Examples

dispel a student whose conduct is deemed unworthy
unworthy of forgiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worthy - deserving respect or attention

Etymology

From un- (not) + worthy, originally from Old English 'weorþ' (worthy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598414-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːði / (BrE)
Not deserving of a particular thing, quality, or treatment.
not deserving

Examples

She felt unworthy of the praise she received for her hard work.
He considered himself unworthy of such generosity from his friends.
The kingdom saw him as unworthy to inherit the throne.

Synset Examples

the undeserving poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undeserving - not deserving; lacking merit or worth
  • Antonym: worthy - deserving respect or attention

Etymology

From Old English un- (not) + worth (worthy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598736-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɝði / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːði / (BrE)
morally reprehensible; deserving of condemnation or contempt.
morally reprehensible; despicable

Examples

She considered his betrayal an unworthy act and severed all ties with him.
The unworthy behavior of the officials shocked the entire community.
He apologized for his unworthy attempt to deceive his friends.

Synset Examples

would do something as despicable as murder
ugly crimes
the vile development of slavery appalled them
a slimy little liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving to be despised; morally reprehensible
  • Synonym: vile - morally bad; wicked
  • Antonym: worthy - deserving respect or approval

Etymology

From un- (not) + worthy (Old English worthig, meaning deserving).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01136517-s
Synset POS: s
