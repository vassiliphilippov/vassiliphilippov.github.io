piaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌæstər / (AmE), / ˈpɪəstə / (BrE)
A monetary unit equal to one hundredth of a lira, used in Turkey and formerly in several other countries.
monetary unit; 1/100 lira (Turkey)

Examples

The coin was worth only one piaster, making it the smallest denomination in circulation.
In some old Turkish markets, goods were still priced in piasters as well as lira.
She exchanged her remaining piasters for souvenirs before leaving Istanbul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kurus - a subunit of the Turkish lira

Etymology

From Italian *piastre*, from Old French *piastre*, ultimately from Latin *plastra* (metal plate or coin).

2. NOUN

/ piˈæstər / (AmE), / piˈæstə / (BrE)
A fractional monetary unit used in Egypt, Lebanon, Sudan, and Syria, equal to one hundredth of a pound or lira.
fractional unit of currency in some Arab countries

Examples

In Egypt, one pound is divided into one hundred piasters.
The price of a cup of tea was fifty piasters.
Coins of various denominations, including the piaster, are used in Sudan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piastre - alternative spelling for the same monetary unit

Etymology

From Italian *piastre*, meaning 'thin metal plate' or 'coin'.
