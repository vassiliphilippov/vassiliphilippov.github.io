Pseudechis porphyriacus

1. NOUN

/ sjuːˈdiːkɪs ˌpɔːrfɪˈraɪəkəs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdiːkɪs ˌpɔːfɪˈraɪəkəs / (BrE)
A large, semiaquatic venomous snake native to eastern Australia, recognized by its glossy black upper body and red or pinkish belly.
Australian red-bellied black snake (species)

Examples

The Pseudechis porphyriacus is often found near water sources in eastern Australia.
Despite its venom, the Pseudechis porphyriacus is usually shy and avoids human contact.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Pseudechis porphyriacus in different national parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-bellied black snake - Common name for Pseudechis porphyriacus, a venomous snake of Australia

Etymology

Latinized from Greek roots: 'pseudes' (false) + 'echis' (viper) and 'porphyriacus' (purple-colored).
