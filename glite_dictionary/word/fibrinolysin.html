fibrinolysin

1. NOUN

/ faɪ.brɪˈnɑː.lɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / fɪ.brɪˈnɒ.lɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme that dissolves fibrin and helps break down blood clots
enzyme dissolving blood clots
medical

Examples

The body produces fibrinolysin to help dissolve unnecessary blood clots after healing.
Doctors studied the activity of fibrinolysin in patients recovering from surgery.
A deficiency in fibrinolysin can result in an increased risk of thrombosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasmin - the main enzyme responsible for breaking down fibrin in blood clots

Etymology

From fibrin + Greek 'lysis' (a loosening or breaking down).
