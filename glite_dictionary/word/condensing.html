condensing

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something denser or more compact, especially by removing or reducing volume or space
process of making denser or more compact

Examples

The condensing of steam into water releases significant amounts of energy.
Modern air conditioners rely on the efficient condensing of refrigerant gases.
After condensing the data, the report became much easier to read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensation - the process of a substance changing from a gas to a liquid
  • Synonym: compressing - the act of pressing something into a smaller space

Etymology

From the verb 'condense', from Latin 'condensare' (to make dense).
