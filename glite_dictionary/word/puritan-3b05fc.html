puritan

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrətən / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪtən / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with moral propriety and decorum, often criticizing others' behavior
a morally strict or censorious person

Examples

My grandmother is a bit of a puritan when it comes to what we watch on television.
He was seen as a puritan for his strict views on appropriate behavior at work.
Some critics dismiss the policy-makers as modern-day puritans bent on banning anything fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by improper things
  • Antonym: libertine - a person who behaves without moral principles

Etymology

From Latin 'puritas' (purity), via French 'puritain'; originally referring to a 16th–17th century English Protestant group advocating moral and religious strictness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrətən / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪtən / (BrE)
a person who adheres to strict moral or religious principles, often opposing pleasure or indulgence
person with strict moral or religious principles

Examples

Her father was a strict puritan who disapproved of dancing and music.
Some critics called him a puritan because he campaigned against alcohol and gambling.
Despite being labeled a puritan, she enjoyed art and theater in moderation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity
  • Antonym: hedonist - a person who believes that the pursuit of pleasure is the most important thing in life

Etymology

From Puritan, a member of a 16th-17th century English Protestant group, from late Latin *puritas* 'purity'.
