thiazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪ.əˌzin / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.əˌziːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing a six-membered ring with four carbon atoms, one nitrogen atom, and one sulfur atom
a type of six-membered heterocyclic compound

Examples

Methylene blue is an example of a synthetic dye derived from the thiazine class.
Chemists study thiazine compounds for their potential applications in medicine and industry.
The basic structure of thiazine makes it significant in the synthesis of antibiotics.

Etymology

From thio- (relating to sulfur) and azine (a class of nitrogen-containing compounds).
