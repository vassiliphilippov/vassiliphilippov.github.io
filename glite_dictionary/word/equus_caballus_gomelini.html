Equus caballus gomelini

1. NOUN

/ ˈekwəs kəˈbæləs goʊˈmɛlɪni / (AmE), / ˈe.kwəs kəˈbæl.əs gəʊˈmɛl.ɪni / (BrE)
A subspecies of European wild horse that became extinct in the early 20th century.
extinct European wild horse subspecies

Examples

The Equus caballus gomelini once roamed the forests of Eastern Europe before its extinction.
Scientists have studied Equus caballus gomelini to understand the impact of human activity on wild horse populations.
Fossils of Equus caballus gomelini provide insight into the biodiversity of early 20th-century Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarpan - another extinct wild horse subspecies of Eurasia

Etymology

From New Latin: Equus (genus for horse), caballus (Latin for horse), and gomelini (honoring zoologist Nikolai Alexeevich Gomel).
