mescaline

1. NOUN

/ mɛsˈkælin / (AmE), / mɛsˈkæliːn / (BrE)
A hallucinogenic compound found in the peyote cactus, used as a psychedelic drug.
hallucinogenic compound in peyote

Examples

The Native American ceremony involved the ritual use of mescaline extracted from the peyote cactus.
Scientists have studied the effects of mescaline on perception and consciousness.
He described vivid hallucinations after ingesting a small dose of mescaline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - a small cactus known for its psychoactive alkaloids, including mescaline

Etymology

From Spanish 'mezcal' (a type of agave) plus chemical suffix '-ine'.
