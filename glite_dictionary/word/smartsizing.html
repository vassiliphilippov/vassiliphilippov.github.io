to smartsizing

1. VERB

/ ˈsmɑrtˌsaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmɑːtˌsaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
making an organization more efficient by restructuring, optimizing, and sometimes reducing its operations and workforce.
making operations more efficient by restructuring or reducing

Examples

The company is smartsizing to boost efficiency and cut unnecessary costs.
After evaluating their workflow, management decided that smartsizing was necessary to remain competitive.
Several departments were restructured during the recent smartsizing initiative.

Synset Examples

The company is smartsizing its operations this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rightsizing - making adjustments to organizational size for optimal efficiency
  • Degree Lower: downsizing - reducing the number of employees in an organization, often for efficiency

Etymology

Blend of 'smart' (intelligent) and 'rightsizing' (adjusting size for optimal efficiency), popularized in business jargon in the late 20th century.
