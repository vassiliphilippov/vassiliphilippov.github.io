Passeridae

1. NOUN

/ pæˈsɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pəˈsɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mostly Old World birds known as true sparrows, formerly grouped with the weaverbirds.
family of true sparrows (birds)

Examples

The Passeridae family includes the common house sparrow found in many urban areas.
Ornithologists have studied the migration patterns of Passeridae across Europe and Asia.
Unlike weaverbirds, Passeridae typically build simple nests without woven structures.

Etymology

From modern Latin Passeridae, from Passer (genus name for sparrow) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
