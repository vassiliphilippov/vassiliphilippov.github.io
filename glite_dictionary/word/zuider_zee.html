Zuider Zee

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪdər ˌzeɪ / (AmE), / ˈzʌɪdə ˌzeɪ / (BrE)
a former inlet of the North Sea on the northern coast of the Netherlands, sealed off from the sea by a dam in 1932 to create the IJsselmeer.
former North Sea inlet in the Netherlands

Examples

Much of the land that was once submerged under the Zuider Zee is now used for farming.
The famous Dutch flood control projects began with the closing of the Zuider Zee in 1932.
After the Zuider Zee was sealed off, new towns were established on the reclaimed land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IJsselmeer - the freshwater lake created after the Zuider Zee was closed off

Etymology

From Dutch Zuiderzee, meaning 'Southern Sea'.
