Albulidae

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical and subtropical marine fish, commonly known as bonefish, valued for sport fishing.
family of fish known as bonefish

Examples

The Albulidae family includes species that are highly prized by recreational anglers.
Members of the Albulidae can be found in warm coastal waters around the world.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Albulidae to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonefish - a silvery marine fish prized by anglers

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Albulus, based on Latin 'albus' (white), referencing the silvery color of the fish.
