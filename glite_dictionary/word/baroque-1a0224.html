baroque

1. ADJ

/ bəˈroʊk / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk / (BrE)
highly ornate and elaborate in style, particularly with complex symmetrical decoration, especially in art, architecture, or design
elaborately ornate in style or decoration

Examples

The palace interior was baroque, with gilded mirrors and intricate carvings adorning every wall.
Her taste in jewelry is distinctly baroque, favoring bold, detailed designs over simple elegance.
They restored the church to its original baroque splendor, complete with elaborate stucco and frescoes.

Synset Examples

the building … frantically baroque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - covered with elaborate decoration; highly decorated

Etymology

From French baroque, probably from Portuguese barroco (an irregular pearl), later applied to the ornate artistic style.

2. ADJ

/ bəˈroʊk / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk / (BrE)
of or relating to the elaborate and highly decorative style of architecture, art, and music popular in Europe from about 1600 to 1750
highly ornate artistic style (1600–1750)

Examples

The cathedral's baroque architecture features dramatic columns and intricate frescoes.
Her baroque compositions are full of ornamentation and complex harmonies.
We admired the baroque paintings in the museum for their lavish detail.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rococo - an even more ornate artistic style that followed baroque in the 18th century
  • Degree Lower: classical - style emphasizing simplicity, balance, and harmony, usually predating baroque

Etymology

From French baroque, perhaps from Portuguese *barroco* (a pearl of irregular shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02985568-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ bəˈroʊk / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk / (BrE)
A highly ornate and extravagant style in art, architecture, and music that originated in Europe in the early 17th century and is characterized by elaborate detail and grandeur.
ornate European art/architectural style, 17th century

Examples

The palace is famous for its baroque architecture and lavish interiors.
She gave a lecture on the influence of baroque art on modern design.
Many churches built in the 17th century feature baroque ornamentation and grand facades.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rococo - an even more ornate European art style following baroque
  • Degree Lower: gothic - an earlier European architectural style, less ornate than baroque

Etymology

From French baroque, possibly from Portuguese barroco (irregular pearl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04705655-n
Synset POS: n
