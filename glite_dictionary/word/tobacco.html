tobacco

1. NOUN

/ təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
The dried and processed leaves of the tobacco plant, used for smoking, chewing, or sniffing.
dried leaves for smoking or chewing

Examples

He gave up tobacco after years of smoking cigarettes.
The farm specializes in growing tobacco for commercial sale.
Chewing tobacco can be just as addictive as smoking it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigarette - a thin cylinder of finely cut tobacco rolled in paper for smoking

Etymology

From Spanish 'tabaco', probably from Taino 'tabako' (a type of roll of tobacco leaves).

2. NOUN

/ təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
an annual or perennial plant of the genus Nicotiana, cultivated for its aromatic leaves and grown as a source of commercial tobacco
a plant of the genus Nicotiana

Examples

The tobacco plant is native to the Americas and widely grown in warm climates.
Several varieties of tobacco are cultivated for their leaves and ornamental value.
Botanists have identified more than sixty different species of tobacco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicotiana - genus of plants including tobacco

Etymology

From Spanish 'tabaco', of unknown origin, possibly via Taino.
