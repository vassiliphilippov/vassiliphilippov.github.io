binuclear

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈnuː.kli.ɚ / (AmE), / baɪˈnjuː.klɪə / (BrE)
having two nuclei, especially referring to cells or molecules with two distinct nuclei.
having two nuclei (cell or molecule)

Examples

Some liver cells are binuclear, containing two distinct nuclei in one cell.
The binuclear complex showed unique chemical properties compared to its mononuclear counterpart.
Researchers observed a binuclear stage during the development of certain protozoa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mononuclear - having a single nucleus
  • Degree Higher: multinuclear - having more than one nucleus

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'nucleus' (kernel, core).
