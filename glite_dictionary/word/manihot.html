Manihot

1. PROPN

/ ˈmænɪˌhɑt / (AmE), / ˈmænɪˌhɒt / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants in the spurge family, including cassava, cultivated for their edible starchy roots.
genus of tropical plants; includes cassava

Examples

Manihot esculenta is cultivated worldwide for its starchy tuberous roots, commonly known as cassava.
Botanists have identified several wild species within the Manihot genus native to South America.
The economic value of Manihot crops is significant in tropical agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - a tropical root crop also called manioc

Etymology

From Tupi *mani'ot*, the name for cassava, adopted into scientific Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæniˌhɒt / (AmE), / ˈmanɪˌhɒt / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants in the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes cassava and related species cultivated for their starchy roots.
genus of tropical plants (cassava group)

Examples

The genus Manihot includes several important crop plants, especially cassava.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of Manihot species.
The cultivation of Manihot has increased in tropical regions due to its economic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - a starchy root vegetable from plants of the genus Manihot

Etymology

From Tupi *mani oca* (house of Mani, a mythological origin for the plant cassava).
