litheness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪð.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪð.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being flexible, supple, and gracefully moving
flexible and graceful quality

Examples

The dancer's litheness allowed her to perform even the most difficult moves with ease.
The tiger moved with the litheness of a creature built for silent hunting.
She admired the litheness of the willow tree's branches swaying in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppleness - the quality of being easily bent or flexible without breaking
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and inflexible

Etymology

From Old English līthe (mild, gentle, flexible), with -ness forming a noun.
