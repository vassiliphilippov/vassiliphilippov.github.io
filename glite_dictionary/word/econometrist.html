econometrist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkɑnəˈmɛtrɪst / (AmE), / ɪˌkɒnəˈmɛtrɪst / (BrE)
an economist who specializes in applying statistical and mathematical methods to economic data
economist using statistical methods

Examples

As an econometrist, she analyzes market trends using complex statistical models.
Many companies hire an econometrist to improve their forecasting accuracy.
The econometrist presented his findings at the international economics conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: econometrician - an economist skilled in the use of econometrics

Etymology

From econometrics, which comes from Greek oikonomia (management of a household) and metron (measure).
