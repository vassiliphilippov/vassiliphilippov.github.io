spoonflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈspuːnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the aroid family with edible tubers that are cooked and eaten like yams or potatoes.
tropical plant with edible tubers

Examples

The farmers harvested spoonflower tubers and sold them at the market.
Many people in South America rely on the spoonflower as a staple crop.
She cooked the spoonflower tubers in the same way she prepares yams.
