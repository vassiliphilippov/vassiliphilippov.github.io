bouncing

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a baby or young child) very healthy and energetic.
vigorous and healthy (esp. of a baby)

Examples

The Smiths have a bouncing baby boy at home.
Everyone admired the bouncing twins in their pram.
She gave birth to a bouncing little girl last week.

Synset Examples

a bouncing baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy

Etymology

Present participle of 'bounce', used idiomatically since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of rebounding after hitting a surface, especially repeatedly
rebounding after impact

Examples

The bouncing of the basketball echoed through the gymnasium.
She giggled at the bouncing of the rubber ball around the room.
After the bouncing stopped, the ball finally came to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting something; the action of doing so
  • Synonym: ricochet - the motion of bouncing or skipping off a surface
  • Antonym: rolling - moving smoothly along a surface without bouncing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07365001-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
Full of energy or lively in manner or movement.
lively; full of energy

Examples

The children ran into the room with bouncing enthusiasm after hearing the good news.
She has a bouncing personality that lights up every conversation.
They performed a bouncing dance at the festival, thrilling the entire audience.

Synset Examples

a bouncing gait
bouncy tunes
the peppy and interesting talk
a spirited dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bouncy - tending to bounce; lively or energetic (adjective)
  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Antonym: lethargic - feeling a lack of energy or enthusiasm; sluggish
