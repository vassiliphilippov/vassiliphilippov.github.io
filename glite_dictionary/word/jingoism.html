jingoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
Extreme patriotism, especially in the form of aggressive or warlike foreign policy or rhetoric.
extreme, aggressive patriotism

Examples

The politician's speech was full of jingoism and calls for military action.
Critics accused the government of resorting to jingoism to distract from domestic problems.
Many historians warn that jingoism can lead to unnecessary conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: patriotism - Love for or devotion to one's country

Etymology

From 'jingo', a term for an aggressive patriot, itself from a refrain in a British music hall song (1878).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
Extreme patriotism, especially in the form of aggressive or belligerent foreign policy.
extreme or aggressive patriotism

Examples

The politician's speeches were filled with jingoism and calls for military action.
Critics accused the newspaper of promoting jingoism in its coverage of the border dispute.
His jingoism made it difficult for him to consider diplomatic solutions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: patriotism - love for or devotion to one's country
  • Synonym: nationalism - advocacy of or support for national interests, often to the exclusion of other interests

Etymology

From 'jingo', a term used in a British patriotic song during the Russo-Turkish War (1877–78).
