Ceratonia siliqua

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtoʊniə səˈliːkwə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtəʊniə sɪˈliːkwə / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean tree, also known as the carob, with edible pods used as a chocolate substitute and food ingredient
Mediterranean tree with edible pods; carob tree

Examples

The pods of the Ceratonia siliqua are often ground into powder for use in baking.
Many ancient cultures valued the Ceratonia siliqua for its nutritious seeds.
You can find Ceratonia siliqua growing in many Mediterranean gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - the tree or its edible tough, sweet pods

Etymology

From Latin Ceratonia (genus name) + siliqua (pod, husk)
