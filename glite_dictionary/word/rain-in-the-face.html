Rain-in-the-Face

1. PROPN

/ ˌreɪn ɪn ðə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌreɪn ɪn ðə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
A chief of the Sioux people (1835–1905), known for his participation alongside Sitting Bull at the Battle of Little Bighorn.
Sioux chief, Battle of Little Bighorn figure

Examples

Rain-in-the-Face was present at the Battle of Little Bighorn in 1876.
Many historians believe Rain-in-the-Face played a crucial role in Custer's defeat.
The story of Rain-in-the-Face is often told in accounts of the Plains Wars.

Etymology

Translation of his Lakota name, which reflected an incident where his face was streaked with war paint or blood during battle.
