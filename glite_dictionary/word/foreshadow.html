to foreshadow

1. VERB

/ foʊrˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / fəˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to give a warning or indication of a future event; to show or suggest something that will happen later
indicate or warn of a future event

Examples

The dark clouds foreshadow a coming storm.
In the novel, the broken mirror foreshadows tragedy for the main character.
The sudden drop in temperature foreshadowed the arrival of winter.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to portend - to be a sign or warning of something to happen
  • Synonym: to predict - to say that an event will happen in the future
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known that was secret

Etymology

From fore- (before) + shadow, originally meaning to prefigure, as by a shadow.
