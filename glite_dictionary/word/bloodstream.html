bloodstream

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌstrim / (AmE), / ˈblʌdstriːm / (BrE)
the blood as it circulates through the body's system of blood vessels
the blood flowing in the circulatory system

Examples

The medicine enters the bloodstream within minutes after being swallowed.
An infection in the bloodstream can be life-threatening if left untreated.
After eating, glucose is absorbed into the bloodstream and transported to the cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulatory system - the system in the body responsible for circulating blood

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈblʌdstriːm / (BrE)
the blood as it circulates through the body's system of arteries and veins
blood circulating in the body

Examples

After taking the medication, it enters the bloodstream within minutes.
Bacteria in the bloodstream can cause serious infections if not treated promptly.
Caffeine is absorbed into the bloodstream quickly after drinking coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulatory system - the system that circulates blood through the body
