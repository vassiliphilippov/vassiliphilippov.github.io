ethanediol

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːθəˈniːˌdaɪˌɑl / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˈniːˌdʌɪɒl / (BrE)
a sweet, colorless, poisonous liquid used chiefly as antifreeze and as a solvent
a toxic liquid antifreeze and solvent

Examples

The mechanic added ethanediol to the radiator to prevent the engine from freezing in winter.
Due to its toxicity, ethanediol must be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
In laboratories, ethanediol is often used as a solvent in certain chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethylene glycol - another name for ethanediol

Etymology

From 'ethane' and the suffix '-diol' indicating two hydroxyl groups.
