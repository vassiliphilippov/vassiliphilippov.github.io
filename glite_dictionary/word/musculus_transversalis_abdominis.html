musculus transversalis abdominis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kjə.ləs trænˌsvɜrˈsælɪs æbˈdɑː.mə.nɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləs trænˌsvɜːˈsælɪs æbˈdɒ.mɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
a flat abdominal muscle with transverse fibers that forms part of the anterior and lateral walls of the abdominal cavity
abdominal muscle with transverse fibers
medical

Examples

In abdominal surgery, the musculus transversalis abdominis is often encountered while creating an incision.
Proper strengthening of the musculus transversalis abdominis can help improve core stability.
The musculus transversalis abdominis lies deep to the internal oblique muscle in the abdominal wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transversus abdominis - common English term for the same abdominal muscle

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'transversalis' (transverse), 'abdominis' (of the abdomen).
