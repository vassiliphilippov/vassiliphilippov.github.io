connotational

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃənl / (BrE)
Relating to or concerned with a connotation or connotations.
relating to connotations

Examples

The word 'home' has a strong connotational meaning, evoking warmth and safety.
Writers often exploit the connotational differences between words to convey subtle shades of meaning.
The debate focused on the connotational versus denotational aspects of language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denotational - relating to denotation; referring to the explicit or primary meaning of a word

Etymology

From connotation + -al, formed from the Late Latin 'connotatio' (marking along with) and the English adjective-forming suffix '-al.'
