well-wishing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛl ˌwɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛl ˌwɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or expression of good will, especially towards someone's success or happiness
expression of good will

Examples

The newlyweds appreciated the well-wishing of their guests during the reception.
After her promotion, she received many cards of well-wishing from colleagues and friends.
The ambassador expressed his well-wishing to the country at the national holiday event.

Synset Examples

much hand-shaking and well-wishing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congratulation - the act of expressing joy or acknowledgment for someone's success or happiness
  • Antonym: condolence - an expression of sympathy, especially after a loss

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (meaning 'good') and 'wishing' (from 'wish').
