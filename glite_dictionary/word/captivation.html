captivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæp.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæp.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of fascination or enchantment; the state of being irresistibly attracted or charmed.
state of being fascinated or charmed

Examples

Her captivation with the ballet was obvious from the excitement in her eyes.
The documentary's stunning visuals held the audience in complete captivation.
He listened to the story with captivation, unable to look away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascination - the state of being intensely interested or attracted
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of feeling uninterested or unengaged

Etymology

From Latin 'captivare' (to capture) and English '-ation' suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæp.təˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæp.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of being intensely attracted to or fascinated by something, often to the point of being unable to look away or lose interest
intense fascination or attraction

Examples

The magician's performance held the children in complete captivation for an hour.
Her captivation with classical music began at a very young age.
The beauty of the mountain landscape was a source of endless captivation for the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascination - a powerful attraction or interest
  • Antonym: boredom - lack of interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'captivatio' (a taking captive), from 'captivus' (captive).
