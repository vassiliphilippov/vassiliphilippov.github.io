untravelled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtrævəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrævəld / (BrE)
(of a place or route) not having been travelled over or through by people.
not having been travelled over

Examples

The hikers took an untravelled path through the dense forest.
She dreamed of exploring untravelled regions of the world.
Much of the island remains untravelled territory even today.

Synset Examples

untraveled roads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untrodden - not walked on; not having been stepped on
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02463536-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtrævəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrævəld / (BrE)
not having traveled much, especially to foreign lands; lacking experience gained by travel
not having traveled much

Examples

The remote village lay on an untravelled road, far from the main highways.
She remained largely untravelled, never venturing outside her hometown.
His opinions were shaped by an untravelled life in a small, familiar community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-travelled - having traveled to many places, especially to foreign lands

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'travelled', the past participle of 'travel'.
