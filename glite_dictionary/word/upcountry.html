upcountry

1. ADV

/ ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (BrE)
toward or in the interior or more remote part of a country or region, away from the coast or the main urban areas
to or in the interior of a country

Examples

After graduating, she decided to move upcountry to start a new life.
Most of the coffee plantations are located upcountry where the air is cooler.
Many city dwellers travel upcountry during the holidays to escape the heat.

Synset Examples

they live upcountry

Etymology

From 'up' + 'country', originally used to contrast interior regions with lowlands or coasts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00492502-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (BrE)
located in or coming from the inland or interior part of a region or country, away from the coast or main cities
from the interior of a country

Examples

Several families from the upcountry towns relocated to the coastal city.
Tourists often visit the upcountry regions for their scenic beauty and cool climate.
The upcountry markets offer different agricultural products than those found near the coast.

Synset Examples

upcountry districts

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'country', where 'up' refers to inland or higher regions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465950-s
Synset POS: s
