Ornithopoda

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a group of herbivorous dinosaurs, including hadrosaurs, iguanodons, and related forms, characterized by bird-like feet and widespread distribution during the Mesozoic era
group of herbivorous, bird-footed dinosaurs

Examples

The discovery of a new Ornithopoda species provided insights into dinosaur evolution.
Fossils from the Ornithopoda group are found on every continent.
Scientists believe that most Ornithopoda were highly social and traveled in herds.

Etymology

From Greek 'ornitho-' (bird) and 'pous, podos' (foot), meaning 'bird-footed'.

2. NOUN

/ ɔɹˌnɪθəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a suborder of herbivorous, bird-hipped dinosaurs, including duck-billed dinosaurs and their early relatives such as hadrosaurs, trachodon, and iguanodon
suborder of bird-hipped, herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of the Ornithopoda have been discovered on every continent, including Antarctica.
Many members of the Ornithopoda developed advanced chewing mechanisms for processing tough plants.
The iguanodon, one of the best-known Ornithopoda, was first discovered in the early 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - member of the duck-billed dinosaurs in Ornithopoda
  • Synonym: iguanodon - early genus in Ornithopoda suborder

Etymology

From Greek ornith- (bird) + podos (foot), referring to bird-like feet.
