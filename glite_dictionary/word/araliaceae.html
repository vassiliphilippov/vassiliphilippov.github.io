Araliaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌræliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌralɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of mostly tropical trees, shrubs, and lianas including genera such as Panax (ginseng) and Hedera (ivy)
family of tropical trees, shrubs, and lianas

Examples

The family Araliaceae includes a number of ornamental plants commonly grown in gardens.
English ivy belongs to the Araliaceae family, which is native to many parts of the world.
Ginseng, a well-known herbal remedy, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Aralia and the suffix -aceae used for plant families.
