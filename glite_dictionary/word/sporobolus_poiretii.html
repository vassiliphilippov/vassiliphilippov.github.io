Sporobolus poiretii

1. NOUN

/ spɔːˈrɑbələs pwɑːrˈeɪtiː / (AmE), / spəˈrɒbələs pwɑːˈeɪtiː / (BrE)
A species of grass native to the West Indies and common in the southern United States, known for its tufted, wiry stems which are often infested with a dark fungus.
species of grass native to West Indies and southern U.S.

Examples

Sporobolus poiretii often thrives in sandy soils of the southern United States.
Researchers noted that Sporobolus poiretii can be identified by its wiry, tufted stems.
An unusual dark fungus commonly infests the stems of Sporobolus poiretii.

Etymology

Named in accordance with botanical Latin; 'Sporobolus' from Greek 'spora' (seed) and 'bolos' (throw), and 'poiretii' after the French botanist Jean Louis Marie Poiret.
