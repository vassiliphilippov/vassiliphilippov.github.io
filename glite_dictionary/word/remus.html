Remus

1. PROPN

/ ˈriːməs / (AmE), / ˈriːməs / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the twin brother of Romulus, said to be one of the founders of Rome.
twin brother of Romulus in Roman myth

Examples

In the legend of Rome's founding, Remus and his brother Romulus were raised by a she-wolf.
The rivalry between Remus and Romulus ended in tragedy.
According to ancient tales, Remus was killed during the construction of Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romulus - legendary founder of Rome and twin brother of Remus
