to foredate

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈdeɪt / (AmE), / fɔːˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to assign or establish a date earlier than the actual or original date; to precede in date
assign an earlier date

Examples

Some historians foredate the origins of the castle to the tenth century.
The document was deliberately foredated to mislead the investigators.
They attempted to foredate the contract to gain an unfair advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to antedate - to date something earlier than its actual date
  • Antonym: to postdate - to assign a date later than the actual or original date

Etymology

From 'fore-' (before) + 'date'.
