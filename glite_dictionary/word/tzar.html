tzar

1. NOUN

/ zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
A male monarch or emperor, especially one who ruled Russia before 1917.
Russian emperor (pre-1917)

Examples

The last tzar of Russia abdicated in 1917, ending centuries of imperial rule.
Many people associate the birth of the ballet in Russia with the patronage of the tzar.
After the fall of the tzar, Russia underwent dramatic political changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czar - a variant spelling referring to the same title
  • Synonym: tsar - a variant spelling for the Russian title of emperor

Etymology

From Russian 'tsar', from Latin 'Caesar' (emperor).
