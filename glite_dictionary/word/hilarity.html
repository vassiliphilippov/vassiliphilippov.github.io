hilarity

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈlɛrəti / (AmE), / hɪˈlarɪti / (BrE)
great merriment and laughter; extreme amusement
great merriment; extreme amusement

Examples

The comedian's jokes filled the room with hilarity from start to finish.
After the surprise, waves of hilarity swept through the entire group.
As the movie progressed, the audience erupted in spontaneous hilarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirth - amusement, especially as expressed in laughter
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness; a mood of depression or melancholy

Etymology

From Latin 'hilaritas' (cheerfulness), from Greek 'hilaros' (cheerful).
