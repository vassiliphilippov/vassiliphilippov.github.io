county

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.ti / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.ti / (BrE)
(in the United States) the largest administrative district within a state, typically with its own government and administrative responsibilities.
US: main local administrative division of a state

Examples

The county is planning to upgrade several rural highways next year.
Voters from every county participated in the statewide election.
The county courthouse is located in the center of town.

Synset Examples

the county plans to build a new road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parish - Louisiana: equivalent local government district
  • Synonym: borough - Alaska: equivalent local government district

Etymology

From Old French *conté*, from Latin *comitatus* (jurisdiction of a count).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.ti / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.ti / (BrE)
(in the UK and some other countries) a territorial division within a country, established for purposes of local government administration.
an administrative region or district
british

Examples

The county announced new funding for rural schools this year.
Visitors to the county can explore historic castles and beautiful countryside.
Elections for county councilors will be held in May.

Synset Examples

the county has a population of 12,345 people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a country or town, especially one with particular features or administrative purposes
  • Synonym: province - an administrative region within a country, similar in function to a county in some countries

Etymology

From Old French *conté* (domain of a count), from Latin *comitātus* (jurisdiction of a count).

Synset POS: n
