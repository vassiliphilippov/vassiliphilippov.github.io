stomach

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
an appetite or desire for food
appetite for food

Examples

After a long hike, she had a strong stomach for supper.
He found his stomach was gone after hearing the bad news.
Children often have a bigger stomach for sweets than adults.

Synset Examples

exercise gave him a good stomach for dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appetite - a natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food

3. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
the large, muscular, pouch-shaped organ in the digestive system, where food is mixed and partially digested before passing to the intestines
muscular organ for digesting food

Examples

After eating too much, his stomach started to ache.
The doctor explained how the stomach breaks down food into nutrients.
He felt butterflies in his stomach before his big speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing the digestive organs, between the chest and pelvis

Etymology

From Latin 'stomachus', from Greek 'stomachos' (throat, gullet, stomach).

4. VERB

/ ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
to be able to eat or tolerate a particular food or situation without feeling sick or upset
to tolerate or bear (food, situation)

Examples

He can't stomach the thought of eating oysters.
Many people find it hard to stomach spicy foods.
She could barely stomach his arrogant attitude during the meeting.

Synset Examples

He cannot stomach raw fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tolerate - to allow or endure something unpleasant
  • Synonym: to endure - to continue to exist or deal with something unpleasant
  • Synonym: to abide - to accept or tolerate something unpleasant

Etymology

From Old French *estomac*, from Latin *stomachus*, meaning 'throat, gullet, stomach' (noun), extended to verb use.

6. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
the part of the body between the chest and the pelvis, especially considered as the location of the abdomen or belly
region between the chest and pelvis

Examples

He lay on his back, his stomach rising and falling as he breathed deeply.
After overeating, my stomach felt painfully full.
She placed her hand on her stomach and laughed at the silly joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing digestive organs; the belly
  • Antonym: chest - the front surface of a person's body between the neck and the abdomen
  • Antonym: pelvis - the lower part of the trunk of the body, between the abdomen and the thighs

Etymology

From Greek *stomachos* (gullet, throat; later, stomach), via Latin *stomachus*.
