loofah

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.fə / (AmE), / ˈluː.fə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the cucumber family with cylindrical fruit, the fibrous interior of which is used as a sponge when mature.
tropical climbing plant with sponge-like fruit

Examples

The loofah grows vigorously in warm climates, producing long, green fruits.
Gardeners often harvest loofah for its unique sponge-like interior.
A mature loofah can be peeled and dried to create natural cleaning sponges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge gourd - alternative name for the loofah plant

Etymology

From Egyptian Arabic *lūf* (summer squash), via Turkish *luf* and Modern Greek *loúfa*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːfə / (AmE), / ˈluːfə / (BrE)
the dried fibrous part of the fruit of a tropical plant, used as a sponge for washing or cleaning
dried plant sponge used for washing

Examples

She scrubbed her back with a loofah to exfoliate her skin.
Natural loofahs are popular alternatives to synthetic sponges.
The loofah hung drying beside the soap in the shower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - a porous cleaning pad used to wash or clean surfaces or the body

Etymology

From Arabic *lūf* (the fruit of the loofah plant).
