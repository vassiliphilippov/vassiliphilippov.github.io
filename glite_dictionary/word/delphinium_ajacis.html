Delphinium ajacis

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˈfɪniəm aɪˈdʒeɪsɪs / (AmE), / dɛlˈfɪniəm aɪˈdʒeɪsɪs / (BrE)
a species of larkspur native to southern Europe, cultivated for its tall spikes of blue, purple, or pink flowers
species of larkspur plant

Examples

Gardeners often plant Delphinium ajacis for its striking blue blooms in early summer.
The unbranched spikes of Delphinium ajacis make it a favorite among floral arrangers.
Delphinium ajacis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larkspur - common name for plants in the genus Delphinium, known for their spike-like flowers

Etymology

Delphinium from Greek delphinion (larkspur), from delphin (dolphin), said to resemble the shape of a dolphin's nose; ajacis refers to Ajax, a hero in Greek mythology.
