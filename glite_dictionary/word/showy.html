showy

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.i / (BrE)
Having a bright, noticeable, or ostentatious appearance intended to attract attention, especially in relation to clothing or style.
conspicuously bright or ostentatious

Examples

She wore a showy red dress that caught everyone's eye at the party.
His showy behavior made him the center of attention during the event.
They avoided showy jewelry, preferring understated elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - ostentatious, attracting attention because of brightness or display
  • Antonym: modest - not showy or ostentatious; unassuming in appearance or style

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.i / (BrE)
attracting attention by displaying brilliance, virtuosity, or conspicuousness, often in a way intended to impress others
attracting attention by brilliance or conspicuousness

Examples

The pianist gave a truly showy performance that captivated the audience.
She wore a showy dress adorned with sequins that sparkled under the lights.
His showy skills on the basketball court drew everyone’s attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - bright or ostentatious in appearance; meant to attract attention
  • Antonym: modest - not displaying one's abilities or achievements; unassuming

Etymology

From 'show' + '-y', first recorded in the early 18th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.i / (BrE)
Attracting attention by being excessively bright, elaborate, or ostentatious, often in a tasteless way.
ostentatious; excessively elaborate

Examples

She wore a showy necklace that sparkled under every light in the room.
The restaurant’s showy décor was more impressive than the food itself.
He prefers simple designs over anything too showy or extravagant.

Synset Examples

a cheap showy rhinestone bracelet
a splashy half-page ad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - attracting attention by being bright or extravagant
  • Antonym: modest - not attracting attention; humble in appearance

Etymology

From 'show' + -y, dating from the 16th century, meaning 'making a display'.

4. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.i / (BrE)
impressive or attractive in a way that is intended to draw attention, often by being excessively bright, elaborate, or extravagant
ostentatiously attractive or flashy

Examples

The actress wore a showy gown covered in sequins to the awards ceremony.
They preferred a simple design rather than something overly showy or elaborate.
His house is quite showy, with gold-plated fixtures and marble floors throughout.

Synset Examples

a glossy TV series

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - attracting attention by being bright or showy
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy; understated
