Salpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælpə / (AmE), / ˈsalpə / (BrE)
A genus of free-floating tunicates belonging to the family Salpidae, found in open ocean waters.
marine animal genus (tunicate)

Examples

Scientists collected several Salpa specimens during the expedition to study their migration patterns.
The transparent body of the Salpa helps it avoid predators in the open ocean.
A school of Salpa can filter large volumes of sea water as they feed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Salpidae - Family of free-floating tunicates to which Salpa belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, from Latin 'salpa', referring to a kind of fish.
