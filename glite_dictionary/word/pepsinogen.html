pepsinogen

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛpˈsɪnədʒən / (AmE), / ˌpɛpˈsɪnədʒən / (BrE)
an inactive precursor of the enzyme pepsin, produced by cells in the stomach lining and converted to pepsin by hydrochloric acid
inactive precursor of pepsin enzyme
medical

Examples

Pepsinogen is secreted by chief cells in the stomach lining and activated to pepsin by gastric acid.
Doctors may measure pepsinogen levels to assess stomach function and diagnose certain diseases.
Pepsinogen conversion to pepsin is a key step in the digestive process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepsin - enzyme in the stomach that breaks down proteins

Etymology

From 'pepsin' + '-gen' (meaning 'producer of'), referring to its role as a precursor.
