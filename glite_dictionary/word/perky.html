perky

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːki / (AmE), / ˈpɜːki / (BrE)
cheerful, lively, and full of energy or confidence.
cheerful and lively

Examples

She walked into the office with a perky smile and lifted everyone's mood.
Despite the early hour, the children were unusually perky and chatty.
After a cup of coffee, he felt much more perky and ready for the day.

Synset Examples

buoyant spirits
his quick wit and chirpy humor
looking bright and well and chirpy
a perky little widow in her 70s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloomy - feeling or showing sadness or depression

Etymology

Probably from perk (to make smart or brisk), of uncertain origin.
