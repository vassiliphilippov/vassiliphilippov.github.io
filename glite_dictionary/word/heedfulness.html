heedfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhid.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhiːd.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being attentive and careful, especially toward responsibilities or important matters
attentive and careful quality

Examples

Her heedfulness in managing the project ensured that nothing important was overlooked.
Drivers are required to show heedfulness when navigating through school zones.
The company's success can be credited to the heedfulness of its employees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or care
  • Synonym: attentiveness - the quality of paying close attention

Etymology

From 'heedful' + '-ness', ultimately from Old English 'hēdan' (to pay attention).
