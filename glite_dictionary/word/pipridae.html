Pipridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪprɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈpɪprɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, brightly colored passerine birds native to Central and South America, commonly known as manakins.
family of birds known as manakins

Examples

The Pipridae are renowned for the elaborate courtship dances performed by males.
Many species in the Pipridae family inhabit dense tropical forests in South America.
Researchers study the vibrant plumage of Pipridae to understand sexual selection in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manakin - a small, brightly colored bird of the family Pipridae

Etymology

From New Latin Pipridae, based on the type genus 'Pipra'.
