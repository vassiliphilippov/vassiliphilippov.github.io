Popillia

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈpɪliə / (AmE), / pɒˈpɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of scarab beetles in the family Scarabaeidae, which includes species such as the Japanese beetle.
genus of scarab beetles

Examples

The Japanese beetle, a member of the genus Popillia, is a major pest of gardens.
Entomologists are studying various Popillia species to control their spread.
Popillia includes several beetles known for damaging crops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Scarabaeidae - family of scarab beetles to which Popillia belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek root 'popillos' (a name of uncertain origin).
