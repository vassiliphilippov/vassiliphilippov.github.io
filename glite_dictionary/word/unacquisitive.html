unacquisitive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkwɪz.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkwɪz.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
not interested in acquiring or possessing things; showing no desire to own or collect things
not interested in acquiring things

Examples

Though he grew up wealthy, he remained remarkably unacquisitive throughout his life.
Her unacquisitive attitude made her immune to the allure of consumer culture.
The monk lived an unacquisitive existence, untouched by material desires.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquisitive - having a strong desire to acquire or possess

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'acquisitive' (seeking to acquire).
