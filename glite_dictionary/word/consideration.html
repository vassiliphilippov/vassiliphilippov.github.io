consideration

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of discussing or formally examining a particular topic, especially within a meeting or official setting
discussion or formal examination (esp. in meetings)

Examples

The committee devoted their entire afternoon to the consideration of the proposal.
No decision was made after the lengthy consideration of the new policies.
The consideration of budget cuts sparked heated debate among the members.

Synset Examples

consideration of the traffic problem took more than an hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - an act of talking about something with others

Etymology

From Latin consideratio (examination, contemplation).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of thinking carefully about something before making a decision or forming an opinion
careful thought or deliberation

Examples

After much consideration, she accepted the job offer.
We gave careful consideration to all the possible solutions.
Your proposal is under consideration by the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberation - long and careful consideration or discussion
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - tendency to act without careful thought

Etymology

From Latin 'consideratio', from 'considerare' (to consider).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05792836-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪd.ɚˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪd.ərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a fact or factor that should be taken into account when making a decision or judgment
factor to consider in decision-making

Examples

Cost is an important consideration when choosing a new apartment.
You should take safety into consideration before hiking alone.
Another consideration is whether we have enough time to finish the project.

Synset Examples

another consideration is the time it would take

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - something that contributes to a result or decision
  • Antonym: irrelevance - something not relevant or important to a decision

Etymology

From Latin consideratio (deliberation, reflection).

4. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
thoughtful and kind regard for the feelings or needs of others
thoughtful regard for others

Examples

It's important to show consideration for your neighbors by keeping the noise down at night.
Her consideration for her colleagues made her a valued team member.
He thanked his friend for the consideration during his difficult time.

Synset Examples

he showed no consideration for her feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtfulness - the quality of thinking about what other people want or need
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of concern for the feelings of others

Etymology

From Latin consideratio (examination, reflection), from considerare (to consider).

5. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an act that shows careful thought or concern for the feelings and needs of others
thoughtful or considerate act

Examples

He showed great consideration by helping his neighbor carry groceries.
Out of consideration for the children, they turned down the volume.
Please take my feelings into consideration before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtfulness - the quality of being considerate and attentive to others
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for or pay attention to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin consideratio (examination, contemplation), from considerare (to consider).
