to parboil

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑrˌbɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːbɔɪl / (BrE)
to partially cook food, especially vegetables, by boiling it briefly
boil food briefly before further cooking

Examples

It is best to parboil potatoes before roasting them to get a crisp texture.
Many recipes require you to parboil vegetables before adding them to stews.
She always parboils her carrots to make them cook faster in the oven.

Synset Examples

Parboil the beans before freezing them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blanch - briefly boil food, then plunge into cold water

Etymology

From Old French 'parboillir' (to boil thoroughly), from Latin 'perbullire', where the 'par-' was mistakenly taken for 'partially' rather than 'thoroughly'.
