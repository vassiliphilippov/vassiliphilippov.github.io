to transmit

1. VERB

/ trænˈsmɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send or pass something from one person, place, or thing to another, especially information, signals, or diseases
send or pass on (e.g., information, signals, diseases)

Examples

The radio tower can transmit signals over great distances.
Doctors are working to ensure the disease does not transmit from person to person.
Please transmit the message to the rest of your team as soon as possible.

Synset Examples

transmit a message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to send - to cause something to go to another place or person
  • Antonym: to receive - to get or be given something that has been sent

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere', from 'trans-' (across) + 'mittere' (send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01438013-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ trænˈsmɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to pass from one person, place, or thing to another
to pass or transfer something to another

Examples

The virus can transmit from person to person through coughing and sneezing.
They took measures to prevent mosquitoes from transmitting malaria in the area.
Some diseases are transmitted by contaminated water.

Synset Examples

communicate a disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move from one place or person to another
  • Antonym: to receive - to be given or acquire something

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere', from 'trans-' (across) + 'mittere' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02236443-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ trænzˈmɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt / (BrE)
to convey or serve as the medium for transmitting something such as sound, light, or energy
to act as a medium for transmission

Examples

Metal wires can transmit electricity efficiently over long distances.
Water can transmit sound far better than air can.
The thick walls failed to transmit any noise from the street.

Synset Examples

Sound carries well over water
The airwaves carry the sound
Many metals conduct heat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - to allow heat, sound, or electricity to pass through
  • Antonym: block - to prevent something from passing or being transmitted

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere' (to send across).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02084180-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ trænˈsmɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send out (a radio or television program) by means of radio waves or other broadcasting technology
broadcast (radio/TV program)

Examples

The radio station will transmit the concert live tonight.
Due to technical issues, we are unable to transmit this program at the scheduled time.
The speech was transmitted to millions of viewers around the world.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to send out radio or television signals for public reception
  • Antonym: to receive - to get or pick up broadcast signals

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere', from 'trans-' (across) + 'mittere' (send).
