muteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjutnəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtnəs / (BrE)
the condition of being unable or unwilling to speak
inability or unwillingness to speak

Examples

After the traumatic event, her muteness lasted for several weeks.
He communicated through gestures because his muteness made speaking impossible.
The child's muteness was mistaken for shyness at first.

Synset Examples

her muteness was a consequence of her deafness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - absence of sound or speech
  • Synonym: speechlessness - inability or unwillingness to speak

Etymology

From Latin mutus (silent, unable to speak) + -ness (forming nouns of quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtnəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtnɪs / (BrE)
the state of refusing to speak when speech is expected
refusal to speak when expected

Examples

His muteness during the meeting was interpreted as disapproval.
The muteness of the witness frustrated the attorney's efforts.
Everyone noticed her sudden muteness after the controversial announcement.

Synset Examples

his silence about my contribution was surprising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - absence of sound or speech
  • Synonym: reticence - reluctance to speak or reveal one's thoughts

Etymology

From French *mutisme* and Latin *mutus* (mute, silent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04659331-n
Synset POS: n
