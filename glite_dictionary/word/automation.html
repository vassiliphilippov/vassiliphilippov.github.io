automation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
equipment and technology used to achieve automatic control or operation without human intervention.
automatic control equipment; self-operating technology

Examples

The car factory relies heavily on automation to increase productivity and reduce errors.
With modern automation, manufacturing processes can run continuously with minimal oversight.
Investing in automation has allowed the company to streamline its entire assembly line.

Synset Examples

this factory floor is a showcase for automation and robotic equipment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robotics - technology dealing with robots and their operation

Etymology

From automate, based on Greek 'automatos' (self-acting) and the suffix -ion.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use or condition of operating by automatic means, often involving machines or computers, instead of by people
operation by automatic means

Examples

Automation has transformed the manufacturing industry by reducing the need for manual labor.
Many businesses invest in automation to improve efficiency and productivity.
The rise of automation in homes includes devices like smart thermostats and robotic vacuums.

Synset Examples

automation increases productivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanization - the introduction of machines or automatic devices into a process

Etymology

From automate + -ion, patterned after French 'automatisation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The use of advanced technology, such as computer systems or robots, to control and operate equipment, often replacing human labor.
use of machines to perform tasks automatically

Examples

Automation has dramatically increased productivity in manufacturing industries worldwide.
Many routine office tasks are now handled through automation, reducing the need for manual data entry.
Critics warn that excessive automation may lead to significant job losses in certain sectors.

Synset Examples

automation replaces human workers by machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robotics - the branch of technology dealing with the design and operation of robots
  • Antonym: manual - done by hand rather than by machine

Etymology

From automate + -ion, influenced by French 'automation', first used in the context of machinery in the 1940s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00103277-n
Synset POS: n
