2. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / pəʊˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of thorny herbs or shrubs in the family Rosaceae, including plants such as salad burnet.
genus of thorny herbs or shrubs (Rosaceae)

Examples

Botanists classify salad burnet in the genus Poterium.
Members of the genus Poterium are often found in dry grasslands and open woods.
The unique flowers of Poterium distinguish it from other genera in the rose family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salad burnet - Common species in the genus Poterium, known for its edible leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'poterium', from Greek 'pōtērion' (cup), relating to the shape of the flower or fruit.
