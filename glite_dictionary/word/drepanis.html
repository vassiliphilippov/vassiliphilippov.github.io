Drepanis

1. NOUN

/ drɪˈpænɪs / (AmE), / drɪˈpænɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Hawaiian honeycreeper birds in the family Drepanididae.
genus of Hawaiian honeycreeper birds

Examples

The ʻIʻiwi is a well-known member of the genus Drepanis.
Scientists have studied the feeding habits of Drepanis species in Hawaii.
Several species within Drepanis are now extinct due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek drepanē (sickle), referring to the curved bills of these birds.

2. PROPN

/ drəˈpænɪs / (AmE), / drɪˈpænɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Drepanididae, formerly native to Hawaii and now extinct.
genus of extinct Hawaiian birds

Examples

The ʻIʻiwi was once classified under the genus Drepanis.
Species within the Drepanis genus were endemic to the Hawaiian Islands.
Extinction of the Drepanis species reflects the loss of Hawaii's unique avifauna.

Etymology

From Greek drepanē (sickle), referring to the sickle-shaped bill of the birds.
