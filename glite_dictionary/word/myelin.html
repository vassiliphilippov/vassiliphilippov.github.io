myelin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəlɪn / (BrE)
a white, fatty substance that forms a protective sheath around the axons of some nerve fibers, aiding in the transmission of nerve impulses
fatty sheath around nerve fibers
medical

Examples

Multiple sclerosis causes the myelin around nerve cells to deteriorate.
The myelin sheath ensures rapid transmission of electrical signals in the nervous system.
Researchers are studying how to regenerate myelin to treat neurological diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myelin sheath - the insulating layer of myelin around nerve fibers

Etymology

From German Myelin, coined from Greek myelos (marrow) + -in (a chemical suffix).
