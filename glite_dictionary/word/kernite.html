kernite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.naɪt / (BrE)
A light, soft mineral composed of hydrated sodium borate in crystalline form, and used as an important source of boron.
hydrated sodium borate mineral; boron source

Examples

The mining company discovered a rich deposit of kernite deep within the mountains.
Kernite is valued highly for its role as a major source of industrial boron.
Scientists examined the crystal structure of kernite under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borax - a mineral consisting of sodium borate, also used as a source of boron
  • Synonym: boron - a chemical element obtained from minerals like kernite

Etymology

Named after Kern County, California, where the mineral was first discovered, plus the suffix -ite.
