insubordination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəbɔrˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəbɔːˈdɪəʃən / (BrE)
the act of refusing to obey orders or showing disobedience to authority, especially in a workplace or military context
refusal to obey authority

Examples

The employee was dismissed for repeated insubordination toward his manager.
Military insubordination is taken very seriously and can result in court-martial.
Her refusal to follow company policy was seen as an act of insubordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disobedience - the refusal or failure to obey rules or someone in authority
  • Antonym: obedience - the act or practice of following commands or instructions

Etymology

From Latin 'insubordinatio', from 'in-' (not) + 'subordinatio' (subordination).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˌbɔɚ.dəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˌbɔː.dɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
refusal to obey orders or show respect to someone in authority; open defiance of authority
defiance of authority

Examples

The manager warned that any further insubordination would result in disciplinary action.
His refusal to follow the supervisor’s instructions was seen as blatant insubordination.
Military commanders often have little tolerance for insubordination among the ranks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with orders or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'insubordinatio', from 'in-' (not) + 'subordinatio' (subordination).
