to besmirch

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsmɝtʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈsmɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to damage the reputation or honor of someone or something; to make dirty or stained, especially in a figurative sense
damage someone's reputation

Examples

She would never besmirch his character with unfounded accusations.
The scandal threatened to besmirch the company's image for years to come.
His rivals tried to besmirch his good name during the election.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sully - to damage the purity or integrity of something
  • Synonym: to defame - to harm someone's good reputation
  • Antonym: honor - high respect; to regard with great respect

Etymology

From Middle English 'bismirċen' (to soil), from 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'smirch' (to stain).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈsmɝːtʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈsmɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to damage the reputation or appearance of someone or something; to make dirty or stained
damage reputation or make dirty

Examples

The scandal threatened to besmirch his otherwise impeccable reputation.
She accused the newspaper of trying to besmirch her good name.
The mud on his clothes besmirched the white carpet as he walked through the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - to make or become less bright or a reputation less pure
  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone
  • Antonym: honor - to regard with great respect; opposite in context of reputation

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + smirch (to soil), from Middle English 'smirch', likely from Old French 'esmircher'.
