uninquisitive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (BrE)
not interested in learning or asking questions; lacking curiosity.
not curious; lacking inquisitiveness

Examples

The uninquisitive student rarely asked questions during class discussions.
Her uninquisitive approach meant she missed many interesting details.
Some people are naturally uninquisitive and accept things as they are.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curious - eager to know or learn something
  • Antonym: inquisitive - having or showing curiosity

Etymology

From un- (not) + inquisitive (from Latin inquirere, to seek after, inquire).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (BrE)
Not curious or inclined to ask questions or seek information.
not curious; not inclined to inquire

Examples

The uninquisitive student rarely participated in class discussions or asked questions.
Her uninquisitive nature led her to accept things as they were without seeking explanations.
Unlike his curious sister, Tom was rather uninquisitive about the world around him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inquisitive - curious; inclined to ask questions

Etymology

From un- (not) + inquisitive, from Latin 'inquisitivus' (eager to search or examine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00883362-a
Synset POS: a
