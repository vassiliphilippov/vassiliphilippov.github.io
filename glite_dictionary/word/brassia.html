Brassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræsi.ə / (AmE), / ˈbrasɪə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids, commonly called spider orchids, characterized by long slender sepals and distinctive yellow to green flowers
genus of tropical American spider orchids

Examples

The Brassia in her greenhouse bloomed with dozens of spiderlike flowers.
Many orchid enthusiasts favor the unique appearance of Brassia species when building their collections.
A rare Brassia orchid won first prize at the flower exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider orchid - common name for orchids of the genus Brassia and related genera with spider-like flowers

Etymology

Named after the British botanist William Brass (fl. 1775–1817).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbræsiə / (AmE), / ˈbræsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids, known as spider orchids, with yellow to green, spiderlike flowers bearing long slender sepals.
a genus of spider orchids

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare collection of Brassia in full bloom.
Many orchid enthusiasts prize the unique, spiderlike flowers of Brassia.
Several species within the Brassia genus are popular among collectors for their unusual appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

Named after William Brass, British botanical illustrator (18th century).
