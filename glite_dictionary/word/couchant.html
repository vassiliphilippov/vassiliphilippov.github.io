couchant

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaʊ.tʃənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.tʃənt / (BrE)
(of an animal) lying with the body on the ground and the head raised, especially with the forelegs extended forward, as in heraldry.
lying with body down and head raised (heraldry)

Examples

The family's coat of arms features a lion couchant beneath a tree.
In the medieval tapestry, several stags are depicted couchant by the riverside.
The herald described the animal as couchant, rather than rampant or passant.

Etymology

From Old French 'couchant' (lying down), present participle of 'coucher' (to lie down, lay).
