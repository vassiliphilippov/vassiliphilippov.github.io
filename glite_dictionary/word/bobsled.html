bobsled

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːbˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.slɛd / (BrE)
to ride in or operate a bobsled, especially down an icy or snowy track
ride in or drive a bobsled

Examples

Every winter, tourists flock to the mountains to bobsled on the famous Olympic track.
He dreamed of competing in the Winter Games and practiced bobsledding every chance he got.
We bobsledded down the hill, laughing at how fast we picked up speed.

Synset Examples

The boys bobbed down the hill screaming with pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luge - to ride a small sled lying feet first
  • Degree Lower: sled - to ride on any sled or sledge

Etymology

From 'bob' (to move up and down) + 'sled', originally referring to a type of toboggan designed for high speeds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01943670-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑbˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.slɛd / (BrE)
a long, narrow sled with runners and a steering mechanism, designed to carry two or more people down an ice track at high speed, especially in competitive races
long, narrow racing sled for multiple people

Examples

The team raced down the icy track in their sleek bobsled.
Winter Olympic events include both the luge and the bobsled competitions.
After months of training, she finally got her chance to ride in a real bobsled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luge - a small, one- or two-person sled ridden in a supine position feet first
  • Synonym: skeleton - a small, single-rider sled ridden face down head first

Etymology

From 'bob' (to move up and down) + 'sled', originally referring to the up-and-down motion of riders.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02864187-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.slɛd / (BrE)
A sled, especially one formerly made from two short sleds coupled together, used for racing down an ice track.
type of racing sled, formerly two sleds joined

Examples

The original bobsled was made by joining two toboggans together.
The team restored an antique bobsled for display at the museum.
In the early days, a bobsled required more teamwork to steer and brake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luge - small one- or two-person sled used for racing
  • Synonym: bobsleigh - another name for bobsled in British English

Etymology

From 'bob', meaning to move up and down, plus 'sled'. Term used since the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02864362-n
Synset POS: n
