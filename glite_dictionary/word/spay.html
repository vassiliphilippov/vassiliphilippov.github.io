to spay

1. VERB

/ speɪ / (AmE), / speɪ / (BrE)
to remove the ovaries of a female animal, especially to prevent it from reproducing
remove the ovaries of a female animal

Examples

We plan to spay our dog next week to prevent unwanted puppies.
The shelter requires all adopted cats to be spayed or neutered.
She is studying veterinary medicine and learned how to spay rabbits in her internship.

Synset Examples

Is your cat spayed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuter - to remove the reproductive organs of an animal, making it unable to breed

Etymology

From Old French *espeer* (to cut with a sword), related to the surgical procedure.
