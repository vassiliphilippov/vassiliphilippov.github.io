Phthorimaea operculella

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɔːrəˈmiːə ˌɑːpərkjuːˈlɛlə / (AmE), / ˌθɔːrɪˈmiːə ˌɒpəkjuːˈlɛlə / (BrE)
A species of greyish-brown moth whose larvae are known as the potato tuberworm, a pest that damages potato crops.
a moth species; potato tuberworm

Examples

The larva of Phthorimaea operculella is a major pest in potato fields worldwide.
Farmers use pheromone traps to monitor populations of Phthorimaea operculella.
Infestations of Phthorimaea operculella can cause significant losses in potato storage facilities.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Phthorimaea and species epithet operculella.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfθɔrəˈmiːə ˌɒpərˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / ˌfθɒrɪˈmiːə ˌɒpəˈkjuːlɪə / (BrE)
A species of small moth whose larvae are pests that feed on potatoes and other plants, mining in their leaves and stems.
species of moth; pest of potatoes

Examples

The larvae of Phthorimaea operculella are notorious for damaging potato crops worldwide.
Farmers monitor fields carefully to detect infestations of Phthorimaea operculella.
Effective pest control is vital when Phthorimaea operculella threatens both potatoes and tobacco.

Etymology

From Greek 'phthorimaia' (destructive) and Latin 'operculum' (lid), referring to features of the species.
