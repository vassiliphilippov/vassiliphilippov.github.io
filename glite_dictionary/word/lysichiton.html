Lysichiton

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɪkɪˌtɒn / (AmE), / laɪˈsɪkɪˌtɒn / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the arum family, native to North America and eastern Asia, commonly known as skunk cabbages.
genus of skunk cabbages (plant)

Examples

The western Lysichiton is noted for its large yellow spathes and pungent smell.
Botanists discovered several Lysichiton plants thriving along the riverbanks.
The distinctive scent of Lysichiton can often be detected in wetlands during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - a common name for several plants with a distinctive odor, including those in genus Lysichiton

Etymology

From Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'chiton' (tunic), referring to the plant's spathe.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɪkɪtɒn / (AmE), / laɪˈsɪkɪtɒn / (BrE)
A genus of North American and East Asian perennial herbs in the arum family, known as skunk cabbages, with large leaves and distinctive foul-smelling flowers.
a genus of skunk cabbages (plants)

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with clusters of Lysichiton, their broad leaves looming over the marshy ground.
Gardeners sometimes plant Lysichiton for its striking appearance despite its strong odor.
In early spring, the bright yellow spathes of Lysichiton can be seen emerging from the melting snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - a common name for plants of the Lysichiton genus, noted for their odor

Etymology

From Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'chiton' (tunic), referring to features of the plant's structure.
