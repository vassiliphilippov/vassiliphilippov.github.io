hypoesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊɪsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
reduced sensitivity to touch or other sensory stimuli, often due to nerve impairment
reduced sensitivity to touch (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient reported hypoesthesia in her left hand following the surgery.
Peripheral nerve damage can lead to hypoesthesia in the affected area.
The neurologist noted hypoesthesia when examining the patient's lower limbs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anesthesia - loss of sensation, especially the inability to feel pain
  • Antonym: hyperesthesia - excessive sensitivity to touch or sensory stimuli

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under, less) and aisthēsis (sensation).
