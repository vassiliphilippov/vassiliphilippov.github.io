Falcatifolium taxoides

1. NOUN

/ fælˌkætɪˈfoʊliəm tæksˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / fælˌkatɪˈfəʊliəm taksˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a species of tree or shrub native to the rainforests of New Caledonia, recognized by its conic crown and pale green, sickle-shaped leaves; serves as a host for the rare parasite yew
rainforest tree or shrub of New Caledonia

Examples

The rare parasite yew can only complete its life cycle on Falcatifolium taxoides.
Botanists documented a new population of Falcatifolium taxoides deep in the New Caledonian rainforest.
The distinctive sickle-shaped leaves make Falcatifolium taxoides easy to identify among other conifers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'falcatifolium' (sickle-shaped leaves) and 'taxoides' (resembling yew).
