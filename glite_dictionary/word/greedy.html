greedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡridi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdi / (BrE)
Having or showing an intense and eager desire for something, often beyond what is reasonable or fair.
intensely desirous; eager for more

Examples

She was greedy for knowledge and read every book she could find.
His greedy ambition pushed him to work day and night.
The investor became greedy for more profit, ignoring all risks.

Synset Examples

avid for adventure
an avid ambition to succeed
fierce devouring affection
the esurient eyes of an avid curiosity
greedy for fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avid - having or showing a keen interest or enthusiasm for something
  • Degree Higher: insatiable - impossible to satisfy
  • Antonym: content - satisfied; not desiring more

Etymology

From Old English *grēdig* (voracious, covetous).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡridi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdi / (BrE)
having an excessive desire for more of something, especially money or food, than is needed or fair
excessively desirous of more; avaricious

Examples

The greedy child tried to grab more than his share of the candy.
She accused the company of being greedy for laying off workers to increase profits.
His greedy behavior alienated him from his friends.

Synset Examples

they are avaricious and will do anything for money
casting covetous eyes on his neighbor's fields
a grasping old miser
grasping commercialism
greedy for money and power
grew richer and greedier
prehensile employers stingy with raises for their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avaricious - having or showing an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: selfless - concerned more with others' needs than one's own; unselfish

Etymology

From Old English grēdig (voracious, covetous), related to greed.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡridi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdi / (BrE)
Wanting to eat or drink more than is reasonable or fair; having an excessive desire for food or drink.
eager for more food or drink than needed

Examples

Don't be greedy with the cake—leave some for everyone else.
He was so greedy at dinner that he ate three helpings before anyone had seconds.
Children can become greedy when there are lots of sweets on the table.

Synset Examples

don't be greedy with the cookies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttonous - excessively eating or drinking; synonym for greedy in this sense
  • Antonym: selfless - putting others' needs before one's own; often used as an antonym of greedy

Etymology

From Old English *grǣdig* (voracious, covetous); related to Old High German *grātig*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
