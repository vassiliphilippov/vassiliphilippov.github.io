workload

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌləʊd / (BrE)
the amount of work that an individual is expected to do within a particular period of time
amount of work assigned or expected

Examples

My workload has increased significantly since the company downsized.
She struggled to keep up with her heavy workload during the busy season.
The manager tried to distribute the workload evenly among the team members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - something carried; a heavy load or responsibility
  • Synonym: responsibility - something one is required to do as part of a job or role

Etymology

Formed from 'work' + 'load', first recorded in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌləʊd / (BrE)
the amount of work that someone has to do within a particular period of time
amount of work to be done

Examples

Her workload increased significantly after the company hired fewer staff.
Teachers often complain about the heavy workload during the exam season.
I'm trying to balance my workload with my personal life this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or responsibility
  • Synonym: responsibility - a duty or task that you are required or expected to do
