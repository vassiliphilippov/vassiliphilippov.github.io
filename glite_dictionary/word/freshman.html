freshman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃmən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃmən / (BrE)
a person in the first year of high school, college, or a particular experience, especially in the United States; sometimes used to describe a first-year participant in a field or organization
first-year student or participant
american

Examples

She made many friends during her freshman year at college.
As a freshman senator, he was eager to make an impression.
The company hired several freshman engineers to join their training program.

Synset Examples

a freshman senator
freshman year in high school or college

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sophomore - a student in the second year of high school or college (US)

Etymology

From fresh + man, originally used for university students in 16th-century England.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃmən / (BrE)
a student in the first year of high school, college, or university
a first-year student

Examples

As a freshman, he sometimes felt overwhelmed by the size of the university.
The college offers a special orientation week for all incoming freshman students.
Last year, she joined the debate club as a freshman and made many new friends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sophomore - student in the second year of high school or college

Etymology

From fresh (new) + man, first recorded in this sense in English universities in the mid-16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃmən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃmən / (BrE)
A person who is new to an activity, organization, or group, especially someone in their first year of involvement.
a new participant; first-year member

Examples

As a freshman in the chess club, he was eager to learn from the older members.
The company welcomes every freshman with a mentorship program to help them get started.
She felt nervous attending her first writers' workshop as a freshman among more experienced authors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to a job or activity
  • Antonym: veteran - someone with experience in a particular field or activity

Etymology

From fresh (new) + man; first recorded in 16th century referring to a first-year student.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10375148-n
Synset POS: n
