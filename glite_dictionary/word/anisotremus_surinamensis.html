Anisotremus surinamensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪsoʊˈtriːməs ˌs(j)ʊərɪnəmˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænɪsəʊˈtriːməs ˌsjʊərɪnəmˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of dusky grey food fish found from Louisiana and Florida southward, commonly known as the black margate.
a dusky grey food fish of the western Atlantic

Examples

The Anisotremus surinamensis is a popular target for anglers along the Gulf coast.
Local markets often sell Anisotremus surinamensis due to its desirable taste and size.
Marine biologists are studying the population of Anisotremus surinamensis in Florida waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black margate - common name for Anisotremus surinamensis, a food fish found in the western Atlantic

Etymology

From Greek anisos (unequal) + trema (hole), referring to features of the genus, and 'surinamensis' meaning 'of Suriname' in Latin.
