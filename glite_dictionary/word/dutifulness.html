dutifulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːtɪfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdjuːtɪfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being conscientious in fulfilling one's duties or obligations
quality of being dutiful

Examples

Her dutifulness impressed her teachers and made her a role model among her classmates.
He performed his tasks with quiet dutifulness, never seeking attention or praise.
Their family's dutifulness to tradition was clear during every holiday celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility
  • Synonym: obedience - compliance with an order, request, or law, or submission to another's authority
  • Antonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something

Etymology

From 'dutiful' + '-ness', with 'duty' from Anglo-French 'deu' (debt, obligation).
