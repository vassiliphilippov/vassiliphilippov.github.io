upwards

1. ADV

/ ˈʌpwərdz / (AmE), / ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
At or towards a later time; starting at a particular point in time and continuing onward.
from a certain time onward

Examples

He has followed tennis from childhood upwards, never missing a tournament.
Children aged five upwards are welcome to join the class.
From the 19th century upwards, the town experienced rapid growth.

Synset Examples

they moved the meeting date up
from childhood upward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onward - continuing forward in time or progress

Etymology

From Middle English upwardes, from upweard (upward) + -es (adverbial genitive suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈʌpwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
towards a higher position, level, or amount; moving or directed from lower to higher, either physically or metaphorically
towards a higher position or amount

Examples

The helium balloon drifted upwards into the sky until it disappeared from view.
Temperatures are expected to rise upwards of 30 degrees Celsius over the weekend.
With each promotion, she advanced upwards in the company hierarchy.

Synset Examples

look up!
the music surged up
the fragments flew upwards
prices soared upwards
upwardly mobile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downwards - towards a lower position or level
  • Synonym: upwardly - in an upward direction (formal or technical equivalent)

Etymology

From Middle English 'upward' (upward), with -s as an adverbial suffix.
