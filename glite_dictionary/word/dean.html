dean

1. NOUN

/ diːn / (AmE), / diːn / (BrE)
an administrator in charge of a specific division, faculty, or group of students within a university or college
university or college administrator

Examples

Dr. Smith has been appointed dean of the engineering faculty.
The dean called a meeting to discuss the new curriculum changes.
As dean, she is responsible for overseeing student affairs in the college.

Etymology

From Old French *deien*, from Latin *decanus* (head of a group of ten).

2. NOUN

/ din / (AmE), / diːn / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the head of the College of Cardinals, responsible for presiding over the college and certain official ceremonies.
head of the College of Cardinals (Catholic Church)

Examples

The dean of the College of Cardinals presided at the papal conclave after the Pope's death.
As dean, he played a key role in mediating discussions among the cardinals.
The new dean was elected following the retirement of his predecessor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal - a leading dignitary of the Roman Catholic Church, one of the College of Cardinals

Etymology

From Old French *deien*, from Latin *decanus* (head of ten).

3. NOUN

/ din / (AmE), / diːn / (BrE)
the most senior or respected member of a particular group or profession
senior or respected group member

Examples

After sixty years in journalism, he was known as the dean of science reporters.
She is often called the dean of American architects for her influential career.
At the annual awards dinner, everyone deferred to the dean of the company.

Synset Examples

he is the dean of foreign correspondents

Etymology

From Old French *deien*, from Latin *decanus* (chief of ten, leader of a group).
