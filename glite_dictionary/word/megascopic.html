megascopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛg.əˈskɑ.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛg.əˈskɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
large enough to be seen with the naked eye, especially referring to rocks or anatomical features.
visible to the naked eye

Examples

The megascopic structure of the mineral could be studied without any magnification.
In anatomy, some tissues reveal megascopic features that are important for diagnosis.
The geologist examined the rock's megascopic properties before sending samples to the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macroscopic - large enough to be seen without a microscope

Etymology

From mega- (large) + -scopic (from Greek skopein, to look).
