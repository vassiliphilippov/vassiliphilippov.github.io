lentisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.tɪsk / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪsk / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub (Pistacia lentiscus) of the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its aromatic resin known as mastic.
Mediterranean shrub yielding mastic resin

Examples

The resin from the lentisk has been harvested for centuries to make mastic gum.
Ancient Greeks valued the lentisk for both its resin and its medicinal properties.
In the Mediterranean landscape, the lentisk shrub thrives in rocky soils and dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastic - aromatic resin obtained from the lentisk

Etymology

From Latin lentiscus, from Greek lentiskos (the mastic tree).
