harikari

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːriˈkɑːri / (AmE), / ˌhɑːriˈkɑːri / (BrE)
A ritual suicide by self-disembowelment with a sword, especially as practiced by samurai in traditional Japanese society.
ritual suicide by self-disembowelment

Examples

In feudal Japan, disgraced samurai would sometimes commit harikari to restore their honor.
The novel describes the protagonist's decision to perform harikari after a shameful defeat.
Documentaries about samurai culture often discuss the significance of harikari in Japanese history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seppuku - another term for ritual suicide by disembowelment practiced by samurai

Etymology

From Japanese 'hara-kiri', literally 'belly cutting': 'hara' (belly) + 'kiri' (cutting).
