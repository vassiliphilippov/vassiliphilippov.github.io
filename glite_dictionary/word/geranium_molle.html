Geranium molle

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm ˈmɒli / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪnɪəm ˈmɒli / (BrE)
a species of wild geranium native to Europe and western Asia, with small pink flowers; often found as a common weed in lawns and vacant lots
a wild geranium species with small pink flowers

Examples

Geranium molle is frequently found in gardens and lawns across Europe.
The small pink flowers of Geranium molle add a delicate touch to wild meadows.
Many gardeners remove Geranium molle because it can quickly spread and become a weed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranesbill - common name for plants of the genus Geranium

Etymology

From Latin: 'geranium' from Greek geranion (crane), and 'molle' meaning 'soft'.
