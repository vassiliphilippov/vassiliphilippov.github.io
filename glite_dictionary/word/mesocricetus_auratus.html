Mesocricetus auratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsoʊkraɪˈsiːtəs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊkrɪˈsiːtəs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the golden hamster, a small, light-colored rodent species commonly kept as a pet.
golden hamster (scientific name)

Examples

The Mesocricetus auratus is a popular choice for children's first pets due to its gentle nature.
Researchers often use Mesocricetus auratus in laboratory studies involving genetics and behavior.
A typical lifespan for Mesocricetus auratus in captivity ranges from two to three years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden hamster - common name for Mesocricetus auratus, a small rodent kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle), 'kríkos' (ring), and Latin 'auratus' (golden).
