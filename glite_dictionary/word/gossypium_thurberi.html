Gossypium thurberi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːsiˈpiːəm ˈθɜːrbəri / (AmE), / ˌɡɒsɪˈpiːəm ˈθɜːbəri / (BrE)
A shrub native to southern Arizona and Mexico, belonging to the cotton genus, known for its hairy leaves and ornamental value.
shrub of southern Arizona and Mexico

Examples

The botanical garden displays a beautiful specimen of Gossypium thurberi near its desert plants exhibit.
In southern Arizona, Gossypium thurberi is valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of Gossypium thurberi to improve cotton cultivation.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gossypium' (cotton genus) and 'thurberi', named after 19th-century botanist George Thurber.
