bey

1. NOUN

/ beɪ / (AmE), / beɪ / (BrE)
(historically) a title of respect for a man, used in Turkey or Egypt, placed after the given name.
title of respect in Turkey or Egypt

Examples

The local governor was known as Ahmed Bey among the townspeople.
In nineteenth-century Egypt, wealthy landowners were often addressed as bey.
During his travels, he was introduced to Mustafa Bey, a respected official.

Synset Examples

he introduced me to Ahmet Bey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasha - another former Turkish title of rank and office

Etymology

From Turkish 'bey', originally from Old Turkic 'beg', meaning 'chieftain' or 'lord'.

2. NOUN

/ beɪ / (AmE), / beɪ / (BrE)
the governor of a district or province in the Ottoman Empire; later a title for Turkish or Ottoman officials and dignitaries.
Ottoman provincial governor or noble

Examples

The sultan appointed a bey to oversee the affairs of the province.
During the Ottoman period, a bey wielded considerable political influence in his district.
The old palace once belonged to a powerful bey of the empire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pasha - a higher-ranking title for officials in the Ottoman Empire
  • Synonym: governor - the official in charge of governing a region or province

Etymology

From Turkish 'bey' (chieftain, lord), of Old Turkic origin.
