avirulent

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪˈvɪr.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈvɪr.jʊ.lənt / (BrE)
not virulent; unable to cause disease
not able to cause disease
medical

Examples

The avirulent strain of bacteria posed no threat to the patient's health.
Scientists used an avirulent form of the virus to develop the vaccine.
Despite exposure, none of the animals became ill due to the avirulent pathogen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virulent - extremely severe or harmful in its effects; highly infectious

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'a-' (not) + 'virulentus' (full of poison).
