tolu

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊluː / (AmE), / ˈtɒljuː / (BrE)
an aromatic yellowish-brown balsam obtained from the Tolu balsam tree, used especially in cough syrups and perfumes
aromatic resin from the Tolu tree

Examples

The pharmacist used tolu as an ingredient in the homemade cough syrup.
A faint scent of tolu lingered in the apothecary's workshop.
Traditional remedies sometimes include tolu for treating respiratory ailments.

Etymology

From Spanish 'tolú', named after Tolú, a town in northern Colombia.
