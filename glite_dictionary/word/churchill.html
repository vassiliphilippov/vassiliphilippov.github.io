Churchill

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃɪl / (BrE)
(1874–1965) British prime minister, orator, and writer, known for his leadership during World War II and for receiving the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953.
British prime minister and leader during WWII

Examples

Churchill is remembered for his stirring speeches that inspired Britain to stand firm during World War II.
The literary achievements of Churchill were recognized with a Nobel Prize in 1953.
Many historians consider Churchill one of the greatest leaders of the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Winston Churchill - The full name of Churchill, the British prime minister during WWII

2. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃɪl / (BrE)
a town in northern Manitoba, Canada, on Hudson Bay, known as an important port for shipping grain and for its polar bear population.
Canadian town on Hudson Bay

Examples

Many tourists visit Churchill each fall to see the migrating polar bears.
Grain from the Canadian prairies is shipped through the port at Churchill.
The remote town of Churchill has been called the "Polar Bear Capital of the World".
