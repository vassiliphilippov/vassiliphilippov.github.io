shim

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪm / (AmE), / ʃɪm / (BrE)
a thin piece of material, such as wood, metal, or stone, used to fill a space or make something level
thin spacer or wedge

Examples

The carpenter slid a shim under the door to make it level.
You may need to add a shim to stop the table from wobbling.
He used a thin metal shim to fill the gap between the parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of material with one thick end and one thin end used to split or hold objects apart

Etymology

From Scottish English 'shim', of uncertain origin, possibly from a dialectal word for a thin slice, related to 'shive'.
