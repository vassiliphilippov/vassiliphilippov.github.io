tine

1. NOUN

/ taɪn / (AmE), / taɪn / (BrE)
a prong or sharp projecting point, especially on a fork, pitchfork, or antler
prong of a fork or antler

Examples

The sharp tine of the fork speared the salad easily.
The deer’s antlers had an extra tine this year, making them look more impressive.
The farmer repaired the broken tine on his pitchfork before heading out to the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prong - a pointed projection, similar object as 'tine'

Etymology

From Old English tīan, related to Dutch teen (toe) and German Zehe (toe).
