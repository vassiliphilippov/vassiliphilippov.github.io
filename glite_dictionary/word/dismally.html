dismally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪzməli / (AmE), / ˈdɪzməli / (BrE)
In a way that is gloomy, cheerless, or depressing.
in a gloomy or cheerless manner

Examples

The rain fell dismally against the window, matching the mood in the room.
He dismally failed to meet the expectations of his teachers.
The play was dismally received by critics and audiences alike.

Synset Examples

in August 1914, there was a dismally sentimental little dinner, when the French, German, Austrian and Belgian members of the committee drank together to the peace of the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a gloomy manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a cheerful manner; with cheer

2. ADV

/ ˈdɪzməli / (AmE), / ˈdɪzməli / (BrE)
In a gloomy or dreadful manner; in a way that causes or suggests sadness or hopelessness.
in a gloomy or dreadful manner

Examples

The team dismally failed to meet their targets this quarter.
She stared dismally out the window, watching the rain fall.
Sales figures dismally dropped despite the company's advertising efforts.

Synset Examples

as he looks at the mess he has left behind he must wonder how the Brits so often managed to succeed in the kind of situation where he has so dismally failed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a gloomy or hopeless manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner

Etymology

From 'dismal' (from Anglo-Norman 'dis mal', meaning 'evil day') + '-ly'.
