Pulsatilla patens

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌl.səˈtɪl.ə ˈpeɪ.tənz / (AmE), / ˌpʊl.səˈtɪl.ə ˈpeɪ.tənz / (BrE)
A short, hairy perennial plant with early spring blue-violet or lilac flowers, native to North America and Siberia.
species of flowering plant

Examples

The delicate blooms of Pulsatilla patens are among the first signs of spring in the prairie.
Gardeners prize Pulsatilla patens for its striking blue-violet flowers and feathery foliage.
Wild populations of Pulsatilla patens are declining due to habitat loss and over-collection.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pulsatilla' from 'pulsare' (to beat or strike), referring to the flower's swaying in the wind, and 'patens' meaning 'spreading' or 'open'.
