arrogance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəɡəns / (AmE), / ˈærəɡəns / (BrE)
The quality of having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities, often expressed in a superior or disdainful manner toward others.
overbearing pride or self-importance

Examples

His arrogance made it difficult for anyone to work with him on the team.
Many people were offended by the arrogance in her speech.
Despite his talent, arrogance was his downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's achievements
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness

Etymology

From Old French 'arrogance', from Latin 'arrogantia' (presumption, pride).
