dynamiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who uses dynamite, especially for revolutionary or political purposes
person using dynamite, often politically

Examples

The police arrested the suspected dynamiter at the scene of the explosion.
In the late 19th century, some revolutionaries were known as dynamiters for their use of explosives.
Literature from that era often depicted the dynamiter as a shadowy figure plotting destruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saboteur - person who deliberately destroys or damages things for political or military advantage

Etymology

From 'dynamite' (explosive) + suffix '-er' (agent noun).
