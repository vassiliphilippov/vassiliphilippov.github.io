reciter

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who repeats or performs something from memory, especially poetry or passages of literature
person who recites from memory

Examples

The reciter impressed the audience with her flawless memory of the poem.
Every reciter must practice regularly to perfect their delivery.
At the competition, each reciter took turns performing classic verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performer - a person who entertains an audience
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens

Etymology

From Latin recitator, from recitare (to read out, recite).
