Olea europaea

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌliːə ˌjʊrəˈpiːə / (AmE), / əʊˌliːə ˌjʊərəˈpiːə / (BrE)
The olive tree, an evergreen species native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its edible fruit and oil.
the olive tree (Mediterranean evergreen)

Examples

The ancient Romans cultivated Olea europaea extensively for both its fruit and oil.
In the museum garden, you can see a 200-year-old Olea europaea specimen.
International research focuses on the genetics of Olea europaea to improve disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive - the small, oval fruit of the olive tree

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'European olive'.
