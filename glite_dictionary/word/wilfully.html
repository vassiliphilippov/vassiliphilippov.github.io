wilfully

1. ADV

/ ˈwɪlfəli / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfəli / (BrE)
In a deliberate, intentional, or stubborn manner; on purpose, often with the implication of disobedience or defiance.
in a deliberate or intentional way

Examples

She wilfully ignored the warning signs posted outside the building.
The child wilfully broke the vase after being told not to touch it.
He wilfully withheld information from the investigators.

Synset Examples

she had willfully deceived me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberately - In a careful, intentional manner
  • Antonym: accidentally - Without intention; by chance

Etymology

From 'willful' + '-ly,' patterned after 'will' (Old English 'willan'). See also: 'willfully' (US spelling).
