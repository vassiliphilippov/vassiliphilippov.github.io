roiled

1. ADJ

/ rɔɪld / (AmE), / rɔɪld / (BrE)
angry or agitated, especially as a result of disturbance or provocation
angry or agitated

Examples

The manager was roiled by the repeated delays in the project.
Citizens were roiled over the abrupt changes to the regulations.
She left the meeting feeling thoroughly roiled by the unfair accusations.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riled - annoyed or irritated
  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or anger

Etymology

From the verb 'roil', ultimately from Old French 'rouillir' (to stir up).

2. ADJ

/ rɔɪld / (AmE), / rɔɪld / (BrE)
(of a liquid) agitated, disturbed, or in a state of turbulence; unsettled or muddy.
turbulent or disturbed (of a liquid)

Examples

After the heavy rain, the river was roiled and full of debris.
The diver hesitated before entering the roiled waters of the bay.
Sediment made the lake roiled and cloudy, blocking the sunlight.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm
  • Antonym: calm - (of a liquid) not disturbed or turbulent; still

Etymology

From the verb 'roil', which derives from Old French 'rouiller' (to render muddy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00088282-s
Synset POS: s
