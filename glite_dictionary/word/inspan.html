to inspan

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌspæn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌspæn / (BrE)
to attach a yoke or harness to animals, especially for drawing a vehicle or plough
attach a yoke or harness to animals

Examples

The farmer needed to inspan the oxen before starting the day's work.
We watched as they inspanned the horses to the old cart.
After breakfast, the team inspanned the donkeys and prepared for the journey.

Synset Examples

inspan the draft animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to outspan - to unharness or detach draught animals from a vehicle

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'inspan', from Dutch 'inspannen' (to harness).
