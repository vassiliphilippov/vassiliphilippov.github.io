precipitant

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tənt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tənt / (BrE)
A substance or agent that causes a solid (precipitate) to form from a solution during a chemical reaction.
substance causing a precipitate (chemistry)
medical

Examples

When silver nitrate is added to the solution, it acts as a precipitant for chloride ions.
The lab technician carefully chose the correct precipitant to isolate the desired compound.
Changing the pH of the mixture can determine which precipitant is most effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precipitate - Solid substance that forms in a solution as a result of a reaction

Etymology

From Latin praecipitans, present participle of praecipitare (to throw headlong), related to 'precipitate'.

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪp.ɪtənt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪtənt / (BrE)
A factor or agent that directly causes or triggers a particular event, especially a chemical reaction or a sudden change.
direct trigger or cause for an event

Examples

The scientist identified the precipitant of the chemical reaction as an unexpected contaminant in the solution.
In economic crises, a sudden policy change can serve as a precipitant for unexpected market turmoil.
The argument acted as the precipitant for months of tension finally erupting among the team members.

Synset Examples

A sudden change acted as a precipitant for the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed
  • Synonym: trigger - an event or thing that causes another event to happen

Etymology

From Latin praeceipitans (falling headlong), from praeceipitare, to throw headlong.
