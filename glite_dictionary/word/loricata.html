Loricata

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔːrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌlɒrɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a former scientific name for the order of reptiles now called Crocodylia, which includes crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials
former scientific term for crocodile order

Examples

In the 19th century, zoologists often used Loricata to refer to crocodilian reptiles.
Recent classifications have replaced Loricata with the modern term Crocodylia.
Some old scientific texts list alligators and crocodiles under the order Loricata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crocodylia - the order of reptiles comprising crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials

Etymology

From Latin 'loricatus' meaning 'armored', in reference to the reptiles' bony skin.
