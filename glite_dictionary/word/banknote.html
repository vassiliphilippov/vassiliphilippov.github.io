banknote

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkˌnəʊt / (BrE)
A piece of paper currency, especially one issued by a central bank and used as legal tender.
piece of paper money

Examples

He handed the cashier a crisp banknote to pay for his groceries.
Tourists are often surprised by the colorful designs on each banknote.
The old banknote was no longer accepted after the government introduced a new series.

Synset Examples

he peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - paper money, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + 'note' (written promise to pay).
