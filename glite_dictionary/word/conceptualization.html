conceptualization

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛptʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɛptjʊəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A concept or idea that has been thoughtfully developed or formulated; the process of forming such a concept.
the process or result of forming a concept

Examples

Her conceptualization of the project was both innovative and practical.
The artist's conceptualization of freedom was reflected in his abstract sculptures.
Clear conceptualization is essential before implementing any new business strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conception - the forming or devising of a concept or idea
  • Synonym: idea - a thought or suggestion as to a possible course of action

Etymology

From concept + -ual + -ization; modeled after French conceptualisation.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛptʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɛptjʊəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming an idea, concept, or explanation in the mind; the act of formulating something mentally
forming a concept or idea

Examples

The conceptualization of the new marketing strategy took several weeks of brainstorming.
Her conceptualization of the novel's main character was both original and compelling.
Scientific progress often begins with the conceptualization of hypotheses that can be tested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conception - the forming or devising of a concept or idea
  • Antonym: realization - the making of something real or concrete

Etymology

From concept + -ualization, based on Latin conceptus (something conceived) and English -alization (forming nouns conveying action or process).
