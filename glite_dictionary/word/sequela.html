sequela

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkwilə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwiːlə / (BrE)
A medical condition that is the consequence of a previous disease, injury, or treatment.
medical complication following disease or injury
medical

Examples

In some cases, chronic pain becomes a sequela of nerve injury.
Scar tissue can often be seen as a sequela of surgery.
Doctors monitor patients for any long-term sequela after hospitalization for infection.

Synset Examples

paralysis is one of the sequelae of poliomyelitis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complication - a secondary disease or condition developing in the course of a primary disease

Etymology

From Latin sequela (that which follows), from sequi (to follow).
