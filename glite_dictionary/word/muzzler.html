muzzler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌz.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌz.lə / (BrE)
a person who puts a muzzle on an animal to prevent it from biting or making noise
person who muzzles animals

Examples

The experienced muzzler made sure every dog was safely fitted before the demonstration.
As a muzzler, he was responsible for controlling aggressive animals at the shelter.
The zoo recruited a muzzler to help with the particularly feisty wolves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handler - a person who trains or takes care of animals
  • Antonym: unmuzzler - someone who removes a muzzle from an animal

Etymology

From 'muzzle' (to put a muzzle on) + agent suffix '-er'.
