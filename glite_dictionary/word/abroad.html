abroad

1. ADV

/ əˈbrɔd / (AmE), / əˈbrɔːd / (BrE)
in or to a foreign country; outside one's own country.
in or to a foreign country

Examples

She has always dreamed of living abroad for a few years.
Many students choose to study abroad to experience new cultures.
After graduation, he found a job abroad in Germany.

Synset Examples

they had never travelled abroad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseas - in or to a foreign country, especially across the sea

Etymology

From Middle English 'abrode', from Old English 'on brād' (in breadth, at large).

2. ADV

/ əˈbrɔd / (AmE), / əˈbrɔːd / (BrE)
in or to a foreign country, especially one across the sea or far from home.
in or to a foreign country

Examples

After graduating, she decided to study abroad for a year in France.
Many students dream of traveling abroad during their vacations.
He worked abroad for several years before returning to his hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseas - in or to a foreign country across the sea

Etymology

From Middle English 'abrode', from Old English 'on brede' (in the open, abroad).

3. ADV

/ əˈbrɔd / (AmE), / əˈbrɔːd / (BrE)
in or to a foreign country or countries
in a foreign country

Examples

After graduation, she decided to study abroad for a year in France.
Many businesses are expanding their operations abroad to reach new markets.
He called his family while traveling abroad on business.

Synset Examples

markets abroad
overseas markets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseas - in or to a foreign country, especially across the sea
  • Antonym: domestically - in or within one's own country

Etymology

From Middle English abrood, from Old English on brōd (in the open, abroad).
