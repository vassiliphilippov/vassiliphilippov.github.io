Thujopsis

1. NOUN

/ θuːˈdʒɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / θjuːˈdʒɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A monotypic genus of coniferous trees closely related to Thuja, consisting of a single species native to Japan.
genus of conifers related to Thuja

Examples

The Thujopsis is often cultivated in parks and gardens for its attractive foliage.
Researchers compared the leaf structure of Thujopsis with other members of the cypress family.
Native to Japan, Thujopsis is prized for its durable wood and unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thuja - genus of conifers called arborvitae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Thuja' (a related genus) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).
