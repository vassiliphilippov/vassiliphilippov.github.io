ramsons

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmzənz / (AmE), / ˈræmzənz / (BrE)
a wild Eurasian plant (Allium ursinum) of the onion family with broad leaves and a strong garlic smell, often found in damp woods.
wild edible plant with garlic smell

Examples

The woods were carpeted with ramsons in spring, their pungent scent filling the air.
She gathered some fresh ramsons to use in her salad.
You can identify ramsons by their broad, green leaves and strong garlicky aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild garlic - another name for Allium ursinum, especially in British English
  • Degree Lower: chives - a related edible plant in the Allium genus

Etymology

From Old English *hramsa*, related to several Germanic languages, referring to wild garlic.
