southward

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊθwɚd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθwəd / (BrE)
In the direction toward the south.
toward the south

Examples

The birds began to fly southward as winter approached.
After crossing the river, the hikers continued southward along the trail.
The storm is moving southward, bringing heavy rain to the coastal towns.

Synset Examples

the ship turned southerly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northward - toward the north
  • Synonym: southerly - toward the south

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊθwərd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθwəd / (BrE)
the direction or point on the compass lying directly opposite north; at 180 degrees.
direction on compass; 180 degrees

Examples

They traveled steadily southward until they reached the coastline.
The compass needle points toward southward when aligned with the Earth's magnetic field.
Explorers set out for the distant southward in hopes of discovering new lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: south - the direction toward the point of the horizon 180° clockwise from east; opposite north
  • Antonym: northward - the direction or point on the compass at 0 degrees, directly opposite south

Etymology

From Old English 'sūðweard', meaning 'toward the south'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊθwɚd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθwəd / (BrE)
toward or moving in the direction of the south
in the direction of the south

Examples

The birds began their southward migration as the weather grew colder.
They set off on a southward journey through the mountains.
A strong wind pushed the clouds southward across the sky.

Synset Examples

a southbound train

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northward - toward or moving in the direction of the north
  • Synonym: southbound - heading toward the south, especially of vehicles or travel

Etymology

From Old English sūthweard, from 'sūth' (south) + 'weard' (toward).
