aeronautics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈnɔtɪks / (AmE), / ˌeərəˈnɔːtɪks / (BrE)
the science, theory, and practice of navigating or operating aircraft through the air or space
the science of flight and air navigation

Examples

She decided to study aeronautics at university because she was fascinated by aircraft.
The museum has a large section dedicated to the history of aeronautics.
Advances in aeronautics have made commercial air travel faster and safer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aviation - the design, development, and operation of aircraft

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'nautikos' (of ships, navigation).
