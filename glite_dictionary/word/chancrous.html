chancrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃæŋkrəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃaŋkrəs / (BrE)
Having or relating to chancres (painful sores, especially those characteristic of syphilis or similar diseases).
having or relating to chancres
medical

Examples

The patient presented with chancrous sores in the genital region, indicating a possible syphilitic infection.
The chancrous lesions were treated promptly to prevent further complications.
A distinct feature of the disease is the development of chancrous ulcers on the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancre - a painless ulcer, especially one developed as a result of syphilis infection

Etymology

From French 'chancreux' (chancrous), from 'chancre' (chancre, ulcer).
