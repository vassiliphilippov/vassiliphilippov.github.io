synchronal

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈkroʊ.nəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkrəʊ.nəl / (BrE)
occurring or existing at the same time, or having the same period or phase
occurring at the same time or phase

Examples

The flashing of the two lights was perfectly synchronal, creating a mesmerizing effect.
In some plants, the blooming of flowers is synchronal within the population, maximizing pollination.
The researchers noted a synchronal occurrence of symptoms among the patients during the outbreak.

recovery was synchronous with therapy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronous - occurring or existing at the same time or rate
  • Antonym: asynchronous - not occurring at the same time or rate

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), from 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
