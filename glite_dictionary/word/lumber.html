lumber

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
(informal, US) A baseball bat, used by the batter to hit the ball.
baseball bat (slang)
informal american

Examples

He stepped up to the plate, gripping his trusty lumber tightly.
After cracking the ball out of the park, he tipped his lumber to the cheering crowd.
Make sure you choose the right lumber before your next at-bat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - an implement with a handle and a solid surface, used in sports for hitting balls

2. VERB

/ ˈlʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
to move in a slow, heavy, or awkward way
move slowly and clumsily

Examples

The bear lumbered through the dense forest, searching for food.
She lumbered up the stairs with bags full of groceries in both hands.
After hours of hiking, he lumbered back to camp, exhausted but satisfied.

Synset Examples

The heavy man lumbered across the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plod - to walk heavily or slowly; to trudge
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps

Etymology

Probably from earlier lumber meaning 'to move in a cumbersome way', originally from lumber (noun), possibly from Lombard, referring to Lombard bankers who were associated with pawn shops full of bulky items.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01929501-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈlʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
to cut trees and prepare them as timber
cut trees into timber

Examples

Many companies lumber the forests to supply wood for construction.
The team lumbered several acres of pine last year.
They were fined for illegally lumbering protected trees in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: log - to cut down trees for timber

Etymology

From lumber (noun), originally referring to disused furniture, later to timber; likely from Lombard (Italian immigrants known for pawn shops); verb sense developed in North America.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01261392-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
wood that has been cut and prepared for use in building or carpentry
wood prepared for building

Examples

The new house was constructed from high-quality lumber shipped from the Pacific Northwest.
Prices for lumber have increased due to high demand and limited supply.
He stacked piles of lumber in the shed to use for future home repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timber - wood that is used for building, especially when still in the form of trees or large pieces

Etymology

From Middle English 'lomere', probably from Lombard, referring to Italian merchants who imported timber.

Source: WordNet
