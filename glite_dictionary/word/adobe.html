adobe

1. NOUN

/ əˈdoʊbi / (AmE), / əˈdəʊbi / (BrE)
A brick made from sun-dried earth and straw, commonly used as a building material in hot, dry climates.
sun-dried brick used for building

Examples

Traditional homes in the region are constructed from adobe to keep the interiors cool.
The thick adobe walls provided excellent insulation against the scorching sun.
Many historic buildings in New Mexico are made entirely of adobe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - natural earth material used for bricks and pottery

Etymology

From Spanish 'adobe', from Arabic 'aṭ-ṭūb' (the brick).

2. NOUN

/ əˈdoʊbi / (AmE), / əˈdəʊbi / (BrE)
A mixture of clay, sand, and sometimes organic material used to make sun-dried bricks for building.
clay mixture for bricks

Examples

The builders mixed adobe with straw to create sturdy bricks.
Traditional homes in the region rely on locally sourced adobe for their construction.
Adobe has excellent thermal properties, keeping houses cool in the summer and warm in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brick - a rectangular block used in building

Etymology

From Spanish 'adobe', from Arabic 'aṭ-ṭūb' (the brick).
