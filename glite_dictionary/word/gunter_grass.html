Gunter Grass

1. PROPN

/ ˈgʊn.tər ɡrɑːs / (AmE), / ˈɡʊn.tə ɡras / (BrE)
(1927–2015) A German novelist, poet, and playwright best known for his novel 'The Tin Drum', and recipient of the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1999.
German novelist and Nobel laureate

Examples

Gunter Grass won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1999 for his profound impact on German literature.
Many critics consider Gunter Grass's 'The Tin Drum' a masterpiece of postwar European fiction.
Gunter Grass was also known for his political activism and outspoken social commentary.
