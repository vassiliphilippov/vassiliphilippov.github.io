muckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌkəl / (BrE)
(chiefly Scottish and Northern English) a large amount, number, or extent of something.
large amount or extent (chiefly Scottish/Northern English)
british

Examples

There was a muckle of work left to do after the storm.
She inherited a muckle of money from her grandmother.
They found a muckle of problems when they inspected the old house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: much - a large amount or quantity
  • Antonym: little - a small amount or quantity

Etymology

From Middle English *mikel*, *mikill* (great, much), from Old Norse *mikill* (great, large).
