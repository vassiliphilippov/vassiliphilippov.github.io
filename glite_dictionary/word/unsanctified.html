unsanctified

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsæŋktəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaŋktɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not made holy or sacred; not consecrated or regarded as holy
not made holy; unconsecrated

Examples

The old temple stood unsanctified after years of abandonment and neglect.
He believed that only the unsanctified altar could be used for their rituals.
Entering the unsanctified ground was strictly forbidden by the monks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sanctified - made holy or consecrated

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'sanctified', from Latin 'sanctificare' (to make holy).
