Yucca baccata

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə bəˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə bəˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a tall species of yucca native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with a woody stem, stiff sword-shaped leaves, and large clusters of white flowers; also known as banana yucca
a species of yucca plant native to the Southwest US and Mexico

Examples

The Yucca baccata produces sweet, fleshy fruits that are eaten by both wildlife and indigenous people.
A cluster of Yucca baccata stands in bloom on the rocky desert hills.
Botanists study Yucca baccata for its unique adaptations to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana yucca - another common name for Yucca baccata

Etymology

From the genus name Yucca, and Latin 'baccata' meaning 'berry-bearing'.
