nonessential

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnɪˈsɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪˈsɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a thing that is not necessary or indispensable
something not necessary

Examples

After reviewing the budget, management decided to eliminate several nonessentials to cut costs.
She packed only the essentials for her trip, leaving behind all nonessentials.
During a crisis, it’s important to distinguish between what is critical and what are merely nonessentials.

Synset Examples

they discarded all their inessentials

Related Terms

  • Antonym: essential - a thing that is absolutely necessary
  • Synonym: inessential - something that is not essential; unimportant

Etymology

From non- (not) + essential, from Latin essentia (essence).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.ɪˈsɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.ɪˈsɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
Not absolutely necessary; not of vital or central importance.
not absolutely necessary

Examples

Access to the internet is nonessential for this job, but it can be helpful.
Many nonessential expenses were cut during the company's restructuring.
Attendance at the meeting is nonessential, so feel free to skip it if you are busy.

Synset Examples

nonessential to the integral meanings of poetry
the character's motives remain accidental to the plot

Related Terms

  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; of the most importance

Etymology

From Latin 'non' (not) + 'essentialis' (of the essence).

Synset POS: s
