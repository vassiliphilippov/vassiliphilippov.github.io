orphanage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹ.fən.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a public institution where children whose parents are deceased or unable to care for them are cared for and housed
public institution for orphans

Examples

After losing his parents, Tom was sent to an orphanage in the city.
The charity raises funds to support the local orphanage and its children.
She volunteered at an orphanage during the summer, helping care for the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: children's home - residential institution for children without parental care
  • Antonym: foster home - a private home offering care for children unable to live with their parents

Etymology

From Medieval Latin orphanatus (state of being an orphan), from Late Latin orphanus (orphan).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.fən.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
The state or condition of being an orphan; the experience of having no living parents.
state of being an orphan

Examples

His early orphanage had a lasting impact on his outlook on life.
Many authors have written about the pain of orphanage and loss.
The trauma of orphanage shaped how she related to others in adulthood.

Synset Examples

his early orphanage shaped his character as an adult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents are dead

Etymology

From French 'orphelinage', from 'orphelin' (orphan) + '-age' (denoting condition or state).
