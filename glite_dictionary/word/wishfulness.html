wishfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃ.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The state of feeling or expressing a strong desire for something unlikely to happen.
yearning for the unlikely

Examples

His wishfulness about becoming a famous actor kept him motivated but sometimes distracted from reality.
There was a certain wishfulness in her voice when she spoke of her childhood home.
The story is filled with wishfulness, as the characters hope for impossible things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longing - a strong feeling of wanting something or someone
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is

Etymology

From 'wishful' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the quality or state of being wishful.
