topical

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or arranged by subjects or topics rather than by chronological or other order.
arranged by subject or topic

Examples

The library catalog is organized in a topical manner, making it easy to find resources by subject.
Her report provided a topical overview of current environmental issues.
They created a topical index to help readers navigate the book more easily.

Synset Examples

a detailed record on both a chronological and a topical basis

Etymology

From Latin 'topica', from Greek 'topikos' (pertaining to a place or topic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02828278-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a subject, idea, or event) relating to current affairs or of immediate relevance or interest; concerned with topics of present or recent interest.
of current interest; relevant now

Examples

The debate was highly engaging because it addressed several topical issues facing society today.
She gave a presentation on the topical subject of renewable energy sources.
Many newspapers feature columns that discuss topical events and trends.

Synset Examples

a topical reference
a topical and timely study of civil liberty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timely - occurring at a suitable or opportune time; relevant
  • Synonym: relevant - closely connected with the subject being discussed
  • Antonym: outdated - no longer current or valid

Etymology

From Latin 'topica', from Greek 'topikos' meaning 'of a place, topical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00671276-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of a medicine or treatment) applied directly to a particular part of the surface of the body.
applied to the surface of the body
medical

Examples

The dermatologist prescribed a topical cream to be applied to the rash twice daily.
Some painkillers are available as topical gels for local relief.
The topical treatment should not be swallowed or applied to broken skin.

Synset Examples

a drug for topical (or local) application
a topical anesthesia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systemic - (of a drug or treatment) affecting the entire body rather than a single part

Etymology

From Late Latin topica (matters of place), from Greek topikos (pertaining to a place or topic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01428768-s
Synset POS: s
