to seduce

1. VERB

/ sɪˈdus / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to persuade someone to abandon duty, principles, or proper behavior, often by offering something attractive or tempting
entice away from duty or principles

Examples

The offer of a higher salary was enough to seduce him away from his long-standing job.
She feared that fame might seduce her into compromising her values.
He was seduced by the promise of instant success, neglecting his responsibilities.

Synset Examples

She was seduced by the temptation of easy money and started to work in a massage parlor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tempt - to try to persuade someone to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something

Etymology

From Latin seducere, meaning 'to lead aside or astray'; from se- (apart) + ducere (to lead).

2. VERB

/ sɪˈdus / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to persuade someone to have sex with you, often by charming or tempting them
persuade to have sex

Examples

In the novel, the main character attempts to seduce his coworker during a late-night meeting.
She claimed that he tried to seduce her with promises of adventure and romance.
Rumors spread that the celebrity had seduced several of his costars while filming.

Synset Examples

Harry finally seduced Sally
Did you score last night?
Harry made Sally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entice - to tempt or persuade someone, especially into doing something wrong or unwise
  • Synonym: to tempt - to attract someone to do something, often something unwise or immoral

Etymology

From Latin seducere, from se- (apart) + ducere (to lead).
