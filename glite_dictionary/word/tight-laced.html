tight-laced

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪtˌleɪst / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtˌleɪst / (BrE)
Having or showing very strict or excessive moral or social standards; exaggeratedly proper.
excessively proper or morally strict

Examples

Her tight-laced upbringing made it hard for her to accept unconventional ideas.
The school had a tight-laced dress code that allowed no room for self-expression.
Although he appeared tight-laced at first, he eventually revealed a sense of humor.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straitlaced - having excessively strict or proper morals
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing great freedom of behavior, not strict

Etymology

Originally referring to corsets laced tightly, metaphorically extended to characterize strictness in morals or behavior.
