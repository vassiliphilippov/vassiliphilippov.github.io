Melia azederach

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːliə əˌzɛdəˈræk / (AmE), / ˌmiːliə əˌzɛdəˈræk / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to northern India and China, with purple blossoms and small inedible yellow fruits, commonly grown as a shade tree in southern United States.
a tree with purple flowers and yellow fruits

Examples

The Melia azederach is often planted as an ornamental tree in gardens across the southern United States.
Researchers studied the medicinal uses of Melia azederach in traditional Chinese remedies.
The shade provided by the Melia azederach makes it a popular choice for urban landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinaberry - another common name for Melia azederach; the same tree

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'melia' (ash tree) and 'azedarach' from Persian 'āzād dirakht' (free tree).
