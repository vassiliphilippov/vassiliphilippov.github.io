to polymerize

1. VERB

/ pəˈlɪməˌraɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməraɪz / (BrE)
to cause a compound to form a polymer through a chemical reaction
cause to form a polymer (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Heat will polymerize the monomer solution into a solid plastic.
Chemists can polymerize different compounds to produce new materials with unique properties.
When exposed to light, the resin will polymerize quickly, forming a hard finish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depolymerize - to break down a polymer into its monomers

Etymology

From 'polymer' + '-ize', modeled on French 'polymériser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00267472-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pəˈlɪməˌraɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməraɪz / (BrE)
to undergo or cause to undergo polymerization, forming a polymer by combining smaller molecules (monomers) into larger, repetitive chains or networks
undergo or cause polymerization

Examples

Certain plastic materials polymerize when exposed to ultraviolet light.
If the solution does not polymerize properly, the final product will be weak.
Chemists can polymerize monomers to create new synthetic fibers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depolymerize - to break down a polymer into its monomer units; opposite of polymerize

Etymology

From 'polymer' + '-ize', influenced by French 'polymériser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00267083-v
Synset POS: v
