ratability

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌreɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being liable to assessment or taxation, especially for local government rates or taxes.
liability to assessment or taxation
legal

Examples

The ratability of the property was challenged by its owners in court.
Changes in government policy affected the ratability of many commercial premises.
Auditors reviewed the ratability of several accounts to ensure accurate tax reporting.

Etymology

From 'rate' (tax or assessment) + '-ability'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌreɪtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being capable of being rated, valued, or assessed.
capacity to be rated or assessed

Examples

The ratability of certain assets can be difficult to determine in volatile markets.
Tax authorities debated the ratability of the new development project for local levies.
A clear definition of ratability is crucial for fair property assessments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rateability - capacity to be rated or assessed (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From rate + -ability, via rate (to appraise, estimate) and the suffix -ability denoting capacity.
