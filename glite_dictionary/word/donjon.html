donjon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈdɒn.dʒən / (BrE)
the main tower of a medieval castle or fortress, often serving as a stronghold or keep
main tower of a medieval castle

Examples

The castle's donjon offered a commanding view of the surrounding countryside.
During the siege, the defenders retreated to the reinforced donjon.
Guided tours allow visitors to climb the ancient steps of the donjon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep - the strongest or central tower of a castle, used as a place of last defense

Etymology

From Old French *donjon* (great tower of a castle), of disputed origin, but possibly related to Latin *dominionem* (dominion, mastery).
