dot-com

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑt kɑm / (AmE), / ˈdɒt kɒm / (BrE)
relating to a business that operates mainly or entirely on the Internet, especially referring to those started during the late 1990s Internet boom.
relating to Internet companies

Examples

After the dot-com bust, many startups struggled to survive in a changed marketplace.
The city attracted countless dot-com entrepreneurs during the tech boom.
She works for a successful dot-com advertising agency.

Synset Examples

a dot-com outfit in San Francisco

Etymology

From 'dot', representing the period in website addresses, and 'com', short for 'commercial'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02858358-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ dɑt ˈkɑm / (AmE), / dɒt ˈkɒm / (BrE)
A company that conducts its main business activities on the internet, usually with a website whose address ends in '.com'.
company operating mainly on the internet

Examples

During the late 1990s, many dot-com startups attracted significant investment.
The dot-com era saw rapid growth in online retail businesses.
Several dot-com companies went bankrupt after the bubble burst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internet company - a company that provides products or services via the internet

Etymology

From 'dot' referring to the period in '.com', and 'com' as the top-level domain originally denoting commercial entities.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08018826-n
Synset POS: n
