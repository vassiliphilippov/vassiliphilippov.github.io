solemn

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑləm / (AmE), / ˈsɒləm / (BrE)
serious and sincere, showing no humor or lightness, especially when expressing beliefs or opinions
serious and sincere; without humor

Examples

She made a solemn vow to complete the project, no matter the obstacles.
The judge delivered the verdict in a solemn tone.
His solemn expression told me that he was absolutely convinced of his decision.

Synset Examples

both sides were deeply in earnest, even passionate
an entirely sincere and cruel tyrant
a film with a solemn social message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earnest - showing sincere and intense conviction
  • Synonym: serious - not joking or trifling; grave in manner or tone
  • Antonym: lighthearted - cheerful and not serious

Etymology

From Latin 'sollemnis' (customary, celebrated at a fixed date, religiously solemn).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑː.ləm / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.əm / (BrE)
serious, formal, and dignified in manner or appearance; showing earnestness or sincerity, especially in keeping promises or ceremonies
formal and serious in manner or promise

Examples

The president delivered a solemn speech addressing the nation after the tragedy.
He made a solemn vow to always tell the truth in court.
There was a solemn atmosphere in the church during the memorial service.

Synset Examples

a grave God-fearing man
a quiet sedate nature
as sober as a judge
a solemn promise
the judge was solemn as he pronounced sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serious - not joking or intended to be funny; thoughtful or earnest in manner
  • Synonym: grave - serious or dignified in manner or appearance
  • Antonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value; carefree and not serious
  • Antonym: jovial - cheerful, friendly, and good-humored in manner

Etymology

From Latin 'sollemnis' (customary, formal, solemn).
