Tajikistan

1. PROPN

/ təˈdʒiːkɪˌstæn / (AmE), / tæˈdʒiːkɪˌstæn / (BrE)
A landlocked mountainous country in Central Asia, north of Afghanistan, formerly a Soviet republic.
country in Central Asia

Examples

Many travelers are drawn to Tajikistan for its stunning Pamir Mountains and unique culture.
After the collapse of the Soviet Union, Tajikistan declared its independence in 1991.
Diplomatic relations between Tajikistan and neighboring countries have improved in recent years.

Etymology

From Tajik, the name of the main ethnic group, plus the Persian suffix -stan meaning 'land of'.

3. PROPN

/ tɑːˈdʒik.ɪ.stæn / (AmE), / tæˈdʒiː.kɪ.stæn / (BrE)
A landlocked mountainous country in Central Asia, north of Afghanistan and formerly a republic of the Soviet Union.
country in Central Asia

Examples

My friend traveled to Tajikistan last summer to go hiking in the Pamir Mountains.
The capital of Tajikistan is Dushanbe, which lies along the Varzob river.
After gaining independence in 1991, Tajikistan experienced a period of civil unrest.

Etymology

From Russian Таджикистан (Tadzhikistan), ultimately from Persian تاجیک‎ (Tājīk) +‎ -ستان‎ (stān, 'land').
