Pinus longaeva

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪnəs lɒŋˈɡiːvə / (AmE), / ˌpiːnəs lɒŋˈɡiːvə / (BrE)
a species of bristlecone pine native to the western United States, noted for its great age and longevity, with twisted shape and short needles in bundles of five
a very long-lived pine tree species

Examples

The Pinus longaeva can live for thousands of years in harsh mountain climates.
Scientists study Pinus longaeva to understand ancient environmental conditions.
The oldest known non-clonal organism is a specimen of Pinus longaeva in the White Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristlecone pine - a common name for several pine species known for their longevity

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' (pine) + 'longaeva' (long-lived).
