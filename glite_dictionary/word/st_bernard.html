St Bernard

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt bərˈnɑrd / (AmE), / seɪnt bəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a large, powerful breed of Swiss alpine dog with a thick coat, traditionally used for rescue in mountainous regions
Swiss breed of large alpine rescue dog

Examples

The famous St Bernard is known for its strength and rescue abilities in the snowy Alps.
They adopted a gentle St Bernard from the local animal shelter last year.
A St Bernard once carried supplies to stranded travelers crossing the mountain pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newfoundland - large working dog breed also known for water rescues
  • Degree Lower: collie - another breed of herding dog, similar in working role

Etymology

Named after the Great St Bernard Pass in the Swiss Alps, where the breed was used for rescue work.
