to materialize

1. VERB

/ məˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become real or actual; to happen or take place as planned or hoped for
become real; happen in reality

Examples

After years of hard work, his plans finally materialized.
The solution did not materialize as quickly as we had hoped.
Her fears materialized when the rumors turned out to be true.

Synset Examples

Her dream really materialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifest - to appear or become visible; to show clearly
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly or cease to exist

Etymology

From French 'matérialiser', from 'matériel' (material).
