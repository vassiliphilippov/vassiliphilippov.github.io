undue

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuː / (BrE)
not justified or authorized; more than what is reasonable or necessary.
not justified; excessive or unwarranted

Examples

The committee found that there was undue pressure placed on the employees to finish the project early.
He was accused of taking undue advantage of his position for personal gain.
Parents should avoid showing undue concern over minor mistakes their children make.

Synset Examples

desire for undue private profit
unwarranted limitations of personal freedom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwarranted - not justified or authorized
  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason; warranted

Etymology

From Latin 'indebitus', via Old French 'undiu'; originally meaning 'not owed'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01951967-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuː / (BrE)
more than is necessary, appropriate, or allowed in a particular situation; excessive or improper.
excessive, not appropriate in the situation

Examples

The manager faced criticism for his undue interference in staff decisions.
She was under undue stress during the final weeks of the project.
The law protects employees from undue pressure to resign.

Synset Examples

undue influence
I didn't want to show undue excitement
accused of using undue force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'undue', from Latin 'indebitus' (‘not owed’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00139211-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈduː / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuː / (BrE)
more than is proper, necessary, or reasonable; excessive or unwarranted
excessive; unwarranted

Examples

The judge cautioned against placing undue emphasis on one piece of evidence.
He left the meeting early to avoid undue stress from a heated debate.
Please do not give undue importance to minor errors in the report.

Synset Examples

excessive charges
a book of inordinate length
his dress stops just short of undue elegance
unreasonable demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: reasonable - fair and sensible; not excessive

Etymology

From Old French 'undue', from Latin 'indebitus' (not owed, unwarranted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01538069-s
Synset POS: s
