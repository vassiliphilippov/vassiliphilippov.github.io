photosynthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊtoʊsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving photosynthesis, especially in reference to organisms or processes that produce energy through the conversion of light.
relating to photosynthesis

Examples

Most plants are photosynthetic and use sunlight to produce their own food.
Algae play an important role as photosynthetic organisms in aquatic ecosystems.
Researchers studied the photosynthetic efficiency of various bacteria under different light conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photosynthesis - the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize food

Etymology

From photo- (light) + synthetic, ultimately from Greek phōs (light) and suntithenai (to put together).
