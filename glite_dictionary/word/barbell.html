barbell

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːˌbɛl / (BrE)
A long metal bar with removable weighted discs at each end, used for weightlifting and exercise.
weightlifting bar with discs

Examples

He added more weight to the barbell before starting his next set.
The gym has several barbells in different sizes for various exercises.
She lifted the heavy barbell over her head during the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumbbell - a short bar with weights at each end, held in one hand for exercise

Etymology

Formed from 'bar' and 'bell', possibly by analogy to 'dumbbell'.
