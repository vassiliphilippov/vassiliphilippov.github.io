Lapland

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæpˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈlæp.lənd / (BrE)
a region in the far north of Europe, largely within the Arctic Circle, traditionally inhabited by the Sami people (formerly called Lapps), covering parts of Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia.
region in northern Europe, home of the Sami

Examples

Every winter, tourists travel to Lapland to see the northern lights and experience life above the Arctic Circle.
The traditional dress and customs of the Sami people are an important part of Lapland's cultural heritage.
Reindeer herding remains a vital part of daily life for many inhabitants of Lapland.

Etymology

From Swedish Lappland, meaning 'land of the Lapps' (now 'Sami').
