posit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.zɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪt / (BrE)
(logic) A proposition that is assumed to be true for the sake of logical reasoning or argument.
assumed proposition in logic

Examples

The scientist introduced a new posit to explain the unexplained phenomena.
Every argument begins with at least one posit used as a foundation for reasoning.
In philosophical discourse, a posit does not need empirical evidence to be considered in discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - self-evident truth used as a starting point in reasoning
  • Synonym: postulate - a thing suggested or assumed as true for reasoning or discussion

Etymology

From Latin positum (thing placed), past participle of ponere (to place, to put).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to assume or suggest something as a fact or principle, especially as a basis for reasoning or argument
assume or suggest as a fact or principle

Examples

Many philosophers posit the existence of a universal moral law.
The scientist posited that life could exist on other planets.
Some theories posit a close relationship between language and thought.

Synset Examples

He posited three basic laws of nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - suppose something to be the case without proof
  • Synonym: postulate - suggest or assume the existence or truth of something as a basis for reasoning
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true, the opposite of posit

Etymology

From Latin 'positus', past participle of 'ponere' (to place, to put).

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put forward or assume as a fact or principle; to suggest or propose (an idea, theory, or argument) for consideration
to put forward as a fact or theory

Examples

The philosopher posited that all knowledge begins with experience.
Some scientists posit the existence of parallel universes.
He posited an interesting theory about language development in children.

Synset Examples

I submit to you that the accused is guilty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propose - to put forward (a plan or suggestion)
  • Synonym: to assume - to take for granted or suppose

Etymology

From Latin *positus*, past participle of *ponere* (to place, put).
