curdled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝːd.əld / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.dld / (BrE)
Transformed from a liquid into a soft semisolid or solid mass, especially due to a chemical or physical process such as souring or coagulation.
turned into a semisolid or solid mass

Examples

After being left out overnight, the milk had curdled and was no longer safe to drink.
His blood ran curdled with fear as he heard the eerie sound.
The sauce curdled because I added lemon juice too quickly.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood
curdled milk
grumous blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulated - changed from a fluid into a thickened mass

Etymology

From 'curdle,' related to 'curd,' of unknown origin, possibly linked to Middle English.
