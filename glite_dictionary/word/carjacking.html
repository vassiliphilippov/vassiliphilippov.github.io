carjacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrˌdʒækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdʒækɪŋ / (BrE)
the crime of violently stealing a car while its driver or passengers are still in it
violent theft of an occupied car

Examples

The police are investigating a carjacking that happened downtown last night.
Reports of carjacking have increased in several cities over the past year.
She fought back during the attempted carjacking and managed to escape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: car theft - the act of stealing a car, regardless of its occupancy

Etymology

Formed from 'car' + 'jacking' (from 'hijacking'), first recorded in the 1990s.
