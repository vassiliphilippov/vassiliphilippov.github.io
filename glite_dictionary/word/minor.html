minor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who is under the age of full legal responsibility or adulthood
a person under legal age

Examples

It is illegal to sell alcohol to a minor in most countries.
The movie is not suitable for anyone considered a minor by law.
She adopted a policy to protect the rights of every minor in her care.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - a person who is fully grown or developed

Etymology

From Latin 'minor' (smaller, younger).

9. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
(of a musical scale or key) characterized by a specific pattern of intervals with a minor third above the tonic, giving a generally somber or melancholic sound.
(music) of a scale or key with a minor third; somber tone

Examples

Many classical pieces are written in minor keys to create a more emotional atmosphere.
The song shifts from minor to major, changing its entire mood.
She found the haunting sound of the minor scale especially beautiful on the piano.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major - (of a scale or key) having a major third above the tonic, producing a bright or happy sound

Etymology

From Latin minor (smaller, lesser). In music, first used in the 17th century to distinguish minor intervals from major.
