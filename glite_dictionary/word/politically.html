politically

1. ADV

/ pəˈlɪtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to politics or social relationships involving authority.
in a political manner

Examples

The new tax law is being discussed politically as well as economically.
He is politically active and attends many rallies.
Her decision was influenced politically rather than personally.

Synset Examples

politically correct clothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political - relating to the government or public affairs
  • Antonym: apolitically - in a manner that is not political

Etymology

Formed from 'political' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix); from Greek 'politikós' (of citizens or the state).

2. ADV

/ pəˈlɪtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to government, politics, or public affairs.
in relation to politics or government

Examples

The country became politically unstable after the resignation of its leader.
She remains politically active despite her busy schedule.
The organization is politically independent and accepts members from all parties.

Synset Examples

politically organized units

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political - relating to government or public affairs

Etymology

From 'political' + '-ly', where 'political' is from Greek 'politikós' (of citizens or the state).
