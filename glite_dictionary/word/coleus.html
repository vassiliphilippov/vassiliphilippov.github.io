Coleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World tropical plants cultivated primarily for their brightly colored and variegated leaves, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of ornamental tropical plants

Examples

Many gardeners love the Coleus for its vivid, patterned foliage and ease of care.
A new variety of Coleus was introduced at the flower show this year.
She planted several Coleus in the shade to brighten up the garden borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plectranthus - a related genus of aromatic, ornamental plants
  • Synonym: Solenostemon - a former genus now included under Plectranthus and Coleus

Etymology

From Greek koleos (sheath), referring to the shape of some floral parts.
