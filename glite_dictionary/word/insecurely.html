insecurely

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə.li / (BrE)
in a tentative or self-conscious way, lacking in confidence or assurance
in a tentative or self-conscious way

Examples

She spoke insecurely during the interview, barely meeting the panel's eyes.
He walked insecurely onto the stage, his nerves visible in every movement.
Whenever she's uncertain, she tends to respond insecurely and avoids making decisions.

Synset Examples

she always acts very insecurely in the presence of her father

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confidently - in a self-assured, assertive manner

Etymology

From 'insecure' + '-ly', where 'insecure' is from Latin 'insecurus' (unsafe, insecure).

2. ADV

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə.li / (BrE)
in a way that is uncertain, unsafe, or likely to fail; with a risk of not being secure or stable
in an uncertain or unsafe manner

Examples

Several old bridges are insecurely fastened and may need immediate repairs.
She balanced the stack of books insecurely on one arm as she opened the door.
Their jobs are insecurely funded by a temporary government grant.

Synset Examples

our positions here at the university are rather insecurely supported by grant money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: securely - in a safe or stable manner
