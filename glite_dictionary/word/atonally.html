atonally

1. ADV

/ əˈtoʊnəli / (AmE), / əˈtəʊnəli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks a tonal center or key; without tonality, especially in music.
without a tonal center (music)

Examples

The composer chose to write this piece atonally, challenging listeners' expectations.
She improvised atonally, creating a jarring and unpredictable musical landscape.
Some critics argue that music performed atonally can evoke more complex emotions than traditional harmony.

Synset Examples

he composes atonally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tonally - in relation to a tonal center or key

Etymology

From "atonal" (from a- 'without' + tonal) + -ly.
