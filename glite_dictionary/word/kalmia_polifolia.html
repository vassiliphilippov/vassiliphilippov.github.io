Kalmia polifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.mi.ə pəˈlaɪ.fə.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈkal.mɪə pəˈlɪ.fə.lɪə / (BrE)
A low, evergreen shrub native to bogs of northwestern North America, with small purple flowers and pale, glaucous-backed leaves.
a bog-dwelling evergreen shrub with purple flowers

Examples

The wetland was covered in Kalmia polifolia, its small purple flowers brightening the boggy landscape.
Botanists surveyed the spread of Kalmia polifolia in the reserve last spring.
In acidic soil, Kalmia polifolia thrived alongside sphagnum moss and cranberries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bog laurel - common name for Kalmia polifolia

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Kalmia (after Peter Kalm, Swedish botanist) + species polifolia (from Latin, resembling Polium, an ancient plant name).
