worm-eaten

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɝmˌiːtən / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmˌiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(of an object) damaged or infested by worms or insects, especially to the extent that its structure is compromised or deteriorated.
damaged by worms or insects

Examples

The old attic was full of worm-eaten furniture and forgotten memories.
She shuddered at the sight of the worm-eaten wood in the abandoned house.
Several worm-eaten pages fell from the crumbling book as I opened it.

Synset Examples

He discarded the worm-eaten apples from the basket.
The old book was worm-eaten and barely legible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decayed - having undergone decomposition or rot
  • Antonym: sound - not damaged or decayed; in good condition
