Eretmochelys imbricata

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɛtməˈkiːlɪs ˌɪmbrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / əˌrɛtməʊˈkiːlɪs ˌɪmbrɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical sea turtle with a hawk-like beak, known for its beautiful shell used to make tortoiseshell and listed as critically endangered.
species of tropical sea turtle

Examples

The Eretmochelys imbricata is critically endangered due to the high demand for its beautiful shell.
Marine biologists study Eretmochelys imbricata to better understand threats to biodiversity in coral reefs.
Illegal trafficking of Eretmochelys imbricata remains a serious issue for wildlife conservation organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawksbill turtle - common name for Eretmochelys imbricata

Etymology

From Greek 'eretmos' (oar) and 'chelys' (turtle), and Latin 'imbricata' (overlapping, like tiles), referring to the structure of its shell.
