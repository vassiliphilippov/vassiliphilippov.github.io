bonding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
(Dentistry) A technique for repairing or restoring a tooth, in which a resin material is applied to the surface and adheres to the enamel.
tooth repair technique using resin
medical

Examples

My dentist recommended bonding to fix the chipped tooth on my front row.
After the bonding procedure, her smile looked completely natural.
Many minor tooth imperfections can be corrected with dental bonding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
the development of a close emotional relationship between people, especially family members or close friends
formation of close emotional relationships

Examples

The retreat was designed to encourage team bonding among employees.
Many parents feel a deep sense of bonding with their newborn.
Spending time together outdoors strengthened the bonding between the siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attachment - a strong emotional connection between people
  • Antonym: alienation - the state of being isolated or estranged from others

Etymology

Derived from 'bond' (Middle English, from Old Norse 'band' meaning 'something that binds').

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or act of fastening or joining things firmly together
fastening or joining things together

Examples

Proper bonding ensures the tiles stay in place for many years.
The bonding of the two metals requires high heat and pressure.
Poor bonding can lead to the parts breaking apart easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joining - the act of connecting or uniting things
  • Antonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From the verb 'bond' (to join), itself from Middle English, from Old Norse 'band' (a tie).
