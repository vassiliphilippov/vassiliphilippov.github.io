shoed

1. ADJ

/ ʃud / (AmE), / ʃuːd / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or thing) wearing shoes or equipped with a covering for the feet, especially referring to horses fitted with horseshoes.
wearing shoes or fitted with foot coverings

Examples

The horses were freshly shoed before the long journey.
He entered the room, still shoed and covered in mud from outside.
Most city dwellers are seldom seen unshoed in public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shod - wearing shoes or fitted with horseshoes; alternative past participle and adjective
  • Antonym: barefoot - without any covering or protection for the feet

Etymology

From the verb 'shoe' + -ed, meaning fitted with shoes or horseshoes.
