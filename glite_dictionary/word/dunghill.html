dunghill

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌŋˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋˌhɪl / (BrE)
A heap or pile of dung or refuse, especially one found on a farm.
heap of dung or refuse

Examples

The farmer added fresh straw to the dunghill behind the barn.
The smell from the dunghill drifted across the fields in the summer heat.
Chickens often scratched for insects at the edge of the old dunghill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure pile - a heap of manure, usually for fertilizing fields
  • Synonym: compost heap - a pile where organic waste is left to decompose for use as fertilizer

Etymology

From Middle English ‘dunghill’, combining ‘dung’ (animal excrement) and ‘hill’ (mound or heap).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌŋˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋhɪl / (BrE)
A state or condition regarded as being dirty, degraded, or contemptible.
foul or degraded condition

Examples

After years of corruption, the town's government was nothing but a dunghill of greed and incompetence.
Some critics view the city's neglected neighborhoods as a dunghill that urgently needs renewal.
He rose from the dunghill of poverty to build a better life for his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filth - extreme dirt or corruption; foulness
  • Synonym: squalor - a state of being extremely dirty and unpleasant

Etymology

From Middle English *dunghil*, from *dung* + *hill*, originally denoting a heap of manure.
