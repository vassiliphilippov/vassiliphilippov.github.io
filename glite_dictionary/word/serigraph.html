serigraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A print produced by forcing ink onto paper or another surface through a screen with areas blocked off by a stencil, commonly known as screen printing or silkscreen printing.
print made by screen printing

Examples

The gallery exhibited several colorful serigraphs by contemporary artists.
He purchased a limited edition serigraph signed by the painter.
Creating a serigraph requires skill in both design and technique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen print - print made by forcing ink through a prepared screen
  • Synonym: silkscreen - type of screen printing process

Etymology

From Latin 'seri-' (silk) + Greek 'graphē' (writing, drawing).
