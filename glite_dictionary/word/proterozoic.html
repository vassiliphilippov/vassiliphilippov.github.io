Proterozoic

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəroʊˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtərəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
the geological eon that lasted from about 2,500 to 544 million years ago, marked by the emergence of oxygen in the atmosphere and early multicellular life
geological eon; 2,500–544 million years ago

Examples

The Proterozoic eon witnessed significant changes in Earth's atmosphere.
Many of Earth's oldest rocks date back to the Proterozoic period.
During the Proterozoic, early forms of multicellular life began to evolve.

Etymology

From Greek proteros (earlier) + zoion (animal), referring to life preceding the Phanerozoic eon.

2. ADJ

/ ˌproʊ.t̬ə.rəˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.tə.rəˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the later of the two divisions of the Precambrian eon, following the Archaean and preceding the Cambrian period, characterized by the appearance of more complex life forms.
relating to the later part of the Precambrian eon

Examples

The Proterozoic eon saw the emergence of multicellular organisms.
Geologists found rocks that date back to the Proterozoic era.
Many important mineral deposits were formed during the Proterozoic period.

Synset Examples

proterozoic life forms

Etymology

From Greek proteros (former, earlier) + zōē (life), referring to 'earlier life'.
