batholithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbæθəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌbæθəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a batholith, a large mass of intrusive igneous rock
relating to a batholith (igneous rock mass)

Examples

The mountain range has a distinct batholithic structure visible in its granite outcrops.
Geologists studied the batholithic formations to understand the region's tectonic history.
A batholithic intrusion can dramatically alter the landscape over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batholith - a very large mass of igneous rock

Etymology

From 'batholith' plus the suffix -ic; 'batholith' from Greek 'bathos' (depth) and 'lithos' (stone).
