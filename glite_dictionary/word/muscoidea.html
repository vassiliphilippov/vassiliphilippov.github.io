Muscoidea

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkoʊ.iːdə / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
a superfamily of two-winged flies, including families such as Muscidae, Gasterophilidae, Calliphoridae, and Tachinidae
superfamily of two-winged flies

Examples

The Muscoidea superfamily includes several families of flies found worldwide.
Researchers are studying the diversity of Muscoidea in agricultural ecosystems.
Many Muscoidea species play important roles in decomposing organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - an insect of the order Diptera, with a single pair of wings

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'musca' (fly) + Greek '-oeidēs' (like, form of).

2. NOUN

/ mɐˈskoʊɪdiə / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of two-winged flies that includes families such as Muscidae, Gasterophilidae, Calliphoridae, and Tachinidae.
superfamily of two-winged flies

Examples

The Muscoidea is a superfamily that includes many common house flies and blowflies.
Researchers have been studying the evolutionary relationships within the Muscoidea.
Parasitic flies that affect livestock often belong to the Muscoidea superfamily.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Musca' (a genus of flies) + '-oidea' (indicating resemblance or relationship).
