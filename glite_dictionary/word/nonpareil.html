nonpareil

1. NOUN

/ nɑn.pəˈrɛl / (AmE), / nɒn.pəˈrɛl / (BrE)
A small, flat chocolate disk coated with small, round beads of colored sugar.
chocolate disk with sugar beads

Examples

She handed me a bag of nonpareils as a sweet afternoon treat.
The bakery decorated the cupcakes with colorful nonpareils for the holiday party.
Kids love to eat nonpareil candies at the movies.

Etymology

From French *nonpareil* (unequaled, peerless), originally meaning something unrivaled.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.pəˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.pəˈrɛl / (BrE)
a person or thing that is unrivaled or has no equal; a model of excellence or perfection
someone or something without equal

Examples

She is the nonpareil of kindness in our community, admired by everyone who knows her.
His skills as a pianist are truly nonpareil, setting a standard for others to follow.
The chef’s dessert was a nonpareil, unmatched by anything I've tasted before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality
  • Antonym: peer - a person who is equal to another in abilities or qualifications

Etymology

From Middle French *nonpareil*, from non- (not) + pareil (equal).
