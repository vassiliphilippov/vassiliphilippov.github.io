Tarpon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːrˌpɑn / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpən / (BrE)
A genus of large, powerful fish found in warm coastal waters, known for their silvery scales and popularity among sport fishers.
a genus of large fish

Examples

The Tarpon is prized by anglers for its fight and impressive leaps when hooked.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of the Tarpon in the Gulf of Mexico.
The silver scales of the Tarpon make it easily distinguishable from other fish.

Etymology

From New Latin, probably of Amerindian origin.
