doodad

1. NOUN

/ ˈduˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈduː.dæd / (BrE)
an object whose name is unknown or forgotten; a small device or gadget, especially when its specific name is not known
a small gadget or object with an unknown name
informal american

Examples

Can you hand me that doodad on the shelf?
He used a clever little doodad to fix the leak in the pipe.
Whenever I clean my desk, I find at least one mysterious doodad I can't identify.

Synset Examples

she eased the ball-shaped doodad back into its socket
there may be some great new gizmo around the corner that you will want to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gizmo - a gadget or small mechanical device
  • Synonym: thingamajig - a thing whose name one has forgotten, does not know, or does not wish to mention
  • Synonym: whatchamacallit - a thing whose name one cannot recall

Etymology

American English, early 20th century; origin unknown, possibly fanciful formation.
