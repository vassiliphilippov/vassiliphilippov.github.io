argumentatively

1. ADV

/ ˌɑːrɡjəˈmɛntɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡjʊˈmɛntətɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows a tendency to argue or dispute
in a disputing or argumentative manner

Examples

She spoke argumentatively, refusing to yield her opinion during the discussion.
When challenged, he responded argumentatively, eager to defend his point of view.
The meeting grew tense as several members began to speak argumentatively about the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contentiously - in a way that causes or is likely to cause an argument
  • Antonym: peaceably - in a manner showing no desire to argue or fight

Etymology

From 'argumentative' + '-ly', with 'argumentative' from Medieval Latin 'argumentativus'.
