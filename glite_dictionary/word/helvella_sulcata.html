Helvella sulcata

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈvɛlə sʌlˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / hɛlˈvɛlə sʌlˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with an irregular, convoluted dark brown cap when young, turning dull grey with age; its stalk is thick and deeply fluted.
a species of fungus with a convoluted cap

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Helvella sulcata after the spring rain.
Mycology enthusiasts often search for Helvella sulcata due to its distinctive fluted stem.
Unlike some other fungi, Helvella sulcata is easily recognized by its irregular, greyish cap.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Helvella; species epithet sulcata meaning 'furrowed' in Latin.
