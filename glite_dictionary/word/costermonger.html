costermonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑstərˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈkɒstəmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who sells fruit and vegetables from a cart, typically in the streets of a city
street seller of fruit and vegetables

Examples

The costermonger shouted to attract customers as he wheeled his barrow down the street.
In Victorian London, a costermonger was a familiar sight in the busy markets.
She bought fresh apples from a friendly costermonger early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawker - a person who sells goods in the street

Etymology

From costard (an old type of apple) + monger (seller).
