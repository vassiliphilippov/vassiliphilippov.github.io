tympanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpənɪst / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpənɪst / (BrE)
a musician who plays the kettledrums (timpani) in an orchestra or band
person who plays the kettledrums

Examples

The orchestra's tympanist played a crucial role in building suspense during the performance.
As a skilled tympanist, she has performed with symphonies around the world.
The conductor praised the tympanist for his precise timing and dynamic control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timpanist - a musician who plays the timpani (kettledrums)

Etymology

From French tympaniste, from Latin tympanum (drum, kettledrum).
