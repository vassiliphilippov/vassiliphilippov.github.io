kinaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪn.əsˈθiː.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌkɪn.ɪsˈθiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
the sensation or perception of movement and position of body parts, especially in muscles and joints
sense of body movement and position
medical

Examples

After his injury, his kinaesthesia was affected, making it difficult for him to sense limb movements.
Yoga exercises are designed to improve balance and kinaesthesia.
Children with certain neurological conditions may have reduced kinaesthesia compared to their peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one's own body parts

Etymology

From Greek 'kínēsis' (movement) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation).

2. NOUN

/ kɪn.iˈθiː.ʒə / (AmE), / kɪn.iˈθiːz.i.ə / (BrE)
the sense that detects the position, movement, and tension of parts of the body
perception of body movement and position
medical

Examples

The dancer's exceptional kinaesthesia allowed her to perform complex movements with grace.
Children develop kinaesthesia as they learn to coordinate their arms and legs.
Physical therapy can help restore kinaesthesia after an injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one's own body parts

Etymology

From Greek kinein (to move) + aisthēsis (sensation).
