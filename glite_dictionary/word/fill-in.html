fill-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪl ɪn / (BrE)
a person who temporarily replaces someone else, especially in a job or role; a substitute
temporary substitute; stand-in

Examples

When the regular teacher was sick, the school called in a fill-in for the day.
They needed a fill-in for their radio host while she was on vacation.
The actor's fill-in performed the dangerous stunts during filming.

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who takes someone else's place temporarily
  • Synonym: replacement - a person or thing that takes the place of another
