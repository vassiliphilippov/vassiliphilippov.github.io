Porcellionidae

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˌsɛliˈɒnɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɔːˌsɛlɪˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of terrestrial isopod crustaceans commonly known as sow bugs, found in damp environments.
family of sow bugs
medical

Examples

The Porcellionidae are commonly found under rocks and decaying wood in gardens.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Porcellionidae family in southern Europe.
Members of the Porcellionidae feed on decomposing plant material, aiding in soil formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow bug - common name for isopod crustaceans in the family Porcellionidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Porcellio + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ pɔrˌsɛliˈɒnɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɔːˌsɛlɪˈɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of woodlice (sow bugs) in the order Isopoda, comprising terrestrial crustaceans commonly found in moist environments
a family of woodlice (sow bugs)

Examples

Many species in the Porcellionidae family play an important role in breaking down decaying organic matter.
Some Porcellionidae have adapted to urban environments, thriving in gardens and under rocks.
Researchers discovered a new genus of Porcellionidae in the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - a small terrestrial crustacean with a rigid, segmented exoskeleton
  • Degree Lower: Oniscidae - another family of woodlice

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Porcellio, from Latin 'porcellus' (little pig) + '-idae' (taxonomic family suffix).
