payoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ɒf / (BrE)
a result, reward, or punishment that follows an action, especially as a recompense for good deeds or retribution for wrongdoing
reward or punishment as a consequence

Examples

Hard work brings its own payoff in personal satisfaction and achievement.
The payoff for his years of loyalty was a well-earned promotion.
Some believe that dishonesty eventually has a negative payoff.

Synset Examples

the wages of sin is death
virtue is its own reward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
  • Antonym: punishment - a penalty inflicted as retribution for an offense
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌɒf / (BrE)
a payment made to someone in a position of trust, often as a bribe to influence their actions or decisions
bribe payment to influence judgment

Examples

The mayor was accused of accepting a payoff from the construction company.
Investigators discovered that the executive had received a large payoff to approve the contract.
She was offered a payoff to overlook the illegal activity, but she refused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - money or gift given to corrupt someone's actions or judgment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13305585-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɒf / (BrE)
the income or profit resulting from a sale or financial transaction
income or profit from a transaction

Examples

The payoff from selling the old house was enough to buy a new car.
Investors are always looking for the highest possible payoff in the stock market.
She calculated the payoff before deciding to sell her shares.

Synset Examples

the average return was about 5%

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - the profit made on an investment or transaction
  • Antonym: loss - the state of losing money on a transaction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13281089-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɒf / (BrE)
the final payment made to settle a debt or obligation
final settlement of a debt

Examples

After years of monthly installments, John finally made the payoff on his mortgage last week.
The car loan will require one large payoff at the end of the five-year term.
We celebrated the payoff of our student loans with a nice dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settlement - the act of paying off a debt or claim
