villein

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlən / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪn / (BrE)
(historical) A person in the Middle Ages who was bound to the land and owned by a feudal lord, having fewer rights than a freeman.
medieval unfree peasant

Examples

In medieval England, the villein was obliged to work the lord’s fields in exchange for protection.
The status of a villein was hereditary, passing from parent to child.
A villein could not leave the manor without the lord’s permission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system

Etymology

From Old French *vilain*, from Medieval Latin *villanus* (farmhand, peasant), from Latin *villa* (country house).
