Pan-Hellenic

1. ADJ

/ pænˌhəˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / panˌhɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or including all the Greek people or states, especially in ancient times.
of or relating to all Greeks collectively

Examples

The ancient Olympic Games were a Pan-Hellenic event that brought together athletes from every Greek city-state.
The council discussed issues of Pan-Hellenic significance, seeking to unite the fractured Greek world.
Her research focused on Pan-Hellenic religious festivals and their cultural impact.

Synset Examples

the Olympic Games were a Panhellenic celebration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hellenic - relating to ancient Greek history, culture, or people

Etymology

From Greek pan- (all) + Hellēnikos (Greek).
