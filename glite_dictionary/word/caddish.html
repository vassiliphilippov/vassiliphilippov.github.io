caddish

1. ADJ

/ ˈkædɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkadɪʃ / (BrE)
behaving in an offensively discourteous or ungentlemanly manner
offensively discourteous or ungentlemanly

Examples

His caddish remarks during dinner offended everyone at the table.
She was tired of his caddish behavior at social events.
The caddish way he treated his friends eventually left him alone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentlemanly - polite and honorable, characteristic of a gentleman
  • Synonym: boorish - rough and bad-mannered; coarse

Etymology

From 'cad', a term for a man behaving dishonorably, with '-ish' forming the adjective.
