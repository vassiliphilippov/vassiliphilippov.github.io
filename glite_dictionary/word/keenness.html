keenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkinnəs / (AmE), / ˈkiːnnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a quick and penetrating intelligence; sharpness of mind or perception
sharpness of intelligence

Examples

Her keenness of thought allowed her to solve complex problems quickly.
The keenness shown by the candidates impressed the interviewers.
His keenness enabled him to spot details that others often missed.

Synset Examples

he argued with great acuteness
I admired the keenness of his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acuteness - sharpness or quickness of mind or perception
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of sharp intelligence or quickness of mind

Etymology

From keen + -ness, originally from Old English 'cene' (bold, sharp).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkinnəs / (AmE), / ˈkiːnnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a sharp or finely-edged point or surface
thinness or sharpness of an edge or point

Examples

The keenness of the blade made it perfect for slicing delicate fabric.
Artisans admire the keenness of a well-forged knife edge.
The surgeon checked the keenness of her scalpel before beginning the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpness - the condition of having a sharp edge or point
  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of having a dull or rounded edge

Etymology

From keen + -ness, keen from Middle English kene, from Old English cēne (bold, brave, sharp).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkiːn.nəs / (BrE)
great enthusiasm or eagerness to do something; strong desire or interest
enthusiasm; eagerness

Examples

Her keenness to start the project impressed everyone in the team.
The children showed great keenness for learning new things during the workshop.
Despite his keenness, he waited patiently for the right opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerness - a feeling of intense enthusiasm or desire
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of enthusiasm or interest

Etymology

From 'keen' + '-ness', with 'keen' from Middle English kene, from Old English cēne (brave, bold, clever).
