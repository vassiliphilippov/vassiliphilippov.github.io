leather-leaved

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛðɚ liːvd / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə liːvd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having leaves that are thick, tough, and resembling leather in texture.
having tough, leathery leaves

Examples

The leather-leaved magnolia is known for its resistance to drought.
Botanists studied several leather-leaved shrubs found in the arid woodland.
The leather-leaved foliage of the plant helps it retain moisture in dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leathery - having a tough, leather-like texture
