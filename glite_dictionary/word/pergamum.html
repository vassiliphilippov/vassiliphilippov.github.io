Pergamum

1. PROPN

/ pərˈɡæməm / (AmE), / pɜːˈɡaməm / (BrE)
an ancient Greek city in western Asia Minor, noted for its rich cultural history and as the place where parchment from sheepskin was developed
ancient Greek city in western Asia Minor

Examples

The ruins of Pergamum attract many historians and tourists interested in ancient civilizations.
The Library of Pergamum was once considered one of the greatest in the ancient world.
Pergamum is renowned as the birthplace of parchment, which revolutionized writing materials.

Etymology

From Latin Pergamum, from Ancient Greek Πέργαμον (Pérgamon), the ancient city's name.
