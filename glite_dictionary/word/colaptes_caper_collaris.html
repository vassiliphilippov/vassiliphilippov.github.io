Colaptes caper collaris

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlæp.tiːz ˈkeɪpər kəˈlærɪs / (AmE), / kəˈlæp.tiːz ˈkeɪpə kəˈlærɪs / (BrE)
A subspecies of woodpecker found in the western United States, distinguished by the red undersurfaces of its wings and tail, commonly known as the red-shafted flicker.
red-shafted flicker, a U.S. woodpecker subspecies

Examples

The Colaptes caper collaris is often seen foraging on the ground in western states.
Birdwatchers noted the distinct red feathers of the Colaptes caper collaris during their hike.
Researchers study the migration patterns of the Colaptes caper collaris every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern flicker - a widespread North American woodpecker species
  • Synonym: Colaptes auratus - the scientific name for the northern flicker species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used for scientific naming: genus Colaptes + caper (Latin, meaning 'goat') + collaris (Latin, 'of the neck, collared').
