Mahomet

1. PROPN

/ məˈhoʊmət / (AmE), / məˈhɒmɪt / (BrE)
A historical English form of the name of the Prophet Muhammad (570–632), the founder of Islam and considered by Muslims as the last messenger of Allah.
historical English name for Muhammad, Prophet of Islam
archaic

Examples

In medieval literature, Mahomet is often depicted through the cultural lens of the time.
The name Mahomet was commonly used in English chronicles of the Middle Ages.
Many historical European texts refer to the Prophet Muhammad as Mahomet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muhammad - the Prophet of Islam (570–632)

Etymology

From Old French Mahomet, from Medieval Latin Mahometus, ultimately from Arabic Muhammad.
