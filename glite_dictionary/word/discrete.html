discrete

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrit / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
separate and distinct; not connected to or part of something else.
separate; distinct; unconnected

Examples

The project was divided into several discrete phases, each managed by a different team.
In mathematics, a discrete variable can only take on certain specific values.
The company is organized into discrete departments, each with its own responsibilities.

Synset Examples

a government with three discrete divisions
on two distinct occasions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinct - clearly separate and different
  • Antonym: continuous - forming an unbroken whole; uninterrupted

Etymology

From Latin discretus (separated, distinct), from discernere (to separate, distinguish).
