clash

1. VERB

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
to crash together with a violent or loud impact
crash together with force

Examples

The two cymbals clash loudly during the orchestra's performance.
During the demonstration, police shields clashed with protestors' barricades.
As the storm raged, the tree branches clashed together in the fierce wind.

Synset Examples

The cars collided
Two meteors clashed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collide - (of two or more objects) hit each other with force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01563925-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
a brief and minor fight or argument between people or groups
a minor short-term fight

Examples

There was a clash between rival fans after the match.
A brief clash broke out during the protest.
The two politicians had a clash over the proposed tax reforms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically longer-lasting than a clash
  • Synonym: skirmish - a brief and usually unplanned fight, similar in meaning to clash
  • Degree Higher: battle - a sustained fight between large organized armed forces
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)

loud, resonant repeating noise

Examples

The clash of cymbals echoed through the concert hall.
A sudden clash came from the kitchen as the pots fell to the floor.
We heard the distinct clash of swords in the old movie scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Synonym: crash - a sudden, loud noise as of two objects hitting each other

5. VERB

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
to come into strong conflict or disagreement, often in a forceful or emotional way
disagree forcefully

Examples

The two leaders clashed during the televised debate on healthcare reform.
My ideas often clash with those of my colleagues at work.
They clashed repeatedly over how to handle the company's future.

Synset Examples

We clashed over the new farm policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - to be in serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion, not to disagree

Etymology

7. VERB

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
(of people, ideas, or things) to be incompatible or to come into conflict; to not match or agree.

Examples

Red and orange often clash when used together in design.
Sometimes our personalities simply clash, despite our best efforts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - to be in opposition or disagreement
  • Antonym: to agree - to be in harmony or accordance

Etymology
