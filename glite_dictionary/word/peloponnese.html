Peloponnese

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛləpəˈnis / (AmE), / ˌpɛləpəˈniːz / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southern Greece, notable as the location of ancient Sparta and other important classical cities
southern peninsula of Greece

Examples

Many ancient battles took place on the Peloponnese during classical times.
The Peloponnese is famous for its stunning landscapes and archaeological sites.
She traveled across the Peloponnese to visit the ruins of ancient Sparta.

Etymology

From Greek Πελοπόννησος (Pelopónnēsos), meaning 'Island of Pelops'.
