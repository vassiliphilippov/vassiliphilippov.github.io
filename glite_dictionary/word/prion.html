prion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈpriː.ɒn / (BrE)
a type of infectious protein particle, lacking nucleic acid, believed to cause certain degenerative diseases of the nervous system
infectious protein particle (microbiology)
medical

Examples

Bovine spongiform encephalopathy is believed to be caused by a prion.
The scientist studied how the prion replicated despite lacking genetic material.
Infectious prions have been implicated in several neurodegenerative disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - an infective agent that typically consists of a nucleic acid molecule in a protein coat

Etymology

Coined c. 1982 from 'proteinaceous infectious particle'.
