prefab

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriˌfæb / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌfæb / (BrE)
manufactured in standard sections or components to be transported and assembled on site
manufactured in sections for assembly

Examples

The company offers a variety of prefab homes that can be constructed in a matter of days.
We toured a prefab classroom built to accommodate the school's growing enrollment.
Many modern offices now use prefab walls that can be reconfigured easily.

Synset Examples

a prefab structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prefabricated - manufactured in advance, often for rapid assembly

Etymology

Shortened from 'prefabricated'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00676437-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpriˌfæb / (AmE), / ˈpriːfæb / (BrE)
a building that is manufactured in sections and then assembled on site; a prefabricated structure
a prefabricated building

Examples

The family lived in a prefab for several years after the war.
Many schools installed temporary prefabs to accommodate more students.
After the tornado, the community was rebuilt with modern prefabs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prefabricated - manufactured in sections to permit quick assembly

Etymology

Shortened form of 'prefabricated', from Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'fabricare' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04005422-n
Synset POS: n
