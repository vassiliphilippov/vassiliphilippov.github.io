appendicular

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɛnˈdɪkjələr / (AmE), / əˌpɛnˈdɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of an appendage or appendages, especially the limbs.
relating to appendages, especially limbs
medical

Examples

The appendicular skeleton consists of the bones of the limbs and girdles.
Doctors studied the appendicular muscles to assess limb strength and function.
Research focuses on appendicular movement in rehabilitation medicine.

Synset Examples

the appendicular skeleton

Related Terms

  • Antonym: axial - relating to the central part of the body, as opposed to the limbs

Etymology

From Latin appendicula (a small appendage), diminutive of appendix.
