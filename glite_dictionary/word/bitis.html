Bitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes in the viper family (Viperidae), including species such as the puff adder and gaboon viper, native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.
genus of venomous vipers

Examples

The Bitis genus is best known for the puff adder, a highly venomous snake found in Africa.
Researchers have discovered new species within the Bitis genus in recent years.
Many herpetologists specialize in studying Bitis snakes due to their medical importance.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Bitis', the genus name assigned in zoological classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes in the viper family (Viperidae), native to Africa and parts of the Arabian Peninsula, including the puff adder and gaboon viper.
genus of African vipers

Examples

The puff adder, a member of the Bitis genus, is responsible for many snakebites in Africa.
Several species of the Bitis genus have distinctive triangular heads and stout bodies.
Herpetologists study the Bitis genus to better understand venom composition and snake behavior.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Bitis', designated in scientific classification.
