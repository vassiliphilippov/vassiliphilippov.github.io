Balsamorhiza

1. NOUN

/ bælˌsæməˈraɪzə / (AmE), / bælˌsæməˈraɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, western American herbs with large, aromatic roots, belonging to the sunflower family.
genus of western American herbs

Examples

The Balsamorhiza species are often found growing in open, dry habitats across the American West.
Early settlers used the roots of Balsamorhiza for medicinal purposes.
Botanists collected samples of Balsamorhiza during their expedition in Oregon.

Etymology

From Greek balsamon (balsam) + rhiza (root), referring to the aromatic roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbælsəmoʊˈraɪzə / (AmE), / ˌbalsəməˈrʌɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of coarse herbs native to western North America, having large roots that contain an aromatic balsam.
genus of western North American herbs

Examples

The root of Balsamorhiza has been traditionally used by Native Americans for its medicinal properties.
Many species within the Balsamorhiza genus are well adapted to dry, rocky soils.
The vibrant yellow flowers of Balsamorhiza brighten the hillsides in spring.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'balsamum' (balsam) and Greek 'rhiza' (root).
