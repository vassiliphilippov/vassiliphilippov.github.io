to decoke

1. VERB

/ ˌdiˈkoʊk / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈkəʊk / (BrE)
to remove carbon deposits from the inside of an engine
remove carbon from an engine

Examples

The mechanic had to decoke the engine before it would run smoothly again.
It's important to decoke older car engines regularly to maintain their performance.
After years of use, the workshop specializes in decoking diesel truck engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - make something free from dirt, marks, or unwanted matter

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'de-' (indicating removal) and 'coke' (residual carbon from incomplete combustion).
