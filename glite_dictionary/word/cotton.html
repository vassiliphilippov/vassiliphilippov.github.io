cotton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
an erect bushy plant or small tree that bears pods containing seeds surrounded by long, soft, hairy fibers, cultivated for its fiber used in making textiles
fiber-producing plant for textiles

Examples

The fields were full of cotton ready for harvest.
Farmers in the region rely on cotton as their main crop.
She visited a museum that displayed the history of cotton cultivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton plant - the plant from which cotton fiber is obtained

Etymology

From Arabic 'quṭn' via Old Italian 'cotone' and Old French 'cotton'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtən / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən / (BrE)
A soft white fabric made from the fibers of the cotton plant, commonly used to make clothes and textiles.
soft fabric made from cotton fibers

Examples

She wore a light cotton dress on the warm summer day.
These curtains are made of pure cotton for a natural look and feel.
Farmers in the region grow cotton to supply the textile mills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linen - fabric made from flax fibers

Etymology

From Arabic 'quṭn', via Old French 'coton' and Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
the soft, white, fluffy fibers that grow around the seeds of the cotton plant, used in making textile products
soft fiber from the cotton plant

Examples

The farmers harvested the cotton before sending it to the factory.
A ball of pure cotton was used to clean the cut.
In the South, many fields are covered with blooming cotton in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - soft fiber from the fleece of sheep or other animals
  • Synonym: linen - cloth made from the fiber of the flax plant

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtən / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən / (BrE)
Thread or yarn made from the fibers of the cotton plant, used for making textiles and garments.
thread or yarn made from cotton fibers

Examples

The shirt was woven from fine cotton that felt soft against the skin.
She bought a spool of white cotton for her sewing project.
After the harvest, the factory spun the cotton into thread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linen - thread or cloth made from flax fibers
  • Synonym: wool - thread or cloth made from sheep's wool

Etymology

From Arabic 'quṭn', via Old Italian 'cotone', Old French 'cotton'.
