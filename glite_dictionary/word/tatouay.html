tatouay

1. NOUN

/ tæˈtuː.aɪ / (AmE), / tatˈuː.aɪ / (BrE)
a naked-tailed armadillo found in tropical South America.
naked-tailed armadillo of South America

Examples

The tatouay is distinguished by its long, naked tail and tough protective shell.
In the forests of Brazil, the tatouay searches for insects among the fallen leaves.
Researchers tracked the movements of a wild tatouay as part of their conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armadillo - burrowing mammal with a bony armor shell

Etymology

From French 'tatouay', likely of indigenous South American origin.
