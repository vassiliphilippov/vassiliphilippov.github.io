Valois

1. PROPN

/ vælˈwɑː / (AmE), / ˈvælwɑː / (BrE)
the French royal house that ruled France from 1328 to 1589
French royal dynasty (1328–1589)

Examples

The Valois dynasty played a central role in the Hundred Years' War.
Many kings from the Valois family contributed to the Renaissance in France.
Catherine de' Medici was one of the most influential queens of the Valois era.

Etymology

From French Valois, named after the Valois region in France.
