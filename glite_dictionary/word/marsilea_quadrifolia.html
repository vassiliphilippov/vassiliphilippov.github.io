Marsilea quadrifolia

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈsɪliə ˌkwɑdrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɪliə ˌkwɒdrɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of aquatic fern native to Europe, Asia, and eastern North America, noted for its four clover-like leaflets.
species of aquatic fern with four clover-like leaflets

Examples

The pond was covered with Marsilea quadrifolia, giving it the appearance of floating clover.
Botanists have studied Marsilea quadrifolia for its unique reproductive cycle.
Gardeners sometimes plant Marsilea quadrifolia as an ornamental aquatics in water features.

Etymology

Genus name from the botanist Luigi Ferdinando Marsili; 'quadrifolia' from Latin meaning 'four-leaved'.
