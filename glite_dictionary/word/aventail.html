aventail

1. NOUN

/ ˈævənˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈævənteɪl / (BrE)
A piece of chain mail attached to a helmet or basinet, designed to protect the head and neck in medieval armor.
chain mail hood attached to a helmet

Examples

The knight's aventail provided extra protection during battle, shielding his neck from sword strikes.
Restoring the helmet, the conservator carefully reattached the original aventail.
With the aventail in place, only the face of the warrior remained exposed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camail - A mail curtain attached to a helmet for neck and throat protection

Etymology

From Old French 'eventail', meaning 'fan' or 'visor', ultimately from Latin 'ventilare' (to fan, to ventilate).
