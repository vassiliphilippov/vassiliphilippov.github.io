lechatelierite

1. NOUN

/ ləˌʃætələˈraɪt / (AmE), / lɛˌʃætɛlɪˈaɪt / (BrE)
a natural colorless glass consisting of almost pure silica, usually formed by the fusion of sand by lightning or meteor impacts
natural pure silica glass

Examples

Scientists identified the clear mineral as lechatelierite after analyzing its chemical composition.
When lightning strikes a sandy beach, it can fuse the grains into lechatelierite.
Fragments of lechatelierite were found at the meteorite impact site.

Etymology

Named after French chemist Henry Le Chatelier (1850–1936).
