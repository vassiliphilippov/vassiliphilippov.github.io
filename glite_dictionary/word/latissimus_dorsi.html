latissimus dorsi

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtɪsɪməs ˈdɔrsaɪ / (AmE), / ləˈtɪsɪməs ˈdɔːsaɪ / (BrE)
a large, flat muscle on each side of the back, helping to move the shoulder and arm
large back muscle
medical

Examples

Many swimmers have well-developed latissimus dorsi muscles due to repetitive arm movements.
Injury to the latissimus dorsi can limit your ability to pull or lift objects overhead.
The latissimus dorsi is often targeted in strength training exercises like pull-ups and rows.

Synset Examples

Strengthening the lat. dorsi improves back stability.

Etymology

From Latin 'latissimus' (broadest) and 'dorsum' (back).
