Cistus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized evergreen shrubs native to southern Europe and North Africa, known for their showy flowers; also called rockrose.
a genus of Mediterranean evergreen shrubs

Examples

The gardens were filled with blooming Cistus bushes in early summer.
Many species of Cistus thrive in dry, rocky soils.
She planted several Cistus to add year-round greenery to her yard.

Etymology

From Latin 'cistus', from Greek 'kistos', name for the rockrose.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized evergreen shrubs native to southern Europe and North Africa, commonly known as rockroses.
genus of Mediterranean evergreen shrubs

Examples

Several species of Cistus are popular in Mediterranean gardens for their pretty flowers and drought tolerance.
The Cistus shrubs in the park bloom with delicate white and pink blossoms each spring.
Botanists identified several new varieties within the Cistus genus during their fieldwork in southern Spain.

Etymology

From Greek kistos (rockrose).
