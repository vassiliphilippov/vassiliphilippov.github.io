tempest

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəst / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪst / (BrE)
a violent disturbance or commotion, often used metaphorically to describe turmoil or upheaval
violent disturbance or commotion

Examples

Her announcement created a tempest of controversy within the organization.
The debate turned into a political tempest that lasted for weeks.
He dismissed the criticism as merely a tempest in a teapot.

Synset Examples

the storms that had characterized their relationship had died away
it was only a tempest in a teapot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - a violent weather event with wind, rain, or snow
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness or absence of disturbance

Etymology

From Old French 'tempeste', from Latin 'tempestas' (storm, weather, season).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛm.pəst / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.pɪst / (BrE)
A violent and windy storm, especially used in literary contexts.
violent windstorm (literary)
archaic

Examples

The tempest left the coastline battered and the ships severely damaged.
During the tempest, trees were uprooted and power lines were knocked down.
Shakespeare's play 'The Tempest' begins with a ship caught in a violent storm.

Synset Examples

a tempest swept over the island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - an intense disturbance in the atmosphere, often with wind and precipitation
  • Antonym: calm - a period or condition of no wind or disturbance

Etymology

From Old French 'tempeste', from Latin 'tempestas' (storm, season, weather).
