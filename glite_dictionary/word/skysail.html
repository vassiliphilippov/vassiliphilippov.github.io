skysail

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈskaɪseɪl / (BrE)
a small sail set above the royal sail on a square-rigged sailing ship
sail above the royal sail

Examples

The crew struggled to set the skysail as the winds grew stronger.
From the deck, the highest skysail was barely visible against the clouds.
During the race, the captain ordered the skysail hoisted to gain more speed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: royal - the sail immediately below the skysail on a square-rigger

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' + 'sail', indicating its position high on the mast.
