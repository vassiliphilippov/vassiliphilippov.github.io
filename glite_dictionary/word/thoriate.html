to thoriate

1. VERB

/ ˈθɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈθɔːriˌeɪt / (BrE)
to impregnate or treat with thorium oxide, typically to increase thermionic emission in materials
impregnate with thorium oxide
medical

Examples

To improve the efficiency of the filament, manufacturers thoriate the tungsten wire.
Engineers often thoriated electrodes in order to boost thermionic emission in vacuum tubes.
After thoriating the cathode, the emission levels increased significantly.

Etymology

From 'thorium' (the chemical element) + '-ate' (verb-forming suffix).
