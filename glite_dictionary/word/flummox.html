to flummox

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌməks / (AmE), / ˈflʌməkʃ / (BrE)
to bewilder or confuse someone completely
to bewilder or confuse utterly

Examples

The complex instructions completely flummoxed the new employees.
Her answer seemed to flummox the interviewer for a moment.
He was flummoxed by the unexpected question during the meeting.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewilder - to cause someone to become perplexed and confused
  • Synonym: puzzle - to confuse or baffle someone mentally

Etymology

Origin uncertain, perhaps dialectal; first recorded in the 19th century.
