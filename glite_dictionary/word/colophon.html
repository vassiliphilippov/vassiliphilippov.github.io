colophon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləˌfɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɒləˌfɒn / (BrE)
a publisher's emblem or imprint, typically printed in a book, often on the title page or at the end
publisher's emblem in a book

Examples

The first edition features a unique colophon on its last page.
She admired the intricate colophon that represented the ancient publishing house.
Collectors often look for early colophons to identify rare books.

Etymology

From Greek kolophōn (summit, finishing touch).
