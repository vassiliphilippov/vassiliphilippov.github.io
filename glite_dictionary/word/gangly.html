gangly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæŋ.li / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋ.li / (BrE)
(of a person) tall, thin, and with long, awkward limbs.
tall, thin, with long limbs

Examples

The gangly boy struggled to keep up with his more athletic classmates during the race.
At thirteen, she grew rapidly and became a gangly teenager, all arms and legs.
Despite his gangly frame, the basketball coach saw great potential in him.

Synset Examples

a gangling teenager
a lanky kid transformed almost overnight into a handsome young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - tall and thin, with long limbs
  • Synonym: gangling - awkwardly tall and thin

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡæŋli / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋli / (BrE)
tall and thin, often with an awkward or loosely built appearance
tall and thin; awkwardly built

Examples

The gangly teenager towered over his classmates at the basketball tryouts.
She had a gangly frame that made her look even taller than she was.
The gangly puppy struggled to control its long legs as it ran.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - tall and thin, often with long limbs
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built
  • Degree Lower: slender - gracefully thin
