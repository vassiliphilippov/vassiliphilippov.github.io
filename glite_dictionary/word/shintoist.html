Shintoist

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪn.toʊ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃɪn.təʊ.ɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Shintoism, the indigenous religion of Japan that emphasizes ritual practices and belief in kami (spirits)
of or relating to Shintoism

Examples

The festival was celebrated in a traditional Shintoist manner, with rituals honoring the local spirits.
Many Shintoist shrines are located throughout Japan, each with its own unique history.
The ceremony included several Shintoist customs, such as offering food and sake to the kami.

Synset Examples

Shinto temples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shinto - the indigenous religion of Japan focused on ritual practices and belief in spirits

Etymology

From Shinto + -ist, modeled after English compounds such as Buddhist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪn.toʊ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃɪn.təʊ.ɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes in or practices Shintoism, the traditional religion of Japan.
believer or practitioner of Shintoism

Examples

Many Shintoists visit shrines during traditional Japanese festivals.
As a Shintoist, he observes various rituals to honor the kami.
The community has several Shintoists who maintain the local shrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhist - a believer in Buddhism
  • Synonym: Christian - a believer in Christianity

Etymology

From Shinto + -ist, denoting a follower or adherent.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09705184-n
Synset POS: n
