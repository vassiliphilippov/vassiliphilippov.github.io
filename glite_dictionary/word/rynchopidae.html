Rynchopidae

1. NOUN

/ rɪnˈkɒpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / rɪnˈkɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds, commonly known as skimmers, consisting of a single genus Rynchops, characterized by their long wings and unique bill shape used for skimming the water surface for food.
family of birds known as skimmers

Examples

The Rynchopidae are easily recognized by their elongated lower mandibles, which help them catch fish while flying.
Unlike other bird families, Rynchopidae have distinctive feeding habits that involve skimming the water's surface.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting several species in the Rynchopidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimmer - a bird of the genus Rynchops, known for skimming water with its elongated bill

Etymology

From New Latin Rynchops (the type genus) + -idae (taxonomic family suffix).
