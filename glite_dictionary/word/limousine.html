limousine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪməˌzin / (AmE), / ˌlɪməˈziːn / (BrE)
a large, luxurious car, usually driven by a chauffeur and often used for special occasions or to transport important people
large luxury car, usually chauffeur-driven

Examples

The bride and groom arrived at the venue in a sleek black limousine.
He hired a limousine to pick up the company executives from the airport.
The celebrity waved to fans as she stepped out of the limousine on the red carpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedan - a passenger car in a three-box configuration
  • Antonym: taxi - a car licensed to transport passengers in return for payment

Etymology

From French *limousine*, originally referring to a type of cloaked carriage from Limousin, a region in France.
