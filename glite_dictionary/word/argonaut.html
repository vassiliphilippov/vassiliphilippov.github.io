Argonaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡənɔːt / (BrE)
a small cephalopod mollusk of warm seas, whose female produces a delicate, papery, spiral shell
warm-sea cephalopod; female has papery shell

Examples

The female Argonaut creates a delicate shell that she uses as a brood chamber for her eggs.
Unlike other octopuses, the Argonaut floats near the ocean surface.
Marine biologists were excited to find a rare Argonaut drifting off the coast.

Etymology

From Greek Argōnautēs, literally 'Argo sailor', from the mythological band of heroes; applied to the mollusk due to its shell, reminiscent of a ship.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡənɔːt / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the band of heroes who accompanied Jason in his quest to find the Golden Fleece.
hero who sailed with Jason for the Golden Fleece

Examples

In the ancient tale, each Argonaut faced perilous challenges alongside Jason.
The Argonauts sailed through treacherous seas in search of the mythical Golden Fleece.
He dreamed of being as brave as an Argonaut venturing into the unknown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jason - leader of the Argonauts in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Golden Fleece - the object of the Argonauts' quest in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Argonautēs, meaning 'sailor of the Argo' (the ship's name was Argo).
