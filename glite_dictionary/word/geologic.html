geologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiːəˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the science of geology, or the structure, history, and processes of the Earth's physical formation
relating to geology or Earth's structure

Examples

The canyon was formed by geologic processes over millions of years.
Scientists studied the geologic history of the region to understand its natural hazards.
The map highlights important geologic features such as fault lines and volcanic rocks.

Synset Examples

geological formations
geologic forces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geological - relating to geology (preferred variant spelling)

Etymology

From Greek geōlogia (study of the earth), with -ic forming the adjective.
