Acrostichum

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑː.stɪ.kəm / (AmE), / əˈkrɒs.tɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical ferns in the subfamily Ceratopteridoideae, known for their leathery fronds and often found in mangrove swamps.
genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The mangrove swamps were dominated by Acrostichum ferns along the water's edge.
Botanists often study Acrostichum for its ability to thrive in saline environments.
The leathery fronds of Acrostichum are distinctive among tropical ferns.

Etymology

From Greek *akrostichon* ('at the tip, a row'), referring to the arrangement of sporangia.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑː.stɪ.kəm / (AmE), / əˈkrɒs.tɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the subfamily Ceratopteridoideae of the family Pteridaceae, sometimes classified in Polypodiaceae.
a genus of ferns

Examples

The Acrostichum species thrive in mangrove swamps along tropical coasts.
Researchers discovered a new variety of Acrostichum growing near the river delta.
Acrostichum ferns are known for their large, leathery fronds.

Etymology

From Greek akrostichos (at the top of the row), referring to the arrangement of spores.
