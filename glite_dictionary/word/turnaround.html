turnaround

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəraʊnd / (BrE)
an act of turning or moving in the opposite direction
act of turning in the opposite direction

Examples

The bus made a swift turnaround and headed back toward the city.
A designated turnaround allowed vehicles to reverse direction safely on the highway.
We had to perform a turnaround because we missed the exit.

Etymology

Formed from 'turn' + 'around' in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrn.əˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn.əˌraʊnd / (BrE)
the amount of time required to prepare a vessel, ship, or aircraft for its return journey after arriving at its destination
time needed to prepare for a return trip

Examples

The crew worked quickly to minimize the turnaround for the ferry.
A shorter turnaround means more daily trips for the cruise ship.
Efficient turnaround procedures are crucial for airlines at busy airports.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəraʊnd / (BrE)
the act or process of unloading, loading, and servicing a vessel or aircraft in preparation for its return trip
servicing for a vessel's or aircraft's next trip

Examples

The airport has reduced the turnaround time for incoming flights to improve efficiency.
After docking, the crew managed the turnaround in under two hours.
Quick turnaround is critical for airlines to keep to their tight schedules.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: layover - a period spent by a vehicle or traveler at an intermediate stop
  • Degree Higher: turnaround time - the time needed to complete the process of turning around a vehicle or process

5. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəˌraʊnd / (BrE)
a change of decision or policy to the opposite of what was previously decided
reversal of an earlier decision

Examples

The company announced a turnaround in its merger decision after pressure from shareholders.
Their surprising turnaround restored confidence among the employees.
The policy's sudden turnaround caught everyone off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - a change to an opposite direction, position, or course of action
  • Synonym: U-turn - a sudden or complete reversal in policy, attitude, or course of action

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'turn around', meaning to reverse direction or position, in extended figurative use since the mid-20th century.
