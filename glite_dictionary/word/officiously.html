officiously

1. ADV

/ əˈfɪʃəsli / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is excessively eager to offer unwanted help or advice, often in an interfering or meddlesome way.
in an interfering or meddlesome manner

Examples

He officiously interrupted the meeting to offer his opinion, even though no one had asked for it.
The security guard officiously demanded to see my identification, making a scene in the lobby.
She officiously arranged everyone's papers, insisting she knew best how they should be organized.

Synset Examples

nothing so fatal as to strive too officiously for an abstract quality like beauty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unobtrusively - in a manner that does not attract attention or interfere

Etymology

From 'officious' + '-ly'; 'officious' from Latin 'officiosus' (obliging, dutiful).
