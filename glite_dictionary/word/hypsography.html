hypsography

1. NOUN

/ hɪpˈsɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / hɪpˈsɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the scientific study and measurement of the elevation and configuration of the Earth's surface above sea level
study of land elevations above sea level
medical

Examples

The researcher specialized in hypsography to better understand mountain formation.
Modern hypsography relies on satellite data to measure elevation.
His findings in hypsography greatly contributed to regional topographic maps.

Etymology

From Greek hypsos (height) + -graphy (writing, description).

medical

Examples

Scientists use hypsography to analyze changes in sea level and land elevation over time.
