venipuncture

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.ɪˌpʌŋk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈviː.nɪˌpʌŋk.tʃə / (BrE)
the puncture of a vein, especially to withdraw blood or administer medication
puncture of a vein for medical purposes
medical

Examples

The nurse performed a venipuncture to draw blood for testing.
Proper technique is essential when performing venipuncture to prevent complications.
Repeated venipuncture can sometimes cause bruising at the injection site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlebotomy - the act of drawing blood from a vein

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) + 'punctura' (a pricking).
