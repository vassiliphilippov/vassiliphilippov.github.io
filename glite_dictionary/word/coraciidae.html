Coraciidae

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌræsiˈaɪədi / (AmE), / kɒˌræsɪˈaɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored birds known as rollers, found in the Old World tropics and subtropics.
family of birds known as rollers

Examples

The Coraciidae are known for their vibrant plumage and acrobatic flight.
Birdwatchers in Africa often hope to spot members of the Coraciidae family during their trips.
The natural history museum displayed a rare specimen from the Coraciidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller - a brightly colored bird from the Coraciidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'korakias' (a bird, related to a crow).
