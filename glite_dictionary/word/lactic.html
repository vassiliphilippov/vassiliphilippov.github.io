lactic

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or obtained from milk, especially sour milk or whey.
relating to milk or milk products

Examples

During exercise, muscles produce lactic acid, which can cause fatigue.
Yogurt is made using lactic fermentation of milk by bacteria.
The lactic content in sour cream gives it a distinct tangy flavor.

Synset Examples

lactic acid
lactic fermentation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milky - resembling or containing milk
  • Degree Lower: acid - a substance with sour taste and corrosive properties; as in 'lactic acid'

Etymology

From Latin 'lac, lactis' (milk).
