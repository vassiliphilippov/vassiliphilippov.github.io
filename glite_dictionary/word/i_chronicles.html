I Chronicles

1. PROPN

/ aɪ ˈkrɑːnɪkəlz / (AmE), / aɪ ˈkrɒnɪkəlz / (BrE)
the first of two Old Testament books recounting the history of Judah and Israel up to the return from the Babylonian Captivity in 536 BC
first Old Testament book of Chronicles

Examples

Many scholars study I Chronicles to understand the history of ancient Israel.
The genealogy of King David can be found in I Chronicles.
In last night's Bible study, we read a passage from I Chronicles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II Chronicles - the second Old Testament book of Chronicles, continuing the history

Etymology

From Latin *Chronica*, from Greek *khronika* (annals, chronicles), meaning 'books of history'.
