to bury

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
to put something deeply into or under something so that it is hidden or covered
put deeply into or under something

Examples

She buried her face in her hands and wept silently.
The dog buried its bone deep in the garden.
He buried himself under a pile of blankets to keep warm.

Synset Examples

She sank her fingers into the soft sand
He buried his head in her lap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to fix firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass

2. VERB

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
to put something in the ground and cover it with soil
put in the ground and cover with soil

Examples

The pirates decided to bury their treasure on the remote island.
After the storm, we had to bury the broken fence posts in the soil.
Farmers sometimes bury seeds to help them sprout.

Synset Examples

They buried the stolen goods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inter - to bury a dead body, usually with formal ceremony

Etymology

Old English *byrgan* (to bury), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a grave or tomb, usually as part of a funeral ceremony
place a body in a grave or tomb

Examples

They decided to bury their beloved dog under the old oak tree in the garden.
The family gathered at the cemetery to bury their grandmother.
According to tradition, the pharaohs were buried with treasures for the afterlife.

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entomb - to place a dead body in a tomb
  • Antonym: to cremate - to dispose of a dead body by burning

Etymology

From Old English byrgan (to enclose, protect, bury).

6. VERB

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
to cover something completely so that it cannot be seen
cover so as to hide from sight

Examples

The little boy tried to bury his toy truck under a pile of leaves.
A thick layer of fog buried the city, making the skyline invisible.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide something so that it cannot be seen
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a covering so something becomes visible
