thwarting

1. NOUN

/ ˈθwɔɹɾɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθwɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of preventing someone from carrying out their plans or efforts
act of hindering or preventing

Examples

The security guard's quick action led to the thwarting of the attempted robbery.
Despite many thwarting of her plans, she remained determined to succeed.
Their persistent efforts faced constant thwarting from unexpected obstacles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or delay to something or someone
  • Synonym: frustration - the prevention of progress or success
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or facilitating

Etymology

From the verb 'thwart', which comes from Middle English 'thwarten' (to oppose, cross), from Old Norse 'thvert' (across).
