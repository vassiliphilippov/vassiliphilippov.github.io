noticeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
easy to see, hear, or recognize; able to be detected or observed.
able to be detected or observed

Examples

There has been a noticeable improvement in her performance this year.
The temperature dropped by a noticeable amount overnight.
After a noticeable pause, he answered the question carefully.

Synset Examples

after a noticeable pause the lecturer continued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detectable - capable of being discovered or identified
  • Antonym: imperceptible - impossible or very difficult to notice or detect

Etymology

From notice + -able; ultimately from Latin notitia (a being known) via Old French noter.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtɪsəbəl / (BrE)
Easy to see or observe; attracting attention or likely to be noticed.
easy to see or attract attention

Examples

There was a noticeable drop in temperature after the storm passed.
Her effort to remain calm was noticeable to everyone in the room.
The stain on his shirt became noticeable under bright light.

Synset Examples

noticeable shadows under her eyes
noticeable for its vivid historical background
a noticeable lack of friendliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspicuous - clearly visible; attracting attention
  • Antonym: inconspicuous - not clearly visible or attracting attention

Etymology

From 'notice' + '-able'. 'Notice' from Old French 'noticer', from Latin 'notitia' (a being known).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01289803-a
Synset POS: a
