archangel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrktʃˌeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːkˌeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
A biennial herb (Angelica archangelica) cultivated for its edible stems, which are candied, and for its roots, which have medicinal uses.
a plant with edible stems and medicinal roots

Examples

The candied stems of archangel are often used to decorate cakes and pastries.
Traditional remedies sometimes include the root of archangel for its supposed medicinal benefits.
Farmers in northern Europe have cultivated archangel for centuries.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *archangelica*, referring to the angelic properties once attributed to the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃˌeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃˌeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
a high-ranking angel, often considered to be above all other angels in the celestial hierarchy, especially in certain religious traditions
a high-ranking angel

Examples

The Bible mentions Michael as an archangel who leads the heavenly hosts.
According to tradition, each archangel has a specific role and responsibility.
Many artists have depicted the image of an archangel in religious paintings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: angel - a spiritual being acting as a messenger or servant of God

Etymology

From Greek 'archangelos' (chief angel), from 'archi-' (chief) + 'angelos' (messenger, angel).
