dingle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A small, sheltered valley or hollow, usually wooded.
small wooded valley or hollow

Examples

We followed a narrow path that led deep into the dingle at the edge of the forest.
The stream wound through the dingle, sheltered by tall trees and thick undergrowth.
Local wildlife thrives in the secluded dingle hidden behind the old farmhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dale - a broad valley, especially in northern England
  • Synonym: glen - a narrow valley, especially in Scotland or Ireland

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *dingle* (deep hollow), related to Old English *dyngel*, of uncertain origin.
