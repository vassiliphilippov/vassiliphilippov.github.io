manservant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌsɝː.vənt / (AmE), / ˈmænˌsɜː.vənt / (BrE)
A male servant who is employed to do personal or household work for someone.
male household servant

Examples

The wealthy gentleman employed a manservant to assist him with daily household tasks.
As the manservant entered the room, he discreetly announced his master's arrival.
During the Victorian era, it was common for noble families to have at least one manservant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maidservant - a female household servant

Etymology

Formed from 'man' + 'servant'.
