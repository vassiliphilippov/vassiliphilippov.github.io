Albula vulpes

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjuːlə ˈvʌlpiːz / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːlə ˈvʊlpiːz / (BrE)
a slender, silvery marine fish found in tropical mud flats and mangrove lagoons, also known as the bonefish
species of slender, silvery marine fish (bonefish)

Examples

Anglers travel to the Bahamas hoping to catch the elusive Albula vulpes in the shallow flats.
The diet of Albula vulpes mainly consists of small crustaceans and worms found in sandy bottoms.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Albula vulpes to help protect its habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonefish - a common name for Albula vulpes, a silvery game fish

Etymology

From Latin: 'albula' meaning 'whitish' and 'vulpes' meaning 'fox'.
