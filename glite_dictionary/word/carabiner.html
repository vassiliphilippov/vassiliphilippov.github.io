carabiner

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈbiːnər / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈbiːnə / (BrE)
a strong, oblong metal ring with a spring-loaded gate, used to quickly and reversibly connect components, especially in climbing and mountaineering
metal loop with a spring gate, used in climbing

Examples

Every climber must double-check that each carabiner is locked before beginning the ascent.
She attached the equipment bag to her harness with a sturdy carabiner.
Modern carabiners are designed to be both lightweight and extremely strong.

Etymology

From German 'Karabinerhaken', meaning 'spring hook for a carbine'.
