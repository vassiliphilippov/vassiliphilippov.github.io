conveyable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈveɪəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈveɪəbəl / (BrE)
(of property, rights, or goods) able to be transferred legally to the ownership of another person.
able to be legally transferred to another
legal

Examples

The land is conveyable under the current estate laws.
Only conveyable assets can be included in the transfer to the trust.
The judge ruled that the property was not conveyable due to an unresolved dispute.

Synset Examples

negotiable bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transferable - able to be transferred to another person or thing

Etymology

From 'convey' + '-able', with 'convey' from Old French 'conveier', from Late Latin 'conviare' (to go along with).
