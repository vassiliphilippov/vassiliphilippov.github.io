seedwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈsiːdˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who sells seeds
female dealer in seeds

Examples

The seedwoman visited the village every spring, bringing packets of rare flowers and crops.
As a successful seedwoman, she was known for her extensive knowledge of horticulture.
The old seedwoman set up her stall at the market, selling seeds to local gardeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seedman - a man who sells seeds

Etymology

Formed from 'seed' + 'woman', modeled after similar terms such as 'saleswoman'.
