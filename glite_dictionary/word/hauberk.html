hauberk

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːbərk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːbək / (BrE)
a long tunic of chain mail, usually without sleeves, worn as defensive armor in the Middle Ages
long chain mail tunic used as armor

Examples

The knight donned his hauberk before heading into battle.
A well-crafted hauberk could protect its wearer from sword blows and arrows.
Museum exhibits include a medieval hauberk found at an archaeological site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - a type of armor consisting of small metal rings linked together

Etymology

From Old French 'hauberc', of Germanic origin; related to Old High German 'halbarg' (coat of mail).
