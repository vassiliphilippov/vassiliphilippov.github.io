net

1. NOUN

/ nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt / (BrE)
a piece of meshed fabric used as a trap for catching fish, birds, or insects
meshed trap for catching animals

Examples

The fisherman repaired his net before heading out to sea.
Farmers often use a net to keep birds away from their crops.
We found a butterfly caught in a net near the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device or enclosure designed to catch and retain animals

Etymology

From Old English *net*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *net* and German *Netz*.
