Gyromitra infula

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˈɪnfjələ / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˈɪnfjʊlə / (BrE)
A species of poisonous fungus with a saddle-shaped, dull yellow to brown cap that is relatively even.
a poisonous saddle-shaped fungus species

Examples

Many mushroom foragers avoid picking Gyromitra infula due to its toxicity.
The Gyromitra infula has a distinctive saddle-shaped cap that can be confused with edible species.
After consuming Gyromitra infula, several hikers experienced severe poisoning symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a group of fungi similar in appearance to true morels, often poisonous

Etymology

From the genus name Gyromitra, Latinized from Greek 'gyros' (circle) + 'mitra' (headband), and species epithet 'infula', Latin for a band or fillet.
