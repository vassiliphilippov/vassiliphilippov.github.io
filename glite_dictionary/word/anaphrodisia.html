anaphrodisia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ə.froʊˈdɪʒ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.frəʊˈdɪz.i.ə / (BrE)
A decline or absence of sexual desire.
lack of sexual desire
medical

Examples

The patient's sudden anaphrodisia concerned both her and her partner.
Some medications can lead to anaphrodisia as a side effect.
Therapy can help individuals experiencing anaphrodisia understand possible psychological factors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphrodisiac - a substance that reduces sexual desire
  • Antonym: libido - sexual desire or drive

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'aphrodisios' (pertaining to Aphrodite, goddess of love).
