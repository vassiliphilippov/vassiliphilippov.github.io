tired

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
feeling a need to rest or sleep because of a lack of strength or energy
feeling a need to rest; lacking energy

Examples

After a long day at work, she felt completely tired and ready for bed.
The tired children fell asleep in the car on the way home.
I am too tired to go out tonight, so I'll stay in and rest.

Synset Examples

tired mothers with crying babies
too tired to eat

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: exhausted - extremely tired; very low on energy
  • Antonym: rested - feeling refreshed after resting

Etymology

From Old English 'tēorian' (to become weary).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
(of words, ideas, or expressions) lacking freshness or originality; used so often as to become boring or uninteresting.
overused; lacking originality

Examples

The author's writing is full of tired clichés and stock phrases.
Critics complained about the tired plot that offered nothing new to audiences.
She wanted to avoid using tired metaphors in her speech.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality; boring due to overuse
  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) overused and unoriginal
  • Antonym: fresh - (of ideas or expressions) new and original
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01693210-s
Synset POS: s
