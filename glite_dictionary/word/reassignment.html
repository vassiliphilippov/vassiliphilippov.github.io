reassignment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of assigning someone or something to a different duty, role, or position
assignment to a different duty or role

Examples

After the merger, her reassignment to a new department was announced.
The company policy allows for the reassignment of employees during restructuring.
Following his reassignment, he had to relocate to another city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assignment - the act of assigning a task or duty

Etymology

From re- (again) + assignment.
