loosely

1. ADV

/ ˈluːsli / (AmE), / ˈluːsli / (BrE)
In a way that is not firmly fixed or tightly controlled; in a loose manner.
in a loose or relaxed way

Examples

The idea is loosely based on true events but includes fictional elements.
She tied the scarf loosely around her neck for a casual look.
The team members are loosely connected, working independently on different projects.

Synset Examples

a union of loosely federated states

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightly - in a firm or secure manner
  • Synonym: freely - without constraint or restriction

Etymology

From 'loose' + '-ly'; 'loose' from Middle English *los*, from Old Norse *lauss* (free, loose).

2. ADV

/ ˈluːsli / (AmE), / ˈluːsli / (BrE)
In a way that is not firmly or tightly fixed; in a relaxed or free manner.
in a relaxed or untight way

Examples

The scarf was draped loosely around her neck.
He held the leash loosely, letting the dog walk ahead.
The books were stacked loosely on the shelf, ready to be rearranged.

Synset Examples

his hands lay loosely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightly - in a firm or secure manner; opposite of loosely

Etymology

From 'loose' + '-ly', with 'loose' from Middle English, from Old Norse 'lauss'.

3. ADV

/ ˈluːsli / (AmE), / ˈluːsli / (BrE)
In a way that is open in structure, not compact, or not tightly packed or connected.
in an open or not tightly packed way

Examples

The fabric was loosely woven, making it light and airy.
Several families formed a loosely organized community in the countryside.
She piled her hair up loosely and let a few strands fall around her face.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightly - in a way that is firmly fixed or closely packed

4. ADV

/ ˈluːsli / (AmE), / ˈluːsli / (BrE)
In a way that is not strict, exact, or limited; without regard to precise details or exceptions.
without strict attention to details

Examples

The rules were interpreted loosely to allow for individual circumstances.
She used the term loosely because it did not exactly fit the situation.
We define 'success' loosely here, to include many different kinds of achievements.

Synset Examples

he interprets the law broadly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadly - in a general way, without considering details
  • Antonym: strictly - with strict attention to detail or exceptions
