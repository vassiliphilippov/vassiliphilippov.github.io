EAS

1. NOUN

/ iː eɪ ɛs / (AmE), / iː eɪ ɛs / (BrE)
abbreviation for Emergency Alert System, a federal warning system in the United States that can be used by FEMA and the President to broadcast emergency alerts nationwide.
US federal emergency broadcast system (abbreviation)
american

Examples

The EAS interrupted every channel with a warning about the approaching hurricane.
School officials tested the EAS as part of their emergency preparedness drill.
The President's message was broadcast nationwide through the EAS system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Emergency Alert System - the full name for EAS, the US emergency broadcast system
  • Synonym: Amber Alert - other US public emergency broadcast alert for missing children

Etymology

Acronym of Emergency Alert System.
