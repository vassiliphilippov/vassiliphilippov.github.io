showplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌpleɪs / (BrE)
a place that is often exhibited and visited for its historical interest or natural beauty
notable place, often exhibited

Examples

The mansion has become a famous showplace for its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
The city park is a showplace that attracts thousands of visitors each year.
Many old castles are preserved as showplaces highlighting the region's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - something that draws visitors or attention

Etymology

Formed from 'show' + 'place', indicating a place meant to be shown off or displayed.
