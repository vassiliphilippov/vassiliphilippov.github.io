Cryptocoryne

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈkɔːriːni / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈkɒrɪniː / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic herbs with broad leaves and long, narrow spathes, commonly used as aquarium plants
genus of aquatic herbs used in aquariums

Examples

Many aquarists enjoy growing Cryptocoryne for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
The leaves of Cryptocoryne tend to melt when first introduced but usually regrow stronger.
Different species of Cryptocoryne can display a wide variety of leaf shapes and colors.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'koryne' (club), referring to the concealed spadix.
