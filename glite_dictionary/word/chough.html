chough

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌf / (AmE), / tʃʌf / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized European crow with red legs and glossy black feathers
a European crow with red legs and black plumage

Examples

A solitary chough perched on the windswept cliff, surveying the coastline below.
The chough is easily identified by its bright red legs and curved bill.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the chough population in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - a large, mostly black bird of the family Corvidae

Etymology

From Old English *ceo (chough), related to Welsh *cawg* (jackdaw).
