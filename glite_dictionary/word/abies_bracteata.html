Abies bracteata

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˌbrækˈtiː.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.biː.iːz ˌbrækˈtiː.ə.tə / (BrE)
A pyramidal fir tree native to southwestern California, noted for its spiny pointed leaves and cone scales with long spines.
fir tree species native to California

Examples

The Abies bracteata is one of the rarest firs, found only in the Santa Lucia Mountains.
Botanists are working to preserve the limited populations of Abies bracteata in California.
The distinctive spiny cones make Abies bracteata easy to identify among other native trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - any tree of the genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin: Abies (fir) and bracteata (having bracts).
