Centaurea scabiosa

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə skəˈbioʊsə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə skəˈbɪəʊsə / (BrE)
A tall European perennial plant with purple flower heads, commonly known as Greater Knapweed.
tall European plant with purple flowers

Examples

In the wildflower meadow, several Centaurea scabiosa plants attracted a variety of butterflies.
Gardeners often plant Centaurea scabiosa to support local pollinators and add vibrant color.
The distinctive purple heads of Centaurea scabiosa make it easy to identify among the grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greater Knapweed - common name for Centaurea scabiosa

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Centaurea and species name scabiosa, referring to scabious-like (resembling the genus Scabiosa).
