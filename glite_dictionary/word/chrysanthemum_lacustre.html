Chrysanthemum lacustre

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəməm ləˈkʌstrə / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθəməm ləˈkʌstrə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to Portugal, closely resembling the oxeye daisy.
a perennial Portuguese plant, like oxeye daisy

Examples

The botanical garden recently planted Chrysanthemum lacustre in its Mediterranean section.
Many gardeners value Chrysanthemum lacustre for its daisy-like flowers and resilience.
Unlike some chrysanthemums, Chrysanthemum lacustre can thrive in the damp soil near lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxeye daisy - a common daisy with white petals and a yellow center

Etymology

From Latin; 'chrysanthemum' meaning 'golden flower' and 'lacustre' meaning 'of lakes'.
