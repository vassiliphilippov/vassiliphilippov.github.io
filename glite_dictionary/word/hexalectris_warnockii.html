Hexalectris warnockii

1. NOUN

/ hɛk.səˈlɛk.trɪs wɔːrˈnɒki.aɪ / (AmE), / hɛk.səˈlɛk.trɪs wɔːˈnɒk.i.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of orchid with slender, nearly leafless reddish-brown stems and loose racemes of reddish-brown flowers, native to open brushy woods in southeastern Arizona and central Texas.
a species of reddish-brown orchid

Examples

The rare Hexalectris warnockii blooms each summer in the woodlands of central Texas.
Botanists discovered several clusters of Hexalectris warnockii during their field survey.
Because Hexalectris warnockii is a leafless orchid, it relies on symbiotic fungi for nutrients.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek hexa (six) + lektris (couch); species named after American botanist Barton Warnock.
