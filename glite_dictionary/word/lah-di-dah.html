lah-di-dah

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɑː di ˈdɑː / (AmE), / ˌlɑː di ˈdɑː / (BrE)
Pretending to be refined or fancy in a way that seems affected or insincere; pretentious or snobbish.
affectedly fancy or pretentious
informal british

Examples

Everyone thought his lah-di-dah accent was rather exaggerated for the small village wedding.
The restaurant had a lah-di-dah atmosphere, but the food was average at best.
She adopted a lah-di-dah tone when discussing her holiday plans.

Synset Examples

She always acted so lah-di-dah at fancy parties.
His lah-di-dah attitude annoyed the down-to-earth guests.
They spoke in a lah-di-dah manner that felt out of touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - Trying to appear more important or clever than you are
  • Antonym: modest - Not trying to impress others; humble

Etymology

Imitative of affected, genteel speech; origin in late 19th-century British English.
