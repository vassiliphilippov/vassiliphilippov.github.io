to consume

1. VERB

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to eat or drink something completely, often with great appetite or enthusiasm
eat or drink completely

Examples

The children managed to consume an entire cake within a few minutes.
After the long hike, she consumed her dinner with remarkable speed.
The group consumed all the snacks before the movie even started.

Synset Examples

Some people can down a pound of meat in the course of one meal
The teenagers demolished four pizzas among them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly

Etymology

From Latin consumere, from con- (altogether) + sumere (to take up, consume).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01199565-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to eat, drink, or otherwise take (something, especially food or drink) into the body, often regularly or as a habit
eat, drink, or regularly take in

Examples

Doctors recommend that adults consume at least five servings of fruits and vegetables every day.
Some people consume caffeine regularly in the form of coffee or tea.
Children should not consume too many sugary snacks throughout the week.

Synset Examples

Have another bowl of chicken soup!
I don't take sugar in my coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ingest - to take food or drink into the body by swallowing or absorbing it
  • Antonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid consuming or partaking in something

Etymology

From Latin consumere (to use up, eat, waste), from com- (altogether) + sumere (to take).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01159300-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
(of an activity, feeling, or influence) to completely fill or occupy someone's attention, time, or energy; to engross fully.
to absorb or occupy fully

Examples

The project consumed all her free time for several months.
Grief consumed him after the loss of his friend.
He was so consumed by his passion for art that he forgot about everything else.

Synset Examples

The effort to pass the exam consumed all his energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engross - to absorb all the attention or interest of someone
  • Synonym: to preoccupy - to dominate or fill the mind to the exclusion of other thoughts

Etymology

From Latin 'consumere', from 'con-' (altogether) + 'sumere' (to take up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00602298-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kənˈsum / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to use up a resource, material, or supply; to expend until nothing is left
use up resources or materials

Examples

The factory consumes vast amounts of electricity during peak hours.
If we consume all of our natural resources at this rate, we may run out soon.
This old heating system consumes more fuel than modern, energy-efficient models.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expend - use up or spend a resource or material
  • Synonym: to deplete - reduce the quantity or amount of something, especially resources
  • Antonym: conserve - protect and prevent the waste or overuse of a resource

Etymology

From Latin consumere, from com- (altogether) + sumere (to take).

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to destroy completely, especially by fire, disease, or another force
destroy completely

Examples

The wildfire consumed thousands of acres of forest in just two days.
His passion for revenge consumed him until there was nothing left of his former self.
The old manuscript was consumed by mold and decay in the damp cellar.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Latin consumere, from com- (altogether) + sumere (take up).
