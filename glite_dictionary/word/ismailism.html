Ismailism

1. NOUN

/ ɪsˈmɑːɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪsˈmeɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
a branch of Shia Islam known for its esoteric interpretation of religious texts and philosophy
branch of Shia Islam with esoteric philosophy

Examples

Ismailism emphasizes the symbolic and allegorical interpretation of the Quran.
The Aga Khan is the spiritual leader of the Nizari branch of Ismailism.
Many important philosophical works were produced under the influence of Ismailism in medieval Persia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Shiism - the branch of Islam that regards Ali and his descendants as the rightful successors of Muhammad
  • Degree Lower: Twelver - the largest branch of Shia Islam, also known as Ithna'ashariyya
  • Antonym: Sunni Islam - the largest branch of Islam, differing in succession beliefs from Shia branches

Etymology

From the name Ismāʿīl, the seventh Shia Imam, with the suffix -ism.
