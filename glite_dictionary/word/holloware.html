holloware

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːloʊˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌweə / (BrE)
dishes such as bowls, teapots, and sugar basins, especially those made of silver or silver plate, used for serving food.
serving dishes, often silver, such as bowls or teapots

Examples

The antique silver holloware was displayed in the dining room cabinet.
They polished the holloware before setting the table for the formal dinner.
Her collection of Victorian holloware includes teapots, serving bowls, and creamers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flatware - cutlery such as knives, forks, and spoons

Etymology

From 'hollow' (having an empty space inside) + 'ware' (goods or articles), originally used to distinguish from flatware.
