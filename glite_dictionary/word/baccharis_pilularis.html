Baccharis pilularis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈkɛərɪs pɪˈluːlərɪs / (AmE), / bəˈkɑːrɪs pɪˈljuːlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A widely spreading evergreen shrub of the southwestern United States, with flower heads arranged in a leafy panicle; commonly known as coyote brush.
a spreading evergreen shrub of the southwestern US

Examples

Baccharis pilularis is commonly found along coastal slopes and hillsides in California.
The garden featured native plants such as Baccharis pilularis to provide habitat for local wildlife.
Researchers studied the fire resistance of Baccharis pilularis in comparison to other shrub species.

Etymology

From Latin Baccharis (a plant genus) + pilularis, meaning 'bearing little balls,' referring to the rounded flower heads.
