Rheum rhaponticum

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːəm ræˈpɒntɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈruːəm ræˈpɒntɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of rhubarb cultivated for its edible stems, often used in pies, sauces, or preserves.
species of edible rhubarb

Examples

The pie was filled with the tart stalks of Rheum rhaponticum fresh from the garden.
Botanists have long debated the origins of cultivated Rheum rhaponticum varieties.
Many preserves are made using Rheum rhaponticum harvested in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - A plant with edible stalks, often cooked in desserts

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Rheum' is the genus name for rhubarb, and 'rhaponticum' possibly derives from Greek 'rha pontikon', meaning 'rhubarb of the Black Sea'.
