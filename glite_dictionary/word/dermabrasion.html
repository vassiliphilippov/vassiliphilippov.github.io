dermabrasion

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜrm.əˈbreɪ.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌdɜː.məˈbreɪ.ʒən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which the outer layers of skin are removed, usually to treat scars or skin imperfections
removal of outer skin layers
medical

Examples

After severe acne, she underwent dermabrasion to smooth her skin.
Dermabrasion is often recommended for reducing the appearance of facial scars.
The dermatologist explained the risks and benefits of dermabrasion before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exfoliation - the process of removing dead skin cells from the skin surface
  • Synonym: abrasion - an area damaged by scraping or rubbing

Etymology

From Latin 'derma' (skin) and 'abrasio' (scraping off).
