coccal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a coccus, especially a spherical bacterium
resembling or relating to a coccus
medical

Examples

The laboratory identified several coccal forms under the microscope.
Some coccal bacteria are responsible for serious infections in humans.
The doctor explained that the infection was caused by a coccal organism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coccus - a spherical bacterium

Etymology

From Greek *kokkos* (berry, grain), referring to the round shape of certain bacteria.
