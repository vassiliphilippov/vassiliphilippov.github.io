accompaniment

1. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpənimənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənimənt / (BrE)
a musical part, vocal or instrumental, that supports or provides a background for other musical parts
supporting musical part

Examples

The piano accompaniment enhanced the beauty of the soloist's performance.
She played a gentle guitar accompaniment while her friend sang.
The choir sang the hymn with an organ accompaniment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - musical backing for another part

Etymology

From French accompagnement, from accompagner (to accompany), from Late Latin accompagnare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07045239-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpənimənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənɪmənt / (BrE)
an event or situation that happens at the same time as or in connection with another
something occurring together with something else

Examples

The spicy salsa served as a flavorful accompaniment to the main dish.
His nervousness was a constant accompaniment to his public speaking engagements.
Thunder was the only accompaniment to the pounding rain outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concomitant - a thing that naturally accompanies or is associated with something else

Etymology

From French 'accompagnement', from accompagner (to accompany).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07299259-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpənɪmənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of accompanying someone or something, especially in order to protect or assist them
the act of going with to protect

Examples

The ambassador travelled with an accompaniment of security personnel.
A police accompaniment was provided for the celebrity during her visit.
He refused to leave the country without an armed accompaniment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person or group accompanying another for protection or guidance

Etymology

From French accompagnement, from accompagner (to accompany).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00829443-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpənɪmənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənɪmənt / (BrE)
something that is added to complement, enhance, or complete something else, especially food or drink served with a meal
something that complements or completes another thing

Examples

The cheese platter was served as an accompaniment to the wine tasting session.
He played soft music as an accompaniment to the romantic dinner.
A green salad makes a refreshing accompaniment to grilled chicken.
