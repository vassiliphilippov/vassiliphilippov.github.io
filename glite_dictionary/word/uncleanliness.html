uncleanliness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈklinli.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkliːnlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being not clean, especially lacking clean habits
lack of cleanliness; dirtiness

Examples

The uncleanliness of the kitchen made it unfit for preparing food.
Some diseases can spread quickly in environments of uncleanliness.
Her mother always stressed that uncleanliness leads to poor health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state or quality of being clean
  • Synonym: dirtiness - the state of being dirty

Etymology

From un- (not) + cleanliness, modeled after clean.
