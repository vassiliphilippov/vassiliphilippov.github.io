shrink

1. VERB

/ ʃrɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk / (BrE)
to become or make something smaller in size, usually as a result of heat, moisture, or physical compression
become or make smaller in size

Examples

If you wash it in hot water, the sweater may shrink considerably.
The company needs to shrink this image before uploading it to the website.
His jeans have shrunk after several washes.

Synset Examples

Hot water will shrink the sweater
Can you shrink this image?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to become or make larger in size

Etymology

From Old English scrincan (to contract, shrivel).
