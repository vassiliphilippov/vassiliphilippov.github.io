eventual

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈvɛntʃuəl / (AmE), / ɪˈvɛntjʊəl / (BrE)
happening at the end of a process or as a final result, especially after a period of time or series of events
happening as a final result

Examples

The committee discussed the eventual outcome of the negotiations.
Many people are saving for their eventual retirement.
The company hopes for the eventual release of the new product next year.

Synset Examples

hope of eventual (or ultimate) rescue
if this trend continues it is not reasonable to expect the eventual collapse of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate - being or happening at the end of a process; final

Etymology

From Medieval Latin eventualis (happening as a result), from Latin eventus (event).
