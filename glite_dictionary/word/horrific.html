horrific

1. ADJ

/ həˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / hɒˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
causing great fear, shock, or horror; extremely disturbing or frightening
causing fear or horror

Examples

The film was so horrific that several people left the theater early.
Survivors gave horrific accounts of the disaster to the press.
The crime scene revealed a horrific level of violence.

Etymology

2. ADJ

/ həˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / hɒˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Extremely shocking or disturbing; causing horror or disgust.
causing horror or disgust

Examples

The news article described the horrific aftermath of the earthquake in vivid detail.
Witnesses spoke about the horrific injuries caused by the explosion.
She could not sleep after seeing a horrific accident on the highway.

Synset Examples

subjected to outrageous cruelty
a hideous pattern of injustice
horrific conditions in the mining industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - very unpleasant, causing horror
  • Synonym: terrifying - causing great fear or terror
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Latin 'horrificus', from 'horrere' (to shudder) + '-ficus' (making).
