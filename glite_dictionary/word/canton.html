canton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɒn / (BrE)
a small territorial division of a country, especially one that functions as an administrative or political unit
administrative division of a country

Examples

Switzerland is divided into 26 cantons, each with its own constitution and government.
The canton system allowed for decentralization of power within the country.
After moving to the French-speaking canton of Geneva, he quickly learned the language.

Etymology

From French 'canton', from Italian 'cantone' (corner, district).

2. VERB

/ ˈkæn.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɒn / (BrE)
to assign or provide lodging, especially for soldiers, in a particular place
house military personnel

Examples

The general decided to canton the troops in several nearby villages for the winter.
Authorities will canton the regiment in secure barracks during the training period.
After marching all day, the commander cantoned his soldiers in local farmhouses for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter - to house soldiers, especially in civilian buildings

Etymology

From French 'cantonner', from 'canton' (corner, district), originally from Italian 'cantone'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02659254-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkæn.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɒn / (BrE)
(of a country or region) to divide or be divided into cantons (administrative districts).
divide into administrative districts (cantons)

Examples

Following the civil war, leaders decided to canton the territory to better administer resources.
The nation was cantoned into several autonomous regions, each with its own local government.
Debates continue about whether to canton the province along linguistic lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partition - to divide into parts or sections

Etymology

From French 'canton', meaning 'corner' or 'district'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02475019-v
Synset POS: v
