viscosimetric

1. ADJ

/ vɪˌskoʊsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / vɪˌskɒsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the measurement of viscosity
related to viscosity measurement

Examples

The viscosimetric method is commonly used to determine the molecular weight of polymers.
Researchers analyzed the fluid's properties using viscosimetric measurements.
Accurate viscosimetric data are essential for quality control in the chemical industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscosity - the thickness or resistance to flow of a liquid

Etymology

Formed from 'viscosity' (from Latin 'viscositas' meaning 'stickiness') and the suffix '-metric' (Greek 'metron' meaning 'measure').
