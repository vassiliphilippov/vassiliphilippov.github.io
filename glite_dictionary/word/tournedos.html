tournedos

1. NOUN

/ tʊr.nəˈdoʊ(z) / (AmE), / tʊə.nəˈdəʊ(z) / (BrE)
A thick, small round steak cut from the beef tenderloin, typically used in French cuisine.
thick steak from beef tenderloin

Examples

The chef prepared the tournedos with a rich red wine sauce for the guests.
Classic tournedos are often wrapped in bacon and served with a slice of foie gras.
I ordered tournedos at the French restaurant because I love tender cuts of beef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filet mignon - small, tender cut of beef from the end of the tenderloin

Etymology

From French 'tournedos', meaning 'turned back' or a reference to the way the steak is cooked or presented.
