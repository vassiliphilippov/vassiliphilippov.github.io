to focalize

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to concentrate or direct attention, activity, or an effect on a particular place or spot, especially within a scientific or medical context
concentrate on a particular spot

Examples

The disease tends to focalize in the lungs before spreading.
Researchers observed that the magnetic field began to focalize at the center of the chamber.
The artist decided to focalize the lighting on the main subject of the painting.

Synset Examples

The infection has localized in the left eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort on something
  • Antonym: diffuse - to spread over a wide area or among a large number of people

Etymology

From French 'focaliser', from 'focal' (of a focus), from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace, later 'point of convergence').

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring into focus or become focused
to become or make focused

Examples

As we adjusted the telescope, the distant star gradually focalized into view.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring (an image) into focus, especially by adjusting the lens of a camera or optical instrument
bring an image into focus

Examples

The photographer needed to focalize the image before taking the picture.
Adjust the lens to focalize the view on the distant mountains.
It took a moment to focalize the blurry scene on the screen.

Synset Examples

Please focus the image: we cannot enjoy the movie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to adjust an optical device so an image becomes clear

Etymology

From French 'focaliser', from 'foyer' (focus) + '-iser' (-ize).

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)

to bring into focus or alignment

Examples

With precise adjustments, the engineer managed to focalize the beam of light onto a single point.
