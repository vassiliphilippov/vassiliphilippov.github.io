astraddle

1. ADV

/ əˈstræd.əl / (AmE), / əˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
Sitting or positioned with one leg on each side of something.
with legs on either side

Examples

The child sat astraddle the fence, watching the parade go by.
She mounted the horse astraddle, ready to ride away.
He placed the board astraddle the gap so they could cross.

Synset Examples

she sat astride the chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astride - with a leg on each side

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' (on, in) + 'straddle' (sit or stand with legs apart).
