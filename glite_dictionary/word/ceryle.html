Ceryle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.iːl / (AmE), / ˈsɪər.aɪl / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the kingfisher family, Alcedinidae, including the belted kingfisher and related species.
genus of birds in the kingfisher family

Examples

The Ceryle genus includes several species of strikingly colored kingfishers.
Observers noted that the Ceryle species displayed unique diving behaviors.
The belted kingfisher is classified under the Ceryle genus by ornithologists.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, originally from Greek *kērulos* (a kind of bird, possibly the kingfisher).
