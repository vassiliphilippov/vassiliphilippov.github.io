grumbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
a complaint expressed in a low, indistinct, or muttered tone, often showing discontent
low, indistinct complaint

Examples

There was a quiet grumbling in the audience as the speaker continued past his allotted time.
His constant grumbling about the weather annoyed everyone around him.
Despite some grumbling, the new policy was eventually accepted by most employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07225752-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) continuous, full, and low-pitched; resembling a persistent rumble or growl.
continuous, low-pitched throbbing sound

Examples

The storm brought a grumbling noise that seemed to shake the windows all night.
We listened to the distant, grumbling thunder as the rain began to fall.
A grumbling sound came from the old engine as it started up.

Synset Examples

the rumbling rolling sound of thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumbling - making a deep, continuous resonant sound

Etymology

Present participle of 'grumble', from Middle English 'grumbelen'.
