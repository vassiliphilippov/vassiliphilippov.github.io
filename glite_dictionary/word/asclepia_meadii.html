Asclepia meadii

1. NOUN

/ æsˈkliːpiə miˈaɪdaɪ / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpɪə miˈaɪdaɪ / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to central North America, known as Mead's milkweed, and classified as threatened.
a threatened milkweed species of central North America

Examples

The conservation of Asclepia meadii is critical for local butterfly populations.
Botanists have documented several new occurrences of Asclepia meadii in restored prairies.
Efforts to reintroduce Asclepia meadii focus on preserving its natural grassland habitat.

Etymology

From genus name Asclepia, after the Greek god Asclepius, and species named for Samuel Barnum Mead, an American naturalist.
