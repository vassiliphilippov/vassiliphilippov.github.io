mugful

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡ.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡ.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount or quantity that fills a mug.
the amount a mug holds

Examples

She poured herself a mugful of hot chocolate and settled by the fireplace.
Every morning, he drinks a mugful of strong coffee to wake up.
The recipe calls for a mugful of warm milk to mix with the dough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the quantity that can be held in a cup
  • Degree Lower: teaspoonful - the quantity that can be held in a teaspoon

Etymology

From 'mug' (a drinking vessel) + 'ful' (full of, as much as a container holds).
