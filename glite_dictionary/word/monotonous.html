monotonous

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑtə­nəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒtənəs / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) sounded or spoken in a single, unchanging tone or pitch; dull and repetitive.
unvarying in tone or pitch

Examples

Her monotonous voice made the lecture difficult to follow.
The monotonous hum of the air conditioner put him to sleep.
He spoke in such a monotonous tone that nobody paid attention.

Synset Examples

the owl's faint monotonous hooting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drone - a low, continuous, unchanging sound
  • Antonym: varied - showing change or variety in tone, pitch, or character

Etymology

From Greek *monotonos* (of one tone), from *monos* (alone) + *tonos* (tone).

2. ADJ

/ məˈnɑː.t̬ə.nəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒt.ən.əs / (BrE)
dull and uninteresting because of being repetitious or lacking in variety; tedious.
lacking in variety; tedious and repetitive

Examples

The lecture became increasingly monotonous and the students struggled to stay awake.
He found his daily routine at the office painfully monotonous.
She tried to break up the monotonous pattern of her days by going for walks.

Synset Examples

a humdrum existence: all work and no play
nothing is so monotonous as the sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
  • Antonym: varied - incorporating a number of different types or elements; showing variation or variety

Etymology

From Late Latin 'monotonus' from Greek 'monotonos', from 'monos' (single) + 'tonos' (tone).
