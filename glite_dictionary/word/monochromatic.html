monochromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnoʊkrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
Consisting of or involving only one color or hue; involving or emitting light of a single wavelength.
having only one color or wavelength

Examples

The artist created a monochromatic painting using only shades of blue.
In physics, a monochromatic light source emits light of a single wavelength.
She chose a monochromatic color scheme for her minimalist living room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychromatic - having many colors or wavelengths

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'khrōma' (color).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmoʊnəkrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of light or other electromagnetic radiation) having only one wavelength.
having a single wavelength (of light)

Examples

Lasers often produce monochromatic light, making them very useful for experiments requiring a single wavelength.
The scientist used a filter to isolate monochromatic radiation for the test.
Sodium vapor lamps emit nearly monochromatic light, which is why everything looks yellow under them.

Synset Examples

monochromatic light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychromatic - (of light) having several wavelengths or colors

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'khrōmatikos' (relating to color).

3. ADJ

/ ˌmɑnəˌkroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˌkrəʊˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Consisting of or displaying only a single color or hue.
having one color only

Examples

The artist's new series features a monochromatic palette of deep blues.
She decorated her living room in a monochromatic scheme, using only shades of gray.
The monochromatic appearance of the building made it look both modern and minimalist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychromatic - having many colors
  • Synonym: monochrome - an image or design in one color

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + khrōmatikos (relating to color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00399877-s
Synset POS: s
