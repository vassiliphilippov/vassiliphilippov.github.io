semilegality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌliˈgælɪti / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌliˈgælɪti / (BrE)
The condition of being only partly lawful or of uncertain legality.
state of partial or doubtful legality

Examples

The company's operations exist in a zone of semilegality, neither fully authorized nor expressly banned.
Living in a state of semilegality makes it difficult for these workers to access public services.
Concerns about the semilegality of gambling websites have prompted recent regulatory changes.

Synset Examples

Farmers who are allowed by the provincial administration to live in semi-legality in the forest reserves are even more vulnerable to the arbitrary exercise of government power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legality - the quality or state of being in accordance with the law
  • Antonym: illegality - the state of being contrary to or forbidden by law

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (half, partly) + 'legality'.
