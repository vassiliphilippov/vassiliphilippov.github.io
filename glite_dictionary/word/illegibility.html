illegibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛdʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛdʒəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of handwriting or print that is impossible or difficult to read
the state of being impossible to read

Examples

The illegibility of his signature caused confusion at the bank.
Many prescriptions are rejected due to the illegibility of doctors' handwriting.
She rewrote the essay because her teacher complained about its illegibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegible - impossible or difficult to read (typically of writing)
  • Antonym: legibility - the quality of writing being easy to read

Etymology

From Latin 'illegibilis' (not readable), from 'in-' (not) + 'legibilis' (that can be read).
