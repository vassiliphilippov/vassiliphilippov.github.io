chorioretinitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrioʊˌrɛtəˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪəʊˌrɛtɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the choroid and retina of the eye, often leading to vision problems
inflammation of the choroid and retina
medical

Examples

The patient developed chorioretinitis after a severe infection.
Ocular toxoplasmosis is a common cause of chorioretinitis worldwide.
Prompt treatment of chorioretinitis is essential to prevent vision loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retinitis - inflammation of the retina
  • Degree Higher: uveitis - inflammation of the uvea (iris, ciliary body, choroid) of the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'chorio-' (membrane) + 'retina' + '-itis' (inflammation).
