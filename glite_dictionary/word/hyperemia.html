hyperemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈimiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈriːmɪə / (BrE)
an excess of blood in a particular organ or body part, often due to increased blood flow
excess blood in a body part
medical

Examples

The redness of the skin was a result of hyperemia following the injury.
Doctors noted hyperemia in the affected limb after the operation.
Chronic hyperemia can be a sign of underlying circulatory disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ischemia - an inadequate blood supply to a part of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper' (over) + 'haima' (blood).
