clutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌtə / (BrE)
unwanted echoes or signals that interfere with the observation of target objects on a radar screen
unwanted signals on radar

Examples

The radar operator had difficulty identifying aircraft due to excessive clutter on the screen.
Weather patterns often produce clutter that obscures important targets on radar displays.
Advanced filtering techniques help reduce the impact of clutter in radar systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noise - random unwanted signals that interfere with the intended signal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07278413-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈklʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌtə / (BrE)
A collection of things lying around in an untidy or disorganized state.
untidy collection of things

Examples

The garage was filled with so much clutter that I could barely move.
She spent the weekend clearing out the clutter from her desk.
Too much clutter in your workspace can make it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a state of disorder or untidiness
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged in a neat and tidy way

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'clotter' (to clot or coagulate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14524151-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈklʌtər / (AmE), / ˈklʌtə / (BrE)
to fill or cover a space with things in a disorderly or untidy way
fill a space in a disorderly way

Examples

The kitchen counters were cluttered with unwashed dishes and grocery bags.
Please don't clutter up your desk with unnecessary papers.
Old magazines and books cluttered the tiny living room, making it hard to move around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - to fill with a large number of things or people
  • Antonym: clear - to remove items to make a space empty or tidy

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'clotter' meaning 'to clot or thicken', related to 'clot'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00182020-v
Synset POS: v
