bracted

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræktɪd / (AmE), / ˈbræktɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant or part) having bracts; possessing small, modified leaves (bracts) associated with flowers or inflorescences.
having bracts (botany)

Examples

The bracted blossoms of the plant make it easily recognizable in the field.
Many wildflowers produce bracted stems to protect their delicate flowers.
Some species are distinguished by their conspicuously bracted inflorescences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebracted - lacking bracts; without bracts

Etymology

From bract + -ed (forming adjectives).
