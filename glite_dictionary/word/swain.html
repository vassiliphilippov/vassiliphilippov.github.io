swain

1. NOUN

/ sweɪn / (AmE), / sweɪn / (BrE)
a man who is the lover of a woman; a suitor or sweetheart, especially in a poetic or old-fashioned context
a male lover; suitor or sweetheart (archaic, poetic)
archaic

Examples

In the old ballads, a hopeful swain would serenade his beloved beneath her window.
She laughed at the flattery of her devoted swain during the spring festival.
Many poets have written about a forlorn swain pining for his lady's affection.

Synset Examples

if I'd known he was her boyfriend, I wouldn't have asked
When the law changed, Pet could finally marry his long-time boyfriend Jim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boyfriend - a man with whom someone is involved romantically
  • Synonym: suitor - a man who pursues a relationship with a woman, often with the intention of marrying
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person with whom someone is having a romantic relationship

Etymology

From Old Norse 'sveinn' meaning 'boy, servant'.
