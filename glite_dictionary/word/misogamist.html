misogamist

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈsɑːɡəmɪst / (AmE), / mɪˈsɒɡəmɪst / (BrE)
a person who has a strong dislike or hatred of marriage
a person who hates marriage

Examples

Despite being accused of being a misogamist, he simply preferred lifelong bachelorhood.
The character in the novel was a self-proclaimed misogamist who found the idea of marriage stifling.
Some misogamists argue that marriage restricts personal freedom and individuality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misogyny - dislike or hatred of women
  • Synonym: misogynist - a person who dislikes or is prejudiced against women

Etymology

From Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'gamos' (marriage).
