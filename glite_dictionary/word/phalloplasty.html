phalloplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.əˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈfal.əˌplast.i / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to reconstruct or repair the penis, often to correct congenital abnormalities or injuries
surgical reconstruction of the penis
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a phalloplasty after the patient suffered a traumatic injury.
Advances in phalloplasty have significantly improved outcomes for transgender men.
He researched different phalloplasty techniques before making a decision about surgery.

Etymology

From Greek phallos (penis) + -plasty (molding, forming surgical repair).
