to observe

1. VERB

/ əbˈzɝv / (AmE), / əbˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to watch something or someone carefully, especially to learn or notice something
watch attentively

Examples

Scientists often observe animal behavior in the wild to understand their habits.
Please observe how she solves the problem before you try it yourself.
He likes to observe people at the park and guess their stories.

Synset Examples

Please observe the reaction of these two chemicals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - to look at something for a period of time with attention
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'observare' (to watch, to heed), from 'ob-' (over) + 'servare' (to keep, to watch).
