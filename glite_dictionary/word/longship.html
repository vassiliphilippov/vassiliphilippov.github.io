longship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌʃɪp / (BrE)
a long, narrow ship with oars and a sail, used by Vikings for trade, exploration, and warfare during the Viking Age
Viking ship with oars and sail

Examples

The museum houses a remarkably well-preserved longship from the 9th century.
Viking raiders crossed the sea in their swift longships.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a longship buried beneath the field.

Synset Examples

The longships had two methods of propulsion: oars and sail.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'langskip', meaning 'long ship'.
