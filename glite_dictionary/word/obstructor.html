obstructor

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌktər / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
a thing or structure that blocks or impedes progress or movement
thing that blocks or impedes progress

Examples

A fallen tree served as an obstructor on the hiking trail after last night's storm.
The committee removed the bureaucratic obstructor to speed up the approval process.
Engineers identified a rusty pipe as the main obstructor to the water flow in the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
  • Synonym: barrier - a physical or conceptual structure that blocks
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps progress

Etymology

From Latin obstructus, past participle of obstruere (to block up), plus the agentive suffix -or.

2. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌktər / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately or systematically prevents or hinders an action, process, or person
person who obstructs or hinders

Examples

The city council accused him of being an obstructor to progress on the housing plan.
As an obstructor of justice, he was charged with interfering in the legal process.
Her reputation as an obstructor made it difficult to build consensus within the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress
  • Antonym: facilitator - a person who makes a process easier or helps progress

Etymology

From Latin 'obstructus', past participle of 'obstruere' (to block up).
