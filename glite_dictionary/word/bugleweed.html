bugleweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːɡəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɡlˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several low-growing evergreen herbs of the genus Ajuga, native to Eurasia, often used as ground cover in gardens.
a low-growing evergreen herb (Ajuga) used as ground cover

Examples

The gardener planted bugleweed along the edges of the pathway to control weeds.
Bugleweed can spread quickly and fill in bare spots in shady areas.
Some varieties of bugleweed produce small, attractive blue or purple flowers in spring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːɡlˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɡlˌwiːd / (BrE)
a mildly narcotic and astringent aromatic herb with small whitish flowers, native to eastern North America, sometimes used in herbal medicine.
an aromatic herb native to eastern North America

Examples

Traditional healers sometimes use bugleweed to help treat mild thyroid problems.
Clusters of wild bugleweed were found growing near the edge of the forest.
Some herbal teas contain bugleweed for its calming effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ajuga - a genus of plants that includes bugleweed

Etymology

From 'bugle', a type of plant (genus Ajuga), + 'weed' (plant considered undesirable or wild).
