punishing

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a task, schedule, pace, etc.) demanding great effort and endurance to the point of exhaustion; extremely arduous or strenuous.
demanding great effort; exhausting

Examples

The team kept up a punishing pace throughout the race, leaving everyone exhausted by the finish line.
She endured a punishing training schedule to prepare for the marathon.
After a punishing day of work, he could barely keep his eyes open.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • Synonym: grueling - extremely tiring and demanding
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding; requiring little effort

Etymology

From the verb 'punish', figuratively extended in 19th century English to describe something that inflicts hardship as if by punishment.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing great difficulty, hardship, or suffering; resulting in punishment or severe demands.
causing hardship or punishment

Examples

The coach designed a punishing training regimen to prepare the athletes for the championship.
The company faced a punishing fine for violating environmental regulations.
She endured a punishing schedule during the busy holiday season.

Synset Examples

the king imposed a punishing tax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - inflicting or intended as punishment

Etymology

Present participle of 'punish', from Old French *puniss-*, stem of *punir*, from Latin *punīre* (to punish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03164770-a
Synset POS: a
