tour

1. NOUN

/ tʊr / (AmE), / tʊə / (BrE)
a period of time spent serving in the military, especially in a specific location or assignment before returning home or being reassigned
period of military service at a post

Examples

He just finished a tour in Afghanistan and is now back home.
Many soldiers complete more than one tour during their military careers.
After his second tour overseas, he received a promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deployment - being stationed or assigned to a military position or area
  • Synonym: rotation - a tour of duty followed by reassignment or relief
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ tʊr / (AmE), / tʊə / (BrE)
to travel to and around a place for pleasure, often visiting various sights
travel around a place for pleasure

Examples

We toured Italy for two weeks and visited many historic sites.
The band toured across Europe, playing in major cities every night.
Next summer, she plans to tour the national parks in the United States.

Synset Examples

We toured the Provence this summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to make a journey, especially over a long distance
  • Synonym: visit - to go to see and spend time in a place for a short period

Etymology

From French 'tour' (a turn, journey), from Latin 'tornus' (lathe, circular movement).

3. NOUN

/ tʊr / (AmE), / tʊə / (BrE)
a journey or trip in which one visits several places, often returning to the starting point, especially for pleasure, study, or business
journey visiting several places

Examples

We went on a guided tour of the ancient castle.
Her European tour lasted for three weeks and included six countries.
The city offers a bike tour that covers all the major attractions.

Synset Examples

they took an extended tour of Europe
we took a quick circuit of the park
a ten-day coach circuit of the island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip made for pleasure
  • Synonym: circuit - a journey or route all the way around a particular place or area

Etymology

From French *tour* (turn, journey), from Latin *tornus* (lathe, tool for turning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311492-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ tʊr / (AmE), / tʊə / (BrE)
a period of time spent doing a particular job, especially one of several periods that workers or soldiers take turns to do
period of work before relief

Examples

After a long tour on night duty, she was finally able to go home.
His third tour in the navy lasted six months before he was rotated out.
During my tour at the hospital, I met many new patients.

Synset Examples

it's my go
a spell of work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a scheduled period of work, especially in jobs that require coverage around the clock

Etymology

From Old French 'tour' (turn, journey), from Latin 'tornus' (turning lathe, tool), referring to a turn or round of duty.
