exuberance

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzuːbərəns / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzjuːbərəns / (BrE)
joyful enthusiasm or abundant energy; the quality of being full of excitement and cheerfulness.
joyful enthusiasm or liveliness

Examples

Her exuberance was contagious, and soon everyone was laughing and dancing.
The team's exuberance after winning the championship was obvious to all the fans.
Despite the rainy weather, his exuberance for the picnic never faded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of depression or low spirits

Etymology

From Latin exuberantia (abundance), from exuberare (to be abundant, to overflow).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzuːbərəns / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzjuːbərəns / (BrE)
the quality of being full of energy, excitement, and cheerfulness; enthusiastic enjoyment or approval
energetic enthusiasm or excitement

Examples

The children’s exuberance was evident as they played in the park all afternoon.
Her exuberance for life made her a joy to be around.
Despite the bad weather, his exuberance never seemed to fade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'exuberantia', from 'exuberans' (overflowing, abundant).
