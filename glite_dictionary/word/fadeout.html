fadeout

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary gradual reduction in the strength of a transmitted signal, often caused by electrical interference
temporary reduction in signal strength

Examples

During the storm, the radio experienced several sudden fadeouts that made the broadcast unintelligible.
Satellite communication can be interrupted by a fadeout caused by atmospheric conditions.
The technician diagnosed the recurring fadeout as interference from nearby electrical equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fading - the variation or loss of signal strength, especially in communications

Etymology

From 'fade' + 'out', describing the diminishing of a signal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪd.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪd.aʊt / (BrE)
a gradual disappearance, especially of sound or an image, until nothing is left; particularly used in film or music.
gradual disappearance (sound or image)

Examples

The movie ended with a gentle fadeout, leaving the audience in silence.
There was a noticeable fadeout as the music slowly disappeared at the end of the track.
The old photograph showed a gradual fadeout of the once vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to gradually disappear or become less distinct

Etymology

From 'fade' + 'out', originally used in the context of film and audio production in the early 20th century.
