eft

1. NOUN

/ ɛft / (AmE), / ɛft / (BrE)
A young newt, especially during its terrestrial, juvenile stage after metamorphosis and before returning to the water as an adult.
juvenile terrestrial stage of a newt

Examples

We spotted an eft crawling among the damp leaves by the pond.
During this stage, the eft is bright orange and lives on land.
The children learned to distinguish an eft from an adult newt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small, typically aquatic, salamander

Etymology

From Old English *efte*; related to Dutch *eft* and German *Echse* (lizard).
