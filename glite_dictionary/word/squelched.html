squelched

1. ADJ

/ skwɛltʃt / (AmE), / skwɛltʃt / (BrE)
subdued, ended, or suppressed; prevented from continuing or growing
subdued or suppressed

Examples

The protest was quickly squelched by the city's authorities.
Rumors of a merger were squelched before they could spread through the company.
The committee's dissent was squelched during the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

the quelled rebellion
an uprising quenched almost before it started
a squelched rumor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressed - forcibly put an end to; subdued
  • Synonym: quenched - put an end to or stifled
  • Antonym: encouraged - given support or confidence; promoted or increased

Etymology

From the verb 'squelch,' likely imitative in origin.
