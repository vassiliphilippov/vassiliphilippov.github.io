barrelhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbærəlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
(historical) A cheap bar or club, typically in the southern United States, known for lively music and dancing.
cheap bar with music and dancing

Examples

The band played lively blues tunes deep into the night at the old barrelhouse.
Stories about raucous times at the barrelhouse have become part of local legend.
In the 1920s, a barrelhouse was often the center of social life in small towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saloon - a bar or tavern, especially in the past in western North America
  • Synonym: speakeasy - an illicit liquor store or nightclub during Prohibition

Etymology

From 'barrel' (as in barrels of alcohol) and 'house'; originally referring to establishments with barrels of liquor on display.
