Malcolm X

1. PROPN

/ ˈmælkəm ɛks / (AmE), / ˈmælkəm ɛks / (BrE)
(1925–1965) An African-American civil rights leader known for his advocacy for Black empowerment, his militancy, and his influential role in the civil rights movement.
African-American civil rights leader, activist

Examples

Malcolm X's speeches inspired many to challenge racial injustice in America.
Many young activists look up to Malcolm X for his uncompromising stance on equality.
The autobiography of Malcolm X remains a powerful account of transformation and advocacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martin Luther King Jr. - prominent civil rights leader known for nonviolent activism

Etymology

Born Malcolm Little; adopted 'X' to symbolize his lost African surname.
