Ulmus glabra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs ˈɡlɑːbrə / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian elm tree, often planted as a shade tree in parks and gardens.
Eurasian elm species

Examples

The Ulmus glabra can reach impressive heights when mature.
Gardeners often choose Ulmus glabra for its broad, spreading crown.
Several old Ulmus glabra trees line the pathways of the city park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wych elm - common English name for Ulmus glabra

Etymology

From Latin: Ulmus (elm) and glabra (smooth, hairless).
