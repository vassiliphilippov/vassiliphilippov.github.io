reassurance

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
the act of making someone feel less worried or more confident about something
the act of making someone feel confident

Examples

She needed some reassurance before giving her presentation to the board.
A gentle word of reassurance can calm anxious students.
He looked for reassurance from his friends during the difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a feeling of relief or support
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of confidence

Etymology

From re- (again) + assurance, from Old French 'asseurer' (to assure).
