majuscule

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʒʌskjul / (AmE), / məˈdʒʌskjuːl / (BrE)
a large, uppercase letter, typically used in ancient manuscripts and inscriptions; also, writing composed of such letters
uppercase letter or script

Examples

Many ancient Roman inscriptions were written entirely in majuscule script.
The professor explained the difference between majuscule and minuscule handwriting styles.
The illuminated manuscript featured beautiful majuscule initials at the start of each chapter.

Synset Examples

capital A
great A
many medieval manuscripts are in majuscule script

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minuscule - a small, lowercase letter or script
  • Synonym: capital - uppercase letter

Etymology

From Latin 'majuscule', diminutive of 'majus', comparative of 'magnus' (great).

2. ADJ

/ məˈdʒʌs.kjul / (AmE), / məˈdʒʌs.kjuːl / (BrE)
(of writing) relating to a style of writing with large, rounded capital letters, used especially in manuscripts from the 4th to 8th centuries.
relating to capital-letter handwriting style

Examples

The historian analyzed the majuscule letters in the ancient manuscript.
Early Christian texts are often written in a majuscule script.
The transition from majuscule to minuscule writing changed the appearance of medieval books.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minuscule - relating to a style of very small or lowercase letters in writing

Etymology

From Latin 'majuscule', diminutive of 'major' (greater).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01477139-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ məˈdʒʌs.kjul / (AmE), / məˈdʒʌs.kjuːl / (BrE)
a large letter, such as a capital or uppercase letter, used at the beginning of sentences, proper names, or for emphasis
large or capital letter (uppercase)

Examples

In ancient manuscripts, the text was written entirely in majuscule script.
The teacher asked the students to write their names in majuscule letters.
The word 'London' begins with a majuscule as it is a proper noun.

Synset Examples

printers once kept the type for capitals and for small letters in separate cases; capitals were kept in the upper half of the type case and so became known as upper-case letters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minuscule - a small letter or lowercase letter
  • Synonym: uppercase - having capital or large letters
  • Synonym: capital letter - a letter of the alphabet in the form used to begin sentences and proper nouns

Etymology

From Latin 'majuscula', diminutive of 'major' meaning 'greater'.
