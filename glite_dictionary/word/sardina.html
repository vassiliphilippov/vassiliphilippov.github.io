Sardina

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdiːnə / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, oily fish in the herring family Clupeidae, commonly known as pilchards.
genus of fish (pilchards)
medical

Examples

The Sardina genus includes several species of commercial importance.
Scientists studied the population dynamics of Sardina in the Mediterranean Sea.
The European pilchard, classified under Sardina, is commonly used in canned foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish found in both Atlantic and Mediterranean, often canned for food

Etymology

Modern scientific Latin, from Italian ‘sardina’ (sardine), ultimately from Sardinia, where such fish are found.
