sevenfold

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛvənˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ən.fəʊld / (BrE)
By seven times; to a degree or amount seven times as great.
seven times as much or many

Examples

The company’s profits increased sevenfold after the new product launched.
In the past decade, the city’s population grew sevenfold.
Donations to the charity have risen sevenfold since last year.

Synset Examples

the population of this village increased sevenfold in the past 100 years

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sixfold - six times as much
  • Degree Higher: eightfold - eight times as much

Etymology

From Middle English 'seuenfold', patterned after 'seven' + 'fold'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛv.ənˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ənˌfəʊld / (BrE)
Having seven parts or being seven times as much or as many.
seven times as much; having seven parts

Examples

Profits increased sevenfold in just five years.
The project has had a sevenfold improvement in efficiency since last year.
Membership of the club grew sevenfold after the introduction of new activities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - twice as much or twice in number
  • Degree Lower: triple - three times as much or three in number
  • Degree Higher: tenfold - ten times as much or ten in number

Etymology

From Old English 'seofonfeald', from 'seofon' (seven) + 'feald' (fold, times).
