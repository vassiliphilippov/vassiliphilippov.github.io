huntress

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntrəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌntrɪs / (BrE)
A woman who hunts animals, especially as a sport or for food.
a female hunter

Examples

The legendary Greek huntress Artemis was worshipped for her skills in the wild.
In the novel, the huntress tracked the wolf through the snowy forest.
As a skilled huntress, she supplied her village with meat during the harsh winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunter - a person who hunts animals

Etymology

From Middle English 'huntys', from 'hunt' + -ess, a suffix forming feminine nouns.
