Musa acuminata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjusə ˌækjəˈmɪnətə / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsə əˌkjuːmɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A species of low-growing Asian banana plant cultivated for its clusters of edible yellow fruit, especially in the West Indies.
species of banana plant

Examples

The commercial bananas found in most supermarkets are usually derived from Musa acuminata.
Researchers study Musa acuminata to improve disease resistance in banana crops.
The plantation specialized in growing Musa acuminata for export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - the common edible yellow fruit produced by Musa species

Etymology

From Latin Musa (the banana genus) and Latin acuminata (pointed), referring to the shape of the plant's leaves.
