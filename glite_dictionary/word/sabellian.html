Sabellian

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɛliən / (AmE), / səˈbɛliən / (BrE)
an extinct Osco-Umbrian language of ancient Italy, known from a small number of inscriptions
extinct Osco-Umbrian language of ancient Italy

Examples

Few texts are preserved in Sabellian, making its study a challenge for linguists.
The Sabellian language became extinct in the early centuries BCE.
Scholars compare Latin and Sabellian to reconstruct the evolution of Italic languages.

Etymology

From Latin Sabellī (the Sabellians), referring to a group of ancient Italic peoples.
