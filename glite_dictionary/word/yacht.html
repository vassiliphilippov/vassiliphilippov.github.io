yacht

1. NOUN

/ jɑt / (AmE), / jɒt / (BrE)
a large, expensive boat that is used for pleasure cruising or racing, usually powered by sails or an engine
a large luxury boat for cruising or racing

Examples

They spent their summer vacation sailing on a luxury yacht in the Mediterranean.
The annual regatta attracts dozens of yachts from around the world.
He dreams of owning a yacht and traveling the seas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boat - a small vessel for traveling on water
  • Degree Higher: ship - a large vessel for transporting people or goods by sea

Etymology

From Dutch 'jacht' meaning 'hunt' or 'hunting ship'.

2. VERB

/ jɑt / (AmE), / jɒt / (BrE)
to travel or go on a voyage in a yacht, especially for pleasure
to travel in a yacht

Examples

They plan to yacht along the Mediterranean coast next summer.
The couple yachted from Miami to the Bahamas during their vacation.
Many wealthy tourists enjoy yachting as a way to explore the Caribbean islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - to travel in a boat with sails, usually for pleasure or sport

Etymology

From Dutch 'jacht' (hunt, hunting ship).
