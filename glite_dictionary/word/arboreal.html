arboreal

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈbɔriəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) living in or often found in trees.
living in or among trees

Examples

The koala is an arboreal marsupial native to Australia.
Many arboreal species have developed strong limbs for climbing.
Sloths move slowly through the arboreal habitats of Central and South American rainforests.

Synset Examples

arboreal apes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - living on the land or ground

Etymology

From Latin arbor (tree), with suffix -eal meaning 'pertaining to'.

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈbɔriəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling trees; living in or among trees
relating to trees

Examples

The koala is an arboreal marsupial native to Australia.
This forest is known for its rich arboreal diversity, including centuries-old oaks and pines.
Some snakes are arboreal, spending most of their lives in the branches above the ground.

Synset Examples

an arborous roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree - a perennial plant with an elongated stem, or trunk, supporting branches and leaves
  • Antonym: terrestrial - living on or in the ground

Etymology

From Latin 'arboreus', from 'arbor' (tree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02648772-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈbɔriəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
having the form or branching structure of a tree; tree-like in appearance.
tree-like in form or structure

Examples

The arboreal structure of the coral made it resemble a miniature underwater forest.
Some sponges have an arboreal appearance, spreading branches like the limbs of a tree.
Geologists noted the arboreal patterns of mineral growth within the rock cavity.

Synset Examples

arborescent coral found off the coast of Bermuda
dendriform sponges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborescent - having a form or appearance similar to that of a tree
  • Synonym: dendriform - having a shape like a tree or branching structure

Etymology

From Latin arbor (tree).
