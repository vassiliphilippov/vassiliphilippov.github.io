caption

1. VERB

/ ˈkæpʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæpʃən / (BrE)
to provide a short title or explanation for a photograph, drawing, or other illustration
add a title or explanation to an image

Examples

The editor asked me to caption all the images in the article before publication.
She carefully captioned each photo in her travel album to remember the details.
We spent all afternoon captioning pictures for the company’s new website.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - to attach a tag or description to something

Etymology

From Latin 'captio' (a taking, seizing), via Old French and English noun form.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02328866-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ʃən / (BrE)
A brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration, photo, diagram, or similar image.
text accompanying an image

Examples

The photo's caption provided the date and location of the event.
Each illustration in the book included a witty caption underneath.
He forgot to add a caption to the chart, making it unclear what the data represented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subtitle - secondary title or explanatory text below the main title or image
  • Synonym: legend - text accompanying a map or diagram explaining symbols

Etymology

From Latin 'captio' (a catching, seizing), later in English meaning a heading or title.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06358261-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæpʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæpʃən / (BrE)
Words shown on a film or television screen that translate or transcribe spoken dialogue, typically appearing at the bottom of the screen.
subtitled translation on screen

Examples

The foreign film included English captions so everyone could understand the story.
He turned on the captions to follow along with the rapid dialogue.
Some streaming services allow you to customize the appearance of captions for easier reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subtitle - text at the bottom of a screen translating or transcribing dialogue

Etymology

From Latin capitio (seizing), from caput (head).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06358958-n
Synset POS: n
