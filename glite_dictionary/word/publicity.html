publicity

1. NOUN

/ pʌbˈlɪsɪti / (AmE), / pʌbˈlɪsɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being open to public observation or access
being open to public view

Examples

The judge emphasized the publicity of the trial to ensure transparency.
Concerns were raised over the publicity of government meetings during the investigation.
The publicity of the proceedings guarantees that justice is seen to be done.

Synset Examples

the publicity of the court room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secrecy - the quality of being hidden or not open to the public

Etymology

From French 'publicité' (publicness), from Latin 'publicus' (public).

2. NOUN

/ pʌbˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / pʌbˈlɪsəti / (BrE)
the activity or process of making something known to the public, especially in order to promote a product, person, cause, or idea
promotion or attention given to something

Examples

The charity received a lot of publicity after the celebrity endorsement.
The new law was introduced with minimal publicity, so many people were unaware of it.
The company launched a major publicity campaign to improve its image.

Synset Examples

the packaging of new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertising - the activity of producing advertisements
  • Synonym: promotion - the activity of supporting or actively encouraging a cause, venture, or aim
  • Antonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or hidden

Etymology

From public + -ity, first recorded in late 18th century.
