sheik

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ʃiːk / (BrE)
the leader of an Arab village, tribe, or family, especially a respected elder or chief
leader of an Arab village or tribe

Examples

The villagers respected their sheik for his wisdom and leadership.
After the sudden passing of the former sheik, his eldest son assumed the position.
As sheik, he was responsible for settling disputes within the tribe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chieftain - the leader of a tribe or clan

Etymology

From Arabic 'shaykh' (chief, elder).
