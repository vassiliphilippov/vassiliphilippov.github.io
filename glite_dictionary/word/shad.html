shad

1. NOUN

/ ʃæd / (AmE), / ʃad / (BrE)
The bony flesh of a herring-like fish, especially caught during its migration to freshwater for spawning, commonly found along the Atlantic coast and used as food.
bony fish eaten as food, esp. Atlantic coast

Examples

We grilled fresh shad for dinner during the spring fishing season.
The market sells both smoked and salted shad caught along the river.
Many chefs prize the delicate flavor of shad despite its many bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silver-colored fish often used as food

Etymology

From Old English sceadd, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ʃæd / (AmE), / ʃad / (BrE)
a herring-like fish that migrates from the sea to fresh water to spawn, valued as food
a migratory herring-like fish

Examples

Fishermen look forward to the annual shad run every spring.
Grilled shad is a seasonal delicacy in some coastal towns.
The river was once famous for the large numbers of shad that migrated upstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish of the family Clupeidae

Etymology

From Old English sceadd, referring to this type of fish.
