Cannabis sativa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænəbɪs səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ˈkanəbɪs səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
a tall, annual plant native to Asia, with pungent-smelling leaves, cultivated for its fibers and for its psychoactive and medicinal properties
species of cannabis plant with psychoactive uses

Examples

Cannabis sativa has been studied for its potential medical benefits, including pain management.
Farmers in some countries are now allowed to cultivate Cannabis sativa for industrial hemp products.
The psychoactive properties of Cannabis sativa are due to compounds found in its flowers and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - the dried leaves and flowers of Cannabis used as a drug
  • Synonym: hemp - non-psychoactive fiber from Cannabis sativa

Etymology

From Latin: cannabis (hemp) and sativa (cultivated).
