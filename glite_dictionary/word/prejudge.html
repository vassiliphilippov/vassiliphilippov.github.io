to prejudge

1. VERB

/ priˈdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / priːˈdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
to judge or form an opinion about something or someone before all the facts are known, especially unfairly
judge beforehand, often unfairly

Examples

Try not to prejudge a person based on their appearance alone.
The committee had clearly prejudged the case before hearing all the evidence.
She warned her colleagues not to prejudge the outcome of the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - to form an opinion or conclusion about
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'judicare' (to judge).
