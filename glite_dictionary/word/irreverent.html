irreverent

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously or treated with veneration
showing lack of respect

Examples

The comedian's irreverent jokes pushed the boundaries of what the audience expected.
Some critics found the artist's irreverent treatment of traditional subjects refreshing.
Her irreverent attitude towards authority made her popular among her peers.

Synset Examples

irreverent scholars mocking sacred things
noisy irreverent tourists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference
  • Synonym: scornful - showing contempt or disdain

Etymology

From Latin 'irreverent-', from 'irreverens' (not showing respect), from 'in-' (not) + 'revereri' (to revere).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
Showing a lack of respect for people or things that are generally respected, especially toward religious or sacred subjects.
showing lack of respect (esp. for the sacred)

Examples

The comedian's irreverent jokes often poked fun at traditional customs and beliefs.
Some found the artist's irreverent take on sacred themes offensive, while others praised her creativity.
His irreverent attitude toward authority sometimes got him into trouble.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverent - showing deep respect, especially for something sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'irreverent-', from 'irreverens', from 'in-' (not) + 'revereri' (to revere).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01787262-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously, often in a playful or lively way.
showing lack of respect, often playful

Examples

The comedian’s irreverent humor challenged social norms and made the audience laugh.
Many teenagers adopt an irreverent attitude toward authority figures.
Her irreverent comments at the formal dinner raised a few eyebrows.

Synset Examples

a certain irreverent gaiety and ease of manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect, the opposite of irreverent
  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect, especially in a rude way

Etymology

From Latin 'irreverent-', from 'irreverens' (not reverent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02287824-s
Synset POS: s
