Asiatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪzɪˈatɪk / (BrE)
relating to Asia or its people, languages, or culture.
of or relating to Asia

Examples

The Asiatic cultures have rich and diverse traditions stretching back thousands of years.
Many Asiatic languages share certain grammatical features.
The Asiatic elephant is smaller than its African counterpart.

Synset Examples

Asian countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asian - of or relating to Asia or its people

Etymology

From Latin 'Asiaticus', from Ancient Greek 'Ασιατικός' (Asiatikos), from 'Asia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02980070-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪzɪˈatɪk / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Asia (now dated or sometimes considered offensive)
native or inhabitant of Asia

Examples

During the 19th century, many Asiatics arrived to work on the railways.
The museum displayed ancient art created by early Asiatics.
In historical texts, the term Asiatic is often used to describe people from Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asian - a native or inhabitant of Asia

Etymology

From Latin Asiaticus, from Asia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09662736-n
Synset POS: n
