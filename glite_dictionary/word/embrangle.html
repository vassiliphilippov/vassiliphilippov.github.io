to embrangle

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɛmˈbraŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to make more complicated, confused, or entangled
to complicate or confuse

Examples

The lawyer's arguments only served to embrangle the already confusing case.
Instead of clarifying the instructions, the added details seemed to embrangle the process further.
She feared that discussing old issues would only embrangle their fragile relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entangle - to twist together or complicate; to confuse
  • Antonym: to simplify - to make less complicated or clearer

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'entangle,' perhaps by influence of 'brangle' (to quarrel or dispute).
