tectonic

1. ADJ

/ tɛkˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the structure, movement, or deformation of the earth's crust
relating to earth's crust structure or movement

Examples

Geologists study tectonic movements to understand earthquake activity.
The Himalayas were formed by tectonic forces pushing together massive landmasses.
Significant tectonic shifts can cause dramatic changes to the earth's surface.

Synset Examples

tectonic plates
tectonic valleys

Etymology

From Greek 'tektonikos' (pertaining to building), from 'tektōn' (builder, carpenter).
