nebulously

1. ADV

/ ˈnɛbjələsli / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊləsli / (BrE)
In a vague, unclear, or indefinite manner.
in a vague or indefinite manner

Examples

Her opinions on the subject were nebulously stated, leaving the audience unsure of her stance.
The future of the project was nebulously discussed during the meeting.
He nebulously alluded to changes without providing any concrete details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear, definite, or specific
  • Antonym: definitely - in a precise and clear manner

Etymology

From 'nebulous' + '-ly'; 'nebulous' from Latin nebulosus (full of mist).
