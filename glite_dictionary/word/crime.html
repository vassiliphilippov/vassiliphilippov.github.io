crime

1. NOUN

/ kraɪm / (AmE), / kraɪm / (BrE)
An act that is forbidden by law and punishable as a serious offense; typically considered morally wrong or harmful to society.
act punishable by law

Examples

The police are working hard to reduce crime in the city.
He was found guilty of committing a serious crime.
The documentary explores the impact of organized crime on society.

Synset Examples

a long record of crimes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an act that breaks a law or rule
  • Degree Lower: misdemeanor - a minor wrongdoing, less serious than a crime
  • Degree Higher: felony - a serious crime, typically one punishable by imprisonment

Etymology

From Old French *crimne*, from Latin *crimen* (charge, accusation, crime).

2. NOUN

/ kraɪm / (AmE), / kraɪm / (BrE)
An action or behavior considered morally wrong or evil, even if not punishable by law.
morally wrong or evil act

Examples

Many people believe that lying is a crime against one's own conscience.
Some see wasting food as a crime when so many are hungry.
The novel deals with emotional crimes committed within families.

Synset Examples

crimes of the heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law
  • Synonym: offense - an act that violates a law, rule, or code of conduct
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old French *crimne*, from Latin *crimen* (charge, accusation, fault, crime).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00768966-n
Synset POS: n
