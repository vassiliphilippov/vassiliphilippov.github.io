to regain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈɡeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪn / (BrE)
to obtain possession, use, or control of something again after losing it
recover something lost

Examples

After months of hard work, he was finally able to regain his strength.
The company is trying hard to regain its reputation after the scandal.
She took a deep breath to regain her composure before speaking again.

Synset Examples

She regained control of herself
She found her voice and replied quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after a setback or loss
  • Antonym: to lose - to cease to have or possess something

Etymology

From Old French 'regaignier', from re- (again) + 'gagner' (to gain).
