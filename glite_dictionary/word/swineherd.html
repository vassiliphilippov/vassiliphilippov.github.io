swineherd

1. NOUN

/ ˈswaɪnˌhɜrd / (AmE), / ˈswaɪnhɜːd / (BrE)
a person who tends or herds pigs
person who tends pigs

Examples

As a young boy, he worked as a swineherd on his family's farm.
The ancient tale tells of a humble swineherd who became a nobleman.
After sunrise, the swineherd led the pigs out to graze in the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd - person who tends sheep
  • Synonym: cowherd - person who tends cattle

Etymology

From Old English 'swȳnhyrde', from 'swīn' (swine, pig) + 'hierde' (herdsman).
