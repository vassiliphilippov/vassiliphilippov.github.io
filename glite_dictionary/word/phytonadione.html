phytonadione

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪtoʊnəˈdaɪoʊn / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtənəˈdʌɪəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic form of vitamin K used to prevent and treat vitamin K deficiency and certain bleeding disorders
synthetic vitamin K compound
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phytonadione to help correct the patient's blood clotting deficiency.
Newborn infants sometimes receive phytonadione to reduce the risk of bleeding disorders.
High doses of phytonadione are rarely necessary unless there is severe vitamin K deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin K - a group of compounds essential for blood clotting

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) and chemical compound naming conventions.
