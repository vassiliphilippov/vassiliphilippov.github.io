underachievement

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚ.əˈtʃiv.mənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndə.əˈtʃiːv.mənt / (BrE)
performance that is lower than what is expected or predicted, especially based on a person's ability or intelligence
poorer than expected performance

Examples

His underachievement in mathematics surprised his teachers, given his high scores on intelligence tests.
The school is implementing new programs to address chronic underachievement among its students.
Despite her talent, she struggled with underachievement throughout her academic career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: achievement - a thing done successfully, typically by effort, courage, or skill

Etymology

From 'under-' (not enough) + 'achievement' (act of accomplishing).
