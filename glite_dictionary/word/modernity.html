modernity

1. NOUN

/ məˈdɝː.nə.t̬i / (AmE), / məˈdɜː.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being modern, up-to-date, or characteristic of the present time
the state of being modern

Examples

The architect's designs reflect a spirit of modernity that appeals to younger generations.
Many people associate modernity with technological advancement and social progress.
This painting captures both tradition and modernity in a single striking image.

Synset Examples

a shopping mall would instill a spirit of modernity into this village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernism - a movement in the arts and culture embracing modern ideas and values
  • Antonym: antiquity - the ancient past, especially before the Middle Ages

Etymology

From Modern French modernité, from moderne (modern) + -ité (-ity).
