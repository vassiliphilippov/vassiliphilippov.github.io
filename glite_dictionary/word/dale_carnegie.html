Dale Carnegie

1. PROPN

/ deɪl ˈkɑrnəɡi / (AmE), / deɪl ˈkɑːnɪɡi / (BrE)
(1888–1955) an American educator and writer known for his influential book 'How to Win Friends and Influence People'.
American writer and self-improvement educator

Examples

Many business leaders credit Dale Carnegie with transforming their approach to communication.
The classic book by Dale Carnegie remains popular more than eighty years after its initial publication.
My manager recommended reading Dale Carnegie for improving our interpersonal skills.
