audad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.dæd / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.dæd / (BrE)
A wild sheep native to northern Africa, known for its reddish-brown coat and curved horns.
wild sheep of northern Africa

Examples

We spotted an audad grazing on the rocky hillside during our expedition.
The audad has adapted well to arid environments, making it difficult to track.
Photographers often travel to North Africa to capture images of the elusive audad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbary sheep - another name for the audad, a wild sheep native to North Africa

Etymology

From Berber *awdad*, referring to the animal.
