plop

1. NOUN

/ plɑp / (AmE), / plɒp / (BrE)
a short, soft sound made when a rounded object drops into liquid without splashing
the sound of something dropping into liquid

Examples

The stone fell into the pond with a gentle plop.
She heard the plop of rainwater dripping from the roof into the bucket.
With a soft plop, the cherry landed in his glass of soda.

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound itself.

Examples

3. VERB

/ plɑp / (AmE), / plɒp / (BrE)
to drop or fall with a soft, splash-like sound, especially into water or a similar substance
to drop or fall with a splash-like sound

Examples

She let the stone plop into the pond and watched the ripples spread.
He plopped the ice cubes into his drink, enjoying the noise they made.
The frog plopped from the lily pad into the water below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to hit or fall into liquid so that drops of it go in all directions

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, first recorded in 19th century English.
