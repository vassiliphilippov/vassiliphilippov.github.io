immoderation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmɑdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmɒdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The quality or state of being excessive or lacking in moderation.
excessiveness; lack of moderation

Examples

His immoderation in eating unhealthy foods led to several health issues.
The politician's speech was marked by immoderation and hostility toward his opponents.
She warned against immoderation in spending, advocating instead for careful budgeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes
  • Synonym: excess - amount that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable

Etymology

From Latin immoderatio (excess, lack of moderation), from immoderatus (immoderate, excessive).
