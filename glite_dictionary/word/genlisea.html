Genlisea

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈlaɪsiə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈliːsiə / (BrE)
A genus of small rootless carnivorous plants found in swamps, with leaves forming tubular traps to capture prey.
genus of carnivorous swamp plants

Examples

Genlisea species are known for their ingenious underground traps used to capture small organisms.
Botanists discovered a new Genlisea in the humid swamps of South America.
The unusual adaptation of Genlisea allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Etymology

Named after Stephan von Genlis, a French botanist.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈlaɪsiə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈliːsiə / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous plants found in tropical African swamps, known for their unique trap mechanisms.
genus of small African carnivorous plants

Examples

Genlisea species use modified leaves to trap and digest small soil organisms.
Botanists discovered a new Genlisea plant thriving in a remote African wetland.
The unique feeding strategy of Genlisea attracts researchers from around the world.

Etymology

Named after Stéphanie Félicité, comtesse de Genlis, a French writer and educator.
