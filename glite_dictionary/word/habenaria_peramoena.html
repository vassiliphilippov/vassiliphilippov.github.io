Habenaria peramoena

1. NOUN

/ həˌbɛnˈɛəriə pɛrəˈmoʊnə / (AmE), / həˌbɛnˈɛəriə pɛrəˈməʊnə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to northeastern and alpine eastern North America, closely related to the purple fringed orchids, with rosy-purple or violet flowers and finely toothed leaf divisions.
a North American orchid species

Examples

The Habenaria peramoena blooms in midsummer along moist meadows and woodland edges.
Botanists identified the specimen as Habenaria peramoena due to its fringed violet petals.
Gardeners cherish Habenaria peramoena for its delicate, rosy-purple flowers and unique foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple fringed orchid - a closely related orchid species with similar appearance

Etymology

From Latin; 'Habenaria' refers to the genus of orchids, 'peramoena' is the species epithet meaning 'very charming' or 'lovely'.
