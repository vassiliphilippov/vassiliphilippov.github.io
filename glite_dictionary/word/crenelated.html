crenelated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛnəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɛnɪˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
having repeated square or rectangular indentations along the top edge, like those of a battlement
with battlement-like indentations

Examples

The ancient castle featured crenelated walls that gave it a majestic appearance.
Many medieval towers have crenelated parapets to allow defenders to take cover while observing or firing on attackers.
The architect incorporated a crenelated design into the facade to evoke a sense of historical grandeur.

Synset Examples

a crenelated molding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlemented - having battlements or resembling a battlement

Etymology

From 'crenel', from Old French *crenel* (notch).
