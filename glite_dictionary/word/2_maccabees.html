2 Maccabees

1. PROPN

/ tu ˈmækəˌbiz / (AmE), / tuː ˈmækəˌbiːz / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of the Bible that describes the life of Judas Maccabeus and the Jews' revolt against Antiochus IV Epiphanes, ending with the defeat of Nicanor in 161 BC.
Apocryphal book recounting the Maccabean revolt

Examples

Many scholars study 2 Maccabees to better understand Jewish history during the Hellenistic period.
Unlike 1 Maccabees, 2 Maccabees focuses more on religious themes and martyrdom.
In their research, they compared the accounts found in 2 Maccabees with other ancient sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 1 Maccabees - A related Apocryphal book describing the same historical period and events

Etymology

From the Greek 'Makabaioi' (Maccabees), the family name of Judas Maccabeus.
