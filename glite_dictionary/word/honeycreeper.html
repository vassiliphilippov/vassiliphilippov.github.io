honeycreeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌkriːpər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌkriːpə / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored songbird found in tropical America, with a curved bill adapted for sucking nectar
small brightly colored tropical American songbird

Examples

Several species of honeycreeper were spotted in the rainforest during our birdwatching tour.
The distinctive curved bill of the honeycreeper helps it feed on nectar from tropical flowers.
The brilliant blue feathers of a male honeycreeper make it easy to identify among the treetops.

Etymology

From 'honey' and 'creeper', referring to its nectar-feeding (honey) and perching (creeper) habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌkripər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌkriːpə / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized finch native to the Hawaiian islands, known for its varied bill shapes and colorful plumage
Hawaiian finch-like bird

Examples

The bright plumage of the honeycreeper helps it stand out among Hawaiian forests.
Several species of honeycreeper have adapted unique bills for feeding on nectar.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered honeycreeper populations.

Etymology

From 'honey' and 'creeper', indicating the bird's feeding habits and climbing behavior.
