Bedlam

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdləm / (AmE), / ˈbɛdləm / (BrE)
A place or scene of wild uproar and confusion; originally a name for an insane asylum.
uproar; chaos; was an asylum

Examples

When news of the mistake broke, the office descended into Bedlam.
The stadium was pure Bedlam after the team scored the winning goal.
In the eighteenth century, 'Bedlam' was notorious as a hospital for the mentally ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Synonym: pandemonium - wild and noisy disorder or confusion
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged or organized

Etymology

From 'Bedlam', a colloquial form of 'Bethlehem', referring to St Mary of Bethlehem hospital in London, historically an asylum for the insane.
