Tolstoy

1. PROPN

/ ˈtoʊlˌstɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɒl.stɔɪ / (BrE)
Leo Tolstoy (1828–1910), a Russian author known for classic novels such as 'War and Peace' and 'Anna Karenina'.
famous Russian novelist (1828–1910)

Examples

Many critics consider Tolstoy one of the greatest novelists in history.
She wrote her thesis about the philosophical ideas of Tolstoy.
The works of Tolstoy are frequently taught in world literature classes.

Etymology

Russian surname Толстой, literally meaning 'thick' or 'stout'.
