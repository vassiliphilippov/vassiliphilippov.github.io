Shakespearian

1. ADJ

/ ʃeɪkˈspɪriən / (AmE), / ʃeɪkˈspɪəriən / (BrE)
Relating to William Shakespeare or his works, especially in style, language, or themes.
relating to Shakespeare or his works

Examples

The director gave the production a distinctly Shakespearian style, using period costumes and original dialogue.
Her writing is often praised for its Shakespearian wit and complexity.
Many Shakespearian plays explore themes of power, love, and betrayal.

Synset Examples

Shakespearean plays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakespearean - Relating to William Shakespeare or his works (alternate spelling)

Etymology

Formed from Shakespeare (the playwright's name) + -ian (denoting relating to).
