abhenry

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbˈhɛnɹi / (AmE), / ˌabˈhɛnɹi / (BrE)
a unit of inductance equal to one billionth (10⁻⁹) of a henry
unit of inductance (10⁻⁹ henry)

Examples

The circuit's inductance was measured at just one abhenry due to its minimal coil size.
In older physics textbooks, you may come across the unit abhenry for small inductances.
A nanohenry is equivalent to an abhenry in the CGS electromagnetic system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: henry - the SI unit of inductance, equal to the inductance when a current change of one ampere per second induces an electromotive force of one volt

Etymology

From 'ab-' (absolute) + henry (unit of inductance, after Joseph Henry).
