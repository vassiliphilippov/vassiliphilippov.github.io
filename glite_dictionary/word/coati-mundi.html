coati-mundi

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑː.tiˈmʌn.di / (AmE), / kəʊˌɑː.tiˈmʌn.di / (BrE)
an omnivorous mammal of Central and South America, related to raccoons, with a long snout and a ringed tail
Central/South American mammal related to raccoons

Examples

The coati-mundi uses its long snout to sniff out insects on the forest floor.
During our trip to Costa Rica, we spotted several coati-mundis climbing trees near the river.
A group of coati-mundi can often be seen foraging together in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - an omnivorous mammal native to Central and South America, also known as coati-mundi

Etymology

From Spanish 'coatí' (from Tupi 'kwati') and 'mundi', possibly from another indigenous language.
