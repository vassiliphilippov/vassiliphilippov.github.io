disharmony

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈhɑrməni / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːməni / (BrE)
The state of being in conflict or lacking agreement or harmony.
lack of harmony or agreement

Examples

The recent changes in management have created significant disharmony within the company.
There was obvious disharmony among the band members during the performance.
Persistent disagreements led to a feeling of disharmony between the two families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord

Etymology

From prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'harmony', from Greek 'harmonia' (agreement, concord).
