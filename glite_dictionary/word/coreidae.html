Coreidae

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈriːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɒˈriːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects known as squash bugs and leaf-footed bugs, characterized by a broad body and leaf-like expansions on the legs of some species
a family of squash bugs and leaf-footed bugs

Examples

Farmers often have to contend with Coreidae infesting their squash and pumpkin crops.
The Coreidae family contains many species that suck sap from plants, sometimes causing significant damage.
Entomologists study Coreidae to understand their role in agricultural ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Coreidae', based on the type genus 'Coreus'.

2. NOUN

/ kɔˈriːɪdiː / (AmE), / kɒˈriːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, mostly tropical and subtropical insects known as squash bugs and leaf-footed bugs, belonging to the order Hemiptera.
family of squash and leaf-footed bugs

Examples

The Coreidae are commonly called leaf-footed bugs because of the flattened segments on their hind legs.
Farmers often monitor for Coreidae infestations in their squash and pumpkin crops.
Some members of the Coreidae family can emit a strong odor when threatened.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Coreus plus the usual family ending -idae.
