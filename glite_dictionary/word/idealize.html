to idealize

1. VERB

/ aɪˈdiəlaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to regard or represent something or someone as perfect or better than in reality
regard as perfect or better than reality

Examples

Many young people tend to idealize celebrities and view them as flawless role models.
It is important not to idealize the past, remembering it as better than it actually was.
She often idealizes her relationships, ignoring obvious problems.

Synset Examples

Man has always idealized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to view or present something in an idealized or unrealistic way
  • Antonym: to deprecate - to express disapproval of; to belittle or make something seem less than ideal

Etymology

From French 'idéaliser', from 'idéal' (ideal).

2. VERB

/ aɪˈdiəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to regard or represent something or someone as perfect or better than reality
regard as perfect or better than reality

Examples

Many people tend to idealize their childhood years, forgetting the difficult moments.
After reading his biography, she began to idealize the writer's life.
It is important not to idealize your role models, as everyone has flaws.

Synset Examples

She idealized her husband after his death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or interesting than it really is
  • Antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are in fact, not idealizing

Etymology

From French 'idéaliser', from 'idéal' (ideal).
