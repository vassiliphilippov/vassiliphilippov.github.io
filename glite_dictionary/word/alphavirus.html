alphavirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fəˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈal.fəˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of arboviruses in the family Togaviridae, some of which can cause encephalitis in horses and humans.
genus of arboviruses (Togaviridae)
medical

Examples

The alphavirus causes severe disease outbreaks among horse populations.
Scientists are studying the transmission cycle of alphavirus to prevent further epidemics.
Cases of alphavirus infection are most common in tropical and subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbovirus - a virus transmitted by arthropods

Etymology

From 'alpha' + 'virus', following virus genus naming patterns.
