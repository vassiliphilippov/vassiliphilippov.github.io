bachelor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr / (AmE), / ˈbatʃələ / (BrE)
a knight of the lowest rank in medieval times, permitted to display only a pennon as his standard
knight of the lowest rank
archaic

Examples

In medieval England, a bachelor served under a higher-ranking knight and could not fly a full banner.
The young bachelor longed for the day he would be promoted to a banneret.
The chronicler noted that the bachelor distinguished himself during the tournament despite his lower status.

Etymology

From Old French 'bacheler' (young man, novice) and Medieval Latin 'baccalarius'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ / (BrE)
a man who has never been married
unmarried man

Examples

After college, he remained a bachelor for several years before getting married.
The party was thrown in honor of the city’s most eligible bachelor.
She rented out a small apartment to a quiet bachelor who rarely had visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinster - an unmarried woman, typically older
  • Antonym: husband - a married man

Etymology

From Old French 'bacheler' (young man; squire), later adopted in Medieval Latin as 'baccalarius'.
