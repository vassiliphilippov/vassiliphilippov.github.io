to organize

1. VERB

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to form or become a member of a labor union
form or join a labor union

Examples

The employees voted to organize after months of contract disputes.
Efforts to organize the warehouse workers were met with resistance from management.
She helped her colleagues organize so they could demand better working conditions.

Synset Examples

The auto workers decided to unionize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - to form or join a labor union

2. VERB

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to plan and direct a complex event or activity; to arrange and coordinate the elements involved
plan and direct a complex undertaking

Examples

It took weeks to organize the international conference successfully.
She was asked to organize the company's annual charity gala.
They worked together to organize a large fundraising campaign for local schools.

Synset Examples

he masterminded the robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate - to organize people or groups so that they work together properly and well
  • Synonym: to arrange - to put things in a particular order or position; to organize an event
  • Antonym: to disorganize - to disturb the organization of; to make something less ordered

Etymology

From Medieval Latin organizare, from Latin organum (instrument, tool).
