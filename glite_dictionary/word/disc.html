disc

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
a round, flat object or shape resembling a circular plate
round, flat circular object

Examples

The frisbee is shaped like a disc for aerodynamics.
The bright disc of the sun was visible above the clouds.
She collected each golden disc from the board game box.

Synset Examples

the moon's disk hung in a cloudless sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk - alternative spelling (especially in American English) for a round, flat object or shape

Etymology

From Latin 'discus', from Greek 'diskos' (quoit, disk, platter).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
(computing) A data storage device consisting of a flat, circular disk coated with magnetic material, used to store digital information.
magnetic data storage device

Examples

She inserted the backup disc into the computer to restore the lost files.
Magnetic discs were commonly used before hard drives became popular.
The data was corrupted on the old disc, so it could not be read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk - Alternative spelling, used especially in American English for computer storage devices

Etymology

From Latin 'discus', via French 'disque' and English 'disk', in the context of computing influenced by early disk storage technology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03712192-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, circular object, especially one used for recording and reproducing sound, such as a phonograph record.
flat audio recording medium

Examples

She placed the disc carefully on the turntable and started the music.
Collectors often search for rare jazz discs from the 1950s.
The old phonograph could still play a disc with remarkable clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a flat, circular device used to store and play recorded sound

Etymology

From Latin discus (quoit, disk), via French disque or directly from Latin.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
a flat, thin, typically circular object or plate
flat circular plate or object

Examples

The athlete hurled the disc across the field with great force.
Please place each sample on a clean glass disc for observation.
They replaced the old brake disc to improve the car's safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk - alternative spelling of 'disc', chiefly in American English

Etymology

From Latin 'discus' (quoit, disk, dish), via French or late Latin.
