laureled

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔr.əld / (AmE), / ˈlɒr.əld / (BrE)
Given or wearing a crown of laurel as a symbol of victory or achievement.
crowned with laurel (victor's symbol)

Examples

The laureled poet received a standing ovation for her work.
A laureled champion stood proudly on the podium after the finals.
He was a laureled war hero, honored by his country for bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowned - Having a crown; honored or garlanded

Etymology

From 'laurel' (the plant), whose leaves were used to crown victors in ancient Greece + '-ed' (forming adjectives).
