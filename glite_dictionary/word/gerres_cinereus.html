Gerres cinereus

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛriz sɪˈnɪriəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛərɪz sɪˈnɪəriəs / (BrE)
A species of mojarra, a common panfish found from Bermuda and the Gulf of Mexico to Brazil.
a mojarra fish species from W Atlantic

Examples

The Gerres cinereus is a popular catch among local fishermen in Bermuda.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Gerres cinereus along the Brazilian coast.
Seafood markets often sell Gerres cinereus as a fresh panfish option.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gerres' and species 'cinereus' meaning 'ash-gray.'
