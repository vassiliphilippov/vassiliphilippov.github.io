population

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Statistics) The complete set of individuals or items from which samples may be drawn for study.
entire group studied (statistics)

Examples

The researchers randomly selected 500 people from the population for their study.
A statistical inference can be made about the population based on data from a sample.
When calculating the mean, it's important to distinguish between a sample and the entire population.

Synset Examples

it is an estimate of the mean of the population

Etymology

From Latin 'populatio' (a people), from 'populus' (people).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
all the people who live in a particular area, city, or country, or a specific group of people within it
total number of people in an area

Examples

The population of the city grew rapidly over the past decade.
A large proportion of the senior population relies on public transportation.
Scientists are studying the effects of pollution on the local population.

Synset Examples

people come and go, but the population of this town has remained approximately constant for the past decade
the African-American population of Salt Lake City has been increasing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhabitants - people who live in a particular place
  • Synonym: demography - the study of populations and their characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'populatio' (a people, multitude), from 'populus' (people).

the act of populating a place

Examples

Synset Examples
