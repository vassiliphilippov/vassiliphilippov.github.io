sufficiency

1. NOUN

/ səˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / səˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being adequate or enough for a particular purpose
quality of being enough or adequate

Examples

The committee will assess the sufficiency of the funding provided for the project.
He doubted the sufficiency of his evidence to convince the jury.
A sufficiency of food and water is essential for survival in an emergency.

Synset Examples

he questioned the sufficiency of human intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adequacy - the state of being adequate
  • Antonym: insufficiency - the state of not being enough

Etymology

From Latin 'sufficientia' (adequacy), from 'sufficiens' (sufficient).

2. NOUN

/ səˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / səˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
the state of having enough resources or means to provide comfort or meet obligations
adequate supply of resources or means

Examples

The company's sufficiency allowed it to weather the economic downturn without layoffs.
Her parents questioned her fiancé's financial sufficiency before consenting to the marriage.
The foundation works to ensure sufficiency of food and shelter for all families in the region.

Synset Examples

her father questioned the young suitor's sufficiency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insufficiency - lack of adequate resources or means
  • Synonym: adequacy - the quality of being sufficient for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin sufficentia, from sufficere (to be sufficient).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ səˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / səˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
an amount that is enough for a particular purpose; the state of being adequate or sufficient
adequate quantity; enough for a purpose

Examples

The shelter had a sufficiency of food and blankets for all the guests during the storm.
There was no sufficiency of evidence to support the claim.
We must ensure a sufficiency of resources before launching the new project.

Synset Examples

enough is as good as a feast
there is more than a sufficiency of lawyers in this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adequacy - the quality of being enough to meet a need
  • Antonym: insufficiency - the state of not being sufficient; not enough
  • Antonym: excess - an amount more than necessary; surplus

Etymology

From Latin 'sufficientia' (ability to supply needs), from 'sufficiens' (sufficient).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
