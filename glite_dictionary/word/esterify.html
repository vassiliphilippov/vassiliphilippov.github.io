to esterify

1. VERB

/ ˈɛstərɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛstərɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause (a compound) to form an ester, typically by reaction with an acid and an alcohol
convert to an ester (chemistry)
medical

Examples

When you esterify a fatty acid with an alcohol, you obtain an ester and water as by-products.
Chemists often esterify compounds to modify their physical and chemical properties.
The reaction will esterify the carboxylic acids under acidic conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to hydrolyze - to break down a compound by reaction with water (chemistry)

Etymology

From 'ester' (chemical compound) + '-ify' (to make or become).
