chilling

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of fear, horror, or unease; frightening or disturbing
causing fear or horror

Examples

The documentary contained chilling details of the unsolved crimes.
Her voice had a chilling effect as she described the events.
There was a chilling silence after the horrifying news was announced.

Synset Examples

a scary movie
the most terrible and shuddery … tales of murder and revenge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frightening - causing fear or alarm
  • Degree Higher: terrifying - extremely frightening
  • Synonym: unsettling - making someone feel uneasy

2. NOUN

/ ˈt͡ʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈt͡ʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something cold or of becoming colder, especially by lowering the temperature deliberately
the process of becoming colder

Examples

The chilling of the drinks took only a few minutes in the freezer.
After the harvest, rapid chilling is required to preserve the freshness of the fruit.
Overnight chilling enhances the texture and flavor of the dessert.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: freezing - the process of a liquid turning into a solid due to cold
  • Degree Lower: cooling - the process of making or becoming less warm; reducing heat
