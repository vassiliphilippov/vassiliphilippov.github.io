Loriinae

1. NOUN

/ lɔːˈraɪ.ɪniː / (AmE), / lɒˈriː.ɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of parrots that includes lories and lorikeets, known for their brightly colored plumage and brush-tipped tongues.
a parrot subfamily (lories and lorikeets)

Examples

The Loriinae are known for their vivid colors and playful behavior.
Many species in the Loriinae subfamily have brush-tipped tongues adapted for feeding on nectar.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot rare members of the Loriinae in the rainforest.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on genus name Lorius, itself from French 'lori' (parrot) and Latin '-inae' (denoting a subfamily).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɔːriˈiːniː / (AmE), / ˌlɒrɪˈiːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of parrots including lories and lorikeets, known for their bright plumage and specialized brush-tipped tongues.
parrot subfamily including lories and lorikeets

Examples

The Loriinae are renowned for their vibrant feathers and playful behavior.
Many species within the Loriinae are native to Australasia and the surrounding islands.
Scientists have studied the unique feeding adaptations of the Loriinae for extracting nectar.

Etymology

From modern Latin (Lorius, a genus of parrot, + -inae, taxonomic suffix for subfamilies).
