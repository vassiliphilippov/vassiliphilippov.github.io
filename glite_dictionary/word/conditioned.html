conditioned

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) physically fit as a result of regular exercise or training.
physically fit due to training

Examples

After months of training, the athlete was fully conditioned for the marathon.
The soldiers must remain conditioned to meet the demands of their missions.
Her conditioned body allowed her to recover quickly from injuries.

Synset Examples

exercised daily to keep herself in condition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - in good physical shape; healthy and strong
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01021192-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃənd / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənd / (BrE)
established or influenced by training, experience, or learning, especially through repeated exposure or association.
established by training or learning

Examples

The dog developed a conditioned fear of loud noises after several storms.
Children can acquire conditioned habits through consistent routines at home.
A conditioned response occurs when a person or animal reacts in a learned way to a specific stimulus.

Synset Examples

a conditioned response

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learned - acquired knowledge or skill through study or experience
  • Antonym: innate - existing from birth; natural rather than learned

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'condition' + -ed; related to psychological terminology from the 19th–20th century.
