Matthew C. Perry

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæθu ˈsi ˈpɛri / (AmE), / ˈmæθjuː ˈsiː ˈpɛri / (BrE)
(1794–1858) An American naval officer known for leading an expedition to Japan and negotiating the 1854 treaty that opened diplomatic and trade relations between the United States and Japan.
American admiral who opened Japan to the West

Examples

Many historians credit Matthew C. Perry with playing a pivotal role in opening Japan to international trade.
The 1854 treaty negotiated by Matthew C. Perry marked a turning point in Japanese-American relations.
A statue of Matthew C. Perry stands in Yokosuka, Japan, commemorating his historic visit.
