Crambe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræm.bi / (AmE), / ˈkræm.bi / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbs with large leaves resembling those of cabbages, native to Europe and Asia.
genus of herbs with cabbage-like leaves

Examples

The oil extracted from Crambe seeds is used in industrial lubricants.
Crambe species are often cultivated for their edible leaves and seeds.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Crambe in different soil types.

Etymology

From Latin crambe, from Greek krambē (a kind of cabbage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræmbi / (AmE), / ˈkrambi / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbs in the family Brassicaceae, with large, cabbage-like leaves.
genus of herbs with large, cabbage-like leaves

Examples

The leaves of Crambe species are sometimes cultivated for use as a vegetable.
Botanists identified the plant as belonging to the genus Crambe.
Some varieties of Crambe are grown for their edible roots and seeds.

Etymology

From Greek *krambē* (cabbage).
