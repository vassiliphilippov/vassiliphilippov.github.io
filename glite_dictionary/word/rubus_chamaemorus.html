Rubus chamaemorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs kæmiˈmɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ˌkæmiˈmɔːrəs / (BrE)
A creeping perennial plant of northern temperate regions, bearing yellow or orange edible berries; also known as the cloudberry.
creeping plant with edible orange-yellow berries

Examples

The Rubus chamaemorus thrives in cool, boggy regions of Scandinavia and northern Canada.
You can identify Rubus chamaemorus by its low growth and distinctive orange berries.
Jams made from Rubus chamaemorus are considered a delicacy in some northern countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudberry - common name for Rubus chamaemorus

Etymology

From Latin: genus Rubus (bramble) and species chamaemorus, from Greek chamai (on the ground) and moros (mulberry).
