drippage

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪp.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪp.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
Liquid that falls or is released in drops, especially from a surface such as the edge of a roof.
liquid falling in drops

Examples

The constant drippage from the gutter kept the walkway wet for days.
After a heavy rain, we noticed significant drippage along the windowsill.
The maintenance crew fixed the roof to prevent further drippage into the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drip - A small drop of liquid that falls or hangs from something.

Etymology

From 'drip' + '-age', an English suffix forming nouns indicating an action or result.
