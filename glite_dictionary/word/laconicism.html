laconicism

1. NOUN

/ ləˈkɑːnɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ləˈkɒnɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of expressing much in few words; terseness or brevity of expression
terse or concise expression

Examples

The laconicism of his speech made a strong impression on the audience.
In literature, laconicism is valued for its power to convey meaning efficiently.
Her emails are known for their laconicism, getting straight to the point without unnecessary detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciseness - the quality of being concise
  • Antonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed

Etymology

From Greek 'Lakonismos', from Lakonizein (to speak like a Spartan), Lakon (Spartan).
