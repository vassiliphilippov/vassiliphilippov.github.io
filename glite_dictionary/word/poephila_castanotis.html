Poephila castanotis

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈfiːlə kæstəˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / pəʊˈfiːlɪə kæs.təˈnəʊ.tɪs / (BrE)
A small Australian finch, also known as the zebra finch, characterized by black-and-white markings resembling those of a zebra.
Australian finch, zebra-like markings

Examples

The Poephila castanotis is popular among bird enthusiasts for its lively song and vibrant plumage.
In their natural habitat, Poephila castanotis build intricate nests using grass and twigs.
Many researchers study the vocalizations of Poephila castanotis to learn about animal communication.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'poephilos' (grass-loving) and Latin 'castanotis' (chestnut-eared).
