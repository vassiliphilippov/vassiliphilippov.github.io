William Lloyd Garrison

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm lɔɪd ˈɡærɪsən / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm lɔɪd ˈɡærɪsən / (BrE)
(1805–1879) An American abolitionist, journalist, and social reformer, best known for publishing an influential anti-slavery newspaper, The Liberator.
American abolitionist and journalist

Examples

William Lloyd Garrison fiercely advocated for the immediate abolition of slavery in the United States.
The newspaper The Liberator was edited by William Lloyd Garrison for over three decades.
Historians credit William Lloyd Garrison with inspiring many activists in the abolitionist movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frederick Douglass - Prominent African-American abolitionist, author, and orator
