to exposit

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɑzɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪt / (BrE)
To explain or set forth an idea or account in detail, often in a scholarly or formal manner.
explain in detail; expound

Examples

The professor took time to exposit the complex philosophical theories to his students.
She was invited to exposit her views on the subject at the international conference.
In his lengthy article, he exposited the relationship between art and society.

Synset Examples

She elaborated on the main ideas in her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expound - to present and explain systematically and in detail
  • Synonym: to elucidate - to make clear; to explain
  • Antonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin exponere (to set forth, explain), via Late Latin expositus (explained).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɑzɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪt / (BrE)
to explain or set forth an idea or meaning in detail; to expound
to explain or expound in detail

Examples

The professor began to exposit the principles of quantum mechanics for the new students.
She often exposits complex theories in a way that is easy to understand.
During the lecture, he exposited the historical background of the poem at length.

Synset Examples

set forth one's reasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expound - to explain or interpret in detail
  • Synonym: to interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin expositus, past participle of exponere (to put forth, explain).
