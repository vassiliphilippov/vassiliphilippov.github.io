demoniacal

1. ADJ

/ dɪˌmoʊniˈækəl / (AmE), / dɪˌməʊnɪˈækəl / (BrE)
Resembling, characteristic of, or possessed by a demon or evil spirit; extremely wicked or frenzied.
like a demon; possessed by evil spirit

Examples

During the ritual, his eyes gleamed with a demoniacal intensity that frightened everyone present.
The villain gave a demoniacal laugh as he set his wicked plan in motion.
Some believed the sudden outburst was driven by demoniacal possession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonic - having the characteristics of demons or evil spirits

Etymology

From Late Latin 'demonīacus' (possessed by a demon), from Greek 'daimoníakos'.
