cloche

1. NOUN

/ kloʊʃ / (AmE), / klɒʃ / (BrE)
a woman's close-fitting hat with a bell-shaped design, popular especially in the 1920s
close-fitting bell-shaped woman's hat

Examples

She wore a stylish cloche to the vintage party, completing her 1920s outfit.
The cloche hat hugged her head and framed her face elegantly.
Many designers revived the classic cloche look in their latest autumn collections.

Etymology

From French 'cloche', meaning 'bell', referring to the hat's shape.

2. NOUN

/ kloʊʃ / (AmE), / klɒʃ / (BrE)
A bell-shaped, usually transparent cover placed over young plants to protect them from cold weather.
protective plant cover

Examples

The gardener placed a cloche over the seedlings to shield them from frost.
A plastic cloche can help extend the growing season for tender vegetables.
Removing the cloche in the morning lets the plants get plenty of sunlight.

Etymology

From French 'cloche' meaning 'bell', referring to its shape.
