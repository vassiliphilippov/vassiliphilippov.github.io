dace

1. NOUN

/ deɪs / (AmE), / deɪs / (BrE)
a small European freshwater fish with a slender bluish-green body, typically found in rivers and streams
small European freshwater fish

Examples

Anglers often seek out the dace for its lively fight when using light tackle.
The crystal-clear stream was full of dace swimming against the current.
Scientists studied the population of dace to monitor water quality in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - small freshwater fish related to carp
  • Synonym: roach - another type of small European freshwater fish

Etymology

From Old French 'dars', possibly related to Old High German 'tarhs' (fish), of uncertain origin.
