incursion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːʃən / (BrE)
an act of entering a place or domain, especially in a way that is sudden or unwanted, often in large numbers
sudden entry into a place or domain

Examples

The new regulations were seen as an incursion into personal freedom.
Environmentalists warn of the incursion of invasive species into the local ecosystem.
The incursion of foreign companies changed the landscape of the technology industry.

Synset Examples

the incursion of television into the American living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - an instance of entering a place, especially by force or in large numbers

Etymology

From Latin 'incursio' (attack, invasion), from 'incurrere' (to run into).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɝːʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːʃən / (BrE)
a sudden or brief attack into enemy territory, especially to make a raid or foray
sudden attack into enemy territory

Examples

The border guards responded quickly to the incursion by enemy troops.
There was an incursion into the disputed territory at dawn yesterday.
The general planned a surprise incursion deep into hostile land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raid - a sudden attack, especially one by police or soldiers
  • Synonym: invasion - an instance of invading a country or region with armed force
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing from enemy forces as a result of their superior power or after a defeat

Etymology

From Latin incursio (a running in, attack), from incurrere (to run into).
