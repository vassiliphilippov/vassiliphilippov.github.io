doggo

1. ADV

/ ˈdɔɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡəʊ / (BrE)
(informal) In hiding or lying quietly to avoid notice; in concealment.
in hiding or lying quiet
informal

Examples

The fugitives lay doggo until the coast was clear.
He decided to stay doggo in the cellar to avoid being seen.
During the surprise inspection, everyone simply went doggo and waited.

Synset Examples

he lay doggo

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from English dialect 'dog' meaning to lie low, or from 'dog' + 'go' as in the phrase 'to lie doggo'.
