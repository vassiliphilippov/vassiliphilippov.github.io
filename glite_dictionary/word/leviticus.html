Leviticus

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈvɪtɪkəs / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪtɪkəs / (BrE)
the third book of the Old Testament, containing laws and religious rituals for the Israelites
the third book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many dietary laws are outlined in Leviticus in great detail.
Scholars have long debated the origins of the holiness code in Leviticus.
She quoted a passage from Leviticus during the lecture on ancient law.

Etymology

From Latin 'Leviticus', from Greek 'Leuitikon', meaning 'of the Levites' (priestly tribe of Israel).
