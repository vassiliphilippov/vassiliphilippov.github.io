despiteful

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspaɪtfəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪtfəl / (BrE)
Showing malicious ill will and a desire to hurt; full of spite or motivated by vindictiveness.
full of spite or ill will
archaic

Examples

Her despiteful words left a lasting wound on his reputation.
The despiteful actions of the villain were central to the story.
Even in defeat, he remained despiteful towards his former friends.

Synset Examples

a despiteful fiend
a truly spiteful child
a vindictive man will look for occasions for resentment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiteful - having or showing a desire to harm, anger, or defeat someone
  • Synonym: vindictive - having or showing a strong desire for revenge
  • Antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature

Etymology

From Middle English 'despitful', from Old French 'despit' meaning spite, plus the suffix '-ful'.
