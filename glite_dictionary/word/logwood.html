logwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
a spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, known for its hard, dark heartwood used to produce a black dye
tree yielding wood for dye

Examples

The heartwood of the logwood tree is highly valued for producing dyes.
Early textile industries depended on logwood to create richly colored fabrics.
In the Caribbean, logwood was once a major export commodity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
the very hard brown to brownish-red heartwood of a tropical American tree, used especially for preparing a purplish-red dye
hard wood yielding dye from a tropical tree

Examples

The dye extracted from logwood was once highly prized for coloring fabrics.
Many artists use logwood as a natural source for deep purple ink.
The furniture maker preferred the strength of logwood for his handcrafted chairs.

Etymology

From 'log' (tree trunk) and 'wood'; refers to the wood of the tree Haematoxylum campechianum, native to Central America.
