copestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpˌstəʊn / (BrE)
the stone that forms the top or highest part of a wall or building, serving as a protective or decorative cap
top stone of a wall or building

Examples

The mason carefully set the copestone to complete the garden wall.
Rainwater trickled down from the copestone after the storm.
Replacing the old copestone restored the elegance of the historic building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capstone - a stone fixed on top of something, typically a wall; often used interchangeably with copestone

Etymology

From cope (Middle English, 'top, covering') + stone.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpˌstəʊn / (BrE)
The final or crowning touch; the culminating point of an achievement or process.
final or crowning touch; culmination

Examples

The new hospital wing was the copestone of the city's ambitious development plan.
Winning the Nobel Prize was the copestone of her scientific career.
Their performance acted as the copestone to a season filled with hard work and dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capstone - the highest point or final stone; a crowning achievement

Etymology

From 'cope' (the top or covering of a structure) + 'stone'.
