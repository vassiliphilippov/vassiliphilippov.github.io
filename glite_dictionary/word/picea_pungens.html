Picea pungens

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiːə ˈpʌndʒəns / (AmE), / pɪˈsiːə ˈpʌndʒəns / (BrE)
A species of evergreen tree known as the blue spruce, native to western North America, with blue-green needles and a conical or columnar shape.
species of blue spruce tree

Examples

The Picea pungens is often planted in parks for its striking blue foliage.
Gardeners admire the resilience of Picea pungens in harsh winter conditions.
The needles of Picea pungens are stiff, sharp, and give the tree its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue spruce - common name for Picea pungens, an evergreen coniferous tree

Etymology

Latin: 'Picea' meaning spruce and 'pungens' meaning sharply pointed, referring to the needles.
