dangerously

1. ADV

/ ˈdeɪndʒərəsli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒərəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is likely to cause harm or injury; in a dangerous manner.
in a harmful or risky manner

Examples

The car skidded dangerously close to the edge of the cliff during the storm.
She was dangerously ill for several weeks but eventually recovered.
The chemicals were stored dangerously near a heat source, posing a serious risk.

Synset Examples

he came dangerously close to falling off the ledge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safely - in a manner that avoids danger
  • Synonym: hazardously - in a way that involves risk or danger

Etymology

From 'dangerous' + '-ly', where 'dangerous' comes from Old French 'dangereus'.
