greenhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhʊd / (BrE)
any of various terrestrial orchids of the genus Pterostylis, characterized by leaves in a basal rosette and green flowers with a hood-like dorsal sepal, often striped with purple, brown, or red.
a terrestrial orchid of the genus Pterostylis

Examples

The rare greenhood orchid can be found in damp woodlands during the spring.
Several species of greenhood are native to Australia and New Zealand.
Botanists studied the unique pollination mechanism of the greenhood flower.

Etymology

From 'green' + 'hood', referring to the green hood-shaped sepal of the flower.
