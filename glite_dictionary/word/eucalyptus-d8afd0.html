Eucalyptus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkæləptəs / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall trees native to Australia, known for their aromatic leaves which yield medicinal oils and for their timber.
genus of tall Australian trees

Examples

The vast forests were dominated by tall Eucalyptus trees reaching toward the sky.
Oil from the leaves of the Eucalyptus is often used in cough remedies.
Many species within the Eucalyptus genus are cultivated worldwide for timber and shade.

Etymology

From modern Latin Eucalyptus; from Greek eu- (well) + kalyptos (covered), referring to the flower buds.
