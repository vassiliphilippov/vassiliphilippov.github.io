Quercus lyrata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs laɪˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs laɪˈreɪtə / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous oak tree of central and southern United States, notable for its acorns which are deeply enclosed in the cup and mature within one year; also known as the overcup oak.
species of oak tree (overcup oak)

Examples

The Quercus lyrata is often found in lowland forests and floodplains in the southern United States.
Botanists studied the unique acorn structure of Quercus lyrata to understand its adaptation.
Because of its tolerance to wet soils, Quercus lyrata is planted for erosion control near rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcup oak - common name for Quercus lyrata, species of oak tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak', and 'lyrata' meaning 'lyre-shaped', referring to the shape of its leaves.
