to ravel

1. VERB

/ ˈrævəl / (AmE), / ˈrævəl / (BrE)
to untangle or separate the threads or elements of something; to disentangle
untangle or disentangle

Examples

She carefully tried to ravel the threads of the old sweater.
The detective managed to ravel the truth from a tangle of lies.
It took hours to ravel the cords that had been left in a heap.

Synset Examples

can you unravel the mystery?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unravel - to undo or separate the threads; to make clear or disentangle

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'ravelen' (to tangle, fray).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01524000-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈrævəl / (AmE), / ˈrævəl / (BrE)
to tangle or complicate something; to make something more confusing or complex
to tangle or complicate

Examples

The unwelcome new evidence only ravelled the mystery further.
Her explanation ravelled the issue instead of clarifying it.
Bureaucratic procedures often ravel even the simplest tasks.

Synset Examples

a ravelled story

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unravel - to make or become untangled or clear
  • Synonym: tangle - to twist together into a confused mass

Etymology

From Dutch *ravelen* (to tangle, fray) or Middle Low German *raveln*.
