LGBTQ

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl dʒiː biː tiː ˈkjuː / (AmE), / ˌɛl dʒiː biː tiː ˈkjuː / (BrE)
the community of people whose sexual orientation or gender identity is not heterosexual or cisgender, including but not limited to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer or questioning individuals
the community of non-heterosexual and/or non-cisgender people

Examples

The LGBTQ community has made significant progress in the fight for equal rights.
Many schools now offer resources and support for LGBTQ students and staff.
Pride Month celebrates the history and achievements of LGBTQ people everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LGBT - the community of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender people

Etymology

Acronym formed from the initial letters of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer or questioning.
