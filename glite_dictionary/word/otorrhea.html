otorrhea

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊtəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌɒtəˈriːə / (BrE)
Discharge or fluid leaking from the external ear.
discharge from the ear
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a clear otorrhea during the examination.
Persistent otorrhea may be a sign of ear infection or trauma.
After swimming, she developed otorrhea and mild ear pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ear discharge - fluid or pus coming from the ear

Etymology

From Greek 'ous, ōtos' (ear) and 'rhoia' (flow).
