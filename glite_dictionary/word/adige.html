Adige

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːdɪdʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈaːdɪdʒeɪ / (BrE)
a river in northern Italy that flows southeast into the Adriatic Sea, passing through cities such as Verona and Trento.
river in northern Italy

Examples

Verona lies on the banks of the Adige and is famous for its Roman amphitheater.
The Adige is the second longest river in Italy after the Po.
Many cyclists enjoy riding along the picturesque Adige valley.

Synset Examples

Verona is on the Adige

Etymology

From Italian 'Adige', ultimately from Latin 'Athesis'.
