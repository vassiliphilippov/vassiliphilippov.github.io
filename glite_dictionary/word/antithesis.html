antithesis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪθəsɪs / (AmE), / ænˈtɪθɪsɪs / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the direct opposite of someone or something else.
exact opposite

Examples

Her views are the antithesis of everything I believe in.
Honesty is the antithesis of corruption.
This modern sculpture is the antithesis of classical art.

Synset Examples

his theory is the antithesis of mine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - something or someone that is completely different from another

Etymology

From Greek antithesis (opposition, contrast), from anti- (against) + thesis (placing).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪθəsɪs / (AmE), / ænˈtɪθɪsɪs / (BrE)
the use of two contrasting or opposing words or ideas placed together, often to create a striking effect or sense of balance
juxtaposition of contrasting ideas for effect

Examples

The writer used antithesis to highlight the difference between darkness and light in his poem.
Her speech was full of antithesis, pairing hope with despair and courage with fear.
"It was the best of times, it was the worst of times" is a famous example of antithesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - a striking difference between two things
  • Synonym: juxtaposition - placing things close together for comparison or contrast

Etymology

From Greek antithesis, meaning 'opposition', from anti- ('against') + tithenai ('to set').
