Ustilaginoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːstɪləˌdʒɪˈnɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌjuːstɪləˌdʒɪˈnɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi known for causing plant diseases such as smut.
genus of plant-pathogenic fungi
medical

Examples

Many rice crops have been devastated by infections caused by Ustilaginoidea species.
The laboratory is studying the life cycle of Ustilaginoidea to find ways to control crop diseases.
Farmers are concerned about the spread of Ustilaginoidea in their wheat fields this season.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, coined from the related genus Ustilago (Latin 'to burn') and Greek -eidos ('form, resemblance').
