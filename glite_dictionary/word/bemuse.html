to bemuse

1. VERB

/ bɪˈmjuz / (AmE), / bɪˈmjuːz / (BrE)
to cause someone to be confused or puzzled, often in a way that makes them uncertain how to react
confuse or puzzle emotionally

Examples

The teacher's vague explanation seemed only to bemuse the students.
His sudden change in attitude bemused everyone at the meeting.
She stared at the complicated instructions, thoroughly bemused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject or situation

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) and 'muse', meaning to think deeply.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈmjuz / (AmE), / bɪˈmjuːz / (BrE)
To confuse or puzzle someone, often to the point of bewilderment.
confuse or puzzle someone

Examples

His shifting arguments continued to bemuse the committee members.
The magician's trick seemed to bemuse the entire audience.
She was bemused by the cryptic instructions on the form.

Synset Examples

Her unexpected response seemed to bemuse him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + muse (to ponder), first used in the late 18th century.
