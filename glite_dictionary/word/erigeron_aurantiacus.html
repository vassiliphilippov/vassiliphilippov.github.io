Erigeron aurantiacus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪdʒərəˌn ɔˌræntiˈeɪkəs / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdʒərɒn ɔːˌræntiˈeɪkəs / (BrE)
A mat-forming herb native to Turkestan, noted for its nearly double orange-yellow flowers.
mat-forming herb with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

Many gardeners prize Erigeron aurantiacus for its compact growth and vibrant orange-yellow blossoms.
In the wild, Erigeron aurantiacus thrives in the rocky soils of Turkestan.
The nearly double flowers of Erigeron aurantiacus make it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Erigeron from Greek 'ērigērōn', meaning 'early old man' (referring to early flowering and downy pappus); species epithet aurantiacus meaning 'orange-colored' in Latin.
