Aerobacter aerogenes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈbæktər ˌɛrəˈdʒiːniːz / (AmE), / ˌɛərəʊˈbæktə ˌɛərəʊˈdʒiːniːz / (BrE)
A species of Gram-negative, aerobic bacteria that produces gas and acid from sugars and is sometimes involved in the souring of milk.
species of Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Aerobacter aerogenes is commonly found in soil and water environments.
The souring of milk can result from the growth of Aerobacter aerogenes under certain conditions.
Researchers studied how Aerobacter aerogenes produces gas from different types of sugars.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Aerobacter' from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'bacter' (rod), and 'aerogenes' meaning 'gas-producing'.
