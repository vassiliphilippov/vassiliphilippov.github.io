Tarquin

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑr.kwɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.kwɪn / (BrE)
according to legend, the seventh and last Etruscan king of Rome, who reigned from 534 to 510 BC and was expelled for his cruelty
legendary last king of Rome

Examples

Many historians debate the veracity of stories about Tarquin and his rule over Rome.
The expulsion of Tarquin marked the beginning of the Roman Republic.
Tarquin was said to have earned his people's hatred through acts of cruelty and oppression.

Etymology

From Latin Tarquinius, the name of the Roman royal family Tarquinii.
