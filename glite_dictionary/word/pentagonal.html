pentagonal

1. ADJ

/ pɛnˈtæg.ə.nəl / (AmE), / pɛnˈtæɡ.ə.nəl / (BrE)
having five sides or relating to a pentagon.
having five sides; like a pentagon

Examples

The architect designed a pentagonal courtyard at the center of the building.
Each face of the dodecahedron is a pentagonal shape.
We admired the unique pentagonal window in the old chapel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hexagonal - having six sides or relating to a hexagon
  • Degree Lower: quadrilateral - a four-sided polygon

Etymology

From Greek 'pentagōnon' (five-angled), from 'penta-' (five) and 'gōnia' (angle).
