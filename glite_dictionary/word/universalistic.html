universalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuː.nɪˌvɝː.səˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˌvɜː.səˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or advocating universalism, especially the belief that a system or set of values applies to all people or situations.
relating to universalism

Examples

Her universalistic approach to ethics seeks principles that apply to all human beings.
The organization promotes universalistic values such as equality and justice.
Many religions have both particularistic and universalistic elements in their teachings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: particularistic - relating to the exclusive attachment to a particular group, interest, or system
  • Synonym: universalism - the belief or doctrine that universal facts exist and can be progressively discovered

Etymology

Formed from "universalism" + "-istic".

2. ADJ

/ juːnɪˌvɜrsəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / juːnɪˌvɜːsəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or concerning the whole; having an inclusive or all-encompassing quality or approach.
relating to the whole or all-inclusive

Examples

The philosophy course explored a universalistic approach to ethics that applies to all people.
Some religions take a universalistic view, embracing all humanity under one doctrine.
Her analysis was criticized for being too universalistic and not accounting for cultural differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: particularistic - relating to a particular group, person, or interest
  • Synonym: universal - including or covering all or a whole collectively or distributively without limit or exception

Etymology

From 'universal' + '-istic', relating to universality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03154954-a
Synset POS: a
