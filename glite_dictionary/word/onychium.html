Onychium

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnɪkiəm / (AmE), / əʊˈnɪkiəm / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial ferns native to the Old World tropics and subtropics, known as clawed ferns; sometimes classified in the family Cryptogrammataceae.
genus of small tropical ferns

Examples

The Onychium ferns are easily identified by their finely divided, claw-shaped fronds.
Several species of Onychium are found thriving in the humid forests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists often debate the correct family placement of Onychium in fern classification.

Etymology

From Greek *onyx* (nail, claw), referring to the claw-like shape of the fern's fronds.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɪkiəm / (AmE), / ɒˈnɪkɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial ferns found in the Old World tropics and subtropics, commonly known as clawed ferns.
genus of tropical clawed ferns

Examples

The ferns belonging to the genus Onychium are characterized by their distinctive claw-shaped leaflets.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within Onychium in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Many horticulturists cultivate Onychium for its delicate, attractive fronds.

Etymology

From Greek onychion, diminutive of onyx (claw, fingernail), referring to the shape of the fern's leaflets.
