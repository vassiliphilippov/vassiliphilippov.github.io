intruder

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruːdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːdə / (BrE)
A person who enters a place without permission, especially to commit a crime or disturb others.
person who enters without permission

Examples

The alarm was triggered when an intruder entered the house through the back door.
Security cameras caught the intruder lurking around the property late at night.
The family was frightened by the sight of an intruder in their living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespasser - a person who unlawfully enters another's property
  • Antonym: guest - a person who is invited to visit or stay in someone's home

Etymology

From Latin 'intrudere' (to thrust in), via Old French 'intruder'.
