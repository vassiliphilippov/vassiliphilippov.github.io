Macrozamia spiralis

1. PROPN

/ ˌmækroʊˈzeɪmiə spaɪˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈzeɪmɪə spaɪˈralɪs / (BrE)
a large, palmlike, evergreen cycad native to New South Wales, Australia, with spirally arranged leaves
a cycad species native to New South Wales

Examples

Many gardens in Sydney cultivate Macrozamia spiralis for its striking, spiral-shaped foliage.
Macrozamia spiralis is an evergreen plant that can live for many decades in the wild.
The distinctive leaves of Macrozamia spiralis make it popular among landscapers in New South Wales.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'macro-' (large) and 'zamia' (a genus of cycads), with 'spiralis' referring to the spiral arrangement of the leaves.
