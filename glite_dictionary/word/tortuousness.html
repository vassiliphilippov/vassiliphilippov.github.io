tortuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃuəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being full of twists, turns, or bends; a winding or twisting form or condition
quality of being twisted or winding

Examples

The tortuousness of the mountain road made driving slow and difficult.
She admired the tortuousness of the old vine that crept along the fence.
The tortuousness of his argument left everyone confused.

Synset Examples

they built a tree house in the tortuosities of its boughs
the acrobat performed incredible contortions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twistiness - the quality of being twisted or winding
  • Antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight or having no twists

Etymology

From Latin 'tortuosus' (full of twists), from 'torquere' (to twist).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being full of twists and turns, or being excessively complex and difficult to follow
quality of being winding or complex

Examples

The tortuousness of the novel's plot left many readers confused.
Scientists studied the tortuousness of the river as it wound through the valley.
She explained her reasoning with such tortuousness that nobody could follow her argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being intricate or complicated
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being easy to understand or straightforward

Etymology

From tortuous + -ness; 'tortuous' from Latin 'tortuosus', from 'torquere' (to twist).
