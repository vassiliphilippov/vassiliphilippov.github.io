insulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of being separated or protected from outside influence; isolation or detachment
state of being isolated or detached

Examples

The English Channel provided natural insulation from continental Europe.
Her emotional insulation helped her cope with criticism.
Geographical insulation allowed the culture to preserve its traditions.

Synset Examples

the insulation of England was preserved by the English Channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the process or fact of being separated from others

Etymology

From Latin insulatus (made into an island), from insula (island).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of protecting something by surrounding it with a material that reduces or prevents the passage of heat, sound, or electricity.
material or process that blocks heat, sound, or electricity

Examples

The attic needed better insulation to keep the house warm in winter.
Adding foam insulation will help reduce noise from the street.
Proper insulation is essential for energy-efficient buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insulate - to protect with a material that blocks heat, sound, or electricity

Etymology

From Latin *insula* (island), meaning 'to make into an island' or isolate.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00828671-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Material used to reduce or prevent the passage of heat, sound, or electricity from one area to another.
material preventing heat, sound, or electricity transfer

Examples

The attic was filled with thick insulation to keep the house warm in winter.
Electrical wires are covered with insulation to prevent accidental shocks.
Soundproof insulation was installed in the studio to reduce outside noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulator - a device or material that insulates
  • Antonym: conductor - a material that allows the flow of heat, sound, or electricity

Etymology

From Latin 'insulatus' (made into an island) via 'insulate'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
