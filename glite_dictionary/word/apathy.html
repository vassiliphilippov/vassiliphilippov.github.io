apathy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəθi / (AmE), / ˈapəθi / (BrE)
lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern; absence of emotion or motivation.
lack of emotion or interest

Examples

Her apathy toward the project was evident in her lack of participation.
Rising political apathy among young people has become a growing concern.
He faced his studies with apathy, showing little interest in the subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense interest or eagerness

Etymology

From Greek apatheia (absence of feeling), from a- (without) + pathos (emotion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpəθi / (AmE), / ˈapəθi / (BrE)
A lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
lack of interest or enthusiasm

Examples

His apathy towards the project frustrated his colleagues, who expected more enthusiasm.
Voter apathy is often blamed for low turnout during elections.
Despite the exciting news, she showed only apathy and didn't react at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Greek apatheia (freedom from feeling, absence of passion), from a- (without) + pathos (feeling).
