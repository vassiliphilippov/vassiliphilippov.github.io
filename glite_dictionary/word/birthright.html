birthright

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
a right, possession, or privilege that a person is entitled to from birth, especially by virtue of being the firstborn child
right or privilege by birth

Examples

He considered freedom of expression to be his birthright.
As the eldest son, the birthright of the family estate belonged to him.
They believed education was a basic birthright of every citizen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - money, property, or title received as an heir at someone's death
  • Synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage granted to a person or group

Etymology

Formed from 'birth' + 'right' in Middle English, modeled on similar constructions in Old English and other Germanic languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrˌθraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθraɪt / (BrE)
a right, privilege, or possession to which a person is entitled from birth
right or privilege by birth

Examples

Education is considered a birthright in many modern societies.
Many people fought to secure civil liberties as their birthright.
Freedom of speech is seen as a basic birthright in democratic countries.

Synset Examples

free public education is the birthright of every American child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - something passed down from parents, especially property or rights

Etymology

From Middle English birtheright, from birth + right.
