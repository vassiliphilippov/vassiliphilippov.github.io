collar

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
to seize or grasp someone by the neck or by the collar of their clothing, often to restrain or apprehend them
seize or grab by the neck or collar

Examples

The officer managed to collar the suspect as he tried to escape.
She quickly collared her dog before it could run into the street.
He collared me after the meeting to ask a few questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly and roughly
  • Antonym: release - to set free or let go
