lubricity

1. NOUN

/ luˈbrɪsəti / (AmE), / luˈbrɪsɪti / (BrE)
a tendency to lewd or lustful behavior; sexual wantonness or lasciviousness, often with a connotation of excess or moral disapproval
lewd or lascivious behavior

Examples

The novel was notorious for its open depiction of lubricity among the characters.
He viewed the paintings as expressions of lubricity, shocking the moral sensibilities of his time.
Critics accused the playwright of indulging in unnecessary lubricity in his latest work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lasciviousness - lewdness or overt sexuality
  • Antonym: chastity - sexual purity or abstinence

Etymology

From Latin lubricitas (slipperiness, wantonness), from lubricus (slippery).
