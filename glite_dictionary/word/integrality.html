integrality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈɡrælə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tɪˈɡræl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being whole, total, or complete; entirety.
quality of being whole or complete

Examples

The integrality of the plan gave everyone confidence in its success.
We must maintain the integrality of the data throughout the process.
She was praised for her commitment to the integrality of her research methods.

Synset Examples

he read the article in its entirety
appalled by the totality of the destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirety - the state of being entire or whole
  • Synonym: totality - the state of being total; completeness
  • Antonym: partiality - the state of being only part, not whole

Etymology

From Latin 'integralis' (making up a whole), from 'integer' (whole).
