lechery

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈlɛtʃəri / (BrE)
excessive or offensive sexual desire; unrestrained indulgence in sexual activity
unrestrained sexual indulgence

Examples

His reputation for lechery made him unpopular in polite society.
The novel portrayed an era marked by decadence and lechery.
He was dismissed from his position because of persistent allegations of lechery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - strong sexual desire

Etymology

From Old French *lecherie*, from *lecher* (to lick, indulge in lust), ultimately from Late Latin *līcāri* (to lick).
