unimproved

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
not developed, improved, or made more valuable, especially not made ready for use, marketing, or habitation.
not developed or enhanced

Examples

Taxes on unimproved land are generally lower than those on developed property.
The house was sold as an unimproved property, requiring significant investment to be habitable.
Many rural areas still have unimproved dirt roads that can become impassable after heavy rain.

Synset Examples

taxes on unimproved land are low
unimproved dirt roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improved - made better or more valuable

Etymology

From un- (not) + improved, from improve (make better).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01292352-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
(of land) in its natural state, not cleared, developed, or altered by human activity.
land: not developed or altered

Examples

The estate includes 100 acres of unimproved land, mostly covered by dense forest.
Prices for unimproved lots are generally much lower than for those with utilities or buildings.
They purchased an unimproved parcel on the outskirts of town to build their dream home.

Synset Examples

a farm with 50 acres of unimproved and 68 acres of improved land
unimproved woodlands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improved - land that has been developed or cleared for use

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + improved, meaning not made better or developed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01293670-s
Synset POS: s
