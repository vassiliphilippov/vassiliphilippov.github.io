isoleucine

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈluːsin / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈljuːsiːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid occurring in proteins, isomeric with leucine, necessary for human nutrition
an essential amino acid in proteins
medical

Examples

Athletes often monitor their isoleucine intake to support muscle recovery after workouts.
Eggs and dairy products are good dietary sources of isoleucine.
Deficiency in isoleucine can lead to symptoms like fatigue and muscle loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leucine - an essential amino acid isomeric with isoleucine

Etymology

From iso- (meaning 'equal') and leucine, reflecting its isomeric relation to leucine.
