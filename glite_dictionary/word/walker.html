walker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
a shoe designed to provide comfort and support when walking, as opposed to for running or formal wear
shoe designed for comfortable walking

Examples

She bought a pair of walkers for her morning exercise routines in the park.
These walkers have extra cushioning to support your feet during long walks.
Many people prefer walkers over regular sneakers for everyday use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
a frame with wheels or casters that supports a baby while learning to walk
device that helps babies walk

Examples

My nephew loves pushing his new walker across the living room floor.
Always keep an eye on your child while they are in a walker.
The walker helps give babies confidence as they take their first steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby walker - a frame on wheels to help babies learn to walk

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who travels or moves about on foot, especially for exercise or recreation
person who travels by foot

Examples

Every morning, the old walker enjoyed a peaceful stroll around the park.
As a dedicated walker, she has explored many urban trails in the city.
The marathon included a category for walkers as well as runners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiker - a person who goes for long walks, especially in the countryside
  • Antonym: runner - a person who runs as a form of exercise or sport

Etymology

From the verb 'walk' + '-er', denoting someone who walks.

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkə / (BrE)
a frame or device on wheels or rubber tips, with handles, used to help people who have difficulty walking move more easily and safely
frame to aid walking

Examples

My grandmother uses a walker to move safely around the house.
The hospital provided a walker after his hip surgery.
Some walkers come with a basket for carrying personal items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zimmer frame - British term for a walking aid similar to a walker
  • Synonym: rollator - rolling walking aid with a seat and wheels
