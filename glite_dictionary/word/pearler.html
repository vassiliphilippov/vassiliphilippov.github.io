pearler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːlər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːlə / (BrE)
a person who dives for molluscs in search of pearls
diver who searches for pearls

Examples

The pearler spent hours searching the ocean floor for valuable shells.
In olden days, many pearlers risked their lives diving without modern equipment.
Stories about legendary pearlers and their finds are common in coastal towns.

Etymology

Formed from 'pearl' + '-er', denoting a person associated with pearls.
