faraway

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑːr.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.rə.weɪ / (BrE)
distant in space or time; remote.
distant in space or time

Examples

She dreamed of visiting faraway lands and experiencing new cultures.
The explorer gazed at the faraway mountains with awe.
We spoke about the faraway future and our hopes for the world.

Synset Examples

faraway mountains
the faraway future
troops landing on far-off shores
far-off happier times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote - far from the main centers of population; distant
  • Antonym: nearby - close at hand; not far away

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑr.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.rə.weɪ / (BrE)
looking as if thinking about something distant or lost in thought; not present mentally
distant in thought

Examples

She had a faraway look in her eyes as she gazed out the window.
His expression was faraway, lost in memories of years past.
During the meeting, her thoughts became faraway and she hardly heard what was said.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distant - not present mentally; lost in thought
