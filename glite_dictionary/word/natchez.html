Natchez

1. PROPN

/ ˈnætʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈnætʃɪz / (BrE)
A town in southwest Mississippi, located on the Mississippi River, known for its historic architecture and role in the antebellum South.
town in southwest Mississippi, USA

Examples

The historic mansions in Natchez attract tourists from all over the country.
During their road trip, they stopped in Natchez to enjoy the view of the Mississippi River.
Annual festivals in Natchez celebrate the town's rich cultural heritage.

Etymology

Named after the Natchez people, a Native American tribe originally inhabiting the area.
