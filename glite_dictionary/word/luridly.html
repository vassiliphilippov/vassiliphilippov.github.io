luridly

1. ADV

/ ˈlʊr.ɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈljʊə.rɪd.li / (BrE)
in a vividly shocking or sensational manner, especially so as to produce a harsh or unnatural effect
in a shocking or sensational manner

Examples

The crime was luridly reported in the tabloids, emphasizing every gruesome detail.
His experiences were luridly recounted in the pages of his memoir.
The novel luridly depicted the underbelly of city life, shocking its readers.

Synset Examples

it was luridly described in the book as the place where mystics took refuge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vividly - in a manner producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images
  • Antonym: dully - in a manner lacking vividness or sensation

Etymology

From 'lurid' + '-ly'. 'Lurid' from Latin 'luridus' (pale yellow, ghastly).
