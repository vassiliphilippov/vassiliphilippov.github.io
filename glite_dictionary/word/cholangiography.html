cholangiography

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌlændʒiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəˌlændʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses X-rays and a contrast medium to examine the bile ducts
X-ray imaging of the bile ducts
medical

Examples

The surgeon ordered a cholangiography to assess potential blockages in the patient's bile ducts.
A cholangiography can help detect gallstones or tumors in the biliary system.
After the cholangiography, the medical team discussed treatment options based on the images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholecystography - X-ray imaging of the gallbladder using a contrast medium

Etymology

From Greek 'chole' (bile) + 'angeion' (vessel) + 'graphy' (writing, recording).
