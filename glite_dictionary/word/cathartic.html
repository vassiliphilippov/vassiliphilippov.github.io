cathartic

1. ADJ

/ kəˈθɑrtɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪk / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) having a strong laxative effect; causing evacuation of the bowels.
strong laxative effect
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a cathartic solution to clear the patient's bowels before the procedure.
Some herbal teas can have a mild or cathartic effect on the digestive system.
This medication is intended for short-term use due to its cathartic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - (of a medicine or food) tending to stimulate or facilitate evacuation of the bowels

Etymology

From Greek *kathartikos* (cleansing), from *kathairein* (to cleanse).

emotionally cleansing or releasing

4. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑrtɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪk / (BrE)
a medicine that causes the evacuation of the bowels; a purgative
a purging medicine (purgative)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a cathartic to help relieve the patient's constipation.
After ingesting the powerful cathartic, he experienced rapid bowel movements.
Herbal remedies can sometimes act as a natural cathartic in the digestive system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purgative - a medicine that causes evacuation of the bowels
  • Degree Lower: laxative - a mild medicine that helps evacuation of the bowels

Etymology

From Greek kathartikos (fit for cleansing), from kathairein (to cleanse).
