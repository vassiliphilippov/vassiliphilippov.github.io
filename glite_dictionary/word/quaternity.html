quaternity

1. NOUN

/ kwəˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
A group or set of four; the number four.
group or set of four

Examples

The ancient diagram represented the cosmos as a quaternity of elements.
In Jungian psychology, a quaternity often symbolizes wholeness or completeness.
The artwork was divided into a quaternity of equally important sections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartet - a group of four people or things
  • Degree Lower: trinity - a group of three closely related things
  • Degree Higher: quintet - a group of five people or things

Etymology

From Latin 'quaternitas', from 'quaterni' (four each).
