Mephitis mephitis

1. NOUN

/ məˈfaɪtɪs məˈfaɪtɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈfɪtɪs mɪˈfɪtɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the striped skunk, the most common and widespread skunk species in North America
scientific name of the striped skunk

Examples

The distinctive black-and-white fur of Mephitis mephitis serves as a warning to potential predators.
Researchers observed the behavior of Mephitis mephitis in its natural habitat in the Midwest.
Roadkill surveys often report Mephitis mephitis as the most frequently encountered skunk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped skunk - the common North American skunk distinguished by a white stripe

Etymology

From Latin 'mephitis' meaning 'noxious vapor', used in scientific taxonomy.
