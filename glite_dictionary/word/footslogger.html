footslogger

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌslɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌslɒɡə / (BrE)
an infantry soldier; someone who fights on foot with small arms, especially in the army.
infantry soldier; foot soldier

Examples

The footslogger marched for miles carrying a heavy pack and rifle.
During the long campaign, every footslogger faced harsh conditions on the ground.
He preferred the life of a footslogger to the glamour of the cavalry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot; infantry soldier
  • Antonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights on horseback, the opposite of a foot soldier

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'slogger' (drudge or hard worker).
