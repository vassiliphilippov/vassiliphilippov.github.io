rift

1. NOUN

/ rɪft / (AmE), / rɪft / (BrE)
a narrow opening or gap, especially in a mass of clouds
gap in a mass, esp. clouds

Examples

A ray of light burst through the rift in the thick cloud cover.
They admired the pale blue sky visible through a rift between the storm clouds.
As the clouds parted, a rift revealed the full moon above.

Synset Examples

the sun shone through a rift in the clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an opening or break in something
  • Synonym: fissure - a narrow crack or opening

Etymology

From Old Norse ript (a breach, breaking), related to rīfa (to tear).

2. NOUN

/ rɪft / (AmE), / rɪft / (BrE)
a narrow opening, crack, or fissure in rock or the earth's surface
narrow crack in rock or earth

Examples

A deep rift ran across the center of the canyon, marking a fracture in the earth.
Geologists studied the rift to understand the movement of tectonic plates in the region.
After the earthquake, a visible rift appeared in the rocky hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage
  • Synonym: cleft - a split or opening, especially in rock

Etymology

From Old Norse *rifa* (to tear, split).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09432904-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪft / (AmE), / rɪft / (BrE)
a serious break or separation in a relationship or between groups, often causing conflict or hostility
serious separation in a relationship

Examples

The political scandal created a rift within the party that lasted for years.
Despite their efforts, a deep rift developed between the two longtime friends.
Efforts to heal the rift between management and employees were ultimately successful.

Synset Examples

they hoped to avoid a break in relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breach - an act of breaking or failing to observe a law, agreement, or code of conduct
  • Antonym: conciliation - the action of stopping someone from being angry; the action of mediating between disputing sides

Etymology

From Old Norse 'rifa' (to split, break open).
