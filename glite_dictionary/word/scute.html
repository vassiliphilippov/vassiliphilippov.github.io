scute

1. NOUN

/ skjuːt / (AmE), / skjuːt / (BrE)
a large, hard, bony or horny external plate, such as those found on turtles, armadillos, or the underside of snakes
bony/horny plate on reptiles or armadillos

Examples

The turtle's scute showed signs of a recent injury.
Scientists measured the scutes to estimate the turtle's age.
The armadillo's back is covered with overlapping scutes for protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - a small thin bony or horny plate protecting the skin of fish and reptiles

Etymology

From Latin 'scutum' meaning 'shield'.
