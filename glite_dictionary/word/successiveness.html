successiveness

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛs.ɪv.nəs / (AmE), / səkˈsɛs.ɪv.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of things occurring one after another in time; sequential order.
the state of following in sequence

Examples

The successiveness of the events made the investigation much easier.
Critics admired the successiveness in how the artist arranged the paintings.
The successiveness of the tasks allowed for a smooth workflow in the office.

Synset Examples

the doctor saw a sequence of patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a continuous series or succession of things
  • Synonym: succession - the process of following in order or sequence

Etymology

From 'successive' + '-ness', with 'successive' from Latin 'successivus' (following in order).
