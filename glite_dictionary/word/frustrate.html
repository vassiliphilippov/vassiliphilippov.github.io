to frustrate

1. VERB

/ ˈfrʌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstreɪt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from achieving something, or to hinder their plans or efforts
prevent or hinder someone's efforts or plans

Examples

The sudden rainstorm frustrated our plans for a picnic in the park.
She was determined not to let his pessimism frustrate her ambitions.
Despite their efforts, the security team frustrated the attempted robbery.

Synset Examples

What ultimately frustrated every challenger was Ruth's amazing September surge
foil your opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thwart - to prevent someone from accomplishing something
  • Synonym: foil - to prevent something bad from succeeding

Etymology

From Latin 'frustrari' (to deceive, disappoint), from 'frustra' (in vain)
