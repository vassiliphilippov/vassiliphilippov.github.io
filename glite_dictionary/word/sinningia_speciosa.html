Sinningia speciosa

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪn.dʒə spiˈsoʊ.sə / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪn.dʒɪə spɪˈsəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A South American herbaceous plant cultivated as a houseplant for its large, attractive leaves and showy, variously colored bell-shaped flowers.
ornamental houseplant with showy flowers

Examples

The Sinningia speciosa produces vibrant blooms that brighten up any indoor space.
Gardeners often refer to Sinningia speciosa by its common name, gloxinia.
With proper care, Sinningia speciosa can flower several times a year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloxinia - common name for Sinningia speciosa

Etymology

Genus name from Wilhelm Sinning, German horticulturist; species Latin 'speciosa' meaning 'showy' or 'splendid'.
