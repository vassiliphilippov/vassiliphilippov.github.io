superoxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˈɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound or anion containing the univalent O2− ion, formed by the addition of a single electron to a molecule of oxygen
a compound containing the O2− anion
medical

Examples

The enzyme superoxide dismutase plays a vital role in removing superoxide radicals from the cell.
Potassium superoxide is used in breathing masks to generate oxygen.
Excess superoxide in the body can cause oxidative stress and damage to tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peroxide - a compound containing an O2^2− anion

Etymology

From 'super-' meaning 'above, beyond' and 'oxide', indicating a higher oxygen content than a normal oxide.

2. NOUN

/ suː.pərˈaɪ.saɪd / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pərˈɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
a chemical anion (O2−) formed by the addition of one electron to an oxygen molecule, especially important in biological and chemical processes
a chemical anion O2−
medical

Examples

The presence of superoxide radicals in the cell can cause oxidative stress and damage.
Researchers measured the levels of superoxide in mitochondrial samples to assess cellular health.
SOD enzymes help to break down harmful superoxide into oxygen and hydrogen peroxide.

Synset Examples

much of the O2 supporting cellular respiration is reduced to the superoxide anion O2-

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peroxide - a compound containing an oxygen-oxygen single bond (O2^2−)

Etymology

From super- (indicating excess) and oxide, referring to the higher oxidation state of oxygen.
