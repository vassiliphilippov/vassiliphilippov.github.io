gambol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæmb(ə)l / (BrE)
a playful or light-hearted activity done for amusement or recreation
light-hearted play or frolic

Examples

The children's gambol in the meadow lasted for hours.
A sudden rain ended their sunny afternoon gambol by the lake.
The puppy's joyful gambol delighted everyone who watched.

Synset Examples

it was all done in play
their frolic in the surf threatened to become ugly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - a lively and playful activity
  • Synonym: play - activity done for enjoyment and recreation rather than a serious purpose

Etymology

From French *gambader* (to skip, hop), from Italian *gamba* (leg).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbəl / (BrE)
to run or jump about playfully and energetically
run or jump about playfully

Examples

Every spring, lambs gambol in the fields near our house.
The children gamboled around the playground, laughing and chasing each other.
Puppies gamboling on the lawn always bring a smile to my face.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly or energetically

Etymology

From Old French *gambader* (to hop, skip), from *gamba* (leg).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01887709-v
Synset POS: v
