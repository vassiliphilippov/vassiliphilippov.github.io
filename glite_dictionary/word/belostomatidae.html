Belostomatidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌloʊstəˈmætəˌdiː / (AmE), / bɪˌlɒstəˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large aquatic insects often called giant water bugs or toe-biters, found in freshwater habitats and known for their painful bite
a family of giant water bugs

Examples

Belostomatidae are often found in slow-moving streams and ponds throughout North America.
Some species in the Belostomatidae family are known to deliver a surprisingly painful bite.
Researchers study Belostomatidae to understand more about aquatic food chains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant water bug - common name for insects of the family Belostomatidae

Etymology

From Greek 'belos' (dart) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the piercing mouthparts.

2. NOUN

/ bɛlˌoʊstəˈmætəˌdi / (AmE), / bɛlˌɒstəˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large aquatic insects known as giant water bugs, electric-light bugs, or toe-biters, found in freshwater habitats
a family of large aquatic water bugs

Examples

Belostomatidae are sometimes referred to as 'toe-biters' due to their painful bite when handled.
The Belostomatidae family includes the largest true bugs found in North America.
Biologists studied the hunting techniques of Belostomatidae in their natural habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin Belostoma (the type genus) + -idae (a family suffix in zoology).
