ephemeron

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛm.əˌrɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛm.ər.ɒn / (BrE)
a living thing or object that lasts only a very short time, especially an insect with a brief adult life
something short-lived; briefly living organism

Examples

The mayfly is a classic example of an ephemeron, living just one day as an adult.
Research into the ephemeron sheds light on the fleeting nature of some insect species.
He used the term ephemeron to describe trends that vanish almost as quickly as they appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemera - things that exist or are used for only a short time

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeron (lasting only a day), from epi- (upon) + hēmera (day).
