penicillin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic or group of antibiotics produced by certain molds, used to treat bacterial infections
antibiotic from Penicillium molds
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed penicillin to treat her throat infection.
Many bacteria have developed resistance to penicillin over the years.
He had a severe allergic reaction to penicillin as a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance that kills or inhibits bacteria

Etymology

From the modern Latin genus name Penicillium, based on Latin 'penicillus' (paintbrush), due to the shape of the mold.
