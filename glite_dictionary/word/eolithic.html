Eolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌiː.əʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the earliest part of the Stone Age, characterized by the first apparent signs of human culture and primitive stone tools.
earliest Stone Age period

Examples

Archaeologists debated the significance of the Eolithic period and its primitive tools.
Few sites from the Eolithic era have been conclusively identified in Europe.
The Eolithic hypothesis remains controversial among some prehistoric scholars.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Paleolithic - Relating to the early phase of the Stone Age marked by stone tools

Etymology

From Greek 'ēōs' (dawn) + 'lithos' (stone).
