eolithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌiː.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌiː.əˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the earliest period of the Stone Age, characterized by the use of the earliest chipped stone tools.
earliest Stone Age period

Examples

The discovery of eolithic tools has sparked debate among archaeologists about early human development.
Evidence of eolithic cultures is often difficult to distinguish from naturally chipped stones.
Many eolithic artifacts have been found in the gravel beds of southern England.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Paleolithic - relating to the early stone age, following the Eolithic period
  • Degree Higher: Neolithic - relating to the later part of the Stone Age, when ground or polished stone weapons and implements prevailed

Etymology

From Greek 'eōs' (dawn) + 'lithos' (stone), modeled after 'Paleolithic' and 'Neolithic'.
