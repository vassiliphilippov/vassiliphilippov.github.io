stretchability

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛtʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌstrɛtʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be stretched without breaking or losing functionality
capacity to be stretched

Examples

The stretchability of the fabric makes it perfect for activewear.
Scientists tested the stretchability of the new polymer under extreme conditions.
Good yoga mats are valued for their comfort and stretchability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elasticity - the ability of an object or material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed

Etymology

From 'stretch' + '-ability', where '-ability' forms nouns expressing ability or capacity.
