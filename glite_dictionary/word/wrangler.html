wrangler

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person, especially a cowboy, whose job is to take care of saddle horses on a ranch or during a cattle drive
person who takes care of saddle horses

Examples

The wrangler rounded up the horses at dawn to prepare for the day's ride.
As a wrangler, she was responsible for feeding and grooming all the saddle horses on the ranch.
The young cowboy dreamed of becoming a skilled wrangler one day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man who herds and tends cattle, usually on horseback, especially in the western US

Etymology

From American English; related to 'wrangle' (to argue noisily), derived from Low German 'wrangeln' (to twist, struggle), later specialized in the U.S. for cowboy usage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who argues noisily or angrily, especially about petty issues
someone who argues loudly

Examples

Jake's reputation as a wrangler made people avoid debates with him at meetings.
The two wranglers kept interrupting each other, making it impossible to reach a decision.
She disliked being called a wrangler, even though she enjoyed lively discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arguer - a person who argues
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to stop arguments

Etymology

From wrangle (to argue) + -er (agent suffix), from Middle English wranglen.
