prothalamion

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊθəˈleɪmiən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊθəˈleɪmɪən / (BrE)
A song or poem written in celebration of a marriage.
marriage celebration poem or song

Examples

Spenser's prothalamion was composed to honor the double wedding of two noble ladies.
The poet presented a prothalamion on the occasion of the royal marriage.
She recited a prothalamion at her sister's wedding ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithalamion - a song or poem celebrating a wedding or newlyweds

Etymology

From Greek pro- (before) + thalamos (bridal chamber); popularized by the poet Edmund Spenser in 1596.
