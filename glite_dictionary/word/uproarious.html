uproarious

1. ADJ

/ ʌpˈrɔriəs / (AmE), / ʌpˈrɔːriəs / (BrE)
full of very loud laughter or uncontrollable amusement; extremely funny or causing boisterous merriment
very loud or extremely funny

Examples

The comedian's uproarious jokes had the entire audience in tears of laughter.
The play was an uproarious success, with people talking about it for weeks afterwards.
Their uproarious laughter echoed through the halls, drawing curious glances from others.

Synset Examples

hilarious broad comedy
a screaming farce
uproarious stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilarious - extremely funny, causing loud laughter
  • Antonym: serious - solemn, not characterized by laughter or merriment

Etymology

From 'uproar' (noise, commotion) + '-ious' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ʌpˈrɔriəs / (AmE), / ʌpˈrɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
Characterized by loud, uncontrollable, and often joyful noise or laughter.
very noisy or full of loud laughter

Examples

The comedian's jokes were so uproarious that the entire audience couldn't stop laughing.
A party became uproarious as the night went on and the music grew louder.
The uproarious cheers of the students could be heard from blocks away after their team's victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisy - making or characterized by a lot of noise
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From English 'uproar' (tumult, noise) + '-ious' (adjectival suffix), from Dutch 'oproeir' via German 'Aufruhr'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01927865-s
Synset POS: s
