reorder

1. NOUN

/ riˈɔrdər / (AmE), / riːˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an order placed again for the same goods or merchandise as previously ordered
a repeated commercial order

Examples

The manager submitted a reorder to replenish the store's inventory.
Please check if a reorder is necessary for the bestselling items.
Our supplier offers discounts when a reorder is placed within 30 days of the original purchase.

Synset Examples

he's the one who sends out all the new orders and reorders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - a request to supply goods or services

Etymology

From re- (again) + order, modeled after the verb 'to reorder'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06542246-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ riˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / riˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to place an order again for goods or services, especially when the original supply has run out
place an order for more goods or services

Examples

The store had to reorder the popular CD several times to keep up with demand.
If you finish your prescription, you can easily reorder your medication online.
After selling out of the product, we decided to reorder it from our supplier.

Synset Examples

The store had to reorder the popular CD several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - to request goods or services be supplied

Etymology

From re- (again) + order, meaning to order again.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00747796-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈɔrdər / (AmE), / riːˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to arrange something again in a different order; to assign a new sequence to.
arrange again in a new order

Examples

You can reorder the list by dragging and dropping the items into a new position.
After the error was discovered, they had to reorder the files according to date.
The chef likes to reorder the spices on his rack every month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to put things in a particular order or position
  • Antonym: disorder - to disturb the order or arrangement of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + order, based on Latin roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00660783-v
Synset POS: v
