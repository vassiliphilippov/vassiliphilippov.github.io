sacrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsak.rə.mənt / (BrE)
a formal religious rite regarded as an outward and visible sign of inward and spiritual divine grace, especially as recognized in Christian churches such as baptism and the Eucharist
formal religious rite in Christianity

Examples

In Catholicism, the act of confession is considered a sacrament that offers forgiveness to believers.
Christians gather to celebrate the sacrament of baptism as a sign of spiritual rebirth.
Marriage is recognized as a sacrament in many Christian denominations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinance - a religious ritual, particularly in Protestant traditions
  • Synonym: rite - a religious or other ceremonial practice or act

Etymology

From Latin 'sacramentum' (a solemn oath or mystery), from 'sacrare' (to consecrate).
