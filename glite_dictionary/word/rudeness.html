rudeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrud.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruːd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being rude; lack of manners or respect for others
lack of manners; insulting behavior

Examples

His rudeness toward the waiter surprised everyone at the table.
People were shocked by the rudeness she displayed during the meeting.
Constant rudeness can damage your professional relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: politeness - quality of showing good manners and respect for others
  • Synonym: discourtesy - lack of courtesy; rude or impolite behavior

Etymology

From Middle English 'rudenes', from Old French 'rudesse', from Latin 'rudis' (rough, unrefined).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːdnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːdnɪs / (BrE)
A quality or condition of being in an uncivilized, rough, or unrefined state; lack of sophistication or cultivation.
wild or unrefined state

Examples

The rudeness of the untouched wilderness was both intimidating and beautiful.
Early settlements struggled against the rudeness of the landscape and climate.
In its original rudeness, the material showed no signs of craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbarism - a state of being uncivilized or primitive
  • Antonym: refinement - the process of removing impurities or becoming civilized

Etymology

From Middle English 'rudenesse', from Old French 'rudesse', from Latin 'rudis' (unwrought, uncultivated).
