thaumatolatry

1. NOUN

/ θɔːməˈtɑːlətri / (AmE), / θɔːməˈtɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship or excessive reverence of miracles
worship of miracles

Examples

The historian noted an increase in thaumatolatry during periods of social upheaval.
Critics argued that the movement bordered on thaumatolatry rather than sincere faith.
His fascination with relics occasionally slipped into outright thaumatolatry.

Etymology

From Greek 'thauma' (wonder, miracle) + 'latreia' (worship, service).
