story

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːri / (AmE), / ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
A lie, especially one that is trivial or harmless.
a trivial or harmless lie

Examples

He told his mother a story about why he was late for dinner.
It's important to teach children to be truthful and not make up stories.
She realized her son was just telling a story when he said the dog ate his homework.

Synset Examples

he told a fib about eating his spinach
how can I stop my child from telling stories?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fib - a trivial or small lie
  • Synonym: lie - an intentionally false statement
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔri / (AmE), / ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
A piece of fiction that narrates a chain of related events, typically written or told to entertain or inform.
fictional account or narrative

Examples

She enjoyed reading a short story before bed every night.
The author published his first story in a well-known magazine.
Children love to listen to a story about magical adventures.

Synset Examples

he writes stories for the magazines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tale - a narrative, especially one that is imaginary or fictitious
  • Degree Higher: novel - a long fictional narrative

Etymology

From Latin 'historia' through Old French 'estorie', originally meaning 'history'; now refers mainly to fictional narratives.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːri / (AmE), / ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
A floor or level of a building; a set of rooms or space at one height within a structure.
a level or floor of a building

Examples

Their apartment is on the fifth story of the building.
The library has three stories filled with bookshelves and reading areas.
A spiral staircase connects each story of the old mansion.

Synset Examples

what level is the office on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floor - the part of a room on which you stand; a level of a building
  • Synonym: level - a relative position or stage on a scale, often used for building floors

Etymology

From Middle English *storie*, earlier referring to a tier of painted scenes, later associated with building levels.

/ ˈstɔɹi / (AmE), / ˈstɔːɹi / (BrE)
an account or description of past events, either factual or fictional, presented in a structured narrative
account or narrative of past events

Examples

His story about the trip was full of fascinating details.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: history - a chronological record of significant events
