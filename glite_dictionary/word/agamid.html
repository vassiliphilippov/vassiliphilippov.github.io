agamid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəmɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɡəmɪd / (BrE)
a lizard belonging to the family Agamidae, typically found in Africa, Asia, and Australia
a lizard of the family Agamidae

Examples

The agamid is easily distinguished from other lizards by the shape of its head and body.
Australian deserts are home to several remarkable species of agamid.
Researchers observed a brightly colored agamid basking on the rocky outcrop.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Agamidae, the family name formed from the genus Agama.
