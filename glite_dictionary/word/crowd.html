crowd

1. VERB

/ kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)
to cause a group of people or animals to move together into a small space, often by urging or forcing
cause people or animals to move together

Examples

Security tried to crowd the protesters into one corner while the event continued.
The teachers had to crowd all the students into the gym during the sudden storm.
The organizers attempted to crowd the goats into a pen for the evening.

Synset Examples

We herded the children into a spare classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - to make people or animals move together as a group

Etymology

From Old English *crūdan* (to press, drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02032568-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to gather together in large numbers, often filling a space.
gather together in large numbers

Examples

Hundreds of fans crowded around the stage to catch a glimpse of the singer.
Shoppers crowded into stores on Black Friday looking for the best deals.
Children crowded through the gate as soon as the playground opened.

Synset Examples

men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to come together as a group, especially for a common purpose
  • Antonym: to disperse - to break up and leave a crowd or gathering

Etymology

From Old English crūdan (to press, hasten), related to Middle Dutch crūden (to press, drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02031458-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)
A large number of people or things gathered closely together in one place.
large group of people or things

Examples

A large crowd gathered in the square to hear the mayor speak.
The flowers attracted a crowd of buzzing bees.
She pushed her way through the crowd to reach the front of the stage.

Synset Examples

a crowd of insects assembled around the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals
  • Synonym: multitude - a great number of people or things
  • Synonym: mob - a large, disorderly crowd of people
  • Antonym: handful - a small number of people or things

Etymology

From Old English crūdan (to press, crowd), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)

fill a space to overflowing

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empty - to remove all contents from; make unoccupied
