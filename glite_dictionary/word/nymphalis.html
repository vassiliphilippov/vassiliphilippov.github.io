Nymphalis

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfælɪs / (AmE), / nɪmˈfalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies in the family Nymphalidae, known for species such as the mourning cloak.
genus of butterflies (Nymphalidae)

Examples

The mourning cloak, or Nymphalis antiopa, is a butterfly often seen in temperate regions.
Researchers identified a new species within the genus Nymphalis during their expedition.
Many species in Nymphalis are noted for their striking wing patterns and coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with large, colorful wings and a slender body

Etymology

From Latin 'nymphalis', relating to a nymph; from Greek 'nymphē' (bride, nymph).

2. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfælɪs / (AmE), / nɪmˈfælɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the butterfly family Nymphalidae, which includes the mourning cloak butterfly and related species.
genus of butterflies (Nymphalidae family)

Examples

The Nymphalis genus includes the well-known mourning cloak butterfly.
Researchers observed several Nymphalis species during their expedition in the northern forest.
The caterpillar of the Nymphalis genus feeds on a variety of host plants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Nymphalidae - family of butterflies commonly called brush-footed butterflies

Etymology

From Latin Nymphalis, from 'nympha' (nymph), after the mythical beings in Greek and Roman mythology.
