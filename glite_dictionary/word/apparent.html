apparent

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɛrənt / (AmE), / əˈparənt / (BrE)
clearly visible or understood; obvious or evident to the mind or senses.
clearly visible or obvious

Examples

The damage from the storm was immediately apparent to the town's residents.
It soon became apparent that the plan would not work as intended.
The difference between the two proposals is apparent to anyone who reads them carefully.

Synset Examples

the effects of the drought are apparent to anyone who sees the parched fields
evident hostility
manifest disapproval
patent advantages
made his meaning plain
it is plain that he is no reactionary
in plain view
a palpable lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily seen or understood; clear to the mind or senses
  • Synonym: evident - clearly seen or understood; obvious
  • Antonym: hidden - not visible or easily noticed
  • Synonym: plain - easy to see or understand; clear

Etymology

From Latin 'apparens', present participle of 'apparere' (to appear).

2. ADJ

/ əˈpɛrənt / (AmE), / əˈparənt / (BrE)
seeming to be true, but possibly not actually so; appearing as such but not necessarily so.
seeming; not necessarily real

Examples

Despite his apparent confidence, he was actually quite nervous before the presentation.
There were several apparent errors in the financial report that needed further investigation.
Her apparent enthusiasm masked her true lack of interest in the project.

Synset Examples

for all his apparent wealth, he had no money to pay the rent
the committee investigated some apparent discrepancies
the ostensible truth of their theories
his seeming honesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostensible - stated or appearing to be true, but not necessarily so
  • Synonym: seeming - appearing to be something, especially when not true
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact

Etymology

From Old French apparent, from Latin apparens (present participle of apparēre, to appear).
