unanimous

1. ADJ

/ juˈnænɪməs / (AmE), / juːˈnænɪməs / (BrE)
(of two or more people) fully in agreement; sharing the same opinion or decision.
fully in agreement

Examples

The board reached a unanimous decision to approve the new policy.
Her proposal was met with unanimous support from her colleagues.
The jury returned a unanimous verdict of not guilty.

Synset Examples

a unanimous decision

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divided - not in agreement; showing difference of opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'unanimus', from 'unus' (one) and 'animus' (mind, spirit).

2. ADJ

/ juˈnænəməs / (AmE), / juˈnænɪməs / (BrE)
(of a group) fully in agreement or acting together as a single entity, with no dissent.
fully in agreement; acting as one

Examples

The board was unanimous in its decision to expand the company overseas.
After a lengthy discussion, the committee reached a unanimous agreement.
The jury returned a unanimous verdict after only three hours of deliberation.

Synset Examples

a solid voting bloc

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divided - (of a group) not in agreement; in conflict or disagreement
  • Synonym: unified - united or made into a whole

Etymology

From Latin unanimus (of one mind), from unus (one) + animus (mind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00787771-s
Synset POS: s
