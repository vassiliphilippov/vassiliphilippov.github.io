hardsub

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌsʌb / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌsʌb / (BrE)
a subtitle that is permanently merged into the video image and cannot be turned off or separated from the video file
subtitle merged permanently in video

Examples

The anime video I downloaded had hardsubs, so I couldn't turn off the subtitles.
Some viewers prefer hardsubs because they ensure everyone sees the correct timing.
When creating hardsubs, the subtitles are baked directly into the video frames.

Synset Examples

Also, since you're not really encoding the video itself, the quality of the subbed version is a lot better for softsubs than hardsubs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softsub - a subtitle stored as a separate file or stream, which can be switched on or off

Etymology

Blend of 'hard' (permanent, unchangeable) and 'subtitle'; modeled after 'hardwired'.
