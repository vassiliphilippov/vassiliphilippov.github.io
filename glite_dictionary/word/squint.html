squint

1. VERB

/ skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
to cross one's eyes so that they point in different directions, especially deliberately or in imitation of strabismus
to cross the eyes

Examples

The children made faces and squinted at each other for fun.
He squinted on purpose to amuse his little sister.
If you squint like that for too long, your eyes might hurt.

Synset Examples

The children squinted so as to scare each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross one's eyes - to turn one's eyes inward so they point toward the nose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00008595-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
(of a person) to be affected by strabismus (cross-eyed); to have eyes that do not look in the same direction.
to be cross-eyed; have strabismus

Examples

Her daughter used to squint before she had corrective surgery.
People who squint may have trouble focusing on distant objects.
The doctor noticed the child began to squint during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strabismus - a condition in which the eyes do not properly align with each other
  • Synonym: cross-eyed - having eyes that turn inwards or outwards rather than looking straight ahead

Etymology

From Middle English 'squinten' (to look askance), probably imitative or influenced by 'squint' (adj.).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02172514-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
A condition in which the eyes are not properly aligned with each other, resulting in a deviation from parallel gaze.
abnormal alignment of the eyes
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with a squint that required corrective surgery.
Her left eye had a noticeable squint from birth.
Regular eye exams can help detect a squint at an early stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strabismus - medical term for misalignment of the eyes
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
to partly close one's eyes, especially when looking at something bright or trying to see more clearly
partly close the eyes

Examples

She had to squint to read the tiny print on the label.
He squinted against the glare of the afternoon sun.
If you squint, you might be able to make out the distant mountains.

Synset Examples

The driver squinted as the sun hit his windshield

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - to look searchingly or with difficulty

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *squinten*, related to Old French *esquinter* (to squint, look askance).

an act of looking at something with your eyes partly closed, usually in order to see more clearly or because of bright light

Examples
