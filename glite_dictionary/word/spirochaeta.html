Spirochaeta

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˌroʊˈkiːtə / (AmE), / spaɪˌrəʊˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a genus of flexible, spiral-shaped, chiefly aquatic bacteria in the family Spirochaetaceae
genus of spiral aquatic bacteria

Examples

Spirochaeta species are characterized by their unique spiral shape and undulating motion.
Researchers collected water samples to examine the presence of Spirochaeta in local ponds.
The flexible cell wall of Spirochaeta allows it to move efficiently through aquatic environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'chaitē' (hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌspaɪroʊˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˌspaɪərəʊˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a genus of flexible, undulating bacteria chiefly found in aquatic habitats; the type genus of the family Spirochaetaceae
genus of flexible aquatic bacteria
medical

Examples

The bacterium Spirochaeta thrives in aquatic environments, especially in mud and stagnant water.
Under the microscope, Spirochaeta is recognizable by its unique helical shape and wavy movement.
Researchers studied how Spirochaeta species adapt to changing water salinity levels.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Spirochaeta, from Greek speira (coil) + chaitē (hair).
