priestly

1. ADJ

/ ˈpristli / (AmE), / ˈpriːstli / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or befitting a priest or the priesthood.
Relating to priests or the priesthood

Examples

His priestly robe set him apart from the rest of the congregation.
She demonstrated a priestly calm during the ceremony.
The community admired his priestly sense of duty and compassion.

Synset Examples

priestly dedication to the people of his parish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerical - relating to clergy or priests
  • Antonym: lay - not belonging to the clergy; non-ordained

Etymology

From priest + -ly, Old English via ecclesiastical Latin and Greek roots.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpristli / (AmE), / ˈpriːstli / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a priest or the priesthood.
relating to priests or priesthood

Examples

The bishop wore his most priestly robes for the ceremony.
She carried herself with a quiet, almost priestly dignity.
The rituals had a distinctly priestly feel, echoing ancient traditions.

Synset Examples

priestly (or sacerdotal) vestments
hieratic gestures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacerdotal - relating to priests or the priesthood
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From priest + -ly (adjectival suffix).
