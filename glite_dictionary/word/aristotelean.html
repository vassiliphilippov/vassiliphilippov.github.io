Aristotelean

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstəˈtɪliən / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtiːlɪən / (BrE)
A follower of Aristotle or someone who supports the philosophy of Aristotelianism.
follower of Aristotle

Examples

As an Aristotelean, she placed great value on reason and empirical observation.
Medieval scholars who identified as Aristoteleans often debated with followers of Plato.
The professor's Aristotelean perspective influenced his interpretation of ancient philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aristotelian - A follower or supporter of Aristotle or Aristotelian philosophy

Etymology

From Aristotle (the Greek philosopher) + -ean (forming adjectives and nouns).
