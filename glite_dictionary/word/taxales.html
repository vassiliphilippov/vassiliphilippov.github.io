Taxales

1. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / tækˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of coniferous plants consisting of the yews (family Taxaceae).
botanical order of yews (Taxaceae)

Examples

Botanists classify yews in the order Taxales, which includes only one family, the Taxaceae.
The Taxales are known for their simple needle-like leaves and red, berry-like arils.
Some taxonomic systems have merged Taxales into other conifer orders, but it is still used in certain classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxaceae - the family of yew trees within Taxales

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Taxales', from 'Taxus' (the type genus, yew) + '-ales' (a standard suffix for plant orders).
