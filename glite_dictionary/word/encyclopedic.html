encyclopedic

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪklɪˈpiːdɪk / (BrE)
comprehensive in range or content; covering a wide variety of information, as an encyclopedia does
comprehensive; wide in scope

Examples

Her encyclopedic knowledge of world history impressed everyone at the conference.
He possesses an encyclopedic memory when it comes to movie trivia.
The textbook provides an encyclopedic overview of biological sciences.

Synset Examples

encyclopedic knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including or dealing with all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • Antonym: narrow - limited in scope or extent; not comprehensive

Etymology

From French 'encyclopédique', from 'encyclopédie' (encyclopedia) based on Greek 'enkuklios paideia' (general education).
