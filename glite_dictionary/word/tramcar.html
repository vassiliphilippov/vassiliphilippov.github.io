tramcar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræm.kɑr / (AmE), / ˈtræm.kɑː / (BrE)
A four-wheeled wagon that runs on tracks in a mine, used especially for transporting materials such as coal.
four-wheeled mining wagon on tracks

Examples

The coal was loaded into a tramcar and transported to the surface.
Workers pushed the heavy tramcar along the narrow track deep inside the tunnel.
Each tramcar could carry several tons of ore at a time.

Synset Examples

a tramcar carries coal out of a coal mine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minecart - a cart used for transporting ore or coal in mines

Etymology

From 'tram' (track or rail) + 'car'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræmˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈtræmkɑː / (BrE)
a rail vehicle powered by electricity, used for passenger transport in urban areas, typically running on tracks along city streets
electric rail vehicle for city transport

Examples

The city's new tramcar system makes commuting much easier for residents.
We watched as the bright yellow tramcar glided smoothly along the tracks downtown.
During our visit to Lisbon, we took a historic tramcar through the winding streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streetcar - an electric vehicle running on city tracks
  • Synonym: trolley - a wheeled vehicle that runs on tracks, especially in US cities

Etymology

From 'tram' (from Scottish 'tram', a shaft or beam) + 'car'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
