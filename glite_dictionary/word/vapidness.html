vapidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæpɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvapɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dull, uninteresting, or lacking in liveliness or sophistication
dullness; lack of liveliness

Examples

The vapidness of the conversation made the meeting feel much longer than it actually was.
Critics have pointed out the vapidness of the film's plot and characters.
She was frustrated by the vapidness of the social scene in the small town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vapid - lacking liveliness or interest
  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of being boring or uninteresting

Etymology

From 'vapid' + English noun suffix '-ness'. 'Vapid' from Latin 'vapidus' (flat, insipid).
