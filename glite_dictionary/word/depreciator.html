depreciator

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpriʃiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃieɪtə / (BrE)
A person who disparages or belittles the value or worth of something.
one who belittles worth of something

Examples

As a constant depreciator of new technology, he rarely embraced innovation.
Her comments in the meeting branded her as a depreciator of her colleagues' achievements.
Despite the depreciator's remarks, the artist remained confident in his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - a person who expresses an unfavorable opinion of something
  • Antonym: admirer - a person who admires or praises something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'depretiare' (to diminish in value) via 'depreciate' + '-or'.
