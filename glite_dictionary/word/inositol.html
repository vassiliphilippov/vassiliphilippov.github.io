inositol

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnoʊsɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒsɪtɒl / (BrE)
a type of sugar alcohol found in many foods and used by the body, often considered part of the vitamin B complex
a sugar alcohol in the vitamin B complex
medical

Examples

Dietary supplements often contain inositol for its potential benefits to mental health.
Researchers are studying the effects of inositol on metabolic disorders.
Some cereals are good natural sources of inositol.

Etymology

From Greek 'inōs' (muscle) and 'itol' (used to form names of alcohols).
