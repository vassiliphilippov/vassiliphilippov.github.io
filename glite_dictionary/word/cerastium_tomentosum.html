Cerastium tomentosum

1. NOUN

/ səˈræstiəm toʊmɛnˈtoʊsəm / (AmE), / sɪˈræstiəm tɒˌmɛntəˈsʊm / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial plant with hairy silver-grey leaves and large white flowers, commonly known as snow-in-summer
a plant with silvery leaves and white flowers

Examples

The garden border was lined with Cerastium tomentosum, creating a striking edge of silver foliage.
Many gardeners plant Cerastium tomentosum to cover slopes and suppress weeds.
After a few seasons, Cerastium tomentosum can spread to form a beautiful white carpet in late spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cerastium' meaning 'horned' (referring to the shape of some parts), and 'tomentosum' meaning 'covered with dense, matted hairs'.
