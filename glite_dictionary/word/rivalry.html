rivalry

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.vəl.ri / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.vəl.ri / (BrE)
competition between people, groups, or organizations who are trying to be more successful than each other, often for the same goal or prize.
competition for the same goal

Examples

The intense rivalry between the two soccer teams drew large crowds to every match.
Sibling rivalry can sometimes create tension in families but also motivate children to succeed.
Their business rivalry led them to innovate and offer better services to customers.

Synset Examples

the teams were in fierce contention for first place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the activity or condition of striving to gain or win something by defeating others
  • Antonym: cooperation - working together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'rivalis' (a person competing for the same stream), from 'rivus' (stream).
