theophany

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːfəˌni / (AmE), / θiˈɒfəni / (BrE)
A visible or otherwise perceptible manifestation of a deity to a human being.
manifestation of a deity to a human

Examples

Many scholars interpret the burning bush encounter as a theophany in the Hebrew Bible.
In classical mythology, a theophany often occurs when a god appears to mortals in disguise.
The festival celebrated the annual theophany said to have taken place on the sacred mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphany - the manifestation or appearance of a divine or supernatural being

Etymology

From Greek theophaneia, from theos (god) + phainein (to show).
