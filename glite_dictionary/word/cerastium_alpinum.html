Cerastium alpinum

1. NOUN

/ səˈræstiəm ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / sɛˈræstiəm ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A small perennial flowering plant of the pink family, found widely in the Arctic regions and on mountains in Europe; also known as alpine mouse-ear.
species of Arctic and alpine flowering plant

Examples

The rocky slopes were dotted with clusters of Cerastium alpinum in full bloom.
Researchers studied how Cerastium alpinum adapts to extreme Arctic climates.
Gardeners sometimes plant Cerastium alpinum in rock gardens for its delicate, white flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *kerastion* (little horn) + Latin *alpinum* (of the Alps).
