cisterna

1. NOUN

/ sɪsˈtɝːnə / (AmE), / sɪsˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
An anatomical sac or cavity filled with fluid, especially lymph or cerebrospinal fluid.
fluid-filled anatomical sac or cavity
medical

Examples

The subarachnoid cisterna acts as a reservoir for cerebrospinal fluid in the brain.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided the cisterna to prevent fluid leakage.
Researchers studied the enlargement of the cisterna in patients with hydrocephalus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hollow space within the body
  • Synonym: sac - a pouch-like anatomical structure

Etymology

From Latin 'cisterna' meaning 'reservoir' or 'tank'.
