monogamousness

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑɡəməsnəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒɡəməsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having only one spouse or partner at a time
state of having one spouse or partner

Examples

The monogamousness of certain bird species has been documented extensively by researchers.
Some people believe that monogamousness is essential for a lasting relationship.
The debate centered on the social value of monogamousness versus other relationship structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monogamy - the practice or state of being married to or having only one partner at a time
  • Antonym: polygamy - the practice of having more than one wife or husband at the same time

Etymology

From 'monogamous' (from Greek 'monogamos' meaning 'married to one person') + '-ness'.
