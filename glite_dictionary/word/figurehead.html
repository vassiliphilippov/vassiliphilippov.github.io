figurehead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡjərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a carved figure or model, often of a person, mounted on the bow of a sailing ship as a decoration or symbol
decorative figure on a ship’s bow

Examples

The old frigate still displayed its original figurehead, a majestic woman with flowing hair.
During restoration, the damaged figurehead was carefully removed and sent to a specialist.
Many sailors believed the figurehead protected their ship from harm during long voyages.

Etymology

From 'figure' + 'head'; originally referring to an ornamental sculpture at the head (bow) of a ship.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡjɚˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a person who holds a nominal position of leadership but has no real power, often used as a front for questionable or illegal activities
leader in name only, front person

Examples

The president was merely a figurehead while real decisions were made behind the scenes.
They appointed her as CEO, but everyone knew she was just a figurehead for the board.
The accused mob boss used a figurehead to hide his involvement in the business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet - a person controlled by others, especially in politics or business

Etymology

Originally referred to the carved figure on the prow of a ship, later used figuratively for symbolic leaders.
