Kashag

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈʃɑːg / (AmE), / kɑːˈʃæɡ / (BrE)
the council that serves as the main advisory and executive board of the Tibetan government-in-exile
Tibetan government-in-exile's council

Examples

The Kashag convened to discuss new policies for the Tibetan community in exile.
As a member of the Kashag, he played a vital role in diplomatic relations with foreign governments.
The decisions of the Kashag are closely followed by Tibetans around the world.

Etymology

From Tibetan ཀའི་ཤག (ka'i shag), meaning 'council room'.
