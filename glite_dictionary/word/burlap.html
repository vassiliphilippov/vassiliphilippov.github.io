burlap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.læp / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.læp / (BrE)
a coarse, heavy fabric made from jute or hemp, used especially for making sacks and upholstery lining
coarse jute or hemp fabric

Examples

They wrapped the tree roots in burlap before planting them in the ground.
A rough burlap sack was used to carry the potatoes.
The craftsman chose burlap for the backing of the rug to give it a rustic look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hessian - coarse woven fabric made from jute, especially known by this name in Britain

Etymology

Origin unknown, possibly from Dutch 'boerenlap' meaning 'farmer's cloth'.
