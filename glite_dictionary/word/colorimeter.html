colorimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.əˈrɪm.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.əˈrɪm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring and analyzing the concentration of substances by determining the intensity of color produced with specific reagents
instrument measuring concentration by color intensity

Examples

The laboratory technician used a colorimeter to determine the concentration of iron in the water sample.
Calibration of the colorimeter is essential to ensure accurate analytical results.
The new chemistry students learned how to operate a colorimeter during today's lab session.

Etymology

From Latin 'color' (color) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
