Coleonyx

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈliː.ə.nɪks / (AmE), / kəʊˈliː.ə.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal geckos known as banded geckos, native to North and Central America.
a genus of banded geckos

Examples

The Coleonyx genus includes several species of banded geckos found in desert regions.
Researchers observed the Coleonyx hunting at night, feeding on insects near rocks.
Some people keep Coleonyx as pets due to their manageable size and gentle nature.

Etymology

From Greek 'koleos' (sheath) and 'onyx' (claw), referring to aspects of the geckos' anatomy.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈliː.əˌnɪks / (AmE), / kəʊˈliː.ənɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal, banded geckos native to North and Central America.
a genus of banded geckos

Examples

The Coleonyx genus includes several species of banded geckos found throughout the southwestern United States.
Researchers observed a Coleonyx hiding under rocks during the day and emerging at night to hunt insects.
Many reptile enthusiasts keep Coleonyx as pets because of their unique coloration and manageable size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gecko - small, nocturnal lizard of the family Gekkonidae

Etymology

From Greek 'koleos' (sheath) and 'onyx' (claw), referring to the reptile's claws.
