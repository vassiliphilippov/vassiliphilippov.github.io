Medicago intertexta

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkaɡoʊ ˌɪntərˈtɛkstə / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkaːɡəʊ ˌɪntəˈtɛkstə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its spiny seed pods and leaves marked with dark spots.
Mediterranean annual plant with spiny pods

Examples

Medicago intertexta thrives in sunny, well-drained soils of the Mediterranean region.
The leaves of Medicago intertexta are distinctive, featuring dark spots and a clover-like appearance.
Farmers sometimes use Medicago intertexta as a cover crop to enrich the soil with nitrogen.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'medica' (alfalfa); 'intertexta' from Latin, meaning 'interwoven'.
