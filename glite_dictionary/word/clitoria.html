Clitoria

1. NOUN

/ klɪˈtɔriə / (AmE), / klɪˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs or vines with pinnate leaves and large, often blue or purple, axillary flowers, including the butterfly pea.
genus of tropical plants with showy flowers

Examples

The Clitoria genus is best known for the butterfly pea, widely cultivated for its vibrant blue flowers.
Several species of Clitoria are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.
The striking blossoms of Clitoria attract many pollinators in tropical climates.

Etymology

From Latin; so named because the flower's shape was thought to resemble the human clitoris.

2. NOUN

/ klaɪˈtɔriə / (AmE), / klɪˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs or vines with pinnate leaves and large axillary flowers, commonly known as butterfly pea.
genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The vivid blue flowers of Clitoria are often used in herbal teas.
Several species within the genus Clitoria are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Clitoria ternatea.

Etymology

Named from Latin 'clitoris', due to the resemblance of the flower to the human anatomy.
