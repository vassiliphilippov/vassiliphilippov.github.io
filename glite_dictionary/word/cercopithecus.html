Cercopithecus

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrkoʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / sɜːkəʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys commonly known as guenons, found chiefly in Africa.
genus of African guenon monkeys

Examples

Researchers observed the behavior of several Cercopithecus species in their natural rainforest habitat.
The Cercopithecus genus includes a wide variety of brightly colored guenon monkeys.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered members of the Cercopithecus genus.

Etymology

From Greek *kerkos* (tail) + *pithēkos* (ape).

2. NOUN

/ sɜːrˌkoʊpɪˈθiːkəs / (AmE), / sɜːˌkɒpɪˈθiːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World monkeys known as guenons, native to Africa
a genus of African guenon monkeys

Examples

The Cercopithecus genus includes many colorful guenon monkeys found in African forests.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Cercopithecus genus in Central Africa.
Most Cercopithecus monkeys are arboreal and highly social.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guenon - a type of Old World monkey of the genus Cercopithecus

Etymology

From Greek 'kerkopithēkos' meaning 'long-tailed monkey'.
