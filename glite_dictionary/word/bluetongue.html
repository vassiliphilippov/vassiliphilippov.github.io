bluetongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a viral disease of sheep and cattle, transmitted by biting midges, characterized by fever and swelling of the tongue and lips
viral disease of livestock

Examples

Several farms were under quarantine after bluetongue was detected in the herds.
Veterinarians tested the sheep for signs of bluetongue after an outbreak in the region.
The government issued warnings to livestock owners during bluetongue season to monitor their animals closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot-and-mouth disease - a contagious viral disease affecting cattle and sheep
