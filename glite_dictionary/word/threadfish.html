threadfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish with greatly elongated front rays on its dorsal and anal fins, found in warm seas.
fish with long fin rays

Examples

The fisherman was excited to catch a rare threadfish during his trip to the Caribbean.
The threadfish is easily recognized by the long, thread-like rays on its fins.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of the threadfish in tropical waters.
