Swammerdam

1. PROPN

/ ˈswɑːmərˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈswæmədæm / (BrE)
Jan Swammerdam (1637–1680), a Dutch naturalist and microscopist known for pioneering work in insect classification and microscopic anatomy, including the early recognition of animal cells and the discovery of red blood cells.
Dutch naturalist and microscopist (1637–1680)

Examples

Swammerdam was one of the first scientists to use a microscope to study insects in detail.
Many biology textbooks mention Swammerdam's discovery of red blood cells.
Swammerdam's meticulous experiments laid the foundation for modern entomology.

Etymology

From the Dutch surname Swammerdam.
