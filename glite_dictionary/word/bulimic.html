bulimic

1. ADJ

/ bjuːˈlɪmɪk, bəˈlɪmɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈlɪmɪk, bjʊˈlɪmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or suffering from bulimia, an eating disorder characterized by episodes of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors such as vomiting or excessive exercise.
relating to or suffering from bulimia
medical

Examples

The clinic provides therapy for bulimic patients seeking recovery.
She struggled with bulimic behaviors throughout her teenage years.
Support groups for bulimic individuals can be very helpful during treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anorexic - relating to or suffering from anorexia, an eating disorder characterized by restricted food intake

Etymology

From bulimia + -ic, where 'bulimia' comes from Greek 'boulimia' (great hunger).

2. NOUN

/ buˈlɪmɪk, bəˈliːmɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈlɪmɪk, bəˈliːmɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from bulimia, an eating disorder characterized by episodes of overeating followed by fasting or purging
person with bulimia (eating disorder)
medical

Examples

The support group provided a safe space for each bulimic to share their experiences and struggles.
As a teenager, she battled for years as a bulimic before seeking help.
Experts say it's important to understand the psychological challenges faced by every bulimic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexic - a person suffering from anorexia nervosa
  • Synonym: bulimia - an eating disorder with bouts of overeating and purging
  • Antonym: healthy - not suffering from an illness, well

Etymology

From New Latin bulimia (abnormal hunger), from Greek boulimia, from bous (ox) + limos (hunger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09855445-n
Synset POS: n
