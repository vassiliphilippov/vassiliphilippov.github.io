unaccented

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈæksɛntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈæksəntɪd / (BrE)
(of a vowel or syllable) pronounced with little or no stress.
having little or no stress (linguistics)

Examples

The final vowel in 'sofa' is unaccented and often barely heard.
In English poetry, unaccented syllables are common at the end of a line.
She found it difficult to distinguish between the accented and unaccented syllables in the new language.

Synset Examples

a syllable that ends in a short vowel is a light syllable
a weak stress on the second syllable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stressed - (of a syllable or vowel) pronounced with emphasis or force
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈæk.sɛn.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈæk.səntɪd / (BrE)
(of a syllable or word) not pronounced with emphasis; carrying no stress.
not carrying stress or emphasis

Examples

In the word 'banana', the first and last syllables are unaccented.
Poetry often arranges accented and unaccented syllables into a rhythmic pattern.
When learning English pronunciation, it's important to identify which syllables are unaccented.

Synset Examples

an atonic syllable carries no stress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accented - (of a syllable or word) pronounced with emphasis; stressed

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'accented', meaning 'not accented'.
