trying

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a situation or experience) difficult, annoying, or hard to endure.
difficult or hard to endure

Examples

The team faced a trying period after losing several key players.
Being stuck in traffic for hours was a trying experience for everyone involved.
She has shown remarkable patience during these trying times.

Synset Examples

fell upon trying times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or hard to endure

2. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
causing strain, irritation, or difficulty; making you feel stressed or tested
irritating or stressful; testing patience

Examples

It was one of the most trying experiences she had ever faced.
The constant noise made for a very trying afternoon in the office.
His patience was severely tested during the trying weeks before the exams.

Synset Examples

nerve-racking noise
the stressful days before a war
a trying day at the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stressful - causing mental or emotional stress
  • Synonym: nerve-racking - causing great anxiety or tension
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Present participle of 'try', originally from Old French 'trier' (to pick out, cull).
