Pellaea

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliːə / (AmE), / pɛˈliːə / (BrE)
A genus of mainly small, rock-dwelling ferns, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae.
a genus of rock-loving ferns

Examples

The Pellaea species thrive in rocky crevices where few other plants grow.
Botanists have discovered several rare Pellaea ferns in the canyon region.
Many gardeners appreciate Pellaea for its delicate, dark green fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'pellos' (dusky, dark), referring to the color of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈliːə / (AmE), / pɛˈliːə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly small, rock-loving ferns, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae.
genus of small rock-loving ferns

Examples

The Pellaea species thrive in rocky, dry conditions uncommon for most other ferns.
Botanists debated which family the genus Pellaea should belong to.
She photographed a rare Pellaea on her hike through the chaparral.

Etymology

From Greek *pellaia* (dusky, dark), referring to the color of the leaves.
