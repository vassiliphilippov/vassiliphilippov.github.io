fornication

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrnɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
voluntary sexual intercourse between people who are not married to each other
sexual intercourse outside marriage

Examples

In some societies, fornication was once considered a criminal offense punishable by law.
Religious leaders often preach against fornication in sermons about moral conduct.
The couple was accused of fornication because they lived together before marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adultery - sexual intercourse between a married person and someone not their spouse

Etymology

From Latin 'fornicatio', from 'fornicari' (to have intercourse), from 'fornix' (arch, vault), referring to the archways where prostitution took place in Ancient Rome.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrnɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
sexual intercourse between people not married to each other, especially when seen as immoral or illegal
sex outside of marriage

Examples

The law at the time treated fornication as a criminal offense.
Many religious traditions strongly discourage fornication outside marriage.
The couple was shunned by their community after rumors of fornication spread.

Synset Examples

adultery is often cited as grounds for divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone who is not their spouse

Etymology

From Latin fornicatio, from fornicari 'to have illicit intercourse'.
