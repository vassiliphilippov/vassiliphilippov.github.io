Helleborus niger

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛləˈbɔrəs ˈnaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs ˈnaɪɡə / (BrE)
A European evergreen plant with white or purplish, rose-like flowers that bloom in winter, commonly known as the Christmas rose.
European evergreen plant with winter-blooming flowers

Examples

The Helleborus niger adds a touch of elegance to winter gardens with its early blooms.
Botanists admire Helleborus niger for its resilience in cold climates.
She planted Helleborus niger near the front porch to enjoy its flowers during the snowy months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas rose - common name for Helleborus niger
  • Degree Higher: Hellebore - a plant of the genus Helleborus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Helleborus' and species 'niger' meaning 'black', referring to the dark roots.
