unordered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrdɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
not arranged in any specific or hierarchical order; lacking sequence or organization.
not arranged in order or sequence

Examples

The files were left in an unordered pile on the desk, making it hard to find anything.
Her thoughts tumbled out in an unordered fashion as she tried to explain.
This database table is unordered, so the records may appear in any sequence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordered - arranged in a logical, systematic, or specified order

Etymology

From un- (not) + ordered (arranged or organized).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01207115-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
not arranged in any particular order; lacking a specific sequence or arrangement.
not arranged in order

Examples

The items in the box were completely unordered, making it hard to find anything quickly.
His notes were unordered and difficult to follow during the presentation.
Data stored in an unordered list can be inefficient to search.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordered - arranged in a specific or correct order

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') to 'ordered'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01672503-a
Synset POS: a
