free-soil

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
(historical) Relating to territories or states in which slavery was prohibited, especially before the American Civil War.
denoting territories where slavery was prohibited (historical)

Examples

The territory was declared a free-soil region by Congress in 1848.
Debates over whether Kansas should join the Union as a free-soil state were intense and often violent.
He joined the free-soil party because of his opposition to the expansion of slavery.

Synset Examples

a free-soil state

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slave state - state in which slavery was permitted (U.S. history)

Etymology

From 'free' + 'soil', used in 19th-century U.S. politics in reference to land where slavery was barred.
