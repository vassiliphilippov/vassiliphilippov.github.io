uncouthly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈkuːθli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkuːθli / (BrE)
In a rude or awkward manner; lacking refinement or grace.
in a rude or awkward manner

Examples

He spoke uncouthly during the interview, shocking his potential employers.
She uncouthly interrupted the formal dinner with loud jokes.
The tourists behaved uncouthly, ignoring the local customs and traditions.

Synset Examples

uncouthly, he told stories that made everybody at the table wince

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
  • Antonym: gracefully - in an elegant or refined manner

Etymology

From 'uncouth' (Old English 'uncūth', meaning 'unknown, strange') plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.
