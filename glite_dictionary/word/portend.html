to portend

1. VERB

/ pɔrˈtɛnd / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to be a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen
be a sign or warning of future event

Examples

Dark clouds portend an approaching storm.
Some believe that a comet sighting can portend disaster.
The rise in unemployment may portend further economic troubles.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bode - to be an omen of a particular outcome
  • Synonym: to foretell - to predict or indicate a future event
  • Synonym: augur - to predict or indicate as an omen

Etymology

From Latin portendere, from por- (forth) + tendere (to stretch).
