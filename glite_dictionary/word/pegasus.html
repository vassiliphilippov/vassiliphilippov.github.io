Pegasus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛɡəsəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡəsəs / (BrE)
a large constellation in the northern sky, near Andromeda and Pisces, named after the winged horse of Greek mythology
constellation in the northern sky

Examples

The stars of Pegasus are prominent in the autumn sky of the Northern Hemisphere.
Amateur astronomers often use the Great Square of Pegasus as a starting point for sky observation.
Pegasus is easy to identify thanks to its distinctive square shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andromeda - a neighboring constellation in the northern sky
  • Synonym: Pisces - a neighboring constellation of the zodiac

Etymology

From Latin Pegasus, from Greek Pēgasos, the mythical winged horse.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɛɡəsəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡəsəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, an immortal winged horse that sprang from the blood of Medusa and is a symbol of poetic or creative inspiration.
winged horse in Greek mythology

Examples

In the ancient myth, Pegasus helps Bellerophon defeat the monstrous Chimera.
Poets often use Pegasus as a symbol of inspiration and creativity.
A statue of Pegasus stands proudly at the entrance to the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bellerophon - hero who tamed Pegasus in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Medusa - Gorgon slain whose blood gave birth to Pegasus

Etymology

From Greek Pēgasos, the mythical winged horse.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
