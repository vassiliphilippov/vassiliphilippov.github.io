purification

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjʊr.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjʊə.rɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something pure by removing unwanted substances or impurities
the removal of impurities

Examples

The purification of drinking water is essential to prevent disease.
Modern factories use advanced technology for the purification of metals.
The purification process removes harmful substances from raw sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refining - the process of making a substance pure by removing unwanted material

Etymology

From Latin purificatio, from purificare (to purify).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpjʊrəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjʊərɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing impurities or contaminants from something to make it clean or pure
the process of making pure or clean

Examples

The purification of drinking water is essential for public health.
The ritual involves the spiritual purification of the participants before entering the temple.
Industrial purification processes remove harmful chemicals from waste before it reaches the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - to make something pure by removing impurities
  • Antonym: contamination - the process of making something impure

Etymology

From Latin purificatio (a cleansing), from purificare (to purify).
