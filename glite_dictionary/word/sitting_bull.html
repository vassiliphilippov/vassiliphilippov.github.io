Sitting Bull

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪtɪŋ bʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ bʊl / (BrE)
(1831–1890) A prominent chief of the Sioux nation who led his people in resistance against the encroachment of settlers and U.S. Army forces; he played a key role in the Battle of Little Bighorn.
Sioux chief, leader at Battle of Little Bighorn

Examples

Sitting Bull is remembered as one of the most influential leaders of Native American resistance in the nineteenth century.
The defeat of General Custer in 1876 was orchestrated by Sitting Bull and his allied tribes.
Many books have been written about Sitting Bull and his enduring legacy among the Sioux people.

Etymology

From Lakota (Sioux) Tȟatȟáŋka Íyotake, meaning 'Sitting Bull.'
