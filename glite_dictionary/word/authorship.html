authorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔθɚʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of being the writer or creator of a particular idea, theory, or written work
the fact of being an author or originator

Examples

The authorship of the report was attributed to two scientists.
His claim to the authorship of the painting was later questioned by historians.
There is ongoing debate regarding the authorship of several famous Shakespearean plays.

Synset Examples

the authorship of the theory is disputed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: author - a person who writes books, articles, or other texts

Etymology

From author + -ship, patterned after other nouns of state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔθɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of being the writer of a book, article, or other written work; the act of creating written works
the act or state of writing; being an author

Examples

The authorship of the controversial novel was long a subject of debate among scholars.
Questions about the authorship of the ancient manuscript remain unresolved.
She took great pride in her authorship of several influential scientific articles.

Synset Examples

writing was a form of therapy for him
it was a matter of disputed authorship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writing - the activity or skill of marking coherent words on paper or screen

Etymology

From author + -ship, modeled after similar formations indicating state or condition.

WordNet ID: oewn-00931533-n
Synset POS: n
