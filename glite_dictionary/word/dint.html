dint

1. NOUN

/ dɪnt / (AmE), / dɪnt / (BrE)
force or power; commonly used in the phrase 'by dint of' to mean 'by means of' or 'as a result of'.
force, means (esp. in 'by dint of')
archaic

Examples

She succeeded by dint of hard work and determination.
The city was built by dint of the efforts of countless workers.
He achieved his goals by dint of persistence alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: means - a method or way of doing something

Etymology

Old English *dynt* (a blow, stroke), of Germanic origin.
