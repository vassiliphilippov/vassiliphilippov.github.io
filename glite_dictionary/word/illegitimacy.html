illegitimacy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈdʒɪtəməsi / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being illegal or not authorized by law
state of being illegal or unlawful

Examples

The court questioned the illegitimacy of the evidence presented by the prosecution.
Many activists protested against the illegitimacy of the newly enacted law.
The company was fined after the illegitimacy of its business practices was revealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegality - the state of being illegal
  • Antonym: legitimacy - the state of being legal or authorized by law

Etymology

From Latin 'illegitimitas', from 'illegitimus' (not lawful).

2. NOUN

/ ɪlɪˈdʒɪtəməsi / (AmE), / ɪlɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (BrE)
the condition of being born to parents who are not legally married to each other
the state of being born out of wedlock

Examples

In many cultures, illegitimacy once carried a strong social stigma.
The rate of illegitimacy has declined as societal attitudes have changed.
Laws discriminating against children born in illegitimacy are increasingly being abolished.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legitimacy - the status of being lawful or born to married parents

Etymology

From illegitimate + -cy; ultimately from Latin illegitimus (not lawful) and the suffix -cy (state or condition).
