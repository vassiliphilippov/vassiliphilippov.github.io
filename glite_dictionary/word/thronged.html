thronged

1. ADJ

/ θrɔŋd / (AmE), / θrɒŋd / (BrE)
(of a place) filled with a large crowd of people; crowded together
filled with a crowd; crowded

Examples

The thronged market square buzzed with excitement during the festival.
We made our way through the thronged corridors to reach the auditorium.
Tourists often avoid the thronged beaches during peak season.

Synset Examples

I try to avoid the thronged streets and stores just before Christmas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowded - (of a place) full of people, leaving little or no room for movement

Etymology

From the verb 'throng' (to crowd), which derives from Old English 'þringen' (to press, crowd).
