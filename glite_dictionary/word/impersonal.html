impersonal

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɝːsənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənl / (BrE)
not showing or involving personal feelings or connection; lacking human warmth or individual attention.
lacking personal connection or warmth

Examples

Her response to our questions was surprisingly impersonal and cold.
The office has a very impersonal atmosphere, with little room for individuality.
Many people find automated emails to be too impersonal for important communication.

Synset Examples

an impersonal corporation
an impersonal remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: personal - showing personal involvement or warmth

Etymology

From Latin 'impersonalis', from 'in-' (not) + 'personalis' (personal).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜrsənəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəl / (BrE)
not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; neutral or unbiased
not influenced by personal feelings

Examples

The manager gave impersonal feedback, focusing only on the facts rather than emotions.
She tried to remain impersonal during the debate to avoid offending anyone.
An impersonal tone is expected in most academic writing.

Synset Examples

impersonal criticism
a neutral observer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutral - not supporting either side; impartial
  • Antonym: biased - prejudiced for or against something

Etymology

From Latin 'impersonalis', from 'in-' (not) + 'personalis' (personal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01619777-s
Synset POS: s
