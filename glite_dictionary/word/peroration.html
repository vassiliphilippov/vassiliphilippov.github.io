peroration

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the concluding part of a speech or discourse, especially one intended to inspire enthusiasm in the audience
concluding section of a speech

Examples

The senator's peroration left the audience in rapturous applause.
He crafted a stirring peroration that brought his arguments together powerfully.
During the peroration, the speaker appealed to shared values and hopes.

Synset Examples

he summarized his main points in his peroration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusion - the end or final part of something, especially a speech or argument

Etymology

From Latin peroratio (concluding part of a speech), from perorare (to speak at length).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A grand or elaborate conclusion to a speech, often delivered in a highly rhetorical or flowery manner.
grand concluding part of a speech

Examples

The senator's peroration was met with a standing ovation from the audience.
His lengthy peroration included countless metaphors and ornate language.
As the lawyer delivered her peroration, the jury listened intently to her final argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech - a formal address or discourse delivered to an audience

Etymology

From Latin 'peroratio', from 'perorari' (to speak at length, conclude a speech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07256383-n
Synset POS: n
