maturity

1. NOUN

/ məˈtʃʊrəti, məˈtʊrəti / (AmE), / məˈtjʊərəti, məˈtʃʊərəti / (BrE)
the state of being fully developed or grown; the quality of showing the mental and emotional characteristics of an adult
state of full development; adulthood

Examples

She demonstrated a remarkable level of maturity for her age.
His ideas lacked the maturity needed for such an important project.
The wine reached its full maturity after several years of aging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immaturity - the state of being not fully developed

Etymology

From Latin maturitas (ripeness, fullness, maturity), from maturus (ripe, mature).

2. NOUN

/ məˈtʃʊrəti, məˈtɜrəti / (AmE), / məˈtjʊərɪti / (BrE)
The date on which a financial obligation, such as a loan or bond, must be repaid in full.
date when obligation must be repaid

Examples

The bond reaches maturity in ten years, at which time the principal will be repaid.
At maturity, the borrower must pay back the full amount of the loan.
Many investors prefer instruments with a short maturity for greater liquidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: due date - the date when payment or an obligation is due

Etymology

From Latin maturitas (ripeness, fullness), from maturus (ripe, timely).

3. NOUN

/ məˈtʃʊrəti, məˈtʊrəti, -ˈtjʊr- / (AmE), / məˈtjʊərəti, məˈtʃʊərəti, məˈtʃɔːrəti / (BrE)
the period in life when physical growth has stopped and an individual is fully developed
period after physical growth is complete

Examples

His responsibilities increased once he reached maturity in his early twenties.
Some animals do not breed until they have attained maturity.
With maturity comes a greater sense of independence and confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulthood - the state or condition of being fully grown or mature
  • Antonym: childhood - the period of being a child, prior to maturity
  • Degree Lower: adolescence - the period of life between childhood and maturity

Etymology

From Latin maturitas (ripeness, fullness).
