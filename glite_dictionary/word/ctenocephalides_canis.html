Ctenocephalides canis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnoʊˌsɛfəˈlaɪdiːz ˈkænɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛnəʊˌsɛfəˈlaɪdiːz ˈkanɪs / (BrE)
a species of flea that commonly infests dogs and can also attack cats
flea species that attacks dogs and cats
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the presence of Ctenocephalides canis on the dog's fur.
Proper treatment can help eliminate Ctenocephalides canis infestations in pets.
Unlike other fleas, Ctenocephalides canis is primarily found on dogs but may also affect cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - small wingless parasitic insect

Etymology

From Greek *ktenos* (comb) + *kephalē* (head) + Latin *canis* (dog).
