preter-human

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːtərˈhjuːmən / (AmE), / ˌpriːtəˈhjuːmən / (BrE)
(of a being) existing or originating beyond human nature or capabilities; surpassing present humanity.
beyond present humanity or human nature

Examples

Some philosophers have speculated about the existence of preter-human intelligence in the universe.
The novel describes preter-human entities influencing the course of history.
Their technology was so advanced, it appeared almost preter-human to early observers.

Synset Examples

The term preter-human suggests beings beyond current humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superhuman - having powers above human ability

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before, beyond) + human (man, human being).
