cordate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) shaped like a heart, with the notch at the base.
heart-shaped (leaf, especially in botany)

Examples

The plant is easily recognized by its cordate leaves which resemble small hearts.
Some violets have cordate foliage that sets them apart from other species.
The cordate leaf shape is an adaptation found in several tree varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart-shaped - shaped like a heart

Etymology

From Latin 'cordatus', from 'cor' meaning 'heart'.
