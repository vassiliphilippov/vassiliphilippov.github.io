Alexander Kerensky

1. PROPN

/ ˌæləgˈzændər kəˈrɛnski / (AmE), / ˌalɪgˈzandə kəˈrɛnski / (BrE)
(1881–1970) Russian revolutionary and politician who led the Russian Provisional Government after the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II, before being overthrown by the Bolsheviks.
Russian revolutionary leader (1881–1970)

Examples

Alexander Kerensky played a key role in the Russian Revolution of 1917.
After the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II, Alexander Kerensky became the head of the Russian Provisional Government.
Many historians debate the actions and decisions of Alexander Kerensky during his brief time in power.
