cyclohexanol

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.kloʊˈhɛk.sə.nɔl / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪ.kləʊˈhɛk.sə.nɒl / (BrE)
a colorless oily alcohol with a camphor-like odor, used as a solvent and in organic synthesis
a colorless oily alcohol (chemical)
medical

Examples

Cyclohexanol is commonly used as an intermediate in the production of nylon.
Proper ventilation is necessary when working with cyclohexanol in the laboratory due to its strong odor.
The safety data sheet lists cyclohexanol as a hazardous chemical requiring protective gloves.

Etymology

From 'cyclohexane' and 'ol' (denoting alcohol), referring to its structure as an alcohol derived from cyclohexane.
