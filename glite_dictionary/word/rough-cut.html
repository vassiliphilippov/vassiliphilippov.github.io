rough-cut

1. ADJ

/ ˈrəfˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkʌt / (BrE)
(of stone or timber) shaped in a rough or unfinished way, without smoothing or final detailing.
shaped roughly, not finished

Examples

The cabin was constructed with rough-cut timber beams, giving it a rustic appearance.
They ordered a slab of rough-cut granite for the outdoor steps.
The sculpture began as a rough-cut block of marble, yet to be refined by the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfinished - not brought to a finished state
  • Synonym: rough-hewn - roughly shaped or cut, especially of timber or stone

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkʌt / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) lacking refinement, sophistication, or polish; unrefined or uncultivated.
unrefined or lacking polish

Examples

His rough-cut manners stood out at the formal dinner.
The rough-cut charm of the old bar appealed to the locals.
Although his talent was obvious, his skills were still a bit rough-cut.

Synset Examples

he had coarse manners but a first-rate mind
behavior that branded him as common
an untutored and uncouth human being
an uncouth soldier — a real tough guy
appealing to the vulgar taste for violence
the vulgar display of the newly rich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarse - lacking in refinement or delicacy
  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
  • Antonym: refined - cultivated, cultured, or polished

Etymology

From 'rough' (not smooth or refined) + 'cut' (to shape), originally referring to a thing not yet finished or polished.
