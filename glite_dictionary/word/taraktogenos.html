Taraktogenos

1. NOUN

/ təˌræktəˈdʒiːnəs / (AmE), / təˌræktəˈdʒiːnɒs / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, known for their oil-rich seeds.
genus of Indonesian and Malaysian trees

Examples

Taraktogenos trees are cultivated for their valuable oil-rich seeds.
The Taraktogenos is found primarily in the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the Taraktogenos in its native habitat.

Etymology

From Greek taraktos (troubled) + -genos (born), referencing the properties of the plant's oil.
