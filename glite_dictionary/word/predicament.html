predicament

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdɪkəmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkəmənt / (BrE)
a difficult or unpleasant situation from which it is hard to escape
difficult or unpleasant situation

Examples

The hikers found themselves in a serious predicament when their map was lost in the storm.
Caught without his wallet at the checkout, he was in an embarrassing predicament.
She turned to her friend for advice, not sure how to get out of her current predicament.

Synset Examples

finds himself in a most awkward predicament
the woeful plight of homeless people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plight - a dangerous, difficult, or otherwise unfortunate situation

Etymology

From Late Latin praedicamentum (something predicated, category), from praedicare (to proclaim), but sense shifted in English.
