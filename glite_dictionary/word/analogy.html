analogy

1. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
an inference that if two or more things are alike in some respects, they are likely to be alike in other respects as well
reasoning based on similarity

Examples

Reasoning by analogy can lead to useful discoveries in science.
The lawyer drew an analogy between the case at hand and a famous previous case.
Teachers often use analogy to help students understand difficult concepts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparison - the act of examining similarities and differences between things
  • Antonym: contrast - the act of emphasizing differences rather than similarities

Etymology

From Greek 'analogia' (proportion, correspondence), via Latin 'analogia'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
(in theology) the view that any similarity between God and creatures is always surpassed by a greater dissimilarity; any analogy between God and humans is inherently inadequate.
theological principle: similarity to God is always outmatched by dissimilarity

Examples

In classical theology, the doctrine of analogy asserts that all human descriptions of God are at best partial and inadequate.
The concept of analogy explains why, despite some resemblances, our understanding of God remains limited.
Scholars debate how far analogy can bridge the gap between human language and divine reality.

Etymology

From Greek analogia (proportion, correspondence), via Latin analogia.

3. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
a comparison between two things, typically for the purpose of explanation or clarification, by showing similarities in some way
comparison showing similarity

Examples

The teacher used an analogy to explain the structure of an atom to the students.
Finding an analogy between the human heart and a pump helped clarify its function for the class.
Her analogy comparing teamwork to the workings of a clock made the idea easier to understand.

Synset Examples

the operation of a computer presents an interesting analogy to the working of the brain
the models show by analogy how matter is built up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simile - a figure of speech comparing one thing with another using 'like' or 'as'
  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From Greek analogia, meaning 'proportion' or 'correspondence.'
