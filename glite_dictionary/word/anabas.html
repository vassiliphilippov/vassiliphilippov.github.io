Anabas

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əˌbæs / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.bas / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Anabantidae, known for their ability to move on land for short distances; commonly called climbing perch.
a genus of freshwater climbing perch fish

Examples

The Anabas is often called the climbing perch because of its unique ability to traverse land between ponds.
Researchers studied the habitat and behavior of the Anabas in Southeast Asian wetlands.
The local fisherman caught several Anabas during the monsoon season.

Etymology

From Greek *anabainein* (to go up), referring to the fish's climbing ability.
