burgess

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdʒəs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdʒɪs / (BrE)
a citizen of an English borough, especially one with full rights or privileges
citizen of an English borough
archaic

Examples

The burgess presented his concerns to the borough council during the town meeting.
In medieval England, only a burgess could vote in elections for Parliament.
Her grandfather was once a respected burgess of the old market town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citizen - a legally recognized subject or national of a state or commonwealth

Etymology

From Old French 'burgeis' (inhabitant of a town), from 'bourg' (town), of Germanic origin.
