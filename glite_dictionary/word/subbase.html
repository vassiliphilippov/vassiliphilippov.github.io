subbase

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌbeɪs / (BrE)
the lowest molding of an architectural base or of a baseboard, typically forming the foundation for the structures above
lowest molding of an architectural base

Examples

The subbase must be installed carefully to provide solid support for the structure.
The ornate subbase contrasts with the more understated baseboard above.
Restorers paid special attention to the original marble subbase when repairing the historic building.

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'base' (foundation).
