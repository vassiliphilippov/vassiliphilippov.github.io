humble

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌm.bəl / (BrE)
having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; not proud or arrogant
modest; not arrogant or proud

Examples

Despite his impressive achievements, he remained humble and always credited his team.
She gave a humble apology for her mistake, expressing sincere regret.
Many admired the humble lifestyle he chose even after becoming famous.

Synset Examples

a humble apology
essentially humble … and self-effacing, he achieved the highest formal honors and distinctions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proud - having or showing high self-esteem or arrogance
  • Synonym: modest - having a moderate or humble estimate of one's own abilities

Etymology

From Latin 'humilis' (low, lowly, humble), from 'humus' (earth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01899020-a
Synset POS: a

of low social rank or birth; not of high position or status
of low social rank or birth

Examples

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbəl / (BrE)
to make someone feel less important or proud; to lower the dignity or pride of someone, often by making them feel ashamed or inferior
make someone feel less important or proud

Examples

Being defeated in the chess tournament really humbled him.
She was humbled by the generous praise she received from her mentors.
Sometimes life has a way of humbling those who become too proud.

Synset Examples

He humiliated his colleague by criticising him in front of the boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish by injuring their dignity
  • Antonym: to exalt - to lift up in rank, power, or character; to praise highly

Etymology

From Old French *humler*, from Latin *humilis* meaning 'low, lowly, humble'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01803805-v
Synset POS: v
