dendritic

1. ADJ

/ dɛnˈdrɪtɪk / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a structure or pattern) resembling or relating to a dendrite, especially in neurons or crystals, often characterized by a branching, tree-like shape.
resembling or relating to a dendrite; branching
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the neuron showed a dendritic pattern with many branches radiating outward.
The crystal's dendritic structure increased its surface area significantly.
Researchers studied the dendritic growth in metals to improve alloy performance.

Synset Examples

dendritic fiber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborescent - resembling a tree in growth or appearance
  • Antonym: linear - arranged in or extending along a straight or nearly straight line (opposite to branching)

Etymology

From Greek dendritēs (of a tree), from dendron (tree).
