antiquated

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.tɪ.kweɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.kweɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
extremely old and no longer useful, acceptable, or effective; belonging to an earlier time
extremely old or outdated

Examples

The company replaced its antiquated computer system with a state-of-the-art network.
Some people argue that the tax laws are antiquated and need to be modernized.
His views on gender roles seem quite antiquated in today's world.

Synset Examples

a ramshackle antediluvian tenement
antediluvian ideas
archaic laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old-fashioned - outdated; not modern or current
  • Antonym: modern - of or relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquatus', past participle of 'antiquare' (to make old), from 'antiquus' (ancient).
