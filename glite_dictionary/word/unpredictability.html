unpredictability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.prɪˌdɪk.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prɪˌdɪk.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unable to be predicted or foreseen; the tendency to change or act in unexpected ways.
the quality of being unpredictable

Examples

The unpredictability of the weather makes it hard to plan outdoor events.
His unpredictability on the field keeps his opponents guessing.
Many people are attracted to the excitement and unpredictability of live performances.

Synset Examples

the volatility of the market drove many investors away

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predictability - the quality of being able to be predicted

Etymology

From un- (not) + predict + -ability, meaning 'quality of being not predictable'.
