take-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈteɪk ʌp / (BrE)
the action or process of tightening, absorbing, or drawing in something, such as slack in a rope or absorption of a substance
act of tightening, absorbing, or reeling in

Examples

The take-up of slack in the cable ensured the load was secure.
Proper take-up of thread is essential for smooth sewing machine operation.
Rapid take-up of water was observed in the dry soil during the first rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈteɪk ʌp / (BrE)
a device that removes slack or takes up motion, especially in machinery such as sewing machines or looms
device reducing slack in machinery

Examples

The sewing machine's take-up ensures the thread is kept taut during stitching.
Film cameras rely on a take-up spool to wind exposed film securely.
The technician adjusted the loom's take-up to improve the tension of the woven fabric.

Synset Examples
