beardless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪrdləs / (AmE), / ˈbɪədləs / (BrE)
lacking hair on the face, especially referring to someone who does not have a beard
without facial hair (no beard)

Examples

At sixteen, he remained beardless while most of his peers had begun to grow facial hair.
The young, beardless knight was often teased by the older soldiers.
In the portrait, the king appeared beardless, which was unusual for men of his era.

Synset Examples

a smooth-faced boy of 14 years

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bearded - having a beard

Etymology

From Middle English berdles, from beard + -less.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪrlˌdəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪəl(d)ləs / (BrE)
(of a person or face) having no beard; lacking facial hair on the chin and cheeks.
without a beard

Examples

He appeared beardless and much younger than his actual age.
In the painting, the king is depicted as beardless and serene.
After shaving, his face was smooth and beardless for the first time in years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bearded - having a beard
  • Synonym: clean-shaven - having a closely shaved face, with no facial hair

Etymology

From 'beard' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without beard'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02161035-s
Synset POS: s
