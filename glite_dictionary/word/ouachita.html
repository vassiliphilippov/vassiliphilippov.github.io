Ouachita

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑːʃɪtɔː / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃɪtɑː / (BrE)
a river in the southern United States, rising in western Arkansas and flowing southeast into eastern Louisiana, where it becomes a tributary of the Red River.
river in Arkansas and Louisiana, USA

Examples

The Ouachita is famous for its scenic beauty and popular canoeing trips.
Heavy rainfall caused the Ouachita to overflow, impacting nearby communities.
The Ouachita meets the Red River in Louisiana, forming a vital waterway in the region.

Etymology

From Caddo (an indigenous language of the region), meaning ‘good hunting grounds’.
