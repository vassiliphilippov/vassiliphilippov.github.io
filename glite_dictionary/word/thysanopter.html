thysanopter

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈsænəptər / (AmE), / θaɪˈsanəptə / (BrE)
An insect belonging to the order Thysanoptera, commonly known as thrips.
insect of the order Thysanoptera

Examples

The thysanopter is often studied by entomologists because of its unique fringed wings.
Farmers monitor thysanopters carefully, as they can be serious crop pests.
A single thysanopter may be hard to see, but large numbers can damage plants quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - small insect of the order Thysanoptera

Etymology

From New Latin Thysanoptera, based on Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) + pteron (wing).
