Coronilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and herbs native to the Old World, belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).
genus of Old World shrubs in pea family

Examples

The Coronilla genus includes several attractive flowering plants often used in gardens.
Botanists have identified new species within Coronilla during recent fieldwork.
Some Coronilla species are valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fabaceae - the pea family to which Coronilla belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'coronilla', meaning 'little crown', referring to the flower's shape.
