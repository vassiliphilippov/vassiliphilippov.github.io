provost

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌvoʊst / (AmE), / ˈprɒvəʊst / (BrE)
the head administrator of certain universities or colleges, often responsible for academic affairs beneath the president or principal
senior university administrator

Examples

The provost announced a new policy to improve faculty diversity.
After years as dean, she was promoted to provost of the university.
Budget decisions must be approved by the provost before implementation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancellor - chief executive officer of a university
  • Synonym: dean - head of a faculty or department in a university

Etymology

From Old French *prévost*, from Latin *praepositus* (placed before, leader).
