rusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti / (BrE)
Impaired in skill or ability due to lack of recent practice.
impaired in skill through neglect

Examples

It's been years since I played the piano, so I'm a bit rusty.
Her Spanish was rusty after years of not using it.
You might feel rusty at first, but your skills will come back quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of practice - not having practiced a skill for some time

Etymology

From Old English *rustig*, from *rust* (corrosion of iron).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti / (BrE)
(of metal) covered with or affected by rust.
covered with rust

Examples

The old bicycle was so rusty it wouldn't move without a lot of oil.
After years at sea, the anchor became extremely rusty.
She found a rusty nail while gardening in the backyard.

Synset Examples

a rusty machine
rusty deposits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroded - damaged by a chemical process, especially rust in metals

Etymology

From Old English 'rustig', from 'rust' (corrosion of iron).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02060498-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti / (BrE)
having the color of rust; brownish-red like iron that has rusted
brownish-red, like rust

Examples

The old bicycle was covered in a rusty hue from years in the rain.
She wore a rusty sweater that matched the autumn leaves perfectly.
A rusty stain marked the edge of the metal fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reddish-brown - tinged with red and brown, similar to rust color
  • Synonym: rust - reddish-brown substance formed on iron when it corrodes

Etymology

From Old English rūstig, from rūst (rust).
