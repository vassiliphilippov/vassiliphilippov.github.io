tubocurarine

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːboʊkjʊˈrɑːriːn / (AmE), / ˌtjuːbəʊkjʊˈrɑːriːn / (BrE)
a toxic alkaloid obtained from certain South American plants, used as a muscle relaxant, especially during surgery
toxic plant alkaloid; muscle relaxant
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered tubocurarine to relax the patient's muscles before the operation.
Many early studies of neuromuscular function used tubocurarine to block nerve transmission.
Tubocurarine is derived from plants traditionally used to make arrow poison in South America.

Synset Examples

curare acts by blocking cholinergic transmission at the myoneural junction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curare - a generic name for various plant extract arrow poisons containing alkaloids like tubocurarine

Etymology

From 'tubo-' (referring to the tube-like container for curare) + 'curarine', from 'curare', a South American arrow poison.
