doubles

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəlz / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəlz / (BrE)
A form of tennis (or other racket sports) played with two players on each side.
tennis played with two players per side

Examples

They decided to play doubles instead of singles this afternoon.
The doubles final was the most exciting match of the tournament.
She excels at doubles and has won several titles with her partner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singles - tennis played with one player on each side

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəlz / (AmE), / ˈdʌblz / (BrE)
A game format in sports such as badminton or tennis in which two players compete together on each side.
game with two players per side

Examples

They decided to play doubles instead of singles in the badminton tournament.
Mixed doubles is a popular event at the annual tennis championship.
She excels at doubles because of her strong teamwork and communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singles - a game with one player on each side

Etymology

From 'double' meaning 'twice as much', used since the 18th century to describe pairs in sport.
