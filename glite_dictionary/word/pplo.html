PPLO

1. NOUN

/ pi pi ɛl oʊ / (AmE), / piː piː ɛl əʊ / (BrE)
a group of mycoplasmas resistant to antibiotics that cause a type of atypical pneumonia in humans
mycoplasma bacteria causing pneumonia
medical

Examples

The presence of PPLO in the patient's respiratory tract was confirmed by laboratory tests.
Treatment of PPLO infections is challenging due to their resistance to many common antibiotics.
Doctors often encounter PPLO when diagnosing cases of atypical pneumonia in young adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mycoplasma - a genus of bacteria lacking a cell wall
  • Degree Higher: bacteria - single-celled microorganisms

Etymology

Acronym for PleuroPneumonia-Like Organisms.
