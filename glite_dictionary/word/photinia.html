Photinia

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɪniə / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous and evergreen East Asian trees and shrubs, often grown for their ornamental white flowers and red fruits.
genus of ornamental trees and shrubs

Examples

The Photinia in our garden produces vibrant red berries every autumn.
Many landscapers choose Photinia for its glossy evergreen foliage and decorative value.
Several species of Photinia are popular as hedge plants in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'photeinos' (shining), referring to the shiny leaves.

2. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɪniə / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous and evergreen trees and shrubs native to East Asia, widely cultivated as ornamentals for their white flowers and red fruits.
genus of ornamental East Asian trees and shrubs

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of Photinia that burst into vivid red each spring.
Landscapers often recommend Photinia for its colorful foliage and resistance to pests.
Several species of Photinia are commonly grown as decorative hedges in temperate gardens.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'phōtinios' (shining), from 'phōs' (light), referring to the glossy leaves.
