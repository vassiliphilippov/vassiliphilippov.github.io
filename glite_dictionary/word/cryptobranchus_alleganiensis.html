Cryptobranchus alleganiensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptəˈbræŋkəs ˌæləˌɡeɪniˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈbræŋkəs ˌæləˌɡeɪniˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a large aquatic salamander native to rivers and streams of eastern North America, also known as the hellbender
large North American aquatic salamander

Examples

The Cryptobranchus alleganiensis is the largest salamander in North America.
Conservationists are working to protect the Cryptobranchus alleganiensis from habitat loss.
Researchers observed several Cryptobranchus alleganiensis in the clear waters of the Appalachian streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellbender - common name for Cryptobranchus alleganiensis, a large aquatic salamander

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + branchia (gills) and Allegany, a region in North America.
