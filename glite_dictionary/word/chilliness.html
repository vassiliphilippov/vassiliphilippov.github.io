chilliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪliːnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪlɪnɪs / (BrE)
a lack of warmth or enthusiasm in manner or feeling; coldness or reserve in attitude or response
lack of warmth or enthusiasm

Examples

Her unexpected chilliness during the meeting surprised everyone.
Despite his usual chilliness, he tried to be polite to the guests.
There was a noticeable chilliness in her voice when she replied.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warmth - friendliness or enthusiasm in manner or feeling
  • Synonym: coldness - lack of warmth or enthusiasm; aloofness

Etymology

From "chilly" + suffix "-ness"; ultimately from Old English "ciele" (cold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being moderately cold or cool, often causing discomfort
moderate coldness; coolness

Examples

She woke up to the chilliness of the early morning air.
The chilliness in the room made everyone put on their sweaters.
Despite the chilliness outside, the house was warm and inviting.

Synset Examples

the chilliness of early morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coolness - the state of being cool or slightly cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the state of being warm; moderate heat
  • Degree Higher: coldness - the state or quality of being cold
  • Degree Lower: coolness - the state or degree of being cool

Etymology

From "chilly" (cold) + suffix "-ness" forming nouns indicating a state or quality.
