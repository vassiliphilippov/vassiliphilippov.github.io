autograft

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌɡræft / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a graft of tissue taken from one part of a person's body and transplanted to another part of the same person
tissue graft from the same individual
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to use an autograft to repair the damaged ligament in the patient's knee.
During the procedure, skin from the thigh was harvested as an autograft to treat the burn wounds.
Because there is no risk of rejection, an autograft is often preferred in reconstructive surgery.

Synset Examples

When fractures fail to unite, autografts of bone can be extremely valuable in helping the bone to heal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allograft - tissue graft from a genetically different member of the same species
  • Antonym: xenograft - tissue graft from a donor of a different species

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'graft' (transplant).
