sclerometer

1. NOUN

/ skləˈrɑːmətər / (AmE), / sklɪəˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the hardness of materials by penetrating them with a diamond-tipped stylus
instrument for measuring hardness of materials
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician used a sclerometer to assess the hardness of the mineral samples.
A sclerometer can provide accurate measurements for quality control in manufacturing industries.
Because the sclerometer has a diamond point, it can test even very hard substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durometer - device for measuring hardness, usually of polymers or elastomers

Etymology

From Greek skleros (hard) + -meter (measuring device).
