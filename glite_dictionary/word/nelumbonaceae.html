Nelumbonaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌlʌmboʊˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / nɪˌlʌmbəʊˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic flowering plants, commonly known as the lotus family, which in some classifications is considered separate from water lilies and contains the genus Nelumbo.
family of aquatic flowering plants (lotus)

Examples

The Nelumbonaceae family is famous for its beautiful lotus flowers.
Botanists often distinguish Nelumbonaceae from other water lily families based on genetic differences.
Only the genus Nelumbo is included in Nelumbonaceae, according to most modern classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nelumbo - genus of aquatic plants known as lotus

Etymology

From genus name Nelumbo + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
