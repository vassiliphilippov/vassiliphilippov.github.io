to arrive

1. VERB

/ əˈraɪv / (AmE), / əˈraɪv / (BrE)
to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey
to reach a destination

Examples

The train will arrive at the station in ten minutes.
We finally arrived at the summit after hours of climbing.
Please let me know when you arrive at your hotel safely.

Synset Examples

She arrived home at 7 o'clock
She didn't get to Chicago until after midnight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
  • Synonym: reach - to arrive at; to come to a place

Etymology

From Old French 'arriver', from Vulgar Latin *arripare (to come to shore), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'rīpa' (shore).

2. VERB

/ əˈraɪv / (AmE), / əˈraɪv / (BrE)
(informal) To achieve success or recognition; to reach the top in one's field.
achieve success or recognition
informal

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally arrived in the business world.
He truly felt he had arrived when he received the industry award.
Young musicians often dream of the moment they will arrive on the international stage.

Synset Examples

After he published his book, he had arrived
I don't know whether I can make it in science!
You will go far, my boy!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Synonym: to make it - to achieve success; to reach one's goals (informal)
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French arriver (to reach shore), from Vulgar Latin *adripare, from Latin ad- (to) + ripa (shore).
