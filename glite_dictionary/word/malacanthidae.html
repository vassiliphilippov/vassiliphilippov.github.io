Malacanthidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈkænθəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈkanθɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes, often with short heads and bright colors, commonly known as tilefishes
family of brightly colored marine fishes (tilefishes)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Malacanthidae family off the coast of Indonesia.
Several members of the Malacanthidae are prized in aquarium collections for their striking coloration.
The distinctive jaw shape helps experts identify a fish as part of Malacanthidae.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'malakos' (soft) and 'akantha' (thorn, spine), referring to features of the fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈkænθɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmaləkænˈθɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, often brightly colored and typically having a short head, including tilefishes.
family of short-headed marine fishes

Examples

Species within the Malacanthidae family are often found in sandy or muddy ocean bottoms.
The vibrant colors of Malacanthidae make them popular among marine biologists.
Some Malacanthidae grow up to a meter long and are important in commercial fishing.

Etymology

From New Latin Malacanthidae, based on Greek malakos (soft) + akantha (thorn, spine), referring to aspects of the fish's anatomy.
