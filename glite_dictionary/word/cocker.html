cocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈkɒkə / (BrE)
A small breed of dog with wavy, silky hair, originally developed in England; a cocker spaniel.
a small breed of dog; cocker spaniel

Examples

The cocker needs to be groomed regularly to keep its coat healthy.
Her family adopted a playful cocker from the local animal shelter.
Many cockers excel in agility competitions due to their intelligence and energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocker spaniel - a breed of small spaniels with wavy hair, used as a gun dog

Etymology

Shortened from 'cocker spaniel', which originally referred to dogs used for hunting woodcock.
