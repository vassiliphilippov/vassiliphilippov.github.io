Thamnophis proximus

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɑːfɪs ˈproʊksɪməs / (AmE), / θæmˈnɒfɪs ˈprɒksɪməs / (BrE)
A species of nonvenomous snake, commonly known as the western ribbon snake, characterized by yellow or reddish stripes and found in North American woodlands, grasslands, and tropical regions.
a nonvenomous striped North American snake species

Examples

The Thamnophis proximus is often seen near ponds and streams in wooded areas.
Researchers studied the diet of the Thamnophis proximus over several months.
The distinctive stripes help Thamnophis proximus blend into grassy habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribbon snake - a common name for several slender, striped snakes of the genus Thamnophis
  • Degree Higher: garter snake - a broader group of striped North American snakes in the same genus

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) and 'ophis' (snake); species name 'proximus' from Latin meaning 'nearest' or 'close'.
