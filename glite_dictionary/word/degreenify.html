to degreenify

1. VERB

/ diˈɡrinɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈɡriːnɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove green elements, such as color or decor, from something, especially a room or space
remove green color or decor
informal

Examples

We decided to degreenify the room by taking down the leafy curtains and emerald throw pillows.
After moving in, she spent hours trying to degreenify her office to match her minimalist style.
The designer suggested they degreenify the living space to create a more neutral and calming atmosphere.

Synset Examples

To degreenify the room, they painted the walls a warm beige.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: greenify - to make something green or add green elements

Etymology

Constructed from the prefix de- (remove, reverse) and green + -ify (cause to become).
