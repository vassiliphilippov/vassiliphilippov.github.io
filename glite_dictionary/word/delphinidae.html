Delphinidae

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˈfɪnɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / dɛlˈfɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine mammals comprising the oceanic dolphins, distinguished by their streamlined bodies, beaklike snouts, and high intelligence
family of oceanic dolphins
medical

Examples

The Delphinidae family includes well-known species such as the bottlenose dolphin and the orca.
Marine biologists study the behavior of Delphinidae to better understand intelligence in marine mammals.
Unlike porpoises, members of the Delphinidae family typically have a pronounced beak and curved dorsal fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a small-toothed cetacean mammal

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek delphin (dolphin) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ dɛlˈfɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / dɛlˈfɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the family of marine mammals that includes all oceanic dolphins
family of oceanic dolphins

Examples

The Delphinidae family includes familiar species such as the bottlenose dolphin and the orca.
Researchers studied vocalizations in several members of the Delphinidae.
Unlike porpoises, all dolphins belong to the Delphinidae family, which is widely distributed throughout the world's oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a small gregarious toothed whale of the family Delphinidae

Etymology

From New Latin Delphinidae, from Delphinus (dolphin genus) and -idae (taxonomic family suffix).
