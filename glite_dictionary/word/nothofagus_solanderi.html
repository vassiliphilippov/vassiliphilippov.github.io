Nothofagus solanderi

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌθəʊˈfeɪɡəs soʊˈlændəri / (AmE), / nɒˌθɒfəˈɡʌs səʊˈlændəri / (BrE)
A species of southern beech tree native to New Zealand, commonly found in forested regions.
New Zealand southern beech tree

Examples

The ancient forests are dominated by Nothofagus solanderi and other native trees.
Ecologists studied the impact of climate change on Nothofagus solanderi populations in New Zealand.
Timber from Nothofagus solanderi is valued for its durability and attractive appearance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Nothofagus from Greek 'nothos' (false) and 'Fagus' (beech); species named after Swedish botanist Daniel Solander.
