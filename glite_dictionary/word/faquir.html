faquir

1. NOUN

/ fəˈkɪr / (AmE), / fəˈkɪə / (BrE)
a Muslim or Hindu holy man who lives as a mendicant monk and is regarded as possessing spiritual powers
a Muslim or Hindu mendicant holy man

Examples

The faquir wandered barefoot through the village, surviving on offerings from the faithful.
Many stories surround the supposed mystical abilities of the faquir.
A faquir often renounces material possessions to devote himself to spiritual practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fakir - a Muslim or Hindu holy man who lives as a mendicant monk

Etymology

From Arabic *faqīr* (poor man), via Urdu and Persian.
