subsidisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbsɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbsɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the provision of financial assistance or other benefits by a government or organization to support an activity, business, or sector
provision of subsidies

Examples

The government's subsidisation of the renewable energy sector has made solar panels more affordable.
Without significant subsidisation, small farmers would not be able to compete in the global market.
Critics argue that excessive subsidisation can lead to inefficiencies and dependency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidy - money given as financial assistance by a government or organization

Etymology

From subsidy + -ation, after French subsidisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbsɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbsɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of providing financial assistance or support, especially from the government, to help reduce the cost of services or commodities
the act of granting a subsidy

Examples

The government's subsidisation of renewable energy has led to lower electricity costs for consumers.
Efforts to increase subsidisation of public transport can help reduce urban congestion.
The subsidisation of certain industries is often debated in economic policy discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidy - a sum of money granted by the state or a public body
  • Synonym: subsidization - alternative American spelling of subsidisation

Etymology

From subsidize + -ation; from Latin subsidium (help, support).
