predecessor

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛd.əˌsɛs.ər / (AmE), / ˈpriː.dɪˌsɛs.ər / (BrE)
a person who held a position or office before the current holder
previous holder of a position

Examples

The new manager praised his predecessor for laying a strong foundation for the department.
She worked closely with her predecessor to ensure a smooth transition.
Unlike his predecessor, the current president takes a more diplomatic approach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: successor - a person who follows another in a position or office

Etymology

From Latin praedecessor (forerunner), from prae- (before) + decessor (retiring, departing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛdəˌsɛsər / (AmE), / ˈpriːdəsɛsə / (BrE)
something or someone that comes before another and often indicates or prepares the way for their arrival or occurrence.
a thing or person that comes before another

Examples

The rapid growth in technology was a clear predecessor to the digital revolution that followed.
Many see dark clouds as a predecessor of an approaching storm.
The traditional method served as a predecessor to the modern techniques now used in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forerunner - an indicator or sign of something to come; predecessor in this sense
  • Antonym: successor - a person or thing that follows another

Etymology

From Latin praedecessor, from prae- (before) + decessor (retiring officer).
