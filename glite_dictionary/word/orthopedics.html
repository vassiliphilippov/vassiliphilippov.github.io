orthopedics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθəˈpidɪks / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈpiːdɪks / (BrE)
the branch of medicine dealing with the prevention or correction of disorders of the bones, joints, and associated muscles and ligaments
medical specialty for bones and joints
medical

Examples

After breaking his leg, he was referred to orthopedics for specialized treatment.
She has published several research papers in the field of orthopedics.
The hospital's orthopedics department is renowned for its advanced surgical techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthopaedics - British spelling of the same branch of medicine

Etymology

From French orthopédie, from Greek orthos (straight) + paidion (child), originally referring to correcting deformities in children.
