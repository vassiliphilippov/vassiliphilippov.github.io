tawniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːni.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being tawny in color, resembling the color of tanned leather
the quality of being tawny in color

Examples

The tawniness of the autumn leaves gave the landscape a golden glow.
Years spent in the sun had given his skin a distinctive tawniness.
The bird’s tawniness made it difficult to spot among the grasses.

Synset Examples

the tawniness of his complexion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawny - of a warm, sandy, or light brownish-yellow color

Etymology

Formed from "tawny" (from Old French *tané*), with the suffix "-ness".
