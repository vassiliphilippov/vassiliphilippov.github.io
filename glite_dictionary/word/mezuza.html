mezuza

1. NOUN

/ məˈzuːzə / (AmE), / məˈzuːzə / (BrE)
A small case containing religious texts from Deuteronomy, traditionally affixed to the doorframe of Jewish homes according to Jewish law.
case with Jewish religious texts on doorframe

Examples

Every Jewish family placed a mezuza on the front door as a symbol of their faith.
Before entering the house, Rachel touched the mezuza and whispered a prayer.
The artisan crafted beautiful cases for the mezuza to match the new home.

Etymology

From Hebrew מְזוּזָה (məzūzāh), meaning 'doorpost'.
