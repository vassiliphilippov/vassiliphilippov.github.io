worsening

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsənɪŋ / (BrE)
Becoming worse or more severe; deteriorating in condition or quality.
becoming worse or more severe

Examples

The worsening air pollution in the city is affecting people's health.
Economists are concerned about the country's worsening economic outlook this year.
Doctors are monitoring her worsening symptoms with great care.

Synset Examples

worried by the worsening storm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improving - becoming better or more favorable

Etymology

From the verb 'worsen' + -ing, from Middle English 'worsen' (to become worse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsənɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or act of becoming worse or making something worse.
process of becoming worse

Examples

After the storm, there was a noticeable worsening of road conditions in the city.
Doctors are concerned about the worsening of air pollution over the past year.
His financial troubles led to a gradual worsening of his mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming progressively worse
  • Antonym: improvement - the process or act of becoming better

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming worse or of declining in quality, value, or condition
becoming worse; decline

Examples

Doctors monitored the patient's worsening over several days.
There has been a noticeable worsening of air quality in the city.
The worsening of diplomatic relations led to further tension.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better or of making something better
  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming progressively worse

Etymology

From the verb 'worsen' + -ing.
