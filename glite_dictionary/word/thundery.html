thundery

1. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndəri / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəri / (BrE)
(of weather or the sky) characterized by or accompanied with thunder.
weather with thunder

Examples

The sky looked thundery, and we expected a storm to start at any moment.
A thundery afternoon kept everyone indoors, listening to the rumble overhead.
Dark thundery clouds gathered rapidly, warning of severe weather on the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stormy - characterized by strong winds and usually rain, thunder, lightning, or snow

Etymology

From 'thunder' + '-y', forming adjectives indicating presence or quality.
