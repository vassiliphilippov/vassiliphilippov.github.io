backhander

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌhændər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhændə / (BrE)
a blow or slap delivered with the back of the hand
a blow with the back of the hand

Examples

She received a stinging backhander across her cheek during the argument.
The coach warned players not to resort to a backhander when they lose their temper.
He was famous for his quick backhander in street fights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - a sharp blow or smack with the open hand

Etymology

From 'backhand' + '-er', denoting one who or that which does a backhand action.
