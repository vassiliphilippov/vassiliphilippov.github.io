keratalgia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəˈtældʒə / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtaldʒɪə / (BrE)
pain in the cornea of the eye
pain in the cornea
medical

Examples

Patients with severe keratalgia often experience sensitivity to light and discomfort.
Treatment for keratalgia depends on its underlying cause, such as infection or injury.
She visited an ophthalmologist after days of persistent keratalgia in her left eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular pain - pain occurring in the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' meaning horn (referring to the cornea) + 'algia' meaning pain.
