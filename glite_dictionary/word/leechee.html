leechee

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈliː.tʃi / (BrE)
A small, round Chinese fruit with a thin, brittle shell, sweet translucent pulp, and a single seed, often eaten fresh or dried.
Chinese fruit with sweet, jellylike pulp

Examples

She offered me a bowl of fresh leechees for dessert after dinner.
The market had dried leechees imported from China.
He peeled the brittle shell from the leechee and popped the sweet fruit into his mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - Chinese fruit with thin shell and sweet pulp
  • Synonym: litchi - Alternative spelling of lychee (the fruit)

Etymology

From Cantonese 荔枝 (laih jī); also spelled lychee or litchi in English.
