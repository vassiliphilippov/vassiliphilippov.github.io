poaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of cooking food by gently simmering it in liquid just below boiling point.
cooking by simmering in liquid

Examples

Eggs prepared by poaching are tender and delicate in texture.
Many chefs prefer poaching fish to preserve its natural flavors.
Poaching is often used for fruits like pears and apples in desserts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boiling - cooking food in rapidly bubbling water or other liquid
  • Synonym: simmering - cooking food gently just below the boiling point

Etymology

From Middle French *pocher* (to poach, to simmer).
