aphetic

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈfɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or form) formed by aphesis; having lost a short unaccented vowel at the beginning of the word.
formed by loss of initial vowel (linguistic)

Examples

The word 'aphetic' is used to describe terms like 'squire', which developed from 'esquire' by aphesis.
Several English words, including 'lone' from 'alone', are considered aphetic forms.
Linguists point to 'till' as an aphetic word derived from 'until'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphesis - the gradual loss of an unstressed initial vowel or syllable

Etymology

From Greek 'aphairesis' (a taking away), by way of the linguistic term 'aphesis'.
