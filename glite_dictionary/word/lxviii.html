The Roman numeral representing the number sixty-eight (68).
Roman numeral for 68

Examples

The inscription on the ancient wall read lxviii, marking the founding year.
She scored lxviii points in the competition, breaking the previous record.
The list of winners reached as high as lxviii by the end of the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sixty-eight - The cardinal number 68

Etymology

From Latin numerals: L (50) + X (10) + V (5) + III (3) = 68.
