catechismal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈkɪzməl / (AmE), / ˌkatɪˈkɪzməl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a catechism, especially one summarizing the principles of Christianity
of or relating to a catechism

Examples

The priest gave a catechismal lecture to the children preparing for confirmation.
Her catechismal knowledge was thorough, as she answered every question about church doctrine.
The book includes a catechismal section at the end of each chapter for review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catechistic - relating to or having the form of a catechism

Etymology

From 'catechism' plus the adjectival suffix '-al'. 'Catechism' from Late Latin 'catechismus', from Greek 'katēkhizein' (to instruct orally).
