disgustedly

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈɡʌstɪdli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner showing strong dislike or revulsion.
in a way showing disgust

Examples

She turned away disgustedly when she saw the rotten food.
He spoke disgustedly about the unfair treatment of workers.
Disgustedly, Anna pushed the dirty plate aside.

Synset Examples

disgustedly, she averted her eyes when they brought in the mutilated body of the horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Antonym: delightedly - in a manner showing delight or pleasure

Etymology

From 'disgust' + '-edly', adverbial suffix.
