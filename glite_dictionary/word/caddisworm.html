caddisworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædɪsˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈkædɪsˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of a caddis fly, which constructs a protective case from silk and surrounding materials such as sand or plant debris
larva of a caddis fly

Examples

The caddisworm camouflaged itself by building a case from tiny pebbles and sand.
Biologists observe how the caddisworm selects materials to construct its unique protective home.
A trout's diet often includes the caddisworm, which lives under stones in clean streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddisfly - insect whose larvae are known as caddisworms

Etymology

From 'caddis', relating to caddis flies, and 'worm', from Old English 'wyrm' (serpent, worm).
