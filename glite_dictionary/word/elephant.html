elephant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛl.ə.fənt / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ɪ.fənt / (BrE)
A very large herbivorous mammal with a trunk and ivory tusks, native to Africa and southern Asia.
large mammal with trunk and tusks

Examples

The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth.
A single elephant can drink up to 50 gallons of water a day.
We watched the elephant use its trunk to pick leaves from the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - extinct large, hairy elephant-like mammal
  • Antonym: mouse - small rodent, sometimes contrasted humorously with elephant

Etymology

From Latin 'elephas', from Greek 'elephas' (ivory, elephant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛl.ə.fənt / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ɪ.fənt / (BrE)
the symbol of the Republican Party in the United States, originating from political cartoons by Thomas Nast in 1874.
symbol of the U.S. Republican Party
american

Examples

The elephant has represented the Republican Party since the 19th century.
Political buttons often feature the elephant during election season in the United States.
Many voters instantly recognize the elephant as a symbol of the GOP.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: GOP - Abbreviation for the Republican Party; also known as the Grand Old Party
  • Antonym: donkey - the symbol of the Democratic Party in the United States

Etymology

The use as a political symbol originates from Thomas Nast's political cartoons in 1874.
