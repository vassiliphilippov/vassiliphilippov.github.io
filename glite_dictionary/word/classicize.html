to classicize

1. VERB

/ ˈklæs.ɪ.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈklæs.ɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to give a classic or classical character or style to something
make classic or classical in style

Examples

The architect aimed to classicize the building's facade, adding columns and traditional details.
After several revisions, the poet decided to classicize his verse to align with ancient Greek forms.
The museum's decision to classicize its modern art exhibition sparked debate among critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - judged over a period of time to be of the highest quality and outstanding of its kind

Etymology

From 'classic' or 'classical' + '-ize', modeled after similar English formations.
