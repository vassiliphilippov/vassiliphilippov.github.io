marc

1. NOUN

/ mɑrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
the residue of grapes or apples that remains after pressing for juice or wine, sometimes used for distillation or as animal feed
grape or apple residue after pressing

Examples

The distillery specializes in a spirit made from marc left over from winemaking.
After pressing the apples, the remaining marc was used as compost in the orchard.
French cuisine sometimes incorporates marc to add depth to certain sauces.

Etymology

From French *marc* (meaning 'pomace' or residue left after pressing).
