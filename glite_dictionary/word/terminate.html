to terminate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɜːrmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to bring something to an end; to cause something to stop or finish
bring to an end or stop

Examples

The company decided to terminate his contract after multiple warnings.
The train will terminate at the next station due to maintenance work.
If the project is not profitable, we may have to terminate it early.

Synset Examples

She ended their friendship when she found out that he had once been convicted of a crime
The attack on Poland terminated the relatively peaceful period after WW I

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to bring something to a conclusion
  • Antonym: to begin - to start or commence something

Etymology

From Latin terminare, meaning 'to limit, end'.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtɝː.mɪ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to be the end of something; to form the conclusion or last part of
be the end of; conclude

Examples

The contract will terminate at the end of the year unless it is renewed.
The trail terminates at a beautiful waterfall in the mountains.
The shocking announcement terminated the meeting abruptly.

Synset Examples

This sad scene ended the movie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to come to or bring to a finish
  • Antonym: to begin - to start; to come into existence

Etymology

From Latin 'terminare' (to limit, end), from 'terminus' (end, boundary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02741293-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtɜːrmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to end the employment of someone; to dismiss from a job or position
end someone's employment; dismiss

Examples

Due to downsizing, the company terminated dozens of employees last month.
Her contract was terminated after repeated violations of company policy.
If you continue to underperform, the firm may have to terminate your employment.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from their job
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • Antonym: hire - to give someone a job

Etymology

From Latin terminare (to set a boundary, to end).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈtɝː.mɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to have an end or come to an end, either literally in space or time, or figuratively; to stop or cease
to come to an end; stop

Examples

The railway line terminates at the city center, where all passengers must disembark.
The contract terminated at midnight, so it is no longer valid.
The festival terminates with a spectacular fireworks display every year.

Synset Examples

the bronchioles terminate in a capillary bed
Your rights stop where you infringe upon the rights of other
My property ends by the bushes
The symphony ends in a pianissimo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to come to a conclusion or finish
  • Synonym: to cease - to stop or bring to an end
  • Antonym: to commence - to begin or start

Etymology

From Latin terminare (to limit, end), from terminus (boundary, limit).

Source: WordNet
