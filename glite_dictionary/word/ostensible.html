ostensible

1. ADJ

/ ɑːˈstɛnsɪbəl / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛnsɪbəl / (BrE)
appearing to be true or real, but not necessarily so; stated or claimed as true, though possibly not genuine.
apparent, but possibly not genuine

Examples

The ostensible reason for the meeting was to discuss the budget, but other issues were addressed.
She was the ostensible leader, though others held the real power.
His ostensible concern for our safety seemed insincere.

Synset Examples

His ostensible purpose was charity, his real goal popularity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparent - clearly visible or understood; seeming
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact; not imaginary or supposed

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ostensibilis', from Latin 'ostendere' (to show).

2. ADJ

/ ɑːˈstɛnsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛnsəbl̩ / (BrE)
stated or appearing to be true, but not necessarily so; professed or pretended.
appearing or claimed to be one thing, but possibly not so

Examples

The company’s ostensible reason for the merger was efficiency, but market control was the real motivation.
She accepted his ostensible invitation to work late, suspecting there was another reason involved.
Although his ostensible concern was the budget, he really wanted more influence in the project.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparent - clearly visible or understood; seeming,
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From French 'ostensible', from Latin 'ostensus', past participle of 'ostendere' (to show).
