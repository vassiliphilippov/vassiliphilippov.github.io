religious

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs / (BrE)
having or showing belief in and reverence for a god or gods
having belief in a deity

Examples

As a religious person, she prays every morning before breakfast.
He was known for his religious devotion and frequent visits to the temple.
Her family holds a religious ceremony every year to celebrate their faith.

Synset Examples

a religious man
religious attitude

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to the human spirit or soul as opposed to material things

Etymology

From Latin religiosus (pious, devout), from religio (reverence, scrupulousness).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs / (BrE)
a member of a religious order, especially someone bound by vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience
member of a religious order

Examples

The religious devoted her life to serving the poor and needy in her community.
Several religious gathered for morning prayers in the chapel.
The monastery was home to over thirty religious who followed a strict daily routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious order and lives in a monastery
  • Synonym: nun - a woman who is a member of a religious order, especially in the Christian tradition

Etymology

From Latin 'religiosus' (devout, pious).
