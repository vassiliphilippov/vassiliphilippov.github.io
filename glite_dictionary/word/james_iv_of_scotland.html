James IV of Scotland

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɔrθ əv ˈskɑtlənd / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɔːθ əv ˈskɒtlənd / (BrE)
(1473–1513) King of Scotland from 1488 to 1513, noted for his marriage to Margaret Tudor and for his death in defeat at the Battle of Flodden.
King of Scotland, 1488–1513

Examples

James IV of Scotland was a key Renaissance ruler who encouraged education and the arts.
The defeat and death of James IV of Scotland at Flodden marked a turning point in Scottish history.
By marrying Margaret Tudor, James IV of Scotland helped pave the way for the later union of the Scottish and English crowns.
