Rhea Silvia

1. PROPN

/ ˈriːə ˈsɪl.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈriːə ˈsɪl.vi.ə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, a vestal virgin who was the mother, by Mars, of the twins Romulus and Remus.
mother of Romulus and Remus in Roman mythology

Examples

In many retellings, Rhea Silvia was fated to give birth to Rome's legendary founders.
The myth describes how Rhea Silvia was visited by Mars in her sacred grove.
Historians debate whether Rhea Silvia's story is based on a real person or is purely legend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romulus and Remus - the legendary twin founders of Rome

Etymology

From Latin Rhea Silvia, from classical sources in Roman mythology.
