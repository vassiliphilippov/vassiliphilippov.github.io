warragal

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrəɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒrəɡəl / (BrE)
A wolflike wild dog of Australia, typically yellowish-brown in color; also known as a dingo.
Australian wild dog (dingo)

Examples

The warragal hunts in packs across the Australian outback.
Early settlers often feared the warragal for its attacks on livestock.
Seeing a warragal in the wild is a rare and memorable experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dingo - a wild dog native to Australia

Etymology

From Dharug (Sydney Aboriginal language) *warrigal*, meaning ‘wild dog’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɒrəɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒrəɡəl / (BrE)
An Australian wild horse; a brumby.
Australian wild horse
archaic archaic

Examples

The old stockman spotted a herd of warragal grazing by the creek at dusk.
Early settlers often tried to capture warragals for use as working horses.
Legends about the speed and toughness of the warragal are common in Australian folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brumby - a wild horse in Australia

Etymology

From an Aboriginal Australian word, possibly from Dharug *warrigal* (wild, wild dog, dingo).
