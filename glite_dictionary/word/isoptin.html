Isoptin

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsɑptɪn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsɒptɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked drug name for verapamil, used as a calcium channel blocker to treat hypertension, angina, certain heart rhythm disorders, and migraine.
trade name for verapamil (calcium channel blocker)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Isoptin to help control his high blood pressure.
Patients with angina may be treated with Isoptin to improve blood flow to the heart.
If you experience side effects while taking Isoptin, contact your healthcare provider immediately.
