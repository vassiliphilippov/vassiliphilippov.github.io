to chasten

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪ.sən / (BrE)
to correct or discipline someone by inflicting punishment or hardship, often with the intention of improving their behavior or character
to correct by punishment or discipline

Examples

The teacher hoped the detention would chasten the disruptive student.
Life’s hardships can chasten even the most confident people.
The defeat served to chasten the previously undefeated team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punish - to inflict a penalty for an offense
  • Synonym: discipline - to train someone to obey rules using punishment or correction

Etymology

From Old French 'chastier', from Latin 'castigare' meaning 'to punish, correct'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪsən / (BrE)
to restrain or moderate, often by discipline or correction; to make someone less proud or self-satisfied
restrain or discipline; make less proud

Examples

Failure can chasten even the most confident leaders.
Her experiences abroad deeply chastened her views on life.
He hoped that time would chasten his ambition and perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - to train or correct by punishment or control
  • Synonym: humble - to make (someone) less proud or arrogant

Etymology

From Old French chastier, from Latin castigare (to pursue, chastise, correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00203298-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪsən / (BrE)
to criticize or punish someone strictly, especially in order to correct or improve their behavior
criticize or punish strictly to improve behavior

Examples

The manager did not hesitate to chasten the team after their poor performance.
He was chastened by his teacher for not doing his homework properly.
Her stern words chastened him, making him reflect on his actions.

Synset Examples

She chastised him for his insensitive remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - to punish or train in order to correct behavior
  • Synonym: scold - to speak to someone angrily because they have done something wrong
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French chastiier, from Latin castigare (to punish, to correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00825981-v
Synset POS: v
