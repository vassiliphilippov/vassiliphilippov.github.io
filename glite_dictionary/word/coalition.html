coalition

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a union or alliance formed by the merging of distinct individuals, groups, or organizations into one body or group; the act of growing or coming together as one
union or alliance of diverse elements

Examples

Several political parties formed a coalition to oppose the current government.
The environmental coalition brought together activists from different backgrounds.
Some plants show a natural coalition of their stems during early development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit
  • Antonym: division - the act or process of separating or being separated

Etymology

From Latin coalitio (a growing together), from coalescere (to grow together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the state or process of being combined into one group or body for a common purpose, often temporarily
the state of being combined into one group

Examples

The two parties formed a coalition to address the economic crisis facing the country.
After months of negotiations, a coalition emerged that represented various social groups.
The fragile coalition quickly dissolved when disagreements arose over policy priorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a group formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations
  • Synonym: union - the action of joining together or the fact of being joined together
  • Antonym: disunity - lack of unity or agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'coalitio' (a growing together), from 'coalescere' (to grow together).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an alliance of individuals, groups, or nations formed for a common purpose, especially a formal agreement for joint action
alliance formed for a common purpose

Examples

The countries formed a coalition to respond to the crisis.
A new political coalition emerged ahead of the election to challenge the ruling party.
Several organizations joined the coalition to support environmental reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations
  • Antonym: faction - a small organized dissenting group within a larger one

Etymology

From Latin coalitio (the act of uniting), from coalescere (to grow together).
