Medina

1. PROPN

/ məˈdiːnə / (AmE), / məˈdiːnə / (BrE)
A city in western Saudi Arabia, regarded as the second holiest city in Islam and the burial place of the Prophet Muhammad.
holy city in Saudi Arabia

Examples

Each year, thousands of pilgrims travel to Medina as part of their religious journey.
The Prophet Muhammad's tomb is located in the city of Medina.
Medina is considered the second holiest site in Islam after Mecca.

Etymology

From Arabic *al-Madīna* (the city), short for *al-Madīna al-Munawwara* (the Radiant City).
