reinstatement

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving someone back their previous job or position after they have lost it or been removed
the act of restoring someone to a former position

Examples

After a lengthy investigation, the judge ordered the reinstatement of the dismissed employee.
Union leaders demanded the immediate reinstatement of workers who had been unfairly suspended.
Her reinstatement came as a surprise to many in the department.

Synset Examples

we insisted on the reinstatement of the colonel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of bringing back something to its original state

Etymology

From re- (again) + installment (placement in office or position).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪnˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of restoring someone or something to a previous position, status, or condition
restoration to a previous position or state

Examples

After a lengthy investigation, she celebrated her reinstatement as department head.
The judge ordered the reinstatement of the employee who was unfairly dismissed.
Union leaders demanded the reinstatement of benefits cut during the previous year.

Synset Examples

her reinstatement to her former office followed quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former owner, place, or condition
  • Antonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from a job or position

Etymology

From the verb 'reinstate', which derives from 're-' (again) and 'instate' (to place in a position).
