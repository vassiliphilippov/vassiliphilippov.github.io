washout

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːʃˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌaʊt / (BrE)
an area where soil or gravel has been removed by water, especially as a result of heavy rain or flooding
erosion of soil or gravel by water

Examples

After the storm, a huge washout made the road impassable for several days.
The construction crew had to repair the washout beneath the railway tracks immediately.
Every spring, heavy rains cause at least one washout along the riverbank.

Synset Examples

from the house they watched the washout of their newly seeded lawn by the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or removal of soil or rock by natural forces

Etymology

From 'wash' + 'out', referring to matter being washed away.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌaʊt / (BrE)
a break or channel formed in the ground by the erosion of soft soil caused by water, typically resulting in damage to roads or railways
channel or break created by water erosion

Examples

Heavy rains caused a washout that closed the railway line for a week.
The hiking trail was impassable after a washout swept away the bridge.
Engineers worked through the night to repair the washout before the main road could reopen.

Synset Examples

it was several days after the storm before they could repair the washout and open the road

Etymology

From the verb 'wash out', describing the action of water eroding and removing soil.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13942206-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who fails to achieve success or who is unsuccessful at something, especially after initial promise or expectation
someone who is unsuccessful

Examples

After flunking out of medical school, he was labeled a washout by his family.
Nobody expected the promising athlete to become a washout after such a strong start.
She feared that one bad performance would make her seem like a washout in her new company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success in doing or achieving something
  • Antonym: success - the achievement of an aim or purpose
