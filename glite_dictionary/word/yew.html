yew

1. NOUN

/ juː / (AmE), / juː / (BrE)
The durable, fine-grained wood of the yew tree, especially valued for making furniture and archery bows.
wood from the yew tree

Examples

The longbow was traditionally made from yew because of its strength and flexibility.
She admired the antique chest carved from yew in the corner of the room.
Furniture made of yew is known for its rich color and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - wood from the ash tree; also used for bows and tools
  • Synonym: oak - wood from the oak tree; commonly used hardwood

Etymology

From Old English *iw*, from Proto-Germanic *īwaz*.

2. NOUN

/ juː / (AmE), / juː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree or shrub with flat needle-like leaves and red, cup-shaped berries, belonging to the genus Taxus, often used for hedges and ornamental planting.
evergreen tree or shrub with red berries

Examples

The garden features a centuries-old yew tree that is carefully trimmed each year.
Yew hedges are popular in formal landscaping for their dense foliage and attractive appearance.
Some ancient churches in England are surrounded by yew trees, which are often considered sacred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxus - Genus of coniferous trees and shrubs known as yews

Etymology

Old English *iw*, from Proto-Germanic *īwaz*, related to Latin *ius* and Greek *hýon*.
