Ruta graveolens

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtə ɡræviˈoʊlənz / (AmE), / ˈruːtə ˌɡrævɪˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A strong-scented perennial European herb with grey-green, bitter-tasting leaves, sometimes used in traditional medicine and cooking.
European perennial herb (rue)

Examples

The leaves of Ruta graveolens were once widely used as a medicinal herb.
Some gardeners plant Ruta graveolens to deter pests because of its strong scent.
Contact with Ruta graveolens can cause skin irritation in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue - Common name of Ruta graveolens, a strong-scented perennial herb

Etymology

From Latin: ruta (rue) + graveolens (strong-smelling).
