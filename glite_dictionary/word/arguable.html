arguable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrɡjuəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡjuəbl̩ / (BrE)
Open to doubt or debate; not clearly true or certain.
open to doubt or debate

Examples

It is arguable whether the new policy will improve outcomes for everyone.
His claim is highly arguable and requires more evidence before being accepted.
The effectiveness of the treatment is still arguable among medical professionals.

Synset Examples

that is a moot question

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indisputable - impossible to doubt or question
  • Synonym: debatable - open to discussion or argument

Etymology

From Latin arguere (to argue) + -able.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrɡjuəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡjuəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be supported or defended by argument; open to debate or question.
open to debate; defensible by argument

Examples

It is arguable that increased spending will result in faster economic growth.
The success of the policy is highly arguable and will depend on many factors.
Her claim is arguable, but it lacks strong supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debatable - open to question or dispute
  • Antonym: indisputable - impossible to doubt or question; certain

Etymology

From argue + -able, modeled after Late Latin *arguabilis*.

Synset POS: s
