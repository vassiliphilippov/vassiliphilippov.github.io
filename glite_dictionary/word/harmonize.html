to harmonize

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to bring different things into agreement or make them compatible; to make harmonious
bring into agreement or harmony

Examples

We worked together to harmonize our different approaches to the project.
The new policy aims to harmonize regulations across all EU countries.
It is important to harmonize your personal values with your professional goals.

Synset Examples

harmonize one's goals with one's abilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - to make two ideas or situations agree
  • Antonym: conflict - a state of disagreement or incompatibility

Etymology

From Greek harmonia (agreement, harmony) via French harmoniser and Medieval Latin harmonizare.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)

Examples
