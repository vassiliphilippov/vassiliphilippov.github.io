buckwheat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌwit / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
A grain-like seed that is ground into flour and used in making foods such as pancakes, noodles, and porridge.
grain-like seed used for flour

Examples

She bought some buckwheat to make pancakes for breakfast.
Gluten-free bread can be made using buckwheat flour instead of wheat flour.
The recipe called for a cup of ground buckwheat mixed with eggs and milk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wheat - a common cereal grain used for flour
  • Synonym: buckwheat flour - flour made from buckwheat seeds

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *boecweite* (beech wheat), so called because its seeds resemble beech nuts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌwiːt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
An annual plant of the genus Fagopyrum, native to Asia, with small pinkish-white flowers and edible triangular seeds used for food and flour.
an annual plant with edible seeds used as food

Examples

She made pancakes using buckwheat flour for a wholesome breakfast.
The fields were blooming with buckwheat during the late summer months.
He enjoyed the nutty taste of buckwheat in his porridge each morning.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'boecweite', meaning 'beech wheat', due to the resemblance of the seed to beech nuts.
