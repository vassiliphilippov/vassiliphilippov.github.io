alfresco

1. ADJ

/ ælˈfrɛs.koʊ / (AmE), / ælˈfrɛs.kəʊ / (BrE)
(especially of eating) in the open air.
in the open air, especially outdoors dining

Examples

We decided to have an alfresco dinner on the patio as the weather was perfect.
They enjoyed an alfresco concert in the park last Saturday evening.
Many cafés offer alfresco seating during the summer months.

Synset Examples

an alfresco lunch
an open-air theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdoors - in or into the open air; outside a building

Etymology

From Italian 'al fresco', meaning 'in the cool (air)'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01696965-s

2. ADV

/ ælˈfrɛskoʊ / (AmE), / ælˈfrɛskəʊ / (BrE)
in the open air; outside, especially when referring to eating or other activities.
in the open air; outside

Examples

They decided to have lunch alfresco on the terrace.
During summer, many cafes offer alfresco dining options.
We enjoyed an alfresco concert in the park last night.

Synset Examples

in summer we play outside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdoors - outside, not inside a building

Etymology

From Italian 'al fresco' (in the cool [air]).

WordNet ID: oewn-00111402-r
Synset POS: r
