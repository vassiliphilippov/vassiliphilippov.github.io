morphea

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈfiːə / (AmE), / mɔːˈfiːə / (BrE)
A localized form of scleroderma characterized by patches of hardened, thickened skin.
localized scleroderma skin condition
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with morphea after noticing several hard, discolored patches on her arms.
Effective treatment of morphea often requires collaboration between dermatologists and rheumatologists.
Unlike systemic scleroderma, morphea usually affects only the skin and not internal organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scleroderma - A chronic disease causing hardening and tightening of the skin and connective tissues

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form, shape), referring to the appearance of lesions.
