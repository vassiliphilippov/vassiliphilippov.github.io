Acrocomia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈkoʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈkəʊmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical feather palms native to Central and South America, characterized by spiny trunks and edible fruits.
tropical American feather palms genus

Examples

The Acrocomia genus includes several species used for their oil-rich seeds.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Acrocomia in tropical forests.
Many rural communities harvest Acrocomia fruits for food and income.

Etymology

From Greek ‘akron’ (tip, summit) + ‘komē’ (hair), referring to the palm's leafy crown.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.rəˈkoʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈkəʊmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of feather palms native to Central and South America, known for their spiny trunks and edible fruits.
a genus of Central and South American feather palms

Examples

Many Acrocomia species are cultivated for their edible fruits and valuable oils.
The spiny trunks of Acrocomia palms make them easily recognizable in tropical landscapes.
Researchers have studied Acrocomia for its potential use in biofuel production.

Etymology

From Greek 'akros' (topmost, high) and 'koma' (hair), referring to the tufted crown of the palm.
