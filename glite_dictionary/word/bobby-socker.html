bobby-socker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbi ˌsɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈbɒbi ˌsɒkə / (BrE)
an adolescent girl, especially in the 1940s, known for wearing bobby socks and being enthusiastic about popular culture
adolescent girl, 1940s bobby-socks wearer
archaic american

Examples

In the 1940s, bobby-sockers would flock to concerts to see their favorite singers perform.
My grandmother said she was a proud bobby-socker and wore her white socks everywhere.
Photographs from the era show groups of bobby-sockers giggling outside movie theaters.

Etymology

From 'bobby socks', a style of short socks popular in the 1940s, plus the -er suffix.
