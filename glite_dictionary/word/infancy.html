infancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fən.si / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.si / (BrE)
the early stage in the development of something; the beginning or initial period when something is just starting
early stage of development

Examples

The company's infancy was marked by many financial struggles.
Space exploration is still in its infancy compared to other scientific fields.
Artificial intelligence is rapidly evolving but remains in its infancy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maturity - the state of being fully developed

Etymology

From Latin 'infantia' (infant condition), from 'infans' (unable to speak).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fən.si / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.si / (BrE)
the earliest stage or period of development or existence; the state of being an infant
earliest stage of development

Examples

During its infancy, the company operated out of a small garage.
The technology is still in its infancy and has much room for improvement.
His interest in music dates back to his infancy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: childhood - the period of being a child between infancy and adolescence

Etymology

From Latin 'infantia' (inability to speak, infancy), from 'infans' (infant, unable to speak).
