Hibiscus elatus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
an erect forest tree native to Cuba and Jamaica, with variably hairy leaves and orange-yellow or orange-red flowers, valued for its moderately dense timber used in cabinetwork and gunstocks
Caribbean forest tree with valuable timber

Examples

The wood of Hibiscus elatus is highly prized by cabinetmakers for its durability and color.
In Jamaica, Hibiscus elatus is commonly known as the Blue Mahoe.
The vibrant orange-red flowers of Hibiscus elatus make it a popular choice for reforestation projects.

Etymology

From Latin genus Hibiscus and Latin elatus meaning 'lofty' or 'high'.
