throne

1. NOUN

/ θroʊn / (AmE), / θrəʊn / (BrE)
A ceremonial chair for a monarch, bishop, or other high-ranking official, used as a symbol of authority or power.
ceremonial chair of authority

Examples

The king entered the grand hall and took his place on the throne.
Centuries ago, rival families fought fiercely for control of the English throne.
As the bishop ascended the throne, the congregation fell silent.

Synset Examples

the king sat on his throne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seat - a thing made or used for sitting on
  • Synonym: dais - a raised platform for a speaker or honored guest
  • Degree Lower: stool - a simple seat without arms or a back

Etymology

From Old French *trone*, from Latin *thronus*, from Greek *thronos* (seat, chair).
