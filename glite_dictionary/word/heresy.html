heresy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrəsi / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəsi / (BrE)
a belief or opinion that is profoundly at odds with the official or orthodox doctrine, especially in religion
belief at odds with orthodoxy

Examples

His controversial views on church doctrine were considered heresy by the religious authorities.
Throughout history, many scientists have been accused of heresy for challenging established beliefs.
In medieval Europe, a charge of heresy could result in severe punishment or even death.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory or doctrine
  • Synonym: heterodoxy - the quality of being unorthodox; deviation from accepted beliefs

Etymology

From Old French *heresie*, from Latin *haeresis* (school of thought, heresy), from Greek *hairesis* (choice, sect).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrəsi / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəsi / (BrE)
a belief or opinion that is profoundly at odds with the accepted doctrine of a particular religion, especially Christianity
belief contrary to religious doctrine

Examples

He was accused of heresy and brought before the inquisition.
Some scientific theories were once considered heresy by religious authorities.
The book explores the impact of heresy in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
  • Synonym: apostasy - abandonment or renunciation of a religious or political belief

Etymology

From Old French *heresie*, from Latin *haeresis* (school of thought, sect), from Greek *hairesis* (choice, heresy).
