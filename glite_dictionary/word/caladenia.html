caladenia

1. NOUN

/ kæləˈdiːniə / (AmE), / kaləˈdiːnɪə / (BrE)
Any of various orchids of the genus Caladenia, known for their distinctive and sometimes spider-like flowers, native to Australia.
genus of Australian orchids

Examples

Several species of caladenia can be found growing wild in Western Australia.
The pink caladenia blooms early in the spring, adding color to the dry woodland floor.
Botanists discovered a new variety of caladenia during their field study.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'aden' (gland), referring to the flower's appearance.
