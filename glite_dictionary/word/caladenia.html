Caladenia

1. NOUN

/ kæləˈdiːniə / (AmE), / kæləˈdiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of terrestrial orchids native to Australia and New Caledonia, known for their delicate, spider-like flowers
genus of Australian terrestrial orchids

Examples

Many species within the Caladenia genus have uniquely shaped and colorful flowers.
Conservationists are working to protect endangered Caladenia orchids from habitat loss.
The spider orchid, a member of the Caladenia genus, attracts pollinators with its distinctive appearance.

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) + aden (gland), referring to the flower's appearance.
