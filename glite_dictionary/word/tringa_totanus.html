Tringa totanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋɡə toʊˈteɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋɡə təˈtɑːnəs / (BrE)
a Eurasian wading bird with brownish upperparts and distinctive long red legs, known as the common redshank
a wading bird with long red legs

Examples

The Tringa totanus nests in marshes and wet meadows across Europe and Asia.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Tringa totanus among the reeds.
The long red legs help Tringa totanus wade through shallow water to find food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common redshank - common name for Tringa totanus, a wading bird with red legs

Etymology

From modern Latin, the scientific name assigned by Linnaeus. 'Tringa' from Neo-Latin (a wader), originally from Greek 'trungas'.
