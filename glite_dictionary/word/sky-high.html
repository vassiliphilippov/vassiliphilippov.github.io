sky-high

1. ADV

/ skaɪˈhaɪ / (AmE), / skaɪˈhaɪ / (BrE)
(used with verbs such as 'blow') To a great height or degree, especially so as to be destroyed or blown to pieces.
completely destroyed or blown apart

Examples

After the gas leak, the explosion blew the factory sky-high.
Protesters threatened to blow the building sky-high if their demands weren’t met.
The demolition crew set the charges and sent the old stadium sky-high.

Synset Examples

they blew the bridge sky-high
the committee blew the thesis sky-high
