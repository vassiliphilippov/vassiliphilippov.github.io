petaloid

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛtəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛtəˌlɔɪd / (BrE)
resembling a flower petal in appearance or form
resembling a flower petal

Examples

The stamens of this species are petaloid and brightly colored.
Some leaves have evolved a petaloid shape to attract pollinators.
The scientist described the unusual flower as having petaloid sepals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petal - each of the segments of the corolla of a flower, usually colored

Etymology

From Greek 'petalon' (leaf, petal) + '-oid' (like, resembling).
