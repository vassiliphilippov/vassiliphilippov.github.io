deglutition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiɡluˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːɡlʊˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of swallowing, especially as a physiological function
the act of swallowing
medical

Examples

Difficulty with deglutition can be an early sign of disease in the throat.
The doctor observed her deglutition to diagnose the swallowing disorder.
Proper deglutition is essential for safe eating and drinking.

Synset Examples

one swallow of the liquid was enough
he took a drink of his beer and smacked his lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallowing - the act of causing food or drink to go down the throat

Etymology

From Latin 'deglutire' (to swallow down).
