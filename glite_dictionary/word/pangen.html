pangen

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈpan.dʒɛn / (BrE)
a hypothetical particle of protoplasm once believed to control heredity; a term used in early theories of genetics.
hypothetical heredity particle (historical biology)

Examples

In the nineteenth century, scientists speculated that a pangen carried hereditary information in cells.
The theory involving the pangen was eventually disproved by advances in genetics.
Darwin proposed the existence of the pangen in his hypothesis of pangenesis.

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) + 'genos' (kind, race), coined in the 19th century for early genetics theories.
