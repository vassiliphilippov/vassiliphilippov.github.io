penology

1. NOUN

/ piˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / piːˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the management of prisons and the rehabilitation of prisoners, especially as a branch of criminology
the study of prisons and rehabilitation
legal

Examples

She is writing her dissertation on penology and the impact of prison reforms.
The university offers a specialized course in penology as part of its criminology program.
Modern penology emphasizes rehabilitation over punitive measures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminology - the scientific study of crime and criminals

Etymology

From Latin *poena* (punishment) + -logy (the study of).
