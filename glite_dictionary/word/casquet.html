casquet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæskɪt / (BrE)
a light open helmet without a visor or beaver, typically worn in historical contexts
light open helmet (historical)
archaic

Examples

The knight donned his casquet before entering the tournament grounds.
Historical reenactors often wear a casquet to accurately represent medieval soldiers.
The painting depicted a soldier in a distinctive casquet with ornate detailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casque - a helmet, especially one with a decorative crest

Etymology

From French 'casquet', diminutive of 'casque' (helmet).
