tarweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several resinous, glandular plants of the genus Madia, native to western North and South America
a resinous plant of the genus Madia

Examples

In late summer, the fields were covered with sticky tarweed plants in full bloom.
The hikers noticed the strong scent of tarweed as they passed through the meadow.
Some species of tarweed are valued for their oil-rich seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several western American plants of the genus Grindelia, with sticky resinous leaves and stems, some species poisonous to livestock and once used medicinally.
resinous western American plant (Grindelia genus)

Examples

The hills were covered in blooming tarweed during late summer.
Ranchers worry that tarweed can be toxic if cattle graze on it.
Herbalists once used extracts of tarweed as a traditional remedy for skin ailments.
