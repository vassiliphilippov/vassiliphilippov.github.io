sassing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsæsɪŋ / (BrE)
Impudent or disrespectful talk, especially as a comeback or retort.
impudent or cheeky talk
informal

Examples

His constant sassing of the teacher finally got him sent to the principal's office.
Grandma won't tolerate any sassing at the dinner table.
If you keep up that sassing, you'll be grounded for a week.

Synset Examples

don't give me any of your sass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sass - impudent or cheeky talk or remarks
  • Synonym: backtalk - disrespectful or impudent talk back to authority
  • Antonym: respect - polite or deferential speech or behavior

Etymology

From 'sass', an alteration of 'sauce' (impudence), influenced by African American Vernacular English.
