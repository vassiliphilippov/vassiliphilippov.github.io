Illicium verum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪsiəm ˈvɛrəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsiəm ˈvɜːrəm / (BrE)
A small tree native to China and Vietnam, known for its star-shaped, anise-scented fruit used as a spice and in medicine as a carminative.
tree producing star anise spice

Examples

Many traditional Chinese dishes include Illicium verum for its distinctive aroma and flavor.
The fruits of Illicium verum are often mistaken for those of similar but toxic species.
Extracts from Illicium verum are valued for their use in herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - the common name for the fruit of Illicium verum, used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin: 'Illicium' meaning 'allurement' or 'enticement', and 'verum' meaning 'true'.
