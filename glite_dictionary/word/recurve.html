to recurve

1. VERB

/ riˈkɝv / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜːv / (BrE)
to curve or bend something back or downward
bend back or downward

Examples

The archer skillfully recurved the bow before taking aim at the target.
Engineers recurved the metal bar to increase its structural strength.
Some plants recurve their leaves to reduce water loss during heatwaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - to shape or force something straight into a curve

Etymology

From Latin recurvus (bent back), from re- (back) + curvus (curved).
