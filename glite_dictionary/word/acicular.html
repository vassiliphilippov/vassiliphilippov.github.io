acicular

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪkjələr / (AmE), / əˈsɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
narrow, long, and pointed in shape, like a needle; especially used to describe certain leaves or crystals
narrow, needle-shaped

Examples

The acicular leaves of the pine tree are adapted to cold climates.
Under the microscope, the crystals appeared acicular and densely packed.
Some minerals form acicular structures, resembling tiny needles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needle-shaped - having the shape of a needle; thin and pointed

Etymology

From Latin 'acicula' (small needle), diminutive of 'acus' (needle).
