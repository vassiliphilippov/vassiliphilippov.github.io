kaolin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ə.lɪn / (BrE)
A fine, usually white clay produced by the weathering of aluminous minerals (such as feldspar), used in ceramics, papermaking, and as an absorbent or filler.
a fine white clay used in ceramics and industry

Examples

The artist chose kaolin to create the porcelain vase because of its brightness and purity.
Paper manufacturers often use kaolin as a filler to improve the surface of their products.
Pharmaceutical companies sometimes include kaolin in medications as an absorbent ingredient.

Etymology

From French 'kaolin', from Chinese 'Gāolǐng', after a mountain in Jiangxi, China, where the clay was first obtained.
