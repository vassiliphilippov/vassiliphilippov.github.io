borrowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔr.oʊ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒr.əʊ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of taking ideas, words, or motifs from another source and using them as one's own, often in a different context
adoption of ideas or words from another source

Examples

The borrowing of philosophical terms from Greek is common in modern languages.
There has been an increase in borrowing of musical styles between different cultures.
Some critics argue that borrowing from other artists lacks originality.

Synset Examples

the borrowing of ancient motifs was very apparent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loanword - a word adopted from another language with little or no modification

Etymology

From Old English 'borgian' (to borrow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒrəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of obtaining money or something else of value from someone with the intention of returning it, often with interest
act of obtaining money or resources from a lender

Examples

Households often rely on borrowing to finance major purchases such as cars or homes.
The government's excessive borrowing contributed to the rise in national debt.
Banks have tightened their borrowing criteria in response to financial instability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan - an amount of money that is borrowed, often with interest, and expected to be paid back

Etymology

From the verb 'borrow,' from Old English *borgian* (to borrow), related to *borg* (pledge, security).

Synset POS: n
