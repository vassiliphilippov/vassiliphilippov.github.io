Spizella pusilla

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈzɛlə pjuˈsɪlə / (AmE), / spaɪˈzɛlə pjuˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A small North American finch, also known as the field sparrow, found in brushy pastures and open fields.
North American field sparrow species

Examples

The Spizella pusilla is often heard singing from the tops of small shrubs in open fields.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Spizella pusilla in restored prairie habitats.
Birdwatchers can identify Spizella pusilla by its pink bill and clear, musical song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field sparrow - Common name for Spizella pusilla, a small North American finch

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Spizella' is a diminutive of Greek spiza (a kind of finch), and 'pusilla' means 'small' in Latin.
