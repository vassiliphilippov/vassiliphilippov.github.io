vitreous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪtriəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪəs / (BrE)
relating to, resembling, or made of glass; having a glassy appearance or texture.
glass-like; relating to glass

Examples

The minerals had a vitreous luster, sparkling under the light.
Some volcanic rocks possess a vitreous texture, appearing almost like colored glass.
Laboratory apparatus often requires vitreous materials for durability and chemical resistance.

Synset Examples

vitreous rocks
vitreous silica

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glassy - having a smooth, shiny appearance like glass
  • Degree Lower: ceramic - made of clay and hardened by heat

Etymology

From Latin 'vitreus' (of glass), from 'vitrum' (glass).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪtriəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪtriəs / (BrE)
relating to or constituting the vitreous humor of the eye, the clear gel-like substance filling the space between the lens and the retina
relating to the vitreous humor of the eye
medical

Examples

The doctor noticed an abnormality in his vitreous humor during the eye examination.
Surgery was required to remove blood trapped in the vitreous cavity.
The vitreous body can sometimes detach with age, causing floaters or flashes.

Synset Examples

the vitreous chamber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aqueous - relating to the aqueous humor of the eye

Etymology

From Latin vitreus (of glass), from vitrum (glass); referring to the glassy appearance of the vitreous humor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02836152-a

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɪtriəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪəs / (BrE)
(of ceramics or similar materials) having a glassy, shiny, and nonporous surface, typically produced by fusing a glassy coating to the material.
having a glassy, nonporous surface (esp. in ceramics)

Examples

The artist preferred using vitreous tiles for her mural because they resist staining.
Many high-quality sinks are made from vitreous china, making them both durable and attractive.
The vase's vitreous glaze gave it a smooth, glossy finish that was easy to clean.

Synset Examples

glassy porcelain
hard vitreous china used for plumbing fixtures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glassy - having a smooth, shiny, glass-like appearance

Etymology

From Latin vitreus (of glass), from vitrum (glass).

Synset POS: s
