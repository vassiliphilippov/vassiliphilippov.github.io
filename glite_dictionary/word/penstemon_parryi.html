Penstemon parryi

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəˌmɒn ˈpæriː / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstəməʊn ˈpæriː / (BrE)
A species of perennial flowering plant with erect stems and pinkish-lavender flowers, native to Arizona.
species of flowering plant native to Arizona

Examples

In the desert garden, the Penstemon parryi added vibrant color in early spring.
Pollinators such as hummingbirds are attracted to the pink-lavender flowers of Penstemon parryi.
Many landscapers in Arizona recommend planting Penstemon parryi due to its drought tolerance.

Etymology

Named for Charles C. Parry, American botanist, within the genus Penstemon.
