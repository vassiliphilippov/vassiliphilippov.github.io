pathetic

1. ADJ

/ pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of scornful pity, especially through weakness, sadness, or ineffectiveness
arousing scornful pity

Examples

The team's pathetic performance disappointed even their most loyal fans.
He offered a pathetic excuse that no one believed for a minute.
Her pathetic attempt to apologize only made things worse.

Synset Examples

how silly an ardent and unsuccessful wooer can be especially if he is getting on in years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - arousing pity, especially in a way that evokes contempt
  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt or scorn

Etymology

From Greek *pathētikos* (capable of feeling), from *pathos* (feeling, emotion) via Latin *patheticus* and French *pathétique*.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of pity and contempt, often due to weakness or inadequacy
arousing both pity and contempt

Examples

Their attempt to climb the mountain was pathetic and ended after just an hour.
Many found the broken toy lying by the roadside to be a pathetic sight.
His excuses for arriving late sounded pathetic and insincere.

Synset Examples

their efforts were pathetic
pitiable lack of character
pitiful exhibition of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - arousing pity, sympathy, or compassion
  • Synonym: pitiable - deserving or arousing pity
  • Antonym: admirable - arousing respect or approval

Etymology

From Greek *pathētikos* (capable of feeling), via Latin *patheticus* and French *pathétique*.

3. ADJ

/ pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness
arousing pity or sadness

Examples

The abandoned puppy looked so pathetic that I couldn't help but take it home.
After the storm, their damaged house was in a truly pathetic state.
Her pathetic attempt to fix the car only made it worse.

Synset Examples

his poor distorted limbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiable - arousing pity; deserving sympathy
  • Synonym: pitiful - deserving or arousing pity
  • Synonym: wretched - in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
  • Synonym: pity - the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering of others
  • Antonym: admired - regarded with respect or warm approval (opposite of pathetic in some contexts)

Etymology

From Greek *pathētikos* (sensitive, capable of feeling), from *pathos* (suffering).
