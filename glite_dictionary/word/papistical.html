papistical

1. ADJ

/ pəˈpɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈpɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the Roman Catholic Church, especially in a disparaging or polemical sense
relating to the Roman Catholic Church, often disparaging
archaic

Examples

His writings were filled with papistical slurs against the clergy.
The new laws were denounced by reformers as dangerously papistical.
During the debate, accusations of papistical influence were hurled at the opposition.

Synset Examples

the Roman Catholic Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papist - a person who supports or belongs to the Roman Catholic Church, often disparaging

Etymology

From French *papistique*, from Medieval Latin *papisticus*, from Latin *papa* (pope).
