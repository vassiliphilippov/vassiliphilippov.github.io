Salsola kali

1. NOUN

/ sælˈsoʊlə ˈkeɪlaɪ / (AmE), / salˈsəʊlə ˈkeɪlaɪ / (BrE)
a bushy plant found in Old World salt marshes and sea beaches, with prickly leaves; historically burned to produce soda ash
a prickly plant of salt marshes, source of soda ash

Examples

In coastal regions, Salsola kali often forms dense thickets along the sandy shorelines.
Glassmakers once burned Salsola kali to extract soda ash for their craft.
The prickly leaves of Salsola kali can make it difficult to walk barefoot through salt marshes.

Etymology

From Latin *salsus* (salty) and Greek *kali* (glasswort), referring to its salty habitat and historical use in glassmaking.
