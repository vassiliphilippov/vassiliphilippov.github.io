madder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædər / (AmE), / ˈmadə / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb (Rubia tinctorum) with small yellow flowers and red roots, formerly used as a major source of the red dye alizarin.
Eurasian herb; source of red dye

Examples

The roots of madder were once harvested to produce a brilliant red dye for textiles.
Botanists have studied madder for its historical role in the fabric industry.
Today, synthetic dyes have replaced madder in most commercial applications.

Etymology

From Old English 'mædere', related to Dutch 'meede' and German 'Madder', ultimately of Indo-European origin.
