COBOL

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɒl / (BrE)
a high-level computer programming language designed for business applications, especially for processing large amounts of data
business-oriented programming language

Examples

Many banks still rely on COBOL systems for critical financial transactions.
She learned COBOL as part of her computer science degree.
Updating the old COBOL code was a challenging task for the IT team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fortran - early high-level programming language for scientific computation
  • Antonym: assembly language - low-level programming language for computers

Etymology

Acronym from Common Business-Oriented Language, developed in the late 1950s.
