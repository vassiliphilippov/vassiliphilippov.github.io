pussytoes

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊsiˌtoʊz / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌtəʊz / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial herb of the genus Antennaria, having leaves covered with whitish down and clusters of small white or pinkish flowers
plant with downy leaves and small flowers

Examples

Clusters of pussytoes appeared along the hiking trail in early spring.
The leaves of pussytoes are soft and covered with a white, woolly down.
Gardeners often plant pussytoes for ground cover because they are hardy and attractive.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the flower clusters to a cat's paws; from 'pussy' (cat) and 'toes'.
