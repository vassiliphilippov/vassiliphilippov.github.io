expressionist

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsprɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
an artist who practices or supports expressionism, a movement emphasizing the representation of emotions and subjective perspectives over objective reality
artist practicing expressionism

Examples

Many critics consider Edvard Munch a leading expressionist of the 20th century.
The gallery features works by a celebrated German expressionist.
Her bold colors and distorted figures reveal her roots as an expressionist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressionism - an art movement emphasizing emotional experience over physical reality

Etymology

From 'expressionism' + '-ist', denoting an adherent to a movement or philosophy.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsprɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of expressionism, an artistic style that emphasizes the depiction of emotions or subjective experiences rather than objective reality.
of or relating to expressionism

Examples

The museum hosted an exhibition of expressionist paintings from the early 20th century.
Her work has an expressionist quality, focusing more on inner emotions than on realistic details.
Many expressionist artists sought to evoke moods through bold colors and dramatic brushstrokes.

Synset Examples

expressionist art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressionistic - relating to or characterized by expressionism

Etymology

From 'expressionism' + '-ist', modeled after similar formations in art terminology.
