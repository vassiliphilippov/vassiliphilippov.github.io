Hippocrepis comosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈkrɛpɪs koʊˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkriːpɪs kəˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a European perennial plant with yellow umbel-shaped flowers and flattened seed pods that break into horseshoe-shaped segments
European plant with yellow flowers and horseshoe-shaped pods

Examples

The wildflower meadow was full of Hippocrepis comosa, adding bright splashes of yellow among the grasses.
Gardeners often plant Hippocrepis comosa on chalky soils, where it thrives in sunny, open spaces.
We identified Hippocrepis comosa by its distinctive horseshoe-shaped seed pods and yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'krepis' (shoe), referring to the shape of the seed pod; 'comosa' from Latin 'comosus' (hairy or tufted).
