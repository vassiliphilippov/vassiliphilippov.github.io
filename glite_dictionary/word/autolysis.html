autolysis

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈtɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the destruction or breakdown of plant or animal tissue by the action of its own enzymes, typically after death or in certain diseases
breakdown of tissue by its own enzymes
medical

Examples

After death, autolysis causes the internal organs to break down.
The laboratory noted evidence of autolysis in the tissue samples.
Preservation techniques are needed to prevent autolysis in collected plant specimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysis - the disintegration of a cell by rupture of the cell wall or membrane

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'lysis' (loosening, dissolving).
