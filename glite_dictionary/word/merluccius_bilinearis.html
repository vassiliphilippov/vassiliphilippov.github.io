Merluccius bilinearis

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrˈluːki.əs baɪlɪˈnɛərɪs / (AmE), / mɜːˈlʊkɪ.əs baɪlɪˈneərɪs / (BrE)
A species of fish known as the silver hake, found off the Atlantic coast of North America.
species of fish (silver hake)

Examples

The Merluccius bilinearis is an important commercial species in the fishing industry.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Merluccius bilinearis along the eastern coast of the United States.
The diet of the Merluccius bilinearis includes smaller fish and crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver hake - common name for Merluccius bilinearis, a fish species

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Merluccius' meaning 'hake' and species 'bilinearis' referring to 'two lines'.
