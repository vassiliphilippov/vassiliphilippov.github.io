Henry V

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈfaɪv / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈfɪf / (BrE)
(1387–1422) King of England from 1413 to 1422, renowned for his military victories in the Hundred Years' War, especially at Agincourt.
King of England, 1413–1422

Examples

Henry V led the English army to a decisive victory at the Battle of Agincourt in 1415.
The reign of Henry V is often remembered for its military successes in France.
Many plays and films have depicted the life and character of Henry V.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Henry IV - father of Henry V and King of England (1367–1413)
  • Degree Higher: Henry VI - son of Henry V and King of England (1421–1471)
