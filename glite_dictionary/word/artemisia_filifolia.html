Artemisia filifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə ˌfɪl.ɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə ˌfɪl.ɪˈfəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
A silver-haired shrub native to central and southern United States and Mexico, often considered a troublesome weed on rangelands.
silver-haired shrub native to central/southern US and Mexico

Examples

The ranchers were concerned about the rapid spread of Artemisia filifolia across their pastures.
Scientists study Artemisia filifolia to understand its impact on native grassland ecosystems.
Prescribed burning is sometimes used to control populations of Artemisia filifolia in rangeland management.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Artemisia (possibly from Ancient Greek Ἀρτεμισία, said to be named after the goddess Artemis or Queen Artemisia) + Latin filifolia meaning 'thread-leaved'.
