Chrysolophus

1. NOUN

/ krɪˈsɒləfəs / (AmE), / krɪˈsɒləfəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored pheasants known as golden pheasants, native to forests in China and parts of Asia.
genus of golden pheasants

Examples

The spectacular plumage of the Chrysolophus makes it a popular attraction in aviaries worldwide.
Researchers studied the mating habits of the Chrysolophus in its native habitat.
The Chrysolophus is admired for its vibrant golden and red feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pheasant - a large long-tailed game bird

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'lophos' (crest).
