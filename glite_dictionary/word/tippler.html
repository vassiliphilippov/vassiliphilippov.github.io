tippler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪplər / (AmE), / ˈtɪplə / (BrE)
a person who regularly drinks small amounts of alcohol, especially habitually
habitual drinker of alcohol

Examples

The old tavern was always filled with tipplers sharing stories over pints of beer.
Even though he was a known tippler, he prided himself on never being drunk.
At the party, she was a cheerful tippler, sipping wine throughout the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcohol

Etymology

From the verb 'tipple' (to drink alcohol habitually), origin uncertain but possibly related to obsolete German 'tippen' (to tap).
