Pulicaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːlɪˈkɛəɹiə / (AmE), / ˌpjuːlɪˈkɛːriə / (BrE)
A genus of temperate Old World herbs in the daisy family, commonly known as fleabane.
plant genus; fleabane

Examples

Botanists discovered several new species of Pulicaria in the region.
The dried leaves of Pulicaria are traditionally used as an insect repellent.
Many gardens grow Pulicaria for its bright yellow flowers and medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - common name for various plants of the genus Pulicaria

Etymology

From Latin 'pulex' (flea), referring to its believed ability to repel fleas.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpuːlɪˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌpjuːlɪˈkeəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as fleabanes, native to temperate regions of the Old World.
genus of flowering plants; fleabane

Examples

Several species of Pulicaria are used in traditional herbal medicine across Europe and Asia.
The daisy-like flowers of Pulicaria add color to many dry meadows.
Botanists recently discovered a new Pulicaria species in the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Latin 'pulicaria', from 'pulex, pulicis' meaning 'flea', due to belief that the plant repelled fleas.
