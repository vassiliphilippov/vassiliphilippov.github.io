upheaval

1. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhivəl / (AmE), / ʌpˈhiːvəl / (BrE)
(Geology) A movement in which part of the earth's crust is raised to a higher elevation, especially during the formation of mountains.
geological rising of land

Examples

The Himalayan mountains were formed by a massive upheaval of the earth's crust millions of years ago.
Seismic activity sometimes results in the sudden upheaval of landforms in the region.
Early geologists studied the upheaval of land in order to understand the origins of mountain ranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uplift - the process by which regions of the Earth's crust are raised to higher elevations

Etymology

From 'upheave' + '-al', first appeared in the mid-19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhivəl / (AmE), / ʌpˈhiːv(ə)l / (BrE)
a sudden and violent change or disturbance, especially causing disorder or turmoil
a violent or sudden disturbance

Examples

The revolution caused significant upheaval throughout the country, leaving many citizens in fear.
Economic upheaval can reshape industries and force companies to adapt quickly.
After the merger, the organization faced months of internal upheaval before achieving stability.

Synset Examples

the convulsions of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or absence of disturbance

Etymology

From up + heave, after upheave (to lift up or raise violently).
