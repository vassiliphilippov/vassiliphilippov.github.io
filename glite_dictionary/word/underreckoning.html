underreckoning

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
an estimation that is lower than the true or actual value
an estimate that is too low

Examples

A consistent underreckoning of production costs led to financial shortfalls.
Historians believe there has been an underreckoning of the population figures in early census records.
The underreckoning of risks contributed to the company's unexpected losses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimation - an estimation that is higher than the true value
  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being less than the actual value

Etymology

From 'under-' (meaning 'too little') + 'reckoning' (from Old English 'gerecenian', to explain, relate, calculate).
