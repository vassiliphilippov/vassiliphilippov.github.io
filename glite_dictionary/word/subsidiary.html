subsidiary

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪdiˌɛri / (AmE), / səbˈsɪdiːəri / (BrE)
a company that is completely or partly owned and controlled by another company
company controlled by another

Examples

The multinational firm owns several subsidiaries around the world.
Our company became a wholly owned subsidiary after the takeover.
Each subsidiary has its own management team and business operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parent company - a company that controls one or more subsidiaries

Etymology

From Latin subsidiarius (serving to aid or support), from subsidium (support).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪd.iˌɛri / (AmE), / səbˈsɪd.i.əri / (BrE)
a person or thing that is subordinate to or controlled by another, especially an assistant or secondary company under the control of a parent company
a subordinate person or organization

Examples

The regional office acts as a subsidiary to the main headquarters, following instructions from the top management.
She was a trusted subsidiary to the director throughout the project.
Many multinational corporations own several subsidiaries around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps or aids another
  • Antonym: superior - a person higher in rank or authority

Etymology

From Latin subsidium (help, support), via late Latin subsidiarius (serving to assist or supplement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10689430-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ səbˈsɪdiˌɛri / (AmE), / səbˈsɪdiəri / (BrE)
serving to assist or support; functioning in a subordinate or supplementary role to something more important
supporting or supplementary

Examples

The company established a subsidiary branch to handle customer service operations.
Most universities have subsidiary organizations that support student life.
The research project received support from several subsidiary institutions.

Synset Examples

the main library and its auxiliary branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - providing additional help or support
  • Antonym: primary - of chief importance; principal

Etymology

From Latin 'subsidiarius', from 'subsidium' (support, assistance).
