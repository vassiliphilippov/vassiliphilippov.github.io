microcytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəʊsɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a blood disorder characterized by the presence of abnormally small red blood cells, often associated with anemia
presence of small red blood cells in blood
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her anemia was caused by microcytosis.
A blood test revealed significant microcytosis in the patient.
Microcytosis is often seen in certain types of iron deficiency anemia.

Etymology

From 'micro-' (small) and Greek 'kytos' (cell) + '-osis' (condition).
