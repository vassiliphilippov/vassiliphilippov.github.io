Maffia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
a secret criminal organization of Sicilian origin, historically opposed to tyranny but later involved in organized crime
Sicilian criminal organization

Examples

The Sicilian Maffia has had a profound influence on the island's history and politics.
Several films and novels depict the rise of the Maffia in the 19th and 20th centuries.
Law enforcement agencies have struggled to dismantle the Maffia's powerful networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mafia - An Italian-American and Sicilian secret criminal organization

Etymology

From Italian 'maffia', referring to the Sicilian organization; see also 'Mafia'.
