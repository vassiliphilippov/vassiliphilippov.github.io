Aneides

1. NOUN

/ əˈnaɪ.əˌdiːz / (AmE), / əˈniːɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small climbing salamanders native to North and Central America, known for their climbing ability and slender bodies.
genus of climbing salamanders

Examples

Researchers have discovered a new species in the Aneides genus in the Appalachian Mountains.
The Aneides exhibit specialized toe pads that enable them to climb trees and rocks.
Conservationists are studying Aneides habitats to better understand their ecological needs.

Etymology

From Greek 'aneidos', meaning 'without form', as given in scientific naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ əˈniː.ɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˈniː.ɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of climbing salamanders found primarily in North America, known for their climbing abilities and distinctive toe pads.
a genus of climbing salamanders

Examples

The Aneides genus contains several species of salamanders specialized for life in trees and on cliffs.
Researchers observed a rare Aneides in the moss-covered forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Members of Aneides are easily identified by their square toe tips, adapted for climbing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian typically with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed irregularly from Greek an- 'not' and eidos 'form, shape'.
