Leo

1. PROPN

/ ˈliː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈliː.əʊ / (BrE)
the fifth sign of the zodiac, said to cover those born between about July 23 and August 22
fifth sign of the zodiac

Examples

My birthday falls under Leo, so people say I'm confident and outgoing.
She checks her horoscope daily because she's a Leo and loves astrology.
According to astrologers, Leo is a fire sign known for creativity and leadership.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cancer - the fourth sign of the zodiac
  • Degree Higher: Virgo - the sixth sign of the zodiac

Etymology

From Latin 'Leo' meaning 'lion', used as the name of the zodiac constellation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈliː.əʊ / (BrE)
a prominent zodiac constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, situated between Cancer and Virgo; also the astrological sign associated with people born between July 23 and August 22.
zodiac constellation between Cancer and Virgo

Examples

Many amateur astronomers enjoy viewing Leo in the night sky during spring.
The star Regulus marks the heart of the constellation Leo.
People born under Leo are said to be confident and outgoing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cancer - a zodiacal constellation preceding Leo
  • Degree Higher: Virgo - a zodiacal constellation following Leo

Etymology

From Latin 'Leo' meaning 'lion', named for its resemblance to a lion in the sky.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ˈliː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈliː.əʊ / (BrE)

person born under Leo (astrology)

Examples

As a Leo, she is known for her confidence and leadership skills.
Many people believe that being a Leo means you are energetic and outgoing.
My best friend, a Leo, always seems to take charge in any group.

  • Degree Higher: Virgo - person born under the sign Virgo (astrology)
