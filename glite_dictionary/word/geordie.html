Geordie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔr.di / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔː.di / (BrE)
A person who is a native or inhabitant of Newcastle-upon-Tyne in northeast England.
native of Newcastle-upon-Tyne
british

Examples

Many Geordie football fans travel to matches across the country to support their team.
Her accent revealed that she was a true Geordie from Newcastle.
As a proud Geordie, he always enjoys returning home for the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newcastle-upon-Tyne - city in northeast England; place from which Geordies come

Etymology

Originated in the 18th century, possibly from the common use of the name George in the area or as a diminutive of 'George'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔr.di / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔː.di / (BrE)
the nonstandard dialect spoken by natives of Newcastle-upon-Tyne and surrounding areas in northeast England
dialect spoken in Newcastle-upon-Tyne area

Examples

She speaks fluent Geordie after living in Newcastle for years.
Many people find the Geordie accent charming and distinctive.
It can be difficult for outsiders to understand strong Geordie dialect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a regional or social variety of a language

Etymology

Originally a diminutive form of the name George; later used for people and dialect of Tyneside, England.
