misty

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪsti / (AmE), / ˈmɪsti / (BrE)
full of, or characterized by, mist or fog; obscure or indistinct due to mist.
full of mist or fog

Examples

We walked through the misty forest at dawn, barely able to see the path ahead.
On a misty morning, the mountains in the distance disappeared from view.
Her memories of childhood were misty, fading around the edges like an old photograph.

Synset Examples

a brumous October morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foggy - filled with or resembling fog

Etymology

From Middle English 'misti', from 'mist' + '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪsti / (AmE), / ˈmɪsti / (BrE)
full of or covered with mist; characterized by a cloud of tiny water droplets in the air, making it difficult to see clearly
filled with or covered by mist

Examples

The garden looked especially misty at dawn after the night rain.
We took a long walk along the misty riverbank in early spring.
Her memories of childhood are as misty as the distant hills on a humid morning.

Synset Examples

the misty evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foggy - full of or resembling fog; obscured by fog
  • Antonym: clear - free from mist, fog, or cloud; easy to see through

Etymology

From Middle English misti, from mist (thin fog) + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02560339-s
Synset POS: s
