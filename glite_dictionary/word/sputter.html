sputter

1. NOUN

/ ˈspʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspʌtə / (BrE)
an utterance or series of words spoken with spitting or popping sounds, often due to anger or excitement
spitting or popping speech (often in anger)

Examples

His angry sputter echoed across the room as he struggled to speak clearly.
The judge listened patiently to the defendant's emotional sputter of protest.
She answered his accusation with a sputter of indignation and disbelief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - speak with involuntary breaks and pauses
  • Synonym: mumble - speak in a low, indistinct way

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound; related to Dutch 'sputteren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07144891-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈspʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈspʌtə / (BrE)
To spit or eject something with explosive or popping sounds, often uncontrollably or irregularly.
spit explosively; eject with popping sounds

Examples

The candle flame began to sputter as the wind blew through the open window.
She coughed and sputtered after accidentally swallowing some water.
The old car sputtered to a halt on the side of the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to force saliva or another substance out of the mouth

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound made by spitting or popping.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00102057-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈspʌtər / (AmE), / ˈspʌtə / (BrE)
A series of explosive or spattering sounds, often made by an engine, fire, or similar source.
spattering or explosive noise

Examples

The sputter of the old car's engine indicated it was struggling to start.
We heard the sputter of fireworks echoing across the lake at night.
A quiet sputter came from the candle as it finally went out.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - a series of short, sharp sounds typically made by liquid hitting a surface

Etymology

Imitative origin, from frequentative of 'spit' in the sense of emitting drops or particles.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ˈspʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspʌtə / (BrE)
to subject (a material or surface) to a process in which atoms are ejected, typically by bombardment with energetic particles
remove atoms from a surface via energetic bombardment

Examples

Certain techniques sputter thin films onto microchips for electronic manufacturing.
Solar wind can sputter atoms off the surfaces of airless planetary bodies.
