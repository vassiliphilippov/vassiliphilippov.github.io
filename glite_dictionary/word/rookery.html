rookery

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʊkəri / (AmE), / ˈrʊkəri / (BrE)
A place where large numbers of birds, especially rooks or other gregarious species, nest and breed together.
breeding site for colonial birds

Examples

The coastal cliffs are home to a bustling rookery of seabirds during the spring.
We watched hundreds of herons return to their rookery at sunset.
The wildlife biologists visited the rookery to count the number of nesting pairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a group of the same species of animals living closely together

Etymology

From 'rook' (a type of bird) + '-ery' (denoting a place for), first recorded in the late 16th century.
