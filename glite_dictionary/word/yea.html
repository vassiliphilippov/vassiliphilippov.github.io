yea

1. ADV

/ jeɪ / (AmE), / jeɪ / (BrE)
(Archaic) Used to introduce an emphatic affirmation, meaning 'not only so, but'.
emphatic archaic affirmation
archaic

Examples

He was wise, yea, he was the wisest of them all.
I will go, yea, I will lead the way myself.
She spoke the truth, yea, she declared it openly before all.

Synset Examples

I therein do rejoice, yea, and will rejoice

Etymology

From Old English 'gēa' (yes, indeed).

2. NOUN

/ jeɪ / (AmE), / jeɪ / (BrE)
An affirmative vote or answer, especially in a formal or legislative context.
affirmative vote (yes)

Examples

All those in favor say yea.
The committee recorded seven yeas and two nays.
The bill passed by a margin of five yeas to three nays.

Synset Examples

The yeas have it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nay - a negative vote (no), especially in formal contexts
  • Synonym: yes - affirmative answer, agreement

Etymology

From Old English gēa, meaning 'yes'.

3. ADV

/ jeɪ / (AmE), / jeɪ / (BrE)
(Archaic) Used to introduce a statement that expands or strengthens what has just been said; not only so, but indeed.
(archaic) introduces an emphatic addition
archaic

Examples

He promised to help, yea, to devote all his resources if necessary.
They rejoiced in their victory, yea, they celebrated for days afterward.
She was grateful for his kindness, yea, she was moved to tears by it.

Synset Examples

I therein do rejoice, yea, and will rejoice

Etymology

From Old English géa (yes, so, verily). Used as an emphatic particle since Middle English.

Source: WordNet
