atrium dextrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əm ˈdɛk.strəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əm ˈdɛk.strəm / (BrE)
the right upper chamber of the heart that receives blood from the venae cavae and coronary sinus
right upper chamber of the heart
medical

Examples

Blood returning from the body flows into the atrium dextrum before passing into the right ventricle.
A blockage in the atrium dextrum can lead to serious circulatory problems.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's atrium dextrum during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrium - upper chamber of the heart that receives blood
  • Antonym: atrium sinister - the left upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (entrance hall, chamber) and 'dextrum' (right).
