Åland Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːlɑnd ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɔːland ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
an archipelago of around 6,000 islands in the Gulf of Bothnia under Finnish administration, located between Finland and Sweden
Baltic archipelago under Finnish control

Examples

The Åland Islands are famous for their scenic beauty and unique cultural heritage.
Finland administers the Åland Islands, granting them a high degree of autonomy.
Many tourists visit the Åland Islands during the summer months for cycling and boating.

Etymology

From Swedish "Åland", possibly from Old Norse "Ahvaland" (land of water) or "aland" (land of rivers).
