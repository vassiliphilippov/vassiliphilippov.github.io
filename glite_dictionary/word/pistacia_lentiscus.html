Pistacia lentiscus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsteɪʃə lɛnˈtɪskəs / (AmE), / pɪˈstaːsɪə lɛnˈtɪskəs / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of the Mediterranean region, commonly known as the mastic tree, cultivated for its aromatic resin
Mediterranean resin-producing shrub

Examples

The Pistacia lentiscus shrub is valued for producing mastic resin used in traditional Greek cuisine.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Pistacia lentiscus leaves and resin.
In the Mediterranean, Pistacia lentiscus is often grown for ornamental purposes as well as resin harvesting.

Etymology

Modern Latin: from genus name Pistacia (from Greek pistakē, pistachio tree) and lentiscus (Latin for mastic tree).
