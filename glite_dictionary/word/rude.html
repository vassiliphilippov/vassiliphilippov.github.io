rude

1. ADJ

/ ruːd / (AmE), / ruːd / (BrE)
displaying a lack of manners or consideration for others; socially incorrect in behavior
impolite; lacking social manners

Examples

It was very rude of him to interrupt their conversation without asking.
She found the waiter's rude behavior unacceptable and asked to speak to the manager.
His rude remarks offended several people at the dinner party.

Synset Examples

resentment flared at such an unmannered intrusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impolite - not having or showing good manners; rude
  • Antonym: courteous - polite and showing respect for others

Etymology

From Latin 'rudis' (unwrought, unrefined).

2. ADJ

/ ruːd / (AmE), / ruːd / (BrE)
(of a person or their manners) lacking in refinement, sophistication, or grace; uncultured or unpolished.
lacking refinement or grace

Examples

His rude behavior at the formal dinner shocked everyone present.
The rude countryman struggled to adapt to the elegant city life.
Her rude remarks betrayed her lack of social grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
  • Antonym: refined - polite, cultured, and having good manners

Etymology

From Old French 'rude', from Latin 'rudis' (unwrought, rough, uncultivated).

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ruːd / (AmE), / ruːd / (BrE)
not having or showing good manners; impolite or discourteous.
impolite; lacking good manners

Examples

It was very rude of him to interrupt the conversation.
She found his comments unexpectedly rude and left the party early.
Please do not be rude to the staff, even if you are frustrated.

Synset Examples

want nothing from you but to get away from your uncivil tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impolite - not having or showing good manners; rude
  • Antonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect
  • Synonym: discourteous - showing rudeness and a lack of consideration for other people

Etymology

From Latin 'rudis' (unwrought, unrefined).

4. ADJ

/ ruːd / (AmE), / ruːd / (BrE)
existing at an early stage of technical or social development; simple or crude in form, construction, or function.
primitive or crude in development
archaic

Examples

Early settlers built their homes with rude tools and basic materials found in the forest.
Archaeologists discovered rude pottery shards that dated back thousands of years.
The mountain villagers survived with rude shelters and little access to modern conveniences.

Synset Examples

the crude weapons and rude agricultural implements of early man
primitive movies of the 1890s
primitive living conditions in the Appalachian mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude - in a natural or raw state; not yet processed or refined
  • Synonym: primitive - relating to the earliest stage of development or existence

Etymology

From Latin rudis (unwrought, crude, rough).
