kok-sagyz

1. NOUN

/ koʊkˈsɑːɡɪz / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɑːɡɪz / (BrE)
A perennial dandelion native to Kazakhstan, cultivated for its fleshy roots that have a high rubber content.
rubber-producing dandelion from Kazakhstan

Examples

During World War II, kok-sagyz was cultivated as an alternative source of natural rubber.
Scientists are studying kok-sagyz for its potential in sustainable rubber production.
The fields of kok-sagyz bloom with yellow flowers in late spring.

Etymology

From Kazakh 'көксағыз' (kök-sagyz), meaning 'blue rubber'.
