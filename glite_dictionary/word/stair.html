stair

1. NOUN

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
a flat, narrow part of a set of steps on which you put your foot when going up or down; a step in a stairway
step in a set of stairs

Examples

She sat on the top stair, tying her shoes before work.
A child dropped his toy, and it rolled beneath the bottom stair.
He wiped each stair carefully as he cleaned the staircase.

Synset Examples

he paused on the bottom step

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step - a flat surface for placing the foot, especially on a staircase
  • Degree Higher: staircase - a set of stairs inside a building

Etymology

From Old French *estre*, from Latin *scalae* (steps), related to 'staircase'.
