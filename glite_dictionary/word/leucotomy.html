leucotomy

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / luːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the cutting or severing of nerve fibers in the brain's frontal lobes, historically used to treat certain mental disorders
surgical cutting of brain nerve tracts
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist discussed the history and consequences of leucotomy as a treatment for severe mental illness.
After undergoing a leucotomy, the patient exhibited significant changes in personality and behavior.
The use of leucotomy has been widely criticized due to its irreversible effects on the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobotomy - a surgical operation involving incision into the prefrontal lobe of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'leuko-' (white) + '-tomy' (cutting), referring to cutting in the white matter of the brain.
