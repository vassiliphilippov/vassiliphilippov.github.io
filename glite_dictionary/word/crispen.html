to crispen

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪspən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪspən / (BrE)
to make (something, especially food) brown and crisp by heating it
make food brown and crisp by heating

Examples

She likes to crispen her bread in the oven for extra crunch.
The chef knows how to crispen potatoes perfectly every time.
Bake the chicken uncovered to crispen the skin.

Synset Examples

toast bread
crisp potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - to become crisp or to make something crisp
  • Synonym: toast - to brown food by exposure to heat
  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become less hard

Etymology

Formed from 'crisp' + '-en', a verb-forming suffix meaning 'to make or become'.
