two-fold

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuːˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtuːfəʊld / (BrE)
twice as great or as many; double in amount or degree.
twice as great; double

Examples

The company reported a two-fold increase in sales over the last year.
Her risk of heart disease was two-fold compared to people without the condition.
A two-fold improvement in efficiency was achieved after the process was redesigned.

Synset Examples

ate a double portion
the dose is doubled
a twofold increase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - twice as much in size, amount, number, or degree

Etymology

From Old English 'twīfeald', from 'twī-' (two) + 'feald' (fold).
