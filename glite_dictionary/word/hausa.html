Hausa

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsə / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsə / (BrE)
a member of a large West African ethnic group chiefly found in northern Nigeria and adjacent regions
a member of a West African ethnic group

Examples

The Hausa are known for their rich cultural traditions in northern Nigeria.
Many Hausa people speak both the Hausa language and English fluently.
She learned about Hausa customs while traveling through West Africa.

Etymology

From Hausa *Hausawa*, the self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsə / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsə / (BrE)
A Chadic language of the Afroasiatic family, spoken mainly in northern Nigeria and Niger and widely used as a trade language in West Africa.
major language of West Africa

Examples

Many merchants communicate in Hausa to conduct business across West Africa.
He is fluent in English, French, and Hausa.
Hausa is sometimes taught in schools as a regional language of importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hausa - a member of the Hausa ethnic group

Etymology

From Hausa 'Hausā', the name of the people and language.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˈhaʊ.sə / (BrE)
A member of a predominantly Muslim ethnic group living mainly in northern Nigeria and neighboring countries.
member of a northern Nigerian ethnic group

Examples

Many Hausa people are known for their rich cultural traditions and vibrant markets.
She studied the language and customs of the Hausa during her time in Nigeria.
The Hausa have a significant influence on the art and music of West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fulani - a member of another major West African ethnic group

Etymology

From Hausa (language and ethnic group's own name for themselves).

Source: WordNet
