garish

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛːrɪʃ / (BrE)
unpleasantly bright, showy, or elaborate in a way that is tasteless or excessive
excessively bright or showy; tasteless

Examples

The living room was decorated in garish colors that hurt my eyes.
Her garish makeup drew attention for all the wrong reasons.
I found the neon lights outside the restaurant a bit too garish for my taste.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy; understated

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'gare' (to stare) + -ish.
