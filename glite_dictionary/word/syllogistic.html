syllogistic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪləˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.əˈdʒɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
a system or method of reasoning in formal logic based on syllogisms, especially as developed by Aristotle; the branch of logic dealing with syllogisms.
system of reasoning using syllogisms

Examples

He wrote an entire book on the principles of syllogistic and its applications to classical logic.
The study of syllogistic was central to medieval philosophical education.
Although modern logic has moved beyond Aristotelian syllogistic, it remains an important foundation.

Synset Examples

Aristotelian syllogistic became known as ‘categorical syllogistic’ and the Peripatetic adaptation of Stoic syllogistic as ‘hypothetical syllogistic’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syllogism - a form of reasoning with two premises and a conclusion

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syllogisticus, from Greek syllogistikos (able to infer or reason).
