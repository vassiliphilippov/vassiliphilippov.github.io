aniseikonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪsaɪˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌænɪsaɪˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A visual defect in which the shape and size of the image seen by each eye are different, causing difficulties with binocular vision.
visual defect with unequal images in eyes
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with aniseikonia after noting discrepancies in her vision.
Aniseikonia can make activities like reading and driving particularly challenging due to inconsistent image perception.
Special corrective lenses are often prescribed to help people suffering from aniseikonia.

Etymology

From Greek 'anis-' (not equal) + 'eikōn' (image) + '-ia' (condition).
