goldenbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldənˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldənˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
Any of various much-branched yellow-flowered shrubs of the genus Chrysothamnus, native to western North America.
yellow-flowered shrub of genus Chrysothamnus

Examples

The goldenbush brightened the arid landscape with its vivid yellow flowers.
We spotted a cluster of goldenbush growing along the hiking trail.
Botanists study the adaptation of goldenbush to the dry climates of western North America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldənˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldənbʊʃ / (BrE)
A North American shrub of the genus Haplopappus, having yellow flowers and typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.
yellow-flowered shrub of genus Haplopappus

Examples

The goldenbush blooms brilliantly in the late summer across the desert landscape.
Several species of goldenbush are prized for their ability to attract butterflies.
Botanists studied how the goldenbush adapts to drought conditions in its native environment.
