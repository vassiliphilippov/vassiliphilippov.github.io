sou'-east

1. ADV

/ saʊˈist / (AmE), / saʊˈiːst / (BrE)
To, toward, or in the direction of the southeast.
toward the southeast

Examples

The ship sailed sou'-east for several hours before changing course.
The wind was blowing sou'-east, making the sea rougher than expected.
He pointed sou'-east to indicate where they should head next.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southeast - toward the southeast direction

Etymology

A contracted form of 'southeast,' with dropped internal letters and an apostrophe; common in nautical usage.

2. NOUN

/ saʊˈist / (AmE), / saʊˈiːst / (BrE)
the compass point that lies midway between south and east, at an angle of 135 degrees from north.
compass direction between south and east

Examples

The wind shifted suddenly from sou'-east to due south overnight.
We sailed towards the sou'-east, keeping the rising sun on our left.
The storm was approaching from the sou'-east, bringing heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southeast - the direction halfway between south and east, at 135 degrees

Etymology

A contraction of 'southeast,' formed by elision.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
