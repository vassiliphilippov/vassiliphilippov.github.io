Richard Roe

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd roʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd rəʊ / (BrE)
A fictitious name used for an anonymous or unnamed male, especially as a defendant in legal proceedings.
placeholder name in legal contexts
legal

Examples

The lawsuit listed Richard Roe as the defendant to protect the real person's identity.
Legal texts sometimes use Richard Roe when the actual name of a party is unknown.
The court document referred to both John Doe and Richard Roe as hypothetical participants.

Synset Examples

They used Richard Roe in the mock trial for anonymity.
In legal documents, the plaintiff is often referred to as Richard Roe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Doe - fictitious name for an anonymous male, especially as a legal plaintiff

Etymology

Modeled after the older use of 'John Doe' in English legal practice; 'Roe' chosen as a generic surname.
