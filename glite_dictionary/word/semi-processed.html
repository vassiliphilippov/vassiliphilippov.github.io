semi-processed

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈprɑsɛst / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈprəʊsɛst / (BrE)
Partially processed; having undergone some but not all steps required to reach a finished state.
partially processed

Examples

The factory receives semi-processed materials and turns them into finished products.
Many foods on supermarket shelves are semi-processed to extend shelf life while retaining nutrients.
They imported semi-processed wool for further refinement and weaving in their own mill.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: unprocessed - not having undergone any processing
  • Degree Higher: processed - having completed the full processing procedure

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix 'semi-' (meaning half or partially) with 'processed'.
