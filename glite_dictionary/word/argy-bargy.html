argy-bargy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.dʒiˈbɑr.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɑː.dʒiˈbɑː.dʒi / (BrE)
a lively discussion or heated argument, often involving disagreement or wrangling
a heated argument or dispute
informal british

Examples

There was a bit of argy-bargy in the meeting over the new proposal.
Political debates often descend into argy-bargy between the candidates.
The neighbours got into an argy-bargy about where to put the rubbish bins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row - a noisy argument or fight
  • Synonym: dispute - an argument or disagreement, especially an official one
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion

Etymology

Reduplicative formation from 'argue' or 'argument'. Chiefly British English.
