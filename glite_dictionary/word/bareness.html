bareness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɛːnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having very little or no decoration, furnishings, or ornamentation
state of being undecorated or empty

Examples

The bareness of the room made it feel cold and unwelcoming.
He admired the bareness of the landscape, untouched by human hands.
The painting's bareness draws the viewer's attention to each simple detail.

Synset Examples

I was struck by the starkness of my father's room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starkness - the quality of being severe or bare in appearance

Etymology

From bare + -ness, indicating the state or quality of being bare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈber.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbeə.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being bleak, empty, or without ornamentation, creating a desolate atmosphere
state of being bleak or empty

Examples

The bareness of the winter trees gave the landscape a haunting, deserted look.
There was a striking bareness in the newly constructed room, with not a single piece of furniture.
The bareness of the mountain top left hikers exposed to the harsh winds.

Synset Examples

the nakedness of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleakness - the quality of being cold, empty, and unpleasant
  • Synonym: nakedness - the state of being untouched, exposed or lacking covering or adornment

Etymology

From bare + -ness, forming a noun from the adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14549150-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛərnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɛənɪs / (BrE)
the state of being unclothed or exposed, especially of a part of the body
state of being unclothed or exposed

Examples

The bareness of his feet made him shiver on the cold floor.
She was embarrassed by the sudden bareness of her arms when her sleeves were ripped.
The artist captured the bareness of the human form in his sketches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudity - the state of being naked; without clothes
  • Antonym: clothed - covered with clothes

Etymology

From 'bare' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being bare.
