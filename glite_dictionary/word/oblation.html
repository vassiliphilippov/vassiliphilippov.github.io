oblation

1. NOUN

/ əˈbleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɒˈbleɪʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian liturgy) the act of offering bread and wine during the Eucharist as a religious sacrifice or gift.
offering of bread and wine in the Eucharist

Examples

During the Mass, the priest performed the oblation with solemn reverence.
The congregation witnessed the oblation of the bread and wine at the altar.
She explained the significance of the oblation in the context of the Eucharist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offering - something presented as a gift or sacrifice, especially in a religious context

Etymology

From Latin oblatio (an offering), from offerre (to offer).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈbleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of offering money or gifts, especially to a church or charity
offering to church or charity

Examples

Every Sunday, parishioners present their oblation during the church service.
The festival included an oblation for the benefit of local charities.
She made a generous oblation to support the restoration of the historic chapel.

Synset Examples

oblations for aid to the poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offering - something given in worship or charity
  • Synonym: donation - a gift, typically of money, to a cause or charity

Etymology

From Latin oblatio (offering), from offerre (to offer).
