Setaria glauca

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛəri.ə ˈɡlɔːkə / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛːrɪə ˈɡlaʊkə / (BrE)
A species of common, bristly grass, also called yellow foxtail, found extensively in temperate regions and often considered a weed.
a common weedy grass (yellow foxtail)

Examples

The field was overgrown with Setaria glauca, making harvest difficult.
Farmers often regard Setaria glauca as a troublesome weed in their crops.
After the rain, clusters of Setaria glauca appeared along the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow foxtail - common name for Setaria glauca, a widespread weedy grass

Etymology

From Latin: Setaria (from seta, 'bristle') and glauca ('bluish-green, grayish').
