dial

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the flat, graduated face of a clock or watch on which the time is shown by hands or other means
face of a clock or watch

Examples

She glanced at the dial and realized she was late for her meeting.
The antique clock's dial was beautifully decorated with gold accents.
The luminous dial allows you to read the time even in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - the surface of a clock or watch that displays the time

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *dialis* (daily), originally referring to sundials.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03191380-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəl / (BrE)
A disc on a telephone, usually marked with numbers, that is rotated a set distance to select each digit when making a call.
rotating numbered disc on a telephone

Examples

He slowly turned the dial to enter the phone number.
Old telephones have a circular dial instead of buttons.
My grandmother's house still has a phone with a rotary dial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: keypad - a set of buttons for entering numbers on a telephone

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dialis (daily), then used for sundial; later applied to clock and telephone faces.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03191502-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəl / (BrE)
A circular graduated display on a measuring instrument, used to show values such as speed, pressure, or time.
circular indicator on instruments

Examples

The speedometer dial was clearly visible behind the steering wheel.
She adjusted the pressure using the gauge’s dial until it reached the correct level.
The old radio had a large round dial to select stations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - instrument for measuring and displaying a value

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *dialis* (daily), originally referring to a sundial.

4. VERB

/ ˈdaɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əl / (BrE)
to operate a telephone by turning a dial or pressing buttons to enter a telephone number
call by entering a telephone number

Examples

He picked up the phone and carefully dialed the emergency number.
Please dial your friend's number for me while I find a pen.
It used to take much longer to dial a number on a rotary phone.

Synset Examples

You must take the receiver off the hook before you dial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to make a telephone call

Etymology

From earlier noun 'dial', from Latin 'dialis' (daily), originally referring to the face of a sundial.
