musquash

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kwɒʃ / (BrE)
A large semiaquatic rodent native to North America, similar to a beaver, with dark glossy brown fur.
semiaquatic North American rodent

Examples

We spotted a musquash building its den along the riverbank.
The musquash is often mistaken for a small beaver due to its appearance.
Trappers once valued musquash fur for its durability and glossy sheen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskrat - a large aquatic rodent of North America, also called musquash

Etymology

From Algonquian (Abenaki) 'muscascus' meaning 'it is red' or 'muskrat.'
