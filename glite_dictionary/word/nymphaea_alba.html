Nymphaea alba

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə ˈælbə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə ˈalbə / (BrE)
A species of water lily with fragrant white flowers, native to Europe and parts of North Africa and Asia.
a white-flowered water lily species

Examples

The pond was covered with the broad leaves and white blooms of Nymphaea alba.
Botanists study Nymphaea alba to understand wetland ecosystems better.
Nymphaea alba is often found in European lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - a plant with large floating leaves and showy flowers, grows in water

Etymology

From Latin: 'Nymphaea' (water lily), from Greek 'nymphaiā', and 'alba' (white), referring to the color of the flowers.
