sextet

1. NOUN

/ sɛkˈstɛt / (AmE), / sɛkˈstɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition written to be performed by six musicians or singers
composition for six performers

Examples

The string sextet performed an original piece at the festival.
Brahms composed a famous sextet for strings in the 19th century.
Their ensemble specializes in jazz sextets and other chamber music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quintet - a musical composition for five performers
  • Degree Higher: septet - a musical composition for seven performers

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', from 'sesto' meaning 'sixth', from Latin 'sextus' (sixth).

2. NOUN

/ sɛkˈstɛt / (AmE), / sɛkˈstɛt / (BrE)
A group of six musicians or singers performing together.
group of six musicians or singers

Examples

The jazz sextet played an energetic set at the club last night.
She joined a vocal sextet that performs at weddings and events.
Beethoven's String sextet is a well-known piece in chamber music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - a group of four musicians or singers performing together
  • Degree Higher: octet - a group of eight musicians or singers performing together

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', derived from 'sesto' (sixth), from Latin 'sextus' (sixth).
