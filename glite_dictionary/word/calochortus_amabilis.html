Calochortus amabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈkɔrtəs əˈmæbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɔːtəs əˈmabɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of globe lily with open branched clusters of clear yellow, egg-shaped flowers, native to northern California.
yellow-flowered globe lily of northern California

Examples

The bright yellow blooms of the Calochortus amabilis light up the California hillsides in spring.
Gardeners in northern California often plant Calochortus amabilis for its distinctive egg-shaped flowers.
Botanists consider Calochortus amabilis to be an important native wildflower in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe lily - common name for Calochortus species with globe-shaped flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Calochortus (from Greek 'kalos' meaning beautiful + 'chortos' meaning grass) and Latin 'amabilis' meaning lovely.
