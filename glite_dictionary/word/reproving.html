reproving

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpruvɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈpruːvɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing or conveying disapproval or censure, especially with the intention of correcting someone
expressing disapproval or correction

Examples

Her teacher gave her a reproving look when she started talking during the lesson.
He raised a reproving finger, warning the children to behave.
With a reproving tone, she reminded him to say thank you.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approving - showing support or agreement
  • Synonym: scolding - speaking angrily as a form of correction

Etymology

From the verb 'reprove', from Old French 'reprover', from Late Latin 'reprobare' (to disapprove).
