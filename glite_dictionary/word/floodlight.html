floodlight

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌdˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌdˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to illuminate an area or object with floodlights
illuminate with floodlights

Examples

After sunset, the stadium was floodlighted for the championship game.
They plan to floodlight the monument for the evening ceremony.
Workers floodlighted the construction site to allow for overnight repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illuminate - to light up something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293009-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌdˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a very bright artificial light with a broad beam, used to illuminate a large area, especially in photography, film, sports, or outdoor events
powerful light with a broad beam

Examples

The stadium was illuminated by powerful floodlights during the night game.
The photographer set up a floodlight to brighten the background for the portrait session.
They replaced the old floodlight with a more energy-efficient LED version.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spotlight - narrow-beamed intense light for illuminating a small area

Etymology

Formed from 'flood' (to fill) + 'light'.
