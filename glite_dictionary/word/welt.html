welt

1. NOUN

/ wɛlt / (AmE), / wɛlt / (BrE)
a strip of material sewn into a seam, especially in shoes or garments, to reinforce or decorate the edge
a reinforced seam or strip in shoes or clothing

Examples

The shoe's welt was stitched carefully to ensure durability.
A decorative welt ran along the edge of the cushion.
The craftsman explained how the welt strengthens the seam of the boot.

Etymology

From Middle Low German *welte* (rolled-up strip of cloth or leather).

2. NOUN

/ wɛlt / (AmE), / wɛlt / (BrE)
a raised, red mark on the skin, usually caused by a blow, pressure, or an allergic reaction
raised mark on the skin

Examples

The leather strap left a long welt across his arm.
She broke out in a welt after touching the plant.
A red welt formed where the insect had bitten him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weal - a red, swollen mark left on flesh by a blow or pressure
  • Degree Lower: bruise - an injury appearing as a purple or dark mark on the skin

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *welle* (ridge, roll).

3. VERB

/ wɛlt / (AmE), / wɛlt / (BrE)
To attach a strip of leather or other material (a welt) to the edge of a shoe as part of the making or repair process.
attach a welt to (shoes)

Examples

The cobbler carefully welted the new pair of leather shoes.
Traditionally, artisans have welted boots by hand for extra durability.
Learning how to welt a shoe is an important step in shoemaking courses.

Synset Examples

welt the shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - To sew or join with thread or a needle, related in shoemaking context

Etymology

From Middle Low German *welte* (ridge of earth; strip of leather).

4. VERB

/ wɛlt / (AmE), / wɛlt / (BrE)
to strike someone hard enough to raise a ridge or mark on the skin, especially with a whip or rod
to strike and raise a mark on the skin

Examples

The coach threatened to welt the boys if they misbehaved again.
He welted the carpet with a stick, raising clouds of dust.
She claimed the guard welted her after she tried to escape.

Synset Examples

The teacher often flogged the students
The children were severely trounced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flog - to beat severely with a whip or rod
  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a whip as punishment
  • Antonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently

Related to the noun 'welt' (a raised mark on the skin), from Middle Low German 'welte' (ridge, strip).
