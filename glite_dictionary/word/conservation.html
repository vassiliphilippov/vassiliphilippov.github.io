conservation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnsɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The principle that certain quantities remain unchanged during chemical reactions or physical transformations.
constancy of quantities in physics

Examples

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
Scientists rely on the principle of conservation of mass when analyzing chemical reactions.
The conservation of angular momentum is crucial to understanding planetary orbits.

Etymology

From Latin conservatio (preservation, keeping), from conservare (to preserve).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The preservation, protection, and careful management of the environment and natural resources.
preservation of environment or resources

Examples

The government launched a new conservation program to protect endangered species.
Many organizations are dedicated to the conservation of forests and wildlife.
Water conservation is crucial in regions affected by drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state or in good condition
  • Antonym: destruction - the act of destroying or ruining something, especially the environment

Etymology

From Latin conservatio (preservation, a keeping safe).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnsɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of preventing loss, injury, decay, or change, especially to protect something valuable such as the environment, resources, or cultural heritage.
prevention of loss or injury

Examples

The national park was established for the conservation of wildlife and natural resources.
Proper conservation of energy can help reduce utility costs and benefit the environment.
She devoted her career to the conservation of historic buildings and monuments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state or in good condition
  • Antonym: destruction - the act of causing something to be destroyed or ruined

Etymology

From Latin 'conservatio' (a keeping, preserving), from 'conservare' (to preserve).
