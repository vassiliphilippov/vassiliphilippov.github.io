flagellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌflædʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌflædʒɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of beating the human body, especially as part of a religious ritual or for sexual arousal
beating for religious or erotic stimulation

Examples

Some religious sects practice flagellation during ceremonies as a form of penance.
There are historical records describing flagellation as a method of spiritual purification.
The novel explores the connection between flagellation and erotic pleasure in certain subcultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipping - a beating with a whip as punishment or torture
  • Synonym: scourging - the act of whipping as punishment or penance

Etymology

From Latin flagellatio (a whipping), from flagellare (to whip) and flagellum (whip).

2. NOUN

/ ˌflædʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌflædʒɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of beating someone with a whip or similar instrument, especially as a form of punishment or religious discipline
beating with a whip as punishment

Examples

The prisoner was subjected to flagellation as a form of discipline.
Historically, flagellation was used by some religious sects as a way to demonstrate penitence.
He studied the history and symbolism behind ritual flagellation in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipping - the act of beating with a whip, especially as punishment
  • Synonym: scourging - the act of whipping or beating, especially as punishment

Etymology

From Latin flagellatio, from flagellare (to whip or scourge).
