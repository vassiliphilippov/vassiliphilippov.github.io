lepidote

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛpɪˌdoʊt / (AmE), / ˈlɛpɪdəʊt / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially a plant) rough to the touch because it is covered with small scales or scurf.
covered with small scales; rough to the touch

Examples

The leaves of this shrub are lepidote, giving them a sandpaper-like texture.
Many desert plants have lepidote surfaces to help reduce water loss.
Scientists noted the lepidote scales covering the stems of the specimen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glabrous - smooth; lacking hairs or scales

Etymology

From Greek lepidōtos (scaly), from lepis, lepid- (scale).
