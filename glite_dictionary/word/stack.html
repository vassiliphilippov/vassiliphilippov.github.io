stack

1. NOUN

/ stæk / (AmE), / stak / (BrE)
a neat or orderly pile of objects, typically arranged one on top of another
neat pile of objects

Examples

She arranged the books in a stack on the table.
There was a stack of plates in the kitchen waiting to be washed.
He built a stack of firewood near the back door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heap - an untidy pile or mass of things
  • Synonym: pile - a number of things lying on top of each other

Etymology

From Old Norse 'stakkr' (haystack).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07979701-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ stæk / (AmE), / stak / (BrE)
a type of storage device or data structure in which the most recently stored item is the next to be retrieved (last in, first out)
LIFO data structure in computing

Examples

The stack allows you to access the last item you placed in it first.
Every function call in this program uses the call stack.
Pushing and popping elements are basic operations on a stack.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queue - a data structure in which the first item added is the first to be removed (FIFO)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
