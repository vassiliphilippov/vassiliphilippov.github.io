Myrtus communis

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɜrtəs kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪːtəs kɒˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
A Mediterranean evergreen shrub with aromatic leaves, white or pink flowers, and dark blue-black berries, commonly known as the common myrtle.
evergreen shrub, common myrtle

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Myrtus communis hedge along the pathway.
Mediterranean cuisines sometimes use berries from the Myrtus communis in their recipes.
She planted Myrtus communis for its fragrant leaves and attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrtle - common name for Myrtus communis

Etymology

From Latin 'myrtus' (myrtle) and 'communis' (common).
