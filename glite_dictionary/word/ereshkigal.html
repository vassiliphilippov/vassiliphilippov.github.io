Ereshkigal

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈrɛʃkɪˌɡɑl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛʃkɪˌɡɑːl / (BrE)
the Mesopotamian goddess of the underworld and death, consort of Nergal in ancient mythology
Mesopotamian goddess of the underworld

Examples

According to the myth, Ereshkigal ruled the land of the dead with her consort Nergal.
The temple was dedicated to Ereshkigal, the fearsome goddess of the underworld.
Stories about Ereshkigal appear in ancient Sumerian and Akkadian texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nergal - Mesopotamian god of war, death, and the underworld

Etymology

From Sumerian 'Ereš-ki-gal,' meaning 'Lady of the Great Earth.'
