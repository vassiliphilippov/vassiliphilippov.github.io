sapid

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæpɪd / (AmE), / ˈsapɪd / (BrE)
having a strong, pleasant, or distinct flavor; flavorful or tasty
having a strong or pleasant flavor

Examples

The soup was especially sapid, with a rich blend of spices and herbs.
She described the wine as sapid, noting its complex and lingering taste.
After a bland meal, the chef promised a more sapid dish next time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insipid - lacking flavor or taste
  • Synonym: flavorful - having a lot of flavor or taste

Etymology

From Latin 'sapidus' (tasty, savory), from 'sapere' (to taste).
