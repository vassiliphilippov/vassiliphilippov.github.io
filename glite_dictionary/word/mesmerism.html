mesmerism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛzməˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛzmərɪzəm / (BrE)
the method or practice of inducing a trance or hypnotic state, especially as practiced in the 19th century
the induction of hypnosis; hypnotic influence

Examples

The healing powers of mesmerism were once widely debated among 19th-century physicians.
She studied mesmerism as part of her research into the history of hypnosis.
The showman claimed he could cure headaches through the mysterious art of mesmerism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - state of consciousness with focused attention, reduced awareness, and increased suggestibility

Etymology

From Mesmer, Franz Anton (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who developed early hypnosis theories, plus -ism.
