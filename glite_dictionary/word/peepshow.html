peepshow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpipˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpiːpˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
an exhibition or device displaying pictures or objects, viewed through a small hole or magnifying glass, often for entertainment or curiosity
exhibition viewed through a small hole

Examples

The museum featured an antique peepshow that entertained visitors in the 19th century.
Children gathered around the peepshow to catch a glimpse of the tiny moving figures inside.
He paid a small fee to look into the peepshow and see the hand-painted images.

Etymology

From 'peep' (to look quickly or secretly) + 'show' (display).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpipˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpiːpʃəʊ / (BrE)
a short pornographic film or display, viewed privately in a small, coin-operated booth
pornographic film in a private booth

Examples

After inserting a coin, he watched a peepshow in the booth at the back of the store.
The old theater was converted into a peepshow arcade in the 1970s.
Curious tourists sometimes stumble upon the city's infamous peepshow booths in the red-light district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striptease - a performance in which a person gradually removes their clothing

Etymology

From 'peep' (to look quickly or furtively) + 'show'. Originally from 18th-century street shows displayed through small holes.
