hydrogen carbonate

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrədʒən kɑːrˈbɒneɪt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrədʒən kɑːˈbɒneɪt / (BrE)
a salt of carbonic acid containing the HCO₃⁻ anion; also known as bicarbonate
chemical compound (bicarbonate)

Examples

Sodium hydrogen carbonate, commonly known as baking soda, is often used in baking.
In the blood, hydrogen carbonate acts as a buffer to help maintain pH balance.
The reaction can produce carbon dioxide gas when hydrogen carbonate reacts with an acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicarbonate - another name for hydrogen carbonate, containing the HCO₃⁻ anion

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'carbonate'; carbonate from Latin 'carbonatus' (carbonated).
