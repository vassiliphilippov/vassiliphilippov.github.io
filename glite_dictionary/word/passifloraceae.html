Passifloraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˌflɔːrəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˌflɒrəˈsiːɪˌaɪ / (BrE)
A family of tropical woody vines, known as the passionflower family, characterized by tendril-climbing plants.
tropical woody vine family (passionflowers)

Examples

The Passifloraceae are best known for their showy passionflowers grown in tropical gardens.
Some species within Passifloraceae produce edible fruits, such as the passionfruit.
Botanists have studied the unique climbing mechanisms found in the Passifloraceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passiflora - genus of flowering plants in the Passifloraceae family

Etymology

From genus name Passiflora (from Latin passio 'passion' + flos, floris 'flower') + standard plant family suffix -aceae.

Examples

Many species in the Passifloraceae family are cultivated for their ornamental flowers.
The Passifloraceae family includes the edible passionfruit vine.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'Passiflora', the type genus, meaning 'passion flower', and '-aceae', a standard botanical suffix for family names.
