chiropteran

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈrɑptərən / (AmE), / kaɪˈrɒptərən / (BrE)
A mammal of the order Chiroptera, including bats, characterized by forelimbs modified as wings and adaptations for echolocation.
bat or related mammal (order Chiroptera)

Examples

The chiropteran is unique among mammals for its ability to fly.
Scientists study chiropterans to better understand echolocation.
This fossil represents an early species of chiropteran from the Eocene epoch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal of the order Chiroptera

Etymology

From Greek 'cheir' (hand) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the wing structure.
