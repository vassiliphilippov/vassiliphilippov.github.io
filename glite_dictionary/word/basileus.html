basileus

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪliəs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪliːəs / (BrE)
the title for a ruler or emperor in the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire
ruler of the Byzantine Empire

Examples

The basileus presided over the Byzantine court with absolute authority.
After the fall of Constantinople, the title basileus was no longer used for the ruler.
In Byzantine history, the basileus held both secular and religious power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor - sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire

Etymology

From Ancient Greek βασιλεύς (basileús), meaning 'king' or 'emperor'.
