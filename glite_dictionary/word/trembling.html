trembling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid, slight shaking movement, often caused by fear, excitement, weakness, or cold
a shaky or quivering movement

Examples

There was a noticeable trembling in her hands as she spoke.
The cold wind caused a trembling throughout his whole body.
He tried to hide his trembling, but his nervousness was clear.

Synset Examples

the shaking of his fingers as he lit his pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaking - the act of moving something or someone up and down or back and forth with quick motions
  • Synonym: quivering - a slight, rapid shaking movement

Etymology

From Middle English 'tremblynge', from Old French 'trembler' (to tremble), from Latin 'tremulare' (to tremble).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (BrE)
shaking slightly and uncontrollably, typically as a result of fear, anxiety, cold, or weakness
shaking slightly and uncontrollably

Examples

She held out her trembling hands to show how nervous she was.
A trembling voice betrayed his anxiety during the interview.
The puppy appeared cold and trembling after being out in the rain.

Synset Examples

the quaking child asked for more
with shaking knees
seemed shaky on her feet
sparkling light from the shivering crystals of the chandelier
trembling hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaking - shaking or trembling, especially from fear or cold
  • Synonym: shaking - moving with slight, rapid, tremulous movements
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving

Etymology

From the verb 'tremble', from Old French 'trembler', from Latin 'tremulare', from 'tremulus', related to 'tremere' (to tremble).
