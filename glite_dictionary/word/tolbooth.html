tolbooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlˌbuθ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlˌbuːθ / (BrE)
A booth at a tollgate where a collector receives payment for passage along a road, bridge, or other route.
toll collection booth or gatehouse

Examples

The driver slowed as he approached the tolbooth to pay the fee.
Old highways in Europe often had a tolbooth at important river crossings.
She waved to the tolbooth attendant after handing over the coins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tollhouse - a house or building where tolls are collected, often larger than a tolbooth

Etymology

From Middle English 'tolbothe', from Old English 'tol' (toll) + 'booth' (a temporary shelter or stall).
