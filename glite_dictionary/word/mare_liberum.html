mare liberum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːreɪ lɪˈbɪərəm / (AmE), / ˌmɑːreɪ lɪˈbɪərəm / (BrE)
a navigable body of water that is open for use by all nations without restriction
international waters open to all nations
legal

Examples

The principle of mare liberum forms the basis of international maritime law today.
Disputes often arise when countries challenge the concept of mare liberum in strategic waterways.
Grotius' treatise 'Mare Liberum' defended the idea that the sea should remain mare liberum for trade and travel.

Etymology

From Latin ‘mare liberum’ meaning ‘free sea’.
