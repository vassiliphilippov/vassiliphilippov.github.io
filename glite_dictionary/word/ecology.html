ecology

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biology that studies the interactions between organisms and their environment
study of organisms and their environment

Examples

She studied ecology at university, focusing on forest ecosystems.
A greater understanding of ecology can help us protect endangered species.
The conference included several presentations on urban ecology and its impact on city planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmental science - the scientific study of the environment and solutions to environmental problems
  • Degree Higher: biology - the science of life and living organisms

Etymology

From Greek 'oikos' (house) + '-logia' (study of), meaning 'study of the household/environment'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the relationship between living organisms and their environment, or the natural environment as it relates to living things
relationship of organisms to their environment

Examples

The construction project could disrupt the island's fragile ecology.
Scientists study ecology to understand how human actions affect the environment.
Climate change is having a significant impact on the global ecology.

Synset Examples

it changed the ecology of the island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecosystem - a community of interacting organisms and their environment

Etymology

From Greek oikos (house, dwelling) + -logia (study of), coined in late 19th century.
