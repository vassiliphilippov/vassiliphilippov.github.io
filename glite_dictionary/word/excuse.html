excuse

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈskjuz / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːz / (BrE)
to ask for permission to leave or withdraw from an engagement or gathering, often out of politeness
ask leave from engagement or gathering

Examples

She politely asked to excuse herself from the meeting to take an urgent call.
If you need to leave early, simply excuse yourself and thank your host.
During dinner, he stood up and excused himself to use the restroom.

Etymology

From Latin excusare ‘to free from blame’.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːs / (BrE)
a very poor or inadequate example of something, often used to express disapproval or criticism
a poor example of something

Examples

That was a pathetic excuse for an apology, and nobody believed it was sincere.
The film was such a poor excuse for a comedy that I left halfway through.
He served us a miserable excuse for dinner that was barely edible.

Synset Examples

/ ɪkˈskjus / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːs / (BrE)

Examples

Employees are often required to submit an excuse if they miss work unexpectedly.
