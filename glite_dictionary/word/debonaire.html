debonaire

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛbəˈnɛr / (AmE), / ˌdɛbəˈneə / (BrE)
(of a man) cheerful, lively, and self-confident, often in a charming or elegant way.
cheerful, confident, and charming (usually of a man)

Examples

He entered the room with a debonaire smile that instantly lightened the mood.
The actor was known for his debonaire charm and impeccable style.
She found his debonaire attitude refreshing compared to the stern formality of others.

Synset Examples

looking chipper, like a man … diverted by his own wit
life that is gay, brisk, and debonair
walked with a jaunty step
a jaunty optimist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaunty - having a lively, cheerful, and self-confident manner
  • Antonym: dour - relentlessly severe, stern, or gloomy in manner or appearance

Etymology

From Old French *debonair* (courteous, gentle), from *de bon aire* (of good disposition).
