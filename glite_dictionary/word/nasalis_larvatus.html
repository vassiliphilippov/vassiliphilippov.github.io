Nasalis larvatus

1. NOUN

/ nəˈseɪlɪs lɑːrˈveɪtəs / (AmE), / nəˈseɪlɪs lɑːˈveɪtəs / (BrE)
a species of monkey native to Borneo, noted for its long, bulbous nose and reddish-brown fur; also called the proboscis monkey
species of monkey with a long bulbous nose

Examples

The Nasalis larvatus is easily recognized by its spectacularly large nose and pot belly.
Researchers observed a troop of Nasalis larvatus feeding on mangrove leaves near the riverbank.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the Nasalis larvatus from habitat loss in Borneo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proboscis monkey - common name for Nasalis larvatus, a Bornean monkey with a long nose

Etymology

From Latin: 'nasalis' (of the nose) + 'larvatus' (masked).
