quiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvə / (BrE)
a container for holding arrows, usually worn on the back, hip, or belt by archers.
container for arrows

Examples

The archer reached for an arrow from her quiver before taking a shot.
He fashioned a leather quiver to carry his arrows during the hunt.
A full quiver hung across his back as he entered the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrow - a shaft shot from a bow

Etymology

From Old French *quivre*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from a Germanic source.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvə / (BrE)
a slight, rapid, trembling or shaking motion
a slight trembling or shaking motion

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shudder - a strong or violent trembling movement
  • Synonym: vibration - rapid back-and-forth motion; can mean shaking

Etymology
