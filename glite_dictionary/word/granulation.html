granulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
new connective tissue and tiny blood vessels that form on the surface of a wound during healing
new tissue in wound healing
medical

Examples

Healthy granulation is a good sign that the patient's wound is healing properly.
The surgeon noted the presence of well-formed granulation during the examination.
Delayed granulation can indicate an underlying infection or poor circulation.

Etymology

From Latin 'granulum' (small grain) + '-ation', indicating the formation of small grain-like tissue.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡræn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a substance into small grains or granules
process of forming granules

Examples

The granulation of sugar makes it easier to dissolve in liquids.
Modern pharmaceuticals often rely on the granulation of active ingredients to enhance drug delivery.
Their research focused on improving the granulation of raw materials to boost manufacturing efficiency.

Synset Examples

the granulation of medicines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granule - a small compact particle or grain

Etymology

From Latin 'granulum' (small grain) + English suffix '-ation'.
