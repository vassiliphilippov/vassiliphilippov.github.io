subverter

1. NOUN

/ səbˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who seeks to undermine or overthrow established political or social systems
person who seeks to undermine authority

Examples

The government viewed him as a subverter of moral and political order.
During the regime, many subverters were arrested for plotting against the leadership.
She was labeled a subverter due to her outspoken opposition to the current system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - a person who advocates or participates in a revolution
  • Antonym: conservative - a person who is resistant to change and supports traditional values or institutions

Etymology

From Latin 'subvertere' (to overturn, overthrow), from 'sub-' (from below) + 'vertere' (to turn).
