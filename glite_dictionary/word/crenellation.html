crenellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɛnəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɛnɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of building battlements with regular gaps (crenels) for shooting through, such as on castle walls
building battlements with gaps

Examples

The crenellation of the castle walls allowed defenders to fire arrows while staying protected.
During the restoration, historians carefully studied the original crenellation patterns.
Medieval fortresses are famous for their distinctive crenellation along the ramparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlement - a parapet with alternating gaps and raised parts, used in fortifications

Etymology

From Old French *crenel* (notch), ultimately from Vulgar Latin *crena* (notch, crenel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɛnəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɛnɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a battlement along the top of a wall, especially of a castle, with regular gaps (embrasures) for shooting through
battlement with gaps on castle walls

Examples

The medieval castle was topped with crenellations that allowed defenders to fire arrows safely.
Architects restored the crenellation to match the building’s original appearance.
Soldiers took up positions behind the crenellations during the siege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlement - a parapet at the top of a wall with regular gaps for shooting

Etymology

From Old French *crenel* (notch), from *cren* (notch), with -ation suffix.
