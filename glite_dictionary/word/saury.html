saury

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔri / (AmE), / ˈsɔːri / (BrE)
A slender, long-beaked marine fish found in temperate Atlantic waters, valued as food in some regions.
slender long-beaked fish

Examples

Grilled saury is a popular dish in Japan during the autumn season.
Fishermen reported a significant increase in the population of saury off the coast this year.
The slender body and sharp beak make the saury easily recognizable among other fish.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'sauria', from Greek 'sauros' meaning 'lizard', referring to its shape.
