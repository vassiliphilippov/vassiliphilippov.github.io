Vigna unguiculata

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡ.nə ˌʌn.ɡjuː.ɪˈkjuː.lə.tə / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡ.nə ˌʌŋ.ɡjuː.ɪˈkjuː.lə.tə / (BrE)
A sprawling annual plant of the pea family, cultivated especially in warm regions for its edible seeds, forage, and as green manure.
a species of plant grown for food and forage

Examples

Vigna unguiculata is a valuable crop in African and Asian agriculture, providing protein-rich food and animal fodder.
Farmers often plant Vigna unguiculata as a cover crop to enrich soil fertility between harvests.
The leaves and pods of Vigna unguiculata are used for both human consumption and livestock feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - common name for Vigna unguiculata, an edible legume

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vigna' after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna, 'unguiculata' meaning 'clawed' (referring to seed pod shape).
