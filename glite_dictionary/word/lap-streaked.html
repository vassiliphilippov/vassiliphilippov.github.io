lap-streaked

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæpˌstriːkt / (AmE), / ˈlæpˌstriːkt / (BrE)
(of a boat or hull) constructed with the edges of the planks overlapping each other.
of a hull with overlapping planks

Examples

The old fishing boat was lap-streaked for added strength and durability.
Skilled craftsmen made the lap-streaked hulls by carefully overlapping the wooden planks.
Many traditional Viking ships were lap-streaked, a technique known for enhancing seaworthiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinker-built - built with overlapping planks in boat construction
  • Antonym: carvel-built - built with planks laid edge to edge, not overlapping

Etymology

From 'lap', meaning to overlap, and 'streak', an old term for a strip or plank in boatbuilding.
