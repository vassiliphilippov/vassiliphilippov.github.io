polyphonous

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪfənəs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪfənəs / (BrE)
having or characterized by polyphony; involving two or more independent melodic lines sounded together
having multiple independent melodies (polyphony)

Examples

The choir performed a polyphonous piece that blended several melodies in harmony.
Baroque composers were known for their polyphonous compositions that showcased intricate musical lines.
Her research focused on the development of polyphonous traditions in medieval church music.

Synset Examples

polyphonic traditions of the baroque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyphonic - having two or more independent melodic lines; relating to polyphony

Etymology

From Greek poluphōnos, from poly- (many) + phōnē (sound, voice).
