O

1. NOUN

/ oʊ / (AmE), / əʊ / (BrE)
The fifteenth letter of the Roman alphabet.
the letter 'O'

Examples

The word 'octopus' begins with the letter O.
She traced a perfect O on the page with her pen.
On the eye chart, can you read the line with the small Os?

Etymology

From Latin 'O', from Greek 'omicron', literally 'small o'.

2. NOUN

/ oʊ / (AmE), / əʊ / (BrE)
The chemical symbol for oxygen, a nonmetallic element that is a colorless, odorless gas making up 21% of Earth's atmosphere.
chemical element symbol: oxygen

Examples

Water is made up of hydrogen and O.
The doctor increased the patient's O level to assist with breathing.
Scientists discovered traces of O in the ancient rock samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxygen - the element with atomic number 8, represented by the symbol O

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'oxygenium', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) + 'genes' (producer, former).

3. NOUN

/ oʊ / (AmE), / əʊ / (BrE)
the human blood group whose red cells have neither A nor B antigens, making it compatible for donation to most other blood types
a human blood group, lacking A and B antigens
medical

Examples

She discovered that her blood type was O after the laboratory tests.
People with O blood are often called universal donors.
The doctor asked if anyone knew their O blood status for the emergency transfusion.

Synset Examples

people with type O blood are universal donors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: A - the blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen
  • Synonym: B - the blood group whose red cells carry the B antigen
  • Synonym: AB - the blood group whose red cells carry both A and B antigens

Etymology

Used as a designation for the blood group system, from the letter O, chosen arbitrarily by early hematologists.
