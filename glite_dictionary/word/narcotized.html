narcotized

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːrkəˌtaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkəˌtaɪzd / (BrE)
under the influence of narcotics, often resulting in drowsiness or insensibility
under the influence of narcotics

Examples

She appeared completely narcotized after the surgery and could barely keep her eyes open.
His narcotized state made it hard for him to respond to questions from the doctor.
The patient was still narcotized and needed time to recover before being moved to a regular ward.

Synset Examples

knocked out by doped wine
a drugged sleep
were under the effect of the drugged sweets
in a stuperous narcotized state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drugged - having taken or been given a drug, especially to induce sleep or unconsciousness

Etymology

From 'narcotic' (from Greek narkōtikos, numbing) + '-ize' + '-ed'.
