subsidy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.sɪ.di / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.di / (BrE)
a sum of money granted by a government or public body to help an industry or business keep the price of a commodity or service low or competitive
government financial grant or support

Examples

The government provided a subsidy to support renewable energy projects across the country.
Farmers received a subsidy to offset the costs of growing essential crops.
The company relied heavily on a subsidy from the government to finance its research and development.

Synset Examples

a subsidy for research in artificial intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by an organization, especially a government, for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue

Etymology

From Latin subsidium (assistance, support).
