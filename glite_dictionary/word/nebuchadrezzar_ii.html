Nebuchadrezzar II

1. PROPN

/ ˌnɛbjəkədˈrɛzər ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˌnɛbjʊkədˈrɛzə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
the king of Babylon from about 605 to 562 BC, known for conquering Jerusalem and exiling the Israelites to Babylonia
Babylonian king; conquered Jerusalem

Examples

The story of Nebuchadrezzar II is recounted in many books of the Old Testament.
Nebuchadrezzar II was responsible for the destruction of the First Temple in Jerusalem.
Historians study the reign of Nebuchadrezzar II to understand the history of ancient Babylonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nebuchadnezzar II - Alternate spelling; Babylonian king who conquered Jerusalem

Etymology

From Akkadian Nabu-kudurri-uṣur (Nabu, protect my heir).
