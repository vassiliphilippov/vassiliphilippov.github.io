multitudinous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.təˈtuː.dɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəs / (BrE)
very numerous; existing in great numbers
very numerous; great in number

Examples

The multitudinous stars filled the night sky with their brilliant light.
She struggled to manage the multitudinous details of organizing the event.
Waves crashed on the shore with a multitudinous roar.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted; innumerable
  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: myriad - countless or extremely great in number

Etymology

From Latin multitudin- (stem of multitudo, meaning 'multitude') + -ous.
