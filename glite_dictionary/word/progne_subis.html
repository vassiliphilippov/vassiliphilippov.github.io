Progne subis

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡni ˈsuːbɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒɡni ˈsuːbɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the purple martin, a large North American swallow with males that are blue-black
species of North American swallow (purple martin)

Examples

Every spring, birdwatchers look forward to the arrival of Progne subis in their gardens.
The distinctive song of Progne subis can often be heard near lakes and open fields.
Researchers banded hundreds of Progne subis to study their migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple martin - a large North American swallow with glossy blue-black males

Etymology

From Latin: Progne (a character in Greek mythology) and subis (swallow).
