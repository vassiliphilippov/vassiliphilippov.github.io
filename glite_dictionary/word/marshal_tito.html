Marshal Tito

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑrʃəl ˈtiːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːʃəl ˈtiːtəʊ / (BrE)
(1892–1980) Yugoslav statesman and military leader who led resistance against German occupation during World War II and later established a communist state.
Yugoslav statesman and leader (1892–1980)

Examples

Marshal Tito played a crucial role in organizing the Yugoslav partisan resistance during World War II.
As the leader of Yugoslavia, Marshal Tito maintained independence from the Soviet Union.
Many streets and squares in the former Yugoslavia are still named after Marshal Tito.

Etymology

Marshal was a military rank and Tito was his chosen nom de guerre; born Josip Broz.
