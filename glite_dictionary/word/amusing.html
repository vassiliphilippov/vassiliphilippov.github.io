amusing

1. ADJ

/ əˈmjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing laughter or enjoyment; entertaining in an enjoyable way
entertaining, causing enjoyment

Examples

The comedian told an amusing story that had everyone laughing.
We spent an amusing afternoon playing board games together.
Her amusing comments lightened the mood at the meeting.

Synset Examples

an amusing speaker
a diverting story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - making you laugh or amusing
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting or entertaining

Etymology

From English 'amuse' (to entertain), derived from Old French 'amuser'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈmjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing laughter or providing entertainment; funny or entertaining in a way that makes people smile or laugh
funny; causing laughter or entertainment

Examples

The comedian's amusing stories kept the audience laughing all night.
We watched an amusing film filled with clever jokes and puns.
His attempts to dance were more amusing than impressive.

Synset Examples

an amusing film with a steady stream of pranks and pratfalls
an amusing fellow
a comic hat
a comical look of surprise
funny stories that made everybody laugh
a very funny writer
it would have been laughable if it hadn't hurt so much
a mirthful experience
risible courtroom antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - making you laugh; humorous
  • Synonym: comic - intended to make people laugh
  • Antonym: serious - not intended to provoke laughter; solemn

Etymology

From amuse + -ing; 'amuse' from Old French amuser (to divert, entertain).
