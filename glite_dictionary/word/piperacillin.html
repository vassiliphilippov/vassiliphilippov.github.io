piperacillin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪpərəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɪpərəˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
a semi-synthetic penicillin antibiotic used to treat moderate to severe bacterial infections
an antibiotic of the penicillin class
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed piperacillin to treat the patient's bacterial infection.
Serious infections are sometimes treated effectively with piperacillin in combination with other antibiotics.
Allergic reactions to piperacillin are similar to those seen with other penicillins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - the group of antibiotics related to penicillium molds

Etymology

Formed from 'piper-' (referring to its piperazine ring structure) and 'acillin' (as in penicillin).
