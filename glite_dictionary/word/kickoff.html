kickoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈkɪkɒf / (BrE)
the beginning or start of an event, activity, or period, especially in sports or organized competitions
start of an event, especially in sports

Examples

The opening speech was scheduled just after the kickoff of the ceremony.
Fans eagerly awaited the kickoff to see which team would take the lead.
The campaign's kickoff attracted a large crowd and generated excitement.

Synset Examples

I was there with my parents at the kickoff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the point at which something begins
  • Antonym: end - the final point of something

Etymology

From the verb 'kick off', originally referring to starting play in football (soccer) by kicking the ball.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈkɪkɒf / (BrE)
(in football) a kick from the center of the field that starts or resumes a game after a score.
the starting kick in football

Examples

The game will begin with a kickoff at 3 PM sharp.
After the home team scored, the visiting team took the kickoff.
We arrived just in time to see the opening kickoff of the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kickoff - the start of an event or activity in a general sense

Etymology

Formed from 'kick' + 'off', indicating the action that begins the game.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈkɪkɒf / (BrE)
the moment when something is scheduled to start, especially the formal or official beginning of an event or activity
scheduled start time of an event

Examples

The kickoff for the conference is scheduled for 9 a.m. sharp.
As soon as the kickoff was announced, everyone hurried to take their seats.
Let's meet fifteen minutes before the official kickoff so we can review the agenda.

Synset Examples

they got an early start
she knew from the get-go that he was the man for her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the point in time at which something begins
  • Synonym: beginning - the act of starting something
  • Antonym: end - the point in time at which something ends

Etymology

From the sporting term 'kick off,' originally in football (soccer) for the start of the game.
