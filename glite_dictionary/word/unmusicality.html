unmusicality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌmjuːzɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌmjuːzɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of lacking musical ability or sensitivity.
lack of musical ability or sensitivity

Examples

His total unmusicality was evident whenever he tried to sing along at parties.
She worried that her unmusicality would hold her back in the choir auditions.
Despite his unmusicality, he enjoyed listening to classical concerts.

Synset Examples

It's not just that he's musically tone-deaf, but he seems to be quite deliberately plugging his metaphorical ears, as if fashioning his unmusicality to some dark design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: musicality - the quality of having musical talent or sensitivity

Etymology

Formed from 'unmusical' + '-ity', following typical English word formation patterns.
