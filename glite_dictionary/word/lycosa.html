Lycosa

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / lɪˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
A genus of large wolf spiders that is the type genus of the family Lycosidae.
genus of wolf spiders

Examples

The genus Lycosa includes several species of large, fast-moving wolf spiders.
Many members of Lycosa are found in grasslands and deserts worldwide.
Researchers observed the hunting behavior of Lycosa during their field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycosidae - the family of wolf spiders to which Lycosa belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'lycosa', from Greek 'lykos' meaning 'wolf', referring to the common name 'wolf spider'.
