Yucca smalliana

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə smɒlˈiːænə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˌsmɔːlˈiːænə / (BrE)
A species of yucca native to the southern United States, characterized by a clump of basal grasslike leaves and a central stalk ending in a cluster of small whitish flowers.
a species of yucca plant

Examples

The sandy soil of the region is ideal for Yucca smalliana to thrive.
Gardeners often choose Yucca smalliana for its striking foliage and low maintenance.
During late spring, Yucca smalliana produces a tall stalk lined with small white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of perennial plants with rosettes of evergreen leaves and tall clusters of flowers

Etymology

From the genus name 'Yucca', and species named after botanist John Kunkel Small.
