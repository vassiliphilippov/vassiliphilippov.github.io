almond

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmənd / (AmE), / ˈɑːmənd / (BrE)
an oval-shaped edible seed of the almond tree, often used as food or for its oil
edible seed of the almond tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped almonds over her morning oatmeal for extra flavor.
The bakery sells a delicious almond cake that is very popular with customers.
Almond milk, made from ground almonds, is a popular dairy alternative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nut - an edible seed, usually encased in a hard shell

Etymology

From Old French 'almande', from Late Latin 'amandula', from Greek 'amygdalē'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlmənd / (AmE), / ˈɑːmənd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to Asia and North Africa, cultivated for its pink blossoms and edible nuts enclosed in a hard green hull
tree that produces edible nuts

Examples

The farmer planted a row of almond trees along the fence.
Every spring, the almond tree outside my window bursts into pink blossoms.
Southern California has an ideal climate for growing almond orchards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond - the edible nut produced by the almond tree

Etymology

From Old French *almande*, from Latin *amygdala*, from Greek *amygdalē* (almond).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
