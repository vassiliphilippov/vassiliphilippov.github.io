Martes americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrtɛs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtɛs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of marten native to North America, valued for its soft, brown fur.
North American marten species

Examples

The trapper was excited to find the tracks of a Martes americana near the creek.
The valuable fur of the Martes americana made it a target for hunters in the 19th century.
Researchers in Canada are studying the declining population of the Martes americana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marten - small carnivorous mammals of the genus Martes

Etymology

From Latin, 'martes' meaning marten and 'americana' meaning American.
