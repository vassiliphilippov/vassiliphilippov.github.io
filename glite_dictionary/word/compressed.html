compressed

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛst / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst / (BrE)
flattened from side to side along the entire length, as in certain leaves or fish
flattened from side to side

Examples

The compressed shape of the flatfish allows it to hide easily on the ocean floor.
Some aquatic plants have compressed stems that help them move with water currents.
The leafstalk appeared compressed, making it distinct from those of other species.

Etymology

From Latin comprimere (to press together), past participle compressus.

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛst / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst / (BrE)
pressed tightly together so that it becomes smaller or more compact
pressed tightly together; compacted

Examples

She handed me a card with a compressed message inside.
His lips were compressed in concentration as he worked.
The file size was greatly compressed to save storage space.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together
  • Antonym: expanded - having been opened up or spread out

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'compress', from Latin 'compressus', past participle of 'comprimere' (to press together).

3. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛst / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst / (BrE)
(of a substance or object) reduced in volume, size, or length by applying pressure.
reduced in volume by pressure

Examples

The scientist filled the tank with compressed air for the experiment.
He used a compressed towel that expanded when soaked in water.
The data was stored in a compressed file format to save space.

Synset Examples

compressed air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensed - made denser or more concentrated; compressed together

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'compress,' which comes from Latin 'compressus', past participle of 'comprimere' (to press together).
