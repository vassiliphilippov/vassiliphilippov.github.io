pearly

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli / (BrE)
(informal) A human tooth, especially when referring to its whiteness or attractiveness.
informal term for a tooth
informal

Examples

He flashed his pearlies in a wide grin.
After the dental cleaning, her pearlies were sparkling.
He lost one of his pearlies during the hockey game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tooth - each of the hard, bony structures in the mouth used for biting and chewing

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɜːrli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli / (BrE)
Having the white color or lustrous quality of pearls.
having the color or luster of pearls

Examples

The bride wore a pearly necklace that shimmered in the light.
His smile revealed a row of pearly teeth.
Mist hovered above the lake, giving the water a pearly glow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pearl - a hard, lustrous spherical mass formed within the shell of a pearl oyster or other mollusk

Etymology

From 'pearl' + '-y' (adjective suffix).
