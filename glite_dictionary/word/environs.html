environs

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪərənz / (BrE)
the area surrounding a particular place, especially a town or city
the surrounding area

Examples

The small village lies in the environs of the ancient castle.
New developments are planned in the environs of the city center.
She preferred to live in the peaceful environs rather than in the bustling downtown.

Synset Examples

the country — the flat agricultural surround

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surroundings - the area around someone or something

Etymology

From French 'environs' (surroundings), from 'environner' (to surround).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪərənz / (BrE)
the areas surrounding a particular place, especially a city or town
areas around a place

Examples

The city and its environs were heavily affected by the flooding last year.
Pollution from factories often spreads into the environs of major towns.
She preferred to live in the peaceful environs rather than in the busy city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the surrounding area or neighborhood of a place
  • Synonym: outskirts - the outer parts of a town or city

Etymology

From French 'environ' (surrounding area), from Old French 'environner', meaning 'to surround'.
