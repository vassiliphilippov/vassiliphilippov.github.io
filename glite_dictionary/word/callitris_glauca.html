Callitris glauca

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪtrɪs ˈɡlɔkə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪtrɪs ˈɡlɔːkə / (BrE)
A small tree or shrub native to southern Australia with bluish-green foliage, belonging to the cypress family.
Australian cypress tree or shrub

Examples

The Callitris glauca is valued for its drought resistance in Australian gardens.
We spotted several Callitris glauca growing along the sandy roadside.
The bluish-green foliage of the Callitris glauca makes it easy to identify among native shrubs.

Etymology

From genus name 'Callitris' (Greek kallos 'beauty' + tri 'three', referring to the leaf arrangement) and Latin 'glauca' meaning 'bluish-green'.
