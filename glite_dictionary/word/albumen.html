albumen

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjumən / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːmən / (BrE)
the white of an egg, consisting mainly of albumin dissolved in water, which surrounds and protects the yolk
the white part of an egg

Examples

Carefully, she separated the albumen from the yolk to make a meringue.
The albumen provides essential nutrients for the developing chick inside the egg.
Some recipes require only the yolk, while others call for the albumen.

Synset Examples

she separated the whites from the yolks of several eggs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egg white - the white part of an egg

Etymology

From Latin 'albumen' (white of egg, whiteness), from 'albus' (white).

2. NOUN

/ ælˈbjumən / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːmən / (BrE)
a water-soluble protein found in many animal tissues and fluids, especially the white of eggs
water-soluble protein, especially in egg whites

Examples

The albumen of an egg contains essential proteins needed for growth.
Scientists analyzed the albumen to measure the egg's quality.
Bakers often separate the yolk from the albumen when preparing meringues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albumin - a group of simple water-soluble proteins, including albumen

Etymology

From Latin 'albumen' (egg white, whiteness), from 'albus' (white).
