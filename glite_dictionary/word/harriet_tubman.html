Harriet Tubman

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæriət ˈtʌbmən / (AmE), / ˈhæriət ˈtʌbmən / (BrE)
(1820–1913) An American abolitionist who escaped slavery and became a leading figure in helping others to freedom via the Underground Railroad.
American abolitionist and Underground Railroad conductor

Examples

Harriet Tubman is remembered for her courage in leading enslaved people to freedom.
Many schools across the United States are named after Harriet Tubman.
The legacy of Harriet Tubman continues to inspire generations advocating for justice.
