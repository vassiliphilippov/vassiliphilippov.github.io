Daniel

1. PROPN

/ ˈdænjəl / (AmE), / ˈdanjəl / (BrE)
A youth in the Old Testament who was taken into the court of Nebuchadnezzar and given divine protection when thrown into a den of lions.
Old Testament prophet protected from lions

Examples

The story of Daniel in the lions' den is one of faith and courage.
Many admire how Daniel trusted in his faith even when facing danger.
In Sunday school, the children learned about Daniel and the miracles that saved him.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Daniyyel' meaning 'God is my judge'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdænjəl / (AmE), / ˈdanjəl / (BrE)
A person known for wisdom and sound judgment, especially as a judge; used with reference to the biblical Daniel.
wise and upright judge (figurative)

Examples

Many regarded the new mayor as a Daniel among politicians, known for his fair decisions.
In times of doubt, she hoped for a Daniel to resolve their dispute impartially.
His colleagues referred to him as a Daniel because of his wise counsel in difficult situations.

Synset Examples

a Daniel come to judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solomon - a biblical king famous for wisdom and judgment
  • Antonym: fool - a person lacking good judgment or sense

Etymology

From the Hebrew personal name Dāniyyēl, meaning 'God is my judge'; reference to the biblical Daniel noted for wisdom.

Synset POS: n

3. PROPN

/ ˈdænjəl / (AmE), / ˈdanjəl / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that recounts the visions and experiences of Daniel in the court of Nebuchadnezzar.
Old Testament book about Daniel's visions

Examples

The story of the fiery furnace is found in the Book of Daniel.
Many scholars have debated the apocalyptic imagery presented in Daniel.
She cited a passage from Daniel during her lecture on biblical prophecy.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Dāniyyēl*, meaning 'God is my judge'.
