George Sandism

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɔrdʒ ˈsændˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔːdʒ ˈsandˌɪzəm / (BrE)
advocacy or embodiment of views or behaviors associated with George Sand, particularly unconventional or feminist attitudes that challenge societal norms
philosophy or style reflecting George Sand's unconventional views

Examples

Her essays were marked by a distinct George Sandism that defied conventional social expectations.
Critics accused the play of promoting George Sandism through its unapologetically progressive heroine.
In nineteenth-century France, George Sandism signified literary rebellion and feminist ideals.

Synset Examples

He embraced George Sandism by challenging societal norms.
The novel's feminist themes exude a strong George Sandism.
Her bold opinions on love reflect a true George Sandism.

Etymology

Formed from the name of the French novelist George Sand (pseudonym of Amantine Lucile Aurore Dupin) and the suffix '-ism', indicating a distinctive practice or philosophy.
