to hypnotise

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪp.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nə.taɪz / (BrE)
to put someone into a state of hypnosis; to induce a trance-like mental state in someone, often for therapeutic or entertainment purposes
induce hypnosis in someone

Examples

The therapist managed to hypnotise the patient to help them overcome their fear of flying.
Stage performers often hypnotise volunteers from the audience for entertainment.
She was so skilled that she could hypnotise someone in just a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mesmerize - to capture someone's attention completely, or to hypnotise; to induce a trance

Etymology

From Greek hypno- (sleep) + -ise (verb-forming suffix).
