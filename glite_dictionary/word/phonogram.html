phonogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəˌɡram / (BrE)
a written symbol that represents a sound, syllable, morpheme, or word
written symbol representing a sound or syllable

Examples

In ancient Egyptian writing, a phonogram could stand for one or more sounds.
The Japanese kana scripts consist of phonograms representing individual syllables.
When learning cuneiform, students often practice identifying phonogram signs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapheme - the smallest unit in a writing system of any given language

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound) + -gramma (something written).
