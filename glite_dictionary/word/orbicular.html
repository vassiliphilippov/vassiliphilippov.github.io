orbicular

1. ADJ

/ ɔrˈbɪkjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
having the shape of a circle, disk, or sphere; rounded or nearly circular in outline
rounded or circular in shape

Examples

The leaves of the plant are orbicular with smooth edges.
She admired the orbicular design of the pendant.
The shellfish had an orbicular shell that was almost perfectly round.

Synset Examples

a spherical object
nearly orbicular in shape
little globular houses like mud-wasp nests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spherical - having the shape of a sphere
  • Synonym: circular - having the form of a circle
  • Synonym: disk-shaped - shaped like a disk

Etymology

From Latin orbiculāris, from orbiculus meaning 'small disk or circle'.

2. ADJ

/ ɔːrˈbɪkjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
circular or nearly circular in shape
circular or round in shape

Examples

The leaves of the plant were orbicular, almost perfectly round.
He described the building's dome as orbicular in appearance.
Some shells are noticeably orbicular, differing from their more elongated relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circular - having the shape of a circle
  • Degree Lower: oval - having the shape of an egg or ellipse, like a stretched circle
  • Degree Higher: spherical - having the form of a sphere; ball-shaped

Etymology

From Latin 'orbiculāris', from 'orbiculus' (a small disk), diminutive of 'orbis' (circle).
