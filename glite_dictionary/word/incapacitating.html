incapacitating

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone or something to be unable to function normally; disabling or making incapable.
causing inability to function; disabling

Examples

The patient suffered an incapacitating stroke and required full-time care.
She was forced to leave work due to an incapacitating back injury.
Exposure to the chemical has incapacitating effects, making swift evacuation necessary.

Synset Examples

a crippling injury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disabling - causing someone to become unable to function; having a disabling effect
  • Synonym: crippling - causing severe damage or impairment
  • Antonym: enabling - giving someone the means to do something

Etymology

From 'incapacitate' + '-ing', ultimately from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'capax' (able, capable).
