unconstitutionality

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˌkɑnstɪˌtuʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ʌnˌkɒnstɪˌtjuːʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being in accordance with a constitution, especially the fundamental law of a country.
the state of being unconstitutional
legal

Examples

The court declared the new law's unconstitutionality after a thorough review.
Lawyers argued about the unconstitutionality of the proposed restrictions on free speech.
There has been much debate on the unconstitutionality of that government policy.

Synset Examples

On April 2013 the Court released two decisions about the unconstitutionality of certain provisions of the Acts of Madrid and Navarre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconstitutional - not permitted by a constitution

Etymology

From un- (not) + constitution + -ality, after 'unconstitutional'.
