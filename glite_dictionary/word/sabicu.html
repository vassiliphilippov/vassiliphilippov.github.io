sabicu

1. NOUN

/ səˈbiːkuː / (AmE), / səˈbiːkuː / (BrE)
the hard, durable wood of the sabicu tree, resembling mahogany and used for construction and furniture
hard wood resembling mahogany

Examples

The table was made from sabicu, giving it a rich, red-brown hue similar to mahogany.
Carpenters often choose sabicu for its strength and attractive appearance.
The boat's deck was constructed using sabicu wood for added durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a durable reddish-brown wood used for furniture

2. NOUN

/ səˈbiːkuː / (AmE), / səˈbiːkuː / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Lysiloma sabicu) that produces a hard, dark brown wood similar to mahogany in texture and value.
West Indian tree with mahogany-like wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from rare sabicu, giving it a deep, rich hue.
Many old Caribbean buildings feature beams made of sturdy sabicu wood.
The botanist explained that sabicu rivals mahogany in both appearance and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a reddish-brown hard wood used for furniture, from various tropical trees

Etymology

From Spanish sabicú, of Taíno origin.
