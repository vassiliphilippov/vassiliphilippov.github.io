pylorus

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈlɔrəs / (AmE), / paɪˈlɔːrəs / (BrE)
the circular opening at the lower end of the stomach, connecting it to the duodenum
opening between stomach and duodenum
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that food passes through the pylorus into the small intestine.
An ulcer near the pylorus can cause digestive problems and pain after eating.
During surgery, the surgeon checked the pylorus to ensure nothing was obstructing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duodenum - the first part of the small intestine, immediately beyond the stomach

Etymology

From Greek 'pylōros' (gatekeeper), from 'pylē' (gate) + 'ouros' (keeper).
