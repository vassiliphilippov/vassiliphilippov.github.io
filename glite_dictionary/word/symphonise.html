to symphonise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪm.fəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fə.naɪz / (BrE)
(of musical sounds or voices) to play or sound together in harmony.
to play or sound together in harmony

Examples

The choir members symphonised beautifully, creating a rich, unified sound.
When the orchestra symphonises, every instrument blends perfectly with the others.
As they played, their melodies symphonised effortlessly, filling the hall with harmony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - to bring into harmony or accord

Etymology

From Greek 'symphōnia' (concord or agreement of sound), via Late Latin and French.
