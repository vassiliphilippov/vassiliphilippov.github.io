undersurface

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌsɝːfɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
the lower side or surface of something
lower side of something

Examples

The undersurface of the leaf was covered in tiny white spots.
She checked the undersurface of the table for any markings.
Dust tends to collect on the undersurface of shelves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface - the outside or top layer of something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'below') + 'surface'.
