rain-wash

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪn ˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪn ˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
the removal of soil or loose material from a surface by the action of rainfall
washing away of soil by rain

Examples

The hillside was severely affected by rain-wash after the heavy storm.
Farmers use cover crops to protect the soil from rain-wash during the rainy season.
Decades of rain-wash have shaped the gentle slopes of the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the process by which soil and rock are removed from the Earth's surface by wind or water flow
  • Antonym: deposition - the geological process by which material is added to a landform or land mass
