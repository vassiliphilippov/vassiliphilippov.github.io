Podargus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈdɑrɡəs / (AmE), / pəʊˈdɑːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, nocturnal birds known as frogmouths, native to Australia and nearby regions, and the type genus of the family Podargidae.
genus of frogmouth birds

Examples

The tawny frogmouth is a well-known member of the genus Podargus.
Scientists have recently revised the classification of several species within Podargus.
Members of Podargus are noted for their excellent camouflage in Australian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Podargidae - the biological family of frogmouth birds

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'podargos', meaning 'swift-footed'.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈdɑrɡəs / (AmE), / pəˈdɑːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of nocturnal birds in the family Podargidae, commonly known as frogmouths, native to Australia and surrounding regions.
genus of nocturnal birds (frogmouths)

Examples

The Podargus genus includes the well-known tawny frogmouth.
Members of the Podargus genus are often mistaken for owls due to their nocturnal habits.
Researchers studied nesting behavior in several species of Podargus across Australia.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'podargos' meaning 'swift-footed', from 'pous' (foot) and 'argos' (swift).
