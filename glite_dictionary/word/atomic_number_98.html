atomic number 98

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ ˈnaɪnti eɪt / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə ˈnaɪnti eɪt / (BrE)
the chemical element californium; a radioactive transuranic element discovered by bombarding curium with alpha particles
the element californium (Cf)

Examples

Scientists first created atomic number 98 in 1950 by bombarding curium with alpha particles.
Atomic number 98 is also known as californium and is used in nuclear reactors.
Very small amounts of atomic number 98 can be found in research laboratories.
