renewal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈnuːəl / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəl / (BrE)
the process of converting wasteland into land suitable for habitation or cultivation
conversion of wasteland into usable land

Examples

The government invested heavily in urban renewal to transform abandoned lots into parks and housing.
Many cities around the world are embarking on land renewal projects to revitalize dilapidated neighborhoods.
Environmentalists supported the renewal of industrial wastelands to create green spaces for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reclamation - the process of making land suitable for cultivation

Etymology

From Latin renovo (to renew).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈnuəl / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəl / (BrE)
the act of filling again or restoring something by supplying what has been used up
act of filling or restoring

Examples

The renewal of supplies ensured the expedition could continue for several more days.
After a busy year, she felt the renewal of her energy during the holiday break.
Watering the plants regularly aids in the renewal of their nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replenishment - the act of making something full or complete again
  • Antonym: depletion - the reduction or exhaustion of resources

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *renewel*, from Old French *renovel*, based on 'renew'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈnuːəl / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəl / (BrE)
the act of making something new again, or extending the period of validity of something
the act of renewing

Examples

We received a notice about the renewal of our lease agreement.
The library offers easy online renewal of borrowed books.
Spring often feels like a time of renewal and growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extension - the action of prolonging something
  • Antonym: expiration - the ending or coming to an end of validity

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman and Old French ‘renouveler’, from Latin ‘renovare’ (make new again).
