combatively

1. ADV

/ kəmˈbætɪvli / (AmE), / kəmˈbætɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows a readiness or eagerness to fight or argue
in an aggressive or fighting manner

Examples

The two politicians debated combatively, each refusing to yield their position.
She answered his questions combatively, making it clear she would not be intimidated.
Throughout the meeting, he spoke combatively and challenged every suggestion.

Synset Examples

‘Don't trespass onto my property,’ the neighbor shouted combatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressively - in a manner showing readiness to attack or confront
  • Antonym: peacefully - in a manner free from disturbance or aggressiveness

Etymology

From 'combative' + '-ly'; 'combative' from Late Latin combattere (to fight with).
