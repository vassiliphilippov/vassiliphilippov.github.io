onerous

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊnərəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊnərəs / (BrE)
(of a task, duty, or responsibility) involving a great deal of effort, difficulty, or trouble; burdensome.
involving great effort or difficulty

Examples

The contract imposed several onerous obligations on the company.
She found the task of caring for the estate increasingly onerous.
Many considered the regulations to be unnecessarily onerous.

Synset Examples

the burdensome task of preparing the income tax return
my duties weren't onerous: I only had to greet the guests
a taxing schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdensome - causing hardship or difficulty
  • Synonym: taxing - physically or mentally demanding
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding

Etymology

From Latin onerosus (burdensome), from onus (burden).
