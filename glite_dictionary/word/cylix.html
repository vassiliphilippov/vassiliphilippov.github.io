cylix

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.lɪks / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.lɪks / (BrE)
a shallow, two-handled drinking cup used in ancient Greece, typically for wine
ancient Greek drinking cup

Examples

The museum displays a beautifully painted cylix from the fifth century BCE.
During the symposium, guests would drink wine from a cylix while reclining on couches.
The intricate designs on the cylix depict scenes from Greek mythology.

Etymology

From Greek kýliks (cup).
