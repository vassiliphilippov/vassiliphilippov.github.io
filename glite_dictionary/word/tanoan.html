Tanoan

1. NOUN

/ təˈnoʊ.ən / (AmE), / təˈnəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a family of North American Indigenous languages spoken in the southwestern United States, including Tiwa, Tewa, and Towa
family of Native American languages, US Southwest

Examples

The Tanoan languages are still spoken by some Pueblo peoples in New Mexico.
Linguists have traced similarities between the Kiowa and Tanoan language groups.
Anthropologists study Tanoan myths and oral traditions as part of their research.

Etymology

From Tano, the Spanish name for the Tewa people, with the suffix -an indicating relation.
