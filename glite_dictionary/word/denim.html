denim

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.ɪm / (BrE)
A strong, durable, twill-woven cotton fabric, typically blue, used chiefly for jeans and other clothing.
strong cotton twill fabric

Examples

She wore a stylish denim jacket over her dress.
The workers' uniforms are made of tough denim to withstand harsh conditions.
Classic blue denim has remained popular in fashion for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jean - a durable cotton twill textile, typically used to make jeans

Etymology

From French 'serge de Nîmes', referring to a twill fabric from Nîmes, France.
