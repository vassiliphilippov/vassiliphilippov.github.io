quinacrine

1. NOUN

/ kwɪˈnækrin / (AmE), / kwɪˈnækrɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used to treat certain parasitic worm infestations and formerly used as an antimalarial medication
a drug for worm infestations and malaria
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed quinacrine to treat the patient’s intestinal parasites.
During World War II, quinacrine was widely used as an antimalarial drug.
Researchers are studying new uses for quinacrine beyond its original purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atabrine - trade name for quinacrine

Etymology

From quin(oline) + acr(idine), referring to its chemical structure.
