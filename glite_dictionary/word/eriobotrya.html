Eriobotrya

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriəˈbɑːtriə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪəˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic evergreen fruit trees, including the loquat, known for their edible fruits.
genus of Asiatic evergreen fruit trees

Examples

The Eriobotrya genus includes the popular loquat tree, valued for its sweet orange fruit.
Botanists compared several species within the Eriobotrya genus to study their growth patterns.
In Asia, Eriobotrya trees are often cultivated in home gardens for both shade and fruit.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'botrys' (cluster, bunch of grapes), referring to the woolly flower clusters.
