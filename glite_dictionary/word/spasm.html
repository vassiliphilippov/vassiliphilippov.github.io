spasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈspazəm / (BrE)
A sudden, involuntary contraction or tightening of a hollow organ, such as a blood vessel, often causing pain or disruption of function.
sudden muscular constriction (medical)
medical

Examples

A coronary artery spasm can restrict blood flow to the heart.
Migraine headaches are sometimes triggered by a spasm of blood vessels in the brain.
During the procedure, the patient experienced a brief intestinal spasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a painful, involuntary muscle contraction

Etymology

From Latin spasmus, from Greek spasmos (convulsion, spasm), from span (to draw, wrench).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈspazəm / (BrE)
a sudden, involuntary and often painful contraction of a muscle or group of muscles
sudden involuntary muscle contraction
medical

Examples

He suffered a painful spasm in his lower back after lifting the heavy box.
A sudden spasm in her leg woke her up in the middle of the night.
If you experience a muscle spasm, gently stretch the affected area to relieve the pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a painful involuntary contraction of a muscle, typically temporary

Etymology

From Greek *spasmos* (pulling, wrenching) via Latin *spasmus*.
