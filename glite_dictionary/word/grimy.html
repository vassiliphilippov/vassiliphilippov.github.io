grimy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡraɪ.mi / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪ.mi / (BrE)
covered with or characterized by a layer of ingrained dirt or soot; dirty and grimy in appearance or texture.
covered with ingrained dirt or soot

Examples

After a day of work in the garden, her grimy hands needed a thorough wash.
The windows of the abandoned house were so grimy that no light could get through.
He wiped the grimy sweat from his forehead after fixing the old car engine.

Synset Examples

a miner's begrimed face
dingy linen
grimy hands
grubby little fingers
a grungy kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with an unclean substance
  • Antonym: clean - not dirty; free from dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

From grim, meaning 'dirt', + -y, adjective suffix. First recorded in the 19th century.
