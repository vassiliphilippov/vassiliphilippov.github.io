cichlid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪklɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪd / (BrE)
Any of a large family of chiefly freshwater fishes native to tropical America, Africa, and Asia, many of which are kept as aquarium fish.
a family of tropical freshwater fish

Examples

The aquarium was filled with colorful cichlid species from Lake Malawi.
Many hobbyists enjoy breeding cichlids because of their vibrant colors and interesting behaviors.
Some African cichlids are known for their unique method of protecting their young.

Etymology

From modern Latin Cichlidae, from Greek kikhlē (a kind of wrasse, later applied to these fishes).
