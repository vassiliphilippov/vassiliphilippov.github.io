biotech

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌtɛk / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.əʊˌtɛk / (BrE)
the science of using living organisms or biological systems to develop products or processes, especially for industrial or medical purposes
applied biological technology

Examples

Many startups in Silicon Valley now focus on biotech innovations to develop new medicines.
She decided to pursue a career in biotech after studying molecular biology in college.
The biotech industry has grown rapidly due to advances in genetic engineering.

Synset Examples

biotechnology produced genetically altered bacteria that solved the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotechnology - the use of living systems and organisms to develop products

Etymology

Short form of 'biotechnology', combining 'bio-' (life) and 'technology'.
