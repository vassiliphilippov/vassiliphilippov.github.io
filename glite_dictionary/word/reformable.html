reformable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being improved, corrected, or changed for the better.
able to be improved or changed

Examples

The experts considered the existing policy reformable with some minor adjustments.
She believed the company's flawed procedures were fully reformable through innovation and transparency.
Some people see the criminal justice system as reformable, while others think it needs a complete overhaul.

Synset Examples

a redeemable sinner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irredeemable - not able to be improved or reformed

Etymology

From re- (again) + Latin formare (to form) + -able (capable of).
