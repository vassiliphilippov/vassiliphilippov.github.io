to overgeneralise

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
To draw a conclusion or make a statement that is too broad and not supported by enough evidence.
draw overly broad conclusions
british

Examples

Psychologists warn students not to overgeneralise based on a single experiment.
It is easy to overgeneralise when discussing cultural differences.
She tends to overgeneralise from her own experiences to everyone else's.

Synset Examples

It is dangerous to overgeneralize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overgeneralize - American English spelling of 'overgeneralise'

Etymology

From prefix over- (excessively) + generalise (from French généraliser).
