douche

1. NOUN

/ duːʃ / (AmE), / duːʃ / (BrE)
A stream of water or medicated fluid directed into or around a body cavity, especially the vagina, as part of medical cleansing or treatment.
body cavity cleansing with water or fluid
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended using a douche to help treat the infection.
Many health experts advise against frequent vaginal douches due to potential health risks.
She purchased a medicated douche on the advice of her physician.

Etymology

From French 'douche', from Italian 'doccia' (shower).

2. NOUN

/ duːʃ / (AmE), / duːʃ / (BrE)
A device consisting of a small syringe with detachable nozzles, used for washing out the vagina or giving enemas.
medical device for vaginal lavage or enema
medical

Examples

The nurse prepared a douche before the medical procedure.
Some people use a douche as part of their personal hygiene routine.
After surgery, the doctor advised her not to use a douche for several weeks.

Etymology

From French *douche* (shower), from Italian *doccia*.
