melanoblast

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈlænəˌblæst / (AmE), / mɪˈlanəˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a type of cell in the developing embryo that gives rise to a melanocyte, which produces melanin pigment in the skin
precursor cell of a melanocyte
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, melanoblasts migrate to different parts of the skin.
Damage to melanoblast cells can result in disorders of pigmentation.
Researchers studied how melanoblasts differentiate into mature melanocytes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: melanocyte - a cell that produces melanin pigment in the skin

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'blastos' (germ, sprout).
