balas

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪləs, ˈbæləs / (AmE), / ˈbæləs, ˈbeɪləs / (BrE)
a pale rose-colored variety of spinel used as a gemstone, often called balas ruby.
rose-colored spinel variety (gemstone)

Examples

The necklace was adorned with a rare balas of exceptional clarity and brilliance.
Collectors prize balas for their delicate rose hues and historical significance.
She inherited a ring set with a balas that once belonged to her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinel - hard glassy mineral used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *balasius*, from Arabic *balakhsh*, after Badakhshan (a region in Central Asia known for these gems).
