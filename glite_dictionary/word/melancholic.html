melancholic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛlənˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or expressing sadness; tending to feel or convey deep sadness or sorrow
expressing or full of sadness

Examples

She gave a melancholic sigh and stared out the rain-speckled window.
The painting has a melancholic atmosphere that moves many viewers.
His melancholic mood worried his friends after hearing the bad news.

Synset Examples

growing more melancholy every hour
her melancholic smile
we acquainted him with the melancholy truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or sorrow; sad and pensive mood
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Greek *melankholikos*, from *melas* (black) + *khole* (bile), once thought to cause sadness.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlənˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
A person who often feels or tends to feel deep sadness or melancholy.
person prone to melancholy

Examples

As a melancholic, he found joy in solitary walks and quiet evenings.
The novel’s protagonist is a classic melancholic, haunted by memories of lost love.
Artists with a melancholic temperament often create deeply moving works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressive - a person suffering from depression
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

From Greek *melankholikos*, from *melas* (black) + *khole* (bile), relating to ancient belief in bodily humors.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10326262-n
Synset POS: n
