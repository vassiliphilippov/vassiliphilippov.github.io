Peperomia sandersii

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛpəˈroʊmiə ˌsændərˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌpɛpərˈəʊmiə ˌsændəˈsiː / (BrE)
A species of tropical houseplant from South America, known for its silvery striped, fleshy foliage.
houseplant with silvery striped foliage

Examples

She decorated her living room with a beautiful Peperomia sandersii in the corner.
The leaves of Peperomia sandersii are easily recognized by their silvery stripes and thick texture.
Gardeners often recommend Peperomia sandersii as an easy-to-care-for houseplant.

Etymology

Peperomia from Greek 'peperi' (pepper) and 'homoios' (resembling), referring to the resemblance to pepper plants. Species name 'sandersii' honors a person named Sanders.
