immune

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
not affected or influenced by something, especially something that others are usually affected by; usually followed by 'to'.
not affected by something

Examples

Some people seem immune to criticism no matter how harsh it is.
After years of disappointment, she felt almost immune to bad news.
He appeared completely immune to their attempts at persuasion.

Synset Examples

immune to persuasion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptible - easily affected or influenced by something

Etymology

From Latin immunis (exempt from public service, free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00072889-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
a person who is not susceptible to a particular infection or disease, typically due to previous exposure or vaccination
person not susceptible to infection

Examples

All of the immunes in the study had previously recovered from the virus.
The vaccine trial aimed to compare the response of immunes with that of non-immune participants.
After years working in infectious disease wards, she wondered if she might be an immune herself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptible - a person liable to be harmed or affected by a disease

Etymology

From Latin immunis (exempt, free from), especially regarding disease.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10219402-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
Relating to or protecting against a particular disease or infection; connected with immunity.
relating to immunity

Examples

The immune response protects the body against harmful pathogens.
Lymphocytes play a crucial role in the immune system.
Research focuses on how the body detects and destroys immune threats.

Synset Examples

the immune system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - the state of being immune, especially from disease

Etymology

From Latin immunis (exempt, free from), from in- (not) + munis (performing duties).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02756634-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
relating to or protected by immunity, especially against disease or infection.
protected from disease or infection

Examples

Because of her vaccination, she was immune to the measles virus.
After years of exposure, the scientists found themselves immune to the bacteria.
Some people are naturally immune to certain diseases due to their genetics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing, especially disease

Etymology

From Latin 'immunis' (exempt, free from public service).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02372182-s
Synset POS: s

5. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
protected or exempt, especially from an obligation or legal liability; not subject to something
protected or exempt (e.g., from an obligation)

Examples

As a diplomat, she was immune from prosecution under international law.
Certain nonprofit organizations are immune from paying local property taxes.
The witness was immune from legal action because of his agreement with authorities.

Synset Examples

immune from taxation as long as he resided in Bermuda
immune from criminal prosecution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exempt - free from an obligation or liability imposed on others
  • Antonym: liable - legally responsible; subject to an obligation

Etymology

From Latin immunis (exempt, free from public service or obligation).
