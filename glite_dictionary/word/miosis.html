miosis

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the reflex contraction of the pupil of the eye, typically in response to bright light or certain drugs
constriction of the pupil
medical

Examples

Prolonged exposure to bright sunlight can cause miosis as the eyes adjust to the intensity.
Certain medications may induce miosis, making it harder to see in low light.
The doctor observed miosis in response to the flashlight during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriasis - dilation of the pupil of the eye

Etymology

From Greek meiosis (a lessening), via New Latin

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the process of cell division in sexually reproducing organisms that results in four nuclei, each with half the chromosome number, forming reproductive cells such as gametes or spores
cell division forming gametes/spores
medical

Examples

During miosis, the chromosome number in the cells is reduced by half.
The process of miosis is essential for the formation of eggs and sperm in animals.
If miosis fails, gametes may end up with an abnormal number of chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mitosis - cell division where the nucleus divides into two genetically identical nuclei
  • Synonym: meiosis - alternative, more common spelling of the term miosis

Etymology

From Greek *meiōsis* (a lessening), referring to the reduction in chromosome number.
