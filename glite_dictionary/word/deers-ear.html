deer's-ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrzˌɪr / (AmE), / ˈdɪəzˌɪə / (BrE)
Any of several tall perennial herbs of the genus Frasera, native to warm, dry upland areas of California, Oregon, and Washington.
a tall perennial herb of the genus Frasera

Examples

The rocky hillside was dotted with clusters of deer's-ear in bloom.
Hikers spotted a rare species of deer's-ear during their trek through the dry uplands.
Botanists study the medicinal properties of deer's-ear found across Oregon.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the leaves, which are said to resemble a deer's ear.
