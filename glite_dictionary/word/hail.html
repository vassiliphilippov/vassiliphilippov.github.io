hail

1. NOUN

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
an enthusiastic or exultant greeting or welcome
enthusiastic greeting or welcome

Examples

The arrival of the hero was met with a joyous hail from the crowd.
He gave a cheerful hail as he entered the bustling room.
Their hail echoed across the hall, signaling the start of the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salutation - a greeting or expression of welcome
  • Antonym: farewell - an expression used to say goodbye

Etymology

From Old Norse 'heill' (health, prosperity), used as a greeting.

2. VERB

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall as small balls or lumps of ice from the sky.
rain as small ice balls

Examples

It started to hail as we were driving home from the mountains.
Last night, it hailed so heavily that some car windows cracked.
If it hails tomorrow, the picnic will be postponed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain - water that falls from clouds in drops
  • Degree Lower: snow - precipitation in the form of flakes of crystalline water ice

Etymology

From Old Norse 'heilig' (hail), related to Old English 'hægl'.

3. NOUN

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
Small balls or lumps of ice that fall from the sky, especially during storms, as a form of precipitation.
precipitation of ice pellets

Examples

The sudden hail damaged cars and roofs in the neighborhood.
We were caught in a storm of hail on our way home from school.
Large pieces of hail covered the ground after the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleet - precipitation of ice pellets mixed with rain or snow
  • Degree Lower: snow - soft white flakes of frozen water vapor falling from the sky
  • Degree Lower: rain - liquid water drops falling from the sky

Etymology

From Old English hægl (hail, hailstorm), of Germanic origin.

8. NOUN

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
a large number of objects or things being thrown or falling through the air at the same time, often in a forceful or rapid way
a rapid outpouring of objects

Examples

The protesters were met by a hail of stones from the angry crowd.
A hail of questions rained down on the speaker during the press conference.
The army advanced through a hail of bullets.

Synset Examples

a hail of pebbles
a hail of bullets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a concentrated outpouring, as of words or blows
  • Synonym: volley - a number of bullets, arrows, or other projectiles discharged at one time

Etymology

From Old Norse 'heilig' and Old English 'hagol', originally referring to frozen rain; figurative use for objects thrown forcefully dates from the 17th century.
