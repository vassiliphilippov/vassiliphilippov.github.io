repression

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) the defense mechanism by which unwanted impulses or ideas are excluded from conscious awareness to reduce anxiety.
the exclusion of distressing thoughts from consciousness
medical

Examples

Many psychoanalysts consider repression a key factor in the development of neuroses.
The patient's repression of traumatic memories was discussed in therapy sessions.
Dreams are sometimes interpreted as expressions of desires kept hidden by repression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - conscious inhibition of unwanted thoughts or urges

Etymology

From Latin repressio (a pressing back), from reprimere (to press back).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of subduing or holding back something or someone by force or constraint
the act of holding back or controlling

Examples

The government used military force for the repression of the protests.
He grew up in a household marked by emotional repression and strict discipline.
The repression of dissenting voices was swift and effective.

Synset Examples

his goal was the repression of insolence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - the act of stopping something by force
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone or something free

Etymology

From Latin 'repressio', from 'reprimere' (to press back) + '-ion'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
A state in which a group or individual is kept under control, often by force, preventing them from expressing themselves or exercising their rights.
forcible control or subjugation

Examples

The government maintained its power through decades of repression against opposition groups.
Many people fled the country to escape political repression and persecution.
After the fall of the regime, stories of religious repression began to emerge.

Synset Examples

the long repression of Christian sects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - the act of stopping something by force; suppressing
  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Latin repressio (a pressing back) from reprimere (to press back).
