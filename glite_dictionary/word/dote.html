to dote

1. VERB

/ doʊt / (AmE), / dəʊt / (BrE)
to show excessive or foolish affection or love for someone or something
show excessive love or affection

Examples

She absolutely dotes on her grandchildren, always bringing them gifts and treats.
He doted on his old dog, making sure she was always comfortable and happy.
The elderly couple dotes on each other after fifty years of marriage.

Synset Examples

Grandmother dotes on the twins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adore - to love and respect someone deeply
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Middle English 'doten' (to act foolishly), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ doʊt / (AmE), / dəʊt / (BrE)
to show signs of foolishness or senility due to old age
become senile or foolish with age
archaic

Examples

In his final years, the old king began to dote and forget the names of his closest advisors.
Literature often portrays ancient rulers who dote and lose touch with reality.
They feared their father would dote as he aged, but his mind remained sharp.

Etymology

From Middle English doten (to be foolish), of uncertain origin, perhaps from Middle Dutch doten.
