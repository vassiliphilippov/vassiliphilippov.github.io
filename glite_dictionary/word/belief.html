belief

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈlif / (AmE), / bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
an idea or impression that someone accepts as true or real, especially without certain proof
an accepted idea or impression

Examples

Her belief in his honesty never wavered, even when others doubted him.
It was only a belief, not something she could prove to be true.
Sometimes a simple belief can give people hope during difficult times.

Synset Examples

his impression of her was favorable
what are your feelings about the crisis?
it strengthened my belief in his sincerity
I had a feeling that she was lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Old French *croire* (to believe), from Latin *credere* (to trust, believe).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlif / (AmE), / bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
an acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof
acceptance that something is true

Examples

Her belief in the team's success inspired everyone.
Many people share the belief that kindness is important.
His belief was challenged by new scientific discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Old French *croire* via Anglo-French *beleave*, influenced by Middle English *beleve* (faith, trust). Related to 'believe'.
