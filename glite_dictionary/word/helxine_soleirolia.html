Helxine soleirolia

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈksaɪni soʊˌleɪroʊˈliə / (AmE), / hɛlˈksaɪni səʊˌleɪrəʊˈliːə / (BrE)
A small, creeping plant with moss-like, rounded leaves, commonly grown as a groundcover or houseplant, native to Corsica.
creeping plant with moss-like leaves

Examples

Helxine soleirolia makes an attractive ground cover for shaded gardens.
Many terrariums feature Helxine soleirolia for its lush, moss-like foliage.
Gardeners often use Helxine soleirolia in hanging baskets for a trailing effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soleirolia soleirolii - synonym; scientific name for the same plant species
  • Synonym: baby's tears - common name for Helxine soleirolia

Etymology

From New Latin Helxine (the genus name) and soleirolia, honoring Joseph-François Soleirol, a French engineer and plant collector.
