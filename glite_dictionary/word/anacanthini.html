Anacanthini

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈkænθəˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌanəˈkanθɪni / (BrE)
a group of soft-finned fish, sometimes considered equivalent to the order Gadiformes in certain classifications
group of soft-finned fish

Examples

The Anacanthini are known for their lack of spiny fins, setting them apart from many other fish.
Scientists debated whether certain deep-sea species belong in the Anacanthini group or should be classified differently.
Modern classifications sometimes merge Anacanthini with the Gadiformes due to similarities in anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gadiformes - the order of fish including cods, haddocks, and their relatives

Etymology

From Greek ana- (without) + akantha (thorn), referring to the absence of spiny fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈkænθɪˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌanəˈkanθɪni / (BrE)
a former order of fishes, including many types of cod and their relatives, sometimes equivalent to Gadiformes in certain classifications
order of fishes including cod and relatives

Examples

The Anacanthini include several important commercial fish species such as cod and haddock.
Modern classifications often merge Anacanthini with the order Gadiformes.
Scientists once classified cod in the order Anacanthini, though taxonomy has changed over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gadiformes - order of ray-finned fishes including cod, haddock, and their relatives

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'akantha' (thorn, spine), referring to the absence of certain fin spines.
