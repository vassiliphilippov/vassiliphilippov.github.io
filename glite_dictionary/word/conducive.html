conducive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈduːsɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdjuːsɪv / (BrE)
making a certain outcome or situation more likely; helping to bring about something.
making something more likely to happen

Examples

The teacher created a classroom atmosphere that was particularly conducive to learning.
Low lighting is not conducive to reading for long periods.
Regular exercise is highly conducive to maintaining good health.

Synset Examples

working conditions are not conducive to productivity
the seaport was a contributing factor in the growth of the city
a contributory factor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contributory - helping to cause or bring about something
  • Antonym: unfavorable - not conducive; making something less likely

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'conducivus', from Latin 'conducere' (to lead together, contribute).
