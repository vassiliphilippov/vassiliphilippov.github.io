Corypha

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈriːfə / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪfə / (BrE)
a genus of large fan palms native to tropical Asia and Australia, known for their huge leaves and tall flower stalks
genus of large tropical fan palms

Examples

The Corypha palm is notable for its massive fan-shaped leaves used in traditional crafts.
Several species of Corypha are cultivated for their edible sap and fibers.
The towering Corypha trees dominate the landscape in parts of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *Corypha*, from Greek *koryphe* (summit, top), referring to the palm's tall structure.

2. NOUN

/ kɔːˈriːfə / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪfə / (BrE)
A genus of large fan palms native to tropical Asia and Australia, known for their massive leaves and tall trunks.
genus of large tropical fan palms

Examples

The leaves of the Corypha are among the largest of any palm species.
Botanists recently discovered a new Corypha palm in Southeast Asia.
The towering Corypha dominates the landscape along the riverbank.

Etymology

From Greek koryphe (κορυφή) meaning 'summit' or 'top', referring to the crown of the palm.
