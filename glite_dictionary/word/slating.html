slating

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a severely critical review or attack, especially in the press or media
severely critical attack; harsh review
british

Examples

The movie received a slating from almost every major newspaper.
Her latest novel got a thorough slating by the critics.
After such a public slating, the politician was forced to rethink his approach.

Synset Examples

the reviewers gave his book a sound slating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pan - to subject to harsh criticism
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'slate' (to criticize harshly) with the noun-forming suffix -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
thin, flat layers of rock, especially slate, used for covering roofs
thin layers of rock for roofing

Examples

The old cottage had a roof made of dark slating that had lasted for over a century.
Traditional slating provides both durability and an appealing appearance for historic buildings.
Repairs to the slating were needed after the severe winter storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slate - a fine-grained gray, green, or bluish metamorphic rock, easily split into smooth, flat pieces

Etymology

From 'slate' (type of rock) + -ing (forming a noun from a verb or material).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or act of covering a roof with slates
covering a roof with slates

Examples

The slating of the cottage took several days to complete.
Traditional slating requires both skill and experience to ensure a watertight roof.
After the storm, repairs included new slating on the old church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roofing - the work of building or repairing roofs

Etymology

From 'slate' + '-ing', indicating an action.
