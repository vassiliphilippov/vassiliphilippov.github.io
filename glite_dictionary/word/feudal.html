feudal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of feudalism or the feudal system.
relating to feudalism

Examples

The country retained a feudal structure well into the nineteenth century.
His family owned vast estates under the feudal system.
Medieval Europe was dominated by feudal relationships between lords and vassals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feudalism - the social system in medieval Europe based on landholding
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times, as contrasted with the remote past

Etymology

From Medieval Latin feodalis, from feodum (fee, fief).
