Xyphophorus helleri

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌfɒfəˈrɔːrəs hɛˈlɛraɪ / (AmE), / zaɪˌfɒfəˈrɒrəs hɛˈlɛrɪ / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish native to Central America, noted for its long sword-like tail and commonly kept in aquariums.
species of freshwater aquarium fish

Examples

The bright colors of the Xyphophorus helleri make it a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts.
Many beginner aquarists start with hardy fish like the Xyphophorus helleri.
The male Xyphophorus helleri can be identified by its extended lower tail fin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordtail - common name for Xyphophorus helleri, a popular aquarium fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Xyphophorus' from Greek 'xiphos' (sword) + 'phoros' (bearing); species 'helleri' named after the botanist Karl Bartholomaeus Heller.
