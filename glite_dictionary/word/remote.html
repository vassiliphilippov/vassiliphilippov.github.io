remote

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
(of a chance or possibility) very unlikely to happen or occur.
unlikely to happen

Examples

There is only a remote possibility of snow this weekend.
His chances of winning the lottery are extremely remote.
It’s a remote risk, but we should be prepared nonetheless.

Synset Examples

an outside chance
a remote possibility
a remote contingency

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: possible - able to happen

Etymology

From Latin remotus, past participle of removere (to remove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01415921-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
(of a place) far away from cities or populated areas; inaccessible and sparsely populated.
far from populated areas; isolated

Examples

They live in a remote mountain village with no paved roads.
The expedition set out toward a remote region only a few had ever visited.
Medical supplies are difficult to deliver to such remote communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places, buildings, or people
  • Antonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered

Etymology

From Latin remotus (removed, distant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020141-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
a device that is used to control a machine or apparatus, such as a television, from a distance
device for controlling a machine from a distance

Examples

Can you hand me the remote?
The remote isn't working, so I can't change the channel.
She bought a universal remote to operate all her devices.

Synset Examples

he lost the remote for his TV

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote control - a device that transmits signals to control a machine from a distance
