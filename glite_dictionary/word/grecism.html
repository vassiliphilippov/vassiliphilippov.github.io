Grecism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡriː.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or idiom borrowed from Greek into another language, especially used for literary effect.
word or idiom borrowed from Greek

Examples

The author's use of a Grecism added an exotic flavor to the narrative.
Many scientific terms in English are actually Grecisms borrowed directly from ancient Greek.
Her essay analyzes the presence of Grecism in early modern European literature.

Synset Examples

A key to identifying the channel of transmission of a Grecism to Yiddish is the geography of the Grecism within Yiddish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latinism - A word or expression borrowed from Latin
  • Synonym: Hellenism - A word or phrase characteristic of Greek language; Greek idiom

Etymology

From French 'gré­cisme', from Latin 'Graecismus', from Greek 'Graikismos' (imitation of Greek).
