brake

1. VERB

/ breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to make a vehicle go slower or stop by using a brake
slow or stop using a brake

Examples

The driver had to brake hard to avoid hitting the cyclist.
Please brake gently when approaching the intersection.
She quickly braked when the traffic light turned red.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: to accelerate - to increase the speed of a vehicle
