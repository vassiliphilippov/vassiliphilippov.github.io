offence

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
the act of attacking an enemy, especially in military or sporting contexts
act of attacking an enemy
british

Examples

The general ordered his troops to take the offence at dawn.
During the game, our team's offence was much stronger than our defence.
Launching the offence early caught the enemy by surprise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defence - the act of protecting against attack

Etymology

From Old French *offense*, from Latin *offensa* (a striking against).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
An act that breaks a law or rule, or is considered morally wrong.
violation of a law or moral rule

Examples

Committing an offence can result in a fine or imprisonment under the law.
He apologized for causing offence with his insensitive remarks.
Driving over the speed limit is considered a minor traffic offence in most countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act punishable by law; illegal activity
  • Degree Lower: misdemeanor - a minor wrongdoing or criminal act
  • Degree Higher: felony - a serious crime, typically punishable by imprisonment

Etymology

From Old French *offense*, from Latin *offensa* (a striking against, injury).

3. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
the team or players in a sport who have possession of the ball or puck and are trying to score points against the opposing team
team with possession and trying to score (sports)
british

Examples

The coach praised the offence for their aggressive play in the second half.
Our team's offence struggled to break through the opposing defense last night.
She is one of the top scorers on the offence this season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the team attempting to prevent the other team from scoring

5. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A lack of politeness or respect that wounds the feelings of others; an act or remark that causes someone to feel insulted or hurt.
an act that causes hurt or insult

Examples

Please forgive me if I gave you offence with my comments.
The remark was not meant as an offence, but she took it personally.
He spoke bluntly, with little concern for causing offence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a remark or action that is rude or offensive
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration or regard for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'offense', from Latin 'offensa' (a striking against).
