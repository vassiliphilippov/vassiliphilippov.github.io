pairing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of bringing a male and female together for reproduction, especially in animals or plants
the act of matching a male and female for reproduction

Examples

The wildlife reserve closely monitors the pairing of endangered birds to boost their numbers.
Selective pairing of plants can produce offspring with desirable traits.
Seasonal changes often trigger pairing behavior in many species.

Synset Examples

the casual couplings of adolescents
the mating of some species occurs only in the spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mating - the act of animals coming together to reproduce

Etymology

From pair + -ing, patterning after verbs like "pair" and "pairing".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00848970-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈperɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of grouping or matching people or things in pairs
act of grouping into pairs

Examples

The chef recommends a wine pairing for each course of the meal.
The teachers organized a pairing of students for the science project.
The pairing of flavors in this dish is truly innovative.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pair - a set of two things considered as a unit
  • Synonym: coupling - the act of joining or linking together

Etymology

From pair + -ing, meaning the action or process of making pairs.

Synset POS: n
