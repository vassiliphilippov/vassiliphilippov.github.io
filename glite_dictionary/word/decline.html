decline

1. VERB

/ dɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to become gradually worse in quality, health, or condition
become worse over time

Examples

After years of neglect, the old building began to decline rapidly.
Her health continued to decline despite the doctors' best efforts.
If economic conditions decline, unemployment rates may rise.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to worsen - to become worse
  • Antonym: to improve - to become or make better

Etymology

From Old French *decliner*, from Latin *declinare* (to turn aside, to bend down).
