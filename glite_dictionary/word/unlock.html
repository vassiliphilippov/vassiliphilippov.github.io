to unlock

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒk / (BrE)
to open or release by using a key, code, or other means to remove a lock
open or release by removing a lock

Examples

I always unlock the front door before my family arrives home.
She forgot to unlock her bike, so she had to wait for help.
The manager will unlock the office at nine o'clock sharp.

Synset Examples

unlock the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lock - to fasten or secure with a lock

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (not) + 'locian' (to lock).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01351387-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒk / (BrE)
(of a door, window, device, etc.) to become open or no longer fastened with a lock, especially without using a key or similar device.
to become unfastened or open (intransitive use)

Examples

When the timer reached zero, the vault unlocked automatically.
With a quiet click, the door unlocked in the middle of the night.
The phone unlocked after I entered the correct passcode.

Synset Examples

The door unlocked from the inside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lock - to become fastened or closed with a lock (intransitive)
  • Degree Higher: open - to make or become no longer closed

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + lock.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00220389-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒk / (BrE)
to set free or release from confinement, restraint, or restriction
set free; release from confinement

Examples

The new policy will unlock opportunities for local businesses.
She hopes the therapy will unlock her true potential.
The scientist’s discovery may unlock the secrets of aging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - to release from confinement or restraint
  • Antonym: lock - to secure or fasten with a lock

Etymology

From Old English un- (not) + locian (to fasten with a lock).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01513921-v
Synset POS: v
