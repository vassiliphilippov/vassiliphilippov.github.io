award

1. VERB

/ əˈwɔrd / (AmE), / əˈwɔːd / (BrE)
to give something, especially as an official honor or reward for achievement
to give as an honor or reward

Examples

The judge will award damages to the injured party if the case is successful.
Each year, the foundation awards scholarships to outstanding students.
They awarded him a medal in recognition of his bravery.

Synset Examples

bestow honors and prizes at graduation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - to give something, especially officially or formally
  • Antonym: refuse - to decline or not give something requested or offered

Etymology

From Old Northern French *awarder*, from Late Latin *adwardare* (to assign by decree).

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ əˈwɔrd / (AmE), / əˈwɔːd / (BrE)
an official decision by a court granting money or other compensation to a party in a legal case
grant given by a court
legal

Examples

The court's award covered the plaintiff's medical expenses and lost wages.
After a lengthy trial, an award of damages was granted to the injured employee.
The judge announced the award at the end of the civil lawsuit.

Synset Examples

he criticized the awarding of compensation by the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - a decision made by a court
  • Synonym: settlement - an official agreement intended to resolve a dispute or lawsuit

Etymology

From Old Northern French *eswarder* (to decide), from Proto-Germanic roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00088483-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈwɔrd / (AmE), / əˈwɔːd / (BrE)
a prize or other item given to recognize achievement, victory, or excellence, especially in a competition or contest
prize or honor for achievement

Examples

She won the highest award for her groundbreaking research.
The film was honored with an award for best screenplay at the festival.
After the competition, each participant received an award in recognition of their efforts.

Synset Examples

the prize was a free trip to Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prize - something given as a reward to the winner of a competition
  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for service or merit
  • Antonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a rule or law; opposite of an award

Etymology

From Old French 'eswarder', from es- (out) + warder (to guard), later influenced by legal terminology.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ əˈwɔrd / (AmE), / əˈwɔːd / (BrE)
to give something, such as a prize, money, or rights, as a result of a decision or on the basis of merit
give as a result of judgment or merit

Examples

The court decided to award damages to the injured party.
Each year, the committee awards scholarships to outstanding students.
She was awarded first prize for her painting.

Synset Examples

the referee awarded a free kick to the team
the jury awarded a million dollars to the plaintiff
Funds are granted to qualified researchers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - to give something, especially officially or formally, to someone
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'ewarder', variant of Old French 'eswarder', from 'es-', out + 'warder', to guard, watch.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
