fanfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈfænˌfeə / (BrE)
a short lively tune played on brass instruments, typically to introduce something or someone important
short lively tune on brass instruments

Examples

The ceremony began with a triumphant fanfare that echoed through the hall.
The king's arrival was announced by a brilliant fanfare from the royal trumpeters.
Every new product launch was accompanied by a dramatic fanfare of brass.

Synset Examples

he entered to a flourish of trumpets
her arrival was greeted with a rousing fanfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourish - a fanfare of trumpets or showy display

Etymology

From French 'fanfare' (trumpet flourish), likely of Spanish origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈfanˌfɛə / (BrE)
A showy or elaborate display intended to attract attention.
showy outward display

Examples

The event opened with much fanfare, but little substance.
She launched her new product with great fanfare and media attention.
The new policy was introduced without any fanfare or public announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - an act of exhibiting or showing something
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest or unassuming

Etymology

From French 'fanfare' (boast, show), possibly of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06902380-n
Synset POS: n
