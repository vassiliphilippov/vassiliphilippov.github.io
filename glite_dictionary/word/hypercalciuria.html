hypercalciuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌkælsiˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəkælsɪˈjʊərɪə / (BrE)
the presence of abnormally high levels of calcium in the urine, often associated with medical conditions such as hyperparathyroidism or osteoporosis
high calcium levels in the urine (medical)
medical

Examples

Hypercalciuria is a common finding in patients with recurrent kidney stones.
The doctor ordered tests to determine whether the patient had hypercalciuria or another metabolic disorder.
Treatment of hypercalciuria may involve dietary changes and medication.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'calcium' + 'uria' (urine).
