radiochemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with radioactive substances and their chemical properties and reactions
chemistry of radioactive substances

Examples

She decided to specialize in radiochemistry after taking an introductory physics course.
Research in radiochemistry has advanced our understanding of radioactive decay processes.
The laboratory is well-equipped for experiments in radiochemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear chemistry - the branch of chemistry dealing with changes in atomic nuclei

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radiation) + chemistry.
