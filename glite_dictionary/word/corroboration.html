corroboration

1. NOUN

/ kəˌrɑːbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌrɒbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
evidence or information that confirms or supports a statement, theory, or finding, especially through documentary proof.
evidence confirming a statement or fact

Examples

The witness provided corroboration for the defendant's alibi during the trial.
Without proper corroboration, the claims made in the report cannot be accepted.
The investigation relied on documentary corroboration to support the prosecution’s case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confirmation - the act of confirming something as true or valid

Etymology

From Latin corroboratio (strengthening), from corroborare (to strengthen).
