Kublai Khan

1. PROPN

/ ˈkuːblaɪ ˈkɑn / (AmE), / ˈkuːblaɪ ˈkɑːn / (BrE)
(1215–1294) The Mongolian emperor of China and grandson of Genghis Khan, who completed the conquest of China, founded the Yuan dynasty, and established his capital at what is now Beijing.
Mongolian emperor who founded the Yuan dynasty

Examples

The reign of Kublai Khan marked a turning point in Chinese history.
Marco Polo described the grandeur of Kublai Khan's court in his writings.
The Yuan dynasty, established by Kublai Khan, significantly influenced Chinese culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Genghis Khan - founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire

Etymology

From Mongolian 'Khubilai Khaan', with 'Khaan' meaning 'Khan' or ruler.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkuːblaɪ ˈkɑːn / (AmE), / ˈkuːblaɪ ˈkɑːn / (BrE)
(c. 1215–1294) Mongolian emperor of China, grandson of Genghis Khan, who completed the conquest of China, founded the Yuan dynasty, and established his capital at present-day Beijing.
Mongolian emperor and founder of China's Yuan dynasty

Examples

The reign of Kublai Khan marked a turning point in Chinese history.
Marco Polo famously visited the court of Kublai Khan in the 13th century.
Kublai Khan established the Yuan dynasty and promoted trade along the Silk Road.

Etymology

From Mongolian Хубилай Хаан (Khubilai Khaan), meaning 'Kublai the Khan (ruler)'.
