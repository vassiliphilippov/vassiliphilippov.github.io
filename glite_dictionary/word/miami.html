Miami

1. PROPN

/ maɪˈæmi / (AmE), / maɪˈæmi / (BrE)
A major city and resort in southeastern Florida, known for its beaches and vibrant cultural diversity.
city in southeastern Florida, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Miami each year to enjoy its famous beaches and nightlife.
After moving to Miami, she quickly adapted to the city's warm climate and lively atmosphere.
Miami has become a cultural melting pot, attracting people from all over the world.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈæmi / (AmE), / maɪˈæmi / (BrE)
A member of an extinct Native American people, part of the Algonquian linguistic group, who formerly lived in northern Indiana and southern Michigan.
member of an extinct Native American people

Examples

The Miami were skilled farmers and hunters before their dispersal in the 19th century.
Artifacts from the Miami have been found throughout northern Indiana.
She has studied the culture and language of the Miami people extensively.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Algonquian - group of indigenous peoples of North America

Etymology

From the Miami people's autonym in their own language, Myaamia.
