decisively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner showing firmness or determination in reaching a decision or taking action.
with firmness or determination

Examples

She answered the question decisively, leaving no room for doubt.
The general acted decisively in the heat of battle.
He put an end to the argument decisively with a well-supported statement.

Synset Examples

‘I will come along,’ she said decisively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - with a steady or strong manner

Etymology

From 'decisive' + '-ly'; 'decisive' from Latin 'decidere' (to decide).

2. ADV

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
to a degree or in a way that leaves no doubt; indisputably or conclusively
in a way that settles something completely

Examples

The court's decision decisively ended the long-standing dispute between the two parties.
Her strong leadership decisively influenced the direction of the project.
The team decisively outperformed their opponents in every aspect of the match.

Synset Examples

the Fisher Act of 1918 decisively raised their status and pay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusively - in a way that is final or decisive
  • Synonym: indisputably - in a way that cannot be challenged or doubted
  • Antonym: tentatively - in a way that is uncertain or lacks confidence

Etymology

From 'decisive' + '-ly', with 'decisive' from Latin 'decidere' (to decide).

Source: WordNet

3. ADV

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that settles an issue with certainty; conclusively.
in a conclusive or final manner

Examples

She decisively rejected the proposal after reviewing all the evidence.
The team decisively won the match, leaving no doubt about the outcome.
He decisively closed the discussion by addressing every concern raised.

Synset Examples

the voted settled the argument decisively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusively - in a way that settles a matter conclusively
  • Antonym: tentatively - in a way that is hesitant or uncertain

Etymology

From 'decisive' + '-ly'. 'Decisive' from French 'décisif', from Latin 'decidere' (to decide).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
