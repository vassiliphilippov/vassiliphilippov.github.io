quiz

1. NOUN

/ kwɪz / (AmE), / kwɪz / (BrE)
a short test or examination, typically consisting of a few questions, often given to students
short test with few questions

Examples

The teacher gave us a surprise quiz on the chapter we studied yesterday.
Please complete the online quiz before tomorrow's class.
He scored full marks on the general knowledge quiz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - a set of questions or problems to assess knowledge or ability
  • Synonym: exam - a formal test of a person's knowledge or proficiency

Etymology

Originated in late 18th century, possibly from English dialect quizzing glass (a monocle).

2. VERB

/ kwɪz / (AmE), / kwɪz / (BrE)
to question someone in order to test their knowledge about a subject
test by questioning

Examples

The teacher will quiz the students on last week's reading tomorrow.
Before the interview, my friend quizzed me with practice questions to help me prepare.
Parents sometimes quiz their children on math facts to reinforce learning.

Synset Examples

The teacher tests us every week
We got quizzed on French irregular verbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - to examine someone's knowledge to assess their understanding
  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or question to determine ability or knowledge

Etymology

Originally a slang term of unknown origin, first used in late 18th century English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00788120-v
Synset POS: v
