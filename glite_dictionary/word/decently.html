decently

1. ADV

/ ˈdiːsəntli / (AmE), / ˈdiːsəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that is decent; in a proper, respectable, or acceptable way.
in a proper or respectable manner

Examples

She always tries to dress decently for work.
The house was decently furnished and comfortable.
Please speak decently to your elders, even if you're upset.

Synset Examples

they don't know how to dress decently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecently - in a way that is not decent; improperly

Etymology

From 'decent' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈdiːsəntli / (AmE), / ˈdiːsəntli / (BrE)
in a suitable or proper manner; appropriately or correctly
in a proper or suitable way

Examples

He always tries to finish his work decently and on time.
The children behaved decently during the ceremony, much to their parents' relief.
If you treat people decently, they are more likely to respect you.

Synset Examples

please do your job properly!
can't you carry me decent?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: properly - in a way that is correct or suitable
  • Synonym: suitably - in an appropriate or fitting manner
  • Antonym: improperly - in a way that is not suitable or proper

Etymology

From Latin 'decentia' (becoming, proper) with the English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
