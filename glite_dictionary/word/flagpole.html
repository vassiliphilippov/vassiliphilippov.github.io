flagpole

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈflæɡˌpəʊl / (BrE)
A surveying instrument consisting of a straight rod painted with alternating red and white bands, each one foot wide, used for sighting by surveyors.
striped surveying rod

Examples

The surveyor positioned the flagpole at the corner of the site for measurement.
During the land survey, two assistants held up flagpoles so their locations could be marked.
The red and white bands on the flagpole made it easy to spot from a distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranging rod - a pole used for marking positions in surveying

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈflæɡˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a tall pole on which a flag is displayed or flown
pole for displaying a flag

Examples

The national flag waved proudly atop the flagpole outside the school.
She watched as the workers carefully raised the company banner up the flagpole.
A new flagpole was installed in the town square for the upcoming festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - a long stick or pole, especially one used as a support or symbol

Etymology

From 'flag' (a piece of cloth used as a symbol) + 'pole' (a long, slender object).
