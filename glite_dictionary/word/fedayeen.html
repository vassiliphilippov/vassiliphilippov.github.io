fedayeen

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈjin / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈjiːn / (BrE)
(plural) Arab guerrilla fighters, especially those who engaged in attacks against Israel.
Arab guerrilla fighters, esp. against Israel

Examples

The fedayeen launched a series of attacks across the border during the conflict.
During the early 1950s, Israeli forces clashed repeatedly with fedayeen fighters.
The book details the history and motivations of the fedayeen movements in the Middle East.

Etymology

From Arabic فدائيون‎ (fidāʾiyyūn), plural of فدائي‎ (fidāʾī, 'one who sacrifices himself').
