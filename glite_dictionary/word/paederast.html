paederast

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːdəˌræst / (AmE), / ˈpiːdəræst / (BrE)
a man who engages in sexual activity with a boy, especially as the passive partner; a term used historically for adult males involved with underage boys
man engaged in sexual acts with a boy (historical)
archaic offensive

Examples

The term paederast is now considered archaic and highly offensive in modern discourse.
In ancient Greek society, a paederast might have played a defined social role.
Legal texts in the 19th century used the word paederast to describe criminal acts.

Etymology

From Greek 'paiderastēs' (lover of boys), from 'pais' (boy) + 'erastēs' (lover), via Latin 'pæderasta'.
