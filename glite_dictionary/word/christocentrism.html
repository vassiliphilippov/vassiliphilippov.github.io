Christocentrism

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪstəˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌkraɪstəʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or practice of placing Christ at the center of one's beliefs, teachings, or theological system
placing Christ at the center of theology or belief

Examples

Many theologians debate the merits and limitations of Christocentrism in contemporary Christian thought.
Some denominations emphasize Christocentrism in their worship and teachings above all other doctrines.
The concept of Christocentrism can shape ethical practices and theological interpretations within a religious community.

Synset Examples

The immediate question is whether Christocentrism inculcates an inherently imbalanced trinitarianism.

Etymology

From Christ + -centrism (from Latin centrum, center, and -ism)
