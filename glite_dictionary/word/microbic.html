microbic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈkroʊbɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈkrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
relating to, involving, or caused by microbes (microscopic organisms such as bacteria, fungi, or viruses)
of or caused by microbes

Examples

The doctor explained that the infection was of microbic origin.
Scientists study microbic activity in soil to understand nutrient cycles.
He emphasized the importance of hygiene in controlling microbic contamination.

Synset Examples

microbial warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microbial - relating to or characteristic of microbes; alternative adjective form

Etymology

From French *microbe* (microbe) + -ic.
