bursa

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːsə / (AmE), / ˈbɜːsə / (BrE)
a small fluid-filled sac in the body, especially one between bones and tendons near a joint, that reduces friction and cushions movement
fluid-filled sac in joints
medical

Examples

The bursa in his shoulder became inflamed after weeks of intense exercise.
Doctors injected medication directly into the swollen bursa to relieve pain.
Bursitis occurs when a bursa becomes irritated or infected.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bursa (purse), referring to its pouch-like shape.
