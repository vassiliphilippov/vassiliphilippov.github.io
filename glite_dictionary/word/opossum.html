opossum

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsəm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
A small, furry, tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia, with a long usually prehensile tail.
small Australian tree-dwelling marsupial

Examples

The opossum used its prehensile tail to hang from a low tree branch.
We saw a family of opossums foraging in the Australian bush at dusk.
An injured opossum was rescued by local wildlife carers and released after recovery.

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian (Powhatan) *aposoum* (white dog or beast).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized nocturnal marsupial found in the Americas, with a hairless prehensile tail and an ability to climb trees.
nocturnal American marsupial with a prehensile tail

Examples

An opossum was spotted rummaging through the garbage cans last night.
The opossum curled its tail around the tree branch as it climbed higher.
During the camping trip, we saw a mother opossum carrying her babies on her back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: possum - marsupial found in Australia and New Guinea
  • Synonym: marsupial - animal of a group that carries and nurses young in a pouch

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *apasum* (white animal).
