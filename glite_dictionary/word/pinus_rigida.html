Pinus rigida

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs rɪˈdʒɪdə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs rɪˈdʒɪdə / (BrE)
a species of large pine tree native to the eastern United States and southeastern Canada, known for its three-needled leaves; also called pitch pine
species of North American pine tree

Examples

The Pinus rigida is well adapted to poor, sandy soils in its native range.
Many restoration projects in New Jersey use Pinus rigida to recreate natural pine barrens.
Botanists studied the cones and bark of the Pinus rigida for signs of disease.

Etymology

Latin: Pinus (pine) + rigida (rigid, stiff), referring to the tree's stiff needles and wood.
