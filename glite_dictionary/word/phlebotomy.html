phlebotomy

1. NOUN

/ flɛˈbɑtəmi / (AmE), / flɪˈbɒtəmi / (BrE)
the surgical incision into a vein, especially for the treatment of certain blood disorders or for drawing blood
surgical incision into a vein
medical

Examples

The nurse performed a phlebotomy to relieve the patient's symptoms.
Modern medicine uses phlebotomy primarily for treating hemochromatosis and other specific blood conditions.
Regular phlebotomy sessions are essential for some patients with excessive iron levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venesection - the act of opening a vein for letting blood
  • Synonym: bloodletting - withdrawal of blood from a patient to prevent or cure illness

Etymology

From Greek 'phlebotomia': 'phleps, phlebos' (vein) + 'tomia' (cutting).
