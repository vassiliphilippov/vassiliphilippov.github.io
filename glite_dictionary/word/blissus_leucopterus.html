Blissus leucopterus

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪsəs luːˈkɑptərəs / (AmE), / ˈblɪsəs luːˈkɒptərəs / (BrE)
A small black-and-white true bug, also known as the chinch bug, that feeds on cereal grasses and can be a pest in agriculture.
a black-and-white insect pest of cereal grasses

Examples

Farmers worry about damage caused by the Blissus leucopterus in wheat and corn fields.
The presence of Blissus leucopterus can significantly reduce crop yields.
Researchers study ways to control Blissus leucopterus populations in agricultural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinch bug - common name for Blissus leucopterus, an agricultural pest insect

Etymology

From Latin: 'Blissus' (genus name), 'leucopterus' meaning 'white-winged'.
