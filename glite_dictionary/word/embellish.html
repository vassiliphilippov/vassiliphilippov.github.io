to embellish

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃ / (BrE)
to make something more beautiful or attractive by decorating it
make (something) more beautiful by decoration

Examples

The artist embellished the plain room with colorful tapestries and paintings.
She embellished her dress with sparkling sequins for the party.
The chef embellished the dish with edible flowers and fresh herbs.

Synset Examples

Flowers adorned the tables everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it
  • Synonym: to adorn - to add beauty or ornamentation to something

Etymology

From Old French embellir, from Latin bellus (beautiful).

2. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃ / (BrE)
to make something more attractive by adding decorative details or features
decorate by adding details

Examples

The artist used bright colors to embellish the mural on the wall.
She likes to embellish her stories with dramatic details.
He decided to embellish the wedding cake with edible flowers.

Synset Examples

Decorate the room for the party
beautify yourself for the special day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding things to it
  • Synonym: to beautify - to improve the appearance of something or someone

Etymology

From Old French 'embellir', from 'bel' (beautiful), from Latin 'bellus' (pretty, handsome).

4. VERB

to make something more attractive by adding decorative details or features; to enhance with details, often in a way that improves or exaggerates the original
make more attractive by adding details

Examples

The artist decided to embellish the mural with gold leaf accents.
He tends to embellish the facts when telling anecdotes to make them more entertaining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - add or apply adornments or ornamentation

From Old French embellir, from Latin bellus (pretty, handsome).
