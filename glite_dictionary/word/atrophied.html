atrophied

1. ADJ

/ ˈætrəˌfid / (AmE), / ˈætrəˌfiːd / (BrE)
(of an organ or part of the body) diminished in size or strength as a result of disease, injury, or lack of use.
shrunken or weakened from disease or disuse
medical

Examples

After months in a cast, his atrophied leg muscles needed intensive physiotherapy.
The patient's atrophied arm made it difficult to perform daily tasks.
Prolonged immobility can leave some muscles atrophied and weak.

Synset Examples

partial paralysis resulted in an atrophied left arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasted - reduced in strength or size due to disease or lack of use
  • Antonym: healthy - in good physical condition, not affected by disease or decline

Etymology

Past participle of 'atrophy', from Greek 'atrophia' meaning 'a wasting away'.
