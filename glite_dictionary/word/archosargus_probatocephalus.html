Archosargus probatocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.koʊˈsɑːr.ɡəs proʊˌbæ.təˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəʊˈsɑː.ɡəs prəʊˌbæt.ə(ʊ)ˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (BrE)
a large food fish of the eastern coast of the United States and Mexico, also known as the sheepshead, recognized for its deep, laterally compressed body and prominent teeth
a large Atlantic food fish (sheepshead)

Examples

Fishermen on the Gulf Coast often catch Archosargus probatocephalus for its firm, white flesh.
The teeth of Archosargus probatocephalus resemble those of a sheep, which is how it earned its common name.
Many anglers consider Archosargus probatocephalus to be a challenging and rewarding fish to catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheepshead - common name of Archosargus probatocephalus, a food fish found along the Atlantic coast

Etymology

From Greek 'archos' (chief) + 'sargos' (a kind of fish) and Greek 'probatocephalus' meaning 'sheep head', referring to its teeth.
