reprehension

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing strong criticism or disapproval
strong criticism or censure

Examples

The harsh reprehension from his supervisor left him feeling discouraged.
Despite public reprehension, the politician refused to apologize for his remarks.
She accepted her teacher's reprehension with humility and promised to improve.

Synset Examples

he had to take the rebuke with a smile on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp criticism or disapproval
  • Antonym: commendation - an act of praising or commending

Etymology

From Latin reprehensio (blame, censure), from reprehendere (to blame).
