Columbidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmbɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbɪdiː / (BrE)
The biological family of birds that includes doves and pigeons.
the family of doves and pigeons

Examples

The Columbidae family includes more than 300 species distributed worldwide.
Researchers have documented interesting mating behaviors among members of Columbidae.
Extensive urban adaptation is seen in several Columbidae species, especially feral pigeons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - any of the smaller species within Columbidae
  • Synonym: pigeon - any species of the family Columbidae, especially larger ones

Etymology

From Latin *columba* (dove) with scientific suffix *-idae* indicating a family.
