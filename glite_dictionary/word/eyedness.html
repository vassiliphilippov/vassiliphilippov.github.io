eyedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the tendency to prefer one eye over the other, especially in tasks such as aiming or looking through a lens
preference for one eye over the other

Examples

His strong eyedness was evident when he always used his right eye to look through the camera.
Tests for eyedness can help in determining vision dominance in sports like archery.
Understanding your eyedness may improve performance in activities that require precise aiming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handedness - the tendency to prefer use of one hand over the other

Etymology

Formed from 'eye' + '-edness', modelled after 'handedness'.
