prize

1. VERB

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
to value highly or regard as very important or precious
value or regard highly

Examples

She has always prized honesty in her friends above all else.
He prizes the old watch his grandfather gave him.
They prized her advice and often asked for her opinion.

Synset Examples

I respect his judgement
We prize his creativity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to consider something important or beneficial; have a high opinion of
  • Synonym: to cherish - to hold something dear; to care for deeply
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; ignore

Etymology

From Middle English *prise*, from Old French *prise* (a taking, a grasping), from Latin *prehendere* (to seize).

2. NOUN

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
an award or reward given to someone who wins a competition, contest, or lottery, often as recognition of achievement
award given for winning a competition

Examples

She won the first prize in the art competition last year.
The prize for the raffle was a brand-new bicycle.
Competitors waited anxiously as the prize was announced by the judges.

Synset Examples

the prize was a free trip to Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a result of an official decision, often for achievement
  • Synonym: reward - something given in recognition of effort, service, or achievement
  • Antonym: penalty - punishment for an offense or failure

Etymology

From Old French *pris* (value, price, reward), from Latin *pretium* (price, value).

to use force, typically with a lever, to move, open, or separate something

7. ADJ

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
of superior quality or value; awarded as the best among others of its kind.
of exceptional quality or value

Examples

These prize roses took first place at the flower show.
He breeds prize cattle known for their superior genetics.
The chef used only prize ingredients in his signature dish.

Synset Examples

choice wines
prime beef
prize carnations
quality paper
select peaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - of very good quality; carefully selected
  • Synonym: prime - of the best possible quality; excellent
  • Synonym: select - of special quality; chosen as the best
  • Antonym: inferior - of lower quality or value

Etymology

From Middle English 'prise', meaning reward or something taken as a reward, from Old French 'pris' (prize), from Latin 'premium' (reward).
