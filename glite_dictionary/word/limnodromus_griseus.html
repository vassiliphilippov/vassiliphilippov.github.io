Limnodromus griseus

1. NOUN

/ lɪm.noʊˈdroʊ.məs ˈɡraɪ.si.əs / (AmE), / lɪm.nəˈdrəʊ.məs ˈɡriː.si.əs / (BrE)
A species of dowitcher, a wading bird with a grey back, commonly known as the short-billed dowitcher, found in North America.
a species of dowitcher (wading bird)

Examples

We spotted a flock of Limnodromus griseus feeding along the muddy shoreline.
The Limnodromus griseus migrates long distances between its breeding and wintering grounds.
Birdwatchers often confuse the Limnodromus griseus with its close relative, the long-billed dowitcher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short-billed dowitcher - common name for Limnodromus griseus, a wading bird
  • Degree Higher: Limnodromus scolopaceus - long-billed dowitcher, a closely related species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Limnodromus' refers to 'marsh runner', and 'griseus' means 'grey' in Latin.
