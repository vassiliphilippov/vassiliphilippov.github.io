grama

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A type of pasture grass native to the plains of South America and western North America, often important for grazing livestock.
pasture grass of American plains

Examples

Blue grama is a popular choice for drought-tolerant lawns in the southwestern United States.
Cattle graze on grama throughout the grasslands of northern Mexico.
The rancher wanted to seed his pastures with hardy grama to improve forage quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue grama - a species of grama grass known for its bluish-green blades
  • Synonym: buffalo grass - another species of shortgrass related to grama grasses

Etymology

From Spanish 'grama', meaning 'grass'.
