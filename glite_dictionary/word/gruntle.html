to gruntle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrʌntəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌntl / (BrE)
to make pleased or content; to mollify
make pleased, mollify; archaic
archaic

Examples

The gentle humor in his speech served to gruntle even the most skeptical audience members.
She hoped a cup of tea would gruntle her irritated guest after a long journey.
The manager tried to gruntle the staff with unexpected praise and an early finish to the day.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mollify - to calm or soothe someone who is angry
  • Antonym: to disgruntle - to make dissatisfied or discontented

Etymology

From Middle English *gruntlen* (to grumble), diminutive of *grunt*; repurposed in humorous opposition to 'disgruntle'.
