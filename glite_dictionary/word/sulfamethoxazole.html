sulfamethoxazole

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌl.fə.mɛθˈɑk.səˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˌsʌl.fə.mɛθˈɒk.səˌzəʊl / (BrE)
an antibiotic of the sulfonamide group, used especially to treat infections of the urinary tract
antibiotic of the sulfonamide group
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sulfamethoxazole to treat his urinary tract infection.
Allergic reactions to sulfamethoxazole can include rash and fever.
She asked her pharmacist about the possible side effects of sulfamethoxazole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfonamide - any of a group of synthetic antibiotics containing the sulfonamide group

Etymology

From 'sulfa', relating to sulfonamide drugs, and 'methoxazole', from methoxy- and -azole indicating part of the chemical structure.
