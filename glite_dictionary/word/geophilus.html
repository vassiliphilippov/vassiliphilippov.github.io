Geophilus

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑfɪləs / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of centipedes in the family Geophilidae, found worldwide and sometimes called earwigs.
genus of centipedes (Geophilidae)

Examples

The centipede Geophilus is commonly found under stones and logs in gardens.
Some species of Geophilus are mistaken for earwigs due to their elongated bodies.
Biologists studied the behavior of Geophilus in different soil types.

Etymology

From Greek 'gē' (earth) and 'philos' (loving), referring to earth-dwelling habits.

2. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːfɪləs / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of centipedes (family Geophilidae) found worldwide, known for their elongated bodies with many leg pairs.
genus of centipedes

Examples

The Geophilus genus includes many species that live in soil and leaf litter.
Biologists collected several Geophilus specimens during the field study.
Some people mistakenly refer to Geophilus centipedes as earwigs due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geophilidae - the family of soil-dwelling centipedes including Geophilus

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) + 'philos' (loving), meaning 'earth-loving'.
