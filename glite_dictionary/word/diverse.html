diverse

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈvɜrs, dɪ- / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːs, dɪ- / (BrE)
very different from each other; distinctly dissimilar or unlike.
distinctly dissimilar or unlike

Examples

The university attracts students from diverse backgrounds and cultures.
Her artwork features diverse styles and influences, making each piece unique.
Our team brings together professionals with diverse skills and perspectives.

Synset Examples

celebrities as diverse as Bob Hope and Bob Dylan
animals as various as the jaguar and the cavy and the sloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: different - not the same as another; unlike in nature, form, or quality
  • Antonym: alike - similar; having resemblance

Etymology

From Latin 'diversus' (turned in different directions, different, separate).

3. ADJ

/ daɪˈvɜrs / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːs / (BrE)
showing a great deal of variety; very different from one another in character or form.
showing variety; different in kind

Examples

The city has a diverse population, with residents from over fifty countries.
The museum's collection is extremely diverse, including everything from ancient pottery to modern sculpture.
Our team brings a diverse range of skills and perspectives to the project.

Synset Examples

Africa is a diverse continent with many cultures.
The estuary is home to diverse wildlife.
The festival celebrated diversity in art and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varied - showing variation; marked by diversity or difference
  • Antonym: uniform - not changing in form or character; remaining the same in all cases and at all times

Etymology

From Latin 'diversus' (turned different ways), past participle of 'divertere' (to turn aside).
