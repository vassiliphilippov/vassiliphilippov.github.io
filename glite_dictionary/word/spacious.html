spacious

1. ADJ

/ ˈspeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈspeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having a great amount of space; large and open in area or extent.
large and open in area or extent

Examples

The spacious living room had enough room for the whole family to relax comfortably.
They chose an apartment with a spacious balcony overlooking the park.
The hotel lobby was surprisingly spacious and filled with natural light.

Synset Examples

a broad lawn
the wide plains
a spacious view
spacious skies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roomy - having plenty of space
  • Antonym: cramped - uncomfortably small or restricted

Etymology

From Latin spatiosus (roomy, spacious), from spatium (space).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈspeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a room or building) having a lot of space; large and open.
having a lot of space

Examples

The new apartment is spacious enough for a large family to live comfortably.
They held the reception in a spacious hall overlooking the river.
The living room felt bright and spacious after the renovation.

Synset Examples

a roomy but sparsely furnished apartment
a spacious ballroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roomy - having plenty of room or space
  • Antonym: cramped - lacking space; very small or confined

Etymology

From Latin 'spatiosus' (roomy, spacious), from 'spatium' (space).
