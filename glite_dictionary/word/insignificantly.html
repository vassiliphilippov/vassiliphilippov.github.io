insignificantly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that is not significant or important; to a negligible or unremarkable degree.
in an unimportant or negligible way

Examples

The results of the experiment differed insignificantly from the original findings.
She contributed insignificantly to the discussion, remaining mostly silent throughout the meeting.
The changes in temperature were insignificantly small and did not affect the outcome.

Synset Examples

some people living insignificantly among us

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significantly - in a significant or important manner

Etymology

From 'insignificant' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt.li / (AmE), / ɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that is not significant or important; to an unnoticeable or minor degree.
in an unimportant or minor way

Examples

The temperature dropped insignificantly overnight, so most people didn't notice the change.
His efforts contributed insignificantly to the final outcome of the project.
The company's profits were insignificantly higher than last year, barely making a difference.

Synset Examples

our budget will only be insignificantly affected by these new cuts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significantly - in a way that is important or large enough to be noticed

Etymology

From 'insignificant' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00006822-r
Synset POS: r
