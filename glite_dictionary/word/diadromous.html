diadromous

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈædrəməs / (AmE), / daɪˈadrəməs / (BrE)
(of fish) migrating between fresh and salt water at different stages of life.
migrating between fresh and salt water (of fish)

Examples

Atlantic salmon are diadromous, moving between rivers and the sea during their life cycle.
Scientists study diadromous species to better understand aquatic migration patterns.
Not all migratory fish are diadromous; some remain in either freshwater or the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anadromous - (of fish) migrating from the sea to freshwater to spawn
  • Synonym: catadromous - (of fish) migrating from freshwater to the sea to spawn

Etymology

From Greek dia- (through, across) + -dromous (running), from dramein (to run).
