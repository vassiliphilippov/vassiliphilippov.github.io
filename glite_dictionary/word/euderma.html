Euderma

1. NOUN

/ juˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / juːˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, which includes the spotted bat.
genus of bats including the spotted bat

Examples

The Euderma genus is best known for the distinctive spotted bat.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Euderma genus in the southwestern United States.
Members of Euderma display unique ear and wing patterns among North American bats.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good) + 'derma' (skin), referring to the animal's skin characteristics.

2. NOUN

/ juˈdɜːrmə / (AmE), / juːˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, commonly known as spotted bats.
genus of bats (spotted bats)

Examples

The Euderma genus includes the distinctive spotted bat found in western North America.
Scientists study Euderma to learn more about the ecology of spotted bats.
Members of the genus Euderma are recognized for their large ears and unique fur patterns.

Etymology

From Greek eu- (good, well) + derma (skin), referring to some characteristic of the animals' skin.
