Psaltriparus

1. NOUN

/ sælˈtrɪpərəs / (AmE), / sælˈtrɪpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, insectivorous songbirds in the family Paridae, including the American bushtit.
genus of small songbirds (bushtits)

Examples

The American bushtit belongs to the genus Psaltriparus.
Ornithologists have studied the behaviors of birds in the Psaltriparus genus for decades.
Only one living species, the American bushtit, is classified under Psaltriparus.

2. NOUN

/ sælˌtrɪˈpɛərəs / (AmE), / sælˌtrɪˈpɑːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the family Aegithalidae, containing the American bushtit and related species.
genus of small passerine birds

Examples

The Psaltriparus genus is best known for containing the American bushtit.
Birdwatchers sometimes spot members of the Psaltriparus genus flitting through the trees.
Recent research examines the evolutionary history of Psaltriparus in North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Aegithalidae - family of small passerine birds known as long-tailed tits and bushtits

Etymology

From Greek psaltis (chanter, singer) + Latin parus (tit, a type of bird).
