to demonise

1. VERB

/ ˈdiːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdiːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to portray or treat someone or something as evil or demonic
portray as evil or demonic
british

Examples

The media often demonise minority groups, leading to increased prejudice.
Some politicians attempt to demonise their opponents during election campaigns.
It is unfair to demonise an entire country based on the actions of a few individuals.

Synset Examples

Power had demonized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demonize - treat or depict as evil; American English spelling
  • Synonym: to vilify - to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
  • Antonym: to humanise - to make something more humane or sympathetic

Etymology

From demon + -ise; influenced by French 'démoniser' or Late Latin 'dæmonizare'.
