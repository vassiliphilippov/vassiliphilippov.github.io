batik

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtik / (AmE), / bəˈtiːk / (BrE)
A fabric that is dyed using a technique in which parts of the material are covered with removable wax to create patterns and prevent those areas from absorbing dye.
fabric dyed with removable wax patterns

Examples

She wore a beautiful batik dress with intricate floral designs.
Traditional Indonesian batik is known for its detailed and colorful patterns.
The museum displayed an impressive collection of batik fabrics from across Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Javanese 'mbatik' (to dot, write, or paint).

2. VERB

/ bəˈtik / (AmE), / bəˈtiːk / (BrE)
to dye (fabric) using a method in which wax is applied to parts of the surface to resist dyes and create patterns
dye fabric with wax-resist technique

Examples

She learned how to batik her scarves in an authentic Indonesian workshop.
Many artists batik their fabrics to achieve unique and colorful patterns.
Yesterday, the students batiked T-shirts as part of the art class project.

Synset Examples

Indonesian fabrics are often batiked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tie-dye - to dye fabric using tying methods to produce patterns

Etymology

From Javanese *batik* (to dot, to write).
