Nardil

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑrdɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɑːdɪl / (BrE)
A branded monoamine oxidase inhibitor (MAOI) used as an antidepressant to treat clinical depression.
antidepressant drug (brand name)
medical

Examples

After months of unsuccessful treatments, his doctor prescribed Nardil to manage his depression.
Some patients experience dietary restrictions when taking Nardil due to potential interactions.
The psychiatrist explained that Nardil is often used when other antidepressants are not effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenelzine - the generic name for the drug sold as Nardil
