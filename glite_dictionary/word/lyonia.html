Lyonia

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈoʊniə / (AmE), / lʌɪˈəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or deciduous shrubs or small trees native to the United States, the Antilles, and regions from eastern Asia to the Himalayas.
genus of shrubs or small trees

Examples

The garden's new section featured several Lyonia shrubs prized for their clusters of white flowers.
Botanists have discovered a new species within the Lyonia genus in the Himalayan region.
Some species of Lyonia are cultivated for their attractive foliage and flowers.

Etymology

Named after John Lyon, Scottish botanist (1765–1814).

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈoʊniə / (AmE), / lʌɪˈəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or deciduous shrubs and small trees found in the United States, the Antilles, eastern Asia, and the Himalaya.
a plant genus of shrubs and small trees

Examples

The Lyonia genus includes species that thrive in acidic soils.
Several Lyonia shrubs are native to the forests of eastern North America.
Gardeners favor Lyonia for its attractive foliage and resilience.

Etymology

Named after John Lyon (1765–1814), a Scottish botanist and plant collector.
