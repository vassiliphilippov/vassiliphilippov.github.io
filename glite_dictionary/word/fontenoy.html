Fontenoy

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɒntəˈnɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌfɒntəˈnɔɪ / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in 1745 near Fontenoy, Belgium, where the French army under Marshal Saxe defeated the English and their allies.
1745 battle: French victory over English and allies

Examples

The Battle of Fontenoy marked a turning point in the War of the Austrian Succession.
Historians still study the tactics employed at Fontenoy by Marshal Saxe and the French army.
After their defeat at Fontenoy, British and allied troops retreated from the battlefield.

Etymology

From the name of Fontenoy, a village in Belgium where the battle took place.
