Constance

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːn.stəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stəns / (BrE)
the council of 1414–1418 that ended the Great Schism in the Roman Catholic Church by resolving papal disputes and promoting church reform.
church council (1414–1418) ending the Great Schism

Examples

The Council of Constance marked a turning point in church history.
Many important reforms were enacted during Constance to restore unity within the Church.
Historians often cite Constance as a major effort to address corruption in medieval Catholicism.

Etymology

Named after the city of Constance (Konstanz) in present-day Germany, where the council was held.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɑn.stəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stəns / (BrE)
a large lake in south-central Europe, situated on the borders of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland, and forming part of the Rhine River; also known as Lake Constance.
a lake on the borders of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland

Examples

Many tourists visit Constance each year to enjoy its picturesque setting and clear waters.
The Rhine River enters Constance from the southeast and exits at the northwest end of the lake.
Sailing is a popular activity on Constance, especially during the warm summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lake Constance - the common English name for the lake Constance refers to

Etymology

From Latin Constantia, the medieval Latin name for the city of Konstanz on the lake's shore.
