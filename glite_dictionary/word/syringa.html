syringa

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A large, hardy shrub with showy and strongly fragrant creamy-white flowers, commonly grown in gardens.
fragrant flowering shrub

Examples

We planted a syringa near the garden gate for its sweet scent in spring.
The creamy-white blossoms of the syringa filled the air with their distinctive fragrance.
A row of syringa shrubs acts as a beautiful, flowering hedge along the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - hardy shrub with fragrant purple or white flowers, closely related or commonly confused with syringa

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'surinx' (pipe, tube, channel), from the pithy stems used historically as pipes.
