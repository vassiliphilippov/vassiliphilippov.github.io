Syringa

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World shrubs or small trees, known as lilacs, noted for their fragrant and showy clusters of flowers.
genus of lilacs (shrubs with fragrant flowers)

Examples

Many gardens feature Syringa for its beautiful and aromatic spring blossoms.
The Syringa species are prized by horticulturists for their fragrant panicles of flowers.
She planted several Syringa along the fence to enjoy their lilac-scented blooms in May.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - A shrub or small tree with fragrant purple or white flowers, belonging to the genus Syringa

Etymology

From Greek syrinx (pipe or tube), referring to the plant's hollow stems.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World shrubs or small trees, commonly known as lilacs, with fragrant flowers in showy clusters.
genus of shrubs: lilacs

Examples

Several Syringa species are cultivated for their beautiful, fragrant flowers.
The garden features a rare variety of Syringa in full bloom each spring.
Botanists collected samples of Syringa from the mountains for classification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - a shrub or small tree of the genus Syringa, with fragrant violet, pink, or white blossoms

Etymology

From Greek syrinx (pipe, tube), referring to the pithy stems.
