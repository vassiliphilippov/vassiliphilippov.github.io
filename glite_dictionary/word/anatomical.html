anatomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈtɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌanəˈtɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the structure of the bodies of humans, animals, or plants
relating to body structure

Examples

The students examined the anatomical structure of the human heart in biology class.
A rare anatomical variation was discovered during the surgical procedure.
The artist's drawing demonstrated a keen understanding of anatomical details.

Synset Examples

anatomical features

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physiological - relating to the functions and processes of living organisms
  • Synonym: anatomic - alternative form of anatomical, especially in technical contexts

Etymology

From Greek anatomē (dissection) via Late Latin anatomicus.

2. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈtɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌænəˈtɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the structure of organisms or their parts, or the science of anatomy.
relating to the structure of organisms

Examples

The surgeon discussed the anatomical differences between the two species.
Detailed anatomical diagrams are essential for medical students.
Her research paper focused on the anatomical structure of the heart.

Synset Examples

anatomical research

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physiological - relating to the normal functions of living organisms and their parts

Etymology

From Late Latin anatomicus, from Greek anatomē (dissection), from ana- (up) + temnein (to cut).

Synset POS: a
