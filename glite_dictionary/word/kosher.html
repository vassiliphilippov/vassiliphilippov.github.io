kosher

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
(of food or premises) prepared or kept in accordance with Jewish dietary laws.
conforming to Jewish dietary laws

Examples

Many Jewish families only eat kosher food in their homes.
The restaurant is certified kosher and follows all dietary regulations.
They keep a strictly kosher kitchen for religious reasons.

Synset Examples

kosher meat
a kosher kitchen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-kosher - not conforming to Jewish dietary laws

Etymology

From Yiddish קאָשער (kosher), from Hebrew כָּשֵׁר (kashér, meaning 'fit, proper').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00429141-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
Food that complies with Jewish dietary laws.
food prepared according to Jewish law

Examples

Is this chicken kosher, or was it not prepared according to the rules?
During Passover, they only eat kosher foods at home.
The restaurant offers a full menu of kosher dishes certified by local authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halal - food permissible under Islamic law

Etymology

From Yiddish *kosher*, from Hebrew *kāshēr* (fit, proper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07576107-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
(of food, premises, or preparation) conforming to the dietary laws of Judaism.
conforming to Jewish dietary laws

Examples

Is this chicken kosher, or was it prepared in a regular kitchen?
The restaurant is certified as kosher and serves only food prepared according to Jewish law.
After switching to a kosher diet, she carefully checked every food label for compliance.

Synset Examples

kosher meat
a kosher kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halal - permissible or lawful according to Islamic law, especially regarding food

Etymology

From Yiddish *kosher* (fit, proper), from Hebrew *kāshēr* (proper, fit, correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00429141-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
Considered proper, legitimate, or in accordance with accepted standards; genuine or trustworthy.
legitimate or acceptable

Examples

The deal didn’t look kosher to the investigators, so they decided to look deeper.
Are you sure this operation is kosher? We can't afford to get into trouble.
Everything was above board and completely kosher according to company policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legitimate - conforming to rules or laws; justifiable
  • Antonym: illegitimate - not authorized by the law; not in accordance with accepted standards

Etymology

From Yiddish *kosher*, from Hebrew *kāshēr* (fit, proper, correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01886153-s
Synset POS: s
