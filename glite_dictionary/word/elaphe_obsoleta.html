Elaphe obsoleta

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæfi əbˈsoʊlɪtə / (AmE), / ɪˈlæfi əbˈsəʊlɪtə / (BrE)
a large, non-venomous, shiny black snake native to North America, commonly known as the black rat snake
large North American black snake

Examples

The Elaphe obsoleta is often found in forests and farmland across the eastern United States.
My biology professor showed us a live Elaphe obsoleta during our herpetology class.
Growing up, I was fascinated by the shiny black scales of the Elaphe obsoleta that lived near our home.

Etymology

From Greek 'elaphē' (a kind of snake) and Latin 'obsoleta' (worn out, obsolete).
