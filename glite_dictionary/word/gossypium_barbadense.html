Gossypium barbadense

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːˈsɪpiəm ˌbɑːrbəˈdɛns / (AmE), / ˌɡɒˈsɪpɪəm ˌbɑːbəˈdɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A species of cotton plant native to the Caribbean and parts of the Americas, cultivated for its long, silky fibers.
species of cotton plant with long fibers

Examples

Gossypium barbadense is prized for its extra-long staple cotton, used in luxury textiles.
Many high-quality cotton products are made exclusively from Gossypium barbadense fibers.
Farmers in tropical regions have cultivated Gossypium barbadense for centuries.

Etymology

From Latin 'gossypium' (cotton) and 'barbadense,' referring to Barbados, where the species was identified.
