Berycomorphi

1. NOUN

/ bɛrɪkoʊˈmɔrfaɪ / (AmE), / bɛrɪkəʊˈmɔːfaɪ / (BrE)
an order of spiny-finned fish within the superorder Acanthopterygii, comprising several deep-sea species
order of spiny-finned fish

Examples

The deep-sea species belonging to the Berycomorphi are often studied by marine biologists.
Many Berycomorphi have elongated bodies and well-developed spines.
Recent discoveries have expanded our understanding of the evolutionary history of Berycomorphi.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Beryx + Greek morphē (form).
