calculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkalkjʊˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process of working out something by using mathematics or logical reasoning.
act of working out by mathematics

Examples

After careful calculation, he determined the project would be profitable.
The scientist double-checked every calculation before publishing her findings.
You need to make a quick calculation to convert feet to meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computation - the action of mathematical calculation

Etymology

From Latin calculatio (a computing, reckoning), from calculare (to reckon), from calculus (small stone used for counting).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkælkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkælkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of using mathematics to find an answer, amount, or result, especially by working with numbers
process of using mathematics to find an answer

Examples

The final calculation showed that we would need more funding for the project.
After several hours of calculation, she determined the exact amount of material required.
You should double-check your calculation to avoid any mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computation - the act of calculating an amount or number
  • Degree Lower: estimation - a rough calculation or guess of value, number, or amount

Etymology

From Latin 'calculatio', from 'calculare' (to reckon), from 'calculus' (a small stone used for counting).
