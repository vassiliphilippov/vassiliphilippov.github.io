donkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋki / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋki / (BrE)
a donkey, used as the symbol of the Democratic Party in the United States, especially in political cartoons
symbol of the US Democratic Party

Examples

Political cartoons often depict the Democratic Party as a donkey to represent its ideals.
The donkey has become an enduring symbol of the party since the 19th century.
Campaign merchandise featuring the donkey is popular among Democratic supporters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elephant - symbol of the Republican Party in the US

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋki / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋki / (BrE)
A domestic animal of the horse family, typically smaller than a horse, with long ears and a braying call, often used for carrying loads; known for being patient but sometimes stubborn.
domestic animal used as a beast of burden

Examples

The farmer loaded supplies onto his donkey before heading to the market.
Children laughed as they watched the donkey bray loudly in the field.
Stubborn as a donkey, he refused to change his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ass - another word for donkey (especially in older or formal contexts)
  • Synonym: mule - the offspring of a male donkey and female horse

Etymology

Of unknown origin, possibly from the dialect word 'dun' (dull gray-brown) plus the diminutive suffix '-key.'
