mangold-wurzel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɡoʊldˌwɜr.zəl / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəʊldˌwɜːzəl / (BrE)
A variety of beet with a large yellowish root, grown mainly as cattle feed.
beet with large root, used as cattle feed

Examples

The farmer harvested several mangold-wurzels to feed his livestock over the winter.
Historically, mangold-wurzel was an important crop in rural England.
They grow mangold-wurzel on the farm mainly for use as cattle fodder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - root vegetable, Beta vulgaris

Etymology

From German 'Mangoldwurzel', meaning 'chard root'.
