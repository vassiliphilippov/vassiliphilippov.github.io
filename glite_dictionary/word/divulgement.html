divulgement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvʌldʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈvʌlɡmənt / (BrE)
the act of making something secret or private known to others
act of disclosing a secret

Examples

The divulgement of classified information caused a major scandal.
Any divulgement of personal details must be done with consent.
He regretted his divulgement of the company's confidential plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disclosure - the action of making new or secret information known

Etymology

From 'divulge' + '-ment', patterned after Latin divulgare (to make public).
