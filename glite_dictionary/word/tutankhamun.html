Tutankhamun

1. PROPN

/ tuːˌtɑnkəˈmuːn / (AmE), / ˌtjuːtænˈkɑːmʊn / (BrE)
an Egyptian pharaoh of the 18th dynasty (reigned c. 1332–1323 BC), famous for his nearly intact tomb discovered in 1922.
Egyptian pharaoh, tomb discovered in 1922

Examples

Many tourists visit the exhibition featuring Tutankhamun's treasures every year.
The discovery of Tutankhamun's tomb provided invaluable insights into ancient Egyptian civilization.
Historians have long debated the cause of Tutankhamun's death.

Etymology

From ancient Egyptian Tutenkh-Amen meaning 'living image of Amun'.
