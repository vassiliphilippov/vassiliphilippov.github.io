to fress

1. VERB

/ frɛs / (AmE), / frɛs / (BrE)
To eat a large amount of food greedily or without restraint.
eat greedily or excessively
informal

Examples

Whenever there's a family gathering, Uncle Leo tends to fress on everything in sight.
She couldn't resist the pastries at the buffet and started fressing the moment she arrived.
After the marathon, the runners fressed on pizza and cake without any guilt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorge - to eat a lot greedily
  • Synonym: to pig out - to eat a lot in one sitting, especially junk food
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat small amounts slowly or cautiously

Etymology

From Yiddish 'frɛsən' (to eat greedily), related to German 'fressen'.
