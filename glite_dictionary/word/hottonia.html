Hottonia

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / hɒˈtɒniə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic herbs, including Hottonia palustris and Hottonia inflata, commonly known as water violets.
genus of aquatic herbs (water violets)

Examples

Hottonia is often found in calm freshwater ponds and lakes across Europe and North America.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Hottonia under different water conditions.
The delicate flowers of Hottonia make it a favorite among aquatic plant enthusiasts.

Etymology

Named after Peter Hotton, Dutch botanist (1648–1709).

/ hɑːˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / hɒˈtəʊniə / (BrE)

Examples

The pond was filled with Hottonia, its delicate flowers floating above the water's surface.

Hottonia thrives in slow-moving streams and shallow ponds across Europe and North America.
