maximum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæksɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmæksɪməm / (BrE)
A point on a curve where the slope changes from increasing to decreasing, representing the highest value in that region.
highest point on a curve

Examples

The graph of the function reaches a maximum at x = 2.
Finding the local maximum is essential for understanding the curve’s behavior.
At this point, the slope is zero and the curve has a clear maximum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimum - the point on a curve where the tangent changes from negative on the left to positive on the right

Etymology

From Latin maximum, neuter of maximus (greatest).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmæk.sə.məm / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.məm / (BrE)
Being the greatest, highest, or most complete possible in degree or amount.
greatest or highest possible

Examples

The machine should not be operated at its maximum speed for long periods.
He reached the maximum level of achievement in his field.
The room has a maximum capacity of 50 people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimum - the least or smallest amount possible
  • Synonym: maximal - greatest or most complete possible (less common variant)

4. NOUN

/ ˈmæksɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmæksɪməm / (BrE)
the greatest amount, value, or degree possible or allowed
greatest possible amount

Examples

You should not exceed the maximum recommended dosage of this medication.
During the summer, the temperature reached its maximum in early August.
The speed limit sets the maximum you are legally allowed to drive on this road.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimum - the smallest possible amount or degree
  • Synonym: peak - the highest point or value
  • Synonym: limit - a point beyond which something may not go

Etymology

From Latin maximum (greatest), neuter of maximus (largest, greatest).
