Alcedo

1. NOUN

/ ælˈsiːdoʊ / (AmE), / ælˈsiːdəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the kingfisher family (Alcedinidae), comprising certain species of kingfishers, including the common kingfisher.
genus of kingfishers (birds)

Examples

The common kingfisher, classified under the genus Alcedo, is found across Europe and Asia.
Taxonomists recently revised several species within Alcedo due to new genetic data.
Birdwatchers often hope to catch a glimpse of an Alcedo in its natural habitat along rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird of the family Alcedinidae

Etymology

From Latin *alcedo* (kingfisher).
