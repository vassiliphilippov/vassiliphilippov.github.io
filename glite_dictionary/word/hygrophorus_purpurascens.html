Hygrophorus purpurascens

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːˈfɔːrəs pɜːrˌpjʊˈræskɛnz / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒˈfɔːrəs pɜːpjuːˈræsɛnz / (BrE)
a species of mushroom with a sticky purplish-red cap and stalk, typically found growing beneath spruce and other conifers
a mushroom species with a purplish-red cap

Examples

The Hygrophorus purpurascens is often seen in autumn beneath spruce trees in northern forests.
Mushroom hunters value Hygrophorus purpurascens for its sticky purple-red cap and distinctive coloration.
Scientists collected several specimens of Hygrophorus purpurascens for study in their mycology lab.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Hygrophorus' from Greek 'hygros' (moist) and 'phoros' (bearing), species name from Latin 'purpurascens' (becoming purple).
