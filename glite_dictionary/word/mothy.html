mothy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːθi / (AmE), / ˈmɒθi / (BrE)
Having holes, worn spots, or damage caused by moths or resembling such deterioration.
damaged or worn like by moths

Examples

He found an old, mothy coat in the attic, covered in holes.
The library's mothy curtains gave the room a neglected air.
She replaced the mothy sweaters before they could fall apart entirely.

Synset Examples

moth-eaten blankets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth-eaten - damaged or worn as if by moths

Etymology

Formed from 'moth' + '-y', meaning 'full of' or 'like'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːθi / (AmE), / ˈmɒθi / (BrE)
infested with or damaged by moths, especially referring to fabric or clothing.
infested with or damaged by moths

Examples

She found an old mothy coat in the back of her grandmother's closet.
The antique rug was beautiful but sadly quite mothy in places.
A mothy sweater lay forgotten at the bottom of the cardboard box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth-eaten - damaged or eaten by moths; shabby or worn

Etymology

Formed from 'moth' + suffix '-y', indicating 'full of' or 'characterized by'.
