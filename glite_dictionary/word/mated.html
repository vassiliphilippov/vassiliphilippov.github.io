mated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a marriage partner; joined or paired with another as a spouse.
having a marriage partner

Examples

They observed a mated pair of swans building their nest by the lake.
Once mated, the birds remain together for the rest of their lives.
Some animals exhibit special behaviors only when mated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmated - not having a spouse or mate

Etymology

From past participle of 'mate,' which derives from Middle Low German *mate* (companion, peer).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of animals or people) having formed a sexual pair for mating or reproduction.
paired for mating or reproduction

Examples

The mated birds built their nest together in the tree.
Once the animals are mated, they are kept together for the breeding season.
The zoo staff separate the mated pairs from the rest of the group to ensure a safe environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmated - not having formed a sexual pair

Etymology

Past participle of 'mate', from Middle Low German *mate* (companion, associate).
