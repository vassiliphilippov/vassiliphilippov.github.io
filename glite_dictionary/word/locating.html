locating

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or act of determining the place or position of something
the act of determining a location

Examples

The engineer was responsible for the locating of underground cables before construction began.
Accurate locating of the lost hiker was crucial for the rescue mission.
Maps and GPS devices assist in the locating of new destinations.

Synset Examples

he got a good fix on the target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - the position determined for an object, especially in navigation

Etymology

From the verb 'locate', derived from Latin 'locare' (to place), from 'locus' (place).

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of putting something in a particular place or position
the act of placing something

Examples

The locating of the new library was carefully considered by the city council.
Proper locating of sensors is crucial for accurate measurements.
He supervised the locating of equipment in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the act of putting something in a particular place

Etymology

From the verb 'locate', which comes from Latin 'locare' meaning 'to place', from 'locus' meaning 'place'.
