gabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæbəl / (BrE)
rapid and indistinct speech that is difficult to understand
rapid, indistinct speech

Examples

The speaker's gabble made it hard for the audience to follow his argument.
Through the gabble of voices, she could barely make out her friend's words.
All I could hear was the gabble of children playing in the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - talk that is foolish, excited, or not clear

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of rapid, meaningless speech.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæb.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡæb.əl / (BrE)
to talk quickly and continuously about unimportant matters, often so that it is hard to understand
talk quickly and unintelligibly

Examples

The children began to gabble excitedly as soon as the bell rang.
She tends to gabble when she's nervous, making her speech hard to follow.
Don't gabble your answer; take your time and speak clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly in a foolish or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably imitative of rapid or inarticulate speech; related to Middle Dutch 'gabbelen'.
