jailhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlhaʊs / (BrE)
A building or institution used to detain people who are in government custody, such as those awaiting trial or serving a sentence.
prison or detention facility

Examples

The sheriff escorted the prisoner back to the jailhouse after the trial was postponed.
The small town's jailhouse was built over a century ago and is now a historical site.
He spent two nights in the county jailhouse before making bail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial
  • Synonym: detention center - a facility for the detention of people awaiting trial or deportation
  • Synonym: lockup - a place for the temporary detention of prisoners

Etymology

From 'jail' (Old French 'jaiole', from Medieval Latin 'gabiola') + 'house'.
