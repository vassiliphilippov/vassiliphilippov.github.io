Lampris guttatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.prɪs ɡʌˈteɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈlam.prɪs ɡʌˈtɑː.təs / (BrE)
a large, colorful, oceanic fish found in temperate and tropical waters, commonly known as the opah
a species of large, colorful oceanic fish (opah)

Examples

The Lampris guttatus, or opah, is remarkable for its bright coloration and large, rounded body.
Fishermen off the coast of Hawaii occasionally catch Lampris guttatus in their nets.
Scientists are studying the unique physiology of Lampris guttatus to understand how it maintains its body temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - common name for Lampris guttatus, a large, colorful oceanic fish

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Lampris' and species 'guttatus' meaning 'spotted'.
