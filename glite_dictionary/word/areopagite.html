Areopagite

1. NOUN

/ ˌæriˈɑpədʒaɪt / (AmE), / ˌariˈɒpədʒaɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Areopagus, the judicial and legislative council of ancient Athens
member of ancient Athenian council

Examples

The philosopher Paul was brought before the Areopagite to defend his teachings.
As an Areopagite, he held significant influence in Athenian politics.
Dionysius the Areopagite is mentioned in historical and religious texts.

Etymology

From Greek Areiopagitēs, from Areios pagos ('Hill of Ares', the meeting place of the council).
