hocus-pocus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊkəsˈpəʊkəs / (BrE)
A meaningless chant or incantation, especially used by magicians or in conjuring tricks.
meaningless magic chant

Examples

The magician waved his wand and uttered hocus-pocus, astonishing the children with his trick.
Many people dismissed the fortune teller's words as nothing but hocus-pocus.
He muttered some hocus-pocus before producing a coin from behind my ear.

Synset Examples

This claim is substantiated by the fact that in the Netherlands, the words ‘Hocus pocus’ are usually accompanied by the additional words ‘pilatus pas’, and this is said to be based on a post-Reformation parody of the traditional Catholic ritual of transubstantiation during mass, being a Dutch corruption of the Latin words ‘Hoc est corpus’, meaning ‘this is (my) body’, and the credo ‘sub Pontio Pilato passus et sepultus est’, meaning ‘under Pontius Pilate he suffered and was buried’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abracadabra - magic word or formula used in conjuring
  • Synonym: prestidigitation - magic tricks; sleight of hand

Etymology

Likely from mock-Latin phrase, possibly parodying 'Hoc est corpus' from the Latin mass.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊkəsˈpəʊkəs / (BrE)
deceptive or meaningless talk or activity, especially intended to trick or mislead someone
deceptive or misleading talk/activity

Examples

The politician's speech was just a lot of hocus-pocus to distract the public from the real issue.
Don't be fooled by his hocus-pocus; he's trying to sell you something that's not worth the money.
All that financial jargon is pure hocus-pocus meant to confuse investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of deception or cheating
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - truthfulness, the quality of being honest

Etymology

Originally a pseudo-Latin phrase used by conjurors; first attested in 17th-century English.

trickery or deception, often with magic

Examples

He claimed there was no hocus-pocus involved, just clever engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
