Mentha citrata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnθə sɪˈtreɪtə / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə sɪˈtrɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of mint with leaves that have a fragrance similar to that of bergamot orange, also known as bergamot mint.
a mint species with a bergamot-like scent

Examples

The essential oil of Mentha citrata is widely used in aromatherapy for its citrusy fragrance.
Gardeners often include Mentha citrata in herb gardens because of its pleasant scent.
Some teas feature Mentha citrata to add a distinctive, bergamot-like flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergamot mint - common name for Mentha citrata
  • Degree Higher: mint - aromatic herbaceous plants of the genus Mentha

Etymology

From Latin: 'Mentha' meaning mint, and 'citrata' from 'citratus', referring to a citrus-like scent.
