obscure

1. VERB

/ əbˈskjʊr / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊə / (BrE)
to make something difficult to see, hear, or understand; to conceal or make unclear
make unclear or conceal

Examples

Thick fog can obscure the view of the mountains completely.
He tried to obscure the truth with vague and confusing statements.
The details were obscured by shadow.

Synset Examples

a hidden message
a veiled threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to prevent something from being seen or known
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something visible or known

Etymology

From Latin obscurare (to darken), from obscurus (dark, obscure).

2. VERB

/ əbˈskjʊr / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊə / (BrE)
(linguistics) To reduce the quality of a vowel sound, making it more neutral, as often happens in unstressed syllables (such as becoming a schwa).
reduce a vowel sound to neutral, e.g. schwa

Examples

In rapid speech, many unstressed vowels are obscured to a schwa.
The teacher explained how certain English syllables become obscured in casual conversation.
Linguists have noted that speakers often obscure vowels in weak syllables.
