Boann

1. PROPN

/ boʊˈæn / (AmE), / bəʊˈæn / (BrE)
In Irish mythology, a Celtic goddess associated with the River Boyne, regarded as the mother of Angus Og.
Celtic goddess; mother of Angus Og

Examples

According to legend, Boann created the River Boyne when she approached the Well of Segais.
The story of Boann is a central part of ancient Irish mythology.
Many poets invoke Boann as the inspiration for their works about the River Boyne.

Etymology

From Old Irish Boand, related to the River Boyne in Ireland.
