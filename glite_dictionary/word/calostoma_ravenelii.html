Calostoma ravenelii

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈstoʊmə ˌrævəˈnɛliː / (AmE), / ˌkælɒˈstɒmə ˌravəˈniːliː / (BrE)
a species of gasteromycete fungus with a leathery stalk, a fruiting body containing a thin gelatinous spore case, and elliptical spores, native to certain forested regions.
a species of gasteromycete fungus

Examples

The fruiting body of Calostoma ravenelii can be found in damp deciduous forests after heavy rains.
Researchers studied Calostoma ravenelii to better understand its unique gelatinous spore case.
During our hike, we spotted a cluster of Calostoma ravenelii growing near rotting logs.

Etymology

Genus from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'stoma' (mouth); species named after American mycologist Henry William Ravenel.
