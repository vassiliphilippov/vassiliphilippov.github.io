lineation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of marking, outlining, or arranging with lines, especially in art, writing, or geology
marking or outlining with lines

Examples

The lineation of the poem gave it a unique visual rhythm.
Geologists studied the rock’s lineation to understand how it formed.
Her sketch showed precise lineation around the figure’s silhouette.

Etymology

From Latin 'linea' (line), via Medieval Latin 'lineatio' (a drawing in lines).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a line or outline that defines or bounds the shape of an object, especially in art, geology, or handwriting
a bounding or outlining line

Examples

The lineation of the drawing gave the figure its distinctive shape.
Geologists studied the lineation present in the exposed rock surface.
Good handwriting depends on clear lineation and consistent spacing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a line that marks the outer limits of an object

Etymology

From Latin 'lineatio' (a marking with lines), from 'linea' (line).
