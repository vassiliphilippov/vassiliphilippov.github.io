Prunus pumilla susquehanae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs pjuːˈmɪlə ˌsʌskwəˈheɪni / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs pjuːˈmɪlə ˌsʌskwəˈheɪni / (BrE)
A small, sprawling American cherry that grows on sandy soils, producing tiny, nearly inedible purplish-black fruit.
a small American cherry shrub

Examples

The Prunus pumilla susquehanae is often found in sandy soils along lake shores and dunes.
Botanists study the Prunus pumilla susquehanae for its unique adaptation to harsh environments.
The fruit of the Prunus pumilla susquehanae is rarely consumed due to its sour taste and small size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand cherry - a type of North American cherry shrub found in sandy areas

Etymology

Prunus and pumilla are from Latin (plum and diminutive of 'small'); susquehanae refers to the Susquehanna region.
