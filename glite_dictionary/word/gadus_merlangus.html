Gadus merlangus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪdəs mərˈlæŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪdəs mɛˈlæŋɡəs / (BrE)
A species of food fish found in the Atlantic waters of Europe, resembling cod and commonly known as whiting.
species of European food fish (whiting)

Examples

The Gadus merlangus is often found off the coasts of northern Europe and is prized for its delicate flavor.
Fishermen in the North Sea catch large quantities of Gadus merlangus each year for local markets.
Dishes made with Gadus merlangus are popular in traditional British cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiting - common name for Gadus merlangus, a food fish of European waters
  • Degree Higher: cod - a large marine fish closely related to Gadus merlangus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gadus' (cod) and Neo-Latin 'merlangus' (from French 'merlan', whiting).
