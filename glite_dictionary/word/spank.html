spank

1. VERB

/ spæŋk / (AmE), / spæŋk / (BrE)
to hit someone, usually a child, on their bottom with an open hand as a punishment or to discipline them
hit on the bottom as punishment

Examples

The teacher threatened to spank the mischievous student if he didn't behave.
Some parents still choose to spank their children as a form of discipline.
He spanked his son lightly for running into the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - to hit someone or something with the flat part of the hand or an object

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the late 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ spæŋk / (AmE), / spæŋk / (BrE)
a slap given with the flat of the hand, especially as a punishment to a child
a slap with the flat of the hand

Examples

He gave his son a quick spank on the bottom for misbehaving.
The child started crying after receiving a spank.
Some parents argue that a spank is not an effective way to discipline a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - a quick blow with the flat of the hand or other flat object
  • Degree Lower: hit - to strike (someone or something) with force

Etymology

Origin unknown; perhaps imitative or related to Swedish dialect spanka (to move jerkily).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00135292-n
Synset POS: n
