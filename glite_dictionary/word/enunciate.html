to enunciate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnʌnsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌnsieɪt / (BrE)
to express or state (an idea, thought, or opinion) clearly and distinctly
to state clearly

Examples

The professor made sure to enunciate his points so everyone could understand.
It is important to enunciate your arguments clearly in a debate.
She always enunciates her intentions to avoid misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express an idea clearly
  • Antonym: mumble - to say something indistinctly or unclearly

Etymology

From Latin enuntiare (to announce, declare).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnʌnsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌnsɪeɪt / (BrE)
to pronounce words clearly and distinctly
pronounce words clearly

Examples

The teacher asked the students to enunciate their answers so everyone could hear clearly.
She practiced daily to enunciate French words with perfect clarity.
If you enunciate well, your audience will understand your speech easily.

Synset Examples

She pronounces French words in a funny way
I cannot say ‘zip wire’
Can the child sound out this complicated word?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express or pronounce clearly
  • Antonym: mumble - to speak quietly and unclearly

Etymology

From Latin enuntiare, enuntiat- (to announce, declare).
