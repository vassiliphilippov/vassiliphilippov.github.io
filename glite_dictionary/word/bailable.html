bailable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪləb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of an offense, charge, or person) eligible for release on bail, or for which bail may be granted.
eligible for bail (legal)
legal

Examples

The judge informed the suspect that his crime was bailable under current laws.
Certain nonviolent offenses are considered bailable in this jurisdiction.
Because the charge was bailable, she was released after posting bond.

Synset Examples

a bailable defendant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonbailable - not eligible for bail

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bāilābilis, from bāilāre (to hand over).

WordNet ID: oewn-00855280-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪləbəl / (BrE)
(of an offense or crime) admitting of bail; allowing a person to be released on bail while awaiting trial or further legal proceedings.
admitting of bail
legal

Examples

The judge ruled that it was a bailable offense, so the defendant was released after posting bail.
Some crimes are nonbailable, while others are considered bailable by law.
The lawyer argued that the charge should be treated as bailable under the current legal statutes.

Synset Examples

a bailable offense

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonbailable - not admitting of bail; a crime or offense for which bail is not allowed

Etymology

From Middle French *baillable* or Latin *baiulabilis*, from *bail* (security for release).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01768195-s
Synset POS: s
