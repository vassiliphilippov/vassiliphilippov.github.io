acetabulum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈtæbjələm / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈtæbjʊləm / (BrE)
the cup-shaped socket in the hip bone that receives the head of the femur to form the hip joint
socket in the hip bone for the femur
medical

Examples

The X-ray showed a fracture in the acetabulum after the car accident.
Proper alignment of the acetabulum is crucial for successful hip replacement surgery.
Pain in the acetabulum can indicate early signs of arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip socket - the socket in the pelvis into which the femoral head fits
  • Synonym: glenoid cavity - the socket in the scapula that receives the head of the humerus

Etymology

From Latin *acetabulum* (a vinegar cup), from *acetum* (vinegar).
