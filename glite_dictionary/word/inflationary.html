inflationary

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or tending to cause an increase in the general level of prices; likely to produce inflation.
causing or relating to inflation

Examples

Rapidly rising oil prices can have an inflationary effect on the whole economy.
Governments often raise interest rates to counter inflationary pressures.
Many economists warned about the inflationary risks of increased public spending.

Synset Examples

inflationary prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deflationary - associated with causing a reduction in the general level of prices (opposite of inflationary)

Etymology

Formed from 'inflation' + '-ary', indicating relation.
