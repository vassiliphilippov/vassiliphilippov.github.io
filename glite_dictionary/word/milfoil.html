milfoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪl.fɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.fɔɪl / (BrE)
a common Eurasian herbaceous plant (Achillea millefolium) with finely divided leaves and clusters of small, usually white flowers, often found in meadows and hedgerows
herbaceous plant with divided leaves and small flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with patches of milfoil swaying gently in the breeze.
Gardeners often plant milfoil for its hardy nature and attractive, feathery foliage.
After the rain, the smell of milfoil was especially strong along the hedgerow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yarrow - another name for Achillea millefolium, a herbaceous plant with finely divided leaves

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'millefeuille', from Latin 'millefolium' (thousand leaves), referring to the plant's finely divided leaves.
