stomatous

1. ADJ

/ ˈstoʊmətəs / (AmE), / ˈstəʊmətəs / (BrE)
Having, relating to, or of the nature of a mouth or mouthlike opening.
having or relating to a mouth
medical

Examples

Certain marine invertebrates are described as stomatous because of their specialized feeding openings.
The stomatous feature in the specimen suggests adaptation for ingestion.
Some fungi possess stomatous structures on their reproductive organs.

Etymology

From Greek *stoma* (mouth) + -ous (having the quality of).
