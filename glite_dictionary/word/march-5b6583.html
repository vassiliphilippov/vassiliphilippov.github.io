march

1. NOUN

/ mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a public procession of people walking together, especially to support or protest a cause
public procession or demonstration

Examples

Thousands gathered to join the march for climate action in the city center.
The annual pride march celebrates diversity and inclusion.
Police blocked traffic to allow the peaceful march to proceed along Main Street.

Synset Examples

the march went up Fifth Avenue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, usually celebratory
  • Synonym: demonstration - a public meeting or march protesting against or supporting something

Etymology

From Old French 'marcher' (to walk, go), of uncertain origin.
