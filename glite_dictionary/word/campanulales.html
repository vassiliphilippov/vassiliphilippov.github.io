Campanulales

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpəˌnjuˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌkampəˌnjuːˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants in the subclass Asteridae, including families such as Campanulaceae and Lobeliaceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Campanulales include a diverse group of plants such as bellflowers and lobelias.
Botanists used to classify certain plant families under the order Campanulales before the system was revised.
Research on the Campanulales has contributed greatly to our understanding of plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Campanula (the type genus) + -ales (indicating an order).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpəˌnjuːˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌkampanjuːˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants in the subclass Asteridae, including the bellflower family (Campanulaceae), lobelias (Lobeliaceae), gourds (Cucurbitaceae), and related families
former order of flowering plants

Examples

The Campanulales included several families such as Campanulaceae and Lobeliaceae.
Some botanists no longer recognize Campanulales as a distinct order in modern classifications.
Research in plant taxonomy has led to changes in the composition of Campanulales.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Asteridae - a subclass of dicotyledonous flowering plants
  • Degree Lower: Campanulaceae - the bellflower family of flowering plants

Etymology

From modern Latin Campanulaceae (the bellflower family), the name of the type family, with the standard botanical suffix -ales for plant orders.
