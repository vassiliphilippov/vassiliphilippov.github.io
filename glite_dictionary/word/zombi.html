zombi

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɑːm.bi / (AmE), / ˈzɒm.bi / (BrE)
A dead body brought back to life by supernatural means, especially in stories or folklore.
supernaturally reanimated corpse

Examples

In the story, a zombi stalks the villagers at night after rising from its grave.
Legend has it that a witch doctor can turn a corpse into a zombi with dark magic.
Many horror films feature a zombi as a terrifying central figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zombie - Supernaturally reanimated corpse; common alternative spelling

Etymology

From West African Kongo (nzambi, meaning 'god'), via Haitian Creole and French.
