Canopus

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnoʊpəs / (AmE), / kəˈnəʊpəs / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the night sky, a supergiant star about 650 light years from Earth in the constellation Carina
very bright star in Carina constellation

Examples

Sailors in the southern hemisphere often use Canopus to navigate at night.
Astronomers have studied Canopus to better understand the life cycles of supergiant stars.
Canopus shines brilliantly and is easily visible in the clear night sky.

Etymology

From Latin 'Canopus', from Greek 'Kanōpos', possibly named after the pilot of Menelaus in Greek legend.
