reticule

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪkjuːl / (BrE)
a small, decorative handbag with a drawstring, carried by women, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries
small drawstring handbag (historical)
archaic

Examples

She carried a delicate reticule embroidered with silver thread to the evening ball.
The heroine tucked her letter carefully into her reticule before leaving the house.
During the Regency era, no fashionable lady was seen without her reticule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purse - small bag for carrying money, especially by a woman

Etymology

From French 'réticule', from Latin 'reticulum' (a small net).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪkjuːl / (BrE)
A network of fine lines, crosshairs, or dots in the focal plane of an eyepiece of an optical instrument, used for measurement or alignment.
crosshairs or fine lines in an optical instrument

Examples

The microscope's reticule allowed the scientist to make precise measurements of the specimen.
He carefully adjusted the reticule to align the telescope with the target star.
A reticule is essential for accurate aiming in surveying instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosshair - fine lines in the shape of a cross in the eyepiece of an optical instrument

Etymology

From French 'réticule', from Latin 'reticulum' (small net).
