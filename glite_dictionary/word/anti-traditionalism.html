anti-traditionalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.taɪ.trəˈdɪʃ.ə.nəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.trəˈdɪʃ.ə.nə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The belief in or practice of opposing traditional customs, beliefs, or practices, especially in cultural or social contexts.
opposition to traditional customs or beliefs

Examples

Following years of anti-traditionalism, the country began to revive its cultural rituals.
Her anti-traditionalism often put her at odds with older generations who valued longstanding practices.
Modern art movements frequently arose out of a spirit of anti-traditionalism and experimentation.

Synset Examples

Following the collapse of the Qing dynasty in 1911, the anti-traditionalism of the Chinese intellectual elite was embraced by the iconoclastic Chinese Nationalists and led to the widespread destruction of religious and ancestral temples in the 1920s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: traditionalism - The following or support of tradition, especially in religion or culture

Etymology

From the prefix anti- (against) and traditionalism.
