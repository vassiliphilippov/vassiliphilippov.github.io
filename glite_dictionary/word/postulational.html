postulational

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːstʃəˈleɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪʃənl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from postulates or axioms, especially as a method or approach in mathematics or logic
relating to postulates or axioms

Examples

The postulational method is fundamental in certain branches of geometry.
His approach is postulational, beginning with unproven assumptions to build a logical system.
A postulational framework can clarify the foundations of a scientific theory.

Synset Examples

axiomatic physics
the postulational method was applied to geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiomatic - relating to or based on axioms

Etymology

From postulate + -ional, from Latin postulare ('to demand; to claim').
