Steatornithidae

1. NOUN

/ stiˌætɔːrˈnɪθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / stiːəˌtɔːˈnɪθɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds comprising the oilbirds, found in northern South America, known for their nocturnal habits and echolocation abilities
family of birds including oilbirds

Examples

The Steatornithidae are unique among birds for their ability to navigate by echolocation in darkness.
Scientists have studied the Steatornithidae to understand their adaptation to cave-dwelling life.
The family Steatornithidae consists of a single extant species, the oilbird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilbird - a nocturnal, fruit-eating bird found in South America, the only member of Steatornithidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'steatos' (fat) + 'ornis' (bird) + the family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ stiˌætɔrˈnɪθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / stiˌatɔːˈnɪθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of nocturnal birds, commonly known as oilbirds, found in South and Central America and known for feeding on fruit and using echolocation.
family of nocturnal fruit-eating birds (oilbirds)

Examples

The Steatornithidae are the only family of birds known to use echolocation to navigate.
Scientists have studied the Steatornithidae for insights into nocturnal vision.
Members of the Steatornithidae are crucial for the dispersal of certain tropical fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilbird - a nocturnal, fruit-eating bird in the family Steatornithidae

Etymology

From Greek 'stear' (fat) and 'ornis' (bird); scientific New Latin.
