Desmognathus

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˌmɑːɡˈneɪθəs / (AmE), / dɛzˌməɡˈneɪθəs / (BrE)
A genus of salamanders in the family Plethodontidae, known commonly as dusky salamanders, native to North America.
a genus of dusky salamanders

Examples

The Desmognathus genus includes several species commonly found in Appalachian streams.
Many researchers study Desmognathus to learn about lungless salamander evolution.
Sightings of Desmognathus have contributed to conservation efforts in forested regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian related to newts

Etymology

From Greek desmos (bond) + gnathos (jaw), referring to their jaw structure.
