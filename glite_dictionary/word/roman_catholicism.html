Roman Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.mən kəˈθoʊ.lɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.mən kəˈθɒl.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the beliefs, practices, and organizational system of the Roman Catholic Church, led by the Pope in Rome
beliefs and practices of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

Many people in Spain practice Roman Catholicism and observe its traditions.
He converted to Roman Catholicism after marrying his wife.
The cathedral is an important site for followers of Roman Catholicism in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - the faith and practices of the Catholic Church in general

Etymology

Formed from 'Roman Catholic' (from Rome, the seat of the Pope) + -ism (indicating a system or doctrine).
