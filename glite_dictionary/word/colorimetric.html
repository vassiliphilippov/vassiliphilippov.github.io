colorimetric

1. ADJ

/ ˌkʌlərɪˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.ə.rɪˈmɛt.rɪk / (BrE)
relating to the quantitative measurement of color, especially by means of instruments
relating to the measurement of color

Examples

The colorimetric analysis showed a slight difference in the dye concentration.
This sensor uses a colorimetric method to detect the presence of specific chemicals.
A colorimetric test can provide quick and accurate results in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorimetry - the science of measuring colors

Etymology

From colorimetry + -ic, ultimately from Latin 'color' (color) and Greek 'metron' (measure).
