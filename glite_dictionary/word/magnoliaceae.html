Magnoliaceae

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants including the magnolias and related genera such as Liriodendron and Manglietia.
family of flowering plants (magnolias, etc.)

Examples

The Magnoliaceae family is renowned for its large, fragrant flowers and ancient evolutionary history.
Botanists debated whether several rare species truly belong to the Magnoliaceae family.
Many gardens feature ornamental trees from the Magnoliaceae, prized for their lavish spring blooms.

Etymology

From Magnolia (genus name, after Pierre Magnol) + Latin '-aceae', a standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / maɡˌnəʊliˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes magnolias and related trees and shrubs, found mainly in temperate and tropical regions
botanical family of magnolias

Examples

The Magnoliaceae family is renowned for its large, fragrant flowers and glossy leaves.
Many ornamental trees in gardens belong to the Magnoliaceae.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the Magnoliaceae to understand plant diversification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Magnolia - genus of trees and shrubs with large, fragrant flowers, type genus of Magnoliaceae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Magnolia (the type genus) and the family suffix -aceae.
