overflight

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɚˌflaɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəˌflaɪt / (BrE)
a flight by an aircraft across or above a specific area, especially when passing over another country's territory
flight over a particular area or territory

Examples

The general protested the unauthorized overflight by military aircraft.
International law often governs the rights of overflight across national borders.
The treaty allows commercial overflight of restricted airspace under certain conditions.

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (across) and 'flight' (the act of flying), modeled on similar aviation terms.
