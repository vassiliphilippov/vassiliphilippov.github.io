acapnic

1. ADJ

/ əˈkæpnɪk / (AmE), / əˈkapnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by acapnia, a deficiency of carbon dioxide in the blood
relating to low blood carbon dioxide
medical

Examples

Arterial blood gas analysis revealed an acapnic state after the patient hyperventilated.
Acapnic conditions may present in patients with acute anxiety and rapid breathing.
The technician noted that the respiratory therapy caused the patient to become acapnic.

Etymology

From 'acapnia' + '-ic', with 'acapnia' from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'kapnos' (smoke) via medical terminology.
