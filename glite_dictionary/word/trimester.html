trimester

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈmɛstɚ / (AmE), / traɪˈmɛstə / (BrE)
A period of three months, especially one of the three three-month periods that make up the duration of human pregnancy.
three-month period; esp. in pregnancy

Examples

My doctor scheduled an ultrasound during the second trimester of my pregnancy.
Many universities divide their academic year into three trimesters instead of two semesters.
Morning sickness typically improves after the first trimester.

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'mensis' (month).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈmɛstər / (AmE), / trɪˈmɛstə / (BrE)
one of three periods into which an academic year is divided, each lasting approximately three months
three-month period in an academic year

Examples

The fall trimester begins in September and ends in December.
She completed her final project during the second trimester of the academic year.
Many universities operate on a trimester schedule rather than a semester system.

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'mensis' (month).
