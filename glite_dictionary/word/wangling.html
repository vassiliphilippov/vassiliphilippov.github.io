wangling

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæŋglɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwæŋglɪŋ / (BrE)
An act of achieving something by clever or dishonest means, often involving scheming or trickery.
act of achieving by trickery or scheming

Examples

The manager's wangling of the figures was eventually discovered during the audit.
Years of wangling allowed her to climb the corporate ladder faster than her colleagues.
His success was more a result of wangling than genuine talent or experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wangle - to achieve something by clever or dishonest means
  • Synonym: manipulation - the action of controlling or influencing something cleverly or unscrupulously

Etymology

From the verb 'wangle,' likely of dialect origin, meaning to achieve by contrivance or trickery.
