obsession

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / əbˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
An unhealthy and persistent preoccupation with a particular idea, thing, or person that dominates one's thoughts and actions.
unhealthy, persistent preoccupation

Examples

Her obsession with cleanliness led her to clean the house every day.
He developed an obsession with winning, which affected his relationships.
The detective's obsession with solving the case drove him to work late every night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixation - an obsessive interest or feeling that dominates one's thoughts
  • Synonym: compulsion - an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way
  • Synonym: addiction - the condition of being dependent on a substance or activity

Etymology

From Latin obsessio (siege, blockade), from obsidere (to besiege).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / əbˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
A persistent, irrational thought or urge that continually preoccupies a person's mind, often leading to repetitive behaviors.
persistent, irrational preoccupation or urge

Examples

His cleaning obsession led him to scrub the kitchen multiple times a day.
She battles an obsession with checking if doors are locked before leaving the house.
Despite therapy, his obsession with orderliness affected his daily routine.

Synset Examples

her compulsion to wash her hands repeatedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsion - an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, often against one's conscious wishes

Etymology

From Latin obsessio (siege, blockade), from obsidere (to besiege).
