Skylab

1. PROPN

/ ˈskaɪˌlæb / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlæb / (BrE)
the first United States space station, operational in Earth's orbit from 1973 to 1979
first American space station (1973–79)

Examples

Skylab was launched in 1973 as America's first space station.
Some astronauts conducted crucial experiments aboard Skylab.
The debris of Skylab re-entered Earth's atmosphere in 1979.

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'lab', abbreviating 'laboratory' to denote a space laboratory in Earth's sky.
