obsolescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑb.səˈlɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɒb.səˈlɛs.ənt / (BrE)
Gradually becoming outdated or no longer used, but not yet completely obsolete.
becoming obsolete; outdated

Examples

Many experts believe that printed encyclopedias are becoming obsolescent in the digital age.
The company's obsolescent machinery was soon replaced by newer technology.
Some traditions remain cherished, while others steadily grow obsolescent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date

Etymology

From Latin 'obsolescens', present participle of 'obsolescere' (to fall into disuse).
