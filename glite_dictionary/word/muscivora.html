Muscivora

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈsɪvərə / (AmE), / mjuːˈsɪvərə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the tyrant flycatcher family (Tyrannidae), native to South America.
genus of tyrant flycatcher birds

Examples

The genus Muscivora includes species known for their agile insect-catching abilities.
Researchers observed a member of Muscivora during their expedition in the Amazon rainforest.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot Muscivora species because of their distinctive feeding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tyrannidae - the family of tyrant flycatchers

Etymology

From Latin musca (fly) + -vora (eating), meaning 'fly-eater'.

2. NOUN

/ mʌsˈsɪvərə / (AmE), / mʌsˈsɪvərə / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the Tyrannidae family, commonly known as tyrant flycatchers, which includes species such as the fork-tailed flycatcher.
genus of tyrant flycatcher birds

Examples

The fork-tailed flycatcher is a well-known member of the genus Muscivora.
Researchers observed several species within Muscivora during their expedition to South America.
The classification of Muscivora has been discussed in recent ornithological studies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tyrannidae - the biological family of tyrant flycatchers

Etymology

From Latin *musca* (fly) + *vorare* (to devour).
