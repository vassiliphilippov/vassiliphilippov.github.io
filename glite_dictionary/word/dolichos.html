Dolichos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɒlɪˌkɒs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical vines, often classified within the genera Dipogon, Lablab, or Macrotyloma, known for their twining stems and leguminous pods.
genus of tropical vine plants

Examples

The seeds of the Dolichos plant are sometimes used as food in parts of Asia.
Several species previously included in Dolichos have been reclassified into other genera.
Gardeners appreciate Dolichos for its ornamental value and attractive flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *dolikhos* meaning 'long', referring to the shape of the pods.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.lɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɒ.lɪ.kɒs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical climbing vines in the pea family, sometimes classified under Dipogon, Lablab, or Macrotyloma.
a genus of tropical climbing vines

Examples

Several species in the Dolichos genus are cultivated for their edible beans.
The Dolichos vine can quickly cover fences with its attractive purple flowers.
Researchers studied the drought tolerance of various Dolichos plants in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lablab - a genus of flowering plants closely related to Dolichos
  • Synonym: Dipogon - a genus of twining legumes often considered part of Dolichos
  • Synonym: Macrotyloma - a genus of legumes with species formerly classified in Dolichos

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long), referring to the long pods produced by some species.
