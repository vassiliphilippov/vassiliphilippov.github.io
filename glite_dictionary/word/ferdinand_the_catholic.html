Ferdinand the Catholic

1. PROPN

/ fərˈdænənd ðə ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / fəˈdɪnənd ðə ˈkæθəlɪk / (BrE)
(1452–1516) King of Castile and Aragon, who ruled jointly with Isabella I; their reign marked the unification of Spain and the initiation of the Spanish Inquisition.
King of Castile and Aragon who unified Spain

Examples

Ferdinand the Catholic and Isabella's marriage was instrumental in unifying Spain.
The reign of Ferdinand the Catholic is remembered for the conquest of Granada and support for Columbus.
Many historians study the policies of Ferdinand the Catholic to understand the rise of early modern Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isabella I of Castile - Queen of Castile; wife and co-ruler with Ferdinand the Catholic
