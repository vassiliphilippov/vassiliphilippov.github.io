milk

1. NOUN

/ mɪlk / (AmE), / mɪlk / (BrE)
any of several nutritive, whitish liquids produced by mammals or certain plants, commonly used as food or drink
nutritive, milky liquid from animals or plants

Examples

Many people use almond milk as a dairy alternative in their coffee.
Some infant formulas are based on goat's milk instead of cow's milk.
Coconut milk is a common ingredient in Southeast Asian cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cow's milk - milk obtained from cows, most commonly consumed
  • Degree Higher: soy milk - milky liquid made from soybeans

Etymology

Old English *meolc*, from Proto-Germanic *meluks*.

2. NOUN

/ mɪlk / (AmE), / mɪlk / (BrE)
The white liquid produced by the mammary glands of female mammals, used for feeding their young, and widely consumed as a beverage or food ingredient by humans.
white liquid from female mammals for feeding young

Examples

Many mammals depend on milk to nourish their young during the early stages of life.
She poured a glass of cold milk to have with her cookies.
Goats, cows, and humans all produce milk for their offspring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cream - thick, fatty part of milk that rises to the top
  • Degree Lower: skimmed milk - milk with most of the cream removed

Etymology

Old English *meolc*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *melk* and German *Milch*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ mɪlk / (AmE), / mɪlk / (BrE)
the white nutritious liquid produced by the mammary glands of female mammals, used as food by humans and other animals
white liquid from mammals, used as food

Examples

Would you like some milk in your tea or coffee?
Cows are raised on the farm primarily for their milk.
Young mammals rely on their mother's milk for nourishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cream - the thick, fatty part of milk that rises to the top
  • Antonym: water - clear, colorless, odorless, and tasteless liquid, opposite of the white milk

Etymology

Old English 'meolc', related to German 'Milch' and Latin 'melg-' (to milk).

4. VERB

/ mɪlk / (AmE), / mɪlk / (BrE)
(informal) to exploit a situation or resource to the greatest possible extent, often for personal gain.
exploit to the maximum
informal

Examples

He was milking the situation for every bit of publicity he could get.
It seemed like she was milking his sympathy after her minor accident.
The company tried to milk the patent for all the revenue it could generate.

Synset Examples

I am milking this for all it's worth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to make full use of or derive benefit from

Etymology

From Old English *meolc* (milk); figurative use based on extracting as much as possible, like drawing milk from an animal.

to extract milk from an animal

Examples

She learned how to milk a goat during her visit to the farm.
