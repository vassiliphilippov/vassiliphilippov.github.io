to purify

1. VERB

/ ˈpjʊrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove impurities or unwanted elements from something, especially a liquid or gas, to make it clean or pure
remove impurities; make clean or pure

Examples

Advanced filters are used to purify drinking water in the laboratory.
Some religions include rituals to purify the body and mind before important ceremonies.
The company developed a system to purify air in industrial workplaces.

Synset Examples

purify the water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to contaminate - to make something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Synonym: to cleanse - to make thoroughly clean or free from impurities

Etymology

From Old French *purifier*, from Latin *purificare* (to make pure), from *purus* (pure) + *facere* (to make).
