sigmoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪɡ.mɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.mɔɪd / (BrE)
curved in two directions, resembling the shape of the letter S
curved like an S

Examples

The diagram showed a sigmoid curve representing population growth over time.
In anatomy, the large intestine contains a section known as the sigmoid colon.
A sigmoid path is often used in mathematical models to represent smooth transitions.

Etymology

From Greek 'sigmoeidēs', from 'sigma' (the letter S) + '-oid' (like).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪɡmɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡmɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to the sigmoid flexure in the large intestine; having the form of the letter S.
relating to the sigmoid flexure; S-shaped
medical

Examples

The sigmoid colon is the part most commonly affected by diverticulitis.
Surgery was required to remove an obstruction in the sigmoid section of the large intestine.
Doctors examined the sigmoid area during the colonoscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectal - relating to the rectum

Etymology

From Greek 'sigmoeidēs' (‘like the letter S’), from 'sigma' (the Greek letter S) + '-oid'.
