Picumnus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkʌmnəs / (AmE), / pɪˈkʌmnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small woodpecker-like birds known as piculets, found mainly in Central and South America.
genus of small birds (piculets)

Examples

The Picumnus genus includes several species of small, insect-eating birds found throughout South America.
Unlike true woodpeckers, members of the Picumnus genus generally have shorter tails.
Ornithologists discovered a new species within the Picumnus genus in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piculet - a small bird of the genus Picumnus, similar to a woodpecker
  • Degree Higher: woodpecker - a bird belonging to the family Picidae, related to Picumnus

Etymology

From Latin, used in scientific classification, based on the type species Picumnus.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈkʌmnəs / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːmnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small woodpecker-like birds, known as piculets, within the family Picidae, native to Central and South America.
genus of small woodpecker-like birds

Examples

The Picumnus genus includes several species of tiny woodpeckers found in South America.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Picumnus genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Many bird watchers hope to spot a Picumnus during their trips to Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piculet - any small woodpecker of the genus Picumnus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Picumnus, the name of the Roman minor deity of agriculture, used in taxonomy.
