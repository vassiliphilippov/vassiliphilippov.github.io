shotgun

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑtˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒtˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a firearm with a smooth bore, typically double-barreled, designed to be fired from the shoulder and shoot a spread of shot at short ranges
a shoulder-fired firearm for shooting shot

Examples

He carried a shotgun while hunting ducks in the marshlands.
The police found a loaded shotgun in the suspect’s vehicle.
She learned to shoot clay targets with a twelve-gauge shotgun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rifle - a gun with a rifled barrel for firing bullets accurately at long range

Etymology

Compound of 'shot' and 'gun', first used in the 18th century.
