Tautoga onitis

1. NOUN

/ tɔˈtoʊɡə oʊˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / tɔːˈtəʊɡə əʊˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A large, dark-colored food fish of the Atlantic coast of North America, also known as the tautog or blackfish.
Atlantic food fish; blackfish (tautog)

Examples

Many anglers along the East Coast prize the Tautoga onitis for its fighting spirit.
The diet of Tautoga onitis consists mainly of shellfish and crustaceans.
Marine biologists conducted a survey of Tautoga onitis populations around the rocky reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tautog - A large, dark-colored fish of the Atlantic, also called blackfish.
  • Synonym: blackfish - Common name for several dark-skinned fish including tautog.

Etymology

From the species' taxonomic Latin name given by Linnaeus.
