Acanthocereus tetragonus

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊˈsɪəriəs ˌtɛtrəˈɡoʊnəs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊˈsɪəriəs ˌtɛtrəˈɡəʊnəs / (BrE)
a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its edible, juicy fruit
a cactus species with edible fruit

Examples

The Acanthocereus tetragonus thrives in arid regions and produces fruit that is enjoyed by locals.
Gardeners often cultivate Acanthocereus tetragonus for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
Hikers in Texas might encounter Acanthocereus tetragonus growing wild among the rocks.

Etymology

From Greek 'acantho-' meaning 'spine' and Latin 'cereus' meaning 'candle'; 'tetragonus' from Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'gonia' (angle), referring to the plant's four-angled stems.
