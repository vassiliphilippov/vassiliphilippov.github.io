matter-of-fact

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæt̬.ɚ əv ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˌmæt.ər əv ˈfækt / (BrE)
(of a person, tone, or manner) unemotional, straightforward, and not influenced by imagination or feelings.
unemotional, straightforward manner

Examples

She answered the questions in a matter-of-fact tone that surprised everyone.
His matter-of-fact explanation made the complicated process seem simple.
Despite the tragedy, her voice remained calm and matter-of-fact.

Synset Examples

local guides describe the history of various places in matter-of-fact tones
a prosaic and unimaginative essay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosaic - lacking imagination; dull; matter-of-fact
  • Antonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or imagination
