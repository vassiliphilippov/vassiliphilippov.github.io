Wisteria chinensis

1. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪriə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of wisteria native to China, known for its clusters of deep purple, fragrant flowers.
Chinese wisteria plant with purple flowers

Examples

The Wisteria chinensis in her garden was in full bloom, covering the pergola with purple blossoms.
Many gardeners prefer Wisteria chinensis for its vigorous growth and fragrant flowers.
Every spring, visitors flock to see the spectacular Wisteria chinensis tunnel at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisteria - a climbing shrub with drooping clusters of flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Wisteria' named after Caspar Wistar (an American anatomist), and 'chinensis' meaning 'from China' in Latin.
