Pinus contorta murrayana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs kənˈtɔrtə ˌmʌriˈænə / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəs kɒnˈtɔːtə ˌmʌriˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A tall subspecies of the lodgepole pine, native to western North America.
subspecies of lodgepole pine tree

Examples

The forests in the Sierra Nevada contain many Pinus contorta murrayana trees.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Pinus contorta murrayana after a wildfire.
A mature Pinus contorta murrayana can reach impressive heights in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodgepole pine - a species of pine tree native to North America

Etymology

Latin botanical name; Pinus is Latin for 'pine', contorta means 'twisted', and murrayana honors botanist M. A. Murray.
