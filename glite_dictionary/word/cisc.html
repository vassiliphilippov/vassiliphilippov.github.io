CISC

1. NOUN

/ siː aɪ ɛs siː / (AmE), / siː aɪ ɛs siː / (BrE)
an agency of the Canadian government that coordinates the intelligence operations of Canadian law enforcement agencies
Canadian criminal intelligence agency

Examples

The CISC plays a vital role in coordinating intelligence on organized crime across Canada.
A conference hosted by CISC focused on emerging security threats in the country.
Local police often collaborate with CISC to share critical information.

2. NOUN

/ siː aɪ ɛs siː / (AmE), / siː aɪ ɛs siː / (BrE)
(in computer science) a type of computer architecture with a large number of specialized instructions built into the CPU.
computer architecture with many CPU instructions

Examples

Many early personal computers used the CISC architecture to handle complex operations directly in hardware.
The debate over CISC versus RISC architectures shaped the development of modern microprocessors.
Our professor explained how CISC processors are designed to execute multi-step operations with a single instruction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: RISC - computer architecture with a reduced set of instructions

Etymology

Acronym from Complex Instruction Set Computer.
