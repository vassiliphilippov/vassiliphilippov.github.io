tax-exempt

1. ADJ

/ tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
not subject to taxation; not required to pay tax
not subject to taxation

Examples

She invested in tax-exempt bonds to reduce her overall tax liability.
Many charitable organizations qualify as tax-exempt under federal law.
Certain retirement accounts allow tax-exempt withdrawals for specific purposes.

Synset Examples

tax-exempt bonds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taxable - subject to being taxed
  • Synonym: untaxed - not taxed

2. NOUN

/ tæks ɪgˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / tæks ɪgˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
A financial security, such as a bond, whose income is not subject to taxation by federal, state, or local authorities.
security not subject to tax

Examples

The investor preferred buying tax-exempt for the benefits of avoiding state income taxes.
Many municipal bonds are classified as tax-exempt by the federal government.
Institutions often include tax-exempt in their portfolios to attract clients seeking tax savings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taxable - liable to be taxed

Etymology

From 'tax' (a compulsory financial charge) plus 'exempt' (free from obligation), indicating freedom from tax obligations.
