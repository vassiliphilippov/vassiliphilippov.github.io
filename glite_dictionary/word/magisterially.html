magisterially

1. ADV

/ ˌmædʒɪˈstɪrəli / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪˈstɪərɪəli / (BrE)
in a manner that is authoritative, dignified, or commanding; like a magistrate or a person of authority
in an authoritative or commanding manner

Examples

The judge addressed the courtroom magisterially, leaving no doubt about her command of the proceedings.
He entered the meeting magisterially and everyone fell silent as he began to speak.
She waved her hand magisterially to signal the start of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

she spoke authoritatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritatively - in an authoritative manner

Etymology

From magisterial + -ly, with roots in Latin 'magister' (master, chief).

2. ADV

/ ˌmædʒɪˈstɪr.i.əli / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪˈstɪə.ri.əli / (BrE)
In an authoritative and sometimes domineering manner; like a person with power or authority.
in an authoritative or domineering manner

Examples

The judge spoke magisterially, silencing the courtroom with a single gesture.
She organized the meeting magisterially, leaving no room for objections.
The director managed the cast magisterially, demanding complete attention and discipline.

Synset Examples

this manager acts dictatorially toward his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritatively - in a manner expressing authority or command
  • Synonym: dictatorially - in a manner like a dictator; overbearingly
  • Antonym: submissively - in a manner showing willingness to submit to others

Etymology

From Latin 'magister' (master) via 'magisterial'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00312823-r
Synset POS: r
