Arundinaria

1. NOUN

/ əˌrʌndɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / əˌrʌndɪˈneərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American bamboo, comprising several species of woody grasses found mainly in the southeastern United States.
a genus of North American bamboo

Examples

The dense stands of Arundinaria provided valuable habitat for local wildlife.
Botanists often study Arundinaria to understand bamboo adaptation in temperate climates.
Traditional crafts in the southeastern United States sometimes use Arundinaria stems for weaving.

Etymology

From Latin arundinaria, from arundo (reed, cane), referring to the plant's cane-like appearance.
