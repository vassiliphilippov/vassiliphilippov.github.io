paramecium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈmiːsiəm / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈmiːsɪəm / (BrE)
a single-celled freshwater organism of the genus Paramecium, having a characteristic slipper-like shape and covered with cilia
single-celled freshwater organism
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, we observed a paramecium swimming rapidly among algae.
A paramecium uses its cilia to move and to capture food.
Scientists often use the paramecium as a model organism in biological research.

Etymology

From New Latin Paramecium, from Greek pará (beside) + mēkos (length).
