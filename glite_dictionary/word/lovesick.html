lovesick

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌvˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈlʌvˌsɪk / (BrE)
feeling unhappy or distracted because of being in love, especially when it is unrequited or not reciprocated
unhappy or distracted because of love

Examples

Ever since Anna left, he has been acting like a lovesick teenager.
The lovesick poet wrote verses about his unreturned affection.
She felt lovesick and could think of nothing but him all day.

Synset Examples

strong men behaving like lovesick boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartbroken - experiencing great emotional pain from lost or unrequited love
  • Synonym: besotted - excessively infatuated, often irrationally

Etymology

Formed from 'love' + 'sick', first recorded in English in the 16th century.
