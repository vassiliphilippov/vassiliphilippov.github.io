Celtic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛltɪk, ˈsɛltɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɛltɪk, ˈsɛltɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European languages that was spoken widely across Europe in the pre-Christian era, including Irish, Scottish Gaelic, Welsh, and others
branch of the Indo-European languages

Examples

The ancient Celtic languages have left traces in modern European place names.
She studied the evolution of Celtic languages for her thesis in linguistics.
Irish and Welsh are two living examples of Celtic languages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European - the family of languages including most of those spoken in Europe and parts of Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'Celtae' (the Celts), from Greek 'Keltoi'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɛltɪk, ˈsɛltɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɛltɪk, ˈsɛltɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Celts, their languages, or their culture
relating to the Celts or their culture

Examples

The Celtic languages include Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Welsh.
She is studying Celtic mythology for her thesis on ancient European cultures.
The museum has a large collection of Celtic artifacts from the Iron Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celt - a member of an ancient group of peoples in Europe

Etymology

From Latin 'Celtae' (the Celts), from Greek 'Keltoi'.
