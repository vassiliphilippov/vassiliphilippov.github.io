self-gratification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˌɡræt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of satisfying one's own desires or pleasures, especially without restraint or regard for discipline
personal indulgence; satisfaction of one's own desires

Examples

Her pursuit of self-gratification often led her to neglect her responsibilities.
Some people argue that self-gratification is at odds with self-discipline and long-term happiness.
Excessive self-gratification can sometimes result in negative consequences for one's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-indulgence - excessive or unrestrained gratification of one's own desires
  • Antonym: self-denial - the act of denying oneself pleasures or desires

Etymology

From self- (oneself) + gratification (from Latin gratificari, to give pleasure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˌɡræt̬əfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌselfˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the pleasure or satisfaction gained from satisfying one's own desires, often used in contexts suggesting indulgence
pleasure from satisfying one's own desires

Examples

He often indulged in self-gratification after a stressful day at work.
Too much self-gratification can make it difficult to develop self-discipline.
Her focus on self-gratification sometimes came at the expense of her long-term goals.

Synset Examples

He often indulged in self-gratification after a long week.
Self-gratification can sometimes lead to unhealthy habits.
Her pursuit of self-gratification led to many spontaneous trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-indulgence - excessive or unrestrained gratification of one's own appetites or desires

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'gratification', from Latin gratificari 'to do favors to'.
