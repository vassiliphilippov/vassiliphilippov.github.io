jot

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒt / (BrE)
a brief, quickly written note
quick handwritten note

Examples

She left me a jot on the kitchen counter before leaving for work.
I scribbled a jot in my notebook so I wouldn't forget the idea.
Even the briefest jot can help you remember something important later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a brief written record or message

Etymology

From Latin 'jota', the name of the Greek letter iota, used figuratively for something very small.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ dʒɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒt / (BrE)
a very small amount; a tiny bit
a very small amount

Examples

He didn't care a jot about what others thought of his decision.
There isn't a jot of evidence to prove their theory.
She added just a jot of salt to enhance the flavor.

Synset Examples

this dish could use a touch of garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very slight amount

Etymology

From Latin "iota," the smallest letter of the Greek alphabet, used to mean something very small.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13796315-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dʒɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒt / (BrE)
to write something quickly and briefly, often as a reminder or note
write briefly or quickly

Examples

Let me jot down your phone number before I forget it.
She always jots a quick note in her planner after meetings.
He jotted something on a scrap of paper and handed it to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - to write down a short message or reminder
  • Synonym: scribble - to write something quickly and carelessly

Etymology

From Latin 'jota', the smallest letter in the Greek alphabet, indicating something very small or slight.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008101-v
Synset POS: v
