trismus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪz.məs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪz.məs / (BrE)
a prolonged and often painful spasm of the jaw muscles, typically causing difficulty in opening the mouth
spasm of the jaw muscles
medical

Examples

Difficulty opening the mouth due to trismus can complicate dental procedures.
Patients with tetanus often experience severe trismus as an early symptom.
The dentist recognized trismus and referred the patient to a specialist.

Etymology

From Greek 'trismos' (a grinding or gnashing), via Latin.
