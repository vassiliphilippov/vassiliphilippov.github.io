bacillary

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæsəˌlɛri / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləri / (BrE)
relating to, produced by, or containing bacilli (rod-shaped bacteria)
relating to bacilli
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the illness as a bacillary infection caused by contaminated water.
Tuberculosis is a well-known bacillary disease affecting millions worldwide.
Microscopic examination revealed a bacillary presence in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacillus - rod-shaped bacterium

Etymology

From Latin 'bacillus' (little rod) + '-ary'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbæsəˌleri / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləri / (BrE)
shaped like a bacillus; resembling or relating to a rod-shaped bacterium, or pertaining to bacilli.
shaped like a bacillus; relating to bacilli
medical

Examples

Certain bacteria exhibit a distinctly bacillary shape under the microscope.
The disease is caused by a bacillary organism that infects the lungs.
In this laboratory, we study bacillary infections like tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacillus - a rod-shaped bacterium

Etymology

From Latin 'bacillus' (small rod), a diminutive of 'baculum' (rod, staff), with scientific suffix '-ary'.

Synset POS: s
