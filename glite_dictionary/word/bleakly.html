bleakly

1. ADV

/ ˈbliːkli / (AmE), / ˈbliːkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses a lack of hope, encouragement, or cheer; in a gloomy or grim manner.
in a hopeless or gloomy way

Examples

He stared bleakly out of the window, unable to see any solution to his problems.
'There's nothing more we can do,' she said bleakly, her voice heavy with resignation.
After hearing the bad news, he laughed bleakly, struggling to find any optimism.

Synset Examples

he wondered bleakly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a manner that is sad and without hope
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic way

Etymology

From 'bleak' (Old Norse 'bleikr', meaning pale, wan) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
