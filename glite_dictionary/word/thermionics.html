thermionics

1. NOUN

/ θɜrˌmaɪˈɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːm.aɪˈɒn.ɪks / (BrE)
the branch of electronics that deals with the emission of electrons from heated substances, especially with thermionic vacuum tubes
study of thermionic phenomena in electronics

Examples

He specialized in thermionics during his postgraduate studies in electrical engineering.
Recent advancements in thermionics have improved the efficiency of certain electronic devices.
The laboratory focused its research on thermionics and its applications in vacuum tube technology.

Etymology

From 'thermionic' (from Greek thermē 'heat' + 'ion') + the suffix '-ics', denoting a field of study.
