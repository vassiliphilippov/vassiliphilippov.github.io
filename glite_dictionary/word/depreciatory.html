depreciatory

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpriʃ.i.əˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / dɪˈpriː.ʃi.ə.tər.i / (BrE)
tending to decrease or cause a decrease in value, especially in relation to currency or assets
causing decrease in value

Examples

The sudden drop in exports had a depreciatory effect on the national currency.
Investors are wary of policies with potentially depreciatory impacts on market confidence.
A series of depreciatory trends undermined the stock's long-term value.

Synset Examples

a depreciating currency
depreciatory effects on prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciatory - tending to increase or cause an increase in value

Etymology

From Latin 'depretiatus' (to lower in value), from 'de-' (down) and 'pretium' (price).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈpriːʃiˌɛˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃiətəri / (BrE)
expressing disapproval or belittling someone or something; tending to diminish or devalue
expressing disapproval or belittling

Examples

The president ignored the depreciatory comments made by his critics during the interview.
Her review of the restaurant was unnecessarily depreciatory and hurt the business's reputation.
Using depreciatory language can damage relationships and undermine confidence.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittling - expressing the act of making someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: appreciative - showing or feeling gratitude or approval

Etymology

From Latin depreciatus, past participle of depreciare (to reduce the value of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
