amphidiploid

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fɪˈdɪp.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈdɪp.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(Genetics) An organism or cell having a complete diploid set of chromosomes from each parent species.
organism with two diploid chromosome sets
medical

Examples

The new variety of wheat is an amphidiploid created by combining two different species.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of the amphidiploid under various environmental conditions.
The amphidiploid plants exhibited traits inherited from both parental species.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both) + 'diploid' (double number of chromosomes).
