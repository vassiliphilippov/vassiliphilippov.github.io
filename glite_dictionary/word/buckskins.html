buckskins

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌskɪnz / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.skɪnz / (BrE)
Breeches made from buckskin leather, especially those worn as part of traditional or historical dress.
breeches made of buckskin leather

Examples

The frontiersman wore buckskins and carried a powder horn on his belt.
His buckskins were worn and stained from years of use.
In the film, the explorer was dressed in traditional buckskins and a coonskin cap.

Etymology

buckskin + -s; refers to garments made from the skin of a male deer (buck).
