freezing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a liquid turns into a solid due to a loss of heat, especially as a result of low temperature
process of liquid turning solid by cold

Examples

The freezing of water occurs at 0°C under standard conditions.
Certain chemicals can lower the freezing point of water.
The quick freezing of food helps preserve its taste and nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melting - the process of becoming liquid as a result of heat
  • Synonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid, especially from liquid state
