endodontics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈdɑn.tɪks / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈdɒn.tɪks / (BrE)
The branch of dentistry concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of diseases affecting the dental pulp and tissues surrounding the roots of teeth.
branch of dentistry concerning dental pulp
medical

Examples

After consulting a specialist in endodontics, she scheduled a root canal to save her tooth.
Advances in endodontics have greatly improved the success rate of complex dental procedures.
He decided to pursue endodontics because he was fascinated by the intricate anatomy of the tooth's interior.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dentistry - the profession or science of treating teeth, gums, and related areas

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) + odous, odont- (tooth) + -ics (study of).
