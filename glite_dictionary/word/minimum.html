minimum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməm / (BrE)
the smallest amount or number that is possible or allowed
smallest possible amount or number

Examples

You need at least the minimum amount of experience to qualify for the job.
The temperature fell to its minimum during the night.
Five dollars is the minimum you can spend to get free shipping.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maximum - the greatest possible quantity
  • Degree Higher: minimum wage - the lowest wage permitted by law or a special agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'minimum' (the smallest thing), neuter of 'minimus' (smallest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməm / (BrE)
In mathematics, the lowest point on a curve, where the slope of the tangent changes from negative to positive.
lowest point on a curve (math)

Examples

The function has a local minimum at x = 2, where the graph reaches its lowest point.
To locate the global minimum, examine where the derivative equals zero and the slope changes sign.
A quadratic equation forms a parabola that opens upward, with its vertex at the minimum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maximum - the highest point on a curve; where the tangent changes from positive to negative

Etymology

From Latin minimum (the smallest quantity), neuter of minimus (smallest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08617830-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈmɪnɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməm / (BrE)
the least or smallest amount, degree, or quantity possible or permitted.
least possible amount or degree (adj.)

Examples

There is a minimum age requirement to enter the competition.
Each player must pay a minimum fee before joining the club.
