claustrophobic

1. ADJ

/ klɔstrəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / klɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
suffering from or affected by claustrophobia; having an abnormal fear of confined or enclosed spaces.
afraid of confined or small spaces

Examples

She feels extremely claustrophobic in elevators and always avoids them when possible.
The small, windowless room made him so claustrophobic that he had to leave immediately.
During the flight, her claustrophobic tendencies became apparent as she struggled to stay calm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agoraphobic - someone with a fear of open or crowded spaces
  • Synonym: claustrophobia - the fear of closed spaces

Etymology

From claustrophobia + -ic, modeled on 'phobic'.

2. ADJ

/ klɔstrəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / klɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of a space) uncomfortably small, closed, or confining; making one feel trapped or uneasy.
uncomfortably closed or confined

Examples

The elevator felt so claustrophobic that I started to panic.
People avoid the claustrophobic basement because it has no windows and a low ceiling.
He hates claustrophobic spaces like crowded buses or small closets.

Synset Examples

a claustrophobic little room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spacious - having lots of space; opposite of confined or cramped

Etymology

From claustrophobia + -ic, modelled on related adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00560838-s
Synset POS: s
