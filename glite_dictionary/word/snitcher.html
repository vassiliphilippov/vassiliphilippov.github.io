snitcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪtʃə / (BrE)
a thief who steals without using violence
non-violent thief

Examples

The snitcher quietly slipped a wallet from the distracted shopper's bag.
Police suspected that the snitcher was responsible for the string of pickpocket incidents downtown.
Unlike a mugger, a snitcher avoids confrontation and relies on stealth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪt͡ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪt͡ʃə / (BrE)
a person who informs police or authorities about the activities of others, often secretly or betraying trust
a person who acts as an informer

Examples

The snitcher provided the police with critical information about the robbery.
No one in the gang trusted the snitcher after his betrayal.
Rumors spread quickly once everyone learned there was a snitcher among them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informer - a person who gives information, especially to the police
  • Synonym: snitch - a person who secretly informs on others
  • Antonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime

Etymology

From 'snitch' (to inform), with the agentive '-er' suffix.
