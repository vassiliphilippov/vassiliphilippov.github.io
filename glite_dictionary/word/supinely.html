supinely

1. ADV

/ suˈpaɪnli / (AmE), / suːˈpaɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that involves lying on one's back with the face upward
lying on the back, face upward

Examples

She lay supinely beneath the stars, gazing at the night sky.
The patient was positioned supinely on the examination table for the procedure.
After her fall, she landed supinely and did not move for several minutes.

Synset Examples

she was stretched supinely on her back

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prone - lying flat, especially face downward

Etymology

From Latin 'supinus' (lying on the back) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ suˈpaɪnli / (AmE), / suːˈpaɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that shows indifference, passivity, or a lack of resistance; in a supine manner
in a passive or indifferent manner

Examples

Despite seeing the injustice, he supinely accepted the decision without protest.
The board supinely agreed to all the changes without any objections.
Rather than take action, the government responded supinely to the growing crisis.

Synset Examples

he called the tune to me and I supinely took it up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passively - in a passive manner; not actively
  • Antonym: actively - in an active or energetic manner; not passively

Etymology

From 'supine' (Latin supīnus, lying on the back) + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00473471-r
Synset POS: r
