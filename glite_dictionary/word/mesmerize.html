to mesmerize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪz / (BrE)
to induce hypnosis in someone; to put someone in a hypnotic state
induce hypnosis

Examples

The therapist was able to mesmerize the patient into a deep state of relaxation.
Stage performers often mesmerize their subjects to demonstrate the power of suggestion.
She claimed that he could mesmerize people with nothing more than his voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hypnotize - to put into a state of hypnosis

Etymology

From Franz Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of animal magnetism.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020664-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪz / (BrE)
to capture and hold the complete attention of someone as if by magic; to fascinate deeply
captivate or fascinate completely

Examples

The magician seemed to mesmerize the entire audience with his performance.
Children are often mesmerized by the dazzling lights of the city.
Her beautiful voice mesmerized everyone in the room, making them pause in silence.

Synset Examples

She magnetized the audience with her tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone deeply
  • Antonym: bore - to cause someone to feel weary and uninterested

Etymology

From the name of Franz Anton Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician whose hypnotic techniques led to the term.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00779567-v
Synset POS: v
