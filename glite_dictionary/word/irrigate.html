to irrigate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪr.ɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪr.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to supply land or crops with water through channels, pipes, or streams, especially to aid growth
to supply land or crops with water

Examples

Farmers irrigate their fields during the dry season to ensure the crops receive enough water.
The new system will help irrigate thousands of acres of farmland efficiently.
Before planting, we irrigated the land thoroughly to improve soil moisture.

Synset Examples

Water the fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water - to pour or supply water, especially to plants

Etymology

From Latin irrigare (to water), from in- (upon) + rigare (to water).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to supply a wound or body part with a continuous flow or spray of liquid, typically to cool, cleanse, or disinfect it
to rinse or cleanse with liquid
medical

Examples

The nurse will irrigate the wound to remove all debris before bandaging it.
Doctors sometimes irrigate the ear canal to clear out excess wax.
To prevent infection, you should irrigate the area thoroughly with sterile saline solution.

Synset Examples

irrigate the wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rinse - to wash lightly with water or another liquid
  • Synonym: to cleanse - to make something thoroughly clean

Etymology

From Latin irrigare (to moisten, water), from in- (in, upon) + rigare (to water).

Synset POS: v
