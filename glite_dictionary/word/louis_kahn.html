Louis Kahn

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːɪs kɑn / (AmE), / ˈluːɪs kɑːn / (BrE)
(1901–1974) United States architect, born in Estonia, known for pioneering modernist architectural designs.
(1901–1974) U.S. architect, modernist

Examples

The modernist buildings designed by Louis Kahn are celebrated for their monumental style.
Many architecture students study the works of Louis Kahn for inspiration.
Louis Kahn's design for the Salk Institute is considered a masterpiece of twentieth-century architecture.
