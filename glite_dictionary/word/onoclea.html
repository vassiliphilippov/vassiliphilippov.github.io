Onoclea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.nəˈkliː.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.nəˈkliː.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of ferns, notable for the sensitive fern, native to North America and East Asia.
genus of ferns (sensitive fern)

Examples

The Onoclea is best known for its species commonly called the sensitive fern.
Gardeners often plant Onoclea in moist, shaded areas for ornamental purposes.
Botanists studied the reproductive cycle of Onoclea in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitive fern - a common North American fern belonging to the genus Onoclea

Etymology

From Greek *onokleia*, name for a kind of fern.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.nəˈkliː.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒn.əˈkliː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns with only one species, the sensitive fern (Onoclea sensibilis), sometimes classified in the Polypodiaceae family.
genus of ferns (sensitive fern)

Examples

The Onoclea genus is best known for the sensitive fern found in North America.
Botanists debate whether Onoclea belongs in the Polypodiaceae or Dennstaedtiaceae family.
You can spot Onoclea sensibilis by its distinct fronds in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitive fern - the only species in the Onoclea genus

Etymology

From Greek onos (donkey) + kleio (to close), referring to the covered fruit clusters.
