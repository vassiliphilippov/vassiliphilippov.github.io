epitope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌtoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛp.ɪ.təʊp / (BrE)
the part of an antigen molecule to which an antibody binds
site on antigen where antibody binds
medical

Examples

The scientist identified the epitope recognized by the monoclonal antibody.
Each epitope is a specific site recognized by components of the immune system.
Vaccines are often designed to include a key epitope from a virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antigen - a substance that stimulates an immune response

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) and 'topos' (place).
