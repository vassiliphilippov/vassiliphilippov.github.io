tribuneship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪb.jun.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.juːn.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a tribune, especially in ancient Rome
office or position of tribune

Examples

He was elected to the tribuneship at a young age, reflecting his popularity among the citizens.
The tribuneship granted its holder the power to veto Senate decisions in ancient Rome.
After his tribuneship, he continued to play a major role in public affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribune - an official in ancient Rome chosen by the people to protect their interests

Etymology

From 'tribune' + '-ship', denoting an office or position.
