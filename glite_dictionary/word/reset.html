reset

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈriːˌsɛt / (BrE)
a button, switch, or device used to restore instruments or controls to their original settings, especially after an error or interruption
device for restoring settings to original state

Examples

After the power surge, he pressed the reset to restore the machine's normal operation.
The console has a small reset at the back for troubleshooting.
If the display freezes, use the reset to reboot the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reset button - a button that when pressed resets a device
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04086067-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛt / (BrE)
to adjust or set again, especially after an error or failure
set or adjust again after failure

Examples

If your phone freezes, you may need to reset it to fix the problem.
After the error, she decided to reset the device and try again.
He had to reset the clock when the power went out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restart - to start again from the beginning

Etymology

From re- (again) + set; used since the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00298450-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˌriːˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛt / (BrE)
to set something again, or to restore it to its original state
set again or restore

Examples

After the storm, they had to reset the outdoor clocks around the campus.
Please reset your password if you have forgotten it.
She reset the alarm to 6:00 AM after realizing she needed to wake up earlier.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ ˌriˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛt / (BrE)
to set a device, instrument, or system back to zero or its initial state.
set to zero or initial state

Examples

Before leaving, please reset all the instruments to zero.
If you enter the wrong code, the system will automatically reset after one minute.
The technician reset the dials before starting a new experiment.
