Brachychiton rupestris

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈkaɪtən ruˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrækiˈkaɪtən ruˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A large tree native to Queensland, Australia, with a swollen trunk and cream-colored, red-blotched flowers; commonly known as the Queensland bottle tree.
tree species native to Queensland, Australia

Examples

The Brachychiton rupestris stands out in the landscape due to its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk.
Gardeners in Australia prize the Brachychiton rupestris for its drought-resistant qualities.
In spring, the Brachychiton rupestris produces striking cream flowers with red blotches inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Queensland bottle tree - common name for Brachychiton rupestris

Etymology

From Greek *brachys* (short) and *chiton* (tunic), and Latin *rupestris* (of rocks).
