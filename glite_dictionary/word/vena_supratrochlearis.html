vena supratrochlearis

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nə ˌsuː.prə.trəˈklɛər.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˌsuː.prə.trəˈklɪə.rɪs / (BrE)
a vein that drains the front of the scalp and joins with the supraorbital vein to form the angular vein
vein draining front of scalp
medical

Examples

In anatomy class, we learned that the vena supratrochlearis is important in draining blood from the forehead.
During the surgical procedure, the surgeon identified the vena supratrochlearis before making an incision above the eye.
Swelling near the eyebrow can sometimes affect the vena supratrochlearis, leading to localized discomfort.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) + 'supra' (above) + 'trochlea' (pulley, referring to the trochlea of the eye).
