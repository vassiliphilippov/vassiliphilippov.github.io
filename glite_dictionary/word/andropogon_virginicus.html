Andropogon virginicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌændrəˈpoʊɡən vəˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌandrəˈpəʊɡɒn vəˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
A tall, tufted grass native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as broomsedge.
a tall grass of the southeastern US

Examples

Andropogon virginicus thrives in poor, sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
Fire can help manage fields overrun by Andropogon virginicus populations.
Farmers often remove Andropogon virginicus to improve the quality of their pastures.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Andropogon' from Greek 'anēr' (man) + 'pōgōn' (beard), referring to the bristly appendages; 'virginicus' refers to Virginia.
