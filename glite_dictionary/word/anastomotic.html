anastomotic

1. ADJ

/ əˌnæstəˈmɑtɪk / (AmE), / əˌnastəˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or having anastomosis (a connection or opening between two things that are normally diverging or branching, especially between blood vessels or other tubular structures in the body)
relating to anastomosis (connection between vessels or tubes)
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor checked the anastomotic site for any signs of leakage.
Complications may arise if proper anastomotic healing does not occur after the procedure.
Research focuses on improving anastomotic techniques to reduce postoperative risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anastomosis - a connection or opening between two vessels, tubes, or parts of the body

Etymology

From French 'anastomotique', from 'anastomose' (anastomosis), ultimately from Greek 'anastomosis' (opening, providing a mouth).
