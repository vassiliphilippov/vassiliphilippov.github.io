aeroembolism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈɛmbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛərəʊˈɛmbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by the formation of gas bubbles in the blood or tissues due to a rapid change in atmospheric pressure, resulting in pain and potentially serious symptoms.
medical: gas bubbles in body due to pressure change
medical

Examples

Divers are at risk of aeroembolism if they ascend too quickly to the surface.
Symptoms of aeroembolism may include joint pain, dizziness, and difficulty breathing after rapid decompression.
Proper training helps pilots and astronauts prevent aeroembolism during operations at high altitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decompression sickness - the condition caused by rapid decrease in atmospheric pressure, often called 'the bends'

Etymology

From 'aero-', relating to air, and 'embolism', from Greek 'embolos' (plug, stopper).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈɛmbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊˈɛmbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A blockage in a blood vessel caused by an air bubble, often occurring accidentally during surgery, injections, or as a complication of scuba diving.
blockage by air bubble in blood vessel
medical

Examples

The patient developed a aeroembolism after air accidentally entered the vein during the operation.
Divers are at risk of aeroembolism if they ascend too quickly to the surface.
Medical professionals take precautions to avoid aeroembolism during intravenous procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embolism - blockage of a blood vessel by a clot or foreign material

Etymology

From 'aero-' (air) + 'embolism' (blockage of a blood vessel), based on Greek roots.
