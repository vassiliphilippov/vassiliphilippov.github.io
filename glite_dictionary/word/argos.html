Argos

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrɡoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡɒs / (BrE)
an ancient city in southeastern Greece, known for its prominence in the Peloponnese during the 7th century BC.
ancient Greek city in the Peloponnese

Examples

The ruins of Argos attract historians and tourists interested in ancient Greece.
According to legend, Hera was especially worshipped in Argos.
During the 7th century BC, Argos was one of the most powerful city-states in the region.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἄργος (Árgos), the name of the city.
