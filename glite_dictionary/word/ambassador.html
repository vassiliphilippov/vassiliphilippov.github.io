ambassador

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbæsədər / (AmE), / æmˈbæsədə / (BrE)
An official representative of the highest rank sent by one country to another as its resident representative.
a highest-ranking diplomatic representative

Examples

The United States appointed a new ambassador to France last year.
As ambassador, she worked to strengthen diplomatic ties between the two countries.
The ambassador attended the state dinner at the royal palace.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: envoy - a diplomatic representative ranked below an ambassador
  • Degree Lower: consul - an official appointed to live in a foreign city to protect a country's interests

Etymology

From Old French ambassadeur and Italian ambasciatore, from Medieval Latin ambactiare (to go on a mission).

2. NOUN

/ æmˈbæsədər / (AmE), / æmˈbæsədə / (BrE)
a person who acts as a representative or promoter of a particular activity, cause, or group, especially in an informal capacity
person serving as a representative or promoter

Examples

As an ambassador for the charity, she speaks at events and visits local schools.
He acted as an ambassador of the sport, inspiring young athletes across the country.
The company appointed her as their brand ambassador to promote their new line of products.

Synset Examples

an ambassador of good will

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envoy - a representative or messenger, especially one on a diplomatic mission

Etymology

From Latin 'ambassiator', from 'ambactus' meaning servant or messenger; influenced by French 'ambassadeur'.
