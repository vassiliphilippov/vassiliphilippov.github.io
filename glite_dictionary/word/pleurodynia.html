pleurodynia

1. NOUN

/ ˌplʊroʊˈdɪniə / (AmE), / ˌplʊərəˈdɪnɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by severe chest pain, often due to inflammation of the muscles between the ribs.
chest pain from muscle inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with pleurodynia after he complained of acute chest pain when breathing.
Pleurodynia can sometimes be mistaken for a heart attack due to its intense symptoms.
Most cases of pleurodynia resolve within a few days, but the pain can be quite severe.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) and 'odynē' (pain).
