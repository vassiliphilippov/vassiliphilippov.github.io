enatic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnætɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈnætɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or descended from the mother's side of a family.
related on mother's side

Examples

The inheritance rules allowed only those of enatic descent to claim the title.
Some cultures place special significance on enatic lines when determining family legacy.
My enatic ancestors came from a different region than my paternal ones.

Synset Examples

my maternal grandmother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnatic - related on the father's side
  • Synonym: maternal - relating to a mother, especially on the mother's side

Etymology

From Latin enatare (to be born from), from e- (from) + nasci (to be born).
