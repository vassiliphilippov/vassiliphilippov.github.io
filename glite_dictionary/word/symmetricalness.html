symmetricalness

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmɛtrɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛtrɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being symmetrical; exact correspondence of form and arrangement on opposite sides of a dividing line or plane.
quality of being symmetrical

Examples

The symmetricalness of the snowflake fascinated the children as they examined it under a microscope.
Architects praised the symmetricalness of the building's facade, which created a sense of harmony.
In mathematics, the symmetricalness of a graph can reveal important properties about its structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetry - the quality of having parts that match each other, especially in a balanced and attractive way

Etymology

From 'symmetrical' + '-ness.'
