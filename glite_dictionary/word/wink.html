wink

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
a rapid closing and opening of one eye, often as a reflex or signal
quick closing and opening of one eye

Examples

She gave him a quick wink as she walked by.
He tried to hide his excitement, but a nervous wink betrayed him.
The bright light made my eye do a reflexive wink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to close and open both eyes quickly

Etymology

Old English *wincian* (to wink or blink)
