carburettor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrbjəˌreɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbjʊˌrɛtə / (BrE)
a device in an internal combustion engine that mixes air with a fine spray of liquid fuel before ignition
engine part mixing air and fuel

Examples

The vintage car's carburettor needed cleaning to ensure a smooth ride.
Mechanics often adjust the carburettor to optimize fuel efficiency and performance.
After replacing the carburettor, the engine started up without any trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel injector - device that introduces fuel directly into the combustion chamber

Etymology

From French *carburateur*, from *carbure* (carbide) + *-ateur* (agent), relating to carbon.
