iceberg

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with crisp, tightly packed, pale green leaves forming a firm round head, commonly used in salads.
variety of crisphead lettuce

Examples

She bought a fresh iceberg for the dinner salad.
Many restaurants serve burgers with shredded iceberg for crunch.
The recipe calls for two cups of chopped iceberg.

Synset Examples

iceberg is still the most popular lettuce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable, often used in salads

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A large floating mass of ice, detached from a glacier, found in the sea.
large floating mass of ice at sea

Examples

The ship struck an iceberg and began to sink rapidly.
Many icebergs break off from Antarctica each year and drift into shipping lanes.
Only a small part of an iceberg is visible above the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacier - a large slow-moving mass of ice on land

Etymology

From Dutch ijsberg, meaning 'ice mountain'.
