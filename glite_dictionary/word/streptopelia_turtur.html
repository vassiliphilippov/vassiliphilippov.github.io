Streptopelia turtur

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛp.toʊˈpiː.li.ə ˈtɜːr.tər / (AmE), / stɹɛp.təˈpiː.li.ə ˈtɜː.tər / (BrE)
the scientific name for the European turtle dove, a wild European dove known for its soft, plaintive cooing
scientific name for European turtle dove

Examples

The scientific name for the turtle dove is Streptopelia turtur, often used in ornithological studies.
Conservation efforts for Streptopelia turtur are crucial due to declining populations across Europe.
Many poets have been inspired by the mournful call of Streptopelia turtur in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle dove - a wild Eurasian dove known for its gentle cooing

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'peleia' (dove); 'turtur' is Latin for 'turtle dove'.
