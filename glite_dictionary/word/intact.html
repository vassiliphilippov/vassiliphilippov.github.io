intact

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtækt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtækt / (BrE)
whole and not damaged; complete and lacking nothing essential
undamaged; whole; complete

Examples

Despite the accident, the package arrived intact with nothing broken or missing.
The ancient vase remained intact after centuries buried underground.
They inspected the equipment and found every part still intact.

Synset Examples

a local motion keepeth bodies integral
was able to keep the collection entire during his lifetime
fought to keep the union intact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole - complete, with no parts missing
  • Antonym: damaged - harmed or impaired; no longer in perfect condition

Etymology

From Latin intactus, from in- (not) + tactus (touched, from tangere ‘to touch’).

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtækt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtækt / (BrE)
remaining whole or undamaged; not altered, broken, or impaired in any way.
undamaged; whole; unbroken

Examples

Despite the accident, the antique mirror remained intact and unscathed.
She was grateful that all her luggage arrived intact after a long journey.
The castle walls have stood intact for over five centuries.

Synset Examples

the vase remained intact despite rough handling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: damaged - impaired or harmed; opposite of intact
  • Synonym: whole - not broken or incomplete; complete and intact

Etymology

From Latin intactus (untouched), from in- (not) + tactus (touched).
