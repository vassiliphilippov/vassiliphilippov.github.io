orange

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A round citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind and sweet, juicy, edible flesh.
a sweet, round citrus fruit

Examples

Would you like an orange or an apple for your snack?
She peeled the orange and handed segments to the children.
Freshly squeezed orange juice is perfect for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a small, sweet citrus fruit similar to an orange

Etymology

From Old French 'orenge', from Arabic 'nāranj', from Persian 'nārang', from Sanskrit 'nāraṅga'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹəndʒ, ˈɔɹɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒɹɪndʒ / (BrE)
A color that is between red and yellow in the spectrum, resembling the hue of ripe oranges.
color between red and yellow

Examples

She wore a bright orange dress to the party.
The leaves turned a beautiful shade of orange in autumn.
We painted the wall orange to create a warm, cheerful atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red - the color at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange
  • Degree Higher: yellow - the color at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange

Etymology

From Old French 'orenge', ultimately from Arabic 'nāranj', originally referring to the fruit; the color is named after the fruit.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒ, ˈɑːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A citrus tree of the genus Citrus, especially Citrus × sinensis, that bears sweet, round, orange-colored fruits.
a citrus tree bearing oranges

Examples

The orange in our backyard produces fruit every winter.
She planted an orange in her garden, hoping it would thrive.
Farmers in Florida grow thousands of orange trees for their fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange (fruit) - the sweet, round citrus fruit with a tough orange rind

Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: yellow - the color between green and orange in the spectrum
