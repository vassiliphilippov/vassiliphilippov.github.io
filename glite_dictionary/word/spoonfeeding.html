spoonfeeding

1. NOUN

/ ˈspuːnˌfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of feeding someone, especially a baby, with a spoon.
feeding someone using a spoon

Examples

The mother spent the morning spoonfeeding her infant.
Sometimes spoonfeeding is necessary when a child is very young or ill.
Hospitals often require spoonfeeding for patients with difficulty swallowing.

Etymology

Formed from 'spoon' and 'feeding', indicating the action of feeding with a spoon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspuːnˌfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of teaching or giving information in an overly simple and detailed way that discourages independent thinking or initiative.
overly simplified, discouraging independence

Examples

Many teachers worry that too much spoonfeeding prevents students from developing critical thinking skills.
The course relied on spoonfeeding instead of encouraging students to explore topics independently.
Some learners prefer spoonfeeding, but others thrive when challenged to find solutions on their own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoon-feed - to provide someone with so much help or information that they are not allowed to think for themselves

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'spoon-feed'; 'spoon' + 'feeding'.
