Genista raetam

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə reɪˈtam / (AmE), / dʒɛˈnɪstə reɪˈtam / (BrE)
a desert shrub native to Syria and Arabia, with small white flowers, traditionally identified as the juniper or broom of the Old Testament
desert shrub of Syria and Arabia; Old Testament juniper

Examples

The Genista raetam thrives in arid regions of the Middle East, particularly in the sandy plains of Syria and Arabia.
Some scholars believe that the plant referred to as juniper in the Old Testament was actually Genista raetam.
The delicate white flowers of Genista raetam make it a striking sight in the desert landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - a type of shrub or small tree often mentioned in biblical contexts
  • Synonym: broom - a group of shrubs in the genus Genista

Etymology

Genista: Latin for broom plant; raetam: from the Arabic name for the plant.
