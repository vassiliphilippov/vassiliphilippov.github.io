reprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈriː.prɪnt / (BrE)
a printed publication, such as a book or article, that has been published again without changes or editing
a publication printed again without changes

Examples

The second reprint of the novel sold out within a week.
This reprint includes the original illustrations and text.
Collectors often look for the first edition rather than a reprint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or printing of a book or publication

Etymology

From the prefix 're-' meaning 'again' and 'print'.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print (a book, article, or other published material) again, especially in a new edition or after the original run has sold out
print again; produce a new edition

Examples

The publisher decided to reprint the classic novel after its popularity surged online.
If this journal article is accepted, we may reprint it in a special anniversary issue.
Many authors hope their out-of-print books will one day be reprinted for new audiences.

Synset Examples

They never reprinted the famous treatise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: print - to produce books, newspapers, or other materials by pressing inked type or plates onto paper

Etymology

From re- (again) + print; first used in the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01739979-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈriː.prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈriː.prɪnt / (BrE)
a separately printed copy of an article, essay, or other material that was originally published in a larger collection or periodical
separately printed copy of original article

Examples

The professor handed out a reprint of his latest journal article to the students.
She kept a reprint of the 1954 Times editorial in her files.
Many scientists request reprints of published research for reference purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reissue - a new issue of something previously published
  • Antonym: original - the first publication, as opposed to a reprint

Etymology

From re- (again) + print, meaning to print again.
