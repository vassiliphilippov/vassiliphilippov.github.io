Sylvilagus palustris

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvaɪləɡəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / sɪlˈvaɪlɪɡəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of wood rabbit found in marshy coastal areas from North Carolina to Florida, known as the marsh rabbit.
marsh rabbit species of southeastern US

Examples

The Sylvilagus palustris is well adapted to life in the wetlands of the southeastern United States.
Scientists have studied Sylvilagus palustris to understand its unique swimming abilities.
Unlike cottontail rabbits, Sylvilagus palustris prefers marshy environments with dense vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh rabbit - common name for Sylvilagus palustris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sylvilagus' meaning forest hare, 'palustris' meaning 'of marshes'.
