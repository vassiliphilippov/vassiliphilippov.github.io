whistle-stop

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəl stɑp / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl stɒp / (BrE)
a brief stop at a small place, especially by a train or during a political tour
brief stop at small place (train, tour)

Examples

The train made a whistle-stop at the remote village late in the evening.
During her campaign, she visited several towns in a whirlwind whistle-stop tour.
The bus itinerary included only a whistle-stop at the museum before moving on.

Synset Examples

The train made a whistle-stop at the small town to pick up passengers.
The bus made a whistle-stop at various attractions along the route.
During the campaign, they held a whistle-stop tour across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layover - a period of waiting between connections, especially in air travel

Etymology

Originally from the practice of trains making unscheduled stops at small stations, signaled by a whistle.
