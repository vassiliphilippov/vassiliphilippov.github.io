Sericocarpus

1. NOUN

/ sɛrɪkoʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛrɪkəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbaceous plants native to the eastern United States, commonly known as white-topped asters.
genus of white-topped asters

Examples

The Sericocarpus plants thrive in open woodlands and grassy meadows across the eastern United States.
Botanists often study Sericocarpus to understand the diversity within the aster family.
White-topped asters of the genus Sericocarpus provide important nectar sources for pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'serikos' (silky) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the silky fruits of the plants.
