facing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a strip of material sewn to the edge of a garment as a lining for ornamentation or to strengthen the edge
lining on the edge of a garment

Examples

The dressmaker added a silk facing to give the collar a clean edge.
A cotton facing was applied to the hem for extra durability.
Without a proper facing, the jacket's edge would have frayed easily.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of material, often decorative, applied to the surface of a building for ornamentation or protection
decorative surface layer of a building

Examples

The marble facing on the hotel gave it an elegant appearance.
They chose a brick facing to complement the building's original design.
The new entrance features a stone facing that enhances the building's curb appeal.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer or surface of one material applied to the surface of another, often for protection or decoration
outer surface of a different material

Examples

The stone facing on the building gives it an elegant appearance.
Many jackets are designed with a silk facing on the lapels.
Engineers recommended adding a concrete facing to reinforce the dam.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00829040-n

4. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a protective covering or material that is applied to the outside of a building to protect or improve its appearance.
protective exterior covering of a building

Examples

The stone facing on the building gave it a classic, elegant look.
Workers repaired the damaged facing after the storm had loosened several panels.
The architect recommended a brick facing to enhance both durability and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladding - outer layer applied to a building for protection or appearance
  • Synonym: veneer - a thin decorative covering of fine material applied to the surface of something

Etymology

From the verb 'face', ultimately from Old French 'facer' (to face).
