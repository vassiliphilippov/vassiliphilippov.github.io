grouper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːpər / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpə / (BrE)
a large, heavy-bodied fish found in warm seas, typically living alone near the sea bottom; a type of sea bass
large bottom-dwelling sea fish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious fillet of grouper with lemon and herbs.
Divers often spot a solitary grouper hiding among the coral reefs.
The fisherman was thrilled to catch a massive grouper during his trip to the Gulf of Mexico.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'garoupa', referring to these types of fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːpər / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpə / (BrE)
The flesh of a large saltwater fish similar to a sea bass, commonly eaten as food.
the flesh of a type of edible saltwater fish

Examples

The restaurant special tonight features grilled grouper with lemon butter sauce.
Fresh grouper is a popular choice in many coastal cuisines around the world.
She ordered the fried grouper sandwich at the beachside cafe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - the flesh of a marine fish used as food

Etymology

From Portuguese 'garoupa', referring to this type of fish.
