Parmenides

1. PROPN

/ pɑːrˈmɛnəˌdiːz / (AmE), / pɑːˈmɛnɪdiːz / (BrE)
(c. 5th century BC) A presocratic Greek philosopher whose metaphysical doctrine stated that being is the fundamental reality and that all change and motion are illusions.
presocratic Greek philosopher; being as ultimate reality

Examples

Parmenides argued that reality is unchanging and any perception of change is merely an illusion.
The philosophy of Parmenides greatly influenced later thinkers such as Plato and Aristotle.
Modern scholars continue to debate the impact of Parmenides on metaphysical thought.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Παρμενίδης (Parmenidēs), a personal name.
