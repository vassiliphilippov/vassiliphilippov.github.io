weakfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish of the Atlantic coast of the United States, valued for its lean flesh as food and as a game fish
a type of Atlantic coastal fish

Examples

The weakfish is prized by anglers along the Atlantic coast for its delicious taste.
In the summer, schools of weakfish migrate closer to shore, attracting many fishermen.
She prepared a simple dish with grilled weakfish and seasonal vegetables for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croaker - another common name for fishes related to weakfish

Etymology

Named for the perceived weakness of its jaw, making it easy to lose from fishing lines.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwikˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish (Cynoscion regalis) found along the Atlantic coast of North America, valued for food and sport, known for its delicate mouth tissue
Atlantic North American game fish

Examples

The weakfish is highly prized by anglers along the Atlantic coast for its delicate flavor.
He caught a large weakfish on his first cast of the morning.
Because the mouth of the weakfish is fragile, fishermen must handle it with care when removing the hook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common freshwater game fish
  • Synonym: bluefish - a North American coastal predatory game fish

Etymology

So called because its mouth tissue is weak, making it prone to tearing when hooked.
