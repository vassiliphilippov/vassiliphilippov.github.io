annalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to annals or the recording of events in chronological order, especially year by year.
pertaining to annals or yearly records

Examples

The historian adopted an annalistic method, documenting events year by year.
This annalistic chronicle provides a detailed timeline of the king’s reign.
Some critics argue that annalistic accounts lack deeper narrative analysis.

Synset Examples

a book with an annalistic approach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronological - arranged according to the order of time

Etymology

From Latin annālis (annual), from annus (year) + -istic.
