hexapod

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛksəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈhɛksəpɒd / (BrE)
an animal with six legs or feet, especially an insect
animal with six legs (esp. insect)

Examples

A bee is a typical example of a hexapod found in gardens.
Unlike spiders, which have eight legs, all insects are classified as hexapods.
Some robotic designs mimic the structure of a hexapod to achieve stability and mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - small arthropod animal with a three-part body and six legs

Etymology

From Greek hexa- (six) + pous, pod- (foot).
