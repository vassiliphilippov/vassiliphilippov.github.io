stroppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈstrɒpi / (BrE)
bad-tempered and difficult to deal with; easily annoyed or argumentative.
bad-tempered and difficult; easily annoyed
british informal

Examples

She can get quite stroppy if things don't go her way.
The manager grew stroppy when questioned about the new policy.
Don’t be so stroppy—just explain what's bothering you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstreperous - noisy, difficult to control
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to agree; not difficult

Etymology

Originally British slang, perhaps alteration of 'obstreperous.'
