anechoic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ɪˈkoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪˈkəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a room or material) designed to absorb sound or electromagnetic waves and not produce echoes.
not producing echoes; sound-absorbent

Examples

The scientists conducted their experiments in an anechoic chamber to eliminate background noise.
Recording studios often use anechoic panels to prevent sound reflections.
Her voice sounded strangely flat in the anechoic environment of the testing room.

Synset Examples

an anechoic chamber

Related Terms

  • Antonym: echoic - producing or characterized by echoes

Etymology

From Greek ἀν- (an-, not) + ἠχώ (ēkhō, echo) + -ic.
