promoter

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / prəˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a person who actively supports or encourages a cause, event, or idea; an advocate or supporter
active supporter or advocate

Examples

As a promoter of animal rights, she organizes many events to raise awareness.
The charity's promoter worked tirelessly to gain more supporters for the cause.
He has always been a strong promoter of environmental protection policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - person who publicly supports a cause or policy
  • Antonym: opponent - person who disagrees with or resists a cause or idea

Etymology

From Latin promotus, past participle of promovere (to move forward) via Old French promoteur.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / prəˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a person or company that organizes and publicizes public events, especially entertainment such as concerts, sports, or shows
person who organizes or publicizes events

Examples

The promoter secured a famous band to headline the summer music festival.
As a promoter, she is responsible for booking venues and advertising concerts.
Many young artists rely on a promoter to gain exposure and attract fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges or coordinates events or activities
  • Synonym: sponsor - a person or organization that supports an event financially or through resources

Etymology

From Latin 'promotor', from 'promovere' meaning 'to move forward'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10613153-n
Synset POS: n
