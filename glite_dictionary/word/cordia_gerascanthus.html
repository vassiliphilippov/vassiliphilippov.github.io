Cordia gerascanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrdiə dʒəˌræskænθəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪə dʒəˌræskænθəs / (BrE)
a tropical American timber tree valued for its durable wood
tropical American timber tree

Examples

The wood of the Cordia gerascanthus is highly prized for making furniture.
Botanists studied the Cordia gerascanthus for its unique resistance to local pests.
Many traditional craftsmen prefer Cordia gerascanthus due to its strength and beauty.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *cordia*, after Valerius Cordus (a German botanist); species epithet from Greek *geraskantos* meaning 'growing old'.
