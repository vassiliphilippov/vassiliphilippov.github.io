before

1. ADV

/ bɪˈfɔr / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔː / (BrE)
In or towards the front; ahead, especially in position or place relative to something else.
at or in the front; ahead

Examples

The leader rode before at the head of the procession.
She stood before everyone else, ready to welcome the guests.
A scout was sent before to check if the path was safe.

Synset Examples

I see the lights of a town ahead
the road ahead is foggy
staring straight ahead
we couldn't see over the heads of the people in front
with the cross of Jesus marching on before

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ahead - further forward in space or position; in front
  • Antonym: behind - at or toward the back of something; in the rear

Etymology

From Old English 'beforan' (in front of, before), from be- (by) + foran (before).

2. ADV

/ bɪˈfɔr / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔː / (BrE)
at an earlier time; previously
at an earlier time; previously

Examples

I've heard that story before, but I still enjoy it every time.
Please make sure you finish your homework before you watch television.
She had never traveled abroad before last summer.

Synset Examples

I had known her before
as I said before
he called me the day before but your call had come even earlier
her parents had died four years earlier
I mentioned that problem earlier

Related Terms

  • Antonym: after - at a later time; subsequently
  • Synonym: earlier - at or near the beginning of a period of time

Etymology

From Old English 'beforan' (in front, earlier), from 'be' (by) + 'foran' (before).
