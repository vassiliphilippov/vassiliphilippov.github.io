uneasiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈizɪnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːzɪnəs / (BrE)
a feeling of discomfort or anxiety, especially in the body or mind
a feeling of discomfort or anxiety

Examples

She felt a growing sense of uneasiness as the storm approached.
His uneasiness grew as he started to feel mildly ill.
There was an air of uneasiness in the waiting room before the test results were announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discomfort - slight pain or unease
  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease
  • Antonym: ease - freedom from discomfort or worry

Etymology

Formed from 'uneasy' + '-ness'. 'Uneasy' is from Middle English, combining 'un-' (not) and 'easy'.
