Todidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdiˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, brightly colored birds found in the Caribbean, known as the todies.
family of Caribbean birds (todies)

Examples

The Todidae are endemic to the Caribbean islands and are known for their vibrant plumage.
Researchers studied the nesting habits unique to birds in the Todidae family.
Due to habitat loss, some species within the Todidae are considered at risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tody - A small, brightly colored Caribbean bird; member of Todidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Todidae', based on 'Tod(us)' (genus) and Latin '-idae' (family suffix).
