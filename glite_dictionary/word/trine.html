trine

1. NOUN

/ traɪn / (AmE), / traɪn / (BrE)
(Archaic) The number three; a group of three.
archaic word for the number three
archaic

Examples

In old English verse, a trine often symbolized harmony or unity.
The magician spoke of a sacred trine guiding the ritual.
She arranged the candles in a trine upon the altar, invoking ancient tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three - the number after two, equivalent to 3

Etymology

From Latin 'trinus' (three each), related to 'tres' (three).
