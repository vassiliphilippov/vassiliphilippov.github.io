subsequently

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt.li / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt.li / (BrE)
After something else has happened; following in time or order.
afterward; at a later time

Examples

He was arrested and subsequently released without charge.
The committee will meet next week, and subsequently issue a report on its findings.
She moved to Paris and subsequently found a job in fashion.

Synset Examples

he apologized subsequently
he's going to the store but he'll be back here later
it didn't happen until afterward
two hours after that

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterwards - at a later time; subsequently
  • Antonym: previously - before the time mentioned

Etymology

From Latin 'subsequentem', present participle of 'subsequi' meaning 'to follow closely'.
