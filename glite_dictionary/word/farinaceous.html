farinaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌfærəˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌfarɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
resembling or containing starch; having a mealy or powdery texture similar to that of flour or starch
resembling or containing starch

Examples

The potatoes had a farinaceous texture, making them ideal for mashing.
Many roots and tubers are considered farinaceous due to their high starch content.
The chef preferred farinaceous vegetables for thickening soups and sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealy - having a dry, powdery texture like meal or flour

Etymology

From Latin farinaceus, from farina meaning 'flour'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɛrəˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌfarɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
composed of or resembling meal or flour in texture or consistency; mealy.
having a meal-like or floury texture

Examples

The potatoes had a farinaceous quality when boiled, making them ideal for mashing.
Some seeds have a farinaceous coating that feels powdery to the touch.
The botanist noted the farinaceous texture of the plant's tuber during her fieldwork.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealy - having a texture or appearance like meal; powdery or crumbly
  • Synonym: granular - having a grainy texture; composed of small particles

Etymology

From Latin farinaceus, from farina (flour, meal).
