delusory

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈluːsəri / (AmE), / dɪˈljuːsəri / (BrE)
giving a false or misleading impression; likely to deceive or mislead someone into believing what is not true.
giving a false impression; deceptive

Examples

The investor was lured by a delusory sense of security in the unstable market.
Her apparent happiness was merely a delusory mask for her anxiety.
The treaty created a delusory hope for lasting peace in the region.

Synset Examples

deceptive calm
a delusory pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceptive - giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading

Etymology

From Latin delūsōrius, from delūdere (to delude).
