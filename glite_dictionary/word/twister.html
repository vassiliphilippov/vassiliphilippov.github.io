twister

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪstər / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstə / (BrE)
A small, rich cake made by twisting strips of dough and deep frying them, similar to a doughnut.
fried twisted pastry

Examples

The bakery sells delicious twisters coated in cinnamon sugar.
She learned how to make twisters from her grandmother's old recipe book.
A plate of fresh twisters was served with coffee for breakfast.

Etymology

From 'twist' combined with the suffix '-er', indicating something that twists or is twisted.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstə / (BrE)
A violently destructive windstorm occurring over land, typically characterized by a funnel-shaped cloud extending toward the ground; a tornado.
a destructive windstorm; a tornado

Examples

The small town was devastated when a twister touched down last spring.
Storm chasers drove hundreds of miles to see a twister in action.
Residents took shelter in their basements as the twister approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tornado - a violently rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground

Etymology

From 'twist' + '-er', referring to the rotation of the wind.
