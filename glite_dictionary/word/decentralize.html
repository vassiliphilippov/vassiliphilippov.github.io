to decentralize

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛntrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to transfer authority, control, or responsibility from a central organization or government to local branches or smaller units
transfer control from center to local units

Examples

Many companies decentralize their operations to respond more quickly to regional needs.
There is a growing movement to decentralize power within government.
After the system was decentralized, local managers gained significant autonomy.

Synset Examples

After the revolution, food distribution was decentralized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to centralize - to bring under central control or authority

Etymology

From French 'décentraliser', based on 'central'.
