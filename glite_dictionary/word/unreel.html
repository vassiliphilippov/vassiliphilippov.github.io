to unreel

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈril / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːl / (BrE)
to unwind or release (something) from or as if from a reel
to unwind from a reel

Examples

He began to unreel the fishing line to check for tangles.
The technician had to unreel several meters of cable for the new connection.
As the story continued, her memories seemed to unreel like an old film.

Synset Examples

unreel the tape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - a cylinder or device for winding and storing line, tape, film, etc.
  • Antonym: wind - to wrap or coil (something) around a reel or spool

Etymology

From prefix un- (reverse action) + reel (a cylinder for winding material); formed in analogy to unwind.
