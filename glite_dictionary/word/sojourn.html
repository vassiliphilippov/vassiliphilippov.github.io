sojourn

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.dʒɝn / (AmE), / ˈsɒdʒ.ɜːn / (BrE)
a temporary stay in a place, especially as a visitor or guest
a temporary stay

Examples

During his sojourn in Paris, he visited many famous art museums.
Their mountain sojourn lasted only two weeks but was unforgettable.
The writer described her brief sojourn at the seaside cottage in her memoir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visit - go to see or spend time in a place as a guest
  • Antonym: residence - the act or period of living in a place

Etymology

From Old French sojorner, based on Latin subdiurnare (to spend the day).

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊˌdʒɝn / (AmE), / ˈsɒdʒən / (BrE)
to stay in a place temporarily; to reside for a short period.
stay temporarily

Examples

During their European tour, the artists sojourned in Paris for several weeks.
He will sojourn at his aunt's cottage while searching for a new apartment.
Travelers often sojourn in remote villages to experience local traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to remain in a place for a period of time
  • Antonym: to reside - to live in a place permanently or for a long time

Etymology

From Old French 'sojorner', from Vulgar Latin '*subdiurnare' (to spend the day).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02715296-v
Synset POS: v
