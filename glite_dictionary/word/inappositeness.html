inappositeness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈæpəzɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈapəzɪtnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being not suitable or appropriate for a particular situation or purpose.
lack of suitability or appropriateness

Examples

The inappositeness of his comments distracted from the topic of discussion.
Her response was marked by a certain inappositeness that made the audience uncomfortable.
Critics noted the inappositeness of the analogy used in the speech.

Synset Examples

greater inaptness of expression would be hard to imagine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriateness - the quality of being unsuitable or improper
  • Antonym: aptitude - the quality of being suitable or appropriate

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + apposite, from Latin appositus (well placed) + -ness (noun-forming suffix).
