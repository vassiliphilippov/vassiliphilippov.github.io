Oryza sativa

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈraɪzə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ɒˈraɪzə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Asian rice plant, cultivated extensively as a staple food crop
species of rice plant

Examples

Researchers are studying Oryza sativa to improve crop yields and disease resistance.
The cultivation of Oryza sativa is vital for food security in many Asian countries.
Scientists compared wild species with domesticated Oryza sativa to understand its evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice - commonly cultivated cereal grain; staple food made from the seeds of Oryza sativa

Etymology

From Latin 'Oryza' (rice) and 'sativa' (cultivated).
