reappraisal

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈpreɪzəl / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈpreɪzəl / (BrE)
the act of appraising, assessing, or evaluating something again, often with a new perspective or updated information
a new assessment or evaluation

Examples

After several years, a reappraisal of the policy revealed significant shortcomings.
The museum announced a reappraisal of its entire art collection.
He called for a reappraisal of the city’s approach to traffic management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal - assessment or evaluation
  • Synonym: review - careful examination or assessment

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'appraisal' (from 'appraise', meaning to assess value).
