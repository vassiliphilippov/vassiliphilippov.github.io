Pima

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːmə / (AmE), / ˈpiːmə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people living mainly in southern Arizona and northern Mexico.
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Pima have a rich tradition of basket weaving and pottery.
Many Pima reside on reservations in southern Arizona.
Anthropologists have studied Pima agricultural techniques for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tohono O'odham - another Native American group of southern Arizona

Etymology

From Spanish, derived from the Pima people's native term for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːmə / (AmE), / ˈpiːmə / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Pima people of Arizona and northern Mexico
language of the Pima people

Examples

Few people today speak Pima as their first language, but revitalization efforts are underway.
The Pima language has several dialects depending on the region.
She studied Pima linguistics for her graduate thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uto-Aztecan - a family of indigenous languages spoken in the western United States and Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Pima people, who call themselves Akimel O'odham.
