injured

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪndʒərd / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəd / (BrE)
physically harmed, especially as a result of an accident or attack
physically harmed or hurt

Examples

Several injured passengers were taken to the hospital after the crash.
The injured player was helped off the field by the medical team.
Emergency services arrived quickly to assist the injured workers.

Synset Examples

injured soldiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wounded - injured by a cut or piercing, especially in battle
  • Synonym: hurt - physically damaged or in pain
  • Antonym: unharmed - not hurt or injured

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'injure', from Latin 'injuriare' (to wrong, injure), from 'iniuria' (injustice, injury).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪndʒɚd / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəd / (BrE)
feeling emotionally hurt, upset, or offended, especially because of someone's actions or words
emotionally hurt or offended

Examples

He felt deeply injured by their harsh criticism and avoided speaking with them for days.
She gave him an injured look, as if his comment had genuinely wounded her feelings.
His injured pride prevented him from apologizing, even though he knew he was wrong.

Synset Examples

she looked offended
face had a pained and puzzled expression
injured feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offended - feeling resentful or hurt due to perceived insult or disregard
  • Synonym: hurt - emotionally pained or distressed
  • Antonym: comforted - feeling reassured or consoled after distress
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01812324-s
Synset POS: s
