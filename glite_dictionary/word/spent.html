spent

1. ADJ

/ spɛnt / (AmE), / spɛnt / (BrE)
having lost all energy, force, or strength; depleted or exhausted
depleted; exhausted

Examples

After the marathon, she felt completely spent and could barely walk home.
The soil was spent after years of intensive farming and needed to be replenished.
By midnight, most of our energy was spent, so we decided to call it a night.

Synset Examples

impossible to grow tobacco on the exhausted soil
the exhausted food sources
exhausted oil wells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - depleted of strength or energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - restored in strength or energy

2. ADJ

/ spɛnt / (AmE), / spɛnt / (BrE)
Having no energy or strength left; completely exhausted or worn out.
completely exhausted; worn out

Examples

After the long hike, I was completely spent and could hardly move.
She felt spent after working late for several nights in a row.
By the end of the marathon, every runner looked utterly spent.

Synset Examples

the day's shopping left her exhausted
he went to bed dog-tired
was fagged and sweaty
the trembling of his played out limbs
felt completely washed-out
only worn-out horses and cattle
you look worn out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired; drained of energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - feeling rested and energetic again

Etymology

Past participle of 'spend', from Old English 'spendan' via Latin 'expendere' (to weigh out, pay).
