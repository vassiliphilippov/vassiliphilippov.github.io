bunko

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkəʊ / (BrE)
a dishonest scheme, especially one involving cheating at gambling or persuading someone to buy something worthless; a swindle or confidence trick
swindle; confidence trick
informal american

Examples

The police arrested the group running a bunko operation in the city.
He fell victim to a bunko scheme that promised double his money overnight.
Many elderly people are targeted by bunko artists pretending to sell valuable investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: con - a trick or scam used to deceive someone
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat or defraud someone

Etymology

Originally American slang, probably from Spanish 'banco' (card table, bank), related to gambling.
