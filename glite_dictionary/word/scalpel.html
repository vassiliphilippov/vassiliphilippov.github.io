scalpel

1. NOUN

/ ˈskælpəl / (AmE), / ˈskælpəl / (BrE)
a small, sharp, straight knife used by surgeons for operations and dissections
small surgical knife

Examples

The surgeon carefully picked up the scalpel to begin the operation.
Medical students learn to handle a scalpel during their anatomy classes.
He sterilized the scalpel before making the first incision.

Etymology

From French 'scalpel', from Latin 'scalpellum' (a small knife), diminutive of 'scalper' (a knife).
