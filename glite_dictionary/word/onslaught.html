onslaught

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnˌslɔt / (AmE), / ˈɒn.slɔːt / (BrE)
a fierce or destructive attack, especially a large-scale one carried out with force and determination
a fierce or destructive attack

Examples

The fortress withstood the initial onslaught but fell during the second wave.
They faced an onslaught of criticism after announcing the policy changes.
The town was evacuated before the onslaught of the hurricane.

Synset Examples

the attack began at dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Antonym: defense - the action of resisting attack

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'onslag' (attack) or Middle Low German 'onslâg'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːn.slɑːt / (AmE), / ˈɒn.slɔːt / (BrE)
a violent or sudden attack, or a large number of people or things that arrive at the same time and cause difficulty
a sudden or violent attack

Examples

The city faced an onslaught of criticism after the new policy was announced.
During the storm, an onslaught of rain flooded the streets within minutes.
The defenders prepared for the enemy’s onslaught at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Synonym: assault - a physical attack
  • Antonym: defense - the act of protecting from attack

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *onslag* (attack), influenced by Dutch *aanslag*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07320778-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnˌslɔt / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌslɔːt / (BrE)
a strong, rapid, and continuous flow of spoken or written communication; a sudden and overwhelming outpouring of something such as questions, complaints, or criticism
sudden intense outpouring, especially of speech or writing

Examples

The politician faced an onslaught of questions from reporters after the press conference.
Our customer service team struggled to manage the onslaught of complaints that arrived after the product recall.
During the meeting, the CEO dealt with an onslaught of criticism regarding the new policy.

Synset Examples

a barrage of questions
a bombardment of mail complaining about his mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid outpouring of many things at once, especially speech or questions
  • Synonym: bombardment - continuous attack or outpouring, often of questions or criticism

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *onslach* (attack, stroke) via obsolete Dutch *onslag*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07097633-n
Synset POS: n
