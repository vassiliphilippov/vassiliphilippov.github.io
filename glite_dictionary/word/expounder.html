expounder

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspaʊndər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaʊndə / (BrE)
a person who explains or interprets something, especially by providing detailed information or commentary
person who explains or interprets

Examples

She is regarded as the leading expounder of modern legal theory in her country.
The documentary featured an expounder of economic history who summarized the key events.
As an expounder of complex scientific ideas, Dr. Kim is unparalleled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreter - a person who explains the meaning of something
  • Synonym: explainer - a person who explains

Etymology

From the verb 'expound', from Old French 'expondre', from Latin 'exponere' (to put forth, explain).
