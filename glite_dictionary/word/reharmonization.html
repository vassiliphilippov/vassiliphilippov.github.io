reharmonization

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌhɑːrmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌhɑːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of revising or altering the harmony of an existing piece of music
alteration of a piece's original harmony

Examples

The jazz pianist performed a reharmonization of the standard, giving it a fresh harmonic structure.
Her creative reharmonization made the old folk tune sound completely new to the audience.
Many arrangers use reharmonization as a tool to reinterpret familiar songs in unique ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a version of a musical composition adapted for performance by a particular set of voices or instruments

Etymology

From re- (again) + harmonization, based on harmony; formed in music theory.
