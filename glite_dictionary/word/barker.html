barker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrkər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːkə / (BrE)
a person who stands outside a carnival or sideshow, calling out to attract customers and encourage them to enter
carnival showman who attracts customers

Examples

The barker shouted enticing promises to the crowds passing the haunted house.
With a booming voice, the barker convinced dozens of people to buy tickets to the sideshow.
Every carnival seemed to have at least one energetic barker at the entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crier - a person who announces or proclaims things loudly in public

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrkɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːkə / (BrE)
(informal) A dog, especially one that barks a lot.
informal term for a dog
informal

Examples

The neighborhood barker kept us up all night with its loud howling.
He adopted an old barker from the animal shelter last year.
You'd better watch out—there's a mean barker loose in the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal (Canis familiaris)
