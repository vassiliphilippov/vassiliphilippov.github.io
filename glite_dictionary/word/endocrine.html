endocrine

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛndəˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊkraɪn / (BrE)
Relating to glands that secrete hormones directly into the bloodstream or to their secretions.
relating to hormone-secreting glands
medical

Examples

The endocrine system regulates many body functions through hormone release.
Certain diseases can affect multiple endocrine glands at the same time.
Researchers study how endocrine disruptors impact human health.

Synset Examples

endocrine system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exocrine - relating to glands that secrete externally through ducts

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) + krinein (to separate).
