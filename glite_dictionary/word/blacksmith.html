blacksmith

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈblakˌsmɪθ / (BrE)
a person who makes and repairs things out of iron by heating and hammering them into shape
a person who forges iron objects

Examples

The village blacksmith repaired tools and made horseshoes for the townspeople.
She apprenticed with a skilled blacksmith to learn the craft of metalworking.
The clang of the blacksmith's hammer echoed through the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smith - a worker in metal, especially by shaping iron

Etymology

From Middle English 'blaksmith', literally 'black' (referring to the black metal iron) + 'smith'.
