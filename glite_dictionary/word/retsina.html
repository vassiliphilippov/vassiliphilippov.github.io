retsina

1. NOUN

/ rɛtˈsiːnə / (AmE), / rɛtˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A Greek white or rosé wine flavored with pine resin, traditionally made in Greece.
Greek resin-flavored wine

Examples

We tried the local retsina during our trip to Athens, and its unique taste surprised us.
The waiter recommended a bottle of retsina to pair with our Greek dishes.
Some people enjoy the pine flavor of retsina, while others find it unusual.

Etymology

From Modern Greek 'retsína', from Italian 'resina' (resin).
