Emile Gaboriau

1. PROPN

/ eɪˈmiːl ɡæbɔːrˈjoʊ / (AmE), / eɪˈmiːl ɡabɔːrˈjoʊ / (BrE)
(1832–1873) French writer regarded as a founder of the detective novel.
French writer, detective novel pioneer

Examples

Many scholars credit Emile Gaboriau with laying the groundwork for modern detective fiction.
The works of Emile Gaboriau were highly influential in shaping the genre.
I recently finished a novel by Emile Gaboriau, and I was impressed by his intricate plotting.
