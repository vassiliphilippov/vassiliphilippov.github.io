generality

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.əˈræl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.əˈræl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being general, widespread, or broadly applicable rather than specific or limited.
the quality of being general or widespread

Examples

The generality of his remarks made them applicable to many situations.
Scientists seek generality in their theories to ensure broad applicability.
She criticized the plan for its lack of generality and narrow focus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specificity - the quality of being specific rather than general
  • Synonym: universality - the quality of being universal or applicable everywhere

Etymology

From Late Latin generalitas, from Latin generalis (general).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.əˈræl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.əˈræl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a statement, idea, or principle that is broad and not specific, and can be applied in many situations
a broad, non-specific idea or principle

Examples

The manager criticized the report for its reliance on vague generalities rather than concrete details.
His speech was filled with generality and lacked any real substance.
While discussing climate change, she preferred to speak in generalities rather than mention particular policies.

Synset Examples

he spoke in broad generalities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specificity - the quality of being clearly defined or identified

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'generalitas', from Latin 'generalis' (general).
