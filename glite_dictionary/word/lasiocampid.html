lasiocampid

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪsi.oʊˈkæmpɪd / (AmE), / ˌlæsi.əˈkæmpɪd / (BrE)
A moth of the family Lasiocampidae, typically medium-sized, stout-bodied, and neutral-colored, with comb-like antennae.
a moth of the family Lasiocampidae

Examples

The lasiocampid rested quietly on the bark, its neutral coloring blending into the background.
Many lasiocampids are active at night and attracted to lights.
The distinctive comb-like antennae are a key feature of a typical lasiocampid.

Etymology

From New Latin Lasiocampidae, combining Greek lasio- (hairy) and kampē (caterpillar), with the suffix -id denoting family members.
