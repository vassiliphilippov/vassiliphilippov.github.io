pincer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnsər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnsə / (BrE)
a hand tool with two handles and curved jaws used for gripping, pulling, or cutting objects, typically nails or wires
hand tool with jaws for gripping or cutting

Examples

The carpenter used a pair of pincers to pull out the old nails.
I found some rusty pincers in my grandfather's toolbox.
Be careful when using pincers to avoid pinching your fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pliers - hand tool with two handles for gripping objects

Etymology

From Old French *pincier* (to pinch), from Vulgar Latin *pinciāre*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnsər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnsə / (BrE)
a grasping claw or structure on the limb of a crustacean or other arthropods, used for holding or manipulating things
grasping claw of a crustacean or arthropod

Examples

The crab used its pincer to defend itself from predators.
A lobster's large pincer can deliver a surprisingly strong grip.
The scientist carefully examined the beetle's pincer under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claw - a curved pointed horny nail on each digit of the foot in birds, lizards, and some mammals

Etymology

From French 'pincier' (to pinch), related to Latin 'pinso' (to beat).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
