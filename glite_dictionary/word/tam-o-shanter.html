tam-o'-shanter

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæm.oʊˈʃæntər / (AmE), / ˌtam.əʊˈʃantə / (BrE)
a round woolen cap with a flat top and usually a pompom in the center, traditionally worn in Scotland
round Scottish woolen cap

Examples

She wore a bright red tam-o'-shanter to the festival, adding a Scottish touch to her outfit.
The old photograph shows children in kilts and tam-o'-shanters posing by the riverbank.
He bought a tam-o'-shanter as a souvenir during his trip to Edinburgh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beret - a round, flat-crowned hat, similar style of cap

Etymology

Named after 'Tam o' Shanter,' the hero of a poem by Robert Burns (1790).
