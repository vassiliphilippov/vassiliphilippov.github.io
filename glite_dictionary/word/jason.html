Jason

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪsən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the leader of the Argonauts and husband of Medea, famous for his quest to obtain the Golden Fleece.
hero in Greek mythology; leader of the Argonauts

Examples

The legend of Jason and the Argonauts has inspired many tales throughout history.
In Greek mythology, Jason is known for his quest to find the Golden Fleece.
Jason and Medea's story is a classic example of love and betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argonaut - one of the heroes who accompanied Jason in his quest for the Golden Fleece
  • Synonym: Medea - Jason's wife, a powerful sorceress in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Ἰάσων (Iásōn), meaning 'healer', via Latin 'Jason'.
