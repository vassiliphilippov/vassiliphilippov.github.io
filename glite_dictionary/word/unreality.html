unreality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnriˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unreal, imaginary, or not existing in fact
the state of being unreal

Examples

After his accident, he experienced a strange sense of unreality and confusion.
The play's dreamlike atmosphere blurs the boundaries between reality and unreality.
Many people feel a sense of unreality during times of extreme stress or shock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • Synonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining impossible or improbable things

Etymology

From un- (not) + reality, dating from the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnriˈaləti / (BrE)
the state of being imaginary, insubstantial, or not existing in fact
state of being imaginary or unreal

Examples

After waking from the vivid dream, a feeling of unreality lingered for hours.
The film's special effects created a sense of unreality that captivated the audience.
In moments of shock, people sometimes experience a strange unreality as if the world isn't quite real.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • Synonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining impossible or improbable things

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'reality' (from Latin 'realis', relating to things).

3. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.riˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.riˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a lack of practical engagement or ineptitude in dealing with real situations; a state in which a person is detached from reality
difficulty dealing with reality

Examples

His sense of unreality made it difficult for him to respond to the emergency.
She accused him of unreality when he ignored the obvious problems at work.
The manager's unreality about the company's finances led to poor decisions.

Synset Examples

But unless you get over his blindness, his unreality about something he has already agreed to, he is going against his own agreements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escapism - the tendency to seek distraction and relief from unpleasant realities

Etymology

From un- (not) + reality, after reality from Late Latin realitas.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
