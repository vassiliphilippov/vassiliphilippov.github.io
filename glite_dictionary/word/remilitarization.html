remilitarization

1. NOUN

/ riˌmɪlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːmɪlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of equipping or arming a region, country, or group with military forces or weapons again after a period of disarmament or demilitarization
the act of militarizing again

Examples

The remilitarization of the region sparked international controversy and concern.
Germany's remilitarization of the Rhineland was a key event before World War II.
Some historians debate whether remilitarization was an inevitable response to international tensions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demilitarization - the act of removing military forces or weapons

Etymology

From re- (again) + militarization, modeled after similar constructs in English and derived from 'military' and 'ization.'
