scream

1. NOUN

/ skrim / (AmE), / skriːm / (BrE)
a loud, sharp, piercing cry or sound, especially as an expression of emotion such as fear, pain, or excitement
loud, sharp piercing cry

Examples

A sudden scream from the next room startled everyone at the party.
The child's scream echoed across the playground when she fell.
We heard a scream in the night and called the police.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a high-pitched, piercing cry or sound
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or low speech sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07137720-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skriːm / (AmE), / skriːm / (BrE)
to utter a sudden, loud, and piercing cry, especially from pain, fear, or excitement
utter a loud, piercing cry

Examples

The crowd began to scream in excitement as the band took the stage.
She couldn't help but scream when she saw the spider on her pillow.
He screamed for help when he got trapped in the elevator.

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly
  • Synonym: shout - to speak or call out loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound of a scream.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ skɹim / (AmE), / skɹiːm / (BrE)

informal

Examples

The story she told at dinner was a total scream and had everyone in stitches.
You’ve got to meet Tom; he’s a real scream at parties.

4. VERB

/ skɹim / (AmE), / skɹiːm / (BrE)
to utter or declare something in a very loud, high voice, especially because of strong emotion
to speak or declare very loudly

Examples

She had to scream over the noise of the crowd to be heard.
He screamed his name at the top of his lungs, hoping someone would recognize him.
Please don’t scream your answer; we can all hear you just fine.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout or speak with a loud voice
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using one's breath without vocal cords

to make a loud, high-pitched, and usually piercing sound

Examples

Synset Examples
