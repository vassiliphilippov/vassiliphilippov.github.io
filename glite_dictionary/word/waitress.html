waitress

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtrəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪtrəs / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to serve food and drinks at tables in a restaurant or cafe.
female server in a restaurant

Examples

The waitress brought our drinks quickly and with a smile.
As a college student, she worked as a waitress in a busy diner.
We asked the waitress for a recommendation from the menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waiter - a man who serves food and drinks at a restaurant or cafe

Etymology

From 'waiter' (person who waits on tables) + feminine suffix '-ess'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10783288-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈweɪ.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.trɪs / (BrE)
to work as a female server in a restaurant, bar, or café, taking orders and serving food and drinks to customers
serve as a female restaurant server

Examples

She used to waitress at a busy diner during her college years.
Many students waitress in the summer to earn extra money.
After moving to the city, she easily found work waitressing in various restaurants.

Synset Examples

I'm waiting on tables at Maxim's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waiter - to serve as a male server in a restaurant or café

Etymology

From 'waitress', a feminine form of 'waiter' (from Old Northern French 'waiter', to watch or wait). Verb usage derived from noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02418420-v
Synset POS: v
