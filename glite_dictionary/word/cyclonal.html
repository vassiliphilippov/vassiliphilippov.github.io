cyclonal

1. ADJ

/ saɪˈkloʊ.nəl / (AmE), / saɪˈkləʊ.nəl / (BrE)
of or relating to a cyclone or the atmospheric conditions around a low-pressure center; characteristic of cyclones.
related to a cyclone or low-pressure system

Examples

The cyclonal activity intensified as the storm moved closer to land.
Meteorologists observed a distinct cyclonal cloud formation on the satellite images.
Winds in the cyclonal region typically rotate counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere.

Synset Examples

cyclonic cloud pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclonic - of, relating to, or characteristic of a cyclone

Etymology

Formed from 'cyclone' + '-al' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈkloʊnəl / (AmE), / saɪˈkləʊnəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a violent tropical storm or cyclone.
relating to a violent tropical storm

Examples

The region experiences frequent cyclonal storms during the summer months.
Emergency teams responded quickly to the cyclonal flooding along the coastline.
Researchers are studying the effects of cyclonal winds on coastal infrastructure.

Synset Examples

cyclonic destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclonic - relating to or associated with a cyclone

Etymology

From 'cyclone' + '-al', where 'cyclone' derives from Greek 'kyklon' (moving in a circle).
