George IV

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ðə ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ðə ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
(1762–1830) King of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and of Hanover from 1820 to 1830, known for his extravagance and his troubled marriage.
British king (1820–1830), son of George III

Examples

George IV succeeded his father, George III, and reigned during a period of cultural transformation in Britain.
The lavish lifestyle of George IV was often criticized by his contemporaries and historians alike.
During the reign of George IV, the foundation stone for the new London Bridge was laid.
