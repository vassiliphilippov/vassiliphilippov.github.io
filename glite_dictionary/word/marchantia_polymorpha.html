Marchantia polymorpha

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈkænʃiə ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / mɑːˈkæntɪə ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
a common species of liverwort with a flat, green, thalloid body, often found in moist, shaded habitats
a common species of liverwort

Examples

Marchantia polymorpha grows abundantly on damp soil and rocks in temperate regions.
Botanists often use Marchantia polymorpha as a model organism in plant studies.
You can identify Marchantia polymorpha by its distinctive umbrella-shaped reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - a simple, non-vascular plant related to mosses

Etymology

From Latin; genus Marchantia named after Italian botanist Francesco Marchant, species polymorpha meaning 'many forms'.
