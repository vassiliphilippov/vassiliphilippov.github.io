Uvularia grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ juːˌvjuːˈlɛəriə ˌɡrændəˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / juːˌvjʊəˈleəriə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to southern and southeastern United States, cultivated for its yellow flowers that are suitable for drying.
a plant with yellow, dryable flowers

Examples

The garden featured a group of Uvularia grandiflora in full bloom each spring.
Dried Uvularia grandiflora blossoms are often used in floral arrangements.
I identified the yellow flowers as Uvularia grandiflora during my hike through the woods.

Etymology

From Latin *uvula* (little grape, referring to the shape of the flower) and *grandiflora* (large-flowered).
