Carduelis cannabina

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduːəlɪs kænəˈbaɪnə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs kænəˈbɪnə / (BrE)
a small Old World finch, commonly known as the linnet, whose male has a red breast and forehead
species of small finch (linnet)

Examples

The Carduelis cannabina can often be spotted in open countryside across Europe.
Male Carduelis cannabina are easily distinguished by their striking red plumage.
Ornithologists studied the migration patterns of Carduelis cannabina throughout the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linnet - common name for Carduelis cannabina, a small finch

Etymology

From Latin: Carduelis (goldfinch genus) and cannabina (relating to hemp, referring to feeding habits).
