favourable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərəbl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
(of weather or winds) giving an advantage or helping something to happen or succeed.
helpful (of weather/wind)

Examples

The sailors waited patiently for a favourable wind before setting sail.
The weather forecast predicts favourable conditions for our picnic tomorrow.
Early spring rains created favourable weather for the crops to grow.

Synset Examples

the days were fair and the winds were favorable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfavourable - not helpful or making something more difficult
  • Synonym: favorable - American English spelling of 'favourable'

Etymology

From Latin 'favourabilis', from 'favorem' (favor).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪv(ə)rəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
indicating or likely to result in success, good luck, or a positive outcome
likely to bring success or good luck
british

Examples

The team waited for a favourable moment before launching their new product.
She believed that the timing was favourable for making such an important decision.
Conditions were not favourable for hiking, so we postponed the trip.

Synset Examples

a favorable time to ask for a raise
lucky stars
a prosperous moment to make a decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorable - American spelling: likely to result in success or good outcome
  • Antonym: unfavourable - having or showing a negative or adverse result

Etymology

From Latin favorabilis, from favor (goodwill, approval).
