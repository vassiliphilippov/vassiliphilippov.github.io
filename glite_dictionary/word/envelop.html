to envelop

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛləp / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛləp / (BrE)
to enclose or surround something or someone completely, often as if with a covering
to cover or surround completely

Examples

The thick mist slowly enveloped the entire valley as the sun set.
Warmth enveloped her as she stepped into the cozy cottage.
The city was enveloped in darkness after the power outage.

Synset Examples

Fog enveloped the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or on top of
  • Antonym: to unwrap - to remove a covering from

Etymology

From Old French 'enveloper', from Latin 'involvere' (to wrap in).
