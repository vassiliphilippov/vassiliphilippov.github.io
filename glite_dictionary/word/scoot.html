to scoot

1. VERB

/ skuːt / (AmE), / skuːt / (BrE)
to move or go quickly or suddenly, especially in a hurried or playful manner
move or go quickly

Examples

The children scoot down the hallway as soon as the bell rings.
She scooted across the street to catch the bus.
If you scoot over a bit, there will be enough room for everyone on the bench.

Synset Examples

She dashed into the yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - to run or travel somewhere very quickly
  • Synonym: bolt - to move suddenly and quickly, usually to escape
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, slowly

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'scout', influenced by 'scoot' as a slang term in American English (19th century).
