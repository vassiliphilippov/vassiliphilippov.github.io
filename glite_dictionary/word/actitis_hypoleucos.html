Actitis hypoleucos

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtaɪtɪs ˌhaɪpoʊˈliːoʊkəs / (AmE), / ækˈtaɪtɪs ˌhʌɪpəʊˈliːəʊkəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the common sandpiper, a small wading bird commonly found near freshwater throughout Eurasia and Africa
the common sandpiper (a small wading bird)

Examples

The Actitis hypoleucos migrates between Europe and Africa every year.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot an Actitis hypoleucos along the riverbank.
The Actitis hypoleucos is recognized by its distinctive bobbing motion while feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common sandpiper - a small migratory wading bird

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Actitis' reflects the genus (from Greek aktites, meaning 'shore-dweller') and 'hypoleucos' means 'below white', referring to its plumage.
