Thysanocarpus

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌsænəˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / θaɪˌsænəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbaceous plants native to upland regions of the Pacific coast of North America, known for their distinctive flattened, fringed seed pods.
genus of Pacific Coast herbs

Examples

The distinctive seed pods of Thysanocarpus make it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.
Botanists identified the rare plant as a species in the Thysanocarpus genus.
Many Thysanocarpus species thrive in well-drained soils along the Pacific coast.

Etymology

From Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) and karpos (fruit), referring to the fringed seed pods.
