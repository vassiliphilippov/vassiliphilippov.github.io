cinematographer

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪnəməˈtɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəməˈtɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to oversee and operate the camera work and lighting in the making of a film or movie.
person who operates the camera in filmmaking

Examples

The cinematographer skillfully captured the breathtaking landscapes that made the movie visually stunning.
She won an award for her outstanding work as cinematographer on the documentary.
A good cinematographer collaborates closely with the director to achieve the desired visual style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director of photography - the chief cinematographer on a film
  • Degree Lower: camera operator - person who physically operates the camera

Etymology

From French *cinématographe* (cinematograph) + Greek *-graphos* (writer).
