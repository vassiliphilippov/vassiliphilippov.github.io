groveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːvələr / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒvələ / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an excessively submissive way, often showing lack of self-respect to gain favor or approval
person who acts in an excessively submissive way

Examples

No one respected him because he acted like a groveller around his boss.
She refused to become a groveller just to get a promotion.
The king surrounded himself with grovellers who told him only what he wanted to hear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important to gain advantage
  • Synonym: toady - a person who flatters or ingratiates themselves with someone important
  • Synonym: to grovel - to behave in an excessively humble or submissive manner

Etymology

From the verb 'grovel', originally meaning 'to lie with one's face to the ground', from Middle English 'groveling'.
