limpidity

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪmˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear and easy to understand; clearness of expression or thought
clearness of expression or thought

Examples

The limpidity of the author's prose made the book enjoyable for readers of all ages.
Her arguments were presented with impressive limpidity, leaving no room for confusion.
The journalist's limpidity ensured that complex topics became accessible to a general audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular in expression or appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'limpidus' (clear), via French 'limpide'.

2. NOUN

/ lɪmˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪmˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear, transparent, or able to transmit light without distortion or diffusion
clarity or transparency

Examples

The limpidity of the mountain lake made it possible to see all the way to the bottom.
Scientists tested the limpidity of the new type of glass for use in camera lenses.
In the sunlight, the limpidity of the crystal became even more apparent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to see through
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque, not allowing light to pass through

Etymology

From Latin 'limpidus' (clear, transparent).

Synset POS: n
