checkout

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃek.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʃek.aʊt / (BrE)
the act of inspecting, verifying, or testing something, especially as part of a procedure or system
act of inspecting or verifying

Examples

The mechanic performed a thorough checkout of the engine before the test flight.
Every piece of equipment must pass a routine checkout before being cleared for use.
After the repairs, the team carried out a system checkout to ensure everything was working properly.

Synset Examples

they made a check of their equipment
the pilot ran through the check-out procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - the act of examining or evaluating closely
  • Synonym: examination - detailed analysis or scrutiny

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'check out,' meaning 'to inspect or examine', combining 'check' + 'out'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌaʊt / (BrE)
the latest time by which a guest must leave their hotel room.
latest time to leave a hotel room

Examples

The checkout time at most hotels is before noon.
We requested a late checkout so we could enjoy the morning at the pool.
She rushed back to her room to pack before checkout.

Synset Examples

the checkout here is 12 noon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: check-in - time when guests may register and occupy a hotel room

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'check out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15206117-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk.aʊt / (BrE)
A counter or area in a supermarket or store where customers pay for their goods.
counter where you pay in a store

Examples

I paid for my groceries at the checkout before leaving the supermarket.
The store opened a new checkout to reduce the waiting time for customers.
She realized she had forgotten her wallet only when she was standing at the checkout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: till - machine or counter for handling payments in a shop
  • Synonym: cash register - machine at a checkout used to register and total transactions

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'check out', meaning to pay for goods before leaving a store.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03015395-n
Synset POS: n
