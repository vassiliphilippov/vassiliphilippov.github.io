periselene

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪsɪˈliːni / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪsɪˈliːni / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a satellite at which it is nearest to the Moon
point in orbit closest to the Moon

Examples

The spacecraft reached periselene before beginning its descent toward the lunar surface.
At periselene, the probe was just 100 kilometers above the Moon's surface.
Adjustments to the satellite's orbit affected the altitude at its next periselene.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apolune - point in lunar orbit farthest from the Moon
  • Synonym: periapsis - point in an orbit closest to the body being orbited

Etymology

From Greek peri- (around, near) + Selēnē (Moon, the Moon goddess).
