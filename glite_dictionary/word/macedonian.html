Macedonian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdəʊniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Macedon, an ancient kingdom located in the northern part of Greece
a native or inhabitant of Macedon

Examples

Many ancient Macedonians played a significant role in the spread of Hellenistic culture.
Archaeologists have discovered tombs belonging to Macedonian nobles.
The army was composed largely of Macedonian soldiers loyal to Alexander the Great.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Makedonikos, relating to Macedon.

3. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌmasɪˈdəʊnɪən / (BrE)
the Slavic language spoken in North Macedonia and neighboring regions
Slavic language of North Macedonia

Examples

She is fluent in both Macedonian and English.
The documentary was subtitled in Macedonian for local audiences.
He is studying Macedonian at the university as part of his linguistics degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bulgarian - Slavic language of Bulgaria
  • Synonym: Serbian - Slavic language of Serbia

Etymology

From Macedonia, the region in southeastern Europe, with the suffix -ian.
