blackcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈblækˌkæp / (BrE)
a small brownish-grey warbler, typically with a black crown, found in Europe and parts of Asia and North Africa
small warbler with a black crown

Examples

A shy blackcap sang in the hedgerow at dawn.
We spotted several blackcaps while walking in the woods yesterday.
The distinctive song of the blackcap is often heard in spring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈblækˌkæp / (BrE)
a small European gull with a black head, especially the black-headed gull (Chroicocephalus ridibundus).
small European gull with a black head

Examples

A flock of blackcaps was seen resting on the sandbar by the river.
Birdwatchers identified the blackcap by its distinctive call and dark head.
In spring, the arrival of blackcaps marks the changing season along the coast.

Etymology

From 'black' + 'cap', referring to the distinct black coloring on the head of the bird.

4. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈblæk.kæp / (BrE)
A North American chickadee (Parus atricapillus) with a distinctive black crown on its head.
a chickadee with a black crown

Examples

A blackcap perched quietly on the feeder, searching for seeds.
We spotted a blackcap during our hike in the pine forest.
Unlike other birds, the blackcap is easily identified by its dark crown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickadee - a small North American bird of the tit family, often with a black cap
