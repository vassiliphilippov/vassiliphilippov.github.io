to mosh

1. VERB

/ mɑʃ / (AmE), / mɒʃ / (BrE)
to dance energetically in a violent manner, typically by jumping and pushing in a crowd, especially at rock or punk music concerts
to dance wildly at concerts
informal

Examples

A group of teenagers began to mosh near the stage as the band played their loudest song.
He loves to mosh at punk concerts and rarely misses a chance to join the pit.
Fans pushed and moshed in front of the stage, caught up in the energy of the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam dance - to dance in a similar energetic and aggressive manner at concerts
