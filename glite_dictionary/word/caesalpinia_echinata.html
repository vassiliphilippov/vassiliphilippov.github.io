Caesalpinia echinata

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ˌɛkɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˌsiːsælˈpɪniə ˌɛkɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical tree with a prickly trunk, valued for its heavy red wood, which is used for cabinetry and produces a red dye.
tropical tree used for red dye and wood

Examples

The prized wood of the Caesalpinia echinata has been exported for centuries.
Dyes extracted from Caesalpinia echinata were once used to color textiles in Europe.
Many musical instruments are still made from the sturdy wood of Caesalpinia echinata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazilwood - common name for Caesalpinia echinata, a tree yielding red dye and valued wood

Etymology

From the genus name 'Caesalpinia,' honoring Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino, and Latin 'echinata' meaning 'spiny' or 'prickly.'
