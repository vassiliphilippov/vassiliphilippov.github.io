individuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality or process of being individual or becoming distinct and separate from others
the state of being individual or distinct

Examples

The psychologist explained that individuation is essential for personal growth.
Her journey toward individuation helped her discover her unique identity.
Over time, individuation allows people to develop their own beliefs and values.

Synset Examples

so absorbed by the movement that she lost all sense of individuality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individuality - the quality that makes one person or thing different from others

Etymology

From Medieval Latin individuatio (rendering individual), based on Latin individuus (indivisible).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of distinguishing or differentiating an individual from others in a group or species
process of distinguishing an individual

Examples

The process of individuation is essential for a child to develop a distinct identity.
Philosophers have debated the mechanisms of individuation for centuries.
In Jungian psychology, individuation refers to integrating different aspects of the self into a unified whole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differentiation - the process of becoming different or making something different

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'individuatio', from Latin 'individuus' (indivisible).

Synset POS: n
