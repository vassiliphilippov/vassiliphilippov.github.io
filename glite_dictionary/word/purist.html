purist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrɪst / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪst / (BrE)
a person who insists on strict correctness or precision, especially in matters of language, style, or practice
someone who insists on strict correctness or precision

Examples

As a purist, she refuses to use any shortcuts in her baking.
The purist in the group insisted that only classical techniques should be used.
Although some call him a purist, he prefers to think of himself as a perfectionist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickler - someone who insists on something unyieldingly; especially standards or rules
  • Antonym: liberal - a person open to new behavior or opinions, typically not strict about rules

Etymology

From French puriste, from pur (pure) +‎ -iste (person concerned with).
