cytoarchitecture

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˈɑrkɪˌtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the cellular makeup and arrangement of cells in a bodily structure, especially tissues or organs
cellular structure and arrangement

Examples

The cytoarchitecture of the cerebral cortex varies between different regions of the brain.
Scientists study cytoarchitecture to understand how the organization of cells relates to brain function.
Research in neuroscience often examines the cytoarchitecture of tissues to diagnose diseases.

Etymology

From cyto- (cell) + architecture, patterned on German 'Zytoarchitektur'.
