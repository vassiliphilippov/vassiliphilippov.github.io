reactivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːækˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the property of being readily susceptible to chemical change or reaction; the degree to which a substance reacts chemically.
susceptibility to chemical change

Examples

The reactivity of sodium makes it dangerous to handle in air or water.
Scientists measure the reactivity of different chemicals to predict possible reactions.
Due to its low reactivity, gold does not tarnish easily.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: activity - the state of being active; chemistry: the effective concentration of a substance in a reaction

Etymology

From react + -ivity, modelled after 'activity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being responsive to stimulation or change, especially to a stimulus or external influence
responsiveness to stimuli or change

Examples

The doctor assessed the patient's reactivity by testing her reflexes.
Chemical reactivity can vary widely between different substances.
His high reactivity to loud noises made busy environments difficult for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsiveness - the quality of reacting quickly and positively
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of responsiveness to stimuli

Etymology

From 'react' + '-ivity', patterned after similar abstract nouns such as 'activity'.
