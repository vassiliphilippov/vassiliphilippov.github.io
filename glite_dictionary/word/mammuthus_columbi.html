Mammuthus columbi

1. NOUN

/ məˈmjuːθəs kəˈlʌmbiː / (AmE), / məˈmjuːθəs kəˈlʌmbiː / (BrE)
an extinct species of mammoth that lived in North America during the Pleistocene epoch, commonly known as the Columbian mammoth
extinct North American mammoth species

Examples

Fossils of the Mammuthus columbi have been found throughout much of North America.
Unlike the woolly mammoth, the Mammuthus columbi was adapted to warmer environments.
The enormous tusks of Mammuthus columbi made it a subject of fascination for paleontologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly mammoth - another extinct species of mammoth, Mammuthus primigenius, known for its thick coat

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Mammuthus' for mammoths and 'columbi' after Christopher Columbus (the Americas).
