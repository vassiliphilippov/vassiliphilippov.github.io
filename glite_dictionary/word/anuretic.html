anuretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.juˈrɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.juˈrɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by an inability to urinate.
unable to urinate (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as anuretic after reporting no urine output for 24 hours.
Some medications can cause anuretic symptoms as a rare side effect.
Severe dehydration may result in an anuretic state, requiring immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anuria - Complete suppression of urine formation by the kidney

Etymology

From medical prefix 'an-' (without) and 'uretic' (relating to urination).
