algorithm

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈælɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
a set of step-by-step rules or instructions for solving a problem or performing a task, especially by a computer
set of rules for problem-solving

Examples

The sorting algorithm efficiently arranges the data in ascending order.
Google's search engine relies on a complex algorithm to deliver relevant results.
Learning how an algorithm works is fundamental to understanding computer science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procedure - a series of actions conducted in a certain order or manner
  • Degree Lower: heuristic - a practical method not guaranteed to be optimal or perfect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'algorismus', after the Persian mathematician al-Khwarizmi.
