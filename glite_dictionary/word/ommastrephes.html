Ommastrephes

1. NOUN

/ əˌmæstrəˈfiz / (AmE), / ɒˌmæstrəˈfiːz / (BrE)
A genus of squids in the family Ommastrephidae, known for their commercial importance and wide distribution in oceanic waters.
genus of oceanic squids

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Ommastrephes in the Pacific Ocean.
Commercial fisheries often target members of Ommastrephes because of their abundance and large size.
The bioluminescent properties of Ommastrephes help them evade predators in deep waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squid - a marine cephalopod mollusk with a slender body and tentacles

Etymology

From Greek 'omma' (eye) + 'streptos' (twisted), referring to the appearance of the squid's eyes.
