to infuriate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfjʊriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjʊərieɪt / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry; to enrage
make extremely angry

Examples

Delays in the delivery process can infuriate even the most patient customers.
Her constant interruptions began to infuriate everyone in the meeting.
It would infuriate him if he found out you lied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrage - to make very angry
  • Degree Higher: enraged - extremely angry (adjective)
  • Degree Lower: to annoy - to irritate slightly
  • Antonym: calm - to soothe, make less angry

Etymology

From Italian *infuriato*, past participle of *infuriare* (to rage), from *in‑* (in) + *furia* (fury).
