watery

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔːtəri / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri / (BrE)
wet with or covered by water or secreted moisture, such as sweat or tears
wet with water or secreted moisture

Examples

His shirt was watery from the sweat after running in the heat.
Her eyes became watery when she heard the sad news.
The athlete wiped his watery brow with a towel.

Synset Examples

wiped his reeking neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damp - slightly wet; moist
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet or moist

Etymology

From Middle English 'watery', from Old English 'wæterig', related to 'water'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02560627-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑtəri / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri / (BrE)
containing too much water; weak and thin in taste or consistency
thin, weak, or diluted (of a liquid)

Examples

He complained that the soup was too watery to be satisfying.
I don't like watery coffee; I prefer a stronger brew.
The sauce turned out watery after she added too much milk.

Synset Examples

washy coffee
watery milk
weak tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking strength, diluted or not strong in taste or effect
  • Antonym: rich - strong, full, or well-developed in taste or texture
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00759980-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːtəri / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri / (BrE)
(of a substance or place) containing or saturated with water; resembling or characteristic of water.
containing or filled with water

Examples

After the storm, the basement floor was watery and covered with puddles.
They tried to plant vegetables in the watery soil, but it was too wet for roots to grow.
Her shoes were soaked after crossing the watery field in the rain.

Synset Examples

watery soil

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: moist - slightly wet or damp
  • Degree Higher: soggy - very wet and soft

Etymology

From Old English wæterig (watery), from wæter (water).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02562029-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːt̬əri / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri / (BrE)
relating to, resembling, or containing water; thin or diluted like water.
resembling or containing water

Examples

The soup was too watery and lacked flavor.
Her eyes became watery when she started to cry.
After the rain, the paint had a watery appearance.

Synset Examples

a watery substance
a watery color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aqueous - relating to, similar to, or containing water
  • Antonym: dry - free from water or moisture

Etymology

From Middle English watiri, from water + -y.
