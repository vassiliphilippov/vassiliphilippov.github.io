wigging

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡɪŋ / (BrE)
(British, informal) A scolding or reprimand.
a scolding; reprimand (British slang)
informal british

Examples

The manager gave me a serious wigging for missing the deadline.
She was expecting a wigging from her parents when she got home late.
After the mistake, the coach delivered a quick wigging to the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - an angry rebuke or reprimand
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval

Etymology

From 'wig', possibly from the idea of having one's wig pulled over their ears, meaning to be rebuked.
