to amaze

1. VERB

/ əˈmeɪz / (AmE), / əˈmeɪz / (BrE)
to be so surprising or puzzling as to be difficult to understand; to bewilder or perplex
to perplex or puzzle

Examples

The technical details of the experiment amaze even the scientists who designed it.
It always amazes me how quickly he solves these complex puzzles.
Her reaction to the news completely amazed her colleagues.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baffle - to totally confuse or perplex
  • Synonym: puzzle - to cause someone to feel confused because something is difficult to understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something less confusing or more clear

Etymology

From Old English amasian (to confound, astonish).

2. VERB

/ əˈmeɪz / (AmE), / əˈmeɪz / (BrE)
to surprise someone greatly; to fill someone with astonishment or wonder
to surprise or astonish greatly

Examples

Her ability to solve complex equations always amazed her teachers.
It never fails to amaze me how quickly children learn new languages.
You would be amazed at what technology can do these days.

Synset Examples

Your ability to speak six languages amazes me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise someone very much
  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or surprise someone a lot
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or dull

Etymology

From Middle English 'amased', from Old English 'amasian' (to confound, overwhelm with wonder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00726794-v
Synset POS: v
