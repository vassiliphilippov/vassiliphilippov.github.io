broom

1. NOUN

/ bruːm / (AmE), / bruːm / (BrE)
a shrub of the genera Cytisus, Genista, or Spartium, with long slender branches and clusters of yellow flowers
a yellow-flowered shrub (plant)

Examples

The hillside was covered in blooming broom, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
In spring, the yellow flowers of the broom brighten up the countryside.
Gardeners sometimes plant broom to help stabilize sandy soils.

Etymology

From Old English *brōm* (broom shrub), used for sweeping.

2. NOUN

/ bruːm / (AmE), / bruːm / (BrE)
A cleaning tool with a bundle of straws or twigs fixed to a long handle, used for sweeping floors.
cleaning tool for sweeping

Examples

She used a broom to sweep the kitchen floor every morning.
After breaking the glass, he quickly grabbed a broom to clean up the mess.
The school's janitor replaced the old broom with a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - a tool with bristles or stiff fibers, used for cleaning or painting

Etymology

From Old English 'brōm', meaning a kind of shrub or brushwood, once also used for the twigs used in brooms.

/ bruːm / (AmE), / bruːm / (BrE)

to sweep with a broom

4. NOUN

/ brum / (AmE), / bruːm / (BrE)
A shrub of the genus Cytisus or related genera, with slender green stems and small yellow flowers, common in Europe and often found in the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of shrubby plants with yellow flowers

Examples

Vast expanses of broom covered the hills with bright yellow blossoms in spring.
Gardeners often plant broom as an ornamental hedge due to its hardiness.
The broom was once used to make traditional besoms for sweeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - an area of open uncultivated land, often with heather, gorse, and broom

Etymology

From Old English 'brōm', meaning 'broom shrub'.
