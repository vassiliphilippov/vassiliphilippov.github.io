rocky

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑki / (AmE), / ˈrɒki / (BrE)
Full of rocks or stones; containing or covered with rocks.
full of rocks or stones

Examples

The hikers struggled to cross the rocky terrain that stretched for miles.
We set up our tent on a rocky beach overlooking the sea.
The garden's soil is too rocky for most plants to grow well.

Synset Examples

rocky fields
stony ground
bouldery beaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stony - full of stones; similar meaning
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɑki / (AmE), / ˈrɒki / (BrE)
full of difficulties or problems; involving hardship or uncertainty
full of difficulties; problematic

Examples

Their relationship had a rocky start but eventually settled down.
After a rocky year, the company finally began to make a profit.
The team's path to the championship was long and rocky.

Synset Examples

the rocky road to success
they were having a rough time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough - difficult or troublesome (in a similar figurative sense)

Etymology

From rock + -y, originally referring to having many rocks, then extended figuratively.

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɑːki / (AmE), / ˈrɒki / (BrE)
causing or marked by frequent jolts or irregular movements; unsteady or uneven
marked by jolts or uneven movement

Examples

We took a rocky drive down the old rural road in his truck.
The boat ride became rocky as soon as the waves picked up.
Their journey through the mountains was rocky, with constant jolts along the narrow trails.

Synset Examples

a rough ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumpy - marked by frequent bumps or jolts
  • Antonym: smooth - not having abrupt variations in movement or surface
