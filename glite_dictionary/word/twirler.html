twirler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɝːlər / (AmE), / ˈtwɜːlə / (BrE)
(baseball) a player who pitches the ball to the batter; a pitcher.
baseball pitcher

Examples

The team's twirler threw a no-hitter last night.
As the top twirler, she led her team to victory.
The rookie twirler impressed everyone with his fastball.

Synset Examples

our pitcher has a sore arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - (baseball) the player who throws the ball to the batter

Etymology

From 'twirl' + '-er,' referring to the motion of pitching.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɜrlər / (AmE), / ˈtwɜːlə / (BrE)
a person, typically a performer, who twirls a baton as part of a routine, especially in marching bands or parades
person who twirls a baton

Examples

The lead twirler dazzled the crowd with her baton tricks during the halftime show.
My cousin practiced every day to become a skilled twirler in the high school band.
Each twirler wore a sparkling costume for the parade performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majorette - a female baton twirler in a marching band

Etymology

From the verb 'twirl' (to spin rapidly) with agentive suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09863232-n
Synset POS: n
