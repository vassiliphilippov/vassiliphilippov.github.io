ewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːɚ / (AmE), / ˈjuːə / (BrE)
a large, open pitcher or jug with a handle and a spout, used for holding and pouring liquids
a large pitcher or jug for pouring liquids

Examples

The ornate ewer stood on the table, ready to be filled with fresh water.
She poured wine from a silver ewer during the banquet.
Antique collectors often seek out beautifully crafted ewers from the Victorian era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - a container for holding and pouring liquids
  • Synonym: jug - a large container with a handle and spout, used for liquids

Etymology

From Old French *eviere* (water pitcher), from Latin *aquam* (water).
