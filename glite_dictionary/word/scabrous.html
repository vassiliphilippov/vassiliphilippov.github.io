scabrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈskæbɹəs / (AmE), / ˈskæbɹəs / (BrE)
rough to the touch; having a surface covered with small projections or scales
rough or scaly to the touch

Examples

The scabrous leaves of the plant felt harsh against my fingers.
A scabrous patch on the lizard’s skin indicated a possible infection.
Some tree trunks are scabrous, making them easy to identify by touch alone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, free from roughness or projections

Etymology

From Latin 'scaber' (rough, scabby).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskæbɹəs / (AmE), / ˈskabɹəs / (BrE)
dealing with or involving salacious or indecent material, especially in literature or art; risqué or obscene.
salacious or indecent (esp. literature/art)

Examples

The author's latest work is a scabrous commentary on contemporary social mores.
Some critics found the film's scabrous humor both shocking and entertaining.
The collection features several scabrous stories that push the boundaries of taste and decency.

Synset Examples

a scabrous novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risqué - slightly indecent or liable to shock, especially by being sexually suggestive
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality and decency

Etymology

From Latin scaber (rough, scabby), later extended to 'indelicate' in English usage.
