demographic

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛməˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɛməˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
a particular group of people within a population, defined by specific characteristics such as age, gender, or income
a population group defined by shared traits

Examples

The company is targeting the 18-to-34-year-old demographic with its new product line.
Marketers often analyze the demographic most likely to buy luxury goods.
Our city’s aging demographic is leading to increased demand for healthcare services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: population - all the inhabitants of a particular area or group

Etymology

From French démographique, from Greek dēmos (people) and -graphia (writing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɛməˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdiːməˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the structure or characteristics of populations, especially as studied in demography.
relating to population statistics

Examples

The company conducted a demographic analysis to better understand its target market.
Government policies often consider demographic changes such as an aging population.
The demographic profile of the region shows a high percentage of young adults.

Synset Examples

demographic surveys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demographical - alternate form, also relating to demography

Etymology

From French démographique, from Greek dēmos (people) + -graphy (writing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02917664-a
Synset POS: a
