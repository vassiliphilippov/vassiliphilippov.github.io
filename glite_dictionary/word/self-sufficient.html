self-sufficient

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf səˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌself səˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
able to provide for oneself without help from others; not needing external assistance
able to manage without help from others

Examples

After years of hard work, the farm became self-sufficient and no longer needed outside supplies.
Many people strive to be self-sufficient by growing their own food and generating their own electricity.
The organization aims to help communities become self-sufficient through education and sustainable projects.

Synset Examples

a self-sufficing economic unit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not relying on others; autonomous
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on others for support

Etymology

From Latin 'sui' (of oneself) and 'sufficere' (to suffice, supply).
