Brugmansia suaveolens

1. NOUN

/ bruɡˈmænziə swɑːviˈoʊlənz / (AmE), / bruːɡˈmænziə swɑːviˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A South American plant cultivated for its very large, trumpet-shaped, night-fragrant flowers.
South American plant with large fragrant flowers

Examples

The garden's collection of Brugmansia suaveolens blooms filled the air with an intoxicating fragrance at dusk.
Many horticulturists prize Brugmansia suaveolens for its dramatic trumpet-shaped flowers and lush foliage.
Be careful when planting Brugmansia suaveolens, as all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested.

Etymology

From the genus name Brugmansia, honoring Dutch botanist Sebald Justinus Brugmans, and Latin suaveolens (sweet-smelling).
