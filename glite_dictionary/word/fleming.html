Fleming

1. PROPN

/ ˈflɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
(Sir Alexander Fleming) a Scottish bacteriologist who discovered penicillin, revolutionizing the treatment of bacterial infections.
Scottish discoverer of penicillin

Examples

Fleming was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1945.
The discovery of penicillin by Fleming marked a turning point in medical history.
Students learned about Fleming's contribution to antibiotics in their science class.

The Fleming proudly displayed the flag of Flanders during the festival.
