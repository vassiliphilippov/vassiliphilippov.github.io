Sturnella magna

1. NOUN

/ stərˈnɛlə ˈmæɡnə / (AmE), / stɜːˈnɛlə ˈmaɡnə / (BrE)
A species of meadowlark found in eastern North America, known for its bright yellow underparts and melodic song.
meadowlark species of eastern North America

Examples

The call of the Sturnella magna is a familiar sound in the springtime fields of the Midwest.
Researchers observed a flock of Sturnella magna nesting in the tall grasses near the nature reserve.
The Sturnella magna displays a striking black 'V' on its yellow breast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadowlark - a type of songbird of the genus Sturnella

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Sturnella' as a diminutive of Latin 'sturnus' (starling) and 'magna' meaning 'large' or 'great'.
