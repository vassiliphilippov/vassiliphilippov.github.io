Monilia albicans

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪliə ælˈbaɪkənz / (AmE), / məˈnɪliə ælˈbɪkænz / (BrE)
a species of parasitic fungus that can infect the mouth, skin, intestines, or vagina, causing candidiasis
a fungus causing candidiasis in humans
medical

Examples

Oral thrush is often caused by an overgrowth of Monilia albicans in the mouth.
Antifungal medications are prescribed to treat infections by Monilia albicans.
A weakened immune system increases susceptibility to Monilia albicans infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Candida albicans - the scientific name now commonly used for Monilia albicans

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Monilia' (a genus of fungi) and 'albicans' meaning 'whitening', referring to the fungus's appearance.
