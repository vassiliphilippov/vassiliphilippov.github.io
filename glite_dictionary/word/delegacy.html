delegacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪɡəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡəsi / (BrE)
A group of representatives or delegates, especially appointed to a convention or assembly.
group of delegates or representatives

Examples

The university sent a delegacy to attend the international conference on education reform.
Each region was asked to appoint a delegacy for the national convention.
A special delegacy presented their country's proposals to the summit organizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - a group of people officially chosen to represent others

Etymology

From Medieval Latin delegatia, from delegatus (delegate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.ə.ɡə.si / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.ɪ.ɡə.si / (BrE)
the act of appointing a delegate or the state of being appointed as a delegate
the appointment of a delegate

Examples

Her recent delegacy to the conference was announced by the board.
The committee discussed the procedures for delegacy and representation.
After his delegacy, he quickly gained the trust of those he represented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - the act of delegating; a group of delegates

Etymology

From Latin delegatus (deputed, assigned) via delegate + -cy (forming nouns of state or office).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00165999-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪɡəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡəsi / (BrE)
The state or office of being an official delegate or agent.
the office or state of being a delegate

Examples

His delegacy to the international conference was recognized by all member states.
During her delegacy, she represented the interests of her country with integrity.
Questions about the extent of his delegacy arose after the controversial decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - the act of delegating or a group of delegates

Etymology

From Medieval Latin delegatia, from Latin delegare (to send on a mission).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14023888-n
Synset POS: n
