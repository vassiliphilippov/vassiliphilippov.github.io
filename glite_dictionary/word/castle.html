castle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
A large fortified building, often dating from the Middle Ages, formerly used as a residence by a ruler or noble, and designed for defense against attack.
large fortified building or residence

Examples

The king retreated to his castle during the invasion.
Many tourists visit Scotland to admire its ancient castles.
From the hill, you could see the massive stone castle overlooking the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortress - a military stronghold, especially a large, permanently garrisoned one

Etymology

From Old French *castel*, from Latin *castellum* (fortified place, diminutive of *castrum*, camp).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
A large and stately mansion or residence, often resembling a fortress in appearance or style.
large and stately mansion

Examples

The wealthy family lived in a magnificent castle overlooking the valley.
Although not fortified, the sprawling estate was often called a castle by locals.
The tour included several historic castles that now serve as luxury hotels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house

Etymology

From Latin 'castellum' (fortress), diminutive of 'castrum' (fort).

3. VERB

/ ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
(in chess) to move the king two squares toward a rook on the same rank and then move that rook to the square over which the king crossed.
chess move involving king and rook

Examples

During the game, she decided to castle early for better king safety.
You cannot castle if either the king or the rook has already moved.
It is often a good strategy to castle on the kingside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - a chess situation where the king is under threat of capture
  • Synonym: mate - (short for 'checkmate') final move in chess resulting in defeat of opponent's king
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
(in chess) a move in which the king and a rook are moved at the same time, switching their positions according to specific rules.
chess move: king and rook switched

Examples

It's often a good idea to castle early in the game to protect your king.
You cannot castle if your king or rook has already moved.
After he decided to castle, his defense became much stronger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castling - the process or act of performing the castle move in chess

Etymology

From Old French *castel*, from Latin *castellum* (fortress, castle); chess sense by analogy to a rook's representation as a castle.
