necessitarian

1. NOUN

/ nəˌsɛsəˈtɛriən / (AmE), / nɪˌsɛsɪˈtɛərɪən / (BrE)
a person who rejects the doctrine of free will, believing that all actions are determined by necessity
person who denies free will

Examples

As a committed necessitarian, he argued that moral responsibility was an illusion.
The debate between the necessitarian and the libertarian has shaped ideas about human freedom.
Few philosophers today identify strictly as necessitarians, though the concept remains influential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinist - someone who believes that all events are determined by causes external to the will
  • Antonym: libertarian - someone who believes in free will

Etymology

From necessity + -arian, after Latin necessitas (necessity).
