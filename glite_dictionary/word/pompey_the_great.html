Pompey the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑm.pi ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pi ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(106–48 BC) A prominent Roman general and statesman, noted for his conflicts with Julius Caesar and his death in Egypt.
Roman general and statesman (106–48 BC)

Examples

Pompey the Great played a crucial role in the final decades of the Roman Republic.
Many historians have analyzed the rivalry between Julius Caesar and Pompey the Great.
After his defeat at Pharsalus, Pompey the Great fled to Egypt, where he met his end.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Julius Caesar - Roman general and leader; political adversary of Pompey the Great

Etymology

From Latin 'Pompeius Magnus', the honorific ‘Magnus’ meaning ‘the Great’.
