2. PROPN

/ ˌkæməˈruːn / (AmE), / ˌkæməˈruːn / (BrE)
A country in Central Africa, located on the continent's western coast, formerly under French and British administration until its independence in 1960.
country in Central Africa

Examples

Many tourists visit Cameroon to experience its vibrant cultural festivals and diverse wildlife.
After gaining independence in 1960, Cameroon has developed into a prominent country in Central Africa.
The national football team of Cameroon is famous for its strong performances in international competitions.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Rio dos Camarões' (River of Prawns), referring to early European explorers’ name for the region.
