giddiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪd.i.nəs / (BrE)
a sensation of dizziness or unsteadiness, especially a feeling that you are about to fall
sensation of dizziness; feeling faint or unsteady

Examples

After standing up too quickly, she was overcome by a sudden giddiness and had to sit down.
The mountain climber battled waves of giddiness as he peered over the edge of the cliff.
Intense heat and dehydration can often lead to a feeling of giddiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizziness - a sensation of spinning or losing one's balance
  • Synonym: vertigo - a sensation of spinning dizziness, often caused by inner ear problems

Etymology

From Middle English 'giddinesse', from 'giddy' (dizzy, foolish) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪdi nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪdɪ nɪs / (BrE)
a tendency to behave in an impulsive or scatterbrained manner, often as a result of excitement or enthusiasm
impulsive, scatterbrained behavior

Examples

Her giddiness made it hard for her to focus on the serious discussion.
There was a sense of giddiness in the room after hearing the exciting news.
His giddiness sometimes led him to make rash decisions without thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flightiness - tendency to be irresponsible or frivolous
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious, not impulsive or scatterbrained

Etymology

From 'giddy' (Old English 'gydig', meaning 'insane, mad, possessed by a god') + -ness.
