editor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for the final content and editorial aspects of a publication, such as a newspaper, magazine, or book
person deciding final text content

Examples

The editor carefully reviewed every article before the magazine went to print.
She was promoted to chief editor after years of hard work at the newspaper.
An experienced editor ensures that the book is free from errors and inconsistencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publisher - person or company responsible for issuing books or periodicals

Etymology

From Latin 'editor' (one who puts forth), from 'edere' (to bring forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛd.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
(Computing) A software program for creating, modifying, rearranging, or deleting data or text.
software for creating or changing data/text

Examples

You can use a simple text editor to write and modify code.
He downloaded a new image editor for his design work.
Most programmers have a favorite editor for editing configuration files.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: word processor - a software application for typing, editing, and formatting text
