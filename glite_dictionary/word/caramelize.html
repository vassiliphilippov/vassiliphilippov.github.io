to caramelize

1. VERB

/ ˈkærəməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəməlaɪz / (BrE)
(of sugar or food containing sugar) to be converted into caramel by heating, causing it to turn brown and develop a sweet, rich flavor.
(of sugar) turn into caramel when heated

Examples

As the onions cook slowly, they caramelize and develop a sweet, rich aroma.
If you heat sugar too long, it will caramelize and turn dark brown.
The chef allowed the sauce to caramelize for extra flavor.

Synset Examples

The sugar caramelized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown - (of food) to turn brown when exposed to heat

Etymology

From French 'caraméliser', from 'caramel'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkærəˌməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəm(ə)laɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into caramel by heating, often used when sugar or foods with sugar are browned in cooking
turn to caramel by heating

Examples

To make the sauce, gently caramelize the sugar in a heavy-bottomed pan.
The onions will slowly caramelize and become sweet after about twenty minutes.
If you caramelize the nuts, they will have a rich, toasty flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown - to cook until browned

Etymology

From French 'caraméliser', from 'caramel'.
