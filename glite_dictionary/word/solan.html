solan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlən / (BrE)
a very large white seabird, the northern gannet, with black wingtips, found mainly in the North Atlantic
a large white seabird (northern gannet)
british

Examples

The solan soared gracefully above the crashing waves, searching for fish.
Many solans nest on the rocky cliffs each spring.
Fishermen sometimes call the northern gannet a solan or solan goose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gannet - a large seabird with white plumage and black wingtips

Etymology

From Scottish and northern English, perhaps related to Old Norse sula (gannet).
