Ceroxylon alpinum

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrɑksɪˌlɑn ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / sɛˌrɒksɪˌlɒn ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A species of palm native to the Andes, known for producing a resinous wax used in candle-making.
Andean palm species yielding wax

Examples

The wax of Ceroxylon alpinum is harvested to manufacture traditional candles in Andean communities.
Botanists are studying the habitat of Ceroxylon alpinum to better understand its ecological role in the Andes.
Visitors to Colombia can see the towering Ceroxylon alpinum palms along mountain slopes.

Etymology

From Greek 'keros' (wax) + 'xylon' (wood), and Latin 'alpinum' (of the Alps/mountains), referring to the waxy palm found in the Andes.
