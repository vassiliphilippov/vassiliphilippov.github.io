Phylloscopus sibilatrix

1. NOUN

/ fɪləˌskɑpəs sɪbɪˈleɪtrɪks / (AmE), / fɪləˌskɒpəs sɪbɪˈleɪtrɪks / (BrE)
A species of European woodland warbler with dull yellow plumage, known for its melodious song.
European woodland warbler species

Examples

The Phylloscopus sibilatrix can often be heard singing in European deciduous forests during spring.
Birdwatchers look for the pale yellow plumage of Phylloscopus sibilatrix among the tree branches.
Conservationists study Phylloscopus sibilatrix to monitor changes in woodland bird populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood warbler - Common name for Phylloscopus sibilatrix

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'skopos' (watcher), and Latin 'sibilatrix' (whistler, female who whistles).
