to bilk

1. VERB

/ bɪlk / (AmE), / bɪlk / (BrE)
to cheat someone out of money or evade paying money owed to them
cheat or evade paying someone

Examples

He tried to bilk the landlord out of three months' rent.
The company was charged with attempting to bilk investors.
She realized too late that she had been bilked by the fraudulent contractor.

Synset Examples

He bilked his creditors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or possessions

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'bilk' (to defraud), of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02318781-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɪlk / (AmE), / bɪlk / (BrE)
to hinder or thwart the efforts, plans, or desires of someone
hinder or thwart someone's plans

Examples

The security measures were designed to bilk any attempts at unauthorized access.
Despite several setbacks, nothing could bilk her determination to succeed.
They tried to bilk the committee's efforts to implement the new policy.

Synset Examples

What ultimately frustrated every challenger was Ruth's amazing September surge
foil your opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frustrate - prevent someone from achieving something
  • Synonym: foil - prevent the success of someone or something

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the late 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02563998-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɪlk / (AmE), / bɪlk / (BrE)
to cheat or defraud someone, especially of money or what is owed
cheat or defraud (esp. of money)

Examples

They accused him of trying to bilk investors out of their savings.
The contractor was sued for bilking homeowners with false repair bills.
She discovered too late that she had been bilked by a clever scam artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or assets
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

Perhaps from archaic English 'bilm', to spoil a score at cribbage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02578849-v
Synset POS: v
