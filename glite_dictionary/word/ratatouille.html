ratatouille

1. NOUN

/ ˌrætəˈtuːi / (AmE), / ˌrætəˈtuːi / (BrE)
a French vegetable stew, typically consisting of tomatoes, eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and herbs, cooked together
French vegetable stew with tomatoes and other vegetables

Examples

She prepared a delicious ratatouille for dinner using fresh vegetables from the garden.
The restaurant's ratatouille was rich in flavor and beautifully presented.
Have you ever tried making ratatouille from scratch at home?

Etymology

French, from Occitan 'ratatolha', a regional dish; influenced by 'touiller' (to stir) in French.
