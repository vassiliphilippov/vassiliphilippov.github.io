pratfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈprætˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈprætˌfɔːl / (BrE)
a fall in which someone lands on their buttocks, often in a comical or humiliating way
a fall onto the buttocks, often comic

Examples

The comedian staged an exaggerated pratfall that had the whole audience laughing.
She slipped on the ice and suffered an embarrassing pratfall in front of her friends.
His performance included a perfectly timed pratfall that looked painful but was all for laughs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - an act of falling or slipping, especially to the ground

Etymology

From 'prat' (slang for buttocks) + 'fall', American English, early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprætˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈpratˌfɔːl / (BrE)
an embarrassing or humiliating mistake or blunder, especially one made publicly
embarrassing mistake or blunder

Examples

Her comment during the meeting was a major pratfall that embarrassed everyone.
The comedian turned his pratfall into the highlight of the show.
The candidate committed a political pratfall when he forgot his opponent’s name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or embarrassing mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - a social or diplomatic mistake

Etymology

From 'prat' (US slang for buttocks) + 'fall', originally used in vaudeville for a comedic fall onto the backside.
