abelmosk

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.əlˈmɑsk / (AmE), / ˌæb.əlˈmɒsk / (BrE)
A bushy herb of tropical Asia, grown for its yellow, pink, or scarlet hibiscus-like flowers.
tropical Asian herb with hibiscus-like blooms

Examples

The garden featured a rare abelmosk with striking yellow blossoms.
Perfume makers sometimes use the seeds of abelmosk for their distinctive scent.
She admired the way the abelmosk stood out among the tropical plants.

Etymology

From Arabic *abu l-misk* (father of musk), referring to the musky scent of its seeds.
