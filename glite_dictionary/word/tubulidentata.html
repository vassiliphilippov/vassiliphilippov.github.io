Tubulidentata

1. NOUN

/ tuːˌbjuːlɪdɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / tjuːˌbjuːlɪdɛnˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of placental mammals, containing only the aardvark as its living representative
order of mammals containing the aardvark

Examples

The order Tubulidentata is unique because it contains only one living species, the aardvark.
Researchers have studied the dental structures that distinguish Tubulidentata from other mammalian orders.
Fossils suggest that extinct relatives of Tubulidentata were once more widespread across Africa and Eurasia.

Etymology

From Latin 'tubulus' (small tube) and 'dens, dentis' (tooth), referring to the unique structure of the teeth.
