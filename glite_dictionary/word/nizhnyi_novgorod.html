Nizhnyi Novgorod

1. PROPN

/ ˌniʒni nəvˈɡɔrd / (AmE), / ˌniʒni nəvˈɡɒrəd / (BrE)
A large industrial city in western Russia, located at the confluence of the Volga and Oka rivers; noted for its historical architecture and as the birthplace of Maksim Gorky.
industrial city in western Russia

Examples

My friend visited Nizhnyi Novgorod last summer and was impressed by its stunning architecture.
The company opened a new branch in Nizhnyi Novgorod to expand its presence in Russia.
Famous writer Maksim Gorky was born in Nizhnyi Novgorod in 1868.

Etymology

From Russian Нижний Новгород (Nizhniy Novgorod), meaning 'Lower Newtown'.
