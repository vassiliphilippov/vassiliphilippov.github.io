awlwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈɔːlwɜːt / (BrE)
a small aquatic plant with clusters of awl-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette and tiny white flowers, found in northern regions
aquatic plant with awl-shaped leaves

Examples

The botanist identified the strange plant as awlwort growing along the rocky lake shore.
Tiny white flowers are a distinguishing feature of awlwort in early summer.
Researchers studied awlwort populations in arctic and alpine water habitats.

Etymology

Named for its awl-shaped leaves, from 'awl' (a pointed tool) + 'wort' (plant in Old English).
