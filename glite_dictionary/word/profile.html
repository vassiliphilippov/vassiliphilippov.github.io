profile

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊfaɪl / (BrE)
A vertical section of the Earth's crust or soil, shown as a diagram, displaying the arrangement of different layers or horizons.
vertical section showing earth or soil layers

Examples

The geologist studied the soil profile to identify different sediment layers.
A cross-sectional profile of the valley revealed distinct rock stratum.
The team drew a detailed profile of the excavation site to document the horizons.

Etymology

From Latin 'profilum', meaning 'a drawing in outline'.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.faɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.faɪl / (BrE)
an analysis or set of data, often shown graphically, that represents the extent to which something displays various characteristics or qualities
analytical representation of characteristics

Examples

The doctor ordered a full lipid profile to assess his cholesterol risk.
Investigators created a criminal profile to help identify the suspect's behavior patterns.
The company presented a market profile showing trends among young consumers.

Synset Examples

a biochemical profile of blood
a psychological profile of serial killers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of elements or structure

Etymology

From Italian 'profilo', meaning 'outline', from 'profilare' (to draw in outline).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07015715-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈproʊ.faɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.faɪl / (BrE)
to write or give a detailed description of someone or something, especially highlighting their qualities or achievements
give a detailed written description

Examples

Several magazines have profiled the young entrepreneur in recent months.
The book profiles leading scientists who changed the world.
In her latest documentary, she profiles the lives of immigrant families.

Synset Examples

The author of this article profiles a famous painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of someone/something in words
  • Synonym: to portray - to depict or describe someone or something in words

Etymology

From Italian profilo (outline), from profilare (to outline), ultimately from Latin filum (thread).

a short article or account giving a description of a person, especially a biographical sketch highlighting their achievements or character
