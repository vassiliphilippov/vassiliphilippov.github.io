isobilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəʊbaɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊbaɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
(of a structure) having identical parts on each side of an axis.
having identical parts on each side of an axis

Examples

Unlike dorsiventral leaves, isobilateral leaves have symmetrical upper and lower surfaces.
The scientist observed an isobilateral arrangement of cells in the plant tissue.
Grasses typically possess isobilateral leaves, which facilitates efficient photosynthesis on both sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilateral - having or relating to two sides

Etymology

From iso- (equal) + bilateral (having two sides), both from Greek roots.
