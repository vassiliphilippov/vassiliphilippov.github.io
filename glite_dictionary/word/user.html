user

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzər / (AmE), / ˈjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who regularly takes a particular drug, especially one that is illegal or habit-forming
person who takes drugs

Examples

The clinic offers support to any user seeking help to quit.
She was arrested as a known cocaine user in the community.
Programs are available to help young users break their addiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addict - a person who is addicted to a substance, especially drugs or alcohol
  • Antonym: nonuser - a person who does not take drugs

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzər / (AmE), / ˈjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who manipulates or takes advantage of others selfishly or unethically for their own benefit
person who exploits others

Examples

James realized too late that Laura was just a user who wanted him for his connections.
She ended the relationship when she saw he was acting like a user, always taking and never giving.
It's easy to be hurt by a user who pretends to care but only exploits your kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploiter - a person who uses others for their own benefit
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help to others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10092334-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzər / (AmE), / ˈjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who uses or operates something; someone who employs or makes use of a particular thing, system, or service.
a person who uses something

Examples

The user must enter a password to access the system.
Smartphone users are encouraged to update their devices regularly.
As a frequent user of public transportation, she always buys a monthly pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer - a person who purchases goods and services
  • Synonym: operator - a person who operates equipment or machinery
  • Antonym: provider - a person or organization that supplies something

Etymology

From Old French 'usier', from Latin 'usor' (one who uses), from 'uti' (to use).
