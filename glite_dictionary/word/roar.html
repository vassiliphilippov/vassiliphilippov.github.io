roar

1. NOUN

/ rɔr / (AmE), / rɔː / (BrE)
A loud, deep sound made by a lion or other large wild animals; also, a similar loud noise.
loud, deep sound made by a lion

Examples

A lion’s roar could be heard echoing through the jungle.
The roar of the lion startled all the animals nearby.
We listened in awe as the roar filled the air at the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - a low, guttural sound made by an animal

Etymology

Imitative of natural sounds, recorded since Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07406880-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ rɔr / (AmE), / rɔː / (BrE)
(of wind, water, or vehicles) to make a loud, deep, and continuous noise.
make a loud, deep, continuous noise

Examples

The river roared past the rocks after the heavy rainstorm.
The engines roared as the airplane prepared for takeoff.
Last night, the wind roared through the empty streets, keeping everyone awake.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, wailing sound

Etymology

From Old English rārian (to roar), of Germanic origin.
