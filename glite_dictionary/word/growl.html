growl

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊl / (AmE), / ɡraʊl / (BrE)
a low, guttural sound made by an animal, often as a sign of anger or warning
low, threatening animal sound

Examples

The growl of the dog warned us not to come any closer.
A low growl came from the tiger as it paced behind the bars.
He heard a sudden growl in the darkness and froze in fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - a sharp, aggressive sound made by animals
  • Degree Higher: roar - a loud, deep sound made by a large animal
  • Degree Lower: whine - a high-pitched complaining sound by animals or people
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ɡraʊl / (AmE), / ɡraʊl / (BrE)
to make a low, deep, rough sound, typically as an expression of anger or warning
make a low, deep sound (like an animal)

Examples

The dog began to growl when the stranger approached the gate.
Thunder seemed to growl in the distant mountains all night long.
He didn't answer directly but just growled his dissatisfaction under his breath.

Synset Examples

he grumbled a rude response
Stones grumbled down the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - to make an aggressive growl with teeth bared
  • Degree Higher: bark - to make a sharp, loud sound (like a dog)
  • Degree Lower: whimper - to make a low, weak sound (like a wounded animal)

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound; first recorded in the early 17th century.
