soliton

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlɪˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪtɒn / (BrE)
(Physics) A stable, solitary wave that travels through a medium without changing shape or losing energy, typically occurring in nonlinear systems.
solitary, stable wave in physics

Examples

The soliton maintained its shape as it traveled down the fiber-optic cable.
Scientists observed a soliton in the shallow water of the canal.
Mathematicians model how a soliton can form in certain nonlinear systems.

Synset Examples

soliton waves can travel long distances with little loss of energy or structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a disturbance that transfers energy through a medium or space
  • Synonym: pulse - a single disturbance moving through a medium

Etymology

From 'solitary' + '-on', a suffix used for subatomic particles.
