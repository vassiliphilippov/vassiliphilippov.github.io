Listeria monocytogenes

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈstɪriə məˌnɑsɪtəˈdʒiːniːz / (AmE), / lɪˈstɪəriə məˌnɒsɪtəˈdʒiːniːz / (BrE)
a species of bacteria causing listeriosis and other serious infections in humans and animals
bacteria causing listeriosis
medical

Examples

Pregnant women are advised to avoid certain foods that may be contaminated with Listeria monocytogenes.
Outbreaks of listeriosis are often traced back to food products tainted by Listeria monocytogenes.
Scientists study Listeria monocytogenes to develop better preventative measures in the food industry.

Etymology

Named after the genus Listeria, honoring Joseph Lister, and from Greek 'monocytos' (single cell) and 'genes' (producing).
