gruelling

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡruːəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruː.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely tiring and demanding; requiring great effort to the point of exhaustion.
extremely tiring and demanding
british

Examples

After a gruelling day of hiking up the mountain, they finally reached the summit.
The tennis match was so gruelling that both players could barely stand by the end.
She went through a gruelling training program to prepare for the marathon.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - requiring great effort; very difficult and tiring
  • Synonym: punishing - demanding great effort or endurance
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding; not exhausting

Etymology

From verb 'gruel' (to exhaust), probably from 'gruel', noun meaning thin broth; sense evolved in the 19th century.
