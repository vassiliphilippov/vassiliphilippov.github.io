Nanking

1. PROPN

/ nænˈkɪŋ / (AmE), / nænˈkɪŋ / (BrE)
a city in eastern China on the Yangtze River, formerly the capital of China and the site of the 1937 Nanjing Massacre.
former capital city of China; Yangtze River

Examples

Many tourists visit Nanking for its rich history and cultural heritage.
The tragedy of the Nanking Massacre is remembered around the world.
In the early twentieth century, Nanking served as the capital of China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nanjing - alternative romanization of Nanking, present-day standard spelling

Etymology

From Mandarin 南京 (Nánjīng), meaning 'southern capital.'
