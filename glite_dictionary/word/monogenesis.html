monogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
Asexual reproduction involving the production and release of spores.
asexual reproduction by spores
medical

Examples

Fungi commonly undergo monogenesis as part of their life cycle.
The process of monogenesis allows certain plants to reproduce efficiently without fertilization.
Researchers studied monogenesis to understand how asexual reproduction influences genetic diversity.

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + genesis (origin, creation).
