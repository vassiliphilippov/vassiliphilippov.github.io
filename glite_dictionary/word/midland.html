midland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.lənd / (BrE)
an area forming the interior part of a country, especially away from the coasts or borders
interior region of a country

Examples

Many farmers live in the midland where the land is rich and fertile.
The midland region is known for its rolling hills and vast plains.
Trade routes often passed through the midland to avoid the dangers of the coast.

Etymology

From Middle English 'mid' (middle) + 'land' (land, territory).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪdˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.lənd / (BrE)
of or relating to the middle part of a country or region; coming from the central area.
from the central part of a region or country

Examples

The midland towns are famous for their traditional markets.
Many people in the midland counties work in agriculture.
She grew up in a quiet midland village far from the coast.

Synset Examples

upcountry districts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central - located in or forming the center
