bulbil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌlbɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌlbɪl / (BrE)
a small bulb or bulb-shaped growth that forms in the leaf axil or in place of flowers and can develop into a new plant
small bulbous plant growth

Examples

Some lilies reproduce by forming bulbils in the leaf axils along the stem.
Gardeners can propagate certain plants by detaching and planting their bulbils.
The bulbil fell to the ground and began to sprout near the parent plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulb - a rounded underground storage organ of some plants

Etymology

From Latin 'bulbillus', diminutive of 'bulbus' (bulb).
