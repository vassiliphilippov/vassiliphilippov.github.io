residence

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
the act or state of living in a particular place
the act of living in a place

Examples

Her official residence in the country lasted more than three years.
Continuous residence in the state is required for citizenship.
During his residence in Paris, he learned to speak French fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwelling - the act of living in a place; a place to live
  • Antonym: absence - the state of not being present or not living in a place

Etymology

From Latin 'residentia' (remaining behind), from 'residēre' (to remain, to reside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
a large and impressive house, especially one used as an official home
large and imposing house

Examples

The ambassador hosted a reception at his official residence last night.
The governor's residence is located at the heart of the city.
Security guards patrol the grounds of the historic residence day and night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large and impressive house

Etymology

From Latin residentia (remaining, dwelling), from residēre (to remain, dwell).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
the official home or establishment where an important person such as a sovereign, president, or governor lives
official house of an important person

Examples

The ambassador hosted the dinner at his residence in the capital.
Security was tight around the presidential residence during the summit.
She was invited to a gala held at the governor’s residence.

Synset Examples

he refused to live in the governor's residence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house, often for the wealthy or powerful
  • Synonym: palace - the official residence of a sovereign

Etymology

From Latin residēre (to remain, reside), via Old French residence.

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
a place where someone lives for a significant period; a home or dwelling.
a place where one lives

Examples

After moving to the city, she changed her residence to be closer to work.
The university requires students to provide proof of residence when they enroll.
He maintains several residences in different countries for business reasons.

Synset Examples

a person can have several residences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home - the place where one lives permanently, especially as a member of a family or household
  • Synonym: abode - a place of residence; a house or home
  • Antonym: workplace - a place where one works (as opposed to where one lives)

Etymology

From Latin residentia (the act of residing), from residēre (to remain, to stay).
