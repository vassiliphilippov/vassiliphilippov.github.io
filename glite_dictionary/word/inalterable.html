inalterable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɔltərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːlt(ə)rəb(ə)l / (BrE)
unable to be changed or altered; unchangeable.
not able to be changed

Examples

Her inalterable beliefs shaped every decision she made throughout her life.
The terms of the contract are inalterable once both parties have signed.
He faced the inalterable laws of nature with acceptance and humility.

Synset Examples

unalterable resolve
an unalterable ground rule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unalterable - not able to be altered or changed
  • Synonym: immutable - unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed or altered

Etymology

From Latin inalterabilis, from in- (not) + alterare (to change).
