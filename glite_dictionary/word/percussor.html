percussor

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌsər / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsə / (BrE)
a small hammer with a rubber head used by doctors to perform percussive examinations or test reflexes
medical hammer for examination or reflex tests
medical

Examples

The doctor used a percussor to check my knee reflexes during the examination.
A percussor is essential for certain neurological tests.
She placed the percussor back on the tray after finishing the physical assessment.

Etymology

From Latin percussor (striker, hammer), from percutere (to strike through).
