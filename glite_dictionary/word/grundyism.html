Grundyism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌndiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌndiːɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Excessive or affected modesty, especially as a result of rigid or conservative social attitudes.
excessive or affected modesty

Examples

The town's strict dress code is a reflection of lingering Grundyism in the community.
Critics accused the council of Grundyism when they banned certain books from the library.
Her refusal to attend the dance was seen as an act of Grundyism by her peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudery - the behavior of being easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity
  • Degree Lower: modesty - the quality of not being too proud or confident about oneself or one's abilities
  • Synonym: puritanism - having or characterized by a strict, often excessive, moral code

Etymology

From Mrs Grundy, a character in Thomas Morton's 1798 play 'Speed the Plough,' symbolizing conventional prudery, + -ism.
