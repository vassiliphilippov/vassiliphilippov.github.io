to actualize

1. VERB

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something real or give it actual existence; to make concrete or real.
make real or concrete

Examples

The company hopes to actualize its vision of a sustainable future by 2030.
She worked hard to actualize her childhood dreams of becoming an artist.
New policies are needed to actualize the changes promised during the campaign.

Synset Examples

our ideas must be substantiated into actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to realize - to make real, to bring into existence

Etymology

From French 'actualiser' (make actual), from 'actuel' (actual).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01648288-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or describe something in a realistic or true-to-life way
represent or describe realistically

Examples

The director sought to actualize the lives of ordinary people in the film.
Her paintings actualize the emotions that words cannot express.
Writers often actualize their experiences through their fictional characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to realize - to make real or give reality to

Etymology

From French 'actualiser', from 'actuel' (actual) + '-iser' (-ize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989902-v
Synset POS: v
