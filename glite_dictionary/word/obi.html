obi

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.bi / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.bi / (BrE)
A religious belief system of African origin involving witchcraft and sorcery, practiced in parts of the West Indies and tropical Americas.
West Indian religious belief system

Examples

In certain Caribbean communities, belief in obi still influences daily life and customs.
The villagers attributed the mysterious illness to the work of obi.
She sought protection by consulting an obi practitioner before her journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obeah - A system of spiritual and magical practices among people of African descent in the Caribbean

Etymology

From West African languages, possibly Igbo 'ọbi' (heart, hut, or shrine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.bi / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.bi / (BrE)
(In the West Indies) followers of a religious practice centered on witchcraft, sorcery, and the supernatural.
follower of West Indian witchcraft practice

Examples

Many believed the mysterious illness was the work of an obi living nearby.
The obi was rumored to possess secret knowledge of powerful spells.
Stories of the obi circulated in the village, sparking both fear and fascination.

Etymology

From African (Igbo) 'ọbi', originally referring to a hut or shrine; later used in Caribbean English for magical practices.
