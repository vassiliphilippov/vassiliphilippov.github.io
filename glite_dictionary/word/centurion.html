centurion

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtʊriən / (AmE), / sɛnˈtjʊəriən / (BrE)
an officer in the ancient Roman army who was in charge of about one hundred soldiers
Roman army officer in charge of 100 men

Examples

The centurion commanded his men with authority and discipline.
In ancient Rome, a centurion was a respected figure among the soldiers.
The film portrayed the life of a centurion during Julius Caesar's campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: officer - a person in a position of authority in the armed forces or police
  • Degree Lower: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From Latin centurio (commander of a hundred), from centum (hundred).
