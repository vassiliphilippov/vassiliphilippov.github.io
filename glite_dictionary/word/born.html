born

1. ADJ

/ bɔrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
Having a natural ability or talent for something from birth.
naturally talented from birth

Examples

She is a born storyteller, captivating everyone with her vivid tales.
He proved to be a born leader, guiding the team effortlessly.
Some believe that true artists are born, not made.

Synset Examples

a natural leader
a born musician
an innate talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - existing from birth; inborn
  • Synonym: natural - having an ability that seems to come from nature rather than training

2. ADJ

/ bɔrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
brought into existence by birth; used to indicate someone's origin or how something came to be.
brought into existence; originating

Examples

The child was born into a family of musicians.
She was born in Paris and moved to London at a young age.
Ideas can be born out of necessity during challenging times.

Synset Examples

he was a child born of adultery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bred - having a specific background or upbringing

Etymology

Past participle of 'bear', from Old English 'beran'.
