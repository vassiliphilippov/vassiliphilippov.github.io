inaccuracy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈæk.jə.rə.si / (AmE), / ɪnˈæk.jʊ.rə.si / (BrE)
The quality or state of being incorrect or containing errors.
state of being inaccurate

Examples

The report was rejected due to its numerous inaccuracy problems.
Small inaccuracy in measurements can lead to significant errors in scientific experiments.
He apologized for the inaccuracy in his previous statement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accuracy - the quality or state of being correct or precise
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or inaccuracy

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'in-' (not) + 'accuracy'.
