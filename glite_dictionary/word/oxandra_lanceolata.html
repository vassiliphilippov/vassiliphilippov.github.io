Oxandra lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈsændrə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsandrə ˌlansiəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical tree species whose wood, known as lancewood, is used commercially for its strength and flexibility.
tropical tree yielding lancewood

Examples

Oxandra lanceolata is prized for its tough, elastic wood used in tool handles and sporting goods.
The dense forests of the Caribbean are home to Oxandra lanceolata trees, valued in the timber industry.
Many of the walking sticks imported from South America are made from Oxandra lanceolata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancewood - Strong, flexible wood from several tropical trees, including Oxandra lanceolata

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Oxandra (genus name) and Latin lanceolata (spear-shaped leaves).
