tandoor

1. NOUN

/ tænˈdʊr / (AmE), / tænˈdʊə / (BrE)
a cylindrical clay oven, traditionally used in northern India and Pakistan for baking bread and roasting meats
traditional clay oven from South Asia

Examples

The naan was freshly baked in a tandoor and served hot.
Many restaurants use a tandoor to prepare their signature chicken dishes.
Cooking in a tandoor gives bread and meat a unique flavor.

Etymology

From Hindi-Urdu 'tanūr', from Persian 'tanūr' (oven).
