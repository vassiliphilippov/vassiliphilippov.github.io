prestidigitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛstɪˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛstɪˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the performance of tricks or illusions using quick and skillful hand movements; sleight of hand
skillful hand tricks in magic

Examples

The magician's prestidigitation left the audience gasping in amazement.
He practiced prestidigitation for years to perfect his magic act.
Few could match her prestidigitation when it came to card tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleight of hand - manual dexterity used in performing tricks

Etymology

From French *prestidigitation*, from Latin *praestigiator* (conjuror), from *praestigiae* (juggler's tricks).
