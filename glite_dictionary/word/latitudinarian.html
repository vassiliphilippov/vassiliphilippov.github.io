latitudinarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌlætɪˌtjuːdɪˈneriən / (AmE), / ˌlætɪˌtjuːdɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who is tolerant and broad-minded, especially regarding differences in religious belief or conduct.
tolerant person, especially in religion

Examples

As a latitudinarian, she welcomed different viewpoints within the congregation.
The committee valued the latitudinarian for his open-minded approach to difficult issues.
During the religious debates, a latitudinarian was often called to mediate between opposing sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with

Etymology

From latitude + -inarian; originally referring to those in the 17th-century Anglican Church tolerant of differing beliefs.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlætɪˌtjudɪˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌlætɪˌtjuːdɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
unwilling to accept authority or strict dogma, especially in matters of religion; tolerant of different opinions or practices
tolerant of differing views, especially in religion

Examples

Her latitudinarian approach to faith allowed her to respect many different religious traditions.
The college prided itself on its latitudinarian atmosphere, where debate and dissent were encouraged.
Historically, the latitudinarian clergy advocated for tolerance within the Church of England.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dogmatic - tending to lay down principles as undeniably true

Etymology

From Latin 'latitudo' (breadth) + English suffix '-arian'; originally referring to those in the 17th-century Church of England favoring broad tolerance.
