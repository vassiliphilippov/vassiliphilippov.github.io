prostrate

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
lying stretched out on the ground, especially face downward, typically as a sign of exhaustion, submission, or adoration
lying stretched out on the ground

Examples

After the marathon, she lay prostrate on the grass, too tired to move.
The worshippers fell prostrate before the altar in prayer.
He was found prostrate on the floor, overcome by emotion.

Synset Examples

found himself lying flat on the floor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supine - lying flat on one's back

Etymology

From Latin 'prostratus', past participle of 'prosternere' (to throw down).

WordNet ID: oewn-01241913-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
lying stretched out on the ground with the face downward
lying face downward

Examples

Exhausted and defeated, he lay prostrate on the cold floor.
The worshippers were prostrate before the altar, showing reverence.
After the long hike, she collapsed prostrate on the grass, catching her breath.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supine - lying on the back, face upward

Etymology

From Latin 'prostratus', past participle of 'prosternere' meaning 'to throw down'.

3. VERB

/ ˈprɑː streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs treɪt / (BrE)
to lie or throw oneself flat on the ground, especially as a sign of humility, submission, or adoration
lay or throw oneself flat on the ground

Examples

The monk prostrated himself before the altar in silent prayer.
She prostrated herself on the ground to beg forgiveness.
At the emperor’s feet, the envoy prostrated himself in a gesture of utter submission.

Synset Examples

She prostrated herself with frustration

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to kneel - to get down on one's knees, usually as a sign of respect or worship

Etymology

From Latin *prostratus*, past participle of *prosternere* (to throw down).

4. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
to make someone helpless or defenceless; to reduce to a state of physical or mental weakness
render helpless or defenceless

Examples

The dictator's army prostrated the resistance within days.
Years of war had prostrated the once-proud nation.
The economic crisis prostrated many small businesses, leaving them unable to recover.

Synset Examples

They prostrated the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to defeat or overpower someone completely
  • Antonym: to empower - to give someone power or authority

Etymology

From Latin prostratus, past participle of prosternere (to throw down).

5. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
(of a person) to lie stretched out on the ground with one's face downward, especially as a gesture of submission or respect.
lie face down in submission

Examples

The monks would prostrate themselves before the altar during prayer.
He prostrated himself at the king's feet as a sign of loyalty.
As a sign of repentance, she prostrated herself on the cold stone floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - to bend the upper part of the body forward as a sign of respect, greeting, or consent

Etymology

From Latin prostratus, past participle of prosternere 'throw down'.
