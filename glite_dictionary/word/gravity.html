gravity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈɡræv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A serious and dignified quality or manner; a solemn or earnest feeling.
solemn and dignified feeling

Examples

She spoke with a gravity that made everyone in the room listen carefully.
The gravity of the occasion was felt by all in attendance.
His face was marked by the gravity of someone who has faced many challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness or lightness in manner

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitas' (weight, seriousness), from 'gravis' (heavy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈɡræv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The force by which objects with mass attract each other, especially the force that makes things fall to the ground on Earth.
force attracting bodies to each other

Examples

Because of gravity, objects fall to the ground when dropped.
The Moon's gravity is about one-sixth that of Earth's.
Scientists study how gravity affects planets and stars throughout the universe.

Synset Examples

the more remote the body, the less the gravity
the gravitation between two bodies is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
gravitation cannot be held responsible for people falling in love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitation - the force that attracts any objects with mass
  • Antonym: weightlessness - the condition of being free from the effect of gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitas' (weight, heaviness), from 'gravis' (heavy).
