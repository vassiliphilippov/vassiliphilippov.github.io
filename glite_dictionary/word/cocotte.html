cocotte

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɒt / (AmE), / kəˈkɒt / (BrE)
a small, usually round cooking dish used for preparing and serving individual portions of food
small individual casserole dish

Examples

The restaurant served the soup in a cocotte to keep it piping hot.
She baked eggs en cocotte for a special Sunday brunch.
Each guest received a cocotte filled with creamy potato gratin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramekin - a small dish for baking and serving individual portions

Etymology

French, diminutive of 'cocot' (little hen), referring to the dish's shape; adopted in English for the cooking vessel.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɒt / (AmE), / kəˈkɒt / (BrE)
an old-fashioned term for a woman who engages in sexual intercourse for money; a prostitute, especially one of a higher social standing or glamour
a courtesan or high-class prostitute
archaic

Examples

In Parisian society, the notorious cocotte was known for her charm and luxurious lifestyle.
The artist frequently sketched scenes of elegant cocottes in the cafés and salons.
Her reputation as a cocotte preceded her wherever she went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment
  • Synonym: courtesan - a prostitute with wealthy or upper-class clients

Etymology

From French 'cocotte' (prostitute), diminutive of 'coc' (rooster, flirt).
