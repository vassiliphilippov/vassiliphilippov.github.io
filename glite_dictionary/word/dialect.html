dialect

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.lɛkt / (BrE)
a form of a language that is spoken in a particular area or by a particular group, with its own distinctive vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation
variety of a language spoken by a group

Examples

Many regions of Italy have their own dialect that differs from standard Italian.
She found it difficult to understand the local dialect during her travels in Scotland.
His grandmother still uses words from a rural dialect rarely heard today.

Synset Examples

the immigrants spoke an odd dialect of English
he has a strong German accent
it has been said that a language is a dialect with an army and navy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: accent - a way of pronouncing words that indicates the place of origin or social background of the speaker
  • Degree Higher: language - a system of communication spoken or written by people in a particular country or area

Etymology

From Greek dialektos (conversation, language, dialect), from dialegesthai (to converse).
