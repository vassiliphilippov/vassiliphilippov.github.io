sloppily

1. ADV

/ ˈslɑːpɪli / (AmE), / ˈslɒpɪli / (BrE)
In a careless, untidy, or messy manner.
in a careless or messy way

Examples

He completed his homework sloppily, with mistakes and crossed-out answers everywhere.
The paint was applied sloppily, leaving streaks on the wall.
If you pack your suitcase sloppily, your clothes will get wrinkled.

Synset Examples

this work was done rather sloppily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without care or attention; with lack of effort or precision
  • Antonym: neatly - in a neat, tidy, or orderly way

Etymology

From 'sloppy' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective.
