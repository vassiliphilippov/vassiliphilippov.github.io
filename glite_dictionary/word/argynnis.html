Argynnis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈɡɪnɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈɡɪnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fritillary butterflies in the family Nymphalidae, known for their brightly colored and spotted wings.
genus of fritillary butterflies

Examples

The Argynnis genus includes several well-known species of fritillary butterflies.
Researchers documented a decline in Argynnis populations due to habitat loss.
Many Argynnis species are admired for their distinctive orange and black wing patterns.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'argynnos' (a proper name), used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈdʒɪnɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɪnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies in the family Nymphalidae, commonly known as fritillaries, found in Eurasia and North America.
a genus of fritillary butterflies

Examples

The Argynnis genus includes several brightly colored fritillary butterflies.
Researchers observed the mating behavior of Argynnis species in their natural habitat.
Some members of Argynnis are rare and protected due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'Argunnis', a proper name in classical literature.
