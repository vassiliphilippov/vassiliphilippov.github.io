heartless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtləs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtləs / (BrE)
showing no kindness, sympathy, or compassion; cruel or unfeeling.
cruel; lacking compassion or sympathy

Examples

Her heartless remark left everyone in the room stunned and silent.
It's heartless to abandon a pet when it becomes inconvenient.
The company made a heartless decision to lay off hundreds of workers before the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - showing a willingness to cause pain or suffering
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

Formed from 'heart' + '-less', meaning 'without a heart'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtləs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtləs / (BrE)
Lacking courage or enthusiasm; not showing spirit or determination.
lacking courage or enthusiasm

Examples

After so many defeats, the team's heartless performance surprised even their biggest critics.
Facing the challenge with a heartless attitude, she showed no sign of optimism or fight.
The heartless response from the players made the coach question their motivation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: timid - lacking in courage or confidence

Etymology

From Old English heortlīes, from 'heorte' (heart) + '-less' (without).
