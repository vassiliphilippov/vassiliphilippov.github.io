Lamium album

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.mi.əm ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.mi.əm ˈæl.bəm / (BrE)
A European plant of the mint family, known as white dead-nettle, with white flowers resembling those of true nettles but without stinging hairs.
European plant, white dead-nettle

Examples

The garden bed was full of Lamium album growing alongside the roses.
Herbalists sometimes use Lamium album for its traditional medicinal properties.
I mistook Lamium album for stinging nettle, but it did not irritate my skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white dead-nettle - Common name for Lamium album, a European plant with white flowers

Etymology

From Latin: Lamium (genus name), album ('white').
