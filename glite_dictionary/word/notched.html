notched

1. ADJ

/ nɑtʃt / (AmE), / nɒtʃt / (BrE)
having an irregularly indented or toothed edge or margin, as if cut or gnawed
having a jagged or indented edge

Examples

The carpenter carefully measured and cut the notched plank for the roof.
Many leaves have notched edges that help them shed water quickly.
Her old key was thin and notched from years of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a saw-like, jagged edge
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, unbroken surface or edge

Etymology

From notch + -ed, indicating possession of notches.

2. ADJ

/ nɑtʃt / (AmE), / nɒtʃt / (BrE)
having a series of indentations or cuts along the edge, especially resembling the teeth of a saw
having indented, saw-like edges

Examples

The notched edges of the leaf help distinguish it from similar species.
She carefully cut a notched pattern into the wooden panel for decorative effect.
The blade became dull after slicing through several notched pieces of metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a jagged, saw-like edge
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, without indentations

Etymology

From 'notch' (a V-shaped cut or indentation), + -ed forming the adjective.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
