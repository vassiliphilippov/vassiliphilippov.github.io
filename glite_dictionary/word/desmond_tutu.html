Desmond Tutu

1. PROPN

/ dɛz.mənd ˈtuːtuː / (AmE), / dɛz.mənd ˈtjuː.tuː / (BrE)
(1931–2021) South African Anglican bishop and human rights activist, renowned for his leadership in the struggle against apartheid.
South African anti-apartheid leader and bishop

Examples

Desmond Tutu received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1984 for his efforts to end apartheid.
Many look up to Desmond Tutu as a symbol of forgiveness and reconciliation.
The legacy of Desmond Tutu continues to inspire activists worldwide.
