filum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm / (BrE)
a slender, threadlike structure, especially one found in biological or anatomical contexts
threadlike structure (biology/anatomy)
medical

Examples

The spinal cord is anchored by the filum terminale at its base.
Under the microscope, the algae appeared as a delicate filum extending across the slide.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided cutting the thin filum to prevent nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - a long, thin strand of material or structure

Etymology

From Latin filum (thread).
