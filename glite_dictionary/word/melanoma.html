melanoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of melanocytes, typically occurring in the skin and known for its tendency to spread rapidly.
malignant skin tumor from melanocytes
medical

Examples

The doctor informed her that the dark mole on her arm was a melanoma that required immediate treatment.
Early detection of melanoma can greatly improve the chances of successful recovery.
He attended a seminar about advances in melanoma research and treatment options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinoma - a cancer arising in the epithelial tissue of the skin or of the lining of the internal organs
  • Synonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor arising from connective or other non-epithelial tissue
  • Antonym: benign - not harmful in effect; in particular, not malignant (of a disease)
  • Degree Higher: malignant - very virulent or infectious; tending to invade normal tissue or to recur after removal

Etymology

From Greek melas, melan- (black) + -oma (tumor).
