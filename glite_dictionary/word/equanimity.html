equanimity

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwəˈnɪməti / (AmE), / ˌɛkwəˈnɪmɪti / (BrE)
calmness and evenness of mind, especially under stress or difficulty
calmness under stress

Examples

Throughout the crisis, she handled every setback with remarkable equanimity.
Even when provoked, he managed to maintain his equanimity during the debate.
Her equanimity in the face of criticism impressed everyone in the office.

Synset Examples

he accepted their problems with composure and she with equanimity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calm and self-controlled state of mind, especially in difficult situations
  • Antonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often causing wildly unthinking behavior

Etymology

From Latin aequanimitas, from aequus (even, level) + animus (mind, spirit).
