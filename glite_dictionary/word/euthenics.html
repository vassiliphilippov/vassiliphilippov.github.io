euthenics

1. NOUN

/ juˈθɛnɪks / (AmE), / juːˈθɛnɪks / (BrE)
the study of methods for improving human well-being and efficiency by enhancing environmental conditions, such as housing, nutrition, and education
study of improving well-being via environment

Examples

She decided to pursue a degree in euthenics to help create healthier communities.
Early advocates of euthenics believed that improving living conditions would promote social progress.
His research in euthenics focuses on the impact of urban design on public health.

Etymology

From Greek 'euthenein' (to thrive), from 'eu' (well) + 'thenein' (to thrive).
