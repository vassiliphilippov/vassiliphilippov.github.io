to bedizen

1. VERB

/ bəˈdaɪzən / (AmE), / bɪˈdʌɪzən / (BrE)
to decorate or dress up in a showy or tasteless way
decorate or dress up gaudily

Examples

She chose to bedizen the cake with an excessive amount of sprinkles and glitter.
The stage was bedizened with garish decorations for the carnival performance.
Children often bedizen themselves with beads and bright feathers during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate something, especially in a beautiful way
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • Antonym: undecorate - to remove decoration from, to make plain

Etymology

From French 'desaisonner' (to dress out of season) via alteration; influenced by 'bedizen' in English.

/ bɪˈdaɪzən / (AmE), / bɪˈdʌɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
to dress or decorate someone or something in a gaudy or tasteless manner
to dress up gaudily or tastelessly

Examples

She managed to bedizen herself with gaudy jewelry for the party.
Every holiday, the store windows are bedizened with tinsel and flashing lights.
He arrived at the event, bedizened in a costume of clashing colors and sequins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate or add beauty to
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding details
  • Antonym: to understate - to present or decorate in a restrained way

Etymology

From French "bedizer" (to dress up), from "be-" (intensive prefix) + "dizer" (to dress), possibly related to "disguise."
