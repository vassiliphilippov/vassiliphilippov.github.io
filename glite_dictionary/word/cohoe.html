cohoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌhəʊ / (BrE)
the fatty pinkish flesh of a small Pacific salmon, valued as food
flesh of a small Pacific salmon

Examples

The chef prepared the cohoe with a simple lemon and dill sauce.
We caught several cohoe while fishing on Lake Michigan last summer.
Smoked cohoe is a delicacy in many Pacific Northwest markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coho - a small salmon of the North Pacific, also called silver salmon

Etymology

Variant of 'coho', from Native American (Salishan) languages via Chinook Jargon 'kə́wx̣ʷ'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊhəʊ / (BrE)
a small salmon found along the northern Pacific coasts and in the Great Lakes, also called coho salmon
a species of small salmon

Examples

A local fisherman caught a large cohoe in the river yesterday.
During migration season, the cohoe can be seen swimming upstream in large numbers.
Smoked cohoe is a delicacy in many coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coho salmon - another name for the cohoe, Oncorhynchus kisutch

Etymology

From Halkomelem (a Salishan language) *kʷəxʷəʔ*, meaning 'silver salmon'.
