Microgramma piloselloides

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈɡræm.ə pɪˌloʊ.səˈlɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəˈɡræm.ə pɪˌləʊ.səˈlɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
A species of epiphytic fern with long rhizomes, native to tropical America.
epiphytic fern of tropical America

Examples

The leaves of Microgramma piloselloides often carpet the trunks of trees in tropical forests.
Botanists documented several specimens of Microgramma piloselloides during their expedition to South America.
Because Microgramma piloselloides is an epiphytic fern, it grows on trees rather than in the soil.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'gramma' (letter, line), and Latinized form of species name 'piloselloides' meaning 'like Pilosella'.
