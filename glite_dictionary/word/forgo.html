to forgo

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈgoʊ / (AmE), / fəˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
to decide not to have or do something that you want or is desirable
to abstain from; go without

Examples

She decided to forgo dessert in order to watch her diet.
Many students forgo sleep to finish their assignments on time.
By choosing public transport, he could forgo the stress of finding parking.

Synset Examples

We are dispensing with formalities
relinquish the old ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relinquish - to give up or abandon something voluntarily
  • Synonym: to dispense with - to manage without something; to forgo
  • Synonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid doing or having something

Etymology

From Old English 'forgān', meaning to pass by or abstain from.
