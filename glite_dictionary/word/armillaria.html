Armillaria

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.mɪˈlɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ɑː.mɪˈlæː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi comprising several species of edible and parasitic mushrooms, some of which cause root rot in trees.
genus of mushrooms, some edible or parasitic

Examples

The forest was infested with Armillaria, posing a serious threat to the trees.
Some species of Armillaria are collected and eaten in several countries.
Scientists study Armillaria to understand its role in causing root diseases in forests.

Etymology

From Latin 'armilla' (bracelet), referring to the ring on the mushroom's stem.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌmɪˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ɑːˌmɪˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi consisting of mushrooms with white spores, an annulus, and sometimes blue juice; some species are edible, while others cause root rot in trees.
genus of mushrooms, some edible, some harmful to trees

Examples

The widespread fungus Armillaria can cause devastating root rot in forests.
Some species of Armillaria are prized by foragers for their edible mushrooms.
Researchers are studying how Armillaria infects and kills healthy trees.

Etymology

From Latin *armilla* (bracelet), referring to the ring (annulus) on the mushroom's stem.
