Jordanella

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɔr.dəˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔː.dəˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small North American freshwater fish, commonly known as flagfishes.
genus of North American flagfishes

Examples

The Jordanella genus is notable for its adaptability to both freshwater and slightly brackish environments.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Jordanella for their vibrant coloration and hardy nature.
Researchers observed unique breeding behaviors in Jordanella species during the spring.

Etymology

Named after American ichthyologist David Starr Jordan.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɔːrdəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔːdəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Cyprinodontidae, known commonly as flagfishes, native to North America.
genus of small North American flagfishes

Examples

The genus Jordanella is popular among aquarium hobbyists for its hardiness.
Researchers studied the behavior of Jordanella in varying water conditions.
Jordanella species are native to slow-moving streams and ponds in Florida.

Related Terms

Etymology

Named after American ichthyologist David Starr Jordan.
