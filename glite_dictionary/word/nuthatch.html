nuthatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌhætʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌthætʃ / (BrE)
a small, short-tailed songbird with strong feet and a sharp beak, known for feeding on small nuts and insects and often climbing tree trunks headfirst
a small songbird that feeds on nuts and insects

Examples

A nuthatch landed on the tree trunk and began searching for insects.
The nuthatch is easily recognized by its habit of climbing down trees headfirst.
In winter, nuthatches often visit bird feeders for seeds and nuts.

Etymology

From nut + hatch, referring to its habit of hacking nuts open.
