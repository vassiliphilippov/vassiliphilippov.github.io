receptacle

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsɛptəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptəkl̩ / (BrE)
a container used to hold or store things
container for holding things

Examples

The kitchen was full of jars and other receptacles for storing spices.
Please use the designated receptacle for recycling plastics.
She emptied the contents into a small glass receptacle and sealed it with a lid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container - an object that can be used to hold or transport something

Etymology

From Latin receptaculum, meaning a place to receive or contain.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04068016-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛptəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptəkəl / (BrE)
an electrical device connected to a power supply, designed to receive a plug or insert for electrical connection
device for receiving electrical plugs

Examples

I plugged my laptop charger into the receptacle near my desk.
The kitchen needs an additional receptacle for small appliances.
If the receptacle is damaged, it should be replaced by a qualified electrician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socket - an opening that allows a plug to be connected to an electrical circuit
  • Synonym: outlet - a device in a wall into which an electrical cord can be plugged

Etymology

From Latin receptaculum (a place for receiving), from recipere (to receive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04068554-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛptəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptəkl / (BrE)
(Botany) The enlarged tip of a stem that bears the floral organs, such as petals, sepals, stamens, and carpels.
enlarged stem tip bearing floral parts (botany)

Examples

In many flowering plants, the receptacle supports and connects the various floral parts.
The strawberry's fleshy part is actually an enlarged receptacle rather than a true fruit.
Botanists often examine the receptacle to understand the arrangement of floral organs.

Etymology

From Latin receptaculum (a place to receive or contain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13150920-n
Synset POS: n
