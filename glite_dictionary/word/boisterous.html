boisterous

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪ.stɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.stər.əs / (BrE)
loud, energetic, and often rough or noisy; full of exuberant and unrestrained spirits
noisy and energetic

Examples

The boisterous crowd cheered loudly as the team scored the winning goal.
Her boisterous laughter could be heard throughout the house.
The party became more boisterous as the night went on.

Synset Examples

boisterous practical jokes
knockabout comedy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowdy - rough and disorderly; noisy
  • Antonym: calm - quiet and peaceful; not noisy

Etymology

From Middle English 'boistous' (rough, coarse), of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪstərəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪstərəs / (BrE)
noisy, energetic, and lacking in restraint or discipline; full of lively and exuberant activity
noisy and energetic; lacking restraint

Examples

The children grew increasingly boisterous as the birthday party went on.
A boisterous crowd filled the stadium, cheering loudly for their team.
His boisterous laughter could be heard from across the street.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - lacking discipline or restraint
  • Synonym: rambunctious - boisterous and difficult to control
  • Synonym: rowdy - noisy and disorderly
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From Middle English boistous, meaning rough or coarse.

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪstərəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪstərəs / (BrE)
(of weather, wind, or water) violently agitated, turbulent, or rough.
turbulent or violently rough (weather, wind, water)

Examples

The boisterous winds rattled the windows all night, making it difficult to sleep.
Sailors dreaded crossing the boisterous seas during the storm.
Heavy rain and boisterous weather forced the cancellation of the outdoor event.

Synset Examples

boisterous winds and waves
the fierce thunders roar me their music
rough weather
rough seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of weather or water) not rough or stormy; free from disturbance

Etymology

From Middle English 'boistous' (rough, coarse), perhaps from Old French 'boistous'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
