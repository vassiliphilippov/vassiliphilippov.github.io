groovy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡruvi / (AmE), / ˈɡruːvi / (BrE)
Very fashionable, attractive, or excellent; used to express enthusiastic approval, especially in informal or historical contexts.
excellent; very fashionable or attractive
informal slang

Examples

That new song is really groovy and everyone is dancing to it.
She wore a groovy outfit to the 1970s-themed party last night.
I think your new apartment looks absolutely groovy!

Synset Examples

he did a bully job
a neat sports car
had a great time at the party
you look simply smashing
we had a grand old time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cool - informal term for excellent or fashionable
  • Antonym: terrible - of very poor quality or bad

Etymology

Originally US slang in the 1930s, from 'in the groove', referring to jazz musicians playing well together.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡruːvi / (AmE), / ˈɡruːvi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Very fashionable or stylish; chic.
very fashionable; chic (informal)
informal british

Examples

She wore a groovy dress that turned heads at the party.
Those groovy new shoes really make a statement!
The designer’s groovy style was featured in the fashion magazine.

Synset Examples

groovy clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - fashionably elegant and sophisticated
  • Synonym: fashionable - following the current style; popular at a particular time

Etymology

From 'groove', originally referring to the groove of a record, popularized in 1960s slang.
