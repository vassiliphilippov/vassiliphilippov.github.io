pterion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
The point on the side of the skull where the frontal, parietal, temporal, and sphenoid bones meet.
craniometric point on the skull
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the pterion before making the initial incision for the cranial operation.
A fracture at the pterion can be particularly dangerous due to the underlying middle meningeal artery.
In anatomy class, we learned to identify the pterion as a key landmark on the skull.

Etymology

From Greek pterion (temple of the head), from pteron (wing), referring to its shape.
