to redesign

1. VERB

/ ˌridɪˈzaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈzaɪn / (BrE)
to design something again, especially in a new or improved way
to design again in a new way

Examples

The architect was hired to redesign the entrance of the building.
After customer feedback, the website team decided to redesign the homepage.
They are planning to redesign the company logo to reflect a more modern image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revamp - to renovate or improve something

Etymology

re- (again) + design, from Latin designare (to mark out)
