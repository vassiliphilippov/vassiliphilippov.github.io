seriema

1. NOUN

/ sɛr.iˈɛ.mə / (AmE), / sɛr.iˈiː.mə / (BrE)
A large, long-legged South American bird with a crest on its head, belonging to the family Cariamidae, found in grasslands and open woodland.
South American long-legged bird

Examples

A seriema stalked through the tall grass, searching for prey.
The seriema is known for its loud, ringing calls at dawn.
Wildlife photographers traveled to Brazil hoping to capture images of a seriema in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'seriema', from Tupi-Guarani 'çariama' or 'saria-ma' (crest and raised).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛriˈeɪmə / (AmE), / ˌsɛriˈiːmə / (BrE)
A large, long-legged South American bird found in grasslands, especially in Brazil and Argentina, known for its loud calls and terrestrial habits.
South American terrestrial bird

Examples

The seriema is often seen striding through the grasslands in search of insects and small reptiles.
Unlike most birds, the seriema prefers to run rather than fly when threatened.
Researchers recorded the distinctive calls of the seriema during their South American expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cariama - another name for the seriema bird, especially the red-legged species

Etymology

From Portuguese 'seriema', from Tupi 'çariama', a local name for the bird.
