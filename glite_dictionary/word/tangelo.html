tangelo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtændʒəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒɪ.ləʊ / (BrE)
A citrus fruit that is a hybrid between a tangerine and a grapefruit or pomelo, known for its juicy, mildly tangy flavor.
hybrid citrus fruit

Examples

The tangelo is prized for its easy-to-peel skin and sweet-tart flavor.
We bought a bag of tangelos at the farmer’s market this morning.
She prefers tangelo juice over regular orange juice for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - small citrus fruit similar to orange
  • Synonym: grapefruit - large citrus fruit with a sharp, tangy flavor

Etymology

Blend of 'tangerine' and 'pomelo' (or 'grapefruit').

2. NOUN

/ tænˈdʒɛloʊ / (AmE), / tanˈdʒɛləʊ / (BrE)
A large, sweet, and juicy citrus fruit that is a hybrid between a tangerine and a grapefruit, with a thick, wrinkled skin.
hybrid citrus fruit (tangerine × grapefruit)

Examples

She peeled a tangelo for her lunch and found it sweeter than a regular orange.
The recipe calls for the juice of one tangelo to add a unique citrus flavor.
At the farmers market, you can find fresh tangelos in season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - small citrus fruit with loose skin
  • Synonym: grapefruit - large, round, yellow citrus fruit

Etymology

Blend of 'tangerine' and 'pomelo' (the latter often used for grapefruit).

3. NOUN

/ tænˈdʒɛloʊ / (AmE), / tanˈdʒɛləʊ / (BrE)
A citrus fruit that is a hybrid between a tangerine and a grapefruit or pomelo, cultivated especially in Florida.
hybrid citrus fruit

Examples

I bought a fresh tangelo from the farmer's market this morning.
Tangelos are juicier than most other citrus fruits and easy to peel.
The tangelo was developed to combine the best traits of tangerines and grapefruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a small sweet citrus fruit with a loose skin
  • Synonym: grapefruit - a large, sour to semi-sweet citrus fruit

Etymology

Blend of 'tangerine' and 'pomelo'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
