child-centered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪld ˌsɛn.tɚd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪld ˌsɛn.təd / (BrE)
focusing on the individual needs, interests, and development of children, often in education or care settings, rather than on traditional instruction or discipline
focused on children's needs or interests

Examples

Many progressive schools use a child-centered approach to learning.
The new curriculum emphasizes child-centered activities over standardized testing.
Her teaching philosophy is deeply child-centered, valuing each student's unique interests and abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child-focused - having the child as the main focus or priority
  • Antonym: teacher-centered - focusing on the teacher's role, authority, or instructional methods rather than the child's needs
