agony

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəni / (AmE), / ˈæɡəni / (BrE)
Extreme mental or physical pain or suffering.
intense suffering or pain

Examples

She was in agony after breaking her leg during the match.
He faced the agony of having to choose between family and career.
The soldier screamed in agony as the medic tried to treat his wound.

Synset Examples

an agony of doubt
the torments of the damned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain or suffering
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical or mental ease and freedom from pain
  • Degree Lower: pain - physical suffering or discomfort

Etymology

From Greek agōnia (contest, struggle) via Latin agonia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæɡəni / (AmE), / ˈæɡəni / (BrE)
Extreme physical or mental pain; a state of acute suffering.
extreme pain or suffering

Examples

She was in such agony after the accident that she could barely speak.
The patient screamed in agony as the nurse tried to clean the wound.
Waiting for the test results was pure agony for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: ecstasy - an intense feeling of joy or delight

Etymology

From Greek agōnia (struggle, anguish), from agōn (contest).
