Botswana

1. PROPN

/ bɑtˈswɑnə / (AmE), / bɒtˈswɑːnə / (BrE)
A landlocked country in south-central Africa, formerly a British protectorate, which gained independence in 1966.
country in south-central Africa

Examples

After traveling through South Africa, we crossed the border into Botswana for a safari.
Botswana has become known for its stable democracy and growing economy.
Gaborone, the capital of Botswana, is one of the fastest-growing cities in Africa.

Etymology

From the Tswana people, the majority ethnic group; 'Bo-' prefix denotes 'land of' in Setswana.
