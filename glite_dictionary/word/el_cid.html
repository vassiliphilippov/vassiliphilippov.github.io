El Cid

1. PROPN

/ ɛl sɪd / (AmE), / ɛl sɪd / (BrE)
the legendary Spanish nobleman and military leader, celebrated as the hero of a 12th-century Spanish epic poem
heroic Spanish nobleman and epic hero

Examples

The exploits of El Cid are immortalized in Spain's national epic, "El Cantar de mio Cid."
Many historians regard El Cid as one of Spain's greatest heroes.
Schoolchildren in Spain often study the life of El Cid in their history classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar - historical name of El Cid, the Spanish knight and leader

Etymology

From Spanish 'El Cid', from Arabic 'al-sid' meaning 'the lord'.
