stablemate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlmeɪt / (BrE)
a horse that is stabled with another or is one of several horses owned by the same person, especially in racing.
a horse sharing a stable with another

Examples

The champion and his stablemate will compete in the same race tomorrow.
His stablemate won the Derby last year, making the trainer very proud.
Each stablemate had its own distinct personality, despite sharing the same barn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rival - a horse or person competing against another
  • Synonym: companion - one who accompanies or associates with another

Etymology

From 'stable' (place where horses are kept) and 'mate' (companion).
