to sentimentalise

1. VERB

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.t̬əl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmen.təl.aɪz / (BrE)
to express or regard something in an overly sentimental way; to indulge in sentimental thoughts or feelings
express with excessive sentimentality
british

Examples

She tends to sentimentalise her childhood memories, forgetting the more difficult moments.
The film was criticised for sentimentalising historical events instead of portraying them realistically.
It's easy to sentimentalise the past when faced with present challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sentimentalize - to express or regard something in an overly sentimental way (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to romanticize - to view or describe something as being better or more attractive than it really is

Etymology

From 'sentimental' + '-ise' (British spelling), from French 'sentimental' and Latin 'sentire' (to feel).

2. VERB

/ ˌsɛntəˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to view or present something in an emotional, nostalgic, or overly sentimental way
view or present sentimentally
british

Examples

Many authors tend to sentimentalise childhood in their writing.
You shouldn't sentimentalise old relationships; not everything was perfect back then.
The film chooses to sentimentalise poverty rather than address its harsh realities.

Synset Examples

Don't sentimentalize the past events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more appealing or idealized than it really is

Etymology

From 'sentimental' + '-ise'; 'sentimental' from French 'sentiment' (feeling), from Latin 'sentire' (to feel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00534265-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌsɛntəˈmɛntəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) sentimental, or to treat in a sentimental way
make or treat as sentimental

Examples

The film's score tends to sentimentalise otherwise realistic scenes of hardship.
Some writers sentimentalise childhood, glossing over its real challenges and fears.
He accused the media of trying to sentimentalise the tragedy for higher ratings.

Synset Examples

Too much poetry sentimentalizes the mind
These experiences have sentimentalized her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sentimentalize - alternative American spelling of 'sentimentalise'

Etymology

From sentimental + -ise, modeled after French sentimentaliser.
