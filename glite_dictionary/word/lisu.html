Lisu

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːsuː / (AmE), / ˈliːsuː / (BrE)
A language of the Loloish branch of the Sino-Tibetan family, spoken primarily in western Yunnan and parts of Sichuan in China, as well as in neighboring regions.
Loloish language spoken in western China and neighboring areas

Examples

The Lisu language is spoken by thousands of people in Yunnan Province.
She is fluent in both Mandarin and Lisu.
Researchers are studying the grammar of Lisu to help preserve the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loloish - Branch of Sino-Tibetan languages including Lisu and others
