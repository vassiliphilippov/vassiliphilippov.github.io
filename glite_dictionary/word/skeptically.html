skeptically

1. ADV

/ ˈskɛptɪkli / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner showing doubt or disbelief; with skepticism.
in a doubtful or skeptical manner

Examples

She raised an eyebrow and glanced skeptically at his wild story.
He listened skeptically as the salesman made his pitch about the miracle product.
The proposal was received skeptically by many members of the committee.

Synset Examples

he looked at her sceptically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubtfully - in a way that shows uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: trustfully - in a way that shows trust or confidence

Etymology

From English 'skeptical' + '-ly', ultimately from Greek 'skeptikos' (inquiring, doubtful).
