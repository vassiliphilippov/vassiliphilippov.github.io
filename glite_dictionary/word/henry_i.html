Henry I

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛnri ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˌhɛnri ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1068–1135) King of England from 1100 to 1135, the youngest son of William the Conqueror, known for conquering Normandy in 1106.
King of England, 1100–1135

Examples

Henry I was known for his administrative reforms and efforts to consolidate royal power.
After his victory at the Battle of Tinchebray, Henry I brought Normandy under English rule.
The reign of Henry I was marked by both political stability and family conflict.
