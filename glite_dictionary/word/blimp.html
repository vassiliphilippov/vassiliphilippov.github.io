Blimp

1. NOUN

/ blɪmp / (AmE), / blɪmp / (BrE)
an elderly, pompous person with reactionary and ultranationalist views, especially one resistant to change; based on a cartoon character by Sir David Low
reactionary, pompous elderly ultranationalist
british

Examples

Many saw the aging senator as a Blimp clinging to outdated traditions.
The newspaper criticized the policy as the brainchild of a Blimp unwilling to embrace modern ideas.
She dismissed him as a Blimp whose views had no place in contemporary society.

Etymology

From the name of Colonel Blimp, a satirical cartoon character created by British cartoonist Sir David Low in the 1930s.
