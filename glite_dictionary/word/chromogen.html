chromogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ·məˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ·məˌdʒɛn / (BrE)
a compound that can be converted into a pigment, often during a chemical reaction or biological process.
compound convertible to a pigment

Examples

The presence of a chromogen in the solution was confirmed by the resulting color change.
Some bacteria produce a chromogen that turns blue when exposed to oxygen.
Researchers identified the chromogen responsible for the vivid red pigment in the plant.

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color) and '-gen' (producing).
