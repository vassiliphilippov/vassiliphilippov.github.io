differential

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving derivatives, especially in mathematics; involving rates of change or slopes of functions
involving derivatives (math)

Examples

The professor introduced the concept of differential equations in class today.
Many scientific problems require a differential approach to analyze changing systems.
Solving a differential equation often involves advanced calculus techniques.

Synset Examples

differential equation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integral - relating to integrals or integration (math)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'differentia' (difference), influenced by 'differentiālis' (pertaining to difference).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02920481-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a quantity representing the instantaneous rate of change of one variable with respect to another, especially as the result of differentiation in calculus
instantaneous rate of change in calculus

Examples

The differential of y with respect to x is commonly written as dy/dx.
To solve the equation, first compute the differential of the function.
In advanced calculus, the concept of a differential provides a foundation for integration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derivative - the measure of how a function changes as its input changes; result of differentiation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin differentialis, from Latin differentia (difference).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06024079-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌdɪf.əˈrɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪf.əˈrɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
A mechanical device, especially in a vehicle, that allows two shafts or wheels to rotate at different speeds, typically used in the drive axles.
mechanical device allowing wheels to turn at different speeds

Examples

The car's differential was replaced after it started making unusual noises.
Modern trucks use a locking differential for better traction on slippery roads.
A limited-slip differential helps improve performance when cornering at high speeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - mechanism transmitting power through gears in a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'differentia' (difference), relating to the action of differing.
