clodhopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑdˌhɑpɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɒdˌhɒpə / (BrE)
A thick, heavy shoe, often coarse and sturdy in appearance, typically associated with country or farm work.
heavy, thick country shoe

Examples

He wore a pair of clodhopper boots that looked like they could withstand any storm.
The old farmer's clodhoppers were always caked with mud after a day's work.
She laughed at the idea of wearing clodhoppers to the elegant party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boot - a sturdy item of footwear covering the foot and ankle, sometimes extending up the leg

Etymology

Formed from 'clod' (a lump of earth) and 'hopper' (one who hops or moves), originally referring to a country person and later their footwear.
