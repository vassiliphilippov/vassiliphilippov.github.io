dovetail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊvˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvteɪl / (BrE)
a joint between two pieces of wood, made by fitting a series of interlocking wedge-shaped projections (tenons) into corresponding notches (mortises) to form a strong, tight connection
interlocking wood joint

Examples

The craftsman used a classic dovetail to connect the corners of the drawer.
A well-made dovetail adds both strength and beauty to furniture.
Learning to cut a perfect dovetail is a milestone for many woodworkers.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a dove's tail, with the term dating to the late 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈdoʊvˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌv.teɪl / (BrE)
to fit or combine together perfectly or conveniently, as if by means of a dovetail joint
fit together perfectly

Examples

Their two proposals dovetail nicely, so implementing both should be straightforward.
The new evidence dovetailed with the suspect's alibi, supporting his account of the events.
She searched for a solution that would dovetail her career goals with her family life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - to be of the right shape and size for something

Etymology

From the carpentry term 'dovetail', referring to a type of joint shaped like a dove's tail.
