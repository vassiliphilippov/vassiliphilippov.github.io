self-willed

1. ADJ

/ sɛlfˈwɪld / (AmE), / sɛlfˈwɪld / (BrE)
determined to do as one wants, even if it means ignoring the wishes or advice of others; habitually disobedient or stubborn
determined to do as one wants; stubborn

Examples

Despite her parents' objections, she remained self-willed and pursued her dreams.
He can be self-willed at times, refusing to accept anyone else's advice.
Their self-willed attitude often led to conflict with authority figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Antonym: compliant - inclined to agree with others or obey rules

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'willed', meaning governed by one's own will.
