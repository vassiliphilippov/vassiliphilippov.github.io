powerhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.əˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a person who is extremely energetic, dynamic, or hardworking
an energetic, dynamic person

Examples

She is a powerhouse in the legal field, working late hours every day.
Their new coach is a powerhouse who motivates the team to perform at their best.
As a powerhouse of creativity, he constantly comes up with innovative ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamo - a highly energetic person
  • Synonym: live wire - a very energetic person

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.əˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a building or facility where electricity is generated for distribution
electrical generating station

Examples

The old powerhouse by the river has been converted into a museum.
After the storm, repairs to the powerhouse were urgently needed to restore electricity.
The city built a new powerhouse to meet increasing energy demands.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊərˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a team or organization that is extremely successful, effective, or dominant in its field
dominant or highly successful team or group

Examples

The university's basketball team has become a real powerhouse in recent years.
Germany is considered a powerhouse in international soccer, regularly reaching the finals.
The small tech startup grew rapidly and is now seen as a powerhouse in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - an organization or team that is very large or powerful
  • Antonym: underdog - a team or person expected to lose or be less successful

Etymology

Originally referred to a power-generating station; figurative use dates from early 20th century.

Known for his negotiation skills, he is considered a powerhouse among Wall Street executives.
