astronomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑːmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
Extremely large in amount, size, or extent; inconceivably vast.
extremely large or vast

Examples

The company reported astronomical profits last year, far exceeding all expectations.
The cost of the repairs turned out to be astronomical, so we decided not to proceed.
Some celebrities are paid astronomical sums for a single appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size, amount, or extent
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size

Etymology

From Latin 'astronomicus', based on 'astronomia' (astronomy).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑːmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to astronomy or the science of celestial objects, space, and the physical universe as a whole
relating to astronomy

Examples

She used an astronomical telescope to study distant planets.
His research focuses on astronomical phenomena such as black holes and supernovae.
The observatory houses an impressive collection of astronomical instruments.

Synset Examples

astronomic telescope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomic - relating to astronomy

Etymology

From Latin astronomicus, from Greek astronomikos, from astron (star) + nomos (law).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02657310-a
Synset POS: a
