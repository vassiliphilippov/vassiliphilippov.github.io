Polistes annularis

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɪstiːz æˈnjuːlərɪs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪstiːz æˈnjuːlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of paper wasp native to the southeastern United States, recognized for its reddish-brown color and distinctive yellow bands.
a species of paper wasp

Examples

Polistes annularis nests are often found in shrubs or on buildings in the southeastern United States.
The reddish-brown coloration helps Polistes annularis blend in with tree bark.
Researchers observed the social behavior of Polistes annularis colonies during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper wasp - a common name for wasps that build nests from paper-like material

Etymology

From the genus name Polistes (derived from Greek 'polistēs', meaning 'founder of a city') and Latin 'annularis', meaning 'ringed'.
