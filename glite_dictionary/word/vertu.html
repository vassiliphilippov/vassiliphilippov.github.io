vertu

1. NOUN

/ vɛɹˈtu / (AmE), / vɛˈtjuː / (BrE)
(archaic) Artistic quality or excellence, especially as appreciated in works of art.
artistic quality, especially in art
archaic

Examples

The painting possesses a certain vertu that captivates even the most discerning critics.
Collectors admired the antique's vertu as much as its historical value.
He devoted much study to the vertu of Renaissance sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - moral excellence; general sense of good quality

Etymology

From Old French *vertu*, meaning 'virtue', from Latin *virtus* (excellence, worth).
