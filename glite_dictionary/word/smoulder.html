smoulder

1. VERB

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
(of a person) to show or feel strong emotion, such as anger or passion, that is suppressed but can be seen in one's manner or expression.
show strong suppressed emotion

Examples

She tried to hide her anger, but it continued to smoulder beneath the surface.
His eyes smouldered with resentment as he listened to their criticism.
Even after the argument ended, her frustration kept smouldering inside her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed anger
  • Synonym: to suppress - to restrain or hold back emotion or expression
  • Antonym: to explode - to express one's feelings suddenly after holding them in

Etymology

From Middle English 'smolderen', probably of Low German or Dutch origin; related to Dutch 'smoren' (to smother).

2. VERB

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
to burn slowly with smoke but no flame
burn slowly without a flame
british

Examples

The logs continued to smoulder long after the flames had died down.
A pile of leaves smouldered in the corner of the garden, releasing a steady stream of smoke.
The campfire was left to smoulder overnight, filling the air with the scent of burning wood.

Synset Examples

a smoldering fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smolder - American English spelling of 'smoulder'
  • Degree Higher: burn - to be on fire; to consume with fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire or light

Etymology

From Middle English smolderen, probably from a Low German source, related to Dutch smoor (to smother).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
A slow, smoky fire that burns without visible flames.
slow, smoky fire without flames

Examples

The campfire dwindled to a quiet smoulder as dawn approached.
A faint smoulder continued in the ashes long after the fireworks ended.
The smoulder of burning leaves filled the air with a pungent smell.

Synset Examples

the smoulder suddenly became a blaze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke - the visible vapor and gases given off by a fire
  • Antonym: to flame - a hot glowing body of ignited gas seen when something is burning

Etymology

From Old English *smoldrian* (to smolder), related to Middle Low German *smelteren*.
