shabby

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃæbi / (AmE), / ˈʃæbi / (BrE)
(of actions, behavior, etc.) mean, unworthy, or despicable; showing a lack of fairness or respect.
mean or despicable (of behavior)

Examples

His friends gave him shabby treatment when he needed them the most.
It was a shabby act to leave her out of the invitation list.
Critics called the company's decision a shabby way to treat loyal customers.

Synset Examples

shabby treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃæbi / (AmE), / ˈʃæbi / (BrE)
In poor condition because of long use or lack of care; showing signs of wear or neglect.
worn out or in poor condition

Examples

After years of use, the shabby carpet was finally replaced with a new one.
He wore a shabby coat that looked several decades old.
The hotel was clean but very shabby around the edges.

Synset Examples

a ratty old overcoat
shabby furniture
an old house with dirty windows and tatty curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tatty - worn and in poor condition
  • Antonym: smart - neat and stylish in appearance

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier English dialect shab (scab, shabby person), related to the word "shab" meaning 'scab' from Middle English.
