costliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔstli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒstli.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being expensive or having great value
the state of being expensive

Examples

The costliness of the jewelry made it unsuitable for daily wear.
Many people complain about the costliness of organic groceries compared to conventional products.
Due to the costliness of repairs, they decided to buy a new car instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensiveness - the quality of being expensive
  • Antonym: cheapness - the state of being inexpensive or low in cost

Etymology

From 'costly' + '-ness', with 'costly' from Middle English from Anglo-Norman 'coste' (cost).
