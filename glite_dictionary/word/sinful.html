sinful

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪnf(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a person, action, or thing) characterized by or full of sin; morally wrong or wicked.
morally wrong or wicked; full of sin

Examples

He regretted living such a sinful life during his youth.
Eating too much chocolate felt almost sinful to her.
The preacher warned against the dangers of leading a sinful existence.

Synset Examples

a sinful person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicked - evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing moral goodness

Etymology

From Old English synfull (‘full of sin’).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪnfəl / (BrE)
(of an action, thought, etc.) morally wrong or wicked because it is considered a sin.
morally wrong; wicked; involving sin

Examples

She felt that eating chocolate cake before dinner was sinful.
The preacher spoke against the sinful behavior he saw in the community.
He tried to resist his sinful thoughts but struggled every day.

Synset Examples

iniquitous deeds
he said it was sinful to wear lipstick
ungodly acts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicked - morally wrong or evil
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing moral virtue

Etymology

From Old English synfull, from syn (sin) + full (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02524601-s
Synset POS: s
