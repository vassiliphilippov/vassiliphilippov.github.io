properly

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑː.pɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.ə.li / (BrE)
in a correct or suitable way; in the way that is considered right or appropriate
in the correct or appropriate way

Examples

It is important to wash your hands properly before eating.
The machine will not work properly unless it is assembled correctly.
She always dresses properly for formal occasions.

Synset Examples

please do your job properly!
can't you carry me decent?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correctly - in a manner that is free from error
  • Antonym: improperly - in a way that is not suitable or correct

Etymology

From proper + -ly; ultimately from Latin proprius (one's own, proper) plus the adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈprɑː.pɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.ə.li / (BrE)
in a way that is exact, precise, or strictly correct; used especially in the phrase 'properly speaking.'
exactly; strictly speaking

Examples

Properly speaking, this is not a novel but a memoir.
The amount of sugar must be measured properly or the recipe will not turn out right.
She insisted that, properly, the term only refers to ancient manuscripts.

Synset Examples

he was doing precisely (or exactly) what she had told him to do
it was just as he said — the jewel was gone
it has just enough salt
Properly speaking, all true work is religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precisely - in an exact or accurate way
  • Synonym: exactly - in a way that is completely correct or accurate

Etymology

From proper + -ly; 'proper' from Latin proprius (one's own, special).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00159432-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈprɑː.pɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.əli / (BrE)
in a way that is appropriate or suitable for the circumstances
in an appropriate or suitable way

Examples

Please make sure the machine is installed properly before using it.
He doesn't know how to dress properly for formal events.
The children were taught to behave properly in public.

Synset Examples

he was appropriately dressed
If you don't behave properly, you'll have to leave!
I met the junior senator from Illinois and I was duly impressed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriately - in a suitable or proper way
  • Antonym: improperly - in a manner that is not proper or appropriate

Etymology

From 'proper' (Latin proprius meaning 'one's own, special') + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00140318-r
Synset POS: r
