to plasticize

1. VERB

/ ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to make (a material) flexible or moldable, usually by adding a plasticizer
make flexible or moldable, esp. by adding plasticizer

Examples

The manufacturer will plasticize the material to improve its flexibility for packaging uses.
Certain additives can plasticize rubber, making it suitable for various industrial applications.
When heated and treated with a solvent, the film will gradually plasticize and become easier to shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - make or become less hard or firm

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-ize', meaning to make plastic or capable of being shaped.

2. VERB

/ ˈplæs.tɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to make a material, especially plastic, more flexible or pliable by adding a plasticizer
make material more flexible by adding plasticizer

Examples

Manufacturers often plasticize PVC to enhance its flexibility for use in cables and hoses.
The company developed a new method to plasticize natural rubber for industrial applications.
If you plasticize the material too much, it may lose its structural integrity.

Synset Examples

plasticized PVC

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to harden - to make or become hard or harder

Etymology

From plastic + -ize, meaning 'to make plastic'.
