panicled

1. ADJ

/ ˈpænɪkəld / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkəld / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) having or arranged in panicles; bearing flowers in branched clusters.
having or arranged in panicles

Examples

The meadow was filled with panicled grasses swaying in the breeze.
Many ornamental shrubs produce panicled clusters of tiny white flowers in summer.
The panicled inflorescence is a characteristic feature of this plant species.

Synset Examples

a panicled inflorescence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panicle - a loose, branching cluster of flowers

Etymology

From 'panicle', ultimately from Latin 'panicula' (tuft, panicle) + -ed.
