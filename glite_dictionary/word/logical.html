logical

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
able to think, reason, or express ideas in a clear, consistent, and rational manner.
capable of clear and rational thinking

Examples

She is a very logical thinker, able to solve complex problems with ease.
After the accident, it took her some time to become logical and coherent again.
His arguments were always logical and well-structured, making him an effective debater.

Synset Examples

a lucid thinker
she was more coherent than she had been just after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucid - expressing oneself clearly and easily understood
  • Synonym: coherent - able to speak or write in a clear and consistent way

Etymology

From Greek logikos (rational), from logos (reason, word).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01932107-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
able to reason in a clear and consistent way, or showing this ability
capable of clear, valid reasoning

Examples

She has a very logical approach to solving complex problems.
His argument was carefully structured and extremely logical.
It's important to stay logical when making difficult decisions.

Synset Examples

a logical mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rational - able to think clearly and sensibly
  • Antonym: illogical - not logical; lacking clear or valid reasoning

Etymology

From Greek logikos (pertaining to reasoning), via Latin logicus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01432984-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by a clear, orderly, and consistent relation of parts; coherent
coherent; orderly and consistent

Examples

Her essay presented a logical progression of ideas that was easy to follow.
The architect's design was praised for being both beautiful and logical.
For a story to be believable, its plot must be logical and flow naturally.

Synset Examples

a coherent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - (of a logical whole) holding together or making sense as a whole

Etymology

From Greek logos (reason, word) via Latin logicus and Old French logique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466504-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
Using clear and sound reasoning or based on known facts; reasonable.
reasonable, based on facts or reasoning

Examples

It was logical to bring an umbrella because the weather forecast predicted rain.
Given her experience and skills, it was a logical decision to hire her for the job.
The argument follows a logical sequence, making it easy to understand.

Synset Examples

rain was a logical expectation, given the time of year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible
  • Antonym: illogical - not logical; lacking sound reasoning

Etymology

From Medieval Latin logicalis, from Latin logica 'logic' (from Ancient Greek λογικός, logikos).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02509114-s
Synset POS: s
