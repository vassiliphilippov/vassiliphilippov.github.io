Rigil

1. PROPN

/ ˈraɪdʒəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪl / (BrE)
A proper name for the brightest star in the constellation Centaurus, also known as Alpha Centauri, which is the second nearest star system to the sun.
brightest star in Centaurus; Alpha Centauri

Examples

The southern sky features Rigil shining brightly next to Hadar.
With the naked eye, observers can locate Rigil in the constellation Centaurus.
Astronomers have studied Rigil extensively because of its proximity to Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alpha Centauri - the star system of which Rigil is a part
  • Degree Lower: Proxima Centauri - the nearest star to the sun, part of Alpha Centauri system

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Rijl' (the foot), referring to its position in the constellation.
