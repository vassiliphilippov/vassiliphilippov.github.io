Betty Friedan

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛti friˈdæn / (AmE), / ˈbɛti friˈdan / (BrE)
(1921–2006) An American feminist, writer, and activist, best known for her book 'The Feminine Mystique' and for founding the National Organization for Women.
American feminist and writer (1921–2006)

Examples

Betty Friedan was instrumental in sparking the second wave of feminism in the United States.
Many credit Betty Friedan with changing public attitudes about women's roles in society.
The organization founded by Betty Friedan continues to advocate for women's rights today.
