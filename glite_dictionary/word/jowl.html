jowl

1. NOUN

/ dʒaʊl / (AmE), / dʒaʊl / (BrE)
the lower part of a person's cheek or jaw, especially when it is full or sagging, often due to age
sagging flesh of the lower cheek or jaw

Examples

With age, his jowls became more prominent and gave his face a distinguished look.
She noticed a slight sagging of her jowls when she looked in the mirror.
The actor's pronounced jowls added to his character's gruff appearance.

Etymology

From Middle English 'chowel', related to Old English 'ceole' (throat, neck).

2. NOUN

/ dʒaʊl / (AmE), / dʒaʊl / (BrE)
the lower part of a person's or animal's cheek, especially the area around the jaw that is often fleshy or hangs loosely
lower cheek or jaw area

Examples

As he grew older, his jowls became more pronounced and sagged slightly.
The bulldog's hanging jowls gave it a distinctive look.
Actors often use makeup to accentuate their jowls for certain roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaw - the lower part of the face; the bone that forms the framework of the mouth

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'jole', related to Old French 'joue' (cheek).
