Medicago arborea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkɑɡoʊ ɑːrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːɡəʊ ɑːˈbɔːrɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to southern European highlands, with downy leaves, yellow flowers throughout the summer, and snail-shaped seed pods
evergreen shrub native to southern Europe

Examples

The Medicago arborea was in full bloom, its bright yellow flowers attracting lots of bees.
Gardeners admire Medicago arborea for its striking foliage and unusual seed pods.
Many Mediterranean landscapes feature Medicago arborea as an ornamental shrub due to its resilience.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Medicago' (genus name), 'arborea' meaning 'tree-like' or 'woody'.
