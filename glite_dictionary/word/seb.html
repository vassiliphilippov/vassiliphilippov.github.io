SEB

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.iːˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌɛs.iːˈbiː / (BrE)
a staphylococcal enterotoxin (type B) used as a potential incapacitating biological warfare agent and linked to food poisoning
staphylococcal enterotoxin B; biological agent
medical

Examples

Researchers detected traces of SEB in the contaminated food sample.
The military once considered SEB as a possible non-lethal biological weapon.
Exposure to SEB can cause severe gastrointestinal symptoms in humans.

Etymology

Acronym from Staphylococcal Enterotoxin B.
