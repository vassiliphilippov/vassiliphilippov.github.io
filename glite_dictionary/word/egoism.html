egoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
(ethics) The theory that the pursuit of one's own interests or welfare is the foundation of morality.
theory that self-interest underpins morality

Examples

Many philosophers have debated whether egoism can truly provide a solid basis for ethical behavior.
His strong belief in egoism led him to prioritize his own needs above all else.
Some critics argue that egoism ultimately undermines social cooperation and trust.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: altruism - the principle or practice of unselfish concern for others

Etymology

From French égoïsme, from Latin ego (I).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to be primarily concerned with one's own interests and welfare, sometimes to the exclusion of others
self-centered concern for one's own interests

Examples

Her egoism made it difficult for her to form genuine friendships.
Philosophers have long debated whether human behavior is driven by egoism or altruism.
The manager's egoism was evident in his inability to consider his team's needs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • Synonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; concern chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure

Etymology

From French 'égoïsme', based on Latin 'ego' meaning 'I'.
