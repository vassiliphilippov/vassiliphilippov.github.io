Fraxinus texensis

1. NOUN

/ frækˈsaɪnəs tɛkˈsɛnsɪs / (AmE), / frækˈsaɪnəs tɛkˈsɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of low-growing ash tree native to Texas, known as Texas ash
a species of ash tree native to Texas

Examples

The Fraxinus texensis is commonly found in rocky limestone hills across central Texas.
Botanists have studied how Fraxinus texensis adapts to drought conditions.
Several birds nest in the branches of the Fraxinus texensis during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a type of tree in the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning ash tree, 'texensis' meaning from Texas.
