plonk

1. NOUN

/ plɑŋk / (AmE), / plɒŋk / (BrE)
a short, resonant sound made when something drops into liquid, especially with a sudden or heavy splash
a sound of dropping into liquid

Examples

The pebble made a loud plonk as it fell into the pond.
She dropped her keys in the bucket with a metallic plonk.
We heard a sudden plonk from the kitchen, followed by laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plop - a soft, rounded sound made when something drops into liquid

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, similar to other onomatopoeic words like "plop".

2. NOUN

/ plɑŋk / (AmE), / plɒŋk / (BrE)
A cheap wine of inferior quality, often considered unrefined or of low value.
cheap, low-quality wine
informal british

Examples

After a long day, they shared a bottle of plonk over dinner.
She bought some plonk for the picnic because it was inexpensive.
He can’t tell the difference between good wine and plonk.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wine - an alcoholic drink made from fermented grapes

Etymology

Probably from Australian slang, perhaps an alteration of French 'blanc' (white), referring to white wine.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07912969-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ plɑŋk / (AmE), / plɒŋk / (BrE)
to put or drop something or oneself down heavily or noisily, often carelessly
set or drop down heavily or noisily

Examples

She plonked the groceries down on the kitchen counter with a sigh.
He plonked himself into the armchair after a long day at work.
Without a word, Mia plonked her bag on the floor and headed upstairs.

Synset Examples

He planked the money on the table
He planked himself into the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - to let or make something fall vertically
  • Synonym: place - to put or set something in a particular position
