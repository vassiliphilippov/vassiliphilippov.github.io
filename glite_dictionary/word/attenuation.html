attenuation

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌtɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a reduction in the force, effect, or intensity of something
weakening in force or intensity

Examples

The attenuation of the signal was caused by the long cable run.
Soundproofing materials are used for the attenuation of unwanted noise.
There was noticeable attenuation in his voice over the phone.

Synset Examples

attenuation in the volume of the sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakening - the process of becoming weaker
  • Antonym: amplification - the process of making something stronger, especially in signal strength

Etymology

From Latin attenuatio(n-), from attenuare 'to make thin'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌtɛn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌtɛnjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of being weakened, reduced in thickness, force, or density, especially in sound, signal, or physical matter.
process of weakening or reducing density

Examples

Modern fiber optic cables minimize the attenuation of data signals over long distances.
The attenuation of the medication's effect means it must be administered more frequently.
Researchers measured the attenuation of sound as it passed through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amplification - the process of increasing in strength or intensity

Etymology

From Latin attenuatio (a making thin or weak), from attenuare (to make thin).
