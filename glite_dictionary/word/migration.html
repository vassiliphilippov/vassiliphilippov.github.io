migration

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The nonrandom movement of an atom or group within a molecule to a new position.
movement of an atom/group within a molecule (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The migration of a hydrogen atom within the molecule results in a structural change.
Chemists study the migration of radicals during complex organic reactions.
A rearrangement can occur by migration of a substituent to a different position in the skeleton.

Etymology

From Latin migratio (a removal, change of abode), from migrare (to move).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people moving together from one place to another, especially over a given period.
group moving from one place to another

Examples

Each year, the migration of farm workers brings new life to the rural towns.
The museum featured an exhibit on the migration of Irish families during the 19th century.
That migration of people transformed the entire region over a single decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to settle elsewhere
  • Antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Latin migratio (a moving from one place to another), from migrare (to move, migrate).

3. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement of people from one country or region to another, often for the purpose of settling permanently or temporarily in a new location
movement of people from one place to another

Examples

The recent increase in migration to urban areas has transformed the region's economy.
Seasonal migration patterns can be influenced by changes in job opportunities.
Government policies often have a significant impact on migration flows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immigration - entering and settling in a foreign country
  • Synonym: emigration - leaving one's own country to settle elsewhere

Etymology

From Latin migratio (change of residence), from migrare (to move from one place to another).

4. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the regular movement of groups of animals from one region to another, particularly for feeding or breeding purposes
seasonal movement of animals

Examples

The annual migration of monarch butterflies covers thousands of miles.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Arctic terns.
Whale migration brings these creatures close to the shore every winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one place to settle in another, especially of people
  • Synonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign region or country

Etymology

From Latin 'migratio' (a change of abode, removal).
