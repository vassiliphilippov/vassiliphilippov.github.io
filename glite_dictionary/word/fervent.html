fervent

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɜrvənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvənt / (BrE)
(Archaic) Extremely hot; burning or glowing intensely.
extremely hot or glowing (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The travelers sought shade from the fervent sun beating down upon the desert sands.
The blacksmith’s workshop glowed with the fervent heat of the forge.
Poets once wrote about the fervent fires that burned through the ancient city.

Synset Examples

the fervent heat … merely communicated a genial warmth to their half-torpid systems
set out … when the fervid heat subsides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervid - very hot or glowing; intense

Etymology

From Latin fervent-, present participle of 'fervere' (to boil, glow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɜrvənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvənt / (BrE)
having or displaying very strong and sincere feelings; passionate or intense.
having intense or passionate feelings

Examples

She gave a fervent speech urging everyone to support the cause.
His fervent hope was that the team would win the championship.
A fervent admirer wrote letters expressing her deep admiration for the artist.

Synset Examples

ardent love
an ardent lover
a fervent desire to change society
a fervent admirer
fiery oratory
an impassioned appeal
a torrid love affair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardent - having very strong feelings; passionate or enthusiastic
  • Synonym: impassioned - filled with or showing strong emotion
  • Degree Lower: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From Latin fervent-, present participle of fervēre (to boil, glow, be hot).
