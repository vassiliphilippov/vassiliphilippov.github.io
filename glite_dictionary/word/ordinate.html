ordinate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɪnət / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
(in mathematics) the value of a point measured along the vertical or y-axis in a coordinate system.
vertical axis coordinate value

Examples

On the graph, the ordinate corresponds to the height of the plotted point above the x-axis.
Every point is defined by its abscissa and ordinate in the Cartesian plane.
If the ordinate is negative, the point lies below the x-axis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abscissa - the value of a coordinate on the horizontal axis

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinatus' (arranged, ordered), past participle of 'ordinare'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɔrdɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to appoint someone to a clerical or religious office, especially as a priest or minister
appoint to a religious or clerical office

Examples

After years of study, she was finally ordinated as a minister in her church.
The bishop will ordinate three new priests this weekend.
Many families attended the ceremony where their loved one was ordinated.

Synset Examples

he was ordained in the Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ordain - to make someone a priest or minister by a special ceremony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ordinatus, past participle of ordinare (to put in order, to appoint).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
