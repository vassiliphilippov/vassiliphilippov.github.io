pathogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæθəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæθəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which a disease develops and progresses
disease development process
medical

Examples

Researchers study the pathogenesis of infections to develop better treatments.
Understanding the pathogenesis of cancer is crucial for creating new therapies.
The article examines the pathogenesis of autoimmune disorders in detail.

Etymology

From Greek 'pathos' (suffering, disease) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
