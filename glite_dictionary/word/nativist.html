nativist

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.t̬ɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
advocating the preservation or advancement of the interests of native inhabitants against those of immigrants or outsiders
favoring native inhabitants over outsiders

Examples

The candidate's nativist rhetoric appealed to some voters but alienated others.
Many nativist policies have been criticized for encouraging exclusion and intolerance.
A rise in nativist sentiment often follows periods of increased immigration.

Synset Examples

the old nativist prejudice against the foreign businessman
the nativistic faith preaches the old values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xenophobic - having or showing a dislike of or prejudice against people from other countries
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - including people from many different countries; open to foreign influences

Etymology

From native + -ist, denoting advocacy for native interests.

2. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting nativism, especially in the context of favoring native-born inhabitants over immigrants, or the view that certain skills or abilities are innate rather than acquired.
relating to nativism or advocacy of native-born interests

Examples

Some politicians have expressed nativist views regarding immigration reform.
The debate between nativist and empiricist theories is important in developmental psychology.
His nativist attitudes made him resistant to cultural diversity initiatives.

Synset Examples

nativist theories
the traditional controversy between the nativistic and empiristic theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nativism - the policy of protecting the interests of native inhabitants
  • Antonym: empiricist - relating to the view that knowledge comes from experience

Etymology

From native + -ist, patterned after similar formations indicating advocacy or adherence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03000018-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that certain ideas or abilities are innate and not acquired through experience, especially in philosophy or psychology
someone who believes in innate ideas or abilities

Examples

As a nativist, she argued that language acquisition is rooted in inborn structures of the mind.
Many nativists reject the idea that all knowledge is gained from experience alone.
Chomsky is often cited as a prominent nativist in modern linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricist - a person who believes that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience

Etymology

From Latin 'nativus' (innate, native), with '-ist' denoting an adherent to a principle or doctrine.
