Pseudemys rubriventris

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdɛmɪs ˌruːbrəˈvɛntrɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɛmɪs ˌruːbrɪˈvɛntrɪs / (BrE)
a freshwater turtle of Chesapeake Bay tributaries, distinguished by red markings on its lower shell
freshwater turtle with red underside

Examples

The Pseudemys rubriventris is often found basking on logs along quiet streams.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Pseudemys rubriventris in Maryland.
Young Pseudemys rubriventris have vibrant red plastrons that fade as they age.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Pseudemys (‘false turtle’) + species name rubriventris (‘red belly’).
