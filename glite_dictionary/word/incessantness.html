incessantness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛsəntnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛsəntnəs / (BrE)
The quality of continuing without pause or interruption; ceaselessness.
quality of being incessant; ceaselessness

Examples

The incessantness of the rain made it difficult to sleep through the night.
He was worn down by the incessantness of the traffic outside his apartment.
She admired the incessantness with which the volunteers offered their help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaselessness - the quality of being continuous and without end
  • Antonym: intermittence - the quality of being not continuous, occurring at intervals

Etymology

From 'incessant' + '-ness', modeled after Late Latin 'incessantem'.
