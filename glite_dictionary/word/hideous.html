hideous

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈhɪdiəs / (BrE)
Extremely ugly or repulsive; shockingly offensive to decency or morality.
extremely ugly or morally offensive

Examples

The room was decorated in a hideous combination of orange and neon green.
The dictator was responsible for hideous acts of cruelty during his regime.
She recoiled from the hideous statue installed in the garden.

Synset Examples

subjected to outrageous cruelty
a hideous pattern of injustice
horrific conditions in the mining industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrific - causing horror or shock
  • Synonym: outrageous - shockingly bad or excessive
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Old French 'hideus', from Latin 'horridus' (frightful, bristling).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈhɪdiəs / (BrE)
Extremely ugly or shocking in appearance; causing horror or disgust.
extremely ugly; horrifying

Examples

The abandoned house was so hideous that no one dared to enter it at night.
She wore a hideous sweater with neon colors that clashed horribly.
The monster in the story had a hideous face, terrifying all who saw it.

Synset Examples

a hideous scar
a repulsive mask

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ugly - unpleasant or repulsive in appearance
  • Antonym: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically

Etymology

From Old French *hideus* (frightful), from Latin *horridus* (frightful, dreadful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00223526-s
Synset POS: s
