Moloch horridus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlɑk hɔːˈraɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈməʊlɒk hɒˈrɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of spiny desert lizard native to Australia, known for feeding primarily on ants.
Australian spiny desert lizard

Examples

The Moloch horridus is covered in spines that help protect it from predators.
In the Australian outback, the Moloch horridus survives by consuming thousands of ants each day.
Scientists studied how the Moloch horridus collects water on its skin in arid environments.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Moloch' after a biblical name, and 'horridus' meaning 'rough' or 'bristly'.
