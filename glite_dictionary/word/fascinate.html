to fascinate

1. VERB

/ ˈfæsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to attract and hold the intense interest or curiosity of someone
attract intense interest or curiosity

Examples

The magician's tricks always fascinate the children in the audience.
She was endlessly fascinated by the mysteries of the universe.
New technology continues to fascinate people of all ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrigue - to arouse curiosity or interest
  • Antonym: bore - to cause someone to feel uninterested

Etymology

From Latin 'fascinatus', past participle of 'fascinare' (to bewitch).

2. VERB

/ ˈfæs.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæs.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to attract and hold the attention or interest of someone very strongly
hold intense interest

Examples

Ancient history continues to fascinate scholars and students around the world.
The magician's tricks fascinated the children for hours.
She fascinates everyone she meets with her charm and wit.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of; charm
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested and tired

Etymology

From Latin fascinatus, past participle of fascinare (to bewitch, charm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01810472-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfæs.ə.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæs.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to hold completely spellbound so as to render motionless, especially through terror, awe, or intense interest.
to hold spellbound; render motionless

Examples

The movements of the snake fascinate the small mouse, leaving it frozen in place.
Stories of haunted castles fascinate the children, making them too scared to move.
The magician's intense gaze seemed to fascinate his volunteer, who stood perfectly still on stage.

Synset Examples

The snake charmer fascinates the cobra

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to attract and hold by charm, delight, or wonder
  • Synonym: to hypnotize - to capture and hold the attention as if by a spell
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or keep away, the opposite of fascinate

Etymology

From Latin fascinare (to bewitch, enchant).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
