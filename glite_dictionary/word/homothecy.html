homothecy

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑːθəsi / (AmE), / həˈmɒθəsi / (BrE)
(Mathematics) An isotropic scaling transformation of an affine space with a single fixed point.
mathematical affine transformation with a fixed point
medical

Examples

Through a homothecy, every point in the plane is moved along a straight line radiating from the center.
A homothecy with a coefficient greater than one enlarges objects while preserving their shape.
The properties of a homothecy are fundamental in the study of similarities in geometry.

Synset Examples

Successive applications of homotheties with coefficients k1 and k2 is either a homothety with coefficient k1k2, if the latter differs from 1, or a parallel translation, otherwise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilation - a transformation that produces an image that is the same shape as the original, but is resized

Etymology

From Greek homóthetos (of equal scale, adjusted to unite).
