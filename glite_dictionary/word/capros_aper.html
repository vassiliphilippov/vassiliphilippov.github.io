Capros aper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.rɒs ˈeɪ.pər / (AmE), / ˈkæp.rɒs ˈeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a small marine fish with a projecting snout, commonly known as the boarfish, found in the eastern Atlantic and Mediterranean Sea.
species of marine fish (boarfish)

Examples

The Capros aper is easily recognized by its distinctive protruding snout and bright red color.
Marine biologists recorded several Capros aper during the Mediterranean survey.
Anglers sometimes refer to the Capros aper as the boarfish due to its pig-like snout.
