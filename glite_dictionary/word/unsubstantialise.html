to unsubstantialise

1. VERB

/ ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to render immaterial or incorporeal; to make unsubstantial
render immaterial or incorporeal

Examples

The story seeks to unsubstantialise the boundaries between reality and dreams.
The philosopher attempted to unsubstantialise the concept of the soul in his writings.
Over time, her memories seemed to unsubstantialise, fading into vague impressions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immaterialize - cause to become immaterial; render incorporeal
  • Antonym: to materialize - cause to become material or corporeal

Etymology

Formed from 'unsubstantial' + '-ise', following English verb-forming pattern.
