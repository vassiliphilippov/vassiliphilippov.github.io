strontianite

1. NOUN

/ strɒnˈtiːəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / strɒnˈtɪənaɪt / (BrE)
A mineral consisting of strontium carbonate (SrCO₃), typically found in fibrous or columnar crystals.
a mineral of strontium carbonate

Examples

The geologist identified the white crystals as strontianite during her field survey.
Pure strontianite is an important source of strontium for industrial use.
Collectors prize strontianite specimens for their rare and striking crystal formations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestite - a mineral consisting of strontium sulfate, often associated with strontianite

Etymology

Named after Strontian, a village in Scotland where it was first discovered, plus the suffix '-ite' for minerals.
