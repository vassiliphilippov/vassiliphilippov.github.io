Rachycentron

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪkiˈsɛntrɑn / (AmE), / ˌreɪkɪˈsɛntrɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish consisting solely of the cobia species.
genus of fish; cobia

Examples

The Rachycentron is represented by only a single living species, the cobia.
Marine biologists studied the unique dorsal fin of the Rachycentron.
Due to its commercial value, Rachycentron has attracted interest in aquaculture research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobia - a large marine fish of the genus Rachycentron

Etymology

From Greek 'rhachis' (spine) + 'kentron' (sting), referring to the fish's spiny fin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪsɪˈsɛntrən / (AmE), / ˌrækɪˈsɛntrən / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish consisting of a single species, the cobia, found in warm waters.
genus of fish including cobia

Examples

The Rachycentron is notable for being the only genus in its family, Rachycentridae.
Marine biologists study Rachycentron to understand the unique traits of cobia.
Cobia, the sole member of Rachycentron, is prized by both anglers and chefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobia - a large, predatory marine fish found in warm waters

Etymology

From Greek rhachis (spine) + kentron (spur).
