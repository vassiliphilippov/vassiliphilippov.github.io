hematocyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmətoʊsɪst / (AmE), / ˈhiːmətəʊsɪst / (BrE)
A cyst filled with blood.
a cyst filled with blood
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed the hematocyst discovered during the operation.
A large hematocyst may cause pain or discomfort if it presses against surrounding tissues.
The doctor explained that a hematocyst results from bleeding within a cystic cavity.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + kystis (bladder, pouch).
