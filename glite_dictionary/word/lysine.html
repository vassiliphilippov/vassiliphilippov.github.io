lysine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsiːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid that is a constituent of proteins and is required in the diet of humans and animals
an essential amino acid
medical

Examples

Milk and eggs are good dietary sources of lysine.
Vegetarians need to ensure adequate lysine intake through legumes and soy products.
The research focused on the role of lysine in muscle growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound that combines to form proteins

Etymology

From German 'Lysin', from 'Lysin(e)' + '-ine', based on Greek 'lysis' (loosening) due to its discovery in the hydrolysis of proteins.
