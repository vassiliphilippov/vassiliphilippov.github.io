abortion

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɔr.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈbɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate ending of a pregnancy so that it does not result in the birth of a baby
termination of pregnancy

Examples

She made the difficult decision to have an abortion early in her pregnancy.
The topic of abortion often sparks heated debate in many countries.
Some clinics provide counseling services for women considering an abortion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miscarriage - spontaneous loss of a pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin abortio (miscarriage), from aboriri (to miscarry).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / əˈbɔːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the failure of a project, plan, or undertaking before it reaches completion
failure of a plan or undertaking

Examples

The company's attempt to launch a new product ended in abortion after several setbacks.
His career plans suffered an abortion due to unforeseen circumstances.
The negotiations came to an abrupt abortion, leaving both sides dissatisfied.

Etymology

From Latin abortio (miscarriage, premature birth, failure).
