Dysdercus

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdɜːrkəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈdɜːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of slender, long-legged bugs that feed on the developing seeds of cotton and stain the fiber, commonly known as cotton stainers
a genus of cotton-staining bugs

Examples

The infestation of Dysdercus led to significant cotton crop losses in the region.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of Dysdercus to develop better pest management practices for cotton farmers.
Because Dysdercus stains cotton, its presence can lower the quality of harvested fibers.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad) + derkesthai (to see), referring to the destructive behavior and appearance.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdɜrkəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈdɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, long-legged bugs that feed on developing seeds of cotton and can stain the fiber.
genus of bugs feeding on cotton seeds

Examples

Several species belonging to the Dysdercus genus are notorious pests in cotton-producing regions.
Scientists studied how the Dysdercus bugs affect cotton fiber quality.
The infestation of Dysdercus led to significant crop losses last year.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) and derkos (to see), used in scientific naming.
