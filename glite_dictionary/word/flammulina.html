Flammulina

1. NOUN

/ flæməˈluːnə / (AmE), / flamjʊˈluːnə / (BrE)
A genus of agaric fungi, including species such as the edible enoki mushroom.
genus of agaric mushrooms

Examples

The genus Flammulina includes the popular enoki mushroom.
Researchers studied several species within Flammulina for their medicinal properties.
You can find Flammulina growing on decaying wood in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'flammula' (small flame), referring to the color of some species' caps.

2. NOUN

/ flæməˈluːnə / (AmE), / flamjʊˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of gilled mushrooms in the family Physalacriaceae, known for species like the velvet shank.
genus of gilled mushrooms

Examples

The scientist identified the mushroom as belonging to the genus Flammulina.
Species of Flammulina are often found growing on tree trunks in winter.
The edible mushroom Enoki is botanically classified under the genus Flammulina.

Etymology

From Latin 'flammula', meaning 'little flame', referring to the orange color of some species.
