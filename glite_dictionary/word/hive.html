hive

1. NOUN

/ haɪv / (AmE), / haɪv / (BrE)
a box or other structure made by people for bees to live in and raise their young
man-made container for bees

Examples

The beekeeper carefully opened the hive to check on the bees.
Several hives stood in the sunny corner of the garden.
They harvested honey from the hive in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beehive - structure in which bees are kept, especially a man-made one

Etymology

From Old English hȳf (basket, hive).

2. NOUN

/ haɪv / (AmE), / haɪv / (BrE)
A place or situation filled with a large number of people or activities, often bustling with energy.
busy place with many people or activities

Examples

The market became a hive as vendors set up their stalls before dawn.
Throughout the holiday season, the shopping mall turned into a hive of excited shoppers.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ haɪv / (AmE), / haɪv / (BrE)
a structure, often natural like a hollow tree, that serves as a home for bees
a natural or artificial home for bees

Examples

The wild bees built their hive in the hollow trunk of an old oak tree.
Smoke drifted from the hive as the beekeeper tried to calm the bees.
We found a natural hive while hiking through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beehive - an artificial structure for keeping bees

Etymology

Old English hȳf (beehive), of Germanic origin, related to Dutch bijenkorf.
