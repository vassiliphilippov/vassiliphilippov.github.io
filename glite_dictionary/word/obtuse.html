obtuse

1. ADJ

/ əbˈtus / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs / (BrE)
(of a leaf shape) having a rounded or blunt apex rather than a pointed one.
leaf with a rounded apex

Examples

The plant was easy to identify by its obtuse leaves, which were broad and rounded at the tip.
In botany class, we learned to distinguish obtuse leaf shapes from those with sharp points.
Some tree species have obtuse leaves that make them easily distinguishable from others with pointed foliage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acute - (of a leaf shape) having a pointed apex

Etymology

From Latin obtusus (blunted, dull), past participle of obtundere (to beat against, blunt).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈtus / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs / (BrE)
(of an angle) greater than 90 degrees and less than 180 degrees.
angle between 90° and 180°

Examples

In a triangle, if one angle is obtuse, the other two must be acute.
The architect measured the obtuse angle formed by the two walls.
You can recognize an obtuse angle by its wide opening.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acute - (of an angle) less than 90 degrees
  • Antonym: right - (of an angle) exactly 90 degrees

Etymology

From Latin obtusus (blunt, dull).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ əbˈtuːs / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs / (BrE)
slow to understand or perceive; lacking in insight or discernment.
slow to understand; lacking insight

Examples

He was too obtuse to realize he had offended his colleagues.
Sometimes, her comments seem deliberately obtuse to avoid addressing the real issue.
The manager grew frustrated with the obtuse responses from the staff during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insightful - having or showing deep understanding; perceptive
  • Antonym: perceptive - having sensitive insight
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; slow in understanding

Etymology

From Latin 'obtusus' meaning 'blunted, dulled', past participle of 'obtuere' (to blunt).

slow to understand or perceive things; not quick-witted or sharp in intellect
slow to understand; not perceptive

Examples

Sometimes her explanations are so clear that only an obtuse person could misunderstand them.
The detective grew frustrated with the witness’s obtuse responses.
