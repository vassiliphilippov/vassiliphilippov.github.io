quartile

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔr.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈkwɔː.taɪl / (BrE)
(statistics) Any of three points that divide a set of ordered data into four equal parts, each containing a quarter of the values.
one of three dividing points in statistics

Examples

The upper quartile of scores represents the top 25% of students in the class.
Income in the lowest quartile is often used to identify households in poverty.
She analyzed the data and found that the second quartile contained most of the average performers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percentile - statistical value dividing a distribution into 100 equal groups

Etymology

From Latin 'quartus' (fourth) + '-ile', by analogy with 'percentile'.
