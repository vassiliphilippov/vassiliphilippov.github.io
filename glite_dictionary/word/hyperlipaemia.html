hyperlipaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərlɪˈpiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəliˈpiːmɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally high concentration of lipids in the blood.
excess lipids in the blood
medical british

Examples

Routine blood tests revealed hyperlipaemia in several patients from the clinic.
Diet and exercise can help manage hyperlipaemia and reduce cardiovascular risks.
The doctor explained that hyperlipaemia often has no symptoms but can be dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - abnormally high levels of lipids in the blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From hyper- (excess) + Greek lipos (fat) + -aemia (blood condition).
