naivete

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈivəˌteɪ / (AmE), / naɪˈiːvteɪ / (BrE)
lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; the quality of being naive.
lack of sophistication or worldliness

Examples

Her naivete made her an easy target for the cunning salesman.
He was criticized for his naivete about the harsh realities of politics.
Despite her naivete, she managed to succeed in the competitive industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - lack of experience or guile
  • Antonym: sophistication - the quality of being worldly-wise and experienced

Etymology

From French 'naïveté', from 'naïf' meaning 'naive'.
