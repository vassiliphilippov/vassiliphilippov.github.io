Yenisei

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (BrE)
The Uralic language spoken by the Yeniseian people, primarily in central Siberia.
Uralic language of the Yeniseian people

Examples

Few people today still speak Yenisei due to its near-extinct status.
Linguists are documenting the Yenisei language to preserve it for future study.
The Yenisei language is most closely associated with the tribes living along the Yenisei River.

Etymology

Named after the Yenisei River in Siberia, near which the language was historically spoken.

2. PROPN

/ ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (BrE)
a major river in Siberia, Russia, which flows northward from the Mongolian border to the Kara Sea.
river in Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Yenisei is one of the longest river systems in the world, stretching over 3,400 miles.
Every spring, the Yenisei floods vast areas, supporting the region’s rich animal life.
The Trans-Siberian Railway crosses the Yenisei near the city of Krasnoyarsk.
