Gaillardia

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈlɑːrdiə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈlɑːdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of western American plants in the daisy family, known for their showy, often brightly colored flowers and hairy stems.
genus of showy American flowering plants

Examples

The garden was brightened by a cluster of Gaillardia in full bloom.
Some varieties of Gaillardia are specially cultivated for their striking red and yellow petals.
Native bees are often attracted to the colorful blossoms of Gaillardia.

Etymology

Named after Gaillard de Charentonneau, an 18th-century French botanist.
