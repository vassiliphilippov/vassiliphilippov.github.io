insightful

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪtfəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtfəl / (BrE)
showing a clear and deep understanding of a complicated problem or situation
showing deep understanding

Examples

Her insightful comments helped the team solve the difficult issue.
The professor wrote an insightful article about the effects of climate change.
Many students found the documentary to be particularly insightful and eye-opening.

Synset Examples

an insightful parent
the chapter is insightful and suggestive of new perspectives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - able to notice or understand things quickly and effectively
  • Synonym: shrewd - having or showing sharp powers of judgement; astute
  • Antonym: superficial - lacking depth of understanding or thoroughness; shallow

Etymology

Formed from 'insight' + '-ful', meaning 'full of insight'.
