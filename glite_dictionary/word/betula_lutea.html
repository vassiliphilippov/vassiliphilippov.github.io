Betula lutea

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə luːˈtiːə / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːlə luːˈtiːə / (BrE)
a tree of eastern North America with thin, lustrous yellow or grey bark, commonly known as yellow birch
tree with yellow or grey bark (yellow birch)

Examples

The forest was dominated by Betula lutea, its yellow bark shining in the sunlight.
Researchers study Betula lutea to understand its role in the eastern North American ecosystem.
The lumber from Betula lutea is valued for its strength and attractive color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow birch - common name for Betula lutea
  • Degree Lower: Betula papyrifera - A related birch species with white bark (paper birch)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Betula' (birch) and 'lutea' (yellow), referring to its yellowish bark.
