anorthite

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔrˌθaɪt / (AmE), / əˈnɔːθʌɪt / (BrE)
a rare plagioclase feldspar mineral found in many igneous rocks, composed primarily of calcium and aluminum silicate
a rare plagioclase feldspar mineral

Examples

The geologist identified a crystal of anorthite embedded in the basalt sample.
Many lunar rocks contain significant amounts of anorthite, giving them a light color.
Anorthite is an important component in the formation of some igneous rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals making up most of the Earth's crust
  • Degree Lower: albite - a sodium-rich plagioclase feldspar mineral

Etymology

From Greek 'anorthos' (not upright), referring to its oblique crystal angles, with '-ite' as a standard mineral suffix.
