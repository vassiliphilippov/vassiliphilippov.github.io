biochemist

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who specializes in biochemistry
scientist specializing in biochemistry

Examples

After years of study, she became a respected biochemist at the university.
The work of a biochemist often involves both laboratory research and teaching.
As a biochemist, he investigates the chemical processes inside living organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemist - a person trained in chemistry

Etymology

From 'biochemistry' + '-ist', where 'biochemistry' is derived from 'bio-' (life) and 'chemistry'.
