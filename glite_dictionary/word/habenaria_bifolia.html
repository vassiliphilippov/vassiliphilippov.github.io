Habenaria bifolia

1. NOUN

/ həˌbɛnˈɛəriə baɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / həˌbɛnˈɛəriə baɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A south European orchid with fragrant greenish-white flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Habenaria.
south European orchid species

Examples

The rare Habenaria bifolia blooms in the meadows of southern Europe each spring.
Botanists studied the fragrance profile of Habenaria bifolia for potential horticultural uses.
Some scientists debate whether Habenaria bifolia belongs in the genus Habenaria or a separate one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for its complex flowers

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Habenaria' from 'habena' (rein, strap) referencing the flower's lip shape, and 'bifolia' meaning 'two-leaved.'
