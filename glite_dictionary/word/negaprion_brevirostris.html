Negaprion brevirostris

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛɡəˈpraɪən ˌbrɛvɪˈrɒstrɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛɡəˈpraɪən ˌbrɛvɪˈrɒstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of large, shallow-water shark found in the Atlantic Ocean from North Carolina to Brazil and off West Africa, known as the lemon shark and considered potentially dangerous.
a species of large Atlantic shark (lemon shark)

Examples

The Negaprion brevirostris is commonly found in warm, shallow waters along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers tagged a Negaprion brevirostris to track its migratory patterns over several months.
Swimmers are advised to be cautious in areas where Negaprion brevirostris is known to school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon shark - common name for Negaprion brevirostris, a large, yellowish shark found in shallow Atlantic waters

Etymology

From Latin: Negaprion, the genus name, and brevirostris, meaning 'short-snouted'.
