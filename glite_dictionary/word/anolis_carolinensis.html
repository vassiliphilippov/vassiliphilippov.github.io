Anolis carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊlɪs ˌkæroʊlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnəʊlɪs ˌkærəlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small, arboreal lizard native to the southeastern United States, capable of changing its skin color, commonly known as the green anole.
a small American lizard (green anole)

Examples

The Anolis carolinensis can change its color from bright green to brown depending on its environment.
Biologists observed a Anolis carolinensis basking on a fence post in a suburban backyard.
Because Anolis carolinensis feeds primarily on insects, it helps control pest populations in its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green anole - common name for Anolis carolinensis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Anolis' and species name 'carolinensis' meaning 'of Carolina'.
