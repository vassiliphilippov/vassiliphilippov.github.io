Parkinsonia aculeata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrkɪnˈsoʊniə ˌækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpɑːkɪnˈsəʊnɪə ˌækjuːˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree with sharp spines, pinnate leaves, and yellow-orange flowers, grown as an ornamental or hedge, and sometimes used as emergency livestock food.
spiny tropical shrub or tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The arid landscape was dotted with Parkinsonia aculeata trees in full bloom.
Some ranchers plant Parkinsonia aculeata as an emergency food source for their livestock.
Gardeners appreciate Parkinsonia aculeata for its resilience and striking yellow-orange flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem thorn - another common name for Parkinsonia aculeata

Etymology

From the genus name Parkinsonia, named after British botanist John Parkinson; aculeata from Latin 'aculeatus' meaning 'prickly' or 'thorny'.
