agnostical

1. ADJ

/ æɡˈnɑstɪkəl / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪkəl / (BrE)
uncertain or skeptical regarding the possibility of absolute knowledge, especially in matters of belief or philosophy
uncertain about the possibility of knowledge

Examples

She took an agnostical position in the debate, refusing to accept either side as ultimately knowable.
His agnostical attitude toward metaphysical questions frustrated those seeking definitive answers.
The philosopher's writings are marked by an agnostical skepticism about the limits of human understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agnostic - a person who believes that nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God; a person who claims neither faith nor disbelief in God

Etymology

From 'agnostic' + '-al', modeled as an adjective from the noun 'agnostic'.
