to melanise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənaɪz / (BrE)
(of an organism or tissue) to make or become black, especially by the formation of melanin.
make or become black (esp. biologically)
medical

Examples

Some fungi melanise when exposed to ultraviolet light as a protective response.
After injury, the tissue may melanise, turning visibly darker over time.
The cuticle of the insect melanises rapidly following its final molt.

Synset Examples

The smoke blackened the ceiling
The ceiling blackened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blacken - to make or become black in color

Etymology

From Greek 'melas, melanos' meaning 'black'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːlənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert into or infiltrate with melanin, causing darkening or pigmentation, especially in biological tissues
to convert or infiltrate with melanin
medical

Examples

In some insects, wounds melanise rapidly as part of their immune response.
The researchers observed that the tissue began to melanise after exposure to certain enzymes.
If bruised, the skin may melanise and become noticeably darker.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depigment - to remove pigment from, especially natural skin pigment

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black, dark) + '-ise', denoting the process of becoming or causing to become.
