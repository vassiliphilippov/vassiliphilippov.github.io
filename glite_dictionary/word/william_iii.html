William III

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1650–1702) King of England, Scotland, and Ireland who ascended the throne with Mary II after the Glorious Revolution.
English king after the Glorious Revolution

Examples

William III and Mary II were crowned as joint sovereigns after the Glorious Revolution.
The reign of William III marked a significant shift in parliamentary power in Britain.
Many supporters of Protestantism welcomed the arrival of William III to the English throne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary II - Queen of England who ruled jointly with William III
  • Antonym: James II - Predecessor and father-in-law of William III, deposed in the Glorious Revolution
