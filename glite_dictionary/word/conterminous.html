conterminous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɜr.mə.nəs / (AmE), / kɒnˈtɜː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
sharing a common boundary or edge; having a touching or adjoining border.
sharing a boundary; adjacent

Examples

The two countries are conterminous along their western borders.
These districts are conterminous with the river, following its twists and turns.
Her property is conterminous with the city park, making her backyard especially scenic.

Synset Examples

Rhode Island has two bordering states: Massachusetts and Connecticut
the side of Germany conterminous with France
Utah and the contiguous state of Idaho
neighboring cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contiguous - sharing a common border; touching
  • Synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else
  • Antonym: separate - not touching or remaining apart

Etymology

From Latin conterminus (having the same boundary), from con- (together) + terminus (boundary).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtɜr.mə.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɜː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
Having the same extent, scope, or duration as something else; coextensive or coincident in limits.
having the same extent or duration

Examples

The treaty established borders that are conterminous with those of the previous agreement.
Their powers were conterminous, each lasting exactly five years.
The two research projects were conterminous in both scope and duration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coextensive - having the same scope or extent
  • Synonym: coterminous - having the same limits or extent; often used synonymously

Etymology

From Latin 'conterminus' (bordering upon, adjoining), from 'con-' (together) + 'terminus' (boundary, end).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00483453-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ kənˈtɜrmənəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɜːmɪnəs / (BrE)
(of two or more things) sharing a common boundary or adjoining without a break; contiguous.
sharing a common boundary; contiguous

Examples

The conterminous countries maintained peace along their common border.
Most European Union states are conterminous with one or more neighboring countries.
The 48 conterminous states in the US exclude Alaska and Hawaii.

Synset Examples

the 48 conterminous states
the contiguous 48 states

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contiguous - sharing a common border; touching

Etymology

From Latin 'conterminus' (having a common boundary), from 'con-' (together) + 'terminus' (boundary).
