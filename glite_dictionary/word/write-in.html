write-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɪn / (BrE)
a vote in an election in which a person's name is written on the ballot by the voter because it does not appear among the printed choices
a vote for an unlisted candidate

Examples

Several voters chose to cast a write-in for their preferred candidate.
The election officials counted all the write-in votes after the polls closed.
She became mayor thanks to a successful write-in campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing, typically in secret

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'write in', referring to the act of entering a name on a form or ballot.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɪn / (BrE)
a candidate in an election whose name is not printed on the ballot and who must be added by voters writing in the name themselves
candidate not on ballot, written in by voters

Examples

Several voters chose to support a write-in rather than one of the listed candidates.
He launched a successful write-in campaign after missing the filing deadline.
The election rules allow for write-in candidates, giving every eligible person a chance to run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominee - a person who is nominated for a position or office
  • Antonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'write in', meaning to add by writing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ɪn / (BrE)
an option in an election allowing a voter to write the name of a candidate not officially listed on the ballot
option to vote for unlisted candidate

Examples

Many voters chose to support a write-in candidate when they were dissatisfied with the official nominees.
The election commission reported an unusually high number of write-in votes this year.
She campaigned as a write-in after missing the official deadline to get on the ballot.

Synset Examples

She decided to do a write-in for the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing and typically in secret

Etymology

From 'write' + 'in', referring to the act of writing a candidate's name in on the ballot.
