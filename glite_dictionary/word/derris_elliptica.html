Derris elliptica

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛrɪs ɛˈlɪptɪkə / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪs ɪˈlɪptɪkə / (BrE)
A species of woody vine native to Southeast Asia, known for its bright green leaves, racemes of rose-tinted white flowers, and roots that contain the insecticide rotenone.
species of vine producing rotenone

Examples

Derris elliptica is often grown for its roots, which are used to produce natural insecticides.
The active compound found in Derris elliptica has been studied for its effectiveness in pest control.
Botanists examined the flowers of Derris elliptica to better understand its classification.

Etymology

From genus name 'Derris', of Greek origin, and Latinized species name 'elliptica' for its elliptical leaves.
