Ilex decidua

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪlɛks dɪˈsɪdjuːə / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɛks dɪˈsɪdjuːə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern and central United States, known for its bright red berries; commonly called possumhaw.
deciduous shrub native to the US

Examples

The Ilex decidua is often planted for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.
Birds are attracted to the bright red berries of Ilex decidua in winter.
In landscaping, Ilex decidua is prized for adding color even when its leaves have fallen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - any plant of the genus Ilex, typically with spiny leaves and red berries

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ilex' meaning holly, and 'decidua' meaning falling off, referring to its deciduous leaves.
