Chlamyphorus truncatus

1. NOUN

/ klæˌmɪfəˌrəs trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / klæˌmɪfəˌrəs trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A very small species of armadillo native to Argentina, characterized by pale silky hair and pink plates on its head and neck, commonly known as the pink fairy armadillo.
species of small Argentine armadillo

Examples

The Chlamyphorus truncatus is rarely seen in the wild due to its secretive habits.
Researchers are working to protect the habitat of Chlamyphorus truncatus in central Argentina.
The pale pink plates of Chlamyphorus truncatus make it one of the most unusual armadillos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink fairy armadillo - common name for Chlamyphorus truncatus

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) + 'phoros' (bearing), and Latin 'truncatus' (cut off, shortened); scientific name coined in taxonomy.
