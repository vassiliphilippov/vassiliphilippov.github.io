astroglia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstroʊˈɡliə / (AmE), / ˌæstrəʊˈɡliːə / (BrE)
tissue in the nervous system made up of star-shaped neuroglial cells called astrocytes
tissue of star-shaped neuroglial cells
medical

Examples

Damage to the astroglia can impact brain function and neural repair.
Researchers have discovered new roles for astroglia in regulating synaptic activity.
The development of astroglia is crucial for maintaining the health of the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrocyte - a type of star-shaped glial cell in the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'glia' (glue), referring to the star-shaped cells and their supportive function.
