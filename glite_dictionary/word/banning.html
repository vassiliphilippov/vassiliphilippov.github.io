banning

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbanɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of officially prohibiting something by law or authority; an official prohibition
official prohibition; act of banning

Examples

The banning of smoking in public places has improved air quality.
Due to repeated violations, the banning of the product was announced by the agency.
Environmentalists support the banning of single-use plastics to reduce pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official order that states something is not allowed

Etymology

From the verb 'ban' (to prohibit), of Old English and Old Norse origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbænɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of officially prohibiting something, especially by law or authority
the act of officially prohibiting

Examples

There was much debate over the banning of smoking in public places.
The banning of certain books has sparked controversy in the community.
Environmental groups are calling for the banning of single-use plastics.

Synset Examples

The debate centered around banning assault rifles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - to officially prohibit something
  • Antonym: allowing - the act of permitting something

Etymology

From the verb "ban" (to prohibit), from Old English *bannan* (to summon, proclaim, command), later "to prohibit".
