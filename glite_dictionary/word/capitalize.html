to capitalize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to write or print (a letter or word) using a capital letter or all capital letters
write with a capital letter

Examples

Please capitalize the first letter of each sentence.
Names of countries should always be capitalized in formal writing.
Remember to capitalize proper nouns on your application form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowercase - to write or print using small letters; not capitals

Etymology

From Latin 'capitalis' (of the head, principal), from 'caput' (head); sense relates to 'capital letter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01696161-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert a company's reserve funds into capital by issuing shares or otherwise allocating them as part of the company's capital
convert reserve funds into capital

Examples

The board decided to capitalize the company's reserves to fund expansion.
Several companies capitalized their retained earnings by issuing new shares to shareholders.
After years of saving profits, the firm plans to capitalize the accumulated funds.

Etymology

From capital + -ize; ultimately from Latin 'capitale' (stock, principal) and the suffix '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00161975-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkæp.ɪ.t̬əlˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ɪ.təl.aɪz / (BrE)
(finance) To estimate and record the present value of a business or income, treating it as capital.
compute present value (finance)

Examples

The company decided to capitalize expected future profits when valuing its assets.
Accountants need to capitalize income streams to assess the firm's worth accurately.
It is essential to capitalize the business properly in order to attract investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amortize - reduce or pay off a debt over time

Etymology

From capital + -ize, modeled after French capitaliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00733144-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
in business and accounting, to treat money spent as a capital asset rather than as an expense to be deducted immediately
treat expenditure as a capital asset (accounting)

Examples

The company decided to capitalize the cost of new equipment instead of recording it as an expense.
Certain development costs can be capitalized and depreciated over time according to accounting standards.
Accountants must decide whether to capitalize or expense these expenditures based on established rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expense - to record money spent as an expense in accounting

Etymology

From capital + -ize; modeled after other verbs meaning 'to make into capital' in finance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00732985-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to gain an advantage from a situation, often by using it to one’s own benefit (usually used with 'on')
gain advantage from a situation

Examples

After the scandal broke, the opposition party tried to capitalize on the government’s misfortune.
She managed to capitalize on her networking skills to land a new job.
Savvy investors capitalize on market fluctuations to maximize their profits.

Synset Examples

he is capitalizing on her mistake
she took advantage of his absence to meet her lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to make full use of and derive benefit from (a resource or situation)
  • Synonym: to take advantage of - to use an opportunity to achieve results, sometimes unfairly
Source: WordNet

6. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to supply a business with capital, especially by combining investments from investors and loans from lenders
supply business with capital

Examples

The company was capitalized through a mix of private investment and bank loans.
Startups often seek to capitalize their businesses by attracting venture capital and loans.
The partners decided to capitalize the new firm with both equity and debt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fund - to provide money for a business or project

Etymology

From 'capital' (wealth or assets) + '-ize', meaning to make or treat in a certain way.

7. VERB

/ ˈkæpɪtlˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to calculate the present value of a business or income, especially for financial or accounting purposes
calculate present value (business)

Examples

The accountants will capitalize the company's future earnings to determine its current value.
When assessing a business for sale, it is important to capitalize its expected income streams.
Banks often capitalize anticipated profits to evaluate loan applications for major projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to estimate the monetary worth of something

Etymology

From capital + -ize, ultimately from Latin caput (head, source).
