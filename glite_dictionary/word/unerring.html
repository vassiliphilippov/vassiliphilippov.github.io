unerring

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
always accurate; never making a mistake or failing
always accurate; never missing

Examples

With unerring precision, the surgeon completed the complex procedure.
She has an unerring sense of direction, never getting lost even in strange cities.
His unerring ability to predict market trends made him a successful investor.

Synset Examples

the Church was … theoretically inerrant and omnicompetent
lack an inerrant literary sense
an unerring marksman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From un- (not) + erring, from Latin errare (to stray, to err).
