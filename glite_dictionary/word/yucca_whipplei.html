Yucca whipplei

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə wɪˈpliːaɪ / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə wɪˈpliːi / (BrE)
A species of yucca native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by a tall spike of creamy white flowers.
a yucca plant with tall spike of white flowers

Examples

The desert landscape was dotted with blooming Yucca whipplei in late spring.
Many gardeners in California cultivate Yucca whipplei for its dramatic flower spikes.
The tall cluster of creamy flowers on the Yucca whipplei attracts many pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a genus of perennial shrubs and trees known for their rosettes of evergreen leaves and tall clusters of white flowers

Etymology

Named after American botanist Amiel Weeks Whipple.
