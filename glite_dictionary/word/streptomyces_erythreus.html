Streptomyces erythreus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptoʊˈmaɪsiːz ɪˌrɪθriˈuːs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈmaɪsiːz ɪˌrɪθriˈuːs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that is the source of the antibiotic erythromycin
bacterium producing erythromycin
medical

Examples

Streptomyces erythreus was first identified as the organism producing erythromycin in the 1950s.
Researchers isolated Streptomyces erythreus from a soil sample to study its antibiotic properties.
The fermentation process using Streptomyces erythreus yields large amounts of erythromycin for medical use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythromycin - an antibiotic produced by Streptomyces erythreus

Etymology

From Greek streptos (twisted) + mykēs (fungus), with the species name from erythros (red).
