testacean

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈsteɪsiən / (AmE), / tɛˈsteɪsiən / (BrE)
any of various single-celled rhizopod organisms of the order Testacea, which are characterized by having a shell.
shell-bearing rhizopod organism

Examples

Some species of testacean thrive in freshwater environments around the world.
Microscopic examination revealed the presence of several testaceans in the soil sample.
The shell of a testacean serves as protection from predators and environmental hazards.

Etymology

From New Latin Testacea, from Latin testaceus (of brick, shell-covered) + -an.
