fateful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtfəl / (BrE)
having very serious, often disastrous, consequences; decisive in its effect, especially leading to ruin or tragedy
having very serious or disastrous consequences

Examples

The country was changed forever by that fateful day in history.
A fateful decision led the company to bankruptcy within a year.
He could not have predicted the fateful consequences of his simple mistake.

Synset Examples

the stock market crashed on Black Friday
a calamitous defeat
the battle was a disastrous end to a disastrous campaign
such doctrines, if true, would be absolutely fatal to my theory
it is fatal to enter any war without the will to win it
a fateful error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrous - causing great damage or suffering
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good luck; bringing good results

Etymology

From fate + -ful, first recorded in the late 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪt.fəl / (BrE)
having an important, often ominous or disastrous, influence on future events; ominously prophetic.
having an ominous or decisive influence

Examples

The declaration marked the beginning of a fateful chapter in world history.
On that fateful night, everything changed for the family.
She hesitated before making the fateful decision to leave her hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good luck; resulting in good things

Etymology

From fate + -ful; first recorded in the early 17th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtfʊl / (BrE)
having an important and often negative effect on the future; determined by fate or destiny.
having a decisive or destined impact

Examples

She looked back on that fateful day when everything changed.
His decision to leave home proved to be fateful for his family.
The treaty was signed at a fateful moment in history.

Synset Examples

a fatal series of events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatal - causing death or disaster

Etymology

From fate +‎ -ful, dating to late 16th century.

4. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪt.fl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪt.fəl / (BrE)
having very significant, usually decisive or sometimes disastrous, consequences
having momentous or decisive consequences

Examples

The leaders remembered the fateful night that changed the course of their nation.
He hesitated before making the fateful decision that would alter his life.
On that fateful day, the stock market crashed and many lost their fortunes.

Synset Examples

that fateful meeting of the U.N. when … it declared war on North Korea
the fatal day of the election finally arrived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentous - of great importance or significance
  • Synonym: destined - certain to happen; unavoidable
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From fate + -ful, meaning 'full of fate, determined by fate'.
