Liberia

1. PROPN

/ laɪˈbɪriə / (AmE), / laɪˈbɪəriə / (BrE)
a country in West Africa, founded in the 19th century by formerly enslaved people from the United States; its capital is Monrovia.
country in West Africa

Examples

She traveled to Liberia to work with a humanitarian organization.
The history of Liberia is closely tied to the migration of African Americans in the 1800s.
Monrovia is the bustling capital city of Liberia.

Etymology

From Latin 'liber' meaning 'free', chosen to symbolize the country's founding by freed slaves.
