Mary

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the mother of Jesus, known as the Virgin Mary, especially venerated in the Roman Catholic Church.
the mother of Jesus in Christianity

Examples

Many artists have depicted Mary in scenes of the Annunciation.
Catholics around the world honor Mary through prayers and feasts.
The church is dedicated to Mary, the mother of Jesus.

Etymology

From Hebrew Miryam, via Greek Maria and Latin Maria.
