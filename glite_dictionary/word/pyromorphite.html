pyromorphite

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈmɔːrfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɪərəˈmɔːfaɪt / (BrE)
a green, yellow, or brownish mineral consisting of lead chloride and phosphate, occurring in the oxidized zone of lead deposits; a minor ore of lead
a mineral consisting of lead chloride and phosphate

Examples

The brilliant green crystals of pyromorphite caught the attention of mineral collectors.
Geologists identified pyromorphite as a minor ore of lead in the old mine.
Under the microscope, pyromorphite displays a hexagonal crystal structure.

Etymology

From German Pyromorphit, from Greek 'pyro' (fire) and 'morphē' (form), due to its crystalline forms resulting when heated.
