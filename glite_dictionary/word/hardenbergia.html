Hardenbergia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrdnˈbɜrdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdn̩ˈbɜːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian woody vines, often with clusters of violet flowers, closely related to Kennedia.
genus of Australian flowering vines

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Hardenbergia bloom in early spring across Australian gardens.
Many gardeners train Hardenbergia vines to climb trellises for decorative effect.
Native bees are attracted to the purple flowers produced by Hardenbergia.

Etymology

Named after Franziska, Countess von Hardenberg, a 19th-century patron of botany.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrdnˈbɜr.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdnˈbɜː.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of Australian woody vines with violet or purple pea-like flowers, belonging to the pea family Fabaceae; closely related to Kennedia.
genus of Australian woody vines

Examples

Hardenbergia is commonly called 'false sarsaparilla' in Australia due to its climbing habit.
Many Australian gardens feature Hardenbergia for its attractive purple blossoms.
The genus Hardenbergia thrives in sunny locations with well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Kennedia - a closely related genus of Australian flowering vines

Etymology

Named after Franziska Countess von Hardenberg (18th c.), a patron of botany.
