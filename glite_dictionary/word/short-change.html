to short-change

1. VERB

/ ʃɔrt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to cheat or treat unfairly by giving less than is deserved, especially by giving less money than is due
cheat by giving less than due

Examples

Tourists are often short-changed at busy markets if they're not paying attention.
He felt short-changed when he received only half of his promised bonus.
If you don't check the bill carefully, you might get short-changed by mistake.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

From the phrase 'short change', originally meaning to give less change (money) than owed; attested since the late 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ʃɔrt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to give someone less money than they are owed, especially when giving change; to cheat someone financially by giving them too little
cheat by not returning enough money

Examples

The cashier accidentally short-changed me by two dollars.
Tourists are sometimes short-changed by dishonest vendors in busy markets.
Make sure you count your change so you don’t get short-changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions

Etymology

From 'short' (insufficient) + 'change' (money returned after a purchase), originally U.S. English (19th century).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
