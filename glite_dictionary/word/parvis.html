parvis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.vɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.vɪs / (BrE)
a court or open space, often with a portico, at the entrance to a church or cathedral
space in front of a church or cathedral

Examples

Tourists gathered in the parvis to take photographs of the stunning cathedral façade.
Musicians performed in the parvis as people entered the church for the evening service.
The architect designed a spacious parvis with benches and flowerbeds leading up to the entrance.

Etymology

From Old French *parvis*, from Latin *paradisus* (enclosure, garden), borrowed via Church usage.
