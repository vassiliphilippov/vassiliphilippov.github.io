falsely

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔlsli / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlsli / (BrE)
In a way that is not true or correct; in an incorrect or deceitful manner.
in an untrue or incorrect way

Examples

He was falsely accused of stealing the money.
The company falsely advertised their product as 100% natural.
The witness falsely claimed to have seen the suspect at the scene.

Synset Examples

to credit Lister with the first formulation of the basic principle of stratigraphy would be to bestow credit falsely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truly - in a truthful or accurate way

Etymology

From Old French *fals* (false), plus the adverbial suffix *-ly*.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɔl.sli / (AmE), / ˈfɔːls.li / (BrE)
in an insincere or untruthful manner; by stating something that is not true or genuine
in a false or insincere manner

Examples

She was accused of falsely claiming the money belonged to her.
His statement falsely suggested that he had nothing to do with the incident.
They were falsely optimistic about the project's chances of success.

Synset Examples

a seduction on my part would land us with the necessity to rise, bathe and dress, chat falsely about this and that, and emerge into the rest of the evening as though nothing had happened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truly - in a truthful or sincere manner

Etymology

From Old English 'fals' (false) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'False' from Latin 'falsus' (deceived, erroneous).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00338017-r
Synset POS: r
