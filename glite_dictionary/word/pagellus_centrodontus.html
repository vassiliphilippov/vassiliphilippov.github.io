Pagellus centrodontus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdʒɛləs ˌsɛntrəˈdɑntəs / (AmE), / pəˈɡɛləs ˌsɛntrəʊˈdɒntəs / (BrE)
A species of edible marine fish found in European coastal waters, commonly known as the common pandora.
edible fish of European coasts

Examples

Local fishermen landed several Pagellus centrodontus at the morning market.
The restaurant specializes in grilled Pagellus centrodontus served with fresh herbs.
Scientists studied the population of Pagellus centrodontus along the Mediterranean coast.

Etymology

From the genus name Pagellus (Greek pagos, 'frost, hill' + -ellus, diminutive) and species name centrodontus (Greek kentron, 'spine' + odous, 'tooth').
