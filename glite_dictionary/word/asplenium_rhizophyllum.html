Asplenium rhizophyllum

1. NOUN

/ æˈspləniəm raɪzoʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / æˈsplɛniəm raɪzəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A species of fern with narrow, lance-shaped fronds that produce roots at the tip; also known as walking fern.
a species of walking fern

Examples

The Asplenium rhizophyllum can spread across rocks by rooting at the tips of its fronds.
I found an Asplenium rhizophyllum while hiking in the Appalachian Mountains.
The unique growth habit of Asplenium rhizophyllum makes it popular among fern enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking fern - a common name for Asplenium rhizophyllum; a fern that roots at the tip

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Asplenium' from Greek 'asplēnon' (a kind of fern) + specific epithet 'rhizophyllum' from Greek 'rhiza' (root) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
