median

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiən / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiən / (BrE)
relating to or constituting the middle value in a set of ordered values, or the average of the two middle values if there is an even number of values.
of the middle value in a set

Examples

The median household income in the city increased last year.
After arranging the scores in order, the median result was clearly higher than the mean.
When the data set has an even number of items, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

Synset Examples

the median value of 17, 20, and 36 is 20
the median income for the year was $15,000

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mean - the average value in a set, often contrasted with median
  • Antonym: mode - the value that appears most frequently in a set, often contrasted with median

Etymology

From Latin medianus (middle, in the middle), from medius (middle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiən / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiən / (BrE)
the value separating the higher half from the lower half of a data set; specifically, the middle number in a sorted list, or the average of the two middle numbers if the list has an even number of values
middle value in a data set

Examples

If the list of numbers is 3, 5, and 7, the median is 5.
When there is an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle ones.
The median is often used instead of the mean to describe typical income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - average value, sum divided by count
  • Synonym: mode - the value that appears most often in a data set

Etymology

From Latin 'medianus', meaning 'of the middle', from 'medius' (middle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06033170-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiən / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiən / (BrE)
situated in, relating to, or extending toward the middle or center of something
in or toward the middle

Examples

The median nerve runs down the center of the arm.
The city built a park along the median strip of the highway.
The incision was made along the median line of the abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - situated at or extending to the side
  • Synonym: central - located at or forming the center

Etymology

From Latin medianus (of the middle), from medius (middle).

4. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiən / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiən / (BrE)
(of an anatomical plane or position) situated in or relating to the imaginary plane dividing the body into right and left halves; lying in the midline.
in the midline; dividing right and left halves (anatomy)

Examples

The median plane divides the body into equal right and left halves.
A median incision is often made along the midline of the abdomen during surgery.
In anatomical diagrams, structures close to the median line are called medial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - relating to the side, away from the midline (anatomy)

Etymology

From Latin 'medianus' (in the middle), from 'medius' (middle).
