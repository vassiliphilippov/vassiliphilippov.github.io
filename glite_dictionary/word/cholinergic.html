cholinergic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊlɪˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌkəʊlɪˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
(of a nerve cell or fiber) releasing or activated by acetylcholine or a related compound.
releasing or activated by acetylcholine
medical

Examples

Certain drugs can enhance cholinergic activity in the brain to improve memory.
The patient was given medication to block cholinergic receptors and reduce muscle spasms.
Scientists are studying how cholinergic pathways influence attention and learning.

Etymology

From acetylcholine + -ergic (producing or activated by).
