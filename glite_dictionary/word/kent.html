Kent

1. PROPN

/ kɛnt / (AmE), / kɛnt / (BrE)
a county in southeastern England on the English Channel, historically the first Anglo-Saxon kingdom colonized by the Romans.
county in southeastern England
british

Examples

She grew up in Kent and still visits her family there every summer.
The rolling hills and orchards of Kent are famous throughout Britain.
Archaeologists in Kent have uncovered ruins from the Roman occupation.

Etymology

From the Celtic *Cantium*, the name for the district used by the Romans.
