to palliate

1. VERB

/ ˈpælieɪt / (AmE), / ˈpalieɪt / (BrE)
to make a disease or its symptoms less severe without removing the cause; to reduce the intensity of pain or discomfort
to ease symptoms or pain

Examples

Doctors may try to palliate the symptoms of the disease with medication.
The new treatment helped palliate her chronic pain, but it did not cure the underlying illness.
Hospice care aims to palliate suffering and improve the quality of life for terminally ill patients.

Synset Examples

This pill will relieve your headaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - to reduce or remove (pain, distress, etc.)
  • Synonym: to alleviate - to make (pain, suffering, or a problem) less severe

Etymology

From Latin 'palliare' (to cloak), from 'pallium' (cloak).

2. VERB

/ ˈpælieɪt / (AmE), / ˈpalɪeɪt / (BrE)
to make the effects of something, especially something bad, seem less serious, often without removing the cause; to alleviate or moderate
lessen the seriousness of something

Examples

Doctors may try to palliate the symptoms of a disease instead of curing it completely.
She attempted to palliate his anger by explaining the circumstances calmly.
Although nothing could change the outcome, his apology helped palliate her feelings of hurt.

Synset Examples

The circumstances extenuate the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mitigate - to make something less severe, serious, or painful
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem, situation, or feeling worse

Etymology

From Late Latin palliatus (cloaked), from palliare 'to cloak', from pallium 'cloak'.
