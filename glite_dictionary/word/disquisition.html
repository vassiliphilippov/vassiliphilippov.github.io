disquisition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskwɪˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a long and detailed discussion or written essay on a particular subject, often exploring it thoroughly and analytically
long detailed discussion or essay

Examples

The philosopher delivered a lengthy disquisition on ethics to his assembled students.
Her disquisition examined the political ramifications of the new policy in great detail.
We listened to his disquisition about the origins of the universe with great interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatise - a formally written and systematic work on a subject
  • Degree Lower: essay - a short piece of writing on a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin 'disquisitio' (investigation), from 'disquirere' (to search diligently).
