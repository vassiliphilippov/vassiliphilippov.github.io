coin

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
a flat, usually round piece of metal that is used as money
a piece of metal money

Examples

He dropped a coin into the vending machine and waited for his drink.
Some rare coins can be worth a lot more than their face value.
The cashier handed me my change in small coins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token - a piece resembling a coin, used in place of money
  • Antonym: banknote - a piece of paper money

Etymology

From Old French 'coing' (wedge, stamp), from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge).

2. VERB

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
to invent or create (a new word, phrase, or expression)
invent or create (new word or phrase)

Examples

He was the first to coin the term 'global village.'
Many modern slang words are coined by young people online.
She coined a clever phrase to describe the new trend.

Synset Examples

coin phrases or words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invent - create or design something that has not existed before
  • Antonym: to adopt - to take up or start to use a word or phrase created by others

Etymology

From Old French *coing* (wedge, stamp for coining), from Latin *cuneus* (wedge).

3. VERB

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
to make (a coin, medal, or similar object) by stamping metal
make coins or medals by stamping

Examples

The government will coin a new series of commemorative medals next year.
This facility coins millions of quarters every month for circulation.
They coined gold pieces to honor the centennial anniversary.

Synset Examples

strike coins
strike a medal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - to make coins, especially officially

Etymology

From Old French *coing* (wedge, stamp), from Latin *cuneus* (wedge).
