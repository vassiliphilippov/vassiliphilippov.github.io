to placate

1. VERB

/ ˈpleɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈplæ.keɪt / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or hostile, often by doing or saying something to please them
make someone less angry or hostile

Examples

The manager tried to placate the upset employees with an apology and a promise of change.
She brought her brother a snack in an attempt to placate him after their argument.
Gifts and kind words did little to placate the angry customer.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mollify - to calm or soothe someone's anger
  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify, especially by giving in to demands
  • Antonym: to infuriate - to make someone extremely angry

Etymology

From Latin placatus, past participle of placare 'to calm, soothe'.
