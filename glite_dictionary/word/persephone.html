Persephone

1. PROPN

/ pərˈsɛfəˌni / (AmE), / pəˈsɛfəni / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Zeus and Demeter, made queen of the Underworld by Hades after being abducted by him; identified with the Roman Proserpina.
queen of the Underworld in Greek mythology

Examples

The myth of Persephone explains the changing seasons in Greek mythology.
Artists throughout history have depicted Persephone as both a symbol of spring and queen of the underworld.
In the story, Persephone is forced to spend part of each year with Hades beneath the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Proserpina - Roman equivalent of Persephone, queen of the underworld
  • Synonym: Demeter - Greek goddess of agriculture, mother of Persephone
  • Synonym: Hades - Greek god of the underworld, abductor and husband of Persephone

Etymology

From ancient Greek Περσεφόνη (Persephónē), a mythological figure.
