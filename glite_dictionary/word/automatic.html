automatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a device or process) operating with minimal human intervention; able to function independently, without the need for manual control.
operating without manual control

Examples

The factory uses automatic machines to increase productivity and reduce errors.
The doors to the building are automatic and open when someone approaches.
Many cars now come with automatic transmissions, making them easier to drive.

Synset Examples

automatic transmission
a budget deficit that caused automatic spending cuts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - operated by hand rather than automatically

Etymology

From Greek 'automatos' meaning 'self-acting' or 'spontaneous'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00182776-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of an action or behavior) done without conscious thought, as if by instinct or reflex.
done without conscious thought

Examples

Her reply was so automatic that she barely realized she'd spoken.
He gave an automatic smile every time someone greeted him.
It's an automatic reaction to say 'bless you' when someone sneezes.

Synset Examples

an automatic ‘thank you’
machinelike efficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instinctive - done without conscious thought, as by instinct
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Greek 'automatos' (self-acting) via Latin and French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503678-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of an action or process) occurring involuntarily, without conscious thought or control; done by instinct or reflex.
involuntary, without conscious control

Examples

The doctor explained that blinking is an automatic response to protect the eyes.
Her reaction was so automatic that she didn't realize she had flinched.
Breathing is usually an automatic function, controlled by the nervous system.

Synset Examples

the automatic shrinking of the pupils of the eye in strong light
a reflex knee jerk
sneezing is reflexive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involuntary - not done by choice or control; occurring without will
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or conscious control

Etymology

From Greek 'automatos' (self-moving), via Latin 'automaticus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02532828-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
A firearm, especially a rifle or pistol, that loads and fires automatically with each pull of the trigger.
self-loading firearm capable of automatic fire

Examples

The suspect was carrying an automatic loaded with live ammunition.
Collectors often distinguish between old revolvers and modern automatics.
The soldiers were each issued an automatic before the mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machine gun - fully automatic gun that continues firing while the trigger is pressed
  • Antonym: semiautomatic - a gun that fires one shot per trigger pull and reloads automatically
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763860-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
A gun, especially a pistol, that continues to fire shots in rapid succession automatically as long as the trigger is pressed or until the ammunition is used up.
self-firing pistol or gun

Examples

The police officer drew his automatic as he entered the building.
Collectors prefer vintage automatics due to their historical significance.
Carrying an automatic without a license is illegal in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolver - a handgun with a revolving chamber
  • Degree Lower: semiautomatic - a gun that fires one shot per trigger pull and automatically reloads

Etymology

From automatic, referring to the weapon's self-acting firing mechanism.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763663-n
Synset POS: n
