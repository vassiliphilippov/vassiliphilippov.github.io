to exacerbate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæsərˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːsəbeɪt / (BrE)
to make a problem, situation, or feeling worse or more severe
make (something) worse or more severe

Examples

The new law could exacerbate the existing housing crisis in the city.
Stress can exacerbate symptoms of certain medical conditions.
Cutting funding to education will only exacerbate the state's problems.

Synset Examples

This drug aggravates the pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aggravate - to make a bad situation worse
  • Antonym: to alleviate - to make a problem or pain less severe

Etymology

From Latin exacerbatus, past participle of exacerbāre (to make worse), from ex- (intensive) + acerbus (harsh, bitter).

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæsɚˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæsəbeɪt / (BrE)
To make a problem, situation, or negative feeling worse or more severe.
make a problem or feeling worse

Examples

Raising taxes now would only exacerbate the country's economic problems.
Her harsh words seemed to exacerbate his anxiety instead of calming him.
Attempts to fix the old machine sometimes exacerbate the issues rather than solve them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aggravate - to make a problem or situation worse
  • Antonym: to alleviate - to make a problem or situation less severe

Etymology

From Latin exacerbatus, past participle of exacerbare, from ex- (thoroughly) + acerbus (harsh, bitter).
