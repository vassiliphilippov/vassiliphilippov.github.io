feces

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːsiz / (AmE), / ˈfiːsiːz / (BrE)
Solid bodily waste discharged from the bowels through the anus.
solid waste excreted from bowels

Examples

The veterinarian collected a sample of feces for testing.
Improper disposal of feces can lead to contamination of water sources.
Doctors may analyze your feces to check for certain infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stool - solid waste matter discharged from the body
  • Synonym: excrement - waste matter discharged from the bowels
  • Antonym: urine - liquid waste excreted by the kidneys

Etymology

From Latin faeces meaning 'sediment, dregs'.
