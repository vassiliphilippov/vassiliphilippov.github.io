Streptocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛp.təˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛp.təˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of African and Asian herbaceous plants, commonly known as Cape primroses, distinguished by their usually stemless growth and attractive flowers
genus of Cape primroses, flowering plants
medical

Examples

Many horticulturists enjoy growing Streptocarpus for their vibrant blossoms indoors.
The Streptocarpus species are native to parts of Africa and Asia.
She displayed a collection of rare Streptocarpus cultivars at the botanical exhibition.

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the twisted fruit capsule.
