streptocarpus

1. NOUN

/ strɛp.toʊˈkɑr.pəs / (AmE), / strɛp.təˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Streptocarpus, typically having a rosette of leaves at the base and primrose-like flowers, often grown as a houseplant.
genus of flowering plants with rosette leaves

Examples

The streptocarpus is prized for its colorful, long-lasting flowers indoors.
She added a vibrant purple streptocarpus to her collection of houseplants.
The botanical garden displays several species of streptocarpus in its conservatory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a spring-flowering plant with pale yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the plant's twisted seedpod.
