undine

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈdin / (AmE), / ʌnˈdiːn / (BrE)
a female water spirit in European folklore, typically depicted as a graceful being inhabiting rivers, streams, or other bodies of water
female water spirit in folklore

Examples

The legend tells of an undine who fell in love with a mortal man.
Artists often depict undines as mysterious figures rising from watery depths.
Stories of undine encounters appear throughout European folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden

Etymology

From Latin 'unda' (wave), via the writings of Paracelsus.
