nolle prosequi

1. VERB

/ ˈnɒli proʊˈsiːkwaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɒli prəˈsiːkwɪ / (BrE)
(of a prosecutor) to formally discontinue or abandon prosecution of a charge by entering a record in court.
to formally drop legal charges
legal

Examples

The district attorney chose to nolle prosequi the case after new evidence came to light.
The judge asked whether the prosecution planned to nolle prosequi any of the charges.
Counsel argued that the state should nolle prosequi given the lack of credible witnesses.

Synset Examples

They nolle prossed the charge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - cease prosecution or legal action

Etymology

From Latin, literally "to be unwilling to pursue".

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑli ˈprɑsɪˌkwaɪ / (AmE), / ˌnɒli ˈprɒsɪˌkwaɪ / (BrE)
a legal order or entry indicating that the plaintiff or prosecutor will not pursue charges or a case any further
legal notice to drop prosecution
legal

Examples

The prosecutor entered a nolle prosequi when new evidence came to light.
After much consideration, the judge allowed a nolle prosequi in the case.
With the filing of a nolle prosequi, all charges against the defendant were dismissed.

Etymology

From Latin 'nolle prosequi' meaning 'to be unwilling to pursue'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɒli proʊˈsiːkwaɪ / (AmE), / ˌnɒlɛ ˈprɒsɪkwʌɪ / (BrE)
a formal notice from a prosecutor stating that charges against the defendant will not be pursued or that the case will be abandoned
formal notice to abandon legal case
legal

Examples

The prosecutor filed a nolle prosequi after determining there was insufficient evidence for conviction.
After months of deliberation, the court received a nolle prosequi and the charges were dropped.
In legal proceedings, granting a nolle prosequi effectively ends the case against the defendant.
