taenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ˈtiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic tapeworms, some species of which infect humans, often using pigs as intermediate hosts
genus of parasitic tapeworms
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a taenia infection after consuming undercooked pork.
Taenia species are responsible for many cases of tapeworm infections in humans worldwide.
Medical researchers study the life cycle of taenia to develop better treatments for parasitic diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapeworm - parasitic flatworm, member of the class Cestoda

Etymology

From Latin taenia (ribbon, band), from Ancient Greek tainia (band, ribbon), referring to the flattened shape of the worm.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ˈtiːnɪə / (BrE)
A narrow band or ribbon, especially one worn around the head as a headband in ancient times.
narrow headband or ribbon

Examples

The statue of Apollo is shown wearing a golden taenia across his forehead.
In ancient Greece, the taenia was often a sign of victory or status.
She tied a silk taenia around her hair in imitation of a classical style.

Etymology

From Latin taenia, from Ancient Greek ταινία (tainia) meaning 'band' or 'ribbon'.
