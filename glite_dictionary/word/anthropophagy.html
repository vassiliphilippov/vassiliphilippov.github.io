anthropophagy

1. NOUN

/ ænˌθrɑːˈpɑːfədʒi / (AmE), / ænˌθrɒˈpɒfədʒi / (BrE)
the practice of eating human flesh; human cannibalism
eating of human flesh; cannibalism
medical

Examples

Some ancient cultures were accused of anthropophagy during times of famine.
The study of anthropophagy sheds light on certain ritual practices in history.
Reports of anthropophagy shocked European explorers in the 16th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannibalism - the practice of eating the flesh of one's own species

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpophagia, from anthrōpos (human) + -phagia (eating).
