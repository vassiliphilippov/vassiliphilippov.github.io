rotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑtər / (AmE), / ˈrɒtə / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as unpleasant, despicable, or contemptible
an unpleasant or contemptible person
british informal

Examples

Only a rotter would betray his friends for money.
I can't believe he lied to us—what a rotter!
She called him a rotter after he broke his promise.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cad - a man who behaves dishonorably, especially toward women
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a person who behaves dishonorably or unscrupulously
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good courteous conduct

Etymology

Derived from 'rot', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'rotten'.
