Modern Hebrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdərn ˈhiːbruː / (AmE), / ˈmɒdn̩ ˈhiːbruː / (BrE)
the standard form of the Hebrew language currently spoken and written in Israel, revived from ancient Hebrew
contemporary form of the Hebrew language

Examples

Many immigrants to Israel study Modern Hebrew to communicate in daily life.
Modern Hebrew uses a simplified grammar compared to Biblical Hebrew.
Linguists study the revival of Modern Hebrew as a spoken language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Hebrew - the Semitic language of the ancient Hebrews

Etymology

Modern Hebrew developed in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, reviving ancient Hebrew (from Hebrew ʿIvrit) as a living, spoken language.
