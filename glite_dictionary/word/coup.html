coup

1. NOUN

/ kuː / (AmE), / kuː / (BrE)
a sudden, illegal, and often violent takeover of a government or political power, typically by a small group
illegal takeover of a government

Examples

The military staged a coup and seized control of the capital overnight.
After the coup, the country's constitution was suspended.
News of the coup shocked international leaders and prompted widespread condemnation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order in favor of a new system
  • Synonym: putsch - a sudden attempt to overthrow a government, typically by a small group

Etymology

From French 'coup' (stroke, blow), as in 'coup d'état' (stroke of state).

2. NOUN

/ kuː / (AmE), / kuː / (BrE)
An impressive and unexpected achievement or act of success.
brilliant achievement or success

Examples

Securing the contract was a major coup for the new company.
Her appointment as CEO was considered a political coup within the industry.
Winning the award was a real coup for the young filmmaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumph - a great victory or achievement
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From French 'coup' (blow, stroke), ultimately from Latin 'colpus'.
