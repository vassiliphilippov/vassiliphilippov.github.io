Acanthocybium

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsaɪbiəm / (AmE), / əˌkanθəˈsɪbɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of fast-swimming predatory fish, commonly known as wahoos, found in tropical and subtropical seas.
genus of predatory fish (wahoos)

Examples

The Acanthocybium is highly prized by sport fishermen for its speed and fight.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Acanthocybium in the Atlantic Ocean.
Chefs often use the flesh of Acanthocybium to prepare gourmet seafood dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wahoo - a fast, predatory fish of tropical seas

Etymology

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsaɪbiəm / (AmE), / əˌkanθəˈsɪbɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of large predatory fish in the mackerel family, Scombridae, including the wahoo.
genus of predatory fish (wahoo)

Examples

The Acanthocybium genus includes species prized by sport fishermen for their speed and power.
Marine biologists observed a rapid decline in the population of Acanthocybium near the reef.
The most well-known member of the Acanthocybium genus is the wahoo, a popular game fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wahoo - a large, fast, predatory fish found in tropical waters

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + kybion (a kind of fish).
