Cotula

1. PROPN

/ koʊˈtuːlə / (AmE), / kəˈtjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, mainly Southern Hemisphere, herbaceous plants in the daisy family, often used as ground covers.
genus of herbaceous plants (daisy family)

Examples

The gardener planted Cotula along the pathway for its attractive, low-growing foliage.
Several varieties of Cotula are popular as ground covers in temperate climates.
In New Zealand, Cotula species are commonly found in alpine and coastal habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Cotula, originally from Greek kotylē (small cup), referring to the shape of the flower head.

/ koʊˈtuːlə / (AmE), / kəˈtjuːlə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants in the daisy family, found in many parts of the world and often used as ground cover
a genus of ground-covering herbs

Examples

The gardeners planted Cotula to create a dense, low-maintenance mat of greenery.
Several species in the Cotula genus are valued for their ability to thrive in poor soils.
Cotula is often chosen as ground cover in landscaping due to its rapid growth and resilience.

Etymology

From New Latin Cotula, from Greek kotylē (small cup), referring to the shape of the flowers.
