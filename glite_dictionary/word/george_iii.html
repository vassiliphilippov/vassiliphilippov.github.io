George III

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ði ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
King of Great Britain and Ireland from 1760 to 1820, known for the loss of the American colonies and the regency due to his illness.
King of Great Britain and Ireland (1760–1820)

Examples

George III reigned during a period of significant change, including the American Revolution.
Many portraits of George III can be found in major British museums.
The mental health struggles of George III led to his son's regency in the early 19th century.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1738–1820) King of Great Britain and Ireland from 1760 to 1820, during whose reign the American colonies were lost and who later suffered from periods of mental illness.
King of Great Britain 1760–1820

Examples

Many history books detail the reign of George III and his role in the American Revolution.
The mental health struggles of George III have been the subject of several plays and films.
During the reign of George III, Britain fought wars with both France and its former American colonies.
