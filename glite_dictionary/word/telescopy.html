telescopy

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛskəpi / (AmE), / təˈlɛskəpi / (BrE)
the art or science of making and using telescopes
art or science of using telescopes

Examples

He devoted his life to the study of telescopy and the mysteries of the universe.
Modern telescopy has revolutionized our understanding of deep space.
Advances in telescopy have made it possible to observe galaxies billions of light years away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - scientific study of celestial objects

Etymology

From 'telescope' + '-y', modelled on terms like 'microscopy'.
