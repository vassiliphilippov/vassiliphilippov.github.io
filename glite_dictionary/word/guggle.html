to guggle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌɡ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌɡ.əl / (BrE)
to make a sound like liquid being poured from a bottle; to gurgle or bubble noisily
make a hollow bubbling or gurgling sound

Examples

Water began to guggle in the bottle as it was tipped upside down.
She listened to the stream guggling gently over the rocks in the quiet woods.
The wine guggled from the carafe into the glasses, making everyone smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurgle - to make a hollow, bubbling sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02183020-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɡʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌɡl̩ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow with a bubbling or gurgling noise, often in an irregular current.
flow with a bubbling or gurgling sound

Examples

Water began to guggle in the old stone fountain after the rainstorm.
She listened as the wine guggled out of the bottle into the decanter.
The brook guggled pleasantly as it wound through the garden.

Synset Examples

babbling brooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurgle - (of liquid) to make a sound like bubbling or splashing

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative of the sound produced by bubbling liquids.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02192368-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌɡl̩ / (BrE)
to drink (especially from a flask or bottle) in a manner that produces a gurgling sound
drink making a gurgling sound

Examples

He guggled the last of the water from his metal flask, the noise echoing in the quiet room.
Thirsty hikers guggle from their bottles after hours on the trail.
She guggling hastily, managed to finish her drink before the next train arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurgle - to make a continuous bubbling sound like that of water flowing through a bottle

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound produced when liquid is poured or drunk quickly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01172846-v
Synset POS: v
