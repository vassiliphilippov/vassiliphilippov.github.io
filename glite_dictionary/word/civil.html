civil

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl / (BrE)
polite in a way that is adequate or minimally courteous but not overly friendly or warm
polite, but not especially friendly

Examples

Although the two colleagues disagreed, they remained civil during the meeting.
He was only civil to his neighbors, never making an effort to be friendly.
She gave a civil reply but did not try to continue the conversation.

Synset Examples

even if he didn't like them he should have been civil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polite - showing good manners or consideration
  • Antonym: rude - not showing respect or consideration

Etymology

From Latin civilis (relating to a citizen or to public life).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl / (BrE)
Relating to ordinary citizens and their activities, as distinguished from those of the military or religion.
relating to citizens, not military

Examples

The mayor met with all civil authorities to coordinate the response to the emergency.
Only civil personnel are allowed in this section of the building, not the military.
The dispute was settled by civil courts, rather than by a military tribunal.

Synset Examples

civil authorities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: military - relating to soldiers or armed forces

Etymology

From Latin civilis (relating to a citizen, civic).
