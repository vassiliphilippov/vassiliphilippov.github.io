laurelwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrəlˌwʊd / (BrE)
A tropical American tree valued for its hard and durable wood; also, the wood of this tree.
tropical American hardwood tree

Examples

The carpenter chose laurelwood for the flooring because of its durability and attractive grain.
In South America, laurelwood is often used for making fine furniture and cabinetry.
The conservationist discussed the importance of protecting laurelwood forests from excessive logging.
