morning

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
A conventional expression of greeting or farewell used to wish someone a good morning.
greeting or farewell used in the morning
informal

Examples

She smiled and said, "morning!" as she walked into the office.
People greeted each other with a cheerful 'morning' at the breakfast table.
He waved and called out 'morning' to his neighbor on his way to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good morning - a polite greeting used in the morning

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the period of time from the first light of day until noon
the early part of the day

Examples

She likes to go jogging every morning before work.
The birds began to sing at the first light of morning.
We watched the sunrise together in the early morning.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afternoon - the part of the day from noon to evening
  • Synonym: dawn - the time at which day begins
  • Antonym: evening - the period of time at the end of the day

Etymology

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
The part of the day between dawn and noon.
period of day from dawn to noon

Examples

She likes to take a walk every morning before breakfast.
We scheduled the meeting for late morning to give everyone time to arrive.
The fresh air in the morning always helps me wake up.

Synset Examples

I spent the morning running errands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afternoon - the period after noon until evening
  • Antonym: evening - the part of the day from the end of the afternoon until night
  • Degree Lower: dawn - the first appearance of light in the sky before sunrise
  • Degree Higher: noon - twelve o'clock in the middle of the day

Etymology

From Old English 'morgen', related to German 'Morgen'.
