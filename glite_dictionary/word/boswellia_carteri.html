Boswellia carteri

1. NOUN

/ bɑsˈwɛliə ˈkɑrtəɹi / (AmE), / bɒsˈwɛlɪə ˈkɑːtəri / (BrE)
a tree of the genus Boswellia that yields an aromatic gum resin (frankincense) burned as incense
tree yielding aromatic frankincense resin

Examples

The resin harvested from Boswellia carteri is highly valued in religious ceremonies.
Essential oils derived from Boswellia carteri are used in aromatherapy for their soothing properties.
In Oman, locals have traditionally collected Boswellia carteri resin for export and medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankincense - an aromatic gum resin obtained from Boswellia trees

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist John Boswell and British botanist Dugald Carteri.
