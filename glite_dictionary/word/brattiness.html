brattiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbræti.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of behaving in an unruly, impolite, or spoiled manner, like a brat.
unruly, impolite behavior (like a brat)

Examples

Her brattiness was apparent when she threw a tantrum in the toy store.
The teacher had little patience for the brattiness some students showed during class.
Even though his brattiness was irritating, his parents found it hard not to laugh.

Synset Examples

Parents have greater endurance for the brattiness of their own children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brattish - behaving like a brat; spoiled and annoying
  • Antonym: politeness - the quality of being polite; respectful and considerate behavior

Etymology

From 'brat' (a badly behaved child) + '-iness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
