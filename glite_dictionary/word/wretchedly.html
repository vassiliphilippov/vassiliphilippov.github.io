wretchedly

1. ADV

/ ˈrɛtʃɪdli / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪdli / (BrE)
In a very unhappy, miserable, or pitiable manner.
in a miserable or unhappy way

Examples

He looked wretchedly at the ground, unable to meet her eyes.
After the storm destroyed her home, she wandered wretchedly through the debris.
Feeling wretchedly alone, he called his friend for comfort.

Synset Examples

‘I can't remember who I am,’ I said, wretchedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserably - in a very unhappy or uncomfortable way

Etymology

From 'wretched' + '-ly', where 'wretched' comes from Old English 'wrecca' (exile, outcast).
