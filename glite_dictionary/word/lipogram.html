lipogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪpəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈlɪpəɡram / (BrE)
a text or literary work deliberately composed so as to omit one or more particular letters of the alphabet
text omitting specific letter(s)

Examples

The author challenged himself to write a lipogram that avoided the letter 'e' entirely.
Gadsby is a famous English novel written as a lipogram omitting the most common vowel.
Composing a lipogram can be a creative exercise for writers and poets.

Etymology

From Greek 'leipein' (to leave) + 'gramma' (letter).
