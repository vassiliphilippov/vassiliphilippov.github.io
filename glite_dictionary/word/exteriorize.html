to exteriorize

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪriəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something external, objective, or visible; to express or represent an idea, feeling, or quality in an outward or observable form
make external or outwardly visible

Examples

The artist sought to exteriorize her emotions through her paintings.
Therapists encourage patients to exteriorize their feelings rather than suppress them.
Language allows us to exteriorize our private thoughts and share them with others.

Synset Examples

language externalizes our thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to internalize - to make something internal or subjective

Etymology

From exterior + -ize, ultimately from Latin exterior (outer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00533687-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪriəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to bring (an organ or tissue) outside the body, especially during surgery
bring outside the body (medicine)
medical

Examples

During the operation, the surgeons had to exteriorize a section of the intestine to repair the damage.
If a wound becomes infected, doctors might need to exteriorize the affected tissue for effective treatment.
The procedure requires great care not to damage the organ while you exteriorize it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to externalize - to make external or bring to the outside

Etymology

From Latin 'exterior' (outer), plus '-ize' (to make or become).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02088351-v
Synset POS: v
