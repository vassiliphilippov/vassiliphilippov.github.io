acrocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.rəˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈæk.rəˌkɑːp / (BrE)
A moss whose main stem ends in the female sex organ (archegonium) and the spore capsule, typically growing upright.
an upright-growing moss with terminal capsule
medical

Examples

The acrocarp species tend to grow in clumps rather than forming carpets.
Unlike a pleurocarp, an acrocarp has its capsules at the tips of its stems.
Botanists observed that the acrocarp produced spore capsules at the end of its main axis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleurocarp - a moss with a creeping stem and lateral capsules

Etymology

From Greek akros (top, highest) + karpos (fruit).
