menagerie

1. NOUN

/ məˈnædʒəri / (AmE), / məˈnædʒəri / (BrE)
a collection of wild animals kept for exhibition, or the place where they are housed and displayed to the public
collection of wild animals for exhibition

Examples

The traveling circus arrived in town with a menagerie of exotic animals.
In the 19th century, wealthy collectors often kept a private menagerie on their estates.
Children were fascinated by the elephant in the menagerie at the city fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoo - an establishment where living animals are kept and exhibited to the public

Etymology

From French ménagerie (management of a household or farm, later collection of animals), from ménage (household).

2. NOUN

/ məˈnædʒəri / (AmE), / mɪˈnædʒəri / (BrE)
A collection of live wild animals kept for exhibition or study; sometimes also the place where such animals are housed.
collection of live animals for display or study

Examples

The circus brought its menagerie of lions, tigers, and monkeys to town.
During the 18th century, it was fashionable for the wealthy to maintain a private menagerie.
The children were fascinated by the rare birds in the royal menagerie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoo - a place where animals are kept for public exhibition

Etymology

From French *ménagerie* (management of a household or farm), later meaning a collection of animals.
