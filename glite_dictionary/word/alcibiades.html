Alcibiades

1. PROPN

/ ˌælsəˈbaɪədiːz / (AmE), / ˌælsɪˈbaɪədiːz / (BrE)
(c. 450–404 BC) An ancient Athenian statesman and general, notable for his shifting alliances and prominent role in the Peloponnesian War.
ancient Athenian general and statesman

Examples

Many historians debate whether Alcibiades's ambition helped or harmed Athens during the Peloponnesian War.
Plato wrote a dialogue featuring Alcibiades as the main character.
The career of Alcibiades is often cited as an example of political intrigue in ancient Greece.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀλκιβιάδης (Alkibiádēs).
