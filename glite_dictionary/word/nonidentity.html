nonidentity

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈaɪdəntəti / (AmE), / nɒnˈaɪdəntɪti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being different or not identical; a distinction or lack of sameness between two or more things.
state of not being identical; distinction

Examples

The two samples show clear nonidentity in both structure and composition.
Legal disputes often hinge on the nonidentity of contracts or documents involved.
Philosophers continue to debate the significance of nonidentity in defining personal continuity.

Synset Examples

No scientific examiner would select only chance variations to establish nonidentity of two specimens when the fundamental points of identification stamp them as originating at a single source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference - a point or way in which things are not the same
  • Antonym: identity - the quality of being the same; sameness

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) + 'identity' (from Latin identitas).
