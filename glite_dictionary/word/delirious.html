delirious

1. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪriəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəs / (BrE)
Experiencing or showing uncontrolled excitement or wild emotion.
in a state of wild excitement

Examples

The delirious fans celebrated their team's victory late into the night.
She was delirious with excitement when she received the good news.
A delirious energy filled the festival crowd as the band took the stage.

Synset Examples

a crowd of delirious baseball fans
something frantic in their gaiety
a mad whirl of pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatic - feeling overwhelming joy or delight
  • Synonym: frenzied - wildly excited or uncontrolled
  • Antonym: calm - not showing strong emotions or excitement

Etymology

From Latin deliriosus (crazy, raving), from delirium (madness).

2. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪriəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəs / (BrE)
in a disturbed state of mind, often with excitement, confusion, or hallucinations, usually as a result of illness or fever
in a state of delirium or wild excitement

Examples

After three days of high fever, she became delirious and started mumbling incoherently.
The delirious patient needed constant supervision for his own safety.
He was so delirious with excitement that he could barely speak.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lucid - clear-minded, rational

Etymology

From Latin deliriosus, from delirio (to be crazy or rave)
