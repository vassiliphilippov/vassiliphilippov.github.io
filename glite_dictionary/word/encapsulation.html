encapsulation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkæpsjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkæpsjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of enclosing something in or as if in a capsule
the act of enclosing in a capsule

Examples

The medication uses encapsulation to release active ingredients slowly over time.
Nanotechnology allows the encapsulation of drugs for targeted delivery to specific cells.
Proper encapsulation of the specimen is essential to prevent contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclosure - the act of enclosing something
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting something free from containment

Etymology

From 'encapsulate' (enclose in a capsule), based on 'capsule' from Latin 'capsula' (small box).

2. NOUN

the act or process of enclosing something in or as if in a capsule; the state of being enclosed
the state of being enclosed

Examples

Biologists study the encapsulation process in certain immune responses.
The encapsulation of delicate parts helps protect them during shipping.
