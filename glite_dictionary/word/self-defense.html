self-defense

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
The act of protecting yourself from physical harm, especially by using force when necessary.
protecting oneself from harm

Examples

She claimed she struck the intruder in self-defense.
Learning self-defense techniques can help you feel more confident walking alone at night.
The jury accepted his argument that he acted in self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-protection - the act of protecting oneself from harm or danger
  • Antonym: attack - act of physically assaulting someone

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'defense', modeled after similar formations in legal and martial contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌself dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
The act of protecting oneself from physical harm or danger, often by using force.
protection of oneself from harm

Examples

She took a karate class to learn self-defense techniques.
Claiming self-defense, he explained why he fought back against his attacker.
The law allows for self-defense under certain circumstances.

Synset Examples

They discussed the proper use of a taser in self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-protection - the act of keeping oneself safe from harm

Etymology

From 'self' + 'defense'; modeled after French 'autodéfense'.
