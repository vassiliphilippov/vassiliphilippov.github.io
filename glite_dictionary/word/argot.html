argot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡɒt / (BrE)
the specialized language or jargon used by a particular group, often to prevent outsiders from understanding
specialized group jargon or slang

Examples

The thieves used a secret argot to communicate without being understood by outsiders.
Medical professionals sometimes use an argot that can confuse patients.
Linguists study the argot of different subcultures to better understand group identity.

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular group or profession
  • Synonym: slang - very informal language or words used by a particular group

Etymology

From French argot (slang of a group), of uncertain origin.
