excursion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskɝːʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜːʃən / (BrE)
a deviation or wandering from the main course or path of a journey; a temporary departure from the planned route or subject
a deviation from a set course or plan

Examples

His brief excursion into personal matters did not distract from the main topic of the meeting.
During the hike, we made an excursion off the marked trail to explore a hidden stream.
Her lecture included an interesting excursion into the history of the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin excursio (an invasion, a running forth), from excurrere (to run out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskɝːʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a short journey or trip made for pleasure, often by a group of people
short pleasure trip

Examples

We went on an excursion to the mountains last weekend.
The school organized a summer excursion to the science museum.
During their vacation, they took several excursions to nearby islands.

Synset Examples

many summer excursions to the shore
it was merely a pleasure trip
after cautious sashays into the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outing - a short journey, usually for pleasure
  • Synonym: trip - a journey, especially for pleasure
  • Degree Higher: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Latin excursio (a running forth), from excurrere (to run out)
