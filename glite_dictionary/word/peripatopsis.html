Peripatopsis

1. NOUN

/ pɛrɪpəˈtɑpsɪs / (AmE), / pɛrɪpəˈtɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of velvet worms in the family Peripatopsidae, found mainly in Asiatic and African tropical regions.
genus of tropical velvet worms

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Peripatopsis in a South African rainforest.
The unique locomotion of Peripatopsis has fascinated biologists for decades.
Members of the genus Peripatopsis are crucial for studying evolutionary links between annelids and arthropods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onychophora - phylum of velvet worms

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'patos' (walked) + '-opsis' (appearance), denoting their mode of locomotion.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəpəˈtɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəpəˈtɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of velvet worms (onychophorans) in the family Peripatopsidae, found mainly in Asiatic and African tropical regions.
genus of velvet worms

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Peripatopsis genus in South Africa.
Unlike insects, members of the Peripatopsis genus have soft, segmented bodies and many legs.
The distribution of Peripatopsis is mostly limited to humid forests in southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onychophora - phylum of velvet worms

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'patos' (path, way).
