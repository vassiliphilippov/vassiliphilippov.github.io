proboscis

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbɑːsɪs / (AmE), / prəˈbɒsɪs / (BrE)
the long flexible trunk or snout of an animal, especially that of an elephant or certain insects
long flexible snout or trunk of an animal

Examples

The elephant used its proboscis to reach the high branches laden with fruit.
Scientists observed the butterfly unfurling its proboscis to sip nectar from the flower.
His comically long nose resembled the proboscis of an anteater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snout - the projecting nose and mouth of an animal

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis', from Greek 'proboskis' (means for feeding, trunk), from 'pro-' (before) + 'boskein' (to feed).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈbɑsɪs / (AmE), / prəˈbɒsɪs / (BrE)
(humorous or figurative) A human nose, especially when it is large or prominent.
large or prominent human nose (humorous)

Examples

His enormous proboscis made him memorable to everyone he met.
With a proboscis like that, it's hard for him to go unnoticed.
The comedian joked about her uncle's impressive proboscis during the family dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nose - the part of the face above the mouth used for breathing and smelling

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis', from Greek 'proboskis' (an elephant's trunk), from 'pro-' (forward) + 'boskein' (to feed).
