cruciality

1. NOUN

/ ˌkruːʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌkruːʃiˈæləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being crucial; critical importance or urgency.
critical importance or urgency

Examples

The cruciality of the decision was evident to everyone in the room.
The report emphasized the cruciality of immediate action to avoid disaster.
Scientists debated the cruciality of this new evidence in understanding the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticality - the state of being critical, especially in importance or risk
  • Antonym: insignificance - the state of lacking importance

Etymology

From 'crucial' (from Latin 'crux', 'crucis') + '-ity'.
