scrambler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈskræmblə / (BrE)
a lightweight motorcycle with rugged tires and suspension, designed for off-road or cross-country riding
off-road motorcycle

Examples

He bought a new scrambler for riding on country trails.
The scrambler handled the rocky hillside with ease.
Vintage scramblers are popular among motorcycle enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt bike - a lightweight motorcycle for use on rough terrain
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04474106-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈskræmbəlɚ / (AmE), / ˈskræmbələ / (BrE)
a device that alters a signal, especially speech, to make it unintelligible during transmission and restores it to its original form at reception
device for scrambling and unscrambling signals

Examples

The military uses a scrambler to secure their radio communications.
He spoke through a scrambler, making his voice impossible to understand without special equipment.
New digital scramblers offer advanced protection against eavesdropping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encryptor - a device or program that encodes information to prevent unauthorized access

Etymology

From 'scramble' (to mix up), with '-er' denoting an agent or device.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04157539-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈskræm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈskræm.blə / (BrE)
a person who moves quickly and unpredictably, especially to avoid obstacles or evade others, often used in sports contexts
person who moves quickly and unpredictably

Examples

The team's quarterback is an excellent scrambler who can avoid being tackled easily.
Her reputation as an agile scrambler on the tennis court intimidated her opponents.
During the game, the young player proved himself to be quite the scrambler, dodging defenders at every turn.

Synset Examples

their quarterback was a good scrambler
scramblers can often unnerve a better tennis player

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner - a person who runs, especially in sports
  • Antonym: stationary - not moving; not intended to be moved

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'scramble' + '-er', denoting someone who performs the action.
