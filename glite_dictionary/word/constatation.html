constatation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.stəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stəˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stating or asserting something as a fact; an assertion or declaration, especially as a premise in an argument
the act of stating or asserting

Examples

The argument begins with the constatation that all humans seek happiness.
His main constatation lacks sufficient evidence to be considered valid by critics.
She challenged the initial constatation that underpinned the entire debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof

Etymology

From French constatation, from constater (to certify, to state).
