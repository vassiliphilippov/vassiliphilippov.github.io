insolent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.səl.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.səl.ənt / (BrE)
showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect for others
rude and disrespectful

Examples

The child gave an insolent reply to the teacher's question.
His insolent tone shocked everyone in the meeting.
She found his insolent behavior towards the staff completely unacceptable.

Synset Examples

a flip answer to serious question
the student was kept in for impudent behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect; impertinent
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or honor towards someone or something

Etymology

From Latin insolent-, insolens (unusual, immoderate, arrogant), from in- (not) + solere (to be accustomed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.səl.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.səl.ənt / (BrE)
showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect for others or for accepted standards
rude and disrespectful

Examples

The student's insolent comment surprised the entire class.
She gave an insolent glare to the manager when reprimanded.
His insolent attitude eventually cost him the job.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or deference

Etymology

From Latin insolentem (unusual, arrogant), from in- (not) + solēre (to be accustomed).
