electromyograph

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmaɪəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈmaɪəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to record the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles
medical device for recording muscle electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor used an electromyograph to help diagnose the patient's nerve disorder.
In research studies, the electromyograph plays a crucial role in measuring muscle response.
Modern electromyographs are highly sensitive and can detect even minor muscle contractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electromyography - the technique or process of recording muscle electrical activity

Etymology

From electro- (electricity), myo- (Greek for 'muscle'), and -graph (writing instrument).
