underpart

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌpɑːt / (BrE)
the lower surface or underside part of an animal's body, especially as distinctively colored or marked
the underside of an animal's body

Examples

The sparrow's pale underpart made it easy to identify among the branches.
Many mammals have lighter underparts to help with camouflage.
The heron's underparts are streaked with gray and white feathers.

Synset Examples

the warbler has a white throat and underparts
a woodland mouse with white underparts

Etymology

Formed from 'under' + 'part', meaning the part underneath.
