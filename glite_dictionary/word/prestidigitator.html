prestidigitator

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛstəˈdɪdʒɪˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˌprɛstɪˈdɪdʒɪteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who performs magic tricks, especially sleight-of-hand, to entertain an audience
person performing magic tricks for entertainment

Examples

The prestidigitator dazzled the audience with his incredible card tricks.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a famous prestidigitator and astounding crowds with her illusions.
Even after years of practice, the young magician admired the skill of the veteran prestidigitator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - a person with magical powers or who performs illusions
  • Synonym: conjurer - a person who performs magic tricks, especially at parties
  • Synonym: illusionist - a person who creates illusions, especially on stage

Etymology

From French 'prestidigitateur', based on Latin 'praestigiator' (juggler, deceiver), from 'prae-' (before) + 'digitus' (finger).
