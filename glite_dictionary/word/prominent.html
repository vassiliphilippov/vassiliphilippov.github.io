prominent

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.nənt / (BrE)
easily noticed or standing out so as to attract attention; important or well-known.
easily noticed; standing out

Examples

The museum is located in a prominent position on the main street.
Her new haircut makes her cheekbones even more prominent.
A prominent feature of the landscape is the tall clock tower.

Synset Examples

an outstanding fact of our time is that nations poisoned by anti semitism proved less fortunate in regard to their own freedom
a new theory is the most prominent feature of the book
salient traits
a spectacular rise in prices
a striking thing about Picadilly Circus is the statue of Eros in the center
a striking resemblance between parent and child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salient - most noticeable or important
  • Synonym: conspicuous - clearly visible; attracting notice
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or noticeable

Etymology

From Latin 'prominere' meaning 'to jut out'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.nənt / (BrE)
important, well-known, or easily noticed; standing out from others due to position or significance
important or easily noticed

Examples

She quickly became a prominent figure in the scientific community.
The statue occupies a prominent position in the city square.
Several prominent citizens attended the mayor's speech.

Synset Examples

a big figure in the movement
big man on campus
he's very large in financial circles
a prominent citizen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notable - worthy of attention or notice; remarkable
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; not easily noticed

Etymology

From Latin 'prōminēre' (to jut out, stand forth).

Synset POS: s
