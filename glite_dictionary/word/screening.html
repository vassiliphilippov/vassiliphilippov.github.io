screening

1. NOUN

/ ˈskriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
an occasion when a movie is shown to an audience
a showing of a movie

Examples

The festival held a special screening of the award-winning documentary.
Tickets for the midnight screening sold out within hours.
They attended a private screening of the director's new film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showing - an act of showing a film or television program

Etymology

From 'screen' (display surface for movies) plus '-ing', forming a noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06901395-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈskriː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
a sheet or layer of metal or plastic mesh used to cover, protect, or filter, especially in windows or doors
mesh material for covering or filtering

Examples

The old porch was covered in rusty screening to keep out insects.
We installed new screening on the windows to improve air flow while keeping debris out.
Aluminum screening is often used for making window screens and doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread

Etymology

Derived from 'screen', originally denoting a partition or barrier, with '-ing' forming the noun of action or material.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04160391-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈskriː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of concealing or protecting something by blocking it from view
concealing by blocking from sight

Examples

The thick foliage provided a natural screening from the neighboring houses.
Military units used smoke for screening their movements on the battlefield.
A row of tall shrubs acted as screening for the private garden.

Synset Examples

the cover concealed their guns from enemy aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen - a barrier or object that blocks or conceals something

Etymology

From 'screen' (to shield or conceal) + -ing suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01051609-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈskriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of testing people or things to identify those with particular characteristics, especially for detecting disease or security risks
testing to identify specific characteristics

Examples

All employees must undergo screening for security clearance before entering the building.
Regular health screening can help detect illnesses at an early stage.
The airport uses strict screening procedures to ensure passenger safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examination - a detailed inspection or investigation
  • Synonym: testing - the act of subjecting something to a test

Etymology

From 'screen' + '-ing', referring to the act of filtering or selecting.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646210-n
Synset POS: n
