G. K. Chesterton

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒiː keɪ ˈtʃɛstərtən / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː keɪ ˈtʃɛstətən / (BrE)
(1874–1936) an English writer and critic, known for his conservative views, Catholic faith, essays, and the Father Brown detective stories.
English writer and creator of Father Brown

Examples

G. K. Chesterton wrote the beloved Father Brown detective stories.
Many critics praise G. K. Chesterton for his thought-provoking essays and sharp wit.
G. K. Chesterton was influential in both religious and literary circles during the early 20th century.
