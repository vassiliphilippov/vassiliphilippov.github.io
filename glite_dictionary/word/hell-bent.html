hell-bent

1. ADJ

/ hɛl bɛnt / (AmE), / hɛl bɛnt / (BrE)
recklessly determined to achieve or do something, regardless of the consequences
recklessly determined

Examples

Despite her injuries, she remained hell-bent on finishing the race.
He's hell-bent on becoming the company's next CEO, no matter what it takes.
The protesters were hell-bent on making their voices heard at the city hall meeting.

Synset Examples

hell-bent on winning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard to danger or consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

Originally an Americanism from the 19th century, combining 'hell' and 'bent' (determined, set).
