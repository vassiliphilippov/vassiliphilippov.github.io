geophysicist

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒi.oʊˈfɪz.ɪ.sɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈfɪz.ɪ.sɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the physical properties and processes of the Earth using methods of physics
scientist studying Earth's physical properties

Examples

The geophysicist presented new data about the Earth's magnetic field at the conference.
After earning her degree, she worked as a geophysicist in the oil industry.
A geophysicist uses seismic waves to explore what lies beneath Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geologist - a scientist who studies the solid Earth and rocks

Etymology

From geo- (Earth) + physicist (expert in physics); based on geophysics.
