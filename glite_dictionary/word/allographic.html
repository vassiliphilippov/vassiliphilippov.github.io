allographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting an allograph, a variant form of a letter or character in a particular language or script.
relating to a variant letter form
medical

Examples

The allographic differences between handwritten and printed texts can be significant.
Linguists often study allographic variations in alphabets across different languages.
Her research focused on the allographic representation of letters in medieval manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allograph - a variant form of a letter or character

Etymology

From 'allograph', itself from Greek 'allo-' (other, different) + 'graph' (writing).
