Doric

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɒrɪk / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Doris and nearby regions, known for distinctive linguistic features.
Ancient Greek dialect from Doris

Examples

Scholars have studied the features of Doric to better understand ancient Greek dialects.
The play was originally written in Doric rather than Attic Greek.
Linguists note that Doric has several unique grammatical forms distinct from other Greek dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ionic - another dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Attic - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Athens

Etymology

From Latin Doricus, from Greek Dōrikós, relating to Doris, a region of Greece.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɔr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Doric order, the simplest and sturdiest of the classical orders of ancient Greek architecture, characterized by plain columns without bases
relating to Doric Greek architecture

Examples

The ancient temple featured impressive Doric columns along its entrance.
Many public buildings in the 19th century were designed in the Doric style.
The architect explained the differences between Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian columns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ionic - relating to the Ionic order of classical architecture, known for its scroll-like capitals
  • Degree Higher: Corinthian - relating to the Corinthian order of classical architecture, the most ornate Greek order

Etymology

From Latin Doricus, from Greek Dōrikos, relating to the Dorian Greeks.
