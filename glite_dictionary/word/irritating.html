irritating

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪrɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a physical stimulus) causing stimulation or excitement in tissue or organs, often provoking a physiological response.
causing physical stimulation or excitation
medical

Examples

The chemical acts as an irritating agent when applied to the skin.
Some plants release irritating substances that cause inflammation on contact.
Exposure to irritating fumes can provoke a strong respiratory response.

Synset Examples

an irritative agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritative - producing irritation; irritating

Etymology

From Latin irritare (to excite, provoke).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪrɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing annoyance, anger, or mild discomfort; making someone feel annoyed or impatient.
causing annoyance or discomfort

Examples

I found the loud buzzing of the fly especially irritating while I was trying to read.
Wearing wool directly on my skin can be irritating during the summer.
She develops an irritating cough whenever she stays in dusty rooms.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing annoyance
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming or comforting effect

Etymology

From Latin 'irritare' (to excite, provoke), via the verb 'irritate'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪr.ɪˌteɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪr.ɪ.teɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing physical discomfort or a sensation of irritation to the body.
causing physical discomfort

Examples

Some types of laundry detergent can be irritating to sensitive skin.
His eyes became red and irritating after swimming in the chlorinated pool.
The wool sweater felt irritating against her neck throughout the day.

Synset Examples

bites of black flies are more than irritating: they can be very painful

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming or relieving effect on discomfort

Etymology

From Latin irritare (to excite, provoke) + English -ing.
