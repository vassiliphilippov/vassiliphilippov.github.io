Avesta

1. PROPN

/ əˈvɛstə / (AmE), / əˈvɛstə / (BrE)
the sacred collection of Zoroastrian texts composed and compiled between the 4th and 6th centuries CE
Zoroastrian sacred texts

Examples

Scholars have studied the Avesta extensively to understand ancient Persian beliefs.
The Avesta is written in an old Iranian language known as Avestan.
Many Zoroastrian rituals today are based on instructions from the Avesta.

Etymology

From Middle Persian 'abestāg', meaning 'text'.
