volute

1. NOUN

/ vəˈluːt / (AmE), / vəˈluːt / (BrE)
a spiral or coil-shaped form or structure, often seen in architecture, engineering, or natural objects
spiral or coil-shaped structure

Examples

The handrail featured a beautifully carved volute at its base.
Engineers studied the volute of the pump to improve water flow.
A shell with a prominent volute was displayed in the museum exhibit.

Synset Examples

a coil of rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - curve that winds around a central point, forming a coil
  • Synonym: helix - object having a three-dimensional spiral shape

Etymology

From Latin 'voluta' (a spiral scroll), from 'volvere' (to roll).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈluːt / (AmE), / vəˈluːt / (BrE)
an ornamental spiral or scroll-shaped design, especially commonly found in the capitals of Ionic columns and in decorative arts
spiral or scroll-shaped ornament

Examples

The Ionic column is easily recognized by its distinctive volute at the capital.
The craftsman carved an elaborate wooden volute to decorate the stairway banister.
She admired the elegant volute design on the vase in the antique shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - curve that winds around a central point and moves progressively farther away

Etymology

From Latin voluta (a spiral scroll), from volvere (to turn).
