arteria vaginalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə vəˈdʒeɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə vəˈdʒeɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
an artery branching from the internal iliac artery that supplies blood to the vagina, the base of the bladder, and the rectum
artery supplying vagina and nearby organs
medical

Examples

The arteria vaginalis is crucial for supplying blood to the lower reproductive tract in females.
During pelvic surgery, care is taken not to damage the arteria vaginalis.
A blockage in the arteria vaginalis may affect blood flow to several pelvic organs.

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) + 'vaginalis' (of the vagina).
