Corsica

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsɪkə / (BrE)
an island and region of France in the Mediterranean Sea, known as the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte
French island and region in the Mediterranean

Examples

He spent his summer vacation exploring the scenic landscapes of Corsica.
Corsica is famous for its rugged mountains and beautiful coastlines.
Napoleon Bonaparte was born on the island of Corsica in 1769.

Etymology

From Latin Corsica, from Ancient Greek Korskē, a pre-Roman name for the island.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsɪkə / (BrE)
a large Mediterranean island that forms a region of France, situated southeast of mainland France and west of Italy.
Mediterranean island, region of France

Examples

He spent his summer vacation hiking across Corsica and exploring its rugged coastline.
The cuisine of Corsica blends French and Italian influences, offering unique flavors.
Many tourists visit Corsica for its beautiful beaches and mountain scenery.

Etymology

From Latin 'Corsica', from Ancient Greek 'Korsika'.
