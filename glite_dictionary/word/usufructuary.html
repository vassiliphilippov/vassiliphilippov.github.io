usufructuary

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːzəˈfrʌktʃuːˌˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌjuːzjuːˈfrʌktʃʊəri / (BrE)
relating to or deriving from a usufruct, especially describing someone who has the right to use and benefit from another's property without damaging it
relating to a usufruct (legal right of use)
legal

Examples

The contract specifies that the usufructuary right must not damage the property.
A usufructuary interest allows use of land without transferring ownership.
These usufructuary rights can be inherited by the children of the holder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usufruct - the legal right to use and enjoy another's property

Etymology

From Latin 'usufructus' (right of using and enjoying), from 'usus' (use) + 'fructus' (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːzəˈfrʌktʃuːˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌjuːzjuːˈfrʌktjʊəri / (BrE)
A person who has the right to use and enjoy the property of another, without owning it, under the right of usufruct.
person with right to use another's property
legal

Examples

As the usufructuary, she could live in the house and collect its profits, but she could not sell it.
The law grants certain rights to a usufructuary, distinguishing them from full ownership.
During his lifetime, he remained the usufructuary of the estate, while his children held the title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lessee - person who leases property; tenant

Etymology

From Latin 'usufructuarius', from 'usus' (use) and 'fructus' (fruit, enjoyment).
