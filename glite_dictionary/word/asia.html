Asia

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃə / (BrE)
The countries and peoples of the Asian continent considered collectively.
the nations of the Asian continent

Examples

Economic growth has been rapid across Asia in the last few decades.
Many languages are spoken throughout Asia.
The cultures of Asia are diverse and rich in tradition.

Etymology

From Latin Asia, from Ancient Greek Ἀσία (Asía), originally referring to Anatolia or lands east of Greece.

2. PROPN

/ ˈeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃə / (BrE)
the largest continent in the world, joined to Europe in the west and home to about sixty percent of the world's population
the world's largest continent

Examples

Many people dream of traveling through Asia to experience its diverse cultures and landscapes.
The ancient Silk Road connected Asia to Europe, facilitating trade and cultural exchange.
China and India are two of the largest countries in Asia by both population and area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Europe - continent joined with Asia to form Eurasia
  • Synonym: Eurasia - combined continental landmass of Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Asia, possibly of Anatolian origin.
