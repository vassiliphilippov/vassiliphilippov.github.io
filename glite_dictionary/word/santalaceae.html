Santalaceae

1. NOUN

/ sænˌtæləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sanˌtæləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical herbs, shrubs, or trees that produce nuts or fruit with a single seed; the sandalwood family.
the sandalwood family of plants

Examples

The Santalaceae family includes the well-known sandalwood tree.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Santalaceae species in tropical forests.
Botanists have identified several medicinal plants within the Santalaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santalum - a genus of plants in the Santalaceae family, including sandalwood

Etymology

From modern Latin Santalaceae, based on Santalum (the sandalwood genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
