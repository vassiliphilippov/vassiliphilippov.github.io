sleet

1. NOUN

/ slit / (AmE), / sliːt / (BrE)
Rain containing some ice, or a mixture of rain and partially melted snow, that falls as a stinging precipitation.
rain mixed with snow or ice

Examples

A cold wind blew as sleet began to strike the windowpanes.
The forecast predicts more sleet and icy conditions throughout the afternoon.
Driving was dangerous because sleet covered the roads and made them slippery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: snow - precipitation in the form of ice crystals
  • Degree Higher: hail - precipitation in the form of balls or irregular lumps of ice

Etymology

From Old English *slēt* (hailstorm, sleet).

2. VERB

/ sliːt / (AmE), / sliːt / (BrE)
(of weather) to fall as a mixture of rain and snow.
fall as rain and snow mixed

Examples

The forecast says it may sleet later this evening, so drive carefully.
It began to sleet just as we were leaving the house for work.
If the temperature stays just above freezing, it will continue to sleet through the night.

Synset Examples

If the temperature rises above freezing, it will probably sleet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain - water falling in drops from clouds
  • Synonym: snow - soft, white, frozen precipitation

Etymology

From Old English *slēat* (a type of rain or hail).
