vulturous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvʌltʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃərəs / (BrE)
living by preying on other animals, especially by catching and consuming living prey; resembling a vulture in predatory habits or appearance.
preying on others; like a vulture

Examples

The vulturous hawk circled above, searching for its next meal.
Some investors were described as vulturous in their aggressive pursuit of failing companies.
Her vulturous gaze made everyone at the table uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - living by preying on others
  • Synonym: rapacious - aggressively greedy or predatory
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly

Etymology

From English 'vulture' + '-ous', modeled after Latin 'vulturinus' (of a vulture).
