to professionalize

1. VERB

/ prəˈfɛʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an activity, organization, or person) professional or give it a professional character or status
make or become professional

Examples

The organization decided to professionalize its management structure to improve efficiency.
Efforts to professionalize coaching have led to stricter certification requirements.
Her goal was to professionalize the volunteer team by providing proper training.

Synset Examples

Philosophy has not always been professionalized and used to be a subject pursued only by amateurs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commercialize - to make something operated principally for financial gain

Etymology

From 'professional' + '-ize', formed in English in the mid-19th century.

2. VERB

/ prəˈfɛʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone professional, especially by introducing standards, training, or by turning an activity into a paid occupation
make or become professional

Examples

Many amateur leagues are working to professionalize their teams to attract better players and sponsors.
The company decided to professionalize its customer service department by hiring trained staff.
She helped professionalize the organization, introducing regular training and formal procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commercialize - to make something available as a commercial product or enterprise

Etymology

From 'professional' + '-ize'; modeled after similar verbs meaning 'to make or become.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00584600-v
Synset POS: v
