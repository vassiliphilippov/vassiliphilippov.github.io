Lactophrys

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / lækˈtɒfrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of boxfishes in the family Ostraciidae, found in warm seas and known for their bony, box-shaped bodies.
genus of boxfishes (Ostraciidae)

Examples

The genus Lactophrys includes several species of boxfishes native to the Atlantic Ocean.
Scientists recently described a new species within the Lactophrys genus found near coral reefs.
Some members of Lactophrys are popular in public aquariums due to their unique appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'lakt-' (milk) and 'ophrys' (brow or eyebrow), referring to features of the fish.
