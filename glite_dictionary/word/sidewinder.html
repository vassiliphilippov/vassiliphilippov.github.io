sidewinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwaɪndə / (BrE)
a type of air-to-air missile that uses an infrared homing device to track and destroy enemy aircraft
an infrared-guided air-to-air missile

Examples

The fighter jet launched a sidewinder to intercept the enemy aircraft.
Old footage showed a sidewinder missile locking onto its heat-emitting target.
Modern air forces still rely on the sidewinder for close-range aerial combat.

Etymology

Named after the sidewinder rattlesnake due to its heat-seeking ability, similar to the snake's infrared sensing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwʌɪndə / (BrE)
a small pale-colored rattlesnake (Crotalus cerastes) found in the deserts of the southwestern United States, known for its distinctive sideways movement.
a rattlesnake of southwestern US deserts

Examples

The sidewinder is known for slithering sideways across the hot desert sands.
I spotted a sidewinder while hiking in Arizona last spring.
The unique movement of the sidewinder helps it stay cool in extreme temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any venomous snake of the genera Crotalus and Sistrurus, noted for its rattle-tipped tail

Etymology

From its distinctive sideways movement when crawling.
