emphatic

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈfætɪk / (AmE), / ɪmˈfætɪk / (BrE)
expressed or done with emphasis; forceful and definite in expression or action
forceful or definite; with emphasis

Examples

Her criticism was particularly emphatic, leaving no room for doubt about her feelings.
The principal gave an emphatic speech about the importance of honesty at school.
He shook his head in an emphatic refusal to participate.

Synset Examples

the document contained a particularly emphatic guarantee of religious liberty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forceful - expressing something in a strong and confident way
  • Antonym: weak - lacking force or emphasis in expression

Etymology

From Greek emphatikos (expressive, significant), from emphainein (to present, display).

Examples

forceful and definite

Examples
