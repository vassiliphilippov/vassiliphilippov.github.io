morphophysiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔːr.foʊˌfɪziˈɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəʊˌfɪziˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the study of anatomical structures in relation to their physiological functions
study of anatomy and function
medical

Examples

She pursued a doctorate in morphophysiology to understand how anatomical structures affect animal movement.
Research in morphophysiology helps explain the connection between the shape of leaves and their photosynthetic efficiency.
The course combined principles of morphophysiology, examining bones and muscles along with their roles in locomotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiology - the study of the functions and processes of living organisms
  • Synonym: anatomy - the study of the structure of living organisms

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form) + physiology (study of functions).
