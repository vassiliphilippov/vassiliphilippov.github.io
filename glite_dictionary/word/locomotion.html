locomotion

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the act or ability of moving from place to place, especially by self-propelled motion
movement from place to place

Examples

The study focused on how animals achieve locomotion across different terrains.
Aquatic creatures have developed unique forms of locomotion to move efficiently through water.
The invention of the wheel revolutionized human locomotion and transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movement - an act of changing physical location or position

Etymology

From Latin 'locomotio', from 'locus' (place) plus 'motio' (motion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
the ability or power to move from one place to another
ability to move from place to place

Examples

Fish have developed a variety of forms of locomotion in water.
Damage to the spinal cord can impair normal locomotion.
The study focuses on the locomotion of insects across different terrains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movement - the act of moving

Etymology

From Latin 'locus' (place) + 'motio' (motion, movement).
