Vinca minor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪŋkə ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈvɪŋkə ˈmɪnə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant with dark green shiny leaves and blue-violet flowers, widely grown as a groundcover; also known as common periwinkle.
a flowering plant used as groundcover

Examples

Vinca minor spreads quickly to create a lush carpet of green in shady gardens.
Many gardeners choose Vinca minor for its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
The blue-violet flowers of Vinca minor add color to early spring landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - another name for Vinca minor, a flowering groundcover plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'vinca', meaning 'to bind', and 'minor', meaning 'smaller'.
