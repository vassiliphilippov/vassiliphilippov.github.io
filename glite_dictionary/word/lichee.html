lichee

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈliː.tʃi / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree native to China, cultivated for its sweet, edible fruit with rough red skin and translucent flesh.
tropical fruit tree with edible fruit

Examples

The orchard was filled with lichee trees bearing clusters of ripe fruit.
I tried a lichee for the first time and loved its sweet, juicy taste.
They served chilled lichee with cream for dessert at the banquet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - common alternative spelling for the same fruit/tree

Etymology

From Cantonese 荔枝 (laih jī), via Chinese.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈliː.tʃi / (BrE)
A small round fruit with a thin, brittle red shell, sweet translucent pulp, and a single seed, native to China; also called lychee.
small Chinese fruit with sweet pulp

Examples

I tasted a fresh lichee for the first time on my trip to China.
He bought a bag of dried lichees from the market.
The dessert was topped with slices of juicy lichee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - alternative spelling of lichee

Etymology

From Chinese (Guangdong) lìzhī.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07782149-n
Synset POS: n
