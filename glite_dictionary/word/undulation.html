undulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a smoothly rising and falling line or form; a wavelike curve or contour
a wavelike curve or contour

Examples

The artist admired the graceful undulation of the mountain ridges in the distance.
The designer incorporated a gentle undulation in the line of the roof for visual interest.
The undulation of the sculpture's surface caught the afternoon light beautifully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a regular rising and falling curve, like a water wave

Etymology

From Latin undulatio(n-), from undula (small wave).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dʒəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌn.djʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(physics) a continuous up-and-down or back-and-forth movement resembling waves.
wave-like motion

Examples

The undulation of the snake's body helped it glide smoothly across the sand.
Engineers studied the undulation of the bridge when exposed to high winds.
You could see a gentle undulation in the surface of the lake as the wind blew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a movement up and down or to and fro in a regular pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'undulatio', from 'unda' meaning 'wave'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dʒəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌn.djʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a smooth, wavelike movement or form; a gentle rising and falling motion or shape resembling waves
wavelike motion or form

Examples

The undulation of the fields made the landscape look almost like an ocean of grass.
She admired the gentle undulation of the dancer's arms as they mimicked the flow of water.
The road followed the undulation of the hills, rising and falling in a relaxing rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a long body of water curling into an arched form and breaking on the shore; a moving ridge or swell
  • Synonym: ripple - a small wave or series of waves on the surface of water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin undulatio (a waving, undulating), from unda (wave).
