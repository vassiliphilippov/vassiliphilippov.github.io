littleneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.nɛk / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl.nɛk / (BrE)
a small, young hard-shell clam, especially a quahog, typically used for eating raw or steamed
a small, young clam (quahog)

Examples

The chef served fresh littleneck clams on a bed of ice as an appetizer.
She bought a dozen littleneck clams for the seafood stew.
Many people prefer littleneck clams because of their tender texture and sweet flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible clam native to eastern North America
  • Degree Higher: cherrystone - a slightly larger hard-shell clam than a littleneck

Etymology

From 'little' + 'neck', referring to its small size among 'neck' clams.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.nɛk / (BrE)
a small young quahog (clam), typically eaten raw and considered a delicacy, especially in New York cuisine
small young quahog clam

Examples

He ordered a dozen littlenecks on the half shell as an appetizer.
Fresh littlenecks are often served with lemon and cocktail sauce.
The chef prepared steamed littlenecks with garlic and white wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - a hard-shelled edible clam native to North America

Etymology

From 'little' + 'neck', possibly referring to the clam's size compared to larger varieties (like cherrystone or chowder clams).
