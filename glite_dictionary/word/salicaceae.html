Salicaceae

1. NOUN

/ sælɪˈkeɪsi.i / (AmE), / salɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants consisting of trees and shrubs, including willows and poplars, characterized by hairy catkins.
family of trees/shrubs: willows, poplars

Examples

Many species in the Salicaceae family are used for timber and environmental restoration projects.
The Salicaceae include both the familiar willow and poplar trees found near water.
Botanists studied the reproductive structures unique to the Salicaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, part of Salicaceae
  • Synonym: poplar - a type of deciduous tree of the genus Populus, in Salicaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin Salicaceae, from Salix (the willow genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsælɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsalɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants consisting of trees or shrubs, including willows (Salix) and poplars (Populus), characterized by hairy catkins
family of willows and poplars (plants)

Examples

The Salicaceae family includes many species commonly found along riverbanks and wetlands.
Poplars and willows both belong to the plant family known as Salicaceae.
Botanists study genetic diversity within the Salicaceae to understand adaptation to different environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - tree or shrub of the genus Salix
  • Synonym: poplar - tree of the genus Populus

Etymology

From Latin *Salix* (willow), the type genus of the family, combined with the botanical suffix -aceae denoting a plant family.
