understudy

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʌdi / (BrE)
to learn another actor's role in order to be able to act as a replacement if necessary
learn a role as a backup actor

Examples

She was asked to understudy the lead actress in the production.
He has understudied several major roles during his career.
When the main actor fell ill, Jane was ready to understudy at a moment's notice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to act as a substitute for an actor's role

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌstʌdi / (BrE)
an actor who learns another's role in order to be able to act as a replacement at short notice
actor prepared to replace another

Examples

When the lead actress fell ill, her understudy stepped in and saved the show.
He worked hard as an understudy before finally getting his own starring role.
Every main character in the production had an assigned understudy in case of emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who substitutes for another, especially a performer

Etymology

From 'under-' (signifying 'subordinate') + 'study' (to learn by practice); originally from the theater in the late 19th century.
