caecal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːkəl / (AmE), / ˈsiːkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling the cecum (the pouch at the beginning of the large intestine).
of or relating to the cecum
medical british

Examples

The caecal region of the intestine can be a site for certain infections.
In some animals, the caecal structure plays a role in digesting plant material.
The doctor noted inflammation in the caecal area during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cecal - pertaining to the cecum, American spelling

Etymology

From Latin 'caecus' (blind), referring to the blind-ended pouch of the intestine.
