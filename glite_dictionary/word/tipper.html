tipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpər / (AmE), / ˈtɪpə / (BrE)
a person who leaves a gratuity or tip, typically for services received
person who leaves a tip

Examples

She has a reputation for being a very generous tipper at restaurants.
The waiter remembered him as a good tipper who always left something extra.
Some people are not great tippers, regardless of the service they receive.

Synset Examples

a generous tipper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patron - a person who gives financial or other support; also can mean a customer

Etymology

From the verb 'tip' (to give a gratuity) + '-er' suffix indicating an agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪpə / (BrE)
a truck with a platform that can be tilted to discharge its load by gravity, commonly used for transporting loose materials such as sand or gravel
truck with tilting platform for unloading
british

Examples

The construction site was busy as a tipper delivered a load of gravel.
A fleet of tippers transported sand to the new housing development.
The driver raised the front of the tipper to unload the soil easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump truck - a truck for carrying loose material with a tilting bed
