victimized

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪzd / (BrE)
Having been treated unfairly or made to suffer as a result of the actions of others; taken advantage of.
treated unfairly; taken advantage of

Examples

Many refugees felt victimized by the unfair treatment they received from the authorities.
She spoke passionately about being victimized at her workplace for years.
Children who are victimized often suffer from low self-esteem and anxiety.

Synset Examples

after going out of his way to help his friend get the job he felt not appreciated but used

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abused - treated with cruelty or violence
  • Synonym: exploited - used unfairly for someone else's advantage
  • Antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From the verb 'victimize', itself from 'victim' + '-ize'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person) treated unfairly or made to suffer as a result of the actions of others; taken advantage of.
treated unfairly; taken advantage of

Examples

Many employees felt victimized by the unfair policies imposed by the new manager.
She spoke out about being victimized at school because of her background.
After the scandal, several innocent people were left feeling victimized by the media.

Synset Examples

after going out of his way to help his friend get the job he felt not appreciated but used

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploited - taken advantage of to benefit someone else
  • Synonym: oppressed - subjected to harsh and unfair treatment
  • Antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or unfair treatment

Etymology

From 'victim' + '-ize', originated in English in the 19th century.
