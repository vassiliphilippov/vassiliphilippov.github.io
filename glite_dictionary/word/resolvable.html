resolvable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɑːlvəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvəbəl / (BrE)
(of a problem or situation) capable of being solved or resolved.
able to be solved

Examples

The conflict between the two neighbors is resolvable if they agree to compromise.
Many technical issues are resolvable with the right expertise.
The committee discussed whether the dispute was truly resolvable or not.

Synset Examples

such problems are perfectly solvable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsolvable - impossible to solve or resolve
  • Synonym: solvable - able to be solved

Etymology

From Latin resolvere (to loosen, solve) + -able.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɑːlvəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being settled, solved, or brought to a conclusion
able to be settled or solved

Examples

The committee hopes the dispute will prove resolvable through dialogue.
Most technical issues are resolvable with enough expertise and patience.
Although the conflict seemed serious, it turned out to be resolvable after honest discussion.

Synset Examples

all disputed points are potentially resolvable
a resolvable quarrel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreconcilable - impossible to resolve or make compatible
  • Synonym: solvable - able to be solved or resolved

Etymology

From Latin 'resolvibilis', from 'resolvere' (to loosen, release).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01993841-s
Synset POS: s
