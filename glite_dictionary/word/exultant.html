exultant

1. ADJ

/ ɪgˈzʌltənt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzʌltənt / (BrE)
filled with or expressing great joy and triumph, especially as a result of a success or victory.
showing great joy and triumph

Examples

The team was exultant after winning the championship against their long-time rivals.
She gave an exultant shout as she crossed the marathon finish line.
An exultant mood swept through the office following the successful project launch.

Synset Examples

rejoicing crowds filled the streets on VJ Day
a triumphal success
a triumphant shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumphant - feeling or expressing jubilation after a victory or achievement
  • Synonym: elated - ecstatically happy and proud
  • Antonym: dejected - sad and depressed, typically due to failure

Etymology

From Latin exsultant-, present participle of exsultare, meaning 'to leap up' or 'rejoice greatly'.
