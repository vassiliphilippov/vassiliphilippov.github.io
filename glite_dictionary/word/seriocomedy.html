seriocomedy

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪriˌoʊˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˌsɪəriəʊˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
A dramatic or literary work that combines both comedic and serious elements.
comedy with serious elements

Examples

The playwright's latest work is a seriocomedy that explores family dynamics with both humor and gravity.
Critics praised the film as a brilliant seriocomedy that balanced laughter and thought-provoking themes.
The director specializes in seriocomedy, blending wit with emotional depth in his productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragicomedy - a drama combining elements of tragedy and comedy

Etymology

From Latin 'serius' (serious) + 'comedy', reflecting a blend of seriousness and humor.
