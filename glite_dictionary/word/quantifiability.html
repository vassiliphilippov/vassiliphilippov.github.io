quantifiability

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːn.tə.faɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkwɒn.tɪ.faɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be measured or expressed as a quantity.
the quality of being measurable

Examples

The quantifiability of results is essential in scientific research to ensure accuracy and repeatability.
Some qualities, like happiness, challenge the quantifiability expected by statisticians.
The quantifiability of risk allows insurers to set premiums more precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measurability - the quality of being able to be measured

Etymology

From quantify + -ability, based on Latin roots 'quantus' (how much) and 'facere' (to make).
