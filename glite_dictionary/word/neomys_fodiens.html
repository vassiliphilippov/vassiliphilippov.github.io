Neomys fodiens

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˌmɪs ˈfoʊdiˌɛnz / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˌmɪs ˈfɒdiˌɛnz / (BrE)
a widely distributed Eurasian water shrew found in wetlands, streams, and rivers across Europe and northern Asia
Eurasian water shrew species

Examples

The Neomys fodiens is easily identified by its fringe of stiff hairs on the edge of its feet.
Scientists monitored the Neomys fodiens population to track wetland ecosystem health.
A Neomys fodiens swims swiftly through the stream in search of insects and small fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water shrew - a semiaquatic shrew that lives near water

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Neomys' (from Greek 'neos' meaning new + 'mys' meaning mouse) and species 'fodiens' (Latin, meaning digging).
