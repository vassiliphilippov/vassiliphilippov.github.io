dominical

1. ADJ

/ dəˈmɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to Sunday as the Lord's Day, or belonging to the Lord (especially in reference to Sunday in Christian tradition)
relating to Sunday or the Lord's Day

Examples

Many Christian communities consider Sunday the dominical day for worship and rest.
The dominical letter was once used to determine Sundays in ancient calendars.
Her research included several references to dominical traditions in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sunday - the day of the week between Saturday and Monday, often observed as a day of worship in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin 'dominicus' (of the Lord), from 'dominus' (lord, master).

2. ADJ

/ dəˈmɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to Jesus Christ or, by extension, to Sunday (the Lord's Day, in Christian tradition)
relating to Jesus Christ or the Lord's Day

Examples

The text features several dominical sayings attributed directly to Jesus.
Early Christians observed the dominical day as a time of worship and rest.
The bishop delivered a sermon on the dominical authority over the Church.

Etymology

From Late Latin dominicalis, from Latin dominicus (of the Lord), from dominus (lord).
