mg

1. NOUN

/ ɛm dʒi / (AmE), / ɛm dʒi / (BrE)
a milligram; one thousandth of a gram, used as a unit of mass
milligram; unit of mass
medical

Examples

The prescription says to take 500 mg of the medicine every morning.
This tablet contains only 50 mg of caffeine.
The vitamin supplement provides 60 mg of vitamin C per serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milligram - one thousandth of a gram (unit of mass)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'milligram', from Latin 'mille' (thousand) + Greek 'gramma' (a small weight).
