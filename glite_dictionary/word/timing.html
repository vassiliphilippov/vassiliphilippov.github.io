timing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
The particular moment or period when something happens, especially in relation to other events or circumstances.
the time when something happens

Examples

Good timing is essential for a successful performance.
The timing of the announcement surprised everyone.
She questioned whether his timing was intentional or just luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan for when things will happen
  • Antonym: delay - an instance of something happening later than expected

Etymology

From time + -ing, denoting the action or result of timing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the control or regulation of when something happens, especially to achieve a particular effect or result
coordination of timing for effect or result

Examples

Good timing is essential in comedy to deliver a punchline effectively.
The engine's performance depends on the precise timing of the spark plugs.
Her timing during the race allowed her to overtake her competitor at just the right moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - coordination of events to operate in unison
  • Synonym: coordination - the organization of elements to enable them to work together effectively
  • Antonym: misalignment - lack of proper alignment or timing

Etymology

From the verb 'time' + -ing, originally referring to musical and mechanical contexts.
