motor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A machine that converts energy, especially electrical or fuel energy, into mechanical motion.
machine converting energy into motion

Examples

The car's motor was making a strange noise after the long drive.
Many household appliances rely on an electric motor to function.
If the motor fails, the machine will stop working completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine - a machine with moving parts that converts power into motion

Etymology

From Latin 'motor', meaning 'mover', from 'movere' (to move).

Synset POS: n

/ ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
relating to nerves or functions that convey impulses from the central nervous system to muscles, causing movement

medical

Examples

Damage to the motor cortex can affect voluntary muscle movement.
Certain diseases may impair motor nerves, leading to muscle weakness.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensory - relating to nerves transmitting impulses toward the CNS from sense organs

Etymology

From Latin 'motor' (mover), from 'movere' (to move), describing something that initiates movement.
