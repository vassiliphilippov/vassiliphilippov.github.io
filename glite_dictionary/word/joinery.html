joinery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪnəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪnəri / (BrE)
The craft or work of a joiner, especially the making of wooden structures and fittings such as doors, stairs, and window frames.
fine woodworking by a joiner

Examples

He specializes in custom joinery for historic homes and buildings.
The house features beautiful joinery around the doors and windows.
Good joinery can greatly enhance the appearance and strength of furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpentry - the activity or skill of making and repairing wooden objects and structures

Etymology

From joiner + -y, denoting the work or craft of a joiner.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪnəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪnəri / (BrE)
the craft or skill of making wooden structures or fittings, such as doors and window frames, by joining pieces of wood together
craft of making wooden joints

Examples

He studied joinery before starting his own woodworking business.
Most of the joinery in the house, including doors and windows, was handcrafted.
The restoration project required traditional joinery skills to match the historic architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpentry - the trade of cutting and joining timber for buildings and other structures

Etymology

From 'joiner' + -y; 'joiner' from Old French joigneor, from joindre ‘to join’.
