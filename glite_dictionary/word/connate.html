connate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒneɪt / (BrE)
(of qualities or attributes) related in nature; having the same origin or nature.
related in nature; similar in origin

Examples

The twins seemed to share connate talents that set them apart from others.
Her connate curiosity led her to explore the world from a young age.
There are connate qualities in all branches of the arts, linking them intrinsically.

Synset Examples

connate qualities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherent - existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute
  • Synonym: congenital - present from birth (often used for qualities or conditions)

Etymology

From Latin connatus, past participle of connasci (to be born with).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒneɪt / (BrE)
(of similar parts or organs) closely joined or united, especially at the base, as in certain botanical or anatomical contexts.
closely joined or united organs (esp. in biology)
medical

Examples

The petals of some flowers are connate, forming a tubular structure.
The leaves are connate at the base, making them look like a single leaf.
In certain plant species, connate sepals provide extra protection for developing buds.

Synset Examples

a connate tomato flower

Etymology

From Latin 'connatus', past participle of 'connasci' (to be born together), from 'con-' (together) + 'nasci' (to be born).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
