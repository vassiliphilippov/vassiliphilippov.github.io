rein

1. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
one of a pair of long narrow straps attached to a horse's bit or headpiece, used to guide or control the animal
strap for guiding a horse

Examples

He gently pulled the rein to slow the horse to a walk.
She let the reins fall loosely while the horse grazed.
The reins snapped as the horse bolted suddenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridle - the headgear used to control a horse, including bit, reins, and straps

Etymology

From Old French *resne*, from Vulgar Latin *retina*, from Latin *retinere* (to hold back).

2. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
to stop or slow a horse or oneself by pulling on the reins or by exercising control
to slow or stop a horse by pulling the reins

Examples

She quickly reined her horse as they approached the busy road.
He reined in his excitement and waited patiently for his turn.
The coach signaled the driver to rein in the horses outside the inn.
