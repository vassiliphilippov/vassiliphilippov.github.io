hootch

1. NOUN

/ huːtʃ / (AmE), / huːtʃ / (BrE)
Illicitly distilled and usually inferior alcoholic liquor; moonshine.
illicitly distilled alcoholic liquor
informal

Examples

During Prohibition, people would gather in basements to drink hootch and dance.
The police seized several bottles of hootch during the raid.
He tried some homemade hootch, but it tasted terrible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally distilled or smuggled alcohol
  • Synonym: illicit liquor - alcoholic drink made or sold illegally

Etymology

Probably from Hoochinoo, the name of an Alaskan people known for brewing liquor.
