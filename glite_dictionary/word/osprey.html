osprey

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.spreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.preɪ / (BrE)
a large fish-eating bird of prey with a white underside and dark upperparts, found worldwide near water
large fish-eating bird of prey

Examples

An osprey soared over the river, scanning the water for fish.
The osprey returns to the same nest year after year to raise its young.
Wildlife photographers waited patiently to capture the moment an osprey dived into the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with broad, rounded wings and a long tail
  • Synonym: eagle - a large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'avis prede' meaning 'bird of prey', influenced later by Old French 'ospreit'.
