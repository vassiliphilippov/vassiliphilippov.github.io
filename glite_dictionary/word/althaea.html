althaea

1. NOUN

/ ælˈθiːə / (AmE), / ælˈθiːə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, typically having smaller flowers than those of the genus Alcea.
genus of mallow plants

Examples

The althaea in the garden bloomed beautifully throughout the summer.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of the althaea plant's roots.
Some species of althaea are commonly used in herbal remedies for coughs and sore throats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a family of flowering plants, Malvaceae
  • Degree Higher: alcea - a genus of plants closely related to althaea, commonly known as hollyhock

Etymology

From Latin *althaea*, from Greek *althaia* (a kind of mallow).
