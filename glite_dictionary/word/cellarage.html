cellarage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.əˌɹɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee or charge for storing goods in a cellar
fee for storing goods in a cellar

Examples

The merchant refused to pay the cellarage for storing his wine barrels over the winter.
Due to limited space, the cellarage at the warehouse has become more expensive.
The bill included not only transport costs but also a significant amount for cellarage.

Etymology

From 'cellar' + '-age', with '-age' denoting a fee or charge related to the use of something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlərɪdʒ / (BrE)
an area or space in a cellar used for storing goods, especially wine or other provisions
storage area in a cellar

Examples

The old manor's extensive cellarage was perfect for aging wine.
They kept the preserves in the cool cellarage beneath the house.
The restaurant boasts a large cellarage for its impressive wine collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellar - an underground room or rooms used for storage

Etymology

From 'cellar' + '-age', denoting place or function.
