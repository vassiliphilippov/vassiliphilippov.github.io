nymphomaniac

1. NOUN

/ nɪm.foʊˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (AmE), / nɪm.fəˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (BrE)
a woman with an abnormally strong or uncontrollable sexual desire
woman with extreme sexual desire

Examples

Some psychologists now avoid labeling anyone a nymphomaniac due to its outdated and judgmental connotations.
The character in the novel was described as a nymphomaniac, driven by insatiable urges.
He unfairly accused her of being a nymphomaniac simply because of her interest in sex.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satyriasis - abnormally strong sexual desire in a man
  • Synonym: hypersexuality - abnormally increased sexual activity or desire in general

Etymology

From Greek 'nymphē' (bride, nymph) + 'mania' (madness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪm.foʊˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (AmE), / ˌnɪm.fəˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (BrE)
(dated, sometimes offensive) A woman with an excessive or uncontrollable sexual desire.
woman with excessive sexual desire
archaic offensive

Examples

She was unfairly labeled a nymphomaniac despite no evidence of excessive sexual behavior.
The novel features a character described as a nymphomaniac struggling with her desires.
Medical science now rejects the outdated term nymphomaniac in favor of more accurate language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satyriasis - excessive sexual desire in men
  • Synonym: hypersexual - having an abnormally high sexual desire

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (bride, young woman) + mania (madness, frenzy).
