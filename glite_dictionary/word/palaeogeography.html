palaeogeography

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊ.dʒiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊdʒɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The study of the physical geography of the Earth in ancient times or during geological epochs.
study of ancient geography
british

Examples

She specializes in palaeogeography, reconstructing the Earth's ancient landscapes for her research.
The course in palaeogeography covered the shifting continents and changing climates of prehistoric times.
Advances in palaeogeography have helped scientists understand the distribution of prehistoric animal fossils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleogeography - the study of ancient geography (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + geography.
