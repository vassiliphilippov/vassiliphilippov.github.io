malpractice

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpræktɪs / (AmE), / mælˈpræktɪs / (BrE)
improper, illegal, or negligent professional behavior that causes harm or injury, especially by a doctor or lawyer
professional wrongdoing causing harm

Examples

The patient filed a lawsuit, alleging that the doctor had committed malpractice during the surgery.
Insurance premiums for malpractice have increased for many healthcare professionals in recent years.
He lost his license after several serious cases of malpractice were proven against him.

Synset Examples

the widow sued her surgeon for malpractice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care, leading to damage

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' (bad) + English 'practice.'

2. NOUN

/ mælˈpræktɪs / (AmE), / mælˈpræktɪs / (BrE)
Improper, illegal, or negligent professional behavior, especially by a person in a position of trust, such as a doctor or lawyer.
improper professional conduct

Examples

The lawyer was disbarred due to repeated malpractice in handling client cases.
He filed a lawsuit against the hospital, citing gross malpractice during surgery.
Journalists uncovered evidence of widespread malpractice in the recent election process.

Synset Examples

he charged them with electoral malpractices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care leading to damage or injury
  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' (bad) + 'practica' (practice).

WordNet ID: oewn-00743428-n
Synset POS: n
