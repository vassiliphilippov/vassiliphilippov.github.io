diary

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəri / (BrE)
A book in which one keeps a daily record of personal experiences, thoughts, or events.
a personal journal or daily record book

Examples

She writes her thoughts in a diary every night before bed.
I found an old diary from my childhood in the attic.
Keeping a diary helped him process his feelings during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a record of experiences, ideas, or reflections kept regularly

Etymology

From Latin 'diarium' (daily allowance, journal), from 'dies' (day).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəri / (BrE)
A book in which one writes each day about personal experiences, thoughts, and observations.
book for daily personal records

Examples

She wrote in her diary every night before going to bed.
He found his grandfather's old diary in the attic, filled with stories from the war.
Keeping a diary can help you reflect on your thoughts and feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a record of events or personal experiences, often less private or more formal than a diary

Etymology

From Latin 'diarium' (daily allowance), from 'dies' (day).
