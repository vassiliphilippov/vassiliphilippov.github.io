sombreness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmbrənəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbrənɪs / (BrE)
a state of partial or total darkness; the quality of being dim or gloomy
a state of partial or total darkness

Examples

The sombreness of the room made it difficult to see anything without a lamp.
At dusk, a feeling of sombreness settled over the quiet street.
The old castle was filled with sombreness, making it seem mysterious and unwelcoming.

Synset Examples

he struck a match to dispel the gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloom - a state of near darkness or deep shadow
  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being full of light or shining strongly

Etymology

From sombre + -ness; 'sombre' from French 'sombre' (dark, gloomy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːm.bɚ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒm.brə.nəs / (BrE)
a state or quality of being serious, solemn, or sad; a feeling of deep sadness or melancholy
seriousness or melancholy mood

Examples

There was a palpable sombreness in the room after they heard the bad news.
The sombreness of the memorial service was felt by everyone who attended.
Her writing is noted for its reflective tone and underlying sombreness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemnity - the state or quality of being serious and dignified
  • Synonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness; a feeling of sadness or depression
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality or state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From somber, influenced by French sombre (dark, gloomy), with noun suffix -ness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmbrənɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbrənɪs / (BrE)
a quality or manner characterized by seriousness and solemnity, often with a subdued or grave tone
serious and solemn manner

Examples

Her sombreness at the ceremony showed how much the event meant to her.
A sense of sombreness filled the room after the disappointing announcement.
Despite the joyful occasion, his speech was marked by an unexpected sombreness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious
  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness and importance of manner
  • Antonym: lightheartedness - the quality of being cheerful and carefree

Etymology

From sombre (from French *sombre*, meaning dark or gloomy) + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality.
