prelature

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛlətʃər / (AmE), / ˈprɛlətjʊə / (BrE)
the office, rank, or jurisdiction of a prelate, especially in the Roman Catholic Church
office or jurisdiction of a prelate

Examples

The archbishop was proud of the achievements during his tenure in the prelature.
That region became a personal prelature under the jurisdiction of the Holy See.
He wrote a book about the organization and history of the prelature in Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelate - a high-ranking member of the clergy

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praelatura' (office of a prelate), from Latin 'praelatus' (prelate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛlətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɛlətjʊə / (BrE)
the body or office of prelates collectively; the group of prelates as a whole.
the group of prelates collectively

Examples

The prelature gathered in Rome to discuss matters of doctrine and discipline.
Changes within the prelature have led to new initiatives in community outreach.
He devoted his career to studying the history of the Catholic prelature in the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelate - a high-ranking member of the clergy, especially a bishop or abbot

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praelatura, from Latin praelatus (prelate).
