Aerides

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˈɪəriːdiːz / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian orchids characterized by fragrant, showy flowers and often used in horticulture; any orchid belonging to this genus
genus of tropical Asian orchids

Examples

The Aerides orchid is prized for its clusters of pink and white flowers.
Many collectors admire Aerides for its sweet fragrance and elegant blossoms.
Botanical gardens often feature several species of Aerides in their tropical displays.

Etymology

From Greek *aēr* (air) and *-ides* (descendant of), referring to the aerial roots typical of these orchids.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˈɪəriːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic orchids native to tropical Asia, known for their stiff leaves and fragrant white flowers in arching clusters.
genus of Asian epiphytic orchids

Examples

The Aerides orchid produces beautiful, highly fragrant blooms every spring.
Collectors prize Aerides species for their arching sprays of long-lasting flowers.
Many Aerides varieties require warm, humid conditions to thrive indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the orchid family, known for beautiful flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) and the suffix '-ides', referring to their air-growing nature.
