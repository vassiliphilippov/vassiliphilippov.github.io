eglantine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡlənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛɡləntiːn / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild rose (Rosa rubiginosa) with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, bright pink flowers, and scarlet hips.
a type of wild rose bush

Examples

A tangle of eglantine bushes climbed over the old garden wall.
In summer, the eglantine fills the air with the scent of its leaves and blooms.
Bright scarlet hips appear on the eglantine after the flowers have faded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetbrier - another name for eglantine, a fragrant wild rose

Etymology

From Old French eglantine, from Latin aculentus (prickly).
