Oto

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
the dialect of the Chiwere language spoken by the Otoe people, a Native American group of the Midwestern United States.
a dialect of the Chiwere language

Examples

Few people today still speak the Oto dialect fluently.
The Oto language is an important part of the tribe's heritage.
Researchers have been documenting the vocabulary of Oto to preserve it for future generations.

Etymology

From the name of the Otoe people, themselves a Native American group.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people of the Siouan language family traditionally inhabiting the valleys of the Platte and Missouri rivers in Nebraska.
member of the Oto people of Nebraska

Examples

An Oto leader participated in the treaty negotiations with the United States government.
She is proud to trace her ancestry back to the Oto tribe.
The museum features artifacts from the Oto and neighboring tribes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Otoe - alternate spelling; member of the same people

Etymology

From the Otoe self-designation 'Jiwére', but adopted into English as 'Oto' from Spanish 'Oto' or French 'Oto', used in early exploration.
