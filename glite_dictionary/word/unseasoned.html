unseasoned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsizənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
(of food) not having salt, spices, or other flavoring added.
(of food) without salt or spices

Examples

The soup tasted bland because it was unseasoned.
He served unseasoned vegetables to highlight their natural flavor.
The chicken was left unseasoned, which disappointed many diners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seasoned - (of food) having salt, spices, or other flavorings added

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'seasoned'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsizənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
lacking experience; not tried or tested in a particular activity or field
inexperienced, untried

Examples

The army was made up of unseasoned recruits who had never seen combat before.
As an unseasoned manager, she struggled with her new responsibilities at first.
The firm hired many unseasoned graduates and trained them in-house.

Synset Examples

unseasoned artillery volunteers
still untested in battle
an illustrator untried in mural painting
a young hand at plowing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexperienced - having little or no experience
  • Antonym: veteran - person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

un- (not) + seasoned (from 'to season', in the sense of training or experience)

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsizənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
not aged or processed to develop desired qualities, especially referring to timber or other materials
not aged or processed (timber, etc.)

Examples

The builder refused to use unseasoned timber for the framing due to its tendency to warp.
Freshly cut logs are usually unseasoned and need time to dry before use.
Furniture made from unseasoned wood is more likely to crack and shrink.

Synset Examples

unseasoned timber

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seasoned - aged or processed to develop desired qualities, especially in wood or other materials
