rockiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒk.i.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being covered with rocks or stones; rough or uneven texture due to rocks
state of being rocky (covered with rocks)

Examples

The rockiness of the shoreline made it difficult for boats to land safely.
Farmers struggled to cultivate crops due to the rockiness of the soil.
Despite the rockiness of the trail, the hikers managed to reach the summit.

Synset Examples

due to the rockiness of the land it was quickly dry even after a heavy rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the state of having a surface that is not smooth

Etymology

From 'rocky' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the quality of being rocky.
