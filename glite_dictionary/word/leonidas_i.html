Leonidas I

1. PROPN

/ liˈɑnɪˌdəs wʌn / (AmE), / liˈɒnɪdəs wʌn / (BrE)
the king of Sparta (died 480 BC), renowned for leading the Greek forces at the Battle of Thermopylae against the Persians
king of Sparta and hero at Thermopylae

Examples

Leonidas I is remembered for his leadership and bravery at the Battle of Thermopylae.
The story of Leonidas I has inspired countless works of literature and film.
Many historians consider Leonidas I a symbol of courage and sacrifice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thermopylae - site of the famous battle led by Leonidas I

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λεωνίδας (Leōnídas), meaning 'son of a lion'.
