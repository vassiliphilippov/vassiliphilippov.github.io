Cathartidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑrtɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large New World vultures, including condors, turkey vultures, and king vultures.
family of New World vultures

Examples

The Cathartidae include some of the largest flying birds in the Americas, such as the Andean condor.
Biologists study the digestive adaptations found in Cathartidae to understand scavenging diets.
Unlike Old World vultures, members of Cathartidae are thought to use their sense of smell to locate food.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek kathartēs (purifier), referring to the scavenging habits of the birds.
