history

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstɚi / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
the total sum of past events, especially those relevant to a particular subject, place, or institution
the sum of past events

Examples

The museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the city.
She studied the history of art at university.
Every country has its own unique history.

Synset Examples

a critical time in the school's history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronicle - a record of events in chronological order
  • Antonym: future - the time yet to come

Etymology

From Greek 'historia' (inquiry, knowledge acquired by investigation) via Latin 'historia'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
The whole series of past events and developments, especially those that are recorded and preserved as a body of knowledge.
recorded past events and knowledge

Examples

The invention of writing marked a new era in history.
She loved reading about ancient history and the rise of civilizations.
This building has a long and fascinating history dating back to the 18th century.
