to factorise

1. VERB

/ ˈfæk.təˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
to resolve a mathematical expression, especially a polynomial, into a product of smaller expressions or factors
express as a product of factors (math)
british

Examples

It is often necessary to factorise a quadratic equation to find its roots.
The teacher explained how to factorise the expression x² - 9 into (x - 3)(x + 3).
Students were asked to factorise the polynomial completely as part of the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to factorize - to resolve a polynomial into factors (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'factor' + '-ise', following the pattern of mathematical verbs.
