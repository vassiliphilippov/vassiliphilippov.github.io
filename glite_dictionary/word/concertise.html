to concertise

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɝː.tə.saɪz / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.tɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to perform or take part in a series of concerts, especially as a professional musician or singer
give concerts professionally

Examples

She hopes to concertise across Asia next year to broaden her audience.
Many famous pianists concertise internationally, performing in multiple countries each season.
After years of teaching, he returned to the stage to concertise with renowned orchestras.

Synset Examples

My niece is off concertizing in Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perform - to present a form of entertainment to an audience
  • Synonym: tour - to travel from place to place giving performances

Etymology

Formed from 'concert' + '-ise', modeled after similar English verbs.
