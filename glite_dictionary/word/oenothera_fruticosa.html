Oenothera fruticosa

1. NOUN

/ iːˈnɒθərə fruːˈtɪkoʊsə / (AmE), / iːˈnɒθərə fruːˈtɪkəʊsə / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of the evening primrose family, with yellow flowers that bloom during the day.
yellow-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The Oenothera fruticosa brightened the garden with its vivid yellow blossoms.
Many pollinators are attracted to the Oenothera fruticosa during the day.
Gardeners often plant Oenothera fruticosa for its cheerful summer flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening primrose - general name for plants of the genus Oenothera

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name from Greek oinos (wine) and thēr (to hunt), species from Latin fruticosus (shrubby).
