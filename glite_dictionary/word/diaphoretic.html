diaphoretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.ə.fəˈrɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪ.ə.fəˈrɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) causing or promoting perspiration.
promoting sweating (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a diaphoretic to help manage the patient's fever.
Ginger is often used in herbal medicine as a natural diaphoretic.
After taking the diaphoretic tea, she began to sweat profusely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sudorific - causing or inducing sweating

Etymology

From Greek diaphoretikos, from diaphorein (to carry through, sweat out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əfəˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əfəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that promotes or induces sweating.
medicine inducing sweating
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a diaphoretic to help reduce the patient's fever.
Many herbal remedies act as mild diaphoretics during a cold or flu.
The use of a diaphoretic was common in traditional medicine to treat chills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sudorific - a substance that induces sweating

Etymology

From Greek *diaphoretikos* (causing perspiration), from *diaphorein* (to carry through, to sweat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03193663-n
Synset POS: n
