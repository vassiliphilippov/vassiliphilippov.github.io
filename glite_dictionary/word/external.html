external

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɜr.nəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜː.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving other countries; foreign rather than domestic.
relating to other countries

Examples

The government discussed its external economic policy at the summit.
Many countries rely on external investment to support their growth.
Diplomats handle both domestic and external affairs.

Synset Examples

external commerce
international trade
developing nations need outside help

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - relating to one's own country; not foreign
  • Synonym: foreign - from or relating to a country that is not one's own

Etymology

From Latin externus (outside, outward), from exter (on the outside).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl / (BrE)
located, happening, or coming from outside a particular place, system, or body; outer.
located or originating outside

Examples

All external doors should be kept locked after hours for security.
The company faced significant external competition from overseas brands.
Doctors examined him for external injuries after the accident.

Synset Examples

the external auditory canal
external pressures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internal - located or existing inside something; opposite of 'external'

Etymology

From Latin externus (outside, outward), from exter (outer).

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜː.nəl / (BrE)
concerned with or relating to outward appearance rather than inner nature; superficial or on the surface only.
outward or superficial; on the surface only

Examples

His external calm hid the turmoil he felt inside.
She has an external concern for how others see her.
The changes were only external and did not affect the company's culture.

Synset Examples

external composure
an external concern for reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or thorough

Etymology

From Latin externus ('outside, outward'), from exter ('on the outside') + -nus.

5. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɝːnəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl / (BrE)
an outward feature or appearance, especially as opposed to what is internal or essential
outward feature or appearance

Examples

The ceremony focused on the externals rather than the deeper meaning.
While the externals of the building looked impressive, the inside needed repairs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appearance - the way that someone or something looks
  • Antonym: interior - the inner or internal part; what is inside
