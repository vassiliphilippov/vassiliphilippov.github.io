thistledown

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪs(ə)lˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈθɪs(ə)ldaʊn / (BrE)
the light, silky, feathery material attached to the seeds of a thistle, which helps the seeds disperse in the wind
silky fluff from thistle seeds

Examples

A gentle breeze carried the thistledown across the meadow.
Children watched the thistledown float softly in the summer air.
The sunlight made the thistledown glow as it drifted by.

Etymology

From 'thistle' + 'down' (in reference to the soft, fluffy appearance).
