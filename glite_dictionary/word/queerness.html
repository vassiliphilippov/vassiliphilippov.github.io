queerness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strange or unusual in attitude or habit
strangeness; unconventionality

Examples

His queerness of manner set him apart from the rest of the group.
There was a certain queerness in her habits that intrigued her neighbors.
People often commented on the queerness of his taste in art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangeness - the quality of being strange or odd
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or usual

Etymology

From 'queer' (strange or odd) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being homosexual or, more broadly, of not conforming to traditional heterosexual norms
non-heteronormative sexual orientation or identity

Examples

Her sense of queerness became an important part of her identity in college.
Many people celebrate queerness as a form of resistance to heteronormativity.
He wrote an essay exploring the role of queerness in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homosexuality - sexual attraction to people of the same sex
  • Antonym: heterosexuality - sexual attraction to people of the opposite sex

Etymology

Derived from 'queer,' originally meaning 'strange' or 'eccentric,' later reclaimed by LGBTQ+ communities.
