specializer

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who concentrates on and is highly skilled in a particular occupation or branch of learning
person highly skilled in a specific field

Examples

As a specializer in marine biology, she spent years researching coral reefs.
Companies often hire a specializer to handle particularly complex projects.
Unlike a generalist, a specializer focuses deeply on one area of expertise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialist - a person highly skilled in a specific field
  • Antonym: generalist - a person with knowledge in many fields

Etymology

From 'specialize' (verb) + '-er' (agent suffix).
