dolomitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdoʊ.ləˈmɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɒl.əˈmɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
Containing or relating to dolomite, a mineral or rock composed mainly of calcium magnesium carbonate.
relating to or containing dolomite

Examples

The region is known for its dolomitic limestone cliffs and unique geological formations.
We collected samples of dolomitic rock for further mineral analysis in the lab.
This soil is considered dolomitic, as it contains a high percentage of magnesium carbonate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcareous - containing or resembling calcium carbonate; chalky or limestone-like
  • Antonym: siliceous - containing or resembling silica

Etymology

From dolomite, named after French geologist Déodat Gratet de Dolomieu, with suffix -ic.
