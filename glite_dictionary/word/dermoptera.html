Dermoptera

1. NOUN

/ dɜrˈmɑptərə / (AmE), / dɜːˈmɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of mammals known as flying lemurs, characterized by the presence of a membrane that enables gliding flight
order of gliding mammals (flying lemurs)

Examples

The order Dermoptera includes only two extant species of flying lemurs.
Researchers observed the gliding behavior unique to mammals in the Dermoptera.
Unlike true lemurs, members of the Dermoptera are not primates.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'pteron' (wing).

2. NOUN

/ dɜrˈmɒptərə / (AmE), / dɜːˈmɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of mammals comprising the flying lemurs, gliding tree-dwelling animals native to Southeast Asia
order of gliding mammals (flying lemurs)

Examples

The Dermoptera have a distinctive membrane of skin that enables them to glide long distances between trees.
Researchers studying Dermoptera have gained insights into mammalian evolution and flight adaptations.
Only the colugos are members of the order Dermoptera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying lemur - a gliding mammal of Southeast Asia, member of order Dermoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'pteron' (wing).
