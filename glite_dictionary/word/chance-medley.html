chance-medley

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæns ˌmɛdli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːns ˌmɛdli / (BrE)
An unpremeditated killing of a person in self-defense, especially as distinguished from murder or manslaughter in old legal usage.
unpremeditated self-defense killing (archaic legal)
archaic legal

Examples

The defendant claimed that the killing was chance-medley, not a deliberate act.
In English common law, chance-medley was used to describe deaths resulting from sudden quarrels.
Legal historians often reference cases of chance-medley to explain the evolution of manslaughter laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manslaughter - the crime of killing a human being without malice aforethought
  • Synonym: homicide - the killing of one person by another

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *chance-medlee* (mixed chance), referring to accidental or chance conflict.
