Adam Smith

1. PROPN

/ ˈædəm smɪθ / (AmE), / ˈædəm smɪθ / (BrE)
(1723–1790) Scottish economist and philosopher known for advocating private enterprise and free trade, and author of 'The Wealth of Nations'.
Scottish economist and philosopher (1723–1790)

Examples

Many regard Adam Smith as the father of modern economics.
The theories of Adam Smith continue to influence economic policy today.
In his book, Adam Smith emphasized the benefits of free markets.
