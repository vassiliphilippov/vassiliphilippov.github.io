Cercospora kopkei

1. NOUN

/ sɜrˈkɑːspərə ˈkɒpkaɪ / (AmE), / sɜːˈkɒspərə ˈkɒpkaɪ / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes yellow spot disease in sugarcane, particularly in Australia
fungus causing yellow spot in sugarcane

Examples

Cercospora kopkei is known to cause yellow spot disease in Australian sugarcane crops.
Researchers are studying Cercospora kopkei to develop resistant sugarcane varieties.
The spread of Cercospora kopkei can lead to significant losses in sugarcane production.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'kerkos' (tail) + 'spora' (spore); species epithet as scientific naming convention.
