truckage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the transport or conveyance of goods by truck
transportation of goods by truck

Examples

The cost of truckage was included in the final invoice for the shipment.
Rising fuel prices have led to an increase in truckage charges nationwide.
Many rural areas depend on truckage for the delivery of essential supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haulage - the commercial transport of goods, especially by road or rail

Etymology

From 'truck' + '-age', denoting an action or service related to trucking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for transporting goods by truck
fee for transportation by truck

Examples

The truckage for shipping the furniture across several states was higher than expected.
Businesses often negotiate lower truckage rates for larger or regular shipments.
Rising fuel prices have led to increased truckage costs for many logistics companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - the goods or the fee for transporting goods by truck, train, ship, or aircraft

Etymology

From 'truck' (vehicle for carrying goods) + '-age' (denoting a fee or charge).
