monocot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əʊˌkɒt / (BrE)
a flowering plant whose seed has a single embryonic leaf (cotyledon), such as grasses, lilies, or orchids
plant with one seed leaf (cotyledon)

Examples

Grass is a classic example of a monocot because it has a single seed leaf.
Many agricultural crops, such as rice and wheat, belong to the monocot group.
Unlike dicots, monocot stems grow by deposits on the inside, not the outside.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicot - flowering plant whose seed has two embryonic leaves (cotyledons)

Etymology

Formed as a shortening of 'monocotyledon', from 'mono-' meaning 'one' and 'cotyledon'.
