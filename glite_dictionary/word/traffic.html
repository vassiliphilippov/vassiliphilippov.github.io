traffic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the buying and selling of goods, especially illegal or illicit trade
illicit trade or commerce

Examples

The government is trying to combat the illegal traffic in endangered species.
She was arrested for her role in the traffic of counterfeit goods across the border.
International treaties exist to stop the traffic in narcotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - the business of buying and selling goods and services

Etymology

From Old French *trafic* (trade), from Italian *traffico* (trade, commerce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01115977-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
to buy and sell goods, especially illegally or in something prohibited
trade, often illegal

Examples

The group used small boats to traffic illegal goods across the border.
Some criminal organizations traffic in endangered animal species.
He was arrested for attempting to traffic narcotics into the country.

Synset Examples

They trafficked with us for gold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - to buy and sell goods and services

Etymology

From Old French 'trafic', from Italian 'traffico', from 'trafficare' (to trade, traffic).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the movement of vehicles or people along roads, streets, or other routes, especially the amount or flow at a particular time or place
the movement of vehicles or people

Examples

Heavy traffic delayed my arrival at the airport this morning.
The city has installed cameras to monitor traffic and improve safety.
During rush hour, traffic moves very slowly through the downtown area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congestion - traffic jam or overcrowding, especially of vehicles
  • Synonym: flow - steady movement of traffic or other things
  • Synonym: jam - a situation in which traffic cannot move forward due to overcrowding

Etymology

From Old Italian *trafico*, from Latin *trans-* (across) + *facere* (to do, make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08442260-n
Synset POS: n

Examples
