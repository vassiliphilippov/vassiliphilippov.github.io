Zonotrichia leucophrys

1. NOUN

/ zoʊ.nəˈtrɪki.ə luːˈkɒfrɪs / (AmE), / zɒ.nəˈtrɪki.ə luːˈkɒfrɪs / (BrE)
a North American sparrow species with a distinctive black-and-white striped crown, commonly known as the White-crowned Sparrow
species of sparrow with striped crown

Examples

The Zonotrichia leucophrys is easily recognized by the bold stripes on its head.
Birdwatchers often seek out the Zonotrichia leucophrys during migration season.
A Zonotrichia leucophrys was spotted at the edge of the forest yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-crowned sparrow - common name for Zonotrichia leucophrys, a North American sparrow with a striped head

Etymology

From Greek 'zōnē' (belt), 'thrix' (hair), and 'leuc-' (white) + 'ophrys' (eyebrow), referring to its markings.
