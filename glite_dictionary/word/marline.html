marline

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːlɪn / (BrE)
a small, usually tarred, two-stranded rope used especially in marine and rigging work to bind and secure larger cables or ropes
small tarred rope used in marine rigging

Examples

The sailor used marline to secure the ends of the rope against fraying.
After years at sea, the marline had become stiff but still held the cables firmly.
Careful winding of marline keeps a ship's rigging in top condition.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *marling* (marline), related to the verb *marlen*, meaning 'to fasten with small cord'.
