contradictoriness

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˌdɪktəriˈnɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˌdɪktəriˈnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being contradictory, especially when opposing ideas or conditions cannot coexist
state of being contradictory

Examples

The contradictoriness of his statements made it hard to trust his story.
Philosophers have debated the apparent contradictoriness of free will and determinism for centuries.
There was a clear contradictoriness in the policy that confused many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a situation in which inconsistent elements are present

Etymology

From 'contradictory' + '-ness', with 'contradictory' from Latin 'contradictorius' (speaking against).
