Grias

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the family Lecythidaceae, known for the anchovy pear tree, native to Central and South America and the Caribbean.
genus of tropical trees; anchovy pear tree

Examples

The Grias is well known for producing the anchovy pear, a popular fruit in the Caribbean.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Grias genus during their expedition in South America.
Leaves from the Grias tree are often large and glossy, making it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy pear - an edible fruit produced by trees of the genus Grias

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name coined by Carl Linnaeus in 1759.
