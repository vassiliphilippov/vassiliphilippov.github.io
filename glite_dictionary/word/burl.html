burl

1. NOUN

/ bɜrl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
a large, rounded growth on the trunk or branch of a tree, often with a knobby or distorted appearance
rounded tree outgrowth

Examples

The woodworker prized the burl for its intricate grain patterns.
A massive burl protruded from the old oak, attracting curious hikers.
Furniture made from burl wood is often highly valued for its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall - abnormal growth on plants, often caused by insects or fungi

2. VERB

/ bɝl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
to remove small knots or lumps from cloth, especially during manufacture
remove knots from cloth

Examples

The textile worker carefully burled the fabric to achieve a smooth finish.
Before the cloth can be dyed, it must first be burled and cleaned.
She spent hours burling each piece of wool by hand.

3. NOUN

/ bɜrl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
A rounded knotty growth on a tree or the wood cut from such a growth, valued for its unique and decorative grain patterns.
decorative wood from a tree outgrowth

Examples

The artist used a piece of burl to create a unique tabletop with swirling patterns.
Collectors prize furniture that features burl veneers for their beauty and rarity.
Each burl slice reveals a distinct and intricate wood grain, making every item one of a kind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - a hard, woody lump in a tree where a branch grew

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; recorded in English in the 14th century, possibly from Middle English 'burle', meaning knot in cloth or wood.

4. NOUN

/ bɝːl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
a small, rounded lump or irregularity in yarn or fabric, either as an imperfection or created intentionally for effect
lump or bump in yarn or fabric

Examples

The weaver noticed a burl in the otherwise smooth woolen thread.
Some fabrics are designed with a burl to add texture and visual interest.
Inspectors rejected the scarf because a large burl marred its surface.
