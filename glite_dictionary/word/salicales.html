Salicales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælɪˈkeɪliz / (AmE), / ˌsalɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes the willow family (Salicaceae)
order of plants including willows and poplars

Examples

The family Salicaceae is classified within the order Salicales.
Botanists study the characteristics that distinguish Salicales from other plant orders.
Willows and poplars are well-known members of the Salicales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salicaceae - the willow family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin *Salix* (willow) + -ales (order, suffix used in taxonomy).
