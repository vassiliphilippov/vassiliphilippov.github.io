Oleandra

1. NOUN

/ oʊliˈændrə / (AmE), / əʊliˈændrə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical epiphytic or terrestrial ferns, typically belonging to the family Oleandraceae or formerly Polypodiaceae.
a genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The greenhouse contains several rare species of Oleandra thriving on tree branches.
Researchers collected samples of Oleandra for their study of tropical fern diversity.
In the humid forest, Oleandra grows both on rocks and as an epiphyte on tree trunks.

Etymology

From Greek 'oleandros' (wild olive), referring to the resemblance of their leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊliˈændrə / (AmE), / ˌəʊliˈændrə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical ferns, usually epiphytic or terrestrial, in the family Polypodiaceae.
a genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The Oleandra species are commonly found growing on tree trunks in the rainforest.
Scientists recently discovered a new Oleandra fern in Southeast Asia.
Several members of the Oleandra genus have distinct, leathery fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'oleon' (oil) + 'aner' (man), used in botanical Latin.
