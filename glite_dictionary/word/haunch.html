haunch

1. NOUN

/ hɑntʃ / (AmE), / hɔːntʃ / (BrE)
the hip, buttock, and upper thigh considered as a single anatomical region, especially in humans or animals
hip, buttock, and upper thigh area

Examples

The wolf crouched on its haunches, ready to spring at its prey.
She sat quietly, knees drawn up to her haunches, watching the sunset.
The statue depicted a man resting his weight on one haunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip - the outward projecting part of the body below the waist and above the thigh
  • Degree Lower: thigh - the part of the leg between the hip and the knee

Etymology

From Old French 'hanche' (hip), from Late Latin 'anca'.

2. NOUN

/ hɑntʃ / (AmE), / hɔːntʃ / (BrE)
the hindquarter, especially the loin and upper leg, of a four-legged animal, often used as a cut of meat
the loin and leg of a quadruped

Examples

The chef roasted a haunch of venison for the main course.
A wolf crouched on its haunches, ready to spring at any moment.
They carried the haunch of meat back to camp for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thigh - the part of a person's leg between the hip and the knee, or similar part in animals

Etymology

From Old French *hanche* (hip), from Late Latin *anca* (hip, haunch).
