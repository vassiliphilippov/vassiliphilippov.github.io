ache

1. VERB

/ eɪk / (AmE), / eɪk / (BrE)
to experience a continuous, dull pain in a part of the body
to feel a continuous, dull pain

Examples

After running the marathon, my legs ached for days.
Her tooth aches whenever she eats something cold.
He ached all over following the strenuous workout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurt - to feel or cause physical pain
  • Degree Higher: throb - to feel pain in a series of beats

Etymology

From Old English *acan* (to suffer pain), related to Old High German *ēhan*.

2. VERB

/ eɪk / (AmE), / eɪk / (BrE)
to feel a continuous, dull pain
to feel a dull, persistent pain

Examples

My legs ache after a long hike in the mountains.
She ached with sadness when her friend moved away.
Sometimes I ache all over when I have the flu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurt - to feel or cause physical pain
  • Synonym: throb - to beat or sound with a regular rhythm (often of pain)

Etymology

From Old English *acan* (to ache, pain), related to Latin *agere* (to drive, move).

3. NOUN

/ eɪk / (AmE), / eɪk / (BrE)
A continuous, dull pain that is not sharp but lasts for a period of time.
persistent, dull pain

Examples

After the long workout, she felt an ache in her lower back.
A dull ache started in his temples and gradually spread across his forehead.
The ache in my legs reminded me that I had walked much farther than usual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical suffering or discomfort
  • Degree Higher: throb - a strong, regular beat or sensation of pain
  • Degree Lower: twinge - a sudden, sharp localized pain

Etymology

From Old English *acan* (to ache, suffer pain), related to Old High German *āhhan*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ eɪk / (AmE), / eɪk / (BrE)
to feel an intense longing or desire for something or someone who is absent
feel a strong longing or desire

Examples

After her best friend moved away, she ached for their daily conversations.
He aches to return home and see his family again.
Sometimes, you just ache for a sense of belonging.

Synset Examples

She ached for a cigarette
I am pining for my lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
  • Synonym: pine - to suffer a mental and physical decline because of a broken heart or strong longing
