spadefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪdˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈspeɪdfʊt / (BrE)
a burrowing toad of the Northern Hemisphere with a hard, spade-like projection on each hind foot, used for digging
burrowing toad with spade-like hind feet

Examples

The desert spadefoot emerges at night after heavy rain to breed.
Scientists studied the burrowing behavior of the spadefoot in its natural habitat.
A spadefoot uses its unique hind feet to dig quickly into sandy soil.

Etymology

From 'spade' (a digging tool) + 'foot', referring to the shape of the toad's hind feet.
