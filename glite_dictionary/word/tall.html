tall

1. ADJ

/ tɔl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
(of a task, demand, or order) impressively difficult or challenging to achieve or fulfill.
impressively difficult; very challenging

Examples

Completing the entire project in one week is a tall order.
It would be a tall task to convince them to change their minds now.
Winning the championship after losing so many games is certainly a tall challenge.

Synset Examples

a tall order

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; simple to do
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ tɔl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
(of a story or claim) so unlikely or exaggerated as to be difficult to believe; improbable or fanciful.
improbable or exaggerated (story)

Examples

Many thought it was just a tall tale, but the explorer insisted it was true.
She didn't believe his tall story about fighting off a bear in his backyard.
That excuse sounds like a tall one if you ask me.

Synset Examples

a tall story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbelievable - so unlikely as to not be believed
  • Antonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ tɔl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
a garment size designed for people who are taller than average, with longer measurements than standard sizes
garment size for tall people

Examples

I ordered the jeans in a tall to make sure they would be long enough.
Many clothing brands offer a tall for both men and women.
They were out of medium tall, so I had to choose a different size.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regular - a standard garment size for average height
  • Antonym: petite - a garment size for people who are shorter than average

4. ADJ

/ tɔl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
having a greater than average height; high in vertical dimension or stature.
of greater than average height

Examples

The tall basketball player easily reached the rim for a slam dunk.
Several tall skyscrapers dominate the city skyline.
That oak tree is the tallest in the park.

Synset Examples

tall people
tall buildings
tall trees
tall ships

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short - of less than average height
  • Synonym: high - extending a great distance upward

Etymology

From Old English *getæl* (quick, prompt), later influenced by Middle English *tal* (brave, bold), and developed to mean high in height.

5. ADJ

/ tɑl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
(of language, stories, or claims) exaggerated, grandiose, or lofty in style, often stretching truth beyond credibility.
exaggerated or grandiose in style

Examples

The politician's tall promises won him few admirers among the realist voters.
I don't believe any of his tall stories about fighting wild animals.
The advertisement's tall claims about miracle cures were quickly challenged by regulators.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
