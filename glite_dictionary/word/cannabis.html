cannabis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænəbɪs / (AmE), / ˈkanəbɪs / (BrE)
a tall, coarse annual plant of the genus Cannabis, with palmate leaves and clusters of small green flowers, cultivated for its tough fibers and for producing narcotic drugs
a plant genus yielding fibers and drugs

Examples

The cultivation of cannabis is regulated by law in many countries.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of cannabis.
Hemp fiber is obtained from the stems of the cannabis plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - the dried leaves and flowers of the cannabis plant used as a drug

Etymology

From Latin 'cannabis', from Greek 'kánnabis', meaning hemp.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænəbɪs / (AmE), / ˈkanəbɪs / (BrE)
a plant whose dried leaves and flowers are used as a psychoactive drug, often smoked or consumed for their intoxicating effects
plant used as a psychoactive drug

Examples

The government is debating the legalization of cannabis for medical use.
Some people use cannabis to help relieve chronic pain or anxiety.
She was arrested for possessing a small amount of cannabis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - dried leaves and flowers of cannabis, used as a drug
  • Synonym: hemp - the plant Cannabis sativa, especially when grown for fiber

Etymology

From Latin 'cannabis', from Greek 'kánnabis', referring to the hemp plant.
