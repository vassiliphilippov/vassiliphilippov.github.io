incontestable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈtɛstəbl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈtɛstəbl / (BrE)
impossible to question or dispute; unquestionably true or certain.
impossible to dispute or question

Examples

The evidence presented in court was incontestable and led to a quick verdict.
Her talent is incontestable, as demonstrated by her many awards and achievements.
It is an incontestable fact that the earth orbits the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - unable to be challenged or denied
  • Antonym: questionable - able to be questioned or doubted

Etymology

From French 'incontestable', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'contestabilis' (able to be contested).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈtɛstəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈtɛstəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to dispute or question; certain and clearly true.
impossible to dispute; undeniably true

Examples

The evidence was incontestable and left no room for doubt.
Her victory was incontestable after the final vote tally was announced.
It is incontestable that climate change poses a significant threat to the planet.

Synset Examples

indisputable evidence of a witness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - impossible to doubt or question; unquestionable
  • Antonym: questionable - open to doubt or challenge

Etymology

From French incontestable, from Latin in- (not) + contestabilis (able to be contested).
