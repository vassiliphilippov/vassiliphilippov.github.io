unamended

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈmɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈmɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of legislation or documents) not altered, revised, or changed; in its original form.
not changed or altered; original form
legal

Examples

The bill was passed in its unamended form by the parliament.
The unamended constitution still governs the organization today.
No changes were made to the unamended agreement during negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amended - changed or modified, especially in a legal context

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'amended' (from Latin 'emendare', to correct).
