Senegalese

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛnɪɡəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌsɛnɪɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
relating to Senegal or its people or culture
of Senegal or its people

Examples

The Senegalese government launched a campaign to boost tourism.
She enjoyed the vibrant colors of traditional Senegalese fabrics.
Many Senegalese writers have contributed to African literature.

Synset Examples

Senegalese villages
Senegalese herdsmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senegal - a country in West Africa

Etymology

From Senegal + -ese (a suffix for demonyms and adjectives of nationality).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03127711-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnɪɡəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌsɛnɪɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Senegal
person from Senegal

Examples

Many Senegalese have settled in cities across Europe and North America.
The Senegalese are known for their vibrant music and rich culture.
A Senegalese chef opened a new restaurant in the downtown area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senegal - a country in West Africa

Etymology

From Senegal (country in West Africa) + -ese (demonym suffix, from Italian -ese).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09750166-n
Synset POS: n
