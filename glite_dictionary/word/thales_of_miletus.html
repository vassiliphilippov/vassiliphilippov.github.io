Thales of Miletus

1. PROPN

/ ˈθeɪliːz əv maɪˈliːtəs / (AmE), / ˈθeɪliːz əv mɪˈliːtəs / (BrE)
a presocratic Greek philosopher and astronomer, regarded as the founder of physical science and known for asserting that water is the origin of all things
presocratic Greek philosopher and astronomer

Examples

Thales of Miletus is often credited as the first philosopher in Western history.
According to legend, Thales of Miletus predicted a solar eclipse in 585 BC.
Many historians regard Thales of Miletus as the founder of scientific inquiry.

Etymology

Thales, Greek Θαλῆς, from Miletus, an ancient Greek city in Asia Minor.
