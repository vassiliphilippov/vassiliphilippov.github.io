west

1. ADV

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
toward, to, or in the direction of the west
toward or in the direction of the west

Examples

After graduating, they decided to head west in search of new opportunities.
The soldiers marched west across the plain at sunrise.
As the sun set, we continued driving west along the coast.

Synset Examples

we moved west to Arizona
situated west of Boston

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east - toward or in the direction of the east

Etymology

From Old English 'west', from Proto-Germanic '*westrą', ultimately from Proto-Indo-European '*wes-' (evening, west).

4. NOUN

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
the direction towards the point of the horizon where the sun sets, opposite to east
direction opposite to east

Examples

The sun sets in the west each evening, marking the end of the day.
They drove toward the west, hoping to see the ocean before sunset.
A strong wind was blowing from the west all night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east - direction opposite of west
  • Degree Lower: north - direction at right angles to both east and west, towards the North Pole
  • Degree Lower: south - direction at right angles to both east and west, towards the South Pole

Etymology

Examples

The house faces west, so the living room gets afternoon sunlight.

Related Terms
