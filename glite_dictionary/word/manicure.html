manicure

1. VERB

/ ˈmænɪˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈmanɪkjʊə / (BrE)
to trim, shape, and polish the fingernails or toenails carefully and neatly
to care for and trim nails

Examples

She likes to manicure her nails every weekend at the salon.
Before the wedding, he manicured his fingernails for a neat appearance.
The technician manicured my hands with great care and skill.

Synset Examples

manicure fingernails

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedicure - to trim and care for toenails

Etymology

From Latin 'manus' (hand) and 'cura' (care), via French 'manucure'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00042372-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈmænɪˌkjʊə / (BrE)
a professional or cosmetic treatment for the hands and fingernails, including cleaning, shaping, and polishing the nails
treatment for hands and fingernails

Examples

She scheduled a manicure before the wedding to make sure her nails looked perfect.
After a stressful week, a relaxing manicure was just what she needed.
The salon offers both manicure and pedicure services for their customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedicure - professional care for the feet and toenails

Etymology

From French 'manucure', from Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'cura' (care).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00662026-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈmænɪˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈmænɪˌkjʊə / (BrE)
to care for the nails and hands by cutting, shaping, and polishing them
to groom nails and hands

Examples

She likes to manicure her nails every weekend, keeping them clean and perfectly shaped.
He carefully manicured his hands before the important interview.
Some people prefer to have a professional manicure their nails at a salon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedicure - to care for the feet and toenails in a similar way

Etymology

From French 'manucure', from Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'cura' (care).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00042210-v
Synset POS: v
