Satureja calamintha glandulosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætəˈriːə ˌkæləmˈɪnθə ɡlænˈdjuːləsə / (AmE), / ˌsætjʊˈriːə ˌkæləˈmɪnθə ɡlænˈdjuːləsə / (BrE)
a low-growing, strongly aromatic perennial herb native to southern Europe and Great Britain, now naturalized in the United States
aromatic European perennial herb

Examples

The leaves of Satureja calamintha glandulosa are often used to flavor culinary dishes.
Herb enthusiasts value Satureja calamintha glandulosa for its aromatic qualities in the garden.
Satureja calamintha glandulosa thrives in well-drained soils and sunny locations.

Etymology

From Latin genus Satureja and species calamintha glandulosa.
