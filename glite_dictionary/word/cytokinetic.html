cytokinetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪtoʊkəˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the division of a cell's cytoplasm following nuclear division, especially during cell division
relating to cell cytoplasm division
medical

Examples

The cytokinetic phase ensures that each daughter cell receives sufficient cytoplasmic material.
Abnormal cytokinetic activity can lead to issues in cell division.
Researchers studied the mechanisms underlying cytokinetic furrow formation in plant cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytokinesis - the division of a cell's cytoplasm after nuclear division

Etymology

From cytokinesis, formed from 'cyto-' (cell) and Greek 'kinesis' (movement), with suffix '-ic.'
