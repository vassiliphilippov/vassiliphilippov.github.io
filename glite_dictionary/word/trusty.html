trusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈtrʌsti / (BrE)
reliable and deserving of trust; dependable.
reliable; deserving of trust

Examples

She keeps a trusty notebook on her desk for jotting down ideas.
He always brings his trusty old camera on vacation.
The hikers depended on their trusty map to navigate the mountain trails.

Synset Examples

a trustworthy report
an experienced and trustworthy traveling companion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
  • Antonym: untrustworthy - not able to be relied on; not dependable

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old Norse traust (trust), influenced by trust + -y (forming adjectives).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈtrʌsti / (BrE)
a prisoner who is considered trustworthy and is given special privileges or responsibilities within a prison
trusted prisoner given privileges

Examples

The warden assigned the cleaning duties to a trusty who had earned his confidence.
As a trusty, he was trusted to run errands outside the cellblock.
After years of good behavior, she became a trusty and was allowed more freedom within the prison.

Etymology

From 'trust' + '-y', referring to someone who can be relied upon.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10752178-n
Synset POS: n
