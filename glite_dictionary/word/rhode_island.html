Rhode Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊd ˌaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd ˌaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, in New England; the smallest US state by area, and one of the original thirteen colonies.
US state; smallest and original colony

Examples

After graduating, she moved to Rhode Island to start her new job.
Tourists often visit Rhode Island for its scenic coastline and historic towns.
The Declaration of Independence was ratified by Rhode Island in 1790.

Etymology

Named after the Greek island of Rhodes and the Dutch word for 'red island' (Roodt Eylandt).
