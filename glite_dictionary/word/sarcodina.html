Sarcodina

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkoʊdɪnə / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊdɪnə / (BrE)
a group of protozoans that move and feed by forming pseudopods, including Actinopoda and Rhizopoda
group of protozoans forming pseudopods
medical

Examples

Many members of the Sarcodina move using temporary extensions called pseudopods.
Scientists classify amoebas and foraminiferans within the Sarcodina.
The diversity of Sarcodina is evident in both freshwater and marine environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek sarkos (flesh) + -ina (plural suffix).

2. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkoʊdɪnə / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊdɪnə / (BrE)
a former major class of protozoans characterized by movement and feeding using pseudopods, including amoebas and related organisms
class of protozoans using pseudopods

Examples

Many amoebas were originally classified under Sarcodina due to their use of pseudopodia.
Some biologists no longer use the term Sarcodina because of advances in genetic classification.
The Sarcodina are studied for their unique locomotion and feeding methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Actinopoda - a subclass of Sarcodina characterized by ray-like pseudopods
  • Synonym: Rhizopoda - another subclass of Sarcodina, amoeboid protozoans

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek sarx (flesh) and -odinos (resembling).
