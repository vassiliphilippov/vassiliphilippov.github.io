squiggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɪɡəl / (BrE)
an irregular mark, line, or scrawl that is difficult or impossible to read, often used to refer to unclear handwriting or signatures
an illegible or irregular scrawl

Examples

Her doctor's handwriting was just a squiggle on the prescription.
He added a quick squiggle at the bottom of the form as his signature.
To me, that note was nothing but an indecipherable squiggle.

Synset Examples

his signature was just a squiggle but only he could make that squiggle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - carelessly written or drawn lines or writing
  • Synonym: scribble - something written or drawn carelessly or illegibly

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɪɡl / (BrE)
a short, twisting or curving line or mark
a short, twisting line

Examples

She doodled a squiggle at the bottom of the page.
There was a strange squiggle next to his signature.
The child drew a blue squiggle on the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - a piece of writing or drawing that is done carelessly or hurriedly
