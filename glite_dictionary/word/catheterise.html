to catheterise

1. VERB

/ kəˈθɛt̬ɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈθiːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to insert a catheter into a part of the body, especially for medical purposes
insert a catheter (medical)
medical british

Examples

The nurse had to catheterise the patient before surgery.
Doctors often need to catheterise the bladder to drain urine during certain procedures.
If the patient is unable to urinate, the physician may decide to catheterise them temporarily.

Synset Examples

catheterize the patient's bladder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catheterize - to insert a catheter into a body part (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'cathétériser', from 'cathéter' (catheter), ultimately from Greek 'kathetēr' (something let down).
