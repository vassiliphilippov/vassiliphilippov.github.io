to lambaste

1. VERB

/ læmˈbeɪst / (AmE), / læmˈbɑːst / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very severely, especially in public
criticize harshly

Examples

The coach did not hesitate to lambaste the team after their disappointing performance.
She was lambasted by the critics for her controversial remarks.
Several politicians lambasted the proposed policy during the debate.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to berate - to scold or criticize angrily
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Probably from blend of 'lamb' (beat) and 'baste' (thrash).
