Eryngium maritimum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪndʒiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪndʒiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (BrE)
A European evergreen perennial plant of the parsley family, with twisted spiny leaves, commonly known as sea holly; its roots were once used as an aphrodisiac.
a species of sea holly (plant)

Examples

The medicinal properties of Eryngium maritimum were valued in traditional herbal remedies.
On their seaside walk, they spotted clusters of Eryngium maritimum with its spiky blue-green leaves.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Eryngium maritimum for its unique foliage and coastal hardiness.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Eryngium' meaning sea holly, 'maritimum' meaning of the sea or coastal.
