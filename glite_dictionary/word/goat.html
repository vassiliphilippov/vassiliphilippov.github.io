goat

1. NOUN

/ goʊt / (AmE), / gəʊt / (BrE)
(Astrology) A person born under the sign of Capricorn.
person born under Capricorn (astrology)

Examples

My sister is a goat, born in early January and very practical.
Among my friends, the goats are known for their determination and ambition.
As a goat, he believes his Capricorn traits influence his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capricorn - the tenth sign of the zodiac, represented by a goat

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊt / (BrE)
a person who is the victim of ridicule, blame, or pranks, especially in a group setting
person subject to ridicule or blame

Examples

After the team's loss, Mark became the goat of the locker room, teased endlessly by his teammates.
Every group of friends seems to have a goat who is always the target of practical jokes.
He didn't mind playing the goat at parties as long as everyone was having fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings, mistakes, or faults of others

3. NOUN

/ ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊt / (BrE)
A hardy domesticated animal related to the sheep, typically having a beard and straight horns.
domesticated animal with beard and horns

Examples

The farmer keeps a goat for its milk and meat.
A goat climbed effortlessly up the steep rocky hillside.
They watched as the goat nibbled leaves from the bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal related to the goat, usually kept for its wool or meat

Etymology

From Old English *gāt*, from Proto-Germanic *gaits*.
