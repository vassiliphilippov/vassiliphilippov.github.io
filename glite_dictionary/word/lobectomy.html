lobectomy

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbɛktəmi / (AmE), / ləʊˈbɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove a lobe from an organ, such as the lung or the brain
surgical removal of a lobe
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a lobectomy to remove the cancerous lobe of his lung.
A lobectomy is often recommended when a brain tumor is confined to a single lobe.
After the lobectomy, her breathing gradually improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resection - surgical removal of part of an organ or structure

Etymology

From Greek 'lobos' (lobe) + '-ectomy' (surgical removal).
