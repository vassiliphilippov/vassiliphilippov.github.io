panegyric

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˈdʒɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪrɪk / (BrE)
A formal public speech or written text expressing high praise for someone or something.
formal speech or text of praise

Examples

The retiring professor was honored with a lengthy panegyric delivered by one of his former students.
At the ceremony, she delivered a moving panegyric to celebrate her mentor's achievements.
The book closes with a panegyric on the importance of compassion and kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eulogy - a speech or piece of writing that praises someone, especially after death
  • Synonym: tribute - an act, statement, or gift that is intended to show gratitude, respect, or admiration
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Greek panēgurikos (of a public assembly or festival), from panēgyris (public assembly).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpænəˈdʒɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪrɪk / (BrE)
(of language or writing) formally expressing praise; highly laudatory.
formally expressing praise

Examples

His panegyric remarks about the retiring professor were met with a standing ovation.
The letter was written in a highly panegyric style, extolling the achievements of the organization.
Many reviewers found the book's panegyric tone excessive and insincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudatory - expressing praise or commendation
  • Antonym: critical - expressing adverse or disapproving comments

Etymology

From Greek *panēgurikos* (of or for a public assembly), from *panēgyris* (public assembly).
