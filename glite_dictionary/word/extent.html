extent

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɛnt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnt / (BrE)
the degree or limit to which something happens or is true; the amount, size, or scope of something
degree or limit of something

Examples

We still do not know the full extent of the problem.
The flood caused damage to a considerable extent.
To some extent, I agree with your assessment.

Synset Examples

the extent of the damage
the full extent of the law
to a certain extent she was right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degree - amount or level of something
  • Synonym: scope - the range of something's coverage
  • Synonym: limit - the greatest amount or degree possible

Etymology

From Latin 'extentus', past participle of 'extendere' (to stretch out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɛnt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnt / (BrE)
the distance, area, or volume over which something extends or is spread
distance, area, or volume something covers

Examples

The extent of the damage was not visible until daylight.
We were amazed by the extent of the city's parklands.
Scientists measured the extent of the glacier's retreat over the last decade.

Synset Examples

the vast extent of the desert
an orchard of considerable extent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scope - the range or extent of something

Etymology

From Latin extentus, past participle of extendere (to stretch out).
