imperceptible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪmpərˈsɛptəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈsɛptəbl̩ / (BrE)
So slight, gradual, or subtle as to be impossible or difficult to notice or perceive.
too slight or subtle to be noticed

Examples

There was an imperceptible crack in the glass that could only be seen under a microscope.
She gave an imperceptible nod to signal her agreement without attracting attention.
The changes in the policy were so imperceptible that hardly anyone noticed.

Synset Examples

an imperceptible drop in temperature
an imperceptible nod
color is unperceivable to the touch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perceptible - able to be seen or noticed

Etymology

From Latin imperceptibilis, from in- (not) + perceptibilis (perceptible).
