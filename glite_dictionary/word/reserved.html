reserved

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɝvd / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
(of a place, seat, or thing) set aside for the use of a particular person or group.
set aside for specific use

Examples

The front row seats were reserved for special guests at the ceremony.
This parking space is reserved for employees only.
She saw a table with a sign that said it was reserved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocated - set aside for a particular purpose or person
  • Antonym: available - free for use by anyone, not set aside
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɜrvd / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
slow or unwilling to show feelings or express opinions; quiet and restrained in manner or speech.
quiet and restrained in manner or speech

Examples

Although Emma is very reserved, her friends know she has a great sense of humor.
The manager remained reserved during the discussion, rarely sharing his personal viewpoint.
He grew more reserved around strangers, preferring to listen rather than speak.

Synset Examples

was habitually reserved in speech, withholding her opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticent - unwilling to speak or reveal thoughts or feelings
  • Antonym: outgoing - sociable and eager to express thoughts and feelings openly

Etymology

From the past participle of 'reserve', from Latin 'reservare' (to keep back or save).
