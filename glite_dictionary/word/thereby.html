thereby

1. ADV

/ ðɛrˈbaɪ / (AmE), / ˌðeəˈbaɪ / (BrE)
as a result of that; by that means
as a result; by that means

Examples

He forgot to lock the door, thereby allowing the thief easy access.
The factory introduced safety measures, thereby reducing workplace accidents significantly.
She broke her promise, thereby losing his trust.

Synset Examples

He knocked over the red wine, thereby ruining the table cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thus - as a result or consequence of this; therefore
  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason; consequently

Etymology

From Middle English 'therby', from Old English 'þǣr' (there) + 'bi' (by).
