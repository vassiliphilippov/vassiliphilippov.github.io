Wei

1. PROPN

/ weɪ / (AmE), / weɪ / (BrE)
the name of several imperial dynasties of China, notably the Cao Wei (220–265 CE) and Northern Wei (386–534 CE)
Chinese imperial dynasties' name

Examples

The Wei dynasty played a crucial role in shaping Chinese history during the Three Kingdoms period.
Many archaeological artifacts from the Wei era have been discovered in northern China.
The collapse of the Wei dynasty led to a time of great upheaval and division.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 魏 (Wèi), the dynastic name.
