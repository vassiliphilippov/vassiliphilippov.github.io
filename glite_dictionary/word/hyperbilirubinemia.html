hyperbilirubinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərbɪˌlɪruːbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəbɪˌlɪrʊbɪˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
an abnormally high level of bilirubin in the blood, often causing jaundice
excess bilirubin in the blood
medical

Examples

Newborns are often screened for hyperbilirubinemia in the first few days of life.
Severe hyperbilirubinemia can cause neurological complications if left untreated.
Doctors monitor patients with liver disease for signs of hyperbilirubinemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - yellowing of the skin and eyes due to high bilirubin

Etymology

From hyper- (above, excessive) + bilirubin (a bile pigment) + -emia (blood condition).
