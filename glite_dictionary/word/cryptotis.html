Cryptotis

1. PROPN

/ krɪpˈtoʊtɪs / (AmE), / krɪpˈtəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, insectivorous mammals known as least shrews, native to the Americas.
genus of small shrews

Examples

The genus Cryptotis includes several species of least shrews native to North and Central America.
Researchers collected data on the habitat preferences of Cryptotis in the tropical rainforest.
The discovery of a new Cryptotis species excited the scientific community.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) and 'otis' (ear).

2. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtoʊtɪs / (AmE), / krɪpˈtəʊtɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small, insectivorous mammals commonly known as least shrews, native to the Americas
a genus of small mammals (least shrews)

Examples

The genus Cryptotis includes several species of small shrews found throughout the Americas.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Cryptotis genus in Central America.
Members of Cryptotis are often called least shrews due to their tiny size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a small, mole-like insectivorous mammal

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + otis (ear), referring to the small or obscured ears of these animals.
