furled

1. ADJ

/ fɝld / (AmE), / fɜːld / (BrE)
rolled up and fastened securely, especially of flags, sails, or umbrellas
rolled up and secured

Examples

The ship's furled sails waited for a fresh breeze before departure.
He carried a furled umbrella, just in case it rained later.
The national flag remained furled while the storm passed.

Synset Examples

furled sails bound securely to the spar
a furled flag
his rolled umbrella hanging on his arm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfurled - not rolled up; spread out or open

Etymology

From the verb 'furl', originating from Old French *ferlier* (to bind) and ultimately from Latin *firmare* (to make firm).
