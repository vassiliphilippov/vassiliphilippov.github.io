saying

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪɪŋ / (BrE)
a well-known word or phrase that is often used in particular situations, especially one that expresses a general truth or piece of advice
well-known word or phrase expressing truth or advice

Examples

There's an old saying that actions speak louder than words.
My grandmother always had a saying for every situation.
Have you heard the saying "better late than never"?

Synset Examples

pardon the expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a short, well-known saying stating a general truth or piece of advice
  • Synonym: adage - a traditional saying expressing a common experience or observation

Etymology

From Old English secgan (to say), influenced by the verb 'say'.
