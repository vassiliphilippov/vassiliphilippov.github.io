Lophodytes

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɑfəˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌlɒfəˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of ducks in the subfamily Merginae, consisting of the hooded merganser and its relatives.
genus of ducks (hooded merganser)

Examples

The hooded merganser is the only species in the genus Lophodytes.
Ornithologists often study Lophodytes to understand duck behavior in North America.
Lophodytes can be identified by their distinctive crests and diving habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mergus - another genus of mergansers, related diving ducks
  • Degree Lower: Anas - a genus of dabbling ducks, not closely related but within the same family

Etymology

From Greek lophos (crest) + dytēs (diver).

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈfɒdəˌtiːz / (AmE), / ləˈfɒdɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of ducks in the subfamily Merginae, known for species such as the hooded merganser.
a genus of ducks (Merginae)

Examples

The Lophodytes genus is best represented in North America by the hooded merganser.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot members of the Lophodytes genus during migration season.
Scientists studied the nesting behavior of Lophodytes across various freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Greek lophos (crest, tuft) + dytēs (diver), referring to the bird's crested appearance and diving habits.
