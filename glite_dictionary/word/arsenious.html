arsenious

1. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈsiːniəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈsiːnɪəs / (BrE)
relating to compounds in which arsenic has a valency of three (trivalent)
describing trivalent arsenic compounds
medical

Examples

Many pesticides used to contain arsenious compounds before stricter safety regulations were introduced.
The chemist analyzed an arsenious oxide sample in the laboratory.
Exposure to arsenious substances can be hazardous without proper protective equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic - a chemical element with symbol As
  • Antonym: arsenic - relating to compounds in which arsenic is pentavalent

Etymology

From Latin arsenicum (arsenic) + -ous (suffix forming adjectives indicating lower valency in chemistry).
