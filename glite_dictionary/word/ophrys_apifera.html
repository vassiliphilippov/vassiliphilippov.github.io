Ophrys apifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊfrɪs əˈpɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs apɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A European orchid whose flowers resemble bumble bees in shape and color, commonly known as the bee orchid.
a European orchid called the bee orchid

Examples

The Ophrys apifera attracts pollinators by mimicking the appearance of a bumble bee.
In some regions, Ophrys apifera can be found growing wild in meadows and grassy slopes.
Botanists admire the unique evolutionary adaptation of Ophrys apifera's flower shape and coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee orchid - common name for Ophrys apifera

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ophrys' (a type of orchid) and 'apifera' meaning 'bee-bearing' (from 'apis', bee, and 'ferre', to bear).
