essay

1. VERB

/ ɛˈseɪ / (AmE), / ɛˈseɪ / (BrE)
to attempt or try to do something, especially in a tentative or experimental way
attempt; try (verb)

Examples

She essayed a smile, despite her nerves.
The team essayed a bold new strategy in the second half.
He essays to explain the theory in simple terms.

Synset Examples

He tried to shake off his fears
The infant had essayed a few wobbly steps
The police attempted to stop the thief
He sought to improve himself
She always seeks to do good in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - to try to do something
  • Synonym: try - to make an effort to do something

Etymology

From French 'essayer' (to try), from Late Latin 'exagiare' (to examine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛseɪ / (BrE)
a tentative or experimental attempt to do or achieve something, especially in a formal or literary context
a tentative or experimental attempt

Examples

His first essay at painting was surprisingly successful.
In her essay to resolve the dispute, she learned a lot about mediation.
The invention, though primitive, was an early essay in flight technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - an act of trying to do something, especially something difficult
  • Synonym: trial - a test of the performance, qualities, or suitability of someone or something

Etymology

From French 'essai', from Late Latin 'exagium' (a weighing), from Latin 'exigere' (to examine, test).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00788872-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛˌseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛseɪ / (BrE)
a short piece of writing on a particular subject, often presenting the author's personal viewpoint or argument
short literary composition on a subject

Examples

She wrote an essay on the importance of environmental protection for her English class.
His essay challenges the common assumptions about technological progress.
I found her essay on modern art extremely insightful and well-argued.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: treatise - a formal and systematic written discourse on a subject
  • Degree Lower: article - a short piece of writing on a particular topic, often for a magazine or newspaper
  • Degree Lower: report - a detailed account or statement describing or analyzing an event or situation

Etymology

From French 'essai' (trial, attempt), from Late Latin 'exagium' (a weighing), from 'exigere' (to examine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06421205-n
Synset POS: n
