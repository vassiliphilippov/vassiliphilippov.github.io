neighbor

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
a person, place, or thing that is located near or next to another of the same kind.
nearby object, place, or entity

Examples

Venus is Earth's closest neighbor in the solar system.
The two towns are close neighbors in the valley.
One galaxy may have several neighbors in its group.

Synset Examples

Fort Worth is a neighbor of Dallas
what is the closest neighbor to the Earth?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighboring - being situated next to or near; adjacent
  • Antonym: distant - far away in space or relationship

Etymology

From Old English 'nēahgebūr', meaning 'near dweller'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.bə / (BrE)
a person who lives near or next to another
person living nearby

Examples

Our new neighbor brought us a welcome gift when we moved in.
It's important to be friendly with your neighbors in the community.
Her neighbor helped her carry groceries up the stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighboring - located or living near or next to a place or person
  • Antonym: stranger - a person whom one does not know

Etymology

From Old English nēahgebūr, from nēah (near) + gebūr (dweller, farmer).

3. VERB

/ ˈneɪbər / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
to live or be situated next to or near another person or place
be situated next to someone or something

Examples

Two farms neighbor the town on either side.
The apartment buildings neighbor the hospital in the city center.
Several restaurants neighbor the new library, making lunch options plentiful.

Synset Examples

the neighboring house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjoin - to be next to or joined with

Etymology

From Old English *nēahgebūr* ('near dweller'): *nēah* ('near') + *gebūr* ('dweller').

/ ˈneɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
(of a place, object, or country) to be located next to or very close to another.
be located near or next to

Examples

The newly built hospital neighbors several schools and residential areas.
France neighbors several countries, including Germany and Spain.
