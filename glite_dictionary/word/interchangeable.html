interchangeable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of two or more things) able to be exchanged or substituted for each other without loss of function or suitability.
able to be substituted for each other

Examples

Many computer components are interchangeable between different models.
The lenses on these cameras are fully interchangeable, making upgrades easy.
Our employees are trained to be interchangeable in various roles within the company.

Synset Examples

interchangeable electric outlets
interchangeable parts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replaceable - able to be replaced by something else
  • Antonym: unique - being the only one of its kind; unlike anything else

Etymology

From interchange + -able, patterned on French 'interchangeable'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (BrE)
(of arguments, roles, or components) able to be switched with one another without affecting the overall result or meaning.
able to be switched without effect

Examples

In this function, the two variables are interchangeable, so swapping them does not change the output.
The terms 'synonym' and 'equivalent' are often used interchangeably in logic textbooks.
In a symmetric relation, the positions of the arguments are interchangeable.

Synset Examples

the arguments of the symmetric relation, ‘is a sister of,’ are interchangeable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchangeable - able to be exchanged for another, often in practical or financial contexts
  • Antonym: distinct - clearly different; not able to be switched or substituted

Etymology

From interchange + -able, indicating ability to be interchanged.

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəbl / (BrE)
(of two or more things) able to be exchanged or substituted for each other without loss of function or suitability.
able to be substituted without loss of function

Examples

The two connectors are interchangeable and can be used in any of the available sockets.
All parts in this model are interchangeable, making repairs much easier.
Some shoes come with interchangeable straps to allow for different styles.

Synset Examples

interchangeable electric outlets
interchangeable parts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replaceable - able to be replaced
  • Antonym: unique - being the only one of its kind, not able to be replaced

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between, among) + 'cambiare' (to exchange), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
