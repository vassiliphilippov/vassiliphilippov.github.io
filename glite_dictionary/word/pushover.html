pushover

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a person who is easily influenced, persuaded, or taken advantage of
person easily influenced or manipulated

Examples

Everyone says Tom is a pushover when it comes to negotiations.
Her friends knew she was no pushover and would stand up for herself.
The coach isn't a pushover—he expects everyone to work hard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doormat - a person who lets others treat them badly without defending themselves
  • Antonym: hardhead - a person who is difficult to influence or manipulate

Etymology

Originally American English (early 20th century), from 'push' + 'over', suggesting something easy to overcome.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a task or activity that is very easy to do or accomplish
something easy to do

Examples

Compared to last year's project, this assignment was a real pushover.
The exam turned out to be a pushover, much easier than anyone expected.
Some thought the negotiations would be a pushover, but they proved surprisingly tricky.

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeze - a task that is very easy to do
  • Synonym: walkover - something easily accomplished or won

Etymology

From the phrase 'push over', originally referring to something easily knocked down (1920s slang).
