realistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌriːəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
representing things in a way that is true to life or as they actually are, without idealization or abstraction
true-to-life; not idealized or abstract

Examples

The artist's realistic painting captured every tiny detail of the street scene.
She prefers realistic novels that portray ordinary people's lives.
The film used advanced effects to create a highly realistic dinosaur.

Synset Examples

realistic portraiture
a realistic novel
in naturalistic colors
the school of naturalistic writers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naturalistic - closely imitating nature or real life

Etymology

Formed from 'real' + '-istic', after French 'réalistique'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01987050-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌriːəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting the philosophical doctrine of realism, the belief that reality exists independently of perception or thought.
pertaining to philosophical realism

Examples

The philosopher was known for his realistic approach to the nature of existence.
A realistic system of thought contrasts with idealistic philosophies.
Many debates in metaphysics are defined by realistic versus anti-realistic perspectives.

Synset Examples

a realistic system of thought

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idealistic - relating to the philosophical doctrine of idealism

Etymology

From French réaliste (realist), from Latin realis (actual, real).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02798114-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌriəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
accepting or representing things as they really are; having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected
sensible and practical; true to reality

Examples

She offered a realistic assessment of the project's timeline based on current resources.
It's important to set realistic goals that you can actually achieve.
The movie provides a realistic depiction of life during the war.

Synset Examples

a realistic description
a realistic view of the possibilities
a realistic appraisal of our chances
the actors tried to create a realistic portrayal of the Africans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - sensible and realistic in approach to situations
  • Antonym: idealistic - characterized by idealism; unrealistically aiming for perfection

Etymology

From French réaliste (realist) + -ic, ultimately from Latin res (thing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01946601-a
Synset POS: a
