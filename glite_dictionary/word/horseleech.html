horseleech

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌlitʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌliːtʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater leech, especially one that was formerly believed to attack horses and other animals
a large freshwater leech

Examples

The pond was infested with horseleeches that clung to any animal entering the water.
A horseleech is much larger than the typical medicinal leech.
Farmers used to believe that horseleeches could cause illness in livestock.

Etymology

From 'horse' + 'leech', referring to its supposed tendency to attach to horses.
