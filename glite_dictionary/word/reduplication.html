reduplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.dʌ.plɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.djuː.plɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of repeating something, especially a word or part of a word; an instance of such repetition
the act of repeating, especially in language

Examples

In many languages, reduplication is used to convey emphasis or plural forms.
The word "bye-bye" is a common example of reduplication in English.
Linguists often study how different types of reduplication affect meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repetition - the act of doing or saying something again

Etymology

From Latin 'reduplicatio', from 're-' (again) + 'duplicare' (to double).

3. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌduːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The syllable or part that is repeated in a word to create a new form, often to modify its meaning or grammatical function.
repeated part of a word form in linguistics

Examples

In the word "bye-bye", the second "bye" is the reduplication of the first syllable.
Some languages use reduplication to express plurality or intensity in words.
The reduplication often follows the initial syllable, as seen in "knock-knock" jokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduplication - the process of repeating all or part of a word to form a new word or grammatical form

Etymology

From Latin reduplicatio (a doubling, repetition), from re- (again) + duplicare (to double).
