patrology

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈtrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of or the body of writings of the early Christian Church Fathers
study or writings of early Church Fathers

Examples

The professor specialized in patrology, focusing on the theological debates of the second century.
Many important doctrines were established in the era covered by patrology.
An understanding of patrology is essential for students of early Christian history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patristics - the study of the early Christian writers known as the Church Fathers

Etymology

From Greek patēr (father) + -logia (study), via Late Latin patrologia.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈtrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of theology concerned with the study of the lives, writings, and doctrines of the Church Fathers
study of the Church Fathers

Examples

She specializes in patrology and has published several papers on early Christian texts.
The university offers a course on patrology for theology students.
His research in patrology focuses on the writings of Augustine and Jerome.

Etymology

From Greek patēr (father) + -logia (study).
