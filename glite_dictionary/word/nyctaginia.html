Nyctaginia

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˈdʒɪniə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈdʒɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Nyctaginaceae, comprising a single species native to North America.
plant genus in Nyctaginaceae family

Examples

The rare Nyctaginia can be found in sandy soils across the southern United States.
Botanists have studied Nyctaginia for its unique floral structure.
The genus Nyctaginia contains only one known species.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'nyx' (night), referring to the night-blooming habits of some related plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˈdʒɪniə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈdʒɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the four o'clock family (Nyctaginaceae), containing only a single species.
genus of flowering plant in Nyctaginaceae

Examples

The rare Nyctaginia is found in arid regions of North America.
Nyctaginia is the sole genus of its kind in its family, making it unique among flowering plants.
Botanists studied the flower structure of Nyctaginia to better understand the Nyctaginaceae family.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'nyx, nyktos' (night) + -ginia (related to pistil), referring to features of the flowers.
