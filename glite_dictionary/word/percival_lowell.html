Percival Lowell

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɜr.sɪ.vəl ˈloʊ.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.sɪ.vəl ˈləʊ.əl / (BrE)
(1855–1916) United States astronomer known for his studies of Mars and the prediction of the existence of Pluto.
American astronomer (1855–1916)

Examples

Percival Lowell founded the Lowell Observatory in Arizona, which played a major role in planetary astronomy.
Many of Percival Lowell's theories about life on Mars were later disproved, but they inspired generations of scientists.
The discovery of Pluto is often credited to the search initiated by Percival Lowell.
