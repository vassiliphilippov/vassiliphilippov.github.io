brindle

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪndəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪnd(ə)l / (BrE)
having a pattern of dark streaks or patches on a lighter background, especially in the fur of animals such as dogs or cats
having streaked or patchy coloring, esp. in animal fur

Examples

The brindle cat curled up on the windowsill, its fur a beautiful mix of browns and greys.
Her dog had a brindle coat that made him stand out from the others at the park.
You could easily spot the brindle kitten among its solid-colored siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped - having stripes; marked with bands or lines of color

Etymology

From Middle English 'brended', from Old Norse 'bröndóttr', meaning streaked or marked.
