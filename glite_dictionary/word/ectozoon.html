ectozoon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛktoʊˈzoʊ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˌɛktəʊˈzəʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
an organism that lives as an external parasite on the body of another animal, such as a flea.
external parasitic organism (e.g., flea)

Examples

The flea is a common example of an ectozoon found on mammals.
Some fish suffer from ectozoa that attach to their skin and gills.
Veterinarians often treat animals for infestations by ectozoa such as ticks and lice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the host's body
  • Synonym: ectoparasite - a parasite that lives on the surface of its host

Etymology

From Greek 'ektos' (outside) + 'zōon' (animal).
