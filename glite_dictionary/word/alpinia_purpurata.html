Alpinia purpurata

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪni.ə ˌpərpjuˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ælˈpɪni.ə ˌpɜːpjʊˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical ornamental plant from the ginger family, native to Pacific islands, known for its striking red bracts.
ornamental tropical ginger plant

Examples

The lush garden featured several Alpinia purpurata plants with vibrant red blossoms.
Florists often use Alpinia purpurata in tropical flower arrangements for its striking appearance.
In Hawaii, Alpinia purpurata is commonly planted along walkways to add color and beauty.

Etymology

From genus 'Alpinia,' named after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini, and Latin 'purpurata' (purple-colored).
