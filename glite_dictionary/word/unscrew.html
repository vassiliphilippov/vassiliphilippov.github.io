to unscrew

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈskru / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruː / (BrE)
to loosen or remove something by turning it, especially a lid, cap, or fastener
loosen or remove by turning

Examples

Could you please unscrew the jar lid for me?
She had to unscrew the back panel to replace the batteries.
I watched him unscrew the top from the thermos carefully.

Synset Examples

unscrew the bottle cap

Related Terms

  • Antonym: screw - to fasten or tighten by turning

Etymology

From prefix un- (expressing reversal) + screw (from Middle French *escroue*)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01355383-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈskru / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruː / (BrE)
to loosen or remove (something) by turning it so that it comes free from being screwed in
loosen or remove by turning

Examples

He used a screwdriver to unscrew the panel from the wall.
Before replacing the bulb, make sure to unscrew the old one carefully.
Can you help me unscrew this jar lid? It's stuck.

Synset Examples

unscrew the outlet plate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: screw - to fasten or tighten by turning a screw or similar mechanism

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'reverse action') + screw (from Middle French escrue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01355231-v
Synset POS: v
