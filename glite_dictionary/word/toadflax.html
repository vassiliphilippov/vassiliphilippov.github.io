toadflax

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdˌflæks / (AmE), / ˈtəʊd.flæks / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with showy yellow and orange flowers, often found as a weed in North America.
a perennial plant with yellow and orange flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened by clusters of toadflax along the path.
Farmers often regard toadflax as a troublesome weed in their fields.
Yellow toadflax has spread widely in parts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linaria - a genus of plants including toadflax

Etymology

From 'toad' + 'flax'; named for a supposed resemblance to flax and the toad association is uncertain.
