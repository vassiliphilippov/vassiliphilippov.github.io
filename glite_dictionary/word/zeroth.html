zeroth

1. ADJ

/ ˈzɪroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊθ / (BrE)
preceding the first in a series; relating to a position before the first
preceding the first; before first

Examples

The zeroth law of thermodynamics forms the basis for temperature measurement.
Her research led to the discovery of a zeroth order effect previously unnoticed.
Scientists often refer to a zeroth approximation as the simplest starting point in a calculation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: first - being number one in a series; coming before all others

Etymology

Modeled after 'first' and other ordinal numbers, from 'zero'.
