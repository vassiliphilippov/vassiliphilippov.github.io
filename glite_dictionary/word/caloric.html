caloric

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɔrɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the number of calories in food or the measurement of energy provided by food.
relating to calories in food

Examples

The nutritionist recommended reducing your caloric intake to help with weight loss.
This snack has a high caloric content but few vitamins or minerals.
Many diet plans focus on balancing caloric consumption with energy expenditure.

Synset Examples

comparison of foods on a caloric basis
the caloric content of foods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calorie - unit of energy commonly used to measure food energy

Etymology

From Latin calor (heat), via French calorique.

WordNet ID: oewn-02686183-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɔrɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or associated with heat, especially in scientific or historical contexts.
relating to heat

Examples

The experiment measured the caloric output of the new engine design.
The caloric properties of different materials determine their suitability for insulation.
Scientists in the 18th century believed in a caloric fluid responsible for heat transfer.

Synset Examples

thermal movements of molecules
thermal capacity
thermic energy
the caloric effect of sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal - relating to heat

Etymology

From Latin calor (heat), via French calorique (heat-producing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02825392-a
Synset POS: a
