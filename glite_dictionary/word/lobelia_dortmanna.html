Lobelia dortmanna

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbiːliə dɔrtˈmænə / (AmE), / ləʊˈbiːlɪə dɔːtˈmanə / (BrE)
An aquatic perennial herb native to Europe and North America, with spongy submerged leaves and racemes of blue flowers held above the water.
a perennial aquatic herb with blue flowers

Examples

Lobelia dortmanna is often found in the shallow waters of northern lakes.
Researchers studied the growth habits of Lobelia dortmanna in its natural habitat.
The delicate blue flowers of Lobelia dortmanna bloom above the water’s surface in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lobelia - common English name for Lobelia dortmanna

Etymology

Lobelia: named after Flemish botanist Matthias de l'Obel; dortmanna: after Dutch botanist Dortmann.
