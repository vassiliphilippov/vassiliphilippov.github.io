needy

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdi / (AmE), / ˈniːdi / (BrE)
people who are poor or lacking the basic necessities of life; the poor considered as a group.
poor or disadvantaged people collectively

Examples

The organization donates food and clothes to the needy in the city.
Many charities focus their efforts on helping the needy and homeless.
During the winter, volunteers collect blankets for the needy.

Synset Examples

they try to help the needy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poor - people who have little money or resources

2. ADJ

/ ˈniːdi / (AmE), / ˈniːdi / (BrE)
needing constant attention, affection, or reassurance from others, often excessively so
requiring excessive attention or affection

Examples

She became increasingly needy in the relationship, always asking for reassurance.
His needy behavior made it difficult for him to maintain friendships.
The child was so needy that he constantly demanded attention from his parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clingy - tending to stay very close to someone and depend on them emotionally
  • Antonym: independent - not needing help or reassurance from others

3. ADJ

/ ˈniːdi / (AmE), / ˈniːdi / (BrE)
lacking the basic necessities of life and requiring help from others; poor or impoverished
poor; in need of help

Examples

The charity provides meals to needy families in the city.
Many needy children rely on donations for school supplies and clothing.
Local organizations are raising funds to help needy residents affected by the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient money or resources; very poor
  • Synonym: destitute - extremely poor and lacking the means to provide for oneself
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets

Etymology

From need + -y, first recorded in Middle English.

4. ADJ

/ ˈnidi / (AmE), / ˈniːdi / (BrE)
(of people) lacking the basic necessities of life and therefore needing help or support, especially financial assistance.
lacking necessities; needing help

Examples

The charity distributed blankets and food to the needy during the winter.
Volunteers dedicated their time to helping needy families in the local area.
Government programs are designed to provide assistance for the needy.

Synset Examples

The Jewish community organized a charity event for the needy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destitute - without the basic necessities of life
  • Synonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets

Etymology

From Old English nēodig, related to 'need'.
