cultus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌltəs / (BrE)
a system or practice of religious worship, especially as expressed in ritual and ceremony
religious system of worship and rituals

Examples

The scholar wrote extensively on the cultus of saints in medieval Europe.
Ceremonial objects played a significant role in the ancient cultus.
His research focused on the cultus surrounding the Virgin Mary in Catholic tradition.

Synset Examples

devoted to the cultus of the Blessed Virgin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cult - a system of religious veneration and devotion directed toward a particular figure or object

Etymology

From Latin 'cultus' meaning 'cultivation, worship', from 'colere' (to cultivate, worship).
