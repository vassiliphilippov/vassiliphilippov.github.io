indemnity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
protection or security against possible financial loss, damage, or liability, especially in the form of insurance or contractual obligation.
protection against loss or damage (often financial)
legal

Examples

The contract included a clause providing indemnity against any claims arising from the project.
She took out indemnity insurance to protect her business from potential lawsuits.
The company sought indemnity for the damages caused by the defective product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - arrangement providing compensation for loss or damage

Etymology

From Latin 'indemnitas', from 'indemnis' (unhurt, free from loss or damage).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
legal exemption from liability for damages or loss; protection against possible financial burden or compensation for injury or damage
legal exemption from liability; protection against loss
legal

Examples

The insurance policy provided full indemnity against natural disasters.
Under the contract, the company received indemnity for any losses arising from lawsuits.
The government offered indemnity to healthcare workers during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury
  • Antonym: liability - the state of being legally responsible for something

Etymology

From Latin indemnitas (security, exemption from loss).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
A sum of money paid as compensation for loss, damage, or injury, especially under the terms of an insurance policy or legal contract.
money paid as compensation for loss or injury
legal

Examples

The insurance company paid an indemnity to cover the damages after the fire.
The contract specified an indemnity in case of unforeseen losses.
She received a large indemnity for the injuries sustained in the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone as recompense for loss, injury, or suffering
  • Synonym: damages - money claimed or awarded in compensation for a loss or injury
  • Antonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract

Etymology

From Latin 'indemnitas' (security, exemption from loss), from 'indemnis' (unhurt, free from loss).
