scapulohumeral

1. ADJ

/ ˌskæpjəloʊˈhjuːmərəl / (AmE), / ˌskapyʊləʊˈhjuːmərəl / (BrE)
Relating to the shoulder blade (scapula) and the upper arm bone (humerus).
relating to shoulder blade and upper arm bone
medical

Examples

Rehabilitation focused on restoring scapulohumeral movement after the patient’s shoulder injury.
The rotator cuff is essential for maintaining scapulohumeral stability during arm motion.
A tear in the scapulohumeral muscles can cause significant pain and limit shoulder mobility.

Etymology

From Latin 'scapula' (shoulder blade) and 'humerus' (upper arm bone), with connecting vowel.
