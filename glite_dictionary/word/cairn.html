cairn

1. NOUN

/ kɛrn / (AmE), / kɛːn / (BrE)
a mound of stones built as a memorial or to mark a boundary or path, often found on hills or moorlands
mound of stones marking place or path

Examples

Hikers added a stone to the cairn at the summit to mark their achievement.
A weathered cairn stood by the fork in the mountain path, guiding travelers.
Some ancient cairns serve as memorials for those lost on the moor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monument - a structure erected to commemorate a person or event
  • Synonym: marker - an object serving as a guide, signal, or boundary indicator

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'càrn', meaning 'heap of stones'.
