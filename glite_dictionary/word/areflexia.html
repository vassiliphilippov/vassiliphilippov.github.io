areflexia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛə.rɪˈflɛk.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.rɪˈflɛk.si.ə / (BrE)
Absence of reflex responses, often indicating nerve or neurological damage.
absence of reflex responses (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted areflexia in the patient's knee during the examination.
Areﬂexia can be a symptom of Guillain-Barré syndrome or other neurological disorders.
Sudden onset of areflexia requires immediate medical evaluation to determine its cause.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflex - an automatic response to a stimulus

Etymology

From Greek prefix a- (without) + Latin reflexus (a bending back).
