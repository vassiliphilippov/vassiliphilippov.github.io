to sexualise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something sexual in nature, character, or quality; to attribute sexual characteristics to someone or something
make sexual in nature or character

Examples

The media often sexualise young celebrities, which can have harmful effects on their well-being.
Some critics argue that advertising tends to sexualise ordinary products to attract attention.
In discussing mythology, scholars note how various cultures sexualise their deities.

Synset Examples

The god was sexualized and married to another god
Some languages sexualize all nouns and do not have a neuter gender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sexualize - US spelling of 'sexualise'

Etymology

From 'sexual' + '-ise', with '-ise' forming verbs meaning 'to make or become'.
