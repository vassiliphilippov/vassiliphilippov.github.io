chino

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a strong cotton twill fabric, often used for making trousers and uniforms
cotton twill fabric used for clothes

Examples

He bought a jacket made from durable chino that was perfect for the outdoors.
The school's uniform consists of navy blazers and tan chino trousers.
She prefers chino fabric for her casual summer pants because it's comfortable and sturdy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khaki - a light brown cotton cloth used for uniforms and clothing

Etymology

From Spanish 'chino' (curled, wavy; Chinese), probably referring to the fabric's origins or style.
