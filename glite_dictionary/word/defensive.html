defensive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
intended to protect against attack, danger, or harm, or related to defending against aggression.
protecting against attack or harm

Examples

The army maintained a defensive position throughout the conflict.
She took a defensive approach to the criticism, explaining her decisions calmly.
Modern locks and alarms are examples of defensive measures to secure a building.

Synset Examples

defensive weapons
a defensive stance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offensive - actively attacking or aggressive, as opposed to defensive
  • Synonym: protective - intended to provide protection

Etymology

From Latin defensivus (serving for protection), from defendere (to ward off).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Behaving in a way that shows you feel people are criticizing or attacking you and you want to justify your actions, words, or opinions.
attempting to justify oneself against criticism

Examples

Whenever someone questions his decisions, he becomes quite defensive and tries to explain himself quickly.
She sounded defensive during the interview when asked about the company's mistakes.
There's no need to get defensive; we're just looking for honest feedback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protective - aiming to protect or shield from harm or injury
  • Antonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront

Etymology

From Latin 'defensivus', meaning 'serving to defend'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01636127-s
Synset POS: s
