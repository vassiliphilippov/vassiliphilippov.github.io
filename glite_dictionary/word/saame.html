Saame

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmeɪ / (BrE)
the Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Sami (Lapp) people in northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula
the Sami language of northern Scandinavia

Examples

Many speakers of Saame are working to preserve their language and culture.
He is fluent in both Norwegian and Saame.
The film includes subtitles in both English and Saame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami - the Finno-Ugric language of the Sami people

Etymology

From Finnish 'saame', the native name for the Sami language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.mi / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.mi / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous nomadic people inhabiting northern Scandinavia, traditionally known for reindeer herding
a member of the indigenous Sámi people

Examples

The Saame have lived in northern Scandinavia for thousands of years, preserving unique languages and traditions.
Many Saame families continue the practice of reindeer herding in Norway and Sweden.
She learned about the culture and folklore of the Saame while visiting the Arctic Circle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Laplander - a traditional English term for a member of the Sámi people

Etymology

From the Sámi word 'Sámi', the people's own name in various dialects.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09739429-n
Synset POS: n
