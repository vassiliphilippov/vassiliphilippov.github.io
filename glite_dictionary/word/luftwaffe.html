Luftwaffe

1. PROPN

/ ˈlʊftˌvɑfə / (AmE), / ˈlʊftˌvafə / (BrE)
the air force of Germany, especially during the Nazi period (1935–1945), or more generally any German air force
German air force, esp. WWII

Examples

The Luftwaffe launched a massive bombing campaign against London during the Blitz.
He studied the tactics employed by the Luftwaffe in World War II.
The Allied forces were constantly on alert for raids by the Luftwaffe.

Etymology

German, from 'Luft' (air) + 'Waffe' (weapon, force)
