uncoordinated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkoʊˈɔrdəˌneɪtəd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkɔːˈdɪnətɪd / (BrE)
lacking skill in controlling movements so that actions are clumsy or awkward
lacking coordination in movement

Examples

After the surgery, his uncoordinated steps made walking difficult.
The puppy was still uncoordinated and often stumbled as it played.
Her movements became more uncoordinated as she grew tired.

Synset Examples

his movements are uncoordinated
an uncoordinated toddler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things
  • Antonym: coordinated - having harmonious and efficient movement or organization

Etymology

From un- (not) + coordinated.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkoʊˈɔrdəˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of plans, actions, or groups) lacking organization or cooperative planning, resulting in poor efficiency or conflicting outcomes.
lacking organization or cooperative planning

Examples

Because of uncoordinated efforts between departments, the project suffered multiple delays.
The festival was marred by uncoordinated planning, causing schedules to overlap and attendees to miss events.
Uncoordinated responses from emergency teams led to confusion at the scene.

Synset Examples

uncoordinated scheduling often resulted in conflicting games

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coordinated - well organized and planned working effectively together

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'coordinated', meaning not organized or harmonized.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01675642-s
Synset POS: s
