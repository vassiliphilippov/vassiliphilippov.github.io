Dendroica

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdroʊɪkə / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɔɪkə / (BrE)
A former genus of small songbirds in the family Parulidae, known as New World warblers.
genus of New World warblers

Examples

Several species formerly classified under Dendroica are now placed in the genus Setophaga.
The Dendroica warblers were extensively studied by ornithologists in North America.
Bird-watchers often referred to the distinctive plumage of the Dendroica group.

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + oikos (dwelling), referring to the tree-dwelling habits of species in this group.

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdrɔɪkə / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɔɪkə / (BrE)
A former genus of North American wood warblers in the family Parulidae, now usually included in the genus Setophaga.
a former genus of wood warblers

Examples

Several species once classified in Dendroica are now part of the genus Setophaga.
The magnolia warbler was formerly known as Dendroica magnolia.
Ornithologists revised the classification of Dendroica based on new genetic evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Setophaga - genus now including species formerly placed in Dendroica

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + oikos (house), referring to their tree-dwelling habits.
