pastureland

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.tʃər.lænd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tʃə.lænd / (BrE)
land covered with grass or vegetation suitable for grazing livestock.
land for grazing animals

Examples

The farmer led his cows out to the pastureland every morning at sunrise.
After years of drought, the pastureland was lush and green once again.
They purchased several acres of pastureland to expand their sheep farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grassland, often for hay or grazing

Etymology

Formed from 'pasture' + 'land'; 'pasture' from Latin 'pastura' (grazing).
