gula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːlə / (AmE), / ˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) excessive eating or gluttony, traditionally counted as one of the Seven Deadly Sins.
excessive eating; a deadly sin

Examples

In medieval texts, gula is frequently depicted as a monstrous figure indulging at a table.
The preacher warned his congregation against gula, emphasizing its dangers to the soul.
In Dante's Divine Comedy, gula is punished severely among the damned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating; a deadly sin

Etymology

From Latin *gula* (throat, gluttony).
