Cycadophytina

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˌdoʊfəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkəʊdəʊfɪˈtʌɪnə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of palmlike gymnosperm plants that includes both surviving cycads (order Cycadales) and several extinct orders, sometimes considered a class (Cycadopsida) or a subdivision (Cycadophytina or Cycadophyta) in plant classification systems.
taxonomic group of palmlike gymnosperms

Examples

Fossils of Cycadophytina have been found in rocks dating back to the Permian period.
Botanists debate whether Cycadophytina represents a natural evolutionary group or a form-based classification.
The garden features several living representatives of Cycadophytina, including rare cycads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cycadales - an order of surviving cycads, palmlike gymnosperms
  • Synonym: Cycadophyta - alternative name for the plant subdivision of cycads

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Cycad-' (from Modern Latin Cycas) and Greek 'phyton' (plant), with scientific taxonomic suffixes.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌkædəˈfaɪtɪnə / (AmE), / saɪˌkadəˈfʌɪtɪnə / (BrE)
a botanical subdivision comprising palmlike gymnosperms, including both extant cycads (order Cycadales) and several extinct groups; sometimes regarded as a class (Cycadopsida) or a subdivision within the plant kingdom
botanical group of palmlike gymnosperms

Examples

Many extinct plants once classified under Cycadophytina played an important role in prehistoric ecosystems.
The Cycadophytina are often recognized for their similarities to modern-day cycads.
Botanists debate whether Cycadophytina should be considered a class or a subdivision in plant taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a type of palmlike gymnosperm belonging to the Cycadales order

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Cycadophyta', based on 'cycad' + Greek 'phyton' (plant).
