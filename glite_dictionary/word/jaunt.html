jaunt

1. VERB

/ dʒɑnt / (AmE), / dʒɑːnt / (BrE)
to go on a short journey or excursion for pleasure
go on a short pleasure trip

Examples

We decided to jaunt off to the seaside for the weekend.
They often jaunt around Europe during the summer holidays.
Last spring, she jaunted through the countryside, stopping at every small village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip for pleasure
  • Antonym: commute - to travel regularly to and from work

Etymology

Probably from earlier noun 'jaunt', such as a tiresome journey, origin uncertain, possibly from Old French 'jointer' (to join).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɑnt / (AmE), / dʒɑːnt / (BrE)
a short journey or excursion taken for pleasure
pleasure trip or excursion

Examples

They decided to take a quick jaunt to the countryside over the weekend.
Our afternoon jaunt through the market was the highlight of the trip.
A spontaneous jaunt to the beach helped them relax after a busy week.

Synset Examples

many summer excursions to the shore
it was merely a pleasure trip
after cautious sashays into the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey, usually for leisure
  • Synonym: trip - a journey or outing from place to place
  • Degree Higher: expedition - a journey with a particular purpose

Etymology

Probably from obsolete French 'jaunder'/'jointer' (to tire a horse), later meaning 'to tire oneself', then 'to make a trip'.

Synset POS: n
