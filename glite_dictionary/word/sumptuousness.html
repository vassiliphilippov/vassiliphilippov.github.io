sumptuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmp.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌmp.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being impressively luxurious, costly, or magnificent
quality of being luxurious or lavish

Examples

The sumptuousness of the palace left every visitor in awe.
He was drawn to the sumptuousness of the fabrics used in the designer's collection.
Despite its sumptuousness, the hotel felt warm and inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury - a state of great comfort or elegance, especially when involving great expense
  • Antonym: plainness - lack of decoration or luxury; simplicity

Etymology

From Latin sumptuosus (costly, expensive), from sumptus (cost, expense).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmp.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌmp.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rich, luxurious, and impressive, especially as shown in surroundings or lifestyle
luxurious quality or opulence

Examples

The sumptuousness of the palace left all the visitors in awe.
She admired the sumptuousness of the silk draperies and golden chandeliers.
Despite his wealth, he preferred simplicity over sumptuousness in his home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury - a state of great comfort and extravagant living
  • Synonym: opulence - great wealth or luxuriousness
  • Antonym: austerity - plain and simple quality or lifestyle, absence of luxury

Etymology

From Latin 'sumptuosus' (expensive, costly) via Old French 'somptueux' and Middle English.
