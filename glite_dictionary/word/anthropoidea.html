Anthropoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌænθrəˈpɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A taxonomic infraorder of primates that includes monkeys, apes, and humans.
infraorder of primates (monkeys, apes, humans)

Examples

The infraorder Anthropoidea comprises the higher primates, such as monkeys and apes.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationships within Anthropoidea based on genetic evidence.
The study of Anthropoidea provides insights into human evolution and behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human being) and '-eidos' (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌænθrəˈpɔɪdiə / (BrE)
a suborder of primates that includes monkeys, apes, and humans; also known as the higher primates
suborder of higher primates
medical

Examples

The Anthropoidea include all the higher primates, such as monkeys and apes.
Fossil discoveries have shed light on the evolution of the Anthropoidea.
Taxonomists debate the exact boundaries of the Anthropoidea and their relation to other primates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Strepsirrhini - suborder of primates including lemurs and lorises

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) + '-oidēs' (like, resembling), meaning 'human-like.'
