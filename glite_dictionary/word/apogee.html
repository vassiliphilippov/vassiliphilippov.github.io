apogee

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpədʒi / (AmE), / ˈapədʒi / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a satellite where it is farthest from the Earth
the farthest orbital point from Earth

Examples

The satellite reached its apogee before beginning its descent to a lower orbit.
At apogee, the spacecraft was more than 35,000 kilometers from the Earth's surface.
Engineers timed the data transmission to occur when the probe was at apogee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perigee - the point in a satellite's orbit closest to Earth

Etymology

From French *apogée*, from Latin *apogaeum*, from Greek *apogaion* (away from Earth), from *apo-* (away) + *gaia* (Earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæp.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈap.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the highest or most climactic point in the development of something; the culmination or peak
highest point; culmination

Examples

The artist reached the apogee of her career with her latest exhibition.
Many believe that classical music hit its apogee in the 18th century.
At the apogee of the celebration, fireworks lit up the night sky.

Synset Examples

their achievements stand as a culmination of centuries of development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zenith - the time at which something is most powerful or successful; peak
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point in the fortunes of a person or organization

Etymology

From French *apogée*, from Latin *apogaeum*, from Greek *apogeion* (away from the earth), originally an astronomical term.
