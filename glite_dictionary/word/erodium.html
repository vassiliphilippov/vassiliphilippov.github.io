Erodium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊdiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the geranium family, found especially in Europe, South America, and Australia, often in mountainous regions.
a genus of geranium-like flowering plants

Examples

The alpine garden featured several species of Erodium thriving among the stones.
Botanists discovered a rare Erodium variety in the hills of southern Spain.
With its delicate pink flowers, Erodium adds charm to rocky landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a genus of flowering plants related to Erodium

Etymology

From Greek *erodios* (heron), referring to the beak-like fruit.
