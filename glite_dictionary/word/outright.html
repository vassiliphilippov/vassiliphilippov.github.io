outright

1. ADV

/ ˈaʊtˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtraɪt / (BrE)
(adverb) Without restrictions, conditions, or further payments; completely and immediately.
without restrictions or further payments

Examples

She decided to outright purchase the car instead of leasing it.
The company bought the property outright, so there are no monthly payments.
He paid for the house outright, avoiding any mortgage.

Synset Examples

buy outright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; fully
  • Antonym: partially - not completely; in part

3. ADV

/ ˈaʊtˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.raɪt / (BrE)
in a manner that is complete, total, or without reservation; completely or thoroughly.
completely; thoroughly

Examples

The company rejected the proposal outright and refused to discuss it further.
She denied the accusation outright, leaving no room for doubt.
He is outright dishonest, not just careless or mistaken.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - in every way or as much as possible
  • Antonym: partially - to some extent but not completely

Etymology

From Middle English outriht, from out + right, meaning 'completely right or direct'.
