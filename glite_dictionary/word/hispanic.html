Hispanic

1. ADJ

/ hɪˈspænɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈspanɪk / (BrE)
relating to Spanish-speaking people or their culture, especially those of Latin American origin living in the United States
relating to Spanish-speaking people or culture

Examples

The city hosts a vibrant Hispanic festival every spring to celebrate local culture.
Many Hispanic artists have contributed to the nation’s music and visual arts scene.
There is an increasing demand for bilingual teachers in Hispanic communities.

Synset Examples

the Hispanic population of California is growing rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latino - relating to people of Latin American origin, especially in the US

Etymology

From Latin Hispanicus, meaning 'of Spain', from Hispania (Spain).

WordNet ID: oewn-03083586-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈspænɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈspænɪk / (BrE)
An American, especially in the United States, whose first language is Spanish or who has origins in a Spanish-speaking country.
Spanish-speaking American person

Examples

Many Hispanics in the United States maintain strong connections to their cultural heritage.
The city hosts an annual festival celebrating Hispanic traditions and food.
As a Hispanic, she grew up speaking both Spanish and English at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latino - A person of Latin American origin or descent, especially in the U.S.

Etymology

From Spanish 'hispánico', from Latin 'Hispanicus', meaning 'of or from Spain or Hispania'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10649009-n
Synset POS: n
