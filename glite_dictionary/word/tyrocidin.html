tyrocidin

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈrɑːsɪdɪn / (AmE), / tɪəˈrɒsɪdɪn / (BrE)
a basic polypeptide antibiotic obtained from a soil bacterium, forming a major component of the antibiotic tyrothricin
a polypeptide antibiotic from soil bacteria
medical

Examples

The discovery of tyrocidin marked a significant advance in antibiotic therapy.
Researchers isolated tyrocidin from cultures of Bacillus brevis found in soil.
Tyrocidin is active against many gram-positive bacteria but can be toxic at higher doses.

Etymology

From Greek 'tyros' (cheese) and 'kinein' (to move), relating to its source and discovery.
