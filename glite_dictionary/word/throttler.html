throttler

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːtlər / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtlə / (BrE)
a person who kills or attempts to kill by strangling
someone who kills by strangling

Examples

The police believed the murderer to be a notorious throttler known for attacking victims at night.
Jack the Ripper was not a throttler, but other criminals in Victorian London used strangling as their method.
Detectives finally caught the throttler after weeks of terrifying the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler - a person who strangles someone, especially to death

Etymology

From the verb 'throttle' (to strangle) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
