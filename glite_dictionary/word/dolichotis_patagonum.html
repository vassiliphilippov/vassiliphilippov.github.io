Dolichotis patagonum

1. NOUN

/ doʊˌlɪkoʊˈtaɪs ˌpætəˈɡoʊnəm / (AmE), / dəʊˌlɪkəʊˈtaɪs ˌpætəˈɡəʊnəm / (BrE)
A large hare-like rodent native to the Pampas of Argentina, also known as the Patagonian mara.
large rodent of Argentina, Patagonian mara

Examples

The Dolichotis patagonum is well known for its long legs and rabbit-like appearance.
Unlike most rodents, the Dolichotis patagonum often pairs for life and raises young together.
Many zoos now include the Dolichotis patagonum in their South American animal exhibits.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Dolichotis (from Greek dolikhos, meaning 'long', and ous, ōtos, meaning 'ear') and the specific epithet patagonum referring to Patagonia.
