trend

1. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
a general direction in which something is developing or changing, especially a gradual change in opinions, behavior, or conditions
general direction of change or development

Examples

There is a clear trend towards remote work in many industries today.
The election results followed a recent trend of increasing support for environmental policies.
Fashion trends often reflect changes in social attitudes and values.

Synset Examples

not openly liberal but that is the trend of the book
a broad movement of the electorate to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendency - an inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
  • Synonym: pattern - a regular or repetitive form, order, or arrangement
  • Antonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected

Etymology

From Old English trendan (to revolve, roll) and Old Norse trenda (to roll, move in a circle).

2. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
a general direction in which something is developing or changing, especially a popular style or taste at a particular time
prevailing style or tendency

Examples

Wearing vintage clothes has become a fashionable trend among young people.
Social media has a huge influence on how trends start and spread worldwide.
The trend for open-plan offices has changed the way companies design their workspaces.

Synset Examples

leather is the latest vogue
he followed current trends
the 1920s had a style of their own

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fad - something that is very popular for a short time
  • Synonym: vogue - the prevailing fashion or style at a particular time
  • Antonym: tradition - a long-established custom or belief

Etymology

From Old English 'trendan' (to move or turn round); related to Middle Low German 'trenden'.

5. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
the general direction in which something, especially a physical feature or line, tends to move or develop
general direction of movement or development

Examples

The trend of the river quickly shifts to the southwest after the mountains.
Geologists studied the trend of the fault line running through the region.
The coastline shows a distinct northeastern trend for several miles.

Synset Examples

the river takes a southern course
the northeastern trend of the coast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direction - the course along which someone or something moves
  • Synonym: course - the path or direction followed by something or someone

Etymology

From Old English 'trand' (turn, revolving), influenced by Middle English 'trenden' (to revolve).
