ten

1. NUM

/ tɛn / (AmE), / tɛn / (BrE)
the number equivalent to the sum of nine and one; 10.
number, one more than nine

Examples

She has exactly ten books on her desk.
The concert starts at ten o'clock sharp.
He counted to ten before opening his eyes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nine - the number equivalent to one less than ten; 9
  • Degree Higher: eleven - the number equivalent to one more than ten; 11

Etymology

From Old English 'tien', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'tien' and German 'zehn'.

6. NOUN

/ tɛn / (AmE), / tɛn / (BrE)
A playing card with ten pips, being one of four such cards in a standard deck, one in each suit.
playing card with ten pips

Examples

He drew the ten of clubs on his first turn.
I needed a ten to complete my straight in poker.
There are four tens in every standard deck of cards.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: jack - playing card ranking above ten
  • Degree Lower: nine - playing card ranking below ten
