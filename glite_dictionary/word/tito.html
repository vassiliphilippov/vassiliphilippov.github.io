Tito

1. PROPN

/ ˈtiːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtiːtəʊ / (BrE)
(1892–1980) Yugoslav statesman who led the resistance to German occupation during World War II and established a communist state after the war.
Yugoslav leader and statesman (1892–1980)
american british

Examples

Tito was a key figure in uniting Yugoslavia during a turbulent period in European history.
Many Yugoslavs admired Tito for his role in resisting fascist occupation.
After World War II, Tito established a unique form of communism independent from the Soviet Union.

Etymology

Adopted name of Josip Broz (from Croatian nickname 'Tito').
