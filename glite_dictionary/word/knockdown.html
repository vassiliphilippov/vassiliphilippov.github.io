knockdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.daʊn / (BrE)
an act of knocking an opponent to the ground with a blow, especially in boxing or martial arts
an act of knocking opponent down (boxing)

Examples

The boxer scored a spectacular knockdown in the second round.
After the knockdown, the referee began the count.
Despite suffering a knockdown, he managed to get back on his feet and continue fighting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knockout - a blow that renders an opponent unconscious

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of furniture) designed to be easily assembled and dismantled, often for ease of transport or storage.
easily assembled and dismantled furniture

Examples

The bed came in a knockdown package that I assembled in under an hour.
We bought a knockdown wardrobe to save on delivery costs.
Many students prefer knockdown furniture because it's easy to move between apartments.

Synset Examples

I bought a knockdown chest at the do-it-yourself store

Etymology

From the verb 'knock down', referring to taking apart or disassembling.
