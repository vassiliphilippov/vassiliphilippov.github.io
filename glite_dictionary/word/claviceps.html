Claviceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈklævɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈklævɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic fungi that infect the ovaries of various grasses, especially cereals such as rye, producing ergot
genus of parasitic fungi on grasses

Examples

Claviceps species are notorious for causing ergotism when their toxic compounds contaminate grain.
Researchers are studying Claviceps to better understand how it infects cereal crops.
A field infected with Claviceps can suffer severe losses due to reduced yield and contaminated harvest.

Etymology

From Latin *clava* (club) and *caput* (head), referring to the club-shaped fruiting body.
