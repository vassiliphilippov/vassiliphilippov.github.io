topminnow

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpˌmɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of Central America, often with a long swordlike tail, popular in aquariums
small freshwater aquarium fish

Examples

The topminnow is a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts for its bright colors and unique tail.
Several species of topminnow can adapt well to home tanks if the water quality is maintained.
A male topminnow often develops an elongated tail fin during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish, often used as bait or kept in aquariums

Etymology

Formed from 'top' + 'minnow', indicating a minnow that swims near the surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpˌmɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a small, usually brightly colored fish of warm fresh or brackish waters that swims near the surface and is sometimes used to control mosquito populations
small surface-feeding fish, often for mosquito control

Examples

The pond was filled with topminnows darting across the surface.
Scientists released topminnows into the marsh to help reduce the mosquito population.
Children watched in fascination as a brightly colored topminnow swam just beneath the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish, often used as bait

Etymology

Formed from 'top' + 'minnow', reflecting the fish's habit of swimming near the water's surface.
