Solenidae

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlɛnɪˌdi / (AmE), / səˈlɛ.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks known as razor clams, characterized by elongated, laterally compressed shells.
family of razor clams (bivalves)

Examples

The Solenidae are commonly found buried in sandy coastal areas.
Several species within the Solenidae family are harvested for food.
Marine biologists study Solenidae to better understand the habitat needs of razor clams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor clam - a long, narrow bivalve mollusk of the Solenidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Solen (a genus of mollusks) and the suffix -idae (denoting animal families).
