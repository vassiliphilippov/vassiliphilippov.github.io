unscrupulousness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈskruːpjələsnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruːpjʊləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing no moral principles; shameless dishonesty
lack of moral principles

Examples

The unscrupulousness of the businessman was exposed during the investigation.
Her unscrupulousness soon became apparent in the way she manipulated the facts.
Political scandals often reveal the unscrupulousness of those in power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty; tendency to lie or cheat
