filiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the relationship between a child and their parents, especially as legally recognized or established
relationship between child and parents
legal

Examples

The court required a DNA test to determine the filiation of the child.
In matters of inheritance, the filiation of heirs plays a crucial role.
Anthropologists study filiation patterns to understand family structures in different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paternity - the state of being a father; fatherhood
  • Synonym: maternity - the state of being a mother; motherhood

Etymology

From Latin filiātion-, filiātiō (a borrowing from French filiation), based on filius 'son'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the inherited characteristics or properties shared with others within one's bloodline or lineage
inherited traits from one's bloodline

Examples

The family's filiation could be traced back to the kings of ancient France.
Genetic studies reveal a clear filiation among the various members of the royal family.
He prided himself on the noble filiation that linked him to famous ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor
  • Antonym: adoption - legal process of taking another's child as one's own

Etymology

From Latin filiatio (relationship of a son), from filius (son).
