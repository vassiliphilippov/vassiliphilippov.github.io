cataplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌplazəm / (BrE)
a soft, moist preparation of meal, clay, or other materials, applied hot to the body to relieve inflammation or pain; a poultice.
soft, hot medical dressing; poultice
medical archaic

Examples

The doctor recommended a cataplasm to ease the swelling on his leg.
Applying a warm cataplasm helped relieve her chest congestion during the winter illness.
In olden times, people often used a clay cataplasm to treat bruises and sprains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poultice - a soft, moist mass applied to the body to relieve soreness

Etymology

From Latin 'cataplasma' (poultice), from Greek 'kataplasma' (application, poultice).
