Magnolia soulangiana

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliə suːlɑːnˈdʒiːənə / (AmE), / maɡˌnəʊlɪə suːlænˈdʒɪənə / (BrE)
A large deciduous shrub or small tree with large rosy to purplish flowers, native to Asia and widely grown as an ornamental.
ornamental shrub or small tree with rosy flowers

Examples

The Magnolia soulangiana in our garden bloomed spectacularly this spring.
Many city parks have planted Magnolia soulangiana for its showy spring flowers.
Gardeners prize Magnolia soulangiana for its stunning rosy and purple blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saucer magnolia - common name for Magnolia soulangiana

Etymology

Named for French botanist Étienne Soulange-Bodin; 'Magnolia' honors botanist Pierre Magnol.
