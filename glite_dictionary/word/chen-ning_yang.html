Chen-Ning Yang

1. PROPN

/ tʃɛn nɪŋ jɑŋ / (AmE), / tʃɛn nɪŋ jæŋ / (BrE)
(1922– ) Chinese-American physicist noted for his work in particle physics, particularly for disproving the conservation of parity with Tsung Dao Lee.
Chinese-American physicist, Nobel laureate

Examples

Chen-Ning Yang won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1957 for his work on parity violation.
Many universities have honored Chen-Ning Yang for his groundbreaking contributions to theoretical physics.
As a young scientist, Chen-Ning Yang moved from China to the United States to pursue further research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tsung Dao Lee - Chinese-American physicist who collaborated with Yang in disproving parity conservation
