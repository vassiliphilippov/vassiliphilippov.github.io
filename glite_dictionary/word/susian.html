Susian

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːziən / (AmE), / ˈsuːziən / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language spoken by the Elamites in the region of Elam (present-day southwestern Iran), with unknown linguistic affinities.
extinct language of ancient Elam

Examples

Linguists have struggled to classify Susian due to its unknown origins.
Ancient inscriptions in Susian have provided valuable information about Elamite civilization.
Few scholars today can read the ancient language known as Susian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elamite - the extinct language spoken by the ancient Elamites in Iran

Etymology

From Latin 'Susiana', the land of Susa, an ancient city in Elam.
