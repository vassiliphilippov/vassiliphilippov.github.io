extinguisher

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃə / (BrE)
a portable device for putting out small fires, typically by ejecting a substance such as water, foam, or a chemical
device for putting out small fires

Examples

Please locate the nearest extinguisher in case of emergency.
The kitchen should always have an extinguisher mounted on the wall.
He used the extinguisher to put out the small fire before help arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire extinguisher - a specific extinguisher designed to put out fires

Etymology

From Latin extinguere (to quench, put out) via English 'extinguish' and '-er' suffix.
