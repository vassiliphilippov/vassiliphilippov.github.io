Guinness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪnɪs / (BrE)
a strong, dark Irish stout beer originally brewed in Dublin, known for its creamy head and distinctive taste.
a famous Irish stout beer

Examples

Would you like a pint of Guinness with your meal?
The pub is famous for serving the best Guinness in town.
He toured the Guinness brewery while visiting Dublin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stout - strong, dark beer

Etymology

Named after Arthur Guinness, the founder of the brewery in Dublin, Ireland (1759).
