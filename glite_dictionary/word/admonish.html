to admonish

1. VERB

/ ədˈmɑːnɪʃ / (AmE), / ədˈmɒnɪʃ / (BrE)
to advise or warn someone firmly, especially to correct or improve their behavior
warn or advise firmly

Examples

The manager had to admonish her team for missing several important deadlines.
She gently admonished the children to speak quietly in the library.
He was admonishing his friend for taking unnecessary risks.

Synset Examples

I warned him not to go too far
I warn you against false assumptions
She warned him to be quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warn - give notice or advice of possible harm or danger
  • Synonym: reprimand - express sharp disapproval or criticism of someone
  • Antonym: praise - express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'admonester', from Latin 'admonere' (to warn, remind).

3. VERB

/ ədˈmɑnɪʃ / (AmE), / ədˈmɒnɪʃ / (BrE)
to warn or reprimand someone firmly, especially to correct or caution against improper behavior
warn or reprimand firmly

Examples

The teacher admonished the students for talking during the exam.
Her manager admonished her for being late to the meeting again.
He gently admonished his friend to take better care of himself.

Synset Examples

He admonished the child for his bad behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin admonere, from ad- (to) + monere (warn).
