accumulator

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
A device, especially a rechargeable battery, that stores electrical energy for later use.
device for storing electrical energy

Examples

The electrician replaced the old accumulator with a new one to store power more efficiently.
Hybrid cars often use an accumulator to store energy generated during braking.
Some early radios were powered by a lead-acid accumulator that needed regular recharging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device that stores and supplies electrical energy

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulare' meaning 'to heap up'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkjumjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
(computing) a register in a computer processor that stores intermediate arithmetic and logic results, especially by adding values to its contents.
computer register that accumulates values

Examples

The CPU's accumulator stores the result of each addition during the calculation.
Instructions are executed sequentially, updating the accumulator with new values each time.
If the accumulator overflows, an error flag may be set by the processor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - a small amount of fast storage in a computer processor

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulare' meaning 'to heap up, accumulate.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02675932-n
Synset POS: n
