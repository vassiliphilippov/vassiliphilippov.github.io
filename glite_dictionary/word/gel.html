gel

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛl / (AmE), / dʒɛl / (BrE)
a thin, translucent colored sheet placed over stage lights to produce different color effects
colored filter for stage lights

Examples

The lighting technician carefully mounted a blue gel over the spotlight.
After changing the gel, the stage was bathed in a soft red glow.
Each scene called for a different color gel to create the right mood.

/ dʒɛl / (AmE), / dʒɛl / (BrE)
to become a gel; to change from a liquid or sol to a semi-solid or jelly-like state
become a gel; turn semi-solid

Examples

As the solution cooled, it began to gel and form a firm structure.
The mixture needs to gel completely before you can unmold it.

3. NOUN

/ dʒɛl / (AmE), / dʒɛl / (BrE)

jelly-like hair styling substance

Examples

I use gel every morning to keep my hair in place.
He applied a generous amount of gel for a slicked-back look.
This gel holds even the thickest hair styles all day.

4. NOUN

/ dʒɛl / (AmE), / dʒɛl / (BrE)
A semi-solid, jelly-like substance in which a liquid is dispersed in a solid network, more solid than a sol.
semi-solid, jelly-like substance

Examples

The scientist poured the solution into a mold, and soon it formed a gel within minutes.
Hair styling products often come in the form of a gel that holds shapes in place.
The enzyme moved slowly through the gel during electrophoresis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sol - a colloidal solution, more liquid than a gel

Etymology

From Latin 'gelu' (frost, ice), related to freezing or solidifying.
