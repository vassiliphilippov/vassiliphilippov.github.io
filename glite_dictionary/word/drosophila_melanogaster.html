Drosophila melanogaster

1. NOUN

/ droʊˌsɒfɪlə məˌlænəˈɡæstər / (AmE), / drəʊˌsɒfɪlə mɪˌlanəˈɡæstə / (BrE)
a species of small fruit fly widely used in genetic research as a model organism
a common fruit fly species used in genetics

Examples

Drosophila melanogaster has been a vital model organism in genetics for over a century.
Many breakthroughs in inheritance and gene mapping came from studies using Drosophila melanogaster.
Geneticists often choose Drosophila melanogaster owing to its short life cycle and easily observable mutations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - a small fly of the family Drosophilidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Drosophila' (the genus, from Greek drosos 'dew' + phila 'loving') and 'melanogaster' (from Greek melano- 'black' + gastēr 'belly').
