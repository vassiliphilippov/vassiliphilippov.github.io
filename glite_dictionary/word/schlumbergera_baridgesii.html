Schlumbergera baridgesii

1. NOUN

/ ʃlʌmˈbɜrɡərə bɑːˈrɪdʒiːsaɪ / (AmE), / ʃlʌmˈbɜːɡərə bærɪdˈʒiːsaɪ / (BrE)
An epiphytic cactus native to Brazil, widely grown as a houseplant, with jointed flat stems and rose-purple flowers that bloom in winter.
Brazilian houseplant cactus with winter-blooming flowers

Examples

The Schlumbergera baridgesii adds a burst of color to the living room during the winter months.
Many plant enthusiasts enjoy growing Schlumbergera baridgesii for its unique jointed stems and beautiful flowers.
With proper care, the Schlumbergera baridgesii can bloom every year around the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas cactus - A popular houseplant with jointed stems and pink-purple flowers, blooming in winter, sometimes used as a common name for Schlumbergera species.

Etymology

Genus name from Frédéric Schlumberger, a French cactus collector; species epithet baridgesii from a plant collector's surname.
