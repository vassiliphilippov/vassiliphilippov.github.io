girasol

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪr.ə.sɑl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪə.rə.sɒl / (BrE)
An opal gemstone characterized by its fiery display of orange, yellow, and red colors.
a type of opal with fiery colors

Examples

The jeweler displayed a rare girasol that shimmered with intense red and orange hues.
A girasol ring makes a striking addition to any gemstone collection.
Collectors admire the unique play of color in each girasol opal they find.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opal - a mineral or gemstone with an iridescent sheen

Etymology

From French 'girasol', from Italian 'girasole' meaning 'turning toward the sun'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrəˌsɒl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərəˌsɒl / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant (Helianthus tuberosus) with hairy stems and leaves, widely cultivated for its edible, knobby tubers; also known as Jerusalem artichoke.
a plant grown for edible tubers, Jerusalem artichoke

Examples

The farmer harvested several girasol tubers for the local market.
Roasted girasol makes a delicious alternative to potatoes.
You can plant girasol in early spring for a fall harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem artichoke - another common name for Helianthus tuberosus, the girasol

Etymology

From Spanish 'girasol' (sunflower), from 'girar' (to turn) + 'sol' (sun), originally referring to sunflower.
