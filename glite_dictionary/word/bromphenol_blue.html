bromphenol blue

1. NOUN

/ broʊmˌfinɒl ˈbluː / (AmE), / brəʊmˌfiːnɒl ˈbluː / (BrE)
a dye that is used as an acid-base indicator, especially with a pH range between 3.0 and 4.6
dye used as acid-base indicator

Examples

In the laboratory, bromphenol blue was used to monitor the progress of gel electrophoresis.
The solution turned yellow because bromphenol blue indicated an acidic pH.
Adding bromphenol blue to the mixture helped the scientist determine when neutralization occurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methyl orange - a common acid-base indicator dye
  • Synonym: litmus - a water-soluble mixture of different dyes used as pH indicator

Etymology

From bromo- (relating to bromine), phenol, and blue (its color in alkaline solution).
