alleviatory

1. ADJ

/ əˈliːviəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈliːviətəri / (BrE)
Reducing pain or sorrow; making suffering or discomfort easier to bear.
making pain or sorrow easier to bear

Examples

The doctor prescribed an alleviatory treatment to ease the patient's chronic pain.
Her kind words had an alleviatory effect on his grief after the loss.
Various alleviatory methods can help manage stress during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleviating - reducing pain or distress
  • Antonym: to exacerbating - making pain or discomfort worse

Etymology

From the Latin 'alleviatus', past participle of 'alleviare' (to lighten), from 'ad-' (to) + 'levis' (light).
