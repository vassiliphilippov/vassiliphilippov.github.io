Chaldea

1. PROPN

/ kælˈdiə / (AmE), / kælˈdiːə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in southern Mesopotamia, corresponding roughly to southern Babylonia, notable in biblical and historical contexts
ancient kingdom in southern Mesopotamia

Examples

Many biblical stories are set in Chaldea, emphasizing its importance in ancient history.
Archaeologists continue to uncover evidence of urban life in Chaldea.
The city of Babylon was once the capital of Chaldea.

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaea, from Greek Chaldaia, from Akkadian Kaldu or Kasdu.

2. PROPN

/ kælˈdiːə / (AmE), / kalˈdiːə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southern Mesopotamia, located between the lower Euphrates River and the Persian Gulf, notable for its powerful New Babylonian dynasty under Nebuchadnezzar II
ancient region of southern Mesopotamia

Examples

Many historians study Chaldea for its contributions to astronomy and mathematics.
Nebuchadnezzar II ruled Chaldea during its greatest period of power.
The ruins of Chaldea lie in present-day southern Iraq.

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaea, from Greek Chaldaia, from Akkadian Kaldu.
