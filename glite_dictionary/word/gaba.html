GABA

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæbə / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbə / (BrE)
an amino acid in the central nervous system that acts as the main inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
medical

Examples

The medication increases levels of GABA to help calm the nervous system.
Low GABA activity has been linked to anxiety disorders.
Researchers are studying how GABA influences sleep and relaxation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotransmitter - chemical substance that transmits nerve impulses
  • Antonym: glutamate - major excitatory neurotransmitter in the brain

Etymology

Acronym from gamma-aminobutyric acid.
