to symmetrize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɪˌtraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmɪˌtraɪz / (BrE)
to make or become symmetric; to arrange or alter so as to achieve symmetry
make or become symmetric

Examples

The artist tried to symmetrize the design to achieve a balanced look.
In mathematics, we often symmetrize equations to simplify the analysis.
The program automatically symmetrizes the data before processing it.

Synset Examples

symmetrized waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetrical - having or showing symmetry
  • Antonym: asymmetrical - not having symmetry; lacking balance

Etymology

From symmetry + -ize, patterned after similar English coinages from Greek-derived roots.
