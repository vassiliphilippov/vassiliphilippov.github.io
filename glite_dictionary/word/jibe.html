jibe

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
an insulting or mocking remark intended to hurt or criticize someone
insulting or mocking remark

Examples

He couldn't help but respond to the jibe with a sharp retort of his own.
Her speech was full of subtle jibes aimed at her political opponent.
Despite their jibes, she remained calm and refused to react.

Synset Examples

his parting shot was ‘drop dead’
she threw shafts of sarcasm
she takes a dig at me every chance she gets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - an unkind or sarcastic remark
  • Synonym: gibe - an insulting or sneering remark
  • Synonym: taunt - a remark made to provoke or mock someone

2. VERB

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
(of a sail or a sailing vessel) to shift suddenly from one side to the other when sailing with the wind, or to cause this to happen.
to shift a sail to the other side (nautical)

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to jibe the mainsail as the wind changed direction.
If you jibe too quickly, you risk losing control of the boom.
They practiced jibing in calm waters before attempting the open sea.

Synset Examples

The sail jibbed wildly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gybe - to shift a sail from one side to the other, especially when running before the wind

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'gybe', from Middle English 'gyben' (to shift a sail).

3. VERB

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
to be in agreement, to match, or to be compatible with something
be in agreement, match

Examples

Her account of the story didn't jibe with the facts uncovered in the investigation.
The numbers in your report don't jibe with those in the official records.
His experience doesn't jibe with my understanding of how the process works.

Synset Examples

The two stories don't agree in many details
The handwriting checks with the signature on the form
The suspect's fingerprints don't match those on the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion or to be consistent
  • Synonym: to coincide - to happen at the same time or correspond in nature
  • Antonym: conflict - to be incompatible or at odds
Source: WordNet
