hormism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhɔːmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A theory in psychology that emphasizes purposive or goal-directed factors as the driving force in behavior.
psychological theory of purposive behavior

Examples

His research was founded on hormism, emphasizing the importance of goal-directed action.
Modern psychologists often contrast hormism with behaviorism in discussions of motivation.
Courses in the history of psychology may discuss the principles of hormism.

Synset Examples

Opposing behaviourism, he argued that behaviour was generally goal-oriented and purposive, an approach he called hormic psychology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: behaviorism - psychological theory focusing on observable behaviors

Etymology

From Greek hormē (impulse, urge) + -ism.
