cyclical

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪklɪkəl, ˈsaɪklɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪkəl, ˈsaɪklɪkəl / (BrE)
Happening in cycles; recurring at regular intervals.
recurring in cycles

Examples

Economic growth is often cyclical, with periods of expansion followed by recession.
The phases of the moon occur in a cyclical pattern each month.
Many natural processes, like the changing seasons, are inherently cyclical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodic - happening at regular intervals
  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption or breaks

Etymology

From Greek kyklikos (circular, cyclic), from kyklos (circle, cycle).
