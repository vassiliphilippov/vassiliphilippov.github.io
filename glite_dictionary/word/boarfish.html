boarfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small marine fish with a projecting snout, found in the eastern Atlantic and Mediterranean
a small fish with a projecting snout

Examples

Divers observed a colorful boarfish swimming near the rocky reef.
The boarfish is easily recognized by its distinctive long snout.
Fishermen sometimes catch boarfish in their nets along the Mediterranean coast.

Etymology

Named for its pig-like ('boar') snout.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, deep-bodied marine fish with large eyes and a long, projecting snout, found in the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific regions
a fish with large eyes and a long snout

Examples

Divers occasionally spot a boarfish hiding among the rocks on the ocean floor.
The boarfish is easily recognized by its distinctive pointed snout and large eyes.
Marine biologists study the habitat of the boarfish in the northeastern Atlantic.

Etymology

So named for its projecting snout, reminiscent of a boar's.
