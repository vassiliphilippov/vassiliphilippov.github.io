convexo-convex

1. ADJ

/ kənˌvɛksoʊ kənˈvɛks / (AmE), / kənˌvɛksəʊ kənˈvɛks / (BrE)
Convex on both sides; having a shape that curves outward on two opposite surfaces, like a lentil.
convex on both sides

Examples

The lens was shaped in a convexo-convex form to focus the light more effectively.
Many high-quality magnifying glasses use convexo-convex lenses for clearer images.
A convexo-convex structure is often used in optical instruments to converge light rays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biconvex - having two outwardly curved surfaces; convex on both sides
  • Degree Lower: planoconvex - flat on one side and convex on the other

Etymology

From Latin 'convexus-convexus', combining 'convexus' (arched, vaulted) twice to indicate convexity on both sides.
