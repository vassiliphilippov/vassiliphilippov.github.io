Acer pennsylvanicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsɚ pɛnˌsɪlˈveɪnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə pɛnˌsɪlˈveɪnɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of maple native to eastern North America, known for its striped bark and large, two-lobed leaves that turn clear yellow in autumn.
maple species with striped bark and yellow autumn leaves

Examples

The Acer pennsylvanicum is often called the striped maple due to its distinctive bark.
Hikers in the Appalachian region can easily identify Acer pennsylvanicum by its large, lobed leaves.
In autumn, the leaves of Acer pennsylvanicum turn a striking clear yellow, brightening the forest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped maple - Another common name for Acer pennsylvanicum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' (maple) and 'pennsylvanicum' (of Pennsylvania), referring to its geographic association.
