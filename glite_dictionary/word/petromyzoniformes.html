Petromyzoniformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtroʊˌmɪzɒnəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəʊˌmɪzɒnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of jawless fishes commonly known as lampreys, distinguished from hagfishes by their structural features.
order of jawless fishes; lampreys

Examples

The order Petromyzoniformes includes all lampreys found in freshwater and marine environments.
Scientists often study Petromyzoniformes to understand the evolution of vertebrates.
Unlike hagfishes, Petromyzoniformes possess a well-developed dorsal fin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - jawless fish of the order Petromyzoniformes
  • Antonym: hagfish - jawless fish of the order Myxiniformes (not lampreys)

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Petromyzon + -iformes (order suffix).
