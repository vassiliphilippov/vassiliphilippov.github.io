ricotta

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑtə / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒtə / (BrE)
A soft Italian cheese with a mild flavor, made from the whey left after making other cheeses.
soft Italian cheese

Examples

She added ricotta to the lasagna for a creamy texture.
The cheesecake recipe calls for two cups of ricotta.
Fresh ricotta pairs well with honey and fruit for a simple dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottage cheese - a soft, lumpy white cheese made from curds

Etymology

From Italian 'ricotta' (literally 'recooked'), from Latin 'recōcta'.
