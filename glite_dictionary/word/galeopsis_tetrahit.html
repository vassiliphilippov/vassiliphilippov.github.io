Galeopsis tetrahit

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːəpsɪs ˈtɛtrəhɪt / (AmE), / ɡaːlɪˈɒpsɪs ˈtɛtrəhɪt / (BrE)
A coarse bristly Eurasian plant with white or reddish flowers and leaves resembling those of a nettle, commonly found as a weed in the United States.
a Eurasian bristly weed with nettle-like leaves

Examples

Galeopsis tetrahit often invades gardens, making it difficult for other plants to thrive.
Botanists studied the spread of Galeopsis tetrahit across local agricultural fields.
Children are sometimes wary of Galeopsis tetrahit because its bristly surface can irritate the skin.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Galeopsis and specific epithet tetrahit, from Greek tetra (four) and hitos (layer).
