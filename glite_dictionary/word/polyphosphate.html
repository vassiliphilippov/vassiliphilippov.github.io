polyphosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈfɑːsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of polyphosphoric acid, often used in water treatment, detergents, and biological processes
salt or ester of polyphosphoric acid

Examples

Industrial waste sites sometimes contain high levels of polyphosphate in the groundwater.
Certain bacteria can break down polyphosphate compounds as part of their metabolic processes.
The water treatment facility uses polyphosphate to prevent scale buildup in the pipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) and 'phosphate', reflecting its structure of multiple phosphate units.
