cyclobenzaprine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkloʊˈbɛnzəˌpriːn / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkləʊˈbɛnzəpriːn / (BrE)
a prescription muscle relaxant drug used to relieve muscle spasms associated with acute injuries or musculoskeletal conditions
muscle relaxant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cyclobenzaprine to help alleviate her muscle spasms.
Side effects of cyclobenzaprine may include drowsiness and dry mouth.
Before taking cyclobenzaprine, tell your physician about any other medications you are using.

Etymology

From 'cyclo-' (referring to a cyclic structure), 'benz' (referring to benzene ring), and 'aprine' (from molecular structure).
