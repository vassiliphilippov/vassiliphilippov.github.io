perfumed

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfjumd / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːmd / (BrE)
having or smelling of a pleasant fragrance, especially due to the addition of perfume.
having a fragrant scent, often from added perfume

Examples

The perfumed envelope brought a smile to her face as she opened her mail.
He bought his mother a set of perfumed soaps for her birthday.
A perfumed breeze drifted through the garden, carrying the scent of blooming flowers.

Synset Examples

perfumed boudoir
perfumed stationery
scented soap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scented - having a noticeable pleasant smell, often from an added fragrance
  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell

Etymology

From the verb 'perfume' + -ed, indicating 'made to smell with perfume'; perfume from Old French 'parfumer'.

2. ADJ

/ pərˈfjumd / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːmd / (BrE)
having a noticeable and often pleasant fragrance, especially from added perfume or natural scent
having a pleasant fragrance

Examples

The perfumed air drifted in through the open window, filling the room with a gentle sweetness.
She wore a perfumed scarf that made her presence known even before she entered the room.
The garden was perfumed with the blooms of jasmine and roses in the evening.

Synset Examples

odoriferous spices
the odorous air of the orchard
the perfumed air of June
scented flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scented - having a fragrance, often from added scent
  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare' (to smoke through), via Old French 'perfumer'.
