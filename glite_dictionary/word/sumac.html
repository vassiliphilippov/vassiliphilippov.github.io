sumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.mæk / (AmE), / ˈsjuː.mæk / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, especially those species with reddish berries, some of which are used as spices or for ornamental purposes; usually refers to non-poisonous varieties.
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus

Examples

The sumac in the garden produces clusters of bright red berries every autumn.
Many Middle Eastern dishes use ground sumac as a tangy spice.
We planted a sumac tree along the edge of the driveway for its vibrant fall colors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poison ivy - a poisonous plant of the genus Toxicodendron, once considered part of Rhus

Etymology

From Old French *sumac*, from Medieval Latin *sumach*, from Arabic *summāq* (red).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
the wood obtained from a sumac tree or shrub, often used for woodworking or decorative purposes
wood from sumac tree or shrub

Examples

The craftsman carved the beautiful bowl out of sumac.
Furniture made from sumac is prized for its unique grain patterns.
They used sumac in the restoration of the antique chair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus

Etymology

From Arabic *summāq*, via Medieval Latin *sumach*, referring to the plant.
