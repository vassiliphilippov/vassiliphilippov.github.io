jersey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
a soft, slightly elastic fabric made by machine knitting, commonly used for clothing such as T-shirts and sportswear
soft, stretchy machine-knit fabric

Examples

This shirt is made of high-quality jersey that feels soft against the skin.
Ballet dancers often wear jersey fabric because it stretches with their movements.
The jersey used for athletic uniforms helps wick away sweat during games.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: interlock - a type of double-knit fabric with more stability than jersey

Etymology

Named after Jersey, one of the Channel Islands, known for producing knitted garments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
A close-fitting pullover shirt, typically made of knitted fabric, worn as part of a uniform or for sports.
close-fitting pullover shirt

Examples

Each player wears a numbered jersey on the field.
Her new jersey is made of lightweight, breathable material.
He framed his favorite team's jersey and hung it on the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweater - a knitted garment typically with long sleeves
  • Synonym: jumper - (British) a knitted pullover garment

Etymology

From Jersey, an island in the English Channel, originally used to refer to knitwear from that region.
