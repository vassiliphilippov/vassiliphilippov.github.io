Membracidae

1. NOUN

/ mɛmˈbræsɪˌdi / (AmE), / mɛmˈbræsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small plant-feeding insects, known as treehoppers, characterized by their enlarged, often ornate, pronotums
family of insects known as treehoppers

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the Membracidae family in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the Membracidae are known for the unique shapes of their pronotums.
The Membracidae are often mistaken for thorns due to their impressive camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treehopper - insect of the Membracidae family, noted for an ornate pronotum

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'membrana' (membrane), referring to their membranous wings.

2. NOUN

/ mɛmˈbræsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mɛmˈbræsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small plant-feeding insects known as treehoppers, characterized by their enlarged and often ornate pronotum.
family of insects known as treehoppers

Examples

The Membracidae are easily recognized by the dramatic shapes of their pronotum.
Many species in the Membracidae family mimic thorns as a form of camouflage.
Researchers study Membracidae to learn more about insect-plant interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treehopper - a small insect belonging to the family Membracidae

Etymology

From New Latin Membracidae, based on the genus Membracis.
