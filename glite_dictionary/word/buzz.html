buzz

1. NOUN

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
a continuous, low, humming sound, like that produced by a bee or a vibrating object
continuous, low humming sound

Examples

The buzz of the bees filled the summer garden.
We could hear the constant buzz of the machinery from the next room.
The buzz of conversation faded as the speaker took the stage.

Synset Examples

the buzz of a bumble bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - a continuous, low, droning sound
  • Synonym: whirr - a low, regular sound like repeated rapid movement
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
a situation of lively activity and excited talk or rumors, especially among a group of people
lively activity and excited talk

Examples

There was a buzz in the office as everyone discussed the surprising news.
The party had a real buzz about it, with conversations happening in every corner.
A buzz of excitement filled the stadium before the big match began.

Synset Examples

the buzz of excitement was so great that a formal denial was issued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - a low, continuous background sound or feeling of activity
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of noise, talk, or activity
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
(of a place) to be filled with a continuous busy or lively noise, usually from people talking or moving about energetically.
be filled with busy or lively noise

Examples

By 8 a.m., the kitchen was already buzzing with activity as the staff prepared breakfast.
The city center buzzes on Saturday nights, with crowds filling the streets and restaurants.
During the festival, the whole town was buzzing with excitement and laughter.

Synset Examples

This office is buzzing with activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - make a low continuous sound, often of activity
  • Degree Higher: roar - make a loud, deep, continuous noise

4. VERB

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
to make a continuous, low, vibrating sound, like that produced by insects such as bees
make a low, vibrating sound

Examples

The bees buzzed loudly as I approached the garden.
His phone began to buzz on the table, signaling an incoming call.
Cicadas often buzz in the trees during hot summer afternoons.

Synset Examples

bees were buzzing around the hive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - make a low, steady continuous sound
  • Synonym: drone - make a continuous low humming sound
  • Degree Lower: chirp - make a short, sharp sound like a small bird or insect

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by bees and similar insects.
