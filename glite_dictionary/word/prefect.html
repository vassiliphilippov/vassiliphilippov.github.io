prefect

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriːfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈpriːfɛkt / (BrE)
a senior official or chief officer, especially in some countries' governments or police forces
chief officer or magistrate

Examples

The prefect of Paris police addressed the public after the incident.
Every region in ancient Rome was governed by a prefect.
The prefect implemented stricter measures to improve city security.

Synset Examples

the prefect of Paris police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer who administers the law
  • Synonym: commissioner - a person appointed to a high administrative position, especially in the police or government

Etymology

From Latin praefectus, meaning 'placed in charge'.
