armiger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɪdʒə / (BrE)
(historical) A person, especially a squire, entitled to bear heraldic arms or who carried the armor of a knight.
person entitled to bear arms or carried knight's armor
archaic

Examples

The young armiger waited patiently, ready to assist his knight before the joust.
As an armiger, he was permitted to display his family's coat of arms.
During the ceremony, the armiger presented the knight with his shield and sword.

Etymology

From Latin *armiger* (armor-bearer), from *arma* (arms) + *-ger* (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɪdʒə / (BrE)
A person, especially a nobleman, entitled to bear heraldic arms.
person entitled to bear heraldic arms

Examples

Only an armiger was permitted to display a family coat of arms above his doorway.
The authority confirmed his right as an armiger to bear those symbols.
Medieval records detailed every local armiger and their family crests.

Etymology

From Latin 'armiger' (armor-bearer), from 'arma' (arms) + 'gerere' (to bear).
