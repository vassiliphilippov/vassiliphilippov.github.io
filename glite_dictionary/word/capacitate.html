to capacitate

1. VERB

/ kəˈpæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) capable; to enable or equip for a particular function or activity
enable or make capable

Examples

The new software will capacitate employees to work more efficiently from home.
Their training programs are designed to capacitate local communities in disaster response.
This course aims to capacitate students with the skills needed for modern industry.

Synset Examples

This instruction capacitates us to understand the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enable - to make able; to give ability, power, or means to do something
  • Antonym: to incapacitate - to prevent from functioning in a normal way; to make incapable

Etymology

From Late Latin *capacitat*- (stem of *capacitas*, 'capacity'), meaning 'made capable of containing or holding.'

2. VERB

/ kəˈpæsɪteɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of sperm) to undergo or to cause to undergo the physical changes needed to fertilize an egg.
make (sperm) able to fertilize an egg
medical

Examples

Mature sperm must capacitate in the female reproductive tract before they can fertilize an egg.
Certain chemicals in the oviductal fluid help to capacitate the sperm.
Without proper capacitateion, fertilization cannot occur.

Etymology

From Latin 'capacitatus', past participle of 'capacitare' (to make capable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00527012-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kəˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to make legally capable or qualified; to grant the legal power or authority to act.
make legally capable or qualified
legal

Examples

The new statute seeks to capacitate individuals with disabilities to manage their own legal affairs.
After years of training, she was finally capacitated to serve as a legal guardian.
The law intends to capacitate nonprofit organizations to enter into such contracts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to incapacitate - to make legally unable or disqualified

Etymology

From Medieval Latin capacitat-, past participle stem of capacitare (to make capable), from Latin capacitas (capacity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00974489-v
Synset POS: v
