bicornate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkɔr.neɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈkɔː.neɪt / (BrE)
Having two horns or horn-shaped parts, especially in anatomical or zoological contexts.
having two horns or horn-shaped parts
medical

Examples

A bicornate uterus can affect pregnancy outcomes.
Certain species of antelope have distinctly bicornate horns.
The fossil was identified by its bicornate skull structure.

Synset Examples

a bicornuate uterus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unicornuate - having one horn or horn-shaped part (mainly in anatomy and zoology)

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'cornu' (horn) + '-ate' (having).
