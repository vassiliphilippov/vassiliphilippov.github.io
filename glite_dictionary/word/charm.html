charm

1. VERB

/ tʃɑrm / (AmE), / tʃɑːm / (BrE)
to protect someone or something by means of magic or supernatural powers, often using an object or ritual believed to have magical properties
protect using magic or charms

Examples

The villagers believed the priest could charm their fields against evil spirits.
He tried to charm his house by hanging protective amulets above the door.
She claimed to charm the newborn by whispering ancient spells.

Etymology

From Latin 'carmen' (song, incantation), via Old French 'charmer'.

7. NOUN

/ tʃɑrm / (AmE), / tʃɑːm / (BrE)
(physics) A type of quark that is the third most massive of all quarks, one of six fundamental flavors in particle physics.
third most massive quark; physics term

Examples

The discovery of the charm quark was a significant milestone in particle physics.
Researchers are studying how the charm quark behaves inside different types of particles.
Particles containing a charm quark are called charmed particles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: strange - a type of quark with a different flavor from charm; less massive
  • Degree Higher: bottom - a type of quark with more mass than the charm quark
  • Synonym: quark - a fundamental particle and a basic constituent of matter

Etymology

From the word 'charm', chosen arbitrarily by particle physicists in the early 1970s for naming quark flavors.
