analphabetism

1. NOUN

/ ˌænˈæl.fə.bəˌtɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌænˈælfəbɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
The state or condition of being unable to read; illiteracy.
inability to read

Examples

The high rate of analphabetism in the region is a major barrier to economic development.
Government programs aim to reduce analphabetism through free education initiatives.
Due to widespread analphabetism, many adults rely on oral communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiteracy - the inability to read or write

Etymology

From French 'analphabétisme', based on Greek 'an-' (not) + 'alphabetos' (alphabet).
