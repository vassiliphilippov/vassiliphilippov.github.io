anti-Semitism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈsɛm.ɪˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈsɛm.ɪ.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
hostility, prejudice, or discrimination against Jewish people
prejudice against Jewish people

Examples

There has been a worrying rise in anti-Semitism across several countries in recent years.
His speech condemned all forms of anti-Semitism and religious intolerance.
Efforts to combat anti-Semitism include education and promoting mutual understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racism - prejudice, discrimination, or antagonism against people based on their race
  • Antonym: philo-Semitism - admiration or support for Jewish people or Judaism

Etymology

From German Antisemitismus, formed from anti- (against) + Semitismus (Semitism), referring to Jews as Semitic people.
