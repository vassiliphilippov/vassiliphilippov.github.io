algid

1. ADJ

/ ˈældʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈaldʒɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their skin) cold to the touch, especially as a result of illness, shock, or physical collapse.
cold to the touch, often due to illness or shock
medical

Examples

The patient was pale and algid after several hours of severe vomiting.
An algid sensation spread over his body as his blood pressure dropped sharply.
In severe cases of malaria, a person may present with an algid appearance.

Synset Examples

a person who is algid is marked by prostration and has cold clammy skin and low blood pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilly - uncomfortably or unpleasantly cold

Etymology

From Latin 'algidus', from 'algēre' (to feel cold).
