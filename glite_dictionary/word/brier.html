brier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
An evergreen Mediterranean shrub (Erica arborea) with fragrant white flowers and hard woody roots, often used to make tobacco pipes.
Mediterranean shrub with woody roots used for pipes

Examples

The best tobacco pipes are often carved from the root of a brier.
A dense brier hedge bordered the edge of the Mediterranean garden.
He admired the delicate white blossoms of the old brier in his yard.

Etymology

From French *bruyère* (heath, heather).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
A tangled mass of thorny or prickly shrubs or plants.
tangled mass of thorns or prickly shrubs

Examples

The old fence was covered with a dense brier that made it impossible to get through.
He cut his hand on a sharp brier while hiking through the woods.
The rabbit darted into a brier to escape the fox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - a prickly shrub, especially a blackberry bush

Etymology

From Old English brēr (briar, bramble).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild rose with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, and bright pink flowers that produce scarlet hips.
prickly Eurasian wild rose

Examples

The path was overgrown with brier and bramble, making it difficult to walk.
In the garden, a brier bloomed, its flowers bright against the green leaves.
She gathered some brier roses to decorate the old cottage window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briar - an alternative spelling for 'brier', often used interchangeably

Etymology

From Old French *bruyère* (heath, heather), from Latin *brucus* (heather).

4. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
A plant with a thorny or prickly stem, especially one of the rose family such as a wild rose or bramble.
thorny plant or stem

Examples

The path was overgrown with brier, making walking difficult.
She pricked her finger on a brier while picking wildflowers.
A tangled brier blocked the garden gate after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - a rough, prickly shrub, usually referring to blackberry or raspberry plants
  • Synonym: thorn - a stiff, sharp-pointed projection on a plant

Etymology

From Old French *bruiere* (heath, heather); possibly related to Frankish *brūskar* (to brush).

5. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə / (BrE)
A very prickly woody vine native to the eastern United States, with tough, round stems, shiny leathery leaves, and small clusters of black inedible berries.
prickly woody vine of eastern U.S.

Examples

The hiker's progress was slowed by a thick patch of brier blocking the trail.
Birds often build nests among the protective thorns of the brier.
He cut away the tangled brier vines that had overtaken the old fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - any prickly shrub, especially wild rose or blackberry

Etymology

Variant of 'briar', from Old English 'brēr' (briar, bramble).
