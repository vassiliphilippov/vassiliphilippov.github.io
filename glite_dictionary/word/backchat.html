backchat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtʃæt / (BrE)
informal remarks or replies that are cheeky or disrespectful, often in a light, teasing, or witty way
light teasing or cheeky reply
informal british

Examples

Her clever backchat during the meeting made everyone laugh.
The teacher was not impressed by his constant backchat in class.
He admired her quick wit and playful backchat during their conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repartee - a quick and witty reply
  • Synonym: retort - a sharp or witty reply

Etymology

From 'back' + 'chat', likely British English origin (late 19th century).
