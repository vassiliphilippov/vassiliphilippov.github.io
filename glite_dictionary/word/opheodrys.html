Opheodrys

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˈɑːdrɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiˈɒdrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, harmless green snakes found in North America.
genus of North American green snakes

Examples

The genus Opheodrys includes snakes commonly known as the rough and smooth green snakes.
Researchers observed that members of Opheodrys are often found in grassy meadows and wetlands.
Unlike some other snakes, Opheodrys species rarely bite and are considered harmless to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green snake - a common name for snakes in the Opheodrys genus

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'dryos' (tree).

2. PROPN

/ ˌɑfiˈɑdrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒfiˈəʊdrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, usually bright green snakes native to North America, commonly known as green snakes.
genus of North American green snakes

Examples

The Opheodrys genus includes the smooth green snake and the rough green snake, both found in North America.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Opheodrys in various forested areas.
Unlike many other snakes, members of Opheodrys are harmless and non-venomous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Opheodrys

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'dryos' (tree), referring to their arboreal habitat.
