Characinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈsɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a former scientific family name for freshwater fishes now classified as Characidae, including piranhas and tetras.
former name of Characidae fish family

Examples

The genus Astyanax, once placed in Characinidae, is now part of Characidae.
Many aquarium guides from the 20th century refer to tetras as belonging to Characinidae.
Scientists revised fish classifications, replacing Characinidae with the name Characidae in taxonomic literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Characidae - a family of freshwater fishes including tetras and piranhas

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Characinus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
