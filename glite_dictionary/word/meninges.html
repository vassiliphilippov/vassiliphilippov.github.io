meninges

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪn.dʒiz / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪn.dʒiːz / (BrE)
the three membranes that envelop the brain and spinal cord
membranes covering brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

The meninges play a crucial role in protecting the central nervous system.
An infection of the meninges is known as meningitis.
Damage to the meninges can result in serious neurological complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meninx - a single membrane covering the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'meninx' (membrane).
