wide-eyed

1. ADJ

/ waɪd aɪd / (AmE), / waɪd aɪd / (BrE)
(of eyes) having the eyes fully open, especially as a result of surprise, wonder, or innocence.
having eyes fully open (from surprise, wonder, etc.)

Examples

She stared at the magician, her wide-eyed amazement clear to everyone.
The children listened wide-eyed to the storyteller's tales of adventure.
His wide-eyed innocence made him an easy target for the prank.

Synset Examples

stared with wide eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-eyed - having eyes open wide, especially from surprise or alertness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01659326-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ waɪd aɪd / (AmE), / waɪd aɪd / (BrE)
showing innocent, naive, or childlike wonder and credulity
naive or innocent; showing childlike wonder

Examples

The children looked wide-eyed at the magnificent fireworks display.
Her wide-eyed enthusiasm for the project was refreshing to everyone on the team.
He listened wide-eyed as his grandfather told stories of the old days.

Synset Examples

childlike trust
dewy-eyed innocence
listened in round-eyed wonder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - having or showing a lack of experience or sophistication
  • Antonym: cynical - believing that people are motivated by self-interest; distrustful of sincerity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02279797-s
Synset POS: s
