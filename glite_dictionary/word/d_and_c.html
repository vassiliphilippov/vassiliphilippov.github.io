D and C

1. NOUN

/ di ænd si / (AmE), / diː ænd siː / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the cervix is dilated and the lining of the uterus is scraped to remove tissue, often for diagnostic or therapeutic reasons
surgical procedure to scrape uterine lining
medical

Examples

After discovering abnormal bleeding, her doctor scheduled a D and C to determine the cause.
A D and C may be needed after a miscarriage to remove remaining tissue.
The patient recovered quickly following her D and C procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilation and curettage - the full term for D and C, meaning the same procedure

Etymology

Abbreviation for dilation and curettage.
