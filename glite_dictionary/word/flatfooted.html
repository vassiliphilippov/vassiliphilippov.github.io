flatfooted

1. ADJ

/ ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of a response or action) forthright and explicit, without evasion or ambiguity.
forthright and explicit

Examples

She gave a flatfooted answer, leaving no room for doubt.
His flatfooted comments surprised the committee with their honesty.
The company issued a flatfooted denial of the allegations.

Synset Examples

a flat-footed refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - straightforward and honest
  • Antonym: evasive - avoiding commitment or self-revelation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00768832-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflatˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Standing or moving with the feet flat on the ground, rather than on the toes.
with feet flat on the ground

Examples

The children stood flatfooted at the edge of the pool, waiting for instructions.
He landed flatfooted after jumping off the step, hurting his ankles.
She remained flatfooted, refusing to move until the music started.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01035060-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
having broad, flat feet that typically turn outward
having broad, flat feet

Examples

His flatfooted stance made it difficult for him to run quickly.
Children who are flatfooted may need special shoes for support.
During the examination, the doctor noticed the patient was flatfooted.

Synset Examples

a slow flat-footed walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatfoot - a condition in which the arch of the foot is flattened

Etymology

From 'flatfoot', combining 'flat' + 'foot', with 'ed' indicating the condition of the feet.

4. ADJ

/ ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
surprised and unprepared, unable to react quickly or effectively
unprepared; unable to react quickly

Examples

The sudden announcement left the management team flatfooted and searching for answers.
We can't afford to be flatfooted when competitors launch new features.
The politician was caught flatfooted by the reporter's unexpected question.

Synset Examples

the new product caught their competitors flat-footed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prepared - ready for action or events

Etymology

Originally from 'flatfoot', meaning someone standing with feet flat, implying slowness or being caught off guard.
