throe

1. NOUN

/ θroʊ / (AmE), / θrəʊ / (BrE)
a violent spasm or pang of pain, often used to describe intense suffering or struggle.
severe spasm or pang of pain

Examples

She was in the throes of labor for several hours before giving birth.
The country is in the throes of a severe economic crisis.
He collapsed, gripped by the throes of excruciating pain.

Synset Examples

the throes of dying
the throes of childbirth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pang - a sudden sharp pain or painful emotion

Etymology

Probably from Old English *thrawan* (to twist, turn).

2. NOUN

/ θroʊ / (AmE), / θrəʊ / (BrE)
a hard or painful trouble or struggle, especially one marking a period of change or transition
painful struggle or tumult

Examples

The region is still in the throes of political unrest after the coup.
She was in the throes of labor for several hours before giving birth.
The company found itself in the throes of a severe financial crisis.

Synset Examples

a country in the throes of economic collapse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - a forceful or violent effort to get free of restraint or resist attack
  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Antonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort

Etymology

From Old English *thrawu* (pain, pang), from Old Norse *þrá* (desire, yearning, pain).
