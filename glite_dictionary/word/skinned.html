skinned

1. ADJ

/ skɪnd / (AmE), / skɪnd / (BrE)
having skin of a specified kind, often used in compound adjectives (e.g., fair-skinned, dark-skinned).
having skin of a specified kind

Examples

The doctor examined the dark-skinned patient carefully.
My sister is very fair-skinned and gets sunburned easily.
Some animals are thick-skinned to protect themselves from harsh environments.
