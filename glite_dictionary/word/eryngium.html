Eryngium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪndʒiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪndʒiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known for their spiny, thistle-like flower heads, commonly called sea holly, found worldwide and often used as ornamentals.
genus of thistle-like ornamental plants

Examples

The spiky blue flowers of Eryngium add striking texture to summer gardens.
She planted several varieties of Eryngium along the garden border for their ornamental value.
Eryngium is often dried and used in floral arrangements due to its unusual appearance.

Etymology

From Latin *eryngium*, from Greek *ēryngion* (a medicinal plant, sea holly).
