denouement

1. NOUN

/ deɪˈnuːmɑn / (AmE), / deɪˈnuːmɒ̃ / (BrE)
the final part of a play, film, or narrative in which the strands of the plot are drawn together and matters are explained or resolved.
final resolution of a narrative

Examples

The denouement of the novel left readers both surprised and satisfied.
After the tense courtroom scenes, the film's denouement revealed the true identity of the suspect.
The play builds slowly to a powerful denouement that ties up all the loose ends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: climax - the most intense or exciting point of something, especially in a narrative

Etymology

From French 'dénouement', from 'dénouer' ('to untie') + '-ment.', tracing to Latin 'nodus' (knot).

2. NOUN

/ deɪˈnuːmɑːnt / (AmE), / deɪˈnuːmɒ̃ / (BrE)
the final part of a story, play, or complex situation in which everything is explained or resolved
the resolution of a story or situation

Examples

The novel's denouement was unexpected and left readers amazed.
After many twists, the film reached its denouement in a dramatic courtroom scene.
The denouement of the debate came when new evidence was revealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolution - the act of solving or settling a problem, dispute, or contentious matter

Etymology

From French 'dénouement', from 'dénouer' meaning 'to untie'.
