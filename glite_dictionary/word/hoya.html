Hoya

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈhɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical climbing or trailing shrubs native to Australia, Asia, and Polynesia, known for their waxy, star-shaped flowers.
a genus of tropical climbing shrubs

Examples

The Hoya plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its fragrant, thick-petaled flowers.
Botanists have discovered several new species within the Hoya genus in Southeast Asia.
A cutting from a mature Hoya can root easily and eventually grow into a flowering vine.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Hoy (c. 1750–1822), English botanist.
