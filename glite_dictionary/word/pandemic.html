pandemic

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / panˈdɛ.mɪk / (BrE)
A disease outbreak that spreads across a large region, affecting an exceptionally high proportion of the population, often worldwide.
widespread disease outbreak

Examples

Many countries closed their borders during the COVID-19 pandemic.
The 1918 influenza pandemic caused millions of deaths worldwide.
Scientists are studying ways to prevent the next global pandemic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time

Etymology

From Greek 'pandēmos' meaning 'of all the people', from 'pan-' (all) + 'dēmos' (people).

2. ADJ

/ pænˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / panˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a phenomenon) widespread or prevalent over a whole country or the world; occurring everywhere.
widespread; occurring everywhere

Examples

After the speech, a pandemic sense of optimism filled the country.
Misinformation about the new policy became pandemic within days of the announcement.
There was a pandemic fear of job loss during the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

pandemic fear of nuclear war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidemic - widespread occurrence of something in a community

Etymology

From Greek 'pandēmos' (common to all people), from 'pan' (all) + 'dēmos' (people).

3. ADJ

/ pænˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / panˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease) occurring over a wide geographical area and affecting an exceptionally high proportion of the population.
widespread epidemic disease (adj.)

Examples

The authorities declared COVID-19 a pandemic health emergency in 2020.
There is growing concern about a pandemic flu outbreak in several countries.
Public health organizations monitor the risk of a pandemic disease spreading rapidly worldwide.

Synset Examples

a pandemic outbreak of malaria

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: epidemic - (of a disease or occurrence) affecting many individuals within a community or region at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'pandēmos' (pertaining to all people), from 'pan' (all) + 'dēmos' (people).

Synset POS: s
