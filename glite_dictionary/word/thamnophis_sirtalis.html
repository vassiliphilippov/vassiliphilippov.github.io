Thamnophis sirtalis

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɑːfɪs sɜrˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / θæmˈnɒfɪs sɜːˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A common garter snake species widespread throughout North America, known for its striped pattern and adaptability to various habitats.
species of common garter snake

Examples

The Thamnophis sirtalis is often seen near ponds and streams in suburban areas.
Researchers observed that Thamnophis sirtalis adapts well to both wild and urban environments.
Many people recognize Thamnophis sirtalis by the bold stripes running along its body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Thamnophis

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) and 'ophis' (snake); Latin 'sirtalis' (like a garter).
