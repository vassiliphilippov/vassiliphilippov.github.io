Gasterophilus intestinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæs.təˌrɑːˈfɪl.əs ɪnˌtɛs.tɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌɡas.təˌrəʊˈfɪl.əs ɪnˌtɛs.tɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of parasitic botfly whose larvae infest the stomachs of horses, causing various health issues.
parasitic botfly species affecting horses
medical

Examples

Veterinarians often check horses for signs of Gasterophilus intestinalis infestation during routine examinations.
The larvae of Gasterophilus intestinalis can cause gastric ulcers in their equine hosts.
Treatment programs are essential to control Gasterophilus intestinalis populations on horse farms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - A type of fly whose larvae are parasitic on mammals
  • Degree Lower: Gasterophilus nasalis - A related species of botfly affecting horses' nasal areas

Etymology

From Greek 'gaster' (stomach) and 'philos' (loving), denoting its stomach-infesting habit; 'intestinalis' from Latin, relating to the intestine.
