Hamelia erecta

1. NOUN

/ hæˈmiːliə ɪˈrɛktə / (AmE), / hæˈmiːliə ɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
A shrub native to Florida, the West Indies, Mexico, and Brazil, with attractive orange to crimson flowers; commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
a tropical shrub with bright red-orange flowers

Examples

The bright flowers of the Hamelia erecta attract hummingbirds and butterflies to the garden.
Many gardeners choose Hamelia erecta for its year-round blooms and resistance to drought.
In tropical landscapes, Hamelia erecta is prized for its vibrant color and easy maintenance.

Etymology

Genus name from the botanist Henri-Louis Duhamel du Monceau; 'erecta' from Latin meaning 'upright'.
