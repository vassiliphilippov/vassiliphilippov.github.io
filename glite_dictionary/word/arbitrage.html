arbitrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbɪˌtrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrɑːʒ / (BrE)
the simultaneous purchase and sale of the same asset in different markets to profit from tiny differences in the asset's price
simultaneous buying and selling for profit

Examples

The company profited from arbitrage by exploiting price differences in stocks traded on two exchanges.
Currency arbitrage is popular among investors who track fluctuations in exchange rates.
He specialized in arbitrage and frequently made quick, low-risk trades across global markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - investment involving risk with hopes of significant profit
  • Degree Lower: risk-free - involving no risk of financial loss
  • Degree Higher: gambling - staking money on uncertain outcomes, typically riskier than arbitrage

Etymology

From French arbitrage (arbitration), from arbitrer (to arbitrate).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑːrbɪˌtrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to buy and sell assets in different markets or forms to profit from price differences between them, especially in finance
profit by trading on price differences

Examples

Many hedge funds arbitrage currency differences to maximize their profits.
Traders often arbitrage between stock exchanges in different countries.
The firm arbitraged the price gap between gold futures and spot prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speculate - to engage in buying and selling with the hope of profiting from market fluctuations

Etymology

From French *arbitrage* (arbitration), adopted into financial language in the 19th century.
