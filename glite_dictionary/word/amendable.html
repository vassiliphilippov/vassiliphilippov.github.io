amendable

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɛndəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmɛndəbl / (BrE)
capable of being amended, corrected, or improved by making changes or additions
able to be corrected or changed

Examples

The contract was written in an amendable way so that revisions could be made easily.
Fortunately, the mistake on the report was amendable and quickly fixed by the editor.
The policy contains an amendable clause to allow for future updates if necessary.

Synset Examples

an amendable flaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correctable - able to be corrected
  • Antonym: irreparable - unable to be repaired or corrected

Etymology

From 'amend' + '-able', formed on the pattern of adjectives denoting ability or capacity.
