wolfish

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a wolf, especially in appearance or behavior
resembling or characteristic of a wolf

Examples

Her wolfish grin unsettled everyone in the room.
The suspect eyed the food with a wolfish hunger after hours of questioning.
His wolfish instincts made him both a fierce competitor and a dangerous adversary.

Synset Examples

ran in wolflike packs
wolfish rapacity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - relating to or resembling a dog or dogs
  • Antonym: human - relating to or characteristic of people

Etymology

From 'wolf' + suffix '-ish', meaning 'like' or 'characteristic of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (BrE)
having a strong or greedy appetite, especially for food; ravenous or voracious.
greedy or ravenous, like a wolf

Examples

After not eating all day, he had a wolfish appetite at dinner.
The children gave their mother a wolfish grin before diving into the cookies.
He looked at the buffet table with a wolfish expression, eager to fill his plate.

Synset Examples

edacious vultures
a rapacious appetite
ravenous as wolves
voracious sharks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry; starving; greedy for food
  • Synonym: voracious - wanting or devouring great quantities of food

Etymology

From 'wolf' + '-ish', meaning 'like a wolf', alluding to the wolf's reputation for hunger.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00010745-s
Synset POS: s
