cutlery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtləri / (AmE), / ˈkʌtləri / (BrE)
Knives, forks, and spoons used for eating or serving food.
eating utensils (knives, forks, spoons)

Examples

Please set the table with the cutlery before dinner begins.
We keep our cutlery in a drawer next to the sink for easy access.
The restaurant provides high-quality cutlery for every guest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverware - Knives, forks, and spoons made of or resembling silver

Etymology

From Old French 'coutellerie', from 'coutel' (knife), from Latin 'cultellus' (little knife).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.lɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.lər.i / (BrE)
A cutting implement or tool, especially those designed for cutting materials such as knives.
implement or tool for cutting

Examples

The workshop specialized in producing high-quality cutlery for carpenters and artisans.
Early surgical procedures relied on basic cutlery that was kept meticulously sharp.
Proper maintenance of cutlery tools extends their lifespan and keeps them effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - a tool with a sharp blade used for cutting

Etymology

From Old French *coutellerie*, from *coutel* (knife), from Latin *cultellus* (knife).
