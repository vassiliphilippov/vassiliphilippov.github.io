hipped

1. ADJ

/ hɪpt / (AmE), / hɪpt / (BrE)
(of a roof) having all sides sloping downwards to the walls, usually without vertical ends or gables.
roof with slopes on all sides

Examples

The old cottage features a hipped roof covered with slate tiles.
Many bungalow designs include a hipped roof for added stability.
A hipped roof is often used in areas prone to strong winds.

Synset Examples

a hipped roof has sloping ends rather than gables

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gable - the triangular upper part of a wall at the end of a ridged roof

2. ADJ

/ hɪpt / (AmE), / hɪpt / (BrE)
having hips, or having hips of a specified kind (usually in combination, e.g. 'broad-hipped')
having hips (of a specified kind)

Examples

The statue depicted a hipped goddess standing gracefully.
Many artists admired the full, hipped figures found in Renaissance paintings.
She preferred to wear dresses that flattered her hipped silhouette.

Synset Examples

broad-hipped

Etymology

From 'hip' (the part of the body), plus '-ed', forming an adjective.
