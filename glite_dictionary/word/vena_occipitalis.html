vena occipitalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ɒkˌsɪ.pɪˈteɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.nə ɒkˌsɪ.pɪˈteɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
An anatomical vein that drains blood from the occipital region at the back of the head.
vein draining the occipital region
medical

Examples

During the dissection, the vena occipitalis was clearly visible near the base of the skull.
The vena occipitalis plays an important role in draining blood from the posterior scalp.
An injury to the vena occipitalis can lead to complications due to excessive bleeding in the occipital region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occipital vein - a vein that drains blood from the occipital region of the scalp

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'occipitalis' (of the occiput, the back of the skull).
