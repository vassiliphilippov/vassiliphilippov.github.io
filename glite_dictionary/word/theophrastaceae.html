Theophrastaceae

1. NOUN

/ θiˌɑːfræˈsteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / θiˌɒfræˈsteɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of mainly tropical American trees and shrubs, similar to those in the Myrsinaceae family and sometimes included within it.
tropical American plant family

Examples

Many species in the Theophrastaceae family are native to Central and South America.
Botanists often debate whether Theophrastaceae should be merged with the Myrsinaceae.
The flowers of Theophrastaceae are typically small and arranged in clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myrsinaceae - a plant family closely related to Theophrastaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Theophrastus, a Greek philosopher and botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˌθiː.ə.fræˈsteɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌθiː.ə.fræˈsteɪ.siː / (BrE)
A family of mainly tropical American trees and shrubs, often similar to those of the Myrsinaceae and sometimes included in that family.
family of tropical American trees and shrubs

Examples

Many rare species within Theophrastaceae thrive only in the humid tropical forests of Central America.
The taxonomist argued that Theophrastaceae should remain a separate family and not be merged into Myrsinaceae.
Some members of Theophrastaceae are notable for their ornamental value due to their colorful fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myrsinaceae - a related family of flowering plants

Etymology

Named after Theophrastus, an ancient Greek botanist and philosopher.
