teeter

1. VERB

/ ˈtitər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtə / (BrE)
to move or stand unsteadily, often rocking or swaying back and forth
move unsteadily, rock or sway

Examples

The old ladder began to teeter as the man climbed higher.
She watched the small boat teeter in the rough waves.
The toddler would often teeter when taking his first steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to totter - to move in an unsteady way, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: steady - firm, secure, not likely to move or change

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in early 19th century American English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtitər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtə / (BrE)
A playground apparatus consisting of a long board balanced on a central support, with children sitting at each end to ride it up and down.
playground apparatus (seesaw)
american

Examples

The children laughed as they rode the teeter in the playground.
At recess, the most popular plaything was the old wooden teeter.
She hurt her knee when she slid off the teeter too quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seesaw - A similar playground apparatus with a board balanced on a central support
