Agapanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡəˈpænθəs / (AmE), / ˌaɡəˈpanθəs / (BrE)
a genus of South African evergreen or deciduous flowering plants, sometimes known as African lilies, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of flowering plants (African lilies)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Agapanthus for its striking blue flowers.
The Agapanthus thrives best in well-drained soil and full sun.
Some species of Agapanthus are hardy enough to survive mild winters.

Etymology

From Greek 'agapē' (love) + 'anthos' (flower).
