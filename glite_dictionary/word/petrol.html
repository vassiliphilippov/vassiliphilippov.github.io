petrol

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtɹəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɹəl / (BrE)
A volatile, flammable liquid derived from petroleum, used mainly as fuel in internal combustion engines.
liquid fuel for engines

Examples

The car ran out of petrol halfway through the journey.
In the UK, people usually refer to gasoline as petrol.
He stopped at the service station to fill the tank with petrol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasoline - the American term for petrol, a liquid fuel for engines

Etymology

From the French 'pétrole', from Latin 'petroleum' (rock oil).
