deossification

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌɒsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which bone tissue loses its mineral content
loss of minerals from bone tissue
medical

Examples

A lack of calcium can lead to deossification and weakened bones.
Researchers are studying ways to prevent deossification in elderly patients.
Chronic kidney disease may accelerate the deossification of the skeletal system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ossification - the process of bone formation

Etymology

From Latin de- (removal) + ossificare (to make into bone) + -ation.
