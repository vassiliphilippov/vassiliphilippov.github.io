Mary Shelley

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈʃɛli / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ˈʃɛli / (BrE)
(1797–1851) English writer best known for creating Frankenstein's monster and for her marriage to the poet Percy Bysshe Shelley.
English writer, creator of Frankenstein

Examples

Mary Shelley wrote Frankenstein when she was just eighteen years old.
Scholars often discuss the influence of Mary Shelley on Gothic literature.
Percy Bysshe Shelley and Mary Shelley traveled extensively through Europe.

English writer, author of Frankenstein

Examples

Many consider Mary Shelley to be a pioneer of science fiction literature.
