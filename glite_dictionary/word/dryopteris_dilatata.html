Dryopteris dilatata

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs dɪləˈteɪtə / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs dɪləˈteɪtə / (BrE)
A species of shield fern native to Europe, with large, spreading fronds.
European shield fern species

Examples

In the damp corners of the garden, the Dryopteris dilatata thrived, its fronds arching gracefully.
Botanists often recommend Dryopteris dilatata for shady woodland gardens due to its hardiness.
The lush foliage of Dryopteris dilatata attracts many nature enthusiasts to European forests.

Etymology

From genus name Dryopteris (from Greek drys 'oak' + pteris 'fern') and Latin dilatata meaning 'widened'.
