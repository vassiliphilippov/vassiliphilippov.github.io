serosa

1. NOUN

/ səˈroʊsə / (AmE), / sɪəˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
A thin membrane that lines the closed cavities of the body, consisting of two layers with a space between filled with serous fluid.
thin membrane lining body cavities
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the serosa covering the intestines during the operation.
Inflammation of the serosa can lead to a condition known as serositis.
The peritoneum is one example of a serosa in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serous membrane - a membrane lining internal body cavities and producing serous fluid
  • Antonym: mucosa - a membrane lining body passages and cavities open to the outside

Etymology

From New Latin, from serous + -a.
