compere

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / kɒmˈpɪə / (BrE)
to act as a master of ceremonies at a public event, introducing performers or speakers
act as a master of ceremonies
british

Examples

She was invited to compere the awards ceremony last night.
He has compered several television shows over the years.
Next week, I'll be compering a charity fashion event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - to act as the person in charge of an event or show

Etymology

From French *compère* (godfather, buddy), originally from Latin *compater* (co-father, godfather).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pɛə / (BrE)
A person who introduces performers at a show, on television, or at a public event, especially in British English.
British show or TV host
british

Examples

The compere kept the audience entertained between acts with his clever jokes.
At the awards show, the compere introduced each celebrity with great enthusiasm.
She worked as a compere for several popular TV talent competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - Person who presents a television or radio program or an event
  • Synonym: presenter - Person who introduces or hosts a broadcast or show

Etymology

From French 'compère' (friend, accomplice), adopted into English in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
