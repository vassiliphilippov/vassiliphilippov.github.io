manipulation

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of controlling or influencing someone or something, especially in a clever or dishonest way, to benefit oneself
clever or dishonest influence for advantage

Examples

Her manipulation of the situation allowed her to get the promotion over her colleagues.
The politician's manipulation of public opinion was widely criticized by the media.
He was unaware of the subtle manipulation that was pushing him to make that decision.

Synset Examples

his manipulation of his friends was scandalous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct people or events
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; opposite of manipulation in the sense of deceit

Etymology

From Latin manipulatio (handling), from manus (hand) + plere (to fill).

2. NOUN

/ məˌnɪpjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of handling or operating something skillfully, especially with the hands or by mechanical means
skilful manual handling or operation

Examples

The surgeon's manipulation of the delicate instruments required years of training.
Precision manipulation is crucial when assembling tiny electronic components.
The device allows for the remote manipulation of objects in hazardous environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handling - the act of touching or managing something with the hands

Etymology

From French *manipulation*, from Latin *manipulus* (handful), from *manus* (hand).
