spill

1. NOUN

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
a sudden fall, especially from an upright position or while in motion
a sudden fall or tumble

Examples

She took a spill while riding her bicycle and scraped her knee.
During the race, one of the skiers had a bad spill on the slope.
He laughed off his spill on the wet floor, insisting he was fine.

Synset Examples

he had a nasty spill on the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - an act of falling to the ground

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
an amount of liquid that has accidentally flowed over the edge of a container, especially onto a surface
liquid accidentally poured or dropped

Examples

There was a spill of coffee on the desk after she knocked over her mug.
The janitor quickly responded to the oil spill in the hallway.

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)

Examples
