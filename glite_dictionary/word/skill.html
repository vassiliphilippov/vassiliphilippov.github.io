skill

1. NOUN

/ skɪl / (AmE), / skɪl / (BrE)
the ability to do something well, especially because of training, practice, or experience
ability to do something well

Examples

Her skill as a pianist impressed the entire audience.
The job requires a high level of technical skill.
With years of practice, his skill at chess improved dramatically.

Synset Examples

the skill of a well-trained boxer
the sweet science of pugilism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ability - possession of the means or skill to do something
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skil' (distinction, discernment, knowledge).

2. NOUN

/ skɪl / (AmE), / skɪl / (BrE)
the ability to do something well, usually as a result of training or experience
acquired ability through training

Examples

He developed his skill as a pianist through years of practice.
This job requires a high level of skill and attention to detail.
The company's success is due to the skill of its engineers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ability - possession of the means or skill to do something
  • Synonym: talent - a natural aptitude or skill
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skil' (distinction, discernment).
