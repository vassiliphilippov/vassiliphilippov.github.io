autoradiography

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˌreɪdiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˌreɪdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a technique for producing an image (radiograph) on a photographic film or detector by means of the radiation emitted from the specimen itself
technique using self-emitted radiation to create images
medical

Examples

Autoradiography is widely used to track the distribution of radioactive substances in biological tissues.
Researchers employed autoradiography to visualize the localization of the labeled compounds within the cells.
The results from the autoradiography confirmed the presence of active metabolic pathways in the sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiography - the process or technique of producing images with X-rays or other radiation

Etymology

From auto- (self) + radiography (photographic imaging using radiation).
