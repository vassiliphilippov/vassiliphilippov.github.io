booker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkər / (AmE), / ˈbʊkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to arrange or schedule performances, appearances, or engagements for artists, entertainers, or public speakers
person who arranges engagements for performers

Examples

The booker managed to secure several headline acts for the festival.
As a booker, she is constantly negotiating with clubs and artists.
The band's booker called to confirm their slot at the venue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another, especially in business

Etymology

From the verb 'book' (to make a reservation or arrangement) plus the agent suffix '-er'.
