rasping

1. ADJ

/ ˈræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) unpleasantly harsh or grating.
unpleasantly harsh or grating in sound

Examples

His rasping voice made the announcement even more alarming.
She was disturbed by the rasping noise of metal against metal.
The old man's cough was deep and rasping, echoing through the silent room.

Synset Examples

a gravelly voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grating - having a harsh and unpleasant sound
  • Antonym: smooth - (of a sound) pleasant and free from harshness or rough edges

Etymology

From 'rasp' (to scrape noisily), plus the adjective suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
A harsh, grating sound, especially of a person's voice when speaking in an irritated or rough tone.
harsh, grating vocal sound

Examples

Her rasping made everyone in the room turn to look at her.
The teacher's rasping carried over the noise of the classroom.
He answered with a rasping that revealed his irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - a low, harsh vocal sound
  • Synonym: croak - a low, rough voice or sound

Etymology

From the verb 'rasp', from Old French 'rasper', from Middle Dutch or Old High German 'raspon' (to scrape or grate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07145482-n
Synset POS: n
