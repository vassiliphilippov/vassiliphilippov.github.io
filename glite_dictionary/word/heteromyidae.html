Heteromyidae

1. NOUN

/ hɛtɚˈmoʊ.i.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / hɛt.əˈməʊ.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of small, burrowing New World rodents with fur-lined cheek pouches and hind limbs adapted for leaping, including pocket mice and kangaroo rats, typically found in arid regions
family of burrowing rodents (pocket mice, kangaroo rats)
medical

Examples

The family Heteromyidae includes both pocket mice and kangaroo rats found in arid environments.
Scientists study Heteromyidae to understand how rodents adapt to deserts.
Many species in Heteromyidae have specialized fur-lined cheek pouches for carrying food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket mouse - a small rodent of the Heteromyidae family
  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - a jumping rodent in Heteromyidae, adapted to deserts

Etymology

From modern Latin Heteromyidae, from Greek heteros (different) + mys (mouse); used in zoological classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈmaɪɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈmaɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small New World burrowing rodents, including pocket mice, kangaroo mice, and kangaroo rats, known for having fur-lined cheek pouches and hind limbs adapted for leaping
family of burrowing rodents (pocket mice, kangaroo rats)

Examples

The Heteromyidae family is well adapted to arid desert environments in North America.
Members of the Heteromyidae can store food in their distinctive cheek pouches.
Kangaroo rats, which belong to the Heteromyidae, are known for their remarkable leaping abilities.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + mys (mouse), with modern taxonomic suffix -idae.
