permutability

1. NOUN

/ pərˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to have the order or sequence changed; ability to be permuted
ability to have order changed

Examples

The permutability of the sequence allows for many different configurations in the algorithm.
Understanding the permutability of these elements is essential for solving the puzzle.
In cryptography, high permutability can increase the strength of an encryption system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permutation - the act of changing the arrangement, especially the order, of a set of things

Etymology

From Latin 'permutabilis' (changeable) + English suffix '-ity'.
