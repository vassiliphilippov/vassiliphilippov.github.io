posturer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstʃərər / (AmE), / ˈpɒstʃərə / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a way intended to impress or mislead others rather than showing genuine feelings or intentions
one who acts to impress or deceive

Examples

The politician was dismissed as a mere posturer who cared little for real solutions.
Some artists are genuine, while others are seen as posturers seeking fame.
He accused his rival of being a posturer instead of a true leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poser - a person who pretends to be something they are not to impress others
  • Synonym: imposter - a person who deceives others by pretending to be someone else

Etymology

From 'posture' (to assume a pose), + '-er' denoting a person who does an action.
