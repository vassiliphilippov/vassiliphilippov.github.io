Judeo-Bolshevik

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒuːdioʊ boʊlˈʃɛvɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːdɪəʊ bɒlˈʃɛvɪk / (BrE)
A derogatory term describing alleged connections between Jews and Bolshevism, used especially in anti-Semitic conspiracy theories and propaganda.
derogatory term linking Jews to Bolshevism
offensive

Examples

The "Judeo-Bolshevik" myth was widely propagated in Nazi Germany to justify anti-Semitic policies.
References to a 'Judeo-Bolshevik conspiracy' appear in numerous extremist publications from the 20th century.
Many historians have documented the use of the term 'Judeo-Bolshevik' in anti-Jewish propaganda.

Synset Examples

The concept of Judeo-Bolshevik ties back to early 20th-century politics.
The phrase Judeo-Bolshevik is often used pejoratively.
Many conspiracy theories revolve around the idea of Judeo-Bolshevik influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-Semitic - hostile to or prejudiced against Jewish people
  • Synonym: Bolshevik - a member of the Russian communist party that seized power in 1917

Etymology

Formed from 'Judeo-' (relating to Jews or Judaism, from Latin 'Judaeus') and 'Bolshevik' (member of the Russian communist party, from Russian 'bol'shevik' meaning 'one of the majority').
