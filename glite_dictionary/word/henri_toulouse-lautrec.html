Henri Toulouse-Lautrec

1. PROPN

/ ɑnˈri tuːˌluːz loʊˈtrɛk / (AmE), / ɑ̃ʁi tu.luz lo.tʁɛk / (BrE)
(1864–1901) A French painter renowned for his vivid portrayals of Parisian nightlife, especially in the cafes and music halls of Montmartre.
French painter of Montmartre nightlife

Examples

The works of Henri Toulouse-Lautrec brilliantly captured the lively spirit of late nineteenth-century Paris.
Many art critics consider Henri Toulouse-Lautrec to be a pioneer of modern poster design.
A new exhibition showcases rare paintings by Henri Toulouse-Lautrec from his Montmartre period.
