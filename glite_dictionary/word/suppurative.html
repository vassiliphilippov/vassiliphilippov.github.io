suppurative

1. ADJ

/ səˈpɜrətɪv / (AmE), / sʌˈpjʊərətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or causing the formation of pus; characterized by suppuration.
producing or associated with pus
medical

Examples

The wound developed a suppurative infection requiring antibiotic treatment.
A suppurative abscess had formed beneath the skin.
Doctors monitored the suppurative process to determine the best course of care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purulent - containing or discharging pus

Etymology

From Late Latin suppurativus, from Latin suppurare (to form pus).
