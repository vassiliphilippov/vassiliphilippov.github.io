burdensome

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɝːdənˌsʌm / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdənˌsʌm / (BrE)
causing difficulty, worry, or hardship; difficult to bear or carry out
causing difficulty or hardship

Examples

Filling out the paperwork was a particularly burdensome chore for the new employee.
The new regulations placed burdensome requirements on small businesses.
Caring for her sick relative became increasingly burdensome over the months.

Synset Examples

the burdensome task of preparing the income tax return
my duties weren't onerous: I only had to greet the guests
a taxing schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onerous - requiring great effort; burdensome
  • Synonym: taxing - demanding a lot of effort; burdensome
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding

Etymology

From burden + -some (suffix forming adjectives), first recorded in Middle English.
