to astonish

1. VERB

/ əˈstɑnɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈstɒnɪʃ / (BrE)
to surprise someone very much; to fill with sudden wonder or amazement
to surprise greatly

Examples

Her creativity continues to astonish even her closest friends.
It will astonish you how quickly the landscape changes after the storm.
His performance astonished the entire audience at the concert.

Synset Examples

Your ability to speak six languages amazes me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amaze - to fill with great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or greatly surprise someone
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or dull

Etymology

From Middle English 'astonissen', from Old French 'estoner', from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder out).
