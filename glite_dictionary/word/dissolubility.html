dissolubility

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪˌsɑːləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdɪˌsɒljʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or property of being able to be dissolved, especially in a liquid
ability to be dissolved

Examples

The dissolubility of different salts varies greatly in water.
Scientists tested the dissolubility of the compound under several conditions.
Understanding the dissolubility of nutrients helps in formulating dietary supplements.

Synset Examples

he measure the dissolubility of sugar in water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solubility - the property of being soluble; the ability to dissolve in a solvent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dissolubilitas, from Latin dissolubilis (able to be dissolved).
