Gila

1. PROPN

/ ˈhiː.lə / (AmE), / ˈhiː.lə / (BrE)
a river in the southwestern United States that rises in western New Mexico and flows west through southern Arizona, joining the Colorado River.
river in New Mexico and Arizona, USA

Examples

The Gila River provides water to many communities in southern Arizona.
Early explorers used the Gila as a route to travel west.
Environmental groups work to protect the ecosystem of the Gila.

Etymology

From Spanish, possibly from an indigenous word in the region.
