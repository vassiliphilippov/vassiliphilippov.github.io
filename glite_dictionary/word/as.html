as

1. ADV

/ æz / (AmE), / æz / (BrE)
Used before an adjective or adverb to indicate equal degree or comparison.
to the same degree; equally

Examples

She is just as talented as her brother.
The cake was as sweet as honey.
He worked as hard as anyone in the class.

Synset Examples

they were equally beautiful
birds were singing and the child sang as sweetly
sang as sweetly as a nightingale
he is every bit as mean as she is

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - having similar qualities or characteristics

Etymology

From Old English 'alswā' meaning 'in the same way'.
