collectible

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktəbəl / (BrE)
(of money, a bill, etc.) subject to or requiring payment, especially as specified in a contract or agreement.
subject to payment

Examples

The invoice is a collectible debt that must be settled within thirty days.
Only legally collectible accounts should be transferred to the agency.
The company wrote off its non-collectible receivables at the end of the year.

Synset Examples

a collectible bill
a note payable on demand
a check payable to John Doe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payable - required to be paid; due

Etymology

From Latin colligere (to gather, collect) via Old French and late Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137973-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
an object that people collect because it is interesting or valuable, not necessarily old or antique
an item worth collecting

Examples

The shop specializes in rare collectibles from the 1980s and 1990s.
She has an impressive display of baseball cards and other collectibles in her living room.
Some collectibles become more valuable over time, while others simply hold sentimental value.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: antique - an old and valuable object
  • Degree Lower: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event
  • Synonym: curio - an unusual object valued as a curiosity

Etymology

From 'collect' + '-ible', modeled on 'collectible' as something able to be collected.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03073217-n
Synset POS: n
