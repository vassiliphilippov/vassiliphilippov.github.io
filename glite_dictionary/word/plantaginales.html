Plantaginales

1. NOUN

/ plænˌtædʒəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / plænˌtadʒɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants comprising the family Plantaginaceae, including plantains and related genera.
order of flowering plants

Examples

The order Plantaginales includes many species found in temperate regions.
Recent molecular studies have supported merging Plantaginales with other plant groups.
Taxonomists debated the classification of Plantaginales for many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantaginaceae - the plantain family of flowering plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Plantago (the genus name for plantains) + -ales (a standard suffix for plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ plænˌtædʒɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / plænˌtadʒɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of flowering plants, coextensive with the family Plantaginaceae in some classification systems
order of flowering plants (Plantaginaceae)

Examples

The family Plantaginaceae is sometimes placed in the Plantaginales order in older classifications.
Botanists have revised the boundaries of Plantaginales based on new genetic information.
Most references now merge Plantaginales into other plant orders due to changes in plant taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantaginaceae - the plantain family, a family of flowering plants

Etymology

From the genus name Plantago (type genus), combined with the Latin suffix -ales indicating an order.
