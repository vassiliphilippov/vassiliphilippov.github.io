kingwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌwʊd / (BrE)
a Brazilian tree (Dalbergia cearensis) that yields a valuable, handsome wood used especially for fine cabinetmaking
valuable Brazilian wood for fine furniture

Examples

The cabinet was made from rare kingwood and featured delicate marquetry.
Collectors pay a premium for antique desks crafted from kingwood.
This veneer uses kingwood, prized for its rich, dark grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - wood from several tropical trees, also prized for furniture

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌwʊd / (BrE)
a dense, violet-streaked hardwood from the Brazilian tree Dalbergia cearensis, valued for fine cabinetmaking and decorative work
violet-streaked hardwood used in cabinetwork

Examples

The cabinet was made from rare kingwood, giving it a unique and luxurious appearance.
Antique desks featuring kingwood veneers are highly prized by collectors.
The craftsman chose kingwood for its striking violet streaks and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - a richly colored hard wood from several tropical trees, often used for furniture

Etymology

Named for its use in fine furniture made for royalty.
