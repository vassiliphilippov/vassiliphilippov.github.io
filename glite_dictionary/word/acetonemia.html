acetonemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪtoʊˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌasɪtəʊˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal increase of ketone bodies in the blood, often seen in conditions such as diabetes mellitus
increase of ketone bodies in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed acetonemia after noticing high ketone levels in her blood tests.
Acetonemia is often a complication of uncontrolled diabetes mellitus.
Special dietary measures were recommended to address his recurrent acetonemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ketonemia - the presence of an abnormally high concentration of ketone bodies in the blood
  • Antonym: normoglycemia - the presence of a normal concentration of glucose in the blood

Etymology

From acetone + -emia (from Greek haima, blood), indicating the presence of acetone in the blood.
