diacetylmorphine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæsətɪlˈmɔrfiːn / (AmE), / daɪˌæsɪtɪlˈmɔːfiːn / (BrE)
A powerful narcotic drug, chemically derived from morphine, that is highly addictive and often used illicitly; also known as heroin.
addictive narcotic drug; heroin

Examples

The researchers studied the effects of diacetylmorphine on the central nervous system.
Some countries allow limited medical use of diacetylmorphine for pain management.
Illicit use of diacetylmorphine can lead to serious health complications and addiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroin - common name for diacetylmorphine, a powerful and addictive narcotic drug
  • Degree Lower: morphine - a less potent precursor narcotic used medically

Etymology

From di- (two) + acetyl (acetyl group) + morphine, describing its chemical structure.
