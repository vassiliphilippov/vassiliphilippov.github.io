reaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːpər / (AmE), / ˈriːpə / (BrE)
a machine used for cutting and gathering crops on a farm
machine for cutting and gathering crops

Examples

The old reaper in the barn hasn't been used in decades.
Modern farms use a combine reaper to harvest wheat quickly.
The reaper revolutionized agriculture by making crop gathering much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvester - a machine or person that gathers crops

Etymology

From reap + -er, originally referring to a person who reaps, later to the machine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːpər / (AmE), / ˈriːpə / (BrE)
a person who gathers crops at harvest, especially by hand or with a simple tool.
person who gathers crops at harvest

Examples

During the wheat harvest, every reaper in the village worked from dawn until dusk.
The reaper moved steadily through the golden field, bundling sheaves of grain.
In traditional farming communities, a skilled reaper was highly valued for a successful harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvester - person or machine that harvests crops

Etymology

From Middle English 'repen', from Old English 'rīpan' (to reap), influenced by 'reap'.
