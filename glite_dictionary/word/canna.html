canna

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænə / (AmE), / ˈkanə / (BrE)
A tropical or subtropical plant of the genus Canna, known for its large, sheathing leaves and clusters of bright, showy flowers.
tropical plant with showy flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the canna stood out in the summer garden.
After the frost, she cut back her canna plants to prepare them for winter.
Many gardeners plant cannas for their dramatic foliage and vibrant blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin 'canna' (reed, cane), from Greek 'kánna'.
