to christen

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪsən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsən / (BrE)
to give a Christian name to (someone) at baptism; to administer the ceremony of baptism to someone
to baptize; give a Christian name

Examples

The priest will christen the baby this Sunday at church.
They decided to christen their daughter in a small, private ceremony.
Do you know who will christen the child at the family gathering?

Synset Examples

The parents had the child baptized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to baptize - to administer the sacrament of baptism

Etymology

From Old English 'cristnian', from Latin 'christianizare' (to make Christian), from 'Christianus' (Christian).
