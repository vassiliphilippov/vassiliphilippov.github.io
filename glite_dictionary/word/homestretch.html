homestretch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌstrɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstrɛtʃ / (BrE)
the straight section of a racetrack leading to the finish line
final straight part of a racetrack

Examples

The horse surged ahead during the homestretch and won the race.
The runners gave everything they had as they entered the homestretch.
With the finish line in sight, the crowd cheered louder in the homestretch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straightaway - the straight part of a racetrack

Etymology

Formed from 'home' + 'stretch', referring to the final part of a race leading home.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌstrɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstrɛtʃ / (BrE)
the final part of an activity, project, or process; the last stage before completion
final stage before completion

Examples

After months of hard work, we're finally in the homestretch of the project.
She kept her focus during the homestretch and finished the marathon strong.
The team pulled together in the homestretch to meet the tight deadline.

Synset Examples

they were on the homestretch when the computer crashed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish line - the end point of a race, especially a marked line
  • Antonym: beginning - the start or initial part of something

Etymology

Originally from horse racing, referring to the stretch of track leading to the finish line.
