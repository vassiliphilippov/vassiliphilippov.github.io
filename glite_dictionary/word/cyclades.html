Cyclades

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪkləˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləˌdiːz / (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilization that flourished on the Cyclades islands in the southern Aegean Sea from about 3000 to 1100 BC
Bronze Age civilization of the Cyclades islands

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Cyclades dating back over 4,000 years.
The Cyclades were known for their distinctive marble figurines and advanced maritime trade.
Many myths and legends are associated with the ancient civilization of the Cyclades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan - Bronze Age civilization of Crete
  • Synonym: Mycenaean - Late Bronze Age civilization of mainland Greece

Etymology

From Greek Κυκλάδες (Kykládes), the name of the island group.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪkləˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləˌdiːz / (BrE)
a group of over 200 islands in the southern Aegean Sea, southeast of mainland Greece, known for their historical and scenic significance
group of Greek islands in the southern Aegean

Examples

The Cyclades are famous for their whitewashed houses and blue-domed churches.
She spent her summer sailing around the Cyclades and exploring their unique landscapes.
Many ancient ruins can be found throughout the Cyclades, attracting historians and tourists alike.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κυκλάδες (Kykládēs), meaning 'encircling islands'.
