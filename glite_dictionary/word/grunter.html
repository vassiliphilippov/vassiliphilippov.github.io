grunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌntər / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌntə / (BrE)
a person who makes low, guttural vocal sounds, typically to express effort or discomfort
person who grunts

Examples

He was known as a grunter on the tennis court, making noise with every serve.
During the heavy lifting session, the gym was full of grunters.
Some people find it distracting when a runner is a loud grunter on the track.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groaner - a person who groans

Etymology

From the verb 'grunt' + the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌntər / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌntə / (BrE)
a domestic pig, especially one thought of as making a grunting sound.
a domestic pig

Examples

The farmer raised over fifty grunters on his land.
Children laughed as the grunter rolled in the mud beside the pen.
That large grunter is the prize sow of the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pig - a domesticated mammal kept for its meat, skin, or as a pet

Etymology

From 'grunt' (the sound made by pigs) + '-er' (one who does), referring to the animal's characteristic sound.
