taphephobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæfɪˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌtafɪˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
An intense or irrational fear of being buried alive.
morbid fear of being buried alive
medical

Examples

Ever since he read stories about premature burials, his taphephobia intensified.
A strong taphephobia led her to avoid cemeteries and funerals altogether.
The nineteenth century saw a rise in taphephobia due to medical uncertainties in declaring death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something

Etymology

From Greek 'taphos' (grave) + 'phobia' (fear).
