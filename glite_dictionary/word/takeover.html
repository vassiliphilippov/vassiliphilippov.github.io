takeover

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkəʊvə / (BrE)
a sudden and decisive seizure of government power, especially by illegal or forceful means
forcible seizure of government power

Examples

The country faced a military takeover after weeks of unrest.
An attempted takeover was quickly suppressed by loyal government forces.
International observers condemned the forceful takeover of the capital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coup - a sudden, violent, and illegal seizure of power from a government

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'take over'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the act of gaining control of a company by buying its shares or merging with it
acquisition of corporate control

Examples

The company resisted a hostile takeover by a larger competitor.
Share prices rose sharply following news of the planned takeover.
The board of directors approved the takeover after lengthy negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquisition - the act of acquiring control of a company
  • Synonym: merger - the combination of two companies into one
  • Antonym: divestiture - the act of selling subsidiary assets or businesses
