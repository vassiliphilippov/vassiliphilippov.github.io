periwig

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəˌwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈpɛrəˌwɪɡ / (BrE)
a wig for men, especially popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, often worn as a symbol of social status or fashion
man's wig popular in the 17th–18th centuries

Examples

Judges in British courts are still sometimes seen wearing a periwig during formal proceedings.
Portraits from the 18th century often depict noblemen sporting elaborate periwigs.
He found an old periwig in the costume trunk and wore it to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wig - an artificial covering of hair for the head

Etymology

From French 'perruque' (wig), influenced by earlier English forms 'perrike' and 'periwig'.
