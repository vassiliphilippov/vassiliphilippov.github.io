eggbeater

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌbiːtər / (AmE), / ˈɛɡˌbiːtə / (BrE)
A kitchen utensil with rotating blades, used for beating eggs or whipping cream by hand.
hand-operated mixer for eggs or cream

Examples

She used an eggbeater to whip the cream until it was light and fluffy.
The old-fashioned eggbeater in my grandmother's kitchen still works perfectly.
He prefers a simple eggbeater over an electric mixer for making omelets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixer - a device for mixing ingredients together
  • Synonym: whisk - a kitchen utensil for whipping or stirring ingredients

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌbiːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛɡˌbiːtə / (BrE)
(informal) A helicopter; an aircraft without wings that obtains lift from the rotation of overhead blades.
informal term for a helicopter
informal

Examples

The medics arrived in an eggbeater to airlift the injured hiker to safety.
He always dreamed of flying an eggbeater, soaring above traffic jams and city streets.
During the war, the old eggbeater was used for vital rescue missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helicopter - an aircraft that obtains its lift from overhead rotating blades

Etymology

From the resemblance of a helicopter's rotating blades to those of an eggbeater (kitchen tool).
