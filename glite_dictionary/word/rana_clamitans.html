Rana clamitans

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnə kləˈmaɪtænz / (AmE), / ˈrɑːnə kləˈmaɪtænz / (BrE)
A North American frog species, commonly known as the green frog, found near marshes and ponds in the United States and Canada.
North American green frog species

Examples

We spotted a Rana clamitans resting on a lily pad in the pond.
The call of the Rana clamitans echoed throughout the wetland in early summer.
Researchers monitored the population of Rana clamitans to study environmental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green frog - Common name for Rana clamitans, a species of frog native to North America
  • Degree Higher: bullfrog - A larger, similar species of North American frog (Rana catesbeiana)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rana' meaning frog, and 'clamitans' meaning calling or crying out.
