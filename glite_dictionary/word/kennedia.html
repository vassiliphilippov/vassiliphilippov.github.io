Kennedia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈniːdiə / (AmE), / kɛˈniːdiə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian woody vines with showy red or purplish flowers
genus of Australian woody vines

Examples

The brilliant flowers of the Kennedia vine add vibrant color to Australian gardens.
Botanists have identified several species within the genus Kennedia.
Many gardeners cultivate Kennedia for its attractive and hardy qualities.

Etymology

Named after Lewis Kennedy (died 1782), English nurseryman.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈniːdiə / (AmE), / kɛˈniːdiə / (BrE)

genus of Australian flowering vines

Examples

The garden featured several Kennedia species climbing along the fence.
Horticulturists value Kennedia for its vibrant and attractive blossoms.
Botanists discovered a new Kennedia species in Western Australia.

Etymology

Named after Lewis Kennedy, an English nurseryman.
