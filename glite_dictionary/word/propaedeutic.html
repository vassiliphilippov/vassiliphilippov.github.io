propaedeutic

1. NOUN

/ proʊpɪˈdjutɪk / (AmE), / prəʊpɪˈdjuːtɪk / (BrE)
An introductory course or instruction preparing students for further study in a particular field.
introductory course or instruction

Examples

All first-year students are required to take a propaedeutic in basic mathematics.
The propaedeutic course laid the groundwork for more advanced studies in physics.
Her propaedeutic in art history helped her appreciate the later, specialized seminars.

Etymology

From Greek propaideuein 'to teach beforehand,' from pro- 'before' + paideuein 'to teach.'
