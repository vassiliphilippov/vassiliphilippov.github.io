lone

1. ADJ

/ loʊn / (AmE), / ləʊn / (BrE)
Having no companions; solitary or isolated.
solitary; without companions

Examples

A lone wolf was spotted near the edge of the forest.
She enjoyed being the lone hiker on the quiet mountain trail.
The lone house stood on a vast, empty plain.

Synset Examples

he was alone when we met him
she is alone much of the time
the lone skier on the mountain
a lonely fisherman stood on a tuft of gravel
a lonely soul
a solitary traveler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - existing alone; without companions
  • Antonym: accompanied - having companions; not alone

Etymology

Shortened form of 'alone', first used in the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ loʊn / (AmE), / ləʊn / (BrE)
preferring to be alone or existing without others; solitary in nature or disposition
preferring solitude; solitary

Examples

He was known as a lone traveler who enjoyed exploring remote places by himself.
She took a lone walk through the forest, enjoying the peaceful solitude.
The lone wolf prefers to live and hunt alone rather than with a pack.

Synset Examples

a lone wolf
a lonely existence
a man of a solitary disposition
a solitary walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - preferring to be alone; existing alone
  • Synonym: lonely - unhappy because of being alone

Etymology

Shortened form of 'alone', from Middle English 'all one'.

Synset Examples
