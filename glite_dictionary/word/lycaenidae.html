Lycaenidae

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, usually brightly colored butterflies, with males often having short forelegs.
family of small, colorful butterflies

Examples

The Lycaenidae are well known for their vibrant wing patterns and colors.
Many species in the Lycaenidae family form mutualistic relationships with ants.
Some entomologists specialize in the study of Lycaenidae due to their unique behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with brightly colored wings and a fluttering flight

Etymology

From New Latin Lycaenidae, from the type genus Lycaena.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnɪdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, usually brightly colored butterflies, whose males have short forelegs, including blues, coppers, and hairstreaks.
family of brightly colored butterflies

Examples

The Lycaenidae are among the most colorful of all butterfly families.
Many species within the Lycaenidae family display intricate patterns on their wings.
Researchers observed the mating habits of several Lycaenidae butterflies in the field.

Etymology

From New Latin Lycaenidae, the family name, based on the genus Lycaena.
