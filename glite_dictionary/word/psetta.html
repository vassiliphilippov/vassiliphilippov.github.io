Psetta

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtə, ˈpsɛtə / (AmE), / ˈsɛtə, ˈpsɛtə / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Bothidae, commonly known as turbots.
genus of flatfish (turbots)

Examples

The species Psetta maxima, known as the turbot, is prized for its delicate flavor.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new species within the genus Psetta.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary relationships among flatfish, particularly those in Psetta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbot - an edible flatfish of the genus Psetta

Etymology

From Greek psetta (a kind of fish, especially a flatfish/turbot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtə / (AmE), / ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Bothidae, including species such as the turbot.
genus of flatfish (turbot)

Examples

The turbot belongs to the genus Psetta, a group of large flatfish.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of Psetta species in the Mediterranean Sea.
Several commercial fisheries target members of the genus Psetta due to their economic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genus Scophthalmus - another genus of flatfish in the family Bothidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'psētta' meaning 'flatfish'.
