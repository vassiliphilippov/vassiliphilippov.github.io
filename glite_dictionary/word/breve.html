breve

1. NOUN

/ bɹiv / (AmE), / ˈbriːv / (BrE)
A mark (˘) placed over a vowel to indicate it is pronounced with a short sound.
U-shaped diacritical mark for a short vowel

Examples

The word 'cup' contains a short 'u', shown with a breve in phonetic notation.
When teaching phonics, the teacher wrote a breve over each short vowel in the words.
A breve over the letter helps learners distinguish between long and short vowel sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macron - A diacritical mark (¯) indicating a long vowel sound

Etymology

From Latin 'brevis' meaning 'short'.
