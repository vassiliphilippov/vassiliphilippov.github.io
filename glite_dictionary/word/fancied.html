fancied

1. ADJ

/ ˈfænsiːd / (AmE), / ˈfænsiːd / (BrE)
created or imagined in the mind; not real or actual
imagined or unreal

Examples

Many people have a fancied grievance against their coworkers that has no basis in reality.
The detective dismissed the story as a fancied account with no supporting evidence.
She attributed her failure to a fancied slight, but no one had actually insulted her.

Synset Examples

a fabricated excuse for his absence
a fancied wrong
a fictional character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginary - existing only in the imagination; not real
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring in fact; not imagined

Etymology

From Middle English 'fancy', short for 'fantasy', from Old French 'fantaisie', from Latin 'phantasia'.
