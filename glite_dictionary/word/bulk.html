bulk

1. NOUN

/ bʌlk / (AmE), / bʌlk / (BrE)
the larger or main part of something; the majority of something
the main or larger part

Examples

The bulk of the students completed the project ahead of schedule.
She handled the bulk of the responsibilities during the relocation.
The bulk of the report focuses on financial performance.

Synset Examples

the majority of his customers prefer it
the bulk of the work is finished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majority - the greater number or part

Etymology

From Middle English 'bulk' meaning 'cargo, heap'; possibly from Old Norse 'bulki' (cargo, load).

2. VERB

/ bʌlk / (AmE), / bʌlk / (BrE)
(of a person) to gain size and weight, especially muscle, through exercise and diet.
gain size and weight through exercise and diet

Examples

Many athletes try to bulk during the winter before trimming down in the spring.
If you want to bulk effectively, you'll need to combine weightlifting with a calorie surplus.
She spent months bulking to prepare for the bodybuilding competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cut - to reduce body fat after a period of bulking in bodybuilding

/ bʌlk / (AmE), / bʌlk / (BrE)

5. NOUN

/ bʌlk / (AmE), / bʌlk / (BrE)
the size, mass, or volume of something, especially when large; the main or greater part of something.
large size or mass; main part

Examples

It is often cheaper to purchase household goods in bulk rather than individually.
The bulk of the report focused on recent financial trends.
Despite his small size, he could move objects of considerable bulk with ease.

6. NOUN

large size; main part

Examples

Vegetables form the bulk of their diet.
He moved with surprising speed for someone of his bulk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies
