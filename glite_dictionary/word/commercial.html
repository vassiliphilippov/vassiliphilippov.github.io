commercial

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl / (BrE)
concerned with or engaged in commerce or business; relating to the buying and selling of goods and services, especially as opposed to purely private or non-profit activities.
related to commerce or business

Examples

The city offers plenty of commercial opportunities for entrepreneurs.
She specializes in commercial law, advising companies on their transactions.
A new commercial district is being developed downtown.

Synset Examples

a commercial attache
commercial paper
commercial law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business - the activity of making, buying, or selling goods or services for profit
  • Antonym: noncommercial - not intended for or related to commerce, business, or profit-making

Etymology

From Latin 'commercium' (trade; commerce), via French 'commercial'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03149226-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmɝːʃəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
connected with or engaged in commerce or business activities, or sponsored by or intended for business enterprises rather than private, academic, or government purposes
connected with commerce or business

Examples

The company specializes in commercial real estate development across major cities.
He drives a commercial truck for a local logistics firm.
This software is intended for commercial use only and not for personal or educational purposes.

Synset Examples

commercial trucker
commercial TV
commercial diamonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business - work relating to commerce or trade
  • Antonym: noncommercial - not intended for commerce or business use

Etymology

From Latin commercium (trade, commerce), via French commercial.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00485135-a

3. ADJ

/ kəˈmɝːʃəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
concerned with making a profit; intended to make money rather than for artistic or other purposes
oriented toward making a profit

Examples

The author admitted that her latest novel was a commercial project designed to appeal to a wider audience.
Many artists struggle to balance creative integrity with the need for commercial success.
The film was criticized for its commercial approach, prioritizing profit over storytelling.

Synset Examples

a moneymaking business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercantile - concerned with trade or commerce, particularly profit-oriented
  • Antonym: noncommercial - not intended to make a profit
  • Synonym: moneymaking - intended to make or produce profit

Etymology

From Latin 'commercium' (trade, commerce), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ kəˈmɝːʃəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
(of goods or products) of a kind or quality suitable for use in commerce, often average or inferior rather than superior.
of a quality suitable for commerce; often average or inferior

Examples

The store only sells the commercial grade of cleaning products, not the ones for laboratory use.
He purchased a commercial variety of seed, which is less expensive but not as high-quality as specialty types.
They used commercial beef in the cafeteria, which some people considered to be of average flavor.

Synset Examples

commercial grade of beef
commercial oxalic acid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrial - of or relating to goods manufactured in quantity for industry
  • Antonym: premium - of higher than average quality

Etymology

From Latin 'commercium' (trade, commerce), via French 'commercial'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02355417-s
Synset POS: s

5. NOUN

/ kəˈmɝːʃəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
an advertisement broadcast on radio or television, paid for by a company or sponsor
radio or television advertisement

Examples

They paused the movie for a commercial break every fifteen minutes.
That catchy jingle from the car commercial is stuck in my head.
He appeared in a television commercial for breakfast cereal last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertisement - a notice or announcement promoting a product, service, or event

Etymology

From Latin 'commercialis', from 'commercium' (trade, commerce).
