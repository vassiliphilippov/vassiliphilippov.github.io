legally

1. ADV

/ ˈliːɡəli / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəli / (BrE)
In a way that is permitted by law or according to the law.
according to the law

Examples

She is legally responsible for the safety of her employees.
All foreign workers must reside in the country legally.
Their marriage was legally recognized by the state.

Synset Examples

we are lawfully wedded now

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegally - in a way that is not permitted by law

Etymology

From legal + -ly, ultimately from Latin legalis (legal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00253289-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈliːgəli / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is allowed by law; according to the law.
in accordance with the law

Examples

The company acted legally to resolve the dispute.
He is legally allowed to work in this country.
Owning the property legally requires proper documentation.

Synset Examples

he acted legally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegally - in a manner that is not allowed by law

Etymology

From legal + -ly, where 'legal' is from Latin 'legalis' (relating to the law).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00125495-r
Synset POS: r
