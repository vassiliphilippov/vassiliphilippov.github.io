thunderbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəbɜːd / (BrE)
(in North American Indigenous mythology) a mythical bird regarded as the spirit of thunder and lightning, often believed to cause storms and serve as a powerful supernatural being.
mythical bird spirit in Indigenous North American lore

Examples

According to legend, the thunderbird brings thunder by flapping its wings.
Carvings of the thunderbird are found on many totem poles in the Pacific Northwest.
The story describes a battle between the thunderbird and a giant whale.

Etymology

Formed from 'thunder' + 'bird'; known from North American Indigenous legends.
