Pseudaletia

1. NOUN

/ suːˌdæˈliːʃiə / (AmE), / sjuːˌdæˈliːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as armyworms, some of which are significant agricultural pests.
genus of moths (armyworm larvae)

Examples

The larvae of Pseudaletia species are known for damaging corn crops.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Pseudaletia to develop better pest control methods.
Several species within the genus Pseudaletia are important to agricultural entomology.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + probably 'Aletia', another moth genus, indicating resemblance but distinction.
