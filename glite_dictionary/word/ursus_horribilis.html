Ursus horribilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜːrsəs hɔːˈrɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs hɒˈrɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
a powerful brownish-yellow bear found in the uplands of western North America; also known as the grizzly bear
North American grizzly bear species

Examples

The Ursus horribilis is known for its impressive strength and size.
Scientists tracked the movements of Ursus horribilis through Yellowstone National Park.
Encounters with Ursus horribilis are rare but can be dangerous if the bear feels threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grizzly bear - large brown bear native to North America

Etymology

From Latin: ursus (bear) + horribilis (terrible, dreadful).
