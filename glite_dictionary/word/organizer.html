organizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who recruits workers to join a labor union
person who recruits union members

Examples

The union organizer visited the factory to talk to the workers about membership.
As a skilled organizer, she persuaded many employees to join the union.
Management was concerned about the influence of the organizer among the staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruiter - person who enlists people for a group or activity

Etymology

From organize + -er, meaning one who organizes.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or coordinates an event, group, or activity, bringing order and structure to it
person who arranges or coordinates events or groups

Examples

As the organizer of the concert, she managed every detail personally.
The organizer made sure all the volunteers were assigned to their tasks.
He was appointed organizer for the annual charity run.

Synset Examples

she was the organizer of the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinator - person who organizes people or groups so that they work together efficiently
  • Antonym: disorganizer - a person who causes confusion or disorder

Etymology

From organize + -er, first used in the early 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10402936-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɡəˌnaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a small electronic device, similar to a hand-held computer, used for keeping personal information such as appointments, contacts, and notes
electronic personal data device

Examples

He relies on his organizer to keep track of appointments and phone numbers.
Before smartphones, many people used an electronic organizer for their schedules.
She set an alarm on her organizer to remind her of the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal digital assistant - a handheld electronic device that functions as a personal information manager
