corpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pəs / (BrE)
the main body or central part of an organ or structure in the body
main part of a bodily organ
medical

Examples

The corpus of the stomach is prone to certain types of ulcers.
Damage to the corpus callosum can affect communication between the brain's hemispheres.
Doctors examined the corpus of the uterus during the surgery.

Etymology

From Latin 'corpus' (body).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrpəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpəs / (BrE)
In finance, the principal amount of money, as distinguished from the income or interest it generates.
principal sum of money (finance)

Examples

The trust's corpus was invested to provide a steady income for the beneficiaries.
Only the interest, not the corpus, can be withdrawn from the account each year.
Over time, the corpus of the endowment grew significantly due to wise investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal - the original sum of money invested or loaned

Etymology

From Latin corpus (body), originally referring to a body or collection, later extended in legal and financial contexts.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrpəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpəs / (BrE)
a collection of written texts, especially the entire works of a particular author or a body of writing on a specific subject
collection of written texts

Examples

The linguist created a corpus of modern American English to study language change.
Her research focused on a corpus of Shakespeare's plays.
The publisher released the author's complete corpus in a new edition.

Synset Examples

he edited the Hemingway corpus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Latin 'corpus' meaning 'body', used figuratively for a body of writing.
