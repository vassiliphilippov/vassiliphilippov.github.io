reformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of correcting or improving someone or something by removing errors and guiding them back to a better path or state
act of correcting or improving

Examples

The reformation of the criminal justice system led to better integration of former offenders.
Through education and counseling, the program aimed at the reformation of troubled youth.
His sincere reformation impressed the parole board and granted him an early release.

Synset Examples

the reclamation of delinquent children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitation - the process of restoring someone to health or normal life by training and therapy
  • Antonym: corruption - the process of becoming morally degraded or dishonest

Etymology

From Latin 'reformatio' (restoration, renewal), from 'reformare' (to form again, change).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making significant changes or improvements to an institution or practice, especially in social, political, or religious contexts
major improvement or change in institutions

Examples

The new government promised a complete reformation of the education system.
After years of corruption, there was an urgent need for reformation within the organization.
The period was marked by a reformation in religious practices across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve something
  • Antonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse

Etymology

From Latin reformatio (restoration, reform), from reformare (to form again, change).
