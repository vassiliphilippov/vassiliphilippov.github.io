footprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.prɪnt / (BrE)
the surface area occupied by an object, especially in reference to equipment, buildings, or other items
area occupied by an object

Examples

The printer's footprint is small enough to fit on a corner of the desk.
The architects tried to minimize the building's footprint to preserve green space.
Due to its large footprint, the new machine takes up most of the lab bench.

Synset Examples

the computer had a desktop footprint of 10 by 16 inches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprint - a mark or impression made by pressure

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.prɪnt / (BrE)
a mark, trace, or evidence suggesting that something or someone was once present, felt, or important, especially used figuratively
trace or evidence left behind

Examples

The archeologists uncovered the footprint of a lost civilization beneath the jungle.
The new stadium left a significant footprint on the character of the neighborhood.
Her research had a lasting footprint on the field of neuroscience.

Synset Examples

the footprints of an earlier civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprint - a mark or impression left by pressure
  • Synonym: trace - a sign or indication that something once existed
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈfʊt.prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.prɪnt / (BrE)
a mark or impression made by a foot or shoe on a surface
mark made by a foot or shoe

Examples

We found a single footprint in the freshly fallen snow.
The muddy footprint on the kitchen floor showed where the dog had been.
The detective examined the footprint carefully for any clues.

Synset Examples

the police made casts of the footprints in the soft earth outside the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a mark left by a person, animal, or vehicle moving over the ground
  • Synonym: impression - a mark produced by pressure of an object
  • Synonym: footfall - sound or mark made by a footstep

Etymology

From Middle English 'fote' (foot) + 'printe' (impression, mark).
