jackrabbit

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (BrE)
to move forward or start with a fast, sudden movement, especially from a stationary position
move or start suddenly and quickly
informal american

Examples

The car jackrabbited off the starting line as soon as the light turned green.
When the ball was thrown, the dog jackrabbited after it with surprising speed.
Engineers warn drivers not to jackrabbit from stoplights, as it wastes fuel.

Etymology

From 'jackrabbit,' referring to the animal's quick, sudden movements; by analogy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (BrE)
a large, fast-running hare found in western North America, known for its long ears and powerful hind legs
large North American hare

Examples

The jackrabbit bounded swiftly across the dry desert plain.
We spotted a jackrabbit hiding among the tall sagebrush during our hike.
Unlike most rabbits, the jackrabbit uses its powerful legs to make long leaps when escaping predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal similar to a rabbit but larger
  • Degree Lower: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears and soft fur

Etymology

Combination of 'jackass' (for its long ears) and 'rabbit'; originally American English.
