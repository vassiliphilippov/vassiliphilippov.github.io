Napoleon

1. PROPN

/ nəˈpoʊliən / (AmE), / nəˈpəʊliən / (BrE)
(1769–1821) French military leader and emperor known for his role in the Napoleonic Wars and shaping European history.
French military leader and emperor (1769–1821)

Examples

Many historians credit Napoleon with introducing significant reforms to the French legal system.
The leadership style of Napoleon is still studied in military academies today.
After his defeat at Waterloo, Napoleon was exiled to the island of Saint Helena.

Etymology

From French 'Napoléon', his given name.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈpoʊliən / (AmE), / nəˈpəʊliən / (BrE)
A card game for several players, similar to whist, in which players bid for the right to choose the trump suit and play for stakes.
a card game similar to whist

Examples

After dinner, they gathered in the living room to play a few rounds of Napoleon.
John taught us the rules of Napoleon during our weekly card night.
Many players enjoy the strategic bidding aspect of Napoleon.

Etymology

Named after Napoleon Bonaparte, possibly due to association with bold play or bidding.
