four-needled

1. ADJ

/ fɔr ˈniːdld / (AmE), / fɔː ˈniːdld / (BrE)
(of a conifer) having four needles per cluster or fascicle.
having four needles (conifers)

Examples

The pine species growing here is four-needled, which helps distinguish it from others in the region.
Botanists identified the four-needled conifer during their survey of the forest.
Many gardeners prefer planting four-needled evergreens for their unique appearance and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: two-needled - (of a conifer) having two needles per cluster
  • Degree Higher: five-needled - (of a conifer) having five needles per cluster
