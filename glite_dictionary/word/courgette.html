courgette

1. NOUN

/ kʊrˈʒɛt / (AmE), / kɔːˈʒɛt / (BrE)
A long, thin vegetable with smooth, dark green skin, eaten cooked as a vegetable; known as zucchini in American English.
long green vegetable (zucchini)
british

Examples

The recipe calls for one chopped courgette and a handful of cherry tomatoes.
She sliced the courgette thinly before adding it to the stir-fry.
Many gardeners grow courgettes during the summer months for their mild flavor and versatility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - A variety of summer squash, called 'courgette' in British English

Etymology

From French 'courgette', diminutive of 'courge' meaning 'gourd'.
