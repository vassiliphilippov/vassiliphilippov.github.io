Carduelis carduelis

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdjuːəlɪs kɑrˈdjuːəlɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs / (BrE)
A small European finch with a crimson face and distinctive yellow-and-black wings, also known as the European goldfinch.
European goldfinch species

Examples

The Carduelis carduelis is admired for its vibrant colors and melodic song.
Birdwatchers in Europe often spot the Carduelis carduelis among thistles and wildflowers.
The crimson face of the Carduelis carduelis makes it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldfinch - a small songbird with yellow plumage, especially the European goldfinch

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Carduelis' referring to goldfinches.
