waxing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwæksɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of applying wax to a surface for protection, shine, or hair removal.
application of wax to a surface

Examples

Car waxing helps protect the paint from environmental damage.
She booked an appointment for a leg waxing before her holiday.
Regular waxing of the floor keeps it looking shiny and new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polishing - the act of making something smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

From the verb 'wax' + -ing, denoting the action or process.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwæksɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the moon) describing the period when the visible part of the moon is increasing.
increasing phase of the moon

Examples

The waxing moon shone brightly in the early evening sky.
During the waxing phase, the moon appears larger each night.
Farmers once relied on the waxing moon to time their plantings.

Synset Examples

the waxing moon passes from new to full

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waning - (of the moon) describing the period when the visible part of the moon is decreasing

Etymology

From Old English 'weaxan' (to grow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02544973-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈwæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwæksɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual increase in the size, strength, or extent of something, especially the illuminated portion of the moon as seen from Earth
gradual increase (especially of the moon)

Examples

The waxing of the moon is clearly visible each night after the new moon.
Farmers once used the waxing and waning of the moon to plan their planting.
In ancient times, people celebrated the waxing of the days after the winter solstice.

Synset Examples

the waxing of the moon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waning - a gradual decrease in the illuminated portion of the moon

Etymology

From Old English 'weaxan' (to grow), related to German 'wachsen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07429166-n
Synset POS: n
