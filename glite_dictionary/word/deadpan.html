deadpan

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌpan / (BrE)
deliberately impassive or expressionless in manner, especially as a form of humor
impassive or expressionless in manner

Examples

His deadpan delivery made the joke even funnier.
She answered the absurd question in a perfectly deadpan manner.
The comedian was known for his deadpan expressions on stage.

Synset Examples

deadpan humor
his face remained expressionless as the verdict was read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressionless - showing no emotion or expression

Etymology

Originally US English, from 'dead' + 'pan' (face), meaning 'expressionless face'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503225-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˈdɛdˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌpan / (BrE)
in a deliberately emotionless and expressionless manner
emotionlessly; without visible feeling

Examples

The comedian delivered every line deadpan, making the punchlines even funnier.
She recited the absurd story deadpan, leaving everyone unsure if she was joking.
He replied deadpan to every question, barely changing his expression.

Synset Examples

she told the joke deadpan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressionless - showing no emotion; without expression

Etymology

From dead (emotionless) + pan (slang for face, from 1920s US English).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00158333-r
Synset POS: r
