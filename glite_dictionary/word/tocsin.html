tocsin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A bell or signal used to warn of danger or an emergency; an alarm bell.
alarm bell; warning signal

Examples

When the tocsin rang, the villagers rushed to the square in fear of an impending attack.
The revolutionary leaders ordered the tocsin sounded to warn citizens of danger.
At the first sound of the tocsin, everyone knew trouble was imminent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm - a warning of danger or emergency

Etymology

From French *tocsin*, from Old Occitan *tocsin* (from *tocar* 'to ring' + *senh* 'bell, sign').

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a bell or other device sounded as a warning or alarm, or the warning sound itself
a warning bell or alarm

Examples

The sudden tocsin startled everyone in the village.
When the enemy approached, the tocsin was sounded from the church tower.
Her speech was a tocsin warning of the dangers ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm - a warning sound or signal of danger

Etymology

From French *tocsin*, from Old Occitan *tocsin*, from *tocar* (to ring) + *senh* (bell).
