Lepas fascicularis

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpæs ˌfæˌsɪkjʊˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈliːpas ˌfasɪkjʊˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of stalked barnacle that attaches to ship hulls, floating wood, or debris in the ocean.
a species of stalked barnacle

Examples

The hull of the old ship was covered with clusters of Lepas fascicularis.
Floating logs washed ashore bearing colonies of Lepas fascicularis barnacles.
Marine biologists studied the distribution of Lepas fascicularis in the open ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose barnacle - a common name for stalked barnacles including Lepas species

Etymology

From Greek lepas (limpet, barnacle) and Latin fascicularis (forming bundles).
