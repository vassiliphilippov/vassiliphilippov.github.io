democrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛm.ə.kræt / (AmE), / ˈdɛm.ə.kræt / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates for democracy and democratic principles.
advocate of democracy

Examples

As a lifelong democrat, she believes that citizens should have the right to vote in fair elections.
The new constitution was praised by every democrat in the country.
Being a true democrat means respecting the voice of the people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autocrat - a ruler with absolute power
  • Degree Lower: republican - a person who supports a republican form of government (not party-specific)

Etymology

From French 'démocrate', from Greek 'dēmokratēs', meaning supporter of democracy.
