molestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊlɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒlɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making unwanted or improper sexual advances or engaging in sexual activity with someone, especially women or children
sexual harassment or abuse

Examples

The organization was founded to support victims of molestation and help them recover.
Schools have implemented stricter policies to prevent molestation of students.
Accusations of molestation were taken very seriously by the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual assault - unwanted sexual contact or activity without consent
  • Synonym: harassment - persistently annoying or attacking someone, especially sexually

Etymology

From Latin 'molestatio' (annoyance), from 'molestare' (to trouble).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊlɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒlɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
persistent harassment or torment, especially by repeated attacks or criticism
persistent harassment or torment

Examples

The constant molestation by her critics eventually wore down her confidence.
He complained of molestation at work through endless nitpicking and unfair accusations.
The villagers suffered years of molestation from enemies who raided their crops and homes repeatedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harassment - persistent annoying or abusive behavior toward someone
  • Synonym: persecution - persistent mistreatment or oppression
  • Antonym: protection - the act of keeping someone safe from harm or harassment

Etymology

From Latin molestatio, from molestare (to trouble) and molestus (troublesome).
