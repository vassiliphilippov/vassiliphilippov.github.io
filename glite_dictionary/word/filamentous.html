filamentous

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪləˈmɛntəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈmɛntəs / (BrE)
thin and thread-like in form or appearance; resembling a filament or thread
thread-like; thin in diameter

Examples

The algae have a filamentous structure, giving them a delicate, hair-like appearance.
Some bacteria grow in filamentous chains that can be seen under a microscope.
The filamentous roots of the plant helped it absorb water more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous - composed of or resembling fibers; thread-like

Etymology

From Latin 'filamentum' (a thread) from 'filum' (thread) + '-ous' (having the qualities of).
