maintenance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪntənəns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪntənəns / (BrE)
the process of keeping something in good working condition by regularly checking and repairing it
keeping something in good condition

Examples

Regular maintenance of your car can prevent costly repairs in the future.
The elevator is out of service today for routine maintenance.
Good computer maintenance includes updating software and removing malware.

Synset Examples

he wrote the manual on car care

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing something that is broken

Etymology

From Old French 'maintenance', from maintenir (to maintain), from Latin 'manu tenere' (to hold in the hand).

/ ˈmeɪntənəns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪntənəns / (BrE)

act of sustaining life or subsistence

Examples

Without adequate maintenance, many refugees faced hunger and disease.
His meager earnings were barely enough for the maintenance of his family.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪntənəns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnt(ə)nəns / (BrE)
the provision of financial or material support necessary for the upkeep of a family or group, especially as required by law
provision of support for a family or group

Examples

He was ordered by the court to pay maintenance to his ex-wife and children.
The charity relies on donations for the maintenance of the orphanage and its residents.
After the divorce, she struggled to secure adequate maintenance for her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - assistance or resources provided for sustenance
  • Synonym: alimony - court-ordered payment for spousal or child maintenance

Etymology

From Old French maintenance, from maintenir (to maintain), from Latin manutenere (to hold in the hand).

4. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪn.tənəns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪn.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
(Law) The improper involvement of a person who has no direct interest in a lawsuit, supporting one party to promote vexatious or unnecessary litigation.
improper support in legal action (law)
legal

Examples

The court found the lawyer guilty of maintenance for supporting a friend's lawsuit without personal stake.
Historically, maintenance was treated as a criminal offense to discourage external interference in court cases.
He was accused of maintenance after providing financial aid to continue litigation he was not involved in.

Synset Examples

unlike champerty, criminal maintenance does not necessarily involve personal profit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champerty - an agreement whereby a person assists a party in a lawsuit in exchange for a share of the proceeds

Etymology

From Old French 'maintenir' (to hold, keep), from Latin 'manu tenere' (to hold in the hand).

5. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪntənəns / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnt(ə)nəns / (BrE)
money that a court orders one person to pay regularly to their former spouse after separation or divorce, to support them financially.
court-ordered support for a former spouse
legal

Examples

After the divorce, he was required to pay maintenance to his ex-wife every month.
The couple went to court to determine the amount of maintenance owed.
She relies on maintenance payments to help cover the household expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimony - court-ordered payment from one spouse to another after divorce or separation
  • Synonym: child support - court-ordered payments to support a child after parents separate

Etymology

From Old French *maintenance* (support, assistance), from maintenir (to maintain), from Latin *manu tenere* (to hold in the hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13304643-n
Synset POS: n
