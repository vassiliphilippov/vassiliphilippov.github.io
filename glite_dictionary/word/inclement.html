inclement

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈklɛmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛm.ənt / (BrE)
(of weather) unpleasantly cold or wet; severe and stormy.
severe or unpleasant weather

Examples

Due to the inclement weather, the outdoor concert was postponed.
School was canceled because of inclement conditions brought by the winter storm.
We had to find shelter quickly as the forecast predicted inclement weather all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stormy - characterized by strong winds and rain
  • Antonym: mild - (of weather) gentle and not severe

Etymology

From Latin 'inclementem', meaning unmerciful, harsh.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈklɛmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛmɛnt / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) showing no mercy or clemency; harsh or severe.
merciless or harsh (of people or behavior)

Examples

The inclement judge delivered the maximum possible sentence without hesitation.
His inclement attitude toward mistakes created a tense environment in the office.
Justice seemed lost in the face of the prison warden's inclement treatment of inmates.

Synset Examples

the harsh sentence of an inclement judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy
  • Synonym: harsh - cruel or severe in treatment or effect
  • Antonym: clement - lenient or merciful

Etymology

From Latin inclementem, from in- (not) + clementem (mild, merciful).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
