disarming

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
tending to make someone less hostile or suspicious, often by charm or friendliness.
making less hostile or suspicious

Examples

She gave him a disarming smile that instantly eased the tension in the room.
His disarming honesty made it hard to stay angry with him for long.
The defendant spoke with a disarming sincerity that won over many jurors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in a way that delights others
  • Antonym: threatening - having a hostile or intimidating quality

Etymology

From the verb 'disarm' + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of reducing or depriving of weapons, especially as part of a military or political process.
act of removing weapons

Examples

The country's disarming of its nuclear weapons was seen as a major step toward peace.
International observers monitored the disarming of rebel groups after the ceasefire agreement.
Successful disarming operations often require cooperation from all parties involved.

Synset Examples

the disarmament of the aggressor nations must be complete

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disarmament - the act of reducing or eliminating military forces or weapons

Etymology

From the verb 'disarm' + -ing; related to 'disarmament'.
