Stizostedion vitreum

1. NOUN

/ stɪˌzoʊˈstɛdiːɒn ˈvɪtriəm / (AmE), / stɪˌzɒsˈtiːdiɒn ˈvɪtriəm / (BrE)
A species of pike-like freshwater fish commonly known as the walleye, native to North America.
species of freshwater fish (walleye)

Examples

The Stizostedion vitreum is highly prized by anglers across the Midwest for its flavorful meat.
Researchers studied the spawning habits of Stizostedion vitreum in various North American lakes.
Commercial fishing regulations often set strict limits on catching Stizostedion vitreum populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walleye - the common name for Stizostedion vitreum

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'stizo' (to prick) and 'ostedion' (little bone); 'vitreum' from Latin meaning 'glassy', referring to the fish's eyes.
