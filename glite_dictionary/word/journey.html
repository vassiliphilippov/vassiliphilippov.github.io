journey

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɝːni / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːni / (BrE)
to travel from one place to another, typically over a long distance or for a significant purpose
to travel from one place to another

Examples

Every summer, the family would journey across the country to visit relatives.
She journeyed through the dense forest in search of the ancient temple.
They are journeying to new lands in hopes of finding a better life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, especially over a distance
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in the same place and not travel

Etymology

From Old French 'jornee' (a day’s work or travel), from Vulgar Latin 'diurnata' (a day).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːni / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːni / (BrE)
An act of traveling from one place to another, especially over a long distance.
act of traveling from one place to another

Examples

After a long journey across the country, they finally arrived at their destination.
She documented every stage of her journey through Asia in her travel blog.
The journey from childhood to adulthood can be challenging and rewarding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure
  • Synonym: voyage - a long journey involving travel by sea or in space

Etymology

From Old French *jornee* (a day's work or travel), from Vulgar Latin *diurnata* (a day's time).
