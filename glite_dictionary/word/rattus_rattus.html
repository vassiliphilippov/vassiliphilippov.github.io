Rattus rattus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætəs ˈrætəs / (AmE), / ˈratəs ˈratəs / (BrE)
A small, black, long-tailed rat originally from Asia, now widespread globally and often found as a household pest.
the black rat, a common pest species

Examples

The presence of Rattus rattus in urban areas poses significant health risks.
Researchers studied the spread of Rattus rattus throughout the coastal regions.
Historians believe Rattus rattus contributed to the transmission of the plague in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black rat - common name for Rattus rattus, a widespread pest rodent
  • Antonym: brown rat - Rattus norvegicus, another common household rat species

Etymology

From Latin, used as the scientific name for the black rat species.
