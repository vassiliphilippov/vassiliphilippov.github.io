affirmativeness

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɝːməˈtɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˌfɜːməˈtɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of expressing agreement or assent; a tendency to respond in an affirmative or positive way.
quality of expressing agreement or assent

Examples

Her affirmativeness during meetings helped the team reach consensus more quickly.
He responded to each suggestion with such affirmativeness that others felt encouraged to share their ideas.
The coach's affirmativeness inspired her players to believe in themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positiveness - the quality of having a positive attitude or manner
  • Antonym: negativeness - the quality of expressing or holding a negative attitude or response

Etymology

From 'affirmative' + '-ness'; 'affirmative' from Latin affirmativus, from affirmare (to affirm).
