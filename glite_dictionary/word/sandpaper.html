sandpaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈsændˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
stiff paper coated with abrasive powder, such as sand or emery, used for smoothing or polishing surfaces
abrasive paper for smoothing surfaces

Examples

He used sandpaper to smooth the rough edges of the wooden table.
The painter recommends using fine-grit sandpaper before applying the final coat.
Make sure you replace the sandpaper on the sander regularly for the best results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emery paper - paper coated with powdered emery, used as an abrasive

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'paper', referring to its original use of sand as the abrasive material.

2. VERB

/ ˈsændˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈsændˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
to rub or smooth a surface with sandpaper, typically to make it even or prepare it for finishing.
to smooth by rubbing with sandpaper

Examples

Before painting, be sure to sandpaper the surface to remove any splinters.
He carefully sandpapered the edges of the table to make them smooth.
After cutting the wood, she spent an hour sandpapering it to achieve a flawless finish.

Synset Examples

sandpaper the wooden surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - to make something flat, even, or free of roughness
