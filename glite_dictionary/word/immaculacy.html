immaculacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmækjʊləsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmakjʊləsi / (BrE)
The state of being perfectly clean, pure, or free from blemish or stain.
the state of being immaculate; purity

Examples

The bride's dress displayed a level of immaculacy rarely seen in wedding attire.
His reputation for moral immaculacy made him a respected leader in the community.
Years of diligent care preserved the immaculacy of the ancient manuscript.

Synset Examples

Sadly he did not uphold his immaculacy but succumbed to his lustful passions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculateness - the quality of being immaculate
  • Synonym: purity - the state of being pure
  • Antonym: stain - a mark or blemish; impurity

Etymology

From Latin immaculatus (unstained) + -acy (forming nouns of quality or state).
