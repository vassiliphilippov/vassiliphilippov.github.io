assignor

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪnɔr / (AmE), / əˈsaɪnɔː / (BrE)
(law) a person or party who legally transfers a right, interest, or property to another (the assignee).
person who transfers a legal right or interest
legal

Examples

The assignor must ensure that all relevant documents are properly signed before transferring ownership.
In this contract, the assignor agrees to transfer all intellectual property rights to the assignee.
If the assignor fails to notify the debtor, the assignment may not be effective.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assignee - the person to whom a right or property is assigned

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman French assignour, from Old French assigner (to assign) + -or (agent noun suffix).
