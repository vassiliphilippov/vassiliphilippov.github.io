reserves

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɜrvz / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːvz / (BrE)
Civilians who are trained as soldiers and can be called upon for military service but are not part of the regular, full-time armed forces.
trained civilians in military reserve forces

Examples

After leaving active duty, he joined the reserves to serve his country part-time.
The government can call up the reserves in times of national emergency.
Many reserves train on weekends but maintain civilian jobs during the week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force raised from the civilian population
