blue-chip

1. ADJ

/ bluː tʃɪp / (AmE), / bluː tʃɪp / (BrE)
Extremely valuable and reliable, especially referring to investments, companies, or assets considered to be a safe and profitable choice.
extremely valuable and reliable

Examples

Many investors prefer to buy blue-chip stocks for their stability and consistent returns.
Her reputation as a blue-chip consultant has earned her clients in multiple industries.
The museum acquired several blue-chip artworks for its modern collection.

Synset Examples

Rembrandt is considered a blue-chip artist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-quality - of very good standard or level; excellent
  • Antonym: risky - involving the possibility of loss or danger; not safe or certain

Etymology

Originally referring to blue poker chips, which had the highest value, and later associated with stocks of companies considered stable and valuable.
