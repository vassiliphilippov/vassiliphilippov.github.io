Richard I of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪ.tʃərd ðə fɜrst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ðə fɜːst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
King of England from 1189 to 1199, renowned for his leadership in the Third Crusade, also known as Richard the Lionheart.
King of England (1189–1199); Third Crusade leader

Examples

Richard I of England was a prominent figure during the Third Crusade, admired for his bravery in battle.
The reign of Richard I of England was marked by frequent military campaigns abroad.
Many legends surround Richard I of England, especially his daring exploits as 'the Lionheart.'
