Setaria

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / sɪˈtɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial grasses known as bristlegrasses, native to warm regions.
genus of grasses, bristlegrasses

Examples

Many species of Setaria are important as forage grasses for livestock.
The bristly seed heads of Setaria can be easily recognized in the wild.
Researchers are studying Setaria for its potential as a drought-resistant crop.

Etymology

From Latin *seta* (bristle), alluding to the bristly flower heads.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / sɪˈtɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial grasses found in warm regions, commonly known as bristlegrasses
a genus of bristlegrasses

Examples

Several species in the genus Setaria are considered troublesome weeds in agriculture.
The distinctive bristly seed heads help identify grasses of the genus Setaria.
Researchers studied the adaptability of Setaria to drought conditions in semi-arid regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'seta' meaning 'bristle', referring to the bristly spikes of the grasses.
