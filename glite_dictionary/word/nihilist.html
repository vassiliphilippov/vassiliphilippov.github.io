nihilist

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɪɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who rejects all established religious and moral principles, often in the belief that life is meaningless; sometimes, an advocate of anarchism.
one who rejects established beliefs or values

Examples

The nihilist rejected all traditional sources of authority in his writings.
Some see the nihilist philosophy as profoundly pessimistic.
She was called a nihilist because of her disdain for social conventions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchist - someone who opposes all forms of official authority and advocates self-governed societies

Etymology

From Russian 'nihilist', from Latin 'nihil' (nothing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɪ.ɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who rejects all religious and moral principles, often believing that life is meaningless
person who rejects religious and moral principles

Examples

As a self-proclaimed nihilist, he found it difficult to commit to any cause or ideology.
Some people accuse modern artists of being nihilists because their work rejects traditional values.
The philosopher was labeled a nihilist for doubting the existence of objective moral truths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - a person who adheres to religious faith or principles

Etymology

From Latin 'nihil' (nothing), via Russian 'nihilist'.
