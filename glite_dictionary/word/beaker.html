beaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːkər / (AmE), / ˈbiːkə / (BrE)
A wide cylindrical container with a flat bottom, usually made of glass or plastic, used in laboratories for mixing, stirring, and heating liquids.
laboratory glass container

Examples

The scientist poured the substance carefully into the beaker to avoid spilling it.
After heating the solution in the beaker, she observed a color change.
Make sure the beaker is clean before starting your experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - A narrow-necked laboratory vessel for holding liquids
  • Synonym: test tube - A small glass tube used in laboratories to hold small amounts of material for experiments

Etymology

From Middle English *beker*, from Old Norse *bikarr* or Middle Dutch *beker*, meaning a cup or goblet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːkər / (AmE), / ˈbiːkə / (BrE)
A cup or container, usually cylindrical and without a handle, often used in laboratories or for drinking.
cylindrical cup or container, usually without handle

Examples

She drank her orange juice from a plastic beaker with no handle.
The laboratory technician poured the solution into a glass beaker.
At the picnic, each child received a colorful beaker for their drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup - a small open container for drinking
  • Synonym: goblet - a bowl-shaped drinking vessel without handles
  • Antonym: mug - a sturdy cup with a handle

Etymology

From Middle English *biker*, from Old Norse *bikarr*, from Latin *bicarium*; possibly influenced by Greek *bikos* (a drinking vessel).
