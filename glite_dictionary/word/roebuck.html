roebuck

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌbʌk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊˌbʌk / (BrE)
A male roe deer.
male roe deer

Examples

A solitary roebuck grazed at the edge of the forest.
During the hike, we spotted a roebuck standing alert among the trees.
The antlers of a mature roebuck are relatively short but distinctive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe deer - a small Eurasian deer with short antlers and a reddish or grey-brown coat

Etymology

From Old English *rābuc*, from *rā* (“roe”) + *buc* (“buck, male deer”).
