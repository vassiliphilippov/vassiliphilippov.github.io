nonlinear

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / nɒnˈlɪniə / (BrE)
(of an equation, system, or function) not expressed or involving terms of the first degree only; not linear.
not involving only first-degree terms

Examples

The scientist explained the behavior of the system using a nonlinear equation.
Climate models are complex because many natural processes are nonlinear.
Nonlinear relationships can produce unexpected results in data analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linear - (of equations or functions) involving only terms of the first degree; arranged in a straight line

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'linear', from Latin 'linearis' (pertaining to a line).
