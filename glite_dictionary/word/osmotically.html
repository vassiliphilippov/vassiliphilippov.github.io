osmotically

1. ADV

/ ɑzˈmɑtɪkli / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɒtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to or involving osmosis.
involving osmosis
medical

Examples

The cells absorbed water osmotically through their membranes.
Salt is used to preserve food by affecting how water moves osmotically across cell walls.
The drug enters the bloodstream osmotically, bypassing traditional absorption routes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osmosis - the movement of a solvent through a semipermeable membrane

Etymology

From 'osmosis' + '-ally', from Greek 'ōsmos' (a pushing, thrusting).
