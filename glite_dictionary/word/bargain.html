bargain

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡən / (BrE)
to discuss and agree on the terms of a transaction or arrangement, often to obtain a better deal
negotiate an agreement

Examples

They spent hours trying to bargain with the car dealer over the final price.
Farmers would often bargain at the market to get the best deal for their goods.
She refused to bargain, insisting the price was already fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - try to reach an agreement through discussion
  • Synonym: compromise - to settle a dispute by mutual concessions
  • Antonym: refuse - to decline to agree or accept terms

Etymology

From Old French 'bargaignier' (to haggle), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑɹɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
something bought or offered for sale at a price lower than its true value; an advantageous purchase.
something bought cheaply; good deal

Examples

I found a fantastic bargain on winter coats during the end-of-season sale.
She always looks for a bargain when shopping online for electronics.
At the flea market, he considered the vintage lamp a real bargain for only five dollars.

Synset Examples

she got a bargain at the auction
the stock was a real buy at that price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal - an agreement or arrangement, especially one that is advantageous

Etymology

From Old French 'bargaignier' (to haggle, trade), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
to negotiate the terms of a transaction or exchange, often with the aim of obtaining a lower price or better terms
negotiate the terms of an exchange

Examples

We spent hours trying to bargain with the street vendors over the price of souvenirs.
She refused to bargain and paid the amount on the price tag.
Tourists often enjoy the experience of bargaining in local markets.

Synset Examples

We bargained for a beautiful rug in the bazaar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - to try to reach agreement by discussion
  • Synonym: haggle - to argue persistently, especially about a price

Etymology

From Old French *bargaignier* (to haggle, bargain), from Medieval Latin *barcaniare*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02264636-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
an agreement between two or more parties, especially after negotiation, fixing what each party must do or provide
agreement after negotiation

Examples

After hours of discussion, they finally reached a bargain that satisfied both sides.
He felt that the bargain they struck was fair and mutually beneficial.
Breaking the bargain could result in serious consequences for both companies.

Synset Examples

he made a bargain with the devil
he rose to prominence through a series of shady deals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • Synonym: contract - a formal or legally binding agreement

Etymology

From Old French *bargaignier* (to haggle, bargain), likely of Germanic origin.
