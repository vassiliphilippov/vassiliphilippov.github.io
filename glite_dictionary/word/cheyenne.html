Cheyenne

1. PROPN

/ ʃaɪˈæn / (AmE), / ʃaɪˈæn / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Wyoming, located in the southeastern corner of the state
capital city of Wyoming, USA

Examples

We spent a night in Cheyenne during our road trip across the United States.
The state government of Wyoming is based in Cheyenne.
Cheyenne hosts an annual rodeo that attracts visitors from all over the country.

Etymology

Named after the Cheyenne Native American people.

2. NOUN

/ ʃaɪˈæn / (AmE), / ʃaɪˈæn / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Cheyenne people of the Great Plains
Algonquian language of the Cheyenne people

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Cheyenne.
A dictionary of the Cheyenne language was published in the 20th century.
Children in the community are taught Cheyenne to keep their heritage alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages including Cheyenne

Etymology

From Dakota *šahíyena*, referring to the Cheyenne people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06921476-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ʃaɪˈæn / (AmE), / ʃaɪˈæn / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the western plains, now living mainly in Oklahoma and Montana
member of Native American people (Oklahoma, Montana)

Examples

Several Cheyenne attended the intertribal council meeting in Oklahoma.
She is proud of her Cheyenne heritage and traditions.
The Cheyenne were historically skilled horsemen and hunters on the Great Plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - member of a group of Native American peoples of North America

Etymology

From Dakota Sioux *šahíyena*, referring to the people.
