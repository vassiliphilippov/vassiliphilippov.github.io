Anemopsis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛm əp sɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɛm ɒp sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the family Saururaceae, containing the single species yerba mansa, native to southwestern North America.
genus of herbaceous plant (yerba mansa)

Examples

Anemopsis is best known for its only species, yerba mansa, found in wet habitats.
Botanists have studied the medicinal properties of Anemopsis for generations.
The flowers of Anemopsis are distinctive with their white petal-like bracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yerba mansa - the sole species of Anemopsis; a perennial herb

Etymology

From Greek anemos (wind) + -opsis (aspect, appearance), likely referring to the plant's habitat or appearance.
