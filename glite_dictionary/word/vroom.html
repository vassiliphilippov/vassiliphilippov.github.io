vroom

1. NOUN

/ vruːm / (AmE), / vruːm / (BrE)
the loud, roaring sound produced by a motor vehicle when it is running at high speed
roaring sound of a fast motor

Examples

The vroom of the sports car could be heard from blocks away.
Kids imitated the vroom of motorcycles as they played with their toy cars.
Suddenly, the garage filled with the vroom of a powerful engine starting up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rev - to increase the running speed of an engine; the sound produced by this action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414220-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ vruːm / (AmE), / vruːm / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or engine) to make a loud, roaring sound while moving, typically imitating the noise of a powerful engine.
make a loud, roaring engine sound

Examples

The sports car vroomed down the highway, turning heads as it passed.
Kids love to vroom around the room with their toy cars.
As the motorcycle vroomed by, everyone covered their ears from the noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rev - to increase the running speed of an engine

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a revving engine.
