rock-loving

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑk ˌlʌvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌlʌvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) thriving in a rocky environment; adapted to live among rocks.
thriving in a rocky environment (plants)

Examples

The rock-loving moss was found clinging to the side of the granite boulder.
Gardeners often select rock-loving plants for their alpine displays and stone gardens.
Many rock-loving species have adapted to survive with very little soil and water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcicolous - (of a plant) growing well in lime-rich, calcareous soils; similar ecological niche
  • Degree Lower: shade-loving - (of a plant) thriving in shaded conditions
  • Degree Higher: sun-loving - (of a plant) thriving in full sunlight
