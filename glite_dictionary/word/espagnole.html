Espagnole

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˌpænˈjoʊl / (AmE), / ɛsˌpanˈjəʊl / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce made from a brown stock, tomatoes, and a caramelized mixture of carrots, onions, and celery, typically seasoned with Madeira; used as a base in French cuisine.
classic French brown sauce

Examples

The chef used Espagnole as the base for his beef stew, giving it a deep, savory flavor.
Learning how to make Espagnole is essential in mastering classic French sauces.
Our house-made demi-glace starts with a carefully prepared Espagnole.

Etymology

From French espagnole (Spanish), referring to the Spanish style.
