riser

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈraɪzə / (BrE)
the vertical part between two stair treads in a set of steps or a staircase
vertical part of a stair step

Examples

The carpenter measured each riser to make sure the stairs were even.
Don't forget to paint the riser as well as the tread for a complete finish.
Building codes often specify the maximum height for a staircase riser.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tread - the horizontal part of a step where the foot rests

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈraɪzə / (BrE)
a vertical pipe in a building that carries water, gas, or waste to different floors
vertical pipe in a building

Examples

The plumber had to replace the old riser to ensure water reached the upper floors.
During the renovation, the main riser was inspected for leaks and corrosion.
Every apartment unit is connected to a common riser that supplies hot water.

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who gets out of bed at a particular time, especially regularly
person who gets out of bed

Examples

He's always been an early riser, starting his day before sunrise.
My sister is a late riser and prefers to sleep in on weekends.
Among the team, only John was a regular riser during the training camp.

Synset Examples

he's usually a late riser

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleeper - a person who is asleep

Etymology

From rise + -er, forming agent nouns.

4. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈraɪzə / (BrE)
someone who gets out of bed at a particular time, especially an early or late time
person who gets up at a certain time

Examples

As a riser, he enjoys the quiet mornings.
My sister is an early riser and always has breakfast before anyone else.
Late risers often find it hard to adjust to early work schedules.

Synset Examples

As a riser, he enjoys the quiet mornings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early bird - a person who gets up or arrives early
  • Antonym: night owl - a person who goes to bed late and prefers staying up late
  • Antonym: late riser - someone who wakes up late

Etymology

From the verb 'rise' + '-er' (one who rises).
