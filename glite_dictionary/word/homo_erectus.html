Homo erectus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ ɪˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ɪˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of early human, noted for its upright posture and relatively small brain, that lived between about 1.9 million and 110,000 years ago
extinct early human species

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that Homo erectus used simple tools and controlled fire.
Scientists believe Homo erectus migrated out of Africa into Asia and Europe.
The skull shape of Homo erectus differs from that of modern humans.

Synset Examples

Homo erectus was formerly called Pithecanthropus erectus

Etymology

From Latin: 'homo' (man, human) + 'erectus' (upright).
