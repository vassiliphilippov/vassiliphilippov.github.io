Hanover

1. PROPN

/ ˈhænˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhænəʊvə / (BrE)
The English royal house that ruled from 1714 to 1901, beginning with George I and ending with Queen Victoria.
English royal house (1714–1901)

Examples

Queen Victoria was the last monarch from the Hanover dynasty.
The Hanover line originated in Germany before taking the British throne.
Many political changes occurred in Britain during the reign of the Hanover family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Windsor - the English royal house that succeeded Hanover, from 1917 to present

Etymology

From the German city name Hannover; adopted by the royal house due to its origin in the Electorate of Hanover.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhænˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhanəʊvə / (BrE)
A port city in northwestern Germany, known for its historical significance and former membership in the Hanseatic League.
port city in northwestern Germany

Examples

The trade fair in Hanover attracts visitors from around the world every year.
During their trip through Germany, they spent two days exploring Hanover.
Historically, Hanover played an important role as a member of the Hanseatic League.
