Megachile

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkaɪli / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkʌɪli / (BrE)
A genus of bees in the family Megachilidae, commonly known as leaf-cutting bees.
genus of leaf-cutting bees

Examples

The Megachile are best known for their distinctive leaf-cutting behavior.
Several species of Megachile are important pollinators in gardens and farms.
Entomologists have identified a new Megachile species in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafcutter bee - bee that cuts leaves to build nests, member of Megachile genus

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) + 'cheilos' (lip), referring to the large mandibles of these bees.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkaɪli / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkaɪli / (BrE)
A genus of bees in the family Megachilidae, commonly known as leaf-cutting bees, which are known for cutting leaves to construct their nests.
genus of leaf-cutting bees

Examples

Many Megachile species play a crucial role in pollination in both natural and agricultural ecosystems.
The distinctive leaf-cutting behavior is characteristic of bees in the Megachile genus.
Scientists discovered a new Megachile species in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek mega (large) + cheilos (lip), referring to the structure of the bees' mouthparts.
