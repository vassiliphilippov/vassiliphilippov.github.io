turnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp / (BrE)
a round root vegetable with white or yellowish flesh, belonging to the mustard family, often eaten cooked or raw
root vegetable of the mustard family

Examples

She added diced turnip to the stew for extra flavor.
Farmers harvested a bumper crop of turnips this season.
Raw turnip can be grated into salads for a crunchy texture.

Etymology

From Latin *napus* (turnip) via Middle English and Old English *nepe* with prefix alteration.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪp / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪp / (BrE)
a round root vegetable with white or yellow flesh and a purple, red, or greenish top, grown as food for people or livestock
edible root vegetable

Examples

I made a hearty stew with potatoes, carrots, and turnip.
A single large turnip was enough to feed the family.
They planted rows of turnips in the field last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - a related root vegetable, also called Swedish turnip or swede

Etymology

From Middle English turnep, possibly from Old French *tournepe*, from Latin *napus* (turnip).
