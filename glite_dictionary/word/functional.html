functional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl / (BrE)
(of a machine, system, etc.) working correctly or able to perform its intended purpose.
working correctly; able to perform

Examples

The air conditioner was finally functional after the repair technician fixed it.
We checked to make sure the safety equipment was functional before starting the experiment.
Although the building is old, the elevator is still functional and safe to use.

Synset Examples

in running (or working) order
a functional set of brakes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broken - not working; not functional
  • Synonym: operational - in working order; able to function

Etymology

From Latin 'functionalis', from 'functio' (performance, execution).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01095421-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl / (BrE)
designed to be practical and useful rather than decorative; intended for or capable of serving a particular function.
designed to serve a specific function or use

Examples

The design of the new office is highly functional, focusing on efficiency and workflow.
We chose a functional sofa that doubles as a guest bed.
Her kitchen is both stylish and functional for preparing large meals.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decorative - intended to look attractive rather than be useful

Etymology
