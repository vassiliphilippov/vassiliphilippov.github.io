Masorete

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsəˌrit / (AmE), / ˈmasəˌriːt / (BrE)
a Jewish scholar, especially one of the scribes from the 6th to 10th centuries CE, who contributed to the development and preservation of the Masorah—authoritative notes and annotations on the Hebrew Scriptures.
Jewish scholar of the Masorah

Examples

The Masorete carefully annotated the ancient manuscripts to preserve the correct pronunciation of each word.
Many linguistic scholars study the work of the Masoretes to understand the transmission of biblical texts.
A renowned Masorete lived in Tiberias during the 9th century, contributing to the tradition of the Masorah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Masorah - authoritative Jewish tradition concerning the correct text of the Hebrew Scriptures

Etymology

From Medieval Hebrew māṣōrēth, from Masorah (the tradition of textual transmission of Hebrew Scripture).
