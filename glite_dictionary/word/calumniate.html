to calumniate

1. VERB

/ kəˈlʌm.jiˌeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlʌm.nɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to make false and malicious statements about someone in order to damage their reputation
falsely accuse; slander

Examples

The politician claimed his opponents tried to calumniate him during the heated campaign.
She was unfairly calumniated by coworkers who were jealous of her promotion.
It is unethical to calumniate someone's character without substantial evidence.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - to make a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone; libel or slander
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'calumniari' (to accuse falsely), from 'calumnia' (false accusation).
