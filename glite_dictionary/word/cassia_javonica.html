Cassia javonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsiə dʒəˈvɑnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkæsɪə dʒɑːˈvɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous ornamental tree native to southeastern Asia and Hawaii, known for its racemes of cream, orange, or red flowers.
ornamental tree with colorful raceme flowers

Examples

The Cassia javonica is prized in tropical gardens for its spectacular floral displays.
Clusters of Cassia javonica blossoms transform city streets during the spring.
Gardeners recommend planting Cassia javonica for its fast growth and vibrant colors.

Etymology

Cassia from Latin, genus name; javonica indicates origin from Java.
