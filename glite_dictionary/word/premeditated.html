premeditated

1. ADJ

/ priˈmɛdəˌteɪtɪd / (AmE), / priːˈmɛdɪteɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an action, especially a crime) done deliberately and with planning, rather than being spontaneous.
planned or deliberate (especially for crimes)

Examples

The judge found the attack to be premeditated and sentenced the defendant accordingly.
It was clear from the evidence that the theft was premeditated rather than a crime of opportunity.
A premeditated action often carries a heavier legal penalty than a spontaneous one.

Synset Examples

a premeditated crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without planning; impulsive

Etymology

From Latin praemeditatus, past participle of praemeditari ('to think over beforehand').
