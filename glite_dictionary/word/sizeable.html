sizeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
fairly large in amount or extent; fairly big
fairly large in size or amount

Examples

The company made a sizeable investment in new technology last year.
We received a sizeable donation from an anonymous supporter.
There was a sizeable crowd waiting outside the stadium before the concert.

Synset Examples

a sizable fortune
an ample waistline
of ample proportions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: large - of more than average size or amount
  • Synonym: substantial - of considerable size or amount
  • Antonym: small - of a size that is less than normal or usual

Etymology

From 'size' + '-able', modelled after similar constructions such as 'palatable'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
fairly large in amount, extent, or degree; considerable.
fairly large; considerable

Examples

They donated a sizeable amount of money to the charity.
Owning a sizeable piece of land in the city is quite rare.
The project required a sizeable investment to get started.

Synset Examples

it cost a considerable amount
a goodly amount
received a hefty bonus
a respectable sum
a tidy sum of money
a sizable fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • Degree Lower: moderate - average in amount, intensity, quality, or degree
  • Degree Higher: immense - extremely large or great, especially in scale or degree

Etymology

From 'size' + '-able' suffix, meaning capable of being sized; late 18th century.
