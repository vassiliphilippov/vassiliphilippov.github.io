robust

1. ADJ

/ roʊˈbʌst / (AmE), / rəˈbʌst / (BrE)
(of language, humor, stories, etc.) strong and direct, sometimes lacking refinement; rough and crude.
strong or crude in style

Examples

The comedian's robust humor was not to everyone's taste.
The novel is filled with robust tales of adventure and mischief.
His robust language shocked the more refined members of the audience.

Synset Examples

a robust tale

Etymology

From Latin robustus (strong, hardy), from robur (oak, strength).

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈbʌst / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌst / (BrE)
(of food or drink) having a strong, rich, and full flavor.
strong and full flavor (food/drink)

Examples

The Chianti was especially robust, with notes of blackberry and spice.
She prefers a robust coffee first thing in the morning to start her day.
This dish pairs well with a robust red wine to balance the flavors.

Related Terms
