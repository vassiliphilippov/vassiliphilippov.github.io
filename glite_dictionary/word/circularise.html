to circularise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɜːr.kjə.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to distribute circulars or notices to a group of people
distribute circulars/notices
british

Examples

The company decided to circularise all its customers about the new policy.
After the meeting, they circularised members with an update on the proposed changes.
Marketing teams often circularise potential clients to raise awareness of their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circularize - distribute circulars to (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'circular', a notice sent to many people, plus '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02047558-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.kjə.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
To canvass or inform people by sending out circular letters, especially to a large group.
send out circular letters to canvass
british

Examples

The company decided to circularise its shareholders about the upcoming changes.
Please circularise the details of the meeting to all staff.
The charity circularised thousands of people to raise awareness of their campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circularize - American English spelling of 'circularise'

Etymology

From 'circular' (letter) + '-ise', indicating 'to make' or 'to do' (British spelling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02460071-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make something widely known or disseminated, especially information or a rumor
make widely known; disseminate

Examples

The company decided to circularise news of the merger to all employees and stakeholders.
Rumors about the policy change were quickly circularised throughout the organization.
Efforts were made to circularise information about the upcoming event to ensure maximum attendance.

Synset Examples

spread information
circulate a rumor
broadcast the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circulate - cause to become widely known or circulated
  • Synonym: spread - to extend over a large area, or make widely known

Etymology

From 'circular' + '-ise', referring to sending out a circular (letter or notice) to many people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00970243-v
Synset POS: v
