algae

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈæl.dʒiː / (BrE)
Simple plant-like organisms, mostly aquatic, that contain chlorophyll but lack true stems, roots, and leaves.
aquatic plant-like organisms

Examples

The scientist studied the growth patterns of algae in the freshwater lake.
Some species of algae can be used to produce biofuel, making them an important resource for energy research.
A green layer of algae formed on the surface of the aquarium water.

Etymology

From Latin 'alga' meaning seaweed.
