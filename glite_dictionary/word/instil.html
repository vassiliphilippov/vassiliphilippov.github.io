to instil

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈstɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪl / (BrE)
to introduce or administer a liquid drop by drop, especially into a body part
administer drop by drop (esp. liquid)

Examples

The instructions say to instil two drops into each eye every morning.
Doctors often instil fluids into the ear to treat infections.
After the injury, a nurse carefully instilled saline into the wound.

Synset Examples

instill medication into my eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instill - alternate spelling (primarily American) for 'instil'

Etymology

From Latin 'instillare' meaning 'to drop in'.
