articulate

1. VERB

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express an idea or feeling clearly in words
express clearly in words

Examples

She struggled to articulate her thoughts during the interview.
The professor articulated his vision for the new program clearly and confidently.
It is important to articulate your goals when starting a new project.

Synset Examples

He formulated his concerns to the board of trustees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: express - to communicate thoughts or feelings in words or by gestures and conduct

Etymology

From Latin articulatus (distinct, clear), past participle of articulare (to divide into joints, utter distinctly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00982485-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express something clearly in speech or to pronounce words distinctly
to pronounce or express clearly

Examples

She struggled to articulate her thoughts during the presentation.
The speech therapist helped him articulate difficult sounds.
Can you articulate each word clearly so everyone understands?

Related Terms

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (to divide into joints, utter distinctly).

3. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
(of an animal or structure) consisting of segments joined together by joints.
made of jointed segments

Examples

Many insects possess an articulate exoskeleton that allows for complex movement.
The crab’s articulate limbs help it scuttle quickly along the ocean floor.
A worm’s body is not articulate, unlike that of an ant with clearly defined joints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jointed - having joints or segments joined by joints

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (to divide into joints).

4. VERB

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to connect by a joint; to provide with joints so as to enable movement between parts
connect by a joint

Examples

The engineer articulated the two metal rods to allow for flexible movement.
Many animal skeletons are articulated to demonstrate how their bones connect at the joints.
Modern prosthetic limbs are carefully articulated to mimic natural movement.

Synset Examples

the carpenter jointed two pieces of wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - to connect by a joint, especially in carpentry

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare 'to divide into joints'.

5. VERB

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of bones or body parts) to be joined by a joint, or to connect with a joint; to form a joint.
connect by a joint (esp. in anatomy)

Examples

In the human knee, the femur and tibia articulate to allow for movement.
The ribs articulate with the vertebrae at two distinct points on each rib.
Some dinosaurs had jaws that could articulate with the skull to widen their gape.

Synset Examples

the ankle bone articulates with the leg bones to form the ankle bones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a point at which parts of an artificial structure are joined

Etymology

From Latin articulare (to divide into joints), from articulus (joint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02628718-v
Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express thoughts or feelings clearly and effectively in speech or writing
express clearly and effectively

Examples

It is important for leaders to articulate their vision clearly to their teams.
Despite being nervous, he articulated his ideas with confidence during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mumble - to speak unclearly so that words are difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (to divide into joints, utter distinctly), from articulus (small joint).

7. ADJ

/ ɑrˈtɪkjələt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
able to express thoughts and ideas clearly and effectively, especially in speech or writing; characterized by clear, expressive language.
expressing ideas clearly and effectively

Examples

She is an articulate spokesperson, always expressing her thoughts with clarity and confidence.
Despite his nervousness, he gave an articulate explanation of the complex topic.
It is important for leaders to be articulate when addressing the public.

Synset Examples

an articulate orator
articulate beings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • Antonym: inarticulate - unable to express thoughts or ideas clearly or effectively

Etymology

From Latin articulatus (distinct, jointed), past participle of articulare (to divide into joints).
