self-assertive

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈsɜrtɪv / (AmE), / ˌself əˈsɜːtɪv / (BrE)
behaving confidently and forcefully to express one's own opinions or desires, sometimes to the point of being aggressive
confidently forceful; assertive

Examples

Her self-assertive attitude often made her stand out in group discussions.
A self-assertive child may challenge authority more frequently than a shy one.
He adopted a self-assertive tone during the debate, refusing to back down.

Synset Examples

an energetic assertive boy who was always ready to argue
pointing directly at a listener is an assertive act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others; meekly obedient

Etymology

From 'self' + 'assertive', meaning assertive about oneself.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈsɝtɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈsɜːtɪv / (BrE)
confidently and forcefully expressing your own views, sometimes in an aggressive or unwelcome way
confident and forceful in expressing oneself

Examples

Her self-assertive attitude sometimes comes across as aggressive in team meetings.
The self-assertive child challenged every rule the teacher made.
Some admired his self-assertive nature, while others found it a bit overbearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others; meekly obedient

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'assertive', the latter from Latin 'assertus' (claimed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00206625-s
Synset POS: s
