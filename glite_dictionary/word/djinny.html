djinny

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪni / (AmE), / dʒɪni / (BrE)
(in Islamic tradition) an invisible spirit, as described in the Koran, believed to live on earth and exert influence by appearing in human or animal form.
supernatural spirit from Islamic tradition

Examples

According to legend, a djinny can grant wishes if summoned correctly.
The story describes a djinny that appeared as a black cat to warn travelers.
Some villagers believe that a mischievous djinny is responsible for the strange happenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: djinn - a supernatural spirit in Islamic belief, usually invisible to humans
  • Synonym: genie - a spirit or supernatural being in Middle Eastern folklore, often granting wishes

Etymology

Variant of 'djinn', from Arabic 'jinn' (supernatural being or spirit).
