erythropoietin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθroʊˈpɔɪɪtɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊˈpɔɪɪtɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced mainly by the kidneys that stimulates the production of red blood cells in the bone marrow
hormone stimulating red blood cell production
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed erythropoietin to help treat his anemia.
Athletes have been caught using erythropoietin to improve their endurance illegally.
A deficiency of erythropoietin can lead to reduced red blood cell counts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epoetin - a synthetic form of erythropoietin used as a medication

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'poiein' (to make) + '-in' (protein suffix).
