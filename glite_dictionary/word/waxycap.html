waxycap

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæksiˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈwæksiˌkap / (BrE)
A fungus of the family Hygrophoraceae, characterized by gills that have a waxy texture or appearance.
fungus with waxy gills (Hygrophoraceae)

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bright red waxycap mushrooms after the rain.
Many species of waxycap are prized for their striking colors and unusual textures.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of several waxycap varieties in European forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygrophoraceae - a family of fungi including waxycap mushrooms
