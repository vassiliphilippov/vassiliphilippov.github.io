catnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt.nɪp / (AmE), / ˈkat.nɪp / (BrE)
A hairy, aromatic perennial herb (Nepeta cataria) with small white, purple-spotted flowers, especially known for its strong attraction to cats and sometimes used in herbal remedies.
an aromatic herb attractive to cats

Examples

The catnip in the garden attracts every neighborhood cat.
She bought a new toy stuffed with catnip for her kitten.
Many herbalists once used catnip to make soothing teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nepeta - another term for catnip, the plant genus
  • Degree Lower: valerian - herb sometimes attractive to cats, alternative stimulant

Etymology

From 'cat' + obsolete 'nip' (to pinch or bite), referring to the plant's effect on cats.
