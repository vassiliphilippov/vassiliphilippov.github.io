patrilineage

1. NOUN

/ ˌpætrɪˈlɪniɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈlɪniɪdʒ / (BrE)
A line of descent traced through the male or paternal side of a family.
descent through the father's line

Examples

Traditionally, inheritance of the estate followed the patrilineage from father to son.
Anthropologists study patterns of kinship, including both patrilineage and matrilineage in different societies.
He could trace his family's patrilineage back for several generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matrilineage - a line of descent traced through the maternal side of the family

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) + 'linea' (line), modeled on the pattern of 'lineage'.
