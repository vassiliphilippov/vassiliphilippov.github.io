radio

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives and amplifies radio signals for listening to broadcasts
electronic device for receiving broadcasts

Examples

I turned on the radio to listen to the morning news.
She bought a new radio for her kitchen counter.
Static crackled as the old radio struggled to find a clear station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wireless - electronic device for receiving broadcasts (British and archaic use)

Etymology

Shortened from radio receiver, from 'radio' (radiation), relating to the use of electromagnetic waves in transmission.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.di.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.əʊ / (BrE)
A system for transmitting sound or signals by broadcasting electromagnetic waves, especially as a medium for communication and entertainment.
communication system using electromagnetic waves

Examples

Many people relied on the radio for news during the emergency.
He works for a local radio station as an announcer.
The invention of the radio revolutionized mass communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: television - system for transmitting visual images and sound

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray, beam), referring to radio waves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04048624-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈreɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
to send a message using radio waves, especially by wireless communication devices
send a message by radio waves

Examples

The pilot radioed the tower to report an emergency.
You should radio your location if you get lost during the hike.
The coast guard radioed a warning to all ships in the area.

Synset Examples

he radioed for help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to send out a program or signal by radio or television
  • Synonym: to transmit - to send out an electronic signal

Etymology

From radio (noun), from Latin 'radius' (ray, beam) and the technology of radio transmission.

4. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
a system of sending and receiving signals or sounds, such as music or speech, through the air using electromagnetic waves; often refers to sound broadcasting services or the medium as a whole.
sound broadcasting medium

Examples

I listen to the news on the radio every morning while eating breakfast.
The documentary was first broadcast on national radio before being adapted for television.
With the advent of internet streaming, traditional radio faces new challenges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: television - system for transmitting visual images and sound
  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit by radio or television

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray, beam), via Italian 'radio' (radius, ray); referring to the transmission of electromagnetic waves.

Source: WordNet
