pentameter

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˈtæmɪtɚ / (AmE), / pɛnˈtæmɪtə / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of five metrical feet.
line of verse with five feet

Examples

The poet chose to write in pentameter to give his verses a formal rhythm.
Many of Shakespeare’s sonnets are written in iambic pentameter.
You can hear the steady beat of pentameter in this dramatic monologue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hexameter - a line of verse with six metrical feet
  • Degree Lower: tetrameter - a line of verse with four metrical feet

Etymology

From Greek 'pentametros' (having five measures), from 'penta-' (five) + 'metron' (measure).
