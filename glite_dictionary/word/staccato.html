staccato

1. ADJ

/ stəˈkɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / stəˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(music) Played or sung with each note sharply separated from the others; disconnected or detached in sound or style.
music: sharply separated notes

Examples

The pianist played the piece in a staccato style, emphasizing each note individually.
Her voice delivered the instructions in short, staccato bursts.
The staccato rhythm of the drums energized the entire performance.

Synset Examples

staccato applause
a staccato command
staccato notes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legato - (music) In a smooth, flowing manner, without breaks between notes

Etymology

From Italian 'staccato', past participle of 'staccare' (to detach).

3. ADJ

/ stəˈkɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / stəˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(of music or sounds) made up of short, sharp, disconnected notes or sounds; marked by each note or sound being sharply separated from the next.
short, sharply separated notes or sounds

Examples

The pianist played the melody in a staccato manner to emphasize the rhythm.
He gave a series of staccato commands to his team, each word sharp and precise.
The staccato applause indicated the audience's excitement and energy.
