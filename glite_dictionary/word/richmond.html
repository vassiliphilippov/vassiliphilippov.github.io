Richmond

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃ.mənd / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.mənd / (BrE)
the capital city of the state of Virginia, located in the east-central region of the United States, and formerly the capital of the Confederacy during the American Civil War
capital city of Virginia, USA
american

Examples

The American Civil War left deep scars on Richmond, which served as the Confederate capital.
We visited Richmond last summer and toured several historic sites downtown.
Many government offices are located in Richmond, the capital of Virginia.

Etymology

Named after Richmond, a town in England; the name was chosen because the view of the James River was said to resemble that of the Thames from Richmond Hill in England.
