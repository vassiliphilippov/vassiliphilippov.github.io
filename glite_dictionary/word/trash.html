trash

1. NOUN

/ træʃ / (AmE), / træʃ / (BrE)
waste material or things that are no longer wanted and are thrown away; rubbish or refuse.
waste material to be thrown away

Examples

Please take out the trash before the truck comes in the morning.
He found an old book in the trash and decided to keep it.
It’s important to sort the trash for recycling properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage - waste material or unwanted things to be thrown away; especially kitchen waste
  • Synonym: rubbish - waste material or things that are no longer wanted

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'trasche' (refuse), of obscure origin.

2. NOUN

/ træʃ / (AmE), / træʃ / (BrE)
(informal, derogatory) People regarded as worthless or contemptible.
worthless or contemptible people (informal, derogatory)
informal slang

Examples

Some people look down on others and call them trash, but that's unfair and hurtful.
He was tired of being treated like trash because of where he grew up.
She warned her son not to hang out with the local trash who were always in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scum - people regarded as morally or socially inferior
  • Synonym: riffraff - disreputable or undesirable people

3. VERB

/ træʃ / (AmE), / træʃ / (BrE)
to throw away or dispose of something because it is no longer wanted or needed
dispose of; throw away
informal

Examples

I finally decided to trash those broken lamps in the attic.
You can trash these receipts after you update the records.
She trashed her old textbooks at the end of the semester.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful or desirable
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or not dispose of something
