suburbia

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɝː.bi.ə / (AmE), / səˈbɜː.bi.ə / (BrE)
the suburbs or the people, lifestyle, and environment associated with residential areas on the outskirts of a city
residential areas outside a city

Examples

After moving to suburbia, they enjoyed the peace and quiet away from downtown.
The film explores the hidden struggles of everyday life in suburbia.
She missed her old friends when her family relocated to suburbia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suburbs - outlying residential areas of a city
  • Antonym: urban - relating to a city or densely populated area
  • Antonym: rural - relating to the countryside, not towns or cities

Etymology

From 'suburb' + '-ia', denoting a place, coined in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ səˈbɜːrbiə / (AmE), / səˈbɜːbiə / (BrE)
people living in suburban areas regarded as a social class or subculture, often characterized by particular lifestyle and cultural norms.
suburban people as a social group

Examples

Many films depict suburbia as a place of conformity and hidden tensions.
After years in the city, they moved to suburbia for a quieter life.
The novel offers a satirical view of American suburbia in the 1960s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suburbanite - a person who lives in a suburb

Etymology

Formed from 'suburb' + '-ia', patterned after names for regions or realms.
