construct

1. VERB

/ kənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to build or put together by combining various parts or elements, often in a planned way
to build by assembling parts

Examples

The engineers constructed a bridge over the river in less than a year.
She carefully constructs each handmade model from recycled materials.
They are constructing a new playground using eco-friendly resources.

Synset Examples

the company fabricates plastic chairs
They manufacture small toys
He manufactured a popular cereal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: build - to make something by putting parts together
  • Synonym: to assemble - to put parts together to make something
  • Antonym: to demolish - to destroy or tear down a structure

Etymology

From Latin 'constructus', past participle of 'construere' (to heap up, build).

WordNet ID: oewn-01656984-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
an abstract or theoretical idea, concept, or model that is inferred from and used to explain specific observations or data
abstract theoretical idea or concept

Examples

Intelligence is often considered a construct that cannot be directly observed but is inferred from tests.
The theory introduces several new constructs to explain human motivation.
'Freedom' is a social construct that varies across cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concept - an idea or principle formed in the mind
  • Synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation for a phenomenon
