Chrysochloridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊˈklɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈklɒrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, burrowing, insectivorous mammals native to southern Africa, known as golden moles, with dense, silky fur and adapted for fossorial life.
family of golden moles

Examples

Members of the Chrysochloridae family are known for their iridescent, golden-hued fur.
Researchers studied the burrowing habits of Chrysochloridae in South African grasslands.
Unlike true moles, Chrysochloridae have especially adapted forelimbs for digging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden mole - Any member of the Chrysochloridae family, especially the common name for these animals

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'chloros' (greenish-yellow) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).
