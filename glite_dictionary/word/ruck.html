ruck

1. NOUN

/ rʌk / (AmE), / rʌk / (BrE)
a crowd, especially of ordinary or undistinguished people or things
a crowd of ordinary people or things

Examples

She aspired to stand out from the ruck and make a name for herself.
Most candidates failed to distinguish themselves from the ruck in the competition.
His creativity set him apart from the ruck of his classmates.

Synset Examples

his brilliance raised him above the ruck
the children resembled a fairy herd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • Antonym: elite - the most powerful, rich, or talented people within a group

Etymology

Possibly from Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect rukk (heap, pile).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rʌk / (AmE), / rʌk / (BrE)
an irregular fold or wrinkle in an otherwise even surface, especially in cloth
irregular fold or wrinkle

Examples

She smoothed out the ruck in the tablecloth before setting the plates down.
A ruck in his shirt made it look untidy during the interview.
The carpet had a ruck near the doorway, causing people to trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced by folding, pressing, or crushing
  • Synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold in something, especially fabric or skin

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian rukk (crease, wrinkle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13930861-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rʌk / (AmE), / rʌk / (BrE)
to become wrinkled or drawn together in folds; to gather into creases or ridges
become wrinkled or gathered

Examples

The fabric began to ruck at the knees after hours of sitting.
If the sheet rucks under you, it can be quite uncomfortable.
Her skirt rucked up as she hurried across the room.

Synset Examples

her lips puckered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pucker - to become or cause to become wrinkled or contracted
  • Antonym: smooth - to make or become even and free from wrinkles

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly related to Middle English dialect words meaning 'crease' or 'wrinkle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01281618-v
Synset POS: v
