Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Philemon

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪs.əl əv pɑl ði əˈpɑs.əl tuː faɪˈliː.mən / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪs.əl əv pɔːl ði əˈpɒs.əl tuː fɪˈliː.mən / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament comprising a letter from Saint Paul to Philemon, asking him to forgive his runaway slave Onesimus
a New Testament book; Paul's letter to Philemon

Examples

The Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Philemon is a unique letter focusing on forgiveness and reconciliation.
She studied the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Philemon for her theology class.
Many scholars debate the implications of the Epistle of Paul the Apostle to Philemon regarding early Christian attitudes toward slavery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philemon - the shorter alternative name for this biblical book
  • Synonym: Epistle of Paul the Apostle to the Hebrews - another Pauline epistle in the New Testament

Etymology

Epistle from Greek 'epistolē' (letter); Philemon from Greek 'Philmōn'.
