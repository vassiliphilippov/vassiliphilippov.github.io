to attitudinize

1. VERB

/ əˈtɪtʃuˌdaɪz / (AmE), / əˈtɪtjuːdənaɪz / (BrE)
to adopt or display an affected or pretentious manner or attitude, especially to impress others
adopt an affected attitude

Examples

She tends to attitudinize when discussing art, making her sound insincere.
The critic accused the actor of attitudinizing rather than offering genuine emotion.
Instead of attitudinizing, try speaking honestly about your experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pose - to assume a particular attitude or stance, often to impress others
  • Synonym: posture - to behave in a way intended to impress or mislead others

Etymology

From 'attitude' + '-ize', 19th century formation.
