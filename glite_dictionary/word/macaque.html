macaque

1. NOUN

/ məˈkæk / (AmE), / məˈkæk / (BrE)
A short-tailed monkey of rocky regions in Asia and Africa, belonging to the genus Macaca.
species of short-tailed monkey

Examples

The researchers observed a group of macaques interacting near the forest edge.
A macaque sat on the branch, grooming its fur in the morning sun.
Some macaques have adapted to steal food from tourists at popular temples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey - a primate of the group that includes monkeys and apes

Etymology

From French 'macaque', possibly via Portuguese from Makassar 'kaka', meaning monkey.
