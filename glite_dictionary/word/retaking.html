retaking

1. NOUN

/ riˈteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking something back, especially control, possession, or an opportunity
the act of taking something back

Examples

The army's retaking of the city was swift and decisive.
After failing, he is preparing for the retaking of his driving test.
The retaking of the throne marked a new era for the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recapture - the act of capturing something again
  • Antonym: surrender - the act of giving something up
