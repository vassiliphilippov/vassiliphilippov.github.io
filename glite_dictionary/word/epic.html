epic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling an epic poem, especially in being grand, heroic, or impressive in style or scope.
grand or heroic like an epic poem

Examples

The film's epic story captivated audiences with its grand scale and emotional depth.
She studied the epic tradition in ancient Greek literature for her thesis.
The battle scene was portrayed with epic intensity, reminiscent of Homeric poems.

Synset Examples

epic tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic - having the characteristics of a hero or heroine

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus', from Ancient Greek 'epikos' (of or for a word or song).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
Very impressive or grand in scale or size; surpassing the ordinary.
impressive or grand in scale

Examples

The team undertook an epic journey across the desert that lasted several months.
The film was praised for its epic special effects and sweeping landscapes.
They threw an epic party that people talked about for years.

Synset Examples

an epic voyage
of heroic proportions
heroic sculpture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic - very brave or impressive in scale
  • Antonym: ordinary - not remarkable or special in any way

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus', from Greek 'epikos' (pertaining to an epic poem).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01388944-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
A long narrative poem, typically detailing the adventures and deeds of a heroic figure or the history of a nation.
long narrative poem about a hero

Examples

Homer's epic 'The Odyssey' has inspired generations of readers and writers.
The ancient poem is considered the national epic of its country.
She compared his journey across the desert to a modern epic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saga - a long story of heroic achievement, typically a prose narrative
  • Degree Lower: ballad - a form of verse, often a narrative set to music

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus', from Ancient Greek 'epikos' (of or for epic poetry), from 'epos' (word, song, story).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06391344-n
Synset POS: n
