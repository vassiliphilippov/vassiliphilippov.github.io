adornment

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɔrn mənt / (AmE), / əˈdɔːn mənt / (BrE)
an addition or decoration that makes something more attractive or less plain
a decorative addition to something

Examples

The necklace served as a beautiful adornment to her elegant dress.
The architect used stained glass windows as an adornment for the otherwise plain church walls.
Holiday adornments brought color and cheer to the city square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something added to make something more attractive
  • Synonym: embellishment - a decorative detail or feature added to something

Etymology

From Old French 'adornement', from Latin 'adornare' (to decorate).

2. NOUN

/ əˈdɔːrnmənt / (AmE), / əˈdɔːnmənt / (BrE)
the act of decorating yourself or something else, especially with something attractive or decorative
the act of decorating oneself or something

Examples

The bride's traditional adornment included colorful bangles and intricate henna designs.
She wore minimal adornment, preferring a natural look for the interview.
Jewelry is a popular form of adornment in many cultures throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - the process or art of decorating something

Etymology

From Old French 'adornement', from Latin 'adornare' (to equip, decorate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
