rapt

1. ADJ

/ ræpt / (AmE), / ræpt / (BrE)
deeply engrossed or absorbed; showing complete attention or delight in something
deeply engrossed or absorbed

Examples

She watched the performance with a rapt expression on her face, not missing a single detail.
The children listened in rapt silence as the storyteller spun his magical tale.
He sat rapt by the window, completely lost in thought.

Synset Examples

that engrossed look of rapt delight
enwrapped in dreams
so intent on this fantastic … narrative that she hardly stirred
rapt with wonder
wrapped in thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrossed - completely absorbed in something
  • Synonym: absorbed - deeply involved or absorbed in thought
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate or give attention

Etymology

From Latin raptus (seized, carried away).

2. ADJ

/ ræpt / (AmE), / ræpt / (BrE)
completely fascinated or absorbed by what one is seeing or hearing; experiencing great joy or delight.
completely fascinated or absorbed

Examples

She listened to the music with a rapt expression on her face.
The children sat in rapt attention as the storyteller spoke.
He watched the fireworks in rapt silence, completely enthralled by the display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entranced - in a state of deep wonder or fascination
  • Synonym: delighted - feeling great pleasure or happiness
  • Antonym: disinterested - not interested or engaged

Etymology

From Latin 'raptus' (seized, carried away), past participle of 'rapere' (to seize).
