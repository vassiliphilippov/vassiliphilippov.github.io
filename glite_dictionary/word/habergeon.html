habergeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæbərˌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈhabədʒən / (BrE)
A light, sleeveless coat of chain mail, worn under the hauberk as armor in the Middle Ages.
light sleeveless chain mail armor

Examples

The knight wore a habergeon beneath his tunic for extra protection.
Medieval soldiers often relied on the habergeon as part of their defensive gear.
A finely crafted habergeon is on display at the local museum of arms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hauberk - a long coat of mail armor worn over other protection

Etymology

From Old French 'haubergeon', diminutive of 'hauberc' (hauberk), from Germanic roots.
