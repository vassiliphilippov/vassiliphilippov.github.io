Paeonia

1. NOUN

/ piˈoʊniə / (AmE), / piˈəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known as peonies, including herbaceous and shrubby species with large, showy flowers.
genus of flowering plants (peonies)

Examples

Many gardeners admire the spectacular blooms found in Paeonia species.
The genus Paeonia includes both herbaceous and tree peonies.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of some Paeonia root extracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peony - a flowering plant of the genus Paeonia, known for its large, showy blooms

Etymology

From Latin *Paeonia*, from Greek *paionia*, plural of *paionios* (plant named after Paion, a physician of the gods).
