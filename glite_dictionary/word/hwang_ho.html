Hwang Ho

1. PROPN

/ hwɑŋ hoʊ / (AmE), / hwæŋ həʊ / (BrE)
a major river in northern China, known for its yellow silt and frequent flooding, flowing east into the Yellow Sea
river in northern China (Yellow River)

Examples

The Hwang Ho has played a vital role in the development of Chinese civilization.
Frequent floods along the Hwang Ho have earned it the nickname 'China's Sorrow'.
The yellow silt deposited by the Hwang Ho creates fertile plains for agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yellow River - another name for Hwang Ho, a major river in China

Etymology

From Chinese Huang He, meaning 'Yellow River'.
