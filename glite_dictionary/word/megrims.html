megrims

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːɡrɪmz / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡrɪmz / (BrE)
(archaic) a state of depression or low spirits; dejection.
state of depression
archaic

Examples

Whenever the days grew gray and cold, she often suffered from the megrims.
After his failure at the audition, he was overcome by a sudden attack of the megrims.
The novel's heroine is frequently lost in the megrims of her solitary thoughts.

Synset Examples

he had a bad case of the blues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blues - feeling of depression or sadness
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness
  • Antonym: elation - a feeling of great joy or pride

Etymology

From Middle French *migraine* (sick headache), originally from Medieval Latin *hemicrania* (pain in one side of the head).
