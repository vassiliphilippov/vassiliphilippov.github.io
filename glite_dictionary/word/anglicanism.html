Anglicanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
The faith, doctrine, and practices of the Anglican Church, particularly as distinguished from other Christian denominations.
beliefs and practices of the Anglican Church

Examples

Many converts to Anglicanism appreciate its blend of tradition and reform.
Religious scholars often debate the unique aspects of Anglicanism compared to other Christian faiths.
The Archbishop gave a lecture on the history and influence of Anglicanism in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Protestantism - the faith and practices of Protestant Christian denominations

Etymology

From Anglican + -ism, indicating a system of beliefs or practices; 'Anglican' from Medieval Latin 'Anglicanus' (English).
