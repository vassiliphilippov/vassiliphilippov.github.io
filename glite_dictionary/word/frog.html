2. NOUN

/ frɑɡ / (AmE), / frɒɡ / (BrE)
a tailless, stout-bodied amphibian with long hind legs for leaping, typically living in or near water
a tailless leaping amphibian

Examples

A green frog jumped into the pond with a splash.
At night, you can hear the croaking of frogs near the creek.
She found a tiny frog hiding under a leaf after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toad - a similar but generally drier-skinned amphibian

Etymology

From Old English frox, frogga, related to Dutch vros, German Frosch.

/ frɑɡ / (AmE), / frɒɡ / (BrE)

decorative braid fastener for garments
