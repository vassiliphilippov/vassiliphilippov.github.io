to hew

1. VERB

/ hju / (AmE), / hjuː / (BrE)
to cut or shape (something) with a sharp tool such as an axe
cut or shape with a tool

Examples

Workers used axes to hew logs for the construction of the cabin.
The artisans hewed intricate shapes from the massive stones.
He managed to hew a small canoe out of a single tree trunk.

Synset Examples

hew out a path in the rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to cut (a hard material) in order to produce an object or design

Etymology

Old English *heawan* (to chop, strike), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01263249-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hju / (AmE), / hjuː / (BrE)
to chop or cut something, especially wood, with a heavy cutting tool such as an axe
cut or chop with an axe or tool

Examples

The lumberjack began to hew the old oak tree with powerful strokes.
Early settlers would hew logs to build their homes from scratch.
Workers hewed large stones from the quarry for the construction project.

Synset Examples

hew an oak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chop - to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows
  • Synonym: to cleave - to split or sever (something), especially along a natural line

Etymology

From Old English *heawan* (to chop, strike, hew), related to German *hauen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01262992-v
Synset POS: v
