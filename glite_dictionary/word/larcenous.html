larcenous

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːr.sə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.sɪ.nəs / (BrE)
having a tendency or inclination to steal; characteristic of theft or thieves.
inclined to steal; thieving

Examples

Despite his charming smile, his larcenous nature soon became apparent to the store owner.
The detective suspected a larcenous motive behind the string of missing valuables.
Her larcenous tendencies got her into trouble throughout her teenage years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thievish - having the characteristics or habits of a thief

Etymology

From Latin 'larcenia' (larceny) via Middle French and English.
