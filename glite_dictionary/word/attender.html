attender

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndər / (AmE), / əˈtɛndə / (BrE)
a person who is present at and participates in a meeting or event
person present at a meeting or event

Examples

She was the youngest attender at the annual conference this year.
Each attender received a name badge upon arrival.
The meeting room was nearly full with regular attenders.

Synset Examples

he was a regular attender at department meetings
the gathering satisfied both organizers and attendees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendee - a person who is present at an event or meeting

Etymology

From attend + -er, meaning one who attends.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09631032-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.də / (BrE)
a person who listens attentively or pays close attention
person who listens attentively

Examples

As an attender at the lecture, he took notes and asked thoughtful questions.
Every good attender listens more than they speak during group discussions.
The therapist appreciated having an active attender in the session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listener - someone who listens
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From attend + -er, meaning one who attends or pays attention.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10185041-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndər / (AmE), / əˈtɛndə / (BrE)
a person who waits on, tends to, or attends to the needs of another
person who attends to another's needs

Examples

The patient was grateful for the constant care of her attender during her recovery.
Each elderly resident is assigned an attender to help with daily activities.
As an attender, he ensured all the guests had everything they needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - a person whose job is to serve or assist others

Etymology

From 'attend' + '-er', forming agent nouns.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09841233-n
Synset POS: n
