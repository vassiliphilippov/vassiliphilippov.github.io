Ictonyx frenata

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtoʊnɪks frəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ɪkˈtəʊnɪks frɪˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A small, carnivorous mammal native to northern and sub-Saharan Africa, commonly known as the striped polecat or African polecat.
African carnivorous mammal; striped polecat

Examples

The Ictonyx frenata is easily recognizable by its distinctive black and white stripes.
Farmers sometimes encounter Ictonyx frenata near their fields as it hunts for rodents.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Ictonyx frenata across different regions of Africa.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'iktonyx' meaning 'weasel clawed'.
