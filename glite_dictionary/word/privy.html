privy

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪvi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi / (BrE)
(followed by 'to') having knowledge of something secret or confidential; informed about something not generally known.
knowing secret or confidential information

Examples

Only a few senior officials were privy to the confidential report.
She was not privy to their private discussions about the company merger.
The details of the agreement were known only to those privy to the negotiations.

Synset Examples

privy to the details of the conspiracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something specific

Etymology

From Old French *privé* (private), from Latin *privatus* (private, apart from public).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi / (BrE)
a small outdoor building with a bench containing holes, used as a simple toilet
outdoor toilet; outhouse

Examples

The old farmhouse still had a privy at the edge of the yard.
Many rural schools once relied on a simple wooden privy for sanitation.
They replaced the crumbling privy with a modern restroom facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outhouse - a small outdoor structure used as a toilet

Etymology

From Old French 'privé', from Latin 'privatus' (private).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi / (BrE)
an outdoor toilet, especially a small building separate from a house; a latrine
outdoor toilet; latrine
archaic

Examples

The old farmhouse still has a privy at the edge of the garden.
During the camping trip, we had to use a wooden privy behind the cabins.
In rural areas, many homes in the past relied on a privy instead of indoor plumbing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latrine - a simple toilet facility, often outdoors
  • Synonym: outhouse - a small building with a toilet, outside a main house

Etymology

From Old French *privé* (private place), from Latin *privatus* (private).

4. ADJ

/ ˈprɪvi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvi / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) hidden from general view or use; private or secluded.
hidden or secluded; private

Examples

The old monastery had a privy garden where the monks would meditate in peace.
She found a privy alcove in the library to study without interruption.
They met at a privy location known only to a few close friends.

Synset Examples

a privy place to rest and think
a secluded romantic spot
a secret garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept from the knowledge of others; hidden
  • Synonym: secluded - (of a place) not seen or visited by many people; sheltered

Etymology

From Old French *privé* (private, secret), from Latin *privatus* (private).
