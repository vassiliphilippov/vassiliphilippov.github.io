Maximilien Robespierre

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæksɪˈmiliˌæn ˌroʊbəsˈpiər / (AmE), / ˌmæksɪˈmiːliˌæn ˌrəʊbɛsˈpjɛə / (BrE)
(1758–1794) A French revolutionary leader who was a prominent member of the Jacobins and became the architect of the Reign of Terror before being executed in a coup d'etat.
French revolutionary leader, architect of the Reign of Terror

Examples

Maximilien Robespierre played a central role during the French Revolution and became infamous for his radical policies.
Historians often debate the motivations and actions of Maximilien Robespierre during the Reign of Terror.
The downfall of Maximilien Robespierre marked a dramatic turn in the course of the revolution.
