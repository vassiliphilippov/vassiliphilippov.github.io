brainsick

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪnˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn.sɪk / (BrE)
Mentally unwell; suffering from madness or insanity (archaic or literary).
affected by madness; insane (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The character in the old play was driven brainsick by grief and loss.
He feared he would grow as brainsick as those he cared for in the asylum.
Her brainsick imaginings filled the empty house with invisible spirits.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - suffering from serious mental illness; mad
  • Synonym: mad - mentally ill; crazy
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

From 'brain' + 'sick', used in English since the 16th century, notably in Shakespeare.
