faithlessly

1. ADV

/ ˈfeɪθlɪsli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθlɪsli / (BrE)
In a disloyal or unfaithful manner; without loyalty or trustworthiness.
in a disloyal or unfaithful manner

Examples

She acted faithlessly by betraying her friend's secret to others.
The knight served his king faithlessly, conspiring with the enemy in secret.
If you promise something, don't behave faithlessly when it becomes difficult.

Synset Examples

he behaved treacherously
his wife played him false

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithfully - in a loyal and faithful manner
  • Synonym: disloyally - in a disloyal manner; without loyalty

Etymology

From 'faithless' + '-ly', where 'faithless' derives from 'faith' and Old French 'feid', ultimately from Latin 'fides' (trust, faith).
