untrod

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtrɑd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrɒd / (BrE)
(literary) Without any paths, tracks, or roads; not traversed or walked on by people.
without paths or tracks; not walked on
archaic

Examples

The hikers ventured deep into the untrod forest, where no clear path could be seen.
She dreamed of exploring untrod lands, far from civilization.
Much of the island remained untrod, covered in wild vegetation.

Synset Examples

trackless wilderness
roadless areas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadless - lacking roads or pathways
  • Synonym: trackless - having no paths or tracks

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'trod', archaic past participle of 'tread'.
