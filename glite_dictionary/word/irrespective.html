irrespective

1. ADV

/ ɪˌrɛspəˈktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛspəkˈtɪv / (BrE)
without considering or paying attention to something; regardless of.
without regard; regardless

Examples

All applicants will be considered irrespective of age or experience.
The law applies irrespective of nationality.
She continued to work hard, irrespective of the challenges she faced.

Synset Examples

he carried on regardless of the difficulties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regardless - without paying attention to the current situation

Etymology

From Latin 'ir-' (not) + 'respicere' (to look back), via 'irrespective' in English (early 19th century).
