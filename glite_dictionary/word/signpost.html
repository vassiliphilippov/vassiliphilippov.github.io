signpost

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪnˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnpəʊst / (BrE)
to mark a place, path, or route with a signpost for direction or information
mark with a signpost

Examples

The hiking trail was carefully signposted to prevent anyone from getting lost.
Volunteers spent the afternoon signposting the new cycle path through the park.
If the route isn't signposted, hikers might easily take a wrong turn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - to mark or attach a label to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00924291-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnˌpəʊst / (BrE)
a post with a sign, especially one that shows directions or gives information about a place
post showing directions or information

Examples

We followed the signpost to reach the hiking trail.
The village was easy to find thanks to the clear signpost at the crossroads.
A new signpost was installed to help tourists navigate the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guidepost - a post with signs showing directions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06807231-n
Synset POS: n
