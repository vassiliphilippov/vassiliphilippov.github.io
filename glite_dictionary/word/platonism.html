Platonism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪtəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrine that abstract concepts or entities exist independently of the names or objects associated with them, as proposed by Plato.
belief in the real existence of abstract entities

Examples

Many mathematicians are influenced by Platonism, believing that mathematical objects exist independently of human thought.
In his essay, she critiques Platonism as being incompatible with scientific realism.
According to Platonism, properties like 'redness' or 'beauty' exist as abstract entities beyond the physical world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominalism - the philosophical view that universals and abstract concepts do not exist independently of their names
  • Synonym: realism - in philosophy, the belief that universals or abstract objects exist independently of the mind

Etymology

Named after the Greek philosopher Plato (427–347 BC).
