Lophius

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊfiəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒfɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of anglerfish known for their large, broad heads and characteristic luring appendage, found in European and North American waters.
genus of anglerfish

Examples

The monkfish, known scientifically as Lophius, is a prized catch for many chefs.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the genus Lophius off the coast of Spain.
Members of the genus Lophius are noted for their unusual method of attracting prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - Common name for fishes of the family Lophiidae, which Lophius belongs to

Etymology

From Greek lophos meaning 'crest', referring to the fish's prominent appearance.
