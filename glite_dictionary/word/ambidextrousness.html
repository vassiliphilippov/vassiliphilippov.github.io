ambidextrousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbɪˈdɛkstrəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌambɪˈdɛkstrəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to use both hands equally well.
ability to use both hands equally well

Examples

His ambidextrousness allowed him to write with either hand during the exam.
The baseball player's ambidextrousness gave him a significant advantage on the field.
Some artists are known for their ambidextrousness, painting with both hands simultaneously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambidexterity - the ability to use both hands equally well

Etymology

From ambidextrous (from Latin ambi- (both) + dexter (right-handed)) + -ness.
