outward

1. ADV

/ ˈaʊt.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd / (BrE)
Towards the outside; away from the centre or interior.
towards the outside

Examples

He moved the box outward to make more space in the closet.
The petals of the flower spread outward as it bloomed.
Push the lever outward to open the panel.

Synset Examples

move the needle further outward!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inward - towards the inside; towards the centre

Etymology

From Old English ūtweard, from ūt (out) + -weard (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00260109-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈaʊt.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd / (BrE)
going away from a place, especially starting a journey
going away from a place

Examples

The outward flight to New York was calm and uneventful.
They prepared supplies for their outward journey into the mountains.
All outward trains depart from platform three.

Synset Examples

the departing train
an outward journey
outward-bound ships

Related Terms

  • Antonym: return - a journey coming back to a place

Etymology

From Old English 'ūtweard', from 'ūt' (out) + 'weard' (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01298781-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd / (BrE)
relating to the external or physical appearance of something, rather than to its inner qualities or thoughts
external; relating to outer appearance

Examples

She was more interested in outward appearances than genuine emotions.
Despite his calm outward demeanor, he was quite nervous inside.
The outward damage to the car was minor, but the engine had serious problems.

Synset Examples

a concern with outward beauty rather than with inward reflections

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inward - relating to the inner thoughts or feelings rather than the external

Etymology

From Old English ūtw(e)ard (toward the outside), from 'ūt' (out) + '-weard' (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00953896-a
Synset POS: a
