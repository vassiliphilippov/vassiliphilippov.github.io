aminomethane

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, flammable gas with a strong odor, consisting of a methane molecule in which one hydrogen atom is replaced by an amino group (NH2); also known as methylamine.
a chemical compound; methylamine
medical

Examples

The chemical aminomethane is used as a building block in the synthesis of pharmaceuticals.
Exposure to aminomethane vapors can cause irritation of the eyes and respiratory system.
Scientists detected aminomethane in samples taken from the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methylamine - a colorless gas used in chemical synthesis; another name for aminomethane

Etymology

From 'amino' (relating to the amino group, -NH2) and 'methane' (the simplest alkane hydrocarbon).
