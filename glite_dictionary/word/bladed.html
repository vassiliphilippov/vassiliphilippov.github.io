bladed

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbleɪdɪd / (BrE)
having a blade or blades; often used in combination to describe an object with a particular type or number of blades.
having a blade or blades

Examples

He purchased a bladed razor for a smoother shave.
The engineer designed a multi-bladed wind turbine for increased efficiency.
Some plants have narrow-bladed leaves that minimize water loss.

Synset Examples

a single-bladed leaf
narrow-bladed grass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blade - the flat cutting part of a tool or weapon

Etymology

From 'blade' + '-ed', forming an adjective meaning 'having a blade'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbleɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a mineral, crystal, or part) having thin, flat, plate-like structures resembling a blade.
having thin, flat, plate-like structures

Examples

The mineral sample consisted of several bladed crystals radiating from a central point.
Some types of arsenopyrite are known for their distinctive bladed appearance.
The geologist described the rock as containing bladed formations unlike any she had seen before.

Synset Examples

bladed arsenopyrite

Etymology

From 'blade' + '-ed', indicating possession of blade-like qualities.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02421528-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈbleɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbleɪdɪd / (BrE)
having one or more blades, or shaped like or equipped with a blade; often used in combination.
having a blade or blades

Examples

The chef used a bladed knife to slice the vegetables thinly.
The helicopter's bladed rotor spun rapidly as it prepared for takeoff.
He admired the sleek, bladed design of the new racing skates.

Synset Examples

he fought on, broken-bladed but unbowed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bladeless - without a blade
