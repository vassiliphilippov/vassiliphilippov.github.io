crossfire

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a lively or heated interchange of ideas and opinions, often involving rapid back-and-forth debate
heated exchange of opinions

Examples

The candidate was caught in the crossfire during the televised debate, struggling to respond to both sides.
Discussions at the meeting quickly broke into a crossfire of opposing viewpoints.
She found herself in the crossfire of a heated argument between her colleagues.

Etymology

Originally a military term for lines of gunfire crossing at a point, later extended figuratively to verbal exchanges.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑsˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire from two or more directions so that the lines of fire cross, making it difficult for someone to avoid being hit
gunfire from crossing directions

Examples

The soldiers were trapped in the crossfire and had nowhere to escape.
During the robbery, a bystander was injured in the crossfire between the police and the suspects.
The reporter hesitated to enter the conflict zone, fearing she might get caught in the crossfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - a surprise attack, especially from a hidden position

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'fire', indicating intersecting lines of gunfire.
