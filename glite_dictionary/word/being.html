being

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːɪŋ / (BrE)
the state or fact of existing; existence
existence; state of existing

Examples

The concept of human being is central to many philosophical debates.
Her poems explore the nature of being and consciousness.
Their culture places a high value on the interconnectedness of all being.

Synset Examples

a point of view gradually coming into being
laws in existence for centuries
he appeared on the face of the earth one day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existence - the fact or state of living or having objective reality

Etymology

From Old English 'beon' (to be), ultimately from Proto-Germanic and Proto-Indo-European roots meaning 'to exist.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːɪŋ / (BrE)
a living creature; a sentient or existing entity, especially one capable of independent action or thought.
a living or sentient creature

Examples

The ancient legend spoke of a mysterious being that protected the forest from harm.
Some people believe there may be intelligent beings elsewhere in the universe.
The scientist studied the behavior of every living being in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creature - a living animal, especially an animal as distinct from a human being
  • Synonym: entity - something that exists as a distinct thing
  • Antonym: object - a thing that can be seen or touched; often contrasted with 'being' as lifeless rather than living

Etymology

From Old English 'beon' (to be), via Middle English 'beinge' (existence).
