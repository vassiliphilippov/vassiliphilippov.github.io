Taxus brevifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksəs ˌbrɛvɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs ˌbrɛvɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of yew tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its irregular branches and hard, close-grained wood.
Pacific yew tree species

Examples

Pharmaceutical companies harvest bark from the Taxus brevifolia to manufacture important cancer drugs.
The Taxus brevifolia is easily recognized by its irregularly branched shape and dark green foliage.
Logging restrictions have been put in place to help conserve the rare Taxus brevifolia in old-growth forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific yew - Common English name for Taxus brevifolia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Taxus' (yew) and 'brevifolia' (short-leaved).
