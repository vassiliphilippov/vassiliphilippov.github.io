Ardisia

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdɪziə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen shrubs, subshrubs, and trees of the family Primulaceae, found in Asia, Australasia, and the Americas.
genus of tropical evergreen plants

Examples

The garden featured a rare variety of Ardisia with glossy red berries.
Some species of Ardisia are prized for their ornamental foliage and flowers.
Botanists collected samples of Ardisia during their expedition in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the Spanish botanist Ardis, Latinized as Ardisius.
