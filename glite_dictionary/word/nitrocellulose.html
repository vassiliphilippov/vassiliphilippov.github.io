nitrocellulose

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈsɛljəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈsɛljʊˌləʊs / (BrE)
a highly flammable compound formed by treating cellulose with nitric acid, used in making lacquers, explosives, and film
highly flammable chemical used in explosives and lacquers
medical

Examples

The film industry once widely used nitrocellulose film, which was prone to catching fire.
Many early plastics contained nitrocellulose as a primary ingredient.
Scientists must handle nitrocellulose with care due to its explosive properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cellulose - an insoluble substance which is the main constituent of plant cell walls

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (saltpetre) and 'cellulose'.
