reflexively

1. ADV

/ rɪˈflɛksɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that is automatic, involuntary, or done without conscious thought, often as a result of a reflex.
in an automatic or involuntary way

Examples

She reflexively reached out to catch the falling glass before it hit the floor.
When asked a difficult question, he reflexively shrugged his shoulders.
Many animals respond reflexively to certain stimuli without any learning or reasoning.

Synset Examples

such effects can be induced reflexly
she answered reflexively, without thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatically - without conscious thought or intention
  • Antonym: deliberately - consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From 'reflexive', which is from Latin 'reflexus' (bent back), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
