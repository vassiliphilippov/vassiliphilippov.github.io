brattish

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrætɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrætɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a child) behaving in an impolitely unruly or spoiled manner.
impolitely unruly or spoiled (of a child)

Examples

The teacher was at her wit's end with the brattish boy who refused to sit still in class.
Her brattish behavior embarrassed her parents at the restaurant.
He acted in such a brattish way that no one wanted to play with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoiled - (of a child) excessively indulged or pampered
  • Antonym: well-behaved - (of a child) behaving properly and politely

Etymology

Formed from 'brat' (a badly behaved child) with the adjective suffix '-ish'.
