to assassinate

1. VERB

/ əˈsæs.ɪ.neɪt / (AmE), / əˈsæs.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to murder an important or prominent person, especially for political or ideological reasons
to murder an important person

Examples

The president was assassinated while traveling abroad.
Terrorists attempted to assassinate the ambassador during his speech.
Historical figures have been assassinated for their political beliefs.

Synset Examples

Anwar Sadat was assassinated because many people did not like his peace politics with Israel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - the unlawful killing of another person
  • Synonym: kill - to cause the death of someone or something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin assassinatus, past participle of assassinare, related to 'assassin'.

2. VERB

/ əˈsæsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈsasɪneɪt / (BrE)
To deliberately damage or destroy someone's reputation or character, especially in a calculated and malicious way.
to deliberately ruin someone's reputation

Examples

The media attempted to assassinate the politician's character during the scandal.
Rumors can quickly assassinate someone's reputation, often without basis in truth.
He was furious when his opponent tried to assassinate his good name in public.

Synset Examples

He assassinated his enemy's character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Italian *assassinare*, related to 'assassin', meaning a murderer, especially one hired for political reasons.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00848838-v
Synset POS: v
