sarcosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑr.kəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.kə.səʊm / (BrE)
a large mitochondrion found in a striated muscle fiber, involved in producing energy for muscle contraction
mitochondrion in striated muscle fiber
medical

Examples

The sarcosome is crucial for supplying energy during intense muscle activity.
Scientists studied the structure of the sarcosome in heart tissue samples.
Damage to the sarcosome can impair muscle contraction and overall function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitochondrion - an organelle responsible for energy production in cells

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'soma' (body).
