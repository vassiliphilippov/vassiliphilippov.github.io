baneberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbeɪnbəri / (BrE)
A poisonous berry produced by plants of the genus Actaea, commonly found in North America and Europe.
poisonous berry from Actaea plant

Examples

The bright red baneberry is attractive but highly toxic if ingested.
Hikers are warned not to eat any baneberry they find in the woods.
Children should be taught to avoid touching or tasting baneberries due to their poisonous nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Actaea - Genus of plants producing baneberries

Etymology

From 'bane' (something causing death or harm) + 'berry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbeɪnbəri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the genus Actaea, found in temperate regions, with clusters of small white or red poisonous berries.
poisonous plant of the genus Actaea

Examples

Children should be warned not to touch the baneberry in the woods due to its toxic berries.
The bright red baneberry stood out among the green undergrowth along the hiking trail.
Gardeners should be cautious when planting baneberry because of its poisonous fruit.

Etymology

From 'bane' (poison) + 'berry', used since the early 18th century for plants with poisonous berries.
