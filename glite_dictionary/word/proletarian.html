proletarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.lɪˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a member of the working class, especially one who does not own property and must sell their labor to survive
member of the working class

Examples

The political party claimed to represent the interests of the proletarian majority.
As a proud proletarian, he believed in fighting for workers' rights.
Many classic novels explore the struggles faced by the proletarian class.

Synset Examples

workers of the world — unite!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worker - a person who works, especially one engaged in manual or industrial labor
  • Antonym: bourgeois - a member of the middle class, especially relating to materialistic values or conventional attitudes

Etymology

From Latin 'proletarius' (citizen of the lowest class).

2. ADJ

/ ˌproʊ.ləˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the working class, especially those who do not own capital and must earn their living by manual labor.
of the working class

Examples

The novel vividly described the proletarian struggles in the early twentieth century.
He adopted a distinctly proletarian style of dress to show solidarity with workers.
Her research focuses on proletarian culture and everyday life under industrialization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working-class - relating to those who do manual labor for wages
  • Antonym: bourgeois - relating to the middle class, typically referencing capitalist property owners

Etymology

From Latin proletarius (citizen of the lowest class), from proles (offspring, progeny).

Synset POS: s
