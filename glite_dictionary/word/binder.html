binder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndə / (BrE)
a machine used in agriculture to cut grain and bind it into sheaves for easier handling
machine that cuts and binds harvested grain

Examples

The old farmer still used a binder to harvest the wheat fields each summer.
Before the invention of the combine harvester, the binder was essential for gathering grain.
The museum displayed a vintage binder alongside other historic farming equipment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndə / (BrE)
something that binds or holds things together, such as a material, substance, or device used to tie or fasten objects.
something that binds objects together

Examples

He used a strong binder to hold the stack of documents in place.
In construction, cement acts as a binder for sand and gravel.
She wrapped a cloth binder around the package to keep it secure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a device that attaches or holds things together
  • Degree Lower: binder twine - twine used for binding grain or other items

Etymology

From Old English 'bindere', from 'bindan' (to bind).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndə / (BrE)
a cover with rings or clamps for holding loose sheets of paper, documents, or magazines
cover for holding loose papers

Examples

She kept all her class notes in a large binder to stay organized.
The magazine collection was stored neatly in a three-ring binder on the shelf.
Before the meeting, he put the reports into a binder for easy distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folder - cover or holder for loose papers
  • Synonym: notebook - book for writing notes, often bound

Etymology

From bind + -er, meaning 'that which binds'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndə / (BrE)
a substance that holds or binds other materials or particles together, especially in mixtures or coatings
substance that binds materials together

Examples

The paint contains a binder that keeps the pigment attached to the wall.
In concrete, cement acts as the main binder for sand and gravel.
Add some egg as a binder to help the mixture hold its shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used to stick objects together
  • Synonym: cement - a binder used to make materials stick together, especially in construction

Etymology

From bind + -er, indicating something that binds.
