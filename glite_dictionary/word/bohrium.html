bohrium

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔriəm / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a synthetic transuranic chemical element with the symbol Bh and atomic number 107
synthetic chemical element (Bh, atomic number 107)

Examples

Scientists first produced bohrium in a laboratory in 1981.
Because bohrium is highly radioactive, it has no practical uses outside scientific research.
The discovery of bohrium filled a gap in the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element 107 - the 107th element in the periodic table

Etymology

Named after Danish physicist Niels Bohr.
