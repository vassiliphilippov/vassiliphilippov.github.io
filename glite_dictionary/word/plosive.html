plosive

1. NOUN

/ ˈploʊ.sɪv / (AmE), / ˈpləʊ.sɪv / (BrE)
A speech sound produced by stopping the flow of air at some point in the mouth and then suddenly releasing it, as in the sounds p, t, k, b, d, and g.
consonant sound made by a sudden air release

Examples

The English 'p' and 'b' are both examples of plosive sounds.
Linguists often study how plosive consonants vary across languages.
Children sometimes substitute one plosive sound for another when learning to speak.

Synset Examples

his stop consonants are too aspirated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - a consonant produced by completely blocking and then releasing air, synonym for plosive
  • Antonym: fricative - a consonant produced by the friction of breath through a narrow opening, antonym to plosive

Etymology

From Latin 'plos-' (stem of 'plaudere', to clap) + '-ive'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpləʊsɪv / (BrE)
A consonant sound made by stopping the airflow and then suddenly releasing it, such as 'p', 't', or 'k'.
a stop consonant sound

Examples

The initial sound in 'pat' is a plosive pronounced with a burst of air.
English has several plosive consonants, including 'b', 'd', and 'g'.
Children often find it difficult to pronounce plosives clearly when learning to speak.

Synset Examples

his stop consonants are too aspirated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - a consonant sound formed by the complete blocking and sudden release of airflow

Etymology

From Latin 'plos-', stem of 'plaudere' (to clap), from 'explosivus' (explosive).
