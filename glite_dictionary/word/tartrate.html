tartrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌtreɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of tartaric acid, commonly used in chemistry and the food industry
salt or ester of tartaric acid

Examples

Potassium tartrate is commonly found in baking powder.
The doctor prescribed a medication containing metoprolol tartrate.
Some wines have crystals of tartrate that form during storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tartaric acid - a dicarboxylic acid often found in plants, especially grapes

Etymology

From French 'tartrate', from 'tartre' (tartar), itself from Medieval Latin 'tartarum'.
