nay

1. ADV

/ neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
Used to introduce a statement that expands or corrects a previous one, often meaning 'not only this but also'.
used to emphasize or intensify a previous statement
archaic

Examples

He was generous, nay, truly selfless in his support of the cause.
The task is difficult, nay, almost impossible to complete.
She was surprised, nay, astonished by the sudden change.

Synset Examples

each of us is peculiar, nay, in a sense unique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeed - used to emphasize a statement
  • Synonym: rather - to a greater degree or extent

Etymology

From Old Norse *nei* (no), related to Old English *nā* (no, not).

2. NOUN

/ neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
a word or vote expressing a negative response or refusal, especially in formal or official procedures such as meetings or parliamentary sessions
a vote or word meaning 'no'
archaic

Examples

The chairman asked for a show of hands, and there were three nays.
The motion passed despite several loud nay votes from the opposition.
The final count recorded more nays than yeas, so the proposal was rejected.

Synset Examples

the nays have it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no - negative response or refusal
  • Antonym: yea - affirmative vote or word meaning 'yes'

Etymology

From Old Norse 'nei' meaning 'no'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07219870-n
Synset POS: n
