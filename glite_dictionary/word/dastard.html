dastard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæstərd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːstəd / (BrE)
A person who acts in a despicably cowardly or dishonorable way.
despicably cowardly person
archaic

Examples

The story's villain was a true dastard, betraying his friends for his own safety.
No dastard should be allowed to lead brave men into battle.
She called him a dastard after he fled the scene, leaving others to face the danger.

Synset Examples

the unprovoked and dastardly attack by Japan on … December 7th

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Synonym: dastardly - characteristic of a dastard; wickedly cowardly

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *dastard* (coward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæstərd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːstəd / (BrE)
A person who is contemptibly cowardly or mean-spirited.
cowardly or mean-spirited person
archaic

Examples

The knight refused to be called a dastard for retreating from the battle.
Only a dastard would betray his friends to save himself.
In the story, the dastard was driven out of town for his cowardice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person

Etymology

From Middle English *dastard*, probably from Old Norse *dást* (weary, exhausted) + English agent suffix *-ard*.
