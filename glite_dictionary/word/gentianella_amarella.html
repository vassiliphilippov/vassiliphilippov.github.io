Gentianella amarella

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃəˈnɛl.ə ˌæm.əˈrɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈnel.ə ˌam.əˈrel.ə / (BrE)
A species of gentian found in Europe and China, having creamy white flowers with fringed corollas.
a gentian species with creamy white fringed flowers

Examples

The meadows were dotted with delicate Gentianella amarella blooming among the grass.
Botanists have recorded Gentianella amarella in several protected habitats across northern Europe.
She identified the plant as Gentianella amarella by its distinct fringed corollas.

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name 'Gentianella' (a diminutive of 'Gentiana') and species name 'amarella' (diminutive of 'amara', meaning 'bitter').
