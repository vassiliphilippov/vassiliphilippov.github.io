Fagus grandifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs ˌɡrændɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs ˌɡrændɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of beech tree native to North America, with light green leaves and edible nuts.
North American species of beech tree

Examples

The Fagus grandifolia is an important species in eastern North American forests.
He collected nuts from a mature Fagus grandifolia to plant in his garden.
Many animals rely on the fruit of Fagus grandifolia as a food source in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American beech - Common English name for Fagus grandifolia

Etymology

From Latin: Fagus (beech) + grandifolia (large-leaved).
