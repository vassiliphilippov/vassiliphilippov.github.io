damnably

1. ADV

/ ˈdæm.nəb.li / (AmE), / ˈdam.nə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely bad or annoying; used for emphasis to mean 'very' in a negative sense.
to an extreme or annoying degree

Examples

The weather was damnably cold that night, making it almost impossible to sleep.
She found his arrogance damnably irritating during the entire meeting.
It is damnably difficult to get a straight answer from the committee.

Synset Examples

kindly Arthur — so damnably, politely, endlessly persistent!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damnable - deserving strong condemnation; extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terribly - to a great or extreme degree

Etymology

From "damnable" + "-ly"; "damnable" from Middle English, from Old French "damnable," from Late Latin "damnabilis."
