cabbala

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbælə / (BrE)
A system of esoteric or occult philosophy, especially one resembling the mystical tradition of the Kabbalah and often regarded as secret or obscure.
esoteric or mystical system like Kabbalah

Examples

He devoted years to the study of cabbala in hopes of unlocking hidden wisdom.
Some secret societies were rumored to practice a form of cabbala passed down through generations.
Their discussions often drifted into the realm of cabbala and the interpretation of ancient mysteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Jewish mystical tradition of esoteric interpretation of the Scriptures

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cabbala', from Hebrew 'qabbālāh' (tradition, received doctrine).
