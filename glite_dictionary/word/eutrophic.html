eutrophic

1. ADJ

/ juˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / juːˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
(of a lake or other body of water) rich in nutrients, especially phosphates and nitrates, which promote the growth of algae and plants, often leading to reduced oxygen levels.
rich in nutrients (of water body)

Examples

The pond became eutrophic after years of fertilizer runoff from nearby farms.
Researchers studied the fish populations in several eutrophic lakes across the region.
As the reservoir turned eutrophic, excessive algae growth caused oxygen levels to drop.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oligotrophic - (of a lake or water body) poor in nutrients and not productive in terms of plant life
  • Degree Lower: mesotrophic - (of a lake or water body) having a moderate amount of nutrients

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (well) + 'trophe' (nourishment).
