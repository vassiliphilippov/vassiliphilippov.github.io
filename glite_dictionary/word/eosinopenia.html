eosinopenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːəˌsoʊnəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌiːəʊsɪnəˈpiːnɪə / (BrE)
a lower than normal number of eosinophils in the blood, usually indicating certain medical conditions or states
reduced eosinophils in blood
medical

Examples

The patient's blood tests revealed eosinopenia, suggesting a possible acute infection.
Corticosteroid therapy is one of the common causes of eosinopenia.
Doctors may look for eosinopenia when evaluating patients with suspected inflammatory diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukopenia - a reduction in the number of white blood cells in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'eōsinos' (of eosin, a red dye) + '-penia' (deficiency).
