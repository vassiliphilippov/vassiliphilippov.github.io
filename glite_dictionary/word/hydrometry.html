hydrometry

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːmətri / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the science or process of measuring the specific gravity of liquids
measurement of specific gravity
medical

Examples

The scientist specialized in hydrometry to determine the quality of different water samples.
Innovations in hydrometry have improved the accuracy of measuring liquid densities in laboratories.
Before adding the chemicals, she checked the specific gravity using hydrometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrometer - an instrument for measuring the specific gravity of liquids

Etymology

From Greek hydor (water) + metron (measure).
