copperhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a venomous, reddish-brown snake native to Australia, known for its sluggish movements
venomous reddish-brown Australian snake

Examples

The copperhead is often found near water in parts of southern Australia.
When threatened, the Australian copperhead tends to remain still rather than attack aggressively.
Hikers should be cautious in the bush, as the copperhead can be difficult to spot among fallen leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agkistrodon contortrix - a venomous pit viper found in North America, also called 'copperhead'

Etymology

Named for its copper-colored head.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəhɛd / (BrE)
a venomous, coppery-brown pit viper (Agkistrodon contortrix) found in upland regions of the eastern United States
a venomous pit viper of the eastern US

Examples

We spotted a copperhead while hiking in the Appalachian foothills.
The copperhead is known for its distinctive, hourglass-shaped bands on its body.
Bites from a copperhead are rarely fatal but require immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonmouth - a related venomous North American pit viper
  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American pit viper with a rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

Refers to its copper-colored head; from 'copper' (the metal) + 'head'.
