Lampropeltis getulus

1. NOUN

/ læmˌproʊˈpɛltɪs gəˈtuːləs / (AmE), / læmˌprəʊˈpɛltɪs gɛˈtuːləs / (BrE)
A species of nonvenomous snake native to the United States, commonly known as the eastern kingsnake, typically black or brown with yellow bands.
a species of nonvenomous kingsnake

Examples

The Lampropeltis getulus is revered for its resistance to venom and striking coloration.
Herpetologists often study Lampropeltis getulus due to its diverse habitat range across the United States.
I spotted a Lampropeltis getulus slithering through the underbrush during my hike in Georgia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingsnake - a common name for several species in the genus Lampropeltis

Etymology

From Greek 'lampros' (shining) + 'peltē' (shield) with species epithet from Latin, referring to its glossy, shielded scales.
