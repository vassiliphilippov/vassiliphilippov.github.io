metabola

1. NOUN

/ məˈtæbələ / (AmE), / mɛˈtabələ / (BrE)
(in entomology) insects that undergo complete metamorphosis, passing through egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages.
insects with complete metamorphosis

Examples

Beetles and butterflies are classic examples of metabola in the insect world.
Scientists study the development of metabola to understand evolutionary adaptations.
The classification of metabola distinguishes them from insects with incomplete metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holometabola - insects with complete metamorphosis

Etymology

From Greek 'metabolē' meaning 'change'.
