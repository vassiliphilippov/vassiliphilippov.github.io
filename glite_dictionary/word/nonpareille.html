nonpareille

1. NOUN

/ nɒn.pəˈrɛl / (AmE), / nɒn.pəˈrɛl / (BrE)
A historical size of printing type, specifically a 6-point type, smaller than minion.
a 6-point printing type size

Examples

The headline was set in nonpareille to fit more words on the page.
Old printing presses often used nonpareille for footnotes or fine print.
The typesetter requested a font smaller than minion, choosing nonpareille for the project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: minion - a size of printing type slightly larger than nonpareille

Etymology

From French 'nonpareille', meaning 'without equal', originally used to denote something unparalleled.
