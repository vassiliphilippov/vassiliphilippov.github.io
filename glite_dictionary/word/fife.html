fife

1. NOUN

/ faɪf / (AmE), / faɪf / (BrE)
a small, high-pitched flute, similar to a piccolo but with a shriller tone, used chiefly to accompany drums in a marching band
small high-pitched flute

Examples

The melody of the fife could be heard over the rhythm of the drums during the parade.
He learned to play the fife as part of his school's marching band.
A traditional fife and drum corps performed at the historical reenactment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piccolo - a small flute with a higher pitch than the ordinary flute

Etymology

From German *Pfeife* (pipe, whistle), related to French *fifre*.
