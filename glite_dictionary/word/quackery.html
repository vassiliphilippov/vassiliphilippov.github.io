quackery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwæk.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈkwæk.ə.ri / (BrE)
dishonest or ignorant medical practices or advice lacking a basis in scientific evidence
fraudulent or ignorant medical practice

Examples

The government cracked down on quackery that endangered patients' lives.
Many historical medical treatments are now dismissed as mere quackery.
He warned the public to avoid the quackery advertised on late-night television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - deceit or trickery for personal gain
  • Antonym: medicine - the science or practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease

Etymology

From 'quack' (charlatan) + -ery, denoting practice or behaviour.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwæk.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈkwæk.ər.i / (BrE)
Fraudulent or deceptive practices, especially in medicine, by someone pretending to have professional knowledge or skill.
fraudulent medical practices

Examples

The government cracked down on quackery in the health industry.
Many desperate patients fell victim to quackery before effective regulations were established.
He exposed the clinic's treatments as nothing more than quackery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatanism - the practice or methods of a charlatan; fraud

Etymology

From 'quack' (a fraudulent medical practitioner) + '-ery'.
