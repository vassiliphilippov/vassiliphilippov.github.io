assizes

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪzɪz / (AmE), / əˈsaɪzɪz / (BrE)
periodic criminal courts formerly held in English counties to administer justice; replaced by Crown courts in 1971.
historic English county criminal courts
legal archaic british

Examples

The circuit judge presided over the assizes in the county every spring.
Before 1971, serious criminal cases in England were often tried at the assizes.
Historical records reveal that the assizes played a crucial role in rural justice.

Etymology

From Old French *assises* (sessions), from Medieval Latin *assisia* (session, sitting).
