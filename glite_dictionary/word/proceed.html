to proceed

1. VERB

/ proʊˈsiːd / (AmE), / prəˈsiːd / (BrE)
to begin or continue doing something, especially according to a set procedure or course of action
begin or continue an action or process

Examples

After the safety checks, we can proceed with the experiment.
The meeting will proceed as planned despite the delay.
Passengers are asked to proceed to gate 22 for boarding.

Synset Examples

We should go farther in this matter
She went through a lot of trouble
go about the world in a certain manner
Messages must go through diplomatic channels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
  • Antonym: stop - to cease moving or acting

Etymology

From Latin procedere, from pro- (forward) + cedere (to go).
