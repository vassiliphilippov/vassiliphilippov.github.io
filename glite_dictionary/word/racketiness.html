racketiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræk.ɪ.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈræk.ɪ.ti.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being loud and filled with constant or disturbing noise
state of being noisy or loud

Examples

The racketiness of the traffic outside made it hard to concentrate.
Guests complained about the racketiness of the party next door.
He preferred the countryside to the racketiness of city life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisiness - the quality of making a lot of noise
  • Antonym: quietness - the state of being quiet; absence of noise

Etymology

From 'racket' (loud noise) + '-iness' (forming nouns meaning 'the state of being').
