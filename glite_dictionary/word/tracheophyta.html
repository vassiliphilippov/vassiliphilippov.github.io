Tracheophyta

1. NOUN

/ trəˌkiːəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / trəˌkiːəˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a former scientific classification for plants that have a vascular system, including ferns, fern allies, and seed plants
group of vascular plants (old classification)

Examples

Tracheophyta includes plants such as ferns, conifers, and flowering plants, all of which have a vascular system.
The classification of Tracheophyta was used widely in older botanical literature.
Most common land plants belong to the Tracheophyta group, distinguished by their conductive tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vascular plant - a plant with specialized tissues for conducting water and nutrients

Etymology

From Greek trācheia (windpipe) + phyton (plant), referring to the vascular 'tracheid' cells.

2. NOUN

/ treɪˌkiːəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / trəˌkiːəʊˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a former classification of plants with a vascular system, including ferns, fern allies, and seed plants
group of vascular plants (former classification)

Examples

The Tracheophyta include both ferns and seed-bearing plants in their grouping.
Researchers debated the exact membership of the Tracheophyta during the 20th century.
In older textbooks, plants were often classified as Tracheophyta if they had vascular tissue.

Etymology

From Greek 'tracheia' (windpipe) + 'phyton' (plant), referring to the vessels in vascular plants.
