Salix viminalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks vɪˈmɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks vɪˈmɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a species of willow with long, flexible twigs commonly used in basketry
a willow species used in basketry

Examples

The flexible twigs of Salix viminalis are ideal for weaving baskets and furniture.
Many traditional craftspeople cultivate Salix viminalis along riverbanks for its pliable branches.
Gardeners appreciate Salix viminalis for both its ornamental value and its usefulness in basketry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osier - a type of willow with flexible stems used in basketry
  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: salix (willow) and viminalis (of osiers, suitable for wickerwork).
