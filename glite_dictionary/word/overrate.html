to overrate

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈreɪt / (BrE)
to estimate or value something or someone as better, more important, or more capable than is actually the case
to value too highly; overestimate

Examples

Many critics tend to overrate the film's artistic value.
It's easy to overrate your abilities when you lack experience.
He overrated his opponent and was surprised by his own easy victory.

Synset Examples

He overestimated his own powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underrate - to underestimate or value less than deserved
  • Synonym: overestimate - to estimate too highly, especially in amount, value, or worth

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + rate (to estimate or value).
