to euthanatise

1. VERB

/ ˌjuːθəˈnɑːtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌjuːθəˈnætʌtaɪz / (BrE)
to intentionally cause the painless death of a person or animal, especially to relieve suffering
to cause painless death, especially to relieve suffering

Examples

The vet had to euthanatise the injured horse to prevent further suffering.
Some believe it is more humane to euthanatise severely ill animals.
After a long debate, the decision was made to euthanatise the dog due to its pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to euthanize - to intentionally cause the painless death of a person or animal, especially to relieve suffering (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek *eu* (well) + *thanatos* (death); compare 'euthanasia'.
