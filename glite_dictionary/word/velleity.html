velleity

1. NOUN

/ vəˈliːəti / (AmE), / vɛˈliːɪti / (BrE)
A wish or inclination that is not strong enough to lead to action or effort to obtain it.
wish without effort to fulfill it

Examples

She had a velleity to change careers but never took any concrete steps.
His velleity for adventure rarely extended beyond reading about distant lands.
Even the faintest velleity to call her evaporated as time passed.

Etymology

From Latin velleitas, from velle (to wish).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈliː.ə.ti / (AmE), / vəˈliː.ɪti / (BrE)
A wish, desire, or inclination that is too weak to lead to action.
a very weak or passive wish

Examples

He had only a velleity to change jobs, never enough motivation to apply anywhere.
Despite my velleity to exercise in the morning, I stayed in bed instead.
Her interest in politics remained a velleity, never turning into real engagement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: desire - a strong feeling of wanting to have something
  • Degree Higher: wish - a desire or hope for something to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'velleitas', from 'velle' (to wish).
