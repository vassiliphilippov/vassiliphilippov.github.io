to bastardize

1. VERB

/ ˈbæstərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪz / (BrE)
(historical, legal) To declare a child to be illegitimate.
declare a child illegitimate (historic or legal)
archaic legal

Examples

Until the 20th century, courts would sometimes bastardize children born out of wedlock.
The law was changed so that children could no longer be officially bastardized by decree.
A cruel verdict in 1850 bastardized the boy, stripping him of his inheritance rights.

Etymology

From bastard + -ize, ultimately from Old French *bastard*.

2. VERB

/ ˈbæstɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪz / (BrE)
to lower the quality or value of something by adding unwanted elements or by changing it in a way that makes it less pure or true to its original form
corrupt or lower the quality of something

Examples

Many critics argue that reality television has bastardized the art of documentary filmmaking.
He refused to bastardize his family's recipes just to appeal to modern tastes.
When companies try to mass-produce traditional crafts, they often bastardize the original designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - to lower in quality or character
  • Synonym: corrupt - to make something less pure or honest

Etymology

From 'bastard' + '-ize'; 'bastard' from Old French 'bastart', meaning illegitimate child.
