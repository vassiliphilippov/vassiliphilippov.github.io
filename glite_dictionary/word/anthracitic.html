anthracitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænθrəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanθrəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling anthracite coal, typically in terms of composition, appearance, or properties.
resembling anthracite coal

Examples

The geologists discovered a layer of anthracitic shale in the old mine.
These rocks show anthracitic characteristics, making them valuable for fuel analysis.
Some local coals are so anthracitic they almost shine with a metallic luster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthracite - a hard, shiny form of coal with high carbon content

Etymology

From anthracite + -ic, meaning 'of or pertaining to anthracite'.
