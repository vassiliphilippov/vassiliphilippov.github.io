spoilage

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or result of something being spoiled or damaged, especially food becoming unfit for use
process or result of being spoiled

Examples

The spoilage of fresh fruit can be reduced by refrigeration.
Food spoilage is a major concern for grocery stores.
Improper storage led to the spoilage of the entire batch of grain.

Synset Examples

her spoiling my dress was deliberate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - gradual destruction or decomposition
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from decay or damage

Etymology

From the verb 'spoil' + '-age', modeled on similar noun formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
The process of becoming spoiled or ruined, especially as a result of decay or contamination.
the process of decay or becoming spoiled

Examples

The spoilage of perishable goods can lead to significant financial losses for businesses.
Proper refrigeration greatly reduces the risk of food spoilage during transportation.
They implemented new packaging techniques to prevent spoilage and extend shelf life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - The process of rotting or decomposing
  • Antonym: preservation - The process of keeping something from decaying or spoiling
