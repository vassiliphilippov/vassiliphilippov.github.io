Oenanthe aquatica

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈɛnænθi əˈkwɑːtɪkə / (AmE), / əʊˈɛnænθi ɔːˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
A European poisonous herb with fibrous roots, commonly found in wet places; also known as water dropwort.
European poisonous herb (water dropwort)

Examples

In some marshes, Oenanthe aquatica grows abundantly along the water's edge.
Farmers were warned about the poisonous nature of Oenanthe aquatica.
Many botanists study Oenanthe aquatica because of its toxic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water dropwort - Common name for Oenanthe aquatica, a European poisonous herb

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Oenanthe from Greek oinos (wine) + anthos (flower); aquatica meaning 'of water'.
