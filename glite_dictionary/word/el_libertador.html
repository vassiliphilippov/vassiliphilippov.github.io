El Libertador

1. PROPN

/ el lɪˌbɛr.təˈdɔr / (AmE), / el lɪˌbɛː.təˈdɔː / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes in Argentina, standing 20,932 feet (6,384 meters) high.
mountain in the Andes, Argentina

Examples

Many climbers aspire to reach the summit of El Libertador every year.
The weather on El Libertador can change rapidly, making ascents challenging.
From the valley below, we saw the snow-capped peak of El Libertador shining in the sunlight.

Etymology

Spanish for 'The Liberator', originally named 'Aconcagua'.

2. PROPN

/ el liˌbɛr.tɑˈðɔr / (AmE), / ɛl ˌliː.bəˈtɑː.dɔː / (BrE)
a title given to Simón Bolívar, the Venezuelan leader who led South American movements for independence from Spanish rule
title for Simón Bolívar, South American liberator

Examples

Simón Bolívar is often referred to as El Libertador for his pivotal role in South America's independence.
Monuments to El Libertador can be found in many Latin American capitals.
The term El Libertador signifies Bolívar's status as a national hero across the continent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Simón Bolívar - Venezuelan revolutionary leader known as El Libertador

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'The Liberator.'
