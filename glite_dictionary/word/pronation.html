pronation

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prəʊˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of rotating the hand or forearm so that the palm faces downward or backward
rotation of hand so palm faces downward
medical

Examples

Excessive pronation of the foot can lead to injury in runners.
Physical therapists monitor pronation and supination during rehabilitation exercises.
Proper pronation is important for absorbing shock when walking or running.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supination - rotation of the hand or forearm so the palm faces upward or forward

Etymology

From Latin 'pronatio', from 'pronus' (bent forward, prone).
