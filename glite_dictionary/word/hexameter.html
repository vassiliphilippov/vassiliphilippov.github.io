hexameter

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæm.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / hɛkˈsæm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
a line of verse containing six metrical feet
verse line with six metrical feet

Examples

The epic uses hexameter to create a grand, rhythmic effect throughout its lines.
Many classical poems were written in dactylic hexameter, especially in ancient Greek and Latin literature.
She analyzed each hexameter in the passage, noting the pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pentameter - verse line with five metrical feet
  • Degree Higher: heptameter - verse line with seven metrical feet

Etymology

From Latin 'hexameter,' from Greek 'hexametros' meaning 'having six measures'.
