widespread

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (BrE)
existing or happening over a large area or among many people; widely distributed or prevalent.
widely prevalent or distributed

Examples

The rumor became so widespread that the city officials had to issue a statement.
There is widespread support for the new policy among educators.
Concerns about climate change are now widespread throughout society.

Synset Examples

a widespread doctrine
widespread fear of nuclear war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevalent - common or widespread in a particular area or at a particular time
  • Antonym: rare - not occurring very often or found in few places

Etymology

Formed from 'wide' + 'spread'. Earliest recorded in English in the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (BrE)
existing or happening over a large area or among many people or things.
covering a large area; extensive

Examples

There was widespread confusion following the sudden announcement.
The disease caused widespread panic in several countries.
Climate change has widespread effects on ecosystems around the world.

Synset Examples

far-flung trading operations
the West's far-flung mountain ranges
widespread nuclear fallout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extensive - covering a large area; having great extent
  • Antonym: localized - limited to a particular area or region
