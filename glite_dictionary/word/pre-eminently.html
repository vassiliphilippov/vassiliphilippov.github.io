pre-eminently

1. ADV

/ priˈɛmɪnəntli / (AmE), / priˈɛmɪnəntli / (BrE)
To a degree that is superior to all others; in a way that shows distinction or prominence above others.
with superiority or distinction

Examples

She is pre-eminently qualified for the position, possessing experience far beyond her peers.
The city is pre-eminently known for its vibrant arts scene.
This novel stands out as pre-eminently original among the author's works.

Synset Examples

a wide variety of pre-eminently contemporary scenes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preeminently - Alternative spelling without hyphen

Etymology

Formed from 'pre-eminent' (from Latin 'praeeminere', to project forward, stand out) plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.
