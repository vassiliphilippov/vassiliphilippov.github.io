censurable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsərəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsjərəbl̩ / (BrE)
Deserving blame or formal disapproval; worthy of censure.
deserving blame or censure

Examples

The manager's handling of the sensitive information was highly censurable and led to disciplinary action.
The teacher reminded the students that any censurable behavior would not be tolerated in the classroom.
Failure to report the error was censurable under the company's code of conduct.

Synset Examples

blameworthy if not criminal behavior
censurable misconduct
culpable negligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or censure
  • Synonym: culpable - deserving blame, especially for something wrong
  • Antonym: praiseworthy - deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From Latin censurabilis, from censura (judgment, assessment).
