to misgive

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡɪv / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡɪv / (BrE)
(of a person's mind or heart) to cause to feel fear, doubt, or apprehension.
cause to feel doubt or fear

Examples

Her heart misgave her as she approached the dark alley.
As the decision day drew nearer, my mind misgave me more and more.
He smiled, but something in her expression misgave him.

Synset Examples

Her heart misgave her that she had acted inexcusably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less fearful or doubtful

Etymology

From Middle English misgiven, from mis- (wrongly) + give.
