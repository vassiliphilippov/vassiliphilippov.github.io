hypermetropy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚˈmɛ.trə.pi / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈmɛ.trə.pi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which distant objects are seen more clearly than near ones; farsightedness.
condition of farsightedness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hypermetropy after she complained of trouble reading close-up.
Wearing corrective lenses helped him manage his hypermetropy.
Hypermetropy often develops with age and may require prescription glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsightedness - the condition of seeing distant objects more clearly than near ones
  • Antonym: myopia - the condition of nearsightedness

Etymology

From Greek hypermetria (excessive measure) + -opia (vision).
