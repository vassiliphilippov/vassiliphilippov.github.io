polyurethane

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈjʊrəˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈjʊərəˌθeɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer used in various forms such as foam, elastomers, or coatings, often as adhesives, paints, or plastics
synthetic polymer used in foams, paints, plastics

Examples

The sofa cushions are made of polyurethane foam for extra comfort.
The craftsman used a polyurethane varnish to protect the wooden floor.
Many athletic shoes have midsoles constructed from lightweight polyurethane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymer - a substance consisting of large molecules made up of many repeating units

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) + 'urethane', referencing its chemical structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈjʊrəˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈjʊərəˌθeɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer used in the production of foams, adhesives, paints, and flexible or rigid plastics
a synthetic polymer used in manufacturing

Examples

Our couch is made from polyurethane foam for extra comfort.
The car seats are covered with a durable polyurethane coating.
They used polyurethane as an adhesive in the construction project.

Etymology

From poly- (many) + urethane (a type of chemical compound).
