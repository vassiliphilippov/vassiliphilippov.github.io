revenue

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛv.əˌnu / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.jə.njuː / (BrE)
Income received by a government from taxes and other sources.
government income from taxation

Examples

The government's revenue from taxes increased significantly this year.
Falling oil prices led to a sharp decline in the country's revenue.
New policies were introduced to boost local revenue through improved tax collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis

Etymology

From Old French 'revenu', past participle of 'revenir' (to return), from Latin 'revenire' (to come back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛv.əˌnu / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.juː / (BrE)
the total income generated by a business or organization before any expenses or deductions are taken out
total income before deductions

Examples

The company's revenue increased significantly last quarter due to higher sales.
They reported annual revenue of over two million dollars before taxes and expenses.
Tourism is a major source of revenue for the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments

Etymology

From Old French *revenu* (income), from revenir (to return), from Latin *revenire* (to return).
