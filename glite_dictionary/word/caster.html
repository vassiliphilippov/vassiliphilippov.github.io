caster

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cast molten metal into finished products by pouring it into molds
person who casts molten metal

Examples

The skilled caster poured the molten bronze into the intricate mold.
Many foundries employ a caster to produce machine parts.
As a caster, she specialized in creating metal sculptures for artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundry worker - a person who works in a foundry

Etymology

From cast + -er, denoting someone who casts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə / (BrE)
a small wheel, usually mounted on a swivel, attached to the bottom of furniture, equipment, or machinery to make it easier to move.
pivoting wheel for moving furniture or equipment

Examples

The office chair rolled smoothly across the floor on its casters.
After replacing the broken caster, the cart moved without any trouble.
Many suitcases today come with swivel casters for easy transport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheel - circular object that rotates and helps something move

Etymology

From cast (to throw, to turn) + -er, referring to something that rolls or turns easily.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə / (BrE)
a small container with a perforated top, used for sprinkling powdered sugar or other fine substances
shaker for sprinkling powdered sugar

Examples

She reached for the caster to sprinkle some sugar on her strawberries.
A silver caster sat in the center of the tea table.
The baker dusted the cake with icing sugar from a small caster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaker - a container with a perforated top for sprinkling salt, pepper, etc.

Etymology

From cast (to throw or scatter), influenced by the action of sprinkling sugar.
