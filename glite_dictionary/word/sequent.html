sequent

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwənt / (BrE)
following in a regular or logical order, with no gaps between events or items.
in regular succession without gaps

Examples

The sequent meetings were scheduled every Monday throughout the semester.
Events occurred in a sequent pattern, each leading directly to the next.
The concerts happened in sequent evenings with no days skipped.

Synset Examples

serial concerts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following continuously without interruption
  • Synonym: successive - following one after the other

Etymology

From Latin 'sequent-' (following), from 'sequi' (to follow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwənt / (BrE)
Occurring or following as a result or consequence; happening in succession.
following as a consequence

Examples

A prolonged drought and the sequent crop failures devastated the region.
The victory was sequent to months of rigorous training and preparation.
Their arguments and the sequent decisions shaped future policy.

Synset Examples

an excessive growth of bureaucracy, with attendant problems
snags incidental to the changeover in management
attendant circumstances
the period of tension and consequent need for military preparedness
the ensuant response to his appeal
the resultant savings were considerable
collateral target damage from a bombing run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequent - happening as a result or effect
  • Synonym: subsequent - coming after something in time; following

Etymology

From Latin 'sequent-', stem of 'sequens', present participle of 'sequi' (to follow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00123654-s
