Plotinus

1. PROPN

/ ploʊˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / plɒˈtaɪnəs / (BrE)
(c. 205–270) A Roman philosopher, born in Egypt, who was the leading figure of Neoplatonism.
Roman philosopher, founder of Neoplatonism

Examples

Plotinus is often regarded as the founder of Neoplatonism.
The works of Plotinus influenced later Christian and Islamic philosophy.
Many scholars study Plotinus to better understand the development of metaphysical thought.

Etymology

From Latin Plotinus, the philosopher's name.
