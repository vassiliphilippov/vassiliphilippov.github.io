isostasy

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsɑːstəsi / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒstəsi / (BrE)
(Geology) The state of equilibrium in which the earth's crust floats at an elevation that depends on its thickness and density, balanced by the buoyancy of the underlying mantle.
geological equilibrium of the earth's crust
medical

Examples

The concept of isostasy explains why mountain ranges gradually sink as they erode over time.
Due to isostasy, land previously covered by glaciers is slowly rising even today.
Scientists study isostasy to understand changes in sea level and crustal movement.

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'stasis' (standing, stoppage).
