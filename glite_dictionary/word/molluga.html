Molluga

1. NOUN

/ məˈluːɡə / (AmE), / məˈluːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed herbs known as carpetweeds, belonging to the family Molluginaceae.
a genus of carpetweeds

Examples

The sandy soil in the area was covered with sprawling Molluga plants.
Some gardeners struggle to remove Molluga from their vegetable beds.
Botanists have identified several species within the Molluga genus.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Molluga', the genus name assigned by botanists.

2. NOUN

/ məˈluːɡə / (AmE), / məˈluːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Molluginaceae, commonly known as carpetweeds, characterized by their small, herbaceous, and often prostrate growth.
genus of plants (carpetweeds)

Examples

Several species within the Molluga genus are well-known for their sprawling mats in warm regions.
The botanist collected samples of Molluga during his fieldwork in the tropics.
Weeds from the Molluga genus often thrive in disturbed soils and gardens.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Molluga', used as a botanical genus name.
