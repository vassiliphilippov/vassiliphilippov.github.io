lebensraum

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbənsraʊm / (AmE), / ˈleɪbənsraʊm / (BrE)
(historical) Territory or space considered necessary for the expansion of a nation's population, especially as associated with Nazi Germany's policies before and during World War II.
space needed for a nation's expansion (historical, esp. Nazi policy)

Examples

The concept of lebensraum played a central role in Nazi ideology and expansionist policies.
Historians have debated the extent to which lebensraum influenced Germany's actions during World War II.
Discussions of lebensraum often address its justification for territorial conquest and colonization.

Etymology

From German Lebensraum, literally 'living space' (Leben 'life' + Raum 'space').
