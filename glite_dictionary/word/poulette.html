poulette

1. NOUN

/ puːˈlɛt / (AmE), / puːˈlɛt / (BrE)
A sauce made from allemande sauce with chopped parsley, commonly used in French cuisine.
French sauce with parsley

Examples

The chef drizzled the delicate fish with a rich poulette to enhance its flavor.
In classic French cooking, a poulette is often paired with eggs or vegetables.
She learned to prepare poulette from a recipe in her grandmother's French cookbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allemande sauce - a traditional French velouté sauce thickened with egg yolks and cream

Etymology

From French 'poulette', diminutive of 'poule' meaning 'hen', but in cuisine denotes a sauce.
