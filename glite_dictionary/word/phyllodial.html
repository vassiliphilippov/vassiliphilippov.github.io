phyllodial

1. ADJ

/ fɪˈloʊdiəl / (AmE), / fɪˈləʊdiəl / (BrE)
(Botany) Having or relating to a phyllode; possessing leaves or leaf-like structures that are phyllodes.
having phyllodes (botany)

Examples

The phyllodial branches are a distinctive feature of this acacia species.
Many Australian plants have phyllodial leaves instead of true leaves.
Scientists studied the phyllodial development to understand adaptation mechanisms in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phyllode - a flattened leaf stalk resembling and functioning as a leaf

Etymology

From phyllode + -ial; 'phyllode' from Greek phyllon (leaf).
