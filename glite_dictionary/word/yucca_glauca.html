Yucca glauca

1. NOUN

/ ˈjukə ˈɡlaʊkə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˈɡlaʊkə / (BrE)
A species of yucca native to the west central United States, characterized by a clump of grasslike leaves at the base and a tall stalk bearing a cluster of small whitish flowers.
a species of yucca plant in central U.S.

Examples

The tough leaves of the Yucca glauca were traditionally used by Native Americans for making rope.
Each spring, the Yucca glauca produces a large stalk covered with white blossoms.
Gardeners value Yucca glauca for its drought resistance and distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapweed - another common name for Yucca glauca

Etymology

From Latin 'Yucca' (the genus name) and Latin 'glaucus' meaning bluish-green or grayish, referring to the leaf color.
