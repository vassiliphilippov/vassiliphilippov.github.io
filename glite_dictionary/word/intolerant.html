intolerant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑlərənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒlərənt / (BrE)
Unwilling to accept or respect opinions, beliefs, or behaviors that differ from one's own.
unwilling to accept differing opinions

Examples

Her intolerant attitude made it difficult for anyone with opposing views to speak freely.
He became increasingly intolerant of criticism as he gained more authority.
A healthy society cannot thrive if its members are overly intolerant of differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tolerant - willing to accept opinions or behaviors different from one's own
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced

Etymology

From Latin 'intolerantem', from 'in-' (not) + 'tolerare' (to endure).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑlɚənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒlərənt / (BrE)
Unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own; narrow-minded or bigoted.
unwilling to accept differing views

Examples

Some people are intolerant of any criticism, no matter how constructive.
The school does not tolerate intolerant attitudes toward students from diverse backgrounds.
He grew more intolerant as he became set in his ways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tolerant - willing to accept opinions or behaviors different from one's own
  • Synonym: bigoted - having or revealing an obstinate belief in the superiority of one's own opinions

Etymology

From Latin 'intolerans', present participle of 'intolerare' (to not bear, to be unable to endure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00289103-s
Synset POS: s
