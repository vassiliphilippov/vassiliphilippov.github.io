loge

1. NOUN

/ loʊʒ / (AmE), / lɒʒ / (BrE)
A small, private area in a theater or grandstand, often separated from the main seating, where a group can watch the performance in relative privacy.
private area in a theater for a small group

Examples

Their family reserved a loge at the opera house for every Friday night performance.
She watched the ballet from the comfort and privacy of her loge.
From our loge, we had an excellent view of the entire stage and the orchestra.

Synset Examples

the royal box was empty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - an enclosed area with seats in a theater, often used for private viewing

Etymology

From French 'loge' (box in a theater), from Old French, meaning 'lodge' or 'hut'.

2. NOUN

/ loʊʒ / (AmE), / ləʊʒ / (BrE)
a small, separate section of seats, usually in the front part of a theater mezzanine or balcony, often regarded as more exclusive or private
separate theater seating area

Examples

We reserved a loge for opening night to enjoy a better view of the stage.
VIP guests were seated in the private loge above the orchestra.
The loge filled quickly with theatergoers seeking comfort and privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - a small, private compartment in a theater

Etymology

From French 'loge' (box, enclosure), ultimately from Old French, meaning 'hut' or 'lodge'.
