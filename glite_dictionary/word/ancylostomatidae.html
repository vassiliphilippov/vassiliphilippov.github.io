Ancylostomatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.sə.loʊ.stəˈmæt.ɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌansiːlɒstəˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic nematode worms commonly known as hookworms, some species of which infect humans and other mammals.
family of parasitic hookworms
medical

Examples

Studies have shown that Ancylostomatidae can cause significant health issues in both humans and animals.
Veterinarians often screen for Ancylostomatidae in pets presenting with signs of anemia.
Understanding the life cycle of Ancylostomatidae is crucial for developing effective prevention programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hookworm - a parasitic nematode worm that inhabits the intestine

Etymology

From New Latin Ancylostoma (a genus name) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
