animalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the representation or depiction of something in the form or likeness of an animal
depiction as an animal

Examples

The artist employed animalization to depict human emotions through various animal forms.
Some myths use animalization to represent gods as creatures such as lions or snakes.
The animalization of certain characters made the fable more engaging for children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anthropomorphism - attribution of human traits to non-human things

Etymology

From 'animal' + '-ization', patterning after Latin animalis (animal) and suffix -ization (action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪmələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone or something cruel or lacking normal human qualities, especially by treating them in a dehumanizing way
making cruel or less human

Examples

The brutal conditions in the prison led to the animalization of many inmates.
Some critics argue that violent video games contribute to the animalization of young people.
The novel explores the animalization of society during times of war and chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dehumanization - the process of depriving a person or group of positive human qualities

Etymology

From animal + -ization, modeled after Latin animalis (having breath) with the common noun-forming suffix.
