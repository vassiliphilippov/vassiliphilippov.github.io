Th

1. NOUN

/ ti ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / tiː ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for thorium, a soft, silvery-white radioactive metallic element used as a power source in nuclear reactors
chemical symbol for thorium (element)

Examples

Th is used as a fuel source in some types of nuclear reactors.
Monazite sands are an important source of Th and rare earth metals.
The element with the symbol Th was discovered in the early 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorium - a soft silvery-white radioactive metallic element

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'thorium', named after Thor, the Norse god of thunder.
