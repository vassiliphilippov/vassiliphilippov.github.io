swindler

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪndlɚ / (AmE), / ˈswɪndləmə / (BrE)
a person who cheats others by deception or fraud, especially to obtain money or property unlawfully
person who cheats others by fraud

Examples

The police arrested the swindler after he deceived several elderly victims out of their savings.
It takes a skilled swindler to create such an elaborate scheme without raising suspicion.
The magazine published a story warning readers about a notorious swindler operating in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraudster - a person who commits fraud; synonym of swindler
  • Synonym: cheat - a person who acts dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Antonym: honest person - a person who does not cheat or use deception; opposite of swindler

Etymology

From German 'schwindler', from 'schwindeln' meaning 'to be dizzy, to cheat.'
