Escherichia coli

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə ˈkoʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə ˈkəʊlaɪ / (BrE)
A species of bacterium commonly found in the intestines of humans and other animals; some strains may cause illness in humans.
bacterium found in intestines, can cause illness
medical

Examples

Some food poisoning outbreaks have been caused by Escherichia coli contamination in undercooked meat.
Scientists study Escherichia coli to understand bacterial genetics and antibiotic resistance.
Proper handwashing helps prevent infections from harmful strains of Escherichia coli.

Etymology

Named after German bacteriologist Theodor Escherich; from Latin 'coli', genitive of 'colon' (large intestine).
