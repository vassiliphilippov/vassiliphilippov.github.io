abatement

1. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪtmənt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of reducing the degree, amount, or intensity of something; reduction.
act of reducing something

Examples

The city introduced strict abatement measures to control air pollution.
Following the abatement of the storm, residents returned to their homes.
The landlord was ordered to carry out noise abatement in the apartment complex.

Synset Examples

laws enforcing noise abatement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the action or fact of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - an instance of growing or becoming greater

Etymology

From Old French 'abetement', from 'abatre' (to beat down), based on Latin 'battere' (to beat).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪtmənt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪtmənt / (BrE)
a reduction in the degree, intensity, or amount of something, especially something negative or undesirable
a reduction in intensity or amount

Examples

After days of heavy rain, there was finally an abatement in the storm.
The company promised to work toward the abatement of noise pollution in the area.
They celebrated the abatement of the city's harsh winter winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the action of making something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: increase - an instance of growing or becoming greater

Etymology

From Old French abatement, from abatre 'to beat down' (see abate).
