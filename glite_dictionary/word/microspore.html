microspore

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.kroʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪ.krəʊˌspɔː / (BrE)
the smaller of the two types of spore produced in some plants, which develops into a male gametophyte
small spore in some plants (male precursor)
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, each microspore appeared round and uniform in size.
The microspore eventually develops into the pollen grain in flowering plants.
Researchers studied the development of the microspore to better understand plant reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: megaspore - the larger of the two types of spores (female precursor) produced in heterosporous plants

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + sporā (seed, spore).
