dolichocephalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊləˌkoʊˈsɛfəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəˈsɛfəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The condition of having a relatively long, narrow head.
the quality of having a long, narrow head
medical

Examples

Dolichocephalism was once used to classify certain populations in anthropological studies.
Some dog breeds are noted for their pronounced dolichocephalism.
Examination of the skull revealed distinct dolichocephalism compared to typical human cranial shapes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycephalism - the condition of having a broad, short head

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kephalē (head) + -ism (condition).
