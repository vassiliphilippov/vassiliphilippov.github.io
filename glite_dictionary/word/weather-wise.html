weather-wise

1. ADV

/ ˈwɛðɚ waɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə waɪz / (BrE)
with regard to or considering the weather or weather conditions
regarding the weather

Examples

Weather-wise, the festival was blessed with perfect conditions all weekend.
Weather-wise, January can be unpredictable in this region.
Weather-wise, tomorrow looks like the best day for our outdoor activities.

Synset Examples

Weather-wise, the trip was a success with sunny days.
Weather-wise, it’s best to plan the picnic for Saturday.
They decided, weather-wise, to postpone the hike.

Etymology

Formed by combining 'weather' with the suffix '-wise' meaning 'in terms of' or 'with respect to'.
