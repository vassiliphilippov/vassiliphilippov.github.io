bearcat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌkæt / (BrE)
an arboreal mammal of Southeast Asia with shaggy black fur and a long prehensile tail, also known as the binturong
arboreal mammal of Asia (binturong)

Examples

The bearcat uses its long tail to grasp branches as it moves through the treetops.
A bearcat may look fierce, but it is generally shy and avoids humans.
Many zoos feature the bearcat in their Southeast Asian animal exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binturong - another name for the bearcat, arboreal civet of Asia
