bibliotics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪbliˈɑtɪks / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliˈɒtɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of documents and handwriting, especially to determine their authorship or authenticity
study of documents and handwriting authenticity

Examples

The court hired an expert in bibliotics to verify the signatures on the contested documents.
Her research in bibliotics revealed that the ancient manuscript had been forged.
Modern bibliotics combines traditional handwriting analysis with digital technology.

Etymology

From Greek 'biblion' (book) + '-ics' (study of, science).
