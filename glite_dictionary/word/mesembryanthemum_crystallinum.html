Mesembryanthemum crystallinum

1. NOUN

/ mɛˌsɛm.bri.ænˈθiː.məm ˌkrɪs.təˈlaɪ.nəm / (AmE), / mɛˌsɛm.brɪ.ænˈθiː.məm ˌkrɪs.təˈlaɪ.nəm / (BrE)
A succulent plant of the fig-marigold family, native to Africa and Europe, with white flowers and fleshy, crystalline foliage resembling ice.
succulent with crystalline foliage, 'ice plant'

Examples

Mesembryanthemum crystallinum thrives in sandy soils with plenty of sunlight and minimal water.
Gardeners often plant Mesembryanthemum crystallinum for its unique, sparkling leaves that resemble frost.
The coastal dunes were covered with Mesembryanthemum crystallinum, making the landscape appear as if dusted with ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice plant - Common name for Mesembryanthemum crystallinum, a succulent with crystalline foliage

Etymology

From Greek 'mesembria' (midday) + 'anthemon' (flower), referring to the flowering time.
