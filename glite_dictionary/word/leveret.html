leveret

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvərət / (AmE), / ˈlɛvərɪt / (BrE)
a young hare, especially one in its first year
a young hare

Examples

We spotted a tiny leveret hiding in the tall grass during our walk.
The mother hare left her leveret alone for most of the day, returning only at dusk.
A leveret is especially vulnerable to predators in its first months of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal similar to a rabbit
  • Degree Higher: kitten - a young cat

Etymology

From Old French leveret, diminutive of levre (hare), from Latin lepus.
