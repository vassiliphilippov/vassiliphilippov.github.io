grumose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡruːˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡruːməʊs / (BrE)
having a lumpy or grainy texture, especially as formed from a liquid turning into a semisolid or solid mass.
having a lumpy or grainy texture

Examples

The pathologist noted that the clot was grumose and difficult to remove.
After hours at room temperature, the sample became grumose and had to be discarded.
Old soup left in the pot can develop a grumose consistency.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood
curdled milk
grumous blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulated - transformed from a liquid into a thickened, semisolid mass
  • Synonym: curdled - having thickened or clotted, especially referring to milk

Etymology

From Latin grumosus (full of lumps), from grumus (a little heap or lump).
