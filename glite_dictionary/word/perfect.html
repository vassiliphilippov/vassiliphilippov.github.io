perfect

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.fɪkt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt / (BrE)
a verb tense or aspect used to describe an action that has been completed at some point before now or before another action
verb tense indicating completed action

Examples

The present perfect is used to describe actions that have happened at an unspecified time.
In English, the perfect often combines 'have' with the past participle of the verb.
German verbs also have a past perfect tense used in formal writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfective - verb aspect emphasizing completed action without focus on the result
  • Antonym: imperfect - verb tense/aspect showing ongoing or repeated action in the past

Etymology

From Latin perfectus, past participle of perficere (to complete, finish).
