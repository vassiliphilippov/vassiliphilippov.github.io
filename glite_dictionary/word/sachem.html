sachem

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.tʃəm / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.tʃəm / (BrE)
a chief or leader of certain North American Indigenous peoples, especially among the Algonquian-speaking tribes
a Native American tribal chief

Examples

The tribal sachem made important decisions for his people.
Several sachems gathered to discuss the treaty with the settlers.
The young man aspired to become sachem after his father's passing.

Etymology

From Narragansett *sâchim*, meaning 'chief'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.tʃəm / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.tʃəm / (BrE)
a leader or chief, especially one of the Tammany Hall political organization in New York City
chief leader, esp. of Tammany Hall

Examples

He became a sachem of Tammany Hall, wielding significant influence over city politics.
Many aspiring politicians sought the favor of the sachem to advance their careers.
In the nineteenth century, the powerful sachem was often seen as the real authority behind the mayor.

Etymology

From Narragansett (a Native American language), meaning 'chief' or 'leader'.
