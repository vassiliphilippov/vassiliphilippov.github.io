soundness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndnəs / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being based on good judgment and reasoning; prudence and sensibility
prudent, sensible judgment

Examples

The soundness of his decisions has earned him respect among colleagues.
We questioned the soundness of her financial advice before investing.
Effective leadership depends on the soundness of long-term planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - cautious and sensible behavior; good judgment
  • Antonym: rashness - acting without careful consideration or good judgment

Etymology

From Old English 'sund' (healthy, safe) + -ness (forming nouns of quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being free from damage, decay, or defect; being whole or in good condition.
state of being undamaged or in good condition

Examples

The soundness of the bridge was confirmed after a thorough inspection.
He doubted the soundness of the old wooden beams in the attic.
Regular maintenance is key to preserving the soundness of your house's foundation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrity - the state of being whole and undivided
  • Antonym: decay - the process of gradually becoming worse or weaker

Etymology

From Middle English 'soundnesse', from 'sound' (whole, healthy) + '-ness'.
