Aglaomorpha

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.li.oʊˈmɔr.fə / (AmE), / ˌaɡ.li.əˈmɔː.fə / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic ferns native to the tropical regions of Asia, known for their large, leathery fronds.
genus of Asian epiphytic ferns

Examples

Several species of Aglaomorpha are prized as ornamental plants in tropical gardens.
Researchers studied how Aglaomorpha ferns adapt to living on tree branches in humid forests.
The distinctive fronds of Aglaomorpha make them easy to identify among other Asian ferns.

Etymology

From Greek 'aglaos' (splendid, bright) and 'morphē' (form, shape), referencing the attractive form of the fronds.
