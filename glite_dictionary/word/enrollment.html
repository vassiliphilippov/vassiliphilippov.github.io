enrollment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈroʊlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊlmənt / (BrE)
the act of officially joining or registering for a course, school, or organization
the act of enrolling

Examples

Enrollment in the university has increased dramatically this year.
Online enrollment allows students to sign up for classes from home.
The school requires proof of residency at the time of enrollment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registration - the process or act of registering

Etymology

From the verb 'enroll', from Old French 'enroller' (to register), from 'rolle' (list, roll).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈroʊlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊlmənt / (BrE)
The group of people registered or enrolled at a particular institution, especially at a school or college, at a given time.
the group of registered people, e.g., students

Examples

The university reported that its enrollment increased by five percent this year.
Next semester's enrollment is expected to reach a new record.
The enrollment of international students has doubled over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registration - the act or process of enrolling or being enrolled
  • Synonym: class - a group of students who enroll or graduate together

Etymology

From enroll + -ment; derived from Old French enroller and Middle English enrolement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08496997-n
Synset POS: n
