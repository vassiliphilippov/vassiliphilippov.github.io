unhappiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈhæpinɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈhæpinɪs / (BrE)
the feeling of being unhappy; a state of sadness or discontent
state of being unhappy or sad

Examples

Her face was clouded with unhappiness as she read the letter.
Periods of unhappiness are a normal part of life for many people.
They tried to hide their unhappiness for the sake of their children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadness - the feeling of being sad; sorrow
  • Antonym: happiness - the state of being happy

Etymology

From un- (not) + happiness, based on happy (from Middle English, from Old Norse happ, meaning 'good luck').

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈhæpinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈhæpinɪs / (BrE)
The state of being unhappy; feelings of discontentment, sorrow, or distress.
state of being unhappy

Examples

His constant unhappiness was evident in the way he spoke and acted.
She tried to hide her unhappiness, but her friends could sense something was wrong.
Periods of unhappiness are a natural part of life, even for the most optimistic people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Synonym: misery - extreme unhappiness or distress
  • Synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by loss

Etymology

From un- (not) + happiness; modelled after 'happiness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14012148-n
Synset POS: n
