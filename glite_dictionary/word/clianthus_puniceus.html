Clianthus puniceus

1. NOUN

/ kliˈænθəs pjuˈnisiəs / (AmE), / klaɪˈænθəs pjuːˈnɪsiəs / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to New Zealand, known for its scarlet or white claw-shaped flowers
evergreen New Zealand shrub with distinctive flowers

Examples

The vibrant flowers of the Clianthus puniceus attract many native birds in New Zealand gardens.
Gardeners prize Clianthus puniceus for its dramatic, beak-like blooms.
In spring, the Clianthus puniceus produces clusters of scarlet flowers that brighten up any landscape.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek *klios* (glory) + *anthos* (flower) for genus; species from Latin *puniceus* (scarlet).
