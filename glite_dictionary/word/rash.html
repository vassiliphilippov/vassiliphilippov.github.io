rash

1. NOUN

/ ræʃ / (AmE), / ræʃ / (BrE)
a group of red spots on the skin, often caused by illness, allergy, or irritation
red spots or eruption on the skin

Examples

The child developed a rash after eating strawberries.
If you notice a rash spreading on your body, consult a doctor.
Some medications may cause a rash as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden appearance of spots or lesions on the skin

Etymology

From Old French *rasche*, from Latin *rasus* (scraped).

2. NOUN

/ ræʃ / (AmE), / ræʃ / (BrE)
a large number of unpleasant or undesirable events that happen suddenly within a short period
sudden series of unpleasant events

Examples

There has been a rash of break-ins in the neighborhood this month.
A sudden rash of complaints overwhelmed the customer service department.
The company faced a rash of lawsuits after the product recall.

Synset Examples

a rash of bank robberies
a blizzard of lawsuits
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08476098-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ræʃ / (AmE), / ræʃ / (BrE)
acting or done without careful consideration of the possible consequences; reckless or impulsive.
reckless; lacking caution

Examples

It was rash of him to invest all his money in one company.
She made a rash decision and regretted it later.
Climbing without proper equipment would be incredibly rash.

Synset Examples

foolhardy enough to try to seize the gun from the hijacker
became the fiercest and most reckless of partisans
a reckless driver
a rash attempt to climb Mount Everest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or danger

Etymology

From Middle French *rasche*, from Old High German *rasch* (quick, eager, rash).

4. ADJ

/ ræʃ / (AmE), / ræʃ / (BrE)
acting or done without careful consideration of the possible consequences; impetuous or reckless
acting without careful thought

Examples

He regretted making such a rash decision without considering the consequences.
It would be rash to invest all your savings in a single company.
Her rash promise left her with no way to back out later.

Synset Examples

do something rash that he will forever repent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action
  • Antonym: careful - giving thought to the possible consequences of an action; exercising caution

Etymology

From Middle French *rasche* (impetuous), from archaic French *rasche* (a rush, act of haste), possibly related to Middle Dutch *rass* (quick, hasty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01906255-s
