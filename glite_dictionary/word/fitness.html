fitness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or role
suitability; appropriateness

Examples

The board assessed the candidate's fitness for leading the new department.
Questions were raised about his fitness to hold public office after the investigation.
We need to evaluate the fitness of each material for use in construction.

Synset Examples

they had to prove their fitness for the position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitability - the quality of being right or appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation
  • Antonym: inappropriateness - the quality of being unsuitable or not appropriate

Etymology

From fit + -ness, with 'fit' from Old Norse 'fitja' (to knit, tie).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being suitable or qualified for a particular role, purpose, or activity
quality of being qualified or suitable

Examples

The board questioned his fitness to serve as the new director.
Her fitness for the role was never in doubt.
We must assess the fitness of all candidates before making a final decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitability - the quality of being right or appropriate for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: qualification - the quality or state of being qualified
  • Antonym: unsuitability - the quality of not being suitable

Etymology

From fit + -ness, formed in English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
The condition of being physically healthy and strong, often as a result of exercise and proper nutrition.
good physical condition; being in shape

Examples

Her daily exercise routine has greatly improved her fitness over the past year.
Many people join gyms to maintain their fitness and stay healthy.
Athletes often have to maintain a high level of physical fitness to perform at their best.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health - the state of being free from illness or injury
  • Antonym: unfitness - the quality or state of not being fit or suitable

Etymology

From 'fit' + '-ness', originally from Old English 'fit' (suitable, proper) and '-ness' (state, quality).
