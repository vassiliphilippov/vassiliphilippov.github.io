pupil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪl / (BrE)
The circular opening in the center of the iris of the eye, which controls the amount of light that enters the eye.
opening in the iris of the eye

Examples

The doctor shined a light into his eye to check his pupil reaction.
In dim light, your pupil widens to let in more light.
A sudden flash caused her pupil to contract quickly.

Etymology

From Latin 'pupilla' (little doll), originally referring to the tiny reflection seen in the eye.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪl / (BrE)
a young person who attends school, especially below university level
school-aged child or student

Examples

Each pupil is expected to wear a uniform to school every day.
The teacher encouraged every pupil to participate in the class discussion.
New pupils will be welcomed at the assembly next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college

Etymology

From Latin 'pupillus' (ward, orphan); influenced by Latin 'pupilla' (little girl, doll).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪl / (BrE)
a student, especially a child, who is enrolled at a school or other educational institution
student at a school

Examples

The teacher praised every pupil for their hard work during the semester.
Each pupil must wear a uniform while attending the primary school.
The school organizes regular meetings between parents and pupil mentors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college

Etymology

From Latin 'pupillus' (orphan, ward), meaning a minor under care.
