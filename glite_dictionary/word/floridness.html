floridness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪdnəs / (BrE)
a quality of being excessively elaborate or ornate, especially in style or language
extravagant elaborateness or ornateness

Examples

The floridness of his prose sometimes distracted from the story itself.
Critics often debate whether floridness adds charm or simply muddles the message.
Some readers appreciate the floridness of 19th-century literature, while others find it excessive.

Synset Examples

he wrote with great flamboyance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floridity - the state of being flowery or excessively ornate in style
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or not elaborate

Etymology

From Latin 'floridus' meaning 'flowery'.
