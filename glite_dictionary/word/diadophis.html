Diadophis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈædəfɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈadəfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender North American snakes in the family Colubridae, known as ringneck snakes.
genus of North American ringneck snakes

Examples

The genus Diadophis is commonly studied by herpetologists due to its widespread presence across North America.
Many field guides include descriptions of Diadophis because ringneck snakes are often found in gardens and forests.
Diadophis species are easily identified by the colorful ring around their necks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringneck snake - a small North American snake with a ring-like marking around its neck

Etymology

From Greek 'dias' (through, across) and 'ophis' (snake).
