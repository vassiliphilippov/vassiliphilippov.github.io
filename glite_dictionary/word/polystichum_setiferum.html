Polystichum setiferum

1. PROPN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈstɪkəm ˌsɛtɪˈfɜːrəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm ˌsɛtɪˈfɜːrəm / (BrE)
A species of European shield fern, widely cultivated for its attractive, feathery fronds and grown in many varieties.
a European shield fern species

Examples

Polystichum setiferum is a popular choice for shaded areas in many European gardens.
Gardeners appreciate Polystichum setiferum for its dense, evergreen fronds.
The delicate texture of Polystichum setiferum makes it a favorite among fern enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polystichum' (genus of ferns) and 'setiferum' meaning 'bristle-bearing'.
