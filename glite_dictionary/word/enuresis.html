enuresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.jʊˈri.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.jʊˈriː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person is unable to control urination, leading to involuntary urination, especially during sleep
involuntary urination, often during sleep
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that enuresis is fairly common among young children and usually decreases with age.
She was embarrassed by her enuresis, but her parents reassured her it was not her fault.
Chronic enuresis may require further medical evaluation to rule out underlying causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedwetting - involuntary urination during sleep, especially in children

Etymology

From Greek enourein (to urinate in), from en- (in) + ouron (urine).
