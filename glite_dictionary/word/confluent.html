confluent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnfluənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfluənt / (BrE)
a stream or river that flows into a larger one; a tributary
a stream that joins a larger one

Examples

The two confluents meet just north of the valley to form a larger river.
Several confluents add to the volume of the main river during the rainy season.
Each confluent contributes its own silt and minerals to the main flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tributary - a river or stream that flows into a larger river or lake

Etymology

From Latin 'confluens', present participle of 'confluere' (to flow together).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnfluənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfluənt / (BrE)
(of streams, rivers, or any flowing entities) flowing together or merging at a common point.
merging or flowing together

Examples

The two rivers become confluent just outside the city limits and form a single wide stream.
Several streams are confluent in this valley during the rainy season, leading to frequent floods.
The disease is characterized by confluent skin lesions that merge into larger patches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divergent - moving apart or separating from a common point

Etymology

From Latin 'confluens', present participle of 'confluere' (to flow together), from 'con-' (together) + 'fluere' (to flow).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
