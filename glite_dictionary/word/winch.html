winch

1. VERB

/ wɪntʃ / (AmE), / wɪntʃ / (BrE)
to pull or lift something up, especially using a winch or similar mechanical device
pull up with a winch

Examples

The workers used a crane to winch the heavy crate onto the truck.
They had to winch the stranded car out of the muddy ditch.
He carefully winched up the anchor before setting sail.

Synset Examples

winch up the slack line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoist - to lift or raise something, especially by using ropes or machinery

2. NOUN

/ wɪntʃ / (AmE), / wɪntʃ / (BrE)
a machine for lifting or pulling heavy objects, consisting of a horizontal drum turned by a handle or motor, around which a rope or cable is wound
machine for lifting or pulling with a winding drum

Examples

The workers used a winch to hoist the heavy equipment onto the truck.
We secured the boat to the dock with the help of a mechanical winch.
If the car gets stuck in mud, a winch can be used to pull it free.

Etymology

From Old English *winc* (reel, pulley), related to Old High German *wincha* (reel).
