ignoratio elenchi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡ.nəˌreɪ.ʃioʊ ɪˈlɛŋ.kaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪɡ.nəˌræ.tɪ.əʊ ɪˈleŋ.kaɪ / (BrE)
A logical fallacy in which an argument presented proves a point irrelevant to the subject at hand, instead of the point at issue.
logical fallacy; irrelevant argument

Examples

The politician's response committed the fallacy of ignoratio elenchi by addressing a different issue than the one raised.
Her essay was full of ignoratio elenchi, making arguments that did not pertain to the thesis question.
Recognizing ignoratio elenchi is vital when analyzing debates to avoid being misled by irrelevant points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red herring - An argument or piece of information intended to distract from the main issue.

Etymology

From Latin 'ignoratio elenchi', meaning 'ignorance of refutation'.
