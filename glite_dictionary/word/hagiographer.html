hagiographer

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæɡiˈɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who writes a biography that idealizes its subject, especially a saint or revered figure
writer of idealizing biography

Examples

As a noted hagiographer, she chronicled the miraculous deeds of medieval saints.
The film’s portrayal of the leader is so glowing, it seems the writers acted as hagiographers.
Some critics accused the author of being a hagiographer rather than a serious historian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biographer - a person who writes an account of another person's life

Etymology

From Greek hagios (holy) and -grapher (writer); originally used for writers of saints' lives.
