U308

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː θriː ˈoʊ eɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuː θriː ˈəʊ eɪt / (BrE)
an impure mixture of uranium oxides obtained during the processing of uranium ore, used as an intermediate in uranium production
impure uranium oxide mixture

Examples

The processing of uranium ore produces U308 as an intermediate product.
Transport regulations for U308 are strict due to its radioactive nature.
Scientists analyzed a sample of U308 to determine its purity levels.

Etymology

Chemical notation: 'U' for uranium and '3O8' indicates three atoms of uranium combined with eight atoms of oxygen, referring to the formula U3O8.
