sorgho

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəʊ / (BrE)
any of several types of sorghum grown primarily for producing syrup
sorghum grown for syrup

Examples

Farmers in the region planted sorgho to produce sweet syrup for the local market.
The workshop taught participants how to process sorgho into molasses.
Historically, sorgho was an important crop in areas lacking access to sugar cane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a cereal grass widely cultivated for its grain

Etymology

From French 'sorgho', likely from Italian 'sorgo', ultimately from Latin 'sorghum'.
