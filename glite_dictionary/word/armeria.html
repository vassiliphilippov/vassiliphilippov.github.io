Armeria

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmɛriə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby or herbaceous low-growing evergreen perennial plants, often grown for their clump-forming foliage and clusters of small flowers.
genus of low-growing evergreen perennials

Examples

Armeria is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and attractive blooms.
Gardeners often plant Armeria along borders for year-round greenery and spring flowers.
The pink flowers of Armeria add a splash of color to coastal landscapes.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Old French armerie (thrift, a type of plant).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɪəriə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing, evergreen, shrubby or herbaceous perennial plants, commonly known as thrift or sea pinks.
genus of evergreen perennial plants

Examples

The rocky coastline was dotted with clusters of Armeria in full bloom.
Gardeners often plant Armeria for its resilience and attractive pink flowers.
Several species within the Armeria genus are popular as ground cover in rock gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name.
