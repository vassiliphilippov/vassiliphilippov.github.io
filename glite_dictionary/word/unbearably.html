unbearably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈbɛrəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɛːrəbli / (BrE)
to a degree that is impossible to tolerate; extremely or excessively
to an intolerable degree

Examples

The noise from the construction site was unbearably loud all day long.
She found the pain after the surgery unbearably intense, even with medication.
It became unbearably cold after the sun went down.

Synset Examples

it was unbearably hot in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intolerably - to an intolerable or insufferable degree
  • Antonym: bearably - in a manner that can be tolerated

Etymology

From 'unbearable' + '-ly'. 'Unbearable' derives from 'bear' (to endure) + '-able'.
