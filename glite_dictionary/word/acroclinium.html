Acroclinium

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.roʊˈklɪn.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˈklɪnɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering herbs and shrubs, native to Australia and South Africa, known for their everlasting flowers; most species are now classified under Helipterum
genus of everlasting flowering herbs or shrubs

Examples

The gardener planted Acroclinium to add vibrant color to the flower beds.
Dried Acroclinium blooms are often used in floral arrangements for their longevity.
Although once called Acroclinium, these flowers are now usually placed in the genus Helipterum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helipterum - a genus of flowering plants closely related to Acroclinium

Etymology

From Greek akron (top, summit) + klinein (to lean), referring to the flower's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈklɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌækrəʊˈklɪnɪəm / (BrE)
a former genus of Australian and South African herbs and shrubs, known as everlasting flowers; most species are now classified under the genus Helipterum.
former botanical genus of everlasting flowers

Examples

Many gardeners grow Acroclinium for its attractive, papery flowers that last well when dried.
Botanists have reclassified most Acroclinium species under the genus Helipterum.
The delicate pink flowers of Acroclinium are popular in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helipterum - genus of flowering plants, includes species previously in Acroclinium

Etymology

From Greek akros (highest) + klinein (to bend).
