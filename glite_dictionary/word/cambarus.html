Cambarus

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈbeɪrəs / (AmE), / kamˈbɑːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater crayfish found primarily in North America.
genus of freshwater crayfish

Examples

Several species of Cambarus are commonly found in Appalachian streams.
Biologists studied the Cambarus population to assess local ecosystem health.
The Cambarus genus contains dozens of different crayfish varieties.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Cambarus, used in zoological nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.bə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈkæm.bə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater crayfish in the family Astacidae, native to North America.
a genus of freshwater crayfish

Examples

The Appalachian region is home to several species of Cambarus crayfish.
Researchers discovered a new Cambarus species living in the underground streams.
The conservation status of many Cambarus crayfish is a concern for biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - a freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Cambarus', based on Greek 'kambaros' (a kind of crab).
