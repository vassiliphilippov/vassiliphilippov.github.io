Nabalus

1. NOUN

/ nəˈbeɪləs / (AmE), / nəˈbeɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of North American and East Asian perennial herbs in the family Asteraceae; sometimes included in the genus Prenanthes.
genus of perennial herbs

Examples

The wildflower guide notes that Nabalus species are common in shaded woods.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Nabalus should be merged with Prenanthes.
Several Nabalus plants bloom in late summer across North America.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈbeɪləs / (AmE), / nəˈbeɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of North American and East Asian perennial herbs, sometimes classified within the genus Prenanthes.
genus of perennial herbs

Examples

Several species in the genus Nabalus are found in temperate North America.
The classification of Nabalus has been subject to debate among botanists.
Wildflowers belonging to Nabalus can be seen blooming in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prenanthes - a related genus of perennial herbs sometimes including Nabalus species

Etymology

From modern Latin Nabalus, used as a genus name in taxonomy.
