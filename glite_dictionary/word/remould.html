to remould

1. VERB

/ riˈmoʊld / (AmE), / ˌriːˈməʊld / (BrE)
to put new treads on a worn tire; to retread.
put new treads on a tire

Examples

The company specializes in remoulding old tires for commercial vehicles.
Many drivers choose to remould their tires to save money.
It is important that the remoulded tires meet safety standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retread - to put new treads on a worn tire

Etymology

From re- (again) + mould, meaning to form again; used in technical contexts for tires.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163880-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈmoʊld / (AmE), / ˌriːˈməʊld / (BrE)
to cast (an object) again by shaping it in a mould, typically after it has been damaged or needs to be repaired
cast again by molding

Examples

After the statue broke, the artist decided to remould it using the original design.
The foundry can remould metal objects that have become damaged over time.
Old car tires are often remoulded to extend their useful life.

Synset Examples

The bell cracked and had to be recast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recast - to cast again, form anew in a mould

Etymology

From re- (again) + mould (to shape in a mould).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01667100-v
Synset POS: v
