fervour

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrvər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvə / (BrE)
intense and passionate feeling; strong emotion or enthusiasm
intense passionate feeling
british

Examples

The crowd cheered with great fervour as the hero took the stage.
She spoke about her beliefs with such fervour that everyone listened intently.
Political debates often inspire intense fervour among supporters.

Synset Examples

his face was flushed with excitement and his hands trembled
he tried to calm those who were in a state of extreme inflammation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervor - Intense and passionate feeling; standard American spelling
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - Intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: apathy - Lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin fervor (boiling, heat, passion) via Old French.

intense and passionate feeling

Examples

His fervour for environmental causes inspired many people to get involved.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apathy - Lack of interest or enthusiasm
