skilful

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɪlfʊl / (BrE)
having or showing great skill, ability, or expertise in doing something
having great skill or ability

Examples

Her skilful hands quickly repaired the broken watch.
The skilful performance impressed everyone in the audience.
He is known for his skilful negotiation techniques in difficult situations.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unskilful - lacking skill or ability
  • Synonym: skilled - having skill; showing expertise
  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient
  • Synonym: expert - highly knowledgeable or skillful

Etymology

From skill + -ful, formed in Middle English.
