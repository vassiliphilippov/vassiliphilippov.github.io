Vitis vinifera

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtɪs vɪˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / ˌvaɪtɪs vɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
The common European grapevine, widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used to produce wine and eaten as table grapes.
common European grapevine species

Examples

Many of the world's finest wines are produced from Vitis vinifera grapes.
Botanists study Vitis vinifera for its diverse varieties and rich genetic heritage.
Europeans have cultivated Vitis vinifera for thousands of years, both for eating and wine-making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapevine - plant that produces grapes, especially for wine

Etymology

From Latin: 'vitis' meaning 'vine', and 'vinifera' meaning 'wine-producing'.
