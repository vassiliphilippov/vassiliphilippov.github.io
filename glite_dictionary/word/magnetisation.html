magnetisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡ.nə.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the extent or degree to which a material is magnetized
degree to which something is magnetized

Examples

The magnetisation of the sample was measured with precise instruments.
As the temperature increased, the magnetisation of the iron bar decreased.
Different materials reach different levels of magnetisation under the same magnetic field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetization - US spelling of 'magnetisation'

Etymology

From magnet + -isation, after French 'magnétisation' or based on Latin 'magnes' (magnet).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmægnətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɡnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a material magnetic, either temporarily or permanently
making a material magnetic

Examples

The magnetisation of the metal rod was achieved by stroking it with a magnet.
During the experiment, students observed the gradual magnetisation of the iron filings.
Heating a material can affect its magnetisation and sometimes cause it to lose its magnetic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetization - US spelling of 'magnetisation'; the process of making a substance magnetic

Etymology

From magnet + -isation, patterned after French 'magnétisation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13531636-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡnətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæɡnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state in which a material becomes magnetic or exhibits magnetic properties
becoming or being magnetic

Examples

The magnetisation of the iron bar increased when it was placed near a strong magnet.
Rapid changes in magnetisation can affect how electronic devices function.
Researchers measured the magnetisation of the material at varying temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetization - alternate spelling, especially used in American English

Etymology

From magnet + -isation, after French magnétisation.
