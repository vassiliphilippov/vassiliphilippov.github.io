Lespedeza striata

1. NOUN

/ lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə straɪˈɑː.tə / (AmE), / lɛs.pɪˈdiː.zə straɪˈɑː.tə / (BrE)
An annual herb native to tropical Asia, now naturalized in the United States, commonly known as Japanese clover.
Asian annual herb; Japanese clover

Examples

Lespedeza striata is often used as a cover crop to prevent soil erosion in the southern United States.
Gardeners appreciate Lespedeza striata for its ability to improve soil fertility naturally.
In some regions, Lespedeza striata has become an important forage plant for livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese clover - Common name for Lespedeza striata, an annual herb naturalized in the U.S.

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of Lespedez (from Spanish botanist Vicente Manuel de Céspedes); 'striata' means 'striped' in Latin.
