hum

1. VERB

/ hʌm / (AmE), / hʌm / (BrE)
to sing a tune with closed lips, producing a wordless melody
sing a tune with closed lips

Examples

She likes to hum her favorite song while working.
He hummed quietly to the baby until she fell asleep.
I often find myself humming a tune when I'm in a good mood.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sing - to produce musical tones with the voice

2. VERB

/ hʌm / (AmE), / hʌm / (BrE)
(of a place) to be full of activity and noise, often from people working or moving about.
be busy and noisy with activity

Examples

The office was humming with activity on Monday morning.
The restaurant hummed with conversation and laughter all evening.
By midday, the market was humming as customers arrived in large numbers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - to be busy and full of lively activity and noise
