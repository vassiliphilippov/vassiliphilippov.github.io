whitish

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat white in color; having a slightly white or pale appearance.
somewhat white in color

Examples

The sky was overcast with a whitish haze that softened the sunlight.
She noticed a whitish patch on the surface of the cheese and decided not to eat it.
The old photograph had faded to a whitish tone over the years.

Synset Examples

milky glass

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white - of the color of milk or fresh snow

Etymology

From 'white' + '-ish', the adjectival suffix indicating resemblance or degree.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat white in color; having a color that tends toward white.
somewhat white in color

Examples

The kitten had a whitish patch on its ear.
Clouds appeared whitish against the morning sky.
She wore a whitish dress that shimmered in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white - having the color of milk or fresh snow, due to the reflection of most wavelengths of visible light
  • Degree Lower: grayish - somewhat gray in color

Etymology

Formed from 'white' + '-ish', a suffix meaning 'somewhat' or 'like'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00393521-s
Synset POS: s
