Amphicarpa bracteata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈkɑrpə brækˈtiːətə / (AmE), / ˌæmfɪˈkɑːpə brækˈtiːətə / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to eastern North America, bearing purple to maroon flowers and edible, usually subterranean, one-seeded pods that resemble peanuts.
North American vine with edible underground pods

Examples

The Amphicarpa bracteata thrives in moist woodlands where its vines climb over shrubs and small trees.
Early settlers foraged for the edible pods of the Amphicarpa bracteata, often called hog peanut.
Botanists noted that Amphicarpa bracteata produces both above-ground flowers and hidden underground seed pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog peanut - Common name for Amphicarpa bracteata, a North American vine with edible pods

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi' (on both sides) and 'karpos' (fruit), with the species epithet from Latin 'bracteata' (having bracts).
