to effectuate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈfɛktʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to happen; to bring about or produce a result
cause to happen; bring about

Examples

The new policy is designed to effectuate significant changes in the educational system.
It took several meetings to effectuate an agreement between the two companies.
Only the board of directors has the power to effectuate major corporate decisions.

Synset Examples

The scientists set up a shock wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring about - to cause something to happen
  • Synonym: to accomplish - to achieve or complete successfully
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

Etymology

From Medieval Latin effectuare, from Latin effectus (accomplished, done).
