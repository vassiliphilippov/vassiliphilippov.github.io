bullocky

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊl.ə.ki / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ə.ki / (BrE)
physically strong and powerful like a bullock; having the strength or build of a bullock
strong and powerful like a bullock

Examples

He had bullocky shoulders that strained the seams of his shirt.
Her bullocky strength surprised everyone during the lifting contest.
The wrestler's bullocky build made him a formidable opponent.

Synset Examples

thick bullocky shoulders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullish - having characteristics of a bull; strong and assertive

Etymology

From 'bullock' (a young bull), with the suffix '-y' forming an adjective meaning 'like a bullock'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʊləki / (AmE), / ˈbʊləkɪ / (BrE)
behaving in an arrogant or boastful manner
arrogant or boastful in manner
informal

Examples

After his promotion, he started to act bullocky around the office.
She dislikes people who are too bullocky at parties.
Don't get bullocky just because you've won one match.

Synset Examples

He was acting a bit bullocky after winning the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Synonym: boastful - showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction in one's achievements
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

Probably from 'bullock' (a young bull), with '-y' suffix forming an adjective, originally Australian English slang meaning tough or aggressive like a bullock team driver.
