Pentastomida

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˌtæstəˈmɪdə / (AmE), / pɛnˌtastəˈmɪdə / (BrE)
A class of parasitic, worm-like animals known as tongue worms, inhabiting the respiratory tracts of vertebrates.
class of parasitic tongue worms

Examples

The lungs of snakes are commonly infested by Pentastomida.
Medical researchers studied how Pentastomida infect their vertebrate hosts.
Infections caused by Pentastomida are considered rare in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue worm - parasitic worm-like animal of the class Pentastomida

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the organism's mouth parts.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛntəˈstɑmədə / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈstɒmɪdə / (BrE)
a subphylum of parasitic worm-like animals, commonly known as tongue worms, that primarily infect the respiratory tracts of vertebrates
parasitic worm-like animals (tongue worms)
medical

Examples

Tongue worms of the Pentastomida infect various reptiles and mammals worldwide.
Researchers studied the unusual life cycle of organisms in the Pentastomida.
Veterinarians sometimes identify Pentastomida infections in exotic pets.

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) and 'stoma' (mouth), referencing their mouth structures.
