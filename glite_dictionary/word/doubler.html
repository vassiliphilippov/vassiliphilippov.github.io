doubler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌblɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌblə / (BrE)
an electronic device that doubles the voltage or frequency of an input signal
device that doubles voltage or frequency

Examples

The technician installed a doubler to increase the system's voltage to the required level.
A frequency doubler is often used in radio transmitters to generate higher frequencies.
When the original signal was too weak, he connected it to a doubler for amplification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiplier - an electronic device that increases voltage, frequency, or another signal property by a multiple

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌblɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌblə / (BrE)
a device or feature designed to increase internet speed by doubling the effective data transfer rate
device that increases internet speed

Examples

After installing the doubler, my downloads finished much faster than before.
This new doubler promises substantial improvements in internet performance for gamers.
Many users reported that the doubler effectively resolved their streaming issues.

Synset Examples

The new feature acts as a doubler for your internet speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerator - a device that increases speed, especially in computing

Etymology

From 'double' plus the agent suffix '-er', indicating something that doubles.
