subscription

1. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an agreement to support or endorse something, often indicated by signing one's name or making a commitment
agreement by signature or endorsement

Examples

Her subscription to the petition was recorded at the city hall.
The agreement required the subscription of all board members to become effective.
By his subscription, he pledged support for the foundation's goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - an act of giving public approval or support to something

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptio' (a signing, signature), from 'subscribere' (to write underneath).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07194480-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
A payment made to receive consecutive issues of a newspaper or magazine for a specified period.
payment for periodical issues

Examples

She renewed her subscription to the magazine for another year.
The newspaper offers a monthly subscription that includes weekend deliveries.
His subscription expired last week, so he missed the latest issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: membership - the state of belonging to a group or organization, often involving periodic payment

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptio' (something written below), from 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13302153-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səbˈskɹɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskɹɪpʃən / (BrE)
a sum of money pledged or contributed, usually to support a cause, project, or publication
pledged or contributed money

Examples

The committee launched a subscription to raise funds for the new hospital.
His subscription to the memorial fund was greatly appreciated by the community.
A bronze plaque listed the names of those who made a subscription toward the building's construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given, especially to a charity or cause

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptio', from 'subscribere' (to write underneath, sign).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the act of signing one's name, especially as a signature on a document to attest or authorize it
the act of signing one's name
legal

Examples

The contract became valid upon the subscription of the parties involved.
Legal documents often require the subscription of witnesses to be enforceable.
The agreement was formalized by the subscription of her signature at the end of the document.

Synset Examples

the deed was attested by the subscription of his signature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written in a distinctive way as identification

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write underneath, sign one's name) via Late Latin subscriptionem.
