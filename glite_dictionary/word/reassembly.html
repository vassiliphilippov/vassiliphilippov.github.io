reassembly

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈsɛm.bli / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
the act of assembling something again after it has been taken apart
putting something back together

Examples

The reassembly of the engine took several hours and required careful attention to detail.
After the renovation, the reassembly of the furniture was the last step.
Proper labeling during disassembly made the reassembly process much easier.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: assembly - the act of putting parts together to make a whole

Etymology

From re- (again) + assembly, ultimately from Old French assembler.
