eponymic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpəˈnɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpəˈnɪmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or bearing the name of an eponym; of or pertaining to a person after whom something is named.
relating to an eponym

Examples

The island's eponymic founder is celebrated in local legends.
Many medical terms are eponymic, named after the doctors who discovered them.
The school's eponymic tradition honors its original benefactor every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eponymous - giving one's name to something

Etymology

Formed from 'eponym' plus the adjectival suffix '-ic'. 'Eponym' is from Greek 'epōnymos', meaning 'giving a name'.
