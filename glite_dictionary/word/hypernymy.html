hypernymy

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɜrnɪmi / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜːnɪmi / (BrE)
the relationship between words in which the meaning of one word includes the meanings of other more specific words; being superordinate in a hierarchy of terms
linguistic relation: superordinate word relationship

Examples

The hypernymy relation links the category 'vehicle' to specific types like 'car' and 'bicycle'.
Understanding hypernymy can help students expand their vocabulary more efficiently.
In lexical databases, hypernymy is used to organize terms by generalization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyponymy - the semantic relation of being subordinate or more specific
  • Synonym: hypernym - a word with a broad meaning that more specific words fall under

Etymology

From 'hypernym' (coined from Greek 'hyper-' meaning 'over' or 'above' and '-onym' meaning 'name') + '-y' (forming nouns denoting states or conditions).
