blackwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈblæk.wʊd / (BrE)
Very dark, dense wood obtained from several tree species, often used for furniture and musical instruments.
dark, dense wood from various trees

Examples

The clarinet was made from high-quality blackwood prized for its tone.
He carved the sculpture out of a single piece of blackwood.
Many antique cabinets feature blackwood for its deep color and durability.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈblæk.wʊd / (BrE)
any of several hardwood trees that produce very dark-colored wood, often used for making furniture and musical instruments.
tree yielding dark-colored hardwood

Examples

Traditional clarinets are often made of blackwood due to its durable and resonant qualities.
The craftsman carefully selected a piece of blackwood for the intricate inlay work.
Several African and Australian trees are known as blackwood because of their deep-colored timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebony - hard, heavy, black wood from tropical trees
