to irrupt

1. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to burst in or intensify suddenly; to break or rush into a place or state abruptly and forcefully
to burst in or intensify suddenly

Examples

Protesters suddenly irrupted onto the stage, disrupting the award ceremony.
Tension irrupted between the rival groups after months of uneasy peace.
A sense of joy irrupted among the fans as their team scored the winning goal.

Synset Examples

Unrest erupted in the country
Tempers flared at the meeting
The crowd irrupted into a burst of patriotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst out or explode suddenly

Etymology

From Latin 'irruptus', past participle of 'irrumpere' (to break in), from 'in-' (into) + 'rumpere' (to break).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a population or group) to increase rapidly and in an uncontrolled or sudden manner, often after a period of stability.
increase rapidly or suddenly (esp. of populations)

Examples

After several years of scarcity, the lemming population suddenly irrupted across the tundra.
Invasive plants can irrupt when environmental conditions favor their rapid growth.
Ecologists observed that the locusts periodically irrupt, causing widespread crop damage.

Synset Examples

The population of India is exploding
The island's rodent population irrupted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst out or break forth suddenly, often used for volcanoes or violent outbursts

Etymology

From Latin irrumpere (to burst in), from in- (into) + rumpere (to break).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00157431-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to enter suddenly or forcibly, especially without invitation or welcome
enter suddenly and forcibly

Examples

The protesters irrupted into the meeting, demanding to be heard.
Without warning, the children irrupted into the quiet library.
A group of fans irrupted onto the field after the final whistle.

Synset Examples

They intruded on our dinner party
She irrupted into our sitting room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intrude - to enter without permission or invitation
  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst forth violently

Etymology

From Latin irruptus, past participle of irrumpere (to break in), from in- (in) + rumpere (to break).
