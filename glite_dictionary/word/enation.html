enation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a natural projection or outgrowth from the surface of a plant organ, such as a small leaflike appendage
outgrowth on a plant body
medical

Examples

Some ferns display small enations along the stem surface.
The botanist examined the unusual enation growing from the leaf margin.
Certain fossil plants show evidence of enations instead of true leaves.

Etymology

From Latin enatus (sprung forth, born), related to the verb nasci (to be born).
