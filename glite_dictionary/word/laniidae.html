Laniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæniˈɪdiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlæniˈɪdiːiː / (BrE)
a family of passerine birds known as shrikes, characterized by their hooked beaks and predatory habits
the shrike bird family

Examples

Members of the Laniidae family are famous for their habit of impaling prey on thorns.
Researchers studied migration patterns among several Laniidae species in Europe.
The hooked beaks of Laniidae birds help them catch and kill their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - any bird of the Laniidae family, known for impaling prey

Etymology

From modern Latin Laniidae, from Latin lania (butcher) + -idae (family suffix).
