recap

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈriːˌkæp / (BrE)
a brief summary or review of what has been discussed, often at the end of a meeting, broadcast, or report
a brief summary or review

Examples

At the end of the presentation, she gave a recap of all the major points discussed.
After a long meeting, the manager provided a quick recap for those who joined late.
The news program always ends with a recap of the day's top stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement or account giving the main points
  • Synonym: review - a formal assessment or summary of something

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'recapitulation', from Latin 'recapitulare' (to go over the headings again).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈriːˌkæp / (BrE)
a used automobile tire that has had new treads applied to extend its usable life
remolded automobile tire with new treads

Examples

After the blowout, he bought a recap to replace his damaged tire.
Many truckers use recaps to save money on tires.
The mechanic warned me that a recap may not last as long as a new tire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retread - a used tire with a new tread applied

Etymology

Shortened from 'recapitalize' or 'recapitulate', but in this sense, probably from 're-' (again) and 'cap' (cover/replace).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04091054-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈriˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈriː.kæp / (BrE)
to repeat or summarize the main points of something briefly
summarize briefly

Examples

At the end of the meeting, she recapped the key decisions for everyone's benefit.
Could you recap what happened in yesterday's episode?
Let me quickly recap the main findings of the report.

Synset Examples

Let's recapitulate the main ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - give a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: to recapitulate - state again the main points; summarize

Etymology

Shortened form of 'recapitulate,' from Latin recapitulare (to go through headings again).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01010930-v
Synset POS: v
