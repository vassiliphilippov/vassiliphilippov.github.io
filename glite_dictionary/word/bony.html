bony

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
composed of or containing bone; made from or consisting of bone
composed of or containing bone

Examples

The archaeologist examined the bony remains found at the site.
Fish often have a bony skeleton that helps support their bodies.
He noticed a bony structure protruding from the rock.

Synset Examples

osseous tissue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osseous - composed of or relating to bone

2. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) very thin, so that the bones can be seen easily under the skin.
very thin; bones showing

Examples

After months of illness, her bony frame was easily visible through her clothes.
The stray dog was so bony that you could count its ribs.
He held out his bony hand to greet me.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, often unattractively so
  • Synonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • Antonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993331-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
having many or prominent bones; thin or angular so that the bones are noticeable
having many or noticeable bones

Examples

She wrapped her fingers around his bony wrist and felt how thin he had become.
The stray dog looked weak and bony after weeks without proper food.
The grilled fish was delicious, but the fillet was rather bony.

Synset Examples

a bony shad fillet
her bony wrist
bony fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeletal - resembling or relating to a skeleton; very thin or emaciated
  • Antonym: fleshy - having much flesh; plump

Etymology

From bone +‎ -y, formed in Middle English.
