Episcia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪʃiə / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American herbs in the family Gesneriaceae, known for their soft, hairy leaves and sometimes colorful foliage.
genus of tropical American herbs

Examples

Episcia species are popular as ornamental houseplants due to their attractive leaves.
Botanists have discovered several new varieties within the Episcia genus.
The soft, velvety texture of Episcia leaves distinguishes them from other tropical plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'episkios' meaning 'shaded' or 'growing in shade'.
