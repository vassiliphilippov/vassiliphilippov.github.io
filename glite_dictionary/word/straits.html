straits

1. NOUN

/ streɪts / (AmE), / streɪts / (BrE)
a situation characterized by difficulty or distress, especially one that is serious or desperate.
a difficult or distressing situation

Examples

After losing his job, he found himself in financial straits.
The company is in dire straits after several unprofitable years.
They helped their neighbors when they were in desperate straits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - state of being difficult
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Antonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort

Etymology

From Middle English streit, from Old French estreit (narrow, tight place), from Latin strictus (drawn tight).
