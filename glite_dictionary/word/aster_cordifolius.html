Aster cordifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər kɔr.dɪˈfoʊ.li.əs / (AmE), / ˈæstə kɔː.dɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.əs / (BrE)
a perennial wood aster native to eastern North America, known for its heart-shaped leaves and pale blue or white flowers
a North American wood aster

Examples

Clusters of Aster cordifolius brighten woodland edges in late summer and early autumn.
The heart-shaped leaves make Aster cordifolius easy to distinguish from other species in the genus.
Gardeners appreciate Aster cordifolius for its resilience and delicate pale blue flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'aster' meaning 'star' (referring to the flower shape) and 'cordifolius' meaning 'heart-leaved'.
