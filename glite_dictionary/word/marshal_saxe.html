Marshal Saxe

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrʃəl sæks / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl saks / (BrE)
(1696–1750) A French marshal celebrated for his military achievements, particularly in the War of the Austrian Succession.
French marshal (1696–1750); military leader

Examples

Marshal Saxe is remembered as one of the most brilliant generals of 18th-century France.
During the War of the Austrian Succession, Marshal Saxe led French forces to several key victories.
Many military historians consider Marshal Saxe a master of battlefield strategy.

Etymology

Title 'Marshal' from Old French 'mareschal,' and 'Saxe' refers to his family origin from Saxony.
