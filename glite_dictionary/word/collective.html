collective

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
done by or characteristic of people acting together as a group; relating to or shared by all members of a group.
done by a group or shared by all

Examples

The company makes decisions by reaching a collective agreement among staff members.
Environmental protection is a collective responsibility that requires everyone's participation.
The community organized a collective effort to clean up the park after the festival.

Synset Examples

the collective mind
the corporate good

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing, not a group

Etymology

From Latin collectivus, from collectus (gathered together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02119981-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of an organization, activity, or resource) set up on the principle of collectivism, especially owned and operated by the workers involved, often under government supervision.
of or relating to collectivism or shared ownership

Examples

Many farmers worked on collective farms during the Soviet era.
The new government established several collective workshops in rural areas.
A collective enterprise often emphasizes cooperation rather than individual profit.

Synset Examples

collective farms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communal - shared by all members of a community; for common use
  • Antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing rather than a group

Etymology

From French *collectif*, from Latin *collectivus* (gathered together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00299159-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of persons or things) forming a whole or aggregate; done or shared by all members of a group.
forming a whole; shared by a group

Examples

The company makes collective decisions after consulting with all employees.
They achieved success through collective effort rather than individual actions.
Our collective responsibility is to protect the environment for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual - single; separate from a group or collective
  • Synonym: communal - shared by all members of a community; for common use

Etymology

From Latin 'collectivus' (gathered together), from 'collectus', past participle of 'colligere' (to gather together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00468799-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
an organization or group in which people work together on shared projects, typically sharing ownership, responsibilities, and benefits
cooperative group or organization

Examples

The artist collective organized a public mural project in the city’s downtown area.
She joined a farming collective that shared equipment and resources among its members.
Many decisions within the worker collective are made through open discussions and consensus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - an organization owned and run jointly by its members

Etymology

From Latin collectivus (gathered together), from colligere (to gather together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08073353-n
Synset POS: n
