Cordova

1. PROPN

/ kɔrˈdoʊvə / (AmE), / kɔːˈdəʊvə / (BrE)
A city in central Argentina, known for its colonial architecture and as the site of a university founded in 1613.
city in central Argentina

Examples

The historic center of Cordova attracts thousands of tourists every year.
She went to study at the National University of Cordova, one of the oldest in South America.
During our trip through Argentina, we spent two days exploring Cordova and its surroundings.

Etymology

Spanish Córdoba, anglicized as Cordova.

2. PROPN

/ kɔːrˈdoʊvə / (AmE), / kɔːˈdəʊvə / (BrE)
A city in southern Spain, historically a center of Moorish culture and learning.
historic city in southern Spain

Examples

During the Middle Ages, Cordova was renowned for its libraries and universities.
The mosque-cathedral in Cordova is a testament to the city's rich Islamic heritage.
Many tourists visit Cordova to experience its unique blend of cultures and architecture.

Etymology

From Spanish "Córdoba"; anglicized form of the city's name.
