emulator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.jəˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who imitates or strives to equal or excel someone else's words or behavior
a person who imitates another

Examples

As a devoted emulator of her mentor, she always tried to match his eloquence and charm.
He became known as an emulator of famous leaders, adopting their speaking styles during debates.
The young artist was proud to be called an emulator, following in the footsteps of the masters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitator - a person who copies the behavior or actions of another
  • Antonym: innovator - a person who introduces new methods, ideas, or products

Etymology

From Latin 'aemulator' (rival, imitator), from 'aemulari' (to rival, emulate).
