resolved

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɑlvd / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
(of a problem, question, or mystery) explained, solved, or settled; no longer open or uncertain.
explained or settled; no longer open

Examples

After many years, the cold case was finally resolved by new forensic evidence.
The issue remained resolved after the two parties reached an agreement.
All customer complaints were resolved within 24 hours of being reported.

Synset Examples

mysteries solved and unsolved
problems resolved and unresolved

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unresolved - not explained, solved, or settled
  • Synonym: solved - successfully found an answer or explanation for

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'resolve', from Latin resolvere (to loosen, unfasten).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɑlvd / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
Determined to do something and not willing to change your mind.
determined; firmly decided

Examples

After months of uncertainty, she felt resolved to take on the new challenge.
He sounded resolved when he announced his plan to move abroad.
Despite many setbacks, the team remained resolved to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

she was firmly resolved to be a doctor
single-minded in his determination to stop smoking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determined - showing firmness and purpose; not easily persuaded to give up
  • Antonym: undecided - not having made a decision or resolution

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'resolve', from Latin 'resolvere' (to loosen, resolve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01998349-s
Synset POS: s
