logjam

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌdʒæm / (BrE)
a large pile of logs that blocks the flow of a river or stream
a mass of logs blocking a river

Examples

The heavy rain caused a logjam that blocked the river for days.
We had to call for help to clear the logjam downstream.
A massive logjam formed after the storm swept loose branches into the current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks a passage or flow
  • Synonym: blockage - something that stops movement through an area

Etymology

From 'log' (a cut piece of wood) + 'jam' (a blockage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌdʒæm / (BrE)
a situation in which progress is stopped by a serious blockage or obstacle, especially due to complicated or unusual activity
a blockage or impasse preventing progress

Examples

A logjam in budget negotiations delayed the start of the project.
The talks reached a logjam that took weeks to resolve.
Efforts to fix the system finally broke through the logjam.

Synset Examples

the legislation ran into a logjam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • Synonym: bottleneck - a point of congestion or blockage

Etymology

Originally referred to an actual obstruction in floating logs on a river.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14039067-n
Synset POS: n
