to behave

1. VERB

/ bɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to conduct oneself in a particular way, especially in relation to others or when responding to a situation
conduct oneself in a particular way

Examples

Children are expected to behave politely in the classroom.
If you behave responsibly, you will gain people's respect.
The dog always behaves well when its owner is around.

Synset Examples

You should act like an adult
Don't behave like a fool
What makes her do this way?
The dog acts ferocious, but he is really afraid of people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - to behave in a particular way
  • Antonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly

Etymology

From Middle English 'behaven', from be- + 'have' (to have, to hold).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to act in a socially acceptable, proper, or polite manner
act in a proper or acceptable way

Examples

If you behave politely, people will respect you.
The teacher asked the students to behave during the field trip.
Many parents teach their children to behave at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

The children must learn to behave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly

Etymology

From Middle English, from be- (intensive prefix) + have; originally meaning 'to bear oneself in a certain way'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02525332-v
Synset POS: v
