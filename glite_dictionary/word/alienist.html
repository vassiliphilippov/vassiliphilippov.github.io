alienist

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪliənɪst / (AmE), / ˈeɪlɪənɪst / (BrE)
(historical) A psychiatrist, especially one who specializes in the legal aspects of mental illness and its effects on criminal responsibility.
psychiatrist dealing with legal aspects of mental illness
archaic legal

Examples

The defense called an alienist to determine the mental state of the accused.
In 19th-century courts, an alienist often evaluated whether a prisoner was fit to stand trial.
The novel features an alienist investigating a series of bizarre crimes in New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatrist - a medical doctor specializing in mental illness

Etymology

From French aliéniste, from aliéné (insane), ultimately from Latin alienare (to estrange).
