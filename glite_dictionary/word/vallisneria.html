Vallisneria

1. NOUN

/ vælɪsˈnɛriə / (AmE), / vælɪsˈnɪərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of submerged aquatic plants, commonly known as eelgrass, widely found in freshwater environments.
genus of freshwater aquatic plants

Examples

Many aquarists choose Vallisneria for their tanks because it is hardy and easy to grow.
Streams in the region are noted for dense beds of Vallisneria, which provide habitat for fish.
The long, ribbon-like leaves of Vallisneria make it an attractive option for planted aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - an aquatic plant of freshwater habitats

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Antonio Vallisneri (1661–1730).
