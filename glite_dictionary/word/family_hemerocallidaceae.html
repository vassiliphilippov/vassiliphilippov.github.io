family Hemerocallidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmərəˌkæləˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhɛmərəˌkælɪdəˈsiː / (BrE)
a subfamily or family in some plant classification systems, including the genus Hemerocallis; not widely accepted as separate from Liliaceae
plant family including Hemerocallis (daylilies)

Examples

The family Hemerocallidaceae is best known for the popular garden plant, the daylily.
Some botanists separate Hemerocallis into the family Hemerocallidaceae rather than including it in Liliaceae.
Modern classifications often do not recognize family Hemerocallidaceae as distinct from the lily family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hemerocallis - a genus of flowering plants known as daylilies
  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a broader plant family including lilies and their relatives

Etymology

Named from the genus Hemerocallis (from Greek 'hemera' meaning 'day' and 'kallos' meaning 'beauty') plus the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
