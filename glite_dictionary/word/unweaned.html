unweaned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwiːnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːnd / (BrE)
(of an infant or young animal) not yet accustomed to food other than its mother's milk.
not yet weaned from mother's milk

Examples

Some newborn animals remain unweaned for several months before transitioning to solid food.
The shelter was caring for several unweaned kittens found abandoned in a barn.
It is not unusual for children in some cultures to stay unweaned through their early toddler years.

Synset Examples

some children remain unweaned until their second or third birthdays

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weaned - accustomed to food other than mother's milk

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'weaned' (accustomed to food other than mother's milk).
