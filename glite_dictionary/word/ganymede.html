Ganymede

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡænəˌmid / (AmE), / ˈɡanɪmiːd / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a handsome Trojan youth who was carried off by Zeus to serve as the cupbearer to the gods.
Trojan youth in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient myths, Ganymede was taken to Olympus by Zeus in the form of an eagle.
The story of Ganymede has inspired countless works of art and literature through the centuries.
Shakespeare's play 'As You Like It' includes a character who adopts the name Ganymede.

Etymology

From Greek Ganymēdēs (Γανυμήδης), composed of ganu- ('joy, gladness') and -mede ('to think of').

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡænɪˌmid / (AmE), / ˈɡænɪˌmiːd / (BrE)
the largest moon of Jupiter, and the largest satellite in the solar system
the largest moon of Jupiter

Examples

Scientists have discovered water ice on Ganymede, Jupiter's largest moon.
The spacecraft sent back detailed images of Ganymede's surface features.
Many astronomers study Ganymede to learn about the moons of our solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Callisto - a large moon of Jupiter, second largest after Ganymede
  • Synonym: Europa - a large, icy moon of Jupiter
  • Synonym: Io - the innermost of the four largest moons of Jupiter

Etymology

From Greek mythology: Ganymede was a beautiful youth abducted by Zeus; the moon was named after him.
