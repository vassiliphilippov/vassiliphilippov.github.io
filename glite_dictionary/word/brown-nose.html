to brown-nose

1. VERB

/ ˈbraʊn ˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn ˌnəʊz / (BrE)
to flatter someone in a way that is insincere and excessive in order to gain favor or advantage, especially with people in authority
flatter to gain favor
informal slang american

Examples

Some employees will brown-nose their bosses to get promotions.
He was accused of brown-nosing the principal in hopes of being made head prefect.
No one likes it when someone brown-noses just to get ahead at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively or insincerely
  • Synonym: to suck up - to behave obsequiously to someone in authority
  • Antonym: snub - to ignore or treat someone with disdain; opposite of flattering for favor

Etymology

From the phrase 'brown nose', alluding to the effect of groveling or ingratiating oneself with someone in authority; first recorded mid-20th century in American English.

2. VERB

/ ˈbraʊnˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌnəʊz / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone in authority by acting in an excessively flattering or obsequious manner
flatter for personal advantage
informal slang american

Examples

I'm not going to brown-nose the manager just to get a promotion.
He keeps brown-nosing the boss in hopes of being noticed.
She brown-nosed her way to the top of the company.

Synset Examples

I can't stand that bumsucker who constantly brown-noses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise someone excessively, often insincerely
  • Synonym: to suck up - to try to gain favor by praising someone too much
  • Antonym: to criticize - to point out faults or shortcomings; opposite of flattering

Etymology

Originally American slang, possibly from the notion of getting one's nose dirty from 'kissing up'.
