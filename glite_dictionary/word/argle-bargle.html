argle-bargle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrɡl ˌbɑrɡl / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡl ˌbɑːɡl / (BrE)
Noisy, meaningless talk or a heated verbal dispute; a wrangling argument.
meaningless talk; wrangling.
informal british

Examples

After hours of argle-bargle, the committee still couldn’t reach a decision.
Their meeting was filled with pointless argle-bargle instead of productive discussion.
She grew tired of all the argle-bargle and left the room in frustration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - An exchange of diverging or opposite views, typically a heated one.
  • Synonym: wrangle - A long and complicated dispute or argument.
  • Antonym: agreement - Harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling.

Etymology

Reduplicated form of 'argle', a variant of 'argue', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡəl ˌbɑːrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡl ˌbɑːɡl / (BrE)
informal or nonsensical talk; meaningless chatter.
nonsense; meaningless talk
informal

Examples

The politician's speech was nothing but argle-bargle designed to confuse the audience.
After two hours of argle-bargle, the meeting ended with no decisions made.
He dismissed the rumors as mere argle-bargle and refused to comment further.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his argument as pure argle-bargle morble whoosh.
The meeting was filled with argle-bargle morble whoosh that went nowhere.
His explanation was just argle-bargle morble whoosh nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing
  • Antonym: sense - words or actions that are reasonable or have meaning

Etymology

Reduplication of 'argle,' a variant of 'argue,' possibly from Scottish dialect.
