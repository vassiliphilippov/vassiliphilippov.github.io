opposer

1. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person who actively resists or objects to something or someone
someone who resists or objects

Examples

The proposal was met with fierce criticism from every opposer at the meeting.
As a vocal opposer of the policy, she spoke out at every opportunity.
Several opposers formed a coalition to challenge the new legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opponent - a person who competes or fights against another in a contest, game, or argument
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports or champions something or someone

Etymology

From oppose (verb) + -er (agent noun suffix).
