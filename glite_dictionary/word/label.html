label

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a piece of paper, cloth, or other material that is attached to an object to give information about it
identifying tag attached to an object

Examples

Each bottle had a label with the product name and expiration date.
Please check the label for washing instructions before cleaning the shirt.
The price on the label was much lower than I expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tag - a small piece of material attached to something for identification

Etymology

From Old French 'label', meaning a strip of cloth or ribbon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪbəl / (BrE)
A radioactive isotope introduced into a compound to trace or study the mechanism of a chemical reaction.
radioactive tracer isotope
medical

Examples

The chemist added a radioactive label to the molecule to study its pathway.
Researchers tracked the movement of glucose in the body using a carbon-14 label.
By following the label, scientists identified each step of the reaction mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracer - a substance used to track a process, especially in chemistry or medicine
