Regulus calendula

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələs kəˈlɛndjələ / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləs kəˈlɛndjʊlə / (BrE)
A small North American songbird, the ruby-crowned kinglet, known for its vocal song and, in males, a distinctive red crown patch.
species of North American kinglet bird

Examples

The Regulus calendula is often spotted flitting energetically among the branches in North American forests.
Male Regulus calendula can be identified by their signature ruby-red crown patch.
During spring migration, birdwatchers listen eagerly for the melodic song of Regulus calendula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruby-crowned kinglet - Common name of Regulus calendula, small North American songbird

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Regulus' (meaning 'little king'), species name 'calendula.'
