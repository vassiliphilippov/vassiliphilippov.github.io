maidhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪd.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪd.hʊd / (BrE)
the period of a girl's childhood or maiden years, especially before adulthood
the childhood or maiden years of a girl
archaic

Examples

Her maidhood was spent in the quiet countryside, learning the ways of her family.
The tale speaks of a princess in her maidhood, untouched by the cares of the world.
Through poetry, she reminisced about her maidhood and the innocence she once had.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girlhood - the childhood or youth of a girl
  • Antonym: boyhood - the childhood of a boy

Etymology

Formed from 'maid' (girl, young woman) + '-hood' (state or condition).
