to mutate

1. VERB

/ ˈmjuːteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːteɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell, gene, organism, etc.) to undergo a change or alteration in the genetic material, usually resulting in a different trait.
undergo a genetic change

Examples

Over time, some viruses can mutate and become more resistant to treatment.
When exposed to radiation, the bacteria may mutate rapidly.
Scientists observed that the plant cells began to mutate after the experiment.

Synset Examples

cells mutate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evolve - to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form
  • Antonym: to remain - to stay the same without change

Etymology

From Latin mutatus, past participle of mutare (to change).
