whitefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
any of various edible freshwater or saltwater fish with white flesh, especially excluding herring; commonly used as a market term for such fish.
edible fish with white flesh, not including herring

Examples

The chef recommended a dish made with fresh whitefish caught that morning.
Many restaurants serve smoked whitefish as a local delicacy.
He bought several pounds of whitefish for the family barbecue.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a silvery freshwater fish found in cold lakes of the Northern Hemisphere, valued as food
a silvery freshwater food fish

Examples

The chef prepared grilled whitefish with a lemon butter sauce for dinner.
Many anglers travel north each year to catch whitefish during the winter season.
Smoked whitefish is a delicacy often served at holiday gatherings.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.fɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of various salmon-like or trout-like cold-water fish found in lakes of the Northern Hemisphere, used as food.
flesh of cold-water fish (food)

Examples

The local market always has fresh whitefish available for sale.
She prepared a delicious meal using smoked whitefish and fresh herbs.
Many traditional recipes from the region call for salted whitefish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - flesh of trout, a freshwater fish, used as food
  • Synonym: salmon - flesh of a large migratory fish, widely eaten as food
