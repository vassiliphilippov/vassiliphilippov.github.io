haemogenesis

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / hiːˌməʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of blood cells within the living body, especially in the bone marrow
formation of blood cells
medical british

Examples

The process of haemogenesis is crucial for maintaining healthy levels of blood cells.
Disorders affecting haemogenesis can lead to anemia or other blood-related diseases.
Research on haemogenesis has helped advance treatments for leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoiesis - the formation of blood cellular components

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -genesis (origin, formation).
