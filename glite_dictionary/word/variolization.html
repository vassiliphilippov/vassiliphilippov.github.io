variolization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌværiələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the obsolete medical practice of inoculating a person with material from a smallpox patient to induce immunity to smallpox
historical method of smallpox inoculation
medical archaic

Examples

Variolization was widely practiced before the development of modern smallpox vaccines.
The process of variolization often carried significant risks, including the spread of the disease.
Historical records describe how variolization helped reduce smallpox mortality rates in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inoculation - the act of introducing a vaccine or pathogen into the body to produce immunity
  • Degree Higher: vaccination - the process of administering a vaccine to protect against disease

Etymology

From New Latin 'variola' (smallpox) + English '-ization'.
