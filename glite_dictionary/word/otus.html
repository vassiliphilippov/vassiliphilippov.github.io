Otus

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Strigidae, containing the scops owls.
genus of scops owls

Examples

The Eurasian scops owl belongs to the genus Otus and is known for its distinctive calls.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Otus genus on the island.
Many Otus species have excellent camouflage, blending into tree bark during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scops owl - small eared owl of the genus Otus

Etymology

From Latin 'otus', meaning 'eared owl', referring to their prominent ear tufts.
