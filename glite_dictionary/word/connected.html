connected

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of an electrical device or appliance) joined to a power source or network so as to function properly.
joined to a power source or network

Examples

Always make sure the computer is connected before turning it on.
The lamp won't work unless it is properly connected to the power outlet.
Several devices can be connected to the same Wi-Fi network at once.

Synset Examples

first check to see whether the appliance is connected

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disconnected - not joined to a power source or network

Etymology

Past participle of 'connect,' from Latin 'connectere' (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01655788-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
in a close relationship or association with another person, group, or organization
joined in close association

Examples

She is connected to several professional organizations through her employer.
The college is connected with a local hospital for student internships.
Many of the clubs are connected and work together on community projects.

Synset Examples

all art schools whether independent or attached to universities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affiliated - officially attached or associated with an organization
  • Synonym: associated - closely joined or connected with another

3. ADJ

/ kəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a device or system) linked to, operated by, or in direct communication with a central computer or network.
linked to a central computer or network

Examples

All the machines in the office are now connected to the central server.
With a connected home, you can control your lights remotely using an app.
The new printer is connected to the network, so everyone can use it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disconnected - not joined to a central computer or network
Source: WordNet

/ kəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
joined or linked together; having a relationship or association
joined or linked together

Examples

The two computers were connected by a high-speed cable for fast data transfer.
She felt connected to her community through volunteering and shared activities.
All the rooms in the new office are connected by a central hallway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disconnected - not joined or linked; having no connection

5. ADJ

/ kəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of devices or systems) joined or linked electronically, especially by a physical wire or circuit for the purpose of communication or control, such as in an alarm system.
joined by wires or circuits (esp. alarms)

Examples

Make sure all the window sensors are connected to the central alarm system.
If the cameras aren't connected, the security system won't function properly.
Each smoke detector is connected to the main fire alarm panel for immediate alerts.

Synset Examples

all the window alarms are connected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wired - connected using wire, especially for electrical or communication purposes

Etymology

From Latin 'connectere' meaning 'to join together'.
