imagery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (BrE)
the ability to form mental pictures or images in the mind
mental image-forming ability

Examples

Athletes often use imagery techniques to improve their performance.
Her vivid imagery helped her recall every detail of the memory.
Guided imagery sessions can reduce stress and promote relaxation.

Synset Examples

he could still hear her in his imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images not present to the senses

Etymology

From Old French *imagerie* (imitation of images, imagery), from *image* (image, likeness).
