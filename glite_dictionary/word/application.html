application

1. NOUN

/ ˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting something onto a surface or spreading a substance over an area
the act of applying something

Examples

The doctor instructed a daily application of the cream to the affected area.
After a fresh application of paint, the walls looked brand new.
The gardener recommended the regular application of fertilizer for healthier plants.

Synset Examples

the doctor prescribed a topical application of iodine
a complete bleach requires several applications
the surface was ready for a coating of paint

Related Terms

  • Antonym: application - a formal request for something, such as a job or admission

Etymology

From Latin applicatio (an attaching, joining).
