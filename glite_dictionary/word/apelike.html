apelike

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪpˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈeɪp.laɪk / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of an ape, especially in appearance or behavior.
resembling an ape

Examples

His apelike features made him stand out in the group.
The actor moved with an apelike gait to portray the creature.
Children sometimes display apelike curiosity when exploring new things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simian - relating to, or resembling apes or monkeys

Etymology

Formed from 'ape' + 'like', meaning like an ape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01263354-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪpˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈeɪplaɪk / (BrE)
Imitating others in a servile or unoriginal manner; resembling the behavior of an ape.
imitative or resembling ape behavior

Examples

Her apelike copying of every trend made her seem unoriginal.
The student’s apelike mimicry of the teacher amused the whole class.
He adopted an apelike manner, echoing his boss’s words and movements precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - tending to copy others; not original

Etymology

Formed from 'ape' and the suffix '-like', indicating resemblance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01219381-s
Synset POS: s
