peripatus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪpətəs / (AmE), / pəˈrɪpətəs / (BrE)
a tropical, velvety-skinned, wormlike animal with characteristics of both arthropods and annelid worms, found in moist forest environments
a tropical wormlike animal with arthropod and annelid features

Examples

Scientists discovered a rare peripatus crawling under a rotting log in the rainforest.
The unique structure of the peripatus has fascinated biologists for generations.
Many species of peripatus are highly sensitive to changes in humidity and temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onychophoran - member of the phylum Onychophora, to which peripatus belongs

Etymology

From Modern Latin Peripatus, genus name, from Greek peripatein (to walk about).
