Peripatus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪpətəs / (AmE), / pəˈrɪpətəs / (BrE)
A genus of velvet worms, representing the type genus of the family Peripatidae, found mainly in tropical regions of the New World.
type genus of velvet worms (Peripatidae)

Examples

Specimens of Peripatus demonstrate unique characteristics that fascinate evolutionary biologists.
Some researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Peripatus in the Amazon rainforest.
The velvet worm Peripatus is often used as an example of a living fossil in zoological studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onycophoran - worm-like invertebrate of the phylum Onychophora

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'patein' (to walk), referring to its mode of movement.
