accrued

1. ADJ

/ əˈkruːd / (AmE), / əˈkruːd / (BrE)
(of money or benefits) accumulated or received in regular or increasing amounts over time, especially as a result of being earned or due.
accumulated over time, usually as a result of being earned or due

Examples

The company paid accrued interest to its bondholders at the end of the year.
She decided to take a vacation using her accrued leave.
He received a bonus based on the accrued profits of the last quarter.

Synset Examples

accrued interest
accrued leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulated - gathered or collected over time; amassed

Etymology

From Old French *acreue* (increase), from the verb *acreistre*, from Latin *crescere* (to grow).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkruːd / (AmE), / əˈkruːd / (BrE)
(of a sum of money or benefit) accumulated or received gradually over a period of time.
accumulated over time

Examples

The company reported $5,000 of accrued interest on its financial statement.
She took three weeks of accrued vacation last year.
Your accrued benefits will be available when you retire.

Synset Examples

accrued interest
accrued leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - gather together or amass over time

Etymology

From the verb 'accrue', from Old French 'accrue' (past participle of 'acreistre'), from Latin 'accrescere' (to increase).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00883689-s
Synset POS: s
