Senecio cruentus

1. NOUN

/ səˈniː.si.oʊ kruˈɛn.təs / (AmE), / sɪˈniː.si.əʊ kruˈen.təs / (BrE)
a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands, widely grown for its brightly colored, daisylike flowers
ornamental flowering plant of the Canary Islands

Examples

The vibrant colors of the Senecio cruentus make it a favorite among gardeners.
She planted Senecio cruentus in the front yard to add splashes of red and purple.
Botanists often study Senecio cruentus for its unusual flower patterns.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senecio' (genus name meaning 'old man') and 'cruentus' (bloody, blood-red), referring to some flower color varieties.
