infeasibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌfiːzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌfiːzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible or impractical to do or achieve
impossibility of being done

Examples

The project's infeasibility became apparent after several failed attempts to launch it.
Due to financial constraints, the engineers concluded the design's infeasibility.
She argued convincingly about the infeasibility of implementing the proposed plan within the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feasibility - the quality or state of being possible to do

Etymology

From infeasible (from French infaisable, from in- 'not' + faisable 'feasible'), plus -ity, a noun-forming suffix from Latin.
