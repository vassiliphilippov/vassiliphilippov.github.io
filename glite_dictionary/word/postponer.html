postponer

1. NOUN

/ poʊstˈpoʊnər / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpəʊnə / (BrE)
a person who postpones work or actions, especially out of laziness or habitual carelessness
one who postpones tasks or actions

Examples

As a chronic postponer, she always finished assignments at the last minute.
Being a postponer can lead to missed opportunities and unnecessary stress.
The manager grew frustrated with the team's postponer, whose delays affected everyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinator - a person who habitually delays or postpones tasks

Etymology

Formed from 'postpone' + '-er', following standard agent noun construction in English.
