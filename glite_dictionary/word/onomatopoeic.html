onomatopoeic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiːɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnəməˌtəʊˈpiːɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) having the property of sounding like the thing it describes; relating to or characterized by onomatopoeia.
relating to words that imitate sounds

Examples

The word "buzz" is an onomatopoeic word because it imitates the sound of a bee.
Many comic books use onomatopoeic expressions like "bang" and "crash" to convey sounds visually.
Children often enjoy the onomatopoeic quality of words like "splash" and "plop" when learning language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomatopoetic - relating to onomatopoeia

Etymology

Formed from 'onomatopoeia', from Greek 'onomatopoiia': 'onoma' (name) + 'poiein' (to make).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) resembling or imitating the sound associated with the object or action it refers to.
imitating natural sounds (of words)

Examples

Many animal names, such as "cuckoo," are onomatopoeic because they mimic the sound the animal makes.
The word "buzz" is onomatopoeic, as it sounds like what it describes.
Children often enjoy using onomatopoeic words when learning to speak.

Synset Examples

onomatopoeic words are imitative of noises
it was independently developed in more than one place as an onomatopoetic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - (of a word) formed by imitation of a sound or action
  • Antonym: descriptive - (of a word) serving to describe rather than imitate a sound

Etymology

From Greek onomatopoiia (making of a name or word), from onoma (name) + poiein (to make).

Source: WordNet
