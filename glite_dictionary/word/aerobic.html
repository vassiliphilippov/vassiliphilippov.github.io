aerobic

1. ADJ

/ ɛˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / ɛəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
needing or using free oxygen for life or activity, especially referring to organisms or processes
requiring oxygen; related to oxygen use

Examples

Aerobic bacteria need oxygen to survive and multiply.
Regular aerobic exercise improves heart and lung function.
The industry uses aerobic fermentation to produce certain foods and medicines.

Synset Examples

aerobic fermentation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaerobic - not needing or relating to the absence of oxygen

Etymology

From French 'aérobie', from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'bios' (life).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069175-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɛˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / eəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
relating to or requiring oxygen, especially referring to exercise that improves the efficiency of the cardiovascular and respiratory systems.
requiring oxygen; related to exercise or respiration

Examples

She attends an aerobic class at the gym every morning.
Swimming is considered an excellent aerobic activity for all ages.
The doctor recommended more aerobic exercise to improve heart health.

Synset Examples

aerobic dance
running is very aerobic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaerobic - not requiring oxygen; referring to exercise or processes occurring without air

Etymology

From French 'aérobie', from Greek 'aēr' (air) and 'bios' (life).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069826-a
Synset POS: a
