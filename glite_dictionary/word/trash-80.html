Trash-80

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræʃ ˈeɪti / (AmE), / ˈtræʃ ˈeɪti / (BrE)
a colloquial name for the TRS-80, an early personal computer introduced by Tandy/RadioShack in the late 1970s
nickname for early TRS-80 computer
informal

Examples

Many programmers got their start coding on a Trash-80 in the 1980s.
His attic still has a working Trash-80 next to a stack of floppy disks.
Despite its nickname, the Trash-80 was groundbreaking for home computing at the time.

Synset Examples

We learned BASIC programming on the old Trash-80.
The Trash-80 was a classic computer from the early 80s.
He proudly displayed his vintage Trash-80 on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TRS-80 - an early personal computer made by Tandy/RadioShack

Etymology

A humorous alteration of 'TRS-80', with 'TRS' pronounced to sound like 'Trash'.
