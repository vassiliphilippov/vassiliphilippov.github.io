physiological

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the normal functions and processes of a living organism or its parts.
relating to normal biological functions

Examples

Regular exercise helps maintain physiological balance in the body.
The study focused on the physiological responses to stress in humans.
Her research examines the physiological effects of sleep deprivation.

Synset Examples

physiologic functions
physiological processes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pathological - relating to disease or abnormal processes

Etymology

From Greek *physiologia* (study of nature, natural science), from *physis* (nature) + *-logia* (study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts.
relating to the functions of living organisms

Examples

The doctor explained the physiological effects of exercise on the body.
Stress can cause physiological changes such as increased heart rate and blood pressure.
Her research focuses on the physiological processes involved in human digestion.

Synset Examples

physiological psychology
Pavlov's physiological theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological - relating to biology or living organisms
  • Antonym: psychological - relating to the mind or mental processes

Etymology

From Latin 'physiologicus' (relating to natural science), from Greek 'physiologia' (natural science).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02960419-a
Synset POS: a
