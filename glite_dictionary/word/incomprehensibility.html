incomprehensibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkɑmprɪˌhɛnsɪˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɒmprɪˌhɛnsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible or extremely difficult to understand
quality of being hard to understand

Examples

The incomprehensibility of the technical jargon made the manual useless to most readers.
Some literary works are famous for their deliberate incomprehensibility and complexity.
He criticized the incomprehensibility of the new tax regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible or very difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin incomprehensibilis (not able to be understood) + English suffix -ity.
