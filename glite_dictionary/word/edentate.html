edentate

1. ADJ

/ iˈdɛnteɪt / (AmE), / iˈdɛnteɪt / (BrE)
having few or no teeth; lacking most or all teeth.
lacking teeth

Examples

The anteater is an edentate mammal, feeding primarily on ants and termites.
Some elderly animals become edentate as they age, losing most of their teeth.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of edentate creatures in the wild.

Synset Examples

anteaters are edentate animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothless - lacking teeth

Etymology

From Latin edentatus, from e- (out, without) + dens (tooth).

2. NOUN

/ iˈdɛnˌteɪt / (AmE), / iːˈdɛn.teɪt / (BrE)
a primitive mammal with few or no teeth, formerly classified in the order Edentata, native to Central and South America, such as armadillos, anteaters, and sloths
a mammal with few or no teeth (e.g., anteaters, sloths)

Examples

The giant anteater is an edentate native to Central and South America.
Early naturalists classified armadillos as edentates due to their reduced teeth.
Despite being called edentates, some species like armadillos still have simple, peg-like teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothless - lacking teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'edentatus' (toothless), from 'e-' (without) + 'dens' (tooth).

Synset POS: n
