forcefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrsfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsfəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being full of physical energy, intensity, or strength.
physical energy or intensity

Examples

The forcefulness of his argument convinced everyone in the room.
You could feel the forcefulness of the wind as it shook the windows.
Her speech was marked by a forcefulness that left a strong impression.

Synset Examples

he hit with all the force he could muster
it was destroyed by the strength of the gale
a government has not the vitality and forcefulness of a living man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Antonym: weakness - the state of lacking strength or force

Etymology

From 'forceful' + '-ness'; 'forceful' from 'force' (via Old French from Latin 'fortis', strong).
