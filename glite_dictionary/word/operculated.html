operculated

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈpɜːr.kjə.leɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əʊˈpɜː.kjʊ.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having an operculum; possessing a lid or covering structure, especially in reference to animals or plants.
having an operculum (lid or covering)

Examples

Many freshwater snails are operculated, meaning they can seal their shells with a hard lid.
The operculated gills of these fish provide extra protection from debris and parasites.
Botanists identified the seed capsule as operculated due to its distinctive lid-like cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operculum - a lid or covering structure in animals and plants

Etymology

From Latin 'operculatus', past participle of 'operculare' (to cover with a lid), from 'operculum' (lid, cover).
