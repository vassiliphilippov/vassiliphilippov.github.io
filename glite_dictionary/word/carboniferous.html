carboniferous

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈbɑnəˌfɛrəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒnɪfərəs / (BrE)
relating to or containing carbon, or capable of producing or yielding carbon, especially as in coal-forming processes.
containing or yielding carbon

Examples

The region is carboniferous and known for its rich coal deposits.
Scientists analyzed carboniferous rocks to study ancient life forms.
The carboniferous content of the material makes it valuable for energy production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonaceous - containing or consisting of carbon

Etymology

From Latin 'carbonifer' (carbon-bearing), from 'carbo' (coal, carbon) and 'ferre' (to bear).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːrbəˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənˈɪfərəs / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Carboniferous period, a geologic era known for its extensive coal-forming forests and deposits
of the Carboniferous geologic period

Examples

The carboniferous limestone in this region is rich in fossils.
Geologists study carboniferous strata to understand Earth's ancient environments.
Coal deposits found in carboniferous rocks fueled the Industrial Revolution.

Synset Examples

carboniferous rock system

Etymology

From Latin 'carbo' (coal) + '-iferous' (bearing), referencing the coal deposits of the era.
