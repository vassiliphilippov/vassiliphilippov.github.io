to declare

1. VERB

/ dɪˈklɛr / (AmE), / dɪˈkleə / (BrE)
(in card games) to name the trump suit or announce no-trump with the final bid of a hand.
announce trump or no-trump in card games

Examples

After much bidding, she finally declared hearts as the trump suit.
The player in the north seat declares no-trump for the current hand.
If you declare spades, your partner must adjust their strategy.

Etymology

From Latin 'declarare', meaning to make clear, reveal, announce.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈklɛr / (AmE), / dɪˈkleə / (BrE)
(of an organization’s officials) to formally announce that a payment, especially a dividend, will be made to shareholders.
formally announce a payment (e.g., dividend)

Examples

The board voted to declare a dividend at the end of the quarter.
The company may declare special payments to reward loyal shareholders.
After a profitable year, the management decided to declare a bonus for employees.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin declarare (to make clear, declare), from de- (thoroughly) + clarare (to make clear).
