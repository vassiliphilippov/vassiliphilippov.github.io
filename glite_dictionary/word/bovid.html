bovid

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊ.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.vɪd / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the genus Bos (cattle) or the family Bovidae, which includes cattle, bison, buffalo, and antelopes
relating to cattle or the Bovidae family

Examples

The bovid skull displayed in the museum comes from an ancient species of wild cattle.
Researchers compared DNA samples from several bovid populations across Africa.
Many bovid animals, such as antelopes and buffalo, play important roles in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle - domesticated bovine animals

Etymology

From Latin 'bos' (ox, cow) + '-id' (relating to), used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.vɪd / (BrE)
a mammal of the family Bovidae, which includes cattle, sheep, goats, antelopes, and similar hollow-horned ruminants
member of the Bovidae family (hollow-horned ruminant)

Examples

The African savannah is home to many species of bovid, including antelopes and buffalo.
Scientists discovered a fossilized skull belonging to a prehistoric bovid in the cave.
Domesticated cattle are the most widely known bovid animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruminant - an animal that chews cud, such as a cow or sheep

Etymology

From modern Latin Bovidae (the animal family name), from Latin bos, bov- (ox, cow).
