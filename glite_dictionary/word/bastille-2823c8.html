bastille

1. NOUN

/ bæˈstil / (AmE), / bæˈstiːl / (BrE)
a jail or prison, especially one operated in a harsh or tyrannical manner
a harsh or tyrannical prison

Examples

The political prisoners were locked away in the infamous bastille for years without trial.
He described the run-down facility as a modern-day bastille ruled by fear.
Many stories evoke the image of a bastille to symbolize oppressive government power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial
  • Synonym: jail - a place of confinement for people accused or convicted of a crime

Etymology

From French 'Bastille', originally the name of a fortress-prison in Paris, destroyed during the French Revolution.
