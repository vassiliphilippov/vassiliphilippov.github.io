Lepisosteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈsɑstiəs / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈsɒstiəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fishes known as gars, native to North and Central America, distinguished by their elongated bodies and long jaws filled with sharp teeth.
genus of freshwater gars

Examples

The fossil record shows that Lepisosteus existed millions of years ago in North America.
Scientists captured several specimens of Lepisosteus for their study on freshwater biodiversity.
With its armored scales, a Lepisosteus can fend off many potential predators in its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gar - a long-bodied freshwater fish with sharp teeth, of the family Lepisosteidae

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) + osteon (bone), referring to the bony scales of these fishes.
