phenol

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnɔl / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɒl / (BrE)
a toxic white crystalline chemical compound, C6H5OH, derived from benzene and used in industry, as a disinfectant, and in medical applications
toxic white crystalline compound, C6H5OH
medical

Examples

Phenol is often used in the production of plastics and resins.
Because of its toxicity, phenol must be handled with great care in the laboratory.
The doctor applied a diluted solution of phenol as a disinfectant to the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbolic acid - another name for phenol, especially as a disinfectant
  • Degree Lower: benzene - the aromatic compound from which phenol is derived

Etymology

From French phénol, from phène (from Greek phaino meaning ‘to show’) + -ol (indicating an alcohol).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnɔl, ˈfiːnɑl / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɒl / (BrE)
a weakly acidic organic compound consisting of a hydroxyl group attached directly to an aromatic hydrocarbon ring, or any compound in a class of substances with this structure
weakly acidic organic compound (aromatic with hydroxyl group)
medical

Examples

Phenol is commonly used as a disinfectant in hospitals due to its antibacterial properties.
Chemists often study the reactivity of substituted phenols in organic synthesis.
Direct exposure to concentrated phenol can cause burns on the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbolic acid - another name for phenol, especially as a disinfectant

Etymology

From French 'phénol', from Greek 'phaino-' (shining) + '-ol' (alcohol).
