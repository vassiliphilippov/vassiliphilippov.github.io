dicotyledonous

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.kɑː.tɪˈliː.dən.əs / (AmE), / ˌdɪ.kɒ.tɪˈliː.dən.əs / (BrE)
(of a flowering plant) having two cotyledons, or seed leaves, in the seed; relating to or characteristic of dicotyledons.
having two seed leaves in the seed (botany)
medical

Examples

Many trees and shrubs are dicotyledonous plants, characterized by broad leaves.
The dicotyledonous embryo contains two seed leaves, unlike monocot plants.
Botanists classify beans as dicotyledonous because of their paired cotyledons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocotyledonous - (of a plant) having one cotyledon in the seed

Etymology

From Greek dis (twice) + kotyledon (seed leaf).
