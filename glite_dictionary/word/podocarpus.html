Podocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees and shrubs, sometimes classified in the family Taxaceae, native to warm regions of the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

The Podocarpus in the garden remains lush and green throughout the year.
Many botanical gardens feature specimens of Podocarpus due to their ornamental foliage.
The wood of the Podocarpus is valued for carpentry and furniture making.

Etymology

From Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the stalked fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊdəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees and shrubs, native mainly to the Southern Hemisphere, sometimes placed in the yew family (Taxaceae)
a genus of evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

The Podocarpus genus includes several ornamental trees used for landscaping.
Many species of Podocarpus are valued for their attractive foliage and timber.
Botanists recently discovered a new Podocarpus species in the mountains of New Guinea.

Etymology

From Greek 'podos' (foot) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the stalked fruit.
