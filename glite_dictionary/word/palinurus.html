Palinurus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪnjʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈnjʊərəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Palinuridae, comprising certain spiny lobsters.
type genus of spiny lobsters

Examples

The genus Palinurus includes several species of spiny lobsters found in European waters.
Marine biologists study Palinurus to understand the evolutionary relationships within lobsters.
Specimens of Palinurus are often displayed in natural history museums.

Etymology

From Latin 'Palinurus', the name of Aeneas's helmsman in Roman mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpælɪˈnʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈnjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of spiny lobsters in the family Palinuridae, found in the eastern Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea.
genus of spiny lobsters

Examples

The Palinurus lobsters are prized for their delicious meat and vibrant coloration.
Several species in the genus Palinurus inhabit rocky reefs along Mediterranean coasts.
Researchers studied the population decline of Palinurus in the eastern Atlantic region.

Etymology

From Latin 'Palinurus', a proper name; in mythology, helmsman of Aeneas.
