slam

1. NOUN

/ slæm / (AmE), / slæm / (BrE)
A loud noise produced by the forceful impact or shutting of two objects, especially a door or lid.
loud impact noise (e.g. shut door)

Examples

The slam of the door echoed through the empty hallway.
She winced at the sudden slam of the heavy book hitting the table.
The car door closed with a loud slam in the quiet night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise; a similar impact sound

4. NOUN

/ slæm / (AmE), / slæm / (BrE)
In bridge and some other card games, a bid or contract to win all or all but one of the tricks in a hand, or the successful accomplishment of such a contract.
winning nearly all tricks in bridge

Examples

They made a slam in last night's bridge game, astonishing their opponents.
Bidding a slam requires confidence in your partner's hand.
Pulling off a slam can be the highlight of a competitive bridge tournament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grand slam - a contract to win all thirteen tricks in bridge
  • Degree Lower: small slam - a contract to win twelve tricks in bridge

Etymology

Possibly influenced by the sense of 'slam' meaning 'to strike forcefully', referring to winning decisively.

Source: WordNet
