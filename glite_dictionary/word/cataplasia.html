cataplasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈpleɪʒə / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
degenerative reversion of cells or tissue to a less differentiated or more primitive form
degenerative reversion of cells/tissue
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed cataplasia in the affected tissue, indicating a return to a more primitive cell type.
Researchers are studying how cataplasia contributes to the progression of certain degenerative diseases.
Chronic inflammation can sometimes trigger cataplasia in previously healthy tissue.

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down, against) + plasis (formation).
