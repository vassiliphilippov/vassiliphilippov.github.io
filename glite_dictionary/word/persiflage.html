persiflage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsəˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.sɪˈflɑːʒ / (BrE)
light and slightly mocking conversation; banter or mild teasing
light teasing or banter

Examples

The persiflage among the friends at dinner kept everyone entertained.
His easy persiflage made the tense meeting feel more relaxed.
Their conversation was full of light persiflage and laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banter - playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks
  • Degree Higher: mockery - teasing and contemptuous language or behavior directed at a particular person or thing

Etymology

From French 'persiflage', from 'persifler' (to banter).
