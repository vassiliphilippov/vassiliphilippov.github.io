anathematisation

1. NOUN

/ əˌnæθəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌnaθəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of declaring someone or something to be accursed or excommunicated, especially by a religious authority
formal act of declaring accursed

Examples

The priest's anathematisation of the heretics was met with shock by the congregation.
In medieval times, anathematisation could lead to social and political isolation.
The council issued a public anathematisation against the teachings deemed heretical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excommunication - official exclusion from participation in church rites
  • Antonym: blessing - formal act of declaring someone favored or holy; opposite meaning

Etymology

From Late Latin 'anathematizare', from 'anathema' (a curse).
