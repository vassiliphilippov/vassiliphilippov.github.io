noncivilised

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsɪv.ə.laɪzd / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪzd / (BrE)
Not having a high state of culture and social development; lacking civilization or refinement.
not having culture or social development

Examples

Some early explorers described remote tribes as noncivilised societies, though such views are now considered outdated.
The ancient ruins suggest that the people who built them were anything but noncivilised.
It is unfair and inaccurate to label cultures as noncivilised simply because they are different.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncivilized - lacking culture or refinement
  • Antonym: civilised - having an advanced state of human development

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'civilised', from Latin 'civilis' (relating to a citizen).
