interbred

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈbrɛd / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) bred from closely related parents, typically within the same family or group.
bred from closely related parents

Examples

The researcher observed that the population was largely interbred due to isolation.
Some species have become endangered because their interbred status leads to genetic weaknesses.
The zoo monitors the genetics of its animals to avoid interbred offspring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inbred - bred from closely related individuals

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'inter-' and past participle of 'breed'.
