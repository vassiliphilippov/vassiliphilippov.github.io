inebriate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈniː.bri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniː.bri.eɪt / (BrE)
to make someone drunk; to intoxicate with alcohol.
make drunk; intoxicate with alcohol

Examples

The bartender refused to inebriate the already drunken guest any further.
Celebrations that inebriate the senses are common during the festival.
It was considered irresponsible to inebriate oneself before an important meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intoxicate - to make someone drunk or affected by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin inebriare (to make drunk), from ebrius (drunk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01193027-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈniː.bri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniː.brɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to make drunk; to intoxicate, or to become drunk from drinking alcohol
to make or become drunk

Examples

He managed to inebriate himself after only two drinks.
The celebration was meant to inebriate the participants with both joy and wine.
She warned him not to inebriate before the important meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intoxicate - to make someone drunk or affected by alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - to make or become not drunk

Etymology

From Latin inebriatus, past participle of inebriare, from in- (in, into) + ebrius (drunk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01192810-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈnibriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniːbrɪət / (BrE)
a person who habitually drinks alcohol to excess; a chronic drunkard
habitual excessive drinker

Examples

After years as an inebriate, he finally sought help for his addiction.
The shelter offers special support services for inebriates trying to get sober.
The novel's main character is a charming but hopeless inebriate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk

Etymology

From Latin inebriatus, past participle of inebriare (to make drunk).

/ ɪˈniː.brieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈniː.brɪeɪt / (BrE)
(literary) To fill (someone) with strong emotion or exhilaration.
to fill with strong emotion or exhilaration

Examples

She felt inebriated with joy after hearing the good news.
The audience was inebriated by the soaring music of the orchestra.
Success seemed to inebriate him, filling him with boundless energy and optimism.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
