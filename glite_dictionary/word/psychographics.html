psychographics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈɡræfɪks / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkəˈɡrafɪks / (BrE)
the study and classification of people according to their attitudes, values, beliefs, interests, and other psychological factors, especially as used in market research
classification by psychological traits (in market research)

Examples

The advertising campaign used psychographics to better understand their target audience's lifestyle and interests.
Companies often collect psychographics to segment their market by values rather than just age or location.
Studying psychographics can reveal why certain products appeal to specific consumer groups.

Synset Examples

Psychographics is often confused with demographics, where historical generations may be defined both by demographics, such as the years in which a particular generation is born or even the fertility rates of that generation's parents, but also by psychographic variables like attitudes, personality formation, and cultural touchstones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demographics - statistical data relating to the population and particular groups within it

Etymology

From psycho- (relating to the mind) + -graphics (study or representation), modeled after 'demographics'.
