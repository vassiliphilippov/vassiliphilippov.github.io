maleo

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
a large black-and-white bird native to Sulawesi, Indonesia, known for burying its eggs in sandy beaches to incubate them; a species of megapode (Macrocephalon maleo)
a large bird from Sulawesi, Indonesia

Examples

The maleo is critically endangered due to habitat loss and egg poaching.
We watched a maleo dig a hole in the sand to lay its eggs.
Conservationists are working to protect the nesting sites of the maleo in Sulawesi.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Macrocephalon maleo; 'maleo' is likely of local Indonesian origin.

2. NOUN

/ məˈleɪoʊ / (AmE), / məˈleɪəʊ / (BrE)
a large black and white bird (Macrocephalon maleo) native to Sulawesi, Indonesia, known for laying eggs in sandy beaches and using geothermal heat to incubate them
Indonesian bird that lays eggs in sand

Examples

The maleo lays its eggs in geothermal sand on Sulawesi's beaches.
Conservationists are working to protect the nesting grounds of the endangered maleo.
Unlike most birds, the maleo relies on the earth's heat to incubate its eggs.

Etymology

From Indonesian 'maleo', the native name for the species.
