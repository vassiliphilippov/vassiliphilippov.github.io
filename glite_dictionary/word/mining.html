mining

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of extracting minerals, such as coal or ores, from the earth
extraction of minerals from the earth

Examples

The expansion of mining operations has created many new jobs in the region.
Environmental groups are concerned about the impact of mining on local wildlife.
Coal mining was once the backbone of the town's economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrying - the extraction of stone from a quarry
  • Antonym: farming - the activity of growing crops or raising animals

Etymology

From the verb 'mine' (to extract minerals), ultimately from Old French 'miner'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing explosive mines in concealed locations to damage or destroy enemy personnel and equipment
planting explosive mines (military)

Examples

The army specialized in mining roads to halt advancing tanks.
Extensive mining around the border made crossing extremely dangerous for both sides.
International law now regulates the mining of civilian areas to protect non-combatants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an explosive device placed in a concealed position on land or at sea
  • Antonym: demining - the removal of explosive mines from an area
