velvetweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvətˌwid / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪtwiːd / (BrE)
A tall annual herb or subshrub from tropical Asia, with velvety leaves, yellow flowers, and strong fiber, now naturalized in southeastern Europe and the United States.
tall Asian herb with velvety leaves and yellow flowers

Examples

The strong fibers of velvetweed are sometimes used to make rope and cloth.
Gardeners in the southern United States often find velvetweed growing wild among their crops.
The leaves of velvetweed are soft and velvety to the touch.
