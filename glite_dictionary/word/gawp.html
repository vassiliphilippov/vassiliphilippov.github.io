to gawp

1. VERB

/ ɡɑp / (AmE), / ɡɔːp / (BrE)
to stare openly and often rudely or foolishly, usually in amazement or curiosity
stare openly, often rudely
british

Examples

Tourists would often gawp at the impressive cathedral as they passed by.
He couldn't help but gawp when he saw the magician pull a rabbit from the hat.
It's rude to gawp at people just because they're dressed differently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gape - stare with mouth open, usually in surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: to ogle - to stare at in a lecherous or overly interested manner
