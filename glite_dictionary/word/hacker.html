hacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæk.ər / (AmE), / ˈhæk.ə / (BrE)
a person who plays golf poorly or lacks skill at the game
an unskilled golfer

Examples

He's known as a hacker on the local golf course but enjoys every game.
Even the best courses won't help a hacker improve overnight.
As a self-admitted hacker, she keeps the game lighthearted and fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duffer - an unskilled or inexperienced golfer
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high level of skill or knowledge, especially in golf

2. NOUN

/ ˈhækər / (AmE), / ˈhækə / (BrE)
A person who enjoys exploring and writing computer programs, often finding creative or unconventional solutions and sometimes engaging in challenging activities like testing the limits of computer systems, usually without malicious intent.
enthusiastic computer programmer, not malicious

Examples

The hacker spent all night developing an elegant solution to the problem.
Many hackers enjoy learning new programming languages for the mental challenge.
At its core, being a hacker is about curiosity and creativity, not causing harm.

Synset Examples

true hackers subscribe to a code of ethics and look down upon crackers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cracker - a person who breaks into computer systems with malicious intent

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang in the 1960s at MIT, from 'hack' (to cut roughly, improvise).
