Artamidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtæmɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɑːˈtamɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as wood swallows, found mainly in Australasia and Southeast Asia.
family of birds (wood swallows)

Examples

The Artamidae are commonly known as wood swallows and are native to Australasia.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Artamidae.
Members of the Artamidae typically exhibit aerial feeding behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodswallow - a songbird of the family Artamidae

Etymology

From New Latin Artamidae, based on the genus Artamus plus the familial suffix -idae.
