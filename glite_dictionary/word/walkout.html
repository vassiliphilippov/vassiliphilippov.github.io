walkout

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk.aʊt / (BrE)
a sudden stoppage of work by employees as a protest, when they leave their workplace together
sudden strike by leaving workplace

Examples

The workers staged a walkout to demand better pay and safer conditions.
After negotiations failed, the union called for a walkout on Monday morning.
The sudden walkout left the factory completely shut down for two days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a refusal to work organized by employees as a form of protest

Etymology

From the verb 'walk' + 'out', originally American English in early 20th century labor contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkaʊt / (BrE)
an act of leaving a meeting or organization as a form of protest.
protest by leaving a meeting or group

Examples

The union staged a walkout after negotiations failed to reach an agreement.
A sudden walkout by several delegates disrupted the conference.
The workers organized a walkout in protest against wage cuts.

Synset Examples

there was a walkout by the Black members as the chairman rose to speak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a refusal to work organized as a form of protest

Etymology

Formed from 'walk' + 'out', referring to the act of walking out as protest; used since early 20th century.
