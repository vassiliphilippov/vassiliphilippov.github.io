flair

1. NOUN

/ flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
a natural ability to do something with distinctive and stylish elegance; a unique sense of style or sophistication
distinctive and stylish elegance

Examples

She decorated her apartment with a flair that impressed all her friends.
His writing has a flair that sets him apart from other journalists.
The chef prepared every dish with a flair for presentation.

Synset Examples

he wooed her with the confident dash of a cavalry officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panache - distinctive and stylish flair or confidence

Etymology

From French 'flair' (sense of smell, instinct, style), from Old French 'fler' (to smell).

2. NOUN

/ flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
a natural ability to do something well, especially in a way that is stylish or original
natural talent or stylish skill

Examples

She has a flair for languages and picks them up quickly.
His unique flair for design makes his work stand out in the industry.
You could see her artistic flair in the way she decorated her apartment.

Synset Examples

he has a flair for mathematics
he has a genius for interior decorating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: aptitude - natural ability to do something

Etymology

From Old French *flaire* (scent), from flairer (to smell), originally referring to a keen sense of smell; meaning evolved to denote instinctive ability or stylish aptitude.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
