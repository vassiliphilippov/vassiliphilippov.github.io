Xerox

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪrəks / (AmE), / ˈzɪərɒks / (BrE)
A trademarked brand of photocopier that makes copies of documents and images using a process involving light and an electrically charged surface.
trademarked brand of photocopier

Examples

The office just bought a new Xerox to handle the increased volume of copying.
She made a Xerox of the report before submitting the original.
The Xerox in the hallway broke down again this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopier - a machine that makes paper copies of documents and images

Etymology

From the trademark Xerox, coined in 1948 from Greek 'xeros' (dry) in reference to the dry copying process.
