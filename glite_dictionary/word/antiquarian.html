antiquarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈkwɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈkweə.ri.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or concerned with antiques or ancient objects.
relating to antiques or ancient objects

Examples

The museum displayed a collection of antiquarian books dating back to the 16th century.
She is known for her antiquarian interests and often visits historical sites.
An antiquarian study revealed the origins of the ancient coins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique - of or belonging to an earlier period; old and valuable

Etymology

From Latin antiquarius (pertaining to antiquity), from antiquus (ancient).

relating to antiques or antiquities

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique - an object having high value because of age

From Latin 'antiquarius' (pertaining to antiquity), from 'antiquus' (ancient).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈkwer.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈkweə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a person who studies, collects, or deals in antiques or ancient objects
person dealing with antiques or antiquities

Examples

The antiquarian spent years restoring medieval manuscripts in his private collection.
Many antiquarian societies preserve the history and artifacts of their regions.
As a noted antiquarian, she is often consulted by museums about ancient ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique dealer - a person who buys and sells antiques
  • Antonym: modernist - a person who favors modern ideas over traditional ones

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquarius' (pertaining to antiquity), from 'antiquus' (ancient).
