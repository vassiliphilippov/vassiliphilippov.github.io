Roystonea regia

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɔɪˈstoʊniə ˈriːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌrɔɪˈstəʊniə ˈriːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A tall feather palm native to southern Florida and Cuba, commonly known as the royal palm.
species of tall feather palm

Examples

The Roystonea regia lined the street, creating a grand tropical avenue.
Botanists were excited to discover a mature Roystonea regia in the wild.
The elegant crown of the Roystonea regia is a distinctive feature in Cuban landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal palm - common name for Roystonea regia, a tall palm species

Etymology

From Latinized genus Roystonea, named after Roy Stone (American engineer), and Latin regia meaning ‘royal’.
