inductive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
leading or tending to induce, influence, or incite someone to do something
tending to induce or influence
archaic

Examples

The preacher’s words were highly inductive to charitable action among the congregation.
A suspenseful plot can be inductive of curiosity in its readers.
Some music is inductive to dance, encouraging even the shyest people to move.

Synset Examples

inductive to the sin of Eve

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inductivus (inducing), from Latin inducere (to lead in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00324911-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
(of a property or phenomenon) arising from or caused by inductance, especially in electrical circuits.
arising from inductance (physics, engineering)

Examples

The circuit's inductive properties must be considered when designing filters.
An inductive reactance increases as the frequency of the alternating current rises.
In transformers, inductive coupling is an essential mechanism for energy transfer.

Synset Examples

inductive reactance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capacitive - arising from capacitance, as in capacitive reactance

Etymology

From Latin 'inductivus', from 'inducere' meaning 'to lead in'; sense in physics from 'inductance'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02759193-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
(of reasoning) involving the process of deriving general principles from specific observations or facts.
reasoning from specific cases to generalizations

Examples

Scientists often use inductive methods to develop theories from their observations.
His argument followed an inductive approach, starting with specific examples.
You need to distinguish between deductive and inductive reasoning when analyzing arguments.

Synset Examples

inductive reasoning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deductive - of reasoning; proceeding from general principles to specific cases

Etymology

From Latin 'inductivus', from 'inducere' (to lead in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01299391-a
Synset POS: a
