daftness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæftnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɑːftnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being silly, foolish, or lacking good sense; informally, madness or insanity.
silliness; foolishness; informally, insanity
informal

Examples

His daftness always makes the whole office laugh, even on tough days.
There was a certain daftness in his plan that made it unlikely to succeed.
Her spontaneous daftness is part of what makes her so endearing to her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madness - the state of being mentally ill or extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind; rationality
  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgement

Etymology

From 'daft' + '-ness'; 'daft' from Old English 'gedæfte' (gentle, meek, foolish).
