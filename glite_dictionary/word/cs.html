Cs

1. NOUN

/ siː ɛs / (AmE), / siː ɛs / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for cesium, a soft silver-white ductile metallic element that is liquid at normal temperatures and is the most electropositive and alkaline metal
chemical symbol for cesium (metallic element)

Examples

The element Cs is used in atomic clocks for its precise frequency standard.
A small amount of Cs can be dangerous due to its high reactivity with water.
Scientists detected traces of Cs in soil samples near the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesium - a soft silver-white ductile metallic element

Etymology

From the Latin 'caesius' (bluish gray), referencing the blue lines in its spectrum. 'Cs' is the standard chemical symbol.
