to unpaint

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈpeɪnt / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪnt / (BrE)
to remove paint from a surface, especially from a wall
remove paint from a surface

Examples

She decided to unpaint the old wooden door to restore its natural look.
Before you can apply the new color, you'll need to unpaint the entire wall.
The workers were hired to unpaint the fence in preparation for repairs.

Synset Examples

It's time to unpaint that wall and choose a new color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - to remove a covering or coating, such as paint, from a surface

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'paint', following an English word-formation pattern for reversal of action.
