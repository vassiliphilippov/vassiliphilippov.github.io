asyndetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæ.sɪnˈdɛ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪnˈdɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a phrase or sentence) lacking conjunctions where they would usually be expected, especially for rhetorical effect.
lacking conjunctions in a phrase

Examples

The author used asyndetic lists to create a fast, breathless pace in the novel.
Julius Caesar’s famous phrase, 'Veni, vidi, vici,' is asyndetic as it omits conjunctions.
In writing, asyndetic coordination can make descriptions more vivid and immediate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syndetic - containing conjunctions

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'syndetos' (bound together).
