Galloway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæləˌweɪ / (BrE)
a hardy breed of usually black beef cattle originating from Scotland, known for its adaptability to harsh climates
Scottish breed of beef cattle

Examples

The farm specializes in raising Galloway cattle due to their resilience in cold weather.
A herd of Galloways grazed peacefully on the Scottish hillside.
Many ranchers choose the Galloway breed for its high-quality beef and gentle temperament.

Etymology

From the region of Galloway in southwest Scotland, where the breed originated.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡæləˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæləweɪ / (BrE)
a district in southwestern Scotland, known for its historic landscapes and coastline
district in southwestern Scotland

Examples

I spent a summer hiking through Galloway and exploring its coastal villages.
The rolling hills of Galloway are popular with nature lovers and walkers.
Galloway has a rich history dating back to ancient Celtic times.

Etymology

From the Gaelic 'Gall-Ghaidhealaibh', meaning 'land of the foreign Gaels'.
