variometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriˈɑːmiˌoʊtər / (AmE), / ˌveəriˈɒmiˌəʊtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure variations in a magnetic field
instrument for measuring magnetic field variations

Examples

The laboratory used a variometer to detect subtle changes in the magnetic field during the experiment.
A portable variometer can be useful for geophysicists working in the field.
The readings from the variometer indicated a fluctuation in local magnetic activity.

Etymology

From Latin 'varius' (varied, changing) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
