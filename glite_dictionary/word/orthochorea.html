orthochorea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθoʊkɔˈriə / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊkɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
A form of chorea characterized by spasmodic movements that occur mainly when the patient is upright or standing.
chorea with spasms when standing
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with orthochorea after observing increased spasms while standing.
Although rare, orthochorea can pose significant challenges to patients trying to walk or maintain balance.
Medical literature describes orthochorea as a condition where involuntary movements worsen when the patient is erect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorea - a neurological disorder marked by involuntary, spasmodic movements

Etymology

From Greek 'ortho-' (upright) + 'chorea' (dance, a neurological disorder).
