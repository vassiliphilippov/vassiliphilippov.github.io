to upraise

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈreɪz / (AmE), / ʌpˈreɪz / (BrE)
(literary) To bring back to life or revive; to cause to become alive again.
revive or bring back to life
archaic

Examples

In legend, the wizard attempted to upraise the fallen king from his eternal sleep.
The novel tells a tale of a priest who can upraise lost souls.
Long ago, witches were believed to upraise spirits from the grave.

Synset Examples

raise from the dead
Slavery is already dead, and cannot be resurrected
Upraising ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resurrect - to bring back to life
  • Synonym: to revive - to restore to life or consciousness

Etymology

Formed from 'up-' (a prefix meaning 'upward') + 'raise'.
