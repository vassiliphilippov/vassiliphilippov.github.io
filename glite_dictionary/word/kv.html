kV

1. NOUN

/ keɪ viː / (AmE), / keɪ viː / (BrE)
a unit of electric potential equal to one thousand volts
unit of potential (1,000 volts)

Examples

The transmission line carries power at 220 kV.
This equipment is rated for use up to 33 kV.
The circuit was tested at a voltage of 11 kV.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: volt - the standard unit of electric potential
  • Degree Higher: MV - a unit of electric potential equal to one million volts

Etymology

Abbreviation of kilovolt, from 'kilo-' (thousand) and 'volt' (after Alessandro Volta).
