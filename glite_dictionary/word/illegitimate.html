illegitimate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪlɪˈdʒɪtəmət / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
(of a child or marriage) not recognized by law as valid; born to parents not legally married to each other.
not recognized as lawful (marriages or offspring)

Examples

The king acknowledged his illegitimate son in his will.
Children born outside of wedlock were once considered illegitimate by law.
Their illegitimate marriage created scandal among the aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legitimate - conforming to the law or rules; lawful

Etymology

From Medieval Latin illegitimatus, from Latin in- (not) + legitimus (lawful).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪ.ləˈdʒɪ.t̬ə.mət / (AmE), / ˌɪ.lɪˈdʒɪ.tɪ.mət / (BrE)
not allowed by law or rules; illegal or forbidden by statute
illegal; forbidden by law

Examples

The company faced charges due to its illegitimate business practices.
An illegitimate strike halted production for several days.
Any profits from this illegitimate enterprise will be confiscated by authorities.

Synset Examples

an illegitimate seizure of power
illicit trade
an outlaw strike
unlawful measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law or statute
  • Antonym: lawful - allowed or recognized by law

Etymology

From Latin illegitimus, from in- (not) + legitimus (lawful).

WordNet ID: oewn-01406764-s
Synset POS: s
