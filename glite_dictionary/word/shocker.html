shocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑkər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkə / (BrE)
a person who is shockingly bad or objectionable in some way.
shockingly bad person
informal

Examples

Everyone agreed that the new manager was a real shocker and quickly lost the team's respect.
After his terrible performance and rude comments, people began calling him a shocker behind his back.
She dated a few nice people, but her last boyfriend was a total shocker.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkə / (BrE)
a piece of news, message, or event in a film, play, or novel that is extremely surprising or sensational
sensational or surprising message in media

Examples

The film's final shocker left the entire audience speechless.
That twist in the story was a real shocker that no one saw coming.
She delivered a shocker during the interview that changed our perception of the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombshell - a sudden and surprising piece of news
  • Synonym: revelation - a surprising and previously unknown fact

Etymology

From English 'shock' + '-er' (one that shocks); first used in late 19th century.
