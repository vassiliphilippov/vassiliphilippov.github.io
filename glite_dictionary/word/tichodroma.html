Tichodroma

1. NOUN

/ tɪkəˈdroʊmə / (AmE), / tɪkəˈdrəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as wallcreepers, native to Eurasia and characterized by their striking red, grey and black plumage and habit of climbing rock faces.
a bird genus (wallcreepers)

Examples

The Tichodroma is recognized for its unique habit of scaling steep cliffs in search of insects.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Tichodroma during trips to the mountains of Europe or Asia.
The genus Tichodroma includes the well-known wallcreeper, admired for its vibrant red wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallcreeper - a small bird of the genus Tichodroma that climbs vertical rock faces

Etymology

From New Latin Tichodroma, from Ancient Greek tikho- (to wall) + dromos (runner), referring to the bird's wall-climbing behavior.
