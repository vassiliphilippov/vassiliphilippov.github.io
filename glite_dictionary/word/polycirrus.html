Polycirrus

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑliˈsɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of soft-bodied polychaete marine worms found in marine environments.
genus of soft-bodied marine worms

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Polycirrus off the coast of Japan.
The tentacles of Polycirrus are used to capture tiny food particles from the water.
A textbook illustration included several members of the Polycirrus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'kirros' (tendril, curl).
