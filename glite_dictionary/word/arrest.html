arrest

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛst / (AmE), / əˈrɛst / (BrE)
the act of detaining someone, especially a criminal, by legal authority
the act of detaining someone by law

Examples

The police made an arrest after a lengthy investigation.
He was placed under arrest for driving without a license.
The arrest of the suspect was broadcast on the news.

Synset Examples

the policeman on the beat got credit for the collar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention - the act of keeping someone in custody
  • Antonym: release - the act of freeing someone from custody

Etymology

From Old French 'arester', from Latin 'arrestare' (to stop, restrain).

2. VERB

/ əˈrɛst / (AmE), / əˈrɛst / (BrE)
to bring something to a sudden stop or halt, especially progress or movement
bring to a sudden stop

Examples

Doctors are working hard to arrest the spread of the infection.
The sudden thunderstorm arrested the team's progress up the mountain.
Strict regulations were enacted to arrest further environmental damage.

Synset Examples

halt the presses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halt - to bring or come to a stop
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress

Etymology

From Old French *arester* (to stop, to stay), from Vulgar Latin *arrestare*, from Latin *ad-* + *restare* (to remain).
