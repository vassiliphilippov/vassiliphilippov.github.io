to swosh

1. VERB

/ swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with a whistling or hissing sound
move with a whistling or hissing sound

Examples

The bubbles swoshed around in the glass as she poured the drink.
He swoshed his paintbrush across the canvas in bold, sweeping motions.
The wind swoshed through the trees, making the leaves dance noisily.

Synset Examples

The bubbles swoshed around in the glass
The curtain swooshed open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swoosh - to move with a rushing or hissing sound
