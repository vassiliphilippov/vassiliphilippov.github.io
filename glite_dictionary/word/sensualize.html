to sensualize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sjʊ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To attribute something to the senses or to treat as originating in sensation.
attribute to the senses

Examples

Some philosophers sensualize all knowledge, claiming it originates solely from sensory experience.
The poet sought to sensualize abstract ideas by linking them to physical sensations.
His theory attempts to sensualize morality by explaining it as a product of bodily desires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensual - relating to or affecting the senses rather than the intellect

Etymology

From 'sensual' + '-ize', modelled on the pattern of similar verbs.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tjʊ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to render something material or appealing to the senses, especially in art or literature.
make material or sensual, especially in art

Examples

The artist sought to sensualize the concept of love through vivid colors and realistic forms.
Some critics argue that the novel sensualizes the abstract idea of freedom, making it tangible to readers.
By incorporating tactile textures, the sculptor sensualized spiritual themes in his new exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to materialize - to make material or tangible

Etymology

From sensual + -ize, meaning to make or render sensual.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01684368-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tʃu.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To make morally or spiritually debased by emphasizing or indulging physical or sexual pleasure.
make debased by focusing on sensual pleasure

Examples

Some critics argue that modern advertising sensualizes art, shifting its focus from meaning to mere physical appeal.
The novelist was accused of trying to sensualize religious themes in his latest book.
Unchecked, society’s relentless pursuit of pleasure can sensualize even the noblest of traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - to lower in moral quality or value
  • Synonym: corrupt - to make morally perverted

Etymology

From sensual + -ize, patterned after similar English verbs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00133786-v
Synset POS: v
