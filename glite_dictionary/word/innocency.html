innocency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnəsənsi / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being innocent; innocence, especially as shown in actions or character
the state of being innocent
archaic

Examples

The innocency of her laughter put everyone at ease.
Many poets praise the innocency found in a child's heart.
He looked back on the innocency of his youth with nostalgia.

Synset Examples

the innocencies of childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being free from guilt or moral wrong
  • Antonym: guilt - the fact of having committed an offense or crime

Etymology

From Latin innocentia (harmlessness, blamelessness); variant form of "innocence".
