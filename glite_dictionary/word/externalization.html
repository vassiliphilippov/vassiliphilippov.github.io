externalization

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɜrnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of expressing or embodying something internally experienced in an outward or visible form.
expressing something internally in outward form

Examples

His artwork was an externalization of his private thoughts and emotions.
Therapy allowed him to achieve the externalization of his anxieties through dialogue.
The play served as a powerful externalization of social issues facing the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - the act of giving a concrete form to something abstract

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) + English '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɝːnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of attributing one's own ideas, feelings, or problems to external causes rather than accepting them as originating within oneself
attribution to outside causes

Examples

The patient's externalization of blame made it difficult for him to accept responsibility.
Psychologists study externalization as a defense mechanism in certain disorders.
His constant externalization of problems strained his relationships with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - the psychological process of attributing one's own feelings or impulses to someone else

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) + English '-alize' + '-ation'.
