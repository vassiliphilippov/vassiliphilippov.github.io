gab

1. VERB

/ ɡæb / (AmE), / ɡæb / (BrE)
to talk a lot, usually about trivial matters, often in a casual or informal way
talk a lot, often idly
informal

Examples

The two friends gabbed on the phone for over an hour last night.
She loves to gab with her neighbors about the latest local news.
We spent the afternoon gabbing about our plans for the summer.

Synset Examples

she was yakking away about her grandchildren

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yak - to talk persistently, often about trivial matters
  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously, usually about trivial matters
  • Antonym: to be quiet - to refrain from speaking

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, reflecting the sound of chatter.

2. NOUN

/ ɡæb / (AmE), / ɡab / (BrE)
informal conversation or chatter, especially of a light and social nature
light informal conversation
informal

Examples

We met at the café for a quick gab before work.
She enjoys a friendly gab on the phone with her neighbors.
The two friends had a lively gab over lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - casual, rapid talk
  • Synonym: gossip - casual talk often about others
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of talk or conversation

Etymology

Probably from Scots or Northern English dialect, related to 'gab' meaning mouth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07149788-n
Synset POS: n
