wizened

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪzənd / (AmE), / ˈwɪz(ə)nd / (BrE)
lean and wrinkled, often as a result of age or illness
lean and wrinkled by age or illness

Examples

The wizened face of the gardener showed years of hard work under the sun.
A wizened old man sat quietly on the park bench, watching the children play.
Her wizened hands gently held the mug, their skin thin and lined with age.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - shriveled or dried up, especially through age or neglect
  • Synonym: shriveled - shrunken and wrinkled due to loss of moisture or old age

Etymology

From Old English wisnian (to dry up, shrink), related to 'waste.'
