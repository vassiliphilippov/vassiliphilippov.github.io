antimatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tiˌmæt.ər / (AmE), / ˈantɪˌmatə / (BrE)
matter composed of elementary particles that are the counterparts, or antiparticles, of those found in ordinary matter; when antimatter and matter meet, they annihilate each other, releasing energy
matter made of antiparticles; opposite of normal matter

Examples

Scientists have yet to create large amounts of antimatter in the laboratory.
When antimatter and matter collide, they annihilate each other and produce a burst of energy.
The science fiction novel imagined a spaceship powered by antimatter engines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matter - physical substance that occupies space and has mass

Etymology

From anti- (against, opposite) + matter, first used in the early 20th century in physics.
