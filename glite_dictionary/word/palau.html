Palau

1. PROPN

/ pəˈlaʊ / (AmE), / pəˈlaʊ / (BrE)
A group of more than 200 islands forming an island country in the western central Pacific Ocean, east of the Philippines.
island country in the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The beautiful beaches of Palau attract divers from around the world.
Researchers discovered unique marine life while exploring the waters around Palau.
Palau is known for its stunning coral reefs and diverse ecosystems.

Etymology

From native Palauan 'Belau'.

2. PROPN

/ pəˈlaʊ / (AmE), / pəˈlaʊ / (BrE)
A country consisting of several islands in the western Pacific Ocean, officially known as the Republic of Palau.
country in the western Pacific Ocean

Examples

She traveled to Palau to enjoy its famous scuba diving sites.
Palau gained full independence in 1994 after being administered by the United States.
The government of Palau is committed to protecting its unique marine environment.

Etymology

From Palauan Belau, the native name for the islands.
