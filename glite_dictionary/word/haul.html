haul

1. NOUN

/ hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
the amount or quantity of something, especially fish or other items, that is caught, collected, or obtained at one time
amount caught or collected at one time

Examples

The fishing boat returned to shore with a haul of over two hundred salmon.
Police displayed the haul of stolen jewelry recovered in the raid.
Yesterday's haul from the market included fresh vegetables and spices.

Synset Examples

the catch was only 10 fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - the total number or amount of fish caught at one time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13777962-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
to transport or carry something, especially something heavy, in a vehicle or by pulling
transport or carry (usually heavy)

Examples

The workers hauled sandbags to the riverbank to prevent flooding.
They hauled the furniture across town in a rented van.
Every autumn, trucks haul pumpkins from the farm to the city markets.

Synset Examples

haul stones from the quarry in a truck
haul vegetables to the market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transport - to carry something from one place to another
  • Synonym: drag - to pull something along forcefully

Etymology

From Middle English halen, from Old French haler, of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01454866-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
to pull or drag something with effort or force, especially slowly or heavily
pull or drag with effort

Examples

The sailors hauled the heavy nets out of the water early each morning.
A truck hauled the broken-down car to the nearest garage.
Workers haul logs out of the forest using powerful machines.

Synset Examples

haul stones
haul nets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground, often with difficulty
  • Synonym: tug - to pull something suddenly or with force
  • Antonym: push - to move something away from oneself by applying force

Etymology

Probably from Middle English halen, from Old French haler (to pull, haul), from Old Norse hala (to draw, pull).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01457421-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
an act of pulling or dragging something, especially with effort or force
an act of pulling or dragging

Examples

The haul up the steep mountain took nearly an hour.
He managed the haul of the heavy box without any help.
Their journey required a difficult haul through thick mud.

Synset Examples

the haul up the hill went very slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull - an act of pulling something
  • Antonym: push - an act of pressing or moving something away from oneself

Etymology

From Middle English halen, from Old French haler, of Germanic origin.
