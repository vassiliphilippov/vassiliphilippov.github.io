rent

1. NOUN

/ rɛnt / (AmE), / rɛnt / (BrE)
A regular payment made for the use of property, land, equipment, or a service that belongs to someone else.
payment for use of property or service

Examples

The monthly rent for his apartment went up this year.
She forgot to pay the rent on time and received a warning.
Many students struggle to afford rent in large cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use of property
  • Antonym: purchase - the act of buying and owning rather than renting

Etymology

From Old French 'rente', from Medieval Latin 'renta', from 'rendere' (to yield).

4. NOUN

/ rɛnt / (AmE), / rɛnt / (BrE)
(in economics) the return or income derived from land or property that exceeds the return from the most poorly productive similar land under the same conditions; often called economic rent.
excess return from land, in economics

Examples

Landowners received a rent whenever their fields yielded higher returns than less fertile plots nearby.
The theory of rent explains differences in land value based on productivity.
Classical economists often analyzed the effects of rent on agricultural profits.

Etymology

From Old French rente (income), from Latin rendita (returned), related to 'render'.
