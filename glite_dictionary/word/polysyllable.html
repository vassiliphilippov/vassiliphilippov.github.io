polysyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.lɪˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.ɪˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
A word containing more than three syllables.
word with more than three syllables

Examples

The teacher asked the students to identify a polysyllable in the sentence.
'Unbelievable' is an example of a polysyllable.
Children often struggle to pronounce a long polysyllable correctly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monosyllable - a word consisting of only one syllable
  • Synonym: polysyllabic - having more than one and usually more than three syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'polysyllabos', from 'poly-' (many) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
