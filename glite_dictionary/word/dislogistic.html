dislogistic

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈlɑːdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) expressing disapproval or disparagement; derogatory.
expressing disapproval; derogatory

Examples

Some critics use dislogistic language when discussing popular culture.
His essay contained several dislogistic remarks about modern art.
Avoiding dislogistic terms can make academic debate more constructive.

Synset Examples

dyslogistic terms like ‘nitwit’ and ‘scalawag’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pejorative - expressing contempt or disapproval
  • Antonym: eulogistic - expressing praise

Etymology

From Greek dyslogistos (calumniatory) via Late Latin dyslogisticus.
