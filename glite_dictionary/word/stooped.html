stooped

1. ADJ

/ stupt / (AmE), / stuːpt / (BrE)
having the back and shoulders rounded forward; not standing erect
having a rounded, bent posture

Examples

The old man walked with a stooped posture, leaning heavily on his cane.
Even as a child, she was noticeably stooped from poor posture.
A stooped figure emerged from the shadows, moving slowly and carefully.

Synset Examples

a little oldish misshapen stooping woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: erect - upright in posture or position
  • Synonym: hunched - having the back arched or bent forward

Etymology

From the verb 'stoop' (to bend forward), originating from Middle English 'stoupen', from Old Norse 'stúpa' (to stoop, bow).
