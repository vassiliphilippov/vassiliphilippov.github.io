preview

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriˌvju / (AmE), / ˈpriːvjuː / (BrE)
an advance showing of a film, play, or other performance for a select audience before it is made available to the general public
advance screening for select audience

Examples

We attended a preview of the new film before its official release.
The preview attracted critics and invited guests from all over the city.
Tickets to the preview sold out within minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - the first public performance of a play or movie

Etymology

From pre- (before) + view (see), first attested in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpriːˌvjuː / (AmE), / ˈpriːvjuː / (BrE)
an advertisement consisting of brief scenes from a forthcoming film, shown before its release to promote it
film advertisement with short scenes

Examples

The cinema showed a preview of the upcoming superhero movie before the main feature.
We watched the preview and decided we wanted to see the film next weekend.
The preview generated a lot of excitement among fans waiting for the movie's release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trailer - an advertisement for a movie consisting of short scenes from the film

Etymology

From pre- (before) + view; first used in film advertising in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06901724-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpriˌvju / (AmE), / ˈpriːvjuː / (BrE)
to watch (a movie, play, or similar work) before it is officially released to the general public
to watch before public release

Examples

Only selected critics were invited to preview the highly anticipated film before its official premiere.
We were lucky enough to preview the award-winning play during a special media event.
The studio will preview their new blockbuster for executives and investors next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen - to show a movie to an audience, often before general release

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + videre (to see), via French "prévoir" and English noun 'preview' (mid-19th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02156246-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈpriːˌvjuː / (AmE), / ˈpriːvjuː / (BrE)
to watch, view, or examine something in advance, especially before it is made generally available
see or examine in advance

Examples

Fans had a chance to preview the movie before its official release.
She likes to preview books before deciding to buy them.
We previewed the new software to see its features.

Synset Examples

He pre-viewed the game trailer and was excited to play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to evaluate or assess something, especially after its release

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + view (from Latin videre, to see).
