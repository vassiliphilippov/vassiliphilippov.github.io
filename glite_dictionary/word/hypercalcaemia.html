hypercalcaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌkælsiˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˌkalsɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
an abnormally high level of calcium in the blood, often caused by excessive bone resorption or certain diseases
excess calcium in the blood (medical)
medical

Examples

After her blood tests revealed hypercalcaemia, the doctor ordered further investigations to find the cause.
Chronic hypercalcaemia can lead to kidney stones and other complications if left untreated.
Some cancers are known to cause hypercalcaemia as a paraneoplastic syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypocalcaemia - abnormally low level of calcium in the blood

Etymology

From hyper- (above normal) + calcaemia (calcium in the blood), from Latin 'calx' (lime) and Greek 'haima' (blood).
