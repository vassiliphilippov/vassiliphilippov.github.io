sunroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌruːm / (BrE)
A room in a house with large windows or glass walls, designed to let in a lot of sunlight.
room with glass walls for sunlight

Examples

They built a sunroom at the back of the house to enjoy the morning light.
The plants thrived in the bright sunroom even during winter.
She loves reading her book in the comfort of the sunroom every afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatory - a room with glass walls and roof for growing plants or relaxing in sunlight
  • Synonym: solarium - a glass-enclosed porch or room designed for sunbathing or as a lounge

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'room', attested since early 20th century as a compound word.
