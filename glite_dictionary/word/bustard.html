bustard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈbʌstəd / (BrE)
a large, heavy-bodied game bird found mainly in open country, known for its strong, swift flight
large terrestrial game bird

Examples

The great bustard is among the heaviest flying birds in the world.
A flock of bustards can often be seen crossing the grassy plains at dawn.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered bustard.

Etymology

From Old French *bistarde*, from Latin *otis tarda*, the bustard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈbʌstəd / (BrE)
a large, heavy-bodied, ground-dwelling bird found in open country in the Old World, known for its size and strong legs
large ground-dwelling bird

Examples

The Great Indian bustard is critically endangered due to habitat loss.
A bustard can weigh as much as 18 kilograms, making it one of the heaviest flying birds.
During the breeding season, the male bustard performs elaborate displays to attract females.

Synset Examples

The bustard is known for its impressive size and flight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ostrich - a very large flightless bird native to Africa

Etymology

From Old French *bistarde*, from Provençal *bistarda*, from Latin *avis tarda* (slow bird).
