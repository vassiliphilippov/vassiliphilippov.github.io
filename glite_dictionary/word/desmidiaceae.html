Desmidiaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˌmɪdiˈæsiˌi / (AmE), / dɛzˌmɪdɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of green freshwater unicellular algae characterized by their symmetrical, often ornate cell shapes.
family of unicellular green algae
medical

Examples

Members of the Desmidiaceae family are often found in acidic freshwater ponds.
The beautiful, symmetrical shapes of Desmidiaceae are admired by many microscopists.
Scientists study Desmidiaceae to better understand algae evolution and diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desmid - A unicellular green alga of the family Desmidiaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Desmidium plus the family suffix -aceae.
