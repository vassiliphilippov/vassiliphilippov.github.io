unsheared

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃɪrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɪəd / (BrE)
(of animal fur or wool) not having been sheared; not cut or clipped.
not cut or clipped (esp. fur or wool)

Examples

The jacket was made from unsheared lambskin, giving it a soft, fluffy texture.
Farmers often display unsheared sheep during the spring festivals to show off their thick wool.
The unsheared beaver pelts were prized for their luxurious, natural appearance.

Synset Examples

unsheared beaver

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheared - of wool or fur that has been cut off

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'sheared' (past participle of 'shear').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃɪrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɪəd / (BrE)
(of an animal or hair) not sheared; not having had hair, wool, or fleece cut off.
not sheared; with hair or wool intact

Examples

The sheep remained unsheared throughout the winter, growing thick wool coats.
He entered the competition with an unsheared beard, wild and impressive.
The farmer pointed out the unsheared lambs grazing in the field.

Synset Examples

a grizzly unshorn beard
unshorn sheep

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheared - having had the hair, wool, or fleece removed by cutting
  • Synonym: unshorn - not shorn; not cut or clipped (as wool, hair, etc.)

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) and sheared (past participle of shear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02162050-a
Synset POS: a
