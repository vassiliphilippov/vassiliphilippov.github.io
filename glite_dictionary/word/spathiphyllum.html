Spathiphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌspæθɪˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌspæθɪˈfɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen, rhizomatous perennial plants native to tropical regions of the Americas, the Philippines, and Indonesia, commonly grown as houseplants for their glossy leaves and white, hoodlike flowers.
genus of tropical perennials (peace lily)

Examples

The Spathiphyllum is prized for its ability to purify indoor air.
Botanists classify the peace lily under the genus Spathiphyllum.
Many homes feature a Spathiphyllum because of its attractive white blooms and resilience.

Etymology

From Greek spathē (spathe) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the spathe-like leaf structures.
