fireproof

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪrˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make (something) resistant to damage or destruction by fire by treating or constructing it with special materials.
make resistant to fire

Examples

All the curtains in the theater were fireproofed as a safety precaution.
Modern construction methods often fireproof buildings to comply with safety regulations.
They hired a company to fireproof the old warehouse before storing flammable materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flameproof - to make resistant to flames or burning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166556-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪərˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəpruːf / (BrE)
Not able to be damaged or destroyed by fire.
not damaged by fire

Examples

The laboratory was equipped with fireproof doors for extra safety.
They stored important documents in a fireproof safe to protect them from fire damage.
Workers wear fireproof clothing in case of accidents involving open flames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flameproof - able to resist burning easily; resistant to catching fire
  • Antonym: combustible - able to catch fire and burn easily

Etymology

Formed from 'fire' + 'proof', following the pattern of words denoting resistance to something (e.g. waterproof).
