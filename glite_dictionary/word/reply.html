reply

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈplaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈplʌɪ / (BrE)
a statement, either spoken or written, given in response to a question, request, criticism, or accusation
a spoken or written response

Examples

She received a polite reply to her letter within a week.
His reply to the accusation was calm and well reasoned.
Please send your reply by email if possible.

Synset Examples

I waited several days for his answer
he wrote replies to several of his critics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answer - a spoken or written response to a question or situation

Etymology

From Old French 'replier' (to fold back, reply), from Latin 'replicare' (to fold back, reply).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈplaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈplʌɪ / (BrE)
a spoken or written answer or response to a question, statement, or communication
an answer or response

Examples

She waited for his reply before continuing the conversation.
I received a prompt reply to my email request.
His reply to the accusation was both calm and clear.

Synset Examples

he growled his reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: response - a spoken or written answer
  • Antonym: question - a sentence or phrase used to find out information, which a reply answers

Etymology

From Old French replier 'to fold back, reply', from Latin replicare 'to fold back, reply'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07214225-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈplaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈplʌɪ / (BrE)
to say or write something as an answer to someone or something
to respond verbally or in writing

Examples

She took a moment to reply before giving her opinion on the subject.
He did not reply to my email for several days.
When asked about her plans, she replied that she was still unsure.

Synset Examples

She didn't want to answer
answer the question
We answered that we would accept the invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answer - to say or write something in response to a question or statement
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to a request or question

Etymology

From Old French *replier* (to fold back, reply), from Latin *replicare* (to fold back, reply).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
