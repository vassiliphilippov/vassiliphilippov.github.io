glycogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪ.kə.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌɪ.kə.dʒən / (BrE)
a substance in the body, mainly stored in the liver and muscles, which serves as a form of energy reserve by being broken down into glucose when needed
a form of stored energy in animals
medical

Examples

The liver releases glycogen into the bloodstream when your blood sugar drops.
During intense exercise, your muscles use stored glycogen for energy.
Doctors monitor glycogen levels to assess how the body manages energy reserves.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glucose - a simple sugar that is an important source of energy

Etymology

From French glycogène, from Greek glykys (sweet) + -genēs (producing).
