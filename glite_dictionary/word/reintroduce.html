to reintroduce

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˌɪntrəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to introduce something or someone again, especially after a period of absence or after it has been removed
introduce again or anew

Examples

After years of decline, conservationists hope to reintroduce wolves into the national park.
The professor decided to reintroduce the topic during the next lecture to ensure everyone understood.
With the renovations complete, the museum will reintroduce its famous Impressionist collection.

Synset Examples

We haven't met in a long time, so let me reintroduce myself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to introduce - to present someone or something for the first time

Etymology

From prefix re- (again) + introduce, from Latin introducere (to lead in).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˌɪntrəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to introduce something or someone again, especially after an absence or a period of disuse
introduce again; bring back

Examples

The organization plans to reintroduce wolves into the park after many years.
She decided to reintroduce classical music to the school's curriculum.
After a brief break, he wanted to reintroduce himself to the audience.

Synset Examples

We haven't met in a long time, so let me reintroduce myself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to introduce - to bring something or someone into use or experience for the first time

Etymology

From re- (again) + introduce, from Latin introducere (to lead in).
