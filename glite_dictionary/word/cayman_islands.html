Cayman Islands

1. PROPN

/ ˈkeɪmən ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈkeɪmən ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a British Overseas Territory in the Caribbean, northwest of Jamaica, known as an international financial center
British territory; Caribbean islands and banking center
british

Examples

The Cayman Islands attract tourists seeking beautiful beaches and clear waters.
Many companies choose to set up offices in the Cayman Islands for its favorable tax laws.
She spent her vacation exploring the coral reefs around the Cayman Islands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Jamaica - island nation in the Caribbean south of the Cayman Islands

Etymology

Named after the Carib word 'caymanas' (crocodiles) by early European explorers.
