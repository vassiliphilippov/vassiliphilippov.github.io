undershot

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌʃɒt / (BrE)
having the lower jaw or part projecting beyond the upper; used especially of animal jaws or mouths.
lower part extends beyond upper

Examples

The bulldog’s undershot jaw is a distinctive feature of the breed.
Some fish species have an undershot mouth to help them feed on the riverbed.
He was self-conscious about his slightly undershot chin.

Synset Examples

an underhung jaw
undershot bulldog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overbite - the condition where the upper teeth project over the lower teeth

Etymology

From prefix 'under-' + past participle of 'shoot', referring to 'sticking out underneath'.
