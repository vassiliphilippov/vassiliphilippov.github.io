hawksbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔksˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɔːksˌbɪl / (BrE)
a tropical sea turtle with a hawk-like beak, valued for its tortoiseshell
a tropical sea turtle with a hooked beak

Examples

The hawksbill is critically endangered due to illegal hunting for its beautiful shell.
Divers often spot the hawksbill near coral reefs, where it searches for sponges to eat.
A young hawksbill turtle was released back into the wild by conservationists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoiseshell turtle - a sea turtle whose shell is used for tortoiseshell material

Etymology

Named for its hawk-like beak.
