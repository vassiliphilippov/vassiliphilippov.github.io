valiance

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæl.i.əns / (AmE), / ˈvæl.i.əns / (BrE)
great courage, especially in the face of danger; heroic bravery
heroic courage or bravery

Examples

The knight's valiance in battle became legendary throughout the kingdom.
She was admired for her valiance when she stood up to injustice despite the risks.
Stories of valiance are often told to inspire the next generation of leaders.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valor - great courage in the face of danger, especially in battle
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger; opposite of bravery

Etymology

From Old French *valiance* (bravery), from *valiant* (brave, worthy), ultimately from Latin *valere* (to be strong).
