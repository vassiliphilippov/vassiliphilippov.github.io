to disallow

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈlaʊ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈlaʊ / (BrE)
to refuse to allow or accept; to officially forbid or reject something
refuse to allow or accept

Examples

The referee chose to disallow the goal due to an offside violation.
Company policy may disallow personal phone calls during work hours.
If the evidence is obtained illegally, the court will disallow it.

Synset Examples

I forbid you to call me late at night
Mother vetoed the trip to the chocolate store
Dad nixed our plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forbid - to order someone not to do something; prohibit
  • Antonym: permit - to allow something; give permission

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart, away) + allow (from Old French alouer).
