factory

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæktəri / (AmE), / ˈfæktəri / (BrE)
A building or group of buildings where goods are manufactured, typically using machines.
place where goods are manufactured

Examples

The new factory opened last year and now employs over 500 people.
Pollution from the factory affected the air quality in the nearby village.
Each factory worker must complete safety training before starting the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - a place where industrial or manufacturing processes take place
  • Degree Lower: workshop - a room or building where things are made or repaired by hand
  • Synonym: mill - a building equipped with machinery for processing raw materials

Etymology

From Latin 'facere' (to do, make) via Old French 'factorie' (establishment for agents).
