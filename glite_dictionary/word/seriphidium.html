Seriphidium

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈrɪfɪdiəm / (AmE), / sɛˈrɪfɪdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of woody plants, chiefly cultivated for their silver or grey and often aromatic foliage; formerly classified within Artemisia
genus of silvery, aromatic woody plants

Examples

The landscaper recommended planting Seriphidium to add texture and silver color to the garden borders.
Botanists once grouped Seriphidium species within the genus Artemisia, but later reclassified them.
The aromatic foliage of Seriphidium makes it a popular choice for drought-resistant landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Artemisia - a related genus of aromatic plants, including wormwood and sagebrush

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used as a genus name in botanical classification.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈrɪfɪdiəm / (AmE), / sɛˈrɪfɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of woody plants, formerly included in Artemisia, noted for their silver or grey, often aromatic foliage.
a genus of aromatic, silver-leaved plants

Examples

Several species of Seriphidium are cultivated for their striking silver foliage in gardens.
Botanists often distinguish Seriphidium from closely related genera based on morphological traits.
The aromatic leaves of Seriphidium are used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Artemisia - a genus of aromatic plants, previously including Seriphidium

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek sērifidion (name of a plant in Dioscorides), used as a genus name in botany.
