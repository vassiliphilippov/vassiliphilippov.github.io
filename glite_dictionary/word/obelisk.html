obelisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊbəlɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒbəlɪsk / (BrE)
a tall, four-sided stone pillar that tapers towards a pointed, pyramidal top, often erected as a monument or landmark
tall, tapering stone monument

Examples

The ancient Egyptians constructed the obelisk to honor the sun god Ra.
A striking obelisk stands in the center of the city square, attracting many tourists.
Many Roman emperors transported Egyptian obelisks to decorate their cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monument - a statue, building, or other structure erected to commemorate a notable person or event

Etymology

From Greek obeliskos (small spit or peg), diminutive of obelos (spit, pointed pillar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊbəlɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒbəlɪsk / (BrE)
A symbol (†) used in printing or writing to indicate a second footnote or reference, usually after an asterisk has been used.
symbol for second footnote or reference

Examples

The obelisk in the text shows that there is a secondary footnote with more information.
When the asterisk is already used, editors often rely on the obelisk for additional references.
You will see an obelisk next to terms that require further explanation in the appendix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - the typographical symbol (†) used for footnotes

Etymology

From Greek 'obeliskos', meaning small spit or pillar, originally referring to a pointed pillar before also being used for the symbol.
