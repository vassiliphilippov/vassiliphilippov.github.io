superficiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˌfɪʃ.iˈæ.lə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˌfɪʃ.iˈæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality of lacking depth of knowledge, thought, or feeling; concern only with the surface or obvious aspects
lack of depth or substance

Examples

The superficiality of his analysis led to criticism from his peers.
Many people are frustrated by the superficiality of social media conversations.
The documentary exposes the superficiality of celebrity culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; concerned only with surface aspects
  • Antonym: depth - the quality of being deep, thorough, or profound

Etymology

From Late Latin superficialitas, from Latin superficialis (of the surface).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˌfɪʃ.iˈæ.lə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˌfɪʃ.iˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of affecting or involving only the surface; lack of depth or thoroughness
lack of depth; surface-level quality

Examples

The superficiality of her analysis was apparent to the experienced scientists.
Critics often complain about the superficiality of reality television shows.
He was frustrated by the superficiality of their small talk at the party.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallowness - lack of depth or substance
  • Antonym: depth - the quality of being deep or thorough

Etymology

From Medieval Latin superficialitas, from Latin superficialis (superficial).
