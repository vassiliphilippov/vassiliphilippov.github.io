earl

1. NOUN

/ ɜrl / (AmE), / ɜːl / (BrE)
A British nobleman ranking below a marquess and above a viscount.
British noble rank below marquess
british

Examples

The earl inherited his title and estate from his father.
During the ceremony, the earl addressed his fellow peers in the House of Lords.
She was surprised to meet an actual earl during her visit to England.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: marquess - a British peer ranking above an earl
  • Degree Lower: viscount - a British peer ranking below an earl

Etymology

From Old English *eorl* (nobleman, warrior).
