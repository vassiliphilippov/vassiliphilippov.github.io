countersubversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərsəbˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəsəbˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
actions or measures intended to detect and prevent subversive activities, especially as part of counterintelligence efforts.
detection and prevention of subversive activities

Examples

The government increased funding for countersubversion operations after detecting the plot.
His job in intelligence focused mainly on countersubversion activities within the organization.
Effective countersubversion is essential to national security agencies worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterintelligence - activities designed to prevent spying or sabotage
  • Antonym: subversion - the act of undermining authority or established systems

Etymology

From counter- (against) + subversion, from Latin subvertere (to overturn).
