fibrillation

1. NOUN

/ fɪbrɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪbrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of forming fine fibers or fibrils, especially in biological tissues.
formation of fine fibers (fibrils)
medical

Examples

The fibrillation of collagen contributes to the strength of connective tissues.
Scientists observed fibrillation in the muscle samples under the microscope.
Certain diseases can disrupt the normal fibrillation process in tissues.

Etymology

From fibril (a small fiber) + -ation (denoting action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪbrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
rapid, irregular, and uncoordinated contraction of muscle fibers, especially in the heart or other muscles
irregular muscle fiber contractions
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with atrial fibrillation after noticing an irregular heartbeat.
Fibrillation of the heart muscle can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
Electrical impulses were used to stop the fibrillation and restore a normal heart rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - an abnormal heart rhythm

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber) + English '-ation'.
