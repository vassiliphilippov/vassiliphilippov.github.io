roentgenography

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛntɡəˈnɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌrɛntɡəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of using X-rays to produce an image of the internal structure of an object, especially the human body
X-ray imaging process
medical

Examples

Roentgenography is often used by doctors to diagnose bone fractures and lung diseases.
Advances in digital roentgenography have improved the quality of medical imaging.
The patient underwent roentgenography to identify the source of chest pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiography - imaging technique using radiation, especially X-rays, to view internal structures

Etymology

From Wilhelm Röntgen, discoverer of X-rays, plus Greek 'graphy' (writing, recording).
