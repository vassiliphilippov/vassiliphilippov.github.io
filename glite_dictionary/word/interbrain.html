interbrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntɚˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌbreɪn / (BrE)
the part of the brain forming the posterior division of the forebrain, connecting the cerebral hemispheres with the midbrain; also called the diencephalon
posterior division of the forebrain; diencephalon
medical

Examples

The interbrain plays a crucial role in relaying sensory information to the cerebral cortex.
Damage to the interbrain can affect vital bodily functions, including temperature regulation and sleep cycles.
In their medical textbook, the students reviewed diagrams showing the structure of the interbrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diencephalon - the posterior part of the forebrain, containing structures like the thalamus and hypothalamus

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + English 'brain'.
