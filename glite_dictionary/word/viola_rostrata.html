Viola rostrata

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə rɒˈstreɪtə / (AmE), / vɪˈəʊlə rɒsˈtrɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, known for its lilac-purple flowers with a long, slender spur.
North American violet species

Examples

The Viola rostrata is often found in moist woodlands throughout the Appalachian Mountains.
She photographed the delicate lilac-purple flowers of the Viola rostrata during her spring hike.
Botanists distinguish Viola rostrata from similar species by its noticeably long floral spur.

Etymology

From Latin, 'Viola' (violet) and 'rostrata' (beaked or provided with a beak, referring to the flower's spur).
