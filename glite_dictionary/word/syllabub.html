syllabub

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (BrE)
a dessert made from sweetened cream beaten with wine or a similar liquor, sometimes with the addition of fruit or flavorings
sweetened cream dessert with wine

Examples

She brought out a bowl of syllabub for dessert, topped with fresh berries.
In eighteenth-century England, syllabub was a popular treat at festive gatherings.
The rich, creamy texture of syllabub makes it perfect for special occasions.

Etymology

From early modern English, likely from 'syllabubbe' of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləbʌb / (AmE), / ˈsɪləbʌb / (BrE)
a sweet, frothy dessert or drink made from milk or cream, sugar, and wine or cider, popular in England from the 16th to 19th centuries
sweet, frothy dessert drink

Examples

On warm summer afternoons, they would serve homemade syllabub in delicate glass cups.
The recipe for syllabub calls for just a few simple ingredients, including cream and white wine.
Her grandmother's syllabub was always the highlight of family gatherings.

Synset Examples

He enjoyed a refreshing syllabub on a hot day.

Etymology

From early modern English, possibly from 'syllable' and an unknown origin, first recorded in the 16th century.
