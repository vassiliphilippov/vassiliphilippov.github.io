Two Kettle

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˈkɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / tuː ˈkɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A member of a division of the Teton Sioux people, part of the Siouan-speaking Native American groups.
division of the Teton Sioux people

Examples

The Two Kettle are known for their historical role among the Teton Sioux tribes.
An elder from the Two Kettle shared stories about their ancestral traditions.
The reservation is home to many members of the Two Kettle band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teton Sioux - A group of Sioux tribes to which the Two Kettle belong
  • Degree Higher: Siouan - A large Native American language family

Etymology

Name translated from the Lakota term Oóhenuŋpa, meaning 'Two Boilings' or 'Two Kettles.'
