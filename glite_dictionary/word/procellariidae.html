Procellariidae

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɛləˈrɪəˌdi / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɛləˈrɪədiː / (BrE)
a family of seabirds including petrels, fulmars, and shearwaters, known for their long wings and oceanic habits
family of seabirds: petrels, fulmars, shearwaters

Examples

The Procellariidae are widely distributed across all the world's oceans, especially in the Southern Hemisphere.
Shearwaters, a subgroup of Procellariidae, are famous for their long migratory journeys.
Certain species within the Procellariidae family nest on remote islands and only return to land to breed.

Etymology

From modern Latin Procellariidae, from the genus Procellaria, based on Latin 'procella' (storm, gale).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɛləˈraɪ.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɛl.əˈraɪ.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of seabirds including petrels, fulmars, and shearwaters, known for their long-distance flight and oceanic habits.
family of seabirds (petrels, fulmars, shearwaters)

Examples

The Procellariidae are well adapted to life at sea, only coming ashore to breed.
Researchers studied several species within the Procellariidae family to understand their migration patterns.
Shearwaters, fulmars, and petrels all belong to the Procellariidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrel - a small seabird in the Procellariidae family
  • Synonym: fulmar - a type of seabird in the Procellariidae family
  • Synonym: shearwater - a long-winged seabird in the Procellariidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Procellariidae, from genus Procellaria, from Latin procella (storm, gale).
