mandolin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmændəˌlɪn / (AmE), / ˌmændəˈlɪn / (BrE)
a musical instrument with a pear-shaped body and four pairs of strings, usually played with a plectrum and related to the lute
stringed musical instrument, lute family

Examples

She strummed her mandolin by the fire while the band played.
The folk musician displayed a vintage mandolin at the concert.
He learned to play the mandolin after mastering the guitar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lute - a plucked string instrument shaped like a half-pear

Etymology

From Italian 'mandolino', diminutive of 'mandola', a type of lute.
