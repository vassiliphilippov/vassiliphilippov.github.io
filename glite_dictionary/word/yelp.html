yelp

1. NOUN

/ jɛlp / (AmE), / jɛlp / (BrE)
a short, sharp, high-pitched cry or bark, especially made by a dog or in pain or excitement
a sharp, high-pitched cry

Examples

The puppy let out a sudden yelp when its tail was stepped on.
Her yelp of surprise startled everyone in the room.
A loud yelp echoed through the park as the dog chased a squirrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - the sharp sound made by a dog
  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud, doleful cry uttered by a dog or wolf

Etymology

From Middle English 'yelpen', from Old English 'gielpan' (to boast, speak loudly).

2. VERB

/ jɛlp / (AmE), / jɛlp / (BrE)
(of an animal) to bark or cry out in a sharp, high-pitched tone, usually expressing pain or excitement.
bark or cry out sharply (animal)

Examples

The dog began to yelp in pain after stepping on a thorn.
We heard the puppies yelping excitedly as they played in the yard.
The frightened fox yelped and darted back into the bushes.

Synset Examples

the puppies yelped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - to make a sharp explosive cry, typical of a dog
  • Degree Higher: howl - make a long loud wailing cry, like a dog or wolf

Etymology

From Middle English *yelpen*, from Old English *gielpan*, meaning to boast or exclaim.
