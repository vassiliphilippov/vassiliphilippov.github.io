carnauba

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnuːbə / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɔːbə / (BrE)
A hard yellowish to brownish wax obtained from the leaves of the carnauba palm, used particularly in floor waxes and polishes.
hard wax from carnauba palm leaves

Examples

The floor was polished to a high shine with carnauba wax.
Many high-end car waxes contain carnauba for a durable, glossy finish.
Manufacturers often use carnauba in food coatings and cosmetics due to its protective qualities.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carnaúba', the name of the palm tree.

2. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈnɑːbʊə / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɔːbə / (BrE)
A Brazilian fan palm (Copernicia prunifera) known for its edible root, fiber from its leaves, and the brittle yellowish wax produced from its leaves.
Brazilian palm tree; source of carnauba wax

Examples

The wax from the carnauba palm is used in a variety of polishes and cosmetics.
Farmers in Brazil harvest carnauba leaves to extract the valuable wax.
The carnauba tree is often called the 'tree of life' due to its many uses.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carnaúba', from Tupi 'karana'iwa'.
