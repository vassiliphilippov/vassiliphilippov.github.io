swimmeret

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmɚˌɛt / (AmE), / ˈswɪmərɛt / (BrE)
one of the paired, small, leg-like appendages on the underside of the abdomen of certain aquatic crustaceans, mainly used for swimming and, in females, for carrying eggs
abdominal swimming appendage of crustaceans

Examples

The female lobster uses each swimmeret to hold onto her developing eggs.
Crayfish propel themselves backward by flicking their swimmerets rapidly.
Scientists observed how the movement of a swimmeret allows the shrimp to steer effectively in the water.

Etymology

Formed from 'swim' + diminutive suffix '-et', indicating small size.
