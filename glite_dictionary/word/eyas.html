eyas

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.əs / (BrE)
a young hawk, especially one that has not yet learned to fly
a young, unfledged hawk

Examples

The falconer cared for the eyas until it was ready to leave the nest.
An eyas is highly dependent on its parents for food and protection.
Researchers studied how the eyas developed its first feathers.

Etymology

From Middle English *nyas*, from Old French *niais* (nestling), from Latin *nidus* (nest), with misdivision of 'an eyas' from 'a nyas'.
