Calluna vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlunə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / kəˈluːnə vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A low evergreen shrub native to Europe and Asia, commonly known as heather, cultivated widely for its pink or purple flowers.
evergreen shrub; common heather

Examples

The hills of Scotland are blanketed with Calluna vulgaris during late summer.
Bees are often attracted to Calluna vulgaris for its abundant nectar.
Many gardeners plant Calluna vulgaris to add color and texture to their rock gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - common name for Calluna vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin; 'Calluna' from Greek 'kallunein' (to clean, as twigs were used for making brooms), 'vulgaris' means common.
