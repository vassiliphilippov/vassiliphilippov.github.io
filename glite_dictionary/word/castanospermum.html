Castanospermum

1. NOUN

/ kæs.tə.noʊˈspɜːr.məm / (AmE), / kas.tə.nəʊˈspɜː.məm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), containing the single species Moreton Bay chestnut, native to Australia.
genus of plant (Moreton Bay chestnut)

Examples

The Castanospermum tree is commonly found along rivers in eastern Australia.
Scientists studied seeds of Castanospermum to understand their germination process.
The glossy leaves of Castanospermum are a distinctive feature of the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moreton Bay chestnut - the only species in the Castanospermum genus, a tree native to eastern Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'kastanon' (chestnut) and 'sperma' (seed).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæstənoʊspərˈmuːm / (AmE), / ˌkæstənəʊspəˈmuːm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), containing a single species, the Moreton Bay chestnut.
genus of flowering plants (Moreton Bay chestnut)

Examples

The Castanospermum tree is native to eastern Australia and produces large, glossy seeds.
Botanists studied the Castanospermum to better understand plant adaptation to wet environments.
The Castanospermum australe, also known as Moreton Bay chestnut, is grown as an ornamental tree.

Etymology

From Greek 'kastanon' (chestnut) and 'sperma' (seed) referring to the chestnut-like seeds.
