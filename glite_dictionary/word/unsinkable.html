unsinkable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɪŋkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪŋkəbl / (BrE)
impossible to sink; incapable of being submerged under water
impossible to sink

Examples

Despite several storms, the engineers claimed the boat was truly unsinkable.
The legend of the unsinkable ship ended with the tragedy of the Titanic.
Many modern materials are marketed as unsinkable for added safety.

Synset Examples

they thought the Titanic was unsinkable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sailable - able to be sailed or not likely to sink

Etymology

From un- (not) + sink + -able (able to), indicating something that cannot be sunk.
