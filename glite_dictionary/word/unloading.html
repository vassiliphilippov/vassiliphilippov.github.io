unloading

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing a load or cargo from a vehicle, ship, or container
removal of cargo or goods

Examples

The crew began the unloading of the shipment as soon as the truck arrived at the warehouse.
Due to rain, the unloading of the cargo was delayed until the following morning.
Safety procedures must be followed during the unloading of hazardous materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loading - the act of putting a load or cargo onto a vehicle or container

Etymology

Formed from 'unload' + '-ing'.
