Anarhichadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌænərɪˈkædədi / (AmE), / ˌænəˌrɪkəˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as wolffishes, characterized by long bodies and strong jaws, found in cold northern seas.
family of marine wolffishes

Examples

Biologists have studied the teeth and feeding behavior of the Anarhichadidae for decades.
The cold waters of the North Atlantic are home to several species within Anarhichadidae.
Members of the Anarhichadidae family are adapted to crush hard-shelled prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolffish - a large marine fish with strong jaws, member of Anarhichadidae

Etymology

From New Latin Anarhichas (the genus name) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
