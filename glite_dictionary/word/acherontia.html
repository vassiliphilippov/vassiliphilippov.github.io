Acherontia

1. PROPN

/ ækəˈrɒntiə / (AmE), / ˌækəˈrɒntɪə / (BrE)
A genus of moths known as death's-head moths, characterized by a skull-like pattern on the thorax.
genus of death's-head moths

Examples

The Acherontia genus includes several striking species of death's-head moths.
Entomologists often study Acherontia for its unique skull-shaped thoracic marking.
Legend associates the Acherontia moth with superstition and folklore in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death's-head moth - a large moth with a skull-like pattern on its thorax

Etymology

From Latin, named after the river Acheron in Greek mythology, associated with the underworld.
