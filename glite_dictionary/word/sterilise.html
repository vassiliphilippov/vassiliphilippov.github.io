to sterilise

1. VERB

/ ˈstɛrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make free from bacteria or other living microorganisms, usually by applying heat or chemicals
make free from bacteria or microorganisms

Examples

Medical equipment must be sterilised before surgery to prevent infection.
She sterilised the baby's bottles by boiling them in water.
Hospitals often use autoclaves to sterilise instruments quickly and thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean in order to destroy bacteria

Etymology

From French stériliser, from Latin sterilis (unfruitful, sterile).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɛr.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal unable to produce offspring, typically through a medical procedure
make infertile, especially by medical means
medical british

Examples

The clinic will sterilise the animals before offering them for adoption.
Some governments once tried to sterilise people against their will for eugenic reasons.
She chose to sterilise her cat to prevent unwanted litters.

Synset Examples

in some countries, people with genetically transmissible disabilities are sterilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sterilize - to make infertile; American spelling
  • Antonym: to fertilize - to make fertile or able to reproduce

Etymology

From French 'stériliser', from Latin 'sterilis' (barren, unfruitful).
