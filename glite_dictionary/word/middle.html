middle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl / (BrE)
the period or point that is equally distant from the beginning and end of something, especially a span of time or a process
central point or period of something

Examples

We reached the middle of the conference just as the keynote speaker began.
She got bored in the middle of the movie and left the cinema early.
Heavy rain fell during the middle of April, causing floods in some areas.

Synset Examples

the middle of the war
rain during the middle of April

Related Terms

  • Antonym: end - the final point of a period or process
  • Antonym: beginning - the starting point of a period or process

Etymology

From Old English 'middel', from Proto-Germanic *medjaz (middle).
