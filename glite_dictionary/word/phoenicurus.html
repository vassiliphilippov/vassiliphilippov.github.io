Phoenicurus

1. NOUN

/ fiːˈnɪkjʊrəs / (AmE), / fiːˈnɪkjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World thrushes that includes some species of redstarts, known for their brightly colored plumage.
genus of Old World thrushes (redstarts)

Examples

The European Phoenicurus species are easily recognized by their bright orange tails.
Birdwatchers in Asia frequently observe several Phoenicurus redstarts in the wild.
The breeding biology of Phoenicurus has been studied extensively across Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redstart - a small songbird commonly found in Eurasia and Africa, often belonging to the genus Phoenicurus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'phoinix' (crimson) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the bird's red tail.

genus of Old World thrushes (redstarts)
