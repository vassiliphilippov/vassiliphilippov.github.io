carnal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnəl / (BrE)
relating to physical, especially sexual, desires and appetites rather than to the spiritual or intellectual.
relating to bodily or sexual desires

Examples

He tried to resist his carnal desires and focus on prayer.
Their relationship was based on more than just carnal attraction.
The book was banned for its explicit depiction of carnal pleasures.

Synset Examples

animal instincts
carnal knowledge
fleshly desire
a sensual delight in eating
music is the only sensual pleasure without vice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensual - relating to physical pleasure, especially sexual
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the soul or non-physical aspects; opposite of bodily

Etymology

From Latin 'carnalis', from 'caro' (flesh).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnəl / (BrE)
relating to the physical body or flesh, especially as opposed to the spiritual or intellectual.
relating to the body or flesh

Examples

Many ancient rituals were steeped in carnal symbolism.
Archaeologists examined the carnal remains discovered at the site.
The play explores the tension between carnal desires and moral duty.

Synset Examples

carnal remains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical - relating to the body as opposed to the mind or spirit
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit as opposed to the body

Etymology

From Latin 'carnalis', from 'caro, carnis' meaning 'flesh'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02987585-a
