Vaisnavism

1. NOUN

/ vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪzəm / (AmE), / vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A major tradition within Hinduism that worships Vishnu as the supreme deity.
Hindu tradition worshipping Vishnu

Examples

He studied Vaisnavism to better understand the role of Vishnu in Hindu beliefs.
The temple was built as a center of Vaisnavism in the region.
Many festivals associated with Vaisnavism are celebrated with great devotion in India.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition worshipping Shiva as the supreme deity

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Vaiṣṇava' (devotee of Vishnu) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪzəm / (AmE), / vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪzəm / (BrE)
a branch of Hinduism that is devoted to the worship of Vishnu as the supreme deity
Hindu sect worshipping Vishnu

Examples

Vaisnavism emphasizes devotion to Vishnu as the highest form of worship.
Many practices in Vaisnavism involve singing hymns dedicated to Vishnu.
Scholars have traced the origins of Vaisnavism to early Hindu scriptures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition devoted to the worship of Shiva

Etymology

From Sanskrit vaiṣṇava (devotee of Vishnu) + -ism.
