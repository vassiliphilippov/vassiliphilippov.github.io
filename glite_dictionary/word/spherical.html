spherical

1. ADJ

/ ˈsfɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsfɪərɪkəl / (BrE)
having the shape of a sphere; relating to or resembling a sphere.
shaped like or relating to a sphere

Examples

The Earth is approximately spherical in shape, though it's slightly flattened at the poles.
They installed a spherical lamp in the center of the room, giving it a modern look.
The artist created a stunning spherical sculpture from marble.

Synset Examples

spherical geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round - shaped like a circle or sphere

Etymology

From Late Latin sphaericus, from Greek sphairikos, from sphaira ('sphere').

2. ADJ

/ ˈsfɪr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsfɛr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
having the shape of a sphere; perfectly round like a ball
shaped like a sphere or ball

Examples

The earth is not a perfect spherical object, but slightly flattened at the poles.
Oil droplets in water tend to form a spherical shape due to surface tension.
The artist created spherical sculptures that looked like giant marbles.

Synset Examples

a spherical object
nearly orbicular in shape
little globular houses like mud-wasp nests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globular - having the shape of a globe; spherical
  • Synonym: orbicular - having a rounded or circular shape; nearly spherical

Etymology

From Late Latin 'sphaericus', from Greek 'sphairikos', from 'sphaira' (sphere).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02048649-s
Synset POS: s
