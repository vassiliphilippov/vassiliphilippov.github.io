amygdalin

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪɡdəlɪn / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡdəlɪn / (BrE)
a bitter, cyanogenic compound found in the seeds of apricots, plums, and bitter almonds
bitter compound in apricot and almond seeds
medical

Examples

Amygdalin is sometimes incorrectly promoted as an alternative cancer treatment.
The seeds of bitter almonds contain high levels of amygdalin.
Researchers study how amygdalin breaks down into hydrogen cyanide in the body.

Etymology

From Latin amygdala (almond), via French amygdaline.
