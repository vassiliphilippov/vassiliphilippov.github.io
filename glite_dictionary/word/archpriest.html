archpriest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃˌprist / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃˌpriːst / (BrE)
a senior priest, often holding a position of authority within certain Christian churches
senior priest in Christian churches

Examples

The archpriest presided over the ceremony with solemn dignity.
He was appointed archpriest of the cathedral after decades of service.
During the Middle Ages, the archpriest often supervised several parishes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: priest - a person authorized to perform worship and other religious ceremonies in Christian churches
  • Degree Higher: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy with authority over a diocese

Etymology

From Medieval Latin archipresbyter, from Greek arkhī- (chief) + presbyteros (elder, priest).
