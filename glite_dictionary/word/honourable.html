honourable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving of honor and respect; having high moral standards and integrity.
deserving honor and respect

Examples

She lived an honourable life, admired by everyone for her honesty and kindness.
He received an award for his honourable service to the community.
It is important to act in an honourable way, even when no one is watching.

Synset Examples

an honorable man
led an honorable life
honorable service to his country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonourable - not deserving of honor or respect; lacking integrity
  • Synonym: honorable - alternative US spelling of 'honourable'

Etymology

From Old French 'honorable', from Latin 'honorabilis' (worthy of honor).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑn(ə)rəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒn(ə)rəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving of respect and admiration because of adherence to ethical and moral principles.
adhering to ethical and moral principles
british

Examples

He was known as an honourable judge who always did the right thing.
She chose the most honourable path, even though it was more difficult.
People admire leaders who are honourable and act with integrity.

Synset Examples

it seems ethical and right
followed the only honorable course of action

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonourable - not deserving respect; lacking moral principles
  • Synonym: honorable - deserving of respect for ethical principles (American spelling)

Etymology

From Old French *honorable*, from Latin *honorabilis* (worthy of honor).

WordNet ID: oewn-02042493-s
Synset POS: s
