histaminase

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstæməˌneɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈstæmɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of histamine, often found in various tissues.
enzyme that breaks down histamine
medical

Examples

The activity of histaminase increases in response to allergic reactions.
Researchers are studying how histaminase levels affect inflammation in the body.
Some medications may inhibit histaminase and influence histamine metabolism.

Etymology

Formed from 'histamine' and enzyme suffix '-ase'.
