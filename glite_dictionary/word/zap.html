zap

1. NOUN

/ zæp / (AmE), / zæp / (BrE)
a sudden burst or jolt of energy or excitement, often with a dramatic effect
sudden burst of energy or excitement
informal

Examples

The scientist gave the sample another zap of laser light to trigger the reaction.
A quick zap of caffeine was all she needed to wake up.
There was an audible zap when the power was restored to the building.

Synset Examples

they gave it another zap of radiation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jolt - a sudden, strong burst or shock
  • Synonym: surge - a sudden powerful movement or burst

2. VERB

/ zæp / (AmE), / zæp / (BrE)
to strike or attack suddenly, especially with a weapon, energy beam, or explosive device
to strike suddenly or attack with force
informal

Examples

The fighter jets zapped the enemy camp with precision-guided missiles.
In the video game, you gain points every time you zap an alien spaceship.
He quickly zapped the wasp with an electric fly swatter before it could sting anyone.

Synset Examples

zap the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast - to hit or attack with explosive force
  • Synonym: attack - to act aggressively against a target
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to hit or strike a target

Etymology

Imitative, first attested in 1929 in comic books to represent the sound of a gun or explosion.
