forthwith

1. ADV

/ fɔrθˈwɪθ / (AmE), / fɔːθˈwɪθ / (BrE)
immediately; without delay or hesitation.
immediately; without delay

Examples

The committee demanded that the document be released forthwith.
He was instructed to leave the premises forthwith.
Any payments due must be made forthwith after receiving the invoice.

Synset Examples

he answered immediately
found an answer straightaway
an official accused of dishonesty should be suspended forthwith
Come here now!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Synonym: instantly - at once; in an instant
  • Antonym: later - at some time in the future

Etymology

From Middle English 'forthwith', equivalent to 'forth' (forward) + 'with.'
