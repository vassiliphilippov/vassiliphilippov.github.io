to preoccupy

1. VERB

/ priˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / priˈɒkjʊˌpaɪ / (BrE)
to absorb or dominate the mind or attention of (a person) to the exclusion of other thoughts; to engage urgently or obsessively.
to absorb the mind; to engross attention

Examples

Lately, worries about the future preoccupy her mind so much that she loses sleep.
His research project continues to preoccupy him, even during his vacation.
Thoughts of the upcoming event preoccupied everyone in the office for days.

Synset Examples

His work preoccupies him
The matter preoccupies her completely — she cannot think of anything else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take up the attention or interest completely
  • Antonym: to distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention to something

Etymology

From Latin "praeoccupare" (to seize beforehand), from "prae-" (before) + "occupare" (to seize).

2. VERB

/ priˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / priˈɒkjʊˌpaɪ / (BrE)
(formal) To take possession of, occupy, or appropriate (a place or thing) before someone else; to acquire for use in advance.
to take possession beforehand

Examples

The military forces preoccupied the border regions before the enemy could arrive.
Several explorers attempted to preoccupy new territories during the age of discovery.
The rebels hoped to preoccupy strategic locations ahead of the advancing army.

Synset Examples

the army preoccupied the hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occupy - to take possession of a place

Etymology

From Latin 'praeoccupare' (to seize beforehand), from 'prae-' (before) + 'occupare' (to seize).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
