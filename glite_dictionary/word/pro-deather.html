pro-deather

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈdɛθər / (AmE), / prəʊˈdɛθə / (BrE)
an informal, often derogatory term for someone who supports the right to choose death (such as euthanasia) or is opposed to certain public health policies
person supporting death choice/opposed to some health policies
informal

Examples

Some critics labelled the activists as pro-deathers due to their stance on end-of-life laws.
Debates over vaccines led to accusations of being a pro-deather from opposing groups.
She was called a pro-deather after speaking against certain health interventions.

Synset Examples

She identified as pro-deather, advocating for personal choice.
The pro-deather group opposes government health mandates.
The pro-deather movement is rallying against vaccinations.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pro-' (in favor of) and 'death', modeled on terms like 'pro-lifer'.
