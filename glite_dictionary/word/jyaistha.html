Jyaistha

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪstə / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪstə / (BrE)
The third month of the Hindu calendar, typically falling in May-June of the Gregorian calendar.
third month of the Hindu calendar

Examples

In northern India, the festival was celebrated during Jyaistha each year.
The full moon of Jyaistha marks several important religious observances.
Temperatures often peak in Jyaistha, making it one of the hottest months.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Jyaiṣṭha', meaning 'the eldest' or 'most senior'.
