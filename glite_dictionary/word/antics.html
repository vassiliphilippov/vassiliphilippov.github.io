antics

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪks / (BrE)
amusing, silly, or playful behavior; actions that are often exaggerated or intended to make people laugh
humorous or silly behavior

Examples

The comedian's antics on stage had the audience in stitches all night.
Parents sometimes struggle to keep up with the antics of young children.
His outrageous antics during the meeting both amused and annoyed his colleagues.

Synset Examples

Wario's antics in the game always make me laugh.
The kids laughed at the antics of Looney Tunes characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pranks - a playful or mischievous act

Etymology

Possibly from Italian antico ('antique, grotesque'), relating to grotesque performances.
