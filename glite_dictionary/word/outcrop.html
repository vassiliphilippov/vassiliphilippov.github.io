outcrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkrɑp / (AmE), / ˈaʊtkrɒp / (BrE)
a visible exposure of bedrock or other geological formation on the surface of the earth
exposed part of rock formation

Examples

The geologists took samples from the outcrop near the riverbank.
A lone tree stood atop the rocky outcrop overlooking the valley.
We found fossils embedded in the outcrop during our field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escarpment - a steep slope or long cliff forming the edge of a plateau or ridge

Etymology

From 'out-' (outside) + 'crop', originally meaning something that comes out or juts out.

2. VERB

/ ˈaʊtˌkrɑp / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.krɒp / (BrE)
(of rock or other material) to become visible or exposed at the earth's surface.
become exposed at the earth's surface

Examples

Large slabs of granite outcrop along the riverbank, forming natural terraces.
In this region, coal beds outcrop near the foothills and are easily accessible.
The geologist explained how sedimentary layers outcrop at the surface due to erosion.

Synset Examples

Big boulders outcropped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to come out or appear from somewhere
  • Antonym: to subside - to go below the surface or become less visible

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outside) + 'crop' (to come forth); geological term since mid-19th century.
