Pope John Paul II

1. PROPN

/ poʊp dʒɑn pɑl ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / pəʊp dʒɒn pɔːl ði ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1920–2005) The head of the Roman Catholic Church from 1978 to 2005, the first non-Italian pope in 455 years and the first pope from Poland.
Polish pope from 1978 to 2005

Examples

Many people traveled to Rome to see Pope John Paul II during his papacy.
Pope John Paul II played a pivotal role in the fall of communism in Eastern Europe.
The canonization of Pope John Paul II was attended by thousands of pilgrims.
