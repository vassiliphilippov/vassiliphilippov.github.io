Molossidae

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɒsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈlɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of bats commonly known as mastiff bats or free-tailed bats, characterized by long tails extending beyond the tail membrane.
family of bats (mastiff/freetail bats)

Examples

The Molossidae are known for their swift flight and loud vocalizations.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Molossidae family in South America.
Many Molossidae species have large, forward-facing ears and a distinctive tail.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Molossidae, from Greek Molossos (an ancient region), referencing the mastiff-type appearance.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈlɒsɪdiː / (AmE), / məˈlɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of bats known as mastiff bats or freetail bats, characterized by their robust bodies and free tails extending beyond the tail membrane.
family of bats (mastiff bats, freetail bats)

Examples

The Molossidae are widely distributed across warm regions of the world.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Molossidae in South America.
Members of the Molossidae are also commonly known as free-tailed bats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a flying mammal of the order Chiroptera

Etymology

From Latin 'molossus', a type of large dog, due to the robust appearance of these bats.
