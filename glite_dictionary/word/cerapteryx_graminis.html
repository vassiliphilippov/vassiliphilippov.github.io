Cerapteryx graminis

1. NOUN

/ sɛr.əˈpɪər.ɪks ˈɡræm.ɪ.nɪs / (AmE), / sɛ.ræpˈtɪə.rɪks ˈɡræm.ɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
A European moth species, known for white antler-like markings on its forewings, whose larvae can damage pastures and grasslands.
a European moth whose larvae damage grasslands

Examples

Farmers often fear the outbreak of Cerapteryx graminis, as the larvae can devastate valuable grassland.
The distinctive white markings of Cerapteryx graminis help entomologists identify this moth species.
Research into controlling Cerapteryx graminis populations is important for protecting European pastures.

Etymology

From New Latin; Cerapteryx from Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'pteryx' (wing), graminis from Latin 'gramen' (grass).
