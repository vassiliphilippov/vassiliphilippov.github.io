etiquette

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛt̬.ɪ.kɛt / (AmE), / ˈɛtɪkɛt / (BrE)
The customary code of polite behavior in society or among the members of a particular profession or group.
rules of polite behavior

Examples

Good etiquette dictates that you should thank your host before leaving a party.
Proper table etiquette requires that you keep your elbows off the dining table.
She took a course on business etiquette to improve her professional interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protocol - official or formal rules of procedure or behavior
  • Synonym: decorum - behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety
  • Antonym: impropriety - failure to observe standards of expected behavior

Etymology

From French 'étiquette' (prescribed behavior), originally meaning 'label' or 'ticket'.
