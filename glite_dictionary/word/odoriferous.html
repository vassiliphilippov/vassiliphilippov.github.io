odoriferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊdəˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
having or emitting a strong or noticeable smell, often in a formal or humorous context
emitting a strong or noticeable smell

Examples

The odoriferous cheese filled the whole room with its potent aroma.
She wrinkled her nose at the odoriferous garbage left on the sidewalk.
The botanist described the odoriferous flowers that attract pollinators from miles away.

Synset Examples

odorous salt pork and weevily hardtack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odorous - having or giving off a smell
  • Degree Higher: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin odorifer (giving odor) + -ous.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊdəˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
having or giving off a pleasant or distinctive smell; fragrant.
having a pleasant fragrance

Examples

The odoriferous blossoms filled the evening air with a sweet aroma.
She kept a jar of odoriferous spices in her kitchen for cooking.
The garden was lush with odoriferous herbs and colorful flowers.

Synset Examples

odoriferous spices
the odorous air of the orchard
the perfumed air of June
scented flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Synonym: odorous - having a smell or odor (pleasant or unpleasant)

Etymology

From Latin odorifer, from odōr (smell) + -fer (bearing).
