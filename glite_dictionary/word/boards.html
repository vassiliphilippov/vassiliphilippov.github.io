boards

1. NOUN

/ bɔrdz / (AmE), / bɔːdz / (BrE)
The wooden or insulated panels that form the outer barrier around an ice hockey rink.
barrier around an ice hockey rink

Examples

The puck ricocheted off the boards and landed in front of the net.
He slammed his opponent into the boards during the heated third period.
Spectators pressed up against the boards to get a closer view of the action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rink - the ice surface on which ice hockey is played

2. NOUN

/ bɔrdz / (AmE), / bɔːdz / (BrE)
the stage of a theatre, especially when referred to in the context of performance (used in the plural)
theatre stage (plural, idiomatic)

Examples

After years away, she finally returned to the boards to star in a new play.
He dreams of treading the boards in London's West End one day.
Many famous actors started their careers on the boards of small local theaters.

Synset Examples

most actors love to stride the boards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage - the area in a theatre where actors perform

Etymology

Refers to the traditional wooden boards that make up a theatre stage.
