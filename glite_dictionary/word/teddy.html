teddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛdi / (AmE), / ˈtɛdi / (BrE)
A woman's sleeveless, one-piece undergarment that combines a camisole and panties, usually made of light fabric and often worn as lingerie or sleepwear.
sleeveless one-piece women's undergarment

Examples

She bought a silk teddy to wear on her wedding night.
The store has a variety of teddies in different colors and styles.
Her red lace teddy was both elegant and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camisole - a woman's sleeveless undergarment for the upper body
  • Synonym: chemise - a woman's loose-fitting undergarment or dress
  • Antonym: corset - a tightly fitting undergarment extending from below the chest to the hips

Etymology

From the personal name 'Teddy,' possibly referring to the 'teddy bear,' denoting comfort or affection, but origin is uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛdi / (AmE), / ˈtɛdi / (BrE)
A soft toy bear, usually made of plush fabric and stuffed with soft material, often given to children.
a soft toy bear

Examples

She hugged her favorite teddy tightly as she drifted off to sleep.
His childhood teddy still sits on the shelf in his bedroom.
The little girl brought her teddy to show and tell at school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teddy bear - a soft toy bear stuffed with soft materials

Etymology

Short for 'teddy bear', named after U.S. President Theodore 'Teddy' Roosevelt.
