sidewise

1. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdwaɪz / (BrE)
from or towards the side; at an angle or obliquely.
from or towards the side; obliquely

Examples

She glanced sidewise at her friend with a knowing smile.
The tree had grown sidewise after being struck by lightning.
He pushed the chair sidewise to make more space in the room.

Synset Examples

a picture lit sideways
scenes viewed sidewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - from or towards the side; obliquely

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + the adverbial suffix '-wise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00456343-r

2. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwaɪz / (BrE)
with one side leading or turned to the front, rather than facing forward directly
with one side forward

Examples

The crab moved sidewise across the sand, barely making a sound.
She glanced sidewise at her friend before speaking.
The artist turned the canvas sidewise to fit it through the doorway.

Synset Examples

turned sideways to show the profile
crabs seeming to walk sidewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - with one side nearer to a particular point; with one side leading

Etymology

/ ˈsaɪd.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.waɪz / (BrE)
In a lateral direction; towards or from one side.
in a lateral direction

Examples

She glanced sidewise at her friend, trying not to laugh.
The box shifted sidewise as the floor tilted unexpectedly.
