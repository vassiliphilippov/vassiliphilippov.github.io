viewpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvjuːˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a particular way of thinking about or considering something; a perspective or point of view
a particular perspective or point of view

Examples

It is important to consider different viewpoints when making decisions.
Her viewpoint on climate change differs from that of her colleagues.
From his viewpoint, the project was a complete success.

Synset Examples

we should consider this problem from the viewpoint of the Russians
teaching history gave him a special point of view toward current events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - a way of thinking about something, similar in meaning to viewpoint
  • Synonym: point of view - an opinion, attitude, or judgment, especially in relation to how something is considered

Etymology

From 'view' + 'point', meaning the point or position from which something is seen or considered.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvjuːˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place from which something can be viewed, often offering a good overall perspective
place offering a view

Examples

We stopped at a viewpoint along the highway to take photos of the mountains.
The trail leads up to a viewpoint overlooking the lake and the surrounding valleys.
From this viewpoint, you can see the city skyline stretching out before you.

Synset Examples

from that vantage point he could survey the whole valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vantage point - a place or position affording a good view

Etymology

From 'view' + 'point', first attested in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08694988-n
Synset POS: n
