rattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈræt.əl / (BrE)
the loosely connected horny segments at the end of a rattlesnake's tail that make a rattling sound when vibrated
horny segments at end of rattlesnake's tail

Examples

The rattle on a rattlesnake's tail is used to warn potential predators.
After shedding its skin, the snake's rattle gained an extra segment.
He examined the rattle closely, counting the individual sections to estimate the snake's age.

2. VERB

/ ˈræt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈræt.əl / (BrE)
to shake or make something shake, causing a series of short, sharp noises
shake and make a rattling noise

Examples

He began to rattle the coins in his pocket nervously.
The wind would rattle the windows during every storm.
Please don't rattle the cage; it frightens the bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move or make something move up and down or from side to side with small, quick movements

Etymology

From Middle English 'ratelen', of imitative origin, reflecting the sound.

3. VERB

/ ˈrætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrætəl / (BrE)
to make a series of short, sharp, knocking or tapping sounds, often by rapidly shaking or hitting something
make a series of short, sharp sounds

Examples

The wind made the old windows rattle all night long.
She could hear the coins rattling in his pocket as he walked.
The train rattled over the bridge, making a loud noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clatter - to make a loud, rattling sound, often by hard objects striking each other
  • Synonym: bang - to make a sudden loud noise
  • Degree Lower: rumble - to make a deep, continuous, resonant sound

Etymology

From Middle English 'ratelen', of imitative origin, representing the sound.

4. NOUN

/ ˈræɾl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrætl / (BrE)
A rapid series of short, loud sounds, especially as heard in the chest with a stethoscope in certain respiratory conditions.
short, loud sound in chest (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor noticed a rattle in the patient's chest during the examination.
A persistent chest rattle can indicate an underlying respiratory problem.
The nurse listened closely for any sign of a rattle through the stethoscope.

Synset Examples

/ ˈrætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrætl̩ / (BrE)

a baby's toy that makes noise when shaken

Examples

The brightly colored rattle quickly became her favorite toy.
He bought a soft rattle as a gift for his newborn niece.
