paschal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæskəl / (AmE), / ˈpæskəl / (BrE)
Relating to Passover or Easter, especially as celebrated in Christian or Jewish tradition.
relating to Passover or Easter

Examples

The church was decorated with white lilies for the paschal celebration.
During the paschal season, many Christians reflect on the themes of renewal and hope.
The tradition of the paschal lamb dates back centuries and holds deep religious significance.

Synset Examples

paschal lamb

Etymology

From Latin paschalis, from Greek paskha, from Hebrew pesach (Passover).
