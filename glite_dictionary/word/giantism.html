giantism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪəntˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪəntɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The condition of abnormally large growth or excessive height in a living being, especially as a medical disorder.
abnormal largeness of stature
medical

Examples

The rare disease known as giantism caused him to reach a height of over seven feet.
Doctors monitored the child's growth closely for signs of giantism.
Some animals exhibit giantism when living on isolated islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantism - the condition of excessive growth; another term for giantism

Etymology

From 'giant' + 'ism', modelled after the mythical giants of folklore.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪəntɪzəm / (BrE)
A condition of excessive growth and height due to the overproduction of growth hormone, especially by the pituitary gland.
abnormally large growth caused by excess growth hormone
medical

Examples

The rare disorder known as giantism can cause extreme height and physical features.
Doctors diagnosed the child with giantism after noticing abnormal growth patterns.
Untreated giantism can lead to serious health complications later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantism - excessive growth due to hormonal imbalance, especially in children

Etymology

From 'giant' + '-ism', referring to the mythic giants noted for their size.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14391693-n
Synset POS: n
