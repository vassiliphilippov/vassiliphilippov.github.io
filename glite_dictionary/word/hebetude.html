hebetude

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛbəˌtud / (AmE), / ˈhɛbɪtjuːd / (BrE)
The state of mental dullness or lethargy; sluggishness or lack of alertness.
mental dullness or lethargy

Examples

A sense of hebetude settled over the audience as the lecture dragged on.
After days without sleep, he was overcome by a heavy hebetude.
The treatment helped to dispel the hebetude that clouded her mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: alertness - the state of being quick to perceive and act

Etymology

From Latin hebetudo (dullness), from hebes (dull, blunt).
