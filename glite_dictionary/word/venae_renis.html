venae renis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnaɪ ˈreɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːni ˈreːnɪs / (BrE)
the veins of the kidney that drain blood from the kidney into the renal vein
veins draining the kidney
medical

Examples

The venae renis play a crucial role in draining blood from the kidneys.
Any obstruction in the venae renis can cause significant kidney complications.
The surgeon identified the venae renis before proceeding with the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renal vein - the vein that carries blood away from the kidney to the inferior vena cava

Etymology

Latin, plural of vena renis ('vein' of 'kidney')
