barracking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbærəkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shouting comments, usually to interrupt or criticize a speaker, especially to show disagreement
shouting to interrupt or criticize a speaker

Examples

The politician remained calm despite the barracking from the crowd.
Loud barracking made it difficult for the speaker to finish his remarks.
Security intervened when the barracking threatened to disrupt the entire event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heckling - the act of interrupting a speaker with critical or mocking comments

Etymology

From 'barrack' (to jeer or shout at), of uncertain origin; possibly from British slang.
