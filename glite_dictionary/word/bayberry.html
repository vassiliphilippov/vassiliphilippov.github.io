bayberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbeɪˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous aromatic shrub native to eastern North America, producing grey-green berries coated with a waxy substance.
aromatic North American shrub with waxy berries

Examples

The candles in the shop were scented with bayberry oil.
She planted a bayberry bush along the walkway for its fragrance.
Wildlife often feeds on the bayberry berries during the winter months.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbəri / (BrE)
A tropical West Indian tree (Pimenta racemosa), the leaves of which are the source of bay rum.
West Indian tree, source of bay rum

Examples

The leaves of the bayberry are distilled to produce bay rum.
Local farmers harvest bayberries for their aromatic oils.
The bayberry thrives in the warm climates of the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay rum - a fragrant liquid used in cosmetics, prepared from the leaves of the bayberry tree
