Papaver

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə / (BrE)
The type genus of the poppy family (Papaveraceae), comprising chiefly bristly, hairy herbs with usually showy flowers, including the common poppy.
type genus of the poppy family

Examples

Several ornamental species of Papaver are cultivated for their bright and attractive flowers.
The genus Papaver includes the opium poppy, which has considerable economic importance.
Botanists gathered samples of Papaver from the wild to study their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a plant of the Papaver genus with showy flowers, often red

Etymology

From Latin 'papaver' meaning 'poppy'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the poppy family (Papaveraceae), typically bristly, hairy herbs with showy flowers.
genus of poppy plants

Examples

The opium poppy is a well-known species within the Papaver genus.
Botanists discovered a new Papaver species in the mountain region.
Many Papaver flowers are cultivated for ornamental purposes worldwide.

Etymology

From Latin papaver (poppy).
