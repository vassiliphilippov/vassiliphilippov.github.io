stray

1. NOUN

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
a domestic animal that has wandered away from its home or owner and is lost, especially a cat or dog
a lost domestic animal

Examples

The animal shelter takes in abandoned strays from all over the city.
She adopted a stray that appeared at her door during a storm.
Volunteers feed the strays that live near the marketplace.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: feral - a wild animal, especially one descended from domesticated individuals

Etymology

From Old French 'estraier' (to wander), based on Latin 'extra' (outside).

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
(of an animal) having no home or having wandered away from its home.
homeless or lost (animal)

Examples

The shelter took in a stray cat that had been living on the street.
A stray dog wandered into our backyard last night.
Volunteers care for stray animals until they can find a new home.

Synset Examples

a stray calf
a stray dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeless - without a home; having no permanent place to live

Etymology

From Middle English 'straien', from Anglo-Norman 'estraier', from Vulgar Latin '*extraviare' (to wander from the road).

3. VERB

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
to move away from the correct or intended place or path, or to wander without a clear direction
to wander from a path or course

Examples

If you stray from the trail, it's easy to get lost in the woods.
She warned her son not to stray too far from the playground.
His thoughts often stray during long meetings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
  • Synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by a current of air or water; to move aimlessly
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in the same place

Etymology

From Old French 'estraier' (to wander), from Vulgar Latin *estragare, from Latin 'extra' (outside).
