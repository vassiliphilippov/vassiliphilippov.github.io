uricosuric

1. ADJ

/ ˌjʊrɪkoʊˈsjʊrɪk / (AmE), / ˌjʊərɪkəʊˈsjʊərɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) promoting increased excretion of uric acid in the urine.
promoting uric acid excretion in urine
medical

Examples

Some medications have a uricosuric effect and help lower uric acid levels in patients with gout.
Doctors may prescribe uricosuric drugs to increase the removal of uric acid through urine.
The patient's response to the uricosuric agent was closely monitored in the clinical trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antilithic - preventing the formation of stones in the body

Etymology

From uric (relating to uric acid) + suric (from Latin 'urin-' meaning urine, and Greek 'sourikos', relating to urine).
