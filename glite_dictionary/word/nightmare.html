nightmare

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt.mɛr / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.mɛə / (BrE)
A very frightening or unpleasant dream.
frightening dream

Examples

She woke up suddenly from a nightmare, her heart pounding.
The little boy was afraid to sleep because he kept having the same nightmare.
After the accident, he began suffering from frequent nightmares.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dream - a series of thoughts, images, and sensations occurring in a person's mind during sleep
  • Degree Higher: night terror - a sleep disorder causing feelings of terror or dread during sleep

Etymology

From Middle English 'nihtemare', from Old English 'niht' (night) + 'mare' (goblin causing nightmares).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌmɛr / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.mɛə / (BrE)
An extremely unpleasant or difficult situation that causes anxiety, distress, or fear, resembling a terrifying dream.
very unpleasant or distressing situation

Examples

Moving to a new city without knowing anyone was a complete nightmare for her at first.
Trying to fix the leaking pipe on my own turned into a plumbing nightmare.
For the organizers, the unexpected storm was a logistical nightmare that disrupted all their plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or horrific experience, especially a protracted one
  • Antonym: dream - a series of thoughts, images, and sensations during sleep

Etymology

From Old English 'niht' (night) + 'mære' (goblin or incubus).
