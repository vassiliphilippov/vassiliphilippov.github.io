Severn

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛvərn / (AmE), / ˈsɛvən / (BrE)
a river in England and Wales that flows into the Bristol Channel; the longest river in Great Britain
the longest river in Great Britain

Examples

The Severn often floods during heavy rains in the region.
Many towns along the Severn benefit from its natural beauty and resources.
The Severn is famous for having the greatest tidal range in the UK.

Etymology

From the Latin Sabrina, of pre-Roman origin, referring to the river.
