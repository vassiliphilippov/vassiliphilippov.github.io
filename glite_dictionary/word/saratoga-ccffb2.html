saratoga

1. NOUN

/ ˌsærəˈtoʊɡə / (AmE), / ˌsærəˈtəʊɡə / (BrE)
A species of large freshwater fish found in Australian rivers, Scleropages leichardti, known for the red or pink spot on each scale.
Australian freshwater fish species

Examples

The saratoga is prized by Australian anglers for its striking appearance and fighting spirit.
Researchers are studying the habitat of the saratoga to ensure its conservation in Queensland rivers.
A mature saratoga can grow to more than a meter in length in the wild.
