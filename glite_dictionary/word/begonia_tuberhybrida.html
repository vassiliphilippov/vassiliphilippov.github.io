Begonia tuberhybrida

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡoʊni.ə ˌtuːbərhaɪˈbrɪdə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊni.ə ˌtjuːbəhʌɪˈbrɪdə / (BrE)
A group of hybrid begonias with tuberous roots, widely grown for their brightly colored flowers.
hybrid begonia with tuberous roots

Examples

The Begonia tuberhybrida is prized for its spectacular, long-lasting blooms in summer gardens.
She planted several Begonia tuberhybrida in hanging baskets for a splash of color on her porch.
Garden centers often carry a wide variety of Begonia tuberhybrida cultivars with different flower colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begonia - a genus of flowering plants, often grown as ornamentals

Etymology

From Latin 'Begonia' (genus name, named after Michel Bégon) and 'tuberhybrida' referring to tuberous hybrid.
