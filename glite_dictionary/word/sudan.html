Sudan

1. PROPN

/ suˈdæn / (AmE), / suˈdɑːn / (BrE)
A country in northeastern Africa, bordering the Red Sea, which became independent from Egypt and the United Kingdom in 1956.
country in northeastern Africa

Examples

The Nile flows through the heart of Sudan, supporting agriculture and communities along its banks.
After years of conflict, Sudan signed a peace agreement with neighboring countries.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient pyramids in Sudan dating back thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sudanese - relating to Sudan or its people

Etymology

From Arabic 'as-Sūdān' meaning 'the blacks', referring historically to Sub-Saharan Africa.

2. PROPN

/ suːˈdæn / (AmE), / suːˈdɑːn / (BrE)
a vast region of northern Africa, south of the Sahara, extending from the Atlantic Ocean to the Red Sea
region of northern Africa south of the Sahara

Examples

The Sahel forms a transition between the Sahara and the Sudan region.
Archaeological research in the Sudan has revealed important insights into early African civilizations.
Rainfall is more abundant in the Sudan than in the arid deserts to its north.

Etymology

From Arabic as-sūdān, meaning 'the blacks' (land of the blacks).
