Araucaria heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.rəˈkɛr.i.ə ˌhɛ.tə.rəˈfaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈkeə.ri.ə ˌhɛ.tə.rəˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
an evergreen coniferous tree native to Norfolk Island and eastern Australia, commonly known as the Norfolk Island pine and often grown as an ornamental plant.
Norfolk Island pine tree species

Examples

The Araucaria heterophylla is often grown as a decorative houseplant in temperate regions.
During our trip to Norfolk Island, we saw many tall specimens of Araucaria heterophylla.
The hotel lobby featured a large potted Araucaria heterophylla that reached up to the ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norfolk Island pine - common name for Araucaria heterophylla

Etymology

From New Latin; Araucaria, the genus, named after Arauco in Chile, plus heterophylla from Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
