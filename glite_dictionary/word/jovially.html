jovially

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒoʊ.vi.əli / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.əli / (BrE)
In a cheerful and friendly manner; with good humor.
in a cheerful, friendly way

Examples

Despite the bad weather, he spoke jovially to everyone at the picnic.
She jovially welcomed the new interns to the office, making them feel right at home.
The host greeted each guest jovially, setting a pleasant tone for the evening.

Synset Examples

he greeted his friend jovially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfully - in a cheerful manner
  • Antonym: gloomily - in a gloomy, downcast manner

Etymology

From jovial + -ly, ultimately from Latin Iovialis (of Jupiter), associated with good humor.
