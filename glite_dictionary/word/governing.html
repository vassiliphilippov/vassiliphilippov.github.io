governing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of controlling or directing according to rule, law, or authority
the act of exercising authority

Examples

The governing of the country requires both wisdom and experience.
Effective governing depends on the fair enforcement of laws.
She studied public policy with a focus on the ethics of governing.

Synset Examples

regulations for the governing of state prisons
he had considerable experience of government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the system or group of people governing an organized community
  • Synonym: administration - the process or activity of running a business, organization, etc.

Etymology

From the verb 'govern', from Old French 'governer', from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, direct).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌvərnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ / (BrE)
Having authority to make and enforce rules, laws, or decisions; exercising control over an organization, country, or group.
having authority or control

Examples

The governing committee decided to update the organization's code of conduct.
Many countries have a governing body that oversees professional sports leagues.
The governing officials were responsible for implementing the new policy.

Synset Examples

governing bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruling - having control or authority over; currently in power
  • Antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or authority

Etymology

Present participle of 'govern', from Old French 'governer', from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, direct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00795630-s
Synset POS: s
