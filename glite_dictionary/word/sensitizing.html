sensitizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something susceptible or responsive to physical or emotional stimuli
making susceptible or responsive to stimuli

Examples

Exposure to certain chemicals can have a sensitizing effect on the skin, leading to allergic reactions.
The therapist used a sensitizing approach to help the patient become more aware of their emotions.
Repeated trauma can be sensitizing, increasing one's vulnerability to stress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitizing - making less sensitive or less responsive to stimuli

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making an organism sensitive to a substance, especially by repeated injections of that substance.
making an organism sensitive by repeated injections
medical

Examples

The treatment involved the sensitizing of patients to a potential allergen using controlled doses.
Repeated sensitizing is often necessary before the immune response becomes detectable.
In laboratory studies, sensitizing can help researchers observe specific allergic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitization - the process of reducing sensitivity in an organism to a substance
