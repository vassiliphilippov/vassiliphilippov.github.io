dedifferentiated

1. ADJ

/ ˌdiːˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
having lost the specialized characteristics of form or function, especially as a result of a pathological or developmental process
lacking specialized form or function
medical

Examples

In some cancers, dedifferentiated cells lose their original function and become more aggressive.
The pathologist noted that the tumor was highly dedifferentiated in appearance.
Researchers study the process by which specialized cells become dedifferentiated after injury.

Synset Examples

the hebephrenic — the most severely dedifferentiated of all schizophrenic patients

Related Terms

  • Antonym: differentiated - having developed specialized form or function

Etymology

From prefix de- (expressing removal) + differentiate.
