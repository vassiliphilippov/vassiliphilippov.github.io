autocrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtəˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəkræt / (BrE)
a ruler who holds absolute power, typically one who exercises it in a cruel or oppressive way
absolute ruler, often oppressive

Examples

The country's autocrat silenced all opposition to his rule.
Many feared that the president was becoming an autocrat after he suspended the constitution.
Throughout history, autocrats have often relied on military power to maintain control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force
  • Synonym: despot - a ruler who holds absolute power, often in a cruel or oppressive way

Etymology

From Greek autokratēs, from autos (self) + kratos (power, rule).
