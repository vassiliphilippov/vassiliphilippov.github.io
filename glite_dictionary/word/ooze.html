ooze

1. NOUN

/ uz / (AmE), / uːz / (BrE)
the slow movement or seepage of a liquid or substance, especially through small openings or porous material
slow seepage or movement of a substance

Examples

The ooze of water from the cracked pipe was barely noticeable at first.
Slow ooze from the cave walls created glistening streaks along the limestone.
A thick ooze started to spread across the floor after the container broke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seepage - the process of a liquid flowing slowly through a porous material or small holes

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'wose' (soft mud), related to Old English 'wōs' (juice, sap).

2. VERB

/ uz / (AmE), / uːz / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to pass or leak out slowly through small openings or holes.
to leak or pass out slowly

Examples

Thick black oil began to ooze from the cracks in the engine.
Sweat oozed down his forehead as he climbed the steep hill.
A sticky syrup oozed from the broken jar onto the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
  • Synonym: trickle - to flow in a small stream

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *wose* (soft mud).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02075902-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ uz / (AmE), / uːz / (BrE)
any thick, sticky liquid or semi-liquid substance, often of a soft, wet, or slimy nature.
thick, sticky liquid or substance

Examples

The surface of the pond was covered in a layer of green ooze.
As he opened the old can, a strange ooze slowly seeped out.
His boots got stuck in the thick ooze at the bottom of the swamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime - a slippery, viscous liquid substance
  • Synonym: sludge - thick, soft, wet mud or a similar viscous mixture

Etymology

Possibly imitative or from Old English 'wāse' (soft mud, slime).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14980800-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ uːz / (AmE), / uːz / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow or leak out slowly in drops or small quantities.
flow or leak slowly in small amounts

Examples

Sweat began to ooze from his forehead in the summer heat.
The sap slowly oozed out of the cut in the tree trunk.
A thick, dark sauce was oozing from the edges of the pie.

Synset Examples

exude sweat through the pores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exude - to discharge (moisture or a smell) slowly and steadily
  • Synonym: drip - to fall or let fall in drops
  • Degree Higher: gush - to flow out forcefully and in large amounts

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *wosen* (to ooze, to sap out), from Old English *wos* (juice, sap).
