stereophotography

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrioʊfəˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəʊfəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a method of photography that produces images with depth by combining two slightly different photographs, typically viewed with a stereoscope
photography producing 3D images with depth

Examples

The museum's exhibit included early examples of stereophotography from the Victorian era.
Advancements in stereophotography allowed scientists to study surfaces in greater detail.
Many collectors admire the intricate viewers used to enjoy stereophotography in the 19th century.

Synset Examples

That sparked the tremendous popularity of stereo photography and stereoscopes that lasted well into the later 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereoscopy - technique for creating or enhancing the illusion of depth in an image
  • Degree Lower: photography - the art or practice of taking and processing photographs

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'photography'.
