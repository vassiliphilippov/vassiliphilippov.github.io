Penstemon newberryi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnstəˌmɑn nuːˈbɛri.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɛnstɪˌmɒn njuːˈbɛri.aɪ / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial plant with deep pink flowers on short, erect leafy stems, found in rocky areas at high elevations from Oregon to California.
mountainous plant with pink flowers of western U.S.

Examples

The brilliant pink flowers of Penstemon newberryi stand out on rocky slopes during early summer.
Gardeners in California prize Penstemon newberryi for its hardiness and showy blooms.
While hiking above the tree line, we encountered a patch of Penstemon newberryi thriving among granite boulders.

Etymology

Named after botanist John Strong Newberry; genus from Greek 'pénte' (five) and 'stēmōn' (thread, stamen).
