to agonise

1. VERB

/ ˈæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to suffer extreme physical or mental pain or anguish
suffer extreme pain or anguish

Examples

He continued to agonise over the decision long after everyone else had moved on.
She agonised for hours before finally accepting the offer.
The patient was left to agonise in pain due to the delayed treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffer - to experience or feel pain or distress
  • Synonym: to writhe - to twist or squirm in pain or discomfort
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

From Greek agonizesthai (to contend for a prize), from agon (contest).

2. VERB

/ ˈæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
(British spelling) to cause someone to suffer intense mental pain or anguish.
cause to suffer mental anguish
british

Examples

The uncertainty will only agonise her further if we do not tell her the truth soon.
He did not intend to agonise his children with frightening stories, but they were deeply disturbed.
The teacher's criticism seemed designed to agonise anyone who made the slightest mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
  • Antonym: comfort - to ease the grief or distress of

Etymology

From Greek agonizesthai (to contend, struggle), from agōn (contest).
