decipherer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪfərər / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪf(ə)rə / (BrE)
a person who converts messages or texts from a code or obscured form into plain language; someone who deciphers
person who deciphers codes or texts

Examples

She became famous as a skilled decipherer of ancient scripts.
The wartime decipherer played a vital role in intercepting enemy communications.
As a decipherer, he spends hours trying to make sense of mysterious manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoder - person who converts coded messages into plain text
  • Antonym: encoder - person who converts plain text into code or cipher

Etymology

From decipher + -er, formed after French 'déchiffrer' (to decode).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪfərər / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪfərə / (BrE)
a person who is able to read and interpret illegible or obscure writing or codes
person skilled at reading obscure text

Examples

The decipherer spent years unraveling the meaning of ancient manuscripts.
Rosetta Stone was key for the decipherer of Egyptian hieroglyphics.
A skilled decipherer can make sense of even the most convoluted handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoder - a person who converts coded text into a readable form
  • Synonym: interpreter - a person who interprets or translates written or spoken text

Etymology

From the verb 'decipher', which comes from Medieval French 'déchiffrer', based on 'chiffre' (cipher).
