insolubility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌsɑːljuˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌsɒl.jʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible or difficult to dissolve in a liquid
being impossible or hard to dissolve

Examples

The insolubility of the compound made it difficult to use in the experiment.
One of the main challenges in drug design is overcoming insolubility in water.
The insolubility of certain minerals is the reason why they remain visible in riverbeds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solubility - the ability to dissolve in a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'insolubilis' (not able to be loosened or dissolved), from 'in-' (not) + 'solubilis' (able to be loosened).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌsɑːl.juˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌsɒl.jʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of a problem or difficulty being impossible to solve
impossibility of being solved

Examples

The insolubility of the issue frustrated even the most experienced negotiators.
Historians have discussed the insolubility of certain long-standing conflicts.
Her team grappled with the apparent insolubility of the technical challenge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solubility - the ability to be solved
  • Synonym: insoluble - impossible to solve

Etymology

From Latin insolubilis (not able to be loosened or solved).
