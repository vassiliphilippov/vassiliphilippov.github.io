Branta

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræn.tə / (AmE), / ˈbræn.tə / (BrE)
A genus of wild geese that includes several species found in the Northern Hemisphere, known for their dark plumage and migratory habits.
genus of wild geese

Examples

The Canada goose, known scientifically as Branta canadensis, is widespread across North America.
Several species within the genus Branta are noted for their striking black and white coloring.
Researchers observed a flock of Branta migrating south for the winter.

Etymology

From Latin 'branta', a name for a kind of goose, of uncertain origin; used in scientific taxonomy.
