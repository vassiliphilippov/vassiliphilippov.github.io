kneeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːlər / (AmE), / ˈniːlə / (BrE)
a person who is in a kneeling position, especially while praying or performing a task
person who is kneeling

Examples

The church was quiet except for the single kneeler praying by the altar.
Each kneeler at the ceremony bowed their head in silence.
A kneeler on the grass watched the game from the sidelines.

Etymology

From 'kneel' (to bend the knee) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːlər / (AmE), / ˈniːlə / (BrE)
A cushion or board, sometimes padded, designed for someone to kneel on, especially during prayer or religious ceremonies.
cushioned board for kneeling on

Examples

The church installed a new kneeler in each pew for the congregation's comfort during prayer.
She placed a kneeler in her garden to make weeding less strenuous on her knees.
As he entered the chapel, he rested on the kneeler before starting his prayers.

Etymology

From 'kneel' + '-er', forming a noun indicating something used for kneeling.
