panorama

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˈræmə / (AmE), / ˌpænəˈrɑːmə / (BrE)
a picture or series of pictures showing a wide, continuous view of a scene
picture showing a wide, continuous view

Examples

The photographer captured a stunning panorama of the mountain range at sunrise.
We admired the panorama displayed at the art gallery, which stretched across an entire wall.
She stitched together several images to create a seamless panorama of the city skyline.

Etymology

From Greek ‘pan’ (all) + ‘horama’ (view), meaning ‘all-seeing’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˈræmə / (AmE), / ˌpænəˈrɑːmə / (BrE)
An unbroken view of a wide area, especially one seen from a high place.
wide, unbroken view of an area

Examples

We stopped at the viewpoint to admire the breathtaking panorama of the mountains.
From the tower, you can see a beautiful panorama of the entire city.
She captured the panorama with her camera to remember the stunning landscape.

Synset Examples

the most desirable feature of the park are the beautiful views

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vista - a pleasing view, especially one seen through a long, narrow opening
  • Synonym: panoramic - related adjective meaning giving a wide view
  • Antonym: close-up - a photograph or view of something at close range

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'horama' (view, sight).
