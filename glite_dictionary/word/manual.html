manual

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈmanjuəl / (BrE)
relating to or done with the hands; involving physical work with the hands.
relating to the hands

Examples

The surgeon's manual skills are essential for delicate operations.
This job requires manual labor rather than using machines.
He demonstrated great manual dexterity when fixing the watch.

Synset Examples

manual dexterity

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis,' from 'manus' (hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02769962-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈmanjuəl / (BrE)
requiring or involving physical work rather than mental effort or machinery
requiring physical work

Examples

He found it exhausting to move from office tasks to manual labor in the warehouse.
The company reduced the number of manual jobs by introducing automated machines.
A manual worker may experience more physical strain than someone with a desk job.

Synset Examples

manual labor
manual laborer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mental - relating to the mind rather than physical activity

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand), from 'manus' (hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02567793-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.ju.əl / (AmE), / ˈman.ju.əl / (BrE)
A book or booklet that gives practical instructions on how to do something or use something.
instruction book or handbook

Examples

The user manual provides step-by-step instructions for assembling the furniture.
Please refer to the training manual if you have any questions about the machine.
She lost the instruction manual that came with her new camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handbook - a concise reference book providing specific information
  • Synonym: guide - a book giving information or instructions

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand), from 'manus' (hand).

Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈmæn.ju.əl / (AmE), / ˈman.ju.əl / (BrE)
(of a device or task) operated or done by a person rather than automatically or by using a machine.
operated by hand or human effort

Examples

The old car had a manual gearbox that required the driver to shift gears manually.
Many jobs in this factory are still manual, involving lifting and carrying heavy boxes.
Switching from an automatic to a manual coffee grinder gives you more control over the process.

Synset Examples

a manual transmission

Related Terms

  • Antonym: automatic - operated by a machine, without human intervention

Etymology

From Latin manualis, from manus (hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00184143-a

5. NOUN

/ ˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈman.ju.əl / (BrE)
(military) a prescribed set of movements or drill in handling a rifle or weapon, performed by soldiers as training or for ceremonial purposes.
military drill in rifle handling

Examples

The soldiers practiced the manual before the inspection.
During the ceremony, each cadet executed the manual flawlessly.
Mastering the manual was essential for new recruits in the regiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - routine training exercise in the military
