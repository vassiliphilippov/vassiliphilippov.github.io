crudeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkruːdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being in a natural, rough, or unrefined condition
state of being unrefined or rough

Examples

The crudeness of the unfinished sculpture gave it a powerful, primitive appeal.
Despite the crudeness of their first attempt, the team was proud of their accomplishment.
Early settlements were often marked by the crudeness of their tools and shelters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rawness - the quality of being raw or unfinished; lack of refinement
  • Antonym: refinement - the process of removing impurities or improving something

Etymology

From crude + -ness; 'crude' from Latin 'crudus' meaning 'raw, rough'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkruːdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpolished, unrefined, or lacking sophistication
quality of being unpolished or unrefined

Examples

The crudeness of the handmade pottery gave it a unique charm.
She was surprised by the crudeness of his first draft before editing.
The film's crudeness was apparent in its unpolished special effects.

Synset Examples

the crudeness of frontier dwellings depressed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rawness - the quality of being raw or lacking refinement
  • Antonym: refinement - the process of removing impurities or making something more polished

Etymology

From crude + -ness, denoting the state or quality of being crude.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkruːdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkruːdnəs / (BrE)
behavior or speech that is rude, vulgar, or lacking in tact and refinement.
lack of refinement or tact; vulgarity

Examples

The comedian's jokes were criticized for their crudeness and lack of sensitivity.
Many people were offended by the crudeness of the comments made at the meeting.
Despite the crudeness of his manners, he was honest and hardworking.

Synset Examples

the whole town was famous for its crudeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - rudeness or offensiveness; lack of refinement in manners or speech
  • Synonym: coarseness - lack of delicacy or refinement
  • Antonym: refinement - elegance and politeness in manners or speech

Etymology

From Latin crudus (raw, rough) via Middle English crudenes.
