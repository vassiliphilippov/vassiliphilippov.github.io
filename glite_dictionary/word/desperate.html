desperate

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛspɚɪt / (AmE), / ˈdes.p(ə).rət / (BrE)
(of a person) behaving recklessly or violently because of extreme urgency or despair.
reckless or violent from urgency or despair

Examples

The desperate fugitive risked everything to escape the police.
Faced with bankruptcy, he made a desperate attempt to rob a store.
The desperate men threatened violence unless their demands were met.

Synset Examples

a desperate criminal
taken hostage of desperate men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - showing a lack of care about danger or consequences
  • Synonym: hopeless - having no hope; despairing
  • Antonym: cautious - being careful to avoid danger or risks

Etymology

From Latin desperatus, past participle of desperare (to lose hope).
