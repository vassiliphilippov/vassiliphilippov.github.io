instroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈɪn.strəʊk / (BrE)
The movement of an engine piston away from the crankshaft during a stroke.
engine piston movement away from crankshaft

Examples

During the instroke of the piston, the cylinder fills with the air-fuel mixture.
Engineers measured the pressure at both the instroke and outstroke for optimal performance.
Wear and tear are most noticeable after repeated instrokes in high-speed engines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outstroke - the stroke of an engine piston moving toward the crankshaft

Etymology

From 'in-' meaning 'toward the inside' + 'stroke' (a movement or blow).
