firebomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɒm / (BrE)
A bomb designed to start fires, especially effective against flammable targets such as fuel or buildings.
bomb designed to cause fires

Examples

The aircraft dropped a firebomb on the warehouse, causing massive destruction.
Protesters claimed that a firebomb had been thrown through the shop window overnight.
During the war, entire neighborhoods were destroyed by firebombs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incendiary bomb - bomb containing flammable substances and designed to start fires

2. VERB

/ ˈfaɪ.ɚˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɒm / (BrE)
to attack a place or vehicle by throwing or planting an incendiary bomb.
attack with an incendiary bomb

Examples

A protest group threatened to firebomb the police station if their demands were not met.
Several cars were firebombed during the unrest across the city last night.
The suspects admitted to firebombing the warehouse in an act of retaliation.

Synset Examples

The rioters fire-bombed the stores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - to attack using any kind of bomb

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'bomb'; based on the noun 'firebomb'.
