Phyllidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as leaf insects, characterized by their broad, flat bodies that resemble leaves, aiding in camouflage.
family of leaf insects

Examples

The Phyllidae are masters of camouflage, blending seamlessly into their leafy surroundings.
Many species in the Phyllidae family are found in tropical forests of Southeast Asia.
Researchers study Phyllidae to understand the evolution of mimicry in insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf insect - insects that resemble leaves, typically members of Phylliidae

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' meaning 'leaf', referring to the leaf-like appearance of these insects.
