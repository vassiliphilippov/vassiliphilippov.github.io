grandstand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)stænd / (BrE)
the main seating area for spectators at a stadium, racetrack, or similar venue
main seating area for spectators

Examples

The roar from the grandstand could be heard for miles.
Fans filled every seat in the grandstand for the championship game.
She waved to her family as she walked past the crowded grandstand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleachers - outdoor stands for spectators, often uncovered

Etymology

From 'grand' (large, impressive) + 'stand' (structure for sitting or standing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrændˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈɡrændˌstænd / (BrE)
A large seating structure at a racecourse, stadium, or sports ground, consisting of multiple tiers of individual seats that are often covered by a roof.
tiers of seats at a sports venue, usually roofed

Examples

The fans filled the grandstand long before the match began to get the best views.
She waved to her family, who were seated high up in the grandstand overlooking the finish line.
Rain poured down, but those in the grandstand stayed dry under its wide roof.

Etymology

From 'grand' (imposing) + 'stand' (structure for spectators), first used in the 19th century for sports venues.

3. VERB

/ ˈɡrænˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)ˌstænd / (BrE)
to behave or perform in a showy or ostentatious manner, especially to attract attention or impress others
perform in a showy way to impress

Examples

The politician was accused of trying to grandstand during the debate to win public admiration.
Rather than focus on the facts, she preferred to grandstand for the cameras whenever possible.
Critics complain that some athletes grandstand after scoring, turning celebrations into personal performances.

Synset Examples

She never misses a chance to grandstand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration, often without merit
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or impressive

Etymology

From the noun 'grandstand', originally referring to the best seats at sporting events, implying playing for the best view or effect.
