Breton

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛt.ən / (AmE), / ˈbrɛt.ən / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken in Brittany, in northwestern France
Celtic language of Brittany, France

Examples

There has been a recent revival in the use of Breton in schools in Brittany.
She is fluent in both French and Breton.
Many traditional songs in Brittany are sung in Breton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh - Celtic language spoken in Wales
  • Synonym: Cornish - Celtic language formerly spoken in Cornwall

Etymology

From French *breton* (Breton), from Latin *Britto* (a Briton, a Briton of Brittany).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛtən / (AmE), / ˈbret.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Brittany, especially one who speaks the Breton language
native of Brittany, France

Examples

Many Bretons still speak their traditional Celtic language.
The festival celebrates the rich heritage of the Breton people.
She traveled to Brittany to learn more about Breton customs and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Briton - a native or inhabitant of Britain

Etymology

From French 'Breton', from Latin 'Britto', meaning 'a Briton'.
