Caricaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæɹɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical trees and shrubs, native to America and Africa, characterized by milky sap and large, palmately lobed leaves.
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The papaya plant belongs to the Caricaceae family, which is native to tropical regions.
Botanists study the diverse fruit-bearing members of the Caricaceae.
Some species in Caricaceae have been cultivated for their edible fruit and medicinal properties.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Carica (type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˈkeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees and shrubs native to America and Africa, characterized by milky sap and large palmately lobed leaves
tropical plant family with milky sap

Examples

The Caricaceae family includes the economically important papaya tree.
Botanists are studying several new species within Caricaceae.
Plants in Caricaceae produce a distinctive milky sap when cut.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Carica', referring to the papaya tree, plus the botanical suffix '-aceae' for plant families.
