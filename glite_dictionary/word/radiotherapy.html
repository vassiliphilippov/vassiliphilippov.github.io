radiotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈθerəpi / (BrE)
the treatment of disease, especially cancer, using controlled exposure to radioactive substances or radiation.
treatment of disease with radiation
medical

Examples

The hospital offers radiotherapy to patients with various types of cancer.
Side effects of radiotherapy can include fatigue and skin irritation.
After surgery, doctors recommended a course of radiotherapy to ensure all cancer cells were destroyed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation therapy - the use of high-energy radiation to treat cancer and other diseases

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radiation) + therapy (from Greek therapeia, healing).
