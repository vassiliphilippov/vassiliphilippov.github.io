solicitor

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsɪtɚ / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪtə / (BrE)
(in the UK and some other countries) a lawyer who gives legal advice, prepares legal documents, and may represent clients in lower courts.
British lawyer giving legal advice
british legal

Examples

After consulting with her solicitor, she decided to proceed with the purchase of the house.
The solicitor prepared all the necessary documents for the business contract.
He works as a solicitor for a well-known law firm in London.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts (UK)
  • Synonym: lawyer - a person who practices or studies law; an attorney or a counselor

Etymology

From Latin 'sollicitator' (one who urges, entreats), via Anglo-Norman and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsɪtər / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪtə / (BrE)
a person who asks for contributions, business, or support, especially by going from door to door or making persistent requests.
person who requests contributions or support

Examples

A solicitor came to my door asking me to support the local animal shelter.
The company hired a solicitor to gather signatures for their petition.
Many residents find the frequent visits from solicitors to be intrusive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canvasser - person who seeks support or votes, often by direct contact
  • Synonym: fundraiser - person who seeks donations or contributions for a cause
  • Antonym: donor - person who gives or donates, opposite role of solicitor

Etymology

From Latin 'sollicitātor' via Old French, from 'sollicitare' (to disturb, solicit).
