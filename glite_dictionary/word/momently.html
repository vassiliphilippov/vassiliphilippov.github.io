momently

1. ADV

/ ˈmoʊmən(t)li / (AmE), / ˈməʊmən(t)li / (BrE)
for a brief moment; for an instant; momentarily
for a very short time
archaic

Examples

The bird perched momently on the branch before flying away.
He was distracted momently by a sudden noise outside the window.
The sun appeared momently from behind the clouds and then disappeared again.

Synset Examples

we paused momentarily before proceeding
a cardinal perched momently on the dogwood branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentarily - for a very short time; briefly

Etymology

From 'moment' + '-ly', formed in analogy with 'momentarily'.

2. ADV

/ ˈmoʊməntli / (AmE), / ˈməʊməntli / (BrE)
at any moment; very soon
very soon; at any moment
archaic

Examples

She will be with you momently.
The meeting is expected to start momently, so please take your seats.
News of the decision should arrive momently, according to the spokesperson.

Synset Examples

she will be with you momently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentarily - for a moment; very soon; related adverb
  • Synonym: imminently - happening very soon; near in time

Etymology

From 'moment' + '-ly', patterned after 'momentarily', from Latin 'momentum'.
