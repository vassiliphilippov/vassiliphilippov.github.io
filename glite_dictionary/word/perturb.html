to perturb

1. VERB

/ pərˈtɜrb / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːb / (BrE)
(in astronomy and physics) To cause a celestial body to deviate from its expected or regular path, especially due to the gravitational influence of another object.
alter a celestial body's orbit

Examples

Small asteroids can perturb a planet’s orbit if they pass close enough.
The gravitational pull of Jupiter can significantly perturb the paths of nearby comets.
Astronomers observed that Neptune’s presence would perturb the orbits of other planets.

Synset Examples

The orbits of these stars were perturbed by the passings of a comet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disturb - to interfere with the normal arrangement or position of something

Etymology

From Latin perturbare (to confuse, disturb), from per- (thoroughly) + turbare (to disturb).

to disturb or alter the normal path, state, or behavior of something, especially in a scientific context such as an electron or atom
disturb from a normal path, esp. in science

Examples

When the system is perturbed by an external force, its natural motion is disrupted.
