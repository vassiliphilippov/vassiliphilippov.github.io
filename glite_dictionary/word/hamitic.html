Hamitic

1. NOUN

/ həˈmɪt̬ɪk / (AmE), / həˈmɪtɪk / (BrE)
a historical term for a group of languages once thought to form a branch of the Afro-Asiatic family, spoken in northern Africa and related to Semitic languages; now considered obsolete in modern linguistics
obsolete term for a North African language group
archaic

Examples

Many 19th-century linguists classified Berber and Cushitic languages as Hamitic.
The term Hamitic is rarely used by contemporary linguists.
You may encounter 'Hamitic' languages in older academic texts about Africa.

Etymology

From Ham, a biblical figure, with '-itic' suffix, used in 19th-20th century linguistic classification.
