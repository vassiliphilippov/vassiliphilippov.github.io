Parietaria

1. NOUN

/ pəˌraɪɪˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / pəˌrʌɪɪˈtɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of non-stinging herbs in the nettle family, often found growing on walls or in waste places
genus of non-stinging nettle herbs

Examples

The old stone wall was covered with Parietaria thriving in the cracks between the bricks.
Some people are allergic to the pollen produced by Parietaria plants.
During our botany walk, we identified several species within the genus Parietaria.

Etymology

From Latin 'parietarius', meaning 'of walls', because the plants often grow on walls.
