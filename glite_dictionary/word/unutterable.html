unutterable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʌt̬.ɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʌt.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a word, sound, or sequence) very difficult or impossible to pronounce correctly.
very hard to pronounce

Examples

The sequence of consonants made the word unutterable for most learners.
Some ancient names are nearly unutterable in modern English.
He paused, stumbling over the unutterable complexity of the phrase.

Synset Examples

an unpronounceable foreign word
unutterable consonant clusters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpronounceable - impossible or very difficult to pronounce

Etymology

From un- (not) + utter (to speak) + -able (capable of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503910-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʌt̬ɚəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʌtərəbəl / (BrE)
too sacred or revered to be spoken or mentioned aloud
too sacred to speak

Examples

To utter the unutterable name was considered a sacrilege in ancient tradition.
The priest would never speak the unutterable word during the ceremony.
Some believe that the divine is unutterable and should remain unspoken.

Synset Examples

the ineffable name of the Deity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or sacred to be expressed in words

Etymology

From un- (not) + utterable (able to be spoken), based on utter (to speak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02063021-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʌt̬ɚəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʌtərəbəl / (BrE)
Too great, extreme, or overwhelming to be expressed or described in words.
too great to be expressed in words

Examples

The tragedy left him with an unutterable sadness he could never put into words.
She gazed at the unutterable beauty of the mountain sunrise.
He felt an unutterable contempt for those who betrayed his trust.

Synset Examples

indefinable yearnings
indescribable beauty
ineffable ecstasy
unspeakable happiness
unutterable contempt
a thing of untellable splendor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to adequately describe
  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or extreme to be expressed in words
  • Antonym: speakable - able to be spoken or expressed

Etymology

From un- (not) + utterable (able to be expressed or spoken).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00947318-s
Synset POS: s
