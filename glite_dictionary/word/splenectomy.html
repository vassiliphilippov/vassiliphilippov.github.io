splenectomy

1. NOUN

/ spləˈnɛktəmi / (AmE), / splɪˈnɛktəmi / (BrE)
the surgical removal of the spleen
removal of the spleen
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a splenectomy after his spleen was ruptured in a car accident.
Doctors recommended a splenectomy to treat the underlying blood disorder.
Recovery from a splenectomy typically involves precautions against certain infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appendectomy - surgical removal of the appendix

Etymology

From Greek splēn (spleen) + -ectomy from ektemnein (to cut out).
