MFLOP

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.flɑp / (AmE), / ˈɛm.flɒp / (BrE)
A unit of computing speed equal to one million floating point operations per second.
unit of computing speed (million floating point operations per second)

Examples

The latest supercomputer is capable of performing over 500 MFLOPs.
Early personal computers could only reach a few MFLOP in speed.
The processor's benchmark was measured in MFLOP to compare its performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megaflop - a unit of computing speed equal to one million floating point operations per second

Etymology

Shortening of 'megaflop', from 'mega' (million) and 'flop' (floating point operation per second).
