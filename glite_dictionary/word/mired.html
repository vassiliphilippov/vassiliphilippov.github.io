mired

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəd / (BrE)
stuck or entangled in a difficult situation, as if trapped in mud and unable to move easily
trapped or hindered in difficulties

Examples

The company became mired in legal disputes that delayed the project for years.
Her campaign was mired by controversy and constant criticism from the media.
Negotiations have been mired in bureaucracy, making progress slow and frustrating.

Synset Examples

the difficulties in which the question is involved
brilliant leadership mired in details and confusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entangled - caught or twisted together; difficult to escape from
  • Synonym: embroiled - involved deeply in an argument or difficult situation
  • Antonym: free - not hampered or hindered; at liberty

Etymology

From the noun 'mire', meaning deep mud; figurative use dates from the 19th century.
