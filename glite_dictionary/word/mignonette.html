mignonette

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnjəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmɪnjəˈnɛt / (BrE)
a Mediterranean annual plant widely cultivated for its dense, spikelike clusters of greenish or yellowish-white flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance.
a fragrant Mediterranean annual plant

Examples

The garden was filled with the scent of mignonette blooming beside the roses.
She chose mignonette for her bouquet because of its delicate, fragrant flowers.
Botanists value mignonette for both its ornamental qualities and its use in perfumes.

Etymology

From French 'mignonette', diminutive of 'mignon' (dainty, darling).
