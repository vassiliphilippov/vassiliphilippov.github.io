bushwhacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃˌwækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwækɪŋ / (BrE)
lying in wait to ambush or attack suddenly, especially as part of guerrilla tactics.
ambushing or attacking from concealment

Examples

The troops suffered heavy casualties from bushwhacking enemies hiding in the dense forest.
During the war, bands of rebels made a practice of bushwhacking passing soldiers.
The general warned his men to be alert for bushwhacking along remote mountain trails.

Synset Examples

bushwhacking guerrillas attacking from ambush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - a surprise attack from a hidden position

Etymology

From 'bushwhack,' originally meaning to make one's way through wild brush, later used for ambush attacks in American English.
