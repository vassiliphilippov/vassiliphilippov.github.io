Sambucus canadensis

1. NOUN

/ sæmˈbjuːkəs kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / samˈbjuːkəs kanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of elderberry native to central and eastern North America, known for its purple-black berries used in making wines and jellies.
North American elderberry species

Examples

The Sambucus canadensis in our backyard produces clusters of edible berries each summer.
Many traditional recipes for elderberry wine call specifically for Sambucus canadensis fruit.
Botanists studied the habitat range of Sambucus canadensis throughout eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the berry of the elder tree, often used in food and drink

Etymology

From Latin: Sambucus meaning 'elder tree' and canadensis meaning 'of Canada'.
