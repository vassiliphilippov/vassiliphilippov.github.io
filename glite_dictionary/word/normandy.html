Normandy

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɔrməndi / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməndi / (BrE)
a historic region in northwestern France, formerly a province, located on the English Channel and now divided into Normandy administrative regions
historic region in northwestern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Normandy to see the D-Day landing beaches and historic towns.
The cuisine in Normandy is renowned for its cheeses and apple cider.
During World War II, Normandy was the site of a major Allied invasion.

Etymology

From Old French Normandie, named for the Norse ('Northmen') settlers in the 10th century.
