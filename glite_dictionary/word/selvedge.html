selvedge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
the edge of a fabric that is woven to prevent it from unraveling or fraying
woven edge of a fabric

Examples

The selvedge of the fabric prevents it from fraying during washing.
Always check if the selvedge is straight before cutting your fabric.
Denim jeans made with a finished selvedge are highly prized for their durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hem - the edge of a piece of cloth that is turned under and sewn

Etymology

From Middle English, alteration of 'self-edge', meaning 'the edge (of cloth) finished by the weaver'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlvɪdʒ / (BrE)
A border or edge of a woven fabric, especially the finished edge or ornamental fringe at either end of an oriental carpet, designed to prevent unraveling.
ornamental edge of woven fabric or carpet

Examples

The selvedge on this carpet features elaborate geometric patterns.
Collectors value carpets with untouched selvedge as it preserves the item’s authenticity.
Repairing the worn selvedge restored the beauty of the antique rug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe - a decorative border of hanging threads or strips on fabric

Etymology

From late Middle English, variant of 'self-edge', referring to the 'self-finished' edge of fabric.
