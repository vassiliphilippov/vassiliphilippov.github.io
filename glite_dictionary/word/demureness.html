demureness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmjʊrnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being demure, especially when displayed in a way that is provocatively modest or coy.
provocative or coy modesty

Examples

She batted her eyelashes with calculated demureness, drawing everyone's attention.
The actress's demureness on stage both charmed and intrigued the audience.
There was a certain demureness in her smile that seemed at odds with her flirtatious words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demure - reserved, modest, and shy

Etymology

From 'demure' + '-ness'; 'demure' from Middle French 'demouré' (sober, reserved).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈmjʊrnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊə.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being reserved, modest, and showing proper decorum.
modest or reserved behavior

Examples

Her demureness at the meeting impressed her colleagues.
The actress played the role with a perfect balance of demureness and confidence.
His demureness masked a surprisingly sharp sense of humor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; not showing excessive pride
  • Antonym: boldness - the quality of being confident and daring

Etymology

From 'demure' + '-ness'; 'demure' from Middle English, possibly from Old French 'demourer' (to linger).
