lumberjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbəˌdʒak / (BrE)
A short, warm outer jacket, typically with a checkered pattern and associated with outdoor work, especially logging.
a short, warm outer jacket

Examples

He put on his lumberjack before heading out into the chilly morning.
The classic red and black lumberjack is coming back into fashion this winter.
She bought a vintage lumberjack at the thrift store for hiking trips.

Etymology

Originally from Canadian English, referring to loggers' jackets.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbəˌdʒæk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut down trees and prepare timber
a person who cuts down trees

Examples

The lumberjack swung his axe and brought down the massive pine tree.
Tourists watched a lumberjack competition, impressed by the speed and skill on display.
Her grandfather worked as a lumberjack in the forests of Canada for over thirty years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logger - a person who cuts down trees for timber

Etymology

From 'lumber' (timber) + 'jack' (generic male term); North American origin, 19th century.
