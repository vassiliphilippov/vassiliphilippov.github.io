embossed

1. ADJ

/ ɛmˈbɔst / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒst / (BrE)
(of an object or surface) decorated with a raised design, pattern, or figure made by pressing or molding.
decorated with a raised pattern

Examples

The invitation was printed on embossed paper, giving it a luxurious feel.
She admired the embossed leather cover of the antique book.
The embossed pattern on the vase shimmered in the sunlight.

Synset Examples

brocaded silk
an embossed satin
embossed leather
raised needlework
raised metalwork

Related Terms

  • Antonym: engraved - decorated with a design cut into a surface

Etymology

From Middle French *embosser* (to make into a boss or bulge), from Old French *bosse* (a bump, swelling).
