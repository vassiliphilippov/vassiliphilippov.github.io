Cremona

1. PROPN

/ krɪˈmoʊnə / (AmE), / krɛˈməʊnə / (BrE)
a city in northern Italy, in Lombardy on the Po River, famed for its tradition of violin making from the 16th to the 18th centuries.
city in northern Italy, famous for violins

Examples

The master luthiers of Cremona produced some of the world’s most sought-after violins.
She traveled to Cremona to visit the famous violin workshops.
Stradivari lived and worked in Cremona for most of his life.

Etymology

From Italian Cremona, the name of the city.
