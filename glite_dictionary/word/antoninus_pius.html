Antoninus Pius

1. PROPN

/ ˌæn.təˈnaɪ.nəs ˈpaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˌæn.təˈnaɪ.nəs ˈpaɪ.əs / (BrE)
(86–161 AD) Roman emperor from 138 to 161, known for a peaceful and prosperous reign; adopted son and successor of Hadrian.
Roman emperor (138–161 AD)

Examples

Antoninus Pius presided over the Roman Empire during a period of relative peace and stability.
Historians often praise Antoninus Pius for his fair administration and lack of military conflict.
A statue of Antoninus Pius stands in the Vatican Museums, celebrating his contributions to Roman history.

Etymology

Latin Antonīnus Pius, from the family name Antoninus and 'Pius' meaning 'dutiful, pious.'
