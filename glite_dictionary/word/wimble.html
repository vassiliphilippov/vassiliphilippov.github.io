wimble

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪmbəl / (BrE)
a hand tool used for boring holes, typically in wood or stone
hand tool for boring holes

Examples

The carpenter selected a wimble to make precise holes in the wooden beam.
Antique collectors often prize a well-preserved wimble for its craftsmanship.
Before the invention of the electric drill, craftsmen relied on a wimble for boring holes.

Etymology

From Old English *wimol*, possibly influenced by Latin *vimula* (a small withe or twig used as a drill).
