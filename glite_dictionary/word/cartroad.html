cartroad

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌrəʊd / (BrE)
a rough road or track suitable for passage by a cart, especially in rural or undeveloped areas
a rough road suitable for carts

Examples

After the rain, the cartroad was muddy and difficult to pass with a heavy load.
Farmers often used the old cartroad to transport crops to the village market.
The narrow cartroad wound through the fields and disappeared into the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a rough path or road, especially one created by the passage of vehicles or animals
  • Synonym: lane - a narrow road, especially in rural areas

Etymology

Formed from 'cart' and 'road', referring to a track for carts.
