Eros

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːroʊs / (AmE), / ˈɪərɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of love, son of Aphrodite, often identified with the Roman Cupid.
Greek god of love; son of Aphrodite

Examples

In ancient Greek art, Eros is often depicted as a winged youth carrying a bow and arrows.
The myth of Eros and Psyche is a timeless tale of love and hardship.
She studied the symbolism of Eros in classical literature for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cupid - Roman god of love, counterpart of Eros

Etymology

From Greek Ἔρως (Eros), meaning 'love' or 'desire'.
