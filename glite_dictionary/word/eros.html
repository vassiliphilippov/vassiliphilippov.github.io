eros

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr oʊs / (AmE), / ˈɪər ɒs / (BrE)
a desire for sexual intimacy; passionate love or the desire for physical and romantic union
sexual desire; passionate love

Examples

The novel explores the tension between eros and friendship.
He wrote poetry celebrating the power of eros in human life.
Some philosophers distinguish between eros, agape, and philia as types of love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - strong sexual desire
  • Degree Lower: romance - a feeling of excitement associated with love

Etymology

From Greek *Eros*, the god of love, from *erōs* (love).
