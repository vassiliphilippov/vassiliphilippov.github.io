tract

1. NOUN

/ trækt / (AmE), / trakt / (BrE)
a short written work on a particular subject, often published as a pamphlet or booklet for distribution
short treatise or booklet

Examples

He handed out a tract explaining the health risks of smoking.
The protest group published a tract outlining their demands.
Religious organizations sometimes distribute a tract to introduce their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pamphlet - a small booklet or brochure containing information or arguments about a single subject

Etymology

From Latin tractatus (discussion, treatise).

2. NOUN

/ trækt / (AmE), / trakt / (BrE)
a system of connected body parts that work together for a specific function, such as digestion or breathing
system of connected body parts with a function

Examples

The digestive tract includes the stomach, intestines, and other organs.
The infection spread throughout the respiratory tract.
Doctors observed inflammation in her urinary tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: system - set of connected things working together as a whole

Etymology

From Latin tractus (a drawing out, extension), from trahere (to draw).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ trækt / (AmE), / trækt / (BrE)
a large area of land, especially one that is not divided into smaller parcels
large area of land

Examples

The developer purchased a large tract of land outside the city limits.
Vast tracts of forest were cleared for agriculture.
They hiked across a remote tract of wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parcel - a piece or section of land
  • Degree Lower: plot - a small area of land
  • Degree Higher: expanse - a wide, continuous area

Etymology

From Latin tractus (a drawing out, extension, region).

4. NOUN

/ trækt / (AmE), / trakt / (BrE)
a bundle of nerve fibers in the brain or spinal cord that follow a specific path and function together
bundle of nerve fibers in the brain
medical

Examples

The optic tract carries visual information from the eyes to the brain.
Damage to the spinal tract can result in loss of sensation.
Researchers studied the role of the corticospinal tract in movement control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting impulses in the body
  • Synonym: pathway - a route of nerve fibers transmitting signals

Etymology

From Latin tractus (a dragging, course, or track).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05483530-n
Synset POS: n
