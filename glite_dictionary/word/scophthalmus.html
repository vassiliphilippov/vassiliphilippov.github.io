Scophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ skɑfˈθælməs / (AmE), / skɒfˈθalməs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Scophthalmidae, which includes several species of turbot.
a genus of flatfish, including turbots

Examples

The genus Scophthalmus includes commercially valuable flatfish like the turbot.
Marine biologists studied several species within the Scophthalmus genus to understand their habitat preferences.
Fossil evidence suggests the ancestors of Scophthalmus date back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbot - a large flatfish, Scophthalmus maximus, commonly found in European waters

Etymology

From Greek 'skophthalmos', meaning 'with a projecting eye'.
