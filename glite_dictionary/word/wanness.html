wanness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.nəs / (BrE)
An unnatural lack of color in the skin, usually caused by illness, emotional distress, or bruising; paleness.
unnatural paleness of skin

Examples

Her wanness was evident after weeks of illness.
The wanness of his face scared his friends when they saw him.
Grief had drained her cheeks of color, leaving only a ghostly wanness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - paleness of the skin, especially due to illness or shock

Etymology

From 'wan' (Old English 'wann', meaning 'pale or lacking color') + '-ness'.
