self-contradictory

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˌtrædəˈktɔri / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kənˌtrædɪkˈtɒri / (BrE)
containing ideas, statements, or aspects that are logically inconsistent with one another; internally conflicting
containing conflicting elements

Examples

His explanation was self-contradictory and did not make logical sense.
The theory is ultimately self-contradictory, undermining its own claims.
To say you hate lying but then lie yourself is a self-contradictory position.

Synset Examples

the figures are at odds with our findings
contradictory attributes of unjust justice and loving vindictiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradictory - mutually opposing; inconsistent

Etymology

From 'self-' (by oneself) and 'contradictory', based on Latin 'contradictorius'.

2. ADJ

/ sɛlfˌkɑntrəˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / sɛlfˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
containing elements that are inconsistent or in conflict with each other, though they may appear true at the same time
containing conflicting elements

Examples

The politician's statement was self-contradictory, claiming both support and opposition to the policy.
Her argument sounded self-contradictory because she gave reasons both for and against the decision.
It is self-contradictory to say you always lie; if that's true, then the statement itself must be a lie.

Synset Examples

it is paradoxical that standing is more tiring than walking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradoxical - seemingly absurd or self-contradictory but possibly true

Etymology

From 'self-' + 'contradictory', based on Latin 'contra' (against) and 'dicere' (to speak).
