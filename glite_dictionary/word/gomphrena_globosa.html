Gomphrena globosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑmˈfriːnə ɡloʊˈboʊsə / (AmE), / ɡɒmˈfriːnə ɡləʊˈbəʊsə / (BrE)
a tropical American plant with round rose, red, or purple flowers that retain their color when dried, commonly known as globe amaranth.
tropical plant with colorful, dryable flowers

Examples

The Gomphrena globosa is prized for its vibrant flowers that do not fade after drying.
Many gardeners grow Gomphrena globosa for use in floral arrangements because of its long-lasting blooms.
You can often find Gomphrena globosa in tropical gardens because it thrives in hot, sunny climates.

Etymology

From Latin; Gomphrena from the Greek name for a kind of amaranth, globosa from Latin 'globosus' (spherical).
