adversity

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvɜr.sə.t̬i / (AmE), / ədˈvɜː.sə.ti / (BrE)
a difficult or unpleasant situation; a condition marked by misfortune or hardship
difficult or unfortunate situation

Examples

She showed remarkable courage in the face of adversity.
Many successful people have overcome great adversity in their lives.
The team bonded together as they faced adversity during the tournament.

Synset Examples

debt-ridden farmers struggling with adversity
a life of hardship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardship - severe suffering or privation
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful or thriving

Etymology

From Old French 'adversite', from Latin 'adversitas' (opposition, hostility).

2. NOUN

/ ædˈvɜːrsəti / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːsɪti / (BrE)
a difficult or unpleasant situation; an instance of ill fortune or misfortune
instance of misfortune or calamity

Examples

She showed remarkable strength in the face of adversity.
After years of adversity, the company finally returned to profitability.
The community pulled together to rebuild after the adversity brought on by the storm.

Synset Examples

a period marked by adversities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being prosperous or successful

Etymology

From Latin 'adversitas' (hostile, unfavorable conditions), from 'adversus' (turned against).
