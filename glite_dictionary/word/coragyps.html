Coragyps

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrædʒɪps / (AmE), / kəˈrædʒɪps / (BrE)
A genus of American vultures in the family Cathartidae, which includes the black vulture.
genus of American vultures

Examples

The Coragyps genus is represented in North America by the black vulture.
Scientists examined the evolutionary history of Coragyps and its relatives in Cathartidae.
The fossil record provides evidence that Coragyps once had a broader range.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cathartidae - family of New World vultures
  • Synonym: black vulture - a large black bird of prey in the genus Coragyps

Etymology

From Greek 'korax' (raven) + 'gyps' (vulture).
