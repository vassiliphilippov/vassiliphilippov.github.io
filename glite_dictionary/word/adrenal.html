adrenal

1. ADJ

/ əˈdriːnəl / (AmE), / əˈdriːnəl / (BrE)
Situated near or relating to the kidneys, especially referring to the adrenal glands that produce hormones.
near the kidneys; relating to adrenal glands
medical

Examples

The adrenal glands produce hormones crucial for the body's response to stress.
Doctors examined his adrenal function to diagnose the hormone imbalance.
She needed adrenal tests after experiencing unusual fatigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suprarenal - situated above or near the kidneys, especially the glands

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (near) + 'renes' (kidneys), meaning 'near the kidneys'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdriːnəl / (AmE), / əˈdriːnəl / (BrE)
either of a pair of endocrine glands located above the kidneys that produce a variety of hormones including adrenaline and cortisol
endocrine gland above the kidney
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the patient's damaged adrenal during the operation.
A tumor was discovered on the left adrenal after several tests and scans.
The adrenal produces hormones essential for managing stress and metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenal gland - the full term for an adrenal, the endocrine gland above the kidney

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (near) + 'renes' (kidneys).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ əˈdriːnəl / (AmE), / əˈdriːnəl / (BrE)
relating to the adrenal glands or their secretions
relating to adrenal glands
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered tests to check adrenal hormone levels.
An adrenal tumor may affect hormone production in the body.
Chronic stress can lead to adrenal fatigue, though the diagnosis is controversial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suprarenal - relating to or situated above the kidneys (another term for adrenal)

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (near) + 'renes' (kidneys), referring to the glands located near the kidneys.
