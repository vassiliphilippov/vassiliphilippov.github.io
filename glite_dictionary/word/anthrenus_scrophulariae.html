Anthrenus scrophulariae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθriːnəs skrəˌfjuləˈrɪiː / (AmE), / ænˈθriːnəs skrɒfjʊˈlɛːrɪiː / (BrE)
A small species of carpet beetle that is black with red and white markings, known for infesting stored products and natural fibers.
a species of small carpet beetle

Examples

The larvae of the Anthrenus scrophulariae can cause significant damage to carpets and furniture.
Museum staff regularly monitor for Anthrenus scrophulariae to protect historical textiles.
Finding an Anthrenus scrophulariae in the house may indicate an infestation of carpet beetles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet beetle - a beetle that damages wool and other natural fibers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Anthrenus' and species epithet 'scrophulariae', referring to its early association with figwort plants (Scrophularia).
