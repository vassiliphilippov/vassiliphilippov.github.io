Australopithecus robustus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstrəloʊpəˈθikəs roʊˈbʌstəs / (AmE), / ˌɒstrələʊpɪˈθiːkəs rɒˈbʌstəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of large-toothed hominid that lived in southern Africa around 1.5 to 2 million years ago, sometimes classified as Paranthropus robustus
extinct southern African hominid species

Examples

Fossils of Australopithecus robustus have been found at several sites in South Africa.
Researchers believe that Australopithecus robustus had powerful jaws adapted for chewing tough plant material.
The discovery of Australopithecus robustus contributed significantly to our understanding of human evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paranthropus robustus - another scientific name for this extinct hominid species

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'australis' (southern) + 'pithekos' (ape) + Latin 'robustus' (strong).
