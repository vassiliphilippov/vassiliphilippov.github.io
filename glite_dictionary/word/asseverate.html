to asseverate

1. VERB

/ əˈsɛvəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɛvəreɪt / (BrE)
to state or declare something firmly and emphatically
state or declare emphatically

Examples

The witness asseverated that he had seen the defendant at the scene of the crime.
She asseverated her innocence with unwavering conviction.
During the meeting, he asseverated his commitment to upholding the company's values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state something forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to declare not to be true

Etymology

From Latin 'asseverare' (to assert seriously), from 'ad' (to) + 'severus' (serious).
