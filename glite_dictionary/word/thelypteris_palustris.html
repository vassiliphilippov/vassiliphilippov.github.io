Thelypteris palustris

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɪptərɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / θəˈlɪptərɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A fern species with deeply divided fronds, typically found in wetlands across the northern temperate regions.
a wetland fern species

Examples

Thelypteris palustris is often found thriving in boggy meadows and marshy woodlands.
Many wildlife reserves protect the fragile habitat of Thelypteris palustris.
If you see a fern with pinnatifid fronds near a pond, it may be Thelypteris palustris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh fern - common name for Thelypteris palustris

Etymology

From Greek *thelys* (female) + *pteris* (fern), and Latin *palustris* (of marshes).
