impendent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of something, especially an event) about to happen or likely to occur very soon; impending.
about to happen; impending
archaic

Examples

With the storm clouds gathering, a downpour seemed impendent.
The nation braced itself for the impendent crisis.
She sensed an impendent change in her future.

Synset Examples

retribution is at hand
some people believe the day of judgment is close at hand
in imminent danger
his impending retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminent - about to happen, impending
  • Synonym: impending - likely to happen soon

Etymology

From Latin impendēns, present participle of impendēre ('to overhang, threaten, be imminent').
