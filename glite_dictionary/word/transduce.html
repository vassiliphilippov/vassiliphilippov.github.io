to transduce

1. VERB

/ trænzˈdus / (AmE), / trænzˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to convert (energy, signals, or information) from one form to another, especially in technical or biological contexts
convert energy or signals to another form
medical

Examples

Cells can transduce signals from their environment into a biochemical response.
The microphone is designed to transduce sound waves into electrical signals.
Many sensors work by transducing mechanical pressure into readable outputs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'transducere' meaning 'to lead across' or 'transfer'.
