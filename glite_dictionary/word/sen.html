sen

1. NOUN

/ sɛn / (AmE), / sen / (BrE)
a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of a yen (Japan), rupiah (Indonesia), or riel (Cambodia)
fractional currency unit in Japan, Indonesia, Cambodia

Examples

The coin was worth five sen before it was discontinued in Japan.
In Indonesia, one hundred sen make up one rupiah.
The collector displayed several rare Cambodian sen from the early twentieth century.

Etymology

From Japanese 'sen', originally from Chinese 'qián' (unit of currency).
