excavation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.skəˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of digging or removing earth, especially as part of an archaeological or construction project
the act of digging

Examples

The excavation at the construction site uncovered several ancient artifacts.
Archaeologists spent weeks carefully conducting the excavation of the burial mound.
Due to the depth of the excavation, safety measures had to be implemented.

Synset Examples

there's an interesting excavation going on near Princeton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digging - the act of making a hole in the ground

Etymology

From Latin excavatio, from excavare (to hollow out), from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of removing earth, minerals, or other materials from the ground, especially for mining or archaeological purposes
removal of earth or minerals from the ground

Examples

The excavation of the coal mine took several years to complete.
Recent excavation at the site has revealed large deposits of iron ore.
Heavy machinery is required for the excavation of valuable minerals from deep underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digging - the act of breaking and moving earth
  • Synonym: mining - the process of extracting minerals from the earth

Etymology

From Latin excavatio, from excavare (to hollow out), from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A hole or cavity in the ground formed by removing earth, especially as part of construction or archaeological activities.
hole or cavity made by digging

Examples

The construction workers dug a deep excavation for the building's foundation.
Archaeologists discovered ancient artifacts in the excavation site.
After the excavation was completed, they reinforced the sides to prevent collapse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit - a large hole in the ground, often for mining or construction
  • Antonym: mound - a raised area of earth, opposite of a hole

Etymology

From Latin excavatio, from excavare (to hollow out), from ex- (out) + cavare (to hollow).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a site where archaeologists or others dig to uncover ancient objects, remains, or historical evidence
an archaeological dig site

Examples

The researchers spent weeks carefully sifting through the excavation for ancient pottery shards.
Security measures were taken to protect the excavation from looters overnight.
Tours of the excavation are available for visitors interested in seeing archeological work firsthand.

Synset Examples

they set up camp next to the dig

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - an informal term for an archaeological site where excavation occurs

Etymology

From Latin excavatio (hollowing out), from excavare (to hollow out, dig out).
