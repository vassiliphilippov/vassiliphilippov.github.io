hydrate

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance or organism) to become combined with water; to absorb or take up water.
absorb water; become combined with water

Examples

It is important to hydrate after exercise to help your body recover.
The powder will hydrate quickly when mixed with water.
Some materials naturally hydrate when exposed to moist air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dehydrate - to remove water from; to lose water

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' (water); first used in scientific contexts in the early 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00214694-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a compound, typically a crystalline substance, that contains water molecules chemically bound within its structure
compound containing bound water

Examples

Many minerals, such as gypsum, are classified as hydrates because they contain water in their crystal structure.
The chemist explained that a hydrate is formed when water molecules bond with another compound.
Copper(II) sulfate commonly appears as a blue hydrate due to its water of crystallization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anhydride - a compound formed by removing water from a hydrate

Etymology

From Greek hydōr (water).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14934591-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to absorb water or moisture; to add water to
add water or moisture to

Examples

It's important to hydrate your body after exercise.
Many creams are designed to hydrate the skin and prevent dryness.
Make sure to hydrate plants regularly, especially during hot weather.

Synset Examples

hydrate your skin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dehydrate - to remove water from; to dry out

Etymology

From French 'hydrater', from Greek 'hydor' meaning 'water'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00214446-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdreɪt / (BrE)
to supply water or liquid to someone or something in order to keep it healthy and properly balanced
to supply water or liquid to maintain health

Examples

It's important to hydrate regularly during intense exercise to avoid cramps.
The nurse will hydrate the patient through an intravenous drip.
You should hydrate before, during, and after workouts for optimal performance.

Synset Examples

the bicyclists must be hydrated frequently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dehydrate - remove water from; lose or cause to lose water

Etymology

From Greek 'hydōr' (water), via French 'hydrater'.
