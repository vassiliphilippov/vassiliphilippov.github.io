columnar

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlʌmnɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmnə / (BrE)
having the shape or form of a column; tall and cylindrical.
having the shape of a column

Examples

The building was supported by columnar pillars made of marble.
Some trees, such as the Lombardy poplar, have a distinctly columnar growth habit.
The columnar structure of the rock formations was striking against the landscape.

Synset Examples

trees with columniform trunks
columnar forms
a columnlike tree trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cylindrical - having the shape of a cylinder

Etymology

From Latin columna (column) with the suffix -ar forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlʌmnɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmnə / (BrE)
having the shape or characteristics of a column; arranged in or resembling columns.
having the shape or qualities of a column

Examples

The temple's columnar architecture impressed every visitor who entered its grand hall.
The columnar basalt formations at the coast are a result of ancient volcanic activity.
Glandular tissue often has a columnar structure under the microscope.

Synset Examples

columnar construction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columned - having columns, typically as part of architectural design

Etymology

From Latin 'columnaris', from 'columna' (column).

Source: WordNet
