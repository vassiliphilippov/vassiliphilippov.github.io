to insolate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.səˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sə.leɪt / (BrE)
to expose something to the rays of the sun; to affect by exposure to sunlight
expose to sunlight

Examples

The laboratory technician will insolate the cultures for several hours to study their reaction to sunlight.
Old manuscripts were ruined because they were insolated on a windowsill for years.
Doctors sometimes insolate certain medical instruments to reduce bacteria through sunlight exposure.

Synset Examples

insolated paper may turn yellow and crumble
These herbs suffer when sunned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sun - to expose to sunlight; to heat or affect by sunlight

Etymology

From Latin insolare, from in- (on) + sol (sun).
