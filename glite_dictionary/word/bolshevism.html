Bolshevism

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
The communist ideology and practices associated with the Bolsheviks, especially as developed in the Soviet Union.
Soviet communist ideology

Examples

The spread of Bolshevism throughout Russia led to profound changes in society.
Some historians debate the long-term effects of Bolshevism on world history.
Many Western governments were alarmed by the rise of Bolshevism after the Russian Revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communism - a political theory advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership of capital

Etymology

From Russian 'bol'shevism', from 'bol'shevik' (member of the majority), based on 'bol'shoy' (greater).
