improbability

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌprɑːbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒbəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unlikely to happen or be true.
quality of being improbable

Examples

Given the team's track record, their victory was an utter improbability to many fans.
The improbability of winning the lottery does not stop people from buying tickets every week.
Scientists were surprised by the improbability of the two rare species meeting in the wild.

Synset Examples

impossibility should never be confused with improbability
the improbability of such rare coincidences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improbable - unlikely to happen or be true
  • Antonym: probability - the quality of being probable; likelihood
  • Degree Lower: possibility - the state of being possible; that which may happen

Etymology

Formed from 'improbable' + '-ity', from Late Latin 'improbabilitas'.
