apprehended

1. ADJ

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛndɪd / (BrE)
fully understood or grasped, especially intellectually; comprehended
fully understood; grasped intellectually

Examples

The underlying principles were not immediately apprehended by the students.
Some philosophical truths can only be apprehended through careful study and reflection.
The meaning of the poem was gradually apprehended as we discussed it in class.

Synset Examples

dangers not yet appreciated
these apprehended truths
a thing comprehended is a thing known as fully as it can be known

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehended - fully understood or grasped
  • Antonym: misunderstood - not correctly understood

Etymology

Past participle of 'apprehend', from Latin 'apprehendere' (to seize, grasp).
