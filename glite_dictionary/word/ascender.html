ascender

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndər / (AmE), / əˈsɛndə / (BrE)
a person who climbs or goes up, especially a mountain or difficult terrain
someone who climbs or goes up

Examples

The experienced ascender reached the summit just before noon.
Each ascender carried essential equipment for the icy slopes.
In the group photo, every ascender looked exhausted but proud of their achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climber - a person who climbs, especially as a sport

Etymology

From Latin ascendere (to climb up), via English 'ascend' + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndər / (AmE), / əˈsɛndə / (BrE)
A part of a lowercase letter that extends above the height of letters such as 'x', as in 'b', 'd', or 'h'.
part of lowercase letter extending above x-height

Examples

The letter 'h' has an ascender that rises above most other lowercase letters.
Designers often pay attention to the length of the ascender when choosing a font.
In some typefaces, the ascender and descender heights are carefully balanced for readability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descender - the part of a letter that extends below the baseline

Etymology

From 'ascend' + '-er', meaning something that ascends or rises.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06843611-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndər / (AmE), / əˈsɛndə / (BrE)
(in printing or typography) The part of a lowercase letter that extends above the main body of other lowercase letters, as in 'b', 'd', 'h', or 'k'.
part of a letter extending above main body

Examples

The ascender on the letter 'h' gives it a taller appearance than most vowels.
When designing a new font, Heather paid special attention to the height of each ascender.
In some typefaces, the ascender almost reaches the height of capital letters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descender - the part of a letter that extends below the baseline

Etymology

From the verb 'ascend' + '-er', indicating one that ascends or rises above.
