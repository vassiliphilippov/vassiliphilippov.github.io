wether

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
A male sheep, especially one that has been castrated and is kept for wool or meat.
castrated male sheep

Examples

The farmer separated the wether from the rest of the flock.
Sheep farmers often keep a few wethers to lead their flocks.
The wether produced high-quality wool used for making textiles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ram - an uncastrated male sheep
  • Antonym: ewe - a female sheep

Etymology

Old English *wether*, related to Old High German *widar* (ram) and Old Norse *veðr* (ram).
