primordial

1. ADJ

/ praɪˈmɔrdiəl / (AmE), / praɪˈmɔːdiəl / (BrE)
existing from the very beginning; in an earliest or original stage or state.
existing from the beginning; original

Examples

Some scientists study the primordial soup from which life first emerged on Earth.
The primordial universe was a hot, dense place before stars or planets formed.
She felt a primordial fear as the storm raged outside, connecting her to ancient ancestors.

Synset Examples

aboriginal forests
primal eras before the appearance of life on earth
the forest primeval
primordial matter
primordial forms of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient - belonging to a very distant past; very old
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Late Latin primordial is (original, first), from Latin primordium (beginning).
