vote

1. NOUN

/ voʊt / (AmE), / vəʊt / (BrE)
The total number of people who cast ballots in an election or poll.
total number of voters

Examples

The vote exceeded expectations, signaling high public engagement.
A record vote was reported at yesterday's election.
They are hoping for an even larger vote in the final round.

Synset Examples

they are expecting a large vote

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnout - the number of people who vote in an election

Etymology

From Latin 'votum' (a vow, wish, promise), via Old French and Latin.
