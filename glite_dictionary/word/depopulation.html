depopulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the reduction in the number of people living in a particular area, often resulting in an area being sparsely inhabited or abandoned
reduction of population in an area

Examples

The small village faced severe depopulation after the factory closed.
War and disease led to the depopulation of the island over several decades.
Government policies were introduced to counter the depopulation of rural regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repopulation - an increase in the number of people living in an area again

Etymology

From Latin de- (removal) + populatio (a people, population), via French dépopulation.
