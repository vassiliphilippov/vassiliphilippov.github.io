distillate

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪl.eɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl.eɪt / (BrE)
a purified liquid collected by condensing vapor during distillation; the product obtained from distilling a substance
liquid obtained by distillation

Examples

The distillate collected in the flask was clear and colorless.
Gasoline is a distillate of crude oil obtained during refining.
After several hours, the distillate contained most of the essential oils from the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distillation - the process of purifying a liquid by heating and cooling

Etymology

From Latin 'destillatus', past participle of 'destillare' (to drop, trickle down).
