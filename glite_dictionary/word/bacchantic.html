bacchantic

1. ADJ

/ bækˈkæntɪk / (AmE), / bækˈkantɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a bacchanalian reveler; wildly drunken or riotously festive.
related to drunken revelry

Examples

The party quickly took on a bacchantic atmosphere as guests poured more wine and danced wildly.
His bacchantic behavior embarrassed his friends at the formal dinner.
The annual festival is known for its bacchantic celebrations, filled with music and revelry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchanalian - characteristic of drunken revelry or riotous celebration

Etymology

From Bacchus, the Roman god of wine, plus -ic (forming adjectives).
