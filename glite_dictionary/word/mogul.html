mogul

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊɡəl / (BrE)
a very wealthy or powerful person in business or industry
powerful businessperson

Examples

The tech mogul announced the launch of a new startup in Silicon Valley.
She was considered a media mogul after acquiring several major news outlets.
Many people dream of becoming a real estate mogul, but few achieve that level of success.

Synset Examples

an oil baron

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tycoon - a wealthy, powerful person in business
  • Synonym: magnate - a wealthy and influential businessperson

Etymology

From Persian *mughal*, literally 'Mongol', referring originally to the Mughal emperors, whose wealth and power became proverbial.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊɡəl / (BrE)
a small mound or bump of hard snow on a ski slope, formed by repeated turns of skiers
a bump on a ski slope

Examples

The steep run was covered in moguls, making it challenging for beginners.
She lost her balance after hitting a large mogul halfway down the slope.
Advanced skiers enjoy navigating the field of moguls at the mountain resort.

Etymology

From German 'Mugel' meaning small hill, knoll.
