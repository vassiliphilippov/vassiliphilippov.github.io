viscometer

1. NOUN

/ vɪsˈkɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / vɪsˈkɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the viscosity of a liquid
instrument for measuring viscosity

Examples

The scientist used a viscometer to analyze the oil's thickness before testing.
A reliable viscometer is essential in the quality control of paint manufacturing.
After calibration, the viscometer produced accurate viscosity readings for various chemical solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscosimeter - alternative term for viscometer

Etymology

From Latin 'viscum' (birdlime, viscous substance) + '-meter' (measuring device).
