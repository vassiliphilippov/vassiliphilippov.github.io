myocardiopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˌkɑr.diˈɑp.ə.θi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˌkɑː.dɪˈɒp.ə.θi / (BrE)
a disease or disorder affecting the heart muscle (myocardium), often of unknown cause
disease of the heart muscle
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myocardiopathy after experiencing symptoms of heart failure.
Early detection of myocardiopathy can improve treatment outcomes for those at risk.
His family history made doctors consider myocardiopathy as a possible cause of his symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiomyopathy - disease of the heart muscle

Etymology

From Greek 'myo-' (muscle) + 'kardia' (heart) + '-pathy' (disease).
