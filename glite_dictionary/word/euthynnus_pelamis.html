Euthynnus pelamis

1. NOUN

/ juːˈθɪnəs pəˈleɪmɪs / (AmE), / juːˈθɪnəs pəˈleɪmɪs / (BrE)
A species of oceanic tuna, also known as the skipjack tuna, highly valued in the Pacific region; reaches up to 75 pounds in weight.
species of oceanic tuna (skipjack)

Examples

The Euthynnus pelamis is a critical species for the global tuna industry, especially in the Pacific Ocean.
Fishermen caught several large Euthynnus pelamis during the annual tuna season.
The diet of Euthynnus pelamis mainly consists of smaller fish, crustaceans, and cephalopods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipjack tuna - common name for Euthynnus pelamis
  • Synonym: Katsuwonus pelamis - alternative scientific name for the skipjack tuna
  • Degree Lower: bonito - a similar species of fish in the same family

Etymology

From Greek 'euthys' (straight) and 'enos' (muscle), and Latin 'pelamis' (young tuna), used in scientific classification.
