moppet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.pɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒp.ɪt / (BrE)
A small child, especially a little girl, often one who is endearing or fondly regarded.
endearing small child, esp. girl

Examples

The little moppet ran up to her mother with outstretched arms.
She gave the moppet a bright red ribbon for her hair.
Everyone at the party adored the cheerful moppet in her pink dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tot - a very young child
  • Synonym: urchin - a mischievous young child, especially one who is poorly or raggedly dressed

Etymology

Probably diminutive form of 'mop', used affectionately for a small child in the 17th century.
