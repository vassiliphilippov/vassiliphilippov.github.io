hastiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪstinəs / (AmE), / ˈheɪstinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being too quick or eager, often resulting in carelessness
excessive quickness or eagerness

Examples

Her hastiness often led to mistakes on important projects.
The hastiness of his decision surprised everyone in the meeting.
You should avoid hastiness when making life-changing choices.

Synset Examples

he soon regretted his haste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rashness - quality of acting without careful thought
  • Antonym: deliberation - careful consideration before action

Etymology

From Middle English 'hasti' + '-ness', from Old French 'hastif'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪstinəs / (AmE), / ˈheɪstinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of acting or making decisions quickly without careful thought; impulsiveness or rashness
quality of being hasty or rash

Examples

His hastiness led him to make mistakes on the project.
She regretted her hastiness after realizing she had misjudged the situation.
The decision was made with too much hastiness and not enough planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act on sudden urges or emotions
  • Antonym: deliberation - careful thought or consideration before making a decision

Etymology

Formed from 'hasty' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
