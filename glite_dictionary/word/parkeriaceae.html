Parkeriaceae

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˌkɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɑːˌkɪəriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns coextensive with the genus Ceratopteris, sometimes included in the family Polypodiaceae.
a family of ferns (Ceratopteris)

Examples

The Parkeriaceae are primarily tropical ferns found in wetlands and marshes.
Some botanists classify Ceratopteris within the Parkeriaceae rather than the Polypodiaceae.
Recent genetic studies have led to new insights about the relationships within the Parkeriaceae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - a large family of ferns, sometimes including Parkeriaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Parkeria with the family suffix -aceae, standard in botanical nomenclature.

family of aquatic ferns (botany)
medical

Examples

The aquatic ferns of the Parkeriaceae are commonly studied for their unique reproductive adaptations.
Researchers debated whether to include Parkeriaceae within the larger Polypodiaceae family.
Botanists collected specimens from the Parkeriaceae in tropical wetlands.

Etymology

From the genus name Ceratopteris and the suffix '-aceae' used for plant family names.
