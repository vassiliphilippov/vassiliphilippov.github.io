Yoruba

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɔr ə bə / (AmE), / jəˈruːbə / (BrE)
A member of a West African ethnic group living mainly in southwestern Nigeria and neighboring regions.
member of a West African ethnic group

Examples

Many Yoruba have migrated to cities in Nigeria and abroad.
The Yoruba are known for their rich traditions in art and music.
He is proud to be a descendant of the Yoruba people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yoruba - the language spoken by the Yoruba people

Etymology

From Yoruba *Yorùbá*, self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɔːrəˌbə, joʊˈruːbə / (AmE), / ˈjɒrʊbə, jəʊˈruːbə / (BrE)
A Niger-Congo language of the Kwa branch spoken by the Yoruba people chiefly in southwestern Nigeria and neighboring regions.
West African language

Examples

She is fluent in Yoruba and uses it at home with her family.
The university offers courses in Yoruba for students interested in African languages.
We listened to traditional stories told in Yoruba during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hausa - A major Chadic language spoken in West Africa

Etymology

From Yoruba 'Yorùbá', the name of the Yoruba people and their language.
