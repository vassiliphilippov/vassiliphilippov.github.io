electrolytic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
a type of capacitor in which two electrodes are separated by an electrolyte, commonly used in electronic circuits for storing electric charge
a capacitor with electrodes and electrolyte

Examples

The amplifier circuit requires an electrolytic for proper filtering.
I replaced the old electrolytic to fix the power supply issue.
Most audio equipment uses at least one electrolytic on the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - a device for storing electrical energy

Etymology

From 'electrolyte', ultimately from Greek 'ēlektron' (amber) and 'lysis' (loosening).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛk.trəˈlɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəˈlɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by electrolysis; involving the movement of ions through an electrolyte under an electric current
relating to electrolysis

Examples

The batteries use an electrolytic solution to conduct electricity.
During the electrolytic process, impurities are removed from the metal.
He studied the electrolytic properties of various chemical compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrolysis - chemical decomposition produced by passing an electric current through a liquid or solution

Etymology

From electrolysis + -ic, indicating 'of or relating to.'

3. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving an electrolyte or electrolysis, usually referring to chemical processes involving the movement of ions in a solution or molten substance.
relating to electrolytes or electrolysis

Examples

The process uses an electrolytic cell to separate the components of the solution.
Corrosion can be accelerated by electrolytic action in wet environments.
She studied the electrolytic properties of saltwater in her chemistry experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrolyte - a substance that produces ions and conducts electricity in solution
  • Synonym: electrolysis - chemical decomposition produced by passing an electric current through a liquid or solution

Etymology

From electrolyte + -ic; 'electrolyte' from Greek 'ēlektron' (amber) and 'lyein' (to loosen).
