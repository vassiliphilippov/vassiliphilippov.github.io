obliterable

1. ADJ

/ əˈblɪtərəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈblɪtərəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be completely destroyed or wiped out so that no trace remains
able to be completely wiped out

Examples

The ancient inscription was barely visible, yet still obliterable by a single careless brushstroke.
These records are not obliterable; they can be preserved for centuries if properly stored.
Some digital data may seem permanent, but much of it is actually obliterable with the right tools.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy or obliterate
  • Synonym: perishable - able to decay or be destroyed

Etymology

From 'obliterate' + -able, after Latin 'obliterare' (to remove all traces).
