venturi

1. NOUN

/ vɛnˈtʊri / (AmE), / vɛnˈtjʊəri / (BrE)
A tube with a narrow constriction that causes a change in fluid pressure and speed, used to control fluid flow, especially in devices like carburetors.
tube with constriction for fluid control

Examples

The engineer installed a venturi in the pipeline to measure the flow rate.
The carburetor uses a venturi to mix air and fuel efficiently.
By narrowing the pipe with a venturi, the pressure drop causes an increase in velocity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carburetor - a device that mixes air with fuel for internal combustion engines

Etymology

Named after Italian physicist Giovanni Battista Venturi (1746–1822), who studied fluid dynamics.
