bogginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑɡinəs / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡinɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being wet and muddy, typically making an area difficult to walk on.
state of being wet and muddy

Examples

The bogginess of the meadow made it impossible to cross without waterproof boots.
After days of heavy rain, the bogginess of the field increased significantly.
Scientists measured the bogginess of the soil to determine its suitability for construction.

Synset Examples

The bogginess of the ground has led to the addition of two drainage systems, plus several lorryloads of sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boggy - wet and muddy; like a bog

Etymology

From 'boggy' + '-ness', where 'boggy' comes from 'bog' (wet, spongy ground).
