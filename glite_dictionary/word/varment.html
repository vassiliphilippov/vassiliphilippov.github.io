varment

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑɹmənt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːmənt / (BrE)
An undesirable or troublesome wild animal, especially one that is predatory.
troublesome wild animal
archaic

Examples

Old ranchers used to say a coyote was just another varment to watch out for.
He set traps around the chicken coop to catch any varment sneaking in at night.
That old dog won't let a single varment onto the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varmint - undesirable or troublesome wild animal (main variant spelling)
  • Synonym: vermin - undesirable small animal or pest

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'varmint', from earlier 'vermin' (pest), itself from Latin 'vermis' (worm).
