squatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtə / (BrE)
a person who settles on land or occupies property without legal right or title
person occupying property illegally

Examples

The abandoned building was taken over by a group of squatters seeking shelter.
Authorities removed the squatters from the vacant lot after several complaints.
She lived as a squatter in the city for two years before finding a legal home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespasser - someone who enters property without permission
  • Antonym: tenant - a person who pays rent to use land or property

Etymology

From squat (verb) + -er, originally American English in the early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtə / (BrE)
a person who settles lawfully on government land with the intention of acquiring legal ownership of it, often by meeting certain requirements
person settling lawfully on government land

Examples

The government encouraged squatters to move west in the 19th century by offering land titles after a period of residence.
Several squatters established farms on the frontier, hoping to eventually gain legal ownership of the land.
In some countries, laws allowed squatters to claim land if they improved and maintained it for a number of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homesteader - someone who acquires ownership of land by living on and cultivating it

Etymology

From the verb 'squat' (to settle on land), possibly from Low German 'squat' (to crouch).
