poster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to put up posters, placards, or billboard advertisements on walls or other surfaces
person putting up posters or bills

Examples

The city hired an official poster to put up public health notices across town.
As a professional poster, he carried brushes and buckets of glue wherever he went.
Bill worked for years as a poster, pasting movie ads on empty buildings.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə / (BrE)
a large printed notice or picture, often used for advertising or decoration and typically displayed in a public place
printed sign used for advertising or decoration

Examples

A brightly colored poster announced the opening of the new restaurant.
She put up a poster about the lost dog on several street corners.
The theater displayed a giant poster of the movie above its entrance.

Synset Examples

a poster advertised the coming attractions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billboard - a large outdoor board for displaying advertisements
  • Degree Lower: flyer - a small printed leaflet used for advertising

Etymology

From post (v.) + -er, referring to something that is posted or displayed.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə / (BrE)
(historical) A horse kept at an inn or post house for use by mail carriers or for hire to travelers.
horse for mail carriers or travelers (historical)
archaic

Examples

The weary mailman requested a fresh poster at the inn before continuing his journey.
In 18th-century England, travelers often changed their poster at each post house along the route.
The old records mention a stable of twenty posters maintained solely for government use.

Etymology

From 'post', referring to a relay system for mail and travel, plus the agentive '-er'.
