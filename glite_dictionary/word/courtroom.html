courtroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtˌruːm / (BrE)
a room where legal cases are heard and decided by a judge and sometimes a jury
room where legal cases are heard

Examples

The jury entered the courtroom in silence, ready to hear the verdict.
After a heated debate, the judge restored order in the courtroom.
Many reporters filled the courtroom to cover the high-profile trial.

Synset Examples

television cameras were admitted in the courtroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal presided over by a judge or judges

Etymology

From 'court' (a place where justice is administered) + 'room'.
