congresswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋ.ɡrəsˌwʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.ɡrəsˌwʊ.mən / (BrE)
A female member of the United States House of Representatives.
female member of U.S. Congress (House)
american

Examples

The congresswoman introduced a bill to improve access to healthcare in rural communities.
At the town hall, the congresswoman addressed concerns about the local economy and job growth.
Following the election, she became the youngest congresswoman in the state's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congressman - A male or generic member of the United States House of Representatives
  • Antonym: senator - A member of the United States Senate

Etymology

Formed from 'congress' + 'woman', modelled after 'congressman'.
