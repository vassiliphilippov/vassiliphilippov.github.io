interlocking

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or state of two or more things connecting or fitting closely together, especially so that movement is only possible in unison.
state of being fitted or meshed together

Examples

The interlocking of the gears ensures that they move without slipping.
Secure interlocking is crucial in constructing sturdy toy blocks.
The puzzle relies on the interlocking of its unique pieces to form the image.

Synset Examples

the engagement of the clutch
the meshing of gears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meshing - the act of gears fitting together
  • Synonym: engagement - the act of gear components fitting together to transmit motion

Etymology

From interlock (verb), from Latin inter- (between) + lock.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of things) linked or fitted closely together, so that they are difficult to separate; joined as by dovetailing.
closely joined or linked together

Examples

The puzzle pieces are designed with interlocking shapes to hold them firmly together.
They built a walkway out of interlocking bricks for added stability.
The interlocking parts of the gear prevented it from being taken apart easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: separate - not joined, being apart

Etymology

From 'interlock' (verb: to lock together), formed by prefix 'inter-' (between, among) and 'lock.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈlɑː.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɒ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of connecting or fitting things closely together so that they cannot move independently
the act of joining things together

Examples

The interlocking of the gears ensures the machine runs smoothly.
A strong interlocking of fingers expressed their unity during the ceremony.
The architect admired the interlocking of steel beams in the building's structure.

Synset Examples

an interlocking of arms by the police held the crowd in check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meshing - the act of fitting or working together closely

Etymology

From 'interlock' + '-ing', formed from Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'lock'.
