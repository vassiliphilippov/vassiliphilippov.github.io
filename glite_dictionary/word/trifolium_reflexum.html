Trifolium reflexum

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm rɪˈflɛksəm / (AmE), / trɪˈfəʊliəm rɪˈflɛksəm / (BrE)
A species of clover native to North America, known as buffalo clover, found especially in the western United States.
a clover species native to North America

Examples

Trifolium reflexum blooms in late spring and attracts many pollinators to the garden.
The conservation team is working to restore habitats for native plants like Trifolium reflexum.
Researchers are studying how fire affects the growth of Trifolium reflexum in prairies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo clover - common name for Trifolium reflexum

Etymology

From Latin; Trifolium meaning 'three-leaved' and reflexum meaning 'bent back'.
