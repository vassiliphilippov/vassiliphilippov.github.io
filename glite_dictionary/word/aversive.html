aversive

1. ADJ

/ əˈvɜrsɪv / (AmE), / əˈvɜːsɪv / (BrE)
causing avoidance or discouragement; tending to repel or dissuade, especially by being unpleasant or undesirable
tending to repel or discourage

Examples

The loud, unexpected noise had an aversive effect on the animals, causing them to flee.
Aversive conditioning uses unpleasant stimuli to prevent unwanted behaviors.
Many people find the taste of grapefruit juice aversive and avoid it.

Synset Examples

aversive conditioning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attractive - having qualities that draw or appeal; pleasing or inviting

Etymology

From Latin 'aversus' (turned away).
