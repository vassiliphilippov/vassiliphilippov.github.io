conciliation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of restoring goodwill or friendly relations after conflict or disagreement
restoring goodwill after conflict

Examples

The leaders hoped that the peace talks would lead to genuine conciliation between the two nations.
After years of disputes, a period of conciliation brought stability to the organization.
Her gesture of apology was intended to initiate conciliation and end the feud.

Synset Examples

there was a brief period of conciliation but the fighting soon resumed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations
  • Antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, often a prolonged one

Etymology

From Latin conciliatio (a bringing together), from conciliare (to unite or win over).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪlɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making peace or lessening hostility or distrust between people or groups.
act of making peace or easing hostility

Examples

After months of tension, both sides agreed to a process of conciliation.
The manager's efforts at conciliation helped prevent a strike at the factory.
Effective conciliation requires listening and understanding the grievances of all parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations
  • Antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From Latin conciliatio (a unite, bringing together), from conciliare (to bring together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a process of bringing disputing parties to an agreement, often through the intervention of a neutral third party, before arbitration or litigation is pursued
dispute resolution by mediation
legal

Examples

The company and the union agreed to try conciliation before considering arbitration.
International organizations often offer conciliation services to help resolve border disputes peacefully.
Many employment disputes are resolved through conciliation instead of going to court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediation - intervention in a dispute to resolve it
  • Degree Higher: arbitration - settlement of a dispute by an impartial referee

Etymology

From Latin conciliatio (a bringing together), from conciliare (to bring together).
