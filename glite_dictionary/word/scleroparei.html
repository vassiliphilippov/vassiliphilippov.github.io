Scleroparei

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˈpɛri / (AmE), / sklɪərəˈpɪəri / (BrE)
an obsolete taxonomic division of bony fishes that included scorpionfishes, sculpins, gurnards, greenlings, and related species
obsolete group of certain bony fishes

Examples

The family of fishes once classified under Scleroparei includes the scorpionfish and the gurnard.
Recent ichthyological studies have abandoned the category of Scleroparei in favor of more precise classifications.
Many reference books now consider Scleroparei to be an outdated taxonomic grouping.

Etymology

From Greek *skleros* (hard) + *para* (beside) + Latinized suffix *-ei*, reflecting hard structures in members.
