tissue

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈtɪs.juː / (BrE)
A group of cells in an organism that have a similar structure and function.
aggregate of similar cells in an organism

Examples

The scientist examined the tissue sample under a microscope for signs of disease.
Scar tissue can develop after an injury heals.
Human muscle tissue is responsible for movement throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: organ - a part of the body composed of different tissues that performs a specific function
  • Degree Lower: cell - the smallest structural unit of an organism

Etymology

From French 'tissu' (woven), from Latin 'textus' (woven), from 'texere' (to weave).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈtɪsju / (BrE)
a soft, thin paper used especially for wiping the nose, cleaning, or wrapping delicate items
soft, thin paper for cleaning or wrapping

Examples

She handed me a tissue when I sneezed during the meeting.
He carefully wrapped the fragile ornament in tissue to protect it from breaking.
Could you pass me a tissue, please?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napkin - a square piece of cloth or paper for wiping the mouth and hands while eating

Etymology

From Old French 'tissu' (woven), from Latin 'texere' (to weave); originally referred to woven fabric before extending to paper.
