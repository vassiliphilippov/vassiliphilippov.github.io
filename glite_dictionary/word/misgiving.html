misgiving

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A feeling of doubt, worry, or uneasiness about whether something is right or will have a good result, especially for moral or ethical reasons.
doubt or unease about correctness or ethics

Examples

She accepted the job offer, despite her misgivings about the company's ethics.
He voiced his misgivings about the plan, fearing it might backfire.
Even with some misgiving, they decided to proceed with the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - A feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: confidence - A feeling of certainty and self-assurance

Etymology

From 'misgive' (Middle English), influenced by 'give' and 'mis-' (wrongly).

2. NOUN

/ mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A feeling of doubt or apprehension about the outcome or consequences of something.
a feeling of doubt or apprehension

Examples

She had a misgiving about signing the contract despite the lawyer's assurances.
His only misgiving was that the plan might not work as expected.
Even after making the decision, a misgiving lingered in his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: confidence - belief in oneself and one’s powers or abilities

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + give, originally meaning 'a bad premonition given to one’s mind'.

3. NOUN

/ mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A feeling of doubt, distrust, or apprehension about someone or something.
feeling of doubt or apprehension

Examples

She expressed a misgiving about trusting him with such important information.
Despite his excitement, he had a misgiving that the plan might backfire.
After their conversation, a small misgiving lingered in her mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: confidence - the feeling or belief that one can rely on someone or something

Etymology

From 'misgive' (to make anxious or doubtful) + '-ing'.
