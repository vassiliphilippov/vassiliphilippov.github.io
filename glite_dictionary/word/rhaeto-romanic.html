Rhaeto-Romanic

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪtoʊ roʊˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˌraɪtəʊ rəʊˈmanɪk / (BrE)
A group of Romance dialects spoken in parts of southeastern Switzerland, northern Italy, and the Tyrol region.
Romance dialects of Switzerland and northern Italy

Examples

The Swiss canton of Grisons is known for its use of Rhaeto-Romanic in official documents.
She studied the grammatical structure of Rhaeto-Romanic for her thesis on minority languages.
Some schools in northern Italy offer classes in Rhaeto-Romanic to preserve the local heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romansh - Official language of Switzerland belonging to the Rhaeto-Romanic group

Etymology

From Latin Rhaetia (an ancient Roman province) and Romance (relating to the Romance languages).
