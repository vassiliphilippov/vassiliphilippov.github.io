gatherer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæðɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðərə / (BrE)
a person who collects or gathers things, especially as part of their work or way of life
person who collects or gathers

Examples

As a gatherer, her main job was to pick wild berries and roots from the forest.
The museum displayed tools used by ancient gatherers thousands of years ago.
In many indigenous cultures, people played the role of both hunter and gatherer.

Synset Examples

they were a society of hunters and gatherers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collector - a person who collects things as a hobby or job

Etymology

From Middle English 'gaderer', from 'gaderen' (to gather) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæðərər / (AmE), / ˈɡæðərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect payments, such as rent or taxes, from others
person who collects payments

Examples

The gatherer visited each tenant to collect their monthly rent.
As a tax gatherer, she traveled from village to village on behalf of the government.
In historical times, the gatherer was often accompanied by guards for protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collector - person who collects payments for debts, rent, or taxes

Etymology

From Middle English gatheren, from Old English gadrian (to gather).
