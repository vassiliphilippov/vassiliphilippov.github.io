gaol

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
A place where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while they are waiting for trial; jail.
British spelling of jail; prison
british

Examples

He spent two years in gaol before his release.
Many historic gaols are now open to tourists in the UK.
The suspect was remanded in gaol pending his trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - A place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime

Etymology

From Old French *gaiole*, from Medieval Latin *gabiola* (cage, prison).

2. VERB

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
to put someone in prison or confine them in jail
put in prison; confine in jail
british archaic

Examples

After the trial, the judge decided to gaol the defendant for five years.
Many political activists were gaoled for speaking out against the regime.
The authorities threatened to gaol anyone caught breaking the curfew.

Synset Examples

The suspects were imprisoned without trial
the murderer was incarcerated for the rest of his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - to confine someone in prison; an alternative spelling and American form
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from imprisonment

Etymology

From Old French *jaiole*, ultimately from Latin *cavea* (cage).
