Engelmannia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛŋɡəlˈmæniə / (AmE), / ˌɛŋɡəlˈmaniə / (BrE)
A genus of erect, hairy perennial plants native to plains and prairies of the southern and central United States, with flowers resembling sunflowers.
genus of American perennial plants

Examples

The wild Engelmannia bloomed early this spring across the Texas prairie.
Botanists observed that Engelmannia thrives in dry, open landscapes.
She photographed a striking cluster of Engelmannia during her field research trip.

Etymology

Named after George Engelmann, German-American botanist (1809–1884).
