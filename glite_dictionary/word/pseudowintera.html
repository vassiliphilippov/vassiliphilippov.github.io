Pseudowintera

1. NOUN

/ suːˌdoʊˈwɪntərə / (AmE), / sjuːˌdəʊˈwɪntərə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs or small trees native to Australia and New Zealand, known for aromatic leaves; includes the horopito.
genus of evergreen shrubs or trees

Examples

Pseudowintera is commonly found in forest understoreys throughout New Zealand.
The leaves of Pseudowintera species have a distinctive peppery flavor.
Botanists are studying Pseudowintera for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: pseudo- (false) + Wintera (a plant genus named after Johann Winter).

a genus of evergreen shrubs or trees (Winteraceae)

Examples

The leaves of Pseudowintera have a distinct peppery taste.
Several species within the Pseudowintera genus are valued for their medicinal properties.
Pseudowintera is commonly found in the temperate forests of New Zealand.

Related Terms

From pseudo- (false) + Wintera (a related genus)
