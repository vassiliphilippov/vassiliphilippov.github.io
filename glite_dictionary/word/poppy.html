poppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Papaver, typically with showy, brightly colored flowers and delicate petals, often cultivated for ornamental purposes or for their seeds.
flowering plant with showy blooms

Examples

Bright red poppies blanketed the fields in spring.
She planted a poppy in her garden for its vibrant flowers.
The delicate petals of the poppy fluttered in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papaver - genus containing poppy plants
  • Degree Higher: opium poppy - species of poppy used to produce opium

Etymology

From Old English 'popæg', from Latin 'papaver' (the poppy plant).
