Acer argutum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ɑrˈɡuːtəm / (AmE), / ˈiːsə ˈɑːɡjuːtəm / (BrE)
A species of small, shrubby maple native to Japan, noted for its leaves with 5 to 7 pointed lobes and yellow autumn foliage.
Japanese shrubby maple species

Examples

The leaves of Acer argutum turn bright yellow in the fall, providing striking autumn color.
Gardeners often plant Acer argutum as an ornamental shrub in shaded areas.
Unlike some other maples, Acer argutum stays relatively small and compact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - tree or shrub of the genus Acer

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' (maple) and 'argutum' (sharp, pointed), referring to the leaf shape.
