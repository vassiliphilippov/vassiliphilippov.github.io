Cebidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛbɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈsiːbɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of New World monkeys that includes all species except marmosets and tamarins
family of New World monkeys (except marmosets, tamarins)

Examples

The family Cebidae includes howler monkeys, capuchins, and squirrel monkeys.
Researchers have studied social behavior patterns in Cebidae for decades.
Unlike marmosets and tamarins, members of the Cebidae family generally have prehensile tails.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Callitrichidae - the family including marmosets and tamarins

Etymology

From New Latin Cebidae, from genus Cebus + -idae (denoting a zoological family).
