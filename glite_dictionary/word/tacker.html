tacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtækər / (AmE), / ˈtækə / (BrE)
a hand-held device used to drive staples into materials, especially for fastening or attaching objects together
hand-held stapling device

Examples

He used a tacker to secure the fabric onto the wooden frame.
Please pass me the tacker so I can finish this upholstery project.
The tacker jammed after I tried to staple too many layers at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stapler - a device for inserting staples into paper

2. NOUN

/ ˈtækər / (AmE), / ˈtakə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to fasten things by tacking them, using tacks or spotwelding
person who fastens things by tacking

Examples

The tacker made sure every fabric edge was securely fixed in place.
During the renovation, a skilled tacker was needed to assemble the wall panels.
Her uncle worked as a tacker in a furniture factory for many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a person or thing that fastens

Etymology

From the verb 'to tack' (to fasten with tacks) + -er (agent suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtækər / (AmE), / ˈtækə / (BrE)
a person who fastens pieces of a garment with long, loose stitches, usually to hold parts together temporarily before final sewing
person who loosely sews garment pieces

Examples

The apprentice worked as a tacker before advancing to a full tailor.
A skilled tacker is essential in assembling complicated garment patterns.
She spent the morning as a tacker, preparing the dress for the seamstress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baster - a person who temporarily joins garment pieces with loose stitches

Etymology

From the verb 'to tack', meaning to fasten temporarily; 'tack' from Middle English 'tacken' (to fasten), from Old Northern French 'taquer' (to fasten).
