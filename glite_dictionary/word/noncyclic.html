noncyclic

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
Not having or involving repeated cycles.
not having repeated cycles

Examples

Unlike the cyclic process, a noncyclic process does not return to its original state.
A noncyclic compound does not contain any ring structures in its molecular makeup.
In some organisms, photosynthesis follows a noncyclic electron flow pathway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cyclic - having repeated cycles

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'cyclic' (having cycles).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01977669-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
Not forming or characterized by a cycle or ring; not cyclic.
not cyclic; not forming a cycle

Examples

The compound is noncyclic and does not contain any ring structure.
In mathematics, a noncyclic group does not have a single element that generates all others.
He studied the differences between cyclic and noncyclic processes in thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cyclic - having a cycle or recurring in cycles

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'cyclic' (from Greek 'kyklos' meaning circle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00679725-a
Synset POS: a
