charwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to clean houses or offices, usually on a part-time or occasional basis
woman employed to do cleaning
archaic british

Examples

The charwoman arrived early every Monday to dust and sweep the offices.
In Victorian novels, a charwoman often worked long hours for very little pay.
The charwoman was well known in the building for her cheerful greetings.

Synset Examples

the char will clean the carpet
Oh, good. The cleaning lady came, so I don't have to be embarrassed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaner - a person who cleans, especially as a job
  • Synonym: housekeeper - a person employed to manage household cleaning and maintenance

Etymology

From English 'char' meaning 'to do odd jobs' and 'woman'.
