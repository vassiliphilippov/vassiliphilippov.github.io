sundress

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdrɛs / (BrE)
a light, loose-fitting sleeveless dress, typically worn in warm weather, with a wide neckline and thin shoulder straps that expose the arms and shoulders
a light sleeveless summer dress

Examples

She wore a bright yellow sundress to the picnic in the park.
A lightweight sundress is perfect for hot summer days at the beach.
The little girl twirled happily in her new floral sundress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - a piece of clothing for women or girls that covers the body and hangs down over the legs

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'dress', indicating a dress suitable for sunny, warm conditions.
