nonechoic

1. ADJ

/ nʌnˌɛˈkoʊɪk / (AmE), / nʌnˌɛˈkəʊɪk / (BrE)
Not echoic; not imitative of natural sounds.
not imitative of sound

Examples

The poem uses nonechoic words to describe the action rather than mimicking actual sounds.
He preferred nonechoic language in his writing to avoid distracting from his message.
Some species use nonechoic vocalizations that do not imitate environmental noises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: echoic - imitative of natural sounds

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + echoic (imitative of sound).
