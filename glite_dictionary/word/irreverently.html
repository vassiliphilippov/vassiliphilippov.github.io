irreverently

1. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛvərəntli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously.
in a disrespectful manner

Examples

He spoke irreverently about traditions that others held dear.
The comedian often jokes irreverently about political figures.
She irreverently dismissed the old rules as outdated nonsense.

Synset Examples

the student irreverently mimicked the teacher in his presence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrespectfully - in a way that lacks respect
  • Antonym: reverently - in a deeply respectful way

Etymology

From 'irreverent' + '-ly'; 'irreverent' from Latin 'irreverent-', from 'in-' (not) + 'reverent-' (revering).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛvərəntli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously.
in a disrespectful manner

Examples

He spoke irreverently about traditions that most people cherished.
The comedian irreverently mocked authority figures during her stand-up routine.
The students treated the formal ceremony irreverently, laughing and whispering throughout.

Synset Examples

in the seventeenth century England had known fifty years of doctrinal quarrels and civil war; clergymen had been turned from their cures, and churches irreverently used

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverently - in a way that shows deep respect
  • Synonym: disrespectfully - in a manner that shows lack of respect

Etymology

From 'irreverent' (from Latin 'irreverens', meaning 'not showing respect'), plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.
