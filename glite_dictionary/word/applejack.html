applejack

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəlˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈap(ə)ldʒak / (BrE)
an alcoholic drink distilled from fermented apple cider, especially associated with North America
distilled apple cider alcoholic drink

Examples

He poured a glass of applejack to warm himself on the cold evening.
Early American settlers often made applejack from surplus apples.
You can use applejack to add depth to autumn cocktails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

From 'apple' + 'jack', where 'jack' means an alcoholic liquor; term dating to colonial America.
