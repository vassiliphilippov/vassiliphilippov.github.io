confirming

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɝːmɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to support or corroborate something, making it more certain or verified
supporting or corroborating

Examples

The witness provided confirming details that matched the initial report.
Researchers found confirming evidence in the new data.
The doctor ordered a second test for confirming the original diagnosis.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroborating - supporting with evidence or authority
  • Antonym: refuting - proving to be false or incorrect

Etymology

From Latin 'confirmare' (to strengthen, establish).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfɜrmɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a test, symptom, or sign) indicating the existence or presence of a suspected condition or pathogen; serving to confirm a diagnosis.
indicating a suspected condition is present
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a confirming test to ensure the diagnosis was accurate.
A confirming biopsy revealed the presence of cancer cells.
This blood sample serves as a confirming indicator of the infection.

Synset Examples

a positive pregnancy test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnostic - serving to identify a disease or condition
  • Degree Lower: suggestive - indicating but not confirming a possible condition
  • Degree Higher: definitive - providing a final, conclusive diagnosis

Etymology

From the verb 'confirm', from Latin 'confirmare' (to strengthen, establish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01825985-a
Synset POS: a
