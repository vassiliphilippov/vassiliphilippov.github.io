refinement

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the process of removing unwanted substances or impurities from a material, such as oil, metals, or sugar, to improve its quality.
purification process (e.g., of oil or metal)

Examples

Modern refinement techniques make it possible to produce extremely pure gold.
The refinement of crude oil yields gasoline and other useful products.
Sugar refinement removes impurities to make it suitable for consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purification - the process of making something pure by removing contaminants

Etymology

From French *raffinement*, from raffiner 'to refine'.

Examples
