biquadratic

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪkwɑːˈdrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪkwɒˈdrætɪk / (BrE)
an algebraic equation or function of the fourth degree
an equation of the fourth degree

Examples

The mathematician solved the biquadratic using advanced algebraic techniques.
A typical biquadratic may have up to four real roots.
Engineers often encounter biquadratic equations in control theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartic - a polynomial or equation of the fourth degree

Etymology

From Latin bi- (twice) + quadratic (square); literally 'double-square'.
