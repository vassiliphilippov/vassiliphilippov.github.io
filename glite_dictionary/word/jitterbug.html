jitterbug

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪt̬ɚˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪtəbʌɡ / (BrE)
a fast, lively American dance with jerky movements that was especially popular in the 1940s
lively 1940s American dance

Examples

They watched the couple perform the jitterbug at the 1940s-themed party.
Many dance halls in the 1940s were filled with people eager to do the jitterbug.
She learned to jitterbug from her grandparents, who were accomplished dancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - a popular style of dance often performed to swing music

Etymology

Originally American English, likely from 'jitter' (nervous excitement) and 'bug' (person fond of something), reflecting the dance's energetic style.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪtərˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪtəbʌɡ / (BrE)
to dance the jitterbug, a fast-paced dance popularized in the 1930s and 1940s, typically performed to swing music
to dance the jitterbug

Examples

We learned how to jitterbug at the dance class last summer.
The couples jitterbugged across the wooden floor to the lively swing band.
Every Saturday night, they would jitterbug until midnight at the old club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music

Etymology

From the noun 'jitterbug', originally denoting a person who danced energetically to swing music.

Synset POS: v
