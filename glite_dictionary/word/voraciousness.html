voraciousness

1. NOUN

/ vɔˈreɪʃənəsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having an excessive or insatiable desire, especially for wealth, food, or other possessions
excessive or insatiable desire

Examples

Her voraciousness for success often led her to prioritize work over family.
The voraciousness of the investors drove property prices to unsustainable levels.
He was criticized for his voraciousness when it came to accumulating wealth.

Synset Examples

the greediness of lawyers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From Latin *vorax* (greedy), from *vorare* (to devour) + English suffix *-ness*.

2. NOUN

/ vɔˈreɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / vɒˈreɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having an excessive or insatiable appetite.
excessive craving for food

Examples

Her voraciousness at the dinner table surprised everyone.
The child's voraciousness for snacks was hard to satisfy.
Despite his voraciousness, he remained remarkably slim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Degree Lower: appetite - a natural desire to eat

Etymology

From Latin 'voracitas' (greediness), from 'vorax' (greedy), from 'vorare' (to devour).

3. NOUN

/ vəˈreɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having an insatiable appetite for food, activity, or experience
insatiable appetite or greed

Examples

Her voraciousness at the dinner table surprised everyone present.
The voraciousness of his curiosity led him to read every book in the library.
Some investors approach the stock market with a voraciousness that borders on obsession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - excessive greed for food
  • Antonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes

Etymology

From Latin vorax (greedy, devouring), from vorare (to devour) + English suffix -ness.
