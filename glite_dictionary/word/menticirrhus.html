Menticirrhus

1. NOUN

/ mɛn.tɪˈsɜːr.əs / (AmE), / mɛn.tɪˈsɜː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Sciaenidae, commonly known as kingfish or whiting, found in coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of marine fish (kingfish/whiting)

Examples

The Atlantic species of Menticirrhus are popular targets for recreational anglers.
Coastal waters along the Gulf of Mexico are home to several Menticirrhus species.
The distinguishing feature of Menticirrhus is the small barbel on their chin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfish - common name for several species in various genera including Menticirrhus
  • Synonym: whiting - another common name for Menticirrhus species

Etymology

From Latin mentum (chin) + cirrhus (curl), referring to the chin barbel characteristic in the genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪr.əs / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪr.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the drum family (Sciaenidae), commonly known as kingfishes or whitings, found in coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of kingfishes or whitings

Examples

The Menticirrhus genus includes several fish popular among anglers along the American coasts.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Menticirrhus genus.
Commercial fisheries often target Menticirrhus species for their mild, white flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfish - a common name for several species in the genus Menticirrhus
  • Synonym: whiting - another common name for some Menticirrhus species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Latin 'mentum' (chin) and 'cirrus' (curl, tendril), referring to the chin barbel characteristic of the genus.
