to improvise

1. VERB

/ ˈɪm.prəˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prəˌvaɪz / (BrE)
to perform, create, or do something without prior preparation; to make or deliver spontaneously
perform or create without preparation

Examples

When the actor forgot his line, he chose to improvise rather than stop the scene.
She had to improvise a recipe with whatever ingredients she found in the kitchen.
The band loves to improvise during their live performances, making each show unique.

Synset Examples

he extemporized a speech at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extemporize - to perform or speak without preparation

Etymology

From French 'improviser', from Italian 'improvvisare', from 'improvviso' meaning 'unforeseen', itself from Latin 'improvisus'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɪm.prəˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prə.vaɪz / (BrE)
to perform, produce, or make something without previous preparation or planning, especially by using whatever is available
perform or create without preparation

Examples

When the actor forgot his lines, he had to improvise to keep the scene going.
With no recipe on hand, the chef decided to improvise a dish from what was in the fridge.
The jazz musicians love to improvise during live performances, creating unique sounds each night.

Synset Examples

he extemporized a speech at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extemporize - to perform or speak without preparation

Etymology

From French improviser, from Italian improvvisare, from improvviso (unexpected).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01732719-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɪm.prəˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prə.vaɪz / (BrE)
to manage by making use of whatever is at hand when the usual means are unavailable
manage with available resources

Examples

With no tools available, we had to improvise a shelter using branches and leaves.
The chef forgot an ingredient and had to improvise with what was left in the pantry.
During the blackout, she improvised a lamp out of a glass jar and a candle.

Synset Examples

after the hurricane destroyed our house, we had to improvise for weeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make do - to manage with what is available
  • Synonym: to adapt - to adjust to different conditions

Etymology

From French 'improviser', from Italian 'improvvisare' (to compose extemporaneously), from Latin 'improvisus' (unforeseen).
