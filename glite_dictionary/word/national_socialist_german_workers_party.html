National Socialist German Workers' Party

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃənəl soʊˈʃɪlɪst ˈdʒɜːrmən ˈwɜːrkərz ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəl səʊˈʃɪlɪst ˈdʒɜːmən ˈwɜːkəz ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
the political party founded in Germany in 1919, led by Adolf Hitler from 1921, which ruled Germany as a dictatorship from 1933 to 1945; also known as the Nazi Party
German political party (Nazi Party) 1919–1945

Examples

The National Socialist German Workers' Party rose to power in Germany in the early 1930s.
Historians have extensively studied the policies of the National Socialist German Workers' Party.
He wrote his thesis on the impact of the National Socialist German Workers' Party on European politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nazi Party - common short form for the National Socialist German Workers' Party

Etymology

From German Nationalsozialistische Deutsche Arbeiterpartei.
