commerce

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mɚs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɜːs / (BrE)
the activities involved in buying and selling goods and services, especially on a large scale
buying and selling of goods and services

Examples

International commerce has grown rapidly over the last few decades.
The internet has transformed the way commerce operates globally.
Local businesses depend on commerce for their survival and growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - the action of buying and selling goods and services

Etymology

From Latin 'commercium' (trade, traffic), from 'com-' (together) + 'merx' (goods, merchandise).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mɝːs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɜːs / (BrE)
communication or dealings between people, especially the exchange of ideas, opinions, or sentiments.
social exchange of ideas or attitudes

Examples

The commerce of great minds often leads to groundbreaking ideas.
Poetry thrives on the commerce of emotions between writer and reader.
Throughout the evening, lively commerce of opinions filled the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversation - an informal exchange of ideas by spoken words

Etymology

From Old French 'commerce', from Latin 'commercium' (trade, intercourse), from 'com-' (together) + 'merx' (goods, merchandise).

Synset POS: n
