brevet

1. NOUN

/ brəˈvɛt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛvɪt / (BrE)
a document conferring a military officer with a temporary higher rank without an increase in pay
temporary military commission

Examples

The general received a brevet raising him to major for his outstanding service.
Her brevet allowed her to command a larger unit, though her pay remained unchanged.
During the war, many officers were granted a brevet to fill urgent leadership roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - an official document appointing someone to office or rank
  • Degree Higher: promotion - the act of raising someone to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From French 'brevet' (certificate, patent), from Old French 'brief' (letter, patent).

2. VERB

/ brəˈvɛt, ˈbrɛv.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛv.ɪt, brəˈvɛt / (BrE)
(of a military authority) to promote a person to a higher military rank, usually without the corresponding pay or privileges of the new grade.
promote (military) without pay/privileges

Examples

The general decided to brevet several officers after the successful campaign.
He was breveted to major for his exceptional service during the war.
The army often brevets junior officers in recognition of gallantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From French 'brevet' (patent, commission), diminutive of 'bref' (letter, official document).

Synset POS: v
