Metasequoia glyptostroboides

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtə səˈkwɔɪə ˌɡlɪptoʊˌstrɒbɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˌmɛtə sɪˈkwɔɪə ˌɡlɪptəʊˌstrɒbɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A large, fast-growing deciduous tree native to China, with flat bright-green leaves and small globular cones, often cultivated as an ornamental; known as the dawn redwood.
species of large deciduous Chinese tree (dawn redwood)

Examples

The Metasequoia glyptostroboides is prized by landscapers for its rapid growth and elegant appearance.
Botanists were amazed when living specimens of Metasequoia glyptostroboides were discovered in China in the 1940s.
Many arboretums in the United States now feature Metasequoia glyptostroboides as a centerpiece tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn redwood - common name for Metasequoia glyptostroboides

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'meta-' (after), 'sequoia' (genus name), and 'glyptostroboides' (resembling Glyptostrobus, another genus of conifers).
