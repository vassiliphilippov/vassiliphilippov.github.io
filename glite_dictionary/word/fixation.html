fixation

1. NOUN

/ fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(Histology) The process of preserving and hardening a tissue sample so that its structure remains as close as possible to its natural state.
preservation of tissue in histology
medical

Examples

Proper fixation of the tissue is essential for accurate microscopic analysis.
The specimen underwent fixation before it was embedded in paraffin.
A common fixation method involves using formaldehyde to preserve cellular structures.

Etymology

From Latin 'fixare' (to fix), from 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

2. NOUN

/ fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of firmly attaching, securing, or fastening something in a set position
firm attachment or fastening

Examples

The fixation of the shelf to the wall required drilling holes and using strong anchors.
Surgical fixation of fractured bones often involves metal plates or screws.
Proper fixation of street signs ensures they remain stable during high winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attachment - the act of attaching something
  • Antonym: release - the act of letting something go

Etymology

From Latin fixare (to fix) via French fixation.

3. NOUN

/ fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
an obsessive and unhealthy preoccupation with someone or something, often to the detriment of normal interests or well-being
obsessive unhealthy preoccupation

Examples

Her fixation on healthy eating made it difficult for her to enjoy meals with friends.
Ever since he lost the game, he developed a fixation with improving his performance.
His fixation on achieving perfection sometimes hindered his progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies the mind

Etymology

From Latin 'fixare' (to fix) via French 'fixation'.

4. NOUN

/ fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal state in which development has stopped prematurely, especially in psychology where emotional or sexual development is arrested at a particular stage
abnormal arrested development (psychology)
medical

Examples

Some adults display a childhood fixation that hinders emotional growth.
Psychoanalysts believe that a fixation can influence behavior throughout life.
The patient's fixation at the oral stage explained certain habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression - a return to a former or less developed state, used in psychology

Etymology

From Latin 'fixatio', from 'fixare' (to fix).
