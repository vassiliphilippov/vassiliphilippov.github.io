Mellaril

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛləˌrɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɛləˌrɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked antipsychotic drug (thioridazine) formerly used to treat schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders.
antipsychotic drug (brand name)
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist prescribed Mellaril to help manage his symptoms.
Some patients experienced side effects while taking Mellaril for schizophrenia.
Due to safety concerns, Mellaril is no longer commonly used in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thioridazine - the generic name of the drug sold as Mellaril
