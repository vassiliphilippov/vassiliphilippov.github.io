Larrea

1. NOUN

/ ləˈreɪ.ə / (AmE), / ləˈreɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of xerophytic evergreen shrubs native to South America and the southwestern United States, including the creosote bush.
genus of xerophytic evergreen shrubs

Examples

Larrea is a dominant shrub in the deserts of the southwestern United States.
Many plant biologists study Larrea to understand adaptation to arid environments.
The Larrea genus includes the well-known creosote bush.

Etymology

Named after Spanish bishop J. A. Hernández Pérez de Larrea (1730–1803).

2. NOUN

/ lɑːˈreɪə / (AmE), / ləˈriːə / (BrE)
A genus of xerophytic evergreen shrubs native to South America and the southwestern United States, known commonly as creosote bushes.
a genus of xerophytic shrubs (creosote bushes)

Examples

The Larrea genus includes the well-known creosote bush, which thrives in desert regions.
Ecologists study Larrea to understand plant adaptation to arid environments.
Many birds and insects rely on Larrea shrubs for shelter and food in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creosote bush - a common name for some species in the genus Larrea

Etymology

Named after Spanish physician and botanist Juan Antonio Hernández Pérez de Larrea.
