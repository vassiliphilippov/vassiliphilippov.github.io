to restart

1. VERB

/ riˈstɑrt / (AmE), / riˈstɑːt / (BrE)
to start something again after it has stopped or been interrupted
start again after stopping

Examples

After a brief technical issue, they managed to restart the presentation.
The computer will automatically restart after the updates are installed.
We agreed to restart talks to resolve the disagreement.

Synset Examples

We resumed the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption
  • Degree Lower: to continue - to persist in an activity without stopping
  • Degree Lower: to begin - to start something for the first time

Etymology

From re- (again) + start.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350758-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈstɑːrt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈstɑːt / (BrE)
to start something again, especially after it has been stopped or paused
start again after stopping

Examples

After the power outage, we had to restart the computer to finish our work.
She pressed the button to restart the engine, hoping it would run smoothly this time.
They will restart negotiations next week, after a brief pause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin something
  • Antonym: stop - to bring to an end; to cease operating

Etymology

From the prefix 're-' (again) + 'start'; formed in English.

Examples

Sometimes you need to restart your phone to fix minor issues.
