Thuja

1. NOUN

/ ˈθuːdʒə / (AmE), / ˈθjuːdʒə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs of the cypress family, commonly known as arborvitae, some species of which are called red cedar
genus of evergreen coniferous trees (arborvitae)

Examples

The Thuja hedge in their garden provided privacy all year round.
Some species of Thuja are commonly referred to as northern white cedar.
Carpenters often use wood from Thuja trees because of its pleasant aroma and resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - another common name for trees of the genus Thuja

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek thuia (a type of tree, probably juniper or cedar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθuːdʒə / (AmE), / ˈθjuːdʒə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family, commonly known as arborvitae or red cedars, widely grown for ornamental use and timber.
a genus of evergreen conifers (arborvitae)

Examples

The Thuja in our backyard provides shade all year round.
Many parks use Thuja hedges for privacy and decoration.
Botanists study the various species within the Thuja genus for their ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - common name for species of Thuja
  • Synonym: red cedar - common name for Thuja species

Etymology

From Latin Thuja, from Ancient Greek θύια (thuia), a type of juniper.
