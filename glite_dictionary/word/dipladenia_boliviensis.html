Dipladenia boliviensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈdiːniə ˌbɒlɪˈviːənsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈdiːniə ˌbɒlɪˈviːənsɪs / (BrE)
A species of shrubby climbing plant with glossy leaves and white funnel-shaped flowers with yellow throats, native to Bolivia and neighboring regions.
a species of shrubby climbing plant

Examples

The Dipladenia boliviensis thrives in warm, sunny climates and is popular as an ornamental vine.
Gardeners appreciate the Dipladenia boliviensis for its abundant white flowers and lush foliage.
She planted a Dipladenia boliviensis by the trellis to add exotic charm to her garden.

Etymology

Dipladenia from Greek 'diplasios' (double) and 'aden' (gland); boliviensis refers to Bolivia, the region of origin.
