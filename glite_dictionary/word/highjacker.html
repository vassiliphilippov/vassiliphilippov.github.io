highjacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒækər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒækə / (BrE)
A person who illegally seizes or takes control of a vehicle, especially by force.
someone who illegally takes control of a vehicle

Examples

The highjacker demanded money before allowing the passengers to leave the bus.
Police arrested the highjacker shortly after he seized the delivery truck.
The train was stopped by a masked highjacker late last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijacker - a person who unlawfully takes control of a vehicle, especially an aircraft

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'hijacker'; from 'to hijack', originally American slang, possibly from 'high' + 'jack' (hold up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒækər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒækə / (BrE)
A person who seizes control of a vehicle, especially an aircraft, by force and directs it to a different destination.
person who seizes a vehicle by force

Examples

The highjacker threatened the pilot and demanded to be flown to another country.
Several highjackers took control of the airplane during the night.
Police quickly responded when they learned there was a highjacker on board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijacker - a person who seizes a vehicle by force

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'hijacker'; from 'hijack', possibly altered from slang 'high' + 'jack' (rob).
