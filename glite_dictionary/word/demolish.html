to demolish

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to eat food quickly and completely, especially with great enthusiasm or hunger
eat quickly and completely
informal

Examples

The kids demolished their ice creams in record time.
She demolished a giant burger after running the marathon.
They demolished the cake before anyone else had a chance to try it.

Synset Examples

Some people can down a pound of meat in the course of one meal
The teenagers demolished four pizzas among them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat hungrily or quickly

2. VERB

/ dəˈmɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
To defeat (an opponent) thoroughly and humiliatingly.
defeat thoroughly and humiliatingly

Examples

The home team demolished their rivals in the finals, winning 6-0.
She demolished her opponent in the debate with clear arguments and wit.
The defending champions demolished the underdogs in the semifinal match.

Synset Examples

The home team demolished the visitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat decisively
  • Synonym: thrash - to defeat heavily in a contest
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated

Etymology

From Latin demoliri (to tear down), from de- (down) + moliri (to build, to undertake).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to destroy something completely, especially a building or other structure
destroy completely, especially a building

Examples

The city plans to demolish the old factory next month to make way for a new park.
The stadium was demolished after being declared unsafe by engineers.
Protesters watched as the historic building was demolished in a matter of hours.

Synset Examples

the wrecking ball demolished the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly it no longer exists or cannot be used
  • Antonym: construct - to build or make something

Etymology

From Latin demoliri (to tear down), from de- (down) + moliri (to build, undertake).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
To completely destroy or ruin something, especially something abstract such as an argument, reputation, or belief.
destroy or ruin completely

Examples

The new evidence demolished the theory that had been accepted for years.
The review completely demolished the author's reputation.
The lawyer skillfully demolished the witness's argument in court.

Synset Examples

demolished my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to cause something to end or no longer exist
  • Antonym: build - to make something by putting parts together

Etymology

From French démolir, from Latin demoliri (to pull down, destroy).
