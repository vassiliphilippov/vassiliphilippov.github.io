apprehensively

1. ADV

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / ˌap.rɪˈhen.sɪv.li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows anxiety or fear that something bad or unpleasant will happen.
with anxiety or worry

Examples

She looked apprehensively at the dark clouds gathering overhead.
He waited apprehensively for the results of the exam.
The dog glanced apprehensively at the stranger.

Synset Examples

we watched anxiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiously - In a manner showing worry or unease

Etymology

Formed from 'apprehensive' (mid 16th century, from apprehend + -ive) + -ly.
