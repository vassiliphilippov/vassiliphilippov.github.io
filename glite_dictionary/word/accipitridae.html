Accipitridae

1. NOUN

/ ækˌsɪpɪˈtrɪdiː / (AmE), / ækˌsɪpɪˈtrɪdɪː / (BrE)
a family of birds of prey including hawks, eagles, kites, harriers, and Old World vultures
family of birds of prey: hawks, eagles, kites, etc.

Examples

The Accipitridae family includes some of the most powerful raptors, such as eagles and hawks.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Accipitridae during their expedition to the rainforest.
The Accipitridae are known for their keen eyesight and predatory skills.

Etymology

From modern Latin Accipitridae, from Accipiter (a genus of hawks), based on Latin accipiter (hawk).

2. NOUN

/ ækˌsɪpɪˈtrɪdiː / (AmE), / ækˌsɪpɪˈtrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds of prey including hawks, eagles, kites, harriers, and Old World vultures
family of birds of prey

Examples

Many species in the Accipitridae family have remarkable eyesight, allowing them to spot prey from great distances.
The golden eagle is a prominent member of the Accipitridae family.
Ornithologists study the Accipitridae to better understand the behavior of birds of prey.

Etymology

From Latin *Accipiter* (hawk) + scientific family suffix *-idae*.
