Ononis repens

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnoʊnɪs rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnɪs rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
A European woody plant with pink flowers, unifoliate leaves, and long tough roots, known for spreading by underground runners; commonly called creeping restharrow.
European woody plant; creeping restharrow

Examples

The sandy dunes were covered with patches of Ononis repens in full bloom.
Botanists studied how Ononis repens helped prevent soil erosion along the coast.
Gardeners sometimes plant Ononis repens as a hardy ground cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restharrow - European plant of the genus Ononis with tough roots, used as a synonym for Ononis repens.

Etymology

From the genus name Ononis (Greek: onos, 'donkey'), with 'repens' meaning 'creeping' in Latin.
