candyfloss

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.di.flɑs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.di.flɒs / (BrE)
A light, fluffy sweet made by spinning sugar that has been heated to a high temperature, typically sold at fairs and carnivals; known as 'cotton candy' in American English.
spun sugar sweet (cotton candy)
british

Examples

The children ran to the stall to buy candyfloss at the fairground.
Her hands were sticky after eating a huge pink swirl of candyfloss.
Balloons and candyfloss are essential parts of any funfair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton candy - spun sugar sweet, American English term
