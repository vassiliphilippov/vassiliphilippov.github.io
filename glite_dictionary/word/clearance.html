clearance

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rəns / (BrE)
the amount of space or distance by which one object is separated from another, allowing passage or preventing contact
space or distance between objects

Examples

The truck barely made it under the bridge due to the low clearance.
Check the clearance between the door and the floor to ensure it will open smoothly.
Aircraft must maintain a safe clearance from other planes during takeoff and landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an empty space or interval; a break in continuity
  • Antonym: obstruction - something that blocks or fills a passage; an obstacle

Etymology

From clear + -ance, patterned after words like allowance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rəns / (BrE)
the amount of vertical space available under something, allowing objects or people to pass beneath it safely
vertical space under an object

Examples

The tunnel's clearance is only 3 meters, so large trucks cannot pass through.
Always check the clearance before driving under a low bridge.
The parking garage has limited clearance for tall vehicles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headroom - the vertical space above someone's head, especially in a vehicle or under an object

Etymology

From clear + -ance, ultimately from Old French clarer (to make clear).

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rəns / (BrE)
official authorization or permission to proceed, especially to enter or leave a restricted area or to take a particular action
official permission to proceed

Examples

The pilot waited for clearance from air traffic control before beginning the takeoff.
We need security clearance to access the restricted area.
Customs can only release the goods after proper clearance has been granted.

Synset Examples

the plane was given clearance to land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - an official document giving someone authorization to do something
  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval
  • Antonym: refusal - denial of permission to do something

Etymology

From clear + -ance, patterned on similar formations indicating the state or action of clearing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01143914-n
Synset POS: n
