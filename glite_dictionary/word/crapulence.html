crapulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræpjələns / (AmE), / ˈkræpjʊləns / (BrE)
Excessive indulgence in drinking alcohol; the sickness or feeling resulting from such overindulgence.
excessive drinking of alcohol
archaic

Examples

His night of crapulence left him feeling ill the next morning.
The poet wrote about crapulence and regret after wild festivities.
Medical texts of the 18th century warned against the dangers of crapulence.

Synset Examples

drink was his downfall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriation - the state of being intoxicated; drunkenness

Etymology

From Medieval Latin crapulentia, from Latin crapula (drunkenness), from Greek kraipalē (headache from drinking).
