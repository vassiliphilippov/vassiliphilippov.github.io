bruin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruː.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbruː.ɪn / (BrE)
a brown bear, especially in northern Europe or as used in fables and literature
brown bear, esp. in literature

Examples

In old European tales, the bruin is often depicted as a cunning animal.
The naturalist photographed a bruin fishing in the river at dawn.
Many children’s stories feature a bruin as a central character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - large bear species (Ursus arctos) found in Eurasia and North America
  • Antonym: polar bear - a large white bear native to Arctic regions

Etymology

From Middle Dutch bruun (brown), used for the bear in medieval stories.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈbruːɪn / (BrE)
a bear, especially as a character in folktales and fables, originally from the epic ‘Reynard the Fox’
bear (in folktales; literary)

Examples

In the fairy tale, bruin outwitted the other animals with his strength and cunning.
Many old European stories feature bruin as a symbol of power and resilience.
The children listened eagerly as their grandmother described how bruin stole the honey from the bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - large, heavy mammal with thick fur and a short tail

Etymology

From Dutch 'bruijn' (brown), nickname for the bear character in ‘Reynard the Fox’.
