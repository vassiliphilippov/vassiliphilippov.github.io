prudery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːdəri / (AmE), / ˈpruːdəri / (BrE)
excessive or affected modesty or propriety, especially in matters relating to sex
excessive or affected modesty

Examples

Her prudery was apparent whenever the conversation turned to romance or intimacy.
The author's prudery led her to avoid any mention of controversial topics in her writing.
Some found his prudery old-fashioned and unnecessary in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: modesty - behavior, manner, or appearance intended to avoid impropriety or indecency
  • Synonym: prudishness - the quality of being easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity

Etymology

From French *pruderie*, from *prude* (prudent or excessively proper woman).
