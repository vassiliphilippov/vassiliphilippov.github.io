brogan

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.ɡən / (BrE)
A coarse, heavy shoe, often made of thick leather, traditionally worn for work or in rough conditions.
heavy, coarse work shoe

Examples

He wore a pair of worn-out brogans while working on the farm.
The old miner’s brogans were caked with mud at the end of the day.
She bought sturdy brogans for hiking in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boot - A covering for the foot and lower leg, often with a sturdy sole

Etymology

From Irish and Scottish Gaelic 'bróg' (shoe), diminutive of Old Irish 'bróc'.
