Caesalpinia

1. NOUN

/ siːzælˈpɪniə / (AmE), / siːzælˈpɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of small spiny tropical trees or shrubs, some species of which are cultivated for their showy flowers; includes the group Poinciana.
genus of tropical flowering trees or shrubs

Examples

The botanical garden features several species of Caesalpinia with their vibrant yellow blossoms.
Caesalpinia trees thrive in warm, tropical climates and require plenty of sunlight to grow.
Researchers are studying Caesalpinia for its potential medicinal properties and ecological benefits.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino (1519–1603).
