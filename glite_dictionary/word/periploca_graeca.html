Periploca graeca

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ɪˌploʊ.kə ˈɡriː.kə / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˌpləʊ.kə ˈɡriː.kə / (BrE)
A deciduous climbing shrub with dark, glossy leaves, ill-scented but interesting flowers, and poisonous yellow fruits, native to southeastern Europe and Asia Minor, often grown for ornamental purposes.
a climbing shrub with dark leaves and poisonous yellow fruit

Examples

The Periploca graeca in our garden climbs beautifully over the archway each summer.
Gardeners appreciate Periploca graeca for its striking foliage and unusual flowers despite its unpleasant scent.
Caution is advised, as the fruits of Periploca graeca are poisonous if ingested.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'periploka' (the genus name) and 'graeca' (Greek), referring to its origin.
