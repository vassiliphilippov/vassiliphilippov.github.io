bondswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who signs a legal bond as surety for someone else's obligations
female surety for a legal bond

Examples

The court accepted her offer to serve as bondswoman for her brother's bail.
As a bondswoman, she was legally responsible if the accused failed to appear.
Only a trusted bondswoman would take on such a financial risk for another person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondsman - a man who signs a legal bond as surety for someone else

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' + 'woman,' by analogy to 'bondsman.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is bound to serve another person without wages, often as a slave or servant
female bound servant or slave
archaic

Examples

In the old estate records, a bondswoman was listed among the household staff.
The story is about a bondswoman who gained her freedom through courage and wit.
Many a bondswoman dreamed of liberty despite harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondsman - a male bound to serve without wages
  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' (meaning a person bound in servitude) + 'woman'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is held in slavery or bondage; a female slave
female slave or woman in bondage

Examples

In the ancient city, a wealthy merchant owned several bondswomen who worked in his household.
The novel tells the story of a bondswoman who fought bravely for her freedom.
Several historical documents mention the treatment of bondswoman in different societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondsman - a man who is a slave or held in bondage
  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freewoman - a woman who is not in bondage or slavery

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' (meaning bondage or captivity) + 'woman', modeled after 'bondsman'.
