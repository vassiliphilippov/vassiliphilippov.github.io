Alnus rubra

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs ˈruːbrə / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs ˈruː.brə / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its hard red wood often used in furniture making
Pacific Northwest tree with hard red wood

Examples

The dense growth of Alnus rubra helped restore the riverbanks after the flood.
Furniture makers often choose Alnus rubra for its reliable, fine-grained wood.
In Pacific Northwest forests, Alnus rubra is an important pioneer species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red alder - common name for Alnus rubra, a large Pacific Northwest tree

Etymology

From Latin: alnus (alder) + rubra (red).
