hilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪləs / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A depression or fissure in an organ where blood vessels, nerves, or ducts enter or leave.
part of an organ where vessels enter or leave
medical

Examples

The hilus of the kidney is where the renal artery, vein, and ureter enter and exit the organ.
Lymph nodes have a hilus through which blood vessels and efferent lymphatic vessels pass.
During the dissection, the students identified the hilus of the lung as the entry point for major bronchi and vessels.

Synset Examples

the hilus of the kidney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilum - depression or fissure where vessels or nerves enter an organ (preferred current term)

Etymology

From Latin 'hilum' (a trifle, the eye of a bean), anatomical sense by analogy.
