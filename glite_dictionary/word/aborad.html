aborad

1. ADV

/ əˈbɔːræd / (AmE), / əˈbɔːrad / (BrE)
In zoology and anatomy, away from the mouth or oral region of an organism.
away from the mouth (anatomy)
medical

Examples

In certain invertebrates, nutrients move aborad from the gut toward the posterior end.
The direction of the incision was made aborad to minimize damage to oral structures.
Cilia on the surface drive food particles aborad away from the mouth opening.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orad - toward the mouth or oral region (anatomical direction)

Etymology

From Latin 'ab' (from, away from) + 'os, oris' (mouth) + -ad (toward).
