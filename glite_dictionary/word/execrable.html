execrable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛksɪkrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪkrəbəl / (BrE)
extremely bad or unpleasant; deserving to be detested or condemned.
extremely bad; detestable

Examples

The conditions in the prison were execrable and shocked all who visited.
His execrable behavior led to his dismissal from the committee.
Critics described the film as execrable, warning audiences to avoid it.

Synset Examples

abominable treatment of prisoners
detestable vices
execrable crimes
consequences odious to those you govern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abominable - causing moral revulsion; extremely bad
  • Synonym: detestable - deserving intense dislike
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving respect or approval

Etymology

From Latin execrabilis (accursed, detestable), from execrari (to curse).
