expository

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɑːzɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
intended to explain or describe something; serving to expound or set forth information clearly.
intended to explain or describe

Examples

Her essays are known for their clear expository style and logical structure.
The teacher prefers expository texts because they help students understand complex ideas.
We studied different types of writing, including argumentative, narrative, and expository.

Synset Examples

clean expository writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descriptive - presenting or depicting details, especially in writing
  • Antonym: narrative - told as a story, or relating to the act of narrating events

Etymology

From Late Latin expositorius (serving to explain), from exponere (to expound).
