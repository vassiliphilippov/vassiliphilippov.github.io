cheiloscopy

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈlɒskəpi / (AmE), / kaɪˈlɒskəpi / (BrE)
the study and analysis of lip prints for the purpose of identifying individuals, especially in forensic investigations
forensic study of lip prints
medical

Examples

Cheiloscopy is increasingly recognized as a reliable method for personal identification in criminal investigations.
Forensic scientists used cheiloscopy to match the suspect's lip print left at the scene.
While not as common as fingerprinting, cheiloscopy can provide critical evidence in certain cases.

Synset Examples

Lip prints and their study, cheiloscopy, have been the topic of research and are also occasionally mentioned in literature by a detective, a crime scene investigator, or an examiner in another field of evidence comparison.

Etymology

From Greek kheilos (lip) + -scopy (to look or observe).
