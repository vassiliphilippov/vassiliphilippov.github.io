Polk

1. PROPN

/ poʊk / (AmE), / pəʊk / (BrE)
James K. Polk (1795–1849), the 11th President of the United States, known for his role in expanding U.S. territory during his presidency.
11th President of the United States (1795–1849)

Examples

Polk served as the 11th President of the United States from 1845 to 1849.
The Mexican-American War began during Polk's presidency.
Many historians credit Polk with significantly expanding the territory of the United States.
