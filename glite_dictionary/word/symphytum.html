Symphytum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmfɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmfɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants commonly known as comfrey, used in herbal medicine and gardening.
genus of plants, comfrey

Examples

Symphytum is widely grown in herb gardens for its medicinal properties.
The roots of Symphytum have been used traditionally to treat wounds and bruises.
Many gardeners plant Symphytum near compost heaps to improve soil quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfrey - common name for plants in the genus Symphytum

Etymology

From Greek *symphytos* meaning 'grown together', referring to the plant's reputed ability to promote healing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmfɪˌtəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmfɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the borage family, commonly known as comfrey, with species widely cultivated for medicinal and ornamental purposes.
a genus of plants known as comfrey

Examples

The herb Symphytum is valued in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
Several species of Symphytum are cultivated in gardens for their attractive foliage and flowers.
Symphytum officinale, or common comfrey, is often used as organic fertilizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfrey - a common name for plants of the genus Symphytum

Etymology

From Greek *symphytos* (grown together), referring to its traditional medicinal use in healing bones.
