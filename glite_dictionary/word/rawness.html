rawness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɔːnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being crude, unfinished, or lacking refinement; incompleteness or imperfection
state of being crude or incomplete

Examples

The critics noted the rawness of the artist's early work, pointing out its lack of polish.
Many readers are drawn to the rawness of autobiographies that reveal unfiltered emotions.
Despite the rawness of the proposal, the committee saw potential in its innovative ideas.

Synset Examples

the study was criticized for incompleteness of data but it stimulated further research
the rawness of his diary made it unpublishable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crudeness - the quality of being in a natural or unrefined state
  • Antonym: refinement - the process or result of improving or perfecting

Etymology

From 'raw' + '-ness', indicating the state or quality of being raw.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔːnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɔːnəs / (BrE)
the state of lacking experience, knowledge, or skill; inexperience.
lack of experience or skill

Examples

The rawness of the new recruits was evident on their first day.
Due to her rawness, she made several predictable mistakes in her first job.
The team's rawness showed in their confused response to the sudden problem.

Synset Examples

procedural inexperience created difficulties
their poor behavior was due to the rawness of the troops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexperience - lack of knowledge or skill from practice
  • Antonym: experience - practical contact with and observation of facts or events

Etymology

From 'raw' (untrained, unrefined) + '-ness' (noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɔnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɔːnəs / (BrE)
a painful or tender sensation in a part of the body, often as if the area is abraded or irritated
painful tenderness or irritation (body)

Examples

He complained of a rawness in his throat after the long lecture.
The rawness of her skin made every touch uncomfortable.
After the surgery, he experienced significant rawness at the incision site.

Synset Examples

the best results are generally obtained by inserting the needle into the point of maximum tenderness
after taking a cold, rawness of the larynx and trachea come on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenderness - sensitivity to pain when touched

Etymology

From the adjective 'raw' (Old English 'hrēaw', uncooked, sore) + suffix '-ness'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɔnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɔːnəs / (BrE)
a chilly damp quality, especially in the air; an unpleasant feeling of cold and moisture
chilly dampness, especially in air

Examples

He shivered in the rawness of the early morning fog.
The rawness in the basement made it uncomfortable to stay there for long.
We could feel the rawness of the wind as we stepped outside.

Synset Examples

the rawness of the midnight air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampness - a slight wetness in the air or on a surface
  • Synonym: chilliness - a sensation or condition of being cold

Etymology

From 'raw' + '-ness', with 'raw' in Middle English meaning cold or chilly.
