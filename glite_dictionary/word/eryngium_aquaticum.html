Eryngium aquaticum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪndʒiəm əˈkwɑːtɪkəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪndʒiəm əˈkwætɪkəm / (BrE)
A coarse, prickly perennial plant with aromatic roots, native to the southeastern United States, belonging to the eryngo genus and sometimes confused with rattlesnake master.
a North American aromatic, prickly perennial plant

Examples

Herbalists prize the aromatic roots of Eryngium aquaticum for traditional remedies.
The wetlands of Georgia are home to large patches of Eryngium aquaticum.
Don’t confuse Eryngium aquaticum with rattlesnake master, though their flowers look alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake master - a similar North American prairie plant often confused with Eryngium aquaticum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Eryngium' refers to a group of spiny plants; 'aquaticum' meaning 'of water.'
