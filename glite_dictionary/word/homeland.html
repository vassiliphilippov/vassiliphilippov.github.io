homeland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmlænd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmlænd / (BrE)
the country where someone was born or has strong historical ties, and often regards as their native land
someone's native country

Examples

After years abroad, Maria longed to return to her homeland and see her family again.
The refugees were forced to leave their homeland during the war.
He proudly spoke about the traditions of his homeland at the cultural event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherland - the country where one was born
  • Synonym: fatherland - the country of one's birth or ancestors
  • Antonym: foreign land - a country that is not one's own

Etymology

Formed from 'home' + 'land', meaning one's own land or native country; in use since the 17th century.
