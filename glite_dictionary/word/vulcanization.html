vulcanization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvʌlkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of treating rubber or similar materials with sulfur at high temperatures to improve their strength, elasticity, or hardness
process of hardening rubber with sulfur

Examples

The vulcanization of rubber revolutionized the tire industry.
Charles Goodyear is credited with discovering vulcanization by accident in the 19th century.
Modern footwear often relies on the vulcanization process for durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curing - the process of hardening or preserving a material

Etymology

From Latin Vulcanus (the Roman god of fire), reflecting the process's use of heat.
