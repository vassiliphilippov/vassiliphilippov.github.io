luminous

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
emitting or reflecting a gentle, soft light; shining with a faint or steady glow
softly bright; radiant

Examples

The luminous stars lit up the night sky.
Her dress had a luminous quality under the soft lights of the stage.
We admired the luminous glow of the lanterns floating down the river.

Synset Examples

a house aglow with lights
glowing embers
lambent tongues of flame
the lucent moon
a sky luminous with stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - sending out light; shining or glowing brightly
  • Degree Higher: brilliant - very bright and radiant
  • Degree Lower: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

Etymology

From Latin 'luminosus' (full of light), from 'lumen' (light).
