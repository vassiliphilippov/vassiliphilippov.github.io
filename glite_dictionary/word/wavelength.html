wavelength

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvˌlɛŋkθ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvlɛŋθ / (BrE)
A way of thinking or feeling that is shared with another person, leading to easy mutual understanding.
mutual understanding or shared thinking

Examples

Whenever they work together, it's clear they're on the same wavelength.
She and her mentor seemed to be operating on an entirely different wavelength.
Good communication comes more easily when colleagues are on the same wavelength.

Synset Examples

they are on the same wavelength

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapport - a close and harmonious relationship
  • Synonym: harmony - agreement or concord
  • Antonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvˌlɛŋkθ / (AmE), / ˈweɪv.lɛŋkθ / (BrE)
the distance between two points in the same phase of consecutive cycles of a wave, such as from crest to crest or trough to trough
distance between points in a wave cycle

Examples

The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 400 to 700 nanometers.
Radio stations broadcast at different wavelengths to avoid interference.
Scientists measured the wavelength of the sound to determine its pitch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frequency - the number of complete cycles of a wave per unit time

Etymology

Formed from 'wave' + 'length', first used in scientific contexts in the mid-19th century.
