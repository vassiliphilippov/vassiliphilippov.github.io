scepticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛptɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a general attitude of doubting the truth or reliability of something, especially claims to ultimate knowledge
attitude of doubt or disbelief

Examples

His scepticism about the new theory led to a lively debate among the scientists.
There is growing scepticism regarding the effectiveness of the proposed policy changes.
Her scepticism was evident when she questioned every assumption presented in the lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: credulity - a tendency to be too ready to believe something

Etymology

From Greek *skeptikos* (thoughtful, inquiring) via Latin and French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɛp.tɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈskɛp.tɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A feeling or attitude of doubting the truth or value of something; an approach marked by doubt and questioning.
doubt or questioning attitude
british

Examples

There was widespread scepticism about the effectiveness of the new policy.
Her claims were met with scepticism from the scientific community.
A certain level of scepticism can be healthy when evaluating extraordinary stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skepticism - doubt or a questioning attitude toward something (American spelling)
  • Antonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true

Etymology

From Greek 'skeptikos' (thoughtful, inquiring), via Latin 'scepticus', and Old French/late Middle English 'scepticisme'.
