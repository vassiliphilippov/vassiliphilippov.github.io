Kanchanjanga

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑn.tʃənˈdʒʌŋ.gə / (AmE), / ˌkæn.tʃənˈdʒʌŋ.gə / (BrE)
the third highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, rising to 28,208 feet (8,586 meters)
mountain on Nepal–India border in the Himalayas

Examples

The climbers prepared for their expedition to Kanchanjanga, aware of its treacherous weather conditions.
She has always dreamed of seeing Kanchanjanga at sunrise, when its peaks glow gold and pink.
Wildlife in the area around Kanchanjanga includes rare species such as the snow leopard and red panda.

Etymology

From Nepali कञ्चनजङ्घा (Kanchanjanga), meaning 'The Five Treasures of the Snow'.
