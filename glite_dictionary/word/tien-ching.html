T'ien-ching

1. PROPN

/ tiɛnˈdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / tiːˈɛn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an industrial city in northeastern China, near the Yellow Sea, also known as Tianjin; China's third largest city
major city in northeastern China

Examples

The port of T'ien-ching has grown rapidly in recent decades.
His family emigrated from T'ien-ching before World War II.
Many factories in T'ien-ching produce steel and machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tianjin - modern spelling of the same city

Etymology

From Mandarin 天津 (Tiānjīn). 'T'ien-ching' is an older Wade-Giles romanization of the modern Pinyin 'Tianjin'.
