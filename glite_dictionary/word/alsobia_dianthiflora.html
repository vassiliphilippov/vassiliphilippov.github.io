Alsobia dianthiflora

1. NOUN

/ ælˌsoʊ.bi.ə daɪˌænθɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / alˌsəʊ.bi.ə dʌɪˌanθɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A low-growing, creeping perennial plant native to Central America, known for its deeply fringed white flowers; sometimes placed in the genus Episcia.
a creeping Central American perennial with fringed white flowers

Examples

The Alsobia dianthiflora is prized for its delicate, fringed white blooms in indoor gardens.
Botanists sometimes classify Alsobia dianthiflora within the genus Episcia due to similarities.
Many plant enthusiasts grow Alsobia dianthiflora as a decorative houseplant.

Etymology

From genus name 'Alsobia', combined with Latin 'dianthiflora' meaning 'with flowers like those of Dianthus'.
