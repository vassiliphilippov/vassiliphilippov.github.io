recital

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsaɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtəl / (BrE)
a detailed spoken or written account or description of something, often listing facts, events, or features in sequence
detailed spoken or written account

Examples

The committee listened patiently to her recital of recent events within the department.
His letter included a lengthy recital of every obstacle he had faced during the project.
She grew weary of his constant recital of grievances and complaints.

Synset Examples

he was forced to listen to a recital of his many shortcomings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enumeration - the action of mentioning a number of things one by one
  • Antonym: summary - a brief statement or account of the main points

Etymology

From Late Latin 'recitalis', related to 'recitare' meaning 'to recite'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsaɪ.t̬əl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪ.təl / (BrE)
a public performance in which someone recites or repeats something memorized, such as a poem or speech
public performance of memorized material

Examples

The school held a poetry recital where students performed their favorite works.
Her flawless recital of the speech impressed both judges and audience.
We attended a dramatic recital at the local community theater last night.

Synset Examples

the program included songs and recitations of well-loved poems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - an act of performing a play, concert, or other form of entertainment
  • Synonym: reading - an event at which something is read aloud to an audience

Etymology

From Latin 'recitalis', from 'recitare' meaning 'to recite'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsaɪt̬əl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtəl / (BrE)
the act of verbally or in writing giving a detailed account or enumeration of facts or events
detailed account of events

Examples

The witness gave a detailed recital of the events leading up to the accident.
His report was more of a chronological recital than an analytical account.
The contract began with a recital of the history of the agreement.

Synset Examples

his narration was hesitant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narration - the act of narrating or telling a story

Etymology

From Middle French *recital*, from Latin *recitare* (to read out, recite).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ rəˈsaɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtəl / (BrE)
A public performance of music or dance, especially by a soloist or a small group.
musical or dance performance by a soloist

Examples

Lisa will give a piano recital at the community center on Friday evening.
The dance school's annual recital showcases the talent of its students.
He was nervous before his first violin recital, but the audience applauded warmly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: concert - a musical performance by several musicians or an orchestra
  • Synonym: solo - a piece or performance by a single musician or dancer

Etymology

From Latin 'recitare' (to read aloud).
