emigration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving one's own country to settle permanently in another
leaving one's country to live elsewhere

Examples

Economic hardship led to a wave of emigration from the country in the 19th century.
After years of political unrest, emigration rates increased dramatically.
His family considered emigration as a solution to find better opportunities abroad.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Latin 'emigratio', from 'emigrare' (to move away).
