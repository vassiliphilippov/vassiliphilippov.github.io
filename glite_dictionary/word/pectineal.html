pectineal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛkˈtɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛkˈtɪnɪəl / (BrE)
relating to the pubic bone or pubis, especially in anatomical contexts
relating to the pubic bone
medical

Examples

The pectineal line is an important anatomical landmark on the pelvis.
During surgery, the pectineal ligament can serve as a guide for certain procedures.
Pain near the pectineal region may indicate an injury to the pubic bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pubic - relating to the region of the pubis

Etymology

From Latin pectineus (pertaining to a comb), from pecten (comb), referring to the pectineal line of the pubic bone.
