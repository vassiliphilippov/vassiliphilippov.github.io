Fraxinus nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
A North American tree species, commonly known as black ash, with spreading branches, dark brown heavy wood, and leaves that turn gold in autumn.
species of North American black ash tree

Examples

The wetlands are home to several Fraxinus nigra specimens, valued for their ecological role.
Native peoples traditionally used Fraxinus nigra wood for basket weaving.
In autumn, the leaves of Fraxinus nigra turn a brilliant golden color before falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black ash - common name for Fraxinus nigra, a North American ash tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning ash tree, and 'nigra' meaning black.
