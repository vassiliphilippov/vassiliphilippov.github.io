gigantic

1. ADJ

/ dʒaɪˈɡæn.tɪk / (AmE), / dʒaɪˈɡæn.tɪk / (BrE)
Extremely large in size, scale, or extent; enormous.
extremely large or enormous

Examples

The gigantic statue towered over the city square, drawing tourists from around the world.
They faced a gigantic task in rebuilding the town after the hurricane.
A gigantic wave crashed onto the shore, drenching everyone standing nearby.

Synset Examples

a gigantic redwood
gigantic disappointment
a mammoth ship
a mammoth multinational corporation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size or amount
  • Synonym: immense - extremely large or great, especially in scale
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount

Etymology

From Latin 'giganticus', from Greek 'gigantikos' (of the race of giants).
