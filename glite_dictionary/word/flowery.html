flowery

1. ADJ

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.i / (BrE)
having or suggesting the qualities, appearance, or fragrance of flowers.
resembling or related to flowers

Examples

She wore a flowery dress to the summer party.
The air in the garden was sweet and flowery after the rain.
His writing style was so flowery that it distracted from his ideas.

Synset Examples

a flowery hat
flowery wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floral - decorated with or relating to flowers

Etymology

From flower + -y, indicating 'full of' or 'having the qualities of.'

WordNet ID: oewn-02740401-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈflaʊəri / (AmE), / ˈflaʊəri / (BrE)
(of writing or speech) using elaborate, ornate, or decorative language, often with excessive detail or embellishment.
ornate or elaborate in speech or writing

Examples

The politician's flowery speech was filled with metaphors and elaborate expressions.
His writing is sometimes criticized as too flowery for scientific publications.
She preferred a more direct style, avoiding flowery language whenever possible.

Synset Examples

a flowery speech
ornate rhetoric taught out of the rule of Plato

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - (of style) elaborate or highly decorated
  • Antonym: plain - (of style) simple and unadorned

Etymology

From 'flower' + '-y', originally meaning 'abounding in flowers'; figurative sense of ornate speech dates from the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02024355-s
Synset POS: s
