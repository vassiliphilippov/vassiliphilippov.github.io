uremia

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈriːmiə / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the accumulation of waste products (particularly urea) in the blood that are normally excreted by the kidneys.
accumulation of waste products in the blood
medical

Examples

The patient developed uremia as a result of chronic kidney failure.
Symptoms of uremia may include nausea, vomiting, and confusion.
Dialysis is often required to manage severe cases of uremia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azotemia - abnormally high levels of nitrogen-containing compounds in the blood

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'urea' + Greek 'haima' (blood), indicating blood relating to urea.
