Gerbillinae

1. NOUN

/ dʒɚˈbɪlɪni / (AmE), / dʒɜːˈbɪlɪniː / (BrE)
A zoological subfamily containing the gerbils, small burrowing rodents native to Africa and Asia.
subfamily of gerbils (rodents)

Examples

The Gerbillinae subfamily includes various species of gerbils commonly found in arid regions.
Biologists study the evolutionary relationships among the Gerbillinae to understand their adaptation to desert life.
Members of Gerbillinae have specialized kidneys that help them conserve water in their dry habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbil - a small burrowing rodent native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From modern Latin Gerbillinae, based on the genus Gerbillus.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɚˌbɪlɪˈneɪ / (AmE), / dʒɜːˌbɪlɪˈniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of rodents commonly known as gerbils, native primarily to Africa and Asia.
rodent subfamily (gerbils)

Examples

The African desert is home to various species of Gerbillinae that have adapted to arid climates.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity within the subfamily Gerbillinae to understand their evolution.
Many popular pet gerbils belong to the Gerbillinae subfamily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbil - small burrowing rodent, common pet

Etymology

From New Latin Gerbillinae, based on 'Gerbillus' (genus) and '-inae' (subfamily, from Latin feminine plural).
