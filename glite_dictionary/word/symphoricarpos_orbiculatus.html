Symphoricarpos orbiculatus

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˌfɔrɪˈkɑːrpəs ɔːrˌbɪkjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / sɪmˌfɒrɪˈkɑːpɒs ɔːˌbɪkjʊˈlætəs / (BrE)
A North American deciduous shrub cultivated for its abundant clusters of coral-red, berrylike fruits, often grown ornamentally.
North American shrub with coral-red berries

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful hedge of Symphoricarpos orbiculatus heavy with coral-red berries.
Many birds are attracted to Symphoricarpos orbiculatus due to its bright fruit clusters in autumn.
She planted Symphoricarpos orbiculatus along the fence to add color during the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coralberry - Common name for Symphoricarpos orbiculatus

Etymology

From Greek symphorein (to bear together) and karpos (fruit); specific epithet from Latin orbiculatus (rounded).
