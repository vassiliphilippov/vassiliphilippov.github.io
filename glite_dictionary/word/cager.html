cager

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdʒə / (BrE)
an athlete who plays basketball, especially used in the earlier days of the sport
basketball player (dated)

Examples

The college cager scored 22 points in last night's game.
Many talented cagers are recruited from high schools each year.
In the early 1900s, cagers played behind wire fences to keep the ball in play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basketball player - a person who plays basketball

Etymology

From 'cage', referring to the wire mesh that once surrounded basketball courts.
