metrorrhagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəˈreɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌmiːtrəˈreɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
abnormal bleeding from the uterus that is not related to menstruation, often indicating disease
abnormal uterine bleeding
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with metrorrhagia after reporting episodes of abnormal uterine bleeding.
Doctors must rule out serious conditions such as cancer when treating metrorrhagia.
Chronic metrorrhagia can significantly affect a woman's quality of life.

Etymology

From Greek mētrā (womb) + -rrhagia (bursting forth, bleeding).
