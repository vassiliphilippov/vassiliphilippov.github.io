grumpiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌmpinəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmpɪnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being irritable or easily annoyed, often in a surly or bad-tempered manner
state of being irritable or bad-tempered

Examples

His grumpiness was apparent to everyone after he missed his morning coffee.
Despite her grumpiness, her friends still enjoyed her company.
The long flight brought out a certain grumpiness in even the most patient travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being happy and positive

Etymology

From 'grumpy' + '-ness', where 'grumpy' likely from dialectal 'grump' (bad mood).
