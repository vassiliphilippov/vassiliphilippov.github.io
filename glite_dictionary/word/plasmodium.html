plasmodium

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmoʊdiəm / (AmE), / plazˈməʊdiəm / (BrE)
a parasitic protozoan of the genus Plasmodium, which causes malaria in humans
malaria-causing protozoan
medical

Examples

The mosquito transmits plasmodium parasites to humans through its bite.
Scientists are developing vaccines to target plasmodium infections.
Microscopic examination of blood can reveal the presence of plasmodium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malaria - an infectious disease caused by Plasmodium parasites

Etymology

From New Latin Plasmodium, from Greek plasma (something molded) + -odium (diminutive).

2. NOUN

/ plæzˈmoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / plæzˈməʊ.dɪ.əm / (BrE)
A mass of cytoplasm containing many nuclei, characteristic of certain life stages of slime molds and some other organisms, that moves and feeds as a single body.
multinucleate mass in slime molds
medical

Examples

In favorable conditions, the plasmodium spreads out in search of food particles.
Scientists observed the movement of the plasmodium under a microscope.
The yellowish plasmodium slowly crept over the decaying leaf.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'plasma' (something molded) + '-idium' (diminutive suffix).
