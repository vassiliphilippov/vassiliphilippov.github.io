Plasmodium

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmoʊdiəm / (AmE), / plazˈməʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic protozoa, some species of which cause malaria in humans and other animals.
genus of protozoa causing malaria
medical

Examples

The Plasmodium genus includes several species that infect humans, such as Plasmodium falciparum.
Researchers study the life cycle of Plasmodium to find new treatments for malaria.
Mosquitoes transmit Plasmodium parasites to humans through their bites.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'plasma' (something molded, form).
