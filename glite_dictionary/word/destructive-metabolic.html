destructive-metabolic

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrʌktɪv ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌktɪv ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to the breakdown of complex molecules into simpler ones, especially as part of catabolism in living organisms
relating to catabolic metabolism
medical

Examples

Some metabolic disorders involve abnormal destructive-metabolic activity in the body.
The liver plays a central role in destructive-metabolic processes that break down nutrients.
Intensive exercise can trigger destructive-metabolic pathways that release energy from stored compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catabolic - relating to catabolism; breaking down molecules in metabolism
  • Antonym: anabolic - relating to the synthesis of complex molecules in metabolism

Etymology

From 'destructive' (causing destruction) and 'metabolic' (from Greek 'metabolē' meaning 'change').
