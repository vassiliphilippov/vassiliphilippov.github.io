crawler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who moves along the ground on hands and knees or close to the ground
a person moving on hands and knees

Examples

The baby became a fast crawler before taking her first steps.
During the drill, each crawler had to move silently through the obstacle course.
Covered in mud, the crawler reached the finish line before anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeper - a person or animal that creeps

Etymology

From the verb 'crawl' + -er suffix, indicating an agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who tries to please someone in authority in order to gain a personal advantage; a sycophant
person who flatters for advantage

Examples

Everyone at the office knew Tom was a crawler who would do anything for a promotion.
She considered him a crawler because he was always complimenting the boss.
The staff disliked the crawler who constantly tried to curry favor with management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important
  • Synonym: flatterer - a person who praises someone insincerely for advantage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10704310-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlə / (BrE)
a terrestrial worm that burrows into the soil and helps to aerate it, often surfacing when the ground is cool or wet; commonly used as bait by anglers
soil-burrowing worm; angling bait

Examples

After the rain, dozens of crawlers could be seen wriggling across the sidewalk.
Anglers often use a crawler as live bait to catch fish such as bass and trout.
A healthy garden benefits from the burrowing actions of the humble crawler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a common worm that lives and aerates soil
