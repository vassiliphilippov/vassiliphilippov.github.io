Prunus lyonii

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs liˈoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs laɪˈəʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Catalina Island in California, also known as Catalina cherry, bearing clusters of white flowers and dark purple fruit
evergreen shrub or tree of Catalina Island

Examples

Prunus lyonii produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring.
Gardeners on the West Coast often plant Prunus lyonii as an ornamental tree because of its attractive fruit.
Wildlife such as birds and squirrels are drawn to the berries of the Prunus lyonii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catalina cherry - common name for Prunus lyonii, an evergreen shrub or small tree of Catalina Island

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'prunus' (plum tree); species named after botanist William S. Lyon.
