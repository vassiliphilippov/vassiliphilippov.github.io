niceness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being pleasant, kind, or agreeable.
quality of being nice

Examples

Her niceness made everyone in the office feel welcome.
Sometimes niceness is mistaken for weakness, but it takes strength to be kind.
People often underestimate the power of simple niceness in daily interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being kind
  • Antonym: meanness - the quality of being mean or unkind

Etymology

Formed from 'nice' + suffix '-ness', denoting a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant, polite, or considerate in manner or behavior
quality of being pleasant or considerate

Examples

Her niceness toward new employees makes her a wonderful mentor.
Despite his niceness, he was not afraid to speak his mind when needed.
I appreciated the niceness of the staff during my stay at the hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Antonym: rudeness - the quality of being impolite or disrespectful

Etymology

From Middle English 'nicenesse', from Old French 'niceté' (silliness, foolishness), from 'nice' (foolish, silly; later refined and pleasant).
