philanthropy

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlænθrəpi / (AmE), / fɪˈlanθrəpi / (BrE)
The desire to promote the welfare of others, typically expressed by the generous donation of money to good causes.
desire to promote human welfare

Examples

The foundation is known for its philanthropy in supporting education and healthcare initiatives.
Her lifelong commitment to philanthropy inspired others to give back to their communities.
Many business leaders engage in philanthropy by donating large sums to charitable organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - generous actions or donations to aid the poor, ill, or helpless
  • Antonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; concern for one's own advantage

Etymology

From Greek philanthropia ‘love of mankind’, from philos ‘loving’ + anthrōpos ‘man, human being’.
