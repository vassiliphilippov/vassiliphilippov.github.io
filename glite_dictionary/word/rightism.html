rightism

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈraɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
support for or belief in the principles of the political right, such as conservatism or traditionalism.
support for right-wing political ideology

Examples

The rise of rightism in the country led to significant changes in government policy.
Some political analysts argue that rightism often emphasizes tradition and economic freedom.
Her shift toward rightism surprised many of her more progressive friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leftism - support for or belief in left-wing political ideology

Etymology

From 'right' (political right) + '-ism'.
