to dicker

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪkər / (AmE), / ˈdɪkə / (BrE)
to negotiate or bargain the terms of a deal, especially over a price
negotiate or bargain terms

Examples

The two merchants began to dicker over the price of the antiques.
She spent nearly an hour dickering with the car dealer before making a decision.
We didn't have time to dicker about the details, so we accepted the initial offer.

Synset Examples

We bargained for a beautiful rug in the bazaar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - to discuss and agree on a price or terms
  • Synonym: haggle - to argue or negotiate over price

Etymology

Probably from an old English dialectal use of 'dicker' (a bundle of ten skins), from Latin 'decuria' (set of ten).
