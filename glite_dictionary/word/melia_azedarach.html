Melia azedarach

1. NOUN

/ məˌlaɪ.ə ˌæz.əˈdɛr.æk / (AmE), / ˌmiː.li.ə ˌæz.ɪˈdær.æk / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to northern India and China, with purple blossoms and small inedible yellow fruits, often grown as a shade tree in warm regions.
tree with purple flowers and yellow fruit

Examples

The Melia azedarach is prized for its ornamental flowers in many gardens across the southern United States.
Botanists studied the spread of Melia azedarach after it was introduced from Asia.
Despite its beauty, the fruit of Melia azedarach is not edible for humans.

Etymology

From Latin *melia* (ash tree), from Greek *melia*; *azedarach* from Persian *āzād dirakht* (noble tree).
