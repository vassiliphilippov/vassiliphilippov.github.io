Canavalia ensiformis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnævəˈliːə ɛnˌsɪfɔːrˈmɪs / (AmE), / kæˌnævəˈliːə ɛnˌsɪfɔːˈmɪs / (BrE)
an annual bushy plant of tropical South America, with long pods containing white seeds, cultivated mainly for forage
tropical forage plant with long pods

Examples

Canavalia ensiformis is often used as a green manure crop to improve soil fertility in tropical regions.
Farmers in South America cultivate Canavalia ensiformis for its nutritious pods and ability to fix nitrogen.
The white seeds of Canavalia ensiformis can also be processed for use as animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack bean - common name for Canavalia ensiformis

Etymology

From Latin: 'canavalia' (a bean genus), 'ensiformis' (sword-shaped, referring to the pods).
