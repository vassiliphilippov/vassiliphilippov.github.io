pickle

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
to preserve food in a solution of vinegar or brine, often with spices
preserve food in vinegar or brine

Examples

Many people pickle cucumbers to make them last through the winter.
You can pickle onions in a mixture of vinegar and spices for extra flavor.
My grandmother taught me how to pickle vegetables using a traditional family recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to treat food so that it will not spoil
  • Synonym: ferment - to undergo or cause a process of fermentation in food

Etymology

From Middle English *pikel* (spicy sauce or brine for preserving food), probably from Middle Dutch *pekel* (brine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
an awkward or difficult situation, especially one that is hard to get out of (informal)
difficult or awkward situation (informal)
informal

Examples

Without my wallet, I'm in quite a pickle and can't pay for the train ticket.
She found herself in a pickle after agreeing to two meetings at the same time.
If you tell him the truth now, you'll get out of this pickle sooner.

Synset Examples

he got into a terrible fix

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - an awkward or difficult situation
  • Synonym: jam - a difficult situation

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.əl / (BrE)
a vegetable, especially a cucumber, preserved in brine or vinegar, often eaten as a condiment or snack
vegetable preserved in brine or vinegar

Examples

She added a crunchy pickle to her sandwich for extra flavor.
He prefers his pickle slices sweet rather than sour.
They make their own pickles using an old family recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gherkin - a small cucumber preserved in vinegar or brine
  • Synonym: relish - a condiment made of chopped pickled vegetables

Etymology

From Dutch *pekel* or Low German *pökel*, meaning brine.
