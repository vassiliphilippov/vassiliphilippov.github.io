Cyphomandra

1. NOUN

/ saɪfəˈmændrə / (AmE), / saɪfəˈmændrə / (BrE)
A small genus of South American trees and shrubs in the nightshade family, best known for the tree tomato (tamarillo).
genus of trees, including tree tomato

Examples

The Cyphomandra is cultivated for its edible fruit known as the tree tomato.
Some botanists classify the tamarillo under the genus Cyphomandra rather than Solanum.
Gardeners in warmer climates often grow Cyphomandra species for their attractive foliage and fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarillo - edible fruit of the Cyphomandra betacea tree, also called tree tomato

Etymology

From Greek 'kyphos' (bent, humped) and 'mandra' (enclosure, stall), referring to plant form.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪfoʊˈmændrə / (AmE), / ˌsɪfəˈmændrə / (BrE)
A genus of South American shrubs and small trees in the nightshade family, including the tree tomato (tamarillo).
genus of shrubs including the tree tomato

Examples

The fruit of the Cyphomandra is commonly known as the tree tomato or tamarillo.
Researchers studied the cultivation requirements for several species within the Cyphomandra genus.
Many tropical gardens now feature Cyphomandra for its attractive fruit and foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarillo - the edible fruit of Cyphomandra betacea, also known as tree tomato

Etymology

From Greek 'kyphos' (bent, hump) + 'mandra' (enclosure), referring to the plant's characteristics.
