pruner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnər / (AmE), / ˈpruːnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to trim and shape trees and shrubs by cutting away unwanted branches or stems
worker who trims trees and shrubs

Examples

The pruner carefully removed the dead branches to encourage healthy growth.
Each spring, a skilled pruner is hired to maintain the orchard's apple trees.
Without a pruner, shrubs can quickly become overgrown and unruly.

Synset Examples

untouched by the pruner's axe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardener - a person who tends and cultivates a garden

Etymology

From the verb 'prune' (to cut back), ultimately from Old French 'prooignier' (to cut off).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnər / (AmE), / ˈpruːnə / (BrE)
a long-handled tool with a curved blade or clipper at the end, used for cutting small branches from trees and shrubs
tool for cutting small trees and branches

Examples

She used a pruner to trim the overgrown branches in her garden.
Make sure your pruner is sharp before tackling any tree work.
The gardener selected a sturdy pruner to shape the small apple tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pruning shears - hand-held scissors for cutting plants

Etymology

From 'prune' (to trim trees or shrubs) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
