catananche

1. NOUN

/ kætəˈnæŋki / (AmE), / katəˈnaŋki / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Catananche, with long-stalked heads of blue or yellow flowers, native to the Mediterranean region.
a Mediterranean plant with blue or yellow flowers

Examples

The delicate blooms of the catananche add a splash of blue to the summer garden.
She planted several catananches along the path for their striking yellow flowers.
Botanists admire the catananche for its resilience in dry, sunny conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin Catananche, from Greek katanankē, a kind of plant.
