Catananche

1. NOUN

/ kætəˈnæŋki / (AmE), / katəˈnaŋki / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean flowering herbs in the family Asteraceae, commonly known as cupid's dart.
genus of Mediterranean herbs

Examples

Catananche is valued in gardens for its delicate blue flowers known as cupid’s dart.
Botanists classify several species of Catananche native to the Mediterranean region.
The genus Catananche is part of the diverse Asteraceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupid's dart - a common name for Catananche caerulea, a species in this genus
  • Synonym: Asteraceae - the plant family including Catananche

Etymology

From modern Latin Catananche, the genus name in botanical taxonomy.
