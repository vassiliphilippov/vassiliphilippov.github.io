utter

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌtər / (AmE), / ˈʌtə / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is complete or absolute; total.
complete or absolute

Examples

She stared at him in utter disbelief, unable to say a word.
The plan ended in utter failure due to poor organization.
After the storm, the street was left in utter chaos.

Synset Examples

utter seriousness
blank stupidity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute - total and complete; not qualified or diminished in any way
  • Antonym: partial - existing only in part; not complete

Etymology

From Middle English *uttre* (outermost), from Old English *ūtera* (outer).
