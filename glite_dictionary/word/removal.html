removal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmuːvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvəl / (BrE)
the act of dismissing someone from an official position or office
dismissal from an official position

Examples

The board voted for the removal of the managing director after several complaints.
His sudden removal from office shocked everyone in the company.
Legal proceedings led to the removal of the corrupt official.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from a job or position
  • Antonym: appointment - the act of assigning someone to a position

Etymology

From remove +‎ -al, originating from Latin removēre (to move back, remove) + -al (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmuː.vəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmuː.vəl / (BrE)
the act of taking something or someone away from a place or position
the act of removing

Examples

The company ordered the removal of all outdated equipment from the facility.
After the storm, crews began the removal of fallen trees from the roads.
The removal of the statue sparked a heated public debate.

Synset Examples

he had surgery for the removal of a malignancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elimination - the act of completely removing or getting rid of something
  • Antonym: addition - the act of adding something

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Middle French *removal*, from Old French *remouvoir* (to remove).
