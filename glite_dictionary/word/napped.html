napped

1. ADJ

/ ˈnæpt / (AmE), / ˈnæpt / (BrE)
(of fabric) having a soft, raised surface produced by brushing or teasing the fibers.
fabric with a soft, raised surface

Examples

She chose a napped velvet sofa for the living room, appreciating its soft texture.
The coat was lined with napped flannel for extra warmth in winter.
Certain painting rollers are made with napped fabrics to help hold more paint.

Synset Examples

a dress of brushed cotton
a fleecy lining
napped fabrics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brushed - (of fabric) having a soft surface from brushing
  • Antonym: smooth - (of fabric) having an even, flat surface

Etymology

From the noun 'nap', referring to the raised surface of certain fabrics.
