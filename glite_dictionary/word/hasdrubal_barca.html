Hasdrubal Barca

1. PROPN

/ hæzˈdruːbəl ˈbɑːrkə / (AmE), / hæzˈdruːbæl ˈbɑːkə / (BrE)
(c. 245–207 BC) A Carthaginian general who commanded an army in Spain and later joined his brother Hannibal in Italy; killed by the Romans at the Battle of Metaurus River.
Carthaginian general; brother of Hannibal

Examples

Hasdrubal Barca led Carthaginian forces in Spain before marching to join Hannibal in Italy.
The defeat and death of Hasdrubal Barca at the Metaurus River was a turning point in the Second Punic War.
Many historians compare the tactics of Hasdrubal Barca to those of his more famous brother, Hannibal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hannibal - Famous Carthaginian general; brother of Hasdrubal Barca

Etymology

From Latin 'Hasdrubal,' from Punic 'ʿAzrubaʿal' meaning 'Help of Baal.'
