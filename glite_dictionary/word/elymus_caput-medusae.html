Elymus caput-medusae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪməs ˌkæpət mɪˈdjuːsiː / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪməs ˌkæpʊt mɛˈdjuːsiː / (BrE)
A species of weedy rye grass characterized by long, bristling awns, known as Medusahead.
a species of weedy rye grass

Examples

Elymus caput-medusae can invade rangelands, making it difficult for native species to thrive.
Scientists have studied the spread of Elymus caput-medusae in western grasslands.
Ranchers often battle Elymus caput-medusae because it reduces the quality of forage for livestock.

Etymology

From Latin 'caput Medusae', meaning 'head of Medusa', referring to the plant's awn resemblance to Medusa's snake hair in mythology.
