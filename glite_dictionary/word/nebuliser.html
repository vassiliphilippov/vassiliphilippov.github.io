nebuliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛbjəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
A device that converts liquid into a fine mist, often used for administering medication or perfume.
device for turning liquid into mist

Examples

The nebuliser delivered the medicine directly into her lungs as a gentle mist.
Many asthma patients keep a nebuliser at home for emergencies.
She bought a portable nebuliser for her travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomizer - a device that turns liquid into a fine spray or mist

Etymology

From Latin 'nebula' (mist, vapor) + English agent suffix '-iser'.
