Usama bin Laden

1. PROPN

/ uˈsɑːmə bɪn lɑːdən / (AmE), / uˈsɑːmə bɪn ˈlɑːd(ə)n / (BrE)
(1957–2011) An Arab terrorist and the founder of al-Qaeda, responsible for organizing various major terrorist attacks, including those on September 11, 2001.
founder of al-Qaeda; Arab terrorist (1957–2011)

Examples

Usama bin Laden was killed by U.S. forces in Pakistan in 2011.
The attacks were masterminded by Usama bin Laden, leader of al-Qaeda.
Many governments considered Usama bin Laden the most wanted terrorist in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: al-Qaeda - a militant Islamist organization founded by Usama bin Laden
