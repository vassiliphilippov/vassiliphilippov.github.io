Caesalpinia decapetala

1. NOUN

/ seɪˌsælpɪˈniːə dɛˌkæpɪˈteɪlə / (AmE), / siːˌsælpɪˈniːə dɛˌkæpɪˈteɪlə / (BrE)
A spreading thorny shrub native to tropical Asia, with large upright clusters of yellow flowers marked with red.
thorny Asian shrub with yellow-red flowers

Examples

The Caesalpinia decapetala quickly dominated the hedgerow with its dense, thorny branches.
Gardeners admire Caesalpinia decapetala for its striking yellow flowers and robust growth.
The spread of Caesalpinia decapetala has led to concerns about its impact on native vegetation.

Etymology

Genus name from the Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino; species name from Latin 'decapetalus' (ten-petaled).
