Erythronium californicum

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪθroʊniəm ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəm / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθrəʊniəm ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəm / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant in the lily family native to California, known as the California dogtooth violet, with creamy white flowers sometimes tinged with yellow.
a California wildflower with creamy white flowers

Examples

We found several Erythronium californicum blooming along the shaded hillside.
The creamy white petals of the Erythronium californicum stood out among the green foliage.
Botanists often refer to Erythronium californicum as the California dogtooth violet.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red), referring to the genus Erythronium, and Latin 'californicum', meaning 'of California'.
