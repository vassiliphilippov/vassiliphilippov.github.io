chemist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
a person qualified to prepare and sell medicinal drugs, especially in a pharmacy; a pharmacist
pharmacist (esp. in British English)
british

Examples

You can ask the chemist for advice on common health problems.
She picked up her prescription at the local chemist.
The chemist recommended an over-the-counter allergy medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacist - a person who is professionally qualified to prepare and dispense medicinal drugs

Etymology

From French *chimiste*, from *chimie* (chemistry), ultimately from Arabic *al-kīmiyāʾ*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or specializes in chemistry, especially as a scientific profession
scientist specializing in chemistry

Examples

Marie Curie was a renowned chemist who made significant contributions to science.
The chemist prepared a solution in the laboratory to test his hypothesis.
Many chemists work in pharmaceutical companies developing new medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacist - person qualified to prepare and dispense medicinal drugs (British English)

Etymology

From French chimiste, from chimie (chemistry).
