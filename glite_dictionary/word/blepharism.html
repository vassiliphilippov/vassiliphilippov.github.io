blepharism

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛf.əˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈblɛf.ə.rɪz.əm / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person blinks continuously
continuous involuntary blinking (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with blepharism after several days of uncontrollable blinking.
Chronic blepharism can interfere with daily activities and quality of life.
Doctors are still researching the causes of blepharism in adults.

Etymology

From Greek 'blepharon' (eyelid) + '-ism' (condition).
