to catenate

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkatɪneɪt / (BrE)
to link or connect things together in a series or chain
link together in a chain

Examples

Polymer chemists often catenate molecules to form long chains.
The researchers were able to catenate the DNA fragments for further analysis.
Certain types of atoms naturally catenate, resulting in complex ring structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to concatenate - to link things together in a series or chain

Etymology

From Latin 'catena' (chain).
