fanciful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
existing only in the imagination and not based on fact; unrealistic or imaginative rather than practical
existing only in the imagination; unrealistic

Examples

Her explanation for the missing cake was so fanciful that nobody believed her.
The story describes a fanciful world inhabited by talking animals.
Some scientists dismissed the theory as too fanciful to be taken seriously.

Synset Examples

the falsehood about some fanciful secret treaties
a small child's imaginary friends
to create a notional world for oneself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or inventiveness
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life

Etymology

From Old French 'fancie' (imagination), from 'fantasie', from Greek 'phantasia' (appearance, imagination).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
over-imaginative or characterized by unrestrained imagination rather than by reason or practicality
full of imagination; unrealistic

Examples

Her fanciful stories always entertained the children.
The architect presented a fanciful design for the new playground.
He dismissed the plans as too fanciful to be taken seriously.

Synset Examples

a fanciful mind
all the notional vagaries of childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or inventiveness
  • Antonym: realistic - having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved

Etymology

From Middle English 'fancy' (imagination) + '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646620-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfæn.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
having a delicately intricate or imaginative design or quality, often involving elaborate or unusual details
curiously intricate or imaginative in design

Examples

The dress was covered in a fanciful pattern of swirling leaves and flowers.
She admired the fanciful carvings that adorned the ancient cathedral.
The artist's fanciful designs delighted visitors with their imaginative detail.

Synset Examples

a fanciful pattern with intertwined vines and flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated
  • Antonym: simple - plain, lacking complexity or decoration

Etymology

From 'fancy' + '-ful', originally from Middle English 'fantasie', from Old French 'fantasie', from Latin 'phantasia'.

Synset POS: s
