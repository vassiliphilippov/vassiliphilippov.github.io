nervure

1. NOUN

/ nɜrˌvjʊr / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvjʊə / (BrE)
any of the vascular bundles or ribs that form the branching framework of conducting and supporting tissues in a leaf or other plant organ
vein or rib in a leaf or plant organ

Examples

The maple leaf's prominent nervure divides it into several distinct sections.
Botanists examine each nervure to help identify plant species.
In some ferns, the nervure forms a complex network throughout the frond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - vascular structure in a leaf or wing

Etymology

From French 'nervure', from Latin 'nervus' (sinew, nerve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɜr.vjʊr / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.vjʊə / (BrE)
a vein or rib that stiffens and supports the wing of an insect
rib or vein in an insect's wing

Examples

The transparent wings of the dragonfly are crisscrossed with prominent nervures.
Each nervure in a butterfly's wing plays a role in both support and flexibility.
Entomologists study the pattern of nervures to help classify different insect species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - any of the tubes forming part of the blood circulation system, or supporting ribs in insect and plant wings

Etymology

From French 'nervure', from Latin 'nervus' (nerve).
