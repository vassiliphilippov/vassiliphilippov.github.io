schoolwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female scholar, especially one skilled in academic argument or disputation
female scholar skilled in argument

Examples

The conference was chaired by a renowned schoolwoman who challenged every participant's assumptions.
In medieval times, it was rare to find a schoolwoman participating in academic debates.
Her reputation as a schoolwoman grew after she published a series of essays on philosophical argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schoolman - a male scholar skilled in academic disputation

Etymology

Formed on the pattern of 'schoolman', from 'school' (as in 'school of thought') + 'woman'.
