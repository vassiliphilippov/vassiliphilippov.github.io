bourgeoisie

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʊrʒwɑːˈziː / (AmE), / ˌbʊəʒwɑːˈziː / (BrE)
the social class that is between the working class and the upper class, typically associated with ownership of property, business, and social influence
the middle or capitalist class

Examples

The rise of the bourgeoisie transformed European society during the 19th century.
Many revolutionary movements have opposed the economic power of the bourgeoisie.
She wrote a novel critiquing the values of the French bourgeoisie in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middle class - the social group between the upper and working classes
  • Antonym: proletariat - the working class, especially in Marxist theory

Etymology

From French 'bourgeoisie' (the body of bourgeois), from 'bourgeois' (town-dweller, citizen).
