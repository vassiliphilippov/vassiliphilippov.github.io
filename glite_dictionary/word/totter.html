to totter

1. VERB

/ ˈtɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈtɒtə / (BrE)
to move in an unsteady way as if about to fall, often with a rocking or swaying motion
move unsteadily, sway or stagger

Examples

The exhausted child began to totter as she walked across the lawn.
After the boat docked, he could only totter along the pier.
The stack of plates seemed about to totter and fall from the waiter’s hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: wobble - to move unsteadily from side to side

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *toteren* (to sway or stagger), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈtɒtə / (BrE)
to walk or move in an unsteady way, typically with short, unsteady steps as if about to fall
walk or move unsteadily with short steps

Examples

The old man began to totter as he tried to cross the busy street.
After skating for the first time, she could only totter over to the bench.
The toddler would totter across the room on unsteady legs, giggling at every step.

Synset Examples

small children toddle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: stride - walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

From Middle English 'toteren' (to shake or sway); likely of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈtɒtə / (BrE)
to move in an unsteady way as if about to fall or collapse
move unsteadily, as if about to fall

Examples

After spraining her ankle, she tried to totter to the chair for support.
The vase began to totter on the edge of the shelf after the earthquake.
He watched the toddler totter uncertainly across the room for the first time.

Synset Examples

The drunk man tottered over to our table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to remain steady and not move or fall

Etymology

From Middle English 'toteren', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
