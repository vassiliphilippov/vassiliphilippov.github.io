filiform

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪlɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪfɔːm / (BrE)
thin and thread-like in shape; resembling a filament or thread, especially in diameter
thin, thread-like in shape

Examples

The coral's filiform branches sway gently with the water current.
Certain bacteria have filiform appendages that help them move.
The probe was so filiform it could pass through tiny blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threadlike - resembling a thread in form or appearance
  • Synonym: filamentous - composed of or resembling filaments
  • Antonym: robust - strong and thick in form

Etymology

From Latin 'filum' (thread) + '-form' (shape, form).
