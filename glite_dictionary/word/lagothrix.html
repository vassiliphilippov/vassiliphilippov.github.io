Lagothrix

1. NOUN

/ ləˈgɒθrɪks / (AmE), / ləˈgɒθrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of South American woolly monkeys with dense fur, found in tropical forests.
genus of South American woolly monkeys

Examples

The Lagothrix genus includes several species of thick-furred monkeys native to South America.
Researchers observed a group of Lagothrix in the Amazon rainforest.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of Lagothrix due to deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly monkey - a type of South American monkey with dense, soft fur

Etymology

From Greek 'lagos' (hare) and 'thrix' (hair), referring to their thick fur.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈɡoʊθrɪks / (AmE), / ləˈɡəʊθrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of South American woolly monkeys characterized by dense, soft fur, native to the Amazon basin.
a genus of South American woolly monkeys

Examples

The Lagothrix genus includes some of the largest New World monkeys in South America.
Researchers observed several Lagothrix individuals foraging in the Amazonian canopy.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Lagothrix populations from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly monkey - a common name for monkeys of the genus Lagothrix

Etymology

From Greek lagos (hare) + thrix (hair), referring to the animal's woolly fur.
