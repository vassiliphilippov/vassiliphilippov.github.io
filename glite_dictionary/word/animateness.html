animateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ɪ.meɪt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæn.ɪ.meɪt.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being animate, especially having animal life as opposed to plant life or inanimate existence.
state of being animate or alive

Examples

Many languages distinguish between nouns based on their animateness.
Debates about animateness are common in philosophy when defining the boundaries of life.
The animateness of certain microorganisms is still a matter of scientific investigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inanimateness - the quality of being inanimate or lifeless
  • Synonym: animation - the process or result of bringing something to life or making it appear alive

Etymology

Formed from 'animate' + the suffix '-ness', from Latin 'animatus' (filled with breath, life).
