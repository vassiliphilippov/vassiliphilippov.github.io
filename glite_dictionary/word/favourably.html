favourably

1. ADV

/ ˈfeɪvərəbli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows approval or support.
in an approving or supportive way
british

Examples

The committee looked favourably on the proposed changes.
Her application was judged favourably compared to other candidates.
The new policy was received favourably by most employees.

Synset Examples

he reviewed the play favorably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorably - in an approving or supportive way (American spelling)
  • Antonym: unfavourably - in a way that shows disapproval or lack of support

Etymology

From 'favourable' + '-ly', with 'favourable' from Old French 'favorable', from Latin 'favorabilis'.
