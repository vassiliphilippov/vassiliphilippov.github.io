to govern

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
to exercise authority over a country, people, or organization; to rule or control by law or power
to rule or control (especially a country)

Examples

After the revolution, a new party began to govern the country.
The committee was set up to govern the university's policies.
She hopes to govern in a fair and transparent manner if elected.

Synset Examples

Who is governing the country now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - to exercise ultimate power or authority over a country or people
  • Synonym: control - to have power over or direct the actions of
  • Antonym: to obey - to follow commands or authority, opposite action to govern

Etymology

From Old French 'governer', from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, to direct).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
(of a word or construction) to require that another word be in a particular case, form, or mood in grammatical structure.
require a certain grammatical form

Examples

In Latin, certain prepositions govern the ablative case rather than the accusative.
The verb 'depend' governs the preposition 'on' in English.
Some moods in Greek are governed by specific types of subordinate clauses.

Synset Examples

most transitive verbs govern the accusative case in German

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - have power over; direct the function or course of something

Etymology

From Old French *governer*, from Latin *gubernare* (to steer, to rule).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
to control or influence something by establishing rules, principles, or regulations
control by rules or regulations

Examples

Strict standards govern the production of organic food in this country.
The school board governs all policies regarding student behavior and dress code.
These procedures are governed by federal law to ensure fairness.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to regulate - to control or maintain according to rules or principles

4. VERB

/ ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
to control, direct, or strongly influence the way that someone behaves or how something happens
control or strongly influence behavior

Examples

His dedication to fairness governs his decisions at work.
Principles of honesty and respect govern her daily interactions with others.
Religious beliefs often govern the conduct of communities around the world.

Synset Examples

His belief in God governs his conduct

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over; dominate
  • Synonym: to regulate - to control or maintain the rate or speed of a process
  • Antonym: to follow - to act according to rules or advice (as an antonym in some contexts, when a person is not governed by something)

Etymology

From Old French 'governer', from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, direct, govern).
