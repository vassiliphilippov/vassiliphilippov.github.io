Aralia spinosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪliə spaɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / əˈreɪliə spaɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A small, deciduous, clump-forming tree or shrub of the eastern United States, also known as devil's walking stick, featuring large compound leaves and spiny stems.
a spiny shrub or small tree native to eastern U.S.

Examples

The sharp thorns along the stem make Aralia spinosa easy to identify in the wild.
Gardeners sometimes plant Aralia spinosa for its dramatic foliage and wildlife habitat.
Hikers in the Appalachians often encounter patches of Aralia spinosa growing along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil's walking stick - another name for Aralia spinosa, a spiny shrub or tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Aralia' (genus name) and 'spinosa' meaning spiny, referring to its thorny stems.
