Saponaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly perennial herbs native to the Old World, commonly known as soapworts, with leaves and roots sometimes used for producing a soapy lather.
genus of soapwort plants

Examples

The roots of Saponaria have been used historically as a gentle soap alternative.
Several species within the Saponaria genus are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of extracts from Saponaria plants.

Etymology

From Latin saponarius, from sapo (soap), due to the soap-like qualities of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈnɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly perennial herbs native to the Old World, commonly known as soapworts, belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.
genus of perennial herbs (soapworts)

Examples

Some species in the genus Saponaria are cultivated for their attractive pink flowers.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Saponaria plants.
The leaves of Saponaria have historically been used as a natural soap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapwort - a common name for plants in the genus Saponaria

Etymology

From Latin *saponarius* meaning 'of or pertaining to soap', from *sapo* 'soap'.
