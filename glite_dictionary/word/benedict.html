Benedict

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛnədɪkt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪdɪkt / (BrE)
(Saint Benedict of Nursia) An Italian monk (c. 480–547), founder of Western Christian monasticism and the Benedictine order around 540 CE.
founder of Western Christian monasticism

Examples

Benedict established the famous monastery at Monte Cassino in Italy.
Many monasteries across Europe followed the Rule of Benedict.
Religious historians often credit Benedict with preserving learning through the Dark Ages.

Etymology

From Latin Benedictus, meaning 'blessed'.
