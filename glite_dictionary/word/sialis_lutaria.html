Sialis lutaria

1. NOUN

/ siˈælɪs luˈtɛriə / (AmE), / saɪˈælɪs luˈtɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A species of dark-colored insect known as the alderfly, characterized by predatory aquatic larvae.
species of aquatic alderfly insect

Examples

The larvae of Sialis lutaria are often found in muddy freshwater habitats.
Adult Sialis lutaria are commonly seen resting on vegetation near streams and ponds.
Biologists studied the life cycle of Sialis lutaria to understand aquatic insect ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderfly - common name for insects of the genus Sialis

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Sialis + Latin lutaria (of mud), referencing the insect's habitat.
