cancellated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.səˌleɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sɪ.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having a latticelike structure with many holes or spaces; resembling a network or lattice
having a latticelike structure

Examples

The internal structure of the bone is cancellated, allowing it to be both strong and lightweight.
Architects designed the ceiling using cancellated patterns for both beauty and function.
Some shells are characterized by their cancellated surfaces, marked by a network of grooves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latticed - constructed with or resembling a lattice

Etymology

From Latin cancellatus, from cancelli (lattice).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.səˌleɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sə.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having an open, latticed, or porous structure, as in certain types of bone or architectural designs
having a latticed or porous structure

Examples

The scientist examined the cancellated structure of the bone under a microscope.
Sponges have a naturally cancellated appearance that aids in water absorption.
Some architects favor cancellated panels for their decorative and lightweight properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latticed - having a structure of crossed strips or bars
  • Synonym: spongy - like a sponge; having a soft, porous, or absorbent structure

Etymology

From Latin cancellatus, meaning latticed or crossbarred.
