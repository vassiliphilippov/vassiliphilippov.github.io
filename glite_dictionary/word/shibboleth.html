shibboleth

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪbəˌlɛθ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪbəˌlɛθ / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or custom that serves as a distinctive marker of identity for a particular group, often used to distinguish insiders from outsiders.
distinctive word or custom of a group

Examples

The way she pronounced certain words was a clear shibboleth marking her regional background.
Using outdated jargon can act as a shibboleth in the workplace, separating newcomers from long-time employees.
Their choice of greeting serves as a social shibboleth, signaling membership in the club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: password - a secret word or phrase used to gain admittance or access
  • Degree Lower: cliché - an overused phrase or idea, sometimes marking a group
  • Degree Lower: truism - a statement that is obviously true and says nothing new or interesting

Etymology

From Hebrew *šibbōlet* (ear of grain, stream), used as a test word to distinguish outsiders in the Bible (Judges 12:6).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪbəˌlɛθ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪbəlɪθ / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or custom that distinguishes a particular group or class; a slogan or commonly repeated idea, especially one considered outmoded or unoriginal.
distinctive slogan or saying of a group

Examples

The political party clung to the old shibboleth of tax cuts, despite changing times.
Skeptics view the company's mission statement as just another corporate shibboleth.
Knowing the shibboleth of the local dialect helped her blend in with the villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catchphrase - a well-known sentence or phrase, especially one that is associated with a particular famous person

Etymology

From Hebrew *šibbōleṯ* (ear of grain), used as a password to distinguish friend from foe in the Bible (Judges 12:6).
