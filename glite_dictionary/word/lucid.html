lucid

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪd / (BrE)
(of language, writing, or speech) clearly expressed and easy to understand.
clear and easy to understand

Examples

Her professor praised her lucid explanation of the complex theory.
The instruction manual was written in such lucid language that anyone could follow it.
His speech was remarkably lucid and persuasive, making complicated ideas accessible to everyone.

Synset Examples

writes in a limpid style
lucid directions
a luculent oration
pellucid prose
a crystal clear explanation
a perspicuous argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • Antonym: obscure - not clear or easily understood

Etymology

From Latin lucidus (clear, bright), from lux (light).

2. ADJ

/ ˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid, surface, or air) clear and transparent; allowing light to pass through so objects behind can be distinctly seen.
clear; transparent (of liquid, air, etc.)

Examples

We admired the lucid waters of the mountain stream on a sunny day.
The air on the remote island felt remarkably lucid after the storm had passed.
Through the lucid glass, she could see every detail of the garden outside.

Synset Examples

the cold crystalline water of melted snow
crystal clear skies
could see the sand on the bottom of the limpid pool
lucid air
a pellucid brook
transparent crystal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
  • Synonym: limpid - completely clear; unclouded
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent

Etymology

From Latin 'lucidus' (clear, bright), from 'lucēre' (to shine).

3. ADJ

/ ˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪd / (BrE)
able to think and express oneself clearly and logically, especially after confusion or difficulty.
expressing thoughts clearly; mentally clear

Examples

After her fever broke, she became lucid and could explain what had happened.
He offered a lucid account of the events despite the chaos around him.
During the interview, her responses were surprisingly lucid and insightful.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - able to speak or think in a logical and clear way
  • Antonym: confused - unable to think clearly or understand what is happening

Etymology
