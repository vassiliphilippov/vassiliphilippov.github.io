Philadelphaceae

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌlædəlˈfeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / fɪˌlædəlˈfeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, usually considered part of the Hydrangeaceae family, that includes the genus Philadelphus (mock orange).
family of flowering plants (plant taxonomy)

Examples

The Philadelphaceae are sometimes treated as a separate family from Hydrangeaceae in plant classifications.
Recent botanical studies often place Philadelphaceae within the Hydrangeaceae family.
The genus Philadelphus, known as mock orange, was traditionally assigned to the Philadelphaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hydrangeaceae - family of flowering plants including hydrangeas and often Philadelphus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Philadelphus plus the standard botanical family ending -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˌlædəlˌfeɪˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / fɪˌlædɛlˈfeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A subfamily of flowering plants that includes the mock orange genus (Philadelphus), sometimes classified within the family Hydrangeaceae.
plant subfamily including mock orange

Examples

Botanists debated whether Philadelphaceae should be a separate family or part of the Hydrangeaceae.
The genus Philadelphus is the main representative of the Philadelphaceae subfamily.
In older classification systems, Philadelphaceae was not always recognized as a distinct group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hydrangeaceae - a family of flowering plants including hydrangeas

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Philadelphus, itself from Greek philadelphos meaning 'loving one's brother'.
