to squirting

1. VERB

/ ˈskwɝːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) being forced out suddenly in a narrow stream, often with some force.
being forced out in a narrow stream

Examples

The bottle was squirting water all over the kitchen floor when I dropped it.
He laughed so hard that milk came squirting out of his nose.
Oil was squirting from the broken pipe, making a mess on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spraying - scattering in small drops, usually over a wide area
  • Degree Lower: trickling - flowing in a thin, gentle stream

Etymology

From 'squirt', possibly of Scandinavian origin, akin to Danish 'skvierte'.
