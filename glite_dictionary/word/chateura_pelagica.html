Chateura pelagica

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈtʊrə pəˈlædʒɪkə / (AmE), / ʃəˈtjʊərə pəˈlædʒɪkə / (BrE)
A North American swift species, also known as the chimney swift, that often nests in unused chimneys and hollow trees.
species of North American swift (chimney swift)

Examples

Many birdwatchers are excited to spot the elusive Chateura pelagica during migration season.
The Chateura pelagica often nests inside old chimneys throughout the eastern United States.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Chateura pelagica over the course of the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney swift - common name for Chateura pelagica, a North American swift

Etymology

From Latin: Chateura (genus name), from Greek khaitē (long hair) and oura (tail); pelagica, from Greek pelagos (sea), referring to its migratory behavior.
