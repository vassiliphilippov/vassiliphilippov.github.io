Trifolium incarnatum

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm ɪnˈkɑrnaːtəm / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊliəm ɪnˈkɑːnætəm / (BrE)
a species of clover native to southern Europe, with spiky heads of crimson flowers, cultivated as a forage plant
a species of crimson-flowered clover

Examples

Trifolium incarnatum is often used as a cover crop to enrich soil fertility on farms.
The brilliant red flowers of Trifolium incarnatum add striking color to pastures in the spring.
Farmers in the United States cultivate Trifolium incarnatum for use as nutritious livestock forage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson clover - common English name for Trifolium incarnatum

Etymology

From Latin: 'trifolium' meaning 'three-leaved', and 'incarnatum' meaning 'flesh-colored' (crimson).
