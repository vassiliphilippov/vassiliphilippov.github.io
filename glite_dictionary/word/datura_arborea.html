Datura arborea

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtʊr.ə ɑrˈbɔːr.i / (AmE), / dəˈtjʊə.rə ɑːˈbrɔː.rɪ / (BrE)
A South American plant cultivated for its large, fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.
South American plant with large trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden featured several Datura arborea plants, their white trumpet-shaped flowers glowing in the evening light.
Many gardeners cultivate Datura arborea for its striking appearance and strong fragrance.
Datura arborea should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angel's trumpet - common name for several plants of the genus Brugmansia or Datura, known for trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Datura', a genus name, and 'arborea', meaning 'tree-like'.
