Gulf Stream

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡʌlf ˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlf ˌstriːm / (BrE)
a warm ocean current that flows from the Gulf of Mexico northward along the eastern coast of the United States and across the Atlantic Ocean toward Europe
a warm Atlantic Ocean current

Examples

The Gulf Stream has a significant impact on the climate of Western Europe.
Scientists study the Gulf Stream to understand changes in ocean temperatures.
Sailors have long relied on the Gulf Stream to speed up their journeys across the Atlantic.

Etymology

Named after the Gulf of Mexico, from which the current originates, and 'stream' meaning a flowing body of water.
