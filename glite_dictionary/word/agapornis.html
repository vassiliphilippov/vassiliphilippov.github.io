Agapornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡəˈpɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌæɡəˈpɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, short-tailed parrots known as lovebirds, native to Africa and Madagascar.
genus of parrots (lovebirds)

Examples

The Agapornis genus includes several species of colorful lovebirds.
Breeders often specialize in different types of Agapornis for the pet trade.
An Agapornis requires a spacious cage and plenty of social interaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovebird - Small parrot of the genus Agapornis, popular as pets

Etymology

From Greek 'agape' (love) and 'ornis' (bird).
