towel

1. VERB

/ ˈtaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊəl / (BrE)
to dry or clean something by rubbing it with a towel
dry or clean with a towel

Examples

After swimming, she toweled her hair dry before getting dressed.
He toweled off quickly after his morning shower.
Please towel the dog before letting him into the living room.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊəl / (BrE)
A piece of absorbent cloth or paper, usually rectangular, used for drying or wiping something.
piece of absorbent cloth or paper for drying

Examples

After her shower, she wrapped a towel around herself and walked into her bedroom.
Please use a paper towel to dry your hands after washing them.
The beach was crowded with people lying on colorful towels in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napkin - a small square of cloth or paper used for wiping the mouth and hands while eating

Etymology

From Old French *toaille*, from Latin *tabula* (board, plank, slab; later, a cloth spread on a table).
