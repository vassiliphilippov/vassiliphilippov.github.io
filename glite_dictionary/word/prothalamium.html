prothalamium

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊθəˈleɪmiəm / (AmE), / ˌprəʊθəˈleɪmiəm / (BrE)
A song or poem written in celebration of a marriage, especially one sung or recited before the wedding ceremony.
song celebrating a marriage

Examples

The poet composed a prothalamium for the couple’s upcoming wedding.
Her newest work is a prothalamium dedicated to her sister's marriage.
A traditional prothalamium was performed before the ceremony began.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: epithalamium - a song or poem in honor of a bride and bridegroom after their marriage

Etymology

From Greek prothalamos (antechamber to the bridal chamber), modeled on 'epithalamium'.
