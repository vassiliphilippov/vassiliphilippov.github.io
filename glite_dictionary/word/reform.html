reform

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to make changes to something in order to improve it, especially by removing faults, abuses, or injustices
make changes for improvement

Examples

The government plans to reform the health care system to make it more equitable.
Activists called on parliament to reform outdated and discriminatory laws.
After years of criticism, the organization decided to reform its policies.

Synset Examples

reform a political system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: corrupt - to make or become morally depraved or wicked

Etymology

From Latin reformare, meaning 'to form again' or 'to change'.
