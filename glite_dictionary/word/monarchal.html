monarchal

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑrkəl / (AmE), / məˈnɑːkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or ruled by a monarch; having the supreme power resting with a monarch.
relating to rule by a monarch

Examples

The country maintained a monarchal structure for centuries before transitioning to a republic.
Many European states once had monarchal governments led by kings or queens.
Debate continues over whether a monarchal system is compatible with modern democracy.

Synset Examples

monarchal government
monarchical systems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarchical - relating to a monarch or monarchy

Etymology

From Late Latin monarchalis, from Greek monarkhēs (monarch).

2. ADJ

/ məˈnɑrkəl / (AmE), / məˈnɑːkəl / (BrE)
Having the characteristics of, or relating to, a monarch; befitting or worthy of a monarch.
pertaining to a monarch

Examples

He adopted a monarchal tone during the formal ceremony.
The palace was decorated with monarchal splendor for the coronation.
Her monarchal demeanor impressed the visiting dignitaries.

Synset Examples

monarchical gestures
monarchal pomp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarchical - pertaining to a monarch or monarchy

Etymology

From Medieval Latin monarchalis, from monarcha (monarch).
