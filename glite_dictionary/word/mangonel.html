mangonel

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæŋɡəˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˌmæŋɡəˈnɛl / (BrE)
A type of medieval catapult operated by manpower, using a lever and sling to launch projectiles.
medieval catapult siege engine

Examples

The army used a mangonel to hurl stones over the castle walls.
During the siege, engineers constructed a massive mangonel to breach the city gates.
Medieval manuscripts often depict the mangonel alongside other siege engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapult - device for hurling projectiles
  • Synonym: trebuchet - type of medieval siege engine using counterweight

Etymology

From Old French 'mangonel', from Medieval Latin 'manganellus', diminutive of 'manganum' (siege engine), from Greek 'manganon' (contrivance, engine).
