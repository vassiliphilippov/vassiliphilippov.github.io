implanted

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈplæntɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːntɪd / (BrE)
(of an idea, habit, or principle) deeply rooted or firmly established in a person’s mind or character.
deeply rooted or firmly fixed

Examples

His implanted convictions were almost impossible to change, no matter how persuasive the arguments.
Many social prejudices are implanted in people from a young age through culture and upbringing.
The trauma left an implanted fear that affected him for years.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingrained - (of a habit, belief, or attitude) firmly fixed or established; difficult to change
  • Synonym: deep-rooted - (of feelings or beliefs) firmly embedded or established; difficult to change
  • Antonym: superficial - not deep or thorough; on the surface only

Etymology

Past participle of implant, from Latin 'implantare' (to plant in).
