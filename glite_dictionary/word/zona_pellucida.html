zona pellucida

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊnə pɛˈluːsɪdə / (AmE), / ˈzəʊnə pɛˈluːsɪdə / (BrE)
a thick, transparent membrane surrounding the mammalian ovum, which is penetrated by sperm during fertilization and persists until the embryo implants in the uterus
membrane around mammalian ovum
medical

Examples

The sperm must pass through the zona pellucida to fertilize the egg.
Researchers study the zona pellucida to understand fertility problems.
After fertilization, the zona pellucida helps protect the developing embryo.

Etymology

From Latin zona (belt, girdle) and pellucida (transparent, clear).
