campanile

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpəˈniːli / (AmE), / ˌkampəˈniːli / (BrE)
a tall bell tower, typically standing alone and not attached to a building, especially found in Italy.
freestanding bell tower

Examples

The famous campanile in Venice is a major tourist attraction.
During the festival, bells rang out from the campanile across the city.
The architect designed a separate campanile rather than attaching the bell tower to the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belfry - the part of a bell tower in which bells are hung
  • Synonym: steeple - a tall tower on a church, often with a spire

Etymology

From Italian 'campanile', from 'campana' meaning 'bell'.
