to excruciate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈskruʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskruːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to subject someone to intense mental or emotional torment or suffering
torment intensely (mentally or emotionally)

Examples

The memory of his failure continued to excruciate him for years after the event.
It can excruciate a person to watch a loved one suffer and be unable to help.
Her conscience would excruciate her every time she thought about the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain on someone, physically or mentally

Etymology

From Latin 'excruciare' (to torture, to torment), from 'ex-' (out) + 'crux' (cross).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈskruːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskruːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to inflict severe pain or torture upon someone; to cause extreme physical or mental suffering
inflict severe pain or torture

Examples

The captors threatened to excruciate the prisoners if their demands were not met.
Historical accounts describe how traitors would be excruciated in medieval dungeons.
Some horror stories excruciate their characters both physically and mentally to create a sense of fear.

Synset Examples

The sinners will be tormented in Hell, according to the Bible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a form of punishment or cruelty

Etymology

From Latin excruciare (to torture), from ex- (out of) + cruciare (to torment, from crux 'cross').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
