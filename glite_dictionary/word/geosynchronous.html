geosynchronous

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒioʊˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈsɪŋkrənəs / (BrE)
(of a satellite or orbit) having an orbital period of exactly 24 hours, so that it returns to the same position in the sky relative to the Earth each day, though its orientation may shift.
having a 24-hour orbital period

Examples

Most communication satellites use geosynchronous orbits to provide consistent coverage to a specific area.
A satellite in a geosynchronous orbit matches the Earth's rotation period.
Weather forecasting often relies on data from geosynchronous satellites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geostationary - appearing to remain fixed over the same point on Earth's equator

Etymology

From geo- (Earth) + synchronous (occurring at the same time); synchronos from Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
