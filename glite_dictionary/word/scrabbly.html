scrabbly

1. ADJ

/ ˈskræbli / (AmE), / ˈskrəbli / (BrE)
(of land or vegetation) sparsely covered with stunted trees or bushes and underbrush; rough and patchy in appearance.
sparsely covered, patchy vegetation

Examples

The hillside was scrabbly, with only a few struggling bushes dotted across the rocks.
We hiked through a scrabbly forest where little light reached the ground.
Her backyard's scrabbly patches made gardening nearly impossible.

Synset Examples

open scrubby woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubby - covered with low or stunted trees or bushes
