mechanisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛkənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition of using machines to perform tasks that were previously done by people or animals
use of machines for tasks

Examples

The mechanisation of agriculture greatly increased food production across the country.
Many traditional jobs were lost with the mechanisation of the textile industry.
Rapid mechanisation has brought both economic growth and social challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of automatic equipment or systems

Etymology

From French 'mécanisation', based on 'mécaniser' (to mechanize), ultimately from 'mécanique' (mechanical).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛkənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of introducing machines or automatic devices to do work that was previously done by people; especially the use of advanced technology or electronic hardware to control equipment.
introduction of machines or automation

Examples

The mechanisation of agriculture greatly increased productivity in the 20th century.
Factory mechanisation led to fewer jobs for manual workers.
With the mechanisation of production lines, companies reduced costs and improved efficiency.

Synset Examples

automation replaces human workers by machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of largely automatic equipment in a system of operation

Etymology

From mechanise + -ation, based on French mécanisation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00103277-n
Synset POS: n
