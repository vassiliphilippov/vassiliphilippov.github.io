culpability

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌlpəˈbɪl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌkʌlpəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
Responsibility for a fault or wrong; the state of being at fault or deserving blame.
responsibility for a fault or blame

Examples

The investigation focused on determining the culpability of each suspect in the crime.
He expressed remorse but denied any culpability for the accident.
Legal proceedings must establish the culpability of the person accused before passing judgment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilt - The fact of having committed a specified or implied offense or crime
  • Antonym: innocence - The state of being not guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

From Latin *culpabilis* (blamable), from *culpa* (fault, blame).
