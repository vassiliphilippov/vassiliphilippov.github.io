styrene

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪˌrin / (AmE), / ˈstʌɪriːn / (BrE)
a colorless, oily liquid hydrocarbon used as a monomer in the production of polystyrene plastics and resins
colorless liquid used to make polystyrene

Examples

Styrene is the key raw material used in the manufacture of polystyrene packaging.
Workers wear protective equipment to avoid inhaling styrene fumes in the factory.
Research is ongoing into the environmental impact of styrene and its derivatives.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polystyrene - a synthetic resin made from styrene

Etymology

From French 'styrène', from 'styrax' (the type of tree from which it was first obtained).
