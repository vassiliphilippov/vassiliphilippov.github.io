sill

1. NOUN

/ sɪl / (AmE), / sɪl / (BrE)
(Geology) A tabular sheet of igneous rock that has been intruded between older layers of sedimentary rock, typically lying parallel to them.
horizontal igneous intrusion
medical

Examples

A prominent sill can be observed cutting across the sedimentary layers in the exposed hillside.
The geologist explained that the sill had formed millions of years ago during volcanic activity.
Unlike a dyke, a sill runs parallel to the bedding planes of the surrounding rocks.

Etymology

From Old English 'syll', meaning 'base' or 'foundation'.

2. NOUN

/ sɪl / (AmE), / sɪl / (BrE)
a horizontal structural member at the base of a building frame or supporting structure, often made of wood, stone, or metal
horizontal base support in a structure

Examples

The carpenter repaired the damaged sill before installing the new window frame.
Water had rotted the wooden sill beneath the door, requiring a complete replacement.
The stone sill at the base of the wall helped support the entire weight of the structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - a strip forming the bottom of a doorway, crossed when entering a house or room

Etymology

From Old English *syll* (base, foundation), related to Old Norse *sylla* (plinth, sill).
