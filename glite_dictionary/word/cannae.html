Cannae

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæni, ˈkɑːni / (AmE), / ˈkæni, ˈkɑːni / (BrE)
An ancient city in southeastern Italy, famous as the site where Hannibal defeated the Romans in 216 BC during the Second Punic War.
ancient city, site of Hannibal's victory

Examples

The Battle of Cannae is often studied in military academies for its tactical brilliance.
During our trip to Italy, we visited the ruins of Cannae and learned about its history.
Hannibal’s victory at Cannae marked a turning point in the Second Punic War.
