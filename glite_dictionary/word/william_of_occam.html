William of Occam

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm əv ˈɑːkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm əv ˈɒkəm / (BrE)
(c. 1285–1349) An English scholastic philosopher known for formulating Occam's Razor, a principle of parsimony in explanation.
English philosopher; formulated Occam's Razor

Examples

The medieval philosopher William of Occam is best known for his formulation of Occam's Razor.
Many cite William of Occam when discussing the importance of simplicity in scientific theories.
Scholars have extensively studied the writings of William of Occam on logic and metaphysics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occam's Razor - the principle that the simplest explanation is usually correct
