to inactivate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
officially release someone from military service or remove a unit from the active list of the armed forces
remove from military active service

Examples

The Army decided to inactivate the battalion at the end of the year.
After twenty-five years of service, the ship was inactivated and removed from the fleet.
Plans were made to inactivate several units as part of the military restructuring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discharge - to officially allow someone to leave military service
  • Antonym: to activate - to make a military unit ready for service

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + activate, based on the use in technical and military contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01100617-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make something inactive; to cause to stop functioning or operating
make inactive; disable

Examples

Researchers found a chemical that can inactivate the virus in laboratory tests.
The technician will inactivate the account until the issue is resolved.
Certain environmental conditions may inactivate the enzyme, stopping its normal function.

Synset Examples

they deactivated the file

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to activate - to make active or operative
  • Synonym: to deactivate - to make inactive or inoperative

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + activate, patterned after 'deactivate'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00191849-v
Synset POS: v
