harbor

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
A sheltered area of water at the coast where ships may anchor safely and take on or discharge cargo or passengers.
sheltered port for ships

Examples

The ship entered the harbor just as the sun was setting.
Fishing boats lined the harbor, ready for the next day's catch.
A lighthouse stood at the entrance of the harbor, guiding vessels safely to shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: port - a town or city with a harbor where ships load or unload
  • Synonym: dock - an area of water in a port where ships are loaded and unloaded

Etymology

From Old English 'herebeorg', meaning 'shelter, refuge'.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to keep (a thought, feeling, or idea) in one's mind, especially secretly or for a long time
keep a feeling or idea secretly

Examples

She continued to harbor doubts about the project's success, even after the presentation.
He secretly harbored resentment toward his colleague for years.
Do you harbor any hopes of winning the competition?

Synset Examples

bear a grudge
entertain interesting notions
harbor a resentment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entertain - keep thoughts or feelings in the mind
  • Synonym: bear - sustain or hold (a feeling, like a grudge)

Etymology

From Old English 'herebeorg' (shelter, lodging), later used figuratively.

3. VERB

/ ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to give shelter or refuge to someone, especially secretly and often with reference to fugitives or criminals
secretly hide or shelter (someone)

Examples

The family decided to harbor the refugees despite the risks.
Anyone found to harbor a fugitive will be prosecuted.
She was accused of harboring a known criminal in her apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep secret
  • Synonym: shelter - to provide protection or refuge
  • Antonym: to expose - to make something visible or known

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
a place or situation where someone feels safe, comfortable, or protected, especially from danger or trouble
place of safety or comfort

Examples

After a long and difficult journey, his home became a harbor from the troubles of the world.
In her grandmother's arms, the frightened child found a harbor where worries faded away.
Many people seek friendship as a harbor when life becomes overwhelming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: refuge - a safe place or shelter from danger

Etymology

From Old English herebeorg (shelter, lodging), from here (army) + beorg (shelter).

to keep a thought or feeling, especially a negative one, in your mind for a long time
keep a thought or feeling secretly

Examples

Many people harbor doubts about the effectiveness of the new policy.

Related Terms

From Old English 'herebeorg' (shelter, refuge), influenced by Anglo-Norman 'herberer' (to shelter).
