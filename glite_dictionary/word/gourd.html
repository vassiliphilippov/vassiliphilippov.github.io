gourd

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ɡɔːd / (BrE)
any of various inedible fruits with hard rinds, often used as utensils or for decoration
hard-shelled inedible fruit

Examples

The artist carved a beautiful design into the gourd to make a decorative bowl.
Many traditional African instruments are made using dried gourd shells.
She displayed a collection of painted gourds on the mantelpiece.

Etymology

From Old French *gourde*, from Latin *cucurbita* (gourd).

2. NOUN

/ ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ɡɔːd / (BrE)
A bottle or container made from the dried shell of a bottle gourd, traditionally used for carrying liquids.
bottle made from dried bottle gourd shell

Examples

The traveler carried water in a gourd as he crossed the desert.
She used a decorated gourd as a traditional drinking vessel during the festival.
Ancient people often hollowed out a gourd to make a container for food or liquids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - a container for holding liquid, typically having a narrow neck

Etymology

From Old French 'gourde', from Latin 'cucurbita' (gourd, pumpkin).

3. NOUN

/ ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ɡɔːd / (BrE)
A climbing or trailing plant of the family Cucurbitaceae, typically producing fruits with hard rinds, some of which are edible or used for ornamental or practical purposes.
a vine bearing hard-rinded fruits

Examples

The gourd vines quickly spread across the garden fence by midsummer.
He carved a water container from a dried gourd found in the fields.
Different species of gourd can be grown for decorative, culinary, or practical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumpkin - a large rounded orange-yellow fruit with a thick rind, edible flesh, and many seeds
  • Synonym: squash - an edible fruit also from the family Cucurbitaceae

Etymology

From Old French *gourde*, from Latin *cucurbita* (gourd).
