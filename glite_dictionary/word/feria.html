feria

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛriə / (AmE), / ˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
(in ecclesiastical use) a weekday on which no liturgical feast or holiday is celebrated; an ordinary weekday.
ordinary ecclesiastical weekday

Examples

In the medieval church calendar, each feria was marked by specific prayers and readings.
There is no feast today; it is a simple feria in the liturgical calendar.
Secunda feria referred to Monday, while Dominicus meant Sunday.

Synset Examples

in the middle ages feria was used with a prefixed ordinal number to designate the day of the week, so ‘secunda feria’ meant Monday, but Sunday and Saturday were always called by their names, Dominicus and Sabbatum, and so feria came to mean an ordinary weekday

Etymology

From Latin *feria* (holiday, day of rest); ecclesiastical use shifted meaning to 'weekday'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪriə, feɾˈi.a / (AmE), / ˈfɛːrɪə, feˈɾi.a / (BrE)
(in Spanish-speaking regions) a local festival or religious fair, typically held in honor of a patron saint.
local festival or fair (Spanish-speaking regions)

Examples

Every year, the city hosts a lively feria with music, dancing, and traditional food.
Tourists flock to the small town for its annual feria celebrating the local patron saint.
Stalls lined the streets during the feria, offering handmade crafts and local delicacies.

Etymology

Spanish, from Latin 'feria' (holiday, festival day).
