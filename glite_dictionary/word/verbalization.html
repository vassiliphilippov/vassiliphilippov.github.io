verbalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing thoughts, ideas, or feelings in words; the process of putting something into words
expressing in words

Examples

Her verbalization of her emotions helped the therapist understand her better.
The teacher encouraged the verbalization of students' ideas in class discussion.
Sometimes, the verbalization of a problem can make it easier to solve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings
  • Antonym: silence - the state of not expressing something or not speaking

Etymology

From 'verbal' + '-ization', based on Latin 'verbum' (word).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.bəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a spoken statement or expression; the words that someone says when expressing an idea or feeling in words
a spoken statement or utterance

Examples

His sudden verbalization of support surprised everyone at the meeting.
Each child's verbalization revealed their understanding of the lesson.
I found her verbalization of her feelings to be both honest and moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utterance - something that is said; a spoken word, statement, or vocal sound

Etymology

From the verb 'verbalize' + '-ation', influenced by French 'verbalisation'.

Synset POS: n
