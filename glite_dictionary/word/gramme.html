gramme

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɡræm / (BrE)
a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram; chiefly British spelling of 'gram'.
British spelling of metric unit of mass
british

Examples

In the UK, food packaging often displays weight in grammes and kilograms.
The recipe calls for 200 grammes of flour.
A letter weighing less than 20 grammes can be sent with a basic postage stamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gram - metric unit of mass; standard spelling in American English

Etymology

From French 'gramme', from Latin 'gramma' (a small weight), from Greek 'gramma' (something written or a small weight).
