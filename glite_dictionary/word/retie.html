to retie

1. VERB

/ riˈtaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtaɪ / (BrE)
to tie something again or differently
tie again or differently

Examples

After the package fell apart, she had to retie the ribbon around it.
The shoelaces came undone, so he stopped to retie them.
Could you retie this knot for me? It's too loose.

Synset Examples

retie the string and make it strong enough now

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to untie - to undo a knot or fastening

Etymology

From re- (again) + tie.
