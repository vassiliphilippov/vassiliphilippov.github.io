to enbug

1. VERB

/ ˈɛn.bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.bʌɡ / (BrE)
To cause irritation or annoyance, especially by introducing bugs or problems, often in a technical context.
to cause irritation or annoyance
informal

Examples

The constant notifications were enbugging me during the meeting.
A poorly written update can enbug users instead of improving their experience.
The pop-up ads really enbugged the team while they tried to finish the project.

Synset Examples

The constant notifications were enbugging me during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate or make someone a little angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone angry or impatient

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'debug', from 'bug' (in the sense of a flaw or irritant) with the prefix 'en-' (to cause to have).
