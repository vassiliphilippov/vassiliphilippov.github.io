bicuspid

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkʌspɪd / (AmE), / baɪˈkʌspɪd / (BrE)
having two cusps or points, especially referring to a tooth with two pointed parts
having two points or cusps
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that the bicuspid teeth play an important role in chewing.
The fossil showed evidence of a bicuspid molar unlike modern mammals.
Some plants, such as certain water lilies, have bicuspid leaves.

Synset Examples

bicuspid teeth
bicuspid leaves

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricuspid - having three cusps or points
  • Degree Lower: cuspid - a tooth with a single cusp or point

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + cuspid- (point, cusp).

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈkʌspɪd / (AmE), / bʌɪˈkʌspɪd / (BrE)
a tooth with two pointed cusps, located between the incisors and the molars, also called a premolar
a tooth with two cusps (premolar)
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that a bicuspid is also known as a premolar.
She had a cavity in her upper left bicuspid that needed to be filled.
Children usually lose their bicuspid teeth around the age of ten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premolar - a tooth situated between the canine and the molars

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'cuspis' (point, tip).
