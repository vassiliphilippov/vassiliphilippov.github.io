Atakapa

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkɑːpə / (AmE), / ˌatəˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people who formerly lived along the Gulf Coast of Louisiana and Texas
member of Indigenous people of Louisiana and Texas

Examples

The Atakapa were known for their subsistence on fishing and hunting along the Gulf Coast.
Many artifacts belonging to the Atakapa have been found in Louisiana.
A descendant of the Atakapa people spoke about preserving their culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karankawa - another Gulf Coast Indigenous people of Texas

Etymology

From Choctaw *atakapa* (man-eater, cannibal).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtækəpə / (AmE), / əˈtækəpə / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Atakapa people, formerly found along the Gulf Coast of Louisiana and Texas
language of the Atakapa people

Examples

Linguists have worked to document the endangered Atakapa language before it disappeared.
Few speakers of Atakapa remained by the twentieth century.
The museum showcased recordings and artifacts relating to Atakapa history and language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tunica - a Native American language geographically and culturally related to Atakapa

Etymology

From the name of the Atakapa people, a Native American group inhabiting parts of Louisiana and Texas.
