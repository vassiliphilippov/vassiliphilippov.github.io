Masai

1. NOUN

/ məˈsaɪ / (AmE), / məˈsaɪ / (BrE)
the Nilotic language spoken by the Maasai people, primarily in Kenya and northern Tanzania
Nilotic language of the Maasai people

Examples

She is studying the Masai language to help with her research in East Africa.
Several schools in Kenya now offer classes in Masai.
Translating the document into Masai made it accessible to local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maa - autonym for the language of the Maasai

Etymology

From Swahili 'Masai', from Maa ('person' or 'people'), referring to the Maasai ethnic group.
