slushy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈslʌʃi / (BrE)
Partially melted, wet, and soft, like melting snow or containing much slush.
like melting snow; wet and soft

Examples

The sidewalks were covered in a thick, slushy layer after the winter storm.
He trudged through the slushy ground, his boots getting soaked with each step.
The weather warmed up, turning the snow into a slushy mess.

Synset Examples

slushy snow
deep slushy mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slush - partially melted snow or ice

Etymology

From 'slush' (partially melted snow or ice) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈslʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈslʌʃi / (BrE)
Excessively sentimental or emotional in a way that seems insincere or lacking depth.
overly sentimental; insincerely emotional

Examples

Critics dismissed the movie as too slushy and predictable for serious audiences.
He found her letter slushy and lacking genuine feeling.
I can't stand slushy love songs that try too hard to be touching.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maudlin - overly sentimental, tearfully emotional
  • Synonym: schmaltzy - excessively sentimental or emotional
  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing excessive emotion or nostalgia
  • Antonym: unsentimental - not expressing strong emotions or sentimentality
