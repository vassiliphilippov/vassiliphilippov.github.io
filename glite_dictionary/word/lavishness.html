lavishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæv.ɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlæv.ɪʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively expensive, elaborate, or luxurious
quality of being excessively luxurious or expensive

Examples

The lavishness of the palace amazed all who visited.
Critics remarked on the lavishness of the wedding decorations.
Many admired the lavishness of the celebration, though some considered it excessive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury - a state of great comfort and extravagant living
  • Antonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with money or food

Etymology

From 'lavish' + '-ness', ultimately from Middle English, probably from Middle French 'lavasse', meaning a deluge or downpour (figuratively, a lavish outpouring).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlævɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or practice of spending or giving in great amounts, often excessively or extravagantly
excessive or extravagant spending or giving

Examples

The lavishness of their wedding surprised everyone who attended the ceremony.
He was known for his lavishness, often spending money on expensive gifts for friends.
Critics argued that the lavishness of the event was inappropriate during times of economic hardship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagance - lack of restraint in spending money or using resources
  • Antonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with money or food

Etymology

From 'lavish' (from Middle English 'lavasse', from Old French, from Latin 'lavare' meaning 'to wash', influenced in sense by abundance).
