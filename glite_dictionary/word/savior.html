savior

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvjɚ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvjə / (BrE)
a person who saves someone from danger, harm, or difficulty
person who rescues from danger

Examples

She called her brother her savior after he pulled her from the burning building.
The firefighter became a savior to many during the flood.
In her eyes, the doctor was nothing less than a savior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - person who saves someone from a dangerous situation

Etymology

From Old French *sauveour*, from Late Latin *salvator* (saviour, deliverer), from Latin *salvare* (to save).
