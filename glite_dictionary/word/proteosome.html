proteosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiˌoʊsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
A form of vaccine formulated with outer membrane proteins from bacteria, often administered by inhalation.
bacterial protein-based inhaled vaccine
medical

Examples

The proteosome vaccine offers a needle-free option for immunization against respiratory diseases.
Researchers are developing proteosome formulations to improve protection against influenza.
Patients received the proteosome via an inhaler during the clinical trial.

Etymology

From 'protein' + '-some' (body); named for its composition of protein particles.
