Prunus glandulosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌɡlændjəˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌɡlandjʊˈləʊsə / (BrE)
a small Chinese shrub with smooth, dark red fruit, cultivated mainly for its red, pink, or white flowers; also called Chinese bush cherry
small Chinese flowering shrub

Examples

The Prunus glandulosa adds vibrant color to spring gardens with its stunning blossoms.
In botanical collections, Prunus glandulosa is prized for both its ornamental flowers and its fruit.
Many landscapers recommend planting Prunus glandulosa along borders for a beautiful display in early spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' meaning 'plum' and 'glandulosa' meaning 'gland-bearing'.
