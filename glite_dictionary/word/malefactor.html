malefactor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈmælɪˌfæktə / (BrE)
a person who commits a crime or other wrong; a criminal or wrongdoer
criminal; wrongdoer

Examples

The authorities finally apprehended the malefactor responsible for the series of burglaries.
In the novel, the malefactor is ultimately brought to justice by the determined detective.
The community was relieved to know that the notorious malefactor had been convicted and imprisoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: wrongdoer - a person who does wrong, especially morally or legally
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or support, especially financial; opposite of malefactor

Etymology

From Latin malefactor (evil-doer), from male (badly) + facere (to do).
