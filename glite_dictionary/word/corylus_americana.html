Corylus americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪləs ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪləs ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (BrE)
A species of nut-bearing shrub native to eastern North America, known as the American hazelnut.
American hazelnut shrub species

Examples

The Corylus americana produces edible nuts that are enjoyed by both wildlife and people.
Gardeners often plant Corylus americana as part of native landscaping projects.
The leaves of Corylus americana turn a striking yellow in the autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the nut produced by Corylus species

Etymology

From Latin Corylus (hazel) and americana, referring to America.
