defence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
The military action or resources used to protect a country against attack by enemies.
protection by military action or resources
british

Examples

The government increased defence spending to strengthen the country's security.
Thousands volunteered to join the defence forces during the conflict.
Modern defence systems are designed to intercept potential threats before they reach the target.

Synset Examples

they died in the defense of Stalingrad
they were developed for the defense program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - military action or resources protecting a country (US spelling)
  • Antonym: attack - an aggressive military action against a place or enemy force

Etymology

From Old French 'defense', from Latin 'defensa' (protection).

/ dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
(in sports) the part of a team, or the team as a whole, that works to prevent the opposing side from scoring points or goals.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
