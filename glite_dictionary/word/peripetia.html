peripetia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəpɪˈtiːə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
A sudden and unexpected change of fortune or reversal of circumstances, especially in a literary work.
sudden reversal of fortune, especially in literature

Examples

The novel's peripetia caught readers by surprise and changed the fate of the main character.
Ancient Greek tragedies often hinge on a powerful peripetia that transforms the protagonist's situation.
A well-executed peripetia can add depth and excitement to a dramatic narrative.

Synset Examples

a peripeteia swiftly turns a routine sequence of events into a story worth telling

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: climax - the most intense, exciting, or important point of a story
  • Synonym: turning point - a moment of significant change in a story or situation

Etymology

From Greek *peripeteia* (sudden change), from *peri* (around) + *piptein* (to fall).
