Rumohra adiantiformis

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈmoʊrə ˌædiˌæntɪˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ruːˈmɒrə ˌædiˌæntɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of fern widely distributed in the tropical regions of the Southern Hemisphere, having leathery, pinnately divided fronds and commonly used in floral arrangements.
a widely distributed tropical fern

Examples

Rumohra adiantiformis is valued by florists for its durability and attractive appearance in arrangements.
Many gardens in subtropical regions feature Rumohra adiantiformis for its lush, green fronds.
The leathery fronds of Rumohra adiantiformis make it ideal for use as a decorative plant indoors.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Rumohra' is a genus name, and 'adiantiformis' means 'resembling Adiantum' (maidenhair ferns).
