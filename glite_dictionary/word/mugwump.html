mugwump

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡwʌmp / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡwʌmp / (BrE)
a person who remains neutral or independent, especially in political matters
neutral or independent person, esp. in politics

Examples

Throughout the heated election, he remained a mugwump, refusing to support either candidate.
The committee's decisions are often swayed by the votes of mugwumps who haven't chosen sides.
Her reputation as a political mugwump made her a sought-after mediator in debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not affiliated with any group or party
  • Antonym: partisan - a firm adherent to a party or cause

Etymology

From Algonquian (Massachusett) *mugquomp* (important person), popularized in U.S. politics in the 1880s.
