Mugwump

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡwʌmp / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡwʌmp / (BrE)
A member of a group of Republicans who left their party to support the Democratic candidate in the 1884 U.S. presidential election.
Republican defector in 1884 U.S. election
american

Examples

Some former Republicans proudly called themselves Mugwumps during the contentious 1884 campaign.
Historians often discuss how the Mugwumps influenced the result of the Cleveland-Blaine election.
By siding with the Democrats, the Mugwumps changed the landscape of U.S. politics.

Etymology

From Algonquian (possibly Massachusett) *mugquomp* meaning 'war leader' or 'great man'.
