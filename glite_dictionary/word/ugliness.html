ugliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌɡlinəs / (AmE), / ˈʌɡlinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant or unattractive in appearance.
unattractiveness; unpleasant appearance

Examples

The old house had a certain ugliness that made it infamous in the neighborhood.
She was hurt by the remarks about her supposed ugliness.
The artist transformed ugliness into something meaningful through his sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beauty - the quality of being pleasing or attractive
  • Synonym: ugly - unpleasant or unattractive in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English 'ugly' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌɡlinəs / (AmE), / ˈʌɡlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being morally bad or wicked.
quality of being wicked

Examples

The novel explores the ugliness of human cruelty and betrayal.
In his speech, he condemned the ugliness that lies behind their actions.
She was moved by the ugliness of injustice present in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - The state of being morally wrong or evil
  • Antonym: virtue - Moral goodness or excellence

Etymology

Formed from 'ugly' + '-ness'; 'ugly' from Old Norse 'uggligr', meaning 'dreadful, fearful'.
