bathysphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθiˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈbæθɪsfɪə / (BrE)
a spherical, deep-sea diving vessel that is lowered by a cable for underwater exploration
spherical deep-sea diving vessel

Examples

In 1934, scientists descended in a bathysphere to record depths, observing deep-sea life up close for the first time.
The original bathysphere is now on display at a maritime museum, attracting oceanography enthusiasts.
Through the thick windows of the bathysphere, the explorers could see thousands of strange, glowing fish.

Etymology

From Greek bathys (deep) + English sphere.
