voluminousness

1. NOUN

/ vəˈluːmɪnəsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈluːmɪnəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being voluminous; great size, extent, or fullness in volume.
the quality of being voluminous

Examples

The voluminousness of her skirt made it the centerpiece of her costume.
He marveled at the voluminousness of the novel, which was over a thousand pages long.
The architect admired the voluminousness of the cathedral's interior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies
  • Synonym: bulkiness - the quality of being large and taking up much space
  • Antonym: compactness - the quality of being closely packed together; the opposite of voluminousness

Etymology

From 'voluminous' + '-ness'. 'Voluminous' from Latin 'volumin-' (roll, coil).
