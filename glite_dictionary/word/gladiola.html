gladiola

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæd.iˈoʊ.lə / (AmE), / ɡlæd.iˈəʊ.lə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Gladiolus, having sword-shaped leaves and spikes of brightly colored funnel-shaped flowers, widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
a flowering plant of the genus Gladiolus

Examples

We planted gladiola bulbs along the garden path to add color in the summer.
A single stem of gladiola made a striking centerpiece on the dining table.
She prefers gladiola to roses because of their tall, elegant blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gladiolus - a plant of the genus Gladiolus, alternate spelling/form

Etymology

From Latin 'gladiolus', meaning 'little sword', referring to the shape of the leaves.
