epicene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsiːn / (BrE)
a person having both male and female sexual characteristics and organs; a person whose sex cannot be unambiguously assigned as male or female at birth
person with both male and female sexual characteristics

Examples

The medical team consulted experts when a newborn was found to be epicene.
Society's understanding of epicene individuals has evolved over time.
Support groups for epicene people help families navigate complex decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphrodite - a person or animal having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Latin 'epicoenus', from Greek 'epikoinos' meaning 'common to all'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌsiːn / (BrE)
having characteristics, especially of style, appearance, or manner, that are typically considered more feminine than is usual or suitable for a man
having effeminate qualities (of a man)

Examples

His epicene style and manner made him stand out in the conservative crowd.
The actor’s epicene beauty was often remarked upon in film reviews.
Some critics described the pop star’s wardrobe as epicene and androgynous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effeminate - (of a man) having characteristics regarded as typical of a woman

Etymology

From Latin epicēnus (common to both sexes), from Greek epikoinos (common).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌsiːn / (BrE)
having characteristics or an appearance that make sexual identity ambiguous or not clearly male or female.
of ambiguous sexual identity

Examples

The actor's epicene beauty made them popular with both male and female audiences.
Some languages use epicene pronouns to avoid specifying gender.
The character in the novel is described as having an epicene charm that intrigues everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: androgynous - having both male and female characteristics
  • Antonym: masculine - having qualities associated with men
  • Antonym: feminine - having qualities associated with women

Etymology

From Latin 'epicenus', from Greek 'epikenos' (common to both sexes).
