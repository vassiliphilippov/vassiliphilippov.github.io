ravelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrævəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈravəlɪŋ / (BrE)
A thread or piece of fiber that has come loose from woven fabric, often at an unfinished edge.
loose thread from fabric

Examples

She trimmed the ravelling from the hem before sewing.
A single ravelling dangled from the old sweater's sleeve.
If you pull too hard, the ravelling will make the hole bigger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fray - a worn or tattered part, especially at the edge of fabric

Etymology

From the verb 'ravel', likely from Dutch 'ravelen' (to tangle, unweave).
