Arecidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈrɛsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a former subclass of monocotyledonous flowering plants that included palms, arums, screw-pines, and duckweeds, comprising about 6,400 species in five families
former subclass of monocot plants

Examples

The palm tree belongs to the Arecidae, a large grouping of monocot plants.
Some botanists classify duckweeds within the Arecidae due to genetic similarities.
Changes in plant taxonomy have led to the reclassification of several families once included in Arecidae.

Etymology

From Latin 'Areca' (a genus of palms) + Greek '-idae' (indicating a subclass or group).

2. NOUN

/ əˈriːsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈriːsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a subclass of monocotyledonous plants that includes several families of trees, shrubs, and aquatic herbs, such as palms and arums
plant subclass including palms and arums

Examples

The Arecidae include several commercially important crops such as the coconut and date palm.
Many aquatic plants, such as duckweeds, are classified within the Arecidae.
Palms, one of the best-known members of the Arecidae, thrive in tropical climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Areca + -idae (a suffix forming taxonomic names).
