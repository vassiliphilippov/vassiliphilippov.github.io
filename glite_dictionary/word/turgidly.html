turgidly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɝːdʒɪdli / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdʒɪdli / (BrE)
in a pompous, inflated, or overly ornate manner, especially in speech or writing
in a pompous or inflated manner

Examples

The professor spoke turgidly about insignificant details in his lecture.
Her novel is written turgidly, making it difficult to follow the main plot.
He defended his proposal turgidly, using unnecessarily complex language.

Synset Examples

he lectured bombastically about his theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastically - in a pompous or pretentious manner

Etymology

From 'turgid' (Latin turgidus, swollen, inflated) + '-ly'.
