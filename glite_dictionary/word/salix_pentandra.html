Salix pentandra

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪlɪks pɛnˈtændrə / (AmE), / ˌseɪlɪks pɛnˈtændrə / (BrE)
A species of willow tree native to Europe, with shiny, leathery leaves, commonly known as the bay willow.
a species of willow tree with shiny leaves

Examples

The glossy leaves of the Salix pentandra make it a popular ornamental tree.
Many wetlands in the eastern United States now feature naturalized Salix pentandra stands.
Gardeners admire Salix pentandra for its attractive, leathery foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay willow - common English name for Salix pentandra

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' (willow) and 'pentandra' (with five stamens).
