Christopher Carson

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɪstəfər ˈkɑrsən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəfə ˈkɑːsən / (BrE)
(1809–1868) United States frontiersman, guide of Fremont's expeditions in the 1840s, and Union general in the American Civil War.
American frontiersman and Civil War general

Examples

Many schools and towns in the West are named after Christopher Carson.
As a guide, Christopher Carson was instrumental in opening up the American West to settlement.
During the Civil War, Christopher Carson served as a Union general.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kit Carson - Nickname of Christopher Carson, American frontiersman and general
