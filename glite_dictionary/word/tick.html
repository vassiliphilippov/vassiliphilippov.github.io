tick

1. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
a short, light, metallic tapping sound, especially one produced by a clock or similar mechanism
a metallic tapping sound

Examples

He listened to the tick of the old grandfather clock in the quiet room.
The tick of the metronome helped her keep time while practicing the piano.
I could hear the tick of my watch as I waited nervously for the interview to start.

Synset Examples

he counted the ticks of the clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click - a short, sharp sound like that made by a switch or latch

3. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
a small parasitic arachnid with a barbed mouthpart, feeding on the blood of warm-blooded animals, often carrying diseases
small blood-sucking arachnid

Examples

She found a tick attached to her dog's ear after their walk in the woods.
Some ticks can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease to humans.
Removing a tick promptly and correctly reduces the risk of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - a very small arachnid, related to ticks but not parasitic on larger animals

Etymology

Old English *ticca*, related to German *Zecke*, meaning 'tick'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01778954-n
Synset POS: n
