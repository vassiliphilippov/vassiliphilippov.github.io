Linnaea borealis

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈneɪ.ə bɒˈraɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈneɪ.ə bɔːˈraɪ.əlɪs / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen subshrub native to northern Europe and Asia, bearing delicate, fragrant, tubular, bell-shaped pink flowers usually in pairs; also known as twinflower.
creeping northern evergreen subshrub (twinflower)

Examples

The delicate pink flowers of the Linnaea borealis are a distinctive sight in Arctic woodlands.
Botanists often search for Linnaea borealis during their summer field studies in northern forests.
She photographed a cluster of Linnaea borealis growing along the mossy forest path.

Etymology

Named after the Swedish botanist Carl Linnaeus, with 'borealis' meaning 'northern' in Latin.
