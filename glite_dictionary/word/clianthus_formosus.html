Clianthus formosus

1. NOUN

/ kliˈænθəs fɔrˈmoʊsəs / (AmE), / klaɪˈænθəs fəˈməsəs / (BrE)
a sprawling shrubby perennial plant native to dry regions of Australia, distinguished by its scarlet flowers with black markings
Australian flowering perennial plant

Examples

The vibrant blossoms of the Clianthus formosus make it a popular choice for Australian native gardens.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of Clianthus formosus in arid environments.
Photographers are often drawn to the striking appearance of Clianthus formosus during its flowering season.

Etymology

From Greek 'kleos' (glory) and 'anthos' (flower); 'formosus' from Latin meaning 'beautiful'.
