sitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə / (BrE)
a person who sits or poses, especially for a painter, sculptor, or photographer.
person posing for an artist

Examples

The artist asked the sitter to hold her pose a little longer.
Throughout history, famous sitters have been immortalized in portrait paintings.
The sitter wore a bright red dress to make the portrait more striking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a person employed to pose for an artist or photographer

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə / (BrE)
A domestic hen that is ready to brood and incubate eggs.
a broody hen

Examples

The farmer moved the sitter to a quiet corner to encourage her to hatch the eggs.
Only one sitter among the hens stayed on her nest all day.
As a reliable sitter, the hen ensured nearly every chick hatched successfully.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə / (BrE)
a person or animal who is sitting, especially one who is posing for an artist or photograph, or being looked after for a period.
person or animal who is sitting

Examples

The artist asked the sitter to remain perfectly still for the portrait.
During the lecture, every sitter in the hall seemed calm and attentive.
The photographer greeted each sitter before beginning the photo session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a person who poses for an artist or photographer

Etymology

From the verb 'sit' + -er, meaning one who sits.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is paid to look after children in their home while the parents are away
person hired to look after children at home

Examples

We hired a sitter for the evening so we could go out to dinner.
The sitter arrived at 6 p.m. to watch the kids until bedtime.
Finding a reliable sitter can be a challenge for busy parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babysitter - a person employed to look after a child or children while the parents are out

Etymology

From 'sit' (to sit) + '-er' (person who does an action), referring to someone who stays with children.
