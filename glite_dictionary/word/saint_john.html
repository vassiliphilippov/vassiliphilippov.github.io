Saint John

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
a river that rises in northern Maine, USA, and flows northeast through New Brunswick, Canada, emptying into the Bay of Fundy
river in Maine and New Brunswick

Examples

The Saint John River is vital to the communities of New Brunswick.
Salmon fishing is popular along the Saint John during the summer months.
The waters of the Saint John eventually flow into the Bay of Fundy.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
a port city in eastern Canada; the largest city in the province of New Brunswick.
city in eastern Canada, New Brunswick

Examples

Saint John is known for its historic uptown area and bustling waterfront.
Many cruise ships stop at Saint John during their tours of eastern Canada.
She grew up in Saint John before moving to Toronto for university.

Etymology

Named after the Saint John River, which was named by French explorer Samuel de Champlain in 1604 for the feast day of Saint John the Baptist.

3. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
A disciple of Jesus Christ in the New Testament, traditionally believed to be the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the book of Revelation.
disciple of Jesus; author of biblical texts

Examples

In his sermon, the priest spoke about the vision of Saint John in the book of Revelation.
Many believe that Saint John wrote the Gospel that emphasizes love and faith.
Paintings often depict Saint John as a young man beside Jesus at the Last Supper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostle - one of the twelve chief disciples of Jesus
  • Synonym: evangelist - writer of one of the four Gospels in the New Testament

Etymology

Saint from Latin 'sanctus' (holy); John from Hebrew 'Yohanan' (God is gracious).
