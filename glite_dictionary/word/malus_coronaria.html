Malus coronaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.ləs ˌkɔɹ.əˈnɛɹ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.ləs ˌkɒɹ.əˈnɑː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, known for its pink blossoms and small yellow fruit; commonly called the American crab apple.
American crab apple tree species

Examples

The Malus coronaria is admired for its beautiful pink blossoms in the spring.
Early settlers used the fruit of the Malus coronaria to make preserves and ciders.
Botanists documented several Malus coronaria trees growing along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab apple - a wild apple tree or its small, tart fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'Malus' meaning 'apple tree', and 'coronaria' referring to use in garlands or crowns.
