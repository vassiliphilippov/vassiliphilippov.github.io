demotic

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑːtɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the form of modern Greek based on everyday, colloquial spoken language rather than formal or literary styles
relating to colloquial modern Greek

Examples

Many newspapers in Greece are published in demotic rather than Katharevousa.
Her thesis compares the use of demotic and classical Greek in poetry of the 20th century.
The government encouraged the teaching of demotic Greek in public schools.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Katharevousa - a formal, archaic variety of Modern Greek

Etymology

From Greek demōtikos (of the people), from dēmos (people).
