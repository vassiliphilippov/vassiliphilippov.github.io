famously

1. ADV

/ ˈfeɪməsli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪməsli / (BrE)
In a way that is widely known or recognized.
in a well-known way

Examples

The chef is famously strict about cleanliness in his kitchen.
She handled the crisis famously, impressing everyone with her calm demeanor.
Einstein famously said that imagination is more important than knowledge.

Synset Examples

in his famously anecdotal style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriously - in a way that is famous or well known, typically for some bad quality or deed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00338513-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈfeɪməsli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪməsli / (BrE)
in an excellent or very successful manner; very well
in a very successful or excellent way

Examples

The two sisters got along famously after moving in together.
She performed famously during her first solo concert.
We worked famously as a team to finish the project ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

he did splendidly in the exam
we got along famously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendidly - in a magnificent or impressive manner
  • Antonym: badly - in an unsatisfactory, unsuccessful, or poor way
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
