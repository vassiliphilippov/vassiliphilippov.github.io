pons asinorum

1. NOUN

/ pɑnz æsɪˈnɔrəm / (AmE), / pɒnz asɪˈnɔːrəm / (BrE)
a problem or test that serves as a critical challenge for inexperienced or unskilled people, especially the fifth proposition of Euclid as a traditional example
critical test for beginners; Euclid's fifth proposition

Examples

Proving the triangle's base angles are equal is known as the pons asinorum of geometry.
For many philosophy students, Kant's critique of pure reason is the intellectual pons asinorum.
The coding exam served as a pons asinorum, revealing who truly understood the basics.

Etymology

From Latin 'pons asinorum' meaning 'bridge of asses,' originally referring to Euclid's fifth proposition which many students struggled to cross.
