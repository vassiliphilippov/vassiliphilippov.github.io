to wrest

1. VERB

/ rɛst / (AmE), / rɛst / (BrE)
to take something from someone or something with great effort, force, or difficulty, often violently or with determined struggle, especially in a figurative or literal sense.
take by force or effort

Examples

The police managed to wrest the stolen bag from the thief's grasp.
It took years of negotiation to wrest control of the company from the old board.
She tried to wrest an apology from him, but he refused to admit fault.

Synset Examples

wrest the knife from his hands
wrest a meaning from the old text
wrest power from the old government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • Synonym: snatch - to quickly take something in a forceful way
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up or hand over, especially after struggle

Etymology

From Old English 'wræstan' (to twist), related to Old Norse 'reista'.
