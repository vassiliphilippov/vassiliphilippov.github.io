padouk

1. NOUN

/ pəˈduːk / (AmE), / pəˈduːk / (BrE)
a tropical tree of southeastern Asia, valued for its reddish wood with a mottled or striped black grain, or the wood itself
tropical Asian tree with reddish wood

Examples

The furniture was made from beautiful padouk harvested in Southeast Asia.
Carpenters favor padouk for its striking color and durability.
A guitar crafted from padouk wood produces a unique, resonant sound.

Etymology

From French 'padouk', possibly from a West African language, referring to the wood.
