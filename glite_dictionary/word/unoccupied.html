unoccupied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒkjʊpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or area) not seized, controlled, or taken over by a particular group, force, or authority.
not seized or controlled; not occupied

Examples

Several border villages remained unoccupied throughout the conflict.
The territory was declared unoccupied after the enemy retreated.
Many families sought shelter in the unoccupied zone during the war.

Synset Examples

unoccupied areas of France

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupied - seized and controlled by a group or authority

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'occupied', from Latin 'occupare' (to seize).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a space, position, or object) not being used, filled, or held by anyone.
not filled or in use

Examples

There are several unoccupied seats at the back of the theater.
The building has remained unoccupied since the company moved out last year.
She prefers to spend her unoccupied hours reading or walking in the park.

Synset Examples

an unoccupied telephone booth
unoccupied hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupied - being used, filled, or held by someone
  • Synonym: vacant - (of a space) not occupied; empty

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', plus 'occupied' (from Latin 'occupare', to seize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01628017-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a space, building, or position) not inhabited, used, or held by anyone; not occupied.
not inhabited or in use

Examples

The house next door has been unoccupied for several months.
Please do not leave the room unoccupied during the experiment.
The hotel has a few unoccupied rooms available for walk-in guests.

Synset Examples

an unoccupied apartment
very little unclaimed and untenanted land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupied - being used or lived in by someone
  • Synonym: vacant - having no one living in it; not occupied

Etymology

From un- (not) + occupy, patterned after Latin roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316300-s
Synset POS: s
