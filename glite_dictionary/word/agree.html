to agree

1. VERB

/ əˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː / (BrE)
to have the same opinion, feeling, or purpose as another person or group; to reach a consensus
to have the same opinion or reach consensus

Examples

After much discussion, they finally agreed on a solution to the problem.
Not everyone will agree with your point of view.
The two companies agreed to work together on the new project.

Synset Examples

No two of my colleagues would agree on whom to elect chairman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion; to not be in accord
  • Synonym: to concur - to agree, to share the same opinion

Etymology

From Old French 'agreer' (to please, be in agreement), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'gratus' (pleasing).

2. VERB

/ əˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː / (BrE)
(of statements, accounts, or characteristics) to be consistent with each other; to match or correspond in details or features.
be consistent or match

Examples

The results of the second experiment agree closely with the initial findings.
The figures in the report do not agree with those provided by the accountant.

Synset Examples

The suspect's fingerprints don't match those on the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to be the same as or equal to something else

Etymology

From Old French 'agreer', from Latin 'adgratare' (to make pleasing), from 'ad-' (to) + 'gratus' (pleasing).

3. VERB

/ əˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː / (BrE)
(of words or forms) to be grammatically consistent in person, number, gender, or case.
to show grammatical agreement

Examples

In Spanish, adjectives must agree with the nouns they modify in gender and number.
The verb does not agree with the subject in this sentence.
In Latin, pronouns agree with the nouns they refer to in case and number.

Synset Examples

Subjects and verbs must always agree in English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accord - (of words or forms) to correspond or harmonize grammatically

4. VERB

/ əˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː / (BrE)
(of food, drink, or conditions) to be agreeable, suitable, or not cause adverse effects, especially to someone's health or well-being.
be agreeable or suitable (often of food or conditions)

Examples

Red wine doesn't agree with him, so he always chooses white.

Related Terms
