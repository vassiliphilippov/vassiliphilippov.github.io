to internationalise

1. VERB

/ ɪn.tərˈnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something have an international character or involve people or organizations from many countries
make international in character
british

Examples

The company decided to internationalise its operations to access new markets.
Several universities are working hard to internationalise their student bodies.
Efforts to internationalise the event have attracted participants from over twenty countries.

Synset Examples

We internationalized the committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to internationalize - make international in character (US spelling)
  • Antonym: to nationalise - bring under national control or make national in character

Etymology

Formed from 'international' + '-ise', after the model of 'nationalise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410044-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nəl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to place (something) under the control or regulation of multiple nations, or to make subject to international authority
put under international control

Examples

The government decided to internationalise its nuclear program for greater oversight.
After years of conflict, the city was internationalised to prevent further disputes.
Attempts to internationalise the management of natural resources met with mixed reactions from local authorities.

Synset Examples

internationalize trade of certain drugs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to nationalise - to bring (something) under the control of a nation or government
  • Antonym: to privatise - to transfer (something) from public or government control to private ownership

Etymology

From international + -ise, formed after international.
