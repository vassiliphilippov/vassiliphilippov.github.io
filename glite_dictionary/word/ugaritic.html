Ugaritic

1. NOUN

/ juːˌɡɛˈrɪtɪk / (AmE), / juːˌɡæˈrɪtɪk / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language once spoken in northern Syria, known from cuneiform texts dating from the 14th to 12th centuries BCE
extinct Semitic language of northern Syria

Examples

Archaeologists discovered Ugaritic writings while excavating the ancient city of Ugarit.
Scholars study Ugaritic to better understand the development of alphabets in the ancient Near East.
The Ugaritic language has provided valuable insights into early Semitic religion and mythology.

Etymology

Named after the ancient city of Ugarit, where the language was discovered.
