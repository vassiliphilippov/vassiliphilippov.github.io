dicky

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
A man's detachable insert, often stiffened or starched, worn to simulate the front of a shirt, typically under a jacket or waistcoat.
detachable false shirt front (men's)
archaic

Examples

He wore a stiff white dicky with his tuxedo to the banquet.
His dicky slipped out of place during the speech, causing some embarrassment.
Edward quickly buttoned his dicky before heading out to the formal dinner.

Etymology

From diminutive of 'dick', probably in playful reference to shirt fronts in the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
a small third seat that folds out at the back of an old-fashioned two-seater car, usually used for occasional passengers
small folding rear seat in old cars
british archaic

Examples

He proudly showed his friends the dicky in his restored vintage roadster.
The children squeezed into the dicky for the short drive to the park.
Few modern cars have a dicky, making them a charming feature of classic automobiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble seat - an outside seat in the rear of a car, especially an older automobile

Etymology

From British English, possibly an alteration of 'dickie' (waistcoat front), with similar connotations of something secondary or additional.
