coroneted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrəˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənətɪd / (BrE)
belonging to the peerage; having or entitled to wear a coronet as a sign of nobility
of or belonging to the nobility

Examples

The museum displayed portraits of several coroneted lords and ladies from the 18th century.
She entered the room accompanied by her coroneted relatives, drawing everyone's attention.
A procession of coroneted nobles filed into the grand hall for the ceremony.

Synset Examples

the princess and her coroneted companions
the titled classes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titled - having a formal title, especially of nobility

Etymology

From 'coronet' (a small crown worn by peers) + '-ed' (forming adjectives).
