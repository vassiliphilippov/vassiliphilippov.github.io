Osmunda cinnamomea

1. NOUN

/ ɑːzˈmʌndə ˌsɪnəˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ɒzˈmʌndə ˌsɪnəməˈniːə / (BrE)
A North American fern with woolly, cinnamon-colored spore-bearing fronds in early spring, later surrounded by green fronds; young fronds are edible.
North American fern with cinnamon-colored fronds

Examples

The Osmunda cinnamomea thrives in moist woodland areas across North America.
In spring, foragers sometimes collect young Osmunda cinnamomea fronds for culinary use.
The cinnamon-colored fronds make Osmunda cinnamomea easy to identify among other ferns.

Etymology

From genus name Osmunda and species epithet from Latin 'cinnamomea', meaning 'cinnamon-colored'.
