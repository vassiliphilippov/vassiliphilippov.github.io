Naja naja

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːdʒə ˈnɑːdʒə / (AmE), / ˈnɑːdʒə ˈnɑːdʒə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Indian cobra, a venomous snake native to South and Southeast Asia
Indian cobra (species of snake)

Examples

During his research trip to India, Dr. Singh observed a Naja naja in its natural habitat.
The venom of Naja naja has been studied for its potential medicinal uses.
A Naja naja can be easily recognized by the distinctive hood it spreads when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a large, venomous snake of the family Elapidae, known for its hood

Etymology

From Latin, following standard binomial nomenclature; genus Naja from Sanskrit 'nāga' (snake).
