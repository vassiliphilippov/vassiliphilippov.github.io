Castilleja sessiliflora

1. NOUN

/ kæˌstɪliːˌeɪ sɛˌsɪləˈflɔrə / (AmE), / kæsˌtɪliːˌeɪ sɛˌsɪlɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A hairy perennial plant of the Great Plains and northern Mexico, known for its pinkish flowers.
hairy plant with pink flowers of the Great Plains

Examples

The prairie was dotted with Castilleja sessiliflora as far as the eye could see.
Botanists observed the blooming of Castilleja sessiliflora in early spring along the trails.
Many gardeners value Castilleja sessiliflora for its resilience and attractive flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name after Domingo Castillejo, an 18th-century Spanish botanist; 'sessiliflora' from Latin 'sessilis' (sitting) and 'flora' (flower), meaning 'with stalkless flowers'.
