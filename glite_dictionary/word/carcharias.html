Carcharias

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈkɛəriəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈkɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of sand sharks, comprising species found in temperate and tropical seas.
genus of sand sharks

Examples

Several species within the genus Carcharias are known for their rows of sharp teeth.
The Carcharias genus inhabits both shallow coastal and deeper marine environments.
Scientists study Carcharias to learn more about the behavior of predatory sharks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand shark - a common name for species in the genus Carcharias

Etymology

From Greek *karcharias* (a kind of shark), from *karcharos* (sharp, jagged).
