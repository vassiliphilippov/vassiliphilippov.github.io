Lithocarpus glaber

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθəˈkɑːrpəs ˈɡleɪbər / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəʊˈkɑːpəs ˈɡleɪbə / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to China and Japan, belonging to the beech family.
a tree species native to China and Japan

Examples

The leaves of Lithocarpus glaber are glossy and remain green all year round.
Botanists studied the distribution of Lithocarpus glaber in the forests of Japan.
The acorns of Lithocarpus glaber provide food for many woodland animals.

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + karpos (fruit), and Latin glaber (smooth), referring to its smooth acorn-like fruit.
