scrunch

1. VERB

/ skrʌntʃ / (AmE), / skrʌntʃ / (BrE)
(of an engine or machinery) to make a harsh, grating noise, especially typical of parts lacking lubrication.
make a harsh engine noise

Examples

The old tractor began to scrunch as we tried to start it on that cold morning.
If the engine starts to scrunch, you should check the oil level immediately.
We heard the gears scrunch when the transmission failed during the test drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - to make a harsh, grating sound
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ skrʌntʃ / (AmE), / skrʌntʃ / (BrE)
a crunching or crushing noise, especially one made when compressing or treading on something
a crunching or crushing noise

Examples

The scrunch of footsteps on the gravel echoed through the silent garden.
She smiled at the satisfying scrunch as she bit into the crisp apple.
The scrunch of snow beneath our boots signaled the arrival of winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch - a loud, grinding or crushing noise
