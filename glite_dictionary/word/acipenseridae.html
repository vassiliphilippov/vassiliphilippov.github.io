Acipenseridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˌpɛnsəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˌpɛnsəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, primitive ray-finned fishes commonly known as sturgeons, found mostly in the northern hemisphere.
family of fishes; sturgeons

Examples

The Acipenseridae family includes some of the world's largest freshwater fish.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Acipenseridae in the Danube River.
Many species in Acipenseridae are endangered due to overfishing and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - a large, long-lived fish of the Acipenseridae family

Etymology

From New Latin Acipenser (the genus name for sturgeons) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˌpɛnˈsɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˌpɛnˈsɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The biological family of large, primitive, mostly freshwater fish known as sturgeons, found in the northern hemisphere.
family of sturgeons (fish)
medical

Examples

The Acipenseridae family includes several species of sturgeons found in rivers and lakes.
Researchers study the Acipenseridae to understand the evolutionary history of ancient fish.
Caviar is harvested from the eggs of fish belonging to the Acipenseridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - large primitive fish of the family Acipenseridae

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Acipenser' (sturgeon) and the zoological suffix '-idae' denoting family.
