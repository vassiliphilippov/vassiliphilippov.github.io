Druze

1. NOUN

/ ˈdruːz / (AmE), / ˈdruːz / (BrE)
A member of an esoteric monotheistic religious group originating in the Middle East, primarily found in Lebanon, Syria, and Israel, who follow a faith that believes in the incarnation of God in Al-hakim.
member of a Middle Eastern religious sect

Examples

Many Druze communities maintain their own unique traditions and religious practices.
The Druze have historically lived in the mountainous regions of Lebanon and Syria.
Although the Druze faith is distinct, its followers often interact closely with their neighbors.

Synset Examples

a Druze is permitted to conform outwardly to the faith of the unbelievers among whom he lives

Etymology

From Arabic durūz (plural of Darazī, a follower of Darazi, an early preacher of the faith).
