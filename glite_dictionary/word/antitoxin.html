antitoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈtɑk.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈtɒk.sɪn / (BrE)
An antibody that counteracts and neutralizes a specific toxin in the body.
an antibody neutralizing a toxin
medical

Examples

The injection of antitoxin helped the patient recover from the snake bite.
Researchers developed a new antitoxin to treat cases of diphtheria.
Doctors administered the antitoxin shortly after the exposure to the harmful bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens
  • Antonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced within living cells or organisms

Etymology

From anti- (against) + toxin, first used in late 19th century scientific English.
