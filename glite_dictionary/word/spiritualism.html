spiritualism

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that the spirits of dead people can communicate with the living, especially through a medium
belief in communication with spirits

Examples

After losing her father, she became interested in spiritualism and attended séances hoping to make contact.
The rise of spiritualism in the 19th century led to a surge in mediums and psychic investigators.
Many people seek comfort and answers through spiritualism, believing they can communicate with loved ones who have passed away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritism - the belief that spirits of the dead can communicate with the living

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritus' (breath, spirit) and English '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuˌælɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that spiritual matters, especially communication with the spirits of the dead, are real and important; or a concern with things of the spirit rather than the material world
belief in or concern with spiritual matters

Examples

She devoted her life to spiritualism and sought meaning beyond the material world.
The rise of spiritualism in the 19th century led to widespread interest in séances and the afterlife.
His interest in spiritualism shaped his personal philosophy and daily practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: materialism - belief that physical matter is the only reality
  • Synonym: spirituality - the quality or state of being concerned with religion or the human spirit

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritus' (spirit) + English '-ism'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Theology) The doctrine that asserts the separate existence of God, independent of matter or the physical universe.
theological doctrine of God's independent existence

Examples

Some branches of Christianity adopt spiritualism as a core concept in their teachings.
Debates between spiritualism and materialism have shaped modern theology.
He wrote extensively about spiritualism and its implications for the existence of God.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: materialism - the doctrine that physical matter is the only or fundamental reality
  • Synonym: theism - the belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator who intervenes in the universe

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritualis' (spiritual) + '-ism'.
