jamming

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate emission or reflection of electromagnetic energy to prevent or disrupt the use of electronic devices or communication systems, especially for military purposes.
deliberate electromagnetic interference; signal disruption

Examples

Military forces employed jamming to block the enemy's radar systems during the operation.
The pilot reported problems with radio communication due to heavy jamming in the area.
Modern warfare often relies on sophisticated jamming to disrupt missiles and communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - unwanted disturbance in radio or electrical signals

Etymology

From 'jam' (to block or interfere with) + -ing.
