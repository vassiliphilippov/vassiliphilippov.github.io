Denisonia superba

1. NOUN

/ dɛnɪˈsoʊniə suˈpɜrbə / (AmE), / dɛnɪˈsəʊniə suˈpɜːbə / (BrE)
A venomous but sluggish reddish-brown snake species native to Australia, known for its distinctive coloration.
Australian venomous reddish-brown snake

Examples

The biologist carefully observed the Denisonia superba as it slithered slowly across the sand.
Despite its venom, the Denisonia superba is not considered aggressive and is rarely encountered by humans.
Conservationists are studying the habitat requirements of the Denisonia superba to protect it from environmental threats.

Etymology

From the genus name Denisonia and species name from Latin 'superba' (superb, magnificent).
