angulate

1. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make or become angular in form or appearance
make or become angular

Examples

The designer chose to angulate the structure for a modern look.
During movement, the insect's legs angulate sharply at each joint.
Over time, the soft branches angulate and form striking geometric patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angular - having angles or sharp corners

Etymology

From Latin 'angulatus', past participle of 'angulare' (to make angular), from 'angulus' (angle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
having angles or an angular shape; forming or shaped like an angle
having angles or angular shape

Examples

The insect's angulate antennae help distinguish it from similar species.
Modern furniture often features angulate forms to create a striking visual impression.
The mountain ridge had a distinctly angulate profile against the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angular - having angles or sharp corners

Etymology

From Latin angulatus, past participle of 'angulare' (to make angular) from 'angulus' (angle).
