Tory

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈtɔːri / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the Conservative Party in the United Kingdom, especially in reference to the party's historical roots and opposition to the Whigs
member of the UK Conservative Party
british

Examples

My grandfather always considered himself a proud Tory and supported the Conservative Party in every election.
The clash between the Tory and Whig parties shaped much of British political history.
Despite her young age, she joined the Tory youth organization to participate in campaign activities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Whig - member of a former UK political party opposed to the Tories

Etymology

From Irish *tóraidhe* (pursuer, outlaw), originally used as a political epithet in 17th-century England.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔri / (AmE), / ˈtɔːri / (BrE)
(in the American Revolution) an American colonist who remained loyal to the British crown; a Loyalist.
loyal American colonist during the Revolution

Examples

Many wealthy merchants were labeled Tories during the Revolutionary War.
His family risked everything by siding with the Tories instead of the rebels.
After the war, most Tories fled to Canada to avoid persecution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loyalist - an American colonist who supported the British during the Revolution
  • Antonym: Patriot - an American colonist who supported independence during the Revolution

Etymology

From earlier political usage in England, originally applied to supporters of the monarchy from Irish *tóraidhe* (pursuer).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɹi / (AmE), / ˈtɔːri / (BrE)
a supporter of traditional political and social institutions; a political conservative, especially in Britain
political conservative, especially in Britain

Examples

He has always identified himself as a proud Tory in British politics.
During the campaign, the Tory candidate promised to uphold traditional values.
Many Tory MPs voted against the proposed reforms in Parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • Antonym: liberal - a person who is open to new behavior or opinions and favors reform

Etymology

From Irish *tóraidhe* (pursuer), originally used as a term of abuse for Irish outlaws, later adopted in English political context in the 17th century.
