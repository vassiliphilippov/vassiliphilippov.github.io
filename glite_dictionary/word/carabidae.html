Carabidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræbəˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˈræbɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of ground beetles, typically dark-colored, fast-moving, and found worldwide, many of which are predatory.
family of ground beetles

Examples

The Carabidae are known for their important role in controlling pest insects in gardens and crops.
Experts have identified over 40,000 species in the Carabidae family worldwide.
Many Carabidae species are fast runners that prey on other invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground beetle - any beetle belonging to the family Carabidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Carabidae, based on the type genus Carabus and the suffix -idae (denoting animal families).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈræbɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˈræbɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles commonly known as ground beetles, characterized by their hard bodies and predatory habits.
family of ground beetles

Examples

The Carabidae are known for their role in controlling pest populations in gardens and farms.
Entomologists study members of Carabidae to understand their diverse hunting behaviors.
Several species within Carabidae are easily recognized by their shiny, dark exoskeletons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground beetle - a common name for members of the family Carabidae

Etymology

From New Latin Carabidae, based on the type genus Carabus.
