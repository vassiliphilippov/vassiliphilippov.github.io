sapiently

1. ADV

/ ˈseɪ.pi.ənt.li / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.pi.ənt.li / (BrE)
in a wise or shrewd manner; showing good judgment or discernment
in a wise or shrewd manner

Examples

She sapiently advised her friends on the best course of action.
The committee acted sapiently to resolve the dispute before it escalated.
He sapiently avoided making unnecessary enemies in the business world.

Synset Examples

he invested his fortune astutely
he was acutely insightful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisely - in a way that shows good judgment; wisely
  • Antonym: foolishly - in a way that lacks good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin sapiēns (wise) + -ly (adverbial suffix).
