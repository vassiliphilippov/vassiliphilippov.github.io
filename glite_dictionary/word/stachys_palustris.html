Stachys palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪkɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈstakɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
a perennial herb of the mint family, with an odorless rhizome, typically found in moist areas of the Northern Hemisphere
perennial herb found in moist areas

Examples

The botanist identified the unusual wetland flower as Stachys palustris.
In early summer, Stachys palustris blooms with clusters of pink-purple flowers along streams and marshes.
Gardeners sometimes plant Stachys palustris to attract bees and support local ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Stachys' (from Greek 'stakhys', meaning 'ear of grain') and Latin 'palustris' (of the marsh).
