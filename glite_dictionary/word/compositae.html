Compositae

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑzɪˌti / (AmE), / kɒmˈpɒzɪti / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants with dense heads composed of many small florets, including daisies, asters, sunflowers, and dandelions
family of flowering plants with composite flower heads

Examples

The family Compositae includes well-known plants such as sunflowers, daisies, and dandelions.
Many wildflowers in Europe belong to the Compositae, making them important for pollinators.
Botanists once used Compositae as the standard name, but now often prefer Asteraceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asteraceae - the modern botanical name for the Compositae family

Etymology

From Latin compositus (arranged); feminine plural, referring to the composite flower structure.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpoʊzəˌtiː / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪtiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants characterized by heads composed of many small florets, including asters, daisies, dandelions, sunflowers, and marigolds; also known as Asteraceae
family of flowering plants (asters, daisies, etc.)

Examples

The Compositae family includes well-known garden flowers such as sunflowers and daisies.
Botanists use Compositae to classify plants with composite flower heads.
Many members of the Compositae family are important for their medicinal or culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asteraceae - the alternative scientific name for the family Compositae

Etymology

From Latin compositus (composed), referring to the composite flower heads.
