amble

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈæmbəl / (BrE)
a leisurely walk taken in a relaxed manner, often in a public place
a slow, relaxed walk

Examples

On Sunday afternoons, they enjoyed an amble through the city park.
She took an amble along the waterfront, enjoying the cool breeze.
Our relaxing amble around the gardens lasted over an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk, especially for pleasure

Etymology

From Old French ambler (to go at a slow pace), from Latin ambulare (to walk)

2. VERB

/ ˈæm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈæm.bl̩ / (BrE)
to walk at a slow, relaxed, and easy pace, especially for pleasure
walk in a slow, relaxed way

Examples

We decided to amble along the riverside, enjoying the sunny afternoon.
He often ambles through the park to clear his mind after work.
The group ambled slowly down the country lane, taking in the scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a leisurely way
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps, often briskly

Etymology

From Middle English *amblen*, from Old French *ambler*, from Latin *ambulat-* (walked about).

Synset POS: v
