midwestern

1. ADJ

/ mɪdˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / mɪdˈwɛstən / (BrE)
Relating to the Midwest region of the United States, typically including states such as Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Iowa, Missouri, Kansas, Nebraska, and sometimes Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota.
of the Midwest region of the US
american

Examples

She grew up in a midwestern town known for its friendly community.
The company's midwestern headquarters are located in Chicago.
His midwestern accent was noticeable during the interview.

Synset Examples

a midwestern city
midwestern accent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midwest - The region of the north-central United States

Etymology

Formed from 'Midwest' (the name of the region) plus the suffix '-ern' (denoting origin or relation).
