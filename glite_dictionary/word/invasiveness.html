invasiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of tending to spread or intrude, especially referring to the ability of organisms or diseases to penetrate and affect healthy tissues or environments.
tendency to spread or invade, especially medically

Examples

The invasiveness of the tumor made it difficult for surgeons to remove it completely.
Some plants are known for their invasiveness, quickly overtaking native species in a new environment.
Strict regulations are needed to control the invasiveness of non-native animal populations.

Synset Examples

Our results provide support for within-host evolution as one but not the sole explanation for the invasiveness of these bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressiveness - the quality of being aggressive, similar to invasiveness in certain contexts

Etymology

From invasive + -ness, forming a noun from the adjective 'invasive'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of requiring entry into the body, especially by incision or by insertion of instruments, as in medical procedures.
quality of entering the body (medicine)
medical

Examples

Many patients prefer treatments that reduce the invasiveness of surgical procedures.
Doctors are always seeking new ways to lower the invasiveness of diagnostic tests.
The invasiveness of the operation made recovery much longer than anticipated.

Synset Examples

Surgeons have traditionally performed this surgery using a conventional open incision into the chest wall or abdomen but today are increasingly offering the operation as a laparoscopic procedure that lessens the invasiveness of the surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasive - Involving entry into the body, especially by cutting or inserting instruments
  • Degree Lower: minimally invasive - Causing the least possible disruption to the body

Etymology

From invasive + -ness; 'invasive' from Latin invas-, past participle stem of invadere (to go into, attack).
