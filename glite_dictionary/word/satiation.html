satiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌseɪsɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being fully satisfied, especially after eating to the point where one cannot consume more.
the state of being fully satisfied

Examples

After a large holiday meal, a feeling of satiation quickly settled over the entire family.
The athlete ate slowly to avoid satiation before his training was complete.
Excessive satiation can sometimes lead to discomfort and sluggishness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiety - the feeling or state of being sated

Etymology

From Latin satiatio (satisfaction, satiety), from satiare (to satisfy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌseɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌseɪʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or state of being fully satisfied, especially after having one's hunger or desire completely fulfilled.
the act of being fully satisfied

Examples

After several large plates of food, a deep sense of satiation settled over the guests.
The satiation of curiosity is sometimes followed by a desire to learn even more.
Scientists study how satiation influences eating behavior and dietary choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfaction - a feeling of pleasure when a need or desire is fulfilled
  • Antonym: hunger - the state of needing or wanting food

Etymology

From Latin satiatio (full satisfaction), from satiāre (to fill, satisfy).
