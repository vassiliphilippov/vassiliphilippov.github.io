Cornus canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs kəˈneɪdənˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A creeping perennial herb in the dogwood family, known for its red berries and whorled leaves, native to northern North America.
creeping herb with red berries, native to North America

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with the distinctive red berries of Cornus canadensis.
Many gardeners plant Cornus canadensis as a decorative groundcover in shady areas.
Hikers often recognize Cornus canadensis by its clustered leaves and white flowers in spring.

Etymology

Genus from Latin 'cornus' (dogwood); species name refers to Canada.
