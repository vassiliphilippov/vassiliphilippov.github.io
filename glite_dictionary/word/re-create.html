to re-create

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈkreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkreɪt / (BrE)
to produce something again; to make something exist or appear as it did before
make again; reproduce

Examples

The artist tried to re-create the beauty of the original painting.
They managed to re-create the atmosphere of the jazz age with music and decor.
Many filmmakers strive to re-create historical events as accurately as possible.

Synset Examples

Re-create the boom of the West on a small scale
she recreated the feeling of the 1920's with her stage setting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reproduce - to make a copy or representation of something

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'create' from 'creare' (to bring forth, create).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01622373-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈkreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriˈkriːeɪt / (BrE)
to make a replica or copy of something; to produce again as closely as possible to the original
make a replica; reproduce

Examples

The artist tried to re-create the lost mural from old photographs.
Scientists are working to re-create ancient climate conditions in the lab.
He spent hours attempting to re-create his grandmother’s famous pie recipe.

Synset Examples

copy that drawing
re-create a picture by Rembrandt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy of; to duplicate
  • Synonym: copy - to produce something that is similar or identical to another thing

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) + 'create'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01697776-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriˈkreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkriːeɪt / (BrE)
to form or produce again in the imagination; to recall and reconstruct an image, scene, or experience in the mind
form again in the imagination

Examples

She tried to re-create the joy of her childhood vacations in her writing.
Artists often re-create the scenes they remember most vividly from their travels.
His mind re-creates lost moments from the past with remarkable detail.

Synset Examples

His mind re-creates the entire world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconstruct - rebuild or form again, especially from memory
  • Synonym: to imagine - form a mental image or concept

Etymology

From re- (again) + create, from Latin creare (to create).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00738804-v
Synset POS: v
