logy

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈləʊɡi / (BrE)
dull, sluggish, or slow to think or move, especially as a result of tiredness, illness, or intoxication.
sluggish or slow to react

Examples

After staying up all night, I felt logy and unfocused during the meeting.
The medicine made her drowsy and slightly logy throughout the afternoon.
He was logy from the long flight and struggled to keep his eyes open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluggish - moving or reacting more slowly than usual
  • Synonym: lethargic - affected by lethargy; sluggish and apathetic

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *log* (heavy, dull).
