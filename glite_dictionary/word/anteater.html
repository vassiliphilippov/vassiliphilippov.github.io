anteater

1. NOUN

/ ˈænˌtir / (AmE), / ˈɑːntˌiːtə / (BrE)
A nocturnal burrowing mammal of African grasslands that feeds mainly on termites; the sole extant member of the order Tubulidentata, also known as the aardvark.
African nocturnal mammal that eats termites

Examples

The anteater emerged from its burrow at dusk to search for termites.
Unlike other mammals, the anteater has a long snout adapted for eating insects.
Researchers tracked the movements of the anteater across the African grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - nocturnal African mammal with a tubular snout that eats termites

2. NOUN

/ ˈæntˌitər / (AmE), / ˈæntiːtə / (BrE)
a small Australian marsupial with a long snout and strong claws, feeding mainly on termites; also known as the numbat
small Australian marsupial; termite eater

Examples

The anteater uses its sticky tongue to catch termites hidden in logs.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered anteater in Western Australia.
A single anteater can eat up to 20,000 termites in a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numbat - small, termite-eating Australian marsupial; also called banded anteater

4. NOUN

/ ˈænˌtitər / (AmE), / ˈænˌtiːtə / (BrE)
a mammal with a long snout and tongue, specialized for feeding on ants and termites, found in Central and South America, Africa, and Asia
mammal that eats ants and termites

Examples

The anteater uses its long tongue to extract ants from narrow crevices.
At the zoo, we watched a giant anteater eating from an anthill.
Unlike most mammals, an anteater has no teeth but relies on its sticky tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangolin - a scaly mammal also called 'scaly anteater'
  • Synonym: aardvark - a nocturnal burrowing mammal that eats ants and termites

Etymology

From 'ant' + 'eater', describing its feeding habit.

6. NOUN

a small mammal with a long snout and claws, covered with spines, that feeds mainly on ants and termites; especially the echidna of New Guinea and Australia
spiny mammal feeding on ants and termites

Examples

An anteater was spotted foraging in the New Guinea forest early this morning.
Unlike other mammals, the anteater lays eggs rather than giving birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - spiny ant-eating monotreme native to Australia and New Guinea
