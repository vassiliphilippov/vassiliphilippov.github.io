yerba buena

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛrbə ˈbweɪnə / (AmE), / ˌjɜːbə ˈbweɪnə / (BrE)
a trailing perennial evergreen herb native to the northwestern United States, with small white flowers, used medicinally
a medicinal perennial herb of northwestern US

Examples

Native Americans have traditionally used yerba buena to make soothing herbal teas.
The leaves of yerba buena emit a refreshing minty aroma when crushed.
We found wild yerba buena growing along the shaded trails in Oregon.

Etymology

Spanish, literally ‘good herb’, used in reference to various aromatic plants.
