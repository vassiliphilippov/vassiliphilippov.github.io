princewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.wʊd / (BrE)
a large tropical American tree (genus Cordia) valued for its creamy white flowers and hard, useful wood
a tropical American tree of the genus Cordia

Examples

The dense wood of the princewood is highly prized for fine furniture making.
We admired the abundant white blossoms of the princewood during our walk through the forest.
Carpenters in the region often use princewood in their woodworking projects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tropical American tree valued for its timber, especially for its hard and durable wood.
tropical American timber tree

Examples

The durable timber from the princewood is highly valued in furniture making.
Many tropical forests are home to the rare princewood tree.
Export regulations limit the logging of princewood due to its ecological importance.
