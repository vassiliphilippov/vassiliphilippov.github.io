Boletus frostii

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs ˈfrɒsti.aɪ / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs ˈfrɒsti.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of mushroom with a bright red cap and a red, net-like stalk, native to North America.
a North American red-capped bolete mushroom

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with brilliant red Boletus frostii specimens after the rain.
Mushroom hunters admire the striking appearance of Boletus frostii, but caution is advised before consuming any wild fungi.
In the field guide, Boletus frostii is noted for its reticulate stalk and sticky red cap.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Boletus' (mushroom) and named after American botanist Charles Christopher Frost.
