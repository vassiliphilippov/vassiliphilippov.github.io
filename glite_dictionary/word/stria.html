stria

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a thin, narrow line or groove, especially one of a number of such ridges, marks, or streaks found in tissue, rocks, or other materials
thin groove or ridge, esp. in tissue or rocks

Examples

The muscle slide revealed a prominent stria running along the tissue sample.
Glaciers carved a visible stria into the exposed rock face.
Each stria in the mineral indicated a separate period of deposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long, narrow band or strip, typically one of several
  • Synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'stria' meaning a furrow or channel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a narrow stripe, line, or band, especially one of a contrasting color or texture from the surrounding area
narrow stripe or band

Examples

A single stria on the butterfly's wing was bright blue.
Medical scans revealed multiple striae on the patient's liver.
The marble was prized for the golden striae running through its white surface.

Synset Examples

chromosomes exhibit characteristic bands
the black and yellow banding of bees and wasps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long, narrow band of a different color or texture

Etymology

From Latin 'stria' (furrow, groove, channel).
