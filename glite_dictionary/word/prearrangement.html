prearrangement

1. NOUN

/ priː.əˈreɪndʒ.mənt / (AmE), / priː.əˈreɪndʒ.mənt / (BrE)
an arrangement or plan made in advance
something arranged beforehand

Examples

We had a prearrangement to meet at the café on Saturday.
Without a prearrangement, it may be difficult to reserve a table at the restaurant.
Their early prearrangement ensured the event went smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a plan or preparation for a future event

Etymology

From pre- (before) + arrangement, ultimately from Old French arranger.
