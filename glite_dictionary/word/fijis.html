Fijis

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːdʒiz / (AmE), / ˈfiːdʒiz / (BrE)
a group of over 800 islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, known collectively as the Fiji Islands, with Viti Levu and Vanua Levu as the largest islands.
group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Examples

Tourists from all over the world come to visit the beautiful Fijis every year.
Marine biologists study the coral reefs surrounding the Fijis to monitor ecosystem health.
The Fijis are renowned for their white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fiji - the main island country in the southwestern Pacific

Etymology

Plural form of 'Fiji', after the native name Viti.
