damselfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæmsəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdæms(ə)lˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored tropical marine fish, typically found on coral reefs
small, colorful tropical marine fish

Examples

A bright blue damselfish darted among the coral branches in the aquarium.
Many damselfish fiercely defend their territory against larger intruders.
We spotted several colorful damselfish while snorkeling near the reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clownfish - a small, brightly colored fish that lives among anemones

Etymology

From 'damsel' (referring to their small size) + 'fish'.
