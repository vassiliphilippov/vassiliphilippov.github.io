bakeshop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkˌʃɒp / (BrE)
A shop or workplace where bread, cakes, and other baked goods are made or sold.
shop selling or making baked goods

Examples

On Sunday mornings, the aroma from the local bakeshop fills the streets.
She opened a small bakeshop specializing in gluten-free pastries.
They stopped by the bakeshop to pick up fresh bread for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bakery - a place where bread and cakes are baked and sometimes sold

Etymology

From 'bake' + 'shop', meaning a place for baking or selling baked goods.
