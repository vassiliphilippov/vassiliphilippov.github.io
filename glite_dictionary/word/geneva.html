geneva

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnivə / (AmE), / dʒəˈniːvə / (BrE)
A type of gin originally made in the Netherlands, flavored with juniper berries; also known as jenever or genever.
Dutch gin flavored with juniper

Examples

He poured himself a glass of geneva before dinner.
Traditional Dutch geneva has a distinct juniper flavor.
Many cocktail recipes from the 19th century called for geneva instead of modern gin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gin - A clear alcoholic spirit flavored with juniper berries

Etymology

From Dutch 'jenever', from French 'genièvre' (juniper), ultimately from Latin 'juniperus'.
