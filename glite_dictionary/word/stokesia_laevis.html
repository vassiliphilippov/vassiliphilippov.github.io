Stokesia laevis

1. PROPN

/ stoʊˈksiːə ˈlaɪvɪs / (AmE), / stəʊˈksiːə ˈlaɪvɪs / (BrE)
An erect perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, known for its large, usually blue flower heads.
flowering plant of southeastern US

Examples

The Stokesia laevis in her garden bloomed with striking blue flowers each spring.
Gardeners often choose Stokesia laevis for its resilience and ornamental blooms.
Many pollinators are attracted to the vibrant blossoms of Stokesia laevis.

Etymology

Named after British botanist Jonathan Stokes (1755–1831); 'laevis' is Latin for 'smooth'.
