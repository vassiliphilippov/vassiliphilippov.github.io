palaeozoology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊzoʊˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəzəʊˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of science concerned with the study of fossil animals.
study of fossil animals

Examples

She decided to specialize in palaeozoology during her graduate studies at the university.
Recent discoveries in palaeozoology have shed new light on the behavior of extinct mammals.
The museum's exhibit on Ice Age animals was curated by experts in palaeozoology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palaeontology - the study of fossils and ancient life forms
  • Synonym: zoology - the scientific study of animals, both living and extinct

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + zoology, meaning 'the study of ancient animals'.
