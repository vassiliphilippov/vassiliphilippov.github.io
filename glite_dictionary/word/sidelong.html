sidelong

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlɒŋ / (BrE)
Inclining or directed to one side; slanting or indirect.
inclined or directed to one side

Examples

She cast a sidelong glance at her friend, unsure whether to speak.
The dog crept through the doorway in a sidelong manner, trying not to be seen.
He gave a sidelong look at the exit, thinking about leaving early.

Synset Examples

moved downward in a sidelong way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - to, toward, or from the side; obliquely

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'side' and 'long,' meaning along the side.

6. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.lɒŋ / (BrE)
(especially of a look or glance) directed to the side, often with doubt, suspicion, or covert intent.
to the side (as of a glance)

Examples

He gave her a sidelong glance as they passed each other in the hallway.
She smiled at him sidelong, unsure if he had noticed her joke.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askance - with suspicion, mistrust, or disapproval (especially of a glance)
  • Antonym: direct - straightforward, without deviation or slant (especially of a look)

Etymology

From Middle English 'sidelong' (side + long), meaning ‘by the side’ or ‘slanting’.
