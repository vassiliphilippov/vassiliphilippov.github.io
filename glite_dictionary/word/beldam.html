beldam

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛl.dəm / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.dəm / (BrE)
an ugly or malicious old woman, often depicted in literature as a witch or hag
an evil-looking old woman
archaic

Examples

The children whispered that the old woman in the woods was a beldam who cast wicked spells.
In the story, a beldam lurks in the shadows, frightening the villagers.
Legends often feature a beldam as the antagonist, preying on unsuspecting travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hag - an old woman who is often ugly or wicked, especially in folklore

Etymology

From Middle English *beldame*, from Old French *bel* (beautiful) + *dame* (lady); originally meant 'grandmother' or 'old woman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛl.dəm / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.dəm / (BrE)
an old woman, often used to refer to one who is ugly or frightening; now archaic or literary.
an old woman (archaic, literary)
archaic

Examples

The mysterious beldam watched the children from her window every afternoon.
In the ancient village, the beldam was both feared and respected for her wisdom.
A rumor spread that the beldam who lived alone in the woods could cast spells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crone - an old woman, especially one who is unpleasant or ugly
  • Synonym: hag - an ugly old woman, often with supernatural associations

Etymology

From Old French *beldame* ('grandmother'), from *bel* (fair) + *dame* (lady).
