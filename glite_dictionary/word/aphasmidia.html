Aphasmidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.fæzˈmɪd.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌæf.æzˈmɪd.i.ə / (BrE)
A subgroup of nematodes distinguished by the absence of phasmids, used in some classification systems.
group of nematodes lacking phasmids

Examples

Many soil-dwelling nematodes are classified within the Aphasmidia subgroup.
The presence or absence of phasmids distinguishes Aphasmidia from other groups.
Some scientists debate the evolutionary significance of Aphasmidia in nematode taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Phasmidia - Nematode group that possesses phasmids

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'phasma' (apparition, phantom) + '-idia' (diminutive plural suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌfæsˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌfæsˈmɪdiə / (BrE)
a subgroup of nematode worms distinguished by the absence of phasmids, used in some biological classification systems
a subgroup of nematode worms (Nematoda)

Examples

Many members of Aphasmidia are studied for their importance in soil ecosystems.
The classification of nematodes into Aphasmidia and Phasmidia is no longer commonly used.
Some scientists debate whether Aphasmidia should be recognized as a separate subgroup.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Phasmidia - another major subgroup of nematodes, characterized by the presence of phasmids

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' meaning 'without' and 'phasma' meaning 'apparition' or 'phantom', referring to the absence of phasmids.
