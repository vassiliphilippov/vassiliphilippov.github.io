wrathful

1. ADJ

/ ˈræθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒθ.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing intense anger; full of wrath
full of intense anger

Examples

The wrathful crowd demanded justice after the announcement.
His wrathful expression made everyone fall silent.
A wrathful storm raged across the coastline, uprooting trees and flooding roads.

Synset Examples

they trembled before the wrathful queen
but wroth as he was, a short struggle ended in reconciliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing anger
  • Degree Higher: furious - extremely angry
  • Degree Lower: annoyed - feeling slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

From Middle English *wrathful*, from Old English *wræth*, meaning anger or rage.
