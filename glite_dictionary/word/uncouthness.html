uncouthness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkuːθ.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkuːθ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being rude, lacking good manners, or unsophisticated.
rudeness or lack of manners

Examples

His uncouthness at the dinner table embarrassed everyone present.
Despite her intelligence, her uncouthness made social gatherings uncomfortable.
The leader's uncouthness was shocking to those accustomed to more refined company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - the quality of being impolite or disrespectful
  • Antonym: sophistication - the quality of being cultured or refined

Etymology

Formed from 'uncouth' (from Old English 'uncūth', unknown, strange, rude) + '-ness'.
