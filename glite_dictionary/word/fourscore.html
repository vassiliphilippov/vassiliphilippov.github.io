fourscore

1. NUM

/ ˈfɔrˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskɔː / (BrE)
eighty; the number equivalent to eight times ten, used especially in historical or literary contexts.
eighty (80), archaic/literary
archaic

Examples

Abraham Lincoln began the Gettysburg Address with "Fourscore and seven years ago."
He lived to the impressive age of fourscore.
The village has seen fourscore harvests pass since the old oak tree was planted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eighty - the number 80

Etymology

From Middle English 'foure score', meaning 'four times twenty'.
