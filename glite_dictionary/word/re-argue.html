to re-argue

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈɑrɡju / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɑːɡjuː / (BrE)
to argue or discuss something again, especially after it has already been considered or decided
argue again

Examples

The legal team decided to re-argue the case after new evidence came to light.
He didn’t want to re-argue the same point they discussed yesterday.
If you disagree with the verdict, you can file a motion to re-argue.

Synset Examples

This politician will be forced into re-arguing an old national campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to give reasons for or against something in a discussion

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'argue'.
