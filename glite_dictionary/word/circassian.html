Circassian

1. NOUN

/ sərˈkæʃən / (AmE), / səˈkæsiən / (BrE)
A member of a mostly Sunni Muslim ethnic group native to northwestern Caucasia.
member of an ethnic group from northwestern Caucasia

Examples

Many Circassians migrated to the Ottoman Empire in the nineteenth century.
The Circassian language belongs to the Northwest Caucasian language family.
He is proud of his Circassian heritage and traditions.

Etymology

From Russian *Cherkess* (‘Circassian’), possibly via Turkish *Çerkes*.

2. NOUN

/ sərˈkæʃən / (AmE), / səˈkæʃən / (BrE)
the language of the Circassian people, spoken in the northwestern Caucasus region
language of the Circassian people

Examples

The Circassian language is known for its complex consonant system.
Few people today are fluent in Circassian, as it is considered endangered.
Linguists have studied Circassian due to its unique grammatical structures.

Etymology

From Circassia, the region in the northwestern Caucasus, and the Russian term ‘Cherkes’.

3. NOUN

/ sərˈkæʃən / (AmE), / səˈkæsiən / (BrE)
a member of a Sunni Muslim ethnic group native to northwestern Caucasia
member of a Caucasian ethnic group

Examples

The Circassian community has preserved its traditions for centuries in the Caucasus.
She is proud of her Circassian heritage and often shares stories about their culture.
Many Circassians were displaced during the 19th century and settled in the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chechen - member of another ethnic group from the Caucasus region

Etymology

From Russian 'Cherkess' (Черке́с), referring to the Circassian people.
