yell

1. VERB

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
to shout or cry out loudly, especially due to pain, excitement, or anger
to shout loudly

Examples

The fans began to yell as their team scored the final goal.
Stop yelling; I can hear you perfectly well from here.
He yelled in pain when he twisted his ankle.

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to speak or call out loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *yellen*, from Old English *giellan* (to scream, yell).

2. NOUN

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
a loud, sharp shout, especially one expressing strong emotion such as anger, pain, or excitement
a loud, sharp shout or cry

Examples

A sudden yell rang out from the crowd when the team scored the winning goal.
She let out a yell of surprise when the puppy jumped onto her lap.
He stifled a yell of pain after accidentally touching the hot stove.

Synset Examples

a cry of rage
a yell of pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry expressing strong emotion
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or quiet utterance

Etymology

From Middle English yellen, from Old English giellan (to yell, shout).

Examples

Synset Examples

the speaker was interrupted by loud cries from the rear of the audience

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly using one's breath rather than one's throat

Likely from Middle English yellen, from Old English giellan (to shout, scream).

4. VERB

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
to utter or declare something in a very loud voice, often as a result of emotion such as anger, excitement, or pain
to shout loudly

Examples

Please don't yell at me; I'm right here and can hear you perfectly well.
He had to yell over the noise of the construction site to be understood.
The coach yelled instructions from the sidelines during the game.

Synset Examples

You don't have to yell — I can hear you just fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to speak or call out very loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly

Etymology

From Middle English yellen, from Old English giellan or giddan (to shout, sing, call out).

Source: WordNet
