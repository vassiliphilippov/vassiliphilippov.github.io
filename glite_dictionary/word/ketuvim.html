Ketuvim

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtuːvɪm / (AmE), / kɛˈtuvɪm / (BrE)
the third section of the Hebrew Scriptures, comprising poetic, wisdom, and historical books
third division of the Hebrew Bible

Examples

Many scholars study the Ketuvim to understand Jewish literary and poetic traditions.
The Ketuvim includes books such as Psalms, Proverbs, and Job.
In Jewish tradition, the Ketuvim is read and interpreted alongside the Torah and the Prophets.

Etymology

From Hebrew כְּתוּבִים (kĕthūvīm), meaning 'writings'.
