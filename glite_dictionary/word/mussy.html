mussy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌsi / (AmE), / ˈmʌsi / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) dirty and untidy; in a state of disorder.
dirty and untidy

Examples

After the kids played all afternoon, the living room looked especially mussy.
Her handwritten notes were so mussy that nobody else could read them.
He always felt uncomfortable in a mussy environment and preferred everything to be in its place.

Synset Examples

a mussy fussy bedroom
a child's messy eating habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messy - dirty or untidy; in a state of disorder
  • Antonym: tidy - neat and organized; in good order

Etymology

Variant of 'messy', likely influenced by 'muss' (to make untidy) from Middle English 'muss(en)'.
