Aplectrum hyemale

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɛktrəm haɪˈɛməli / (AmE), / əˈplɛktrəm hɪˈiːməli / (BrE)
A North American orchid species with a single leaf and yellowish-brown flowers, commonly known as putty root.
species of North American orchid

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with blooming Aplectrum hyemale in early spring.
Botanists studied the distinctive leaf pattern of Aplectrum hyemale during their fieldwork.
Gardeners sometimes refer to Aplectrum hyemale as the putty root due to its sticky roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putty root - Common name for Aplectrum hyemale, a North American orchid

Etymology

From Greek 'aplectrum' (a kind of cord or handle) and Latin 'hyemale' (of winter).
