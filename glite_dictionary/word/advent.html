Advent

1. NOUN

/ ˈædˌvɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæd.vənt / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the anticipated second coming of Jesus Christ, especially as judge at the Last Judgment.
the Second Coming of Christ in Christianity

Examples

Many Christians await the Advent with hope and anticipation.
The doctrine of the Advent is central to Christian eschatology.
Artists have depicted the Advent in paintings throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Coming - the prophesied return of Christ to Earth in Christian eschatology

Etymology

From Latin 'adventus' (arrival, approach), from 'advenire' (to arrive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɛnt / (BrE)
The period including the four Sundays before Christmas, observed by many Christians as a time of waiting and preparation for the celebration of the birth of Christ.
the four-week period before Christmas

Examples

Many churches hold special services during Advent to prepare for Christmas.
The children opened a new door on their Advent calendar each day leading up to Christmas.
She enjoys the traditions and reflection that come with Advent each year.

Etymology

From Latin 'adventus' (arrival), especially 'Adventus Domini' (the coming of the Lord).
