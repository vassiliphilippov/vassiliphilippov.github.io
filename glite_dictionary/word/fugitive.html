fugitive

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (BrE)
a person who is running away or hiding, especially from the police or law enforcement
person fleeing from law enforcement

Examples

The police have been searching for the fugitive for several weeks.
After escaping from prison, the fugitive hid in an abandoned cabin.
Authorities warned the public not to approach the dangerous fugitive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escapee - someone who has escaped from captivity or confinement
  • Synonym: runaway - a person who has run away, especially from imprisonment or law enforcement
  • Antonym: captive - a person who has been captured or imprisoned

Etymology

From Latin 'fugitivus' (fleeing, runaway), from 'fugere' (to flee).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (BrE)
a person who has escaped from a place or is hiding, especially to avoid arrest or an unwanted situation
person who flees or escapes

Examples

The police searched the city for the fugitive after he escaped custody.
Many fugitives seek safety in neighboring countries during times of conflict.
The journalist interviewed a fugitive who had fled oppressive working conditions.

Synset Examples

fugitives from the sweatshops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escapee - a person who has escaped from somewhere, especially prison
  • Synonym: refugee - a person who has been forced to leave their country to escape danger
  • Antonym: captured - having been caught after fleeing

Etymology

From Latin fugitivus (fleeing, apt to flee), from fugere (to flee).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (BrE)
lasting for only a short time; fleeting or transient
lasting for a brief time

Examples

The beauty of the sunset was a fugitive moment, gone in seconds.
She tried to capture the fugitive feelings in her poem before they faded away.
The artist conveyed the fugitive nature of fashion in her quick sketches.

Synset Examples

a fleeting glance
fugitive hours
rapid momentaneous association of things that meet and pass
a momentary glimpse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleeting - lasting for only a very short time
  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin 'fugitivus' (fleeing), from 'fugere' (to flee).
