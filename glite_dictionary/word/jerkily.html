jerkily

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɝːkɪli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkɪli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by sudden, awkward, or abrupt motions; with jerky or uneven movements.
with abrupt, jerky motions

Examples

The car moved jerkily down the uneven road, making everyone inside bounce.
He tried to open the old door, but it only opened jerkily on its rusty hinges.
She waved jerkily at her friend across the street, her movements full of excitement.

Synset Examples

She rose stiffly, jerkily from the window seat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothly - in an even, continuous manner, without jerkiness
  • Synonym: jerky - characterized by abrupt, uneven, or sudden movements

Etymology

From 'jerky' (abrupt, with sudden movements) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɝː.kɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.kɪ.li / (BrE)
in a sudden, uneven, or spasmodic manner; moving with abrupt starts and stops
in a spasmodic or jerky way

Examples

The car moved jerkily down the rough mountain road.
He wrote jerkily with his left hand after the injury.
The old film played jerkily on the projector, skipping several frames.

Synset Examples

the mouth was slightly open, and jerked violently and spasmodically at one corner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothly - in an even, regular or steady way
  • Synonym: spasmodically - with sudden, brief, or intermittent movements

Etymology

From 'jerky' + '-ly', 'jerky' possibly from the verb 'jerk' (to move with a sudden motion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465509-r
Synset POS: r
