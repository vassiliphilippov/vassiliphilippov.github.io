booking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
an engagement or scheduled period of employment for a performer or performing group, usually for a limited time
temporary job for a performer or group

Examples

The band secured several bookings at local venues this month.
Her agent called to say she had a new booking for the weekend.
Due to high demand, their bookings were fully scheduled for the entire summer.

Synset Examples

the play had bookings throughout the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - a formal arrangement to do something or go somewhere at a fixed time
  • Synonym: gig - a live performance, especially by a musician or group

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement made in advance to reserve a seat, room, service, or appointment
reservation or arrangement made in advance

Examples

I made a booking at the restaurant for 7 p.m. tomorrow.
The hotel was full, but they found our booking in the system.
All bookings must be confirmed by email within 24 hours.

Synset Examples

wondered who had made the booking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reservation - an arrangement to have something held for later use

Etymology

From the verb 'book' (to reserve), ultimately from Old English 'bōc' (book).
