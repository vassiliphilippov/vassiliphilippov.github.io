anticlimactic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.klaɪˈmæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.klaɪˈmæk.tɪk / (BrE)
Having an effect that is disappointing or less exciting than expected, especially after a build-up to a climax.
disappointing after a climax

Examples

The novel’s ending felt anticlimactic after such a suspenseful plot.
Their annual meeting was surprisingly anticlimactic, with no major announcements.
Many viewers found the movie's final scene anticlimactic and unsatisfying.

Synset Examples

everything after the discovery of the murderer was anticlimactic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climactic - Coming to or relating to a climax; highly exciting or important
  • Synonym: disappointing - Not meeting expectations; causing disappointment

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'climactic' (relating to a climax).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.klaɪˈmæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.klaɪˈmæk.tɪk / (BrE)
describing something that has a disappointing or less exciting conclusion than expected, often following a build-up.
disappointingly less exciting than expected

Examples

The movie's ending was anticlimactic, leaving the audience feeling let down after all the excitement.
Her speech built up to what felt like an anticlimactic finish.
After hours of tension, the final scene was surprisingly anticlimactic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climactic - relating to the most intense or exciting part of something

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'climactic', from 'climax' (from Greek 'klimax', meaning ladder or peak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02640076-a
Synset POS: a
