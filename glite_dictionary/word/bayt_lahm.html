Bayt Lahm

1. PROPN

/ baɪt ˈlɑm / (AmE), / baɪt ˈlɑːm / (BrE)
A small town near Jerusalem in the West Bank, traditionally regarded as the birthplace of Jesus and the early home of David; also known as Bethlehem.
town near Jerusalem, also Bethlehem

Examples

Many pilgrims visit Bayt Lahm each year to see the Church of the Nativity.
The political situation in Bayt Lahm has affected tourism and local businesses.
According to tradition, King David was born in Bayt Lahm before becoming king of Israel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bethlehem - another name for Bayt Lahm, especially in English and in religious context

Etymology

From Arabic 'Bayt Lahm' meaning 'House of Meat' or 'House of Bread'; known in English as Bethlehem.
