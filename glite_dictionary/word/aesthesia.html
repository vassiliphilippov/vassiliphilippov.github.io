aesthesia

1. NOUN

/ iˈstiːʒə / (AmE), / iˈstiːzɪə / (BrE)
The capacity for sensation or perception; mental responsiveness and awareness.
capacity for sensation or awareness

Examples

The patient's aesthesia was carefully tested by the neurologist.
Art heightens our aesthesia and allows us to experience the world more vividly.
Damage to certain nerves can reduce aesthesia in affected areas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaesthesia - loss of sensation
  • Synonym: sensation - physical feeling or perception

Etymology

From Greek aisthēsis (perception, sensation).
