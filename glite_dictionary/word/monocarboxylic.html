monocarboxylic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmoʊ.nə.kɑrˌbɑkˈsɪl.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nə.kɑː.bɒkˈsɪl.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) containing one carboxyl group (-COOH) in its molecule.
containing one carboxyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Acetic acid is a simple monocarboxylic acid widely used in industry.
Most fatty acids are monocarboxylic, containing a single carboxyl group at one end of the carbon chain.
Formic acid is the smallest monocarboxylic acid found naturally in some insects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dicarboxylic - containing two carboxyl groups

Etymology

From 'mono-' (one) and 'carboxylic', relating to the carboxyl group (-COOH).
