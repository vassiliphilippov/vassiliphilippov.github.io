tympan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpən / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpən / (BrE)
a percussion instrument consisting of a hollow cylinder with a membrane stretched over each end, similar to a drum
percussion instrument; drum-like

Examples

The museum had an ancient tympan on display along with other historic instruments.
He played the tympan skillfully, adding rhythm to the traditional song.
Some early orchestras included the tympan as part of their percussion section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - a percussion instrument with a hollow body and membrane, played by beating

Etymology

From Latin 'tympanum', from Ancient Greek 'τυμπανον' (týmpanon, meaning 'drum').
