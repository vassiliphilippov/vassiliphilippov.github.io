Fratercula arctica

1. NOUN

/ frəˌtɜrkjələ ɑrˈktɪkə / (AmE), / frəˌtɜːkjʊlə ɑːkˈtɪkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Atlantic puffin, a seabird with a colorful beak found in the northern Atlantic Ocean
Atlantic puffin species of seabird

Examples

The Fratercula arctica can be found nesting on coastal cliffs in Iceland during the summer.
Scientists are tracking populations of Fratercula arctica to monitor changes in the marine ecosystem.
Children at the aquarium were fascinated by the vibrant beaks of Fratercula arctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffin - the common name for Fratercula arctica, a seabird of the northern Atlantic

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fratercula' (little brother, a reference to its monk-like appearance) and 'arctica' (northern, arctic).
