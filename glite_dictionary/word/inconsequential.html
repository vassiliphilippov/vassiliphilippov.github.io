inconsequential

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌkɑːnsɪˈkwenʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsɛkwənʃl / (BrE)
Not important or significant; lacking worth or consequence.
not important; insignificant

Examples

The committee dismissed his suggestion as inconsequential to the final decision.
Minor mistakes in the report are inconsequential compared to the overall results.
They argued for hours about the most inconsequential details of the plan.

Synset Examples

his work seems trivial and inconsequential
the quite inconsequent fellow was managed like a puppet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - not important or worthy of attention
  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value
  • Antonym: consequential - following as a result; important or significant

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'consequential' (having consequence).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌkɑnsɪˈkwɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəl / (BrE)
not following logically as a consequence; not important or significant.
not important; lacking logical consequence

Examples

His argument was dismissed as inconsequential and irrelevant to the main issue.
The error was so inconsequential that it did not affect the outcome of the experiment.
They spent hours debating inconsequential details that had no impact on the final decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - not important or noteworthy
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + consequential (following as a result).

Synset POS: s
