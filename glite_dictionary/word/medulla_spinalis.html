medulla spinalis

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʌlə spaɪˈnælɪs / (AmE), / mɛˈdʌl.ə spaɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
the spinal cord; the part of the central nervous system running through the vertebral canal that conducts nerve impulses to and from the brain
the spinal cord; nervous system structure
medical

Examples

Damage to the medulla spinalis can result in loss of movement or sensation below the injury site.
Doctors monitored the patient's medulla spinalis function after the car accident.
Inflammation of the medulla spinalis may cause severe neurological symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal cord - main cord of nervous tissue extending from the brain along the back

Etymology

From Latin: 'medulla' (marrow) and 'spinalis' (of the spine), used in medical terminology.
