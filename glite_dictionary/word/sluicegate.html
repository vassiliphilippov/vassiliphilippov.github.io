sluicegate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsluːsˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsluːsɡeɪt / (BrE)
a device consisting of a valve or gate that regulates the flow of water through a sluice or channel
water flow regulator in a sluice

Examples

The engineer adjusted the sluicegate to allow more water into the irrigation channel.
During the storm, the sluicegate was closed to prevent flooding downstream.
Routine maintenance of the sluicegate is essential for effective water management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - an artificial channel for conducting water, with a gate to regulate flow
  • Synonym: floodgate - a gate that can be opened or closed to admit or exclude water, especially from a river, canal, or reservoir

Etymology

Formed from 'sluice' (from Old French escluse, from Latin exclusa) and 'gate'.
