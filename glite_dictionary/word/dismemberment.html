dismemberment

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɛmbɚmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈmembəmənt / (BrE)
the act of removing the limbs of a person or animal, or dividing something into parts; the state of being cut to pieces
removal of limbs or being cut to pieces

Examples

The forensic report indicated that the victim had suffered dismemberment after death.
Many ancient punishments involved dismemberment as a form of deterrence.
The grisly movie depicted the dismemberment of the monster by the villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutilation - the act of severely injuring or damaging a body part

Etymology

From Old French *desmembrer* (to dismember), from Latin *dismembrāre*, from *dis-* (apart) + *membrum* (limb).
