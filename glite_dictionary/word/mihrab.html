mihrab

1. NOUN

/ miˈrɑb / (AmE), / ˈmiː.hræb / (BrE)
a niche or decorative design in the wall of a mosque or on a Muslim prayer rug, indicating the direction of Mecca
prayer niche in a mosque or on a prayer rug

Examples

The mosque was beautifully decorated, with a golden mihrab at the front pointing towards Mecca.
She placed her prayer rug so that the mihrab faced in the correct direction for prayer.
The intricate patterns of the mihrab were admired by all who visited the mosque.

Etymology

From Arabic miḥrāb (a niche indicating the direction of Mecca).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiː.rɑːb / (AmE), / ˈmiː.rɑːb / (BrE)
A niche in the wall of a mosque that indicates the direction of Mecca and towards which Muslims pray.
niche in mosque wall indicating Mecca

Examples

The intricate design of the mihrab drew the attention of every visitor to the mosque.
During prayer, worshippers face the mihrab to align themselves with Mecca.
This ancient mosque is known for its beautifully decorated mihrab dating back to the 12th century.

Etymology

From Arabic 'miḥrāb', meaning 'prayer niche'.
