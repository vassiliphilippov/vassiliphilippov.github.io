appressed

1. ADJ

/ əˈprɛst / (AmE), / əˈprɛst / (BrE)
(especially of an object or part) pressed closely against or lying flat upon another surface.
pressed closely against something

Examples

The appressed leaves help the plant retain moisture in dry environments.
The cat's ears were appressed to its head in fear.
The bark is covered with appressed scales that are difficult to remove.

Synset Examples

adpressed hairs along the plant's stem
igneous rocks … closely appressed by this force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adpressed - pressed closely against something; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Latin adpressus, past participle of adprimere (‘to press to’), from ad- (‘to’) + premere (‘to press’).
