casino

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsinoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈsiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A card game in which players capture face-up cards on the table using cards from their hand.
a card game using captured cards

Examples

We spent the evening playing casino with friends after dinner.
He learned how to play casino while on vacation in Italy.
Casino quickly became her favorite family card game.

Etymology

From Italian 'casino' (small house), also applied to gaming establishments and later to the card game.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈsinoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈsiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a building or room where people gamble and are often entertained with other amusements
public building for gambling and entertainment

Examples

We spent the evening at the casino, trying our luck at blackjack and roulette.
Las Vegas is famous for its extravagant casinos and vibrant nightlife.
The new casino on the waterfront attracts tourists from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gambling house - a place for legal or illegal gambling

Etymology

From Italian 'casino', diminutive of 'casa' (house).
