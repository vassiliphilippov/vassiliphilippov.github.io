trepidly

1. ADV

/ ˈtrɛpɪdli / (AmE), / ˈtrɛpɪdli / (BrE)
in a timid, fearful, or apprehensive manner; with trepidation
in a fearful or timid manner

Examples

She entered the dark, unfamiliar room trepidly, clutching her flashlight tightly.
He trepidly approached his supervisor to ask for a day off.
The kitten moved trepidly towards the new toy, unsure if it was safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearfully - in a manner showing fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: boldly - in a manner showing courage or confidence; opposite of fearful

Etymology

From 'trepid', ultimately from Latin 'trepidus' (agitated, alarmed), with the suffix '-ly'.
