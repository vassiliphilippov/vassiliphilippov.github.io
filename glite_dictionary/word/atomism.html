atomism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬əmˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæt.əm.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the theory that all matter is composed of tiny, indivisible, and indestructible particles called atoms
theory that matter consists of atoms

Examples

Atomism was first proposed by ancient Greek philosophers to explain the nature of matter.
Modern science has built upon atomism to develop our understanding of chemical reactions.
Some philosophical debates still consider the implications of atomism versus other models of reality.

Synset Examples

the ancient Greek philosophers Democritus and Epicurus held atomic theories of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic theory - scientific theory that matter is made up of atoms

Etymology

From Greek atomos (indivisible) + -ism (system or theory).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬.ə.mɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈæt.ə.mɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Psychology) The theory that all mental phenomena can be reduced to simple elements such as sensations and feelings, which combine by association to form complex ideas.
theory that mental phenomena are built from simple elements

Examples

Many psychologists debated whether atomism could fully account for the complexity of human thought.
The theory of atomism posits that sensations and feelings are the building blocks of the mind.
Some criticized atomism for oversimplifying the richness of mental life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associationism - the theory that mental processes operate by the association of ideas

Etymology

From Greek atomos (indivisible) + -ism.
