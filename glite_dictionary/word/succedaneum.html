succedaneum

1. NOUN

/ səkˌsiːdəˈniːəm / (AmE), / sʌkˌsiːdəˈniːəm / (BrE)
a substitute, especially a medicine used in place of another
substitute medicine or remedy
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a succedaneum when the original medication was out of stock.
Researchers sought a suitable succedaneum for the discontinued drug.
Traditional healers often use a succedaneum when the usual remedy is unavailable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or used in place of another

Etymology

From Latin succedāneum, meaning 'substitute'.
