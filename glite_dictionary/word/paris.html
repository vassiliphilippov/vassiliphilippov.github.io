Paris

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈpær.ɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the prince of Troy whose abduction of Helen led to the Trojan War.
prince of Troy in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the legend, Paris was responsible for starting the Trojan War.
The judgment of Paris is a famous episode in Greek mythology.
Many artists have depicted Paris and Helen in their works.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈpærɪs / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of France, known as an international center of culture, art, fashion, and commerce
capital city of France

Examples

They spent a week in Paris exploring world-famous art museums and monuments.
The cuisine in Paris is renowned for its quality and innovation.
Fashion designers from all over the world travel to Paris for Fashion Week.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lutetia Parisiorum', later shortened to 'Paris' after the Parisii tribe who settled the area.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpærɪs / (AmE), / ˈparɪs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the family Melanthiaceae, sometimes placed in the subfamily Trilliaceae, native to Europe and Asia.
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The rare species in the Paris genus are protected in several countries.
Botanists found a new population of Paris during their fieldwork in China.
Most species in the Paris genus prefer shady woodland habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'Paris', the classical name for the plant.
