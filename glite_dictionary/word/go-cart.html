go-cart

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.kɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.kɑːt / (BrE)
a small wheeled vehicle that can be pushed by a person, typically used for carrying loads or children.
pushed wheeled cart or carriage

Examples

The children took turns pushing the go-cart around the garden.
He loaded the old books onto a go-cart for the trip to the library.
Vendors use a go-cart to display their goods at the market.

Synset Examples

he used a handcart to carry the rocks away
their pushcart was piled high with groceries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handcart - a cart pushed or pulled by hand for carrying goods
  • Synonym: pushcart - a small cart that is pushed by hand, often by vendors

Etymology

From 'go' + 'cart', referring to a cart that 'goes' when pushed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ kɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ kɑːt / (BrE)
a small framework mounted on wheels or casters, used to support and help babies learn to walk
framework on wheels for babies learning to walk

Examples

The baby pushed the go-cart across the living room, giggling with excitement.
She learned to walk quickly thanks to a sturdy go-cart her parents bought.
Modern parents often choose a go-cart with safety features for their children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walker - a frame used by babies or elderly people to assist with walking

Etymology

From 'go' (move) + 'cart' (vehicle on wheels).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04552591-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ kɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ kɑːt / (BrE)
a small four-wheeled vehicle in which a baby or child is pushed around
four-wheeled child's pushcart or stroller

Examples

She carefully buckled her infant into the go-cart before heading out for a walk.
The old-fashioned go-cart had large, spoked wheels and a cloth canopy.
He pushed his baby sister in a go-cart through the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroller - a similar four-wheeled vehicle for babies or young children
