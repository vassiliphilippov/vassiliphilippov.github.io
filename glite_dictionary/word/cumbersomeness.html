cumbersomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmbərsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbəsəmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to carry, use, or manage due to large size, weight, or awkward shape
quality of being hard to manage due to bulk

Examples

The cumbersomeness of the old furniture made it difficult to move up the narrow stairs.
Despite its cumbersomeness, the package contained only a few small items.
The report criticized the cumbersomeness of the bureaucracy involved in the approval process.

Synset Examples

the movers cursed the unwieldiness of the big piano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwieldiness - the quality of being difficult to handle or manage

Etymology

From English 'cumbersome' + '-ness'; 'cumbersome' from Middle English 'combersome', influenced by 'encumber'.
