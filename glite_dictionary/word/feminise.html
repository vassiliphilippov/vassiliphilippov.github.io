to feminise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛmɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnʌɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to take on more feminine qualities or characteristics
make more feminine in character
british

Examples

Some chemicals in the environment can feminise male fish, affecting their ability to reproduce.
The company aims to feminise its branding to appeal to a broader demographic.
In literature, the author sought to feminise traditional narratives by focusing on female perspectives.

Synset Examples

feminized language
feminized frogs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to masculinise - to make something more masculine

Etymology

From Latin 'femina' (woman) + -ize, via French 'féminiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00568176-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛm.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to give a feminine quality or appearance to; to make or become more characteristic of women or girls
make or become more feminine
british

Examples

The new uniform policy aims to feminise the company's dress code.
Some critics argue that modern design trends tend to feminise traditional architecture.
The stylist used soft colors and accessories to feminise the overall look of the room.

Synset Examples

This hairdo feminizes the man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to masculinise - to give a masculine quality or appearance to; to make more characteristic of men or boys

Etymology

From Latin 'femina' (woman) + English suffix '-ise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00567399-v
Synset POS: v
