drawn-out

1. ADJ

/ drɔn aʊt / (AmE), / drɔːn aʊt / (BrE)
(of speech) spoken slowly with vowels lengthened more than usual; prolonged in time or sound.
spoken or sounded slowly, with prolonged vowels

Examples

Her drawn-out explanation made the meeting feel even longer.
He replied to every question in a slow, drawn-out manner, stretching every vowel.
The teacher's drawn-out speech made it hard for students to stay focused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolonged - lasting for a longer time than usual
  • Antonym: brief - lasting only a short time

2. ADJ

/ drɔn aʊt / (AmE), / drɔːn aʊt / (BrE)
lasting longer than necessary or expected, often in a tedious or tiresome way.
tediously long in duration

Examples

The meeting turned into a drawn-out debate that lasted well into the evening.
She found the process of refunding her ticket to be unnecessarily drawn-out.
Their drawn-out farewell made everyone in the room feel uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

a drawn-out argument
an extended discussion
a lengthy visit from her mother-in-law
a prolonged and bitter struggle
protracted negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolonged - continuing for a long time; extended duration
  • Synonym: lengthy - unusually long, especially so as to be tiresome
  • Antonym: brief - lasting only a short time; concise
