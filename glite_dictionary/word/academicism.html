academicism

1. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A style of painting and sculpture characterized by formal technique and traditional standards, especially as influenced by European art academies.
formal artistic style influenced by art academies

Examples

His early work shows a strong influence of academicism, evident in the precise brushwork and balanced compositions.
The artist rejected academicism in favor of more experimental techniques and subjects.
Many critics accused the school's teachers of promoting outdated academicism rather than encouraging creativity.

Synset Examples

The setting and treatment of the subject here are typical of his artistic approach, which lies between the idealized academicism of Bouguereau and the social realism of Daumier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - someone who follows classical art traditions, often linked to academic styles
  • Antonym: avant-garde - innovative or experimental art, contrasted with academic styles

Etymology

From French ‘académisme’, from ‘académie’ meaning ‘academy’, referring to official art institutions.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkædəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
An emphasis on formal academic rules, theories, or conventions, often at the expense of practical application or creativity.
strict adherence to academic conventions

Examples

The author's work was criticized for its excessive academicism and lack of real-world relevance.
She tried to avoid the trap of academicism by focusing on practical applications in her research.
Some scholars believe that academicism limits creativity and innovation in academic disciplines.

Synset Examples

This original book not only initiates such an engagement, it avoids the academicism of much political theory by demonstrating how concepts of justice actually shape public policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academism - adherence to academic conventions or style
  • Antonym: pragmatism - a practical approach to problems and affairs

Etymology

From French 'académisme'; derived from 'academy' plus the suffix '-ism'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈkæd.əˌsɪ.zəm / (AmE), / əˈkæd.əm.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
strict adherence to formal academic rules or scholarly orthodoxy, often at the expense of originality or innovation
scholarly orthodoxy; academic rule-following

Examples

The university's rigid academicism discouraged students from exploring novel theories.
Many artists rebelled against the academicism of traditional art schools in the nineteenth century.
Critics argue that too much academicism can stifle creativity and independent thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodoxy - accepted or traditional beliefs, especially in religion or doctrine
  • Antonym: innovation - the act of introducing new ideas or methods; novelty

Etymology

From French 'académisme', based on 'académie' (academy), influenced by Latin 'academia'.
