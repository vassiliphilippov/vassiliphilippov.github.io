ineluctable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɪlək.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛl.ʌk.tə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to avoid or escape; inescapable.
impossible to avoid or escape

Examples

The country's economic decline seemed ineluctable despite the government's efforts.
She faced her ineluctable fate with quiet dignity.
The book explores whether death is truly an ineluctable end.

Synset Examples

inescapable conclusion
an ineluctable destiny
an unavoidable accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inescapable - impossible to escape from
  • Synonym: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable

Etymology

From Latin 'ineluctabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'eluctari' (to struggle out, escape).
