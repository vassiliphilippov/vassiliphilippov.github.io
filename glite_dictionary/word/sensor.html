sensor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsensə / (BrE)
a device that detects and responds to a physical stimulus such as heat, pressure, light, or motion.
device that detects stimuli

Examples

The security system uses a motion sensor to detect intruders at night.
Modern cars are equipped with rain sensors that automatically activate the windshield wipers.
Her research focuses on developing new sensors for measuring air quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detector - a device that discovers the presence of something

Etymology

From Latin 'sentire' (to perceive, feel), via 'sensus'.
