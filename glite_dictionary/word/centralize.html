to centralize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlaɪz / (BrE)
bring (activities, power, or organization) under a single central authority or location.
bring under central control

Examples

The government decided to centralize health services to improve efficiency.
Efforts to centralize data collection have met with both support and resistance.
After the merger, the company centralized all administrative functions in New York.

Synset Examples

The Russian government centralized the distribution of food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decentralize - distribute power or functions away from a central authority

Etymology

From French *centraliser*, from 'central' + '-ize'.
