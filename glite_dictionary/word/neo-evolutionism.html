neo-evolutionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊ ɪˈvɑːluˌʃɪnˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ ɪˈvɒl.juːˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
a school of anthropology that studies long-term cultural change and the similar patterns of development found in different, unrelated cultures
anthropological school studying cultural evolution patterns

Examples

Neo-evolutionism examines how similar cultural developments may emerge independently in societies across the globe.
Many anthropologists in the mid-20th century adopted neo-evolutionism to explain parallel cultural advancements.
A major criticism of neo-evolutionism is its reliance on broad generalizations about human societies.

Synset Examples

Neo-evolutionism was the first in a series of modern multi-lineal evolution theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultural evolution - the development of cultures over time through accumulation and transmission of knowledge and customs
  • Antonym: unilinear evolution - anthropological theory proposing that cultures progress through the same sequence of stages

Etymology

From neo- (new) + evolutionism (theory of evolution, especially in culture or society).
