illustrative

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstrətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstrətɪv / (BrE)
serving to explain or clarify something by providing examples or illustrations.
serving to explain by example

Examples

The professor gave an illustrative example to help clarify the concept.
These charts are particularly illustrative of the current economic trends.
The book includes many illustrative diagrams to aid understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanatory - serving to explain something; making clear
  • Synonym: demonstrative - serving as proof or illustration

Etymology

From Latin 'illustrativus', from 'illustrare' (to make light, to illustrate).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstrətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstrətɪv / (BrE)
Serving to explain or make something clear by providing examples, pictures, or demonstrations.
serving to explain by example or demonstration

Examples

The professor used an illustrative diagram to explain the complex process.
Her speech was full of illustrative anecdotes that helped make her points clear.
This document is intended to be illustrative rather than comprehensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanatory - serving to explain something
  • Synonym: demonstrative - serving to demonstrate or show clearly

Etymology

From Medieval Latin illustrativus, from Latin illustratus (illuminated, made clear).
