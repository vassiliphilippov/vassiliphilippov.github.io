Lagostomus maximus

1. NOUN

/ ləˌɡɑːstəʊməs ˈmæksɪməs / (AmE), / ləˌɡɒstəʊməs ˈmæksɪməs / (BrE)
A large, social, burrowing rodent native to South America, commonly known as the plains viscacha, larger than chinchillas.
a large South American burrowing rodent (plains viscacha)

Examples

Fossils of Lagostomus maximus have helped scientists study the ancient grasslands of South America.
The burrows made by Lagostomus maximus often serve as shelter for other species.
Unlike chinchillas, Lagostomus maximus lives in large, noisy colonies on the open plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscacha - a similar South American burrowing rodent
  • Degree Lower: chinchilla - a smaller South American rodent related to Lagostomus maximus

Etymology

From Latin: genus Lagostomus and species maximus (meaning 'largest').
