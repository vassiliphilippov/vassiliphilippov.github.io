animated

1. ADJ

/ ˈænɪˌmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈænɪˌmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
full of life, energy, or excitement; lively and spirited
full of life or energy

Examples

The children became animated when their favorite song started playing.
She spoke with an animated expression, captivating her audience.
Their animated discussion filled the room with excitement.

Synset Examples

an animated and expressive face
animated conversation
became very animated when he heard the good news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: spirited - full of energy, enthusiasm, or determination
  • Antonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin animatus (endowed with life), from animare (to give life to), from anima (life, spirit).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæn.əˌmeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæn.ɪ.meɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of pictures, images, or objects) made to appear to move as if alive, especially using film or computer graphics.
made to seem to move like living things

Examples

The children watched an animated film about forest animals at the theater.
Modern video games use animated characters to create immersive worlds.
The museum displayed several animated models that demonstrated how dinosaurs might have moved.

Synset Examples

an animated cartoon
animated puppets

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live-action - featuring real actors and physical sets, not animation
  • Synonym: cartoon - a movie or television show consisting of animated sequences

Etymology

From Latin *animatus* (filled with life, spirit), past participle of *animare* (to animate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01570001-s
Synset POS: s
