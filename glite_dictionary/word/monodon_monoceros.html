Monodon monoceros

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑnəˌdɑn məˈnɑsəˌrɑs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnədɒn məˈnɒsərɒs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the narwhal, an Arctic whale whose males have a long, spiral ivory tusk
the scientific name for the narwhal

Examples

The Arctic researcher focused her study on the behavior of Monodon monoceros during the winter months.
Scientists observed a pod of Monodon monoceros migrating through the icy waters of the northwest passage.
The unique tusk of Monodon monoceros has intrigued marine biologists for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narwhal - medium-sized Arctic whale with a long tusk

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'odōn' (tooth), referring to the single tusk.
