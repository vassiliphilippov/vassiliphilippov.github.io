skirmish

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɝː.mɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɜː.mɪʃ / (BrE)
a brief and unplanned fight, especially between small groups or as part of a larger conflict
short, minor fight or clash

Examples

A skirmish broke out between protesters and police near the city hall.
The two rival gangs had a brief skirmish before the police arrived.
There were several skirmishes along the border before the ceasefire was agreed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: battle - a large-scale or prolonged fight, especially between organized armed forces
  • Degree Higher: engagement - a battle or fight between armed forces
  • Synonym: clash - a brief encounter or fight
  • Degree Higher: war - a prolonged armed conflict

Etymology

From Old French *eskirmir* (to fence, fight), from a Germanic root.

2. VERB

/ ˈskɝː.mɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɜː.mɪʃ / (BrE)
to take part in a brief or minor fight or conflict, especially as an outpost or preliminary action before a larger battle
take part in a brief or minor fight

Examples

The two armies skirmished briefly at dawn before the main battle began.
Wildlife photographers often skirmish with each other over the best locations.
The teams skirmished over tactics before settling into serious negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to engage in a physical or armed struggle

Etymology

From Old French escarmouche, from Italian scaramuccia.
