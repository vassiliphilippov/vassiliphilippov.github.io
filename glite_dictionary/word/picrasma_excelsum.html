Picrasma excelsum

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkræzmə ɪkˈsɛlsəm / (AmE), / pɪˈkræzmə ɪkˈsɛlsəm / (BrE)
A West Indian tree whose wood is the source of Jamaica quassia, used medicinally as a bitter tonic.
West Indian tree; source of Jamaica quassia

Examples

The bark of Picrasma excelsum is traditionally used to make Jamaica quassia extract.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of Picrasma excelsum in herbal medicine.
The wood from Picrasma excelsum is known for its intensely bitter taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quassia - A bitter medicinal substance from certain tropical trees

Etymology

From genus name 'Picrasma' (Greek 'pikros' meaning 'bitter') and Latin 'excelsum' meaning 'lofty, tall'.
