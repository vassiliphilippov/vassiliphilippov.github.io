polyhedral

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
having the form or characteristics of a polyhedron; relating to or resembling a solid figure with many flat faces.
relating to a polyhedron

Examples

The crystal's polyhedral shape fascinated the scientists.
Certain viruses have polyhedral structures under a microscope.
She constructed a polyhedral model using colored paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyhedron - a solid figure with many flat faces

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'hedron' (face, base).
