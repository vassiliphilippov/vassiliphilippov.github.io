unpermissiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.pərˈmɪs.ɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.pəˈmɪs.ɪv.nəs / (BrE)
a lack of permissiveness or indulgence and a tendency to restrict behavior within set limits
lack of permissiveness or indulgence

Examples

The unpermissiveness of his parenting style led to strict household rules.
Some critics argue that unpermissiveness in schools stifles creativity and independence.
Her upbringing was marked by a general unpermissiveness toward social media use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissiveness - the quality of being permissive or tolerant

Etymology

From un- (not) + permissive (tolerant, lenient) + -ness (noun-forming suffix).
