homelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌlɛsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmlɪsnəs / (BrE)
The condition or state of having no home, especially of living on the streets or without permanent shelter.
the state of having no home

Examples

The city launched several programs to address homelessness among veterans.
Chronic homelessness often results from a combination of economic and personal factors.
Rising rents are increasing the risk of homelessness for many families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: housing - houses and apartments considered collectively

Etymology

From 'homeless' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being homeless.
