Faroese

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛəroʊˈiz / (AmE), / ˌfeərəʊˈiːz / (BrE)
a North Germanic language, closely related to Icelandic, spoken in the Faroe Islands.
North Germanic language of the Faroe Islands

Examples

She has been studying Faroese to better understand the culture of the Faroe Islands.
The novel was translated from Faroese into several European languages.
Only a small population worldwide speaks Faroese fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Icelandic - North Germanic language spoken in Iceland
  • Synonym: Danish - North Germanic language spoken in Denmark

Etymology

From the Faroe Islands + -ese (denoting languages or inhabitants).
