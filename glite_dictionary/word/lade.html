to lade

1. VERB

/ leɪd / (AmE), / leɪd / (BrE)
to remove (a liquid or substance) with or as if with a ladle
remove with or as if with a ladle

Examples

She used a small cup to lade water from the bucket.
They laded soup out of the large pot for the guests.
The worker began to lade the excess oil from the surface.

Synset Examples

ladle the water out of the bowl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladle - to serve or remove with a ladle

Etymology

Old English *hladan* (to load, draw water)

2. VERB

/ leɪd / (AmE), / leɪd / (BrE)
to fill or load, especially with cargo or a burden
fill or load with cargo
archaic

Examples

They laded the boat with fresh produce early in the morning.
The workers lade the trucks with bags of grain every harvest season.
He lading the wagon so high that it threatened to tip over.

Synset Examples

load a car
load the truck with hay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: load - to put a load on or in something

Etymology

Old English 'hladan' (to draw out water, load) related to Old Norse 'hlaða', German 'laden'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01492646-v
Synset POS: v
