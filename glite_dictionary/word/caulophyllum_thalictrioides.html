Caulophyllum thalictrioides

1. NOUN

/ kɔl.əˈfɪl.əm ˌθæl.ɪkˌtraɪ.oʊˈɪd.iːz / (AmE), / kɔː.lɒˈfɪl.əm ˌθæl.ɪkˌtrɪ.ɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A tall herb native to eastern North America and Asia, with blue, berrylike fruit and a thick knotty rootstock, formerly used medicinally.
a herb with blue berries, used medicinally

Examples

Native Americans used Caulophyllum thalictrioides for various medicinal purposes.
The blue berries produced by Caulophyllum thalictrioides are a distinguishing feature of the plant.
In early spring, Caulophyllum thalictrioides can be found growing in shaded woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue cohosh - common name for Caulophyllum thalictrioides

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Caulophyllum + specific epithet thalictrioides (meaning 'Thalictrum-like').
