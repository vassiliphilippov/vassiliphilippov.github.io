pervasive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
(especially of something unwelcome) spreading widely throughout an area or a group of people.
spreading widely throughout

Examples

There is a pervasive sense of fear throughout the city after the incident.
The influence of technology has become pervasive in modern society.
She noticed a pervasive smell of fresh paint as she entered the house.

Synset Examples

armed with permeative irony … he punctures affectations
the pervasive odor of garlic
an error is pervasive if it is material to more than one conclusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widespread - existing or happening over a large area or among many people

Etymology

From Latin 'pervasus', past participle of 'pervadere' (to spread through).
