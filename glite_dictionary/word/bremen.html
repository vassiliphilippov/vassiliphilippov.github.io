Bremen

1. PROPN

/ ˈbreɪmən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪmən / (BrE)
A city in northwestern Germany on the Weser River, historically a leading member of the Hanseatic League.
city in northwestern Germany

Examples

The port facilities in Bremen are among the most modern in Europe.
During the Middle Ages, Bremen was a key trading center.
She traveled from Berlin to Bremen by train last summer.

Etymology

From German Bremen, the name of the city, possibly of Slavic origin.
