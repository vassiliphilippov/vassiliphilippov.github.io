Anthozoa

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθoʊzoʊə / (AmE), / ænˈθəʊzəʊə / (BrE)
a large class of sedentary marine animals, including sea anemones and corals, with no medusoid phase in their life cycle
class of marine animals: sea anemones and corals

Examples

Scientists have discovered new species within the Anthozoa class near tropical reefs.
Most corals found in shallow waters belong to the Anthozoa group of marine animals.
The life cycle of Anthozoa does not include a medusoid stage, unlike many other cnidarians.

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) + zōion (animal), referring to the flower-like appearance of many species.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθoʊzoʊə / (AmE), / ænˈθəʊzəʊə / (BrE)
a large class of sedentary marine animals, including sea anemones and corals, in which the medusoid phase is absent
class of marine coelenterates (corals, anemones)

Examples

The reef is composed of various Anthozoa, including many species of coral.
Scientists study Anthozoa to learn more about marine biodiversity and ecosystem health.
Unlike jellyfish, Anthozoa lack a medusa stage in their life cycle.

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) + zōion (animal), referring to their flower-like appearance.
