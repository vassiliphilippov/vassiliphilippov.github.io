Tetraneuris

1. NOUN

/ tɛtrəˈnjʊrɪs / (AmE), / tɛtrəˈnjʊərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of hairy yellow-flowered plants native to the western United States, belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).
genus of yellow-flowered plants (Asteraceae)

Examples

Several species of Tetraneuris can be found in arid regions of the western United States.
The delicate yellow flowers of Tetraneuris bloom throughout the spring and summer.
Botanists recently discovered a new Tetraneuris population thriving at high altitudes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - any plant of the family Asteraceae, with a composite flower head

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning 'four' and 'neuron' meaning 'nerve,' referring to a characteristic of the flower structure.
