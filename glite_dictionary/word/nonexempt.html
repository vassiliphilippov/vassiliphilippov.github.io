nonexempt

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnɪɡˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪɡˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) not exempt from an obligation, rule, or liability.
not exempt; subject to rules or obligations

Examples

All nonexempt employees are eligible for overtime pay under the new policy.
As a nonexempt taxpayer, she must file her tax returns every year.
The nonexempt workers voiced concerns about their working hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exempt - free from an obligation or liability imposed on others

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'exempt' (from Latin eximere, to take out).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
(of goods, funds, or persons) not exempt; subject to taxation, duties, or legal requirements.
not exempt; subject to tax or legal rules

Examples

The court determined that the nonexempt assets must be sold to pay creditors.
Employees classified as nonexempt are entitled to overtime pay under the law.
Only nonexempt income will be subject to additional tax this year.

Synset Examples

taxable income
nonexempt property

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exempt - not subject to taxation or legal requirements

Etymology

From non- (not) + exempt (from Latin exemptus, past participle of eximere ‘take out’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02408942-a
Synset POS: a
