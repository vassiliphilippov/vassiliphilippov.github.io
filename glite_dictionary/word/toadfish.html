toadfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A bottom-dwelling fish with scaleless, slimy skin and a broad, thick head with a wide mouth, found in shallow coastal waters.
bottom-dwelling fish with scaleless, slimy skin

Examples

The toadfish hides among rocks on the ocean floor, waiting for unsuspecting prey.
Fishermen often avoid catching toadfish because of their unattractive, slimy appearance.
We saw a large toadfish resting motionless in the shallow bay.

Etymology

Named for its toad-like appearance, from 'toad' (the amphibian) + 'fish'.
