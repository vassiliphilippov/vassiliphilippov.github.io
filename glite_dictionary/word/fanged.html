fanged

1. ADJ

/ fæŋd / (AmE), / fæŋd / (BrE)
having fangs; possessing long, sharp teeth used for biting or piercing.
having fangs

Examples

The fanged snake hissed and bared its teeth at the approaching predator.
Legends often describe fanged monsters lurking in the darkness.
The vampire's fanged smile sent chills down her spine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fangless - lacking fangs

Etymology

From 'fang' (long, pointed tooth) + '-ed' (adjectival suffix).
