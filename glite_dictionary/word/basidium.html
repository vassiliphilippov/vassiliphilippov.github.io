basidium

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / bəˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a microscopic, club-shaped structure in certain fungi which typically produces four spores (basidiospores) at its tip
fungal spore-producing structure
medical

Examples

The basidium plays a crucial role in the reproductive cycle of mushrooms.
Under the microscope, each basidium could be seen bearing four tiny spores.
Scientists study the basidium to better understand fungal classification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascus - spore-producing structure in ascomycete fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek basidion (small pedestal), diminutive of basis (base).
