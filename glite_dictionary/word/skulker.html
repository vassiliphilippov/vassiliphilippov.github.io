skulker

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlkər / (AmE), / ˈskʌlkə / (BrE)
a person who avoids work or duty, especially by pretending to be ill or incapable
person who shirks duty

Examples

The manager warned that any skulker pretending to be sick would face consequences.
As a notorious skulker, he often avoided assignments by feigning headaches.
No team wants a skulker slowing down progress and morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirker - someone who avoids work or duty
  • Antonym: dutiful - showing a sense of duty or responsibility

Etymology

From 'skulk' (to move stealthily or avoid work), possibly from Scandinavian origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlkər / (AmE), / ˈskʌlkə / (BrE)
a person who hides or moves about stealthily, often to avoid notice or for secretive purposes
someone who hides or moves stealthily

Examples

The skulker watched from the shadows, waiting for the right moment to act.
Security guards are trained to spot a skulker loitering near entrances.
In the woods, a skilled skulker could remain hidden for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skulk - to move about stealthily or to hide, often with a bad motive

Etymology

From the verb 'skulk,' which traces to Middle English, likely from Old Norse 'skulka' (to shirk, lurk).
