to lionize

1. VERB

/ ˈlaɪəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone as a celebrity; to give someone a great deal of public attention or praise
treat as a celebrity; praise publicly

Examples

The city council chose to lionize the local athlete after his remarkable achievements.
Many fans lionized the novelist, flocking to her book signings and public appearances.
Critics warned against lionizing business leaders without considering the impact of their decisions.

Synset Examples

The film director was celebrated all over Hollywood
The tenor was lionized in Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to publicly acknowledge someone with praise or honor
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something

Etymology

From lion, after the practice in 19th-century London of making a great fuss over notable visitors (the 'lions' of society).
