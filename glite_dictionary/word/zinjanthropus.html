Zinjanthropus

1. NOUN

/ zɪnˈjænθrəpəs / (AmE), / zɪnˈjanθrəpəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early hominins to which Australopithecus boisei was formerly assigned
extinct hominin genus (formerly included A. boisei)

Examples

The fossil known as Zinjanthropus was discovered by Mary Leakey at Olduvai Gorge in 1959.
For decades, researchers debated whether Zinjanthropus deserved separate genus status from Australopithecus.
Textbooks now refer to Zinjanthropus as Australopithecus boisei following later revisions in classification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus boisei - an extinct species of early hominin formerly classified under Zinjanthropus

Etymology

From Arabic 'Zanj' (East African coast) + Greek 'anthropos' (man).

2. NOUN

/ zɪnˈjænθrəpəs / (AmE), / zɪnˈjanθrəpəs / (BrE)
A former genus of extinct hominids to which Australopithecus boisei was once assigned.
extinct hominid genus (obsolete)

Examples

The fossil known as Zinjanthropus was discovered in Olduvai Gorge in 1959.
Scientists later reclassified Zinjanthropus as Australopithecus boisei after further study.
The name Zinjanthropus is no longer widely used in modern paleoanthropology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus boisei - an extinct species of hominin; once classified within Zinjanthropus

Etymology

From Arabic Zanj (East African coast) + Greek anthrōpos (human).
