Anguilla sucklandii

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡwɪl.ə sʌkˈlænd.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ænˈɡwɪl.ɪə sʌkˈlænd.i.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of eel native to New Zealand, commonly known as the New Zealand longfin eel.
species of New Zealand eel

Examples

The Anguilla sucklandii plays an important role in New Zealand's river ecosystems.
Scientists are conducting a study on the migration patterns of Anguilla sucklandii.
Traditional Māori communities value Anguilla sucklandii as a significant food source.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'anguilla' (eel); species name honors William Suckling, a New Zealand naturalist.
