enchantment

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtʃæntmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntmənt / (BrE)
A magical spell or charm placed on someone or something.
a magical spell or charm

Examples

The fairy cast an enchantment over the entire forest.
When the enchantment was broken, the prince returned to his true form.
Legends tell of a powerful enchantment hidden in the ancient castle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spell - a spoken or written word or formula that is believed to have magic power

Etymology

From Old French enchantement, from enchanter (to enchant), from Latin incantare (to chant a magic spell).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of great delight or pleasure caused by something wonderful and unusual
great delight caused by something wonderful

Examples

The children listened with enchantment to the magician's incredible stories.
There was a sense of enchantment in the air as the lanterns lit up the evening.
Her voice held everyone in a state of enchantment throughout the entire performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or happiness
  • Antonym: disenchantment - loss of enchantment or disappointment

Etymology

From Old French enchantement, from enchanter (to enchant), from Latin incantare (to chant upon, bewitch).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of great pleasure or delight, as if under a magical spell.
a magical or delightful state

Examples

The children listened in enchantment as the storyteller spun her magical tale.
She gazed at the painting in a state of enchantment that made her forget the world around her.
The forest at dawn filled him with a sense of enchantment he could not describe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewitchment - the act of being enchanted as if by magic
  • Synonym: spell - a state caused by magical words or influence
  • Antonym: disenchantment - the feeling of disappointment when something is no longer enchanting

Etymology

From Old French 'enchanteement', from Latin 'incantamentum' (spell, charm).

Source: WordNet
