bothered

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑðərd / (AmE), / ˈbɒðəd / (BrE)
feeling or showing discomfort, worry, or slight annoyance, often as a reaction to a situation or comment
feeling discomfort or annoyance

Examples

He always seems bothered when someone questions his decisions.
Maria appeared bothered by the loud noises coming from the street.
Despite the harsh criticism, he didn't look bothered at all.

Synset Examples

refused to be fazed by the objections

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbothered - not feeling or showing discomfort or annoyance
  • Synonym: annoyed - feeling slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

Past participle of 'bother', likely from English dialect 'pother' (commotion, fuss).
