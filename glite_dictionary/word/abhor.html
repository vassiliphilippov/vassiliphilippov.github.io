to abhor

1. VERB

/ əbˈhɔr / (AmE), / əbˈhɔː / (BrE)
to regard with deep hatred or disgust; to detest intensely
to hate or detest intensely

Examples

He claimed to abhor all forms of violence.
Many people abhor cruelty to animals.
She abhorred the corruption she witnessed in government.

Synset Examples

I loathe that man
She abhors cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for
  • Synonym: to detest - to dislike intensely

Etymology

From Latin 'abhorrēre', meaning 'to shrink back from; to shudder at'.
