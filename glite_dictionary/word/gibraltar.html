Gibraltar

1. PROPN

/ dʒɪˈbrɔltər / (AmE), / dʒɪˈbrɒltə / (BrE)
A British Overseas Territory located on a limestone promontory at the southern tip of Spain, known for its strategic position at the entrance to the Mediterranean Sea.
British territory at the southern tip of Spain

Examples

The British navy has long maintained a presence in Gibraltar for its strategic significance.
Tourists flock to Gibraltar to see the famous Rock and its Barbary macaques.
Many ships pass through the waters near Gibraltar on their way into the Mediterranean.

Etymology

From Arabic *Jabal Ṭāriq* (mountain of Tariq), named after the Umayyad general Tariq ibn Ziyad.
