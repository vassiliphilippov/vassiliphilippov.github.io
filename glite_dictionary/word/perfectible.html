perfectible

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfɛktəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪbəl / (BrE)
capable of becoming or being made perfect; able to be improved to a state of perfection.
capable of being made perfect

Examples

The philosopher argued that human beings are perfectible through education and moral development.
Some believe that society is perfectible, while others think flaws are inevitable.
The software is still perfectible and will improve with future updates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; containing faults or defects
  • Degree Higher: perfect - having all the required or desirable elements, qualities, or characteristics; as good as it is possible to be

Etymology

From Late Latin perfectibilis, from Latin perfectus (perfect) + -ibilis (-ible, able to be).
