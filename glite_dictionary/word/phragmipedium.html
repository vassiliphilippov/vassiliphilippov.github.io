Phragmipedium

1. NOUN

/ fræɡˌmɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / fræɡˌmɪˈpiːdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids known for their distinctive slipper-shaped flowers, often referred to as lady slippers.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The botanical garden added a rare Phragmipedium to its orchid collection last spring.
Collectors prize the unusual beauty and shape of Phragmipedium flowers.
Researchers have studied Phragmipedium species to understand their unique pollination mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paphiopedilum - genus of lady slipper orchids from Asia
  • Synonym: Cypripedium - genus of North American lady slipper orchids

Etymology

From Greek 'phragma' (fence, barrier) and 'pedilon' (slipper, sandal), referring to the flower's structure.

2. NOUN

/ fræɡˌmɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / fræɡˌmɪˈpiːdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids, known for their slipper-shaped flowers, sometimes called lady slippers.
genus of tropical American orchids (lady slippers)

Examples

Many orchid enthusiasts cultivate Phragmipedium for their uniquely shaped flowers.
The discovery of a new Phragmipedium species sparked excitement among botanists.
Several rare Phragmipedium orchids are protected due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypripedium - a related genus of lady slipper orchids
  • Synonym: Paphiopedilum - another related genus of lady slipper orchids

Etymology

From Greek *phragmos* (fence, enclosure) + Neo-Latin *Phragmipedium*.
