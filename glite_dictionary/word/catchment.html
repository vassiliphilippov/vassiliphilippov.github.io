catchment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈkatʃmənt / (BrE)
An area from which water is collected by the natural landscape into a river, basin, reservoir, or other structure; also, a structure for collecting water.
area or structure collecting water

Examples

The city built a large catchment to store water during the rainy season.
Heavy rainfall increased the flow in the river's catchment area.
Farmers depend on the catchment to supply water for irrigation throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watershed - An area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers, basins, or seas

Etymology

From 'catch', meaning to capture, and '-ment', a noun-forming suffix.
