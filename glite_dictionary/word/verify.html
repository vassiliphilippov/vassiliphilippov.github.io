to verify

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to attach or append a legal statement confirming the truth of a pleading or petition
formally confirm a legal document
legal

Examples

The plaintiff must verify the petition before it is submitted to court.
Attorneys are required to verify certain documents in legal proceedings.
She had to verify her affidavit to confirm its accuracy under oath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to authenticate - to prove that something is genuine

Etymology

From Latin verificare, from verus (true) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01014404-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvɛrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to confirm that something is true, accurate, or correct
confirm truth or accuracy

Examples

The accountant must verify all the financial documents before submission.
Please verify your email address to complete the registration process.
The detective tried to verify the alibi provided by the suspect.

Synset Examples

Please verify that the doors are closed
verify a claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to validate - to check or prove the validity of something
  • Synonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something
  • Antonym: to falsify - to make something false or to prove to be false

Etymology

From Latin verificare, from verus (true) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00666401-v
Synset POS: v
