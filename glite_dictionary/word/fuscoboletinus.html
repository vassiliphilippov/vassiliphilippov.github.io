Fuscoboletinus

1. NOUN

/ fus.koʊ.bəˈliː.tɪ.nəs / (AmE), / fʌs.kəʊ.bəˈliː.tɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, distinguished by a dark-colored spore print ranging from purple to chocolate-gray.
genus of fungi in Boletaceae

Examples

The Fuscoboletinus species are often found in association with coniferous trees.
Mycologists identified the distinctive spore print as a clue to the Fuscoboletinus genus.
A new Fuscoboletinus was discovered in the boreal forests last summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'fuscus' (dark) + 'Boletinus', referencing the dark spore print of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfʌskoʊboʊˈlɛtɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌfʌskəʊbəʊˈlɛtɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, characterized by spore prints that are purple, porphyry-red, dark vinaceous-brown, or chocolate-gray.
genus of fungi in Boletaceae

Examples

Several species in the genus Fuscoboletinus are found in coniferous forests across North America.
The purple spore print is a key feature that helps identify Fuscoboletinus among boletes.
Mycologists collected samples of Fuscoboletinus for further study under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boletus - a genus of mushrooms with pores instead of gills, related family

Etymology

From Latin 'fuscus' (dark) + 'Boletinus', a diminutive of 'boletus' (mushroom).
