Vulpes velox

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlpiːz ˈvɛlɑks / (AmE), / ˈvʌlpiːz ˈviːlɒks / (BrE)
A small grey fox native to the plains of western North America, commonly known as the swift fox.
small fox of western North American plains

Examples

The Vulpes velox is rarely seen during the day, as it is mostly nocturnal.
Habitat loss has led to a decline in Vulpes velox populations across the Great Plains.
Biologists conducted a survey to monitor the health of Vulpes velox in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift fox - common name for Vulpes velox

Etymology

From Latin vulpes (fox) and velox (swift), referring to its speed.
