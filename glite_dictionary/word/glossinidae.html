Glossinidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of flies, closely related to the Muscidae, that includes the tsetse flies.
family of flies including tsetse flies

Examples

Members of the Glossinidae are responsible for transmitting African trypanosomiasis, also known as sleeping sickness.
Research on Glossinidae focuses on controlling the spread of disease in sub-Saharan Africa.
The larvae of Glossinidae develop differently compared to those of other common flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsetse fly - a bloodsucking African fly that transmits sleeping sickness; member of Glossinidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Glossina (the tsetse fly) plus the taxonomic suffix -idae (family).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of flies closely related to the housefly family, including tsetse flies, which are known for transmitting disease in Africa.
family of flies including tsetse flies

Examples

Many species within the Glossinidae family are vectors for African trypanosomiasis.
Scientists are studying the reproductive behavior of Glossinidae to develop better control methods.
The Glossinidae family is most prominent in sub-Saharan Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsetse fly - a large biting fly of Africa, known for transmitting sleeping sickness

Etymology

From New Latin Glossina (type genus, tsetse fly) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
