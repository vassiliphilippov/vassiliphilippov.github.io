to intuit

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtuːɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtjuːɪt / (BrE)
to know or understand something immediately by instinct or intuition, without the need for conscious reasoning
understand by intuition

Examples

She could intuit his mood just by the way he entered the room.
Many successful leaders seem to intuit the right decisions before seeing all the facts.
As an artist, he learned to intuit the feelings behind people's words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - to become aware of something without reasoning
  • Degree Lower: guess - to estimate or suppose something without sufficient information
  • Degree Higher: to know - to be aware of through observation, inquiry, or information

Etymology

From Latin 'intuitus' (a looking at, contemplation), from 'intuērī' (to look at).
