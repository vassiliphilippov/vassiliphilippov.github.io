roister-doister

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪstər ˌdɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪstə ˌdɔɪstə / (BrE)
a person who acts in a noisy, boisterous, or riotously festive manner
boisterous reveller
archaic

Examples

The roister-doister made everyone laugh with his outrageous singing.
Back in college, he was quite the roister-doister at every party.
During the festival, a group of roister-doisters paraded through the streets, making merry.

Synset Examples

His roister-doister friends always made gatherings unforgettable.
The party was full of roister-doisters celebrating wildly.
They were a bunch of roister-doisters, laughing and dancing all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person who is enjoying themselves in a lively and noisy way
  • Synonym: carouser - a person who drinks and celebrates noisily

Etymology

Rhyming compound based on 'roister' (to behave in a loud, boisterous way), influenced by the character name Ralph Roister Doister in a 16th-century play.
