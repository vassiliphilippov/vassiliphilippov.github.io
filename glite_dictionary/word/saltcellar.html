saltcellar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌsɛlər / (AmE), / ˈsɒltˌsɛlə / (BrE)
a small container, typically with a lid, used for holding salt and placed on the dining table
small table container for salt

Examples

She reached for the saltcellar to season her soup at dinner.
The antique saltcellar was made of silver and beautifully engraved.
At formal banquets, each place setting included its own small saltcellar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltshaker - a container with a perforated top for sprinkling salt

Etymology

From Middle English 'saut saler', from Old French 'salier' (salt box), from Latin 'salarium' (salt money).
