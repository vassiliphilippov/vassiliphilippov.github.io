douroucouli

1. NOUN

/ duːˌroʊkuːˈliː / (AmE), / ˌdʊərʊˈkuːli / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal monkey native to Central and South America, known for its large eyes and thick fur
nocturnal monkey of Central and South America

Examples

The douroucouli spends the daylight hours sleeping in tree hollows.
Researchers observed the nocturnal habits of the douroucouli in the Amazon rainforest.
A douroucouli has unusually large eyes to help it see at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl monkey - another name for douroucouli or Aotus monkey

Etymology

From an indigenous South American language (via Portuguese), originally referring to owl monkeys of the genus Aotus.
