forceful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrsfəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsfəl / (BrE)
expressing something in a strong and confident way, or involving the use of physical strength or power
strong, vigorous, or assertive

Examples

The politician delivered a forceful speech that impressed the entire audience.
She has a forceful personality and always stands up for her beliefs.
New laws were enacted as a forceful response to the crisis.

Synset Examples

a forceful speaker
a forceful personality
forceful measures
a forceful plan for peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'force' + '-ful', formed in English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrs.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːs.fəl / (BrE)
expressing opinions or actions in a strong and confident way; assertive and definite
strong and assertive in expression or action

Examples

Her forceful argument persuaded everyone in the meeting to support her proposal.
He has a forceful personality that commands attention whenever he enters a room.
The lawyer made a forceful case for his client’s innocence.

Synset Examples

the document contained a particularly emphatic guarantee of religious liberty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphatic - expressed clearly and with emphasis
  • Antonym: weak - lacking strength or force; not assertive

Etymology

From force + -ful, meaning 'full of force'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00157841-s
Synset POS: s
