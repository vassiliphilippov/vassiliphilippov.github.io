Paronychia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈnɪkiə / (AmE), / ˌparəˈnɪkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing annual or perennial herbs or small woody plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as whitlowworts.
a genus of whitlowwort plants

Examples

Paronychia is commonly found in sandy soils across North America and Europe.
Many species of Paronychia are used in traditional herbal remedies.
The delicate flowers of Paronychia are often overlooked due to their small size.

Etymology

From Greek *paronychia*, originally referring to a fingernail infection, later applied to these plants due to their historic medicinal use.

From Greek 'paronychia', referring to a kind of whitlow (inflammation around a fingernail).
