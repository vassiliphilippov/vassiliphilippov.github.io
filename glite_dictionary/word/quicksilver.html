quicksilver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkˌsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌsɪlvə / (BrE)
the metallic element mercury, a heavy, silvery, and toxic liquid metal at ordinary temperatures
liquid metallic element mercury

Examples

The old thermometer contains quicksilver to measure temperature accurately.
Artists sometimes used quicksilver in making mirrors before it was known to be toxic.
Spilling quicksilver can be dangerous because of its poisonous vapors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury - the element with symbol Hg and atomic number 80; also known as quicksilver

Etymology

From Old English 'cwicseolfor', from 'cwicu' (alive) + 'seolfor' (silver), referring to its liquid, 'living' appearance.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪkˌsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌsɪlvə / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) liable to sudden and unpredictable changes of mood or behavior; mercurial.
changeable or unpredictable in mood or behavior

Examples

Her quicksilver temper made her a challenging partner in business negotiations.
The child's quicksilver moods meant you never knew whether he would laugh or cry next.
Despite his quicksilver personality, he managed to inspire loyalty in his friends.

Synset Examples

erratic behavior
fickle weather
mercurial twists of temperament
a quicksilver character, cool and willful at one moment, utterly fragile the next

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercurial - (of a person) subject to sudden or unpredictable changes of mood or mind
  • Synonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially regarding one's loyalties or affections

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the shifting, fluid properties of 'quicksilver,' another name for mercury, the element.
