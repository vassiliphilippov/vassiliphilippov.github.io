Colbert

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈbɛr / (AmE), / kɒlˈbɛə / (BrE)
Butter creamed with parsley, tarragon, and beef extract, used especially as a garnish in French cuisine.
butter mixed with herbs and beef extract

Examples

The steak was topped with a generous portion of Colbert to enhance its flavor.
Classic French dishes often make use of Colbert to add richness and aroma.
He prepared a compound butter by blending parsley, tarragon, and beef extract to create Colbert.

Etymology

Named after Jean-Baptiste Colbert, French statesman (1619–1683), in reference to the French culinary tradition of naming dishes after notable people.
