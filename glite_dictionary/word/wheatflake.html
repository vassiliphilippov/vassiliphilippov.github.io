wheatflake

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːtˌfleɪk / (AmE), / ˈwiːtˌfleɪk / (BrE)
a small, crisp, flattened piece of wheat, typically eaten as a breakfast cereal
crisp flake cereal made from wheat

Examples

She poured milk over her wheatflakes for a quick breakfast.
The cereal aisle had several brands of wheatflakes to choose from.
A bowl of wheatflakes provides a good source of fiber in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornflake - crisp flake made from corn

Etymology

From 'wheat' (the grain) + 'flake' (a small, thin piece).
