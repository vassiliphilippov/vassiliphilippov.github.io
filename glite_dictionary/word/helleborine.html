helleborine

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrin / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːriːn / (BrE)
any of several orchid species belonging to the genus Cephalanthera, with slender stems and usually white or greenish flowers, found in woodlands of Europe and Asia.
woodland orchid of genus Cephalanthera

Examples

The helleborine blooms in shaded woodland areas during late spring and summer.
Gardeners admire the delicate white flowers of the helleborine for their subtle beauty.
We spotted a rare broad-leaved helleborine nestled among the ferns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for its complex flowers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin helleborina, apparently by folk association with hellebore plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛl.ə.bəˈraɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɛl.ɪ.bəˈriːn / (BrE)
any of several perennial orchids of the genus Epipactis, usually having greenish or purple-tinged flowers and found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
orchid of the genus Epipactis

Examples

We discovered a rare helleborine growing by the woodland stream during our hike.
The orchid enthusiast spent years cultivating different species of helleborine in her garden.
Some helleborines are protected by law due to their declining numbers in the wild.

Etymology

From Modern Latin *helleborina*, from *Helleborus*, a genus of plants, referring to the resemblance of the leaves.

3. NOUN

/ ˌhɛl.ə.bəˈraɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɛl.ɪ.bəˈriːn / (BrE)
A small temperate or tropical orchid with mottled or striped leaves and spikes of small yellowish-white flowers, often arranged in a twisted raceme.
a type of small orchid plant

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with rare helleborine orchids.
She photographed a helleborine blooming beside the path.
Many gardeners hope to cultivate helleborine in their native plant sections.

Etymology

From Modern Latin helleborine, diminutive of helleborus (hellebore), due to similarity in leaf shape.
