usurpation

1. NOUN

/ juːˌsɝːˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / juːˌsɜːˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of wrongfully taking and holding power or a position, especially a throne or authority, by force
wrongful seizure of power or authority

Examples

The king's usurpation of the throne led to years of civil war.
He was accused of usurpation after unlawfully taking control of the council.
History books often discuss the usurpation of imperial authority during that era.

Synset Examples

a succession of generals who ruled by usurpation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - the act of taking control suddenly or by force
  • Antonym: abdication - the act of giving up power, often in contrast to usurpation

Etymology

From Latin usurpatio (a taking into use or possession).

2. NOUN

/ juːˌsɚˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / juːˌsɜːˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking or occupying someone else's property, position, or rights without legal authority or permission
taking property or rights without legal authority
legal

Examples

The illegal usurpation of the land led to a lengthy court battle.
His usurpation of his neighbor's property did not go unnoticed by the authorities.
Centuries ago, acts of usurpation were a common source of disputes among nobles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespass - entry to someone's land or property without permission
  • Synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession, often forcibly or without consent
  • Antonym: lawful possession - holding property with legal right or permission

Etymology

From Latin usurpatio (a taking into use), from usurpare (to seize for use).
