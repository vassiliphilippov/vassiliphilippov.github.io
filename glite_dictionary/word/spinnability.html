spinnability

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌspɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a material, especially a textile fiber, that makes it suitable for being spun into yarn or thread
suitability of fibers for spinning

Examples

The spinnability of the cotton was tested before it was processed into yarn.
Research focuses on improving the spinnability of synthetic fibers to match that of natural ones.
Low spinnability often leads to frequent breakage during the spinning process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to make thread by twisting fibers together

Etymology

From spin + -ability, indicating the capacity to be spun.
