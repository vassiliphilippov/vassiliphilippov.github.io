backgrounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊndər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌɡraʊndə / (BrE)
an official meeting or document providing reporters with explanatory information on the background of an action, event, or policy, often not for direct quotation
press briefing to provide background information

Examples

The press was invited to a backgrounder to clarify the government's position.
During the backgrounder, officials answered questions off the record.
Journalists often attend a backgrounder before covering a major policy announcement.

Synset Examples

the secretary gave us a backgrounder on public health issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefing - a meeting for giving information or instructions

Etymology

From 'background' + '-er' (agent noun suffix), first used in journalism in early 20th century.
