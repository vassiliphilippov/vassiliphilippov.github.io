lodge

1. NOUN

/ lɑdʒ / (AmE), / lɒdʒ / (BrE)
a small hotel or inn, especially one in the countryside or near natural attractions, that provides accommodation to travelers
small hotel or inn for travelers

Examples

We spent two nights at a cozy mountain lodge during our hiking trip.
The safari lodge offered guided tours and wildlife viewing for its guests.
Many travelers choose a lakeside lodge for a relaxing getaway close to nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inn - a small hotel providing lodging, food, and drink, especially in the countryside
  • Synonym: hotel - an establishment providing accommodation, meals, and other services for travelers and tourists
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03546876-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ lɑdʒ / (AmE), / lɒdʒ / (BrE)
a traditional house or dwelling, especially one used by some Native American peoples
traditional Native American dwelling

Examples

The tribe built a sturdy lodge from willow branches and mud for the winter months.
Each family had their own lodge arranged in a circle around the central fire.
The tour included a visit to a reconstructed Sioux lodge on the reservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wigwam - a domed Native American dwelling made of bark, reed mats, or skins
  • Synonym: tepee - a cone-shaped tent traditionally used by some Native American peoples
Source: WordNet
