Phyllodoce

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɑːdəˌsi / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒdəˌsi / (BrE)
a small genus of evergreen shrubs found in Arctic and alpine regions, known for their clustered flowers and hardy nature
a genus of Arctic and alpine evergreen shrubs

Examples

Phyllodoce species are commonly found growing in the harsh climates of mountain slopes.
The delicate pink flowers of Phyllodoce add color to the Arctic tundra during summer.
Botanists studied several types of Phyllodoce to understand how shrubs survive extreme cold.

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + dokē (resemblance), referring to its leaf-like characteristics.

A small genus of evergreen shrubs found in Arctic and alpine regions, known for their narrow leaves and bell-shaped flowers.
genus of Arctic and alpine evergreen shrubs
medical

Examples

Botanists study the adaptation of Phyllodoce to cold alpine environments.
Several species within the genus Phyllodoce produce colorful, bell-shaped flowers.
The Phyllodoce shrubs thrive in the harsh, windy climate of Arctic tundra.

From Greek Phyllodoce, a name from Greek mythology, used in botanical nomenclature.
