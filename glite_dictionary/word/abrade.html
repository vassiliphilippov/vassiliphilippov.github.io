to abrade

1. VERB

/ əˈbreɪd / (AmE), / əˈbreɪd / (BrE)
to rub or wear away the surface of something by friction or scraping
rub or wear away by friction

Examples

Years of strong winds began to abrade the paint on the side of the house.
If you use steel wool, it might abrade the surface of the table.
Sand particles can slowly abrade rocks along the riverbank.

Synset Examples

scour the counter tops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - to remove something from a surface by rubbing
  • Antonym: smooth - to make a surface even by reducing roughness

Etymology

From Latin abradere (to scrape off), from ab- (away) + radere (to scrape).

2. VERB

/ əˈbreɪd / (AmE), / əˈbreɪd / (BrE)
to wear away the surface of something through friction or scraping
to wear away by rubbing or scraping

Examples

Prolonged exposure to sand can abrade the paint on a car's exterior.
Dental tools are designed to abrade plaque from the surface of teeth.
He accidentally abraded his knee when he fell on the rough pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away, typically by natural forces
  • Synonym: scrape - to remove material from a surface by rubbing

Etymology

From Latin 'abradere' (to scrape off), from 'ab-' (away) + 'radere' (to scrape).
