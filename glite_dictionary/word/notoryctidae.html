Notoryctidae

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌtɔːriˈktɪdiː / (AmE), / nəʊˌtɒrɪkˈtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing, mole-like marsupials native to Australia, commonly known as marsupial moles.
family of Australian marsupial moles

Examples

The family Notoryctidae includes two known species of marsupial moles.
Fossil records suggest that Notoryctidae have unique adaptations for underground life.
Research into Notoryctidae has revealed fascinating information about their sensory organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial mole - common name for animals in the Notoryctidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek notos (back) and oryktēs (digger) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˌrɪkˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtəˌrɪkˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, burrowing, insectivorous marsupials from Australia, known as pouched moles.
family of Australian pouched moles

Examples

The Notoryctidae are unique among marsupials for their adaptation to a subterranean lifestyle.
Members of the Notoryctidae can be found only in arid regions of Australia.
Research on the Notoryctidae has provided insights into convergent evolution among burrowing mammals.

Etymology

From genus name Notoryctes (from Greek 'notos' meaning 'back' and 'oryktēs' meaning 'digger'), plus the zoological family suffix '-idae'.
