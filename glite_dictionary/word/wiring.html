wiring

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of wires used to distribute electricity in a building, machine, or device
system of electrical wires

Examples

The old house needs new wiring to meet current safety standards.
Faulty wiring was responsible for the power outage last night.
The technician inspected the wiring in the control panel for signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuitry - the arrangement of circuits for electrical current
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04603182-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or work of installing electrical wires in a building, device, or system
installation of electrical wires

Examples

The house needed new wiring before we could move in.
He specializes in electrical wiring for commercial buildings.
Old wiring can be a safety hazard if not properly maintained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rewiring - the act of replacing or repairing electrical wires

Etymology

From 'wire' + '-ing'; 'wire' from Old English 'wīr' (metal thread).
