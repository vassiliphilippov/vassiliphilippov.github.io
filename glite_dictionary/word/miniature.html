miniature

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪniətʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətjʊə / (BrE)
very small in size; much smaller than normal for its kind
very small in size

Examples

This museum features a miniature train that runs around a small-scale village.
She collects miniature books that fit in the palm of your hand.
The miniature roses on the table added a delicate touch to the decor.

Synset Examples

a miniature camera

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - extremely small in size
  • Synonym: minute - very small; minor
  • Antonym: gigantic - extremely large in size

Etymology

From Italian ‘miniatura’ (manuscript illumination); originally referring to small portraits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətʃə / (BrE)
A very small copy, model, or version of something, especially of a person or thing.
very small copy or model of something

Examples

The artist crafted a detailed miniature of the historic castle.
She collects miniatures of famous landmarks from around the world.
A miniature of the vintage car sat on his desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a three-dimensional representation of a person or thing
  • Synonym: replica - an exact copy or reproduction, especially on a smaller scale
  • Antonym: life-size - of the same size as the person or thing represented

Etymology

From Italian *miniatura* (manuscript illumination), from Latin *miniare* (to color with red lead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03774770-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniətʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətjʊə / (BrE)
a small painting or illustration, especially one found in an illuminated manuscript or book
small painting or illumination in a book

Examples

The medieval manuscript was filled with delicate miniatures of saints and angels.
Art historians study miniatures to learn about the culture and techniques of the Middle Ages.
A dazzling miniature adorned the beginning of each chapter in the ancient text.

Etymology

From Italian *miniatura* (manuscript illumination), from Latin *miniare* (to color with minium, red lead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03774932-n
Synset POS: n
