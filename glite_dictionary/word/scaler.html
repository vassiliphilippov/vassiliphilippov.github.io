scaler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlə / (BrE)
an electronic device that counts rapid pulses, especially those too frequent to be individually recorded
electronic pulse-counting device

Examples

The laboratory technician monitored the radioactive decay events using a scaler.
To handle the high frequency of incoming signals, the team installed a digital scaler.
Modern particle detectors often rely on a scaler to tally particle hits accurately.
