lanugo

1. NOUN

/ ləˈnuːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ləˈnjuːɡəʊ / (BrE)
the fine, soft hair that covers the body of a human fetus, usually shed before birth
fine hair on a fetus before birth
medical

Examples

In some premature infants, lanugo is still visible on their shoulders and backs.
The presence of lanugo is considered a normal part of fetal development.
Traces of lanugo may linger on a newborn's body for a short period after birth.

Etymology

From Latin 'lanugo' (down, soft hair), from 'lana' (wool).
