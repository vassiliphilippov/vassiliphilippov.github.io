Panonychus

1. PROPN

/ pæˈnɑːnɪkəs / (AmE), / pəˈnɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tiny spider mites in the family Tetranychidae, some species of which are known as agricultural pests.
genus of spider mites (Tetranychidae)

Examples

Several species of Panonychus are notorious for damaging crops like citrus and apple trees.
The red spider mite, known as Panonychus ulmi, is a prominent pest in orchards.
Researchers are studying ways to control Panonychus infestations without harmful pesticides.

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) and 'onychos' (claw, nail).

2. PROPN

/ pæˈnɑːnɪkəs / (AmE), / pæˈnɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of mites in the family Tetranychidae, known for including species that are plant pests.
genus of mites (Tetranychidae)

Examples

Several species within the genus Panonychus are notorious agricultural pests.
The red spider mite, a member of Panonychus, can damage fruit trees.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Panonychus under laboratory conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) and 'onychus' (claw or nail), referring to characteristics of the genus.
