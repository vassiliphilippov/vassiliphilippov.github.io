calender

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləndər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
a machine that smooths or glazes paper or cloth by pressing it between plates or passing it through rollers
machine for smoothing paper or cloth

Examples

The freshly made paper was passed through a calender to achieve a glossy finish.
Modern textile factories rely on automatic calenders to produce smooth fabrics.
He operated the calender every day, ensuring the cloth came out perfectly polished.

Etymology

From French 'calandre', from Late Latin 'calendra', meaning 'roller'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkæləndər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
to press paper, cloth, or other material between rollers or plates to smooth, glaze, or thin it into sheets
press material with rollers for smoothing or glazing

Examples

The factory calenders the fabric to achieve a smooth finish.
Before packaging, the manufacturer calendered the paper for extra gloss.
They are calendering the sheets to make them thinner and more flexible.

Synset Examples

calender paper

Etymology

From French *calandre*, from Medieval Latin *calendra*, referring to a machine for pressing fabrics.
