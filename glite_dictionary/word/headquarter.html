to headquarter

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛdˌkwɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to base the main offices or administrative center of an organization in a particular place
to base main offices in a location

Examples

The tech company chose to headquarter its European operations in Dublin.
Many multinationals headquarter their regional divisions in Singapore for strategic reasons.
The agency will headquarter its new branch in downtown Los Angeles.

Synset Examples

the company is headquartered in New Jersey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to locate - to set up or establish in a particular place

Etymology

From noun 'headquarters' (main administrative center).
