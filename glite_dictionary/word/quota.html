quota

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtə / (BrE)
an official limit on the quantity or value of goods that can be imported or exported during a particular period
an official import or export limit

Examples

The government set a quota on the number of cars that could be imported each year.
The textile quota was reached early due to high demand from retailers.
Talks focused on reducing the quota for agricultural products entering the country.

Synset Examples

the quota for Japanese imports was negotiated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - a restriction or boundary on quantity or extent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quota (pars) 'how large a share, how much'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtə / (BrE)
an officially fixed or limited number or quantity of people or things, especially one that a person or group is expected to reach or not exceed
officially fixed number or share

Examples

Each salesperson must reach their quota by the end of the quarter.
The fishing industry has a quota to prevent overfishing.
She exceeded her monthly quota and earned a bonus.

Synset Examples

all the salesmen met their quota for the month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the action or process of distributing something
  • Synonym: limit - a maximum amount or number that is allowed

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quota (pars) 'how large a share,' from Latin quotus 'of what number.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtə / (BrE)
a fixed share or quantity of something that is officially allowed or expected from each member of a group
proportional share allotted

Examples

Each country in the agreement was given a quota for oil production.
The manager set a monthly sales quota for every employee.
Our team failed to meet its quota this quarter due to supply chain issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the act of distributing or assigning something
  • Synonym: allowance - an amount of something permitted or allotted
  • Antonym: surplus - an amount greater than needed, the opposite of a set share

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quota (pars) 'how large (a part)', from Latin quotus 'of what number'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
