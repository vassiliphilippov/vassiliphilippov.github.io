subtlety

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌt̬əlti / (AmE), / ˈsʌtl̩ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult to detect, perceive, or analyze; delicacy of distinction or meaning
quality of being difficult to detect or analyze

Examples

The subtlety of his argument was lost on most of the audience.
Appreciating art often requires an eye for subtlety and nuance.
She admired the subtlety in the flavor of the dish, which revealed itself gradually.

Synset Examples

you had to admire the subtlety of the distinctions he drew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle difference in meaning or opinion
  • Antonym: obviousness - the quality of being easily perceived or understood

Etymology

From Old French subtilité, from Latin subtilitas ('fineness, delicacy').

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌt̬.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.əl.ti / (BrE)
a fine or delicate distinction in meaning, opinion, or attitude; a nuance
a fine distinction or nuance

Examples

The subtlety of his humor is sometimes lost on a general audience.
She admired the subtlety in the artist's use of color and light.
It takes great skill to notice every subtlety in the negotiation process.

Synset Examples

without understanding the finer nuances you can't enjoy the humor
don't argue about shades of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle difference in meaning or expression
  • Synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things
  • Antonym: obviousness - the quality of being easily perceived or understood

Etymology

From Old French 'subtilite', from Latin 'subtilitas', meaning fineness, delicacy.
