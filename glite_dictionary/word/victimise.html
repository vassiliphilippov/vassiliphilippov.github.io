to victimise

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, often by making them a victim of a crime, injustice, or prejudice
treat someone unfairly; make a victim of
british

Examples

The new law aims to protect people who have been victimised because of their beliefs.
She felt victimised at work after reporting unethical behavior.
Some teenagers reported being victimised by bullies at school.

Synset Examples

I was victimized by this con-man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to victimize - treat someone cruelly or unfairly (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone with persistent hostility or cruelty
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From English 'victim' + '-ise', from Latin 'victima' (sacrificial animal).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone unfairly by making them suffer or by punishing them unjustly
treat or punish someone unfairly

Examples

Many workers felt their supervisor would victimise anyone who dared to complain.
She claimed that she had been unfairly victimised because of her political beliefs.
Whistleblowers are often victimised for revealing uncomfortable truths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, often because of their beliefs
  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • Antonym: to exonerate - to clear someone from blame or fault

Etymology

From Latin 'victima' (sacrificial animal) via English 'victim' + '-ise'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02506268-v
Synset POS: v
