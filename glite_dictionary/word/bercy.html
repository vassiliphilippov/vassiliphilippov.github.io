Bercy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrsi / (AmE), / ˈbɛːsi / (BrE)
A sauce made of butter creamed with white wine, shallots, and parsley, used especially as an accompaniment to meat or fish.
sauce with butter, white wine, shallots, and parsley

Examples

The grilled fish was served with a delicate Bercy sauce.
He prepared a classic steak with Bercy for the dinner party.
You can brighten the flavor of chicken by adding Bercy on top before serving.

Etymology

Named after Bercy, a district in Paris, France.
