equalitarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌikwəˌlɛtəˈrɪənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌiːkwəˌlætəˈrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or advocacy of the equality of all people, especially in political, economic, or social life.
belief in equality for all people

Examples

Many reformers have championed equalitarianism as a guiding principle in shaping social policy.
The debate over equalitarianism continues to influence discussions of economic justice.
She wrote essays promoting equalitarianism in education and employment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egalitarianism - the doctrine that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities

Etymology

Formed from 'equalitarian' (from 'equal') plus the suffix '-ism' indicating a doctrine or belief.
