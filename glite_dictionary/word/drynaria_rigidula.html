Drynaria rigidula

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈnɛəriə rɪˈdʒɪdjulə / (AmE), / draɪˈnɛəriə rɪˈdʒɪdjʊlə / (BrE)
A large fern species native to Asia, Polynesia, and Australia, which grows as an epiphyte or on rocks.
a large epiphytic fern species

Examples

Drynaria rigidula is often found growing on tree trunks in tropical forests.
Botanists studied the reproduction habits of Drynaria rigidula in the wild.
The fronds of Drynaria rigidula can reach impressive lengths in humid environments.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Drynaria' and species name 'rigidula', meaning 'somewhat rigid'.
