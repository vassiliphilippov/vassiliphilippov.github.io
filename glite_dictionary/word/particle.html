particle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪkl / (BrE)
a very small piece or amount of something
a tiny piece or amount

Examples

Not a particle of dust could be found on the polished shelves.
She felt a particle of doubt but pushed it aside quickly.
There wasn’t a particle of truth in his excuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a very small spot or piece of something
  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or portion
  • Degree Higher: chunk - a thick, solid piece of something

Etymology

From Latin particula (a small part), diminutive of pars (part).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a very small piece of matter having mass and internal structure but so small as to be treated as having negligible dimensions, especially in physics or chemistry
extremely small body of matter in science

Examples

Physicists study how each particle interacts with others at the quantum level.
Dust is made up of countless tiny particles too small to see with the naked eye.
The electron is a fundamental particle in atomic structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atom - the basic unit of a chemical element
  • Synonym: molecule - a group of atoms bonded together
  • Degree Lower: subatomic particle - a particle smaller than an atom, such as a proton or electron

Etymology

From Latin 'particula' (a small part or portion).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
A function word, typically an adverb or preposition, used with a verb to form a phrasal verb in English.
function word in phrasal verbs

Examples

In the phrasal verb 'give up', 'up' is the particle that changes the meaning of the verb.
Learning to recognize the particle in phrasal verbs helps improve your understanding of English idioms.
Not every word that follows a verb is a particle; some are simply prepositions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preposition - a word placed before a noun to show relation
  • Synonym: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb

Etymology

From Latin particula (a small part, diminutive of pars).
