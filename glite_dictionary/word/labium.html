labium

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əm / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.əm / (BrE)
a liplike structure bordering a bodily opening, especially any of the four folded structures of a woman's vulva
liplike anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's labium for any signs of irritation.
In human anatomy, the labium refers to the folds of tissue surrounding the vulva.
Proper identification of each labium is important in medical examinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip - the fleshy folds forming the edge of the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' meaning 'lip'.
