Acipenser huso

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɪp.ɛn.sər ˈhjuː.soʊ / (AmE), / əˌsɪp.ɛnsə ˈhjuː.səʊ / (BrE)
A species of sturgeon native to the Black and Caspian seas, valued for its caviar and isinglass.
species of sturgeon (beluga), source of caviar

Examples

Acipenser huso is highly prized for producing some of the world's finest caviar.
The conservation status of Acipenser huso has raised concerns among environmentalists.
Scientists study Acipenser huso to learn more about the effects of overfishing in the Caspian Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beluga - Common name for Acipenser huso, a large sturgeon species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus 'Acipenser' (sturgeon) + species name 'huso', original naming in scientific classification.
