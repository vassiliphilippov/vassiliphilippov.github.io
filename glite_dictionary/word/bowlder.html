bowlder

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of 'boulder', meaning a large smooth rounded mass of rock, detached from its place of origin
large detached rock; variant spelling
archaic

Examples

The climbers rested near a giant bowlder at the base of the hill.
During the flood, a massive bowlder was swept onto the road, blocking traffic.
Archaeologists discovered a prehistoric carving on a bowlder in the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boulder - a large smooth rounded mass of rock

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'boulder', from Middle English 'bulderstone' (a large stone), related to Swedish dialect 'bullersten'.
