cross-country

1. ADV

/ krɔsˈkʌntri / (AmE), / krɒsˈkʌntri / (BrE)
across open countryside rather than following established roads, paths, or tracks
across open countryside, not on roads

Examples

The pipeline stretches cross-country for hundreds of miles.
They trekked cross-country to reach the remote village.
The wild horses ran cross-country, past meadows and wooded hills.

Synset Examples

the river runs cross-country
the road runs cross-country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-road - away from established roads, especially with vehicles

2. ADV

/ krɔsˈkʌntri / (AmE), / krɒsˈkʌntri / (BrE)
In a direction or manner that does not follow established roads or tracks, often over natural terrain.
off-road; across open country

Examples

The runners moved cross-country to reach the next checkpoint faster.
They decided to ski cross-country instead of staying on the marked trails.
We cycled cross-country through fields and forests during our summer trip.

Synset Examples

they liked to race cross-country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-road - not on a paved road; over rough terrain

Etymology

From 'cross' (across) and 'country' (land), referring to movement across open terrain.
