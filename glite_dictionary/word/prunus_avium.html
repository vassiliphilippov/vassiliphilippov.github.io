Prunus avium

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˈeɪvi.əm / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˈeɪvɪəm / (BrE)
a large Eurasian tree species that bears small, dark, bitter fruit in the wild and sweet, edible fruit when cultivated; also known as the wild cherry or sweet cherry.
Eurasian tree, source of sweet cherry fruit

Examples

The orchard was filled with Prunus avium trees, their branches heavy with ripening cherries.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Prunus avium in different European climates.
The jam was made from fruit harvested from a wild Prunus avium specimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet cherry - another name for Prunus avium; cultivated for its edible fruit
  • Synonym: wild cherry - refers to Prunus avium in its wild form; produces small, bitter fruit

Etymology

From Latin; 'prunus' meaning 'plum tree' and 'avium' meaning 'of birds'.
