faithful

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
The people who are loyal to a particular religion or attend a particular church regularly.
loyal adherents of a religion or church

Examples

The priest addressed the assembled faithful during the Sunday service.
Every year, thousands of the faithful make a pilgrimage to the holy site.
The faithful gathered outside the cathedral despite the rain.

Etymology

From Old French 'feitful', from 'feit' (faith) + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθfʊl / (BrE)
(of a person) having sexual relations only with their husband, wife, or partner; not being unfaithful.
not sexually unfaithful to a partner

Examples

He promised to remain faithful to his wife throughout their marriage.
Despite the long distance, she stayed faithful to her boyfriend.
Being faithful in a relationship is important to many people.

Synset Examples

he remained faithful to his wife

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfaithful - having sexual relations outside one's relationship; not loyal to a partner

Etymology

From Middle English *feithful*, from Old French *feidful*, from *feid* (faith), from Latin *fides* (faith, trust).

exactly representing or following the original; accurate and true to the source or facts.
accurately representing an original

Examples

Her translation is remarkably faithful to the tone and meaning of the original text.
We admired the faithful depiction of historical events in the documentary.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfaithful - not remaining true to the original; inaccurate or false

5. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
the people who are loyal and steadfast supporters of a leader, cause, or especially a religion; often used to refer collectively to devoted followers
loyal supporters, especially of a religion

Examples

Every year, the church holds a special service for the faithful who gather from around the country.
The mayor addressed the faithful at the rally, thanking them for their unwavering support.
Thousands of faithful braved the cold to attend the midnight vigil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: believer - a person who has religious faith
  • Synonym: devotee - an enthusiastic follower or admirer
  • Antonym: infidel - a person who does not believe in a particular religion

Etymology

From Old French *feithful*, from *feid* (faith), from Latin *fides* (faith, trust).
