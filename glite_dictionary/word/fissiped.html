fissiped

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪs.ɪˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.ɪ.pɛd / (BrE)
a terrestrial carnivorous mammal with toes separated to the base, such as dogs, cats, and bears; formerly used in classification
terrestrial carnivore with separated toes

Examples

The dog is an example of a fissiped mammal, adapted to life on land rather than in water.
Unlike seals and walruses, members of the fissiped group have digits that are not fused into flippers.
Naturalists once classified cats and raccoons together under the order fissiped for their separated toes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pinniped - aquatic carnivorous mammals with fin-like limbs (seals, sea lions, walruses)

Etymology

From Latin 'fissipes', from 'fissus' (split) + 'pes' (foot).
