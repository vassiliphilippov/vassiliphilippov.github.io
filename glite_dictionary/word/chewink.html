chewink

1. NOUN

/ tʃuˈwɪŋk / (AmE), / tʃuˈwɪŋk / (BrE)
a common towhee (Pipilo erythrophthalmus) found in eastern North America, known for its distinctive call
eastern North American towhee

Examples

The chewink is easily recognized by its rufous sides and long, rounded tail.
We often hear the distinct call of a chewink while hiking in the woods.
Birdwatchers spotted several chewinks near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towhee - a type of North American songbird in the family Passerellidae

Etymology

Imitative of the bird's call; first recorded in the 18th century.
