Anas platyrhynchos

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.nəs ˌplæt.ɪˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈa.nəs ˌplat.ɪˈrɪŋ.kɒs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the mallard, a widespread wild duck species from which most domestic ducks descend
mallard duck species (scientific name)

Examples

The Anas platyrhynchos is commonly seen in parks and wetlands around the world.
Studies of Anas platyrhynchos populations have provided insight into migratory bird behavior.
Many domestic duck breeds are descended from the wild Anas platyrhynchos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallard - the common wild duck from which domestic ducks descend

Etymology

From Latin: 'anas' meaning 'duck' and 'platyrhynchos' from Greek 'platy-' (broad) and 'rhynchos' (beak).
