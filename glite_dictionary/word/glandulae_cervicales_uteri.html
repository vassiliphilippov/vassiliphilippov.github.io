glandulae cervicales uteri

1. NOUN

/ ɡlænˈdjuːli ˌsɜːrˈvaɪkəliːz juːˈtɪraɪ / (AmE), / ɡlanˈdjuːliː ˌsɜːvɪˈkaːliːz juːˈtɪraɪ / (BrE)
mucus-secreting glands located in the mucous membrane of the uterine cervix.
glands in the uterine cervix
medical

Examples

The glandulae cervicales uteri play an essential role in maintaining the cervical mucus.
Inflammation of the glandulae cervicales uteri can lead to abnormal discharge.
Pathologists examined the glandulae cervicales uteri for any signs of disease during the biopsy.

Etymology

From Latin: 'glandulae' meaning 'glands', 'cervicales' relating to the neck or cervix, and 'uteri' meaning 'of the uterus'.
