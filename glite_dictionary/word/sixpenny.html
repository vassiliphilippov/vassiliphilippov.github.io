sixpenny

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪkspəni / (AmE), / ˈsɪkspəni / (BrE)
Of little value; trivial or insignificant.
of little worth; trivial

Examples

She dismissed his concerns as sixpenny complaints not worth her attention.
The play was criticized for its sixpenny plot and predictable ending.
He filled the report with sixpenny observations that added little substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From 'sixpenny', originally denoting something costing six pence; later figurative use for things considered cheap or unimportant.
