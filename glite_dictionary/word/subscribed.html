subscribed

1. ADJ

/ səbˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪbd / (BrE)
(of a document) having a signature written at the end, especially as a mark of approval or validity.
having a signature at the end (of a document)
legal

Examples

The lawyer presented the subscribed contract as evidence in court.
All parties must ensure the document is duly subscribed before submission.
A subscribed will is considered valid under most legal systems.

Synset Examples

the subscribed will

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signed - having a signature; marked by a person's signature

Etymology

Past participle of 'subscribe', from Latin 'subscribere' (write underneath, sign).
