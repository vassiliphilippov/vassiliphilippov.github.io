letting

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
a property, such as a house or flat, that is leased or rented out; or the act of leasing such a property
property rented out; act of leasing
british

Examples

We found a lovely letting on the outskirts of the city.
The agency specialises in short-term lettings for students and professionals.
He managed several lettings in central London over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental - the act of renting or a rented property

Etymology

From the verb 'let' (to lease), from Old English 'lǣtan' (to allow).
