austenitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːstəˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːstɪˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of steel or alloys) containing or relating to austenite, a solid solution of iron with carbon and sometimes other elements, typically nonmagnetic and with good corrosion resistance.
containing or relating to austenite

Examples

Stainless steel with an austenitic structure is widely used for kitchen equipment.
The engineer recommended austenitic alloys for their resistance to corrosion.
This austenitic steel remains nonmagnetic even after cold working.

Synset Examples

austenitic alloy steel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: martensitic - relating to martensite (a hard form of steel microstructure)

Etymology

From austenite, named after Sir William Chandler Roberts-Austen, British metallurgist, plus the suffix -ic.
