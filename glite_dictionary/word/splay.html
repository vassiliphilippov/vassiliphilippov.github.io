splay

1. NOUN

/ spleɪ / (AmE), / spleɪ / (BrE)
an outward bevel or slanted surface around a door or window that gives the appearance of increased size
outward bevel around a door or window

Examples

The stone splay around the window let in more light to the room.
Historic buildings often feature a wide splay to enhance the entrance’s visual impact.
She admired the deep splay framing the old oak door.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04289018-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ spleɪ / (AmE), / spleɪ / (BrE)
to spread or turn (something) outwards or apart, especially the limbs or fingers
spread or turn outward (limbs, etc.)

Examples

The dancer began to splay her fingers gracefully as she moved.
The frog's legs splayed wide as it leapt onto the lily pad.
He splayed his toes out to keep his balance on the slippery rock.

Synset Examples

These birds can splay out their toes
ballet dancers can rotate their legs out by 90 degrees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to open or extend over a large area or range; to stretch out

Etymology

From Middle English 'spleyen', from Old French 'espleier' (to display, spread out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02049364-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spleɪ / (AmE), / spleɪ / (BrE)
to spread (things, especially limbs, fingers, or other body parts) out or apart widely
spread out or apart widely

Examples

The dancer splayed her arms to emphasize the movement.
The bird splayed its tail feathers in a dramatic display.
His legs splayed awkwardly as he slipped on the ice.

Synset Examples

He splayed his huge hands over the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to open or expand something outwards or over a wider area

Etymology

From Old French 'esplaier' (to spread out), from Latin 'explicare' (to unfold, spread out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02049604-v
Synset POS: v

4. ADJ

/ spleɪ / (AmE), / spleɪ / (BrE)
turned or spread outward, especially in an awkward or ungainly way.
turned or spread outward (awkwardly)

Examples

The athlete had splay knees after years of running on uneven ground.
He noticed the splay branches jutting unevenly from the old tree trunk.
His new shoes caused his toes to splay awkwardly while walking.

Synset Examples

splay knees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to open, expand, or extend over a larger area
  • Antonym: straight - not bent or turned outward

Etymology

From Middle English splayen, a shortening of displayen (to display), from Old French desploiier.
