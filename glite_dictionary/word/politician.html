politician

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.lɪˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.ɪˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a person who is professionally involved in politics, especially as a holder of or a candidate for an elected office
person professionally involved in politics

Examples

The politician addressed the crowd with promises of reform.
Many people distrust career politicians after years of government scandals.
She aspired to become a politician to make a difference in her country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statesman - a skilled, experienced, and respected political leader

Etymology

From French *politicien*, from politique (politics).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːləˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who uses cunning, scheming, or underhanded methods to gain advantage or power in an organization.
scheming or manipulative person

Examples

He was considered a politician in the company, always working behind the scenes to advance his own agenda.
Some say you have to be a bit of a politician to survive the corporate ladder.
Don't trust him—he's a real politician who knows how to manipulate every situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schemer - a person involved in making secret or underhanded plans
  • Synonym: manipulator - someone who controls or influences others in a clever, often unscrupulous way

Etymology

From politician in the sense of one skilled in political maneuvering; from French *politicien*; ultimately from Greek *politikós* ('of citizens' or 'of the state').

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.ləˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.ɪˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a person who is professionally involved in politics, especially as an elected official or leader in government administration
a person engaged in politics or government leadership

Examples

As a respected politician, she worked tirelessly to improve education policy.
The politician addressed a crowd of supporters at the rally.
Many people feel that trust in politicians has declined over recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statesman - an experienced and respected politician, especially one noted for wisdom and integrity
  • Antonym: civil servant - a person employed in the public sector on behalf of a government department or agency

Etymology

From French *politicien*, from politique (politics), ultimately from Greek *politikós* (of citizens, civic).
