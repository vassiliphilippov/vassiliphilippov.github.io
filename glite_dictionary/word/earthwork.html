earthwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːθˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a defensive wall or rampart made of earth, especially as a fortification
defensive wall made of earth

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered an ancient earthwork that once protected the settlement.
The soldiers built an earthwork to defend their position during the siege.
Many Civil War battlefields still have visible earthwork fortifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank around a fort
  • Synonym: battlement - a parapet at the top of a wall, typically of a fort or castle

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'work', indicating a construction made from soil.
