gum

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
wood or timber obtained from any of various gum trees, especially the sweet gum.
wood from a gum tree

Examples

The cabinets were made from polished gum for a smooth and attractive finish.
Many furniture makers prefer gum due to its durability and appealing grain.
After the storm, several logs of gum were delivered to the sawmill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet gum - deciduous North American tree valued for its hardwood and resin

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
the firm pink tissue in the mouth that surrounds the bases of the teeth and covers the jawbone
tissue in the mouth around the teeth

Examples

You should brush your teeth gently to avoid damaging your gums.
Her gums bled slightly during the dental check-up.
Healthy gums are usually pink and do not swell or bleed easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gingiva - technical term for the tissue of the gums

Etymology

From Old English 'guma', from Latin 'gingiva' (gum of the mouth).

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)

Examples

5. NOUN

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
any of several trees, especially of the genera Eucalyptus, Liquidambar, or Nyssa, that produce gum or resinous sap.

Examples

The forest was dominated by towering gum trees that provided shade and shelter for wildlife.
Farmers often tap the gum for its valuable sap, which is used in varnishes and adhesives.
The Australian landscape is famous for its many species of gum trees, particularly the eucalyptus.

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *gumm*, from Latin *gummi* (gum, resin), ultimately from Greek *kommi*.
