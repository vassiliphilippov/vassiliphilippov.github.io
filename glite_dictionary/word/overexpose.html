to overexpose

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose someone or something to something, such as light or an experience, to an excessive degree
expose too much to something

Examples

Be careful not to overexpose the film to sunlight or your photos will be ruined.
Some psychologists believe that children can become numb to violence if they are overexposed to it at a young age.
The celebrity worried that being in too many commercials would overexpose her and make the public lose interest.

Synset Examples

As a child, I was overexposed to French movies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underexpose - expose too little (especially of photographic film)

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) + 'expose'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose film, a photo, or an image to too much light, causing it to become too bright or washed out
expose to too much light (photography)

Examples

If you overexpose your photos, they will appear washed out and lack detail.
The bright sunlight overexposed the film, ruining all of the vacation pictures.
She tried not to overexpose the image while adjusting the camera settings.

Synset Examples

the photographic film was overexposed and there is no image

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underexpose - expose film or image to insufficient light

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'expose' (to subject to light or view).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02118105-v
Synset POS: v
