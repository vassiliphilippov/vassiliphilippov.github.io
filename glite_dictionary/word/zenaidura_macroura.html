Zenaidura macroura

1. NOUN

/ zɛˌneɪɪˈdʊrə ˌmækˈrɔrə / (AmE), / zɛˌneɪɪˈdjʊərə ˌmækrˈɔːrə / (BrE)
A North American wild dove with a soft, mournful call, commonly known as the mourning dove.
North American mourning dove species

Examples

The distinctive call of the Zenaidura macroura is often heard at dawn in rural areas.
Birdwatchers frequently spot the Zenaidura macroura perched on telephone wires.
The gentle cooing of the Zenaidura macroura adds to the peaceful atmosphere of the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning dove - common name for Zenaidura macroura, a North American wild dove

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Zenaidura' honoring Zénaïde Laetitia Julie Bonaparte, and species name 'macroura' from Greek 'makros' (long) + 'oura' (tail).
