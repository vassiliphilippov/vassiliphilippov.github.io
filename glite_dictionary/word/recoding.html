recoding

1. NOUN

/ riˈkoʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈkəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of converting information from one code or format to another
conversion from one code to another

Examples

The data analyst spent the afternoon recoding the survey responses into numerical values.
Genetic recoding can result in proteins being synthesized in new ways.
The software update included automatic recoding of outdated file formats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recode - to convert something into a different code or format

Etymology

From 're-' (again) and 'coding' (assigning a code), based on the verb 'recode'.
