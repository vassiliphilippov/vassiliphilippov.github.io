calyculus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪkjələs / (AmE), / kaˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A small cup-shaped structure in plants or animals, such as a taste bud, optic cup, or the cavity in coral that holds a polyp.
small cup-shaped anatomical structure

Examples

The coral's calyculus provides protection for the delicate polyp inside.
Each taste bud is housed in a tiny calyculus on the tongue.
Microscopic analysis revealed the presence of several calyculi within the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the outermost whorl of a flower, typically forming a protective layer around a bud; also means a cup-shaped structure

Etymology

From Latin 'calyculus', diminutive of 'calyx' (cup, husk, outer covering).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪkjʊləs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A group of small bracts resembling a calyx, occurring beneath the actual calyx in some flowers, such as carnations or hibiscus.
group of bracts beneath a flower calyx
medical

Examples

The carnation's calyculus is easily seen beneath its showy petals.
A botanist pointed out a well-developed calyculus in the hibiscus flower he dissected.
Some species display a prominent calyculus that can be mistaken for part of the true calyx.

Etymology

From Latin diminutive of 'calyx' meaning 'bud' or 'outer covering of a flower'.
