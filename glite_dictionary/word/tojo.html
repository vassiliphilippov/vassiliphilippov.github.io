Tojo

1. PROPN

/ ˈtoʊ.dʒoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.dʒəʊ / (BrE)
(1884–1948) Japanese army officer and Prime Minister during World War II, who authorized the attack on Pearl Harbor and led Japan until his arrest and execution as a war criminal.
Japanese WWII leader, initiated Pearl Harbor attack

Examples

Tojo played a central role in shaping Japan's military strategy during World War II.
The decision to attack Pearl Harbor was made under Tojo's leadership.
After the war, Tojo was tried and executed for his crimes.

Etymology

Japanese surname. In this sense, refers to Hideki Tojo.
