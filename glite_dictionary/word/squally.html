squally

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑli / (AmE), / ˈskwɒli / (BrE)
(of weather) characterized by brief periods of strong winds, often with rain or snow.
with short violent wind or rain spells

Examples

The sailors were prepared for a squally night at sea.
A squally wind whipped through the coastal village, rattling windows and doors.
We postponed our hike due to the forecast of squally showers throughout the afternoon.

Synset Examples

a grey squally morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stormy - (of weather) involving storms, severe and turbulent
  • Antonym: calm - (of weather) free from wind or disturbance

2. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑːli / (AmE), / ˈskwɒli / (BrE)
marked by sudden periods of noisy disturbance or commotion
subject to brief noisy commotion

Examples

Their squally relationship made family gatherings unpredictable and tense.
The meeting turned squally when several people began arguing loudly.
After a squally afternoon, the office finally regained its usual quiet.

Synset Examples

a home life that has been extraordinarily squally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

3. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑli / (AmE), / ˈskwɒli / (BrE)
characterized by brief, noisy disturbances or commotion.
prone to noisy commotion

Examples

Their squally argument disrupted the entire office for an hour.
Our weekends used to be squally with the children fighting over toys.
The meeting grew squally as tempers flared and voices rose.

Synset Examples

a home life that has been extraordinarily squally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; calm

Etymology

From 'squall' (a sudden, loud outburst) + -y.
