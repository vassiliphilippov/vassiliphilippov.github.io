froth

1. NOUN

/ frɑθ / (AmE), / frɒθ / (BrE)
A mass of small bubbles that forms on or in a liquid, often produced by agitation or fermentation.
a mass of bubbles on a liquid

Examples

There was a layer of froth at the top of his cappuccino.
The waves crashed onto the shore, leaving behind a white froth.
She skimmed the froth off the boiling jam to keep it clear.

Synset Examples

the beer had a thick head of foam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid

Etymology

From Old Norse 'frotha' (foam), related to Old English 'froþ' (foam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09305630-n

2. VERB

/ frɑθ / (AmE), / frɒθ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to form a mass of small bubbles on the surface; to become covered with froth or foam.
to form bubbles or foam

Examples

The bartender made the milk froth before pouring it into the coffee.
As the sea grew rougher, the waves began to froth along the shore.
If you shake the bottle vigorously, the liquid will froth up quickly.

Synset Examples

The river was foaming
sparkling water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - to produce or cover with mass of small bubbles; to foam
