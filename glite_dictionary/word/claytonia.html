Claytonia

1. NOUN

/ kleɪˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / kleɪˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of mainly North American succulent herbs with white or pink flowers, commonly known as spring beauties
genus of North American succulent herbs

Examples

Many wildflower enthusiasts search for Claytonia each spring because of its delicate blooms.
The leaves of Claytonia are edible and sometimes used in salads by foragers.
In the shaded woods, patches of Claytonia carpeted the forest floor with tiny pink and white flowers.

Etymology

Named after John Clayton (1694–1773), a colonial Virginia botanist.

2. NOUN

/ kleɪˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / kleɪˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly North American succulent herbs with white or pink flowers, usually found in terminal racemes.
genus of North American succulent herbs

Examples

Claytonia species are often referred to as spring beauty in North America.
The garden's alpine section features several varieties of Claytonia.
Researchers observed that Claytonia thrives in moist woodland habitats.

Etymology

Named after John Clayton (1694–1773), English-born colonial Virginia botanist.
