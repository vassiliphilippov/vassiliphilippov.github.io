Betula pubescens

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə pjuːˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːlə pjuːˈbɛsɛns / (BrE)
A species of birch native to Europe, recognized by its dull white to pale brown bark and somewhat drooping, hairy branches; also known as the downy birch.
a European species of birch tree

Examples

The Betula pubescens is commonly found in moist woodlands across northern Europe.
Gardeners often plant Betula pubescens for its tolerance to wet soils and ornamental value.
Unlike the silver birch, Betula pubescens has more drooping and hairy branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downy birch - common name for Betula pubescens

Etymology

From Latin: 'Betula' (birch) and 'pubescens' (downy, referring to its hairy shoots).
