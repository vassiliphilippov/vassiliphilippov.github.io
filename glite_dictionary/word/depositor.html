depositor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪtə / (BrE)
a person or organization that places money in a bank or similar financial institution for safekeeping
person depositing money in a bank

Examples

Every depositor is required to fill out a form to open a new savings account.
The bank guarantees the safety of all funds held by each depositor.
If a bank fails, government insurance often protects the money of the depositors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: withdrawer - a person who withdraws money from a bank account
  • Synonym: account holder - a person who has an account at a bank

Etymology

From Latin depositum (that which is deposited) via deposit + -or.
