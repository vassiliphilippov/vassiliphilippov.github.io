ruefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːf(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness or regret, especially because of a mistake or disappointment.
sadness or regret over a mistake

Examples

There was a distinct ruefulness in his smile as he recounted the story of his missed opportunity.
Her ruefulness was evident when she apologized for the misunderstanding.
A note of ruefulness crept into his voice as he remembered the decision he now regretted.

Synset Examples

he drank to drown his sorrows
he wrote a note expressing his regret
to his rue, the error cost him the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something that happened
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness and pleasure

Etymology

From 'rue' (to regret) + '-ful' + '-ness'. 'Rue' from Old English 'hrēowan' (to make sorry).
