alexander

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændər / (AmE), / ˌalɪɡˈzandə / (BrE)
A European herb (Smyrnium olusatrum) resembling celery, often found in British coastal regions and sometimes cultivated as a potherb.
European herb similar to celery

Examples

The ancient Romans used alexander as a vegetable and seasoning in their cooking.
Wild alexander now grows abundantly along the British coastline.
She added chopped alexander to the soup for a distinctive flavor.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'alexandrum', referring to parsley of Alexandria.
