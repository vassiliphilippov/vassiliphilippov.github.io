lutfisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊtˌfɪsk / (AmE), / ˈlʊtˌfɪsk / (BrE)
a traditional Scandinavian dish of dried cod soaked in a lye solution until it develops a gelatinous consistency, then boiled
Scandinavian dish of lye-soaked cod

Examples

Every Christmas, my grandmother prepares lutfisk according to our family's Scandinavian recipe.
Some people love the unique texture of lutfisk, while others find it too unusual.
In Sweden, serving lutfisk with potatoes and white sauce is a holiday tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lutefisk - Scandinavian dish of dried cod prepared with lye

Etymology

From Swedish 'lutfisk', from 'lut' (lye) + 'fisk' (fish).
