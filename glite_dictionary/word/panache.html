panache

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnæʃ / (AmE), / pəˈnaʃ / (BrE)
a plume of feathers, especially one worn on a helmet as an ornament
feathered plume on a helmet

Examples

The knight rode proudly, his helmet adorned with a bright red panache.
Her costume included a golden helmet topped with an extravagant panache.
The general's panache signified his rank during the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plume - an ornamental feather or tuft of feathers

Etymology

From French 'panache' (tuft of feathers), from Italian 'pennacchio', from Latin 'pinna' (feather).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈnæʃ / (AmE), / pəˈnaʃ / (BrE)
distinctive and stylish confidence, flair, or elegance; a showy manner
distinctive stylish flair or elegance

Examples

She handled the situation with remarkable panache, impressing everyone in the room.
The chef presented each dish with such panache that dining became a memorable experience.
He delivered his speech with panache, captivating the audience from start to finish.

Synset Examples

he wooed her with the confident dash of a cavalry officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flair - a natural ability to do something well with style

Etymology

From French 'panache' (a plume, a tuft), originally relating to the plume on a helmet, then figuratively 'flamboyant style'.
