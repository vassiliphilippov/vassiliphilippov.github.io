Agastache foeniculum

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæstəki foʊˈnɪkjələm / (AmE), / əˈɡæstəkɪ fəʊˈnɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
A much-branched North American herb with a fragrance similar to fennel, commonly known as anise hyssop, used in gardens and herbal teas.
anise-scented North American herb

Examples

The garden was filled with the pleasant aroma of Agastache foeniculum in bloom.
Many pollinators, such as bees and butterflies, are attracted to Agastache foeniculum.
Herbalists use Agastache foeniculum leaves to make a flavorful tea.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Agastache' (Greek 'aga' much + 'stachys' ear of grain) and Latin 'foeniculum' (fennel).
