cenobitical

1. ADJ

/ ˌsiːnəˈbɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəˈbɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to cenobites or their practice of communal monastic living
relating to communal monastic life

Examples

The monks adhered to a cenobitical lifestyle, sharing all possessions and duties in their abbey.
Historically, cenobitical communities were pivotal in spreading monastic ideals across medieval Europe.
She devoted her thesis to the analysis of cenobitical practices among early Christian monastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cenobitic - of or pertaining to communal monastic life
  • Antonym: eremitic - of or relating to hermits or solitary monastic life

Etymology

From Late Latin 'coenobiticus', from Greek 'koinobion' (communal life).
