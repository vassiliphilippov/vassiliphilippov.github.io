impressionable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person) easily influenced or affected by others, especially at a young age.
easily influenced or affected

Examples

Teenagers can be especially impressionable during challenging times.
The movie was banned to protect impressionable children from violent content.
At that impressionable age, she looked up to her older sister for guidance.

Synset Examples

an impressionable youngster
an impressionable age
a waxy mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or affected by something
  • Antonym: resistant - not easily influenced or affected by something

Etymology

From French 'impressionnable', from 'impression' (from Latin 'impressio').
