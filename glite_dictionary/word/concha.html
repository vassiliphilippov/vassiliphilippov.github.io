concha

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tʃə, ˈkɑŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kə, ˈkɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A structure resembling a shell in shape, especially in the ear or nose.
shell-shaped anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the patient's nasal concha before proceeding with the operation.
Swelling of the auricular concha can affect hearing.
The inferior concha is one of the three turbinates in the nasal cavity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbinates - curved bony structures in the nasal cavity

Etymology

From Latin concha (shell), originally from Ancient Greek konkhē (shell).
