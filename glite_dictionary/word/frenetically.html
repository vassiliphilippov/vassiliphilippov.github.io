frenetically

1. ADV

/ frəˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / frəˈnɛtɪkəli / (BrE)
In a wildly energetic, hurried, or frantic manner.
in a frenzied or frantic way

Examples

She typed frenetically in an effort to meet the project deadline.
The reporters frenetically chased the celebrity through the crowded street.
Children ran frenetically around the park, laughing and shouting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantically - in a hurried, panicked, or desperate way

Etymology

From French frénétique, from Latin phreneticus, from Greek phrenētikos (delirious, frenzied).
