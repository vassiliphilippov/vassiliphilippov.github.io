scandalmongering

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spreading scandalous or malicious gossip, especially with the intention of causing harm or excitement
spreading malicious gossip

Examples

The tabloid was regularly accused of scandalmongering and sensationalism.
He lost many friends due to his involvement in scandalmongering at the office.
Social media can sometimes become a platform for widespread scandalmongering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving details that are not confirmed as true
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07150335-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæn.dəlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of writing or media) involving or characterized by the search for and sensational presentation of scandal.
reporting or spreading scandal

Examples

The newspaper was accused of scandalmongering rather than reporting the facts objectively.
She grew tired of the scandalmongering coverage in celebrity magazines.
His scandalmongering articles often stirred up unnecessary controversy among readers.

Synset Examples

sensational journalistic reportage of the scandal
yellow press

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalist - presenting stories in a shocking or exaggerated way
  • Synonym: yellow journalism - journalism that is based upon sensationalism and crude exaggeration

Etymology

From 'scandal' + 'monger' (from Old English 'mangere', a dealer or trader).
