to outvote

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈvoʊt / (AmE), / aʊtˈvəʊt / (BrE)
to defeat someone or a group by receiving more votes than them
defeat by having more votes

Examples

Despite strong opposition, the board members outvoted the proposal's critics.
If we all show up, we can outvote the other side and pass the motion.
The student council outvoted the teachers on the new event policy last year.

Synset Examples

The Democrats outvoted the Republicans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outnumber - be more numerous than
  • Synonym: outvoted - be defeated in a vote by a majority (past participle form)

Etymology

From the prefix 'out-' meaning 'more than' + 'vote'.
