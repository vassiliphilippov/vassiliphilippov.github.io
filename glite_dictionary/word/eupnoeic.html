eupnoeic

1. ADJ

/ juːpˈniːɪk / (AmE), / juːpˈniːɪk / (BrE)
breathing normally; able to pass air in and out of the lungs without difficulty
breathing normally
medical

Examples

After surgery, the patient remained eupnoeic throughout her recovery.
The doctor noted that the child was alert and eupnoeic during the examination.
Healthy adults are typically eupnoeic at rest, with no signs of respiratory distress.

Synset Examples

the boy was disappointed to find only skeletons instead of living breathing dinosaurs
the heavy-breathing person on the telephone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dyspnoeic - having difficulty breathing

Etymology

From Greek 'eupnoos' (breathing well), from 'eu-' (well) + 'pnoē' (breath).
