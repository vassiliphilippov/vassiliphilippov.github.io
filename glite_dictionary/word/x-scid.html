X-SCID

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛks ˌɛs ˈsiː ˌaɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛks ˌɛs ˈsiː ˌaɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
A severe combined immunodeficiency disease in male children, caused by a mutation of a gene on the X chromosome that impairs T cell development and function.
genetic immune system disorder (X-linked SCID)
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the infant with X-SCID after he failed to respond to routine vaccinations.
Gene therapy offers hope for children born with X-SCID by repairing the defective gene.
Early detection of X-SCID is crucial for effective treatment and survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severe combined immunodeficiency - a group of disorders resulting in a severely weakened immune system

Etymology

Acronym of 'X-linked severe combined immunodeficiency'.
