schmaltz

1. NOUN

/ ʃmɑlts / (AmE), / ʃmɑːlts / (BrE)
excessive sentimentality, especially in art, music, or speech; overly emotional or mawkish expression
excessive sentimentality, especially in art or music

Examples

The film was criticized for its schmaltz and over-the-top emotional scenes.
Sometimes a little schmaltz is just what you need at the holidays.
His music is full of schmaltz, appealing to those who love a sentimental tune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive expression of feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Synonym: mawkishness - excessively sentimental, emotional, or sappy behavior or expression

Etymology

From Yiddish שמאַלץ (shmalts), literally 'fat, grease', used figuratively for sentimentality.

2. NOUN

/ ʃmɑlts / (AmE), / ʃmɑːlts / (BrE)
excessive sentimentality or emotion, especially in music, literature, or drama; something considered overly sentimental
excessive sentimentality

Examples

The film was criticized for its schmaltz and lack of realism.
She added just enough schmaltz to make the song touching without being sappy.
Some holiday commercials are filled with so much schmaltz that they feel insincere.

Synset Examples

I prefer stories without all the schmaltz and melodrama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Synonym: melodrama - exaggerated dramatic or emotional behavior
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation and self-control, absence of excessive display

Etymology

From Yiddish 'shmalts' (rendered chicken fat), figuratively used for sentimentality.
