to overreach

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈritʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈriːtʃ / (BrE)
to defeat or outwit (someone) by cleverness or cunning, often by exceeding one's own limits or by trickery
defeat by cleverness or trickery

Examples

He tried to overreach his opponent in the negotiations, but ended up exposing his own weakness.
The chess player successfully overreached her rival with a brilliant series of moves.
If you try to overreach your competitors by cutting corners, it may backfire.

Synset Examples

I beat the traffic
She outfoxed her competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outwit - to defeat by being more clever
  • Synonym: to outsmart - to gain an advantage over by being cleverer
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail by not reaching a necessary standard or by being outmaneuvered

Etymology

From Old English ofer (over) + rǣcan (to reach).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈritʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈriːtʃ / (BrE)
to try to do more than you are capable of, often resulting in failure
to attempt too much and fail

Examples

If you overreach in your training, you could end up injured.
The company overreached by expanding into too many markets at once.
Politicians often overreach when they make promises they can't deliver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overextend - to try to do more than is possible or safe
  • Antonym: to underachieve - to perform below one's potential or expectations

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + reach (to stretch out or extend), first used in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02531534-v
Synset POS: v
