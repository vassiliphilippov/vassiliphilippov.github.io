evidentiary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃɚi / (AmE), / ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃəri / (BrE)
relating to or serving as evidence, especially in a legal context
relating to evidence

Examples

The judge questioned the evidentiary value of the photographs presented in court.
Certain documents were excluded on evidentiary grounds during the trial.
Her lawyer challenged the evidentiary basis of the accusation.

Synset Examples

evidential signs of a forced entry
its evidentiary value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidential - relating to or providing evidence

Etymology

From evidence + -ary, formed as an English derivative to mean 'of or pertaining to evidence'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃəri / (BrE)
relating to or serving as evidence, especially in a court of law
relating to evidence, esp. in law

Examples

The lawyer objected to the document's evidentiary value in the trial.
A new evidentiary procedure was introduced to improve the investigation.
These photographs have no evidentiary significance without proper authentication.

Synset Examples

evidentiary technique
an evidentiary fact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects that help to prove something is true or not true

Etymology

From evidence + -ary, patterned after Latin adjectival suffixes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02734459-a
Synset POS: a
