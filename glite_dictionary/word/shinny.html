shinny

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɪni / (AmE), / ˈʃɪni / (BrE)
to climb awkwardly, especially by grasping with the arms and legs
climb awkwardly, scrambling

Examples

The children tried to shinny up the tree to get a better view.
She managed to shinny over the fence despite her injured arm.
He quickly shinnied up the pole to retrieve the cat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scramble - to climb or move quickly using one's hands and feet
  • Synonym: climb - to go up something, usually using the feet and often the hands

Etymology

Alteration of 'shin' (to climb by gripping with the arms and legs) + -y.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪni / (AmE), / ˈʃɪni / (BrE)
An informal version of hockey played with improvised equipment, often by children on streets, ice, or fields using a ball or can as a puck.
informal street or ice hockey game

Examples

On snowy days, children gather in the park to play shinny on the frozen pond.
We settled disputes in our shinny games by flipping a coin for who got the ball next.
The neighborhood kids improvised sticks and played shinny in the alley until dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shinny (verb) - to climb by alternately gripping the hands and legs

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'shinty', a Scottish game similar to hockey.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469374-n
Synset POS: n
