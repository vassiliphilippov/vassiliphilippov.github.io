Malvales

1. NOUN

/ mælˈveɪliːz / (AmE), / mælˈveɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as Malvaceae, Bombacaceae, Elaeocarpaceae, Sterculiaceae, and Tiliaceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Malvales order contains many economically important plants, including cotton and cacao.
Recent genetic research has clarified the evolutionary relationships within the Malvales.
Botanists study the diverse floral structures found throughout the Malvales.

Etymology

From Latin 'Malva' (mallow) with the taxonomic suffix '-ales' indicating an order of plants.

2. NOUN

/ mælˈveɪliːz / (AmE), / malˈveɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of flowering plants that includes families such as Malvaceae, Bombacaceae, Elaeocarpaceae, Sterculiaceae, and Tiliaceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Malvales include economically important plants such as cotton and cacao.
Recent studies have reshaped the classification of the Malvales order.
Botanists debated the precise boundaries of Malvales for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malvaceae - the mallow family; a family within Malvales

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Malvales', formed from 'Malva' (the mallow plant) and the standard taxonomic suffix '-ales' for plant orders.
