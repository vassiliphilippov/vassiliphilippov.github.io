Zocor

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˌkɔːr / (AmE), / ˈzɒkɔː / (BrE)
A trademarked medicine (simvastatin) used orally to reduce blood cholesterol levels, especially after heart attacks.
cholesterol-lowering medication (simvastatin)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Zocor to help manage my high cholesterol levels.
After his heart attack, Tom was advised to take Zocor daily.
The patient experienced fewer symptoms after starting Zocor as part of his treatment plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simvastatin - cholesterol-lowering drug, generic form of Zocor
