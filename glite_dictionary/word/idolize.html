to idolize

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəlaɪz / (BrE)
to admire or love someone or something very much or too much, often without recognizing their faults
admire or love excessively

Examples

As a child, he used to idolize his older brother and wanted to be just like him.
Many fans idolize movie stars and follow their careers closely.
She idolized her teacher, believing he could do no wrong.

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adore - to love and respect deeply, often with admiration
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike for someone or something

Etymology

From French *idolâtrer*, from Latin *idololatria* (worship of idols), from Greek *eidōlon* (idol) + *latreia* (worship).
