Phoxinus

1. NOUN

/ foʊkˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / fɒkˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the minnow family, commonly found in streams and rivers of Eurasia.
a genus of small freshwater minnows

Examples

The European Phoxinus is often found in clear, fast-flowing streams.
Scientists discovered a new species of Phoxinus in the river.
Many anglers use Phoxinus as bait for larger fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish; commonly used name for fish in the Phoxinus genus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek phoxinos (pointed, sharp), likely referring to the fish's snout shape.
