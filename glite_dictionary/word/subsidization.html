subsidization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.sɪ.dəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.sɪ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of providing financial assistance, typically by a government, to support an industry or activity
the act of giving a subsidy

Examples

The government's subsidization of renewable energy has encouraged more companies to invest in solar power.
Many critics argue that heavy subsidization of agriculture can distort market prices.
International subsidization of developing countries helps support education and healthcare initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidy - a sum of money granted by the government or a public body to assist an industry or business

Etymology

From subsidy + -ation, influenced by subsidize (from Latin subsidium ‘support’).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbsɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbsɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving money or other benefits as a subsidy, or the state of receiving such support
the act of providing a subsidy

Examples

The government's subsidization of the arts helped many theaters avoid closure during tough times.
Farmers rely heavily on subsidization to keep their businesses afloat.
International subsidization practices are often debated in trade negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidy - money given by a government or organization to support an activity

Etymology

From subsidy + -ization, after the verb 'subsidize' (from Latin subsidium ‘assistance’).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
