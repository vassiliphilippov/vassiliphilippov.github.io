Pyrrhula

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈruːlə / (AmE), / pɪˈrʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, stout-billed passerine birds known as bullfinches, found in Europe and Asia.
genus of birds (bullfinches)

Examples

The Eurasian Pyrrhula is easily recognized by its distinctive coloring.
Researchers observed several members of the Pyrrhula genus during their field study.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Pyrrhula in the wild due to its attractive plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfinch - a small songbird, Pyrrhula pyrrhula, of the finch family

Etymology

From New Latin Pyrrhula, from Latin pyrrhula (bullfinch).
