destination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the ultimate goal or purpose that someone intends to achieve or that something is meant to accomplish
the goal or purpose aimed for

Examples

For her, becoming a doctor was the destination that justified all her hard work.
Our strategy needs to be clear about the destination we want to reach as a company.
In the pursuit of knowledge, the journey is often as important as the destination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result
  • Synonym: objective - something you are trying to achieve
  • Antonym: means - a method or way of doing something, as opposed to the end or goal

Etymology

From Latin destinatio (a determination, purpose), from destinare (to determine, intend).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the place to which someone or something is going or being sent, especially as the end of a journey
the place someone is going to

Examples

After a long flight, we finally reached our destination in Paris.
Make sure your luggage is labeled with your destination to avoid any confusion.
The train's final destination is the central station downtown.

Synset Examples

a crowd assembled at the finish
he was nearly exhausted as their destination came into view

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result, often figuratively used for destination
  • Antonym: origin - the place where something starts or begins, the opposite of destination

Etymology

From Latin destinatio (determination, appointment), from destinare (to make firm, establish).
