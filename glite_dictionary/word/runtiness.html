runtiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌnti.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being unusually small or undersized, especially in comparison to others of the same kind
smallness of stature or size

Examples

The runtiness of the puppy made it stand out among its siblings.
Despite his runtiness, he was quick and full of energy on the field.
She worried that the plant's runtiness would affect its chances of survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size or amount
  • Antonym: largeness - the quality or state of being large

Etymology

From 'runt' (a small or stunted animal, especially one of a litter) + '-iness' (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).
