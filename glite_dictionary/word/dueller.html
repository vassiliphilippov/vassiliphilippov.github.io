dueller

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːələr / (AmE), / ˈdjuːələ / (BrE)
a person who participates in a duel, especially one involving weapons and formal rules
person who fights in a duel

Examples

At dawn, the two duellers faced each other across the misty field.
Stories of famous duellers were popular in 19th-century literature.
The code of honor required the dueller to accept the challenge without hesitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duelist - a person who fights duels (alternate spelling, especially American)

Etymology

From 'duel' (from Latin 'duellum', meaning 'war' or 'fight between two'), plus the agent suffix '-er'.
