Pholas

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊləs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks of the family Pholadidae, known as piddocks, which bore into wood, clay, or rock.
genus of burrowing marine bivalves

Examples

The Pholas is well known for boring into soft rock along shorelines.
Scientists collected several specimens of Pholas during the marine survey.
Early naturalists observed the unique behavior of the Pholas as it burrowed into wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piddock - A burrowing marine bivalve mollusk of the family Pholadidae

Etymology

From Greek pholas, meaning 'stone-boring (mollusk)' or 'lurker in holes'.
