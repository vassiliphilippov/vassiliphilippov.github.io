Scipio Africanus

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɪpi.oʊ ˌæfrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈskɪpɪəʊ ˌæfrɪˈkɑːnəs / (BrE)
(circa 237–183 BC) A prominent Roman general who led the invasion of Carthage in the Second Punic War and defeated Hannibal at the Battle of Zama.
famed Roman general of Second Punic War

Examples

Scipio Africanus is credited with defeating Hannibal at the decisive Battle of Zama.
Historians regard Scipio Africanus as one of Rome's greatest military commanders.
The strategies of Scipio Africanus are still studied in military academies today.

Etymology

Latin: Scipio, family name; Africanus, title meaning 'the African'; gained after victory in Africa.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɪpioʊ ˌæf.rɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪpɪəʊ ˌæf.rɪˈkɑː.nəs / (BrE)
(circa 237–183 BC) A Roman general who led the invasion of Carthage during the Second Punic War and defeated Hannibal at the Battle of Zama.
Roman general who defeated Hannibal

Examples

Scipio Africanus is often credited with turning the tide of the Second Punic War in Rome's favor.
Historians regard Scipio Africanus as one of the greatest military strategists of ancient times.
After defeating Hannibal at Zama, Scipio Africanus was given the honorary title 'Africanus' by the Roman senate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hannibal - Carthaginian general defeated by Scipio Africanus at Zama

Etymology

Latin name: Publius Cornelius Scipio Africanus. 'Africanus' awarded as an honorific for his victory in Africa.
