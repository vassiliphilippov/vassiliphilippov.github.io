Lyrurus tetrix

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈrʊərʌs ˈtɛtrɪks / (AmE), / lɪˈrʊərʌs ˈtɛtrɪks / (BrE)
A large northern European grouse species, the male of which is mostly black with a distinctive lyre-shaped tail, commonly known as the black grouse.
a large black European grouse

Examples

During their spring display, male Lyrurus tetrix fan their lyre-shaped tails and call loudly.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Lyrurus tetrix in several regions.
The unique courtship dance of Lyrurus tetrix attracts birdwatchers from across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - common name for Lyrurus tetrix, a large northern European grouse

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Lyrurus' (from Greek 'lyra', lyre, alluding to the tail shape) plus species name 'tetrix'.
