to maunder

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔndər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndə / (BrE)
to wander or move aimlessly and without clear purpose
wander aimlessly

Examples

He would often maunder through the fields, lost in his thoughts.
The old dog maundered along the lane, stopping to sniff here and there.
As the afternoon faded, they maundered around the quiet streets with no real destination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without clear purpose or destination

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete word 'maunder' (to beg), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɔndər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndə / (BrE)
to talk in a rambling or aimless manner, often about unimportant matters
talk aimlessly or ramble

Examples

The old man would often maunder on about his childhood for hours.
She began to maunder nervously during the interview, barely making any sense.
He tends to maunder when he's tired, drifting from topic to topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal variant of 'meander', or from Middle English maundren, meaning to beg.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɔndər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndə / (BrE)
to talk or speak in a rambling, aimless, or indistinct way, often in a low voice
to talk aimlessly or indistinctly

Examples

He often maunders on about his childhood, barely making sense.
The old man would maunder to himself as he walked down the street.
She maundered through her story, losing her listeners' attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: mumble - to speak indistinctly so that the words are not clear

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete 'maunder' (to beg), possibly from Middle English 'maund' (basket), from Latin 'mandare' (to entrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01046405-v
Synset POS: v
