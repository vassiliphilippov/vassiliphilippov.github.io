Satureja calamintha officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈraɪ.ə ˌkæləˈmɪnθə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsatjʊˈriːə ˌkæləmˈɪnθə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A mint-scented perennial herb native to central and southern Europe, also known as calamint.
mint-scented European perennial herb

Examples

The garden was filled with fragrant Satureja calamintha officinalis in full bloom.
Herbalists often recommend Satureja calamintha officinalis for its soothing properties.
We identified a cluster of Satureja calamintha officinalis growing wild along the hillside.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Satureja' (a genus of savory plants) and 'calamintha' (from Greek 'kalamínthē', meaning mint), 'officinalis' indicating use in medicine.
