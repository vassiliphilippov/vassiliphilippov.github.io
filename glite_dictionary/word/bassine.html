bassine

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsiːn / (AmE), / bəˈsiːn / (BrE)
a coarse leaf fiber obtained from palmyra palms, chiefly used in making brushes and brooms
coarse plant fiber for brushes and brooms

Examples

The stiff bristles of the old broom were made from bassine.
Some traditional brushes are crafted using bassine to provide extra durability.
She ordered a new doormat woven with bassine fibers for her front porch.

Etymology

From French 'bassine', from Portuguese 'bacina', relating to the palmyra palm.
