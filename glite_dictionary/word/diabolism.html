diabolism

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˈabəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
belief in or worship of devils, especially Satan
belief in or worship of devils

Examples

Stories of diabolism spread during the witch trials of the seventeenth century.
The novel explores themes of diabolism and possession.
Accusations of diabolism were once used to justify harsh punishments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satanism - the worship of Satan or powers regarded as evil

Etymology

From French *diabolisme*, from Late Latin *diabolus* (devil).
