Gardenia

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈdiːniə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈdiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical shrubs and small trees, known for their fragrant white or yellow flowers, native to the Old World
genus of fragrant tropical shrubs

Examples

Several species in the Gardenia genus are prized for their fragrant blooms and glossy leaves.
The Gardenia's striking white flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in many gardens.
Botanists have classified the new shrub as part of the Gardenia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - a genus of shrubs and vines noted for fragrant flowers

Etymology

Named after Alexander Garden (1730–1791), Scottish-born American naturalist.
