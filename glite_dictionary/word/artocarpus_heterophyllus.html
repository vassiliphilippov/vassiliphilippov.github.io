Artocarpus heterophyllus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.toʊˈkɑːr.pəs ˌhɛ.təˈrɑː.fɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təˈkɑː.pəs ˌhɛ.təˈrɒ.fɪl.əs / (BrE)
A tropical tree of South and Southeast Asia, cultivated for its very large edible fruit and seeds, commonly known as the jackfruit tree.
jackfruit tree species

Examples

The Artocarpus heterophyllus is widely grown in India for its massive, delicious fruit.
Researchers studied the nutritional value of seeds from Artocarpus heterophyllus.
The wood of Artocarpus heterophyllus is used in making musical instruments and furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackfruit - A large tropical fruit with a spiky outer skin and sweet, fleshy inside; the fruit of Artocarpus heterophyllus

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Artocarpus' (from Greek 'artos' meaning bread and 'karpos' meaning fruit), and species name 'heterophyllus' (from Greek 'heteros' meaning different, 'phyllon' meaning leaf).
