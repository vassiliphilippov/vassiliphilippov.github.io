tinderbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪndɚˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈtɪndəbɒks / (BrE)
A small container used to hold tinder and other materials for starting a fire, typically used before the invention of matches.
container for tinder to start a fire

Examples

The old explorer carried a tinderbox to light his campfires.
Before matches were invented, every household had a tinderbox.
She found a rusty tinderbox among her grandfather’s possessions.

Etymology

From tinder (combustible material for starting a fire) + box.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪndɚˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈtɪndəbɒks / (BrE)
a situation or place that is likely to become suddenly violent or dangerous, especially because of existing tensions
potentially explosive situation or place

Examples

Political tension in the region turned the city into a tinderbox overnight.
With so many grievances left unresolved, the workplace was a tinderbox waiting for a spark.
During the riots, reporters described the atmosphere downtown as a tinderbox.

Synset Examples

the Balkans are the tinderbox of Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powder keg - a situation or place likely to erupt in conflict or violence

Etymology

Literally, a box for holding tinder; figurative use from the tendency of a tinderbox to easily catch fire.
