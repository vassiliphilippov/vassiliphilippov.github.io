tovarich

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈvɑrɪtʃ / (AmE), / təˈvarɪtʃ / (BrE)
a comrade or companion, especially used among communists in the Soviet Union or Russia
Russian term for comrade, esp. in communism

Examples

He greeted his fellow workers with a loud 'Hello, tovarich!' as he entered the factory.
During the revolution, every party member addressed the others as tovarich.
The novel depicts a Soviet officer who insists on being called tovarich instead of sir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comrade - a companion who shares one's activities or affiliation, especially in a political context

Etymology

From Russian товарищ (tovarishch), meaning 'comrade' or 'companion'.
