curtness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɜːt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rudely brief or abrupt in speech or manner
abrupt or rude brevity in manner

Examples

Her curtness during the meeting caught everyone by surprise.
He apologized for the curtness of his reply, realizing it had seemed rude.
Despite her curtness, her instructions were perfectly clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptness - suddenness or lack of smoothness in manner or action
  • Antonym: politeness - showing good manners and consideration for others

Etymology

From 'curt' + '-ness'. 'Curt' from Latin 'curtus' (shortened, cut short).
