Protoavis

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtoʊəvɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˈtəʊəvɪs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primitive birds from the Triassic period, believed to have lived about 70 million years before Archaeopteryx
extinct primitive bird genus from the Triassic

Examples

Fossils of Protoavis have sparked debate among paleontologists about the origins of birds.
Comparing Protoavis to Archaeopteryx provides insights into avian evolution.
Some scientists question whether Protoavis should truly be classified as a bird.

Etymology

From Greek proto- (first) + avis (bird, Latin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈeɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈeɪvɪs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of Triassic reptiles with some bird-like features, sometimes regarded as one of the earliest avian types
extinct Triassic genus with bird-like features

Examples

The discovery of Protoavis fossils has sparked debates about the origins of birds.
Many paleontologists question whether Protoavis should truly be classified as an early bird.
The skeletal structure of Protoavis includes both reptilian and avian characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'prōto-' (first) + Latin 'avis' (bird).
