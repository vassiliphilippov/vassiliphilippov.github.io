morphallaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔːr.fəˈlæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəˈlæk.sɪs / (BrE)
the regeneration of a body part on a smaller scale, particularly as observed in some invertebrates
a type of small-scale regeneration in biology
medical

Examples

Researchers observed morphallaxis in hydras after the removal of their tentacles.
Some invertebrates rely on morphallaxis to recover from injury rather than growing entirely new organs.
The study describes how morphallaxis differs from other types of regeneration in animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epimorphosis - regeneration involving growth of new tissues from undifferentiated cells

Etymology

From Greek 'morphē' (form) and 'allaxis' (change, exchange).
