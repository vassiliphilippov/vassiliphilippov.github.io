bullace

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.əs / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ɪs / (BrE)
a small wild or partly domesticated Eurasian plum with ovoid fruit growing in clusters
a type of wild Eurasian plum

Examples

The hedgerows were heavy with ripe bullace in early autumn.
She made delicious jam from wild bullace picked on her morning walks.
The bullace tree produces fruit that is smaller than most cultivated plums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damson - a small dark purple plum

Etymology

From Middle English bolas, from Old French beloce (plum).
