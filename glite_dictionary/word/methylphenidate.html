methylphenidate

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəlˈfinɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəlˈfiːnɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
A central nervous system stimulant used to treat attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and narcolepsy.
central nervous system stimulant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methylphenidate to help manage his symptoms of ADHD.
Some students misuse methylphenidate in an attempt to improve concentration during exams.
Long-term use of methylphenidate should be monitored by a healthcare professional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ritalin - trade name for methylphenidate

Etymology

From 'methyl' (chemical group) and 'phenidate' (derived from phenyl and acetate).
