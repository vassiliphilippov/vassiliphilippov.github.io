to occidentalise

1. VERB

/ ɑkˌsɪdəntəˈlaɪz / (AmE), / ɒkˌsɪdəntəˈlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) more Western in character, style, or ideas
make Western in character

Examples

After the 19th century, many countries began to occidentalise their education and legal systems.
The government hoped to occidentalise the city’s infrastructure and social customs.
Some critics argue that attempts to occidentalise traditional art forms may lead to loss of cultural identity.

Synset Examples

The country was Westernized after it opened up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to westernize - to adopt or impose Western customs or ideas

Etymology

From Latin 'occidentalis' (western), from 'occidens' (west), plus the suffix '-ize'.
