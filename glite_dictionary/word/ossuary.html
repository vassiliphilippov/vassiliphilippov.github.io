ossuary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.sjuˌɛɹ.i, ˈɑː.suˌɛɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈɒs.jʊ.ə.ɹi / (BrE)
A container or structure used to hold the bones of the dead.
container for human bones

Examples

The ancient monastery featured an ossuary filled with the remains of monks from centuries past.
After the cemetery was relocated, the bones were respectfully placed in a communal ossuary.
Visitors to the catacombs were astonished by the ornate designs made from skulls in the ossuary.

Etymology

From Latin 'ossuarium', from 'os' (bone).
