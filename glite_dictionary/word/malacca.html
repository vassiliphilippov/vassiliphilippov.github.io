Malacca

1. PROPN

/ məˈlækə / (AmE), / məˈlækə / (BrE)
a state in the southern region of the Malay Peninsula in Malaysia, known as the third smallest Malaysian state
state in Malaysia, southern Malay Peninsula

Examples

Many tourists visit Malacca each year to explore its rich colonial history and vibrant culture.
The river that runs through Malacca is lined with colorful murals and bustling cafes.
Trade in Malacca flourished due to its strategic location on the Strait of Malacca.

Etymology

From Portuguese *Malaca*, derived from Malay *Melaka*, the local name for the region.
