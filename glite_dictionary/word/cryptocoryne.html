cryptocoryne

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈkɔːriːni / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈkɒrɪniː / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen perennial aquatic plants native to tropical Asia, often cultivated in aquariums for their attractive foliage.
genus of aquatic plants native to Asia

Examples

Many aquarists choose cryptocoryne for their tanks due to its hardiness and attractive leaves.
The riverbanks in Malaysia are home to several species of cryptocoryne.
She specializes in cultivating rare cryptocoryne in her botanical collection.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'koryne' (club), referring to the hidden club-shaped spadix.
