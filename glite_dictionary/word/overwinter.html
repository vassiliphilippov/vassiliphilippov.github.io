to overwinter

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈwɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɪntə / (BrE)
to spend or survive through the winter season, especially in a particular place or state
spend or survive the winter

Examples

Many birds overwinter in warmer climates to escape the cold.
The researchers studied how some insects overwinter under tree bark.
The expedition team had to overwinter in the Arctic after their ship was trapped in ice.

Synset Examples

We wintered on the Riviera
Shackleton's men overwintered on Elephant Island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hibernate - to spend the winter in a dormant state

Etymology

From over- + winter; attested since the 17th century.
