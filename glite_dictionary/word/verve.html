verve

1. NOUN

/ vɝv / (AmE), / vɜːv / (BrE)
energy, enthusiasm, or lively style, especially in artistic performance or expression.
energy or lively style

Examples

The pianist played with such verve that the audience was captivated.
Her writing is full of verve and originality.
He tackled every challenge with verve and confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - liveliness and energy
  • Synonym: spirit - enthusiasm and energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - lack of energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From French verve (enthusiasm, spirit), from Latin verba (words).
