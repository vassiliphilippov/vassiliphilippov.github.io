Commelinaceae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛlɪˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kɒˌmɛlɪˈneɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a large and widely distributed family of perennial herbs or climbers, commonly known as spiderworts
family of perennial herbs or spiderworts

Examples

The Commelinaceae include many species that are popular in horticulture for their colorful flowers.
Botanists have studied the global distribution of the Commelinaceae to understand plant evolution.
Tradescantia is a well-known genus within the Commelinaceae family.

Etymology

From the name of Dutch botanists Jan and Caspar Commelin, with Latin botanical suffix -aceae (family).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛlɪˈneɪsi.i / (AmE), / kɒˌmɛlɪˈneɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of mostly perennial herbaceous plants or climbers, widely distributed and including the spiderworts
family of perennial herbs or climbers (spiderworts)

Examples

Several ornamental plants in the Commelinaceae family are popular for their attractive blue flowers.
The Commelinaceae are also known as the spiderwort family.
Botanists have identified over 700 species in the Commelinaceae worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the genus Commelina, named for Dutch botanists Jan and Caspar Commelin.
