brethren

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛðrən / (AmE), / ˈbrɛðrən / (BrE)
The lay members of a male religious order; also used as a plural form of 'brother' in religious contexts.
lay male members of a religious order
archaic

Examples

The friar addressed his brethren with a warm and heartfelt message.
The brethren gathered in the chapel for evening prayers.
Among the brethren, there was a sense of unity and purpose.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brother - a male member of a religious order or community

Etymology

Archaic plural of 'brother', from Old English 'brēþer', plural of 'brōþor'.
