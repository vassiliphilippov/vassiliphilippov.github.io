obstipation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.stəˈpeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒb.stɪˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
severe constipation caused by obstruction of the intestines
severe constipation from intestinal obstruction
medical

Examples

The patient presented with obstipation and severe abdominal distension due to a bowel obstruction.
Chronic obstipation requires urgent medical attention to prevent serious complications.
Physicians must distinguish between simple constipation and obstipation when evaluating abdominal complaints.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: constipation - a condition in which you have difficulty emptying your bowels

Etymology

From Latin obstipare (to block up, stop up) + -ation.
