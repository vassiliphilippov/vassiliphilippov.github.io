Delonix regia

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈloʊ.nɪks ˈriː.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / dɛˈlɒ.nɪks ˈriː.dʒɪə / (BrE)
A species of showy tropical tree native to Madagascar, known for its large clusters of bright scarlet and orange flowers; widely grown in tropical regions as an ornamental plant.
showy tropical tree with red-orange flowers

Examples

The Delonix regia brightens the city streets every summer with its vivid blossoms.
Gardeners value the Delonix regia for its wide canopy and ornamental appeal.
During spring, the Delonix regia is covered in a spectacular display of scarlet flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal poinciana - another name for Delonix regia, a showy tropical tree
  • Synonym: flamboyant - another common name for Delonix regia, the tropical tree

Etymology

From modern Latin (botanical usage): 'Delonix' from Greek 'delos' (conspicuous) + 'onyx' (claw), referring to the clawed petals; 'regia' from Latin (royal).
