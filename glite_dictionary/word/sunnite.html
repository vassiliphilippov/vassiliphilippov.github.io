Sunnite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnaɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Sunni branch of Islam, which accepts the first four caliphs as the rightful successors to Muhammad
member of Sunni branch of Islam

Examples

He identified himself as a Sunnite during the discussion on Islamic traditions.
The Sunnite community observes unique customs and interpretations of religious law.
Many Sunnite scholars have contributed significantly to Islamic philosophy and thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sunni - a member of the largest branch of Islam, Sunni Islam

Etymology

From Arabic 'Sunnī' (adherent of the Sunna) + English suffix '-ite' (denoting adherence).
