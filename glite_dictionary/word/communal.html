communal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːn(ə)l / (BrE)
relating to a local administrative district or community, especially with reference to local government, services, or elections
relating to a local district or community

Examples

The government held communal elections in all provinces last year.
Every town has its own communal council responsible for local affairs.
He attended a meeting of the communal authorities to discuss public projects.

Synset Examples

communal elections in several European countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: municipal - relating to a town or local government area

Etymology

From French 'communal', from Late Latin 'communalis' (of the community).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02711654-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunəl / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəl / (BrE)
shared or used by all members of a group; relating to ownership, use, or action by a community collectively rather than by individuals.
shared by a group or community

Examples

The villagers maintained a communal garden that provided fresh vegetables for everyone.
There was a communal sense of responsibility during the festival preparations.
The team achieved their success through communal effort and cooperation.

Synset Examples

dipping each his bread into a communal dish of stew
a communal settlement in which all earnings and food were shared
a communal effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group
  • Antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing, not shared

Etymology

From Latin 'communalis', from 'commūnis' meaning 'common, shared'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00495057-s
Synset POS: s
