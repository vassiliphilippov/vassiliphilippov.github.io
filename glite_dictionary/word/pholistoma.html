Pholistoma

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlɪstəmə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of straggling herbs native to the southwestern United States, commonly known for their bristly stems and small flowers.
genus of straggling herbs in southwestern U.S.

Examples

Pholistoma can often be found growing in shaded canyons throughout southern California.
Botanists have identified several species within the genus Pholistoma.
The bristly stems of Pholistoma distinguish it from other local wildflowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'pholis' (scale) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to aspects of the plant's floral structure.
