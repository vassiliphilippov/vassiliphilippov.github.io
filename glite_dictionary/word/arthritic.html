arthritic

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈθrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person who is affected by arthritis.
person with arthritis
medical

Examples

The clinic specializes in treatments for arthritics of all ages.
An arthritic may require special care and physical therapy.
She volunteers at a support group for elderly arthritics.

Synset Examples

the hands of an elderly arthritic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints

Etymology

From Greek arthritikos (pertaining to the joints or arthritis).

2. ADJ

/ ɑrˈθrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by arthritis; having symptoms or qualities of arthritis, such as stiffness or pain in the joints.
related to or affected by arthritis
medical

Examples

After years of hard labor, her hands became arthritic and stiff.
He has an arthritic knee that troubles him in cold weather.
The old man walked slowly, his arthritic joints protesting with each step.

Synset Examples

my creaky old joints
rheumy with age and grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritic - a person affected by arthritis

Etymology

From Greek arthritikos (of the joints), from arthron (joint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177339-s
Synset POS: s
