crenated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkriː.neɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkriː.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Having a margin with rounded scallops or notches; especially of leaves or biological cells.
with a rounded, scalloped edge

Examples

The biologist observed that the red blood cells had a crenated appearance under the microscope.
Many oak leaves are described as crenated rather than smooth.
After being placed in a hypertonic solution, the cell membrane became noticeably crenated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serrated - having a jagged or saw-toothed edge

Etymology

From Latin 'crenatus' (notched), from 'crena' (notch).
