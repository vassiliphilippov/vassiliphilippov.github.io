afraid

1. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
used to politely express regret, concern, or a mild apology, especially when giving bad news or contradicting someone
polite way to express regret or bad news

Examples

I'm afraid I can't help you with that problem.
We're afraid your order will be delayed due to high demand.
She said, 'I'm afraid that's not possible at the moment.'

Synset Examples

I'm afraid I won't be able to come
he was afraid he would have to let her go
I'm afraid you're wrong
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00546213-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
feeling fear or worry about a possible danger, event, or situation.
filled with fear or worry

Examples

She felt afraid when she heard the strange noise outside the window.
Many children are afraid of the dark and need a night light to sleep.
He was too afraid to speak up during the meeting.

Synset Examples

afraid even to turn his head
suddenly looked afraid
afraid for his life
afraid of snakes
afraid to ask questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scared - feeling frightened or worried
  • Antonym: brave - showing no fear; courageous

Etymology

From Old English 'afrǣdan', from 'a-' (intensive prefix) + 'frǣdan' (to take counsel, devise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00078253-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
not wanting or willing to do something; having feelings of aversion, reluctance, or hesitation.
unwilling or reluctant

Examples

He was afraid to ask for help, even though he needed it.
Many people are afraid of trying new things at first.
She felt afraid to speak her mind in the meeting.

Synset Examples

afraid of hard work
afraid to show emotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling or hesitant to do something
  • Antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something

Etymology

From Old English 'āfrǣdan' (to frighten), influenced by Old French 'afrei' (afraid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01296519-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
worried or concerned about something, often because you think it may have a bad result; feeling fear, anxiety, or insecurity about something.
feeling fear or worry

Examples

She looked afraid when she heard the thunder outside.
I'm afraid I can't come to your party this weekend.
Many children are afraid of speaking in public.

Synset Examples

She was afraid that I might be embarrassed
terribly afraid of offending someone
I am afraid we have witnessed only the first phase of the conflict

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scared - feeling fear or fright
  • Antonym: confident - feeling certain and unafraid

Etymology

From Old English *afǣred* (frightened), past participle of *afǣran* (to frighten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00546451-s
Synset POS: s
