Denis Diderot

1. PROPN

/ dəˈni diˈdəˌroʊ / (AmE), / dəˈniː diˈdɛrəʊ / (BrE)
(1713–1784) French philosopher and writer, a leading figure of the Enlightenment and the principal editor of the Encyclopédie.
French philosopher and editor of the Encyclopédie

Examples

Denis Diderot was instrumental in shaping the ideas of the French Enlightenment.
The Encyclopédie, edited by Denis Diderot, became a cornerstone of modern knowledge.
Many philosophers of the eighteenth century, including Denis Diderot, championed reason and progress.
