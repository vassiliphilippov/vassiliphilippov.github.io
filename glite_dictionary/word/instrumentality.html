instrumentality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəˌmɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊmənˈtælɪti / (BrE)
a means, tool, or device used to achieve a particular purpose or end
means or device for achieving an end

Examples

The microscope became the essential instrumentality for advances in biological research.
Money is often just an instrumentality to achieve one's larger goals.
Legislation serves as the instrumentality through which governments implement public policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: means - method, tool, or way of achieving something
  • Synonym: device - a thing made for a particular purpose, especially mechanical or electronic

Etymology

From Medieval Latin instrumentalitas, from Latin instrumentum (tool, apparatus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.strə.menˈtæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strə.menˈtæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)

government agency or organ

Examples

The city established a new instrumentality for environmental protection.
Several governmental instrumentalities oversee public health policies in the country.
He worked for an instrumentality responsible for housing development.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəˌmɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrjuːmənˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of serving as a means or agency for achieving a purpose.
quality of being useful for a purpose

Examples

The instrumentality of technology in education cannot be overstated.
His instrumentality in negotiating peace was widely recognized by the community.
The success of the project depended on the instrumentality of several key partners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility - the state of being useful or beneficial

Etymology

From Medieval Latin instrumentalis (of an instrument) + -ity.
