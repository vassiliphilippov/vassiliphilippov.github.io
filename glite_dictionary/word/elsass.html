Elsass

1. PROPN

/ ɛlˈzas / (AmE), / ɛlˈzas / (BrE)
the German name for Alsace, a region of northeastern France noted for its wines and unique cultural history
German name for Alsace, French region

Examples

Many traditional dishes in Elsass show a blend of French and German influences.
She visited several famous vineyards in Elsass during her trip to France.
The architecture in Elsass reflects its unique cultural history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alsace - region of northeastern France, known for wines and mixed French-German heritage

Etymology

German, from Old High German 'Ellasaz', meaning 'settlement of the Ill (river)'.
