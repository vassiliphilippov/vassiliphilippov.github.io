Almoravid

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.məˈræ.vɪd / (AmE), / ˌæl.məˈræ.vɪd / (BrE)
a member of a Muslim Berber dynasty that ruled parts of northwestern Africa and Spain from the 11th to the 12th century
member of a Berber Muslim dynasty (11th–12th c.)

Examples

The Almoravid dynasty played a crucial role in the history of medieval Spain and North Africa.
A group of Almoravids invaded the Iberian Peninsula, significantly influencing its culture.
The architectural style introduced by the Almoravid rule can still be seen in Morocco today.

Etymology

From Arabic al-Murābiṭūn (المرابطون), meaning 'those who are garrisoned in a ribat'.
