Acanthus mollis

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəs ˈmɑlɪs / (AmE), / əˌkænθəs ˈmɒlɪs / (BrE)
A species of southern European perennial plant with large, glossy leaves and whitish, purple-veined flowers, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
southern European ornamental plant

Examples

The lush foliage of Acanthus mollis adds dramatic impact to garden borders.
Many classical stone carvings were inspired by the leaves of Acanthus mollis.
Gardeners prize Acanthus mollis for its tall spikes of showy, veined flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear's breeches - common name for Acanthus mollis, widely grown ornamental

Etymology

From Latin: 'acanthus', meaning 'thorn' or 'spine', and 'mollis', meaning 'soft'.
