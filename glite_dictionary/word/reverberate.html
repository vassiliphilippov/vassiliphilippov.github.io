to reverberate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɜrbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of metals or ore) to treat, heat, or refine in a reverberatory furnace.
process in a reverberatory furnace

Examples

The workers reverberate the ore to extract the pure metal.
Copper was commonly reverberated in these types of furnaces during the early industrial era.
To achieve the required purity, the engineer decided to reverberate the sample again.

Synset Examples

reverberate ore

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɜrbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be repeated several times as it is reflected off different surfaces; to echo or resound.
(of a sound) to echo or resound

Examples

The sound of the bell reverberated through the vast cathedral, lingering in the air.
Her laughter reverberated around the empty room, making it feel less lonely.
Thunder reverberated throughout the valley, startling the hikers with its intensity.

Synset Examples

the hall resounded with laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to be repeated or heard again as a reflection of sound
  • Synonym: to resound - to fill a place with sound; to echo loudly

Etymology

From Latin reverberatus, past participle of reverberare (to strike back).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝːbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of sound, a noise, or an effect) to be reflected, echoed, or bounced back from a surface.
be reflected or echoed back

Examples

The sound of thunder reverberated through the canyon, making the ground tremble.
Her footsteps reverberated off the empty hallway walls, creating a deep echo.
In the auditorium, every word reverberated clearly, allowing the audience to hear perfectly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - (of sound) to be reflected back and heard again

Etymology

From Latin reverberare (to beat back), from re- (again) + verberare (to lash, beat).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈvɜrbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of an event or action) to have a long-lasting or continuing effect; to influence situations or people over a period of time.
have a continuing effect

Examples

The consequences of the policy change still reverberate throughout the community years later.
Her words reverberated in my mind long after the conversation ended.
Decisions made in the past can reverberate into future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resonate - to produce a lasting or strong effect

Etymology

5. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝː.bɚ.eɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜː.bə.reɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound, heat, light, or shock wave) to be repeatedly reflected, echoed, or sent back from a surface.
to echo or be reflected repeatedly

Examples

The sound of the explosion reverberated through the narrow streets for several seconds.
Sunlight reverberated off the glass buildings, making the street almost blindingly bright.
The shockwaves from the blast reverberated across the valley, rattling windows miles away.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to reflect a sound; similar meaning

Etymology

From Latin reverberatus, past participle of reverberare (to strike back, to reflect).
