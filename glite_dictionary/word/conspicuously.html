conspicuously

1. ADV

/ kənˈspɪkjuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈspɪkjuːəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is clearly visible or attracts notice, often because it stands out.
In a noticeably prominent way

Examples

The artist's signature was conspicuously placed at the bottom of the painting.
She conspicuously avoided answering questions about her past.
The warning signs were conspicuously absent from the dangerous area.

Synset Examples

the new car was prominently displayed in the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominently - in a way that is easily seen or noticed
  • Antonym: inconspicuously - in a way that does not attract attention

Etymology

From 'conspicuous' (visible, noticeable) + '-ly'. 'Conspicuous' from Latin conspicuus (visible).

2. ADV

/ kənˈspɪk.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / kənˈspɪk.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that clearly attracts attention or is easily noticed.
in a noticeable or attention-attracting way

Examples

The award was conspicuously displayed in the lobby for everyone to see.
He conspicuously avoided answering the journalist's question during the interview.
Her generosity was conspicuously absent when it came time to donate to the cause.

Synset Examples

there have been plenty of general declarations about willingness to meet and talk, but conspicuously no mention of time and place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily seen or understood
  • Antonym: discreetly - in a careful, unobtrusive way to avoid drawing attention

Etymology

From 'conspicuous' (from Latin 'conspicuus' meaning 'visible') + '-ly'.
