gyration

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒʌɪˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of moving in a circle or spiral, especially as a repeated or continuous motion
circular or spiral movement

Examples

The gyration of the dancers on stage was mesmerizing.
A sudden gyration of the machine startled the workers nearby.
Satellites maintain their orbit through a constant gyration around the Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the act of turning around a central point
  • Synonym: revolution - a single complete turn (as of a wheel)
  • Antonym: stillness - the absence of movement or motion

Etymology

From Latin 'gyratio', from 'gyrare' (to turn round).

2. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒaɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a single complete turn or rotation, especially around an axis or orbit
a complete turn or rotation

Examples

The dancer completed a perfect gyration during her performance.
Each gyration of the ferris wheel gave riders a new view of the park.
The scientist recorded the gyration of the satellite as it orbited the planet.

Synset Examples

the plane made three rotations before it crashed
the revolution of the earth about the sun takes one year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the act of turning around a central point or axis
  • Synonym: revolution - a complete circular movement around a point or axis

Etymology

From Latin gyratio (a circling), from gyrare (to turn in a circle).
