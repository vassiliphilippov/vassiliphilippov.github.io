Arilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈærɪləs / (AmE), / ˈærɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of assassin bugs in the family Reduviidae, known for their predatory habits.
genus of assassin bugs (Reduviidae)

Examples

The wheel bug, a member of the genus Arilus, is common in the southeastern United States.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Arilus genus during their field studies.
Many Arilus species are considered beneficial because they prey on pest insects.

Etymology

From New Latin, used in zoological classification.
