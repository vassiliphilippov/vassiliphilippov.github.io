schmoose

1. VERB

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
to chat casually and in a friendly or informal manner, often to gain favor or network
chat informally or socialize

Examples

The guests began to schmoose over drinks before dinner was served.
He loves to schmoose with colleagues at networking events.
They spent hours schmoosing at the café, sharing stories and laughs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: converse - engage in conversation

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmuesn* (to chat, converse), from Hebrew *shĕmûʿāh* (report, rumor).

2. NOUN

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
an informal conversation, especially one that is friendly or gossipy
informal conversation; friendly chat
informal

Examples

They had a long schmoose over coffee before going back to work.
The two old friends enjoyed a schmoose about their families and shared memories.
Let's have a quick schmoose before the meeting starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schmooze - to chat in an informal and friendly way; an informal conversation
  • Synonym: conversation - talk between two or more people
  • Synonym: discussion - an act of talking about something with another person or a group

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmues* (chat, talk), itself from Hebrew *shmu'ot* (things heard, news).
