Miltonia

1. NOUN

/ mɪlˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / mɪlˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids, comprising several epiphytic species and natural hybrids, native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Miltonia orchid is known for its striking, pansy-like flowers and sweet fragrance.
Botanists recently discovered a rare species within the Miltonia genus in the Atlantic Forest.
Many orchid enthusiasts cultivate Miltonia plants for their beautiful blooms and unique hybrids.

Etymology

Named after Earl Fitzwilliam Milton, English nobleman and orchid enthusiast, with the suffix -ia denoting genus in Latin.

2. NOUN

/ mɪlˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / mɪlˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids, mainly found in the Brazilian Atlantic Forest, including several epiphytic species and natural hybrids.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Miltonia orchid is prized for its striking flowers and fragrant scent.
Botanists discovered a new natural hybrid within the Miltonia genus in Brazil.
Many collectors cultivate Miltonia species for their unique coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oncidium - genus of related tropical American orchids sometimes called 'dancing-lady orchids'

Etymology

Named in honor of Charles Wentworth-Fitzwilliam, 5th Earl Fitzwilliam, whose courtesy title was Viscount Milton.
