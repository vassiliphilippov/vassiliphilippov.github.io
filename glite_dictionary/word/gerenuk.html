gerenuk

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɛnək / (AmE), / ˈɡɛrənʊk / (BrE)
a slender East African antelope with a long neck and backward-curving horns, known for its ability to stand upright to browse on trees
East African antelope with long neck

Examples

A gerenuk can reach leaves on high branches by standing upright on its hind legs.
We spotted a group of gerenuks grazing in the savanna during our safari.
The gerenuk is sometimes called the giraffe gazelle because of its unusually long neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running, deerlike hoofed mammal native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Somali *gáránúug*, its local name.
