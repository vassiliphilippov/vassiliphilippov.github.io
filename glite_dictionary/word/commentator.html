commentator

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːm.ənˌteɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌteɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who writes or speaks to report and analyze news or current events
writer or speaker on current events

Examples

The political commentator wrote a detailed analysis of the upcoming election.
As a renowned commentator, she explains global events in her weekly column.
The magazine hired a new commentator to cover economic news and trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyst - a person who analyzes information or events

Etymology

From Latin 'commentator' (interpreter, annotator), from 'commentari' (to comment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mənˌteɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌteɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who provides expert analysis or explanation, especially on radio or television, about events or topics as they happen
expert who comments on events

Examples

The sports commentator gave insight into each team's strategy during the match.
Political commentators discussed potential outcomes of the election live on TV.
She became a well-known commentator for her in-depth coverage of international affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyst - a person who analyzes or gives opinions on something

Etymology

From Latin 'commentator' (interpreter), from 'commentari' (to comment).

Synset POS: n
