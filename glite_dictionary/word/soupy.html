soupy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuːpi / (AmE), / ˈsuːpi / (BrE)
thick or dense in consistency or appearance, like that of soup; resembling soup in texture.
thick or dense like soup

Examples

The air was so humid and dense that it felt almost soupy.
After hours of rain, the field became soupy and impossible to walk through.
A soupy fog descended on the city, obscuring everything from sight.

Synset Examples

a soupy fog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thick - having a relatively large distance between opposite sides; not thin
  • Antonym: watery - containing too much water; thin in consistency

Etymology

From 'soup' + '-y', a common English adjectival suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsuːpi / (AmE), / ˈsuːpi / (BrE)
overly sentimental or insincerely emotional, especially in art, writing, or music
overly sentimental or emotional (informal)
informal

Examples

The movie's ending was so soupy that it made half the audience groan.
Her speech became soupy and melodramatic, losing its original sincerity.
Critics found the novel soupy and manipulative, full of cheap emotional tricks.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maudlin - tearfully or excessively sentimental
  • Synonym: schmaltzy - excessively sentimental, especially in art or music
  • Synonym: slushy - overly sentimental or emotional in writing or speech
  • Antonym: unsentimental - not emotional or sentimental
