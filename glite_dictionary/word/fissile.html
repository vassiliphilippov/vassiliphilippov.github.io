fissile

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪsaɪl / (BrE)
(of a material or atom) capable of undergoing nuclear fission when struck by a neutron, usually releasing energy and neutrons.
capable of undergoing nuclear fission

Examples

Uranium-235 is a fissile isotope used in nuclear reactors and weapons.
Scientists search for fissile materials to ensure nuclear safety and control.
Only certain heavy nuclei are fissile and suitable for sustaining a chain reaction.

Synset Examples

a fissionable nucleus
fissionable material

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissionable - capable of undergoing nuclear fission

Etymology

From Latin 'fissilis', from 'findere' meaning 'to split'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪsaɪl / (BrE)
Capable of being split or divided easily along natural grain or planes of weakness, especially in crystals or wood.
easily split along grain or planes

Examples

The mineral is fissile and breaks cleanly along flat surfaces.
Some types of shale are highly fissile, making them easy to split into thin layers.
This wood is too fissile for use in building strong furniture.

Synset Examples

fissile crystals
fissile wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splittable - capable of being split

Etymology

From Latin fissilis (that may be split), from fissus, past participle of findere (to split).

Synset POS: a
