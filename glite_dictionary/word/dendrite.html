dendrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnˌdraɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛnˌdraɪt / (BrE)
a short branched extension of a nerve cell, which conducts electrical signals toward the cell body
branching neuron extension
medical

Examples

The dendrite receives information from neighboring neurons and transmits it to the cell body.
Damage to a neuron’s dendrites can affect brain function and communication between cells.
Researchers observed new dendrite growth in response to learning and memory exercises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: axon - the long process of a neuron that conducts impulses away from the cell body

Etymology

From Greek dendritēs (of a tree), from dendron (tree), referring to the branched appearance.
