slaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
a person who is engaged in the business of buying and selling slaves
person engaged in slave trade

Examples

The ship was manned by a notorious slaver who profited from human misery.
During the 18th century, a slaver could make a fortune on a single voyage.
Abolitionists fought to bring slavers to justice for their crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave trader - a person who buys and sells slaves

Etymology

From slave + -er (agent noun suffix), meaning one who trades slaves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10629717-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
a person who owns or deals in slaves
person who holds slaves

Examples

The plantation owner was a notorious slaver in the region.
Historians have documented the lives of many slavers during the 18th century.
The abolition of slavery ended the power once held by the slaver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaveholder - a person who owns slaves
  • Antonym: abolitionist - a person who advocates ending slavery

Etymology

From slave + -er (agent suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10629612-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈsleɪ.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɑː.və / (BrE)
to allow saliva to dribble or run from the mouth; to drool
dribble saliva from the mouth

Examples

The hungry dog began to slaver at the smell of cooked meat.
The baby would often slaver when she was teething.
His mouth slavered as he stared at the enormous feast before him.

Synset Examples

The baby drooled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - to let saliva flow from the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English slaveren, probably of imitative origin.
