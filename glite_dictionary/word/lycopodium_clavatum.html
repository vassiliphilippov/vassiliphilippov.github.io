Lycopodium clavatum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpoʊ.di.əm kləˈveɪ.təm / (AmE), / lɪ.kəˈpəʊ.di.əm kləˈveɪ.təm / (BrE)
A species of club moss, also known as wolf's-foot clubmoss, stag's-horn clubmoss, and ground pine, found in temperate regions and often used in herbal medicine.
a species of club moss (ground pine)

Examples

Lycopodium clavatum is frequently found growing in moist, acidic soils in northern forests.
The spores of Lycopodium clavatum have been used in traditional medicine and pyrotechnics.
Botanists often study Lycopodium clavatum to understand the evolution of vascular plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'lykos' (wolf) and 'pous' (foot), via Latin 'Lycopodium', and Latin 'clavatum' (club-shaped).
