wrecking

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of demolishing or tearing down a structure completely
demolition of a structure

Examples

The old stadium underwent wrecking last year to make room for a new park.
City officials scheduled the wrecking of the unsafe building.
After months of debate, the historic hotel finally faced wrecking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolition - the act of deliberately destroying a building

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of deliberately causing destruction or ruin, especially of a building or structure
deliberate destruction or ruin

Examples

The wrecking of the old theater made way for a new shopping mall.
After the hurricane, teams began the wrecking of unsafe buildings along the coastline.
Environmentalists protested the wrecking of the historic bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolition - the act of tearing down buildings or structures
  • Antonym: construction - the act of building or creating something
