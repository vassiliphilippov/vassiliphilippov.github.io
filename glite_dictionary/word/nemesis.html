nemesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛmɪsɪs / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the source of someone's downfall or undoing, especially one that cannot be avoided
an agent of downfall or retribution

Examples

For years, his arrogant attitude proved to be his greatest nemesis.
Rising sea levels are a potential nemesis for many coastal cities.
Procrastination is often the nemesis of productivity.

Synset Examples

the bane of my life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bane - a cause of great distress or annoyance
  • Synonym: scourge - a person or thing that causes great trouble or suffering
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or relief

Etymology

From Greek Nemesis (Νέμεσις), the goddess of retribution.
