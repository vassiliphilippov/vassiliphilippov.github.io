Nemesis

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛməsɪs / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) the goddess who enacts divine retribution and vengeance against those who succumb to hubris.
goddess of retribution in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, Nemesis punished mortals who displayed excessive pride or arrogance.
The ancient Greeks believed that Nemesis maintained balance by bringing justice to those who overstepped their bounds.
In classical art, Nemesis is often depicted with a sword and scales, symbolizing her role as a judge.

Etymology

From Greek Némesis (Νέμεσις), meaning 'distribution, retribution'.
