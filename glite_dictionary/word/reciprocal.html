reciprocal

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (BrE)
(of an action or arrangement) given, felt, or done in return by each of two or more parties; mutual.
done in return by each party; mutual

Examples

The two companies signed a reciprocal trade agreement that benefited both sides.
Members of the club enjoy reciprocal privileges at partner clubs worldwide.
A reciprocal relationship is built on trust and mutual respect.

Synset Examples

reciprocal aid
reciprocal trade
mutual respect
reciprocal privileges at other clubs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual - shared by two or more parties
  • Antonym: one-sided - involving only one party; not reciprocal

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way, alternating).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (BrE)
Mathematics: relating to or denoting the multiplicative inverse of a number or function; the quantity which, when multiplied by a given quantity, gives one as the product.
multiplicative inverse; math term

Examples

To find the reciprocal of a fraction, simply exchange its numerator and denominator.
The reciprocal function is fundamental in calculus and algebra.
Multiplying a number by its reciprocal always results in one.

Synset Examples

the reciprocal ratio of a:b is b:a

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse - opposite in effect or nature, often used in mathematics for quantities such that their product is one

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way, alternating).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (BrE)
A hybridization experiment involving two crosses in which the sexes associated with each genotype are reversed, to observe the influence of parental sex on the resulting offspring.
hybridization with reversed parental sexes
medical

Examples

The scientist set up a reciprocal to study the inheritance patterns in the hybrid plants.
By performing a reciprocal, researchers can detect maternal effects in genetic transmission.
The reciprocal revealed that the trait depended on which parent contributed which allele.

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way, alternating).

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (BrE)
(mathematics) a number that, when multiplied by a given number, results in 1; the multiplicative inverse.
multiplicative inverse in mathematics

Examples

The reciprocal of 5 is one-fifth, or 0.2.
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
Each positive number has a unique reciprocal that when multiplied with it gives 1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse - the multiplicative inverse or opposite

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (alternating, returning the same way).

5. NOUN

/ rəˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkl / (BrE)
a number, expression, or concept that is related to another in such a way that their product or combination is one or some constant; the multiplicative inverse of a number.
the inverse of a number, term, or concept

Examples

In mathematics, the reciprocal of 5 is 1/5.
You obtain the reciprocal of a fraction by swapping its numerator and denominator.
The reciprocal of a nonzero number, when multiplied by the original number, gives one.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse - something that is the opposite or reverse in relation
