to grok

1. VERB

/ ɡɹɑk / (AmE), / ɡɹɒk / (BrE)
to understand something thoroughly and intuitively
understand intuitively or deeply
informal

Examples

It took me a while to truly grok the concept of quantum entanglement.
Some programmers instantly grok new coding paradigms, while others struggle.
Once you grok how the system works, everything becomes much clearer.

Synset Examples

Do you comprehend the meaning of this letter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comprehend - to understand or grasp mentally
  • Synonym: to understand - to perceive the meaning of something
  • Antonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand or interpret correctly

Etymology

Coined by Robert A. Heinlein in his 1961 novel 'Stranger in a Strange Land', from a fictional Martian language.
