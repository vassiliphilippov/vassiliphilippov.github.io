Tai Dam

1. NOUN

/ taɪ ˈdɑm / (AmE), / taɪ ˈdɑːm / (BrE)
a branch of the Tai language family spoken by the Tai Dam people, primarily in northern and parts of southern Vietnam.
language spoken by the Tai Dam people of Vietnam

Examples

Many Tai Dam communities have preserved their traditional language and culture.
The Tai Dam language uses a unique script distinct from other Tai languages.
Linguists study Tai Dam to better understand the history of Southeast Asian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai languages - a family of related languages spoken in Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Tai, the broader language group, and Dam, meaning 'black,' referring to the traditional clothing of the Tai Dam people.
