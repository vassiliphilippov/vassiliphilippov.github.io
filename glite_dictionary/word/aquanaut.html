aquanaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈækwəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈakwənɔːt / (BrE)
A person who spends extended periods living and working underwater, especially for scientific research.
underwater worker or researcher

Examples

The aquanaut remained submerged for over a week to study coral reefs.
As an aquanaut, she collected important data during her underwater mission.
Training to become an aquanaut requires both physical endurance and scientific knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diver - a person who swims or works underwater using special equipment

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'aqua' (water) and Greek 'nautēs' (sailor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈækwəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈakwənɔːt / (BrE)
a person who swims, works, or explores underwater, especially using special breathing equipment such as scuba gear
underwater explorer or worker

Examples

The research team included an experienced aquanaut who guided the underwater survey.
As an aquanaut, she spent weeks living and working in an underwater habitat.
Many aquanauts undergo extensive training before participating in oceanic missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diver - a person who dives into water, often with specialized equipment

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) and Greek 'nautēs' (sailor).
