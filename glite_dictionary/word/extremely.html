extremely

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈstriːmli / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːmli / (BrE)
to a very great degree; very much; to an extreme extent.
to a very great degree

Examples

The new film was extremely popular with audiences around the world.
She was extremely pleased with the results of her exam.
It's extremely dangerous to drive without wearing a seatbelt.

Synset Examples

highly successful
He spoke highly of her
does not think highly of his writing
extremely interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highly - to a high degree or extent
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree or extent
  • Degree Higher: utterly - completely, in an absolute way

Etymology

From 'extreme' + '-ly', modeled after Middle English and Latin origins.

to a very great degree
