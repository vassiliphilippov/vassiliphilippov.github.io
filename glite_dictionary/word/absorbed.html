absorbed

1. ADJ

/ əbˈzɚbd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
(of energy or a substance, especially light, heat, or liquid) taken in or retained without being reflected or transmitted.
taken in without reflection

Examples

Most of the absorbed light is converted to heat within the material.
The sunscreen reduces the amount of UV rays absorbed by the skin.
Certain materials are designed to maximize absorbed energy and minimize reflection.

Synset Examples

the absorbed light intensity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflected - (of light, heat, or energy) sent back from a surface, not absorbed

Etymology

Past participle of 'absorb', from Latin absorbere (to swallow up).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈzɔrbd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
deeply interested or wholly engaged in something; giving all one's attention to something
deeply engrossed or fully attentive

Examples

She was so absorbed in her book that she didn't hear her phone ring.
The children listened with absorbed attention to the storyteller.
While painting, he became completely absorbed in his work.

Synset Examples

that engrossed look of rapt delight
enwrapped in dreams
so intent on this fantastic … narrative that she hardly stirred
rapt with wonder
wrapped in thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrossed - completely occupied by or giving full attention to something
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied

Etymology

Past participle of 'absorb', from Latin 'absorbere' (to swallow up).
