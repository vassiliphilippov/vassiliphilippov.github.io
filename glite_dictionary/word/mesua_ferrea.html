Mesua ferrea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːsuˈɑː fəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌmiːsjʊˈɑː fɛˈriːə / (BrE)
A handsome evergreen tree native to South Asia, grown for its fragrant white flowers and very hard wood.
evergreen South Asian tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The Mesua ferrea is admired for its striking, fragrant blossoms in many Indian gardens.
Wood from the Mesua ferrea is valued for its exceptional hardness and durability.
Botanists often study the medicinal properties of Mesua ferrea leaves and flowers.

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name, with 'Mesua' as the genus and 'ferrea' meaning 'iron-like' in Latin, referring to its hard wood.
