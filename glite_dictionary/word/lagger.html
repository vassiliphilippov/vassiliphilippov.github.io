lagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæɡər / (AmE), / ˈlæɡə / (BrE)
a person who falls behind others, especially by taking longer than necessary to do something
person who falls behind

Examples

He was always the lagger in our running group, finishing several minutes after everyone else.
During the project, Tim became a lagger by constantly missing deadlines.
We can't afford to have a lagger slow down the entire team's progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laggard - someone who makes slow progress and falls behind others
  • Antonym: leader - someone who goes ahead or directs others

Etymology

Formed from 'lag' + '-er', following a common pattern for agent nouns in English.
