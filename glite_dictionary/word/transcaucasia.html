Transcaucasia

1. PROPN

/ ˌtrænz.kɔːˈkeɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌtrænz.kɔːˈkeɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
a geographical region located south of the Caucasus Mountains, including Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan
region south of the Caucasus Mountains

Examples

Scientists have long studied the rich biodiversity of Transcaucasia due to its unique climate and geography.
Political tensions have sometimes flared among the countries of Transcaucasia.
The wines produced in Transcaucasia enjoy an excellent reputation internationally.

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'trans-' (across, beyond) + 'Caucasia' (relating to the Caucasus region).
