elecampane

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləkæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪkamˈpeɪn / (BrE)
A tall Eurasian plant of the daisy family, with yellow flowers and roots formerly used in herbal medicine.
a Eurasian medicinal plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The bitter root of elecampane was once widely used in traditional remedies.
She planted elecampane in her herb garden for its tall stems and bright yellow blooms.
Some herbal teas include dried elecampane root for its supposed health benefits.

Etymology

From Old French elecampane, from Latin enula campana (literally 'field elecampane').
