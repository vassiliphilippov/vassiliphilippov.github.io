selector

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktər / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a device or switch used to choose between different options or settings
device for choosing options/settings

Examples

Turn the selector to the desired temperature setting before operating the oven.
The radio has a selector that lets you choose between AM and FM bands.
Use the mode selector on the camera to switch between photo and video functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit

Etymology

From Latin 'seligere', past participle 'selectus' (to choose), plus English '-or' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktər / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person who chooses or selects something or someone from a group, especially for a particular purpose or position
person who makes selections

Examples

The cricket team's selector announced the final squad for the tournament yesterday.
As a selector for the talent show, she had to pick only five contestants for the finals.
It takes a skilled selector to build a great playlist from thousands of songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chooser - a person who chooses
  • Degree Lower: selector switch - a switch used to select an option or channel

Etymology

From select + -or, a suffix forming agent nouns from verbs (Latin origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10451389-n
Synset POS: n
