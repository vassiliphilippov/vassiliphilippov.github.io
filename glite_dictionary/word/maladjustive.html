maladjustive

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌs.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌs.tɪv / (BrE)
failing to adapt adequately to the conditions or demands of one's environment
failing to adapt properly

Examples

The child exhibited several maladjustive behaviors in response to the new school environment.
Therapists work with individuals to change maladjustive patterns of thinking and behavior.
Prolonged stress can lead to maladjustive responses that affect mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maladjusted - failing to cope well with demands of environment
  • Antonym: well-adjusted - able to adapt well to conditions or social situations

Etymology

From mal- (bad) and 'adjustive'; modelled on 'maladjusted'.
