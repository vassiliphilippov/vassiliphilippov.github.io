coxswain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːk.sən / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.swən / (BrE)
The person who steers and directs a ship's boat or a racing crew, especially in rowing.
person who steers a boat or racing crew

Examples

The coxswain expertly guided the rowing team through the narrow channel.
As coxswain, her job was to motivate the crew and set the pace during the race.
The navy appointed a new coxswain to lead the lifeboat team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmsman - person who steers a ship or boat

Etymology

From Middle English coxswain, from 'cock' (small boat) + 'swain' (servant; from Old Norse sveinn, boy, servant).
