prosodion

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊdiˌɑn / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊdiˌɒn / (BrE)
a hymn or piece of religious music performed during a procession, particularly in Eastern Orthodox liturgical traditions
hymn sung in a religious procession

Examples

The choir sang the prosodion as the clergy entered the church.
A solemn prosodion echoed through the cathedral during the religious festival.
Ancient manuscripts describe the use of a prosodion in early processional ceremonies.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek προσώδιον (prosódion), meaning 'processional hymn'.
