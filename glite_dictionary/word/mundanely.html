mundanely

1. ADV

/ mʌnˈdeɪnli / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪnli / (BrE)
In an ordinary, unremarkable, or worldly manner.
in an ordinary or worldly manner

Examples

He answered the extraordinary question rather mundanely, as if it were completely ordinary.
The meeting began mundanely with routine announcements before any important topics were discussed.
She mundanely described her day, focusing on everyday tasks and chores.

Synset Examples

terrestrially changeable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mundane - ordinary or lacking interest; of this world rather than spiritual

Etymology

Formed from 'mundane' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'mundanus' (of the world).

2. ADV

/ mənˈdeɪnli / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪnli / (BrE)
In an ordinary, dull, or unremarkable manner.
in an ordinary or dull way

Examples

He spoke mundanely about his daily routine, making it sound completely unremarkable.
The meeting proceeded mundanely, without any unexpected developments or excitement.
She described her travels mundanely, focusing on the uneventful details rather than the adventures.

Synset Examples

the young man spoke so mundanely of university life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinarily - in an ordinary manner
  • Antonym: interestingly - in an interesting manner

Etymology

From 'mundane' + '-ly', originally from Latin 'mundanus' (of the world).
