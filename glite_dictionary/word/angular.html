angular

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
Having angles or sharp corners; characterized by a shape with clear angles rather than curves.
having angles or sharp corners

Examples

The modern building had an angular design with sharp, clean edges.
Her face was strikingly angular, with pronounced cheekbones and a narrow chin.
The sculpture's angular form contrasted with the soft curves of the furniture nearby.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rounded - having a smooth, curved shape without angles

Etymology

From Latin 'angularis', from 'angulus' meaning 'angle'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a measurement or quantity) measured by an angle or by the rate of change of an angle, as in physics or mathematics.
measured by angle or rate of angle change

Examples

The scientist explained how angular velocity differs from linear velocity in rotating objects.
In astronomy, angular distance helps to describe the position of stars in the sky.
Engineers must calculate the angular acceleration to design stable spinning machines.

Synset Examples

angular momentum

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linear - measured along a straight line, as opposed to angular

Etymology

From Latin 'angularis', from 'angulus' (angle).
