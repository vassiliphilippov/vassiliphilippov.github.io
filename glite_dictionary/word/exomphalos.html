exomphalos

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈsɒmfəˌlɒs / (AmE), / ɛkˈɒmfəlɒs / (BrE)
A congenital umbilical hernia in which abdominal organs protrude into the base of the umbilical cord at birth.
congenital umbilical hernia at birth
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with an exomphalos that required immediate surgical intervention.
Prenatal scans can often detect an exomphalos before birth.
Doctors explained that the baby's exomphalos would be managed in a neonatal intensive care unit.

Etymology

From Greek exomphalos, from ex- (out) + omphalos (navel).
