Leicester

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛstər / (AmE), / ˈlɛstə / (BrE)
A largely agricultural county in central England, known for its historic towns and rural landscapes.
county in central England
british

Examples

Many farms and market towns can be found throughout Leicester.
She visited Leicester last summer to learn about its agricultural history.
The rolling hills of Leicester are a defining feature of its landscape.

Etymology

From the Old English 'Ligora ceaster', meaning 'the fortified town on the River Legro'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈlɛstər / (AmE), / ˈlɛstə / (BrE)
A city in Leicestershire, central England, known for its industrial history and Roman origins.
city in central England

Examples

My cousin moved to Leicester to attend university last year.
Football fans celebrated throughout Leicester after the team's unexpected victory.
The Roman ruins in Leicester attract many tourists each year.

Etymology

From Old English 'Ligora Ceaster', meaning fort of the people called Ligore, influenced by Latin 'castra' (camp, fort).
