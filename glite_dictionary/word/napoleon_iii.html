Napoleon III

1. PROPN

/ nəˈpoʊliən ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / nəˈpəʊliən ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1808–1873) Emperor of the French from 1852 to 1871, nephew of Napoleon I.
French emperor (1852–1871)

Examples

Napoleon III ruled France during the turbulent years of the Second Empire.
Historians often debate the political strategies of Napoleon III.
The defeat of Napoleon III in 1870 marked the end of his reign.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Napoleon I - French military leader and emperor, uncle of Napoleon III
  • Antonym: Louis XVIII - King of France before the Second Empire, predecessor of Napoleon III's reign

Etymology

Named after his uncle, Napoleon I, following dynastic succession.
