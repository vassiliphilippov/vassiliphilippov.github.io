croquette

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / krɒˈkɛt / (BrE)
a small roll or ball of minced cooked meat, fish, or vegetables mixed with a thick white sauce, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried until crisp
breaded, deep-fried minced meat or vegetable roll

Examples

The chef garnished the plate with a perfectly golden croquette beside the roast chicken.
She prepared potato croquettes for the family dinner.
A salmon croquette makes a delicious appetizer at parties.

Etymology

From French 'croquette', derived from 'croquer' (to crunch).
