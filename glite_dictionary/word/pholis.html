Pholis

1. PROPN

/ ˈfoʊlɪs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Pholidae, commonly known as gunnels, found in coastal waters of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of marine fish (gunnels)

Examples

The genus Pholis includes several species of slender, elongated gunnels.
Researchers collected specimens of Pholis along the rocky Pacific coastline.
The distinctive dorsal fin is characteristic of fish in the Pholis genus.

Etymology

From Greek pholis (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊlɪs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of elongated, marine fishes known as gunnels, which are the type genus of the family Pholidae.
genus of marine gunnel fishes

Examples

The genus Pholis is commonly found along rocky coastal shores in the Northern Hemisphere.
Marine biologists discovered several new species within the Pholis genus during their expedition.
Members of the genus Pholis are known for their elongated bodies and ability to hide in crevices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pholidae - family of marine fishes called gunnels

Etymology

From Greek *pholis* (a type of fish, possibly meaning 'scale').
