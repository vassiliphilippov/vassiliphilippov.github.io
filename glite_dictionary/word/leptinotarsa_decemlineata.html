Leptinotarsa decemlineata

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.tɪ.noʊˈtɑr.sə dɛˌsɛm.lɪˈniː.ə.tə / (AmE), / lɛp.tɪ.nəʊˈtɑː.sə dɛˌsɛm.lɪˈniː.ə.tə / (BrE)
A black-and-yellow beetle, commonly known as the Colorado potato beetle, that feeds on potato leaves in both adult and larval stages; originally native to the eastern Rocky Mountains, but now found worldwide.
Colorado potato beetle (scientific name)

Examples

The larvae of Leptinotarsa decemlineata can quickly defoliate potato crops if left unchecked.
Farmers monitor their fields carefully to detect infestations of Leptinotarsa decemlineata.
Chemical pesticides are often used to control Leptinotarsa decemlineata populations in agricultural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Colorado potato beetle - common name for Leptinotarsa decemlineata, a beetle pest of potatoes

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Leptinotarsa + species decemlineata meaning 'ten-striped'.
