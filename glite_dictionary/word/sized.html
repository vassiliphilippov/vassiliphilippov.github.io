sized

1. ADJ

/ saɪzd / (AmE), / saɪzd / (BrE)
having a specified size, often used in combination with adjectives or nouns to indicate dimension or magnitude
having a specified size (often used in combinations)

Examples

The restaurant offers meals in small-, medium-, and large-sized portions.
They bought a pocket-sized notebook for travel notes.
Many family-sized cars are now available with hybrid engines.

Etymology

Formed by combining 'size' with the past participle suffix '-ed', used to form adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ saɪzd / (AmE), / saɪzd / (BrE)
having a surface that has been treated or coated with sizing (a strengthening or sealing substance)
treated with sizing (substance)

Examples

The artist preferred sized canvas for better paint adhesion.
Paper must be sized to prevent ink from bleeding through the fibers.
Many textiles are sized during manufacture to increase their strength.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsized - not treated or coated with sizing

Etymology

From 'size' (substance used in preparing materials), derived from Old French 'sise', meaning a kind of glue or binding agent.
