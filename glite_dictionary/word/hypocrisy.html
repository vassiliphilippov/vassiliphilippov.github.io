hypocrisy

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform; insincerity, especially in expressing agreement without genuine conviction.
insincerity; pretense of moral belief or agreement

Examples

Her speech was full of hypocrisy because she did not follow her own advice.
The public grew tired of the politician's hypocrisy regarding environmental policy.
Calling for unity while spreading rumors is an example of hypocrisy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincerity - lack of genuine feeling or honesty; similar to hypocrisy
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere; opposite of hypocrisy

Etymology

From Greek hypokrisis (acting of a part, pretence), from hypokrinesthai (to play a part, pretend).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform; pretense of virtue or sincerity.
pretense of having beliefs or virtues

Examples

His criticism of others was pure hypocrisy since he never followed the rules himself.
Many people are quick to spot hypocrisy in politicians who do not practice what they preach.
She was accused of hypocrisy when she broke the very guidelines she had set for her employees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • Synonym: duplicity - deceitfulness; double-dealing

Etymology

From Greek hypokrisis (acting of a part, hypocrisy) via Old French hypocrisie.

3. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The practice of pretending to have moral standards or beliefs that one's own behavior does not follow.
pretending to have moral standards

Examples

Many politicians are accused of hypocrisy when their actions contradict their public statements.
He condemned the hypocrisy of preachers who do not practice what they preach.
Her angry speech exposed the hypocrisy within the organization.

Synset Examples

The hipoisie gathered at the café to discuss art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincerity - the absence of pretense or hypocrisy; honesty in expression or action
  • Synonym: duplicity - deceitfulness; double-dealing; two-faced behavior

Etymology

From Greek hypokrisis (acting of a stage actor), via Latin hypocrisis.
