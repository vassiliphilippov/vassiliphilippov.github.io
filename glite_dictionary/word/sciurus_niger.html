Sciurus niger

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs ˈnaɪɡər / (AmE), / sɪˈjʊərəs ˈnaɪɡə / (BrE)
a species of large tree-dwelling squirrel native to eastern and central United States, commonly known as the fox squirrel
large North American tree squirrel species

Examples

The Sciurus niger is the largest species of tree squirrel found in North America.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Sciurus niger in mixed hardwood forests.
Unlike gray squirrels, Sciurus niger often prefers open woodlands and edge habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox squirrel - common name for Sciurus niger, a large North American tree squirrel

Etymology

From Latin: 'sciurus' meaning squirrel and 'niger' meaning black, referring to the species' original scientific description.
