Parietales

1. NOUN

/ pəˌraɪ.əˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / pəˌraɪ.ɪˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of dicotyledonous plants, including families such as Papaveraceae, classified in the subclass Dilleniidae
former order of dicotyledonous plants

Examples

The poppy family was once classified under the order Parietales in botanical texts.
Recent genetic research has led to the reclassification of many plants formerly included in Parietales.
Students of botany often encounter the term Parietales when studying historical plant taxonomy.

Etymology

From Latin 'paries, parietis' (wall), referring to placentation pattern in some members.
