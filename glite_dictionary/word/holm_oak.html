holm oak

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm oʊk / (AmE), / həʊm əʊk / (BrE)
the hard wood obtained from the evergreen oak tree native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its strength and durability
hard wood from the holm oak tree

Examples

The furniture was handcrafted from solid holm oak for exceptional durability.
They used holm oak beams in the restoration of the old chapel.
The dense grain of holm oak makes it an excellent material for flooring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree bearing acorns, of which holm oak is a species

Etymology

From Old Norse *holmr* (a small island, relating to habitat) and 'oak' for the tree species.
