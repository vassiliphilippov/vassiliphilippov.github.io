curative

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊrətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərətɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or treatment that cures disease or relieves pain.
medicine or treatment that cures disease

Examples

The ancient herbal curative was believed to heal many ailments.
Despite searching for years, doctors could not find a curative for the rare disease.
The healer recommended a powerful curative to relieve his pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedy - a medicine or treatment for a disease or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'curare' (to care, to cure).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkjʊr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkjʊə.rə.tɪv / (BrE)
Able to cure disease or restore health; having healing properties.
having the power to cure or heal

Examples

Many spa resorts boast curative waters believed to ease various ailments.
The doctor prescribed a herbal tea with curative properties for her sore throat.
Scientists are investigating the curative potential of a new experimental drug.

Synset Examples

curative powers of herbal remedies
her gentle healing hand
remedial surgery
a sanative environment of mountains and fresh air
a therapeutic agent
therapeutic diets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapeutic - having healing or curative effects
  • Synonym: remedial - intended to remedy or cure
  • Antonym: harmful - causing harm or injury, the opposite of curative

Etymology

From Latin 'curare' (to take care of, to cure) + '-ive'.
