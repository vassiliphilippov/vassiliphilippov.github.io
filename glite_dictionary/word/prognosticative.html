prognosticative

1. ADJ

/ prɑgˈnɑstəˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / prɒgˈnɒstɪkətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or useful for making predictions, especially about future events or outcomes
relating to prediction

Examples

The scientist's prognosticative model accurately forecasted climate changes over the next decade.
Economic indicators often have a prognosticative value when assessing future market trends.
His prognosticative skills made him a valuable consultant during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predictive - relating to prediction or forecasting; able to foresee future events

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'prognosticativus', from Latin 'prognosticatus', past participle of 'prognosticare' (to foretell).
