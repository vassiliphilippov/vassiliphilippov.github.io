to expostulate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɑstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval or disagreement with someone, especially in order to persuade them not to do something
to reason in protest or dissuasion

Examples

She tried to expostulate with him, but he refused to listen to her advice.
He expostulated with the manager about the unfair decision.
Although I expostulate with her every time, she still ignores my warnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remonstrate - to make a forcefully reproachful protest

Etymology

From Latin 'expostulatus', past participle of 'expostulare' (to demand, to require).
