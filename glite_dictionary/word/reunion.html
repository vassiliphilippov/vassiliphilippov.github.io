reunion

1. NOUN

/ riˈjuːniən / (AmE), / riˈjuːniən / (BrE)
an instance of people or things coming together again after being apart
an act of coming together again

Examples

The family held a reunion every summer to catch up with distant relatives.
After years apart, their emotional reunion brought tears to everyone’s eyes.
The college is organizing a reunion for alumni next month.

Synset Examples

monetary unification precipitated the reunification of the German state in October 1990

Related Terms

  • Antonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From French 'réunion', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'unio' (union, joining together).

2. NOUN

/ riˈjunjən / (AmE), / riˈjuːniən / (BrE)
A gathering or party of people who were previously associated, such as classmates or family members, who meet after a period of separation.
gathering of former associates

Examples

Our high school reunion was a wonderful opportunity to reconnect with old friends.
She traveled across the country to attend her family reunion last summer.
The college alumni planned a reunion to celebrate the university's centenary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reunite - to come together again after being apart

Etymology

From French 'réunion', from 'réunir' meaning 'to reunite'.
