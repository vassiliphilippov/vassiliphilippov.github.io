tope

1. VERB

/ toʊp / (AmE), / təʊp / (BrE)
(archaic) To drink alcohol, especially in large quantities.
drink alcohol excessively (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In his youth, he would often tope late into the night at the tavern.
The old sailor still topes with his old friends every time they reunite.
After years of toping, his health had inevitably declined.

Synset Examples

The husband drinks and beats his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - To consume liquid, especially alcohol
  • Antonym: to abstain - To refrain from consuming alcohol or other substances

Etymology

From Hindi 'ṭōp' (to drink alcohol), adopted into English during the colonial period.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtəʊp / (BrE)
a dome-shaped monument or shrine, especially one built by Buddhists to house sacred relics; a stupa.
Buddhist dome-shaped shrine or monument

Examples

The ancient tope attracts pilgrims from all over the world each year.
Archaeologists discovered relics buried beneath the old tope.
The visitors admired the intricate carvings decorating the surface of the tope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupa - a mound-like or hemispherical Buddhist shrine for relics

Etymology

From Hindi 'ṭop', from Sanskrit 'stūpa' (meaning mound, stupa).
