management

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the group of people responsible for directing and controlling a business or organization
group directing a business

Examples

The company’s management announced a new strategy to improve growth.
There are often tensions between management and employees during contract negotiations.
She was promoted to management after years of hard work in the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the group that manages or runs an organization
  • Antonym: staff - the employees of an organization, excluding management

Etymology

From manage + -ment, ultimately from Italian 'maneggiare' (to handle) and Latin 'manus' (hand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒm(ə)nt / (BrE)
The act or process of managing, controlling, or supervising something, such as a business or organization.
the act of managing

Examples

Effective management is essential for the success of any project.
He took over the management of the company last year.
Environmental management requires cooperation between different sectors.

Synset Examples

he was given overall management of the program
is the direction of the economy a function of government?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the process or activity of running a business, organization, etc.

Etymology

From Italian *maneggiare* (to handle), from Latin *manus* (hand).
