Usneaceae

1. NOUN

/ juːsniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / juːsniˈiːsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of fruticose lichens that includes genera such as Usnea, Evernia, Ramalina, and Alectoria.
a family of fruticose lichens

Examples

The Usneaceae family comprises lichens that are often referred to as beard lichens due to their appearance.
Members of the Usneaceae can be found growing on tree branches in many forests worldwide.
Researchers have studied the chemical compounds produced by Usneaceae for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From the genus name Usnea + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

a family of fruticose lichens with prostrate, erect, or pendulous thalli, including genera such as Usnea, Evernia, Ramalina, and Alectoria
family of fruticose lichens

Examples

Several lichen species in the Usneaceae family are used in traditional medicine across the world.
The unique structure of Usneaceae makes them easily identifiable under a microscope.
Researchers studied the distribution of Usneaceae in the temperate forests of the region.

From New Latin Usnea (type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
