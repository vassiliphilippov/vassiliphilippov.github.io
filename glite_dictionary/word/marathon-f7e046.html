marathon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
A long-distance running race with an official distance of 26 miles 385 yards (42.195 kilometers).
long-distance running race

Examples

Running a marathon requires months of training and dedication.
She completed her first marathon in under four hours.
The city hosts an annual marathon that attracts runners from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race - a competition of speed, often on foot

Etymology

From the Greek legend of Pheidippides, who is said to have run from Marathon to Athens to bring news of victory.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
a long and difficult task or activity that requires great effort and endurance
long and demanding task or effort

Examples

The project turned into a marathon as the team worked late every night for two weeks.
Writing her thesis was a marathon that tested her patience and determination.
Preparing for the annual tax season is always a marathon for accountants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sprint - a short and fast race or effort

Etymology

From the Battle of Marathon in ancient Greece, where a messenger is said to have run from Marathon to Athens.
