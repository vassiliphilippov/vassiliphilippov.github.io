orthoepy

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθoʊəpi / (AmE), / ɔːˈθəʊɪpi / (BrE)
the study or customary pronunciation of words in a language
correct or customary pronunciation

Examples

She consulted a guide on orthoepy to ensure she pronounced the word correctly.
Modern dictionaries include marks to indicate the recommended orthoepy of entries.
Students of linguistics may study orthoepy to understand regional variations in speech.

Synset Examples

the pronunciation of Chinese is difficult for foreigners
that is the correct pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronunciation - the way in which a word is pronounced

Etymology

From Greek ortho- (correct) + epos (speech).

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθoʊəpi / (AmE), / ɔːˈθəʊəpi / (BrE)
the study or correct pronunciation of words, especially as established by tradition or authority
study of correct word pronunciation
archaic

Examples

The field of orthoepy has guided dictionaries in presenting recommended pronunciations.
Students of phonetics often study orthoepy to understand traditional standards of speech.
His lectures on orthoepy explored the relationship between spelling and pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronunciation - the way in which a word is pronounced

Etymology

From Greek orthoepeia: orthos (correct) + epos (speech).
