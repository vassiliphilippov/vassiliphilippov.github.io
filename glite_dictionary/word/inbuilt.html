inbuilt

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbɪlt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbɪlt / (BrE)
existing as an inherent or essential part; built in by nature or design.
inherent or built-in

Examples

The furniture comes with several inbuilt safety features for children.
She had an inbuilt sense of determination that helped her succeed.
The system relies on its inbuilt capacity to adapt to new conditions.

Synset Examples

the Ptolemaic system with its built-in concept of periodicity
a constitutional inability to tell the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; essential
  • Synonym: innate - inborn; natural
  • Antonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature; coming from outside
