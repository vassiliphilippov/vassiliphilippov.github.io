null

1. ADJ

/ nʌl / (AmE), / nʌl / (BrE)
having no legal or binding force; invalid or void
invalid or without legal force
legal

Examples

The contract was declared null by the court due to a missing signature.
Any agreement made under duress is considered null and cannot be enforced.
If the will is found to be null, the estate will be distributed according to state law.

Synset Examples

null and void

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - having no legal effect; invalid

Etymology

From Latin nullus (not any, none).
