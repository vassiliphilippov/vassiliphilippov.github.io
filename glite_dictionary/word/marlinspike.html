marlinspike

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrlɪnˌspaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɑːlɪnˌspaɪk / (BrE)
a pointed iron tool used by sailors to separate strands of rope or cable, especially in splicing
pointed iron sailor’s rope tool

Examples

The sailor used a marlinspike to untwist the stubborn rope strands.
Every good seaman keeps a marlinspike in his tool kit for repairs.
She learned how to splice lines with a marlinspike during her training.

Etymology

From Dutch *marlingstek*, from *marling* (small cord) + *stek* (stick, spike).
