to scintillate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪn.təˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to emit flashes of light; to sparkle or shine brightly
emit flashes of light; sparkle brightly

Examples

The chandelier crystals scintillate whenever the sun shines through the window.
Her diamond earrings scintillated as she turned her head.
Stars scintillate brilliantly against the clear night sky.

Synset Examples

Unquarried marble sparkled on the hillside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with bright flashes of light
  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or bright light

Etymology

From Latin scintillare (to sparkle), from scintilla (spark).

5. VERB

/ ˈsɪntɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪntɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a material) to emit brief flashes of light, especially as a result of being struck by charged particles or high-energy photons.
emit flashes when struck (physics)
medical

Examples

The phosphor material will scintillate when hit by beta particles in the detector.
Some crystals scintillate under X-ray exposure, producing measurable flashes of light.
The screen was engineered to scintillate in response to high-energy gamma photons.

Synset Examples

the phosphor scintillated

Etymology

From Latin scintillare (to sparkle), from scintilla (spark).
