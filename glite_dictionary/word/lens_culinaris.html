Lens culinaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛnz kʊˈlɪnərɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɛnz kjuːˈlɪnərɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the lentil plant, an annual herb native to Eurasia widely cultivated for its edible seeds and leafy stalks used as animal fodder.
scientific name for the lentil plant

Examples

Lens culinaris is the primary species cultivated for lentil consumption worldwide.
Farmers in the region rely on Lens culinaris both for its nutritious seeds and as fodder for livestock.
Several varieties of Lens culinaris are grown depending on local climate and soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lentil - edible seed of Lens culinaris

Etymology

From Latin 'lens' (lentil) and 'culinaris' (relating to cooking).
