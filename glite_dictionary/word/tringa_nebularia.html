Tringa nebularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋɡə nɛˈbjuːləriə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋɡə nɛˈbjuːləriə / (BrE)
a large European sandpiper with greenish legs, commonly known as the greenshank
large European sandpiper species

Examples

The Tringa nebularia is often observed wading along shallow European lakeshores.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Tringa nebularia across northern Europe.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Tringa nebularia in the wetland reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenshank - the common English name for Tringa nebularia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tringa' from Greek 'trungas', a bird mentioned by Aristotle; 'nebularia' meaning 'of the mist'.
