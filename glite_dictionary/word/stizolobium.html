Stizolobium

1. NOUN

/ stɪzəˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / stɪzəˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical herbs and woody vines with trifoliate leaves and showy flowers borne in axillary clusters
genus of tropical herbs and vines

Examples

The velvet bean, once classified as Stizolobium, is now known as Mucuna pruriens.
Botanists studied several species within the Stizolobium genus for their unique floral arrangements.
Some traditional medicines use plants from the Stizolobium group for their potential health benefits.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'stizein' (to mark, tattoo) and 'lobos' (pod, lobe).

2. PROPN

/ stɪzəˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / stɪzəˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs and woody vines with trifoliate leaves and showy flowers in axillary clusters, found chiefly in warmer regions.
genus of tropical herbs and vines

Examples

The genus Stizolobium includes several species valued for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
Researchers collected samples from the Stizolobium plants growing in the tropical forest understory.
Many farmers in Asia cultivate species within Stizolobium for use as green manure.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek stizo (to tattoo, prick) and lobos (lobe), referring to characteristics of the plant.
