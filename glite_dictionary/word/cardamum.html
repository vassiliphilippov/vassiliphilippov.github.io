cardamum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdəməm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəməm / (BrE)
The aromatic seeds of a plant used as a spice in cooking, especially for seasoning pickles and barbecue sauces, similar to cinnamon and cloves.
aromatic spice seeds for seasoning

Examples

The chef added a pinch of cardamum to the stew for extra flavor.
I love using cardamum in my homemade chai tea blend.
Cardamum and cinnamon are often paired together in baking recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - aromatic spice from the seeds of certain plants, commonly used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'cardamomum', from Greek 'kardamōmon', the name of the spice.
