aldol

1. NOUN

/ ˈældɒl / (AmE), / ˈaldɒl / (BrE)
A colorless, oily organic compound formed by the condensation of two molecules of acetaldehyde, containing both an alcohol group (–OH) and an aldehyde group (–CHO).
colorless oily organic compound (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The chemist synthesized aldol by condensing acetaldehyde in the laboratory.
Because aldol contains both alcohol and aldehyde groups, it is important in organic synthesis.
Researchers study the aldol reaction as a key step in producing complex molecules.

Etymology

From German Aldol, combining 'Ald' (from 'Aldehyd', meaning aldehyde) and 'ol' (alcohol).
