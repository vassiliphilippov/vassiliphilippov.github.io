eudemon

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdiːmən / (AmE), / juːˈdiːmən / (BrE)
A benevolent or good spirit, especially in ancient Greek philosophy and mythology.
benevolent or good spirit

Examples

In ancient Greece, a eudemon was thought to guide individuals with kindness.
The philosopher believed that a eudemon watched over every virtuous soul.
She hoped a eudemon would protect her on her journey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demon - a supernatural being, often evil

Etymology

From Greek 'eudaimōn' (εὐδαίμων), meaning 'having a good spirit'.
