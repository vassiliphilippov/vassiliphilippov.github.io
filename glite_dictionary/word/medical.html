medical

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl / (BrE)
a thorough physical examination, including various tests depending on the person's age, sex, and health; especially used in British English
physical examination (esp. British)
british

Examples

All new employees must undergo a medical before starting work.
She failed her annual medical due to high blood pressure.
After the accident, he was sent for a full medical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check-up - a medical examination to test your general state of health

Etymology

From Latin 'medicus' (physician), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl / (BrE)
Requiring or suitable for treatment by medicine, especially rather than by surgery.
treatable by medicine, not surgery

Examples

The doctor assured her that the condition was strictly medical and not surgical.
The patient received medical care for his illness rather than undergoing an operation.
Some diseases require medical treatment while others may need surgery.

Synset Examples

medical treatment
pneumonia is a medical disease

Etymology

From Latin medicus (physician), from mederi (to heal).

WordNet ID: oewn-01172532-a

relating to the science or practice of medicine or to the treatment of illness and injuries
relating to medicine or healing
