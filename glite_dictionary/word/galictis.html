Galictis

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪktɪs / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the weasel family, native to Central and South America, commonly known as grisons.
a genus of small carnivorous mammals (grisons)

Examples

The Galictis genus includes two species commonly found in Central and South America.
Zoologists have studied the Galictis to better understand the behavior of grisons.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitats of Galictis species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grison - a small, weasel-like mammal native to Central and South America

Etymology

From Greek 'galē' (weasel) and 'iktis' (marten).
