ill-favoured

1. ADJ

/ ɪl ˈfeɪvərd / (AmE), / ɪl ˈfeɪvəd / (BrE)
(of a person or face) having an unattractive or unpleasant appearance.
having an unattractive appearance

Examples

He was considered ill-favoured, but his kindness made him popular nonetheless.
The portrait depicted an ill-favoured man with harsh features and a stern expression.
Despite her ill-favoured looks, she carried herself with dignity and grace.

Synset Examples

an ill-favored countenance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unattractive - not pleasing in appearance
  • Antonym: good-looking - pleasing in appearance

Etymology

Formed from 'ill' (bad) + 'favoured', originally meaning 'endowed with good looks'.
