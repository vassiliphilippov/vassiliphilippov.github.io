standby

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstændˌbaɪ / (BrE)
a person or thing that can be relied on in emergencies or when needed as a replacement or backup
reliable backup or substitute

Examples

In case the main speaker is unable to attend, we have a standby ready.
That old recipe book is my standby for quick meals.
Candles are a useful standby during power outages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backup - an alternative or substitute in case the primary fails
  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or reliable person or thing
  • Antonym: necessity - something that is needed or indispensable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04309057-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstan(d)bʌɪ / (BrE)
an actor who is prepared to replace a regular performer in a production if needed
actor ready to replace another

Examples

As a standby, she rehearsed the lead role every week in case she was needed.
The production kept a standby for the main actor in case of illness.
When the star fell ill, the standby stepped in and delivered an outstanding performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understudy - an actor who learns another's role and can replace them
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10756760-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈstændˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstænd.bʌɪ / (BrE)
kept ready to be used if needed, especially in an emergency or when the usual option fails
kept ready for emergency use

Examples

We have a standby generator in case of power failure.
A standby crew was on site during the entire event.
The hospital has standby equipment for emergency situations.

Synset Examples

a standby generator
a standby crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backup - a substitute or reserve available for use if needed
