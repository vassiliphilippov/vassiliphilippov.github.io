secretiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrətɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to share information or disclose one's thoughts, actions, or intentions
tendency to keep things secret

Examples

Her constant secretiveness made it difficult to trust her completely.
Some leaders use secretiveness to protect sensitive information from their competitors.
His secretiveness raised suspicions among his friends and colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open and transparent; frankness

Etymology

From 'secretive' + '-ness', modelled on Latin secretus (set apart, hidden).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈkriːtɪvnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the tendency or habit of keeping things secret or not revealing information to others
tendency to keep things secret

Examples

Her secretiveness made it difficult to trust her completely.
He was known for his secretiveness about personal matters.
The company's secretiveness fueled rumors about its future plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open to sharing information

Etymology

From 'secretive' + '-ness', where 'secretive' derives from 'secret', from Latin 'secretus' (set apart, hidden).
