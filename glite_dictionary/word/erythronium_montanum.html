Erythronium montanum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθroʊniəm mɒnˈtænəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊniəm mɒnˈtɑːnəm / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the northwestern United States, noted for its large white flowers with orange markings, often found near the snow line.
perennial herb with white flowers from NW US

Examples

The delicate Erythronium montanum blooms just as the snow begins to melt in alpine meadows.
Hikers spotted clusters of Erythronium montanum near the trail above the tree line.
Botanists studied how Erythronium montanum adapts to cold, mountainous environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red), referring to the genus' typical flower color. 'Montanum' is Latin for 'of the mountains.'
