Appalachia

1. PROPN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪtʃə / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈlætʃɪə / (BrE)
a region in the eastern United States, extending from southern New York to northern Alabama, Mississippi, and Georgia, particularly associated with the Appalachian Mountains and known for its coal mining history and distinct culture
region in eastern U.S. linked to Appalachian Mountains
american

Examples

Many families in Appalachia have relied on coal mining for generations.
The folk music of Appalachia has influenced genres across the United States.
Researchers studied poverty rates in rural communities throughout Appalachia.

Etymology

From the name of the Appalachian Mountains, derived from 'Apalachee', a Native American tribe of northern Florida.
