mournfully

1. ADV

/ ˈmɔrnfəli / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnfəli / (BrE)
In a way that expresses sadness, grief, or sorrow.
in a sad or sorrowful manner

Examples

He mournfully gazed at the fading photograph, lost in memories of the past.
The bells tolled mournfully throughout the grey morning, marking a solemn farewell.
She shook her head mournfully when she heard the disappointing news.

Synset Examples

the young man stared into his glass mournfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a sad or unhappy way
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic way

Etymology

From 'mournful' + '-ly'. 'Mournful' from Middle English, based on 'mourn' (to feel or show deep sorrow).
