undependability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɪˌpɛndəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˌpɛndəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of not being reliable or trustworthy; the state of being unable to be depended on.
lack of reliability or trustworthiness

Examples

Her constant lateness was a clear sign of her undependability.
The team's failure was partly due to the undependability of their equipment.
Managers are frustrated by the undependability of temporary workers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reliability - the quality of being trustworthy or of performing consistently well
  • Antonym: dependability - the quality of being dependable or reliable

Etymology

Formed from "undependable" + "-ity".
