Pogonia

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈgoʊniə / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
a small but widely distributed genus of orchids found in damp or boggy areas of the north temperate zone, closely related to Cleistes
a genus of orchids of damp habitats

Examples

The rare Pogonia orchid can be found in the bogs of eastern North America.
Several species within the genus Pogonia are protected due to habitat loss.
Botanists studied how Pogonia adapts to different wetland environments.

Etymology

From Greek *pōgōn* meaning 'beard', referring to the bearded lip of the flower.
