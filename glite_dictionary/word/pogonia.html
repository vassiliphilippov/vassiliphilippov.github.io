pogonia

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
A hardy bog orchid of the genus Pogonia, characterized by slender rootstocks, erect stems with few leaves, and a solitary flower.
a genus of bog orchids

Examples

The rare pogonia thrives in acidic wetlands across eastern North America.
Several species of pogonia bear pink or white flowers in late spring.
Conservationists monitor the local pogonia population to protect these unique orchids.

Etymology

From Latin Pogonia, from Greek pogōn (beard), describing the bearded look of the flower.
