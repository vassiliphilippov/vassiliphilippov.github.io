shillyshally

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪliˌʃæli / (AmE), / ˈʃɪliˌʃæli / (BrE)
the act of hesitating or procrastinating instead of taking immediate action
hesitation; procrastination

Examples

His constant shillyshally annoyed his colleagues, who preferred to make decisions quickly.
We lost a great opportunity because of too much shillyshally on the team's part.
After an hour of shillyshally, they finally agreed on a course of action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastination - the act of delaying or postponing something
  • Antonym: decisiveness - ability to make decisions quickly

Etymology

Reduplication of 'shall I' used to express indecision.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɪliˌʃæli / (AmE), / ˈʃɪliˌʃæli / (BrE)
to hesitate or be indecisive, especially by delaying taking action or making a decision
hesitate; delay making a decision
archaic

Examples

Don't shillyshally; we need an answer by tomorrow.
She tends to shillyshally when important decisions must be made.
The meeting was delayed because the committee kept shillyshallying over minor details.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - to delay or postpone action; to put off doing something

Etymology

Alteration of 'shill I, shall I?', an old-fashioned reduplicated question meaning 'shall I, shall I?'
