Mustela putorius

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛlə pjuːˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / mʌˈstɛlə pjuːˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
a dark brown mammal of the weasel family, native to the woodlands of Eurasia, which emits a strong, unpleasant odor when threatened; the European polecat
European polecat species

Examples

The Mustela putorius is known for its distinctive scent used to deter predators.
Researchers tracked several Mustela putorius individuals through their woodland habitat to study behavior.
Unlike ferrets, which are domesticated, the Mustela putorius remains a wild species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - common English name for Mustela putorius

Etymology

From Latin: 'mustela' (weasel) and 'putorius' (stench, malodorous).
