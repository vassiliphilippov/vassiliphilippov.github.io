to hedgehop

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛdʒˌhɑp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒˌhɒp / (BrE)
(of an aircraft or pilot) to fly very close to the ground, typically just above hedges or obstacles.
fly very close to the ground

Examples

The daring pilot loved to hedgehop across the fields at sunrise.
During training, they practiced hedgehopping as part of low-level flight exercises.
The old biplane hedgehopped over the countryside, startling grazing sheep as it passed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - to fly an aircraft low over a place, especially in a showy or intimidating way

Etymology

Formed from 'hedge' + 'hop', referring to the practice of flying low enough to seemingly hop over hedges.
