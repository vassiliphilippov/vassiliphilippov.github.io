Ymir

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːmɪr / (AmE), / ˈiːmɪə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the primeval giant killed by Odin and his brothers, whose body was used to create the world.
primeval giant in Norse mythology

Examples

In Norse mythology, Ymir was believed to be the ancestor of all giants.
According to legend, the gods fashioned the earth from Ymir's flesh and bones.
The ancient saga describes how Odin and his brothers slew Ymir to shape the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odin - chief god in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: giant - mythical being of great size and strength

Etymology

From Old Norse *Ymir*, possibly related to the word for 'twin' in Indo-European languages.
