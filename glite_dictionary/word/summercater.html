summercater

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmərˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsʌməˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
(Maine) a person who temporarily resides in coastal Maine during the summer months, typically as a seasonal visitor.
summer visitor to Maine (colloquial)
informal

Examples

Many summercaters return to the same Maine village year after year.
Local businesses rely on the increase in summercaters during June, July, and August.
Some residents find the influx of summercaters both a blessing and a challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer resident - person residing in a place only for the summer
  • Antonym: local - a permanent resident (opposite of summer visitor)

Etymology

Blend of 'summer' and a colloquial variation of 'commuter' or 'vacater'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚˌkeɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌməˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
(Maine) A person who stays temporarily in Maine during the summer, especially as a vacationer or seasonal resident.
summer visitor to Maine
informal

Examples

Many locals refer to the summercater influx as the start of the busy season.
The summercater left town in September, returning to his home in Boston.
Small businesses often rely on summercaters for a boost in summertime sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer resident - someone residing in a place only during summer months

Etymology

From 'summer' + regional colloquial extension '-cater,' denoting a person associated with a particular time or activity.
