enterolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.tə.roʊ.lɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə.rəʊ.lɪθ / (BrE)
A stone-like mass (calculus) formed in the intestines.
intestinal stone or calculus
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed an enterolith that was causing a blockage in the patient's intestine.
Chronic digestive issues can sometimes lead to the formation of an enterolith.
An enterolith may require surgical intervention if it becomes too large or obstructive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculus - a stone formed in the body, such as in the kidney or gallbladder

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) and 'lithos' (stone).
