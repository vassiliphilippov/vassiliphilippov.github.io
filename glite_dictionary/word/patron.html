patron

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.trən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.trən / (BrE)
a person who frequents and regularly supports a business, such as a shop, restaurant, or theatre
regular customer or client

Examples

The coffee shop is always bustling with loyal patrons every morning.
As a regular patron of the theatre, she knew all the staff by name.
Many patrons left positive reviews for the restaurant after their meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services from a business

Etymology

From Latin 'patronus' (protector, master), related to 'pater' (father).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.trən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.trən / (BrE)
a person who gives financial or other support to a person, organization, cause, or activity
supporter or benefactor

Examples

The arts festival would not have been possible without the generous support of its main patron.
She became a patron of several charities, donating both time and money.
As a patron of young writers, he provided mentorship and funding for new literary projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or other help to a person or cause
  • Synonym: sponsor - one who finances or promotes a person, organization, or event
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who actively opposes or is hostile to something

Etymology

From Latin 'patronus' (protector, advocate, from 'pater' father).
