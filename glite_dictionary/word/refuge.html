refuge

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛf.judʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛf.juːdʒ / (BrE)
a person, place, or thing that provides help, protection, or relief in times of trouble or danger
a source of help or protection

Examples

In times of crisis, close friends became his refuge from the outside world.
She found refuge in a quiet café when the storm began.
Many see music as a refuge during stressful periods.

Synset Examples

his only recourse was the police
took refuge in lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from bad weather or danger
  • Synonym: haven - a place of safety or refuge
  • Antonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury; the opposite of safety or protection

Etymology

From Latin refugium (a place to flee back to), from refugere (to run away).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛf.judʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛf.juːdʒ / (BrE)
a place that provides safety or protection from danger or trouble
a place of safety or protection

Examples

The hikers found refuge in a cave during the storm.
Many people sought refuge from the war in neighboring countries.
Her garden was a peaceful refuge from the stress of everyday life.

Synset Examples

He ran to safety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from danger
  • Antonym: peril - serious and immediate danger

Etymology

From Old French *refuge*, from Latin *refugium* (a place to flee back to), from *refugere* (to flee back).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛf.judʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛf.juːdʒ / (BrE)
a place or situation providing safety or shelter from danger or hardship
shelter from danger or hardship

Examples

During the storm, many families found refuge in the local community center.
The cave provided refuge for hikers caught in the sudden downpour.
Wild animals sought refuge among the thick bushes to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from bad weather or danger
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Antonym: exposure - the condition of being unprotected or vulnerable

Etymology

From Latin refugium (a place to flee back to), from refugere (to flee back).
