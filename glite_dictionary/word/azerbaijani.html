Azerbaijani

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːzərbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (AmE), / ˌæzəbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Azerbaijan
a person from Azerbaijan

Examples

The Azerbaijani I met in Baku spoke flawless English.
Many Azerbaijanis celebrate Novruz as their main spring festival.
She is proud of her Azerbaijani heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azerbaijan - country in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia

Etymology

From Azerbaijan, the name of the country, plus the suffix -i denoting origin or nationality.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːzərbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (AmE), / ˌæzəbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken chiefly in Azerbaijan and northwestern Iran
the Turkic language of Azerbaijan

Examples

She learned to speak Azerbaijani fluently while living in Baku.
Many Azerbaijani words share roots with Turkish vocabulary.
He is studying Azerbaijani for his research on Turkic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - the Turkic language spoken in Turkey

Etymology

From Azerbaijan, the name of the country, with suffix -i denoting affiliation or origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06939784-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌɑːzərbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (AmE), / ˌazəbaɪˈdʒɑːni / (BrE)
Relating to Azerbaijan, its people, or its culture.
of Azerbaijan or its people/culture

Examples

She prepared an Azerbaijani dish for the family gathering.
The exhibition showcases traditional Azerbaijani carpets and textiles.
He is fascinated by Azerbaijani music and folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azerbaijan - country in the Caucasus region of Eurasia

Etymology

From Azerbaijan, the country name, with -i, a common suffix for demonyms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02666265-a
Synset POS: a
