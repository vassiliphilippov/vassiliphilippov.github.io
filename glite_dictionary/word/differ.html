to differ

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪfɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪfə / (BrE)
to have or express a different opinion; to disagree
to have a different opinion

Examples

Although we differ on some issues, we both want the best for the team.
Scholars often differ in their interpretations of historical events.
I must politely differ with you on that point.

Synset Examples

I beg to differ!
She disagrees with her husband on many questions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to agree - share the same opinion
  • Synonym: to disagree - not have the same opinion; differ; dissent

Etymology

From Latin 'differre' (to carry apart, differ).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00806464-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪfər / (AmE), / ˈdɪfə / (BrE)
to be unlike or distinct from another person or thing in some respect
to be different; to be unlike

Examples

Twins often differ in personality despite looking alike.
Our opinions differ on how to solve the problem.
The new model differs significantly from last year's version.

Synset Examples

These two tests differ in only one respect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vary - to change or differ in amount or level
  • Antonym: to resemble - to be similar to

Etymology

From Latin 'differre' (to carry apart, differ), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'ferre' (to carry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02672316-v
Synset POS: v
