Quercus lobata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs loʊˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ləˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
a tall, deciduous oak tree native to California, with leathery leaves and slender, pointed acorns; commonly known as the valley oak
California valley oak tree species

Examples

The Quercus lobata is an iconic part of California's Central Valley landscape.
Botanists conducted a survey of Quercus lobata populations after the wildfires.
Many animals rely on the acorns from Quercus lobata as a source of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley oak - common name for Quercus lobata
  • Degree Higher: oak - tree or shrub of the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning oak; 'lobata' referring to its lobed leaves.
