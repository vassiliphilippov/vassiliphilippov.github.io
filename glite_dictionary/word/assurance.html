assurance

1. NOUN

/ əˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / əˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
confidence in yourself and your abilities; a feeling of certainty and self-reliance.
self-confidence; certainty in one's abilities

Examples

Her speech was delivered with such assurance that everyone listened attentively.
After years of practice, he performed the routine with quiet assurance.
The trainee lacks assurance in her abilities, but experience will help.

Synset Examples

his assurance in his superiority did not make him popular
after that failure he lost his confidence
she spoke with authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that you can do something well or succeed
  • Antonym: self-doubt - lack of confidence in oneself and one's abilities

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurance', from 'asseurer' (to assure), based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'securus' (secure).

2. NOUN

/ əˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / əˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
(British) Insurance, especially life insurance, that provides coverage for an event that is certain to occur, such as death.
British term for life insurance
british

Examples

Many people in the UK take out assurance policies to provide for their families after their death.
He discussed the differences between life assurance and life insurance with his financial advisor.
The company's main products include pensions, investment plans, and assurance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - coverage by contract to compensate for loss, damage, illness, or death

3. NOUN

/ əˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / əˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
a firm promise or commitment to do something, give something, or refrain from an action
a binding promise or guarantee

Examples

The company offered an assurance that repairs would be completed by Friday.
She sought assurance from the manager that the issue would not happen again.
He gave his assurance that he would never disclose the confidential information.

Synset Examples

an assurance of help when needed
signed a pledge never to reveal the secret

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - a formal assurance or promise, especially in writing, that certain conditions will be fulfilled
  • Synonym: pledge - a solemn promise or commitment to do or give something
  • Synonym: promise - a declaration or assurance that one will do something or that a particular thing will happen

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurance', from 'asseurer' (to assure); ultimately from Latin 'securus' (secure).

4. NOUN

/ əˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / əˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
a statement intended to make someone feel confident or certain about something
statement to inspire confidence

Examples

Despite the manager's repeated assurance, employees still worried about possible layoffs.
We received an assurance from the company that the repairs would be finished by Friday.
Her warm assurance put everyone at ease during the meeting.

Synset Examples

the President's assurances were not respected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - a formal promise or assurance that certain conditions will be fulfilled
  • Synonym: promise - an assurance that one will do something or that something will happen
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurance', from 'asseurer' (to secure) and Latin 'securus' (safe).
