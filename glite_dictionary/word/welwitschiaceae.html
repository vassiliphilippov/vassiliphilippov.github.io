Welwitschiaceae

1. NOUN

/ wɛlˌwɪtʃiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / wɛlˌwɪtʃiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of desert plants including the unique species Welwitschia, native to southwestern Africa.
family of desert plants (Welwitschia)

Examples

The Welwitschiaceae family consists of a single species, Welwitschia mirabilis.
Botanists study the adaptation mechanisms of Welwitschiaceae to harsh desert environments.
Welwitschiaceae is distinguished by its unusual leaf structure and extreme longevity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnetaceae - a related family of gymnosperms sometimes used in some classifications

Etymology

Named after Friedrich Welwitsch, the Austrian botanist who discovered the plant, with Latin botanical suffix -aceae (family).

2. NOUN

/ wɛlˌwɪtʃiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / wɛlˌwɪtʃiˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of gymnosperm plants, consisting of a single living species (Welwitschia mirabilis), native to deserts in southwestern Africa
plant family of Welwitschia

Examples

The Welwitschiaceae are known for their sole surviving species, Welwitschia mirabilis.
Botanists often study Welwitschiaceae to understand gymnosperm evolution.
Welwitschiaceae plants can survive in extremely arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnetaceae - a related family of gymnosperm plants

Etymology

Named after Friedrich Welwitsch, Austrian botanist; Latin -aceae denotes a plant family.
