Ceratopetalum

1. NOUN

/ sɛrətoʊˈpɛtələm / (AmE), / sɛrəˌtəʊˈpɛtələm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Cunoniaceae, native to Australia and neighbouring regions.
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The florist displayed a bouquet that included the delicate flowers of Ceratopetalum.
Several species of Ceratopetalum are prized for their ornamental value in Australian gardens.
Researchers studied the unique petal structure characteristic of the Ceratopetalum genus.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + petalon (petal), referring to the horn-shaped petals.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætoʊpɪˈtæləm / (AmE), / sɛˌratəʊpɪˈtæləm / (BrE)
A genus of dicotyledonous trees and shrubs, native to Australasia, in the family Cunoniaceae.
genus of flowering trees/shrubs in Cunoniaceae

Examples

The Ceratopetalum genus includes several species popular in Australian gardens for their striking flowers.
Many botanists study Ceratopetalum for its unique floral structures and ecological importance.
Ceratopetalum species, such as the New South Wales Christmas bush, are well-known for their ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek 'keratos' (horn) and 'petalon' (petal), referring to the horn-shaped petals.
