chequered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəd / (BrE)
having a pattern of squares in two or more different colors, typically in an alternating arrangement.
patterned with colored squares

Examples

The picnic blanket was bright and chequered with red and white squares.
He wore a chequered shirt to the party, making him stand out in the crowd.
They laid a chequered tablecloth on the table before setting out the dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checked - having a pattern of squares of different colours or shades

Etymology

From Middle English 'chekered', derived from 'checker', related to the game of chess and the checkered board pattern.
