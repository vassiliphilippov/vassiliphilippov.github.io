Celastraceae

1. NOUN

/ səˌlæstrəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sɪˌlastrəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and woody vines, typically with bright-colored fruits, known as the staff-vine or bittersweet family
plant family of trees and woody vines

Examples

Several species in the Celastraceae family are prized for their ornamental berries.
Botanists have identified over a hundred genera within the Celastraceae.
Many members of the Celastraceae are found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Celastrus, plus family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ səˌlæstrəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sɪˌlastrəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and woody vines usually bearing bright-colored fruits, found especially in tropical and subtropical regions
plant family of trees, shrubs, and vines

Examples

Many tropical forests contain members of the Celastraceae family, known for their colorful berries.
The botanist specialized in studying the various species within the Celastraceae.
Plants in the Celastraceae are often used in traditional medicine due to their diverse properties.

Etymology

From the type genus Celastrus, from Greek kelastros (a kind of evergreen shrub) + botanical family suffix -aceae.
