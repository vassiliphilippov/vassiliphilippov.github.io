2. NOUN

/ ˈhæmpʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmpʃə / (BrE)
A British breed of hornless, dark-faced domestic sheep known for its meat production.
British breed of hornless sheep

Examples

The farmer raised a flock of Hampshire sheep for their high-quality meat.
At the agricultural show, her prize-winning Hampshire drew much attention from the judges.
Because of their rapid growth, Hampshire lambs are popular in commercial farming.

Etymology

Named after Hampshire, a county in southern England where the breed originated.
