Indonesian

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndoʊˈniʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəˈniːziən / (BrE)
of or relating to Indonesia, its people, or their language.
relating to Indonesia or its people/language

Examples

She ordered an Indonesian dish at the restaurant.
The conference included several Indonesian speakers.
They learned about Indonesian culture in their world geography class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - a person from Indonesia or the language spoken there

Etymology

From Indonesia, the country name, plus the English adjectival suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02983739-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.doʊˈniː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dəʊˈniː.zi.ən / (BrE)
the official form of Malay used as the national language of Indonesia
the national language of Indonesia

Examples

She is fluent in both Indonesian and English.
The document was translated into Indonesian for local officials.
Many schools in Indonesia teach all subjects in Indonesian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - the Austronesian language from which Indonesian is derived

Etymology

From Indonesia + -an, denoting relation or belonging.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06951975-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊˈniʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈniːziən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Indonesia
person from Indonesia

Examples

Several Indonesians attended the international conference on climate change.
She befriended an Indonesian during her studies in Singapore.
The Indonesian shared stories about his hometown in Java.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - the language of Indonesia

Etymology

From Indonesia + -an (demonymic suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09733660-n
Synset POS: n
