Lemur catta

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːmər ˈkætə / (AmE), / ˈliːmjʊə ˈkatə / (BrE)
A species of lemur native to Madagascar, distinguished by its long, bushy tail with black and white rings.
a ring-tailed lemur species

Examples

The Lemur catta is easily recognized by its distinctive striped tail and social behavior.
At the zoo, children watched a group of Lemur catta leap from branch to branch.
Scientists have studied how the Lemur catta communicates using vocalizations and scent marking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring-tailed lemur - lemur species with a ringed tail

Etymology

From Latin: 'lemur' meaning 'ghost' or 'spirit' and 'catta' (cat), referring to its appearance and cat-like movements.
