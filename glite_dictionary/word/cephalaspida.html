Cephalaspida

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlæspɪdə / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlæspɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct group of primitive, armored, fish-like vertebrates known from the Silurian and Devonian periods; their taxonomy is not completely established.
extinct group of armored fish-like vertebrates

Examples

Fossil remains of Cephalaspida provide insight into the evolution of early vertebrates.
The Cephalaspida possessed distinct head shields that protected them from predators.
Paleontologists have debated the exact classification of Cephalaspida due to incomplete fossil evidence.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek kephalē (head) + aspis (shield), referencing the shield-like head armor.

2. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlæspɪdə / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˈspɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct group of primitive, armored, fish-like vertebrates, classified as a suborder; notable for their bony head shields
extinct suborder of armored jawless fish

Examples

Fossil evidence of Cephalaspida reveals details about early vertebrate evolution.
The museum's exhibit explained how Cephalaspida adapted to their prehistoric aquatic environments.
Scientists classify Cephalaspida as among the most ancient jawless fish.

Etymology

From Greek *kephalē* (head) + *aspis* (shield).
