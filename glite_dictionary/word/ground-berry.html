ground-berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping shrub of eastern North America with white bell-shaped flowers, spicy red berrylike fruit, and aromatic leaves that produce wintergreen oil.
creeping shrub with spicy red fruit

Examples

The ground-berry grows wild in many eastern North American forests, spreading its aromatic leaves close to the soil.
Many hikers enjoy tasting the spicy red fruit of the ground-berry during late summer.
Oil extracted from the leaves of the ground-berry is commonly known as wintergreen oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintergreen - another common name for this plant and plants producing wintergreen oil

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊnd ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, low-growing shrub with scarlet flowers and succulent, cranberry-like fruit, sometimes classified in the genus Styphelia.
a shrub with scarlet flowers and cranberry-like fruit

Examples

The ground-berry is prized for its sweet, succulent fruit in some regions.
Botanists often debate whether the ground-berry belongs in the genus Styphelia or elsewhere.
Children enjoyed searching for ground-berries along the forest floor during summer.
