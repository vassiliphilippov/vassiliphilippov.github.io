earmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a mark or tag placed on the ear of a domestic animal for identification purposes
identification mark on an animal's ear

Examples

Each sheep received a small earmark to distinguish it from others in the flock.
The farmer checked the earmark before returning the cow to its pen.
Loss of a clear earmark can make tracing cattle ownership difficult.

Etymology

From Middle English 'ermark', from 'ear' + 'mark'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a distinctive mark or feature that identifies a person or thing
distinctive identifying feature

Examples

Patience is often considered the earmark of a true leader.
The earmark of her writing is its sharp wit and clarity.
These paintings bear every earmark of the artist's early period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - a distinguishing characteristic, trait, or feature

Etymology

From Middle English *ermarke*, from ear + mark, originally referring to a mark clipped on the ear of livestock.

3. VERB

/ ˈɪrˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to designate or set aside a resource for a particular purpose or recipient
assign for a specific use

Examples

The government decided to earmark additional funds for disaster relief efforts.
She will earmark some of her savings for a trip next year.
Each year, the charity earmarks a portion of donations to support education programs.

Synset Examples

I will earmark this money for your research
She sets aside time for meditation every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: reserve - to save something for a particular purpose or person

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'earmark', marking the ear of livestock as a sign of ownership.
