Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

1. NOUN

/ məˌzoliːəm æt ˌhælɪˈkɑrnəsəs / (AmE), / məˌzɒliːəm ət ˌhælɪˈkɑːnəsəs / (BrE)
a monumental tomb built in 352 BC at Halicarnassus as a memorial to King Mausolus, regarded as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World; destroyed in 1402
ancient tomb, one of Seven Wonders of the World

Examples

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was considered a marvel of ancient engineering and architecture.
Historians often cite the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus as an inspiration for later monumental tombs.
Many artists have tried to recreate what the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus might have looked like before its destruction.

Etymology

Halicarnassus from ancient Greek Ἁλικαρνᾱσσός (Halikarnassós); mausoleum from King Mausolus's name.
