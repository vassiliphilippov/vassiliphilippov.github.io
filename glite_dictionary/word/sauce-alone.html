sauce-alone

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɔs əˈloʊn / (AmE), / ˌsɔːs əˈləʊn / (BrE)
A European herb (Alliaria petiolata) with leaves that smell like garlic, commonly known as garlic mustard.
European herb with garlic-scented leaves

Examples

The leaves of sauce-alone can be used in salads for a mild garlic flavor.
Many foragers seek out sauce-alone in spring woodlands for its culinary uses.
Some gardeners regard sauce-alone as an invasive plant despite its edible qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic mustard - a European herb with garlic-scented leaves, another name for Alliaria petiolata

Etymology

Possibly from a Middle English or regional term referring to the use of its leaves in sauces eaten alone.
