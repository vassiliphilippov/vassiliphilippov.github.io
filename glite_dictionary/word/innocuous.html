innocuous

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɑkjuəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjuəs / (BrE)
Not causing harm or injury; not likely to provoke strong emotion.
not harmful or offensive

Examples

The medicine appeared completely innocuous but caused severe side effects in rare cases.
Her remark was intended to be innocuous, but it was misunderstood by the group.
Most of the insects in the garden are innocuous and pose no threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: dangerous - able or likely to cause harm or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'innocuus', meaning harmless.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɑːkjuəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjuəs / (BrE)
Not harmful or offensive; lacking the intent or capacity to cause injury or harm.
not harmful or offensive

Examples

The conversation was entirely innocuous, with nothing that could offend anyone.
Although the insect looks scary, its sting is actually innocuous to humans.
Her comment seemed innocuous at first, but it was later misunderstood.

Synset Examples

an innocent prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: dangerous - able or likely to cause harm or injury

Etymology

From Latin innocuus, from in- (not) + nocuus (harmful).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɑkjuəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjuəs / (BrE)
Not likely to cause offense or provoke a strong reaction; harmless and inoffensive.
harmless or inoffensive

Examples

Her comment was completely innocuous and did not upset anyone at the meeting.
The doctor assured me that the procedure was innocuous and involved no risk.
They exchanged a few innocuous pleasantries before starting the discussion.

Synset Examples

it was an innocuous remark
confined himself to innocuous generalities
unobjectionable behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: offensive - causing someone to feel deeply hurt or upset

Etymology

From Latin innocuus, from in- ‘not’ + nocuus ‘hurtful’.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
