firth

1. NOUN

/ fɝːθ / (AmE), / fɜːθ / (BrE)
a long, narrow inlet of the sea; an estuary, especially on the coast of Scotland
long narrow estuary, esp. in Scotland

Examples

The River Clyde widens into a broad firth before it meets the sea.
They enjoyed kayaking along the scenic firth separating the mainland from the islands.
Many Scottish towns are situated on the edge of a firth, taking advantage of sheltered waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fjord - a long, deep, narrow inlet of the sea between high cliffs, especially in Norway

Etymology

From Old Norse 'fjörðr' (fjord, inlet).
