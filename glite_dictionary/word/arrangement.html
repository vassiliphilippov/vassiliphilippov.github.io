arrangement

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a piece of music that has been adapted for performance by a particular set of voices or instruments
musical adaptation for voices or instruments

Examples

The jazz band played an arrangement of a classic rock song.
Her arrangement for string quartet was praised by the conductor.
He wrote a new arrangement of the folk tune for choir and piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a musical work that has been changed to suit a different performance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07052146-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a group of things or people that have been organized or placed in a particular order; the result of arranging.
orderly grouping; organized placement

Examples

The florist created a beautiful arrangement of roses and lilies for the wedding.
The seating arrangement at the dinner party ensured that everyone felt included.
She admired the careful arrangement of books on the library shelf.

Synset Examples

a flower arrangement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - a group of people arranged for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: disarray - a state of disorder or untidiness

Etymology

From French 'arrangement', from arranger (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07955013-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a way in which things are organized or classified; the structure or order resulting from arranging parts or elements
organized structure or classification system

Examples

The arrangement of the seats made the room feel much more spacious.
We need a new arrangement for storing these files so they are easier to find.
The arrangement of chapters in this book helps readers understand the topic progressively.

Synset Examples

he changed the arrangement of the topics

he tried to understand their system of classification

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the act or process of arranging or ordering things
  • Synonym: system - a coherent set of principles or arrangements; an organized scheme

Etymology

From French *arrangement*, from arranger (to arrange).
