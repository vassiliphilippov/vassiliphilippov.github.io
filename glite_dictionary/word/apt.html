apt

1. ADJ

/ æpt / (AmE), / æpt / (BrE)
quick to learn or understand; having a natural ability to acquire knowledge or skills
quick to learn or understand

Examples

The new employee proved to be an apt learner, mastering the tasks in just a few days.
She was always an apt student, picking up complex concepts with ease.
His apt mind allowed him to solve challenging problems quickly.

Synset Examples

an apt pupil
you are a clever man … you reason well and your wit is bold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas
  • Antonym: slow - not quick to learn or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'aptus' meaning 'fit, suitable'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01337910-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ æpt / (AmE), / æpt / (BrE)
suitable or appropriate in the circumstances; strikingly fitting or pertinent.
suitable or appropriate; fitting

Examples

Her remarks were especially apt given the seriousness of the situation.
He made an apt comparison that clarified the complex topic for everyone.
The quote she chose was apt for the introduction of her essay.

Synset Examples

the successful copywriter is a master of apposite and evocative verbal images
an apt reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: inapt - not appropriate or suitable

Etymology

From Latin aptus (fit, suitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00139582-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ æpt / (AmE), / æpt / (BrE)
having a tendency to do something; likely or inclined (especially with 'to' followed by a verb).
having a tendency; likely to do something

Examples

Young children are apt to imitate the behavior they see in adults.
He is apt to forget names if he is not reminded.
People living near rivers are apt to experience flooding during heavy rains.

Synset Examples

he is apt to ignore matters he considers unimportant
I am not minded to answer any questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likely - probable or having a good chance of happening
  • Synonym: prone - having a tendency or inclination; likely to do something
  • Antonym: unlikely - not probable; not likely to happen

Etymology

From Latin aptus (fit, suited, proper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01295534-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ æpt / (AmE), / æpt / (BrE)
likely or having a tendency to do something, especially something undesirable or unpleasant.
likely or prone to do something

Examples

Children are apt to make mistakes when they are learning something new.
He is apt to forget important dates unless he writes them down.
Cars left unlocked in this area are apt to be stolen.

Synset Examples

he is apt to lose
she is liable to forget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likely - having a high probability of occurring or being true
  • Synonym: liable - likely to do or to be something, often negative
  • Antonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true

Etymology

From Latin aptus (fit, suitable).
