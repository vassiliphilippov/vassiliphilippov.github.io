Curculionidae

1. NOUN

/ kɝːˌkjuːliˈɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˌkjuːliˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles, commonly known as true weevils or snout beetles, characterized by their elongated snouts.
family of true (snout) weevils

Examples

Scientists found several species of Curculionidae in the forest during their field study.
The Curculionidae family includes many pests that affect crops worldwide.
Entomologists have extensively cataloged the vast diversity within the Curculionidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weevil - a small beetle with an elongated snout, many of which are pests

Etymology

From New Latin Curculionidae, from Curculio (a weevil genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kɝːˌkjuːliˈɒnɪdiː / (AmE), / kɜːˌkjuːliˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles, commonly known as true weevils or snout beetles, characterized by their elongated snouts.
family of true weevils (snout beetles)

Examples

There are more than 40,000 species in the Curculionidae family worldwide.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control pests that belong to the Curculionidae.
The scientist collected several Curculionidae specimens for her entomology research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weevil - A beetle of the family Curculionidae, usually with a long snout.

Etymology

From New Latin Curculionidae, from Curculio (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
