tracheophyte

1. NOUN

/ treɪˈkiːəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / trəˈkiːəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A plant belonging to a large group that has a vascular system for transporting water and nutrients, including ferns, gymnosperms, and angiosperms.
vascular plant such as ferns and flowering plants
medical

Examples

Ferns are a type of tracheophyte that reproduce using spores instead of seeds.
All tracheophyte species possess specialized tissues for transporting water and minerals.
Scientists believe that the evolution of the tracheophyte was a major step in the colonization of land by plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonvascular plant - Plant lacking a vascular system, such as mosses and liverworts

Etymology

From Greek 'tracheia' (windpipe, vessel) + 'phyton' (plant).
