old-timer

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
an elderly man, especially one who has been involved in an activity or community for a long time
elderly man; veteran
informal

Examples

The old-timer shared fascinating stories from his youth in the village.
Everyone at the club respected the old-timer who had been a member for decades.
You could always count on advice from an old-timer like him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular area or field
  • Antonym: newcomer - a person who has recently arrived or started in a place

Etymology

From 'old' + 'timer', originally U.S. slang from the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊld ˌtaɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊld ˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
a person who has long experience in a particular activity, place, or organization, especially someone respected for their service or knowledge
experienced or long-serving person

Examples

As an old-timer in the business, she always offers valuable advice to new employees.
Many old-timers gathered to celebrate the factory's centennial anniversary.
The old-timer shared stories about the way things used to be in the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with much experience in a field, especially a long-serving member
