lithography

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈθɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / lɪˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of printing from a flat surface, such as a stone or metal plate, by treating the image area so that ink adheres only to it
printing process using a flat surface

Examples

The artist specialized in lithography, creating intricate prints using traditional methods.
Many famous posters from the 19th century were produced by lithography.
She took a class to learn the basics of lithography and printmaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a printmaking technique where an image is incised into a surface

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + -graphia (writing).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈθɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / lɪˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a method of printing in which an image is drawn with a greasy substance on a flat stone or metal plate and then inked and pressed onto paper
printing process using stone or metal plates

Examples

The artist used lithography to produce limited edition prints.
Advances in lithography have revolutionized the printing industry over the years.
She studied lithography during her time at the art school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - method of printing by incising a design onto a hard surface
  • Synonym: etching - printing technique using acid to carve a design into metal

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + -graphy (writing).
