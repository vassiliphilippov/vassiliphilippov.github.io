hydric

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ.drɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɪd.rɪk / (BrE)
(of soil or habitat) having or characterized by a high level of moisture; wet or saturated.
characterized by high moisture

Examples

Many wetland plants thrive in hydric soils with frequent flooding.
The restoration plan focuses on preserving the hydric habitat of native amphibians.
Hydrologists monitor changes in hydric conditions to protect endangered species.

Synset Examples

a hydric habitat

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mesic - having a moderate amount of moisture
  • Antonym: xeric - characterized by a dry, low-moisture environment

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' meaning 'water', with the suffix '-ic'.
