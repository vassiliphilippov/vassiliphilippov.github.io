Zaglossus

1. NOUN

/ zəˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / zəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
a genus of echidnas known as long-beaked echidnas, native to New Guinea
genus of long-beaked echidnas

Examples

The Zaglossus is distinguished from other echidnas by its long, slender snout.
Scientists have discovered three living species of Zaglossus in the forests of New Guinea.
Fossil evidence suggests that Zaglossus once inhabited parts of Australia as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - egg-laying spiny mammal; member of family Tachyglossidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek prefix 'za-' (very) and Greek 'glōssa' (tongue).

2. NOUN

/ zəˈɡlɒsəs / (AmE), / zəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of long-beaked echidnas, native to New Guinea, in the family Tachyglossidae.
genus of long-beaked echidnas

Examples

The Zaglossus genus includes several species of long-beaked echidnas found in New Guinea.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the Zaglossus genus during an expedition.
Unlike the short-beaked echidna, members of Zaglossus are much larger and have longer snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - a spiny egg-laying mammal from the family Tachyglossidae
  • Synonym: Tachyglossus - another genus in the family Tachyglossidae

Etymology

From Greek 'za' (very) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to its distinctive tongue.
