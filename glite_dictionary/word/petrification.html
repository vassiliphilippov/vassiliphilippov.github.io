petrification

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtrəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which organic material, such as wood, is turned into stone through gradual mineralization, while retaining its original structure.
turning organic matter into stone

Examples

The petrification of trees can take millions of years in the right conditions.
Visitors marveled at the petrification of ancient wood displayed in the museum.
Scientists study the petrification process to learn about prehistoric ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossilization - the process of becoming a fossil
  • Antonym: decay - process by which organic matter breaks down naturally

Etymology

From Medieval Latin petrificatio, from Latin petra (stone) + facere (to make).

2. NOUN

the process of turning into stone

Examples

Scientists studied the petrification process in samples collected from the volcanic region.
The museum had an entire section dedicated to petrification and fossilized remains.
