trammel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræməl / (AmE), / ˈtræməl / (BrE)
A device or restraint used for training a horse to amble, typically by restricting its movement.
restraint for training horses to amble

Examples

The trainer attached a trammel to the horse's legs to help it learn the amble gait.
Historically, a trammel was often used in the early stages of a horse's training.
Using a trammel can encourage smoother and more controlled movement in horses being taught to amble.

Etymology

From Old French tramail (net for catching fish), from Late Latin tremaculum.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræməl / (AmE), / ˈtræməl / (BrE)
A fishing net made of three layers, with two outer layers of coarse mesh and an inner layer of fine mesh, used to entangle fish.
three-layered fishing net

Examples

The fishermen lowered the trammel into the water at dusk to catch the night’s haul.
A traditional trammel works by entangling fish as they try to swim through the layered meshes.
She learned to mend a torn trammel from her grandfather, who had fished with them for decades.

Etymology

From Old French tramail, from Late Latin tremaculum (net with three layers), from Latin tres (three) + macula (mesh).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtræməl / (AmE), / ˈtræməl / (BrE)
A restriction or impediment to freedom of action; something that confines, restrains, or limits movement or progress.
restraint or impediment to freedom

Examples

The new regulations acted as a trammel on innovation in the industry.
He broke free from the trammels of tradition and started his own business.
Fearing political trammels, she chose to work independently.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think freely
