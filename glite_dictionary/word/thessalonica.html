Thessalonica

1. PROPN

/ ˌθɛs.ə.ləˈnaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌθɛs.ə.ləˈnaɪ.kə / (BrE)
a historic port city in northeastern Greece, situated on an inlet of the Aegean Sea, now known as Thessaloniki; the country's second largest city
historic port city in northeastern Greece

Examples

The apostle Paul wrote letters to the early Christians in Thessalonica.
Archaeological sites in Thessalonica attract tourists from all over the world.
After leaving Athens, we traveled north to the bustling city of Thessalonica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thessaloniki - modern name of Thessalonica, port city in northeastern Greece

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θεσσαλονίκη (Thessaloníkē), named after Thessalonike, a half-sister of Alexander the Great.
