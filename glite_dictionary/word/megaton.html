megaton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛgəˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈmɛgətʌn / (BrE)
A unit of explosive power equivalent to one million tons of TNT.
unit of explosive force (one million tons)

Examples

The bomb had a yield of one megaton and was capable of devastating an entire city.
Scientists estimated the asteroid impact released energy equal to ten megatons of TNT.
A modern nuclear weapon may have an explosive force measured in megatons.

Etymology

From mega- (Greek 'megas', meaning 'great') plus ton.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡətʌn / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the explosive power of a nuclear weapon, equal to the energy released by one million tons of TNT.
unit of explosive power (one million tons of TNT)

Examples

The new bomb was estimated to have a yield of 10 megatons.
Scientists discussed the impact of a megaton explosion on the environment.
The device has an explosive force equivalent to several megatons of TNT.

Etymology

Formed from mega- (meaning 'million') + ton.
