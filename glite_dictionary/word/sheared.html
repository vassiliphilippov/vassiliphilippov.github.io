sheared

1. ADJ

/ ʃɪrd / (AmE), / ʃɪəd / (BrE)
(of hair, wool, or fur) cut or clipped off, especially as if using shears or clippers.
having hair or wool cut off

Examples

The freshly sheared sheep looked much smaller without their thick wool.
After being sheared for summer, the dog felt much cooler in the heat.
The sheared poodle pranced around the yard showing off its new haircut.

Synset Examples

picked up the baby's shorn curls from the floor
naked as a sheared sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorn - having had hair, wool, or fleece cut off
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02161837-a

2. ADJ

/ ʃɪrd / (AmE), / ʃɪəd / (BrE)
(of fur, wool, or similar material) cut or trimmed to a uniform length, especially for use in clothing or textiles.
fur or wool cut to uniform length

Examples

The jacket is made from sheared mink, giving it a soft, even texture.
She prefers sheared wool carpets because they feel smoother underfoot.
A sheared lambskin coat is both warm and stylish for winter.

Synset Examples

a coat of sheared lamb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unshorn - not sheared; with natural, untrimmed wool or hair

Etymology

From the verb 'shear', Old English *scearan* (to cut).
