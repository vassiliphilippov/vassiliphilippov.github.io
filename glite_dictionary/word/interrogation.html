interrogation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally or systematically questioning someone, especially for information or an official investigation
formal or official questioning

Examples

The suspect was taken to the police station for interrogation after the incident.
After hours of interrogation, he finally confessed to the crime.
The detective's interrogation revealed inconsistencies in the witness's story.

Synset Examples

there was a question about my training
we made inquiries of all those who were present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioning - the action of asking questions

Etymology

From Latin interrogatio (a questioning), from interrogare (to question).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈreɪ.ɡə.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈreɪ.ɡə.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally and systematically asking someone a series of questions, especially to obtain information or evidence
formal questioning, often by authorities

Examples

After hours of intense interrogation, the suspect finally confessed.
The police carried out an interrogation to find out more about the incident.
Lawyers are present during an interrogation to protect the rights of the person being questioned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioning - the act of asking questions, especially for information

Etymology

From Latin interrogatio, from interrogare meaning 'to question'

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈɹoʊ.ɡəˌʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈɹɒ.ɡəˌʃən / (BrE)
A transmission sent to elicit a response from a transponder, especially in telecommunications and aviation systems.
signal sent to trigger transponder reply

Examples

The radar system sends an interrogation to aircraft transponders to determine their identity and altitude.
When the beacon receives the interrogation, it automatically transmits a coded reply signal.
Modern air traffic control relies on rapid interrogation and response cycles to track multiple aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: query - a request for information from a computer or database

Etymology

From Latin interrogatio (a questioning), via Old French interrogation.

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal or systematic process of asking questions, especially in order to get information; less commonly, a sentence or question that seeks a reply.
formal questioning or question seeking a reply

Examples

The detective's interrogation focused on the suspect's whereabouts that night.
During the interview, she responded calmly to every interrogation.
His careful phrasing turned the simple remark into an interrogation.

Synset Examples

he asked a direct question
he had trouble phrasing his interrogations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence or expression asking for information or a reply
  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information

Etymology

From Latin interrogatio (a question, inquiry), from interrogare (to ask, question).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07211342-n
Synset POS: n
