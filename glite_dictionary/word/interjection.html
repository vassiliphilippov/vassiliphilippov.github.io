interjection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a word or phrase used as an abrupt exclamation expressing emotion, such as surprise, pain, or joy
abrupt exclamatory word or phrase

Examples

The word 'ouch' is an interjection used to express pain.
In English, the interjection 'wow' conveys surprise or admiration.
Teachers often explain how an interjection can add emotion to a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation - an abrupt or sudden utterance expressing strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin interiectio, from intericere 'to throw between' (inter- 'between' + iacere 'to throw').

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of interrupting with a comment, remark, or action; an abrupt interruption in speech, conversation, or activity
an abrupt interruption or comment

Examples

Her sudden interjection caught everyone off guard during the meeting.
There was a brief interjection from the audience before the speaker continued.
He made an interjection to clarify his earlier point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - an act or instance of interrupting

Etymology

From Latin 'interjectio', from 'intericere' (to throw between).
