deflationary

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃənəri / (BrE)
(of economic conditions or policies) tending to cause a decrease in consumer prices or an increase in the purchasing power of money.
tending to cause falling prices or rising money value

Examples

The government introduced deflationary policies to combat excessive inflation.
A deflationary period can lead to reduced consumer spending and economic slowdown.
Analysts warned that the central bank's actions might have a deflationary effect on the economy.

Synset Examples

deflationary measures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflationary - tending to cause increases in consumer prices or decreases in purchasing power

Etymology

From 'deflation' (Latin 'deflare', to blow away) + '-ary'.
