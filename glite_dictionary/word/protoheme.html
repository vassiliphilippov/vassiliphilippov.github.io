protoheme

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtəˌhim / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊhiːm / (BrE)
a complex red organic pigment containing iron, found in hemoglobin and other proteins, where it binds oxygen
iron-containing pigment in hemoglobin
medical

Examples

The structure of protoheme allows it to actively participate in oxygen transport within red blood cells.
Researchers studied the properties of protoheme to better understand its role in metabolic processes.
A deficiency in protoheme can lead to disorders affecting the body's ability to carry oxygen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme - iron-containing compound forming the nonprotein part of hemoglobin

Etymology

From Greek 'prōto-' (first) and 'haima' (blood).
