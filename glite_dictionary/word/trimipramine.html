trimipramine

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈmɪprəˌmiːn / (AmE), / trɪˈmɪprəmiːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat depression, anxiety, and sometimes insomnia
tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed trimipramine to help manage her depression and insomnia.
Some patients report feeling less anxious after starting trimipramine treatment.
Despite its age, trimipramine remains an option for certain cases of major depressive disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amitriptyline - a tricyclic antidepressant drug similar in use
  • Synonym: imipramine - a related tricyclic antidepressant drug
  • Synonym: antidepressant - a drug used to treat depression

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: 'tri-' (three), 'mipramine' from derivatives of imipramine.
