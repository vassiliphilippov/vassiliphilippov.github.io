Martynia arenaria

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈtɪniə ɛrˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / mɑːˈtɪniə əˈnɛːriə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant sometimes classified in the genus Martynia.
species of flowering plant

Examples

The desert habitat perfectly suits the Martynia arenaria with its specialized seed pods.
Botanists debated whether Martynia arenaria should remain in the Martynia genus or be reclassified.
Collectors prized the unusual flowers of Martynia arenaria in their botanical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martynia - a genus of flowering plants native to the Americas

Etymology

Martynia: named after John Martyn, English botanist; arenaria: from Latin 'arenarius', meaning 'growing in sand'.
