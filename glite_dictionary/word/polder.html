polder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒldə / (BrE)
an area of low-lying land that has been reclaimed from a body of water and is protected by dikes, especially in the Netherlands
reclaimed low-lying land protected by dikes

Examples

The Dutch are famous for their ability to create polders by reclaiming land from the sea.
A network of windmills once helped drain water from the polder to keep it dry.
Many people in the Netherlands live on polders that are several meters below sea level.

Etymology

From Dutch 'polder', meaning reclaimed land that is protected by dikes.
