foreign

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.ən / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ən / (BrE)
relating to or coming from a country or language other than one's own; unfamiliar or exotic due to being from another place.
from another country; not native

Examples

Many students take a foreign language as part of their education.
He felt lost in the foreign city, where nothing looked familiar.
The company imports foreign goods from several different countries.

Synset Examples

foreign nations
a foreign accent
on business in a foreign city

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - relating to one's own country; opposite of foreign

Etymology

From Latin 'foris' (outside).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038139-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrən / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən / (BrE)
introduced from an outside source and not belonging naturally to what contains it; not native or inherent.
not native; introduced from outside

Examples

The engineer checked the equipment for foreign objects that could cause malfunctions.
Milk may sometimes contain foreign particles, so it must be filtered before processing.
Doctors work to remove any foreign material from wounds to prevent infection.

Synset Examples

water free of extraneous matter
foreign particles in milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraneous - irrelevant or unrelated to the subject; coming from outside

Etymology

From Latin 'foris' (outside), via Old French 'forain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02124744-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪn / (BrE)
not naturally belonging to or part of something; strange or unconnected to the essential nature of a person or thing
not naturally part of something; strange

Examples

The idea of lying is foreign to his honest nature.
Vegetarianism was once quite foreign to the local cuisine.
The humor in that show is foreign to my taste.

Synset Examples

an economic theory alien to the spirit of capitalism
the mysticism so foreign to the French mind and temper
jealousy is foreign to her nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alien - strange and not part of the usual character of someone or something
  • Antonym: native - belonging naturally to a place or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'foris' (outside), via Old French 'forain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01352775-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.ən / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ən / (BrE)
relating to or dealing with other countries, especially in contrast to one's own country.
relating to other countries

Examples

The government announced changes in its foreign policy to strengthen international relations.
Many universities offer degrees in foreign affairs and international studies.
The foreign minister met with his counterparts from several neighboring countries.

Synset Examples

foreign trade
a foreign office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - relating to one's own country; not foreign

Etymology

From Latin 'foris' (outside).
