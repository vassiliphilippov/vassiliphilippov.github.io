Achillea

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪliə / (AmE), / əˈkɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, often aromatic herbs in the daisy family, native to northern temperate regions and including yarrow and milfoil.
genus of aromatic herbs including yarrow

Examples

Many gardeners plant Achillea for its clusters of tiny, long-lasting flowers.
The leaves of Achillea are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
Species within the genus Achillea are prized for their drought resistance and aromatic foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yarrow - a common plant of the genus Achillea, known for its feathery leaves and flat-topped clusters of flowers
  • Synonym: milfoil - another common name for plants of the genus Achillea, especially Achillea millefolium

Etymology

From Latin 'Achillea', named after the Greek hero Achilles, who, according to legend, used yarrow to treat wounds.
