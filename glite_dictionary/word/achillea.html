achillea

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪliə / (AmE), / əˈkɪliːə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Achillea, native to Europe, with small white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters; commonly known as yarrow
a European plant with white flowers; yarrow

Examples

Gardeners often plant achillea in borders for its attractive clusters of flowers.
The dried leaves of achillea have been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
I spotted a patch of wild achillea growing by the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yarrow - a common name for plants of the genus Achillea

Etymology

From Latin Achillea, named after the Greek hero Achilles, who was said to have used yarrow to treat wounds.
