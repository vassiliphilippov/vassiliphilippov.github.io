to pulverise

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌlvəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌlvəraɪz / (BrE)
To destroy or demolish something completely, reducing it to ruin or fragments.
destroy completely; reduce to fragments

Examples

The bomb pulverised the entire city block in seconds.
The opposition was pulverised in the final match, losing by a huge margin.
Years of neglect had pulverised the once-beautiful mansion into ruins.

Synset Examples

the wrecking ball demolished the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to completely destroy a building or structure
  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy utterly; obliterate

Etymology

From Latin 'pulvis' (dust).

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌlvəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌlvəraɪz / (BrE)
to crush or grind something into a fine powder or dust
crush into powder or dust

Examples

The chef used a mortar and pestle to pulverise the spices.
Industrial grinders can pulverise rocks into fine dust for construction purposes.
She pulverised the dried herbs before adding them to the mix.

Synset Examples

pulverize the grains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - to reduce something to small particles or powder by crushing it
  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or is damaged
  • Antonym: to coarsen - to make or become rough or less fine

Etymology

From Latin 'pulverisare', from 'pulvis' (dust, powder).
