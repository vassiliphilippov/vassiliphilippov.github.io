FinCEN

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnˌsɛn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnˌsɛn / (BrE)
the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network, a bureau of the U.S. Department of the Treasury responsible for combating money laundering and other financial crimes
U.S. Treasury bureau fighting financial crimes
american

Examples

The bank reported suspicious transactions to FinCEN as required by federal law.
FinCEN issues regulations to help prevent money laundering in the financial system.
Lawyers frequently consult FinCEN guidance when ensuring compliance with anti-money laundering rules.
