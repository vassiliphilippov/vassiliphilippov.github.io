Thunnus albacares

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌn.əs ˌæl.bəˈkɛr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.əs ˌæl.bəˈkɛə.riːz / (BrE)
A species of large, predatory tuna, commonly known as the yellowfin tuna, found in tropical oceans worldwide.
species of yellowfin tuna

Examples

Thunnus albacares is highly prized by commercial fisheries due to its fast growth and large size.
The diet of Thunnus albacares consists mainly of smaller fish, squid, and crustaceans.
Conservationists are concerned about declining populations of Thunnus albacares in certain parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowfin tuna - Common name for Thunnus albacares, a large tuna species

Etymology

From Latin: Thunnus (tuna) and albacares (white flesh).
