tawny-brown

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔni ˌbraʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɔːni ˌbraʊn / (BrE)
of a light brown to brownish-orange color, resembling the color of tanned leather
light brown or brownish-orange color

Examples

The lion's coat was a distinctive tawny-brown that blended perfectly with the savanna grass.
She wore a tawny-brown suede jacket that matched her boots.
The old book's pages had faded to a soft tawny-brown over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawny - a warm, sandy, or orange-brown color

Etymology

From 'tawny' (Middle English taunye) and 'brown' (Old English brūn), referring to the color of tanned leather.
