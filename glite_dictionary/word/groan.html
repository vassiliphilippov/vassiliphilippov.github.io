groan

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn / (BrE)
a low, mournful sound expressing pain, discomfort, or disapproval
sound of pain or disapproval

Examples

A loud groan echoed through the room as the patient tried to sit up.
She let out a groan of frustration when her computer crashed again.
There was a collective groan from the audience at the bad joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moan - a long, low sound expressing physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: cheer - a shout of encouragement or approval

Etymology

From Old English grānian (to groan), related to Old High German grīnan (to grimace, to growl).

2. VERB

/ ɡroʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn / (BrE)
to make a low, deep sound showing pain, discomfort, or displeasure
make a low sound expressing pain or displeasure

Examples

The children groaned when they saw the amount of homework assigned for the weekend.
She groaned in pain after twisting her ankle on the stairs.
He groaned loudly as he struggled to lift the heavy box.

Synset Examples

The students groaned when the professor got out the exam booklets
The ancient door moaned when opened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moan - to make a low sound expressing pain or unhappiness

Etymology

From Old English grānian (to groan), related to German 'grämen'.

Synset POS: v
