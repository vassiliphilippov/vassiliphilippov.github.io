vena cerebri media

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə səˈriː.braɪ ˈmiː.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə sɛˈriː.bri ˈmiː.di.ə / (BrE)
either of two cerebral veins that drain blood from the lateral surface of the brain toward the cerebral venous sinuses
a cerebral vein; drains brain's lateral surface
medical

Examples

The vena cerebri media plays a crucial role in draining the lateral hemispheres of the brain.
A blockage in the vena cerebri media can lead to venous infarction in the brain tissue.
During the neurosurgical procedure, the surgeon carefully identified the vena cerebri media to avoid damaging vital blood flow.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'cerebri' (of the brain), 'media' (middle).
