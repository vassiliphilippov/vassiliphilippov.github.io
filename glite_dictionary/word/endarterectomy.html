endarterectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnˌdɑrtəˈrɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌɛnˌdɑːtərˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove the inner lining of an artery that has become clogged with atherosclerosis
surgical removal of clogged artery lining
medical

Examples

The patient underwent an endarterectomy to restore blood flow to his carotid artery.
Complications from the endarterectomy were minimal, and recovery was swift.
Doctors may recommend an endarterectomy for individuals with significant arterial blockages.

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'arteria' (artery) + 'ektomē' (excision, cutting out).
