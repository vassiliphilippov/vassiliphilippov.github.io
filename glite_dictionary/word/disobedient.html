disobedient

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.əˈbi.di.ənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈbiː.di.ənt / (BrE)
refusing or failing to obey rules or someone in authority
refusing to obey authority

Examples

The teacher found it hard to manage the disobedient children in her class.
The dog's disobedient behavior made training difficult for its owner.
His disobedient attitude often got him into trouble at school.

Synset Examples

unruly teenagers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests; submissive to authority
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control; not following rules or discipline

Etymology

From Latin disobedient-, disobediens, present participle of disobedire (to disobey).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiənt / (BrE)
refusing or failing to obey rules, orders, or authority
refusing to obey

Examples

The teacher found the disobedient students difficult to manage during class activities.
Her disobedient puppy refused to sit or stay when told.
If you continue to be disobedient, there will be consequences for your actions.

Synset Examples

disobedient children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedient - willing to obey rules or authority
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control; not following rules or discipline

Etymology

From Latin disobedientem, from dis- (not) + oboediens (obedient).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01618017-a
Synset POS: a
