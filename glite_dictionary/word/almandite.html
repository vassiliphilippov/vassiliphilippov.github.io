almandite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.mənˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈalməndʌɪt / (BrE)
A deep red variety of garnet consisting of iron aluminum silicate, used as a gemstone.
deep red variety of garnet

Examples

The jeweler showed us a beautiful ring set with a large almandite garnet.
Geologists discovered deposits of almandite in the metamorphic rocks of the region.
Her necklace featured alternating beads of almandite and gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

From French 'almandine', from Middle Latin 'almandina', influenced by 'Almand' (German for 'German').
