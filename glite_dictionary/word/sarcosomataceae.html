Sarcosomataceae

1. NOUN

/ sɑːrˌkoʊsəˌmætəˈsiː / (AmE), / sɑːkəˌsəʊməˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of ascomycetous fungi consisting of about 10 genera and 57 species, found mainly in temperate regions
family of ascomycetous fungi

Examples

The Sarcosomataceae are known for their gelatinous fruiting bodies in forest litter.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Sarcosomataceae family in the Pacific Northwest.
Members of the Sarcosomataceae can be identified by their dark, tough fruiting structures.

Etymology

From Sarcosoma (a genus within the family) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant and fungal families, from Latin).
