to alkalinise

1. VERB

/ ˈælkəˌlaɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈælkəˌlaɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become alkaline; to increase the alkalinity of a substance
make or become alkaline

Examples

Adding baking soda will alkalinise the solution and raise its pH.
Farmers often alkalinise fields by spreading lime to improve crop yields.
Certain medications are used to alkalinise urine in treating some medical conditions.

Synset Examples

applying lime will alkalinise soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to make or become acidic

Etymology

From alkali, from Arabic 'al-qalī' (ashes of saltwort), combined with the suffix '-ise' (making a verb)

2. VERB

/ ˈæl.kə.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.lɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to become or make alkaline; to increase the pH so that a substance is less acidic
become or make alkaline

Examples

Adding baking soda will often alkalinise the solution and neutralize excess acidity.
Some treatments are designed to alkalinise the urine, making it less irritating to the bladder.
The water was alkalinised to reduce metal corrosion in the plumbing system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to become or make acidic

Etymology

From 'alkali' (Arabic 'al-qaly', the ashes) + '-ise' (verbal suffix).
