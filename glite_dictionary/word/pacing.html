pacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(music) The speed or rate at which a musical composition is played or performed.
speed of music performance

Examples

The pacing of the piece was too slow for the energetic dance routine.
She adjusted the pacing to create more excitement in the middle of the composition.
Good pacing can dramatically affect the listener's emotional response to the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempo - The rate of speed of a musical piece

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of walking back and forth with slow, regular steps, especially because of anxiety or deep thought
walking back and forth with slow, steady steps

Examples

She was pacing the room, anxiously waiting for the phone to ring.
His pacing calmed him as he prepared for the final exam.
The constant pacing in the hallway distracted the other students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stride - a long, decisive step or the manner of walking
