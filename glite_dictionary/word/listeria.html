listeria

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / lɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
any bacterium of the genus Listeria, some species of which can cause foodborne illness in humans and animals
genus of bacteria, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Packaged salads were recalled after tests detected listeria contamination.
Infection with listeria can be especially dangerous for pregnant women.
Scientists isolated the listeria bacteria from the contaminated cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmonella - genus of bacteria that can cause food poisoning

Etymology

Named after Joseph Lister, British surgeon and pioneer of antiseptic surgery.
