Blastocladiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæstoʊklæˈdiːəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˌblastəʊklæˈdiːəliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi that reproduce asexually by means of thick-walled resting spores, which produce zoospores upon germination; sometimes classified in Oomycetes.
an order of fungi with unique asexual reproduction
medical

Examples

Members of the Blastocladiales exhibit a unique method of asexual reproduction involving resting spores.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Blastocladiales in tropical wetlands.
The Blastocladiales are sometimes grouped with the Oomycetes due to similarities in their life cycles.

Etymology

From the genus name Blastocladia, with the taxonomic ending -ales denoting an order.

2. NOUN

an order of fungi that reproduce asexually by thick-walled resting spores, which germinate to produce zoospores; sometimes classified in the class Oomycetes
order of fungi with spore-based asexual reproduction
medical

Examples

Members of the Blastocladiales are studied for their unique method of asexual reproduction.
The fungal order Blastocladiales includes species that infect aquatic plants.
Researchers identified several Blastocladiales species in the pond's ecosystem.

Etymology

From Greek 'blastós' (bud, sprout) + 'klados' (branch), referring to the branching structures, with taxonomic suffix -ales.
