Styrax japonicum

1. NOUN

/ ˌstaɪræks dʒəˈpoʊnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌstaɪræks dʒəˈpəʊnɪkəm / (BrE)
a deciduous shrubby tree native to China and Japan, known for its fragrant white flowers
shrubby tree native to China and Japan

Examples

The Styrax japonicum brightened our garden with its delicate clusters of white flowers.
Botanists often study Styrax japonicum for its ornamental value and adaptability.
They planted a young Styrax japonicum near the pond to enhance the landscape.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Styrax' (the genus) and 'japonicum' (Latin for 'of Japan').
