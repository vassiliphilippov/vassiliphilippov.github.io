Sambo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈsæm.bəʊ / (BrE)
(historical, offensive) A stock character in literature and culture typified by exaggeratedly stereotypical features, often used to caricature Black people. The term and character are now considered highly offensive and racist.
offensive Black caricature in literature/culture
offensive archaic

Examples

The children's book from the 19th century features the Sambo character prominently.
The discussion addressed the harmful impact of the Sambo stereotype in American media.
Modern scholars condemn the depiction of Sambo as racist and damaging.

Synset Examples

The book explores the character of Sambo in depth.

Etymology

Probably from Spanish *zambo* ('bow-legged', 'person of mixed African and Indigenous ancestry').
