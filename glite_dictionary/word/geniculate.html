geniculate

1. ADJ

/ dʒəˈnɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
bent at a sharp angle, especially like a knee joint; sharply angled or bent
bent at a sharp angle

Examples

The geniculate stalk of the plant allows it to catch sunlight more effectively.
Some architectural features, such as geniculate columns, stand out for their sharp bends.
The nerve takes a geniculate turn before entering the inner ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angular - having angles or sharp corners
  • Antonym: straight - not bent or curved; direct in line or shape

Etymology

From Latin 'geniculatus', from 'geniculum' (little knee), diminutive of 'genu' (knee).
