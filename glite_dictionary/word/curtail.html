to curtail

1. VERB

/ kɚˈteɪl / (AmE), / kɜːˈteɪl / (BrE)
to reduce or limit something by cutting it short; to end something before its full length or extent
reduce or cut short

Examples

Due to budget cuts, the school had to curtail extracurricular activities.
The new policy seeks to curtail excessive spending without affecting essential services.
His trip was curtailed by an unexpected family emergency.

Synset Examples

My speech was cut short
Personal freedom is curtailed in many countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - make smaller in size, amount, or number
  • Synonym: to shorten - make or become shorter
  • Antonym: to extend - to increase in length or duration

Etymology

From Old French 'court', 'curtailer' (to shorten), from Latin 'curtus' (short).

2. VERB

/ kɚˈteɪl / (AmE), / kəˈteɪl / (BrE)
To reduce or limit something, especially by cutting a part of it or restricting its extent.
reduce or restrict; cut short

Examples

The company had to curtail its spending due to budget cuts.
New laws were enacted to curtail smoking in public places.
They decided to curtail their vacation because of the weather.

Synset Examples

curtail drinking in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in extent, number, volume, or scope

Etymology

From Old French 'courtault' (made short), from 'courter' (to shorten), from Latin 'curtus' (short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00237442-v
Synset POS: v
