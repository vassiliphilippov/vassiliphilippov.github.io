ATF

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪ ˌti ː ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌtiː ˈɛf / (BrE)
The Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives, a federal agency responsible for enforcing laws related to alcohol, tobacco, firearms, explosives, and arson in the United States.
U.S. agency enforcing alcohol, tobacco, firearms, and explosives laws
american

Examples

The ATF is investigating a series of illegal firearm sales across state lines.
After the explosion, agents from the ATF arrived to examine the scene for evidence.
He applied for a job with the ATF after graduating from law school.

Etymology

Acronym for Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives.
