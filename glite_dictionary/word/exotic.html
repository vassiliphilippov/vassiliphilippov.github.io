exotic

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɑt̬ɪk / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒtɪk / (BrE)
Originating in or characteristic of a foreign country; strikingly unusual or different in appearance, nature, or effect, especially because of being from another part of the world.
from a foreign country; strikingly unusual

Examples

The garden was filled with exotic flowers from South America.
She prepared an exotic dish using rare spices.
His apartment is decorated with many exotic artifacts collected during his travels.

Synset Examples

alien customs
exotic plants in a greenhouse
exotic cuisine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreign - from or characteristic of a country or language other than one's own
  • Antonym: native - originating or produced in a particular place or environment; not foreign or imported

Etymology

From Greek exōtikos (foreign), from exō (outside).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɑː.tɪk / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
strikingly unusual or different, often in a way that is interesting or attractive
strikingly unusual or different

Examples

The garden was filled with exotic plants brought from distant countries.
Her collection includes several exotic pieces of jewelry from Asia and Africa.
He loves traveling to exotic locations that are off the beaten path.

Synset Examples

an exotic hair style
protons, neutrons, electrons and all their exotic variants
the exotic landscape of a dead planet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unusual - not common or ordinary; remarkable
  • Antonym: familiar - well known from long or close association

Etymology

From Greek exōtikos (foreign, from elsewhere), from exō (outside).
