Iridoprocne bicolor

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌraɪdoʊˈprɒkni baɪˈkɔlər / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪdəˈprɒkni baɪˈkɒlə / (BrE)
A bluish-green and white North American swallow, known for nesting in tree cavities; also called the tree swallow.
North American tree swallow species

Examples

The Iridoprocne bicolor is easily recognized by its iridescent blue-green feathers and white underparts.
Birdwatchers often spot Iridoprocne bicolor near lakes and wetlands in the spring and summer.
Unlike many swallows, the Iridoprocne bicolor prefers to nest in natural or artificial tree cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree swallow - Common name for Iridoprocne bicolor, a North American swallow

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Iridoprocne' from Greek 'iris' (rainbow, iridescent) and 'Procne' (a mythological swallow); 'bicolor' means 'two-colored' in Latin.
