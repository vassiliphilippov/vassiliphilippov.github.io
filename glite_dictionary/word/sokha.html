sokha

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈsɒkə / (BrE)
An old Russian wooden plow, typically consisting of two beams and two shares, designed to be drawn by a single horse.
traditional Russian wooden plow

Examples

The farmer used a sokha to till the soil in the spring.
As modernization spread, the sokha gradually disappeared from rural Russian landscapes.
Unlike modern plows, the sokha required considerable skill to operate effectively.

Synset Examples

The sokha adapts the body ard to a single-animal harness following the pattern of a shaft-drawn cart and adds a spade-like component that turned over the soil.

Etymology

From Russian соха (sokhá), meaning 'plow.'
