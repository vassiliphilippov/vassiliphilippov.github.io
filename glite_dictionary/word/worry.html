worry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜri / (AmE), / ˈwʌri / (BrE)
A feeling of anxiety or unease about actual or potential problems.
a feeling of anxiety or unease

Examples

She felt a constant worry about her son’s health.
Financial worry can keep people awake at night.
His main worry was passing the exam.

Synset Examples

his worry over the prospect of being fired
it is not work but worry that kills
he wanted to die and end his troubles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from anxiety or worry

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrgan' (to strangle, choke), later evolving to mean 'harass' and then 'cause anxiety.'
