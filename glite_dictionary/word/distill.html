to distill

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to extract the essential elements or substances from a liquid by the process of distillation
extract by distillation

Examples

The laboratory will distill pure water from seawater for the experiment.
They distill essential oils from lavender flowers using steam.
The process uses heat to distill alcohol from the fermented mixture.

Synset Examples

distill the essence of this compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - to remove impurities or unwanted elements from a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'destillare' (to drip down), via Middle English and Old French.

4. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to purify a liquid by heating it until it produces vapor and then cooling the vapor so that it becomes a liquid, separating out impurities and increasing concentration
purify or concentrate a liquid by distillation

Examples

The laboratory technician used special equipment to distill the seawater and obtain fresh water.
Whiskey is produced by distilling fermented grain mash in copper stills.
They distilled the crude oil to separate its useful components.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - to make something clean by removing impurities
  • Antonym: to contaminate - to make something impure by adding substances that are harmful or unwanted

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance) to change from a vapor to a liquid by condensation, often in drops, as part of a purification or separation process.
change from vapor to liquid by condensation

Examples

After boiling, the vapor will distill and collect as liquid in the flask.
Rain forms when water vapor in the clouds distills into droplets.
