characteristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɛrəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
Typical of a particular person, place, or thing; showing the features that make someone or something distinct.
typical or distinctive feature

Examples

The artist's use of bright colors is characteristic of her early work.
That dry wit is very characteristic of him.
The rolling hills are characteristic of this region of the country.

Synset Examples

heard my friend's characteristic laugh
red and gold are the characteristic colors of autumn
stripes characteristic of the zebra

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinctive - having a quality that makes something different and easily noticed
  • Synonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular type of person or thing
  • Antonym: atypical - not representative of a type, group, or class; not typical

Etymology

From French caractéristique, from Greek kharaktēristikos (making a mark).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkærɪktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
A feature or quality that distinguishes a person, place, or thing from others.
distinguishing feature or quality

Examples

Honesty is an important characteristic of a good leader.
Each species has its own unique characteristics.
A sense of humor is a positive characteristic in many relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality or characteristic, typically of one’s personal nature
  • Synonym: attribute - a quality or feature regarded as a characteristic
  • Synonym: feature - a distinctive attribute or aspect

Etymology

From Greek kharaktēristikos (characteristic), from kharaktēr (mark, symbol).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04738737-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
the integer part (positive or negative) of a logarithm, distinct from the decimal (mantissa) part; e.g., in log 643 = 2.808, the characteristic is 2.
integer part of a logarithm

Examples

When working with tables of logarithms, always separate the characteristic from the mantissa.
The characteristic of log 500 is 2 because the number is between 100 and 1000.
A negative number may have a positive characteristic if it is the integer part of a small logarithm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mantissa - the decimal (fractional) part of a logarithm

Etymology

From Greek kharaktēristikos (making a mark, characteristic), via Latin characteristicus.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəktəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkarɪktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a measurable property or feature of a device, substance, or system, especially when assessed under specific conditions
measurable property or feature

Examples

The electrical characteristic of this sensor must be checked before installation.
Each material has its own unique characteristic under laboratory conditions.
We measured the thermal characteristic of the component to ensure it met the specifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: property - a quality or attribute that something possesses
  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality or feature
  • Degree Higher: peculiarity - a feature or detail that makes something unique or unusual

Etymology

From Greek kharaktēristikos (indicating a character), from kharaktēr.
