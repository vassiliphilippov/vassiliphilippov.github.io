Chamaeleo chamaeleon

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːliˈoʊ kəˈmiːliˌɒn / (AmE), / kəˌmiːliˈəʊ kəˈmiːliˌɒn / (BrE)
a species of chameleon native to parts of Africa, southern Europe, and the Middle East, noted for its ability to change color.
species of chameleon

Examples

The Chamaeleo chamaeleon is well-known for its remarkable ability to change the color of its skin.
Researchers observed a Chamaeleo chamaeleon blending into the leafy branches of a Mediterranean tree.
Unlike some tropical species, the Chamaeleo chamaeleon is often found in drier, open habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - any lizard of the family Chamaeleonidae, known for changing skin color

Etymology

From Latin scientific name, based on Greek 'khamailéōn' (chameleon).
