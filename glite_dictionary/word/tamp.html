tamp

1. NOUN

/ tæmp / (AmE), / tæmp / (BrE)
a tool used for pressing or packing down a substance, such as tobacco in a pipe or material in a hole
tool for packing down material

Examples

He used a tamp to gently pack tobacco into his pipe bowl.
The worker grabbed the tamp to press the powder into the drill hole.
After loading the mixture, she reached for her tamp to finish the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamper - a tool used for pressing or packing down material, often larger or used for soil
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ tæmp / (AmE), / tæmp / (BrE)
to press or pack something down tightly
press or pack down tightly

Examples

After pouring the soil, you should tamp it down to support the plant roots.
To extract the perfect espresso, always tamp the coffee grounds evenly.
She used a metal rod to tamp the explosives in the hole before detonating.

Synset Examples

tamp the coffee grinds in the container to make espresso

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to fill or press tightly into a space
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or firmly pressed

Etymology

Probably from French 'tamponner', from 'tampon' (plug, stopper).
