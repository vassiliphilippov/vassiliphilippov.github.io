to disintegrate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɪn.təˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪn.tɪ.ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(of a material or object) to cause to break up into smaller parts, especially by undergoing fission or losing particles.
break into particles; undergo fission

Examples

The radioactive material will eventually disintegrate and release energy.
High-energy particles can disintegrate an atomic nucleus, splitting it into smaller parts.
If not stored properly, the isotope may disintegrate and emit harmful radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution
  • Antonym: combine - to join or mix together

Etymology

From Latin disintegratus, from dis- (apart) + integratus (make whole).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈtəɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to break into smaller parts or lose unity or cohesion, typically as a result of decay or external force
break apart; lose unity

Examples

The old manuscript began to disintegrate in my hands as I turned the pages.
As negotiations failed, the coalition government started to disintegrate.
The tablet will disintegrate quickly when it comes into contact with water.

Synset Examples

The material disintegrated
the group disintegrated after the leader died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - break into pieces; lose effectiveness
  • Synonym: fragment - to break or cause to break into pieces
  • Antonym: to integrate - combine to become a whole

Etymology

From Latin disintegratus, past participle of disintegrare (to break up into pieces), from dis- (apart) + integer (whole).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsɪn.tɪˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪn.tɪ.ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(of a stored charge, magnetic flux, or current) to be lost, typically as energy dissipates or as part of a physical process.
lose a stored charge or current

Examples

In certain types of capacitors, the charge can disintegrate quickly if the device is faulty.
When a strong magnetic field is removed, the magnetic flux may disintegrate within milliseconds.
High-energy particles may disintegrate during nuclear fission, releasing their stored energy.

Synset Examples

the particles disintegrated during the nuclear fission process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - to lose strength or energy gradually, especially of nuclear particles or electric charge

Etymology

From Latin disintegrare, from dis- (apart) + integer (whole).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00399980-v
Synset POS: v
