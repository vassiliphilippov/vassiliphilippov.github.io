grout

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊt / (AmE), / ɡraʊt / (BrE)
a thin mixture of cement, sand, and water used to fill cracks or gaps in masonry, tiles, or brickwork
thin mortar for filling cracks in masonry

Examples

After installing the tiles, she applied grout to seal the gaps between them.
The mason used a special grout to repair the cracks in the old brick wall.
Cleaning the grout regularly helps prevent mold from developing in the bathroom.

Etymology

From Middle English 'grout' (coarse meal, malt), related to Old English 'grut' (coarse meal, groats).

2. VERB

/ ɡraʊt / (AmE), / ɡraʊt / (BrE)
to fill the spaces between tiles or seal joins with grout, a fluid cement or mortar
to fill with grout

Examples

After setting the tiles, you should grout the gaps to finish the floor.
The contractor will grout around the bathtub this afternoon.
Remember to thoroughly grout all corners to prevent water leaks.

Synset Examples

grout the bathtub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close or make secure against leakage

Etymology

From Old English *grūt* (coarse meal), later referring to coarse mortar.
