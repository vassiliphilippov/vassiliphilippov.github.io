avertable

1. ADJ

/ əˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / əˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be prevented or avoided; possible to ward off.
able to be avoided or prevented

Examples

Many accidents are avertable with proper precautions.
The disease is largely avertable through vaccination programs.
Much of the damage from the storm was avertable if warnings had been heeded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidable - able to be avoided; preventable
  • Antonym: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable

Etymology

From Latin 'avertere' (to turn away) + English suffix '-able'.
