Bombycilla

1. NOUN

/ bɑːmˈbɪsɪlə / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɪsɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds including the waxwings, known for their soft, silky plumage and distinctive red tips on wing feathers.
genus of birds: waxwings

Examples

The genus Bombycilla includes several species of waxwings found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Ornithologists studied the mating habits of Bombycilla in North America.
Unlike other songbirds, Bombycilla species display distinct red tips on their wing feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxwing - a songbird with soft plumage and red tips on the wings, member of Bombycilla

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, derived from Latin *bombyx* (silk, silkworm) and -cilla (tail), referring to the silky plumage.

2. NOUN

/ bɑːm.bɪˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / bɒm.bɪˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, sleek passerine birds known as waxwings, characterized by silky plumage and wax-like tips on the wing feathers.
genus of birds: waxwings

Examples

The genus Bombycilla includes all species of waxwings found in the Northern Hemisphere.
Birdwatchers are often excited to spot a member of Bombycilla during migration season.
The silky feathers and striking appearance distinguish birds of Bombycilla from others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxwing - A songbird with waxy tips to the wing feathers in the genus Bombycilla

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'bombux' (silk) and Latin 'cilla' (tail).
