Saint Andrew

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈændru / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈændruː / (BrE)
a disciple of Jesus in the New Testament, the brother of Peter, and the patron saint of Scotland.
disciple of Jesus; patron saint of Scotland

Examples

Every year, Scotland celebrates Saint Andrew's Day with traditional food and music.
Legend says that Saint Andrew was crucified on an X-shaped cross.
The town's cathedral is named after Saint Andrew, highlighting his importance to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Peter - disciple of Jesus; brother of Saint Andrew
