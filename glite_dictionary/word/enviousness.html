enviousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vi.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi.əs.nəs / (BrE)
A feeling of resentment and desire for something that someone else possesses.
feeling of envy

Examples

She could not hide her enviousness when her colleague received a promotion.
His enviousness was obvious as he stared at his friend's new car.
Their constant enviousness of each other's achievements damaged their friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envy - the feeling of wanting what someone else has

Etymology

From 'envious' + '-ness', with 'envious' from Old French 'envieus', from Latin 'invidiosus'.
