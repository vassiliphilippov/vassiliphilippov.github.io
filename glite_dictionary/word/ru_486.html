RU 486

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr ˌjuː fɔːr ˈeɪti sɪks / (AmE), / ˌɑː ˌjuː fɔː ˈeɪti sɪks / (BrE)
A drug used to induce abortion by blocking the action of progesterone, primarily in the early stages of pregnancy.
abortion-inducing drug
medical

Examples

The availability of RU 486 has changed the landscape of medical abortion in many countries.
She chose to use RU 486 after discussing her options with her doctor.
Many debates surround the regulation of RU 486 in different parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mifepristone - the generic name for RU 486, an abortion-inducing drug

Etymology

Trade name; 'RU' refers to Roussel Uclaf, the French pharmaceutical company that developed the drug, and '486' is its lab code.
