Chaldee

1. ADJ

/ kælˈdiː / (AmE), / kælˈdiː / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Chaldea, its people, their language, or their culture.
of ancient Chaldea, its people, language, or culture

Examples

The Chaldee language was once widely used in Babylonia.
He studied ancient Chaldee inscriptions found on clay tablets.
Artifacts with Chaldee script were displayed in the museum.

Etymology

From Latin 'Chaldaeus', from Greek 'Khaldaíos', from Akkadian 'Kaldu' (Chaldea).

2. NOUN

/ kælˈdi / (AmE), / kalˈdiː / (BrE)
an inhabitant of ancient Chaldea, a historical region in southern Babylonia.
inhabitant of ancient Chaldea

Examples

The ancient Chaldee was renowned for his contributions to astrology and astronomy.
Archaeologists have uncovered new artifacts belonging to a Chaldee settlement near the Euphrates River.
Many historical texts were written in the language of the Chaldee people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chaldean - a person from or relating to ancient Chaldea

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Chaldaios, from Akkadian Kaldu.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09661306-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kælˈdi / (AmE), / kælˈdiː / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people of Chaldea, especially one reputed to be skilled in astrology or occult learning
an ancient Babylonian wise man or astrologer
archaic

Examples

The ancient texts describe a Chaldee who could interpret dreams for the king.
In the court, the Chaldees were consulted for their wisdom in mystical matters.
He claimed descent from a Chaldee renowned for predicting eclipses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrologer - person who practices astrology

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Khaldaîos, referring to an inhabitant of Chaldea (Babylon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09925418-n
Synset POS: n
