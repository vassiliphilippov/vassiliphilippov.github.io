Coluber

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːbər / (AmE), / kəˈljuːbə / (BrE)
A genus of non-venomous snakes known commonly as racers, found in various regions around the world.
genus of non-venomous racer snakes

Examples

The Coluber genus includes several species commonly referred to as racers.
A Coluber can be identified by its slender body and swift movements.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Coluber genus in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racer - a fast-moving non-venomous snake

Etymology

From Latin 'coluber' meaning 'snake'.
