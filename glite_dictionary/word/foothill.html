foothill

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.hɪl / (BrE)
a low hill at the base of a mountain or mountain range, forming the lower slopes
low hill at a mountain’s base

Examples

The small village was nestled at the base of a foothill, surrounded by forest.
Wildflowers blanketed the foothill during the early spring months.
A narrow road winds along the foothill, offering breathtaking views of the valley below.

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'hill', indicating a hill at the foot (base) of a mountain.
