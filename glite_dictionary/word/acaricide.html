acaricide

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɛrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / əˈkɑːrɪsaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to kill mites or ticks.
chemical for killing mites or ticks

Examples

The farmer sprayed an acaricide to control the mite infestation in the orchard.
Many household products contain acaricides to prevent ticks and mites from spreading disease.
Proper application of an acaricide can help protect livestock from harmful parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insecticide - a chemical used to kill insects

Etymology

From Latin *acari* (mite) + -cide (killer).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkær.ə.saɪd / (AmE), / əˈkær.ɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to kill mites and ticks.
chemical agent for killing mites and ticks

Examples

The farmer used an acaricide to protect his crops from mite infestations.
A new acaricide has been developed that is less toxic to beneficial insects.
Proper application of an acaricide can significantly reduce tick populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insecticide - a substance used to kill insects
  • Synonym: miticide - a chemical agent specifically for killing mites
  • Synonym: fungicide - a substance that kills fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'acarus' (mite) and English '-cide' (killer).
