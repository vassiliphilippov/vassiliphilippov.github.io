to transitivize

1. VERB

/ ˈtrænsɪtəˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrænsɪtɪvaɪz / (BrE)
(in linguistics) To make a verb transitive, typically by changing its form or adding elements so it takes a direct object.
make (a verb) transitive

Examples

Linguists often transitivize verbs by adding appropriate suffixes or particles.
Some English verbs can be transitivized by attaching a preposition to them.
The teacher explained how the passive construction requires a verb to be transitivized.

Synset Examples

adding ‘out’ to many verbs transitivizes them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intransitivize - to make a verb intransitive

Etymology

From 'transitive' + '-ize', a verb-forming suffix.
