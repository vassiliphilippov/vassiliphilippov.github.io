Ate

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪti / (AmE), / ˈɑːteɪ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess who personifies ruin, folly, and delusion, especially those leading to rash acts and their punishment.
Greek goddess of ruin and folly

Examples

In the ancient tale, Ate drove heroes to make reckless decisions that brought about their downfall.
Literary texts often refer to Ate as the spirit of rashness and punishment.
The influence of Ate can be seen in many tragic stories of Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nemesis - Greek goddess of retribution
  • Synonym: Eris - Greek goddess of strife and discord

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἄτη (Atē), the name of the goddess representing ruin or mischief.
