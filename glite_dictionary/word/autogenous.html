autogenous

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːdʒənəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒdʒənəs / (BrE)
Originating or produced from within; occurring naturally within the body or organism rather than being caused by external factors.
originating from within (especially the body)
medical

Examples

The surgeon opted for an autogenous bone graft, using tissue from the patient's own body.
Certain bacteria can produce autogenous spores under stress conditions.
Healing was enhanced by the use of autogenous tissue, which lowered the risk of rejection.

Etymology

From Greek autogenēs (self-generated), from autos (self) + -genēs (born, produced).
