to cybernate

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪbərˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəˌneɪt / (BrE)
to control or operate a function, process, or system by means of a computer
control by computer

Examples

Many modern factories are partially cybernated to improve efficiency and reduce labor costs.
The airline plans to cybernate its booking system, making reservations fully automated.
As technology advances, more workplaces will cybernate their basic operations.

Synset Examples

They computerized the car industry
we live in a cybernated age
cybernate a factory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to computerize - to equip or control with computers

Etymology

Formed from 'cyber-' (from cybernetics) and '-ate,' following patterns of words like 'automate.'
