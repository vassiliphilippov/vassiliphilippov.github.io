Nilotic

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈlɑtɪk / (AmE), / naɪˈlɒtɪk / (BrE)
a group of languages spoken in East Africa, belonging to the Chari-Nile branch of the Nilo-Saharan language family
East African language group

Examples

Luo is one of the many Nilotic languages spoken in Kenya.
The linguist published a comparative study of Nilotic and Bantu languages.
Many Nilotic languages share similar grammatical structures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Nilo-Saharan - a large language family of Africa including Nilotic languages

Etymology

From Latin Niloticus (of the Nile), from Greek Neilos (Nile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07010768-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ naɪˈlɑːtɪk / (AmE), / naɪˈlɒtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Nilotic group of languages spoken in Central and Eastern Africa.
relating to Nilotic languages

Examples

The Nilotic languages are primarily spoken along the Nile and its surrounding regions.
Researchers study Nilotic syntax to understand its linguistic structures.
The dictionary included an appendix on Nilotic vocabulary.

Synset Examples

Nilotic syntax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nilo-Saharan - a proposed major language family including Nilotic languages

Etymology

From Latin Niloticus, meaning 'of the Nile', ultimately from Greek Neilos (Nile).

3. ADJ

/ naɪˈlɑːtɪk / (AmE), / naɪˈlɒtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Nile River or the peoples inhabiting the regions around it
of the Nile River or its peoples

Examples

The Nilotic tribes have a rich history and culture that stretches along the riverbanks.
Archaeologists uncovered ancient Nilotic artifacts near the delta of the Nile.
The museum's collection includes Nilotic art, reflecting the influence of the great river.

Etymology

From Latin Niloticus, from Nile (the river) + -tic (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
