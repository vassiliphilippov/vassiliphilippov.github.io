to automatise

1. VERB

/ əˈtɑːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something operate automatically, especially through the use of technology; to convert a process or system to automatic operation
make automatic; convert to automatic operation
british

Examples

Many factories automatise their production lines to increase efficiency.
They plan to automatise the entire payment process by next year.
The company is automatising manual tasks to reduce human error.

Synset Examples

automatize the production
automate the movement of the robot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - to make something operate automatically
  • Antonym: manual - done by hand rather than automatically

Etymology

From French 'automatiser', based on 'automate'. Related to earlier English 'automatize'.

2. VERB

/ əˈtɑːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something operate automatically or as an automaton; to convert into a process controlled by machines or automation
make automatic; convert to automation
british

Examples

The company plans to automatise all its production lines by 2025.
They hope new software will automatise much of the paperwork involved in the process.
Over time, repetitive tasks were automatised to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automatize - to make automatic; to automate
  • Synonym: to automate - to convert to automatic operation

Etymology

British variant of 'automatize', from 'automaton' + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481152-v
Synset POS: v
