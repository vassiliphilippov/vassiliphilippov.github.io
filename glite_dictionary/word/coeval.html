coeval

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈivəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈiːvəl / (BrE)
a person living at the same time as another or of approximately the same age; a contemporary
a person of the same age or era

Examples

Many scientists considered Darwin and his coevals to be the pioneers of evolutionary theory.
The artist's work was largely ignored by his coevals but celebrated after his death.
Her coevals struggled with similar challenges during that turbulent decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary - a person living at the same time as another

Etymology

From Latin coaevalis, from co- (together) + aevum (age).

3. NOUN

/ koʊˈivəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈiːvəl / (BrE)
a person who is of the same age or nearly the same age as another
person of same or similar age

Examples

She often compared herself to her coevals to assess her progress in life.
The museum displayed portraits of several coevals of the famous painter.
As coevals, they shared similar experiences growing up during the same era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary - person living at the same time; peer
  • Synonym: peer - person of equal standing or age

Etymology

From Latin coaevalis, from co- (together) + aevum (age, lifetime).
