Meconopsis cambrica

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkəˈnɑpsɪs ˈkæmbrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌmiːkəˈnɒpsɪs ˈkæmbrɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western Europe, widely cultivated for its showy pale yellow flowers; also known as the Welsh poppy.
a perennial plant with yellow flowers (Welsh poppy)

Examples

The Meconopsis cambrica adds a splash of yellow to shady garden borders.
Many British gardens feature the Meconopsis cambrica for its delicate appearance.
Botanists often refer to the Meconopsis cambrica as the Welsh poppy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh poppy - common name for Meconopsis cambrica

Etymology

From Greek 'mecon' (poppy) and Latin 'Cambrica' (of Wales), referring to the poppy from Wales.
