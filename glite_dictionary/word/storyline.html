storyline

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔriˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɔːriˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the series of events that form the main plot in a book, play, film, or other narrative
the main plot of a narrative

Examples

The film's storyline was both intriguing and unpredictable, keeping the audience guessing until the very end.
She quickly lost interest in the television show because the storyline became repetitive and dull.
Writers spent months developing the storyline before beginning to write the script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - the main events of a play, novel, film, or similar work

Etymology

From 'story' + 'line', first used in the mid-20th century to mean the plot of a narrative.
