petabit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtəˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 terabits, or 10^15 bits, used in computing and data communication
unit of 10^15 bits (digital information)

Examples

The new data center boasts a storage capacity measured in petabits.
With modern streaming technology, traffic can reach several petabits per day.
Researchers transferred over one petabit of data during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: terabit - a unit of information equal to 1,000 gigabits (10^12 bits)
  • Degree Higher: exabit - a unit of information equal to 1,000 petabits (10^18 bits)
  • Synonym: petabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 8 petabits or 10^15 bytes

Etymology

From 'peta-' (denoting a factor of 10^15, from Greek 'pente' meaning 'five') + 'bit'.
