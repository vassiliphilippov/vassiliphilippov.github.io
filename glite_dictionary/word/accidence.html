accidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈaksɪdəns / (BrE)
the part of grammar that deals with the inflection of words, such as changes in tense, number, or case
inflection in grammar

Examples

The textbook devotes an entire chapter to accidence, covering verb conjugations and noun declensions.
Students often struggle with the accidence of irregular verbs when learning new languages.
Traditional grammar courses distinguish between accidence and syntax as separate areas of study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syntax - the arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences

Etymology

From Latin 'accidentia' (happenings), referring to things that modify a word; later used for word forms in grammar.
