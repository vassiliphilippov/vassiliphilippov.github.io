Pachyrhizus tuberosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækɪˈraɪzəs tuːˈbɛrəsəs / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈrʌɪzəs tjuːˈbɪərəsəs / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to the Amazon basin, known for its large, edible tuberous roots.
tropical edible-rooted climber

Examples

The indigenous people cultivated Pachyrhizus tuberosus for its nutritious roots.
Local markets often sell the tubers of Pachyrhizus tuberosus as a staple food.
Researchers are studying Pachyrhizus tuberosus for its potential in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam bean - common name for several edible-rooted Pachyrhizus species

Etymology

From genus name Pachyrhizus (from Greek 'pakhus' meaning thick and 'rhiza' meaning root), species epithet from Latin 'tuberosus' (full of lumps, tuberous).
