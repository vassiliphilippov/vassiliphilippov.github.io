detritus

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtraɪdəs / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪtəs / (BrE)
Loose fragments or debris resulting from the destruction or disintegration of something; waste or remains.
fragments or remains of something destroyed

Examples

After the landslide, the valley was covered in detritus from the collapsed hillside.
The archaeologists sifted through the detritus of the ancient settlement to find clues about daily life.
Bits of plastic and other detritus littered the beach after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed

Etymology

From Latin detritus (a rubbing away), from deterere (to wear away).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈtraɪtəs / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪtəs / (BrE)
Loose fragments of rock, silt, and other material that have been worn away from larger masses by erosion or weathering.
rock fragments and silt from erosion

Examples

The river carried detritus downstream, depositing it along the banks.
A layer of detritus covered the forest floor after the heavy rain.
Geologists studied the detritus at the base of the cliffs to analyze the effects of erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of waste or remains

Etymology

From Latin detritus (a wearing away), from dēterere (to wear away).
