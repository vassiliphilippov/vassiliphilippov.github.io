homonym

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːməˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɒmənɪm / (BrE)
A word that is spelled and pronounced the same as another word but has a different meaning.
word spelled and pronounced the same as another, with a different meaning

Examples

The word "bat" is a homonym because it can mean both an animal and a piece of sports equipment.
English has many homonyms that can confuse language learners, like "bank" (river edge) and "bank" (financial institution).
When writing a dictionary, it's important to list each homonym separately to avoid misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homograph - word spelled the same as another but with different meaning and sometimes pronunciation
  • Synonym: homophone - word pronounced the same as another but spelled differently, with different meaning

Etymology

From Greek homōnymos, from homos (same) + onyma (name).
