Arabidopsis thaliana

1. NOUN

/ əˌræb.ɪˈdɑp.sɪs θəˈliː.ə.nə / (AmE), / əˌræb.ɪˈdɒp.sɪs θəˈlɪə.nə / (BrE)
a small flowering plant of the mustard family, widely used as a model organism in plant genetics and biology
model organism in plant genetics

Examples

Arabidopsis thaliana is commonly used in laboratories for plant genetic research.
Researchers selected Arabidopsis thaliana because its genome is relatively simple and well-mapped.
The flowering cycle of Arabidopsis thaliana makes it ideal for experiments on plant development.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Arabidopsis' from Arabic + Greek 'opsis' (appearance), 'thaliana' named after Italian botanist Johannes Thal.
