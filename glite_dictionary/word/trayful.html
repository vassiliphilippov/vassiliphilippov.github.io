trayful

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪ.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a tray; as much as a tray can hold.
as much as fills a tray

Examples

She carried a trayful of drinks out to the garden for her guests.
The baker placed a trayful of fresh bread rolls on the counter each morning.
He accidentally dropped a trayful of dirty dishes in the restaurant kitchen.

Etymology

Formed from 'tray' + '-ful', a suffix meaning 'as much as will fill'.
