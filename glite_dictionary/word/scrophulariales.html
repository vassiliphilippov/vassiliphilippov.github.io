Scrophulariales

1. NOUN

/ skroʊˌfjʊlˌɛriˈeɪliz / (AmE), / skrɒˌfjʊljəˈrɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants, formerly used in some botanical classification systems, often including the figwort family and others
order of flowering plants (taxonomy)

Examples

In older textbooks, the figwort family was classified within the Scrophulariales.
Some botanists now place families formerly in Scrophulariales under Lamiales.
The classification of Scrophulariales has changed significantly since the advent of molecular phylogenetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamiales - an order of flowering plants that includes mints and many former Scrophulariales members

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Scrophularia (a genus name) + -ales (order suffix in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ skrəˌfjuljəˈraɪəˌliz / (AmE), / skrəˌfjʊl.jʊˈreɪ.liːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants, formerly used in some botanical classification systems, often now included within the order Lamiales or Polemoniales.
order of flowering plants (botany, former classification)

Examples

Some texts still use Scrophulariales as a distinct order of flowering plants.
Recent research has reclassified the families previously grouped under Scrophulariales.
The order Scrophulariales included families such as Scrophulariaceae and Plantaginaceae.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on genus Scrophularia plus the suffix -ales (denoting botanical orders).
