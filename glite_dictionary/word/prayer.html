prayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr / (AmE), / ˈpreə / (BrE)
an earnest or urgent request for something, especially from a person in authority or in a difficult situation
earnest or urgent request

Examples

The workers sent a prayer to management for better safety measures.
Her prayer for understanding was met with silence.
They filed a formal prayer to the court asking for leniency.

Synset Examples

an entreaty to stop the fighting
an appeal for help
an appeal to the public to keep calm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entreaty - an earnest or humble request
  • Synonym: appeal - a serious or urgent request
  • Degree Higher: demand - an insistent and peremptory request, often not earnest

Etymology

From Old French *preiere*, from Medieval Latin *precaria*, from Latin *prex* (prayer, request).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr / (AmE), / ˈpreə / (BrE)
an act of addressing a deity, especially in worship, petition, confession, or thanksgiving
act of addressing a deity

Examples

She closed her eyes and offered a silent prayer for her family's safety.
Every morning, he begins his day with prayer and meditation.
The congregation bowed their heads in prayer during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

the priest sank to his knees in prayer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplication - the action of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly

Etymology

From Old French *preiere*, from Medieval Latin *precaria* (petition, prayer), from Latin *precari* (to entreat).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr / (AmE), / ˈpreə / (BrE)
a solemn request for help or expression of thanks addressed to God or another deity
reverent request to a deity

Examples

He began his day with a quiet prayer for guidance and strength.
Many people find comfort in offering a prayer during difficult times.
She wrote a prayer to express her gratitude after the good news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplication - the action of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly

Etymology

From Old French 'preiere', from Medieval Latin 'precaria', from Latin 'precari' (to entreat, to pray).

4. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr / (AmE), / ˈpreə / (BrE)
a set or established text recited as part of a religious service or when praying
set text recited in prayer

Examples

The congregation recited the prayer together every Sunday morning.
He memorized a special prayer for his grandmother’s health.
Each religion has its own unique prayer used in ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplication - an act of asking for something earnestly or humbly, especially in prayer
  • Synonym: blessing - a short prayer asking for divine favor

Etymology

From Old French *preiere*, from Latin *precaria* (petition), from *precari* (to ask, entreat).
