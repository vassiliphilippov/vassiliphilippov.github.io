stolid

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɒlɪd / (BrE)
showing little or no emotion or sensibility; not easily excited or moved
showing little emotion or excitement

Examples

Even during the chaos of the emergency, the doctor remained stolid and focused on his work.
Her father was a stolid man who rarely laughed or cried, no matter the occasion.
Despite the shocking news, his expression stayed stolid, betraying no hint of what he truly felt.

Synset Examples

her impassive remoteness
he remained impassive, showing neither interest in nor concern for our plight
a silent stolid creature who took it all as a matter of course
her face showed nothing but stolid indifference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassive - not showing or feeling emotion
  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin stolidus (dull, stupid).
