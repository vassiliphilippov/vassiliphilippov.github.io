deformity

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
A condition in which part of the body or its appearance is misshapen or abnormal, especially in a way considered undesirable or unattractive.
misshapen or abnormal bodily appearance

Examples

The accident left him with a noticeable deformity on his hand.
Many birth deformities can be corrected with surgery today.
She became self-conscious about her facial deformity as she grew older.

Synset Examples

there were distinguishing disfigurements on the suspect's back
suffering from facial disfiguration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disfigurement - the action or result of spoiling the appearance of something or someone

Etymology

From Middle English deformite, from Old French deformité, from Latin deformitas (ugliness), from deformis (misshapen).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːmɪti / (BrE)
A condition in which a part of the body is misshapen or malformed, often as a result of congenital factors, injury, or disease.
malformed or misshapen body part

Examples

The child was born with a deformity of the spine that required surgery.
Advances in medicine have made it possible to correct certain facial deformities.
The deformity of his hand did not prevent him from becoming a skilled pianist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malformation - an abnormal formation or structure, especially in the body
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal, especially in shape or structure

Etymology

From Old French 'deformité', from Latin 'deformitas' (ugliness, disfigurement), from 'deformis' (misshapen).
