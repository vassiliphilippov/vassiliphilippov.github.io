inadequacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being insufficient or lacking in what is required
the state of being inadequate

Examples

The report highlighted the inadequacy of current safety procedures at the plant.
Many students feel a sense of inadequacy when faced with challenging tasks.
The inadequacy of funds led to the project's cancellation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficiency - the state of not being enough or adequate
  • Antonym: adequacy - the state of being sufficient or satisfactory

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'adequatus' (made equal), via French 'inadéquat'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the state of not having enough ability, quality, or competence to meet a requirement or standard
lack of competence or ability

Examples

His feeling of inadequacy kept him from applying for the promotion.
The team's inadequacy became apparent during the final game.
There is a clear inadequacy in their training for such complex tasks.

Synset Examples

pointed out the insufficiencies in my report
juvenile offenses often reflect an inadequacy in the parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficiency - lack of something necessary or required
  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Latin inadequatus (not equal to the task) + -acy, forming nouns of state or quality.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being insufficient for a particular purpose; lack of enough of something needed
insufficiency; lack of enough

Examples

There is a clear inadequacy of funding for public schools in this area.
Many critics pointed out the inadequacy of the government's response to the disaster.
The report highlighted the inadequacy of resources available for the new healthcare program.

Synset Examples

the inadequacy of unemployment benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficiency - state of not having enough of something
  • Antonym: adequacy - the quality of being sufficient or enough

Etymology

From in- (not) + adequacy, from Latin adaequatus (made equal to).
