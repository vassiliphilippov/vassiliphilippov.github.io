pill

1. NOUN

/ pɪl / (AmE), / pɪl / (BrE)
a small, round, solid dose of medicine to be swallowed whole
small solid dose of medicine

Examples

He took a pill every morning to control his blood pressure.
The doctor prescribed a new pill for her allergies.
She swallowed the pill with a glass of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tablet - compressed medicinal solid, often larger and flat

Etymology

From Latin 'pila' (ball), via Old French and Middle English.

Examples
