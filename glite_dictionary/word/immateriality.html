immateriality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmæɾəriˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌmatɪəriˈalɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not having physical substance or not consisting of matter
lack of physical substance

Examples

The philosopher argued at length about the immateriality of the soul.
Quantum physics challenges our understanding of immateriality and matter.
Many religions emphasize the immateriality of spiritual beings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: materiality - the quality of being material or having physical substance

Etymology

From Late Latin 'immaterialitas', from 'im-' (not) + 'materialis' (material).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmæt̬.ɪ.ɚˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˌmæt.ɪəˈræl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being irrelevant or having no importance to the matter at hand; complete irrelevance requiring no further consideration
state of being irrelevant or unimportant

Examples

During the trial, the judge ruled that the witness's statement was of immateriality to the case.
His objections were dismissed due to their clear immateriality in the discussion.
The defense lawyer argued that the evidence lacked relevance and was therefore of total immateriality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being related to the subject

Etymology

From late Latin 'immaterialitas', from Latin 'im-' (not) + 'materialis' (material).
