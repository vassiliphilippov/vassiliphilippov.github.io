bovine

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊ.vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.vaɪn / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to cattle, especially those of the genus Bos.
relating to cattle

Examples

The farm specializes in raising bovine animals for dairy and beef production.
A bovine virus can quickly spread among herds if not controlled.
Researchers studied the bovine population to understand disease transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle - large domesticated bovine animals kept for milk or meat
  • Synonym: bos - the genus comprising cattle and related animals

Etymology

From Latin bovinus, from bos (ox, cow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈboʊvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊvaɪn / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) slow-moving, stolid, or dull, like a cow or ox.
dull or slow-moving (like a cow/ox)

Examples

Her bovine expression made it hard to tell how she was feeling.
The team criticized his bovine response to urgent problems.
Despite the chaos, he remained seated, moving with bovine calm.

Synset Examples

showed a bovine apathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stolid - (of a person) calm, dependable, showing little emotion or animation
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin bovinus (of cattle), from bos, bov- (ox, cow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00810847-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.vaɪn / (BrE)
Any animal of the genus Bos, especially domestic cattle and related animals such as oxen, bison, and buffaloes.
animal of the genus Bos, e.g., cattle

Examples

The field was filled with grazing bovine of various breeds.
Veterinarians studied the diseases common to bovine living in wet climates.
The farm specializes in the care of bovine as well as sheep and goats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle - domesticated bovine animals such as cows and bulls
  • Synonym: ox - domesticated bovine animal used as a draft animal
  • Synonym: bison - large wild bovine animal of North America and Europe

Etymology

From Latin *bovinus*, from *bos* (ox, cow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02404662-n
Synset POS: n
