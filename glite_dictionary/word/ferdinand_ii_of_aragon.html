Ferdinand II of Aragon

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrdɪˌnænd ðə ˌsɛkənd əv ˌærəˈgɑn / (AmE), / ˌfɜːdɪˌnænd ði ˈsɛkənd əv ˌærəˈɡɒn / (BrE)
King of Castile and Aragon (1452–1516), who, with his wife Isabella I, unified Spain, completed the Reconquista, instituted the Spanish Inquisition, and supported Columbus's 1492 voyage.
King of Aragon and Castile, united Spain (1452–1516)

Examples

Ferdinand II of Aragon and Isabella I are credited with unifying Spain in the late 15th century.
The voyage of Christopher Columbus in 1492 was supported by Ferdinand II of Aragon.
Under the reign of Ferdinand II of Aragon, the Spanish Inquisition was established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isabella I of Castile - Queen of Castile, wife of Ferdinand II, unified Spain
