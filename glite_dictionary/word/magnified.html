magnified

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæɡnəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
made to appear larger than normal, especially to an abnormal degree
enlarged to an abnormal degree

Examples

The tiny insect appeared magnified when viewed through the microscope.
The effects of the problem were magnified by the media's coverage.
Through the thick glass, her eyes looked strangely magnified.

Synset Examples

thick lenses exaggerated the size of her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlarged - made bigger or increased in size
  • Antonym: diminished - made smaller or reduced

Etymology

From Latin "magnificare" (to make great), via Middle English "magnifien".
