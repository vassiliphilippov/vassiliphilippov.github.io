Galeocerdo cuvieri

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡeɪliəˈsɜrdoʊ kuˈvjeɪri / (AmE), / ˌɡeɪliəˈsɜːdəʊ kjuːˈvjɛːri / (BrE)
A large, dangerous warm-water shark species, commonly known as the tiger shark, distinguished by its striped or spotted body.
species of shark; tiger shark

Examples

The Galeocerdo cuvieri is known for its wide, blunt snout and distinct body markings.
Marine biologists track the movements of Galeocerdo cuvieri to better understand shark migration patterns.
Several documentaries have featured the Galeocerdo cuvieri because of its reputation as a formidable predator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger shark - a large shark species with dark stripes on its body

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'galeos' (shark) and 'cerdo' (fox), with species name after French naturalist Georges Cuvier.
