comparison

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛrɪsən / (AmE), / kəmˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
the act of considering two or more things to discover similarities or differences between them
the act of comparing things

Examples

A detailed comparison of the two reports showed subtle differences in findings.
She drew a comparison between her old job and her new one.
It was clear from the comparison that both artists were influenced by the same traditions.

Synset Examples

they made a comparison of noise levels
the fractions selected for comparison must require pupils to consider both numerator and denominator

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contrast - the act of comparing to highlight differences

Etymology

From Latin 'comparatio', from 'comparare' (to pair, match, compare).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛrəsən / (AmE), / kəmˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
the act or process of considering how things are alike or different; the relation based on similarities and differences between two or more things
relation between things based on similarities and differences

Examples

It is interesting to make a comparison between the two novels.
A fair comparison of the candidates requires considering more than just their experience.
In comparison to last year, the new model is much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Latin comparatio (comparison), from comparare (to compare).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13876005-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛɹɪsən / (AmE), / kəmˈpæɹɪsən / (BrE)
the fact of being similar enough to be compared; the qualities or state of being comparable.
the state of being able to be compared

Examples

There is simply no comparison between the taste of homemade bread and store-bought loaves.
In terms of durability, this phone is beyond comparison with anything else on the market.
Any comparison between his early and later works reveals significant growth in his style.

Synset Examples

no comparison between the two books
beyond compare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - the state of being similar
  • Antonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different

Etymology

From Latin comparatio, from comparare (to compare).
