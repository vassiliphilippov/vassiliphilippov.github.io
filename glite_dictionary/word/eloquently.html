eloquently

1. ADV

/ ˈɛloʊkwəntli / (AmE), / ˈɛləkwəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is fluent, persuasive, and expressive in speaking or writing.
in a fluent or persuasive manner

Examples

She spoke eloquently about the importance of education at the conference.
The lawyer eloquently presented his case to the jury, winning their support.
During the debate, he eloquently defended his position with clarity and passion.

Synset Examples

he argued articulately for his plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulately - in a clear and effective manner

Etymology

From Latin 'eloquens' (speaking out), via Old French 'eloquent'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛloʊkwəntli / (AmE), / ˈɛləkwəntli / (BrE)
In a fluent, forceful, and persuasive manner; using language effectively and expressively.
in a fluent and persuasive way

Examples

She spoke eloquently about the importance of education at the conference.
The author eloquently captures the struggles of the characters in her novel.
He eloquently argued his case, impressing everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

he expressed his ideas eloquently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing

Etymology

From 'eloquent' + '-ly', with 'eloquent' from Latin 'eloquentia' (fluency of speech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00329241-r
Synset POS: r
