to concenter

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɛntər / (AmE), / kənˈsɛntə / (BrE)
to bring into or come together at a common center or focus; to concentrate or converge (ideas, efforts, or physical things)
bring into a common center or focus
archaic

Examples

The social movements began to concenter their efforts on education reform.
All attention seemed to concenter upon the speaker at the moment of his announcement.
She hoped to concenter her thoughts and bring clarity to the complex situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentrate - focus attention, efforts, or resources
  • Antonym: to disperse - spread out or scatter in different directions

Etymology

From Latin 'concentrum', from 'con-' (together) + 'centrum' (center).
