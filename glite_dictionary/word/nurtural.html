nurtural

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrtʃɚəl / (AmE), / ˈnɜːtʃərəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from nurture, especially as opposed to nature.
resulting from nurture (not nature)

Examples

The psychologist emphasized the nurtural influences on a child's development.
Many researchers now study both nurtural and natural factors affecting intelligence.
Her success was largely attributed to nurtural causes, such as her supportive family environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'natural'; from 'nurture' + '-al'.
