upbringing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌbrɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌbrɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the care and training a child receives while growing up, especially as it relates to moral and social development
the way a child is raised

Examples

Her strict upbringing influenced many of her decisions as an adult.
Despite his humble upbringing, he became a successful entrepreneur.
They credited their positive attitudes to a happy upbringing.

Synset Examples

they debated whether nature or nurture was more important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurture - the care and attention given to someone or something as they grow
  • Synonym: training - the process of learning the skills you need to do a particular job or activity
  • Synonym: background - a person's education, experience, and social circumstances

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'bringing', meaning to bring up (a child), first used in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌbrɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌbrɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the way a child is raised, including the care, education, and values they receive from their parents or guardians
the manner in which someone is raised

Examples

Her strict upbringing shaped her work ethic and values.
Despite his privileged upbringing, he remained humble and kind.
He attributed his success to the upbringing he received from his grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for someone properly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'up-' and 'bringing', originally meaning the rearing or raising of a child.
