sciarid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.ər.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ər.ɪd / (BrE)
A small blackish fly of the fungus gnat family, often destructive to mushrooms and plant seedlings.
a small blackish fungus gnat

Examples

Sciarid flies can devastate mushroom crops if not properly controlled.
Greenhouse growers often battle sciarid infestations among their young seedlings.
To minimize losses, preventative measures against sciarids are recommended in mushroom cultivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus gnat - small fly of the Sciaridae or related families, often found around fungi and damp soil

Etymology

From New Latin Sciaridae, the family name, derived from Greek 'skiaros' meaning 'shadowy, dusky'.
