Cadra cautella

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdrə kɔːˈtɛlə / (AmE), / ˈkædrə kɔːˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A small moth whose larvae infest and feed on stored products of vegetable origin, such as grains and nuts, causing damage with their webbing.
a moth that infests stored plant products

Examples

Warehouse managers are concerned about infestations of Cadra cautella in stored grain.
The larvae of Cadra cautella produce silk webbing that contaminates food products.
Proper storage techniques can help prevent Cadra cautella from damaging dried fruits and nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond moth - Common name for Cadra cautella, the moth that infests stored goods

Etymology

Scientific binomial from the genus Cadra and Latin species name cautella.
