rotten

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑtən / (AmE), / ˈrɒtən / (BrE)
having decayed or decomposed, typically with an offensive smell; affected by rot
decayed or decomposed; foul

Examples

The abandoned fruit was completely rotten and covered in mold.
A rotten log lay on the forest floor, crumbling at the touch.
The smell from the rotten meat made it clear something had gone bad in the fridge.

Synset Examples

dead and rotten in his grave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decayed - having undergone decomposition
  • Antonym: fresh - not decomposed or decayed

Etymology

From Old Norse *rotinn* (decayed), from *rot* (decay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01074208-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɑtən / (AmE), / ˈrɒtən / (BrE)
damaged or decayed so badly as to be no longer sound or usable
damaged or decayed; unsound

Examples

The old shed collapsed because its rotten floorboards could no longer support the weight.
You should replace the rotten beams in the attic before they cause structural problems.
The fence was unsafe due to several rotten posts at the base.

Synset Examples

rotten floor boards
rotted beams
a decayed foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decayed - having undergone decomposition; weakened by decay
  • Antonym: sound - in good condition; not decayed or damaged

Etymology

From Old English *rotian* (to rot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02283641-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɑtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈrɒtn̩ / (BrE)
Very bad; of extremely poor quality or unpleasant.
very bad; of poor quality
informal

Examples

The service at that restaurant was rotten, so we won't go back.
She said it was a rotten movie and not worth seeing.
I had a rotten day at work—nothing went right.

Synset Examples

a lousy play
it's a stinking world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - of very poor quality; extremely bad
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good or outstanding

Etymology

From Old English *rotian* (to rot), related to Old Norse *rotna*.
