titi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːti / (AmE), / ˈtiːti / (BrE)
A deciduous shrubby tree of eastern North America with deeply fissured bark, sprays of small fragrant white flowers, and sour-tasting leaves.
deciduous shrubby tree of eastern North America

Examples

The forest was dotted with blooming titi trees in early spring.
Local wildlife often feeds on the leaves of the titi.
We identified several titis by their fragrant white flowers along the trail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪti / (AmE), / ˈtiːti / (BrE)
A tree native to low-lying coastal areas of the southeastern United States, noted for its glossy leaves and racemes of fragrant white flowers.
coastal tree with glossy leaves and white flowers

Examples

The wetland was dominated by titi trees that bloomed every spring.
We took photos of the titi in full flower along the riverbank.
The titi provides important habitat for many native bird species.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtiːti / (AmE), / ˈtiːti / (BrE)
A small South American monkey with long, silky fur and a long, nonprehensile tail, belonging to the genus Callicebus.
small South American monkey

Examples

The titi monkey is known for its affectionate behavior towards its mate.
Researchers observed a group of titi monkeys moving gracefully through the forest canopy.
Unlike some other primates, the titi has a long but nonprehensile tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey - a primate of the group that includes the New World monkeys and Old World monkeys

Etymology

Probably from a local South American indigenous language, modified in Spanish and adopted into English.
