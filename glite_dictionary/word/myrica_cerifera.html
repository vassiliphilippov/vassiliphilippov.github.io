Myrica cerifera

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈraɪkə səˈrɪfərə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪkə sɛˈrɪfərə / (BrE)
an evergreen aromatic shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States, with small hard berries coated in white wax often used for making candles
aromatic evergreen shrub/tree of southeastern US

Examples

The berries of Myrica cerifera were once harvested to make bayberry candles.
Gardeners value Myrica cerifera for its hardiness and aromatic foliage.
Wildlife often feed on the waxy berries produced by Myrica cerifera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax myrtle - common name for Myrica cerifera, an evergreen aromatic shrub or small tree
  • Synonym: bayberry - common name for shrubs or small trees of the genus Myrica, including Myrica cerifera

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name Myrica and species epithet cerifera, meaning 'wax-bearing'.
