inquiry

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
An act of seeking information, knowledge, or truth about something; an investigation or examination.
act of seeking knowledge or investigation

Examples

The judge launched a formal inquiry into the company's financial activities.
After receiving the report, the committee began its inquiry into the incident.
We made an inquiry about the new policy to better understand its impact.

Synset Examples

their pottery deserves more research than it has received

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the act or process of examining something carefully, especially to discover the truth
  • Synonym: research - systematic investigation to establish facts

Etymology

From Late Latin 'inquirere', from Latin 'in-' (into) + 'quaerere' (seek).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwaɪəri, ˈɪnˌkwaɪri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
an official or systematic investigation, especially into a matter of public concern
official investigation or probe

Examples

The government has launched an inquiry into the causes of the disaster.
A public inquiry was held to determine if any laws were broken.
The findings of the official inquiry were released to the press yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the action of investigating something or someone; formal or systematic examination
  • Synonym: probe - an inquiry or investigation, especially a thorough one

Etymology

From Latin 'inquirere' (to seek after, examine), from 'in-' (into) + 'quaerere' (to seek).

3. NOUN

an act of asking for information; a question or investigation into something
an act of asking for information

Examples

Please direct any inquiries about the course to the admissions office.
After her inquiry, he explained all the project details.

Synset Examples

there was a question about my training
we made inquiries of all those who were present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence worded or expressed so as to elicit information
  • Synonym: investigation - a systematic examination or research

Etymology

From Latin 'inquirere' (to seek after, ask about), from 'in-' (into) + 'quaerere' (to seek).
