hemimetamorphosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˌmɛtəˈmɔrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
a type of insect development in which the young resemble adults but lack fully developed wings and reproductive organs, undergoing incomplete metamorphosis without a pupal stage
incomplete metamorphosis in insects
medical

Examples

Grasshoppers undergo hemimetamorphosis, with nymphs resembling miniature adults.
Dragonflies exhibit hemimetamorphosis as they develop from aquatic larvae to winged adults.
Unlike butterflies, which have a pupal stage, true bugs experience hemimetamorphosis during their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holometabolism - complete metamorphosis in insects

Etymology

From Greek hemi (half) + metamorphosis (change of form).
