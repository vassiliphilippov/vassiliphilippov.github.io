Magicicada septendecim

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædʒɪˈsaɪkədə ˌsɛptɛnˈdiːsɪm / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪˈsɪkədə ˌsɛptɛnˈdɛkɪm / (BrE)
a species of North American cicada that emerges in large numbers every 13 to 17 years after spending most of its life underground as a nymph
North American periodical cicada species

Examples

The Magicicada septendecim emerges from the ground in vast swarms once every seventeen years.
Entomologists study Magicicada septendecim to understand the evolutionary advantages of its long lifecycle.
Residents marvelled at the noise created by thousands of Magicicada septendecim during the recent emergence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodical cicada - a cicada species with periodic mass emergences every 13 or 17 years

Etymology

From Latin: 'magicus' (magical) and 'septendecim' (seventeen), referring to the 17-year emergence cycle.
