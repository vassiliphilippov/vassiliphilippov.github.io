to silver-plate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪl.vɚˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vəˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to coat (an object) with a thin layer of silver, typically by electroplating
cover with a thin layer of silver

Examples

Several companies will silver-plate your old jewelry to restore its shine.
The antique watch had been silver-plated to improve its appearance.
They offered to silver-plate the cutlery for a small additional fee.

Synset Examples

silverplate a watch

Etymology

Formed from 'silver' + 'plate', referencing the process of adding a silver surface.
