bacillus

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
a rod-shaped, spore-producing bacterium, often found in soil and sometimes forming chains
a rod-shaped soil bacterium
medical

Examples

Certain species of bacillus are used in the production of antibiotics.
Under the microscope, the bacteria were identified as a bacillus due to their shape.
The soil sample contained a high number of bacillus organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - a microscopic single-celled organism

Etymology

From Latin 'bacillus', diminutive of 'baculum' (rod or staff).
