toughened

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌfənd / (AmE), / ˈtʌfənd / (BrE)
made stronger and more resilient, especially by being treated with heat or chemicals.
strengthened by treatment

Examples

The windows were made of toughened glass to increase safety.
Many cookware sets are now produced from toughened steel for durability.
The lab tested the toughened material for its resistance to impact.

Synset Examples

a sword of tempered steel
tempered glass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempered - made stronger by controlled heating and cooling

Etymology

From the verb 'toughen' (to make tough) + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʌfənd / (AmE), / ˈtʌfənd / (BrE)
Made physically stronger or more durable, often by undergoing a special process.
made stronger or tougher by treatment

Examples

The chef uses toughened glass for all the kitchen countertops to prevent breakage.
Her toughened resolve helped her overcome many challenges throughout the year.
Safety goggles are made from toughened plastic to protect the eyes from injury.

Synset Examples

the tough bottoms of his feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardened - made hard or more resistant, often by treatment or experience
  • Antonym: softened - made softer or less rigid, often by treatment
