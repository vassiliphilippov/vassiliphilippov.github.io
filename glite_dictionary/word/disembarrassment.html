disembarrassment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbæɹ.smənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbɑː.smənt / (BrE)
something that relieves or removes embarrassment or difficulty; a means of extricating someone from an awkward situation.
relief from embarrassment or difficulty

Examples

The timely arrival of her friend was a welcome disembarrassment from an awkward conversation.
He searched for any disembarrassment that would help him escape the difficult situation gracefully.
Winning the award acted as a disembarrassment for his previous mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing removal) + 'embarrassment' (from French *embarrasser*, to hinder).
