musette

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈzɛt / (AmE), / mjuːˈzɛt / (BrE)
a small French bagpipe, especially one popular in the 17th and 18th centuries
a small French bagpipe

Examples

The shepherd played a gentle tune on his musette as the sun set over the hills.
Many Baroque composers wrote music intended specifically for the musette.
The museum displays several types of historic musettes from different regions of France.

Etymology

From French 'musette', diminutive of 'muse' (bagpipe).
