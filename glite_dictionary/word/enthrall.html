to enthrall

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθrɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrɔːl / (BrE)
to capture the complete attention or fascination of someone; to hold spellbound
hold spellbound; captivate

Examples

The magician's performance enthralled the entire audience for hours.
Children are often enthralled by stories of magic and adventure.
The movie was so gripping that it enthralled viewers from beginning to end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest of; similar meaning
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel weary and uninterested; opposite of enthrall

Etymology

From Middle English 'enthrallen', from en- (make) + thrall (slave), originally meaning to enslave.
