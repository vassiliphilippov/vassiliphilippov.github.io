atavism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬əˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈatəˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the reappearance in an individual of characteristics of some remote ancestor that have been absent in intervening generations
reappearance of ancestral trait

Examples

The scientist noted the atavism in the animal, which showed features typical of its ancient ancestors.
Some rare conditions in humans are explained by atavism, where dormant genes become active again.
The presence of a tail in a human infant is a well-documented case of atavism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversion - return to a former or ancestral type
  • Synonym: recurrence - the fact of something happening again

Etymology

From French *atavisme*, from Latin *atavus* (ancestor).
