Helenium hoopesii

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈliniəm huːpˈsiːaɪ / (AmE), / hɪˈliːniəm huːpˈsiːaɪ / (BrE)
A stout perennial herb native to the western United States, with drooping orange-yellow flower heads; known to cause spewing sickness in sheep.
perennial herb of western US with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

Helenium hoopesii is often found growing in moist mountain meadows across Colorado and Wyoming.
Farmers are cautious with grazing sheep near Helenium hoopesii due to its toxic effects.
The distinctive drooping yellow petals make Helenium hoopesii easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *helenion* (a plant name), species named after Thomas Hoopes, an American horticulturist.
