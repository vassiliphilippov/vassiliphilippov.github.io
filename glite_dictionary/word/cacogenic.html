cacogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkækəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of genes, traits, or influences) tending to cause degeneration or producing inferior offspring.
causing degeneration in offspring
medical

Examples

Certain environmental toxins may have a cacogenic effect on the next generation.
The researcher studied the cacogenic traits associated with various hereditary diseases.
Strict measures were adopted to prevent the cacogenic propagation of harmful genetic mutations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eugenic - tending to improve the genetic quality of offspring

Etymology

From Greek kakos (bad) + -genic (producing).
