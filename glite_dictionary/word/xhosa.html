Xhosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊsə, ˈzoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊsə, ˈzəʊsə / (BrE)
a member of a South African people living chiefly in the Eastern Cape Province
a people of South Africa

Examples

The Xhosa are known for their rich traditions and vibrant music.
Many Xhosa live in the Eastern Cape region of South Africa.
She interviewed a Xhosa elder about family customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zulu - a South African ethnic group related to the Xhosa
  • Synonym: Xhosa language - the language spoken by the Xhosa people

Etymology

From Xhosa *amaXhosa*, the self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊsə, ˈzoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊsə, ˈzɒsə / (BrE)
A Bantu language spoken primarily in South Africa, closely related to Zulu and the country's second most common home language.
a major South African Bantu language

Examples

Many South Africans are fluent in both Xhosa and English.
She learned basic Xhosa phrases before her trip to Cape Town.
The Xhosa language is known for its distinctive click sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zulu - a closely related Bantu language spoken in South Africa

Etymology

From Xhosa (isiXhosa), the name of the people and the language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07007360-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊsə, ˈkɔːsə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊsə, ˈkɔːsə / (BrE)
A member of a Bantu-speaking ethnic group of southern South Africa, known for their distinctive language and culture.
member of a South African ethnic group

Examples

Several Xhosa leaders played important roles in the anti-apartheid movement.
The Xhosa have a rich tradition of music and oral storytelling.
As a child, he learned to speak Xhosa at home before starting school in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zulu - member of a southern African ethnic group closely related to the Xhosa

Etymology

From Xhosa, the indigenous name of the people and their language.
