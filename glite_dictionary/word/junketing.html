junketing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋ.kə.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋ.kɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of going on an excursion or trip, especially for pleasure, often at another's expense or as part of official duties.
pleasure excursion or outing

Examples

The senator was criticized for his frequent junketing to luxury resorts at taxpayers' expense.
Their annual junketing included wine tastings and sightseeing tours throughout the region.
She wrote an article exposing government officials' lavish junketing disguised as business trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outing - a short trip, usually for pleasure
  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey made for pleasure

Etymology

From the noun 'junket' (social outing or trip), ultimately from Old French 'jonquette', a kind of cream cheese, later extended to mean a festive entertainment.
