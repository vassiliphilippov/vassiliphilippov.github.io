guerrilla

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
a member of a small independent group taking part in irregular fighting, typically against larger regular forces, usually using sabotage and harassment tactics
member of irregular armed force

Examples

The guerrilla fighters launched a surprise attack on the military convoy.
Villagers lived in fear of both government troops and guerrilla forces in the region.
After years of conflict, many former guerrillas laid down their weapons and joined the peace process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurgent - a person fighting against an established government or authority
  • Antonym: soldier - a person serving in an army, especially a non-commissioned member of the armed forces

Etymology

From Spanish 'guerrilla' (little war), diminutive of 'guerra' (war).

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɪl.ə / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɪl.ə / (BrE)
A member of an irregular armed group that uses unconventional tactics such as sabotage and harassment against a stronger military force.
irregular armed fighter

Examples

The guerrilla fighters launched a surprise attack on the convoy at dawn.
He joined a guerrilla group to oppose the government regime.
Villagers often supported the guerrilla with food and shelter during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurgent - a person who rises in active revolt
  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance against an established government or leader
  • Antonym: soldier - a member of an army, especially one who is not an officer

Etymology

From Spanish 'guerrilla' (little war), diminutive of 'guerra' (war).
