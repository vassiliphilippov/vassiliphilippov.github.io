Colocasia

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.loʊˈkeɪ.zi.ə / (AmE), / kə.ləˈkeɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial tuberous herbs native to tropical Asia, including the taro plant.
genus of tuberous herbs, includes taro

Examples

The leaves of Colocasia are often used in traditional dishes in parts of Asia.
Several species of Colocasia are cultivated for their edible corms.
Botanists have identified new varieties within the genus Colocasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant with starchy tuberous roots, often used as food

Etymology

From Greek kolokasia, a name for the Egyptian lotus or the taro plant.
