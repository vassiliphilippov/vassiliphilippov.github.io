naboom

1. NOUN

/ nɑːˈbuːm / (AmE), / nəˈbuːm / (BrE)
A small tree of dry open areas in southern Africa, with upright, angled branches resembling a candelabrum.
tree of southern Africa with candelabra-like branches

Examples

The naboom thrives in arid regions where few other trees can survive.
We stopped in the shade of a tall naboom during our hike across the savanna.
The distinctive shape of the naboom makes it easy to recognize from a distance.

Etymology

From Afrikaans ‘naboom’ (literally ‘now tree’), probably after Dutch ‘na’ (near) + ‘boom’ (tree).
