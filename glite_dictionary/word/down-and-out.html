down-and-out

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (BrE)
a person who has no money, no job, and no home; someone who is destitute
person who is destitute or homeless

Examples

After losing his job, he ended up living on the streets as a down-and-out.
The shelter offers warm meals and beds for every down-and-out who walks through its doors.
Despite being a down-and-out, he remained hopeful for a better future.

Synset Examples

he tried to help the down-and-out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place
  • Synonym: pauper - a very poor person

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'down and out', describing someone knocked down and out of luck, common in early 20th-century English.
