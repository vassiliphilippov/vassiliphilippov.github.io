sulfonylurea

1. NOUN

/ sʌlˌfoʊnɪlˈjʊriə / (AmE), / sʌlˌfɒnɪlˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
any of several drugs that lower blood sugar levels, commonly used to treat type 2 diabetes mellitus
antidiabetic drug class for type 2 diabetes
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a sulfonylurea to help control his blood sugar levels.
Patients who are unable to tolerate metformin may be given a sulfonylurea instead.
Some people experience low blood sugar as a side effect of sulfonylurea medications.

Etymology

From sulfonyl (a chemical group) and urea (an organic compound), referring to their chemical structure.
