unfocussed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
(of an image, view, or gaze) not brought into clear focus; blurry or indistinct.
not in clear focus; blurry

Examples

His eyes grew unfocussed as he stared into the distance.
The photograph was too unfocussed to see any details clearly.
After waking up suddenly, her vision remained unfocussed for a few moments.

Synset Examples

at their edges, things were pretty much unfocused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfocused - variant spelling meaning not brought into clear focus
  • Antonym: focussed - (of an image) brought into sharp focus

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'focused'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
not concentrated or directed at a single point, objective, or subject; lacking focus or clear direction.
not concentrated or lacking clear focus

Examples

The team's strategy was unfocussed, leading to confusion among the players.
His unfocussed energy made it difficult for him to finish any of his projects.
The photograph was unfocussed, so the details were blurry and indistinct.

Synset Examples

diversity … in our huge unfocused country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: focused - concentrated or directed at a single point
  • Synonym: scattered - spread out in different directions, lacking order or unity

Etymology

From un- (not) + focussed, from focus (Latin focus, meaning hearth or point of concentration).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00544995-s
Synset POS: s
