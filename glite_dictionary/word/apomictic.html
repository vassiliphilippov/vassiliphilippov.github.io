apomictic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈmɪktɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈmɪktɪk / (BrE)
(of reproduction) not involving the fusion of male and female gametes; reproducing by means other than sexual fusion, such as by parthenogenesis or apomixis.
reproducing without fusion of gametes
medical

Examples

Many plants in the genus Taraxacum have an apomictic mode of reproduction, producing seeds without fertilization.
Some ferns can reproduce in an apomictic fashion, bypassing the need for sexual reproduction.
Research into apomictic processes offers insights into alternatives to genetic recombination in nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual - involving the fusion of male and female gametes
  • Synonym: parthenogenetic - relating to reproduction from an ovum without fertilization

Etymology

From New Latin apomixis, from Greek apo- (away from) + mixis (mixing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈmɪktɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈmɪktɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) reproducing by or relating to apomixis; capable of asexual reproduction without fertilization.
relating to asexual reproduction in plants
medical

Examples

Certain grasses are known to be apomictic, producing seeds without pollination.
The apomictic reproduction of this species allows for rapid colonization of new areas.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity among apomictic and sexually reproducing plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual - involving sexual reproduction
  • Synonym: apomixis - asexual reproduction without fertilization in plants

Etymology

From 'apomixis', combining Greek 'apo-' (away from) and 'mixis' (mingling), with '-ic' forming the adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02644528-a
Synset POS: a
