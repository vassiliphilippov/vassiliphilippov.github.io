lacebark

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈleɪsˌbɑːk / (BrE)
a small tree or shrub native to New Zealand, noted for its clusters of honey-scented white flowers and bark traditionally used for making cordage
New Zealand tree with fragrant white flowers, bark used for cordage

Examples

The lacebark is prized in New Zealand for its attractive, fragrant blooms.
Early Māori used lacebark fiber to make strong cords and ropes.
A cluster of paper-white flowers adorned the garden’s only lacebark.

Etymology

From the appearance of the bark, which peels off in lace-like strips.
