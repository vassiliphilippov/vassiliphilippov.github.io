Henry Louis Gehrig

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈluːɪs ˈɡɛrɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈluːɪs ˈɡɛrɪɡ / (BrE)
(1903–1941) American baseball player famed for his record-breaking career and for being diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, now often called Lou Gehrig's disease.
American baseball player (1903–1941)

Examples

Henry Louis Gehrig is remembered not only for his baseball records but also for his courage in battling illness.
Many know Henry Louis Gehrig as the 'Iron Horse' of Major League Baseball.
The legacy of Henry Louis Gehrig lives on through research into ALS, the disease named after him.
