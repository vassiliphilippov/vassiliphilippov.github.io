sheng nu

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌŋ ˈnuː / (AmE), / ʃɛŋ ˈnuː / (BrE)
(in China) an educated woman who remains unmarried by her late twenties or older, especially as regarded by society.
unmarried woman in China (usually educated, late twenties+)

Examples

As a sheng nu, she faces constant questions from relatives about her marital status.
The documentary explores the experiences of sheng nu in modern Chinese cities.
Despite being labeled a sheng nu, she is successful and content with her life.

Synset Examples

Society often pressures sheng nu to marry young.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 剩女 (shèngnǚ), meaning 'leftover woman'.
