gobby

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑbi / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbi / (BrE)
(informal, British) excessively talkative or outspoken, especially in a loud or brash manner
talkative or outspoken (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

Her gobby attitude often lands her in trouble with teachers.
Everyone knew him as the gobby one in the group, always voicing his opinions loudly.
She admitted she was a bit gobby at the party and apologized for interrupting.

Synset Examples

Being gobby helps her make new friends easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouthy - excessively talkative or expressing opinions loudly
  • Antonym: reserved - slow or reluctant to speak; not outspoken

Etymology

British slang; from 'gob' (mouth, informal British/Irish) + -y.
