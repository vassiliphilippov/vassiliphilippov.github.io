kaffir

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæfər / (AmE), / ˈkæfə / (BrE)
An extremely offensive and insulting term for a Black African, especially used in South Africa; now regarded as a racial slur and not used in polite speech or writing.
offensive slur for Black African (esp. South Africa)
offensive

Examples

He was shocked to hear someone use the word kaffir in a conversation.
The term kaffir is deeply hurtful and considered unacceptable in modern society.
Many historical documents unfortunately contain the word kaffir as a racial slur.

Etymology

From Arabic *kāfir* (unbeliever), adopted into Afrikaans and later English in colonial South Africa, later becoming a racial slur.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæfɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæf.ə / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum grown for its grain, used as food for humans and livestock; noted for its resemblance to Indian corn but with sawtooth-edged leaves.
sorghum variety used as food crop

Examples

This field is planted with kaffir, which provides a valuable food source for the cattle.
Traditionally, kaffir grain is ground into flour and used for making porridge.
Farmers in dry regions often choose kaffir because it is drought-resistant and yields a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a widely cultivated cereal grass

Etymology

Probably from Arabic 'kāfir' (nonbeliever), referring to its geographic origin, but note: the term is considered offensive in other contexts.
