sidereal

1. ADJ

/ saɪˈdɪriəl / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəriəl / (BrE)
Relating to the stars or constellations, especially as distinct from the sun or planets; used in astronomy.
relating to stars or constellations

Examples

Astronomers calculate sidereal time based on the Earth's rotation relative to distant stars.
The sidereal year is slightly longer than the solar year.
She studied the sidereal system to understand the movement of constellations.

Synset Examples

sidereal bodies
the sidereal system

Etymology

From Latin 'sidereus' (starry, of the stars), from 'sidus' (star, constellation).

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈdɪriəl / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəriəl / (BrE)
(of time divisions) based on or determined by the apparent daily motion of the stars.
related to time measured by the stars

Examples

Astronomers use sidereal time to track the positions of stars in the night sky.
The sidereal day is slightly shorter than the solar day.
Calculating a sidereal year involves observing the Earth's position relative to distant stars.

Synset Examples

sidereal time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solar - of or relating to the sun, especially in measuring time

Etymology

From Latin 'sidereus' (starry), from 'sidus' (star, constellation).
