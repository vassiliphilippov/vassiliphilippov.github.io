sigmoidoscope

1. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈmɔɪdoʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / sɪɡˈmɔɪdəskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument, consisting of a flexible fiberoptic tube, used for examining the sigmoid colon
instrument for examining sigmoid colon
medical

Examples

The doctor used a sigmoidoscope to check for polyps in the patient’s colon.
Routine screenings may involve the use of a sigmoidoscope to detect early signs of colon disease.
A flexible sigmoidoscope makes the procedure less uncomfortable for patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of a hollow organ

Etymology

From 'sigmoid', referring to the 'sigmoid colon', plus '-scope' from Greek 'skopein' (to look at).
