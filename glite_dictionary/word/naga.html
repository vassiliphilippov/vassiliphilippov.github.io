Naga

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈnɑːɡə / (BrE)
a group of Kamarupan languages spoken in northeastern India and western Myanmar (Burma)
languages spoken in NE India and W Myanmar

Examples

The Naga languages have distinct characteristics from other language families in the region.
Researchers have published new studies on the grammar of several Naga dialects.
Many Naga communities preserve their languages through traditional storytelling and songs.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'nāga' (snake, serpent) and used as an ethnonym for various tribes in Northeast India.
