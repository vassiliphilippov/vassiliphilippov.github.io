degraded

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡreɪdɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdɪd / (BrE)
Reduced in quality, value, or status; made inferior or less valuable.
reduced in value or quality

Examples

The company released a degraded version of its flagship product to cut costs.
Over time, the degraded coinage lost the trust of the public.
Because of repeated censorship, the newspaper's reputation became degraded.

Synset Examples

a debased currency
The worth of citizenship is devalued when information is censored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debased - lowered in value or quality
  • Synonym: devalued - reduced in worth or importance
  • Antonym: improved - made better or of higher value

Etymology

From Latin degradare, from de- (down) + gradus (step).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡreɪdɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdɪd / (BrE)
Having lost moral restraint; lacking the standards of proper behavior or morality.
lacking moral restraint

Examples

The once respectable politician became degraded through years of corruption and vice.
His degraded behavior shocked everyone at the dinner party.
She feared her brother would fall into a degraded lifestyle after losing his job.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauched - unrestrained by morals; indulging in sensual pleasures
  • Synonym: dissolute - lacking moral restraint; licentious
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'degradare' (to reduce in rank).
