nonclassical

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
not relating to or following the principles of classical forms, styles, or theories.
not classical in form or style

Examples

Quantum mechanics is considered a nonclassical theory of physics.
The musician experimented with nonclassical harmonies in his composition.
Researchers study nonclassical effects in certain types of light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: classical - relating to traditional forms or rules, especially in art, literature, or science

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'non-' to 'classical'.
