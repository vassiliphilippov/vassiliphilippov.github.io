Acacia pycnantha

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə pɪkˈnænθə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə pɪkˈnanθə / (BrE)
a shrubby Australian tree with clusters of fragrant golden yellow flowers, widely grown as an ornamental; commonly known as the golden wattle
Australian tree with golden yellow flowers

Examples

The Acacia pycnantha is celebrated as Australia's national floral emblem.
Many gardens feature Acacia pycnantha for its bright and fragrant yellow blossoms.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Acacia pycnantha in different soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden wattle - common name for Acacia pycnantha

Etymology

From Latinized Neo-Latin: 'Acacia', a genus name from Greek 'akis' (thorn, point); 'pycnantha' from Greek 'pyknos' (dense) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to its dense flower clusters.
