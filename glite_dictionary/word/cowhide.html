cowhide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (BrE)
leather made from the skin of a cow, often used for making shoes, clothing, or upholstery.
leather from the skin of a cow

Examples

The saddle was made of thick, durable cowhide.
He purchased a cowhide rug for his living room.
Most traditional boots are crafted from genuine cowhide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leather - a material made from tanned animal hide or skin

Etymology

From Middle English 'cow' + 'hide' (animal skin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (BrE)
A heavy, flexible whip braided from leather made from the hide of a cow.
whip made of cowhide leather

Examples

The cowboy used a cowhide to direct the cattle during the roundup.
He purchased an old cowhide at the antique market, fascinated by its craftsmanship.
Cracking the cowhide, the rancher signaled the start of the day's work.

Etymology

From 'cow' + 'hide,' referring to the skin of a cow used to make the whip.

4. VERB

/ ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhaɪd / (BrE)
to whip or flog someone with a cowhide whip, especially as punishment
to whip with a cowhide whip

Examples

The overseer threatened to cowhide any man who disobeyed the rules.
In the old novel, the villain tried to cowhide his adversary in public.
She was unjustly cowhided for speaking her mind in the harsh times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a lash or whip

Etymology

From 'cow' + 'hide', originally referring to the whip made of a cow’s hide.
