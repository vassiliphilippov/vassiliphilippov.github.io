Scleropages jardinii

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrˈɒpədʒiz ʤɑrˈdɪni / (AmE), / sklɪərəˈpeɪdʒiːz dʒɑːˈdiːniː / (BrE)
A species of large freshwater fish native to northern Australia and New Guinea, noted for crescent-shaped reddish spots on each scale.
Australian spotted arowana fish species

Examples

The Scleropages jardinii is highly prized by freshwater aquarium enthusiasts for its striking scales.
Researchers observed Scleropages jardinii populations in several remote Australian waterways.
Unlike the Asian arowana, Scleropages jardinii has distinct crescent-shaped spots on its scales.

Etymology

From genus Scleropages (Greek: 'hard plate'), and the species name jardinii, after Sir William Jardine, Scottish naturalist.
