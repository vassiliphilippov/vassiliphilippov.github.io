Equetus lanceolatus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwiːtəs ˌlænsiəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈkwiːtəs ˌlanceəˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
a black-and-white drumfish species with an erect, elongated dorsal fin, native to the western Atlantic Ocean
a species of black-and-white drumfish

Examples

The Equetus lanceolatus is easily identified by its striking black-and-white coloration and tall dorsal fin.
Marine biologists observed a group of Equetus lanceolatus swimming near the coral reef.
The aquarium features an impressive display of Equetus lanceolatus in their natural habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Equetus' is a genus of fish, 'lanceolatus' means 'spear-shaped'.
