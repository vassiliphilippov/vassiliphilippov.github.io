Boidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of nonvenomous snakes including boas and some pythons, found mainly in the Americas, Africa, and Asia.
family of snakes including boas and pythons

Examples

Scientists classify boas and anacondas in the snake family Boidae.
The Boidae are known for their method of subduing prey by constriction rather than venom.
Members of Boidae can be found in tropical regions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pythonidae - family of nonvenomous snakes including true pythons
  • Synonym: Boa - any large snake of the family Boidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'bōas' (boa) and '-idae', a zoological family suffix.
