thyme

1. NOUN

/ taɪm / (AmE), / taɪm / (BrE)
A small aromatic plant of the mint family, whose leaves are used as a culinary herb for flavoring a wide variety of foods.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh thyme over the roasted potatoes for added flavor.
A pinch of dried thyme gives this soup a subtle earthy taste.
The recipe calls for both thyme and rosemary to season the chicken.

Etymology

From Old French 'thim', from Latin 'thymum', from Greek 'thýmon'.

2. NOUN

/ taɪm / (AmE), / taɪm / (BrE)
a small aromatic plant of the mint family, with small leaves that are used as a culinary herb, especially in Mediterranean dishes; plants of the genus Thymus.
small aromatic culinary herb

Examples

She sprinkled fresh thyme over the roasted vegetables for extra flavor.
The scent of thyme filled the air as he brushed past the herb garden.
Dried thyme is often used in soups and stews during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - an aromatic herb related to thyme, used in cooking

Etymology

From Old French 'thym', from Latin 'thymum', from Greek 'thumon'.
