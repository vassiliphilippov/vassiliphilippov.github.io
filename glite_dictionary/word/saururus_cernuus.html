Saururus cernuus

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrʊərəs ˈsɜrnjuːs / (AmE), / sɔːˈrʊərəs ˈsɜː.njuːs / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb that grows in wetlands, with slender, curled spikes of small white flowers; commonly known as lizard's tail.
North American wetland plant; lizard's tail

Examples

Saururus cernuus thrives in moist, shady wetlands across the southeastern United States.
Gardeners often plant Saururus cernuus to add texture and interest to water gardens and ponds.
The long, white flower spikes of Saururus cernuus resemble lizards' tails, giving the plant its common name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard's tail - Common name for Saururus cernuus, a swamp plant with curved flowering spikes

Etymology

Genus from Greek 'sauros' (lizard) and 'oura' (tail), in reference to the shape of its flower spike; 'cernuus' is Latin for 'nodding' or 'drooping'.
