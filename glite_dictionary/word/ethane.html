ethane

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless gaseous alkane hydrocarbon (C2H6) used as a fuel and in the production of chemicals
a colorless, odorless alkane gas (C2H6)
medical

Examples

Natural gas often contains substantial amounts of ethane alongside methane.
The chemical industry uses ethane to produce ethylene via steam cracking.
Laboratory analysis revealed that the mixture consisted mostly of ethane and other light hydrocarbons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: propane - a similar alkane hydrocarbon with three carbon atoms
  • Degree Lower: methane - the simplest alkane hydrocarbon with one carbon atom

Etymology

From ethyl (the radical) + -ane (hydrocarbon suffix), introduced in the 19th century.
