to equip

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide someone with the skills or knowledge needed for a particular purpose or activity
to provide with knowledge or skills

Examples

Her education equipped her for a wide range of careers.
We aim to equip students with problem-solving skills for the workplace.
He felt poorly equipped to handle the unexpected challenge.

Synset Examples

She was never equipped to be a dancer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: train - to teach someone the skills for a job or activity

Etymology

From French 'équiper' (to fit out), originally from Old Norse 'skip' (ship).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00514258-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide a person or place with the necessary items, equipment, or skills for a particular purpose
provide with necessary items or skills

Examples

The school decided to equip each classroom with new computers and smart boards this year.
Before heading into the forest, the hikers made sure to equip themselves with maps and plenty of water.
Modern hospitals are carefully equipped to handle a wide range of emergencies.

Synset Examples

The expedition was equipped with proper clothing, food, and other necessities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to furnish - to provide or supply with furniture or equipment
  • Synonym: outfit - to supply with necessary clothes or equipment

Etymology

From French *équiper*, originally from Old Norse *skipa* (to arrange, fit out a ship).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02344409-v
Synset POS: v
