to skreigh

1. VERB

/ skreɪ / (AmE), / skreɪ / (BrE)
to utter a harsh, abrupt scream or shriek
to scream harshly and abruptly
archaic

Examples

The child began to skreigh when he saw the shadow in the hall.
At dawn, the gulls would skreigh above the rocky shore, waking all the villagers.
She skreighed with terror as the door slammed shut behind her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - to utter a high-pitched, piercing cry or sound
  • Synonym: scream - to give a loud, sharp, piercing cry

Etymology

From Scots 'skreigh', from Middle Dutch 'scrēghen' (to shriek).
