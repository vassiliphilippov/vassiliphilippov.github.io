eudaemon

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdiː.mən / (AmE), / juːˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
a benevolent or kindly spirit, especially in ancient Greek mythology or philosophy
benevolent spirit in mythology

Examples

In ancient Greek tales, a eudaemon would often guide heroes toward virtuous acts.
Philosophers sometimes describe conscience as a kind of inner eudaemon.
The villagers believed that a eudaemon protected their fields from harm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being

Etymology

From Greek 'eudaimōn' (having a good spirit, happy), from 'eu' (good) + 'daimōn' (spirit).
