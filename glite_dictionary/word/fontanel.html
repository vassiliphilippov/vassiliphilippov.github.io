fontanel

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑn.təˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˌfɒn.təˈnɛl / (BrE)
a soft spot on a baby's skull where the bones have not yet joined, covered by membrane
membranous gap in infant's skull
medical

Examples

The pediatrician checked the baby's fontanel during the examination.
You can gently feel the fontanel on the top of an infant's head.
By the age of two, the fontanel usually closes as the skull bones fuse together.

Etymology

From French 'fontanelle', from Old French, diminutive of 'fontaine' (fountain), inspired by the pulsing of blood under the membrane.
