embolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmbələs / (AmE), / ˈɛmbjʊləs / (BrE)
A blood clot, air bubble, or other abnormal particle that travels through the bloodstream and may cause a blockage in a blood vessel.
an abnormal particle in the bloodstream
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed an embolus that was blocking the patient's artery.
A large air bubble can act as an embolus and lead to serious complications.
Doctors quickly identified the embolus as the source of the brain's lack of blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombus - a blood clot formed within the vascular system

Etymology

From Latin 'embolus' (wedge, stopper), from Greek 'embolos' (peg, stopper, plug).
