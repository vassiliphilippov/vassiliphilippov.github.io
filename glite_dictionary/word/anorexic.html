anorexic

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈrɛksɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrɛksɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from anorexia nervosa, an eating disorder characterized by an intense fear of gaining weight and a distorted body image
person with anorexia nervosa
medical

Examples

She spoke about her experiences as an anorexic during the support group meeting.
The documentary followed the daily life of an anorexic struggling with recovery.
Doctors emphasize the importance of early intervention for an anorexic to improve outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexia nervosa - an eating disorder involving self-starvation and excessive weight loss

Etymology

From anorexia (from Greek an- 'without' + orexis 'appetite') + -ic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈrɛksɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrɛksɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from anorexia nervosa or showing symptoms of severe thinness associated with the disorder.
suffering from anorexia nervosa; pathologically thin
medical

Examples

The doctor expressed concern over her anorexic appearance and recommended immediate treatment.
He became anorexic after months of severe dieting and stress.
Their campaign raised awareness about the struggles faced by anorexic patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulimic - Suffering from bulimia nervosa
  • Antonym: obese - Very overweight; having excessive body fat

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'orexis' (appetite).
