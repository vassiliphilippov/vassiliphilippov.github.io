bazar

1. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
A shop or market where a variety of goods are sold, especially common in South Asian or Middle Eastern countries.
shop or market selling various goods

Examples

We visited the bustling bazar in the heart of the city.
She bought handmade jewelry at a local bazar during her trip.
Vendors in the bazar sold everything from spices to clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bazaar - a market or shop selling various goods

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'bazaar', from Persian 'bāzār' (market).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
a street or area lined with small shops and stalls, especially in Middle Eastern or South Asian countries; a market.
street or area with small shops; market

Examples

The city's ancient bazar was bustling with shoppers buying spices and fabrics.
Tourists love to explore the colorful bazar to find unique handmade goods.
We wandered down the narrow alleys of the old bazar, sampling street food along the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - a regular gathering for the sale of goods; marketplace
  • Synonym: bazaar - alternative spelling of 'bazar'; a market, especially in the Middle East or South Asia

Etymology

From Persian *bāzār* (market), via Turkish and Urdu.
