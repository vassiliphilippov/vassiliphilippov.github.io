likely

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkli / (BrE)
seeming to be true or possible; plausible or credible.
plausible or credible

Examples

That doesn't sound like a very likely explanation for what happened.
It's not likely that he simply forgot the meeting.
She accepted the most likely scenario after hearing the evidence.

Synset Examples

not a very likely excuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing
  • Antonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to happen

Etymology

From Old Norse *lík-ligr* (probable, suitable), related to 'like'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00648191-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˈlaɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkli / (BrE)
Probably; with considerable certainty or without much doubt.
probably; with considerable certainty

Examples

She will likely arrive before noon, so please be ready.
Given the dark clouds, it will likely rain this afternoon.
He will likely accept the offer, considering his current situation.

Synset Examples

He is probably out of the country
in all likelihood we are headed for war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probably - with likelihood; almost certainly
  • Degree Lower: possibly - by chance; perhaps; with less certainty

Etymology

From Old Norse *líklega* (probably), related to 'like' and the suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00139421-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkli / (BrE)
expected to happen or become true; probable or having a good chance of occurring or being the case.
probable; expected to happen

Examples

It is likely that the concert will be sold out before Friday.
The most likely explanation is a simple misunderstanding.
Scientists believe it is likely that climate change will accelerate without intervention.

Synset Examples

potential clients

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlikely - not expected to happen; improbable
  • Synonym: probable - likely to happen or to be true

Etymology

From Old Norse *lík-ligr* (suitable, probable), related to 'like'.

having a good chance of happening or being true; probable.
probable; having a good chance

Examples
