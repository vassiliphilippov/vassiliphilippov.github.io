Pope Gregory VII

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˌɡrɛɡəri səˈvɛnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌɡrɛɡəri səˈvɛnθ / (BrE)
the pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1073 to 1085, known for asserting papal authority over the church and secular rulers
11th-century pope who asserted papal authority

Examples

Pope Gregory VII excommunicated Emperor Henry IV during the Investiture Controversy.
Historians regard Pope Gregory VII as a key figure in the reform of the medieval church.
The doctrine of papal supremacy was strongly defended by Pope Gregory VII.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papacy - the office or authority of the pope
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters; of worldly matters
