sulky

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlki / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki / (BrE)
a light two-wheeled vehicle for one person, typically drawn by one horse, used especially in harness racing
two-wheeled horse-drawn racing vehicle

Examples

The jockey climbed into the sulky and prepared for the race.
Many harness races feature drivers seated in sulkies behind their horses.
She admired the lightweight design of the sulky as it sped around the track.

Etymology

Apparently from 'sulky' (adjective) in the sense of riding alone.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlki / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki / (BrE)
Bad-tempered or silently moody and unsociable, especially as a result of disappointment or anger.
sullen or moodily silent

Examples

After being told she couldn't go to the party, she became sulky and refused to speak.
He sat in the corner with a sulky expression, ignoring everyone around him.
It's hard to have a good time when someone is in a sulky mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullen - bad-tempered and sulky; gloomy
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From English dialect sulk (morose mood), likely of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'sulka' (to sulk).
