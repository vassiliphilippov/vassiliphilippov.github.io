low-lying

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
situated at a small elevation above the ground, sea level, or the surrounding area
at a small elevation above the ground

Examples

The flood warning is for low-lying areas near the river.
Dense fog formed over the low-lying fields at dawn.
Many crops were damaged in the low-lying coastal regions after the storm.

Synset Examples

low-lying clouds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-lying - situated at a greater elevation above the ground or sea level
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01209954-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially of land) situated below the normal level or the surrounding area, often close to water and prone to flooding.
situated below the normal level (of land)

Examples

After days of rain, the low-lying fields were completely flooded.
The village is in a low-lying area and is vulnerable to rising sea levels.
Much of the country's agricultural land is low-lying, making it ideal for rice cultivation.

Synset Examples

a low-lying desert

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-lying - situated above the normal or surrounding level
