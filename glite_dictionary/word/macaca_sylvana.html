Macaca sylvana

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːkə sɪlˈvænə / (AmE), / məˈkɑːkə sɪlˈvɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of tailless macaque found on rocky cliffs and in forests in northwestern Africa and Gibraltar, also known as the Barbary macaque.
a tailless macaque of North Africa and Gibraltar

Examples

The Macaca sylvana is the only species of macaque found in Europe.
Researchers observed a group of Macaca sylvana interacting near the famous cliffs of Gibraltar.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Macaca sylvana from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary ape - common name for Macaca sylvana

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'macaca' (macaque); species name from Latin 'sylvanus' meaning 'of the forest'.
