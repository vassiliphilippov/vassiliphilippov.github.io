Scomberesocidae

1. NOUN

/ skəmˌbɪrɪˈsɑsɪˌdi / (AmE), / skɒmˌbɛrɪˈsɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of slender, elongated fish known as sauries, found chiefly in temperate and tropical seas
family of fish including sauries

Examples

The family Scomberesocidae includes several species of sauries found in both Atlantic and Pacific oceans.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of fish in the Scomberesocidae family.
Fishermen rarely catch members of Scomberesocidae in colder waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saury - elongated, slender fish of the family Scomberesocidae

Etymology

From New Latin Scomberesox (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ skɒm.bər.ɪˈsoʊ.sɪ.daɪ / (AmE), / skɒm.bər.ɪˈsəʊ.sɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of slender, elongated marine fish known as sauries, found in temperate and tropical seas.
family of marine fish (sauries)
medical

Examples

Members of the Scomberesocidae family are commonly known as sauries and are found in open oceans.
The Scomberesocidae are characterized by their slender bodies and elongated jaws.
Marine biologists studied the migratory patterns of several Scomberesocidae species in the Pacific Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Belonidae - a family of fish known as needlefish

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek *skombros* (mackerel) and *esox* (pike), plus scientific suffix *-idae* denoting a family.
