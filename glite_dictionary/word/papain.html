papain

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪɪn / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪɪn / (BrE)
a proteolytic enzyme obtained from unripe papaya, commonly used as a meat tenderizer and in various medicinal applications
proteolytic enzyme from papaya
medical

Examples

The chef added papain to the marinade to make the meat more tender.
Researchers are studying how papain can aid digestion in certain health conditions.
Some over-the-counter ointments contain papain to help treat wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by a living organism that acts as a catalyst in biochemical reactions

Etymology

From French *papaïne*, from *papaye* (papaya).
