Liliidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈlaɪ.ɪdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈlɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a botanical subclass of monocotyledonous flowering plants, historically including lilies, orchids, and related families
a subclass of monocotyledonous plants

Examples

The subclass Liliidae includes many familiar plants such as lilies and orchids.
Botanical textbooks often discuss Liliidae when outlining the classification of monocots.
Members of Liliidae are characterized by having flower parts in multiples of three.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Monocotyledon - a large group of flowering plants having a single cotyledon
  • Degree Lower: Liliaceae - the lily family, a family within Liliidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from 'Liliaceae' (lily family) + '-idae' (taxonomic suffix denoting a group).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪliˈiːdi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌlɪlɪˈiːdiːiː / (BrE)
a subclass of monocotyledonous plants, comprising several families such as Liliaceae and Orchidaceae
plant subclass of monocotyledons
medical

Examples

The family Orchidaceae is classified within the subclass Liliidae.
Many bulbous flowering plants belong to the Liliidae subclass.
Botanists often debate the number of families included under Liliidae.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Lilium + the suffix -idae (indicating a taxonomic group).
