Aphididae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfɪˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌafɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, soft-bodied insects known as aphids or plant lice that feed on plant sap.
family of small plant-feeding insects

Examples

Aphididae are significant agricultural pests due to their rapid reproduction and ability to transmit plant viruses.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Aphididae family in the Amazon rainforest.
Control of Aphididae populations is important for maintaining healthy crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect; member of Aphididae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Aphis', the type genus, combined with the zoological suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfɪˈdɪdiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌafɪˈdɪːdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of small, soft-bodied insects commonly known as aphids or plant lice, which feed on plant sap.
family of aphids (plant lice)
medical

Examples

Gardeners often struggle to control infestations of Aphididae on their rose bushes.
Researchers are studying the ways Aphididae transmit plant viruses in crops.
The Aphididae family includes many species that are major agricultural pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small plant-eating insect, member of Aphididae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Aphididae, based on Aphidoidea (the superfamily name), ultimately from Greek 'aphis' (plant louse).
