Nikita Sergeyevich Khrushchev

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkitə sərˈɡjejɪvɪtʃ kruːˈʃtʃɛf / (AmE), / nɪˈkiːtə sɜːˈɡjeɪəvɪtʃ ˈkrʊʃtʃɔːf / (BrE)
(1894–1971) Soviet statesman who served as the First Secretary of the Communist Party (1953–1964) and Premier (1958–1964); known for denouncing Stalin and leading the Soviet Union during part of the Cold War.
Soviet leader and Premier, 1953–1964

Examples

Nikita Sergeyevich Khrushchev led the Soviet Union during some of the most tense years of the Cold War.
At the United Nations in 1960, Nikita Sergeyevich Khrushchev famously pounded his shoe on the desk.
The reforms and policies of Nikita Sergeyevich Khrushchev dramatically altered the course of Soviet history.
