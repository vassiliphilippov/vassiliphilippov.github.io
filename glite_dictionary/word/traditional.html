traditional

1. ADJ

/ trəˈdɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
following ideas, customs, or ways of behaving that have existed for a long time, rather than being new or modern.
following or derived from tradition

Examples

The meal was prepared in a traditional manner using family recipes.
He wore a traditional costume for the festival.
Many people still value traditional forms of art and music.

Synset Examples

traditional history
traditional morality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - following what is generally done or believed
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin 'traditio' (a handing over, surrender, tradition), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ trəˈdɪʃənl / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on long-established customs, beliefs, or practices; adhering to time-honored principles or methods.
relating to established customs or beliefs

Examples

The family followed a traditional recipe that had been passed down for generations.
Many people choose a traditional wedding ceremony, valuing longstanding customs over new trends.
Her views on education are quite traditional, favoring established methods and routines.

Synset Examples

the simple security of traditional assumptions has vanished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - based on what is generally done or believed
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past

Etymology

From Latin traditionem (a handing over, delivery), from tradere (to deliver, hand over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695220-s
Synset POS: s
