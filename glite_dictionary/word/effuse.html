to effuse

1. VERB

/ ɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to pour out or emit a liquid, light, or emotion freely and abundantly
pour out freely

Examples

The volcano began to effuse lava down its slopes during the minor eruption.
The perfume seemed to effuse from the open bottle, filling the room with its scent.
He tried to effuse gratitude, but his words felt forced and insincere.

Synset Examples

effused brine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out (something, such as light, heat, sound, or gas)

Etymology

From Latin 'effundere' (to pour out), via Late Latin 'effusus'.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, or other substance) to flow out or spill forth.
to flow or spill out

Examples

Water began to effuse from the broken pipe, flooding the basement within minutes.
Light seemed to effuse from the cracks in the old wooden door.
As the chemical reacted, gas started to effuse into the laboratory air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out (something such as a gas or light)
  • Synonym: to exude - to discharge slowly and steadily

Etymology

From Latin effundere (to pour out), from ex- (out) + fundere (to pour).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00547160-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to give out or emit something, especially a quality, feeling, or smell, often in an abundant or noticeable way
emit or give out (quality, feeling, etc.)

Examples

The garden seemed to effuse a sense of peace and tranquility.
She tried not to effuse too much excitement during the meeting.
The bakery effused the delicious aroma of freshly baked bread.

Synset Examples

The room effuses happiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emit - to send out (light, gas, heat, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin effundere, effusus 'pour out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292048-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to express (emotions, feelings, or words) freely and abundantly
express abundantly or freely

Examples

In her speech, she effused emotions of gratitude and happiness.
He never hesitated to effuse his emotions during important family gatherings.
The author effuses emotions throughout the novel, making the characters feel vivid and real.

Synset Examples

She tends to effuse her emotions freely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour out - to express something freely; to speak or write in a heartfelt way

Etymology

From Latin 'effūsus', past participle of 'effundere' (to pour out).
