to disabuse

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈbjuz / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈbjuːz / (BrE)
to persuade someone that a belief or idea they have is incorrect
correct a mistaken belief

Examples

It is difficult to disabuse her of the notion that she is always right.
The teacher tried to disabuse the students of their misconceptions about the topic.
He was finally disabused of his false beliefs after reading the scientific article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correct - make right; remove errors or faults
  • Antonym: to mislead - cause to have a wrong idea or impression

Etymology

From French 'désabuser', from dés- (dis-) + abuser (to deceive), ultimately from Latin 'abūsus'.
