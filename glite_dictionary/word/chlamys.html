chlamys

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmɪs / (AmE), / ˈklamɪs / (BrE)
A short mantle or cape, typically fastened at one shoulder, worn by men in ancient Greece.
short cape worn in ancient Greece

Examples

The ancient statue depicts a warrior draped in a chlamys over his shoulder.
During the festival, actors dressed in traditional Greek attire, complete with a chlamys.
He wore a chlamys to signify his status as a young nobleman.

Etymology

From Greek khlamýs (cloak, short mantle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæmɪs / (AmE), / ˈklamɪs / (BrE)
(Botany) The collective outer parts of a flower, consisting of the calyx and corolla, which enclose the stamens and pistils.
outer floral parts (calyx and corolla)
medical

Examples

The chlamys of the flower protects the reproductive organs within.
In some species, the chlamys is brightly colored to attract pollinators.
Botanists study the structure of the chlamys to classify flowering plants.

Etymology

From Latin chlamys, from Ancient Greek χλαμύς (khlamús, "cloak"), referring to the enclosing structure.
