pollucite

1. NOUN

/ pəˈluːsaɪt / (AmE), / pɒˈluːsaɪt / (BrE)
a rare mineral consisting mainly of hydrated cesium aluminum silicate, and the principal ore of cesium
rare mineral; main source of cesium

Examples

Pollucite is the principal ore used in the extraction of cesium for industrial applications.
Large deposits of pollucite are found in Canada and Zimbabwe.
Scientists study pollucite to understand the geochemistry of cesium-bearing minerals.

Etymology

From Latin Pollux, after its discovery paired with the mineral castorite (from Castor), referring to the mythological twins.
