Micruroides euryxanthus

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌkruːrɔɪˈdiːz ˌjʊrɪkˈsænθəs / (AmE), / mɪˌkruːrɔɪˈdiːz ˌjʊərɪkˈsænθəs / (BrE)
A species of small, venomous coral snake found from Central America to the southwestern United States, known for its brightly colored bands.
a species of venomous coral snake

Examples

The Micruroides euryxanthus is recognized by its distinctive red, black, and yellow bands.
Herpetologists study the Micruroides euryxanthus to understand its venom and behavior.
Sightings of Micruroides euryxanthus are most common in the deserts of Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a group of venomous snakes with brightly colored bands

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'micros' meaning small, 'oura' meaning tail, and 'euryxanthus' meaning broadly yellow.
