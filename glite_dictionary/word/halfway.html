halfway

1. ADV

/ ˈhæfˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf.weɪ / (BrE)
At or to a point that is in the middle between two things or at an equal distance from two points.
in the middle between two points

Examples

We got halfway up the mountain before it started to rain.
The solution lies halfway between logic and intuition.
She paused halfway through the book to make a cup of tea.

Synset Examples

he was halfway down the ladder when he fell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midway - at or near the middle point between two places or things
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256895-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæfˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf.weɪ / (BrE)
At an equal distance between two points or extremes; in the middle.
in the middle between two points

Examples

The house is situated halfway between the village and the city.
We met halfway down the road to discuss our plans.
She stopped running halfway up the hill to catch her breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midway - at or near the middle

Etymology

From Middle English 'half wey', literally 'half way'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00331404-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADV

/ ˌhæfˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈweɪ / (BrE)
At or to a point equally distant between two extremes, such as places, times, or states.
in the middle between two points

Examples

We met halfway between our two houses for lunch.
He stopped jogging when he reached the halfway point of the trail.
The project is only halfway done, so there's still work to do.

Synset Examples

at the halfway mark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midway - in the middle between two points or places

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'way', first used in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01018435-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˌhæfˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈweɪ / (BrE)
involving only half or a portion; not complete or whole
incomplete or partial

Examples

The committee only implemented halfway reforms, which failed to solve the main problems.
She criticized the policy as a halfway solution that would not be effective in the long run.
We don't have time for halfway measures; we need a comprehensive plan.

Synset Examples

halfway measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partial - not complete; existing only in part
  • Antonym: complete - having all parts; whole

Etymology

From Middle English 'half way', a compound of 'half' and 'way'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00519635-s
Synset POS: s
