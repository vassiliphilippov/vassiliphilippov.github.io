Algonquian

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkwiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪən / (BrE)
a member of any of the Native American groups traditionally speaking an Algonquian language, originally inhabiting the subarctic and woodland regions of eastern Canada and the northeastern United States
member of Algonquian-speaking Native American groups

Examples

The Algonquian were known for their extensive trade routes stretching across North America.
Many Algonquian peoples lived in the woodlands east of the Mississippi River.
Historical accounts describe interactions between English settlers and the Algonquian during the colonial period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian language - one of the indigenous languages spoken by Algonquian peoples

Etymology

From Canadian French 'Algonquin', possibly from Maliseet (an Algonquian language) 'elakómkwik' (they are our relatives/our allies).

2. ADJ

/ ælˈɡɑŋkwiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪən / (BrE)
relating to the Algonquian peoples, their languages, or their culture, which are indigenous to North America
relating to Algonquian peoples or languages

Examples

The Algonquian languages are spoken by many indigenous groups in northeastern North America.
She studied the traditional stories of Algonquian peoples for her anthropology thesis.
Several Algonquian tribes lived along the Atlantic coast before European arrival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquin - a specific Algonquian-speaking tribe and their language

Etymology

From Canadian French 'Algonquin', after the Algonquin people, influenced by the Maliseet word 'elakómkwik' (they are our relatives/kin).

3. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkwiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪən / (BrE)
A family of related North American Indigenous languages spoken from eastern Canada to the Great Plains, including Cree, Ojibwe, and Blackfoot.
family of North American Indigenous languages

Examples

Cree and Ojibwe are two well-known languages within the Algonquian family.
The Algonquian languages are spoken by various Indigenous groups across North America.
She is studying the grammar of several Algonquian languages for her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquin - language of the Algonquin people, member of the Algonquian family

Etymology

From Canadian French 'Algonquin', itself from either Maliseet 'elakómkwik' (they are our relatives/allies) or the Algonquin people's name for themselves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06919747-n
Synset POS: n
