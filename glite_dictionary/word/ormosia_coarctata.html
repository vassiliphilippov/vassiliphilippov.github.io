Ormosia coarctata

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈmoʊsiə koʊˈɑrktətə / (AmE), / ɔːˈməʊsiə kəʊˈɑːktətə / (BrE)
a species of tree native to the West Indies, similar to Ormosia monosperma but larger, with smaller leaflets and seeds
West Indian tree species

Examples

The dense wood of the Ormosia coarctata is highly valued by local craftsmen.
Botanists discovered several mature Ormosia coarctata trees deep within the forest reserve.
Unlike its relative, Ormosia coarctata has significantly smaller seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ormosia monosperma - a related tree species with larger leaflets and seeds
