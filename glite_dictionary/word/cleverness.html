cleverness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛvərnəs / (AmE), / ˈklɛvənɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to invent or create things easily; the power of creative imagination
creative inventiveness; imaginative ability

Examples

The cleverness of her solution impressed the entire board of directors.
It takes a certain cleverness to imagine such an innovative design.
Children often surprise adults with the cleverness of their ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the ability to invent things or solve problems in clever new ways

Etymology

From 'clever' + '-ness'. 'Clever' has uncertain origin, possibly East Anglian dialect.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛvɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈklɛvənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas; ingenuity or smartness
quality of being clever or ingenious

Examples

Her cleverness was apparent in the way she solved the complex puzzle.
The architect's cleverness is evident in the innovative design of the building.
He admired her cleverness in finding a solution nobody else had considered.

Synset Examples

a plot of great ingenuity
the cleverness of its design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the quality of being clever, original, and inventive
  • Antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense

Etymology

Formed from 'clever' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04737625-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɛvɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈklɛvənɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being quick, original, and witty in thought or action
quick and witty intelligence

Examples

Her cleverness helped the team solve the problem quickly.
Everyone admired his cleverness during the lively debate.
Sarah's cleverness always shines through in her witty remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to use words and ideas in a quick and inventive way
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of wit or intelligence; slow to understand

Etymology

From 'clever' + '-ness', the suffix forming nouns denoting quality.
