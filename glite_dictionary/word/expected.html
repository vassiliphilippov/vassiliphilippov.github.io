expected

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛktɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktɪd / (BrE)
regarded as likely to happen or be the case; anticipated.
regarded as likely; anticipated

Examples

The expected results were announced at the end of the conference.
Her arrival was expected, so everyone was already waiting for her.
We prepared thoroughly for the expected changes in company policy.

Synset Examples

prepared for the expected attack

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unexpected - not regarded as likely to happen; not anticipated
  • Synonym: anticipated - expected, awaited or looked forward to

Etymology

From the verb "expect" (from Latin *exspectare* meaning 'look out for'), plus the English -ed suffix.
