geometer

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːmətər / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a mathematician who specializes in the study of geometry
mathematician specializing in geometry

Examples

Euclid is perhaps the most famous geometer of antiquity.
The accomplished geometer published several papers on non-Euclidean spaces.
As a budding geometer, she spent hours studying the properties of triangles and circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometry - the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relationships of points, lines, surfaces, and solids

Etymology

From Greek geōmetria (geometry), from geōmetrein (to measure the earth).
