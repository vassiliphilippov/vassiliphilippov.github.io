Santalales

1. NOUN

/ sænˈtæleɪliːz / (AmE), / sænˈtæleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants characterized by a one-celled inferior ovary; many species are parasitic or partly parasitic, often on roots
an order of parasitic or semi-parasitic plants

Examples

The Santalales include several species of mistletoe, well known for their parasitic behavior.
Botanists study the Santalales to understand the evolution of plant parasitism.
Many members of the Santalales are important ecologically because they draw nutrients from their host plants.

Etymology

From modern Latin Santalales, based on the genus Santalum (sandalwood).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæn.təˈleɪlˌiz / (AmE), / ˌsæntəˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants characterized by a one-celled inferior ovary, with many species being parasitic or partially parasitic, usually on roots
an order of mainly parasitic flowering plants

Examples

The Santalales include many species with unusual flowering structures.
Some members of the Santalales are known for their parasitic lifestyle on host plants.
Botanists have long debated the evolutionary origins of Santalales.

Etymology

From the family name Santalaceae, itself from Latin *santalum* (sandalwood).
