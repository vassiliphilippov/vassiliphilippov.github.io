Leonurus cardiaca

1. NOUN

/ liˈɑːnərəs kɑrˈdiːəkə / (AmE), / liˈɒnərəs kɑːˈdiːəkə / (BrE)
a bitter Old World herb of hedgerows and woodland margins, having toothed leaves and white or pale pink flowers; commonly known as motherwort
herb known as motherwort

Examples

Leonurus cardiaca has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for heart and nerve conditions.
The white or pale pink flowers of Leonurus cardiaca appear along country hedgerows in late spring.
Botanists studied the ecological benefits of Leonurus cardiaca in supporting pollinator populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherwort - common name for Leonurus cardiaca, a medicinal herb

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Leonurus' meaning 'lion's tail' and species 'cardiaca' referring to 'heart', relating to its former medicinal use.
