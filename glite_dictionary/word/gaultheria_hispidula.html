Gaultheria hispidula

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔlˈθɪriə hɪˈspɪdjulə / (AmE), / ɡɔːlˈθɪəriə hɪˈspɪdjʊlə / (BrE)
A slow-growing, low evergreen shrub native to northern North America and Japan, with white flowers and small white hairy berries; also known as creeping snowberry.
a North American/Japanese evergreen shrub

Examples

The forest floor was covered in patches of Gaultheria hispidula, its white berries gleaming among the moss.
Botanists noted that Gaultheria hispidula prefers moist, acidic soils in cool climates.
Hikers often mistake Gaultheria hispidula for other low-growing shrubs due to its delicate size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping snowberry - common name for Gaultheria hispidula

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gaultheria' (after botanist Jean-François Gaultier) and species 'hispidula' (diminutive of 'hispidus', bristly).
