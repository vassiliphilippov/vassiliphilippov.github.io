Blighia

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪgiə / (AmE), / ˈblaɪgiə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen trees and shrubs from West Africa, known for their fleshy, three-seeded edible fruits.
genus of West African trees and shrubs

Examples

The Blighia genus includes species valued for their distinctive fruits.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Blighia in various climates.
The ackee, a fruit from the Blighia genus, is a staple in West African cuisine.

Etymology

Named after Captain William Bligh (1754–1817), British naval officer.
