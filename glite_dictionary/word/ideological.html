ideological

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪdiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on a set of beliefs or a system of ideas, especially those that form the basis of political or economic theory and policy.
relating to a system of beliefs or ideas

Examples

The new law was criticized for being driven by ideological motives rather than practical concerns.
Her ideological beliefs shaped the policies she proposed during her campaign.
Debates about education often become ideological, focusing on values rather than facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrinal - relating to doctrine or a set of beliefs; a synonym in certain contexts
  • Antonym: pragmatic - dealing with things practically rather than according to theory or ideology; often an antonym

Etymology

From French idéologique, from idéologie (ideology) + -ique (-ical).

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪdiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with or relating to ideas, especially those forming the basis of economic or political theory and policy
relating to ideas or ideology

Examples

The debate became highly ideological, with each side refusing to compromise.
His ideological beliefs influenced every decision he made.
The newspaper is known for its ideological perspective on current affairs.

Synset Examples

ideological application of a theory
the drama's symbolism was very ideological

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideologic - relating to or characteristic of ideology; an alternative adjective
  • Antonym: pragmatic - dealing with things practically rather than theoretically or ideologically

Etymology

From French idéologique, from idéologie (ideology), ultimately from Greek idea and logos (word, reason).

Synset POS: s
