anaphoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈfɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌænəˈfɒrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving anaphora, especially the use of a word or phrase to refer back to another word or phrase earlier in a sentence or context.
relating to anaphora in language
medical

Examples

The pronoun 'she' is used in an anaphoric way to refer to 'Maria' in the previous sentence.
Understanding anaphoric references is important for accurate reading comprehension.
The linguist wrote an article explaining the use of anaphoric expressions in modern English.

Synset Examples

anaphoric reference

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cataphoric - relating to cataphora, where a word or phrase refers forward to another

Etymology

From Greek anaphorikos (relating to referring back), from anaphora.
