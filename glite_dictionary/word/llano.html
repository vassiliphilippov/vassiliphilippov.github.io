llano

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈljɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A wide, grassy, almost treeless plain, especially one found in Latin America.
grassy, treeless plain (Latin America)

Examples

The vast llano stretched to the horizon, dotted occasionally by small herds of cattle.
Early settlers built their homes along the edges of the llano to avoid the harsh winds.
During the rainy season, wildflowers bloom across the llano, transforming the plain into a sea of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie - a large open area of grassland, especially in North America

Etymology

From Spanish *llano* (plain, flat land), from Latin *planum* (flat surface).
