energetism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnərdʒɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophical theory that all phenomena can be understood as manifestations or transformations of energy.
theory that all phenomena are forms of energy

Examples

The philosophy of energetism proposes that all matter and processes are essentially just forms of energy.
Many debates in early twentieth-century science were influenced by energetism and its challenge to traditional materialism.
Ostwald is best known for his advocacy of energetism, which shaped his views on science and philosophy.

Synset Examples

Ostwald's philosophical outlook, known as energetism or energetic monism, was strongly influenced by his scientific background and by the state of physical science at the end of the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energetics - the branch of science that studies energy and its transformations

Etymology

From German 'Energetismus', from 'Energie' (energy) + '-ismus' (-ism).
