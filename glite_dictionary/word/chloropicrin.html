chloropicrin

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˈpɪkrɪn / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˈpɪkrɪn / (BrE)
a heavy, colorless liquid chemical compound used as a pesticide and as tear gas, known for causing irritation to the eyes and respiratory system
chemical compound used as pesticide and tear gas

Examples

The greenhouse was fumigated with chloropicrin to eradicate soil-borne pests.
Workers must wear protective equipment when handling chloropicrin due to its toxic properties.
Exposure to chloropicrin can cause severe eye irritation and difficulty breathing.

Etymology

From chloro- (relating to chlorine) + picrin (from Greek 'pikros', meaning bitter, from its bitter taste and relation to picric acid).
