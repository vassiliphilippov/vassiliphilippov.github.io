dry-shod

1. ADJ

/ ˈdraɪ ˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌʃɒd / (BrE)
Having or keeping the feet or shoes dry, especially when crossing wet ground or water.
with dry feet or shoes

Examples

Despite heavy rain, the new boots kept her dry-shod all day.
He made it across the stream dry-shod, thanks to the stepping stones.
No one expected to reach the barn dry-shod after last night’s downpour.

Synset Examples

a land bridge over which man and beasts could have crossed dry-shod

Etymology

Formed from 'dry' + 'shod' (past participle of 'shoe').
