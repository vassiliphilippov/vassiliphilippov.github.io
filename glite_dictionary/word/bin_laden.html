bin Laden

1. PROPN

/ bɪn ˈlɑdən / (AmE), / bɪn ˈlɑːdən / (BrE)
Osama bin Laden (1957–2011), an Arab terrorist who founded al-Qaeda and orchestrated numerous attacks, including those on September 11, 2001.
founder of al-Qaeda, Arab terrorist leader

Examples

bin Laden was considered one of the most wanted men in the world after the September 11 attacks.
The death of bin Laden was a significant moment in the fight against terrorism.
Many governments increased security measures in response to threats posed by bin Laden and his organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osama bin Laden - Full name of bin Laden, founder of al-Qaeda
  • Synonym: al-Qaeda - Islamist militant group founded by bin Laden

Etymology

Arabic surname meaning 'son of Laden'; 'Osama bin Laden' is the full name.
