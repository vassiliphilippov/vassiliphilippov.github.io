Kichaga language

1. NOUN

/ kiˈtʃɑːɡə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / kɪˈtʃɑːɡə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken by the Chaga people in northern Tanzania
Bantu language of northern Tanzania

Examples

The Kichaga language is still spoken by many people living on the slopes of Mount Kilimanjaro.
Linguists have studied the Kichaga language to understand its unique phonetic features.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Kichaga language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swahili language - a widely spoken Bantu language in East Africa

Etymology

From Swahili 'Ki-' (language prefix) + 'Chaga' (an ethnic group in Tanzania).
