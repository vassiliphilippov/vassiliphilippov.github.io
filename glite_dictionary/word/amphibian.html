amphibian

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed motor vehicle designed to operate both on land and water
vehicle that travels on land and water

Examples

The army deployed an amphibian to cross the flooded river and continue the mission.
During the tour, we rode on an amphibian that drove into the lake.
The amphibian navigated the rough shoreline before gliding smoothly into the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibious vehicle - a vehicle that can operate on both land and water

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios' (living a double life), used in English for animals and later for vehicles with dual modes.

2. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
a cold-blooded vertebrate animal, such as a frog or salamander, typically living both on land and in water, with aquatic larvae that undergo metamorphosis into the adult form
cold-blooded vertebrate living on land and water

Examples

Frogs and salamanders are well-known amphibians found in many parts of the world.
The amphibian spends its early life underwater as a tadpole before moving onto land.
Deforestation threatens the survival of many rare amphibians in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: reptile - a cold-blooded vertebrate with scales, such as a snake or lizard
  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with long hind legs

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios' meaning 'living a double life' (amphi- 'both' + bios 'life').

/ æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
an aircraft that is designed to take off from and land on both water and land
aircraft for land and water operations

Examples

Pilots must take a special course to operate an amphibian safely.
The new amphibian features advanced technology for smooth water landings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaplane - an aircraft designed only for takeoff and landing on water

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios' (living a double life).

4. ADJ

/ æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / amˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of animals belonging to the class Amphibia, which includes frogs, toads, salamanders, and similar creatures that typically live both on land and in water.
of or relating to amphibians (frogs, etc.)

Examples

The scientists discovered an amphibian species that can tolerate extreme temperatures.
She studied the amphibian skin's ability to absorb water from its environment.
Museum exhibits often feature amphibian skeletons alongside those of reptiles and mammals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reptilian - relating to or characteristic of reptiles

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios' (living a double life), from 'amphi-' (both) + 'bios' (life).
