antiqueness

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtik.nəs / (AmE), / ænˈtiːk.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being ancient or characteristic of an earlier time; the state of embodying features or styles from the past
the quality of being ancient or old-fashioned

Examples

The antiqueness of the old library gave it a timeless charm.
Many collectors are drawn to the antiqueness of handcrafted furniture.
The antiqueness of her dress made it stand out at the party.

Synset Examples

We may discover something venerable in the antiqueness of the work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquity - the ancient past, especially before the Middle Ages
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality of being modern; the state of being current or up to date

Etymology

From 'antique' + '-ness', the suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
