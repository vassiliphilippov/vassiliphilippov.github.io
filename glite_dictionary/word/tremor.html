tremor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
an involuntary shaking or quivering movement, especially caused by illness, fear, or nervousness
involuntary shaking or vibration

Examples

The patient's hands displayed a noticeable tremor as a symptom of the disease.
She tried to hide the tremor in her voice when she delivered the sad news.
A slight tremor ran through his body as he stepped onto the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiver - a slight shaking movement caused by cold, fear, or excitement
  • Synonym: shake - to move with short, quick movements

Etymology

From Latin tremor (a trembling), from tremere (to tremble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
A slight or minor earthquake; a small seismic movement of the earth's surface.
a small earthquake

Examples

The city experienced a mild tremor that rattled windows but caused no damage.
A series of minor tremors preceded the larger earthquake.
Residents felt a tremor early in the morning and rushed outside.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground

Etymology

From Latin tremor (a shaking, quaking), from tremere (to tremble).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
an involuntary or uncontrollable shaking movement, often caused by weakness, stress, or disease
an involuntary shaking movement

Examples

The patient experienced a mild tremor in his right hand.
The earthquake caused a slight tremor that was felt across the city.
She tried to hide the tremor in her voice as she spoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly back and forth or up and down
  • Synonym: quiver - to tremble or shake with a slight rapid motion
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being steady and not shaking

Etymology

From Latin tremor (shaking, trembling), from tremere (to tremble).
