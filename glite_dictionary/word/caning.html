caning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
work or material made by weaving slender branches, especially willow, into a mesh pattern, often used for furniture or baskets.
material of woven slender branches

Examples

The antique chair had a seat made of caning that showed signs of wear.
Skilled artisans demonstrated traditional caning techniques at the craft fair.
The restoration process included replacing the broken caning on the armchair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicker - material made from woven plant stems or twigs

Etymology

From 'cane', referring to the stems of certain plants used for weaving.
