narrowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being narrow; having little width
the quality of being narrow

Examples

The narrowness of the path made it difficult for two people to walk side by side.
He was concerned about the narrowness of the doorway when moving the furniture in.
The narrowness of her perspective limited her understanding of the issue.

Synset Examples

the narrowness of the road

Related Terms

  • Antonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Synonym: narrow - having a small distance from side to side
  • Antonym: broadness - the quality or state of being broad or wide

Etymology

From 'narrow' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being narrow.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
an unwillingness to accept opinions or behaviors different from one's own; intolerance
intolerance toward differing views or behavior

Examples

His narrowness of mind made discussions with him frustrating and fruitless.
She was disliked for the narrowness she showed whenever new ideas were suggested.
Political narrowness can hinder progress and cooperation in a diverse society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tolerance - willingness to accept others' opinions or behavior
  • Antonym: open-mindedness - being receptive to new ideas or opinions
  • Synonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept others' views or behavior

Etymology

From the adjective 'narrow' + suffix '-ness' (forming nouns).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being small in amount, distance, or difference, especially when referring to an amount by which someone wins or achieves something
small margin or difference

Examples

The narrowness of the vote surprised even seasoned political analysts.
Despite the narrowness of their lead, the team managed to hold on to victory.
He was well aware of the narrowness of his escape from the accident.

Synset Examples

the president was not humbled by his narrow margin of victory
the landslide he had in the electoral college obscured the narrowness of a victory based on just 43% of the popular vote

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margin - an amount by which something is won or falls short
  • Antonym: width - the quality of being broad or wide, as opposed to narrowness

Etymology

From narrow + -ness, denoting the state or quality of being narrow.

4. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being limited in range, scope, or extent; lack of breadth or inclusiveness
limited range or scope

Examples

The narrowness of the curriculum restricts students' learning opportunities.
His arguments were criticized for their narrowness and lack of perspective.
She admired the depth but not the narrowness of his expertise.

Synset Examples

the problem with achievement tests is the narrowness they impose on students
the attraction of the book is precisely its narrowness of focus
frustrated by the narrowness of people's horizons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: breadth - the quality of having a wide range or scope
  • Synonym: narrow - limited in width, range, or scope (adjective form)

Etymology

From 'narrow' (Middle English 'narwe'), combined with the suffix '-ness' to indicate a state or quality.
