Rauvolfia

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈvɒlfiə / (AmE), / raʊˈvɒlfiə / (BrE)
A genus of pantropical shrubs and small trees, some species of which are poisonous.
genus of poisonous shrubs and small trees

Examples

Several species of Rauvolfia are used in traditional medicine for their alkaloid compounds.
The roots of Rauvolfia plants have long been studied for their medicinal properties.
Botanists discovered a new variety within the Rauvolfia genus in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

Named after the 16th-century German physician and botanist Leonhard Rauwolf.
