Chamaedaphne calyculata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmiˈdæfni ˌkælɪˈkjuleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈdæfni ˌkalɪˈkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
A shrub found in northern temperate bogs, with evergreen leathery leaves and small white cylindrical flowers.
bog shrub with evergreen leaves

Examples

The wetland was dotted with patches of Chamaedaphne calyculata along the boardwalk.
Botanists surveyed the population of Chamaedaphne calyculata to monitor changes in the ecosystem.
The leathery leaves of Chamaedaphne calyculata help it thrive in cold, acidic bogs.

Etymology

From Greek *chamai* (on the ground) + *daphne* (laurel); *calyculata* from Latin meaning 'having small cups' (referring to parts of the flower).
