piebald

1. ADJ

/ ˈpaɪˌbɔld / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.bɔːld / (BrE)
having irregular patches of two or more colors, typically referring to an animal's coat
having differently colored patches

Examples

The old circus horse, now retired, was a striking piebald.
Tourists admired the piebald appearance of the wild ponies in the field.
He wore a piebald costume with large black and white patches.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particolored - having various or many different colors
  • Synonym: pied - having two or more different colors in blotches

Etymology

From pie (magpie) + bald; originally referring to the black-and-white coloring of a magpie.
