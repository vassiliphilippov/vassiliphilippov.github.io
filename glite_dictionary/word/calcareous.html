calcareous

1. ADJ

/ kælˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / kælˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
composed of or containing calcium carbonate, or having the characteristics of chalk or limestone
containing calcium carbonate; chalky

Examples

The soil in this region is highly calcareous, which affects what kinds of plants can grow here.
Many marine organisms build calcareous shells as protection against predators.
Geologists found layers of calcareous rock throughout the ancient cave system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalky - resembling or containing chalk
  • Synonym: limy - containing or resembling lime
  • Antonym: siliceous - containing or relating to silica

Etymology

From Latin calcareus (of lime), from calx (lime, limestone).
