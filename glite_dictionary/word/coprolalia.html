coprolalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.prəˈleɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.prəˈleɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by the involuntary and repetitive use of obscene or socially inappropriate language, often associated with certain mental disorders such as Tourette's syndrome
involuntary use of obscene language
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that coprolalia is a symptom that sometimes occurs with Tourette's syndrome.
People with coprolalia may feel embarrassed by their uncontrollable outbursts of inappropriate words.
Despite her struggle with coprolalia, she continued to participate actively in social activities.

Etymology

From Greek 'kopros' (dung) + 'lalia' (speech).
