transcript

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
a written or typed copy of spoken words, especially an official or verbatim record of speech, conversation, or events
written record of spoken words

Examples

The court provided a transcript of the entire trial to the defense attorney.
After the meeting, the assistant prepared a transcript for the official records.
She requested a transcript of the interview for her research.

Synset Examples

he read a transcript of the interrogation
you can obtain a transcript of this radio program by sending a self-addressed envelope to the station

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - an account in writing or some other permanent form
  • Synonym: transcription - the process of making a written copy of speech or data

Etymology

From Latin transcriptum, past participle of transcribere (to transcribe), from trans- (across) + scribere (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
an official copy of a written record, especially of academic achievements or legal proceedings
official copy of a written record

Examples

The university required an official transcript from all previous schools attended.
After the trial, the lawyer reviewed the transcript of the witness's testimony.
She submitted her high school transcript with her college application.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - an official written account of something that has been said, done, or occurred

Etymology

From Latin transcriptum, past participle of transcribere (to transcribe).
