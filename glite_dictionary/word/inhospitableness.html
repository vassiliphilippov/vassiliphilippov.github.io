inhospitableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɑːspɪtəblnɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɒspɪtəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being unfriendly or unwelcoming to guests or strangers.
quality of being unfriendly or unwelcoming

Examples

The inhospitableness of the mountain villagers surprised the exhausted travelers.
His inhospitableness made visitors feel unwelcome in his home.
Some regions are known for the inhospitableness of their climate as well as their people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhospitality - the quality of being inhospitable or unwelcoming
  • Antonym: hospitality - friendly and welcoming behavior toward guests

Etymology

From inhospitable + -ness, ultimately from Latin inhospitalis (not hospitable).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɑːspɪtəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɒspɪtəbəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being inhospitable, especially an environment or region lacking suitable conditions for life or growth.
state of being inhospitable

Examples

The inhospitableness of the desert makes it difficult for most plants and animals to survive there.
Explorers were daunted by the inhospitableness of the Arctic landscape.
Despite the inhospitableness of the mountainous region, some communities have managed to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflexibility - the quality of being unwilling to change or adapt
  • Antonym: hospitality - the friendly reception and treatment of guests or strangers

Etymology

From inhospitable + -ness, following the pattern of English abstract nouns.
