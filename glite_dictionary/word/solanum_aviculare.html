Solanum aviculare

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm ˌævɪˈkjulɛr / (AmE), / sɒˈleɪnəm ˌævɪˈkjʊlɛə / (BrE)
A species of nightshade native to Australia, sometimes cultivated for its clusters of purple flowers and edible yellow, egg-shaped fruit.
Australian nightshade with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The garden's collection includes Solanum aviculare because of its attractive purple flowers.
Many bush foods in Australia feature the yellow fruit of Solanum aviculare.
Scientists studied the medicinal properties of Solanum aviculare in treating certain ailments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' (nightshade) and 'aviculare' meaning 'of small birds', possibly referencing the fruit.
