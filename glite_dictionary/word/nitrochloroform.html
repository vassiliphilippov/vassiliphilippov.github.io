nitrochloroform

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈklɔːr.əˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.trəʊˈklɔː.rə.fɔːm / (BrE)
A heavy, colorless, and insoluble liquid chemical compound that induces tears and vomiting, used as a pesticide and in tear gas.
chemical compound used as pesticide and tear gas
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored nitrochloroform in a tightly sealed container due to its toxic effects.
Exposure to nitrochloroform can quickly cause eye irritation and severe discomfort.
Farmers once used nitrochloroform as a pesticide to control soil pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloropicrin - A chemical compound used as a pesticide and in tear gas

Etymology

Formed from 'nitro-' (relating to nitric acid derivatives) and 'chloroform' (from Greek 'chloros' meaning greenish yellow and 'form' from 'formic acid').
