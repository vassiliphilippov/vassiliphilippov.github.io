St. John

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
A port city in eastern Canada, serving as the largest city in the province of New Brunswick.
city in New Brunswick, Canada

Examples

St. John is known for its historic uptown and vibrant waterfront community.
Many cruise ships stop at St. John during their voyages along the eastern coast of Canada.
The population of St. John has steadily grown over the past decade.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
A river originating in Maine, flowing northeast through New Brunswick, and emptying into the Bay of Fundy.
river in Maine and New Brunswick

Examples

The St. John is a major waterway for both Maine and New Brunswick.
Several towns depend on the St. John for their water supply and recreation.
We took a scenic drive along the St. John during our trip to Canada.

3. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
(In the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, traditionally regarded as the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the Book of Revelation.
apostle; author of biblical texts

Examples

Many believe that St. John authored the Book of Revelation while exiled on Patmos.
The Gospel according to St. John contains distinct narratives compared to the other Gospels.
Some churches are named after St. John to honor his legacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostle - one of the twelve disciples of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Johannes', via Old French 'Saint Jehan'.
