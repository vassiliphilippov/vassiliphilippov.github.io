aniseikonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ɪ.saɪˈkɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪ.saɪˈkɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of aniseikonia, a condition in which the images seen by the two eyes differ in size or shape.
relating to aniseikonia (visual defect)
medical

Examples

After her cataract surgery, the patient reported aniseikonic vision, with objects appearing larger in one eye.
Special lenses can be used to correct aniseikonic distortion and restore comfortable binocular vision.
The optometrist explained that aniseikonic symptoms could include headaches and eye strain due to unequal image sizes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniseikonia - a visual defect in which the images seen by the two eyes differ in size or shape

Etymology

Formed from 'aniseikonia' (from Greek 'an-' not + 'iso-' equal + 'eikon' image) and the suffix '-ic'.
