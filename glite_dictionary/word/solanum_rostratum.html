Solanum rostratum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm rɒˈstrætəm / (AmE), / sɒˈleɪnəm rɒˈstrætəm / (BrE)
A North American nightshade species with prickly leaves and yellow flowers in racemes, commonly known as buffalobur.
prickly North American nightshade plant

Examples

The fields were overrun with Solanum rostratum after the summer rains.
Researchers studied the spread of Solanum rostratum in the southwest United States.
The prickly leaves of Solanum rostratum can be a hazard to grazing animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalobur - Common name for Solanum rostratum, a prickly nightshade plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' meaning nightshade and 'rostratum' meaning beaked or provided with a beak.
