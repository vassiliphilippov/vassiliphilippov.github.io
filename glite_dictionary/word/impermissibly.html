impermissibly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪmpərˈmɪsəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈmɪsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is not permitted or allowed by rules or laws.
in a manner not allowed

Examples

The judge ruled that the evidence had been obtained impermissibly.
The chemicals in the water were impermissibly high, exceeding safety standards.
He was impermissibly late for the meeting despite repeated warnings.

Synset Examples

the radon level in the basement was impermissibly high

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissibly - in a manner that is allowed or permitted

Etymology

From 'impermissible' (not permitted) plus the adverb-forming suffix '-ly'.
