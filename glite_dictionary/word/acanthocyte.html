acanthocyte

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / əˈkanθəˌsʌɪt / (BrE)
an abnormal red blood cell with spiky or thorny projections on its surface, often seen in certain medical conditions
abnormal red blood cell with spiky projections
medical

Examples

The blood smear revealed several acanthocytes, indicating a possible lipid metabolism disorder.
Doctors observed acanthocytes under the microscope, which helped them diagnose a rare blood condition.
Presence of acanthocytes is often associated with disorders such as abetalipoproteinemia.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn) + -cyte (cell).
