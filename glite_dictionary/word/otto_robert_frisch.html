Otto Robert Frisch

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtoʊ ˈroʊbərt ˈfrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒtəʊ ˈrəʊbət ˈfrɪʃ / (BrE)
(1904–1979) British physicist, born in Austria, who with Lise Meitner identified nuclear fission and described the potential of a nuclear chain reaction.
Austrian-British physicist; co-discovered nuclear fission

Examples

Otto Robert Frisch worked with Lise Meitner to explain the process of nuclear fission.
The discovery by Otto Robert Frisch changed the course of nuclear physics.
Few physicists have had as lasting an impact as Otto Robert Frisch in the field of atomic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lise Meitner - Austrian-Swedish physicist who worked with Otto Robert Frisch on nuclear fission
  • Degree Higher: nuclear fission - Type of nuclear reaction discovered by Frisch and Meitner
