placative

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpleɪkətɪv / (BrE)
intended to pacify or appease, especially by making concessions or yielding to demands.
meant to pacify or appease

Examples

He made several placative gestures in an attempt to calm the angry crowd.
Her placative tone helped resolve the heated discussion peacefully.
The company issued a placative statement to address the workers' complaints.

Synset Examples

the appeasing concessions to the Nazis at Munich
placating (or placative) gestures
an astonishingly placatory speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placatory - intended to placate or pacify
  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended to gain goodwill or reduce hostility
  • Antonym: provocative - causing anger or another strong reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'placare' (to calm or appease), influenced by English 'placate'.
