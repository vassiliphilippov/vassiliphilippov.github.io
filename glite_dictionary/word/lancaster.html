Lancaster

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæŋˌkæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈlæŋkɑːstə / (BrE)
a city in northwestern England, known for its historical castle and role as the county town of Lancashire.
city in northwestern England

Examples

We visited Lancaster last summer to tour its famous medieval castle.
The university in Lancaster is renowned for its research programs.
Heavy rain caused flooding in parts of Lancaster this week.

Etymology

From Old English 'Loncastre', meaning 'Roman fort on the River Lune'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈlæŋˌkæstər / (AmE), / ˈlæŋkəstə / (BrE)
the English royal house reigning from 1399 to 1461, whose emblem was a red rose
English royal house (1399–1461)

Examples

The Lancaster dynasty played a central role in the Wars of the Roses.
Henry IV was the first monarch from the Lancaster branch of the royal family.
Many supporters of Lancaster adopted the red rose as their symbol.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: York - the English royal house opposing Lancaster in the Wars of the Roses; its emblem was a white rose

Etymology

From the name of the English city of Lancaster; the royal house was named for its chief seat.
