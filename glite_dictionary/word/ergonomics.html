ergonomics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɜr.ɡəˈnɑ.mɪks / (AmE), / ˌɜː.ɡəˈnɒm.ɪks / (BrE)
The study of designing equipment, devices, and workplaces to fit the human body and its movements for efficiency and safety.
study of efficient, safe workplace design

Examples

The company invested in ergonomics to reduce workplace injuries and improve productivity.
Proper ergonomics at your desk can help prevent repetitive strain injuries.
She studied ergonomics as part of her industrial engineering degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human factors - the study of how humans interact with systems and products

Etymology

From Greek 'ergon' (work) + 'nomos' (law, rule).
