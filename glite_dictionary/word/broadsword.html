broadsword

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌsɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdsɔːd / (BrE)
a large sword with a wide, straight blade and usually two cutting edges, designed for cutting rather than stabbing
a sword with a broad, cutting blade

Examples

The knight drew his broadsword and prepared for battle.
Historians display the broadsword as an example of medieval weaponry.
He practiced swinging the heavy broadsword in the training yard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saber - a sword with a curved blade, single edge, and a guard for the hand
  • Antonym: rapier - a slender, sharply pointed sword

Etymology

From broad (wide) + sword; first used in English in the late 16th century.
