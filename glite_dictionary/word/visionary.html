visionary

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
a person who has the ability to imagine or plan the future with great foresight or creativity
person with great foresight or imagination

Examples

As a visionary, she introduced ideas that were decades ahead of her time.
Many successful tech entrepreneurs are considered modern-day visionaries.
The artist was labeled a visionary for his innovative approach to sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who dreams or is lost in thought; sometimes used for someone with impractical ideas
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and deals with it accordingly

Etymology

From Latin 'visionarius' (relating to a vision), from 'visio' (a seeing, vision).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
(of ideas or plans) not practical or likely to be realized; based on speculation or imagination rather than realism.
not practical; speculative

Examples

Many considered his visionary plans for urban development to be unrealistic and unworkable.
The company dismissed her visionary proposals as too ambitious and speculative for their current resources.
While the project's goals were visionary, investors wanted a more practical and attainable approach.

Synset Examples

airy theories about socioeconomic improvement
visionary schemes for getting rich

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice; focused on what is possible or achievable
  • Antonym: realistic - having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected
  • Synonym: impractical - not adapted for use or action; not sensible or realistic

Etymology

From vision + -ary, influenced by French visionnaire.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
a person who is prone to fanciful ideas or unrealistic plans, with little concern for what is achievable
person with fanciful or unrealistic ideas

Examples

He was dismissed as a visionary whose ideas could never work in the real world.
The project was abandoned, for even its main supporter was known to be something of a visionary.
Some critics accused her of being a visionary detached from practical concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who dreams or has unrealistic ideas
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are and deals with them practically

Etymology

From Latin visio (a seeing) + -ary.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
