cramped

1. ADJ

/ kræmpt / (AmE), / kræmpt / (BrE)
(of a space) uncomfortably small or restricted; having little room to move.
restricted or uncomfortably small space

Examples

The family lived in a cramped apartment with barely enough space for their belongings.
He felt uncomfortable in the cramped elevator filled with people.
The office was so cramped that employees could hardly move around their desks.

Synset Examples

cramped quarters
trying to bring children up in cramped high-rise apartments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spacious - (of a space) large and roomy

Etymology

From Middle English 'crampe' (cramp), influenced by Old French and Latin 'crampus' (curved, bent).
