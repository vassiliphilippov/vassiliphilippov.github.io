snafu

1. ADJ

/ snæˈfu / (AmE), / snæˈfuː / (BrE)
in a state of complete confusion or chaos, often as a result of an error or mismanagement
in a state of confusion or chaos
informal

Examples

The project turned into a snafu when critical files were accidentally deleted.
Our travel plans became a snafu after the airline canceled our flights without warning.
Yesterday's meeting was a complete snafu, with overlapping agendas and technical problems.

Synset Examples

situation normal — all fucked-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a state of disorder or confusion
  • Synonym: fiasco - a complete failure, especially in a ludicrous or humiliating way

Etymology

Acronym from US military slang: 'Situation Normal, All Fouled Up' (originally using a vulgar term for 'fouled').

2. NOUN

/ snæˈfu / (AmE), / snæˈfuː / (BrE)
A chaotic or confused situation; a mess.
chaotic or confused situation
slang

Examples

After the new software update, the entire system became a snafu and no one could work.
There was a major snafu at the airport when the computers stopped working.
The team's meeting schedule turned into a snafu when no one showed up at the right time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a situation that is confused or full of difficulties
  • Synonym: fiasco - a complete failure, especially in a ludicrous or humiliating way
  • Degree Higher: catastrophe - an event causing great and often sudden damage or suffering; a disaster

Etymology

Acronym from 'Situation Normal, All Fucked Up', originated in US military slang during World War II.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076103-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ snæˈfu / (AmE), / snæˈfuː / (BrE)
to cause to be in a state of complete confusion or disarray, especially as a result of error or mismanagement
to cause confusion or chaos
slang informal american

Examples

The last-minute changes completely snafued the project schedule.
If we don’t communicate clearly, we could snafu the whole operation.
The delivery was snafuing our plans for the grand opening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddle - to bring into a disordered or confusing state
  • Antonym: to organize - to arrange or order systematically

Etymology

Originally US military slang, acronym for ‘Situation Normal: All Fouled Up’ (euphemistically; originally ‘Fucked Up’).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
