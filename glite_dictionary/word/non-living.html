non-living

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈlɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / nɒnˈlɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Not alive; not endowed with life.
not alive; without life

Examples

Stones and water are examples of non-living things in the environment.
Unlike plants or animals, a robot is considered non-living.
The science teacher asked the students to categorize objects as living or non-living.

Synset Examples

the inorganic world is inanimate
inanimate objects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inanimate - not alive, especially not in the manner of animals or humans

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'living', from Old English 'libban' (to live).
