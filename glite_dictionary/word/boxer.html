boxer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksər / (AmE), / ˈbɒksə / (BrE)
a workman whose job is to pack items into containers, especially boxes.
person who packs items into boxes

Examples

The boxer carefully packed the fragile goods into sturdy cardboard boxes.
A team of boxers worked late to get all the holiday orders shipped on time.
She found a summer job as a boxer at the local distribution center.

Etymology

From 'box' (container) + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksər / (AmE), / ˈbɒksə / (BrE)
a breed of medium-sized, strong, short-haired dog with a brindle or fawn coat and a square-jawed muzzle, originally developed in Germany
a breed of medium-sized, strong dog

Examples

My family adopted a young boxer from the animal shelter last year.
The boxer is known for its playful nature and boundless energy.
She takes her boxer for a long walk every morning before work.

Etymology

From German 'Boxer', referring to the dog's playful, boxing-like use of its forepaws.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02110741-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːksər / (AmE), / ˈbɒksə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in the sport of boxing, fighting with their fists according to set rules
person who competes in boxing

Examples

The boxer trained intensively for months before his championship bout.
She admired the discipline and determination of the young boxer she saw in the gym.
Several retired boxers attended the charity event to promote youth sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter - a person who fights, especially as a professional boxer or soldier

Etymology

From box (verb: to fight with fists) + -er (agent suffix), first used in the 17th century.
