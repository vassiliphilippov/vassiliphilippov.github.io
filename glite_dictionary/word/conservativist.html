conservativist

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.və.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
a person who is reluctant to accept changes and new ideas; someone adhering to conservative principles
person resistant to change or new ideas

Examples

As a self-described conservativist, he opposed most technological innovations at the company.
The group's leaders included both conservativists and progressives, leading to many heated debates.
Her conservativist stance made her unwilling to support the proposed curriculum changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • Antonym: progressive - a person advocating or implementing social reform or new, liberal ideas

Etymology

From 'conservative' + '-ist', indicating an adherent or advocate of conservatism.
