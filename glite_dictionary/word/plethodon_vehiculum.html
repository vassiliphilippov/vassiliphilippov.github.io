Plethodon vehiculum

1. NOUN

/ ˌplɛθəˈdɒn vəˈhɪkjələm / (AmE), / ˌpliːθəˈdɒn vɪˈhɪkjuːləm / (BrE)
A species of small salamander found along the Pacific coast of North America, distinguished by a narrow, light stripe along its back.
North American woodland salamander species

Examples

Researchers observed several Plethodon vehiculum under logs during the spring survey.
The Plethodon vehiculum is notable for its slender body and distinctive dorsal stripe.
Habitat loss has put the Plethodon vehiculum at risk in certain areas of its range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian typically resembling a lizard, belonging to the order Caudata

Etymology

From New Latin, Plethodon (genus) + vehiculum (Latin for 'vehicle', referring to the species name).
