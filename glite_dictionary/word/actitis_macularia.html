Actitis macularia

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtaɪ.tɪs mækjʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ækˈtɪ.tɪs mækjʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the spotted sandpiper, a common North American sandpiper species found near water bodies
scientific name of spotted sandpiper bird

Examples

The Actitis macularia is often seen bobbing its tail along riverbanks.
Researchers observed the migration patterns of Actitis macularia across the continent.
Many birdwatchers are excited to spot Actitis macularia during spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted sandpiper - a small North American sandpiper with spotted underparts

Etymology

From Greek aktitis (a shore-dweller) and Latin macularia (spotted).
