to iodize

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪ.əˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.daɪz / (BrE)
to treat or impregnate with iodine, especially to prevent infection
treat with iodine to prevent infection
medical

Examples

The nurse will iodize the wound to prevent infection.
Doctors often iodize cuts before stitching them up.
You should iodize your injury if you don't have other disinfectants available.

Synset Examples

iodize a wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean something to destroy bacteria

Etymology

From iodine + -ize, meaning 'to make or treat with iodine'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00079922-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪədaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or impregnate with iodine, especially to add iodine to (a substance such as salt)
to add iodine to something

Examples

Many countries iodize salt to prevent iodine deficiency in the population.
The company decided to iodize its products for better health benefits.
Government regulations require food producers to iodize table salt.

Synset Examples

iodize salt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to iodinate - to treat with iodine; synonym for iodize

Etymology

From iodine + -ize.
