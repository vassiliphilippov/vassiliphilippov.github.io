Oberon

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊ.bəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.bər.ɒn / (BrE)
(in medieval and Renaissance folklore) the king of the fairies and husband of Titania.
king of the fairies in folklore

Examples

In Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream', Oberon quarrels with Titania over a changeling boy.
The tale describes how Oberon used magic to influence the lives of mortals.
Among fairy kings in mythology, Oberon is perhaps the most renowned.

Etymology

From Old French 'Auberon', ultimately from Germanic origin.
