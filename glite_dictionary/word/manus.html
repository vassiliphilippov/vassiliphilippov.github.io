manus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːnəs / (BrE)
the anatomical term for the hand, especially the prehensile part of the upper limb in humans and other animals
anatomical term for the hand
medical

Examples

The skeleton revealed that the manus was well-developed, suggesting great dexterity.
Some primates have a highly flexible manus adapted for grasping branches.
In anatomy class, we studied the bones and muscles of the human manus.

Synset Examples

he had the hands of a surgeon
he extended his mitt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand - the end part of a person's arm beyond the wrist; the prehensile organ of the upper limb

Etymology

From Latin manus (hand).
