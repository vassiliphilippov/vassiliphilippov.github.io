Pisces

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪsiz / (AmE), / ˈpɪsiːz / (BrE)
A person born when the sun is in the astrological sign of Pisces, traditionally between February 19 and March 20.
person born under the sign of Pisces

Examples

As a Pisces, she is said to be compassionate and imaginative.
Many Pisces feel a deep connection to art and music.
My brother is a Pisces, so his birthday falls in March.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Aquarius - a person born under the sign of Aquarius
  • Degree Higher: Aries - a person born under the sign of Aries

Etymology

From Latin 'Pisces', meaning 'fishes', the name of a zodiac constellation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪsiːz / (AmE), / ˈpɪsiːz / (BrE)
a former zoological classification for the group of vertebrates that includes all fish, now rarely used in scientific contexts
former zoological group for fish

Examples

In older biology textbooks, Pisces is listed alongside other vertebrate classes.
The term Pisces is no longer widely used in modern taxonomy.
Some scientists once grouped all aquatic vertebrates under Pisces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish - aquatic, gill-bearing animals lacking limbs with digits

Etymology

From Latin 'piscis' (fish), used as a plural in taxonomy.
