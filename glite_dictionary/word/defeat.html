defeat

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfit / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
an instance of losing a battle, contest, or competition; failure to win or succeed
an act of losing; failure in competition

Examples

The team suffered a crushing defeat in the final match and missed out on the championship.
After their unexpected defeat, the players gathered to discuss what went wrong.
Despite the defeat, the candidate vowed to run for office again in the next election.

Synset Examples

it was a narrow defeat
the army's only defeat
they suffered a convincing licking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victory - an instance of winning or succeeding in a struggle or contest
  • Synonym: loss - an instance in which one does not win in a contest; the opposite of a win

Etymology

From Old French 'defaite' (undoing, overthrow), from Late Latin 'defecta'.

2. VERB

/ dəˈfit / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
to prevent the success or passage of a proposal, plan, or motion, especially by vote or argument
prevent passage of a proposal or plan

Examples

The committee voted to defeat the controversial bill.
Parliament managed to defeat the amendment after lengthy debate.
The city council will likely defeat the proposal if enough members oppose it.

Synset Examples

kill a motion
he shot down the student's proposal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to officially accept or allow a proposal or plan

Etymology

From Old French *desfeter* (to undo, destroy), from Latin *dis-* (apart) + *facere* (to do).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfit / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
a sense of frustration or discouragement experienced when one fails to achieve a goal or is prevented from succeeding
feeling of frustration after failure

Examples

After losing the final match, a crushing sense of defeat settled over the team.
He struggled to hide his defeat when he failed the entrance exam again.
The politician’s speech revealed a rare moment of defeat and vulnerability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustration - the feeling of being upset or annoyed as a result of being unable to change or achieve something
  • Antonym: victory - the overcoming of an enemy or antagonist; success

Etymology

From Old French *defaite* (overthrow, alteration), from Latin *deficere* (to fail, to be lacking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07556254-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ dɪˈfit / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
to win a victory over someone or something in a contest, conflict, or competition; to overcome
to win a victory over

Examples

The team managed to defeat its rivals in the final match.
It took her years to defeat her fear of public speaking.
He hopes to defeat the defending champion in tomorrow’s race.

Synset Examples

You must overcome all difficulties
defeat your enemies
He overcame his shyness
He overcame his infirmity
Her anger got the better of her and she blew up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with or controlling (a problem or difficulty)
  • Synonym: to conquer - to take control of (a place or people) by military force
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting to an opponent or authority
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated; fail to win

Etymology

From Old French *defaire* (to undo, destroy), from Latin *disfacere* (to undo, unmake).
