Harrisia

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɪsiə / (AmE), / həˈrɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of slender, often treelike spiny cacti with solitary, showy nocturnal white or pink flowers, native to Florida, the Caribbean, and South America.
genus of spiny cacti with nocturnal flowers

Examples

Harrisia species are cultivated for their striking nocturnal flowers and unusual forms.
Researchers discovered a new species of Harrisia in a remote part of South America.
Some Harrisia cacti can reach the size of small trees in their natural habitat.

Etymology

Named after William Harris (1860–1920), Jamaican botanist.
