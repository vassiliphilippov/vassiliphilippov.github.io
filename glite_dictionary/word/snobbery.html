snobbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɑb.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsnɒb.ər.i / (BrE)
the behavior or attitude of people who think they are better than others, especially because of social class or wealth
condescending attitude based on social class

Examples

Her snobbery was obvious when she refused to eat at any restaurant without a Michelin star.
The film satirizes the snobbery of the upper classes in early 20th-century England.
His snobbery made it difficult for him to relate to people from different backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elitism - belief that a society or system should be led by an elite
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance

Etymology

From snob + -ery, first recorded in English in the early 19th century.
