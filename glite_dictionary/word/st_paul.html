St. Paul

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt pɔl / (AmE), / seɪnt pɔːl / (BrE)
The capital city of Minnesota, located in southeastern Minnesota on the Mississippi River, and one of the Twin Cities alongside Minneapolis.
capital city of Minnesota, USA

Examples

The headquarters of several major companies are located in St. Paul.
I traveled from Chicago to St. Paul by train last summer.
Minneapolis and St. Paul are known as the Twin Cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minneapolis - largest city of Minnesota, adjacent to St. Paul

Etymology

Named after Saint Paul the Apostle, a key figure in early Christianity.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt pɔl / (AmE), / seɪnt pɔːl / (BrE)
A Christian missionary and apostle, originally known as Saul, who played a major role in spreading Christianity and authored several New Testament Epistles.
Christian missionary and apostle

Examples

St. Paul traveled extensively to spread the teachings of Christianity across the Roman Empire.
Many of the Epistles in the New Testament are attributed to St. Paul.
Before converting, St. Paul was known as Saul and persecuted early Christians.

Synset Examples

Paul's name was Saul prior to his conversion to Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saul of Tarsus - Original name of St. Paul before converting to Christianity
  • Synonym: apostle - One of the primary disciples of Jesus in Christianity
