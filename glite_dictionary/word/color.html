color

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
the visual quality of objects or light sources as determined by the way they reflect or emit light, perceived in terms of hue, lightness, and saturation
visual property relating to hue and light

Examples

The vibrant color of the painting caught everyone's attention.
Sunlight changes the color of leaves throughout the year.
She chose a dress in her favorite color for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hue - a particular shade or tint of a color
  • Synonym: shade - a degree or variation of a color
  • Antonym: colorless - without color; lacking hue or brightness

Etymology

From Latin 'color', meaning hue or tint.
