shelver

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlvər / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to put items, such as books, onto shelves
person who shelves items

Examples

The library shelver carefully organized the returned books onto the correct stacks.
As a student, he worked part-time as a shelver at the local bookstore.
The shelvers met every morning to receive instructions from the library supervisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shelve - to put (something) on a shelf

Etymology

Formed from 'shelve' (to put on a shelf) with agent noun suffix '-er'.
