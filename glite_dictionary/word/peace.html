peace

1. NOUN

/ pis / (AmE), / piːs / (BrE)
A state of calm and tranquility in the mind, free from worry or anxiety.
absence of mental stress or anxiety

Examples

After weeks of stress, she finally found peace in meditation.
Listening to the sound of the ocean brought him a deep peace.
He closed his eyes, seeking peace before his big presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - freedom from disturbance or agitation
  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease

Etymology

From Old French 'pais', from Latin 'pax' meaning 'peace, compact, agreement'.

3. NOUN

/ pis / (AmE), / piːs / (BrE)
A state of harmonious relations and freedom from conflict or disputes between individuals or groups.
harmonious relations; absence of conflict

Examples

The two countries finally achieved peace after years of negotiation and hostility.
She values peace in her home and avoids unnecessary arguments with family members.
The new policy helped restore peace among the employees who had been disputing for months.

Synset Examples

the roommates lived in peace together

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony
  • Synonym: harmony - agreement in feeling or opinion

Etymology

From Old French 'pais', from Latin 'pax, pacem' (peace, compact, agreement, treaty of peace, tranquility, absence of war).

4. NOUN

/ pis / (AmE), / piːs / (BrE)
A situation or period in which there is no war or fighting between countries or groups.
freedom from war; absence of conflict

Examples

After decades of conflict, the two nations finally achieved peace.
The treaty brought peace to the war-torn region for the first time in years.
People celebrated in the streets at the announcement of peace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: war - armed conflict between countries or groups
  • Synonym: truce - an agreement to stop fighting temporarily

Etymology

From Latin pax (peace), via Old French pais and Anglo-Norman pes, pais.
