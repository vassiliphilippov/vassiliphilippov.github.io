Juniperus silicicola

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs sɪˌlɪsɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs sɪˌlɪsɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A species of juniper native to swampy coastal regions of the southeastern United States, closely related to the eastern red cedar.
juniper species of U.S. coastal swamps

Examples

The dense foliage of Juniperus silicicola provides important shelter for coastal wildlife.
Botanists surveyed the spread of Juniperus silicicola in the marshlands of Florida.
Many gardens in the southeastern U.S. feature Juniperus silicicola for its ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern red cedar - a closely related species of juniper, Juniperus virginiana

Etymology

From Latin: 'juniperus' meaning 'juniper', and 'silicicola' meaning 'dweller on silica (sand)'.
