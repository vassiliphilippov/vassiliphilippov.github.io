quadruped

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːdrəˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdrʊˌpɛd / (BrE)
an animal, especially a mammal, that has four legs used for walking
four-legged animal

Examples

The horse is a classic example of a quadruped.
Paleontologists discovered ancient quadruped fossils in the desert.
Many mammals, such as dogs and cats, are quadrupeds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biped - an animal with two legs used for walking

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrupes', from 'quattuor' (four) + 'pes' (foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑː.drəˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.drʊˌpɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having four feet or legs.
having four feet or legs

Examples

The horse is a classic example of a quadruped mammal.
Ancient reptiles included both biped and quadruped forms.
Most mammals in the savanna are quadruped creatures, adapted for running.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biped - an animal having two feet

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrupes', from 'quattuor' (four) + 'pes' (foot).
