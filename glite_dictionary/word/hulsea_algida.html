Hulsea algida

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌlsi əˈlɡɪdə / (AmE), / ˈhʌlsi ælˈɡɪdə / (BrE)
A low, tufted plant native to the northwestern United States, with hairy stems each topped by a flower head with short, narrow yellow rays.
a plant with yellow-rayed flower heads, native to northwest US

Examples

The high mountain slopes were scattered with clusters of Hulsea algida in full bloom.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Hulsea algida across different elevations.
Hikers admired the distinctive hairy stems and bright yellow flowers of the Hulsea algida along the trail.

Etymology

From the genus Hulsea; species name from Latin 'algida' meaning 'cold' or 'chilly', referring to its alpine habitat.
