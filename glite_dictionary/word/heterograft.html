heterograft

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬ərəˌɡræft / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəˌɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a tissue graft from an animal of one species to an individual of another species, used especially as a temporary treatment for severe burns
graft of tissue between species
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a heterograft to temporarily cover the patient's extensive burn wounds.
Research on heterograft rejection has advanced our understanding of immune responses.
A heterograft may help protect the injured area while the patient's own skin heals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xenograft - a tissue graft from a donor of a different species; often used synonymously with 'heterograft'

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + graft (transplant).
