Chunga

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃuŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtʃuŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Cariamidae, found in South America and known for the species Chunga burmeisteri, commonly called the black-legged seriema.
genus of South American birds (Cariamidae)

Examples

The only extant species of the genus Chunga is the black-legged seriema.
Ornithologists have long studied the habits of Chunga in its native habitat.
The distribution of Chunga is limited to parts of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cariamidae - the bird family that includes seriemas
