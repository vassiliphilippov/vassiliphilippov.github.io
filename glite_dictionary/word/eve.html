eve

1. NOUN

/ iv / (AmE), / iːv / (BrE)
The day or period of time immediately before an event, especially a festival or significant occasion.
day or time before an event

Examples

Many families gather together on Christmas eve for a special dinner.
The company announced its decision on the eve of the big merger.
Protests erupted on the eve of the government election.

Synset Examples

he always arrives on the eve of her departure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening - the period of the day between late afternoon and night

Etymology

From Old English ǣfen (evening), later influenced by the sense of 'the time before an event'.

3. NOUN

/ iv / (AmE), / iːv / (BrE)
the latter part of the day, from late afternoon until nightfall; evening or twilight
latter part of the day

Examples

We sat by the fireplace and talked quietly throughout the eve.
The golden hues of the eve painted the sky with breathtaking colors.
During the cool eve, the garden came alive with the sounds of crickets.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the evening light across the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening - the period between late afternoon and night
  • Degree Higher: nightfall - the time when night begins; dusk

Etymology

From Old English ǣfen (evening), related to Old High German abend.
