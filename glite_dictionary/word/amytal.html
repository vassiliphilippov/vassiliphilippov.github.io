Amytal

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪˌtɑl / (AmE), / ˈæmɪtɒl / (BrE)
A brand name for the sodium salt of amobarbital, used as a sedative and hypnotic barbiturate.
barbiturate sedative-hypnotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Amytal to help the patient with severe insomnia.
Due to its addictive potential, Amytal is only used under strict medical supervision.
Researchers studied the effects of Amytal on anxiety before surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amobarbital - a barbiturate drug used as a sedative and hypnotic
