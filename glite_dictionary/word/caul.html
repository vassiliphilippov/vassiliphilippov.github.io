caul

1. NOUN

/ kɔl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
a thin membrane, especially the part of the peritoneum that attaches to the stomach and colon and covers the intestines
membrane covering intestines
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully lifted the caul to access the underlying organs.
In rare cases, a baby may be born with a caul covering its head or face.
Damage to the caul can lead to complications in abdominal surgery.

Etymology

From Middle English calle, probably from Old French cale (cap, hood).

2. NOUN

/ kɔl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
a thin, inner membrane covering the head of a newborn at birth, especially when still partly enclosed in the amniotic sac
membrane covering newborn's head at birth

Examples

The baby was born with a caul covering his head, which the doctor carefully removed.
Some cultures consider a caul at birth to be a sign of good luck.
The midwife explained that a caul is a rare but harmless birth occurrence.

Etymology

From Middle English calle, probably from Old Northern French cale (cap, hood).
