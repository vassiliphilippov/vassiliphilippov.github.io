vulcaniser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlkənaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who vulcanizes rubber to improve its strength and elasticity
person who vulcanizes rubber

Examples

The experienced vulcaniser repaired dozens of tires every week at the factory.
After years as a vulcaniser, he became an expert in rubber technology.
Every automobile manufacturer employs at least one skilled vulcaniser on site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulcanizer - US spelling: a person who vulcanizes rubber

Etymology

From 'vulcanize', itself from Latin Vulcanus, the Roman god of fire, after whom the process was named.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪzə / (BrE)
a machine or device used to treat rubber with heat and sulfur to improve its strength and elasticity
device for vulcanizing rubber

Examples

The factory installed a new vulcaniser to increase its rubber production capacity.
After maintenance, the vulcaniser operated more efficiently and produced stronger tires.
Rubber sheets are placed inside the vulcaniser for several hours during the process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulcanization - the chemical process of treating rubber with sulfur and heat

Etymology

From 'vulcanize', after Vulcan, the Roman god of fire.
