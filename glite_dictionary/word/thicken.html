to thicken

1. VERB

/ ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to become thick or thicker in consistency, texture, or density
become thick or thicker

Examples

As the stew simmered, it began to thicken and fill the kitchen with a delicious aroma.
Cornstarch is often used to help soups thicken without altering the flavor.
The fog started to thicken as night approached, making it difficult to see the road.

Synset Examples

The sauce thickened
The egg yolk will inspissate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thin - to become thin or thinner

Etymology

From Middle English thiknen, from Old English thiccnian, from thicce (thick).

2. VERB

/ ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to make or become thick or thicker; to cause a substance or mixture to become less fluid or more dense
make or become thicker

Examples

Simmer the soup until it thickens to your desired consistency.
Cornstarch can be used to thicken a sauce quickly.
The artist let the paint thicken on the canvas to create texture.

Synset Examples

Thicken the sauce
inspissate the tar so that it becomes pitch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thin - to make or become less thick or dense
  • Antonym: dilute - to make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • Synonym: to congeal - to change from a fluid to a thickened or solid state

Etymology

From Middle English thiknen, from Old English thiccan (to thicken), from thicce (thick).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00432280-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to make or become more dense, viscous, or less transparent
make or become denser or more viscous

Examples

Add cornstarch to thicken the soup if it is too watery.
As the paint dries, it will gradually thicken and become more difficult to spread.
The fog began to thicken, making it impossible to see the road ahead.

Synset Examples

thicken the sauce by adding flour

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thin - to make or become less thick or dense

Etymology

From 'thick' + -en, where '-en' is a verb-forming suffix of Old English origin.

Source: WordNet
