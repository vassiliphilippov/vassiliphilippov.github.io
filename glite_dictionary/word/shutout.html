shutout

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt.aʊt / (BrE)
A contest, especially in sports, in which one side prevents the other from scoring any points or goals.
game where opponent scores nothing

Examples

The pitcher led his team to a shutout by not allowing a single run.
It was an embarrassing shutout for the home team, who failed to score.
Last night's hockey game ended in a 3-0 shutout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean sheet - (British) a game where a team concedes no goals, primarily soccer/football

Etymology

Formed from 'shut' + 'out', reflecting the idea of keeping the opposing team completely out of scoring.
