extraordinary

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstrɔːrdəˌneri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrɔːdnri / (BrE)
very unusual, remarkable, or much greater than what is normal or expected
very unusual or remarkable

Examples

She showed extraordinary courage during the rescue.
The chef prepared an extraordinary meal that impressed all the guests.
Winning three gold medals in one day is an extraordinary achievement.

Synset Examples

an extraordinary desire for approval
it was an over-the-top experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of attention; striking
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin 'extraordinarius', from 'extra' (outside) + 'ordinarius' (usual, regular).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstrɔrdiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrɔːdənəri / (BrE)
(of an official) serving a special or unusual function in addition to those of regular officials, often appearing in official titles.
holding a special additional official function

Examples

The president appointed an ambassador extraordinary to represent him at the summit.
He was named minister extraordinary with special powers during the crisis.
As an envoy extraordinary, she had authority beyond that of a regular diplomat.

Synset Examples

an ambassador extraordinary

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinary - normal; not having special rank or function

Etymology

From Latin extraordinarius (out of the usual order), from extra (outside) + ordinarius (regular).

very unusual or remarkable

Examples

Related Terms
