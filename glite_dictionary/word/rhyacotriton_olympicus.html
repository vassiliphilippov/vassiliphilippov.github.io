Rhyacotriton olympicus

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌækəˈtraɪtɑn əˈlɪmpɪkəs / (AmE), / raɪˌakəˈtrɪtən ɒˈlɪmpɪkəs / (BrE)
A small, large-eyed semiaquatic salamander native to the northwestern United States, commonly known as the Olympic torrent salamander.
species of small semiaquatic salamander

Examples

Scientists recently discovered several Rhyacotriton olympicus in an isolated stream in Washington.
The unique coloration of the Rhyacotriton olympicus helps it blend with mossy rocks.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the Rhyacotriton olympicus from pollution.

Etymology

From Greek *rhyax* (torrent) + *triton* (newt) and Latin *olympicus* (of Olympus), referring to its habitat in the Olympic Peninsula.
