eternally

1. ADV

/ ɪˈtɝːnəli / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəli / (BrE)
For all time; without end or limitation.
without end; forever

Examples

He promised to love her eternally, no matter what happened.
Some believe the soul lives on eternally after death.
The stars in the night sky seem to shine eternally above us.

Synset Examples

no one can live forever
brightly beams our Father's mercy from his lighthouse evermore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all time; without end
  • Antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin 'aeternalis', from 'aeternus' (eternal).
