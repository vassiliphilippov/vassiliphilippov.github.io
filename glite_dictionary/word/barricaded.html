barricaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbærəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbærɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
blocked off with a barrier or obstacle, preventing entry or exit
blocked by a barrier

Examples

The police found the suspects in a barricaded house and negotiated for hours.
During the protest, activists barricaded the entrance to stop vehicles from passing.
All roads leading to the palace were barricaded for security reasons.

Synset Examples

a barricaded street
barred doors
the blockaded harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barred - fitted with or closed by bars, preventing movement
  • Synonym: blockaded - blocked off, especially to prevent access or communication

Etymology

From French 'barricader', from 'barrique' (barrel).
