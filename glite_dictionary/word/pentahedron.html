pentahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛntəˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure with five flat faces.
solid figure with five faces

Examples

The pyramid shown is a type of pentahedron because it has five faces.
In geometry class, we compared the properties of a cube to those of a pentahedron.
A pentahedron can have different shapes, such as a square pyramid or a triangular prism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tetrahedron - a solid with four plane faces
  • Degree Higher: hexahedron - a solid with six plane faces (e.g., a cube)

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) + 'hedron' (face or base).
