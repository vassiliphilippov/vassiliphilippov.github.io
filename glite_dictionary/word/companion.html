companion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpæn.jən / (BrE)
a person, often paid, whose job is to accompany, assist, or live with another, especially for company or care
a paid person who accompanies another

Examples

After her surgery, an elderly companion helped Mrs. Green with daily tasks and errands.
The wealthy businessman hired a companion to travel with him on his long trips.
Some families employ a live-in companion to provide company and care for their loved ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aide - a person who assists someone
  • Synonym: attendant - a person who assists or serves another

Etymology

From Old French compagnon, based on Latin companionem, from com- (with) + panis (bread).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpanjən / (BrE)
a person who spends a lot of time with another as a friend or associate
close friend or associate

Examples

She was always seen with her loyal companion by her side.
He became my trusted companion during our long journeys.
Many people seek a companion to share their experiences and joys.

Synset Examples

drinking companions
comrades in arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - someone you know well and regard with affection and trust

Etymology

From Old French compagnon, from Late Latin companio (one who eats bread with another), from Latin com- (together) + panis (bread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09965384-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpanjən / (BrE)
to accompany or serve as a companion to someone; to be with someone, especially as a friend or associate.
to accompany as a companion

Examples

She companioned her grandmother on the long journey back to their homeland.
During his illness, friends and neighbors companion him so that he never felt lonely.
He companioned the writer during her travels through Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone as a companion or escort

Etymology

From Latin 'companionem' (one who eats bread with another), from 'com-' (with) + 'panis' (bread).

4. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpanjən / (BrE)
a person who travels with you or accompanies you during a journey
traveling partner or fellow traveler

Examples

He invited his best friend to be his companion on the cross-country road trip.
Each hiker chose a companion for the trek up the mountain.
As her loyal companion, he made the journey much more enjoyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - person who shares an activity or journey with you
  • Antonym: solo traveler - person who travels alone without companions

Etymology

From Old French compagnon, from Late Latin companionem (messmate), from Latin com- (with) + panis (bread).
