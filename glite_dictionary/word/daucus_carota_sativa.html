Daucus carota sativa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊkəs kəˈroʊtə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ˈdaʊkəs kəˈrəʊtə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
the cultivated variety of the carrot, a perennial plant grown as an annual for its edible orange root
cultivated carrot variety

Examples

Daucus carota sativa is widely grown for its sweet, crunchy roots.
The bright orange color of Daucus carota sativa makes it easy to spot in vegetable gardens.
Farmers select specific varieties of Daucus carota sativa for different climates and soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable, Daucus carota sativa

Etymology

From Latin: 'Daucus' (the carrot genus), 'carota' (carrot), 'sativa' (cultivated).
