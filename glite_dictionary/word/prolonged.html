prolonged

1. ADJ

/ pɚˈlɔŋd / (AmE), / prəˈlɒŋd / (BrE)
made longer in space; extended or elongated in length
made longer in space; extended

Examples

The artist drew a prolonged shadow that stretched across the entire canvas.
The new bridges featured prolonged spans to cross the wide river.
A prolonged beam of light shone through the narrow window.

Synset Examples

Picasso's elongated Don Quixote
lengthened skirts are fashionable this year
the extended airport runways can accommodate larger planes
a prolonged black line across the page

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elongated - made longer; stretched out in length
  • Antonym: shortened - made shorter; reduced in length

Etymology

From Latin 'prolongare', from 'pro-' (forth) + 'longus' (long).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈlɔŋd / (AmE), / prəˈlɒŋd / (BrE)
Continuing for a longer time than usual; lasting longer than expected or desired.
lasting longer than usual

Examples

After a prolonged silence, she finally answered his question.
The city has suffered from a prolonged drought this year.
Their prolonged absence raised concerns among friends and neighbors.

Synset Examples

a drawn-out argument
an extended discussion
a lengthy visit from her mother-in-law
a prolonged and bitter struggle
protracted negotiations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short - lasting or taking a small amount of time
  • Synonym: lengthy - continuing for a long time or longer than usual
  • Synonym: protracted - lasting for a long time or longer than expected

Etymology

From Latin 'prolongare', from 'pro-' (forward) + 'longus' (long).
