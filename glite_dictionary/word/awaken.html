to awaken

1. VERB

/ əˈweɪkən / (AmE), / əˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop sleeping or to become conscious or aware of something
cause to wake up or become conscious

Examples

The loud thunder awakened him in the middle of the night.
She tried to awaken her friend for the early morning hike.
It took several attempts to awaken the sleeping child.

Synset Examples

He was roused by the drunken men in the street
Please wake me at 6 AM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - to stop sleeping; become awake
  • Synonym: to arouse - to cause someone to become awake or alert

Etymology

From Middle English 'awaken', from Old English 'awæcnan', meaning 'to arise, to wake up'.

2. VERB

/ əˈweɪkən / (AmE), / əˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to make someone realize or become aware of something, especially for the first time
make someone realize or become aware

Examples

Traveling abroad can often awaken people to different cultures and perspectives.
The disaster awakened the public to the urgent need for better preparation.
Her story awakens a sense of responsibility in those who hear it.

Synset Examples

They were awakened to the sad facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding; make aware
  • Synonym: alert - to make someone aware of a danger or problem; warn

Etymology

From Old English 'awæcnian', meaning 'to arise, to wake up'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00738100-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈweɪkən / (AmE), / əˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to stop sleeping; to wake up or cause to wake up
stop sleeping or wake up

Examples

She managed to awaken just before her alarm went off.
The children awakened early on Christmas morning, full of excitement.
Loud noises outside awakened him in the middle of the night.

Synset Examples

She woke up to the sound of the alarm clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - to stop sleeping or make someone stop sleeping
  • Antonym: sleep - to be in a state of rest in which the body is not active and the mind is unconscious

Etymology

From Old English 'awæcnan', meaning 'to arise, to wake up', based on the verb 'wacan' (to wake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018519-v
Synset POS: v
