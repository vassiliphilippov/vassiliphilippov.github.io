lycanthropy

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkænθɹəpi / (AmE), / laɪˈkænθrəpi / (BrE)
The supposed magical ability or condition of transforming from a human into a wolf, as in folklore or mythology.
ability to turn into a wolf (folklore)

Examples

Ancient legends often describe lycanthropy as a curse imposed upon those who break sacred oaths.
The novel explores the psychological aspects of lycanthropy and its impact on the protagonist's identity.
Reports of lycanthropy were common during medieval times, explaining mysterious animal attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: werewolf - a human who turns into a wolf, either voluntarily or by a curse

Etymology

From Greek lykos (wolf) + anthrōpos (man).
