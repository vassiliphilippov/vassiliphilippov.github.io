2. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
A person who belongs to the sex that does not bear children or produce eggs, typically having XY chromosomes and male reproductive organs.
a person of the sex that cannot bear children

Examples

The male was taller than anyone else in the group.
In many species, the male plays a different role in parenting than the female.
As a male, he was not able to experience pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: female - a person who belongs to the sex that can have babies

Etymology

From Latin 'mas' (male), later from 'masculus' (male, masculine).

Synset POS: n

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
of or denoting the sex that produces gametes (such as sperm or pollen) which can fertilize the female's egg or ovule.

Examples

The male lion is distinguished by its impressive mane.

In some bird species, only the male has brightly colored feathers.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
an animal, especially of a species with two sexes, that produces sperm cells to fertilize female eggs
an animal that produces sperm

Examples

The lion male typically leads the pride and protects its territory.
In these birds, the male displays bright plumage to attract a mate.
The veterinarian determined that the puppy was a male after a quick examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: female - an animal that produces eggs (ova) that can be fertilized by male gametes

Etymology

From Latin 'mas' (male, masculine), influenced by Old French 'masle', later altered to 'male'.
