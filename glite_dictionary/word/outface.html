to outface

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈfeɪs / (AmE), / aʊtˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to overcome or intimidate someone by staring them down or displaying bold confidence
intimidate by staring down

Examples

She managed to outface her accuser during the tense courtroom exchange.
No matter how angry he got, she would always try to outface him with a calm, steady gaze.
The rookie hoped to outface more experienced players with her unwavering confidence.

Synset Examples

He simply stared down his opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stare down - to defeat or subdue someone by staring at them steadily

Etymology

From out- (meaning 'beyond') + face (as a verb), first recorded in 16th century.
