parsnip

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.snɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.snɪp / (BrE)
A long, tapering whitish root vegetable of the parsley family, with a sweet flavor, eaten cooked.
edible whitish root vegetable

Examples

She added chopped parsnip to the soup for extra sweetness.
Roasted parsnip makes a delicious and healthy side dish.
He grew several rows of parsnip in his vegetable garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable similar to a parsnip

Etymology

From Middle English 'persnepe', from Old French, from Latin 'pastinaca' (parsnip, carrot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.snɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.snɪp / (BrE)
a long, pale yellow root vegetable of a plant of the parsley family, cultivated for its edible root
edible root vegetable

Examples

I roasted carrots and parsnips for dinner last night.
The parsnip is sweeter after the first frost of winter.
She added chopped parsnip to the soup for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable related to the parsnip

Etymology

From Middle English 'parsnip', from Old French 'pasnaie', from Latin 'pastinaca' (parsnip).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.snɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.snɪp / (BrE)
a long, whitish root vegetable of the parsley family, eaten as food
edible whitish root vegetable

Examples

Roasted parsnip is a delicious addition to any winter meal.
The soup was made from onions, potatoes, and parsnip.
He harvested a large parsnip from his vegetable garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable, closely related to parsnip

Etymology

From Middle English 'pasnepe', partly based on Old French 'pasnaie' and influenced by 'turnip'.
