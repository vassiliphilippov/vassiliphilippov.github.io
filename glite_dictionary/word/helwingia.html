Helwingia

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈwɪndʒiə / (AmE), / hɛlˈwɪndʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous dioecious shrubs native to woodland thickets in low mountains of eastern Asia, particularly Japan.
genus of Asian deciduous shrubs

Examples

The Helwingia is notable for its unusual practice of bearing flowers on the surfaces of its leaves.
Many rare species of Helwingia can be found in Japanese mountain woodlands.
Gardeners value Helwingia for its resilience and attractive foliage.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Georg Andreas Helwing (1666–1748).
