spininess

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being covered with prickly thorns or spines.
being covered with thorns or spines

Examples

The spininess of the cactus protected it from hungry animals in the desert.
Its spininess makes the hedgehog difficult for predators to handle.
The spininess of the plant caught on her clothing as she walked by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with spines or thorns

Etymology

From 'spiny' + '-ness', where 'spiny' comes from Latin 'spina' (thorn).
