Jotun

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊ.tʊn / (AmE), / ˈjəʊ.tʊn / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) one of a race of giants who were often in conflict with the gods (the Aesir).
giant from Norse mythology

Examples

According to legend, the Jotun were constantly plotting against the gods of Asgard.
In some stories, a Jotun might even aid the Norse gods in their quests.
The mighty Jotun towered over the humans, casting a shadow across the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a very large, supernatural humanlike being in mythology
  • Antonym: Aesir - the principal gods of Norse mythology, often opposed to the Jotun

Etymology

From Old Norse jǫtunn (giant).
