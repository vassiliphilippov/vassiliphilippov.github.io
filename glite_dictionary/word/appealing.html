appealing

1. ADJ

/ əˈpiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive or interesting; able to arouse interest or favorable attention
attractive or interesting

Examples

The new design is much more appealing to younger customers.
Her friendly smile made her instantly appealing to everyone she met.
I find the idea of working from home very appealing these days.

Synset Examples

He added an appealing and memorable figure to popular American mythology
an appealing sense of humor
the idea of having enough money to retire at fifty is very appealing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From the verb 'appeal' + -ing, meaning 'attractive to the mind or senses'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈpilɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a character in literature or drama) evoking sympathetic or empathic feelings; capable of inspiring empathy or affection.
evoking sympathy or empathy (esp. of a character)

Examples

The author created an appealing protagonist who quickly won over the readers' hearts.
Her appealing nature made it easy for the audience to empathize with her character's struggles.
The film's success was largely due to its appealing cast of characters.

Synset Examples

the sympathetic characters in the play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathetic - (of a character) evoking sympathy or compassion
  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or pleasant; not likely to evoke empathy or sympathy

Etymology

From the verb 'appeal', ultimately from Latin 'appellare' (to address, appeal to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02384845-a
Synset POS: a
