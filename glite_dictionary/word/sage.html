sage

1. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Salvia, especially Salvia officinalis, with grayish-green aromatic leaves used in cooking and herbal medicine
aromatic herb of the genus Salvia

Examples

She sprinkled sage over the roasted chicken to add flavor.
The sage in the garden attracts bees and butterflies.
Many traditional remedies use dried sage leaves for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savory - an aromatic plant used in cooking, related culinary herb

Etymology

From Old French saige, from Latin salvia, from salvus (healthy).

2. ADJ

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
having or showing deep wisdom, especially as a result of age and experience
wise from age and experience

Examples

The professor offered sage advice to the graduating students.
Her sage comments helped us avoid a costly mistake.
Many people sought the sage counselor’s guidance during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing knowledge, experience, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Old French sage (wise), from Latin sapius (wise, discerning).

3. ADJ

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
of a grey-green color, like that of sage leaves
grey-green color (like sage leaves)

Examples

The living room was painted a soft sage to create a calming atmosphere.
She wore a jacket in a muted sage that complemented her eyes.
I chose sage towels for the bathroom because they add a touch of nature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: olive - a yellowish-green color
  • Degree Higher: forest green - a darker green color

Etymology

From the color of sage leaves; ultimately from Latin 'salvia' (sage plant).

4. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is deeply wise, especially in spiritual or philosophical matters, and who is respected for their guidance
a wise and respected spiritual or philosophical mentor

Examples

The old sage was sought out by villagers for advice on matters of life and death.
Throughout history, many have looked to a sage for guidance during uncertain times.
He spent several months learning from a sage renowned for his philosophical teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise man - a man who is wise and can give sound advice
  • Antonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or wisdom

Etymology

From Old French sage (wise), from Latin sagus (prophetic, wise).

5. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with grey-green leaves used as a seasoning in cooking, especially with meats and poultry
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled chopped sage over the roasted chicken for added flavor.
Dried sage is often used in stuffing recipes during the holidays.
The aroma of fresh sage filled the kitchen as dinner was prepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosemary - an aromatic herb used in cooking
  • Synonym: thyme - an aromatic herb used for seasoning

Etymology

From Old French 'sauge', from Latin 'salvia'.
