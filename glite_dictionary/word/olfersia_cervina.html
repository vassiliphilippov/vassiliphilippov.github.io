Olfersia cervina

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈfɜːrsiə sɜrˈviːnə / (AmE), / ɒlˈfɜːsɪə sɜːˈviːnə / (BrE)
a tropical American fern with leathery lance-shaped fronds, sometimes classified in the genus Polybotrya
species of tropical American fern

Examples

The leathery fronds of Olfersia cervina make it a distinctive species in tropical forests.
Botanists sometimes classify Olfersia cervina within the genus Polybotrya due to similarities in structure.
Gardeners in Central America often cultivate Olfersia cervina for its ornamental foliage.

Etymology

Genus named after Ignaz von Olfers, a German naturalist; 'cervina' from Latin meaning 'of a deer'.
