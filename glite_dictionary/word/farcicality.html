farcicality

1. NOUN

/ fɑːrˈsɪkəlɪti / (AmE), / fɑːˈsɪkəlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being ludicrous, absurd, or resembling a farce.
the quality of being like a farce; absurdity

Examples

The farcicality of the entire situation left everyone in the room laughing uncontrollably.
Critics commented on the farcicality of the play, describing it as both hilarious and thought-provoking.
She was frustrated by the farcicality of the bureaucratic procedures she had to endure.

Synset Examples

Like all of my designs, it's a glorified warning, an ode to the farcicality of the fashion industry and the obsessives that surround it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurdity - the quality of being ridiculous or wildly unreasonable
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being solemn or earnest

Etymology

From farcical + -ity, modeled on the French 'farcicalité'.
