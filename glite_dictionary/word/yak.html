yak

1. NOUN

/ jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
loud or persistent talk, especially idle or trivial chatter
noisy or idle talk
informal

Examples

The meeting was delayed by all the yak in the hallway.
After an hour of pointless yak, we finally got to the main topic.
She was tired of his constant yak about celebrities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - talk rapidly or incessantly about trivial matters
  • Synonym: babble - talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative of chatter or derived from the Tibetan animal's name, metaphorically applied to idle talk.

2. VERB

/ jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
to talk incessantly and sometimes trivial matters, often in an informal or trivial way
talk a lot, often about trivial things
informal

Examples

He tends to yak on the phone for hours every evening.
We sat around the coffee table yakking about old times.
She was still yaking when everyone else had finished eating.

Synset Examples

she was yakking away about her grandchildren

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously, usually about trivial matters
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length about insignificant things
  • Antonym: to be quiet - to not speak, to remain silent
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00743542-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
a large, long-haired wild or domesticated ox native to Tibet and central Asia, valued for its milk, meat, and wool
large long-haired ox of Tibet

Examples

The farmer raised several yaks for their milk and warm wool.
Wild yaks roam the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.
Tourists were amazed to see a domesticated yak carrying supplies up the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ox - domesticated bovine animal, often used for draft work

Etymology

From Tibetan 'g.yag' (male yak).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
