interstice

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜːrstɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːstɪs / (BrE)
a small space or gap between things, especially between tissues or parts of an organ
small gap or space between things

Examples

The tiny interstices between the bricks allowed the wind to pass through.
Fluid can pass through the interstices of the cell membrane.
Dust accumulates in the interstices of the woven fabric.

Synset Examples

the interstices of a network

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an opening or space between things

Etymology

From Latin interstitium (interval, intervening space), from inter- (between) + sistere (to stand).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜːrstəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːstɪs / (BrE)
A small, narrow space or gap between things or parts.
small gap between things

Examples

Dust collected in the interstices of the old wooden floorboards.
Tiny plants grew in the interstices between the rocks.
Light filtered through the interstices of the woven basket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an opening or break in something
  • Synonym: space - an area or expanse that is free, available, or unoccupied

Etymology

From Latin 'interstitium' (interval, gap), from 'inter-' (between) and 'sistere' (to stand).
