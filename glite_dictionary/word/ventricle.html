ventricle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
one of the four connected cavities in the brain that contain cerebrospinal fluid and are continuous with the central canal of the spinal cord
cavity in the brain containing cerebrospinal fluid
medical

Examples

The third ventricle is located between the two halves of the thalamus.
Obstruction of a brain ventricle can lead to increased pressure on the brain.
Cerebrospinal fluid flows from one ventricle to another, cushioning the brain.

Etymology

From Latin 'ventriculus', diminutive of 'venter' meaning 'belly' or 'stomach'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a chamber of the heart that receives blood from an atrium and pumps it into the arteries
heart chamber that pumps blood to arteries
medical

Examples

The left ventricle is responsible for pumping oxygenated blood to the body.
Damage to the right ventricle can lead to serious health problems.
Doctors detected a defect in one of the heart's ventricles during the scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrium - upper heart chamber that receives blood
  • Antonym: atrium - upper chamber of the heart that sends blood to a ventricle

Etymology

From Latin 'ventriculus', meaning 'little belly', diminutive of 'venter' (belly).
