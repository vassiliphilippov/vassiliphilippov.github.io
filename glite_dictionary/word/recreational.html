recreational

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or done for enjoyment and leisure rather than for work or duty
done for enjoyment or leisure

Examples

Many parks offer recreational activities such as hiking and swimming.
The center provides facilities for both recreational and competitive sports.
She enjoys recreational reading in her free time.

Synset Examples

an amateur painter
gained valuable experience in amateur theatricals
recreational golfers
reading matter that is both recreational and mentally stimulating
unpaid extras in the documentary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisure - free time not taken up with work
  • Antonym: professional - relating to a paid occupation or career

Etymology

From recreation + -al, based on Latin recreatio (refreshment, restoration).

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or denoting activities done for enjoyment when one is not working
relating to leisure or enjoyment

Examples

The city has invested in several recreational facilities for the community.
Many people take up recreational sports to stay active and healthy.
The park offered a range of recreational activities, such as hiking and boating.

Synset Examples

a recreational area with a pool and ball fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisure - free time when one is not working
  • Antonym: occupational - relating to or caused by one's job or profession

Etymology

From recreation + -al; recreation from Latin 'recereatio' (restoration), via Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02798655-a
Synset POS: a
