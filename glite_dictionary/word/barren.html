barren

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrən / (AmE), / ˈbærən / (BrE)
(of a woman or female animal) unable to produce offspring; sterile.
unable to produce offspring

Examples

After years of trying, the couple discovered that the wife was barren.
Veterinarians determined the mare was barren and could not be bred.
Old tales sometimes blame a witch's curse if a field or woman is barren.

Synset Examples

a barren woman
learned early in his marriage that he was sterile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fertile - able to produce offspring; capable of reproducing
  • Synonym: sterile - incapable of producing offspring or fruit

Etymology

From Old French *barain* (sterile), of uncertain origin.

3. ADJ

/ ˈbærən / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən / (BrE)
(of land or a place) unable to support life, growth, or habitation; providing no shelter or sustenance.

Examples

The hikers trekked across miles of barren desert before reaching the next village.
Satellite images revealed a barren stretch of land with no vegetation or wildlife.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɛr.ən / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən / (BrE)
An area of land, especially a wilderness, that is uninhabited and unsuitable for cultivation.
uninhabited, uncultivatable wilderness

Examples

Explorers ventured into the barrens in search of new routes across the continent.
Little grows in the vast barren that stretches north of the forest line.
Birds are rare in these barrens due to the harsh climate and sparse vegetation.

Synset Examples

the barrens of central Africa
the trackless wastes of the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasteland - land that is barren or unfit for cultivation

Etymology

From Old French *barain* (sterile), of uncertain origin.
