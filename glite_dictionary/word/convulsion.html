convulsion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvʌlʒən / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
a sudden, violent, and involuntary contraction of a group of muscles, often associated with certain medical conditions such as epilepsy
violent, involuntary muscle contraction
medical

Examples

The patient experienced a severe convulsion after the medication was administered.
Witnesses described the child falling to the ground and going into a convulsion.
Some fevers in young children can trigger a convulsion lasting several minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - a sudden attack of illness, especially epilepsy or another disorder with convulsions

Etymology

From Latin 'convulsio' (a wrenching or shattering), from 'convellere' (to tear away).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvʌlʒən / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
A sudden, violent disturbance or upheaval, especially in a physical, political, or social context.
violent disturbance or upheaval

Examples

The country underwent a major convulsion during the revolution.
Economic convulsions in the region led to widespread instability.
The earthquake caused a convulsion that shook the entire city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption, especially in society or nature
  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty

Etymology

From Latin convulsio (a tearing loose), from convellere (to tear up).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a sudden, violent, and uncontrollable movement of the body, often caused by muscle contractions; also used figuratively for a sudden, uncontrollable outburst.
sudden uncontrollable movement or outburst

Examples

The patient suffered a convulsion after being exposed to the flashing lights.
Her joke sent the whole room into convulsions of laughter.
A violent convulsion shook his body before he lost consciousness.

Synset Examples

a paroxysm of giggling
a fit of coughing
convulsions of laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - a sudden attack of illness, especially a fit of violent involuntary movements
  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
  • Antonym: calm - stillness or lack of sudden movement

Etymology

From Latin 'convulsio' (a wrenching), from 'convellere' (to tear away).
