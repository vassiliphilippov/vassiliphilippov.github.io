Synchytrium endobioticum

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈkaɪtrɪəm ˌɛndoʊbaɪˈɑːtɪkəm / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkɪtrɪəm ˌɛndəʊbaɪˈɒtɪkəm / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes potato wart disease, affecting potato tubers and reducing crop yield
fungus causing potato wart disease

Examples

Potatoes infected with Synchytrium endobioticum develop characteristic warty growths on their tubers.
Strict quarantine measures help prevent the spread of Synchytrium endobioticum in agricultural regions.
Researchers are studying resistant potato varieties to combat Synchytrium endobioticum infestations.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Synchytrium + species epithet endobioticum, reflecting its growth within host tissue.
