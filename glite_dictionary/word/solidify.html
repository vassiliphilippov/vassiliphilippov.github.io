to solidify

1. VERB

/ səˈlɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / səˈlɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become solid; to change from a liquid or gas to a solid state
to become solid

Examples

Allow the mixture to cool so that it will solidify into a gel.
When metal is poured into a mold, it will solidify as it cools.
The lava quickly began to solidify after the eruption.

Synset Examples

The metal solidified when it cooled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to liquefy - to become liquid
  • Synonym: to congeal - to become semi-solid or thickened

Etymology

From French *solidifier*; from Latin *solidus* (solid) + *-ify*.

2. VERB

/ səˈlɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / səˈlɪdɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become solid or more solid
make or become solid

Examples

Allow the chocolate to cool so it can solidify into a firm shape.
The decision helped solidify their partnership for years to come.
After being left out overnight, the cement mixture began to solidify rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - to make or become hard or harder
  • Antonym: to liquefy - to make or become liquid

Etymology

From French solidifier, based on Latin solidus (solid).
