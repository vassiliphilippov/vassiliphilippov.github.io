doublet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌblət / (AmE), / ˈdʌblɪt / (BrE)
A close-fitting jacket worn by men, especially during the Renaissance period.
Renaissance close-fitting men's jacket

Examples

The museum displayed an embroidered doublet worn by a 16th-century nobleman.
During Shakespearean plays, actors often wear a doublet to achieve historical accuracy.
The doublet was typically padded and tailored to emphasize the wearer's shoulders.

Etymology

From Middle French *doublet*, diminutive of *double* (double), referring to the double thickness of the original garment.
