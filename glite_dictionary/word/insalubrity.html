insalubrity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈluː.brə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈljuː.brɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unhealthy or harmful to health.
unhealthiness; harmfulness to health

Examples

The insalubrity of the factory environment led to frequent outbreaks of illness among workers.
City officials were concerned about the insalubrity of the water supply after the flooding.
The insalubrity of the prison was evident in its damp walls and foul air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unhealthiness - The quality of being unhealthy
  • Antonym: salubrity - The quality of being healthful

Etymology

From Latin 'insalubritas', from 'insalubris' (unhealthy).
