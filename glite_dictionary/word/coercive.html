coercive

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɝːsɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːsɪv / (BrE)
using force or threats to make someone do something; serving or intended to compel by force.
using or involving force or threats

Examples

The government passed new coercive laws to suppress dissent.
His management style was criticized as overly coercive and controlling.
The coercive tactics used during interrogations were widely condemned.

Synset Examples

authority is directional instead of coercive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsive - resulting from or relating to an irresistible urge
  • Antonym: persuasive - able, intended, or tending to persuade
  • Synonym: forceful - using force or strong power to achieve an effect

Etymology

From Latin coercere (to restrain, to enclose), via French coercitif.
