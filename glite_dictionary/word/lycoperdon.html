Lycoperdon

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɑːpərˌdɒn / (AmE), / lɪˈkɒpədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of puffball fungi whose fruiting body narrows toward a base of spongy mycelium.
genus of puffball fungi

Examples

Several species of Lycoperdon can be found in deciduous forests.
The Lycoperdon produces a puff of spores when pressed or stepped on.
Mycologists often study the unique shape of Lycoperdon fruiting bodies.

Etymology

From Greek 'lykos' (wolf) + 'perdon' (to break wind), referring to its spore discharge.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɒpərˌdɒn / (AmE), / laɪˈkɒpəˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of fungi whose fruiting bodies have a globular shape that tapers toward a spongy base, commonly known as puffballs
a genus of puffball fungi

Examples

The Lycoperdon species can be found growing in woodlands throughout late summer and autumn.
When mature, a Lycoperdon puffball releases a cloud of spores if pressed.
Scientists collected samples of Lycoperdon for analysis in their fungal study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a type of fungus that releases spores in a cloud when mature

Etymology

From Greek lycos (wolf) + perdon (to break wind), referring to the release of spores like a 'wolf's fart'.
