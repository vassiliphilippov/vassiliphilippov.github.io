Sylvilagus

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvɪləɡəs / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɪləɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of North and Central American rabbits that includes the cottontails.
genus of North American rabbits

Examples

The eastern cottontail, a species of Sylvilagus, is common in suburban backyards.
Many wildlife biologists study Sylvilagus populations to monitor ecosystem health.
Unlike hares, members of the genus Sylvilagus typically have smaller bodies and shorter legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottontail - a common name for rabbits of the genus Sylvilagus

Etymology

From Latin *silva* (forest) + *lagus* (hare).
