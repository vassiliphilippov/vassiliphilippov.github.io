convexity

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛksəti / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛksəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a surface or form that curves or bulges outward, like the exterior of a sphere or circle.
outward-curving shape or surface

Examples

The convexity of the lens helps it to focus light accurately onto the retina.
Architects admired the gentle convexity of the building's façade.
Raindrops tend to form a perfect convexity on waxed surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concavity - a shape that curves or is hollowed inward

Etymology

From Latin 'convexus' (vaulted, arched) + '-ity' forming nouns of quality.

2. NOUN

/ kɑnˈvɛksəti / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛksəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being convex; the property of having a surface or boundary that curves or bulges outward, like the exterior of a sphere or circle
the quality of being convex

Examples

The convexity of the lens allows it to focus light effectively.
A sphere is a classic example of perfect convexity in geometry.
Architects admired the building’s gentle convexity and elegant curves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concavity - the quality of being concave; an inward curve or hollowed surface

Etymology

From Late Latin convexitas, from Latin convexus (vaulted, arched).
