horny

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɔrni / (AmE), / ˈhɔːni / (BrE)
made of horn or of a material that resembles horn in appearance or texture
made of horn or resembling horn

Examples

The turtle's shell is covered with tough, horny plates that protect its body.
Some birds have strong, horny beaks adapted for cracking seeds.
The craftsman polished the horny surface of the ram's horn to make a drinking cup.

Etymology

From horn + -y, meaning 'made of or resembling horn'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɔrni / (AmE), / ˈhɔːni / (BrE)
having horns or hornlike projections on the body, such as in animals or plants
having horns or hornlike projections

Examples

The horny plates on the turtle's shell protect it from predators.
That species of toad is known for its horny projections above the eyes.
Some coral species develop a horny outer layer that helps them survive in rough waters.

Synset Examples

horny coral
horny (or horned) frog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned - having horns

Etymology

From horn + -y, meaning 'having the characteristics of horn.'

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɔrni / (AmE), / ˈhɔːni / (BrE)
feeling or showing strong sexual desire.
sexually aroused or desirous

Examples

She felt extremely horny after watching the romantic movie.
He joked that he gets especially horny in the summertime.
Even though she was tired, she admitted she was still a little horny.

Synset Examples

feeling horny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aroused - experiencing heightened sexual excitement
  • Synonym: lustful - showing or feeling strong sexual desire
  • Antonym: chaste - abstaining from sexual activity; not feeling or expressing sexual desire

Etymology

Originally meaning 'horn-like', later slang sense (sexually aroused) attested from the 19th century.
