Shingon

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡɒn / (BrE)
a major school of Japanese Buddhism that emphasizes mystical practices, ritual use of mantras and mudras, and the belief in an ultimate truth beyond expression
Japanese school of Buddhism

Examples

Many practitioners of Shingon Buddhism perform elaborate rituals involving mantras and hand gestures.
Kōbō Daishi is credited with founding the Shingon sect in Japan in the early 9th century.
The temple offers classes on the history and philosophy of Shingon.

Etymology

From Japanese 真言 (Shingon), meaning 'true word', a translation of the Sanskrit 'mantra'.
