nibble

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪbəl / (BrE)
a gentle bite, especially one taken in a careful or tentative manner
a gentle bite

Examples

She felt a nibble on her ear and started to laugh.
The fish gave a gentle nibble at the bait but didn't bite.
His affectionate nibble on her hand made her smile.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bite - an act of using the teeth to cut into or compress something

Etymology

Probably from Low German nibbeln, from nibben (to nib), related to German nippen (to sip).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈnɪb.əl / (AmE), / ˈnɪb.əl / (BrE)
to eat by taking small bites, often intermittently or in a restrained manner
eat by taking small bites

Examples

During the long meeting, she would quietly nibble on a granola bar.
Instead of eating a big lunch, he prefers to nibble throughout the day.
The child nibbled at his toast, barely making a dent in it.

Synset Examples

He pieced at the sandwich all morning
She never eats a full meal — she just nibbles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: munch - to eat with steady, often audible bites
  • Antonym: gobble - to eat quickly and in large mouthfuls

Etymology

From Middle English 'niblen', possibly of Low German or Dutch origin, akin to Dutch 'nibbelen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01176591-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪbəl / (BrE)
to bite something gently or take small bites, often repeatedly
bite gently or take small bites

Examples

The rabbit began to nibble on the fresh lettuce leaves in the garden.
She likes to nibble a piece of chocolate while reading in bed.
He gently nibbled her ear, making her laugh.

Synset Examples

The woman tenderly nibbled at her baby's ear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gnaw - to bite or chew something repeatedly, especially something hard

Etymology

From Late Middle English, probably from Low German nibbeln, frequentative of nibben (to nip).

5. NOUN

/ ˈnɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪbəl / (BrE)
A group of four bits, or half of an eight-bit byte, used in computing and digital systems.
four-bit unit in computing

Examples

In older computer systems, a nibble was often used to represent a single hexadecimal digit.
Each color component in this format uses a four-bit nibble for data storage.
To optimize memory, the programmer packed two decimal digits into one nibble each.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: byte - a group of eight bits in computing

Etymology

Formed as a play on the word 'byte', suggesting a smaller piece; 'nibble' means 'to take small bites'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13647355-n
Synset POS: n
