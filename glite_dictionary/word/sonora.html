Sonora

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔːrə / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
a genus of small colubrid snakes commonly known as ground snakes, found in North America
genus of small ground snakes

Examples

The Sonora genus includes several species of colorful, burrowing snakes.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Sonora genus in the southwestern United States.
Unlike many other colubrids, Sonora snakes are rarely seen above ground during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground snake - common name for snakes in the genus Sonora

Etymology

From the Spanish Sonora, the name of a region in Mexico where these snakes are found.

2. NOUN

/ səˈnɔːrə / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
a genus of small, burrowing colubrid snakes commonly known as ground snakes, native to North America.
a genus of ground snakes

Examples

The desert is home to several species within the genus Sonora.
Researchers discovered a new member of Sonora in Arizona's grasslands.
Species belonging to Sonora are often mistaken for other small snakes.

Etymology

Named after the Sonora region in northwestern Mexico.
