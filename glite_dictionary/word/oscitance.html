oscitance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪtəns / (BrE)
The act of yawning; an involuntary, wide-mouthed intake of breath, usually caused by tiredness or boredom.
the act of yawning

Examples

During the lecture, his frequent oscitance signaled his boredom to the speaker.
The physician noted an increase in oscitance among patients who reported poor sleep.
Uncontrollable oscitance in the meeting made it hard for anyone to focus.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yawn - to open the mouth wide and take a deep breath as a result of tiredness or boredom

Etymology

From Latin oscitantia (yawning, drowsiness), from oscitare (to yawn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsɪtəns / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪtəns / (BrE)
A state of drowsiness or inattentiveness shown by yawning; the act or state of yawning.
drowsiness marked by yawning

Examples

After lunch, a wave of oscitance swept through the classroom as students fought to stay awake.
The professor noticed the oscitance in the audience during the lengthy lecture.
His constant oscitance made it clear that he hadn't slept well the night before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yawning - the involuntary opening of the mouth with deep inhalation, often due to drowsiness

Etymology

From Latin oscitans, oscitantis, present participle of oscitare (to yawn, to be drowsy).
