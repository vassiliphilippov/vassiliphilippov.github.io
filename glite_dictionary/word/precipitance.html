precipitance

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.təns / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.təns / (BrE)
The quality of acting or happening with excessive or rash haste, often without careful thought or warning.
rash or hasty action

Examples

Her precipitance in making decisions often led to mistakes.
The project failed due to the precipitance of its execution.
He regretted his precipitance in leaving his job without another offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rashness - the quality of being reckless or acting without thinking
  • Antonym: deliberation - careful consideration before action

Etymology

From Latin 'praecipitans' (hurling headlong), via French 'précipitant' and English 'precipitate'.
