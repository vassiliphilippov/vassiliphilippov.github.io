accentor

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛntər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛntə / (BrE)
a small, sparrow-like songbird native to mountainous regions of Eurasia, belonging to the family Prunellidae.
small Eurasian songbird

Examples

The accentor is often seen hopping among rocks in the alpine meadows.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare brown accentor during their trek.
Although similar in size to sparrows, the accentor has a more melodic song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunnock - a common species of accentor found in Europe

Etymology

From Latin *accentor* (singer, one who sings with someone), from *ad* (to) + *cantor* (singer).
