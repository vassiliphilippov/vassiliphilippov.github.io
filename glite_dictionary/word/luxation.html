luxation

1. NOUN

/ lʌkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / lʌkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the displacement or misalignment of a joint or organ, especially the complete dislocation of a bone from its joint
displacement of a joint or organ
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a luxation of the shoulder following the accident.
Prompt reduction is essential to manage a hip luxation and prevent complications.
Veterinarians often encounter luxation of the patella in certain dog breeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dislocation - an injury in which a bone is forced out of its normal position in a joint

Etymology

From Latin 'luxatio', from 'luxare' (to dislocate).
