retributory

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtrɪbjəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɪbjʊtəri / (BrE)
Relating to or involving retribution; given as punishment or reward in response to actions, according to what is deserved.
relating to retribution or deserved punishment

Examples

The court took a retributory approach when deciding the sentence.
Many societies have included retributory elements in their codes of justice.
He believed in retributory actions to restore moral balance.

Synset Examples

retributive justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retributive - relating to or involving retribution

Etymology

From Latin retributus (given in return), from retribuere (to give back).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈtrɪbjəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɪbjʊtəri / (BrE)
relating to, involving, or having the nature of retribution; characterized by giving punishment or reward in return for an action
relating to retribution or punishment

Examples

The judge's sentence reflected a retributory approach to justice, emphasizing punishment over rehabilitation.
Many ancient laws were based on retributory principles, such as 'an eye for an eye.'
He believed that retributory actions were necessary to maintain social order.

Synset Examples

retributive justice demands an eye for an eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retributive - relating to or involving retribution

Etymology

From Latin 'retributus,' past participle of 'retribuere' (to give back).
