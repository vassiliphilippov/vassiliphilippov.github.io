Immanuel Kant

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈmænjuəl kɑnt / (AmE), / ɪˈmanjuəl kant / (BrE)
(1724–1804) An influential German philosopher best known for his work in epistemology and ethics, especially the 'Critique of Pure Reason'.
German philosopher, major figure in idealism

Examples

Many students encounter the theories of Immanuel Kant in introductory philosophy courses.
Immanuel Kant's work laid the foundation for much of modern Western philosophy.
Debates about morality often refer back to the arguments made by Immanuel Kant.

Etymology

Immanuel (first name) + Kant (surname); 'Kant' is of German origin.
