spiciness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.si.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.si.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being flavored with spices, especially to a degree that produces a strong, pungent, or hot taste.
the quality of being spicy

Examples

The spiciness of the curry made my mouth tingle.
Some people enjoy the spiciness of hot peppers, while others find it overwhelming.
He balanced the spiciness of the dish with a refreshing yogurt sauce.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mildness - the quality of having a gentle or not strong flavor
  • Synonym: hotness - the quality of tasting very hot or peppery due to spices

Etymology

From 'spicy' + '-ness', formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsinəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being lively, mildly indecent, or slightly shocking, especially in behavior or language
mildly indecent or lively quality

Examples

The comedian's jokes had just enough spiciness to keep the audience entertained without crossing the line.
Her writing is admired for its spiciness, offering sharp wit and a hint of daring.
The conversation at dinner was full of spiciness, making everyone laugh at the clever double meanings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquancy - pleasantly stimulating or exciting quality
  • Synonym: risqué - slightly indecent, typically in a humorous way
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of liveliness or excitement

Etymology

From spice + -iness, influenced by 'spicy' (having excitement or piquancy).
