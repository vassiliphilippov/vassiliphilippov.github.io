gleam

1. NOUN

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
a brief or faint flash or beam of light, especially a reflected one
a brief flash of light

Examples

A sudden gleam from the polished mirror caught my eye.
There was a faint gleam of light on the horizon as dawn approached.
The diamond gave off a bright gleam when it hit the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glint - a tiny, quick flash of light
  • Synonym: sparkle - a series of small flashes of light

Etymology

From Middle English *gleme*, from Old English *glēam* (radiance, brightness).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
a small or brief appearance or reflection of light; a faint or brief brightness
a small or brief flash of light

Examples

A gleam of sunlight caught the edge of the mirror.
She saw a gleam of light in the distance as the sun set.
The jewels sparkled with every gleam of the chandelier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimmer - a faint or wavering light
  • Synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light
  • Degree Higher: glow - a steady radiance of light or heat without flame

Etymology

From Middle English gleem, from Old English glǣm (brightness, gleam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04961711-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
to shine with a soft or reflected light, often appearing as if wet or polished
shine with a soft, often wet-looking light

Examples

The dew on the grass made the lawn gleam in the early morning sunlight.
Freshly waxed floors gleam under the hallway lights at night.
Her eyes gleamed with excitement when she saw the surprise.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to glisten - to shine with a sparkling light, especially as if wet
  • Synonym: shimmer - to shine with a flickering or wavering light

Etymology

From Middle English *glemen* (to shine), from Old English *glǣmen* (to shine, gleam), related to Old English *glǣm* (brightness)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02167378-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
to shine with a faint or brief light, or to appear suddenly or briefly
shine faintly or briefly

Examples

A golden light began to gleam through the clouds at dawn.
Her eyes would gleam with excitement whenever she talked about her travels.
A smile gleamed briefly on his face before disappearing.

Synset Examples

A terrible thought gleamed in her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimmer - to shine with a weak or unsteady light
  • Degree Higher: flash - to shine suddenly and briefly
  • Degree Higher: glow - to shine steadily with a soft light

Etymology

From Middle English *glemen* (to shine), from Old English *glǣman* (to brighten).

5. VERB

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
to shine with a steady, bright, or reflected light
to shine brightly or reflect light

Examples

The sun began to gleam through the morning clouds.
Her eyes gleamed with excitement as she opened the present.
Streetlights gleamed on the wet pavement after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shine - to emit light; to be bright by reflecting or emitting light
  • Synonym: glow - to give out steady light without flame
  • Antonym: dull - not bright or shiny

Etymology

From Middle English *glemen* (to shine) and Old English *glǣman* (to glimmer).

Source: WordNet
