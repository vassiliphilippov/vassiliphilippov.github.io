sagittiform

1. ADJ

/ səˈdʒɪt̬ɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / səˈdʒɪtɪfɔːm / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other organ) shaped like an arrowhead, with the point projecting forward and lacking flaring base lobes.
arrowhead-shaped (botanical term)

Examples

The botanist identified the unusual plant by its distinctive sagittiform leaves.
Many aquatic plants exhibit sagittiform foliage to help reduce water resistance.
The illustration showed a sagittiform leaf, easily recognized by its arrowhead shape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sagittate - (of a leaf) shaped like an arrowhead with pointed lobes at the base

Etymology

From Latin 'sagitta' (arrow) + '-form' (shape, form).
