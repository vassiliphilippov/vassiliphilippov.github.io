dicamptodontid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkæmptəˈdɒntɪd / (AmE), / daɪˌkæmptəˈdɒntɪd / (BrE)
A salamander belonging to the family Dicamptodontidae, typically found near cold streams throughout the year in North America.
a family of northwestern salamanders

Examples

The giant dicamptodontid is native to the forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Researchers discovered several dicamptodontids living in a secluded mountain stream.
Unlike other salamanders, a dicamptodontid can often be found during cooler months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian of the order Caudata, typically resembling a lizard in appearance

Etymology

From New Latin Dicamptodontidae, family name formed from genus Dicamptodon and standard zoological suffix -idae.
