Daboecia

1. NOUN

/ dəˈbiːsiə / (AmE), / dəˈbɪsɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family (Ericaceae), native to western Europe, known for their bell-shaped flowers.
a genus of evergreen heaths

Examples

The Daboecia genus is easily recognized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.
Several species of Daboecia are cultivated in gardens for their attractive foliage and blooms.
He discovered a rare Daboecia growing wild in the misty hills of Ireland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erica - a related genus of heath plants in the Ericaceae family
  • Synonym: Calluna - another related genus of heaths in the Ericaceae family

Etymology

Named after St. Dabeoc, a 6th-century Irish saint.

Daboecia is often cultivated for its attractive purple flowers and evergreen leaves.

Gardeners appreciate Daboecia for its long blooming season and resistance to deer.
