smashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breaking something into small pieces with force
the act of breaking into pieces

Examples

The smashing of the vase echoed through the quiet room.
Police investigated the smashing of several shop windows during the protest.
The smashing of ice cubes made it easier to fill the glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakage - the act of breaking something

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely good, impressive, or excellent; very enjoyable or attractive (especially in informal British English)
excellent; impressive (informal)
informal british

Examples

You look smashing in that new dress!
We had a smashing time at Julia's birthday party last night.
That was a smashing performance by the orchestra.

Synset Examples

he did a bully job
a neat sports car
had a great time at the party

we had a grand old time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great - very good; excellent
  • Antonym: awful - very bad; terrible

Etymology

Originally British slang; derived from 'smash' with the -ing suffix, attested from the early 20th century.
