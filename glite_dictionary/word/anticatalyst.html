anticatalyst

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈkæt.əl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈkætəlɪst / (BrE)
(chemistry) A substance that slows down a chemical reaction or reduces the effectiveness of a catalyst.
substance that retards a chemical reaction
medical

Examples

The research team added an anticatalyst to the reaction mixture to observe its effect on the process.
Introducing an anticatalyst can help prevent unwanted side reactions during chemical synthesis.
The presence of an anticatalyst significantly reduced the yield of the desired product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a particular chemical reaction
  • Antonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'catalyst'.
