specialisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a specific area of study or work that someone concentrates on as their main interest or expertise, especially in a profession or academic field
focus or area of expertise in a field
british

Examples

Her specialisation is in environmental engineering, where she has built a reputation for innovative research.
After medical school, he chose cardiology as his specialisation.
Language acquisition is now my main specialisation at the university.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - focus or area of expertise in a field (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (individual), via French 'spécialisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of concentrating on and becoming expert in a particular subject or skill; the act of making something suitable for a specific purpose
the act of specialising; becoming expert in a field
british

Examples

Her specialisation in marine biology required years of study and research.
The trend toward specialisation has led to highly focused job roles in technology.
Economic growth in the region was spurred by the specialisation of local industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - American spelling of 'specialisation'
  • Antonym: generalisation - the process of making something less specific; the opposite of specialisation

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (of a kind or species). The suffix '-ation' forms nouns denoting actions or processes.

3. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which a body part or cell becomes adapted to perform a specific function.
biological adaptation of body part or cell

Examples

During embryonic development, specialisation allows cells to take on unique roles in the body.
The specialisation of leaves into thorns helps protect certain plants from herbivores.
Nerve cells exhibit extreme specialisation to transmit electrical signals efficiently.

Synset Examples

cell differentiation in the developing embryo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differentiation - (Biology) The process by which cells or tissues change to serve a specific function

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (particular, special) via Medieval Latin 'specialisatio'; in scientific use since mid-19th century.
