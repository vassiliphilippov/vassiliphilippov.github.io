nasal

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəl / (BrE)
an elongated rectangular bone forming the bridge of the nose in humans and many animals
bone forming the bridge of the nose
medical

Examples

A fracture of the nasal can cause the bridge of the nose to become deformed.
The surgeon carefully repositioned the nasal after the injury.
Humans typically have two small nasal bones side by side at the bridge of the nose.

Etymology

From Latin nasal is (‘of the nose’), from nāsus (‘nose’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.zəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by allowing air to escape through the nose while keeping the mouth closed.
consonant pronounced through the nose

Examples

In English, the sounds represented by 'm', 'n', and 'ng' are all nasal consonants.
The linguist explained why some languages have more nasals than others.
When he had a cold, even his normal speech sounded like a nasal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oral - a speech sound made through the mouth without nasal passage of air

Etymology

From Latin nasalis, from nasus (nose).

3. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.zəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having a quality that sounds as if it comes through the nose, as when the nose is blocked or pinched.
sounding as if through the nose

Examples

His cold made his nasal voice even more pronounced during the meeting.
She spoke in a slightly nasal tone that was easy to recognize.
The singer's nasal quality gave her voice a unique character.

Synset Examples

a whining nasal voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasally - in a manner that sounds as if coming through the nose
  • Antonym: denasal - lacking a nasal quality in speech or sound

Etymology

From Latin nasalis, from nasus (nose).
