genie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːni / (BrE)
(in Islamic folklore) an invisible spirit, often said to inhabit the earth and to have the power to influence human affairs, sometimes taking human or animal form.
invisible spirit in Islamic folklore

Examples

The old lamp was said to contain a powerful genie who could grant three wishes.
Stories about a genie helping those who freed it are common in Middle Eastern folklore.
She dressed as a genie for the costume party, complete with a magic lamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jinn - a supernatural being or spirit in Muslim folklore

Etymology

From French génie, from Arabic jinnī (spirit), from jinn (plural).
