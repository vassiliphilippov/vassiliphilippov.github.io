demineralization

1. NOUN

/ diˌmɪnərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪnəraɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal loss of mineral salts, especially from bone, often resulting in weakening or disease
abnormal loss of mineral salts
medical

Examples

Osteoporosis is associated with significant demineralization of the bones, increasing the risk of fractures.
Fluoridated water can help prevent dental demineralization in children.
After prolonged immobilization, patients may experience demineralization due to lack of weight-bearing activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remineralization - the restoration of mineral content, especially in teeth or bones

Etymology

From de- (removal) + mineral + -ization (process of making or becoming).

2. NOUN

/ diˌmɪnərəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of minerals and mineral salts, especially from water or other liquids
removal of minerals from a liquid

Examples

Demineralization of water is essential for use in laboratory experiments.
The plant uses advanced technology for the demineralization of industrial wastewater.
Reverse osmosis is one method of achieving demineralization in municipal water systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desalination - the process of removing salt from seawater

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal) + mineral + -ization.
