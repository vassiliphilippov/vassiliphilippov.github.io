entrepot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːntrəˌpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒntrəˌpəʊ / (BrE)
A port or city where goods can be imported, stored, and then exported without the payment of import duties.
duty-free trading port or city

Examples

Singapore developed as a major entrepot for Southeast Asian trade routes.
Centuries ago, Venice prospered as an entrepot for spices and luxury goods.
The city thrived as an entrepot, attracting merchants from across the globe.

Synset Examples

Bahrain has been an entrepot of trade between Arabia and India since the second millennium BC

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free port - a port where goods may be imported or exported and generally not subject to customs duty

Etymology

From French 'entrepôt', from entreposer (to store), from entre- (between) + poser (to place).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːntrəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒntrəpəʊ / (BrE)
a port, city, or warehouse where goods are stored or imported and exported, often for transshipment.
trading post or storage depot for goods

Examples

Singapore became a major entrepot for trade between East and West in the 19th century.
The goods were stored temporarily in an entrepot before being shipped to their final destination.
As a bustling entrepot, the city attracted merchants from around the world.

Synset Examples

storehouses were built close to the docks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building where raw materials or manufactured goods may be stored
  • Synonym: depot - a place for the storage of large quantities of equipment, food, or goods

Etymology

From French 'entrepôt' (warehouse, depot), from Latin 'interpositum' (something placed between).
