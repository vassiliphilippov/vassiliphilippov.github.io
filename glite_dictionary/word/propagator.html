propagator

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.pəˌɡeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.əˌɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who spreads news, ideas, or information to others
person who spreads news or ideas

Examples

The internet has turned many individuals into propagators of news and opinions.
As a propagator of new theories, she often sparked lively debate among her peers.
False information can easily be amplified by an unwitting propagator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disseminator - a person who spreads information widely
  • Synonym: spreader - someone who spreads something, especially information

Etymology

From Latin 'propagare' (to spread, extend, increase) via 'propagator'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːpəˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who propagates plants, especially by encouraging their growth and reproduction, often under controlled conditions such as in a greenhouse.
person who propagates plants

Examples

The experienced propagator carefully tended the young seedlings in the greenhouse.
As a skilled propagator, she developed new varieties of ornamental plants for the nursery.
Many garden centers employ at least one propagator to manage their plant production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horticulturist - a person who grows and studies garden plants

Etymology

From Latin propagare (to set forward, extend, propagate) + -or (agent noun).
