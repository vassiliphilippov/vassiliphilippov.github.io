manicotti

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈkɑti / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkɒti / (BrE)
large pasta tubes, typically stuffed with meat or cheese and baked in tomato sauce
stuffed pasta tubes baked in sauce

Examples

She made a delicious pan of manicotti filled with spinach and ricotta cheese.
The restaurant's manicotti is stuffed with ground beef and topped with marinara sauce.
He ordered manicotti for dinner, craving the warm, cheesy pasta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannelloni - large pasta tubes often stuffed and baked, similar to manicotti

Etymology

Borrowed from Italian 'manicotti', plural of 'manicotto' meaning 'muff' or 'cuff' (from 'mano' hand).
