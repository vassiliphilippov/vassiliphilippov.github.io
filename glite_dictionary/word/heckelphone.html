heckelphone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛkəlˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛkəlfəʊn / (BrE)
a double-reed woodwind instrument similar to an oboe but pitched an octave lower
a low-pitched woodwind instrument

Examples

The composer wrote a part for the heckelphone to add depth to the woodwind section.
Few orchestras own a heckelphone because it is a rare and specialized instrument.
The heckelphone produces a rich, deep sound that contrasts with the higher oboe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double reed

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Heckel, its German inventor, and German 'phon' meaning 'sound'.
