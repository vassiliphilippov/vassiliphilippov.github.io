Exocet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksəˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɛksəʊˌsɛt / (BrE)
a French-made guided missile designed for attacking ships, notable for its use in naval warfare
French anti-ship missile

Examples

The warship was struck by an Exocet missile during the conflict.
Many countries have purchased the Exocet for their naval forces.
The Exocet gained notoriety in the Falklands War.

Etymology

From French 'Exocet', meaning 'flying fish', from Latin 'exocoetus'.
