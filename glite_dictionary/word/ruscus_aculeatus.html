Ruscus aculeatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌskəs ˌækjʊˈleɪətəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌskəs ˌakjuːˈleɪətəs / (BrE)
a Eurasian shrub with stiff, flattened stems that resemble leaves, commonly known as butcher's-broom and used for making brooms
Eurasian shrub (butcher's-broom) with stiff, leaf-like stems

Examples

Ruscus aculeatus is often planted in gardens for its evergreen foliage and decorative berries.
Traditional herbal medicine sometimes uses extracts from Ruscus aculeatus.
The tough stems of Ruscus aculeatus were once used to make brooms by butchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher's-broom - common name for Ruscus aculeatus, a shrub with stiff, green stems

Etymology

From Latin, where 'ruscus' means 'butcher's-broom' and 'aculeatus' means 'prickly'.
