unionism

1. NOUN

/ ˈjunjəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnjənɪzəm / (BrE)
the system of principles, organization, and theory relating to labor unions and collective bargaining
theory and system of labor unions

Examples

Many workers in the late nineteenth century embraced unionism to improve their wages and working conditions.
The rise of unionism led to significant social and economic reforms.
Debates about unionism continue to shape labor policies worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade unionism - the theory or movement supporting trade unions specifically
  • Antonym: antiunionism - opposition to labor unions and their activities

Etymology

From 'union' + '-ism', reflecting belief in or support for labor unions.
