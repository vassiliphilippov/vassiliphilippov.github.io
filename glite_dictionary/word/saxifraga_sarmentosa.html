Saxifraga sarmentosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə sɑːrˈmɛntoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə sɑːˈmɛntəsə / (BrE)
An eastern Asiatic saxifrage plant with racemes of small red-and-white flowers, spreading by numerous creeping stolons; also known as the strawberry begonia.
an Asiatic saxifrage plant

Examples

The Saxifraga sarmentosa grows well in hanging baskets due to its trailing stolons.
Gardeners prize Saxifraga sarmentosa for its attractive foliage and delicate red-and-white flowers.
She identified the plant as Saxifraga sarmentosa after noticing its runners and uniquely colored blooms.

Etymology

From Latin: saxifraga (stone-breaker, a genus name) + sarmentosa (having runners or stolons).
