Mergus merganser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrɡəs mɜrˈɡænsər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːɡəs mɜːˈɡænzə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common merganser, a large fish-eating duck native to Europe and North America.
scientific name for the common merganser

Examples

The Mergus merganser can often be seen fishing in cold, fast-moving rivers.
Ornithologists tracked the migration patterns of Mergus merganser populations across Europe.
A male Mergus merganser is identifiable by its striking white plumage and dark green head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common merganser - large fish-eating duck of Eurasia and North America

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Mergus* (a diver, a waterbird) + specific epithet *merganser* (from Latin *mergus*, diver, and *anser*, goose).
