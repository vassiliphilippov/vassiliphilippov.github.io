to polymerise

1. VERB

/ pəˈlɪm.ɚ.aɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪm.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a compound) to undergo polymerization and form a polymer
cause to undergo polymerization
british

Examples

The heat will polymerise the monomers, resulting in a solid plastic.
Some chemicals can polymerise rapidly if exposed to ultraviolet light.
The catalyst is added to polymerise the liquid mixture during manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to polymerize - to cause (a compound) to undergo polymerization; American spelling

Etymology

From polymer +‎ -ise, after polymerization; chiefly British spelling of 'polymerize'.

2. VERB

/ pəˈlɪməˌraɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməraɪz / (BrE)
to undergo a chemical reaction in which small molecules join together to form a polymer
undergo polymerization

Examples

When heated, the substance will polymerise to form a rigid plastic.
Some resins polymerise rapidly in the presence of ultraviolet light.
If left exposed to air, the liquid may polymerise over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to polymerize - undergo polymerization (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'polymer' + '-ise', based on Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'meros' (part).
