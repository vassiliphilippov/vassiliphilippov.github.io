Leucadendron

1. NOUN

/ luːkəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / luːkəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs with silvery white leaves and solitary terminal flowers with conspicuous silvery bracts, native to southern Africa.
genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The garden features several varieties of Leucadendron known for their striking foliage.
A single Leucadendron can bring dramatic color to the landscape in winter.
Many florists use Leucadendron stems in bouquets for their unique silvery appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *leukos* (white) and *dendron* (tree), referring to the silvery foliage.

2. NOUN

/ luːkəˈdɛnˌdrɒn / (AmE), / ljuːkəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs native to South Africa, known for their silvery leaves and conspicuous terminal flowers with silvery bracts.
genus of evergreen South African plants

Examples

The Leucadendron is prized for its striking silvery foliage in garden landscapes.
Several species within the Leucadendron genus are cultivated for use as ornamental plants.
Many florists use Leucadendron stems in floral arrangements because of their unique bracts.

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white) + dendron (tree).
