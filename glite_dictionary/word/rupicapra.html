Rupicapra

1. NOUN

/ ruːpɪˈkæprə / (AmE), / ruːpɪˈkaprə / (BrE)
The genus of goat-antelopes that includes the chamois, native to mountainous regions of Europe.
genus of goat-antelopes (chamois)

Examples

The genus Rupicapra includes two species, found in the mountains of Europe and Anatolia.
Researchers studied the alpine habitat of Rupicapra to understand its adaptations.
Populations of Rupicapra have been impacted by climate change and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamois - a species of goat-antelope native to European mountains, member of genus Rupicapra

Etymology

From Latin 'rūpīcapra', meaning 'wild goat'.
