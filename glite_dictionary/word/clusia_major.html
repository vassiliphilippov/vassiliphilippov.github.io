Clusia major

1. NOUN

/ ˈkluːʒə ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈkluːzɪə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a species of tropical American tree with solitary white or rose-colored flowers, belonging to the Clusia genus.
tropical American tree with white or rose flowers

Examples

The glossy leaves of Clusia major make it a popular ornamental tree in tropical gardens.
Researchers studied the pollination patterns of Clusia major in different regions.
Clusia major is valued for its resilience to salt spray along coastal areas.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Clusia' named after Carolus Clusius, Flemish botanist; 'major' meaning 'greater, larger'.
