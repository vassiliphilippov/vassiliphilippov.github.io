femtometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛm.toʊˌmiː.tər / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.təˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth (10⁻¹⁵) of a meter, used mainly in nuclear physics
unit of length; one quadrillionth of a meter

Examples

The diameter of a proton is about one femtometer.
Physicists often use the femtometer to measure distances within an atomic nucleus.
A femtometer is so small that it is far beyond the scale of everyday objects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: picometer - a metric unit of length equal to one trillionth of a meter
  • Degree Lower: attometer - a metric unit of length equal to one quintillionth of a meter
  • Degree Higher: nanometer - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter

Etymology

From 'femto-' (a metric prefix denoting 10⁻¹⁵, from Danish 'femten', fifteen) + 'meter'.
