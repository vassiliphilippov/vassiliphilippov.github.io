mull

1. VERB

/ mʌl / (AmE), / mʌl / (BrE)
to heat (a drink, especially wine or cider) with sugar and spices to make it hot and flavorful
heat a drink with sugar and spices

Examples

She decided to mull some red wine with cloves and cinnamon for the holiday party.
During the winter, many people mull cider as a comforting evening treat.
The chef mulled the beverage to bring out a warm, fragrant flavor.

Synset Examples

mulled cider

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'mol', meaning 'to grind', referring to the spices used.

2. VERB

/ mʌl / (AmE), / mʌl / (BrE)
to think about something deeply and at length, especially before making a decision
think deeply about; consider carefully

Examples

She sat by the window to mull over the choices she had to make.
He likes to mull his ideas before sharing them with the team.
After receiving the job offer, I needed some time to mull it over.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to consider - think carefully about, especially in making decisions
  • Antonym: to ignore - refuse to consider or pay attention to

3. NOUN

/ mʌl / (AmE), / mʌl / (BrE)
a promontory or headland, especially in Scotland, often used in place names
Scottish promontory or headland

Examples

We visited the Mull of Galloway, which offers spectacular views of the sea.
The Mull of Kintyre is famous for its scenic beauty and rugged coastline.
Tourists often hike to the Mull to experience the dramatic cliffs and coastal landscapes.

Synset Examples

the Mull of Kintyre

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headland - a narrow piece of land that sticks out from the coast into the sea
  • Synonym: cape - a large headland extending into a body of water

Etymology

From Gaelic 'maol' meaning a rounded hill or promontory.
