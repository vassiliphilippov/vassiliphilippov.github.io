Diplococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of spherical bacteria that typically occurs in pairs of joined cells
genus of spherical paired bacteria
medical

Examples

The scientist identified the bacteria under the microscope as belonging to the genus Diplococcus.
Cases of pneumonia were once commonly attributed to Diplococcus organisms.
Research into the antibiotic resistance of Diplococcus has yielded significant findings.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'diplo-' (double) and 'kokkos' (berry, referring to spherical shape).
