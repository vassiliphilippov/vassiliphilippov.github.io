ultramodernity

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˌmɑdərˈnɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˌmɒdəˈnɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being extremely modern or advanced in style, ideas, or technology
state of being extremely modern

Examples

The architecture of the museum reveals a deliberate sense of ultramodernity.
Some critics argue that ultramodernity has erased distinctions between traditional forms and contemporary innovations.
The novel’s ultramodernity challenged readers who preferred more conventional narratives.

Synset Examples

An Italian state examiner, jolted by my edition of Cavalcanti, expressed admiration at the almost ultramodernity of Guido's language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From ultra- (Latin for 'beyond') + modern + -ity.
