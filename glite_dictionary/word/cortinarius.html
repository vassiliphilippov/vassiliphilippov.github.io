Cortinarius

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrtəˈnɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪˈneəriəs / (BrE)
a large genus of gilled mushrooms characterized by rusty spores and a cobweb-like partial veil (cortina), common in woodland areas
genus of mushrooms with cobwebby veils

Examples

The Cortinarius genus includes many species that are difficult to identify without examining their rusty spores.
She found a beautiful specimen of Cortinarius while foraging in the forest.
Mushroom hunters are cautioned to avoid eating wild Cortinarius due to several poisonous species in the genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'cortina' (curtain or veil), referring to the cobweb-like veil protecting the gills.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrtɪˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪˈneəriəs / (BrE)
a large genus of agaric fungi characterized by rusty spores and cobweb-like veils (cortinae), including many woodland species
large genus of agaric fungi

Examples

Many species in the Cortinarius genus are found in temperate forests around the world.
The rust-colored spores help mycologists identify members of the Cortinarius group.
Some mushrooms in the Cortinarius genus can be toxic and should not be eaten.

Etymology

From Latin 'cortina' (curtain), referring to the cobweb-like veil covering the gills.
