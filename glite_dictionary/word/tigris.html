Tigris

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (BrE)
a major river in western Asia, flowing through Turkey and Iraq and joining the Euphrates to form the Shatt al-Arab
river in western Asia

Examples

The Tigris and the Euphrates rivers have supported civilizations for thousands of years.
Baghdad lies on the banks of the Tigris river, which is vital to the city's water supply.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient relics along the Tigris in Iraq.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphrates - a major river in western Asia, running parallel to the Tigris

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Tigris, from Old Persian Tigra ('fast'), possibly of Sumerian origin.
