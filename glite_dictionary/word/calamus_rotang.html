Calamus rotang

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləməs roʊˈtɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæləməs rəʊˈtæŋ / (BrE)
A climbing palm native to Sri Lanka and southern India, known for its long stems used to make malacca canes.
climbing palm used for malacca canes

Examples

The stems of Calamus rotang are harvested to make malacca walking canes.
In traditional crafts, Calamus rotang is valued for its flexible but strong stems.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Calamus rotang in the forests of southern India.

Etymology

Calamus from Latin, originally from Greek *kalamos* (reed, cane); rotang from Malay 'rotan' (cane).
