Zizania

1. NOUN

/ zɪˈzeɪniə / (AmE), / zɪˈzeɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses known as wild rice, whose seeds are used as food, especially in North America and East Asia.
genus of grasses known as wild rice

Examples

The fields were planted with Zizania, which was later harvested as wild rice.
Botanists are studying the growth patterns of Zizania in wetland habitats.
Several species within the genus Zizania are cultivated for their nutritious grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild rice - the edible grain of grasses of the genus Zizania

Etymology

From Latin Zizania, adopted from Ancient Greek ζιζάνια (zizania), meaning 'weeds'.
