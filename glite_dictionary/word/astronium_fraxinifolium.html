Astronium fraxinifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈnoʊniəm ˌfræksɪˈnɪfəliəm / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈnəʊnɪəm ˌfraksɪˈnɪfəliəm / (BrE)
A tall tropical tree native to eastern Brazil, valued for its hard, durable zebrawood used in veneer, furniture, and construction.
Brazilian timber tree yielding zebrawood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Astronium fraxinifolium, giving it a striking striped appearance.
High-end veneers often use wood from Astronium fraxinifolium for its unique grain and durability.
Conservationists are concerned about the overharvesting of Astronium fraxinifolium in Brazilian forests.

Etymology

From the genus name Astronium and Latin fraxinifolium, meaning 'ash-leaved.'
