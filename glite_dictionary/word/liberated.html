liberated

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a gas or substance) released from chemical combination by a chemical reaction.
released from chemical combination

Examples

Carbon dioxide is liberated when an acid reacts with baking soda.
The reaction caused oxygen gas to be liberated from the compound.
When heated, ammonia is liberated from ammonium salts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: released - set free or allowed to escape

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'liberate', from Latin 'liberare' (to set free).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
Free from traditional social restraints or conventions, especially in behavior or attitudes.
free from social restraints

Examples

She led a liberated life, choosing her own path regardless of social expectations.
The 1960s saw a rise in liberated attitudes toward relationships and gender roles.
He admired her liberated outlook, which inspired others to challenge conventions.

Synset Examples

an emancipated young woman pursuing her career
a liberated lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - adhering to accepted customs and standards

Etymology

From Latin liberatus, past participle of liberare (to set free).
