afield

1. ADV

/ əˈfild / (AmE), / əˈfiːld / (BrE)
far away from home or from one's usual place; in or to a distant place
far from home or usual surroundings

Examples

The scientists ventured afield in search of rare plant species.
She often travels afield for her research projects.
The company's business has expanded afield to several overseas markets.

Synset Examples

looking afield for new lands to conquer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abroad - in or to a foreign country
  • Antonym: home - at or to one's place of residence

Etymology

From Middle English 'a feld', meaning 'on the field'.
