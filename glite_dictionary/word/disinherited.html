disinherited

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd / (BrE)
deprived of the right to receive an inheritance or rightful heritage, especially by a legal decision or family choice
deprived of an inheritance

Examples

After the argument, the eldest son found himself disinherited from the family estate.
The king’s disinherited brother plotted to regain his lost rights.
She felt like a disinherited child when her father changed his will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispossessed - deprived of possession, especially of land or property

Etymology

Formed from 'disinherit' (from Old French 'desheriter'), plus the English past participle suffix -ed.
