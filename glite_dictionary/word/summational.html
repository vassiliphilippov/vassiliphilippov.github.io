summational

1. ADJ

/ sʌˈmeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / sʌˈmeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from summation; produced by adding things together.
relating to summation or the act of adding up

Examples

The report included a summational analysis of the survey results.
Her summational remarks captured the main themes of the discussion.
In mathematics, a summational approach is often used to simplify complex data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summative - serving to sum up or as a summary

Etymology

From 'summation' + suffix '-al', with 'summation' from Latin 'summatio' (total, sum).
