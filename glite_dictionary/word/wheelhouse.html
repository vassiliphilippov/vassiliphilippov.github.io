wheelhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an enclosed compartment on a ship from which it is steered and navigated
enclosed compartment for navigating a ship

Examples

The captain remained in the wheelhouse during the storm to guide the ship safely to shore.
Visitors were allowed to tour the wheelhouse and see the old wooden steering wheel.
Thick fog made it difficult for those in the wheelhouse to see more than a few meters ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - the elevated platform or structure on a ship from which it is commanded

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an area in which a person is particularly skilled, knowledgeable, or comfortable; a subject or activity that is within one's range of expertise or interest
area of expertise or interest

Examples

Public speaking is right in her wheelhouse, so she volunteered to lead the presentation.
The new project is outside my wheelhouse, but I'm eager to learn more about it.
He was asked to comment on politics, which is definitely not in his wheelhouse.

Synset Examples

as the campaign swings to the south, that should be right in his wheelhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialty - a particular area in which someone is an expert
  • Synonym: forte - an activity or area in which someone excels
  • Antonym: weakness - an area in which someone is not skilled or knowledgeable

Etymology

Originally refers to the part of a boat where the steering wheel is housed, later extended metaphorically to denote a zone of control or proficiency.
