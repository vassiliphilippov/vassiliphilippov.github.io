Stenotomus chrysops

1. NOUN

/ stɛˌnɑːtəməs ˈkraɪsɒps / (AmE), / stɛˌnɒtəməs ˈkraɪsɒps / (BrE)
A species of porgy, a fish found in Atlantic coastal waters of North America from South Carolina to Maine, valued as a panfish.
a species of porgy fish found in Atlantic waters

Examples

The Stenotomus chrysops is commonly caught by recreational anglers along the eastern coast of the United States.
Marine biologists study the life cycle of Stenotomus chrysops to monitor the health of Atlantic coastal ecosystems.
At the seafood market, freshly caught Stenotomus chrysops are often sold under the name 'scup.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scup - common name for Stenotomus chrysops, a North American fish
  • Synonym: porgy - common name for fish in the family Sparidae

Etymology

From Greek *stenos* (narrow) + *tomos* (cut) and Greek *chrysops* (golden eye); scientific binomial name.
