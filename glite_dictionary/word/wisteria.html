wisteria

1. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A climbing woody vine of the genus Wisteria, native to Asia and North America, with long drooping clusters of bluish, purple, white, or pinkish flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
climbing flowering vine; ornamental plant

Examples

The wisteria climbed the stone wall, covering it with cascades of purple blossoms.
A mature wisteria can live for decades and become a stunning centerpiece in any garden.
They trained the wisteria along the pergola, where it shaded the patio in summer.

Etymology

From the genus name Wisteria, named after American anatomist Caspar Wistar (1761–1818).

2. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A climbing shrub with pendulous clusters of fragrant pale bluish-lilac flowers, belonging to the genus Wisteria, often grown as an ornamental plant.
ornamental flowering climbing plant

Examples

The garden looked beautiful in spring when the wisteria was in full bloom.
A canopy of purple wisteria shaded the patio, filling the air with a sweet scent.
She planted a wisteria vine to grow along the trellis outside her window.

Etymology

From New Latin Wisteria, named after American physician Caspar Wistar (1761–1818).
