Reich

1. NOUN

/ raɪk / (AmE), / raɪk / (BrE)
(historical) The German state, especially during periods when the country was ruled by an imperial or authoritarian regime.
the German state (historical period)

Examples

The Third Reich is infamous for its role in World War II and the Holocaust.
Historians have studied the collapse of the Weimar Reich extensively.
Many buildings from the old Reich era still stand in Berlin today.

Etymology

From German 'Reich' meaning 'realm' or 'empire'.
