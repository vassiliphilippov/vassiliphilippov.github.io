chao ta

1. ADJ

/ tʃaʊˈtɑ / (AmE), / tʃaʊˈtɑː / (BrE)
(informal) In a state of chaos or great disorder; very disorganized or confused.
in a chaotic or disorganized state
informal

Examples

After the storm, the garden was chao ta and needed hours of cleaning.
My schedule was chao ta this week, with meetings back-to-back every day.
By midnight, the kitchen was chao ta after everyone tried to cook their own meals.

Synset Examples

Traffic was chao ta during the rush hour.
Her room was chao ta after the party, clothes everywhere.
The market was chao ta with vendors shouting everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - completely disordered and unpredictable
  • Synonym: messy - untidy or disordered; not neat

Etymology

From Singlish and colloquial Malay, adapted from Hokkien or Mandarin Chinese, literally meaning 'burnt' (figurative: messed up).
