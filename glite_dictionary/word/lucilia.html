Lucilia

1. NOUN

/ luːˈsɪliə / (AmE), / luːˈsɪliə / (BrE)
a genus of greenbottle flies, noted for their metallic green coloration and role in decomposition
a genus of greenbottle flies

Examples

Lucilia species are commonly found around decaying matter and are important in forensic entomology.
The metallic green color of Lucilia flies makes them easily distinguishable in the field.
Some farmers use Lucilia larvae in maggot therapy to clean wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenbottle - any of several metallic green flies, especially of the genus Lucilia
  • Degree Lower: Calliphora - a related genus of bluebottle flies

Etymology

From Latin Lucilia, a feminine given name, used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ luˈsɪliə / (AmE), / luˈsɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of metallic green blow flies commonly known as greenbottle flies, found worldwide and often associated with decaying matter.
genus of greenbottle flies

Examples

The metallic-green flies of the genus Lucilia are commonly seen near decaying organic matter.
Species of Lucilia play a significant role in forensic entomology by helping to estimate the time of death.
Some Lucilia flies are used in maggot therapy to clean wounds in medical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calliphora - a related genus of blow flies, sometimes called bluebottles

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucilia', used as a genus name in taxonomy.
