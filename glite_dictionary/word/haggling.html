haggling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
an argument or discussion, especially about the price of something, in which people try to reach an agreement that suits them both
bargaining or arguing, especially over price

Examples

There was a lot of haggling over the price of the antique vase at the market.
After fifteen minutes of haggling, they finally agreed on a fair deal.
She disliked the endless haggling that came with shopping in street markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargaining - discussion in order to reach agreement on price or conditions
  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement

Etymology

From Middle English 'haglen', probably of Scandinavian origin.
