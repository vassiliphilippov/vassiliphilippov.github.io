Tupaiidae

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈpaɪ.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tjuːˈpaɪ.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, squirrel-like mammals known as tree shrews, native to Southeast Asia and previously sometimes classified as prosimian primates.
family of mammals; tree shrews

Examples

Scientists have long debated whether Tupaiidae should be classified as primates or in their own order.
The tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia are home to many species within the Tupaiidae family.
Research on Tupaiidae has provided valuable insights into the evolutionary relationships among mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - small, squirrel-like mammal in the family Tupaiidae

Etymology

From Latinized form of the Malay word 'tupai' (squirrel) with the zoological family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ tuːˈpeɪ.i.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tjuːˈpeɪ.ɪ.iːˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, squirrel-like mammals known as tree shrews, sometimes classified with primates.
family of mammals known as tree shrews

Examples

The Tupaiidae family includes several species of agile, tree-dwelling mammals.
Recent studies debate whether Tupaiidae should be classified as true primates or in a separate group.
Compared to other small mammals, members of the Tupaiidae have a unique combination of traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - small, squirrel-like mammal native to Southeast Asia
  • Degree Higher: primate - order of mammals including lemurs, monkeys, and humans

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Tupaia + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
