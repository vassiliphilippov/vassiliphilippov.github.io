nonaggression

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈɡreʃ.ən / (BrE)
a policy or principle of not initiating hostilities or aggression, especially between nations
policy of not initiating hostilities

Examples

The two countries maintained a strict policy of nonaggression throughout the negotiations.
A nonaggression agreement was proposed to reduce the risk of conflict in the region.
Leaders emphasized the importance of nonaggression in their joint statement after the summit.

Synset Examples

they signed a nonaggression pact

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior or attitudes

Etymology

Formed from Latin prefix non- (not) + aggression (from Latin aggressio, attack).
