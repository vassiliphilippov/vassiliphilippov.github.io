luxuriously

1. ADV

/ ləkˈʒʊriəsli / (AmE), / lʌɡˈʒʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that involves great comfort, wealth, or elegance.
in a rich or comfortable manner

Examples

They dined luxuriously at the city's finest restaurant.
She stretched out luxuriously on the silk sheets, enjoying the warmth of the sun.
The couple traveled luxuriously through Europe in a private train carriage.

Synset Examples

he lives high

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simply - in a plain or unadorned manner
  • Synonym: lavishly - in an extravagant or generous manner

Etymology

From 'luxurious' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'luxuria' (excess).

2. ADV

/ lʌgˈʒʊriəsli / (AmE), / lʌkˈʃʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely comfortable, elegant, or enjoyable, involving great expense or indulgence.
in a luxurious, indulgent way

Examples

She stretched out luxuriously in her velvet chair, enjoying the warmth of the fire.
The guests dined luxuriously on fine china and crystal under the chandelier.
He slept luxuriously in the king-sized bed, wrapped in silk sheets.

Synset Examples

she sprawled out luxuriously on the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - very comfortable, elegant, or expensive; involving luxury

Etymology

From 'luxurious' + '-ly'. 'Luxurious' from Latin 'luxuriosus', from 'luxuria' (excess, luxury).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00511054-r
Synset POS: r
