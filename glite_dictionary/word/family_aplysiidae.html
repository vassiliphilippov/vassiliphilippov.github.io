family Aplysiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli æˌplɪsiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli aplɪsɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine gastropod mollusks commonly known as sea hares, characterized by a soft, often slug-like body and two rhinophores on their head.
family of sea hares (marine mollusks)

Examples

The family Aplysiidae includes several species of sea hares found in temperate and tropical oceans.
Many marine biologists study the nervous system of animals in the family Aplysiidae because of their large neurons.
Some members of the family Aplysiidae can release a purple ink as a defense mechanism when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea hare - marine mollusk of the family Aplysiidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Aplysiidae', based on the genus 'Aplysia', from Greek 'aplysia' (unwashed thing), alluding to their slippery bodies.
