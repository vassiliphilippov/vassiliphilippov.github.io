jargoon

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑrˈɡun / (AmE), / dʒɑːˈɡuːn / (BrE)
a colorless, pale yellow, or smoky variety of zircon, used as a gemstone
a variety of zircon used as a gemstone

Examples

The jeweler examined the jargoon closely to assess its clarity and color.
Among her collection was a beautiful jargoon ring set in silver.
Some collectors seek rare gemstones like jargoon for their unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zircon - a mineral used as a gemstone; the base mineral of jargoon

Etymology

From French 'jargon' (colorless zircon), from Italian 'giargone', ultimately from Persian 'zar-gūn' (gold-colored).
