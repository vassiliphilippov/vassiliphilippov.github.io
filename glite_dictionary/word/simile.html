simile

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪməli / (AmE), / ˈsɪmɪli / (BrE)
a figure of speech that compares two different things using the words 'like' or 'as'
figure of speech comparing with 'like' or 'as'

Examples

She used a simile to compare his smile to sunshine.
The poem is full of vivid similes and metaphors.
Teachers often ask students to identify a simile in a piece of writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From Latin *simile* (likeness, comparison), from *similis* (like, similar).
