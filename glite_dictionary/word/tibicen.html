Tibicen

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪbɪˌsɛn / (AmE), / ˈtɪbɪˌsɛn / (BrE)
A former genus name for some cicadas commonly known as harvest flies.
former genus of cicadas (harvest flies)

Examples

The genus Tibicen was once used for many North American cicadas.
Entomologists now classify most Tibicen species under the genus Neotibicen.
The song of the Tibicen cicada is a familiar sound in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - a large insect known for its loud song

Etymology

From Latin 'tibicen', meaning 'flute player' or 'piper', alluding to the cicada's sound.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪbɪsɛn / (AmE), / ˈtɪbɪsɛn / (BrE)
A former genus of cicadas commonly known as harvest flies, whose species are now mostly classified under other genera.
a former genus of cicadas (harvest flies)

Examples

The loud song of the Tibicen can be heard on summer afternoons.
Many North American species formerly belonged to the genus Tibicen.
The taxonomy of Tibicen has been revised several times by entomologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - a large insect known for loud, distinctive songs

Etymology

From Latin *tibicen* (piper, flute-player), referring to the sound made by the cicadas.
