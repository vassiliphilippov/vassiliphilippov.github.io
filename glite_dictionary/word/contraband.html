contraband

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːntrəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəˌbænd / (BrE)
goods that are imported, exported, or possessed illegally, especially because they are prohibited by law
illegally imported or prohibited goods

Examples

The customs officers discovered contraband hidden in the cargo hold of the ship.
Possession of contraband can lead to heavy fines and imprisonment.
During the inspection, several boxes of contraband cigarettes were seized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggled goods - goods brought into a country illegally
  • Antonym: legal goods - goods that are imported or exported lawfully

Etymology

From Italian contrabbando, from contra (against) + bando (proclamation).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.trəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.bænd / (BrE)
(of goods) distributed, imported, or sold illegally; prohibited by law from being traded.
illegally distributed or sold

Examples

Some contraband cigarettes were found hidden in the shipment.
The police seized several boxes of contraband alcohol during the raid.
Shops selling contraband electronics often operate in secret.

Synset Examples

the black economy pays no taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law
  • Antonym: licit - permitted by law

Etymology

From Italian *contrabando*, from Latin *contra* (against) + *bannum* (proclamation, ban).
