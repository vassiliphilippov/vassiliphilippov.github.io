to reorganise

1. VERB

/ riˈɔr.ɡə.naɪz / (AmE), / riˈɔː.ɡə.naɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something again in a new way, especially after a change or setback
arrange or organize again
british

Examples

After the merger, the company had to reorganise its entire management structure.
The school decided to reorganise the timetable following the disruption caused by the storm.
She took some time to reorganise her priorities after a difficult year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reorganize - to arrange or organize again (American spelling)
  • Degree Lower: to organise - to arrange systematically; to order

Etymology

From re- (again) + organise, ultimately from French organiser.

2. VERB

/ riˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to organize something again or in a different way, especially to improve efficiency or adapt to new conditions
organize again or differently
british

Examples

The management decided to reorganise the entire department structure last year.
If the company is to survive, it must reorganise its finances immediately.
After the merger, we had to reorganise our teams to avoid redundancy.

Synset Examples

We must reorganize the company if we don't want to go under

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reorganize - organize again or differently (American spelling)

Etymology

From re- (again) + organise, modelled on the French réorganiser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02437970-v
Synset POS: v
