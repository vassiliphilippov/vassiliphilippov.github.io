pyogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪ.oʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪ.əˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a bacterium or infection) producing pus.
producing pus (medical)
medical

Examples

A pyogenic infection requires prompt medical treatment to prevent complications.
Certain bacteria, such as Staphylococcus aureus, are well known for their pyogenic nature.
The wound became pyogenic and started to discharge thick, yellow pus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppurative - causing or associated with the production of pus

Etymology

From Greek 'puon' (pus) + '-genic' (producing).
