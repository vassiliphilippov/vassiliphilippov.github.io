to abscise

1. VERB

/ æbˈsaɪz / (AmE), / æbˈsiːz / (BrE)
(of a plant or part) to remove or separate by means of abscission; to shed or cause to fall off, especially naturally or as a result of a physiological process.
remove or separate by abscission

Examples

The tree begins to abscise its leaves in autumn as the temperature drops.
Certain plants abscise their fruit early as a survival mechanism.
Researchers studied how hormones can prompt cells to abscise damaged tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to let fall or drop (leaves, skin, etc.) by natural process

Etymology

From Latin abscisus, past participle of abscindere, meaning 'to cut off'.

2. VERB

/ æbˈsaɪz / (AmE), / æbˈsaɪz / (BrE)
(of a plant organ) to detach or fall off naturally, usually following the formation of a scar at the base.
to shed (plant parts) naturally

Examples

Many trees abscise their leaves in autumn as temperatures drop.
The plant will abscise unhealthy fruit to conserve resources.
After flowering, the rosebush abscised its petals and prepared for dormancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to lose or let fall (leaves, hair, skin, etc.) naturally

Etymology

From Latin abscindere, meaning 'to cut away', from ab- 'from' + scindere 'to cut'.
