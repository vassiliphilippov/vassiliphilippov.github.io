Dendroica tigrina

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdrɔɪkə taɪˈɡriːnə / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɔɪkə tɪˈɡriːnə / (BrE)
A species of North American wood warbler, the Cape May warbler, noted for its olive green and yellow striped plumage with black markings.
a North American wood warbler species

Examples

The Dendroica tigrina is often spotted during spring migration in the northern United States.
Birdwatchers delight in observing the striking plumage of Dendroica tigrina among the conifers.
The diet of Dendroica tigrina includes insects and nectar gathered from flowering trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cape May warbler - Common name for Dendroica tigrina, a North American wood warbler with distinctive striped plumage

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Dendroica' from Greek 'dendron' (tree) + 'oikos' (dwelling), 'tigrina' from Latin 'tigris' (tiger), referring to stripes.
