jumbled

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌmbəld / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmbəld / (BrE)
mixed together in a confused or disorderly way; not arranged neatly or clearly
mixed up or disorderly

Examples

The contents of the drawer were completely jumbled after the move.
She handed me a jumbled stack of papers to organize.
His thoughts were so jumbled that he struggled to explain what happened.

Synset Examples

a disorderly pile of clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disordered - lacking order or organization
  • Antonym: tidy - arranged neatly and in order

Etymology

From the verb 'jumble', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'jumble' (noun) meaning a confused mixture.
