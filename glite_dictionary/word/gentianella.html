Gentianella

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃəˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tɪəˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small herbs in the family Gentianaceae, with flowers resembling gentians; sometimes included within the genus Gentiana.
a genus of small gentian-like herbs

Examples

Several species of Gentianella bloom in alpine meadows during the summer.
The botanist discovered a rare Gentianella near the edge of the forest.
Unlike true gentians, Gentianella flowers often have shorter stems and smaller blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gentiana - genus of flowering plants including the true gentians

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'Gentiana', from Latin 'gentiana' (gentian).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃəˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃɪˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs in the family Gentianaceae, with flowers resembling those of the gentian.
genus of herbaceous plants with gentian-like flowers

Examples

Gentianella species are found in alpine meadows across Europe and South America.
The flowers of Gentianella are often mistaken for those of true gentians.
Botanists reclassified several plants into the Gentianella genus after genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gentiana - a related genus of flowering plants known as gentians

Etymology

Diminutive of Latin gentiana (gentian), used in botanical Latin.
