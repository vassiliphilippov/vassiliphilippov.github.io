Saone

1. PROPN

/ soʊn / (AmE), / soʊn / (BrE)
a river in eastern France which rises in Lorraine and flows south to join the Rhone.
river in eastern France, tributary of the Rhone

Examples

The Saone is famous for its scenic beauty and excellent boating opportunities.
Lyon lies at the confluence of the Saone and the Rhone rivers.
Many vineyards can be found along the banks of the Saone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhone - major river in southeastern France, joins the Saone at Lyon

Etymology

From French Saône, from Latin Sāona.
