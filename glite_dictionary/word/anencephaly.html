anencephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌænənˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌænɛnˈsɛfəˌli / (BrE)
A severe congenital condition in which a major part of the brain, skull, and scalp is absent at birth due to incomplete neural tube development.
birth defect: missing parts of brain and skull
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the baby with anencephaly after an ultrasound revealed the absence of a major portion of the brain.
Because anencephaly is incompatible with life, the parents were given genetic counseling before making decisions.
Prenatal screening has improved the early detection of anencephaly and other neural tube defects.

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + enkephalos (brain).
