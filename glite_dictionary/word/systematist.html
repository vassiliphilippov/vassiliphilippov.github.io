systematist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmətɪst / (BrE)
a biologist who specializes in the classification and organization of organisms based on their structure, origin, and behavior
biologist specializing in organism classification

Examples

As a systematist, Dr. Lee contributed to the discovery and classification of several previously unknown plant species.
Modern systematists often rely on genetic data to clarify relationships among organisms.
The work of a systematist is crucial for understanding biodiversity and evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomist - a scientist who classifies organisms

Etymology

From system + -atist, based on systematics.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who arranges or organizes things into a system or order
a person who organizes things systematically

Examples

Marie was a systematist who always created clear outlines for every project.
As a systematist, he brought structure to what had previously been a chaotic collection of ideas.
Throughout history, the most influential scientists have often been effective systematists.

Synset Examples

Aristotle was a great orderer of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges or structures things
  • Antonym: disorganizer - a person who creates disorder, the opposite of a systematist

Etymology

From New Latin systematista, from systema (system) + -ist.
