zip

1. NOUN

/ zɪp / (AmE), / zɪp / (BrE)
a fastening device consisting of two strips of fabric with interlocking metal or plastic teeth and a sliding tab to open or close them
fastening device with sliding tab

Examples

She fixed the broken zip on her jacket before leaving the house.
The zip on my backpack got stuck halfway up.
He struggled to close the zip on his suitcase because it was too full.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zipper - a fastening device with interlocking teeth, especially in American English

Etymology

Shortened form of 'zipper', originally a trademark from the early 20th century.

3. VERB

/ zɪp / (AmE), / zɪp / (BrE)
to fasten or close something using a zipper
fasten using a zipper

Examples

She quickly zipped her suitcase before leaving for the airport.
Please zip your backpack so nothing falls out.
After putting on his coat, he forgot to zip it up and felt chilly.

Synset Examples

Zip up your jacket — it's cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unzip - to open something using a zipper

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound made by a zipper.

/ zɪp / (AmE), / zɪp / (BrE)
to move very quickly or with great speed
move very quickly

Examples

The children zipped through the hallways on their scooters.

She zipped down the mountainside on her snowboard, leaving everyone behind.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - move with urgent speed

Etymology

Imitative of the sound associated with something moving swiftly; also relates to the noun 'zip' (a sudden movement or energy).
