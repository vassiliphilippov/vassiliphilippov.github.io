soundable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊndəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndəbl / (BrE)
(of depth) able to be measured for depth, especially with a sounding line or other equipment.
able to be measured for depth

Examples

The river was only soundable near the shore, where the depth was accessible.
Many parts of the lake remain soundable, allowing regular safety checks by boat crews.
Before mapping the channel, they confirmed it was soundable with their equipment.

Etymology

From 'sound' (to measure depth) + '-able'.
