Dacrydium colensoi

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækˈraɪdiəm koʊˈlɛnsoʊ.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌdækˈraɪdiəm kəˈlɛnsəʊ.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of New Zealand silver pine with a conical shape and long slender flexible branches, adapted to cold, wet summers and high altitudes.
species of New Zealand silver pine

Examples

The dense forests at high altitude are dominated by Dacrydium colensoi and other native species.
Scientists are concerned about the impact of climate change on Dacrydium colensoi populations in New Zealand.
The slender branches of Dacrydium colensoi help it survive in the cold, wet mountain environments.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'dakryon' (tear), referring to the resin; species name after William Colenso, a New Zealand botanist.
