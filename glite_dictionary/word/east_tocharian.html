East Tocharian

1. NOUN

/ iːst təˈkɛəriən / (AmE), / iːst təˈkɑːriən / (BrE)
the eastern dialect of the extinct Tocharian language, spoken in the Qarašähär and Turpan regions of ancient Xinjiang, China
eastern dialect of Tocharian language

Examples

Scholars have found ancient manuscripts written in East Tocharian in the Turpan region.
Linguists often compare East Tocharian and West Tocharian to study their phonological differences.
The study of East Tocharian provides valuable insights into early Indo-European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tocharian - the Indo-European language group once spoken in the Tarim Basin, China
  • Antonym: West Tocharian - the western dialect of the Tocharian language

Etymology

Formed from 'east' and 'Tocharian', the name of the ancient Indo-European language group.
