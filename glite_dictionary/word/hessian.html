hessian

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈhɛsɪən / (BrE)
(historical) A man's high boot of the early 19th century, often adorned with tassels.
19th-century man's high tasseled boot
archaic

Examples

The officer strode into the room, his hessians gleaming in the candlelight.
Fashionable men of the Regency period commonly wore hessians with their trousers.
He polished his hessians every morning before riding out.

Etymology

Named after the Hessian soldiers from Hesse, Germany, who wore similar boots.
