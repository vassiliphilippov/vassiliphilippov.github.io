dacoity

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkɔɪti / (AmE), / dəˈkɔɪti / (BrE)
Robbery committed by a gang of armed bandits, especially as traditionally practiced in South Asia.
armed gang robbery (esp. in South Asia)

Examples

The village suffered several dacoity attacks during the summer, leaving residents fearful.
The police launched a special operation to curb dacoity in the region.
He was arrested and charged with involvement in a violent dacoity last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property illegally from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From Hindi ḍākaitī (robbery by a gang of bandits), from ḍākā (robber).
