Hypochoeris

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊˈkɪr.ɪs / (AmE), / hɪ.pəˈkɪər.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, known for their milky sap and including several species of widespread weeds.
genus of flowering plants with milky sap

Examples

Hypochoeris species are commonly found in meadows and pastures across Europe and America.
The yellow flowers of Hypochoeris can easily be mistaken for dandelions.
Gardeners often remove Hypochoeris from lawns because it is considered a weed.

Etymology

From Greek hypochoeris, from hypo- (under) and choiros (pig), possibly referring to its growth near where pigs fed.

2. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊˈkɪr.ɪs / (AmE), / hɪ.pəˈkɪə.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as cat's-ear, widely distributed and including several weeds.
genus of herbaceous plants (cat's-ear)

Examples

The Hypochoeris genus is commonly found in grasslands across Europe and Asia.
Many gardeners consider some Hypochoeris species to be persistent weeds.
Researchers collected samples from different Hypochoeris populations to study their genetic variation.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Hypochoeris, based on Greek hypokhoeir (field plant closely related to sow-thistle).
