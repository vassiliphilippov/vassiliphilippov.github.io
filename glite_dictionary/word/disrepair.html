disrepair

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.rɪˈper / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.rɪˈpeə / (BrE)
The state of being in poor condition due to neglect or lack of maintenance; in need of repair.
poor condition from neglect

Examples

The old house had fallen into disrepair after years of neglect.
Much of the railway network is now in a state of disrepair.
The garden wall was crumbling and clearly in disrepair.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ruin - Complete destruction or severe deterioration of something; often used for buildings or structures far beyond disrepair
  • Antonym: repair - The act of restoring something to good condition

Etymology

From Old French desrepair, based on the verb 'repair'.
