Tenrecidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnˈrɛsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛnˈrɛsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small insectivorous mammals native to Madagascar and parts of Africa, including the tenrecs and their extinct relatives
family of tenrecs (insectivorous mammals)

Examples

The family Tenrecidae exhibits a remarkable diversity of size and shape among its members.
Madagascar is the primary home of the mammals classified as Tenrecidae.
Researchers have discovered several extinct species belonging to Tenrecidae in fossil records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenrec - a small, insectivorous mammal native to Madagascar and Africa

Etymology

From modern Latin Tenrecidae, based on the genus Tenrec.

2. NOUN

/ tɛnˈrɛsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɛnˈrɛsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized mammals native to Madagascar and parts of Africa, including tenrecs and their extinct relatives.
family of mammals including tenrecs

Examples

The Tenrecidae display a remarkable diversity of forms and ecological adaptations.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Tenrecidae family in Madagascar.
Unlike most other mammals, members of the Tenrecidae can have very low body temperatures.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Tenrec (the type genus) and the suffix -idae used for animal family names.
