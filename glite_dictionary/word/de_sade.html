de Sade

1. PROPN

/ də ˈsɑd / (AmE), / də ˈsɑːd / (BrE)
The Marquis de Sade (1740–1814), French soldier and writer noted for works describing sexual cruelty, from whose name the term 'sadism' is derived.
French writer associated with sadism

Examples

Many scholars have analyzed the controversial works of de Sade for their impact on literature and philosophy.
The name de Sade is often mentioned in discussions of extreme themes in French history.
Some critics believe that de Sade's writings challenge conventional moral boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadism - deriving pleasure from inflicting pain, term derived from de Sade's name

Etymology

French surname, notably associated with Donatien Alphonse François, Marquis de Sade.
