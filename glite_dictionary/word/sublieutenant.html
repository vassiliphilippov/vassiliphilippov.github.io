sublieutenant

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌluːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / sʌb.lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
an officer ranking immediately below a lieutenant, especially in the navy or certain military forces
military rank below lieutenant

Examples

He was promoted from sublieutenant to lieutenant after just two years of service.
As a sublieutenant, she was responsible for leading her small team aboard the ship.
The navy ceremony honored the new sublieutenant and welcomed her to the officer corps.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lieutenant - an officer ranking just above sublieutenant

Etymology

From sub- (meaning 'under') and lieutenant, modeled on French 'sous-lieutenant'.
