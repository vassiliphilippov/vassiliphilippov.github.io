Polystichum lonchitis

1. PROPN

/ poʊˈlɪstɪkəm lɒnˈkaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm lɒnˈkɪtɪs / (BrE)
An evergreen fern native to Europe and North America, commonly cultivated for its attractive, leathery fronds.
a species of evergreen fern

Examples

The shaded garden was filled with Polystichum lonchitis thriving beneath the trees.
Many gardeners value Polystichum lonchitis for its hardy, evergreen foliage.
Researchers studied how Polystichum lonchitis adapts to different soil conditions in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Polystichum' meaning 'many rows' (referring to sori arrangement) and species name 'lonchitis' from Greek 'lonchitis', meaning 'spear-shaped'.
