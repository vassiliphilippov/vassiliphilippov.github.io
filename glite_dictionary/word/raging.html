raging

/ ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a natural force, feeling, or activity) very intense, violent, or forceful.
very intense or violent

Examples

The raging storm toppled trees and caused widespread damage across the city.
After hours of raging debate, the meeting finally ended in a stalemate.
The hikers struggled to cross the raging river as the current grew stronger.

Synset Examples

the fighting became hot and heavy
a hot engagement
a raging battle
the river became a raging torrent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - having strong force or feeling
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling violence, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

Present participle of 'rage', from Old French 'ragier'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially of natural forces) showing extreme violence or intensity, as if expressing anger.
extremely violent or intense (esp. of nature)

Examples

The raging storm knocked out power across the entire city.
Waves crashed against the shore as the raging sea threatened to flood the harbor.
They could hear the raging wind howling through the trees all night.

Synset Examples

angry clouds on the horizon
furious winds
the raging sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furious - extremely angry or violent (used similarly of storms, winds, etc.)
  • Antonym: calm - not showing violence or disturbance (esp. of weather or the sea)

Etymology

From English 'rage' + '-ing'. 'Rage' from Old French *rage*, from Latin *rabies* (madness, fury).
