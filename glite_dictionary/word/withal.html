withal

1. ADV

/ wɪˈðɔl / (AmE), / wɪˈðɔːl / (BrE)
In addition; besides; together with this (used especially in archaic or literary contexts).
archaic: in addition; besides
archaic

Examples

She was wise, and withal kind to all she met.
His speech was harsh, withal honest and sincere.
Though weary, he was withal determined to complete the task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nonetheless
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; despite anything to the contrary

Etymology

From Middle English 'withal', from Old English 'wiþ eall' (with all).

2. ADV

/ wɪˈðɔl / (AmE), / wɪˈðɔːl / (BrE)
Nevertheless; in spite of that; all the same (used to introduce a concession).
despite that; nevertheless
archaic

Examples

The journey was long and perilous; withal, they pressed on toward their goal.
He may be strict, withal he is fair and just in his dealings.
Her dress was simple, but withal it had an elegance that caught everyone's eye.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it

he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nonetheless
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; nevertheless
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'mid eall' meaning 'with all'.
