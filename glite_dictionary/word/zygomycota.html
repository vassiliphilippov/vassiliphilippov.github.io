Zygomycota

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌɡoʊmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / zaɪˌɡəʊmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a former division of fungi characterized by the production of zygospores during sexual reproduction
a former group of fungi producing zygospores
medical

Examples

The bread mold belongs to the Zygomycota, a group known for their rapidly growing hyphae.
Scientists used to classify these fungi as Zygomycota based on their reproductive structures.
Advances in genetics have led to the reorganization of the Zygomycota into different phyla.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fungus - a group of organisms including molds, mushrooms, and yeasts

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek zygōn (to join, yoke) + mycota (fungi).

2. NOUN

/ zaɪɡoʊmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / zaɪɡəʊmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a division of fungi characterized by the formation of sexually produced zygospores, including many molds and related species
division of fungi producing zygospores
medical

Examples

Some common bread molds belong to the Zygomycota.
The Zygomycota are known for producing thick-walled zygospores during sexual reproduction.
Researchers have reclassified some species previously placed within the Zygomycota.

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) + 'mykes' (fungus), referring to the joined spores.
