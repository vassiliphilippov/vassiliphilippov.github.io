quartervine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrtərˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəˌvaɪn / (BrE)
A woody flowering vine native to the southern United States, whose stems display a cross-shaped pattern in cross-section.
woody flowering vine of southern US

Examples

The stems of the quartervine are easily recognized by the cross-shaped pattern visible when cut.
In the southern United States, quartervine is often found climbing along fences and trees.
Botanists use the unique cross-section of the quartervine’s stem to identify the species in the field.
