repartee

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpɑːrˈti / (AmE), / ˌrɛpɑːˈtiː / (BrE)
quick and witty or clever replies in conversation; the ability to reply in this way
quick, witty replies

Examples

The comedian's repartee with the audience kept everyone laughing throughout the show.
Her sharp repartee made her popular at dinner parties.
They admired his skillful repartee during the spirited debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to use words in a clever and amusing way
  • Synonym: banter - playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks

Etymology

From French 'repartie' (replied sharply), from 'repartir' (to retort).
