stoichiometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌstoʊɪkiˈɑmətri / (AmE), / ˌstɔɪkiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the calculation of the relative quantities of reactants and products involved in a chemical reaction.
quantitative relationships in chemical reactions
medical

Examples

A solid understanding of stoichiometry is essential for balancing chemical equations.
She used stoichiometry to determine the amount of product that would form.
In the exam, questions on stoichiometry often require careful unit conversions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - the scientific study of the properties and behavior of matter

Etymology

From Greek 'stoikheion' (element) + 'metron' (measure).
