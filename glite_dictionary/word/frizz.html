frizz

1. NOUN

/ frɪz / (AmE), / frɪz / (BrE)
A mass of small, tight curls or the condition of being formed into such curls, typically referring to hair.
hair in tight curls; curly, frizzy state

Examples

After walking in the rain, her hair turned into a wild frizz.
Humidity often leaves my hair in a stubborn frizz by the afternoon.
She used special products to tame the frizz in her curls.

Synset Examples

her hair was in a frizz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curl - a piece of hair that forms a spiral shape
  • Antonym: smoothness - the state of being even and without curls or waves (as opposed to frizz)

Etymology

Probably from the French 'friser' (to curl), related to 'frisette'.

2. VERB

/ frɪz / (AmE), / frɪz / (BrE)
to form (hair or fiber) into small, tight curls or to become tightly curled
to form tight curls, especially in hair

Examples

She likes to frizz her hair before going out for parties.
Humidity can frizz even the straightest hair.
The stylist will frizz the ends to give your hairstyle extra volume.

Synset Examples

crimp hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimp - to make small regular folds or waves in something, especially hair

Etymology

From French *friser* (to curl the hair), possibly from Old French *fris* (curl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01226397-v
Synset POS: v
