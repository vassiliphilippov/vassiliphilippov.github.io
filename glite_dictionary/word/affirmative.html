affirmative

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɜrmətɪv / (AmE), / əˈfɜːmətɪv / (BrE)
expressing praise, approval, or agreement; indicating consent or a positive response
expressing approval or agreement

Examples

She gave an affirmative answer, smiling as she nodded her head.
The committee's report was highly affirmative of the new policy.
The teacher responded with an affirmative gesture to show her approval.

Synset Examples

approbative criticism
an affirmative nod

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approving - showing approval
  • Antonym: negative - expressing denial or refusal

Etymology

From Latin 'affirmativus', from 'affirmare' (to affirm).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɝːmətɪv / (AmE), / əˈfɜːmətɪv / (BrE)
A word or statement expressing agreement or consent, especially as a formal reply of 'yes'.
a reply expressing agreement or 'yes'

Examples

She responded with an affirmative when asked if she understood the rules.
The committee voted in the affirmative, approving the proposal unanimously.
His answer was a clear affirmative to the question.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: negative - a word or statement expressing disagreement or 'no'

Etymology

From Latin 'affirmativus', from 'affirmare' (to assert).
