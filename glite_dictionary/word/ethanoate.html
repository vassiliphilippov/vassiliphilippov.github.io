ethanoate

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈθænoʊˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈθanəʊˌeɪt / (BrE)
A salt or ester derived from acetic acid, especially one containing the group CH3COO.
salt or ester of acetic acid

Examples

Sodium ethanoate is commonly used in heating pads and hand warmers.
The reaction produced an ethanoate and water as byproducts.
Many artificial flavors are created by combining alcohols with ethanoate esters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetate - a salt or ester of acetic acid; common synonym

Etymology

From 'ethanoic acid' (IUPAC name for acetic acid) + '-ate', denoting a salt or ester.
