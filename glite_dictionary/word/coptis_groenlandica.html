Coptis groenlandica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑptɪs ɡroʊnˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkɒptɪs ɡrɛnˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant native to North American woodlands, with trifoliate leaves, yellow rootstock, and white flowers.
North American woodland plant species

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Coptis groenlandica, its white flowers standing out among the moss.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Coptis groenlandica in the remote woodland.
She carefully dug near the roots of Coptis groenlandica to observe its yellow rootstock.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Coptis' is the genus name, and 'groenlandica' means 'of Greenland'.
