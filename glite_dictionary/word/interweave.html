to interweave

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈwiv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈwiːv / (BrE)
to combine or blend two or more things by weaving them together, or in a manner similar to weaving
combine by weaving together

Examples

The artist chose to interweave strands of silk and cotton in the tapestry.
In her novel, the author skillfully interweaves historical facts with fiction.
Different melodies interweave to create a rich, harmonious sound throughout the piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or weave things together

Etymology

From Old English 'wefan' (to weave), with the prefix 'inter-' from Latin, meaning among or between.
