paramedical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpær.əˈmɛd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈmɛd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to people trained to assist doctors and nurses, especially in emergency medical situations, but who are not fully qualified medical doctors
trained to assist medical staff

Examples

He completed his training as a paramedical worker and joined the search and rescue team.
The organization provides paramedical support during sporting events and festivals.
Several paramedical staff assisted the nurses during the disaster response.

Synset Examples

ambulance drivers are paramedical to give emergency medical aid
ambulance drivers are paramedical personnel

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: medical - relating to the science of medicine or treatment of illness and injuries

Etymology

From French 'paramédical', from Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'medical'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈmɛdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌparəˈmɛdɪkəl / (BrE)
a person trained to assist medical professionals and provide emergency medical treatment, such as a paramedic or other emergency responder
person trained to assist medical professionals

Examples

The injured hikers were treated by a paramedical before the ambulance arrived.
Many paramedicals work closely with doctors and nurses in emergency situations.
He was praised for his quick thinking and professionalism as a paramedical on the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paramedic - a person trained to provide emergency medical care

Etymology

From para- (beside, alongside) + medical, indicating auxiliary role to medical professionals.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10417875-n
Synset POS: n
