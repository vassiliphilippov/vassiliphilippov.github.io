Zoisia

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɔɪziə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪzɪə / (BrE)
a genus of lawn grasses native to southeastern Asia and New Zealand, grown especially in warm regions for turf and lawns
genus of warm-region lawn grasses

Examples

Many golf courses in the southern United States use Zoisia for its durability in warm climates.
The gardener recommended planting Zoisia to create a low-maintenance lawn.
Unlike some other grasses, Zoisia can tolerate both heat and drought conditions.

Etymology

Named after Austrian botanist Karl von Zois.
