Brueghel

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɔɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔɪɡəl / (BrE)
(1525–1569) A Flemish painter renowned for his detailed landscapes and peasant scenes, considered one of the greatest artists of the Northern Renaissance.
Flemish landscape and genre painter (1525–1569)

Examples

Many art historians regard Brueghel as a master of depicting rural life.
The museum's collection includes several original works by Brueghel.
Brueghel's paintings are famous for their intricate detail and lively compositions.

Etymology

Dutch family name; from the town of Breugel in the Netherlands.
