dioecian

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈiːʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈiːsɪən / (BrE)
having male and female reproductive organs on separate plants or animals
male and female organs on separate individuals

Examples

Many holly trees are dioecian, requiring both male and female plants for successful fruiting.
Some species of fish are dioecian, producing distinct male and female individuals.
Unlike monoecious corn, spinach is a dioecian plant with separate sexes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monoecious - having both male and female reproductive organs on the same individual

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'oikos' (house), via New Latin 'dioecius'.
