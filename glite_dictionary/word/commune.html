commune

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːm.juːn / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.mjuːn / (BrE)
the smallest administrative district in some European countries, such as France or Italy, having its own local government
smallest administrative district (e.g., in France, Italy)

Examples

Many French towns are governed as a commune with their own local council.
The commune of Rome is responsible for providing many public services.
Each commune in Italy elects a mayor and a municipal council.

Etymology

From French 'commune', from Medieval Latin 'commūnia' (a municipal corporation or community).

2. VERB

/ kəˈmjun / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːn / (BrE)
(in the Catholic Church) to receive Communion; to partake in the sacrament of the Eucharist.
receive Communion in the Catholic Church

Examples

She chose to commune at the early morning mass on Sunday.
After preparing himself spiritually, he was ready to commune and receive the sacrament.
Those wishing to commune must first participate in the rite of confession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - to receive the Eucharist (archaic variant and synonym in religious sense)

Etymology

From Latin *communicare* (to share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00762447-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kəˈmjun / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːn / (BrE)
to share an intimate or spiritual feeling or connection with someone or something, especially in a deep, meaningful way
to communicate intimately or spiritually

Examples

She found peace as she communed with the forest during her long walk.
The artist often communes with her innermost thoughts before beginning to paint.
Many people seek quiet places to commune with their spiritual beliefs.

Synset Examples

He seemed to commune with nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or associate with something or someone
  • Synonym: associate - to be closely connected or joined with something
  • Antonym: to isolate - to separate from others or from connection

Etymology

From Latin 'communicare' (to share, communicate), related to 'communis' (common).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00762232-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mjun / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.mjuːn / (BrE)
A group of people or families living together and sharing possessions, responsibilities, and resources.
group sharing living and resources

Examples

The members of the commune shared meals and chores equally among themselves.
She joined an artists' commune in the countryside to live a simpler, communal life.
After college, they spent a year traveling and living in a commune in Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collective - a cooperative enterprise managed by its workers
  • Synonym: community - a group of people living in the same place or having common interests

Etymology

From French 'commune', from Medieval Latin 'commūnia' (community).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
