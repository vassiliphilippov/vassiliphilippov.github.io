tercel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrsəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːsəl / (BrE)
a male hawk, especially a male peregrine or gyrfalcon
male hawk (esp. peregrine or gyrfalcon)

Examples

Trained tercels were highly valued among medieval falconers.
The tercel swooped gracefully to catch its prey in midair.
A female peregrine falcon is larger than the male, known as the tercel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiercel - an alternative spelling for 'tercel', meaning a male hawk
  • Degree Higher: falcon - a bird of prey in the family Falconidae

Etymology

From Old French 'terçuel', from Latin 'tertiolus' (diminutive of 'tertius', meaning third), referring to the belief that only one in three eggs hatches a male.
