Polypodium glycyrrhiza

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈpoʊdiəm ɡlɪˌsɪrəˈhaɪzə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm ɡlɪˌsɪrəˈhɪzə / (BrE)
a species of fern native to western North America, known for its sweet-tasting rhizomes
a fern with sweet-tasting rootstock

Examples

The rhizomes of Polypodium glycyrrhiza have a distinct sweet flavor, sometimes compared to licorice.
Botanists studied Polypodium glycyrrhiza in the forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Native Americans traditionally chewed Polypodium glycyrrhiza roots for their pleasant taste.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus 'Polypodium' (from Greek polypodion, meaning 'many-footed') + species epithet 'glycyrrhiza' (from Greek glukos, 'sweet' + rhiza, 'root').
