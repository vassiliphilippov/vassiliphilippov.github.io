Pinnotheres

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈnɑːθəˌriːz / (AmE), / pɪˈnɒθəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of small crabs, known as pea crabs, that live commensally inside the shells of various mollusks.
genus of small pea crabs

Examples

Biologists discovered a new species within the genus Pinnotheres living inside oysters along the coastline.
The tiny Pinnotheres crabs form a commensal relationship with their mollusk hosts.
Detailed drawings of Pinnotheres are often included in marine biology textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea crab - A small crab living in the shells of mollusks; member of genus Pinnotheres

Etymology

From Greek *pínnos* (pinna, a kind of mussel or shell) and *thēras* (hunter).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnəˈθɪriːz / (AmE), / ˌpɪnəˈθɪəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of small crabs, known as pea crabs, that live commensally inside the shells of mollusks and other marine animals.
genus of small commensal crabs (pea crabs)
medical

Examples

The genus Pinnotheres includes tiny crabs known for living in oyster shells.
Some species in Pinnotheres are considered harmless symbionts with bivalve hosts.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Pinnotheres genus off the Pacific coast.

Etymology

From Greek 'pinnē' (a kind of shellfish) and 'therēs' (hunter).
