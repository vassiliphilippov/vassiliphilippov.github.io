butea kino

1. NOUN

/ buːˈtiːə ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːə ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a reddish, dried juice obtained from the dhak tree, used especially as an astringent in medicine and tanning
dried juice from the dhak tree, used as an astringent

Examples

Traditional medicine sometimes uses butea kino to treat diarrhea and other digestive issues.
The butea kino extracted from the dhak tree is valued for its tannin-rich, astringent properties.
Researchers analyzed the chemical composition of butea kino for possible pharmaceutical applications.

Etymology

From the scientific name of the dhak tree, Butea monosperma, plus 'kino', a term for plant exudates.
