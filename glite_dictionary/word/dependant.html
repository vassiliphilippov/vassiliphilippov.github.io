dependant

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of something) contingent on or determined by something else; requiring something for its existence or occurrence.
contingent on something else

Examples

Whether we can go hiking tomorrow is dependant on the weather forecast.
The company's future is dependant on securing new contracts this year.
Access to the bonus is dependant upon meeting specific performance targets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent - contingent on something else

Etymology

From Latin *dependēns*, present participle of *dependēre* (to hang from, depend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00558400-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who relies on another, especially for financial support, such as a child or spouse living with the provider
a person financially supported by another

Examples

After losing his job, Mark struggled to provide for his dependants.
Many employees receive tax benefits if they have dependants living at home.
The insurance policy covers the dependants of the main applicant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent - alternative spelling, chiefly US English

Etymology

From Old French *dependant*, present participle of *dependre* (to depend), from Latin *dependere* (to hang down, depend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10024466-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
Addicted to a drug; unable to function normally without a particular substance.
addicted to a drug
medical british

Examples

After years of using prescription painkillers, he became dependant on them to get through the day.
Some patients are dependant on medication for both physical and psychological relief.
The centre offers support to those dependant on alcohol or other substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent - unable to stop taking a drug; addicted

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, be dependent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047626-s
Synset POS: s
