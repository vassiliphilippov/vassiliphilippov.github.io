Tragopogon dubius

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræɡəˈpoʊɡən ˈdub.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌtræɡəˈpɒɡən ˈdjuː.bɪ.əs / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with hollow stems, long narrow tapered leaves, and solitary pale yellow flowers, naturalized throughout the United States.
a perennial plant with pale yellow flowers

Examples

Tragopogon dubius is commonly found along roadsides and in open fields across North America.
Gardeners sometimes plant Tragopogon dubius for its attractive yellow blooms and unique seed heads.
The hollow stems of Tragopogon dubius make it easily identifiable compared to other wildflowers.

Etymology

From Greek *tragos* (goat) + *pogon* (beard), referring to the hairy seeds; Latin species epithet *dubius* means 'doubtful'.
