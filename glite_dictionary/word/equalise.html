to equalise

1. VERB

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make things equal in amount, degree, or value; to bring to the same level or standard
make things equal; bring to same level
british

Examples

The new law aims to equalise pay between men and women.
The coach worked hard to equalise opportunities for all players on the team.
It is important to equalise the workload so no one feels overwhelmed.

Synset Examples

let's equalize the duties among all employees in this office
The company matched the discount policy of its competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equalize - make equal, uniform, corresponding, or matching (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to standardize - to make things conform to a standard

Etymology

From French 'égaliser', from 'égal' (equal), from Latin 'aequalis'.

2. VERB

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make things equal in amount, number, or value; especially to make the score the same for both sides in a competition
make things or scores equal
british

Examples

The striker scored to equalise just before half-time.
They hoped to equalise the chances for all participants.
Her goal in the final minute helped equalise the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equalize - make things or scores equal; US spelling

Etymology

From Latin aequālis (equal) + -ise (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
