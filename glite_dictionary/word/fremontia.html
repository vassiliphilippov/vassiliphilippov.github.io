Fremontia

1. NOUN

/ friːˈmɒntiə / (AmE), / frɪˈmɒntiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs, commonly known as flannelbush, native to western North America and valued for their large yellow flowers.
genus of shrubs called flannelbush

Examples

Many gardeners in California plant Fremontia for its striking yellow blossoms.
The Fremontia thrives in dry, rocky soils and requires little water once established.
Wildlife such as bees and butterflies are attracted to the flowers of Fremontia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannelbush - common name for shrubs of the genus Fremontia

Etymology

Named after John C. Frémont, American explorer and botanist.

2. NOUN

/ friːˈmɒntiə / (AmE), / friːˈmɒntiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs native to western North America, commonly known as flannelbush, with showy yellow flowers and soft, felt-like leaves.
a genus of flowering shrubs (flannelbush)

Examples

The Fremontia is prized in gardens for its bright yellow blossoms and drought tolerance.
We saw several large Fremontia shrubs along the hiking trail in California.
Because of its distinctive woolly leaves, Fremontia is often called flannelbush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannelbush - A shrub of the genus Fremontia, known for soft, felt-like leaves and yellow flowers

Etymology

Named after John C. Frémont, American explorer and botanist.
