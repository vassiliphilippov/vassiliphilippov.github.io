elucubration

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːkjəˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːkjʊˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
A written work produced through intense study or laborious effort, especially late at night.
work or writing produced by study
archaic

Examples

The professor's latest elucubration was a lengthy treatise on medieval philosophy.
Her elucubration on the subject was admired for its depth and clarity.
He presented his elucubration to the literary society for discussion.

Etymology

From Latin elucubratio (nocturnal study), from elucubrare (to work by lamplight).
