Hippocastanaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˌkæstəˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˌkastəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of trees including horse-chestnuts, characterized by showy flowers and inedible, nutlike seeds in a leathery capsule
family of trees including horse-chestnuts

Examples

The Hippocastanaceae are best known for the ornamental horse-chestnut trees found in parks and gardens.
Researchers studied the dispersal methods of seeds produced by the Hippocastanaceae family.
Most members of the Hippocastanaceae have large, palmate leaves and attractive blossoms.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Hippocastanaceae, from Hippocastanum (Latin for horse-chestnut) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˌkæstəˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˌkæstəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of trees characterized by showy flowers and inedible nut-like seeds enclosed in a leathery capsule, commonly known as horse chestnuts and buckeyes.
a family of flowering trees (horse chestnuts, buckeyes)

Examples

The Hippocastanaceae include trees such as the familiar horse chestnut and several species of buckeye.
Botanists once classified the horse chestnut under the Hippocastanaceae, but modern systems often merge it with other families.
Members of the Hippocastanaceae are easily recognizable by their large, showy flower clusters and spiny seed capsules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - a common tree in the Hippocastanaceae family
  • Synonym: Aesculus - the genus to which horse chestnuts and buckeyes belong

Etymology

From modern Latin Hippocastanum (the horse chestnut genus) + -aceae (family suffix in botany).
