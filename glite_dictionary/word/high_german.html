High German

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈdʒɝː.mən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
the standard form of the German language that developed from West Germanic dialects, used as the official language of Germany and parts of Switzerland and Austria.
the standard form of the German language

Examples

Luther's Bible translation helped shape High German as the standardized written language of Germany.
Many Swiss people speak a dialect at home but use High German in formal settings.
The university offers courses in both Bavarian dialect and High German.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Low German - a West Germanic language spoken mainly in northern Germany
  • Synonym: German - the language spoken in Germany and parts of Austria and Switzerland

Etymology

From the distinction between High (upland) German dialects of southern Germany and Low (lowland) German of the north.
