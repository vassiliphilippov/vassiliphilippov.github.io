stepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpər / (AmE), / ˈstɛpə / (BrE)
a horse trained to lift its feet high off the ground while walking or trotting
horse that lifts feet high while walking or trotting

Examples

The show judge admired the elegant gait of the stepper in the ring.
Her family owns a stepper that regularly competes in carriage driving events.
The stepper's high-stepping action impressed everyone at the parade.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpər / (AmE), / ˈstɛpə / (BrE)
a professional dancer, especially one skilled in dance styles that involve precise steps or footwork
professional dancer skilled in precise footwork

Examples

The talented stepper captivated the audience with her flawless routine.
He has become a well-known stepper in the urban dance scene.
As a competitive stepper, she practices for hours every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dancer - a person who dances, either professionally or as a hobby

Etymology

From 'step' + '-er', indicating a person who steps (dances).

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpər / (AmE), / ˈstɛpə / (BrE)
a type of electric motor that moves or rotates in small, precise steps, allowing for controlled movement
electric motor that moves in small steps

Examples

A stepper is ideal for applications that require precise positioning, such as 3D printers.
The robot's arm movements are powered by small steppers for accuracy.
You need a special controller to drive a stepper efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stepper motor - an electric motor that moves in discrete steps, synonymous with 'stepper' in this usage
