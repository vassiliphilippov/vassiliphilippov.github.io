millifarad

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˈfæræd / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈfæræd / (BrE)
a unit of capacitance equal to one thousandth (10⁻³) of a farad
unit of capacitance (1/1000 of a farad)

Examples

The capacitor has a value of 2 millifarads, which is suitable for this circuit.
Measured in millifarads, the component stores less charge than one with a higher capacitance.
Most common small electronics use capacitors rated in microfarads or millifarads.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: farad - the SI unit of capacitance
  • Degree Lower: microfarad - one millionth of a farad (10⁻⁶ F), unit of capacitance

Etymology

From the prefix 'milli-' (thousandth) and 'farad,' named after Michael Faraday.
