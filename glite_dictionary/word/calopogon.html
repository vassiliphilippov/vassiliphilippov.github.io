Calopogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈpoʊ.ɡɑn / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈpəʊ.ɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids native to North America, commonly known as grass pinks.
a genus of North American terrestrial orchids

Examples

The Calopogon orchid blooms in late spring and is admired for its striking pink flowers.
Botanists discovered a new population of Calopogon in the wet meadows of the wildlife reserve.
Many gardeners cultivate Calopogon for its beauty and adaptability to various soil types.

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) and pogon (beard), referring to the flower's appearance.
