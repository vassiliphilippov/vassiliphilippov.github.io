backing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækɪŋ / (BrE)
support, especially financial resources, provided to enable a project or activity to take place.
financial support for a project

Examples

The new theater company was able to launch thanks to substantial backing from local businesses.
She found it difficult to get financial backing for her start-up.
Without adequate backing, many scientific projects would never get off the ground.

Synset Examples

the foundation provided support for the experiment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or encouragement given to a person or cause

Etymology

From 'back' (to support) + -ing; attested since the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer or piece of material forming the back of something, added to give support or strength
supporting material added at the back

Examples

The frame had a sturdy backing to help it stay upright.
We glued a sheet of cardboard as a backing for the photograph.
The rug's rubber backing prevents it from slipping on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that gives assistance or holds up another thing
  • Synonym: reinforcement - an additional material or structure that strengthens something

Etymology

From 'back' + '-ing', denoting the act or result of backing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02772465-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækɪŋ / (BrE)
support, encouragement, or approval given to a person, group, or cause
support or approval given

Examples

The project relied heavily on the financial backing of its investors.
Her proposal ultimately succeeded thanks to the strong backing from management.
Without his father's backing, he would not have been able to start his own business.

Synset Examples

his vigorous backing of the conservatives got him in trouble with progressives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or approval given to someone or something
  • Antonym: opposition - active resistance or disagreement

Etymology

From the verb 'back' (to support) with the noun-forming suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
