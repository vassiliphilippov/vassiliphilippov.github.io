Patella vulgata

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛlə vʌlˈgeɪtə / (AmE), / pəˈtiːlə vʌlˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
A common species of edible marine limpet found on rocky European coasts.
species of marine limpet

Examples

The rocky shoreline was covered with Patella vulgata, clinging tightly to the stones.
Researchers observed the feeding patterns of Patella vulgata at low tide.
Chefs in some coastal regions use Patella vulgata in traditional seafood dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - a marine mollusc with a conical shell

Etymology

From Latin: 'patella' meaning 'little pan' and 'vulgata' meaning 'common'.
