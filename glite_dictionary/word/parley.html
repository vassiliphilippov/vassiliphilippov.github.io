parley

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrˌli / (AmE), / ˈpɑːli / (BrE)
a discussion or conference held to negotiate terms, especially between enemies or opposing sides during a conflict
negotiation between enemies

Examples

The two commanders agreed to a parley to discuss the terms of a possible truce.
After hours of fierce fighting, both sides called for a parley to avoid further bloodshed.
The pirates held a parley with the merchant crew before deciding their fate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiation - formal discussion aimed at reaching an agreement

Etymology

From French 'parler' meaning 'to speak', itself from Latin 'parabolare', to talk or converse.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑːrli / (AmE), / ˈpɑːli / (BrE)
(of opposing sides, especially enemies) to hold a conference or discussion with the aim of reaching an agreement or peace.
hold a negotiation, especially between enemies

Examples

The two commanders agreed to parley in a neutral location under a flag of truce.
After hours of fighting, the leaders decided to parley rather than risk further losses.
They parleyed late into the night, trying to avoid bloodshed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss something formally in order to reach an agreement
  • Antonym: fight - engage in a battle or conflict

Etymology

From Old French 'parler' (to speak), from Latin 'parabolare' (to speak in parables).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
