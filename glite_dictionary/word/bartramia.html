Bartramia

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈtreɪmiə / (AmE), / bɑːˈtreɪmɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, including the upland sandpiper.
genus of birds in Scolopacidae

Examples

The upland sandpiper is the only living species classified under the genus Bartramia.
Many ornithologists study Bartramia to understand its migration patterns across North America.
Specimens of Bartramia are held in natural history museums for research purposes.

Etymology

Named after American naturalist William Bartram (1739–1823).
