royalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəlti / (BrE)
members of a royal family, considered as a group; royal persons collectively
members of a royal family

Examples

The wedding was attended by royalty from several European countries.
Photographers waited outside the palace hoping to catch a glimpse of royalty.
Despite his fame, he was as nervous as anyone when meeting royalty for the first time.

Synset Examples

the wedding was attended by royalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobility - the aristocratic class; people of noble rank

Etymology

From Old French 'roialte' (royal status), from Latin 'regalis' (regal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈrɔːɪəlti / (BrE)
a sum of money paid to the holder of a patent, copyright, or resource for the right to use, publish, or extract their property
payment for use of intellectual property or resources

Examples

The author earns a royalty on every copy of the novel sold.
She signed a contract to receive royalties from her patented invention.
The artist was paid a royalty for each time his song was streamed online.

Synset Examples

he received royalties on his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license fee - a fee paid for the authorization to use something, especially intellectual property

Etymology

From Old French 'roialte' (royal status), later transferred to the sense of a payment made to a sovereign, then broadened.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13278881-n
Synset POS: n
