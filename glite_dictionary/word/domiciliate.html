to domiciliate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɑmɪˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to establish a home in a particular place or community; to reside or settle in a specific location
to establish residence in a place

Examples

After moving to Canada, the family decided to domiciliate in Toronto.
It can take time to domiciliate fully in a new country and community.
The company sought to domiciliate its headquarters in a more favorable tax jurisdiction.

Synset Examples

my parents reside in Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reside - to live in a particular place
  • Synonym: settle - to begin to live in a new place and make it one's home

Etymology

From Latin 'domiciliāre' (to make a home), from 'domicilium' (dwelling place).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɑːməˈsɪlieɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to provide someone with accommodation or a place of residence; to establish or set someone in a home
provide accommodation; establish residence

Examples

The government plans to domiciliate refugees in the renovated apartment complex.
After the war, many families were domiciliated in temporary shelters by relief organizations.
The charity aims to domiciliate the homeless in safe, permanent housing.

Synset Examples

The immigrants were housed in a new development outside the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house - to provide with living quarters; accommodate
  • Synonym: shelter - to provide with shelter or protection

Etymology

From Latin domus (house) + -ciliare (to make inhabit), via Medieval Latin domiciliāre.

3. VERB

/ ˌdoʊ.mɪˈsɪl.i.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɒ.mɪˈsɪl.i.eɪt / (BrE)
to establish residence in a particular place; to make a home in a specified location, often for legal or administrative purposes
establish residence; make a home in a place
legal

Examples

Before you can apply for benefits, you must domiciliate in the country for at least six months.
The law requires foreign workers to domiciliate in the state where they are employed.
They decided to domiciliate in the city center after accepting new jobs.

Synset Examples

You must domiciliate in the state to register.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reside - to live in a particular place
  • Degree Lower: move - to change residence
  • Synonym: settle - to establish a permanent residence

Etymology

From Latin 'domiciliatus', past participle of 'domiciliare' (to make one's home).
