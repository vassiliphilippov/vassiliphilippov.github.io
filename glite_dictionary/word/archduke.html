archduke

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrtʃˈduk / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈdjuːk / (BrE)
a sovereign prince, especially a male member of the former ruling house of Austria, ranking immediately below a king or emperor.
Austrian noble title below king or emperor

Examples

The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914 sparked World War I.
As an archduke, he wielded significant influence over the politics of the Habsburg Empire.
The archduke greeted visiting dignitaries at the imperial court in Vienna.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duke - a male noble of high rank, just below a prince or king
  • Degree Higher: emperor - the sovereign ruler of an empire

Etymology

From Middle French archiduc, from Medieval Latin archidux, from Greek arkhi- (chief) + Latin dux (leader).
