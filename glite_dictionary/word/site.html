site

1. NOUN

/ saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
a location on the internet consisting of one or more web pages maintained by a person, organization, or company; a website
a collection of web pages on the internet

Examples

The company launched its new site last week to attract more customers.
Many students use the university's site to access course materials and schedules.
His personal site features a blog, a portfolio, and contact information.

Synset Examples

the Israeli web site was damaged by hostile hackers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: website - a collection of related web pages under a single domain name
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06370600-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
a place or position where something is or occurs, especially in relation to its surroundings
a specific place or position

Examples

The scientists identified the binding site on the molecule.
The historical document was found at the excavation site.
Please mark the exact site where the plant should be placed.

Synset Examples

the sites are determined by highly specific sequences of nucleotides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: location - a particular place or position

Etymology

From Latin situs (position, location).
