Zephyr

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɛfɚ / (AmE), / ˈzɛf.ɪə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the west wind, often associated with gentle breezes and the arrival of spring.
Greek god of the west wind

Examples

According to mythology, Zephyr brought gentle spring winds to the land.
Artists often depict Zephyr as a youthful figure with flowing hair and wings.
The legend says that Zephyr fell in love with the nymph Chloris.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Boreas - Greek god of the north wind
  • Degree Higher: Notus - Greek god of the south wind
  • Degree Higher: Eurus - Greek god of the east wind

Etymology

From Greek *Zéphuros*, god of the west wind.
