Poa nemoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.ə nɛˈmɔːr.əlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊ.ə nɛˈmɒr.əlɪs / (BrE)
A slender European grass that grows in shady places, also found in northeastern America and temperate Asia.
species of slender grass in shady places

Examples

Poa nemoralis thrives in the shaded woodlands of northern Europe.
Some gardeners use Poa nemoralis for ground cover in areas with little sunlight.
Researchers found that Poa nemoralis has adapted well to temperate climates in North America.

Etymology

From Latin 'poa', meaning 'grass', and 'nemoralis', meaning 'of groves or woods'.
