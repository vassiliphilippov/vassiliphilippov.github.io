wheat

1. NOUN

/ wiːt / (AmE), / wiːt / (BrE)
the grain of the common wheat plant, used for food, especially when ground into flour
grain used for flour and food

Examples

The bakery uses only organic wheat in its bread and pastries.
Many cereals contain whole wheat for extra fiber and nutrients.
Farmers harvested a bumper crop of wheat this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour - powder from ground grains, especially wheat

Etymology

Old English 'hwǣte', related to 'white' due to the color of the grain.

2. NOUN

/ wiːt / (AmE), / wiːt / (BrE)
A cereal plant that is widely grown for its grain, which is used to make flour for bread, pasta, and other foods.
cereal plant; grain used for flour

Examples

The farmer harvested the wheat fields in late summer.
Whole wheat bread is considered healthier by many nutritionists.
A shipment of wheat was exported from the port last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a cereal grain used in brewing and animal feed
  • Synonym: rice - a cereal grain that is a staple food in many cultures
  • Synonym: corn - a widely grown cereal plant, also known as maize

Etymology

From Old English 'hwǣte', related to 'white,' referring to the pale color of the grain.

3. NOUN

/ wiːt / (AmE), / wiːt / (BrE)
a pale yellow or dull yellow color, often resembling the hue of wheat grains
pale yellow color

Examples

The new sofa was upholstered in a lovely wheat shade that brightened the room.
She chose wheat curtains to match the neutral tones of the walls.
Painters often recommend wheat as a versatile color for living spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beige - a light brownish-yellow color
  • Degree Lower: ivory - a creamy white color
  • Degree Higher: golden - a rich, deep yellow color

Etymology

From the color of wheat grains, referencing their characteristic pale yellow hue.
