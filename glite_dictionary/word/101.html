101

1. NOUN

/ wʌn oʊ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn əʊ wʌn / (BrE)
An introductory course on a subject at a college or university, often used more generally to mean a basic introduction to any topic.
introductory course or basic introduction
american

Examples

If you need to learn the basics of marketing, you should enroll in Marketing 101.
The conference began with a 101 on cybersecurity for all attendees.
Before moving on, let’s do a quick 101 on how this technology works.

Synset Examples

The course was an engaging 101 for beginners in programming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introductory course - a course for beginners on a subject
  • Antonym: advanced course - a higher-level or specialized course on a subject

Etymology

Used originally as a course numbering convention in US and Canadian colleges, where '101' designates basic-level courses.

2. NOUN

/ wʌn oʊ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn əʊ wʌn / (BrE)
used to denote an introductory course or the basics of a subject, especially in American educational contexts or as a figurative way to refer to the fundamentals.
introductory course; basics
american

Examples

Before tackling advanced economics, you should take 101 to understand the basics.
His article is a helpful 101 on managing personal finances.
She gave me a crash course, basically a 101 in cooking Italian food.

Synset Examples

The workshop covered the basics and then some, a true 101 on marketing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basics - the essential or fundamental aspects of something
  • Synonym: introduction - an initial explanation or course on a subject

Etymology

From the numbering system for entry-level college courses in North America, where '101' designates foundational classes.
