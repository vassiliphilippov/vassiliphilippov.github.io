Valium

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæliəm / (AmE), / ˈval.i.əm / (BrE)
The trade name for a drug (diazepam) used as a tranquilizer to relieve anxiety, relax muscles, and treat certain seizure disorders.
trade name for a tranquilizer (diazepam)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Valium to help manage her anxiety before the surgery.
Because he was extremely tense, his physician decided that Valium was appropriate for short-term use.
The patient responded quickly after receiving an injection of Valium during the seizure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diazepam - A benzodiazepine drug used to treat anxiety, muscle spasms, and seizures

Etymology

Trademark, introduced in the 1960s by Hoffmann-La Roche for diazepam.
