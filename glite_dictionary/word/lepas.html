Lepas

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of goose barnacles, which are stalked marine crustaceans of the family Lepadidae.
genus of goose barnacles

Examples

Several specimens of Lepas can be found attached to floating debris in the ocean.
Biologists study Lepas to understand the dispersal of marine organisms.
The Lepas genus includes several species of stalked barnacles found worldwide.

Etymology

From Greek lepas, lepados (limpet, shellfish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːpəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpas / (BrE)
A genus of stalked barnacles, the type genus of the family Lepadidae.
genus of stalked barnacles

Examples

Several species within the genus Lepas are found attached to floating debris in the ocean.
Scientists discovered a new Lepas specimen during their expedition in the Pacific.
The distinctive shell of Lepas makes it easy for marine biologists to identify.

Etymology

From Latin *lepas*, from Greek *lepas* (limpet, barnacle).
