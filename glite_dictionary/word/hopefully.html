hopefully

1. ADV

/ ˈhoʊpfəli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfəli / (BrE)
used to express the hope that something will happen or be the case
expressing hope for something

Examples

Hopefully the rain will stop before the ceremony starts.
Hopefully, we can resolve this issue by tomorrow morning.
She looked at the test results hopefully, wishing for good news.

Synset Examples

hopefully the weather will be fine on Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perhaps - possibly, but not certainly
  • Antonym: unfortunately - used to introduce a disappointing or regrettable fact

Etymology

From 'hopeful' + '-ly', modeled after similar adverbs.

2. ADV

/ ˈhoʊpfəli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfəli / (BrE)
In a hopeful manner; with hope that something will happen or be the case.
in a hopeful manner

Examples

She waited hopefully for news of her application.
The children looked hopefully at the empty stage, expecting the show to begin.
We searched hopefully for a sign that the weather would improve.

Synset Examples

we searched hopefully for a good position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: despairingly - in a despairing manner; without hope

Etymology

Formed by adding the adverbial suffix -ly to 'hopeful', itself from 'hope'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00201672-r
Synset POS: r
