upstairs

1. ADV

/ ʌpˈstɛɹz / (AmE), / ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
On or to an upper floor of a building.
on or to a higher floor

Examples

She went upstairs to fetch her coat before leaving.
The bedrooms are located upstairs, while the kitchen is on the ground floor.
If you need the bathroom, it’s upstairs on the right.

Synset Examples

they lived upstairs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstairs - on or to a lower floor

Etymology

From 'up' + 'stairs', literally meaning 'up the stairs'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

/ ʌpˈstɛɹz / (AmE), / ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
(informal) In or with respect to the mind; mentally; relating to one's mental faculties.

informal

Examples
