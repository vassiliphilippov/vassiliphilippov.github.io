spit

1. NOUN

/ spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
A narrow strip of land projecting into the sea or another body of water.
narrow projecting strip of land

Examples

A narrow spit of land separated the lagoon from the open sea.
Waves had deposited a long sandy spit that curved along the coast.
Birds nested on the spit, safe from mainland predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peninsula - a piece of land almost surrounded by water or projecting into a body of water

Etymology

From Old English *spitu* (spit, pointed rod), referring to something that projects outward.

/ spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)

Examples

A thin line of spit hung from the baby's lip as he smiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saliva - the clear fluid secreted in the mouth

Etymology

From Old English *spittan* (to eject from the mouth).
