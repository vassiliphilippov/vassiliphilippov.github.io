to sensitise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.səˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to make a material sensitive to light, especially to a particular colour, by coating it with a photographic emulsion
make a material sensitive to light (photography)

Examples

The technician carefully sensitised the film before exposure in the laboratory.
Older photographic plates must be sensitised with a special emulsion to capture blue light.
Before digital photography, most amateur photographers learned how to sensitise their own paper for prints.

Synset Examples

sensitize the photographic film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sensitize - alternative spelling (American English)

Etymology

From French sensibiliser, from sensible (sensitive) + -iser (-ize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00574748-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something more aware of or responsive to particular events, situations, or substances.
make more sensitive or responsive

Examples

Exposure to certain chemicals can sensitise the skin, causing allergic reactions.
The school organized a workshop to sensitise students to environmental issues.
Repeated criticism can sensitise a person to negative feedback.

Synset Examples

She sensitized me with respect to gender differences in this traditional male-dominated society
My tongue became sensitized to good wine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitize - to make less sensitive or responsive
  • Synonym: to sensitize - alternate spelling (American English) of 'sensitise'

Etymology

From French 'sensibiliser', from sensible (sensitive); also related to the English word 'sensitive'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02120261-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (someone) more aware of or responsive to certain facts or feelings; to make sensitive
make more aware or responsive
british

Examples

The campaign aimed to sensitise the public to the dangers of smoking.
Teachers are working to sensitise students to issues of diversity and inclusion.
It is important to sensitise medical staff to patients' emotional needs.

Synset Examples

He was not sensitized to her emotional needs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitise - make less sensitive or less aware
  • Synonym: to sensitize - alternate (US) spelling of 'sensitise'

Etymology

From French 'sensitiser', based on 'sensitif' (sensitive), ultimately from Latin 'sensus' (feeling, perception).
