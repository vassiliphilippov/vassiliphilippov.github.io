cumfrey

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.fri / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fri / (BrE)
a perennial herb of Europe and western Asia, with large hairy leaves, used especially as a groundcover in shaded areas
perennial herb used as groundcover

Examples

The cumfrey in my garden spreads quickly and helps suppress weeds.
Gardeners often use cumfrey as a natural mulch for shaded borders.
I planted cumfrey to attract pollinators and improve soil health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfrey - a perennial herb of the borage family

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'comfrey', from Old English 'comfreġe'.
