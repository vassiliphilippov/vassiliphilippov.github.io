pacification

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæs.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæs.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the efforts by a government to suppress or eliminate insurgency and resistance, often through military or police action combined with efforts to restore peace and order
actions to suppress insurgency

Examples

The government launched a major pacification campaign in the troubled region to quell the uprising.
Critics argue that pacification often leads to civilian unrest if not paired with genuine reform.
During the occupation, the army focused on pacification rather than outright confrontation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterinsurgency - military or political action taken to combat insurgency

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificatio', from 'pacificare' (to make peace).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of calming or appeasing someone, especially to make them less angry or hostile
the act of calming or appeasing someone

Examples

The pacification of the angry crowd required both patience and diplomacy.
Her expertise in the pacification of anxious children was well known among teachers.
Despite his efforts at pacification, the two parties continued to argue.

Synset Examples

a wonderful skill in the pacification of crying infants
his unsuccessful mollification of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollification - the act of soothing or appeasing someone
  • Antonym: aggravation - the act of making someone more angry or hostile

Etymology

From Latin pacificatio (making peace), from pacificare (to make peace), from pax (peace).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an official agreement or treaty that ends conflict and establishes peace between opposing parties
treaty or agreement to end hostilities

Examples

After decades of conflict, the leaders finally signed a pacification that brought an end to the war.
The pacification of the region was marked by a treaty celebrated with public festivities.
Many historians debate the long-term effects of the pacification imposed by the occupying forces.

Synset Examples

peace came on November 11th

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace treaty - a formal agreement to end war or conflict

Etymology

From Latin pacificatio (act of making peace), from pacificare (to make peace).
