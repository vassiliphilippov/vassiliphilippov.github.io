sericulture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrəˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the cultivation of silkworms for the production of raw silk
the cultivation of silkworms for silk

Examples

Sericulture has been practiced in China for thousands of years.
Many rural families depend on sericulture as their main source of income.
Advances in sericulture have improved the quality and production of silk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk farming - the practice of breeding silkworms to produce silk

Etymology

From Latin 'sericum' (silk) + 'culture' (cultivation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrɪˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the production of raw silk by raising silkworms, especially as an industry or agricultural practice
raising silkworms for silk production

Examples

China has a long history of sericulture dating back thousands of years.
Advancements in sericulture techniques have improved the quality of silk produced.
Many rural families depend on sericulture as their main source of income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - larva of the silk moth used in sericulture

Etymology

From Latin 'sericum' (silk) and 'culture' (cultivation).
