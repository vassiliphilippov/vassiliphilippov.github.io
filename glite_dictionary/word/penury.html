penury

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnjəri / (AmE), / ˈpɛnjəri / (BrE)
the state of being extremely poor; extreme poverty or destitution
extreme poverty

Examples

After years of misfortune, the family was reduced to penury and struggled to survive.
Many artists of the past created their masterpieces while living in penury.
The novel portrays the penury experienced by those living in war-torn regions.

Synset Examples

their indigence appalled him
a general state of need exists among the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being poor
  • Antonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of wealth

Etymology

From Latin 'penuria' meaning 'want, scarcity'.
