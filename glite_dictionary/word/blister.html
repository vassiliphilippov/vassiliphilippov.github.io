blister

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
a raised flaw or bubble on a surface, caused when a layer of a substance does not properly adhere, such as an air bubble in a coat of paint
raised flaw or bubble on a surface

Examples

The new paint job was ruined by a large blister near the door frame.
If moisture gets trapped, a blister can form beneath the varnish.
After applying the resin, he noticed a small blister had developed in the finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a small, round pocket of air or gas within a liquid or substance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14488096-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
A small swelling on the skin filled with clear fluid, usually caused by friction, burning, or other damage.
skin swelling filled with fluid

Examples

After hiking all day, she discovered a painful blister on her heel.
Wearing new shoes can sometimes cause a blister to form on your foot.
He popped the blister accidentally while adjusting his bandage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sore - an area of skin that is painful or inflamed
  • Antonym: ulcer - an open sore on the skin or mucous membrane

Etymology

From Old French *blestre* (swelling), of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
(Botany) A swelling on a plant that resembles a blister on skin, often caused by disease, insect damage, or environmental stress.
swelling on a plant, like a skin blister

Examples

The leaves developed a blister after being attacked by aphids.
This type of fungal infection causes round blisters on the stems of tomato plants.
If you notice a blister forming on the leaf, it may be a sign of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall - abnormal growth on plants often caused by insects or fungi
