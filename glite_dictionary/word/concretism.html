concretism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋ.krəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.krɪˌtɪz.əm / (BrE)
The practice or principle of expressing abstract ideas in concrete, specific terms, especially in art or literature.
rendering abstract ideas in concrete terms

Examples

The poet's concretism brought vivid images to life instead of relying on vague notions.
Modern artists often employ concretism to ground their messages in physical reality.
Her writing style combines philosophical ideas with concretism, making the concepts more relatable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstraction - the quality of being abstract or the process of forming abstract ideas

Etymology

From concrete + -ism, modeled after similar terms like 'realism.'
