Lysimachia quadrifolia

1. NOUN

/ laɪsɪˈmækiə ˌkwɒdrəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / lɪsɪˈmakɪə ˌkwɒdrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of yellow-flowered plant native to North America, commonly known as whorled loosestrife.
North American yellow-flowered plant species

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with clusters of Lysimachia quadrifolia in full bloom.
She collected Lysimachia quadrifolia specimens for her botany project.
Many gardeners admire the delicate yellow flowers of Lysimachia quadrifolia.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Lysimachia' after Lysimachus, an ancient king; 'quadrifolia' meaning 'four-leaved'.
