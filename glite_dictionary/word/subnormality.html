subnormality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.nɔːrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.nɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being below what is considered normal, especially in terms of intelligence or mental ability
the condition of being below normal, esp. mentally
medical

Examples

The specialist diagnosed the child with mild subnormality after extensive cognitive testing.
Social attitudes toward subnormality have changed significantly in recent decades.
Educational programs are available for those with intellectual subnormality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal; regular condition

Etymology

From 'sub-' (below) + 'normal' + '-ity' (state or condition); from Latin 'sub' (under) and Latin 'normalis'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˌnɔːrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌnɔːˈmæləti / (BrE)
the condition of having less than normal intellectual development or ability
impaired intellectual development
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient's cognitive difficulties were due to subnormality present since birth.
Educational support is essential for children with subnormality to help them achieve their potential.
Experts are researching how genetics may contribute to developmental subnormality in some individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental retardation - significantly below average intellectual functioning
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal, especially intellectually

Etymology

From sub- (below) + normal + -ity; first used in early 20th century medical literature.
