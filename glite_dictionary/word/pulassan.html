pulassan

1. NOUN

/ pʊˈlɑːsən / (AmE), / pʊˈlɑːsən / (BrE)
An East Indian fruit tree (Nephelium mutabile) producing fruit similar to but sweeter than the rambutan.
East Indian fruit tree, related to rambutan

Examples

The pulassan tree grows best in tropical climates and is popular for its sweet fruit.
She prefers eating pulassan over rambutan because of its richer flavor.
Local farmers harvest pulassan during the rainy season for sale at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - a tropical fruit with hairy skin, from Southeast Asia

2. NOUN

/ pʊˈlɑːsən / (AmE), / pʊˈlæsən / (BrE)
A tropical fruit from Southeast Asia, similar to the rambutan but with sweeter flesh.
tropical fruit resembling rambutan

Examples

The pulassan is prized in local markets for its juicy, sweet taste.
Unlike the rambutan, the pulassan has a tougher shell and a more delicate flavor.
She tried a pulassan for the first time while visiting Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - a tropical fruit with hairy red skin and sweet flesh
