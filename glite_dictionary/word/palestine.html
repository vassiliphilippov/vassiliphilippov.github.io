Palestine

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæl.ə.staɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪ.staɪn / (BrE)
A territory in the Middle East comprising the West Bank and Gaza Strip, recognized by some as a state but currently largely under Israeli occupation.
territory in Middle East; West Bank and Gaza Strip

Examples

Many countries have recognized Palestine as a sovereign state despite ongoing conflicts in the region.
Negotiations over the borders of Palestine have continued for decades with no final agreement.
The capital of Palestine is claimed to be East Jerusalem by the Palestinian government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Israel - country in the Middle East, bordering Palestine

Etymology

From Latin 'Palaestina', from Greek 'Palaistinē', referring to the region between the Mediterranean Sea and the Jordan River.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpæl.ə.staɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪ.staɪn / (BrE)
a former British mandate territory on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean, divided between Jordan and Israel in 1948.
former British mandate in the Middle East

Examples

The end of the British mandate in Palestine in 1948 led to major changes in the region.
Many families in Palestine were affected by the division of territory after World War II.
The League of Nations granted Britain a mandate over Palestine following the First World War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Transjordan - historical region east of the Jordan River, created from part of the British Mandate of Palestine
  • Synonym: Israel - country formed in part from the former British Mandate of Palestine

Etymology

From Latin 'Palaestina', from Greek 'Palestina', originally referring to the region once inhabited by the Philistines.

3. PROPN

/ ˈpæl.ə.staɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪ.staɪn / (BrE)
an ancient region in southwestern Asia, on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea, regarded as a holy land by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam
ancient region in southwestern Asia; holy land

Examples

During the Crusades, thousands of pilgrims traveled to Palestine to visit holy sites.
Archaeologists have discovered many ancient artifacts in Palestine.
The area known as Palestine has been home to diverse cultures throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Land - another term for the region considered sacred by several religions
  • Synonym: Canaan - an ancient name for the same region

Etymology

From Latin 'Palaestina', from Greek 'Palaistinē', referring to the region of the Philistines.

Synset POS: n
