dissembling

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛmblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmblɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of concealing one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs by pretending or giving a false appearance
concealing true intentions by pretense

Examples

His constant dissembling made it hard to know what he truly believed.
The company's dissembling during the investigation damaged its public image.
Through clever dissembling, she managed to avoid answering the difficult questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone by hiding the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin dissimulāre (to disguise, conceal), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛmblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmblɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of concealing one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs; deception by pretending or feigning
the act of concealing intent; deception

Examples

There was a sense of dissembling in his carefully crafted statement to the police.
Her talent for dissembling made her a formidable poker player.
The politician’s dissembling was eventually exposed by investigative journalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere

Etymology

From Middle English dissemble (to disguise, conceal), from Old French dissimuler, from Latin dissimulāre, meaning 'to disguise'.
