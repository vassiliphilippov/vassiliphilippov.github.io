self-interest

1. NOUN

/ sɛlfˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / sɛlfˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
the act of considering only your own advantage and welfare rather than that of others
focus on one's own advantage or welfare

Examples

His decision was guided purely by self-interest, not by any sense of duty.
There are situations where self-interest aligns with the common good.
Critics accused her of acting out of self-interest rather than genuine concern for others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; concern exclusively for oneself
  • Antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' and 'interest', patterning after similar compounds dating to the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
the fact of considering only what is best for oneself, without regard for others
concern for one's own advantage

Examples

His decisions were motivated purely by self-interest, not by concern for others.
In politics, accusations of self-interest often damage a candidate’s reputation.
They acted out of self-interest rather than for the good of the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfishness - the quality of being concerned excessively or exclusively with oneself
  • Antonym: altruism - the principle or practice of unselfish concern for others
