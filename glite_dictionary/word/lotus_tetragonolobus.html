Lotus tetragonolobus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs ˌtɛtrəˌɡɒnəˈloʊbəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs ˌtɛtrəˌɡɒnəˈləʊbəs / (BrE)
a sprawling annual European plant in the legume family, known for its four-winged edible pod; also called the asparagus pea.
European annual with four-winged edible pods

Examples

The farmer grew Lotus tetragonolobus for its unique, edible pods.
In gourmet kitchens, Lotus tetragonolobus is valued as the asparagus pea.
Botanists classify Lotus tetragonolobus as part of the legume family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asparagus pea - common name for Lotus tetragonolobus

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Lotus' plus specific epithet 'tetragonolobus', meaning 'four-angled pod'.
