intimacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntəməsi / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪməsi / (BrE)
a usually secretive or illicit sexual relationship
secret or illicit sexual relationship

Examples

Their intimacy was known to only a few close friends.
Rumors spread about an intimacy between the politician and his assistant.
The novel explores the consequences of a forbidden intimacy in a small town.

Etymology

From Latin intimus (innermost).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.t̬ə.mə.si / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.məsi / (BrE)
a close, familiar, and usually affectionate or loving personal relationship with another person or group
close personal relationship or feeling

Examples

There was a newfound intimacy between the two friends after their long conversation.
Trust and intimacy are essential for a healthy marriage.
During the retreat, participants explored emotional intimacy and deepened their bonds with each other.

Synset Examples

their closeness grew as the night wore on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closeness - a feeling of being close or connected to someone
  • Antonym: distance - a lack of closeness or connection in a relationship

Etymology

From Latin 'intimus' (inmost), via French 'intimité'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tə.mə.si / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.mə.si / (BrE)
a close or warm personal relationship or feeling of familiarity between people
close or warm friendship

Examples

Their intimacy grew over years of working together on challenging projects.
She valued the intimacy they shared, feeling truly understood for the first time.
The absence of secrets brought a rare sense of intimacy to their friendship.

Synset Examples

the absence of fences created a mysterious intimacy in which no one knew privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closeness - the quality of being close in friendship or emotional connection
  • Antonym: aloofness - the state of being distant, remote, or withdrawn

Etymology

From Latin *intimitas* (closeness), from *intimus* (inmost).
