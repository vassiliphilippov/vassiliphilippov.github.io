os sphenoidale

1. NOUN

/ oʊs sfɪˌnoɪˈdeɪli / (AmE), / ɒs sfɪˌnɔɪˈdɑːli / (BrE)
the butterfly-shaped bone at the base of the skull, forming part of the eye socket and cranial floor
sphenoid bone at base of skull
medical

Examples

The os sphenoidale plays a crucial role in supporting the brain and forming the structure of the eye sockets.
During surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the os sphenoidale.
Fractures of the os sphenoidale can have serious implications for cranial stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphenoid bone - the butterfly-shaped bone at the base of the skull

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + sphenoidale, from Greek sphēnoeidēs (wedge-shaped).
