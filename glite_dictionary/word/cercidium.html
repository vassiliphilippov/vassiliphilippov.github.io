Cercidium

1. NOUN

/ sərˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / səˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of spiny shrubs or small trees, sometimes placed in the genus Parkinsonia, commonly known as paloverde.
a genus of spiny shrubs or small trees (paloverde)

Examples

The desert landscape is dotted with Cercidium trees, their green bark standing out in the sunlight.
Scientists recently reclassified several Cercidium species into the genus Parkinsonia.
Gardeners in arid regions often plant Cercidium for its drought tolerance and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paloverde - a small tree or shrub with green bark, native to the southwestern US and Mexico
  • Synonym: Parkinsonia - a genus of flowering plants to which Cercidium is sometimes considered identical

Etymology

From Greek kerkidion, diminutive of kerkis (a shuttle, referring to the shape of the seed pod).
