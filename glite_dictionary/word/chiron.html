Chiron

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaɪrɑn / (AmE), / ˈkaɪrɒn / (BrE)
an asteroid discovered in 1977 with an orbit mainly between Saturn and Uranus
asteroid with unique orbit between Saturn and Uranus

Examples

Chiron was the first discovered of a new class of objects known as centaurs in the solar system.
Astronomers observed Chiron exhibiting both asteroid and comet-like behavior.
The orbit of Chiron lies between Saturn and Uranus, making it remarkable among similar objects.

Etymology

Named after Chiron, a centaur from Greek mythology.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkaɪˌrɒn / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.rən / (BrE)
A wise and learned centaur in Greek mythology who tutored many famous heroes, including Achilles, Asclepius, Hercules, and Jason.
wise centaur in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, Chiron was known for his kindness and medical knowledge.
Many heroes, such as Hercules and Achilles, were trained by Chiron in their youth.
Stories of Chiron often highlight the contrast between his wisdom and the wild nature of other centaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centaur - a mythical creature with the upper body of a human and the lower body of a horse

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Χείρων (Kheírōn), meaning 'hand' or relating to skillful hands.
