shellfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
an aquatic invertebrate animal with a soft body, usually enclosed in a shell, such as a mollusk or crustacean, often used as food
aquatic invertebrate with a shell

Examples

Many people are allergic to shellfish such as shrimp and oysters.
The restaurant offers a wide variety of fresh shellfish on its menu.
We collected shellfish along the rocky shoreline during low tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusk - invertebrate animal with a soft unsegmented body, often with a shell
  • Synonym: crustacean - aquatic arthropod with a hard shell and jointed limbs
  • Antonym: fish - cold-blooded aquatic animal with gills and fins

Etymology

From Middle English 'schellefishe', combining 'shell' and 'fish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
the edible meat of aquatic animals with a shell, such as mollusks or crustaceans
meat of aquatic animal with shell

Examples

Many people are allergic to shellfish such as shrimp and lobster.
The restaurant serves only the freshest shellfish from local waters.
He ordered a variety of steamed shellfish for the appetizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - aquatic animal with a hard shell, e.g., crab or lobster
  • Synonym: mollusk - aquatic animal with a soft body and shell, e.g., clam or oyster

Etymology

From Old English 'scell' (shell) + 'fisc' (fish).
