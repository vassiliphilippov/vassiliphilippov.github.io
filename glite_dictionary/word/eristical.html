eristical

1. ADJ

/ ɛˈrɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Given to arguing or disputation for its own sake, often using specious or fallacious reasoning.
argumentative for its own sake

Examples

His eristical style often derailed meetings into pointless debates.
The philosopher was criticized for being overly eristical in scholarly discussions.
Online forums can attract eristical participants who argue just for the sake of arguing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eristic - argumentative for the sake of debate rather than truth

Etymology

From Greek *eristikos* (fond of wrangling), from *eris* (strife, dispute).
