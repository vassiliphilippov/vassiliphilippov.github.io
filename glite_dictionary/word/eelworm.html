eelworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈilˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈiːl.wɜːm / (BrE)
a small roundworm, especially one that is free-living or parasitic on plants, often causing damage to crops
small plant-parasitic roundworm

Examples

The potato crop was destroyed by an infestation of eelworms.
Farmers often take precautions to prevent eelworm damage in their fields.
Certain types of eelworm are serious pests for root vegetables such as carrots and beets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nematode - a type of roundworm of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From 'eel' (the fish, for resemblance) + 'worm'.
