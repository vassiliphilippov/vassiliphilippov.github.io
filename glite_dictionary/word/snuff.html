snuff

1. NOUN

/ snʌf / (AmE), / snʌf / (BrE)
the blackened or burnt part of a candlewick which remains after burning
charred part of a candlewick

Examples

Please remove the snuff from the candle before lighting it again.
The old candlestick was covered with bits of snuff left from past evenings.
She used a special tool to trim the snuff and keep the flame steady.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'snuffen' (to sniff), referring to extinguishing a candle by pinching or cutting the burnt wick.

2. NOUN

/ snʌf / (AmE), / snʌf / (BrE)
A powdered smokeless tobacco product, typically inhaled through the nose.
powdered smokeless tobacco for inhalation

Examples

Many people in the 18th century used snuff instead of smoking tobacco.
He took a small pinch of snuff and inhaled it through his nose.
The gentleman carried a silver box filled with snuff in his coat pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco - plant substance used in cigarettes and other products

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'snuf', related to 'snuffen' (to sniff).
