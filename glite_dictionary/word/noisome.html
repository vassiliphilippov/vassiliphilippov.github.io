noisome

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔɪsəm / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪsəm / (BrE)
having an extremely unpleasant smell; causing nausea or disgust
extremely unpleasant or offensive, especially to smell

Examples

The trash gave off a noisome odor that made everyone leave the room.
A noisome stench drifted from the abandoned building after the storm.
Residents complained about the noisome fumes coming from the new factory.

Synset Examples

a nauseating smell
nauseous offal
a sickening stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseating - causing nausea or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Middle English 'noisome', from 'noie' (harm, trouble), from Anglo-Norman, from Old French 'nuisance'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɔɪsəm / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪsəm / (BrE)
Having a very unpleasant or offensive smell; harmful or injurious to health.
offensive-smelling or harmful

Examples

The garbage had a noisome odor that filled the entire alleyway.
He recoiled from the noisome fumes rising from the stagnant pond.
A noisome stench wafted into the room when the door was opened.

Synset Examples

a foul odor
the kitchen smelled really funky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malodorous - having a bad smell
  • Antonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell

Etymology

From Middle English 'noisom', from 'annoy' + '-some'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01057333-s
Synset POS: s
