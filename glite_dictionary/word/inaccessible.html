inaccessible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.əkˈsɛs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.əkˈses.ə.bəl / (BrE)
unable to be reached or entered; very difficult or impossible to approach, obtain, or understand.
impossible or very difficult to reach

Examples

The mountain summit remained inaccessible due to heavy snowfall and dangerous winds.
Many online resources are still inaccessible to people with disabilities.
The philosopher's writing style is so complex that some readers find it inaccessible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unreachable - not able to be reached
  • Antonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered

Etymology

From Latin inaccessibilis, from in- (not) + accessibilis (approachable).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ækˈsɛs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.əkˈsɛs.ə.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be accessed, reached, or obtained; difficult or impossible to approach or use
unable to be reached or obtained

Examples

The remote mountain village was almost inaccessible during the winter months.
Much of the ancient manuscript remains inaccessible to modern readers.
There are resources deep underground that are currently inaccessible with today's technology.

Synset Examples

a rare work, today almost inaccessible
timber is virtually unobtainable in the islands
untouchable resources buried deep within the earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unattainable - not able to be achieved or obtained
  • Antonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered

Etymology

From Latin inaccessibilis, from in- (not) + accessibilis (approachable).
