Artemisia tridentata

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪz.i.ə traɪˈdɛn.teɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə traɪˈden.teɪ.tə / (BrE)
a large, aromatic shrub native to arid regions of western North America, with silvery-grey, three-lobed leaves, commonly known as big sagebrush
aromatic shrub of western North America

Examples

The vast plains were covered with Artemisia tridentata, filling the air with its distinctive scent.
Many animals in the Great Basin rely on Artemisia tridentata as an important source of food and shelter.
Researchers studied the resilience of Artemisia tridentata to prolonged drought conditions in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - common name for aromatic shrubs of western North America

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Artemisia' (after the Greek goddess Artemis) and species 'tridentata' (three-toothed), referring to the leaf shape.
