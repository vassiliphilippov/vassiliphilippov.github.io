Sunni

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːni, ˈsʌni / (AmE), / ˈsʊni, ˈsʌni / (BrE)
A member of the largest of the two main branches of Islam, which accepts the first four caliphs as rightful successors to Muhammad and emphasizes the authority of the Sunna.
major branch of Islam

Examples

The majority of Muslims worldwide identify as Sunni.
He grew up in a predominantly Sunni community in Egypt.
Tensions sometimes arise between Sunni and Shia groups in the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shia - the second largest branch of Islam, distinct from Sunni Islam

Etymology

From Arabic 'sunnī', meaning 'one of the tradition (Sunna)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːni, ˈsʊni / (AmE), / ˈsʊni, ˈsuːni / (BrE)
A member of the largest branch of Islam, which accepts the first four caliphs as the rightful successors to Muhammad.
member of the main branch of Islam

Examples

The majority of Muslims in Indonesia are Sunni.
Tensions have sometimes arisen between Sunni and Shiite communities in the Middle East.
He was raised in a Sunni Muslim family and learned about Islamic traditions from an early age.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shiite - a member of the branch of Islam that supports Ali and his descendants as the rightful successors to Muhammad

Etymology

From Arabic 'sunnī', meaning 'one who follows the traditions (sunna) of the Prophet'.
