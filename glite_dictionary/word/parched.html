parched

1. ADJ

/ pɑrtʃt / (AmE), / pɑːtʃt / (BrE)
(of land, plants, or substances) dried out with heat; very dry, especially as a result of prolonged exposure to the sun.
dried out by heat or sun

Examples

After weeks without rain, the fields were completely parched.
The hikers searched for water under the parched desert sun.
Her lips felt parched after hours in the hot wind.

Synset Examples

a vast desert all adust
land lying baked in the heat
parched soil
the earth was scorched and bare
sunbaked salt flats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorched - burned or dried out by intense heat
  • Synonym: baked - hardened or dried by heat
  • Antonym: soaked - thoroughly wet; saturated with liquid

2. ADJ

/ pɑrtʃt / (AmE), / pɑːtʃt / (BrE)
(of grains or seeds) toasted or roasted slightly, often by dry heat.
lightly roasted or toasted (esp. of grains)

Examples

Long journeys often included bags of parched corn as an easy snack.
He offered us parched barley, a staple food in his region.
Native Americans commonly prepared parched seeds for winter storage.

Synset Examples

parched corn was a staple of the Indian diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toasted - (of food) browned by exposure to dry heat
  • Synonym: roasted - (of food) cooked by dry heat in an oven or over a fire
