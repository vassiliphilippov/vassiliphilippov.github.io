Philip Melanchthon

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɪlɪp məˈlæn(k)θən / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp mɪˈlænθən / (BrE)
(1497–1560) German theologian and scholar, successor to Martin Luther and a leading figure in the Protestant Reformation in Germany.
German Reformation theologian (1497–1560)

Examples

Philip Melanchthon was instrumental in shaping Protestant doctrine during the Reformation.
The collaboration between Martin Luther and Philip Melanchthon influenced generations of theologians.
Scholars regard Philip Melanchthon as a key architect of educational reform in Germany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martin Luther - German theologian and primary leader of the Protestant Reformation

Etymology

From Greekized form of his birth name, Philipp Schwartzerdt (German 'black earth').
