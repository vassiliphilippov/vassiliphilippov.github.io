Swertia speciosa

1. NOUN

/ swɚˈtiː.ə spiˈsoʊ.sə / (AmE), / swɜːˈtiː.ə spiˈsəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A tall herb native to the northwestern United States, with panicles of white flowers tinged with green; sometimes classified within the genus Swertia.
a tall northwestern U.S. herb with white-green flowers

Examples

The rare Swertia speciosa blooms each summer in the mountain meadows of Idaho.
Botanists debated whether Swertia speciosa should be classified under a different genus.
A patch of Swertia speciosa added striking color to the wildflower field.

Etymology

From the genus name Swertia (after the Swiss botanist Emanuel Sweart) and Latin speciosa, meaning 'showy' or 'beautiful'.
