plaque

1. NOUN

/ plæk / (AmE), / plɑːk / (BrE)
(Pathology) A small abnormal patch or deposit on or inside the body, such as on the skin, inside arteries, or in other tissues.
abnormal patch or deposit in the body
medical

Examples

The MRI scan showed a plaque on the patient's spinal cord.
Dental plaque can harden and lead to gum disease if not removed.
Doctors discovered a fatty plaque obstructing the artery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesion - an area of abnormal tissue change

Etymology

From French 'plaque' (plate, patch), from Middle Dutch 'placke' (patch).

2. NOUN

/ plæk / (AmE), / plɑːk / (BrE)
A flat ornamental tablet, typically made of metal or stone, that is fixed to a wall or surface to commemorate a person, event, or achievement.
commemorative tablet or plate

Examples

The museum installed a plaque to honor the founder's contributions.
A bronze plaque on the building marks the site of the historic event.
They unveiled a plaque dedicated to the victims of the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tablet - a flat slab of stone or metal with writing, often commemorative
  • Degree Lower: plaque - an accumulation of substances in the body, such as dental plaque

Etymology

From French 'plaque' (plate), from Middle Dutch 'placke' (patch, slab).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
