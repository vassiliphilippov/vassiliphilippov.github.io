indelicacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkəsi / (BrE)
an act, remark, or manner that is impolite or lacking in tact or sensitivity
impolite act or expression

Examples

His comment at dinner was a clear indelicacy that made everyone uncomfortable.
She apologized for her indelicacy in mentioning such a private matter.
The host was shocked by the guest’s indelicacy during the formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactlessness - lack of sensitivity in dealing with others
  • Synonym: rudeness - lack of courtesy or politeness

Etymology

From Latin 'indelicate', from 'in-' (not) + 'delicatus' (delicate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being indelicate or likely to offend; lack of sensitivity or refinement
lack of delicacy; offensiveness

Examples

The comedian's indelicacy shocked some members of the audience.
Her indelicacy in discussing personal topics made people uncomfortable.
He was criticized for the indelicacy of his remarks at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: delicacy - the quality of being delicate, sensitive, or refined
  • Synonym: offensiveness - the quality of being offensive

Etymology

From Latin 'indelicate' + '-acy', after 'delicacy'.
