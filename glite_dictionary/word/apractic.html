apractic

1. ADJ

/ əˈpræktɪk / (AmE), / əˈpræktɪk / (BrE)
having uncoordinated muscular movements, often as a symptom of a disorder in the central nervous system
having uncoordinated muscular movements
medical

Examples

The patient's apractic movements made it difficult for him to grasp and use everyday objects.
After his stroke, he developed an apractic gait that affected his walking.
Doctors noted the apractic symptoms during the neurological examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apraxic - affected by apraxia; showing impaired ability to perform purposeful movements

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'praktikos' (practical, doing); related to apraxia.
