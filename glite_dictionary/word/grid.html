grid

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
an electrode placed between the cathode and anode of a vacuum tube that regulates the flow of electrons through the tube
electrode in a vacuum tube

Examples

The grid in the vacuum tube helps control the amplification of the signal.
If the grid is damaged, the tube cannot properly regulate electron flow.
Adjusting the voltage on the grid changes the current passing through the tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrode - a conductor through which electricity enters or leaves an object or substance

/ ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a cooking utensil consisting of parallel metal bars, used for grilling fish or meat over a fire
parallel bars for grilling food

Examples

He placed the fish on the grid over the campfire.

After cleaning the grid, she prepared it for the next round of grilling.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a perforated or corrugated metal plate in a storage battery, used as a conductor and support for the active material
metal battery plate for conductance/support

Examples

The positive grid in the battery must be made of lead alloy to resist corrosion.
Damaged grids can cause a loss of capacity in lead-acid batteries.
The grid serves both as a conductor and as a support for the active material within each cell.

Etymology

From Latin 'cratis' (wattled framework), through French 'grille'.

4. NOUN

/ ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
A network of regularly spaced horizontal and vertical lines that forms squares or rectangles, often used as a guide for locating or arranging things.
network of intersecting lines

Examples

The city streets are laid out in a grid pattern.
Engineers use a grid to plan the placement of buildings.
The puzzle had a 10-by-10 grid of empty squares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lattice - an interlaced structure or pattern resembling a grid

Etymology

From Latin 'cratis' (wickerwork, lattice).

5. NOUN

/ ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a system of interconnected high-voltage transmission lines through which electrical power is distributed across a large area or region
system for electrical power distribution

Examples

A sudden storm caused the entire grid to lose power, plunging neighborhoods into darkness.
Upgrading the national grid is essential to support renewable energy sources like wind and solar.
Many remote communities are not connected to the main grid and rely on local generators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power grid - system of electrical transmission lines for distributing power
  • Antonym: off-grid - not connected to the main electrical grid

Etymology

From Latin 'gridale' (grating), referring to a network or lattice structure. The sense of electricity distribution arose by analogy to a netlike network.
