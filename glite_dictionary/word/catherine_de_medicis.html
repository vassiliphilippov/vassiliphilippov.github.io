Catherine de Medicis

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæθərɪn də ˈmɛdɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæθərɪn də ˈmɛdɪsɪs / (BrE)
(1519–1589) The queen of France as the wife of Henry II and regent during the minority of her son Charles IX, influential in 16th-century French politics.
queen and regent of France, 1519–1589

Examples

Catherine de Medicis played a pivotal role in French politics during the Wars of Religion.
Many historians debate whether Catherine de Medicis was a ruthless power broker or a pragmatic leader.
The court of Catherine de Medicis was renowned for its lavish balls and intrigue.

Etymology

From Italian Caterina de' Medici, a member of the powerful Medici family of Florence.
