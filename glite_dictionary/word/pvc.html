PVC

1. NOUN

/ pi vi si / (AmE), / piː viː siː / (BrE)
an irregular heartbeat caused by early contraction of the ventricles; frequent occurrences may indicate risk of more serious heart problems
irregular heartbeat from early ventricular contraction
medical

Examples

The patient's ECG showed several PVCs during the stress test.
Frequent PVCs can be a sign of underlying heart disease.
Doctors may monitor PVCs to assess the risk of ventricular fibrillation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - abnormal heart rhythm

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'premature ventricular contraction', used in medical terminology.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpiːˌviːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌpiːˌviːˈsiː / (BrE)
a tough, synthetic plastic polymer made from vinyl chloride, used especially in pipes, cables, and a wide range of products
synthetic plastic polymer (polyvinyl chloride)

Examples

Most modern plumbing pipes are made from PVC because it is durable and corrosion-resistant.
The electrician recommended using PVC cables for the new installation due to their safety features.
Old rubber hoses were replaced with PVC tubing to improve the system's performance.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'polyvinyl chloride.'
