reparation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of repairing something or making it function properly again
the act of repairing or restoring

Examples

After the storm, the city began the reparation of damaged roads and bridges.
The reparation of the old clock required special tools and patience.
Engineers performed reparation on the faulty equipment to ensure it met safety standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of restoring something to working order

Etymology

From Latin reparatio (restoration), from reparare (to repair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
compensation or action given to make amends for a wrong or injury
compensation for a wrong

Examples

The country agreed to pay reparation to those who suffered during the conflict.
As an act of reparation, he apologized publicly and donated to charity.
Victims sought reparation for the damage caused by the company.

Synset Examples

how can I make amends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amends - compensation or action to make up for a wrongdoing
  • Synonym: compensation - something given to offset a loss or injury
  • Antonym: punishment - penalty for a wrong or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'reparatio' (restoration, repair), from 'reparare' (to repair).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making amends, offering compensation, or paying for damage, loss, or injury caused to another person or group
compensation for harm or injury

Examples

The government offered reparation to the victims of the accident.
She demanded reparation for the damage caused to her reputation.
Many countries have paid reparation after wars as a form of compensation.

Synset Examples

an act for which there is no reparation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury
  • Synonym: atonement - making amends for a wrong or injury

Etymology

From Old French *reparation*, from Latin *reparatio* (a repairing, restoration).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13313636-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(usually plural) compensation paid by a defeated country to another for damage or injury during a war.
war compensation paid by defeated country

Examples

Germany struggled to pay the reparations imposed after the Treaty of Versailles.
The victors demanded substantial reparations for damages caused during the conflict.
Failure to fulfill reparations obligations led to economic instability in the defeated country.

Synset Examples

Germany was unable to pay the reparations demanded after World War I

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indemnity - money paid as compensation, especially by a country after war

Etymology

From Latin 'reparatio' (restoration, mending) via Old French 'reparation'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
