catalyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæt.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person or thing that quickly causes change or an important event to happen.
agent that causes change or events

Examples

Her speech was the catalyst for change within the organization.
The economic crisis served as a catalyst for political reform.
A personal tragedy became the catalyst that drove him to write his first novel.

Synset Examples

the invasion acted as a catalyst to unite the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - something that initiates an event or process
  • Synonym: cause - someone or something that brings about a result

Etymology

From catalysis, which comes from Greek katalusis (dissolution), from kata- (down) + lysis (loosening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkætəlɪst / (BrE)
A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without undergoing any permanent change itself.
substance that speeds up a chemical reaction
medical

Examples

Adding a catalyst can speed up the chemical reaction significantly.
In biology class, we learned how an enzyme acts as a catalyst in our bodies.
The presence of a platinum catalyst made the industrial process much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a biological catalyst

Etymology

From Greek katalusis (dissolution), from kataluein (to dissolve), from kata- (down) + luein (to loosen).
