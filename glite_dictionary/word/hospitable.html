hospitable

1. ADJ

/ hɑˈspɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / hɒˈspɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
willing to accept or consider new ideas, people, or experiences in a friendly and open way
open and welcoming to new ideas or people

Examples

The company is hospitable to innovation and encourages employees to share creative ideas.
She has always been hospitable to opinions different from her own.
Universities should be hospitable to diverse viewpoints in the classroom.

Synset Examples

hospitable to new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • Synonym: receptive - willing to listen to or accept new suggestions, ideas, or experiences
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly or opposed to something

Etymology

From Latin hospitābilis (hospitable), from hospes (host, guest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01993216-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ hɑˈspɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / hɒˈspɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an environment, place, etc.) providing favorable conditions for life, growth, or activity.
favorable to life or growth (of a place)

Examples

Desert plants have adapted to survive in a less hospitable climate than most.
The scientist searched for a hospitable environment to study new forms of bacteria.
Mars is currently not hospitable to human life due to its harsh conditions.

Synset Examples

soil sufficiently hospitable for forest growth
a hospitable environment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhospitable - not providing favorable conditions for life or growth

Etymology

From Latin 'hospitabilis' (hospitable, guest-friendly), from 'hospes' (guest, host).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01244974-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ hɑːˈspɪtəbəl / (AmE), / hɒˈspɪtəbəl / (BrE)
friendly and generous toward guests or strangers, making them feel welcome and comfortable
friendly and generous to guests or strangers

Examples

Our neighbors are always hospitable, inviting us in for tea whenever we pass by.
The family was incredibly hospitable, offering us food and a place to stay during the storm.
She has a hospitable nature and loves to entertain guests at her home.

Synset Examples

a good-natured and hospitable man
a hospitable act
hospitable invitations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfriendly - not kind or welcoming to guests or strangers
  • Synonym: welcoming - making people feel welcome; friendly to guests

Etymology

From Latin 'hospitalis' (of a guest or host), from 'hospes' (guest, host).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01246699-a
Synset POS: a
