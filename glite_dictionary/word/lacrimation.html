lacrimation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlækrəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlakrɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of producing or shedding tears, especially as a response to emotion or irritation
production or shedding of tears
medical

Examples

Exposure to onions can trigger excessive lacrimation in many people.
Certain medical conditions are characterized by persistent lacrimation.
His allergy medications helped reduce lacrimation caused by pollen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tearing - shedding tears
  • Synonym: crying - the act of shedding tears, often from emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' (tear) + '-ation' (action or process).
