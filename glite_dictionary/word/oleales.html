Oleales

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈliː.eɪlz / (AmE), / əʊˈliː.ə.liːz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of flowering plants, containing the family Oleaceae; in some classifications, included in the order Gentianales
former plant order (Oleaceae family)

Examples

The family Oleaceae was traditionally placed within the order Oleales.
Recent genetic studies have led to the dissolution of Oleales as a formal order.
Botanists once classified olives, ash trees, and lilacs under Oleales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oleaceae - the olive family of flowering plants

Etymology

Modern Latin, from 'Olea' (olive genus) + '-ales' (standard order suffix).
