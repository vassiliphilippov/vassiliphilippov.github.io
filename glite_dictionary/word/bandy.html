bandy

1. VERB

/ ˈbændi / (AmE), / ˈbændi / (BrE)
to toss or strike (a ball) back and forth, especially in a sport or playful context
toss or hit a ball back and forth

Examples

The children bandied the ball across the yard for hours.
She bandied the tennis ball with her friend on the driveway.
They bandied ideas back and forth just as easily as they did the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - to hit a ball with a bat or similar object

Etymology

From Middle French *bander* (to hit a ball to and fro), from *bande* (a band or strip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01149762-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbændi / (AmE), / ˈbandi / (BrE)
to mention or discuss (an idea, rumor, or topic) casually or repeatedly, often without serious consideration
mention or discuss casually

Examples

The committee bandied several ideas about before making a decision.
Rumors of his resignation were bandied around the office.
We shouldn't bandy accusations without any evidence.

Synset Examples

We bandied around these difficult questions

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: debate - discuss a subject formally; argue about (a subject)
  • Degree Lower: to suggest - to mention or propose as an idea without much discussion

Etymology

From Middle French 'bander' (to bandy, to strike back and forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00815313-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˈbæn.di / (AmE), / ˈbæn.di / (BrE)
(of legs) curved outward at the knees so that the knees are far apart when standing.
curved outward at the knees (of legs)

Examples

The child was born with bandy legs but improved with age.
He was teased at school for his noticeably bandy knees.
Some athletes develop slightly bandy legs from years of intense training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowlegged - having legs that curve outward at the knees
  • Antonym: knock-kneed - having knees that bend inward and touch when standing

Etymology

Perhaps from French bandé (bent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01022122-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ ˈbændi / (AmE), / ˈbændi / (BrE)
(of people) to exchange blows, especially as part of a dispute or fight.
to exchange blows or hits

Examples

After the disagreement, the two men began to bandy blows in the street.
Boxers have been known to bandy punches for several exhausting rounds.
Tempers flared, and soon they started to bandy fists in a chaotic altercation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to engage in a physical struggle or combat
  • Synonym: brawl - to fight noisily in a public place

Etymology

Probably from Old French *bander* (to fight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140872-v
Synset POS: v
