asymptotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæs.ɪmˈtɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪmˈtɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling an asymptote; approaching a value or curve but never quite reaching it, especially in mathematics.
relating to an asymptote

Examples

The graph of the function shows an asymptotic approach to zero.
As values increase, the curve becomes nearly asymptotic to the horizontal axis.
Many statistical distributions display asymptotic behavior as sample size increases.

Synset Examples

an asymptotic function

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asymptote - a straight line that a curve approaches but never meets

Etymology

From Greek asymptōtos (not falling together, not coinciding), from a- (not) + syn (together) + piptein (to fall).
