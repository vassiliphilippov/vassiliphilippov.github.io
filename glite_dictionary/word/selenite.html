selenite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛləˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪnaɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the selenite ion (SeO32−)
compound containing selenite ion
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized a new selenite to study its biological activity.
Some bacteria are capable of reducing selenite to elemental selenium.
Exposure to high levels of selenite can be toxic to living organisms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: selenate - a compound containing the selenate ion (SeO42−)

Etymology

From selenium plus -ite, following analogous naming conventions for mineral and ion names.
