to impart

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɑrt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to give a particular quality, feeling, or characteristic to something or someone
give a quality to something

Examples

A sense of elegance is imparted by the antique furniture in the living room.
His thoughtful remarks imparted a deeper meaning to the discussion.
The spices impart a unique flavor to the dish.

Synset Examples

Her presence lends a certain cachet to the company
The music added a lot to the play
She brings a special atmosphere to our meetings
This adds a light note to the program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lend - to give a particular quality or character to something
  • Synonym: to add - to increase or enhance the quality or value of something

Etymology

From Latin impartire (to share or give a part of), from in- (in) + partīre (to divide).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɑrt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to communicate or pass on knowledge, information, or skills to others
communicate or transmit knowledge or skills

Examples

Teachers have a duty to impart knowledge to their students.
The master chef was eager to impart his cooking skills to young apprentices.
She took every opportunity to impart wisdom she had gained over the years.

Synset Examples

give a secret to the Russians
leave your name and address here
impart a new skill to the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas
  • Synonym: to convey - to make ideas, feelings, or information known to someone
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired by another

Etymology

From Latin impartire, from in- 'in' + partīre 'to divide, share'.
