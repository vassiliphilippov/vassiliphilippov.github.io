sheepwalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpwɔːk / (BrE)
a piece of land or a farm that is principally used for grazing and raising sheep
land used for grazing sheep

Examples

The flock roamed freely across the sheepwalk during the summer months.
Many of the region's hills have been maintained as sheepwalks for centuries.
He invested in a large sheepwalk to expand his farming business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass suitable for grazing animals

Etymology

From 'sheep' + 'walk,' meaning an area where sheep walk or graze.
