square-toed

1. ADJ

/ skwɛr toʊd / (AmE), / skweə təʊd / (BrE)
(of shoes, boots, etc.) having a toe that is squared rather than rounded or pointed in shape.
having a square-shaped toe

Examples

He bought a pair of square-toed boots for the new job.
Her square-toed loafers stood out among the more classic styles.
The designer's latest collection features square-toed shoes in a variety of colors.

Synset Examples

square-toed shoes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pointed-toe - having shoes or boots with a toe that comes to a point

2. ADJ

/ ˈskwɛrˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈskweə təʊd / (BrE)
(of a person or attitude) extremely conventional, old-fashioned, or proper, often to an exaggerated degree.
exaggeratedly proper or conventional

Examples

Despite her young age, Maria had a square-toed view of social behavior.
His square-toed opinions were often at odds with his creative colleagues.
The square-toed teacher insisted on strict dress codes and formal language in class.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straitlaced - excessively strict in manners or morals
  • Synonym: conventional - following accepted customs and proprieties
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions; willing to discard traditional values
