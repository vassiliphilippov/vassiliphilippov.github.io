redeemable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːməbəl / (BrE)
capable of being improved, reformed, or saved from error or sin
capable of being improved or reformed

Examples

She believed that even the most troubled youth was still redeemable.
The novel's protagonist is a redeemable character, despite his many flaws.
The judge saw the defendant's remorse as a sign he was redeemable.

Synset Examples

a redeemable sinner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorrigible - not able to be corrected, improved, or reformed

Etymology

From Latin 'redimere' meaning 'to buy back, redeem'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00638167-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdiməbl / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːməbəl / (BrE)
able to be recovered or bought back by paying money or meeting a specified condition
able to be recovered upon payment or a condition

Examples

These tickets are only redeemable for store credit, not cash.
The pawned jewelry is redeemable within thirty days of payment.
Some coupons are redeemable only at participating locations.

Synset Examples

redeemable goods in a pawnshop

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irredeemable - not able to be redeemed or recovered

Etymology

From Latin *redimere* (to buy back, redeem) via Old French *redimer*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01963051-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːməbəl / (BrE)
(of something) able to be exchanged for cash or its equivalent, such as goods, services, or money.
able to be exchanged for cash or equivalent

Examples

The winning lottery ticket is only redeemable within ninety days of the draw date.
These points are redeemable for merchandise or flights with certain airlines.
The bond is redeemable at the end of its five-year term.

Synset Examples

a cashable check
cashable gambling chips
redeemable stocks and bonds
a redeemable coupon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashable - able to be converted into cash
  • Antonym: irretrievable - not capable of being retrieved or recovered, the opposite of redeemable in some financial contexts

Etymology

From Late Latin redimere (to buy back) + -able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920079-s
Synset POS: s
