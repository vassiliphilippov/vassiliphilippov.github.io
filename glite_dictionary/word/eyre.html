Eyre

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˈɛː / (BrE)
a large shallow salt lake in south central Australia, the country's largest lake and lowest point, situated about 35 feet below sea level
the largest lake in Australia

Examples

Lake Eyre rarely fills with water, often remaining a vast salt pan for years at a time.
Scientists conduct important environmental studies in the region surrounding Eyre.
Birdwatchers flock to Eyre during the occasional floods when thousands of waterbirds arrive.

Etymology

Named after Edward John Eyre (1815–1901), the British explorer who was the first European to see the lake.
