nun

1. NOUN

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
The fourteenth letter of the Hebrew alphabet, corresponding to the letter N in the Latin alphabet.
14th letter of the Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew letter nun is often used as an abbreviation in religious texts.
In Hebrew, a word spelled with nun at the beginning may change its meaning.
The scribes wrote a large nun at the start of the passage to highlight its importance.

Etymology

From Hebrew נוּן (nūn).

2. NOUN

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
a woman who belongs to a religious community and usually lives under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience
woman in a religious order

Examples

She decided to become a nun after spending several years volunteering in the community.
The nuns at the convent dedicate their lives to prayer and helping others.
At the museum, there was a painting of a medieval nun reading in a candlelit room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious community

Etymology

From Late Latin 'nonna' (nun, tutor), originally a term of respect for an old woman.

3. NOUN

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
A buoy, typically cone-shaped, used as a navigational marker in water to indicate channels or hazards.
cone-shaped navigational buoy

Examples

The red nun buoy marks the starboard (right) side of the channel when returning from sea.
As the boat approached the harbor, the captain pointed out the nun and can buoys to the crew.
Fishermen often use the nun as a reference point for their favorite fishing spots.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: can buoy - A cylindrical-shaped navigational buoy, the opposite of a nun buoy
  • Synonym: buoy - A floating device used as a navigational aid or marker
