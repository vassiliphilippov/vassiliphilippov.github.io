kaluresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæljəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæljuːˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the condition of having an abnormally high concentration of potassium in the urine
excess potassium in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The lab tests revealed significant kaluresis in the patient's urine sample.
Certain diuretic medications can cause kaluresis as a side effect.
Monitoring kaluresis is important for patients with kidney disorders.

Etymology

From Greek 'kali' (potassium) and 'uresis' (urination, from 'ouron' meaning urine).
