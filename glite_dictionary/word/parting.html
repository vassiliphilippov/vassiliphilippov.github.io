parting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪŋ / (BrE)
a line on the scalp visible where the hair is divided and combed in opposite directions
line on scalp where hair is divided

Examples

She wore her hair in a neat parting on the left side.
His parting was hardly visible after he changed hairstyles.
The hairstylist suggested a side parting to frame her face.

Synset Examples

his part was right in the middle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part - the line where hair is separated and combed in different directions

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of leaving or saying goodbye to someone; a farewell or departure, often accompanied by polite or emotional expressions
the act of leaving someone; a farewell

Examples

Their parting was filled with kind words and warm embraces.
He dreaded the moment of parting at the train station.
Shakespeare called parting 'such sweet sorrow.'

Synset Examples

he disliked long farewells
he took his leave
parting is such sweet sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye

Etymology

From Middle English partyn (to separate), from Old French partir, from Latin partire (to divide).
