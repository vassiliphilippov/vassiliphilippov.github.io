slipway

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈslɪp.weɪ / (BrE)
A sloping structure by the water used for launching or removing ships, especially from a shipyard or repair facility.
sloping structure for launching ships

Examples

The workers guided the boat carefully down the slipway and into the harbor.
After repairs were finished, the ship was lowered into the water from the slipway.
Each vessel leaves the yard via a steep slipway that leads directly into the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launching ramp - a ramp for launching boats into water
