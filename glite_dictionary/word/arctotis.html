Arctotis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to southern Africa and commonly known as African daisy.
genus of South African daisies

Examples

The vibrant orange blooms of Arctotis add a splash of color to the garden each summer.
Many gardeners choose Arctotis for its drought tolerance and striking foliage.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Arctotis species in southern Africa.

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + ous, otos (ear), referring to the shape of the plant's leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and subshrubs native to southern Africa, known for their brightly colored daisy-like flowers.
African daisy genus

Examples

The Arctotis blooms added vibrant color to the garden throughout the summer.
Gardeners often choose Arctotis for its drought tolerance and striking flowers.
Several species within the Arctotis genus are popular in ornamental horticulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African daisy - common name for species of Arctotis

Etymology

From Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'otos' (ear), referring to the shape of the leaves or appendages in some species.
