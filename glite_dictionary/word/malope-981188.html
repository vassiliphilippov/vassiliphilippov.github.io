malope

1. NOUN

/ məˈloʊpi / (AmE), / məˈləʊpi / (BrE)
A western Mediterranean annual plant with deep purple-red flowers, each surrounded by three large heart-shaped bracts.
Mediterranean annual plant with purple-red flowers

Examples

We planted malope in the garden to enjoy its vibrant purple-red blooms.
The malope thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil.
Among Mediterranean annuals, malope stands out for its showy, heart-shaped bracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a plant of the genus Malva with similar flowers and leaves

Etymology

From Modern Latin Malope, the botanical genus name, derived from Greek malōpē (a plant name, perhaps related to mallow).
