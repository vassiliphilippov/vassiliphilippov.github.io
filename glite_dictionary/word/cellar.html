cellar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlə / (BrE)
an underground room or storage space, especially one used for storing wine or other provisions
underground storage room, esp. for wine

Examples

They kept their finest vintage in the cellar beneath the house.
The restaurant has an impressive wine cellar that attracts collectors and connoisseurs.
After the renovation, the old cellar was transformed into a cozy tasting room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basement - a room below ground level in a building, often used for storage or living space

Etymology

From Old French *cellier* (storeroom), from Latin *cellarium* (pantry, storeroom), from *cella* (chamber).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlə / (BrE)
an underground room or space, especially one used for storing root vegetables or other food items
underground storage room for vegetables

Examples

The farmer kept the potatoes in a cool cellar through the winter.
We found old jars of pickled vegetables in the root cellar.
Building a cellar was essential for food preservation before refrigeration existed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basement - a floor of a building partly or entirely below ground level, used for storage or living space

Etymology

From Old French 'celier', from Latin 'cellarium' (storeroom, pantry), from 'cella' (chamber).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ə / (BrE)
a room below ground level in a building, typically used for storage
room below ground used for storage

Examples

The family stored old wine bottles in the cellar beneath their house.
After the storm, they checked the cellar for any signs of flooding.
He kept his tools and gardening supplies in the cool, dark cellar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basement - a floor of a building partly or entirely below ground level

Etymology

From Old French 'seler', from Latin 'cella' (small room, store chamber).
