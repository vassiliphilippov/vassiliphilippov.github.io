George Orwell

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ˈɔrwɛl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈɔːwɛl / (BrE)
(1903–1950) British writer and journalist, known for his novels about social injustice such as 'Animal Farm' and 'Nineteen Eighty-Four'.
British writer and journalist (1903–1950)

Examples

Many consider George Orwell’s '1984' a warning about government surveillance and loss of freedom.
The works of George Orwell have influenced generations of readers around the world.
As a journalist, George Orwell was equally renowned for his essays and political commentary.

Etymology

Pen name of Eric Arthur Blair; 'Orwell' was chosen after the River Orwell in Suffolk, England.
