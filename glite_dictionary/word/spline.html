spline

1. NOUN

/ splaɪn / (AmE), / splaɪn / (BrE)
a flexible strip of wood or rubber used as a guide for drawing smooth curved lines, especially in technical drawing or drafting
flexible drafting strip for drawing curves

Examples

The architect used a spline to draw the graceful curve of the roof.
In traditional drafting, a spline helps create accurate, flowing lines.
He secured the spline at several points to map out the desired shape.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'splen' meaning 'splinter'.

2. NOUN

/ splaɪn / (AmE), / splaɪn / (BrE)
a thin, flexible strip of wood or metal used in construction, carpentry, or drawing for making smooth curves or as a joining piece
thin strip of wood or metal

Examples

The carpenter used a spline to create the curved edge of the table.
A metal spline was inserted into the joint to reinforce the frame.
She drew the outline using a flexible spline to ensure a smooth curve.

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch splijn (thin strip or slat) or German Spline (thin strip for construction).
