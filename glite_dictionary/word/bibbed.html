bibbed

1. ADJ

/ bɪbd / (AmE), / bɪbd / (BrE)
having a bib, especially as part of a garment such as an apron or overalls.
having a bib (esp. on clothing)

Examples

She wore a bibbed apron while baking to protect her clothes.
The children in the choir were dressed in matching bibbed smocks.
A bibbed overall can be practical for gardening or painting.

Synset Examples

a bibbed apron

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bibless - lacking a bib

Etymology

From 'bib' (a piece of cloth worn from the neck on the chest) + '-ed'.
