chitterlings

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtɚlɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtəlɪŋz / (BrE)
the small intestines of pigs, prepared and cooked as food
pig intestines used as food

Examples

My grandmother makes delicious chitterlings every Christmas.
He cleaned the chitterlings thoroughly before boiling them.
Some people serve chitterlings with vinegar and hot sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripe - the edible stomach lining of a cow or other ruminant, used as food
  • Synonym: offal - the edible internal organs and entrails of an animal

Etymology

From Middle English 'chitling', of uncertain origin; related to 'chitter' in dialect for entrails.
