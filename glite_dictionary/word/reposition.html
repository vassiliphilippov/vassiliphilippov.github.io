to reposition

1. VERB

/ ˌriːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to move something to a different place or position
move something to another position

Examples

The decorator decided to reposition the sofa near the window for better lighting.
Surgeons had to reposition the bone fragments to ensure proper healing.
After seeing the reflection, he chose to reposition the mirror on the opposite wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to change the position of something
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from a place

Etymology

From re- (again) + position, ultimately from Latin positio (placing, situation).

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌriː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to move something to a different place or put it in a new position
move to a different position

Examples

She had to reposition the mirror so it reflected more light into the room.
The company decided to reposition its product in the market to attract new customers.
During the exercise, he was asked to reposition his legs for better balance.

Synset Examples

Shift one's position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to change the place or position of something
  • Synonym: shift - to cause something to move or change position, especially slightly

Etymology

From re- (again) + position, following the pattern of late Latin 'reponere' (to put back).
