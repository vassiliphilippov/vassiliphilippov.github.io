Michel de Montaigne

1. PROPN

/ miːˈʃɛl də mɒnˈteɪn / (AmE), / miːˈʃɛl də mɒ̃ˈtɛɲ / (BrE)
(1533–1592) French writer considered the originator of the modern essay, known for his influential works on philosophy and human nature.
French essayist and philosopher (1533–1592)

Examples

The works of Michel de Montaigne played a crucial role in developing the essay as a literary form.
Many philosophers have cited Michel de Montaigne as a major influence on modern thought.
During university, I read several essays by Michel de Montaigne for my literature course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essay - short piece of writing on a particular subject
