siderophilin

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌdɛrəˈfɪlɪn / (AmE), / saɪˌdɛrəˈfɪlɪn / (BrE)
a globulin protein in blood plasma that binds and transports iron throughout the body
iron-carrying blood protein
medical

Examples

Siderophilin plays a key role in regulating iron levels in the bloodstream.
Researchers discovered that siderophilin deficiency can lead to abnormal iron metabolism.
Serum siderophilin measurements are helpful in diagnosing iron-related disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transferrin - a specific siderophilin that binds and transports iron in blood plasma

Etymology

From Greek 'sideros' (iron) and 'philos' (loving), with '-in' for proteins.
