cytogeny

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtɑdʒəni / (AmE), / saɪˈtɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the origin and development and variation of cells
origin and development of cells
medical

Examples

The study of cytogeny provides important insights into how complex organisms develop from a single cell.
Recent research in cytogeny has revealed new mechanisms of cellular variation.
Understanding cytogeny is essential for advances in cancer biology and regenerative medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytology - the study of cells, especially their structure and function

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) and 'genesis' (origin, creation).
