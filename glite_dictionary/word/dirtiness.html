dirtiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɝːti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɜːtɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dirty or impure
state of being dirty

Examples

After the children played outside, the dirtiness of their hands surprised their mother.
The inspector criticized the dirtiness of the restaurant's kitchen.
She was embarrassed by the dirtiness of her clothes after the long hike.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state or quality of being clean

Etymology

From 'dirty' + '-ness', with 'dirty' from Old English 'dirt' (excrement, filth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɜr.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɜː.ti.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dirty or unsanitary
state of being dirty or unclean

Examples

The dirtiness of the kitchen made it unfit for food preparation.
Her mother scolded her for the dirtiness of her room.
Excessive dirtiness can lead to health problems in public spaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean
  • Synonym: filth - extreme dirt or foulness
  • Synonym: uncleanness - the state or quality of being unclean

Etymology

From 'dirty' + '-ness', where 'dirty' comes from Middle English 'dritty' (covered with excrement), related to 'dirt'.

Examples

Some people enjoy the dirtiness of satirical writing, while others find it offensive.
The film's script was censored to reduce the level of dirtiness in the dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscenity - offensive or indecent expression, especially in language or writing
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the quality of being free from obscenity or indecency in speech or writing

From 'dirty' + '-ness', patterned after English adjective and noun formation.
