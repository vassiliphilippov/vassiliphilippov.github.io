permanent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrmənənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːmənənt / (BrE)
a hairstyle in which the hair is treated with chemicals and heat to create lasting waves or curls
hair style made by chemical waving

Examples

After getting a permanent, her hair was full of soft curls.
Many salons offer a permanent as one of their most popular services.
She decided to try a permanent before her high school graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perm - short form for a permanent wave hairstyle

Etymology

Shortened from 'permanent wave'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mə.nənt / (BrE)
lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely; continuing or enduring without marked change in status or condition.
lasting; enduring without change

Examples

She listed her permanent address on the application form.
The museum seeks to create a permanent collection of modern art.
The effects of the new law could be permanent if not properly reviewed.

Synset Examples

permanent secretary to the president
permanent address
literature of permanent value

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent
  • Synonym: enduring - lasting for a long time

Etymology

From Latin 'permanens', present participle of 'permanere' (to remain to the end).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01758923-a

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mə.nənt / (BrE)
Lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged forever; not capable of being reversed or returned to the original condition.
lasting, irreversible

Examples

The accident resulted in permanent brain damage, altering his life forever.
Many tattoos are considered permanent, though some removal techniques do exist.
Scientists warn about the permanent effects of certain environmental changes.

Synset Examples

permanent brain damage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreversible - not able to be undone or altered
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin permanens, permanentis (remaining to the end), from permanēre (to endure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01764039-s
Synset POS: s
