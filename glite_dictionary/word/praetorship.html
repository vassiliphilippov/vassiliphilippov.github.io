praetorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpritərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpriːtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, rank, or term of a praetor in ancient Rome
the office or term of a Roman praetor

Examples

He achieved considerable fame during his praetorship through his wise judgments.
After his successful praetorship, he was elected consul the following year.
The law was established in the time of Cato's praetorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praetor - an elected magistrate in ancient Rome

Etymology

From Latin praetura (praetorship), from praetor (magistrate) + -ship.
