to reconcile

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛkənˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛkənsaɪl / (BrE)
to accept or come to terms with something unpleasant or inevitable
accept something unpleasant as inevitable

Examples

After a long struggle, she finally reconciled herself to the loss of her job.
He had to reconcile himself with the fact that things might never change.
Over time, they gradually reconciled themselves to living in a new country.

Synset Examples

He resigned himself to his fate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • Synonym: resign oneself (to) - to accept something unpleasant that cannot be changed

Etymology

From Latin 'reconciliare' meaning 'to bring together again'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00734417-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to restore friendly relations between or bring into agreement after a disagreement or conflict
restore friendly relations; bring into agreement

Examples

After years of silence, the brothers finally decided to reconcile.
Efforts to reconcile the two sides of the dispute proved successful.
She tried to reconcile with her old friend after their falling out.

Synset Examples

After some discussion we finally made up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to become friends again after a quarrel
  • Antonym: to alienate - to make someone feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From Latin 'reconciliare' (to bring together again, win over), from 're-' (again) + 'conciliare' (to make friendly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00766947-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to make two or more things compatible or consistent with each other; to bring into agreement or harmony
to bring into agreement or harmony

Examples

It is often difficult to reconcile work commitments with family life.
She struggled to reconcile her beliefs with the harsh reality.
The project aims to reconcile environmental protection with economic development.

Synset Examples

harmonize one's goals with one's abilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - to bring into agreement or harmony

Etymology

From Latin conciliare (to bring together), via Old French reconcilier.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00483404-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to make two or more ideas, facts, or situations agree or be compatible with each other, often after a disagreement or difference
make compatible or consistent

Examples

It was difficult to reconcile his beliefs with the facts.
We must reconcile our budget constraints with the project's requirements.
She struggled to reconcile her memories of home with the reality she faced.

Synset Examples

The scientists had to accommodate the new results with the existing theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - bring into agreement or accord
  • Antonym: conflict - be incompatible or at odds

Etymology

From Latin 'reconciliare' (to bring together again), from 're-' (again) + 'conciliare' (to make friendly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00483824-v
Synset POS: v
