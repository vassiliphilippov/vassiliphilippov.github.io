burial

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛriəl / (BrE)
The act or ceremony of placing a dead body in a grave, usually with rites or rituals.
act of placing a dead body in a grave

Examples

The family held a private burial for their grandfather in the local cemetery.
Traditional burial practices differ widely across cultures and religions.
Plans for the burial were made shortly after the funeral service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cremation - the disposal of a dead body by burning to ashes
  • Synonym: interment - the act of placing a body in a grave or tomb; burial

Etymology

From Old English byrġels (burial place, tomb), related to 'bury'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛriəl / (BrE)
the act or process of hiding or concealing something under the ground
act of hiding something underground

Examples

The burial of hazardous waste must be carefully managed to prevent contamination.
Archaeologists discovered the burial of several objects beneath ancient ruins.
He arranged the burial of the time capsule in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interment - the act of placing a dead body in the ground; burial of a corpse
  • Antonym: exhumation - the act of digging something out of the ground, especially a body

Etymology

From Old English *byrgels* (tomb, grave), related to *byrgan* (to bury).
