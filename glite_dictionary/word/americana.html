Americana

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
objects or artifacts, such as books, furniture, or art, that are characteristic of the United States or its culture.
objects characteristic of American culture

Examples

The museum collection features a stunning array of Americana, including antique quilts and early American paintings.
She has been collecting Americana for decades, focusing on items from the 19th century.
The festival celebrated Americana with displays of folk art, vintage signs, and classic cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Americanism - a custom, trait, or tradition originating in the United States

Etymology

From Latin America (the continent) + -ana (things associated with).
