commitment

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
a statement or promise in which someone formally pledges to do something or to support a particular course of action
a formal pledge or promise

Examples

We received a written commitment from the contractor to finish the work by July.
Her commitment to donate a portion of her salary inspired others to give as well.
The company made a public commitment to reduce its carbon emissions over the next decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise - a declaration that one will do or refrain from doing something
  • Synonym: guarantee - an assurance or pledge, often legally binding
  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement

Etymology

From Latin committere (to bring together, to entrust, to commit), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the official act of sending someone to prison or to a mental hospital, especially by a court order
official consignment to institution or prison
legal

Examples

The judge ordered the commitment of the defendant to the state mental hospital.
After a brief hearing, the court ruled for his commitment to prison.
Her lawyer appealed the commitment, claiming the confinement was unjustified.

Etymology

From commit + -ment, ultimately from Latin committere (to entrust) and Latin -mentum (suffix forming nouns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01168182-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
an obligation or engagement, especially one that involves a financial or contractual responsibility
financial or contractual obligation

Examples

The company had several long-term commitments to its partners in Asia.
Due to prior financial commitments, she was unable to invest in the new project.
He managed his commitments carefully to avoid any legal disputes.

Synset Examples

his business commitments took him to London

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligation - a duty or commitment; something one is required to do
  • Synonym: liability - a thing for which someone is responsible, especially a financial debt

Etymology

From commit + -ment, dating from the late Middle English period.

4. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause, activity, or course of action; a promise or firm decision to do or support something
dedication to a cause or activity

Examples

His long commitment to social justice inspired many others to join the cause.
She questioned their commitment to finishing the project on time.
Joining the team requires a serious commitment of time and effort.

Synset Examples

they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - the quality of being committed or devoted to a task or purpose
  • Synonym: devotion - strong attachment or loyalty to a cause or activity
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or commitment

Etymology

From commit + -ment, ultimately from Latin committere (to entrust, commit).
