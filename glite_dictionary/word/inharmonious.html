inharmonious

1. ADJ

/ ɪn.hɑrˈmoʊ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ɪn.hɑːˈməʊ.ni.əs / (BrE)
Lacking harmony or agreement among parts; not forming a pleasing or consistent whole.
not harmonious; discordant

Examples

The inharmonious colors in the painting made it difficult to look at for long.
Their inharmonious relationship eventually led to many disputes within the team.
The novel's plot was criticized for its inharmonious combination of serious and comic elements.

Synset Examples

an inharmonious (or incongruous) mixture of architectural styles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmonious - having parts that are in agreement or form a pleasing whole

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (not) and 'harmonious', from Latin 'harmonia'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.hɑːrˈmoʊ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɑːˈməʊ.ni.əs / (BrE)
lacking harmony; not forming a pleasing or consistent whole; discordant.
not in harmony; discordant

Examples

The colors she chose for the living room were inharmonious and clashed badly.
Their inharmonious voices made the choir performance unpleasant.
An inharmonious relationship among team members can hinder progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement
  • Antonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + harmonious, from Greek harmonia (agreement, harmony).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01167616-a
Synset POS: a
