WIMP

1. NOUN

/ wɪmp / (AmE), / wɪmp / (BrE)
A hypothetical subatomic particle with large mass that interacts weakly with ordinary matter and is thought to be a constituent of dark matter.
hypothetical dark matter particle

Examples

Scientists have proposed the existence of WIMPs to explain the unseen mass in galaxies.
No WIMP has yet been detected by particle physics experiments, despite decades of searching.
Theoretical models predict billions of WIMPs may pass through your body every second without interaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark matter - matter that does not emit light or energy, detectable by its gravitational effects

Etymology

Acronym of 'Weakly Interacting Massive Particle'.
