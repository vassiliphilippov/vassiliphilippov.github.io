sprain

1. NOUN

/ spreɪn / (AmE), / spreɪn / (BrE)
an injury to a joint caused by stretching or tearing its ligaments, resulting in pain and swelling
injury to a joint's ligaments

Examples

He suffered a sprain after twisting his ankle during the game.
Applying ice immediately can help reduce swelling from a sprain.
The doctor examined my wrist and confirmed it was just a sprain, not a fracture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strain - an injury involving muscles or tendons rather than ligaments

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *espraindre* (to wring), from Latin *exprimere* (to press out).

2. VERB

/ spreɪn / (AmE), / spreɪn / (BrE)
to injure a joint by suddenly twisting it, causing pain and swelling but not dislocation or fracture
injure a joint by twisting

Examples

She accidentally sprained her wrist while playing tennis last weekend.
If you sprain your ankle, it's important to rest and apply ice.
He sprained his knee during the soccer match and had to leave the field.

Synset Examples

wrench one's ankle
The wrestler twisted his shoulder
the hikers sprained their ankles when they fell
I turned my ankle and couldn't walk for several days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to turn sharply or wrap around, often causing injury
  • Synonym: strain - to injure by stretching too much

Etymology

Probably from Middle French 'espreindre' (to wring) or from Old French 'espreindre', ultimately from Latin 'exprimere' (to press out).
