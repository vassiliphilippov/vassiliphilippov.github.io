degenerate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʒɛnərət / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərət / (BrE)
lacking moral standards or self-control; unrestrained by convention or morality, often regarded as morally corrupt or depraved
morally corrupt; unrestrained by convention

Examples

The degenerate lifestyle of the wealthy elite shocked many in the community.
Some considered the nightclub a place for degenerate behavior and excess.
She refused to associate with degenerate individuals who ignored all social norms.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depraved - morally corrupt; wicked
  • Antonym: virtuous - having high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin degenerate, past participle of degenerare (to decline, deteriorate), from de- (down from) + genus (birth, kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01553629-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ dɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
to become worse or inferior in quality, character, or condition; to decline or deteriorate
to become worse over time

Examples

The conversation began politely but soon degenerated into a heated argument.
If left untreated, the disease may degenerate rapidly, leading to serious complications.
Social order can degenerate when the rule of law is not maintained.

Synset Examples

Her condition deteriorated
Conditions in the slums degenerated
The discussion devolved into a shouting match

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better

Etymology

From Latin 'degenerare' (to decline from one's kind, to deteriorate), from 'de-' (down from) + 'genus' (race, kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00092045-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərət / (BrE)
a person regarded as morally corrupt or having declined from a former or normal state, especially with reference to sexual behavior
morally corrupt person

Examples

Some critics argued that the novel's hero was a degenerate unworthy of admiration.
The neighborhood became known as a refuge for degenerates and criminals.
He was labeled a degenerate because of his scandalous lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprobate - a morally unprincipled person
  • Antonym: upstanding citizen - a person regarded as honest and virtuous

Etymology

From Latin 'degeneratus', past participle of 'degenerare' (to degenerate, decline).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10438792-n
Synset POS: n
