gyneolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌgɪniˈɒlətri / (AmE), / ˌgɪniˈɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship or excessive reverence of women
worship of women

Examples

Some critics described the poet’s work as a case of gyneolatry rather than feminism.
The novel explores themes of gyneolatry in a patriarchal society.
His tendency toward gyneolatry made genuine relationships difficult.

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman) + 'latreia' (worship).
