cyprian

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪp.ri.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɪp.ri.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling the ancient orgiastic worship of Aphrodite on Cyprus.
relating to worship of Aphrodite on Cyprus

Examples

The poet described the festival as a cyprian celebration of love and ecstasy.
Her paintings evoke a cyprian atmosphere, hinting at ancient sensual rituals.
Historians debate whether the ceremony truly reflected cyprian traditions of Aphrodite's cult.

Etymology

From Latin Cyprianus (of Cyprus), referring to the island associated with Aphrodite's cult.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪpriən / (AmE), / ˈsɪpriən / (BrE)
A woman who engages in sexual intercourse for money; a prostitute (archaic or literary).
archaic term for a prostitute
archaic

Examples

The poet alluded to the life of a cyprian in his verse.
In certain novels of the era, the cyprian was depicted as both tragic and alluring.
A lavish party was attended by nobles, actors, and the occasional cyprian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

From Latin 'Cypria', referring to Cyprus, home of Aphrodite, the goddess of love.
