perishability

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being likely to decay, spoil, or be destroyed, especially over a short period.
quality of being liable to spoil or decay

Examples

The perishability of fresh produce makes it essential to transport it quickly to markets.
Food manufacturers must account for the perishability of dairy products when planning distribution.
Advances in packaging technology can help reduce the perishability of certain foods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: durability - the ability to withstand decay or wear

Etymology

From 'perishable' + '-ity', from Old French and Latin roots meaning 'liable to perish'.
