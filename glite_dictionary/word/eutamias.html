Eutamias

1. NOUN

/ juːˈteɪ.mi.əs / (AmE), / juːˈteɪ.mi.as / (BrE)
A genus of chipmunks native to western America and Asia.
genus of western American and Asian chipmunks

Examples

The Siberian chipmunk belongs to the genus Eutamias and is native to northern Asia.
Scientists studied the habitat adaptation of various Eutamias species in different regions.
Eutamias chipmunks are recognized for their distinct stripes and burrowing behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small, striped rodent found mainly in North America

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek eu- (good) + tamias (steward, housekeeper).
