hereunder

1. ADV

/ hɪrˈʌndər / (AmE), / hɪəˈʌndə / (BrE)
in a following part of this document, statement, or matter; below this point
below in this document; further on
legal

Examples

Any obligations not specified hereunder shall not be binding on the parties.
Payment will be made according to the schedule outlined hereunder.
The rights granted hereunder shall remain effective until termination.

Synset Examples

the landlord demises unto the tenant the premises hereinafter called the demised premises
the terms specified hereunder

Etymology

From 'here' + 'under'; used in legal and formal contexts since the 15th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00357692-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ hɪrˈʌndər / (AmE), / hɪəˈʌndə / (BrE)
according to or under the terms of this document, law, or agreement
under the terms of this document
legal

Examples

The parties agree to the obligations stated hereunder.
Any disputes will be resolved as provided hereunder.
All warranties are expressly set forth hereunder.

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'here' + 'under', meaning 'under this' (document or item, especially in legal texts).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00033624-r
Synset POS: r
