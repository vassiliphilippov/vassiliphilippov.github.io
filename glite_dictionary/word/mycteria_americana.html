Mycteria americana

1. NOUN

/ mɪkˈtɪriə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / mɪkˈtɪəriə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A large American stork, also known as the wood stork, with a downward-curved bill, found in wooded swamps of the New World tropics.
American stork (wood stork) species

Examples

The Mycteria americana can often be seen wading through shallow waters in Florida's wetlands.
Researchers are tracking the migration patterns of Mycteria americana to study habitat changes.
The long, curved bill of the Mycteria americana helps it catch fish and amphibians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood stork - Common name for Mycteria americana, large American stork

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name 'Mycteria' from Greek 'mykter' (nose) referring to the bill, 'americana' indicating American origin.
