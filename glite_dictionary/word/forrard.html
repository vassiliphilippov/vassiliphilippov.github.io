forrard

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔr.ərd / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.əd / (BrE)
(dialect, informal) At, to, or toward the front; forward.
dialect word for 'forward'
informal archaic

Examples

The old sailor shouted, 'Move forrard, lads, and make ready the anchor!'
She walked forrard to greet the visitors as soon as they arrived.
On ships, orders such as 'Go forrard!' were once commonplace among the crew.

Synset Examples

he faced forward
step forward
she practiced sewing backward as well as frontward on her new sewing machine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - At or toward the front

Etymology

Dialectal variant of 'forward', influenced by colloquial pronunciation.
