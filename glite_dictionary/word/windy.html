windy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪndi / (AmE), / ˈwɪndi / (BrE)
(of an action or process) marked by speed, force, or unpredictability, as if like the wind.
resembling wind in speed or force

Examples

He made a windy escape from the meeting, rushing out before anyone could stop him.
The quarterback completed a windy dash across the field to score the winning touchdown.
Her windy entrance took everyone by surprise, as she burst into the room with energy and speed.

Synset Examples

a windy dash home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift - moving or capable of moving with great speed
  • Antonym: gentle - mild or moderate in action or effect

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪndi / (AmE), / ˈwɪndi / (BrE)
using or containing more words than necessary; long-winded or verbose in expression.
using too many words; verbose

Examples

The politician's windy speech left the audience impatient and bored.
Her windy explanation of the process made it hard to follow her main point.
Tired of windy lecturers, the students preferred concise presentations.

Synset Examples

long-winded (or windy) speakers
verbose and ineffective instructional methods
newspapers of the day printed long wordy editorials
proceedings were delayed by wordy disputes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbose - using more words than are needed; wordy
  • Synonym: long-winded - talking or writing at length, especially in a tedious way
  • Antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • Synonym: wordy - using more words than necessary
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɪn.di / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.di / (BrE)
(of weather, places, or conditions) characterized by or exposed to a lot of wind or breezes.
characterized by a lot of wind

Examples

The coast was especially windy in the afternoon, making it difficult to walk along the beach.
We decided to postpone the picnic because the weather was too windy for comfort.
Standing on the windy hilltop, she could see the entire valley below.

Synset Examples

blowy weather
a windy bluff

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of weather) free from wind or storm
  • Degree Lower: breezy - pleasantly windy; with a light wind
  • Degree Higher: stormy - (of weather) with strong winds and rain, worse than windy
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
