erythroderma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪθroʊˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪθrəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by widespread abnormal redness of the skin.
abnormal widespread redness of the skin
medical

Examples

The patient developed erythroderma as a reaction to the medication.
Severe cases of erythroderma require immediate medical attention.
The dermatologist diagnosed the persistent redness as erythroderma.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'derma' (skin).
