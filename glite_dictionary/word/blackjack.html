2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈblæk.dʒæk / (BrE)
A gambling card game in which players try to obtain cards with a total value as close as possible to 21, without exceeding it, and beat the dealer's total.
card game aiming for 21

Examples

He won a lot of money playing blackjack at the casino.
The dealer shuffled the deck and dealt the first round of blackjack.
Many people consider blackjack a game of both luck and skill.

  • Synonym: twenty-one - another name for the gambling card game blackjack

3. VERB

/ ˈblækˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈblak.dʒak / (BrE)
to compel or intimidate someone by using threats or pressure
to threaten or pressure someone

Examples

The company tried to blackjack its suppliers into lowering their prices.
She refused to let anyone blackjack her into making a decision she didn't support.
They blackjacked him with constant threats of legal action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by using threats
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, especially to make them do what one wants

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'blackjack,' referring to a club used as a weapon, hence the sense of coercion.
