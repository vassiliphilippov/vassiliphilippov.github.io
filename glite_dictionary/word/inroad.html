inroad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪnrəʊd / (BrE)
an advance into an area that affects it; a significant encroachment or intrusion, often into a market, field, or sphere
encroachment or intrusion

Examples

The new technology is beginning to make inroads into traditional manufacturing methods.
With improved advertising, the company made significant inroads into the Asian market.
Education reforms have started to create inroads against illiteracy in remote areas.

Synset Examples

they made inroads in the United States market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encroachment - the act of gradually taking over someone else's rights or property
  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering a place or situation without permission

Etymology

From Old English 'inrād' (an invasion), from 'in-' + 'rād' (road, ride).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.rəʊd / (BrE)
A sudden attack or incursion into a place, typically to seize or plunder it; an invasion or hostile attack.
hostile attack or incursion

Examples

The army made a daring inroad into enemy territory under the cover of darkness.
Viking inroads frequently threatened the stability of coastal villages during the Middle Ages.
Historians studied the inroad as a turning point in the region's political development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incursion - a sudden attack or invasion
  • Synonym: raid - a sudden attack for the purpose of stealing or causing damage
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing from enemy forces after an attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'inrode', from 'in-' + 'road' (in the sense of 'riding' or 'journey').
