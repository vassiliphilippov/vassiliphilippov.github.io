pasteurizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.tʃɚˌaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈpæs.tjʊəˌraɪ.zə / (BrE)
A machine or apparatus used for pasteurizing liquids, especially milk, by heating them to a specific temperature to destroy harmful microorganisms.
machine for pasteurizing liquids

Examples

The dairy installed a new pasteurizer to ensure all their milk products are safe for consumption.
After cleaning the pasteurizer, the technician prepared it for the next batch of juice.
Modern pasteurizers can process thousands of liters of milk every hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasteurization - the process of heating a liquid to destroy harmful microorganisms

Etymology

From the surname of Louis Pasteur, French scientist who developed pasteurization, plus the agent suffix '-izer'.
