Macrozamia communis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈzeɪmiə kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / ˌmækrəʊˈzeɪmɪə kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
a large palmlike evergreen cycad native to New South Wales, Australia, known for its stiff, glossy leaves and attractive appearance
Australian evergreen cycad species

Examples

The Macrozamia communis is a common sight in the forests of eastern Australia.
Gardeners value Macrozamia communis for its resilience and striking foliage.
The seeds of Macrozamia communis are toxic if ingested without proper preparation.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Macrozamia' (macro- 'large' + -zamia, type of cycad), and Latin 'communis' meaning 'common'.
