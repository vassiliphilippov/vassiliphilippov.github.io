fruitage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruː.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfruː.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the yield or production of fruit by a plant or tree
yield of fruit

Examples

The farmer measured the fruitage of each tree at the end of the season.
This variety of orange is grown mainly for its abundant fruitage.
After several years of careful cultivation, the orchard finally reached its peak fruitage.

Synset Examples

a tree highly recommended for its fruitage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crop - total yield of cultivated plants

Etymology

Formed from 'fruit' + suffix '-age', similar to 'leafage' or 'foliage'.
