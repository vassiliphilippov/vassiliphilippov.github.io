2. ADJ

/ slaɪst / (AmE), / slaɪst / (BrE)
(of food, especially meat or bread) cut into thin pieces or sections.
cut into thin pieces (food)

Examples

The chef arranged the sliced ham on a platter for the guests.
She bought a loaf of sliced bread to make sandwiches for lunch.
He prefers his turkey sliced thin for his sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsliced - not cut into pieces

Etymology

From the verb 'slice', which comes from Old French *esclicer* (to split), from *es-* (out) + *clicer* (to cut).
