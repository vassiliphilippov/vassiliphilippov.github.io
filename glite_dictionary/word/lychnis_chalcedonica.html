Lychnis chalcedonica

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪk.nɪs ˌkæl.sɪˈdɑ.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌlɪk.nɪs ˌkæl.sɪˈdɒ.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial garden plant with bright scarlet flowers in dense terminal clusters, commonly known as Maltese cross.
Eurasian garden perennial with scarlet flowers

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Lychnis chalcedonica make it a favorite in cottage gardens.
Gardeners often plant Lychnis chalcedonica to attract butterflies and add bright color to borders.
In early summer, the dense scarlet blooms of Lychnis chalcedonica create a striking display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maltese cross - Common name for Lychnis chalcedonica, a scarlet-flowered garden plant

Etymology

From Latin *Lychnis*, the genus name, and *chalcedonica*, referring to Chalcedon (an ancient city in Asia Minor).
