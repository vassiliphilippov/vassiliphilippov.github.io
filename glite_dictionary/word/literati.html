literati

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪtəˈrɑːti / (AmE), / ˌlɪtəˈrɑːti / (BrE)
People who are well-educated and interested in literature; the literary intellectuals or scholarly elite.
the literary intellectual elite

Examples

The literati gathered at the book launch to discuss the author's latest novel.
In the 19th century, the city's literati shaped public opinion through essays and critiques.
She aspired to join the circle of literati who influenced the country's cultural trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligentsia - the intellectual elite or educated class, especially in cultural or artistic circles

Etymology

From Italian 'literati' (learned men), from Latin 'litteratus' (lettered, learned).
