to surpass

1. VERB

/ sərˈpæs / (AmE), / səˈpɑːs / (BrE)
To be greater or better than someone or something; to exceed a specified standard or amount.
be greater than; exceed

Examples

Her achievements surpass all expectations.
The new bridge surpasses the old one in both length and beauty.
He hoped his skills would surpass those of his competitors.

Synset Examples

Their loyalty exceeds their national bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - be greater in number or amount than
  • Synonym: to outstrip - move faster than and overtake
  • Antonym: to fall short - fail to reach a desired amount or standard

Etymology

From Old French 'surpasser', from sur- (over) + passer (to pass).
