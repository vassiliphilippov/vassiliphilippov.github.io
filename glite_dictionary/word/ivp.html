IVP

1. NOUN

/ aɪ vi pi / (AmE), / aɪ viː piː / (BrE)
an X-ray examination of the kidneys and ureters after injection of a radiopaque dye
X-ray of kidneys with dye injection
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an IVP to assess the function of her kidneys.
An IVP can reveal obstructions or abnormalities in the urinary tract.
Before his surgery, he underwent an IVP as part of the preoperative tests.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Intravenous Pyelogram: from Latin intra- (within) + venosus (veined) and Greek pyelos (basin, i.e. pelvis of the kidney) + -gram (record or writing).

2. NOUN

/ aɪ vi pi / (AmE), / aɪ viː piː / (BrE)
a medical imaging procedure in which a contrast dye is injected into a vein to highlight the kidneys and urinary tract on X-rays
X-ray test for kidneys and urinary tract
medical

Examples

My doctor ordered an IVP to investigate the cause of my frequent urinary infections.
The radiologist reviewed the results of the patient's IVP before making a diagnosis.
Patients are usually asked to fast for several hours before having an IVP.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intravenous pyelogram - an X-ray exam using contrast dye to view the kidneys, ureters, and bladder

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'intravenous pyelogram'.
