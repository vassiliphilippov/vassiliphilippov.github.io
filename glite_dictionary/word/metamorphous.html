metamorphous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfoʊs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəʊs / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from metamorphosis, especially of rocks changed in form or structure by heat and pressure.
relating to metamorphosis, especially of rocks

Examples

Marble is a classic example of a metamorphous rock formed from limestone.
The geologist explained the metamorphous changes that granite undergoes deep within the Earth.
The region is known for its abundance of metamorphous rock formations created by ancient tectonic activity.

Synset Examples

metamorphic stage
marble is a metamorphic rock that takes a high polish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metamorphic - relating to the process of change, especially in rocks by heat and pressure

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōsis (transformation), via Latin metamorphosis.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəs / (BrE)
Produced by or resulting from metamorphosis; having undergone a transformation, especially of structure or form.
produced by metamorphosis

Examples

Many amphibians become metamorphous as they transition from their larval to adult forms.
The process turns a caterpillar into a metamorphous butterfly.
Some minerals exhibit metamorphous characteristics after years of geological transformation.

Synset Examples

most insects are metamorphic as witness the stages as a butterfly develops from a caterpillar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metamorphic - characterized by or pertaining to metamorphosis

Etymology

From Greek 'metamorphōsis' meaning transformation.
