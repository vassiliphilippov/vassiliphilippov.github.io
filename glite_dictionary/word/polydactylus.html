Polydactylus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Polynemidae, commonly known as threadfins, found in tropical and subtropical waters.
a genus of threadfin fish

Examples

Species in the genus Polydactylus are commonly found in the Indian and Pacific Oceans.
The Polydactylus fish is known for the long filaments that extend from its pectoral fins.
Researchers observed several new behaviors in young Polydactylus specimens during the study.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'daktylos' (finger), referring to the multiple thread-like filaments on their fins.

2. NOUN

/ pɑliˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Polynemidae, known as threadfins, characterized by their multiple pectoral filaments.
a genus of threadfin fishes

Examples

The genus Polydactylus includes several commercially important species found in tropical seas.
Ichthyologists recently discovered a new species within the Polydactylus genus off the coast of Indonesia.
Species in the Polydactylus genus are often identified by their long, thread-like pectoral fins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polynemidae - the family of threadfin fishes

Etymology

From Greek polydaktulos, meaning 'many-fingered', referring to the multiple pectoral filaments.
