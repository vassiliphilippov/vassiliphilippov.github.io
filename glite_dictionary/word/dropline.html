dropline

1. NOUN

/ ˈdroʊˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdrəʊˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A type of newspaper or magazine headline in which the top line is aligned to the left margin and each successive line is indented further to the right.
indented style of headline

Examples

The editor chose a dropline for the front page to make the headline stand out.
Learning to create a dropline is part of every journalism student's training.
That section of the newspaper often uses a dropline for feature stories.
