constructor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌktər / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
a person or company that contracts for and supervises the building or construction of something, especially buildings
person supervising construction

Examples

The constructor oversaw every stage of the new hospital's development.
As a leading constructor, the company was responsible for several city landmarks.
The contract was awarded to a constructor with decades of experience in bridge building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: builder - a person or company that constructs buildings or other structures
  • Antonym: demolisher - a person or company that destroys or knocks down structures

Etymology

From Latin 'constructus', past participle of 'construere' (to heap up, build).
