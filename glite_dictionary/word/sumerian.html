Sumerian

1. NOUN

/ suːˈmɛriən / (AmE), / suːˈmɪəriən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people who lived in Sumer, in southern Mesopotamia, one of the earliest known civilizations
member of ancient Sumerian people

Examples

The ancient Sumerian civilization developed one of the earliest writing systems in the world.
Archaeologists have discovered several Sumerian artifacts in the ruins of southern Mesopotamia.
A Sumerian might have participated in rituals at the ziggurats in Ur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - a member of the ancient people of Babylon
  • Synonym: Akkadian - a member of an ancient Semitic people living in Akkad

Etymology

From Sumer, the name of the ancient region, + -ian.

2. ADJ

/ suˈmɪriən / (AmE), / suːˈmɪəriən / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Sumer or its inhabitants, language, or culture
of ancient Sumer or its people

Examples

The Sumerian civilization is considered one of the world's earliest urban cultures.
Archaeologists have discovered many Sumerian tablets inscribed with cuneiform script.
The museum displayed artifacts from the Sumerian period of Mesopotamian history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - relating to ancient Babylonia or its people
  • Synonym: Akkadian - relating to ancient Akkad or its people

Etymology

From Sumer, the name of the ancient region in southern Mesopotamia, + -ian.
