Lemnos

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛm.nɔs / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.nɒs / (BrE)
a Greek island in the northern Aegean Sea, known for its reddish-brown medicinal clay
Greek island in the Aegean Sea

Examples

The ancient Greeks prized Lemnos for its medicinal earth used in healing rituals.
After their voyage, the sailors docked at Lemnos to trade for the island's famous clay.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts on Lemnos dating back to prehistoric times.
