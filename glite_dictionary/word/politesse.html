politesse

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈtɛs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtɛs / (BrE)
formal politeness or refined courtesy in speech or behavior; especially consideration towards others in social situations
formal politeness or refined courtesy

Examples

His politesse charmed everyone at the formal dinner.
She appreciated the politesse with which he addressed the guests.
A certain level of politesse is still expected in diplomatic meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior, especially in a social context
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of manners or courtesy

Etymology

From French 'politesse', from 'poli' (polite), from Latin 'politus' (polished, refined).
