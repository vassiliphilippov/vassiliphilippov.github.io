bo'sun

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊsən / (AmE), / ˈbəʊsən / (BrE)
a petty officer on a merchant ship responsible for supervising the work of the crew and maintaining the ship's equipment and fittings
ship's officer in charge of crew and equipment

Examples

The bo'sun gave orders to the deckhands to secure the ropes before the storm hit.
After years as a sailor, she became the ship's bo'sun and managed maintenance duties.
The experienced bo'sun quickly organized the crew for emergency repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boatswain - the original full form, official title for a ship's crew supervisor

Etymology

Shortened and altered from 'boatswain', from Old English 'bātswegen' (boat attendant).
