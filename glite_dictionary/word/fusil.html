fusil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːzəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːzɪl / (BrE)
a light flintlock musket used especially in the 17th and 18th centuries
light flintlock musket
archaic

Examples

The soldier rested his fusil against the wall before entering the barracks.
Collectors eagerly sought the rare 18th-century fusil at auction.
With a well-oiled fusil, the infantryman was armed for battle in the early 1700s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musket - a type of long gun used before the rifle

Etymology

From French 'fusil' (flintlock musket), from Latin 'focilis' (firesteel).
