trumpetfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpətˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a tropical Atlantic fish with a long tubular snout, known for swimming with its snout pointed downward
long-snouted tropical Atlantic fish

Examples

The trumpetfish hovered vertically among the coral, blending in with sea whips.
Snorkelers often spot trumpetfish as they swim slowly with their elongated bodies.
A trumpetfish can change its color to match its surroundings and avoid predators.

Etymology

Named for its trumpet-shaped snout, from 'trumpet' + 'fish'.
