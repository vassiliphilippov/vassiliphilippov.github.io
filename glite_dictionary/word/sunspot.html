sunspot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.spɒt / (BrE)
a dark, cooler area that appears temporarily on the surface of the sun, caused by intense magnetic activity
dark spot on the sun's surface

Examples

Astronomers use special telescopes to observe sunspots on the solar surface.
The number of sunspots increases and decreases in a regular eleven-year cycle.
A large sunspot was visible through my telescope yesterday.
