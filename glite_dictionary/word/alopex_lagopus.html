Alopex lagopus

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpɛks ˈlæɡəpəs / (AmE), / əˈləʊpɛks ˈlæɡəpəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Arctic fox, a small, thickly-furred fox native to Arctic regions, with a brownish coat in summer and white in winter.
scientific name of the Arctic fox

Examples

The Alopex lagopus is well adapted to survive the harsh Arctic winters.
Researchers are studying how the Alopex lagopus changes its fur color with the seasons.
Unlike the red fox, the Alopex lagopus can withstand extremely low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic fox - A small fox native to the Arctic, known for its thick white winter coat

Etymology

From Greek alōpēx (fox) and lagōpous (hare-footed), referring to its furry feet.
