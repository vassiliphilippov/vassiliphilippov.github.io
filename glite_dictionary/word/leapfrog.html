leapfrog

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpˌfrɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːpˌfrɒɡ / (BrE)
a children's game in which players take turns jumping over one another after each has assumed a crouched position
children's game involving jumping over others

Examples

The children played leapfrog in the park until it got dark.
She remembers enjoying leapfrog with her friends at school.
As a camp activity, the counselor organized a game of leapfrog for the younger kids.

Etymology

From 'leap' + 'frog', named for the jumping motion reminiscent of a frog.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːpˌfrɑg / (AmE), / ˈliːpˌfrɒg / (BrE)
an advance or improvement that surpasses obstacles or competitors, especially by innovative or unexpected means
significant advance by surpassing obstacles

Examples

The new technology was seen as a leapfrog over all previous designs.
Small startups can sometimes make a leapfrog by adopting disruptive business models.
The company's investment resulted in a leapfrog in market share and brand recognition.

Synset Examples

the company still believes the chip is a leapfrog in integration and will pay huge dividends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakthrough - a sudden important advance or discovery
  • Antonym: setback - an obstacle or reversal in progress

Etymology

From the children's game 'leapfrog,' in which players leap over each other's backs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00487141-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈliːp.frɔːɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːp.frɒɡ / (BrE)
to move forward by jumping over something or someone, especially by vaulting with hands and legs as in the children's game; also, to surpass or overtake others by sudden progress.
vault over or move past by jumping

Examples

The students took turns trying to leapfrog over the tall bench in gym class.
Her company managed to leapfrog its competitors with an innovative new product.
He leapfrogged the hurdles with impressive agility during the practice run.

Synset Examples

He leapfrogged his classmates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - to jump over something in a single movement, using hands or a pole

Etymology

From the children's game 'leapfrog', named for its similarity to frogs leaping over each other.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01969918-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈliːp.frɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːp.frɒɡ / (BrE)

progress with sudden large advances

Examples

The company hopes to leapfrog its competitors by investing in cutting-edge research.

Related Terms
