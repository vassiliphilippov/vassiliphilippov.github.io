accurst

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɜrst / (AmE), / əˈkɜːst / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) Under a curse; cursed or doomed.
under a curse (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

The hero wandered through the accurst land, searching for redemption.
They spoke of the accurst treasure that brought misfortune to all who possessed it.
In the ballad, the accurst knight was doomed to roam the earth forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accursed - being under a curse, cursed

Etymology

Archaic form of 'accursed', from Middle English 'acursed', past participle of 'acursen'.
