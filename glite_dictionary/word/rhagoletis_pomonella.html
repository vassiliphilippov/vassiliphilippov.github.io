Rhagoletis pomonella

1. NOUN

/ rəˌɡɑːlətɪs pəˈmɑːnələ / (AmE), / rəˌɡɒlətɪs pəˈmɒnələ / (BrE)
a species of fruit fly whose larvae bore into and feed on apples; commonly known as the apple maggot
a species of fruit fly, apple maggot

Examples

The larvae of Rhagoletis pomonella cause significant damage to apple crops each year.
Farmers often monitor Rhagoletis pomonella populations to prevent infestations in their orchards.
Research on Rhagoletis pomonella has provided insights into the evolution of host specialization in insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple maggot - common name for Rhagoletis pomonella

Etymology

From the genus name 'Rhagoletis' and species name 'pomonella', derived from Latin 'pomum' (fruit, apple).
