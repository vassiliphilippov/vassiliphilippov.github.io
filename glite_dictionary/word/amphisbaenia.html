Amphisbaenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪzˈbiːniə / (AmE), / ˌamfɪzˈbiːnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of legless, burrowing lizards known as worm lizards, serving as the type genus of the family Amphisbaenidae.
genus of legless burrowing lizards

Examples

The fossil record of Amphisbaenia provides insight into their ancient origins.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Amphisbaenia in South America.
Because of their burrowing lifestyle, members of Amphisbaenia are seldom seen above ground.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Amphisbaenidae - the family of worm lizards to which Amphisbaenia belongs

Etymology

From Greek amphisbaina (a mythical serpent with a head at each end), referring to the appearance of these reptiles.
