verifiability

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛrəfəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪfəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be confirmed as true or accurate; the capability of being verified.
ability to be verified

Examples

The verifiability of the results is essential for the scientific community.
Journalists must consider the verifiability of information before publishing a report.
The theory has been criticized for its lack of verifiability.

Synset Examples

Many difficult problems arise as regards the verifiability of beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verifiableness - the state of being verifiable
  • Antonym: verifiability - opposite: cannot be verified
  • Synonym: falsifiability - the capacity for a statement to be proven false
  • Synonym: verifiable - able to be checked or proven

Etymology

From verify + -ability, modeled on Latin veritas (truth).
