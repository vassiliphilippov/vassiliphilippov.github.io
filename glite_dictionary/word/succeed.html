to succeed

1. VERB

/ səkˈsiːd / (AmE), / səkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to achieve the desired aim or result; to attain success
achieve a goal or be successful

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally succeeded in publishing her first novel.
If we succeed with this plan, the company will expand internationally.
They succeeded despite facing numerous obstacles along the way.

Synset Examples

The enterprise succeeded
We succeeded in getting tickets to the show
she struggled to overcome her handicap and won

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • Synonym: to achieve - to successfully bring about or reach a desired objective

Etymology

From Latin succedere (come after, prosper), from sub- (under) + cedere (go).

2. VERB

/ səkˈsiːd / (AmE), / səkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to take over a position, office, or title after someone else, especially by legal right or order of occurrence
take over after someone (e.g., in office, title)

Examples

After the CEO's retirement, his son will succeed him as head of the company.
Prince William is expected to succeed his father as king one day.
When the mayor resigned, her deputy was chosen to succeed her.

Synset Examples

Carter followed Ford
Will Charles succeed to the throne?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to precede - to come before in position or time

Etymology

From Latin 'succedere' (to come after).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02411715-v
Synset POS: v
