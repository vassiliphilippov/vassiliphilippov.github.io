thighbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪbəʊn / (BrE)
the long, thick bone in the human thigh, extending from the pelvis to the knee; also called the femur
the main bone of the thigh

Examples

The surgeon explained that the fracture was in the middle of the thighbone.
After the accident, his thighbone took months to heal properly.
The fossil included a remarkably well-preserved thighbone from an ancient species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femur - the bone of the thigh or upper hind limb, articulating at the hip and the knee
