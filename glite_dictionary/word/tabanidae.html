Tabanidae

1. NOUN

/ təˈbænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / təˈbanɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, biting flies known commonly as horseflies and deer flies, characterized by their stout bodies and bloodsucking habits.
family of horseflies

Examples

Scientists collected specimens of Tabanidae from several wetland habitats.
The Tabanidae are notorious for their painful bites on livestock and humans.
Several species within Tabanidae are important in the study of disease transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsefly - a large, biting fly of the Tabanidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus 'Tabanus'.
