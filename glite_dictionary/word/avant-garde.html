avant-garde

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.vɑːnˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌæv.ɒ̃ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
A group of people who develop new and experimental ideas and methods in art, music, or literature, often seen as innovative or ahead of their time.
innovative artistic or intellectual group

Examples

The avant-garde challenged traditional forms of painting with their bold, experimental works.
Many critics dismissed the avant-garde as too radical, but their influence changed the course of modern music.
A new avant-garde emerged in the city, blending technology with performance art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanguard - the foremost part of an advancing army or movement; forefront of an action or movement
  • Antonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities that are regarded as normal or conventional

Etymology

From French avant-garde, meaning 'vanguard' or 'advance guard'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌævɑnˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌavɒ̃ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
favoring or introducing new and experimental ideas and methods, especially in the arts; radically original or innovative
radically innovative; experimental (especially in arts)

Examples

The avant-garde artist challenged traditional ideas about painting.
Critics praised the avant-garde film for its originality and daring style.
Her avant-garde fashion designs often surprised and intrigued the public.

Synset Examples

an avant-garde theater piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovative - introducing new ideas; original and creative
  • Antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From French 'avant-garde' meaning 'vanguard' or 'advance guard'.
