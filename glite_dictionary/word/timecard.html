timecard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmkɑːd / (BrE)
a card used to record the times an employee starts and finishes work each day
card recording work start and end times

Examples

Please remember to hand in your timecard at the end of the week.
The manager checked each timecard before processing payroll.
If you forget to punch your timecard, your hours might not be counted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timesheet - a sheet for recording hours worked by an employee

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a card used with a time clock to record the times an employee starts and finishes work each day
card recording employee work hours

Examples

Each employee must punch their timecard when arriving and leaving work.
The supervisor collected the timecards at the end of every week for payroll processing.
Losing your timecard can result in missing work hours on your paycheck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time sheet - a sheet for recording the amount of time worked by employees

Etymology

Formed from 'time' and 'card' in late 19th century workplace usage.
