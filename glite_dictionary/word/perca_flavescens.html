Perca flavescens

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːkə fləˈvɛsəns / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkə fləˈvɛsəns / (BrE)
A North American freshwater fish of the perch family, commonly known as the yellow perch.
North American yellow perch species

Examples

Anglers enjoy fishing for Perca flavescens in the Great Lakes region.
The distinctive gold stripes make Perca flavescens easy to identify among other freshwater species.
Populations of Perca flavescens are monitored as indicators of ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow perch - Common name for Perca flavescens, a North American freshwater fish

Etymology

From Latin: 'Perca' (perch) and 'flavescens' (yellowish), referring to its coloration.
