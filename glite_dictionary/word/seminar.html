seminar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnɑː / (BrE)
a class or course in which a small group of students discuss a subject with an expert or teacher
small group academic discussion class

Examples

She enrolled in a seminar on modern European history this semester.
During the seminar, students are encouraged to participate actively in discussion.
The professor leads a weekly seminar for graduate students on advanced research methods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lecture - an educational talk to an audience, especially students

Etymology

From German 'Seminar', from Latin 'seminarium' (seed plot, nursery).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsem.əˌnɑr / (AmE), / ˈsem.ɪ.nɑː / (BrE)
a meeting at which a group of people discuss a subject or exchange ideas, often led by an expert or instructor
group discussion meeting

Examples

The professor held a weekly seminar to encourage open discussion among the students.
She attended an online seminar on climate change solutions last weekend.
During the seminar, participants were encouraged to ask questions and share their perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - a meeting at which a group engages in intensive discussion and activity on a particular subject or project
  • Degree Lower: lecture - an educational talk to an audience, especially to students in a university or college

Etymology

From German 'Seminar', from Latin 'seminarium' (seed plot, nursery).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08344746-n
Synset POS: n
