Artemisia absinthium

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə æbˈsɪn.θi.əm / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə æbˈsɪn.θi.əm / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial herb native to Eurasia and North Africa, with strongly bitter leaves, used in making the liqueur absinthe and in herbal medicine
aromatic herb used in absinthe

Examples

The famed liqueur absinthe is traditionally made from Artemisia absinthium among other ingredients.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Artemisia absinthium in traditional European remedies.
You can still find Artemisia absinthium growing wild in parts of southern France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - common name for Artemisia absinthium, a similar bitter herb

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name 'Artemisia' after Artemis, Greek goddess; species 'absinthium' from Latin for wormwood.
