synecdoche

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɛkdəˌki / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛkdəki / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which a part is used to represent the whole, or the whole is used to represent a part.
figure of speech (part for whole, whole for part)

Examples

The phrase 'all hands on deck' is a synecdoche in which 'hands' refers to sailors.
Literary critics often analyze how authors use synecdoche to create vivid imagery.
Calling a car 'wheels' is a common example of synecdoche in everyday speech.

Etymology

From Greek synekdokhē (a receiving together or substitution), from syn- (together) + ekdekhesthai (to take up).
