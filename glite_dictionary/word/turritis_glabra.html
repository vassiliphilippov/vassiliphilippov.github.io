Turritis glabra

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɪtɪs ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / tʌˈrɪtɪs ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
An erect annual or biennial herb, also known as tower mustard, found widely across Europe and sometimes in North America.
erect herb, tower mustard

Examples

The fields near the river were covered with Turritis glabra in early spring.
Botanists documented the spread of Turritis glabra into new habitats across Europe.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Turritis glabra for other types of cress due to its appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tower mustard - common name for Turritis glabra

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Turritis' and species 'glabra' meaning 'smooth'.
