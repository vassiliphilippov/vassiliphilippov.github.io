interrogator

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɹɑːɡəˌtɔɹ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɹɒɡəˌteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who questions someone, especially in an aggressive or harsh manner
a harsh or aggressive questioner

Examples

The accused was subjected to hours of tough questioning by a relentless interrogator.
Many former prisoners spoke of the cruelty shown by their interrogator during captivity.
She remained calm even as the interrogator tried to intimidate her with his aggressive methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioner - a person who asks questions

Etymology

From Latin 'interrogator', from 'interrogare' (to ask, question).
