Ramman

1. PROPN

/ ˈræmˌɑːn / (AmE), / ˈræmˌæn / (BrE)
An ancient Semitic god of storms and wind, corresponding to the Babylonian god Adad.
ancient Semitic storm and wind god

Examples

The ancient Mesopotamians worshipped Ramman as the god who controlled storms and rainfall.
In comparative mythology, Ramman is often identified with other storm deities such as Adad.
Offerings were made to Ramman to appease his wrath during times of drought and tempest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Adad - Babylonian god of storms, corresponding to Ramman

Etymology

From Akkadian and West Semitic tradition; related to the root r-m-n meaning 'thunder' or 'storm.'
