to chlorinate

1. VERB

/ ˈklɔːrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔː.rɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to treat or combine (something) with chlorine, especially to disinfect or purify it
treat with chlorine

Examples

Municipal water supplies are often chlorinated to kill bacteria and other pathogens.
The company needs to chlorinate the pool every week to keep the water clean.
Before using the well, you should chlorinate it to remove any contaminants.

Synset Examples

chlorinated water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dechlorinate - to remove chlorine from something

Etymology

From 'chlorine' + '-ate', forming verbs meaning to treat with or add chlorine.

2. VERB

/ ˈklɔːrɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrɪneɪt / (BrE)
to treat or disinfect something with chlorine, especially to kill bacteria or prevent contamination
treat with chlorine to disinfect

Examples

The maintenance staff chlorinate the pool twice a week during the summer.
It's important to chlorinate drinking water to prevent the spread of disease.
After heavy rain, the city will often chlorinate the public water supply as a precaution.

Synset Examples

chlorinate a pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean something to destroy bacteria or viruses

Etymology

From chlorine + -ate, indicating to treat with chlorine. Originally formed in the 19th century.
