nightly

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtli / (BrE)
happening or appearing every night
occurring every night

Examples

The nightly news broadcast keeps viewers updated on world events.
She takes a nightly walk to help clear her mind before bed.
Their nightly routine includes reading a story to the children.

Synset Examples

nightly television now goes on until 3:00 or 4:00 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daily - happening every day
  • Antonym: weekly - happening every week

Etymology

From Middle English 'nightli', based on 'night' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈnaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtli / (BrE)
(adverb) Happening or done every night; at the end of each day.
every night; nightly occurrence

Examples

She waters the plants nightly to keep them healthy.
The security guard makes his rounds nightly without fail.
We used to walk the dog nightly after dinner.

Synset Examples

she checks on her roses nightly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daily - happening or done every day

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'nihtlic' (nightly), from 'niht' (night).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00412120-r
Synset POS: r
