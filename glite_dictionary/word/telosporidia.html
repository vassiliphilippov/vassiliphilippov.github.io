Telosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləspəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌtɛləspɒˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of parasitic protozoans that form spores containing one or more infective sporozoites
group of spore-forming parasitic protozoans
medical

Examples

Researchers observed the Telosporidia under a microscope to study their infectious cycle.
Many species within the Telosporidia cause diseases in invertebrate hosts.
The complex life cycle of Telosporidia involves the formation of sporozoites that infect new cells.

Etymology

From Greek telos (end, purpose) + sporos (seed, spore); used in taxonomic classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˌspɔːˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌtɛləˌspɔːˈrɪdiə / (BrE)
a subclass of parasitic protozoans (sporozoans) that form spores containing one or more infective sporozoites
subclass of parasitic protozoans

Examples

Scientists observed that members of Telosporidia produce spores at a certain stage of their life cycle.
Some diseases in animals are caused by protozoa belonging to the subclass Telosporidia.
The life cycle of Telosporidia involves the formation of spores that can infect new hosts.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'tele-' (end) and 'spora' (spore), with the suffix '-idia' indicating descent or class.
