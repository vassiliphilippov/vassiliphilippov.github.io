prelate

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛlət / (AmE), / ˈprɛlət / (BrE)
A high-ranking member of the clergy, especially one with jurisdiction over other clergy, such as a bishop or abbot.
senior clergyman or church dignitary

Examples

The prelate delivered a stirring sermon to the congregation.
In the Middle Ages, a prelate often held great political power.
The council was attended by several distinguished prelates from across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, usually in charge of a diocese
  • Synonym: abbot - the head of an abbey of monks

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *praelatus* (someone set above others), past participle of *praeferre* (to place before).
