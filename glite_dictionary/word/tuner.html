tuner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːnər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives, demodulates, and amplifies transmitted signals, especially radio or television broadcasts.
electronic signal receiver and amplifier

Examples

The radio's tuner allows you to select stations from all over the world.
My old stereo has a separate tuner for AM and FM signals.
She adjusted the tuner until the music sounded clear and crisp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receiver - a device for receiving broadcast signals

Etymology

From 'tune' + '-er', originally signifying something that allows tuning to a particular frequency.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːnər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a person who adjusts a musical instrument so that it produces the correct pitch or tone
person who tunes musical instruments

Examples

The piano tuner visited our house every six months to keep the instrument sounding perfect.
After the concert, the violin tuner made some quick adjustments backstage.
She trained as a tuner and now works for a famous orchestra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technician - a person skilled in the technique of an art or craft
  • Synonym: musician - a person who plays a musical instrument

Etymology

From 'tune' (to adjust pitch) + agent suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-83192005-n
Synset POS: n
