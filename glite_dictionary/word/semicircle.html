semicircle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmiˌsɜːrkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌsɜːkəl / (BrE)
a plane figure formed by half of a circle, including its diameter and the arc
half of a circle (plane figure)

Examples

The children sat in a semicircle around the teacher to listen to the story.
She drew a perfect semicircle on the chalkboard for the geometry lesson.
The chairs were arranged in a semicircle so everyone could see the performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: circle - a round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from a fixed center
  • Degree Lower: arc - a part of the circumference of a circle

Etymology

From Latin 'semicirculus', from 'semi-' meaning 'half' and 'circulus' meaning 'circle'.
