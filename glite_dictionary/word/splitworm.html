splitworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪtˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsplɪtˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A greyish-brown moth, especially whose larva is the potato tuberworm, known for damaging potato tubers.
a moth whose larva damages potatoes

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control the splitworm in their potato fields.
The larvae of the splitworm bore into potato tubers, causing major crop losses.
Experts recommend checking stored potatoes regularly to prevent splitworm infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato tuberworm - Larva of a moth that infests potato tubers
