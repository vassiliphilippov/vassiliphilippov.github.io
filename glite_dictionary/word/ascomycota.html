Ascomycota

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈmaɪkətə / (AmE), / ˌæskəʊˈmaɪkətə / (BrE)
A large division of fungi, also known as sac fungi, characterized by the formation of spores in sac-like structures called asci.
major division of sac fungi

Examples

Scientists studied the unique reproductive structures of Ascomycota found in the decaying wood.
Many species within Ascomycota are important sources of antibiotics and other pharmaceuticals.
The yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae, used in baking and brewing, belongs to the Ascomycota division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - A group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'askos' (sac) and '-mycota' (fungi).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈmaɪkətə / (AmE), / ˌæskəʊˈmaɪkətə / (BrE)
a major group (division or subdivision) of fungi characterized by producing spores in sac-like structures called asci; includes many molds, yeasts, and morels
a major group of fungi (sac fungi)
medical

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by members of the Ascomycota.
Yeasts, which belong to the Ascomycota, are used in baking and brewing.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Ascomycota during their field study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Basidiomycota - a large division of fungi that produce spores on basidia; club fungi

Etymology

From Greek 'askos' (sac) + 'mykes' (fungus) + '-ota' (plural ending in taxonomy).
