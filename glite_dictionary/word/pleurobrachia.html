Pleurobrachia

1. NOUN

/ pluːˌroʊˈbreɪkiə / (AmE), / plʊəˌrɒˈbrækɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, transparent marine animals known as sea gooseberries, belonging to the phylum Ctenophora.
genus of sea gooseberries (ctenophores)

Examples

The delicate tentacles of Pleurobrachia are used to capture tiny plankton in the ocean.
Researchers collected several Pleurobrachia specimens during the marine biology survey.
Unlike jellyfish, Pleurobrachia moves by beating rows of cilia called combs.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side) and 'brachion' (arm).
