Artocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.t̬oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees native to Asia, including species such as breadfruit and jackfruit, now cultivated throughout tropical regions.
genus of tropical evergreen trees (breadfruit, jackfruit)

Examples

The Artocarpus genus includes the widely cultivated jackfruit and breadfruit trees.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Artocarpus in different tropical environments.
Local farmers have planted several varieties of Artocarpus to boost fruit production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackfruit - large tropical fruit from Artocarpus heterophyllus
  • Synonym: breadfruit - large tropical fruit from Artocarpus altilis

Etymology

From Greek 'artos' (bread) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the breadfruit.
