vena cervicalis profunda

1. NOUN

/ ˈvenə sərˌvɪkəlɪs proʊˈfuːndə / (AmE), / ˈveɪnə səˌvɪkəlɪs prəˈfjuːndə / (BrE)
A deep vein in the neck that accompanies the artery of the same name.
deep vein in the neck
medical

Examples

Bleeding from the vena cervicalis profunda can be difficult to control during neck surgery.
The vena cervicalis profunda drains blood from the deep structures of the neck.
Careful dissection is required to avoid injuring the vena cervicalis profunda in this region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'cervicalis' (of the neck), 'profunda' (deep).
