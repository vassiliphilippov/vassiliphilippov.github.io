nonoptimality

1. NOUN

/ nɒnˌɑp.tɪˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / nɒnˌɒp.tɪˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of not being optimal; not being the best, most effective, or most desirable.
state of not being optimal

Examples

Careful analysis revealed the nonoptimality of their proposed solution.
The designer's goal is to minimize nonoptimality in the final product.
An excessive margin of error is a sign of nonoptimality in the prediction model.

Synset Examples

The level of nonoptimality is defined as the ratio of the filtering performances for the simplified and optimal estimators.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimality - the state of being optimal or most favorable

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) and 'optimality' (from Latin 'optimus', meaning 'best').
