Orchis papilionacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.kɪs pəˌpɪl.i.əˈneɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪs pəˌpɪl.i.əˈneɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean orchid species with purple flowers, each having a fan-shaped, spotted or striped rose-red lip.
a Mediterranean orchid species

Examples

The Orchis papilionacea blooms abundantly in the Mediterranean springtime.
Botanists admire the striking colors of the Orchis papilionacea in its natural habitat.
We photographed a field full of Orchis papilionacea during our hike in southern Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae with complex flowers

Etymology

From Latin: orchis (orchid) + papilionacea, meaning 'butterfly-like'.
