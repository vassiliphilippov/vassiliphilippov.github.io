ferrocyanide

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛroʊˈsaɪəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɛərəʊˈsaɪənaɪd / (BrE)
a salt formed by the combination of ferrocyanic acid and a base, usually prepared by reacting a cyanide with iron(II) sulfate
a salt of ferrocyanic acid

Examples

The laboratory stored potassium ferrocyanide in a clearly labeled container.
Wastewater treatment processes sometimes use ferrocyanide compounds to remove heavy metals.
Researchers studied the properties of ferrocyanide salts for industrial applications.

Etymology

From ferro- (relating to iron) + cyanide (a compound containing the group –CN).
