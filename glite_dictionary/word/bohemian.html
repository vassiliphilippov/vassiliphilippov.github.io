Bohemian

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈhiːmiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈhiːmiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Bohemia, a region in the western part of the Czech Republic
native or inhabitant of Bohemia

Examples

Many Bohemians emigrated to America during the 19th century.
The traditional music of the Bohemian people is celebrated each year at the festival.
Her grandmother was a proud Bohemian who taught her the customs of the region.

Etymology

From Latin 'Bohemius', from 'Bohemia', the historical region in Central Europe.

2. ADJ

/ boʊˈhiːmiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈhiːmiən / (BrE)
relating to Bohemia, its people, or their language
of Bohemia or its people/language

Examples

The Bohemian language is historically spoken in what is now the Czech Republic.
Her ancestors were proud Bohemian immigrants who settled in America.
Traditional Bohemian customs are celebrated during the annual festival.

Etymology

From French *bohémien*, originally referring to people from Bohemia.
