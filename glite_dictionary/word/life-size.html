life-size

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪf.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.saɪz / (BrE)
having the same dimensions or proportions as the person or thing represented; of actual size.
of actual or original size

Examples

The museum features a life-size model of a woolly mammoth.
She posed next to a life-size cardboard cutout of her favorite actor.
The artist created a life-size portrait that stunned the viewers with its realism.

Synset Examples

a life-size sculpture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miniature - a very small, reduced-size representation
  • Synonym: full-size - of the full or normal size

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪf.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.saɪz / (BrE)
having the same size as the person or thing represented; as large as in real life.
same size as the original

Examples

The museum displays a life-size model of a Tyrannosaurus rex.
She posed for a life-size portrait in oils.
A life-size bronze statue was unveiled in the town square.

Synset Examples

a life-size sculpture

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: miniature - a copy or model much smaller than the original
  • Degree Higher: gigantic - very large in size, much bigger than normal

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'size', first recorded in the mid-19th century.
