witch-hunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a person who seeks out and punishes individuals because of their opinions, often in a zealous or unfair manner
one who punishes others for their opinions

Examples

During the political turmoil, every critic of the administration was labeled a witch-hunter.
He acted like a witch-hunter, accusing anyone with a different viewpoint.
Online debates sometimes bring out modern witch-hunters eager to shame their opponents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitor - a person making in-depth inquiries, often harshly or unfairly
  • Synonym: persecutor - a person who harasses or oppresses others especially because of their beliefs

Etymology

From 'witch' + 'hunter', originally referring to people who hunted supposed witches.
