sudra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːdrə / (AmE), / ˈsuːdrə / (BrE)
a member of the lowest of the four Hindu castes, traditionally assigned to menial work and service roles
lowest Hindu caste; servant or laborer

Examples

In traditional Indian society, a sudra was expected to serve the higher castes.
He identified himself as a sudra and worked as a farm laborer.
The caste system classified people like him as sudra, limiting their social mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varna - each of the four broad Hindu social classes

Etymology

From Sanskrit śūdra (शूद्र).
