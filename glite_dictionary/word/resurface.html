to resurface

1. VERB

/ riˈsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / riˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to appear or become visible again on the surface after being hidden, immersed, or absent
to appear again on the surface

Examples

After hours without a trace, the missing diver finally resurfaced near the boat.
Old rumors sometimes resurface when new evidence comes to light.
The whale will resurface several times before diving deep again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to come out into view or become visible

Etymology

From re- (again) + surface (the outside or top layer).

WordNet ID: oewn-01994862-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / riˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to put a new surface on something, especially a road or floor
put a new surface on something

Examples

The city plans to resurface the main road next summer.
They decided to resurface the tennis court before the tournament.
After years of wear, the wooden floor had to be resurfaced to look new again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pave - to cover a surface, especially a road, with flat stones, bricks, or concrete

Etymology

From re- (again) + surface (noun and verb form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01268213-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈsɜrfəs / (AmE), / riˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to appear again after being absent, inactive, or hidden for some time
to appear again after being hidden

Examples

After several years away from the music scene, the singer finally resurfaced with a new album.
Rumors about the old scandal resurfaced during the election campaign.
He vanished for months but resurfaced unexpectedly at his sister's wedding.

Synset Examples

The missing man suddenly resurfaced in New York

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reappear - to appear again after disappearing
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
