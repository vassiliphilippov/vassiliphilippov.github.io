peripatetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrəpəˈtɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Aristotle or his philosophy.
relating to Aristotle or his philosophy

Examples

His theories reflect a distinctly peripatetic approach to ethics.
She gave a lecture on peripatetic views in ancient philosophy.
The book traces the influence of peripatetic thought throughout history.

Synset Examples

Aristotelean logic

Etymology

From Greek peripatētikos (‘given to walking about’), used for Aristotle and his followers who lectured while walking.

WordNet ID: oewn-03039979-a

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place, especially for work or duty
a person who travels frequently

Examples

As a peripatetic, he rarely stayed in one town for more than a month.
The ancient philosophers were often peripatetics, sharing their ideas as they traveled.
Her career as a peripatetic took her to several countries every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - a person who moves about without a fixed home or goal
  • Synonym: itinerant - a person who travels from place to place, often for work
  • Antonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and stays there permanently

Etymology

From Greek *peripatētikos* (given to walking about), from *peri-* (around) + *patein* (to walk).

traveling from place to place, especially on foot or for work
traveling from place to place

Examples

She led a peripatetic lifestyle, moving from city to city every few months.

For years, he worked as a peripatetic musician, performing in different towns each night.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: itinerant - traveling from place to place, especially for work
