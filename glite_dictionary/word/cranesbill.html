cranesbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪnzˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈkreɪnzˌbɪl / (BrE)
Any of various perennial plants of the genus Geranium, characterized by palmately divided leaves and often showy five-petaled flowers.
a plant of the genus Geranium

Examples

Several species of cranesbill thrive in shady woodland gardens.
The delicate flowers of the cranesbill attract bees and butterflies in the summer.
She planted blue cranesbill along the edge of the path for added color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a genus of plants with divided leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

Named for the beak-like shape of the seed capsule, resembling a crane's bill.
