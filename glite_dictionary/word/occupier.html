occupier

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪər / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or uses a particular place, especially for a long time or as a resident
person living in or using a place

Examples

The current occupier of the house has lived there for over twenty years.
Please write the name of the occupier on the tenancy agreement.
As the former occupier moved out, the apartment became available for rent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or on a long-term basis
  • Synonym: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
  • Antonym: visitor - a person visiting a place, usually for a short time

Etymology

From occupy + -er, formed in English from Latin *occupare* (to seize).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪə / (BrE)
a member of a military force who resides in a conquered foreign country after taking control of it
military resident in conquered territory

Examples

International law places obligations on the occupier to protect the civilian population.
The foreign occupier maintained strict control over the city and its resources.
After the war, the occupier withdrew, leaving behind a changed society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invader - a person or force that enters a place to take control by force

Etymology

From occupy + -er, denoting 'one who occupies'.
