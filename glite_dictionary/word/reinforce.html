to reinforce

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːs / (BrE)
to make something stronger or more effective, especially by adding support or additional material
make stronger or more effective

Examples

The engineer suggested we reinforce the bridge before allowing heavier traffic to cross.
You can reinforce your argument by providing more evidence and expert testimony.
They decided to reinforce the wall with steel beams to improve its stability.

Synset Examples

he reinforced the concrete

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make less strong

Etymology

From Old French ‘reinforcer’, from Latin ‘re-’ (again) + ‘infortis’ (strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00222898-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːs / (BrE)
to strengthen or support an attitude, behavior, or response, especially by providing rewards or encouragement
strengthen or support, especially with rewards

Examples

The teacher used praise to reinforce positive behavior in the classroom.
Small rewards can reinforce students' desire to study regularly.
It's important to reinforce healthy habits with encouragement and support.

Synset Examples

Let's reinforce good behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Old French 'reenforcier', based on Latin 'in-' (in, into) + 'fortis' (strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00789322-v
Synset POS: v
