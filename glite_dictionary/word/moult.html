moult

1. NOUN

/ moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
the process by which certain animals periodically shed their outer covering, such as feathers, skin, or exoskeleton
periodic shedding of skin or covering

Examples

The snake undergoes a moult several times a year as it grows.
After its annual moult, the crab emerges with a larger, softer shell.
Many birds look scruffy during their moult in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molt - alternative spelling (American English) for the shedding process in animals

Etymology

From Middle English 'mouten', from Old French 'muer', from Latin 'mutare' (to change).

2. VERB

/ moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
(of an animal or bird) to shed old feathers, hair, or skin to make way for new growth.
shed old feathers, hair, or skin

Examples

Most birds moult once or twice a year to renew their feathers.
The snake will moult its old skin several times as it grows.
Our cat begins to moult heavily in the spring, leaving fur all over the house.

Synset Examples

our dog sheds every Spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - (of an animal) to lose hair, skin, feathers, etc. naturally

Etymology

From Middle English 'molten', from Old English 'multan' (to make soft), influenced by Latin 'mūtāre' (to change).
