diaphoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəfəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪəfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
excessive or profuse sweating, often as a symptom of illness or a reaction to medication
excessive sweating (medical term)
medical

Examples

The patient was experiencing diaphoresis along with a rapid heartbeat.
Severe diaphoresis can be a sign of an underlying medical condition.
Doctors noted diaphoresis as one of the symptoms during the examination.

Synset Examples

perspiration is a homeostatic process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspiration - the process of sweating
  • Antonym: anhidrosis - absence of sweating

Etymology

From Greek diaphorēsis (perspiration), from diaphorein (to carry through)
