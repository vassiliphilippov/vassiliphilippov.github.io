William S. Burroughs

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ɛs ˈbɜroʊz / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ɛs ˈbɜːrəʊz / (BrE)
(1914–1997) an American writer known for his influential works on the life of drug addicts and his role in the Beat Generation.
American writer and Beat Generation figure

Examples

Many critics regard William S. Burroughs as a key influence on postmodern literature.
The works of William S. Burroughs often explore themes of addiction and identity.
William S. Burroughs was associated with other Beat writers like Allen Ginsberg and Jack Kerouac.
