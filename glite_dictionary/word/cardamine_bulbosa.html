Cardamine bulbosa

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdæmɪn ˌbʌlˈboʊsə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdamɪni ˌbʌlˈbəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of small white-flowered cress commonly found in wet areas of eastern North America.
a white-flowered cress of eastern North America

Examples

Cardamine bulbosa grows abundantly along the edges of swampy meadows in the spring.
The delicate white flowers of Cardamine bulbosa brighten wetlands across eastern North America.
Botanists recently discovered a large population of Cardamine bulbosa in a protected marshland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a plant of the mustard family with edible leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cardamine', a genus of the mustard family, and 'bulbosa' meaning bulbous, referring to the plant's bulb-like base.
