Praetorian

1. NOUN

/ priˈtɔriən / (AmE), / priːˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
a member of the Praetorian Guard, the elite bodyguard of Roman emperors in ancient Rome.
member of Roman imperial bodyguard

Examples

The emperor trusted his Praetorian with guarding the entrance to his private chambers.
Several Praetorians were implicated in the plot against the ruler.
Historically, the Praetorian Guard held significant political power in ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Praetorian Guard - elite bodyguard unit of Roman emperors

Etymology

From Latin 'praetorianus', relating to the praetor (a Roman magistrate) or the emperor's guard.

2. ADJ

/ priˈtɔriən / (AmE), / priˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
Characteristic of or resembling the corruptible or venal soldiers of the Roman Praetorian Guard, especially with respect to political corruption or intrigue.
corrupt or venal like the Praetorian Guard

Examples

The senator warned against the rise of a Praetorian influence within the government.
Many viewed the secret police as a Praetorian force loyal only to the ruler.
A Praetorian mentality can undermine democratic institutions through unchecked political power.

Synset Examples

a large Praetorian bureaucracy filled with ambitious … and often sycophantic people makes work and makes trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - dishonest or immoral, especially in politics

Etymology

From Latin "Praetorianus," relating to the Praetorian Guard, the Roman emperor’s bodyguards often noted for their power and corruption.
