counterpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
A musical technique or style in which two or more distinct melodic lines are played or sung at the same time.
simultaneous combination of melodies in music

Examples

Bach's compositions are renowned for their intricate use of counterpoint.
Learning counterpoint is essential for many students of classical music theory.
The choir performed a piece featuring beautiful counterpoint between the soprano and tenor voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyphony - music with two or more independent melodic lines

Etymology

From Old French contrepoint, from Latin contra- (against) + punctus (point, note).

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to compose or arrange (music) with two or more independent melodic lines; to set in contrast, especially in music.
compose music with contrasting melodies

Examples

The composer skillfully counterpoints the flute melody with the cello line.
In his latest piece, he counterpointed a gentle piano theme with energetic strings.
She enjoys counterpointing different rhythms to create a complex musical texture.

Synset Examples

Bach perfected the art of counterpointing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - compose or provide harmony to music

Etymology

From Middle French 'contrepoint', from Italian 'contrappunto', from 'contra' (against) + 'punctus' (point, note).

Synset POS: v
