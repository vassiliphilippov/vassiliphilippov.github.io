duo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdu.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.əʊ / (BrE)
a piece of music written for two performers
a musical piece for two performers

Examples

The composer wrote a beautiful duo for violin and piano.
They performed a challenging duo at the concert last night.
This album features a remarkable guitar and flute duo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duet - a musical composition for two voices or instruments

Etymology

From Italian 'duo', derived from Latin 'duo' (two).
