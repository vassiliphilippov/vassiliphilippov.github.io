condenser

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a lens or system of lenses used to focus or concentrate light onto an object, especially in optical instruments such as microscopes
lens focusing light onto an object

Examples

The microscope's condenser must be positioned correctly to get a clear image.
By adjusting the condenser, you can control the amount of light reaching the specimen.
Older projectors used a powerful condenser to focus the lamp's beam onto the slide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - a piece of glass or other transparent substance used to focus or disperse light rays

Etymology

From Latin 'condensare' (to make thick, press together), reflecting its function of concentrating light.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a device or apparatus that condenses a vapor by cooling it, turning it into a liquid
device that converts vapor to liquid

Examples

The condenser in the laboratory cooled the steam into water for collection.
After repairing the condenser, the air conditioning unit worked efficiently again.
Most distillation setups require a condenser to recover the purified liquid.

Etymology

From Latin condensare (to make thick, condense), via French condenseur.

3. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
A device, often consisting of a hollow coil, that removes heat from a substance in order to condense it from a gas to a liquid.
device for condensing gases

Examples

The air conditioning unit uses a condenser to cool and liquefy the refrigerant gas.
After the steam passes through the turbine, a condenser turns it back into water.
They installed a new condenser in the laboratory's distillation apparatus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evaporator - device that causes a substance to evaporate by adding heat
  • Synonym: heat exchanger - device transferring heat between fluids

Etymology

From Latin 'condensare' (to make dense or thick).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
an electrical device that stores an electric charge, typically consisting of two conductors separated by an insulator; also known as a capacitor
electrical device for storing charge

Examples

The engineer replaced the faulty condenser in the radio circuit.
Older manuals often use the term condenser instead of capacitor.
A condenser can smooth out voltage fluctuations in an electrical circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - an electrical device that stores energy in an electric field

Etymology

From Latin 'condensare' (to thicken, condense); used in early electrical terminology for storing energy.
