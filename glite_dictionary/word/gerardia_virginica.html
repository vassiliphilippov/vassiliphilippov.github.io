Gerardia virginica

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈrɑrdiə vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / dʒəˈrɑːdiə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial North American plant with sparsely branched stems and terminal racemes of bright yellow, foxglove-like flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Gerardia.
North American perennial with yellow, foxglove-like flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with the bright yellow flowers of Gerardia virginica in late summer.
Botanists noted that Gerardia virginica often grows in sandy soils along woodland edges.
Gardeners appreciate Gerardia virginica for its delicate, foxglove-like blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gerardia - a genus of flowering plants with showy, tubular flowers

Etymology

Gerardia named after English botanist John Gerard; 'virginica' refers to Virginia, USA.
