to bewhisker

1. VERB

/ bɪˈwɪskər / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪskə / (BrE)
to provide or furnish with whiskers
supply with whiskers

Examples

The artist decided to bewhisker the old man's portrait for comic effect.
Some costumes are designed to bewhisker the wearer with elaborate faux facial hair.
The puppet maker would often bewhisker his creations to give them more personality.

Synset Examples

a whiskered jersey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiskered - having whiskers; furnished with whiskers

Etymology

From be- (a prefix meaning 'to make or cause to have') + whisker.
