leishmaniasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌliʃməˈnaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌliːʃmənɪˈɑːsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with protozoa of the genus Leishmania, transmitted by the bite of certain sandflies, leading to skin sores and sometimes involving internal organs.
disease caused by Leishmania parasites
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of leishmaniasis have been reported in several tropical regions this year.
The doctor diagnosed her skin lesions as a form of leishmaniasis after a trip abroad.
Research into vaccines against leishmaniasis is ongoing due to its global impact.

Etymology

From New Latin Leishmania (genus name) + -iasis (indicating disease or condition).
