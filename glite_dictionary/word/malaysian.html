Malaysian

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪʒən / (AmE), / məˈleɪziən / (BrE)
the standardized form of the Malay language spoken in Malaysia and, in some cases, neighbouring countries.
the Malay language spoken in Malaysia

Examples

She studied Malaysian for several years before moving to Kuala Lumpur.
The film was subtitled in both English and Malaysian.
Many signs in Malaysia are written in both Malaysian and English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - the Austronesian language of the Malay people, spoken in Malaysia, Indonesia, and other regions

Etymology

From Malaysia + -an, referring to the language of the country.

WordNet ID: oewn-06951828-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ məˈleɪʒən / (AmE), / məˈleɪzɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Malaysia, its people, culture, or language.
relating to Malaysia or its people

Examples

The Malaysian economy has grown rapidly in recent decades.
We enjoyed delicious Malaysian cuisine at the festival.
The Malaysian flag features a crescent and a fourteen-point star.

Synset Examples

Malaysian police crack down hard on drug smugglers
Malayan crocodiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malayan - relating to Malaya or the Malay Peninsula

Etymology

From Malaysia + -an, denoting association or origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ məˈleɪʒ(ə)n / (AmE), / məˈleɪziən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Malaysia
a native or inhabitant of Malaysia

Examples

Many Malaysians celebrate both traditional and modern festivals.
The government invited all Malaysians to participate in the national census.
She met several Malaysians while traveling through Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malaysian - relating to Malaysia, its people, or its languages (adjective)

Etymology

From Malaysia + -an, indicating origin or nationality.
