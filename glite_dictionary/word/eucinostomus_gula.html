Eucinostomus gula

1. NOUN

/ juːˌsɪnəˈstoʊməs ˈɡuːlə / (AmE), / juːˌsɪnɒˈstɒməs ˈɡuːlə / (BrE)
A species of silvery mojarra fish found along sandy shores of the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of silvery mojarra fish

Examples

During the coastal survey, several Eucinostomus gula were observed near the shoreline.
The Eucinostomus gula prefers sandy bottoms in shallow waters of the western Atlantic.
Researchers tagged a Eucinostomus gula to study its migratory patterns.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good), 'kinein' (to move), 'stoma' (mouth), and Latin 'gula' (throat), referring to anatomical features.
