Quechuan

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛt͡ʃwən / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwən / (BrE)
relating to the Quechua people, their culture, or their language.
relating to the Quechua people or language

Examples

The museum features a rich collection of Quechuan textiles and artifacts.
Many Quechuan traditions are still practiced in rural areas of Peru.
She studied the Quechuan language during her time in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - the indigenous people and their language of the central Andes

Etymology

From Quechua, the indigenous people and their language of the Andes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03120273-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwən / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwən / (BrE)
the language or family of languages spoken by the Quechua people of the Andes, including the language of the Incas
language of the Quechua, Incas

Examples

Many villages in Peru still speak Quechuan as their primary language.
He studied the grammar and vocabulary of Quechuan during his time in South America.
The Inca Empire used Quechuan for official communication across its vast territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - the people of the central Andes speaking the Quechuan languages

Etymology

From Spanish 'quechua', from native (Quechua) 'qheswa', the name of the language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06929859-n
Synset POS: n
