Geraniales

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˌreɪniˈeɪliz / (AmE), / dʒəˌreɪnɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants in the subclass Rosidae, including geraniums and related families such as Euphorbiaceae, Geraniaceae, and Rutaceae
order of flowering plants (includes geraniums)

Examples

Many ornamental plants popular in gardens belong to the Geraniales order.
The family Geraniaceae, part of Geraniales, includes the common geranium.
Botanists classify several economically important species within Geraniales.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Geranium and the suffix -ales (used for plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˌreɪniˈeɪliz / (AmE), / dʒəˌreɪniˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants in the subclass Rosidae, including geraniums and related families such as Geraniaceae, Euphorbiaceae, and Rutaceae.
a plant order including geraniums and related families

Examples

Geraniales includes the geranium family as well as other related plant families.
Many botanists study the characteristics of plants within the order Geraniales.
The garden contains several rare species from the order Geraniales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geraniaceae - the family of flowering plants including geraniums, within Geraniales
  • Synonym: Euphorbiaceae - a family of flowering plants, the spurge family, within Geraniales

Etymology

From the genus name Geranium, with Latin -ales indicating an order.
