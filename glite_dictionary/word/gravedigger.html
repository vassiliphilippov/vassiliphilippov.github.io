gravedigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌdɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvˌdɪɡə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to dig graves for burial
person who digs graves for burial

Examples

After years of working as a gravedigger, he knew every tree and stone in the cemetery.
The play features a witty gravedigger scene that adds comic relief to the tragedy.
Modern cemeteries often use machinery, reducing the need for a traditional gravedigger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexton - a church officer responsible for the maintenance of church property, often including grave digging

Etymology

Formed from 'grave' + 'digger', indicating one who digs graves.
