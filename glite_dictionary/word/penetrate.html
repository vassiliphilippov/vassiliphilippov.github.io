to penetrate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪˌtreɪt / (BrE)
to succeed in understanding or grasping the true nature or meaning of something complex or obscure
come to understand deeply

Examples

It took years for scientists to penetrate the mysteries of black holes.
Her keen intellect allows her to penetrate complex philosophical arguments quickly.
Despite their efforts, they could not penetrate the real motives behind his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comprehend - to grasp mentally; to understand
  • Synonym: fathom - to understand something deeply

Etymology

From Latin penetratus, past participle of penetrare (to put or get into, enter).
