pinafore

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəˌfɔː / (BrE)
A sleeveless garment resembling an apron, typically worn over other clothing, especially by children or as a uniform.
sleeveless apron-like dress

Examples

The little girl wore a neat pinafore over her dress to keep it clean.
Nurses used to wear a white pinafore as part of their uniform.
She spilled juice on her pinafore but was glad her clothes underneath stayed spotless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apron - protective garment worn over the front of clothes

Etymology

From 'pin' + 'afore' (meaning 'pinned in front'); first used in the 18th century.
