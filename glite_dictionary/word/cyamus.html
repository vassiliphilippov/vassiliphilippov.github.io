Cyamus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈeɪməs / (AmE), / saɪˈeɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of amphipod crustaceans known as whale lice, which are external parasites found on whales.
genus of whale lice (parasitic crustaceans)

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a colony of Cyamus infesting the skin of a blue whale.
The presence of Cyamus can indicate the health status of certain whale species.
Researchers collected samples of Cyamus for further study on their parasitic behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale louse - parasitic crustacean living on whales

Etymology

From Greek 'kyamos' (bean), due to the shape of the organism.
