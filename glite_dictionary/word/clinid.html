clinid

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪd / (BrE)
A small marine fish of the family Clinidae, typically found in coral reefs and seagrass beds.
a small coral reef fish (Clinidae family)

Examples

The vibrant reef was home to several clinids darting between seagrass blades.
Marine biologists conducted a study on clinid populations in the coastal region.
A clinid can often be found sheltering among rocks and corals.

Etymology

From New Latin Clinidae, the scientific family name.
