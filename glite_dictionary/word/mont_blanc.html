Mont Blanc

1. PROPN

/ mɑn ˈblɑŋk / (AmE), / mɒ̃ ˈblɑ̃ / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the Alps, located on the border between France and Italy, south of Geneva
highest mountain in the Alps

Examples

We could see Mont Blanc in the distance as we drove through the Alps.
Climbers from around the world attempt to scale Mont Blanc every year.
The snow-capped peak of Mont Blanc is particularly beautiful at sunrise.

Etymology

French, literally meaning 'White Mountain.'
