white-bread

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪtˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌbrɛd / (BrE)
Typical of or associated with the white middle class, often implying blandness, conventionality, or lack of diversity.
conventional; typical of white middle class
informal

Examples

Many critics found the movie too white-bread to reflect real urban life.
He grew up in a white-bread suburb far from any big city.
The festival was criticized for having a white-bread lineup that lacked diversity.

Synset Examples

white-bread America
a white-bread college student

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstream - representing the dominant attitudes or culture

Etymology

From the blandness commonly attributed to ordinary white bread as compared to other varieties.
