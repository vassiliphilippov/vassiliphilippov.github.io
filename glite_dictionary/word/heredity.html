heredity

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɛdəti / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdəti / (BrE)
The passing of physical or mental characteristics genetically from one generation to another.
genetic transmission from parent to offspring

Examples

Heredity plays a significant role in determining a person's eye color.
Many diseases are influenced by both heredity and environmental factors.
Scientists study heredity to understand how traits are passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - the reception of genetic qualities by transmission from parent to offspring

Etymology

From Latin hereditas (inheritance), from heres (heir).

2. NOUN

/ həˈrɛdəti / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdəti / (BrE)
the passing on of physical or mental characteristics genetically from one generation to another
genetic transmission of traits

Examples

Human eye color is determined by heredity and the combination of genes inherited from parents.
Scientists study heredity to understand how traits are passed across generations.
The debate continues about whether intelligence is influenced more by heredity or by environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - the process by which genetic information is passed from parent to offspring
  • Antonym: environment - the external factors influencing an organism, contrasted with heredity

Etymology

From Latin 'hereditas' (inheritance).
