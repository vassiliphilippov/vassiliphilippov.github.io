to animadvert

1. VERB

/ ˌænəmædˈvɜrt / (AmE), / ˌænɪmədˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to express blame or censure; to make a harshly critical remark
to express blame or critical comment

Examples

The novelist dared to animadvert upon the political climate of his country.
She would often animadvert on her students' lack of discipline in class.
Critics did not hesitate to animadvert against the film's confusing plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'animadvertere' meaning 'to notice, to censure' (from 'animus' mind + 'advertere' to turn toward).

2. VERB

/ ˌænəməˈdvɜrt / (AmE), / ˌænɪmədˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to comment critically or unfavorably, often in a formal or public way
to comment critically or unfavorably
archaic

Examples

The professor did not hesitate to animadvert on the shortcomings of the proposal.
She was known to animadvert harshly against those who broke the rules.
During the debate, several speakers animadverted on the government's handling of the crisis.

Synset Examples

John spoke up at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or point out the faults of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'animadvertere' meaning 'to notice, to turn the mind to.'
