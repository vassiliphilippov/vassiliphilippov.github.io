Thyreophora

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌriːəˈfɔrə / (AmE), / θɪˌrɪəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A group of armored dinosaurs, including the stegosaurs and ankylosaurs, characterized by protective bony plates or armor.
group of armored dinosaurs

Examples

The discovery of new Thyreophora fossils has expanded our understanding of dinosaur armor.
Both stegosaurs and ankylosaurs are classified within the Thyreophora.
Researchers study Thyreophora to learn how their armor protected them from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stegosaurus - a genus of herbivorous, plated dinosaurs
  • Synonym: Ankylosaurus - a genus of heavily armored, herbivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreos' (shield) + 'phoros' (bearing), meaning 'shield-bearers'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪəriˈɑːfərə / (AmE), / ˌθɪəriˈɒfərə / (BrE)
a suborder of ornithischian dinosaurs known for their body armor, including stegosaurs and ankylosaurs
suborder of armored dinosaurs

Examples

The Thyreophora were distinguished by their extensive bony armor and defensive adaptations.
Scientists have discovered new fossils belonging to the Thyreophora in North America.
Stegosaurs and ankylosaurs, both members of the Thyreophora, roamed the Earth during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stegosaur - a type of plated armored dinosaur
  • Synonym: ankylosaur - a type of heavily armored dinosaur

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreos' (shield) + 'phoros' (bearing).
