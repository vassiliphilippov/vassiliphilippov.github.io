fingerpost

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəpəʊst / (BrE)
a guidepost shaped like a hand with an outstretched finger, used to indicate direction
guidepost with a pointing finger

Examples

The old village fingerpost pointed the way to three neighboring towns.
Tourists stopped at the fingerpost to check which path led to the coast.
Many rural roads in England are still marked by traditional fingerposts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signpost - a post with signs giving directions

Etymology

From 'finger' + 'post', referring to the hand-shaped pointer.
