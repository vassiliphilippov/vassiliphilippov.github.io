menu

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnju / (AmE), / ˈmɛnjuː / (BrE)
A list of the dishes that may be ordered in a meal, especially in a restaurant.
list of dishes for a meal

Examples

She scanned the menu before deciding what to order for dinner.
The restaurant's menu changes with the seasons to use fresh ingredients.
He asked the waiter to recommend something from the menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of fare - a list of food items available or served in a restaurant or at a meal

Etymology

From French 'menu' (detailed list), from Latin 'minutus' (small, detailed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07580648-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnju / (AmE), / ˈmɛnjuː / (BrE)
a list of the food and drinks that are available at a restaurant or café
list of dishes at a restaurant

Examples

Could you please bring me the menu so I can decide what to order?
The menu offers a variety of vegetarian and vegan options for customers.
They updated the menu to include more gluten-free dishes after customer requests.

Synset Examples

the menu was in French

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of fare - an alternative term for a restaurant menu
  • Synonym: à la carte - ordering individual dishes from a menu, rather than a fixed meal

Etymology

From French *menu* (detailed list), from Latin *minutus* (small, detailed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06504930-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnju / (AmE), / ˈmɛnjuː / (BrE)
(computing) a list of commands or options displayed on a computer screen from which a user can choose.
list of options on a computer screen

Examples

You can access the settings through the drop-down menu at the top of the screen.
The File menu contains commands for opening and saving documents.
A pop-up menu appeared when I right-clicked the desktop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toolbar - a strip of icons or buttons for performing actions in a software application

Etymology

From French 'menu' (detailed list), originally applied to restaurant lists and later adapted to computing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06505383-n
Synset POS: n
