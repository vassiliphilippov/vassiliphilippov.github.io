Luther

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːθər / (AmE), / ˈluːθə / (BrE)
(1483–1546) German theologian and reformer who initiated the Protestant Reformation, advocating justification by faith.
German theologian; leader of the Reformation
american british

Examples

Luther challenged the authority of the Catholic Church with his Ninety-five Theses in 1517.
The teachings of Luther had a profound impact on the development of modern Christianity.
Many historians regard Luther as the father of the Protestant Reformation.
