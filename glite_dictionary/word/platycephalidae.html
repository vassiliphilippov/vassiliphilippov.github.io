Platycephalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˌsəˈfælɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˌsɛˈfælɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, known as flatheads, characterized by their flattened heads and common in the Indo-Pacific region.
family of flathead fishes

Examples

The Platycephalidae family includes many species of bottom-dwelling fish found near reefs.
Biologists studied the distinctive jaw structure of several Platycephalidae specimens.
Fishermen in Australia often catch members of the Platycephalidae while trawling near the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flathead - a fish of the Platycephalidae family, notable for its flattened head

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'platys' (broad, flat) and 'kephalē' (head), with the family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ plætɪˌsɛfəˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / plætɪˌsiːfəˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of scorpaenoid flathead fishes found primarily in the Indo-Pacific region.
a family of flathead fishes

Examples

The Platycephalidae are commonly known as flatheads and inhabit shallow coastal waters.
Scientists are studying the habitat preferences of several species within the Platycephalidae family.
Members of the Platycephalidae display a distinctive flat head and elongated body.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Platycephalus (the type genus), from Greek platys (flat) + kephalē (head) + -idae (family suffix).
