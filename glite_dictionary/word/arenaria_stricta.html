Arenaria stricta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.əˈnɛr.i.ə ˈstrɪk.tə / (AmE), / ˌar.ɪˈnɑː.ri.ə ˈstrɪk.tə / (BrE)
A low perennial tufted plant native to southeastern North America, belonging to the sandwort genus.
species of low perennial sandwort

Examples

Arenaria stricta thrives in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
Botanists have identified Arenaria stricta as a key species in certain grassland ecosystems.
The delicate white flowers of Arenaria stricta bloom in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandwort - any plant of the genus Arenaria

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arenaria' meaning 'of sand', and 'stricta' meaning 'upright' or 'straight'.
