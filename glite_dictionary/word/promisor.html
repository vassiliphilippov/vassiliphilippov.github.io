promisor

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.mɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.mɪ.sɔː / (BrE)
a person who makes a promise, especially one who is legally bound to fulfill it
person who makes a promise
legal

Examples

The promisor is obligated to fulfill the terms agreed upon in the contract.
If the promisor fails to deliver on their promise, legal action may be taken.
The court asked whether the promisor had provided sufficient consideration for the agreement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: promisee - a person to whom a promise is made

Etymology

From Latin promissum (promise) + -or, a suffix meaning 'one who'.
