anxiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈaŋkʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of worry or unease, typically about something with an uncertain outcome
state of being anxious or uneasy

Examples

She could not hide her anxiousness before the job interview.
His anxiousness grew as the storm clouds gathered overhead.
Despite her anxiousness, she managed to speak confidently during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'anxious' + '-ness', formed from Latin 'anxius' (worried) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋkʃəsnəs / (BrE)
A state of being anxious; the quality of feeling uneasy, worried, or nervous, often over something with an uncertain outcome.
the state of feeling anxious or worried

Examples

Her constant anxiousness made it difficult for her to relax, even during quiet evenings at home.
A feeling of anxiousness crept over him before every important meeting.
The doctor noted that his patient's anxiousness had increased over the last few weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease

Etymology

From 'anxious' + '-ness,' forming the noun of quality from the adjective.
