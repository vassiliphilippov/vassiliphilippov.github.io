hydroxynervonic acid

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksi nɚˈvoʊnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksi nɜːˈvəʊnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a derivative of nervonic acid containing a hydroxyl group, found as a component of certain cerebrosides.
chemical derivative of nervonic acid
medical

Examples

Researchers identified hydroxynervonic acid as a key molecule in brain lipid metabolism.
Levels of hydroxynervonic acid were measured to study its role in neurological disorders.
The presence of hydroxynervonic acid in cerebrosides suggests its importance in nerve tissue function.

Synset Examples

Oxynervonic acid is an important constituent of certain cerebrosides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervonic acid - a monounsaturated fatty acid found in nerve tissue

Etymology

Formed from 'hydroxy-' (indicating the presence of a hydroxyl group) and 'nervonic acid'.
