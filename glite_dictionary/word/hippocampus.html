hippocampus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈkæmpəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkampəs / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fish with a horse-like head and curled tail, commonly known as seahorses.
a genus of seahorses (marine fish)

Examples

The coral reef is home to several species of hippocampus.
Researchers observed the hippocampus blending into the sea grass as a form of camouflage.
Many aquariums feature the unique marine creature known as the hippocampus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seahorse - a small marine fish with a horse-like head and prehensile tail

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'kampos' (sea monster) referring to the mythological creature and the shape of the animal.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈkæmpəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkæmpəs / (BrE)
a part of the brain, located in the inner region of each temporal lobe, that plays a crucial role in memory formation and is involved in emotion and motivation
brain region involved in memory
medical

Examples

The hippocampus is essential for converting short-term memories into long-term memories.
Damage to the hippocampus can result in severe memory loss and difficulty forming new memories.
Researchers have found that the size of the hippocampus may be linked to spatial navigation skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amygdala - brain region involved in emotion and fear responses

Etymology

From Greek 'hippokampos', meaning 'sea horse', due to its shape ('hippos' = horse, 'kampos' = sea monster).
