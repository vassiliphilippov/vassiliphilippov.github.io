extractible

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstræktəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be extracted; capable of being removed or obtained from something, especially by a physical or chemical process.
able to be extracted

Examples

Oil is extractible from seeds using industrial methods.
The chemicals in the soil are easily extractible with the right solvents.
The report showed which trace metals were extractible from the mineral samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extractable - capable of being extracted; variant spelling

Etymology

Formed from 'extract' + the suffix '-ible', from Latin '-ibilis', meaning 'able to be'.
