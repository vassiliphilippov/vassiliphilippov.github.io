porte-cochere

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːrt koʊˈʃɛr / (AmE), / ˌpɔːt kɒˈʃɛə / (BrE)
A covered entrance large enough for vehicles to pass through, typically extending through a building to an inner courtyard.
covered carriage entrance to courtyard

Examples

The grand mansion has a beautiful porte-cochere at its entrance, allowing carriages to pull up under cover.
Guests appreciate being dropped off at the porte-cochere during bad weather.
The renovated hotel kept the original porte-cochere to preserve its historic charm.

Etymology

From French 'porte-cochère', meaning 'coach gate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrt koʊˈʃɛr / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtə kɒˈʃɛə / (BrE)
A covered structure at the entrance of a building, designed to provide shelter to people getting in or out of vehicles.
covered entrance shelter for vehicles

Examples

The guests were dropped off beneath the porte-cochere during the heavy rain.
An elegant porte-cochere welcomed visitors to the historic mansion.
The hotel added a new porte-cochere to provide better access for arriving vehicles.

Etymology

From French porte cochère (literally 'coach door'), referring to its function for carriages.
