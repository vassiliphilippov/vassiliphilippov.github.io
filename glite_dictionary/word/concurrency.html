concurrency

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkɜrənsi / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the state of being in agreement or accordance, especially with respect to opinions or results
agreement or accordance (of opinions/results)

Examples

There was a strong concurrency among the committee members regarding the decision.
Scientific progress often comes from the concurrency of independent research findings.
Their views are in perfect concurrency on this issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: concord - agreement or harmony between people or groups
  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or harmony

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrere' (to run together, to coincide) via Medieval Latin 'concurrentia'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈkɝːənsi / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the state of two or more actions, events, or circumstances occurring or acting together at the same time
simultaneous occurrence of events or actions

Examples

The unexpected concurrency of several emergencies placed the hospital staff under great pressure.
His research focuses on the concurrency of economic and social changes in the region.
There was a remarkable concurrency between technological innovation and legal reform at that time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneity - the state of happening at the same time
  • Antonym: sequence - a following of one action after another

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrentia', from 'concurrere' meaning 'to run together'.
