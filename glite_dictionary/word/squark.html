squark

1. NOUN

/ skwɑrk / (AmE), / skwɑːk / (BrE)
a type of elementary particle, classified as a quark, with an electric charge of -1/3 and a strangeness of -1
a type of quark particle

Examples

Scientists detected evidence of a squark in the high-energy experiment.
The squark has a charge of minus one-third and possesses strangeness.
Physicists studied the properties of the squark to better understand particle interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quark - a fundamental particle and a basic constituent of matter

Etymology

Formed by blending 'strange' and 'quark'; named for its property of 'strangeness'.
