to babysit

1. VERB

/ ˈbeɪbiˌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiˌsɪt / (BrE)
to take care of a child, animal, or sometimes another person's possessions for a period of time, usually while the owner is away
take care of someone or something temporarily

Examples

Can you babysit my cat this weekend while I'm away?
She often babysits for her little brother in the evenings.
Last summer, I babysat my neighbors’ plants during their vacation.

Synset Examples

I baby-sit the neighbor's plants when she is out of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - look after or keep under attentive observation
  • Synonym: to look after - to take care of someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'baby' + 'sit', originally meaning to watch over a baby.

2. VERB

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi.sɪt / (BrE)
to look after children temporarily while their parents are away
look after children temporarily

Examples

Can you babysit my kids while I run to the store?
She often babysits for her neighbors on weekends to earn extra money.
I babysat my little brother last night while my parents went out.

Synset Examples

I cannot baby-sit tonight: I have too much homework to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babysitter - a person who takes care of children temporarily

Etymology

Blend of 'baby' and 'sit', dating from mid 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02461692-v
Synset POS: v
