Hexanchus

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæŋkəs / (AmE), / hɛkˈsaŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks in the family Hexanchidae, known as cow sharks, characterized by having six or seven gill slits.
genus of cow sharks

Examples

The genus Hexanchus includes several species of cow sharks found in deep waters worldwide.
Paleontologists have discovered fossil records of Hexanchus dating back to the Jurassic period.
Unlike most sharks, members of Hexanchus have six or seven gill slits instead of five.

Etymology

From Greek hex (six) + agkhos (bend, hook), referring to the six or seven gill slits.
