chucker-out

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃʌkɚˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌtʃʌkəˈaʊt / (BrE)
a person employed to eject troublemakers from a bar, club, or public meeting
person who ejects troublemakers
informal british

Examples

The chucker-out escorted the rowdy guests from the club without incident.
If anyone gets too aggressive, the chucker-out will deal with them promptly.
Back in the day, every pub had its trusted chucker-out to keep the peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bouncer - a person employed to prevent trouble and eject unruly people at a bar or club

Etymology

Formed from 'chuck out' (to eject) + agent noun suffix '-er'.
