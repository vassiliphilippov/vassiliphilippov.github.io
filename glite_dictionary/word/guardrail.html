guardrail

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdˌreɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdˌreɪl / (BrE)
A strong railing placed at the edge of a stairway, road, or bridge to prevent people or vehicles from falling off.
safety railing by road or stairs

Examples

The car skidded off the road and crashed into the guardrail.
Please make sure to hold the guardrail when using the stairs.
Highway workers installed a new guardrail after the curve to improve safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - a fence or other obstacle that prevents movement or access
  • Synonym: handrail - a rail fixed to posts or a wall for people to hold on to for support

Etymology

From 'guard' (to protect) + 'rail' (a bar serving as a barrier).
