moneywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌniˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is skilled in handling large-scale financial transactions
woman skilled in large-scale finance

Examples

The board hired a new moneywoman to oversee the merger negotiations.
As a respected moneywoman, she manages complex investment portfolios for several international firms.
Becoming a successful moneywoman requires both expertise in finance and strong leadership skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moneyman - male person skilled in large scale financial transactions

Etymology

Formed from 'money' + 'woman', patterned after 'moneyman'.
