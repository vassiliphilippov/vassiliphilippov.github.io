raceway

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪsweɪ / (BrE)
A track or course over which races are held, especially for cars, horses, or other vehicles or animals.
track for races

Examples

The new raceway will open to the public next weekend.
Thousands of fans filled the grandstands at the raceway for the annual event.
After years of planning, the city finally built a state-of-the-art raceway for drag racing competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racetrack - a track for racing horses, cars, etc.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪsweɪ / (BrE)
a channel or canal designed to direct the flow of water, especially to supply a waterwheel or mill
canal for directing water flow

Examples

The old mill still uses the original raceway to direct water to its wheel.
Engineers designed a new raceway to improve the efficiency of the hydroelectric plant.
Excess debris in the raceway can obstruct the flow and reduce power output.

Etymology

From the words 'race' (a current of water) + 'way'; 'race' in this context comes from Old Norse 'rās' (a rush or current).
