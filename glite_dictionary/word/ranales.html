Ranales

1. NOUN

/ ræˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ræˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes a variety of woody and herbaceous families, such as Ranunculaceae and Magnoliaceae, chiefly used in historical botanical classification systems
historical order of flowering plants

Examples

The family Magnoliaceae was traditionally classified under the order Ranales.
Many older textbooks refer to water lilies as members of the Ranales.
With advances in plant taxonomy, Ranales has largely been replaced by newer classification systems.

Etymology

From Latin 'rana' (frog), originally referring to the buttercup family, combined with '-ales', a suffix used in plant orders.

2. NOUN

/ ræˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / rəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants including families such as Ranunculaceae, Magnoliaceae, and Lauraceae; now largely obsolete in modern classification
former order of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The family Magnoliaceae was once classified under the Ranales order in traditional taxonomy.
Some botanists still refer to the Ranales when describing relationships among certain plant families.
Recent revisions in plant taxonomy have largely replaced the use of Ranales with more modern groupings.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Ranunculus (a plant genus) + -ales (denoting orders in botanical nomenclature).
