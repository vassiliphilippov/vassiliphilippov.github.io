Cimex lectularius

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.mɛks lɛkˌtʌl.iˈɛə.ri.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.mɛks lɛkˌtʌl.ɪˈɛə.rɪ.əs / (BrE)
A species of bedbug commonly found in temperate regions, known for infesting beds and feeding on human blood.
common bedbug species
medical

Examples

Cimex lectularius is the scientific name for the common bedbug that can infest homes and hotels.
Pest control experts are trained to identify Cimex lectularius infestations by spotting the bugs and their eggs.
An increase in international travel has contributed to the spread of Cimex lectularius in major cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbug - A small parasitic insect that feeds on human blood, especially in beds

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cimex' meaning 'bug' and 'lectularius' meaning 'of a bed'.
