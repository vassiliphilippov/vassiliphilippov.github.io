Prajapati

1. PROPN

/ prɑːˈdʒɑːpəti / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɑːpəti / (BrE)
In Hinduism, a creator god regarded as personifying the creative force; often identified with Brahma.
a creator god in Hinduism

Examples

In early Vedic texts, Prajapati is revered as the lord of all creatures and a principal creator.
Some legends consider Prajapati to be synonymous with Brahma, the supreme creator god.
Devotees gathered at the temple to offer prayers to Prajapati during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brahma - the creator god in Hinduism, often equated with Prajapati

Etymology

From Sanskrit prajā-pati (lord of creatures).
