links

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks / (AmE), / lɪŋks / (BrE)
a golf course, typically built on sandy soil near the coast, known for its natural terrain and few trees
coastal golf course with natural terrain

Examples

The championship will be held on the historic links next to the coast.
Early morning fog often sweeps across the links, making play more challenging.
Golfers travel from around the world to experience traditional Scottish links.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf course - an area of land designed for playing golf

Etymology

From Old English 'hlinc' (rising ground, ridge), referring to sandy ridges along a shore.
