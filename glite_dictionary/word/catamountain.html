catamountain

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈmaʊntɪn / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈmaʊntɪn / (BrE)
a bushy-tailed wildcat of Europe resembling the domestic cat, regarded as the ancestor of the domestic cat
European wildcat; ancestor of the domestic cat

Examples

The catamountain is rarely seen in the dense forests of Scotland.
Some naturalists believe the catamountain is the direct ancestor of today's domestic cats.
The fur of the catamountain is thicker and bushier than that of a household pet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildcat - a wild member of the cat family
  • Degree Lower: domestic cat - tamed and domesticated cat species

Etymology

From Middle English 'catamountain', from Old French 'cat de montagne' (cat of the mountain).
