flagitious

1. ADJ

/ fləˈɡɪʃəs / (AmE), / fləˈɡɪʃəs / (BrE)
extremely wicked or criminal; heinously immoral or evil.
extremely wicked or criminal

Examples

The prosecutor described the defendant's actions as flagitious during the closing argument.
Only the most flagitious individuals could commit such a horrendous offense.
Throughout history, flagitious deeds have shocked the conscience of entire societies.

Synset Examples

a flagitious crime
heinous accusations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heinous - shockingly wicked or evil
  • Synonym: atrocious - extremely wicked or cruel
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin flagitiosus (shameful, disgraceful).
