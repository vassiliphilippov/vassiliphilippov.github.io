Pteropus capestratus

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈroʊpəs kæˌpɛstrətəs / (AmE), / tɛˈrəʊpəs kaˈpɛstrətəs / (BrE)
A species of fruit bat native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, known for its large size and nocturnal habits.
a species of fruit bat

Examples

The Pteropus capestratus plays an important role in pollinating tropical plants.
Researchers tracked the movements of Pteropus capestratus populations across the island.
A Pteropus capestratus colony can often be found roosting in the tall trees near rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit bat - a bat of the family Pteropodidae that feeds mainly on fruit

Etymology

From Greek 'pteron' (wing) and Latin 'capistratus' (having a strap or band, referring to markings).
