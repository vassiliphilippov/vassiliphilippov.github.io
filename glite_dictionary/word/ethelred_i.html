Ethelred I

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈɛθəlˌrɛd ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 835–871) King of Wessex and Kent, and elder brother of Alfred the Great; he fought against the invading Danes and was succeeded by Alfred upon his death.
king of Wessex and Kent (died 871)

Examples

Ethelred I became king of Wessex and Kent after the death of his father.
During Ethelred I's reign, the Danes launched several invasions into southern England.
Alfred supported his brother Ethelred I in the battles against the Viking invaders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Alfred the Great - King of Wessex and successor to Ethelred I
  • Synonym: Ethelred II - Later king of England, sometimes called 'Ethelred the Unready'

Etymology

From Old English Æþelræd, meaning ‘noble counsel’.
