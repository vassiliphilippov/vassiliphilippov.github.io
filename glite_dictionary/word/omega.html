omega

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈmeɪɡə / (AmE), / əʊˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
the final part of a process, series, or sequence; the end.
the final part; the end of a sequence

Examples

Philosophers often discuss the alpha and the omega when reflecting on the nature of beginnings and endings.
The project marked the omega of her long career in public service.
In the novel, the apocalypse is depicted as the omega of human civilization.

Synset Examples

the Alpha and the Omega, the first and the last, the beginning and the end

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alpha - the beginning of a series or sequence

Etymology

From Greek ō mega, literally ‘great O’, the last letter of the Greek alphabet.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈmeɪɡə / (AmE), / əʊˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
the last (24th) letter of the Greek alphabet, used symbolically in various contexts
last letter of the Greek alphabet

Examples

The symbol omega is often used in scientific equations to represent angular velocity.
He signed his letter with the Greek omega to symbolize the end of an era.
In biblical terms, Christ is sometimes referred to as the Alpha and the Omega.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alpha - the first letter of the Greek alphabet

Etymology

From Greek ōmega, meaning 'great o', from ō (variant of o) + mega (great).

Source: WordNet
