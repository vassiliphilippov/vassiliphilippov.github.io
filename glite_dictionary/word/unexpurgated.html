unexpurgated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɛkspɚˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛkspəˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a text) not having had material deleted, especially material considered offensive or objectionable.
not having material removed or censored

Examples

The publisher released an unexpurgated version of the controversial novel.
She insisted on reading the unexpurgated diaries to ensure nothing was omitted.
Many collectors sought out the unexpurgated edition for its historical accuracy.

Synset Examples

volumes of the best plays, unexpurgated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expurgated - having had offensive or objectionable material removed

Etymology

From un- (not) + expurgated, from Latin expurgare (to cleanse out).
