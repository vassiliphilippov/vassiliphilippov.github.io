vituperation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.tə.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪ.tjʊ.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
harsh and abusive criticism or language; strong verbal condemnation.
abusive or harsh criticism

Examples

The politician's speech was met with vituperation from his opponents.
Online forums are sometimes rife with vituperation and personal attacks.
She responded to the criticism with calm dignity, refusing to engage in vituperation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invective - insulting, abusive, or highly critical language
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin vituperatio (blame, reproach), from vituperare (to blame, censure).
