fantods

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌtɑdz / (AmE), / ˈfæntɒdz / (BrE)
a state of uneasiness, nervousness, or irritability, often with an element of restlessness
nervous irritability or uneasiness
archaic

Examples

When he heard the eerie noise in the dark hallway, he got the fantods.
The thought of public speaking always gave her the fantods.
That gloomy old house is enough to give anyone the fantods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jitters - a feeling of nervousness or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet; absence of nervousness or agitation

Etymology

Origin uncertain; popularized in 19th-century American English, appearing in the works of Mark Twain.
