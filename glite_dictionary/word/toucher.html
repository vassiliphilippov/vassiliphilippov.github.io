toucher

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃə / (BrE)
a person who shows physical affection when greeting others, such as by hugging or touching
person who greets with physical affection

Examples

She’s a real toucher, always reaching out to give friends a hug or a pat on the back.
Some people are uncomfortable with touchers at social gatherings.
If you’re not a toucher, just let others know you prefer a handshake.

Synset Examples

He’s known as a toucher, always greeting with hugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hugger - person who greets others with hugs
  • Antonym: non-toucher - person who avoids physical contact when greeting

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃə / (BrE)
a person who is physically affectionate and tends to express friendliness or warmth through touch
person who is physically affectionate

Examples

Touchers often make others feel comfortable and cared for by their natural warmth.
As a toucher, he always greets friends with a friendly pat on the back.
Some people appreciate a toucher, while others may prefer more personal space.

Synset Examples

Being a toucher, she makes everyone feel welcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hugger - a person who likes to hug others

Etymology

From 'touch' (Middle English) + '-er', agentive suffix.
