Lanthanotus borneensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlænθəˈnoʊtəs bɔrˈneɪənˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˌlanθəˈnəʊtəs bɔːˈniːənˌsɪs / (BrE)
A species of stout-bodied lizard native to Borneo, also known as the Borneo earless monitor lizard.
a lizard species native to Borneo

Examples

The Lanthanotus borneensis is prized by herpetologists for its unique evolutionary lineage.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of Lanthanotus borneensis in Borneo.
Few people have ever seen a live Lanthanotus borneensis due to its secretive nature.

Etymology

From Greek 'lanthanein' (to be hidden) and 'ous' (ear), referring to its hidden ear openings; 'borneensis' denotes origin from Borneo.
