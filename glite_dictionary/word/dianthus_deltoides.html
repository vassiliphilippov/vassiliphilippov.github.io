Dianthus deltoides

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs dɛlˈtɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs dɛlˈtɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a low-growing Eurasian plant of the pink family with loosely mat-forming foliage and single pale pink flowers with crimson centers
species of Eurasian pink-flowered plant

Examples

Gardeners often use Dianthus deltoides as a colorful ground cover in rock gardens.
The delicate flowers of Dianthus deltoides attract bees and butterflies throughout the summer.
She planted several Dianthus deltoides along the border to create a soft pink accent.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Dianthus' derives from Greek 'dios' (divine) + 'anthos' (flower); 'deltoides' means 'shaped like the Greek letter delta'.
