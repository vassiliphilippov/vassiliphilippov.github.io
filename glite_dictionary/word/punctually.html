punctually

1. ADV

/ ˈpʌŋktʃuəli / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktʃuəli / (BrE)
At exactly the appointed or expected time; on time.
at the appointed or expected time

Examples

The train departed punctually at 9 a.m., exactly as scheduled.
She always makes sure to arrive punctually for her appointments.
If you finish your work punctually, you will have more free time.

Synset Examples

she always arrives on time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: late - after the expected or usual time
  • Synonym: promptly - without delay; immediately or very soon

Etymology

From Latin punctualis (exact), from punctum (a point) + -ly.
