import

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pɔːt / (BrE)
a commodity, service, or product brought into one country from another for sale, use, or processing
goods/services brought from abroad

Examples

The government increased taxes on luxury imports to protect local industries.
China is one of the world's largest import markets for agricultural products.
Most of the country's oil is an import from the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: export - a commodity, service, or product sent from one country to another for sale or use

Etymology

From Latin 'importare', meaning 'to bring in'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ɪmˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːt / (BrE)

transfer data into a database or document

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: export - to transfer electronic data out of a database or document

4. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːt / (BrE)
(formal) To indicate or mean; to signify.
indicate or signify (formal use)

Examples

This decision imports a significant change in the company's direction.
His words imported more concern than he openly expressed.
In legal documents, the phrase often imports an obligation or duty.

Synset Examples

I'm afraid this spells trouble!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to signify - to be a sign of; to mean
  • Synonym: to imply - to strongly suggest the truth or existence of something

Etymology

From Latin *importare* (to bring in), through French *importer*; figurative sense of 'to mean' arises in legal and literary Latin.
