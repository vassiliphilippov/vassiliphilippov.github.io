Proto-Norse

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊ ˈnɔrs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊ ˈnɔːs / (BrE)
the earliest stage of the North Germanic languages, spoken in Scandinavia until about 700 AD
early North Germanic language of Scandinavia

Examples

Linguists study Proto-Norse to understand the origins of Scandinavian languages.
Inscriptions in Proto-Norse can be found on ancient runestones across Sweden and Norway.
The transition from Proto-Norse to Old Norse was marked by significant phonological changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Old Norse - the North Germanic language spoken in Scandinavia from about 700 to 1350

Etymology

From 'proto-' (Greek, meaning 'first' or 'earliest') + 'Norse' (the North Germanic peoples/language).
