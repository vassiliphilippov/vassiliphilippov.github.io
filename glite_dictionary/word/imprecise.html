imprecise

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪm.prɪˈsaɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɪˈsaɪs / (BrE)
not exact, accurate, or clearly defined
not exact or accurate

Examples

Her explanation was too imprecise to be useful in solving the problem.
The results are imprecise because the equipment was not calibrated.
He gave an imprecise estimate of the repair costs.

Synset Examples

imprecise astronomical observations
the terms he used were imprecise and emotional

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precise - exact, accurate, and clearly defined
  • Synonym: inexact - not exact or accurate

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + precise, from Latin præcīsus (precise).
