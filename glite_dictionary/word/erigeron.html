Erigeron

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪdʒəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdʒərɒn / (BrE)
A genus of usually perennial herbs with daisy-like flowers, commonly known as fleabanes, some species of which have been used medicinally.
genus of perennial herbs (fleabanes)

Examples

The garden featured a bright patch of Erigeron along the pathway.
Some traditional remedies use Erigeron for its supposed medicinal properties.
Several species of Erigeron thrive in rocky soils and sunny locations.

Etymology

From Greek erigerōn, meaning 'early old man', referring to the hoary (greyish-white) appearance of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪrɑn / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪərɒn / (BrE)
A genus of mostly perennial herbs in the daisy family, with flower heads resembling asters; some species have been used medicinally.
genus of daisy-like flowering herbs

Examples

The garden was bordered with Erigeron to add color and attract pollinators.
Some traditional remedies have used leaves from certain Erigeron species for medicinal purposes.
Many Erigeron plants bloom in early summer, brightening meadows with their daisy-like flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'ērigerōn', meaning 'early old man', referring to the hoary seeds and early flowering.
