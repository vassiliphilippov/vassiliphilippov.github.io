umbilical

1. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling the umbilicus (navel) or the umbilical cord.
Of or relating to the navel or umbilical cord
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an umbilical incision to access the abdominal cavity.
Newborns are often monitored for umbilical infections after the cord falls off.
The fetus receives nutrients through the umbilical cord during pregnancy.

Synset Examples

umbilical cord

Etymology

From Latin umbilicalis, from umbilicus (navel).

WordNet ID: oewn-02851820-a

2. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl / (BrE)
a membranous duct connecting a fetus with the placenta, through which nutrients, gases, and wastes are exchanged; also known as umbilical cord
membranous duct between fetus and placenta
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully inspected the umbilical before proceeding with the delivery.
Damage to the umbilical can disrupt the supply of oxygen to the fetus.
Scientists study the umbilical to understand prenatal development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbilical cord - the cord-like structure connecting fetus to placenta

Etymology

From Latin umbilicalis, relating to the navel (umbilicus).

WordNet ID: oewn-05528130-n
Synset POS: n
