rofecoxib

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊfəˈkɑksɪb / (AmE), / ˌrəʊfəˈkɒksɪb / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (COX-2 inhibitor), formerly marketed as Vioxx, used to relieve pain and inflammation before being withdrawn in 2004 due to safety concerns.
withdrawn anti-inflammatory drug (Vioxx)
medical

Examples

After reports of increased cardiovascular risk, rofecoxib was withdrawn from pharmacies worldwide.
Researchers studied the effects of rofecoxib compared to other painkillers.
My doctor once prescribed rofecoxib for my arthritis pain before it was taken off the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celecoxib - another COX-2 inhibitor anti-inflammatory drug
