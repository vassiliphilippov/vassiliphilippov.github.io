myodynia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈdɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈdɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
pain in a muscle or group of muscles.
pain in a muscle
medical

Examples

Intense myodynia can occur after vigorous exercise in people who are not accustomed to physical activity.
The patient reported persistent myodynia following her viral infection.
Chronic myodynia may require both physical therapy and analgesic medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myalgia - pain in a muscle or muscles

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'odynē' (pain).
