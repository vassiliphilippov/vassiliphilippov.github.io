gyroplane

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪ.roʊˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪ.rəʊˌpleɪn / (BrE)
An aircraft with unpowered rotating horizontal blades for lift and a conventional propeller for forward movement.
rotor-based aircraft with propeller propulsion

Examples

The gyroplane rose smoothly from the field, its blades spinning freely in the wind.
In the early 20th century, inventors experimented with the gyroplane as an alternative to airplanes and helicopters.
A pilot must complete special training before flying a gyroplane due to its unique handling characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autogyro - aircraft with unpowered rotor for lift and a propeller for thrust

Etymology

From Greek 'gyros' (circle) and 'plane', referring to circular motion and flight.
