eyra

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrə / (AmE), / ˈaɪrə / (BrE)
a long-bodied, long-tailed wildcat native to tropical America, typically reddish or brownish in color
a tropical American wildcat

Examples

The eyra is well adapted to life in the dense forests of Central and South America.
Unlike larger cats, the eyra hunts mainly during the day.
Local guides pointed out an eyra slinking through the underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaguarundi - a small South American wildcat, also known as the eyra

Etymology

From Portuguese 'eira', a local Brazilian name for the animal.
