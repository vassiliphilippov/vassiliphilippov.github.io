epileptic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of epilepsy.
relating to epilepsy
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed that it was an epileptic seizure.
He takes medication to manage his epileptic condition.
Certain flashing lights can trigger epileptic episodes in sensitive individuals.

Synset Examples

epileptic seizure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - A neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of seizures

Etymology

From Greek epileptikos, from epilēpsia (seizure).

WordNet ID: oewn-02956997-a

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who has epilepsy
person with epilepsy
medical

Examples

As an epileptic, she must take her medication daily to prevent seizures.
The support group meets weekly to help epileptics and their families.
Advances in treatment have greatly improved the quality of life for many epileptic patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of sensory disturbance or convulsions

Etymology

From Greek epileptikos (subject to seizures), from epilēpsis (seizure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10081983-n
Synset POS: n
