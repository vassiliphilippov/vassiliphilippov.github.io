Saint David

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
The patron saint of Wales, a Christian bishop and preacher who lived circa 520–600.
patron saint of Wales

Examples

Every year, the Welsh celebrate Saint David's Day on March 1st.
Many churches in Wales are dedicated to Saint David.
Legends say that Saint David performed miracles during his lifetime.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Davidus', referring to the Christian saint David.
