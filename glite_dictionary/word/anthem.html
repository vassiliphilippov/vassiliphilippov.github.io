anthem

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.θəm / (AmE), / ˈæn.θəm / (BrE)
a song that is sung to express devotion or loyalty to a group, nation, or cause, often used officially by a country or school
song of loyalty or devotion

Examples

The national anthem is played before major sporting events.
She proudly sang the school anthem at graduation.
Many people stood and removed their hats as the anthem began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a religious song or song of praise

Etymology

From Greek 'antiphōna' via Old English and Medieval Latin, originally meaning a song of praise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.θəm / (AmE), / ˈæn.θəm / (BrE)
a song of devotion or praise to God, a saint, or a nation, often expressing national or religious identity
song of devotion or praise

Examples

The crowd stood in silence as the national anthem was played before the match.
Many churches open the service with an uplifting anthem sung by the choir.
That song became an unofficial anthem for the civil rights movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a song of praise to God or a deity
  • Degree Higher: national anthem - the official song of a nation, used at official events

Etymology

From Greek 'antiphōna' (responsory hymn), via Old English and Medieval Latin.
