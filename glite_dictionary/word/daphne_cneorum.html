Daphne cneorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæfni nəˈjɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈdæfni knɪˈjɔːrəm / (BrE)
a low, evergreen shrub widely cultivated for its dense clusters of fragrant pink to deep rose flowers
evergreen shrub with fragrant pink flowers

Examples

The Daphne cneorum in her garden blooms every spring with a delicate fragrance.
Gardeners prize Daphne cneorum for its attractive pink flowers and compact, evergreen habit.
Many botanical gardens feature Daphne cneorum in their alpine plant collections.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Daphne' (the genus name, from Greek Δάφνη for 'laurel'), and 'cneorum' meaning 'like an olive' in Greek.
