Loranthus

1. NOUN

/ lɔˈrænθəs / (AmE), / lɒˈrænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flowering plants in the family Loranthaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of parasitic tropical plants

Examples

The branches of the tree were covered with Loranthus, affecting its growth.
Researchers discovered a new use for Loranthus in traditional medicine.
Some species of Loranthus are known to harm their host plants by extracting water and nutrients.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the Greek 'loron' (strap) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the shape of the flowers.
