Maja squinado

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪə skwɪˈnɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə skwɪˈnɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a large species of spider crab found in European waters, especially in the Mediterranean and northeast Atlantic
large European spider crab species

Examples

The Maja squinado is often sought after by Mediterranean fishermen for its meat.
Divers spotted several Maja squinado on the rocky seabed off the coast of Portugal.
The Maja squinado can be recognized by its spiny carapace and long legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider crab - a type of crab with long legs, family Majidae

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Maja' as the genus and 'squinado' a common Mediterranean name for the species.
