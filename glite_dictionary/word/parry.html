parry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpæri / (BrE)
an act of blocking or deflecting an attack, especially with a circular motion of a sword in fencing
defensive move in fencing

Examples

The fencer performed a quick parry to deflect his opponent’s blade.
A skilled parry can turn the momentum of the bout in your favor.
With a deft parry, she avoided being hit during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riposte - an immediate counterattack after a parry in fencing
  • Synonym: block - an act of stopping an attack or movement in sports or combat
  • Antonym: attack - an offensive move in fencing or combat

Etymology

From French 'parer' (to ward off, defend), from Italian 'parare'.

3. VERB

/ ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpæri / (BrE)
to avoid answering a question, fulfilling a duty, or dealing with an issue, often by cleverly deflecting it
deflect or avoid (a question or issue)

Examples

When asked about the budget cuts, the minister tried to parry the question with a vague response.
She skillfully parried criticism during the interview, refusing to be drawn into specifics.
The spokesperson kept parrying reporters’ questions about the scandal.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpar.i / (BrE)
an act of blocking or deflecting an opponent's blow, especially in boxing or fencing
defensive block in boxing or fencing

Examples

The boxer's quick parry deflected his opponent's powerful jab.
She scored a point with a swift parry during the fencing match.
A well-timed parry can change the outcome of the bout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - a defensive move to stop a blow in sports
  • Synonym: dodge - a quick movement to avoid a blow

Etymology

From French 'parer' (to ward off), from Italian 'parare' (to prepare, defend).
