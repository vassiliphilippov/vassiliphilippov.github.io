splat

1. NOUN

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
a thin, flat piece of wood forming the central support in the backrest of a straight chair
vertical strip in a chair backrest

Examples

The antique chair was missing its splat, leaving the back unsteady.
Craftsmen paid close attention to the design of the splat in Georgian chairs.
A decorative splat can add elegance to even a simple wooden chair.

Etymology

Likely from a variant of 'slat' meaning a thin strip of wood.

2. VERB

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
to make a flat, soft, and often wet noise when hitting a surface and flattening on impact
to hit and flatten on impact, making a wet noise

Examples

The paintball splatted against the window, leaving a colorful smear.
Water balloons often splat on the pavement, bursting as they hit the ground.
He slipped on the wet floor and splatted onto his back.

Synset Examples

The snowballs splatted on the trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - to crush something so that it becomes flat or soft

3. NOUN

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
a sound or mark made when something wet or soft hits a surface with force
sound or mark of wet impact

Examples

The egg hit the sidewalk with a loud splat.
A big splat of paint landed on her shirt.
He slipped in the mud and landed with a messy splat.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

5. VERB

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
to produce a sharp slapping or squashing sound, especially when something soft or semi-liquid strikes a surface
make a sharp squashing sound

Examples

Raindrops splat loudly on the windowpane during the storm.
She accidentally stepped on a grape and it splat beneath her shoe.

Related Terms
