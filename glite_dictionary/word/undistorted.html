undistorted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈstɔɹ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈstɔː.tɪd / (BrE)
not changed, altered, or misrepresented; remaining true or accurate to the original form or facts.
not changed or misrepresented

Examples

The scientist ensured the data remained undistorted throughout the entire experiment.
Through the clear glass, the image appeared undistorted and sharp.
She valued undistorted news reports that presented facts objectively.

Synset Examples

his judgment was undistorted by emotion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: distorted - changed from the original form; twisted or misrepresented
  • Synonym: true - accurate; in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'distorted', which comes from Latin 'distortus', past participle of 'distorquere' (to twist apart).
