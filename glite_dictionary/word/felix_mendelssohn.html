Felix Mendelssohn

1. PROPN

/ fəˈlɪks ˈmɛndəlˌsoʊn / (AmE), / fəˈliːks ˈmɛndl̩shəʊn / (BrE)
(1809–1847) A German composer, pianist, and conductor of the Romantic period, noted for his orchestral and choral works.
German Romantic composer and musician

Examples

Many music historians recognize Felix Mendelssohn as a pivotal figure of the early Romantic era.
The works of Felix Mendelssohn are often performed in classical music concerts worldwide.
Felix Mendelssohn composed the famous "Wedding March," still popular at weddings today.

Etymology

Felix Mendelssohn: 'Felix' from Latin meaning 'happy' or 'fortunate'; 'Mendelssohn' is German, 'son of Mendel'.
