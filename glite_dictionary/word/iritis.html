iritis

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / aɪˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the iris, the colored part of the eye, often causing pain, redness, and vision problems
inflammation of the iris
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with iritis after he complained of severe eye pain and sensitivity to light.
If left untreated, iritis can lead to persistent vision problems and even blindness.
She was prescribed steroids to reduce the inflammation caused by iritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uveitis - inflammation of the uvea (middle layer of the eye, including the iris)
  • Degree Lower: conjunctivitis - inflammation of the conjunctiva of the eye

Etymology

From Greek iris (rainbow, iris of the eye) + -itis (inflammation).
