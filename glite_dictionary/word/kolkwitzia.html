Kolkwitzia

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈkwɪtsiə / (AmE), / kɒlˈkwɪtsiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plant from China, consisting of a single species known as the beauty bush, noted for its ornamental pink flowers.
genus of flowering shrubs; beauty bush

Examples

The Kolkwitzia in my garden blooms beautifully every spring with its delicate pink flowers.
Botanists often refer to Kolkwitzia as the beauty bush because of its ornamental appeal.
She planted a Kolkwitzia along the walkway to add vibrant color to her landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty bush - A flowering shrub known for its pink trumpet-shaped flowers, the only species in the Kolkwitzia genus

Etymology

Named after German botanist Richard Kolkwitz (1873–1956).

2. NOUN

/ kɑlˈkwɪtsiə / (AmE), / kɒlˈkwɪtsiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs native to China, containing a single species popularly known as the beauty bush.
genus of flowering shrub (beauty bush)

Examples

The Kolkwitzia in my garden is covered with delicate pink flowers every spring.
Botanists admire Kolkwitzia for its ornamental value and hardiness.
She planted a Kolkwitzia hedge along the fence to add some vibrant color to her yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty bush - the common name for Kolkwitzia amabilis, the only species in the genus Kolkwitzia

Etymology

Named after German botanist Richard Kolkwitz (1873–1956).
