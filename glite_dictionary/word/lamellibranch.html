lamellibranch

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛlɪˌbræŋk / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlɪˌbraŋk / (BrE)
a marine or freshwater mollusk with a soft body and platelike gills, enclosed within two shells hinged together; a bivalve
bivalve mollusk with platelike gills
medical

Examples

The oyster is a common lamellibranch found in shallow marine waters.
Scientists studied the gill structure of each lamellibranch species in the estuary.
This museum display includes a variety of fossilized lamellibranchs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - A mollusk with two hinged shells
  • Antonym: gastropod - Mollusks such as snails or slugs with a single shell or none

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella' (thin plate) + Greek 'branchia' (gills).

2. ADJ

/ ləˈmɛl.əˌbræŋk / (AmE), / ləˈmɛl.ɪˌbraŋk / (BrE)
A mollusk of the class Bivalvia, such as a clam, oyster, or mussel, having a shell consisting of two hinged parts.
bivalve mollusk (clam, oyster, etc.)

Examples

Scientists discovered several lamellibranch fossils embedded in the limestone.
The oyster is a common lamellibranch found in coastal waters around the world.
This museum exhibit showcases the diverse forms of lamellibranch shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - an aquatic mollusk with a shell consisting of two hinged parts

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella' (thin plate) and Greek 'branchia' (gills).
