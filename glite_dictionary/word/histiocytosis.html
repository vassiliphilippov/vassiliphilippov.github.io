histiocytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstioʊsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪstɪəʊsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A blood disorder marked by an abnormal increase in the number of macrophages (a type of white blood cell).
disease with abnormally increased macrophages
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that histiocytosis can affect multiple organs within the body.
Research into treatments for histiocytosis has advanced significantly over the past decade.
Children diagnosed with histiocytosis often require specialized care and monitoring.

Etymology

From New Latin histio- (tissue) + -cyte (cell) + -osis (condition).
