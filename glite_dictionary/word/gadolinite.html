gadolinite

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡædəlˈɪnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɡadəˈlɪnʌɪt / (BrE)
A black or brownish mineral consisting mainly of silicates of iron, beryllium, and various rare earth elements, and used as an ore of rare earth metals.
a rare earth mineral (silicate)

Examples

The geologist discovered a deposit of gadolinite in the mountain range.
Samples of gadolinite were analyzed for their rare earth content.
Early studies of gadolinite led to the discovery of several new elements.

Etymology

Named after Finnish chemist Johan Gadolin (1760–1852), who first discovered one of its components.
