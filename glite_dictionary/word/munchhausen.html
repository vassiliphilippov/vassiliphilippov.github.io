Munchhausen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊntʃˌhaʊzən / (AmE), / ˈmʊntʃˌhaʊzən / (BrE)
A German nobleman famed for telling wildly exaggerated stories about his adventures; his name is now used to refer to anyone who tells preposterous or implausible tales.
famed teller of tall tales; synonym for exaggerator

Examples

Don't be such a Munchhausen; nobody believes you swam across the Atlantic!
The novel's main character is a modern-day Munchhausen, always spinning outrageous stories.
With the flair of a true Munchhausen, he recounted how he wrestled a bear with his bare hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibber - person who tells small lies
  • Synonym: storyteller - person who tells stories, often exaggerated
  • Antonym: truth-teller - person who tells the truth

Etymology

From the German name Münchhausen, belonging to Baron Hieronymus Karl Friedrich von Münchhausen (1720–1797).
