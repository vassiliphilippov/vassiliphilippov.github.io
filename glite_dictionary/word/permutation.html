permutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɜr.mjəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.mjʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a complete change in the character, condition, or arrangement of something
a complete transformation or change

Examples

The company's restructuring led to a dramatic permutation in its business model.
Throughout history, societies have undergone permutation due to technological advances.
The novel depicts the permutation of a peaceful village into a bustling city.

Synset Examples

the permutations … taking place in the physical world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a marked change in form, nature, or appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'permutatio' (an exchange, change), from 'permutare' (to change thoroughly).

2. NOUN

/ pərˌmjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pɜːˌmjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
each of the possible ways in which a set of objects can be arranged or ordered; the act or result of changing the arrangement of elements in a group.
arrangement or ordering of a set

Examples

There are 120 possible permutations of the five books on the shelf.
The code uses a complex permutation of letters and numbers to ensure security.
Trying every permutation of the puzzle pieces took him all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combination - a selection of objects without regard to order

Etymology

From Latin 'permutatio' (change, alteration), from 'permutare' (to change thoroughly).
