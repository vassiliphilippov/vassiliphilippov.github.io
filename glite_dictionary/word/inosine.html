inosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.oʊˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɪn.əʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside formed by the deamination of adenosine, used in biological research and sometimes in medical contexts such as organ transplantation
a nucleoside formed from adenosine
medical

Examples

The scientist added inosine to the culture medium to observe its effect on cell growth.
Doctors sometimes use inosine as a temporary energy source during organ transplantation procedures.
Research on inosine has provided insights into RNA editing and nucleotide metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenosine - a nucleoside consisting of adenine and ribose

Etymology

From Greek 'inos' (fiber, muscle), with the suffix '-ine' used for chemicals.
