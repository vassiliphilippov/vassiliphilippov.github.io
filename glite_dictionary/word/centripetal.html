centripetal

1. ADJ

/ sɛnˈtrɪpɪtəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪpɪtəl / (BrE)
tending to unify or bring things together toward a central point or common goal
tending to unify or bring together

Examples

The leader's speech had a strong centripetal effect on the divided group.
Cultural symbols can act as a centripetal force within a community.
The new policy aims to be centripetal by uniting various regional teams.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centrifugal - moving away from a center or tending to separate

Etymology

From New Latin centripetus, from Latin centrum (center) + petere (to seek).

2. ADJ

/ sɛnˈtrɪpɪtəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪpɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber or impulse) originating outside and passing toward the central nervous system.
moving toward the central nervous system
medical

Examples

The centripetal nerve fibers carry sensory information from the skin to the brain.
During reflex testing, doctors analyze how centripetal impulses travel toward the spinal cord.
Damage to centripetal neurons may affect a patient’s ability to feel touch or pain.

Synset Examples

sensory neurons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afferent - (of a nerve fiber or impulse) conducting toward the central nervous system
  • Antonym: centrifugal - (of a nerve fiber or impulse) conducting away from the central nervous system

Etymology

From New Latin centripetalis, from Latin centrum (center) + petere (to seek, to go to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00334509-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ sɛnˈtrɪpətl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪpɪtəl / (BrE)
Moving or tending to move toward a center; directed inward toward the center of rotation or axis.
tending to move toward a center

Examples

The moon is kept in orbit by the Earth's centripetal force.
A centripetal motion brings the spinning ice skater's arms closer to her body.
During circular movement, centripetal acceleration always points toward the center.

Synset Examples

centripetal force

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centrifugal - moving or tending to move away from a center

Etymology

From New Latin 'centripetus', from Latin 'centrum' (center) + 'petere' (to seek).
