game-changing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪm ˌtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪm ˌtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
having a significant effect on the outcome of a situation; dramatically altering the current state of affairs
having a significant, transformative impact

Examples

The launch of the smartphone was a game-changing moment in the tech industry.
Her discovery had a game-changing effect on cancer research.
Introducing solar energy to rural areas proved to be game-changing for local communities.

Synset Examples

The new policy proved to be game-changing for the company.
Her insights offered a game-changing perspective.
This technology is truly game-changing for the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformative - causing a major change
  • Synonym: revolutionary - completely new and changing a lot
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or impactful

Etymology

From 'game' (meaning a contest or competition) and 'changing' (altering), referring to something that changes the outcome of a contest or situation.
