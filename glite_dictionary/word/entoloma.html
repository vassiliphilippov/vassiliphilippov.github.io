Entoloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of agaric fungi characterized by pink spores and lacking both a volva and annulus, some species of which are poisonous.
genus of pink-spored fungi

Examples

Some species of Entoloma mushrooms are highly toxic and should never be eaten.
The field guide contains illustrations of several Entoloma varieties found in Europe.
Mycologists use spore color and absence of an annulus to identify Entoloma specimens.

Etymology

From Greek 'entos' (within) + 'loma' (fringe), referring to the inner margin of the gills.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of agaric fungi with pink spores, lacking both a volva and an annulus; some species are poisonous.
genus of pink-spored agaric fungi

Examples

The Entoloma genus includes several species that are difficult to distinguish from edible mushrooms.
Poisoning has been reported after the accidental consumption of wild Entoloma mushrooms.
Mycologists identified the specimen as belonging to Entoloma based on its pink spores and lack of a volva.

Etymology

From Greek ento- (within) and loma (fringe), referring to the inward-curved margin of the mushroom cap.
