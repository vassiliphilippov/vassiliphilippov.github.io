astrolabe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstrəˌleɪb / (AmE), / ˈastrəˌleɪb / (BrE)
a historical instrument used to make astronomical measurements, typically of the altitudes of celestial bodies and for navigation
historical astronomical navigation instrument

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient astrolabe once used by early navigators.
Mariners relied on the astrolabe to determine their position at sea before modern technology.
He studied the stars with an astrolabe while learning about historical astronomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sextant - an instrument used to measure the angle between two visible objects, typically in navigation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin astrolabium, from Greek astrolabion, meaning 'star-taker' (astron 'star' + lambanein 'to take').
