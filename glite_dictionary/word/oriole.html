oriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
a brightly colored American songbird, typically with black and orange or yellow plumage in the male
American songbird with bright plumage

Examples

A bright oriole perched on the branch, its orange feathers gleaming in the sunlight.
Every spring, we see a male oriole building a nest in our backyard.
The Baltimore oriole is known for its striking coloration and distinctive song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a type of European songbird with black plumage

Etymology

From New Latin *oriolus*, from Medieval Latin, from Latin *aureolus* (golden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
A mainly tropical songbird, typically with the male having bright orange and black plumage.
tropical songbird with orange and black male

Examples

The male oriole is easily recognized by its striking orange and black feathers.
In spring, orioles migrate north to their breeding grounds.
We hung fruit from the tree to attract an oriole to our garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a common Eurasian and North American songbird

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *oriolus*, perhaps imitative of its cry.
