Leucadendron argenteum

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːkəˈdɛndrən ɑːrˈdʒɛntiəm / (AmE), / ˌluːkəˈdɛndrən ɑːˈdʒɛntiəm / (BrE)
a small South African tree with long, silvery, silky leaves, known as the silver tree
a South African tree with silvery leaves

Examples

The Leucadendron argenteum is valued for the striking appearance of its shiny foliage.
Threats to Leucadendron argenteum in the wild include habitat destruction and invasive species.
On their visit to Cape Town, the group admired a grove of Leucadendron argenteum in bloom.

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'dendron' (tree); 'argenteum' is Latin for silver.
