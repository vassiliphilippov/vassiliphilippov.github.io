Harry Truman

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæri ˈtruːmən / (AmE), / ˈhæri ˈtruːmən / (BrE)
(1884–1972) the 33rd President of the United States, serving from 1945 to 1953, who succeeded Franklin D. Roosevelt and authorized the use of atomic bombs on Japan.
33rd U.S. President (1945–1953)

Examples

Harry Truman became president in 1945 after Roosevelt's death.
During his presidency, Harry Truman made several significant foreign policy decisions.
Many historians debate the legacy of Harry Truman in both domestic and international affairs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States, predecessor to Truman
  • Degree Higher: Dwight D. Eisenhower - 34th President of the United States, successor to Truman
