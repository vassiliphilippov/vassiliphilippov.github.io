Sapotaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈteɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees and shrubs that exude milky sap and often produce edible fleshy fruit, including species such as sapodilla and star apple
family of tropical milky-sapped trees and shrubs

Examples

The Sapotaceae family includes several trees that are valued for their delicious fruits and durable timber.
Many members of the Sapotaceae produce edible fruits like the sapodilla and star apple.
Botanists have discovered new species within the Sapotaceae in tropical rainforests.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Sapota (a genus) + -aceae, standard suffix for plant family names in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsæpəˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees or shrubs, often with milky sap and edible fleshy fruit
family of tropical trees or shrubs

Examples

The Sapotaceae are known for producing fruits like sapodilla that are enjoyed in many tropical regions.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Sapotaceae on their expedition.
Members of the Sapotaceae have latex-producing bark and are found throughout the tropics.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Sapota, the type genus, and the standard botanical suffix -aceae.
