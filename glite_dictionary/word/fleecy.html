fleecy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfliːsi / (AmE), / ˈfliːsi / (BrE)
(of a fabric or material) having a soft, fluffy surface like fleece, often produced by brushing.
having a soft, fluffy nap like fleece

Examples

The fleecy blanket kept her warm on the chilly night.
He wore a jacket with a fleecy lining for extra comfort.
The baby’s toy had a fleecy texture that was gentle against her skin.

Synset Examples

a dress of brushed cotton
a fleecy lining
napped fabrics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napped - (of fabric) having a raised, fuzzy surface
  • Synonym: brushed - (of fabric) having a texture produced by brushing to raise fibers

Etymology

From 'fleece' (the wool covering a sheep) + -y, forming adjectives meaning 'like or of the nature of.'
