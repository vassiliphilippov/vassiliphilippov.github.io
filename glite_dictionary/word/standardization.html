standardization

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændəɹdɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstændədəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of establishing or imposing standards or regulations to ensure uniformity or consistency
enforcing or creating standards

Examples

The government announced new standardization measures for food safety labels.
International standardization helps ensure products can be used worldwide without modification.
The standardization of test procedures improved the reliability of the results.

Synset Examples

a committee was appointed to recommend terminological standardization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • Antonym: customization - modifying something to suit a particular individual or task

Etymology

From standard + -ization, modeled after French standardisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstændərdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstændədaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or result of making something conform to a standard; the condition in which a standard has been successfully established and applied.
process of making things conform to a standard

Examples

The standardization of procedures helps ensure consistent quality across all factories.
International standardization is crucial for the compatibility of electronic devices.
Thanks to standardization, replacement parts can be sourced from various suppliers without issue.

Synset Examples

standardization of nuts and bolts had saved industry millions of dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality or state of being uniform
  • Antonym: diversification - the process of making or becoming more diverse or varied

Etymology

From standard + -ization; the suffix -ization from French -isation, based on Latin -izare.
