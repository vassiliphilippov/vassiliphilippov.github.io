muzhik

1. NOUN

/ muːˈʒiːk / (AmE), / muːˈʒiːk / (BrE)
(in Russian history) a peasant or agricultural laborer, especially one prior to the Russian Revolution of 1917.
Russian peasant, especially before 1917

Examples

The muzhik worked tirelessly in the fields to support his family.
Before the Revolution, a typical Russian muzhik had little land of his own.
The novel describes the harsh life of a muzhik in 19th-century Russia.

Etymology

From Russian мужик (muzhík), meaning 'peasant' or 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ muːˈʒiːk / (AmE), / mʊˈʒiːk / (BrE)
A Russian peasant, especially one from before the Russian Revolution of 1917.
Russian peasant (pre-1917)

Examples

The novel paints a vivid picture of the daily struggles faced by the muzhik in Tsarist Russia.
After the revolution, many muzhiks gained access to land they previously worked as serfs.
He tells stories about his grandfather, who was once a poor muzhik living in a rural Russian village.

Etymology

From Russian мужи́к (muzhík), meaning 'peasant man'.
