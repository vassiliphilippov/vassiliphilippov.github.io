incoordination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkoʊˌɔrdɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A lack of coordination in bodily movements, especially resulting in clumsiness or inability to perform tasks smoothly.
lack of coordination in movements
medical

Examples

The patient displayed noticeable incoordination when attempting to walk across the room.
Alcohol consumption can temporarily cause incoordination and slurred speech.
Symptoms such as incoordination and dizziness often indicate a problem with the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coordination - the ability to use different parts of the body together smoothly and efficiently

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + coordination.
