Chamaemelum

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːmɪləm / (AmE), / kəˈmiːmɪləm / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as chamomile.
genus of chamomile plants

Examples

The species Chamaemelum nobile is widely known as Roman chamomile.
Botanists often debate whether Chamaemelum should be included within Anthemis.
Herbal teas sometimes contain dried flowers of the Chamaemelum genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anthemis - a related genus of plants sometimes including chamomile

Etymology

From Greek khamaimēlon, meaning 'earth apple'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːmələm / (AmE), / kəˈmiːmɪləm / (BrE)
a small genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as chamomile, sometimes included in the genus Anthemis.
genus of plants; chamomile

Examples

The species in the genus Chamaemelum are well known for their use in herbal teas.
Botanists sometimes debate whether to place these plants under Chamaemelum or Anthemis.
Gardeners often grow Chamaemelum for its attractive flowers and pleasant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anthemis - a related plant genus in the daisy family

Etymology

From Greek *chamaimēlon* ('earth apple'), referring to the scent of chamomile flowers.
