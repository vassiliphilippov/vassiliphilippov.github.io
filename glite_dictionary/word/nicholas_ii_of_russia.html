Nicholas II of Russia

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪkələs ðə ˌsɛkənd əv ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈnɪkələs ðə ˌsɛkənd ɒv ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
the last emperor of Russia, forced to abdicate in 1917 during the Russian Revolution and executed with his family in 1918.
last czar of Russia (1868–1918)

Examples

Nicholas II of Russia was the last czar, abdicating the throne after the February Revolution of 1917.
Historians often debate whether Nicholas II of Russia could have prevented the Russian Revolution.
The tragic fate of Nicholas II of Russia and his family is remembered as the end of the Romanov dynasty.
