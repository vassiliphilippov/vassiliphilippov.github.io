midships

1. ADV

/ ˈmɪdˌʃɪps / (AmE), / ˈmɪdʃɪps / (BrE)
in or toward the middle part of a ship; near the center of a vessel.
in or toward the middle of a ship

Examples

The cargo was loaded midships to maintain balance during the voyage.
The crew gathered midships for the safety drill.
To avoid rougher motion, passengers were advised to stay midships during the storm.

Synset Examples

in the late 19th century, engines were placed in front, amidships, and at the rear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amidships - in or toward the middle part of a ship; near the center of a vessel

Etymology

From 'amidships', altered from 'amid' + 'ship(s)'.
