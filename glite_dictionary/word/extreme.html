extreme

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriːm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːm / (BrE)
the point or part that is farthest from the center or middle of something
point farthest from the center

Examples

The poles are at the extremes of the Earth’s axis.
She placed the markers at the extremes of the measuring tape.
Opinions at the extremes are often the least common.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: center - the middle point of something

Etymology

From Latin extremus (outermost, utmost), superlative of exterus (on the outside).
