geothermic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒi.oʊˈθɜːr.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈθɜː.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from the heat produced inside the Earth's crust.
relating to Earth's internal heat

Examples

The team studied geothermic energy sources to find sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels.
Iceland uses its geothermic activity to heat homes and provide electricity.
Certain regions are known for their geothermic geysers and hot springs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geothermal - relating to or produced by the internal heat of the Earth

Etymology

From geo- (Earth) + thermic (relating to heat), modeled after Greek roots.
