libertarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbərˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈteəriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in maximizing individual liberty in thought, speech, and action, with minimal interference from authority or government.
belief in individual freedom and minimal government

Examples

Many people are drawn to libertarianism because they value personal responsibility and freedom.
Debates about economic policy often reference libertarianism as an alternative to more government control.
His political views shifted toward libertarianism after he began questioning state intervention.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liberalism - political philosophy advocating liberty and equality
  • Antonym: authoritarianism - political system favoring strict obedience to authority

Etymology

Formed from 'libertarian' + '-ism'; 'libertarian' from 'liberty'.
