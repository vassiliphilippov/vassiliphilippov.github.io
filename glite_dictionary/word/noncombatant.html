noncombatant

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.kəmˈbæt.ənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kəmˈbæt.ənt / (BrE)
a member of the armed forces who does not take part in fighting, such as a chaplain, medic, or other personnel
armed forces member not engaged in combat

Examples

During the battle, the noncombatant medical staff worked tirelessly to treat wounded soldiers.
International law protects noncombatant members of the military, such as chaplains and medics, from being targeted.
The army provided special badges to identify noncombatant personnel on the field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combatant - a person actively engaged in fighting, especially in war

Etymology

From non- (not) + combatant (one who fights), based on French combattant.

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.kəmˈbæt.ənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kəmˈbæt.ənt / (BrE)
(of a person) not engaged in fighting, especially during a war; used to refer to civilians or others not involved in military combat.
not engaged in fighting; civilian in war

Examples

During the evacuation, all noncombatant personnel were given priority.
The Red Cross vehicles transported noncombatant refugees to safety.
Under international law, targeting noncombatant populations is considered a war crime.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combatant - a person actively engaged in fighting, especially in war
  • Synonym: civilian - a person not in the armed services or police force

Etymology

From non- (not) + combatant (one engaged in fighting), after French non-combattant.

3. NOUN

/ nɑnˈkɑːbətənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒmbətənt / (BrE)
a member of the armed forces whose duties do not involve fighting, such as a chaplain or medical officer
armed forces member not engaged in fighting

Examples

All noncombatant personnel were quickly evacuated from the danger zone.
During the war, the surgeon served as a noncombatant, focusing on treating the wounded.
International law protects noncombatant members of the military, such as medics and chaplains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combatant - a person who takes part in fighting or combat

Etymology

From Latin 'non' (not) + 'combatant' (one who fights), from 'combattere' (to fight).
