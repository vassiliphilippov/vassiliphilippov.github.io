police

1. VERB

/ pəˈlis / (AmE), / pəˈliːs / (BrE)
to maintain order, safety, or security in an area by regularly patrolling it, especially as a member of a police force
to patrol to maintain order or security

Examples

The security team polices the neighborhood every night to prevent crime.
National parks are policed to protect wildlife from illegal hunters.
During the festival, extra officers were assigned to police the downtown area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrol - to go around an area at regular intervals to check for trouble or ensure safety

Etymology

From French *policer* (to govern, administer), from *police* (public order), from Latin *politia* (civil administration).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlis / (AmE), / pəˈliːs / (BrE)
the official organization whose job is to enforce laws, prevent crime, and maintain public order
the law enforcement organization

Examples

The police arrived at the scene within minutes of the accident.
She called the police to report a suspicious package outside her house.
The police are investigating the cause of the fire.

Synset Examples

the law came looking for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law enforcement - the activity of ensuring obedience to laws, often by police
  • Antonym: criminal - a person who breaks the law, the opposite group to police

Etymology

From French 'police' (public order), from Latin 'politia' (civil administration)

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
