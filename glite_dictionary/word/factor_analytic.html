factor analytic

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæktɚ æn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈfaktə ˌan.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by factor analysis, a statistical method used to describe variability among observed variables in terms of fewer unobserved variables.
relating to factor analysis

Examples

The researchers conducted a factor analytic study to identify the underlying dimensions of personality traits.
Her factor analytic approach provided new insights into the structure of motivation.
Several factor analytic models were tested to compare their fit with the observed data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor analysis - a statistical method for examining relationships among variables

Etymology

From 'factor analysis', a statistical method introduced in early 20th century psychology, plus the adjectival suffix '-ic'.
