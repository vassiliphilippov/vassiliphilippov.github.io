sneak

1. NOUN

/ snik / (AmE), / sniːk / (BrE)
a person who informs on others or acts as a decoy, especially for the police.
person who acts as an informer or decoy

Examples

Nobody trusted him after he was caught being a sneak for the police.
The other kids avoided her, calling her a sneak whenever she told on them.
He felt betrayed when he realized his best friend was actually a sneak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informer - a person who gives information to the authorities, especially in secret
  • Synonym: snitch - an informant, especially one who betrays others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10110781-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ sniːk / (AmE), / sniːk / (BrE)
a person who moves about stealthily, often with the intention of doing something wrong or unlawful
a person who moves stealthily

Examples

The neighbors reported a sneak lurking around the backyards late at night.
The police caught the sneak before he could break into the house.
He acted like a sneak, always trying to avoid being noticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prowler - someone who moves stealthily around a place, often with criminal intent
  • Synonym: burglar - a person who breaks into buildings to commit theft

Etymology

Probably from Old English *snīcan* (to creep, crawl).

/ sniːk / (AmE), / sniːk / (BrE)

steal or take secretly
