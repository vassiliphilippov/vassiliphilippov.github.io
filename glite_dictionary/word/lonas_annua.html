Lonas annua

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊnəs ˈæn.ju.ə / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.nəs ˈæn.ju.ə / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southwestern Mediterranean region, known for its yellow daisylike flowers.
Mediterranean shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

Lonas annua blooms in late spring, adding a splash of yellow to Mediterranean hillsides.
Gardeners appreciate Lonas annua for its drought tolerance and cheerful appearance.
The aromatic leaves and bright flowers make Lonas annua a distinctive choice for native plant gardens.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Lonas' and specific epithet 'annua' meaning 'annual'.
