backwoodsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌwʊdz.mən / (AmE), / ˈbækˌwʊdz.mən / (BrE)
a man who lives in or comes from a sparsely inhabited, forested, or frontier region, often one with little contact with urban society
a man from a remote forested area

Examples

The rugged backwoodsman built his own cabin deep in the pine forest.
She admired the independence of the local backwoodsmen who lived off the land.
Tales about the legendary backwoodsman were passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frontiersman - a person who lives on the frontier, especially in the American West

Etymology

From 'backwoods' (remote forested areas) + 'man', in common use since 18th century North America.
