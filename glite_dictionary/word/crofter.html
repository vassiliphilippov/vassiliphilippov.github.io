crofter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔf.tər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒf.tə / (BrE)
a person who owns or rents and works a small farm, especially in Scotland or northern England
a small-scale farmer in Britain
british

Examples

The Scottish crofter worked tirelessly on his land throughout the seasons.
Many crofters in the Highlands rely on sheep farming as their main source of income.
Her grandfather was a crofter who cultivated barley and raised cattle in the remote valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - person who works or manages a farm

Etymology

From 'croft' (a small enclosed field), with agent suffix '-er'; especially associated with Scottish usage.
