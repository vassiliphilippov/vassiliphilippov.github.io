flasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃər / (AmE), / ˈflæʃə / (BrE)
a person, usually a man, who exposes their genitals in public, often for sexual excitement
person who exposes genitals in public

Examples

The police arrested the flasher after multiple complaints from people in the park.
Children were startled when a flasher exposed himself near the playground.
A notorious flasher was finally identified by surveillance cameras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhibitionist - a person who behaves in an extravagant way to attract attention, especially sexually by exposing the body

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃər / (AmE), / ˈflɑːʃə / (BrE)
a light that repeatedly turns on and off, used as a signal or for sending messages
a signaling light that flashes

Examples

When the car broke down, she turned on the flasher to warn other drivers.
Ambulances are equipped with a flasher that signals when they're responding to emergencies.
The security system uses a red flasher to alert guards to a breach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blinker - a light that flashes, used as a signal, especially on vehicles
  • Synonym: indicator - a signal light on a vehicle that flashes to show turning intention
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02855432-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈflæʃə / (BrE)
an electrical device that automatically turns a lamp on and off, often used in advertising displays or signal lights
device for turning lights on and off automatically

Examples

The neon flasher in the store window caught everyone's attention.
Electricians replaced the broken flasher in the traffic signal.
A malfunction in the flasher caused the sign to stay lit instead of blinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blinker - a device that flashes lights on and off, especially in automobiles

Etymology

From English 'flash' (to emit a beam of light) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
