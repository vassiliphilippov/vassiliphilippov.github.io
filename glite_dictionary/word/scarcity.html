scarcity

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛr.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈskɛː.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being in short supply; an insufficient amount of something needed or desired
insufficient supply; shortage

Examples

The scarcity of clean water is a growing concern in many regions.
During the war, there was a scarcity of basic necessities like food and medicine.
A sudden scarcity of skilled workers has slowed down the construction project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plentifulness
  • Synonym: shortage - a state in which there is not enough of something needed

Etymology

From Old French 'escars' (scanty), from Latin 'excarpsus', past participle of 'excarpere' (to pluck out).
