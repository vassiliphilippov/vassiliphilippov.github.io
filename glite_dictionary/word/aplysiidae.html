Aplysiidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌplaɪziˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / æˌplɪzɪˈɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of large marine gastropod molluscs known as sea hares, characterized by a soft body and winglike extensions
family of marine gastropods called sea hares

Examples

The Aplysiidae family includes several species that are studied for their unique nervous systems.
Many marine biologists have researched the chemical defenses used by Aplysiidae to deter predators.
A common inhabitant of shallow coastal waters, Aplysiidae species are often identified by their large, soft bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea hare - a type of large marine mollusc in the family Aplysiidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Aplysia + family suffix -idae.
