animation

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making animated cartoons by photographing a series of drawings, computer graphics, or objects to create the illusion of movement
process of making animated cartoons

Examples

He studied animation at art school to pursue a career in filmmaking.
The movie industry has seen tremendous growth in computer-generated animation.
She dreams of working at a studio known for its classic animation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartoon - a film or television show made using animation

Etymology

From Latin animatio (a bringing to life), from animare (to give life).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
liveliness or energy in manner or movement; the quality of being active, spirited, or full of life.
liveliness; spirited quality

Examples

She spoke with great animation, captivating everyone in the room.
There was a noticeable animation in the crowd when the band started playing.
His face lit up with animation as he described his new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liveliness - quality of being lively or energetic
  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energetic activity
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'animatio', from 'animare' meaning 'to give life' or 'to animate'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving life, energy, or liveliness to something; the quality of being full of life or vigor
the act of enlivening or energizing

Examples

Her speech filled the room with animation and inspired everyone present.
The animation of the festival was evident in every colorful performance.
With animation in his voice, he described his adventures to his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being lively or animated
  • Antonym: lifelessness - the quality of lacking energy or vitality

Etymology

From Latin 'animatio' (a bringing to life), from 'animare' (to give life), from 'anima' (soul, spirit).
