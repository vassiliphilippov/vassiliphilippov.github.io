Chimaera monstrosa

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪərə mɒnˈstroʊsə / (AmE), / kaɪˈmɪərə mɒnˈstrəʊsə / (BrE)
A large species of cartilaginous fish found in European waters, commonly known as the rabbitfish or large-eyed chimaera.
a large European chimaera species (fish)

Examples

The Chimaera monstrosa is often found at depths below 200 meters in the Atlantic Ocean.
Marine biologists recently observed a Chimaera monstrosa near the coast of Portugal.
The unique appearance of the Chimaera monstrosa has earned it the nickname ‘rabbitfish’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbitfish - common name for Chimaera monstrosa

Etymology

From Latin 'chimaera' (chimera) and 'monstrosa' (monstrous), referring to its unusual appearance.
