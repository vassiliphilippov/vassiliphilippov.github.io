nationalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports their own country's interests and believes in the importance of national independence or strong national government
person advocating national independence or strong government

Examples

He described himself as a proud nationalist who always put his country's interests first.
During the debate, she was labeled a nationalist for her strong stance on border control.
The uprising was led by a group of young nationalists demanding independence from colonial rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriot - a person who loves and supports their country
  • Antonym: internationalist - a person who supports cooperation among nations

Etymology

From nation + -alist, patterned after socialist, first attested in the late 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnæʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlɪst / (BrE)
showing strong support for one's own nation and putting its interests above those of others
strongly supportive of one's own nation

Examples

Her nationalist views were evident in her speeches about economic independence.
The party adopted a nationalist stance, prioritizing domestic industries over foreign competition.
The movement grew out of nationalist sentiments following years of foreign rule.

Synset Examples

nationalist aspirations
minor nationalistic differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and vigorous support for one's country
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - including or containing people from many different countries; not nationalistic

Etymology

From nation + -alist, influenced by nationalism (from French nationalisme, from nation).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports their own country and is prepared to defend its interests, sometimes to the exclusion or detriment of other nations
a strong supporter of their nation

Examples

He was known as a fierce nationalist who fought for his country's independence.
Some critics argue that nationalist movements can lead to exclusionary policies.
The party appealed to the voters' nationalist sentiments during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriot - a person who loves their country and supports its interests
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - a person who considers themselves a member of all countries and rejects narrow national loyalties

Etymology

From 'national' + '-ist', forming agent nouns indicating a person who supports a particular doctrine or system.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10427055-n
Synset POS: n
