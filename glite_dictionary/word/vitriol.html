vitriol

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtriˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪɒl / (BrE)
bitter and abusive language; harsh or venomous criticism or feeling of deep-seated ill will.
bitter, abusive language

Examples

Her comments were full of vitriol and left the audience stunned.
Political debates sometimes devolve into exchanges of vitriol rather than reasoned arguments.
He was unprepared for the wave of vitriol directed at him on social media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invective - insulting, abusive, or highly critical language
  • Antonym: adulation - excessive praise or flattery

Etymology

From Latin 'vitriolum' (sulphuric acid), originally referring to a sulfate; figurative use for caustic speech dates from the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪtriˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪɒl / (BrE)
to injure, burn, or attack with sulfuric acid or another strong acid, particularly as an act of violence
to attack or injure with acid
archaic legal

Examples

The criminal threatened to vitriol his rival unless he left the area immediately.
She described in court how her attacker attempted to vitriol her, leaving her scarred for life.
In the nineteenth century, several cases were reported where envious individuals would vitriol their opponents out of revenge.

Etymology

From Latin *vitriolum* (sulphate of iron), from *vitrum* (glass), referring to the glassy appearance of some vitriol salts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtriˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪɒl / (BrE)
A highly corrosive acid, specifically sulfuric acid, historically known as oil of vitriol; widely used in industry and manufacturing.
sulfuric acid; a strong corrosive acid

Examples

The chemical plant stores large quantities of vitriol for industrial use.
He accidentally spilled vitriol on his glove, causing it to corrode rapidly.
Historically, vitriol was used in alchemy and early chemistry experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfuric acid - a strong mineral acid with the chemical formula H2SO4

Etymology

From Latin 'vitriolum', from 'vitrum' meaning 'glass', named for the glassy appearance of sulfate salts.
