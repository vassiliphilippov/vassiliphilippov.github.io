polytechnic

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈtɛknɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtɛknɪk / (BrE)
an educational institution that provides instruction in a variety of technical and industrial subjects, especially engineering and applied sciences
technical college or school

Examples

He studied electrical engineering at the local polytechnic.
Many polytechnics have been upgraded to university status in recent years.
The polytechnic offers courses in applied sciences and mechanical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technical college - a college that specializes in courses in science and technology
  • Degree Higher: university - an institution for higher education and research

Etymology

From French 'polytechnique', from Greek 'polutekhnikos' (skilled in many arts).
