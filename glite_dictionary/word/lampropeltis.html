Lampropeltis

1. NOUN

/ læmˈprɑːpɛltɪs / (AmE), / lamˈprɒpɛltɪs / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous snakes commonly known as king snakes and milk snakes, native to the Americas.
genus of king and milk snakes
medical

Examples

Lampropeltis species are often kept as pets because of their striking coloration and docile nature.
The Lampropeltis genus includes the well-known eastern kingsnake.
Research on Lampropeltis has contributed to our understanding of snake mimicry and adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king snake - a nonvenomous snake of the genus Lampropeltis
  • Synonym: milk snake - a nonvenomous snake species of the genus Lampropeltis

Etymology

From Greek 'lampros' (bright, shining) and 'pelte' (small shield); refers to the snakes' shiny scales.

2. NOUN

/ læmˈprɑːpɛltɪs / (AmE), / lamˈprɒpɛltɪs / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous snakes including the king snakes and milk snakes, native to the Americas.
genus of king and milk snakes

Examples

The genus Lampropeltis includes several colorful king snake species.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the genus Lampropeltis.
Members of Lampropeltis are known for their immunity to other snakes' venom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king snake - a common name for several species in the genus Lampropeltis
  • Synonym: milk snake - a type of snake in the genus Lampropeltis, known for its color patterns

Etymology

From Greek 'lampros' (bright) and 'peltis' (small shield), referring to the shiny scales.
