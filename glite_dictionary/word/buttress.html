buttress

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtrəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrɪs / (BrE)
a structure built against a wall to support or strengthen it, usually made of stone or brick
wall support structure

Examples

The cathedral's massive buttress helps prevent the walls from collapsing outward.
Rainwater ran down the old brick buttress at the side of the building.
Medieval architects often designed churches with flying buttresses for additional support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that bears the weight of another thing or keeps it upright

Etymology

From Old French *bouterez*, related to *bouter* (to thrust against).

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌtrəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrəs / (BrE)
to support, strengthen, or reinforce something, especially an argument or position
support or strengthen

Examples

The lawyer sought to buttress his case with additional evidence.
She buttressed her argument by citing several scientific studies.
The professor encouraged students to buttress their essays with solid facts.

Synset Examples

buttress your thesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reinforce - to strengthen or make more forceful
  • Antonym: to undermine - to weaken or damage, especially gradually or insidiously

Etymology

From Old French 'boterace' (a support), relating to 'bouter' (to strike, push).

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌtrəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrəs / (BrE)
to support or reinforce a structure with a projecting support built against its wall
support or reinforce with a buttress

Examples

Massive stone supports were added to buttress the cathedral walls.
Engineers decided to buttress the bridge to prevent collapse during storms.
They had to buttress the old library to ensure its structural safety.

Synset Examples

Buttress the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear the weight of or uphold
  • Antonym: to undermine - to weaken or damage, often gradually

Etymology

From Old French *boteres*, from *bouter* (to strike, butt).
