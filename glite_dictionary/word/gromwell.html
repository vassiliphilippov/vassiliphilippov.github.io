gromwell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑmˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒm.wəl / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with branching stems, typically found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
European hedgerow plant

Examples

We spotted a patch of gromwell growing along the woodland path.
The gromwell is known for its tough, wiry stems and small white flowers.
In spring, gromwell often appears in hedgerows across the countryside.

Etymology

From Middle English gromwel, from Old French gronwel, probably of Germanic origin.
