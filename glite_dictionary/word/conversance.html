conversance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɜrsəns / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːsəns / (BrE)
knowledge or familiarity with a particular subject or situation; acquaintance or experience with something.
knowledge or familiarity with something

Examples

His conversance with classical literature impressed the committee.
She has a broad conversance with European history, especially the Renaissance period.
Lack of conversance with the software delayed the team's progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarity - close acquaintance with or knowledge of something
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From French *connaissance* (knowledge), from Old French *conoissance*, based on Latin *cognoscere* (to get to know).
