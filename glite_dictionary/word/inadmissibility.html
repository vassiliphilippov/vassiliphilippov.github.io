inadmissibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ædˌmɪs.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˌmɪs.ɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being allowed or acceptable, especially with reference to court evidence or legal proceedings
state of being not admissible
legal

Examples

The judge ruled the inadmissibility of the evidence due to improper handling by the police.
Her application for a visa was denied on grounds of inadmissibility to the country.
Lawyers debated the inadmissibility of hearsay testimony during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: admissibility - the quality of being allowed or acceptable, especially in legal proceedings

Etymology

From in- (not) + admissibility, based on admissible (from Latin admittĕre, to allow) + -ity.
