hostility

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈstɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / hɒˈstɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or feelings toward someone or something
unfriendliness; antagonistic attitude

Examples

Despite the warm welcome from most people, she sensed clear hostility from her rival.
The negotiations broke down amid an atmosphere of mutual hostility.
His sudden display of hostility surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

he could not conceal his hostility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antagonism - active opposition or hostility toward someone
  • Antonym: friendliness - kind or amicable disposition towards others

Etymology

From Latin 'hostilitas', from 'hostilis' (hostile).

2. NOUN

/ hɑˈstɪləti / (AmE), / hɒˈstɪləti / (BrE)
intense unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or feelings; deep-seated ill-will
deep-seated ill-will or antagonism

Examples

There was a sense of hostility between the two rival teams.
She tried to hide her hostility toward her new coworker.
Negotiations broke down due to growing hostility on both sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antagonism - active opposition or hostility
  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly, absence of hostility
  • Synonym: enmity - the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Latin hostilitas (enmity), from hostilis (of an enemy).

3. NOUN

/ hɑːˈstɪləti / (AmE), / hɒˈstɪləti / (BrE)
acts of violence or aggression, especially those that are hostile and often unprovoked.
violent or aggressive acts

Examples

The two countries ended decades of hostility with a peace treaty.
After years of hostility, the neighbors finally made amends.
The soldiers prepared for the possibility of renewed hostility along the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior towards others
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; absence of war or conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'hostilitas', from 'hostilis' (of an enemy).

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
