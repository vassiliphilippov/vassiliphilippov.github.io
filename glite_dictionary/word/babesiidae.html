Babesiidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌbiːziˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌbiːziˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of protozoan parasites that infect the red blood cells of animals, including cattle, and can cause disease
family of protozoan animal parasites
medical

Examples

Veterinarians are concerned about the spread of Babesiidae in local cattle herds.
Infection by members of the Babesiidae family can lead to serious health problems in livestock.
Researchers are developing new vaccines to protect animals from diseases caused by Babesiidae parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babesia - a genus of protozoan parasites causing babesiosis

Etymology

From genus name Babesia + New Latin scientific suffix -idae (denoting a family in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌbiːsiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌbiːsiˈiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of protozoan parasites (piroplasms), some of which cause disease in cattle and other animals
a family of protozoan parasites affecting animals
medical

Examples

The Babesiidae family includes several species that infect red blood cells in mammals.
Veterinarians study Babesiidae to better understand tick-borne diseases in livestock.
New treatments are being developed to combat infections caused by parasites in the Babesiidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babesia - a genus of protozoan parasites in the family Babesiidae

Etymology

From Babesia (the type genus) + Latin -idae (family suffix in taxonomy).
