Herpestes ichneumon

1. NOUN

/ hɜrˈpɛstiz ɪkˈnuːmən / (AmE), / hɜːˈpɛstiːz ɪkˈnjuːmɒn / (BrE)
a species of mongoose native to northern Africa, known for its slender build and in ancient times believed to eat crocodile eggs
African mongoose species

Examples

The Herpestes ichneumon is found throughout northern Africa and the Mediterranean basin.
Ancient Egyptians believed the Herpestes ichneumon protected them by eating crocodile eggs.
Researchers observed that the Herpestes ichneumon is adaptable to various habitats, including wetlands and forests.

Etymology

From Latin and Greek: Latin 'herpestes', Greek 'ichneumon' meaning 'tracker.'
