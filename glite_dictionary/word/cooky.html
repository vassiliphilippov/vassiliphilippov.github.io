cooky

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
a cook, especially one employed at a ranch or camp
camp or ranch cook
informal american

Examples

The cooky rang the bell to call everyone in for breakfast at dawn.
When the cooky quit, the cowboys had to cook for themselves until a replacement arrived.
Tom worked as a cooky on a ranch every summer during college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cook - someone who prepares food

Etymology

Diminutive from 'cook', likely influenced by regional American slang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
A small, flat, sweet baked cake, usually containing flour, sugar, and fat; an alternative spelling of 'cookie'.
small flat sweet cake; alternative spelling of 'cookie'

Examples

My grandmother always bakes delicious chocolate chip cooky during the holidays.
Would you like a cooky with your tea?
He reached for another cooky from the jar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookie - small, flat, sweet baked cake
  • Synonym: biscuit - British: a small, flat, sweet baked cake

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'cookie', from Dutch 'koekje' (little cake).
