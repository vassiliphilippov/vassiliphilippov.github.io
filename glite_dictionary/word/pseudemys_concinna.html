Pseudemys concinna

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdiːmɪs kɒnˈsɪnə / (AmE), / sjuːˈdiːmɪs kɒnˈsɪnə / (BrE)
A large species of river turtle found in the southern United States and northern Mexico, commonly known as the river cooter.
species of large river turtle

Examples

The Pseudemys concinna can often be seen basking on logs along southern U.S. rivers.
Researchers are studying the habitats of Pseudemys concinna to promote conservation efforts.
Unlike other turtles, Pseudemys concinna prefers slow-moving streams and rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: river cooter - common name for Pseudemys concinna, a large river turtle

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false) and 'emys' (freshwater turtle); species name from Latin 'concinna' (neat, elegant).
