Baptisia australis

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪʃiə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪʃɪə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America, commonly known as wild indigo, with blue, pea-like flowers in racemes.
a plant with blue racemes, wild indigo

Examples

The brilliant blue flowers of Baptisia australis make it a favorite in ornamental gardens.
Many pollinators, including bees, are attracted to Baptisia australis during its blooming season.
Gardeners appreciate how hardy Baptisia australis is, even in poor soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild indigo - another common name for Baptisia australis

Etymology

From Latin: Baptisia from Greek 'bapto' (to dip), australis meaning 'southern'.
