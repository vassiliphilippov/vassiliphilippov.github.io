undaunted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdɔntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈdɔːntɪd / (BrE)
not discouraged or disheartened; remaining determined and confident, especially in the face of difficulty or danger.
not discouraged or disheartened

Examples

She remained undaunted by the many obstacles in her path.
The hikers pressed on, undaunted by the worsening weather.
Despite several setbacks, his undaunted spirit inspired everyone around him.

Synset Examples

wholly undismayed by the commercial failure of the three movies he had made

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daunted - discouraged or intimidated
  • Synonym: fearless - lacking fear; brave

Etymology

From Latin 'undauntare', from 'un-' (not) + 'daunt' (to intimidate, from Old French 'danter').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdɔntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈdɔːntɪd / (BrE)
not intimidated or discouraged by difficulty, danger, or disappointment; resolutely courageous.
not discouraged; resolutely courageous

Examples

She remained undaunted by the harsh criticism and continued her research.
The team was undaunted by the early setbacks and played on with determination.
Despite the storm, the hikers pressed on, undaunted by the worsening weather.

Synset Examples

undaunted in the face of death

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daunted - intimidated or discouraged

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'daunt' (from Old French 'danter', from Latin 'domitare', to tame).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
