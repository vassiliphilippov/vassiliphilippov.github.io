exhilarating

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪləreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making you feel very happy, excited, and full of energy
making you feel very happy or excited

Examples

The roller coaster ride was an exhilarating experience for everyone.
She found hiking at dawn to be both peaceful and exhilarating.
Winning the championship was the most exhilarating moment of his career.

Synset Examples

the exhilarating effect of mountain air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing strong feelings of enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull

Etymology

From Latin exhilarare (to make cheerful), from ex- (thoroughly) + hilarare (to make cheerful).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪləreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making you feel very excited, happy, and full of energy
making you feel excited and energetic

Examples

The roller coaster ride was truly exhilarating, leaving everyone breathless with excitement.
She found hiking through the mountain trails to be an exhilarating experience.
Winning the championship provided an exhilarating rush of joy and energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; tedious

Etymology

From Latin exhilarare (to make cheerful), from ex- (thoroughly) + hilarare (to make cheerful), from hilarus (cheerful).

Synset POS: s
