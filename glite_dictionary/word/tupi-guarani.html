Tupi-Guarani

1. NOUN

/ tuˌpi ɡwɑːˈrɑːni / (AmE), / tuːˌpiː ɡwɑːˈrɑːni / (BrE)
a family of closely related indigenous languages spoken in South America, especially in Brazil and Paraguay
family of South American indigenous languages

Examples

Several tribes in Brazil speak languages from the Tupi-Guarani family.
Guarani, a principal language of Paraguay, belongs to the Tupi-Guarani group.
Linguists have extensively studied the Tupi-Guarani languages for their historical significance.

Etymology

From Tupi and Guarani, the names of two major languages in the family, both of indigenous South American origin.
