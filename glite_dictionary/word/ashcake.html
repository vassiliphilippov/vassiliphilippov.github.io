ashcake

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈæʃ.keɪk / (BrE)
A type of cornbread wrapped in cabbage leaves and baked in hot ashes, traditionally found in the southern United States.
cornbread baked in ashes (Southern US)
american

Examples

My grandmother taught me how to make ashcake over an open fire during camping trips.
Many pioneers relied on ashcake as a simple but filling food on the frontier.
The aroma of freshly baked ashcake brought back memories of family gatherings in the South.
