Syzygium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈzɪdʒiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees of the myrtle family, native to the East Indies but cultivated in various regions; includes several species grown for their edible fruit or ornamental value.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

The Syzygium tree produces clusters of edible, aromatic fruit known as rose apples.
Gardeners in tropical climates often plant Syzygium for its dense, glossy foliage and attractive flowers.
Several species of Syzygium are valued for their medicinal properties and culinary uses.

Etymology

From Greek 'syzygia', meaning 'conjunction' or 'pairing', in reference to the paired leaves or flowers.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈzɪdʒiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs of the myrtle family, native to the East Indies and cultivated in other regions.
genus of tropical evergreen trees/shrubs

Examples

The Syzygium genus includes the well-known clove tree as well as many ornamental species.
Fruit from some Syzygium species, such as the rose apple, are commonly eaten in tropical regions.
Botanists recently discovered a new Syzygium species in the rainforests of Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eugenia - closely related genus of the myrtle family

Etymology

From Greek syzygia (union, conjunction), referring to the paired leaves or fruits in some species.
