appreciative

1. ADJ

/ əˈpriʃətɪv / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃətɪv / (BrE)
feeling or showing that you are grateful for something
feeling or showing gratitude

Examples

She was very appreciative of their help during her illness.
The manager sent an appreciative email thanking the team for their hard work.
An appreciative audience applauded the performers warmly.

Synset Examples

was appreciative of his efforts
an appreciative word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks for something received

Etymology

From Latin 'appretiatus' (to value), via French 'apprécier'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈpriːʃətɪv / (AmE), / əˈpriːʃətɪv / (BrE)
feeling or showing gratitude or recognition of value; having a favourable opinion or critical judgment
feeling or showing appreciation

Examples

The audience gave an appreciative round of applause after the performance.
She smiled with an appreciative nod as she accepted the gift.
He wrote an appreciative letter to his mentor, thanking her for her support.

Synset Examples

appreciative of a beautiful landscape
an appreciative laugh from the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks for something received
  • Antonym: unappreciative - not showing appreciation or gratitude

Etymology

From Latin 'appretiatus' (valued), past participle of 'appretiare' (to appraise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00778235-s
Synset POS: s
