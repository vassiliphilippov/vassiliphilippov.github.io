saltwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a bushy plant found in Old World salt marshes and sea beaches, with prickly leaves; formerly burned to produce crude soda ash
Old World marsh plant with prickly leaves

Examples

The marsh was covered with saltwort, thriving in the salty soil by the shore.
Historically, people would burn saltwort to extract soda ash for glassmaking.
Botanists study various species of saltwort to understand their adaptation to saline environments.

Etymology

From 'salt' and 'wort' (an old word for plant or herb).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltwət / (BrE)
A low, strong-smelling coastal shrub of warm regions in the New World, with unisexual flowers in cone-like spikes and thick succulent leaves.
coastal shrub with succulent leaves

Examples

Several species of saltwort thrive along the saline coasts of the southern United States.
The thick, fleshy leaves of the saltwort store water to survive in harsh seaside conditions.
Botanists studied the saltwort to understand its adaptation to salty environments.

Etymology

From 'salt' + 'wort', with 'wort' from Old English 'wyrt' (plant, herb).
