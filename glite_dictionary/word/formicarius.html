Formicarius

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmɪkəriəs / (AmE), / fɔːˈmɪkəriəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Formicariidae family, comprising birds known as antthrushes native to Central and South America.
genus of antthrush birds

Examples

The genus Formicarius contains several species of ground-dwelling antthrushes.
Researchers identified a new member of the Formicarius genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Many species within Formicarius are known for their secretive behavior in dense forest habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' meaning 'ant', referring to the birds' ant-eating habits.

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmɪkəriəs / (AmE), / fɔːˈmɪkəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Formicariidae, commonly known as antthrushes, found in Central and South America.
genus of antthrush birds

Examples

Scientists have observed that members of the genus Formicarius primarily feed on ants and other insects.
Several species within Formicarius are native to the rainforests of South America.
The plumage of Formicarius birds helps them remain camouflaged on the forest floor.

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' meaning 'ant', referring to the birds' ant-eating habits.
