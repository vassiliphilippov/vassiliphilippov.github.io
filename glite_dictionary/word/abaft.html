abaft

1. ADV

/ əˈbæft / (AmE), / əˈbɑːft / (BrE)
towards or at the rear (stern) of a ship or tail of an aircraft
toward the rear of a ship/aircraft

Examples

The lifeboats are located abaft the main mast.
Passengers must not go abaft the safety barrier during takeoff.
The damaged section of the ship was abaft the engine room.

Synset Examples

stow the luggage aft
ships with square sails sail fairly efficiently with the wind abaft
the captain looked astern to see what the fuss was about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aft - towards the rear (stern) of a ship
  • Antonym: forward - toward the front (bow) of a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From Middle English 'abaft', from 'a-' (on, in) + 'baft' (behind).
