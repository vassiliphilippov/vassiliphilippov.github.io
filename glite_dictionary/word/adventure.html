adventure

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvɛntʃɚ / (AmE), / ədˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
an unusual, exciting, and sometimes hazardous experience or activity, especially one involving exploration or risk
exciting or risky experience or activity

Examples

Our trip through the jungle turned into a real adventure when our guide got lost.
She has always dreamed of embarking on an adventure around the world.
The movie is full of adventure from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - a bold or daring feat
  • Antonym: ordeal - a difficult or unpleasant experience

Etymology

From Old French aventure (chance, destiny), from Latin adventūra (a thing about to happen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00797976-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ədˈvɛntʃər / (AmE), / ədˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
to risk something with the hope of gaining a positive result, especially by taking a chance or trying something hazardous
to risk or undertake boldly
archaic

Examples

He was not afraid to adventure his fortune on the high seas in search of riches.
Many entrepreneurs adventure their savings in hopes of building a successful business.
She adventured into unfamiliar territory, trusting her instincts despite the risks.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - to expose to danger or loss
  • Synonym: gamble - to take risky action for possible gain

Etymology

From Old French aventure (chance, accident) and the verb aventurer (to risk, venture), from Latin adventura (things about to happen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550045-v
Synset POS: v
