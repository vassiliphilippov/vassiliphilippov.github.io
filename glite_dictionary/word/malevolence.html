malevolence

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛvələns / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələns / (BrE)
The state or condition of wishing harm or evil to others; ill will.
wishing harm or ill will to others

Examples

The villain's malevolence was evident in the cruel way he treated his rivals.
She could sense a feeling of malevolence in his glare across the room.
Stories of ghosts are often filled with malevolence and revenge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin 'malevolentia', from 'male' (badly) + 'velle' (to wish).

2. NOUN

/ məˈlɛvələns / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələns / (BrE)
the quality or state of having or showing a desire to cause harm to others; ill will or spitefulness
the desire to cause harm; ill will

Examples

She could sense the malevolence in his cold, unwavering stare.
Stories of witches often feature dark forests filled with malevolence and danger.
His voice was calm, but there was a hint of malevolence underneath his words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness
  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil

Etymology

From Latin malevolentia (ill will), from male (badly) + volentia (willing)
