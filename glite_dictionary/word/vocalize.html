to vocalize

1. VERB

/ ˈvoʊ.kəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to express or state something clearly, especially by speaking
to express or state clearly

Examples

She was not afraid to vocalize her concerns during the meeting.
He rarely vocalizes his true feelings to anyone.
During the discussion, students were encouraged to vocalize their opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (of voice), via French 'vocaliser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to produce sounds with the voice, especially by speaking or singing
produce sounds with the voice

Examples

While reading aloud to the children, she would carefully vocalize each word.
Many birds vocalize to communicate with each other in the wild.
He struggled to vocalize his thoughts after the shocking news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently
  • Synonym: to speak - to say words with the voice; talk
  • Antonym: be silent - to not make any sound with one's voice

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (vocal), from 'vox, vocis' (voice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00747173-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a consonant, usually a liquid) to pronounce or articulate as a vowel rather than as a consonant.
pronounce as a vowel

Examples

In certain dialects, the consonant 'l' is often vocalized between vowels.
Some linguists study when liquids are vocalized in various languages.
The 'r' in this position may become vocalized rather than pronounced as a consonant.

Synset Examples

between two consonants, this liquid is vowelized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vowelize - to pronounce or convert into a vowel

Etymology

From Latin vocalis (vocal) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00985667-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to sing each note of a scale or melody using the same vowel sound, especially as a vocal exercise
sing notes on one vowel

Examples

Before singing the piece, the choir members were asked to vocalize the scale on a single vowel.
She prefers to vocalize melodies on 'ah' during her warm-ups.
The instructor encouraged students to vocalize each note carefully to improve pitch accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - a system for teaching pitch and sight singing using syllables

Etymology

From Latin 'vocālis' (vowel) + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to produce sounds with the voice, especially by using the vocal cords to speak or sing
produce sounds with the voice

Examples

Infants gradually learn to vocalize as they develop language skills.
The instructor encouraged the students to vocalize their thoughts clearly during the discussion.
Some birds are able to vocalize a wide range of complex sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express (a sound) clearly; to speak distinctly
  • Synonym: to sing - to perform a song or produce musical tones with the voice
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly using breath but not the vocal cords

Etymology

From Medieval Latin vocalizare, from Latin vocalis (vocal).

Source: WordNet
