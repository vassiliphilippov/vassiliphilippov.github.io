Manannan

1. PROPN

/ məˈnænən / (AmE), / məˈnanən / (BrE)
A Celtic god of the sea, regarded as the son of Ler in Irish mythology.
Celtic god of the sea

Examples

In ancient stories, Manannan is often associated with magical islands and mysterious mists.
The sailors offered prayers to Manannan before setting out to sea.
Legends say that Manannan possessed a cloak of invisibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poseidon - Greek god of the sea in ancient mythology

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Manannán', a deity in Celtic mythology.
