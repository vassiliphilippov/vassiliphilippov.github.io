constantan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.stənˌtæn / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stənˌtæn / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and nickel with high electrical resistance and a low temperature coefficient, used in electrical resistance wires
alloy of copper and nickel for resistance wires

Examples

The heating element is made of constantan wire for its stability.
To ensure accurate measurements, laboratories often use constantan in thermocouples.
Because of its properties, constantan is preferred for electrical resistance applications.

Etymology

From German 'Konstantan', named for its approximately constant resistance.
