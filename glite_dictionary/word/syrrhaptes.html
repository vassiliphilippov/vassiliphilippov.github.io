Syrrhaptes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈræptiz / (AmE), / sɪˈræptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of sandgrouse birds found in central Asia, belonging to the family Pteroclididae.
genus of Asian sandgrouse birds

Examples

The Syrrhaptes genus includes the well-known Pallas's sandgrouse.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Syrrhaptes in the steppes.
The unique feet of Syrrhaptes help them adapt to sandy habitats.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Syrrhaptes, formed from Greek syrrhaptos (sewn together), referring to the birds' fused toes.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈræptiz / (AmE), / sɪˈraptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Pteroclididae, known as sandgrouse, found in central Asia.
genus of sandgrouse birds

Examples

The Syrrhaptes genus includes bird species adapted to arid regions.
Several members of the Syrrhaptes are known for their feathered legs.
Researchers observed a flock belonging to the Syrrhaptes genus near the salt flats.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Syrrhaptes, the genus name assigned in taxonomy.
