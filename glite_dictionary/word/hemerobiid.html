hemerobiid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛm.əˈroʊ.bi.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛm.əˈrəʊ.bi.ɪd / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored lacewing insect of the family Hemerobiidae, known for its delicate wings and predatory larvae.
small dark-colored lacewing insect

Examples

The entomologist collected a rare hemerobiid during his field study.
Hemerobiid larvae are often used in biological pest control in greenhouses.
A single hemerobiid can consume dozens of aphids in its lifetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacewing - any insect of the family Chrysopidae or Hemerobiidae with delicate, netted wings

Etymology

From New Latin Hemerobiidae, the family name, from Greek hēmerobios (living for a day).
