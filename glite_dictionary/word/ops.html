Ops

1. PROPN

/ ɑps / (AmE), / ɒps / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the goddess of abundance and fertility, wife of Saturn, and counterpart to the Greek goddess Rhea.
Roman goddess of abundance and fertility

Examples

The ancient Romans honored Ops during the festival of Opalia.
As the wife of Saturn, Ops was often invoked for blessings of fertility and abundance.
Mythologists often compare Ops to the Greek goddess Rhea due to their similar roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - Greek goddess of fertility, wife of Cronus
  • Synonym: Cybele - Phrygian mother goddess, associated with fertility

Etymology

From Latin 'Ops' (help, abundance), the name of the goddess.
