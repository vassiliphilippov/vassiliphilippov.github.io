nuisance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːsəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
(Law) An act, condition, or thing that unlawfully interferes with the use or enjoyment of property, or that endangers life, health, or morals, or is offensive.
legal interference with property or well-being
legal

Examples

The factory was sued for being a nuisance because its noise disrupted the neighborhood.
Smoke drifting from the landfill became a public nuisance affecting residents’ health.
The court ruled that the night club’s loud music constituted a nuisance to local homeowners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something causing irritation or bother

Etymology

From Old French 'nuire' (to harm), from Latin 'nocere' (to harm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːsəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
a person, thing, or situation that causes annoyance, inconvenience, or trouble
person or thing causing annoyance or inconvenience

Examples

The loud construction work next door is a real nuisance when I'm trying to study.
Some people find telemarketing calls to be an annoying nuisance.
Please don't be a nuisance and let your dog bark all night.

Synset Examples

a bit of a bother
he's not a friend, he's an infliction
washing dishes was a pain in the neck before we got a dish washer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes irritation or mild anger
  • Antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happiness or enjoyment, opposite of inconvenience or annoyance

Etymology

From Old French 'nuisance' (harm, injury), from Latin 'nocere' (to harm).
