ephemerid

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛmərɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛmərɪd / (BrE)
A short-lived insect, especially a mayfly, belonging to the order Ephemeroptera.
short-lived insect; mayfly

Examples

The adult ephemerid emerges, reproduces, and dies all within the span of a single day.
Children watched as a swarm of ephemerids danced above the still surface of the pond.
Biologists study the life cycle of the ephemerid for insights into rapid development and adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - A type of short-lived flying insect in the order Ephemeroptera

Etymology

From Greek 'ephēmeros' (lasting a day) + '-id', indicating belonging to a family/group.
