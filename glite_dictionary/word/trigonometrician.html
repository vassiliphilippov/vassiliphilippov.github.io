trigonometrician

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌɡɑː.nə.məˈtrɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / trɪˌɡɒ.nə.məˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a mathematician who specializes in the study or application of trigonometry
mathematician specializing in trigonometry

Examples

The trigonometrician devised new methods for calculating angles in navigation.
In the 18th century, a trigonometrician played a crucial role in mapmaking and surveying.
Her ambition was to become a renowned trigonometrician and contribute to advancements in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematician - an expert in or student of mathematics

Etymology

From trigonometry + -ician (suffix denoting a specialist).
