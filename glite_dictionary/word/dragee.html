dragee

1. NOUN

/ drɑːˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / dræˈʒeɪ / (BrE)
a small, often silver-colored, hard candy used for decorating cakes and other desserts
decorative candy bead for cakes

Examples

She carefully placed dragees around the edge of the wedding cake for a sparkling effect.
The bakery sells cupcakes topped with shiny silver dragees.
Please do not eat the metallic dragees—they are only for decoration.

Etymology

From French 'dragée', meaning a sugar-coated confection.

2. NOUN

/ drɑˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈdræʒi / (BrE)
A sweet consisting of a nut or piece of fruit coated in hard sugar, often used as decoration or given as a gift.
sugar-coated nut or fruit sweet

Examples

The silver dragee on top of the cake added a touch of elegance.
She offered me a dragee from a small, decorative tin.
Almond dragees are a traditional favor at weddings in many cultures.

Etymology

From French 'dragée', meaning sugarplum or candy.

3. NOUN

/ dræˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈdræʒ.eɪ / (BrE)
A small pill or candy that is sugar-coated, especially a medicated sweet.
sugar-coated medicated pill or candy

Examples

The pharmacist handed her a dragee to help ease her sore throat.
Children were once given a sugar-coated dragee to mask the bitter taste of medicine.
He mistook the decorative dragee on the cake for a regular candy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pill - a small tablet of medicine
  • Synonym: tablet - a small, round solid dose of medicine

Etymology

From French 'dragée', from Latin 'drageia', referring to a sugar-coated medicine or sweet.
