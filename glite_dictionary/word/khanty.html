Khanty

1. PROPN

/ ˈxɑnti, ˈkɑnti / (AmE), / ˈxanti, ˈkanti / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous Ugrian people living in northwestern Siberia, east of the Ural Mountains
member of an indigenous Siberian people

Examples

The Khanty have preserved many of their ancient traditions despite outside influences.
Anthropologists have long studied the customs and folklore of the Khanty.
Many Khanty still practice traditional reindeer herding in remote regions of Siberia.

Etymology

From Russian ханты (Khaty), the self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnti / (AmE), / ˈkanti / (BrE)
A Ugric language, related to Hungarian, spoken by the Khanty (Ostyak) people of western Siberia.
a Ugric language of western Siberia

Examples

Linguists have studied the Khanty language to understand the evolution of Ugric languages.
Many traditions of the Khanty people are preserved in their native language.
There are efforts to document and revitalize Khanty as it is considered endangered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mansi - A related Ugric language of western Siberia
  • Synonym: Hungarian - A Finno-Ugric language related to Khanty

Etymology

From Russian Ханты (Kanty), the self-designation of the people.
