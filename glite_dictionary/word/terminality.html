terminality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrmɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌtɜːmɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being terminal, especially with reference to an illness expected to result in death.
the state of being terminal (fatal)

Examples

The doctor discussed the terminality of the disease with the patient's family.
Ethical debates often arise around end-of-life care and the terminality of certain conditions.
Determining the terminality of an illness requires thorough medical assessment.

Synset Examples

A directive may not always be honored due to the inability of medicine to determine the terminality of the patient's condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terminal - having an illness that cannot be cured and will lead to death

Etymology

From 'terminal' + '-ity', modeled after Latin 'terminalis' (terminal, relating to an end) and the suffix '-ity' (state or condition).
