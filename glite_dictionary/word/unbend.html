to unbend

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɛnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɛnd / (BrE)
(Nautical) To unfasten a sail from a spar or a stay.
to detach a sail from a spar or stay

Examples

The crew were ordered to unbend the mainsail before the storm hit.
After docking, they began to unbend the sails for maintenance.
It's important to unbend the jib carefully to avoid damage to the fabric.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɛnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɛnd / (BrE)
to make or become straight after being bent or curved
make or become straight

Examples

After months in a brace, he was finally able to unbend his knee.
She gently tried to unbend the wire hanger to use it again.
The yoga instructor showed us how to unbend our backs after stretching.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bend - to shape or force something straight into a curve or angle

Etymology

Formed by prefix 'un-' (reversing action) + 'bend'.

Examples
