parliamentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑrləˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləˈmentəri / (BrE)
(of a system of government) having the executive power resting with a cabinet selected from and accountable to the parliament or legislature.
relating to government accountable to parliament

Examples

Many democracies, such as the United Kingdom, have a parliamentary system.
A parliamentary government requires the support of the majority in the legislature to remain in power.
Australia is known for its stable parliamentary democracy.

Synset Examples

parliamentary government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: presidential - (of a system of government) where the president has significant separation from the legislature

Etymology

From English parliament + -ary, ultimately from Anglo-Norman *parlement* (parliament).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
Relating to, enacted by, or in accordance with a parliament or its members.
relating to parliament or its procedures

Examples

The government promised parliamentary reform to modernize its procedures.
Several countries operate under a parliamentary system rather than a presidential one.
A parliamentary committee will review the proposed legislation next week.

Synset Examples

parliamentary reform
a parliamentary body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislative - relating to laws or the making of laws

Etymology

From parliament + -ary; parliament from Anglo-Norman 'parlement', from 'parler' (to speak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02785218-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
Relating to, or in accordance with, the rules and customs of a legislative or deliberative assembly.
of a legislative assembly's rules or procedures

Examples

She raised a point of order under the parliamentary rules governing the debate.
The committee followed strict parliamentary procedure during the meeting.
He wrote a manual on parliamentary law for organizations and clubs.

Synset Examples

parliamentary law

Etymology

From French parlementaire (relating to parliament), from 'parlement' (parliament), itself from Old French 'parler' (to speak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00719500-s
Synset POS: s
