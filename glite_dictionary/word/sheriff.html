sheriff

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛrɪf / (AmE), / ˈʃɛrɪf / (BrE)
the principal law-enforcement officer in a county, especially in the US, who is often elected and responsible for keeping the peace and performing administrative duties
chief county law-enforcement officer

Examples

The newly elected sheriff promised to improve community policing in the county.
After the robbery, the sheriff quickly assembled a search party to track down the suspects.
The sheriff has the authority to evict tenants under a court order.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: constable - a peace officer with less authority than a sheriff
  • Degree Higher: marshal - an officer of higher or specialized rank, sometimes performing similar functions to a sheriff

Etymology

From Old English 'scīrgerēfa', meaning 'shire reeve' (administrator of a shire or county).
