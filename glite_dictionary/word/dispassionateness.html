dispassionateness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpæʃənətnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈpæʃənətnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being impartial and unemotional; objective detachment.
state of being objective or detached

Examples

Her dispassionateness in handling difficult situations earned her the respect of her colleagues.
The judge was known for his dispassionateness when ruling on emotionally charged cases.
It is important to maintain dispassionateness when evaluating the evidence.

Synset Examples

her manner assumed a dispassion and dryness very unlike her usual tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectivity - the quality of being objective and unbiased
  • Synonym: impartiality - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fairness
  • Synonym: dispassion - lack of passion or emotion; objectivity

Etymology

From 'dispassionate' (lacking passion) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
