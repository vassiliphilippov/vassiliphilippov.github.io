to flambé

1. VERB

/ flɒmˈbeɪ / (AmE), / flɒmˈbeɪ / (BrE)
to pour liquor over food and ignite it, typically as a method of cooking or for presentation
cook food by igniting liquor poured over it

Examples

The chef will flambé the dessert at your table for a dramatic effect.
He carefully flambéed the steak with brandy to enhance its flavor.
They watched in awe as the cook began flambéing the bananas foster.

Etymology

From French 'flambé' (flamed), past participle of 'flamber' (to flame).
