premises

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛm.ɪ.sɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɛm.ɪ.sɪz / (BrE)
the land and the buildings on it, especially used for business or official purposes
buildings and the land they are on

Examples

The bakery has recently expanded its premises to include a new showroom.
Smoking is strictly prohibited on these premises.
The restaurant moved to larger premises downtown.

Synset Examples

bread is baked on the premises
they were evicted from the premises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: property - something owned, such as land or buildings
  • Synonym: site - a piece of ground for a specific use

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praemissa', meaning 'things set before', later used in the sense of property in legal documents.
