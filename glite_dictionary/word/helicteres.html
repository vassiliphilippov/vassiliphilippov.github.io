Helicteres

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪkˈtɪriz / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪkˈtɪəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees native to tropical America and Asia, characterized by their cylindrical, spirally twisted fruits.
genus of tropical shrubs and small trees

Examples

The Helicteres genus includes several shrubs prized for their medicinal properties.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Helicteres genus in Southeast Asia.
In some cultures, the fruits of Helicteres are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek *helicter* (twisted), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ hɛˈlɪktəriːz / (AmE), / hɛˈlɪktəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees native to tropical America and Asia, characterized by cylindrical fruits that are spirally twisted around one another.
genus of tropical shrubs and small trees

Examples

Several species of Helicteres are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
The fruit of Helicteres is easily identified by its characteristic spiral shape.
Botanists collected new specimens of Helicteres during their expedition to Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *helicteres*, from Greek *heliktēres* meaning 'spirals' or 'twists'.
