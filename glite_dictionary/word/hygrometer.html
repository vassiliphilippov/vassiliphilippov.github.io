hygrometer

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / haɪˈɡrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the relative humidity of the atmosphere.
instrument for measuring humidity

Examples

The scientist calibrated the hygrometer before beginning the weather observations.
A hygrometer is often used in greenhouses to monitor moisture levels for plants.
After a week of rain, the hygrometer showed high humidity in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barometer - instrument for measuring atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (wet, moist) + 'metron' (measure).
