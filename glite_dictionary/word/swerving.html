swerving

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɝːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of suddenly changing direction to avoid something or as a reaction
sudden change of direction

Examples

The driver's sudden swerving prevented a serious accident on the highway.
Excessive swerving can indicate that a driver is distracted or impaired.
The cyclist's swerving startled the pedestrians on the sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to change direction suddenly
