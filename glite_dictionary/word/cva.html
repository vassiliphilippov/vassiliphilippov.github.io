CVA

1. NOUN

/ siː viː eɪ / (AmE), / siː viː eɪ / (BrE)
A sudden loss of consciousness or neurological function due to an interruption of the blood supply to the brain; stroke.
medical term for stroke
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted to the hospital after suffering a CVA during dinner.
Doctors explained that a CVA can cause lasting neurological deficits if not treated quickly.
Her family recognized the symptoms of a CVA and called emergency services immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - sudden loss of brain function caused by interrupted blood supply

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cerebrovascular accident' (from Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) + 'vasculum' (vessel) + 'accident').
