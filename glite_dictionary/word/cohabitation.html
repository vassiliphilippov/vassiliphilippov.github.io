cohabitation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of living together and having a sexual relationship, especially without being married
living together as an unmarried couple

Examples

Modern couples often choose cohabitation over marriage for practical or financial reasons.
Many studies have examined the effects of cohabitation on long-term relationship stability.
Some countries have laws that grant rights to partners who are in a cohabitation arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marriage - the legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners in a relationship

Etymology

From Latin cohabitatio (living together), from cohabitare (to live together).
