half-track

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæfˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌtræk / (BrE)
(of a vehicle) having caterpillar tracks on the rear wheels and ordinary wheels at the front, typically used for military vehicles.
vehicle with rear tracks and front wheels

Examples

The army displayed a restored half-track from World War II at the museum.
A half-track vehicle can handle rough terrain better than a normal truck.
Engineers modified the old bus into a half-track for use in snowy areas.

Synset Examples

half-track armored vehicles

Etymology

From 'half' + 'track', indicating a vehicle that is partially tracked.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌtræk / (BrE)
a military vehicle with wheels at the front for steering and continuous tracks at the back for propulsion, used for traveling over rough ground
military vehicle with wheels and tracks

Examples

The army convoy moved steadily forward, each half-track loaded with supplies.
He restored an old half-track from World War II as a hobby project.
The museum's collection includes a rare German half-track used on the Eastern Front.
