proposal

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
a plan or suggestion put forward for consideration or discussion by others
a suggested plan or idea

Examples

She put forward a proposal to improve the company's remote work policy.
After much debate, the committee finally approved the new proposal.
His proposal called for a complete overhaul of the transportation system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan offered for consideration
  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: offer - a proposal presented for acceptance or rejection
  • Antonym: rejection - the act of refusing a proposal or offer

Etymology

From Latin 'proponere' (to set forth, propose), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
the act of suggesting a plan, idea, or course of action for consideration by others
suggestion or plan put forward

Examples

The manager reviewed each proposal before making a decision.
Her proposal to improve office communication was met with enthusiasm.
They debated the merits of his proposal at the meeting.

Synset Examples

they listened to her proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: recommendation - a suggestion or piece of advice about what someone should do
  • Antonym: rejection - the dismissing or refusing of a proposal or idea

Etymology

From Latin 'proponere' (to propose) via Old French 'proposer'.

3. NOUN

/ prəˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
an act of asking someone to marry you
an offer of marriage

Examples

He surprised her with a romantic proposal at the top of the mountain.
After weeks of nervous anticipation, his proposal was met with tears of joy.
She turned down his proposal, choosing to focus on her career instead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: engagement - the period or agreement following a marriage proposal
  • Antonym: rejection - the act of refusing a marriage proposal

Etymology

From Latin 'propositum' (something put forward), via Old French 'proposer' (to set forth, propose).
