cavalry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlri / (AmE), / ˈkævəlri / (BrE)
soldiers who fight on horseback, especially as a distinct military unit
troops trained to fight on horseback

Examples

The cavalry charged across the field, scattering the enemy's lines.
In the 19th century, the role of cavalry began to change with the invention of new weapons.
The arrival of the cavalry turned the tide of the battle.

Synset Examples

500 horse led the attack

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers who fight on foot
  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare and the branch that uses them

Etymology

From Italian 'cavalleria', from 'cavallo' (horse), ultimately from Latin 'caballus' (horse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlri / (AmE), / ˈkævəlri / (BrE)
a part of an army trained to fight on horseback or, in modern times, using armored vehicles; a highly mobile military unit
highly mobile army unit

Examples

The cavalry charged across the plain to support the infantry.
In modern warfare, tanks are considered the equivalent of cavalry.
Historically, the arrival of the cavalry often changed the outcome of a battle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers fighting on foot

Etymology

From Italian 'cavalleria', from Latin 'caballus' (horse).
