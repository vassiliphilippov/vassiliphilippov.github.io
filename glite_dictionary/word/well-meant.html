well-meant

1. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈmɛnt / (AmE), / wɛl ˈmɛnt / (BrE)
Intended to be helpful or kind, even if the result is not successful or is misunderstood.
intended with good intentions

Examples

Her well-meant advice was misunderstood by her colleagues.
Despite his well-meant efforts, the situation only became more complicated.
The well-meant gesture failed to cheer her up as intended.

Synset Examples

a well-intentioned but clumsy waiter
a well-meaning but tactless fellow
the son's well-meaning efforts threw a singular chill upon the father's admirers
blunt but well-meant criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-intentioned - having or showing good intentions
  • Synonym: well-meaning - having good intentions but sometimes resulting in bad effects
  • Antonym: malicious - intending to do harm

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (in a good or satisfactory way) and the past participle 'meant' of 'mean'.
