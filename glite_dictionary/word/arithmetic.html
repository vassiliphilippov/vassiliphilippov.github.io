arithmetic

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɪθmətɪk / (AmE), / əˈrɪθmətɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving arithmetic, especially the processes of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division
relating to arithmetic or calculations

Examples

The students learned various arithmetic operations in their math class.
She made an arithmetic error when adding the columns of numbers.
The test focuses on arithmetic skills rather than advanced mathematics.

Synset Examples

arithmetical computations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arithmetical - relating to or involving arithmetic

Etymology

From Greek arithmētikos (skilled in counting), from arithmos (number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03056383-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪθmətɪk / (AmE), / əˈrɪθmətɪk / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that deals with the properties and manipulation of numbers, especially through operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division
branch of mathematics concerned with numbers

Examples

Young students first learn basic arithmetic before moving on to more advanced math topics.
Her job requires quick mental arithmetic to process large amounts of data efficiently.
The teacher explained that understanding arithmetic is essential for everyday problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematics - the abstract science of number, quantity, and space

Etymology

From Greek arithmētikē (tekhnē) ‘(art) of counting’, from arithmos ‘number’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06013397-n
Synset POS: n
