landscape

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)skeɪp / (BrE)
a genre of art that depicts natural scenery such as mountains, valleys, trees, rivers, and forests
genre of art depicting natural scenery

Examples

The museum hosts an exhibit of 19th-century French landscapes.
She prefers painting landscape rather than portrait subjects.
His latest landscape captures the beauty of the Scottish Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, or photograph of a person

Etymology

From Dutch 'landschap', originally meaning 'region' or 'tract of land', later used for paintings of scenery.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)skeɪp / (BrE)
an area of land that can be seen in a single view, especially one that is beautiful or has a particular appearance
an expanse of scenery you can see at once

Examples

The mountain landscape was breathtaking as the sun rose over the peaks.
She painted the stunning landscape she saw from her window every morning.
Tourists stopped to admire the picturesque landscape stretching out below the hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scenery - the natural features of a landscape considered in terms of their appearance
  • Synonym: view - what can be seen from a particular place
  • Degree Lower: cityscape - a view or image of a city
  • Degree Higher: panorama - an unbroken view of the whole region surrounding an observer

Etymology

From Dutch 'landschap' (region, tract of land) originally used for paintings depicting natural scenery.
