shades

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪdz / (AmE), / ʃeɪdz / (BrE)
used to express that something strongly reminds you of someone or something from the past, often in the phrase 'shades of'.
reminder of someone/something (esp. from past)

Examples

Shades of my old school days came back as I entered the hall.
The detective's style had shades of Sherlock Holmes.
There were definitely shades of last year's chaos in the event's organization.

Synset Examples

aren't there shades of 1948 here?

2. NOUN

/ ʃeɪdz / (AmE), / ʃeɪdz / (BrE)
(informal) Sunglasses; spectacles with darkened or polarized lenses to protect the eyes from sunlight.
sunglasses (informal)
informal

Examples

He put on his shades before stepping outside into the bright sunlight.
She always carries her shades in her bag, just in case the sun comes out.
He wore mirrored shades that made him look mysterious.

Synset Examples

he was wearing a pair of mirrored shades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunglasses - Lenses set in a frame to protect the eyes from sunlight
