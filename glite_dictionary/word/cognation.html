cognation

1. NOUN

/ kɑɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Anthropology) the state of being related by blood; biological kinship as opposed to affinity by marriage.
blood relationship; biological kinship
medical

Examples

Under traditional law, cognation is used to determine inheritance rights within the family.
The study focused on the cognation between distant members of the tribe.
Genealogists trace cognation to map out extended biological family connections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consanguinity - kinship by blood
  • Antonym: affinity - relationship by marriage, not by blood

Etymology

From Latin cognatio (relationship by blood), from cognatus (related by birth).

2. NOUN

/ kɑɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
Descent or lineage traced through the maternal side of a family.
maternal line of descent

Examples

Genealogists mapped the cognation of the royal family to confirm the queen's ancestry.
In some cultures, inheritance rights are determined by cognation rather than agnation.
Research into cognation has revealed complex patterns of maternal lineage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnation - line of descent traced through the paternal side of the family

Etymology

From Latin cognatio (relationship by blood, kinship), from cognatus (related by birth).
