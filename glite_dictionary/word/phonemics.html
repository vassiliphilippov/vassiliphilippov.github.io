phonemics

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈniːmɪks / (AmE), / fəʊˈniːmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the study of the sound system of a language, specifically the analysis and classification of its phonemes.
study of a language's sound system
medical

Examples

The professor specializes in phonemics and teaches courses on language sound systems.
Knowledge of phonemics is essential for analyzing how different languages structure their sounds.
Her research in phonemics contributed to a better understanding of regional dialect variations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the systematic organization of sounds in languages
  • Synonym: phonetics - the study of the physical sounds of human speech

Etymology

From phoneme + -ics, after linguistics.
