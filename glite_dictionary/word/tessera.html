tessera

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛsərə / (AmE), / ˈtɛsərə / (BrE)
a small block of stone, tile, or glass used in the construction of mosaics
small mosaic tile

Examples

The ancient Roman floor was beautifully decorated with thousands of tesserae.
Each colorful tessera was placed carefully to form an intricate design.
Glass tesserae gleamed in the light, giving the mosaic a radiant effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tile - a thin, flat slab of material used for covering surfaces

Etymology

From Latin *tessera* (a tile, cube), from Greek *tessera* (four, cube).
