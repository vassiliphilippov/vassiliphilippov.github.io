Physalis viscosa

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈsælɪs vɪˈsoʊsə / (AmE), / fɪˈsælɪs vɪˈsəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of plant found on sea beaches from Virginia to South America, with greenish-yellow flowers and orange or yellow berries.
a coastal plant with yellow berries

Examples

The Physalis viscosa bushes along the beach produce bright yellow berries in the summer.
Researchers studied the habitat of Physalis viscosa to understand how it adapts to salty environments.
A local guide pointed out Physalis viscosa growing near the dunes, explaining its unique medicinal uses.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Physalis' means 'bladder' (referring to the inflated calyx) and 'viscosa' means 'sticky' or 'viscous'.
