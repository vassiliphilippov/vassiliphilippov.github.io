brazilwood

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪlˌwʊd / (AmE), / brəˈzɪl.wʊd / (BrE)
a tropical tree with a prickly trunk, whose dense red wood is used for producing red dye and for fine cabinetry
tropical tree with red wood used for dye and cabinetry

Examples

The finest violin bows are often made from brazilwood due to its excellent qualities.
In the 16th century, traders exported brazilwood for its valuable red dye.
The craftsman selected a piece of brazilwood to create an ornate cabinet.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'brasilia', from Portuguese 'pau-brasil' (red wood); 'brasil' may come from 'brasa' (ember), referring to the color of the wood.

2. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪlˌwʊd / (AmE), / brəˈzɪlˌwʊd / (BrE)
A dense, reddish wood from trees of the genus Caesalpinia and related genera, valued for making violin bows and as a source of red dye.
a dense wood used for bows and dye

Examples

The finest violin bows are often made from brazilwood.
In the past, brazilwood was exported as a valuable dye source.
The rich red color of the cloth was produced using brazilwood chips.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'pau-brasil', with 'brasil' referring to the reddish color ('brasa' meaning 'ember').
