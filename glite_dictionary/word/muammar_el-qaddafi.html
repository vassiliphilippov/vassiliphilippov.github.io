Muammar el-Qaddafi

1. PROPN

/ muːˈɑːmɑːr ɛl kəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / mʊˈɑːmɑː ɛl kæˈdæfi / (BrE)
(1942–2011) The Libyan political and military leader who ruled Libya from 1969, after coming to power in a coup, until his death in 2011.
Libyan political leader (ruled 1969–2011)

Examples

In 1969, Muammar el-Qaddafi led a coup that overthrew the Libyan monarchy.
International relations with Libya changed significantly under Muammar el-Qaddafi's rule.
Many Libyans were affected by the policies introduced by Muammar el-Qaddafi.
