to exhilarate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɪl.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪl.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
to make (someone) feel very happy, animated, or elated; to fill with lively excitement
make very happy or excited

Examples

The breathtaking view at the summit exhilarated the entire hiking group.
She finds that running in the morning truly exhilarates her for the rest of the day.
The successful launch of the new product exhilarated the whole team.

Synset Examples

The children were thrilled at the prospect of going to the movies
He was inebriated by his phenomenal success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrill - cause to feel a sudden excitement and pleasure
  • Antonym: to depress - make (someone) feel sad or dispirited

Etymology

From Latin 'exhilarare', from 'hilarare' (to make cheerful), from 'hilaris' (cheerful).
