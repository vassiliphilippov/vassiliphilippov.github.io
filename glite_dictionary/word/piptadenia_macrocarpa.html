Piptadenia macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ pɪp.təˈdiː.ni.ə ˌmæ.krəˈkɑːr.pə / (AmE), / pɪp.təˈdiː.ni.ə ˌmæ.krəˈkɑː.pə / (BrE)
A Brazilian shrub with doubly pinnate leaves and small flower spikes, producing flat or irregularly constricted pods; sometimes classified within the genus Piptadenia.
Brazilian shrub with pinnate leaves and spicate flowers

Examples

Botanists collected samples of Piptadenia macrocarpa in the Amazon rainforest.
The flat pods of Piptadenia macrocarpa are a distinctive feature of the species.
Some taxonomists debate whether Piptadenia macrocarpa should remain in its current genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'piptaínein' (to fall) and 'makros' (large) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to large seedpods.
