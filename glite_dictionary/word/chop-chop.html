chop-chop

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃɑpˌtʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒpˌtʃɒp / (BrE)
(informal) Quickly; at once; with speed.
quickly; with speed
informal

Examples

We need to leave now, so chop-chop!
She told the children to finish their homework chop-chop before dinner.
If you want to catch the train, you'd better move chop-chop.

Synset Examples

he works quickly
John got ready in short order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly

Etymology

From Chinese Pidgin English, from Cantonese 速速 (cuk1 cuk1, 'quickly').

2. ADV

/ tʃɑp tʃɑp / (AmE), / tʃɒp tʃɒp / (BrE)
used to tell someone to hurry up; quickly, without delay
used to urge someone to hurry
informal

Examples

The train is leaving soon, so chop-chop if you want to catch it.
She told the children to clean up their toys, chop-chop.
If you want to get there on time, you'd better move chop-chop.

Synset Examples

We need to finish this project chop-chop.
Get your bags packed chop-chop; the bus is leaving!
Come on, let's move chop-chop before it rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - move or act more quickly; be quick
  • Antonym: to slow down - reduce speed; act less quickly

Etymology

From Cantonese Chinese 'chok-chok' (quickly, hurry), introduced to English via pidgin English in the 19th century.
