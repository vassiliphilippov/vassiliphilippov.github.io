hydroxyzine

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːk.sɪˌziːn / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒk.sɪˌziːn / (BrE)
an antihistamine drug used as a tranquilizer to treat anxiety, allergies, and motion sickness
antihistamine drug for anxiety and allergies
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydroxyzine to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
Hydroxyzine can also be used to reduce allergic reactions such as hives or itching.
Before traveling, he took hydroxyzine to prevent motion sickness.

Etymology

From 'hydroxy-' (indicating a hydroxyl group) + '-zine', a common suffix in chemical nomenclature.
