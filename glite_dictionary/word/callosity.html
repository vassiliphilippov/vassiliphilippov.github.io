callosity

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsɪti / (BrE)
an area of skin that has become thick or hard due to repeated pressure or friction, especially on the hands or feet
thickened area of skin

Examples

After long hours of playing guitar, he developed a callosity on his fingertip.
The doctor examined the callosity on the runner's foot for signs of infection.
Wearing tight shoes often causes a callosity to form on the heel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callus - a thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue

Etymology

From Latin callositas, from callosus (hard-skinned).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
emotional hardness or lack of feeling; unfeelingness or hardheartedness.
emotional hardness; hardheartedness

Examples

Despite her tragic story, his callosity prevented any show of sympathy.
The callosity in his voice made it clear he was unmoved by her tears.
Years of hardship had given him a callosity that no kindness could penetrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity to others' feelings
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'callositas' (hardness, insensibility), from 'callosus' (thick-skinned).
