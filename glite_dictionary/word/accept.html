to accept

1. VERB

/ əkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to consider something as true or valid; to believe or admit
believe/admit as true or valid

Examples

Many scientists accept the theory of evolution based on the evidence.
She refused to accept his explanation for why he was late.
We must accept that mistakes were made and try to learn from them.

Synset Examples

I cannot accept the dogma of this church
accept an argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or believe something as true or valid
  • Synonym: to believe - to accept something as true

Etymology

From Latin acceptare, frequentative of accipere (to take, receive).
