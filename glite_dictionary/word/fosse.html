fosse

1. NOUN

/ fɑs / (AmE), / fɒs / (BrE)
A ditch or trench, especially one dug as a fortification and often filled with water.
defensive ditch, sometimes water-filled

Examples

The ancient castle was surrounded by a wide fosse to repel invaders.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a defensive fosse near the fortress.
A wooden bridge once spanned the fosse, leading to the main gate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moat - a deep, wide ditch surrounding a castle, fort, or town, typically filled with water

Etymology

From Latin 'fossa' (ditch, trench).
