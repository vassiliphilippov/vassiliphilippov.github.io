affine

1. ADJ

/ əˈfaɪn / (AmE), / əˈfaɪn / (BrE)
(Anthropology) Related by marriage, as opposed to by blood.
related by marriage (anthropology)

Examples

In many cultures, the obligations to one's affine differ from those to one's blood relatives.
The anthropologist studied the interactions between affines and consanguines in the tribe.
Marriage ceremonies often create new bonds between affine families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consanguineous - related by blood

Etymology

From Latin affinis (related, bordering on, allied).

2. ADJ

/ əˈfaɪn / (AmE), / əˈfiːn / (BrE)
(Mathematics) Relating to or involving affine transformations or the properties of affine geometry, which preserves points, straight lines, and planes.
relating to affine transformations (mathematics)

Examples

In geometry, an affine space allows for the study of parallelism but not distances or angles.
The affine transformation preserved the straight lines in the diagram, though it changed their lengths.
Many computer graphics algorithms rely on affine transformations to manipulate shapes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: linear - relating to or resembling a line; of equations of the first degree
  • Degree Higher: projective - relating to projective geometry, which extends affine geometry by adding points at infinity

Etymology

From Latin 'affinis' meaning 'related' or 'connected', later adopted in mathematics to describe geometries involving linear mappings and translation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02618025-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ əˈfaɪn / (AmE), / əˈfaɪn / (BrE)
(Anthropology) A person who is related to someone by marriage rather than by blood.
relative by marriage (anthropology)

Examples

In many cultures, an affine is treated with the same respect as a blood relative.
Anthropologists use the term affine to distinguish spouses and in-laws from consanguine kin.
Both blood relatives and affines attended the traditional family ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consanguine - a person related by blood

Etymology

From Latin 'affinis' meaning 'related' or 'connected'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09795963-n
Synset POS: n
