avarice

1. NOUN

/ ˈævəɹɪs / (AmE), / ˈævərɪs / (BrE)
extreme greed for wealth or material gain.
extreme greed for wealth

Examples

The avarice of the corporate leaders led to widespread public outrage.
History is filled with tales of kings brought down by their own avarice.
Despite his success, he was never troubled by avarice and lived modestly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous, especially in giving

Etymology

From Old French *avarice*, from Latin *avaritia* (greed), from *avarus* (greedy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæv.ə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪs / (BrE)
an insatiable or extreme desire for wealth or material gain; greed, especially when regarded as a vice
extreme greed for wealth; one of the deadly sins

Examples

His avarice led him to exploit his workers for personal gain.
The play depicts avarice as the source of the protagonist's downfall.
Throughout history, avarice has been condemned as a destructive human trait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous, giving more than expected

Etymology

From Old French *avarice*, from Latin *avaritia* (greed).
