chlamydia

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of bacteria that causes infections in the eyes, lungs, and genitourinary tract of humans and animals; also refers to diseases caused by these bacteria.
bacteria causing various infections
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that chlamydia is a common sexually transmitted infection.
Researchers are studying how chlamydia infects the respiratory tracts of birds.
Symptoms of chlamydia can sometimes go unnoticed for weeks.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Greek 'chlamys' (cloak), referring to the organism's membrane.

2. NOUN

/ kləˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdiə / (BrE)
A common sexually transmitted infection caused by bacteria of the genus Chlamydia, often producing no symptoms but potentially leading to serious health complications if untreated.
sexually transmitted bacterial infection
medical

Examples

Many young adults are unaware they have chlamydia because it often has no symptoms.
If left untreated, chlamydia can cause serious health problems, especially for women.
It is important to get tested regularly for sexually transmitted infections such as chlamydia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonorrhea - a sexually transmitted bacterial infection
  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum

Etymology

From New Latin Chlamydia (genus name), from Greek 'chlamys' (cloak), describing the bacteria's covering.
