backhand

1. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈbakhænd / (BrE)
(of handwriting) having the letters slanting backward.
letters slanting backward (in handwriting)

Examples

The teacher pointed out that his backhand writing made the essay difficult to read.
In the nineteenth century, backhand scripts were popular among some English writers.
Her signature was distinctively backhand, with every letter slanting to the left.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - (of handwriting) having letters slanting forward
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02292626-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈbakhænd / (BrE)
(of a stroke in racket sports) made with the back of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke, typically across the body.
stroke made with back of hand facing forward

Examples

The player won the point with a powerful backhand volley.
She practiced her backhand swing to improve consistency on the tennis court.
His backhand shot surprised his opponent during the match.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehand - stroke made with palm facing the direction of the stroke
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01037522-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈbakhænd / (BrE)
a stroke or return in a sport such as tennis, made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke
a sports stroke with back of hand leading

Examples

She hit a perfect backhand down the line to win the point.
His backhand is stronger than his forehand when playing tennis.
The coach taught her a new grip for her backhand stroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehand - a stroke with the palm or front of the hand facing the direction of the stroke
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00567727-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈbækˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhænd / (BrE)
to hit (a ball) with a backhand stroke, especially in tennis or similar sports
hit with a backhand stroke

Examples

She effortlessly backhanded the ball across the court and won the point.
If you backhand the return, make sure your grip is firm.
The player backhanding the shot kept the rally alive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehand - to hit (a ball) with a forehand stroke

Etymology

From 'backhand' (noun), originally referring to the reverse side of the hand in 18th-century English.
