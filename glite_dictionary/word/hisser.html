hisser

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪsər / (AmE), / ˈhɪsə / (BrE)
a person who expresses disapproval by making a hissing sound
person who hisses in disapproval

Examples

The comedian tried to ignore the persistent hisser in the audience.
A single hisser can disrupt an entire performance if not addressed.
Security quickly escorted the loudest hisser from the theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booer - person who expresses disapproval by booing

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'hiss' + '-er', denoting an agent noun.
