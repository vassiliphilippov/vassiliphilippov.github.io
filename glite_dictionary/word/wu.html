Wu

1. NOUN

/ wu / (AmE), / wu / (BrE)
a group of related Chinese dialects spoken in the Yangtze River delta, including cities like Shanghai and Suzhou
dialect group of Chinese language

Examples

Many inhabitants of Shanghai speak Wu as their native language.
The Wu dialects are distinct from Mandarin in both pronunciation and vocabulary.
Linguists study Wu to understand the diversity of Chinese languages.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 吴 (Wú), referring to the historical Wu region of China.
