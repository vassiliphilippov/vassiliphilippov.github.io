to coruscate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔːrəskeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəskeɪt / (BrE)
to flash, sparkle, or reflect light in bright beams or flashes
to flash or sparkle with light

Examples

The diamond necklace began to coruscate under the spotlights.
During the fireworks display, the night sky seemed to coruscate with color.
Sunlight made the snowy landscape coruscate with breathtaking brilliance.

Synset Examples

Unquarried marble sparkled on the hillside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - to emit or reflect light in a series of bright flashes
  • Degree Lower: glimmer - to shine faintly with a wavering light
  • Degree Higher: dazzle - to shine so brightly as to blind someone temporarily

Etymology

From Latin 'coruscare' (to flash, gleam).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔr.əˌskeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ə.skeɪt / (BrE)
to be lively or brilliant; to exhibit virtuosity or dazzling skill
be lively or brilliantly skillful

Examples

Her piano playing coruscated with energy and technical brilliance.
The comedian's wit coruscated through the entire evening, keeping the audience engaged.
Fresh ideas coruscate from the creative minds in the department.

Synset Examples

The musical performance sparkled
A scintillating conversation
his playing coruscated throughout the concert hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine brightly with flashes of light; to be lively
  • Synonym: to scintillate - to emit flashes of light; to be brilliantly skillful or witty

Etymology

From Latin coruscare (to flash, glitter).
