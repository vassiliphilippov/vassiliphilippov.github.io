Tricholoma

1. NOUN

/ trɪkəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / trɪkəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of gilled mushrooms with white spores, fleshy stalks, and notched gills, including both edible and inedible species
genus of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many edible mushrooms in the Tricholoma genus are highly prized by foragers.
The Tricholoma species can be difficult to identify due to their diverse appearances.
Some Tricholoma mushrooms are toxic, so proper identification is important before consumption.

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) and 'loma' (fringe), referring to the hairy fringe on some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms with white spores, a fleshy stalk, and notched gills, including both edible and inedible species.
genus of mushrooms with white spores

Examples

Several species in the Tricholoma genus are edible, but some can be quite toxic.
The woodlands in autumn are perfect for spotting Tricholoma mushrooms among the fallen leaves.
Mycologists study the characteristics of Tricholoma to identify new species.

Etymology

From Greek trikhos (hair) and lôma (fringe), referring to the hairy margin of some species.
