Truman

1. PROPN

/ ˈtruːmən / (AmE), / ˈtruːmən / (BrE)
(1884–1972) The 33rd President of the United States, serving from 1945 to 1953, who authorized the use of atomic bombs against Japan and led the country through the end of World War II and the start of the Cold War.
33rd U.S. President, 1945–1953

Examples

Truman became president after the death of Franklin D. Roosevelt in 1945.
The decision by Truman to use atomic bombs ended World War II.
Many historians study Truman's leadership during the early Cold War years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Harry S. Truman - Full name of President Truman
  • Antonym: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd U.S. President, Truman's predecessor
