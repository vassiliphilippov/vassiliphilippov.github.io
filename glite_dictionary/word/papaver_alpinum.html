Papaver alpinum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
a species of alpine poppy native to Europe, with white, yellow, or orange flowers, often found in mountainous regions
a species of alpine poppy plant

Examples

During our hike, we spotted the delicate Papaver alpinum blooming among the rocks.
The Papaver alpinum is known for thriving in harsh alpine climates.
Botanists often study Papaver alpinum to understand how plants adapt to cold environments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Papaver' meaning poppy, 'alpinum' meaning 'of the Alps'.
