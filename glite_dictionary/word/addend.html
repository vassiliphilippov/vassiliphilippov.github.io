addend

1. NOUN

/ ˈædˌɛnd / (AmE), / ˈadɛnd / (BrE)
a number that is added to another in a mathematical operation of addition
number added to another

Examples

In the equation 5 + 3 = 8, both 5 and 3 are addends.
You need to write each addend clearly to avoid mistakes in your calculation.
The sum of the addends gives you the total answer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: augend - a number to which another is added (in addition)

Etymology

From Latin addendus, gerundive of addere (to add).
