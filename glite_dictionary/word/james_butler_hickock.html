James Butler Hickock

1. PROPN

/ dʒeɪmz ˈbʌtlər ˈhɪkɒk / (AmE), / dʒeɪmz ˈbʌtlə ˈhɪkɒk / (BrE)
(1837–1876) An American frontier lawman, gambler, and folk hero known for his adventures in the Old West, commonly called Wild Bill Hickok.
legendary American frontier lawman

Examples

James Butler Hickock became a legendary figure for his exploits as a lawman in the American Old West.
Many stories about James Butler Hickock have been romanticized in books and movies.
The town of Deadwood is famous as the place where James Butler Hickock met his end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wild Bill Hickok - nickname and more common name for James Butler Hickock, Old West lawman
