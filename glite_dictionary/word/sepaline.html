sepaline

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiː.pəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiː.pəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a sepal, especially in shape, color, or function (botany)
resembling a sepal (botany)
medical

Examples

The plant's leaves are sepaline in both texture and color.
Some flowers develop sepaline bracts that protect the bud before it opens.
Botanists noted the sepaline qualities of the outer floral organs in this species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petaloid - resembling a petal

Etymology

Formed from 'sepal' with the suffix '-ine' (resembling, of the nature of).
