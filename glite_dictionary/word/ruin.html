ruin

1. VERB

/ ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
to cause great and often irreparable damage or destruction to something
destroy or spoil something completely

Examples

She accidentally ruined her dress with a permanent marker.
Heavy rainfall ruined the farmer's entire crop.
If you leave your phone in water, you could completely ruin it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Antonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition; repair or renovate

Etymology

From Old French *ruiner*, from Latin *ruina* (a collapse, downfall), from *ruere* (to fall, to rush).
