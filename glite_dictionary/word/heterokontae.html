Heterokontae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈkɒnti / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈkɒnti / (BrE)
A class of yellow-green algae characterized by having flagella of unequal length.
class of yellow-green algae
medical

Examples

Heterokontae are distinguished by flagella of different lengths on their motile cells.
Researchers studied the unique cell structure of Heterokontae in the laboratory.
The pond sample contained various types of Heterokontae among other microorganisms.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) and kontos (pole, referring to the flagella).
