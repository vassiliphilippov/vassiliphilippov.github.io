indisposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a slight illness or feeling of being unwell that prevents someone from doing something
slight illness; mild unwell feeling

Examples

Her indisposition forced her to cancel the meeting at the last minute.
He stayed home from work due to a minor indisposition.
Despite her indisposition, she managed to attend the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illness - state of being ill or sick
  • Antonym: health - state of being free from illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin indispositio (disorder, lack of arrangement) via French indisposition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a slight lack of willingness or readiness to do something; reluctance
slight reluctance or unwillingness

Examples

Julia's indisposition to speak openly made it difficult to resolve the disagreement.
He showed a marked indisposition to participate in the group project.
Despite her indisposition to travel, she agreed to attend the conference.

Synset Examples

a reluctance to commit himself
his hesitancy revealed his basic indisposition
after some hesitation he agreed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination to do something
  • Antonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness to do something

Etymology

From Latin indispositio (disorder), from in- (not) + dispositio (arrangement).

Synset POS: n
