Pythonidae

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈθɑnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / paɪˈθɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of nonvenomous snakes, commonly known as pythons, found mainly in the Old World and distinct from the family Boidae.
family of nonvenomous snakes (pythons)

Examples

Members of the Pythonidae family include some of the world's largest snakes.
The discovery of a new species within Pythonidae excited herpetologists worldwide.
Unlike the Boidae, Pythonidae are predominantly found in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boidae - family of snakes including boas, closely related to Pythonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Pythonidae, from Python (type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈθɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pʌɪˈθɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of nonvenomous snakes, known as pythons, found in the Old World and distinct from the Boidae family in some classifications
family of Old World nonvenomous snakes (pythons)

Examples

The Pythonidae family includes some of the largest snakes in the world, such as the reticulated python.
Unlike boas of the Boidae family, all members of Pythonidae are found in the Old World.
Research on Pythonidae has helped scientists understand snake evolution and diversification in Asia and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Boidae - family of boas and similar snakes, often contrasted with Pythonidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'Python', the name of a serpent in myth, plus '-idae', a zoological family suffix.
