hypertensive

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpərˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Having abnormally high blood pressure.
having high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him as hypertensive after several blood pressure readings above normal.
Hypertensive patients are advised to reduce their salt intake and exercise regularly.
Because she was hypertensive, she needed regular monitoring and medication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypotensive - having abnormally low blood pressure
  • Synonym: hypertension - the condition of having high blood pressure

Etymology

From 'hyper-' (above, excessive) and 'tensive' from Latin 'tensus' (stretched).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a person who has abnormally high blood pressure
person with high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The clinic has developed a special program to monitor hypertensive patients closely.
Doctors recommend regular exercise for anyone who is hypertensive.
Family members of a hypertensive should also be aware of their risks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypotensive - a person with abnormally low blood pressure

Etymology

Formed from 'hyper-' (over, above) and 'tensive' from Latin 'tensus' (stretched), relating to tension (blood pressure).
