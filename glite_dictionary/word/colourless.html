colourless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlər.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.ə.ləs / (BrE)
lacking color or brightness; dull or weak in color.
lacking color; dull
british

Examples

The walls in that room were completely colourless, making the space feel dull and empty.
She wore a colourless dress that blended in with the grey sky.
After hours underwater, the fish appeared almost colourless in the dim light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorless - lacking color or brightness (American spelling)
  • Antonym: colorful - full of color; bright and varied in color

Etymology

From colour + -less, an English suffix meaning 'without'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlələs / (BrE)
Lacking in variety, interest, or distinctive qualities; dull or uninteresting.
dull or uninteresting; lacking variety
british

Examples

The professor's lectures were so colourless that many students struggled to stay awake.
He gave a colourless report that failed to capture anyone's attention.
Despite her impressive resume, her interview answers were rather colourless.

Synset Examples

a colorless and unimaginative person
a colorless description of the parade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: vivid - full of life, color, or interest

Etymology

From colour + -less, meaning 'without colour', later extended figuratively to lack of interest or distinctiveness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00409108-a
Synset POS: a
