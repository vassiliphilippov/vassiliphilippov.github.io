Agkistrodon contortrix

1. NOUN

/ æɡˌkɪstrəˌdɑn kənˈtɔrtrɪks / (AmE), / æɡˌkɪstrəˌdɒn kənˈtɔːtrɪks / (BrE)
A species of coppery-brown pit viper native to upland regions of the eastern United States, commonly known as the copperhead.
copperhead snake species (eastern U.S.)

Examples

The Agkistrodon contortrix is widely distributed across the eastern and central United States.
If you see a snake with a coppery head in the woods, it might be an Agkistrodon contortrix.
Researchers studied the behavior of Agkistrodon contortrix in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copperhead - a common name for Agkistrodon contortrix, a North American pit viper

Etymology

From Greek agkistron (hook) and odon (tooth); species name from Latin contortus (twisted).
