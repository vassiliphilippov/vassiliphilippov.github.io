Dwight F. Davis

1. PROPN

/ ˈdwaɪt ɛf ˈdeɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˈdwaɪt ɛf ˈdeɪvɪs / (BrE)
(1879–1945) United States tennis player and philanthropist who founded the Davis Cup international tennis competition.
U.S. tennis player; founder of Davis Cup

Examples

Dwight F. Davis established the Davis Cup, which is still one of the most prestigious tournaments in team tennis.
Many tennis historians credit Dwight F. Davis with revolutionizing international tennis competition.
The legacy of Dwight F. Davis lives on through the annual Davis Cup matches.
