profit

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
The financial gain obtained when the revenue from business activity exceeds the expenses, costs, and taxes involved in sustaining the activity.
financial gain after expenses

Examples

The company reported a record profit this quarter despite rising costs.
After deducting all expenses, their net profit was surprisingly high.
She reinvested her profits from the previous year back into the business.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loss - the amount by which expenses exceed revenues
  • Synonym: gain - an increase in wealth or resources; profit

Etymology

From Old French *profit* (benefit, advantage), from Latin *profectus* (progress, profit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪt / (BrE)
The state or quality of being beneficial or advantageous; advantage or benefit.
beneficial or advantageous quality

Examples

The new law was enacted for the profit of all citizens.
You will find no profit in arguing over trivial matters.
They debated whether the intervention brought any profit to the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Antonym: loss - the fact or process of losing something valuable

Etymology

From Latin 'profectus' (progress, profit) via Old French 'profit' (benefit, advantage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05164957-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
to gain an advantage or benefit from something, especially not necessarily financial
gain an advantage or benefit

Examples

The team profited greatly from her leadership skills.
Both countries profited from increased trade and cooperation.
He profited by listening to experienced colleagues in his field.

Synset Examples

She profited from his vast experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - to receive an advantage; profit, gain
  • Antonym: to lose - to not gain what could be beneficial

Etymology

From Old French 'profit', from Latin 'proficere' (to advance).

4. VERB

/ ˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
to gain an advantage or benefit, especially financially or materially

Examples

Related Terms
