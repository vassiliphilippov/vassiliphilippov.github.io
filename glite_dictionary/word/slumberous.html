slumberous

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌmbɚəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbərəs / (BrE)
quiet, peaceful, and likely to make you feel sleepy; inducing or suggestive of sleep
suggestive of sleep; quiet and tranquil

Examples

The slumberous music made everyone feel calm and relaxed after dinner.
A slumberous haze hung over the quiet village as the sun set.
The library's slumberous atmosphere made it an ideal place to read and reflect.

Synset Examples

a slumberous June morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepy - tending to make someone feel sleepy; drowsy
  • Antonym: wakeful - unable to sleep or alert, especially at night

Etymology

From Latin 'slumber' (to sleep), plus '-ous' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈslʌmbɚəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbərəs / (BrE)
full of or inclined to sleep; suggestive of or causing drowsiness.
sleepy or inclined to sleep

Examples

He gazed at her with slumberous eyes, barely managing to stay awake.
The slumberous afternoon heat made everyone feel lazy and slow.
A slumberous silence fell over the room as night approached.

Synset Examples

slumberous (or slumbrous) eyes
‘slumbery’ is archaic
the sound had a somnolent effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somnolent - sleepy; causing drowsiness

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'slumber' + '-ous'; influenced by Latin 'somnus' (sleep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00190741-s
Synset POS: s
