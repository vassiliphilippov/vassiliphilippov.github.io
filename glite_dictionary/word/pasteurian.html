Pasteurian

1. ADJ

/ pæsˈtʊriən / (AmE), / pæsˈtjʊəriən / (BrE)
Relating to Louis Pasteur or his scientific experiments, especially those concerning microbiology and vaccination.
of or relating to Louis Pasteur or his experiments

Examples

Her research followed strict Pasteurian principles in ensuring laboratory sterility.
Many Pasteurian techniques are still used in modern microbiology labs.
The Pasteurian approach to vaccination revolutionized public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pasteurize - to treat (milk, wine, etc.) with heat to destroy harmful organisms, as developed by Louis Pasteur

Etymology

From the name of Louis Pasteur, French chemist and microbiologist, with the adjective-forming suffix -ian.
