aviator

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.vi.eɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.vɪ.eɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who operates or flies an aircraft, especially as a pilot
person who flies an aircraft

Examples

Amelia Earhart became a famous aviator for her solo flights across the Atlantic Ocean.
The aviator skillfully landed the plane despite adverse weather conditions.
Many early aviators contributed to the development of modern aviation technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - a person who operates the controls of an aircraft

Etymology

From Latin 'avis' (bird) with English '-ator' suffix; popularized in early 20th-century aviation.
