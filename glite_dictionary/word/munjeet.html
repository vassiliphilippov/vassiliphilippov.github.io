munjeet

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈdʒit / (AmE), / mʌnˈdʒiːt / (BrE)
A perennial East Indian creeping or climbing herb, Rubia cordifolia, used especially in Asia as a source of red dye.
East Indian dye-producing herb

Examples

The red color of the fabric was produced using munjeet as a natural dye.
Traditional Indian artwork sometimes features pigments derived from munjeet roots.
Textile manufacturers in the 19th century often relied on munjeet for their vibrant red hues.

Etymology

From Hindi 'manjīt', ultimately from Sanskrit 'mañjiṣṭhā' (Rubia cordifolia).
