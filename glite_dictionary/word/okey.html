okey

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
An official approval, permission, or endorsement to proceed with something.
official approval or permission
informal american

Examples

We need the manager's okey before starting the project.
She won't sign the contract without her lawyer's okey.
The engineer received an okey from the chief to begin work.

Synset Examples

they gave us the O.K. to go ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - the act of agreeing or giving permission
  • Antonym: disapproval - the act of not agreeing or refusing permission

Etymology

Possibly American English, abbr. of ‘oll (or orl) korrect’, a humorous alteration of ‘all correct’.
