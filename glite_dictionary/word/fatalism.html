fatalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪtəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that all events are fixed in advance and human beings are powerless to change them
doctrine that events are predetermined and inevitable

Examples

She viewed her inability to alter the outcome with a sense of fatalism.
Some people approach life's uncertainties with fatalism, believing everything is already decided.
His fatalism prevented him from trying to change his situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinism - theory that all events are caused by preceding events or natural laws
  • Antonym: free will - the power to act without the constraint of necessity or fate

Etymology

From Latin 'fatalis' (decreed by fate) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪtəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that all events are predetermined and inevitable, leading to a passive acceptance of whatever happens
belief that events are predetermined and inevitable

Examples

Many people facing chronic illness develop a sense of fatalism about their future.
His fatalism made him accept setbacks without protest or complaint.
The novel explores themes of destiny and fatalism among its main characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events are determined by causes external to the will
  • Antonym: free will - the power of acting without the constraint of necessity or fate

Etymology

From Latin 'fatalis' (decreed by fate) + '-ism'.
