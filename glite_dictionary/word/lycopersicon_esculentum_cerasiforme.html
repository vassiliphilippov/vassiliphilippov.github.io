Lycopersicon esculentum cerasiforme

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌkɑːpərˈsaɪkən ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəm ˌkɛrəsəˈfɔːrmi / (AmE), / lʌɪˌkɒpəˈsɪkən ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəm ˌkɛrəsɪˈfɔːmiː / (BrE)
A cultivated plant species bearing small red to yellow fruits, commonly known as the cherry tomato.
species of cherry tomato plant

Examples

The greenhouse was filled with rows of Lycopersicon esculentum cerasiforme, each bearing clusters of colorful fruit.
Botanists studied Lycopersicon esculentum cerasiforme to understand the genetic diversity of cherry tomatoes.
Salads often include the fruit of Lycopersicon esculentum cerasiforme for their sweet flavor and attractive color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry tomato - small round tomato, typically red or yellow

Etymology

From Latin: 'lycopersicon' (wolf peach), 'esculentum' (edible), 'cerasiforme' (cherry-shaped).
