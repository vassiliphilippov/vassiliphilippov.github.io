anterior

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Occurring earlier in time; preceding.
earlier in time; preceding

Examples

The species developed certain traits in the anterior phase of evolution.
The anterior chapters of the report dealt with last year's data.
His arrival was anterior to the rise of the new government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior - coming after in time; later

Etymology

From Latin anterior, comparative of ante (before).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A tooth located at the front of the mouth, especially an incisor or canine.
front tooth, especially incisor or canine
medical

Examples

Orthodontists pay special attention to the alignment of the anteriors.
Damage to the anterior can affect a patient's ability to bite into food.
In her dental records, several anteriors were noted as missing.

Synset Examples

his malocclusion was caused by malposed anteriors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior - a tooth situated toward the back of the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'anterior' (nearer the front).

3. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
situated at or toward the front, especially with reference to the head or front part of an organism or structure
situated at the front or head end
medical

Examples

The anterior part of the brain is responsible for complex thinking and decision-making.
Insects have their eyes located on the anterior section of the head.
The surgeon made an incision on the anterior surface of the knee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior - situated at or toward the rear or back, especially in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin 'anterior' (former, earlier), comparative of 'ante' (before).
