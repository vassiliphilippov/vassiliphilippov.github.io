leucorrhea

1. NOUN

/ luː.kəˈriː.ə / (AmE), / luː.kəˈriː.ə / (BrE)
a white or yellowish discharge of mucus from the vagina, especially when abnormal or indicative of infection
vaginal discharge (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that leucorrhea can be a sign of an infection that needs treatment.
Women may notice leucorrhea during different phases of their menstrual cycle.
Persistent leucorrhea should always be evaluated by a healthcare provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discharge - the act of releasing a substance from the body

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white) + rhoia (flow, discharge).
