Callitris glaucophylla

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪtrɪs ˌɡloʊkoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪtrɪs ˌɡlɔːkəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of small tree or shrub native to southern Australia, belonging to the cypress family.
Australian cypress tree species

Examples

The Callitris glaucophylla is widely used for windbreaks in rural Australia.
Botanists identified the specimen as Callitris glaucophylla during their field survey.
The fragrant wood of Callitris glaucophylla is valued for its durability.

Etymology

From the genus name Callitris (Greek kallos 'beauty' + trisos 'threefold') and species epithet glaucophylla (Greek glaukos 'bluish-green' + phyllon 'leaf').
