groundbreaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊn(d)ˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a ceremony to mark the beginning of a construction project, usually by turning the first sod of earth
ceremonial start of construction

Examples

The mayor attended the groundbreaking for the new library downtown.
A traditional groundbreaking ceremony was held before construction began on the hospital.
Yesterday's groundbreaking marked the start of the city's largest infrastructure project.

Etymology

From 'ground' (the earth) + 'breaking' (the act of breaking), referring to breaking the earth to start a project.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
introducing new ideas or methods; innovative and pioneering
innovative, pioneering

Examples

The scientist's groundbreaking research has changed the field of genetics forever.
She published a groundbreaking novel that challenged traditional narrative techniques.
Their groundbreaking technology has the potential to revolutionize communication.

Synset Examples

stylistically innovative works
innovative members of the artistic community
a mind so innovational, so original

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovative - introducing or using new ideas or methods
  • Synonym: pioneering - involving new ideas or methods and being the first of its kind
  • Antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'breaking ground' (beginning excavation), later used figuratively for innovation.
