yonder

1. ADJ

/ ˈjɑndər / (AmE), / ˈjɒndə / (BrE)
at some distance but within sight; that or those over there
distant but within sight
archaic

Examples

We could see the herd grazing in the fields yonder.
She pointed to the cabin yonder on the hillside.
From yonder tower, you can see the entire valley.

Synset Examples

yonder valley
the hills yonder
what is yon place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yon - distant but within sight (dialectal or archaic form)

Etymology

From Middle English yonder, from Old English geond (beyond, over) + -er (comparative suffix).

WordNet ID: oewn-00448792-s

2. ADV

/ ˈjɑndɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɒndə / (BrE)
at or in a place that is indicated, typically at some distance from the speaker; over there.
at or to a distant place indicated
archaic

Examples

Do you see the farm yonder across the field?
The old oak tree stands yonder by the riverbank.
She waved to her friend who was waiting yonder near the bridge.

Synset Examples

the house yonder
scattered here and yon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: here - at or in this place
  • Synonym: there - in, at, or to that place or position

Etymology

From Middle English yonder, related to yon, ultimately from Old English geond (beyond, over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00082658-r
Synset POS: r
