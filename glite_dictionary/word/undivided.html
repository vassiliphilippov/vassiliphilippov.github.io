undivided

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a group or opinion) completely united and not split by disagreement or conflict.
completely united; not split by disagreement

Examples

The committee presented an undivided front on the proposal.
Her speech was met with undivided approval from the audience.
The team remained undivided despite the challenges they faced.

Synset Examples

presented an undivided front

Related Terms

  • Synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: divided - split by disagreement or conflict

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'divided', from Latin 'dividere' (to divide).

(of attention, affection, etc.) given completely to one person or thing; not shared or distributed among several things.
given wholly to one thing; not shared

Examples
