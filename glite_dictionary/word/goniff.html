goniff

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːn.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈɡɒn.ɪf / (BrE)
A thief or dishonest person; a scoundrel (often used as a term of abuse).
a thief or dishonest person
informal

Examples

The old shopkeeper warned me to watch my wallet around that goniff.
Everyone knew Benny was a goniff, but somehow he always got away with it.
Don't trust that goniff with your money—he'll steal it in a heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crook - a dishonest person or criminal
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who is truthful and trustworthy

Etymology

From Yiddish 'ganef', from Hebrew 'gannāb' (thief).
