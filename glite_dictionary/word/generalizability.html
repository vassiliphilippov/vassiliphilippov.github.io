generalizability

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˌzaɪləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The extent to which the results of a study, experiment, or observation can be applied to a wider population or different situations.
applicability to wider cases or populations

Examples

The generalizability of these results remains a subject of debate among scientists.
Because the sample was limited, the study's generalizability is questionable.
Researchers must consider generalizability when designing experiments that seek broad conclusions.

Synset Examples

Although useful, such samples are not representative of the scientific workforce as a whole and thus have limited generalizability
This study is limited in its power and generalizability.
When altering specific methodological or participant characteristics of a previous study, they serve to test generalizability of the earlier findings under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universality - the quality of being true or applicable everywhere or in all cases
  • Antonym: specificity - the quality of being specific rather than general

Etymology

Formed from 'generalize' + the suffix '-ability', meaning capable of being made general.
