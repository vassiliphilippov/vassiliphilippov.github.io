spotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtər / (AmE), / ˈspɒtə / (BrE)
a person who observes or detects something, especially as a first observer or to provide information
person who observes or detects

Examples

The bird spotter identified a rare species in the forest.
A spotter stood by the edge of the field, ready to alert the team.
During the storm, a weather spotter reported heavy hail to the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who watches something carefully

Etymology

From the verb 'spot' (to notice or detect) + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtər / (AmE), / ˈspɒtə / (BrE)
a person employed at a dry-cleaning establishment whose job is to remove stains from clothing or fabric
person who removes stains at dry-cleaners

Examples

The spotter at the dry-cleaners managed to remove even the most stubborn coffee stains.
As a skilled spotter, she could tackle difficult marks that ordinary cleaning couldn't fix.
The owner hired an experienced spotter to handle delicate wedding gowns.

Etymology

From 'spot' (stain) + '-er', denoting an agent noun.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɑt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɒt.ə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to apply spots, markers, or identifiers to objects, usually for tracking or identification purposes
worker who applies markers or identifiers

Examples

The spotter carefully placed each identifying tag on the new equipment.
A spotter ensures that every item is marked before leaving the warehouse.
Every garment passed through the hands of the spotter for quality control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marker - a person or device that marks or labels items for identification

4. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈspɒtə / (BrE)
a person whose job or role is to watch carefully for something specific, such as warning of danger or noting particular events
person who observes for a specific purpose

Examples

The army uses a spotter to identify enemy positions from afar.
A weather spotter tracked the approaching storm and reported it to authorities.
She worked as a spotter in the factory, ensuring safety procedures were followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - person who watches or monitors something

Etymology

From 'spot' (to see or notice) + agent suffix '-er'.
