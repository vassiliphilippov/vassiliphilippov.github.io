convenient

1. ADJ

/ kənˈviːniənt / (AmE), / kənˈviːnɪənt / (BrE)
(archaic) Large and spacious; roomy and comfortable.
archaic: roomy, spacious
archaic

Examples

The convenient hall could easily accommodate hundreds of guests for the celebration.
They built a convenient house to host travelers along the old trade route.
Many castles featured convenient chambers designed for large gatherings in medieval times.

Synset Examples

a commodious harbor
a commodious building suitable for conventions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commodious - roomy and comfortable; spacious

2. ADJ

/ kənˈvinjənt / (AmE), / kənˈviːniənt / (BrE)
suitable for your needs, making things easier or more comfortable, or fitting your purposes well
suitable; making things easier or more comfortable

Examples

The hotel is in a convenient location close to the city center.
Just call me whenever it's convenient for you.
She found a convenient way to solve the problem.

Synset Examples

a convenient excuse for not going

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconvenient - not suited to your needs; causing difficulty or discomfort
  • Synonym: handy - useful or easily accessible

Etymology

From Latin conveniens (agreeing, suitable), from convenire (to come together, be fitting).
