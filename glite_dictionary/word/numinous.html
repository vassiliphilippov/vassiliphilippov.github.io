numinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuː.mɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
having a strong spiritual quality, suggesting the presence of a divinity; evoking a sense of the sacred or supernatural.
suggesting the presence of a divinity

Examples

The ancient temple had a numinous atmosphere that inspired awe in visitors.
As the choir sang, a numinous feeling filled the church.
She described the forest as a numinous place, mysterious and spiritually charged.

Synset Examples

a numinous wood
the most numinous moment in the Mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to affecting the human spirit or soul
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking spiritual or supernatural qualities; ordinary

Etymology

From Latin 'numen' (divine power, deity) + '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnuː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
having a strong religious or spiritual quality, indicating or suggesting the presence of a divinity; awe-inspiring or mysterious.
spiritually mysterious or awe-inspiring

Examples

The old cathedral had a numinous atmosphere that made visitors speak in hushed voices.
She felt a numinous presence in the forest, as if spirits watched over her.
The poet tried to capture the numinous beauty of dawn in his verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul; not physical or material
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull; of this earthly world rather than a heavenly or spiritual one

Etymology

From Latin 'numen' (divine power or will, nod), with the suffix '-ous'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02781171-a
Synset POS: a
