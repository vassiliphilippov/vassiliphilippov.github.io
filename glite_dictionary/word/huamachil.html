huamachil

1. NOUN

/ wɑːˈmɑː.tʃil / (AmE), / wɑːˈmɑː.tʃɪl / (BrE)
a thorny tropical American tree (Pithecellobium dulce) with clusters of yellow flowers, edible curved pods, and wood, dye, and gum of economic value
tropical American tree with edible pods and useful timber

Examples

The huamachil produces edible pods that are enjoyed by many local communities in Central America.
Carpenters value the wood of the huamachil for its durability and strength.
Local artisans use the yellow dye extracted from the huamachil in traditional textiles.

Etymology

From Spanish 'huamáchil', borrowed from Nahuatl 'cuahmachili'.
