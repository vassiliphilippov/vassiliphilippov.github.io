resignation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of giving up a job, position, or right; the official statement that one is leaving a job or office
formal act of giving up a job or position

Examples

His sudden resignation surprised everyone in the office.
She handed in her resignation after much consideration.
The CEO's resignation led to significant changes at the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdication - the act of renouncing or relinquishing a throne, right, or power
  • Antonym: appointment - the act of assigning a person to a position or office

Etymology

From Latin resignatio, from resignare (to unseal, cancel, give up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal written statement by which an employee or official states that they are leaving their job or position
formal notice to leave a job or position

Examples

His manager reluctantly accepted his resignation after years of dedicated service.
The board reviewed her resignation and appointed a new director.
After much thought, he handed in his resignation to the company president.

Synset Examples

he submitted his resignation as of next month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement - the act of leaving a job and stopping work permanently
  • Antonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment

Etymology

From Latin 'resignatio' (the act of giving up), from 'resignare' (to unseal, annul).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the acceptance of an unpleasant situation or fate without protest or resistance
accepting something unpleasant without protest

Examples

There was a look of resignation on her face as she heard the disappointing news.
He listened in silent resignation, realizing there was nothing he could do to change the outcome.
With a sigh of resignation, she accepted her fate and returned to her desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptance - the process of accepting something
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Latin 'resignatio' (a giving up, surrender), from 'resignare' (to unseal, give up).
