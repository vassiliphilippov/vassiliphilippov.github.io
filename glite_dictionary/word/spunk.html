spunk

1. NOUN

/ spʌŋk / (AmE), / spʌŋk / (BrE)
tinder or material used for starting a fire, especially in former times.
material for starting a fire
archaic

Examples

The traveler gathered some dry spunk to help ignite his campfire.
In olden days, spunk was often carried in a tinderbox for lighting lamps.
She showed the children how to make a small flame using steel, flint, and spunk.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *sponk* (spark).

2. NOUN

/ spʌŋk / (AmE), / spʌŋk / (BrE)
courage, determination, or spirit, especially in the face of difficulty or danger.
courage; determination; spirit

Examples

Despite her small size, she showed remarkable spunk during the challenging competition.
It takes a lot of spunk to stand up for what is right, even when others disagree.
His spunk inspired the whole team to keep going when things got tough.

Synset Examples

he kept fighting on pure spunk
you haven't got the heart for baseball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guts - courage and determination
  • Synonym: nerve - courage or bravery
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage

Etymology

From Scottish spunk (spark, spirit), possibly from Gaelic spong (tinder, touchwood).
