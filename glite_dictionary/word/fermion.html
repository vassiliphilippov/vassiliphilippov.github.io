fermion

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜr.mi.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.mi.ɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle, such as an electron, proton, or neutron, that obeys Fermi-Dirac statistics and is subject to the Pauli exclusion principle
type of subatomic particle (electron, proton, etc.)

Examples

Electrons, protons, and neutrons are all examples of fermions.
Because fermions obey the Pauli exclusion principle, no two of them can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously.
The classification of particles as fermions or bosons is fundamental in quantum physics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boson - a subatomic particle that obeys Bose-Einstein statistics, not subject to Pauli exclusion principle

Etymology

Formed from the name of physicist Enrico Fermi and the suffix -on (as in electron).
