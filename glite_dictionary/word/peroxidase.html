peroxidase

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑksɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksɪˌdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme, especially found in plants, that catalyzes the oxidation of a substance by a peroxide, often hydrogen peroxide
enzyme catalyzing reactions with peroxide
medical

Examples

The activity of peroxidase increased significantly under stress conditions in the plant.
Researchers used a peroxidase test to detect the presence of hydrogen peroxide.
Horseradish peroxidase is commonly used in biochemical assays.

Etymology

From 'peroxide' and the enzyme suffix '-ase'.
