newscast

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A broadcast of news or commentary on current events, typically on television or radio.
news broadcast on TV or radio

Examples

The evening newscast covered the latest developments in the election.
She watches the morning newscast while having breakfast.
Our local station's newscast begins at six o'clock every night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news bulletin - a short broadcast of important news
  • Synonym: news report - an account or statement about current events

Etymology

Formed from 'news' + 'cast', meaning to broadcast news. Popularized in early 20th century America.
