Belarus

1. PROPN

/ bɛˈlærəs / (AmE), / bɛˈlɑːrʊs / (BrE)
a landlocked country in Eastern Europe, formerly part of the Soviet Union, with its capital at Minsk.
country in Eastern Europe

Examples

Belarus declared its independence from the Soviet Union in 1991.
The capital city of Belarus is Minsk, known for its Soviet-era architecture.
Many tourists visit Belarus to experience its unique culture and history.

Etymology

From Russian Белару́сь (Belarús’), meaning 'White Russia.'
