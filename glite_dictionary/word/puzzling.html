puzzling

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌzlɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult to understand or explain; mysterious or perplexing.
difficult to understand or explain

Examples

Her sudden disappearance was extremely puzzling to everyone in the office.
The instructions for assembling the device were so puzzling that I had to ask for help.
I find his motives rather puzzling, as he never explains what he wants.

Synset Examples

I didn't grasp the meaning of that enigmatic comment until much later
prophetic texts so enigmatic that their meaning has been disputed for centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious
  • Synonym: perplexing - causing confusion or difficulty
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From the verb 'puzzle' (to confuse), which probably originated as a frequentative of obsolete 'pose' (to perplex).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌz.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌz.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult to understand or explain; causing confusion or perplexity.
difficult to understand; confusing

Examples

The scientist's explanation was so puzzling that few people could follow it.
She found the old map in the attic, covered in puzzling symbols and numbers.
It was a puzzling case that kept detectives working late into the night.

Synset Examples

sent confusing signals to Iraq
perplexing to someone who knew nothing about it
a puzzling statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusing - difficult to understand; unclear or bewildering
  • Synonym: perplexing - causing someone to feel baffled or puzzled

Etymology

From the verb 'puzzle' + '-ing.' 'Puzzle' likely from English dialect *pusle* (bewilder), possibly from Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00432472-s
Synset POS: s
