to replicate

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make or do something again in exactly the same way, especially to achieve the same result
repeat or reproduce an action or result

Examples

The scientists tried to replicate the experiment to confirm their results.
It is difficult to replicate such outstanding success year after year.
She couldn’t replicate the amazing cake she made for his birthday last time.

Synset Examples

He could never replicate his brilliant performance of the magic trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to do something again
  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something

Etymology

From Latin replicare (to fold back, repeat), from re- (again) + plicare (to fold).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make an exact copy of something; to reproduce or duplicate, especially in a scientific or technical context
to make an exact copy

Examples

Scientists are able to replicate the experiment in different laboratories around the world.
When exposed to the virus, the cells rapidly replicate and spread the infection.
This genetic material can replicate itself with high precision.

Synset Examples

replicate the cell
copy the genetic information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an identical copy of something
  • Synonym: copy - to make a reproduction or imitation of something
  • Antonym: to originate - to begin, to bring into existence

Etymology

From Latin replicare (to fold back, repeat), from re- (again) + plicare (to fold).

WordNet ID: oewn-01738810-v
