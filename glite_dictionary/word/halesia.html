Halesia

1. NOUN

/ həˈliːziə / (AmE), / həˈliːziə / (BrE)
A genus of small deciduous trees or shrubs native to eastern North America and China, known for their bell-shaped flowers.
genus of bell-flowered trees/shrubs

Examples

The garden included a beautiful Halesia tree covered in delicate white flowers.
Many gardeners plant Halesia for its ornamental spring blossoms.
Among the flowering shrubs in the park, Halesia stood out for its elegant appearance.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Stephen Hales (1677–1761).

2. NOUN

/ həˈliːziə / (AmE), / həˈliːzɪə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous small trees and shrubs native to China and eastern North America, commonly known as silverbells.
genus of deciduous trees/shrubs (silverbells)

Examples

The Halesia genus is prized for its delicate white bell-shaped flowers in spring.
Many gardens feature Halesia trees for their ornamental value.
Botanists classify several species of Halesia in eastern North America.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Stephen Hales (1677–1761).
