relaxation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The process in which a muscle or muscle fibers gradually lengthen and return to their resting state after contraction.
muscle returning to resting length
medical

Examples

After contraction, the relaxation of the muscle allows the arm to straighten again.
Proper relaxation of muscle fibers is essential for normal movement and function.
The doctor explained that muscle relaxation takes longer in some neuromuscular disorders.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːlækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The process by which a system returns to equilibrium, typically at an exponential rate, after being disturbed.
return to equilibrium after disturbance (physics)

Examples

The relaxation of the magnetic field occurs shortly after the external force is removed.
Thermal relaxation times vary depending on the material properties and system size.
Scientists measured the relaxation rate of the excited electrons as they returned to their ground state.

Etymology

From Latin relaxatio, from relaxare (to loosen, relax).
