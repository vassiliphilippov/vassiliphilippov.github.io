Davallia bullata mariesii

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæli.ə bʊˈleɪtə ˌmær.iˈiː / (AmE), / dəˈvæl.i.ə bʊˈlɑː.tə ˌmær.iˈiː / (BrE)
A feathery fern native to tropical Asia and Malaysia, known for its delicate, divided fronds.
feathery Asian fern

Examples

The garden featured a rare Davallia bullata mariesii among its collection of exotic ferns.
Botanists carefully studied the growth patterns of Davallia bullata mariesii in its native habitat.
She admired the intricate fronds of the Davallia bullata mariesii in the greenhouse display.

Etymology

From the genus name Davallia, named after English botanist Edmond Davall; 'bullata' from Latin meaning 'bubble-shaped,' and 'mariesii' honoring Charles Maries, a British plant collector.
