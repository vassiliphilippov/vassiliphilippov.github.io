burnup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɜːn.ʌp / (BrE)
the amount of nuclear fuel that has been consumed or used up in a reactor, often measured to indicate fuel efficiency or usage.
amount of fuel consumed in a nuclear reactor

Examples

The engineers monitored the burnup of the reactor to determine when refueling would be necessary.
Achieving a higher burnup allows nuclear plants to operate longer between fuel changes.
Accurate measurement of burnup is crucial for maintaining both safety and efficiency in nuclear facilities.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝn.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɜːn.ʌp / (BrE)
a high-speed motorcycle race conducted on a public road, often illegally or informally
high-speed motorcycle street race
informal

Examples

Police cracked down on the burnup that took place on Main Street last night.
A group of bikers organized a midnight burnup outside the city limits.
Local residents have complained about noise from the illegal burnups in their neighborhood.
