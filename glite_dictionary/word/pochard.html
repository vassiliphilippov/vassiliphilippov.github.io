pochard

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃərd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtʃ.ɑːd / (BrE)
a heavy-bodied diving duck with a grey-and-black body and a reddish head, found in Eurasia and Africa
a type of diving duck

Examples

We spotted a pochard swimming among the reeds at the lake’s edge.
The distinctive red head of the male pochard makes it easy to identify.
Several pochards migrate to southern Europe during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

From French 'pochard', of uncertain origin.
