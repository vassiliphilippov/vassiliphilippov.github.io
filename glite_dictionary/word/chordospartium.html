Chordospartium

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrdoʊˈspɑːrtiəm / (AmE), / kɔːdɒˈspɑːtiəm / (BrE)
A small genus of trees native to New Zealand, including species known as weeping tree broom, some of which are endangered.
genus of small New Zealand trees

Examples

The rare Chordospartium is found only in certain regions of New Zealand.
Botanists are working to conserve the endangered species of Chordospartium.
The weeping tree broom, a type of Chordospartium, is notable for its graceful branches.

Etymology

From Greek 'chordē' (string, cord) + 'Spartium' (a related plant genus).
