J. Edgar Hoover

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪ ˈɛdɡər ˈhuːvər / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪ ˈɛdɡə ˈhuːvə / (BrE)
(1895–1972) American lawyer who served as the first Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) from 1924 to 1972.
first Director of the FBI, 1924–1972

Examples

J. Edgar Hoover led the FBI for nearly half a century, shaping American law enforcement.
The controversial legacy of J. Edgar Hoover is still debated by historians today.
A biography about J. Edgar Hoover explores his influence on U.S. politics and civil rights.
