Entolomataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌentoʊloʊˈmætəˌsiː / (AmE), / ˌɛntələʊˈmætəsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Agaricales, characterized by pink-spored, terrestrial gilled mushrooms.
family of pink-spored gilled mushrooms

Examples

The Entolomataceae are notable for their pink spore prints and diverse habitats.
Many mycologists study Entolomataceae to understand fungal biodiversity in forests.
The family Entolomataceae includes several genera with important ecological roles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Agaricales - an order of fungi including the gilled mushrooms

Etymology

From the genus name Entoloma + Latin -aceae, a standard suffix for plant and fungal families.

family of pink-spored gilled fungi

Examples

The Entolomataceae family includes mushrooms commonly recognized by their pink spores and attached gills.
Researchers are studying the diversity of Entolomataceae in temperate forests worldwide.
Within the Entolomataceae, the genus Entoloma is known for its varied and often colorful species.

Etymology

From the genus Entoloma, with the suffix -aceae denoting a family, used in biological classification.
