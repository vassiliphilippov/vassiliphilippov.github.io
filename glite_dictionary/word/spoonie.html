spoonie

1. NOUN

/ ˈspuːni / (AmE), / ˈspuːni / (BrE)
a person with a chronic illness or disability, especially one who manages their energy levels carefully
person with chronic illness or disability
slang

Examples

Many spoonies connect online to share advice and support one another.
As a spoonie, she plans her day around her limited energy.
The conference included sessions tailored for spoonies and those supporting them.

Etymology

From 'spoon theory,' a concept by Christine Miserandino using spoons as a metaphor for energy used in daily tasks by people with chronic illness.
