Schizaea

1. NOUN

/ skɪˈziː.ə / (AmE), / skɪˈziː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender ferns of the family Schizaeaceae, commonly known as curly grass ferns, found mostly in tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of small tropical ferns

Examples

The Schizaea genus is easily recognized by its grass-like appearance.
Some species of Schizaea are used in traditional medicine in tropical countries.
Botanists discovered a rare Schizaea fern growing in the rainforest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Schizaeaceae - family of ferns to which Schizaea belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'skhizein' (to split), referring to the divided fronds.

2. NOUN

/ skɪˈzeɪ.ə / (AmE), / skɪˈziː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, cosmopolitan ferns in the family Schizaeaceae, known as curly grass ferns, especially common in tropical regions.
genus of small, tropical curly grass ferns

Examples

The Schizaea genus includes ferns known for their slender, grass-like appearance.
Many species of Schizaea thrive in tropical and subtropical climates worldwide.
Botanists often refer to Schizaea as curly grass ferns due to their unique frond shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From Greek *schizein* (to split), referring to the divided fronds.
