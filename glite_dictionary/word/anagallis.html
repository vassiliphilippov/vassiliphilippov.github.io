Anagallis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡælɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly Old World herbs in the primrose family, known for their small, brightly colored flowers, such as the scarlet pimpernel.
genus of flowering herbs (scarlet pimpernel)

Examples

The botanist carefully documented the growth patterns of several Anagallis species in the wild meadow.
Gardeners often plant Anagallis for its delicate and attractive blooms.
The Anagallis, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, thrives in well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet pimpernel - a common flowering plant, Anagallis arvensis, with scarlet flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'anagallis', meaning 'pimpernel', via Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡælɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly Old World herbs in the primrose family, known for their small, brightly colored flowers.
genus of herbs with small flowers

Examples

The most well-known member of the Anagallis genus is the scarlet pimpernel.
Botanists often study Anagallis to understand plant adaptation in Old World regions.
Many gardens feature Anagallis for its attractive, colorful blossoms.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Anagallis, from Greek anagallis, a kind of plant.
