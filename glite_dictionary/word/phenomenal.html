phenomenal

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
extraordinarily good or impressive; remarkable
extraordinarily good or impressive

Examples

Her performance in the final was simply phenomenal and left the audience in awe.
The new restaurant received phenomenal reviews from critics and customers alike.
Our team achieved phenomenal success in just a few short months.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable
  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of attention; striking
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not special or exceptional

Etymology

From Greek phainomenon (appearance) via late Latin and French.

2. ADJ

/ fəˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / fəˈnɒm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or connected with a phenomenon or phenomena; observable or perceptible, especially as distinct from theoretical or underlying causes.
relating to a phenomenon; observable

Examples

Her research focused on the phenomenal aspects of perception rather than abstract theories.
The study examined phenomenal changes in weather patterns over fifty years.
In philosophy, the distinction between noumenal and phenomenal reality is an important one.

Synset Examples

phenomenal science

Etymology

From Greek phainomenon (appearance), via Late Latin phenomenalis.
