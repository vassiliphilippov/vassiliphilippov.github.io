chauffeuse

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈfɜs / (AmE), / ʃəʊˈfɜːz / (BrE)
a woman employed to drive a private or hired car for someone else; a female chauffeur
female chauffeur

Examples

The hotel sent a chauffeuse to collect us from the airport in a luxury sedan.
As a chauffeuse, her professionalism and discretion were highly valued by her celebrity clients.
She broke gender barriers in the 1950s by becoming the first female chauffeuse at the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chauffeur - a person employed to drive a car for someone else

Etymology

Borrowed from French, feminine form of 'chauffeur'.
