reedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈridi / (AmE), / ˈriːdi / (BrE)
tall, thin, and upright, like a reed; slender in shape
tall, thin, and upright, like a reed

Examples

The reedy stems swayed gently in the breeze by the riverbank.
She moved through the reedy grass, her feet barely making a sound.
His reedy frame made him seem taller than he really was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin; slim
  • Synonym: wiry - lean, thin, and strong

Etymology

From the noun 'reed' + -y, meaning 'having the qualities of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈridi / (AmE), / ˈriːdi / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) having a tone that is thin, high, and somewhat like a reed instrument.
having a thin, high sound like a reed instrument

Examples

His reedy voice carried easily across the crowded hall.
The clarinet produced a reedy sound that stood out in the orchestra.
She sang in a reedy tenor that was both fragile and bright.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sonorous - having a deep, full sound
  • Synonym: nasal - having a sound produced through the nose

Etymology

From 'reed' (tall, slender-leaved plant or reed instrument) + '-y' (having the quality of).
