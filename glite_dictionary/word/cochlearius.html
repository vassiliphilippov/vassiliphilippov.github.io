Cochlearius

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.kliˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / kɒk.lɪˈɛəri.əs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the heron family, known as boatbills, characterized by their distinctive broad bills.
a genus of boat-billed heron

Examples

The Cochlearius is easily recognized by its wide, scoop-shaped bill.
Ornithologists have studied the habitat of Cochlearius along Central and South American waterways.
Unlike other herons, Cochlearius prefers to hunt at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boatbill - a bird with a broad, spatulate bill in the heron family

Etymology

From Latin cochlearius meaning 'pertaining to a spoon' (referring to the bird's broad beak).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊkliˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒklɪˈɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American wading birds known as boatbills, distinguished by their broad, scoop-shaped bills
genus of boatbill birds

Examples

The Cochlearius genus is characterized by birds with broad, boat-shaped bills.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Cochlearius in Central America.
The plumage of Cochlearius species provides excellent camouflage among riverbank vegetation.

Etymology

From Latin cochlear (spoon, ladle), referring to the shape of the bill.
