cataclinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈklaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈklʌɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a valley or river) running in the direction of the dip of the surrounding rock strata.
aligned with the dip of rock strata (geology)

Examples

The geologist pointed out that the river flowed in a cataclinal direction, following the downward slope of the strata.
Unlike the previous valley, this one is distinctly cataclinal, matching the underlying rock dip.
The map shows several cataclinal valleys that have formed due to the tilted sedimentary layers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaclinal - (of valleys and rivers) running opposite to the dip in rock strata

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down) + klinein (to lean), relating to inclination.
