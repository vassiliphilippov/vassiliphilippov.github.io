shiitake

1. NOUN

/ ʃiˈtɑːkeɪ / (AmE), / ʃɪˈɑːki / (BrE)
an edible East Asian mushroom, with a dark brown to blackish cap and a tough, inedible stem, often used in cooking
edible East Asian mushroom

Examples

She added sliced shiitake mushrooms to the stir-fry for extra flavor.
The chef used dried shiitake to give the soup a rich, earthy taste.
Many vegetarian recipes use shiitake as a meat substitute because of its chewy texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fleshy spore-bearing growth of a fungus, often edible

Etymology

From Japanese 椎茸 (shiitake), from 椎 (shii, type of tree) + 茸 (take, mushroom).
