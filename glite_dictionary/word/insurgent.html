insurgent

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who rises in active revolt against established authority, especially one who takes part in armed resistance in order to improve conditions
a person rebelling against authority

Examples

The army was deployed to suppress the insurgent forces in the region.
Several insurgents surrendered after the peace negotiations began.
Government officials described the attack as the work of insurgent groups seeking to destabilize the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance against an established government or ruler
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to the established ruler or government, especially in the face of a revolt

Etymology

From Latin insurgentem, present participle of insurgere (to rise up).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who is involved in armed rebellion against an established authority, often using irregular tactics such as sabotage or harassment
member of an armed rebellion

Examples

The government deployed additional troops to areas controlled by the insurgents.
An insurgent group claimed responsibility for the attack on the outpost.
Reports indicate that insurgent activity has increased in the northern provinces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance against an established government or ruler
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to the established ruler or government

Etymology

From Latin insurgent-, present participle of insurgere (to rise up) via French insurgent.

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
rising in active revolt against the civil authority or government
revolting against authority or government

Examples

The insurgent forces launched a surprise attack on the capital.
Several insurgent groups have formed in response to the government's policies.
The region has seen insurgent activity increase over the last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellious - showing a desire to resist authority or control
  • Antonym: submissive - willing to obey or be controlled by authority

Etymology

From Latin insurgent-, present participle of insurgere, meaning 'to rise up'.
