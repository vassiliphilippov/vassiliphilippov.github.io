to hypnotize

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪp.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nə.taɪz / (BrE)
to put someone into a state of hypnosis, making them highly responsive to suggestion or direction
induce hypnosis in someone

Examples

The therapist was able to hypnotize the patient and help them recall childhood memories.
During the stage show, the magician attempted to hypnotize several audience members.
Some people believe you can't hypnotize someone against their will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mesmerize - to capture someone's complete attention or induce a trance-like state

Etymology

From French 'hypnotiser', from Greek 'hupnos' (sleep).
