severance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛv.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ər.əns / (BrE)
the act of ending a relationship, connection, or association between people or groups
ending of a relationship or connection

Examples

The bitter severance between the two companies shocked the business community.
After years of friendship, their severance was difficult for everyone involved.
Political upheaval led to the severance of diplomatic ties between the nations.

Synset Examples

they hoped to avoid a break in relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the state of being separated or the process of separating
  • Antonym: union - the act of joining or being joined together

Etymology

From Old French *sevrance*, from *sevrer* (to sever), from Latin *separare* (to separate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvɚəns / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərəns / (BrE)
the act of ending a connection or relationship; the process of cutting off or separating something
the act of cutting off or separating

Examples

The company announced the severance of ties with its overseas partner.
After the severance of the rope, the boat drifted away from the dock.
The severance of diplomatic relations led to further tensions between the two countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Old French 'sevrance', from 'sevrer' meaning 'to separate'.
