Glucotrol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːkəˌtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡluːkəˌtrəʊl / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used to treat type 2 diabetes by increasing the release of insulin from the pancreas.
brand name antidiabetic drug
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Glucotrol to help manage my blood sugar levels.
After starting Glucotrol, she noticed her glucose readings were more stable.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking Glucotrol.

Related Terms
