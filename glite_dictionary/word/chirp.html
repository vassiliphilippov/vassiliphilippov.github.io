chirp

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝːp / (AmE), / tʃɜːp / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched, sharp sound made especially by small birds or insects
a short, sharp bird or insect sound

Examples

At dawn, the chirp of sparrows filled the garden with a pleasant melody.
We could hear the chirp of crickets coming through the open window at night.
The sudden chirp startled her as a robin flew past her head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweet - a short, high-pitched sound, especially by a small bird

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, dating to the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ tʃɝːp / (AmE), / tʃɜːp / (BrE)
(of a small bird, insect, or electronic device) to make a short, high-pitched sound or series of sounds.
make a short, high-pitched sound

Examples

The sparrows began to chirp loudly at dawn.
A cricket continued to chirp outside the open window all night.
Her phone gave a tiny chirp to signal a new message.

Synset Examples

the birds were chirping in the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweet - make a short, high-pitched sound (especially of birds)

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01054173-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tʃɝːp / (AmE), / tʃɜːp / (BrE)
(of a small bird or insect) to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound or to sing with such sounds.
make short, high-pitched sounds (bird/insect)

Examples

Birds began to chirp as the sun rose over the meadow.
You can hear crickets chirping loudly on warm summer nights.
The canary chirped cheerfully from its cage in the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweet - to make a short, high-pitched sound (bird)
  • Synonym: chirrup - to make repeated chirping sounds, typically of birds

Etymology

Imitative origin, resembling the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01734544-v
Synset POS: v
