metacyesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəsaɪˈisɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəsaɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
a pregnancy in which the embryo develops outside the uterus, typically in a fallopian tube or elsewhere in the abdomen
pregnancy outside the uterus
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with metacyesis after imaging showed the embryo developing in the fallopian tube.
Early detection of metacyesis is crucial to prevent serious complications for the mother.
Doctors explained that metacyesis can pose significant health risks if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ectopic pregnancy - a pregnancy that occurs outside the uterus

Etymology

From Greek meta- (beyond) + kyesis (pregnancy).
