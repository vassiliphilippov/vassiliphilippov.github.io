bombastically

1. ADV

/ bɑmˈbæstɪkli / (AmE), / bɒmˈbæstɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is pompous, pretentious, or grandiose, often using inflated language.
in a pompous or grandiose manner

Examples

The CEO spoke bombastically about the company's minor achievements.
His novels are known for being written bombastically, often overflowing with grand language.
She decorated her house bombastically for the holidays, leaving nothing understated.

Synset Examples

the building was bombastically spacious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastic - using high-sounding but unimportant language
  • Antonym: plainly - in a simple or unadorned manner

Etymology

From 'bombastic' + '-ally', with 'bombastic' from French 'bombastique' and Italian 'bombace' (cotton padding).

2. ADV

/ bɑmˈbæstɪkli / (AmE), / bɒmˈbæstɪkli / (BrE)
In a pompous or pretentious manner, using inflated or overblown language.
in a pompous or pretentious way

Examples

The professor spoke bombastically, using elaborate words that confused the audience.
She always presents her ideas bombastically, making them sound more important than they really are.
He wrote bombastically in his essay, exaggerating his vocabulary to impress the reader.

Synset Examples

he lectured bombastically about his theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastic - using inflated and pretentious language
  • Antonym: plainly - in a simple or clear manner, without embellishment

Etymology

From 'bombastic', which comes from French 'bombastique' and Italian 'bombace' (cotton padding), referring figuratively to padded or inflated language.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
