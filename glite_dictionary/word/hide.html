hide

1. NOUN

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, especially a large one, treated and prepared for use as leather
animal skin used as leather

Examples

The couch was upholstered in genuine cow hide.
They used buffalo hide to make sturdy boots and saddles.
The hunter stretched the deer hide in the sun to dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leather - material made from the tanned skin of an animal

Etymology

Old English hȳd (skin, cover), from Proto-Germanic *hūdiz.

3. NOUN

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, especially when it has been removed for use as leather
animal skin used as leather

Examples

The bison's hide was thick and tough, used by Native Americans for shelter and clothing.
The craftsman worked carefully to tan the hide before turning it into a saddle.
Snake hide is often used for making stylish boots and belts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the outer layer of tissue covering the body of an animal
  • Degree Higher: leather - material made from the tanned hide of an animal

Etymology

Old English hȳd, related to Dutch huid and German Haut, all meaning 'skin'.
