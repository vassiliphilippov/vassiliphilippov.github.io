cheiloschisis

1. NOUN

/ kaɪloʊˈskɪsɪs / (AmE), / kaɪləʊˈskɪsɪs / (BrE)
A congenital split or cleft in the middle of the upper lip.
a congenital cleft in the upper lip
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the infant was born with cheiloschisis and would need corrective surgery.
Prenatal screening can sometimes detect cheiloschisis before birth.
Children with cheiloschisis may face challenges with feeding and speech if not treated early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleft lip - a congenital split in the upper lip

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'schisis' (split).
