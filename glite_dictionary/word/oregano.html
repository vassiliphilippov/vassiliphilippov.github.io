oregano

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛɡəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ɒˈrɛɡənəʊ / (BrE)
A Mediterranean herb with aromatic, pungent leaves used to flavor meats, stews, soups, and other dishes.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

Sprinkle a pinch of oregano over the pizza before baking for extra flavor.
She grows fresh oregano in her garden to use in salads and sauces.
The recipe calls for dried oregano to season the grilled chicken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marjoram - an aromatic herb related to oregano, used in cooking

Etymology

From Spanish orégano and Latin orīganum, from Greek origanon (wild marjoram).

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɛɡəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ɒˈrɛɡəˌnəʊ / (BrE)
a perennial Eurasian herb of the mint family with aromatic leaves, used in cooking, especially Mediterranean dishes
aromatic culinary herb

Examples

Fresh oregano gives this pasta sauce a distinctive, robust flavor.
She sprinkled chopped oregano on top of the pizza before baking it.
The chef prefers dried oregano when making traditional Greek salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marjoram - a closely related aromatic herb, sometimes called 'sweet marjoram'

Etymology

From Spanish orégano and Latin orīganum, from Greek orīganon, meaning 'brightness of the mountain'.
