criterion

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈtɪəriən / (AmE), / kraɪˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
a standard or principle by which something is judged, decided, or evaluated
standard for judging or evaluating

Examples

The most important criterion for admission is academic excellence.
We need to establish clear criteria to select the right candidate.
Cost is one criterion we should consider when making our decision.

Synset Examples

the schools comply with federal standards
they set the measure for all subsequent work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - something used as a measure, norm, or model
  • Synonym: measure - a reference point to compare other things
  • Synonym: criteria - plural form of criterion; standards for judging

Etymology

From Greek *kriterion* (means of judging), from *krinein* (to judge, decide).

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈtɪriən / (AmE), / kraɪˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
a principle or standard by which something may be judged or decided
a standard for judging or deciding

Examples

Academic achievement is often used as a criterion for college admission.
The main criterion for selection will be the applicant's ability to work independently.
A key criterion when buying a new car is its fuel efficiency.

Synset Examples

they live by the standards of their community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - a level of quality or attainment used as a measure, norm, or model
  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference against which things may be compared

Etymology

From Greek *kriterion* (means for judging), from *krinein* (to judge, decide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05933552-n
Synset POS: n
