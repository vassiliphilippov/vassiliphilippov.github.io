trimness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being neat, orderly, or well arranged in appearance or condition
neatness or orderliness

Examples

The trimness of her desk impressed her coworkers.
He takes great pride in the trimness of his garden.
Regular maintenance ensures the trimness of the property.

Synset Examples

in good trim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being neat and tidy
  • Antonym: untidiness - the lack of neatness or order

Etymology

From 'trim' (neat, orderly) + -ness, forming a noun.
