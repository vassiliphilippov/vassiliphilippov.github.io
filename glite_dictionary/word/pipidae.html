Pipidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪpɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic frogs known for lacking tongues, including species such as the African clawed frog.
family of tongueless aquatic frogs

Examples

Members of the Pipidae family are fully aquatic and lack tongues.
The African clawed frog, a well-known amphibian, belongs to the Pipidae.
Researchers study Pipidae because of their unique evolutionary adaptations among frogs.

Etymology

From New Latin Pipidae, based on the type genus Pipia (now Pipidae) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪpɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic, tongueless frogs found mainly in sub-Saharan Africa and South America
a family of tongueless aquatic frogs

Examples

The Pipidae are unique among frogs for lacking tongues entirely.
Scientists study the Pipidae to learn about aquatic adaptation in amphibians.
Species in the Pipidae family include the well-known African clawed frog.

Etymology

From New Latin *Pipidae*, based on the type genus *Pipa*.
