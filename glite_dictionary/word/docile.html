docile

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːsəl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsaɪl, ˈdɒsaɪl / (BrE)
quiet and easily taught, led, or controlled; ready to accept instruction
easily taught or managed

Examples

The docile puppy followed every command given by its owner.
Unlike her wild brothers, she remained docile throughout the visit to the vet.
Teachers appreciate students who are docile and eager to learn.

Synset Examples

the docile masses of an enslaved nation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient - willing to do what one is told
  • Antonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline

Etymology

From Latin 'docilis' (easily taught), from 'docere' (to teach).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsaɪl / (BrE)
ready and willing to be taught; easy to train or manage
ready and willing to be taught

Examples

The docile students listened attentively to every instruction their teacher gave.
Golden retrievers are often considered docile and easy to train compared to other breeds.
He was a docile child who rarely questioned what he was told.

Synset Examples

docile pupils eager for instruction
teachable youngsters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teachable - willing and able to learn or be taught
  • Synonym: obedient - willing to do as told by others
  • Antonym: intractable - difficult to manage or control

Etymology

From Latin docilis (easily taught), from docēre (to teach).
