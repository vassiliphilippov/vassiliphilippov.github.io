suspension

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɛnʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən / (BrE)
the system of springs, shock absorbers, and other devices that connects a vehicle's wheels to its chassis, allowing for a smoother ride and better handling
vehicle system of springs and shock absorbers

Examples

The car's suspension was damaged after driving over a deep pothole.
Upgrading the suspension can improve both comfort and handling on rough roads.
Mechanics spent hours working on the truck's suspension system to ensure a smooth ride.

Etymology

From Latin 'suspensio(n-)' meaning 'the act of hanging up'.

/ səˈspɛnʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən / (BrE)

5. NOUN

/ səˈspɛnʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən / (BrE)
a mixture in which fine particles are dispersed throughout a fluid, supported by buoyancy without dissolving
mixture of fine particles in a fluid

Examples

The muddy water was a suspension of clay and silt particles.
Blood is a complex fluid containing cells in suspension.
The laboratory test required a suspension of the drug in saline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solution - a mixture where a substance is dissolved in another
  • Degree Lower: colloid - a mixture where very small particles are evenly dispersed in another substance

Etymology

From Latin suspendere (to hang up).
