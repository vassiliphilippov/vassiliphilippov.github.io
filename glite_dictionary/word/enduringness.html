enduringness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʊrɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of lasting for a long time or resisting stress, wear, or force; durability.
the quality of being durable or long-lasting

Examples

The enduringness of the bridge is a testament to its excellent construction.
Scientists are studying the enduringness of new materials under extreme conditions.
Her enduringness in the face of adversity inspired everyone around her.

Synset Examples

they advertised the durability of their products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From 'endure' (Middle English, from Old French 'endurer', from Latin 'indurare' – to harden) + '-ness'.
