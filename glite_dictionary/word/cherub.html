cherub

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəb / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəb / (BrE)
A sweet, innocent-looking baby or young child, often depicted as beautiful and angelic.
a sweet, innocent baby or child

Examples

The baby slept peacefully, looking like a cherub with rosy cheeks.
At the family picnic, her daughter ran around laughing, a little cherub with golden curls.
Everyone admired the newborn, calling him a perfect cherub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angel - a spiritual being regarded as a messenger of God

Etymology

From Late Latin 'cherub', from Hebrew 'kerūv', originally referring to an angelic being.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəb / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəb / (BrE)
A type of angel, typically depicted as a chubby, healthy-looking child with wings, considered an angel of the second order, traditionally associated with knowledge.
angel of the second order, often shown as a winged child

Examples

In Renaissance paintings, a cherub is often shown with a sweet, innocent expression.
The sculpture of a cherub adorned the garden fountain, adding a touch of whimsy.
She called her newborn baby a little cherub because of his round cheeks and angelic smile.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seraph - an angelic being of the highest order
  • Degree Higher: angel - a spiritual being acting as a messenger of God
  • Synonym: putto - a figure of a chubby male child, especially in Renaissance art

Etymology

From Hebrew kĕrūb, via Latin cherub and Greek cheroub.
