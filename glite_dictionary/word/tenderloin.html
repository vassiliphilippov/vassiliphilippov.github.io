tenderloin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛndɚˌlɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛndəˌlɔɪn / (BrE)
a part of a city, especially in some American cities, notorious for vice, crime, and corruption
urban area known for vice and crime
american

Examples

The police increased their patrols in the tenderloin to address rising crime.
She grew up near the tenderloin and often heard stories about its notorious past.
The city's tenderloin was once filled with gambling dens and illicit clubs.

Etymology

Originally used for a district in New York City, supposedly referring to the 'richness' (as in salary or bribes) of police serving there.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛndərˌlɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛndəˌlɔɪn / (BrE)
A cut of tender meat from the loin muscle along either side of the spine, often considered the most tender part of the animal.
a very tender cut of meat

Examples

The chef prepared a perfectly cooked tenderloin for the main course.
Beef tenderloin is often the highlight of a special dinner menu.
He ordered the grilled pork tenderloin with a side of roasted vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filet mignon - a small, thick piece cut from the tenderloin

Etymology

From 'tender' + 'loin', referring to its tenderness and location.
