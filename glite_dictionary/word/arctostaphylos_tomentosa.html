Arctostaphylos tomentosa

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrktoʊˈstæfɪloʊs toʊˈmɛntoʊsə / (AmE), / ɑːktəˈstæfɪləs təʊˈmɛntəʊsə / (BrE)
an erect, openly branched shrub native to California, whose young twigs are covered with woolly hairs
a Californian woolly-twigged shrub

Examples

The Arctostaphylos tomentosa shrub thrives on the coastal hills of California.
Botanists studied the woolly stems of Arctostaphylos tomentosa for their unique adaptation.
Many gardens feature Arctostaphylos tomentosa because of its drought resistance and attractive foliage.

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + staphylē (grape), referring to the fruit, and Latin tomentosus (covered with hairs).
