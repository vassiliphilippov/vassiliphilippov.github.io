expiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making amends or reparation for wrongdoing or guilt; compensation for a wrong
making amends for a wrong

Examples

The ceremony was performed as an act of expiation for past misdeeds.
He believed that only through expiation could he find peace for his sins.
Her donation to charity was seen as a form of expiation for her earlier mistakes.

Synset Examples

we were unable to get satisfaction from the local store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atonement - the act of making up for a sin or wrongdoing
  • Synonym: reparation - the making of amends for a wrong one has done

Etymology

From Latin expiatio (atonement, purification), from expiare (to make amends).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making amends or reparation for guilt or wrongdoing, especially as a way of appeasing a deity
atonement for wrongdoing or sin

Examples

He performed an act of expiation to seek forgiveness for his misdeeds.
The ritual served as a symbolic expiation for the community's past transgressions.
Through charity and prayer, she hoped to find expiation for her sins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atonement - the action of making amends for a wrong or sin
  • Degree Lower: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven

Etymology

From Latin expiatio (atonement), from expiare (to atone, to purify).
