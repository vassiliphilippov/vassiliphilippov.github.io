seasonable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːzənəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəbl / (BrE)
occurring or done at an appropriate or proper time; timely
occurring at an appropriate time

Examples

We waited until a seasonable hour to begin the ceremony.
Her advice proved seasonable as the situation quickly escalated.
A seasonable rain helped the crops grow after a long drought.

Synset Examples

a timely warning
with timely treatment the patient has a good chance of recovery
a seasonable time for discussion
the book's publication was well timed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timely - done at a suitable or opportune moment
  • Antonym: untimely - occurring at an inappropriate or unsuitable time

Etymology

From Middle English 'sesonable', from 'season' + '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːzənəbl / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəbl / (BrE)
appropriate for or typical of a particular season; suitable or timely for the season
appropriate for the season

Examples

A seasonable frost covered the fields early in the morning.
She wore seasonable clothing to stay comfortable during the chilly autumn day.
The chef prepared seasonable dishes using fresh summer vegetables.

Synset Examples

a hard but seasonable frost
seasonable clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timely - happening at the right or suitable time; appropriate
  • Antonym: unseasonable - not usual or appropriate for the season

Etymology

From Middle English sesonable, from Old French saisonnable, from saison (season).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01497734-a
Synset POS: a
