humankind

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmənˌkaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənkaɪnd / (BrE)
all human beings considered collectively; the human race.
all human beings; the human race

Examples

Advances in technology have transformed the way humankind communicates and works.
She believes that protecting the environment is a duty of all humankind.
Throughout history, humankind has sought answers to profound questions about existence.

Synset Examples

all the world loves a lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mankind - all human beings collectively; often considered less gender-neutral

Etymology

Formed from 'human' and 'kind' meaning 'type' or 'race'.
