Parus atricapillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛər.əs ˌæ.trɪ.kəˈpɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.rəs ˌæ.trɪ.kəˈpɪl.əs / (BrE)
A species of small North American chickadee with a distinctive black cap and bib.
black-capped chickadee species

Examples

The Parus atricapillus is easily recognized by its black cap and white cheeks.
During winter, flocks of Parus atricapillus visit backyard feeders for seeds.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Parus atricapillus in various forest environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-capped chickadee - common name for Parus atricapillus, a North American songbird

Etymology

From Latin: 'Parus' meaning 'titmouse' and 'atricapillus' meaning 'black-capped'.
