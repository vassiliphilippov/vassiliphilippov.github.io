James Joyce

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdʒɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdʒɔɪs / (BrE)
(1882–1941) An influential Irish writer known for his literary innovations, particularly stream of consciousness narrative.
influential Irish modernist writer

Examples

Many consider James Joyce's 'Ulysses' to be one of the greatest novels of the twentieth century.
The literary techniques pioneered by James Joyce continue to influence writers today.
In her thesis, she analyzed the stream of consciousness style developed by James Joyce.
