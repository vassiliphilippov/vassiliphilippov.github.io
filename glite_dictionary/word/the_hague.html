The Hague

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈheɪɡ / (AmE), / ðə ˈheɪɡ / (BrE)
a city in the western Netherlands, seat of the Dutch government and known as a center for international law and diplomacy
Dutch city, center for international law

Examples

Many international organizations have their headquarters in The Hague.
The war crimes tribunal convened in The Hague to hear the case.
She plans to visit The Hague during her trip to the Netherlands.

Synset Examples

Many treaties are signed in The Hague every year.
The international court is located in The Hague.
She traveled to The Hague for the peace conference.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Den Haag', short for '’s-Gravenhage', meaning 'the Count’s hedge' or 'enclosure'.
