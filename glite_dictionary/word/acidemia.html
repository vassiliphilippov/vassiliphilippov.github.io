acidemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˈdiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈdiːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the blood has an increased concentration of hydrogen ions, resulting in a pH below 7
high acidity in the blood (medical condition)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with acidemia after laboratory tests showed an abnormally low blood pH.
Prompt treatment is necessary for severe acidemia to prevent organ damage.
Metabolic disorders can often lead to acidemia in critically ill patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalemia - a condition in which the blood has a reduced concentration of hydrogen ions (pH above 7)

Etymology

From Latin acidus (sour) + -emia (condition of the blood).
