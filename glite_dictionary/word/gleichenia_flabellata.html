Gleichenia flabellata

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈkiːniə fləˌbɛlətə / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˈkiːniə fləˈbɛlətə / (BrE)
A large fern native to Australasia, characterized by fan-shaped, repeatedly forked fronds; sometimes classified within the genus Gleichenia.
Australasian fern species with fanlike fronds

Examples

The Gleichenia flabellata thrives in moist, shaded areas of the Australian bushland.
Botanists often admire the distinctive forked fronds of Gleichenia flabellata.
A specimen of Gleichenia flabellata was displayed in the botanical garden's fern collection.

Etymology

From the genus name Gleichenia, honoring German botanist W.F. von Gleichen, and Latin flabellatus ('fan-shaped').
