Sulidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large seabirds including gannets and boobies, characterized by long pointed wings and diving habits
family of seabirds: gannets and boobies

Examples

The Sulidae are well known for their spectacular plunge-diving to catch fish.
Members of the family Sulidae include both gannets and boobies found in oceans worldwide.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of several Sulidae species on the remote island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gannet - large seabird in the Sulidae family
  • Synonym: booby - seabird in the Sulidae family, known for its lack of fear

Etymology

From New Latin Sulidae, from type genus Sula (gannet, booby) + -idae (family suffix).
