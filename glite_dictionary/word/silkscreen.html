silkscreen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌskriːn / (BrE)
a print made by forcing ink through a fine mesh screen that has areas blocked off by a stencil, creating a design on the surface below
print made with a mesh stencil process

Examples

The artist showcased a vibrant silkscreen of a city skyline at the gallery.
She learned how to create a silkscreen in her printmaking class last semester.
A limited-edition silkscreen of the band's logo was sold to fans at the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serigraph - a print made by the same screen printing technique

Etymology

From 'silk' (the original mesh material) + 'screen'.

WordNet ID: oewn-04226785-n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪlkˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌskriːn / (BrE)
to print or produce an image or design by means of a silkscreen process.
to print using a silkscreen process

Examples

The artist decided to silkscreen her designs onto a series of T-shirts for the exhibition.
Posters for the event were silkscreened in bright, bold colors at the local print shop.
He spent all afternoon silkscreening limited edition prints for his loyal customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen-print - to print by forcing ink through a prepared screen of fine material

Etymology

Derived from 'silk' and 'screen', referencing the original use of silk mesh in the printing technique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01753362-v
Synset POS: v
