ton

1. NOUN

/ tʌn / (AmE), / tʌn / (BrE)
A British unit of weight equal to 2,240 pounds (1,016 kilograms).
British weight unit (2,240 pounds)
british

Examples

A shipping container can weigh more than one ton when fully loaded.
The bridge was designed to carry vehicles of up to fifty tons.
That old anchor weighs a ton by British standards, which is 2,240 pounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ton (US) - A US unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds

Etymology

From Old French *tonne* (cask, tun); related to the measure's origins as the weight of a tun of wine.

2. NOUN

/ tʌn / (AmE), / tʌn / (BrE)
A unit of weight in the United States equal to 2,000 pounds (about 907 kilograms).
US unit of weight (2,000 pounds)
american

Examples

The truck was capable of hauling one ton of material at a time.
Each shipment weighed over five tons, making logistics more complex.
In the US, a ton is commonly used for measuring large weights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tonne - metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 kilograms
  • Antonym: long ton - British unit of weight equal to 2,240 pounds

Etymology

From Old French *tonne* (cask, barrel), originally referring to a large cask of specific capacity.
