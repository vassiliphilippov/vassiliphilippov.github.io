probabilistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌproʊbəˈbɪlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒbəˈbɪlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, involving, or based on probability, especially as opposed to certainty or determinism.
relating to probability

Examples

Many probabilistic models are used to predict the spread of diseases.
Her approach to decision making is highly probabilistic, relying on likelihood rather than certainty.
The outcome of the experiment was explained using probabilistic reasoning.

Synset Examples

probabilistic quantum theory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deterministic - relating to determinism; opposite of probabilistic

Etymology

From probability + -istic, modeled after probability.

2. ADJ

/ ˌproʊbəˌbɪˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒbəˌbɪˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the doctrine of probabilism in Roman Catholic philosophy, which holds that in matters of moral uncertainty, it is acceptable to follow a probable opinion even if the opposite is more probable.
of or relating to Catholic probabilism

Examples

In Jesuit debate, a probabilistic approach guided many confessional decisions.
The bishop outlined the probabilistic stance when dealing with moral uncertainty in doctrine.
He wrote extensively on probabilistic methods in canon law and the confessional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probabilism - the Roman Catholic doctrine allowing a less probable opinion to be followed in moral uncertainty

Etymology

From Medieval Latin probabilisticus, relating to probabilism; ultimately from Latin probabilis (provable, credible).
