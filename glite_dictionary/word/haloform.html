haloform

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a chemical compound with the formula CHX₃, where X is a halogen atom, such as chloroform or bromoform
a type of trihalogenated methane compound
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized a new haloform for testing its antiseptic properties.
Chloroform is a well-known example of a haloform used in the past as an anesthetic.
Researchers found traces of multiple haloforms in the treated water sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloroform - a haloform with three chlorine atoms
  • Synonym: bromoform - a haloform with three bromine atoms

Etymology

From halo- (meaning 'halogen') + form (from 'formic acid').
