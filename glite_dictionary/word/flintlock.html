flintlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪntˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈflɪntˌlɒk / (BrE)
an old type of gunlock using a piece of flint to spark and ignite the powder charge in firearms
old firearm mechanism using flint for ignition
archaic

Examples

The museum displays an 18th-century flintlock alongside other historic weapons.
He loaded the flintlock carefully, readying it for the re-enactment.
Collectors value a well-preserved flintlock for its craftsmanship and history.

Etymology

From 'flint' (a hard stone) and 'lock' (mechanism for firing a gun); first recorded in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɪnt.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈflɪnt.lɒk / (BrE)
an old type of firearm that uses a flint striking ignition mechanism, especially common before the 19th century
old firearm with flint ignition

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully engraved flintlock pistol from the 18th century.
Soldiers loaded gunpowder and a lead ball into their flintlock rifles before battle.
He handed down his grandfather's old flintlock as a family heirloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musket - a type of long gun formerly used by infantry
  • Degree Lower: matchlock - an earlier type of firearm with a different ignition mechanism
  • Degree Higher: percussion cap - a later firearm ignition mechanism replacing flintlock

Etymology

From 'flint' + 'lock', referring to the flint used to spark the ignition and the gun's firing mechanism.
