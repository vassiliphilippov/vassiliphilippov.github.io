third-dimensionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝːd.daɪˌmɛn.ʃəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌθɜːd.daɪˌmɛn.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the property or quality of having three dimensions, such as length, width, and depth
the quality of having three dimensions

Examples

The third-dimensionality of the sculpture made it stand out from the flat paintings.
Virtual reality strives to enhance the sense of third-dimensionality for users.
Some artists experiment with depth to create third-dimensionality on a two-dimensional canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensionality - the state of being three-dimensional
  • Degree Lower: dimensionality - the number of dimensions something has

Etymology

Formed from 'third' + 'dimensionality', relating to three-dimensional space.
