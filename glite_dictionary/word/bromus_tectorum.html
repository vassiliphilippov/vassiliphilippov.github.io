Bromus tectorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊməs tɛkˈtoʊrəm / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊməs tɛkˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of annual or winter annual grass native to the Mediterranean, known for its softly hairy leaves and invasive nature in other regions.
a species of annual Mediterranean grass

Examples

Bromus tectorum has spread rapidly across western North America, becoming a significant invasive species.
Researchers are studying the effects of Bromus tectorum on native plant communities.
Eradication of Bromus tectorum requires careful management to prevent reestablishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheatgrass - common name for Bromus tectorum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Bromus', the genus name, and 'tectorum', meaning 'of roofs', referring to its growth on roofs in its native range.
