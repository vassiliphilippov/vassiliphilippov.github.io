Loxodonta

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɑksəˈdɑn.tə / (AmE), / ˌlɒk.səˈdɒn.tə / (BrE)
A genus of elephants native to Africa, including the African bush elephant and African forest elephant.
genus of African elephants

Examples

The African bush elephant belongs to the genus Loxodonta.
Scientists recently discovered new genetic distinctions within Loxodonta.
Unlike the Asian elephants, members of Loxodonta have larger ears and differently shaped teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant - a large herbivorous mammal with a trunk, of which Loxodonta is a genus

Etymology

From Greek loxos (slanting, oblique) and odont- (tooth), referring to the shape of the animal's teeth.

2. NOUN

/ loʊkˈsɑːdəntə / (AmE), / lɒkˈsɒdəntə / (BrE)
A genus of elephants native to Africa, including the African bush elephant and the African forest elephant.
genus of African elephants

Examples

The African bush elephant belongs to the genus Loxodonta.
Scientists have identified two species within the genus Loxodonta: the bush and the forest elephant.
Fossil remains confirm that Loxodonta has existed in Africa for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elephas - a genus of elephants, including the Asian elephant

Etymology

From Greek loxos (slanting) + odous, odont- (tooth), referencing the slanted enamel ridges of their molar teeth.
