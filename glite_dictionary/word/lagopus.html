Lagopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæɡəpəs / (AmE), / ˈlæɡəpəs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the grouse family, commonly known as ptarmigans, found in arctic and subarctic regions
genus of birds known as ptarmigans

Examples

The genus Lagopus includes several species adapted to cold environments.
Researchers observed the molting patterns of Lagopus in the tundra.
Members of the Lagopus genus are well-camouflaged against snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ptarmigan - a bird of the genus Lagopus, especially in arctic and subarctic regions

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *lagōpous*, meaning 'hare-foot', referring to the bird's feathered feet.
