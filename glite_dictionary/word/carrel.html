carrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəl / (AmE), / ˈkarəl / (BrE)
a small individual study area in a library, often enclosed or partially enclosed, designed for one person to work or study undisturbed
small private study area in a library

Examples

She preferred to study in a carrel where it was quiet and she could focus.
Each carrel in the library has its own lamp and power outlet.
Graduate students can reserve a carrel for the entire semester if needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubicle - a small partitioned-off area of a room, such as in an office or library

Etymology

From Middle French carrel (enclosure, enclosure in a cloister), from Latin quadrum (square).
