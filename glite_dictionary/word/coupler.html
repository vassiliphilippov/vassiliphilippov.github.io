coupler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌplɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌplə / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to connect the ends of adjacent objects, such as wagons, pipes, or cables
device connecting adjacent objects

Examples

The train's coupler was securely fastened to link the carriages together.
You need a special coupler to join the two garden hoses.
Technicians inspected the coupler to ensure it could handle the heavy load.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connector - device for joining two things together

Etymology

From couple + -er, denoting an agent or device that couples.
