zoospore

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈzəʊˌspɔː / (BrE)
An asexual spore in certain algae and fungi that is capable of movement using flagella.
motile asexual spore in some algae and fungi
medical

Examples

The zoospore swims through water using its flagella before settling to grow into a new organism.
Under the microscope, we observed each zoospore actively moving toward a nutrient source.
During certain stages of their lifecycle, water molds produce zoospores that disperse rapidly in moist environments.

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) + spora (seed, sowing).
