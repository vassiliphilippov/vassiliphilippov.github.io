Sphyrapicus

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈræpɪkəs / (AmE), / sfɪˈræpɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of woodpeckers in the family Picidae, commonly known as sapsuckers, native to North America
genus of woodpeckers (sapsuckers)

Examples

Four species of Sphyrapicus are found in North America, each with different migration patterns.
The yellow-bellied sapsucker is a member of the Sphyrapicus genus.
Researchers tagged several Sphyrapicus individuals to study their feeding habits in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapsucker - a type of woodpecker in the genus Sphyrapicus

Etymology

From Greek sphura (hammer) + pikos (woodpecker).
