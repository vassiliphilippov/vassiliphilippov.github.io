shucks

1. INTJ

/ ʃʌks / (AmE), / ʃʌks / (BrE)
used to express mild disappointment, embarrassment, or irritation.
expression of mild disappointment or irritation
informal

Examples

Shucks, I was hoping to win the game tonight.
"Shucks, I forgot to bring my wallet," he said with a sigh.
Shucks, that's too bad about your plans getting canceled.

2. NOUN

/ ʃʌks / (AmE), / ʃʌks / (BrE)
Things regarded as having little or no value; often used in the phrase 'not worth shucks'.
things of little value
informal

Examples

He said their opinions weren't worth shucks to him.
The old bike isn't worth shucks anymore.
These coins are so damaged they're not worth shucks.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - something of no value; used in 'not worth a damn'
  • Synonym: red cent - something of little value; used in the phrase 'not worth a red cent'

Etymology

From 'shuck', meaning the husk of an ear of corn, regarded as worthless.
