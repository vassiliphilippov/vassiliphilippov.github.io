Corinth

1. PROPN

/ kəˈrɪnθ / (AmE), / kəˈrɪnθ / (BrE)
a modern Greek port near the site of the ancient city, historically significant and second only to Athens in ancient times
modern Greek city and port

Examples

The ancient ruins near Corinth attract thousands of tourists each year.
After sailing through the canal, the ferry docked at Corinth.
Archaeologists continue to uncover artifacts in the modern city of Corinth.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κόρινθος (Kórinthos).
