alpha-interferon

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə ˌɪntərˈfɪroʊn / (AmE), / ˌælfə ˌɪntəˈfɪərəʊn / (BrE)
a type of interferon produced naturally or manufactured for medical use, known for its effects on the immune system and its antiviral and anti-cancer properties
a type of immune-regulating protein used in medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed alpha-interferon to help boost the patient's immune response.
Scientists studied the effects of alpha-interferon on different types of viral infections.
Treatment with alpha-interferon can sometimes cause flu-like symptoms in patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interferon - a protein produced by cells that helps regulate the immune response

Etymology

From 'alpha' (the first letter of the Greek alphabet, used to indicate a variant) and 'interferon' (a protein produced by cells in response to viruses).
