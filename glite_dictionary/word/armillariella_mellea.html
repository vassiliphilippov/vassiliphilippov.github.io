Armillariella mellea

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌmɪləˌriˈɛlə məˈliːə / (AmE), / ɑːˌmɪləˌriːˈɛlə mɛˈliːə / (BrE)
a species of honey-colored edible mushroom, commonly found near tree roots in late summer and fall; should not be eaten raw
a species of edible honey-colored mushroom

Examples

The woods were filled with Armillariella mellea growing around the roots of dying trees.
You should never eat Armillariella mellea raw, as it can cause stomach upset.
Foragers often seek out Armillariella mellea in the fall for its rich, earthy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey fungus - common name for Armillariella mellea, an edible mushroom

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Armillariella' and species epithet 'mellea' meaning 'honey-colored.'
