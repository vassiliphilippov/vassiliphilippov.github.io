whine

1. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
to complain in a feeble or petulant way, often with a high-pitched voice
complain in a feeble or petulant way

Examples

She tends to whine whenever she doesn't get her way.
Stop whining and start looking for a solution to the problem.
The children whined about staying indoors all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet, annoyed way
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old English hwīnan (to whiz, whistle) and related to Old Norse hvīna (to whiz, whistle).

2. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
(of an object) to move making a high, continuous, and often annoying sound.
move making a high, continuous sound

Examples

The bullets whined overhead as we ducked behind cover.
A shell whined through the air before exploding nearby.
The saw blade whined as it cut through the metal pipe.

Synset Examples

The bullets were whining past us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - to make a continuous low sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01843222-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
(of a machine, object, or animal) to make a long, high-pitched, and usually unpleasant sound.
make a high-pitched, unpleasant noise

Examples

The electric saw began to whine as it cut through the metal.
Sometimes my computer's fan will whine loudly when it's overheating.
We heard the wind whine through the narrow gaps in the window frame.

Synset Examples

The door creaked when I opened it slowly
My car engine makes a whining noise

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: buzz - make a continuous humming sound
  • Degree Higher: squeal - make a long, very high and loud cry or sound

Etymology

From Old English hwīnan (to whistle or whizz).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02176108-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
an act of complaining in a high-pitched, plaintive or irritating manner
a plaintive or irritating complaint

Examples

Stop your whine and help solve the problem instead of complaining about it.
Her constant whine about the weather annoyed everyone in the office.
There was a whine in his voice as he protested the new rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: grumble - a low muttered complaint
  • Antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old English hwīnan (to whiz, whistle, or squeal).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
to complain or speak in a high-pitched, tearful, or irritating manner, especially in a prolonged way
complain in a tearful or high-pitched manner

Examples

The child began to whine when he didn't get his way.
I wish you would stop whining about your problems and do something about them.
She whined in a tearful voice that life was unfair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet, low voice
  • Antonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction

Etymology

Perhaps of imitative origin, or from Old English *hwīnan* (to whiz, whistle).
