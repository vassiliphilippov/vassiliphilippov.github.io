venae epigastricae superiores

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.ni ˌɛp.ɪˌɡæsˈtrɪ.ki suːˈpɪə.riˌɚz / (AmE), / ˈviː.ni ˌɛp.ɪˌɡæsˈtrɪ.ki suːˈpɪə.riˌɒːz / (BrE)
veins that accompany the superior epigastric artery, located in the abdominal wall.
veins following the superior epigastric artery
medical

Examples

The venae epigastricae superiores play a crucial role in draining blood from the upper abdominal wall.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the venae epigastricae superiores.
Enlargement of the venae epigastricae superiores can indicate a problem with blood flow in the abdomen.

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins), 'epigastricae' (of the region above the stomach), 'superiores' (upper).
