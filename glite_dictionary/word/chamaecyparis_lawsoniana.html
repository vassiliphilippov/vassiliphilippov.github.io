Chamaecyparis lawsoniana

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːsɪˈpeərɪs ˌlɔːsəˈnaɪənə / (AmE), / kəˌmiːsɪˈpeərɪs ˌlɔː.səˈnaɪ.ən.ə / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to western North America, also called Lawson cypress or Port Orford cedar, commonly grown for its timber and ornamental value
large North American evergreen tree

Examples

The wood of the Chamaecyparis lawsoniana is highly valued for its durability and aroma.
Landscapers often choose Chamaecyparis lawsoniana for its ornamental beauty in large gardens.
Researchers have studied disease resistance in Chamaecyparis lawsoniana populations along the Pacific coast.

Etymology

From Greek *chamai* (on the ground) + *kyparissos* (cypress), and from the name of Lawson, a 19th-century Scottish botanist.
