spirochaete

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪroʊˌkiːt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəˌkiːt / (BrE)
a type of flexible, spiral-shaped bacterium, some of which cause diseases in humans and animals
spiral-shaped bacterium, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Lyme disease is caused by a spirochaete transmitted through tick bites.
The scientist observed the characteristic spiral movement of the spirochaete under the microscope.
Some spirochaetes are harmless, but others can lead to serious health conditions such as syphilis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - a single-celled microorganism

Etymology

From German Spirochäte, from Greek speira (coil) + German Chäte from Greek khaite (hair).
