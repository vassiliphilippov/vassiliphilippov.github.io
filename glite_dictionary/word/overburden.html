overburden

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdən / (BrE)
the layer of soil and rock covering a mineral deposit, especially one that must be removed before mining can proceed
soil/rock layer above mineral deposits

Examples

Before mining could begin, the crew had to remove the thick overburden from the site.
The cost of stripping the overburden often determines whether a coal seam is economical to extract.
Excavators worked for weeks to clear the overburden and expose the mineral deposit beneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsoil - the layer of soil beneath the topsoil

Etymology

From 'over-' (above) + 'burden' (load or weight).

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdn / (BrE)
An excessive load or burden; something that is too heavy to be carried or endured.
an excessive burden

Examples

The financial crisis became an overburden for the already struggling company.
He felt the responsibilities of his new position were an overburden he could not manage.
Relieving mothers of their household overburden can improve family well-being.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: burden - a heavy load (literal or figurative)
  • Degree Lower: load - something to be carried, weight or responsibility

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'burden' (load, weight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03870532-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdn / (BrE)
to give someone too much work, responsibility, or pressure; to load someone or something with excessive demands.
give too much work or responsibility

Examples

The company often overburdens its employees with tight deadlines and extra shifts.
She felt overburdened by the number of projects assigned to her at once.
We must ensure we do not overburden the healthcare system during flu season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load with a heavy load or responsibility
  • Synonym: overload - to load with too great a burden or cargo
  • Antonym: to relieve - to remove or reduce a burden or responsibility

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + burden (from Old English byrthen, 'load, weight').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00752848-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdən / (BrE)
to give someone or something more weight, work, or responsibility than they can handle; to load with excess burden
to load with too much weight or responsibility

Examples

Don't overburden your employees with unnecessary paperwork and long meetings.
The old truck was overburdened with stones and broke down on the way.
She felt overburdened by her many responsibilities at home and work.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: burden - to load heavily, to place a burden on
  • Synonym: overload - to load with too great a load; to overfill

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + burden (Middle English, from Old English *byrthen*).
