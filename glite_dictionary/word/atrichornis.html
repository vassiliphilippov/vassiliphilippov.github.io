Atrichornis

1. NOUN

/ əˌtrɪkˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / əˌtrɪkˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
a genus of Australian birds comprising the scrub-birds, which are the only members of the family Atrichornithidae
genus of Australian scrub-birds

Examples

The genus Atrichornis is known for the elusive nature of its member species.
Scientists are conducting a survey to monitor Atrichornis populations in southeastern Australia.
Few people have ever seen an Atrichornis in the wild due to their secretive habits.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'a-' (without), 'thriks' (hair), and 'ornis' (bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˌætrɪˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌætrɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds native to Australia, forming the type genus of the family Atrichornithidae, commonly known as scrubbirds.
genus of Australian scrubbirds

Examples

The Atrichornis genus includes the rare noisy scrubbird of southwestern Australia.
Researchers have conducted extensive studies on the habitat of Atrichornis species.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the two known Atrichornis birds from extinction.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'trichos' (hair) + 'ornis' (bird), referring to birds with little or no bristle feathers.
