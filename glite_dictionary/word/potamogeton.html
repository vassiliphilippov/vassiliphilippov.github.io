Potamogeton

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˈdʒiːtɑn / (AmE), / pəˌtæməˈdʒiːtən / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic herbs commonly known as pondweeds, found in still or slow-moving waters, with leaves that usually float on the surface.
a genus of aquatic herbs (pondweeds)

Examples

The slow-moving river was filled with Potamogeton, its flat leaves floating on the surface.
Researchers documented several species of Potamogeton in the pond during the summer survey.
Many fish species use Potamogeton as shelter and breeding grounds in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Greek *potamos* (river) + *geiton* (neighbor), referring to plants growing near water.

Potamogeton species are often found in lakes and ponds across the Northern Hemisphere.
