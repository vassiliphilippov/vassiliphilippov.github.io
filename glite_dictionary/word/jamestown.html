Jamestown

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmstaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmstaʊn / (BrE)
a historic settlement in Virginia, established in 1607 as the first permanent English colony in America
first permanent English settlement in America
american

Examples

Many students visit Jamestown to learn about early American colonial life.
Jamestown was founded in 1607 and played a key role in the history of the United States.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts from the original Jamestown settlement along the James River.

Etymology

Named after King James I of England.
