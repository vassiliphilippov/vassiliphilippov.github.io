Passiflora laurifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˈflɔɹə ˌlɔːriəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə ˌlɔːrɪəˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical vine of the passionflower family, native to the West Indies and South America, cultivated for its sweet, yellow edible fruit.
a passionflower vine with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The Passiflora laurifolia produces a fragrant, sweet fruit popular in tropical regions.
Farmers in the Caribbean often cultivate Passiflora laurifolia for its high market value.
I tasted a delicious dessert made with the yellow fruit of Passiflora laurifolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionfruit - the fruit produced by various species of Passiflora

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Passiflora and species epithet laurifolia, meaning 'laurel-leaved'.
