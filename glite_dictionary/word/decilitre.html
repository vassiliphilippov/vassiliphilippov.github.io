decilitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to one tenth of a liter (0.1 liter).
metric unit of volume (0.1 liter)

Examples

The recipe calls for half a decilitre of olive oil.
A single decilitre of blood was enough for the laboratory tests.
He drank two decilitres of water before the race.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: liter - metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • Degree Lower: centilitre - metric unit of volume equal to one hundredth of a liter

Etymology

From French 'décilitre', from 'déci-' (ten) and 'litre' (liter).
