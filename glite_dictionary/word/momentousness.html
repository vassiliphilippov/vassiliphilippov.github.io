momentousness

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmɛntəsnəs / (AmE), / məʊˈmɛntəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being very important or significant.
great importance or significance

Examples

The momentousness of the decision was recognized by everyone in the room.
He failed to grasp the momentousness of the occasion and acted carelessly.
Historians often discuss the momentousness of that treaty for world politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importance - the quality of being important
  • Antonym: insignificance - the state of having little or no importance

Etymology

From 'momentous' + '-ness'; 'momentous' from 'moment' + '-ous', ultimately from Latin 'momentum'.
