shill

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪl / (AmE), / ʃɪl / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be a genuine customer or supporter, typically to promote a product, service, or event and encourage others to participate or buy
one who feigns interest to promote something

Examples

The casino hired a shill to pretend to win big and attract more gamblers.
At the auction, the shill kept upping the bids to create excitement.
It was obvious he was just a shill for the company, praising every product they sold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - a person who entices others into danger or traps
  • Synonym: stooge - a person who serves merely to support or assist others, especially in a subordinate or compliant way

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from early 20th-century US carnival slang.

2. VERB

/ ʃɪl / (AmE), / ʃɪl / (BrE)
to act as a decoy or promoter, especially by pretending to be a genuine customer in order to encourage others to participate or buy; to promote or endorse deceptively, often for personal gain
act as a decoy or deceptive promoter

Examples

He was accused of shilling for the company by posting fake positive reviews online.
During the auction, she shilled to drive up the price of the painting.
The celebrity was criticized for shilling products she clearly never used herself.

Synset Examples

This man shilled and bid for an expensive carpet during the auction in order to drive the price up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tout - to attempt to sell or promote something, often aggressively or slyly
