Ceylon

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈlɑn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn / (BrE)
the former name of the island country now known as Sri Lanka, located in the Indian Ocean off the southeastern coast of India
former name of Sri Lanka

Examples

During British colonial rule, Sri Lanka was known as Ceylon.
Many famous teas are still marketed under the name Ceylon.
She studied the history of Ceylon before its independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sri Lanka - the current name of the island nation in the Indian Ocean formerly known as Ceylon

Etymology

From Portuguese Ceilão, from Tamil Ilankai via Sinhalese Sinhala.

2. PROPN

/ siˈlɑn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn / (BrE)
the former name of Sri Lanka, an island republic in the Indian Ocean, known by this name until 1972
former name of Sri Lanka (island nation)

Examples

During the colonial era, Ceylon was a major exporter of tea and spices.
My grandmother was born in Ceylon before it became Sri Lanka.
The ancient cities of Ceylon attract historians and tourists alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sri Lanka - island nation in the Indian Ocean, formerly Ceylon

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Ceilão', from Sinhala 'Siṃhala', the native name for the island.
