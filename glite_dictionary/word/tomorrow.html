tomorrow

1. NOUN

/ təˈmɑːroʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɒrəʊ / (BrE)
the day after today
the day after today

Examples

We need to finish the project by tomorrow.
She has an important meeting scheduled for tomorrow morning.
Let’s postpone our picnic until tomorrow if the weather improves.

Synset Examples

what are our tasks for tomorrow?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: today - the present day
  • Antonym: yesterday - the day before today

Etymology

From Old English tō morgenne (on the morrow); related to 'morrow'.

2. ADV

/ təˈmɑːroʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɒrəʊ / (BrE)
On the day after today.
on the next day after today

Examples

Let's meet tomorrow to continue our discussion.
She promised to call me tomorrow with the final decision.
The weather is expected to improve tomorrow after the storm passes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: today - on this present day
  • Antonym: yesterday - on the day before today

Etymology

From Middle English 'tomorwe', from Old English 'tō morgen' (to morning).

3. NOUN

/ təˈmɑːroʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɒrəʊ / (BrE)
the future, especially the near future; the time that is yet to come
the (near) future; time ahead

Examples

Many young people are working to build a better tomorrow for everyone.
The company is preparing for the tomorrow of technological innovation.
We must invest in education to secure our tomorrow.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: future - time that is to come

Etymology

Old English to morgenne (on the morrow).
