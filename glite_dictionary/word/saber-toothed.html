saber-toothed

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪbər tuːθt / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə tuːθt / (BrE)
Having long, curved upper canine teeth resembling a saber, typically used to describe certain extinct predatory animals.
having large, saber-shaped canine teeth

Examples

The fossil remains revealed a saber-toothed predator unlike any living cat.
The saber-toothed tiger is one of the most famous prehistoric mammals.
Paleontologists were excited to discover a new saber-toothed species in the tar pits.

Synset Examples

a saber-toothed tiger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine-toothed - having prominent canine teeth

Etymology

From 'saber' (a type of curved sword) and 'toothed' (having teeth).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪbər tuːθt / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə tuːθt / (BrE)
(Of an animal) having long, curved upper canine teeth resembling a saber, especially used to describe certain extinct species such as the saber-toothed tiger.
having long, saber-like teeth

Examples

The saber-toothed cats roamed North America during the Ice Age.
Museum visitors admired the skull of a fierce saber-toothed predator.
Some prehistoric mammals developed saber-toothed features for hunting large prey.

Synset Examples

a saber-toothed tiger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber-toothed tiger - an extinct predatory mammal with long, curved canine teeth

Etymology

Formed from 'saber' + 'toothed', referring to resemblance to a saber sword.
