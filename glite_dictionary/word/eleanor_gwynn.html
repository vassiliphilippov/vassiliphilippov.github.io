Eleanor Gwynn

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛlənɔr ˈɡwɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪnɔː ˈɡwɪn / (BrE)
(1650–1687) An English actress and comedienne who became famous as the mistress of King Charles II.
English actress and royal mistress

Examples

Eleanor Gwynn was renowned for her wit and charm at the Restoration court.
Many plays of the era were inspired by the lively performances of Eleanor Gwynn.
The life of Eleanor Gwynn has often been romanticized in literature and theater.
