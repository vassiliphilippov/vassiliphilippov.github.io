Deutschmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɪtʃˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪtʃˌmɑːk / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Germany before the adoption of the euro in 2002
former German currency unit

Examples

When Germany switched to the euro, the Deutschmark ceased to be legal tender.
He still has a collection of old Deutschmarks from his travels in the 1990s.
The Deutschmark was known for its stability in the decades following World War II.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: euro - currency that replaced the Deutschmark in Germany and other EU countries

Etymology

From German 'Deutschmark' (Deutsch 'German' + Mark, a unit of currency).
