mercilessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.sɪl.ɪs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sɪl.ɪs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing no mercy; lack of compassion or forgiveness
lack of mercy or compassion

Examples

The dictator's mercilessness was evident in how he treated dissenters.
Many were shocked by the mercilessness of the punishment imposed on the prisoners.
Despite his opponent's pleas, the boxer fought with complete mercilessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthlessness - the quality of being without pity or compassion
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for the sufferings of others
  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone

Etymology

From 'merciless' + '-ness'; 'merciless' from Old French merciles, from 'mercy' + '-less'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.sɪl.ɪs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sɪl.ɪs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having or showing no mercy or compassion; complete lack of pity or kindness.
lack of mercy or compassion

Examples

The mercilessness of the regime shocked the world.
She was appalled by his utter mercilessness toward those in need.
The film portrays the mercilessness of war in haunting detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitilessness - the quality of having no pity; synonym for mercilessness
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others; antonym of mercilessness
  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown to someone; antonym of mercilessness

Etymology

From merciless + -ness, where 'merciless' comes from 'mercy' (from Old French 'merci', Latin 'mercedem').
