Porto-Novo

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɔːrtoʊˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtəʊˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
the capital city of Benin, located in the southwestern part of the country on a coastal lagoon
capital city of Benin

Examples

Many government buildings are located in Porto-Novo, the official capital of Benin.
The lagoon near Porto-Novo is vital for the local economy and transportation.
During their trip to Benin, they spent several days exploring Porto-Novo.

Etymology

From Portuguese, meaning 'New Port'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌpɔrtoʊ ˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtəʊ ˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
the official capital of Benin, located in the southwestern part of the country on a coastal lagoon
capital city of Benin

Examples

Many government offices are located in Porto-Novo, even though Cotonou is Benin's largest city.
Her family moved from a rural village to Porto-Novo when she was a child.
The lagoon near Porto-Novo is an important ecosystem for local fishermen.

Etymology

From Portuguese, meaning 'New Port', reflecting the city's colonial history.
