Plectognathi

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtɑɡ.nə.θaɪ / (AmE), / plɛkˈtɒɡ.nə.θaɪ / (BrE)
An order of fishes that includes boxfishes, triggerfishes, puffers, and related species, characterized by their fused jaw bones and often rigid bodies.
order of fishes including puffers and triggerfishes

Examples

The Plectognathi are notable for their distinctive body shapes and tough skins.
Some aquarium enthusiasts specialize in keeping members of the Plectognathi order due to their unique appearance.
The Plectognathi includes the well-known pufferfish, famous for its ability to inflate when threatened.

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted) and 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the fused jaw structure.
