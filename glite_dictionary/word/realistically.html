realistically

1. ADV

/ ˌriəˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that closely resembles real life or the original object; in a lifelike or true-to-life manner
in a lifelike or true-to-life manner

Examples

The portrait was realistically painted, capturing every detail perfectly.
In the film, the dinosaurs were realistically animated to make them look alive.
She described the economic situation realistically, without exaggeration or denial.

Synset Examples

the figure was realistically painted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accurately - in a way that is correct and without mistakes

Etymology

From 'realistic' + '-ally', patterned after adverbs formed from adjectives ending in '-ic'.

2. ADV

/ ˌriːəˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows an awareness of the actual facts or situation, avoiding wishful thinking or illusion.
in a realistic manner

Examples

Realistically, we can't finish the project by next week.
She approached her goals realistically, knowing that success would take time.
Realistically, not everyone will agree with the new policy.

Synset Examples

let's look at the situation realistically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrealistically - in a way that is not realistic; idealized or impractical

Etymology

From realistic + -ly, formed from French réaliste (from réalisme, realism) + English -ic + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00216815-r
Synset POS: r
