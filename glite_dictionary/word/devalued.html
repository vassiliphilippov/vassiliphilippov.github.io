devalued

1. ADJ

/ diˈvæljuːd / (AmE), / diːˈvæljuːd / (BrE)
reduced in value, importance, or worth, especially in relation to currency or reputation
reduced in value or worth

Examples

The country's currency became devalued after the economic crisis.
She felt devalued at work when her efforts weren't recognized.
Over time, old traditions can become devalued in a rapidly changing society.

Synset Examples

a debased currency
The worth of citizenship is devalued when information is censored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debased - lowered in quality or value
  • Antonym: appreciated - increased in value or worth

Etymology

From 'devalue' (from de- + value), meaning to reduce the value of something.
