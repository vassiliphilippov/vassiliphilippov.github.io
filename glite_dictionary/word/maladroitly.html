maladroitly

1. ADV

/ ˌmæləˈdrɔɪtli / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈdrɔɪtli / (BrE)
In a clumsy, awkward, or inept manner.
in a clumsy or awkward way

Examples

He maladroitly attempted to fix the lamp, only making things worse.
The speaker maladroitly handled the audience's tough questions during the debate.
She maladroitly navigated the narrow hallway, bumping into several tables along the way.

Synset Examples

he dealt with the situation maladroitly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsily - in a clumsy manner
  • Antonym: deftly - in a skillful, clever manner

Etymology

Formed from 'maladroit' (from French 'maladroit' meaning 'clumsy') plus adverbial -ly suffix.
