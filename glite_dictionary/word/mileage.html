mileage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the number of miles a vehicle can travel using a particular amount of fuel; fuel efficiency expressed as miles per gallon
fuel efficiency in miles per gallon

Examples

The new car gets much better mileage than my old one.
Drivers often compare mileage ratings before buying a vehicle.
Improving your mileage can save money and reduce environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel economy - efficiency with which a vehicle uses fuel

Etymology

From mile + -age, patterned after similar words to indicate amount or number of miles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of money paid or allowed for each mile traveled, especially as a travel expense allowance
travel allowance per mile traveled

Examples

The company reimburses employees for business trips based on mileage driven.
She kept a log of her mileage to claim her travel expenses.
Many organizations offer a standard mileage rate for use of personal vehicles.

Etymology

From mile + -age, modeled after other nouns indicating a charge or allowance per unit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13315052-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the total distance travelled or capable of being travelled, measured in miles
distance measured in miles

Examples

The car gets excellent mileage on the highway, making it ideal for long trips.
She tracked the mileage of her runs to prepare for the marathon.
What is the total mileage on your vehicle?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilometrage - distance measured in kilometers

Etymology

From mile + -age (suffix forming nouns denoting action or result).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05138459-n
Synset POS: n
