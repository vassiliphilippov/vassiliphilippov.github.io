ADA-SCID

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈeɪ ɛs ˈsiː ˈaɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈeɪ ˌɛs ˌsiː ˌaɪ ˌdiː / (BrE)
A form of severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID) caused by a mutation in the gene encoding the enzyme adenosine deaminase.
immunodeficiency disorder due to ADA mutation
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with ADA-SCID after repeated infections and low immune cell counts.
Gene therapy has been developed as a treatment option for patients with ADA-SCID.
Early detection of ADA-SCID can improve long-term health outcomes for affected children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severe combined immunodeficiency - a group of disorders characterized by defective immune system function

Etymology

ADA from 'adenosine deaminase' and SCID from 'severe combined immunodeficiency'.
