diminutiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɪnjətɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely small in size or extent
the quality of being very small

Examples

The diminutiveness of the sculpture made it easy to overlook at first glance.
She was enchanted by the diminutiveness of the tiny handwritten notes.
Its diminutiveness is precisely what makes the watch so appealing to collectors.

Synset Examples

hence the minuteness of detail in the painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minuteness - the quality of being very small or detailed
  • Synonym: smallness - the condition of being small in size
  • Antonym: largeness - the quality of being large or great in size

Etymology

From Latin 'dīminūtīvus' (tending to diminish) + English '-ness' (state or quality).
