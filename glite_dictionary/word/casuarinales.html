Casuarinales

1. NOUN

/ kæzjuˌɛrɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / kæzjuːəˌrɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly Australian trees and shrubs that includes the casuarinas; belonging to the family Casuarinaceae.
order of Australian trees and shrubs

Examples

The Casuarinales are notable for their needle-like foliage that mimics pine trees.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the Casuarinales extensively.
Many species within Casuarinales are found in coastal regions of Australia.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on genus name Casuarina.
