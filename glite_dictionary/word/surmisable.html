surmisable

1. ADJ

/ sərˈmaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / səˈmʌɪzəbl / (BrE)
capable of being inferred or guessed from limited evidence; able to be surmised.
able to be inferred or surmised

Examples

The motive behind his actions was surmisable from his nervous glances.
It is surmisable that she knew more than she admitted.
Based on the evidence presented, several outcomes remained surmisable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surmise - to infer or guess without strong evidence
  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood

Etymology

From the verb 'surmise' + suffix '-able', meaning able to be surmised.
