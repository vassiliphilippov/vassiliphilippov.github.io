turf

1. NOUN

/ tɝf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
the surface layer of soil held together by the roots of grass, often used for lawns or sports fields
grass-covered surface layer of soil

Examples

The landscaper laid new turf in the backyard to create a lush green lawn.
Players ran across the freshly watered turf as the match began.
After the storm, patches of turf needed to be replaced on the golf course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sod - a section of grass-covered surface earth held together by roots

Etymology

From Old English 'turf', related to Old Norse 'torf' (piece of sod).

WordNet ID: oewn-09487049-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ tɝf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
to cover a surface, especially the ground, with turf (grass and its roots)
to cover ground with grass roots

Examples

The landscapers will turf the entire garden next week to create a fresh, green lawn.
After laying the drainage pipes, the workers turfed over the yard to restore its appearance.
We spent the weekend turfing the play area to make it safer for children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sod - to cover with a piece of turf or grass

Etymology

From Old English turf (sod, piece of earth with grass).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01237064-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ tɝf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
an area or territory regarded as belonging to a particular group, especially a juvenile gang
a group's claimed territory (esp. gang)

Examples

The two gangs fought over control of their turf and would not let outsiders in.
After the new group moved in, disputes arose over whose turf this neighborhood was.
Police increased patrols in the area to prevent turf wars between rival gangs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territory - an area defended or claimed by a person or group

Etymology

Originally referring to grass or sod; extended metaphorically to mean territory.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08707509-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ tɝf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
an area or sphere in which a person, group, or organization has influence or authority
area of influence or authority

Examples

The two departments are constantly fighting over turf within the company.
She fiercely defended her turf against any attempts to reduce her responsibilities.
Political turf wars often slow down important reforms.

Synset Examples

a bureaucracy … chiefly concerned with turf … and protecting the retirement system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of control or authority
  • Synonym: territory - an area controlled by a person or group

Etymology

From Old English 'turf' meaning sod or piece of grass-covered earth; figurative meaning developed in the 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
