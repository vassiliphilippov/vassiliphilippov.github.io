pejorative

1. ADJ

/ pɪˈdʒɔrətɪv / (AmE), / pɪˈdʒɒrətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) expressing contempt or disapproval.
expressing disapproval or contempt

Examples

The term 'geek' was once a pejorative label but has become more neutral over time.
He used a pejorative expression to describe his rival, which many found offensive.
Writers are often advised to avoid pejorative language when discussing sensitive topics.

Synset Examples

dyslogistic terms like ‘nitwit’ and ‘scalawag’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derogatory - expressing a low opinion or disrespect
  • Antonym: neutral - not expressing approval or disapproval

Etymology

From Late Latin pejorativus, from Latin pejor (worse).
