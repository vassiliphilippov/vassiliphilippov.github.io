to gravitate

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrævɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪteɪt / (BrE)
to move or be attracted towards something, often by the force of gravity
move or be attracted, often by gravity

Examples

Under the influence of gravity, the planets gravitate toward the sun.
After the collision, the objects began to gravitate together in space.
Stars in a galaxy gravitate around a common center of mass.

Synset Examples

The stars gravitate towards each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attract - to pull towards something, especially physically

Etymology

From Latin gravitat-, past participle stem of gravitare (to make heavy), from gravis (heavy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01839222-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɡrævɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪteɪt / (BrE)
to be attracted or drawn towards a person, place, or thing, usually because of a particular quality or interest
be attracted or drawn toward something

Examples

Young musicians often gravitate towards jazz for its creative freedom.
Many people gravitate to big cities in search of new opportunities.
She gravitated naturally to leadership roles throughout her career.

Synset Examples

Boys gravitate towards girls at that age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attract - to draw by appeal or interest

Etymology

From Italian 'gravitare', from Latin 'gravitat-', meaning 'made heavy', from 'gravis' meaning 'heavy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02737871-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡrævɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪteɪt / (BrE)
to move or be drawn toward something or someone, especially by a natural tendency or attraction
move or be drawn toward something

Examples

Many students naturally gravitate toward courses that interest them the most.
Over time, artists often gravitate to cities known for their vibrant creative communities.
Our discussions always gravitated back to memories of our childhood days.

Synset Examples

The conversation gravitated towards politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attract - to cause to move toward by appealing or pulling
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or push away

Etymology

From Latin gravitat-, past participle stem of gravitare (to make heavy), from gravis (heavy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02738023-v
Synset POS: v
