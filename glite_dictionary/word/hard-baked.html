hard-baked

1. ADJ

/ hɑrdˈbeɪkt / (AmE), / hɑːdˈbeɪkt / (BrE)
Baked until hard, especially of food that has been cooked for a long time or at high temperature so as to lose moisture and become firm or rigid.
baked until hard

Examples

The hard-baked crust made the pastry difficult to cut.
Some traditional potteries use a hard-baked clay technique for durability.
After hours in the oven, the bread was hard-baked and nearly impossible to slice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hard - solid, firm, or rigid; difficult to deform
  • Degree Higher: burnt - overcooked and blackened due to too much heat
