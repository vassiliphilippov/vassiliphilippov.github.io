diethylbarbituric acid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌiːθəl.bɑːrˌbɪtʃʊr.ɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / daɪˌiːθəl.bɑːbɪˌtʃʊər.ɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A barbiturate compound used medically as a hypnotic drug to induce sleep.
a barbiturate used as a hypnotic drug
medical

Examples

After several sleepless nights, the doctor prescribed diethylbarbituric acid to help her rest.
Overuse of diethylbarbituric acid can lead to dependence and tolerance in some patients.
Researchers are studying alternatives to diethylbarbituric acid for safer treatment of insomnia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - any salt or ester of barbituric acid, used as a sedative or hypnotic
  • Synonym: hypnotic - a drug that induces sleep

Etymology

Formed from 'diethyl-' (two ethyl groups) and 'barbituric acid' (the parent compound of the barbiturate class), from Latin 'barbituricum acidum'.
