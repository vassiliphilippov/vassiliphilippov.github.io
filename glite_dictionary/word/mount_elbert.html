Mount Elbert

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛlbərt / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛlbət / (BrE)
The highest peak in the Rocky Mountains, located in central Colorado, rising to 14,431 feet (4,399 meters).
highest peak in the Rocky Mountains

Examples

Many hikers travel to Colorado to climb Mount Elbert each year.
The view from the summit of Mount Elbert is truly breathtaking.
Snow still covered parts of Mount Elbert in late June.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Hitt Elbert, a nineteenth-century governor of the Colorado Territory.
