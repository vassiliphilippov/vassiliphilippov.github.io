Salviniaceae

1. NOUN

/ sælˌvɪniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / salˌvɪnɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic ferns that includes the genera Azolla and Salvinia.
family of aquatic ferns (Azolla and Salvinia)

Examples

The Salviniaceae are widely distributed in tropical and temperate wetlands.
Research on Salviniaceae has focused on their unique reproductive biology among ferns.
Many rice farmers manage Salviniaceae species in paddies to prevent the spread of pests.

Etymology

From Salvinia, the type genus, named after Italian scientist Antonio Maria Salvini.

2. NOUN

/ sælˌvɪniˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / sælˌvɪniˈeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic ferns that includes the genera Azolla and Salvinia
a family of aquatic ferns

Examples

The Salviniaceae are known for their unique reproduction among aquatic ferns.
Scientists study Salviniaceae to understand the ecological impact of invasive species like Salvinia.
Members of the Salviniaceae family can be found in freshwater habitats around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azolla - a genus of small aquatic ferns in Salviniaceae
  • Synonym: Salvinia - a genus of floating aquatic ferns in Salviniaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Salvinia, named after Italian scientist Anton Maria Salvini (1633–1729), plus the botanical family suffix -aceae.
