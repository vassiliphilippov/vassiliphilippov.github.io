ineradicable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnɪˈrædɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnɪˈrædɪkəbl / (BrE)
unable to be completely removed, destroyed, or wiped out.
impossible to remove or destroy

Examples

Some prejudices are so ingrained that they seem ineradicable.
The community struggled with ineradicable myths about their health.
Her passion for art proved ineradicable despite years of discouragement.

Synset Examples

ineradicable superstitions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indelible - impossible to remove or forget
  • Antonym: eradicable - able to be removed or destroyed

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + eradicare (to root out).
