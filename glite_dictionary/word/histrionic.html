histrionic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɪstriˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪstriˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
Excessively dramatic or theatrical in manner or style; relating to acting or stage performances, often with artificial or affected behavior.
overly theatrical or dramatic

Examples

The actor's histrionic performance was both captivating and over the top.
During the meeting, she became histrionic, waving her arms dramatically to make her point.
He dismissed her complaints as histrionic exaggerations, saying she always overreacted.

Synset Examples

histrionic gestures
an attitude of melodramatic despair
a theatrical pose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - exaggerated and dramatic, like in theater
  • Antonym: natural - not affected; genuine or casual in manner

Etymology

From Latin *histrionicus* (of an actor), from *histrio* (actor).
