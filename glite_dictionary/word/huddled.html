huddled

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌd.əld / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əld / (BrE)
closely crowded or gathered together, typically for protection or comfort.
crowded or gathered closely together

Examples

The refugees sat huddled together under blankets in the cold shelter.
Children were huddled in a corner, whispering quietly to one another.
Several sheep remained huddled near the fence, trying to stay warm.

Synset Examples

give me … your huddled masses
the huddled sheep turned their backs against the wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clustered - gathered or grouped closely together
  • Antonym: dispersed - scattered or separated

Etymology

Past participle of 'huddle', of uncertain origin, dating from the 16th century.
