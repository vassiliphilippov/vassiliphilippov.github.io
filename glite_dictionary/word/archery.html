archery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃəri / (BrE)
The sport or skill of shooting arrows with a bow, especially at a target.
sport of shooting arrows with a bow

Examples

She has been practicing archery for many years and competes in tournaments.
Archery requires concentration, precision, and a steady hand.
The ancient Olympics included archery as one of the main events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrow - a thin, pointed projectile shot from a bow
  • Synonym: bow - a weapon for shooting arrows, made of a curved piece of wood

Etymology

From Old French 'archerie', from 'arquier' (archer), from Latin 'arcus' (bow).
