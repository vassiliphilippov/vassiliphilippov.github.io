highjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒæk / (BrE)
the seizure of a vehicle in transit, especially to rob it or force it to go to a different destination
the act of seizing a vehicle in transit

Examples

The highjack of the plane created a tense situation for all on board.
After the highjack, authorities increased security at major airports.
He was arrested for planning a highjack of a delivery truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijacking - the act of unlawfully seizing control of a vehicle

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'hijack,' of uncertain origin, possibly from 'high' and 'jack' (as in 'lumberjack').

2. VERB

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒæk / (BrE)
to seize control of a vehicle or object, especially by force or threat, often used for planes or other forms of transport. Variant spelling of 'hijack'.
seize control by force (variant of hijack)

Examples

The militants tried to highjack the train but were stopped by security forces.
A group of criminals planned to highjack a cargo truck on the highway.
During the crisis, reports emerged that rebels had highjacked several vehicles.

Synset Examples

The Cubans commandeered the plane and flew it to Miami

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijack - to seize control of a vehicle by force (standard spelling)

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'hijack', origin uncertain but likely from 'high' + 'jack' (slang for a robber or thief), early 20th-century American English.
