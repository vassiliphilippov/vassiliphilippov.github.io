Callorhinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of fur seals in the family Otariidae, including the northern fur seal (Callorhinus ursinus)
genus of northern fur seals

Examples

The Callorhinus genus is best known for the northern fur seal, a species important to marine ecosystems.
Scientists have studied Callorhinus to understand how fur seals adapt to cold ocean environments.
The migration patterns of Callorhinus are a subject of ongoing marine biology research.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'rhis, rhinos' (nose) referring to shape of the snout.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fur seals, including the northern fur seal (Callorhinus ursinus), found in the North Pacific Ocean.
genus of fur seals

Examples

The Callorhinus genus is best known for the northern fur seal, Callorhinus ursinus.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of Callorhinus to understand environmental changes.
The decline in Callorhinus populations has raised concerns among conservationists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur seal - a seal of the family Otariidae, valued for its thick fur

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'rhis, rhinos' (nose), referring to the animal's features.
