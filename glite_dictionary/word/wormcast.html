wormcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrmˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmkɑːst / (BrE)
a cylindrical mass of soil excreted by an earthworm or lugworm, often found on the surface of soil or sand.
cylindrical mass of soil from a worm

Examples

After the rain, the garden was dotted with fresh wormcasts on the lawn.
Fishermen often look for wormcasts on the beach to find bait.
The presence of wormcasts indicates healthy soil full of earthworm activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - a mass of matter deposited or excreted by an animal

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrmˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmkɑːst / (BrE)
a fossilized or hardened trail left by a worm, often seen as a winding or coiled mark in soil or rock
fossil trail left by a worm

Examples

The paleontologist carefully examined the wormcast embedded in the rock formation.
Children often find wormcasts on the surface of the soil after a rainstorm.
This museum display includes several examples of ancient wormcast fossils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm track - a track or trail made by a worm, often preserved as a fossil
  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal, especially a worm
