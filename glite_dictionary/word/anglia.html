Anglia

1. PROPN

/ ˈæŋɡliə / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪə / (BrE)
the Latin name for England, used especially in historical, academic, or poetic contexts
the Latin name for England

Examples

The historian referred to Anglia in his description of medieval Britain.
Many old university addresses used the Latin form Anglia instead of England.
On some ancient maps, England is labelled as Anglia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: England - a country that is part of the United Kingdom

Etymology

From Latin 'Anglia', meaning England; derived from 'Angli', the Angles, a Germanic tribe.
