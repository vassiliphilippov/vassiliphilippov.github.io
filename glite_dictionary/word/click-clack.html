click-clack

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪkˌklæk / (AmE), / ˈklɪkˌklæk / (BrE)
A series of quick, sharp, alternating clicking and clacking sounds.
repetitive clicking and clacking sound

Examples

The click-clack of her high heels echoed down the hallway.
You could hear the click-clack of the old typewriter from across the room.
Late at night, the rhythmic click-clack of the train wheels lulled him to sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clatter - a continuous rattling sound, as of hard objects falling or striking each other

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by clicking and clacking.
