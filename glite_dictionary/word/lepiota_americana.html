Lepiota americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːpiˈoʊtə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˌliːpɪˈəʊtə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
A species of mushroom in the genus Lepiota, characterized by a pale cap and a stem that is swollen at the base.
a mushroom with a pale cap and swollen base

Examples

The Lepiota americana can be recognized by its distinctive pale cap and swollen stalk base.
Researchers found several Lepiota americana specimens in the wooded area behind the laboratory.
It is advised not to eat mushrooms that resemble Lepiota americana without expert identification.

Etymology

From genus name Lepiota, from Greek lepis (scale), and Latin americana, referring to America.
