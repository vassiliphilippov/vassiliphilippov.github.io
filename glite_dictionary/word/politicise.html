to politicise

1. VERB

/ pəˈlɪtəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to give a political character or bias to something, especially an issue, action, or discussion
give a political character to something
british

Examples

The media has politicised the debate over healthcare reform.
Some argue that education should not be politicised by government agendas.
Activists worry that environmental issues are often politicised rather than addressed objectively.

Synset Examples

politicize the discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to politicize - give a political character to (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'politiser', from 'politique' (political), ultimately from Greek 'politikos'.
