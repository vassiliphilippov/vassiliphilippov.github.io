Anacardium occidentale

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈkɑːr.di.əm ˌɑk.sɪˈden.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈkɑː.dɪ.əm ˌɒk.sɪˈdɛn.teɪl / (BrE)
A tropical American evergreen tree known for producing cashew nuts, with kidney-shaped edible seeds that are safe to eat only after roasting.
tropical evergreen tree; the cashew tree

Examples

The Anacardium occidentale is cultivated in many tropical countries for its valuable nuts.
Farmers harvested the nuts from the Anacardium occidentale before processing them for export.
The leaves of Anacardium occidentale provide shade in plantations as the trees mature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashew tree - tree that produces cashew nuts
  • Synonym: cashew nut - the edible nut from Anacardium occidentale

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name from Greek ana- (up) + kardia (heart), referencing the nut’s shape.
