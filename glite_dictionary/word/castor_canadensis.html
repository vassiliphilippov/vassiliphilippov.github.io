Castor canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɔr kəˈneɪ.dɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæs.tɔː kəˈneɪ.dɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the North American beaver, a large semi-aquatic rodent native to most of North America except Florida
North American beaver (species)

Examples

The lodge built by the Castor canadensis can contain several generations of beavers.
Scientists have studied the habits of Castor canadensis to better understand wetland ecosystems.
Unlike its European relative, Castor canadensis is not found in Florida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - a large semiaquatic rodent known for building dams and lodges

Etymology

From Latin: castor (beaver) + canadensis (of Canada).
