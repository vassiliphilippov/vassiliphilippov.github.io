shorthand

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔːrtˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːthænd / (BrE)
written or expressed in an abbreviated or symbolic form; using a system of rapid writing or symbols to represent words and phrases quickly.
written in abbreviated, symbolic form

Examples

She took shorthand notes during the meeting for efficiency.
Many journalists use shorthand systems to keep up with fast-paced discussions.
The court reporter's shorthand transcript was later typed up in full.

Synset Examples

shorthand notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviated - shortened or condensed in form

Etymology

From 'short' + 'hand', originally describing rapid manual note-taking.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02293703-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔːrtˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːthænd / (BrE)
a method of rapid writing that uses symbols or abbreviations to represent words and phrases
abbreviated rapid writing system

Examples

He took notes in shorthand during the meeting for greater efficiency.
Learning shorthand is useful for secretarial work and journalism.
The reporter’s shorthand was so fast that she kept up with every speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenography - the process or skill of writing in shorthand

Etymology

From short + hand, referring to a concise way of writing by hand.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06361497-n
Synset POS: n
