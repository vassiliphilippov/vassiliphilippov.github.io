precognition

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkɑːˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkɒˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability or phenomenon of knowing about an event before it happens, especially as a paranormal experience
knowledge of an event before it happens

Examples

Some people claim to have precognition of disasters before they occur.
The scientist dismissed her dreams of future events as mere coincidence, not precognition.
Fiction often explores characters with the power of precognition to add intrigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clairvoyance - paranormal ability to gain information about an object, person, location, or event
  • Antonym: retrocognition - knowledge of a past event through paranormal means

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + cognitio (knowledge, perception).
