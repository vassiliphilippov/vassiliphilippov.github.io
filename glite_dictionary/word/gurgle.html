gurgle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɜːrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːɡl̩ / (BrE)
to drink from a container in such a way that a gurgling sound is produced
drink with a gurgling sound

Examples

He gurgled the cold water straight from his flask after the long hike.
She picked up the canteen and gurgled it noisily as her friends laughed.
The traveler gurgled from his bottle, the sound echoing through the quiet camp.

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, related to words for bubbling sounds in many languages.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01172846-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a bubbling sound like water flowing from a bottle with a narrow neck or similar noises
bubbling sound of flowing liquid

Examples

The gurgle of the stream was soothing as we walked along the path.
She listened to the gurgle of water pouring from the old bottle.
A faint gurgle could be heard from the pipes behind the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - a continuous murmuring sound, as of water flowing over stones
  • Synonym: murmur - a low, continuous sound, especially of water or voices

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07399341-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɡɝɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːɡl / (BrE)
to utter words or sounds with a bubbling or choking noise, typically resulting from liquid in the throat
make bubbling speechlike sounds

Examples

The injured man tried to gurgle a plea for help before falling silent.
With water in her throat, she could only gurgle his name.
He gurgled a laugh despite the pain, surprising everyone nearby.

Synset Examples

‘Help,’ the stabbing victim gurgled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to utter sounds or words incoherently, often as a stream of noise
  • Synonym: murmur - to speak in a soft, low, and indistinct voice

Etymology

Imitative of the sound, related to 'gurgling' noises produced by liquid.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00987366-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈɡɝːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːɡl̩ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow in an irregular current with a bubbling sound.
make a bubbling sound while flowing

Examples

The water in the stream began to gurgle as it flowed over the rocks.
She could hear a kettle gurgling softly on the stove.
Wine gurgled into the glass with a pleasant, bubbling sound.

Synset Examples

babbling brooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to make the sound of water flowing gently over stones
  • Degree Higher: roar - to make a loud deep sound, as of water
  • Degree Lower: trickle - to flow in a thin stream or drops

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound of bubbling water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02192368-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈɡɜrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːɡl̩ / (BrE)
to make a sound similar to liquid flowing or bubbling with a gentle, irregular noise
make a bubbling or murmuring sound

Examples

The mountain stream began to gurgle softly as spring arrived.
The baby gurgled happily in her crib when she saw her father.
He could hear the coffee pot gurgling in the kitchen as it brewed.

Synset Examples

The baby gurgled with satisfaction when the mother tickled it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - make a continuous murmuring sound like water flowing

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound of bubbling water.

Source: WordNet
