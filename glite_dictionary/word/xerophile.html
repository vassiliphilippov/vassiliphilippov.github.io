xerophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪrəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a plant or organism that is adapted to live in environments with very little water
organism adapted to dry conditions

Examples

The cactus is a classic example of a xerophile thriving in the desert.
Certain bacteria are regarded as xerophiles because they can survive in extremely dry environments.
Botanists study xerophiles to understand how plants evolve drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to living in water or very moist conditions
  • Antonym: mesophyte - plant that grows in environments with a moderate amount of water

Etymology

From Greek 'xēros' (dry) and 'philos' (loving).
