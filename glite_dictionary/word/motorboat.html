motorboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtərˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəbəʊt / (BrE)
a small boat that is powered by an internal-combustion engine
boat powered by an engine

Examples

They spent the afternoon cruising the lake in their motorboat.
The loud hum of the motorboat could be heard across the water.
We rented a small motorboat to explore the bay's coastline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: speedboat - a type of fast motorboat designed for racing or recreation
  • Antonym: rowboat - a small boat propelled by oars

Etymology

motor + boat; denoting a boat driven by a motor engine.

2. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtərˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəˌbəʊt / (BrE)
to travel or ride in a boat propelled by a motor
ride in a boat with a motor

Examples

We decided to motorboat across the lake to reach the island.
Tourists often motorboat along the river to enjoy the scenery.
They have motorboated on this lake every summer since childhood.

Etymology

From 'motor' + 'boat', referring to a boat powered by an engine.
