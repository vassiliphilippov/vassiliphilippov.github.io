yarrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈjærəʊ / (BrE)
a common Eurasian perennial herb (Achillea millefolium) with aromatic leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, usually white or pale pink flowers, naturalized in many other regions and often found in meadows or pastures
aromatic perennial herb with small white flowers

Examples

Yarrow is sometimes used in herbal teas for its purported healing properties.
Wild yarrow grows along the edges of the meadow, its white flowers attracting butterflies.
She carefully collected yarrow from the hedgerow to press in her botanical journal.

Etymology

Old English gearwe, from Proto-Germanic *garwō.
