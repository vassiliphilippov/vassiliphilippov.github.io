flow

1. NOUN

/ floʊ / (AmE), / fləʊ / (BrE)
the amount of a fluid, such as water or air, that passes a point in a particular period of time
the rate at which fluid moves

Examples

The river's flow increased rapidly after the heavy rain.
Engineers measured the flow of air through the ventilation system.
A steady flow of water is crucial for the operation of the turbine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rate - a measure of how quickly something happens

Etymology

From Old English 'flōwan', related to Old Norse 'flóa' and Dutch 'vloeien'.
