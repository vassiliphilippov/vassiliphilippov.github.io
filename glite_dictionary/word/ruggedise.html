to ruggedise

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌɡ.ɪ.daɪz / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡ.ɪ.daɪz / (BrE)
to make or produce in a form able to withstand rough handling or harsh conditions
make durable for rough use
british

Examples

The company will ruggedise its laptops for use by field engineers.
Military equipment is often ruggedised to survive extreme environments.
They plan to ruggedise the smartphones for industrial workers.

Synset Examples

Detroit ruggedized the family car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ruggedize - American English spelling of 'ruggedise'

Etymology

From 'rugged' + '-ise' (British spelling; also -ize in American English), meaning to make rugged.
