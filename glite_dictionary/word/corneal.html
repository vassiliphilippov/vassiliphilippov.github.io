corneal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrniəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːniəl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the cornea, the transparent front part of the eye
relating to the cornea of the eye
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a corneal abrasion after being accidentally scratched in the eye.
Doctors performed a corneal transplant to restore her vision.
Wearing contact lenses overnight can increase the risk of corneal infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornea - the transparent front part of the eye covering the iris and pupil

Etymology

From 'cornea' (from Medieval Latin cornea, from Latin cornu meaning 'horn', referring to its horn-like transparency) + -al (adjective suffix).
