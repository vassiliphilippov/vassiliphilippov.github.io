Anomia

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks, commonly known as saddle oysters, belonging to the family Anomiidae.
genus of marine saddle oysters

Examples

The delicate shells of Anomia species are often found attached to rocks or other shells.
Researchers collected several specimens of Anomia from the estuary.
Unlike other bivalves, Anomia is known for its thin, translucent shell.

Etymology

From Greek anōmia (lawlessness), referring to the irregular shells.

genus of saddle oysters
