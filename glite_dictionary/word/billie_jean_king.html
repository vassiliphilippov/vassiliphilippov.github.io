Billie Jean King

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪli dʒin ˈkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪli dʒiːn ˈkɪŋ / (BrE)
(born 1943) A renowned American tennis player, widely recognized for her achievements in women's tennis and advocacy for gender equality in sports.
American tennis player and activist

Examples

Billie Jean King won 39 major tennis titles during her remarkable career.
The famous 'Battle of the Sexes' match featured Billie Jean King defeating Bobby Riggs in 1973.
Billie Jean King has been a leading advocate for gender equality in athletics for decades.
