whimsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈwɪmzi / (BrE)
The quality of being playfully quaint, unpredictable, or acting on impulse rather than reason.
playful or unpredictable behavior

Examples

Her artwork is filled with whimsy and unexpected details.
The garden's design was guided by whimsy rather than strict planning.
He added a touch of whimsy to the project, making it more enjoyable for everyone.

Synset Examples

I despair at the flightiness and whimsicality of my memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caprice - a sudden, unpredictable change of mood or behavior
  • Antonym: predictability - the quality of being able to be predicted or expected

Etymology

From whim-wham (fanciful object or notion), altered to whimsy in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈwɪmzi / (BrE)
a playful or fanciful idea or notion, often unusual or capricious
playful or fanciful idea

Examples

The artist's paintings are full of whimsy and unexpected details.
She decorated her room with a touch of whimsy that made it feel magical.
His plan to host a midnight picnic was pure whimsy.

Synset Examples

the theatrical notion of disguise is associated with disaster in his stories
he had a whimsy about flying to the moon
whimsy can be humorous to someone with time to enjoy it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notion - an idea or belief about something
  • Synonym: fantasy - imaginative creation or idea, often unreal

Etymology

From whim-wham (a fanciful object), likely altered by analogy to fantasy.
