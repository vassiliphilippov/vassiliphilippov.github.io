midair

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈeə / (BrE)
a point or region in the air, especially above the ground
a point in the air above ground

Examples

The two dancers appeared to freeze in midair during their spectacular leap.
The magician made the ball float in midair, astonishing the audience.
The acrobat caught the trapeze bar while still in midair.

Synset Examples

the planes collided in midair

Etymology

Formed from 'mid' (middle) and 'air'.
