Doryopteris pedata

1. NOUN

/ dɔːriˈɑptərɪs pɪˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌdɔːriˈɒptərɪs pɪˈdiːtə / (BrE)
A tropical American fern species marked by fronds that are deeply lobed or palmate in shape.
a tropical American fern species

Examples

The lush undergrowth in the rainforest included several specimens of Doryopteris pedata.
Botanists studied the unique frond structure of Doryopteris pedata during their field expedition.
Gardeners appreciate Doryopteris pedata for its distinctive, palmate leaves and tropical appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Doryopteris' from Greek dory (spear) + pteris (fern), species epithet 'pedata' from Latin (foot-like, referring to the frond shape).
