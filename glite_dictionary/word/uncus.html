uncus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkəs / (BrE)
(Biology) A hook-shaped process or part, especially in the structure of animals or plants.
hook-shaped process (biology)
medical

Examples

The uncus is prominent in the anatomy of certain insects' genitalia.
Damage to the uncus of the brain can affect olfactory function.
The surgeon carefully avoided the uncus during the operation.

Etymology

From Latin 'uncus' meaning 'hook'.
