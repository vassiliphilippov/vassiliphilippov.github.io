Zionism

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A movement that began in the late 19th century among Jews worldwide with the goal of establishing and supporting a Jewish state in Palestine.
movement for a Jewish state in Palestine

Examples

The rise of Zionism in the 19th century had a profound impact on Jewish history.
Many debates about the Middle East include discussions of Zionism and its modern implications.
Her grandfather was an early supporter of Zionism and worked for the establishment of Israel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalism - belief in the interests or culture of a particular nation-state

Etymology

From German *Zionismus*, from Zion (a hill in Jerusalem regarded as a symbol of the Jewish homeland) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A movement for the development and protection of a Jewish national homeland in what is now Israel, originally established in Palestine.
movement for a Jewish homeland in Israel/Palestine

Examples

The rise of Zionism in the late 19th century led to renewed Jewish immigration to Palestine.
Her studies focus on the role of Zionism in Middle Eastern history.
Debates about Zionism continue to shape politics in the region today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-Zionism - opposition to the movement for a Jewish homeland

Etymology

From Zion, a Hebrew term for Jerusalem, combined with -ism.
