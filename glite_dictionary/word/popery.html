popery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊpəri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊpəri / (BrE)
An offensive term for the beliefs, practices, or rituals of the Roman Catholic Church, especially as used by critics or opponents.
offensive term for Roman Catholic practices
offensive archaic

Examples

The pamphlet railed against what it called popery and Catholic influences in government.
He was accused of secretly supporting popery during the heated debate.
In 17th-century England, fear of popery led to widespread anti-Catholic sentiment.

Etymology

From 'pope' + '-ery', modeled after similar constructions indicating practices or systems.
