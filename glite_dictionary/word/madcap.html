madcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmadˌkap / (BrE)
a person who acts in a reckless, impulsive, or irresponsible way
reckless or impulsive person

Examples

The film follows a madcap who constantly gets his friends into trouble.
She has always been a bit of a madcap, trying wild ideas without thinking them through.
Everyone warned him not to trust that madcap with anything important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daredevil - a person who enjoys taking dangerous risks
  • Antonym: careful person - someone who behaves cautiously and avoids risks

Etymology

From 'mad' (crazy) + 'cap', once referring to a fool's cap worn by jesters.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmædˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmad.kæp / (BrE)
acting quickly and without thinking about the consequences; impulsive or reckless.
impulsive; acting without thought

Examples

The play is filled with madcap antics and unpredictable moments.
Their madcap plan to drive across the country without a map ended in disaster.
She has a madcap sense of adventure that often gets her into trouble.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded decision
liable to such impulsive acts as hugging strangers
an impetuous display of spending and gambling
madcap escapade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impetuous - acting quickly without thought or care
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard to danger or consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid danger or mistakes

Etymology

From 'mad' + 'cap', originally referring to a foolish or reckless person (16th century English).
