aplite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.laɪt / (AmE), / ˈæp.laɪt / (BrE)
A light-colored, fine-grained igneous rock composed mainly of quartz and feldspars, typically found in granite formations.
fine-grained granitic rock

Examples

The geologist identified a vein of aplite running through the granite outcrop.
Thin sections of aplite reveal its remarkably uniform texture under the microscope.
The quarry produces both granite and fine-grained aplite for construction use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granite - a hard, coarse-grained igneous rock

Etymology

From German Aplit, from Greek haplous (simple), referring to its simple mineral composition.
