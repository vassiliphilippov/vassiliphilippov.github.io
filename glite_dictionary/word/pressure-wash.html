to pressure-wash

1. VERB

/ ˈprɛʃər wɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə wɒʃ / (BrE)
to clean (a surface) using a high-pressure water spray, often to remove dirt, paint, or mildew
clean with a high-pressure water spray

Examples

We decided to pressure-wash the deck before applying a new coat of paint.
It's important to pressure-wash the driveway to remove moss and grime.
Before selling their house, they pressure-washed the siding for a fresh look.

Synset Examples

pressure-wash the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power-wash - to wash using a high-pressure water spray

Etymology

Formed from 'pressure' and 'wash', describing the cleaning technique involving pressurized water.
