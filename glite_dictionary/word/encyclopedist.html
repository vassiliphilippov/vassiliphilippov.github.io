encyclopedist

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪst / (BrE)
a person who compiles or writes articles for an encyclopedia
person who compiles encyclopedias

Examples

Denis Diderot was a noted encyclopedist of the Enlightenment era.
As an encyclopedist, she specialized in assembling comprehensive articles on historical topics.
The encyclopedist devoted years to verifying every fact before publication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encyclopedian - another term for an encyclopedist, used rarely

Etymology

From French encyclopédiste, referring to contributors to Diderot's Encyclopédie.
