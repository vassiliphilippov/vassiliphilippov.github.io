neap

1. NOUN

/ niːp / (AmE), / niːp / (BrE)
A tide with the least difference between high and low water, occurring at the first and third quarters of the moon.
tide with least difference between high and low water

Examples

During a neap, the difference between high and low tide is at its minimum.
The harbor is safest to enter during a neap because the currents are weaker.
Scientists recorded the water level changes throughout the neap last week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spring tide - a tide with the greatest difference between high and low water

Etymology

From Old English ‘nep’, of uncertain origin, related to tidal terminology.
