abandonment

1. NOUN

/ əˈbændənmənt / (AmE), / əˈbændənmənt / (BrE)
the voluntary surrender of property or a right to property, without any intention of reclaiming it or transferring it to another
voluntary surrender of property rights
legal

Examples

The company’s abandonment of its unused factory meant it could not reclaim the property later.
Without proper notice, the abandonment of the land was considered intentional and complete by the courts.
Legal abandonment requires a clear act and intent to relinquish all ownership rights.

Etymology

From Old French 'abandoner' (to relinquish), ultimately from Latin 'abandonare'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbændənmənt / (AmE), / əˈbændənmənt / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing support or help from someone to whom one owes allegiance or responsibility
withdrawal of support or help

Examples

After his abandonment of the company during its crisis, his reputation suffered greatly.
The sudden abandonment of the project left everyone confused and frustrated.
Many animals suffer from abandonment when their owners move away.

Synset Examples

his abandonment of his wife and children left them penniless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desertion - the act of leaving or abandoning someone in need

Etymology

From Old French 'abandoner' (to give up), from 'à bandon' (at will).

3. NOUN

/ əˈbændənmənt / (AmE), / əˈbændənmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving something up completely; the process of leaving or discarding something or someone.
the act of giving something up

Examples

The abandonment of the old house left it to decay for years.
After much thought, he announced the abandonment of his plans to move abroad.
She felt a deep sense of loss at the abandonment by her closest friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renunciation - the formal rejection of something, typically a belief or claim
  • Antonym: retention - the act of keeping possession of something

Etymology

From French 'abandonner' (to surrender, give up), from Old French 'abandoner'.
