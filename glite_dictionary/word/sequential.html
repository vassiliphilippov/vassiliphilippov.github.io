sequential

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈkwɛn.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛn.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
arranged or happening in a particular order or sequence, with each part following the previous one without gaps
arranged in sequence or order

Examples

The events of the novel are presented in a sequential order, making the plot easy to follow.
She numbered the files in sequential fashion to keep everything organized.
The training modules must be completed in a sequential manner to ensure proper learning.

Synset Examples

serial concerts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following continuously; in unbroken sequence
  • Antonym: random - made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision

Etymology

From Latin 'sequentia' (sequence) + English suffix '-al'.
