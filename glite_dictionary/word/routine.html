routine

1. NOUN

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a short performance, especially as part of a longer program, often designed to entertain
short performance in a program

Examples

The comedian performed his famous routine to loud applause from the audience.
Her dance routine was a highlight of the evening's talent show.
They rehearsed their comedy routine for weeks before the competition.

Synset Examples

he did his act three times every evening
she had a catchy little routine
it was one of the best numbers he ever did

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - a short performance as part of a show
  • Synonym: number - a self-contained performance in a show
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06905066-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a regular way of doing things that does not vary; a habitual method or procedure
a habitual or regular method

Examples

She followed her morning routine before heading to work.
Changing your daily routine can help improve your productivity.
He quickly settled into a comfortable routine at his new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - a regular practice or tendency
  • Synonym: ritual - a set procedure for a religious or other ceremony
  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving

Etymology

From French 'routine', from 'route' (road, way).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01028424-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a sequence of programmed instructions that performs a specific task within a larger computer program
set of instructions in a computer program

Examples

The new software update included a routine for encrypting user data.
Each routine in the program is responsible for a specific operation.
Experienced programmers often reuse the same routine to perform common tasks like sorting data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subroutine - a routine that is called by another routine within a program
  • Synonym: function - a named section of a program that performs a specific task
  • Synonym: procedure - a set of coded instructions that perform a specific task, often used in programming

Etymology

From French *routine* (habit, custom); computing sense from mid-20th century as technology developed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06594579-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
happening as a usual part of daily life; ordinary, not exceptional or unusual.
ordinary; happening regularly

Examples

For her, a routine checkup at the doctor's office revealed an unexpected result.
Most employees perform a variety of routine tasks every day at work.
Delays caused by weather are nothing unusual; they are quite routine for this flight.

Synset Examples

a placid everyday scene
it was a routine day
there's nothing quite like a real … train conductor to add color to a quotidian commute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everyday - ordinary, occurring daily
  • Synonym: ordinary - not unusual or special
  • Antonym: exceptional - unusual; not typical or routine

Etymology

From French 'routine' (custom, habit), from 'route' (way, path).
