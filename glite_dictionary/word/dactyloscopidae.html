Dactyloscopidae

1. NOUN

/ dækˌtɪloʊˈskɒpɪdiː / (AmE), / dækˌtɪlɒˈskɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marine fishes, commonly known as sand stargazers, that live burrowed in sandy seabeds and are known for their upward-facing eyes.
family of sand stargazer fishes

Examples

The Dactyloscopidae are well known for their habit of burying themselves in sand with only their eyes exposed.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the Dactyloscopidae family along the coast of South America.
Due to their cryptic behavior, Dactyloscopidae are often overlooked by casual divers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand stargazer - a small marine fish of the Dactyloscopidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Dactyloscopidae, based on genus Dactyloscopus, from Greek daktylos (finger) and skopos (watcher).

2. NOUN

/ dækˌtɪloʊˈskɒpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / dækˌtɪləʊˈskɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, elongated fishes known as sand stargazers, found in sandy coastal waters of the Americas
family of sand stargazer fishes

Examples

The Dactyloscopidae are known for burying themselves in the sand with only their eyes exposed.
Several members of the Dactyloscopidae family inhabit shallow waters along the American coastline.
Researchers recently discovered a new species in the Dactyloscopidae family off the coast of Brazil.

Etymology

From Greek 'daktylos' (finger) and 'skopos' (watcher), referring to aspects of the fishes' body and behavior.
