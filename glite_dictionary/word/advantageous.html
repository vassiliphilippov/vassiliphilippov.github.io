advantageous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs / (AmE), / ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs / (BrE)
giving someone or something a benefit or favorable position; resulting in an advantage
giving an advantage; beneficial

Examples

The new tax laws are advantageous to small businesses.
It would be advantageous for you to start saving earlier rather than later.
This location is advantageous because it is close to public transportation.

Synset Examples

a contract advantageous to our country
socially advantageous to entertain often

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneficial - producing good or helpful results
  • Antonym: disadvantageous - resulting in a disadvantage; unfavorable

Etymology

From Old French avantageux, from avantage (advantage).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs / (AmE), / ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs / (BrE)
helpful or beneficial in achieving a particular purpose, sometimes without regard for fairness
helpful or beneficial; serving one’s interest

Examples

It would be advantageous for the company to expand into new markets.
She took an advantageous position near the exit to leave early.
The terms of the agreement proved advantageous to both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneficial - producing good results or helpful effects
  • Antonym: disadvantageous - causing problems or difficulties; not beneficial

Etymology

From Old French avantageux, from avantage (advantage), based on Latin abante (from before).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00935102-s
Synset POS: s
