Crassula

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræs.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈkras.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of succulent herbs and small shrubs with woody stems, belonging to the family Crassulaceae.
a genus of succulent plants

Examples

Many gardeners enjoy growing Crassula because of its attractive fleshy leaves.
The most famous Crassula species is the jade plant, popular as a houseplant worldwide.
Botanists have identified over 200 species within the Crassula genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'crassus' meaning 'thick' or 'fat', referring to the thick, fleshy leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræs.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈkræsjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and small shrubs in the family Crassulaceae, having woody stems and succulent aerial parts, native mainly to southern Africa.
genus of succulent plants in Crassulaceae

Examples

Several species of Crassula are commonly grown as houseplants due to their attractive foliage.
The jade plant belongs to the Crassula genus and is native to South Africa.
Botanists often study Crassula for its unique adaptations to arid environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'crassus' meaning 'thick', referring to the thick leaves.
