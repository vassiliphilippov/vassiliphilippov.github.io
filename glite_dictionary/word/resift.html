to resift

1. VERB

/ riˈsɪft / (AmE), / riˈsɪft / (BrE)
to sift (something) again, especially to further refine or separate by sifting a second time
to sift again

Examples

After combining the ingredients, she decided to resift the flour to remove any remaining lumps.
If your mixture is still clumpy, it's best to resift it before proceeding with the recipe.
He had to resift the sand to ensure all the pebbles were removed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sift - to put (a substance) through a sieve; to separate finer from coarser particles

Etymology

From re- (again) + sift.
