to dematerialize

1. VERB

/ diˌməˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˌməˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become immaterial or disappear from view; to cease to have a physical form
become immaterial or disappear

Examples

The magician made the coin dematerialize before the audience's eyes.
As the hologram began to lose power, it slowly dematerialized into thin air.
In the story, ghosts are able to dematerialize at will and pass through walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: to materialize - to become visible or appear in physical form

Etymology

From de- (removal) + material + -ize; first recorded in English in the 19th century.
