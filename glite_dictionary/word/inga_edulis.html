Inga edulis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡə ɛˈduːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡə ɛˈdjuːlɪs / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical America, valued for its white flowers and often cultivated as an ornamental or shade tree
tropical evergreen tree with white flowers

Examples

The shade offered by the Inga edulis made the park bench the perfect place to read.
Farmers often plant Inga edulis to help restore soil fertility in tropical regions.
The sweet, edible pulp of Inga edulis pods is enjoyed by both children and adults.

Etymology

From Latin: Inga (genus name), edulis (edible), referring to the edible pulp of its seed pods.
