Berberis vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɜrbərɪs vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌbɜːbərɪs vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs / (BrE)
An upright deciduous shrub native to Europe, with clusters of red, juicy berries, widely naturalized in the United States; also known as common barberry.
a European shrub with red berries (common barberry)

Examples

The berries of Berberis vulgaris are often used in traditional jams and jellies.
Gardeners frequently plant Berberis vulgaris for its attractive foliage and bright red fruit.
Berberis vulgaris can be found growing wild along roadsides in many parts of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common barberry - another name for Berberis vulgaris, a shrub with red berries

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Berberis and species epithet vulgaris meaning 'common'.
