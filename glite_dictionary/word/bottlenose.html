bottlenose

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt.əlˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl.nəʊz / (BrE)
a dolphin with a rounded forehead and a well-developed beak, found mainly in the northern Atlantic and Mediterranean.
type of dolphin with rounded forehead

Examples

A bottlenose leaped playfully out of the water near our boat.
Scientists tracked a pod of bottlenose dolphins along the Mediterranean coast.
The bottlenose is easily recognized by its rounded forehead and prominent beak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a marine mammal known for intelligence and sociability

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːt.əlˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl.nəʊz / (BrE)
A species of beaked whale found in the northern Atlantic, distinguished by a bulbous forehead.
northern Atlantic beaked whale

Examples

The bottlenose is well adapted to deep-sea diving and can reach great depths in search of food.
Researchers have observed pods of bottlenose whales migrating along the northern Atlantic coastline.
Unlike dolphins, the bottlenose whale has a distinctive bulbous forehead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottlenose dolphin - a common dolphin species with a bottle-shaped snout

Etymology

From the resemblance of its nose to the shape of a bottle.
