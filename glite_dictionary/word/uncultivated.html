uncultivated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌltəˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd / (BrE)
Lacking education, refinement, or intellectual interests; not socially or intellectually developed.
lacking refinement or education

Examples

His uncultivated manners made him stand out at the formal dinner.
She considered his interests too narrow and uncultivated for her taste.
Despite his wealth, he remained uncultivated and uninterested in the arts.

Synset Examples

lowbrow tastes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultivated - having or showing good education, taste, and manners

Etymology

From un- (not) + cultivated, from Latin 'cultivatus' (prepared for crops, refined).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01337027-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking education, refinement, or knowledge of the arts.
lacking education or refinement

Examples

Some critics regarded him as uncultivated because he showed little interest in literature or the arts.
Her uncultivated manners set her apart in the refined company she found herself in.
He felt uncultivated next to his well-read and sophisticated colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultured - having refined taste, manners, and education
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From un- (not) + cultivated, from Latin cultīvātus, past participle of cultīvāre (to till, to cultivate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01956562-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of land or fields) not prepared or used for growing crops.
not prepared for growing crops (of land)

Examples

Much of the region is covered with uncultivated land and dense forest.
The farmer left the field uncultivated for several years to restore its fertility.
Wildflowers flourished in the uncultivated meadow behind the house.

Synset Examples

uncultivated land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultivated - land prepared for crops

Etymology

From un- (not) + cultivated, from Latin 'cultivatus' (tilled, cultivated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01839509-a
Synset POS: a
