dentition

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of baby teeth emerging through the gums; the formation and arrangement of teeth in the mouth
emergence of baby teeth; tooth arrangement
medical

Examples

The child's dentition began at around six months of age.
Proper care during the early stages of dentition is important for oral health.
Fever and irritability are common symptoms during dentition in infants.

Etymology

From Latin 'dentitio', from 'dens' (tooth).

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the number, kind, and arrangement of teeth in a person or animal
arrangement and type of teeth
medical

Examples

The fossil's dentition suggests it belonged to a herbivorous dinosaur.
Human dentition differs significantly from that of most other mammals.
Veterinarians examine an animal's dentition to estimate its age.

Etymology

From Latin "dentitio" (teething), from "dens" (tooth).
