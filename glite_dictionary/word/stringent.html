stringent

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈstrɪn.dʒənt / (BrE)
(of rules, requirements, or conditions) strict, precise, and exacting.
strict, precise, and exacting

Examples

The new regulations are far more stringent than the previous guidelines.
Many companies have adopted stringent security measures to protect their data.
Admission to the program is based on stringent academic standards.

Synset Examples

rigorous discipline
tight security
stringent safety measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigorous - strict and demanding
  • Antonym: lenient - not strict; tolerant

Etymology

From Latin 'stringent-', present participle of 'stringere' (to draw tight).
