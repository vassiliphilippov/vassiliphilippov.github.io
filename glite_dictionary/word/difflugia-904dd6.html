Difflugia

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfluːdʒiə / (AmE), / dɪˈfluːɡiə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater amoeboid protozoans characterized by their shell-like outer covering and found in various aquatic environments.
genus of freshwater amoeboid protozoans

Examples

Scientists observed a colony of Difflugia under the microscope to study its shell structure.
Difflugia is commonly found in ponds and slow-moving streams across the world.
The unique shells of Difflugia are composed of tiny particles gathered from their environment.

Etymology

From New Latin, named in the 19th century from Latin 'diffluere' (to flow away), referring to the amoeboid movement.
