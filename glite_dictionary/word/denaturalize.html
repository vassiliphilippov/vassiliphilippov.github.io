to denaturalize

1. VERB

/ diˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to deprive (someone) of the rights and duties of citizenship, typically by legal process
deprive of citizenship

Examples

After his criminal conviction, the government decided to denaturalize him.
The law allows the authorities to denaturalize individuals who obtained citizenship through fraud.
Many feared they could be denaturalized during the political crackdown.

Synset Examples

The former Nazi was denaturalized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to naturalize - to grant citizenship to someone

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + naturalize, ultimately from Medieval Latin naturalizare.

2. VERB

/ diˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈnatʃərəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make less natural or to make unnatural.
make less natural or unnatural

Examples

Excessive editing can denaturalize the flow of a documentary film.
Some critics argue that city life tends to denaturalize human behavior.
The constant use of digital effects may denaturalize the actor's performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to naturalize - make something more natural or conform to nature
  • Synonym: artificialize - make something more artificial

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + naturalize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00413785-v
Synset POS: v
