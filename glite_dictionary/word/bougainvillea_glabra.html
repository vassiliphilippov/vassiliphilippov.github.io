Bougainvillea glabra

1. NOUN

/ boʊˌɡæn.vɪˈliː.ə ˈɡleɪ.brə / (AmE), / bəʊˌɡan.vɪˈliː.ə ˈɡlæb.rə / (BrE)
A species of ornamental woody vine native to Brazil, known for its vibrant, papery bracts and continuous flowering.
species of ornamental Brazilian flowering vine

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a Bougainvillea glabra in full bloom, covered in dazzling magenta bracts.
She planted a Bougainvillea glabra along the fence, where it flourished in the summer sun.
Hummingbirds were frequently seen hovering near the Bougainvillea glabra to sip nectar from its tiny flowers.

Etymology

Named after French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville; 'glabra' from Latin meaning 'smooth' or 'hairless'.
