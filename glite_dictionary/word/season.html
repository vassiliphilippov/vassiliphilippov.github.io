season

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
A period of the year characterized by particular events or holidays, often recurring annually.
period marked by recurring holidays

Examples

Every year, the city is beautifully decorated for the season of lights.
Retailers often see an increase in sales during the holiday season.
The Christmas season brings families together to celebrate.

Synset Examples

it was the Christmas season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiday - a day of celebration or recreation when no work is done
  • Synonym: festival - a day or period of celebration, typically for religious reasons

Etymology

From Old French 'seson', from Latin 'sationem' (sowing, planting), later generalized to include periods of the year.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
a period of the year characterized by a particular activity, event, or phenomenon in a particular field
a period marked by special events or activities

Examples

The basketball team performed exceptionally well this season and made it to the finals.
Strawberries are sweetest during their peak season in early summer.
Tickets are hard to find at the start of the opera season.

Synset Examples

he celebrated his 10th season with the ballet company
she always looked forward to the avocado season

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-season - the time when a particular activity, event, or business is not active or popular

Etymology

From Latin 'sationem' (sowing), later Middle English 'sesoun' via Old French.

3. VERB

/ ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
to strengthen or make more experienced, especially through exposure to challenging conditions
make stronger or more experienced

Examples

The long winters in Alaska season even the most optimistic newcomers.
Years of living abroad can season a young professional, giving them resilience and perspective.
The demanding job quickly seasoned her, and she adapted to the challenges with confidence.

Synset Examples

This trip will season even the hardiest traveller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to toughen - to make physically or emotionally stronger
  • Antonym: to soften - to make less strong or resilient

Etymology

From Latin 'sationem' (sowing), by way of Old French 'saison' (season, right time), originally referencing growth and development.

5. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
Each of the four divisions of the year (spring, summer, autumn, and winter) marked by particular weather patterns and daylight hours, resulting from the Earth's changing position with regard to the sun.
division of the year by weather or astronomy

Examples

The leaves change color when a new season begins.
Farmers plan their crops around the changing seasons.
Winter is often considered the harshest season of the year.

Synset Examples

the regular sequence of the seasons

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spring - the season between winter and summer
  • Degree Higher: summer - the warmest season of the year
  • Degree Lower: autumn - the season between summer and winter
  • Degree Lower: winter - the coldest season of the year

Etymology

From Old French *saison*, from Latin *sationem* (sowing, planting).
