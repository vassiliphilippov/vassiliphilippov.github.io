highly

1. ADV

/ ˈhaɪli / (AmE), / ˈhaɪli / (BrE)
in a high position, level, or rank; having an important status or high standing
in a high position or status

Examples

Only highly skilled employees are considered for management positions.
The information was available only to highly ranked officials in the government.
She was promoted to a highly influential position within the company.

Synset Examples

details known by only a few highly placed persons

Etymology

From high + -ly (Old English hēah + -līce).

2. ADV

/ ˈhaɪli / (AmE), / ˈhaɪli / (BrE)
to a high degree, level, or rate; particularly used to describe something of significant value, standard, or amount.
to a high degree or level

Examples

The company offers highly competitive salaries to attract top talent.
She is a highly skilled engineer with many years of experience.
Many highly paid executives work in the city center.

Synset Examples

highly paid workers

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree or extent

Etymology

From high + -ly, combining the adjective 'high' with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈhaɪli / (AmE), / ˈhaɪli / (BrE)
to a high degree or extent; with great respect or admiration
to a high degree; with much respect

Examples

She was highly recommended for the position due to her impressive skills.
Most critics spoke highly of the film after its premiere.
The new design is highly efficient compared to the old model.

Synset Examples

highly successful
He spoke highly of her
does not think highly of his writing
extremely interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: barely - only just; by a small amount

Etymology

From 'high' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective 'high'.
