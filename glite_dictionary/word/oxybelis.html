Oxybelis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈbɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈbiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, arboreal snakes known as vine snakes, native to the Americas.
a genus of vine snakes

Examples

The Oxybelis genus contains several species of vine snakes found in Central and South America.
Researchers observed an Oxybelis climbing through the dense foliage of the rainforest.
Unlike other snakes, Oxybelis often moves slowly and relies on camouflage among vines.

Etymology

From Greek oxys (sharp) + belos (dart).

2. PROPN

/ ˌɑksɪˈbiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈbiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, arboreal snakes commonly known as vine snakes, found in Central and South America.
a genus of vine snakes

Examples

The genus Oxybelis includes several species of vine snakes known for their elongated bodies.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Oxybelis genus in the Amazon rainforest.
A herpetologist explained the hunting behavior of Oxybelis snakes to the audience.

Etymology

From Greek 'oxy' (sharp) and 'belos' (dart, arrow), referring to the snake's pointed snout.
