sou'easter

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈiːstər / (AmE), / səʊˈiːstə / (BrE)
A strong wind blowing from the southeast.
strong southeast wind

Examples

The fishing boat struggled to reach shore against the force of the sou'easter.
A violent sou'easter lashed the coast all night, causing widespread damage.
Sailors prepared for the incoming sou'easter by securing their vessels in the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nor'easter - a strong wind or storm from the northeast
  • Synonym: sou'wester - a strong wind or storm from the southwest

Etymology

Contraction of 'southeaster', modeled after terms like 'nor'easter'.
