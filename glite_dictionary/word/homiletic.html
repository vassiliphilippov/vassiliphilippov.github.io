homiletic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːməˈlɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to homiletics, the art of preaching or writing sermons
relating to preaching or sermons

Examples

The bishop delivered a homiletic lecture on the importance of compassion.
His writing style is often described as homiletic, aiming to inspire moral reflection.
The seminary offered a course in homiletic techniques for aspiring preachers.

Synset Examples

homiletic speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermonic - relating to, characteristic of, or resembling a sermon
  • Synonym: homiletics - the art of preaching or writing sermons

Etymology

From Late Latin homileticus, from Greek homilētikos (conversational, affable), from homilia (discourse, sermon).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːməˈlɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a homily or sermon; preachy in tone or style.
relating to a homily or sermon

Examples

Her homiletic lecture reminded the class of a sermon at church.
The bishop's speech was more homiletic than academic, appealing to moral values.
His essays often take a homiletic tone, encouraging personal reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preachy - having the tone or style of a sermon

Etymology

From Greek homilētikos (conversational), from homilos (assembly, crowd).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03085002-a
Synset POS: a
