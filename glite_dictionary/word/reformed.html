Reformed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
of or relating to certain Protestant Christian churches and theology, especially those following Calvinist traditions and arising from the Reformation, as distinct from Lutheranism.
relating to Calvinist Protestant churches

Examples

The Reformed churches emphasize the sovereignty of God in salvation.
Many Dutch immigrants in America established Reformed congregations.
She grew up attending a Reformed Protestant church in South Africa.

Synset Examples

Dutch Reformed theology

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Lutheran - relating to the branch of Protestant Christianity founded on the teachings of Martin Luther
  • Synonym: Calvinist - relating to the Protestant theological tradition founded by John Calvin

Etymology

From the past participle of reform, referring to churches reformed during the Protestant Reformation.
