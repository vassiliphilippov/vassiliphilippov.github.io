Antigonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈɡoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈɡəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Caproidae, commonly known as boarfishes, found in deep waters worldwide
genus of boarfishes (marine fish)

Examples

The deep-sea fish Antigonia is often studied for its unique body shape.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Antigonia genus off the coast of New Zealand.
Many marine biologists consider Antigonia important for understanding boarfish evolution.

Etymology

From Greek Antigone, a name in mythology, adapted for scientific naming.

2. PROPN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈɡoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈɡəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Caproidae, commonly referred to as boarfishes, characterized by their deep, laterally compressed bodies
genus of marine boarfishes

Examples

The species Antigonia capros is one of the most recognized members of this genus.
Scientists collected several specimens belonging to the genus Antigonia during the deep-sea survey.
Members of Antigonia are notable for their uniquely shaped, laterally compressed bodies.

Etymology

Modern Latin; named after Antigone, a figure from Greek mythology.
