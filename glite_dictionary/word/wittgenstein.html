Wittgenstein

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪtɡənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɪtɡənʃtaɪn / (BrE)
(1889–1951) Austrian-born British philosopher whose work greatly influenced modern logic and the philosophy of language.
British philosopher influential in logic and philosophy of language

Examples

Many contemporary philosophers have studied Wittgenstein and his later works on language.
The philosophy class devoted an entire semester to analyzing Wittgenstein's theories.
Discussions about logic often include references to Wittgenstein and his influence on the subject.

Etymology

Named after Ludwig Wittgenstein (1889–1951), a philosopher born in Austria.
