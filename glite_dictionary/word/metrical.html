metrical

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or having the rhythmical arrangement of syllables in poetry or music; pertaining to meter.
relating to poetic or musical rhythm

Examples

The poem's metrical structure gives it a musical quality that enhances its meaning.
Her analysis focused on the metrical patterns found in classical Greek verse.
Many songs rely on a strong metrical beat to guide the listener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythmic - having a regular repeated pattern of sounds or movements
  • Antonym: ametrical - not following a set meter

Etymology

From Latin 'metricalis', based on Greek 'metron' (measure).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to the meter as a standard of measurement.
relating to the meter (measurement)

Examples

Many countries have adopted the metrical system for standardizing weights and measures.
The recipe lists ingredients using metrical units instead of imperial ones.
He converted all the dimensions into metrical equivalents for the international team.

Synset Examples

the metric system
metrical equivalents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric - relating to the metric system of measurement

Etymology

From Late Latin metrum (measure, meter) + English -al.

Synset POS: a
