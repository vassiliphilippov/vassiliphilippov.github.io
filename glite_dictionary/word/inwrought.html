inwrought

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈrɔt / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɔːt / (BrE)
having a decorative pattern worked or woven in
decoratively woven or worked in

Examples

The ancient tapestry was inwrought with gold threads and intricate motifs.
The poet described her gown as inwrought with silver stars and delicate vines.
The palace walls were inwrought with elaborate designs that showcased the artisans' skill.

Synset Examples

an inwrought design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidered - decorated with patterns sewn on with thread

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'in-' and 'wrought', past participle of 'work'.
