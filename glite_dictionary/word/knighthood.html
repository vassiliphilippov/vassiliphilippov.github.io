knighthood

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.hʊd / (BrE)
The rank, title, or collective body of knights, especially as part of an aristocratic order.
the rank or body of knights

Examples

He was honored with knighthood for his services to literature.
The tradition of knighthood dates back to the Middle Ages.
Many aspire to achieve knighthood as recognition for their accomplishments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalry - the medieval knightly system including its moral, social, and religious codes

Etymology

From Old English cniht (boy, servant) + -hood (state or condition).
