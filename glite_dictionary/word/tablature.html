tablature

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæblətʃər / (AmE), / ˈtablətʃə / (BrE)
a system of musical notation that indicates which fingers to use on an instrument, rather than the pitches to be played, commonly used for fretted stringed instruments such as the guitar or lute
musical notation showing finger placement

Examples

Learning to read tablature made it much easier for her to play new guitar songs.
Many online tutorials provide tablature for beginners who cannot read standard notation.
The lute pieces were written in tablature instead of modern sheet music.

Etymology

From French 'tablature', from Italian 'tablatura', from Latin 'tabula' (a board or table).
