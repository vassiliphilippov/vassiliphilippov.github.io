Gadus morhua

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪdəs mɔrˈhuə / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪdəs mɔːˈruːə / (BrE)
the Atlantic cod, a commercially important marine fish found in the North Atlantic Ocean, valued for its meat and oil
Atlantic cod species

Examples

The fishing industry relies heavily on Gadus morhua for both local consumption and export.
Overfishing has caused the population of Gadus morhua to decline dramatically in recent decades.
Researchers are studying Gadus morhua to understand its migratory patterns and breeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic cod - the common name for Gadus morhua

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gadus' meaning cod, 'morhua' meaning codfish.
