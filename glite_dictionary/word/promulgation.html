promulgation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːməlˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒməlˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official announcement or formal declaration that puts a new law or ordinance into effect
official enactment or announcement of a law
legal

Examples

The promulgation of the new policy was announced in the government gazette.
The law takes effect only after its official promulgation by the president.
Following the promulgation of the ordinance, all residents were required to comply with the new regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enactment - the process of passing a law

Etymology

From Latin promulgatio (proclamation, publication), from promulgare (to publish, make known).

the formal act of making an official announcement or declaration publicly known, especially by a government or authority
formal public announcement or proclamation

Examples

Following the promulgation of the law, citizens were required to comply immediately.
The government proceeded with the promulgation of several key policy changes.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːməlˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒm(ə)lˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
an official public announcement or formal proclamation, especially one issued by an authority
official public announcement

Examples

The government's promulgation of the new law was broadcast on national television.
Details of the event were shared following the official promulgation by the city council.
His resignation became effective after the formal promulgation in the company bulletin.

Synset Examples

the announcement appeared in the local newspaper
the promulgation was written in English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announcement - a formal public statement about a fact, occurrence, or intention
  • Synonym: declaration - an official or formal statement, especially one that is publicly made

Etymology

From Latin promulgatio (publication, proclamation), from promulgare (to make known publicly).
