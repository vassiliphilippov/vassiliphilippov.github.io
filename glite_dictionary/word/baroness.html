baroness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærənɛs / (AmE), / ˈbærənɪs / (BrE)
A woman who holds the rank of baron in her own right or is the wife or widow of a baron.
female baron or wife/widow of a baron

Examples

The baroness hosted a grand banquet at her estate.
After her husband's death, she became known as the baroness of Ashford.
The baroness was respected for her leadership in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a man holding the rank of baron or nobleman
  • Degree Higher: duchess - a woman holding the rank of duchess or wife/widow of a duke

Etymology

From Old French baronesse, from baron (nobleman) + -ess (feminine suffix).
