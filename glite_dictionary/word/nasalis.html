Nasalis

1. NOUN

/ nəˈsælɪs / (AmE), / nəˈsɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys found in Southeast Asia, known for the proboscis monkey (Nasalis larvatus) with its distinctive large nose.
genus of monkeys including the proboscis monkey

Examples

The Nasalis genus is best known for its sole member, the proboscis monkey.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of Nasalis in the mangrove swamps of Borneo.
The unique nose of the Nasalis is believed to play a role in vocal communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proboscis monkey - a monkey species with a large nose, native to Borneo

Etymology

From Latin 'nasalis' meaning 'of the nose', referring to the prominent nose of the proboscis monkey.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈseɪlɪs / (AmE), / nəˈseɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys known as proboscis monkeys, native to Southeast Asia and characterized by their long noses.
genus of proboscis monkeys

Examples

The Nasalis genus contains only a single species, the proboscis monkey.
Scientists observed the unique nasal features that distinguish Nasalis from other primate genera.
The habitat of Nasalis is largely restricted to the island of Borneo.

Etymology

From Latin 'nasus' (nose), referring to the distinctive nose of the proboscis monkey.
