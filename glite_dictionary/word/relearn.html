to relearn

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈlɜrn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to learn something again because you have forgotten it or have not done it for a long time
learn again after forgetting or neglecting

Examples

After the injury, she had to relearn how to use her left hand.
Many adults decide to relearn a language they studied in school.
When his computer crashed, he was forced to relearn the basics of the software.

Synset Examples

After the accident, he could not walk for months and had to relearn how to walk down stairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to learn - gain knowledge or skill by studying or experience

Etymology

From re- (again) + learn.
