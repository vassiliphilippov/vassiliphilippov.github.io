Viocin

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊsɪn / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊsɪn / (BrE)
A basic polypeptide antibiotic (trade name) administered, often with other drugs, in the treatment of tuberculosis.
antibiotic used to treat tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Viocin as part of her tuberculosis treatment regimen.
Some strains of bacteria have developed resistance to Viocin over the years.
Patients receiving Viocin are closely monitored for potential side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viomycin - a polypeptide antibiotic used to treat tuberculosis, also known as Viomycin
