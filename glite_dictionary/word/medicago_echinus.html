Medicago echinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːɡoʊ ɛˈkaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːɡəʊ ɪˈkaɪnəs / (BrE)
an annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by spiny seed pods and leaves with dark spots
a Mediterranean annual plant with spiny pods

Examples

Medicago echinus thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout the Mediterranean region.
The spiny seed pods of Medicago echinus can easily attach to animal fur.
Botanists recorded the flowering period of Medicago echinus during their spring survey.

Etymology

From the genus name Medicago (from Latin, referring to plants originally from Media) and species epithet echinus (Greek for 'hedgehog,' referring to the spiny seed pods).
