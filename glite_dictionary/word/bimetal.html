bimetal

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a material formed by bonding together sheets of two different metals, often to combine their distinct properties
material of two bonded metals

Examples

A bimetal strip is crucial in the mechanism of many household thermostats.
The company specializes in manufacturing bimetal to improve heat sensitivity in devices.
Electrical engineers often use bimetal components in circuit breakers for overload protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a mixture of two or more metals

Etymology

From bi- (two) + metal, indicating its composition of two metals.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
composed of two different metals or alloys, especially in sheets bonded together
made of two different metals bonded together

Examples

The thermostat uses a bimetal strip to sense temperature changes.
A bimetal rod bends when heated because the two metals expand at different rates.
Engineers often select bimetal components for their unique thermal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a mixture of two or more metals

Etymology

From bi- (two) + metal, indicating composition of two metals.
