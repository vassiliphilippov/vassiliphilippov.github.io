Myroxylon balsamum pereirae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɑksiˌlɒn bæˈlsæməm pəˈrɛəreɪ / (AmE), / mɪˈrɒksɪlən ˈbælsəməm pəˈrɪəreɪ / (BrE)
A tree native to South and Central America that yields an aromatic resin called balsam of Peru, used in perfumes and flavorings.
tree yielding balsam of Peru

Examples

The resin collected from Myroxylon balsamum pereirae is widely used in the perfume industry.
Traditional healers in Central America value Myroxylon balsamum pereirae for its medicinal properties.
Exportation of Myroxylon balsamum pereirae has contributed to local economies due to the high demand for its balsam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam of Peru - aromatic resin derived from Myroxylon balsamum pereirae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'myron' (perfume, balsam) and 'xylon' (wood); 'balsamum' from Latin for 'balsam.'
