nociceptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnoʊsɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌnəʊsɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
relating to the perception or signaling of pain, especially as caused by harmful or potentially harmful stimuli to nerves.
relating to pain perception or response
medical

Examples

The study focused on nociceptive responses in patients undergoing surgery.
Some painkillers target nociceptive pathways to relieve discomfort.
Doctors observed a nociceptive reflex when the area was stimulated.

Synset Examples

a nociceptive spinal reflex

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing or characterized by pain

Etymology

From Latin 'nocere' (to harm) and 'receptive'.
