Endoprocta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈprɑktə / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈprɒktə / (BrE)
a small phylum of aquatic, mainly marine, invertebrate animals sometimes classified as a subphylum of Bryozoa
aquatic invertebrate phylum

Examples

The Endoprocta are notable for their crown of tentacles used in feeding.
Researchers found new species of Endoprocta along the rocky coastline.
Unlike Bryozoa, Endoprocta have their anus located within the ring of tentacles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryozoa - a phylum of aquatic invertebrate animals, sometimes called moss animals

Etymology

From Greek 'endo-' (within) + 'proktos' (anus), referring to the position of the anus within the body.
