Prumnopitys andina

1. NOUN

/ prum-noh-PIH-tis an-DEE-nuh / (AmE), / prum-nə-PIH-tɪs an-DIH-nə / (BrE)
A South American evergreen tree or shrub, native to Chile and Argentina, also known as the Chilean plum yew.
South American evergreen tree or shrub

Examples

The Prumnopitys andina is highly valued for its durable wood and attractive foliage.
Several Prumnopitys andina were planted in the botanical garden for conservation purposes.
Because of deforestation, the Prumnopitys andina is now considered a vulnerable species.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name Prumnopitys and 'andina' meaning 'of the Andes'.
