family Gobiesocidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ɡoʊˌbiːsoʊˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ɡəʊˌbɪsiːˈdəɪ / (BrE)
A family of small, marine fishes known as clingfishes, notable for their suckerlike pelvic fins which allow them to attach to surfaces.
family of clingfishes (marine fishes)

Examples

The family Gobiesocidae is known for its unique pelvic fins that form a suction disc.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the family Gobiesocidae near the coral reefs.
Members of the family Gobiesocidae can cling tightly to rocks and seaweed in turbulent waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clingfish - a small fish with a sucker formed by the pelvic fins

Etymology

From scientific Latin Gobiesocidae, based on type genus Gobiesox.
