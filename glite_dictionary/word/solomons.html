Solomons

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑləmənz / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmənz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, the northernmost of which belong to Papua New Guinea, while the remainder form the independent state of Solomon Islands within the Commonwealth.
group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Solomons have a rich history influenced by various cultures and colonial powers.
Tropical storms often affect the Solomons during the rainy season.
She traveled to the Solomons to study marine biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solomon Islands - an independent country in the southwestern Pacific, part of the Solomons archipelago

Etymology

Named after King Solomon, as first used by Spanish explorer Álvaro de Mendaña in the 16th century.
