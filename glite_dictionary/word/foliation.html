foliation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of coating glass with metal foil.
coating glass with metal foil

Examples

The foliation of the window panes gave them a shimmering, golden appearance.
Artisans perfected the technique of foliation in cathedral glass.
The museum described the foliation process used in the restoration of ancient mirrors.

Etymology

From Latin 'folium' (leaf), referring to thin sheets of metal.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Botany) The process by which leaves form and develop on a plant.
process of forming leaves
medical

Examples

The foliation of the plant occurs rapidly in spring as the weather warms.
Early foliation can leave plants vulnerable to late frosts.
Botanists study foliation patterns to understand the growth cycle of different species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defoliation - The removal or loss of leaves from a plant
  • Synonym: leafing - The production of leaves by a plant

Etymology

From Latin folium (leaf) + -ation (indicating action or process).

/ ˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Geology) the arrangement or formation of thin, leaflike layers or sheets within a rock, often as a result of metamorphic processes.
arrangement of layers in a rock (geology)

Examples

The distinct foliation seen in the schist was formed under immense pressure.
Geologists studied the foliation patterns to determine the history of the rock formation.
The presence of foliation indicates that the rock underwent regional metamorphism.

Etymology

From Latin 'folium' (leaf) + '-ation', referencing a leaflike structure.
