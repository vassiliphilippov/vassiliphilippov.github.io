pietistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪ.əˈtɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.əˈtɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Pietism, a movement within Lutheranism emphasizing personal piety and devotion.
relating to Pietism (religious movement)

Examples

The church promoted a pietistic approach to worship, emphasizing personal spirituality.
Her pietistic beliefs guided her daily actions and moral decisions.
Nineteenth-century missions were often driven by pietistic fervor.

Synset Examples

the Pietistic movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devout - having or showing deep religious feeling or commitment

Etymology

From Pietism (a Protestant movement), based on German 'Pietismus', from Latin 'pietas' (piety) + '-istic'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
Showing or characterized by exaggerated or hypocritical displays of piety or religious devotion.
excessively or hypocritically pious

Examples

Her pietistic attitude alienated both friends and family.
The novel criticizes the pietistic hypocrisy of certain social classes.
He gave a pietistic speech that sounded insincere to most listeners.

Synset Examples

a sickening sanctimonious smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others

Etymology

From German pietistisch, from Pietist (a member of a religious movement emphasizing piety) + -ic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01786645-s
Synset POS: s
