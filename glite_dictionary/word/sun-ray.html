sun-ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (BrE)
an artificial ultraviolet light ray produced by a sunray lamp, often used for therapeutic or tanning purposes
artificial ultraviolet light from a lamp

Examples

The dermatologist recommended short exposure to sun-rays for treating certain skin conditions.
Special lamps emit sun-rays that simulate natural sunlight.
Do not look directly at the sun-ray lamp when it is turned on.
