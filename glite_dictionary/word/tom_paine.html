Tom Paine

1. PROPN

/ tɑm peɪn / (AmE), / tɒm peɪn / (BrE)
(1737–1809) An English-born American political activist and writer whose works supported the American Revolution and the French Revolution.
English-born writer & political activist, revolutionary thinker

Examples

Tom Paine wrote influential pamphlets that inspired American colonists to seek independence.
Many historians credit Tom Paine with helping to shape modern democratic ideas.
Several museums feature exhibits dedicated to the life of Tom Paine.
