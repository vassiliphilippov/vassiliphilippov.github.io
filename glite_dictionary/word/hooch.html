hooch

1. NOUN

/ huːtʃ / (AmE), / huːtʃ / (BrE)
illicitly distilled alcoholic liquor, often considered inferior in quality
illicit or inferior alcoholic liquor
informal

Examples

During Prohibition, people often made their own hooch in hidden stills.
That bottle of hooch tasted harsh, but it was all we could find.
The authorities confiscated several gallons of illegal hooch from the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally made or distributed distilled alcohol
  • Degree Higher: whisky - distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash

Etymology

Shortened from Hoochinoo, the name of an Alaskan tribe known for brewing alcoholic liquor; 19th century American English.
