flex

1. NOUN

/ flɛks / (AmE), / flɛks / (BrE)
an act of bending, tightening, or tensing a muscle, especially to show strength
act of tensing or bending a muscle

Examples

He did a quick flex for the camera after finishing his workout.
With a dramatic flex of her arm, she showed off her new muscle definition.
During the contest, each competitor performed a signature flex on stage.

Synset Examples

he gave his biceps a flex to impress the ladies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexion - the act of bending a limb or joint
  • Antonym: to relax - to make or become less tense

Etymology

From Latin 'flectere' (to bend).

2. VERB

/ flɛks / (AmE), / flɛks / (BrE)
to bend or cause something to bend, forming a curve; to become curved rather than straight
to bend or curve

Examples

The metal rod began to flex under the weight of the heavy boxes.
Glass can flex slightly before it shatters under stress.
The tree branch will flex in strong winds but rarely snaps.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology
