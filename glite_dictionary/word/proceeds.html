proceeds

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.siːdz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.siːdz / (BrE)
money obtained from a particular event or activity, especially the sale of goods or property.
money received from a sale or event

Examples

The company donated all the proceeds from the auction to charity.
After selling the house, they divided the proceeds equally among the heirs.
All proceeds from the fundraising event will support cancer research.

Synset Examples

the average return was about 5%

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue - total income generated by a business or activity
  • Degree Lower: profit - financial gain after expenses are subtracted

Etymology

From the noun use of the verb 'proceed', from Old French proceder, from Latin procedere 'go forward'.
