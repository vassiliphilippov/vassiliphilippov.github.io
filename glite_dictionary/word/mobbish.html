mobbish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːbɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɒbɪʃ / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a mob, especially in being disorderly or lawless
like a mob; disorderly or lawless

Examples

The protest took on a mobbish atmosphere as anger flared among the crowd.
His mobbish followers ignored the law and acted on impulse.
Some feared the discussion would descend into a mobbish shouting match.

Synset Examples

fanned mounting tension into mobbish terrorizing
moblike mentality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob-like - resembling a mob; having qualities of a mob
  • Antonym: orderly - well organized or controlled; not disorderly

Etymology

From 'mob' (short for 'mobile' meaning the common people) + '-ish' (forming adjectives).
