saprozoic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæp.rəˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsæp.rəˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
(of certain animals) feeding on dead or decaying animal matter.
feeding on dead or decaying animal matter

Examples

Many protozoa are described as saprozoic because they absorb nutrients from decomposing matter.
Certain worms exhibit saprozoic behavior by consuming decaying animal tissue.
A saprozoic organism plays a vital role in the ecosystem by recycling dead animal remains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saprophagous - feeding on decaying organic matter

Etymology

From Greek sapros (rotten) + zoon (animal) + -ic.
