decency

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being polite, respectful, and behaving in a socially acceptable way
quality of being polite and respectable

Examples

She showed great decency by helping the stranger in need.
There is a certain level of decency expected in the workplace.
Basic human decency requires us to treat others with respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards of behavior
  • Antonym: indecency - the quality of being offensive or improper

Etymology

From Latin 'decentia', from 'decens' (fitting, proper).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənsi / (BrE)
behavior or standards that conform to accepted notions of propriety or morality; the quality of being decent.
behavior conforming to propriety or morality

Examples

He acted with proper decency even when others did not.
It is a matter of decency to treat everyone with respect.
Many people believe that decency in public life is declining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards of behavior
  • Antonym: indecency - lack of decency; behavior that is improper or immoral

Etymology

From Latin 'decentia' (becoming, appropriate), from 'decens' (fitting, proper).
