lugubriousness

1. NOUN

/ luˈɡubriəsnəs / (AmE), / luˈɡjuːbrɪəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being excessively mournful or gloomy, especially in a way that seems exaggerated or insincere
excessive mournfulness or gloominess

Examples

Her constant lugubriousness at social gatherings made her difficult to be around.
He sank into a mood of lugubriousness after hearing the disappointing news.
The play's atmosphere was heavy with lugubriousness from the opening scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloominess - the quality of being sad or depressed
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin 'lugubris' (mournful) + English suffix '-ness.'
