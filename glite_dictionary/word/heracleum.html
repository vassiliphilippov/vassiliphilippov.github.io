Heracleum

1. NOUN

/ həˈrækliəm / (AmE), / hɛˈræklɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the parsley family, characterized by thick rootstocks and large umbels of white flowers, commonly known as cow parsnip or hogweed.
genus of plants in the parsley family

Examples

The invasive Heracleum species can pose a risk to both wildlife and humans.
Many gardeners struggle to control Heracleum in their backyards due to its rapid spread.
Some Heracleum plants, like giant hogweed, contain phototoxic sap that can cause skin irritation.

Etymology

From Latin 'Heracleum', from Greek 'Herakleion', named after the mythological hero Heracles (Hercules).

2. NOUN

/ həˈrækliəm / (AmE), / hɛˈrækliəm / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed perennial plants, typically with thick rootstocks and large umbels of white flowers, including cow parsnip and giant hogweed.
genus of perennial plants with large umbels

Examples

The Heracleum genus includes both cow parsnip and the highly toxic giant hogweed.
Scientists studied the spread of Heracleum species in temperate regions.
Be careful: some Heracleum plants can cause severe skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cow parsnip - a common species in the genus Heracleum
  • Synonym: giant hogweed - a large invasive plant of the genus Heracleum

Etymology

From Latin 'Heracleum', after Heracles (Hercules), reflecting the large size of the plants.
