Muscidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmʌsɪdɪː / (BrE)
a family of two-winged flies including the common housefly and related species
family of flies, includes housefly

Examples

The Muscidae family includes many species that are common in urban environments.
Entomologists study the Muscidae to understand the spread of diseases.
The housefly, a member of the Muscidae, is known for its worldwide distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housefly - a common fly found in homes, scientific name Musca domestica

Etymology

From New Latin Muscidae, from Latin musca (fly).
