puerperium

1. NOUN

/ pjuːˈɜrpiəriəm / (AmE), / pjʊəˈpɜːpəriəm / (BrE)
the period after childbirth during which the mother's reproductive organs return to their normal state and the body recovers from pregnancy
period after childbirth when the body recovers
medical

Examples

The obstetrician monitored her closely throughout the puerperium to ensure proper recovery.
Hormonal changes during the puerperium can significantly affect a new mother's physical and emotional well-being.
Infections acquired in the puerperium may require prompt medical attention.

Synset Examples

a perinatologist cared for her during the puerperium

Etymology

From Latin 'puerpera' (a woman in childbed), from 'puer' (child) + 'parere' (to bring forth).
