Sciaena

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːnə / (AmE), / saɪˈiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Sciaenidae, commonly known as croakers.
genus of croaker fishes

Examples

The Sciaena genus includes several important species for commercial fisheries.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sciaena in the Atlantic Ocean.
Many members of Sciaena are commonly referred to as croakers due to the sounds they produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croaker - a common name for fish in the family Sciaenidae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek skiaina (a kind of fish).
