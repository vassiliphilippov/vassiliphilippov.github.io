to dominate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑː.mə.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to have a commanding position over; to overlook from a position above
overlook from a height

Examples

From its hilltop perch, the castle dominates the entire valley below.
A tall skyscraper dominates the city skyline, making it visible from miles away.
Their apartment balcony dominates the busy intersection, giving them a great view of downtown.

Synset Examples

The villa dominates the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to have a view over something from a higher position

Etymology

From Latin dominari (to rule, have power over), from dominus (master).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02701971-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɑːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to be more important, noticeable, or significant than something else
be more significant than

Examples

The new skyscraper will dominate the city skyline, making other buildings seem small.
Her achievements dominate every conversation during family gatherings.
The debate about climate change tends to dominate discussions at international conferences.

Synset Examples

the tragedy overshadowed the couple's happiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overshadow - appear more important or significant than; outshine
  • Synonym: eclipse - to overshadow or surpass in significance

Etymology

From Latin dominari (to rule, to dominate), from dominus (master).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02750155-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdɑː.məˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to have control, influence, or power over someone or something; to master or defeat completely
have control or power over

Examples

The company continued to dominate the market despite strong competition.
She let fear dominate her decisions, leading to missed opportunities.
In the chess game, he managed to dominate his opponent from the very start.

Synset Examples

Her pain completely mastered her
The methods can master the problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise power over something or someone
  • Synonym: master - to gain complete control or understanding of something
  • Antonym: to submit - to yield to another's authority or control
  • Antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure

Etymology

From Latin dominari, from dominus (master, lord).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02545000-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈdɑːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to have control or power over someone or something; to be the most important or noticeable part of something
to control or have power over

Examples

In the match, one team began to dominate after halftime, controlling the ball completely.
Fear can sometimes dominate our thoughts and prevent us from taking risks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise power over; to direct
  • Antonym: subordinate - to treat or regard as of lesser importance than something else

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule, to dominate), from 'dominus' (master).

5. VERB

/ ˈdɑmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to be greater or more important than others in number, quantity, power, status, or influence
be greater in number, power, or influence

Examples

Large corporations dominate the technology sector, shaping innovation and consumer trends worldwide.
In this region, women dominate the workforce, making up over 65% of employees.
Tall pine trees dominate the landscape, blocking sunlight from reaching the forest floor.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predominate - to be the largest or most important part
  • Synonym: reign - to hold supreme power or authority; to be predominant
  • Antonym: to submit - to yield or surrender to the control or authority of another

Etymology

From Latin dominari (to rule, to master), from dominus (master, lord).
