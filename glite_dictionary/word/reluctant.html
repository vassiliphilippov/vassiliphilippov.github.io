reluctant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
unwilling or disinclined to do something; hesitant or not eager to become involved.
unwilling or hesitant

Examples

She was reluctant to accept the invitation at first, but eventually agreed.
Many employees are reluctant to speak up during meetings.
As a child, he was always reluctant to try new foods.

Synset Examples

they were usually reluctant to socialize
reluctant to help

Related Terms

  • Antonym: willing - ready or prepared to do something
  • Antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic about doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari' meaning 'to struggle against'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
unwilling and hesitant to do something; not eager
unwilling or hesitant

Examples

She was reluctant to speak in front of the large audience.
His reluctant agreement was better than none at all.
The child gave a reluctant smile when asked about the trip.

Synset Examples

a reluctant smile
loath to admit a mistake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something
  • Antonym: willing - ready or prepared to do something
  • Synonym: hesitant - uncertain or slow to act

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari' (to struggle against), from 're-' (against) + 'luctari' (to struggle).

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
Unwilling or not eager to do something; showing hesitation or resistance.
unwilling; not eager

Examples

She was reluctant to accept the invitation at first, but eventually agreed.
Many employees are reluctant to speak up in meetings for fear of criticism.
He gave a reluctant nod, unsure whether he wanted to proceed.

Synset Examples

foreigners stubbornly reluctant to accept our ways
fresh from college and reluctant for the moment to marry him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: willing - ready or prepared to do something
  • Synonym: hesitant - uncertain or slow in acting or speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari' meaning 'to struggle against'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00815538-s
Synset POS: s
