Erignathus

1. NOUN

/ ɛrɪɡˈneɪθəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪɡˈneɪθəs / (BrE)
A genus of large Arctic seals known as bearded seals, characterized by their prominent whiskers and found on sea ice.
genus of Arctic bearded seals

Examples

The Erignathus is well known for its thick whiskers, which help it sense food on the ocean floor.
Biologists observed several Erignathus basking on the Arctic ice during their research expedition.
The Erignathus plays an important role in the ecosystem as both predator and prey in polar regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearded seal - a large Arctic seal (Erignathus barbatus) with prominent whiskers

Etymology

From Greek 'eri-' (very) and 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the prominent jaw or beard.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪɡˈneɪθəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪɡˈneɪθəs / (BrE)
A genus of seals that includes the bearded seal, found in Arctic and sub-Arctic regions.
genus of seals (bearded seal)

Examples

The genus Erignathus is best known for its only living species, the bearded seal.
Researchers studied the habits of Erignathus in the coastal waters of Greenland.
The thick whiskers of Erignathus help it forage for food on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearded seal - a large seal with long whiskers found in Arctic regions

Etymology

From Greek 'eri-' (woolly) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the bearded appearance.
