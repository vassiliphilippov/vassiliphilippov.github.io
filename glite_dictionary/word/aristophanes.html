Aristophanes

1. PROPN

/ ˌærəˈstɑfəniːz / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɒfəniːz / (BrE)
(c. 448–c. 380 BC) An ancient Greek dramatist, renowned for his comedies, who is considered one of the greatest comic playwrights of classical Athens.
ancient Greek comic playwright

Examples

Many scholars study the works of Aristophanes to understand ancient Greek society.
Aristophanes wrote some of the most influential comedies in classical literature.
We read several comedies by Aristophanes in our literature class.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀριστοφάνης (Aristophanēs).
