to horse-trade

1. VERB

/ ˈhɔrs ˌtreɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs ˌtreɪd / (BrE)
to negotiate deals, agreements, or compromises in a shrewd, tough, or pragmatic manner, often involving much bargaining or reciprocal concessions.
negotiate with much bargaining

Examples

The politicians continued to horse-trade until they reached a compromise on the bill.
Both companies horse-traded for hours before agreeing to the final terms of the merger.
It's not unusual for diplomats to engage in horse-trading behind the scenes during major negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - to negotiate the terms of an agreement
  • Synonym: haggle - to argue persistently over the cost of something or terms of an agreement

Etymology

Alludes to the spirited and shrewd bargaining traditionally associated with trading horses.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɔrsˌtreɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌtreɪd / (BrE)
to negotiate or bargain, especially in a shrewd or hard way to achieve one's own advantage
negotiate or bargain shrewdly

Examples

The two companies began to horse-trade over the terms of the merger.
He's famous for his ability to horse-trade behind the scenes in political deals.
After hours of horse-trading, they finally reached a compromise.

Synset Examples

He’s known for his skill in horsetrade negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - to discuss something in order to reach an agreement
  • Synonym: bargain - to negotiate the terms of an agreement or deal

Etymology

From the traditional practice of bartering horses, known to require sharp bargaining skills.
