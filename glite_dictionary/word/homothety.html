homothety

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑːθəti / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒθəti / (BrE)
(mathematics) a transformation of an affine space that scales all points with respect to a fixed point by the same ratio, resulting in an isotropic scaling.
scaling transformation with one fixed point (math)
medical

Examples

The triangle was mapped onto itself by a homothety centered at the origin.
A homothety with a ratio of 2 expands every point away from the center by a factor of two.
In geometry class, we learned that homothety preserves the shape but not the size of a figure.

Synset Examples

Successive applications of homotheties with coefficients k1 and k2 is either a homothety with coefficient k1k2, if the latter differs from 1, or a parallel translation, otherwise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilation - a transformation that produces an image that is the same shape as the original, but is a different size (mathematics)

Etymology

From Greek homothetos (of equal magnitude), from homos (same) + thetos (placed).
