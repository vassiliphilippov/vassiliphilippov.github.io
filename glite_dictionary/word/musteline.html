musteline

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌstɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A member of the weasel family; a mammal belonging to the family Mustelidae, such as a weasel, badger, otter, or mink.
member of the weasel family (Mustelidae)

Examples

The musteline is well adapted for hunting small mammals in dense undergrowth.
Several species of musteline are found in both Europe and North America.
Curious children watched as the zookeeper explained how the musteline hunts for its food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustelid - any member of the weasel family, Mustelidae

Etymology

From Latin mustela (weasel).
