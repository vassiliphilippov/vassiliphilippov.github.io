amidship

1. ADV

/ əˈmɪdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈmɪdʃɪp / (BrE)
in or toward the middle part of a ship or vessel.
in the middle of a ship

Examples

The lifeboats were stored amidship for easy access in an emergency.
She walked amidship to check the stability of the vessel.
Cargo is often balanced amidship to ensure the ship remains level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midships - in or toward the middle part of a ship

Etymology

From 'amidships', which combines 'amid' (in the middle of) and 'ship'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈmɪdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈmɪdʃɪp / (BrE)
Located in the middle part of a ship or aircraft.
in the middle part of a ship or aircraft

Examples

The captain ordered the repairs to begin amidship, where the hull was damaged.
Passengers seated amidship usually experience less motion during a rough voyage.
The lifeboats are stored amidship for easy access from either side of the vessel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fore - the front part of a ship or aircraft
  • Antonym: aft - the rear part of a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From 'amidships', a combination of 'amid' and 'ship', meaning in the middle of the ship.

3. ADV

/ əˈmɪdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈmɪdʃɪp / (BrE)
at or near the center of a ship; toward the middle part of a vessel
toward the middle of a ship

Examples

The captain instructed the crew to gather amidship for a safety drill.
Repairs were needed amidship after the vessel hit some floating debris.
Passengers often prefer cabins located amidship to minimize movement from waves.

Synset Examples

in the late 19th century, engines were placed in front, amidships, and at the rear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amidships - in or toward the middle of a ship
  • Antonym: astern - toward the rear (stern) of a ship
  • Antonym: forward - toward the front (bow) of a ship

Etymology

From prefix 'a-' (on) + Middle English 'midship' (middle of the ship).
