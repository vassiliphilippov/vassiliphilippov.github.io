rifleman

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlmən / (AmE), / ˈraɪfləmən / (BrE)
a soldier, typically an infantryman, whose primary weapon is a rifle
soldier armed with a rifle

Examples

The rifleman took cover behind a tree as the enemy advanced.
Each rifleman was equipped with sufficient ammunition for the mission.
The company included several skilled riflemen who led the assault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier belonging to the infantry

Etymology

Formed from 'rifle' + 'man' in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlmən / (AmE), / ˈraɪflmən / (BrE)
A soldier or person skilled in the use of a rifle, especially as a member of a military unit.
skilled user of a rifle, often a soldier

Examples

The army assigned a highly trained rifleman to scout ahead of the platoon.
As a seasoned rifleman, he quickly found a vantage point to cover his team.
During the competition, each rifleman demonstrated exceptional accuracy at long distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marksman - a person skilled at shooting, especially with a gun

Etymology

From 'rifle' (the firearm) + 'man'.

Synset POS: n
