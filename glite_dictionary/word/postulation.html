postulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal statement or request presented to an authority, especially in writing, to ask for something or state a position
formal submission to an authority

Examples

The committee received a postulation from the local residents demanding improved road safety measures.
Her postulation to the board outlined the urgent need for new equipment.
Historical records show frequent postulation by citizens appealing to the city council.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request, usually signed by many people

Etymology

From Latin postulationem (a demand, request), from postulare (to ask, demand).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
An assumption or claim accepted without proof as a basis for reasoning or argument, especially in logic or philosophy.
an assumed basis for argument

Examples

The scientist began his theory with the postulation that all objects fall at the same rate in a vacuum.
Before proceeding, let us agree on the postulation that everyone in the group is rational.
Her philosophical argument depends heavily on the postulation of free will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - a statement taken to be true as a premise for reasoning
  • Synonym: hypothesis - a supposition made as a starting point for further investigation

Etymology

From Latin 'postulatio', from 'postulare' (to demand, claim).
