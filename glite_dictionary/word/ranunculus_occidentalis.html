Ranunculus occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant species of buttercup native to western North America, known for its yellow flowers.
a perennial buttercup of western North America

Examples

Ranunculus occidentalis is commonly found in moist meadows throughout the Pacific Northwest.
The bright yellow blooms of Ranunculus occidentalis make it easy to spot during spring hikes in California.
Botanists studied Ranunculus occidentalis to understand its adaptation to different environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western buttercup - Common name for Ranunculus occidentalis

Etymology

From Latin 'ranunculus' (little frog, referring to moist habitats) and 'occidentalis' (western).
