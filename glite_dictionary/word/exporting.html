exporting

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of selling and sending goods to another country for sale
the business of selling goods abroad

Examples

Many developing nations rely on exporting agricultural products for a significant part of their income.
The government's new policies are designed to boost exporting to international markets.
She has spent her career advising companies on the ins and outs of exporting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importing - the activity of bringing goods into a country from abroad for sale
  • Synonym: export - the act of sending goods to another country for sale (noun/verb)

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare' (to carry out), via Middle English 'export' (noun and verb).
