carefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cautious and attentive to potential risks or dangers
quality of being cautious

Examples

Her carefulness prevented the project from running into unexpected problems.
He handled the ancient vase with great carefulness, ensuring it was not damaged.
The scientist's carefulness was evident in the detailed notes and accurate results.

Synset Examples

a man of caution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of regard for danger or consequences

Etymology

From 'careful' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of paying close attention to detail and avoiding mistakes or harm.
attentiveness; avoiding mistakes

Examples

His carefulness in handling the fragile vase prevented any accidents.
The project was completed with great carefulness and precision.
Teachers appreciate students who show carefulness in their assignments.

Synset Examples

I admired the carefulness of his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - Serious attention or consideration applied to doing something correctly or to avoid damage or risk
  • Synonym: cautiousness - The trait of being cautious; avoiding unnecessary risks or errors
  • Antonym: carelessness - Lack of attention or consideration; not taking care or avoiding mistakes

Etymology

From 'careful' + '-ness'; 'careful' from Old English 'carful' (full of care).
