slutchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌtʃi / (AmE), / ˈslʌtʃi / (BrE)
(of a place or atmosphere) dirty, muddy, or generally unpleasant.
dirty or unpleasant (of a place)

After the heavy rain, the camp developed a slutchy atmosphere that kept everyone indoors.
Years of neglect left the building with a distinctly slutchy feel.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslʌtʃi / (AmE), / ˈslʌtʃi / (BrE)
(of a place or surface) dirty, slushy, or muddy, often unpleasantly wet and messy.
dirty, muddy, or messy (esp. for places)

Examples

During the rainy season, the road to the cabin became especially slutchy.
She disliked walking her dog in the park when the paths were slutchy and hard to cross.
After the festival, the field was left slutchy and litter-strewn.

Synset Examples

I avoid slutchy places when choosing a restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - covered with or full of mud
  • Synonym: dirty - not clean; covered with dirt
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or mess

Etymology

Likely from 'slutch,' a dialect word for mud or slush, with '-y' forming the adjective.
