katabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the breakdown of complex molecules in living organisms to form simpler ones, typically with the release of energy; relating to catabolism.
relating to the breakdown of molecules (catabolism)
medical

Examples

During starvation, the body enters a katabolic state to provide energy.
Certain hormones such as cortisol have a strong katabolic effect on muscle tissue.
Scientists studied the katabolic pathways involved in energy release during exercise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anabolic - relating to the synthesis of complex molecules in living organisms from simpler ones (opposite of katabolic)
  • Synonym: catabolic - relating to or characterized by catabolism (variant spelling)

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down) + ballein (to throw), via catabolic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from the metabolic breakdown of complex molecules into simpler ones, often with the release of energy
relating to destructive metabolism
medical

Examples

During fasting, the body enters a katabolic state to provide energy from stored resources.
Intense exercise can activate katabolic pathways that break down muscle proteins.
Many hormones regulate either katabolic or anabolic processes within the cell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anabolic - relating to constructive metabolism; metabolic building up of complex molecules

Etymology

From Greek 'katabole' (a throwing down), via 'katabolein' (to throw down), related to metabolism terminology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00108795-a
Synset POS: a
