Fort Lauderdale

1. PROPN

/ fɔrt ˈlɔːdərˌdeɪl / (AmE), / fɔːt ˈlɔːdədeɪl / (BrE)
a city in southeastern Florida, United States, on the Atlantic coast, known for its beaches and as a popular destination for college students during spring break.
city in southeastern Florida, USA

Examples

Every year, thousands of students flock to Fort Lauderdale for spring break festivities.
I visited Fort Lauderdale last summer and spent most of my time on the beach.
The cruise ship departed from the port of Fort Lauderdale early in the morning.

Etymology

Named after a series of forts built around 1838, during the Second Seminole War, which were named for Major William Lauderdale.
