to jell

1. VERB

/ dʒɛl / (AmE), / dʒɛl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or mixture) to become gelatinous or set into a jelly-like consistency.
become gelatinous; set like jelly

Examples

After simmering for several hours, the broth began to jell.
The mixture will jell if you let it cool in the refrigerator overnight.
As the sauce cooled, it started to jell into a thick, spreadable consistency.

Synset Examples

the liquid jelled after we added the enzyme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gel - to become a gel or jelly-like substance
  • Synonym: to solidify - to become solid or more solid

Etymology

From 'jelly', influenced by analogous verbs like 'melt'.
