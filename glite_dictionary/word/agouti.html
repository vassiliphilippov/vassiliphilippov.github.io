agouti

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡuːti / (AmE), / əˈɡuːti / (BrE)
an agile, rabbit-sized rodent native to Central and South America and the West Indies, with long legs and valued as food in some regions
a Central/South American rodent

Examples

The agouti is often seen foraging for nuts and fruits on the forest floor.
In some parts of South America, the agouti is considered a delicacy.
Researchers tracked the agouti population to study its role in seed dispersal.

Etymology

From French 'agouti,' which is from the Guarani word 'akuti'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡuːti / (AmE), / əˈɡuːti / (BrE)
A small, agile rodent of Central and South America and the West Indies, similar in size to a rabbit and valued as food in some regions.
rodent of Central and South America

Examples

The agouti is often hunted for its meat in rural South American communities.
We spotted an agouti darting through the underbrush during our hike in Costa Rica.
Unlike many rodents, the agouti is active during the day and uses its sharp teeth to crack open nuts.

Etymology

From French 'agouti', from Guarani 'akuti', referring to the animal.
