Monomorium

1. PROPN

/ məˌnɑːˈmɔːriəm / (AmE), / məˌnɒˈmɔːriəm / (BrE)
A genus of ants in the family Formicidae, which includes several species of small, often invasive ants.
genus of ants (Formicidae family)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Monomorium in the desert ecosystem.
Several Monomorium ants invaded the laboratory, prompting further study.
The genus Monomorium includes the well-known pharaoh ant, a household pest.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (alone, single) and 'morion' (a type of helmet), used in scientific Latin nomenclature.
