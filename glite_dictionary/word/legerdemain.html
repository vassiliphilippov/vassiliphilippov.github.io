legerdemain

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒərdəˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒə dəˈmeɪn / (BrE)
skillful use of one's hands to perform tricks; sleight of hand or deception.
sleight of hand; trickery

Examples

The magician's legerdemain left the audience gasping in amazement.
With remarkable legerdemain, he made the coin disappear right before our eyes.
Her political legerdemain allowed her to avoid answering the difficult question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleight of hand - manual dexterity in performing tricks
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone

Etymology

From Old French 'leger de main' (light of hand).
