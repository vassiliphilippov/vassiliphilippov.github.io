autotroph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔt̬oʊˌtroʊf / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌtrɒf / (BrE)
an organism, such as a plant or certain bacteria, that produces its own food from inorganic substances, usually through photosynthesis or chemosynthesis
organism that produces its own food

Examples

Most green plants are autotrophs, making their own food using sunlight.
Algae and some bacteria act as autotrophs in aquatic ecosystems.
Unlike animals, autotrophs do not depend on others for food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterotroph - organism that obtains its food by consuming other organisms

Etymology

From Greek autos (self) + trophe (nutrition).
