Ewe

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛweɪ / (BrE)
A Kwa language spoken in southeastern Ghana, southern Togo, and parts of Benin.
a language of Ghana, Togo, and Benin

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Ewe.
The Ewe language has many dialects across different regions.
He studied Ewe at university to better communicate with local communities.

Etymology

From the native name 'Eʋegbe', meaning 'Ewe language'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪweɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪwiː / (BrE)
a member of a West African people living mainly in southern Benin, Togo, and southeastern Ghana
member of a West African ethnic group

Examples

Many Ewe have preserved their traditional customs and festivals.
She is an Ewe from southeastern Ghana with strong ties to her community.
The Ewe are known for their rich musical and weaving traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ewe - the Niger–Congo language spoken by the Ewe people

Etymology

From the Ewe self-designation ɛʋe.
