glycerite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsəraɪt / (BrE)
A medicinal preparation made by dissolving or suspending a substance in glycerin.
medicine made with glycerin as solvent
medical

Examples

The pharmacy prepared a glycerite of the herbal extract for topical use.
A glycerite offers an alcohol-free alternative for delivering medicinal ingredients.
Some patients prefer a glycerite because of its sweet taste and gentle effect.

Etymology

From glycerin + -ite, denoting a derivative or product related to glycerin.
