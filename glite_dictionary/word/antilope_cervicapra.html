Antilope cervicapra

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtaɪloʊp sərˌvɪkɑːprə / (AmE), / ænˈtɪləʊp səːˌvɪkɑːprə / (BrE)
a species of antelope native to India, known for the male's dark back and long spiral horns; commonly called the blackbuck
Indian antelope with spiral horns

Examples

The Antilope cervicapra is also commonly known as the blackbuck in India.
Male Antilope cervicapra are distinguished by their striking dark backs and elegant spiral horns.
Wildlife reserves often protect the Antilope cervicapra due to its declining population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbuck - common name for Antilope cervicapra, Indian antelope with spiral horns

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'antilope' (antelope) and 'cervicapra' (from 'cervus' meaning deer and 'capra' meaning she-goat).
