Simon Bolivar

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪmən ˈbɒləˌvɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɪmən ˈbɒlɪˌvɑː / (BrE)
(1783–1830) A Venezuelan military and political leader who played a key role in the independence of several South American countries from Spanish rule and founded Bolivia.
Venezuelan leader and South American liberator

Examples

Simon Bolivar is remembered as one of the great heroes of Latin American independence.
Many South American countries honor Simon Bolivar with statues and city names.
The country of Bolivia is named after Simon Bolivar in recognition of his leadership.
