bonito

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
the flesh of various Pacific food fishes of the genus Sarda, related to but smaller than tuna, used as food
flesh of a Pacific fish related to tuna

Examples

Grilled bonito is a traditional dish in many Japanese households.
The chef recommended trying the bonito fillet with a citrus glaze.
They caught several pounds of bonito during their fishing trip.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tuna - the flesh of the large food fish related to bonito

Etymology

From Spanish 'bonito', meaning 'pretty' or 'lovely', used as a fish name.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
a medium-sized fish of the mackerel family, several species of which are valued for food, especially in Japanese cookery where its flesh is dried and flaked
a food fish related to tuna, used in Japanese cuisine

Examples

The chef used bonito flakes to add flavor to the soup.
Dried bonito is a key ingredient in making traditional Japanese dashi stock.
At the market, she found both fresh tuna and smoked bonito.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipjack tuna - a species of tuna closely related to bonito, often used to make dried flakes in Japanese cuisine

Etymology

From Spanish 'bonito', meaning 'pretty' or from Portuguese 'bonito', probably referring to the appearance of the fish.

3. NOUN

/ bəˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
A marine fish of the mackerel family, intermediate in size and characteristics between mackerels and tunas, found in warm seas.
a marine fish related to mackerel and tuna

Examples

Fishermen were delighted to catch several large bonitos near the coast this morning.
The grilled bonito was served with a tangy citrus sauce and fresh vegetables.
Schools of bonito are often seen swimming near the surface in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tuna - a large and powerful oceanic fish related to mackerels
  • Degree Lower: mackerel - a smaller, fast-swimming fish of the same family as bonito

Etymology

From Spanish bonito, meaning 'pretty' or 'fish of the mackerel family'.
