snowbank

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbæŋk / (BrE)
a mound or heap of snow, often formed by wind or plowing
a mound or heap of snow

Examples

After the storm, a large snowbank blocked the end of the driveway.
Children love to jump off the snowbank after a heavy snowfall.
The car skidded and came to rest against a snowbank on the side of the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - a mass of snow formed by wind into a mound or bank
