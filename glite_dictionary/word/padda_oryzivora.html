Padda oryzivora

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːdə ɔːˌraɪzɪˈvɔːrə / (AmE), / ˈpadə ɔːˌraɪzɪˈvɔːrə / (BrE)
a small finch-like weaverbird from Indonesia, often found in rice fields; also known as the Java sparrow
Indonesian weaverbird (Java sparrow)

Examples

Many aviculturists keep the Padda oryzivora because of its distinctive plumage and cheerful song.
Rice farmers are concerned about flocks of Padda oryzivora feeding in the fields during harvest season.
The Padda oryzivora has become popular as a cage bird in many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Java sparrow - common name for Padda oryzivora

Etymology

From New Latin, the species name; 'Padda' is the genus, 'oryzivora' means 'rice-eating' (from Latin 'oryza' (rice) + 'vorare' (to devour)).
