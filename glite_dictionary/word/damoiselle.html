damoiselle

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæmwɑɪˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌdamwɑɪˈzɛl / (BrE)
(archaic) A young unmarried woman; a damsel.
archaic term for young unmarried woman
archaic

Examples

The knight bowed deeply to the noble damoiselle before speaking.
Many a damoiselle awaited her beloved’s return from the battlefield.
In old tales, a damoiselle would often be rescued by a gallant hero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damsel - a young unmarried woman; maiden

Etymology

From Old French 'damoisele', meaning 'young lady'.
