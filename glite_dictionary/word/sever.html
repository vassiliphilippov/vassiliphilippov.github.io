to sever

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛvər / (AmE), / ˈsɛvə / (BrE)
to cut something off completely from a whole, especially by cutting
to cut off from a whole

Examples

The power lines were severed by the storm, causing a blackout in the neighborhood.
Negotiations finally severed all diplomatic ties between the two countries.
He accidentally severed the rope with his knife while climbing.

Synset Examples

His head was severed from his body
The soul discerped from the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to remove or separate something from something larger
  • Antonym: to connect - to join or link together

Etymology

From Old French 'sevrer' (to separate), from Latin 'separare' (to separate).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛvə / (BrE)
to cut off something completely, or to end a relationship or connection completely
to cut off or end (something) completely

Examples

He decided to sever all ties with the company after the dispute.
After years of disagreement, they finally severed their partnership.
She found it difficult to sever her relationship with old friends.

Synset Examples

sever a relationship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to remove something by cutting
  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or disconnect from something
  • Antonym: join - to connect or bring together

Etymology

From Old French *sevrer* (to separate), from Latin *separare* (to sever, separate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01563766-v
Synset POS: v
