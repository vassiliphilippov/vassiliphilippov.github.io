Melursus

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈlɝːsəs / (AmE), / mɛˈlɜːsəs / (BrE)
A genus of bears consisting of the sloth bear, sometimes not distinguished from the genus Ursus in some classifications.
genus of sloth bear

Examples

The sloth bear is classified under the genus Melursus.
Some taxonomists debate whether Melursus should be a separate genus from Ursus.
The distinctive long coat of Melursus species helps protect them from insect bites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - genus of bears including the brown bear and others

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'melas' (black) and 'ursus' (bear).
