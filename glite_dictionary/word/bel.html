bel

1. NOUN

/ bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
a logarithmic unit for measuring the intensity of sound or power level, equal to ten decibels
unit equal to ten decibels

Examples

The typical volume level in a quiet office is about six bels below the level of normal conversation.
Engineers prefer to express signal losses in bels or decibels for convenience.
The sound intensity increased by one bel, which is equivalent to a tenfold increase in power.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: decibel - a unit equal to one tenth of a bel, commonly used to express sound intensity

Etymology

Named after Alexander Graham Bell, the inventor and scientist.
