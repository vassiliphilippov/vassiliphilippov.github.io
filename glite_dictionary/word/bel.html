Bel

1. PROPN

/ bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
A Babylonian god of the earth, one of the supreme triad of deities (with Anu and Ea), earlier identified with En-lil.
Babylonian earth god; part of deity triad

Examples

Many ancient texts refer to Bel as one of the chief deities in the Babylonian pantheon.
Archaeologists discovered a temple dedicated to Bel in the ruins of Babylon.
In Babylonian mythology, Bel is sometimes equated with the earlier Sumerian god En-lil.

Etymology

From Akkadian *Bēlu* meaning 'lord' or 'master', used as a title for several gods in Mesopotamian religion.
