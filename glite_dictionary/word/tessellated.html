tessellated

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛsəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛsɪleɪtɪd / (BrE)
decorated with small pieces of colored material, such as glass or stone, fitted together to form a mosaic
decorated in a mosaic pattern

Examples

The ancient floor was beautifully tessellated with intricate geometric designs.
Sunlight streamed across the tessellated tiles of the cathedral entrance.
Visitors admired the tessellated pavement, which sparkled in different colors.

Synset Examples

a tessellated pavement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosaic - a pattern or picture made by arranging together small pieces of stone, glass, etc.

Etymology

From Latin tessellatus (of small cubes), from tessella (a small cube).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛsəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛsɪleɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having a pattern of small shapes, especially squares, that fit together with no gaps or overlaps; checkered or mosaic-like in appearance.
marked with a tiled or mosaic pattern

Examples

The museum's floor was beautifully tessellated with colorful marble tiles.
Under the microscope, the butterfly's wing appeared tessellated with intricate patterns.
The architect designed a tessellated ceiling that created a striking effect in the grand hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosaic - picture or pattern produced by arranging small pieces of stone, tile, or glass
  • Synonym: checkered - marked by a pattern of squares of different colors

Etymology

From Latin tessellatus, from tessella (small cube used in mosaics).

Synset POS: s
