Mashhad

1. PROPN

/ mæʃˈhæd / (AmE), / mæʃˈhæd / (BrE)
a holy city in northeastern Iran, significant in Shiite Islam and a major pilgrimage site
holy city in northeastern Iran

Examples

Millions of pilgrims travel to Mashhad each year to visit the Imam Reza shrine.
During their tour of Iran, they spent several days exploring the historical sites of Mashhad.
Mashhad is known for its religious significance and vibrant bazaars.

Etymology

From Persian مشهد‎ (Mashhad), meaning 'place of martyrdom'.
