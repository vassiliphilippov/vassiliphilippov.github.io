shovel

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
A small tool with a broad, flat blade and a handle, used for scooping coals or ashes in a fireplace.
fireplace tool for scooping ashes

Examples

He used the shovel to remove hot coals from the fireplace.
After the fire burned out, she cleared the ashes with a shovel.
A set of fireplace tools usually includes a poker and a small shovel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire iron - a tool made of iron used in tending a fire

Etymology

From Old English *scofl* (shovel), related to scofan (to shove).

5. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
A tool with a broad curved blade and typically a long handle, used for lifting and moving loose material such as soil, sand, or snow.
tool for lifting or moving loose material

Examples

She used a shovel to clear the snow from the driveway.
The gardener grabbed his shovel to plant the new tree.
Please hand me that shovel so I can dig a hole for the mailbox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spade - a tool similar to a shovel, used for digging

Etymology

From Old English 'scofl' or 'sceofel', related to 'scofan' (to shove, push).
