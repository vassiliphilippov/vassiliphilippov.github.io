Globicephala melaena

1. NOUN

/ gloʊbɪˈsɛfələ məˈliːnə / (AmE), / glɒbɪˈsɛfələ mɛˈliːnə / (BrE)
A species of small dark-colored whale found along the Atlantic coast of the United States, known for its largest male acting as the leader of the group; commonly called the long-finned pilot whale.
a species of small Atlantic pilot whale

Examples

The Globicephala melaena is known for its strong social bonds within groups.
Marine biologists often observe pods of Globicephala melaena off the coast of New England.
A stranded Globicephala melaena drew attention from wildlife rescue teams last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot whale - common name for whales of the genus Globicephala

Etymology

From Latin: 'globus' (sphere) + 'cephalus' (head), referring to the whale's rounded head; 'melaena' from Greek 'melaina' (black).
