Fumaria

1. NOUN

/ fuˈmɛriə / (AmE), / fjuːˈmeəriə / (BrE)
a genus of annual herbs with small, tubular flowers having a single petal spurred at the base, commonly known as fumitory
genus of annual herbs (fumitory)

Examples

Many species of Fumaria thrive in fields and disturbed soils across Europe and Asia.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the rapid spread of Fumaria in their flowerbeds.
The delicate pink-white flowers of Fumaria are distinctive for their spurred petals.

Etymology

From Latin *fumaria*, meaning 'smoke,' possibly referring to the plant's pallor or aroma.
