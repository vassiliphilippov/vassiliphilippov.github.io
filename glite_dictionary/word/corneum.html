corneum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.ni.əm / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the epidermis in vertebrates, consisting of dead, flattened cells that shed regularly
outer layer of the skin's epidermis
medical

Examples

The stratum corneum acts as a barrier to protect underlying tissues from infection and dehydration.
Moisturizers help maintain the flexibility of your corneum and prevent excessive dryness.
Certain skin conditions can cause the corneum to become thickened or flaky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidermis - the outer layer of cells covering an organism

Etymology

From Latin 'corneus' meaning 'horny', due to the tough nature of the layer.
