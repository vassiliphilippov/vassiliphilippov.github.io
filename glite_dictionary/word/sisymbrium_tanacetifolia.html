Sisymbrium tanacetifolia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪmbriəm tæˌnæsɪtɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪmbriəm tanəˌsɛtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a perennial herb with small yellow flowers, native to mountain regions of southern Europe and sometimes classified in the genus Sisymbrium
perennial herb with yellow flowers, European mountains

Examples

The rocky slopes were dotted with Sisymbrium tanacetifolia, their yellow flowers bright in the sunlight.
Botanists studied the distribution of Sisymbrium tanacetifolia in the mountain habitats of southern Europe.
Gardeners sometimes confuse Sisymbrium tanacetifolia with other yellow-flowering herbs due to its similar appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Sisymbrium and species epithet tanacetifolia (having leaves like tansy, from Tanacetum + folia meaning 'leaves').
