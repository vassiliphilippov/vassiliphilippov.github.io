habit

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
an addiction to the regular and excessive use of drugs, especially one that is difficult to give up
addiction to drug use

Examples

After years of struggling, he finally broke his habit and started a new life.
The weight of her drug habit affected not only her health but her family as well.
He spent all his savings trying to support his habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - the state of being physically or mentally dependent on a substance

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhabɪt / (BrE)
a distinctive outfit, especially one worn by a woman while riding horseback
riding outfit, especially for women

Examples

She arrived at the stables dressed in her elegant riding habit.
Victorian ladies were known for their tailored habits when attending equestrian events.
The museum has an exhibit featuring a 19th-century woman's riding habit.

Etymology

From Old French *habit* (appearance, clothing), from Latin *habitus* (condition, appearance, dress).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
a regular or automatic pattern of behavior, especially one acquired by frequent repetition and often performed unconsciously
regular or automatic behavioral pattern

Examples

After years of waking at dawn, it became a habit he couldn’t break.
She developed the habit of checking her phone before getting out of bed.
Biting his nails was a nervous habit he wanted to stop.

Synset Examples

owls have nocturnal habits
she had a habit twirling the ends of her hair
long use had hardened him to it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional or habitual practice commonly observed by people
  • Synonym: routine - a sequence of actions regularly followed; a fixed program
  • Degree Higher: addiction - compulsive need for and use of a habit-forming substance

Etymology

From Latin 'habitus' (condition, appearance, bearing), from 'habere' (to have, to hold).

Examples
