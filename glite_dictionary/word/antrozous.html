Antrozous

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtroʊzoʊs / (AmE), / ænˈtrəʊzəʊs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, which includes the pallid bat.
genus of bats including the pallid bat

Examples

The genus Antrozous is known for its distinctive pallid bat species.
Researchers are studying the behavior of Antrozous in its natural desert habitat.
The characteristics of Antrozous separate it from other vesper bats.

Etymology

From Greek 'antris' (cave) and 'zoon' (animal), referring to cave-dwelling habits.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtroʊzoʊs / (AmE), / ænˈtrəʊzəʊs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, whose only living species is the pallid bat.
genus of bats including the pallid bat

Examples

Scientists recently studied Antrozous to learn more about the habitat of the pallid bat.
The genus Antrozous is distinguished by its pale coloration and large ears.
Researchers compared Antrozous with other genera in the family Vespertilionidae.

Etymology

From Greek 'antron' (cave) and 'zoon' (animal).
