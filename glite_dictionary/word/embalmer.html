embalmer

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑlmər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːmə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to preserve dead bodies with chemicals to prevent decay
person who preserves dead bodies with chemicals

Examples

The embalmer carefully prepared the body before the funeral service.
She decided to train as an embalmer after finding the work meaningful.
Many funeral homes employ at least one experienced embalmer on staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortician - a person whose job is to prepare bodies for burial or cremation
  • Synonym: undertaker - someone whose business is preparing dead bodies for burial or cremation and making funeral arrangements

Etymology

From the verb 'embalm', which comes from Old French 'embaumer', from en- (in) + baume (balm).
