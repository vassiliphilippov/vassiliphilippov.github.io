abusive

1. ADJ

/ əˈbjusɪv / (AmE), / əˈbjuːsɪv / (BrE)
using or expressing offensive, insulting, or hurtful language or behavior toward someone
using insulting or hurtful language

Examples

His abusive remarks deeply hurt everyone in the meeting.
The manager was fired for sending abusive emails to his staff.
After years of abusive language, she decided to quit the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulting - rude or offensive in a way that hurts someone
  • Antonym: respectful - showing or expressing politeness or honor

Etymology

From Medieval Latin abusivus, from Latin abusus (a misuse or wrong use).

2. ADJ

/ əˈbjusɪv / (AmE), / əˈbjuːsɪv / (BrE)
involving the use of physical or psychological harm, especially through cruel or violent treatment
using physical or psychological maltreatment

Examples

The child was removed from his abusive household for his own safety.
She took legal action against her abusive employer.
He had a history of engaging in abusive relationships.

Synset Examples

abusive punishment
argued … that foster homes are abusive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willingly causing pain or suffering to others
  • Antonym: kind - showing a gentle, friendly, or considerate nature

Etymology

From Latin 'abusus' (a misuse or wrong use), from 'abuti' (to misuse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164128-s
Synset POS: s
