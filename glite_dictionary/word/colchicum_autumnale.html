Colchicum autumnale

1. NOUN

/ kolˈkɪkəm ɔːˈtʌmnəli / (AmE), / kɒlˈkɪkəm ɔːˈtʌmnəli / (BrE)
a bulbous autumn-flowering plant with white, purple, or lavender-and-white flowers, native to western and central Europe, also known as the autumn crocus
a European autumn-flowering plant (autumn crocus)

Examples

The garden's Colchicum autumnale bloomed in a rare shade of lavender this year.
In Europe, Colchicum autumnale is admired for its vibrant autumn flowers.
Never ingest Colchicum autumnale, as the entire plant is highly poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autumn crocus - common name for Colchicum autumnale

Etymology

From modern Latin, where Colchicum is the genus (from Colchis, a region of ancient Georgia) and autumnale means 'of autumn.'
