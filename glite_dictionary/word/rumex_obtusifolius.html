Rumex obtusifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.mɛks əbˌtuː.səˈfoʊ.li.əs / (AmE), / ˈruː.mɛks ɒbˌtjuː.sɪˈfəʊ.li.əs / (BrE)
a European dock with broad, blunt-tipped leaves and bitter rootstock, common as a weed in North America, also known as broad-leaved dock
a species of broad-leaved dock plant

Examples

The leaves of Rumex obtusifolius are easily recognized in the wild.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Rumex obtusifolius from their lawns.
Rumex obtusifolius is widespread in North American fields and roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad-leaved dock - common English name for Rumex obtusifolius
  • Degree Lower: Rumex crispus - curly dock, a related dock species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rumex' (genus name for dock plants) and 'obtusifolius' meaning 'blunt-leaved'.
