echoic

1. ADJ

/ ɛˈkoʊɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈkəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or speech sound) formed by or resembling the imitation of a natural sound; onomatopoeic.
imitating a natural sound (of words)

Examples

The word 'buzz' is an echoic term that mimics the sound it represents.
Many animal names in children’s books are deliberately echoic to help with recognition.
The linguist gave several echoic examples, such as 'hiss' and 'clang'.

Synset Examples

onomatopoeic words are imitative of noises
it was independently developed in more than one place as an onomatopoetic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomatopoeic - (of a word) formed by imitating a sound associated with what is named

Etymology

From Latin 'echoicus', from Greek 'ēchoikos' (of or like an echo).

2. ADJ

/ ɛˈkoʊɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈkəʊɪk / (BrE)
having the quality of or relating to an echo; resembling an echo in sound or effect.
like or characteristic of an echo

Examples

The large, empty hall gave the music an echoic quality.
Her voice sounded echoic as it bounced off the stone walls.
The canyon’s echoic environment amplified every sound we made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - a sound or series of sounds caused by the reflection of sound waves

Etymology

From Greek 'ēkhōikos', from 'ēkhō' (echo).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02015916-s
Synset POS: s
