ceiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an upper limit, especially on an amount or a number that is allowed
maximum permitted limit

Examples

The company imposed a ceiling on pay raises this year.
A ceiling on credit card interest rates was introduced to protect consumers.
There is a strict ceiling on the number of students admitted each term.

Synset Examples

he put a ceiling on the number of women who worked for him
there was a roof on salaries
they established a cap for prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cap - an upper limit on an amount or value
  • Antonym: floor - a lower limit, especially on an amount or number

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the overhead upper surface of the inside of a room or other covered space
overhead interior surface of a room

Examples

The paint on the ceiling had started to peel after the water leak.
A large crystal chandelier hung from the center of the ceiling.
She lay in bed, staring up at the ceiling and thinking about her day.

Synset Examples

he hated painting the ceiling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roof - the external upper covering of a building
  • Antonym: floor - the lower horizontal surface of a room

Etymology

From Old French cele (heaven, sky), based on Latin caelum (heaven, sky).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(Meteorology) The altitude of the lowest layer of clouds covering more than half the sky, important in aviation and weather reporting.
lowest cloud layer altitude (meteorology)

Examples

Pilots were warned that the ceiling was too low for safe landing.
The weather report indicated a ceiling of 800 feet, restricting visibility for small aircraft.
Due to a low ceiling, several flights were delayed until conditions improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud base - the lowest altitude of the visible portion of a cloud

Etymology

From Old French 'ceil' (sky, heaven) by way of 'ceil' + '-ing'; sense extended by analogy from interior top to sky as upper limit.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can fly under specified conditions
maximum altitude an aircraft can reach

Examples

The aircraft can reach a ceiling of 40,000 feet under optimal conditions.
Pilots must be aware of their aircraft's ceiling for safety reasons.
Due to engine limitations, its operational ceiling is lower than that of similar jets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altitude - height above a given level, especially sea level
  • Antonym: floor - the minimum altitude permitted or achievable

Etymology

From Latin 'caelum' (sky, heaven) via Old French and Middle English.
