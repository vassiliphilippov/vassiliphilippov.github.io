motherland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðərlænd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəlænd / (BrE)
the country where a person was born or with which they feel a strong cultural connection
one's country of birth or origin

Examples

After years abroad, she finally returned to her motherland to be with her family.
Many songs and poems celebrate love for one's motherland.
Immigrants often speak of their motherland with both nostalgia and pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatherland - one's native country, especially when considered as a source of cultural identity

Etymology

Formed from 'mother' + 'land', by analogy with 'fatherland'.
