unseeded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsidɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a player or team in a sports competition) not assigned a ranking as a seed, typically indicating a lower skill level or lesser likelihood of success.
not assigned a ranking in a competition

Examples

The unseeded player surprised everyone by reaching the semifinals.
As an unseeded team, they faced the tournament favorites in the first round.
Despite being unseeded, she played with great confidence and determination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seeded - having been assigned a ranking or seed in a competition

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'seeded', from 'seed' (ranking in tournaments).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02262727-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsidɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of land) not having had seed sown on it; not planted with a crop.
(of land) not sown with seed

Examples

After the heavy rains, the unseeded fields became overgrown with wild grass.
The farmer left part of his land unseeded to allow the soil to recover.
Walking past the unseeded plot, she noticed it looked bare compared to the planted ones.

Synset Examples

farmland still unsown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsown - (of land) not sown with seed or a crop

Etymology

From un- (not) + seeded (past participle of 'seed').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01838525-s
Synset POS: s
