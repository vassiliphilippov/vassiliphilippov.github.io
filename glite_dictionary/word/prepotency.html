prepotency

1. NOUN

/ priˈpoʊtənsi / (AmE), / priˈpəʊtənsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being greater in power, influence, or importance than others; predominance.
predominance or superior power

Examples

The company’s financial prepotency allowed it to dominate the market.
His prepotency in the negotiations was clear from the start.
Cultural prepotency often shapes the values of entire societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predominance - the state of being greater in number or influence

Etymology

From Latin praeponere (to put before) and potentia (power).
