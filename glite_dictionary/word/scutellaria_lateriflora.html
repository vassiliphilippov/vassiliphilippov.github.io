Scutellaria lateriflora

1. NOUN

/ skuːtəˈlɛrɪə ˌleɪtərəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / skjuːtɛˈlɑːrɪə ˌlætərɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a North American herb of the mint family (skullcap), formerly used as an antispasmodic
North American mint herb; antispasmodic uses
medical

Examples

Many herbalists use Scutellaria lateriflora to help calm nerves and reduce anxiety.
Traditional healers relied on Scutellaria lateriflora as a remedy for muscle spasms.
Recent studies examine the chemical compounds found in Scutellaria lateriflora for possible medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skullcap - common name for several plants in the genus Scutellaria

Etymology

From Latin: *Scutellaria* (genus name, meaning 'little plate') + *lateriflora* ('with flowers on the side').
