recidivism

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsɪdəˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪdɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency of a convicted criminal to reoffend or relapse into criminal behavior.
relapse into criminal behavior
legal

Examples

Many prison reform advocates are concerned about high levels of recidivism among released inmates.
Programs aimed at reducing recidivism focus on education and job training.
The judge cited the defendant's history of recidivism when explaining the lengthy sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially bad habits or criminal activity
  • Antonym: rehabilitation - the process of restoring someone to a useful place in society after imprisonment or addiction

Etymology

From French recidivisme, from Medieval Latin recidīvus (recurring), from Latin recidere (to fall back).
