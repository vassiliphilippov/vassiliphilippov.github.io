to diminish

1. VERB

/ dəˈmɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem less important, impressive, or valuable; to reduce in authority, dignity, or reputation
make less important; reduce reputation

Examples

His comments were intended to diminish her achievements in the company.
Criticism from the press did not diminish the leader's reputation among supporters.
Don't allow others to diminish your sense of self-worth.

Synset Examples

don't belittle your colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Synonym: to deprecate - to express disapproval of or make little of
  • Antonym: to exalt - to raise someone in rank, power, or character

Etymology

From Latin 'deminuere' (to lessen, reduce) via Old French 'diminuer'.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
to become or make something become less in size, importance, or value
become or make less in size or importance

Examples

The country's influence on international affairs has diminished in recent years.
Her enthusiasm for the project began to diminish after several setbacks.
The pain will gradually diminish as the wound heals.

Synset Examples

The amount of homework decreased towards the end of the semester
The cabin pressure fell dramatically
her weight fell to under a hundred pounds
his voice fell to a whisper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrease - to become or make less in quantity, size, or intensity
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make or become smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old French 'diminuer', from Latin 'diminuere' (to lessen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00151509-v
Synset POS: v
