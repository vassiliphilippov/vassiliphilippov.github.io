discontinuous

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuːəs / (BrE)
(of a function or curve) having one or more points where it is not continuous; having jumps or breaks in its value.
having breaks; not continuous (maths)

Examples

The graph of the function is discontinuous at x = 2 due to a jump.
A discontinuous curve can complicate numerical analysis.
If the derivative does not exist everywhere, the function may be discontinuous at certain points.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - (of a function, curve, etc.) unbroken or without interruption

Etymology

From Latin discontinuus, from dis- (apart, not) + continuus (continuous).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˌkɑnˈtɪnjuəs / (AmE), / dɪsˌkɒnˈtɪnjuəs / (BrE)
not continuous; having breaks, interruptions, or gaps in time, sequence, or space.
not continuous; having interruptions or gaps

Examples

The parade was marked by discontinuous bursts of cheering from the crowd.
A discontinuous line of clouds stretched across the evening sky.
Her medical treatment was discontinuous, causing delays in her recovery.

Synset Examples

discontinuous applause
the landscape was a discontinuous mosaic of fields and forest areas
he received a somewhat haphazard and discontinuous schooling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - forming an unbroken whole without interruption
  • Synonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady

Etymology

From Latin prefix dis- (apart) + 'continuous' (from Latin continuus).
