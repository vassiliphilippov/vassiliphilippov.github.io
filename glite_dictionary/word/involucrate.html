involucrate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɑːljəˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒljʊˌkreɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) having an involucre, a whorl or rosette of bracts surrounding a flower or flower cluster.
having an involucre (botany)

Examples

The involucrate heads of the sunflower are characteristic of the family Asteraceae.
Some species in this genus produce conspicuously involucrate flower clusters.
The presence of involucrate bracts helps botanists identify many composite flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involucre - a whorl or rosette of bracts surrounding a flower or cluster

Etymology

From Latin 'involucratus', past participle of 'involucrare', from 'involucrum' (wrapper or covering).
