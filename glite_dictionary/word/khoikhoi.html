Khoikhoi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪˌkɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪˌkɔɪ / (BrE)
any of the Khoisan languages spoken by the Khoikhoi, a pastoral people of Namibia and South Africa
Khoisan language of southern Africa

Examples

He is studying the Khoikhoi language as part of his research on indigenous African tongues.
The Khoikhoi are known for their distinctive click-based languages, which are part of the Khoisan group.
A dictionary of Khoikhoi was recently published to help preserve the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Khoisan - group of indigenous peoples and their languages in southern Africa

Etymology

From Khoikhoi, the name that the people use for themselves, meaning 'real people' in their language.
