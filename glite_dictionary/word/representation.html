representation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɹɛpɹɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a statement of fact made by a party during negotiations to induce another party to enter into a contract, especially in legal contexts; misrepresentation can make a contract voidable
factual statement in contract law
legal

Examples

The contract was voided due to a false representation made by the seller.
The buyer relied on the representation that the goods were new.
Several representations in the agreement turned out to be inaccurate.

Synset Examples

the sales contract contains several representations by the vendor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misrepresentation - the act of giving false or misleading representations
