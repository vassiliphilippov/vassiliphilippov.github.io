vomitory

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑː.mɪˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈvɒ.mɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
an entrance or passage in an amphitheater or stadium through which crowds enter or leave
amphitheater or stadium entrance

Examples

The stadium's vomitory quickly filled with people at the end of the concert.
Ancient Roman amphitheaters were designed with multiple vomitories for efficient crowd movement.
A steward stood by the vomitory, checking tickets before allowing entry.

Etymology

From Latin vomitorium, from vomere (to discharge), originally referring to a passage for crowds leaving a theater.
