univalent

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) not paired with its homologous chromosome during synapsis in meiosis.
chromosome unpaired during meiosis
medical

Examples

During meiosis, a univalent chromosome fails to pair with its homolog.
The presence of a univalent can lead to errors in chromosome segregation.
Researchers observed several univalent chromosomes in the mutant plants.

Synset Examples

a univalent chromosome

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'valent' (having power), patterned after chemical terminology.

2. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
Having a valence of one; capable of combining with or replacing one atom of hydrogen.
having a valence of one

Examples

Sodium is a univalent element that forms compounds by gaining or losing one electron.
A univalent antibody can bind to a single antigen site.
The chemist explained that hydrogen is typically univalent in most molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monovalent - having a valence of one
  • Degree Higher: bivalent - having a valence of two

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' meaning one and 'valentia' meaning strength or capacity.
