republic

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A political system in which the supreme power rests with citizens entitled to vote, and is exercised by representatives chosen directly or indirectly by them.
political system with elected representatives

Examples

The United States is often described as a republic rather than a pure democracy.
Many ancient nations, such as Rome, became a republic after overthrowing their monarchs.
After gaining independence, the country decided to form a republic governed by elected officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • Antonym: monarchy - a system of government with a monarch at the head

Etymology

From Latin 'res publica' meaning 'public affair' or 'commonwealth'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A form of government in which the head of state is not a monarch, and political power rests with the people or their elected representatives.
government without a monarch

Examples

France became a republic after the monarchy was abolished.
In a republic, citizens elect leaders to represent their interests.
Ireland is an example of a republic in Europe.

Synset Examples

the head of state in a republic is usually a president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monarchy - a form of government with a monarch at the head

Etymology

From Latin 'res publica' (the public affair).
