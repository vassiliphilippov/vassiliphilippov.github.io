manufactory

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tər.i / (BrE)
an establishment or place where goods are manufactured, especially on a large scale; a factory
factory or manufacturing establishment
archaic

Examples

The town grew rapidly after a large manufactory was established on its outskirts.
During the 19th century, the textile manufactory employed hundreds of workers.
Many early manufactories relied on water power before the advent of steam engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factory - a building where goods are manufactured in large quantities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'manufactoria,' from Latin 'manu' (by hand) + 'facere' (to make).
