garage

1. VERB

/ ɡəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡærɑːʒ / (BrE)
to keep or store (a vehicle) in a garage
store a vehicle in a garage

Examples

We always garage the car during the winter to protect it from snow.
Do you garage your motorcycle, or leave it outside?
After buying their new house, they finally had a place to garage their van.

Synset Examples

we don't garage our car
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02287791-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡær.ɑːʒ / (BrE)
a building or establishment where cars and other vehicles are serviced and repaired
repair shop for vehicles

Examples

I dropped my car off at the garage for its annual service.
The nearest garage was two miles away, so we had to call for a tow truck.
He owns a small garage that specializes in classic cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - a room or building where things are manufactured or repaired

Etymology

From French 'garage', from 'garer' (to shelter), originally meaning a place to store vehicles.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03421550-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡærɑːʒ / (BrE)
a building or part of a building used for parking and storing motor vehicles, especially cars.
building for parking cars

Examples

He keeps his vintage cars in a large garage behind the house.
The apartment complex offers each resident a private garage.
She pulled into the garage as it started to rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carport - an open-sided shelter for vehicles, typically attached to a house

Etymology

From French 'garage', from 'garer' meaning 'to shelter, dock'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03421399-n
Synset POS: n
