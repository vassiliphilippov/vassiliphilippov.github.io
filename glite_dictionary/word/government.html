government

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
The system or form by which a community, state, or other political unit is governed.
system by which a community is governed

Examples

The country adopted a new government after years of instability.
Different forms of government include democracy, monarchy, and dictatorship.
Critics argue that the government should be more transparent in its decisions.

Synset Examples

tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the process or activity of running a business, organization, or government
  • Antonym: anarchy - absence of government or authority

Etymology

From Old French *governer*, from Latin *gubernare* (to steer, govern).

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
The act or process of governing or exercising authority over a country, organization, or people.
the act of governing or ruling

Examples

Many citizens protest when they feel the government oversteps its authority.
She wrote a book about the history and theory of government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anarchy - absence of government or authority

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnmənt, ˈɡʌvərmənt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvəmənt, ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
the group of people or the organization that officially controls and directs a country or state
controlling authority of a country or state

Examples

The government announced new policies to boost the economy.
Many citizens protested against the decisions made by their government.
After the election, a new government was formed.

Synset Examples

the government reduced taxes
the matter was referred to higher authorities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the executive branch of a government, or the group in charge
  • Synonym: regime - a government, especially an authoritarian one
  • Antonym: anarchy - absence of government and order

Etymology

From Old French *governement*, from Latin *gubernare* (to steer, govern).
