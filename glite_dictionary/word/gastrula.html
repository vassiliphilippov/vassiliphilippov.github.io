gastrula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrələ / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrʊlə / (BrE)
An early stage of an animal embryo with two or more layers of cells, formed from the blastula during development.
embryonic stage formed after blastula
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the gastrula stage follows the blastula stage.
Scientists use a microscope to study changes occurring within the gastrula.
The formation of the three germ layers begins during the gastrula phase.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blastula - an early stage embryo consisting of a hollow sphere of cells
  • Synonym: ectoderm - the outermost layer of cells in the developing embryo
  • Synonym: mesoderm - the middle layer of cells in the developing embryo
  • Synonym: endoderm - the innermost layer of cells in the developing embryo

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek 'gastēr' (stomach) + '-ula' (diminutive suffix).
