subdued

1. ADJ

/ səbˈduːd / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːd / (BrE)
(of color, style, or quality) restrained, quiet, or not extreme.
restrained in style or quality

Examples

The room was decorated in subdued shades of blue and gray, creating a calm atmosphere.
Her subdued manner contrasted with his energetic presentation.
They spoke in subdued voices so as not to disturb the meeting.

Synset Examples

a little masterpiece of low-keyed eloquence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muted - soft or not bright in color or sound; restrained
  • Antonym: bold - showy or striking in appearance or manner

Etymology

Past participle of 'subdue', from Latin 'subduco' (to lead away, withdraw).

5. ADJ

/ səbˈduːd / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːd / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) quiet and controlled, not noisy, excited, or forceful; restrained or softened in manner or tone.
quiet and under control; restrained

Examples

After the argument, the children were suddenly subdued and unusually quiet.
Her subdued voice hinted that something was wrong.
The once lively celebration turned subdued after the disappointing news.

Synset Examples

children were subdued and silent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrained - kept under control, not showing emotion or excitement
  • Antonym: rowdy - noisy and disorderly

Etymology

Past participle of 'subdue', from Latin 'subducere' (to withdraw, bring under).
