Pseudoscorpionida

1. NOUN

/ suːdoʊˌskɔːrpiˈoʊnɪdə / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˌskɔːpɪˈəʊnɪdə / (BrE)
An order of small arachnids, commonly called false scorpions, characterized by pincers like those of true scorpions but lacking a stinger.
order of arachnids called false scorpions

Examples

Although Pseudoscorpionida resemble true scorpions, they lack a tail and stinger.
The Pseudoscorpionida are often found in leaf litter and under stones.
Scientists study Pseudoscorpionida to better understand arachnid diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false scorpion - common name for arachnids of the order Pseudoscorpionida
  • Antonym: scorpion - arachnid known for its segmented tail ending with a venomous stinger

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + Latin scorpio (scorpion) + -ida (taxonomic suffix).
