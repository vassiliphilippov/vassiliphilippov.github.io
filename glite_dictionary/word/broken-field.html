broken-field

1. ADJ

/ ˌbroʊ.kən ˈfiːld / (AmE), / ˌbrəʊ.kən ˈfiːld / (BrE)
(of movement, especially in sports) involving frequent and sudden changes in direction, often to avoid obstacles or opponents.
frequent directional changes (esp. in sports)

Examples

The running back's broken-field dash left several defenders grasping at air.
Her broken-field style made her hard to catch during the game.
A player with excellent broken-field skills can swiftly evade multiple opponents.
