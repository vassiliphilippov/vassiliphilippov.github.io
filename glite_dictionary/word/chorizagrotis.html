Chorizagrotis

1. PROPN

/ kəˌraɪzəˈgroʊtɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪzəˈɡrəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as army cutworms, some of which are agricultural pests.
genus of moths with cutworm larvae

Examples

The larvae of Chorizagrotis species are notorious for damaging crops in many regions.
Entomologists are studying Chorizagrotis to better control outbreaks of army cutworms.
Several types of Chorizagrotis moths can be found in North American fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: army cutworm - larval stage of moths in the genus Chorizagrotis, known as a pest

2. NOUN

/ kəˌraɪzəˈgroʊtɪs / (AmE), / kəˌraɪzəˈgrəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as army cutworms.
genus of moths (army cutworms)

Examples

The Chorizagrotis genus includes species whose larvae devastate crops as army cutworms.
Scientists collected several specimens of Chorizagrotis for their research on agricultural pests.
Many entomologists study Chorizagrotis to better understand cutworm outbreaks in farmland.

Etymology

From Greek 'choris' (separated) and 'Agrotis' (another moth genus, from Greek 'agros' for field).
