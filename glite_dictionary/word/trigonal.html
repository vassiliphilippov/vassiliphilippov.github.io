trigonal

1. ADJ

/ trɪˈɡɑnəl / (AmE), / trɪˈɡɒnəl / (BrE)
having or relating to threefold symmetry, especially in geometry or crystallography
having threefold symmetry

Examples

The mineral quartz often crystallizes in a trigonal system.
A trigonal pattern can be seen in certain types of snowflakes.
Some molecules have a trigonal planar structure due to the arrangement of their atoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hexagonal - having sixfold symmetry
  • Degree Higher: tetragonal - having fourfold symmetry

Etymology

From Greek 'trigonon' (triangle) plus '-al'.
