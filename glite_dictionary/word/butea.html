Butea

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːtiə / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of trees or shrubs native to South and Southeast Asia, known for species such as the flame-of-the-forest.
genus of South Asian trees or shrubs

Examples

The Butea tree is admired for its vibrant orange-red flowers in spring.
Researchers noted the medicinal uses of various parts of the Butea plant.
Farmers planted Butea along the field’s edge for shade and soil improvement.

Etymology

Named after John Stuart, 3rd Earl of Bute, Scottish patron of botany.
