to acquire

1. VERB

/ əˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
(of a tracking system) to locate and begin to track a moving object, such as by radar.
locate a moving target by tracking system

Examples

Once the missile was launched, the radar quickly acquired its target.
The system can acquire and track up to 50 moving objects at once.
It took several seconds for the telescope to acquire the passing satellite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - follow the movements of something, often using technology

Etymology

From Latin 'acquirere' (to get, obtain).
