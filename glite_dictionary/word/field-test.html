to field-test

1. VERB

/ ˈfiːld ˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌtɛst / (BrE)
to test (something) under actual operating conditions in the intended environment, rather than in a laboratory or controlled setting
test under real-world conditions

Examples

The company decided to field-test the new prototype before launching it nationwide.
Researchers will field-test the vaccine in several rural communities this year.
After several months of development, the software was finally field-tested by volunteers in real classrooms.

Synset Examples

The Army field tested the new tanks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - to try something out or examine its quality, reliability, or suitability

Etymology

From 'field' (meaning outdoors or in real-world conditions) + 'test'. Modelled on similar compounds formed in the mid-20th century.
