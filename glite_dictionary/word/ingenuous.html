ingenuous

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs / (BrE)
(of a person or action) innocent and unsuspecting; unable to hide feelings; honest and straightforward.
innocent and open; straightforward

Examples

Her ingenuous smile made it clear she was unaware of the secret.
He gave an ingenuous response, not even trying to hide his disappointment.
The child's ingenuous curiosity led to many innocent questions.

Synset Examples

an ingenuous admission of responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artless - free from deceit, cunning, or craftiness; naturally simple or innocent
  • Synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; innocent
  • Antonym: disingenuous - not candid or sincere; pretending that one knows less than one really does

Etymology

From Latin 'ingenuus' (native, candid, frank).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəs / (BrE)
(of a person or action) innocent and unsuspecting; showing a childlike openness and simplicity.
innocent and open; naive

Examples

Her ingenuous smile made it hard not to trust her intentions immediately.
He gave an ingenuous account of what happened, clearly unaware of the possible consequences.
Despite her age, she maintained an ingenuous curiosity about the world around her.

Synset Examples

a child's innocent stare
his ingenuous explanation that he would not have burned the church if he had not thought the bishop was in it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - having or showing a lack of experience, judgment, or sophistication
  • Antonym: worldly - experienced and sophisticated; not naive

Etymology

From Latin ingenuus (native, freeborn, candid, frank).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02280420-s
Synset POS: s
