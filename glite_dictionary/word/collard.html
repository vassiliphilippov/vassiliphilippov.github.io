collard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlərd / (AmE), / ˈkɒləd / (BrE)
A type of kale with large, smooth, edible leaves, often cooked as a vegetable.
type of leafy green vegetable

Examples

She prepared a delicious stew using fresh collard leaves from the garden.
Southern cuisine is famous for its slow-cooked collard greens.
The farmer grows both kale and collard varieties during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - a hardy cabbage with curled or wrinkled leaves

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'colewort', from Middle English 'cole' (cabbage) + 'wort' (plant).
