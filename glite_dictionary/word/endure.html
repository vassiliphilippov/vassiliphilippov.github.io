to endure

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊə / (BrE)
to face and withstand something difficult, usually with courage or determination
face and withstand hardship or difficulty

Examples

She had to endure months of grueling training to finish the marathon.
Many people endure hardships in pursuit of a better life.
He quietly endured the criticism from his colleagues, never letting it disturb his focus.

Synset Examples

She braved the elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withstand - to resist or stand up to something successfully
  • Antonym: to succumb - to give in or yield to something

Etymology

From Latin 'indurare' (to harden).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02713111-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊə / (BrE)
(of a state or situation) to continue or last for a specified period of time, especially something unpleasant.
to last or persist over time

Examples

The drought continued to endure for several months.
Her popularity has endured despite many controversies.
This tradition will likely endure for generations to come.

Synset Examples

The bad weather lasted for three days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last - to continue for a specified time
  • Synonym: to persist - to continue to exist especially for longer than expected

Etymology

From Latin 'indurare', meaning 'to make hard' or 'to harden'.
