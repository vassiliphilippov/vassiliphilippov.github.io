Zygocactus truncatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌzaɪɡoʊˈkæktəs trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌzaɪɡəʊˈkaktəs trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A South American cactus with jointed stems and usually red flowers, commonly grown as a houseplant; sometimes classified under the genus Schlumbergera.
South American houseplant cactus with jointed stems

Examples

The Zygocactus truncatus blooms beautifully indoors during the winter months.
Many gardeners prefer Zygocactus truncatus for its ease of care and vibrant red flowers.
She bought a Zygocactus truncatus at the nursery to brighten her living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas cactus - A commonly used name for Schlumbergera species grown as houseplants

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and 'kaktos' (cactus), and Latin 'truncatus' (cut off, truncated).
