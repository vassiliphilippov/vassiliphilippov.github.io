submissive

1. ADJ

/ səbˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / səbˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
willing to obey someone else or to do what others want without resistance
inclined to obey or yield to others

Examples

Her submissive attitude made it easy for others to take advantage of her.
He gave a submissive nod in response to his manager’s instructions.
The dog became submissive after being trained by its new owner.

Synset Examples

submissive servants
a submissive reply
replacing troublemakers with more submissive people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient - willing to obey orders or rules
  • Antonym: domineering - asserting one's will over others in an arrogant way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin submissivus (humble, yielding), from Latin submittere (to yield, send under).

2. ADJ

/ səbˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / səbˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
Willing to obey or yield to others, showing a readiness to submit, especially in an excessively humble or servile way.
ready to submit to others; very compliant

Examples

She remained submissive in the face of harsh criticism from her superiors.
The cult demanded submissive loyalty from all of its members.
His submissive attitude made him vulnerable to exploitation at work.

Synset Examples

slavish devotion to her job ruled her life
a slavish yes-man to the party bosses
she has become submissive and subservient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliant - willing to agree with others; obedient
  • Synonym: obedient - doing what one is told to do; submissive
  • Antonym: domineering - asserting one's will over others in an arrogant way

Etymology

From Latin *submittere* (to lower, yield), from *sub-* (under) + *mittere* (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00794246-s
Synset POS: s
