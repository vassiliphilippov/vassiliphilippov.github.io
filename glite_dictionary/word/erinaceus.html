Erinaceus

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛrəˈneɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Erinaceidae, including hedgehogs
genus of hedgehogs

Examples

The European hedgehog belongs to the genus Erinaceus.
Scientists have identified several species under the genus Erinaceus.
Erinaceus is notable for its members' spiny coats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - a small mammal covered with spines

Etymology

From Latin 'erinaceus' meaning 'hedgehog'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Erinaceidae, comprising the common hedgehogs of Europe and Asia
genus of hedgehogs

Examples

The genus Erinaceus includes several species of hedgehogs found throughout Europe and Asia.
Researchers studied the distribution of Erinaceus in various habitats.
Unlike other members of the family, Erinaceus species are well-known for their distinctive spiny coats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - a small nocturnal mammal with spines on its back

Etymology

From Latin *Erinaceus* (hedgehog).
