immodesty

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑdəsti / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdəsti / (BrE)
the quality of being vain, conceited, or lacking modesty
lack of modesty; conceit

Examples

His immodesty was evident in the way he talked about his accomplishments.
She was criticized for her immodesty during the interview, as she seemed overly confident.
The judge frowned upon the defendant's immodesty when he boasted about his wealth in court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest or humble
  • Synonym: vanity - excessive pride in one's appearance or achievements

Etymology

From Latin 'immodestia', from 'immodestus' (not modest).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑdəsti / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdəsti / (BrE)
the act or quality of behaving without modesty, especially by exposing one's body in a way considered indecent or attracting undue attention to one's own genitals
lack of modesty; indecent exposure

Examples

His blatant immodesty shocked everyone at the public event.
Parents worry about the immodesty displayed in some modern fashion trends.
Exposing oneself in public is an act of immodesty often condemned by the law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest or reserved in behavior

Etymology

From Latin immodestia (immoderation, lack of modesty), from immodestus (improper, immoderate).

Synset POS: n
