chameleon

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiliən / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliən / (BrE)
A small, slow-moving lizard found mainly in Africa and Madagascar, known for its ability to change the color of its skin and for its long, projectile tongue used to catch prey.
color-changing lizard of Africa and Madagascar

Examples

The chameleon changed its color to blend in with the green leaves.
A chameleon can shoot its tongue out quickly to catch insects.
Many people are fascinated by the chameleon's ability to adapt its appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a type of reptile with scaly skin and four legs

Etymology

From Greek 'khamaileōn', from 'khamai' (on the ground) + 'leōn' (lion).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmiliən / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliən / (BrE)
a person who changes their opinions, behavior, or appearance to suit different situations or people
changeable or inconstant person

Examples

He's a real chameleon, always adapting to whoever he's talking to.
Her chameleon personality makes it hard to know where she truly stands.
In the corporate world, being a chameleon can help you fit in with different teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape-shifter - a person or thing that frequently changes their form or identity
  • Antonym: steady - a person who is firm and unchanging in character or behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'chamaeleon', from Greek 'khamaileōn', meaning 'ground lion', originally used for the animal.
