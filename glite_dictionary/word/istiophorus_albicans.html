Istiophorus albicans

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪstioʊˈfɔrəs ælˈbaɪkænz / (AmE), / ˌɪstiəˈfɔːrəs ælˈbaɪkænz / (BrE)
A species of large, fast predatory fish in the billfish family, commonly known as the Atlantic sailfish, found in warmer sections of the Atlantic Ocean.
a species of Atlantic sailfish

Examples

Many anglers travel to Florida in hopes of catching an Istiophorus albicans during the season.
The Istiophorus albicans is known for its impressive leaps above the ocean surface.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Istiophorus albicans using satellite tags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailfish - a large fish with a long bill and a sail-like dorsal fin

Etymology

From Greek *istio-* (sail) and *phoros* (bearing), Latin *albicans* (whitish), referring to its sail-like dorsal fin and coloration.
