rhodolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.dəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.dəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a red or pink variety of garnet used as a gemstone
red or pink type of garnet gemstone

Examples

The ring was set with a brilliant rhodolite surrounded by tiny diamonds.
She preferred the pinkish hue of rhodolite over deeper red gems.
Many jewelers value rhodolite for its unique color and sparkle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose) and '-lite' (stone).
