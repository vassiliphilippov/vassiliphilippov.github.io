branched

1. ADJ

/ bræntʃt / (AmE), / brɑːntʃt / (BrE)
having one or more branches; dividing into branches or subdivisions.
having branches or divisions

Examples

The branched tree provided shelter for many birds.
A branched river system can cover a large area.
Many coral species have a distinctly branched appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branchless - having no branches

2. ADJ

/ bræn(t)ʃt / (AmE), / brɑːn(t)ʃt / (BrE)
having divisions or extensions resembling branches; split or forked into two or more parts.
divided into branches or forks

Examples

The tree had branched limbs that stretched toward the sky.
She studied the branched structure of coral under the microscope.
Forked lightning illuminated the night with its branched pattern.

Synset Examples

the biramous appendages of an arthropod
long branched hairs on its legs on which pollen collects
a forked river
a forked tail
forked lightning
horseradish grown in poor soil may develop prongy roots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forked - divided into two or more parts, especially at the end

Etymology

Formed from 'branch' + '-ed', indicating possession of branches or division.
