Nantucket

1. PROPN

/ nænˈtʌkɪt / (AmE), / nænˈtʌkɪt / (BrE)
an island off the coast of Massachusetts, formerly a major whaling center and now a popular resort destination
island off Massachusetts; resort, former whaling center

Examples

They spent their summer vacation on Nantucket, enjoying the beaches and historic towns.
In the nineteenth century, Nantucket was renowned for its whaling industry.
Many artists travel to Nantucket for inspiration from its scenic landscape.

Etymology

Name derived from a word in the Wampanoag language, possibly meaning 'faraway land.'
