insularism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sjə.ləˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.ləˌrɪz.əm / (BrE)
the state or quality of being isolated or detached, especially as a result of being on an island or having a narrow outlook
state of being isolated or detached

Examples

The country's insularism was evident in its strict immigration policies and reluctance to engage in international trade.
Some historians argue that England's historical insularism shaped its unique culture and political system.
Critics warned that economic insularism could limit opportunities for growth and innovation.

Synset Examples

the insulation of England was preserved by the English Channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insularity - the quality of being isolated, especially referring to narrowness in outlook

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' (island) + '-ism' (state or condition).
