Solenostemon scutellarioides

1. NOUN

/ soʊ·lɪˌnɑsˈtɛmɒn skuˌtɛləˌriˈɔɪlɪdiz / (AmE), / sɒlɪˌnɒstɛmɒn skjuːˌtɛləˌrɪɔɪliːdz / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to southeastern Asia, known for its large, brightly colored or blotched leaves and spikes of blue-violet flowers; also called coleus and sometimes placed in other genera such as Plectranthus or Coleus.
ornamental plant with colorful leaves, also called coleus

Examples

Gardeners prize Solenostemon scutellarioides for its vibrant foliage.
In tropical gardens, Solenostemon scutellarioides adds a splash of color throughout the year.
Many people know Solenostemon scutellarioides by its common name, coleus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coleus - common name for Solenostemon scutellarioides

Etymology

From Greek 'solen' (tube) + 'stemon' (stamen), and modern Latin 'scutellarioides' (resembling Scutellaria).
