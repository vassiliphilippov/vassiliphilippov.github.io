Nelumbo nucifera

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈlʌm.boʊ nuːˈsɪf.ər.ə / (AmE), / nɪˈlʌm.bəʊ njuːˈsɪf.ər.ə / (BrE)
a species of aquatic plant native to eastern Asia, widely grown for its large pink or white flowers, also known as the sacred lotus
species of aquatic plant (sacred lotus)

Examples

The seeds of the Nelumbo nucifera have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
Tourists flock to the lake each summer to see the magnificent pink blooms of the Nelumbo nucifera.
Many Asian cuisines feature roots from the Nelumbo nucifera in soups and stir-fries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lotus - an aquatic plant with large showy flowers, especially of genus Nelumbo

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature; 'Nelumbo' from Sinhala 'nelum' (lotus) and 'nucifera' meaning 'nut-bearing' in Latin.
