Hyemoschus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɛməskəs / (AmE), / haɪˈɛməskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal, hoofed mammals known as water chevrotains, native to forests of central and west Africa.
a genus of water chevrotains

Examples

The Hyemoschus is notable for its shy nature and nocturnal habits in the dense African forests.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Hyemoschus to aid in conservation.
Unlike true deer, Hyemoschus lack antlers but have elongated canine teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chevrotain - small, deer-like, hoofed mammal also called mouse deer

Etymology

From Greek 'hyemos' (rain) and 'moschos' (calf, musk), indicating association with wet habitats and musk deer.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈɛməsˌkəs / (AmE), / haɪˈɛməskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal, hoofed mammals known as water chevrotains, native to western and central Africa.
genus of African water chevrotains

Examples

The Hyemoschus is notable for its secretive, nocturnal behavior in dense African forests.
Scientists studied the Hyemoschus to better understand the ecology of water chevrotains.
Unlike other chevrotains, Hyemoschus is strictly nocturnal and aquatic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chevrotain - small, deer-like ruminant (mouse deer)

Etymology

From scientific Latin, named by taxonomists as part of zoological classification.
