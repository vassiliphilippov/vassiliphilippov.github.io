much

1. ADV

/ mʌtʃ / (AmE), / mʌtʃ / (BrE)
to a great degree or extent; very or considerably.
to a great degree or extent (adv.)

Examples

She is feeling much better after her treatment.
The weather is much colder this week than last.
You don't look much older than you did in high school.

Synset Examples

she's much better now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
  • Antonym: little - to a small degree or extent

Etymology

From Old English micel (great, much), related to Old High German mihhil.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00059624-r
Synset POS: r
