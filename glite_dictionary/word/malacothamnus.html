Malacothamnus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / ˌmaləkəˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or small trees in the mallow family, native to California and known as chaparral mallows.
genus of Californian shrubs (mallow family)

Examples

The rare Malacothamnus can be seen blooming in the Californian chaparral during spring.
Several species within the Malacothamnus genus are valued for their ornamental flowers.
Researchers are studying the distribution of Malacothamnus to better understand habitat resilience.

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) and 'thamnos' (shrub).
