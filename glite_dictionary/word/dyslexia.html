dyslexia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlɛksiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɛksiə / (BrE)
A learning disorder marked by difficulty in reading, spelling, and decoding words, despite normal intelligence.
learning disorder affecting reading
medical

Examples

Children with dyslexia may struggle to match letters to sounds when learning to read.
His teachers worked closely with him after he was diagnosed with dyslexia.
Although she has dyslexia, she excels in art and problem-solving activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alexia - inability to read due to brain injury

Etymology

From Greek dys- (difficult) + lexis (word, speech).
