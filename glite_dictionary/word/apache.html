Apache

1. NOUN

/ əˈpætʃi / (AmE), / əˈpætʃi / (BrE)
a member of any of several Athapaskan-speaking Indigenous peoples native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, historically known for resisting colonization in the 19th century
a member of a Southwestern Native American people

Examples

The Apache fiercely defended their territory against encroachment in the 19th century.
She traced her ancestry to an Apache community in New Mexico.
Many Apache traditions and languages are still preserved today.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Apache', probably from Zuni 'apachu' (enemy).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpætʃi / (AmE), / əˈpætʃi / (BrE)
the language or group of closely related languages spoken by the Apache people of the southwestern United States
language of the Apache people

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of Apache and its dialects in depth.
She is fluent in both English and Apache.
Many elders in the community still speak Apache as their first language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo - language of the Navajo people, closely related to Apache

Etymology

From Zuni ‘apachu’, meaning 'enemy', used by the Spanish to refer to the Apache people.
