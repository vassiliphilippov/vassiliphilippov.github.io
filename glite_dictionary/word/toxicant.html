toxicant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪˌkænt / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪkənt / (BrE)
a poisonous substance; something that has the qualities or effects of a poison
poisonous substance

Examples

Researchers found high levels of the toxicant in the drinking water.
Exposure to this toxicant can cause severe health problems in both humans and animals.
The company was fined for improperly disposing of a hazardous toxicant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes illness or death when taken into the body
  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms

Etymology

From Latin toxica (poison).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːksɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪkənt / (BrE)
A substance that causes injury, illness, or death in a living organism.
harmful or poisonous substance
medical

Examples

Exposure to the toxicant led to several cases of respiratory illness among the workers.
Researchers are studying the effects of the toxicant on aquatic life in the river.
The presence of a toxicant in the food supply prompted a nationwide recall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - A substance that can cause death or serious harm to a living being if taken or absorbed
  • Synonym: toxin - A poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms

Etymology

From Latin 'toxicum' (poison), influenced by 'toxic'.
