angelology

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪndʒəˈlɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌeɪndʒəˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of theology concerned with the study of angels
study of angels in theology

Examples

She developed a deep interest in angelology during her religious studies degree.
The book offers a comprehensive overview of angelology throughout Christian history.
Many scholars debate the importance of angelology in contemporary theology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demonology - the study of demons, especially as a branch of theology
  • Degree Higher: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious belief

Etymology

From Medieval Latin angelologia: angelus (angel) + -logia (-logy, the study of).
