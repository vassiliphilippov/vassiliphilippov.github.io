skimpiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmpi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskɪmpɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being very small, short, or revealing, especially in reference to clothing.
quality of being small or revealing (clothes)

Examples

The skimpiness of her dress shocked some of the older guests at the party.
Many fashion critics debated the skimpiness of the new summer collection.
Due to its skimpiness, the swimsuit was not allowed at the public pool.

Synset Examples

She cared only for brightness of colour, ease of fit, and a brevity and skimpiness of skirt absolutely astounding in these days of crinolines and trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimpy - very short or revealing, especially of clothing

Etymology

From 'skimpy' + '-ness'. 'Skimpy' likely from 'skimp', meaning to provide scantily.
