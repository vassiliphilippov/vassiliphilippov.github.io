doura

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊrə / (AmE), / ˈdaʊərə / (BrE)
A type of sorghum plant grown in dry regions of Asia and North Africa, often used for food and fodder.
variety of sorghum grown in dry places

Examples

Doura is an important crop in arid regions where other grains cannot thrive.
Farmers in North Africa often grow doura for both human consumption and livestock feed.
The market offered several varieties of doura alongside other local grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses cultivated for grain, especially in dry regions

Etymology

From Arabic 'durra', meaning sorghum or grain.
