Plectania

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈteɪniə / (AmE), / plɛkˈteɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Pezizaceae, closely related to and sometimes included in the genus Peziza.
genus of fungi in Pezizaceae

Examples

The species Plectania melastoma is often found in coniferous forests in the spring.
Several fungi belonging to the genus Plectania display cup-shaped fruiting bodies.
Researchers have debated whether certain species belong to Plectania or should be classified under Peziza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peziza - a closely related genus of fungi in Pezizaceae

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted) plus New Latin '-ia' (used for genus names).

2. NOUN

/ plɛkˈteɪniə / (AmE), / plɛkˈteɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Pezizaceae, closely related to and sometimes included in the genus Peziza.
genus of fungi in Pezizaceae

Examples

The cup-shaped fruiting bodies of Plectania are often found on decaying wood in forests.
Some taxonomists include Plectania within the broader genus Peziza.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Plectania in northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peziza - a genus of cup fungi; related genus in Pezizaceae family

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted or woven) plus '-ania' (common in genus names).
