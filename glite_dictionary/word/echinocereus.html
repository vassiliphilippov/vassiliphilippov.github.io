Echinocereus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkɪnoʊˈsɪriəs / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪnəʊˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A large genus of low-growing, shrubby, ribbed cacti native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.
genus of low-growing ribbed cacti

Examples

The Echinocereus thrives in arid climates and produces vibrant flowers in the spring.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Echinocereus genus during their expedition in Mexico.
He has an impressive collection of Echinocereus in his greenhouse, each with unique ribbed stems.

Etymology

From Greek echinos (hedgehog) and Latin cereus (candle), referencing the plant's spiny appearance and shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌekɪnoʊˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪnəʊˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)

a genus of ribbed cacti

Examples

The Echinocereus genus includes many species known for their vibrant flowers.
Botanists discovered a new species in the Echinocereus group during their expedition.
Many Echinocereus cacti thrive in the arid deserts of the American Southwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a type of succulent plant with spines

Etymology

From Greek 'echinos' (hedgehog) and Latin 'cereus' (candle), referring to the plant's spiny form.
