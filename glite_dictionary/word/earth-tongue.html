earth-tongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθ ˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ ˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
Any of several club-shaped fungi of the genus Geoglossum, typically found in soil or grassy habitats.
club-shaped fungus of the genus Geoglossum

Examples

The earth-tongue appeared at the edge of the meadow after several days of rain.
Mycology enthusiasts sometimes seek out the rare earth-tongue during autumn walks.
The glossy black earth-tongue is often mistaken for other small fungi in damp woodlands.

Etymology

Named for its tongue-like appearance and growth from the earth; from 'earth' + 'tongue'.
