criminalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl.nəs / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being a criminal; the state of being involved in crime
state of being a criminal

Examples

The prosecutor argued that the defendant's criminalness was evident from his repeated offenses.
Societal views on criminalness often affect rehabilitation policies.
Researchers studied the perception of criminalness among different social groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminality - the state or quality of being criminal
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of being not guilty or free from crime

Etymology

From 'criminal' + '-ness', a common noun-forming suffix.
