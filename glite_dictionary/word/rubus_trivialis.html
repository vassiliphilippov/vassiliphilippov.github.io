Rubus trivialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.bəs trɪˈvaɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / ˈruː.bəs trɪˈvɪ.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of dewberry native to southern North America, closely related to the blackberry.
a North American dewberry species

Examples

The fields in Texas are often dotted with patches of Rubus trivialis in spring.
Botanists study Rubus trivialis to understand its differences from related species.
The fruit of Rubus trivialis ripens earlier than many other wild berries in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - a trailing plant related to the blackberry, producing small black or blue fruit
  • Degree Higher: blackberry - a dark edible fruit and also the plant of the genus Rubus

Etymology

From Latin: 'rubus' meaning bramble, and 'trivialis' meaning common or of the crossroads.
