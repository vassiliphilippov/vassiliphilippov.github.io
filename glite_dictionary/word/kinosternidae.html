Kinosternidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪnəˈstɜrnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɒsˈtɜːnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small aquatic turtles known as mud turtles and musk turtles, found in North and Central America.
a family of aquatic turtles

Examples

The Kinosternidae are a family of turtles commonly found in slow-moving streams and ponds.
Biologists studied the habits of Kinosternidae to better understand their adaptation to aquatic environments.
Several species within the Kinosternidae are known for their ability to emit a musky odor as a defense mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mud turtle - common name for turtles in the Kinosternidae family
  • Synonym: musk turtle - another common name for some Kinosternidae turtles

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kinein' (to move) and 'sternon' (chest/breast), plus '-idae' (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɪnoʊstərˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɒˈstɜːnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized freshwater turtles, including mud turtles and musk turtles, found primarily in the Americas.
family of freshwater turtles (mud/musk turtles)

Examples

The Kinosternidae family includes both mud turtles and musk turtles common in slow-moving waters.
Scientists are studying the habitats of Kinosternidae to aid in their conservation.
Members of the Kinosternidae can be identified by their distinctive domed shells.

Etymology

From Greek 'kinein' (to move) + 'sternon' (chest), used in taxonomy for this turtle family.
