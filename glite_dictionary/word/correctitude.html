correctitude

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛktəˌtuːd / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
Correct or proper behavior; conformity to accepted standards of conduct or correctness.
correct or proper behavior

Examples

She always strives for correctitude in her professional dealings.
His air of correctitude sometimes makes him seem distant or unapproachable.
The school is known for its insistence on correctitude among its students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectitude - morally correct behavior or thinking; righteousness
  • Synonym: propriety - conformity to conventionally accepted standards of behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'correct' plus the suffix '-itude', modeled after Latin-derived nouns like 'rectitude'.
