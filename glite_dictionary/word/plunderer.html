plunderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndɚər / (AmE), / ˈplʌndərə / (BrE)
a person who steals goods, especially by force and in times of war or civil disorder
a person who steals goods by force

Examples

The plunderer seized valuables from the abandoned villages during the conflict.
Medieval castles had to protect themselves from attack by every kind of plunderer.
The invading army was notorious for being led by a ruthless plunderer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looter - a person who steals goods, especially during a riot or war
  • Synonym: robber - a person who takes property unlawfully by force or threat
  • Antonym: protector - a person who defends or guards others

Etymology

From Middle High German 'plunderen' meaning 'to plunder'. Related to 'plunder'.
