James Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪmz ˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪmz ˈbeɪ / (BrE)
a large, shallow bay forming the southern extension of Hudson Bay in Canada, located between western Quebec and northeastern Ontario.
bay in northeastern Canada

Examples

The Cree have lived along the shores of James Bay for centuries.
Researchers conducted environmental studies in the waters of James Bay.
Winter ice covers much of James Bay by late November.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hudson Bay - a large body of saltwater in northeastern Canada, into which James Bay extends
