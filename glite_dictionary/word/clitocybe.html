Clitocybe

1. NOUN

/ ˌklaɪtəˈsaɪbi / (AmE), / ˌklɪtəˈsaɪbi / (BrE)
A genus of fungi with white to pale yellow spore deposits, fleshy centrally attached stalks, and closely spaced gills.
a genus of gilled fungi

Examples

Several species within the genus Clitocybe are commonly found in woodland areas during autumn.
It is important to correctly identify Clitocybe mushrooms, as some species can be toxic.
The cap of a Clitocybe mushroom is typically funnel-shaped and pale in color.

Etymology

From Greek 'klitos' (sloping) and 'kybe' (head), referring to the shape of the cap.

2. NOUN

/ klɪˈtɑsaɪbi / (AmE), / klɪˈtɒsɪbi / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms with white to pale yellow spore deposits, fleshy centrally attached stalks, and closely attached gills.
genus of mushrooms with pale spores and attached gills

Examples

The Clitocybe genus contains several species of white-spored mushrooms found in European woodlands.
Mycologists often study Clitocybe because some species are toxic to humans.
A careful examination of the gills is necessary to accurately identify a Clitocybe specimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - an umbrella-shaped fungus
  • Synonym: Agaric - a type of gilled mushroom

Etymology

From Greek 'klitos' (slope, hill) and 'kybe' (head), referring to the shape of the cap.
