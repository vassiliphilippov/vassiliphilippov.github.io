Auschwitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈaʊʃwɪts / (AmE), / ˈaʊʃwɪts / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration and extermination camp in southwestern Poland, where millions of Jews and others were imprisoned and murdered during World War II.
Nazi concentration camp in WWII Poland

Examples

Many Holocaust survivors lost family members at Auschwitz.
The museum at Auschwitz serves as a powerful reminder of the horrors of the Holocaust.
He studied the history of Auschwitz for his research on World War II atrocities.

Etymology

Named after the nearby town Oświęcim (Auschwitz in German), Poland.
