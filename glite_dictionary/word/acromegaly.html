acromegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.rəˈmɛɡ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
A disorder characterized by abnormal enlargement of the extremities and facial features, caused by excessive secretion of growth hormone, typically due to a pituitary tumor.
abnormal enlargement of extremities and face due to hormonal disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with acromegaly after noticing the unusual growth of his hands and facial features.
People with acromegaly often experience joint pain and changes in physical appearance.
Early treatment of acromegaly can prevent serious health complications.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (extremity) + 'megas' (large).
