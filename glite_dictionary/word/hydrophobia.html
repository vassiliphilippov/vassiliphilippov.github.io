hydrophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of water, often occurring as a symptom of rabies.
extreme fear of water
medical

Examples

The patient developed intense hydrophobia after being bitten by a rabid animal.
Stories about sailors with hydrophobia circulated widely in the nineteenth century.
His doctor explained that hydrophobia is one of the most alarming signs of rabies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquaphobia - fear of water (psychiatric term)
  • Synonym: rabies - a viral disease causing hydrophobia as a symptom

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'phobos' (fear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
A symptom of rabies in humans characterized by a fear of swallowing liquids, due to throat spasms induced by attempting to drink.
fear of swallowing liquids in rabies
medical

Examples

One classic symptom of rabies is hydrophobia, causing sufferers to panic at the sight of water.
The doctor's diagnosis was confirmed by the patient's severe hydrophobia when given a glass to drink.
Early signs of hydrophobia may include reluctance or inability to swallow fluids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabies - A viral disease causing acute encephalitis in mammals, often accompanied by hydrophobia in humans

Etymology

From Greek hydrophobos, from hydor (water) + phobos (fear).

3. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
an old term for rabies, an acute viral disease of the nervous system in warm-blooded animals, usually transmitted by the bite of a rabid animal
old term for rabies
medical archaic

Examples

The old textbook described hydrophobia as a deadly threat to both humans and animals.
Symptoms of hydrophobia include difficulty swallowing and a fear of water.
In the nineteenth century, rabies was commonly referred to as hydrophobia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabies - acute viral disease of the nervous system, usually fatal, transmitted by bites of infected animals

Etymology

From Greek hydrophobia (fear of water), reflecting a characteristic symptom of rabies.
