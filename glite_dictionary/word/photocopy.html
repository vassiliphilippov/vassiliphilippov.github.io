photocopy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊtəˌkɑpi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌkɒpi / (BrE)
a copy of a document, picture, or other material made using a photocopier
copy made using a photocopier

Examples

Please make a photocopy of this letter and file it for record keeping.
The application requires a photocopy of your identification card.
She handed me a photocopy of the original painting for reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - an identical reproduction of an original

Etymology

From photo- (light) + copy; originally from Greek phōs (light) and Latin copia (abundance, copy).

2. VERB

/ ˈfoʊ.t̬oʊˌkɑː.pi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.təʊˌkɒ.pi / (BrE)
to make a photographic copy of a document or image using a machine that copies by xerography.
make a copy using a photocopier

Examples

Could you photocopy these pages for the meeting?
She photocopied her passport to submit with the application.
The librarian reminded us not to photocopy entire books due to copyright restrictions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something

Etymology

From 'photo-' (relating to light) + 'copy'; first used in the mid 20th century after the invention of xerographic machines.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
