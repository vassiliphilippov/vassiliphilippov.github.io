turbulently

1. ADV

/ ˈtɝː.bjələnt.li / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bjʊ.lənt.li / (BrE)
In a violent, disorderly, or stormy manner.
in a violent or stormy manner

Examples

The plane shook turbulently as it flew through the storm clouds.
Waves crashed turbulently against the rocky shore during the hurricane.
Feelings stirred turbulently within him as he listened to the news.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or tranquil manner

Etymology

From Latin turbulēntus (full of commotion, stormy) + English adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈtɝː.bjə.lənt.li / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bjʊ.lənt.li / (BrE)
In a turbulent manner; with agitation or disorder.
in a turbulent manner

Examples

The water in the rapids flowed turbulently after the heavy rains.
His emotions shifted turbulently as he listened to the upsetting news.
Storm clouds moved turbulently across the darkening sky.

Synset Examples

the river rolls turbulently boiling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm manner; without agitation or disturbance

Etymology

From "turbulent" + "-ly".
