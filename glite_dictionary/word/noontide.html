noontide

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːn.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈnuːn.taɪd / (BrE)
the middle of the day; noon
the middle of the day

Examples

At noontide, the sun cast its brightest rays across the fields.
Many traditions hold celebrations at noontide, symbolizing the peak of daylight.
The village square was bustling with life during noontide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noon - 12 o'clock in the daytime; midday

Etymology

Formed from 'noon' + 'tide' (Old English 'tīd' meaning 'time' or 'season').
