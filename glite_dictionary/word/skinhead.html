skinhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (BrE)
a person with a shaved head, especially a member of a youth subculture characterized by distinctive dress and sometimes associated with particular social or political attitudes
person with a shaved head; youth subculture member

Examples

The group of skinheads stood on the corner, their shaved heads gleaming in the sunlight.
He became a skinhead in his youth but left the movement after a few years.
Not all skinheads share the same beliefs or attitudes.

Etymology

Formed from 'skin' + 'head', referring to the appearance of a shaven scalp.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (BrE)
A member of a youth subculture, originally in Britain and later also in the US, whose members typically have closely shaved heads; some groups are associated with racist or violent behavior.
youth with shaven head; subculture member

Examples

The police arrested several skinheads after the violent demonstration on Saturday night.
He joined a group of skinheads in his teens but later left the movement behind.
Many people wrongly assume that all skinheads hold racist views, although the group has diverse members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punk - member of a rebellious youth subculture, especially in the late 1970s and 80s
  • Antonym: hippie - member of a countercultural movement, especially in the 1960s

Etymology

From 'skin' + 'head', referring to the shaved head appearance of group members.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈskɪnˌhɛd / (BrE)
a member of a youth subculture, originating in England in the late 1960s, characterized by closely cropped hair, working-class clothing such as work shirts, jeans with suspenders, and heavy boots, sometimes associated with aggressive or racist behavior
member of a working-class youth subculture

Examples

Several skinheads were spotted at the concert, dressed in their distinctive boots and suspenders.
During the 1970s, the rise of skinheads brought new styles and attitudes to British youth culture.
The film depicts the lives of young skinheads struggling with identity in an urban environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punk - member of a rebellious subculture emerged in the mid-1970s
  • Antonym: hippie - member of a countercultural movement of the 1960s and 70s known for pacifism and unconventional style

Etymology

From 'skin' + 'head', referring to the closely cropped or shaven hairstyle of members of the subculture.
