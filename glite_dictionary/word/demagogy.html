demagogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛməˌɡɑdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɛməɡɒdʒi / (BrE)
Political activity or practices that seek to win support by appealing to the emotions and prejudices of ordinary people, rather than by using rational argument.
use of emotion/prejudice in politics

Examples

Many leaders have resorted to demagogy during times of social unrest to rally popular support.
The rise of demagogy in recent years has undermined rational debate in politics.
His speech was criticized for its blatant demagogy and lack of factual substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagoguery - political practices appealing to prejudice and emotion

Etymology

From Greek 'demagogia', from 'demagogos' (leader of the people).
