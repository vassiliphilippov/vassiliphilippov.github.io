Cuterebridae

1. NOUN

/ kuˌtɛrəˈbrɪdiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌtɛərəˈbrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of botflies found in the New World, whose larvae are parasitic in mammals.
family of New World botflies

Examples

The larvae of the Cuterebridae family are notorious for developing within mammalian hosts.
Researchers studied how Cuterebridae infestations impact livestock health in South America.
Veterinarians are trained to remove Cuterebridae larvae from infected animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Cuterebra; '-idae' is a standard suffix for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ kuːtəˈrɛbrɪˌdi / (AmE), / kjuːtɛˈrɛbrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, hairy botflies, found in the Americas, whose larvae are parasitic on mammals.
family of New World botflies

Examples

The larvae of the Cuterebridae family can infest rodents, rabbits, and even humans.
Veterinarians often encounter cases of pets infected by Cuterebridae larvae.
Members of the Cuterebridae are commonly known as New World botflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are parasitic on mammals

Etymology

From New Latin Cuterebridae, from genus Cuterebra + -idae (family suffix).

Synset POS: n
