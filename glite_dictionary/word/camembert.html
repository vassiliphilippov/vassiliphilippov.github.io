Camembert

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæməmˌbɛrt / (AmE), / ˈkaməmˌbɛː / (BrE)
a rich, soft, creamy French cheese with an edible white rind, traditionally made from cow's milk
soft creamy French cheese

Examples

She served a fresh baguette with slices of Camembert for lunch.
The creamy texture of Camembert makes it perfect for baking as an appetizer.
He bought some French Camembert at the local cheese shop for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brie - a soft French cheese similar to Camembert but usually milder

Etymology

From French Camembert, named after the village of Camembert in Normandy, France.
