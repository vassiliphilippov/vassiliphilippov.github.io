to reassure

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears and restore their confidence
give comfort or confidence; remove doubts

Examples

The mother tried to reassure her child during the thunderstorm.
Can you reassure me that everything will be fine?
His calm voice helped to reassure the nervous passengers.

Synset Examples

I reassured him that we were safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to make someone feel less worried
  • Antonym: alarm - to make someone fearful or anxious

Etymology

From French 'rassurer', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'assicurare' (to make sure).
