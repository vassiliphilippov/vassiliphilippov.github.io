unfamiliar

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fəˈmɪl.jɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fəˈmɪl.i.ə / (BrE)
not known, experienced, or recognized by someone; not familiar
not known or recognized

Examples

She felt anxious in the unfamiliar city, unsure of where to go next.
Many children are hesitant to try unfamiliar foods for the first time.
The manual was written in terms that were completely unfamiliar to me.

Synset Examples

a name unfamiliar to most
be alert at night especially in unfamiliar surroundings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: familiar - well known or easily recognized

Etymology

From un- (not) + familiar, from Latin familiaris (of a household or family, familiar).
