Moroccan

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːkən / (AmE), / məˈrɒkən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Morocco
person from Morocco

Examples

There are many Moroccans living in cities across Europe.
The Moroccan greeted us warmly and offered us mint tea.
As a proud Moroccan, he loves to share his country's traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morocco - a country in North Africa
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ məˈrɑː.kən / (AmE), / məˈrɒk.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to Morocco or its people or culture.
of or relating to Morocco

Examples

The Moroccan city of Marrakesh is famous for its vibrant markets and architecture.
She wore a beautifully crafted Moroccan leather bag to the event.
Many tourists enjoy experiencing Moroccan cuisine, especially the traditional tagine dishes.

Synset Examples

Moroccan mosques cannot be entered by infidels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morocco - a country in Northwest Africa

Etymology

From Morocco + -an, denoting origin or nationality.
