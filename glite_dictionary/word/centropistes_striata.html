Centropistes striata

1. NOUN

/ sɛn.trəˈpɪs.tiz straɪˈeɪ.tə / (AmE), / sɛn.trəˈpɪs.tiːz straɪˈaɪ.tə / (BrE)
A bluish black-striped sea bass found along the Atlantic coast of the United States.
a species of Atlantic sea bass

Examples

Fishermen along the Atlantic often catch Centropistes striata during the summer months.
The diet of Centropistes striata primarily consists of small fish and crustaceans.
Marine biologists study the spawning behavior of Centropistes striata to protect its population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black sea bass - common name for Centropistes striata, an Atlantic coast fish

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Centropistes' and species 'striata', meaning 'striped' in Latin.
