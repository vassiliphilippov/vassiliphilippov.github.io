Amianthum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmiˈænθəm / (AmE), / ˌæmiˈænθəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the lily family, including species such as fly poison; sometimes classified in the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of North American plants (fly poison)

Examples

Amianthum is notable for its toxic properties and attractive white flowers.
Some botanists currently place Amianthum in the family Melanthiaceae rather than Liliaceae.
The fly poison plant, or Amianthum, grows in open woodlands across eastern North America.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmaɪænθəm / (AmE), / əˈmaɪænθəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Melanthiaceae, known for containing the species commonly called fly poison.
genus of flowering plants (fly poison)

Examples

The rare plant belonging to the genus Amianthum is often called fly poison.
Botanists have long debated the classification of Amianthum within plant families.
The leaves of Amianthum are sometimes mistaken for those of harmless garden plants.
