Tillandsia usneoides

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈlændziə ˌjuːsniˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / tɪˈlandziə ˌjuːsnɪˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of epiphytic plant with long, gray-green hairlike strands, commonly known as Spanish moss, found hanging from trees in the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and South America.
species of epiphytic plant (Spanish moss)

Examples

Tillandsia usneoides drapes majestically from oak trees throughout the southern United States.
Gardeners often cultivate Tillandsia usneoides for its ornamental appearance and minimal soil requirements.
Researchers studied the air purification properties of Tillandsia usneoides in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish moss - common name for Tillandsia usneoides, a hanging epiphytic plant

Etymology

From the genus name Tillandsia, honoring Swedish botanist Elias Tillandz, and the species epithet usneoides, meaning 'resembling Usnea' (a genus of lichens), from Latin.
