cheap

1. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
Low in price; costing little compared to others of the same kind.
low-priced; costing little

Examples

The furniture in that store is surprisingly cheap for its quality.
We decided to stay at a cheap hotel to save money during our trip.
She prefers to buy cheap clothes so she can change styles often.

Synset Examples

it would have been cheap at twice the price
inexpensive family restaurants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexpensive - low in price; not costing much
  • Antonym: expensive - high in price; costing a lot of money

Etymology

From Old English céap (barter, bargain, price).

2. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
Of very poor quality or flimsy; not well made or durable.
of poor quality; flimsy

Examples

The fabric felt cheap and started tearing after just a few wears.
People often regret buying cheap shoes that fall apart quickly.
The movie was disappointing, filled with cheap special effects and poor acting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoddy - of poor quality; badly made
  • Synonym: inferior - of lower quality or value
  • Antonym: quality - of high standard; well made
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02354508-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
unwilling to spend money to the point of causing embarrassment; overly stingy
embarrassingly stingy or reluctant to spend

Examples

It's so cheap of him to split the bill down to the last cent.
Don't be cheap—leave a proper tip for good service.
She comes across as cheap because she's always looking for ways to avoid paying her share.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingy - unwilling to spend money; mean with money
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give and spend money freely

Etymology

From Old English 'céap' (trade, bargain, purchase), related to buying and selling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116856-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
tastelessly showy or gaudy; lacking in good taste and subtlety, often in a way meant to attract attention
tastelessly showy or gaudy

Examples

Her cheap jewelry sparkled in the sunlight, drawing everyone’s attention for all the wrong reasons.
The hotel's lobby was decorated in cheap gold and bright lights that felt overwhelming.
He wore a cheap suit with loud colors that looked more garish than fashionable.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawdry - showy but cheap and of poor quality
  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Antonym: tasteful - showing good aesthetic judgment and sensitivity
