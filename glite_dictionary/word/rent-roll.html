rent-roll

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnt ˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɛnt rəʊl / (BrE)
a list or register showing the names of tenants and the amounts of rent they pay to a landlord or property manager
list of tenants and their rents

Examples

The property manager updated the rent-roll to reflect the new tenants and their lease agreements.
At the end of each month, investors review the rent-roll to assess the building's income.
Accurate maintenance of the rent-roll is essential for tracking rental payments and occupancy rates.

Etymology

From 'rent' (payment for use of property) and 'roll' (register or list), historically referring to records kept on rolled parchment.
