Pontus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɒn.təs / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.təs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the ancient personification of the sea, regarded as the father of Nereus.
Greek mythological personification of the sea

Examples

In ancient myths, Pontus was revered as the original sea god.
The genealogy of Greek gods lists Pontus as the father of numerous sea deities.
Artists often depicted Pontus as a powerful figure emerging from the waves.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πόντος (Póntos), meaning 'sea'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɑn.təs / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.təs / (BrE)
an ancient region in northern Asia Minor, situated along the Black Sea, which reached its height under Mithridates VI before being incorporated into the Roman Empire.
ancient region in northern Asia Minor

Examples

The kingdom of Pontus posed a formidable challenge to Roman expansion in Asia Minor.
Many coastal cities in Pontus prospered from trade along the Black Sea.
After the defeat of Mithridates VI, Pontus became a Roman province.

Etymology

From Latin Pontus, from Ancient Greek Πόντος (Póntos), meaning 'sea'.
