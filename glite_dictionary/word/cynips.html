Cynips

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪnɪps / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪps / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Cynipidae, comprising certain gall wasps.
genus of gall wasps (Cynipidae)

Examples

The oak tree in the park was infested with galls produced by Cynips wasps.
Biologists collected specimens of Cynips to study their complex life cycles.
Several species within the genus Cynips induce characteristic swellings on plant tissues.

Etymology

From Greek kunips (gnat, midge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.nɪps / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ɪps / (BrE)
A genus of gall wasps in the family Cynipidae, characterized by causing galls on oak trees.
genus of gall wasps

Examples

The Cynips genus is responsible for creating distinctive galls on oak leaves.
Researchers collected several Cynips specimens to study their life cycles.
Gardeners often notice the effects of Cynips when strange growths appear on their oak trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - any wasp of the family Cynipidae, known for inducing galls on plants

Etymology

From Latin *Cynips*, the original name given by Linnaeus for this genus.
