sapphism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsæfɪzəm / (BrE)
sexual attraction or relationship between women; female homosexuality, especially in literary or historical contexts.
female homosexuality

Examples

The novel explores themes of sapphism through its depiction of intimate relationships between the female characters.
In the early twentieth century, discussions of sapphism were often confined to academic and literary circles.
Her poetry was celebrated for its open embrace of sapphism, challenging social conventions of her time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesbianism - sexual orientation characterized by attraction of women to women

Etymology

From the name Sappho, an ancient Greek poetess from Lesbos famed for her poetry about love between women, plus the suffix -ism.
