diving

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or action of entering water headfirst, typically from a height such as a springboard or platform
plunging headfirst into water

Examples

She practiced her diving at the local swimming pool every afternoon.
Professional diving requires skill, training, and courage.
The team's diving routine impressed the judges with its precision and grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping - propelling oneself off a surface into the air

Etymology

From 'dive' + '-ing', with roots in Old English 'dýfan' (to dip, submerge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport or activity of jumping into water from a platform or springboard, often while performing acrobatic movements
the sport of jumping into water

Examples

She has been training for diving competitions since she was eight years old.
Olympic diving requires both skill and grace to impress the judges.
The school offers classes in synchronized swimming and diving for all levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimming - the sport or practice of moving through water by using the limbs

Etymology

From the verb 'dive', Old English 'dȳfan' (to dip, plunge).
