Plantago rugelii

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ ruːˈɡɛliː / (AmE), / plænˈteɪɡəʊ ruːˈɡɛliː / (BrE)
A species of plantain native to North America, characterized by broad leaves and reddish leafstalks.
North American plantain species

Examples

The leaves of Plantago rugelii are often used in traditional remedies.
You can identify Plantago rugelii by its reddish leafstalks and broad foliage.
During our hike, we spotted several clumps of Plantago rugelii near the forest edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - Any plant of the genus Plantago, especially those with broad leaves

Etymology

Named after Rugel, a 19th-century German-born botanist; genus from Latin 'plantago' (plantain).
