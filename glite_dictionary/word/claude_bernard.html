Claude Bernard

1. PROPN

/ klɔd bɛrˈnɑrd / (AmE), / kləʊd bɛəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
(1813–1878) French physiologist renowned for his research on the digestive system and the liver's glycogenic function.
French physiologist (1813–1878)

Examples

Claude Bernard is often considered one of the founders of experimental medicine.
Medical students study Claude Bernard's contributions to physiology in their early courses.
The work of Claude Bernard revolutionized our understanding of internal bodily processes.
