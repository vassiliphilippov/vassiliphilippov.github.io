to extinguish

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to destroy or kill (a group of people, animals, etc.) completely, especially in large numbers
kill or destroy completely in large numbers

Examples

War and famine nearly extinguished the indigenous tribes of the region.
The pollution threatens to extinguish several species of fish in the river.
If unchecked, the disease could extinguish entire communities in remote areas.

Synset Examples

the plague wiped out an entire population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy completely
  • Synonym: to exterminate - to kill all of a group
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep alive or maintain

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere' (to quench, put out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00471632-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to bring something to an end; to terminate or eliminate something, especially customs, ideas, or debts
bring to an end; terminate or eliminate

Examples

The government aimed to extinguish certain outdated practices through new legislation.
Decades of education gradually extinguished prejudice in that community.
The company worked hard to extinguish its financial liabilities.

Synset Examples

Let's eliminate the course on Akkadian hieroglyphics
Socialism extinguished these archaic customs
eliminate my debts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy completely; to wipe out entirely
  • Antonym: preserve - to maintain something in its original state or prevent it from ending

Etymology

From Latin exstinguere (to quench, destroy), from ex- (out) + stinguere (to quench).

3. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to put an end to something; to destroy or end the existence of (often used figuratively or formally)
to put an end to; destroy

Examples

The dictator tried to extinguish all opposition to his rule.
Her sudden kindness helped to extinguish his feelings of resentment.
The disease threatened to extinguish the entire population of the village.

Synset Examples

The Nazis snuffed out the life of many Jewish children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to snuff out - to kill or put an end to (figurative)
  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of
  • Antonym: to create - to bring into existence; opposite of destroy or extinguish

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere', meaning 'to quench, extinguish'.

4. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to cause a fire, flame, or light to stop burning or shining; to put out
put out a fire, flame, or light

Examples

It took the firefighters hours to extinguish the burning building.
Please extinguish the candles before leaving the room.
The rain finally helped to extinguish the flames in the forest.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quench - to put out or suppress a fire, flame, or feeling
  • Antonym: to ignite - to set on fire; to cause to start burning

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere' (to quench, destroy), from 'ex-' (out) + 'stinguere' (to quench).

5. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to put out (a cigarette, cigar, or similar item) by pressing or crushing it until it stops burning
put out by crushing (e.g. cigarette)

Examples

She extinguished her cigarette in the ashtray before entering the non-smoking area.
He quickly extinguished his cigar by pressing it into the sand.
Please extinguish all smoking materials before boarding the bus.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stub out - to extinguish (a cigarette or similar item) by crushing the burning end against a surface
  • Antonym: light - to ignite or cause to begin burning (e.g. a cigarette)

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere,' meaning 'to quench, put out.'
