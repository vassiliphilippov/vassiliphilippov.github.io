detergency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the quality or property of having cleansing power, especially as found in detergents or cleaning agents.
quality of cleansing power

Examples

The detergency of a soap depends on its ability to emulsify oils and dirt.
Scientists are developing new agents with increased detergency for industrial cleaning.
The laundry powder was tested for detergency to ensure it could remove stubborn stains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detergent - a substance used for cleaning
  • Synonym: cleansing - having the quality of cleaning

Etymology

From Latin 'detergere' (to wipe away), plus English suffix '-ency'.
