perceivable

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsiːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / pəˈsiːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be perceived, noticed, or understood; capable of being apprehended by the senses or the mind.
able to be perceived or understood

Examples

There was a perceivable change in his mood after he heard the news.
The difference between the samples was barely perceivable to the naked eye.
Her excitement was clearly perceivable in her voice as she spoke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptible - not able to be perceived

Etymology

From perceive + -able, from Latin percipere (to seize, understand).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsiːvəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈsiːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be noticed or recognized, especially by sight or hearing.
able to be noticed or recognized

Examples

The faint outline of the mountains became perceivable through the early morning fog.
There was a barely perceivable change in his expression when he heard the news.
The sound of running water was only perceivable when the room was completely silent.

Synset Examples

perceivable through the mist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptible - impossible to notice or perceive
  • Synonym: detectable - able to be discovered or noticed

Etymology

From perceive (Middle English, from Old French *percevoir*, from Latin *percipere* 'seize, understand') + -able.
