chequer

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
to mark or decorate with a pattern of squares or crossed lines, especially in a regular design
mark with squares or crossed lines
british

Examples

Pale sunlight chequered the forest floor through the tree branches.
The artist chequered the border of the canvas in blue and white.
As a child, he would chequer his notebooks with elaborate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checker - to mark or decorate with squares (American spelling)

Etymology

From Old French 'eschequier' (game board), from 'eschec' (chess), ultimately from Arabic 'shah' (king).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01696873-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
a flat, round piece used for playing the game of checkers
game piece in checkers
british

Examples

He picked up a red chequer and jumped over two of his opponent's pieces.
The box was missing one black chequer, so they couldn't finish the game.
She crowned her chequer by reaching the opposite side of the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checker - a flat, round piece used in the game of checkers (American term)
  • Degree Higher: king - a promoted checkers piece that can move backwards and forwards

Etymology

From Middle English cheker, from Old French eschequier, from Medieval Latin scaccarium (chessboard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03015009-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
to mark or pattern with alternating squares or patches of different colors or shades
mark with alternating patterns or colors
british

Examples

Sunlight chequered the forest floor through the thick canopy above.
The tiled floor chequered the entrance hall with bands of white and blue.
Shadows from the blinds chequered her face in a delicate pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checker - to mark or pattern with a checkerboard arrangement

Etymology

From Old French 'eschequier' (a chessboard), from 'eschec' (chess).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00438275-v
Synset POS: v
