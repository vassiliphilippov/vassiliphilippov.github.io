Flemish

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛm.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflɛm.ɪʃ / (BrE)
the variety of Dutch spoken in northern Belgium, one of the country's official languages
Dutch-related language of northern Belgium

Examples

Many residents of Belgium speak Flemish as their first language.
The television program was broadcast in Flemish with English subtitles.
He learned Flemish after moving to Antwerp for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - the official language of the Netherlands and northern Belgium

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *Vlamisch*, from *Vlaem* (Flanders).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflɛm.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflɛm.ɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Flanders, its people, language, or culture
relating to Flanders or its people

Examples

The Flemish language is spoken by many people in northern Belgium.
She admired the intricate details in the Flemish tapestry displayed in the museum.
Several famous Flemish artists played a major role in the Renaissance period.

Synset Examples

the Flemish population of Belgium
Flemish painters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belgian - of or relating to Belgium or its people

Etymology

From Dutch 'Vlaamsch', referring to the Flemings of Flanders.

3. NOUN

/ ˈflɛmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflɛmɪʃ / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group speaking Flemish and living in northern and western Belgium
member of the Flemish people of Belgium

Examples

Members of the Flemish community have a distinct cultural heritage in Belgium.
The Flemish are known for their rich tradition in art and literature.
Although he lived in Brussels, he identified strongly as Flemish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fleming - a native or inhabitant of Flanders
  • Antonym: Walloon - a member of a French-speaking people in southern Belgium

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *Vlamisch*, from *Vlaemsch* (relating to Flanders, a region in Belgium).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08500799-n
Synset POS: n
