metalepsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which a word or phrase from one figurative expression is used in a new context, substituting one metonymy for another.
figure of speech using one figurative sense for another

Examples

In classical rhetoric, writers often employ metalepsis to create layered or complex references.
The use of metalepsis in the poem added a subtle irony to the narrative voice.
Her analysis focused on Shakespeare's frequent deployment of metalepsis as a means of challenging conventional metaphors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metonymy - Substitution of the name of an attribute for that of the thing meant

Etymology

From Greek metalēpsis (a taking in exchange), from meta- (beyond) + lambanein (to take).
