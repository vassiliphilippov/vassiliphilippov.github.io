necromania

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛk.rəˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (BrE)
an abnormal or irresistible sexual attraction to dead bodies
sexual attraction to corpses
medical

Examples

The psychologist explained that necromania is a rare but recognized condition in forensic psychiatry.
Cases involving individuals with necromania are often the subject of criminal investigations and legal concern.
The documentary explored the origins and historical reports of necromania throughout various cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necrophilia - sexual attraction to dead bodies

Etymology

From Greek 'nekros' (dead) + 'mania' (madness, frenzy).
