inordinately

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɔrdnətli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːdnətli / (BrE)
To an unusually large or excessive degree; excessively.
excessively; to an unusual degree

Examples

The movie was inordinately long, stretching over three hours without a break.
She became inordinately upset over the minor misunderstanding.
Their cat is inordinately affectionate, following them everywhere they go.

Synset Examples

she was inordinately smart
it will be an extraordinarily painful step to negotiate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a greater than normal or reasonable degree
  • Antonym: moderately - to a reasonable or average extent; not excessively

Etymology

From Latin 'inordinatus' meaning 'disordered' or 'excessive', with English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
