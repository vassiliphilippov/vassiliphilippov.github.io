Anasa tristis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæsə ˈtrɪstɪs / (AmE), / əˈnæsə ˈtrɪstɪs / (BrE)
A large, dark-colored North American bug of the squash bug family that feeds on the sap of plants in the gourd family, often considered an agricultural pest.
species of North American squash bug

Examples

The garden suffered heavy damage after Anasa tristis invaded the squash plants.
Farmers use various methods to control Anasa tristis populations in their fields.
Careful inspection revealed that Anasa tristis was the cause of the wilted vines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash bug - common term for bugs of the species Anasa tristis

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Anasa' and species 'tristis' meaning 'sad.'
