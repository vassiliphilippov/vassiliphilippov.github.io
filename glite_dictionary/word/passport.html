passport

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.pɔːt / (BrE)
An official document issued by a government certifying the holder's identity and citizenship, allowing them to travel abroad and re-enter their home country.
official travel document

Examples

You need a valid passport to travel internationally.
She renewed her passport before her trip to Europe.
The immigration officer stamped my passport at the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visa - an endorsement allowing entry to a foreign country

Etymology

From French 'passeport', from passer (to pass) + port (port, harbor, gate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːspɔːt / (BrE)
a quality or trait that makes it possible for someone to be accepted or to gain access to something
quality that allows acceptance or entry

Examples

A university degree can be a passport to a successful career.
Her confidence was the passport she needed to join their group.
Talent is often seen as the passport to fame in the entertainment industry.

Synset Examples

her pleasant personality is already a recommendation
his wealth was not a passport into the exclusive circles of society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - something that makes a person or thing attractive or acceptable

Etymology

From French 'passeport' (literally 'pass port'), meaning a document for passing through a port.

Synset POS: n
