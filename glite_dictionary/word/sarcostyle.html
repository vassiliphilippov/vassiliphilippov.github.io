sarcostyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːrkəˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɑːkəˌstaɪl / (BrE)
one of the contractile filaments that make up a striated muscle fiber, involved in muscle contraction
muscle filament in striated muscle fiber
medical

Examples

Each striated muscle fiber contains hundreds of sarcostyles arranged in parallel.
The breakdown of a sarcostyle can impair muscle contraction efficiency.
Microscopic analysis revealed damage to several sarcostyles within the muscle tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myofibril - a basic rod-like unit of a muscle cell, of which sarcostyles are a type

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) + 'stylos' (pillar, column).
