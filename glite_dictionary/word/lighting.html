lighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or skill of arranging or providing artificial light for a space, event, or scene
providing artificial light

Examples

Good lighting can completely transform the atmosphere of a room.
The director worked closely with the lighting designer to create dramatic effects on stage.
Proper lighting is essential for reading and studying efficiently.

Synset Examples

an interior decorator must understand lighting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the act of lighting up a place; lighting
  • Antonym: darkness - the absence of light

Etymology

From the verb 'light' + -ing, meaning the act or process of supplying light.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Equipment and techniques used to provide artificial light for the stage, film, or television and to create visual effects.
equipment for artificial stage or film light

Examples

The lighting in the play created a dramatic and mysterious atmosphere.
Professional lighting is essential for shooting high-quality movies.
She adjusted the lighting before the concert began to highlight the main performer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - artificial lighting; the act of lighting a place
  • Synonym: spotlight - a strong beam of light on a small area, especially the stage
  • Antonym: darkness - absence of lighting or light

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the condition or quality of having abundant light or illumination in a place; the arrangement or use of lights to achieve a particular effect
the amount or arrangement of light in a place

Examples

We need better lighting in the kitchen because it is too dim.
The museum's careful lighting highlights the beauty of each painting.
She chose soft lighting for a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

Synset Examples

they played as long as it was light
as long as the lighting was good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the provision of light to make something visible

Etymology

From the verb 'light' (to illuminate) with the suffix '-ing'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of setting something on fire or igniting it
the act of igniting something

Examples

The lighting of the bonfire marked the start of the festival.
He was fined for the illegal lighting of fireworks in the park.
The sudden lighting of a match startled everyone in the dark room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition - the act of setting something on fire
  • Antonym: extinguishing - the act of putting out a fire or light

Etymology

From the verb 'light', from Old English *lȳhtan* (to emit light, to kindle).
