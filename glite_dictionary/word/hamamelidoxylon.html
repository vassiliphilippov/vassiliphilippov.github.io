Hamamelidoxylon

1. NOUN

/ həˌmæməˌliːdəˈzaɪlən / (AmE), / həˌmaməˌliːdəˈzɪlən / (BrE)
A genus of fossil plants whose wood is identical with or similar to that of the witch hazel.
genus of fossil plants like witch hazel

Examples

The discovery of Hamamelidoxylon fossils sheds light on plant evolution during the Mesozoic era.
Botanists compared the wood structure of Hamamelidoxylon to that of modern witch hazel.
Paleobotanical studies often reference Hamamelidoxylon due to its distinctive anatomical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - a shrub of the genus Hamamelis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Hamamelis' (witch hazel) and Greek 'xylon' (wood).
