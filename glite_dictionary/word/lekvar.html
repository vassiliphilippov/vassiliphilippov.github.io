lekvar

1. NOUN

/ lɛkˌvɑɹ / (AmE), / lɛkˌvɑː / (BrE)
a thick sweet spread or filling made from puréed prunes, apricots, or other dried fruits, often used in pastries
thick sweet fruit spread

Examples

My grandmother always fills her homemade pastries with lekvar instead of other types of jam.
The recipe calls for a generous layer of lekvar between the cake layers.
You can find lekvar in the international foods aisle at many grocery stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a sweet spread of fruit and sugar, typically less thick than lekvar

Etymology

From Hungarian 'lekvár' (jam, fruit preserve), ultimately from Czech 'lepkavý' (sticky).
