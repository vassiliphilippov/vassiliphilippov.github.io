Ameiurus melas

1. NOUN

/ əˌmaɪˈjʊərəs ˈmiːlæs / (AmE), / əˌmaɪˈjʊərəs ˈmiːləs / (BrE)
A species of catfish, commonly known as the black bullhead, native to freshwater habitats in the eastern United States.
species of catfish (black bullhead)

Examples

The Ameiurus melas is often found in muddy rivers and lakes throughout the eastern United States.
Fishermen sometimes mistake the Ameiurus melas for other species of bullhead catfish.
The diet of Ameiurus melas consists mainly of aquatic insects, small fish, and plant material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black bullhead - Common name for Ameiurus melas, a North American catfish

Etymology

From Greek ameinos (better) + oura (tail), and Latin melas (black).
