emery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛməri / (AmE), / ˈɛməri / (BrE)
a hard grey-black mineral mixture composed chiefly of corundum, used as an abrasive, especially for polishing or grinding surfaces
abrasive mineral mixture

Examples

The jeweler used emery paper to smooth the rough edges of the ring.
Industrial grinders often have wheels coated with emery for polishing metal surfaces.
She carefully filed her nails with an emery board until they were smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corundum - a hard mineral, aluminum oxide, used as an abrasive

Etymology

From Old French 'esmeri', from Late Latin 'smericulum', from Greek 'smiris' (emery).
