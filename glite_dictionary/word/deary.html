deary

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪəri / (AmE), / ˈdɪəri / (BrE)
A person who is loved or cherished; used as a term of endearment.
term of endearment for a loved person

Examples

Come here, deary, and tell me all about your day.
It’s all right, deary, I’ll take care of everything.
The old shopkeeper called each customer deary as they entered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dear - a loved or cherished person; affectionate form of address

Etymology

Diminutive of 'dear'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪəri / (AmE), / ˈdɪəri / (BrE)
A person who is loved or dear to someone, often used as a term of endearment.
beloved person; term of endearment

Examples

My deary, would you like a cup of tea?
He whispered, "Good night, deary," before turning off the lights.
She hugged her grandmother, calling her "my sweet deary."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a loved one; term of endearment
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person loved by someone; term of affection

Etymology

From 'dear' + -y, forming a diminutive or affectionate noun.
