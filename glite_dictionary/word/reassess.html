to reassess

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɛs / (BrE)
to consider or evaluate something again, especially with the possibility of making changes
to evaluate again or reconsider

Examples

After new evidence emerged, the committee decided to reassess the case.
It's important to reassess your goals periodically to stay on track.
The company will reassess its policies in response to customer feedback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to examine or assess something again
  • Synonym: to evaluate - to assess or judge the value or quality of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + assess, first attested in the mid-20th century.
