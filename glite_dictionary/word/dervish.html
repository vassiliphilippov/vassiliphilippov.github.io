dervish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɜrvɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɜːvɪʃ / (BrE)
A member of a Muslim (especially Sufi) religious order known for devotional exercises such as whirling, chanting, or other bodily movements, usually leading an ascetic lifestyle.
a Sufi Muslim ascetic known for devotional dances

Examples

The dervish spun gracefully, lost in prayer and meditation.
As a young man, he joined a Sufi order and became a dervish.
Crowds gathered to watch the dervishes perform their whirling dance during the festival.

Etymology

From Turkish 'derviş', from Persian 'darvīsh' meaning 'poor', 'ascetic'.
