to cozen

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌzən / (AmE), / ˈkʌz(ə)n / (BrE)
to trick or deceive someone by artful deception; to cheat or defraud
deceive or cheat by trickery

Examples

He tried to cozen the old woman out of her savings with a clever story.
She realized too late that she had been cozened by his charming manner and false promises.
The swindler made a living by cozening innocent tourists on the busy streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain advantage; to deceive
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or outwit

Etymology

Probably from Italian 'cozzonare' (to cheat), from 'cozzone' (a horse trader, cheating dealer).

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌzən / (AmE), / ˈkʌz(ə)n / (BrE)
to deceive, cheat, or trick someone, especially to gain something dishonestly
deceive or cheat (someone)

Examples

The unscrupulous salesman tried to cozen elderly customers into buying faulty products.
She refused to let anyone cozen her out of her inheritance.
Throughout history, swindlers have found ways to cozen honest people.

Synset Examples

He cozened the money out of the old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or outwit someone by cunning
  • Antonym: be honest - to act with honesty, not deceiving or cheating

Etymology

From Middle English cosen, probably from French cousiner (to deceive by claiming kinship), from cousin (relative).

3. VERB

/ ˈkʌzən / (AmE), / ˈkʌzən / (BrE)
to deceive or cheat someone; to act dishonestly or trick someone
deceive or cheat

Examples

He tried to cozen the tourists by selling them fake jewelry.
The con artist cozened many people out of their savings.
She warned her friends not to cozen others, no matter how tempting it might seem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English cosin, perhaps from French cousiner (to claim kinship fraudulently), from Old French cosin (cousin).

Synset POS: v
