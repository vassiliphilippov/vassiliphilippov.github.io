Leontodon autumnalis

1. NOUN

/ liːˈɑntədɑn ɔːˈtʌmnəlɪs / (AmE), / liːˈɒntədɒn ɔːˈtʌmnəlɪs / (BrE)
A European herbaceous plant with yellow flowers that blooms in the fall and is naturalized in the United States, also known as autumn hawkbit.
fall-blooming yellow-flowered herb

Examples

The field was dotted with the bright yellow blooms of Leontodon autumnalis in late September.
Botanists noted that Leontodon autumnalis has become widespread across parts of North America.
We identified several patches of Leontodon autumnalis during our autumn nature walk.

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Leontodon' meaning 'lion's tooth,' and species 'autumnalis' meaning 'of autumn.'
