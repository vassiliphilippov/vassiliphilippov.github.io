to uniformize

1. VERB

/ ˈjunəfərˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪfəˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to make uniform or consistent in form, character, or operation
make uniform or consistent

Examples

The new policy aims to uniformize graduation requirements across all departments.
Scientists worked to uniformize the measurements before analyzing the data.
Efforts to uniformize the product labels have improved customer understanding.

Synset Examples

the data have been uniformized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - to make something conform to a standard or norm
  • Antonym: to diversify - to make or become more diverse or varied

Etymology

From uniform + -ize, modeled after similar formations like 'standardize.'
