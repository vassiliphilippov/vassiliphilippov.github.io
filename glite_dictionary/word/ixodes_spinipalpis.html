Ixodes spinipalpis

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz spɪˌnɪpælˈpɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz spɪˌnɪpalˈpɪs / (BrE)
a species of hard-bodied tick found in North America that transmits the Lyme disease spirochete to animals such as cottontail rabbits and wood rats
tick species transmitting Lyme disease
medical

Examples

Researchers found that Ixodes spinipalpis commonly infests wood rats in the western United States.
The role of Ixodes spinipalpis in transmitting Lyme disease is significant among small mammals.
Unlike other ticks, Ixodes spinipalpis rarely bites humans but poses risks to wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small arachnid that is a parasite on animals and humans
  • Synonym: Ixodes scapularis - species of tick that is a main vector of Lyme disease

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Ixodes' (the genus of ticks), and Latin 'spina' (spine) + 'palpus' (feeler).
