tolerance

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːlərəns / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərəns / (BrE)
the willingness to accept or respect beliefs and practices that are different from one's own
acceptance of differing beliefs or practices

Examples

A society that values tolerance encourages open discussion of different viewpoints.
Schools often promote tolerance to help students understand and appreciate diversity.
Her tolerance of other religions made her a respected community leader.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept differing beliefs or practices
  • Synonym: acceptance - the act of accepting or welcoming something

Etymology

From Latin tolerantia (endurance, forbearance), from tolerare (to endure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːlərəns / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərəns / (BrE)
the ability of an organism to endure or be unaffected by adverse environmental conditions
ability to endure unfavorable conditions

Examples

Some plants show remarkable tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.
The fish's tolerance of polluted water makes it suitable for urban streams.
Genetic variation can influence an animal's tolerance to temperature extremes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - the ability to avoid being affected by something harmful
  • Antonym: susceptibility - the tendency to be easily affected or harmed by something

Etymology

From Latin tolerantia (endurance, forbearance), from tolerare (to endure).
