Aristolochiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˌstoʊlɒˈkaɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌærɪˌstɒlɒˈkɪːliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants characterized by a tubular petaloid perianth and an inferior ovary, including the birthwort family
order of flowering plants (botanical term)

Examples

The Aristolochiales are best known for including the birthwort family.
Recent genetic studies have revised the classification of the Aristolochiales.
Plants in the Aristolochiales often have unusual floral structures.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Aristolochia and standard taxonomic suffix -ales (denoting plant orders).
