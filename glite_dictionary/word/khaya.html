Khaya

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African trees in the mahogany family, valued for their hard, reddish-brown timber.
African mahogany tree genus

Examples

The dense wood of Khaya is prized for making fine furniture and veneers.
Many forests in West Africa are home to the majestic Khaya species.
The plantation was established to cultivate Khaya due to its valuable timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - Hard reddish-brown timber from several tropical trees

Etymology

From West African languages, recorded in scientific Latin as 'Khaya'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of African mahogany trees valued for their timber.
genus of African mahogany trees

Examples

The wood from Khaya trees is prized for making fine furniture and boats.
Many forests in West Africa are home to various species of Khaya.
Researchers are studying the growth habits of different Khaya species for reforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - tropical hardwood tree or timber

Etymology

From scientific Latin, ultimately from African languages (original local names).
