ceftriaxone

1. NOUN

/ sɛfˈtraɪ.əˌzoʊn / (AmE), / sɛf.trɪˈæks.əʊn / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat severe bacterial infections, often administered by injection
an injectable antibiotic (a cephalosporin)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ceftriaxone to treat the patient’s severe pneumonia.
Laboratory tests showed the bacteria were sensitive to ceftriaxone.
She was given ceftriaxone intravenously for her urinary tract infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a group of antibiotics related to the fungus Acremonium
  • Degree Lower: penicillin - an antibiotic derived from Penicillium fungi

Etymology

From cephalosporin + tri- + -axone, relating to its chemical structure.
