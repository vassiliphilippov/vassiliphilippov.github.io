bridge-and-tunneler

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɪdʒ ənd ˈtʌnələr / (AmE), / ˌbrɪdʒ ənd ˈtʌnələ / (BrE)
a person who commutes between suburban and urban areas, typically crossing bridges and tunnels to reach the city
person commuting via bridges/tunnels

Examples

Her colleagues often tease her for being a bridge-and-tunneler because of her long commute.
The city has many bridge-and-tunnelers who travel in from New Jersey every day.
Being a bridge-and-tunneler requires patience, especially during rush hour traffic.

Synset Examples

The bridge-and-tunneler lifestyle has its perks.
Being a bridge-and-tunneler can be exhausting.
As a bridge-and-tunneler, she commutes every day.

Etymology

Formed from 'bridge' and 'tunnel', referencing the major infrastructure used by such commuters.
