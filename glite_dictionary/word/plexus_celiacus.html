plexus celiacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs siˈlaɪəkəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs sɛlˈiːəkəs / (BrE)
a large network of sympathetic nerves located in the abdomen behind the stomach, also known as the celiac plexus or solar plexus
nerve network in abdomen (celiac plexus)
medical

Examples

The plexus celiacus plays an important role in relaying signals to the abdominal organs.
During the operation, the surgeon identified the plexus celiacus behind the stomach.
Chronic pain in the abdomen can sometimes originate from the plexus celiacus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar plexus - another name for the celiac plexus, a network of nerves in the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (network, braid) and 'celiacus' (relating to the abdomen).
