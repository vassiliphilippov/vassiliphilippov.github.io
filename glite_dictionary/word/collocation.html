collocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a habitual combination of words that commonly occur together in a language
habitual word combination

Examples

The collocation 'make a decision' is more natural than 'do a decision' in English.
Strong collocations are important to learn for more fluent speech and writing.
In linguistics, a collocation refers to words that frequently appear together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - a small group of words standing together as a conceptual unit

Etymology

From Latin 'collocatio' (placing together), from 'collocare' (to place together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of placing things together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast
positioning things together

Examples

The collocation of the two sculptures highlighted their differences.
Good lighting depends on the careful collocation of contrasting colors in a room.
The artist is known for the bold collocation of shapes in her work.

Synset Examples

it is the result of the juxtaposition of contrasting colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juxtaposition - the fact of placing things next to each other, especially for contrast

Etymology

From Latin 'collocatio' (a placing together), from 'collocare' (to place together).
