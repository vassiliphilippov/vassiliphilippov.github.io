armilla

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmɪlə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪlə / (BrE)
An ancient astronomical instrument made of metal hoops representing circles of the celestial sphere, used by early astronomers to determine the positions of stars.
ancient astronomical instrument of hoops

Examples

The ancient astronomer studied the stars carefully with his armilla.
Museums often display an armilla to illustrate early astronomical practices.
Using an armilla, scholars were able to map out constellations centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armillary sphere - a model of objects in the sky consisting of a series of rings

Etymology

From Latin 'armilla' (bracelet), referring to the ring-like structure.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɪlə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪlə / (BrE)
An ornamental bracelet or armlet worn around the wrist or arm, especially in antiquity.
ancient ornamental bracelet

Examples

The museum's collection features a golden armilla from the Roman Empire.
Archaeologists discovered a bronze armilla during the excavation of the burial site.
In ancient times, an armilla signified wealth and social status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracelet - an ornamental band or chain worn around the wrist
  • Synonym: armlet - an ornamental band worn around the upper arm

Etymology

From Latin 'armilla' (bracelet, little arm), diminutive of 'armus' (arm).
