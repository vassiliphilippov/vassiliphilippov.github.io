Esau

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːsɔʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːsɔː / (BrE)
(In the Old Testament) the eldest son of Isaac, who sold his birthright to his brother Jacob.
eldest son of Isaac in the Old Testament

Examples

In the biblical story, Esau traded his birthright for a meal.
Many consider the story of Esau and Jacob an important lesson about patience.
Esau is often depicted as a skilled hunter in religious texts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jacob - the younger twin brother of Esau, recipient of the birthright, in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Esav', meaning 'hairy' or 'rough'.
