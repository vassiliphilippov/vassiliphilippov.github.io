throatwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊtˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊtˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A European bellflower with blue-purple to lilac flowers, formerly used to treat sore throats.
European bellflower used medicinally

Examples

The herbalist recommended throatwort as a remedy for sore throats.
In the garden, the delicate blue flowers of throatwort stood out among the greenery.
Historically, throatwort was often used to make soothing teas.

Etymology

From 'throat' + 'wort' (an old word for plant or herb).
