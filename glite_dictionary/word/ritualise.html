to ritualise

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪtʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an activity or behavior) a ritual, often by giving it a fixed, formal, or symbolic pattern
make into a ritual
british

Examples

Many societies ritualise the coming-of-age process with elaborate ceremonies.
She tends to ritualise her morning routine, turning it into a calming practice.
The annual dinner has been ritualised over the years, complete with speeches and toasts.

Synset Examples

The growing up of children has become ritualized in many cultures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ritualize - make or evolve into a ritual (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'ritual' + '-ise', influenced by Latin 'ritus' (rite).
