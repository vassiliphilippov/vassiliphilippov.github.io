Eburophyton austinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːbjʊˈrɒfaɪtɒn ɔːˈstiːneɪ / (AmE), / ˌiːbjʊˈrəʊfaɪtən ɔːˈstiːniː / (BrE)
A species of waxy, nearly leafless plant with clustered stems and racemes of white flowers, native to northwestern United States and northern California, extending east to Idaho.
species of white, nearly leafless plant native to NW US

Examples

The rare Eburophyton austinae thrives in the shaded forests of northern California.
Researchers documented a new population of Eburophyton austinae during their field study.
Unlike many plants, Eburophyton austinae lacks green leaves and relies on fungi for nutrients.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'eburos' (ivory) and 'phyton' (plant); species name honors someone named Austin.
