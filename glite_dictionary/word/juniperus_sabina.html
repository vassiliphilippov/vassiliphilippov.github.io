Juniperus sabina

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs səˈbiːnə / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs səˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A species of low, spreading or procumbent juniper native to central and southern Europe and western and central Asia, commonly known as savin juniper.
a species of spreading juniper shrub

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare specimen of Juniperus sabina in its alpine section.
Many landscapers use Juniperus sabina as a groundcover for rocky slopes.
The pungent aroma of Juniperus sabina makes it easily recognizable among other shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - any shrub or small tree of the genus Juniperus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Juniperus' (juniper) and 'sabina' (of the Sabines, an ancient Italian people).
