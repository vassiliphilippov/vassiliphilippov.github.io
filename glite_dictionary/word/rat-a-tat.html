rat-a-tat

1. NOUN

/ ˌrætəˈtæt / (AmE), / ˌratəˈtat / (BrE)
a rapid series of short, sharp taps or sounds, such as those made by knocking on a door or by drumbeats
series of short, sharp taps or sounds

Examples

The rat-a-tat of the drummer echoed through the hall during the concert.
There was a persistent rat-a-tat at the door, demanding someone's attention.
She mimicked the rat-a-tat of a machine gun with her fingers on the table.

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound of rapid tapping or drumming.
