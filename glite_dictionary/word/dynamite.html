dynamite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.nəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.nə.maɪt / (BrE)
An explosive material made by absorbing nitroglycerin into a porous substance such as wood pulp, typically containing a nitrate to make it more stable and safer to handle.
powerful nitrate-based explosive

Examples

The miners used dynamite to blast through the rock.
Careless handling of dynamite can be extremely dangerous.
Alfred Nobel invented dynamite in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - a substance or device that can explode

Etymology

Coined by Alfred Nobel from Greek *dynamis* (power).

2. VERB

/ ˈdaɪ.nəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.nə.maɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or blow up something using dynamite
blow up with dynamite

Examples

The old building was dynamited to make way for a new mall.
Engineers will dynamite sections of the mountain to construct the tunnel.
Protesters feared the government would dynamite the historic site.

Synset Examples

The rock was dynamited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or cause to burst violently and noisily

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power), via French dynamite (the explosive invented by Alfred Nobel).
