prodigal

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑdɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈprɒdɪɡəl / (BrE)
a person who spends money or resources freely and recklessly; a wastefully extravagant person
wastefully extravagant person

Examples

The young prodigal squandered his inheritance on parties and luxury goods.
After years abroad, the prodigal returned home seeking forgiveness from his family.
Many cautionary tales warn against living like a prodigal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
  • Antonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'prodigalus' (wasteful), from 'prodigo' (to drive forth, lavish).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.dɪ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈprɒd.ɪ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
spending money or resources freely and recklessly; wastefully extravagant.
recklessly wasteful or extravagant

Examples

Despite winning the lottery, he became prodigal and spent his fortune within a year.
Her prodigal shopping habits left her in severe debt by the end of the year.
The prodigal use of water during the drought drew criticism from environmentalists.

Synset Examples

prodigal in their expenditures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagant - spending much more than necessary; lacking restraint
  • Antonym: frugal - sparing or economical, not wasteful

Etymology

From Latin 'prodigalis' (wasteful), from 'prodigus' (lavish).
