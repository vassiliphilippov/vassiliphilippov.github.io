Nesokia

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈsoʊkiə / (AmE), / nɛˈsəʊkiə / (BrE)
A genus of large burrowing rats commonly known as bandicoot rats, found mainly in southern Asia and Egypt.
a genus of bandicoot rats

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Nesokia in the wetlands of southern Asia.
The burrows made by Nesokia can cause damage to irrigation systems in agricultural areas.
Unlike other rodents, the Nesokia thrives in both rural and urban settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandicoot rat - a large, burrowing rat found in Asia and Egypt

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from the combination of the genus name 'Nesokia'.

2. NOUN

/ nɛˈsoʊkiə / (AmE), / nɛˈsəʊkiə / (BrE)
a genus of rodents commonly known as bandicoot rats, native to parts of Asia.
a genus of bandicoot rats

Examples

The Nesokia genus includes species found in agricultural areas of South Asia.
Research on Nesokia provides insight into controlling rodent pests in rice fields.
The burrowing habits of Nesokia make them a challenge for farmers.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek nēsos (island) and -okia (from other rodent genus names).
