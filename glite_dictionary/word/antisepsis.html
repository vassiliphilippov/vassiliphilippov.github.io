antisepsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsep.sɪs / (BrE)
The process of eliminating or preventing pathogenic microorganisms from non-living objects to prevent infection.
prevention of infection by eliminating microbes
medical

Examples

Strict antisepsis is essential during surgical operations to prevent infections.
The doctor explained the importance of antisepsis before handling medical equipment.
Advancements in antisepsis have greatly reduced the risk of hospital-acquired infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterilization - the process of making something completely free of all living microorganisms
  • Synonym: disinfection - removal of harmful microorganisms, though not necessarily all organisms

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + sēpsis (putrefaction).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈsɛp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsɛp.sɪs / (BrE)
the practice of preventing infection by inhibiting the growth and multiplication of microorganisms, especially on living tissue
the prevention of infection by inhibiting microorganisms
medical

Examples

Strict antisepsis measures are essential during surgical procedures to reduce the risk of infection.
The concept of antisepsis revolutionized medicine in the nineteenth century.
Proper handwashing is a simple but effective form of antisepsis in healthcare settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning to destroy most microorganisms except spores

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) and sēpsis (putrefaction).
