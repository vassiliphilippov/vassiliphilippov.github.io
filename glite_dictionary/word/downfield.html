downfield

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnfiːld / (BrE)
in the direction of the defending team's end of the playing field
toward opponent's end of field (sports)

Examples

The quarterback threw the ball downfield to his wide receiver.
After intercepting the pass, she sprinted downfield for a touchdown.
The coach instructed the players to move downfield quickly after the kickoff.

Synset Examples

he caught the ball and ran downfield 15 yards

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upfield - in the direction away from the opponent's end of the field

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌfiːld / (BrE)
(in sports) situated or moving toward or closer to the end zone or goal area of the defending team's side of the field.
toward defending team's end of playing field (sports)

Examples

The quarterback threw a downfield pass to the wide receiver.
Several downfield blockers helped clear a path for the running back.
Defenders kept their eyes on downfield threats throughout the game.

Synset Examples

he threw to a downfield receiver

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'field' in sports context, referring to movement or position toward the opponent's end.
