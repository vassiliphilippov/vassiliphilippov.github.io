Sarcocephalus latifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑrkoʊˈsɛfələs ˌlætəˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəʊˈsɛfələs ˌlætɪˈfəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
a stout, spreading or semi-climbing tropical shrub found in Africa, known for its round, brownish-red, warty fruit.
African tropical shrub with warty fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Sarcocephalus latifolius is often collected for traditional medicine in several African countries.
Botanists recently identified a dense thicket of Sarcocephalus latifolius growing along the riverbank.
Due to its hardy nature, Sarcocephalus latifolius is commonly found in both forested and open habitats.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'kephalē' (head), and Latin 'latifolius' (broad-leaved).
