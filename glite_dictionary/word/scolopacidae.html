Scolopacidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌskoʊləˈpæsəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌskɒləˈpæsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wading birds that includes sandpipers, woodcocks, snipes, tattlers, curlews, godwits, and dowitchers.
family of wading birds (sandpipers etc.)

Examples

The Scolopacidae family comprises many species of slender-billed waders.
Many members of the Scolopacidae migrate long distances between breeding and wintering grounds.
She wrote her dissertation on the feeding behaviors of birds in the Scolopacidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a wading bird of the family Scolopacidae, often found near water

Etymology

From modern Latin Scolopacidae, based on the genus Scolopax (woodcock) + -idae (family suffix).
