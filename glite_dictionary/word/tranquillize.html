to tranquillize

1. VERB

/ træŋˈkwɪˌlaɪz / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something calm or quiet, especially by the use of drugs
make calm or quiet, often with drugs

Examples

The veterinarian decided to tranquillize the injured wolf before treating its wounds.
She tried to tranquillize her nerves with deep breathing exercises before the exam.
The zoo staff tranquillized the escaped tiger to transport it safely back to its enclosure.

Synset Examples

quiet the dragons of worry and fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - make someone or something peaceful or less agitated
  • Antonym: to agitate - to make someone anxious or disturbed

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, quiet) + English verb-forming suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtræŋkwɪˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtræŋkwɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal calm or quiet, especially by giving them a drug; to sedate
to make calm or sedate with drugs

Examples

Before the surgery, the vet decided to tranquillize the animal to prevent any distress.
The doctor had to tranquillize the patient after he suffered a severe panic attack.
Wild animals are often tranquillized before being transported to new habitats.

Synset Examples

The patient must be sedated before the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedate - to calm or quiet, especially with a drug
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to make more active or alert; opposite of calming

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, quiet) + English suffix '-ize'.
