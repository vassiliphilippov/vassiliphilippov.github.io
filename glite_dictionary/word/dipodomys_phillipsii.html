Dipodomys phillipsii

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪpəˈdoʊmɪs fɪˈlɪpsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪpəˈdəʊmɪs fɪˈlɪpsɪaɪ / (BrE)
A species of jumping rodent, known as Phillips's kangaroo rat, native to desert regions of North America and Mexico; one of the largest members of the family Heteromyidae.
Phillips's kangaroo rat species

Examples

The Dipodomys phillipsii thrives in arid regions where it digs extensive burrows.
Researchers studied the diet of Dipodomys phillipsii to understand its adaptation to desert life.
Unlike some other rodents, Dipodomys phillipsii can survive with very little water intake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - Any of several jumping rodents of the genus Dipodomys

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name ‘Dipodomys’ (from Greek ‘dipo-’ two-footed + ‘-domys’ mouse) and species epithet honoring Phillips.
