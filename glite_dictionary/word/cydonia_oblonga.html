Cydonia oblonga

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdoʊniə ɒˈblɒŋɡə / (AmE), / sɪˈdəʊniə ɒˈblɒŋɡə / (BrE)
a small Asian tree with pinkish flowers and pear-shaped fruit, known as the quince; widely cultivated for its edible fruit
species of tree; the quince

Examples

Cydonia oblonga is prized for both its fragrant blossoms and its flavorful fruit.
Farmers have grown Cydonia oblonga in Mediterranean regions for centuries.
Jellies and preserves made from Cydonia oblonga are popular in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quince - the edible fruit or the tree Cydonia oblonga

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Cydonia (an ancient city in Crete associated with quinces) and Latin oblonga (oblong).
