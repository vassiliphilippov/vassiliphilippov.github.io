Sagittaria

1. NOUN

/ sædʒɪˈtɛriə / (AmE), / sædʒɪˈtɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic herbs found in temperate and tropical regions, characterized by arrow-shaped leaves and white, stalked flowers.
genus of aquatic herbs with arrow-shaped leaves

Examples

The pond was filled with Sagittaria, their arrow-shaped leaves floating atop the water.
Botanists studied the growth of Sagittaria in various wetland environments.
She planted Sagittaria along the edge of her water garden for its distinctive foliage.

Etymology

From Latin 'sagittaria', meaning 'of arrows', referring to the arrow-shaped leaves.
