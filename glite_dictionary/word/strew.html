to strew

1. VERB

/ struː / (AmE), / struː / (BrE)
to cover or scatter something over a surface in a careless or irregular way; to be spread or dispersed over an area
scatter or spread over a surface

Examples

Debris strewed the highway after the storm, making travel dangerous.
The field was strewn with wildflowers of every color imaginable.
Children's toys were strewn across the living room floor.

Synset Examples

Dead bodies strewed the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw or spread things over an area in irregular fashion
  • Antonym: gather - to collect things together in one place

Etymology

From Old English *streowian* (to scatter, spread), related to German *streuen*.

2. VERB

/ struː / (AmE), / struː / (BrE)
to scatter or spread things untidily over a surface or area
scatter or spread over a surface

Examples

The children strew their clothes across the bedroom floor every morning.
Leaves had been strewn over the path by the strong wind.
She began strewing rose petals along the aisle for the wedding ceremony.

Synset Examples

strew toys all over the carpet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw things in various random directions
  • Antonym: gather - to bring things together into one place

Etymology

From Old English *streawian* (to scatter), related to Old Norse *strá* (straw).

Source: WordNet
