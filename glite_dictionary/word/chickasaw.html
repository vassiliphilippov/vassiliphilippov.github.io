Chickasaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.əˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.əˌsɔː / (BrE)
The Muskhogean language spoken by the Chickasaw people, indigenous to the southeastern United States.
the language of the Chickasaw people

Examples

Linguists have worked hard to document the Chickasaw language before it is lost.
She is studying Chickasaw to connect with her heritage.
There are only a few fluent speakers of Chickasaw left today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - a family of Native American languages of the southeastern United States

Etymology

From the name of the Chickasaw people, possibly from Choctaw *chikashsha* (rebel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.əˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.əsɔː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from northern Mississippi, belonging to the Muskhogean language family
member of a Native American people

Examples

Her grandfather is a proud Chickasaw who shares stories of their heritage.
The Chickasaw were known for their military prowess among Southeastern tribes.
Many Chickasaw now live in Oklahoma after being relocated in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Choctaw - a member of a closely related Native American people

Etymology

From Chickasaw (self-designation 'Chikasha')

Synset POS: n
