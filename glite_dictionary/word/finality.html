finality

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / faɪˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being final or settled; the condition of having reached an irreversible or definite conclusion
quality of being final or definite

Examples

The judge's decision carried an air of finality that ended the debate.
There was a sense of finality in her voice when she said goodbye.
The finality of death is difficult for many people to accept.

Synset Examples

the finality of death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being certain or definite
  • Antonym: indecisiveness - the quality of not being settled or final

Etymology

From Late Latin finalitas, from Latin finalis (final).
