basophil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell containing granules that are easily stained by basic dyes; involved in inflammatory and allergic responses
a white blood cell with granules stained by basic dyes
medical

Examples

The doctor noted an increased number of basophils in the patient’s blood test.
Basophils play a role in the body’s immune response to allergens and inflammation.
Staining revealed several basophils among the other white blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eosinophil - a type of white blood cell with granules stained by eosin
  • Synonym: neutrophil - a type of white blood cell with neutral staining granules

Etymology

From Greek 'basis' (base) + 'philos' (loving); referring to its affinity for basic dyes.
