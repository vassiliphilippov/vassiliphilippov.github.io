to substantiate

1. VERB

/ səbˈstænʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / səbˈstanʃieɪt / (BrE)
to provide evidence or proof to support or confirm something
provide evidence to support

Examples

The scientist was able to substantiate her theory with experimental results.
You need to substantiate your claim with more concrete facts.
His research failed to substantiate the initial findings.

Synset Examples

his story confirmed my doubts
The evidence supports the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to corroborate - to confirm or give support to a statement, theory, or finding
  • Antonym: to disprove - to show that something is false or incorrect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin substantiare (to substantiate), from Latin substantia (substance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00667804-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səbˈstænʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / səbˈstænʃieɪt / (BrE)
to give bodily form or concrete representation to something abstract
give bodily form to something abstract

Examples

The artist's sculpture substantiates his vision of hope and resilience.
Her performance substantiated the spirit of the entire generation for the audience.
Sometimes a powerful story can substantiate the fears and dreams we all share.

Synset Examples

He embodies all that is evil wrong with the system
The painting substantiates the feelings of the artist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embody - to give a tangible or visible form to an idea or quality

Etymology

From Medieval Latin substantiare, from Latin substantia (substance).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ səbˈstæn.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / səbˈstæn.ʃieɪt / (BrE)
to make something real or concrete; to give form or substance to something abstract
make real or concrete

Examples

The architect worked hard to substantiate his vision with detailed blueprints and models.
Without funding, it's difficult to substantiate our research proposals into real-world solutions.
Her dreams of starting a charity finally began to substantiate with the community's support.

Synset Examples

our ideas must be substantiated into actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to realize - to make real or bring into being
  • Synonym: to materialize - to become real or actual
  • Antonym: to imagine - to form a mental image of something not present

Etymology

From Medieval Latin substantiatus, from Latin substantia (substance).

4. VERB

/ səbˈstænʃieɪt / (AmE), / səbˈstanʃieɪt / (BrE)
to make something more solid, firm, or established; to strengthen or support
to strengthen or make more solid

Examples

Regular meetings help to substantiate cooperation between the two departments.
Additional investments are needed to substantiate the company's growth in this region.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make weaker
