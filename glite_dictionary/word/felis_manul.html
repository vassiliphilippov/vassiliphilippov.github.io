Felis manul

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːlɪs məˈnuːl / (AmE), / ˌfiːlɪs məˈnjuːl / (BrE)
a small wildcat native to the mountains of Siberia, Tibet, and Mongolia, also known as Pallas's cat
mountain wildcat of Siberia and Central Asia

Examples

The Felis manul is well adapted to the harsh climates of the Siberian steppes.
Conservationists are working to protect Felis manul habitats in Mongolia.
Unlike other wildcats, Felis manul has a distinctive, stocky appearance with dense fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pallas's cat - another common name for Felis manul

Etymology

From Latin genus and species name used in scientific classification; 'Felis' meaning cat, 'manul' derived from the Mongolian name for the animal.
