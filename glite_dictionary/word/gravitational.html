gravitational

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡrævəˈteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the force of gravity
relating to gravity or gravitation

Examples

Physicists study the gravitational attraction between objects to understand the laws of motion.
The Earth's gravitational field keeps the Moon in orbit around our planet.
These satellites take measurements of tiny gravitational changes across the globe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitational pull - the force of gravity exerted by a body

Etymology

From gravitation + -al, modeled on 'relational' adjectives.
