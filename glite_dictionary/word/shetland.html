Shetland

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛt.lənd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛt.lənd / (BrE)
A small breed of sheepdog, resembling a collie, originally developed in the Shetland Islands of Scotland.
breed of small sheepdog from Shetland Islands

Examples

The Shetland is known for its intelligence and agility in herding sheep.
They have owned several Shetlands over the years and compete in dog shows.
A Shetland makes a loyal family companion with its gentle and friendly nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shetland Sheepdog - another name for Shetland, the breed
  • Synonym: collie - a related breed of sheepdog, larger in size

Etymology

Named after the Shetland Islands, Scotland, where the breed originated.

2. PROPN

/ ˈʃɛtlənd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛtlənd / (BrE)
an archipelago of about 100 islands in the North Atlantic Ocean, situated to the north of mainland Scotland
group of islands north of Scotland

Examples

The Shetland Islands are renowned for their rugged landscapes and rich wildlife.
She hopes to visit Shetland next summer to experience the famous Up Helly Aa fire festival.
Many bird enthusiasts travel to Shetland to observe rare seabirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orkney - group of islands north of Scotland, south of Shetland
  • Synonym: Hebrides - islands off the west coast of mainland Scotland

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Hetland' (meaning 'land of the Hith', a reference to the archipelago's early inhabitants or geographical features).
