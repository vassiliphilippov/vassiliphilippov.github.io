organification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔr.ɡən.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔː.ɡən.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which organs are formed in living organisms, especially during development or by the incorporation of inorganic substances into organic molecules
formation of organs in living organisms
medical

Examples

The thyroid gland relies on organification to create essential hormones from iodine.
Defects in organification can lead to developmental abnormalities in embryos.
Scientists study organification to better understand organ development in mammals.

Etymology

From 'organ' + '-ification', patterned after Latin 'organum' (instrument, organ) and '-fication' (making into).
