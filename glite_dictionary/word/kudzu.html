kudzu

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊd.zuː / (AmE), / ˈkʊd.zuː / (BrE)
A fast-growing East Asian vine with hairy trifoliate leaves, purple flowers, and starchy tuberous roots; it is often considered an invasive species in the southern United States.
fast-growing invasive vine

Examples

Kudzu quickly covered the abandoned farmhouse and fields within just a few summers.
Farmers in some states battle kudzu to keep it from overwhelming their crops.
The roots of kudzu can be processed to make a starchy powder used in Asian cuisine.

Etymology

From Japanese 'kuzu', the name of the plant.
