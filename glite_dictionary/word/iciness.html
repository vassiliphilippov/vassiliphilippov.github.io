iciness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.si.nəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.si.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unfriendly, cold, or lacking in affection or enthusiasm
lack of warmth or friendliness

Examples

His words were filled with iciness, making everyone in the room uncomfortable.
She was hurt by the sudden iciness in her friend's response.
Despite the iciness of his greeting, she tried to remain polite.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coldness - lack of warmth in manner or feeling
  • Antonym: warmth - friendliness and affection

Etymology

From English "icy" + "-ness"; "icy" from Old English "īs" (ice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.si.nəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.si.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very cold, like ice, especially due to a cold environment
extreme coldness; state of being icy

Examples

The iciness in the air made my breath visible as I walked outside.
She shivered at the iciness of the water when she first jumped in.
There was a distinct iciness in the breeze coming off the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coldness - the state of being cold in temperature
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality of being warm in temperature

Etymology

From 'icy' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being icy.
