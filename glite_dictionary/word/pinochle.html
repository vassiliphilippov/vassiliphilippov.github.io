pinochle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌnʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːnɒkəl / (BrE)
A card game for two or more players, played with a deck of forty-eight cards, in which players score points by forming combinations of cards and by winning tricks.
a card game using a special deck

Examples

My grandparents taught me how to play pinochle when I was a child.
Every Friday evening, the group gathers for a spirited game of pinochle.
He shuffled the unique pinochle deck carefully before dealing the cards.

Etymology

From German 'Pinochle', possibly from French 'binocle' (a type of card game), influenced by 'binocle' (eyeglasses).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌnʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːnɒkəl / (BrE)
A card game for two or more players, played with a forty-eight card deck consisting of two sets of each ace through nine, in which players score points by forming combinations of cards.
a card game for two or more players

Examples

We spent the evening playing pinochle with our neighbors.
He learned to play pinochle from his grandparents as a child.
A standard pinochle deck consists of forty-eight cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bezique - a related card game played with a deck of sixty-four cards

Etymology

From German 'Pinochle', original form 'Binochle', of uncertain origin, possibly from French 'binocle' (pair of eyeglasses, referring to two decks).
