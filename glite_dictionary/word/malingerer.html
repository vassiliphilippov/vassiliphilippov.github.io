malingerer

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪŋɡərər / (AmE), / məˈlɪŋɡərə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be ill or incapable in order to avoid work or duty
person who feigns illness to avoid duty

Examples

The manager suspected he was a malingerer after his frequent, unexplained absences.
During the war, malingerers were often punished for avoiding their duties.
No one wanted to be labeled a malingerer by their colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to malinger - to pretend to be ill to avoid work or duty

Etymology

From French *malingre* (weak, sickly), related to 'malinger'.
