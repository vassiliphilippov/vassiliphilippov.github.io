berceuse

1. NOUN

/ bɛrˈsɜz / (AmE), / bɛəˈsɜːz / (BrE)
A gentle song or piece of music composed to lull a child to sleep; a lullaby.
lullaby; soothing song for children

Examples

The pianist played a berceuse that gently soothed the restless infant.
Chopin's berceuse is celebrated for its delicate and comforting melody.
She hummed a soft berceuse to help her baby fall asleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lullaby - a soothing song used to lull children to sleep

Etymology

French, from 'bercer' (to rock), ultimately from Latin 'berciare'.
