Passerina cyanea

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæsəˈraɪnə saɪˈɒniə / (AmE), / ˌpasəˈriːnə saɪˈeɪnɪə / (BrE)
A small North American songbird, the indigo bunting, noted for the deep blue coloring of the breeding male.
species of small blue North American bunting

Examples

Birdwatchers often hope to catch a glimpse of the striking Passerina cyanea during spring migration.
The bright blue plumage of the male Passerina cyanea makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Passerina cyanea across different parts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo bunting - common name for Passerina cyanea, a small blue North American bunting

Etymology

From Latin 'Passerina' (a genus of buntings) and 'cyanea' meaning 'dark blue'.
