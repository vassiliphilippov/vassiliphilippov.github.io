cost

1. VERB

/ kɑst / (AmE), / kɒst / (BrE)
(of an item or service) to have a specified price; to require payment of a certain amount of money.
to have a specified price

Examples

The new laptop cost me nearly a month's salary.
How much does this painting cost?
Tickets for the concert cost $50 each.

Synset Examples

These shoes cost $100

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - to ask a particular amount as a price for goods or services
  • Synonym: sell for - to be offered for a certain price

Etymology

From Old French *coster*, from Latin *constare* (to stand firm, to cost).
