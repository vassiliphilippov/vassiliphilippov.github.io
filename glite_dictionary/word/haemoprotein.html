haemoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˈproʊtiːn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
A conjugated protein that contains a compound of iron and porphyrin as its prosthetic group, such as hemoglobin or myoglobin.
iron-porphyrin protein (e.g., hemoglobin)
medical british

Examples

Hemoglobin is a well-known haemoprotein responsible for oxygen transport in the blood.
Scientists study the structure of haemoproteins to better understand cellular respiration.
Certain enzymes that act as haemoproteins play a key role in metabolic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoprotein - a conjugated protein with an iron-porphyrin group (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + protein, originally formed in scientific contexts.
