scraping

1. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
a harsh, grating noise made by scraping one object against another
a harsh noise made by scraping

Examples

The constant scraping of chairs on the floor made it hard to concentrate.
She winced at the scraping of metal against the chalkboard.
The eerie scraping echoed through the empty corridor.

Synset Examples

the scrape of violin bows distracted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - a harsh sound produced by scraping

2. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
a small fragment or amount that has been removed from a surface by scraping, often collected for analysis or disposal
fragment or residue removed by scraping

Examples

The lab technician placed the scrapings from the sample slide into a test tube.
After cleaning the pan, she threw out the scrapings left from baking.
For the study, doctors examined skin scrapings for signs of infection.

Synset Examples

they collected blood scrapings for analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaving - a thin slice or small piece removed from a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'scrape' + -ing, originally meaning the action or result of scraping.

/ ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (BrE)

archaic

Examples
