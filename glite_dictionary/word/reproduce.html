to reproduce

1. VERB

/ ˌriːprəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
(of an animal, plant, or microorganism) to produce offspring or new individuals of the same kind.
produce offspring; procreate

Examples

Many fish can reproduce several times throughout their lives.
Under the right conditions, yeast cells reproduce rapidly.
Endangered animals may struggle to reproduce in captivity.

Synset Examples

The Bible tells people to procreate
These bacteria reproduce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procreate - to produce offspring, especially by sexual reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'reproducere', from 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to lead forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00054856-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːprəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to make a copy or duplicate of something; to produce something that closely resembles the original
make a copy or duplicate

Examples

It is illegal to reproduce copyrighted music without permission.
The artist managed to reproduce the painting with remarkable accuracy.
This printer can reproduce images in high resolution.

Synset Examples

reproduce the painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - to make an identical or similar version of something
  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something

Etymology

From Latin 'reproducere', from 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to bring forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01740703-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.prəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌriː.prəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to create again or make a copy of a sound, image, idea, mood, or atmosphere
to recreate or copy something such as a sound or image

Examples

This state-of-the-art speaker can reproduce the subtle notes of a live concert.
It is difficult to reproduce the warm atmosphere of her old studio in a new space.
The artist managed to reproduce the intricate patterns of the tapestry in his drawing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy of; to duplicate
  • Synonym: copy - to make a thing that is similar or identical to another

Etymology

From Latin 'reproducere', from 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to bring forth).

4. VERB

/ ˌriːprəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to repeat or recite something exactly as learned or memorized
repeat something exactly from memory

Examples

During the test, he was able to reproduce entire pages of text from memory.
The student learned the speech so well that she could reproduce it word for word.
You must be able to reproduce these formulas on the exam without looking at your notes.

Synset Examples

For the exam, you must be able to regurgitate the information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to repeat information mechanically, especially from memory
  • Synonym: to recite - to repeat aloud from memory

Etymology

From Latin *reproducere* (to lead or bring forth again), from *re-* (again) + *producere* (to bring forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00961210-v
Synset POS: v
