Schizaea pusilla

1. NOUN

/ skɪˈziː.i.ə pjuːˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / skɪˈziː.ɪ.ə pjuːˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
A rare, small fern native to northeastern North America, known for its slender, spiraling fronds and forming dense tufts.
rare small North American fern

Examples

The coastal bog was home to the rare Schizaea pusilla, hidden among the mosses.
Botanists were excited to discover a clump of Schizaea pusilla during their field survey.
Efforts are underway to protect habitats where Schizaea pusilla still survives in the wild.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Schizaea and Latin pusilla (small, tiny).
