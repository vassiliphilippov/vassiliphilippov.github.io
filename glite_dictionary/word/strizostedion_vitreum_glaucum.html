Strizostedion vitreum glaucum

1. NOUN

/ straɪˌzɒstəˈdiːən vɪˈtriːəm ˈɡlɔːkəm / (AmE), / strɪˌzɒstəˈdiːən vɪˈtriːəm ˈɡlɔːkəm / (BrE)
a variety of the walleye fish native to the Great Lakes region in North America
a walleye variety from the Great Lakes

Examples

The Strizostedion vitreum glaucum is prized by anglers throughout the Great Lakes for its fighting ability.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Strizostedion vitreum glaucum in Lake Erie.
Populations of Strizostedion vitreum glaucum have been monitored by fishery biologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walleye - a freshwater fish found in North America

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Strizostedion' is a genus name, 'vitreum' means 'glassy', and 'glaucum' means 'bluish-green'.
