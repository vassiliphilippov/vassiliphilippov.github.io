hue

1. VERB

/ hjuː / (AmE), / hjuː / (BrE)
(of an object or surface) to take on a particular color or become colored.
to become colored

Examples

The autumn leaves hued into shades of gold and red as the season changed.
The sky hued with purple at dusk, signaling the end of the day.
Her cheeks hued a delicate pink after the brisk walk.

Synset Examples

In highlights it hued to a dull silver-grey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - to give something a particular color

2. NOUN

/ hjuː / (AmE), / hjuː / (BrE)
the quality of a color as determined by its dominant wavelength; a particular shade or tint of a color
a particular shade or tint of a color

Examples

The walls were painted in a soft blue hue that created a calming atmosphere.
Artists often experiment with different hues to evoke certain emotions in their work.
The sunset bathed the sky in a rich golden hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - a slight variation or degree of a color
  • Synonym: tint - a slight or pale coloration
  • Synonym: tone - a quality of color, especially as affected by lightness or darkness

Etymology

From Old English *hīw* (appearance, form, color), related to Old Norse *hýr* (skin, hue).
