to summerize

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməˌaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a vehicle, building, etc.) ready or suitable for use during the summer months
prepare for summer use

Examples

It's important to summerize your car before the hot weather arrives.
We always summerize our cottage by opening windows and cleaning out the fireplace.
The hotel staff worked hard to summerize the building after a long winter.

Synset Examples

summerize your car
summerize a house

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to winterize - make ready for winter use

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'winterize', from 'summer' + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməraɪz / (BrE)
to prepare (something such as a car or house) for use or conditions in summer
make ready for summer

Examples

Before the hot weather arrived, we decided to summerize our house by installing window screens.
It's important to summerize your car by checking the air conditioning and fluid levels.
The campground staff will summerize the cabins each year by airing them out and making repairs.

Synset Examples

summerize your car
summerize a house

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to winterize - to prepare something for use or protection in winter

Etymology

Formed from 'summer' + '-ize', patterned after 'winterize'.
