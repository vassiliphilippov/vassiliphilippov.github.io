reshuffling

1. NOUN

/ riˈʃʌflɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈʃʌflɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shuffling or rearranging something again, especially a deck of cards or an organizational structure
the act of shuffling again

Examples

After the initial deal, the dealer called for a reshuffling of the cards.
The company is planning a reshuffling of its leadership team next month.
A complete reshuffling of the cabinet was announced after the election results.

Synset Examples

the gambler demanded a reshuffle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reshuffle - to shuffle or rearrange again

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'shuffling', from 'shuffle'.
