blasphemy

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæsfəmi / (AmE), / ˈblasfəmi / (BrE)
the act or offense of showing contempt or lack of reverence for God or something considered sacred
showing contempt for the sacred

Examples

In medieval times, people could be executed for committing blasphemy against the church.
The artist was accused of blasphemy after displaying controversial works about religious figures.
Many religions consider mocking their sacred texts a form of blasphemy.

Synset Examples

desecration of the Holy Sabbath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrilege - violation or profanation of something held sacred
  • Antonym: reverence - deep respect for someone or something, especially the sacred

Etymology

From Greek *blasphēmia* (slander, profane speech), via Late Latin *blasphemia*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblæs.fə.mi / (AmE), / ˈblɑːs.fə.mi / (BrE)
Speech or actions that show disrespect or lack of reverence for God or something considered sacred.
disrespectful speech or acts toward the sacred

Examples

The novel was banned for its alleged blasphemy against religious beliefs.
He was accused of blasphemy after making jokes about sacred texts.
In some countries, blasphemy is still considered a serious crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanity - language that shows disrespect for sacred things
  • Antonym: reverence - deep respect for someone or something sacred

Etymology

From Greek blasphēmia (evil-speaking, profanation), via Late Latin blasphemia.

Synset POS: n
