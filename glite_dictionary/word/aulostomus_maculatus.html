Aulostomus maculatus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈlɒstəʊməs ˌmækjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈlɒstəməs ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
a species of tropical Atlantic fish, known as the trumpetfish, with a long snout that often swims vertically with its head down
tropical Atlantic trumpetfish species

Examples

The Aulostomus maculatus is easily recognized by its elongated, tubular snout and distinctive swimming style.
Marine biologists often spot the Aulostomus maculatus hovering vertically among coral reefs.
Divers sometimes observe Aulostomus maculatus blending in with sea whips as it searches for prey.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Aulostomus' (from Greek 'aulos' meaning pipe and 'stoma' meaning mouth), with species epithet 'maculatus' meaning spotted in Latin.
