outcome

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkʌm / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.kʌm / (BrE)
the result or effect of an action, situation, or event
result or effect

Examples

The outcome of the experiment was not what the scientists expected.
No one could have predicted the outcome of the election.
Her hard work had a positive outcome on her grades.

Synset Examples

he listened for the results on the radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence of an action
  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From out + come, meaning 'what comes out (of something)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkʌm / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkʌm / (BrE)
the result or effect of an action, situation, or event
result or effect of an action

Examples

The outcome of the experiment was not what the scientists expected.
We are all waiting anxiously to find out the outcome of the election.
His recovery after surgery was a positive outcome that surprised the doctors.

Synset Examples

he acted very wise after the event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence of an action
  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Antonym: cause - something that brings about an effect or result

Etymology

From out + come, first recorded in English in the 18th century.
