red-letter

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛd ˌlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌlɛtə / (BrE)
(of a day or occasion) memorable, especially as being particularly special, happy, or important.
memorable or important (of an occasion)

Examples

Their anniversary was always a red-letter day in their family calendar.
Graduation was a true red-letter occasion for all the students involved.
Our trip to Italy will always be a red-letter memory for me.

Synset Examples

on red-letter nights a large gathering greeted the celebrity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorable - worth remembering or easily remembered, especially because of being special or unusual
  • Antonym: ordinary - not special or different in any way

Etymology

From the tradition of marking special days in red ink on calendars.
