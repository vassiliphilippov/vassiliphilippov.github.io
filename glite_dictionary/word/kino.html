kino

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A reddish gum obtained from various tropical trees, used as an astringent and in tanning.
reddish gum from tropical trees, used in tanning

Examples

The bark of certain eucalyptus trees exudes kino when cut.
Traditional medicine sometimes uses kino as a remedy for diarrhea.
The tanner applied kino to the leather to make it more durable.

Etymology

From Hindi 'kīno', also found in other Indian languages; adopted into English in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (such as Pterocarpus marsupium) that yields a reddish resin often used medicinally or in tanning.
tree yielding medicinal resin

Examples

The bark of the kino tree is a valuable source of medicinal resin.
Traditional healers sometimes prescribe kino for treating diarrhea and other ailments.
Tanners use kino extract for its astringent properties in leather processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - resinous substance obtained from trees

Etymology

From Hindi 'kino', ultimately from Sanskrit 'kīnakaḥ', referring to the resin.
