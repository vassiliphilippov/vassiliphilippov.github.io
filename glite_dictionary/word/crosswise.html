crosswise

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɔs.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs.waɪz / (BrE)
lying or placed across something, especially forming or resembling the horizontal part of a cross; arranged at right angles or transversely
lying or placed across; transverse

Examples

The beams were laid crosswise to provide extra support.
She cut the fabric crosswise to make strips for quilting.
He noticed a pattern of cracks running crosswise over the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transverse - lying or extending across something
  • Antonym: longitudinal - placed lengthwise rather than across

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'wise', formed on analogy with words like 'lengthwise'; 'cross' from Old English 'cros'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈkrɔsˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌwaɪz / (BrE)
lying or extending across the length or width of something; in a cross direction.
lying or extending across

Examples

A long beam was placed crosswise on top of the supports.
The tablecloth had stripes running crosswise rather than lengthwise.
He drew a line crosswise across the page to divide it in half.

Synset Examples

a crosswise street
the crosswise dimension

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length
  • Synonym: transverse - situated or extending across something; crosswise (technical/scientific)

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'wise', with 'wise' meaning 'in the manner or direction of'.
