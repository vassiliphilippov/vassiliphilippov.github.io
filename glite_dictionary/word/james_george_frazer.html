James George Frazer

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdʒɔrdʒ ˈfreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈfreɪzə / (BrE)
(1854–1941) An English social anthropologist known for his influential studies on primitive religion and magic, including his work 'The Golden Bough'.
English social anthropologist (1854–1941)

Examples

James George Frazer's comparative studies of mythology had a profound impact on twentieth-century anthropology.
Many scholars still reference James George Frazer when discussing the origins of religious rituals.
'The Golden Bough', written by James George Frazer, is regarded as a classic in the field of anthropology.
