Najadaceae

1. NOUN

/ nəˌdʒædəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / naˌdʒadəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A monotypic family of aquatic plants with narrow leaves and small flowers, typically found in freshwater habitats.
aquatic plant family with narrow leaves

Examples

The Najadaceae are commonly known as the water nymph family in aquatic botany.
Biologists have studied the distribution of Najadaceae in freshwater systems worldwide.
Members of the Najadaceae exhibit adaptations to submerged environments.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'Naias, Naiad-' (a water nymph), with botanical family suffix '-aceae'.

aquatic plant family (Najadaceae)

Examples

The Najadaceae includes aquatic plants once common in freshwater habitats.
