perspicacious

1. ADJ

/ pɜːrspɪˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / pəːspɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a ready insight into and understanding of things; acutely perceptive and wise.
having keen insight and understanding

Examples

Her perspicacious remarks quickly revealed the underlying issues in the debate.
He relied on his perspicacious mind to detect flaws in the plan.
The detective's perspicacious observations led to the timely capture of the suspect.

Synset Examples

much too perspicacious to be taken in by such a spurious argument
observant and thoughtful, he was given to asking sagacious questions
a source of valuable insights and sapient advice to educators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing clear understanding and perception
  • Synonym: sage - wise and judicious
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin perspicax, perspicacis (sharp-sighted) + -ious.

2. ADJ

/ pɚˌspɪˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / pɜːˌspɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having a ready insight into and understanding of things; able to notice and understand things that are not obvious.
having keen insight; discerning

Examples

Her perspicacious remarks helped the team see the underlying issues in the proposal.
The detective's perspicacious analysis led to the quick resolution of the case.
He was known as a perspicacious investor who could spot trends before anyone else.

Synset Examples

too clear-eyed not to see what problems would follow
chaos could be prevented only by clear-sighted leadership
much too perspicacious to be taken in by so spurious an argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Synonym: insightful - showing clear understanding of a situation
  • Antonym: obtuse - slow to understand; lacking quickness of perception

Etymology

From Latin 'perspicax' (sharp-sighted), from 'perspicere' (to look through).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00775362-s
Synset POS: s
