Phocoena sinus

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈsiːnə ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / fəˈsiːnə ˈsɪnəs / (BrE)
A small species of porpoise, commonly known as the vaquita, native to the Gulf of California and critically endangered.
species of porpoise, the vaquita

Examples

The critically endangered Phocoena sinus is found only in the northern part of the Gulf of California.
Conservation groups are working to protect the habitat of Phocoena sinus from illegal fishing practices.
Researchers have struggled to study the elusive Phocoena sinus due to its small population and secretive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaquita - Common name for Phocoena sinus, a small porpoise species

Etymology

From Latin 'phocoena' (porpoise) and 'sinus' (bay), referring to its habitat.
