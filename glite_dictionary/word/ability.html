ability

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the possession of the means or skill to do something; the quality of being able to perform a task or activity
capacity or skill to do something

Examples

Her ability to solve complex problems made her a valuable asset to the team.
Athletes train for years to improve their physical ability and performance.
He demonstrated remarkable ability as a pianist, even at a young age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capability - the power or ability to do something
  • Antonym: inability - lack of ability; not being able to do something

Etymology

From Latin habilitas (aptitude, skill), from habilis (able, apt).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the possession of the necessary skill, power, or qualities to do something or achieve something
possession of the qualities to do something

Examples

She demonstrated a remarkable ability to solve complex problems quickly.
Employers often look for candidates with the ability to work independently.
The team's ability to adapt to changing situations led to their success.

Synset Examples

danger heightened his powers of discrimination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capability - the power or ability to do something
  • Antonym: inability - lack of ability to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'habilitas' (aptitude, suitability), from 'habilis' (able).
