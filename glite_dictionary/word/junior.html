junior

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːniər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
A son who has the same first name as his father, typically distinguished as 'Junior' in his name.
son with same name as father

Examples

Martin Luther King Junior followed in his father's footsteps as a civil rights leader.
The invitation was addressed to William H. Thompson Junior to avoid confusion with his father.
Many families use the title Junior when a son is named after his father.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: senior - a father with the same first name as his son

Etymology

From Latin 'iunior', comparative of 'iuvenis' meaning 'young, younger'.
