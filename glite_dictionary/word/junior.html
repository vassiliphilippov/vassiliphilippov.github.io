Junior

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːniər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
used after a man’s name to indicate that he is the younger of two family members, especially a son named after his father
used after a younger man’s name (same as his father’s)

Examples

His name is Michael Williams Junior, just like his father.
The invitation was addressed to James Smith, Junior.
After John Junior graduated, his father congratulated him warmly.

Synset Examples

John Junior
John Smith, Jr.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Senior - used after a man’s name to distinguish him as the older of two with the same name

Etymology

From Latin *junior* (younger), comparative of *iuvenis* (young).
