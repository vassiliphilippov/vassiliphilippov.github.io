allocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or action of distributing resources or items according to a plan or system
distribution according to a plan

Examples

The committee discussed the allocation of funds for the new project.
Effective allocation of resources is crucial for organizational success.
There was controversy surrounding the allocation of relief supplies after the disaster.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apportionment - the action of dividing and distributing according to a plan
  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients

Etymology

From Latin allocatio, from allocare 'to allocate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an amount or share of something that is officially set aside for a particular purpose
a share set aside for a purpose

Examples

Each department received its allocation of funds for the new project.
The water allocation for the region was reduced due to drought.
There was some debate about the fair allocation of resources among the different teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allotment - a portion of something allocated to someone
  • Synonym: distribution - the act of giving out shares of something

Etymology

From Latin 'allocatio', from 'allocare' (to assign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13310490-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(computing) the process of assigning specific areas of a computer disk to particular data or instructions.
assignment of disk areas (computing)

Examples

Efficient allocation of disk space can improve file retrieval times.
The system uses dynamic allocation to manage memory and disk resources.
Incorrect allocation may lead to data fragmentation on the drive.

Etymology

From Latin *allocatio*, from *allocare* (to assign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00165400-n
Synset POS: n
