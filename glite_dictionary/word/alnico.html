Alnico

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.nɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈal.nɪ.kəʊ / (BrE)
a trademarked alloy composed chiefly of aluminum, nickel, and cobalt, used to make strong permanent magnets
a magnetic alloy of aluminum, nickel, and cobalt

Examples

Loudspeakers often use Alnico magnets for their strong magnetic field.
The engineer chose Alnico because it retains magnetism at high temperatures.
Vintage guitar pickups made with Alnico are highly sought after for their tone.

Etymology

Formed from the initial syllables of aluminum, nickel, and cobalt.
