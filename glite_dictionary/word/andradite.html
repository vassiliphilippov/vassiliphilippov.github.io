andradite

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrædaɪt / (AmE), / ænˈdrædaɪt / (BrE)
A variety of garnet mineral composed of calcium iron silicate, occurring in colors from yellow and green to brown and black, and used as a gemstone.
a type of garnet mineral gemstone

Examples

The brilliant green variety of andradite is known as demantoid.
Collectors prize andradite for its exceptional luster and rare colors.
Geologists discovered new deposits of andradite in the remote mountain range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

Named after Brazilian mineralogist José Bonifácio de Andrada e Silva (1763–1838).
