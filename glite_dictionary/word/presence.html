presence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛzəns / (BrE)
The state or fact of existing, occurring, or being present in a place or situation.
state of being present

Examples

The presence of mold in the old house concerned the inspector.
She felt a strong presence in the room, even though she was alone.
The test will detect the presence of harmful bacteria in the water.

Synset Examples

he tested for the presence of radon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absence - the state of being away or not present

Etymology

From Old French *presence*, from Latin *praesentia* (being at hand, present).
