feminism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛm.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.ɪ.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief in and advocacy of the social, political, and economic equality of women with men
belief in equal rights for women

Examples

She has been passionate about feminism since her college days.
Debates about feminism have shaped modern conversations on gender equality.
His essay explored the impact of feminism on contemporary society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misogyny - dislike of, contempt for, or ingrained prejudice against women

Etymology

From French féminisme, based on Latin femina (woman).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in and advocacy of equal rights and opportunities for women; the movement supporting women's equality with men in all spheres of society.
belief in and movement for women's equality

Examples

The rise of feminism in the 20th century challenged traditional gender roles.
She studied the history of feminism for her sociology degree.
Modern feminism continues to advocate for women's rights and social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gender equality - the state of equal access to resources and opportunities regardless of gender

Etymology

From French féminisme, from Latin fēmina (woman).
