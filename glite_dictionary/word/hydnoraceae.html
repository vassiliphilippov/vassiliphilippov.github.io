Hydnoraceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪdˌnɔːrəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / hɪdˌnɔːrəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of parasitic flowering plants found in Africa and Argentina, which attach to the roots of other plants to obtain nutrients
family of parasitic flowering plants

Examples

Hydnoraceae includes unusual plants that lack chlorophyll and survive by parasitizing other species' roots.
The rare flowers of Hydnoraceae are mostly hidden underground, emerging only during their brief blooming season.
Botanists study Hydnoraceae to understand how parasitic plants evolve and interact with their hosts.

Etymology

From the genus name Hydnora, from Greek hudnōr ('truffle') referring to the fungus-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ haɪdˌnɔːrəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / hɪdˌnɒrəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants native to Africa and Argentina, known for being root parasites on other plants
parasitic plant family of Africa and Argentina

Examples

The Hydnoraceae are unique among flowering plants for their lack of leaves and dependence on host roots.
Among the strangest plants in the desert ecosystem are those in the Hydnoraceae family.
Botanists have studied Hydnoraceae to understand how parasitic plants evolve specialized adaptations.

Etymology

From New Latin Hydnoraceae, from Hydnora (genus name) + -aceae (family suffix).
