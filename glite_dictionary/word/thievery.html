thievery

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːvəri / (AmE), / ˈθiːvəri / (BrE)
the act or practice of stealing; theft
the act of stealing; theft

Examples

Thievery has become a major concern for shop owners in the city center.
He was arrested for repeated acts of thievery in the neighborhood.
The museum installed cameras to deter thievery after several items went missing.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of taking someone else's property without permission
  • Synonym: burglary - the act of entering a building illegally to steal
  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property from a person or place by threat or force

Etymology

From Old English 'thīofrēow' (thievery, theft), based on 'thief'.
