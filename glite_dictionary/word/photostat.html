photostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊtəˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəˌstæt / (BrE)
a photocopy made by a Photostat machine, especially used before the widespread adoption of modern photocopiers
an early photocopy (Photostat machine)

Examples

He handed me a photostat of the original letter for my records.
The library keeps photostats of rare manuscripts for researchers.
I requested a photostat because the original document was too fragile to handle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopy - a paper copy of a document made with a photocopier

Etymology

From Photostat, a trademark for an early copying machine (early 20th century).

2. VERB

/ ˈfoʊtəˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊstæt / (BrE)
to make a photocopy using a Photostat machine, especially as was common before the age of digital copying
make a copy with a Photostat machine
archaic

Examples

The librarian offered to photostat the document for archival purposes.
He photostatted the contract so both parties had a copy.
Requests to photostat rare manuscripts are no longer common in modern libraries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopy - to make a copy of a document or image using a photocopier

Etymology

From Photostat, the trademarked name of an early photo-copying device (early 20th century, U.S.).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01740550-v
Synset POS: v
