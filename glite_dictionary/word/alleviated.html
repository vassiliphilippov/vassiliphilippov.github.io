alleviated

1. ADJ

/ əˈli.viˌeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əˈliː.vi.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of pain or sorrow) made less severe or easier to endure.
made less severe or easier to bear

Examples

The patient's symptoms were greatly alleviated after the new treatment began.
Her anxiety was alleviated by the reassuring words of her friend.
Once the noise stopped, the tension in the room was noticeably alleviated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relieved - no longer feeling pain, anxiety, or distress
  • Antonym: aggravated - made worse or more severe

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviare', meaning to lighten or relieve.
