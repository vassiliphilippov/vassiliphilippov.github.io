Anchusa officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkjuːsə əˌfɪsəˈnaɪlɪs / (AmE), / ænˈkjuːzə ɒˌfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
a perennial or biennial herb cultivated for its delicate, usually blue flowers, also known as common bugloss
herb with blue flowers, common bugloss

Examples

Anchusa officinalis is often grown in gardens for its bright blue, star-shaped flowers.
Herbalists have long valued Anchusa officinalis for its traditional medicinal uses.
The meadow was dotted with blooming Anchusa officinalis throughout the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin 'anchusa', a plant name, and 'officinalis', meaning 'used in medicine'.
