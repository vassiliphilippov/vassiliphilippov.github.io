stripped-down

1. ADJ

/ ˌstrɪptˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌstrɪptˈdaʊn / (BrE)
Having only the essential or minimal features; lacking unnecessary or extra elements.
reduced to essentials

Examples

The stripped-down user interface made the software easier for beginners.
They offered a stripped-down version of the show to save on production costs.
Her stripped-down approach to decorating creates a clean and modern look.

Synset Examples

a stripped new car
a stripped-down budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bare-bones - having only the most basic elements
