occasional

1. ADJ

/ əˈkeɪʒənəl / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒnəl / (BrE)
happening or appearing at irregular or infrequent intervals; not regular or constant.
happening at irregular intervals

Examples

She gets occasional headaches, but nothing serious.
He made occasional visits to his old hometown after moving away.
The company holds occasional meetings to discuss long-term strategy.

Synset Examples

episodic in his affections
occasional headaches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regular - happening at fixed intervals
  • Degree Higher: frequent - happening often
  • Degree Lower: rare - happening very seldom

Etymology

From medieval Latin occasionalis, from Latin occasio (occasion).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkeɪʒənəl / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒənl / (BrE)
happening or done irregularly or only from time to time; not frequent or regular
happening irregularly from time to time

Examples

She attends the club as an occasional visitor, not a regular member.
The company hires occasional workers during the busy holiday season.
We have occasional meetings to discuss the project's progress.

Synset Examples

casual employment
a casual correspondence with a former teacher
an occasional worker

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frequent - happening often or at short intervals
  • Synonym: sporadic - occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places; scattered or isolated

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'occasionalis', from Latin 'occasio' (opportunity, occasion).

Source: WordNet
