phase

1. NOUN

/ feɪz / (AmE), / feɪz / (BrE)
(astronomy) The particular appearance or stage in the cycle of illumination of a celestial body, especially one of the recurring shapes of the illuminated part of the Moon as seen from Earth.
astronomical stage of illumination

Examples

The full phase of the Moon is especially bright and visible all night.
Astronomers track each phase of Venus to study its orbit around the Sun.
During the waxing crescent phase, only a small sliver of the Moon is illuminated.

Synset Examples

the full phase of the moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunar phase - one of the recurring shapes of the Moon's illuminated portion

Etymology

From Greek phasis (appearance), from phainō (to show, to appear).
