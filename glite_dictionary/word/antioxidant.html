antioxidant

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈɑksɪdənt / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈɒksɪdənt / (BrE)
A substance that inhibits oxidation, especially one used to counteract the deterioration of stored food products or protect cells from damaging effects of free radicals.
substance preventing oxidation

Examples

Many fruits and vegetables are rich in antioxidants that help protect the body from damage.
The cream contains a powerful antioxidant to slow the aging process of the skin.
Vitamin E is a well-known antioxidant used in dietary supplements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oxidant - a substance that promotes oxidation
  • Degree Higher: free radical - an atom or molecule with an unpaired electron, often causing oxidation

Etymology

From anti- (against) + oxidant (an agent promoting oxidation).
