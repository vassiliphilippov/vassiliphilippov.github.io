hepatocarcinoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpətoʊˌkɑrsəˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəʊˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the liver arising from liver cells; liver cancer.
malignant liver tumor
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hepatocarcinoma after a series of medical tests.
Recent studies have improved the prognosis for people suffering from hepatocarcinoma.
Early detection of hepatocarcinoma can greatly increase the chances of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver cancer - general term for cancer of the liver

Etymology

From Greek 'hēpar' (liver) + 'carcinoma' (cancerous tumor).
