modeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of creating a representation of something, often on a smaller scale or as a prototype
act of creating a representation

Examples

The architect used modeling to explore different designs for the building.
Scientists rely on climate modeling to predict future environmental changes.
His hobby involves modeling airplanes with remarkable attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simulation - the imitation of a situation or process
  • Synonym: representation - the depiction or portrayal of something

Etymology

From model + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or result of shaping a substance, such as clay or wax, into a sculpture by molding
sculpture made by molding

Examples

The artist's modeling in clay captured the subtle curves of the human body.
Many masterpieces in museums are the product of careful modeling in wax or bronze.
His love for modeling led him to create lifelike animal figures from soft clay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpture - three-dimensional art created by shaping materials

Etymology

From model + -ing. Influenced by French modeler and Italian modellare (to model).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03785154-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a preliminary sculpture in wax or clay made as the basis for a finished work of art
preliminary sculpture in wax or clay

Examples

The artist created a modeling in clay before casting the final statue in bronze.
Her modeling of the animal helped guide the sculptors during the finishing process.
Many sculptors consider modeling a crucial first step in developing complex sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a three-dimensional representation used as a pattern or prototype

Etymology

From 'model', from Latin 'modulus' (a small measure).

Source: WordNet
