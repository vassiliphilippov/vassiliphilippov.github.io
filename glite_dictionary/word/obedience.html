obedience

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiːdiəns / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːdiəns / (BrE)
behavior in which a person follows the wishes or rules of someone in authority, especially parents, in order to please them or show respect
following rules or wishes to please authority

Examples

Her obedience to her parents' wishes was evident in every decision she made.
The teacher praised the boy's obedience in always following the classroom rules.
They expected complete obedience from their children, especially regarding family traditions.

Synset Examples

their children were never very strong on obedience
he went to law school out of respect for his father's wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of obeying a command or request
  • Antonym: rebellion - refusal to obey authority or rules

Etymology

From Old French 'obedience', from Latin 'obedientia' (compliance), from 'obedire' (to obey).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbiːdiəns / (AmE), / əˈbiːdiəns / (BrE)
the quality or practice of being willing to do what you are told to do by someone in authority; compliance with orders or requests
willingness to obey; compliance

Examples

The child's obedience to her parents was noticed by everyone at the party.
Military training emphasizes strict obedience to superiors' commands.
Religious leaders often speak about the importance of obedience to moral principles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disobedience - refusal or failure to obey rules or someone in authority
  • Synonym: compliance - the act of obeying an order or rule

Etymology

From Old French 'obedience', from Latin 'oboedientia' (compliance, obedience).

3. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiːdiəns / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːdiəns / (BrE)
the practice of doing what someone in authority tells you to do, or the quality of being willing to obey
the act or quality of obeying

Examples

The teacher praised the students' obedience during the fire drill.
His obedience to his parents was admired by everyone in the community.
Religious orders often require vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disobedience - refusal or failure to obey rules or someone in authority
  • Synonym: compliance - the act of obeying an order or rule

Etymology

From Old French *obedience*, from Latin *obedientia* (compliance, obedience).
