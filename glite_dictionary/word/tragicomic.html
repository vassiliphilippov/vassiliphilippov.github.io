tragicomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtrædʒiˈkɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmɪk / (BrE)
Combining both tragic and comic elements, especially in a work of literature or art.
both tragic and comic

Examples

His latest play is a tragicomic exploration of family relationships.
The movie managed to strike a tragicomic balance, making audiences both laugh and cry.
She found herself in a tragicomic predicament when her wedding cake collapsed just as the ceremony began.

Synset Examples

the tragicomic disparity … between man's aspirations and his accomplishments

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: comedic - relating to or characterized by comedy
  • Degree Lower: tragic - causing or characterized by extreme distress or sorrow

Etymology

From Italian 'tragicomico', from 'tragedia' (tragedy) + 'comico' (comedy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmɪk / (BrE)
Combining elements of both tragedy and comedy; relating to or characteristic of tragicomedy.
having both tragedy and comedy

Examples

The tragicomic play left the audience both laughing and in tears.
His life had a tragicomic quality, blending misfortune and absurd humor.
This film offers a tragicomic perspective on family relationships.

Synset Examples

a playwright specializing in tragicomic drama

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tragic - characterized by extreme distress or sorrow; relating to tragedy
  • Degree Lower: comic - amusing or humorous; relating to comedy

Etymology

From 'tragicomedy', which originates from Italian 'tragicomedia', from Latin 'tragicomoedia' (tragedy and comedy).

Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌtrædʒ.ɪˈkɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒ.ɪˈkɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
Combining both tragic and comic elements; having qualities that are both sad and amusing.
having both tragic and comic qualities

Examples

The novel is a tragicomic account of a family's struggle with loss and laughter.
His tragicomic mistakes always seemed to blend sorrow with ridiculous humor.
They watched with mixed emotions, finding the situation strangely tragicomic.

Synset Examples

her life … presented itself to me as a tragicomical adventure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comic - amusing or funny; relating to comedy
  • Antonym: tragic - causing or characterized by extreme distress or sorrow

Etymology

From Italian 'tragicomico', from Latin 'tragicomicus', combining 'tragic' and 'comic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01270872-s
Synset POS: s
