to smutch

1. VERB

/ smʌtʃ / (AmE), / smʌtʃ / (BrE)
to make a dirty mark, smudge, or stain on something; to soil by smudging
make a smudge or dirty mark
archaic

Examples

He accidentally smutched the page with ink from his pen.
The child smutched his face while playing with paint.
She tried not to smutch her white dress as she worked in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smudge - to make a dirty mark or blot on something
  • Antonym: clean - to make something free from dirt or marks

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'smutch' meaning a stain or smudge, related to 'smudge'.
