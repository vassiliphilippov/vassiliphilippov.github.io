escaped

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈskeɪpt / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪpt / (BrE)
having gotten free, especially from confinement or captivity
having gotten free from confinement

Examples

The police are searching for an escaped prisoner who broke out last night.
After the storm, several escaped animals were found wandering the city streets.
Authorities quickly recaptured the escaped convict before he could leave the county.

Synset Examples

a convict still at large
searching for two escaped prisoners
dogs loose on the streets
criminals on the loose in the neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fugitive - a person who has escaped from captivity or is in hiding
  • Antonym: captured - having been caught after escaping or being at large

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'escape', from Old French 'escaper', from Latin 'excappare' (to get out of one's cape).
