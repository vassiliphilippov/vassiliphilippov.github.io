Picris

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkrɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪkrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of weedy Old World herbs with yellow flowers, often containing a bitter-tasting substance; also known as bitterweed.
genus of yellow-flowered herbs (bitterweed)

Examples

Many Picris species can be found growing along roadsides in Europe.
The yellow flowers of Picris resemble those of dandelions, but the leaves have a distinctly bitter taste.
Botanists identified the weed as belonging to the genus Picris.

Etymology

From Greek pikris, from pikros (bitter), referring to the plant's taste.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkrɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪkrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of yellow-flowered plants in the daisy family, commonly known as bitterweed, native to the Old World and often containing a bitter-tasting substance
a genus of yellow-flowered plants (bitterweed)

Examples

Several species in the Picris genus are commonly found in dry grasslands across Europe.
The bitter taste of Picris leaves makes them unpalatable to most grazing animals.
Botanists identified the weed as Picris after examining its distinctive yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *pikris* (bitter herb), from *pikros* meaning 'bitter', referring to the plant's taste.
