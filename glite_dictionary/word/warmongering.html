warmongering

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or practice of encouraging or advocating war or conflict, especially for political gain
encouragement or advocacy of war

Examples

The senator was frequently criticized for his warmongering and calls for military intervention.
Many citizens protested against the government's warmongering in international affairs.
His warmongering speeches alarmed both allies and opponents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pacifism - opposition to war or violence
  • Synonym: militarism - belief or desire of a government or people that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively

Etymology

Formed from 'war' + 'mongering' (promoting or encouraging something, usually undesirable).
