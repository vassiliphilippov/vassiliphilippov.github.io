hepadnavirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpædˈneɪvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛpædˈneɪvɪərəs / (BrE)
a genus of DNA viruses that infect animals, including the virus causing hepatitis B in humans
genus of DNA viruses including hepatitis B virus
medical

Examples

The hepadnavirus family includes the hepatitis B virus that affects humans worldwide.
Researchers discovered a new type of hepadnavirus in wild squirrels.
Control of hepadnavirus infections is important in both veterinary and human medicine.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēpar' (liver), 'DNA' (deoxyribonucleic acid), and 'virus'.
