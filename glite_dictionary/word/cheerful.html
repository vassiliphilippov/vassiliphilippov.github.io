cheerful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪɹ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪə.fəl / (BrE)
happy and showing it; noticeably in good spirits or creating a happy feeling in others
happy and in good spirits

Examples

She greeted us with a cheerful smile that brightened the entire room.
Despite the rainy weather, the children remained cheerful throughout their picnic.
His cheerful personality makes him a pleasure to work with.

Synset Examples

her cheerful nature
a cheerful greeting
a cheerful room
as cheerful as anyone confined to a hospital bed could be

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great happiness
  • Antonym: gloomy - feeling or causing sadness or depression
  • Synonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment

Etymology

From 'cheer' (from Old French 'chere', meaning 'face' or 'expression') + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪə.fəl / (BrE)
having or showing a happy and optimistic attitude, even when circumstances might not support such positivity
happy and optimistic

Examples

Despite the bad weather, she remained cheerful throughout the trip.
His cheerful greeting lifted everyone's mood on Monday morning.
The staff tried to keep a cheerful attitude, even during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimistic - expecting the best; hopeful and confident
  • Antonym: gloomy - having a negative or pessimistic outlook

Etymology

From cheer + -ful, first used in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01668480-s
Synset POS: s
