Allium neopolitanum

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪəm ˌniːoʊpəˈlɪtənəm / (AmE), / əˈlɪəm ˌniːəʊpəˈlɪtənəm / (BrE)
A perennial European plant of the onion genus with white, star-shaped flowers, also known as Neapolitan garlic or Daffodil garlic.
a European onion species with white flowers

Examples

Allium neopolitanum blooms in spring, producing clusters of delicate white flowers in the garden.
Many gardeners cultivate Allium neopolitanum for both its attractive appearance and mild onion scent.
The bulbs of Allium neopolitanum can naturalize easily in Mediterranean climates.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Allium' meaning garlic or onion; 'neopolitanum' referring to Naples, Italy, indicating its native region.
