tic

1. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
a sudden, repetitive, and habitual involuntary movement or twitching of a muscle, especially in the face
involuntary facial twitch
medical

Examples

The boy developed a nervous tic in his left eye during exams.
Her facial tic became more noticeable when she was stressed.
Doctors reassured him that most tics in children are temporary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction

Etymology

From French *tic* (twitching, convulsive contraction), first used in 18th century medical texts.
