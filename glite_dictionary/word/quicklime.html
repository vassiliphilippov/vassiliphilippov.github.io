quicklime

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkˌlaɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌlaɪm / (BrE)
A white, caustic, alkaline substance (calcium oxide) obtained by heating limestone, used in industrial processes and to produce calcium hydroxide.
white caustic oxide (calcium oxide)

Examples

The workers handled the quicklime with caution due to its caustic nature.
When water was added to quicklime, it produced a hissing sound and released heat.
In ancient construction, quicklime was an essential component for making mortar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: calcium hydroxide - a compound made from quicklime and water

Etymology

From 'quick' (living) and 'lime' (Old English līm, sticky substance), referring to its reactive properties.
