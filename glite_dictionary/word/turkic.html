Turkic

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːrkɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪk / (BrE)
relating to the peoples who speak Turkic languages, or to the Turkic languages themselves
relating to Turkic languages or peoples

Examples

The Turkic languages are spoken across a large area from Eastern Europe to Siberia.
Several Turkic peoples have distinct histories and traditions.
The museum features artifacts from various Turkic cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - the language of Turkey; a member of the Turkic language family

Etymology

From Turkish Türk + -ic, referring to the Turkic language family.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03138992-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪk / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages spoken in central and western Asia, including Turkish, Uzbek, and Kazakh, forming a subfamily of the Altaic language family
subfamily of related Central Asian languages

Examples

The Turkic languages are spoken by millions of people across Eurasia.
Uzbek is one of several Turkic languages found in Central Asia.
Linguists have studied the common features of Turkic tongues for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - the official language of Turkey, a member of the Turkic family

Etymology

From Turk, the name of the ethnolinguistic group, with the suffix -ic forming an adjective and noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06939235-n
Synset POS: n
