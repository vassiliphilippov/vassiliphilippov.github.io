Tipuana

1. NOUN

/ tiˈpwɑːnə / (AmE), / tɪˈpjʊːənə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering trees in the pea family, native to South America, especially known for the tipu tree (Tipuana tipu).
genus of South American flowering trees

Examples

The Tipuana is widely planted as a street tree in some subtropical cities.
Many birds nest in the branches of the Tipuana near the river.
The leaves of the Tipuana turn golden before they fall in winter.

Etymology

From South American indigenous languages, adopted into Modern Latin as a genus name.

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈpwɑːnə / (AmE), / tɪˈpwɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the legume family, native to South America, consisting of a single species: the tipu tree.
genus of South American tree

Examples

The Tipuana is often planted as an ornamental tree in urban areas.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Tipuana in different climates.
The shade provided by Tipuana helps cool city streets in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipu tree - a South American tree of the genus Tipuana
