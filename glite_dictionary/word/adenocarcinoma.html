adenocarcinoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪnoʊˌkɑːrsɪˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌædɪnəʊˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that originates in the glandular tissue of the body.
malignant tumor in glandular tissue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with adenocarcinoma of the lung after several tests.
Colon adenocarcinoma accounts for the majority of malignant tumors found in the large intestine.
Treatment options for adenocarcinoma may include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinoma - A malignant tumor that arises from epithelial tissue.

Etymology

From Greek adēn (gland) + karkinōma (cancerous tumor).
