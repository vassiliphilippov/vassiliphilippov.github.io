avail

1. NOUN

/ əˈveɪl / (AmE), / əˈveɪl / (BrE)
use or benefit; help or advantage that can be gained from something.
use; benefit; advantage

Examples

All his efforts were of no avail in solving the problem.
The medicine was of little avail against the pain.
She protested, but to no avail.

Synset Examples

of no avail
there's no help for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Synonym: advantage - a condition giving a greater chance of success

Etymology

From Old French 'avail' (profit, worth), from 'valoir' (to be worth), based on Latin 'valere' (to be strong, be worth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05157079-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈveɪl / (AmE), / əˈveɪl / (BrE)
to make use of something, especially an opportunity or resource
make use of something

Examples

If you avail yourself of this offer, you will save money.
Guests are encouraged to avail themselves of the hotel facilities.
She decided to avail herself of the free legal advice.

Synset Examples

She helped herself to some of the office supplies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to utilize - to make practical or effective use of
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not take advantage of

Etymology

From Old French 'avaler', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'valere' (to be strong, be well).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01196102-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈveɪl / (AmE), / əˈveɪl / (BrE)
to be of use or help to someone; to be beneficial or advantageous
be of use; help

Examples

All his efforts did not avail him in the end.
No amount of training will avail if you lack motivation.
Logic and reason will avail little against strong emotions.

Synset Examples

It will avail them to dispose of their booty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - be helpful or provide an advantage to
  • Synonym: help - make it easier for someone to do something
  • Antonym: hinder - make it difficult for someone to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'availler', from Late Latin 'advalere' (to be strong, to be of value).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02554745-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈveɪl / (AmE), / əˈveɪl / (BrE)
(formal) to make use of something for one's own benefit; to take advantage of an opportunity or resource (often used reflexively, as 'avail oneself of').
make use of; take advantage of

Examples

She decided to avail herself of the free counseling services offered by the university.
Visitors are encouraged to avail themselves of the complimentary breakfast each morning.
If you do not avail yourself of these opportunities, you may regret it later.

Synset Examples

He availed himself of the available resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to utilize - to make practical use of something
  • Synonym: exploit - to use something for one's own advantage, sometimes unfairly
  • Antonym: to ignore - to not take notice or make use of something

Etymology

From Old French 'avalir', from Latin 'ad valere' (to be of worth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
