Fraxinus dipetala

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræksɪnəs dɪpɪˈtɑlə / (AmE), / ˈfraksɪnəs dɪpɪˈtɑːlə / (BrE)
A species of shrubby ash native to California, noted for its showy off-white flowers.
a shrubby California ash tree species

Examples

The gardens featured several Fraxinus dipetala shrubs in full bloom.
Botanists studied the unique flowers of Fraxinus dipetala during their field survey.
Because of its drought tolerance, Fraxinus dipetala is popular in native Californian landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a tree or shrub of the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' (ash tree) + 'dipetala' (two-petaled), referring to the flower structure.
