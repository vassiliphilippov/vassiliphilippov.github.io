Chapultepec

1. PROPN

/ tʃəˌpʊltəˈpɛk / (AmE), / tʃəˌpʊltəˈpɛk / (BrE)
a battle in 1847 during the Mexican–American War, in which American forces achieved a decisive victory over Mexican troops near Mexico City
battle near Mexico City, 1847

Examples

The Battle of Chapultepec was a turning point in the Mexican–American War.
Historians often discuss the significance of Chapultepec in American military history.
During the 1847 assault on Chapultepec, future U.S. presidents fought alongside other officers.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chapoltepēc* (at the hill of the grasshoppers), originally the name of the hill and castle near Mexico City.
