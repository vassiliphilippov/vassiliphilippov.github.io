Tocharian

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈkɛəriən / (AmE), / təʊˈkɛəriən / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family that was spoken in central Asia during the first millennium A.D.
an ancient Indo-European language of central Asia

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts discovered in the Tarim Basin are written in Tocharian.
Linguists study Tocharian to better understand the spread of Indo-European peoples.
The extinction of Tocharian left few written records for scholars to analyze.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European - the family of languages including most of those spoken in Europe and many in southwest and south Asia
  • Synonym: Tocharian A - one of the two main dialects of the Tocharian language, also called East Tocharian
  • Synonym: Tocharian B - one of the two main dialects of the Tocharian language, also called West Tocharian

Etymology

From Latin 'Tocharus', relating to the Tochari people of Central Asia.
