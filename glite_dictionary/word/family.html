family

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a group of people who live together, usually consisting of parents and their children; a household
group of people living together; household

Examples

My family moved to the city when I was seven years old.
She spends weekends with her family in their country house.
Many cultures place great importance on respect for one's family.

Synset Examples

the teacher asked how many people made up his home
the family refused to accept his will

Related Terms

  • Synonym: household - all the people who live together in a single home

Etymology

From Latin familia (household, family servants), from famulus (servant).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfæməli / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic group ranking above genus and below order, containing one or more genera that share common characteristics.
biological classification above genus

Examples

The rose family includes apples, cherries, and many other plants.
All beetles belong to their own family within the animal kingdom.
The scientist classified the newly discovered species into the same family as tigers.

Synset Examples

sharks belong to the fish family

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genus - taxonomic group below family and above species
  • Degree Higher: order - taxonomic group above family

Etymology

From Latin 'familia' (household, family), originally meaning all of the servants of a household, later also the household members themselves.
