obligatory

1. ADJ

/ əˈblɪɡ.əˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / əˈblɪɡ.ə.təri / (BrE)
required by a law, rule, custom, or circumstance; compulsory.
required by rule or custom

Examples

Attendance at the staff meeting is obligatory for all employees.
Wearing seatbelts is obligatory in most countries.
He made the obligatory complaint about the weather before starting his speech.

Synset Examples

he made all the obligatory apologies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optional - not required; left to personal choice
  • Synonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory

Etymology

From Latin 'obligatorius', from 'obligare' (to bind).

2. ADJ

/ əˈblɪɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈblɪɡətəri / (BrE)
Required by law, rule, or custom; having to be done because it is demanded by an authority or moral code.
required by law or rule

Examples

In many countries, military service is obligatory for young men.
Wearing seat belts is obligatory when driving a car.
Her attendance at the meeting was obligatory rather than voluntary.

Synset Examples

attendance is obligatory
an obligatory contribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandatory - required by rule or law
  • Antonym: optional - left to one's choice; not required

Etymology

From Medieval Latin obligatorius, from Latin obligare (to bind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00851498-a
Synset POS: a
