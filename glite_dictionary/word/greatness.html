greatness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being very important, distinguished, or eminent
the state of being important or eminent

Examples

Her contributions to science secured her place in history for her greatness.
Many strive for greatness, but few achieve lasting recognition.
The team's greatness was evident throughout the championship season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eminence - the state of being famous, respected, or important
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or unremarkable

Etymology

From Middle English 'greatnesse', from 'great' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unusually large in size, extent, or quantity.
unusual largeness; size or extent

Examples

The greatness of the mountain made everyone stop and stare in awe.
Scientists marveled at the greatness of the iceberg floating past their ship.
The greatness of the city's population was evident during the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: largeness - the quality of being large in size, extent, or capacity

Etymology

From 'great' + '-ness', Old English 'grēat' (big, thick, coarse).
