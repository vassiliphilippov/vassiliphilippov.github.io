to unfreeze

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfriːz / (AmE), / ʌnˈfriːz / (BrE)
to make or become soft or liquid after being frozen; to thaw
make or become liquid after freezing

Examples

You need to unfreeze the chicken before cooking it.
The lake will unfreeze in the spring when the weather gets warmer.
She left the meat out to unfreeze overnight.

Synset Examples

The sun melted the ice
the ice thawed
the ice cream melted
The heat melted the wax
The giant iceberg dissolved over the years during the global warming phase
dethaw the meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thaw - to become liquid or soft after being frozen
  • Synonym: to defrost - to remove frost or ice; to thaw
  • Antonym: freeze - to become hard or solid as a result of extremely cold temperatures

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (reverse) and 'freeze'.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈfriːz / (AmE), / ʌnˈfriːz / (BrE)
to make available or allow the use of something, especially assets or funds, that were previously restricted or blocked
make assets or funds available again

Examples

The government decided to unfreeze the company's assets after the investigation ended.
After several years of restrictions, the court ordered the bank to unfreeze the client's funds.
International agencies urged the authorities to unfreeze humanitarian aid accounts immediately.

Synset Examples

release the holdings in the dictator's bank account

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - to make assets or funds unavailable or inaccessible

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'freeze', meaning to reverse the action of freezing (here, used figuratively for assets or processes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00270568-v
Synset POS: v
