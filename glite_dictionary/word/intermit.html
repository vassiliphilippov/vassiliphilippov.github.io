to intermit

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to stop or pause an activity for a short time before continuing
stop or pause temporarily

Examples

Rain would intermit throughout the afternoon, never stopping for long.
If your symptoms intermit, it's important to still see a doctor.
The speaker chose to intermit her lecture for a brief break.

Synset Examples

We pause for station identification
let's break for lunch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - to stop doing something for a short time before continuing
  • Antonym: resume - to begin something again after stopping

Etymology

From Latin intermittĕre, from inter- ‘between’ + mittere ‘to send’.
