redpoll

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a small finch with a red crown, found in northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere
small finch with a red crown

Examples

A flock of redpolls visited our garden feeders during the winter.
The redpoll is easily identified by the bright red patch on its head.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a redpoll during the migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siskin - a small green and yellow finch

Etymology

From red + poll, referring to the bird's characteristic red patch on its head.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a small finch of northern regions, having a red crown and often a rosy breast and rump
small finch with red crown

Examples

A flock of redpolls visited the garden last winter.
The redpoll can be recognized by its distinctive red cap.
She was excited to spot a redpoll among the sparrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siskin - a small finch with a slender bill, related to the redpoll
