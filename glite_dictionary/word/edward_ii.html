Edward II

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdwərd ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈɛdwəd ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1284–1327) King of England from 1307 to 1327, noted for his defeat at Bannockburn and eventual deposition.
King of England, 1307–1327

Examples

Edward II faced a famous defeat at the Battle of Bannockburn against the Scots.
The turbulent reign of Edward II ended when he was deposed in 1327.
Historians often debate whether Edward II was murdered or died of natural causes in prison.
