Salpichroa

1. NOUN

/ sælˈpɪkɹoʊə / (AmE), / sælˈpɪkrəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants native to temperate regions of North and South America, commonly known as cock's eggs.
genus of temperate American herbaceous plants

Examples

The Salpichroa species grows rapidly in temperate gardens across South America.
Botanists have classified several ornamental plants under the genus Salpichroa.
Gardeners sometimes refer to Salpichroa as cock's eggs due to the appearance of its fruit.

Etymology

From Greek salpinx (trumpet) and chroa (color), referring to aspects of the plant's flowers or berries.
