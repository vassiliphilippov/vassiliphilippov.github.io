apogamy

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / əˈpɒɡəmi / (BrE)
(Botany) The development of an embryo, particularly a sporophyte, without fertilization, as occurs in some ferns and other plants.
embryo development without fertilization (botany)
medical

Examples

The occurrence of apogamy in ferns allows them to reproduce even when fertilization fails.
Scientists have observed apogamy in several species of flowering plants under laboratory conditions.
The study focused on the genetic implications of apogamy in certain mosses and ferns.

Etymology

From Greek apo- (away from) + gamia (marriage), referring to reproduction without fertilization.
