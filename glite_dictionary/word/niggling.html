niggling

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) Too small or unimportant to be worth serious attention; trivial or petty.
trivial; petty; of little importance
informal

Examples

I spent hours worrying about niggling details that didn't really matter.
She had a niggling feeling that something was being overlooked.
Try not to get distracted by niggling problems when working on your project.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petty - of little importance; trivial
  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

From the verb 'niggle', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Scandinavian languages.
