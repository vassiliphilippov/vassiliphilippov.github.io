photomosaic

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊmoʊˈzæɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊməʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
a composite picture made by arranging several aerial photographs or images so that they form a continuous representation of an area
composite picture from multiple photographs

Examples

The photomosaic revealed the entire landscape in remarkable detail, making it easier to plan the route.
Scientists created a photomosaic of the rainforest using hundreds of drone images taken over several days.
A photomosaic assembled from satellite pictures helps researchers monitor changes in the coastline.

Etymology

Formed from 'photo-' (photograph) and 'mosaic' (picture formed from small pieces), reflecting the method of construction.
