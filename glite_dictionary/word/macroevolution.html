macroevolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊˌɛ.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊˌiː.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
evolutionary change occurring on a large scale over geological time, leading to the formation of new taxonomic groups above the species level
evolutionary change above species level
medical

Examples

The fossil record provides evidence for the patterns of macroevolution over millions of years.
Debates in biology often focus on the mechanisms that drive macroevolution.
The emergence of mammals from reptilian ancestors is an example of macroevolution in action.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microevolution - evolutionary changes within a species or population over a short period

Etymology

From macro- (large) + evolution.
