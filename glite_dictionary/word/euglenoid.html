euglenoid

1. NOUN

/ juˈɡliː.nɔɪd / (AmE), / juˈɡliː.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a single-celled organism found in marine and freshwater environments, typically green or colorless, and moving with a flagellum; member of the group Euglenophyta
single-celled flagellate organism
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, I observed a euglenoid swimming rapidly using its whip-like flagellum.
Many euglenoids are able to photosynthesize, making them important in aquatic ecosystems.
The biology class studied a colorful euglenoid collected from a freshwater pond.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'Euglena' (a genus), from 'eu' (good) + 'glēnē' (eyeball), referring to the organism's eye-like structure, plus '-oid' (resembling).
