relay

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈriː.leɪ / (BrE)
a fresh team of animals, especially horses or other draft animals, used to relieve and replace a tired team during a long journey or task
fresh team of animals to relieve a tired one

Examples

The travelers arranged for a relay of horses at each station along the route.
A fresh relay of oxen pulled the wagon over the mountain pass.
Without a relay of mules, the journey would have taken much longer.

Etymology

From Old French relai (resting place), related to relayer (to relieve).

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈriːˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪˌleɪ / (BrE)
to control or operate (a device or signal) by means of a relay, especially an electrical one
control by means of a relay

Examples

The engineer designed the circuit to relay the power automatically.
Modern traffic lights are often relayed to control the flow of vehicles.
A backup system is relaying signals in case of main system failure.

Etymology

From French 'relayer', adapted in technical use for electrical switching.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈriˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪː.leɪ / (BrE)
An electrical device in which a current in one circuit activates a switch to control the flow of current in another circuit.
electrical switching device

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty relay to restore power to the control system.
A relay can be used to isolate different parts of an electrical circuit.
When the engine starts, a relay switches on the fuel pump automatically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit

4. NOUN

/ ˈriːleɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːleɪ / (BrE)
a group of workers or a team that takes over duties from another group, especially to continue work without interruption
a group of workers relieving another

Examples

The night relay arrived promptly at midnight to continue construction on the bridge.
A new relay of nurses took over the ward at the end of the shift.
There was always a fresh relay of workers ready to replace the tired team.

Etymology

From Old French 'relai' (fresh set of hounds or horses), related to 'relais' (to leave behind, release), from Late Latin 'relaxare' (relax, loosen).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈriˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːleɪ / (BrE)
a race in which teams of runners, swimmers, or other athletes each complete a part of the total distance one after another
team race with sequential participants

Examples

The 4x100 meter relay is one of the most exciting events in track and field.
Each swimmer in the relay must wait for their teammate to touch the wall before starting.
Our school's relay team won first place at the state championships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race - a competition of speed

Etymology

From French 'relayer' (to relieve, to take turns), probably from Old French 'relayer', based on 'relai' (a relay or change of horses), from 're-' (again) + 'laisser' (to leave).

the act of passing something along from one person or group to another, especially in order to accomplish a task more efficiently or continuously
the act of passing something along

Examples

7. VERB

/ rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːleɪ / (BrE)
to receive and then pass on (information or a message) to another person or group
pass on information or messages

Examples

Could you relay this message to the rest of the team?
The embassy relayed important information to the government officials.
I am relaying the instructions exactly as I received them.

Synset Examples

Please relay the news to the villagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transmit - to send or convey from one person or place to another
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired by another

Etymology

From Old French relayer (to replace, renew), based on Latin re- (again) + laxare (to loosen).
