pharmaceutical

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɑːrməˈsuːtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməˈsjuːtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the preparation, use, or sale of medicinal drugs
relating to medicinal drugs
medical

Examples

The pharmaceutical industry spends billions on new drug research every year.
She works in a pharmaceutical laboratory that develops vaccines and antibiotics.
Strict regulations govern the pharmaceutical advertising seen on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicinal - having healing properties; used to cure or treat medical conditions

Etymology

From Greek pharmakeutikos (pertaining to drugs or medicine), from pharmakeuein (to prepare drugs), from pharmakon (drug).

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɑːrməˈsuːtɪkl̩ / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməˈsuːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a drug or medicine prepared or dispensed in pharmacies and used in medical treatment
drug or medicine for medical treatment

Examples

New pharmaceuticals are rigorously tested before being approved for public use.
The company specializes in developing pharmaceuticals for chronic illnesses.
Patients rely on a wide range of pharmaceuticals to manage their health conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medication - a drug or remedy used for medical treatment
  • Synonym: drug - a chemical substance used to treat, cure, or prevent disease

Etymology

From Greek pharmakeutikos (pertaining to drugs or medicine), from pharmakeuein (to use medicine or drugs).
