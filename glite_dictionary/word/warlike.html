warlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌlaɪk / (BrE)
suggesting war or having the characteristics of war; militaristic or hostile in nature
suggesting war or military life

Examples

The general adopted a warlike stance toward neighboring countries.
His speeches had a warlike tone that worried the public.
The parade featured soldiers in warlike uniforms, marching through the town square.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving war or violence; calm
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.laɪk / (BrE)
having or showing a tendency toward war or hostility; inclined to use force or aggressive methods.
inclined to war; aggressive

Examples

The president delivered a warlike speech, threatening severe action against the neighboring country.
Some cultures have a reputation for being more warlike than others throughout history.
Their warlike stance on international issues has drawn criticism from peace organizations.

Synset Examples

militant nations
hawkish congressman
warlike policies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - not inclined to war or aggression
  • Synonym: militant - aggressively active, especially for a cause

Etymology

From Middle English 'werlike' (war-like), from Old English 'wer' (war) + 'līc' (like).
