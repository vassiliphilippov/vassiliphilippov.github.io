disreputableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈrɛpjətəblnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈrɛpjʊtəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being not respected or reputable; lacking honor or good reputation.
lack of respectability or good reputation

Examples

The disreputableness of the company made investors hesitant to trust it.
Her actions added to the general disreputableness of the organization.
Rumors and scandals increased the disreputableness associated with his name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrepute - the state of being held in low esteem or lacking respect
  • Antonym: respectability - the quality of being socially acceptable or respected

Etymology

From 'disreputable' (from Latin 'reputare', to think over), plus the suffix '-ness'.
