Matisse

1. PROPN

/ məˈtis / (AmE), / məˈtiːs / (BrE)
Henri Matisse (1869–1954), a French painter and sculptor renowned as a leading figure of Fauvism.
French artist, leader of Fauvism

Examples

Many art historians consider Matisse a master of color and form in modern painting.
The bold use of color in this piece is reminiscent of Matisse and the Fauvist movement.
We visited an exhibition dedicated to Matisse at the museum last weekend.

Etymology

From the French surname Matisse; Henri Matisse is the namesake.
