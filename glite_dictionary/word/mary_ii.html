Mary II

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ði ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
Queen of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1689–1694), who ruled jointly with her husband William III and was the eldest daughter of James II.
Queen of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1689–1694)

Examples

Mary II reigned jointly with William III after the Glorious Revolution of 1688.
Many historians credit Mary II with helping to secure Protestant succession in England.
The reign of Mary II marked significant changes in the British monarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William III - King of England, Scotland, and Ireland, co-monarch with Mary II
  • Degree Lower: Mary I - Queen of England from 1553 to 1558, also known as 'Bloody Mary'
