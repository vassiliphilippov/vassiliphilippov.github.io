lukewarmness

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːkˈwɔrm.nəs / (AmE), / ˌluːkˈwɔːm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking enthusiasm, passion, or intensity; also, a moderately warm temperature
lack of enthusiasm or moderate warmth

Examples

The lukewarmness of his response disappointed those who expected greater excitement.
Many critics noted the lukewarmness with which the proposal was received.
She approached the project with a certain lukewarmness, lacking any real enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: zeal - great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause
  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
  • Synonym: lukewarm - showing little enthusiasm; moderately warm

Etymology

Formed from 'lukewarm' (from Middle English 'lew' + 'warm') with suffix '-ness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌluːkˈwɔːrmnəs / (AmE), / ˌluːkˈwɔːmnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being only moderately warm; a temperature resembling that of the skin; lack of enthusiasm or passion.
moderate warmth; lack of enthusiasm

Examples

The lukewarmness of the soup made it unappetizing to the guests.
His lukewarmness toward the project was obvious during the meeting.
The lukewarmness of the bath water was perfect for relaxing after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lukewarm - moderately warm or unenthusiastic
  • Degree Higher: warmth - the quality of being warm in temperature or feeling
  • Degree Lower: coolness - the quality of being cool (lower in temperature than lukewarm)
