to rarify

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈreərɪfaɪ / (BrE)
make something more refined, intricate, or complex, often by subtle distinctions or elaboration
make more refined or complex

Examples

The author sought to rarify the narrative by adding layers of symbolism and subtlety.
Designers sometimes rarify their creations to appeal to a more discerning audience.
She wanted to rarify the presentation by incorporating more nuanced arguments.

Synset Examples

refine a design or pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - to make something purer or more elegant
  • Antonym: to simplify - make something simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'rarificare' (to make rare, to thin), from 'rarus' (rare) + 'facere' (to make).
