teaspoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌspun / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌspuːn / (BrE)
A small spoon used for stirring tea or coffee and for measuring small quantities of ingredients, typically holding about one fluid dram (approximately 5 milliliters).
small spoon for tea or measuring ingredients

Examples

He added two teaspoons of sugar to his coffee.
Please hand me a teaspoon for my yogurt.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of vanilla extract.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tablespoon - a large spoon used for serving or measuring food
  • Degree Higher: dessertspoon - a medium-sized spoon, larger than a teaspoon but smaller than a tablespoon

Etymology

Formed from 'tea' + 'spoon', referring to its original use for tea.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌspun / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌspuːn / (BrE)
A unit of measure equal to the amount that a teaspoon can hold, used especially in cooking or to describe a small quantity of something.
unit of measure for small amounts

Examples

The recipe calls for one teaspoon of cinnamon.
Add a teaspoon of sugar to your tea for extra sweetness.
He measured out a teaspoon of salt for the soup.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tablespoon - a larger spoon and unit of measure used in cooking
  • Degree Lower: pinch - a very small amount, especially of powder or spice

Etymology

Formed from 'tea' and 'spoon', originally referring to a spoon used for stirring tea; later used for the unit of measure.
