chewy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃuː.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃuː.i / (BrE)
(of food) requiring a lot of chewing; having a tough or elastic texture.
requiring much chewing

Examples

The steak was so chewy that it took me minutes to finish one bite.
Some candies are deliberately made chewy to be fun to eat.
Fresh bagels can be pleasantly chewy on the inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - difficult to cut or chew (of food)
  • Antonym: tender - easy to cut or chew (of food)

Etymology

From 'chew' + '-y', meaning 'able to be chewed' or 'having the quality of being chewed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃu.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃuː.i / (BrE)
(of food or substance) requiring a lot of chewing because it is tough or rubbery.
requiring much chewing

Examples

The steak was so chewy that it took me several minutes to finish each bite.
Children often love chewy candies for their texture and flavor.
These chewy granola bars make a perfect snack for hiking.

Synset Examples

chewy caramels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - difficult to cut or chew; not tender
  • Antonym: tender - (of food) easy to chew or cut

Etymology

From 'chew' + '-y', an adjective suffix, formed in English.
