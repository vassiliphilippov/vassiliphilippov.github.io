installment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɑlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɔːlmənt / (BrE)
one of several parts into which a story, article, or other work is divided, released separately at intervals, especially in magazines or on television
a part of a serial publication

Examples

The author released the latest installment of the novel online each Monday.
Fans eagerly awaited the next installment of the popular crime drama.
This magazine will feature a special installment of the investigative series next month.

Etymology

From install + -ment, originally referring to the 'placing' or 'setting up' of something; this sense evolved in publishing context in the 19th–20th centuries.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɑlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːlmənt / (BrE)
a sum of money paid as part of a series of payments for something, especially a debt, usually at regular intervals
regular part-payment of a debt

Examples

Each month, she paid an installment on her student loans.
The car was finally his after he made the last installment.
You can buy the sofa now and pay in twelve easy installments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the act of paying money

Etymology

From French *installer* (to install, to place in office), from Italian *installare*, from Medieval Latin *installare*. The payment sense reflects the idea of setting up or placing in sequence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13322737-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɔlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːlmənt / (BrE)
one of the parts or episodes of a broadcast serial, published or aired as part of a series
part of a broadcast serial

Examples

The next installment of the drama series will air on Monday night.
Fans eagerly awaited each installment of the podcast to see how the story unfolded.
The detective show released a new installment every week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episode - a single broadcast or published part of a serial

Etymology

From English 'install', based on the idea of placing or settling in stages; general sense from French 'installer.'
