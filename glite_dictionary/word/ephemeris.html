ephemeris

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛmərɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛmərɪs / (BrE)
An annual publication that provides astronomical tables showing the positions of celestial bodies throughout the year.
astronomical table publication

Examples

The astronomer consulted the ephemeris to track the position of Mars.
Each year, a new ephemeris is published for professional and amateur astronomers alike.
Sailors historically relied on the ephemeris to aid in navigation at sea.

Synset Examples

today computers calculate the ephemerides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almanac - an annual calendar containing important dates and statistical information

Etymology

From Latin 'ephemeris' (diary, journal), from Greek 'ephēmeros' (daily).
