glance

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæns / (AmE), / ɡlɑːns / (BrE)
a brief or hurried look
quick look or glimpse

Examples

She gave the clock a quick glance before hurrying out the door.
At a glance, the document appeared to be in order.
He could tell from her glance that something was wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimpse - a brief or partial view

Etymology

From Middle English 'glancen' (to dart aside), influenced by Old French 'glacer' (to strike a glancing blow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879303-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡlæns / (AmE), / ɡlɑːns / (BrE)
to look quickly or briefly at someone or something
look quickly or briefly

Examples

She glanced at her watch before heading out the door.
He glanced quickly at the menu, unsure what to order.
During the meeting, Susan glanced around the room, hoping to catch someone’s eye.

Synset Examples

She only glanced at the paper
I only peeked — I didn't see anything interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peek - look quickly, typically in a secretive or cautious way
  • Antonym: stare - look at someone or something with fixed eyes for a long time
  • Antonym: gaze - look steadily and intently, especially in admiration or thought

Etymology

From Middle English ‘glancen’ (to strike obliquely, shine, glance), from Old French ‘glancer’ (to strike lightly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02169748-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡlæns / (AmE), / ɡlɑːns / (BrE)
(of an object or blow) to strike or hit something at an angle, causing it to deflect or change direction slightly.
to hit at an angle and deflect

Examples

The ball glanced off the edge of the table and rolled onto the floor.
The bullet glanced off his jacket, leaving only a small mark.
Her car glanced a tree but continued down the embankment.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01896993-v
Synset POS: v
