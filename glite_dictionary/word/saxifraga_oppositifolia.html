Saxifraga oppositifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfræɡə ɒˌpɒsɪtɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfræɡə ɒˌpɒsɪtɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
a low-growing alpine plant forming dense cushions with bright reddish-lavender flowers, native to rocky areas of Europe, Asia, and western North America
alpine plant with reddish-lavender flowers

Examples

The Saxifraga oppositifolia blooms early in the spring, adding vivid color to alpine landscapes.
Botanists discovered a large patch of Saxifraga oppositifolia thriving on the rocky slopes.
Gardeners admire Saxifraga oppositifolia for its resilience and striking flowers in mountainous areas.

Etymology

From Latin: 'saxifraga' meaning 'stone-breaker' and 'oppositifolia' meaning 'opposite-leaved'.
