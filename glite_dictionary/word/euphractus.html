Euphractus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfræktəs / (AmE), / juːˈfræktəs / (BrE)
A genus of armadillos within the family Dasypodidae, including the six-banded armadillo (Euphractus sexcinctus).
a genus of armadillos

Examples

The six-banded armadillo belongs to the genus Euphractus.
Researchers studied several Euphractus specimens to understand their unique armor plating.
The genus Euphractus is native to South America and well known for its burrowing habits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dasypodidae - the family of mammals that includes armadillos

Etymology

From Greek 'euphraktos', meaning well-fenced or well-armored, referring to the armadillo's protective shell.
