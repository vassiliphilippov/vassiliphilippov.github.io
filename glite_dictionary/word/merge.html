to merge

1. VERB

/ mɜrdʒ / (AmE), / mɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to combine or unite to form a single entity, especially organizations, groups, or ideas
to combine or unite into one

Examples

The two companies decided to merge to increase their market share.
Streams merge in the valley to form a single river.
After months of negotiations, their ideas gradually merged into a unified plan.

Synset Examples

We merged our resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix or combine together
  • Antonym: separate - to move or keep apart; not joined

Etymology

From Latin 'mergere' (to dip, plunge, immerse).
