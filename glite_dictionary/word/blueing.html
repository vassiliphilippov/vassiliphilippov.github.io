blueing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪŋ / (BrE)
a process that makes something blue or bluish, especially for protective or decorative purposes
process of making something blue

Examples

The blueing of steel protects it from rust and gives it a distinctive color.
Fabric manufacturers often use blueing methods to achieve certain shades for their products.
Traditional blueing techniques are valued in antique firearm restoration.

Etymology

From the verb 'blue' + the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance, usually a blue dye or powder, added to laundry or hair to counteract yellowing and give a whiter or bluish appearance
substance used to whiten laundry or hair

Examples

She added a bit of blueing to the wash to keep her white shirts looking bright.
Some elderly people use blueing to give their grey hair a more silvery tone.
In older times, blueing was a common laundry product found in many households.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluing - alternative spelling of blueing (substance to whiten laundry)
