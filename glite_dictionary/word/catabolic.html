catabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈbɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the breakdown of complex molecules in living organisms to form simpler ones, often with the release of energy.
relating to destructive metabolism
medical

Examples

Intense exercise may increase catabolic activity in the muscles.
The hormone cortisol has catabolic effects on body tissues.
A catabolic state can lead to muscle wasting and weakness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anabolic - relating to constructive metabolism or synthesis of complex molecules

Etymology

From Greek 'katabole' (a throwing down), from 'kata-' (down) + 'ballein' (to throw).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkæt̬.əˈbɑː.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæt.əˈbɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by catabolism, the metabolic process of breaking down complex molecules into simpler ones, releasing energy.
relating to catabolism; metabolic breakdown
medical

Examples

The patient's muscles became weak due to a catabolic response caused by prolonged illness.
Athletes need to balance their diets to avoid excessive catabolic activity, which can break down muscle tissue.
Certain hormones, like cortisol, have a catabolic effect on the body's metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anabolic - relating to the synthesis of complex molecules in living organisms

Etymology

From 'catabolism,' which comes from Greek 'katabole' meaning 'a throwing down.'
