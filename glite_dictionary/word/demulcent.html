demulcent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmʌlsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌlsənt / (BrE)
having a soothing or softening effect, especially on irritated skin or mucous membranes
having a soothing or softening effect
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a demulcent cream to relieve the patient's irritated skin.
Many herbal teas contain demulcent ingredients that help soothe a sore throat.
Aloe vera gel is valued for its demulcent properties in treating sunburns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emollient - having the quality of softening or soothing the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'demulcere' (to caress, soothe), via Late Latin 'demulcent-', present participle of 'demulcere'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈmʌlsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌlsənt / (BrE)
A substance, especially a medication, that soothes or protects irritated or inflamed tissue, particularly of the skin or mucous membranes.
soothing medication for irritated tissue
medical

Examples

The pharmacist recommended a demulcent to relieve her sore throat.
Applying a demulcent can help protect damaged skin from further irritation.
Many cough syrups contain a demulcent to soothe the lining of the throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soother - something that relieves annoyance or pain

Etymology

From Latin *demulcentem*, present participle of *demulcēre* (to soothe).
