Caesar

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
Julius Caesar (100–44 BC), Roman general, statesman, and dictator, known for his conquest of Gaul and pivotal role in the fall of the Roman Republic.
Roman general and dictator (100–44 BC)

Examples

Caesar crossed the Rubicon, initiating a civil war in Rome.
Many historians consider Caesar one of the greatest military minds of all time.
The assassination of Caesar marked a turning point in Roman history.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesar', family name of Julius Caesar.
