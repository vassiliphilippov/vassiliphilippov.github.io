sexton

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.stən / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.stən / (BrE)
an officer of a church responsible for the care of the building and its sacred objects, often also acting as a caretaker or bell-ringer
church officer caring for sacred objects

Examples

The sexton was busy preparing the altar for Sunday's service.
As sexton, she ensured the church was always well maintained and the candlesticks polished.
The old sexton rang the bell every morning at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacristan - a church officer in charge of the sacristy and sacred items

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sacristanus' (sacristan), via Old French 'secrestein'.
