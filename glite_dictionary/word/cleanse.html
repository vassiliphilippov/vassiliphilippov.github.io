to cleanse

1. VERB

/ klɛnz / (AmE), / klɛnz / (BrE)
to make (someone's body or part of it) clean, especially by washing
wash to make clean (body or part)

Examples

Before prayer, he would always cleanse his hands and face.
Doctors recommend cleansing wounds thoroughly to prevent infection.
She cleansed her skin with a gentle soap every morning.

Synset Examples

clean up before you see your grandparents
clean your fingernails before dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make something free from dirt; to remove dirt from

Etymology

From Old English *clǣnsian* (to purify, make clean).

WordNet ID: oewn-00035769-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klɛnz / (AmE), / klɛnz / (BrE)
to rid a person, place, or thing of something unwanted, especially guilt, sin, or undesirable elements
purge of undesirable elements or sins

Examples

The priest said that confession would cleanse his soul of sin.
Some political movements aim to cleanse society of unwanted ideologies.
Meditation helped her to cleanse her mind of negative thoughts.

Synset Examples

Purgatory is supposed to cleanse you from your sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - remove impurities or foreign elements
  • Antonym: to contaminate - make something impure by exposure to or addition of a harmful substance

Etymology

From Old English 'clænsian', related to 'clean' (pure, free from dirt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00502977-v
Synset POS: v
