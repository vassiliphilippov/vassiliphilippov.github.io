zamburek

1. NOUN

/ zæmˈbʊrɛk / (AmE), / zæmˈbʊrɛk / (BrE)
a type of camel-mounted artillery used in the early modern era by Arab, Afghan, Persian, and Indian armies
camel-mounted artillery unit

Examples

The Persian army deployed a zamburek unit to protect its flanks during the battle.
Historians describe how zambureks combined mobility and firepower on the battlefield.
The museum features a restored zamburek used in 18th-century India.

Etymology

From Persian 'zam-bûrak' (a small cannon mounted on a camel).
