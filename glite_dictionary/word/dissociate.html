to dissociate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsoʊsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsieɪt / (BrE)
to separate oneself from or break involvement with a person, group, or idea
to separate oneself from someone or something

Examples

After the scandal, she decided to dissociate herself from the company.
He tried to dissociate his personal beliefs from his professional responsibilities.
Many politicians publicly dissociate themselves from controversial policies.

Synset Examples

She disassociated herself from the organization when she found out the identity of the president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: associate - to connect or join with someone or something
  • Synonym: distance (oneself) - to make oneself less involved or connected

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + sociare (to join).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02435427-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsoʊʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
(of a molecule) to break down temporarily or reversibly into simpler molecules, ions, or atoms, often as part of a chemical process.
break down chemically into simpler parts
medical

Examples

At high temperatures, water molecules can dissociate into hydrogen and oxygen.
Many acids dissociate in water to produce positive hydrogen ions.
The compound does not fully dissociate at room temperature, resulting in a weak electrolyte.

Synset Examples

acids dissociate to give hydrogen ions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: associate - to join together or connect in a chemical process

Etymology

From Latin dissociare (to separate, disjoin), from dis- (apart) + sociare (to join).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00210024-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsoʊʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to consider or treat two or more things as separate or unrelated
regard as unconnected

Examples

It is important to dissociate science from personal beliefs when evaluating evidence.
The company tried to dissociate itself from the scandal by issuing a statement.
She could not dissociate the sound of rain from her childhood memories.

Synset Examples

you must dissociate these two events!
decouple our foreign policy from ideology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not joined or united
  • Antonym: associate - to connect or link two or more things in one's mind

Etymology

From Latin dissociatus, past participle of dissociare (to separate, disjoin), from dis- (apart) + sociare (to join).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
