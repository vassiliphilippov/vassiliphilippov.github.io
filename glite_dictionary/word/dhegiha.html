Dhegiha

1. NOUN

/ dəˈgiːhə / (AmE), / dəˈgiːhə / (BrE)
A branch of the Siouan language family, comprising languages spoken by certain Native American tribes such as the Omaha, Ponca, Osage, Kansa, and Quapaw.
branch of the Siouan languages

Examples

Linguists have classified Omaha and Osage as part of the Dhegiha group of Siouan languages.
The Dhegiha branch includes languages that are still studied and revitalized among Native American communities.
A comparative analysis of the Dhegiha languages reveals shared grammatical structures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

From the Dhegiha word *Degiha*, meaning 'those who dwell here'.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈɡiːhə / (AmE), / dəˈɡiːhə / (BrE)
A member of any of several Siouan-speaking Native American peoples, especially the Omaha, Ponca, Osage, Kaw, or Quapaw, who speak one of the Dhegiha languages.
member of a Siouan-speaking Native American group

Examples

The Dhegiha share common ancestral and linguistic roots.
Many Dhegiha traditions have been preserved by the Omaha and Osage peoples.
Anthropologists have studied Dhegiha migration patterns across the Midwest.

Etymology

From the Dhegiha word *Degiha* meaning 'those who dwell here', used by the peoples themselves.
