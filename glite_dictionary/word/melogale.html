Melogale

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːləˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Mustelidae, commonly known as ferret-badgers, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of ferret-badgers

Examples

The Melogale genus includes several species of ferret-badgers found in Asia.
Researchers observed a Melogale in the forest during their field study.
Unlike true badgers, members of the Melogale genus are more slender and agile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferret-badger - Small carnivorous mammal of the genus Melogale

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'gale' (weasel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Mustelidae, comprising the ferret-badgers found in South and Southeast Asia.
genus of ferret-badgers

Examples

The Melogale genus includes several species of ferret-badgers native to Asia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Melogale genus in the forests of Vietnam.
Unlike otters, animals in the Melogale genus are adapted to both terrestrial and arboreal habitats.
