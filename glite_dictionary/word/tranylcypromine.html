tranylcypromine

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræn.ɪl.saɪˈprəʊ.miːn / (AmE), / ˌtran.ɪl.saɪˈprəʊ.miːn / (BrE)
an antidepressant drug that functions as a monoamine oxidase inhibitor and is used to treat major depressive disorder
antidepressant monoamine oxidase inhibitor
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist prescribed tranylcypromine for patients resistant to other antidepressants.
Due to dietary restrictions, patients on tranylcypromine must avoid certain foods high in tyramine.
Before switching medications, she consulted her doctor about discontinuing tranylcypromine safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenelzine - a similar monoamine oxidase inhibitor antidepressant

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: combining elements of 'trans', 'anil', 'cyclopropyl', and 'amine'.
