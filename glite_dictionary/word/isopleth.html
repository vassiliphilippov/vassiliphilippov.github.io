isopleth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌplɛθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌplɛθ / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points where a given variable has the same value, such as temperature or rainfall.
line on a map connecting points of equal value

Examples

The meteorologist drew an isopleth on the weather map to represent areas with equal rainfall.
By following each isopleth, scientists can identify regions with the same temperature.
Elevation changes are typically shown with isopleths marked at specific intervals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contour line - a line joining points of equal elevation on a map
  • Synonym: isotherm - a line connecting points of equal temperature
  • Synonym: isobar - a line on a map connecting points of equal atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + plethos (quantity, number).
