ostomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstəmi / (AmE), / ˈɒstəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an artificial opening is created in the body to allow for the elimination of bodily wastes
surgical procedure creating a waste elimination opening
medical

Examples

After the surgery, the patient learned how to care for his ostomy.
The support group provides useful tips for living with an ostomy.
Doctors discussed both the risks and benefits of performing an ostomy to treat her condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colostomy - surgical opening from the colon through the abdominal wall
  • Synonym: ileostomy - surgical opening from the ileum through the abdominal wall

Etymology

Formed from 'ost-' (relating to an opening) and '-omy' (surgical operation), modeled after terms like colostomy and ileostomy.
