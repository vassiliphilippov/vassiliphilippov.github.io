Phocoena phocoena

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈsiːnə foʊˈsiːnə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːnə fəˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A small cetacean species known as the common porpoise, found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
the common porpoise, a marine mammal

Examples

Researchers observed a pod of Phocoena phocoena near the coast during their marine survey.
The Phocoena phocoena is sometimes called the harbour porpoise because it frequents coastal waters.
Despite conservation efforts, populations of Phocoena phocoena have declined in some regions due to fishing nets and pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porpoise - a small toothed whale of the family Phocoenidae

Etymology

From Latin phocoena (porpoise); species name doubled for emphasis in scientific nomenclature.
