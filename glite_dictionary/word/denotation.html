denotation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːnoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of indicating or referring to something by name
act of indicating by name

Examples

The denotation of the word ‘rose’ is the flower itself, not any romantic ideas associated with it.
In linguistics, the study of denotation helps distinguish literal meanings from connotations.
Careful denotation of medical terms ensures there is no misunderstanding between doctors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connotation - an idea or feeling that a word invokes in addition to its literal meaning

Etymology

From Medieval Latin denotationem (indicating, designating), from Latin denotare (to mark out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːnoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The literal or primary meaning of a word, distinct from the feelings or ideas that the word suggests; the class of actual things or entities to which a term refers.
literal or primary meaning of a word

Examples

The denotation of the word 'rose' is a type of flower.
It is important to distinguish between the denotation and the connotation of a term.
In linguistics, denotation refers to the actual objects or concepts a word represents.

Synset Examples

the extension of ‘satellite of Mars’ is the set containing only Demos and Phobos

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connotation - the ideas or feelings beyond a word's literal meaning
  • Synonym: reference - the action of mentioning or alluding to something, especially in language

Etymology

From Latin denotatio (indication), from denotare (to mark out).
