lutein

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtiɪn / (AmE), / ˈluːtiːɪn / (BrE)
a yellow carotenoid pigment found in plants, egg yolks, and animal fats, often used as a dietary supplement and known for its potential benefits to eye health
a yellow carotenoid pigment in plants and egg yolks
medical

Examples

Dark leafy greens like spinach are a good source of lutein.
Researchers studied the effects of lutein supplements on vision health.
Egg yolks contain a significant amount of lutein, which gives them their rich color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotenoid - any of a group of yellow, orange, or red pigments found in plants

Etymology

From Latin luteus (yellow).
