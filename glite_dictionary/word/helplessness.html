helplessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlplɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪsnɪs / (BrE)
The state of being unable to take care of oneself or to act without the help of others.
the state of being unable to help oneself

Examples

She felt a deep sense of helplessness as she watched the disaster unfold on television.
His injury left him with a frustrating helplessness he was not used to experiencing.
Many people experience helplessness when dealing with situations beyond their control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - the state of lacking power or control over a situation
  • Antonym: independence - the state of being able to manage without help from others

Etymology

From 'helpless' + '-ness', meaning the quality or state of being helpless.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to take action or control events; lack of power or ability to help oneself or others
state of being unable to act or help

Examples

A sense of helplessness overwhelmed him when he heard the bad news.
The disaster left many people experiencing profound helplessness.
She tried to hide her helplessness from her children.

Synset Examples

in spite of their weakness the group remains active

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - lack of power or ability to influence
  • Antonym: control - the power to influence or direct people or things

Etymology

From 'helpless' + '-ness'. 'Helpless' from Old English 'help' + 'leas' (without).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪsnəs / (BrE)
the feeling or state of being unable to do anything to change or improve a situation
feeling of being unable to act

Examples

A deep sense of helplessness overcame her as she watched the disaster unfold.
During the blackout, our helplessness became painfully obvious when we couldn't contact anyone.
He tried to comfort his son, who expressed his helplessness over the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - lack of ability or means to act or influence
  • Antonym: confidence - the feeling or belief that one can rely on something; self-assurance

Etymology

From 'helpless' (Middle English, from 'help' + -less) + '-ness'.
