rally

1. NOUN

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
a motor race in which drivers compete over public roads, often in stages, typically testing both speed and endurance
motor race over public roads

Examples

The Monte Carlo rally is one of the most famous events in motorsport.
He trained for months to compete in a grueling cross-country rally.
Spectators lined the roads to watch the vintage car rally pass through their town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race - a competition of speed
  • Synonym: grand prix - a major automobile or motorcycle race

Etymology

From French 'ralliement', influenced by the sport's organization format.
