American

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən / (BrE)
relating to the United States of America, its people, language, or culture
of or relating to the USA

Examples

She enjoys listening to American music and watching Hollywood movies.
The American flag has fifty stars representing each state.
Many students come to study American literature at university.

Synset Examples

American citizens
American English
the American dream

Etymology

From Latin 'America', named after Amerigo Vespucci, Italian explorer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02938825-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or citizen of the United States of America
a person from the United States

Examples

Many Americans celebrate Independence Day with fireworks and parades.
As an American, he enjoyed visiting his relatives in Europe.
The American was known for his friendly and outgoing personality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Americanus, from America, the name of the continent, after explorer Amerigo Vespucci.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09758057-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the continents and islands of North, Central, or South America, or their peoples and cultures.
of or relating to the Americas (the continents)

Examples

The tropical rainforests are an important part of American biodiversity.
She studied American geography, focusing on both Central and South America.
Many American languages are spoken only in isolated indigenous communities.

Synset Examples

the American hemisphere
American flora and fauna

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: American - relating to the United States or its people

Etymology

From America + -an, denoting relation to the continents of America.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02938616-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən / (BrE)
The variety of the English language spoken and written in the United States.
US variety of English

Examples

She speaks American at work, but her family uses British English at home.
The novel was written in American rather than British English.
Students are required to analyze the differences between American and other forms of English.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: British English - the English language as used in the United Kingdom
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06960241-n
Synset POS: n
