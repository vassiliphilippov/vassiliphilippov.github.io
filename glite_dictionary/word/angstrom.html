angstrom

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋstrəm / (AmE), / ˈæŋstrəm / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (0.1 nanometer), commonly used to measure wavelengths of light and atomic dimensions
unit of length, 0.1 nanometer

Examples

The helium atom has a diameter of about one angstrom.
Ultraviolet light typically has wavelengths between 100 and 4000 angstroms.
X-ray wavelengths are often measured in angstroms in scientific literature.

Etymology

Named after Swedish physicist Anders Jonas Ångström (1814–1874).

2. NOUN

a unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (0.1 nanometers), used especially to measure wavelengths of light and atomic dimensions
unit of length (10⁻¹⁰ meters)

Examples

The diameter of a typical hydrogen atom is about one angstrom.
Ultraviolet light has wavelengths in the range of hundreds of angstroms.
Scientists use the angstrom to measure lengths at the atomic scale.
