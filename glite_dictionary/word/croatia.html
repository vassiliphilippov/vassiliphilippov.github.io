Croatia

1. PROPN

/ kroʊˈeɪʃə / (AmE), / krəʊˈeɪʃə / (BrE)
A country in southeastern Europe on the eastern coast of the Adriatic Sea, formerly part of Yugoslavia, independent since 1991.
a country in southeastern Europe

Examples

Many tourists visit Croatia for its stunning Adriatic coastline and historic cities.
After gaining independence in 1991, Croatia developed a rapidly growing tourism industry.
The capital of Croatia is Zagreb, a city known for its vibrant culture and architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Serbia - country bordering Croatia to the east, also a former Yugoslav republic
  • Synonym: Slovenia - country bordering Croatia to the northwest, also a former Yugoslav republic

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Croatia', from Croat 'Hrvatska'.
