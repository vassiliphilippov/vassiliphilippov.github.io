Fagaceae

1. NOUN

/ fəˈɡeɪsiaˌi / (AmE), / faˈɡeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly monoecious trees and shrubs that includes beeches, chestnuts, and oaks.
the beech family of trees and shrubs

Examples

The Fagaceae family includes some of the most common forest trees, such as oak and beech.
Many botanists study the reproductive strategies of Fagaceae species to understand forest ecology.
Chestnuts, which belong to the Fagaceae, were once a staple food in many European regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - A tree in the Fagus genus, member of Fagaceae
  • Synonym: oak - A tree in the Quercus genus, member of Fagaceae
  • Synonym: chestnut - A tree in the Castanea genus, member of Fagaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'Fagus' (beech) + '-aceae', a standard botanical suffix for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ fæˈɡeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / fæˈɡeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly monoecious trees and shrubs, including beeches, chestnuts, and oaks
plant family of beeches, chestnuts, and oaks

Examples

Many temperate forests are dominated by members of the Fagaceae, such as oaks and beeches.
Recent studies examine the evolutionary relationships within the Fagaceae across different continents.
The nuts produced by trees in the Fagaceae are important food sources for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - a tree of the genus Fagus, member of Fagaceae
  • Synonym: oak - trees of the genus Quercus, member of Fagaceae
  • Synonym: chestnut - trees of the genus Castanea, member of Fagaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin Fagaceae, based on Fagus (the beech genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
