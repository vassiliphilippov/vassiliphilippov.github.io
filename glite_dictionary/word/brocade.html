brocade

1. NOUN

/ broʊˈkeɪd / (AmE), / brəˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a rich thick fabric, often silk, woven with a raised pattern, typically in gold or silver thread
thick patterned woven fabric

Examples

The queen's gown was made of shimmering brocade embroidered with gold thread.
He admired the intricate patterns woven into the brocade drapes.
The antique chair was upholstered in a luxurious brocade that glowed in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damask - a reversible patterned fabric, typically of silk, wool, linen, cotton, or synthetic fibres
  • Synonym: tapestry - a thick textile fabric with pictures or designs formed by weaving

Etymology

From Italian *broccato*, from *broccare* (to emboss).

2. VERB

/ broʊˈkeɪd / (AmE), / brəʊˈkeɪd / (BrE)
to weave a raised design into fabric, often with gold or silver threads
weave a decorative design into fabric

Examples

Traditional artisans brocade luxurious fabrics for royal garments using gold and silver threads.
The craftsman brocaded an intricate floral pattern into the silk.
She spent hours brocading the velvet with delicate geometric designs.

Etymology

From Spanish *brocado*, from Italian *broccato*, from *brocco* (small nail, point), referring to the raised design.
