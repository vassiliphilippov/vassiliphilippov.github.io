withers

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪðərz / (AmE), / ˈwɪðəz / (BrE)
the highest part of the back at the base of the neck of a horse or other quadruped, especially where a saddle rests
highest part of an animal's back at neck base

Examples

The veterinarian measured the horse at the withers to determine its height.
A saddle that fits poorly can cause discomfort near the animal's withers.
She stood beside the pony and patted it gently on its withers.

Etymology

From Old English *wiðer* (against, contrary), originally referring to the part at the meeting of the shoulders.
