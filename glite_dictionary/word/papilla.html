papilla

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpɪlə / (AmE), / pəˈpɪlə / (BrE)
a small, nipple-shaped projection of tissue, such as at the base of a hair, tooth, or feather
small projection of tissue
medical

Examples

The hair papilla plays a crucial role in hair growth and regeneration.
She learned about the dental papilla in her anatomy class.
A papilla can also be found at the base of a bird's feather.

Etymology

From Latin papilla (nipple, small protuberance).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈpɪlə / (AmE), / pəˈpɪlə / (BrE)
A small, nipple-shaped projection on the surface of a tissue, such as the tongue, involved in taste, touch, or smell.
small nipple-shaped projection, esp. on tissue
medical

Examples

Tiny papillae on the surface of the tongue contain taste buds.
The sense of touch in some animals is heightened by specialized papillae on their skin.
Each papilla plays a role in detecting flavors and textures.

Synset Examples

the papillae of the tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste bud - A sensory organ found on the tongue, involved in tasting

Etymology

From Latin 'papilla' meaning 'nipple'.

3. NOUN

/ pəˈpɪlə / (AmE), / pəˈpɪlə / (BrE)
(botany) A small, nipple-like projection or outgrowth on the surface of a petal, leaf, or other plant part.
tiny projection on a plant surface

Examples

The petal surface is covered with tiny papillae that give it a velvety texture.
Under the microscope, you can clearly see the papillae along the edges of the leaf.
Some orchids have prominent papillae at the tip of each petal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - a thing that protrudes or bulges out from a surface
  • Antonym: depression - a sunken area on a surface, the opposite of a bump or projection

Etymology

From Latin 'papilla' meaning 'nipple'.
