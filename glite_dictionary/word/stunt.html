stunt

1. VERB

/ stʌnt / (AmE), / stʌnt / (BrE)
to perform a difficult or daring physical act, especially as entertainment
perform a daring act

Examples

The acrobat will stunt on the high wire during tonight's show.
Many actors refuse to stunt for safety reasons in action movies.
He learned to stunt on his motorcycle after months of practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perform - to carry out an action or function

2. VERB

/ stʌnt / (AmE), / stʌnt / (BrE)
to prevent the normal growth or development of someone or something
prevent normal growth or development

Examples

Poor nutrition can stunt a child's physical and mental development.
Overcrowded roots in a pot will stunt the growth of plants.
The drought significantly stunted the harvest this year.

Synset Examples

You will stunt your growth by building all these muscles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone or something to develop or succeed
  • Antonym: to encourage - to help or promote the development of someone or something

3. NOUN

/ stʌnt / (AmE), / stʌnt / (BrE)
a difficult, unusual, or dangerous action, usually performed to attract attention or entertain people
difficult or dangerous feat for attention

Examples

The actor performed a dangerous stunt without using a double.
He became famous for his daring motorcycle stunts.
The magician wowed the crowd with an impressive stunt involving fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - an action intended to deceive or impress, often used in context of performance
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ stʌnt / (AmE), / stʌnt / (BrE)
an animal, especially a whale, that has been prevented from reaching its full growth; an undersized individual due to arrested development
an animal prevented from full growth

Examples

The marine biologist studied a stunt among the whale population, noting its smaller size.
Environmental factors can cause a stunt in some animal species, preventing normal growth.
The stunt was easily distinguished from the other whales by its unusually short length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarf - an animal or plant that is much smaller than the typical size for its species

Etymology

From the verb 'stunt' (to hinder growth), likely of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'stunta' (to be blunt, dull).
