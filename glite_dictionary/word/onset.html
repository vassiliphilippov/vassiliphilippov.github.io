onset

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌsɛt / (BrE)
a military attack or offensive against an enemy
military attack or offensive

Examples

The army's onset caught the enemy completely by surprise.
During the fierce onset, many soldiers were injured on both sides.
The general ordered an immediate onset at first light.

Synset Examples

the attack began at dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Antonym: defense - the action of defending from or resisting attack

Etymology

From Old English *onsǣtan* (to assault), influenced by set (‘to cause to go somewhere’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌsɛt / (BrE)
the beginning or earliest stage of something, especially something unpleasant or difficult
beginning or early stage

Examples

The doctor explained that quick treatment is crucial at the onset of symptoms.
With the onset of winter, temperatures dropped rapidly across the country.
They evacuated the area at the onset of the hurricane warning.

Synset Examples

the onset of pneumonia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point at which something starts
  • Antonym: end - the final part of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'onset', from 'on' + 'set' (attack, setting on).
