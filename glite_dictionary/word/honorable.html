honorable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbəl / (BrE)
Having or showing high moral standards and a strong sense of what is right or just.
having high moral standards

Examples

She made an honorable decision despite pressure to act otherwise.
The judge was known for his honorable conduct throughout his career.
It is honorable to admit your mistakes and take responsibility.

Synset Examples

it seems ethical and right
followed the only honorable course of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethical - morally right or conforming to accepted standards
  • Antonym: dishonorable - lacking moral principles or not deserving respect

Etymology

From Old French *honorable*, from Latin *honorabilis* (worthy of honor).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbl / (BrE)
Deserving honor and respect because of high moral standards or noble actions.
worthy of honor or respect

Examples

He was known as an honorable judge who always acted fairly.
Serving her community was the most honorable aspect of her career.
They praised him for his honorable conduct during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

an honorable man
led an honorable life
honorable service to his country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonorable - not deserving honor or respect
  • Synonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct

Etymology

From Old French 'honorable', from Latin 'honorabilis' (worthy of honor).

/ ˈɑːnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbl / (BrE)
deserving of honor, esteem, or respect; having high moral qualities or a good reputation
deserving of respect or honor

Examples

It was an honorable decision to admit his mistake and apologize.

  • Antonym: dishonorable - not deserving honor or respect; shameful
