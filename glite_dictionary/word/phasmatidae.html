Phasmatidae

1. NOUN

/ fæzˈmæ.tɪˌdiː / (AmE), / fazˈmat.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of insects, commonly known as stick insects, characterized by their elongated bodies that resemble twigs or sticks.
family of stick insects

Examples

The Phasmatidae family includes some of the longest insects in the world.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Phasmatidae during their expedition to Borneo.
Many members of the Phasmatidae rely on their stick-like appearance for camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick insect - insect of the family Phasmatidae, resembling a stick

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phasmatidae', based on 'Phasma', a genus name from Greek 'phasma' meaning apparition or phantom, referring to their camouflage.

2. NOUN

/ fæsˈmæ.tɪˌdi / (AmE), / fæsˈmat.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of stick insects characterized by their elongated, twig-like bodies and camouflage abilities
a family of stick insects

Examples

The Phasmatidae are renowned for their remarkable camouflage, allowing them to blend seamlessly into their environment.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Phasmatidae family in the tropical rainforest.
Many members of the Phasmatidae can remain motionless for hours, further enhancing their disguise.

Etymology

From modern Latin Phasmatidae, based on Greek phasma (apparition, phantom), referring to the insects' camouflage.
