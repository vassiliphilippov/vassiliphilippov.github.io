flakey

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
made of or resembling flakes; tending to break into thin, flat pieces.
made of or like flakes

Examples

The flakey texture of the pastry made it especially delicious.
He noticed a flakey residue left on his skin after using the soap.
The paint on the old wall became flakey and started to peel off in thin layers.

Synset Examples

flaky soap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaky - variant spelling, same meaning: made of or resembling flakes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02739918-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
consisting of or easily breaking into flakes; tending to form flakes
easily forming flakes

Examples

The pastry was so flakey that it melted in my mouth.
She brushed off the flakey paint from the old chair before repainting it.
I love the flakey layers of a good croissant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaky - made of or easily forming flakes (standard spelling)

Etymology

From 'flake' + '-y', denoting having the quality of flakes.
