penumbral

1. ADJ

/ pəˈnʌm.brəl / (AmE), / pɪˈnʌm.brəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the partial shadow at the edge of the full shadow (umbra), especially during an eclipse.
relating to a partial shadow near an umbra

Examples

During a lunar eclipse, the penumbral shadow causes only a slight dimming of the moon.
Scientists observed penumbral regions where the sunlight was partially blocked.
The photograph captured both the penumbral and umbral parts of the shadow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: umbral - relating to the complete shadow (umbra)

Etymology

From Latin 'paene' (almost) + 'umbra' (shadow), meaning 'almost shadow'.
