Tyto alba

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtoʊ ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˈtɪtəʊ ˈalbə / (BrE)
The barn owl, a medium-sized, white and buff owl species commonly found in barns and other structures, known for helping control rodent populations.
barn owl species; rodent predator

Examples

The Tyto alba is recognized for its heart-shaped facial disc and silent flight.
Farmers appreciate the presence of Tyto alba because it helps reduce rodent populations naturally.
While studying nocturnal birds, the researchers focused on Tyto alba sightings around rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn owl - common name for Tyto alba, a buff and white owl species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, Tyto (owl genus name) + alba (Latin for 'white').
