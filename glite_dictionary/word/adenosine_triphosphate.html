adenosine triphosphate

1. NOUN

/ əˌdɛnəˌsin traɪˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / əˌdiːnəʊˌsiːn traɪˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a compound found in living cells that stores and provides energy for many biochemical cellular processes
energy-carrying molecule in cells
medical

Examples

Muscle contraction relies heavily on adenosine triphosphate for the required energy.
The production of adenosine triphosphate occurs mainly in the mitochondria of cells.
Scientists often refer to adenosine triphosphate as the 'energy currency' of the cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ATP - abbreviation for adenosine triphosphate

Etymology

From adenosine (a nucleoside) and triphosphate (indicating three phosphate groups).
