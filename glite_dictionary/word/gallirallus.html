Gallirallus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈræləs / (AmE), / ˌɡælɪˈraləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, native to New Zealand and the southwest Pacific, including several flightless species.
genus of rails (birds) in New Zealand and Pacific

Examples

The extinct Chatham Island rail belonged to the genus Gallirallus.
Researchers have studied several species within Gallirallus to understand flightlessness.
Many Gallirallus species are known for inhabiting remote Pacific islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rallus - another genus of rails

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'gallina' (hen) and 'Rallus' (the genus name for rails).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈræləs / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈraləs / (BrE)
A genus of rails native to New Zealand and nearby Pacific islands, comprising several species of ground-dwelling birds.
genus of rails of New Zealand

Examples

The extinct Chatham Island rail belonged to the genus Gallirallus.
Several flightless birds in Oceania are classified within Gallirallus.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of Gallirallus to understand rail dispersal in the Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - any of several birds in the Rallidae family

Etymology

From Latin: gallus (chicken) + rallus (rail, a type of bird).
