exception

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
grounds for criticism or objection; a reason to disapprove or challenge something.
grounds for criticism or objection

Examples

His honesty is above exception and cannot be questioned.
The manager's decision was accepted without exception from the team.
She lives a life that is beyond exception in every way.

Synset Examples

his authority is beyond exception

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objection - an expression or feeling of disapproval or opposition
  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin exceptio (the act of excepting, objection).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that does not follow a rule, generalization, or pattern
person or thing not following a rule

Examples

Usually, students must submit homework on time, but Sarah was granted an exception because she was ill.
Every rule has its exception, making it wise to stay flexible.
He was the rare exception in a family of doctors, choosing to become an artist instead.

Synset Examples

all her children were brilliant; the only exception was her last child
an exception tests the rule

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rule - a prescribed guide for conduct or action
  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected

Etymology

From Latin exceptio (exclusion, objection), from excipere (to take out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05829606-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately leaving someone or something out; someone or something that is not included
deliberate act of omission; someone or something left out

Examples

With the exception of Mark, everyone arrived on time to the meeting.
No one is allowed in this area, with the exception of authorized personnel.
She eats fruit every day, with the exception of Mondays.

Synset Examples

with the exception of the children, everyone was told the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of deliberately not including something
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including something or someone

Etymology

From Latin exceptio (a leaving out), from excipere (to take out).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
