thrower

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who throws something, especially by a rapid movement of the arm
person who throws something

Examples

The javelin thrower won a gold medal at the Olympics.
As a discus thrower, she practices her technique every day.
In dodgeball, the thrower aimed for his opponent's feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - a player who throws the ball in baseball

Etymology

From the verb 'throw' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a craftsman who shapes pottery on a potter's wheel and bakes it in a kiln
person who shapes pottery on a wheel

Examples

The experienced thrower demonstrated how to center the clay on the wheel.
She apprenticed as a thrower before opening her own studio.
Every thrower develops a unique style that is reflected in their pottery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potter - a person who makes pottery

Etymology

From the verb 'throw' (to shape clay on a wheel) + -er (agent suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10480372-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊər / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to twist silk or rayon filaments into thread or yarn
a person twisting silk or rayon into yarn

Examples

The experienced thrower carefully twisted the silk filaments into a strong, even thread.
In the textile factory, each thrower was responsible for several spools of rayon.
Becoming a skilled thrower required both patience and dexterity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinner - a person who spins fibers into thread or yarn

Etymology

From 'throw', meaning to twist or spin fibers, originating from Old English 'thrawan' (to twist, turn).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
