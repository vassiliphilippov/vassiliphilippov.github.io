parroquet

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈkeɪ / (BrE)
an archaic term for any of numerous small, slender, long-tailed parrots
archaic term for small, long-tailed parrot
archaic

Examples

The vibrant colors of the parroquet made it a prized pet in Victorian households.
Early explorers often described flocks of parroquets in their travel journals.
A parroquet perched on the tropical branch, chattering noisily among the foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parakeet - a small, slender, long-tailed parrot

Etymology

From French *paroquet* (parakeet), ultimately of uncertain origin.
