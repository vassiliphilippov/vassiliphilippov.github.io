shrimp

1. VERB

/ ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
to catch or fish for shrimp, especially as a commercial or recreational activity
fish for shrimp

Examples

They used to shrimp along the coast every summer morning.
After retiring, he bought a boat so he could shrimp in the Gulf of Mexico.
Local fishermen often shrimp at night when the tide is right.

2. NOUN

/ ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
(informal, often disparaging) A person who is short or small in size, especially one considered weak or insignificant.
small or short person (disparaging)
informal

Examples

He was such a shrimp compared to his tall older brother.
Stop picking on me just because I'm a shrimp on the basketball team!
Despite being called a shrimp all through school, he grew up to be very confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runt - a small or weak person (disparaging)
  • Antonym: giant - a very large or tall person

3. NOUN

/ ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
a small, edible shellfish with a long body and ten legs, widely used as food
small edible shellfish

Examples

She ordered a plate of grilled shrimp for dinner.
Fishermen catch shrimp along the coast and sell them at the market.
He is allergic to shrimp and avoids eating seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prawn - another type of small edible crustacean similar to shrimp

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrimpe', possibly of Low German origin.

4. NOUN

/ ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
a small, slender-bodied marine crustacean with a long tail and a single pair of pincers, many species of which are edible
small edible marine crustacean

Examples

The chef added fresh shrimp to the pasta dish.
We saw dozens of shrimp swimming near the coral reef.
Some people are allergic to shrimp and other shellfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prawn - similar edible crustacean, often larger than a shrimp
  • Degree Higher: lobster - large edible marine crustacean with large claws
  • Synonym: crab - edible marine crustacean with a broad, flat body and pincers

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrympe', likely of Low German origin.
