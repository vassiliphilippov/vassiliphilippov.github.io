to lighten

1. VERB

/ ˈlaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make something less heavy, severe, or oppressive, especially in terms of pressure, burden, or stress
make something less severe or oppressive

Examples

Regular breaks during the workday can lighten the mental strain employees experience.
Her friends helped to lighten the burden after her family crisis.
A good conversation can often lighten your stress and improve your mood.

Synset Examples

relieve the pressure and the stress
lighten the burden of caring for her elderly parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alleviate - make (suffering, deficiency, or a problem) less severe
  • Synonym: ease - make (something unpleasant or intense) less severe
  • Antonym: to worsen - make or become worse

Etymology

From Middle English lighten, from Old English līhtan (to make light, alleviate).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtən / (BrE)
to make a place or thing appear more cheerful or bright, especially by using lighter colors
make more cheerful or bright with color

Examples

Choosing white curtains can lighten a dark hallway and make it feel more spacious.
A colorful rug will lighten the mood of your living room instantly.
They repainted the office in yellow to lighten the atmosphere for the staff.

Synset Examples

The paint will brighten the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brighten - make or become more bright or cheerful

3. VERB

/ ˈlaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become more cheerful or less serious in mood or atmosphere
make more cheerful

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to make someone feel happier

Etymology

From Middle English lightnen, from Old English līhtnian (to illuminate), related to 'light'.

4. VERB

/ ˈlaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtən / (BrE)
to make something lighter in weight or less heavy

Examples
