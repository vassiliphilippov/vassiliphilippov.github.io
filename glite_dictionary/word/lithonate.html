Lithonate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəˌneɪt / (BrE)
A trade name for lithium carbonate, a white powder used in making glass, ceramics, and as a drug to treat certain psychiatric disorders such as bipolar disorder.
trade name for lithium carbonate, a psychiatric drug
medical

Examples

Her doctor prescribed Lithonate to help stabilize her mood swings.
Patients taking Lithonate should have their blood lithium levels monitored regularly.
The psychiatrist discussed the benefits and risks of Lithonate with his new patient.
