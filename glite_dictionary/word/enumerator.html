enumerator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnuməˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːməˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person employed to collect census or survey data, typically by visiting individual homes and recording responses
person who collects census or survey data

Examples

The enumerator visited every house on the street to record the number of residents.
During the national census, an enumerator knocked on our door to collect data.
Each enumerator received specialized training before starting the survey work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey taker - a person who collects responses to surveys
  • Synonym: census taker - person who collects population information for a census

Etymology

From Latin enumerator (counter), from enumerare (to count out, enumerate).
