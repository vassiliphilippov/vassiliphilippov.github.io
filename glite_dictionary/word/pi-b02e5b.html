pi

1. NOUN

/ paɪ / (AmE), / paɪ / (BrE)
the sixteenth letter of the Greek alphabet (Π, π), used in science and mathematics.
the 16th Greek letter (Π, π)

Examples

The Greek letter pi is commonly used to represent the mathematical constant 3.14.
In physics, symbols such as pi are frequently used in equations and formulas.
The professor wrote the capital pi on the board to illustrate the concept of product notation.

Etymology

From Greek πῖ (pî), the sixteenth letter of the Greek alphabet.

2. NOUN

/ paɪ / (AmE), / paɪ / (BrE)
the mathematical constant representing the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter, approximately equal to 3.14159
the mathematical constant 3.14159...

Examples

The area of a circle is calculated by multiplying pi by the radius squared.
Archimedes was one of the first mathematicians to estimate the value of pi.
The teacher explained how pi appears in many formulas involving circles.

Etymology

From Greek letter π (pi), symbol for the mathematical constant since the 18th century.
