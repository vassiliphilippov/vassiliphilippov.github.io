mujahadeen

1. NOUN

/ muːˌʒɑːhəˈdiːn / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn / (BrE)
A group of Muslim guerrilla fighters, especially those engaged in military or religious struggle (jihad) against non-Muslim forces.
Muslim guerrilla fighters in jihad

Examples

The mujahadeen fought fiercely against the invading army in the mountainous region.
Many members of the mujahadeen later became influential political figures in their home countries.
News reports often described the mujahadeen as freedom fighters or insurgents, depending on the source.

Synset Examples

some call the mujahidin international warriors but others just call them terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jihad - A struggle or fight against the enemies of Islam; spiritual struggle within oneself against sin

Etymology

From Arabic مُجَاهِدِين‎ (mujāhidīn), plural of مُجَاهِد‎ (mujāhid), meaning 'one who struggles'.
