reverberant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɝːbɚənt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbərənt / (BrE)
(of a space or sound) having a quality of echoing or being filled with repeated reflections of sound.
having an echoing quality

Examples

The choir's voices were amplified in the reverberant hall, creating a majestic sound.
The empty warehouse had a strangely reverberant atmosphere when she called out.
Each drumbeat sounded deep and reverberant in the morning mist.

Synset Examples

a reverberant room
the reverberant booms of cannon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echoing - (of a sound) repeating due to reflection off surfaces

Etymology

From Latin reverberare (to strike back, to reflect), through Late Latin reverberant- ‘striking back’.
