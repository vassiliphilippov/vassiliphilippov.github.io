endowment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
money or property given to an institution, person, or group as a source of income, especially the capital that supports ongoing operations or activities.
funds or property supporting an institution

Examples

The university's endowment grew significantly thanks to generous donations from alumni.
A large endowment allows the hospital to fund research and provide scholarships to students.
She helped to establish a new endowment for the arts center, ensuring its financial stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fund - money set aside for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Old French *endowment*, from *endouer* (to endow), from Latin *dotare* (to provide with a dowry).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
a natural quality or ability that someone is born with
natural ability or quality

Examples

Her musical endowment was evident from a very young age.
He owed his intellectual endowment to both nature and nurturing.
The school recognizes students with exceptional endowment in science and mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: gift - special ability or capacity
  • Antonym: incapacity - lack of ability or power

Etymology

From Old French 'endouer' (to provide with a dowry), from Latin 'dotare' (to endow), from 'dos' (dowry).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving funds or property to an institution to provide it with a permanent source of income
act of giving funds for permanent income

Examples

The university received a large endowment from a former student.
Her endowment allowed the research center to expand its work.
They established an endowment to fund scholarships for future generations.

Synset Examples

his generous endowment of the laboratory came just in the nick of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - something voluntarily given to another

Etymology

From Old French 'endowment', from 'endouer' (to endow), ultimately from Latin 'dotare' (to provide with a dowry).
