sewn

1. ADJ

/ soʊn / (AmE), / səʊn / (BrE)
fastened or joined with stitches, typically using thread and a needle.
fastened with stitches

Examples

The patch was carefully sewn onto the sleeve of his jacket.
All the pieces had to be sewn together before the dress was complete.
A handmade doll with sewn eyes and a soft fabric body sat on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to join or fasten with stitches
  • Antonym: unstitched - not fastened with stitches

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'sew', from Old English 'siwan', from Proto-Germanic '*siwjan'.
