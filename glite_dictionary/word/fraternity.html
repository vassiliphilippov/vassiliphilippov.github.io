fraternity

1. NOUN

/ frəˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
a social organization at a college or university for male students, often with shared interests and activities
college social club for male students
american

Examples

He joined a fraternity during his first year at university.
The fraternity organized a charity event to support local families.
Living in the fraternity house taught him valuable lessons about teamwork and responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sorority - a social club at a college or university for female students
  • Synonym: brotherhood - an association or community of men, often with a common interest

Etymology

From Latin fraternitas (brotherhood), from frater (brother).

2. NOUN

/ frəˈtɜrnəti / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
all the people who share a particular profession or occupation, regarded as a group; a professional or occupational brotherhood
group of people in the same profession

Examples

The fraternity of scientists welcomed new research in their field.
She was highly respected by the legal fraternity for her integrity and expertise.
The entire journalism fraternity mourned the loss of their colleague.

Synset Examples

the medical fraternity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - an occupation that requires special education or training
  • Synonym: community - a group of people sharing a common characteristic or interest
  • Antonym: individual - a single person rather than a collective group

Etymology

From Latin fraternitas (brotherhood), from frater (brother).
