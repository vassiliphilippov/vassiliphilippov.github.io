haematochezia

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmætəˈkiːziə / (AmE), / hiːˌmætəˈkiːziə / (BrE)
the passage of stools containing blood, typically due to bleeding in the lower gastrointestinal tract
passage of blood in stool (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected haematochezia after observing fresh blood in the patient's stool.
Sudden onset of haematochezia can indicate significant lower gastrointestinal bleeding.
Chronic haematochezia often requires a colonoscopy to identify the bleeding source.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melena - the passage of black, tarry stools due to upper gastrointestinal bleeding

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + chezein (to defecate).
