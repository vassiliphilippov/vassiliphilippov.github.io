Baron Alexander von Humboldt

1. PROPN

/ ˈbærən ˌælɪɡˈzændər vɒn hʊmˈboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈbærən ˌælɪɡˈzandə vɒn ˈhʊmbɔːlt / (BrE)
(1769–1859) A German naturalist and explorer known for his scientific expeditions in Central and South America and for his influential work describing the natural world.
German naturalist and explorer (1769–1859)

Examples

Baron Alexander von Humboldt was a pioneering scientist whose explorations influenced many areas of research.
The discoveries made by Baron Alexander von Humboldt helped lay the foundations for modern geography.
Many places and species are named after Baron Alexander von Humboldt in recognition of his contributions to science.
