waxwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈwæksˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a life-sized figure of a person made of wax, typically displayed in museums
life-sized wax effigy

Examples

The museum is famous for its waxwork figures of movie stars and historical leaders.
She posed for a waxwork artist who recreated her likeness for the exhibition.
Tourists marvelled at the realistic waxwork of the queen on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effigy - a sculpture or model of a person, often made as a likeness
  • Synonym: manikin - a model of a human body, often used for display or demonstration

Etymology

From 'wax' (the substance) + 'work' (crafted item), referring to objects crafted from wax.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈwaksˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a twining shrub native to North America, known for its yellow capsules containing scarlet seeds; also called American bittersweet (Celastrus scandens)
twining shrub with yellow capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

The hedge was covered with waxwork twining over the old fence.
In autumn, the bright berries of waxwork are especially striking against the foliage.
Gardeners often plant waxwork for its colorful yellow capsules and scarlet seeds.
