theocentrism

1. NOUN

/ ˌθi.oʊˈsɛn.trɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌθiː.əʊˈsɛn.trɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief that God is the central and most important aspect of human existence and the universe
the belief that God is central to existence

Examples

Some theologians argue that theocentrism places God above all human concerns.
Her philosophy is rooted in theocentrism, shaping her approach to ethics and life.
Debates between theocentrism and anthropocentrism are important in environmental discussions.

Synset Examples

Edwards's theocentrism suggests an inclusive rather than exclusive posture of God vis-à-vis the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anthropocentrism - the belief that humans are the central or most significant entities

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + Latin centrum (center) + -ism.
