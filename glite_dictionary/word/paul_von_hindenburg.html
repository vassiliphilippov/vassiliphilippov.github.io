Paul von Hindenburg

1. PROPN

/ pɔl vɑn ˈhɪndənbɜrɡ / (AmE), / pɔːl fɒn ˈhɪndənbɜːɡ / (BrE)
(1847–1934) German field marshal and statesman; president of the Weimar Republic who appointed Adolf Hitler as chancellor in 1933.
German field marshal and president of the Weimar Republic

Examples

Paul von Hindenburg played a crucial role in the political transition of Germany during the early 20th century.
As president, Paul von Hindenburg faced immense pressure and political turmoil in the Weimar Republic.
Many historians debate whether Paul von Hindenburg could have prevented Adolf Hitler's rise to power.
