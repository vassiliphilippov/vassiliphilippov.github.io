reluctance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlʌktəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktəns / (BrE)
(physics) the opposition that a material or circuit presents to magnetic flux, analogous to resistance in electrical circuits.
opposition to magnetic flux (physics)

Examples

The iron core has very low reluctance, making it ideal for magnetic circuits.
Engineers calculated the reluctance of the path to optimize the transformer design.
Magnetic flux decreases when the reluctance in the circuit increases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - opposition to electric current (physics)

Etymology

Modeled on 'resistance', from Latin 'reluctari' (to struggle against).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlʌktəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktəns / (BrE)
an unwillingness or lack of enthusiasm to do something
unwillingness or lack of enthusiasm

Examples

Despite her reluctance, she finally agreed to speak at the conference.
He showed great reluctance to leave his hometown and move abroad.
There was a noticeable reluctance among the staff to accept the new policy.

Synset Examples

a reluctance to commit himself
his hesitancy revealed his basic indisposition
after some hesitation he agreed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or delaying due to uncertainty or indecision
  • Antonym: eagerness - enthusiastic willingness to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari' (to struggle against, resist).
