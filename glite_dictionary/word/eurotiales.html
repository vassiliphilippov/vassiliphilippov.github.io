Eurotiales

1. NOUN

/ jʊəˌroʊtiˈeɪliz / (AmE), / jʊəˌrɒtɪˈɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi having a closed ascocarp (cleistothecium) with the asci scattered rather than arranged in a hymenium
order of fungi with scattered asci

Examples

Many species in the Eurotiales produce economically important molds used in food production.
The Eurotiales are characterized by their unique mode of spore formation within cleistothecia.
Researchers have identified new antibiotics derived from fungi within the Eurotiales order.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Eurotium + -ales (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌjʊəroʊˈtaɪəliːz / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈtaɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi characterized by a closed ascocarp (cleistothecium) with the asci scattered rather than grouped in a hymenium
order of fungi with closed ascocarp

Examples

Many species in the Eurotiales are important for their role in food spoilage and mycotoxin production.
The mold Penicillium belongs to the Eurotiales order.
Researchers study the Eurotiales to better understand fungal reproduction and classification.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Eurotium' (a genus of fungi) + '-ales' (a taxonomic order suffix).
