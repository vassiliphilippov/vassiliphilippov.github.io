flesh

1. NOUN

/ flɛʃ / (AmE), / flɛʃ / (BrE)
the soft, edible part of a fruit, found beneath the skin or rind and surrounding the seed(s)
soft edible part of a fruit

Examples

The flesh of the mango is juicy and sweet.
She scooped out the soft flesh of the avocado for her salad.
The peach's flesh bruised easily when handled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulp - the soft, moist substance of fruits or vegetables

Etymology

From Old English flǣsc (meat, flesh), related to German Fleisch (meat).

2. NOUN

/ flɛʃ / (AmE), / flɛʃ / (BrE)
the soft substance of a human or animal body, consisting mainly of muscle and fat, between the skin and bones
soft tissue of the body (muscle and fat)

Examples

The lion tore into the antelope's flesh with its sharp teeth.
After the accident, the surgeon stitched the flesh covering the broken bone.
Her arm was bruised, but the bullet had only grazed the flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meat - the flesh of animals used as food

Etymology

From Old English 'flǣsc', related to Old High German 'fleisc' (meat), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ flɛʃ / (AmE), / flɛʃ / (BrE)
the human body regarded as physical substance, especially in contrast to the spirit or soul
the human body; physical substance

Examples

Some philosophers believe the flesh is weak compared to the mind.
He tried to resist temptation, but his flesh gave in.
In the old tale, the battle between spirit and flesh is a central theme.

Synset Examples

Leonardo studied the human body
he has a strong physique
the spirit is willing but the flesh is weak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body - the physical structure of a person or an animal
  • Antonym: spirit - the nonphysical part of a person, contrasted with the body

Etymology

Old English flæsc (flesh, meat), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch vlees and German Fleisch.

4. VERB

/ flɛʃ / (AmE), / flɛʃ / (BrE)
to remove adhering flesh from animal hides in preparation for making leather
remove flesh from hides (leather preparation)

Examples

The workers carefully flesh the hides before the next stage of leather processing.
It is important to flesh an animal skin thoroughly to produce high-quality leather.
Before tanning, they fleshed the hides by hand using special knives.
