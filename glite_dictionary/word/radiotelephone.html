radiotelephone

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
A telephone that transmits and receives voice signals using radio waves instead of wired connections.
telephone using radio waves

Examples

The ship maintained contact with the harbor master using a radiotelephone during the entire voyage.
Early police cars were equipped with a radiotelephone for quick emergency response.
The researcher made a call from the remote field station using a radiotelephone.

Etymology

Formed from 'radio' + 'telephone'; 'radio' from Latin 'radius' (ray, beam), 'telephone' from Greek 'tēle' (far) and 'phōnē' (voice).

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈtelɪfəʊn / (BrE)
A telephone that transmits sound by radio waves instead of by wire.
telephone using radio waves

Examples

The ship's captain contacted the harbor using the radiotelephone.
With a radiotelephone, pilots can communicate over long distances without cables.
In remote areas, a radiotelephone can be vital for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone - a system for transmitting voices over a distance using wire or radio
  • Synonym: walkie-talkie - a portable two-way radio communication device

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radio waves) + telephone (from Greek tele- 'far' + phōnē 'sound').
