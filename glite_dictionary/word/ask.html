to ask

1. VERB

/ æsk / (AmE), / ɑːsk / (BrE)
to expect or demand something as an obligation or requirement
demand or require as an obligation

Examples

The new policy asks employees to submit reports every Friday morning.
Are we asking too much when we expect students to complete extra assignments over the weekend?
The company asked that all staff members attend the mandatory training session.

Synset Examples

We require our secretary to be on time
Aren't we asking too much of these children?
I expect my students to arrive in time for their lessons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need or demand as necessary
  • Synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, as a right
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00757790-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ æsk / (AmE), / ɑːsk / (BrE)
to request or demand something from someone
request or demand (verb)

Examples

I need to ask you for a small favor.
She didn’t ask anyone before making her decision.
He nervously asked his boss for a day off.

Synset Examples

She asked him for a loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to politely ask for something
  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for something forcefully or insistently

Etymology

Old English ascian; related to Old Norse æskja (to ask).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00754499-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ æsk / (AmE), / ɑːsk / (BrE)
to put a question to someone in order to get information or an answer
put a question to someone

Examples

Don't be afraid to ask if you need help.

Synset Examples

He had to ask directions several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inquire - to ask for information
  • Antonym: answer - to respond or reply to a question

Etymology

Old English ascian, of Germanic origin.

5. VERB

/ æsk / (AmE), / ɑːsk / (BrE)
to request or demand a particular price or condition for something, especially when selling or negotiating
demand a price or condition

Examples

The artist is asking $500 for her latest painting, but she might accept less.
They are asking strict conditions before agreeing to the deal.
When selling old books, he usually asks a fair price based on their condition.

Synset Examples

He is asking $200 for the table
The kidnappers are asking a million dollars in return for the release of their hostage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - to ask for forcefully or with insistence
  • Antonym: offer - to present or propose (the opposite role in a transaction)

Etymology

From Old English ascian, āscian (to seek, inquire).
