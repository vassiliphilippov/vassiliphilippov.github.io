fornix

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrnɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnɪks / (BrE)
an arched bundle of nerve fibers at the base of the brain connecting the hippocampus to other brain regions, including the thalamus and mamillary bodies
arched bundle of nerve fibers in the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the fornix can result in memory impairment and other cognitive deficits.
The MRI scan revealed abnormalities in the patient's fornix.
Researchers are studying the role of the fornix in the development of Alzheimer's disease.

Etymology

From Latin *fornix* (arch, vault), referring to its arched shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrnɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnɪks / (BrE)
An arch-shaped structure in the body or architecture, especially the arched roof of an anatomical space.
arch-shaped structure, often anatomical

Examples

The fornix of the brain plays an important role in memory function.
In anatomy class, we studied the fornix as part of the limbic system.
The vault of the ancient temple resembled a giant fornix in shape.

Etymology

From Latin 'fornix' (arch, vault).
