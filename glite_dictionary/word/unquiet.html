unquiet

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwaɪ.ət / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwaɪ.ɪt / (BrE)
characterized by unrest, disturbance, or a lack of calm and peace
characterized by unrest or disturbance

Examples

After the announcement, the city experienced several unquiet nights filled with protests.
He spent an unquiet evening, unable to shake off his anxiety.
The country has a long history of unquiet times marked by political conflict.

Synset Examples

unquiet days of riots
following the assassination of Martin Luther King, ours was an unquiet nation
spent an unquiet night tossing and turning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; constantly in motion
  • Antonym: tranquil - calm; peaceful; free from disturbance

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'quiet', from Latin 'quietus' (calm, quiet).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwaɪ.ət / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwaɪ.ət / (BrE)
characterized by anxiety, restlessness, or disturbance; not calm or peaceful
restless; anxious; not calm

Examples

She spent an unquiet night, unable to shake her worries about the exam.
The unquiet atmosphere in the room made everyone feel uneasy.
His unquiet mind raced with troubling thoughts long after midnight.

Synset Examples

spent an anxious night waiting for the test results
cast anxious glances behind her
those nervous moments before takeoff
an unquiet mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; anxious or constantly in motion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From un- (not) + quiet, modeled after Latin roots meaning 'not quiet'.
