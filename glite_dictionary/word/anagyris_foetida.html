Anagyris foetida

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈdʒaɪrɪs ˈfiːtɪdə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡʏərɪs ˈfiːtɪdə / (BrE)
a Mediterranean shrub with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, whose crushed leaves emit a strong, unpleasant odor
Mediterranean shrub with odorous leaves

Examples

Botanists studied the strange smell produced when Anagyris foetida leaves were crushed.
The Anagyris foetida shrub can be identified by its backward curving seed pods.
In Mediterranean gardens, Anagyris foetida is grown occasionally despite its unpleasant odor.

Etymology

From Latin: Anagyris (genus name) and foetida (meaning 'stinking').
