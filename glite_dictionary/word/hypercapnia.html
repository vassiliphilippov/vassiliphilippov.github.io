hypercapnia

1. NOUN

/ haɪpərˈkæpniə / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈkapnɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which there is an abnormally high concentration of carbon dioxide in the blood
excess carbon dioxide in the blood
medical

Examples

Respiratory failure may result in hypercapnia and a dangerous drop in blood oxygen levels.
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease can lead to hypercapnia if not properly managed.
Doctors monitor patients for signs of hypercapnia during surgery under anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypocapnia - abnormally low concentration of carbon dioxide in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper' (over, above) + 'kapnos' (smoke), referring to excess carbon dioxide.
