nutlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌt.lət / (AmE), / ˈnʌt.lɪt / (BrE)
A small nut, especially a single-seeded segment of a fruit that resembles a tiny nut, commonly used in botany.
a small nut; tiny botanical nut

Examples

The plant produces tiny nutlets that are loved by birds.
Each flower develops into four small nutlets after pollination.
The botanist carefully examined the nutlet under a microscope.

Etymology

Formed from 'nut' + diminutive suffix '-let'.
