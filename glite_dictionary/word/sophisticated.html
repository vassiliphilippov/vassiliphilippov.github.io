sophisticated

1. ADJ

/ səˈfɪstəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a high degree of worldly knowledge, refinement, and social savoir-faire.
worldly-wise; refined; cultured

Examples

Only a sophisticated audience will appreciate the subtle humor in this play.
Their sophisticated lifestyle was shaped by years of traveling and studying abroad.
He has become quite sophisticated after living in Paris for five years.

Synset Examples

sophisticated young socialites
a sophisticated audience
a sophisticated lifestyle
a sophisticated book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultured - having or showing good education and manners
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or sophistication

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sophisticatus', past participle of 'sophisticare' (to tamper with), from Greek 'sophos' (wise).

2. ADJ

/ səˈfɪstəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
highly developed or complex; having a refined and intricate structure or design.
advanced and complex

Examples

The engineers designed a sophisticated security system to protect the building.
Her sophisticated taste in art impressed all her friends.
Modern smartphones are incredibly sophisticated compared to those from a decade ago.

Synset Examples

advanced technology
a sophisticated electronic control system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple - not complex; easy to understand or do
  • Synonym: advanced - ahead in progress or complexity

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sophisticatus, past participle of sophisticare (to adulterate or tamper with).

3. ADJ

/ səˈfɪstəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
appealing to or characteristic of refined intellectual tastes, complexity, or subtlety; intellectually stimulating or cultured
intellectually appealing or refined

Examples

The film was praised for its sophisticated script and subtle themes that engaged the audience's intellect.
Her taste in music is very sophisticated, favoring complex arrangements and deep lyrics.
They enjoyed a sophisticated conversation about philosophy and art over dinner.

Synset Examples

a sophisticated drama

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultured - showing good education, taste, and manners
  • Antonym: unsophisticated - lacking refinement or complexity; simple or naïve

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sophisticatus' (tampered with), from 'sophisticare' (to tamper with), based on Greek 'sophos' (wise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01336561-s
Synset POS: s
