suicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
a person who deliberately kills themself
person who deliberately kills themself

Examples

He was shocked to learn that his friend had become a suicide.
In Victorian literature, a suicide was often viewed with social stigma.
The authorities found a note left by the suicide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-murderer - someone who kills themself intentionally

Etymology

From Latin sui (of oneself) + caedere (to kill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪsaɪd / (BrE)
the act of intentionally killing oneself
the act of killing oneself

Examples

She struggled with depression for years before ultimately committing suicide.
News reports highlighted an increase in suicide rates among teenagers.
He talked openly about his past thoughts of suicide during the interview.

Synset Examples

it is a crime to commit suicide

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: self-harm - the act of deliberately injuring your own body without suicidal intent
  • Antonym: murder - the unlawful killing of another person with intent

Etymology

From Latin 'suicidium', from 'sui' (of oneself) + 'caedere' (to kill).
