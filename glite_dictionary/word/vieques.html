Vieques

1. PROPN

/ viˈeɪkɛs / (AmE), / viˈeɪkɛs / (BrE)
a small island off the eastern coast of Puerto Rico, formerly used as a United States Navy training site.
island off Puerto Rico

Examples

The protests on Vieques led to the eventual withdrawal of the U.S. Navy.
Many tourists now visit Vieques for its beautiful beaches and bioluminescent bay.
The history of Vieques reflects both its strategic importance and vibrant local culture.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Vieques,' possibly derived from the Taíno word 'Bieke.'
