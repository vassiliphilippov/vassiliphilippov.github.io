cytogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪtoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the branch of genetics that studies the structure and function of the cell, especially the chromosomes
relating to the study of cell genetics
medical

Examples

The cytogenetic analysis revealed an abnormal number of chromosomes in the tissue sample.
Recent advances in cytogenetic techniques have improved our ability to diagnose certain genetic disorders.
A cytogenetic study was performed to determine the cause of the patient's infertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic - relating to genes or heredity

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + 'genetic' (origin, relating to genes).
