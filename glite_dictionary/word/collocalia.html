Collocalia

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɑːkəˈleɪiə / (AmE), / kɒl.əˈkɑː.li.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small swiftlets found chiefly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific, some of which are known for producing edible nests.
genus of swiftlets (birds)

Examples

Collocalia is a genus that includes several species of swiftlets found throughout Asia.
The edible nests produced by some Collocalia species are highly prized in certain cuisines.
Scientists have recently reclassified some birds formerly placed in Collocalia into separate genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swiftlet - a small bird of Southeast Asia, known for its edible nests

Etymology

From Greek kolla (glue) + kalos (beautiful), referencing the birds' nest-building habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.ləˈkeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.əˈkæː.li.ə / (BrE)
A genus of swifts known as swiftlets, birds of the family Apodidae found mainly in Southeast Asia and Oceania.
a genus of swiftlets (birds)

Examples

The edible-nest swiftlet belongs to the genus Collocalia.
Researchers observed several Collocalia species nesting in the limestone caves.
Many cultures harvest nests made by birds of the Collocalia genus for culinary purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swiftlet - a small, swift-like bird of the Collocalia genus

Etymology

From Greek 'kolla' (glue) and 'kalia' (nest), referring to the nests built by some species.
