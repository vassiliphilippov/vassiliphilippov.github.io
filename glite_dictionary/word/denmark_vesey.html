Denmark Vesey

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛnˌmɑːrk ˈviːzi / (AmE), / ˈdɛnmɑːk ˈviːzi / (BrE)
(1767–1822) A freed African American slave in the United States who was known for planning a major slave uprising in South Carolina; he was executed for his role in the conspiracy.
freed slave and insurrectionist in South Carolina

Examples

Historians have studied the legacy of Denmark Vesey and his planned rebellion in Charleston.
A statue commemorates Denmark Vesey as a symbol of resistance and the struggle for freedom.
The trial of Denmark Vesey had a profound impact on laws regarding slavery in the American South.
