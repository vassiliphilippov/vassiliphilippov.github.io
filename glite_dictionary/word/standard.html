standard

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ˈstændəd / (BrE)
a flag or banner, especially one serving as a symbol for a nation, army, or organization
distinctive flag or banner

Examples

The royal standard was raised above the palace to signal the king's presence.
Each battalion marched under its own unique standard during the parade.
The medieval knight proudly carried his family standard into battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design, especially in a procession

Etymology

From Old French 'estandard', meaning rallying place or flag, of Germanic origin.

9. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ˈstændəd / (BrE)
the commodity or measure (such as gold or silver) on which the value of a country's currency is based in a monetary system
basis of value in a monetary system

Examples

The country abandoned the standard of silver in favor of gold in the 19th century.
During the gold standard era, each dollar represented a fixed quantity of gold.
Many economists debate the benefits of returning to a commodity-based standard for currency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold standard - a monetary system where the value of currency is defined in terms of gold

Etymology

From Old French 'estandard', from Frankish *standhard (a standard, flag, rallying point).
