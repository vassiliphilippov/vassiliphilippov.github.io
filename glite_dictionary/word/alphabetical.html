alphabetical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.fəˈbɛt.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæl.fəˈbet.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or expressed by a writing system that uses an alphabet.
relating to an alphabetic writing system

Examples

The language uses an alphabetical script based on Latin letters.
Researchers studied the development of alphabetical writing in ancient societies.
Her thesis explored differences between pictographic and alphabetical writing systems.

Synset Examples

alphabetical writing system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabetic - relating to the alphabet or in order as per the alphabet

Etymology

From Medieval Latin alphabeticus, from Late Greek alphabētikos.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02624611-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌælfəˈbɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌalfəˈbɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of things) arranged in the order of the letters of the alphabet.
ordered by the alphabet

Examples

The list was organized in alphabetical order by last name.
Please sort these files into alphabetical order before filing them away.
All the terms in the glossary are presented alphabetically.

Synset Examples

an alphabetic arrangement
dictionaries list words in alphabetical order

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronological - arranged in order according to time
  • Synonym: alphabetic - using or relating to an alphabet; or arranged by alphabet

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'alphabeticus', from Greek 'alphabetikos', from 'alphabetos' (alphabet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101395-a
Synset POS: a
