diner

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who is eating a meal, especially in a restaurant
person eating a meal

Examples

The diner complained to the manager about the cold soup.
Every diner in the restaurant seemed to be enjoying themselves.
As a solo diner, he brought a book to read during his meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services, especially at a shop or restaurant

Etymology

From Old French *disner* (to dine), from Latin *disjejunare* (to break a fast).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnə / (BrE)
a railroad passenger car designed for serving meals to passengers while the train is in motion
railroad car for serving food

Examples

The train's diner was full of travelers enjoying a hot breakfast.
She reserved a table in the diner for dinner during the long journey.
After boarding the express, they went straight to the diner for coffee and snacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dining car - a railroad car where meals are served to passengers

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnə / (BrE)
A small, usually inexpensive restaurant, often resembling a railway dining car, that serves simple meals quickly.
restaurant resembling a dining car

Examples

We stopped at a roadside diner for breakfast during our road trip.
The classic American diner often features chrome decor and a long counter with stools.
Late at night, the only place still open was a small diner near the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restaurant - an establishment where meals are served to customers

Etymology

Originally from American English, deriving from 'dining car' in reference to railroad cars where food was served.
