Sindhi

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndi / (AmE), / ˈsɪndi / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Sind, a region in southeastern Pakistan
person from Sind, Pakistan

Examples

Many Sindhi have migrated to cities around the world for work and education.
Her grandfather is a proud Sindhi who grew up along the banks of the Indus River.
The Sindhi are known for their rich culture and distinctive cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sindhi - the Indo-Aryan language spoken in Sind

Etymology

From Sind, the name of the region, with the suffix -i forming demonyms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndi / (AmE), / ˈsɪndi / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken in Sind (in Pakistan) and also in parts of western India
an Indo-Aryan language of Sind and western India

Examples

Many people in Karachi speak Sindhi as their first language.
She decided to learn Sindhi to better understand her family's heritage.
The poet recited his work in Sindhi at the cultural festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urdu - another Indo-Aryan language spoken in Pakistan and India
  • Synonym: Punjabi - an Indo-Aryan language spoken in Punjab region

Etymology

From the region Sindh in Pakistan, with -i suffix denoting language or people.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndi / (AmE), / ˈsɪndi / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language of Sind, also spoken in parts of western India
language spoken in Sind and western India

Examples

Many people in Karachi speak Sindhi as their first language.
She is learning to write in Sindhi to communicate with her relatives.
The Sindhi script is based on a variant of the Arabic alphabet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urdu - an Indo-Aryan language widely spoken in Pakistan and India

Etymology

From Sindh, a region in southeastern Pakistan; 'Sindhi' means 'of Sindh.'
