warranter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr.ən.tər / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ən.tə / (BrE)
a person or organization that gives a guarantee or warranty to another party, especially affirming the quality, performance, or legal standing of something
person giving a warranty or guarantee
legal

Examples

The warranter must ensure that all the terms of the contract are met.
If the product fails, the warranter is responsible for repairs or replacement.
Each warranter signed the agreement to confirm their guarantees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - a person who guarantees payment or fulfillment of an obligation

Etymology

From 'warrant' (to guarantee) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
