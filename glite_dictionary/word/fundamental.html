fundamental

1. ADJ

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntl / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntl / (BrE)
forming a necessary base or core; of central importance.
of central or essential importance

Examples

Understanding the fundamental principles of mathematics is crucial for advanced study.
There was a fundamental flaw in the design that caused the machine to fail.
Honesty is a fundamental value in building trust within a team.

Synset Examples

the fundamental laws of the universe
a fundamental incompatibility between them
these rudimentary truths
underlying principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation or starting point
  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fundamentalis (of the foundation), from Latin fundamentum (foundation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfʌn.dəˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌfʌn.dəˈmɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
the lowest tone in a harmonic series, from which the overtones are integer multiples, forming the perceived pitch of a musical sound
lowest tone in a harmonic series

Examples

The fundamental determines the pitch you hear when you play a note on a piano.
Many instruments reinforce both the fundamental and its harmonics to produce a richer sound.
When the fundamental is missing, the brain can still perceive its pitch from the overtones.
