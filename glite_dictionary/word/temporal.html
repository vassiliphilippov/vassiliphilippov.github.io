temporal

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpərəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl / (BrE)
Relating to the material or earthly world rather than to spiritual or religious matters.
worldly rather than spiritual

Examples

The church once held vast temporal power in Europe.
His interests were more temporal than spiritual, focusing on material success.
She renounced all temporal possessions to dedicate her life to meditation.

Synset Examples

worldly goods and advancement
temporal possessions of the church

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiritual - connected with religious or sacred matters rather than material things
  • Synonym: secular - not connected with religion or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Latin temporalis (of time; earthly, secular), from tempus (time).

6. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A noun phrase that specifies the time at which a state or action occurs in a sentence.
linguistic term for time-specifying phrase

Examples

In the sentence 'She left at noon,' 'at noon' functions as the temporal.
Linguists analyze the temporal to understand when actions take place in a clause.
The temporal is essential for clarifying the time frame of an event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locative - the noun phrase indicating location in a sentence
  • Synonym: temporal adverbial - an adverbial phrase expressing time

Etymology

From Latin temporalis, meaning 'of time', from tempus (time).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06344471-n
Synset POS: n
