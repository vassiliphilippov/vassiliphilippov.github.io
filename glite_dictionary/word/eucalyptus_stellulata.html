Eucalyptus stellulata

1. NOUN

/ juːkəˈlɪptəs stɛl(j)uˈleɪtə / (AmE), / juːkəˈlɪptəs stɛl(j)ʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A small Australian eucalyptus tree with rough, dark bark at the base, known for producing a red kino gum.
species of eucalyptus tree

Examples

Botanists recorded the presence of Eucalyptus stellulata throughout the highland woodlands.
The bark of Eucalyptus stellulata is rough and dark at the base of the trunk.
Early settlers used a red gum produced by Eucalyptus stellulata for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - genus of trees and shrubs native to Australia known for aromatic leaves

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek εὐκάλυπτος (eucalyptus, well-covered) + Latin stellulata ('star-like'), referring to the star-shaped fruit.
