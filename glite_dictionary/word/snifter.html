snifter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪftər / (AmE), / ˈsnɪftə / (BrE)
a short-stemmed, wide-bottomed glass with a narrow top, typically used for serving brandy or cognac
brandy or cognac glass

Examples

He swirled the brandy in his snifter before taking a sip.
A snifter helps concentrate the aroma of fine cognac.
The bartender poured a generous amount of whiskey into a snifter for the customer.

Etymology

From 'snift', a dialect term meaning 'to sniff', referencing the way the glass enhances aroma.
