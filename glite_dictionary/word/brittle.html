brittle

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.əl / (BrE)
hard but liable to break or shatter easily; having little elasticity or flexibility.
easily broken or shattered

Examples

The dry branches had become so brittle that they snapped with the slightest touch.
Some plastics turn brittle in cold weather and may break unexpectedly.
Her bones grew brittle with age, making her more susceptible to fractures.

Synset Examples

brittle bones
glass is brittle
‘brickle’ and ‘brickly’ are dialectal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: robust - strong and unlikely to break or fail

Etymology

From Old English 'brytel' (fragile), related to 'to break'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪɾl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.əl / (BrE)
A hard, thin sheet of caramelized sugar, often with nuts, eaten as a sweet.
hard caramelized sugar candy

Examples

She made a batch of almond brittle for the holiday party.
The brittle cracked easily when he bit into it.
Peanut brittle is a traditional treat during the winter months.

WordNet ID: oewn-07615354-n

3. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.əl / (BrE)
(of metal or glass) readily breaking or cracking without significant deformation; not annealed and therefore easily fractured.
easily cracked or fractured material

Examples

The brittle glass shattered into tiny pieces upon impact.
Due to improper processing, the metal remains brittle and prone to breaking.
Handle the brittle material with care to avoid accidental fractures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: ductile - (of metal) able to be drawn out into a thin wire; not brittle

Etymology

From Old English brytel (easily broken), related to break.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01526333-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) lacking warmth and generosity of spirit; appearing unfriendly or emotionally cold.
emotionally cold; lacking warmth

Examples

Her brittle demeanor made it hard for others to approach her with their problems.
He delivered the news with a brittle smile, showing little genuine emotion.
Despite her polite words, there was a brittle edge to her voice.

Synset Examples

a brittle and calculating woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - lacking affection or warmth in manner
  • Antonym: warm - showing enthusiasm, affection, or kindness
