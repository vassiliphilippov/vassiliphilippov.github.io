hertz

1. NOUN

/ hɜrts / (AmE), / hɜːts / (BrE)
the standard unit of frequency, equal to one cycle per second
unit of frequency (cycles per second)

Examples

The frequency of this sound wave is 500 hertz, which you can hear easily.
Most household electricity in the U.S. operates at 60 hertz.
A radio station might broadcast at 100 million hertz, or 100 megahertz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kilohertz - one thousand hertz
  • Degree Higher: megahertz - one million hertz
  • Degree Higher: gigahertz - one billion hertz

Etymology

Named after Heinrich Rudolf Hertz (1857–1894), German physicist.
