Laburnum alpinum

1. NOUN

/ ləˈbɜrnəm ælˈpɪnəm / (AmE), / ləˈbɜːnəm ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A species of ornamental shrub or small tree of the genus Laburnum, known for its bright yellow flowers, native to central and southern Europe.
ornamental shrub or tree of the genus Laburnum

Examples

The Laburnum alpinum in our garden produces a stunning display of yellow blossoms each spring.
Gardeners value Laburnum alpinum for its elegant form and ornamental flowers.
Caution is advised as all parts of Laburnum alpinum are toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laburnum - a genus of small European trees with hanging clusters of yellow flowers

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Laburnum' is the genus name, and 'alpinum' means 'of the Alps' in Latin.
