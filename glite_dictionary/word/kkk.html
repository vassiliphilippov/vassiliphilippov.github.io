KKK

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪ keɪ ˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ keɪ ˈkeɪ / (BrE)
A secret society of white Southerners in the United States that was formed in the 19th century to oppose the emancipation of slaves, using terrorist tactics to suppress Black people.
U.S. white supremacist secret society

Examples

The KKK was responsible for many acts of violence and intimidation against African American communities.
He wrote a report analyzing the influence of the KKK in the early 20th century American South.
Despite efforts by law enforcement, KKK activities continued in some areas for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ku Klux Klan - White supremacist secret society in the United States

Etymology

Initialism of Ku Klux Klan, from Greek 'kuklos' (circle) and 'clan'.
