domesticated

1. ADJ

/ dəˈmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) tamed and adapted for life with humans, especially for farming, companionship, or other domestic use.
tamed for human use

Examples

Maize is a domesticated crop grown worldwide for food and livestock feed.
Many domesticated animals depend on humans for survival.
The domesticated cat differs significantly from its wild ancestors.

Synset Examples

domestic animals
domesticated plants like maize

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wild - living or growing in a natural state; not domesticated
  • Synonym: tame - not wild or dangerous, especially as a result of training

Etymology

From Latin domesticare (to make domestic) + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ dəˈmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
accustomed to home life; comfortable and adept in household routines.
accustomed to home life

Examples

After years in the city, he became quite domesticated and enjoyed cooking and gardening at home.
She's so domesticated that she finds joy in everyday chores and home improvement projects.
He was never very domesticated until he started raising children with his partner.

Synset Examples

some men think it unmanly to be domesticated; others find gratification in it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domestic - relating to the home or family life
  • Antonym: wild - not accustomed to home or human life

Etymology

From Latin 'domesticus' (of the home) + English suffix '-ated'.
