Ixodes pacificus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈksəʊdiːz pəˈsɪfɪkəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz pəˈsɪfɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of tick found in western North America, known as the western black-legged tick, and a principal vector of Lyme disease in the western United States, especially northern California
species of tick, vector for Lyme disease in western US
medical

Examples

Ixodes pacificus is commonly found in areas with abundant oak and brush in northern California.
The bite of Ixodes pacificus can transmit the bacterium responsible for Lyme disease.
Wildlife researchers study the life cycle of Ixodes pacificus to understand Lyme disease risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - small arachnid parasite that feeds on the blood of mammals, birds, etc.
  • Synonym: Lyme disease - an infectious disease transmitted by ticks, causing fever, rash, and joint pain

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Ixodes' (tick genus) and species name 'pacificus' (of the Pacific).
