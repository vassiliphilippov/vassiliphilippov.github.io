moan

1. NOUN

/ moʊn / (AmE), / məʊn / (BrE)
A low sound made by a person expressing pain, unhappiness, or disapproval.
a low sound expressing pain or disapproval

Examples

A quiet moan escaped her lips as she tried to move her injured arm.
The moan from the audience showed their disappointment with the results.
He let out a low moan of pain after bumping his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groan - a low sound made in response to pain or despair
  • Antonym: cheer - a shout of encouragement, praise, or joy

Etymology

From Old English mānan (to moan, lament, complain).

2. VERB

/ moʊn / (AmE), / məʊn / (BrE)
to make a low, long sound expressing pain, discomfort, or displeasure
make a low sound of pain or discomfort

Examples

She couldn't help but moan softly when she bumped her knee against the table.
The old dog began to moan as the thunderstorm rolled in.
Students moan whenever the teacher announces a surprise quiz.

Synset Examples

The students groaned when the professor got out the exam booklets
The ancient door moaned when opened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groan - to make a deep sound in response to pain or despair
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From Old English 'mánian' (to complain, lament).
