ADH

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ diː ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ diː ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus and released by the posterior pituitary gland that regulates water balance in the body by reducing urine production
hormone regulating water balance
medical

Examples

A low level of ADH can result in excessive thirst and urination.
Doctors tested her blood for ADH to determine the cause of her symptoms.
The release of ADH helps the kidneys retain water during dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasopressin - another name for antidiuretic hormone

Etymology

Abbreviation of antidiuretic hormone.
