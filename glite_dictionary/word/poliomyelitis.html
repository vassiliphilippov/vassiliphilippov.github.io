poliomyelitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlioʊˌmaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊliəʊˌmaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
An acute infectious viral disease causing inflammation of the nerve cells in the brain stem and spinal cord, often resulting in paralysis.
acute viral disease causing paralysis
medical

Examples

Doctors in the 20th century worked tirelessly to eradicate poliomyelitis worldwide.
Vaccination programs have dramatically reduced the incidence of poliomyelitis in many countries.
The child was diagnosed with poliomyelitis after experiencing sudden muscle weakness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polio - abbreviation and informal name for poliomyelitis

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek polios (gray) + myelos (marrow) + -itis (inflammation).
