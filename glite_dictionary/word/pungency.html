pungency

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌn.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈpʌn.dʒən.si / (BrE)
A strong and sharp quality of taste or smell; the property of being pungent.
strong, sharp taste or smell

Examples

The pungency of freshly chopped onions made her eyes water instantly.
Some cheeses are famous for their distinctive pungency and bold flavors.
Critics praised the pungency of the author's wit in her latest novel.

Synset Examples

the pungency of mustard
the sulfurous bite of garlic
the sharpness of strange spices
the raciness of the wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpness - the quality of being sharp in taste or smell
  • Antonym: blandness - lacking strong flavor or odor

Etymology

From Latin 'pungentia', from 'pungere' meaning 'to prick'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌndʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈpʌndʒənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being sharp, caustic, or biting, especially in speech or wit
sharp or biting quality, especially in wit

Examples

Her pungency made the debate far more entertaining for the audience.
The critic's review was full of pungency, sparing no one from sharp remarks.
He was known for the pungency of his remarks, never missing an opportunity for a witty jab.

Synset Examples

he commented with typical pungency
the bite of satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidity - sharpness or bitterness in tone or taste
  • Antonym: blandness - lacking strong features or characteristics

Etymology

From Latin pungens, pungent- (pricking).
