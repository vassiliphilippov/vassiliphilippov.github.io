Sicilian

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪliən / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪliən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Sicily, an island region of Italy.
a person from Sicily

Examples

Her grandmother is a proud Sicilian who grew up in Palermo.
Many Sicilians celebrate traditional festivals unique to the island.
He learned the art of cooking from a local Sicilian chef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian - a native or inhabitant of Italy

Etymology

From Latin 'Sicilianus', from 'Sicilia' (Sicily).

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈsɪliən / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪliən / (BrE)
relating to Sicily or its people, language, or culture
of or relating to Sicily

Examples

Her grandmother prepares delicious Sicilian pastries every Sunday.
The film explores Sicilian traditions and family life.
He spoke with a strong Sicilian accent that was easy to recognize.

Synset Examples

the Sicilian Mafia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sicily - an island and region of Italy in the Mediterranean

Etymology

From Latin Sicilia (Sicily) + English suffix -an.
