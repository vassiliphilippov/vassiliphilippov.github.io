Artemisia dracunculus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrtəˈmɪziə drəˈkʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈmɪziə drəˈkʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to southeastern Russia and central Asia, commonly known as tarragon, valued for its flavorful leaves used in cooking.
aromatic herb (tarragon) used in cooking

Examples

Fresh Artemisia dracunculus adds a delicate, anise-like flavor to many French dishes.
Chefs often use Artemisia dracunculus to enhance the taste of sauces and dressings.
The leaves of Artemisia dracunculus are prized for their use in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarragon - common name for Artemisia dracunculus, the aromatic cooking herb

Etymology

From Latin; Artemisia from the Greek goddess Artemis, dracunculus meaning 'little dragon' in Latin.
