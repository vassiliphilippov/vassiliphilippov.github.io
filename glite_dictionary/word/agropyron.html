Agropyron

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡroʊ.pɪˌrɒn / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒ.pɪ.rɒn / (BrE)
a genus of perennial grasses found in temperate and cool regions, commonly known as wheatgrass or dog grass
a genus of perennial grasses (wheatgrasses)

Examples

The botanist identified the grass as Agropyron due to its distinctive seed heads.
Fields of Agropyron are important habitats for many species of wildlife.
Certain species within Agropyron are cultivated for soil stabilization and animal forage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatgrass - a common name for species in the Agropyron genus

Etymology

From Greek agros (field) + pyros (wheat).
