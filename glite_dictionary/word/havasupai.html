Havasupai

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːvəˈsuːpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌhævəsuːˈpaɪ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people living in Cataract Canyon, Arizona
member of an Arizona Native American people

Examples

The Havasupai have lived in the Grand Canyon region for centuries.
Many Havasupai maintain traditional customs alongside modern livelihoods.
As a Havasupai, she shared stories passed down by her ancestors.

Etymology

From the Havasupai language, meaning 'people of the blue-green water'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːvəˈsuːpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌhævəˈsuːpaɪ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Havasupai people of northwestern Arizona
the language of the Havasupai people

Examples

The Havasupai language is currently spoken by fewer than 500 people.
She is conducting fieldwork to record traditional stories in Havasupai.
Linguists are working to preserve Havasupai for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Havasupai people, meaning 'people of the blue-green water' in their own language.
