icosahedron

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌkɑsəˈhidrən / (AmE), / aɪˌkɒsəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure with twenty plane faces.
polyhedron with twenty faces

Examples

The Dungeons & Dragons game uses a twenty-sided icosahedron as one of its dice.
A regular icosahedron is composed of equilateral triangles for each of its twenty faces.
The structure of some viruses exhibits the shape of an icosahedron under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dodecahedron - polyhedron with twelve faces
  • Degree Lower: octahedron - polyhedron with eight faces
  • Synonym: polyhedron - solid with many plane faces

Etymology

From Greek eikosi (twenty) + hedra (seat, base, face).
