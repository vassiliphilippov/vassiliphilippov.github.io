lymphogranuloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪm.foʊˌɡræn.jəˈloʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌlɪmf.əʊˌɡræn.jʊˈləʊ.mə / (BrE)
A swelling or enlargement of a lymph node, typically resulting from infection or inflammation.
swelling of a lymph node
medical

Examples

A lymphogranuloma can develop as a response to certain bacterial infections.
The doctor examined the patient for signs of lymphogranuloma in the neck area.
Untreated, lymphogranuloma may lead to further complications if not promptly diagnosed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphadenopathy - disease or swelling of the lymph nodes

Etymology

From New Latin: 'lympho-' (relating to lymph) + 'granuloma' (granular tumor).
