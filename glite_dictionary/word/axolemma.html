axolemma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.soʊˈlɛmə / (AmE), / ˌæk.səʊˈlɛmə / (BrE)
the thin outer membrane surrounding the axon of a nerve cell
membrane of a nerve cell's axon
medical

Examples

Damage to the axolemma can disrupt the transmission of nerve impulses.
The axolemma contains numerous ion channels critical for nerve function.
Researchers observed changes in the axolemma after the injury.

Etymology

From Greek 'axṓn' (axis, axle) and 'lemma' (husk, sheath, membrane).
