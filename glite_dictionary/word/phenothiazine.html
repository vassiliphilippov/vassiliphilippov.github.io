phenothiazine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnoʊˈθaɪəziːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəʊˈθaɪəziːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound formerly used in veterinary medicine to treat internal parasites in farm animals, also the parent structure for several drugs
compound used to treat animal parasites; drug structure type
medical

Examples

Phenothiazine was once widely used to protect cattle and sheep from parasitic worms.
Modern pharmaceuticals have replaced phenothiazine in most routine veterinary treatments.
The scientist explained how phenothiazine serves as a base structure for many antipsychotic drugs.

Etymology

From pheno- (relating to benzene) + thiazine (a compound containing sulfur and nitrogen), reflecting its chemical structure.
