assortment

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɔrtmənt / (AmE), / əˈsɔːtmənt / (BrE)
the action or process of arranging things into different classes or categories based on type
act of classifying into categories

Examples

The careful assortment of materials made the recycling process much easier.
Effective inventory management depends on the proper assortment of goods by category.
The museum's impressive assortment of artifacts was organized by era and region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of organizing into classes or categories

Etymology

From French *assortiment*, from assortir ('to assort').

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɔrtmənt / (AmE), / əˈsɔːtmənt / (BrE)
a collection of different types of people or things grouped together
collection of various types

Examples

The gift basket included an assortment of gourmet cheeses, crackers, and chocolates.
On the shelves was an impressive assortment of rare and antique books.
The bakery offers a wide assortment of pastries every morning.

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display
he had a variety of disorders
a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - a number of different types of things, especially in the same category
  • Synonym: selection - a group of things chosen from a larger number

Etymology

From French *assortiment* (arrangement of sorts), from assortir (to sort).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08415730-n
Synset POS: n
