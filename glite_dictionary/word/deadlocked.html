deadlocked

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdˌlɑkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌlɒkt / (BrE)
at a complete standstill due to opposing forces, so that no progress can be made
at a standstill due to opposition

Examples

Negotiations remained deadlocked for weeks, with neither side willing to compromise.
The committee was deadlocked 3–3 after hours of debate.
Talks between the two countries are deadlocked over the border issue.

Synset Examples

the chess game ended with white stalemated
the two factions are deadlocked over fringe benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemated - brought to a standstill, especially in a contest or conflict
  • Antonym: resolved - of a conflict or issue: brought to a conclusion

Etymology

From 'deadlock' + past participle suffix '-ed'.
