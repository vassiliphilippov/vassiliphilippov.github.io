to abacinate

1. VERB

/ ˌæbəˈsɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˌabəˈsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to blind someone by holding a red-hot metal plate close to their eyes
blind by burning the eyes with hot metal

Examples

Historical records indicate that some rulers would abacinate their enemies as a form of punishment.
The cruel captors threatened to abacinate the prisoners if they did not confess.
After being abacinated, the victim was left permanently blind and in agony.

Synset Examples

The prisoners were abacinated by their captors

Etymology

From Latin abacinatus, past participle of abacinare (to blind by placing hot metal near the eyes), from ab- (from) + baca (berry, referring to a globular object) + -inare (verb-forming suffix).
