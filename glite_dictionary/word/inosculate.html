to inosculate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnɑskjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒskjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of two or more things, especially blood vessels, branches, or tubes) to join or open into each other; to unite by merging or interconnecting.
join or merge together (esp. of vessels)
medical

Examples

The two branches of the tree inosculated over time, forming a natural graft.
Certain blood vessels may inosculate during the healing process, restoring circulation.
In some cases, the roots of neighboring plants can inosculate underground.

Synset Examples

the blood vessels anastomose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anastomose - (of blood vessels or structures) to join together or open into one another

Etymology

From Latin inosculatus (to enter the mouth of), from in- ‘in’ + osculum ‘little mouth’.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnɑskjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒskjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of two things) to join together or become open to each other, especially by interconnecting channels such as blood vessels or plant tissues.
to join or connect, especially by channels
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor attempted to inosculate the blood vessels to restore circulation.
Botanists noted that the roots of the trees would often inosculate underground, forming natural grafts.
After injury, some capillaries may inosculate, creating alternative pathways for blood flow.

Synset Examples

anastomose blood vessels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anastomose - to connect or open into each other, especially blood vessels or channels

Etymology

From Latin inosculare (to bring the mouths together), from in- (into) + osculum (little mouth).
