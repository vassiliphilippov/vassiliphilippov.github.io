lad

1. NOUN

/ læd / (AmE), / læd / (BrE)
a boy or young man; often used as a familiar or affectionate term of address for a male child
boy or young man

Examples

The old coach praised the lad for his determination on the field.
He was just a shy lad when he first moved to the city.
"Cheer up, lad," his father said, handing him a cup of tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boy - a male child or young male person
  • Antonym: girl - a female child or young woman

Etymology

From Middle English 'ladde', of uncertain origin, possibly from Old Norse or Middle Dutch.
