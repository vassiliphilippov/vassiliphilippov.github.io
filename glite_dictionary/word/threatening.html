threatening

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) darkened by clouds and seeming likely to produce rain or a storm.
(of sky) dark with clouds, suggesting rain

Examples

The threatening clouds gathered overhead, making the afternoon seem much darker than usual.
We decided to postpone our hike because the sky looked particularly threatening.
A threatening sky hung over the stadium, and soon heavy rain began to fall.

Synset Examples

a heavy sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen

Etymology

From the verb 'threaten' + -ing.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (BrE)
Suggesting the presence of danger or harm; indicating that something bad may happen.
indicating danger or harm

Examples

Thunderclouds gathered overhead, casting a threatening shadow over the fields.
She gave him a threatening glare that silenced the room.
The dog's threatening bark kept strangers away from the gate.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - suggesting something bad is going to happen
  • Synonym: sinister - giving the impression that something harmful will happen
  • Antonym: promising - showing signs of good things to come

Etymology

From 'threaten' + '-ing', formed from Middle English 'threten'.
