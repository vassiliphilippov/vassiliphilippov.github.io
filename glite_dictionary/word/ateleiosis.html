ateleiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌætlˌiːiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌætlɪˌɪːˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A form of infantilism marked by physical underdevelopment while intelligence remains normal.
physical underdevelopment with normal intelligence
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with ateleiosis due to her stunted growth and normal cognitive abilities.
Despite his ateleiosis, he excelled academically throughout his school years.
Research into ateleiosis has contributed to our understanding of growth hormone deficiencies.

Etymology

From Greek atelēs (incomplete) + -osis (condition).
