fervency

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜːrvənsi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvənsi / (BrE)
Intense and passionate feeling; great warmth or enthusiasm.
intense passion or enthusiasm

Examples

Her fervency for social justice inspired everyone around her.
He delivered his speech with a fervency that moved the audience to tears.
You could feel the fervency in her voice as she described her dream.

Synset Examples

he spoke with great ardor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervor - intense and passionate feeling
  • Synonym: passion - strong or barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'ferventia' (boiling, glowing heat), from 'fervere' (to boil).
