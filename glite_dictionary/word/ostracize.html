to ostracize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑstrəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəsaɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately exclude someone from a group or society; to shun or expel from a community or group
to exclude from a group or society

Examples

After the scandal, many former friends chose to ostracize him from their social circle.
The community decided to ostracize anyone who broke the club's code of conduct.
She felt ostracized at school because of her unusual interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid someone or something

Etymology

From Greek 'ostrakizein' (to banish by voting with potsherds), from 'ostrakon' (potsherd, shell).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑːstrəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəsaɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately exclude someone from a group or refuse to communicate or associate with them
deliberately exclude from a group

Examples

After criticizing the team's decision, he felt the group began to ostracize him.
Several students ostracized their classmate after the rumors spread.
She worries her peers will ostracize her for being different.

Synset Examples

Ever since I spoke up, my colleagues ostracize me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid someone or something
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet or accept someone warmly

Etymology

From Greek *ostrakizein* (to banish by voting with potsherds), via Latin *ostracizare*.
