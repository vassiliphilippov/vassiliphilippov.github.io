cefotaxime

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈtæksiːm / (AmE), / sɛfəˈtæksiːm / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic given by injection to treat severe bacterial infections in the lungs, throat, ears, or urinary tract
a parenteral cephalosporin antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cefotaxime for the patient's severe lung infection.
Pediatricians sometimes administer cefotaxime to treat complicated ear infections.
After failing oral antibiotics, she was admitted for intravenous cefotaxime therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a group of broad-spectrum antibiotics related to penicillins

Etymology

From cephalosporin (antibiotic group) + -taxime, of chemical nomenclature origin.
