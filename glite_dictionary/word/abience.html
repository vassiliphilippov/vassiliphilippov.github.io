abience

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.əns / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.əns / (BrE)
(psychology) an urge to withdraw from or avoid a situation, object, or stimulus.
urge to withdraw or avoid (psychology)
medical

Examples

In some patients, abience may manifest as a strong desire to avoid social gatherings.
The child's abience toward loud noises became apparent during the experiment.
Psychologists often study both abience and adience to understand approach-avoidance behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adience - urge to approach or move toward a situation or object (psychology)

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'ab-' (from, away) + the noun-forming suffix '-ience', modelled after 'ambience'.
