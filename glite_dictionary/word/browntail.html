browntail

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊn.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn.teɪl / (BrE)
A small brown and white European moth (Euproctis chrysorrhoea), introduced into eastern North America, which is a pest of shade and fruit trees.
a moth species that is a tree pest

Examples

The browntail is notorious for damaging apple and oak trees in New England.
Homeowners are concerned about browntail caterpillar infestations in the spring.
Experts warn that browntail moth populations are increasing this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsy moth - a similar invasive moth species, pest of trees

Etymology

From 'brown' + 'tail', referring to the moth's brown tail tuft.
