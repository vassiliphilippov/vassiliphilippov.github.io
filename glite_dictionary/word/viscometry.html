viscometry

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈskɑːmətri / (AmE), / vɪsˈkɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the scientific measurement of the viscosity of a fluid
measurement of a fluid's viscosity
medical

Examples

Accurate viscometry is essential in the formulation of many pharmaceutical products.
The laboratory specializes in viscometry to test the properties of lubricating oils.
Before bottling the wine, the producer used viscometry to ensure the liquid had the desired consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscosity - the thickness or resistance to flow of a liquid

Etymology

From visco- (relating to viscosity) + -metry (measurement), modeled after words like 'volumetry'.
