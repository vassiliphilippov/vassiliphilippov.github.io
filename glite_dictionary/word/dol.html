dol

1. NOUN

/ dɒl / (AmE), / dɒl / (BrE)
a unit used to measure pain intensity, now largely obsolete
unit of pain intensity
medical

Examples

Researchers once used the dol to quantify subjective pain in clinical trials.
The patient reported a pain level of five dols during the test.
The dol scale is rarely used in modern pain assessment.

Etymology

From Latin dolor (pain).
