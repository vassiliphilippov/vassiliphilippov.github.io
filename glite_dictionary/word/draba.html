draba

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪbə / (AmE), / ˈdreɪbə / (BrE)
A low-growing, cushion-forming plant of the genus Draba, with rosette-forming leaves and small flowers in terminal clusters.
a genus of small, cushion-forming plants

Examples

Several species of draba can be found growing in alpine meadows.
Botanists collected samples of draba to study their adaptation to arid climates.
The small white flowers of draba made a striking contrast against the rocky hillside.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Draba, from Greek 'drabē' meaning 'sharp' or 'acrid'.
