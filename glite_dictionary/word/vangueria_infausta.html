Vangueria infausta

1. NOUN

/ væŋˈɡwɪriə ɪnˈfɔstə / (AmE), / væŋˈɡwɪəriə ɪnˈfɔːstə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to southern Africa known for its edible fruit, commonly called the African medlar.
southern African tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Vangueria infausta is often eaten raw or made into jam by local communities.
A mature Vangueria infausta can reach up to five meters in height in the wild.
Researchers are studying the nutritional value of Vangueria infausta to promote its use as a food source.

Etymology

From the genus name Vangueria, derived from the Malayan 'vanggueri', and Latin 'infausta' meaning 'unlucky.'
