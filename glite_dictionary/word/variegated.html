variegated

1. ADJ

/ ˈver.i.əˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈveə.ri.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having patches, spots, or streaks of different colors; marked by variety of color or appearance
having different colors, multicolored

Examples

The variegated leaves of the plant added a striking touch to the garden.
The artist used a variegated palette to bring the landscape to life.
He admired the variegated patterns in the marble floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many colors
  • Antonym: monochromatic - having or consisting of only one color

Etymology

From Latin variēgātus, from varius (various) + -gatus, based on gignere (to produce).
