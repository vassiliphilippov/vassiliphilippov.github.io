mandator

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.deɪ.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪ.tɔː / (BrE)
a person or authority who gives a mandate or official instruction to someone else
one who gives a mandate

Examples

The mandator issued strict guidelines to the committee overseeing the project.
The contract specified the rights and obligations of both the mandator and the mandatory.
The new regulations were implemented under the direct orders of the mandator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandate - an official order or command

Etymology

From Latin mandātor (one who commands), from mandāre (to command).
