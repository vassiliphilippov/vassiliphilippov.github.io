Xanthorrhoea

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθɔːˈriːə / (AmE), / zanˌθɒˈriːə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian perennial plants known as grass trees, characterized by thick, woody stems and long, narrow leaves, sometimes classified in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.
genus of Australian grass trees

Examples

The distinctive Xanthorrhoea can be found throughout many parts of Australia, standing tall above the surrounding vegetation.
Scientists recently studied how fire affects the growth patterns of Xanthorrhoea species.
The resin from Xanthorrhoea was traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for making tools.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + rhodon (flow), referring to the yellow resin produced by these plants.
