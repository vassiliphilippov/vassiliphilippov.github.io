unsatiably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈseɪʃəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪtɪəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be satisfied; with relentless or unappeasable intensity.
in an unsatisfiable manner

Examples

She pursued her goals unsatiably, never content with her achievements.
The dog ate unsatiably, as if it hadn't eaten in days.
He read books unsatiably, always searching for new knowledge.

Synset Examples

he clawed insatiably at the traditional precepts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insatiably - in a manner that cannot be satisfied

Etymology

Formed from 'unsatiable' (from Latin 'insatiabilis', unsatisfiable) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈseɪʃəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪtɪəbli / (BrE)
to an unsatiable or impossible-to-satisfy degree; in a way that cannot be satisfied
to an unsatiable degree

Examples

He unsatiably pursued knowledge well into his old age.
The dragon unsatiably devoured every sheep in the village.
She was unsatiably curious about everything that happened around her.

Synset Examples

she was insatiably hungry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insatiably - to an insatiable degree, without being satisfied
  • Antonym: satiably - in a way that can be satisfied

Etymology

From 'unsatiable', which comes from Latin 'insatiabilis' (not to be satisfied), with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00378591-r
Synset POS: r
