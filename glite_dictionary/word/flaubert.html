Flaubert

1. PROPN

/ floʊˈbɛr / (AmE), / flɔːˈbɛː / (BrE)
Gustave Flaubert (1821–1880), a French novelist noted for his influential works such as 'Madame Bovary'.
French novelist (1821–1880)

Examples

Many scholars consider Flaubert a master of literary realism.
The novel 'Madame Bovary' by Flaubert is a classic of French literature.
Flaubert's detailed style influenced countless authors after him.

Etymology

From the French surname Flaubert.
