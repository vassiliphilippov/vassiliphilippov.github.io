to opt

1. VERB

/ ɑpt / (AmE), / ɒpt / (BrE)
to make a choice from a range of possibilities; to choose one thing instead of another
choose or select

Examples

Many people opt for online shopping because of its convenience.
You can opt to take the earlier train if you wish.
After much thought, she opted for a smaller apartment downtown.

Synset Examples

I always choose the fish over the meat courses in this restaurant
She opted for the job on the East coast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a resolution
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose

Etymology

From Latin 'optare' (to choose or desire).
