ETA

1. PROPN

/ ˌiː tiː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː tɪ ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a Basque separatist organization founded in 1959, known for its campaign of violence seeking independence for the Basque Country from Spain.
Basque separatist group (1959–2018)

Examples

The Spanish government intensified its efforts to dismantle ETA's operations in the region.
After decades of violence, ETA announced a permanent ceasefire in 2011.
The authorities arrested several suspected ETA members near the French border.

Synset Examples

in 1968 ETA launched a campaign of political assassinations of government officials

Etymology

From Basque ETA, abbreviation of 'Euskadi Ta Askatasuna' (Basque Country and Freedom).
