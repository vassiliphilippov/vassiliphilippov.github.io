accident

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksəˌdɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæksɪdənt / (BrE)
An unexpected event, especially one that causes damage, injury, or harm.
unexpected mishap causing harm or damage

Examples

There was a terrible accident on the highway involving several cars.
She broke her arm in a skiing accident last winter.
The glass fell off the table by accident, so nobody was really to blame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mishap - an unlucky or unfortunate accident
  • Degree Higher: disaster - a sudden event that causes great damage or loss
  • Degree Lower: incident - an event or occurrence, often minor or insignificant
  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose, not accidental

Etymology

From Latin 'accidens', present participle of 'accidere' (to happen, fall out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæk.sɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɪ.dənt / (BrE)
an event that happens unexpectedly or by chance and without apparent cause
unexpected or chance event

Examples

Winning the lottery was a happy accident that changed her life.
Discovering the cure was a lucky accident during unrelated research.
Their meeting in Paris was a pure accident, completely unplanned.

Synset Examples

winning the lottery was a happy accident
the pregnancy was a stroke of bad luck
it was due to an accident or fortuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - an occurrence of events at the same time by chance
  • Synonym: fortune - an unexpected event, often associated with luck
  • Antonym: intention - something done deliberately or planned

Etymology

From Latin accidentem, from accidere 'to happen, befall'.
