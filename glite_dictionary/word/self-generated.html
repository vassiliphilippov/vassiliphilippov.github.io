self-generated

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
Produced or created by itself or by one's own action, without external influence.
produced by oneself or itself

Examples

The machine was powered by a self-generated electrical current.
She felt a self-generated sense of motivation that kept her going.
Many plants exhibit self-generated movements in response to light and gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-induced - initiated or caused by oneself

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'generated' (produced).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00182462-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnɚˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
originating or produced by itself, without external influence or cause.
produced by itself, without outside cause

Examples

The company relies on self-generated electricity to power its operations.
His motivation was entirely self-generated, not the result of external pressure.
A self-generated idea can sometimes be more powerful than one suggested by others.

Synset Examples

spontaneous laughter
spontaneous combustion
a spontaneous abortion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spontaneous - occurring naturally, without external cause

Etymology

Formed from "self-" (reflexive) + "generated" (produced).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02290110-a
Synset POS: a
