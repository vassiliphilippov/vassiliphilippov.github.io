occidentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the ideas, customs, or characteristics typical of Western civilization or cultures, especially as opposed to those of the East
Western ideas or customs

Examples

Modern art was sometimes criticized for its occidentalism and departure from traditional values.
In her essay, she explores how occidentalism shapes global perceptions of modernity.
The museum's exhibition challenged stereotypes rooted in both occidentalism and orientalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orientalism - the ideas, customs, or characteristics of Eastern cultures, especially as viewed from the West

Etymology

From Latin 'occidens' (west, sunset) + English '-ism'.
