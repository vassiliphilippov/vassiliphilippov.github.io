underperformer

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərpərˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˌʌndəpəˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who does not perform as well as expected, especially in relation to their potential or abilities
person who performs below expectations

Examples

Despite her high intelligence, she was labeled an underperformer at school.
The company took steps to help underperformers reach their full potential.
He tried to understand why some talented students became underperformers in certain subjects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overachiever - a person who performs better than expected
  • Synonym: underachiever - a person who achieves less success than expected

Etymology

Formed from 'underperform' (to perform below expectations) + '-er' (one who does).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərpərˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˌʌndəpəˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a company, investment, or person that does less well than expected or than others in the same field
someone or something doing worse than expected

Examples

This year, the tech company was viewed as an underperformer compared to its competitors.
Their investment in retail stocks proved to be an underperformer within the portfolio.
Managers decided to replace the underperformer to boost team productivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overperformer - a person or business that does better than expected
  • Antonym: outperformer - someone or something that performs better than others

Etymology

From 'underperform' (to do less well than expected) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08093414-n
Synset POS: n
