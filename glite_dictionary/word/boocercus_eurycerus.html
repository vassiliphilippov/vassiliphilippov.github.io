Boocercus eurycerus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈsɜːrkəs ˌjʊərɪˈsɪrəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈsɜːkəs ˌjʊərɪˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
A large forest antelope of central Africa with a reddish-brown coat marked by white stripes and spiral horns; also known as the bongo.
species of large African forest antelope

Examples

The Boocercus eurycerus is known for its striking white stripes and spiral horns.
Wildlife photographers travel to central Africa to capture images of the elusive Boocercus eurycerus.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of the Boocercus eurycerus from deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bongo - another name for Boocercus eurycerus, a large African forest antelope
