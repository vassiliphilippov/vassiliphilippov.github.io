Anemone tetonensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni tɛtəˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni tɛtəˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to the Rocky Mountains, characterized by silky foliage and bluish-white flowers
a Rocky Mountain wildflower with bluish-white blooms

Examples

The Anemone tetonensis is a striking feature of the spring meadows in the Grand Teton National Park.
Botanists often seek out Anemone tetonensis for its delicate bluish-white flowers.
Gardeners value Anemone tetonensis for its attractive, silky foliage and resilience in alpine conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windflower - another term for plants in the genus Anemone

Etymology

From genus name Anemone (from Greek anemōnē, 'windflower') and specific epithet referencing the Teton region.
