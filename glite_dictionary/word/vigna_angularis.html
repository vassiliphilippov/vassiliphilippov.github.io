Vigna angularis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡnə æŋˈɡjʊlərɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə æŋˈɡjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
A bushy annual plant native to East Asia, widely cultivated for its edible seeds known as adzuki beans, used particularly in Chinese and Japanese cuisine.
species of bean cultivated in East Asia

Examples

In Japan, Vigna angularis is harvested in autumn for use in traditional sweets.
The seeds of Vigna angularis are rich in protein and fiber.
Farmers prefer cultivating Vigna angularis because it's well-suited to temperate climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adzuki bean - the edible seed of the Vigna angularis plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vigna' (the plant genus), and 'angularis' meaning 'angular'.
