gracility

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈsɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɡrӕˈsɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
elegance and beauty of movement or expression; graceful quality.
elegance and beauty of movement

Examples

The gracility of the dancer impressed the entire audience.
He moved through the crowded room with surprising gracility.
The sculpture captures the gracility of a bird in flight.

Synset Examples

a beautiful figure which she used in subtle movements of unparalleled grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace - elegance of movement; courteous goodwill

Etymology

From Latin gracilitas (slenderness, grace), from gracilis (slender, graceful).
