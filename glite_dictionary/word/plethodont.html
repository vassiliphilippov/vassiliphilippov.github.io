plethodont

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛθəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˈplɛθədɒnt / (BrE)
A member of the family of mostly terrestrial lungless salamanders that breathe through their moist skin and rarely enter water.
a lungless salamander

Examples

The Appalachian Mountains are home to several species of plethodont salamanders.
Unlike most amphibians, a plethodont breathes entirely through its skin and mouth lining.
Scientists discovered a new plethodont species living in the moist leaf litter of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungless salamander - a salamander that lacks lungs and breathes through its skin

Etymology

From modern Latin Plethodontidae, the scientific name for the family.
