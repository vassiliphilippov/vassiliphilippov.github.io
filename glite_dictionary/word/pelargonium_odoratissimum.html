Pelargonium odoratissimum

1. NOUN

/ pɛlɑrˈɡoʊniəm oʊˌdɔrəˌtɪsɪˈmʊm / (AmE), / pɛlɑːˈɡəʊniəm əʊˌdɒrətɪˈsɪməm / (BrE)
A species of geranium with round, fragrant leaves and small white flowers, often cultivated for its pleasant scent.
geranium species with fragrant leaves

Examples

Pelargonium odoratissimum is a favorite among gardeners for its apple-scented leaves.
You can grow Pelargonium odoratissimum indoors as a fragrant houseplant.
Her window box overflowed with the delicate blooms of Pelargonium odoratissimum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a type of flowering plant with rounded leaves and various colored flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pelargonium' (the genus, from Greek pelargos meaning 'stork', referring to the shape of the fruit) and Latin 'odoratissimum' (most fragrant).
