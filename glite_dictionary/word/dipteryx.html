Dipteryx

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈtɛrɪks / (AmE), / dɪpˈtɛrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees of the pea family, known for producing the tonka bean.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The Dipteryx tree is valued for its durable hardwood and fragrant seeds.
Scientists studied the distribution of Dipteryx species across the Amazon rainforest.
Farmers harvest tonka beans from the Dipteryx trees for use in perfumes and foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonka bean - fragrant seed from Dipteryx trees used in flavoring

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'pteryx' (wing), referring to characteristics of the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ dɪpˈtɛr.ɪks / (AmE), / dɪpˈtɛr.ɪks / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees, including species that produce tonka beans.
genus of tropical American trees (tonka beans)

Examples

The Dipteryx genus is well known for producing the fragrant tonka bean.
Several species within Dipteryx are cultivated for their valuable seeds and timber.
Botanists recently discovered a new Dipteryx species in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek dipteros (two-winged), referring to the winged fruit of the tree.
