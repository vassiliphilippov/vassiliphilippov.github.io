pock

1. NOUN

/ pɑk / (AmE), / pɒk / (BrE)
a small blister, pustule, or mark left on the skin by an eruptive disease, such as smallpox
a blister or mark from an eruptive disease

Examples

The doctor examined the pock on the patient's cheek, which was a remnant of smallpox.
Old paintings sometimes depict people with at least one visible pock from the disease.
After her illness, a single pock remained on her forehead as a faint reminder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin or within body tissue after a wound or injury has healed

Etymology

Old English *pocca* (bag, pustule), related to Dutch *pok* (pock, pustule).

2. VERB

/ pɑk / (AmE), / pɒk / (BrE)
to mark or scar with pockmarks or small pits, especially on the skin
to scar with pockmarks

Examples

The smallpox outbreak left his face pocked for life.
Over time, acid rain began to pock the statue's surface.
Years of acne can pock otherwise healthy skin.

Synset Examples

The skin disease scarred his face permanently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - to leave a mark on the skin after injury
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, without marks or indentations

Etymology

From Middle English *pokke*, meaning 'pustule, blister'; related to pox.
