Syringa villosa

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A species of lilac native to northern China, known for its ovate leaves and abundant rose-lilac flowers that bloom in early summer.
species of lilac, northern China

Examples

The botanical garden planted a row of Syringa villosa along the main pathway.
Gardeners prize Syringa villosa for its hardy nature and beautiful early summer blooms.
In spring, the Syringa villosa fills the air with a sweet fragrance.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Syringa from Greek syrinx (pipe, tube), referring to the plant's pithy stems.
