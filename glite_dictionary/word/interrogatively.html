interrogatively

1. ADV

/ ˌɪntəˈrɑːɡətɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrɒɡətɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that expresses a question or curiosity.
in a questioning or curious manner

Examples

She raised her hand interrogatively, waiting for the lecturer to notice her question.
The teacher looked interrogatively at the class after asking whether everyone understood.
He tilted his head interrogatively, unsure of what she meant.

Synset Examples

the baby looked around curiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiously - in a way that shows curiosity

Etymology

From 'interrogative' (questioning) + '-ly'. 'Interrogative' from Latin 'interrogativus', from 'interrogare' (to ask).

2. ADV

/ ɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a manner that involves asking questions or expresses uncertainty.
in a questioning manner

Examples

She looked at him interrogatively, waiting for an explanation.
He raised his eyebrows interrogatively when he heard the news.
The teacher listened interrogatively as the student explained his reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioningly - in a manner expressing doubt or inquiry

Etymology

From 'interrogative' + '-ly'; 'interrogative' from Latin 'interrogativus', from 'interrogare' (to ask, question).
