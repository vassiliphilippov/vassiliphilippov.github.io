cross-modal

1. ADJ

/ krɒs ˈmoʊdəl / (AmE), / krɒs ˈməʊdəl / (BrE)
involving or relating to different sensory modalities, such as sight and hearing, especially in reference to processing or integration of information between them
involving different sensory modalities

Examples

Researchers are studying cross-modal interactions between vision and touch in perception.
The brain's cross-modal processing allows us to integrate sounds with images.
Children develop cross-modal recognition as they learn to associate voices with faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multimodal - involving more than one mode or sensory modality

Etymology

Formed from 'cross-' meaning 'across' and 'modal' referring to sensory modality.
