economy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
the use of time, money, or resources in a way that avoids waste; efficiency
efficient use of resources

Examples

The new design allowed for an economy of space in the kitchen.
She completed the project with remarkable economy of effort.
An economy of words can make your writing clearer and more powerful.

Synset Examples

economy of effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficiency - the quality of achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense
  • Antonym: waste - the careless or excessive use of something, especially resources

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), via Latin and Old French.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
the careful management of money or resources to avoid waste; frugality
frugality in spending or resources

Examples

His family valued economy and rarely made unnecessary purchases.
Traveling by train instead of flying was a wise economy on their part.
She managed her household with great economy, ensuring nothing was wasted.

Synset Examples

the Scots are famous for their economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift - careful use of money and resources
  • Antonym: extravagance - excessive or unnecessary spending of money or resources

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04900982-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
the system by which a country or region organizes and manages its production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services
system of production and consumption

Examples

The global economy has experienced major changes in recent years.
A strong economy typically provides more job opportunities for citizens.
Government policies can significantly impact the health of the economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - system or arena in which commercial dealings are conducted
  • Synonym: finance - the management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large companies

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), from oikos (house) and nomos (law, custom).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08383680-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
an act or instance of saving money by reducing costs or expenditure
act of saving money; cost reduction

Examples

By making a few simple economies, we were able to cut our monthly expenses.
The company introduced several economies to help balance the budget.
Taking the bus instead of driving is a small economy, but it adds up over time.

Synset Examples

it was a small economy to walk to work every day
there was a saving of 50 cents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saving - an amount of money saved; the act of economizing
  • Antonym: expenditure - the act of spending money

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), via Latin oeconomia and Old French yconomie.
