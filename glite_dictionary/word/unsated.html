unsated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪtɪd / (BrE)
not having satisfied one's desire or appetite; not sated.
not satisfied; not sated

Examples

Her unsated curiosity drove her to keep asking questions long after the lecture ended.
He left the table with an unsated appetite, wishing there had been more to eat.
Their unsated ambition pushed them to achieve even more despite their earlier successes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sated - having had one's fill; satisfied
  • Antonym: satiated - fully satisfied, especially with food

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix un- (not) to 'sated', from Latin satis (enough).
