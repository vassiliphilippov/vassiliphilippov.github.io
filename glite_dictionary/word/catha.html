Catha

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːθə / (BrE)
A genus of African evergreen shrubs noted for their thick leaves and white flowers, including the khat plant.
genus of African evergreen shrubs

Examples

Catha is native to eastern Africa and parts of the Arabian Peninsula.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of leaves from Catha species.
The best-known member of the genus Catha is the khat plant.

Etymology

From Greek 'katha', the name for khat in Arabic.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæθə / (AmE), / ˈkæθə / (BrE)
A genus of African evergreen shrubs with thick leaves and white flowers, best known for the species Catha edulis (khat).
genus of African evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Catha genus includes the well-known khat plant, which is chewed for its stimulant effects.
Researchers studied the chemical compounds present in Catha species found across eastern Africa.
Botanists classified the unusual shrub as part of the Catha genus after observing its leaf structure.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Catha', the genus name, likely derived from Arabic 'qāt' (khat).
