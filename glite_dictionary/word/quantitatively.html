quantitatively

1. ADV

/ ˌkwɑːntɪˈteɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ˌkwɒntɪˈteɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to, measures, or is expressed in terms of quantity or numerical value
in a quantitative or numerical manner

Examples

The study measured the results quantitatively to ensure accuracy.
Economic growth can be assessed quantitatively using GDP figures.
The effectiveness of the new drug was analyzed quantitatively in clinical trials.

Synset Examples

this can be expressed quantitatively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualitatively - in a way that relates to quality rather than quantity

Etymology

From 'quantitative' + '-ly'; 'quantitative' from Latin 'quantitas' (quantity).
