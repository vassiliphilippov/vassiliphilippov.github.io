formative

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
having a strong influence on the development or growth of someone or something; capable of shaping or molding.
influencing development or growth

Examples

Her early childhood was a formative period that shaped her personality and values.
A formative experience abroad taught him self-reliance and adaptability.
These years were formative for the company's philosophy and direction.

Synset Examples

a formative influence
a formative experience
the plastic forces of nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influential - having great influence on someone or something

Etymology

From Latin formativus, from formare (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00588059-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.mə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mə.tɪv / (BrE)
(Linguistics) a word part or affix that carries syntactic or morphological meaning, such as a prefix, suffix, or root.
a morpheme or affix in linguistics

Examples

The prefix "un-" serves as a formative in the word "unhappy".
Suffixes like "-ing" are common formatives in English verbs.
Each formative in the sentence carries grammatical meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morpheme - the smallest meaningful grammatical unit in a language

Etymology

From Latin formativus, from formare (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06317470-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
(of a cell, tissue, or organ) capable of forming new cells and tissues; concerned with or related to growth or development.
able to form new cells or tissues
medical

Examples

Stem cells in the bone marrow are highly formative and essential for producing blood cells.
The formative tissue in plants allows them to heal and grow after injury.
During embryonic development, formative zones play a crucial role in organ formation.

Synset Examples

a formative zone in developing bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generative - having the ability to produce or create, especially life

Etymology

From Latin formativus (serving to form), from formare (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00588290-s
Synset POS: s
