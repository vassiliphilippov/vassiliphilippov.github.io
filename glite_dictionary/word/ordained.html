ordained

1. ADJ

/ ɔrˈdeɪnd / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪnd / (BrE)
Having been officially appointed or consecrated as a minister, priest, or member of the clergy.
officially appointed as clergy

Examples

She became an ordained minister after years of theological study.
Only ordained clergy are authorized to perform weddings in this denomination.
As an ordained priest, he leads the congregation every Sunday.

Synset Examples

an ordained priest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lay - not ordained; belonging to the laity
  • Synonym: consecrated - officially declared sacred, especially for religious purposes

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinare' (to put in order, appoint), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ɔrˈdeɪnd / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪnd / (BrE)
(of a rule, condition, or period) fixed or established, especially by order or authority.
fixed or established by order

Examples

The meeting will take place at the ordained hour.
She followed the ordained procedures set out by the committee.
Plans unfolded as the ordained sequence of events was carried out.

Synset Examples

at the time appointed (or the appointed time)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointed - fixed or set officially; assigned for a purpose or time
  • Synonym: prescribed - set down as a rule or guide; established authoritatively
  • Antonym: arbitrary - based on random choice or personal whim, not fixed by rule

Etymology

From Latin ordinare (to arrange, appoint), past participle ordinatus.
