playable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being played or suitable for play; able to be used, performed, or acted upon in the context of a game, sport, instrument, or media.
able to be played or used

Examples

The course was muddy, but still playable after the rain.
She found the old piano surprisingly playable despite its age.
The new game has a very playable demo available online.

Synset Examples

a playable lie in golf
the baseball fan reached out and caught a foul that was judged playable
the ball field was playable
harpsichord music is readily playable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplayable - not able to be played or used

Etymology

From 'play' + '-able', meaning able to be played.
