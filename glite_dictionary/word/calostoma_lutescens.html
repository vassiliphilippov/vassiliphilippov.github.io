Calostoma lutescens

1. NOUN

/ kæ.ləˈstoʊ.mə luːˈtɛs.ənz / (AmE), / ka.lɒˈstəʊ.mə luːˈtes.enz / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a leathery stalk and a globular fruiting body with a pale yellow spore case, found among gasteromycetes.
species of fungus with yellow spore case

Examples

Researchers discovered a rare specimen of Calostoma lutescens in the damp forest undergrowth.
The distinctive pale yellow spore case helps mycologists identify Calostoma lutescens among similar fungi.
Unlike some other fungi, Calostoma lutescens grows a leathery stalk supporting its globose fruiting body.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'stoma' (mouth), and Latin 'lutescens' (yellowish).
