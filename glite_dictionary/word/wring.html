wring

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ɹɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of twisting and squeezing something, especially to remove liquid
twisting squeeze or action of wringing

Examples

She gave the wet towel a quick wring before hanging it up.
With a firm wring, he tried to squeeze out all the water from the cloth.
After a gentle wring, the lettuce leaves were ready for the salad.

Synset Examples

gave the wet cloth a wring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - an act of turning or rotating something

Etymology

From Old English *wringan* (to twist, press), related to German *ringen* (to wrestle).

2. VERB

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to obtain something from someone by using force, pressure, or threats
obtain by force or threats

Examples

The journalist managed to wring a confession from the reluctant witness.
The government tried to wring more tax money from the nation's wealthiest citizens.
She felt he was trying to wring sympathy from her by exaggerating his hardships.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to twist and squeeze something, often to remove liquid or to change its shape
twist and squeeze out of shape

Examples

She had to wring the wet towel before hanging it to dry.
He wrung his hands nervously while waiting for the results.
The laundry was so soaked that I had to wring out each shirt by hand.
