Baudelaire

1. PROPN

/ boʊdəˈlɛər / (AmE), / bəʊdəˈlɛə / (BrE)
Charles Baudelaire (1821–1867), a French poet known for his dark, evocative imagery and influential collection 'Les Fleurs du mal'.
French poet (1821–1867) known for vivid, macabre verse

Examples

Many scholars consider Baudelaire a pivotal figure in the transition to modern poetry.
Her essay explored the influence of Baudelaire on later French Symbolist writers.
I picked up a collection of Baudelaire's poems at the bookstore yesterday.

Etymology

From the French surname Baudelaire, notably referring to Charles Baudelaire.
