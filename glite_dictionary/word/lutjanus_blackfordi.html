Lutjanus blackfordi

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtʃæn.jəs ˈblæk.fɔːr.daɪ / (AmE), / luːˈtʃan.jəs ˈblak.fɔː.daɪ / (BrE)
a species of snapper with a pinkish-red head and body, found in the Atlantic coastal waters of North America and the Gulf of Mexico, valued as a food fish
a species of snapper fish

Examples

Fishermen prize the Lutjanus blackfordi for its firm, white flesh and delicate flavor.
The Lutjanus blackfordi is commonly caught along the Gulf Coast during the summer months.
Marine biologists study populations of Lutjanus blackfordi to monitor the health of coastal ecosystems.
