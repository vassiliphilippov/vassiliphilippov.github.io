patchouly

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃuːli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːli / (BrE)
A heavy, musky perfume made from the oil of the patchouli plant, often used in fragrances and incense.
heavy perfume from the patchouli plant

Examples

She wore a scarf scented with patchouly, filling the room with its rich aroma.
The shop was filled with incense and the distinctive fragrance of patchouly.
Many perfumes in the 1970s featured patchouly as a base note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchouli - the plant (Pogostemon cablin) from which patchouly oil is derived

Etymology

From Hindi 'pacholi', from Tamil 'paccilai' (green leaf), referring to the plant from which the oil is distilled.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃuːli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːli / (BrE)
A small East Indian shrubby mint (Pogostemon cablin) whose fragrant oil from its leaves is used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
fragrant herb used in perfumes

Examples

The scent of patchouly filled the room as soon as she opened the bottle.
He grows patchouly in his garden for its aromatic leaves.
Many perfumes use patchouly oil as a base note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchouli - alternative spelling for the same plant and oil

Etymology

From Tamil pachchai (green) + ilai (leaf), through Hindi and French.
