sand-blind

1. ADJ

/ ˈsændˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsændˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
having greatly reduced or partial vision; partially blind.
having greatly reduced vision
archaic

Examples

The old man was sand-blind and could barely distinguish shapes in the dim light.
Her sand-blind eyes searched the room, struggling to make out faces.
He grew nearly sand-blind after years in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blind - unable to see; lacking sight
  • Synonym: partially sighted - having limited vision; not fully blind

Etymology

From sand + blind, originally referring to blindness caused by sand or dust in the eyes.
