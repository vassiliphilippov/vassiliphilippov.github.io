rapacious

1. ADJ

/ rəˈpeɪʃəs / (AmE), / rəˈpeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having an insatiable appetite for food; devouring or craving food in large amounts.
having a very strong appetite for food

Examples

The children attacked the pizza with rapacious enthusiasm after soccer practice.
A pack of rapacious wolves chased the herd through the forest.
His rapacious appetite was legendary at family gatherings.

Synset Examples

edacious vultures
a rapacious appetite
ravenous as wolves
voracious sharks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry; very eager for food
  • Synonym: voracious - wanting or devouring great quantities of food

Etymology

From Latin 'rapax' (greedy, grasping), from 'rapere' (to seize).

2. ADJ

/ rəˈpeɪʃəs / (AmE), / rəˈpeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Aggressively greedy or grasping for wealth or possessions.
aggressively greedy

Examples

The rapacious landlord constantly raised the rent to extract more money from his tenants.
Environmental groups criticized the corporation for its rapacious exploitation of natural resources.
Her rapacious appetite for fame led her to betray her closest friends.

Synset Examples

a rapacious divorcee on the prowl
ravening creditors
paying taxes to voracious governments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedy - having an intense and selfish desire for something
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'rapax, rapac-' (greedy, grasping), from 'rapere' (to seize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00031251-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rəˈpeɪʃəs / (AmE), / rəˈpeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of animals) living by catching and eating other animals; predatory.
predatory animal; catching live prey

Examples

The rapacious hawk circled above the field, searching for its next meal.
Wolves are known for their rapacious hunting instincts in the wild.
Many rapacious species have adaptations that make them effective hunters.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - living by preying on other animals

Etymology

From Latin rapax, rapac- (grasping, greedy), from rapere (to seize).
