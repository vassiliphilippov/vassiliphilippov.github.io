taxonomy

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsɑnəmi / (AmE), / tækˈsɒnəmi / (BrE)
the science of classifying organisms into structured groups based on similarities in structure, origin, or other characteristics
classification of organisms

Examples

The new taxonomy grouped several species together based on genetic similarities.
Modern taxonomy has become more precise due to advances in DNA analysis.
She studied plant taxonomy at university, focusing on tropical species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging by class or category

Etymology

From Greek taxis (arrangement) + -nomia (method).

3. NOUN

/ tækˈsɑnəmi / (AmE), / tækˈsɒnəmi / (BrE)
the scientific study of classifying living organisms into structured categories according to their characteristics and relationships
scientific classification of organisms
medical

Examples

The taxonomy of plants has evolved significantly since the time of Linnaeus.
Modern taxonomy relies heavily on genetic data to classify organisms.
She studied the taxonomy of insects for her graduate thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the process of arranging or sorting into categories
  • Synonym: systematics - the branch of biology concerned with classification and naming of organisms

Etymology

From Greek taxis (arrangement) + -nomia (method).
