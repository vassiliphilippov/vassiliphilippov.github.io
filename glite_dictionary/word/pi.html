PI

1. NOUN

/ piː aɪ / (AmE), / piː aɪ / (BrE)
the person who is primarily responsible for the design, conduct, and management of a research project, especially in academic or scientific settings
lead scientist of a research project

Examples

The PI is responsible for submitting the final report to the funding agency.
As the PI, Dr. Williams supervised the entire research team.
She was promoted to PI after securing her own grant for the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal investigator - the main scientist or researcher in charge of a research project

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Principal Investigator'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ piː aɪ / (AmE), / piː aɪ / (BrE)
an antiviral drug, specifically a protease inhibitor, used to treat HIV by blocking the activity of HIV protease and preventing viral replication, often as part of combination therapy
antiviral drug for HIV; protease inhibitor
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a new PI as part of the patient's HIV treatment regimen.
Research has led to the development of several effective PIs in fighting resistant strains of HIV.
Combination therapy often includes more than one PI to improve treatment outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protease inhibitor - class of drugs that inhibit protease enzymes, used in HIV treatment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04020369-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌpiːˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiːˈaɪ / (BrE)
a private investigator; someone who can be employed as a detective to collect information and assist in criminal investigations
private investigator (detective)

Examples

The PI followed the suspect for several days before gathering enough evidence.
She hired a PI to find her missing brother when the police ran out of leads.
The movie features a tough PI solving a mysterious case in Los Angeles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private investigator - a person hired to investigate and solve cases privately, not as official police
  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'private investigator'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
