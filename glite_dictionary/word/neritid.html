neritid

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɛrɪtɪd / (BrE)
A marine snail of the family Neritidae, found in coastal waters, with a short spiral shell and an operculum.
marine snail of the Neritidae family

Examples

The neritid clung tightly to the rocks as the tide receded.
Marine biologists discovered several species of neritid along the sheltered shoreline.
A neritid shell can be distinguished by its distinct short spiral shape.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Neritidae (family name), named after Nerites, a sea deity in Greek mythology.
