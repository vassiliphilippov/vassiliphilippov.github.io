ambusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌbʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈæmˌbʊʃə / (BrE)
a person or animal who hides and waits in order to make a surprise attack on someone or something
someone who attacks from hiding

Examples

The ambusher waited in silence until the convoy appeared on the road.
A skilled ambusher can blend into the environment and remain undetected for hours.
Police believe the ambusher was familiar with the victim's daily routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks someone or something
  • Antonym: defender - a person who defends against attack

Etymology

From 'ambush' (to make a surprise attack from a hidden place) plus agent suffix '-er'.
