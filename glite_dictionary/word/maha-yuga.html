Maha-Yuga

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.həˈjuː.ɡə / (AmE), / ˌmɑː.həˈjuː.ɡə / (BrE)
(in Hindu cosmology) a great age, comprising a complete cycle of four Yugas, totalling 4.32 million years.
Hindu cosmological time period (4.32 million years)

Examples

According to Hindu texts, one Maha-Yuga consists of four successive yugas.
The duration of a Maha-Yuga is said to be 4.32 million years.
Many ancient Indian scriptures measure cosmic cycles in units of the Maha-Yuga.

Synset Examples

One full cycle of four Yugas is one Mahā-Yuga (4.32 million solar years).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yuga - an epoch or age in Hindu cosmology

Etymology

From Sanskrit mahāyuga, from mahā 'great' + yuga 'age, era'.
