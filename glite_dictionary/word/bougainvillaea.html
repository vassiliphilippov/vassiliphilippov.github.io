bougainvillaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌbuːɡɪnˈvɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbəʊɡɪnˈvɪliə / (BrE)
A tropical woody climbing plant with bright, colorful papery bracts, often grown as an ornamental vine.
ornamental tropical woody vine

Examples

Their garden was covered in a cascade of bougainvillaea blossoms.
A brilliant pink bougainvillaea climbed over the fence, adding vibrant color to the yard.
She trimmed the bougainvillaea to keep its vines from overwhelming the patio.

Etymology

Named after French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville.
