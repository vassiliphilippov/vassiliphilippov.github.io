russet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌsɪt / (BrE)
a coarse homespun fabric, typically colored reddish brown
a reddish-brown homespun fabric

Examples

In the old paintings, peasants were often depicted wearing russet to symbolize their humble origins.
The merchant displayed bolts of russet alongside finer silks and velvets in the marketplace.
She chose a gown of plain russet for the autumn festival, valuing comfort over luxury.

Etymology

From Old French 'rousset' (reddish), from 'rous' (red), from Latin 'russus' (red).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌsɪt / (BrE)
of a reddish-brown color
reddish-brown color

Examples

The old barn was painted a deep russet that blended into the autumn landscape.
Her russet hair glowed in the evening sunlight.
Maple leaves turn a lovely russet shade in the fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auburn - reddish-brown, especially referring to hair
  • Synonym: chestnut - dark brown color with a hint of red

Etymology

From Old French *rousset* (reddish), diminutive of *rous* (red), from Latin *russus* (red).
