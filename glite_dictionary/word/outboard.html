outboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbɔːd / (BrE)
A motorboat fitted with an outboard motor.
motorboat with an outboard motor

Examples

We decided to rent an outboard for a day of fishing on the lake.
The old outboard handled the choppy water remarkably well.
Several outboards were moored along the dock, ready for the weekend crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outboard motor - an engine mounted externally on the rear of a boat

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbɔːd / (BrE)
a motor for small boats that is mounted externally at the stern and used to provide propulsion
external engine for boats

Examples

The fishing boat was powered by a 40-horsepower outboard.
He repaired the outboard before their trip across the lake.
Many small rescue boats use a portable outboard for easy maneuverability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inboard - an engine enclosed within the hull of a boat
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03864520-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbɔːd / (BrE)
Situated or positioned away from the central line or axis of a vessel or aircraft, especially towards the side.
away from midline of vessel/aircraft

Examples

Maintenance was required on the outboard section of the airplane's left wing.
The outboard engines are mounted far from the boat's centerline.
Place the emergency equipment in the outboard compartments for easy access.

Synset Examples

the outboard section of a wing
outboard rigging

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inboard - located closer to the center or midline of a vessel or aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'out' + 'board' (side of a ship or aircraft), used since the early 20th century in nautical and aeronautical contexts.
