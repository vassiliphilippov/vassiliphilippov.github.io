to catabolise

1. VERB

/ kəˈtæbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈtæbəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) to break down complex molecules into simpler ones, typically releasing energy, by means of catabolism.
break down molecules via catabolism
medical

Examples

The liver cells catabolise excess amino acids to produce energy.
During fasting, muscle proteins are catabolised to supply the body with needed nutrients.
Hormones like cortisol can increase the rate at which tissues catabolise stored fats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anabolise - to build up complex molecules from simpler ones (antonym)

Etymology

From 'catabolism' + '-ise', ultimately from Greek katabole (throwing down).
