heinousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.nəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.nəs.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being shockingly evil, cruel, or morally reprehensible.
the quality of being shockingly evil or cruel

Examples

The heinousness of the crime shocked the entire community.
She could not believe the heinousness of his actions against innocent people.
The lawyer emphasized the heinousness of the offense during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heinous - shockingly evil or wicked

Etymology

From 'heinous' + '-ness'; 'heinous' from Old French *hainos* (hateful), from *haine* (hatred).
