to symbolize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or express something indirectly by a symbol, image, or example
represent by a symbol

Examples

The dove often symbolizes peace in many cultures.
For many, the broken chain symbolized new-found freedom after years of oppression.
The colors in the painting symbolize different emotions and states of mind.

Synset Examples

What does the Statue of Liberty symbolize?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - stand for or depict something
  • Synonym: to signify - be a sign or indication of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *symbolizare*, from Greek *symbolon* (token, sign).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or be a symbol of something; to use a symbol to stand for an idea, quality, or action
represent using a symbol

Examples

A dove often symbolizes peace in many cultures.
The red ribbon symbolizes support for HIV/AIDS awareness.
In this novel, the dark forest symbolizes uncertainty and fear.

Synset Examples

The poet symbolizes love in this poem
These painters believed that artists should symbolize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - to portray or stand for something
  • Synonym: to denote - to be a sign of; to indicate

Etymology

From French symboliser, from symbole (symbol), from Latin symbolum, from Greek sumbolon (token, sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989629-v
Synset POS: v
