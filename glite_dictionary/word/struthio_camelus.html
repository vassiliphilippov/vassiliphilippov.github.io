Struthio camelus

1. NOUN

/ struːˌθi.oʊ kəˈmiːləs / (AmE), / struːˌθiː.əʊ kəˈmiːləs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the ostrich, a large, fast-running, flightless bird native to Africa and the largest living bird.
ostrich; largest living bird

Examples

The Struthio camelus is widely farmed for its meat, feathers, and leather.
Unlike most birds, Struthio camelus has only two toes on each foot.
Wild Struthio camelus populations roam the grasslands and savannahs of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - A large, flightless bird native to Africa; the English common name for Struthio camelus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Struthio' meaning ostrich, and 'camelus' meaning camel.
