Cabomba caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑmbə ˌkerəˌlɪniˈænə / (AmE), / kəˈbɒmbə ˌkærəˌlɪniˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a species of aquatic plant native to eastern North America, with floating and submerged leaves and white, yellow-spotted flowers
aquatic plant species of eastern North America

Examples

The aquarium was filled with healthy Cabomba caroliniana that provided hiding places for the fish.
After learning that Cabomba caroliniana is an invasive species in some regions, she removed it from her pond.
Botanists studied how Cabomba caroliniana adapts to varying water conditions in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Cabomba + specific epithet caroliniana meaning ‘from Carolina’.
