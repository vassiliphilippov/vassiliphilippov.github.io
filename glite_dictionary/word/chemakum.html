Chemakum

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˈmɑːkəm / (AmE), / tʃɪˈmækəm / (BrE)
an extinct Chemakuan language formerly spoken by the Chemakum people of the Olympic Peninsula, Washington State, USA
extinct Native American language

Examples

The last native speaker of Chemakum died in the early twentieth century.
Linguists have studied Chemakum to understand its relationship to other Native American languages.
The vocabulary of Chemakum is preserved in a few written records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chemakuan - a small family of Indigenous languages including Chemakum and Quileute

Etymology

Named after the Chemakum people indigenous to the Olympic Peninsula.
