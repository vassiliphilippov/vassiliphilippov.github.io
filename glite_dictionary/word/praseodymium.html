praseodymium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpreɪziˈoʊdimiəm / (AmE), / ˌpræsiəˈdɪmiəm / (BrE)
a soft, yellowish-white metallic element of the rare earth group, symbol Pr, used in alloys and glass coloring
a rare earth metallic element (Pr)

Examples

The alloy contained praseodymium to improve its strength and resistance to corrosion.
Scientists recovered praseodymium from the mineral monazite using an ion-exchange process.
Praseodymium compounds are commonly used to color glass and ceramics.

Etymology

From Greek 'prasios' (leek-green) and 'didymos' (twin), referring to its green salts and its origin in didymium.
