carom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəm / (AmE), / ˈkærəm / (BrE)
A shot in billiards or similar games where the cue ball strikes one ball and then another.
billiards shot hitting two balls

Examples

He won the point with a brilliant carom across the table.
Her favorite move is a double carom that surprises her opponents every time.
The referee declared the carom invalid because the cue ball missed the second object ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannon - A similar shot in English billiards where the cue ball strikes two object balls in a single shot

Etymology

From French carambole (red ball in billiards), likely from Spanish carambola (star fruit), used as the red ball's name.

2. VERB

/ ˈkærəm / (AmE), / ˈkærəm / (BrE)
(of an object, especially a ball) to strike something and rebound, often at an angle.
to strike and rebound (as a ball)

Examples

The cue ball caromed off the cushion and struck the red ball.
The puck caromed off the post and into the goal.
He caromed around the corner, barely missing the pedestrians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricochet - to rebound one or more times off a surface

Etymology

From French *carambole*, via Spanish *carambola*, referring to the red ball used in billiards.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01601316-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkær əm / (AmE), / ˈkær əm / (BrE)
(of an object) to rebound or bounce off something after hitting it, usually at an angle.
to bounce off after hitting

Examples

The basketball caromed off the backboard and into the net.
During the accident, the truck caromed into a fence before coming to a stop.
The hockey puck caromed wildly around the rink after hitting the goalpost.

Synset Examples

The car caromed off several lampposts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricochet - to bounce off a surface after striking it
  • Synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting something

Etymology

From carom (noun), alteration of 'carambole', from French, originally Spanish 'carambola' (a stroke in billiards).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01896842-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹ.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæɹ.əm / (BrE)
A glancing rebound, especially one in which a ball bounces off another ball or surface at an angle.
a glancing rebound (esp. in games)

Examples

The ball made a carom off the side of the table and landed in the pocket.
He won the match with a lucky carom in the final shot.
After hitting the wall, the puck took a sharp carom back towards the player.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricochet - the motion of an object rebounding off a surface

Etymology

Probably from French 'carambole', the red ball in billiards.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
