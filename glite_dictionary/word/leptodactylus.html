Leptodactylus

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.toʊˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / lɛp.təʊˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of frogs in the family Leptodactylidae, found mainly in South and Central America.
genus of tropical frogs

Examples

The frog genus Leptodactylus includes many species native to tropical regions.
Many researchers study the distinctive calls of Leptodactylus frogs during the rainy season.
Some species of Leptodactylus can be found in both forests and savannas.

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (thin, slender) and 'daktulos' (finger).

2. PROPN

/ lɛptəˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / lɛptəˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of frogs that is the type genus of the family Leptodactylidae, sometimes placed in the family Bufonidae in some classifications.
a genus of tropical frogs

Examples

The genus Leptodactylus includes several species found in Central and South America.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Leptodactylus genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Many frogs of the Leptodactylus genus are known for their loud calls during the rainy season.

Etymology

From Greek leptos (slender) + dactylos (finger).
