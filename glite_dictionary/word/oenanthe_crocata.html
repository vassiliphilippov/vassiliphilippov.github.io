Oenanthe crocata

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnænθi kroʊˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / əʊˈnænθi krəʊˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a European plant with tuberous roots and yellow juice, highly poisonous; also known as hemlock water-dropwort
European poisonous plant (hemlock water-dropwort)

Examples

All parts of the Oenanthe crocata plant are highly toxic if ingested.
The Oenanthe crocata can be mistaken for edible celery due to its similar appearance.
Farmers in Europe are warned to remove Oenanthe crocata from grazing pastures to protect livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hemlock - Common name for several highly poisonous plants including Oenanthe crocata

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Oenanthe' from Greek 'oinos' (wine) + 'anthos' (flower), 'crocata' meaning saffron-colored (referring to the juice).
