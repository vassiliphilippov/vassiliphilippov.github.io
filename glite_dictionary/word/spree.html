spree

1. NOUN

/ spriː / (AmE), / spriː / (BrE)
A short period of intense or uncontrolled activity, often involving enjoyment or excess.
brief burst of intense activity or indulgence

Examples

They went on a shopping spree and bought everything they wanted.
After finishing her exams, she treated herself to a weekend-long movie spree.
Police reported a recent crime spree in several neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - a period of excessive indulgence, often in eating, drinking, or activity
  • Synonym: orgy - a wild gathering with excessive behavior or indulgence

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Irish spre (energy, spirit) or Scots spreath (cattle raid).

2. VERB

/ spri / (AmE), / spriː / (BrE)
to engage in an activity without restraint and with excessive enthusiasm, often for a limited period
indulge freely in an activity

Examples

After finishing her exams, she decided to spree at the mall all afternoon.
They spree on desserts every Friday as a reward for a hard week.
If you keep spreeing on video games, you might neglect your studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - to indulge in an activity, especially eating or drinking, to excess
