s.t.p.

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.tiːˈpiː / (AmE), / ˌɛs.tiːˈpiː / (BrE)
standard temperature and pressure; a standard set of conditions for experimental measurements, typically 0°C (273.15 K) and 1 atmosphere pressure.
standard temperature and pressure (science)

Examples

Gas volumes are often measured at s.t.p. to ensure consistent results.
The experiment should be conducted at s.t.p. for accurate comparison.
At s.t.p., one mole of an ideal gas occupies 22.4 liters.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'standard temperature and pressure'.
