Kazakstan

1. PROPN

/ kɑːˈzækˌstæn / (AmE), / kəˈzækˌstæn / (BrE)
a landlocked country in Central Asia, south of Russia and northeast of the Caspian Sea, historically known as an Asian Soviet republic from 1936 to 1991; variant spelling of Kazakhstan.
variant spelling of Kazakhstan, Central Asian country

Examples

The climbers planned their expedition through Kazakstan's vast steppes and mountains.
Some older maps still use the spelling Kazakstan instead of Kazakhstan.
He was born in Kazakstan before it gained independence from the Soviet Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakhstan - the standard spelling for the Central Asian country

Etymology

Variant of Kazakhstan; from Russian 'Kazakhstan', from Kazakh 'Qazaqstan'.
