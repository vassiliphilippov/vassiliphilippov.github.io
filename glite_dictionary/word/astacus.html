Astacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstəkəs / (AmE), / ˈæstəkəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater crayfish found in Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the family Astacidae.
genus of Old World crayfish

Examples

The European crayfish, classified under the genus Astacus, is threatened by invasive species.
Researchers collected several specimens of Astacus from the river for genetic analysis.
Unlike North American crayfish, those of the genus Astacus are native to the Old World.

Etymology

From Greek 'astakos' meaning lobster or crayfish.
