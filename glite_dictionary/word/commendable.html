commendable

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɛndəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛndəbl / (BrE)
deserving praise or approval for being good or worthy
deserving praise or approval

Examples

Her commendable efforts to help the community earned her public recognition.
It is commendable that you took the initiative to solve the problem yourself.
The firefighters showed commendable courage during the emergency.

Synset Examples

applaudable efforts to save the environment
a commendable sense of purpose
laudable motives of improving housing conditions
a significant and praiseworthy increase in computer intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudable - deserving praise or commendation
  • Synonym: praiseworthy - worthy of praise
  • Antonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or criticism

Etymology

From Latin 'commendare' (to praise, recommend) via Late Latin 'commendabilis'.
