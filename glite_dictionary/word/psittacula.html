Psittacula

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtækjʊlə / (AmE), / sɪˈtækjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, known as Afro-Asian ringnecked parakeets.
genus of Afro-Asian ringnecked parakeets

Examples

The Psittacula genus includes several brightly colored parakeet species.
Some species in the Psittacula group are popular as pets due to their intelligence.
The Alexandrine parakeet is a well-known member of the Psittacula genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parakeet - a small to medium-sized parrot, especially from the genus Psittacula

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of Latin 'psittacus' (parrot), from Greek 'psittakos'.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈtækjələ / (AmE), / sɪˈtækjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, known as Afro-Asian ringnecked parakeets, including several species with brightly colored plumage
genus of ringnecked parakeets

Examples

The Psittacula genus includes popular pet parakeets such as the Indian ringneck.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of various Psittacula species in their natural habitats.
Several members of Psittacula are known for their striking green plumage and red beaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parakeet - a small to medium-sized parrot with a long tail

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of Latin 'psittacus' meaning 'parrot'.
