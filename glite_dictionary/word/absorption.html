absorption

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the body takes up nutrients from digested food into the bloodstream or tissues
uptake of nutrients after digestion
medical

Examples

Vitamin C enhances the absorption of iron from plant-based foods.
Poor absorption in the intestines can lead to nutritional deficiencies.
After digestion, the nutrients undergo absorption in the small intestine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - the process of incorporating absorbed nutrients into body tissues

Etymology

From Latin absorptio (a sucking in), from absorbere (to swallow up).

3. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔr.pʃən / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːp.ʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The process by which incident radiated energy is taken up and retained by a medium, rather than being reflected or transmitted.
uptake of energy by a medium (physics)

Examples

The absorption of sunlight by Earth's atmosphere warms the planet's surface.
X-rays have high absorption rates in dense materials like bone.
The experiment measured the absorption of infrared radiation by water vapor.

Synset Examples

the absorption of photons by atoms or molecules

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflection - the process by which energy is bounced back from a surface instead of being absorbed
  • Antonym: transmission - the process by which energy passes through a medium instead of being absorbed

Etymology

From Latin absorptio (a swallowing up), from absorbere (to swallow up).

4. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrpʃən, æbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən, æbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
In chemistry, the process by which one substance is taken up and incorporated into another, such as a liquid or gas being dissolved by a solid or liquid.
process of taking up one substance by another (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The absorption of water by the sponge occurs rapidly due to its porous structure.
Activated charcoal is known for its absorption of toxins in the digestive system.
The experiment showed that the absorption of the gas increased as the temperature rose.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adsorption - the process in which atoms, ions, or molecules adhere to a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'absorptio', from 'absorbere' meaning 'to suck in'.
