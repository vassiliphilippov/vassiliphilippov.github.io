Michaelmastide

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkəlməsˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkəlmɪsˌtaɪd / (BrE)
The period or season around Michaelmas, the feast of St. Michael (September 29), especially as observed in former times.
season around Michaelmas, September 29
archaic

Examples

The harvest festival fell during Michaelmastide, bringing the whole village together in celebration.
In old England, Michaelmastide marked the time for hiring new servants and settling rents.
During Michaelmastide, the days grew shorter and preparations for winter began.

Etymology

From Michaelmas (the feast of St. Michael) + tide (season, period).
