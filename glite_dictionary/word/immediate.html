immediate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
very close or connected in space or time; happening or situated without anything or anyone between.
very close in space or time

Examples

She called the police for immediate assistance after the accident.
The store is in the immediate vicinity of the train station.
We need to address this problem in the immediate future.

Synset Examples

contiguous events
immediate contact
the immediate vicinity
the immediate past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contiguous - sharing a common border; touching
  • Synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else

Etymology

From Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between, direct).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
(of a process or effect) having no intervening medium; direct.
having no intervening medium; direct

Examples

His decision had an immediate impact on the outcome of the negotiations.
The substance produces an immediate reaction when it comes into contact with air.
The doctor recommended immediate treatment to prevent further complications.

Synset Examples

an immediate influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - without intervening factors or steps
  • Antonym: mediate - acting through an intervening agency or medium

Etymology

From Latin immediatus (without anything between), from in- (not) + mediatus (intervening).

done at once, without delay

4. ADJ

/ ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
(of a result, cause, etc.) occurring directly before or after something else, without anything or anyone in between.
occurring directly before or after

Examples

An immediate cause of stress in the workplace is unclear communication.
The doctor focused on treating the immediate effects of the injury before addressing long-term concerns.

Related Terms

5. ADJ

/ ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
existing or occurring at the present moment or in the present place; happening, being, or applied without delay.
of the present time or place

Examples

The immediate concern was ensuring everyone's safety during the emergency.
Only authorized personnel are allowed in the immediate area around the construction site.
We must address the immediate needs of the community before planning long-term projects.

Synset Examples

the immediate revisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - belonging to the present time; happening now
  • Synonym: instant - happening or existing at once; immediate
  • Antonym: delayed - happening or arriving later than expected or usual

Etymology

From Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between, direct), from 'in-' (not) + 'mediatus' (intervening), from 'medius' (middle).
