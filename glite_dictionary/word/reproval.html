reproval

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈproʊvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈprəʊvəl / (BrE)
an expression of criticism or disapproval
expression of criticism or disapproval

Examples

Despite the gentle reproval, he did not change his behavior.
Her parents’ reproval was enough to make her rethink her actions.
The teacher offered a mild reproval instead of a harsh punishment.

Synset Examples

he had to take the rebuke with a smile on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp criticism or disapproval
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'reprove', ultimately from Old French *reprover* and Latin *reprobare* (disapprove).
