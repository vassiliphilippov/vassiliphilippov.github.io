fraternization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrætərnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrætənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of associating with others in a friendly or brotherly way, especially when such association is discouraged, such as with an enemy or opposing group
friendly association, esp. with an enemy

Examples

Military rules strictly prohibit fraternization between officers and enlisted personnel.
During the occupation, any fraternization with local civilians was discouraged by the authorities.
The manager warned the employees that fraternization with competitors could lead to disciplinary action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendship - close association based on mutual affection
  • Antonym: enmity - the state of being actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From French 'fraternisation', from Latin 'frater' meaning 'brother'.
