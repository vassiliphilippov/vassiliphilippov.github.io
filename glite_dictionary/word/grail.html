grail

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪl / (AmE), / ɡreɪl / (BrE)
the cup or chalice said to have been used by Jesus at the Last Supper and sought after in medieval legend, especially in the quest for the Holy Grail
the legendary cup from the Last Supper

Examples

The knights embarked on a quest to find the legendary grail.
Some believe that the grail holds mystical powers.
The story of King Arthur often involves the search for the grail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Grail - the cup believed to have been used by Christ at the Last Supper, also representing a sought-after object

Etymology

From Old French *graal*, from Medieval Latin *gradalis* (a dish, cup).

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪl / (AmE), / ɡreɪl / (BrE)
an ultimate goal or object of a prolonged and difficult search or pursuit
an ultimate goal or object of pursuit

Examples

For many scientists, a theory that unifies gravity and quantum mechanics is the grail of modern physics.
Finding a cure for cancer remains the medical grail for researchers worldwide.
For years, the perfect recipe was his personal grail, always just out of reach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holy grail - a cup or chalice sought in medieval legend, especially by the Knights of the Round Table

Etymology

From Old French *graal*, from Medieval Latin *gradalis* (a dish, cup, or goblet). The figurative sense arose from the legend of the Holy Grail.
