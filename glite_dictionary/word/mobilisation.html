mobilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊbɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of assembling and preparing troops, resources, or people for war or another emergency
preparation and assembly for war or emergency
british

Examples

The government ordered the mobilisation of all available troops along the border.
Rapid mobilisation of resources was crucial during the natural disaster.
The country's extensive mobilisation efforts surprised international observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobilization - preparation and assembly for war or emergency (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'mobilisation', from 'mobiliser' (make mobile).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊ.bə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊ.bɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of gathering and organizing people or resources for use or action, especially in preparation for a particular purpose or event
gathering and organizing for action

Examples

The government ordered the mobilisation of military units along the border.
Rapid mobilisation of volunteers helped control the flooding in the city.
Effective disaster response relies on the mobilisation of local and national resources.

Synset Examples

mobilization of the country's economic resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobilization - American English spelling of 'mobilisation'
  • Antonym: demobilisation - the act of standing down or disbanding forces or activities

Etymology

From French mobilisation, from mobiliser (to mobilize), ultimately from Latin mobilis (movable).
