Kalapooia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈpuːiə / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈpuːiə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people of the Willamette Basin region in Oregon, United States.
a Native American group from Oregon

Examples

The Kalapooia played a vital role in the region's trade networks long before European settlement.
Anthropologists have studied the language and traditions of the Kalapooia people.
Several towns in Oregon honor the legacy of the Kalapooia by bearing their name.

Etymology

From the tribe's self-designation in their language, also rendered as Calapooia or Kalapuya.
