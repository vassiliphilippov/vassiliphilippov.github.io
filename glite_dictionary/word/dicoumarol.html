dicoumarol

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkuːməˌrɔl / (AmE), / dɪˈkuːmərəʊl / (BrE)
an anticoagulant drug, formerly widely used, now largely replaced by warfarin
anticoagulant drug, superseded by warfarin
medical

Examples

The patient was previously treated with dicoumarol to prevent blood clots.
With the advent of warfarin, dicoumarol became less commonly prescribed.
Researchers studied the effects of dicoumarol on coagulation in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: warfarin - a commonly used anticoagulant drug

Etymology

From di- (two) + coumarin (a fragrant organic compound).
