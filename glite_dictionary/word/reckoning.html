reckoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
a bill or statement of money owed for goods or services received
bill for an amount due

Examples

After dinner, the waiter brought the reckoning to the table.
He asked for the reckoning before leaving the inn.
Let us settle the reckoning before we depart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - a written statement showing how much money is owed for goods or services
  • Synonym: invoice - a list of goods sent or services provided, with a statement of the sum due

Etymology

From Middle English 'rekenyng', from Old English 'gerecenian' (to explain or recount), influenced by 'reckon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of calculating or estimating something, especially using numbers or quantities
calculation; estimation using numbers

Examples

The navigator's reckoning was based on the ship's speed and heading.
After a quick reckoning, he realized they were short on funds.
The ancient method of reckoning required skill in mental arithmetic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics; computation

Etymology

From Old English *gerecenian* (to explain), later influenced by Middle English *reknen* (to count).
