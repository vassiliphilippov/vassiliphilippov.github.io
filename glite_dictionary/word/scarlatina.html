scarlatina

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɑːr.ləˈtiː.nə / (AmE), / ˌskɑː.ləˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease, especially affecting children, characterized by fever and a red rash; scarlet fever
scarlet fever; infectious disease with red rash
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with scarlatina after observing the rash and high fever.
In the nineteenth century, scarlatina was a common and sometimes deadly childhood illness.
Her parents were relieved when the symptoms of scarlatina began to improve with treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet fever - another name for scarlatina, an infectious disease with characteristic rash

Etymology

From Italian *scarlattina*, diminutive of *scarlatto* (scarlet).
