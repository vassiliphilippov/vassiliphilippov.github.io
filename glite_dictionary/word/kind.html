kind

1. ADJ

/ kaɪnd / (AmE), / kaɪnd / (BrE)
Showing a gentle and forgiving nature, especially under provocation or in the face of mistakes.
gentle and forgiving in nature

Examples

Despite the rude comment, she remained kind and offered to help.
He is always kind to his coworkers, even when they make mistakes.
Our teacher was very kind about the misunderstanding during class.

Synset Examples

our neighbor was very kind about the window our son broke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassionate - feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Antonym: cruel - having or showing a desire to cause pain or suffering

Etymology

From Old English cynd(e), gecynd (nature, race, kind), related to 'kin'.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kaɪnd / (AmE), / kaɪnd / (BrE)
(of an environment or condition) agreeable or beneficial; conducive to comfort or well-being.
agreeable or beneficial (of conditions)

Examples

The cool sea breeze was especially kind to the hikers after their long trek.
This lotion is kind to sensitive skin, making it suitable for everyday use.
Mountain weather can be unpredictable, but today it was unusually kind for our climb.

Synset Examples

a dry climate kind to asthmatics
the genial sunshine
hot summer pavements are anything but kind to the feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentle - moderate; not harsh or severe (of weather, etc.)
  • Antonym: harsh - severe or unpleasant (of conditions)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01245155-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ kaɪnd / (AmE), / kaɪnd / (BrE)
A group or category of things or people that share similar characteristics or qualities.
type or category

Examples

There are several kinds of fruit available at the market.
She is not the kind of person who gives up easily.
This kind of music is very popular among teenagers.

Synset Examples

sculpture is a form of art
what kinds of desserts are there?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type - category of people or things having common characteristics
  • Synonym: sort - a category of things distinguished by a common feature
  • Synonym: species - group of living organisms consisting of similar individuals

Etymology

From Old English *cynd* (‘nature, kind, species’), from Proto-Germanic *kundiz*, related to 'kin'.

4. ADJ

/ kaɪnd / (AmE), / kaɪnd / (BrE)
having or showing a gentle, considerate, and helpful nature; caring and compassionate
gentle, caring, and considerate

Examples

She was always kind to everyone she met, offering help whenever she could.
His kind words comforted her after a difficult day.
It was very kind of you to bring soup when I was sick.

Synset Examples

kind to sick patients
a kind master
kind words showing understanding and sympathy
thanked her for her kind letter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unkind - not having or showing sympathy or kindness
  • Synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

From Old English 'gecynde', meaning 'natural, native', later developing the sense of 'kindly, compassionate'.
