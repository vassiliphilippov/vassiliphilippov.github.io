drawn

1. ADJ

/ drɔn / (AmE), / drɔːn / (BrE)
(of curtains or draperies) closed or pulled shut, blocking light or view.
curtains/draperies pulled shut

Examples

She preferred to keep the drawn curtains closed on hot afternoons.
When I entered, the living room was dark, with all the drawn drapes blocking the sunlight.
Behind the drawn shades, the room felt quiet and private.

Synset Examples

the drawn draperies kept direct sunlight from fading the rug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - not closed; curtains pulled apart

Etymology

Past participle of 'draw', from Old English 'dragan' (to pull, drag).

2. ADJ

/ drɔn / (AmE), / drɔːn / (BrE)
(of a person's face) looking tired, worried, or suffering, often noticeably pale or tense.
looking tired or strained (of a face)

Examples

After several nights without sleep, her face looked noticeably drawn.
He glanced up, his features drawn with worry over the upcoming exam.
The stress of the past few months had left him appearing pale and drawn.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue or worry
  • Synonym: careworn - appearing tired and unhappy because of prolonged worry
  • Antonym: radiant - showing health and happiness

Etymology

Past participle of 'draw', used figuratively for being drawn tight or tense.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02441812-s
Synset POS: s
