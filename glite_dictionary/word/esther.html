Esther

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament that tells the story of a Jewish woman who became queen of Persia and saved her people from massacre
Old Testament book about a Jewish queen

Examples

The story of Esther is read each year during the Jewish festival of Purim.
Many biblical scholars study the Book of Esther for its themes of courage and identity.
Esther played a crucial role in saving the Jewish people from danger in Persia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Esther - Another name for the same Old Testament book

Etymology

From Hebrew *Estēr*, via Greek and Latin.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a Jewish woman who became the queen of Persia and saved her people from a massacre, commemorated by the Jewish festival of Purim.
Jewish queen of Persia in the Bible

Examples

The story of Esther is read aloud during the festival of Purim.
Many artists have depicted Esther approaching the king in their paintings.
Jews celebrate the courage of Esther as a symbol of faith and bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Purim - Jewish festival commemorating Esther's saving of the Jews

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Estēr', originally from Old Persian.
