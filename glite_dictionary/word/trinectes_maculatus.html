Trinectes maculatus

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈnɛk.tiːz ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / traɪˈnɛk.tiːz ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A small, flatfish species found in coastal streams of North and Central America, commonly known as the hogchoker.
species of flatfish (hogchoker)

Examples

The Trinectes maculatus is commonly found along the Atlantic coast of the United States.
Because of its tough skin and small size, Trinectes maculatus is not considered a good food fish.
Marine biologists often study Trinectes maculatus to monitor ecosystem health in estuaries.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Trinectes' referring to the genus, and 'maculatus' meaning 'spotted'.
