influenza

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfluˈɛnzə / (AmE), / ˌɪnfluˈenzə / (BrE)
an acute, highly contagious viral infection causing fever, aches, and respiratory symptoms; also known as 'the flu'
contagious viral infection; flu
medical

Examples

She stayed home from work because she caught influenza from a coworker.
Every winter, hospitals prepare for an increase in influenza cases.
A vaccine can help protect you from the most common strains of influenza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flu - common informal term for influenza

Etymology

From Italian 'influenza' (influence), originally referring to the influence of the stars on epidemics.
