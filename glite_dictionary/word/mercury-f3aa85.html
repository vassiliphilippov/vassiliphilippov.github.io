mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
the temperature as measured by a mercury thermometer, often used figuratively to refer to temperature in general
temperature reading on a mercury thermometer

Examples

The mercury hit 100 degrees during the heatwave last summer.
As winter set in, the mercury dropped to below freezing every night.
When the sun came out, the mercury quickly began to rise.

Synset Examples

the mercury was falling rapidly

Etymology

From the element mercury, commonly used in thermometers due to its thermal properties.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
a heavy, silvery, toxic metallic element (symbol Hg) that is liquid at ordinary temperatures, used in thermometers and various industrial processes
a metallic element, liquid at room temperature

Examples

Spilled mercury from the broken thermometer needed to be cleaned up carefully.
Industrial pollution sometimes releases mercury into rivers, posing serious health risks.
The element mercury is unique because it remains liquid at room temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicksilver - another name for the element mercury

Etymology

From Latin Mercurius, the name of the Roman god; also used for the planet and the element due to quicksilver's mobility.
