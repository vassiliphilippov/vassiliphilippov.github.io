Mercurialis

1. NOUN

/ mɜrˌkjʊriˈælɪs / (AmE), / mɜːˌkjʊəriˈælɪs / (BrE)
a genus of slender herbaceous plants in the family Euphorbiaceae, commonly known as dog's mercury.
plant genus in the family Euphorbiaceae

Examples

Mercurialis is commonly found in woodland areas across Europe.
The leaves of Mercurialis can be mistaken for those of edible plants.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several Mercurialis species.

Etymology

From Latin Mercurialis, named after the Roman god Mercury, possibly due to the plant's supposed medicinal properties.

2. NOUN

/ mərˌkjʊəriˈælɪs / (AmE), / məːˌkjʊərɪˈɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a genus of slender herbaceous plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), native to Europe and parts of Asia
genus of herbaceous plants in spurge family

Examples

Mercurialis species are often found growing in woodland areas throughout Europe.
The biologist collected samples from the Mercurialis genus for further study.
Some members of Mercurialis have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Etymology

From Latin 'mercurialis', referring to the planet Mercury; in reference to the plant's supposed medicinal properties connected with Mercury in Roman mythology.
