pluralisation

1. NOUN

/ plʊr.ə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / plʊə.rə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a word or form plural; the attribution of plurality to something
the act of making plural

Examples

The pluralisation of English nouns usually involves adding '-s' or '-es'.
Language teachers often explain the rules for pluralisation early on in the course.
Incorrect pluralisation can lead to confusion in written communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluralization - American English spelling of 'pluralisation'

Etymology

From Latin 'pluralis' (plural) + English suffix '-ation'.
