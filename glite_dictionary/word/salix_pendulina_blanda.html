Salix pendulina blanda

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks pɛnˈdjuːlɪnə ˈblændə / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks pɛnˈdjuːlɪnə ˈblændə / (BrE)
A hybrid variety of willow tree, typically not strongly weeping in appearance.
hybrid variety of willow tree

Examples

The Salix pendulina blanda is often planted in parks for its ornamental value.
Unlike many other willows, the Salix pendulina blanda is not strongly weeping in habit.
Gardeners appreciate the Salix pendulina blanda for its resilience and moderate growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - deciduous tree or shrub with slender branches and narrow leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning willow, 'pendulina' meaning hanging, and 'blanda' meaning charming or mild.
