titterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪt̬ɚər / (AmE), / ˈtɪtərə / (BrE)
a person who laughs in a nervous, restrained, or self-conscious manner
person who laughs nervously

Examples

The group of titterers at the back of the classroom struggled to contain their laughter.
As soon as the teacher made a joke, a lone titterer broke the silence.
At the awkward pause, several titterers couldn't help but let out nervous giggles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - a light, silly laugh

Etymology

From 'titter' (to give a short, nervous laugh) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
