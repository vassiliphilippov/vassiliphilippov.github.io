shamelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪmləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being without shame or embarrassment, often displaying immodest or bold behavior
lack of shame or embarrassment

Examples

Her shamelessness shocked everyone at the formal dinner.
He handled the criticism with complete shamelessness, refusing to apologize for his actions.
The film explores a world where shamelessness is rewarded instead of condemned.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shame - a painful feeling of humiliation or distress caused by the consciousness of wrong or foolish behavior
  • Synonym: immodesty - lack of modesty; shamelessness or bold exposure

Etymology

From 'shameless' + '-ness', after Old English 'sceamleas' ('shame' + '-less').
