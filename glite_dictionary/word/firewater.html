firewater

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Strong alcoholic spirits, especially whisky or rum.
strong alcoholic spirits

Examples

After a long day on the trail, the cowboys shared a bottle of firewater by the campfire.
Some believe that too much firewater can lead to trouble and regret.
During the fur trade era, firewater was often exchanged for animal pelts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquor - alcoholic drink, especially distilled spirits
  • Degree Lower: whisky - a type of distilled alcoholic beverage made from grain mash
  • Degree Lower: rum - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane byproducts

Etymology

Originally used by North American Indigenous peoples and settlers to describe distilled spirits traded by early European colonists, referring to the burning sensation of strong alcohol.
