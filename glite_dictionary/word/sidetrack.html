sidetrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪˌtræk / (BrE)
a short stretch of railroad track connected to the main line and used for storing trains or allowing others to pass
railroad track branch for storage or passing

Examples

The freight cars were left on the sidetrack overnight until the main line was clear.
A train pulled into the sidetrack to allow an express service to pass.
Maintenance crews used the sidetrack to repair damaged railcars out of the way of traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siding - a short railway track connected to the main track, used for storage or passing

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌtræk / (BrE)
to cause someone to turn away from the main subject or course; to distract or divert from the intended path or focus
to distract or divert from the main course

Examples

Please do not sidetrack the discussion with unrelated topics.
She tends to sidetrack easily when working on group projects.
The speaker was sidetracked by questions from the audience, losing her train of thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distract - to turn one's attention away from something
  • Antonym: focus - to pay particular attention to something

Etymology

Originally referring to diverting trains onto a secondary track; from 'side' + 'track'.
