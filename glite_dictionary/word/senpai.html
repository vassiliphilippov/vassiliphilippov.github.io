senpai

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.paɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.paɪ / (BrE)
a person who is older, more experienced, or holds a higher status, especially in an educational, workplace, or social hierarchy; often used in Japanese contexts or among English speakers influenced by Japanese culture
a senior or mentor, especially in Japanese contexts

Examples

My art senpai helped me improve my drawing skills by giving helpful feedback.
In the club, the younger members always greet their senpai with respect.
She hopes her senpai will notice her hard work this semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentor - an experienced and trusted advisor
  • Antonym: kōhai - a junior or less experienced person in Japanese social hierarchy

Etymology

From Japanese 先輩 (senpai), meaning senior or mentor.
