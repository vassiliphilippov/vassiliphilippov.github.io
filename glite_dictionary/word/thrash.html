thrash

1. NOUN

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
a vigorous kick or movement of the legs, especially used in swimming while treading water
a swimming kick for treading water

Examples

The instructor demonstrated the correct thrash to help beginners stay afloat longer.
Using a powerful thrash, she managed to keep her head above water during the test.
He mastered the thrash technique before moving on to more advanced swimming strokes.
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
(of a person) to dance energetically in a violent or frenzied manner, especially by performing the slam dance in punk or heavy metal music contexts.
to dance the slam dance violently

Examples

The crowd began to thrash wildly as the band hit the chorus.
He loves to thrash at punk concerts with his friends.
Fans thrashed in the mosh pit, matching the music's intensity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam dance - a style of dancing involving aggressive body contact, popular at punk rock concerts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01901878-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
(of the heart) to beat so rapidly and irregularly that its output decreases to the point where it effectively cannot pump blood.
(of heart) beat rapidly and ineffectively
medical

Examples

The patient's heart began to thrash uncontrollably after the shock.
When the heart starts to thrash, blood flow to the brain may stop abruptly.
Doctors were alarmed to see the ventricles thrash during the arrhythmic episode.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01884383-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
(of a computer system) to spend excessive time moving data between memory and storage rather than performing useful computational work, often due to insufficient memory.
(computing) excessively swap data, not compute

Examples

If too many programs are running, the computer will thrash and slow down considerably.
The server started thrashing after the database query overloaded the memory.
The system administrator noticed that the machine was thrashing due to a lack of available RAM.

Synset Examples

The system is thrashing again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swap - (computing) to exchange data between memory and storage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01842802-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
to beat someone or something hard, especially as a punishment or in a fight
beat hard or severely

Examples

The coach threatened to thrash any player who disobeyed the rules.
He thrashed his opponent in the boxing ring last night.
The angry farmer thrashed the thief with a stick after catching him in the act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly or violently

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *threschen* (to thrash, thresh), related to beating grain out of husks.

8. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
to separate seeds from a plant, especially grain, by beating
separate seeds from grain by beating

Examples

For centuries, farmers would thrash wheat by hand to remove the kernels.
In some rural areas, oxen are still used to thrash the grain.
The workers thrashed the barley on the threshing floor until all the seeds were free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thresh - to separate grain from a plant, typically by beating

Etymology

Probably related to Old English *þerscan* (to thresh). Spelling and meaning influenced by 'thresh'.
