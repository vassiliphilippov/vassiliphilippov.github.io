Sardinia

1. PROPN

/ sɑrˈdɪniə / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪniə / (BrE)
an island region of Italy in the Mediterranean, historically a kingdom that played a central role in the unification of Italy in the 19th century
Italian island region; historic kingdom

Examples

Many tourists visit Sardinia to experience its beautiful beaches and unique cuisine.
The Kingdom of Sardinia played an important role in the unification of Italy.
She spent her summer traveling across Sardinia, exploring its historic towns and natural landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sardinia', from Greek 'Sardō (Σαρδὼ)', the ancient name of the island.

Mediterranean island west of Italy

Examples

We spent our summer vacation exploring the beautiful beaches of Sardinia.
Sardinia is famous for its unique cuisine and rich cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Latin Sardinia, from ancient Greek Sardō (Σαρδώ), possibly named after the legendary hero Sardus.

3. NOUN

/ sɑːrˈdɪniə / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small oily fish in the herring family, commonly known as pilchards.
genus of fish (pilchards)

Examples

The genus Sardinia includes several species commonly referred to as pilchards.
Marine biologists study the distribution of fish in the genus Sardinia along Mediterranean coasts.
Canned pilchards are often derived from species in the Sardinia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small edible fish of the herring family
  • Degree Higher: Clupea - genus of fish closely related to Sardinia

Etymology

Named after the island of Sardinia, where these fish were once abundant.
