pupa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːpə / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpə / (BrE)
an insect at the stage of development between larva and adult, during which it is usually inactive and undergoing transformation
insect's transformation stage between larva and adult

Examples

During metamorphosis, the caterpillar transforms into a pupa before becoming a butterfly.
We found a pupa attached to the underside of the leaf in the garden.
The insect remained in its pupa stage for several weeks before emerging as an adult.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: larva - an immature, active form of an insect that develops into a pupa
  • Degree Higher: imago - the final, adult stage of an insect after metamorphosis

Etymology

From Latin pupa (girl, doll, puppet).
