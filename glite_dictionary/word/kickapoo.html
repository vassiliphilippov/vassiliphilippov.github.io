Kickapoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkəˌpu / (AmE), / ˈkɪkəˌpuː / (BrE)
The Algonquian language spoken by the Kickapoo people.
Algonquian language of the Kickapoo people

Examples

She is studying the Kickapoo language as part of her research on Native American cultures.
Only a few hundred people still speak Kickapoo fluently today.
Linguists are working to preserve Kickapoo for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages including Kickapoo

Etymology

From the name of the Kickapoo people, possibly from the Meskwaki word 'kiikapawa', meaning 'he moves about, he moves here and there'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkəˌpu / (AmE), / ˈkɪkəˌpuː / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally living in southern Wisconsin and northern Illinois.
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Kickapoo maintained their own distinct traditions despite pressures from neighboring tribes.
A group of Kickapoo settled in Kansas after migrating from their original homeland.
The museum's new exhibit highlights the culture and history of the Kickapoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kickapoo language - the language spoken by the Kickapoo people

Etymology

From the self-designation of the people in the Kickapoo language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09678744-n
Synset POS: n
