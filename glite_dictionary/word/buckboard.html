buckboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.bɔːd / (BrE)
an open, horse-drawn carriage with four wheels and a seat attached to a flexible board between the axles
type of open horse-drawn carriage

Examples

The old ranch still keeps a buckboard in the barn for special occasions.
Tourists enjoyed rides through the countryside in a restored buckboard.
He delivered supplies in a sturdy buckboard during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagon - a vehicle with four wheels, usually pulled by horses

Etymology

From 'buck' (to move jerkily, as a horse) and 'board', referring to the flexible plank forming the carriage's base.
