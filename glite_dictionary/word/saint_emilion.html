Saint Emilion

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænt ɪˈmiːl.jən / (AmE), / ˌsæ̃ t eɪ.miˈljɔ̃ / (BrE)
A full-bodied red wine produced around the town of Saint Emilion in the Bordeaux region of France.
type of Bordeaux red wine

Examples

He selected a bottle of Saint Emilion to pair with the roast lamb.
Many collectors seek rare vintages of Saint Emilion for their cellars.
The rich aroma of blackberry and plum is typical of a good Saint Emilion.

Etymology

Named after the town of Saint-Émilion in Bordeaux, France.
