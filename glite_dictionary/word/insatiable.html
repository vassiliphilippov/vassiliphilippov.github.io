insatiable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈseɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪʃəbl / (BrE)
(of a desire or appetite) impossible to satisfy; always needing more.
impossible to satisfy

Examples

Her insatiable curiosity led her to travel the world in search of new experiences.
Despite his insatiable hunger for success, he remained humble and hardworking.
The fans have an insatiable appetite for the team's latest merchandise.

Synset Examples

an insatiate appetite
an insatiable demand for old buildings to restore
his passion for work was unsatiable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satiable - able to be satisfied
  • Synonym: unquenchable - impossible to quench or satisfy

Etymology

From Latin insatiabilis, from in- ‘not’ + satiare ‘fill, satisfy’.
