Crotalus adamanteus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈteɪləs ˌædəˈmæn.ti.əs / (AmE), / krəʊˈtæləs ˌadəˈmantɪəs / (BrE)
a large venomous rattlesnake of the southeastern United States, recognized by its diamond-shaped markings; also known as the eastern diamondback rattlesnake
large rattlesnake with diamond patterns

Examples

The Crotalus adamanteus is considered the largest rattlesnake species in North America.
A Crotalus adamanteus was spotted crossing the hiking trail in southern Georgia.
Scientists studied the habitat of the Crotalus adamanteus to improve conservation efforts.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Crotalus' derives from Greek 'krotalon' (rattle) and 'adamanteus' (diamond-like).
