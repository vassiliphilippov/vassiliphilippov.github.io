Scorpaena grandicornis

1. NOUN

/ skɔːrˈpiː.nə ˌɡræn.dɪˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / skɔːˈpeɪ.nə ˌɡræn.dɪˈkɔː.nɪs / (BrE)
A species of marine scorpionfish found in the Indo-Pacific region, known for its venomous spines.
a species of venomous scorpionfish

Examples

The Scorpaena grandicornis is known for its venomous spines, which can cause painful stings.
Divers are often warned to avoid contact with Scorpaena grandicornis due to its excellent camouflage and sharp spines.
Researchers observed the behavior of Scorpaena grandicornis in its natural coral reef habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpionfish - any of numerous marine fishes of the family Scorpaenidae, many of which are venomous

Etymology

From Latin: 'Scorpaena' (a type of fish) and 'grandicornis' meaning 'large-horned'.
