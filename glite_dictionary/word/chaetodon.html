chaetodon

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈtɑːdən / (AmE), / kiːˈtɒdən / (BrE)
Any fish of the genus Chaetodon, known as butterflyfish, having bright colors and laterally compressed bodies, typically found in tropical seas.
genus of butterflyfish

Examples

The colorful chaetodon swam gracefully among the coral reefs.
Researchers observed several species of chaetodon near the island's lagoon.
Butterflyfish, especially those of the genus chaetodon, are popular among marine aquarists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterflyfish - Common name for fishes of the family Chaetodontidae, to which Chaetodon belongs

Etymology

From Greek *chaite* (hair, bristle) + *odous* (tooth), referring to the bristle-like teeth of these fish.
