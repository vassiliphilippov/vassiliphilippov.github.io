topic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
a subject that is discussed, written about, or studied
subject of discussion or study

Examples

Climate change has become a popular topic in both politics and the media.
She brought up a new topic at the meeting to keep the conversation moving.
That book covers almost every topic related to cooking and nutrition.

Synset Examples

he didn't want to discuss that subject
it was a very sensitive topic
his letters were always on the theme of love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject - the thing being discussed, written about, or considered
  • Synonym: theme - the main subject of something such as a book, speech, or discussion

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place; subject), via Latin 'topica.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪk / (BrE)
a subject or matter that is discussed, written about, or thought about
subject of discussion or thought

Examples

Today's meeting will focus on the topic of climate change.
She quickly changed the topic when she realized the conversation was becoming uncomfortable.
Can we stay on the topic and not get distracted?

Synset Examples

he kept drifting off the topic
he had been thinking about the subject for several years
it is a matter for the police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject - a person or thing that is being discussed, described, or dealt with
  • Synonym: theme - the subject of a talk, piece of writing, or exhibition; a topic

Etymology

From Greek 'topikos' (pertaining to a place, or ‘commonplace’), via Latin 'topica'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05822417-n
Synset POS: n
