Phlebotomus papatasii

1. NOUN

/ fləˈbɑtəˌmoʊs ˌpæpəˈteɪsaɪ / (AmE), / fləˈbɒtəməs ˌpæpəˈtɑːsiː / (BrE)
A species of small sand fly whose bloodsucking females can transmit sandfly fever and leishmaniasis.
species of disease-carrying sand fly
medical

Examples

The bite of a Phlebotomus papatasii can transmit serious diseases to humans and animals.
Researchers are studying the behavior of Phlebotomus papatasii to find better ways to control its population.
Phlebotomus papatasii is recognized as a primary vector of leishmaniasis in many tropical regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Phlebotomus, from Greek 'phlebotomos' (vein-cutter), and Latinized species name 'papatasii'.
