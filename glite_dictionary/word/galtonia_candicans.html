Galtonia candicans

1. NOUN

/ ɡælˈtoʊniə kænˈdɪkənz / (AmE), / ɡælˈtəʊniə kanˈdɪkənz / (BrE)
A species of southern African herb with white bell-shaped flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
southern African herb with white flowers

Examples

Galtonia candicans adds striking white blooms to summer gardens.
Many gardeners choose Galtonia candicans for its elegant bell-shaped flowers.
In mild climates, Galtonia candicans may remain in the ground over winter.

Etymology

Named after Francis Galton, English statistician and explorer, and Latin 'candicans' meaning 'whitish.'
