grandness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrænd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being splendid, impressive, or imposing in size, appearance, or style.
splendid or imposing quality

Examples

The grandness of the old cathedral left the tourists speechless.
Many are drawn to the grandness of nature found in national parks.
He admired the grandness of her vision for the company.

Synset Examples

the grandness of the architecture
impressed by the richness of the flora

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - The quality of being magnificent or splendid

Etymology

From 'grand' + '-ness', with 'grand' from Old French 'grand', from Latin 'grandis' (large, great).
