Dipteronia

1. NOUN

/ dɪp.təˈroʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / dɪp.təˈrəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of large deciduous shrubs native to central and southern China, with large clusters of winged seeds that turn red as they mature.
genus of deciduous Chinese shrubs

Examples

The Dipteronia plants are admired for their ornamental winged seeds.
Botanists discovered a new species of Dipteronia in southern China.
The autumn landscape was brightened by the crimson seeds of the Dipteronia shrub.

Etymology

From Greek 'dipteros' (two-winged), referring to the winged seeds.

2. NOUN

/ dɪp.təˈroʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / dɪp.təˈrəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of large deciduous shrubs native to central and southern China, noted for their large clusters of winged seeds that turn red as they mature.
genus of shrubs native to China

Examples

The Dipteronia produces distinctive red, winged seeds in late summer.
Botanists discovered a new population of Dipteronia in southern China.
Unlike most shrubs, Dipteronia displays a striking color change as its seeds mature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acer - a related genus including maple trees

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' meaning 'two' and 'pteron' meaning 'wing', referring to the two-winged seeds.
