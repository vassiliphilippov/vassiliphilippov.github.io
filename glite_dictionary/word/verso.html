verso

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsəʊ / (BrE)
the left-hand page of an open book, or the page that appears on the left when a book is open, typically bearing even page numbers.
left-hand page of an open book

Examples

The author's signature appeared on the verso of the title page.
Collectors inspect both the recto and verso of rare manuscripts for authenticity.
Each illustration is accompanied by a full description printed on the facing verso.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recto - the right-hand page of an open book (opposite of verso)

Etymology

From Latin 'versus' (turned), the past participle of 'vertere' (to turn), referring to the side of a leaf turned over.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsəʊ / (BrE)
the side of a coin or medal that does not bear the principal design, usually opposite the obverse
reverse side of a coin or medal

Examples

The commemorative coin features a portrait on the obverse and a national emblem on the verso.
Collectors sometimes prefer the verso of the medal for its rare markings.
The verso of the coin was carefully examined for authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obverse - the principal or front side of a coin or medal
  • Synonym: reverse - the opposite side of a coin or medal, also called verso

Etymology

From Latin 'versus' (turned), also used in bookmaking for the left-hand page.
