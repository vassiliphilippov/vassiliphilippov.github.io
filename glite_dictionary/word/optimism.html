optimism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑp.təˌmɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tɪ.mɪz.əm / (BrE)
A tendency to expect the best possible outcome or to emphasize the most positive aspects of a situation.
expectation of the best outcome

Examples

Her optimism helped the whole team stay motivated during difficult times.
Despite the setbacks, he approaches every new project with optimism.
The speaker encouraged us to face the future with courage and optimism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimism - A tendency to expect the worst possible outcome

Etymology

From French optimisme, from Latin optimum (the best).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to expect the best possible outcome or to emphasize the most positive aspects of a situation
expecting the best; positive outlook

Examples

Her optimism inspired everyone around her, even during difficult times.
Despite several setbacks, he maintained his optimism about the project's success.
A sense of optimism filled the city as the new year began.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimism - the tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe the worst will happen
  • Synonym: hopefulness - the state of being hopeful; expecting something good

Etymology

From French 'optimisme', from Latin 'optimum' (the best thing).
