Argyroxiphium sandwicense

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡiroʊˈzɪfiəm sændˈwiːsɛns / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡɪr.əʊˈzɪf.i.əm sandˈwiː.sɛns / (BrE)
A rare, low-growing plant native to volcanic craters on Hawaii, with rosettes of narrow, pointed silver-green leaves and profuse clusters of red-purple flowers on a tall stem.
a rare Hawaiian plant with silver-green leaves and red-purple flowers

Examples

The Argyroxiphium sandwicense thrives in the harsh environment of Hawaii's volcanic craters.
Park guides protect the endangered Argyroxiphium sandwicense from hikers who might trample it.
Botanists study Argyroxiphium sandwicense for its unique adaptation to the volcanic landscape.

Etymology

Genus from Greek 'argyros' (silver) and 'xiphium' (sword lily); species from 'Sandwich Islands' (historic name for Hawaii).
