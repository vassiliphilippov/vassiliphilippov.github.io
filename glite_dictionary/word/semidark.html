semidark

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈdɑrk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈdɑːk / (BrE)
partly dark; lacking full light or brightness.
partly dark, lacking full light

Examples

She waited in the semidark hallway, barely able to see the outline of the door.
The room was semidark, with just enough light to make out the furniture.
In the semidark theatre, the audience settled into their seats before the show began.

Synset Examples

semidark room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dim - not bright; with limited light
  • Antonym: bright - strongly lit; giving off much light

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partially') and 'dark'.
