Bethlehem-Judah

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛθlɪˌhɛm ˈdʒuːdə / (AmE), / ˈbɛθlɪˌhɛm ˈdʒuːdə / (BrE)
a small town near Jerusalem on the West Bank of the Jordan River, known as the early home of David and traditionally regarded as the birthplace of Jesus Christ
town near Jerusalem; birthplace of Jesus

Examples

Many pilgrims travel to Bethlehem-Judah to visit its historic sites each year.
According to the Bible, David was born in Bethlehem-Judah.
The Nativity story centers on the birth of Jesus in Bethlehem-Judah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bethlehem - town south of Jerusalem, identified as birthplace of Jesus

Etymology

From Hebrew *Beit Lechem* (house of bread) in the region of Judah.
