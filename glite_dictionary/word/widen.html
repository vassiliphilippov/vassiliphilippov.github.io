to widen

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
to make (an article of clothing) larger, especially by altering its seams or fabric
make clothing larger by altering

Examples

She asked the tailor to widen her trousers at the waist after she gained weight.
Can you widen this jacket so that it fits more comfortably?
They decided to widen the sleeves of the dress for a looser fit.

Synset Examples

Let out that dress — I gained a lot of weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let out - to make (a garment) larger by enlarging its seams
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00304721-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
to become or make something broader, wider, or more extensive
to become/make wider or broader

Examples

The city plans to widen the main road to reduce traffic congestion.
As the river flows downstream, it tends to widen before reaching the delta.
Her eyes widened in surprise when she heard the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

The road widened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrow - to make or become less wide or more restricted

Etymology

From wide + -en (a suffix forming verbs from adjectives in Old and Middle English).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00304246-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
to make something wider; to increase the width of something
make or become wider

Examples

The city decided to widen the bridge to reduce traffic congestion.
As the river moves downstream, it tends to widen noticeably.
They plan to widen the pathways in the park for cyclists and pedestrians.

Synset Examples

widen the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to broaden - to make broader or more extensive

Etymology

From wide + -en (a verb-forming suffix) in English.

4. VERB

/ ˈwaɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become greater in scope, range, or area; to broaden
make or become broader, expand scope

Examples

The city plans to widen several roads to ease traffic congestion.
The organization hopes to widen its reach to include more diverse communities.
Travel can widen your perspective and introduce you to new ideas.

Synset Examples

The law was extended to all citizens
widen the range of applications
broaden your horizon
Extend your backyard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to broaden - to make or become broader or more comprehensive
  • Antonym: narrow - to reduce in width or scope

Etymology

From wide + -en, a verb-forming suffix from Old English –nian.
