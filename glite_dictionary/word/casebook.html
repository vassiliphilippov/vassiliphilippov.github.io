casebook

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪsˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsbʊk / (BrE)
a book containing detailed written records of cases, used as a reference or source of information for study or subsequent work
book of detailed case records

Examples

The law student spent hours reading the casebook before her exam.
Doctors often refer to a casebook to compare new cases with past experiences.
His medical casebook documented several rare conditions encountered during his career.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06515025-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪsˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsbʊk / (BrE)
(of a case, example, etc.) displaying all the typical features of something; considered a classic or standard instance.
typical or classic example

Examples

The doctor described her symptoms as a casebook example of pneumonia.
His reaction was a casebook display of anger management issues.
It was a casebook case of fraud, following all the usual patterns.

Synset Examples

a casebook schizophrenic
a textbook example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textbook - typical; a classic instance

Etymology

From 'casebook' (a book containing written records of legal or medical cases) + used attributively.
