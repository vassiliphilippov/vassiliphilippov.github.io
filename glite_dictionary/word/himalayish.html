Himalayish

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmɑːləjɪʃ / (AmE), / hɪˈmaləjɪʃ / (BrE)
a group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken in the Himalayan regions of Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan, and Sikkim
group of Himalayan languages

Examples

Many minority communities in the region continue to speak Himalayish languages.
Linguists have classified several dialects in eastern Nepal as belonging to Himalayish.
The diversity of Himalayish is evident in Bhutan’s different valleys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibeto-Burman - major language family to which Himalayish languages belong

Etymology

Formed from 'Himalaya', the mountain range, and the English suffix '-ish', denoting relation.
