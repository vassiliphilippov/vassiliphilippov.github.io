to explain

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)
to give a reason or justification for something; to account for why something happens or is the case
give a reason or justification for

Examples

Rising costs alone do not explain the sudden decline in sales.
Her nervousness helped to explain her awkward behavior during the interview.
Heavy traffic might explain why he was late again this morning.

Synset Examples

Your need to sleep late does not excuse your late arrival at work
Her recent divorce may explain her reluctance to date again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to justify - show or prove to be right or reasonable
  • Synonym: to account for - give a reason or explanation for something
  • Synonym: excuse - seek to remove blame or justify

Etymology

From Latin explanare (to make plain, flatten out), from ex- (out) and planus (flat, level, clear).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)
to make (something) clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it
make clear; describe to aid understanding

Examples

Can you explain the homework assignment to me one more time?
The scientist tried to explain the complex theory in simple terms.
She explained why she was late to the meeting.

Synset Examples

He explained the laws of physics to his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make clearer or easier to understand
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make something more difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin explicare (unfold, make clear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941308-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)
to make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it
make clear by giving information

Examples

She tried to explain the rules to the new players.
The teacher will explain the concept with simple examples.
Can you explain how this device works in a few sentences?

Synset Examples

The committee explained their plan for fund-raising to the Dean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of something in words
  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make something more difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'explanare' (to make level, make clear), from 'ex-' (out) + 'planus' (flat, level).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01067478-v
Synset POS: v
