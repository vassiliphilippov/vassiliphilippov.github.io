jejuneness

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒuːn.nəs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being naive, simplistic, or lacking maturity and experience of life
naivety; lack of experience or sophistication

Examples

Her jejuneness was evident when she trusted the stranger's unlikely story.
The essay's jejuneness made it unconvincing to more experienced readers.
He outgrew the jejuneness of his youth through years of travel and study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Antonym: sophistication - the quality of being experienced and cultured

Etymology

From Latin jejūnus (empty, barren) + English suffix -ness.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈdʒuːn.nəs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, simplistic, or lacking in sophistication or substance
dullness or lack of sophistication

Examples

The jejuneness of his writing failed to impress the judges at the competition.
Critics complained about the jejuneness of the movie's plot and dialogue.
Despite its promising premise, the book disappointed many with its overall jejuneness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jejunity - the state of being jejune; lack of nutritive value or interest

Etymology

From Latin jejunus (fasting, barren, meager) via jejune + -ness.

3. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒuːn.nəs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking nutritive value; the condition of being empty or insubstantial, especially in a nutritional sense
quality of lacking nutritive value

Examples

The jejuneness of the diet left the children feeling weak throughout the day.
Critics commented on the jejuneness of the hospital meals, noting their lack of essential nutrients.
In times of famine, people suffer not only from hunger but also from the jejuneness of whatever food is available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - the quality of being without flavor or zest
  • Antonym: substantiality - the quality of being substantial or nourishing

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunus' (fasting, hungry, barren), via 'jejune'.
