pyknotic

1. ADJ

/ pɪkˈnɑːtɪk / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell nucleus) characterized by or relating to pyknosis, in which the nucleus becomes shrunken and intensely stained due to chromatin condensation, often as a sign of cell death.
having or showing pyknosis in cell nuclei
medical

Examples

The pathologist noted the presence of pyknotic nuclei in the tissue sample.
Under the microscope, several cells showed pyknotic changes characteristic of apoptosis.
A pyknotic nucleus appears small and dark when stained with hematoxylin and eosin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyknosis - the irreversible condensation of chromatin in a cell nucleus undergoing cell death

Etymology

From Greek pyknotikos, from pyknos ('thick, dense').
