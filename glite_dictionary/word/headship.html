headship

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of being the leader or head of an organization, group, or institution
position of being a leader or head

Examples

After his promotion, he assumed the headship of the department.
The headship of the committee was a challenge she gladly accepted.
Her headship brought about many positive changes in the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leadership - the action or position of leading a group

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + '-ship', a suffix denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of being a headmaster or headmistress, especially in a school
the office of headmaster or headmistress

Examples

She took up the headship of the school after the previous headteacher retired.
Many teachers aspire to headship after years in the classroom.
The process of applying for headship can be both challenging and rewarding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal - the head of a school, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'head' meaning leader and suffix '-ship' denoting position or state.
