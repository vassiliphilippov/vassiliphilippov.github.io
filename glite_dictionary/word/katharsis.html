katharsis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑr.sɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑː.sɪs / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis and literary theory) the process of releasing, and thereby providing relief from, strong or repressed emotions.
emotional release or cleansing

Examples

The play provides a sense of katharsis for the audience as unresolved tensions are dramatically released.
Artists often speak of experiencing katharsis through the act of creating their work.
Group therapy can sometimes act as a means of katharsis for participants dealing with trauma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catharsis - emotional release or cleansing (alternative, more common spelling)

Etymology

From Greek *katharsis* (purification, cleansing).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑrsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːsɪs / (BrE)
(medical) The process of purging the body, especially the bowels, by using a cathartic.
medical purging of the bowels
medical

Examples

The physician prescribed a strong laxative to induce katharsis in the patient.
Ancient medical practices often relied on katharsis to treat a variety of ailments.
The use of herbal remedies for katharsis dates back centuries in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catharsis - psychological release of emotions

Etymology

From Greek *katharsis* (cleansing, purification).
