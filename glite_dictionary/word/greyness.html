greyness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being grey; a neutral color midway between white and black.
the state of being grey; grey color

Examples

The greyness of the sky made the day feel gloomy and cold.
She disliked the greyness of the city in winter.
The painting captures the subtle greyness between black and white.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grayness - American spelling for the same color quality

Etymology

From grey + -ness, formed on the model of similar color nouns.
