Caroline

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæroʊˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
Relating to the period of the reigns of Kings Charles I and Charles II of England or their era.
of the period of Charles I or II of England

Examples

The Caroline era is known for its distinctive art and architecture.
Historians often study the Caroline court to understand 17th-century politics.
The play was written during the Caroline period in England.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Jacobean - of or relating to the period of the reign of James I of England
  • Degree Higher: Georgian - of or relating to the period of the reigns of the British kings named George

Etymology

From New Latin Carolinus, from Carolus (Charles), referring to Charles I and II of England.
