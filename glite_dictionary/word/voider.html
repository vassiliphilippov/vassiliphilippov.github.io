voider

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪdər / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪdə / (BrE)
a piece of chain mail designed to cover an area of the body not protected by armor plate, used in medieval armor.
chain mail piece for gaps in armor

Examples

The knight wore a voider beneath his arm to protect the vulnerable joint.
Restorers carefully recreated the voider using authentic medieval techniques.
Without a voider, gaps under the plate armor could be exploited by opponents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - a type of armor consisting of small metal rings linked together

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪdər / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪdə / (BrE)
a basket or container used to hold dirty clothes before washing or wet clothes for drying
laundry basket for dirty or wet clothes

Examples

He placed his wet towels in the voider after his morning shower.
The old-fashioned voider stood by the laundry room door, ready for dirty clothes.
She emptied the week's laundry from the voider and began sorting by color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laundry basket - a basket for holding clothes that need washing
