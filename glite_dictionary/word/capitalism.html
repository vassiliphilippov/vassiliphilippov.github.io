capitalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
An economic system in which industries, businesses, and property are owned privately for profit, rather than by the state.
economic system based on private ownership

Examples

The country adopted capitalism after years of government control of the economy.
Critics argue that capitalism can lead to inequality between rich and poor.
Many people believe that innovation thrives under capitalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: socialism - an economic system in which the means of production are commonly or state-owned
  • Antonym: communism - a political and economic ideology advocating common ownership and absence of social classes

Etymology

From French 'capitalisme', from 'capital' (wealth, stock).
