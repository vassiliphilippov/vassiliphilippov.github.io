Gleicheniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˌkiːniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˌkiːniˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns in the order Filicales, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
a family of tropical and subtropical ferns

Examples

The Gleicheniaceae are commonly found in moist, tropical forests around the world.
Researchers have studied the adaptive features of ferns within the Gleicheniaceae family.
New species in the Gleicheniaceae have been discovered in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From the genus name Gleichenia, named after botanist W. F. von Gleichen, with botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˌkiːniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˌkiːniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns in the order Filicales, characterized by forked, climbing or scrambling fronds
a family of ferns

Examples

The Gleicheniaceae are found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
Members of the Gleicheniaceae family often have highly divided, forked leaves.
Botanists have debated the classification of Gleicheniaceae within the larger group of ferns.

Etymology

From the genus name Gleicheni- (after German botanist W.F. von Gleichen) + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
