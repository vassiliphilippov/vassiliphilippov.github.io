nugatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuːɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈnjuːɡətəri / (BrE)
of no real value; worthless or insignificant
worthless; of no value

Examples

The committee dismissed his objections as nugatory and irrelevant.
They found the contract clause to be legally nugatory in court.
Most experts agreed that the changes made were largely nugatory and had no real effect.

Synset Examples

a nugatory law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - having considerable worth or value

Etymology

From Latin nugatorius (trifling), from nugari (to trifle).
