squaw

1. NOUN

/ skwɑ / (AmE), / skwɔː / (BrE)
(offensive) An American Indian woman; a disparaging and offensive term for a Native American woman.
derogatory term for a Native American woman
offensive

Examples

The novel included language such as squaw that is now recognized as deeply offensive.
Historians advise against using the word squaw due to its derogatory connotations.
Many Native communities have campaigned to have place names with squaw replaced with respectful alternatives.

Etymology

From an Algonquian language (likely Narragansett or Massachusett) meaning 'woman', but now widely considered offensive in English.
