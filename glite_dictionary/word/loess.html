loess

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.əs / (AmE), / ˈlɛs / (BrE)
A fine-grained, yellowish or buff, unstratified deposit of clay and silt left by the wind, often forming fertile soil.
fine, wind-deposited clay and silt

Examples

The thick layer of loess along the riverbank supports rich agricultural land.
Much of northern China’s farmland is built on fertile loess deposits.
Wind-blown loess offers important clues about past climate conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silt - fine sand, clay, or other material carried by running water

Etymology

From German Loess, originally from Swiss German lösch (loose, crumbly).
