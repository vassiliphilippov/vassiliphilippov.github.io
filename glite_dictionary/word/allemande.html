allemande

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈmɑnd / (AmE), / ˌaləˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
a rich sauce made from velouté thickened with egg yolks and cream, often flavored with lemon
a creamy egg-thickened sauce

Examples

The chef finished the asparagus dish with a generous spoonful of allemande.
Classic veal dishes are often served with allemande to enhance their flavor.
She learned to make allemande during her culinary training in Paris.

Etymology

From French 'allemande' (German), referring originally to 'German sauce'.
