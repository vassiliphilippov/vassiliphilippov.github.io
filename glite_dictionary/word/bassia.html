Bassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsiə / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the goosefoot family, often known as summer cypress, including species used as ornamental plants and sometimes for forage.
a genus of plants known as summer cypress

Examples

The ornamental Bassia in her garden turned a bright red in autumn.
Farmers sometimes plant Bassia as a source of forage for livestock.
Some species of Bassia are valued for their drought resistance.

Etymology

From Latin, adopted as a botanical genus name, originating from a personal name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæsiə / (AmE), / ˈbasɪə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the amaranth family, including the summer cypress and related species.
a plant genus, including summer cypress
medical

Examples

The summer cypress, a popular ornamental plant, belongs to the Bassia genus.
Several species in the Bassia genus are adapted to arid environments and poor soils.
Botanists recently reclassified some Bassia plants into other genera based on genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kochia - A related genus of plants, sometimes merged with Bassia in botanical classification

Etymology

From Latinized form of Russian botanist Fedor Bass, whose name was used for the genus.
