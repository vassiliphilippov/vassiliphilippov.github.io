keratotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.əˈtɑ.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the cornea of the eye, often to correct vision problems
surgical incision into the cornea
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a keratotomy to treat the patient’s severe astigmatism.
Advances in keratotomy have greatly improved outcomes for those with corneal diseases.
Many patients recover quickly after undergoing a keratotomy procedure.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn, referring to the cornea) + 'tomia' (cutting).
