pyrrhuloxia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪr.əˈlɒk.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɪr.əˈlɒk.si.ə / (BrE)
a crested grey-and-red songbird (Cardinalis sinuatus) native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, similar to the cardinal
Southwestern U.S. and Mexican songbird

Examples

The striking plumage of the pyrrhuloxia makes it a favorite among birdwatchers in the southwestern desert.
Unlike the northern cardinal, the pyrrhuloxia has a strongly curved yellow bill.
We spotted several pyrrhuloxias feeding on seeds near the cactus plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal - North American songbird with a crest and red plumage

Etymology

From scientific Latin, combining the genus name Pyrrhula with Ancient Greek loxos (oblique, crosswise), referring to its beak shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪrəˈlɑksiə / (AmE), / ˌpɪrʊˈlɒksɪə / (BrE)
a crested, grey-and-red bird of the southwestern United States and Mexico, closely related to the cardinal
crested bird of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

A brightly colored pyrrhuloxia perched on a cactus in the morning sun.
Birdwatchers often travel to Arizona to catch a glimpse of the elusive pyrrhuloxia.
The pyrrhuloxia is sometimes mistaken for the northern cardinal due to its similar crest.

Etymology

From New Latin Pyrrhuloxia, combining 'Pyrrhula' (genus of finches) and Ancient Greek 'loxia' (crosswise), referring to the bill shape.
