phlox

1. NOUN

/ flɑks / (AmE), / flɒks / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Phlox, native chiefly to North America, grown for its clusters of colorful flowers
a flowering North American plant

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming phlox that attracted butterflies all summer long.
She planted several varieties of phlox along the walkway for a burst of color.
Tall phlox can add height and visual interest to your flower beds.

Etymology

From Greek 'phlox' meaning 'flame,' referring to the bright flowers.
