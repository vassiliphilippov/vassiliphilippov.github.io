leukocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːkəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈluːkəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a white blood cell, one of the cells of the immune system that help protect the body by engulfing and digesting bacteria and fungi
white blood cell
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a high leukocyte count may indicate an infection.
A decrease in leukocyte levels can weaken the immune system.
Researchers observed how each leukocyte targeted and destroyed bacteria in the blood sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white blood cell - another term for leukocyte; a blood cell involved in immune response
  • Antonym: erythrocyte - red blood cell, which carries oxygen but is not part of the immune system

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'kytos' (cell).
