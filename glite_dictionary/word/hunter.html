hunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌnɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌntə / (BrE)
a person who actively searches for or seeks out something, especially as a pursuit or hobby
person who searches for something

Examples

He became a successful hunter of rare antiques at local auctions.
As a job hunter, she spent hours each day searching the listings online.
The show follows a group of tornado hunters chasing storms across the Midwest.

Synset Examples

a treasure hunter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seeker - a person who is attempting to find or obtain something

Etymology

From Old English 'hunta' (hunter), related to 'huntian' (to hunt). Originally referred to pursuing animals, later broadened.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tə / (BrE)
a pocket watch with a hinged metal lid that protects the glass crystal
pocket watch with protective lid

Examples

He inherited his grandfather's antique hunter, which still had its original engravings.
A hunter is often favored by collectors for its distinctive lid and classic design.
She prized her gold hunter as both a functional timepiece and a family heirloom.

Etymology

From the practice of hunters using this style of watch, as the lid protected the crystal while riding or moving outdoors.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tə / (BrE)
a person who hunts wild animals for food, sport, or profit
person who hunts animals

Examples

The hunter tracked the deer silently through the dense forest.
During the winter, local hunters search for game in the mountains.
As a skilled hunter, he knew how to move without making a sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poacher - person who hunts animals illegally
  • Antonym: prey - an animal that is hunted and killed for food
  • Synonym: trapper - person who catches wild animals, typically for their fur

Etymology

From Old English 'hunta' (hunter), related to 'huntian' (to hunt).
