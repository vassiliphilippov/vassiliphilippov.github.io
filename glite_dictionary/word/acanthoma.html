acanthoma

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænˈθoʊmə / (AmE), / əˌkanˈθəʊmə / (BrE)
a benign skin tumor originating in the epidermal layer
benign epidermal skin tumor
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the unusual growth on her arm as an acanthoma.
Treatment for an acanthoma typically involves removal by minor surgery.
Although an acanthoma is benign, monitoring for any changes is important.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + -oma (tumor).
