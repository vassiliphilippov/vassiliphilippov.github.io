unassumingness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.əˈsuː.mɪŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈsjuː.mɪŋ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being modest, natural, and without pretension or arrogance
modesty; lack of pretension

Examples

Her unassumingness made her popular among colleagues who valued humility over bravado.
The unassumingness of the artist's home reflected his simple lifestyle.
Many admired the unassumingness with which she accepted her award.

Synset Examples

the simple sincerity of folk songs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; humility
  • Antonym: pretentiousness - the state of being pretentious or showy

Etymology

From 'unassuming' + '-ness'. 'Unassuming': from un- (not) + assuming ('adopt a manner').
