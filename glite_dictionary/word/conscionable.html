conscionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (BrE)
Acceptable to one's conscience; in accordance with what is right and just.
morally acceptable, just

Examples

She refused to sign the contract because it was not a conscionable agreement.
The lawyer argued that only a conscionable solution would resolve the dispute fairly.
It is important to ensure all decisions are conscionable and honest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconscionable - not guided by conscience; unreasonably unethical or unfair

Etymology

From Late Latin conscionabilis (according to conscience), from Latin conscientia (conscience).
