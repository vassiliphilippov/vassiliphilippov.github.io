Chamaeleon

1. PROPN

/ kəˈmiːliən / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliən / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the southern polar region of the sky, located near Apus and Mensa
a faint southern constellation

Examples

The constellation Chamaeleon is best observed from the Southern Hemisphere during the autumn months.
Astronomers use Chamaeleon as a guide to locate other nearby constellations such as Mensa and Apus.
Although faint, Chamaeleon contains several interesting star-forming regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'chamaeleon', from Greek 'khamaileōn' (chameleon), named after the lizard because of the constellation's shape.
