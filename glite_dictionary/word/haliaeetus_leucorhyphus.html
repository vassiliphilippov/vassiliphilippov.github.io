Haliaeetus leucorhyphus

1. NOUN

/ ˌheɪliəˈiːtəs ˌluːkəˈraɪfəs / (AmE), / ˌheɪlɪˈiːtəs ˌluːkəˈraɪfəs / (BrE)
A large species of fish-eagle found in southeast Europe and central Asia, also known as Pallas's fish-eagle.
a species of fish-eagle

Examples

The Haliaeetus leucorhyphus is considered vulnerable due to loss of habitat in its native range.
Researchers observed a Haliaeetus leucorhyphus nest by the riverbank during their study.
Unlike other eagles, the Haliaeetus leucorhyphus primarily feeds on fish and occasionally waterfowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pallas's fish-eagle - common name for Haliaeetus leucorhyphus

Etymology

From Greek hali- (sea) + aetos (eagle), and leuco- (white) + rhyphos (tail).
