Casmerodius

1. NOUN

/ kæzˌmɛrˈoʊdiəs / (AmE), / kazˌmɛrˈəʊdɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, white egrets, formerly used for certain species now usually included in the genus Ardea or Egretta.
genus of large white egrets

Examples

The Casmerodius can be distinguished by its all-white plumage and slender neck.
Researchers found several Casmerodius nests along the wetland edge.
Taxonomists once classified the great egret as Casmerodius, though this is now outdated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a type of heron with long white feathers
  • Degree Higher: heron - wading bird with long legs and neck

Etymology

From Greek kasmeros (majestic) + -odios, related to herons.

2. NOUN

/ kæzməˈroʊdiəs / (AmE), / kazməˈrəʊdiəs / (BrE)
A genus of large white egrets in the heron family, Ardeidae.
genus of white egrets

Examples

The great egret is classified under the genus Casmerodius by some taxonomists.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare member of the Casmerodius genus near the wetland.
The genus Casmerodius includes species known for their striking white feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a type of heron with predominantly white plumage

Etymology

From Greek 'kasmeros' (cutting, shearing) and 'eidōs' (form, likeness), used in scientific naming.
