cinch

1. NOUN

/ sɪntʃ / (AmE), / sɪntʃ / (BrE)
A trick-taking card game related to all fours, in which players bid for the right to choose trumps.
a card game similar to all fours

Examples

They spent the evening playing cinch with friends from the neighborhood.
The rules of cinch require players to bid before naming a trump suit.
He learned to play cinch from his grandparents, who played every weekend.

2. NOUN

/ sɪntʃ / (AmE), / sɪntʃ / (BrE)
A band or strap that passes around a horse's belly to hold a saddle in place.
strap securing a horse's saddle

Examples

Make sure the cinch is tight before mounting the horse.
He replaced the old cinch with a new, sturdier one for better support.
The rider checked the cinch to ensure the saddle would not slip during the ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girth - A band attached to a saddle, used to secure it on a horse by being fastened around its belly

Etymology

From Spanish 'cincha', meaning 'girth', itself from Latin 'cingula', meaning 'girdle, belt'.
