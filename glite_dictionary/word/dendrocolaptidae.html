Dendrocolaptidae

1. NOUN

/ dɛndroʊkəˈlæptəˌdi / (AmE), / dɛndrəʊkəˈlæptɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Neotropical birds known as woodcreepers, characterized by their stiff tail feathers and tree-climbing habits.
family of Neotropical woodcreeper birds

Examples

The family Dendrocolaptidae includes species adapted to foraging on tree trunks.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Dendrocolaptidae in the Amazon rainforest.
Several members of Dendrocolaptidae have distinctive curved bills used for extracting insects from bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcreeper - a bird of the Dendrocolaptidae family, known for climbing tree trunks

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek dendron (tree) and kolaptes (pecker).
