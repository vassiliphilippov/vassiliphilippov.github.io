Orchis mascula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkɪs ˈmæskjələ / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪs ˈmæskjʊlə / (BrE)
A Eurasian orchid with showy pink or purple flowers arranged in a loose spike, commonly found in meadows and woodlands.
species of Eurasian orchid

Examples

We spotted several Orchis mascula growing in the shaded corner of the field.
The Orchis mascula blooms in spring, adding vibrant color to woodland areas.
Botanists collected samples of Orchis mascula for their research on wild orchids.

Etymology

From Latin; 'orchis' meaning orchid and 'mascula' meaning male.
