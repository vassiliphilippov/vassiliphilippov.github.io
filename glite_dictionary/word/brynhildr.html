Brynhildr

1. PROPN

/ brɪnˈhɪldər / (AmE), / brɪnˈhɪldə / (BrE)
a Valkyrie and queen in Norse and Germanic legend, especially in the Nibelungenlied, who loved Siegfried; after his deceit, she had him killed and then took her own life
Valkyrie and queen in Norse legend

Examples

The tragic figure of Brynhildr is central to many Norse sagas and Germanic epics.
In Wagner's operas, Brynhildr is portrayed as a powerful and conflicted Valkyrie.
The legend of Brynhildr and Siegfried has inspired countless adaptations in literature and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brunhilde - alternate Germanic form of Brynhildr in myth and literature
  • Synonym: Siegfried - hero of Norse and Germanic legend, lover of Brynhildr

Etymology

Old Norse Brynhildr, from 'brynja' (mail-coat) + 'hildr' (battle).
