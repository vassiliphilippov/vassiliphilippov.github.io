Tulipa suaveolens

1. NOUN

/ tuˈliːpə swɑːviˈoʊlɛnz / (AmE), / tjuːˈliːpə swɑːviˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A species of small, early-blooming tulip, often cultivated for its bright flowers.
a species of early-blooming tulip

Examples

The vibrant red petals of the Tulipa suaveolens made it a highlight of the spring garden.
Botanists often study Tulipa suaveolens as one of the earliest tulip species to bloom.
Many historic gardens feature plantings of Tulipa suaveolens for their ornamental value.

Etymology

From Latin 'Tulipa', the genus name, and 'suaveolens', meaning 'sweet-smelling'.
