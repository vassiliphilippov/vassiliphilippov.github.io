Archidiskidon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈdɪskɪdɒn / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈdɪskɪdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of extinct elephants in the family Elephantidae, known from the fossil record.
genus of extinct elephants

Examples

Fossils of Archidiskidon have provided valuable insight into the evolution of elephants.
The extinct genus Archidiskidon once roamed parts of Eurasia during the Pleistocene epoch.
Paleontologists continue to debate the classification of Archidiskidon within the elephant family tree.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Elephantidae - family of large, herbivorous mammals including modern elephants and their extinct relatives

Etymology

From Greek: 'archi-' meaning 'chief' or 'ancient', and 'diskidon', diminutive of 'diskos' (disc), referring to a dental characteristic.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈdɪskɪˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈdɪskɪdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of extinct elephant-like mammals in the family Elephantidae, known from fossils.
extinct genus of elephant family

Examples

Fossils of Archidiskidon have been discovered in several regions of Eurasia.
The molar teeth of Archidiskidon show characteristics distinct from those of modern elephants.
Paleontologists study Archidiskidon to understand the evolutionary history of Elephantidae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Elephantidae - the biological family that includes modern elephants and their extinct relatives

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' (chief, ancient) and 'diskidon', diminutive of 'diskos' (disk, plate), referencing the structure of molar teeth in this genus.
