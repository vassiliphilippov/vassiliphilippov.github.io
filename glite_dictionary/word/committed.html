committed

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) involved in an exclusive romantic or sexual relationship, showing loyalty to one partner.
in an exclusive romantic relationship

Examples

After years of dating, they decided to become committed to each other.
He explained that he was committed and not interested in dating anyone else.
It's important to be honest about whether you're ready for a committed relationship.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - not in a romantic relationship
  • Synonym: attached - having a romantic partner; not single

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪt̬ɪd / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing dedication to a cause, activity, or belief; pledged or bound to a particular course or group
dedicated or pledged to a cause or belief

Examples

She is a committed environmentalist who works tirelessly for conservation causes.
Only truly committed members attend every meeting, rain or shine.
As a committed teacher, he always puts his students' needs first.

Synset Examples

committed church members
a committed Marxist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedicated - devoted to a task or purpose
  • Antonym: detached - not feeling or showing emotional involvement

Etymology

From the past participle of 'commit', from Latin committere (to unite, connect, entrust).
