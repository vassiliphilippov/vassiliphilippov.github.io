calx

1. NOUN

/ kælks / (AmE), / kælks / (BrE)
A white, chalky or crystalline oxide, especially calcium oxide, used in producing calcium hydroxide and in other chemical processes.
white or crystalline oxide, especially calcium oxide

Examples

Laboratory technicians measured the amount of calx produced from the reaction.
The formation of calx was once thought to indicate air in the metal.
When heated, certain metals leave behind a white calx as residue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lime - a white caustic alkaline substance, especially calcium oxide

Etymology

From Latin calx (lime, limestone).
