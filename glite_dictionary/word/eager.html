eager

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡər / (AmE), / ˈiːɡə / (BrE)
A high wave, often dangerous, caused by tidal flow, especially as a result of colliding tidal currents or in a narrow estuary.
dangerous tidal wave

Examples

Boats are often warned about the eager that forms in the estuary during high tide.
The sudden eager caught the fishermen off guard, nearly capsizing their vessel.
Local guides advise avoiding the river when a strong eager is expected.

Etymology

From Old English *ēagor* (flood, sea)

2. ADJ

/ ˈiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈiːɡə / (BrE)
wanting very much to do or have something, or showing keen excitement or interest.
keen or enthusiastic; impatiently expectant

Examples

The children were eager to open their presents on Christmas morning.
She gave him an eager look as she waited for his reply.
New employees are often eager to prove themselves at work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - feeling or showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology
