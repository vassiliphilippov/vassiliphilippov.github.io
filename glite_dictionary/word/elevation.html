elevation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
In ballet, the height achieved by a dancer during a leap or jump.
height of a dancer's jump in ballet

Examples

The principal dancer's elevation during the performance captivated the entire audience.
Her elevation improved dramatically after months of strength training and practice.
Judges praised his elevation in the final round of the international ballet competition.

Synset Examples

a dancer of exceptional elevation

Etymology

From Latin elevatio (a lifting up), from elevare (to raise).

Examples
