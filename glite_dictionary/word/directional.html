directional

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or indicating a particular direction or directions, especially in space or as designed to respond to signals from certain directions only.
relating to a particular direction

Examples

The engineer installed a directional antenna to improve the signal quality.
This directional lighting creates dramatic shadows on stage.
A directional microphone is sensitive to sound from one specific angle.

Synset Examples

a directional microphone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: omnidirectional - receiving or transmitting equally in all directions

Etymology

From direction + -al, formed in English.

/ dəˈrɛkʃənl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
giving guidance or leadership; providing direction or guidance towards a goal or course of action.
giving guidance or leadership

Examples

Her directional influence kept the project on track and moving toward its goals.
In times of uncertainty, strong directional leadership is crucial for progress.

3. ADJ

/ dəˈrɛkʃənəl, daɪˈrɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or indicating movement, development, or orientation toward a particular aim or goal, especially not limited to physical direction.
relating to aim or orientation toward a goal

Examples

The company's directional strategy focused on innovation and expansion into new markets.
Recent research shows significant directional changes in climate policy worldwide.
Her directional leadership helped guide the organization through uncertain times.

Synset Examples

he tried to explain the directional trends of modern science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal-oriented - focused on achieving specific goals
  • Antonym: non-directional - not having or lacking a particular direction or aim

Etymology

From direction + -al, indicating relation to direction or course.
