exactingness

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzæktɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːktɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very demanding, strict, or requiring great accuracy or attention to detail
quality of being demanding or strict

Examples

The exactingness of her instructions made it impossible to deviate from the plan.
As a surgeon, he is known for the exactingness he brings to every operation.
Some students struggle with the exactingness required in advanced mathematics courses.

Synset Examples

Since I was a guest in their home, I was not about to critique the dinner with the exactingness of a professional restaurant reviewer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigor - the quality of being extremely thorough, exhaustive, or accurate
  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected

Etymology

From 'exacting' + '-ness', where 'exacting' derives from Latin 'exigere' (to demand).
