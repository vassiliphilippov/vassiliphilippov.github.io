fairly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɛrli / (AmE), / ˈfeəli / (BrE)
in a way that is in accordance with the rules, the law, or standards of honesty; without cheating or fraud
in an honest, rule-abiding way

Examples

They competed fairly and accepted the results with grace.
It is important to grade each student's work fairly to maintain trust.
The teams agreed that the match had been played fairly by everyone.

Synset Examples

they played fairly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honestly - in an honest or truthful way
  • Antonym: dishonestly - in a dishonest, unfair, or deceitful way

Etymology

From fair + -ly, dating to Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɛrli / (AmE), / ˈfeəli / (BrE)
in a fair, just, or impartial manner; without bias or favoritism
in a fair and impartial way

Examples

The teacher always tries to treat her students fairly regardless of their backgrounds.
We should divide the chores fairly so that no one feels overburdened.
The company was praised for dealing fairly with its customers.

Synset Examples

deal fairly with one another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justly - in a just, fair, or morally correct way
  • Antonym: unfairly - in an unjust or biased way

Etymology

From fair (Middle English, from Old English fæger: pleasing, attractive) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

4. ADV

/ ˈfɛrli / (AmE), / ˈfeəli / (BrE)
to some extent or degree; moderately but not completely or extremely.
to some extent; moderately

Examples

The task was fairly easy, so she completed it in just an hour.
That movie was fairly entertaining, but I've seen better.
His explanation was fairly clear, though some parts were confusing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rather - to some degree; quite (British English)
  • Synonym: somewhat - to some degree; slightly
  • Degree Higher: extremely - to a very high degree; much more than fairly
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small extent; less than fairly

Etymology

From Middle English 'ferly' (wonder, marvel), later influenced by 'fair' (in accordance with the rules or standards).
