to speak

1. VERB

/ spiːk / (AmE), / spiːk / (BrE)
to give a speech or formal talk to an audience
give a speech or talk

Examples

The mayor was invited to speak at the graduation ceremony.
She will speak to the shareholders at tomorrow's meeting.
Many guests were asked to speak about their experiences during the conference.

Synset Examples

The chairman addressed the board of trustees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address - to speak formally to an audience
  • Synonym: lecture - to deliver an educational speech to an audience
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00991233-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spiːk / (AmE), / spiːk / (BrE)
to exchange thoughts or ideas by talking with someone; to have a conversation
to talk or converse

Examples

Whenever we speak, we always end up laughing about something.
The manager wanted to speak with her about the project.
Have you spoken to your parents yet about your decision?

Synset Examples

We often talk business
Actions talk louder than words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk - to speak in order to give information or express ideas; converse
  • Synonym: converse - to talk informally with another or others

Etymology

Old English *specan*, *sprecan* (to speak, utter words), related to Dutch *spreken*, German *sprechen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00964479-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ spiːk / (AmE), / spiːk / (BrE)
(of an object or thing) to produce a characteristic or natural sound, often in a way that seems expressive or meaningful.
produce a characteristic or expressive sound

Examples

The thunder spoke across the midnight sky.
When the old piano finally spoke, its notes filled the room with warmth.
With every beat, the drums spoke to the crowd in their own language.

Synset Examples

The drums spoke

Etymology

From Old English 'specan', 'sprecan', meaning to utter words or sounds.
