Picrasma excelsa

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkræzmə ɪkˈsɛlsə / (AmE), / pɪˈkræzmə ɪkˈsɛlsə / (BrE)
A West Indian tree whose wood is a source of the drug Jamaica quassia, used medicinally and as a flavoring agent.
West Indian tree; Jamaica quassia source

Examples

The wood of Picrasma excelsa is often harvested for medicinal purposes.
Traditional herbalists use extracts from Picrasma excelsa to prepare digestive tonics.
Picrasma excelsa is native to the Caribbean and valued for its bitter compounds.

Etymology

From genus name 'Picrasma' (Greek 'pikros', bitter) and species 'excelsa', Latin for 'lofty' or 'high'.
