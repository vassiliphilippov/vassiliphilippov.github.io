liquidambar

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪkwɪˈdæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˈdæmbə / (BrE)
an aromatic resinous substance obtained from certain sweet gum trees, used in perfumes and medicines
aromatic resin from sweet gum trees

Examples

The perfumer used liquidambar to give the fragrance a warm, balsamic note.
Early physicians valued liquidambar for its supposed medicinal properties.
A sticky liquidambar oozed from the bark of the sweet gum tree after it was scored.

Etymology

From New Latin Liquidambar, genus name, from Latin liquidus (liquid) + Arabic ambar (amber).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪkwəˈdæmbɑr / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˈdæmbɑː / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Liquidambar, known for their star-shaped leaves and spiky fruits.
a tree of the Liquidambar genus

Examples

The leaves of the liquidambar turn brilliant red and orange in the autumn.
A mature liquidambar can reach up to 40 meters in height.
The seeds of the liquidambar are contained within distinctive spiky fruit balls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetgum - a common name for trees of the Liquidambar genus

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidus' (liquid) + 'ambar' (amber), referring to the aromatic resin the trees produce.
