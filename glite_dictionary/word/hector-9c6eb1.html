to hector

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛktər / (AmE), / ˈhɛktə / (BrE)
to behave in a bossy or bullying way towards someone, often by intimidating or overawing them
bully or intimidate someone

Examples

He would often hector his colleagues with unsolicited advice.
The manager was known to hector new employees on their first day.
During the meeting, she hectored anyone who disagreed with her plans.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to act aggressively toward someone weaker
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, especially for advantage
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Latin Hector, the Trojan hero, whose name became associated with blustering bravery in English.
