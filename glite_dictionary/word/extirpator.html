extirpator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.stɚˌpeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛk.stɪˌpeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a farm implement designed to remove weeds or unwanted plants from soil
farm tool for removing weeds

Examples

The farmer used an extirpator to clear the weeds from his field before planting.
Modern extirpators are often attached to tractors for large-scale weed removal.
Regular use of an extirpator can help maintain healthy crop growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeder - a tool for removing weeds

Etymology

From Latin 'extirpare' (to root out or destroy).
