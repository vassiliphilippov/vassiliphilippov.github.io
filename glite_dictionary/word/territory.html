territory

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈter.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
an area of land under the jurisdiction and control of a sovereign state or authority
area under control of a country

Examples

The government claimed that the disputed island was their territory.
Foreign troops entered the nation's territory without permission.
New trade regulations apply to every part of the territory.

Synset Examples

American troops were stationed on Japanese soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area controlled by a ruler or government
  • Synonym: province - an administrative division or unit of a country
  • Synonym: colony - a territory under the political control of another country

Etymology

From Latin 'territorium' (land around a town), from 'terra' (land).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪtəri / (BrE)
An area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state, or marked off for administrative, political, or other specific purposes.
area of land under jurisdiction or administration

Examples

The disputed territory has been the cause of tension between the two countries.
Many indigenous peoples were forced to move from their ancestral territory.
The wildlife reserve covers a large territory of forest and grassland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - an area, especially part of a country or the world
  • Synonym: province - an administrative division or area of a country
  • Degree Higher: country - a nation with its own government, occupying a particular territory

Etymology

From Latin 'territorium', meaning 'land around a town, domain'.
