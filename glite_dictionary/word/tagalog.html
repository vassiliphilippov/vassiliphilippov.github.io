Tagalog

1. PROPN

/ təˈɡɑːlɔɡ / (AmE), / təˈɡɑːlɒɡ / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken by the Tagalog people of the Philippines, and the basis of the national language Filipino
Austronesian language of the Philippines

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Tagalog.
Many Filipinos learn Tagalog as their first language at home.
The film included subtitles in both Tagalog and English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Filipino - the standardized form of Tagalog used as the national language of the Philippines

Etymology

From Tagalog 'Tagálog', the name of the ethnic group.

2. NOUN

/ təˈɡɑːlɔɡ / (AmE), / təˈɡɑːlɒɡ / (BrE)
a member of a people native to the Philippines, chiefly inhabiting central Luzon, including Manila
person native to central Luzon, Philippines

Examples

Many Tagalog live in and around Manila, the capital of the Philippines.
She belongs to the Tagalog ethnic group and speaks the language fluently.
The traditions of the Tagalog are an important part of Filipino culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Filipino - a native or inhabitant of the Philippines

Etymology

From Tagalog 'Tagálog', from 'taga-', meaning 'native of', and 'ilog', meaning 'river'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10707987-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ təˈɡɑːlɔɡ / (AmE), / təˈɡɑːlɒɡ / (BrE)
a language of the Philippines, spoken especially in and around Manila, on which the national language Filipino is based
a major language of the Philippines

Examples

She is fluent in Tagalog, having grown up in Manila.
Many Filipino families speak both English and Tagalog at home.
He is taking a course to improve his Tagalog comprehension.

Etymology

From Tagalog *Tagalog* (river dweller), from *taga* ('from') and *ilog* ('river').
