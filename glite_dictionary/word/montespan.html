Montespan

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɒntɛˈspɑ̃ / (AmE), / ˌmɒntɛˈspɑ̃ / (BrE)
(Françoise-Athénaïs de Rochechouart de Mortemart, 1641–1707) A French noblewoman who was the influential mistress of King Louis XIV until he turned his favor to Madame de Maintenon.
French noblewoman; mistress of Louis XIV

Examples

Montespan was famous for her influence over the French court in the 17th century.
Historians often discuss the relationship between Louis XIV and Montespan when examining the politics of Versailles.
The rivalry between Montespan and Madame de Maintenon remains a subject of great interest.

Etymology

From the title 'Marquise de Montespan,' named after a French town; title held by Françoise-Athénaïs de Rochechouart.
