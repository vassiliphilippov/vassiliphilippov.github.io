John L. Lewis

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɑn ɛl ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒn ɛl ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(1880–1969) United States labor leader, president of the United Mine Workers of America (1920–1960) and president of the Congress of Industrial Organizations (1935–1940).
American labor leader (1880–1969)

Examples

John L. Lewis played a pivotal role in organizing labor strikes during the Great Depression.
The career of John L. Lewis greatly influenced American labor unions in the twentieth century.
Many historians consider John L. Lewis to be one of the most significant leaders in labor history.
