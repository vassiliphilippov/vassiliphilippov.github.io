Cacatua galerita

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkɑːtuː.ə ɡæˈlɛrɪtə / (AmE), / kæˈkɑːtjʊə ɡaˈlɛrɪtə / (BrE)
A species of large white cockatoo native to Australia and nearby islands, distinguished by its yellow, erectile crest.
a large white cockatoo with a yellow crest

Examples

The Cacatua galerita is easily recognized by its bright yellow crest and loud call.
Wild Cacatua galerita are a common sight in many Australian suburbs.
The zoo recently welcomed a new Cacatua galerita to its aviary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur-crested cockatoo - common English name for Cacatua galerita

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Cacatua' is the genus for cockatoos and 'galerita' refers to 'crested'.
