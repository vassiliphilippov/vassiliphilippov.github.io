accompanying

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌmpəniɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpəniɪŋ / (BrE)
happening with or as a result of something else; occurring together or as a consequence.
occurring with or as a result of something

Examples

The new medication was effective, but there were several accompanying side effects.
An accompanying booklet provides further explanation for first-time users.
The bad weather and its accompanying delays frustrated many travelers.

Synset Examples

an excessive growth of bureaucracy, with attendant problems
snags incidental to the changeover in management
attendant circumstances
the period of tension and consequent need for military preparedness
the ensuant response to his appeal
the resultant savings were considerable
collateral target damage from a bombing run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequent - happening as a result of something else
  • Synonym: attendant - occurring with or as a result of something else

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'accompany,' from Old French 'acompagnier' (to accompany), from 'compagnon' (companion).
