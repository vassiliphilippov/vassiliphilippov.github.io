tintack

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌtæk / (BrE)
A small nail or tack made of tinned iron, typically used for fastening light materials.
small tinned iron nail or tack

Examples

He used a tintack to secure the label onto the wooden crate.
The old bulletin board was full of rusty tintacks.
She pried the tintack out of the wall with a small screwdriver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tack - a small sharp-pointed nail used for fastening

Etymology

Formed from 'tin' (the metal) and 'tack' (a small nail), indicating a tack made of or coated with tin.
