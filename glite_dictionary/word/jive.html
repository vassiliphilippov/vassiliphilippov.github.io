jive

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪv / (AmE), / dʒaɪv / (BrE)
a style of jazz music played by big bands, popular in the 1930s, characterized by flowing rhythms and less complex arrangements than later jazz styles
1930s jazz style with flowing rhythms

Examples

The band played an upbeat jive that got everyone dancing.
He collected old records featuring classic jive tunes from the 1930s.
Many music historians credit jive as influencing later jazz developments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - a style of jazz music with a strong, lively rhythm, popular in the 1930s and 1940s

2. VERB

/ dʒaɪv / (AmE), / dʒaɪv / (BrE)
to dance to jive music, typically in a lively and energetic style
dance to jive music

Examples

At the party, everyone started to jive as soon as the band played their favorite song.
She jived across the dance floor with effortless energy and a big smile.
The instructor taught us how to jive in our first swing dance lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boogie - to dance energetically to popular music

Etymology

From African-American slang of the 1920s, possibly from earlier meaning 'deceptive talk'.
