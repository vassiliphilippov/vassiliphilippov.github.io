NWbN

1. NOUN

/ ɛn dʌbəl.juː biː ɛn / (AmE), / ɛn dʌbəl.juː biː ɛn / (BrE)
The compass point that is one point north of northwest, equal to 326°15′ on the compass.
compass point between northwest and north-northwest

Examples

The boat maintained a steady course of NWbN through the night.
The wind shifted and began to blow from the NWbN, cooling the air considerably.
Navigators often refer to NWbN when charting detailed directions at sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: northwest - the compass point at 315 degrees
  • Degree Higher: north-northwest - the compass point at 337.5 degrees

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'northwest by north', used in traditional mariner's compass terminology.
