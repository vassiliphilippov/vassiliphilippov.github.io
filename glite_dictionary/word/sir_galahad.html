Sir Galahad

1. PROPN

/ sər ˈɡæləhæd / (AmE), / sə ˈɡæləhæd / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) the most virtuous knight of the Round Table, renowned for his purity and his quest for the Holy Grail.
knight of Arthurian legend; seeker of the Holy Grail

Examples

The legend tells that Sir Galahad was the only knight pure enough to behold the Holy Grail.
Stories of Sir Galahad emphasize his unblemished character and unwavering faith.
In the old tales, Sir Galahad's quest inspired generations of knights to strive for virtue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sir Lancelot - another famous knight of the Round Table in Arthurian legend

Etymology

From Old French Galaad, from Medieval Latin Galaadus, a character in Arthurian legend.
