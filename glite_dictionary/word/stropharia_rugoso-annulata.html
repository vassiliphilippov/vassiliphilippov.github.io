Stropharia rugoso-annulata

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈfɛəriə ruˌgoʊsoʊ æn.jəˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / strɒˈfɛəriə ruːˌɡəʊsə æn.jʊˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large edible mushroom with a broad, dark brown cap, long stalk, and white gills that become purplish brown as it matures.
a large edible mushroom species

Examples

The chef added freshly picked Stropharia rugoso-annulata to the pasta for a rich, earthy flavor.
In the wild, Stropharia rugoso-annulata grows in wood chips and garden beds.
Many mushroom enthusiasts cultivate Stropharia rugoso-annulata for its edibility and robust growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wine cap - common name for Stropharia rugoso-annulata

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Stropharia' and Latin 'rugoso-annulata' meaning 'wrinkled ring'.
