ploughboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a boy who leads or guides the animals that pull a plow, especially as a farm worker
boy leading animals that pull a plow

Examples

The ploughboy rose at dawn to begin his work in the fields.
In the old painting, a ploughboy walks behind a team of oxen.
Stories of the hardworking ploughboy were common in rural England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploughman - a man who guides a plow or drives animals pulling it

Etymology

From 'plough' (Old English plōh) + 'boy' (Middle English boi, boye).
