Hamamelis virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmilɪs vɜrdʒɪˈneɪnə / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈmiːlɪs vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnɪə / (BrE)
A shrub native to eastern North America, known for its small yellow flowers that appear after the leaves have fallen; commonly called witch hazel.
North American shrub, also called witch hazel

Examples

Hamamelis virginiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental yellow flowers in late autumn.
The bark and leaves of Hamamelis virginiana have been used in traditional remedies for skin irritations.
In the fall, the woods were brightened by the golden blossoms of Hamamelis virginiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - Common name for Hamamelis virginiana, a shrub with medicinal uses

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Hamamelis' and species name 'virginiana' referring to Virginia.
