homeostasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊ.mi.oʊˈsteɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊ.mi.əʊˈsteɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the tendency of a living organism or cell to maintain a stable internal environment despite changes in external conditions, through self-regulating processes
maintenance of stable internal conditions in organisms
medical

Examples

The body relies on homeostasis to keep its temperature within a safe range.
A lack of water disrupts homeostasis and can lead to serious health problems.
Sweating is one way our bodies maintain homeostasis during hot weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilibrium - a state where opposing forces or influences are balanced

Etymology

From Greek 'homoios' (similar) + 'stasis' (standing, state).
