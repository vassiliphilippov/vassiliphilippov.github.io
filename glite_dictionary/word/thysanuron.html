thysanuron

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈsæn.jʊr.ɒn / (AmE), / θɪˈsan.jʊ.rɒn / (BrE)
A primitive, wingless insect belonging to the order Thysanura, commonly known as a bristletail.
primitive wingless insect (bristletail)

Examples

The thysanuron is often found under stones and in leaf litter in damp environments.
Scientists studied the diet of the thysanuron in its natural habitat.
Unlike many insects, a thysanuron never develops wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristletail - a primitive, wingless insect with bristle-like tails

Etymology

From New Latin Thysanura, from Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) + oura (tail).
