armband

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈɑːmˌbænd / (BrE)
A band of cloth or other material worn around the upper arm, often as a symbol of rank, mourning, or identification.
band worn around the upper arm

Examples

The captain wore a black armband to honor his late teammate.
Security staff were easily identified by their red armbands.
During the ceremony, each participant donned a white armband as a sign of peace.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈɑːmbænd / (BrE)
A band, usually of cloth or metal, worn around the upper arm, often as a means of identification or to signify mourning or affiliation.
band worn around the upper arm

Examples

Every player on the team wore a black armband to honor the late coach.
Volunteers were given a red armband so they could be easily identified.
She wore a commemorative armband during the ceremony.
