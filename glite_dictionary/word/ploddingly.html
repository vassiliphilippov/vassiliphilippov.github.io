ploddingly

1. ADV

/ ˈplɑdɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈplɒdɪŋli / (BrE)
In a slow, heavy, and laborious manner, often implying lack of imagination or energy.
in a slow, laborious manner

Examples

She ploddingly read through the lengthy report, struggling to maintain focus.
The story moves ploddingly, without much excitement or variation in pace.
He ploddingly completed each task on his list, never rushing or cutting corners.

Synset Examples

this writer ploddingly accumulates detail after detail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laboriously - with a lot of effort and difficulty
  • Antonym: energetically - in a vigorous and active manner
