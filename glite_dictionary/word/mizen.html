mizen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪz.ən / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.ən / (BrE)
the after and usually shorter mast of a ship, such as a yawl, ketch, or dandy, typically the third mast from the bow in vessels with three or more masts
aft or third mast on some sailing ships

Examples

The crew climbed the mizen to adjust the sails during the storm.
A ketch has a mizen located aft of the main mast.
He stood at the base of the mizen, watching the sun set over the horizon.

Etymology

From Middle English 'mesan', from Old French 'mizaine', from Italian 'mezzana' (meaning 'middle sail').

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪzən / (AmE), / ˈmɪzᵊn / (BrE)
A fore-and-aft sail set on the mizzenmast of a sailing vessel.
sail set on the mizzenmast

Examples

The crew quickly hoisted the mizen to take advantage of the changing wind.
He adjusted the mizen for better balance while steering the ship.
During the storm, the mizen was torn but the other sails remained intact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mizzen - variant spelling and synonym: sail set on the mizzenmast

Etymology

Altered form of 'mizzen,' from Middle French 'misaine' (foremast), ultimately from Old Italian 'mezzana' (middle).
