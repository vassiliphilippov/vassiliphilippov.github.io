to imbue

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbju / (AmE), / ɪmˈbjuː / (BrE)
to fill or permeate someone or something with a particular quality, feeling, or idea
to fill or permeate with a quality or feeling

Examples

His writing is imbued with a deep sense of nostalgia for his childhood.
The artist sought to imbue the sculpture with a sense of movement and life.
Centuries-old traditions still imbue the ceremonies of the village.

Synset Examples

soak the bandage with disinfectant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infuse - to fill something with a quality or emotion
  • Synonym: soak - to make thoroughly wet or saturated

Etymology

From Latin 'imbuere' (to moisten, stain).

3. VERB

/ ɪmˈbju / (AmE), / ɪmˈbjuː / (BrE)
to permeate or suffuse a material or object with color; to dye or tint deeply
to suffuse or dye with color

Examples

The artist sought to imbue the canvas with a rich, golden hue.
Morning sunlight seemed to imbue the mountains with a soft pink glow.
The dye will imbue the fabric with deep, vibrant colors.

Etymology

From Latin imbuere (to moisten, wet, stain).
