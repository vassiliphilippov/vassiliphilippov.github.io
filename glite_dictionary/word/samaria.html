Samaria

1. PROPN

/ səˈmɛriə / (AmE), / səˈmeəriə / (BrE)
an ancient city in central Palestine, established in the 9th century BC as the capital of the northern kingdom of Israel; its site is now in northwestern Jordan
ancient city, former capital of northern Israel

Examples

The ruins of Samaria offer insight into the history of the ancient Israelites.
Many biblical events are said to have taken place in or near Samaria.
In the 9th century BC, Samaria became the new capital of the northern kingdom.

Etymology

From Hebrew Shomron, the original name of the city.
