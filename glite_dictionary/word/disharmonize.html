to disharmonize

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈhɑrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (sounds, voices, etc.) to be harsh and unpleasant; to make discordant
cause to sound harsh or discordant

Examples

The improperly tuned instruments began to disharmonize the orchestra's performance.
Too many clashing voices can disharmonize an otherwise pleasant choir.
Modern architecture can sometimes disharmonize with the classic style of older neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to harmonize - to bring into harmony; to make agreeable or consonant
  • Synonym: discord - lack of harmony between sounds; dissonance

Etymology

Formed from prefix dis- (indicating negation or removal) and harmonize (to make harmonious), from Greek harmonia (agreement, harmony).
