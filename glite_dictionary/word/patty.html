patty

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæti / (AmE), / ˈpæti / (BrE)
A small, flat mass of chopped or ground food, such as meat or vegetables, often shaped and cooked for eating.
small flattened piece of food

Examples

He placed a beef patty on the grill and waited for it to sizzle.
The chef shaped the salmon mixture into a patty before frying it.
Vegetarians often substitute a bean patty for meat in their burgers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burger - a sandwich consisting of a cooked patty of ground meat, usually beef, in a sliced bun
  • Synonym: croquette - a small roll of chopped food, typically meat or fish, coated in breadcrumbs and fried

Etymology

Diminutive of 'pat', meaning a small flat cake or lump.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæti / (AmE), / ˈpæti / (BrE)
A small pie or pasty, often filled with meat, vegetables, or other ingredients and baked or fried.
small pie or pasty

Examples

She bought a hot chicken patty from the bakery for lunch.
Jamaican patties are popular snacks filled with spicy beef or vegetables.
Every year, the festival features a contest for the best homemade patty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasty - a small pastry case filled with meat or vegetables

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæti / (AmE), / ˈpæti / (BrE)
A small, round, flat sweet or candy, typically made with chocolate, mint, or other flavors.
small round flat candy

Examples

She offered me a patty filled with rich chocolate and a creamy mint center.
My favorite treat at the shop is the homemade peanut butter patty.
Each guest received a small patty wrapped in decorative foil as a party favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint patty - a type of round, flat candy with mint flavor

Etymology

From earlier meaning 'little pie or cake', based on French 'pâté' (paste or pie).
