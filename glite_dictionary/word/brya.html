Brya

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of prickly shrubs and small trees native to the Caribbean, valued for their durable hardwood.
genus of Caribbean shrubs and trees

Examples

The wood of Brya species is prized for its strength and durability.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Brya trees in Jamaica.
Caribbean artisans often use Brya for making fine furniture and musical instruments.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name given by botanists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of prickly shrubs and small trees native to the Caribbean region, known for producing durable hardwood.
genus of Caribbean hardwood shrubs and trees

Examples

The famous Jamaican rain tree belongs to the Brya genus, prized for its strong timber.
Carpenters value the durable wood produced by Brya species for fine furniture.
Many Brya trees grow well in dry, sunny conditions across the Caribbean islands.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a local Caribbean name (specific origin uncertain).
