neuroanatomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnʊroʊ.əˈnæt̬.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊ.əˈnæt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the anatomy of neural tissue or the nervous system
relating to the anatomy of nerves or brain
medical

Examples

The neuroanatomic structure of the brain is complex and highly organized.
Researchers examined the neuroanatomic changes associated with early stages of Alzheimer's disease.
The MRI scan provided detailed images of the patient's neuroanatomic abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuroanatomical - relating to the anatomy of the nervous system

Etymology

From 'neuro-', meaning nerve, and 'anatomic', from Greek 'anatome' (dissection).
