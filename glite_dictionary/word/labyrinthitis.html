labyrinthitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæb.ə.rɪnˈθaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌlæb.ɪ.rɪnˈθaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the inner ear, typically causing vertigo and loss of balance
inflammation of the inner ear
medical

Examples

After experiencing severe dizziness and nausea, she was diagnosed with labyrinthitis by her doctor.
The symptoms of labyrinthitis can include sudden vertigo, hearing loss, and ringing in the ear.
Rest and hydration were recommended to help her recover from a bout of labyrinthitis.

Etymology

From Latin 'labyrinthus' (labyrinth) + Greek '-itis' (inflammation).
