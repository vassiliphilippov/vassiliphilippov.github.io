span

1. NOUN

/ spæn / (AmE), / spæn / (BrE)
the act of sitting or standing with one leg on each side of something; the position of being astride
act of sitting or standing astride

Examples

The cowboy adjusted his span on the horse before the race began.
She maintained a confident span as she climbed onto the bicycle.
His span in the saddle showed years of riding experience.

2. NOUN

/ spæn / (AmE), / spæn / (BrE)
an old unit of length, approximately 9 inches, based on the width of an expanded human hand
unit of length, about 9 inches

Examples

The board was measured as three spans long.
In olden times, a span was a handy way to estimate short distances.
He stretched his hand across the cloth and marked a span near the edge.

Etymology

From Old English *spann* (distance from thumb to little finger with hand extended).

4. NOUN

/ spæn / (AmE), / spæn / (BrE)
A part of a structure, such as a bridge, that stretches across an obstacle like a river, road, or valley.
section of a bridge or structure crossing an obstacle

Examples

The central span of the bridge was lifted for repairs last month.
Engineers measured the length of the span crossing the river.
A new span was added to accommodate increased traffic over the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure supporting the weight of a bridge or roof
  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used in construction

Etymology

From Old English 'span', meaning the distance measured by a handspan, later extended to mean something that bridges a gap.
