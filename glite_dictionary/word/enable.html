to enable

1. VERB

/ ɪˈneɪbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪbl̩ / (BrE)
to make someone or something able to do something; to give the necessary means, power, or opportunity
make able; provide means or power

Examples

This new training program will enable employees to work more efficiently.
A grant from the government enabled the museum to reopen after renovations.
Better technology is enabling doctors to diagnose diseases earlier than ever before.

Synset Examples

This skill will enable you to find a job on Wall Street
The rope enables you to secure yourself when you climb the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empower - to give someone the authority or power to do something
  • Antonym: to disable - to make unable to function or act

Etymology

From Old French enabler, from Latin habilis (able).
