Geranium robertianum

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm ˌrɒbərˈtiːənəm / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪniəm ˌrɒbəˈtiːənəm / (BrE)
a low-growing, sticky herb with small reddish-purple flowers, found widely in the Northern Hemisphere
herbaceous plant with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The wooded area was dotted with vibrant patches of Geranium robertianum in bloom.
Gardeners often remove Geranium robertianum to prevent it from spreading uncontrollably.
In early summer, clusters of Geranium robertianum brighten the forest floor with their purple flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Geranium' (genus of plants), and 'robertianum' from Medieval Latin, referring to St. Robert, after whom the plant is named.
