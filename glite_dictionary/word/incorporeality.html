incorporeality

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɔrˌpɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɔːpɔːrɪˈalɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being immaterial or without a physical body; not consisting of matter
state of being immaterial or bodiless

Examples

Many religious traditions emphasize the incorporeality of the soul as distinct from the body.
The philosopher discussed the incorporeality of certain abstract concepts like justice and love.
Ghost stories often center on the eerie incorporeality of their supernatural beings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immateriality - the quality of not being composed of matter; lack of physical substance
  • Antonym: corporeality - the quality of being physical or having a body

Etymology

From Late Latin 'incorporealitas', from 'incorporeus' (bodiless, immaterial).
