isolex

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.soʊ.lɛks / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.səʊ.lɛks / (BrE)
An isogloss delimiting the area in which a particular word or vocabulary item is used.
linguistics: boundary for a vocabulary item

Examples

The isolex clearly separates the region where the word 'pail' is used from that where 'bucket' predominates.
Linguists mapped the isolex for the local word meaning 'corn' across several neighboring villages.
Discovering a new isolex helped researchers understand vocabulary changes across dialect areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogloss - A boundary marking the limit of a particular linguistic feature

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'lexis' (word, speech).
