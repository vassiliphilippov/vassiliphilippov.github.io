agnostic

1. ADJ

/ æɡˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to agnosticism or to an agnostic; holding the view that the existence of ultimate reality, such as God, is unknown and possibly unknowable.
relating to agnosticism or an agnostic

Examples

His agnostic beliefs prevent him from committing to any particular religion.
The discussion took an agnostic approach to the question of life's meaning.
She described herself as agnostic on the issue, neither agreeing nor disagreeing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - person who disbelieves or lacks belief in the existence of God or gods

Etymology

From Greek 'agnōstos' (unknown, unknowable), via late 19th-century English.

2. NOUN

/ æɡˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
a person who is doubtful or noncommittal about something, especially regarding the existence of a deity or the truth of a particular claim
person who is doubtful or noncommittal

Examples

She considers herself an agnostic when it comes to political ideologies.
As an agnostic, he prefers to keep an open mind about complex scientific debates.
Many agnostics demand stronger evidence before accepting new claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to question or doubt accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true or real, often without proof

Etymology

From Greek 'agnōstos' (unknown, unknowable), formed from 'a-' (not) + 'gnōstos' (known).

3. ADJ

/ æɡˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
not having a definite belief about whether something is true or can be known; skeptical and noncommittal about claims to knowledge
uncertain or noncommittal about claims to knowledge

Examples

As a scientist, she remained agnostic about the ultimate explanation for the phenomenon.
He took an agnostic position on the debate, refusing to support either side without more evidence.
The committee was agnostic regarding the proposed policy changes until further information became available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - having doubts or reservations; denying certainty
  • Antonym: dogmatic - inclined to lay down principles as undeniably true

Etymology

From Greek 'agnōstos' (unknown, unknowable), from 'a-' (not) + 'gnōstos' (known).

4. NOUN

/ æɡˈnɑːstɪk / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
a person who believes that nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God or of anything beyond material phenomena; a person who neither believes nor disbelieves in God
person doubting knowledge of God

Examples

She considers herself an agnostic because she feels there's no way to know if God exists.
Many agnostics argue that religious truth is unknowable and prefer to suspend judgment.
The debate included both a passionate agnostic and a convinced believer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - a person who does not believe in the existence of God or gods
  • Antonym: theist - a person who believes in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

From Greek 'agnōstos' (unknown, unknowable), formed from 'a-' (not) and 'gnōstos' (known).
