toothless

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuθ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈtuːθ.ləs / (BrE)
Having no teeth; lacking teeth.
having no teeth

Examples

After having most of her teeth removed, the grandmother was almost completely toothless.
The snake's toothless jaws make it harmless to humans.
Conservationists discovered a toothless fish species in the remote lake.

Synset Examples

most birds are toothless
a toothless old crone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dentate - having teeth or toothlike projections

Etymology

From 'tooth' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtuθləs / (AmE), / ˈtuːθləs / (BrE)
(of a rule, law, organization, etc.) lacking the power or force to be effective or to achieve its intended purpose.
lacking effectiveness or power

Examples

The new environmental regulation proved toothless after industry lobbyists weakened its main provisions.
Many consider the committee toothless because it has no authority to enforce its recommendations.
Critics say the treaty is largely toothless without a mechanism for imposing penalties.

Synset Examples

a toothless piece of legislation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffective - not producing any significant effect
  • Synonym: impotent - lacking power or ability
  • Antonym: powerful - having great power or influence
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00839517-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈtuθləs / (AmE), / ˈtuːθləs / (BrE)
Having no real power or effectiveness; lacking the ability to act or enforce.
lacking power or effectiveness

Examples

The proposed law was criticized as being toothless and unlikely to change anything.
Without proper funding, the organization remained toothless in the face of the crisis.
Some believe the new policy is toothless because it lacks any real enforcement measures.

Synset Examples

The committee's rules felt toothless and ineffective.
The campaign was toothless without proper funding.
Her toothlessness in the debate was apparent to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffective - not producing the intended result
  • Synonym: powerless - without power or ability to act
