CUPAL

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːˌpæl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːˌpal / (BrE)
A composite material or plating made of copper and aluminum, typically used for its conductive and structural properties.
copper-aluminum composite material

Examples

The electrical contacts were made from CUPAL to ensure both strength and conductivity.
Engineers selected CUPAL for the connectors due to its resistance to corrosion.
Because CUPAL combines copper’s conductivity with aluminum’s lightness, it is popular in manufacturing.

Etymology

Blend of 'copper' and 'aluminum'.
