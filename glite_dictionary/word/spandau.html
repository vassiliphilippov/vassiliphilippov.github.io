Spandau

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæn.daʊ / (AmE), / ˈspæn.daʊ / (BrE)
a German-made machine gun, especially the MG 08 used during the two World Wars
German machine gun (historical)

Examples

The British troops faced heavy fire from a Spandau during the offensive.
Collectors value authentic World War I Spandau machine guns for their historical significance.
He described the distinctive sound of a Spandau echoing across the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machine gun - automatic gun that fires bullets rapidly

Etymology

Named after Spandau, a district of Berlin where the gun was manufactured.
