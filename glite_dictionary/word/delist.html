to delist

1. VERB

/ diˈlɪst / (AmE), / diˈlɪst / (BrE)
to remove (a security or company) from the official list of a stock exchange so that it is no longer publicly traded there
remove from a stock exchange listing

Examples

The company decided to delist its shares from the New York Stock Exchange.
After repeated violations, the exchange threatened to delist the firm for failing to meet requirements.
If profits do not improve, the board may vote to delist the stock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: list - to register or admit (a security) to trading on a stock exchange

Etymology

Formed from prefix de- (removal) and list (registered item).
