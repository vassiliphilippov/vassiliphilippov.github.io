Onychomys

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊˈnɪkəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈnɪkəməɪz / (BrE)
A genus of small, carnivorous rodents native to North America, commonly known as grasshopper mice.
genus of carnivorous grasshopper mice

Examples

The Onychomys is unique among mice for its predatory behavior and piercing howl.
Scientists observed Onychomys hunting insects in the desert at night.
Unlike other mice, Onychomys feed on scorpions and beetles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper mouse - a small, carnivorous rodent of North America, known for feeding on insects

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek onyx (claw) and mys (mouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɒnɪˈkoʊmɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒnɪˈkəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodent known as grasshopper mice, native to North America and noted for their predatory behavior.
genus of grasshopper mice

Examples

The Onychomys species are unusual among rodents for their carnivorous diet.
Researchers observed the hunting habits of Onychomys in the Sonoran Desert.
Unlike other mice, Onychomys is known for its distinctive vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper mouse - A common name for species of the genus Onychomys

Etymology

From Greek 'onyx' (claw) and 'mys' (mouse), referring to the animal's prominent claws.
