oratorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːrəˈtɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɒrəˈtɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the art or practice of public speaking, or characteristic of an orator
relating to public speaking or orators

Examples

The senator delivered his speech in a deeply oratorical style, captivating the entire audience.
Her essays are marked by an oratorical tone that reflects her experience in public speaking.
He won the competition with his vivid and oratorical prose.

Synset Examples

oratorical prose
harangued his men in an oratorical way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetorical - relating to using language effectively, especially to persuade
  • Antonym: conversational - relating to conversation or informal speaking

Etymology

From Latin oratorius (pertaining to an orator) + -al.
