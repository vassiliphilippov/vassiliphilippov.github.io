East Indian

1. ADJ

/ ist ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
of or relating to the East Indies or its people, culture, or products
relating to the East Indies

Examples

The East Indian spices were highly prized in European markets.
She studied the impact of East Indian trade on British history.
Many East Indian recipes use a blend of aromatic herbs and spices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Indian - relating to the West Indies

Etymology

From 'East Indies', a historic European term for South and Southeast Asia, plus the suffix '-an'.

2. NOUN

/ ist ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the East Indies, especially the Indian subcontinent or Southeast Asia
native of the East Indies

Examples

Many East Indians settled in coastal cities during the 19th century.
The cuisine of East Indians is renowned for its rich spices and diverse flavors.
A group of East Indians gathered to celebrate a traditional festival.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Indian - a native or inhabitant of the West Indies

Etymology

Refers to the eastern regions of India and nearby Southeast Asian islands, as distinguished from the West Indies.

Synset POS: n
