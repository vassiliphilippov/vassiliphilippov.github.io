xeranthemum

1. NOUN

/ zɛrˈænθəməm / (AmE), / zɪəˈranθəməm / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Xeranthemum, native to southern Europe, with silvery or papery flower heads and purplish tubular flowers.
a southern European plant genus with silvery flower heads

Examples

The dried xeranthemum retained its silvery sheen long after being picked.
Gardeners often plant xeranthemum for its papery flowers that last through the winter.
A bouquet of xeranthemum adds a unique charm to any floral arrangement.

Etymology

From Greek xēranthēmon, from xēros (dry) + anthemon (flower), referring to the 'everlasting' quality of its flowers.
