rackety

1. ADJ

/ ˈrækəti / (AmE), / ˈrækəti / (BrE)
noisy in an uncontrolled or boisterous way; making or characterized by loud noise
noisy; boisterous

Examples

The rackety crowd next door kept us awake all night.
Children burst into the rackety playground, shouting and laughing.
The old bus made a rackety journey down the bumpy road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisy - making a lot of noise
  • Antonym: quiet - making very little or no noise

Etymology

From 'racket' (loud noise) + -y (adjective suffix), first attested in the 19th century.
