slump

1. VERB

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to fall suddenly and by a large amount, or to decline markedly, especially in quantity, value, or activity
fall or decline suddenly and markedly

Examples

The company's profits slumped after a series of poor investments.
After running the marathon, she slumped onto the bench, exhausted.
Share prices slumped to their lowest level in five years.

Synset Examples

The real estate market fell off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • Antonym: to soar - to rise rapidly or fly high

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from Scandinavian, related to Swedish 'slumpa' (to fall or slide suddenly).

Examples

In disappointment, she slumped against the wall, unable to hide her frustration.

3. NOUN

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
A period of economic decline characterized by high unemployment, low prices, and reduced levels of trade and investment.
period of economic decline

Examples

The country is experiencing a severe slump that has put millions out of work.
After the housing bubble burst, the industry entered a prolonged slump.
Investors are cautious because of the recent slump in the global economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect slumpa (fall or slide) and Danish slump (a fall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14512697-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to sit, lean, or fall heavily and limply, especially with a bent or drooping posture
sit or fall with a drooping posture

Examples

6. NOUN

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
a period or state of decline or deterioration in performance, quality, or activity
period of decline or deterioration

Examples

After a strong start, the company suffered a prolonged slump in profits during the second quarter.
The baseball player's hitting slump lasted nearly two months before he regained his form.
Attendance at the museum fell into a noticeable slump over the winter months.

Synset Examples

a drop-off in attendance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • Antonym: boom - a period of significant growth or increase, especially economically

Etymology

Perhaps of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish slump 'a fall, a slip'.
