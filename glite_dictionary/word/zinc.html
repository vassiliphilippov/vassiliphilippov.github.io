zinc

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk / (AmE), / zɪŋk / (BrE)
a bluish-white metallic element, used especially to make alloys and to protect iron and steel from rusting
a metallic chemical element

Examples

The factory uses zinc to galvanize steel beams and prevent rust.
Doctors may recommend zinc supplements to support immune function.
Coins are often coated in zinc to improve their durability.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: iron - a strong, hard magnetic metal used in construction and manufacturing

Etymology

From German Zink, of uncertain origin, first used in the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ zɪŋk / (AmE), / zɪŋk / (BrE)
to coat or cover (metal) with a layer of zinc, especially to protect against rust
coat or cover with zinc

Examples

Automotive parts are often zinced to prevent corrosion.
The company zincs all its steel products as part of the finishing process.
Before painting, the metal surface was thoroughly zinced to ensure durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to galvanize - coat with a protective layer of zinc, usually by electrochemical process
