cockscomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑksˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒksˌkəʊm / (BrE)
the fleshy red crest on the head of a domestic fowl or other gallinaceous birds
fleshy red crest on a bird's head

Examples

The rooster's cockscomb stood tall and bright against its white feathers.
In some bird species, the cockscomb can indicate health and vitality.
Chickens with a large cockscomb are often chosen for breeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comb - the fleshy crest on a bird's head

Etymology

From cock (rooster) + comb (crest or fleshy growth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑksˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒksˌkəʊm / (BrE)
a cap worn by court jesters, typically adorned with a strip of red cloth resembling a rooster's comb
jester's cap with red strip

Examples

The jester danced into the hall, his cockscomb bobbing merrily with each step.
In medieval plays, a cockscomb was an unmistakable part of the fool's costume.
She made a cockscomb for the school play, complete with bright red fabric.

Etymology

From resemblance to the comb of a cock (rooster); the word combines 'cock' (rooster) and 'comb'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑksˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒksˌkəʊm / (BrE)
a tropical annual plant (Celosia cristata) cultivated for its brightly coloured, featherlike or crested clusters of red or yellow flowers
ornamental annual plant with bright flowers

Examples

The vibrant red cockscomb added a splash of color to the garden beds.
Gardeners often plant cockscombs for their unusual, crested flowers.
She admired the yellow cockscomb blooming next to the marigolds.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the comb of a rooster ('cock'), from Middle English, combining 'cock' (rooster) and 'comb'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑksˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒks.kəʊm / (BrE)
(archaic) A conceited man who is overly proud of his own appearance or accomplishments; a fop or dandy.
an overly vain, self-important man (archaic)
archaic

Examples

He strutted through the ballroom like a true cockscomb, drawing every eye to his flashy outfit.
The novel mocks the cockscomb who thinks too highly of his clever wit and fashion sense.
In the old court, a cockscomb would often be the subject of ridicule for his vanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man unduly devoted to style, neatness, and fashion in dress and appearance
  • Synonym: fop - a man overly concerned with his clothes and appearance

Etymology

From 'cock's comb', referring to the crest of a rooster, likening its showiness to a vain person.
