Salix triandra

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks traɪˈændrə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks traɪˈandrə / (BrE)
a species of willow native to Europe and western Asia, with light green leaves and commonly cultivated for basketry
species of willow used in basketry

Examples

The flexible branches of Salix triandra are ideal for weaving high-quality baskets.
Farmers in Europe often grow Salix triandra to supply materials for local craftspeople.
Botanists identified the specimen as Salix triandra due to its distinctive light green leaves.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Salix (willow) + triandra, from Greek tri- (three) + andros (male), referring to flower stamens.
