Ouse

1. PROPN

/ uːz / (AmE), / uːz / (BrE)
a river in northeastern England that flows southeast to join the Trent and form the Humber estuary
river in northeastern England

Examples

The Ouse is known for its historic significance and scenic beauty.
Flooding along the Ouse often affects nearby villages in Yorkshire.
We took a boat trip down the Ouse during our visit to England.

Etymology

From Old English ǣs (water) and from Celtic origins related to water and rivers.
