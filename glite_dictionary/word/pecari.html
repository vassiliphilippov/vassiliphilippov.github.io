Pecari

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈkɑːri / (AmE), / pɪˈkɑːri / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Tayassuidae, commonly known as peccaries.
genus of peccaries (mammals)

Examples

The collared peccary belongs to the genus Pecari and is found throughout the Americas.
Scientists recently studied the skull structure of members of the genus Pecari.
The genus Pecari includes species adapted to a wide range of environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - pig-like mammal in the Tayassuidae family native to the Americas

Etymology

From Latin 'pecari', a local South American name for the peccary.
