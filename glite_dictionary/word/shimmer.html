shimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmə / (BrE)
a soft, wavering or flickering light; a faint or tremulous gleam
a faint, flickering light

Examples

The shimmer of moonlight on the lake made the night feel magical.
A faint shimmer in the distance caught her eye as the sun set.
He admired the shimmer of dew on the spider web in the morning.

Synset Examples

the shimmer of colors on iridescent feathers
the play of light on the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint light or shine
  • Synonym: glimmer - a faint or wavering light
  • Degree Higher: glow - a steady light, often softer than a shine

Etymology

From Middle English *shymeren*, related to 'shine'.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmə / (BrE)
to shine with a soft or wavering light
shine with a soft or wavering light

Examples

The lake began to shimmer under the morning sun.
Her dress seemed to shimmer as she moved beneath the lights.
Heat waves made the distant road shimmer in the afternoon.

Synset Examples

Beech leaves shimmered in the moonlight

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gleam - to shine with a steady, bright light

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, reflecting the wavering quality of the light.

3. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmə / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) to shine with a soft, tremulous, or flickering light; to glimmer with a subdued or wavering brightness.
to shine softly with a flickering light

Examples

The silk scarf seemed to shimmer in the evening light.
Moonlight caused the surface of the lake to shimmer gently.
She watched as the heat made the road ahead shimmer in the midday sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a steady light
  • Synonym: glitter - to shine with a bright, shimmering reflected light
  • Antonym: dull - not shining or reflecting much light

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin or related to Middle English shimeren, possibly from Old English scimerian (to glitter, shine faintly).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
