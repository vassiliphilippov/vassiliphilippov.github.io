Codariocalyx

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌdɛri.oʊˈkælɪks / (AmE), / kəʊˌdarɪəʊˈkælɪks / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, sometimes considered a subgroup of Desmodium
genus of flowering plants (legumes)

Examples

The species in the genus Codariocalyx are known for their nitrogen-fixing properties.
Some botanists classify these plants under Codariocalyx instead of Desmodium.
Recent research has focused on the distribution of Codariocalyx in tropical Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Desmodium - a closely related genus of flowering legume plants

Etymology

From Greek kodarion (small bell) + kályx (calyx). Used in scientific Latin.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌdæri.oʊˈkælɪks / (AmE), / kəʊˌdarɪəʊˈkælɪks / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the family Fabaceae, sometimes separated from the genus Desmodium in some classifications
plant genus in the bean family

Examples

Several species formerly classified under Codariocalyx have now been placed in other genera.
Botanists debated whether Codariocalyx should be considered separate from Desmodium.
The Codariocalyx plants are recognized for their distinctive seed pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Desmodium - a related genus of flowering plants in the legume family

Etymology

From Greek *kodaros* (club) + *kalyx* (calyx, outer part of a flower).
