clowder

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈklaʊ.də / (BrE)
a group of cats
group of cats

Examples

A clowder of stray cats gathered near the old barn every evening.
She watched as a clowder lounged on the porch in the summer sun.
The animal shelter was home to a lively clowder of playful kittens and older cats.

Etymology

Origin unknown; recorded since the late 18th century, possibly an alteration of 'clutter' or influenced by 'crowd'.
