plasmablast

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæzməˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈplazməˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a cell that is an early precursor to a plasma cell, involved in the immune response by producing antibodies
precursor cell to a plasma cell
medical

Examples

Researchers observed a significant increase in plasmablasts following vaccination.
The differentiation of a plasmablast into a plasma cell is a crucial immune process.
Flow cytometry is commonly used to identify plasmablast populations in blood samples.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: plasma cell - a mature white blood cell that secretes antibodies

Etymology

From 'plasma' (from Greek 'plasma' meaning 'something molded') + 'blast' (from Greek 'blastēs' meaning 'bud' or 'germ').
