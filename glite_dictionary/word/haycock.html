haycock

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.kɒk / (BrE)
a small, cone-shaped pile of hay left in a field to dry before being gathered into a larger stack or barn
small cone-shaped pile of drying hay

Examples

The farmer built a haycock in the corner of the field to let the grass finish drying.
Children played hide-and-seek around the old haycock near the barn.
Each haycock stood evenly spaced, ready to be gathered into a larger stack.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hayrick - a large stack of hay, usually in the open air

Etymology

From 'hay' (grass cut for fodder) + 'cock' (a heap or pile), Middle English origin.
