anachronistically

1. ADV

/ əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli / (AmE), / əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is out of its proper time period or context.
in an anachronistic way

Examples

The movie portrays ancient Roman characters acting anachronistically, using modern slang and gestures.
She viewed the medieval artwork anachronistically, comparing it to current social trends.
It would be misleading to interpret those laws anachronistically with today's standards.

Synset Examples

let's look at this phenomenon anachronistically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anachronistic - belonging to a period other than that being portrayed

Etymology

From 'anachronistic' + '-ally', with 'anachronistic' from Greek 'ana-' (against) + 'chronos' (time).
