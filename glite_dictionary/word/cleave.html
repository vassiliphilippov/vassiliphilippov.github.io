to cleave

1. VERB

/ kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
to stick closely to something or someone; to remain attached or faithful
adhere or stick together

Examples

Despite the strong wind, the paper managed to cleave to the wet pavement.
The child would cleave to his mother whenever he felt afraid.
These beliefs have cleaved to the community through many generations.

Synset Examples

The dress clings to her body
The label stuck to the box
The sushi rice grains cohere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cling - to hold on tightly or adhere closely
  • Synonym: stick - to attach or become fixed to a surface or object
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or remove from something to which it is attached

Etymology

From Old English *clēofan* (to adhere), related to Old High German *kleiben* (to stick, to adhere).

2. VERB

/ kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
to split or cut something, especially with a sharp tool or instrument
split or cut with a sharp instrument

Examples

Using a heavy axe, he cleaved the log in two with a single blow.
The chef skillfully cleaves the chicken along the backbone before roasting.
She cleaved the ice with the blade to create a fishing hole.

Synset Examples

cleave the bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - to divide or break something into parts
  • Synonym: to sever - to cut something into two or more pieces

Etymology

From Old English *clēofan* (to split, cleave), related to Old High German *kliofan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01559354-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
to split or cut something, especially with a sharp instrument; to make by cutting into or through
to split or cut (make by cutting)

Examples

The sculptor used a chisel to cleave the marble in two.
Rivers gradually cleave deep valleys through mountains over millions of years.
Lightning can sometimes cleave tree trunks cleanly down the middle.

Synset Examples

The water is going to cleave a channel into the rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - to break or cause to break forcibly into parts
  • Synonym: cut - to divide something with a sharp instrument
  • Antonym: join - to put or bring two or more things together

Etymology

From Old English *cléofan*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *klieven* and German *klieben* (split).
