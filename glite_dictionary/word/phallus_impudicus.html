Phallus impudicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæləs ɪmˈpjuːdɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈfaləs ɪmˈpjuːdɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus known as the common stinkhorn, formerly used in preparing a salve for rheumatism.
species of fungus (common stinkhorn)

Examples

We found a Phallus impudicus growing in the woods near our cottage.
Some traditional remedies once used Phallus impudicus to prepare a salve for rheumatism.
The strong odor of Phallus impudicus makes it easy to identify in damp forests.

Etymology

From Latin: phallus (penis, due to shape) + impudicus (shameless, referring to appearance).
