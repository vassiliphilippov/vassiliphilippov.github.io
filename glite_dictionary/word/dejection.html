dejection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness and low spirits; a state of being depressed or discouraged.
a state of sadness or depression

Examples

She felt a wave of dejection after hearing the disappointing news.
His face showed clear dejection when the team lost the match.
Despite his dejection, he tried to remain optimistic for his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or depression
  • Antonym: elation - a feeling of great joy or pride

Etymology

From Latin dejectio (downcast, depression), from deicere (to throw down).
