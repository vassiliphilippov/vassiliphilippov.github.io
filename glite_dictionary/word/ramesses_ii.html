Ramesses II

1. PROPN

/ ræˈmɛs.iːz ðə ˈsɛk.ənd / (AmE), / ræˈmɛs.iːz ði ˈsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
an Egyptian pharaoh who reigned from 1304 to 1237 BC, renowned for building many monuments and for his military achievements
Egyptian pharaoh, 1304–1237 BC

Examples

The colossal statues at Abu Simbel were built by Ramesses II to commemorate his reign.
Historians often refer to Ramesses II as 'Ramesses the Great' because of his monumental building projects.
Many ancient inscriptions describe the military campaigns led by Ramesses II.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Rhamsēs, from Egyptian Ra-mes-su ('Ra has fashioned him').
