marmite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.maɪt / (BrE)
A large, usually metal pot, often with legs, used especially for cooking soup or stews.
large cooking pot, often with legs

Examples

The chef stirred the broth slowly in the marmite over the open fire.
Traditional kitchens often use a large marmite for preparing stews for many people.
An old cast-iron marmite sat in the corner, used by generations of cooks.

Etymology

From French 'marmite' (pot, cooking pot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːmaɪt / (BrE)
A soup or stew cooked in a large, heavy pot, often referring to both the dish and the cooking vessel itself.
soup or stew cooked in a large pot

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional marmite for the family to enjoy on a cold evening.
She filled the marmite with fresh vegetables and herbs to make a hearty soup.
At the rustic inn, guests were served a steaming marmite straight from the pot.

Etymology

From French 'marmite', meaning 'cooking pot'.
