Ardea occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdiːə ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈdiːə ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (BrE)
a large white heron native to Florida and the Florida Keys, known as the great white heron
large white heron of Florida

Examples

The Ardea occidentalis is often seen wading in shallow waters of the Florida Keys.
Unlike other herons, the Ardea occidentalis has pure white plumage.
Birdwatchers travel from afar to photograph the rare Ardea occidentalis in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ardea' meaning 'heron' and 'occidentalis' meaning 'western'.
