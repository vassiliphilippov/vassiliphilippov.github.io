Thomas Aquinas

1. PROPN

/ təˈmɑːs əˈkwaɪnəs / (AmE), / təˈmɒs əˈkwaɪnəs / (BrE)
(1225–1274) Italian Dominican friar, philosopher, and theologian recognized for integrating faith and reason and proposing philosophical proofs for the existence of God.
Italian philosopher and theologian (1225–1274)

Examples

Many of today's philosophical arguments about faith and reason trace back to Thomas Aquinas and his writings.
The teachings of Thomas Aquinas continue to influence Christian doctrine and education worldwide.
In philosophy class, we studied Thomas Aquinas's five ways to prove the existence of God.
