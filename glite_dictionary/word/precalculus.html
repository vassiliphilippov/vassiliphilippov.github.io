precalculus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
a mathematics course that covers advanced algebra, trigonometry, and other topics to prepare students for calculus
preparatory math course before calculus

Examples

Many students take precalculus in their final year of high school.
You need to pass precalculus before enrolling in calculus at the university.
Her strong background in precalculus helped her succeed in introductory calculus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: calculus - the branch of mathematics concerning rates of change and accumulation

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'calculus' (mathematics study of rates of change).
