Aspergillaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˌdʒɪlˈeɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪlˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi that includes several common molds found in various environments.
family of fungi including common molds

Examples

Members of the Aspergillaceae family are often found in decaying organic material.
Many species within Aspergillaceae are important industrially for their role in fermentation processes.
Researchers are studying the diversity of Aspergillaceae in different soil types.

Etymology

From the genus name Aspergillus + Latin suffix -aceae (used for biological families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˌdʒɪləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪləˈsiːɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that includes several common molds, some of which can be significant in medicine and industry
family of fungi including common molds

Examples

The Aspergillaceae family contains many species important in food production and spoilage.
Some molds in the Aspergillaceae can cause respiratory infections in humans.
Scientists are studying the Aspergillaceae to develop new antibiotics.

Etymology

From the genus name Aspergillus, itself from Latin 'aspergillum' (a holy water sprinkler), due to the shape of the fungus's spore-bearing structure.
