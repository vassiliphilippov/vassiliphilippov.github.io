USSR

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuːˌɛsˌɛsˈɑːr / (AmE), / ˌjuːˌɛsˌɛsˈɑː / (BrE)
the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics; a former federation of communist republics in eastern Europe and northern Asia, established in 1922 and dissolved in 1991
former Soviet federation (1922–1991)

Examples

The collapse of the USSR marked a turning point in world history.
She studied Russian history with a special focus on the USSR.
Several countries gained independence after the fall of the USSR.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soviet Union - former federation of socialist republics in Eurasia (1922–1991)

Etymology

Acronym from Union of Soviet Socialist Republics.
