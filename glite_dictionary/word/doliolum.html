doliolum

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈlaɪələm / (AmE), / dəʊˈlʌɪələm / (BrE)
A small, free-swimming oceanic tunicate with a barrel-shaped, transparent body, belonging to the genus Doliolum.
barrel-shaped marine tunicate

Examples

The doliolum propels itself through the ocean by contracting its barrel-shaped body.
Scientists observed a colony of doliolum drifting near the coral reef.
Because of its transparency, the doliolum is difficult to spot in open water.

Etymology

From Latin doliolum (diminutive of dolium, meaning 'barrel').
