newsstand

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌstænd / (BrE)
a small stall or shop where newspapers, magazines, and other periodicals are sold
stall selling newspapers and magazines

Examples

She bought a magazine at the newsstand on her way to work.
The newsstand near the subway sells international newspapers every morning.
After school, he stopped by the newsstand to check out the latest comic books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiosk - small structure used as a newsstand or selling place
  • Synonym: bookstall - stall selling books and sometimes periodicals

Etymology

From 'news' + 'stand', meaning a stand for selling news publications. First attested in the late 19th century.
