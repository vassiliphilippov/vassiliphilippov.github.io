Fasciola

1. NOUN

/ fæʃiˈoʊlə / (AmE), / fæʃɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flatworms, known as liver flukes, that infect the livers of various mammals including humans.
genus of parasitic liver flukes
medical

Examples

The most common species of Fasciola affecting humans is Fasciola hepatica.
Veterinarians often test cattle for Fasciola infections to prevent liver damage.
The life cycle of Fasciola involves both snails and mammals as hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver fluke - a type of parasitic flatworm that infects the liver

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciola', meaning a small band or strip.

2. NOUN

/ fæˈsiːoʊlə / (AmE), / fæˈsiːələ / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flatworms, known as liver flukes, that infect the livers of various mammals, including humans.
genus of parasitic liver flukes
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the life cycle of Fasciola to understand how liver flukes spread among livestock.
Infection with Fasciola can cause significant liver damage in sheep and cattle.
The discovery of a new Fasciola species was reported in the medical journal last year.

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciola', meaning 'small band or strip', referring to the shape of the fluke.
