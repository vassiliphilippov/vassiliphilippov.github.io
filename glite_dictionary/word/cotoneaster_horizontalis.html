Cotoneaster horizontalis

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌtoʊniˈæstər ˌhɔraɪˈzɒntəlɪs / (AmE), / kəʊˌtəʊniˈæstə ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntælɪs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub with flat, spreading branches in a herringbone pattern, reddish flowers, and orange-red berries, commonly used as ground cover.
deciduous ground-cover shrub with herringbone branches

Examples

The Cotoneaster horizontalis in my garden forms a dense mat along the stone wall.
Gardeners often choose Cotoneaster horizontalis for its attractive berries and ability to control soil erosion.
We planted Cotoneaster horizontalis as a low-maintenance ground cover in the front yard.

Etymology

Cotoneaster: from Latin 'cotoneum' (quince) and '-aster' (resembling); horizontalis: Latin for 'horizontal', referring to its spreading habit.
