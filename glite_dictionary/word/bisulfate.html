bisulfate

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester containing the anion HSO4−, derived from sulfuric acid by the replacement of only one hydrogen atom
a salt or ester of sulfuric acid (HSO4−)

Examples

Sodium bisulfate is often used as a pH decreaser in swimming pools.
The reaction produced a precipitate of potassium bisulfate.
Ammonium bisulfate is used in some industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfate - a salt or ester of sulfuric acid containing the anion SO4²−

Etymology

From 'bi-' (two) + 'sulfate', indicating partial replacement of hydrogen in sulfuric acid.
