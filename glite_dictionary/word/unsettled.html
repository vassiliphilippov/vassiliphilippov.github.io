unsettled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɛtld̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛtld / (BrE)
not yet resolved, decided, or established; not having reached a final state or conclusion
not yet resolved or decided

Examples

The question of ownership remains unsettled after years of legal dispute.
The weather was very unsettled throughout the whole month of June.
Much of the region was still unsettled during the early nineteenth century.

Synset Examples

unsettled territory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: settled - firmly established or decided

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'settled', past participle of 'settle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316789-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɛtld̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛtld / (BrE)
not resolved or decided; remaining in doubt or uncertainty.
not resolved; uncertain or undecided

Examples

The dispute over the property remains unsettled after years of negotiation.
Her future plans are still unsettled, causing her some anxiety.
There are several unsettled questions about the new policy.

Synset Examples

an unsettled issue
an unsettled state of mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resolved - firmly determined or decided; settled
  • Antonym: settled - stable or resolved; not likely to change

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'settled', from Middle English 'setlen' (to settle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02137894-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɛt.əld / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of weather or conditions) likely to change; not stable or firmly fixed.
(of weather) likely to change; unstable

Examples

The forecast says the unsettled weather will last all week.
Her plans for the summer remain unsettled due to changing circumstances.
Autumn often brings unsettled conditions with frequent showers and gusty winds.

Synset Examples

a changeable climate
the weather is uncertain
unsettled weather with rain and hail and sunshine coming one right after the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: changeable - liable to change; variable
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change; steady

Etymology

From un- (not) + settled (past participle of settle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02514603-s

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɛt̬əld / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛtəld / (BrE)
not firmly established, fixed, or resolved; lacking stability or certainty.
not fixed or stable; unresolved

Examples

After moving several times in a year, he still felt unsettled in the new city.
The region remained politically unsettled for decades after the revolution.
Her finances were unsettled, making it difficult to plan for the future.

Synset Examples

an unsettled lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: settled - firmly established or stable
  • Synonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to change

Etymology

un- (not) + settled (from Old English setlan, to seat or set)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02134270-a
Synset POS: a
