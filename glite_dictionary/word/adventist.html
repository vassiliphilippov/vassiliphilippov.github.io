Adventist

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vən.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈæd.vən.tɪst / (BrE)
a member of a Christian denomination that believes in and expects the imminent second coming of Christ
member of a Christian group expecting Christ's return

Examples

My neighbor is an Adventist and often invites me to their church services.
The Adventist community is known for its emphasis on healthy living.
During the 19th century, many Adventists eagerly anticipated the return of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seventh-day Adventist - a member of the main Adventist denomination observing Saturday Sabbath

Etymology

From Latin 'adventus' (arrival, coming) + '-ist' (follower); refers to belief in the Second Advent of Christ.
