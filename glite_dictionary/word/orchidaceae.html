Orchidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrkɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of perennial plants, commonly known as orchids, characterized by fleshy tubers or rootstocks and distinctive flowers, found worldwide.
family of flowering plants (orchids)

Examples

Many rare species within the Orchidaceae family are prized by collectors for their unique blooms.
The Orchidaceae family is one of the largest in the plant kingdom, with thousands of species worldwide.
Botanists study the complex pollination mechanisms of Orchidaceae to understand plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the Orchidaceae family, known for its unusual flowers

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Orchidaceae,' based on Greek 'orkhis' (testicle, from the shape of the roots) and the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae.'

2. NOUN

/ ɔr.kɪˈdeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / ɔː.kɪˈdeɪ.siː / (BrE)
the family of flowering plants commonly known as orchids, comprising numerous species with distinctive, often showy flowers
family of orchid plants

Examples

Many tropical rainforests are home to hundreds of Orchidaceae species.
The beauty and diversity of Orchidaceae make them popular among plant collectors.
Scientists study the pollination mechanisms unique to the Orchidaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, especially cultivated for its flowers

Etymology

From modern Latin Orchidaceae, based on Greek 'orkhis' (orchid).
