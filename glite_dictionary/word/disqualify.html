to disqualify

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to officially declare someone unfit or ineligible to take part in a competition, activity, or position because they have broken rules or do not meet the requirements
declare someone ineligible or unfit

Examples

The referee had to disqualify the athlete after discovering the foul.
If you arrive late for the exam, they may disqualify you from taking it.
Breaking the rules could disqualify a contestant from the competition.

Synset Examples

She was disqualified for the Olympics because she was a professional athlete

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to qualify - to be eligible or fit; meet the required standard

Etymology

From Late Latin 'disqualificare', from Latin prefix 'dis-' (apart) + 'qualificare' (to make of a certain quality).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something ineligible or unsuitable for an activity, position, or privilege
make ineligible or unsuitable

Examples

If you lie on your application, it may disqualify you from the scholarship program.
Any criminal conviction will automatically disqualify an applicant for the position.
The presence of peanuts in the sauce could disqualify it for people with allergies.

Synset Examples

Your income disqualifies you

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to qualify - to make or become eligible or suitable

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'disqualificare', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'qualificare' (to make fit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302119-v
Synset POS: v
