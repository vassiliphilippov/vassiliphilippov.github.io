Phasmida

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæzmɪdə / (AmE), / ˈfæzmɪdə / (BrE)
a group of insects comprising the stick insects and leaf insects, sometimes regarded as a suborder within Orthoptera.
group including stick and leaf insects

Examples

Many species within the Phasmida are masters of camouflage, blending seamlessly with twigs and leaves.
Researchers debated whether Phasmida should be classified as a distinct order or a suborder of Orthoptera.
The Phasmida exhibit a remarkable range of mimicry, making them fascinating subjects for entomology students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phasmatodea - the scientific order including stick and leaf insects

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'phasma' (apparition, phantom), referring to their camouflaged appearance.
