Rainier

1. PROPN

/ reɪˈnɪr / (AmE), / reɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a mountain peak in central Washington State, USA; at 14,410 feet (4,392 meters), it is the highest summit in the Cascade Range
highest peak in Washington State, USA

Examples

Climbers from around the world come to attempt summiting Rainier each year.
From Seattle, you can see Rainier towering majestically on a clear day.
The national park surrounding Rainier offers numerous hiking trails and scenic views.

Etymology

Named after Rear Admiral Peter Rainier, a British naval officer.
