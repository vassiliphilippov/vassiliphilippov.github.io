petting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of caressing or fondling someone in an affectionate or amorous manner, especially as non-genital sexual foreplay.
affectionate caressing or amorous play

Examples

She felt uncomfortable when their friendly petting started to become more intimate.
Teenagers often engage in petting at parties as a way of exploring attraction.
The counselor discussed the boundaries between casual affection and petting with the students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caressing - gently stroking or touching lovingly
  • Synonym: foreplay - sexual activity preceding intercourse

Etymology

From the verb 'pet' (to caress) + -ing, early 20th-century American usage.
