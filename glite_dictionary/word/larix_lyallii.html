Larix lyallii

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛərɪks laɪˈælaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlarɪks lɪˈælɪaɪ / (BrE)
A species of medium-sized larch tree native to the Rocky Mountains, closely related to the western larch (Larix occidentalis).
larch species native to the Rocky Mountains

Examples

The Larix lyallii is well adapted to cold, mountainous environments.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Larix lyallii in the Rockies.
The needles of Larix lyallii turn golden yellow in autumn before falling off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Larix occidentalis - a related species of larch known as western larch

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Larix' (larch), species named after David Lyall, Scottish botanist (1817–1895).
