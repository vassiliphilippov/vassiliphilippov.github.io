thought-provoking

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt prəˌvoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt prəˌvəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
stimulating careful consideration, discussion, or reflection; making you think deeply about something
makes you think deeply

Examples

The speaker delivered a thought-provoking lecture that inspired many questions from the audience.
Her article was so thought-provoking that it changed many readers' perspectives on climate change.
I find documentaries about ancient civilizations especially thought-provoking.

Synset Examples

a challenging hypothesis
a thought-provoking book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenging - stimulating interest or thought
  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging new ideas or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: uninspiring - not stimulating interest or thought

Etymology

Formed from 'thought' and present participle of 'provoke', meaning to incite thought.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt prəˌvoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt prəˌvəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
making you think seriously or carefully about something; stimulating careful consideration or reflection
stimulating serious thought

Examples

The documentary was incredibly thought-provoking and changed how I view the issue.
Her thought-provoking questions encouraged vigorous debate among the panelists.
That speech was one of the most thought-provoking I've ever heard.

Synset Examples

The meeting was really engaging and thought-provoking.
The evilutionist's theories were controversial but thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or new ideas; similar in meaning to 'thought-provoking'

Etymology

From 'thought' + 'provoking', meaning 'causing to think'.
