anagoge

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡoʊdʒi / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡəʊdʒi / (BrE)
A mystical or allegorical interpretation, especially of Scripture, intended to reveal spiritual or transcendent meanings.
mystical or allegorical interpretation of Scripture

Examples

The medieval theologian employed anagoge to discern the deeper spiritual meanings within the biblical text.
Through careful anagoge, the passage was interpreted as referring to the soul's ascent to heaven.
His lecture explored the use of anagoge in understanding allegorical themes in religious literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegory - a symbolic narrative or interpretation

Etymology

From Late Latin anagoge, from Ancient Greek anagōgē (spiritual uplift, ascent).
