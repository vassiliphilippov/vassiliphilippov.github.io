highland

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪlənd / (BrE)
(of a region or area) situated in or relating to high or hilly land.
of or relating to high or hilly country

Examples

The highland climate is known for its cool temperatures and frequent fog.
She preferred hiking in highland areas because of the breathtaking views.
The highland villages are famous for their traditional festivals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowland - of or relating to low or flat country

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'high' and 'land', referring to elevated land.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
an area of land that is at a higher elevation, especially mountainous or hilly terrain
elevated or mountainous land

Examples

These sheep are well-suited to the highland climate with its cooler temperatures.
The village sits at the edge of the highland, overlooking the river below.
Forests and streams are common features of the Scottish highland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowland - land that is at a lower elevation, especially flat or gently sloping terrain
