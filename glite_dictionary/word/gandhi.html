Gandhi

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑndi / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːndi / (BrE)
Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (1869–1948), Indian political and spiritual leader who advocated nonviolent resistance in the struggle for India's independence from Great Britain.
Indian leader and advocate of nonviolent resistance

Examples

Gandhi led India to independence through a campaign of nonviolent resistance.
The teachings of Gandhi continue to inspire civil rights movements around the world.
Many people have visited the places where Gandhi once lived and worked.

Etymology

From Gujarati ગાંધી (Gāndhī), a surname; especially referring to Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑːndi / (AmE), / ˈɡandi / (BrE)
(Indira Gandhi) The first female Prime Minister of India, daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru, who held office from 1966 to 1977 and again from 1980 until her assassination in 1984.
Indian Prime Minister (Indira Gandhi, 1917-1984)

Examples

Gandhi was India's first and, to date, only female Prime Minister.
Many people admired Gandhi for her determination and strong leadership.
The assassination of Gandhi in 1984 shocked the entire nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indira Gandhi - Indian Prime Minister (1917-1984); daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru
  • Synonym: Nehru - Jawaharlal Nehru, first Prime Minister of India and father of Indira Gandhi

Etymology

From the Gujarati surname Gandhi, meaning 'grocer'; notably borne by several prominent Indian leaders.
