bignoniad

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡˈnoʊniæd / (AmE), / bɪɡˈnəʊnɪad / (BrE)
Any woody plant belonging to the family Bignoniaceae, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
woody plant of the Bignoniaceae family

Examples

The bignoniad is prized for its large, showy flowers and fast-growing wood.
Many species within the bignoniad group are used as ornamental trees in gardens.
Researchers studied the leaf structure of a tropical bignoniad for potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From the botanical family name Bignoniaceae, itself derived from Latinized form of Jean-Paul Bignon, a French patron of science.
