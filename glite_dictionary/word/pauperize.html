to pauperize

1. VERB

/ ˈpɔːpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɔːpəraɪz / (BrE)
to reduce someone to poverty; to make someone very poor
make very poor; reduce to poverty

Examples

The corrupt regime did much to pauperize the rural population.
War and mismanagement can pauperize entire communities.
Critics argued that the policy would eventually pauperize the middle class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impoverish - make someone poor
  • Antonym: to enrich - make someone wealthy

Etymology

From French *pauperiser*, from Latin *pauper* (poor).
