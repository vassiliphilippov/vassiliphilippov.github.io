candlepins

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌpɪnz / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌpɪnz / (BrE)
A bowling game played with slender, straight pins and smaller balls, commonly found in New England, USA.
a bowling game with slender pins

Examples

Many people in New England enjoy playing candlepins because of its unique challenge.
The alley offered both tenpin and candlepins for different bowling experiences.
She grew up competing in candlepins tournaments every winter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tenpin bowling - the standard form of bowling using larger, fatter pins

Etymology

The name refers to the shape of the pins, which resemble candles. First used in late 19th-century New England.
