dulcimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlsəmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌlsɪmə / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument with an elliptical body, a fretted fingerboard, and typically three strings, used especially in American folk music
American folk string instrument

Examples

She played a haunting melody on the dulcimer during the festival.
The dulcimer is often featured at Appalachian music gatherings.
He carefully tuned his vintage dulcimer before the performance.

Etymology

From Latin 'dulcis' (sweet), and Greek 'melos' (song or melody), indicating a sweet-sounding instrument.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlsəmər / (AmE), / ˈdʌlsɪmə / (BrE)
a musical instrument with a trapezoidal soundbox and metal strings that are struck with small hammers
trapezoidal stringed instrument struck with hammers

Examples

The musician captivated the audience with a lively folk tune played on the dulcimer.
She inherited an antique dulcimer from her grandmother.
At the festival, children lined up to try striking the dulcimer with its delicate hammers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zither - stringed musical instrument with a flat body and many strings

Etymology

From Latin dulcimer, from dulcis (sweet) and Greek melos (song, melody).
