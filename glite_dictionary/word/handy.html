handy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhændi / (AmE), / ˈhændi / (BrE)
good at using your hands to make or fix things; skillful with the hands
skillful with the hands

Examples

My father is very handy around the house and can fix almost anything.
She's handy with a paintbrush and often decorates her own furniture.
If you're handy with tools, this project should be easy for you.

Synset Examples

handy with an axe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or lacking skill with the hands
  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient

2. ADJ

/ ˈhændi / (AmE), / ˈhændi / (BrE)
useful, convenient, or readily available for use
useful and convenient

Examples

This tool is really handy for fixing things around the house.
She always keeps a notebook handy for jotting down ideas.
That app is very handy when you need directions in a new city.

Synset Examples

a handy gadget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convenient - fit or suited to one's comfort, purpose, or needs
  • Synonym: useful - able to be used for a practical purpose or in several ways
  • Antonym: awkward - difficult to use, handle, or manage

Etymology

From Middle English 'hendi' (clever, skillful), from Old English 'handig' (handy, clever), from 'hand'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈhændi / (AmE), / ˈhændi / (BrE)
(of a thing or place) conveniently placed and easy to reach or use.
conveniently placed; easy to reach

Examples

Can you move the telephone to a more handy location?
She kept the notepad handy so she could jot down ideas whenever they occurred to her.
There's a handy shelf by the door for your keys and wallet.

Synset Examples

found a handy spot for the can opener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convenient - fitting well with a person's needs, activities, and plans
  • Antonym: inaccessible - hard or impossible to reach or approach

Etymology

From hand + -y, originally meaning 'at hand' or 'within reach'.
