hoo-hah

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuˌhɑ / (AmE), / ˈhuːhɑː / (BrE)
A commotion or noisy disturbance, often involving excitement or disagreement.
loud commotion or disturbance
informal

Examples

There was quite a hoo-hah when the company announced its new policy.
The politician's comment sparked a hoo-hah in the media.
After the hoo-hah died down, everyone went back to work as usual.

Synset Examples

they were amazed by the furious disturbance they had caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or excitement
  • Synonym: furor - an outbreak of public anger or excitement
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of disturbance or commotion
