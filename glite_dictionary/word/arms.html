arms

1. NOUN

/ ɑrmz / (AmE), / ɑːmz / (BrE)
the official heraldic symbols used to represent a family, state, or organization, especially as part of a coat of arms
official heraldic symbols

Examples

The family proudly displayed their arms above the fireplace.
The university's arms appear on its official documents and banners.
Many European cities have their own unique arms dating back centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - a unique heraldic design displayed on a shield or escutcheon

Etymology

From Old French 'armes', from Latin 'arma' meaning 'arms, weapons', later extended to 'heraldic devices'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrmz / (AmE), / ɑːmz / (BrE)
weapons and military equipment regarded collectively
weapons; military equipment

Examples

The country agreed to reduce its stockpile of arms as part of the peace treaty.
A ban on the export of arms was imposed by the United Nations.
The rebels surrendered their arms after lengthy negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weapons - instruments used in fighting or war

Etymology

From Latin arma (tools, weapons), plural of armum (tool, weapon); originally referring to weapons for war.
