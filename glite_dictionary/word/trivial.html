trivial

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪviəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪviəl / (BrE)
Of little value, importance, or significance; minor or insignificant.
of little importance or value

Examples

Despite her worries, the mistake was actually quite trivial and easily fixed.
He dismissed the complaint, insisting it was too trivial to address.
They spent hours arguing over trivial details that made no real difference.

Synset Examples

a few superficial editorial changes
only trivial objections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - not significant or important
  • Synonym: minor - lesser in importance or seriousness
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value
  • Antonym: significant - having meaning or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'trivialis' (commonplace), from 'trivium' (crossroads, a place where three roads meet).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪv.i.əl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪv.i.əl / (BrE)
Focusing on things of little value or importance; overly concerned with insignificant details.
concerned with unimportant matters

Examples

He disliked engaging in trivial conversations that focused only on gossip.
She often dismissed his concerns as trivial and not worth discussing.
Some people become so absorbed in trivial matters that they miss what truly matters.

Synset Examples

a trivial young woman
a trivial mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value
  • Antonym: serious - showing careful thought or concern; not trivial

Etymology

From Latin 'trivialis', meaning 'commonplace' or 'ordinary', from 'trivium' (crossroads).
