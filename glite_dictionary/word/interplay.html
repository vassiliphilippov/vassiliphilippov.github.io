interplay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tɚˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.təˌpleɪ / (BrE)
the way in which two or more things have an effect on each other or interact
interaction of two or more things

Examples

The interplay between science and art can lead to innovative ideas.
Economic growth often results from the interplay of multiple social and political factors.
We discussed the subtle interplay of light and shadow in the painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interaction - reciprocal action or influence between things or people

Etymology

Formed from 'inter-' (between) and 'play' (movement, action), first attested in the 19th century.
