kerite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɛr.aɪt / (BrE)
A compound made by vulcanizing tar or asphalt with animal or vegetable oils and sulphur, resembling rubber and used as an insulating material.
rubber-like insulating material

Examples

Early telegraph cables were often insulated with kerite to protect the wires from moisture.
The company specialized in producing kerite for use in electrical installations.
Advances in materials eventually replaced kerite with more modern insulators.

Synset Examples

Kerite was formerly used as an insulating material in telegraphy.
