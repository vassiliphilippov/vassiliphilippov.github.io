Jasminum sambac

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæzˈmɪnəm ˈsæmbæk / (AmE), / ˌdʒæzˈmɪnəm ˈsæmbæk / (BrE)
An East Indian evergreen vine cultivated for its clusters of fragrant white flowers, commonly used in perfumes and teas.
plant with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The Jasminum sambac blossoms are often used in traditional garlands and religious ceremonies.
Many teas are delicately flavored with the aromatic flowers of Jasminum sambac.
The garden was fragrant with blooming Jasminum sambac vines climbing the trellis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - a genus of shrubs and vines with fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Jasminum (jasmine) and the specific epithet sambac, of uncertain origin, possibly from Arabic or Persian.
