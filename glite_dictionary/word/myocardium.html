myocardium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑːr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈkɑː.dɪ.əm / (BrE)
the thick, muscular middle layer of the wall of the heart responsible for contracting and pumping blood
muscular middle layer of the heart wall
medical

Examples

The myocardium plays a crucial role in maintaining the heart’s pumping ability.
Damage to the myocardium can lead to serious heart conditions, including a heart attack.
Doctors assess the thickness of the myocardium when diagnosing certain cardiac diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: endocardium - the inner lining of the heart
  • Degree Higher: epicardium - the outer layer of the heart wall

Etymology

From Greek 'myo-' (muscle) + 'kardia' (heart).
