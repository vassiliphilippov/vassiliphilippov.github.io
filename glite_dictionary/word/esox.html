Esox

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛˌsɑks / (AmE), / ˈiːˌsɒks / (BrE)
a genus of predatory freshwater fish that includes pikes and pickerels, the only genus in the family Esocidae
a genus of freshwater fish (pikes, pickerels)

Examples

The genus Esox includes several species, such as the Northern Pike and the Muskellunge.
Fossil records indicate that Esox has existed since the Eocene epoch.
Anglers often seek fish belonging to the Esox genus for their size and fighting spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a large, slender predatory freshwater fish of the genus Esox

Etymology

From Latin esox (a large fish, probably pike), from Greek ἴσοξ (ísōx).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.sɑks / (AmE), / ˈiː.sɒks / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Esocidae, including pike and related species.
genus of freshwater pike fish

Examples

The northern pike is a well-known member of the genus Esox.
Species within Esox are popular among freshwater anglers in Europe and North America.
Scientists recently revised the classification of several Esox species based on genetic data.

Etymology

From Latin *esox* meaning 'pike', originally from a Celtic word for pike.
