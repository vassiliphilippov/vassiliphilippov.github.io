obsidian

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɪdiən / (AmE), / əbˈsɪdiən / (BrE)
a hard, dark, glass-like volcanic rock formed by the rapid cooling of lava without crystallization
dark volcanic glass

Examples

The archaeologist discovered tools made from obsidian at the ancient site.
Obsidian is prized for its sharp edges and smooth, shiny surface.
The necklace features a pendant carved from polished obsidian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volcanic glass - natural glass formed by volcanic processes

Etymology

From Latin 'obsidianus (lapis)' meaning 'stone of Obsius', said to be after the person who discovered it.
