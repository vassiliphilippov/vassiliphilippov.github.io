Phylloxeridae

1. NOUN

/ fɪl.ɒkˈsɛr.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / fɪl.ɒkˈsɛr.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, plant-parasitic insects closely related to aphids, known for attacking roots and leaves of various plants, especially grapevines.
family of plant-parasitic insects

Examples

The Phylloxeridae have devastated vineyards in several countries due to their parasitic attacks on grapevines.
Research on Phylloxeridae has helped protect crops from serious infestations.
Many species within the Phylloxeridae family are notorious for causing plant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect of the superfamily Aphidoidea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Phylloxera' (the type genus) + '-idae' (a standard zoological family suffix from Greek).

2. NOUN

/ fɪˌlɒksəˈrɪːdiː / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒksəˈrɪːdiː / (BrE)
A small family of plant-parasitic insects closely related to aphids, several of which are important agricultural pests.
a family of plant-parasitic insects

Examples

Grape vines are threatened by insects from the Phylloxeridae family, which feed on plant roots and leaves.
The most notorious member of the Phylloxeridae is Phylloxera vastatrix, a destructive pest for vineyards.
Entomologists are studying the life cycle of Phylloxeridae to develop better pest management strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect often found on plants

Etymology

From the genus name Phylloxera (Greek 'phyllon' meaning leaf and 'xeros' meaning dry) with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for families.
