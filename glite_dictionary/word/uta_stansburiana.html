Uta stansburiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːtə stænzˈbʊəriˌænə / (AmE), / ˈjuːtə stænzˈbjʊəriˌænə / (BrE)
A small, abundant lizard species native to the arid western United States, commonly known as the side-blotched lizard.
a common lizard of the western US

Examples

The Uta stansburiana is often spotted basking on rocks throughout the deserts of California.
Researchers studied the adaptability of Uta stansburiana to extreme environmental conditions.
Predators of Uta stansburiana include snakes, birds, and larger lizards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side-blotched lizard - common name for Uta stansburiana

Etymology

Genus name from Greek, species named after Howard Stansbury, an American explorer.
