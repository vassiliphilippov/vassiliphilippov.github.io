viscid

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪsɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪd / (BrE)
having a thick, sticky consistency; adhesive or glutinous
thick and sticky; adhesive

Examples

The frog's tongue is viscid, allowing it to catch insects easily.
A viscid substance coated the leaves after the syrupy rain.
The glue grew more viscid as it dried, making it hard to spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency between solid and liquid
  • Synonym: sticky - having the property of adhering or sticking to a surface
  • Antonym: fluid - having the property of flowing easily

Etymology

From Latin 'viscidus' (sticky), from 'viscum' (birdlime).
