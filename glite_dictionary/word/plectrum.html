plectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˈplɛk.trəm / (BrE)
A small thin piece, usually of plastic, metal, or other material, used to pluck or strum the strings of a musical instrument such as a guitar.
device for plucking instrument strings

Examples

He used a plectrum to play the fast solo on his electric guitar.
Traditionally, mandolin players prefer a stiff plectrum for a brighter sound.
The package includes several different sizes of plectra for various playing styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick - a plectrum, especially for guitars

Etymology

From Latin 'plectrum', from Greek 'plēktron' (something to strike with).
