mezzo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtsəʊ / (BrE)
A female singing voice that is intermediate in range between soprano and contralto; also, a singer with such a voice.
female singing voice between soprano and contralto

Examples

The lead role was performed by a renowned mezzo from the local opera company.
She trained as a soprano but found her true range as a mezzo.
Many famous operatic arias are written specifically for a mezzo voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mezzo-soprano - a female singing voice between soprano and contralto
  • Degree Higher: soprano - the highest female singing voice
  • Degree Lower: contralto - the lowest female singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'mezzo' meaning 'middle', short for 'mezzo-soprano'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtsəʊ / (BrE)
the female singing voice that lies between contralto and soprano in range; a mezzo-soprano
female voice between soprano and contralto

Examples

She was cast as Carmen because her mezzo suit the role perfectly.
The choir sought a new mezzo to complete their ensemble.
After years as a mezzo, she finally debuted in a soprano role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mezzo-soprano - the female voice type between contralto and soprano, often the full term
  • Degree Higher: soprano - the highest female singing voice
  • Degree Lower: contralto - the lowest female singing voice

Etymology

From Italian mezzo (middle, half), referring to its position between soprano and contralto.
