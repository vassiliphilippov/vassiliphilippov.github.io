symptomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
showing symptoms of a particular disease or condition, or relating to symptoms; indicative of a particular problem.
relating to symptoms or their indication

Examples

The doctor prescribed medication for symptomatic relief of the patient's cough.
A fever may be symptomatic of a more serious underlying condition.
The study focused on symptomatic patients rather than those without any signs.

Synset Examples

symptomatic relief
symptomatic treatment
a symptomatic classification of diseases

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asymptomatic - showing no symptoms of disease
  • Synonym: indicative - serving as a sign or indication of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'symptomaticus', from Greek 'symptomatikos' (pertaining to symptoms).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
serving as a sign or indication of a particular disease or condition
indicative of a disease or condition

Examples

The patient's persistent cough was symptomatic of a serious underlying illness.
Rising temperatures and headaches may be symptomatic of dehydration.
The manager said the delays were symptomatic of deeper issues within the company.

Synset Examples

a diagnostic sign of yellow fever
a rash symptomatic of scarlet fever
symptomatic of insanity
a rise in crime symptomatic of social breakdown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicative - pointing out or serving as a sign of something
  • Antonym: asymptomatic - showing no symptoms of disease

Etymology

From Greek 'symptōmatikos', from 'symptōma' meaning occurrence or symptom.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00358334-s
Synset POS: s
