to deafen

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛfən / (AmE), / ˈdɛfən / (BrE)
to make someone unable to hear, either temporarily or permanently, especially by a loud noise
make unable to hear

Examples

The explosion was so loud it almost deafened me.
Continuous loud music can deafen people if they are not careful.
The alarm's shrill sound threatened to deafen anyone nearby.

Synset Examples

a deafening noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deaf - unable to hear

Etymology

From English 'deaf' + '-en' (verb forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛfən / (AmE), / ˈdɛfən / (BrE)
to make (a room, building, etc.) soundproof, or protected from outside noise
make soundproof

Examples

The studio engineers worked hard to deafen the recording booth before the session began.
They plan to deafen the new conference room so outside traffic noise won't disturb meetings.
She hired a contractor to deafen her apartment and minimize sound from the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundproof - to make impervious to sound

3. VERB

/ ˈdɛfən / (AmE), / ˈdɛfən / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be so loud as to make it difficult or impossible to hear anything else.
to be unbearably loud

Examples

The roar of the engine would deafen anyone standing nearby.
Fireworks exploded all around, threatening to deafen the crowd.
Their laughter was so loud it almost seemed to deafen us.

Synset Examples

a deafening noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafening - extremely loud (sound or noise)
  • Antonym: muffle - to make a sound quieter or less distinct

Etymology

From 'deaf' + '-en', meaning to make deaf.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
