Melampodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləmˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛlampəʊdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and subshrubs native to warm regions of North America, known for their yellow flowers and use as ornamental plants.
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Melampodium plants throughout the summer.
She chose Melampodium for its resilience and bright yellow flowers.
Many gardeners appreciate Melampodium as a low-maintenance ornamental genus.

Etymology

From Greek melas (black) and podion (little foot), referring to parts of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləmˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛləmˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and subshrubs native to warm regions of North America, belonging to the family Asteraceae.
genus of North American herbs and subshrubs

Examples

Several species in the Melampodium genus are popular as ornamental garden plants.
The bright yellow flowers of Melampodium bloom throughout the summer.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Melampodium genus in Texas.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to dark-colored parts of the plant.
