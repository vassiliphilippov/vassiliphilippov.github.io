Falco columbarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈfælkoʊ ˌkoʊləmˈbɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈfælkəʊ ˌkɒləmˈbeəriəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the merlin, a small species of falcon found in Europe and America, with dark plumage and a black-barred tail, often used in falconry.
scientific name for the merlin (a small falcon)

Examples

The Falco columbarius is often seen hunting over open moorland during the winter.
Ornithologists observed several Falco columbarius nesting near the coastal cliffs.
Falconers have prized the Falco columbarius for its speed and agility for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merlin - a small species of falcon; common name for Falco columbarius

Etymology

From Latin: 'Falco' (falcon) and 'columbarius' (of doves), referring to its pigeon-hunting habits.
