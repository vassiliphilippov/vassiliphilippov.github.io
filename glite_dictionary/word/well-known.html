well-known

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈnəʊn / (BrE)
known by many people; familiar or recognized by a large number of people.
famous or widely recognized

Examples

She is a well-known author, admired for her best-selling novels.
These well-known facts are often taught in introductory science classes.
The museum displays art by several well-known contemporary painters.

Synset Examples

a well-known politician
well-known facts
a politician who is well known
these facts are well known

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known by many people, especially for something significant
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known or not easily understood

2. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈnoʊn / (AmE), / wɛl ˈnəʊn / (BrE)
known or recognized by many people; familiar through frequent experience or contact.
familiar to many people; widely recognized

Examples

His well-known generosity made him loved by everyone in the community.
The author is well-known for her mystery novels and engaging characters.
She greeted him with the well-known smile that always brightened the room.

Synset Examples

a long-familiar face
a well-known voice reached her ears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known by many people, especially for a notable reason
  • Antonym: unknown - not known or familiar to people
