exostosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɑːˈstoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɒsˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a benign growth projecting from the surface of a bone, often covered with cartilage
benign bone outgrowth
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the exostosis that was causing discomfort in the patient's foot.
An exostosis can sometimes interfere with joint movement or cause pain.
Repeated stress on the bone may increase the risk of developing an exostosis.

Etymology

From Greek exostōsis, from ex- (out) + osteon (bone).
