quadrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drənt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rənt / (BrE)
one of four equal parts into which the circumference of a circle can be divided; a quarter of a circle
a quarter of a circle

Examples

The city park is divided into four quadrants by two main roads crossing at right angles.
Each quadrant of the circle represents a different department in the company.
He measured the angle within the first quadrant of the chart.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: semicircle - half of a circle or its circumference

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrans,' meaning 'a quarter', from 'quattuor' (four).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drənt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rənt / (BrE)
an instrument, typically shaped as a quarter-circle, used for measuring the altitude of celestial objects above the horizon
instrument for measuring celestial altitude

Examples

The sailor used a quadrant to determine the ship's position at sea.
Before telescopes, astronomers relied on the quadrant for measuring the altitude of stars.
He proudly displayed an antique quadrant in his study, a relic of early navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sextant - an instrument for measuring angles in navigation, similar to a quadrant

Etymology

From Latin *quadrans* (a quarter).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drənt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rənt / (BrE)
one of four equal parts of a circle, bounded by two radii at right angles to each other and the connecting arc
one fourth of a circle

Examples

The pie was divided into four quadrants for the experiment.
Each quadrant of the circle represents a different phase of the project.
The treasure map showed the location hidden in the top-right quadrant of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sector - a portion of a circle bounded by two radii and their included arc

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrans', meaning 'a quarter'.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drənt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rənt / (BrE)
any of the four sections into which a plane is divided by two perpendicular lines, especially the axes on a graph or coordinate system.
one of four sections of a plane by coordinate axes

Examples

The point (4,5) lies in the first quadrant of the coordinate plane.
Each quadrant on the graph represents a different sign combination for x and y values.
She divided the map into four quadrants to analyze the survey results.

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrans' (a quarter), from 'quattuor' (four).
