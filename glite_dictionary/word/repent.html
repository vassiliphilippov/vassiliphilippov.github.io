to repent

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpɛnt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛnt / (BrE)
To feel or express sincere regret or remorse about one's wrongdoing or sin.
feel remorse or regret for wrongdoing

Examples

After lying to his friend, he began to repent his actions deeply.
Many people repent for mistakes they made in their youth.
She repented sincerely and asked for forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - feel sad, repentant, or disappointed over something
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed

Etymology

From Old French 'repentir', from Latin 'repoenitere' (to regret).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈpɛnt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛnt / (BrE)
to feel or express sincere regret or remorse about wrongdoing or sin, and to turn away from such behavior
feel or express regret for sin or wrongdoing

Examples

She decided to repent for her past mistakes and change her life.
The preacher urged the congregation to repent and seek forgiveness.
After realizing the harm he caused, he began to repent sincerely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - to feel sad or sorry about something one has done or failed to do
  • Synonym: to atone - to make amends or reparation for a wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy

Etymology

From Old French *repentir*, from Latin *repoenitēre* (to cause to repent).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
