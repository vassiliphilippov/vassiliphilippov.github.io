cross-sentential

1. ADJ

/ krɒs ˌsɛnˈtɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / krɒs ˌsɛnˈtɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving relationships or phenomena that extend across multiple sentences.
relating to phenomena across sentences

Examples

The cross-sentential reference makes it clear that the pronoun refers to the subject of the previous sentence.
Researchers studied the cross-sentential effects of discourse markers in academic writing.
Understanding cross-sentential coherence is important for effective reading comprehension.

Etymology

Formed from 'cross-' (across, between) and 'sentential' (relating to a sentence), from Latin 'sententia' (sentence, opinion).
