to allocate

1. VERB

/ ˈæləˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæləkeɪt / (BrE)
to distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose or according to a plan
distribute or assign for a purpose

Examples

The committee will allocate funds to each department based on their needs.
Time was allocated for questions at the end of the presentation.
We are allocating extra resources to address the urgent repairs.

Synset Examples

I am allocating a loaf of bread to everyone on a daily basis
I'm allocating the rations for the camping trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular task or responsibility
  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out shares or portions of something
  • Antonym: to withhold - to hold back or refuse to allocate or give

Etymology

From Latin 'allocatus', past participle of 'allocare' (to assign, allocate), from 'ad-' (to) + 'locare' (to place).
