Kyphosus

1. PROPN

/ kaɪˈfoʊsəs / (AmE), / kɪˈfəʊsəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish (family Kyphosidae) commonly known as sea chubs.
a genus of marine fish

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Kyphosus off the coast of Hawaii.
The Kyphosus genus includes several species known as sea chubs.
Marine biologists study the diet and behavior of Kyphosus to understand reef ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek kyphos (hump-backed), referring to the shape of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈfoʊsəs / (AmE), / kɪˈfəʊsəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Kyphosidae, commonly known as sea chubs, found in temperate and tropical oceans.
a genus of marine fish (sea chubs)

Examples

Several species within the genus Kyphosus are known for their distinctive hump-backed appearance.
The Kyphosus fish are often found grazing on algae in rocky reef environments.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Kyphosus genus off the coast of Australia.

Etymology

From Greek kyphos (curved, humpbacked), referring to the body shape of these fish.
