Leontocebus

1. NOUN

/ liˌɑn.toʊˈsiː.bəs / (AmE), / liˌɒn.təʊˈsiː.bəs / (BrE)
A genus of small South American monkeys known as tamarins, characterized by their claw-like nails and found in tropical forests.
a genus of tamarin monkeys

Examples

The golden-mantled Leontocebus inhabits the rainforests of the western Amazon basin.
Many researchers study the social behavior of Leontocebus groups in the wild.
Captive breeding programs have helped protect several species within the Leontocebus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarin - a small South American monkey of the family Callitrichidae

Etymology

From Greek 'leōn' (lion) and 'kēbos' (monkey), referring to the appearance of some species.

2. NOUN

/ liˌɑːntəˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / liˌɒntəˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of small monkeys known as tamarins, found in South America.
a genus of tamarin monkeys

Examples

The genus Leontocebus includes several species of saddle-back tamarins.
Researchers observed Leontocebus in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of Leontocebus are known for their agility and social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarin - small South American monkeys of the family Callitrichidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'leon' (lion) and 'cebus' (monkey), used in zoological taxonomy.
