realm

1. NOUN

/ rɛlm / (AmE), / rɛlm / (BrE)
a country or territory ruled by a king or queen; a kingdom
kingdom or royal domain

Examples

The ancient realm was known for its stunning castles and loyal knights.
He became ruler of the realm after his father's death.
Legends tell of a hidden realm beyond the mountains, guarded by magic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country ruled by a king or queen

Etymology

From Old French 'reaume' (kingdom), from Latin 'regalis' (royal).

2. NOUN

/ ɹɛlm / (AmE), / ɹɛlm / (BrE)
a field or domain in which a particular thing is prominent or has influence
domain or area of influence

Examples

Within the realm of science, new discoveries are made every year.
The internet has opened up a whole new realm of communication.
She is recognized as an expert in the realm of classical music.

Synset Examples

the untroubled kingdom of reason
a land of make-believe
the rise of the realm of cotton in the south

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of knowledge, activity, or interest
  • Synonym: sphere - an area of activity, interest, or expertise

Etymology

From Old French *reaume* (kingdom), altered from Latin *regnum* (kingdom).
