rebroadcast

1. NOUN

/ riːˈbrɔːdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbrɔːdˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a television or radio program that is broadcast again after its original airing
repeat of a TV or radio program

Examples

The network scheduled a rebroadcast of the popular documentary on Friday night.
Many fans caught the rebroadcast of the game when they missed it live.
The rebroadcast included added commentary and interviews with the cast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - a television or radio program shown or transmitted again

Etymology

From re- (again) + broadcast (transmit by radio or television).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈbrɔdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbrɔːdˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to broadcast (a program, film, etc.) again after its original broadcast
to broadcast again

Examples

The documentary was rebroadcast last night for viewers who missed it the first time.
The station plans to rebroadcast the interview due to popular demand.
They rebroadcast classic films every Sunday evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program or information by radio or television

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + broadcast.
