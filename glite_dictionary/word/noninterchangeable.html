noninterchangeable

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˌɪntərˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of things or terms) not able to be switched with each other without affecting the meaning, function, or result.
not able to be switched without changing meaning

Examples

In this formula, the variables are noninterchangeable and must be placed in the correct order.
The parts are noninterchangeable, so each must be fitted to its specific counterpart.
Certain roles are noninterchangeable because each requires unique skills.

Synset Examples

the arguments of the symmetric relation, ‘is the father of’, are noninterchangeable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged or substituted without affecting the result

Etymology

Formed from non- (not) + interchangeable (able to be exchanged).
