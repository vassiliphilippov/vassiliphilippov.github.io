sale

1. NOUN

/ seɪl / (AmE), / seɪl / (BrE)
an act or instance of selling something; the transfer of ownership from seller to buyer in exchange for payment
act of selling; transaction

Examples

After weeks without success, she finally made her first sale today.
The sale of the house was completed last month.
He celebrated every sale with a small victory dance.

Synset Examples

he has just made his first sale
they had to complete the sale before the banks closed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purchase - the act of buying something

Etymology

From Old English sala, from Old Norse sala (sale, selling).

3. NOUN

/ seɪl / (AmE), / seɪl / (BrE)
an act or contract in which ownership of property is transferred from the seller to the buyer for a fixed price in money
act or contract of selling property

Examples

The sale of the building was finalized after weeks of negotiation.
They signed the sale agreement at the lawyer's office.
The company reported strong sales of its new product last quarter.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English sala (a selling), from Old Norse sala (sale).
