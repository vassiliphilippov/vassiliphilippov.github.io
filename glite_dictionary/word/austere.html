austere

1. ADJ

/ ɔˈstɪr / (AmE), / ɔːˈstɪə / (BrE)
showing strict self-discipline, avoiding luxury and comfort; practicing great self-denial.
practicing strict self-denial or simplicity

Examples

He lived an austere life, giving up all unnecessary comforts and pleasures.
The monastery was known for its austere rules and daily routines.
Despite his wealth, her father maintained an austere approach to spending.

Synset Examples

Be systematically ascetic … do … something for no other reason than that you would rather not do it
a desert nomad's austere life
a spartan diet
a spartan existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascetic - leading a simple life with self-discipline and no luxury
  • Synonym: spartan - marked by simplicity and lack of luxury

Etymology

From Latin austerus (severe), from Greek austēros (harsh, severe).

2. ADJ

/ ɔˈstɪr / (AmE), / ɔːˈstɪə / (BrE)
having a stern or strict appearance or manner; severe or unfriendly in aspect
strict or stern in appearance or manner

Examples

The headmaster entered with an austere look that silenced the entire classroom.
Her austere manner kept the children on their best behavior.
He wore an austere expression, giving no hint of warmth or amusement.

Synset Examples

an austere expression
a stern face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stern - serious and unrelenting in attitude or manner
  • Synonym: forbidding - unfriendly or threatening in appearance
  • Antonym: gentle - mild in temperament or manner

Etymology

From Latin austērus, from Greek austēros (harsh, severe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01303135-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɔːˈstɪr / (AmE), / ɔːˈstɪə / (BrE)
having a plain and simple style or appearance; without unnecessary decoration or luxury
severely simple in style or appearance

Examples

The house was decorated in an austere style with only the essentials on display.
Her austere bedroom had plain white walls and no pictures or ornaments.
The austere furnishings gave the office a cold, businesslike feel.

Synset Examples

a stark interior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - simple or ordinary in character or appearance
  • Antonym: elaborate - involving many details and complicated design

Etymology

From Latin 'austerus', from Greek 'austēros' (harsh, severe).
