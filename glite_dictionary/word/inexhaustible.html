inexhaustible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnɪɡˈzɔstəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnɪɡˈzɔːstəbl / (BrE)
impossible to use up completely; unlimited in supply or capacity.
impossible to use up completely

Examples

The ocean seemed to provide an inexhaustible source of food for the community.
His energy for solving problems appeared inexhaustible during the long negotiation.
There is an inexhaustible supply of creativity among the team members.

Synset Examples

an inexhaustible supply of coal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exhaustible - able to be used up completely
  • Synonym: limitless - without any limit; endless

Etymology

From Latin inexhaustibilis, from in- (not) + exhaurire (to exhaust) + -ibilis (-ible).

2. ADJ

impossible to use up completely; unable to be exhausted or depleted.
impossible to use up entirely

Examples

She seemed to have an inexhaustible supply of interesting stories.
The sun is an inexhaustible source of energy for our planet.

Synset Examples

an inexhaustible supply of coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitless - without any limit; boundless
  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin inexhaustibilis, from in- 'not' + exhaustibilis 'able to be exhausted'.
