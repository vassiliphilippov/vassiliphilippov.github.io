Cassiopeia

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæsiəˈpiə / (AmE), / ˌkasiəˈpiːə / (BrE)
a prominent W-shaped constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located near the North Star (Polaris)
W-shaped constellation near Polaris

Examples

The constellation Cassiopeia is easy to spot in the night sky due to its distinctive W shape.
Astronomers often use Cassiopeia as a reference point when locating Polaris.
In Greek mythology, Cassiopeia was punished by being placed among the stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andromeda - a constellation named after the daughter of Cassiopeia in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek mythology: Cassiopeia, the wife of Cepheus and mother of Andromeda, placed among the stars.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkæsi.oʊˈpiː.ə / (AmE), / ˌkæsi.əˈpiː.ə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the wife of Cepheus and mother of Andromeda, noted for her vanity, who was later placed among the stars as a constellation.
queen in Greek mythology, mother of Andromeda

Examples

In ancient tales, Cassiopeia boasted about her beauty, angering the sea god Poseidon.
The constellation Cassiopeia can be seen clearly in the northern sky during autumn.
As the myth goes, Queen Cassiopeia claimed she was more beautiful than the Nereids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cepheus - king of Ethiopia, husband of Cassiopeia
  • Synonym: Andromeda - daughter of Cassiopeia and Cepheus, Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Kassiopeia (Κασσιόπεια), a queen in myth.
