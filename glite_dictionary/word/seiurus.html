Seiurus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small songbirds in the family Parulidae, which includes ovenbirds and water thrushes native to the Americas.
genus of ovenbirds and water thrushes

Examples

The Seiurus genus has been the subject of many ornithological studies.
Birdwatchers often look for members of the Seiurus genus along woodland streams.
Ovenbirds, once classified under Seiurus, are known for their distinctive song.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, coined as a genus name for certain birds.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds in the family Parulidae, including the ovenbird and some water thrushes.
genus of birds including ovenbirds and water thrushes

Examples

The genus Seiurus includes the well-known ovenbird found across North America.
Researchers studied the distribution of Seiurus species in various wetland habitats.
The water thrush, a member of the genus Seiurus, is often seen near streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovenbird - a North American bird in the genus Seiurus
  • Synonym: water thrush - a North American warbler in the genus Seiurus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, probably derived from Latin 'seiurus', of uncertain origin, used in taxonomy.
