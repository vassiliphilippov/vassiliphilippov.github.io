Alexander III

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪgˈzændər ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˌælɪgˈzændə ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1845–1894) The Emperor of Russia from 1881 to 1894, son of Alexander II, known for his conservative rule and opposition to political reform.
Russian czar (1845–1894), son of Alexander II

Examples

Alexander III implemented strict policies to maintain autocratic rule in Russia.
The reign of Alexander III was marked by political repression and increased censorship.
Unlike his reformist father, Alexander III reversed many liberal policies in the Russian Empire.
