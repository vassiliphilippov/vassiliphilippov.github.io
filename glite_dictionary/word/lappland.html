Lappland

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæpˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈlæp.lænd / (BrE)
A region in northernmost Europe, traditionally inhabited by the Sami people, encompassing parts of Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia.
region in northern Europe inhabited by Sami

Examples

The indigenous Sami people have inhabited Lappland for centuries, adapting to its harsh Arctic environment.
Tourists visit Lappland to experience the Northern Lights during the long winter nights.
Reindeer herding remains an important part of the culture in Lappland.

Etymology

From Swedish 'Lappland', literally 'Land of the Lapps' (Sami).
