to flocculate

1. VERB

/ ˈflɑːkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause (individual particles in a liquid) to aggregate into a clump or mass, forming a fluffy or lumpy cluster
cause particles to clump together

Examples

The scientist used a reagent to flocculate impurities from the water sample.
Adding lime to the wastewater will flocculate the suspended solids, making them easier to remove.
Strong agitation can prevent chemicals from properly flocculating the particles during the treatment process.

Synset Examples

The chemist flocculated the suspended material

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulate - to cause a fluid to change into a thickened or solid state
  • Antonym: to disperse - to cause particles to move apart and spread out

Etymology

From Latin 'flocculus' (a small tuft of wool).

2. VERB

/ ˈflɑːkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance or particles) to form or cause to form into small clumps or masses; to aggregate into a flocky or fluffy mass, especially in a liquid.
form clumps or lumps (in a liquid)
medical

Examples

When certain chemicals are added to water, the impurities may flocculate and settle at the bottom.
In wastewater treatment, suspended solids often flocculate naturally over time.
The protein began to flocculate once the temperature was increased.

Synset Examples

the protoplasms flocculated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precipitate - to cause (a substance) to be deposited in solid form from a solution

Etymology

From Latin flocculus, diminutive of floccus (a tuft of wool).
