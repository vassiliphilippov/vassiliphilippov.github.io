splenitis

1. NOUN

/ spləˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / splɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the spleen.
inflammation of the spleen
medical

Examples

The doctors diagnosed the patient with splenitis after noting significant swelling and pain in his abdomen.
Because splenitis can be caused by various infections, early treatment is essential for recovery.
Her persistent fever and fatigue were eventually traced to splenitis caused by a bacterial infection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hepatitis - inflammation of the liver
  • Degree Higher: perisplenitis - inflammation of the tissues surrounding the spleen

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek splēn (spleen) + -itis (inflammation).
