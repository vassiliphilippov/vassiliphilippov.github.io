cheval-de-frise

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˌvɑːl də ˈfriːz / (AmE), / ʃəˌval də ˈfriːz / (BrE)
a defensive barrier consisting of spikes or barbed wire mounted on a movable frame, used to block cavalry or attackers
spiked defensive barrier

Examples

The soldiers erected a cheval-de-frise to protect their position from cavalry charges.
Museum visitors can see a restored cheval-de-frise from the Revolutionary War era.
During the siege, engineers placed a cheval-de-frise at the city gates to prevent enemy breaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - structure that blocks or restricts movement
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that obstructs or hinders progress

Etymology

From French 'cheval de frise' (Frisian horse), referencing defensive barriers used in Friesland.
