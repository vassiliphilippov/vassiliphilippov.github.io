Lewis

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(Meriwether Lewis) United States explorer and soldier who led the expedition from St. Louis to the mouth of the Columbia River (1774–1809).
American explorer and leader of the Lewis and Clark expedition

Examples

Meriwether Lewis and William Clark played pivotal roles in mapping the American West.
The journals of Lewis provide fascinating insights into the early 19th-century expedition.
Many historians credit Lewis for his scientific observations during the journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clark - William Clark, co-leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition
