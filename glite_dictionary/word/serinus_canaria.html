Serinus canaria

1. NOUN

/ səˈraɪnəs kəˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / sɪˈraɪnəs kəˈnɛəriə / (BrE)
A small songbird native to the Canary Islands and Azores, commonly kept as a cage bird for its melodious song; also known as the canary.
songbird native to Canary Islands, popular cage bird

Examples

The Serinus canaria is admired worldwide for its beautiful singing ability.
Many aviculturists breed Serinus canaria for their vibrant plumage and cheerful songs.
In the wild, Serinus canaria inhabits the subtropical forests of the Canary Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canary - a small songbird of yellow or greenish color, kept for its singing

Etymology

From Latin; 'Serinus' is a genus of finches, 'canaria' refers to the Canary Islands.
