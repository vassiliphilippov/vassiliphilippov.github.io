profiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of recording and analyzing a person's behavior or characteristics to predict their ability, identity, or to assign them to a group, often for assessment or investigation purposes
analysis of behavior or traits for assessment or identification

Examples

The police used profiling to narrow down their list of suspects in the case.
Some criticize airport security profiling as being discriminatory and ineffective.
In marketing, profiling helps companies target their advertisements to specific audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological profiling - the process of inferring psychological traits from observed behavior, especially in criminal investigations
  • Antonym: stereotyping - assigning generalized traits to a group based on limited information

Etymology

From 'profile' + '-ing'. 'Profile' from Italian *profilare*, meaning 'to draw in outline'.
