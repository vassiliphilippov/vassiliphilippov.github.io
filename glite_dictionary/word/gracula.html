Gracula

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / ɡrəˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the starling family, containing the hill mynas, known for their ability to mimic sounds.
genus of birds including hill mynas

Examples

The Gracula genus is renowned for its members' remarkable vocal abilities.
Many bird enthusiasts keep Gracula species as pets due to their intelligence.
Scientists recently discovered a new subspecies within the Gracula genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hill myna - a bird species of the genus Gracula, known for imitating sounds

Etymology

From Latin 'gracula' meaning 'jackdaw' or a similar crow-like bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrækjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈɡrækjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the starling family, Sturnidae, that includes the hill mynas, known for their ability to mimic sounds.
genus of birds including hill mynas

Examples

The Gracula genus is well known for its members’ vocal abilities.
Many bird enthusiasts admire the striking appearance of birds in the Gracula genus.
The hill myna, a member of the Gracula genus, is prized as a pet for its talking ability.

From Latin 'gracula' meaning 'jackdaw'.
