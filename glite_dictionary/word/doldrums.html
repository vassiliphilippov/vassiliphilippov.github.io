doldrums

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊl.drəmz / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.drəmz / (BrE)
a region near the equator with calm and light winds, especially between the northern and southern trade winds in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans
region of calm winds near the equator

Examples

Sailors dreaded becoming stranded in the doldrums because the wind would often fail for days.
Crossing the doldrums was always a major challenge for ships in the age of sail.
The yacht was stuck in the doldrums, barely moving for hours under the hot sun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊl.drəmz / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.drəmz / (BrE)
a period or state of inactivity, stagnation, or slump, especially in business or artistic activity
state of inactivity or stagnation

Examples

After the holidays, sales often fall into the doldrums until spring arrives.
The artist was stuck in the doldrums and unable to come up with new ideas.
The local economy remained in the doldrums for several years after the factory closed.

Synset Examples

economic growth of less than 1% per year is considered to be economic stagnation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnation - a state of not flowing or not changing, especially in economic growth
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or activity

Etymology

Originally a nautical term from 18th-century English, likely from 'dold' (stupid, dull) plus '-rum' (a suffix forming nouns).
