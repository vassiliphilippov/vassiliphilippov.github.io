barnburner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌbɝnɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌbɜːnə / (BrE)
An event, such as a game, performance, or contest, that is exceptionally exciting or impressive.
exciting or impressive event
informal american

Examples

Last night's basketball game was a barnburner that went into double overtime.
The election turned into a real barnburner, with results coming down to the final votes.
Her performance at the festival was such a barnburner that the crowd gave her a standing ovation.

Synset Examples

the rock concert was a real barnburner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showstopper - an event or performance that attracts a lot of attention because it is very impressive

Etymology

American English, originally referring to a radical member of the New York Democratic party in the 1840s who would "burn down the barn to get rid of the rats," later applied metaphorically to exciting events.
