psychotherapeutics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌθɛrəˈpjutɪks / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of psychiatry concerned with the use of psychological methods to treat mental disorders
branch of psychiatry using psychological treatment
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in psychotherapeutics during her medical training.
Advances in psychotherapeutics have greatly improved patient outcomes.
The conference featured leading experts in psychotherapeutics discussing new therapy approaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychotherapy - the treatment of mental disorder by psychological rather than medical means

Etymology

From psycho– (mind) + therapeutics (treatment), ultimately from Greek therapeuein (to take care of, treat).
