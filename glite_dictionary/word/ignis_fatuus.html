ignis fatuus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪɡ.nɪs ˈfætʃ.u.əs / (AmE), / ˈɪɡ.nɪs ˈfæt.ju.əs / (BrE)
A pale, elusive light seen at night over marshy ground, often thought to mislead travelers; also called 'will-o'-the-wisp'.
pale light over marshy ground

Examples

The old legend warned travelers not to follow the ignis fatuus across the misty marshes.
Some scientists believe that ignis fatuus is caused by the combustion of gases in wetlands.
At midnight, a faint ignis fatuus appeared above the bog, flickering in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will-o'-the-wisp - a phosphorescent light seen at night over marshy ground; synonym for ignis fatuus

Etymology

From Latin 'ignis' (fire) and 'fatuus' (foolish), meaning 'foolish fire'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡ.nɪs ˈfætʃ.u.əs / (AmE), / ˌɪɡ.nɪs ˈfætjʊ.əs / (BrE)
An illusion or deceptive hope that misleads people; sometimes refers to a phosphorescent light appearing at night over marshy ground, also known as 'will-o'-the-wisp.'
a misleading illusion or deceptive hope

Examples

The promise of quick riches proved to be an ignis fatuus, leading many to disappointment.
Chasing the approval of everyone is often like pursuing an ignis fatuus—always out of reach.
At night, the traveler mistook the marsh light for a village, not realizing it was only an ignis fatuus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will-o'-the-wisp - a phosphorescent light seen over marshy ground; a misleading hope
  • Synonym: mirage - an optical illusion, especially in the desert

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ignis fatuus, meaning 'foolish fire.'
