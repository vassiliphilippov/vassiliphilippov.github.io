Mohria caffrorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊriə kæˈfrɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈmɔːriə kæˈfrɔːrəm / (BrE)
A sweetly scented African fern characterized by narrow bipinnate fronds.
African fern with scented, narrow fronds

Examples

The healthy leaves of the Mohria caffrorum added a lush green touch to the garden.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Mohria caffrorum growing near the riverbank.
The sweet fragrance of Mohria caffrorum was noticeable even from a distance.

Etymology

Mohria: named after German botanist Paul Mohr; caffrorum: from Latin, meaning 'of the Kaffirs,' referring to southeastern Africa.
