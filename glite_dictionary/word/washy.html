washy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑʃi / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃi / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially a drink) thin, watery, or lacking in strength or flavor.
thin or watery, lacking flavor

Examples

The soup tasted surprisingly washy, as if it had been watered down.
He complained about the washy coffee served at the meeting.
After adding too much milk, the tea had a rather washy flavor.

Synset Examples

washy coffee
watery milk
weak tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watery - (of a drink) containing too much water, lacking in strength or flavor
  • Synonym: weak - (of a liquid, especially a drink) lacking strength or flavor
  • Antonym: strong - (of a drink) having a lot of flavor or strength

Etymology

From 'wash' + '-y', dating to the 16th century, originally meaning like water or thin as if mixed with too much water.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑʃi / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃi / (BrE)
(of color) faded or weak; lacking in freshness or brilliance.
faded, weak in color

Examples

The artist avoided washy tones to create a more vibrant painting.
After years of sunlight, the curtains looked washy and lifeless.
She preferred bold colors to the washy pastels in the old room.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faded - having lost brightness or vividness of color
  • Synonym: pale - light in color or lacking in intensity

Etymology

From wash + -y, originally referring to watered-down or diluted colors.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406038-s
Synset POS: s
