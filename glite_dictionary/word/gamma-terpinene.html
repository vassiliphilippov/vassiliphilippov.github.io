gamma-terpinene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə tərˈpɪniːn / (AmE), / ˈɡæmə tɜːˈpɪniːn / (BrE)
a natural organic compound, classified as a terpene hydrocarbon, occurring in the oils of various plants
a terpene hydrocarbon found in plant oils

Examples

gamma-terpinene is commonly found in the essential oils of citrus fruits such as lemons.
Researchers studied the antioxidant properties of gamma-terpinene in laboratory experiments.
Some cleaning products use natural fragrances derived from gamma-terpinene extracted from plants.

Synset Examples

γ-Terpinene and δ-terpinene (also known as terpinolene) are natural and have been isolated from a variety of plant sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terpene - a large class of hydrocarbons produced by plants

Etymology

From Greek gamma (Γ, third letter of the Greek alphabet) and terpinene, a type of terpene hydrocarbon.
