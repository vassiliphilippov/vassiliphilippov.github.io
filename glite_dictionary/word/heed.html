heed

1. NOUN

/ hid / (AmE), / hiːd / (BrE)
careful attention or consideration to something, especially to advice or warnings.
careful attention or consideration

Examples

She gave little heed to the doctor's advice and continued her normal routine.
The company failed to pay heed to the complaints of its customers.
Without proper heed, important details can easily be overlooked.

Synset Examples

his attentiveness to her wishes
he spends without heed to the consequences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attention - notice taken of someone or something
  • Antonym: disregard - lack of attention or consideration

Etymology

From Old English hēdan (to pay attention, care for).

2. VERB

/ hid / (AmE), / hiːd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something, especially advice or a warning
pay close attention to

Examples

You should heed your doctor's advice when it comes to your health.
If you heed the warning signs, you can avoid many problems.
She always heeds her parents' guidance before making important decisions.

Synset Examples

Heed the advice of the old men

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ignore - to refuse to pay attention to something
  • Synonym: notice - to become aware of or pay attention to something

Etymology

From Old English 'hēdan' (to mind, regard).
