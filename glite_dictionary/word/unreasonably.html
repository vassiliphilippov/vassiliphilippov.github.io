unreasonably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈriːzənəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzənəbli / (BrE)
To a degree that is excessive or not guided by reason or fairness.
to an excessive or unfair degree

Examples

The landlord raised the rent unreasonably despite the poor condition of the apartment.
The manager was unreasonably strict about minor mistakes.
She felt the referee was unreasonably harsh with his decisions.

Synset Examples

his prices are unreasonably high

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reasonably - to a fair or moderate degree
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00036472-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈriːzənəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzənəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is not fair, sensible, or based on good judgment.
in an unfair or irrational manner

Examples

She became unreasonably angry at the slightest criticism.
The landlord unreasonably raised the rent without any explanation.
It's unreasonably difficult to get a response from their customer service team.

Synset Examples

she reacted unreasonably when she learned she had failed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reasonably - in a fair or sensible manner

Etymology

From 'unreasonable' + '-ly', with 'unreasonable' from Latin 'rationabilis' (reasonable) plus prefix 'un-'.
