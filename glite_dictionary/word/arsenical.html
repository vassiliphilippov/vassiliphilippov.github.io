arsenical

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈsɛnɪkəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈsɛnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, containing, or derived from arsenic.
containing or relating to arsenic
medical

Examples

Prolonged exposure to arsenical compounds can be hazardous to health.
The laboratory disposed of all arsenical waste according to strict safety guidelines.
Certain pesticides once contained arsenical ingredients, but their use has been largely discontinued.

Synset Examples

arsenic vapor

Etymology

From French 'arsenical', based on Latin 'arsenicum' (arsenic).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈsɛnɪkəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈsɛnɪkəl / (BrE)
a pesticide, drug, or other substance that contains arsenic, especially one used for controlling pests or as a medical treatment
a substance containing arsenic, used as pesticide or drug
medical

Examples

Many farmers used arsenical compounds to control insect pests before safer alternatives were available.
The patient showed symptoms of poisoning after accidentally ingesting an arsenical.
Regulations now restrict the use of arsenical pesticides to prevent environmental contamination.

Etymology

From Latin arsenicum (arsenic), from Greek arsenikon (yellow orpiment, arsenic).
