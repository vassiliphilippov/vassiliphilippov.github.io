chatelaine

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃætəˈleɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃatəleɪn / (BrE)
A chain worn at a woman's waist, used for carrying keys, a purse, or other small items, especially popular in the 18th and 19th centuries.
waist chain for carrying keys or small items

Examples

The antique chatelaine she wore was decorated with tiny silver charms.
Museum displays often feature a Victorian chatelaine complete with scissors and thimble.
In the 19th century, a chatelaine was a practical accessory for keeping important items close at hand.

Etymology

From French châtelaine, originally meaning 'mistress of a château', later applied to the waist chain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃætəˌleɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃætleɪn / (BrE)
a woman who is in charge of a large house, especially a chateau or country estate
woman in charge of a large house

Examples

The noble family's chatelaine oversaw the servants and managed the household accounts.
As chatelaine of the manor, she hosted elaborate dinners for guests.
In many Victorian novels, the chatelaine is portrayed as both gracious and authoritative.

Etymology

From French 'châtelaine', feminine of 'châtelain' (lord of a castle), from Latin 'castellanus' (keeper of a castle).
