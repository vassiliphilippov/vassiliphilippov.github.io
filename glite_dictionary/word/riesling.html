Riesling

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːz.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːz.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a variety of white grape of German origin, used for making aromatic white wines, especially in Europe and California
a variety of white grape; aromatic white wine

Examples

The chef recommended a dry Riesling to pair with the spicy Thai dish.
Many vineyards in California produce award-winning Riesling wines each year.
She prefers white wines like Riesling for their floral and fruity aromas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - a popular variety of white grape used for wine

Etymology

From German Riesling, the name of the grape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːzlɪŋ / (BrE)
A fragrant dry or sweet white wine made from a variety of grape grown especially in the Rhine valley and similar regions.
type of white wine; Rhine grape variety

Examples

A chilled glass of Riesling pairs perfectly with spicy Asian cuisine.
They toured German vineyards and sampled several Rieslings from the Mosel region.
At dinner, he recommended a crisp Riesling to complement the seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - a popular white wine grape and the wine made from it
  • Antonym: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grape varieties

Etymology

From German 'Riesling', the name of the grape and wine.
