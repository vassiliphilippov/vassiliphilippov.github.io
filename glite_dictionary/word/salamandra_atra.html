Salamandra atra

1. NOUN

/ sæləˌmændrə ˈɑtrə / (AmE), / sæləˌmændrə ˈætrə / (BrE)
A species of salamander native to the Alps, notable for being ovoviviparous and for its entirely black coloration.
a black Alpine salamander species

Examples

The Salamandra atra is well adapted to the cold, mountainous environments of the Alps.
Researchers observed several Salamandra atra under rocks after a summer rain.
Unlike many other salamanders, the Salamandra atra gives birth to live young rather than laying eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian of the order Caudata

Etymology

From Latin: 'salamandra' meaning salamander, and 'atra' meaning black or dark.
