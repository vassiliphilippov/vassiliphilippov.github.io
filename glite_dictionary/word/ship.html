2. NOUN

/ ʃɪp / (AmE), / ʃɪp / (BrE)
a large vessel designed to carry passengers or goods by sea
large seagoing vessel for passengers or cargo

Examples

The massive ship sailed across the Atlantic in just six days.
Hundreds of containers were loaded onto the ship at the port.
She dreamed of traveling the world on a luxury ship.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boat - a small vessel for traveling on water
  • Synonym: ferry - a boat or ship for carrying passengers and vehicles across water

Etymology

From Old English 'scip', related to Old Norse 'skip' and German 'Schiff', meaning ship or vessel.
