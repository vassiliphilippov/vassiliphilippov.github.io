Lycaena hypophlaeas

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnə ˌhaɪpoʊˈfleɪəs / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːnə ˌhaɪpɒˈfliːəs / (BrE)
a small copper-colored butterfly of central and eastern North America, also known as the American copper
a North American copper butterfly species

Examples

The Lycaena hypophlaeas is often seen fluttering low in open meadows during summer.
Many children enjoy watching the distinctive bright wings of the Lycaena hypophlaeas in local parks.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Lycaena hypophlaeas in several northeastern states.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Lycaena' and species epithet 'hypophlaeas'.
