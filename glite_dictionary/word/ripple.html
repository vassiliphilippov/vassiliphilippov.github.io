ripple

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəl / (BrE)
An oscillation of small amplitude superimposed on a steady value, such as a small variation in voltage or current in electronic circuits.
small oscillation in electronics

Examples

The power supply filter reduces the ripple voltage to ensure stable operation of the circuit.
Engineers measured the ripple on the output signal and adjusted the components accordingly.
A high ripple can cause noise and instability in sensitive electronic equipment.

Etymology

Originally from the verb 'to ripple', referring to small waves or movements; adapted for technical use in electronics.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəl / (BrE)
A small wave or series of waves on the surface of a liquid, caused by a slight breeze or disturbance.
small wave on a liquid's surface

Examples

A pebble dropped into the pond caused a ripple to spread across the water.
The cool breeze made tiny ripples on the surface of the lake.
She watched the ripples form as she gently stirred her tea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wave - a moving ridge or swell on the surface of a liquid
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəl / (BrE)
(of liquid) to flow or move with a series of small waves or undulations, often making a soft, bubbling noise.
flow with small waves, often with a bubbling sound

Examples

The stream began to ripple over the rocks, filling the air with a soothing sound.
Leaves floated gently as the shallow water continued to ripple past.
She listened to the creek rippling through the quiet forest.

Synset Examples

babbling brooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to make a continuous, gentle sound of water flowing

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, referring to the sound made by small waves.

4. VERB

/ ˈrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəl / (BrE)
to form or cause small waves or undulations on the surface of water or another liquid
form small waves on a surface

Examples

The wind began to ripple the calm surface of the lake.
She tossed a stone and watched it ripple the water.
A gentle breeze rippled the pond, creating tiny waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undulate - move with a smooth wavelike motion
  • Antonym: smooth - without unevenness or ripples

Etymology

Originated in early 17th century, possibly from frequentative of 'rip' or influenced by 'ripple' (noun).
