stephanotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛfəˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌstiːfəˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen climbing shrubs known for their fragrant, waxy, white flowers, often used in floral arrangements.
genus of fragrant climbing shrubs

Examples

The bride carried a bouquet of stephanotis mixed with white roses.
Stephanotis thrives in warm, humid climates and needs plenty of sunlight.
Florists often use stephanotis for its beautiful scent and elegant appearance in wedding arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madagascar jasmine - common name for stephanotis floribunda, a popular cultivated species

Etymology

From Modern Latin Stephanotis, from Greek 'stephanos' (crown), referring to the flower's shape.
