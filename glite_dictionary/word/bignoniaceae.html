Bignoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡˌnoʊniˈeɪsii / (AmE), / bɪɡˌnəʊnɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including trees, shrubs, or woody vines, often with fruit resembling gourds or capsules, known as the bignonia or trumpet creeper family
family of flowering plants (bignonia/trumpet creeper)

Examples

Many ornamental vines belong to the Bignoniaceae, renowned for their showy trumpet-shaped flowers.
The Bignoniaceae family includes both tropical trees and woody climbers found in forests worldwide.
Botanists identified the mysterious plant as a member of the Bignoniaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Bignonia, itself named after French librarian Jean-Paul Bignon, combined with the standard Latin family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ bɪgˌnoʊniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɪgˌnəʊniˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including trees, shrubs, woody vines, or herbs, often with gourd-like or capsular fruit, native mostly to tropical and subtropical regions; sometimes known as the trumpet-creeper family
family of flowering plants (trumpet-creeper family)

Examples

The Bignoniaceae are well known for their attractive trumpet-shaped flowers.
Several species in the Bignoniaceae family are popular ornamental plants in warm climates.
Research has focused on the medicinal properties found in various members of Bignoniaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Bignonia, named after Abbé Jean-Paul Bignon (1662–1743), a French royal librarian, plus the botanical suffix -aceae indicating a plant family.
