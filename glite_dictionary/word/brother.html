Brother

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
A title used for a monk or male member of a religious order, often used as a form of address in the Roman Catholic Church and some other Christian denominations.
monk's title in Catholic Church

Examples

After taking his vows, he was known as Brother Thomas within the abbey.
Brother Michael greeted the visitors at the monastery gate each morning.
The church announced that Brother John would lead the morning prayers.

Synset Examples

a Benedictine Brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Father - Title for a priest in the Catholic Church
  • Synonym: Sister - Title for a nun in the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Old English 'brōthor', used as a title in religious orders.
