Thracian

1. ADJ

/ ˈθreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθreɪʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Thrace, its people, or its culture
of Thrace or its people/culture

Examples

The museum has a collection of Thracian artifacts dating back to ancient times.
Her research focused on Thracian language and mythology.
The Thracian warriors were known for their distinctive helmets.

Etymology

From Latin Thracius, from Greek Thrakios, relating to Thrace.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθreɪʃən / (BrE)
the extinct Indo-European language once spoken by the ancient people of Thrace until the early Middle Ages
extinct language of ancient Thrace

Examples

Scholars have studied ancient inscriptions to learn more about the Thracian language.
Very few written records of Thracian have survived to the present day.
The Thracian language is thought to have some similarities with Phrygian and other ancient Indo-European tongues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phrygian - an ancient Indo-European language spoken in Phrygia, closely related to Thracian

Etymology

From Latin Thracianus, from Thracia (Thrace), the region historically inhabited by the Thracians.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06955983-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈθreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθreɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who lived in or came from ancient Thrace, a historical region in Southeast Europe.
inhabitant of ancient Thrace

Examples

The tombs discovered in Bulgaria belonged to a noble Thracian family.
Historians believe that the ancient Thracians had a distinct language and culture.
A skilled Thracian warrior was often recruited into the Roman army.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - an inhabitant of ancient Greece
  • Synonym: Roman - an inhabitant of ancient Rome

Etymology

From Latin Thracianus, from Greek Thrakios, from Thrake (Thrace).

Synset POS: n
