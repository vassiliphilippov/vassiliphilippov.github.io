coconut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌkʌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkənʌt / (BrE)
The edible white flesh found inside the shell of a coconut, often used shredded in cooking and baking.
edible white flesh of a coconut

Examples

She sprinkled toasted coconut on top of the chocolate cake.
The recipe calls for a cup of shredded coconut.
I love the texture that fresh coconut adds to curries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copra - dried coconut meat used for extracting oil

Etymology

From Portuguese and Spanish 'coco', meaning 'head' or 'skull', referring to the coconut's shape and markings.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.kə.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kə.nʌt / (BrE)
a large, hard-shelled oval fruit with a fibrous husk, containing thick white edible flesh and a central cavity filled with liquid when fresh
large edible nut with white flesh and liquid

Examples

She cracked open a coconut to drink the refreshing water inside.
The recipe calls for shredded coconut to sprinkle on top of the cake.
During the festival, children enjoyed eating fresh coconut slices on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut palm - the tropical palm tree that produces the coconut

Etymology

From Portuguese and Spanish 'coco', meaning 'head' or 'skull', due to the nut's resemblance to a face.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkəˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkənʌt / (BrE)
a tall tropical palm tree (Cocos nucifera) that bears large, hard-shelled fruits called coconuts, widely cultivated in tropical regions
a tropical palm tree that produces coconuts

Examples

The sandy shore was lined with tall coconut trees swaying gently in the breeze.
After the storm, several coconut palms had fallen across the road.
Farmers in the region rely heavily on coconut cultivation for their livelihood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - a type of tree with large, fan-shaped or feather-like leaves, common in warm regions

Etymology

From Portuguese and Spanish coco (coconut), possibly from coco (grinning face), for the fruit's appearance.

Source: WordNet
