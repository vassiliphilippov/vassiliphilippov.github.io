Spinoza

1. PROPN

/ spɪˈnoʊzə / (AmE), / spɪˈnəʊzə / (BrE)
(1632–1677) A Dutch philosopher known for developing a pantheistic system of philosophy.
Dutch philosopher (1632–1677)

Examples

Many scholars regard Spinoza as a pivotal figure in the Enlightenment.
The works of Spinoza have influenced modern philosophical thought.
During philosophy lectures, we often discuss Spinoza and his views on God and nature.

Etymology

From the Latinized form of Baruch Spinoza, his full name.
