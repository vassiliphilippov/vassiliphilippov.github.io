cordovan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdəˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəvən / (BrE)
a high-quality, durable leather, originally made from horsehide in Cordoba, Spain, often used in shoes and leather goods
type of fine leather from Spain

Examples

His new shoes were made from luxurious cordovan leather.
The craftsman specialized in belts and wallets crafted from cordovan.
Collectors value vintage briefcases constructed with genuine cordovan.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Cordobán', meaning 'from Córdoba' (a city in southern Spain), where it was first produced.
