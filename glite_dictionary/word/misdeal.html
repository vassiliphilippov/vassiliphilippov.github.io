misdeal

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˈdiːl / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈdiːl / (BrE)
an act of dealing cards incorrectly according to the rules of a card game
an incorrect deal of cards

Examples

A misdeal was declared, and the cards had to be collected and reshuffled.
If you spot a misdeal, let the dealer know before play begins.
He apologized to the group for the unexpected misdeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal - an act of distributing cards to players

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'deal' (to distribute cards).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01087491-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈdiːl / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈdiːl / (BrE)
to deal playing cards incorrectly or in violation of the rules
to deal cards incorrectly

Examples

If you accidentally misdeal the cards, the round must be restarted.
The tournament director had to intervene after a player misdealt for the second time.
She apologized for misdealing and collected the cards to try again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deal - to distribute cards to players in a game

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'deal' (to distribute cards).
