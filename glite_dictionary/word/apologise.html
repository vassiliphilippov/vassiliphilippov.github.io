to apologise

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑː.lə.dʒaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to express regret for a fault, mistake, or wrongdoing; to say one is sorry
say one is sorry for a fault
british

Examples

She apologised for forgetting his birthday and promised to make it up to him.
The company apologised for the delay in responding to customer inquiries.
He quickly apologised after realizing he had interrupted the meeting.

Synset Examples

I apologized for being late
He apologized for the many typos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apologize - express regret; American English spelling

Etymology

From Greek 'apologia' (a speech in defense), via Late Latin and Old French.
