thiabendazole

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪəˈbɛndəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˌθaɪəˈbɛndəzəʊl / (BrE)
a synthetic compound used as an antifungal agent and to treat parasitic worm infections
antifungal and antiparasitic drug
medical

Examples

The veterinarian prescribed thiabendazole to treat the sheep for parasitic worms.
Treatment with thiabendazole effectively cleared the fungal infection from the crops.
Researchers are studying the safety profile of thiabendazole in pediatric patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthelmintic - a drug that expels or destroys parasitic worms
  • Synonym: antifungal - a substance that destroys or inhibits the growth of fungi

Etymology

From thi- (referring to sulfur), abendazole (from chemical structure related to benzimidazole).
