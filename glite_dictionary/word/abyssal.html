abyssal

1. ADJ

/ əˈbɪs.əl / (AmE), / əˈbɪs.əl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring in the depths of the ocean, typically between 2,000 and 5,000 meters below the surface
of the deep ocean (2,000–5,000 meters)

Examples

The discovery of new species in the abyssal zone has captivated marine biologists.
Sediments collected from abyssal plains can reveal much about Earth's history.
Some fish have evolved unique features to survive in abyssal environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelagic - relating to the open sea, especially away from the coast and sea floor
  • Synonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a sea or lake

Etymology

From Latin abyssus (bottomless pit) via French abyssal.

2. ADJ

/ əˈbɪsəl / (AmE), / əˈbɪsəl / (BrE)
extremely deep or unfathomable; relating to or resembling an abyss, especially in depth
extremely deep; like an abyss

Examples

Scientists have discovered strange creatures living in the abyssal zone of the Pacific Ocean.
She felt an abyssal loneliness that seemed impossible to escape.
The submarine dived into the abyssal depths, far below the reach of sunlight.

Synset Examples

the abyssal depths of the ocean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep - extending far down from the top or surface

Etymology

From Greek abussos (bottomless), via Late Latin abyssalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00693960-s
Synset POS: s
