enveloping

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
surrounding and closing in on all sides; completely enclosing or hemming in.
surrounding on all sides

Examples

The fog was enveloping the entire city, reducing visibility to almost nothing.
She walked through the enveloping darkness of the forest, barely able to see a few steps ahead.
An enveloping sense of calm filled the room as the music began to play.

Synset Examples

the army's enveloping maneuver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrounding - to be all around something or someone
  • Antonym: open - uncovered or not enclosed

Etymology

From the verb 'envelop', from Middle French 'enveloper', from Latin 'involupare'.
