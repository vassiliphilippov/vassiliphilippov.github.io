slopseller

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɑpˌsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɒpˌsɛlə / (BrE)
a person who deals in cheap ready-made clothing, especially in the past
dealer in cheap ready-made clothing
archaic

Examples

The slopseller on the corner offered affordable coats to laborers and sailors.
In eighteenth-century London, a slopseller provided essential garments for the working class.
Most of his income came from his work as a slopseller in the crowded marketplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothier - a person or company that makes or sells clothes

Etymology

From 'slop' (cheap ready-made clothing) + 'seller'. 'Slop' derives from Middle English, originally referring to loose outer garments.
