Haliotis tuberculata

1. NOUN

/ hæliˈoʊtɪs tjuːˌbɜrkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / hæliˈəʊtɪs tjuːˌbɜːkjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of abalone found near the Channel Islands, known for its flattened shell and edible flesh.
a species of abalone

Examples

Marine biologists have documented the declining population of Haliotis tuberculata around the Channel Islands.
The European or ormer abalone is scientifically known as Haliotis tuberculata.
Overfishing poses a serious threat to Haliotis tuberculata and other related abalone species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abalone - a common name for marine snails in the family Haliotidae

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Haliotis* meaning ‘sea ear’, and species name *tuberculata* referring to a tuberculate (knobby) shell.
