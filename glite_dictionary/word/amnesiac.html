amnesiac

1. NOUN

/ æmˈniːʒiˌæk / (AmE), / æmˈniːzɪæk / (BrE)
A person suffering from amnesia, or relating to amnesia.
person with amnesia

Examples

The amnesiac struggled to recall details about his childhood after the accident.
After waking up in the hospital, she realized she was an amnesiac and could not remember her name.
Doctors worked with the amnesiac to try to restore some of his lost memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - loss of memory, often due to injury or disease

Etymology

From Greek amnēsiakos (pertaining to forgetfulness), from amnesia.

2. NOUN

/ æmˈniːʒiːˌæk / (AmE), / æmˈniːziːˌæk / (BrE)
a person affected by amnesia, who is unable to remember certain information or experiences
person with amnesia

Examples

The amnesiac struggled to recall his past after the accident.
In the story, a mysterious amnesiac arrives in town with no memory of her identity.
Doctors worked with the amnesiac to help him recover some of his lost memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - loss of memory, especially due to brain injury or psychological causes

Etymology

From amnesia + -ac (suffix forming nouns denoting persons affected by something).
