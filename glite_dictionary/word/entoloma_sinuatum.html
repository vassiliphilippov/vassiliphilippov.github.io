Entoloma sinuatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntoʊˈloʊmə ˌsɪnjuˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌɛntəʊˈləʊmə ˌsɪnjʊˈeɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of deadly poisonous mushroom, with a large cap that starts white or lead-colored and turns yellowish or tan
deadly poisonous mushroom species
medical

Examples

Mushroom foragers are warned never to pick Entoloma sinuatum due to its deadly toxicity.
The cap of Entoloma sinuatum changes from white to yellowish as it matures.
Consuming Entoloma sinuatum can result in severe poisoning, making correct identification crucial.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Entoloma' (genus name) + Latin 'sinuatum' (wavy-edged).
