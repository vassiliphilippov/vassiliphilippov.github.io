napalm

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪˌpɑlm / (AmE), / ˈneɪpɑːm / (BrE)
A highly flammable, gelatinous substance made from gasoline thickened with aluminum soaps, used in fire bombs and flamethrowers.
highly flammable jellied incendiary

Examples

The military used napalm during the conflict, causing widespread devastation.
Protests erupted when reports surfaced about napalm being deployed in civilian areas.
Scientists studied the long-term environmental effects of napalm in war zones.

Etymology

From naphthenic and palmitic acids, chemicals used in its original manufacture.
