barndoor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbɑːndɔː / (BrE)
An opaque, adjustable flap attached to a lamp fixture, used especially in photography and stage lighting to control the direction and spread of light.
adjustable flap on a lamp to control light

Examples

The photographer adjusted the barndoor to focus the light only on the subject.
Stage technicians often use a barndoor to block unwanted spill from the spotlight.
We need to attach a barndoor to this lamp to prevent glare in the background.
