Acocanthera venenata

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈkænθərə vɪˈniːnətə / (AmE), / ˌakəʊˈkanθərə vɛˈniːnətə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa, known for its poisonous properties.
South African poisonous shrub or tree

Examples

The sap of Acocanthera venenata contains toxins that can be harmful if ingested.
Gardeners in South Africa are cautious when handling Acocanthera venenata.
Botanists are studying Acocanthera venenata for its medicinal and toxic properties.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Acocanthera' and species epithet 'venenata', Latin for 'poisoned'.
