dissoluteness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɑːl.juːt.nəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɒl.juːt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking moral restraint and indulging excessively in sensual pleasures
lack of moral restraint; indulgence in sensual pleasure

Examples

His dissoluteness eventually led to his downfall in both career and reputation.
The story depicts the dissoluteness of the aristocracy during that era.
She was troubled by the dissoluteness she witnessed at the lavish parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licentiousness - lack of moral discipline, especially in sexual conduct
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin dissolūtus (loose, dissolute), from dis- (apart) + solvere (to loosen).
