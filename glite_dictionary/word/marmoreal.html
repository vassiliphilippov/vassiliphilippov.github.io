marmoreal

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːrˈmɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌmɑːˈmɔːriəl / (BrE)
resembling marble, especially in smoothness, hardness, or whiteness; characteristic of marble.
resembling or characteristic of marble

Examples

The dancer's marmoreal complexion gave her an ethereal, statue-like appearance.
In the quiet museum, rows of marmoreal busts gazed silently over the gallery.
His face took on a marmoreal calm, betraying no hint of emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marble - a hard crystalline metamorphic rock, used in sculpture and architecture

Etymology

From Latin 'marmoreus', from 'marmor' (marble).
