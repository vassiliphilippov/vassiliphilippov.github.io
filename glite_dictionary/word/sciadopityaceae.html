Sciadopityaceae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌædoʊˌpɪtiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / saɪˌadəʊˌpɪtɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of coniferous trees comprising a single genus, formerly included in the Taxodiaceae family.
family of coniferous trees (single genus)

Examples

The Sciadopityaceae family is represented solely by the Japanese umbrella pine.
Recent phylogenetic studies have led to the separation of Sciadopityaceae from Taxodiaceae.
Botanists debate the evolutionary origins of Sciadopityaceae within the conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxodiaceae - former family grouping containing Sciadopityaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus *Sciadopitys* plus the standard family suffix *-aceae*.
