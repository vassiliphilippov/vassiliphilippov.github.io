cement

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
A substance that hardens to act as an adhesive material, especially used in building to bind stones or bricks together.
hardening adhesive substance, esp. in building

Examples

The workers mixed the cement with water to create a sturdy foundation.
Dental cement is used to hold crowns and bridges in place.
Once the cement hardens, the bricks will be firmly bonded together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortar - a mixture used in building to bond bricks or stones

Etymology

From Latin 'caementum' (stone chips used in making mortar), from 'caedere' (to cut, hew).

2. NOUN

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a powdery substance made from calcined limestone and clay, used with water, sand, or gravel to bind them together when making concrete and mortar
powder for making concrete and mortar

Examples

The workers mixed cement with sand and water to make strong concrete.
He accidentally spilled a bag of cement on the floor during the renovation.
Wet cement should be handled carefully to avoid skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concrete - a building material made from cement, water, sand, and gravel
  • Synonym: mortar - a mixture of cement, sand, and water for bonding bricks and stones
