Teutonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtənɪst / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtənɪst / (BrE)
a specialist in the history, culture, or language of the Teutonic people, especially regarding their influence on England
scholar of Teutonic history or language

Examples

Mary became a renowned Teutonist after publishing several books on Anglo-Saxon influence in England.
The conference attracted every prominent Teutonist from universities across Europe.
As a Teutonist, he specialized in the linguistic roots of Old English words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Germanist - a specialist in German language, literature, or culture

Etymology

From 'Teuton' (referring to Germanic peoples) + '-ist' (a person concerned with or expert in a subject).
