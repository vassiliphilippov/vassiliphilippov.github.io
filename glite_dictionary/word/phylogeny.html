phylogeny

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈlɑdʒəni / (AmE), / faɪˈlɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the evolutionary history and development of a species or group of related organisms
evolutionary history of a species or group
medical

Examples

Modern genetic techniques have revolutionized our understanding of phylogeny.
The phylogeny of birds suggests a close relationship with certain dinosaurs.
Researchers constructed a phylogeny to clarify the species' evolutionary origins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ontology - the origin and development of an individual organism
  • Synonym: evolution - the process by which species develop and diversify over time

Etymology

From Greek 'phylon' (tribe, race) + 'geneia' (origin, birth).
