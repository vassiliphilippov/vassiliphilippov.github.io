balloting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbælətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæl.ət.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of voting, especially by secret ballot, to make a decision or choose between alternatives
process of voting by ballot

Examples

The company used balloting to determine the winner of the employee of the month award.
After much debate, the group resorted to balloting to choose their new leader.
Secret balloting ensured that voters could make their choices without pressure.

Synset Examples

there were only 17 votes in favor of the motion
they allowed just one vote per person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voting - the general act of casting a vote in an election or decision
  • Synonym: election - the act or process of choosing someone for a public office by voting

Etymology

From 'ballot', from Italian 'ballotta' (small ball used for voting).
