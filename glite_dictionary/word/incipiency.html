incipiency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪpiənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpiənsi / (BrE)
the state or fact of beginning to exist or become apparent
the beginning or emerging stage of something

Examples

The incipiency of the project was marked by uncertainty and excitement.
Economic reforms were evident in their incipiency, with only minor changes taking place at first.
Many historians debate the incipiency of the civil rights movement in America.

Synset Examples

he placed the incipience of democratic faith at around 1850
it is designed to arrest monopolies in their incipiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inception - the beginning of something
  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning or start
  • Antonym: conclusion - the end or finish of something

Etymology

From Latin 'incipiens', present participle of 'incipere' (to begin), plus '-cy' forming nouns of quality.
