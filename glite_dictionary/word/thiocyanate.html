thiocyanate

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪ.oʊˈsaɪ.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.əʊˈsaɪ.ə.neɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from thiocyanic acid, typically formed by reacting alkaline cyanides with sulfur
a salt of thiocyanic acid

Examples

The laboratory detected thiocyanate ions in the chemical sample.
High concentrations of thiocyanate can be toxic to certain organisms.
Industrially, thiocyanate compounds are used in the synthesis of dyes and pharmaceuticals.

Etymology

From 'thio-' (sulfur) and 'cyanate' (a salt derived from cyanic acid).
