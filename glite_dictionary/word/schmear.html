schmear

1. NOUN

/ ʃmɪr / (AmE), / ʃmɪə / (BrE)
(informal) A collection or group of related things considered together, often used in the phrase 'the whole schmear'.
a group or collection of things
informal american

Examples

He decided to purchase the whole schmear, not just the sofa.
She packed up the food, drinks, and games—the entire schmear for the picnic.
The dealer offered him the car, warranty, and insurance as one schmear.

Synset Examples

he bought the whole schmeer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batch - a quantity of things produced or handled together
  • Synonym: set - a group or collection of things

Etymology

From Yiddish שמיר (shmir), originally meaning 'spread' or 'smear; applied figuratively in American English.
