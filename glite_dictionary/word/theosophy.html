theosophy

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːsəfi / (AmE), / θɪˈɒsəfi / (BrE)
A religious or philosophical system that seeks direct knowledge of God and the nature of the soul through mystical intuition, often blending ideas from different traditions.
mystical religious philosophy

Examples

She devoted her life to studying theosophy and exploring different mystical traditions.
Many famous thinkers in the nineteenth century were influenced by theosophy.
The lectures focused on the basic tenets of theosophy and its impact on spiritual movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysticism - belief in direct experience of ultimate reality or God through intuition or insight
  • Antonym: rationalism - belief that reason is the chief source of knowledge, contrasting with mysticism

Etymology

From Greek theosophia, meaning 'wisdom concerning God'.
