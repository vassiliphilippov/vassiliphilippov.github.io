Thelypteris palustris pubescens

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɛlɪˈptɛrɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs pjuːˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌθɛlɪˈptɛrɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs pjuːˈbɛsəns / (BrE)
a subspecies of fern found in northeastern North America, distinguished by its softly hairy fronds and preference for wet habitats
a hairy marsh fern subspecies of North America

Examples

The Thelypteris palustris pubescens is often found growing in wet, marshy soils near lakes and ponds.
Botanists identified the sample as Thelypteris palustris pubescens based on the dense hair covering its fronds.
In northeastern states, Thelypteris palustris pubescens is an important indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh fern - common name for Thelypteris palustris, a related fern species

Etymology

From Greek thelys (female) + pteris (fern), Latin palustris (of the marsh), Latin pubescens (downy, hairy).
