Ceratophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætəfəˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / sɛˌrætəfɪˈleɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic flowering plants known as hornworts, consisting of the genus Ceratophyllum.
family of aquatic hornwort plants

Examples

The Ceratophyllaceae are unique among aquatic plants for lacking true roots.
Recent studies have shown that Ceratophyllaceae play an important role in freshwater ecosystems.
Many aquarium enthusiasts cultivate species from the Ceratophyllaceae family for their resilience and beauty.

Etymology

From the genus name Ceratophyllum, from Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'phyllon' (leaf); -aceae is a standard plant family suffix in Latin.
