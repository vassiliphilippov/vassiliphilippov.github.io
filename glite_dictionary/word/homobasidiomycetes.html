Homobasidiomycetes

1. NOUN

/ hoʊmoʊbəˌsɪdioʊˈmaɪsiːts / (AmE), / həʊməʊbæsɪdɪəʊˈmaɪsiːts / (BrE)
a group of basidiomycetous fungi, including mushrooms and puffballs, used in some classification systems
group of basidiomycetous fungi

Examples

Recent studies have reclassified certain fungi formerly included in the Homobasidiomycetes.
The Homobasidiomycetes comprise most of the fungi commonly recognized as mushrooms.
Mycologists debate whether puffballs belong in the Homobasidiomycetes or a different class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gasteromycetes - a group of fungi including puffballs, sometimes considered separate from Homobasidiomycetes
  • Synonym: Hymenomycetes - a group of fungi including mushrooms, sometimes placed within Homobasidiomycetes

Etymology

From Greek homo- (same) + basidium (small base, a structure in fungi) + -mycetes (fungi).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊbəˌsɪdiəˈmaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊbəsɪdɪəˈmiːtiːz / (BrE)
a subclass of basidiomycetous fungi including mushrooms and puffballs, used in some classification systems
subclass of fungi including mushrooms and puffballs

Examples

The Homobasidiomycetes include many of the familiar edible mushrooms.
Certain classification systems group puffballs under the Homobasidiomycetes.
Mycology students studied the evolutionary traits of the Homobasidiomycetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gasteromycetes - class of fungi including puffballs and similar forms
  • Synonym: Hymenomycetes - class of fungi including most mushrooms and bracket fungi

Etymology

From Greek homo- (same) + basidium (a small pedestal, in mycological sense) + -mycetes (fungi).
