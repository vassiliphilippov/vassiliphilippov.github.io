maximization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæksɪməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaksɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something as large or great as possible
making something as great as possible

Examples

The company's goal is the maximization of profits each quarter.
Environmental policies should focus on the maximization of resource efficiency.
Her research is dedicated to the maximization of learning outcomes in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimization - the act of making something as small as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'maximum' (the greatest) + English suffix '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæksɪməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæksɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the mathematical process of finding the greatest value of a function or quantity
mathematical process of finding a maximum

Examples

The maximization of profit is a central goal for many businesses.
In calculus, we often study the maximization of functions to solve optimization problems.
The engineer used maximization techniques to improve the system's efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimization - the mathematical process of finding the minimum value of a function

Etymology

From Latin 'maximum' (greatest) plus English suffix '-ization'.
