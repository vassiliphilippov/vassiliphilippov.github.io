aoristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the aorist tense, a verb form used to indicate a simple, undefined action, especially in Ancient Greek or other inflected languages.
relating to the aorist tense

Examples

The aoristic form in Ancient Greek often expresses an action without indicating its duration.
Many Indo-European languages once had an aoristic tense, but it has disappeared in most modern usages.
His thesis focused on the aoristic aspect in classical Greek poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aorist - a verb tense in some languages expressing a simple, completed action

Etymology

From Greek aoristos (indefinite, without boundaries), via Late Latin aoristus and English suffix -ic.
