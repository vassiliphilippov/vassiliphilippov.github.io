hydrilla

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɪlə / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɪlə / (BrE)
A submerged freshwater plant with whorled, lance-shaped leaves and small, solitary flowers, native to the Old World and widely naturalized in southern United States, where it often clogs waterways.
invasive aquatic plant species

Examples

Large areas of the lake have become choked with hydrilla, making boating difficult.
Environmental agencies are working to find ways to control the spread of hydrilla in Florida.
Because hydrilla grows quickly, it can outcompete native aquatic plants and disrupt local ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrilla, diminutive form of Greek hydra (water serpent).
