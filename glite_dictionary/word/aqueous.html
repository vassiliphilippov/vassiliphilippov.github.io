aqueous

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.kwi.əs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.kwi.əs / (BrE)
relating to, containing, or dissolved in water; watery.
relating to water or watery solutions

Examples

The chemist prepared an aqueous solution of sodium chloride.
Some minerals form only in an aqueous environment where water is present.
The aqueous phase must be separated from the organic solvents before analysis.

Synset Examples

aqueous solutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watery - resembling or containing water

Etymology

From Latin 'aqueus' (watery), from 'aqua' (water).

WordNet ID: oewn-02647112-a

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.kwi.əs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.kwi.əs / (BrE)
produced by the action of water; relating to or resulting from the presence or effect of water, especially in chemical or geological processes.
produced by the action of water

Examples

The minerals were formed under aqueous conditions deep within the Earth.
Some rocks display patterns that indicate aqueous alteration over millions of years.
The laboratory simulated aqueous processes to study the effects of water on the samples.

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) + '-ous' (having the quality of).
