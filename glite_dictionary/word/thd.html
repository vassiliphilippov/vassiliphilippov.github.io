ThD

1. NOUN

/ tiː eɪtʃ ˈdiː / (AmE), / tiː eɪtʃ ˈdiː / (BrE)
A Doctor of Theology; an advanced academic degree awarded for theological studies.
Doctor of Theology degree

Examples

After years of study, she finally earned her ThD from the seminary.
Candidates for the ThD program must have a master's degree in theology or a related field.
His expertise in biblical studies is reflected in his ThD thesis on New Testament literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - Doctor of Philosophy degree
  • Synonym: DD - Doctor of Divinity degree (theology)

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin Theologiae Doctor (Doctor of Theology).
