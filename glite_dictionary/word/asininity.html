asininity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˈnɪnəti / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈnɪnɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely foolish or stupid, especially in a stubborn way
stubborn foolishness or stupidity

Examples

His comment revealed a level of asininity that surprised everyone at the meeting.
The asininity of ignoring expert advice was obvious when the plan failed.
She couldn't believe the asininity of trying the same thing over and over expecting different results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having good judgment; opposite of foolishness

Etymology

From Latin 'asininus' (of or like an ass, foolish) + '-ity' (forming nouns of quality).
