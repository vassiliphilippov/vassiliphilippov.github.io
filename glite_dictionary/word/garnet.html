garnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrnət / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪt / (BrE)
any of a group of hard, glassy minerals (silicates of various metals) typically red or reddish-brown, used as gemstones and abrasives
hard mineral used as a gemstone and abrasive

Examples

She wore a beautiful necklace with a garnet pendant.
Industrial garnet is commonly used in sandblasting and waterjet cutting.
The deep red color of garnet is prized by gem collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruby - a precious stone consisting of corundum in color varieties varying from deep crimson or purple to pale rose

Etymology

From Middle English 'garnet', from Old French 'grenate', from Medieval Latin 'granatum' (from 'granum', meaning 'grain', referring to the stone's granular appearance).
