bloodguilt

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌɡɪlt / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌɡɪlt / (BrE)
The state of having guilt for causing another person's death, especially through bloodshed or murder.
guilt from causing someone's death

Examples

The jury struggled with the weight of bloodguilt in their deliberations.
He lived in exile for years, tormented by his bloodguilt.
Ancient societies often imposed severe penalties for cases of bloodguilt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocence - the state of not being guilty of a crime or offense
  • Synonym: guilt - the fact of having committed a specified or implied offense or crime

Etymology

From Old English 'blodgyld', meaning guilt of bloodshed.
