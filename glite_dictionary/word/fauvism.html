Fauvism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.vɪ.zəm / (BrE)
an early 20th-century art movement characterized by bold, nonnatural colors and simplified forms, pioneered by artists such as Matisse and Derain
early 20th-century art movement with vivid colors

Examples

Matisse was a leading figure of Fauvism, renowned for his use of intense color.
The bold, expressive brushwork of Fauvism broke away from traditional artistic conventions.
Many art students study Fauvism to understand the evolution of modern art.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Expressionism - an art movement emphasizing the expression of emotional experience

Etymology

From French fauvisme, from fauve ('wild beast'), a term used by critics to describe the artists' bold style.
