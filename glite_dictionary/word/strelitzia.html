Strelitzia

1. NOUN

/ strɛˈlɪtsiə / (AmE), / strɛˈlɪtsiə / (BrE)
a genus of large perennial evergreen herbs native to South Africa, known for their banana-like leaves and striking, exotic flowers
genus of tropical flowering plants (bird of paradise)

Examples

The Strelitzia is prized for its vibrant and unusual flowers.
Many gardeners grow Strelitzia in greenhouses to replicate its native habitat.
A mature Strelitzia can reach several feet in height with broad, banana-shaped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird of paradise - common name for plants in the Strelitzia genus, known for showy flowers

Etymology

Named after Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, wife of King George III of Great Britain.
