variant

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˈveəriənt / (BrE)
differing from others of the same kind; exhibiting variation or change.
differing or showing variation

Examples

The painting's colors are variant throughout the canvas, creating a dynamic effect.
His handwriting is variant in both size and style from line to line.
Some traditions have several variant forms depending on the region.

Synset Examples

letters variant in size

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variable - liable to change; not constant
  • Antonym: uniform - not varying; always the same

Etymology

From Latin 'varians,' present participle of 'variare' (to change).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02515460-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˈvɛːrɪənt / (BrE)
a form or version of something that differs in some way from other forms or versions of the same thing
a different form or version

Examples

The new COVID-19 variant spread rapidly across several countries.
This recipe is a spicy variant of the traditional dish.
Scientists discovered a previously unknown variant of the gene.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - the process or result of changing or varying
  • Synonym: version - a particular form of something differing in certain respects
  • Antonym: original - the first form from which copies or variations are made

Etymology

From Latin 'variant-', present participle of 'variare' (to change).
