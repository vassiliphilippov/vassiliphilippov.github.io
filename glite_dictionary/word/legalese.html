legalese

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːɡəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌliːɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
language containing technical terms and complex constructions typical of legal documents, often difficult for non-lawyers to understand.
technical legal language

Examples

The contract is written in such dense legalese that most people would struggle to understand it.
He tried to explain the law without all the confusing legalese.
The lawyer translated the legalese into plain language for her client.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a profession or group; difficult for others to understand
  • Antonym: plain English - clear, straightforward language free of jargon and complexity

Etymology

From 'legal' (relating to law) + '-ese' (suffix used to form names of languages or styles).
