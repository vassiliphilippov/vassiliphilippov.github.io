unifying

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuːnəˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
making or tending to make people or things united; bringing together as a whole.
bringing together; creating unity

Examples

The leader's unifying message brought the community together after the crisis.
A unifying theme runs throughout the entire novel, connecting its various parts.
Music can have a powerful unifying effect on people from different backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divisive - causing disagreement or hostility; creating division
  • Synonym: uniting - joining together; acting to unite

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːnəˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
bringing separate parts together to form a single unit or whole
bringing parts together as one

Examples

His speech acted as a unifying force for the divided community.
The team found a unifying theme in their diverse research projects.
The leader's role is often described as a unifying presence during times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divisive - causing disagreement or disunity
  • Synonym: to unite - to bring together to form a single unit

Etymology

From the verb 'unify', from Latin 'unificare' (to make one), from 'unus' (one).
