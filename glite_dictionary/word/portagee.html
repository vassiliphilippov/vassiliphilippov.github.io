Portagee

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəˌdʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtədʒi / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) A colloquial or dialectal term used to refer to a Portuguese person or to Portuguese cultural heritage.
colloquial term for Portuguese person or heritage
informal offensive

Examples

Some local fishermen are proud to be Portagee and share their traditions.
He told stories about his Portagee grandfather coming to America.
The festival honors the contributions of the Portagee community to the town.

Synset Examples

Many Portagee families settled in the area generations ago.
The Portagee heritage is celebrated every year in the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese - a person from Portugal or relating to Portugal

Etymology

Altered pronunciation and spelling of 'Portuguese' in some dialects, especially in North America.
