pone

1. NOUN

/ poʊn / (AmE), / pəʊn / (BrE)
A type of cornbread, often made without milk or eggs and baked or fried, especially in the southern United States.
a type of southern cornbread
american

Examples

She served a hot pone with butter alongside the stew.
In some Southern families, pone is a staple at the dinner table.
The recipe called for frying the pone in a cast iron skillet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornbread - bread made from cornmeal, common in the U.S. South

Etymology

Probably from Virginia Algonquian *apan* (baked), referring to bread.
