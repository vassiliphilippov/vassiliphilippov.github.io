Capreolus

1. NOUN

/ kæˈpriːoʊləs / (AmE), / kəˈpriːələs / (BrE)
A genus of deer including the roe deer, found in Europe and Asia.
genus of roe deer

Examples

Scientists observed several Capreolus grazing quietly at the edge of the forest.
The distribution of Capreolus extends from western Europe to parts of East Asia.
In the study of mammalian evolution, Capreolus serves as an important genus of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe deer - a small Eurasian deer of the genus Capreolus

Etymology

From Latin 'capreolus' meaning 'roe deer' or 'wild goat'.
