crumb

1. VERB

/ krʌm / (AmE), / krʌm / (BrE)
to remove crumbs from (a surface, especially a table)
remove crumbs from a surface

Examples

After dinner, she crumbed the table with quick, practiced strokes.
The waiter politely crumbed the cloth before serving dessert.
He used a small brush to crumb the table between courses.

2. NOUN

/ krʌm / (AmE), / krʌm / (BrE)
a very small piece or fragment of bread, cake, or other baked food, often broken off from a larger piece
small piece of bread or cake

Examples

She brushed the crumb off the table after breakfast.
A single crumb of cake was left on the plate.
He found a crumb in his pocket hours after lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morsel - a small piece or amount of food
  • Degree Higher: chunk - a large thick piece of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumb', from Old English 'cruma' (fragment, small piece), of Germanic origin.
