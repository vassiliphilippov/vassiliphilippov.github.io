Geomyidae

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɑːmi.aɪˌdiː / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒmɪ.iːˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing rodents commonly known as pocket gophers, native to North and Central America.
family of North American pocket gophers

Examples

Species in the Geomyidae family are known for their extensive burrowing habits.
Researchers discovered a new pocket gopher belonging to Geomyidae in Central America.
The diet of Geomyidae consists mainly of roots and tubers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - a burrowing rodent of North America, member of the family Geomyidae

Etymology

From New Latin Geomyidae, based on the type genus Geomys and suffix -idae indicating a family in zoological taxonomy.
