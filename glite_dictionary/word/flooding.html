flooding

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
a psychological treatment in which a person is intensely exposed to a feared object or situation until their anxiety decreases or they become desensitized
exposure therapy technique in psychology
medical

Examples

Her therapist recommended flooding to address her severe fear of elevators.
During flooding sessions, the patient remained in the presence of dogs until his anxiety subsided.
Some people find flooding too intense and prefer more gradual forms of exposure therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic desensitization - gradual exposure therapy for phobias
  • Synonym: exposure therapy - therapy involving exposure to feared stimuli

Etymology

From the verb 'flood', metaphorically meaning 'to overwhelm', applied to therapy in the mid-20th century.
