delf

1. NOUN

/ dɛlf / (AmE), / dɛlf / (BrE)
an excavation or quarry, especially one dug for mining stone, clay, or other materials
excavation or quarry
archaic

Examples

The abandoned delf had filled with water after years of disuse.
They sourced the clay for the bricks from a local delf.
Walking past the old delf, he recalled stories of miners who once worked there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarry - an open pit from which stone or minerals are extracted
  • Synonym: mine - an excavation for extracting minerals

Etymology

From Middle English 'delf', from Old English 'gedelf' (a dig, excavation), related to 'delve'.
