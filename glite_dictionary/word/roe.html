roe

1. NOUN

/ roʊ / (AmE), / rəʊ / (BrE)
the mass of eggs or spawn of certain crustaceans, such as lobsters or shrimp, often used as food
egg mass of certain crustaceans

Examples

Lobster roe turns bright red when cooked and is considered a delicacy by some.
The chef garnished the dish with a spoonful of shrimp roe.
Fishermen collected the roe from crabs to sell at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caviar - salted roe of sturgeon or other large fish, delicacy

Etymology

From Middle English *rowe*, from Old Norse *hrogn* (spawn, eggs).

2. NOUN

/ roʊ / (AmE), / rəʊ / (BrE)
the eggs or egg-filled ovary of a fish, used as food; typically has a grainy texture
fish eggs or egg-filled ovary

Examples

Salmon roe is often used to garnish sushi dishes in Japanese cuisine.
The chef spooned a delicate layer of roe onto the blini.
Some people enjoy eating roe because of its rich flavor and unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caviar - processed, salted roe of sturgeon or other large fish, eaten as a delicacy

Etymology

From Middle English 'roe', from Old English 'rā' (fish eggs).
