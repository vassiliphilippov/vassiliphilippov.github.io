bongo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a large forest antelope of central Africa, having a reddish-brown coat with white stripes and spiral horns
a large African forest antelope

Examples

A solitary bongo moved quietly through the dense undergrowth.
The bongo's striking stripes help camouflage it in the forest shadows.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered bongo from poaching.

Etymology

From Kele (Bantu language of Gabon) *bongo* (name of the animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a small drum, typically one of a pair, played with the hands and used especially in Latin American music
small hand-played drum

Examples

He played the bongo during the lively drum circle in the park.
The musician added rhythm to the song with his skillful bongo drumming.
Children were eager to try tapping on the bongo in music class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conga - a tall, narrow drum from Cuba, also used in Latin American music

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish 'bongó', possibly of Bantu origin.
