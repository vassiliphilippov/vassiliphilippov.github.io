linebacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌbækər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnˌbækə / (BrE)
an American football player who plays on the defensive team just behind the line of scrimmage, responsible for defending against both running and passing plays
defensive football player behind the linemen
american

Examples

The linebacker tackled the running back before he could gain any yards.
As a rookie linebacker, she quickly became a key player on the team's defense.
Teams often rely on a skilled linebacker to read the quarterback's plays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defender - player whose role is to stop the opposing team from scoring

Etymology

From 'line' (referring to the line of scrimmage) and 'back' (player positioned behind); the term originated in American football in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌbækər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnˌbækə / (BrE)
(in American football) a defensive player who positions themselves close behind the line of scrimmage to stop running plays or defend against passes.
defensive football player behind line of scrimmage
american

Examples

The team's star linebacker made an incredible tackle, stopping the runner at the one-yard line.
After the snap, the linebacker quickly read the play and intercepted the pass.
Training as a linebacker requires both strength and agility to cover both running backs and tight ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive end - position on the defensive line, usually at the edge
  • Synonym: cornerback - defensive back who covers receivers

Etymology

From 'line' (as in line of scrimmage) + 'back' (referring to being behind the line).

a defensive player in American or Canadian football who lines up behind the line of scrimmage and is responsible for stopping running plays or covering passes
defensive football position behind linemen

Examples

The linebacker made a crucial tackle to stop the running back.
As a rookie linebacker, he quickly earned a spot in the starting lineup.
The coach praised the linebacker for his leadership and quick reactions.

Related Terms

Formed from 'line' (referring to the line of scrimmage) and 'back' (a player behind the line).
