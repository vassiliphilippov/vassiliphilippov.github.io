monastic

1. NOUN

/ məˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / məˈnastɪk / (BrE)
a male member of a religious community who lives in a monastery, devoting himself to spiritual work and contemplation
male member of a religious community

Examples

The monastic spent most of his day in silent meditation within the monastery walls.
Many people admire the dedication shown by a monastic to his spiritual practices.
Living as a monastic requires discipline, devotion, and a deep commitment to faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monk - a male member of a monastic religious order
  • Antonym: nun - a female member of a religious community

Etymology

From Late Latin monasticus, from Ancient Greek monastikos (solitary), from monazein (to live alone).

2. ADJ

/ məˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / məˈnæstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a communal life sequestered from the world under religious vows, such as that led by monks or nuns.
relating to secluded religious communal life

Examples

The monks led a monastic existence, dedicated to prayer and study.
She admired the monastic discipline and simplicity of their daily routine.
His monastic lifestyle involved strict rules and little contact with the outside world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloistered - separated from the outside world; sheltered, especially in a religious context

Etymology

From Late Latin monasticus, from Greek monastikos (of a monk), from monazein (to live alone).

Synset POS: s
