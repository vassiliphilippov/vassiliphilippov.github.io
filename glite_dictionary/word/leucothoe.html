Leucothoe

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːˈkɑːθoʊ iː / (AmE), / luːˈkɒθəʊ iː / (BrE)
A genus of American and Asiatic deciduous and evergreen shrubs of the heath family, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of shrubs (heath family)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Leucothoe for its attractive evergreen leaves and graceful arching branches.
The wooded area was filled with Leucothoe shrubs, blooming delicately in the spring.
Several species within the Leucothoe genus are popular choices for shade gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a shrubland habitat or plant of the Ericaceae family

Etymology

From Greek Leucothoe, a mythological figure; used in modern taxonomy for this genus.
