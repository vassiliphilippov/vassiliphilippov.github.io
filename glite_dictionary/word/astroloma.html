Astroloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to Australia and Tasmania, known for their tubular flowers.
a genus of evergreen shrubs of Australia and Tasmania

Examples

Many species of Astroloma have distinctive red or pink tubular flowers.
Gardeners in Australia often cultivate Astroloma for its attractive foliage and blooms.
The Astroloma shrubs thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) and 'loma' (fringe), referring to the star-shaped hairs on the plants.
