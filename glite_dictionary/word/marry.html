to marry

1. VERB

/ ˈmæri / (AmE), / ˈmæri / (BrE)
to become the husband or wife of someone; to take someone as one's spouse in a legal or religious ceremony
to take as a husband or wife

Examples

They decided to marry after dating for three years.
She will marry her childhood sweetheart this spring.
Many couples choose to marry in a traditional ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divorce - to legally dissolve a marriage
  • Synonym: to wed - to get married; another formal word for marry

Etymology

From Old French *marier*, from Latin *maritare* (to wed, to marry).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæri / (AmE), / ˈmæri / (BrE)
to perform a marriage ceremony, especially as an officiant
perform a marriage ceremony

Examples

The priest will marry the couple in a small chapel in the countryside.
Only licensed officials can legally marry two people in this state.
She was honored to marry her best friends in a touching ceremony.

Synset Examples

The minister married us on Saturday
We were wed the following week
The couple got spliced on Hawaii

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wed - to officiate or perform a wedding ceremony

Etymology

From Old French *marier*, from Latin *maritare* (to wed, to give in marriage).
