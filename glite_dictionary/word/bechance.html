to bechance

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtʃæns / (AmE), / bɪˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
to happen, occur, or take place, especially by chance or accident
happen or occur by chance
archaic

Examples

Should some misfortune bechance you, remain steadfast in hope.
If a rare opportunity should bechance us, we must be ready.
Such a meeting could only bechance through the most extraordinary circumstances.

Synset Examples

It happens that today is my birthday
These things befell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befall - (of something bad or luck) happen to someone

Etymology

From Middle English bechauncen, from be- (prefix) + Old French cheauncier (to happen).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈtʃæns / (AmE), / bɪˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
(archaic) To happen to someone or something; to befall.
(archaic) happen to; befall
archaic

Examples

Should any misfortune bechance you, remember this advice.
It may bechance that help arrives when least expected.
Whatever bechances in the coming days, stay true to your purpose.

Synset Examples

He promised that no harm would befall her
What has become of my children?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befall - (formal, literary) happen to someone, especially something bad

Etymology

From Middle English behappen, from be- + chance.
