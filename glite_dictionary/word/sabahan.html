Sabahan

1. ADJ

/ səˈbɑːhən / (AmE), / səˈbɑːhən / (BrE)
of or relating to Sabah, a state of Malaysia on the island of Borneo, or its people
from or relating to Sabah (Malaysia)

Examples

Many Sabahan dishes feature fresh seafood and tropical fruits.
The Sabahan rainforest is known for its incredible biodiversity.
She is proud of her Sabahan heritage and traditions.

Synset Examples

Sabahan tribesmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sabah - a state in Malaysia, located on the island of Borneo

Etymology

From Sabah, the name of the Malaysian state, plus the English adjectival suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03124283-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ səˈbɑːhən / (AmE), / səˈbɑːhən / (BrE)
a person from Sabah, a state in Malaysia
person from Sabah, Malaysia

Examples

Many Sabahans speak several languages, including Malay and English.
The Sabahan cuisine is known for its fresh seafood and unique local flavors.
She is proud of her heritage as a Sabahan and often shares stories about her hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sabah - a state in Malaysia on the island of Borneo

Etymology

From Sabah, the name of a Malaysian state, with the English suffix -an denoting origin or belonging.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09741460-n
Synset POS: n
