concealed

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsild / (AmE), / kənˈsiːld / (BrE)
kept hidden or out of sight, often intentionally or for a particular reason
hidden; kept out of sight

Examples

The detective found a concealed door behind the bookshelf.
She carried a concealed camera in her purse to document the meeting.
The building had a concealed staircase for emergencies.

Synset Examples

a concealed weapon
a concealed compartment in his briefcase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed
  • Antonym: exposed - made visible or unhidden; not concealed

Etymology

Past participle of 'conceal', from Old French 'conceler', from Latin 'concelare' (to hide).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsild / (AmE), / kənˈsiːld / (BrE)
kept out of sight; not visible or accessible to view.
not visible; hidden from view

Examples

The treasure was concealed behind a false wall in the basement.
She carried a concealed weapon for personal protection.
The entrance to the cave was concealed by thick undergrowth.

Synset Examples

concealed (or hidden) damage
in stormy weather the stars are out of sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; not visible; covered or secreted from view
  • Antonym: exposed - not concealed or hidden; open to view

Etymology

From Latin 'concelare' (to hide), from 'con-' (together) + 'celare' (to hide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02527874-s
Synset POS: s
