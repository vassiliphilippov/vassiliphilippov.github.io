Ambrosia psilostachya

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbroʊʒə saɪloʊˈstækiə / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊzɪə sɪləʊˈstækiə / (BrE)
a coarse perennial ragweed with creeping roots, native to dry, barren lands in the southwestern United States and Mexico
species of perennial ragweed

Examples

Ambrosia psilostachya is commonly found along roadsides in arid regions of the southwest.
The deep roots of Ambrosia psilostachya help it survive in dry, nutrient-poor soils.
Gardeners may try to remove Ambrosia psilostachya because of its aggressive spreading habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - a plant of the genus Ambrosia, known for its allergenic pollen

Etymology

Ambrosia from Greek 'ambrosia' (food of the gods); psilostachya from Greek 'psilos' (bare) and 'stachys' (spike).
