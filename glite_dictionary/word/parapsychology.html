parapsychology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.ə.saɪˈkɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpær.ə.saɪˈkɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The study of mental phenomena, such as telepathy or clairvoyance, that appear to contradict known physical laws and suggest the possibility of mental causation of events.
study of phenomena beyond normal psychology

Examples

He has devoted his career to the study of parapsychology and unexplained phenomena.
University courses in parapsychology often focus on topics like extrasensory perception and telekinesis.
Many scientists remain skeptical about the claims made by advocates of parapsychology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychology - the scientific study of the human mind and its functions

Etymology

From para- (beyond) + psychology, first used in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæɹəsaɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌparəsʌɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The study of mental phenomena such as telepathy, clairvoyance, and psychokinesis that are outside the scope of traditional scientific understanding.
study of paranormal mental phenomena

Examples

During her doctorate, she focused her research on parapsychology and its scientific controversies.
Many people remain skeptical about the findings of parapsychology.
He joined the laboratory to investigate claims related to parapsychology.

Synset Examples

She studied para-psychology at university.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: psychology - the scientific study of the human mind and its functions

Etymology

From para- (alongside, beyond) + psychology; formed in the early 20th century.
