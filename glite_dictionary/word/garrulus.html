Garrulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡærjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈɡarjʊlʊs / (BrE)
The type genus of the subfamily Garrulinae, comprising Old World jays, especially the Eurasian jay.
genus of Old World jays

Examples

The genus Garrulus includes the well-known Eurasian jay seen throughout Europe and Asia.
Many ornithologists study the behavior of Garrulus species to understand their complex vocalizations.
The scientific name for the Eurasian jay is Garrulus glandarius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a bird of the crow family known for its colorful plumage and noisy calls

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulus', meaning 'chattering' or 'talkative'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡærjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈɡarjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World jays in the family Corvidae, found in Europe and Asia
genus of Old World jays

Examples

The Eurasian jay belongs to the genus Garrulus, which is widespread in Europe.
Scientists have identified several species within the genus Garrulus.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a member of Garrulus in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a bird of the crow family, often brightly colored

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulus' meaning 'chattering' or 'talkative', referring to the noisy calls of these birds.
