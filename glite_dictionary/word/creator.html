Creator

1. NOUN

/ kriˈeɪtər / (AmE), / kriˈeɪtə / (BrE)
a name or title for God, particularly in Judeo-Christian beliefs, as the being who created the universe.
a name or title for God as creator

Examples

Many people pray to the Creator for guidance and strength.
The story of the universe's origin credits the Creator with bringing everything into existence.
She gave thanks to the Creator for the blessings in her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: God - the supreme being in monotheistic religions

Etymology

From Latin 'creator' (maker), from 'creare' (to create).
