leggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛɡi / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡi / (BrE)
(of a plant) having long, thin stems with few leaves, often as a result of insufficient light or overcrowding.
having long, spindly stems (of plants)

Examples

The tomato plants grew leggy because they weren't getting enough sunlight.
If a seedling becomes leggy, it may fall over under its own weight.
Gardeners try to prevent leggy growth by rotating the trays for even light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindly - long, thin, and weak in appearance (plants or limbs)

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛɡi / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡi / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having long legs in proportion to the rest of the body.
having long legs

Examples

The model was described as leggy and elegant on the runway.
That breed of dog is famous for being particularly leggy, allowing it to run swiftly.
The leggy basketball player easily reached the ball above the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-legged - having long legs
  • Antonym: short-legged - having short legs
