apprehensive

1. ADJ

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌaprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
anxious or fearful that something bad or unpleasant will happen
anxious or fearful about the future

Examples

She felt apprehensive before her job interview, worrying about what might go wrong.
Many residents are apprehensive about the new construction in their neighborhood.
Despite their apprehensive mood, the team decided to proceed with the launch.

Synset Examples

apprehensive about her job
not used to a city and worried about small things
felt apprehensive about the consequences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease
  • Antonym: confident - feeling certain and positive, not worried

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere (to seize), via French appréhensif.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
anxious or fearful that something bad or unpleasant will happen
worried or fearful about the future

Examples

She felt apprehensive about starting her new job in a different city.
The hikers were apprehensive of the approaching storm as the sky grew darker.
He tried to hide how apprehensive he was before his first public speech.

Synset Examples

apprehensive for one's life
apprehensive of danger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease about an imminent event or potential outcome
  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty, especially about the future

Etymology

From Medieval Latin apprehensivus, from Latin apprehendere (to seize, grasp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00079659-s
Synset POS: s
