germinal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɜrmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of an idea or event) providing the basis for further development; giving rise to later growth or influence.
giving rise to further development

Examples

The germinal concept of democracy eventually transformed entire societies.
Her germinal research paved the way for significant discoveries in the field.
This meeting was germinal in shaping the organization's philosophy.

Synset Examples

seminal ideas of one discipline can influence the growth of another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seminal - Strongly influencing later developments

Etymology

From Latin 'germinalis', from 'germen' meaning 'seed, sprout'.
