senile

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiː.naɪl / (AmE), / ˈsiː.naɪl / (BrE)
having or showing the weaknesses or diseases of old age, especially a loss of mental faculties
mentally or physically infirm with age

Examples

After his ninety-fifth birthday, he became noticeably more senile and forgetful.
The doctor explained that senile dementia affects memory and reasoning in elderly patients.
She worried her father's senile behavior might mean he could no longer live alone.

Synset Examples

his mother was doddering and frail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrepit - worn out or ruined because of age or neglect
  • Antonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy

Etymology

From Latin 'senilis' (of old age), from 'senex' (old man).
