Betula populifolia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtʃuːlə ˌpɑpjəˈloʊfiə / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə ˌpɒpjʊˈlɪfiə / (BrE)
A medium-sized birch tree of eastern North America, with white or pale grey bark, commonly known as the gray birch.
gray birch tree of eastern North America

Examples

The forest behind his house was filled with Betula populifolia, their pale bark brightening the landscape.
Scientists studied the resilience of Betula populifolia after wildfires in the region.
Hikers easily recognized Betula populifolia by its distinctive white bark and pointed leaves.

Etymology

From Latin: Betula (birch) + populifolia (with leaves like poplar).
