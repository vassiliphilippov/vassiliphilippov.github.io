cryoanesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪ.oʊ.æn.əsˈθiː.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌkraɪ.əʊ.æn.ɪsˈθiː.zɪə / (BrE)
loss of sensation or insensibility produced by the application of cold, often for medical purposes
insensibility produced by cold
medical

Examples

The surgeon used cryoanesthesia to numb the area before removing the wart.
Modern dermatology sometimes relies on cryoanesthesia for minor skin procedures.
Because of her sensitivity to regular anesthesia, cryoanesthesia was chosen for her treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation with or without loss of consciousness

Etymology

From Greek kryos (frost) + anesthesia (insensibility).
