Micmac

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈmɪk.mæk / (BrE)
A member of an Algonquian-speaking indigenous people native to the Maritime Provinces of Canada.
member of a Canadian indigenous people

Examples

The Micmac have a long tradition of storytelling in their culture.
A delegation of Micmac leaders attended the national conference on indigenous rights.
He is proud of his Micmac heritage and participates in community events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mi'kmaq - alternative spelling and self-designation of the Micmac people

Etymology

From Mi'kmaq, the people's own name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈmɪk.mæk / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Micmac (Mi'kmaq) people of eastern Canada and northeastern Maine.
Algonquian language of the Micmac people

Examples

She is studying the Micmac to preserve the region's indigenous heritage.
The dictionary contains hundreds of words from the Micmac language.
Very few young people speak Micmac fluently today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mi'kmaq - an alternative spelling for the people and their language

Etymology

From the Micmac (Mi'kmaq) word 'Míkm aq',' meaning 'my friends.'
