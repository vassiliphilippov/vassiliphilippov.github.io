journalese

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɜrnəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɜːnəˈliːz / (BrE)
a style of writing typically used in newspapers, characterized by short sentences, clichés, and simplified language
newspaper writing style

Examples

Many editors discourage the use of journalese in news reporting.
Her article was criticized for its reliance on clichés and tired journalese.
He tried to avoid journalese and make his stories more engaging for the readers.

Etymology

Formed from 'journal' (newspaper or magazine) and the suffix '-ese' (denoting style or language).
