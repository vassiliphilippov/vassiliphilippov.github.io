Canute the Great

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnut ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈnjuːt ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 995–1035) King of Denmark, Norway, and England, who ruled England after defeating Edmund II and uniting the kingdoms under his control.
King of Denmark, Norway, and England (c. 995–1035)

Examples

Many historians regard Canute the Great as one of the most effective rulers of early medieval Europe.
Canute the Great unified the crowns of England, Denmark, and Norway under his reign.
The legend of Canute the Great rebuking his flattering courtiers at the seashore is often retold in English history.

Etymology

From the Old Norse name Knútr, Latinized as Canutus.
