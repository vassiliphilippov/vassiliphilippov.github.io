necking

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
the narrow molding or band at the top of a column, just below the capital, in classical architecture
architectural molding at top of column

Examples

The architect insisted that the necking on each column be ornately carved.
Damaged necking can detract from the appearance of historic buildings.
Careful restoration preserved the original necking of the ancient temple.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
kissing and caressing in a romantic or sexual way, especially as foreplay without involving the genitals.
kissing and caressing as romantic foreplay
informal

Examples

We caught the young couple necking in the park after dark.
Teenagers often spend hours necking at parties when their parents aren't around.
The movie showed a romantic scene of the two lovers necking by the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petting - caressing and touching as foreplay without sexual intercourse

Etymology

From the verb 'neck,' meaning to caress or kiss amorously, originally American English slang (early 20th century).
