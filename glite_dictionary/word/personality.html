personality

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɜːrsəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the combination of qualities and characteristics that form an individual's distinctive character, including behavioral, emotional, and mental traits
the distinctive qualities of a person

Examples

Her personality is warm and outgoing, making her easy to talk to.
Despite identical appearances, the twins have very different personality traits.
He was hired for the job as much for his engaging personality as for his skills.

Synset Examples

their different reactions reflected their very different personalities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character - the mental and moral qualities distinctive to an individual

Etymology

From Latin 'personalitas', from 'persona' (mask, character).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɝːsəˈnælɪti / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
a person, especially one who is famous or notable in a particular field or area
notable or famous person

Examples

The charity event attracted several well-known personalities from television and sports.
She has become a respected media personality over the past decade.
He dreamed of being a Hollywood personality since he was a child.

Synset Examples

she is a Hollywood personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sports
  • Antonym: unknown - a person who is not famous or well known

Etymology

From Latin *personalitas*, from *persona* (mask, character).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
