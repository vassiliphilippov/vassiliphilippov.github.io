ephedra

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛdrə / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːdrə / (BrE)
A genus of jointed, nearly leafless desert shrubs of the family Ephedraceae, having reduced, scalelike leaves and reddish fleshy seeds, often found in arid regions.
desert shrub with jointed stems and scalelike leaves

Examples

The ephedra plant thrives in harsh desert environments with very little water.
Scientists studied the medicinal properties of ephedra to understand its effects on human health.
Herbal supplements containing ephedra have been subject to regulatory scrutiny.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Latin 'Ephedra' (a kind of horsetail), from Greek 'ephedra' (a kind of plant).
