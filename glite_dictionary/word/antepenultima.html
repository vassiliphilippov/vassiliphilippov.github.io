antepenultima

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.pəˈnʌl.tɪ.mə / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.pɛˈnʌl.tɪ.mə / (BrE)
the third syllable from the end of a word, when counting backward
third-to-last syllable of a word

Examples

In the word 'photograph', the antepenultima is the syllable 'pho'.
Stress in some languages often falls on the antepenultima.
Teachers may ask students to identify the antepenultima in new vocabulary words.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: penult - the second syllable from the end of a word
  • Degree Lower: ultima - the last syllable of a word

Etymology

From Latin 'antepenultima', feminine of 'antepenultimus' (before the last but one); from 'ante-' (before) + 'paene' (almost) + 'ultimus' (last).
