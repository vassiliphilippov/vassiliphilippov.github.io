unkempt

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɛmpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɛmpt / (BrE)
(of a person or their appearance) having an untidy or disheveled appearance, especially with hair not neatly combed.
untidy or disheveled in appearance

Examples

After a long day hiking, his unkempt hair stood out in all directions.
The old jacket and unkempt beard gave the man a rugged look.
She glanced in the mirror and noticed her unkempt appearance before the meeting.

Synset Examples

wild unkempt hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheveled - untidy or messy, especially of hair or appearance
  • Antonym: neat - tidy and in order, especially in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English unkempt, from un- (not) + kemben (to comb); ultimately from Old English cemban.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɛmpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɛmpt / (BrE)
(of a person or place) not properly cared for; messy or untidy in appearance.
not properly cared for; messy

Examples

His hair looked particularly unkempt after two weeks in the wilderness.
The abandoned house had an unkempt yard full of weeds and overgrown bushes.
She arrived at work in an unkempt state, clearly rushing out the door.

Synset Examples

an unkempt garden
native vistas and unkempt rambling paths
an unkempt appearance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tidy - neat and in good order
  • Antonym: neat - clean and orderly in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English unkempt, from un- (not) + kempt (combed), from Old English cemdan (to comb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02435702-s
Synset POS: s
