propenal

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpɛnæl / (AmE), / prəʊˈpɛnæl / (BrE)
A pungent, colorless, unsaturated liquid aldehyde (chemical formula C3H4O) made from propene; also known as acrolein, used in organic synthesis and as a biocide.
pungent unsaturated liquid aldehyde (acrolein)
medical

Examples

Propenal is commonly used in the synthesis of various organic compounds.
Due to its toxicity, propenal must be handled in a well-ventilated area.
Researchers studied the effects of propenal exposure on lung tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrolein - another name for propenal, used commonly in chemistry

Etymology

Formed from 'propene' and '-al' (indicating an aldehyde).
