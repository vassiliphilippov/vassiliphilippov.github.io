to muckrake

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌkˌreɪk / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌreɪk / (BrE)
to search out and publicly expose real or alleged misconduct, corruption, or scandal, especially concerning public figures.
investigate and expose misconduct or scandals

Examples

The journalist promised to muckrake until the truth about the scandal was revealed.
Several reporters muckraked abuses of power in government during that era.
He became famous for his relentless muckraking against corporate corruption.

Synset Examples

This reporter was well-known for his muckraking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry to discover and examine facts
  • Antonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a crime or mistake

Etymology

Coined in early 20th century America, from 'muckraker', itself from 'muckrake', a rake for manure, metaphorically used since John Bunyan’s Pilgrim’s Progress and popularized by Theodore Roosevelt for journalists who exposed corruption.
