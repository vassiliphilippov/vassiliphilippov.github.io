sweptback

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɛptˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈswɛptˌbæk / (BrE)
(of wings or similar structures) angled rearward from the point of attachment, especially in aircraft for aerodynamic purposes.
angled rearward (esp. aircraft wings)

Examples

The new fighter jet features sweptback wings for improved speed and maneuverability.
Engineers tested several sweptback designs before choosing the final wing configuration.
Older airplanes often had straight wings, but most modern jets use sweptback wings to reduce drag.

Synset Examples

aircraft with sweptback wings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - having no inward or backward angle; direct rather than angled back

2. ADJ

/ ˈswɛptˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈswɛptˌbæk / (BrE)
(of hair) combed or styled so that it flows backward away from the face.
hair styled flowing backward

Examples

He always kept his hair sweptback for a neat and professional appearance.
The actor's sweptback hairstyle became his signature look in the 1950s.
With her sweptback hair, she looked both elegant and confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slicked-back - hair smoothed backward, often with product
