Hygrophorus sordidus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːfəˌrəs ˈsɔːrdɪdəs / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒfərəs ˈsɔːdɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a large white cap, a dry stalk, and white gills.
an edible mushroom species

Examples

Foragers often look for Hygrophorus sordidus in moist woodland areas during the autumn.
The white cap and dry stalk are distinguishing features of Hygrophorus sordidus.
Some chefs prize Hygrophorus sordidus for its delicate flavor in soups and sautés.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hygrophorus - a genus of mushrooms characterized by waxy caps and gills

Etymology

From genus name Hygrophorus (Greek 'hygro-' meaning wet, '-phoros' meaning bearing) and Latin 'sordidus' meaning dirty or dull.
