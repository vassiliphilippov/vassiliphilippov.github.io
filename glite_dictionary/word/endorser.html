endorser

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who expresses strong approval or support for someone or something, especially in public or official contexts
person who expresses strong approval

Examples

The celebrity was a well-known endorser of the product, appearing in several commercials.
She acted as an endorser for the campaign, lending it credibility among young people.
Every successful political candidate seeks at least one influential endorser before the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a cause or individual
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds fault

Etymology

From endorse + -er, ultimately from Medieval Latin indorsare (to write on the back).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who signs a check or negotiable security to transfer ownership or legal rights to another party
person who signs to transfer rights
legal

Examples

The endorser must sign the back of the check before depositing it at the bank.
If the endorser fails to provide a valid signature, the transfer cannot be completed.
Every endorser assumes certain legal liabilities after signing a negotiable instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indorser - person who signs their name to transfer a legal document; alternate spelling
  • Antonym: endorsee - the person to whom a negotiable instrument is transferred by endorsement

Etymology

From Medieval Latin indorsare (to endorse), based on Latin dorsum (back).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
