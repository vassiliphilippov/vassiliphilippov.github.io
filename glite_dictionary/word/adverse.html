adverse

1. ADJ

/ ˈæd.vɝːs / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɜːs / (BrE)
(of a force or condition) acting or moving in an opposing or contrary direction, especially so as to hinder or oppose movement.
moving in an opposite or opposing direction

Examples

Sailing was difficult because of adverse winds.
The ship struggled against an adverse current on its way to port.
Hikers faced adverse weather conditions, with strong headwinds slowing their progress.

Synset Examples

adverse currents
a contrary wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - opposite in direction; moving against

Etymology

From Latin adversus (opposed, hostile), past participle of advertere (to turn toward).

2. ADJ

/ ædˈvɜrs / (AmE), / ˈædvɜːs / (BrE)
harmful or unfavorable; acting against one's interests or welfare.
harmful or unfavorable

Examples

The hikers continued despite the adverse weather conditions.
She overcame many adverse circumstances to achieve success.
Investors may avoid putting money into countries with adverse economic climates.

Synset Examples

adverse circumstances
made a place for themselves under the most untoward conditions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorable - giving an advantage; supportive or beneficial
  • Antonym: beneficial - resulting in good; advantageous

Etymology

From Latin 'adversus' meaning 'turned against, hostile'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01000455-s
Synset POS: s
