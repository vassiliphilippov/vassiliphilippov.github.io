Henry Tudor

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈtuːdər / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈtjuːdə / (BrE)
the first Tudor king of England (1485–1509), who ended the Wars of the Roses, defeated Richard III, and united the houses of York and Lancaster by marrying Elizabeth of York
first Tudor king of England, 1485–1509

Examples

Henry Tudor claimed the English throne after defeating Richard III at Bosworth Field.
Many historians consider Henry Tudor's reign the beginning of modern England.
Through his marriage to Elizabeth of York, Henry Tudor united rival factions and stabilized the monarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Henry VII - another name for Henry Tudor as King of England
  • Antonym: Richard III - the last Plantagenet king, defeated by Henry Tudor
