millet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlət / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
The small seed of various annual cereal grasses, especially used for food.
small seed of cereal grasses

Examples

Farmers in dry regions often grow millet as it thrives in poor soils.
The porridge was made from ground millet and served with honey.
Birdseed mixtures commonly include millet as a main ingredient.

Etymology

From French 'millet', diminutive of 'mil' (millet), from Latin 'milium' (millet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
any of various small-grained annual cereal grasses of several genera, grown chiefly for their edible seeds or as forage for livestock
a type of small-seeded cereal grass

Examples

Farmers in the region cultivate millet due to its ability to thrive in arid climates.
The bread was made from millet flour, giving it a slightly nutty flavor.
Bird seed mixes often contain millet as a main ingredient.

Etymology

From Old French 'mil', from Latin 'milium' (millet).
