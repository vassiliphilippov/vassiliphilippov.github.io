shell-shocked

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɛl ʃɑkt / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl ʃɒkt / (BrE)
in a state of deep shock or trauma, originally as a result of warfare or violent experience
deeply shocked or traumatized

Examples

After hearing about the accident, she was completely shell-shocked and could barely speak.
He looked shell-shocked when he came out of the boss's office.
Many people felt shell-shocked by the sudden changes in their lives.

Synset Examples

The news left her shell-shocked for days.
He looked shell-shocked after hearing the verdict.
After the accident, he was shell-shocked and silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traumatized - deeply affected by trauma
  • Antonym: unshaken - not disturbed or affected; calm after an event

Etymology

Originally used to describe soldiers affected by explosion shockwaves in World War I.
