to coexist

1. VERB

/ ˌkoʊ.ɪɡˈzɪst / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ɪɡˈzɪst / (BrE)
to exist at the same time or in the same place as someone or something else
exist together at the same time or place

Examples

Wild animals and humans often coexist in the same environment.
For centuries, different religions have coexisted peacefully in the city.
It is possible for tradition and innovation to coexist within a culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exist - to be real or present

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together) + 'existere' (to exist).

2. VERB

/ ˌkoʊ.ɪɡˈzɪst / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ɪɡˈzɪst / (BrE)
(of people or groups) to exist together at the same time or in the same place, especially without conflict.
exist together peacefully

Examples

For decades, the two cultures have managed to coexist in relative harmony.
Can economic growth and environmental protection coexist in our society?
The park is a place where wildlife and humans coexist peacefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tolerate - accept the existence or presence of something without interference
  • Antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one

Etymology

From Latin co- (together) + existere (to exist).
