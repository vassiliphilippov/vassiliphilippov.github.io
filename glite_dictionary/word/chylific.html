chylific

1. ADJ

/ kaɪˈlɪfɪk / (AmE), / kaɪˈlɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a tissue or organ) producing or converting into chyle.
producing or converting into chyle
medical

Examples

The chylific properties of the small intestine are essential for fat absorption.
Damage to chylific tissues can impair the formation of chyle.
Medical texts sometimes refer to the lacteals as chylific vessels.

Etymology

From Greek 'chylos' (juice) + '-ific' (making).
