to emphasise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to give special importance or attention to something; to stress or highlight a point in speech, writing, or action
give special importance or stress
british

Examples

The teacher tried to emphasise the importance of honesty during her lesson.
He used bold text to emphasise the key points in his report.
She raised her voice to emphasise that she was serious.

Synset Examples

Her gesture emphasized her words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - American spelling of 'emphasise', meaning to give special importance or attention to something
  • Synonym: underline - to mark or highlight something as important, often by drawing a line beneath it
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important

Etymology

From Greek 'emphaínein' (to indicate), via Latin 'emphasizare', influenced in form by 'emphasis'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛm.fəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (BrE)
to give special importance or attention to something; to stress
give special importance to
british

Examples

The principal wanted to emphasise the importance of honesty to the students.
She always emphasises teamwork during meetings with her staff.
In his speech, the coach emphasised the need for perseverance and hard work.

Synset Examples

Dr. Jones emphasizes exercise in addition to a change in diet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to understate - to describe or represent something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make something stand out as important
  • Synonym: stress - to give special importance or significance to something in speech or writing

Etymology

From Greek 'emphainein' (to exhibit, indicate), via Latin and French.
