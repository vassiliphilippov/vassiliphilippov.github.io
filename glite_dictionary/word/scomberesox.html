Scomberesox

1. NOUN

/ skɑːm.bəˈriː.sɑks / (AmE), / skɒm.bəˈriː.sɒks / (BrE)
a genus of slender, elongated fish in the family Scomberesocidae, commonly known as sauries
a genus of elongated fish (sauries)

Examples

The genus Scomberesox includes several species of saury found in temperate and tropical oceans.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new member of the Scomberesox genus off the Pacific coast.
Studies show that fish in the Scomberesox genus are important prey for many seabirds.

Etymology

From Greek 'skombros' (mackerel) and 'esox' (pike), reflecting morphological resemblance.
