trickiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkinəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being tricky, deceitful, or difficult to handle; the state of being clever in a way that may be dishonest or hard to anticipate.
the quality of being tricky or deceptive

Examples

The trickiness of the negotiations became clear when both sides started using clever tactics.
She underestimated the trickiness of the puzzle and spent hours trying to solve it.
His trickiness made him a challenging opponent in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'tricky' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state or quality of being tricky.
