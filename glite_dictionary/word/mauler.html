mauler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːlə / (BrE)
a fighter, especially in boxing, known for aggressively battering or attacking an opponent
aggressive fighter or boxer

Examples

The undefeated mauler quickly overwhelmed his opponent in the first round.
Fans admired her reputation as a relentless mauler in the ring.
Jack Dempsey, a legendary mauler, was feared for his powerful and aggressive fighting style.

Synset Examples

Jack Dempsey was called a mauler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slugger - a boxer or fighter who relies on powerful punches
  • Synonym: boxer - a person who takes part in boxing matches

Etymology

From 'maul' (to beat or handle roughly) plus agent suffix '-er'.

large, strong hand (esp. of a fighter)

Examples

You don't want to get on the wrong side of his powerful mauler in a fight.
