Inga laurina

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡə lɔːˈriːnə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡə lɔːˈriːnə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Central America, the West Indies, and Puerto Rico, with spikes of white flowers; commonly planted to provide shade for coffee plantations.
tropical tree used as coffee shade

Examples

Farmers often plant Inga laurina to provide natural shade for coffee crops.
The distinctive white flowers of Inga laurina attract many pollinators to the plantation.
Inga laurina thrives in tropical climates across Central America and the Caribbean.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Inga and species epithet laurina, meaning 'laurel-like'.
