penicillinase

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪləˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme produced by certain bacteria that deactivates penicillin, making the bacteria resistant to the antibiotic
enzyme that destroys penicillin
medical

Examples

The presence of penicillinase in some bacteria makes them resistant to penicillin treatment.
Researchers are developing drugs that can inhibit penicillinase activity in infections.
Testing for penicillinase production helps determine the most effective antibiotic therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-lactamase - an enzyme that breaks down beta-lactam antibiotics, including penicillins

Etymology

Formed from 'penicillin' + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
