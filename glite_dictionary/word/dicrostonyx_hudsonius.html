Dicrostonyx hudsonius

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkrɑstənɪks hʌdˈsoʊniəs / (AmE), / daɪˌkrɒstənɪks hʌdˈsəʊniəs / (BrE)
A species of lemming native to northern Canada, also known as the Hudson Bay lemming.
species of northern Canadian lemming

Examples

Dicrostonyx hudsonius inhabits the tundra regions of northern Canada.
Scientists studied the population cycles of Dicrostonyx hudsonius over several years.
The thick winter fur of Dicrostonyx hudsonius helps it survive in cold climates.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Dicrostonyx' from Greek dikroos (forked) + stonyx (prominent tooth), and 'hudsonius' referring to Hudson Bay.
