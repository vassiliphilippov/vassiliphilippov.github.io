honeycombed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌniˌkoʊmd / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪˌkəʊmd / (BrE)
having a structure that is full of small holes or cavities, resembling a honeycomb.
full of small holes or cavities

Examples

The mountain was honeycombed with caves and tunnels.
The old bread was honeycombed with air pockets, making it light and airy.
After years of erosion, the rock became honeycombed with tiny holes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perforated - having holes in it
  • Antonym: solid - having no holes or cavities

Etymology

From 'honeycomb', referring to the wax structure made by bees, plus the suffix '-ed'.
