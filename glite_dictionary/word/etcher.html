etcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛtʃə / (BrE)
a person who engraves designs, drawings, or text onto a surface using acid or other etching techniques
person who engraves using acid or etching techniques

Examples

The etcher carefully prepared her tools before starting on the copper plate.
As an accomplished etcher, he exhibited his work in several major galleries.
Many notable artists began their careers as apprentices to a skilled etcher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraver - person who engraves designs, especially on metal or wood

Etymology

From 'etch' (to engrave with acid) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
