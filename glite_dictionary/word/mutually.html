mutually

1. ADV

/ ˈmjuːtʃuəli / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves two or more people or groups equally; shared by two or more parties
in a shared or reciprocal way

Examples

Their decision was mutually beneficial and satisfied both parties involved.
The two companies agreed to end the partnership mutually.
We must find a solution that is mutually acceptable to everyone.

Synset Examples

the agreement was mutually satisfactory
the goals of the negotiators were not reciprocally exclusive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocally - in a way that involves mutual action or influence

Etymology

From mutual + -ly, with 'mutual' from Latin 'mutuus' (borrowed, lent).
