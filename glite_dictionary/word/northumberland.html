Northumberland

1. PROPN

/ nɔrˈθʌmbərlənd / (AmE), / nɔːˈθʌmbələnd / (BrE)
the northernmost county of England, known for its Roman remains, including Hadrian's Wall
county in northern England

Examples

Many tourists visit Northumberland each year to see the iconic Hadrian's Wall.
The landscape of Northumberland is famous for its rugged beauty and historic castles.
She grew up in a small village in Northumberland surrounded by rolling hills.

Etymology

From Old English 'Norþhymbre' (people north of the Humber River), with '-land' meaning territory.
