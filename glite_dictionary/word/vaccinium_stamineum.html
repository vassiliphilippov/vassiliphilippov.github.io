Vaccinium stamineum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˌstæmɪˈnʌm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm ˌstæmɪˈnʌm / (BrE)
A small, branching shrub native to the eastern United States, commonly found in marshy areas, bearing greenish or yellowish berries that are typically unpalatable to humans but eaten by deer.
a shrub of eastern US with unpalatable berries

Examples

The marshy area was thick with Vaccinium stamineum, its berries attracting grazing deer.
Botanists surveyed the growth of Vaccinium stamineum to understand its role in the wetland ecosystem.
Though humans rarely eat its fruit, Vaccinium stamineum is an important food source for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deerberry - Common name for Vaccinium stamineum
  • Degree Higher: blueberry - Edible berry of various species in the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

From Latin: Vaccinium (the genus name for blueberries and related plants) and stamineum (referring to the prominent stamens in the flowers).
