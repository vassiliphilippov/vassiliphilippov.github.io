Myrtillocactus geometrizans

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˌtɪloʊˈkæktəs dʒiˌɑːmɪtrɪˈzænz / (AmE), / mɜːˌtɪlɪəʊˈkaktəs dʒiˌɒmɪtrɪˈzænz / (BrE)
A species of tree-like cactus native to western Mexico, valued for its small oblong edible berrylike fruit.
tree-like Mexican cactus with edible fruit

Examples

The Myrtillocactus geometrizans is often grown ornamentally for its striking geometric appearance.
In western Mexico, the fruit of the Myrtillocactus geometrizans is sometimes eaten fresh or used in local dishes.
Botanists admire the tall, branching form of Myrtillocactus geometrizans in arid landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'myrtillus' (little myrtle) due to the fruit's resemblance to myrtle berries, and Greek 'geometrizans,' meaning 'geometric,' referring to the plant's angular stems.
