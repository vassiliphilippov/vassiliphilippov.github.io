biological

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.əˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
related by blood; connected through genetic relationship rather than by adoption or marriage.
genetically related (of parent or child)

Examples

He met his biological mother for the first time as an adult.
The court awarded custody to the biological father.
Although raised by adoptive parents, he wanted to know his biological roots.

Synset Examples

biological child
natural parent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adoptive - not biologically related but obtained by legal adoption

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -logical (from logos, study).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01408930-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
connected with biology or with living things; relating to the science of life and living organisms.
relating to biology or living things

Examples

The patient participated in a biological study focused on cell regeneration.
She is fascinated by the biological processes that sustain life on Earth.
Researchers are exploring the biological basis of mental health conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biologic - another form meaning relating to biology
  • Antonym: chemical - relating to chemistry (sometimes contrasted with biological)

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -logical, from logos (study, reason).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02676508-a
Synset POS: a
