disorienting

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔriˌɛntɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːri.əntɪŋ / (BrE)
Making someone confused or unable to think clearly or find their way.
causing confusion or loss of direction

Examples

The constant noise and flashing lights were extremely disorienting for new visitors.
She found the maze disorienting and struggled to keep track of her path.
Waking up in a strange hotel room can feel quite disorienting at first.

Synset Examples

making so many turns to the right and then the left was completely disorienting
a sharp blow to the head can be disorienting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusing - causing bewilderment or lack of clarity

Etymology

From 'disorient' (to cause to lose sense of direction), based on 'orient' (to place or align).
