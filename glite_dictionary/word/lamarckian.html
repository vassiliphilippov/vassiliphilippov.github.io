Lamarckian

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɑrkiən / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːkiən / (BrE)
A person who believes in Lamarckism, the theory that characteristics acquired during an organism's life can be inherited by offspring.
believer in Lamarckism

Examples

Her grandfather was a noted Lamarckian who often argued for the inheritance of acquired traits.
Few scientists today consider themselves Lamarckians, as modern genetics supports other mechanisms of evolution.
The debate between the Lamarckian and Darwinian schools was fierce in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Darwinian - a believer in Darwinism, the theory of natural selection

Etymology

From the name of French naturalist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck (1744–1829) + -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ləˈmɑrkiən / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːkiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Lamarckism, the theory that characteristics acquired or lost during an organism's lifetime can be inherited by its offspring.
relating to Lamarckism

Examples

Some early nineteenth century scientists promoted Lamarckian ideas about evolution.
The debate between Lamarckian and Darwinian evolution persists in academic circles.
He gave a lecture on Lamarckian inheritance and its historical significance.

Synset Examples

Lamarckian theories

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Darwinian - relating to Charles Darwin's theories of evolution by natural selection

Etymology

From the name of French biologist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck (1744–1829) + -ian.
