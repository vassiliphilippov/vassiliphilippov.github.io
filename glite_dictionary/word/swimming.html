swimming

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of eyes) filled with or about to overflow with tears.
(of eyes) brimming with tears

Examples

Her swimming eyes betrayed her overwhelming sadness at the news.
He turned away, his swimming eyes unable to hide his emotion.
The heartfelt speech left everyone's eyes swimming with tears.

Synset Examples

swimming eyes
sorrow made the eyes of many grow liquid

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or sport of moving through water by using the limbs
the act of moving through water

Examples

She has always enjoyed swimming as a way to relax after work.
Competitive swimming requires both strength and technique.
The children took up swimming lessons every Tuesday evening.

Synset Examples

it was the swimming they enjoyed most
they took a short swim in the pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swim - the act of swimming (noun form)

Etymology

From Old English 'swimman' (to move in water), with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun.
