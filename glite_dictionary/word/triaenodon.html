Triaenodon

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈænəˌdɑn / (AmE), / traɪˈænədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of requiem sharks in the family Carcharhinidae, known especially for the species Triaenodon obesus (the whitetip reef shark).
genus of requiem sharks

Examples

The Triaenodon genus is well-known among marine biologists for its unique dental structure.
In coral reef ecosystems, Triaenodon species play an important predatory role.
A documentary featured the elusive whitetip reef shark, classified under the genus Triaenodon.

Etymology

From Greek 'triaina' (trident, spear) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the shape of the teeth.

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈænəˌdɑn / (AmE), / traɪˈænədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of requiem sharks in the family Carcharhinidae, known for species such as the whitetip reef shark.
a genus of sharks

Examples

The Triaenodon genus includes the well-known whitetip reef shark found in tropical oceans.
Researchers studied a specimen of Triaenodon during their expedition to the Maldives.
Many divers hope to spot a member of the Triaenodon genus on coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large, predatory fish of the order Selachimorpha

Etymology

From Greek 'triaine' (trident) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the tooth shape.
