djinni

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪni / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪni / (BrE)
A supernatural spirit in Islamic mythology, believed to be invisible and capable of influencing people, sometimes appearing as humans or animals.
supernatural spirit in Islamic mythology

Examples

According to legend, a powerful djinni guarded the ancient treasure in the desert.
Stories of the djinni have fascinated people in many cultures for centuries.
She believed a djinni was responsible for the mysterious events in her village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genie - a supernatural being, often granting wishes in folklore
  • Synonym: jinn - a supernatural being in Islamic mythology; plural of djinni

Etymology

From Arabic 'jinnī', referring to a spirit or supernatural being.
