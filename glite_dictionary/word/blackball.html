blackball

1. VERB

/ ˈblækˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to exclude or expel someone from a group, community, or organization, often by secret vote
expel from a group or community

Examples

After his actions were revealed, the club decided to blackball him from membership.
Several members voted to blackball the controversial candidate.
She feared they might blackball her if she spoke out against the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ostracize - to deliberately exclude someone from a group or society
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to join a group or organization

Etymology

From the practice of using a black ball as a negative vote in secret ballots in clubs and societies, 18th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈblækˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to vote against or exclude someone from a group, club, or organization, often by secret ballot
exclude or vote against someone

Examples

Several members tried to blackball her application to the club, but she was eventually accepted.
He was unfairly blackballed from joining the organization after the incident.
The committee is known to blackball candidates for even minor infractions.

Synset Examples

The President vetoed the bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to prevent someone from joining or taking part
  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to admit or receive

Etymology

From the practice of using black balls to signify a negative vote in secret ballots, 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɔːl / (BrE)
an act of excluding someone from a group, organization, or activity by casting a negative vote or veto, especially in secret ballot voting
exclusion by negative vote or veto

Examples

The club threatened to blackball anyone who broke the rules.
He feared a blackball at the committee meeting would end his candidacy.
After multiple complaints, the board decided on a blackball against the member.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veto - the power or act of rejecting a decision or proposal
  • Antonym: approval - consent or agreement given for something

Etymology

Refers to the historical practice of using a black ball to cast a negative vote in secret ballots.

Synset POS: n
