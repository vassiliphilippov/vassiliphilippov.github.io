to communise

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
to make something Communist in character or to bring into accord with Communist principles
make or become Communist

Examples

The new regime sought to communise the country's industries and agriculture.
After the revolution, many institutions were rapidly communised to reflect the new ideology.
There were heated debates on whether it was wise to communise private property.

Synset Examples

communize the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communize - to make something Communist

Etymology

From French 'communiser', from 'communisme' (communism).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410206-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.mjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.juː.naɪz / (BrE)
to make (property or resources) owned collectively or by the state, especially by implementing communist principles
make property state-owned

Examples

The revolutionaries promised to communise all farmland after seizing power.
Following the coup, the government moved quickly to communise industries and resources across the country.
Debate erupted when the council suggested plans to communise the nation's utilities.

Synset Examples

The new government communized all banks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - to transfer (a business or property) from private to state ownership
  • Antonym: to privatize - to transfer from public or government control or ownership to private enterprise

Etymology

From French 'communiser', from 'commun' (common)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00409777-v
Synset POS: v
