no-count

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
of little or no value; worthless or insignificant, especially referring to a person or thing
worthless; of no value, esp. of a person
informal american

Examples

He was always considered a no-count troublemaker by the neighbors.
She refused to date any more no-count guys who never kept their promises.
The old truck turned out to be a no-count piece of junk that never ran.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - having value or worth

Etymology

Originally American English; derived from 'no' (none) and 'count' (to be of importance or worth).
