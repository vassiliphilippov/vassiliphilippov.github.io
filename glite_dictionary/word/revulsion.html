revulsion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of disgust or loathing
strong feeling of disgust

Examples

She felt a wave of revulsion at the sight of the spoiled food.
His revulsion was obvious when he heard the unethical proposal.
There was a sense of revulsion among the crowd after the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval
  • Antonym: attraction - a feeling that makes someone interested or drawn to something

Etymology

From Latin revulsio (a pulling away), from revellere (to pull away).
