dyskinesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskɪˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˌdɪskɪˈniːziə / (BrE)
Abnormal or impaired voluntary movement, often resulting in involuntary or erratic muscle activity.
abnormal voluntary muscle movement
medical

Examples

The patient developed dyskinesia as a side effect of the medication used to treat Parkinson's disease.
After several months on the new drug, involuntary movements and dyskinesia became more pronounced.
Chronic dyskinesia can significantly affect quality of life for individuals with neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: akinesia - loss or impairment of the power of voluntary movement
  • Degree Lower: bradykinesia - slowness of movement
  • Degree Higher: hyperkinesia - excessive or uncontrolled movement

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + kinesis (movement).
