Synaptomys

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈnæptəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / sɪˈnaptəmaɪz / (BrE)
A genus of small North American rodents known as bog lemmings, found in wetland habitats.
genus of bog lemmings (rodents)

Examples

The bog lemmings of the genus Synaptomys are found primarily in wetlands across North America.
Researchers studied the population decline of Synaptomys in eastern Canada.
Habitat loss threatens several species within the Synaptomys genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'synaptein' (to join) + 'mys' (mouse).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæptəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / sɪˈnaptəˌmʌɪz / (BrE)
a genus of small North American rodents commonly known as bog lemmings, found in wet, grassy areas
a genus of bog lemmings (rodents)

Examples

The Synaptomys is recognized for its preference for boggy habitats across North America.
Researchers observed that the population of Synaptomys declined due to habitat loss.
Unlike other rodents, Synaptomys species are well-adapted to wet, grassy environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small, short-tailed rodent of the Arctic tundra and neighboring areas

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'synaptein' (to join) and 'mys' (mouse).
