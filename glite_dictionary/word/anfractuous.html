anfractuous

1. ADJ

/ ænˈfræk.tʃu.əs / (AmE), / ænˈfræk.tʃu.əs / (BrE)
(of a path or structure) full of twists and turns; winding or intricate.
full of twists and turns

Examples

The path through the anfractuous canyons challenged even the most experienced hikers.
She traced the anfractuous patterns of veins on the leaf with her finger.
The coastline’s anfractuous cliffs made navigation by boat particularly hazardous.

Synset Examples

anfractuous cliffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winding - having a lot of bends or curves
  • Antonym: straight - having no bends or curves

Etymology

From Latin anfractuosus, from anfractus (a bending, winding).
