dangle-berry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæŋ.ɡəlˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈdaŋ.ɡ(ə)lˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A huckleberry of the eastern United States with pink flowers and sweet blue fruit, commonly found in woodlands and bogs.
North American huckleberry with blue fruit

Examples

The woods were filled with ripe dangle-berries that attracted birds and hikers alike.
She used dangle-berries from the bog to make a delicious homemade jam.
Botanists documented the habitat preferences of the dangle-berry in the eastern states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - a North American shrub producing edible berries
