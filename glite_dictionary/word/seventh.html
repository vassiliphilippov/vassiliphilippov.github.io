seventh

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛv.ənθ / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ənθ / (BrE)
Each of seven equal parts into which something is divided; one part out of seven.
one of seven equal parts

Examples

The prize money was divided into sevenths among the winners.
She ate a seventh of the chocolate bar.
A seventh of the total population voted in the election.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sixth - one part in six equal parts
  • Degree Higher: eighth - one part in eight equal parts

Etymology

Formed from 'seven' with the ordinal suffix '-th'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvənθ / (AmE), / ˈsɛvənθ / (BrE)
the person or thing in position number seven in a series; one of seven equal parts
person or thing in position seven; one of seven parts

Examples

He finished in seventh place in the race, just ahead of his teammate.
This is the seventh in a series of ten lectures on medieval art.
Each of us received a seventh of the profits after the project was completed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sixth - the person or thing in position number six in a series
  • Degree Higher: eighth - the person or thing in position number eight in a series

Etymology

From Old English *seofotha*, related to 'seven' with the ordinal suffix '-th'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɛvənθ / (AmE), / ˈsɛvənθ / (BrE)
Coming next after the sixth and just before the eighth in position; being number seven in a sequence.
number seven in a sequence

Examples

She finished in seventh place out of ten runners.
This is the seventh time he has called today.
My birthday is on the seventh of August.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sixth - coming next after the fifth and just before the seventh
  • Degree Higher: eighth - coming next after the seventh and just before the ninth

Etymology

From Old English *seofotha*, related to 'seven' plus the ordinal suffix '-th'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɛv.ənθ / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ənθ / (BrE)
the musical interval between a note and the note seven scale degrees above or below it, often spanning seven consecutive note letters
interval of seven scale degrees in music

Examples

The melody leaps up by a seventh at the climax of the song.
He struggled to sing the descending seventh in the final chorus.
A dominant seventh chord creates tension before resolving to the tonic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: octave - the musical interval spanning eight scale degrees
  • Degree Lower: sixth - the musical interval spanning six scale degrees
