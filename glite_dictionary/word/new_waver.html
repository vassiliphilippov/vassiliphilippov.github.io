New Waver

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈweɪvər / (AmE), / njuː ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
a filmmaker who adopts the styles or philosophies characteristic of the New Wave movement in cinema
filmmaker following New Wave movement

Examples

The director was renowned as a New Waver, always experimenting with narrative techniques.
Many film students admire the work of New Wavers because of their bold approach to storytelling.
Her reputation as a New Waver solidified after her debut film challenged cinematic conventions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Wave - an innovative movement in film, especially French cinema of the late 1950s and 1960s

Etymology

From 'New Wave', translating the French 'Nouvelle Vague', a cinema movement originating in 1950s France, plus '-er' denoting an adherent.
