Menopon

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈpɑn / (AmE), / ˌmiːnəˈpɒn / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic lice commonly found on chickens and other birds.
a genus of chicken lice

Examples

The veterinarian identified the infestation as caused by Menopon species.
Proper hygiene in poultry farms can help prevent Menopon from spreading among chickens.
Microscopic examination revealed several Menopon lice on the bird's feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small, wingless parasitic insect
  • Synonym: poultry louse - a louse that infests poultry such as chickens

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name introduced by French zoologist Louis Pierre Auguste Chevrolat in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnəˌpɒn / (AmE), / ˈmɛnəpɒn / (BrE)
A genus of lice that are parasitic on birds, especially chickens.
a genus of bird lice, especially on chickens

Examples

Menopon infestation can cause irritation and reduced egg production in chickens.
Veterinarians often check for Menopon when examining backyard poultry.
Proper cleaning of chicken coops helps prevent outbreaks of Menopon among birds.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Menopon, used in zoological taxonomy.
