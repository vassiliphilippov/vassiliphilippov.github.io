satinet

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætɪˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌsatɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
a fabric resembling satin in finish but made partly or entirely from cotton or synthetic fiber, rather than silk.
cotton-based fabric with satin-like finish

Examples

The jacket was tailored from a durable satinet that looked elegant yet practical.
Early American workwear often used satinet because it was both sturdy and affordable.
He admired the soft sheen of the satinet lining in his new coat.

Etymology

From French 'satinette', diminutive of 'satin'.
