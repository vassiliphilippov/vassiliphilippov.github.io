Bombyx mori

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑm.bɪks ˈmɔːr.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɒm.bɪks ˈmɔː.rɪ / (BrE)
a species of domesticated Asian moth, the larva of which is the silkworm and the primary producer of commercial silk
domesticated silkworm moth species

Examples

Bombyx mori has been domesticated for thousands of years for silk production.
The life cycle of Bombyx mori includes egg, larva (silkworm), pupa, and adult moth.
Researchers use Bombyx mori as a model organism in genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - the larval stage of Bombyx mori, producer of silk

Etymology

From Latin Bombyx (silkworm) and mori (of the mulberry tree).
