Vanessa virginiensis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɛsə vərˌdʒɪniˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / vəˈnesə vɜːˌdʒɪniˈensɪs / (BrE)
A North American butterfly species with dark brown wings marked by white and golden-orange spots, commonly known as the American painted lady.
American painted lady butterfly

Examples

We spotted a beautiful Vanessa virginiensis resting on a wildflower during our hike.
The wings of Vanessa virginiensis have striking orange and white patterns.
Butterfly enthusiasts often photograph Vanessa virginiensis in gardens across North America.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vanessa' is a genus name, and 'virginiensis' means 'of Virginia'.
