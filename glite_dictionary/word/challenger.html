challenger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃælɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person or team that competes against you, especially one who is aiming to defeat a champion or titleholder
person competing to defeat another

Examples

The reigning champion faced a tough challenger in the final match.
Many fans were eager to see how the new challenger would perform against the veteran team.
Despite being the challenger, she remained confident and focused throughout the competition.

Synset Examples

he had respect for his rivals
he wanted to know what the competition was doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opponent - a person who competes against or fights another
  • Antonym: defender - the existing holder of a championship or title being challenged

Etymology

From the Old French 'challengier', meaning 'to accuse, dispute', from Latin 'calumniari' (to accuse falsely).
