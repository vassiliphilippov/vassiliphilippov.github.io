Sayda

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdə / (BrE)
an ancient Phoenician city, known as the principal city of ancient Phoenicia, located on the Mediterranean coast in present-day Lebanon; also known as Sidon.
ancient Phoenician city (now Sidon, Lebanon)

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered new artifacts during excavations in Sayda.
The city of Sayda was a prominent center of trade in the ancient world.
Modern Sidon evolved from the ancient city known as Sayda in Phoenician times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sidon - alternative name for Sayda, ancient Phoenician city

Etymology

From Arabic صيدا (Ṣaydā), which referred to the ancient city of Sidon.
