Zurvanism

1. NOUN

/ zɜrˈvɑːnɪzəm / (AmE), / zɜːˈvɑːnɪzəm / (BrE)
A Zoroastrian sect that teaches Zurvan as the ultimate source of the universe.
sect of Zoroastrianism emphasizing Zurvan as the source

Examples

Zurvanism emerged as a branch of Zoroastrianism during the Sassanian era.
Scholars debate the influence of Zurvanism on later religious traditions.
The main tenet of Zurvanism is that time, or Zurvan, is the origin of all things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoroastrianism - ancient Persian religion centered on the teachings of Zoroaster

Etymology

From the Middle Persian 'Zurvān', the name of the deity Zurvan, plus '-ism' denoting a belief system.

2. NOUN

/ zɜːrˈvɑːnɪzəm / (AmE), / zɜːˈvɑːnɪzəm / (BrE)
a heretical Zoroastrian doctrine that teaches Zurvan is the ultimate source of the universe and that both Ahura Mazda and Ahriman are his offspring
heretical Zoroastrian doctrine on the origin of the universe

Examples

The ancient religion of Zurvanism proposed that time was the fundamental principle of the world.
Scholars debate the influence of Zurvanism on later religious and philosophical movements.
Some texts refer to Zurvanism as a distinct branch of Zoroastrianism during certain periods of Iranian history.

Etymology

From Zurvan (a deity in Zoroastrianism) + -ism (doctrine or belief).
