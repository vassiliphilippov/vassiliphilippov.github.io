angiotensin

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒioʊˈtɛnsɪn / (AmE), / ˌandʒiəʊˈtɛnsɪn / (BrE)
a peptide hormone that causes blood vessels to narrow, increasing blood pressure
hormone that narrows blood vessels
medical

Examples

The release of angiotensin plays a vital role in regulating blood pressure.
Doctors may prescribe drugs that block angiotensin to treat hypertension.
Researchers study how angiotensin affects kidney function in patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasoconstrictor - a substance that causes blood vessels to narrow

Etymology

From Latin 'angio-' (vessel) + 'tensin' (from tension, relating to pressure).
