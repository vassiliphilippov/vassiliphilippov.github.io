chitchat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (BrE)
casual and light informal conversation about unimportant topics, typically to be sociable
casual informal conversation

Examples

We enjoyed some friendly chitchat during our coffee break.
Her email was mostly chitchat with nothing important to report.
Before the meeting started, they exchanged a bit of chitchat about their weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banter - playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks
  • Synonym: conversation - talk, especially informal exchange of news and ideas
  • Antonym: discussion - the act of talking about something seriously

Etymology

Reduplication of 'chat', influenced by 'chit'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (BrE)
to engage in light, informal conversation about unimportant topics
talk casually about unimportant things

Examples

We sat and chitchatted over coffee for almost an hour.
After the meeting, the colleagues chitchatted about their weekend plans.
They enjoy chitchatting in the break room during lunch.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about other people's private lives
  • Degree Higher: to discuss - to talk about something in detail

Etymology

Reduplication of 'chat', likely imitative in origin, from late 19th century.
