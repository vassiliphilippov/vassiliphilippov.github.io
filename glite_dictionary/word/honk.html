honk

1. VERB

/ hɑŋk / (AmE), / hɒŋk / (BrE)
(of a goose or similar bird) to make a loud, harsh cry like a goose.
make a goose-like cry

Examples

We could hear the geese honk loudly as they flew overhead.
Every morning, a flock of geese honk outside my window.
The children laughed as the goose honked at them.

Synset Examples

The geese were honking

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ hɑŋk / (AmE), / hɒŋk / (BrE)
a loud, harsh sound, especially the cry of a goose or a sound resembling it
loud, harsh sound, like a goose's cry

Examples

We could hear the distant honk of geese flying overhead.
The traffic jam was filled with the loud honk of car horns.
Every morning, a honk outside the window signaled the arrival of the delivery truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toot - a short, sharp sound, especially made by a horn or whistle

3. VERB

/ hɑŋk / (AmE), / hɒŋk / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh sound like that of a horn, especially a car horn; to sound a horn
make a loud, harsh sound like a horn

Examples

The impatient driver honked his horn several times in the traffic jam.
Geese often honk loudly as they migrate across the sky.
Please don’t honk outside the hospital late at night.

Synset Examples

The horns of the taxis blared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blare - to make a loud, harsh sound, especially loudly announced or played
  • Synonym: beep - to make a short, high-pitched sound (often of a horn or electronic device)

4. VERB

/ hɑŋk / (AmE), / hɒŋk / (BrE)
to make the horn of a car produce its loud warning sound
sound a car horn

Examples

Please don't honk your horn late at night; people are sleeping.
The driver honked as he turned the corner to warn pedestrians.
She honked several times when the car in front wouldn't move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beep - to sound the horn of a vehicle with a short, high-pitched note

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by the horn or by geese.
