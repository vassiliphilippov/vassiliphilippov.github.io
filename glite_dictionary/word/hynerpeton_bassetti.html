Hynerpeton bassetti

1. PROPN

/ haɪnɚˈpɛtɑn bəˈsɛti / (AmE), / haɪnəˈpɛtən bəˈsɛti / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early tetrapod amphibians from the Devonian period, having well-developed forelimbs, known from fossils found in Pennsylvania
extinct Devonian amphibian genus

Examples

The fossil remains of Hynerpeton bassetti provided insights into the evolution of early tetrapods.
Scientists discovered Hynerpeton bassetti in Devonian rocks in Pennsylvania, suggesting the region was once a lush habitat.
Researchers believe that Hynerpeton bassetti had well-developed limbs suited for movement both in water and on land.

Etymology

From Hyner, Pennsylvania (fossil site) + Greek 'herpeton' (creeping animal); species named after Dr. John Basset.
