arteria infraorbitalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriə ˌɪnfrəɔːrˈbaɪtəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌɪnfrəɔːbɪˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
the artery branching from the maxillary artery that supplies blood to the region beneath the eye, from the lower eyelid to the upper lip
artery supplying area below the eye
medical

Examples

During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the arteria infraorbitalis beneath the eye.
The arteria infraorbitalis provides vascular supply to the upper cheek and lower eyelid.
Blockage of the arteria infraorbitalis can result in numbness of the upper lip and cheek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infraorbital artery - artery supplying blood to the area below the orbit
  • Degree Higher: maxillary artery - major artery of the face from which the arteria infraorbitalis branches

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) and 'infraorbitalis' (below the orbit).
