lineman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnmæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnmən / (BrE)
A player in American football who is positioned on the line of scrimmage and is responsible for blocking or defending, depending on their role.
American football player on line of scrimmage
american

Examples

The defensive lineman sacked the quarterback during the third quarter.
As a lineman, he needs both speed and strength to overpower the opposition.
Every lineman practiced new blocking techniques before the championship game.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnmən / (BrE)
a player in American football who lines up on the line of scrimmage, typically as an offensive or defensive lineman
football player on the line of scrimmage

Examples

The offensive linemen worked together to protect their quarterback during every play.
Defensive linemen must be quick and strong to break through the opposing team's blockers.
The coach praised the linemen for their outstanding performance in yesterday's game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - player who catches passes in football
  • Antonym: quarterback - player who directs the offense

Etymology

From 'line' + 'man', denoting a player positioned on the line of scrimmage.
