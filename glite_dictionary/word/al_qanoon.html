Al Qanoon

1. PROPN

/ æl kɑˈnuːn / (AmE), / æl kɑˈnuːn / (BrE)
A terrorist organization formed in Pakistan in 2002 as a coalition of extremist militant groups including Lashkar-e-Taiba, Lashkar-e-Jhangvi, Jaish-e-Muhammad, and elements of al-Qaeda.
Pakistani extremist militant coalition group (2002-)

Examples

The government announced new sanctions on Al Qanoon and its members.
International intelligence agencies have monitored Al Qanoon due to its links with multiple terrorist groups.
Al Qanoon was involved in numerous attacks across South Asia in the early 2000s.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-qānūn', meaning 'the law'; used here as a proper name for the organization.
