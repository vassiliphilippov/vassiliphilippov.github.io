canned

1. ADJ

/ kænd / (AmE), / kænd / (BrE)
(of food or drink) preserved and sealed in a can or jar for long-term storage.
preserved in a can or jar

Examples

Many people keep a supply of canned soup in their pantry for emergencies.
The recipe calls for fresh tomatoes, but canned ones can be used as a substitute.
He prefers the taste of canned peaches over the fresh ones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - (of food) not preserved, recently made or obtained; not canned, frozen, or otherwise preserved

2. ADJ

/ kænd / (AmE), / kænd / (BrE)
(of music, laughter, or other sound) recorded in advance for later use or broadcast, rather than being live.
pre-recorded for broadcast

Examples

The sitcom added canned laughter to make the jokes seem funnier.
Many television commercials use canned music rather than hiring musicians.
Some viewers find canned applause distracting during game shows.

Synset Examples

a transcribed announcement
canned laughter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live - (of sound or broadcast) not prerecorded; occurring in real time

Etymology

Derived from the metaphor of something being preserved or packaged in a can, suggesting it is pre-prepared.
