liquidator

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪtə / (BrE)
a person, often appointed by a court, who winds up the affairs of a company by liquidating its assets and settling its debts
person who winds up a company's affairs
legal

Examples

The court appointed a liquidator to oversee the closing of the bankrupt company.
As a liquidator, she was responsible for selling off all remaining assets to pay creditors.
Creditors met with the liquidator to discuss the distribution of recovered funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receiver - a person appointed to manage the property or finances of a company or individual during insolvency

Etymology

From 'liquidate' (from Latin liquidare, meaning to melt, make clear) + '-or', denoting a person who does an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪtər / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who kills another, especially in a premeditated or systematic manner, often for political or criminal reasons
person who kills, often systematically

Examples

The regime sent a notorious liquidator to eliminate political opponents abroad.
He was known as the chief liquidator of the criminal syndicate's enemies.
The investigation uncovered evidence linking the liquidator to several high-profile assassinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin - a person who murders an important person for political or religious reasons
  • Synonym: killer - a person who kills someone, especially deliberately
  • Synonym: murderer - a person who has committed murder
  • Antonym: victim - a person who is killed or harmed by another

Etymology

From liquidate + -or, denoting an agent; related to liquidate in the sense of eliminating someone.
