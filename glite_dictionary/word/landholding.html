landholding

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌhoʊl.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌhəʊl.dɪŋ / (BrE)
An area of land owned, leased, or held, especially for farming or real estate purposes.
piece of land owned or held

Examples

The family's largest landholding was sold to a real estate developer.
She inherited a small landholding from her grandfather in the countryside.
Changes in government policy greatly affected the value of his landholding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate - an area of land owned by one person, especially in the country

Etymology

land + holding; 'holding' from Middle English, meaning property or estate held.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the ownership or possession of land, especially as an asset or basis of social status
ownership or possession of land

Examples

The law was designed to regulate landholding across the region to prevent monopolies.
After inheriting the family estate, she expanded her landholding by buying nearby farmland.
Changes in landholding patterns have led to significant social upheavals in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: property - something owned; a possession, especially land or buildings
  • Synonym: tenancy - the possession or occupancy of land, buildings, or other property as a tenant

Etymology

From 'land' + 'holding', referring to something that is held or owned.
