calumniously

1. ADV

/ kəˈlʌmnɪəsli / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmnɪəsli / (BrE)
In a false, slanderous, or defamatory manner; with the intent to harm someone's reputation by making untrue statements.
in a slanderous or defamatory manner

Examples

The politician was calumniously accused of bribery in an attempt to ruin his career.
She argued that the article had calumniously misrepresented her actions.
The defendant denied ever speaking calumniously about her former employer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanderously - in a manner that defames someone with false statements
  • Degree Lower: maliciously - with the intention of doing harm

Etymology

From Latin calumnia (false accusation) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
