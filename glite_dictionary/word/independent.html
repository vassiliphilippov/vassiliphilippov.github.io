independent

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
a writer or artist who works for different employers without a long-term contract; a freelancer
a freelance writer or artist

Examples

After years of working at magazines, she became an independent and now submits articles to various publications.
Many artists choose to be independents so they can have more creative freedom.
As an independent, he decides which projects to take on and when to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freelancer - a person working for different employers without long-term commitment

Etymology

From Latin 'independens' (not depending on something else).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10130190-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of a clause) capable of standing alone as a complete simple sentence, not dependent on another clause.
(of a clause) able to stand alone as a sentence

Examples

An independent clause expresses a complete thought and can function as a sentence on its own.
The teacher asked the students to identify the independent clause in each complex sentence.
If you remove the subordinate clause, the independent clause still makes sense by itself.

Synset Examples

the main (or independent) clause in a complex sentence has at least a subject and a verb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dependent - (of a clause) unable to stand alone as a sentence; subordinate

Etymology

From Latin 'independens', meaning 'not hanging from something else.'

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
not controlled or influenced by a particular party, group, or interest; free from outside authority or influence.
not controlled by a party or group

Examples

She prefers to vote for an independent candidate rather than one affiliated with a major party.
The commission is designed to be independent from political influence.
As an independent journalist, he avoids working for large media corporations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomous - self-governing; acting independently
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on or controlled by something else

Etymology

From Latin 'independens,' from 'in-' (not) + 'dependere' (to hang from, depend).
