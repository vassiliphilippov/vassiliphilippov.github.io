Eruca

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈruːkə / (AmE), / ɪˈruːkə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs in the family Brassicaceae native to the Mediterranean, including the commonly cultivated arugula (rocket).
genus of Mediterranean herbs (arugula)

Examples

Eruca is widely cultivated for its peppery leaves, commonly known as arugula or rocket.
Botanists classify arugula under the genus Eruca in the family Brassicaceae.
Several wild species of Eruca grow naturally in Mediterranean countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - an edible Mediterranean plant, also called rocket
  • Synonym: rocket - common British name for Eruca sativa

Etymology

From Latin *eruca* (a kind of cabbage or colewort).
