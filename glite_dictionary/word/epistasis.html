epistasis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪstəsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪstəsɪs / (BrE)
the interaction between genes in which the effect of one gene is suppressed or modified by another unrelated gene
suppression of a gene by another gene
medical

Examples

The researchers found evidence of epistasis between the two genes involved in disease resistance.
Understanding epistasis is essential for predicting complex genetic traits in organisms.
Certain mutations can have no visible effect because of epistasis with other genes.

Etymology

From Greek 'epistasis' meaning 'a stopping' or 'hindrance', from 'epi-' (upon) and 'stasis' (a standing still).
