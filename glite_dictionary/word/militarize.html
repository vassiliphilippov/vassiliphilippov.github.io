to militarize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip or adapt (a country, organization, or area) for military use or give a military character to
to make military in character or use

Examples

The government decided to militarize the border area in response to rising tensions.
Some critics argue that police forces are becoming increasingly militarized in their tactics and equipment.
The regime attempted to militarize young people through mandatory military training programs.

Synset Examples

militarize the Civil Service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarize - to remove military forces or character from

Etymology

From French 'militariser', from 'militaire' (military).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip or prepare (a country, organization, or area) with military forces or give it a military character
make military or equip with armed forces

Examples

After years of peace, the government decided to militarize the disputed region.
The new regime began to militarize its police force, equipping them with military vehicles.
Some analysts believe attempts to militarize space could lead to a new arms race.

Synset Examples

militarize Germany again after the war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarize - to remove military forces or character from an area
  • Synonym: arm - to supply with weapons

Etymology

From French 'militariser', from 'militaire' (military).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01101280-v
Synset POS: v
