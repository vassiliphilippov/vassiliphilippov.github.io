pdl

1. NOUN

/ piː diː ɛl / (AmE), / piː diː ɛl / (BrE)
a unit of force in the foot–pound–second (FPS) system, equal to the force needed to accelerate a mass of one pound at a rate of one foot per second squared; approximately 0.1382 newtons
unit of force in FPS system

Examples

The calculated force was 200 pdl according to the FPS system.
A force of one pdl will accelerate a pound mass at one foot per second squared.
Engineers sometimes encounter pdl in older technical literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newton - SI unit of force

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'poundal', itself formed from 'pound' and '-al', following the pattern of 'newton'.
