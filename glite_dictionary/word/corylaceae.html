Corylaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˈleɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈleɪsi.i / (BrE)
A former family of flowering plants comprising hazels and related trees and shrubs, now usually included in the family Betulaceae.
former plant family including hazels

Examples

The Corylaceae were once considered a distinct family but are now classified within Betulaceae.
Many species in the Corylaceae are important for their nuts and timber.
Botanists often debate the classification and limits of the Corylaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Betulaceae - the birch family of trees and shrubs, now including Corylaceae
  • Synonym: Corylus - genus of hazels formerly placed in Corylaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Corylaceae, based on genus name Corylus, from Latin corylus (hazel).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɹɪˈleɪsi.i / (AmE), / kɒɹɪˈleɪsi.i / (BrE)
a former subfamily of flowering plants, including the genus Corylus (hazels), used in some classification systems.
plant subfamily including hazels

Examples

The hazel tree belongs to the subfamily Corylaceae in some classification systems.
Botanists used the term Corylaceae to refer to a group including hazelnuts and hornbeams.
Recent taxonomic revisions have replaced Corylaceae with the subfamily Coryloideae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Corylus - genus of deciduous trees and shrubs including hazels

Etymology

From New Latin Corylaceae, from Corylus (hazel) + -aceae (family suffix).
