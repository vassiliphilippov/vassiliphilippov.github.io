cityscape

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtiˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪtiˌskeɪp / (BrE)
a view, visual appearance, or representation of a city or urban area, especially as seen from a distance or in art
view or depiction of a city

Examples

The photographer captured the cityscape at sunset, with skyscrapers silhouetted against the sky.
The new development dramatically altered the historic cityscape.
Artists often find inspiration in the vibrant colors and patterns of the urban cityscape.

Synset Examples

the dominant character of the cityscape is its poverty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - a view or depiction of an expanse of scenery in nature

Etymology

Formed from 'city' + 'scape', similar to 'landscape', first appeared in English in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtiˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪskeɪp / (BrE)
a painting or other artistic representation depicting a city or an urban area
painting or image of a city

Examples

The gallery is hosting an exhibition of cityscape paintings by local artists.
She is best known for her vibrant cityscape watercolors, which capture the energy of downtown life.
This cityscape shows the skyline at dusk, with all the buildings glowing in the evening light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - a painting representing natural scenery

Etymology

Formed from 'city' + '-scape', modeled after 'landscape'.
