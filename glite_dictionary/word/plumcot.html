plumcot

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈplʌmˌkɒt / (BrE)
A fruit that is a hybrid between a plum (Prunus domestica) and an apricot (Prunus armeniaca).
hybrid fruit: plum and apricot

Examples

The farmer grew delicious plumcots that were sweeter than regular plums.
Have you ever tasted a plumcot? It's a delightful mix of plum and apricot flavors.
At the market, I bought some ripe plumcots for a summer fruit salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluot - a hybrid fruit closely related to the plumcot, primarily plum with some apricot

Etymology

Blend of 'plum' and 'apricot'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈplʌmˌkɒt / (BrE)
A fruit that is a hybrid between a plum and an apricot.
hybrid fruit of plum and apricot

Examples

The farmer offered us a fresh plumcot that tasted both sweet and tangy.
She used plumcots to make a unique summer pie for the festival.
Have you ever tried a plumcot, or do you prefer traditional fruits?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluot - a hybrid fruit of plum and apricot with more plum characteristics
  • Synonym: aprium - a hybrid fruit of apricot and plum with more apricot characteristics

Etymology

Blend of 'plum' and 'apricot', first used in the early 20th century.
