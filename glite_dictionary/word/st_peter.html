St. Peter

1. PROPN

/ seɪn ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / sənt ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a disciple of Jesus Christ, leader of the Apostles, regarded in Catholic tradition as the first Pope and the vicar of Christ on earth
disciple of Jesus and regarded as the first Pope

Examples

St. Peter is traditionally considered the first Pope of the Catholic Church.
According to the Gospels, Jesus gave the keys of heaven to St. Peter.
Many churches around the world are dedicated to St. Peter and bear his name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Simon Peter - another name for St. Peter, disciple of Jesus
  • Synonym: pope - the Bishop of Rome and leader of the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Petrus', from Greek 'Petros' (meaning 'rock', given by Jesus to Simon).
