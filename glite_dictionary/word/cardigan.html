Cardigan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪɡən / (BrE)
A breed of Welsh corgi that is slightly bowlegged, has rounded ears, and a long tail.
a breed of Welsh corgi dog

Examples

The Cardigan is known for its long tail and sturdy frame.
Unlike the Pembroke corgi, the Cardigan has rounded ears and is slightly bowlegged.
Many dog lovers appreciate the affectionate nature of the Cardigan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Pembroke - A breed of Welsh corgi with pointed ears and a short tail

Etymology

Named after the Cardiganshire region in Wales, where the breed originated.
