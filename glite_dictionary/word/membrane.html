membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a thin, flexible layer of tissue that covers, lines, or connects organs or cells in animals and plants
thin layer of tissue in animals or plants

Examples

The cell is surrounded by a membrane that controls what enters and exits.
Doctors repaired the torn membrane during surgery.
Certain chemicals can pass through the membrane, while others cannot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a thin layer covering a surface
  • Synonym: tissue - a group of similar cells that perform a function

Etymology

From Latin membrāna (a skin, parchment), from membrum (limb, member).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a thin, flexible sheet of material that acts as a barrier or lining, especially one found naturally or used in technology
thin flexible sheet or barrier

Examples

The scientist examined the membrane under a microscope to study its properties.
A waterproof membrane was installed beneath the tiles to prevent leaks.
The drum's sound changes depending on how tightly the membrane is stretched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a thin layer or covering of material
  • Synonym: sheet - a broad, thin piece of material

Etymology

From Latin membrāna (parchment, thin skin).
