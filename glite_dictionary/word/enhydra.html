Enhydra

1. PROPN

/ ɛnˈhaɪdrə / (AmE), / ɛnˈhɪdrə / (BrE)
A genus of sea otters, including the common sea otter found along the northern Pacific coasts.
genus of sea otters

Examples

The genus Enhydra is best known for the species commonly called the sea otter.
Researchers studied the behavioral patterns of Enhydra in their natural habitat.
Enhydra populations have faced threats due to hunting and environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea otter - a large marine otter native to the North Pacific, member of the genus Enhydra

Etymology

From Greek 'enhydra', meaning 'in water', from 'en' (in) + 'hydor' (water).
