dragoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡəmæn / (AmE), / ˈdræɡəmən / (BrE)
an interpreter and official guide, especially in countries of the Near East, formerly employed to assist diplomats, travelers, and merchants, particularly in the Ottoman Empire.
interpreter and guide in the Near East

Examples

During the diplomatic mission, a trusted dragoman facilitated the conversation between the ambassador and local officials.
Most dragomans in the Ottoman Empire were highly educated and fluent in several languages.
The British consul relied on his dragoman to negotiate terms with the regional rulers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreter - a person who translates spoken words between languages

Etymology

From Italian dragomanno, via Middle Greek dragoumanos, from Arabic tarjumān (interpreter).
