solon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.lɒn / (BrE)
a wise and respected lawmaker or statesman, especially one regarded as a distinguished leader in national or international affairs
distinguished lawmaker or statesman

Examples

The new senator quickly became known as a solon among his peers for his wisdom and fairness.
Historians often refer to Abraham Lincoln as a solon of American democracy.
At the summit, every solon in attendance was consulted before the final agreement was reached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statesman - an experienced, respected political leader
  • Synonym: politician - a person involved in politics, especially as a holder of or a candidate for an elected office
  • Antonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to desires and prejudices rather than rational argument

Etymology

From Solon, the name of a famous Athenian lawgiver in ancient Greece.
