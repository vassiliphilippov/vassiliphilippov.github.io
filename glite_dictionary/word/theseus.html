Theseus

1. PROPN

/ ˈθiː.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈθiː.si.əs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a legendary hero and king of Athens, celebrated for his many adventures and for slaying the Minotaur.
Greek hero and king of Athens

Examples

Theseus is best known for navigating the Labyrinth and killing the Minotaur.
The legend of Theseus has inspired countless works of literature and art.
As the founder-king, Theseus united the scattered communities of Attica into the city of Athens.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θησεύς (Thēseús).
