prodromal

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈdroʊməl / (AmE), / prəʊˈdrəʊməl / (BrE)
(of a symptom or phase) indicating the onset or early stage of a disease or attack.
indicating early stage of a disease
medical

Examples

The doctor recognized the prodromal symptoms of measles before the rash appeared.
Some patients report prodromal headaches before the onset of a migraine.
Early intervention during the prodromal phase can improve treatment outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: acute - of a disease, having a rapid onset
  • Degree Higher: chronic - of a disease, persisting for a long time

Etymology

From Greek prodromos (precursor, forerunner), from pro- (before) + dromos (running, course).
