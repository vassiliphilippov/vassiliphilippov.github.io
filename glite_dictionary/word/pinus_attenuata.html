Pinus attenuata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs ˌætəˈnjuːətə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs ˌætəˈnjuːətə / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States, characterized by clusters of three needles and cones with a distinctive knob on each scale
a Pacific coastal pine species with knobby cone scales

Examples

Foresters surveyed the forest to monitor the health of the Pinus attenuata population.
The cones of the Pinus attenuata are unique for their prominent knobs on each scale.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect stands of Pinus attenuata along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knobcone pine - common name for Pinus attenuata

Etymology

From Latin: 'pinus' meaning 'pine' and 'attenuata' meaning 'narrowed' or 'tapered'.
