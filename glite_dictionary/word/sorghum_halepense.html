Sorghum halepense

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɔrɡəm həˈlɛpɛnsi / (AmE), / ˌsɔːɡəm həˈlɛpɛnsiː / (BrE)
a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region, often grown as fodder but considered a problematic weed in many regions, especially in southern United States
tall grass species, often a weed

Examples

Sorghum halepense is notorious for invading crop fields and reducing yields.
The farmer struggled to control the spread of Sorghum halepense in his pasture.
Many southern states regard Sorghum halepense as an invasive weed that is difficult to eradicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Johnson grass - common name for Sorghum halepense, a perennial grass species

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sorghum' is the genus name derived from Italian 'sorgo,' and 'halepense' refers to Aleppo in Syria, denoting geographic origin.
