atmospherics

1. NOUN

/ ˌætməsˈfɛrɪks / (AmE), / ˌatməsˈfɛərɪks / (BrE)
crackling or hissing noises in radio or telephone receivers, caused by electrical interference in the atmosphere
electrical noise interference

Examples

The radio was filled with atmospherics that made the broadcast difficult to understand.
During the storm, atmospherics interfered with the pilot's communication system.
Old shortwave radios often picked up atmospherics during electrical storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: static - crackling noise caused by interference in radio or telephone signals

Etymology

From 'atmosphere' + '-ics', referring to atmospheric electrical disturbances.
