James Prescott Joule

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈprɛskət dʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈprɛskət dʒuːl / (BrE)
(1818–1889) English physicist who established the mechanical theory of heat and discovered the first law of thermodynamics.
English physicist; pioneer of thermodynamics

Examples

The work of James Prescott Joule was fundamental in the development of thermodynamics.
Many physics students learn about James Prescott Joule and his experiments with heat and energy.
The SI unit of energy, the joule, is named after James Prescott Joule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joule - unit of energy named after James Prescott Joule
