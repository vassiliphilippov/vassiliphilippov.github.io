unenviable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɛnviəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛnviəbl / (BrE)
(of a situation, task, or position) difficult, unpleasant, or not desirable, often causing pain or embarrassment.
difficult or undesirable (situation)

Examples

She found herself in the unenviable position of having to fire several employees.
Reporters have an unenviable task when covering disasters.
He inherited the unenviable responsibility of making budget cuts.

Synset Examples

awkward (or embarrassing or difficult) moments in the discussion
an awkward pause followed his remark
a sticky question
in the unenviable position of resorting to an act he had planned to save for the climax of the campaign

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enviable - desirable or worthy of envy
  • Synonym: undesirable - not wanted or desirable

Etymology

From un- (not) + enviable, meaning not desirable or attractive.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɛnviəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛnviəbəl / (BrE)
not desirable or attractive; unpleasant or undesirable and unlikely to be envied by others
not desirable or enviable

Examples

She found herself in the unenviable position of having to choose between her job and her friend.
The team has the unenviable task of raising funds after multiple failures.
He gained unenviable notoriety for his role in the scandal.

Synset Examples

unenviable notoriety

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desirable - wanted or wished for because it is attractive or useful

Etymology

From un- (not) + enviable, itself from envy + -able.
