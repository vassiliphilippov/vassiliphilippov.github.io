Cervus unicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvəs ˌjuːnɪˈkɔlər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs ˌjuːnɪˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
A large deer species native to southern and southeast Asia, characterized by antlers with three tines; also known as the sambar.
species of Asian deer (sambar)

Examples

The Cervus unicolor is commonly found in the forests of India and Sri Lanka.
Wildlife researchers tracked a group of Cervus unicolor during their spring migration.
The impressive antlers of the Cervus unicolor make it distinctive among Asian deer species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sambar - common name for Cervus unicolor, large Asian deer

Etymology

From Latin; 'cervus' meaning 'deer' and 'unicolor' meaning 'of one color'.
