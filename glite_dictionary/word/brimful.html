brimful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪmfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪmfʊl / (BrE)
Filled to the brim; full to the point of overflowing.
full to the brim

Examples

The glass was brimful with fresh orange juice.
Her heart was brimful of excitement as she opened the letter.
Rain had left the riverbanks brimful after the storm.

Synset Examples

a brimful cup
I am brimful of chowder
a child brimming over with curiosity
eyes brimming with tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brimming - full to the brim
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled

Etymology

From Middle English brimful, literally 'brim' + 'full'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪmfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪmfʊl / (BrE)
full to the brim; filled to capacity, often almost overflowing
filled to the brim; almost overflowing

Examples

The glass was brimful of sparkling water and ice.
Her heart was brimful of excitement on the first day of school.
The basket was brimful with freshly picked apples.

Synset Examples

a brimful cup
I am brimful of chowder
a child brimming over with curiosity
eyes brimming with tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - filled to capacity, containing as much as possible
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied

Etymology

From 'brim' (the upper edge) + 'full', originally attested in Middle English.
