Microdipodops

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊdɪˈpɑːdɑps / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəʊdɪˈpɒdɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as kangaroo mice, native to arid regions of western North America.
genus of kangaroo mice rodents

Examples

The genus Microdipodops includes several species adapted to desert life.
Researchers found a new population of Microdipodops in the Nevada sand dunes.
Unlike other rodents, Microdipodops can survive on very little water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo mouse - small rodent of North American deserts, belonging to the genus Microdipodops

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'dipous' (two-footed) + 'ops' (appearance).

The rare Microdipodops is specially adapted to survive in desert conditions.

Related Terms
