Auriparus flaviceps

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɪˈpɛərəs ˈfleɪvɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈpɛːrəs ˈflavɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
A very small yellow-headed titmouse found in western North America, commonly known as the verdin.
yellow-headed titmouse species of western North America

Examples

The Auriparus flaviceps is often seen flitting through desert shrubs in search of insects.
A birdwatcher was excited to spot an Auriparus flaviceps in Arizona last spring.
Because of its distinctive yellow head, the Auriparus flaviceps is easy to identify among titmice.

Etymology

From Latin: 'aurum' (gold) + 'parus' (titmouse) and 'flaviceps' (yellow-headed).
