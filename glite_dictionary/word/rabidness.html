rabidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈræbɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A state of extreme or unrestrained excitement, enthusiasm, or passion.
extreme excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

The rabidness of the crowd at the concert made it an unforgettable experience.
His rabidness for the sport inspired everyone around him to work harder.
The critics remarked on the rabidness of her devotion to the cause.

Synset Examples

poetry is a sort of divine madness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervor - intense and passionate feeling
  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being calm and free from agitation

Etymology

Formed from 'rabid' + '-ness', modeled on Latin 'rabidus' (raving, mad).
