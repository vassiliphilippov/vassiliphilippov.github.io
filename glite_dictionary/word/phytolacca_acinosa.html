Phytolacca acinosa

1. NOUN

/ faɪtoʊˈlækə əˈsɪnoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊˈlækə æˈsɪnəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of pokeweed native to southeastern Asia and China, known for its fleshy roots and clusters of dark berries.
species of pokeweed from Asia

Examples

Phytolacca acinosa is commonly grown in gardens for its ornamental berries.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of Phytolacca acinosa in traditional Asian medicine.
The berries of Phytolacca acinosa are attractive but should not be ingested without proper preparation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pokeweed - North American plant with similar appearance and properties

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Phytolacca' from Greek 'phyton' (plant) + 'lacca' (lac, resin); species 'acinosa' from Latin 'acinus' (grape), referring to the shape of the fruit clusters.
