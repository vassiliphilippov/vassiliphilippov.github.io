Heterostraci

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtərəˈstræsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈstræsiː / (BrE)
an extinct group of armored jawless fishes from the Paleozoic era, with unclear taxonomic placement
extinct armored jawless fish group

Examples

Scientists have discovered well-preserved fossils of Heterostraci in ancient sedimentary rocks.
Heterostraci are important for studying the early evolution of vertebrate armor.
The classification of Heterostraci remains a subject of debate among paleontologists.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + ostrakon (shell), referring to their varied armor plates.
