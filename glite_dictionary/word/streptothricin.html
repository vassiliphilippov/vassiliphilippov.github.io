streptothricin

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛp.toʊˈθraɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / stɹɛp.təʊˈθrɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
a basic antibiotic substance derived from a soil actinomycete, used in medical and scientific contexts
antibiotic from soil actinomycete
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that streptothricin can inhibit the growth of certain bacteria in laboratory tests.
The soil sample yielded an actinomycete that produced streptothricin as a secondary metabolite.
Despite its promising antibacterial activity, streptothricin is rarely used due to its toxicity in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms

Etymology

From International Scientific Vocabulary: 'strepto-' (twisted) + 'thrix' (hair) + '-in' (chemical suffix).
