crotonbug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊtənˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊtənˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
A small, light-brown cockroach, originally brought to the United States from Europe, commonly found in households as a pest.
a small household cockroach pest

Examples

We found a crotonbug in the kitchen cupboard last night.
The building was infested with crotonbugs, especially near the plumbing.
She called the exterminator after spotting several crotonbugs in her apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a beetle-like insect found in homes worldwide
  • Synonym: German cockroach - a species of small, light brown cockroach – Blattella germanica

Etymology

Probably from Croton (a water supply system in New York City) + bug, as these cockroaches were first noticed in buildings supplied by Croton water.
