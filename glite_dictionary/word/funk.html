funk

1. NOUN

/ fʌŋk / (AmE), / fʌŋk / (BrE)
A style of music that blends jazz, blues, and soul, characterized by a strong rhythmic groove and heavy bass line, often emphasizing the first beat of each bar.
rhythmic music style blending jazz, blues, and soul

Examples

The band's new album mixes traditional jazz with funk to create a modern sound.
Her love of funk music is reflected in her energetic dance moves.
They played a set full of classic funk tunes from the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul - a style of African-American popular music combining elements of gospel and rhythm and blues

Etymology

African American English, probably originally from 'funky' meaning strong, earthy, or soulful.

2. NOUN

/ fʌŋk / (AmE), / fʌŋk / (BrE)
A state of nervousness or depression, especially one that affects mood or motivation.
state of nervous depression

Examples

After losing his job, he fell into a deep funk for several weeks.
She tried listening to music to pull herself out of her funk.
Don't let a temporary funk stop you from enjoying life.

Synset Examples

he was in a funk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - a state of severe low mood; mental illness marked by sadness and loss of interest
  • Synonym: despondency - a state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - a positive, happy emotional state
