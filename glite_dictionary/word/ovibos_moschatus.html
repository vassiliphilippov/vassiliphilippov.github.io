Ovibos moschatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɪˌboʊs moʊˈskɑːtəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvɪˌbɒs mɒsˈkɑːtəs / (BrE)
A large, shaggy-coated bovid mammal native to the Arctic regions of Canada and Greenland, intermediate in size and anatomy between an ox and a sheep; also known as the musk ox.
large Arctic mammal; musk ox

Examples

The Ovibos moschatus is well adapted to surviving harsh Arctic winters.
Scientists observed a herd of Ovibos moschatus grazing on the tundra.
Ovibos moschatus populations have stabilized due to conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musk ox - the common name for Ovibos moschatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'ovibos' meaning 'sheep ox', and 'moschatus' meaning 'musk-scented'.
