deterrence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːəns / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛr.əns / (BrE)
the act of discouraging someone from taking an action through fear of the possible consequences, especially by way of warning or threat
the act of discouraging by warning

Examples

The government issued a strong warning as a form of deterrence against illegal dumping.
Visible security cameras serve as a deterrence to potential thieves.
The threat of heavy fines provides deterrence for companies that might pollute the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevention - the action of stopping something from happening
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'deterre' (to frighten from, discourage), via French 'détérance'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɜɹəns / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛɹəns / (BrE)
The act or process of discouraging someone from taking an action, especially by instilling fear of the potential consequences.
the act of discouraging action through fear of consequences

Examples

Many countries believe that nuclear weapons serve as a form of deterrence against potential attacks.
The strict laws are intended to act as a deterrence to would-be criminals.
Visible cameras function as a deterrence to theft in stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevention - the act of stopping something from happening or arising
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something

Etymology

From Latin deterrēre, from de- (away) + terrēre (to frighten).

3. NOUN

/ dəˈtɜɹəns / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛrəns / (BrE)
The act of discouraging someone from taking an action or preventing an event by instilling fear, doubt, or a sense of danger.
discouraging action by fear or doubt

Examples

The harsh penalties serve as a form of deterrence to would-be criminals.
Many believe that nuclear weapons act as a powerful deterrence against war.
Effective deterrence requires both credible threats and reliable enforcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevention - the act of stopping something from happening
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'deterrere' (to frighten from), from 'de-' (away) + 'terrere' (to frighten).
