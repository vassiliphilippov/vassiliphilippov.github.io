Spartium

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrtiəm / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, notably including the Spanish broom (Spartium junceum).
genus of flowering plants; Spanish broom

Examples

Spartium is commonly grown as an ornamental plant in Mediterranean gardens.
The vibrant yellow flowers of Spartium add a splash of color to dry landscapes.
Botanists often refer to Spanish broom as Spartium junceum.

Etymology

From Latin 'spartium' meaning 'broom' (the plant), originally from Greek 'spártion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrʃiəm / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the pea family Fabaceae, containing only one species, the Spanish broom
genus of plant containing Spanish broom

Examples

Spartium is native to the Mediterranean region and is known for its fragrant yellow flowers.
The only species in the genus Spartium is Spanish broom, widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
Botanists classify Spanish broom under the genus Spartium due to its unique characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish broom - a tall shrub with yellow flowers, the only member of genus Spartium

Etymology

From Latin 'spartium' (broom), from Greek 'spártion' (rope, broom).
