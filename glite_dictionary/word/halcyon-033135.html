halcyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhælsiən / (AmE), / ˈhælsiən / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored kingfisher found in warm regions of the Old World, especially in Asia and Africa
large Old World kingfisher bird

Examples

The vibrant blue feathers of the halcyon flashed as it darted across the riverbank.
Many birdwatchers travel to the tropics hoping to spot a rare halcyon in its natural habitat.
The halcyon perched quietly on a branch, scanning the water below for fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird that typically has a long bill and dives to catch fish

Etymology

From Latin 'halcyon', from Ancient Greek 'alkyṓn', referring to a mythical bird associated with calm seas.

4. NOUN

/ ˈhæl.si.ən / (AmE), / ˈhalsɪən / (BrE)
A mythical bird said to breed during the winter solstice in a floating nest and believed to have the power to calm the winds and waves.
mythical bird calming storms

Examples

According to legend, the halcyon could calm the sea so she could nest on the waves undisturbed.
Poets often use the halcyon as a symbol of peace and tranquility in literature.
During ancient times, sailors prayed for the protection of the halcyon before venturing out in winter.

Etymology

From Latin 'halcyon', from Greek 'alkyōn', referring to a legendary bird, identified with the kingfisher.
