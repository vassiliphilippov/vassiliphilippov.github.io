to concoct

1. VERB

/ kənˈkɑkt / (AmE), / kənˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to invent or devise a plan, idea, or story, often by combining various elements
devise or invent (a plan, idea, etc.)

Examples

She managed to concoct an excuse to avoid the meeting.
The team worked late into the night to concoct a strategy for success.
He loves to concoct elaborate stories to entertain his friends.

Synset Examples

He thought up a plan to get rich quickly
no-one had ever thought of such a clever piece of software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: to invent - to create or design something that has not existed before

Etymology

From Latin concocere, from com- (together) + coquere (to cook).

2. VERB

/ kənˈkɑkt / (AmE), / kənˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to prepare something, especially food or drink, by mixing different ingredients together
prepare by mixing ingredients

Examples

She managed to concoct a delicious meal from leftover vegetables and spices.
The bartender will concoct a special cocktail for the event.
They concocted a soup using whatever ingredients they had in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

concoct a strange mixture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine two or more substances
  • Synonym: blend - to mix different substances together to form a single substance

Etymology

From Latin concocere (to cook together), from con- (together) + coquere (to cook).
