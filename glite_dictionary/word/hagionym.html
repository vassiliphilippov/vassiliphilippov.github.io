hagionym

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪɡiəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪɪəʊˌnɪm / (BrE)
The name of a saint used as a proper noun, especially in place names or other naming contexts.
name of a saint used as a proper noun

Examples

The town was named after the hagionym of Saint Peter.
In medieval Europe, it was common to use a hagionym for new churches and settlements.
The linguist wrote an article about the influence of hagionyms in European toponymy.

Synset Examples

This Native place name was used for a while in parallel with the hagionym Saint-Francois-Xavier (1850s) as well as another ‘official’ toponym, Grantown, named after Cuthbert Grant.

Etymology

From Greek hagios (holy) + -onym (name).
