Raskolnikov

1. PROPN

/ ræˈskoʊlnɪˌkɑf / (AmE), / rasˈkɒlnɪˌkɒf / (BrE)
a fictional character in Dostoevsky's novel ‘Crime and Punishment’, who murders for philosophical reasons, believing himself above moral law
fictional murderer in ‘Crime and Punishment’

Examples

Many readers find Raskolnikov's internal struggle with guilt both gripping and profound.
The name Raskolnikov has become synonymous with tortured intellectuals in literature.
In his dreams, Raskolnikov confronts the moral consequences of his actions.

Etymology

From Russian Раскольников (Raskol'nikov), the character's surname in Dostoevsky's novel.
