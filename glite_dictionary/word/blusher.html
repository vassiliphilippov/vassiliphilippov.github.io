blusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃə / (BrE)
a pink or red cosmetic powder or cream applied to the cheeks to add color or emphasize the cheekbones
cosmetic for coloring cheeks

Examples

She swept a rosy blusher across her cheeks before leaving the house.
The makeup artist recommended a cream blusher for a more natural look.
I chose a peach-toned blusher to complement my skin tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rouge - powder or cream cosmetic for coloring the cheeks

Etymology

From 'blush' (to redden), referring to the color effect the product gives.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃə / (BrE)
a yellowish edible mushroom (agaric) that typically turns red when touched, commonly found in woodland areas
edible mushroom that reddens when touched

Examples

We found several blushers growing beneath the oak trees in the autumn woods.
The distinctive feature of the blusher is its red staining when handled or cut.
Before consuming a wild mushroom, be sure it is a genuine blusher and not a similar toxic species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agaric - a type of mushroom with gills under the cap
