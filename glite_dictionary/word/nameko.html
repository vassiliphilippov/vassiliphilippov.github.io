nameko

1. NOUN

/ nɑːˈmeɪkoʊ / (AmE), / nəˈmɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a small, amber-brown mushroom (Pholiota nameko), widely cultivated and used in Japanese cuisine
a type of Japanese cultivated mushroom

Examples

Miso soup with nameko mushrooms is a popular dish in Japan.
The market sells fresh nameko for use in various stir-fries and soups.
She added nameko to the hotpot for extra flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiitake - a type of edible mushroom commonly used in Asian cuisine

Etymology

From Japanese ナメコ (nameko), referring to the species Pholiota nameko.
