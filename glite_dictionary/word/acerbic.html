acerbic

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɜrbɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɜːbɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) sharply critical, biting, or harsh in tone.
sharply critical or biting in tone

Examples

Her acerbic comments during the meeting offended several colleagues.
The critic's acerbic review left the director visibly upset.
He was known for his acerbic wit, often making biting jokes at others' expense.

Synset Examples

an acerbic tone piercing otherwise flowery prose
a barrage of acid comments
her acrid remarks make her many enemies
bitter words
blistering criticism
caustic jokes about political assassination, talk-show hosts and medical ethics
a sulfurous denunciation
a vitriolic critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - sarcastic in a scathing and bitter way
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or harsh in tone

Etymology

From Latin *acerbus* (sour, harsh).

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɝː.bɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɜː.bɪk / (BrE)
Having a sour or bitter taste.
having a sour or bitter taste

Examples

The grapefruit had an acerbic flavor that woke up my senses.
Some herbal teas can taste surprisingly acerbic when brewed too long.
The chef used lemon juice to add an acerbic note to the dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tart - having a sharp or acid taste
  • Antonym: sweet - having the pleasant taste characteristic of sugar or honey

Etymology

From Latin 'acerbus' (sour, harsh, bitter).
