Mafia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːfiə / (AmE), / ˈmæfiə / (BrE)
A secret criminal organization operating in the United States, typically structured in 'families,' believed to have links to the original Sicilian Mafia.
organized crime syndicate in the U.S.

Examples

He was arrested for his alleged ties to the Mafia.
Several films depict the rise and fall of Mafia families in New York.
Witnesses feared retaliation from the Mafia if they testified in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - organized crime group; US slang for the Mafia

Etymology

From Italian 'mafia,' referring to the Sicilian criminal society.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
A secret criminal organization originating in Sicily, known for engaging in organized crime and maintaining its own code of conduct.
Sicilian criminal organization

Examples

The police launched an investigation into the local Mafia after a series of robberies.
Many films have portrayed the violent world of the Mafia in Sicily.
The Mafia maintained strict codes of silence among its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a criminal organization; especially one involved in illegal activities
  • Synonym: Cosa Nostra - another name for the Sicilian Mafia

Etymology

From Italian *mafia*, of uncertain origin, possibly from Sicilian slang meaning 'boldness', 'bravado', or 'protection'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08262470-n
Synset POS: n
