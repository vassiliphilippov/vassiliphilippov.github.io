eggplant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɛɡˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A vegetable with a smooth, dark purple (occasionally white or yellow), shiny skin and a soft, spongy flesh, used in cooking.
vegetable with shiny purple skin

Examples

She sliced the eggplant and roasted it with olive oil and garlic.
The recipe calls for one large eggplant, diced into cubes.
He grew tomatoes and eggplants in his backyard garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aubergine - British term for the same vegetable as eggplant

Etymology

From egg + plant, originally referring to white, egg-shaped varieties resembling eggs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɛɡˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A hairy, upright plant native to Southeast Asia, widely cultivated for its large, glossy, edible fruit, commonly used as a vegetable.
plant cultivated for its edible fruit

Examples

The farmer grew rows of eggplant alongside tomatoes and peppers in his garden.
She made a delicious curry using chopped eggplant and spices for dinner.
If you roast eggplant, its flesh becomes soft and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aubergine - the same plant, chiefly British term

Etymology

From 'egg' + 'plant', referring to some early varieties with egg-shaped white fruit.
