gasolene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsəliːn / (AmE), / ˈɡasəliːn / (BrE)
a volatile flammable liquid derived from petroleum, used chiefly as fuel for internal-combustion engines
liquid fuel for engines

Examples

The old car wouldn't start because it was out of gasolene.
Many rural areas still use generators powered by gasolene.
He accidentally spilled gasolene while filling the mower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasoline - volatile liquid fuel for engines (standard spelling)

Etymology

From 'gasoline', variant influenced by chemical naming patterns in English.
