Castoridae

1. NOUN

/ kæˈstɔrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kaˈstɒrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, semiaquatic rodents comprising the beavers.
the beaver family (rodents)

Examples

The family Castoridae includes all species of beavers.
Fossils of Castoridae can be found in North America and Eurasia.
Biologists study Castoridae to understand the evolution of aquatic mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - a large, semiaquatic rodent known for building dams

Etymology

From modern Latin Castoridae, based on Castor (the type genus) and the suffix -idae used for animal families.
