turboprop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.boʊˌprɑːp / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bəʊˌprɒp / (BrE)
an aircraft that is powered by a gas turbine engine which drives a propeller
aircraft with a turbine-driven propeller

Examples

The turboprop climbed swiftly after takeoff, its engines producing a distinctive whir.
Many regional airlines operate turboprop aircraft due to their efficiency on short routes.
The company ordered several new turboprops to update its aging fleet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jet - an aircraft powered by jet engines
  • Antonym: propeller plane - an aircraft powered by a conventional (non-turbine) propeller engine

Etymology

Formed from 'turbo-' (from turbine) and 'prop' (short for propeller), first used in the mid-20th century.
