radiophoto

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdi.oʊˈfoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdi.əʊˈfəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A photograph that is transmitted electronically using radio waves.
a photograph sent by radio waves

Examples

The journalist received a radiophoto of the disaster site within minutes of the event.
During World War II, radiophotos were often sent to deliver urgent images to newspapers overseas.
Although obsolete today, the radiophoto was once a breakthrough in news reporting technology.

Etymology

Formed from 'radio' (from Latin 'radius', ray, beam) and 'photo' (from Greek 'phōt-', light).
