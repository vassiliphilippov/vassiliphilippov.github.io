sustained

1. ADJ

/ səˈsteɪnd / (AmE), / səˈsteɪnd / (BrE)
continuing for an extended period without interruption or weakening
maintained continuously over time

Examples

The company experienced sustained growth throughout the year despite market challenges.
After hours of sustained effort, she finally completed her thesis.
The pilot managed to achieve sustained flight with the new aircraft design.

Synset Examples

sustained flight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous - going on without stopping
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

2. ADJ

/ səˈsteɪnd / (AmE), / səˈsteɪnd / (BrE)
(of an electric arc or process) continuing for a prolonged period without interruption.
continuous; uninterrupted (technical)

Examples

The engineer monitored the sustained arc to ensure stable operation.
A sustained electric arc can cause excessive wear on electrodes.
The device is designed to handle sustained currents without overheating.

Synset Examples

heat transfer to the anode in free burning arcs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intermittent - not continuous or steady; occurring at irregular intervals
  • Synonym: continuous - without interruption or break; ongoing

Etymology

From sustain + -ed; 'sustain' from Old French *sustenir*, from Latin *sustinere* (to hold up, support).
