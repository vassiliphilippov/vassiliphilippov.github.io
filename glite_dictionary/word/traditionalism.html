traditionalism

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃ(ə)nəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃ(ə)nəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice of following or upholding tradition, especially in cultural or religious matters
adherence to tradition

Examples

The community's emphasis on traditionalism has preserved its customs for generations.
Her traditionalism was evident in her resistance to modern wedding practices.
Critics argue that excessive traditionalism can hinder social progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatism - preference for maintaining existing traditions and institutions
  • Antonym: progressivism - support for social reform or new ideas; opposition to maintaining traditions

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-al' + '-ism', indicating belief in or practice of tradition.

2. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
(in philosophy or theology) The doctrine that all knowledge originates from divine revelation and is preserved and transmitted through tradition.
doctrine: knowledge from revelation and tradition

Examples

The church adhered to traditionalism, emphasizing the authority of sacred tradition in shaping doctrine.
19th-century debates about faith often centered around traditionalism versus rational inquiry.
Some philosophers challenged traditionalism, arguing for the role of empirical evidence in the acquisition of knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodoxy - belief in or conformity to traditional doctrines, especially in religion

Etymology

From traditional + -ism, indicating a belief system centered on tradition.

3. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice of strictly following traditional methods, ideas, or customs, especially in religion, art, or culture
strict adherence to traditional methods

Examples

The university is known for its traditionalism in educational methods and curriculum.
Some critics argue that excessive traditionalism can hinder cultural progress and innovation.
His traditionalism shaped every aspect of his personal and professional life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatism - commitment to traditional values and ideas with opposition to change
  • Antonym: progressivism - support for or advocacy of social reform or new, liberal ideas

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-alism', influenced by Late Latin 'traditionem' (a handing over, a giving up) and the suffix '-ism' indicating a system or practice.
