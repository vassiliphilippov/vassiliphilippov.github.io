to behead

1. VERB

/ bɪˈhɛd / (AmE), / bɪˈhɛd / (BrE)
to cut off the head of a person or animal, especially as a form of execution
cut off the head (execute)

Examples

During the medieval era, traitors were beheaded in public as a warning to others.
The revolutionaries planned to behead the captured general at dawn.
He was sentenced to death and beheaded for his crimes.

Synset Examples

the French King was beheaded during the Revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decapitate - to cut off the head, especially as a punishment

Etymology

From Old English behēafdian (to deprive of the head), from be- + hēafod (head).
