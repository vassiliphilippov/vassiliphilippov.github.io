aldotriose

1. NOUN

/ ˌældoʊˈtraɪoʊs / (AmE), / ˌældəʊˈtraɪəʊz / (BrE)
An aldose sugar containing three carbon atoms; specifically, glyceraldehyde is the only aldotriose.
three-carbon aldose sugar
medical

Examples

Aldotriose is the simplest form of aldose sugar containing only three carbon atoms.
Researchers studied the structure of aldotriose to better understand carbohydrate chemistry.
Glyceraldehyde is classified as an aldotriose in organic chemistry textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aldotetrose - an aldose with four carbon atoms
  • Synonym: triose - a monosaccharide with three carbon atoms

Etymology

From ‘aldo-’ (referring to aldehyde group) and ‘triose’ (a sugar with three carbon atoms).
