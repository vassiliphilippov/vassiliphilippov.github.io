Serbia

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜr.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.bi.ə / (BrE)
A historical region in central and northern Yugoslavia, inhabited by Serbs since the 6th and 7th centuries; now primarily part of the modern state of Serbia.
historical region in Yugoslavia, home of Serbs

Examples

The history of Serbia is marked by its position on the crossroads of Central and Southeast Europe.
After the breakup of Yugoslavia, Serbia became an independent republic.
Many cultural traditions in Serbia date back to the medieval period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Serb - a member of the South Slavic people of Serbia and neighboring regions

Etymology

From Latin 'Serbia', from Serb (ethnic group name).
