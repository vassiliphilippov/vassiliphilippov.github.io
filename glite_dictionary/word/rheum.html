Rheum

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːəm / (AmE), / ˈriːəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the buckwheat family, including species such as rhubarb.
a plant genus including rhubarb

Examples

The leaves of Rheum species are often large and triangular.
Scientists study different species within the Rheum genus for medicinal purposes.
The most commonly cultivated member of Rheum is used in pies and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - a plant of the genus Rheum, often grown for its edible stalks

Etymology

From Latin 'rheum', from Greek 'rheon' (rhubarb).
