David

1. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
A Christian saint who is the patron saint of Wales, c. 520–600, known for his devout lifestyle and founding of religious communities.
patron saint of Wales

Examples

Every year, Welsh people celebrate David on Saint David's Day.
Pilgrims visit St. David's Cathedral in Wales to honor the saint.
The life of David is an important part of Welsh history and culture.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Dāwīdh' meaning 'beloved'; associated with the 6th-century Welsh saint.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
In the Old Testament, the second king of Israel, known for defeating Goliath and establishing Jerusalem as the capital; traditionally credited as the author of many Psalms.
biblical king of Israel, slew Goliath

Examples

The story of David and Goliath is one of the most famous tales in the Bible.
Many of the Psalms are traditionally believed to have been written by David.
Jerusalem became the capital of Israel during the reign of King David.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Goliath - giant Philistine warrior defeated by David
  • Degree Lower: Saul - the first king of Israel, predecessor to David
  • Degree Higher: Solomon - son of David, third king of Israel

Etymology

From Hebrew Dawid, possibly meaning 'beloved'.
