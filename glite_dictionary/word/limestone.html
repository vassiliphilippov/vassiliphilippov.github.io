limestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate, formed from the remains of marine organisms
type of sedimentary rock

Examples

The ancient building was constructed using local limestone.
Over millions of years, marine fossils contributed to the formation of limestone.
A limestone quarry was opened to provide materials for the town's expansion.

Etymology

From lime (in the sense 'calcium oxide') + stone.
