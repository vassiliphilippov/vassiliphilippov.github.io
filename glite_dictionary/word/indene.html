indene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˈɪndiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, liquid hydrocarbon obtained from coal tar or petroleum, used in making synthetic resins
a liquid hydrocarbon used in resins
medical

Examples

The chemical company produces indene for use in manufacturing synthetic resins.
Researchers studied the environmental effects of indene released from petroleum refining.
Indene is often extracted from coal tar during its processing in industrial plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrocarbon - a compound consisting entirely of hydrogen and carbon

Etymology

From Latin 'indicus' (of India) and '-ene' (hydrocarbon suffix), referencing its discovery in coal tar relating to indigo dye.
