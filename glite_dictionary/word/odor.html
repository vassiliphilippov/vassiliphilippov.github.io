odor

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
a distinctive smell, especially one that is noticeable by the sense of olfaction
a particular smell

Examples

A faint odor of smoke lingered in the empty room.
The odor of fresh bread filled the bakery every morning.
He noticed an unpleasant odor coming from the refrigerator.

Synset Examples

she loved the smell of roses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aroma - a pleasant, often distinctive smell
  • Synonym: scent - a distinctive smell, especially a pleasant one
  • Antonym: stench - a very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'odor' (smell, scent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdər / (AmE), / ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
A distinctive smell, especially an unpleasant one, detected by the sense of smell.
a distinctive smell

Examples

The kitchen had a strong odor of garlic after dinner was prepared.
He noticed an unpleasant odor coming from the refrigerator.
The flower's sweet odor filled the entire room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scent - a distinctive smell, especially one that is pleasant
  • Synonym: aroma - a distinctive, typically pleasant smell
  • Degree Higher: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Degree Higher: stench - a very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'odor' (smell, scent).
