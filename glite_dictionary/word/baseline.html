baseline

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪslaɪn / (BrE)
In baseball, the direct path along which a player must run between bases to be safe.
path between bases in baseball

Examples

The runner slid along the baseline to avoid the tag at third base.
It's an automatic out if you step too far outside the baseline.
The umpire watched closely as the player sprinted down the baseline.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪslaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪslaɪn / (BrE)
A standard or reference point used for comparison or measurement.
reference point for comparison

Examples

Before starting the experiment, scientists must establish a baseline for comparison.
The team's current performance sets the baseline for future improvements.
Her blood pressure was measured to determine a baseline before treatment began.

Synset Examples

they established a baseline for the budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard by which something can be measured or judged

Etymology

Formed from base + line, early 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪslaɪn / (BrE)
the back line marking each end of a tennis or handball court, over which the server must not step
back line on a tennis/handball court

Examples

The player stood just behind the baseline as she prepared to serve.
He hit a powerful shot from the baseline that landed near his opponent's feet.
During the match, she barely stepped on the baseline, resulting in a fault.

Etymology

From 'base' + 'line', referring to the line marking the boundary at the base of the playing area.
