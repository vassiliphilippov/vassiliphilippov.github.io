to elevate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛləˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪveɪt / (BrE)
to raise something to a higher position or level
raise to a higher position

Examples

The surgeon needed to elevate the patient's leg after the operation.
Teachers strive to elevate their students' performance through encouragement.
The platform can elevate heavy objects with minimal effort.

Synset Examples

Raise your hands
Lift a load

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - to move something to a higher position
  • Synonym: raise - to move something to a higher position or level
  • Antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position

Etymology

From Latin elevare (to lift up), from e- (up) + levare (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01978076-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛləˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪveɪt / (BrE)
to give someone a higher or more important position or rank
promote to higher position

Examples

After years of hard work, the company decided to elevate her to the position of manager.
The board chose to elevate him above his peers due to his outstanding leadership.
She was elevated to a senior role within the organization last year.

Synset Examples

John was kicked upstairs when a replacement was hired
Women tend not to advance in the major law firms
I got promoted after many years of hard work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher position
  • Antonym: to demote - to lower someone to a less important position

Etymology

From Latin elevare, from e- (out, up) + levare (to raise).
