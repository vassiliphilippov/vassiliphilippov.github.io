axle

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.səl / (AmE), / ˈæk.səl / (BrE)
a shaft or rod on which a wheel or pair of wheels rotates or turns
shaft on which a wheel rotates

Examples

The car's front axle was damaged in the collision.
A bicycle has a single axle through each wheel.
Technicians inspected the axle for signs of wear and tear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaft - a long, narrow part forming the handle of a tool or the body of a spear; also used mechanically

Etymology

From Old French 'axel', alteration of Old English 'eaxl' (shoulder, axis).
