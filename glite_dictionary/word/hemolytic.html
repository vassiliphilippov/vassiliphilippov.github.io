hemolytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to, involving, or causing the destruction of red blood cells (hemolysis)
causing destruction of red blood cells
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hemolytic anemia after reviewing her blood test results.
A hemolytic reaction can sometimes occur after a blood transfusion if the blood types are not compatible.
Some bacterial infections produce hemolytic toxins that damage red blood cells.

Synset Examples

hemolytic anemia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysis - the destruction of red blood cells

Etymology

From hemolysis + -ic; hemolysis from Greek haima (blood) and lysis (loosening, dissolving).
