Sparta

1. PROPN

/ ˈspɑr.tə / (AmE), / ˈspɑː.tə / (BrE)
an ancient Greek city-state famous for its military discipline and power, dominant in the Peloponnesus before the 4th century BC
ancient Greek city-state, famed for military strength

Examples

The warriors of Sparta were renowned for their discipline and bravery.
Many historians study the rise and fall of Sparta to understand Greek history.
In ancient times, Sparta rivaled Athens in power and prestige.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Athens - the capital of ancient Greece, noted for art and philosophy

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Σπάρτη (Spártē), the name of the city.
