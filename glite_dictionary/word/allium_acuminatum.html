Allium acuminatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm əˌkjuːməˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm əˌkjuːmɪˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A North American wild onion with a strong onion odor and a cluster (umbel) of pink flowers on a leafless stalk, found from British Columbia to California and east to Wyoming and Colorado.
North American wild onion species

Examples

During our hike, we spotted several clusters of Allium acuminatum blooming on the hillside.
Native peoples traditionally harvested the bulbs of Allium acuminatum for food.
The strong onion scent of Allium acuminatum makes it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild onion - any uncultivated species of onion, especially those with edible bulbs
  • Degree Higher: Allium - the genus of flowering plants that includes onions, garlic, and their relatives

Etymology

From Latin: 'allium' meaning 'garlic' or 'onion' and 'acuminatum' meaning 'pointed', referring to the shape of its flower buds.
