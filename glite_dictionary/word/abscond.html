to abscond

1. VERB

/ æbˈskɑnd / (AmE), / æbˈskɒnd / (BrE)
to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest, often taking something with oneself
leave secretly to avoid detection or arrest

Examples

After the fraud was exposed, the CFO tried to abscond with company funds.
The suspect managed to abscond before the police arrived at the scene.
Many feared the caretaker might abscond with the valuable paintings.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: escape - to break free from confinement or control

Etymology

From Latin ab- (away) + condere (to hide, to put together).
