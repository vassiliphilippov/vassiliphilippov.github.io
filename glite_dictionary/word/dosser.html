dosser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɒsər / (AmE), / ˈdɒsə / (BrE)
a person who sleeps wherever they can, often because they are homeless or lack permanent accommodation
person sleeping anywhere, often homeless
british informal

Examples

After losing his job, Tom became a dosser and spent nights sleeping in the train station.
The park benches are often used by dossers seeking shelter for the night.
She helped a dosser by bringing him a warm blanket during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home who wanders from place to place
  • Synonym: homeless person - someone who does not have a home

Etymology

From British slang 'doss', meaning 'to sleep', possibly from the French 'dos' (back), suggesting lying on one's back.
