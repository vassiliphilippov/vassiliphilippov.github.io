pollock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlək / (AmE), / ˈpɒlək / (BrE)
A commercially important North Atlantic fish of the cod family, valued as food and game fish.
North Atlantic food and game fish

Examples

The fisherman caught a large pollock off the coast of Norway.
Many fish sticks are made from pollock because of its mild flavor.
Scientists are monitoring the population of Atlantic pollock to ensure sustainable fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large sea fish closely related to pollock

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'pollag' (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlək / (AmE), / ˈpɒlək / (BrE)
The lean white flesh of a North Atlantic fish, eaten as food and similar to cod.
lean white flesh of North Atlantic fish

Examples

Many fish and chip shops use pollock instead of cod for their fried dishes.
The fillets of pollock are flaky and mild in flavor, making them popular in sandwiches.
Frozen pollock is often used to make imitation crab meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - lean white fish often used as food
  • Synonym: haddock - lean white-fleshed fish of the North Atlantic

Etymology

From the Scots 'polack' or 'pollack', likely from Middle English 'pollok', meaning a kind of fish.
