Boletus roxanae

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs rɒkˈsæni / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs rɒkˈsɑːni / (BrE)
A species of bolete fungus with a rusty red cap, white pore surface that turns yellow with age, and a pale yellow stalk.
species of bolete fungus

Examples

The mycologist identified the specimen as Boletus roxanae thanks to its rusty cap and pale yellow stalk.
You should consult a field guide before collecting Boletus roxanae for culinary purposes.
The forest floor was dotted with patches of Boletus roxanae after the recent rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolete - a type of mushroom in the family Boletaceae
