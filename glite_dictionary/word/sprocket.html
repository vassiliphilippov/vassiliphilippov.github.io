sprocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɑk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈsprɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
A thin wheel with teeth that fit into the links of a chain, used to transmit motion in machinery or bicycles.
toothed wheel for engaging a chain

Examples

The bicycle chain slipped off the sprocket and had to be fixed.
He replaced the worn-out sprocket to keep the machinery running smoothly.
When cleaning your bike, make sure to remove dirt from around the sprocket for optimal performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gear - toothed wheel used with others to alter motion in machinery

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal English, related to 'spoke'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsprɒkɪt / (BrE)
A tooth or projection on the rim of a gear wheel, especially one designed to engage with a chain or track.
tooth on the rim of a gear wheel

Examples

The bicycle chain wraps around each sprocket to transfer power from the pedals to the wheel.
When the sprocket became worn, the gear mechanism started to slip.
Technicians inspected every sprocket on the conveyor system for signs of damage.

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal English 'sprocket' (a kind of bar or lever) or related to 'spark' in mechanical senses.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsprɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsprɒkɪt / (BrE)
a wheel or roller with teeth along the rim, used to engage with and drive a film, paper, or chain by pulling it through a machine
toothed wheel for moving film, paper, or chain

Examples

The camera's sprocket feeds the film smoothly from one frame to the next.
A damaged sprocket can cause the paper to jam inside the printer.
Technicians replaced the worn sprocket to restore proper movement in the film projector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gear - a wheel with teeth that meshes with another to transmit motion

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from dialectal 'sprocket' (a projection) or related to 'spoke'.
