slobberer

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːbərər / (AmE), / ˈslɒbərə / (BrE)
a person, especially a baby or animal, who dribbles or lets saliva run from the mouth
person or animal who dribbles saliva

Examples

The puppy was such a slobberer that we kept a towel by the door.
That old dog is quite the slobberer when he gets excited.
Her baby is a real slobberer and always needs to wear a bib.

Synset Examples

that baby is a dribbler; he needs a bib

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribbler - a person or animal who lets saliva flow from the mouth

Etymology

From 'slobber' (to let saliva spill from the mouth) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
