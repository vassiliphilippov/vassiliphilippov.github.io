demurely

1. ADV

/ dɪˈmjʊrli / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊəli / (BrE)
in a modest, reserved, or shy manner
in a modest or shy way

Examples

She smiled demurely and lowered her eyes during the conversation.
The actress stood demurely on stage while accepting her award.
He complimented her dress, and she thanked him demurely.

Synset Examples

the army girl, tall and demurely pretty, threw a quick side-glance at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modestly - in a modest manner
  • Antonym: boldly - in a confident and daring manner, opposite of 'demurely'

Etymology

From 'demure' + '-ly'; 'demure' from Old French 'demur', past participle of 'demorer' (to delay, linger).
