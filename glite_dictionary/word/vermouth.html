vermouth

1. NOUN

/ vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / vəˈmuːθ / (BrE)
a type of fortified white wine flavored with various aromatic herbs and spices, often used as an aperitif or in mixed drinks
aromatized fortified wine for drinks

Examples

She ordered a vermouth on the rocks as an aperitif before dinner.
A classic martini contains gin and a small amount of vermouth.
Some chefs use vermouth in cooking to add complexity to sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martini - a cocktail typically made with gin and vermouth

Etymology

From French 'vermouth', from German 'Wermut' (wormwood), one of the original flavoring ingredients.
