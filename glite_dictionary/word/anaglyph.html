anaglyph

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈænəɡlɪf / (BrE)
a stereoscopic image, often in contrasting colors, that appears three-dimensional when viewed with special glasses, typically using red and cyan lenses
three-dimensional image using color layers

Examples

The movie included several anaglyph scenes that required 3D glasses to view correctly.
She created an anaglyph of the landscape, bringing a vivid sense of depth to the picture.
To appreciate the effect, you need to wear red-cyan glasses when looking at any anaglyph.

Etymology

From Greek 'anaglyphos' (carved in relief), from 'ana-' (up) + 'glyphein' (to carve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈænəɡlɪf / (BrE)
a carving or design in low relief; a figure or ornament that is raised slightly from its background
carving in low relief

Examples

The ancient temple was decorated with intricate anaglyphs that depicted scenes from mythology.
The artist specialized in creating anaglyph panels for architectural ornamentation.
We admired the delicate anaglyph work on the museum's marble columns.

Etymology

From Greek anaglyphos (carved in relief), from ana- (up) + glyphein (to carve).
