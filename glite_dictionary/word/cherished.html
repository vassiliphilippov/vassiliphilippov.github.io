cherished

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛrɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrɪʃt / (BrE)
Deeply loved, valued, or held in affectionate regard.
deeply loved or valued

Examples

Her grandmother gave her a cherished locket that she wore every day.
The team's victory was a cherished memory for all the players involved.
He looked through a box of his cherished childhood photographs.

Synset Examples

a cherished friend
children are precious
a treasured heirloom
so good to feel wanted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasured - regarded or treated as precious, highly valued
  • Synonym: precious - of great value; beloved
  • Antonym: neglected - not given proper care, attention, or affection

Etymology

Past participle of 'cherish', from Old French 'cherir' (to hold dear), from Latin 'carus' (dear).
