showcase

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌkeɪs / (BrE)
a situation or event that presents something in a way that displays its best qualities
situation for presenting something attractively

Examples

The festival served as a showcase for new talent in the music industry.
The museum's new wing is a showcase of contemporary art.
Their cooking competition acted as a showcase for local ingredients and traditions.

Synset Examples

it was a showcase for democracy in Africa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - an arrangement or event to show something
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌkeɪs / (BrE)
a glass container used for storing and displaying items, especially in a shop, museum, or home
glass display case for items

Examples

The gold necklace was displayed in a glass showcase in the jewelry store.
Visitors admired the rare stamps arranged inside the museum's largest showcase.
She keeps her porcelain dolls safe in a locked showcase in the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display case - a case with a glass front for displaying items

Etymology

Formed from 'show' + 'case', meaning a case for showing items.
