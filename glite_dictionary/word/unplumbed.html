unplumbed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈplʌmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplʌmd / (BrE)
not having been measured or explored in depth; too deep to have been sounded or investigated.
not measured or explored in depth

Examples

The ship navigated across the unplumbed depths of the ocean, where no measurement had ever been made.
Her imagination wandered into unplumbed regions of the mind, searching for inspiration.
Scientists are fascinated by the unplumbed mysteries lurking beneath the Antarctic ice shelves.

Synset Examples

the profound depths of the sea
the dark unfathomed caves of ocean
unplumbed depths of the sea
remote and unsounded caverns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsounded - not measured for depth; unexplored
  • Synonym: unfathomed - not explored or fully understood

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + past participle of 'plumb', which derives from Latin 'plumbum' (lead), originally referring to measuring water depth with a lead weight.
