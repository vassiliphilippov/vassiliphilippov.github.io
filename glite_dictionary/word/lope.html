lope

1. NOUN

/ loʊp / (AmE), / ləʊp / (BrE)
A smooth, relaxed three-beat gait of a horse, between a trot and a gallop.
smooth three-beat horse gait

Examples

The horse broke into a gentle lope across the meadow.
Her pony's lope was easy and comfortable to ride.
At the trainer's signal, the horse switched smoothly from a trot to a lope.

Etymology

From Middle English 'lopen' (to leap), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ loʊp / (AmE), / ləʊp / (BrE)
to move or run with long, easy, relaxed steps
run with long, easy steps

Examples

The horse continued to lope across the field in an easy, rhythmic motion.
She watched the children lope down the path, their backpacks bouncing with every step.
He prefers to lope along the trail rather than sprint, enjoying the scenery as he goes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - to move swiftly on foot
  • Degree Lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
  • Degree Higher: gallop - to move very fast, especially for a horse

Etymology

From Middle English lopen, from Old Norse hlaupa (to leap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01932683-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ loʊp / (AmE), / ləʊp / (BrE)
A long, easy, and steady gait, typically a slow, relaxed pace of running or moving.
slow, steady running gait

Examples

The horse broke into a gentle lope across the open field.
She settled into a comfortable lope that she could maintain for miles.
His relaxed lope made it clear he was in no hurry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallop - a fast gait of a horse or similar animal
  • Synonym: jog - a slow, steady run

Etymology

From Middle English lopen (to leap), from Old Norse hlaupa (to leap, run).

Source: WordNet
