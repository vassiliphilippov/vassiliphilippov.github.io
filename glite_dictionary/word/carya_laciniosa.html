Carya laciniosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛə.ri.ə læˌsɪ.niˈoʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.rɪ.ə læˌsɪ.nɪˈəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A species of hickory native to eastern North America, distinguished by its large nut and shaggy bark.
species of hickory tree with large nut

Examples

The Carya laciniosa is commonly found along riverbanks in the Midwest and eastern United States.
The nuts of the Carya laciniosa are larger than those of most other hickories.
Botanists carefully studied the bark patterns of Carya laciniosa to differentiate it from similar species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - a related species of hickory with shaggy bark and smaller nuts

Etymology

From Latin; 'Carya' is the Latinized form of the Greek 'karya' meaning 'nut tree'; 'laciniosa' is Latin for 'torn' or 'fringed', referring to the bark.
