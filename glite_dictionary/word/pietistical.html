pietistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪ.əˈtɪs.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.ɪˈtɪs.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to Pietism or its practices, beliefs, or followers.
relating to Pietism

Examples

The movement promoted a pietistical approach to religious life.
Her pietistical attitudes reflected the teachings of the early reformers.
Some critics considered the novel's tone overly pietistical and moralistic.

Synset Examples

the Pietistic movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pietistic - Relating to Pietism or showing piety; alternate adjective

Etymology

From Pietism, a Lutheran movement in late 17th-century Germany, plus the adjective suffix '-ical'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪ.əˈtɪs.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.ɪˈtɪs.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Showing an excessive or affected display of religious piety, often in a hypocritical way.
excessively or hypocritically pious

Examples

The preacher's pietistical speeches often felt insincere to many in the congregation.
She wore a pietistical expression, but her actions told a different story.
His pietistical attitude seemed more concerned with appearances than genuine faith.

Synset Examples

a sickening sanctimonious smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others
  • Degree Lower: devout - having or showing deep religious feeling or commitment

Etymology

From 'pietist' (devoutly religious person) plus the suffix '-ical'; ultimately from German 'Pietist' and Latin 'pietas' (piety).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01786645-s
Synset POS: s
