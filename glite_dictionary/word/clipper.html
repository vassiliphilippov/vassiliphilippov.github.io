clipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpər / (AmE), / ˈklɪpə / (BrE)
a tool resembling scissors, used for cutting hair or fingernails
tool for cutting hair or fingernails

Examples

He used a clipper to trim his beard neatly every morning.
The barber picked up his electric clipper and began to cut the customer's hair.
I need a new nail clipper because mine has become dull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scissors - a cutting tool with two blades joined in the middle
  • Synonym: trimmer - device for cutting or shortening hair or other materials

Etymology

From 'clip' (to cut), with the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpər / (AmE), / ˈklɪpə / (BrE)
a fast sailing ship with a sleek hull and tall masts, used in the 19th century, especially for long-distance cargo transport
fast 19th-century sailing ship

Examples

The clipper sped across the Atlantic, breaking previous records for the fastest crossing.
Many fortunes were made on the tea trade thanks to the swift clipper ships.
A beautifully restored clipper is now on display at the maritime museum.

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to move quickly), reflecting the ship's speed.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpə / (BrE)
a tool with two sharp blades used for cutting grass, hedges, or shrubbery
tool for cutting grass or shrubs

Examples

He used the clipper to shape the bushes in the garden.
These clippers make trimming the grass along the path much easier.
Please hand me the clipper so I can tidy up the hedge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shears - large scissors for cutting plants or hedges

Etymology

From 'clip' (to cut) + '-er', denoting an agent noun.

4. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpər / (AmE), / ˈklɪpə / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that prevents a signal from exceeding a certain amplitude by clipping the peaks of a waveform
electronic circuit limiting signal amplitude

Examples

A clipper circuit is used to protect sensitive electronics from voltage spikes.
Audio engineers sometimes use a clipper to eliminate distortion from an overloaded amplifier.
The technician adjusted the clipper so that the output voltage remained within a safe range.

Synset Examples

a limiter introduces amplitude distortion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limiter - an electronic device or circuit that restricts the amplitude of a signal

Etymology

From 'clip' (to cut) + -er, indicating something that cuts or limits.
