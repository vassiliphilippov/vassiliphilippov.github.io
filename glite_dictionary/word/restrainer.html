restrainer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstreɪnər / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnə / (BrE)
a chemical added to a photographic developer to slow down development and reduce fog on film
chemical that slows photographic development

Examples

Potassium bromide is often used as a restrainer in black-and-white photographic development.
The addition of a restrainer helps prevent unwanted fogging on the film.
She adjusted the amount of restrainer to fine-tune the image quality.

Etymology

From restrain + -er, indicating something that restrains or slows a process.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈstreɪnər / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnə / (BrE)
a person who directs or holds back others, preventing them from acting freely or excessively
person who holds back or controls others

Examples

The security guard acted as a restrainer during the chaotic protest.
Sometimes a teacher needs to be a restrainer in a classroom full of energetic children.
Without a restrainer, the crowd could have become unruly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - a person who directs or regulates something
  • Antonym: enabler - a person who encourages or allows a behavior

Etymology

From restrain + -er, indicating 'one who restrains'.
