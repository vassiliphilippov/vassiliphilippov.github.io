Sinitic

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Chinese people, their language, or their culture, especially referring to the group of Chinese languages
relating to Chinese people, language, or culture

Examples

Many scholars study the evolution of Sinitic languages across different regions of China.
Cantonese and Mandarin are both considered Sinitic dialects.
The museum features artifacts from early Sinitic civilizations.

Synset Examples

Sinitic dialects

Etymology

From Late Latin Sīniticus, from Sinae (China).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the group of Sino-Tibetan languages that includes Chinese and its varieties.
subgroup of Sino-Tibetan languages

Examples

Mandarin, Cantonese, and Hakka are considered Sinitic languages within the Sino-Tibetan family.
Linguists have debated the classification of Sinitic languages for decades.
The spread of Sinitic dialects has greatly influenced the vocabulary of neighboring regions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sino-Tibetan - a major language family including Chinese and Tibetan languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Sinae' (China) + '-itic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06942395-n
Synset POS: n
