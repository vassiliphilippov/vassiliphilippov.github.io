shoeful

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃuːfʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a shoe can contain or hold.
amount a shoe can hold

Examples

After the storm, I found a shoeful of sand inside my sneakers.
He poured out a shoeful of muddy water after hiking through the marsh.
A single shoeful of rice was all she needed for the experiment.

Synset Examples

he emptied out a shoeful of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the amount a cup will hold
  • Synonym: handful - the amount a hand can hold

Etymology

Formed by analogy with similar compounds like 'cupful' or 'handful', from 'shoe' + suffix '-ful'.
