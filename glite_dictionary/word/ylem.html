ylem

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈaɪləm / (BrE)
(in cosmology) the primordial substance from which matter in the universe is thought to have originated according to the big bang theory.
original substance after big bang (cosmology)

Examples

According to some cosmological models, the universe began as a hot, dense mass of ylem.
The theory proposes that all matter evolved from the original ylem.
Scientists debated the exact characteristics of the pre-elemental ylem in the early universe.

Etymology

From Middle English, ultimately from Greek hylē (matter, wood).
