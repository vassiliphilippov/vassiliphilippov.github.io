Cyperus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpɪrəs / (AmE), / saɪˈpɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of grasslike, rhizomatous herbs in the sedge family (Cyperaceae), found throughout most regions of the world except very cold areas.
genus of grasslike sedge plants

Examples

The wetland is dominated by Cyperus and other sedge species.
Botanists identified the plant as a member of the Cyperus genus.
Many Cyperus species, such as papyrus, have historical importance.

Etymology

From Latin 'cyperus', from Ancient Greek 'kypeiros' (a type of sedge).
