apetalous

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɛtələs / (AmE), / əˈpɛtələs / (BrE)
(of flowers) having no petals.
having no petals (in flowers)

Examples

Some species of willow produce apetalous flowers that lack the showy petals found in other plants.
The botanist noted that the rare plant had apetalous blooms, distinguishing it from related species.
Certain wind-pollinated plants tend to have apetalous flowers since petals are unnecessary for attracting pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: petal - a brightly colored part of a flower

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'petalon' (petal, leaf).
