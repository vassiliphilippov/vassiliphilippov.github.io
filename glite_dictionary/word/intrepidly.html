intrepidly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtrɛpɪdli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛpɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no fear; fearlessly.
without fear; fearlessly

Examples

She intrepidly defended her position against intense criticism from her peers.
The explorers intrepidly crossed the frozen tundra despite the severe weather conditions.
He intrepidly spoke up during the meeting, even though his opinion was unpopular.

Synset Examples

fearlessly, he led the troops into combat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearlessly - without fear; in a fearless manner
  • Antonym: timidly - in a way that lacks courage; shyly or fearfully

Etymology

From 'intrepid' (from Latin 'intrepidus', from 'in-' (not) + 'trepidus' (alarmed, fearful)) + '-ly'.
