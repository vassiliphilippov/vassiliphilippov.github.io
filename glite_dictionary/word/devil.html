devil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
(in the Judeo-Christian and Islamic religions) the chief spirit of evil and adversary of God; the tempter of mankind and master of Hell.
chief spirit of evil; tempter in religions

Examples

Many religious texts describe the devil as the embodiment of evil and the tempter of humanity.
Some believe that the devil tries to lead people away from the path of righteousness.
In art and literature, the devil is often depicted as a horned, menacing figure presiding over Hell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satan - the chief evil spirit; the Devil in Christianity and Islam
  • Antonym: angel - a spiritual being acting as a messenger of God, often seen as the opposite of the Devil

Etymology

From Old English 'deofol', from Late Latin 'diabolus', from Greek 'diabolos' (slanderer, accuser).
