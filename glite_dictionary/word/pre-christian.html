pre-Christian

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriˈkrɪstʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkrɪstʃən / (BrE)
Relating to the time before the beginning of the Christian era.
before the Christian era

Examples

Archaeologists discovered several pre-Christian burial sites in the region.
The festival has its origins in ancient pre-Christian rituals.
Many pre-Christian traditions were later incorporated into early Christian celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: post-Christian - Relating to the period after the spread of Christianity

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'Christian'.
