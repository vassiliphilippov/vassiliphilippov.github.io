Petasites sagittatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtəˌsaɪtiːz sæˈdʒɪtəˌtəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛtəˌsiːtiːz səˈdʒɪtətəs / (BrE)
A North American plant of the daisy family, also known as arrowleaf sweet coltsfoot, with sweet-scented leaves and flowers.
a North American sweet-scented herb

Examples

The leaves of the Petasites sagittatus are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
In early spring, Petasites sagittatus produces clusters of white sweet-scented flowers.
Botanists have documented the habitat range of Petasites sagittatus across North America.

Etymology

From Latin 'petasites' (a kind of plant) + 'sagittatus' (arrow-shaped), referring to the arrow-shaped leaves.
