tittle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪtəl / (BrE)
A very small or insignificant amount; a tiny or barely noticeable trace.
a tiny or negligible amount

Examples

He didn't care a tittle for what others thought of him.
There isn't a tittle of truth in her story.
Not a tittle of evidence was found to support the claim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iota - an extremely small amount
  • Synonym: speck - a very small spot or amount
  • Synonym: bit - a small degree or amount

Etymology

From Latin 'titulus' (inscription, heading), later used in medieval Latin as 'tittulus' for a small stroke or point.
