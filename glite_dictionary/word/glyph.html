glyph

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪf / (AmE), / ɡlɪf / (BrE)
a carved or inscribed symbol or figure, especially one forming part of a writing system or representing something in relief
carved or inscribed symbol or figure

Examples

The ancient temple walls were covered with mysterious glyphs depicting animals and gods.
Archaeologists carefully examined the glyph to decipher its meaning in the context of the civilization's language.
Many Mayan glyphs remain undeciphered, leaving some aspects of the culture a mystery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character - a symbol used in writing or printing
  • Synonym: pictogram - a pictorial symbol representing a word or phrase

Etymology

From Greek 'gluphē' (carving), from 'gluphein' (to carve).
