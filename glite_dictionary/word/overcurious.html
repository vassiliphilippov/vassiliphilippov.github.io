overcurious

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈkjʊriəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkjʊəriəs / (BrE)
excessively curious; showing too much curiosity, especially in matters that do not concern one
excessively curious

Examples

His overcurious nature often led him to ask questions that others found intrusive.
It is considered rude to be overcurious about your colleagues' private affairs.
The cat was so overcurious that it knocked over the vase exploring every corner of the house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: curious - eager to know or learn something
  • Synonym: inquisitive - having or showing an eager interest in learning things

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) + 'curious', both from Latin origins.
