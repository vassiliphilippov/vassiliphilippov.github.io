cromulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɑːmjʊlənt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒmjʊlɪnt / (BrE)
(informal, often ironic) Acceptable or legitimate; adequate or valid, though sometimes used to humorously suggest that something is just barely so.
acceptable or legitimate (often ironic)
informal american

Examples

His excuse was perfectly cromulent, though I suspected he was joking.
That's a cromulent word, even if it sounds made up.
While her argument was cromulent, it hardly convinced the jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valid - having a sound basis in logic or fact; reasonable
  • Synonym: legitimate - conforming to the law or to rules
  • Antonym: bogus - not genuine or true; fake (contrasting term, sometimes used as antonym)

Etymology

Coined in the 1996 episode 'Lisa the Iconoclast' of the American TV show The Simpsons.
