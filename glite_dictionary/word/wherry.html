wherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛri / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed, usually shallow-draft sailing barge, used especially in East Anglia for transporting goods or passengers on rivers and broads
sailing barge from East Anglia

Examples

The museum displays a restored wherry once used to carry coal along the Norfolk Broads.
We took a trip on the river in an old wherry built in the nineteenth century.
Local trade relied heavily on the wherry before railways became common.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *wherye*, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛri / (BrE)
a light, narrow rowboat used for racing or for carrying goods and passengers, especially in rivers, harbors, and inland waters
a type of light rowboat

Examples

The fishermen loaded their catch into the wherry and rowed back to the harbor.
Tourists enjoyed a leisurely ride down the river in a traditional wherry.
During the regatta, each team raced a sleek wherry across the lake.

Etymology

From Middle English 'whery', of uncertain origin, possibly from Dutch or Low German.
