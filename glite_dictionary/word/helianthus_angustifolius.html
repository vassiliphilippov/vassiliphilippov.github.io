Helianthus angustifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈænθəs æŋˌɡʌstəˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθəs æŋˌɡʌstəˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
A species of sunflower native to eastern North America, characterized by narrow leaves and typically found in boggy areas.
species of North American sunflower with narrow leaves

Examples

Helianthus angustifolius brightens wetlands with its yellow blooms each autumn.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Helianthus angustifolius in eastern bogs.
You can identify Helianthus angustifolius by its long, slender leaves and vibrant sunflower-like flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: Helianthus (sunflower) and angustifolius (narrow-leaved).
