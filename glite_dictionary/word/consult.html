to consult

1. VERB

/ kənˈsʌlt / (AmE), / kənˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to seek information or advice from someone, especially an expert
seek advice from someone

Examples

Before changing his medication, he decided to consult his doctor.
You should consult a financial advisor before investing your money.
We will consult with the committee to finalize the plans.

Synset Examples

Consult your local broker
They had to consult before arriving at a decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advise - to offer suggestions or recommendations about what should be done
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to advice or information

Etymology

From Latin consultare (to deliberate, counsel)

WordNet ID: oewn-00879090-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈsʌlt / (AmE), / kənˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to seek information or advice from (a source, especially a book or expert) before making a decision or taking action
seek information or advice from

Examples

I always consult my notes before an important meeting.
You should consult a doctor if the symptoms persist.
Before deciding on the destination, they consulted several travel guides.

Synset Examples

You should consult the dictionary
refer to your notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refer - to look at or in (something) for information or help
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to; disregard

Etymology

From Latin consultare (to deliberate, consider), frequentative of consulere (to consult, deliberate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00878614-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈsʌlt / (AmE), / kənˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to discuss something with someone or a group in order to get advice or exchange ideas before making a decision
discuss to seek advice or ideas

Examples

The doctors decided to consult before proceeding with the complicated surgery.
If you are unsure about your taxes, you should consult a professional advisor.
The committee will consult with local residents before making any changes.

Synset Examples

We conferred about a plan of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to exchange opinions or discuss; to consult together

Etymology

From Latin 'consultare', frequentative of 'consulere' (to deliberate, consult).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00878196-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kənˈsʌlt / (AmE), / kənˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to give professional or expert advice to organizations or individuals, often in a formal or paid capacity
to give professional advice

Examples

The company hired her to consult on their new marketing strategy.
Many lawyers consult for various businesses in addition to their regular practice.
He decided to consult for several tech startups after leaving his full-time job.

Synset Examples

The professor consults for industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advise - to offer suggestions about the best course of action

Etymology

From Latin consultare (to deliberate, to discuss), frequentative of consulere (to consult, to deliberate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00877973-v
Synset POS: v
