to commix

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɪks / (AmE), / kəˈmɪks / (BrE)
to mix or blend together; to combine two or more things into one
to mix or combine together
archaic

Examples

The artist sought to commix different styles to create something original.
Old traditions often commix with new customs in vibrant festivals.
She commixed the ingredients carefully, ensuring a smooth and even batter.

Synset Examples

resourcefully he mingled music and dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend into one; to put together
  • Synonym: blend - to mix smoothly and inseparably together
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not combined

Etymology

From Latin 'commiscere' (to mix together), from 'com-' (together) + 'miscere' (to mix).
