Dioscorea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈskɔːr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪ.əˈskɔː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial climbing or twining plants known as yams, valued for their starchy tubers, native to tropical and subtropical regions
a genus of yam plants

Examples

Dioscorea includes a wide variety of species used as food staples in tropical countries.
Botanists classify most edible yams under the genus Dioscorea.
Some Dioscorea species are cultivated not only for food, but also for medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - the starchy root or plant of the genus Dioscorea

Etymology

From the name of the ancient Greek physician Dioscorides.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈskɔriə / (AmE), / ˌdɪəˈskɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of climbing plants, commonly known as yams, native to tropical and subtropical regions and distinguished by their tuberous roots
genus of yam plants

Examples

Many species within the genus Dioscorea are cultivated for their starchy tubers.
The botanist collected samples of Dioscorea from the tropical rainforest.
Farmers in West Africa grow several varieties of Dioscorea for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - the edible tuber produced by plants of the genus Dioscorea

Etymology

Named after the Greek physician Dioscorides.
