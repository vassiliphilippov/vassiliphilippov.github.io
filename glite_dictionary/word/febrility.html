febrility

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈbrɪləti / (AmE), / fɛˈbrɪləti / (BrE)
A state of having an elevated body temperature, often as a symptom of infection or illness; feverishness.
state of having a fever
medical

Examples

The child's febrility indicated a possible infection that needed attention.
Prolonged febrility can sometimes be a sign of more serious underlying conditions.
The doctor assessed the patient's level of febrility to determine the next steps in treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature, usually due to illness
  • Antonym: afebrile - without fever; not feverish

Etymology

From Latin 'febrilis' (feverish) + English suffix '-ity'.
