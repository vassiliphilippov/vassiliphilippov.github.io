limb

1. NOUN

/ lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
the outer edge of the apparent disc of a celestial body, such as the Sun, Moon, or a planet, as seen from Earth
edge of a celestial body's disc

Examples

The eclipse began as the moon's limb gradually covered the sun.
A bright flare was observed at the western limb of the planet.
Through the telescope, you could clearly see the uneven outline of the moon's limb.

Etymology

From Latin 'limbus' (border, edge).
