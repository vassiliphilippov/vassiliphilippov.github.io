to vascularise

1. VERB

/ ˈvæskjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
(of an organ or tissue) to develop blood vessels, or to supply with blood vessels.
form or develop blood vessels
medical

Examples

During healing, the tissue will vascularise to promote better blood flow.
Researchers observed that the graft began to vascularise after several days in the host.
If a tumor starts to vascularise, it can grow much more rapidly.

Synset Examples

The egg yolk vascularized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vascularize - Alternative spelling with same meaning: develop blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel) + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈvæskjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaskjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
To form blood vessels in (a tissue or organ); to make vascular.
form blood vessels in tissue
medical british

Examples

After the surgical procedure, the tissue needs to vascularise to support healing.
Scientists are studying methods to help artificial organs vascularise more quickly.
If a graft fails to vascularise, it may not survive in the body.

Synset Examples

the yolk sac is gradually vascularized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vascularize - form blood vessels in tissue (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel) + '-ise', meaning to make or become.
