manorial

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɔriəl / (AmE), / məˈnɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a manor, especially as it existed in the feudal system; connected with the administration or affairs of a manor.
relating to a manor or its administration

Examples

The estate's manorial records provide valuable insight into medieval rural life.
She studied the manorial system as part of her history course.
The manorial court once settled disputes among the villagers.

Synset Examples

manorial accounts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manor - a large country house with lands; historically, the basic unit of territorial organization in the feudal system

Etymology

From manor + -ial, the latter from Latin -ialis, forming adjectives.
