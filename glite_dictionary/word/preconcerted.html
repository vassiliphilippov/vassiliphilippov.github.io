preconcerted

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːkənˈsɜːrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɜːtɪd / (BrE)
(of an action or plan) previously arranged or agreed on before being carried out.
previously arranged or agreed on

Examples

The two politicians met in a preconcerted manner to discuss the alliance.
They executed the robbery according to a preconcerted plan.
Her arrival at the precise moment was clearly preconcerted with the organizers.

Synset Examples

following preconcerted plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prearranged - arranged in advance
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without premeditation or planning

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'concertare' (to arrange by mutual agreement).
