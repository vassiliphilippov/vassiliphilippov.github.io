discoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔɪd / (BrE)
having a flat, circular shape or form; disk-shaped
having a flat, circular shape

Examples

The fungus produces discoid spores that are easily carried by the wind.
Archaeologists uncovered a discoid stone dating back to the Bronze Age.
The rash appeared as a discoid patch on the patient's cheek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk-shaped - having the shape of a disc or disk; round and flat

Etymology

From Greek 'diskoeidēs' (like a disk), from 'diskos' (disk).
