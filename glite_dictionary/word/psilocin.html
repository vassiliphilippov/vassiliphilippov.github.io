psilocin

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊsɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒsɪn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring psychedelic compound found in several species of mushrooms, responsible for their psychoactive effects
psychedelic compound in mushrooms

Examples

Researchers isolated psilocin from several varieties of hallucinogenic mushrooms for laboratory testing.
The legal status of psilocin varies widely around the world due to its psychoactive properties.
After ingestion, the body quickly converts psilocybin into psilocin, which produces its signature effects.

Synset Examples

The mind-altering effects of psilocin are highly variable and subjective and resemble those of LSD and DMT.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psilocybin - the phosphorylated precursor of psilocin in psychedelic mushrooms
  • Synonym: LSD - a synthetic psychedelic compound with similar effects to psilocin
  • Synonym: DMT - a naturally occurring psychedelic compound, similar in effects to psilocin

Etymology

From German Psilocin, coined from the genus Psilocybe (a type of mushroom) and -in (chemical suffix).
