anticoagulative

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.koʊˈɡjæ.lə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.kəʊˈɡjæ.lə.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the effect of preventing blood from clotting; concerning an anticoagulant
preventing blood from clotting
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anticoagulative therapy to reduce the risk of thrombosis.
Some plants contain natural anticoagulative compounds that may affect blood clotting.
Researchers are studying the anticoagulative properties of new synthetic drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticoagulant - a substance that prevents blood from clotting

Etymology

From 'anticoagulant' (from Latin 'anti-' meaning against and 'coagulare' meaning to curdle).
