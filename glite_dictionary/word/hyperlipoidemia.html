hyperlipoidemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pərˌlɪp.oʊ.aɪˈdi.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˌlɪp.əʊ.aɪˈdiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
the presence of abnormally high levels of lipids (fats) in the blood
excess lipids in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that hyperlipoidemia increases the risk of heart disease.
After several tests, she was diagnosed with hyperlipoidemia and prescribed medication.
Lifestyle changes can help manage hyperlipoidemia and lower cholesterol levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - abnormally high concentration of fats or lipids in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'lipos' (fat) + '-emia' (blood condition).
