capsulated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæpsjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkapsjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of seeds or spores) enclosed in a capsule.
enclosed in a capsule (biology)

Examples

The plant produces capsulated seeds that disperse when the capsule splits open.
Some fungi release their capsulated spores only under specific environmental conditions.
Researchers studied the germination rate of various capsulated species in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encapsulated - enclosed in a capsule

Etymology

From Latin 'capsula' (small box) + English suffix '-ated'.
