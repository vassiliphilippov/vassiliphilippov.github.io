impassive

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːsɪv / (BrE)
not showing or feeling any emotion; giving no sign of emotion.
not showing emotion

Examples

Despite the shocking news, her impassive expression did not change.
He remained impassive during the heated argument, never showing any anger or frustration.
The judge's impassive demeanor made it hard to guess what he was thinking.

Synset Examples

her impassive remoteness
he remained impassive, showing neither interest in nor concern for our plight
a silent stolid creature who took it all as a matter of course
her face showed nothing but stolid indifference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotionless - showing no emotion
  • Synonym: stolid - showing little or no emotion, often dull or unresponsive
  • Antonym: expressive - showing or capable of showing feeling

Etymology

From Latin impassivus (not susceptible to feeling), from in- (not) + passivus (capable of feeling or suffering).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːsɪv / (BrE)
showing no emotion or feeling; not revealing any thoughts or feelings in one's expression or manner
showing no emotion or feeling

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, Maria remained impassive and focused on her task.
He listened to the bad news with an impassive expression, giving nothing away.
The judge's impassive face made it impossible to know which way she was leaning.

Synset Examples

deadpan humor
his face remained expressionless as the verdict was read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressionless - not showing any emotion through facial expression
  • Antonym: emotional - showing or expressing strong feelings

Etymology

From Latin impassivus (not suffering), from in- (not) + passivus (capable of feeling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503225-s
Synset POS: s
