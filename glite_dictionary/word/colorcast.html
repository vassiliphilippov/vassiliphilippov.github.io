to colorcast

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlɚˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈkʌləˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to broadcast television or a program in color rather than in black and white
broadcast in color (television)

Examples

The network began to colorcast its evening news in the late 1960s.
They plan to colorcast the entire cycle of shows, upgrading the production value.
After decades of black-and-white television, people were excited to see their favorite programs colorcast for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program by radio or television

Etymology

From 'color' + 'broadcast', emerging in the early days of color television.
