mombin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑm.bɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒm.bɪn / (BrE)
A common tropical American shrub or small tree that produces purplish edible fruit, also known as the fruit itself.
tropical American fruit-bearing tree or shrub

Examples

During her trip to Brazil, she tasted a fresh mombin for the first time.
The local market sells ripe mombins alongside bananas and mangoes.
The juice from the mombin fruit is often made into refreshing drinks in tropical regions.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'mombim', of Tupi origin.

2. NOUN

/ mɑmˈbɪn / (AmE), / mɒmˈbɪn / (BrE)
A tropical fruit, either yellow or purplish, that grows on trees of the Spondias genus, native to the Americas and West Africa.
a purplish or yellow tropical fruit

Examples

The market vendor offered us a basket of fresh mombin among other exotic fruits.
I've never tasted a mombin before, but I heard its flavor is both tart and sweet.
During our trip to Brazil, we enjoyed a delicious juice made from mombin.

Etymology

From French *mombin*, from a West African language, possibly Yoruba.
