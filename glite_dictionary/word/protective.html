protective

1. ADJ

/ prəˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
designed or intended to protect someone or something from harm or danger
intended to protect from harm or danger

Examples

The workers wore protective clothing to shield themselves from chemicals.
She has a very protective attitude toward her younger brother.
Applying a protective coating to the metal will help prevent rust.

Synset Examples

a protective covering
the use of protective masks and equipment
protective coatings
kept the drunken sailor in protective custody
animals with protective coloring
protective tariffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive - intended to defend or protect
  • Antonym: vulnerable - exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed

Etymology

From Latin 'protectivus', from 'protegere' (to cover in front).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
showing care or concern and a willingness to shield someone or something from harm or criticism, often followed by 'of' (e.g., protective of his reputation)
tending to shield or guard; caring

Examples

She was always protective of her younger brother at school.
Managers are often protective of their staff’s privacy.
He became extremely protective of his creative work after the theft.

Synset Examples

protective of his reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive - quick to defend oneself or another from criticism or attack

Etymology

From French *protectif*, from Latin *protectus*, past participle of *protegere* (to cover in front).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311985-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ prəˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
Wanting to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger.
wanting to keep safe from harm

Examples

She is always very protective of her younger brother.
The cat became protective when it sensed danger nearby.
Her protective instincts made her an excellent nurse.

Synset Examples

a protective mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive - behaving in a way intended to protect from harm
  • Antonym: careless - not giving sufficient care or attention

Etymology

From protect + -ive, ultimately from Latin 'protectus' (protected).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02458250-s
Synset POS: s
