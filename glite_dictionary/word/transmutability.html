transmutability

1. NOUN

/ trænzˌmjutəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / trænzˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to change from one form, nature, or substance into another.
ability to be changed into a different form

Examples

The transmutability of certain metals fascinated ancient alchemists.
Her research focuses on the transmutability of cultural traditions over generations.
Philosophers have long debated the transmutability of the human soul.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
  • Synonym: convertibility - the quality of being able to be converted into another form

Etymology

From 'transmute' + '-ability', ultimately from Latin 'transmutare' (to change across).
