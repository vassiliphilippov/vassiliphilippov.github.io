to transcend

1. VERB

/ trænˈsɛnd / (AmE), / trænˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to go beyond the limits of something; to be greater in scope, size, or importance than a particular standard or limit
go beyond a limit or standard

Examples

Her generosity seems to transcend all expectations, inspiring everyone around her.
The beauty of the mountains can transcend language and culture.
The theory aims to transcend the traditional boundaries of physics.

Synset Examples

Their loyalty exceeds their national bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to be greater than a number or amount; to go beyond a limit

Etymology

From Latin transcendere, from trans- (across) + scandere (to climb).

2. VERB

/ trænˈsɛnd / (AmE), / trænˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to be or go beyond the limits of something; to surpass or be superior to a standard or limit
to surpass or go beyond limits

Examples

His achievements truly transcend those of his peers in the field.
The beauty of her music transcends cultural boundaries and language barriers.
In difficult times, people often transcend their own limits to help others.

Synset Examples

She exceeded our expectations
She topped her performance of last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to be greater than a particular number or amount; to go beyond a limit
  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
  • Antonym: to fall short - to not reach the standard or amount needed

Etymology

From Latin transcendere, from trans- (across) + scandere (to climb).
