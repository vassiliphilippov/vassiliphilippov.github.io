graven

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvən / (BrE)
cut, carved, or impressed into a surface; engraved.
carved or engraved into a surface

Examples

The museum displayed several graven tablets from ancient civilizations.
He admired the graven letters on the old monument.
A graven image hung above the fireplace, its details sharp and deep.

Synset Examples

an incised design
engraved invitations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - cut or carved into a surface; marked deeply and firmly

Etymology

Past participle of 'grave' (to carve), from Old English *grafan* (to dig, carve).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvən / (BrE)
cut or carved into a desired shape, especially in stone or wood; sculptured.
sculpted or carved into shape
archaic

Examples

The ancient temple was adorned with graven lions and mythical creatures.
He warned them not to worship graven images, as forbidden in their faith.
The artist's graven figures seemed almost lifelike in their detail.

Synset Examples

graven images
sculptured representations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - cut or carved into the surface of something; inscribed
  • Synonym: sculpted - shaped or carved as a sculpture
  • Antonym: unworked - not shaped or carved by tools

Etymology

Past participle of the archaic verb 'grave,' from Old English *grafan* (to carve, engrave).
