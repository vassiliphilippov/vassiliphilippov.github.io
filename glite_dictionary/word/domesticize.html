to domesticize

1. VERB

/ dəˈmɛstɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a wild animal or plant) docile, tractable, or suitable for domestic life; to tame
make something docile or tame

Examples

It took several generations to domesticize the once-feral horses on the island.
The zookeeper managed to domesticize the wild fox over time.
Researchers are studying ways to domesticize certain wild plants for agriculture.

Synset Examples

He tames lions for the circus
reclaim falcons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tame - to make an animal less wild; to domesticate
  • Synonym: to domesticate - to adapt (an animal or plant) for domestic use or life

Etymology

Formed from 'domestic' + '-ize', following the pattern of verbs indicating transformation.
