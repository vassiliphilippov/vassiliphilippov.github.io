to tranquilize

1. VERB

/ ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtraŋkwɪlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something calm, especially by giving a drug; to make calm or still
make calm, often with a drug

Examples

The veterinarian had to tranquilize the animal before the surgery.
Strong medication was used to tranquilize the patient during the procedure.
Park rangers often tranquilize wild animals to safely relocate them.

Synset Examples

quiet the dragons of worry and fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - to make or become calm
  • Antonym: to agitate - to disturb or upset someone

Etymology

From French 'tranquilliser', from 'tranquille' (calm, quiet).

2. VERB

/ ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtraŋkwɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal calm or quiet, especially by giving a drug
make calm or quiet with a drug

Examples

The veterinarian decided to tranquilize the injured lion before moving it to safety.
Doctors sometimes tranquilize patients before minor surgical procedures to reduce anxiety.
The team tranquilized the wild horse to transport it safely to the sanctuary.

Synset Examples

The patient must be sedated before the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedate - to calm or quiet, especially by administering a sedative
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to encourage activity or arouse, especially physiologically

Etymology

From French *tranquiliser*, from Latin *tranquillus* (quiet, calm).
