marquess

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑr.kwəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.kwɪs / (BrE)
a British nobleman ranking below a duke and above an earl
British noble rank below duke
british

Examples

The marquess inherited several large estates from his father.
During the ceremony, the marquess was seated next to the duke.
She researched her ancestry and discovered a distant marquess in the family line.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duke - a nobleman of the highest hereditary rank
  • Degree Lower: earl - a British noble ranking below a marquess

Etymology

From Old French 'marquis', from medieval Latin 'marchionis' (lord of a border region).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrkwəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkwɪs / (BrE)
A nobleman in certain countries who ranks above a count and below a duke.
a nobleman ranking above a count

Examples

He was granted the title of marquess after years of loyal service to the crown.
The marquess presided over the estate's annual banquet with great dignity.
Historically, a marquess was responsible for defending border territories.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duke - a nobleman ranking highest below a monarch
  • Degree Lower: count - a nobleman ranking below a marquess
  • Synonym: marquis - alternative spelling of 'marquess' (chiefly used in continental Europe)

Etymology

From Old French 'marquis', from Medieval Latin 'marcensis' (ruler of a border territory).
