recantation

1. NOUN

/ riˌkænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌkænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally withdrawing or publicly disavowing a previously held statement, belief, or opinion
formal withdrawal of a statement or belief

Examples

After public outcry, the politician issued a recantation of his earlier comments.
The scientist's recantation shocked the academic community, as his original theory had gained widespread support.
She refused to sign a recantation, standing by her beliefs despite the pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retraction - the act of withdrawing a statement or accusation
  • Synonym: denial - a statement that something is not true
  • Antonym: affirmation - a statement declaring something is true

Etymology

From Late Latin recantatio, from Latin recantare ‘to recall, revoke’ (re- ‘back’ + cantare ‘to sing’).
