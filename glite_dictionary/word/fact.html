fact

1. NOUN

/ fækt / (AmE), / fækt / (BrE)
A thing that is known or proved to be true.
something known or proven to be true

Examples

It is a fact that water boils at 100 degrees Celsius at sea level.
The lawyer argued that the facts supported his client's innocence.
Before we make a decision, let's be sure we know all the facts.

Synset Examples

scientific hypotheses are not facts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fiction - something invented or imagined, not true

Etymology

From Latin 'factum' (a thing done), from 'facere' (to do).

2. NOUN

/ fækt / (AmE), / fækt / (BrE)
A thing that is known or proved to be true, especially an event or situation that actually happened or existed.
something known to be true

Examples

It's important to check whether a statement is supported by fact before sharing it.
Many people confuse opinion with fact in online debates.
The report is based entirely on fact, not speculation.

Synset Examples

your fears have no basis in fact
how much of the story is fact and how much fiction is hard to tell

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fiction - something invented or imagined, not factual
  • Synonym: truth - the state of being true or in accordance with fact

Etymology

From Latin 'factum' (a deed, an act), from 'facere' (to do, make).

3. NOUN

/ fækt / (AmE), / fækt / (BrE)
A statement that can be shown to be true or that has been verified as correct.
verified statement; truth

Examples

He presented the facts to support his case in court.
It is a fact that water boils at 100 degrees Celsius under normal conditions.
The magazine article was full of interesting facts about the animal kingdom.

Synset Examples

he supported his argument with an impressive array of facts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opinion - a belief or judgment that is not based on proof

Etymology

From Latin factum (deed, thing done), from facere (to do).

4. NOUN

/ fækt / (AmE), / fækt / (BrE)
a piece of information that is known to be true or has really occurred, especially as opposed to opinions or beliefs
a true piece of information or event

Examples

It is a fact that water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
The jury must base its decision on the facts presented in court.
She insisted that her story was based entirely on fact, not speculation.

Synset Examples

first you must collect all the facts of the case

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opinion - a belief or judgment not necessarily based on fact
  • Antonym: fiction - something invented or imagined; not fact

Etymology

From Latin 'factum' meaning 'a thing done,' from 'facere' (to do).
