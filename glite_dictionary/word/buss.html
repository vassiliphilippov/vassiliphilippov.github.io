buss

1. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
an old-fashioned or literary word for a kiss; the act of touching or caressing with the lips
an old-fashioned word for kiss
archaic

Examples

She gave him a gentle buss on the cheek before leaving.
In the poem, lovers exchanged a secret buss beneath the rose bush.
The children giggled after sharing a quick buss on the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiss - the act of touching with the lips as a sign of love, affection, or greeting

Etymology

From Middle English bus, likely imitative of the sound of a kiss or from Dutch 'bus' (a kiss).
