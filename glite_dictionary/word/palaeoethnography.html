palaeoethnography

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˌɛθˈnɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˌɛθˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The study of the cultures and ways of life of Paleolithic humans, as reconstructed from archaeological remains.
study of Paleolithic human cultures

Examples

She specializes in palaeoethnography, focusing on the daily lives of early humans in Europe.
Advances in palaeoethnography have revealed much about tool use among Paleolithic societies.
The museum's new exhibit highlights the importance of palaeoethnography in understanding human prehistory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ethnography - the systematic study and recording of human cultures

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + ethnos (people, nation) + -graphy (writing, study of).
