cha-cha

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑˌtʃɑ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.tʃɑː / (BrE)
to dance the cha-cha, a lively Latin American dance with a syncopated rhythm
to dance the cha-cha

Examples

Every Saturday night, they cha-cha at the local dance club.
She cha-chaed across the stage with perfect rhythm during her performance.
We're going to learn how to cha-cha in our next dance class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

From the name of the cha-cha dance, which originates from Cuban Spanish 'cha cha', imitating the sound of the maracas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑ ˌtʃɑ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː ˌtʃɑː / (BrE)
a lively modern ballroom dance of Latin American origin characterized by small steps and swaying hip movements
lively Latin American ballroom dance

Examples

The couple performed a flawless cha-cha at the dance competition last night.
Learning the basic steps of the cha-cha can be both fun and challenging for beginners.
She enjoys dancing the cha-cha at Latin music nights in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a ballroom dance of Cuban origin with a slow rhythm

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish, imitating the sound of the rhythm used in the music.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00536594-n
Synset POS: n
