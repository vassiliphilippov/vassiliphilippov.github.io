Nicholas I

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪkələs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈnɪkələs ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
Czar of Russia from 1825 to 1855, known for leading Russia into the Crimean War.
Russian czar (1825–1855)

Examples

Nicholas I ruled Russia during a period of intense political repression and expansion.
The outbreak of the Crimean War occurred under the reign of Nicholas I.
Historians often debate the legacy of Nicholas I in Russian and European history.
