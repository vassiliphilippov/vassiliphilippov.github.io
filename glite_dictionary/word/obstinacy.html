obstinacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑbstɪnəsi / (AmE), / ˈɒbstɪnəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being stubborn and refusing to change one's opinion or behavior; stubborn persistence
stubbornness; refusal to yield

Examples

Her obstinacy made it impossible to reach a compromise during the meeting.
Despite everyone's advice, his obstinacy drove him to continue on the same path.
The obstinacy of the old mule frustrated the farmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn; refusal to change one's mind
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or compromise

Etymology

From Latin obstinacia (steadfastness, stubbornness), from obstinatus, past participle of obstinare (to persist).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑbstɪnəsi / (AmE), / ˈɒbstɪnəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being stubbornly persistent in adhering to one's own ideas or desires
stubborn persistence

Examples

Her obstinacy made it impossible to reach a compromise.
Despite all evidence, his obstinacy prevented him from admitting he was wrong.
The negotiations failed because of the obstinacy of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being unwilling to change one's mind or actions
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt

Etymology

From Latin obstinatia (firmness, stubbornness), from obstinatus (resolved, determined).
