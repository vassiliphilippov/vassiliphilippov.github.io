Godhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌhɛd / (BrE)
the essential nature or divine status of God, especially in Judeo-Christian theology; God regarded as the supreme being.
the divine nature or essence of God

Examples

Many philosophers have debated the true nature of the Godhead.
The doctrine of the Trinity describes three persons in one Godhead.
Art from the Renaissance often depicted the Godhead in symbolic form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • Synonym: divinity - the state or quality of being divine; deity

Etymology

From Middle English godhed (divinity), from Old English god + -hed (state, condition).
