Bhagavad-Gita

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːɡəvəd ˈɡiːtɑː / (AmE), / ˌbʌɡəvəd ˈɡiːtə / (BrE)
a Hindu sacred text, part of the Mahabharata, presenting a spiritual dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna on duty, righteousness, and the meaning of life
sacred Hindu scripture and philosophical text

Examples

Many consider the Bhagavad-Gita to be one of the most important spiritual classics in the world.
He quoted from the Bhagavad-Gita during his graduation speech to emphasize the importance of duty.
The teachings of the Bhagavad-Gita have inspired philosophers and leaders for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahabharata - ancient Sanskrit epic from India, containing the Bhagavad-Gita

Etymology

From Sanskrit *bhagavad-gītā* meaning 'song of the Lord.'
