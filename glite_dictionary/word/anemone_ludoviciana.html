Anemone ludoviciana

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛmoʊni ˌluːdəˌvɪsiˈænə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni ˌluːdəˌvɪsiˈænə / (BrE)
A short, hairy perennial plant with blue-violet or lilac flowers that bloom in early spring, native to North America and Siberia.
a perennial plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The Anemone ludoviciana blooms early in the spring, covering meadows with shades of blue-violet.
Many gardeners value Anemone ludoviciana for its delicate appearance and hardiness in cold climates.
You can find Anemone ludoviciana thriving in the wild across parts of North America and Siberia.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Anemone' from Greek 'anemōnē' (windflower), species epithet 'ludoviciana' indicating 'of Louisiana' or 'of St. Louis'.
