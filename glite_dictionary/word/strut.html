strut

1. NOUN

/ strʌt / (AmE), / strʌt / (BrE)
A proud and stiff way of walking, often intended to draw attention or display self-importance.
a proud, self-important walk

Examples

He walked into the room with a noticeable strut, clearly enjoying the attention.
The champion's strut across the stage showed his confidence.
Her exaggerated strut made everyone smile during the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - a confident and sometimes arrogant walk

Etymology

From Old English *strūtian* (to stand out stiffly).

2. VERB

/ strʌt / (AmE), / strʌt / (BrE)
to walk in a proud or confident way, often to attract attention or show off
walk in a proud, showy manner

Examples

He loves to strut down the hallway as if he owns the place.
The actors strutted onto the stage, exuding confidence and charm.
Peacocks strut their colorful feathers to attract attention.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - to walk in a very confident and arrogant or aggressive way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English strouten (to bulge out, swell), related to Old English strūtian (to stand out stiffly).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ strʌt / (AmE), / strʌt / (BrE)
a bar or rod used to support or strengthen a structure by resisting longitudinal compression
supporting bar or rod

Examples

The engineers installed a steel strut to reinforce the bridge's framework.
A wooden strut was used to keep the ceiling from collapsing during repairs.
Aircraft wings often contain a strut for added stability and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - a device that supports or holds something together

Etymology

From Old English *strūtian* (to stand out stiffly).
