unsupported

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈpɔr.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈpɔː.tɪd / (BrE)
not held up or kept in position by a support; lacking physical or structural support.
not held up; lacking support

Examples

Heavy snow caused the roof to collapse when it was left unsupported by beams.
The shelf remained unsupported after the bracket broke.
The structure is dangerous because many floors are unsupported.

Synset Examples

removal of the central post left the roof unsupported

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supported - held up or kept in position by something

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + supported, from support (from Old French supporter, from Latin supportare 'to carry under').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02360857-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈpɔr.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈpɔː.tɪd / (BrE)
not backed by evidence, justification, or proof; not upheld or corroborated.
not supported by evidence or proof

Examples

The report made several unsupported claims about the company's financial health.
Her argument was filled with unsupported assumptions that weakened her position.
He dismissed the unsupported rumors as baseless gossip.

Synset Examples

unsupported accusations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsubstantiated - not supported by evidence; unproven

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'supported'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02361582-a
Synset POS: a
