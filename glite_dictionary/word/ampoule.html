ampoule

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpul / (AmE), / ˈæm.pjuːl / (BrE)
A small sealed glass or plastic container, typically holding a single dose of a drug for injection.
sealed container for injectable drugs
medical

Examples

The nurse carefully broke the ampoule before drawing the medication into the syringe.
Each ampoule contains a single, sterile dose intended for immediate use.
Doctors often use a ampoule to store and administer certain vaccines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vial - A small container, typically cylindrical and made of glass, used for holding liquid medicines

Etymology

From French 'ampoule', from Latin 'ampulla' (a small flask).
