ropiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.pin.əs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.pi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being cohesive and sticky, resembling or forming threads or strands
quality of being cohesive and sticky

Examples

The ropiness of the melted cheese made it perfect for pizza.
Some soups can develop ropiness if left standing too long in the pan.
The ropiness in certain yogurts results from natural bacterial cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickiness - the quality of being sticky
  • Synonym: viscosity - thickness or resistance to flow; a measure of stickiness in fluids

Etymology

From 'ropy' (like rope) + '-ness'.
