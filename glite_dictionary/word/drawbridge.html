drawbridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔːˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bridge that can be raised, lowered, or drawn aside to prevent or allow passage, especially to permit boats or ships to pass beneath it or to block access.
a bridge that can be raised or lowered

Examples

The castle was protected by a drawbridge that was raised every night for security.
The boat could pass through the canal only after the drawbridge was lifted.
Children watched in awe as the drawbridge slowly lowered to let cars across.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suspension bridge - a bridge supported by cables suspended from towers
  • Antonym: fixed bridge - a stationary, non-movable bridge

Etymology

From Middle English 'drawebrigge', from 'draw' + 'bridge', referring to its ability to be drawn up.
