Selaginella eremophila

1. NOUN

/ sɛləˌdʒɪnələ ˌɛrəˈmɒfɪlə / (AmE), / sɛləˌdʒɪnɪlə ˌɛrəˈmɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of prostrate spikemoss native to California, characterized by spreading, moss-like growth and small, scale-like leaves.
species of prostrate spikemoss native to California

Examples

The rare Selaginella eremophila thrives in the arid region of southern California.
Botanists conducted a survey of Selaginella eremophila populations along the desert trail.
Due to its unique adaptations, Selaginella eremophila can survive with minimal water in sandy soils.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Selaginella' is a genus name from Latin 'selago', and 'eremophila' is from Greek 'eremos' (desert) and 'philos' (loving).
