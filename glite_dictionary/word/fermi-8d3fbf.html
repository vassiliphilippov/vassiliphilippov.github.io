fermi

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrmi / (AmE), / ˈfɛːmi / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth of a meter (10⁻¹⁵ meters), used especially in nuclear physics.
one quadrillionth of a meter

Examples

The diameter of a typical atomic nucleus is about 10 fermis.
Physicists measure subatomic distances in units such as the fermi.
A proton is roughly one fermi in size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femtometer - a metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth of a meter

Etymology

Named after Italian-American physicist Enrico Fermi (1901–1954).
