Apalachicola

1. PROPN

/ ˌæpələˌtʃɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌæpələˌtʃɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
a river in northwestern Florida formed by the confluence of the Chattahoochee and Flint rivers, flowing south to the Gulf of Mexico
river in northwestern Florida

Examples

The Apalachicola is known for its rich biodiversity and vital role in the local ecosystem.
We crossed the Apalachicola River while driving through northwestern Florida last summer.
The Apalachicola flows into the Gulf of Mexico, supporting important fisheries along the coast.

Etymology

From a Native American (Muskogean) name of uncertain origin.
