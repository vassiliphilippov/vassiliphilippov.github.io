lucrativeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.krə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluː.krə.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of producing profit or financial gain
profitability; ability to generate profit

Examples

The lucrativeness of the new venture attracted numerous investors.
She was drawn to the lucrativeness of careers in technology.
Analysts questioned the future lucrativeness of the oil industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitability - the quality of yielding profit
  • Antonym: unprofitability - the quality of not yielding profit

Etymology

From Latin lucrativus (profitable), from lucrari (to gain), from lucrum (gain, profit).
