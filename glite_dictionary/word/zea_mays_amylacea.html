Zea mays amylacea

1. NOUN

/ ziː.ə ˈmeɪz ˌæm.ɪˈleɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ziː.ə ˈmeɪz ˌæm.ɪˈleɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A variety of corn with kernels composed almost entirely of soft starch, used mainly for flour.
type of corn with soft, starchy kernels

Examples

Traditional South American cuisines use Zea mays amylacea to make various types of flour and breads.
Scientists analyzed the starch content of Zea mays amylacea to improve crop yields.
The local market sells several varieties of maize, including Zea mays amylacea for specialty recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour corn - corn with soft, starchy kernels, often ground into flour

Etymology

From scientific Latin: Zea (genus name), mays (species name, maize), amylacea (from Latin amylaceus, 'starchy').
