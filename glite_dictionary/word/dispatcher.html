dispatcher

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃə / (BrE)
an official responsible for signaling the beginning of a race or competition
race official who starts events

Examples

The dispatcher raised his flag and the runners took their positions at the starting line.
After a brief announcement, the dispatcher fired the starting gun for the marathon.
The horse race began as soon as the dispatcher signaled the start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starter - official who gives the signal to start a race

Etymology

From the verb 'dispatch' (send off quickly), with suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃər / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃə / (BrE)
an employee whose job is to assign vehicles or workers to routes, tasks, or locations, especially in transportation or emergency services, ensuring efficient operation
person assigning vehicles or personnel

Examples

The dispatcher coordinated the delivery trucks to avoid delays during the snowstorm.
Before leaving the station, the bus driver checked in with the dispatcher.
As a dispatcher, her quick decisions kept the entire fleet running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - person who directs the movement or activity of vehicles or personnel

Etymology

From the verb 'dispatch,' meaning to send off, from Spanish 'despachar' or Italian 'dispacciare.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10037147-n
Synset POS: n
