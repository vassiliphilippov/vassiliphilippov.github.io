chemoreceptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌkiːmoʊrɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊrɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
relating to or capable of detecting chemical stimuli, especially through chemoreceptors.
detecting chemical stimuli
medical

Examples

Certain fish have highly chemoreceptive organs that allow them to detect prey in murky water.
The olfactory system is a classic example of a chemoreceptive sense in mammals.
Specialized chemoreceptive cells in the nose respond to airborne chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemoreceptor - a sensory cell or organ responsive to chemical stimuli

Etymology

From chemo- (chemical) + receptive (receiving stimuli).
