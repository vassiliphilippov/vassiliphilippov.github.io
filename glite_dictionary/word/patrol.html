patrol

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the act of regularly watching or traveling through an area to maintain order or security
regular security activity in an area

Examples

The security team made a patrol of the building every two hours.
Forest rangers conduct a patrol each morning to check for signs of fire.
He volunteered for nightly patrol to help keep the neighborhood safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - to watch over for protection or security

Etymology

From French *patrouille*, from patrouiller 'to paddle in mud', probably of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00826469-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to guard or monitor an area by moving around it regularly to maintain security or order
to guard an area by moving around it

Examples

The police patrolled the neighborhood every night to keep it safe.
Soldiers patrol the border to prevent illegal crossings.
Security officers were patrolling the building after the alarm was triggered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - to protect or watch over an area or person

Etymology

From French *patrouiller* (to paddle, prowl, patrol).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02460361-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a group of people or vehicles that regularly travel around an area to check that it is safe and secure
group performing security checks

Examples

A police patrol drives through the neighborhood every hour to deter crime.
The security patrol reported no unusual activity during their rounds last night.
A border patrol was dispatched to monitor the national frontier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person or group that protects a place or people
  • Synonym: sentry - a soldier stationed to keep watch

Etymology

From French 'patrouille', meaning 'a patrol', from 'patrouiller' (to patrol).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08346040-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a group of people, especially police officers or soldiers, assigned to guard an area or gather information by moving around it
group assigned to guard or scout an area

Examples

A patrol was sent out at dawn to survey the area for threats.
The border patrol discovered several unauthorized crossings during the night.
A police patrol regularly drives through the neighborhood to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squad - a small organized group of soldiers, often for a specific task

Etymology

From French 'patrouille', probably from 'patrouiller' (to tramp about).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08233097-n
Synset POS: n
