Loganiaceae

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌɡæniˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / lɒˌɡæniˈeɪsi.i / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous flowering plants in the order Gentianales, including trees, shrubs, and lianas, some of which are poisonous.
family of dicotyledonous flowering plants
medical

Examples

Many species within the Loganiaceae family contain alkaloids that can be toxic to humans and animals.
The Loganiaceae are distributed primarily in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
Strychnos nux-vomica, a member of the Loganiaceae, is the source of the poison strychnine.

Etymology

From the genus name Logania, named after James Logan (1674–1751), Scottish scientist and botanist, with Latin family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˌɡæniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ləʊˌɡænɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous flowering plants in the order Gentianales, comprising shrubs, trees, and climbers found mainly in tropical regions
family of tropical flowering plants

Examples

Many poisonous species belong to the Loganiaceae family.
The Loganiaceae include trees, shrubs, and woody climbers native mainly to tropical regions.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several Loganiaceae plants.

Etymology

From genus name *Logania* + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
