crossly

1. ADV

/ ˈkrɔsli / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsli / (BrE)
In a manner showing annoyance or anger; in an ill-natured or irritated way.
in an annoyed or angry manner

Examples

She spoke crossly to her brother after he broke her favorite mug.
The teacher looked crossly at the students who were whispering in class.
He answered crossly, making it clear he didn’t want to discuss the issue.

Synset Examples

she looked at her husband crossly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - in a manner showing anger
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm and composed manner

Etymology

From 'cross' (ill-tempered) + -ly (adverb suffix).
