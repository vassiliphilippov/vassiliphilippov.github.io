Brahminism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbræmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
The religious and social system associated with orthodox Hinduism, emphasizing the role and authority of Brahmins.
orthodox Hindu religious and social system

Examples

Many historical texts discuss the influence of Brahminism on ancient Indian society.
The rituals and practices of Brahminism were central to the Vedic period.
Modern Hinduism retains some beliefs and customs rooted in Brahminism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hinduism - The major religion and cultural tradition of South Asia, developed from Vedic religion

Etymology

From 'Brahmin' (a Hindu caste) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbræmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
The ancient religious system of India based on the teachings and rituals of the Vedas, Brahmanas, and Upanishads, emphasizing the role of the priestly Brahmin caste.
ancient Indian religious system based on Vedic texts

Examples

The influence of Brahminism can be seen in many Hindu rituals and ceremonies today.
Ancient Indian society was deeply shaped by the philosophies and social structures of Brahminism.
Scholars often study Brahminism to understand the origins of Hindu religious beliefs.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'brāhmaṇa' (relating to Brahmins), via English 'Brahmin'.
