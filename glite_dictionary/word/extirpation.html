extirpation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkstərˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstəˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a body part or tissue
surgical removal of tissue or organ
medical

Examples

The complete extirpation of the tumor was necessary to prevent recurrence.
Following extirpation of the infected tissue, the patient's recovery was rapid.
Advances in surgery have made the extirpation of diseased organs much safer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excision - the act of cutting out or removing tissue surgically

Etymology

From Latin extirpatio, from extirpare (to root out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.stɚˈpeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.stəˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the complete removal or destruction of something; the act of eradicating or pulling out by the roots
total removal or eradication

Examples

The extirpation of invasive species from the island restored balance to the ecosystem.
Efforts toward the extirpation of smallpox were ultimately successful.
The government's harsh policies led to the extirpation of many cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eradication - the act of destroying or removing something completely

Etymology

From Latin extirpatio (rooting out), from exstirpare (to root out).
