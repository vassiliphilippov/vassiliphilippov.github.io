meperidine

1. NOUN

/ məˈpɛrəˌdin / (AmE), / mɛˈpɛrɪdiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic narcotic drug used to treat moderate to severe pain, also known by the trade name Demerol
a synthetic narcotic painkiller (Demerol)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed meperidine to manage the patient's post-surgical pain.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking meperidine for an extended period.
The nurse checked the dosage of meperidine before administering the injection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pethidine - British name for meperidine, the same synthetic narcotic drug
  • Synonym: morphine - a natural narcotic painkiller, often compared to meperidine
