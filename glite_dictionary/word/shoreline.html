shoreline

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔːrˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the line where a body of land meets a body of water, such as a sea, lake, or river
the boundary between land and water

Examples

We walked along the shoreline collecting seashells and enjoying the sound of the waves.
After the storm, debris was scattered all over the shoreline.
The birds gathered on the shoreline at sunset, looking for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - the land next to the sea
  • Synonym: waterfront - the land along a body of water

Etymology

Formed from 'shore' + 'line', both from Old English roots.
