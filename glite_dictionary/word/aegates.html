Aegates

1. PROPN

/ iːˈɡeɪtiːz / (AmE), / iːˈɡɑːtiːz / (BrE)
a group of small islands off the west coast of Sicily in the Mediterranean Sea
islands off Sicily in the Mediterranean

Examples

The naval Battle of the Aegates Islands ended the First Punic War.
The Aegates are known for their picturesque coastlines and crystal-clear waters.
Ancient historians frequently referenced the Aegates when discussing Roman and Carthaginian history.

Etymology

From Latin "Aegates" (plural), the name of the islands in classical antiquity.
