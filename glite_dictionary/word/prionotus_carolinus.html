Prionotus carolinus

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈoʊnətəs ˌkæroʊˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈɒnətəs ˌkærəˈlaɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of large sea robin fish found in coastal waters from Nova Scotia to Florida in the western Atlantic Ocean.
large sea robin fish species

Examples

The Prionotus carolinus is often caught by anglers along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Prionotus carolinus in estuarine environments.
While swimming off Cape Cod, we spotted a colorful Prionotus carolinus near the sandy bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea robin - a common name for marine fish in the family Triglidae, known for large pectoral fins

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Prionotus', the genus name, and 'carolinus', referring to the Carolinas in the United States.
