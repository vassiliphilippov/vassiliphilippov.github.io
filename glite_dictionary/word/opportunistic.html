opportunistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːpərtuːˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˌtjuːˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Taking advantage of opportunities as they arise, often without regard for principles or ethics.
taking unprincipled advantage of opportunities

Examples

Some investors take an opportunistic approach, buying stocks only when prices suddenly drop.
His opportunistic behavior during the crisis angered his colleagues.
The opportunistic thief stole unattended bags at every opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities, often unethically
  • Antonym: principled - acting in accordance with moral or ethical principles

Etymology

From 'opportunity' + '-istic', patterned after 'opportunist'.
