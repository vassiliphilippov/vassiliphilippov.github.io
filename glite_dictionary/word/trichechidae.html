Trichechidae

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɛkɪˌdi / (AmE), / trɪˈkɛkɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic mammals comprising only the manatees.
family of aquatic mammals (manatees)

Examples

The family Trichechidae consists exclusively of the three species of manatees.
Scientists have studied the evolution of Trichechidae to better understand marine mammal adaptation.
Unlike dugongs, members of Trichechidae inhabit rivers as well as coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large aquatic mammal in the family Trichechidae

Etymology

From New Latin Trichechus (genus name for manatee) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
