pageboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒ.bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒ.bɔɪ / (BrE)
a hairstyle in which the hair is cut straight and smooth with the ends curled under, usually extending to the jaw or shoulders
straight hairstyle with ends curled under

Examples

She wore her hair in a classic pageboy for the elegant event.
The pageboy was a popular choice among actresses in the 1950s.
After the haircut, his new pageboy style gave him a fresh look.

Etymology

Named after the hairstyles traditionally worn by page boys in Medieval European courts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒ.bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒ.bɔɪ / (BrE)
a boy employed to run errands or act as a messenger, especially in a hotel or large house
boy messenger or errand-runner

Examples

The hotel pageboy delivered the message to my room within minutes.
A young pageboy in a smart uniform waited by the entrance to assist guests.
In historical dramas, a pageboy often carries notes for the nobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: page - a young male attendant or servant

Etymology

From 'page' (a young male servant or attendant) + 'boy'.
