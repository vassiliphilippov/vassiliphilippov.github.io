Turbatrix aceti

1. NOUN

/ tɝːˈbætrɪks əˈsiːtaɪ / (AmE), / tɜːˈbætrɪks əˈsiːtaɪ / (BrE)
a minute nematode worm, commonly known as the vinegar eel, that feeds on microorganisms in fermenting substances such as vinegar
a nematode worm found in vinegar

Examples

Turbatrix aceti are often found in unfiltered vinegar, feeding on bacteria and yeast.
Scientists use Turbatrix aceti as a model organism in various biological studies.
If vinegar appears cloudy, it may be due to the presence of Turbatrix aceti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinegar eel - common name for Turbatrix aceti, a nematode found in vinegar

Etymology

From Latin *turbatrix*, meaning 'female disturber', and *aceti*, meaning 'of vinegar'.
