alteration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːltəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a change or modification in a state, condition, or form
a change or modification

Examples

The company made a significant alteration to its marketing strategy last year.
Any alteration in the schedule must be approved by the manager.
There was a noticeable alteration in his tone after the meeting.

Synset Examples

the change was intended to increase sales
this storm is certainly a change for the worse
the neighborhood had undergone few modifications since his last visit years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modification - a small change or adjustment to something
  • Synonym: change - the act or instance of making or becoming different
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being unchanged or steady

Etymology

From Latin 'alteratio', from 'alterare' (to change), from 'alter' (other).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔltəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːltəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making changes to something, especially by revising or rethinking it
the act of making changes; revision

Examples

Any alteration to the contract must be approved by both parties.
The dress needed a slight alteration to fit perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revision - the process of reconsidering something and making changes to it

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔl.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːl.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making changes to something, especially so that it is different from its original state
the act of changing something

Examples

The alteration to the contract was approved by both parties.
After the alteration of her dress, it fit her perfectly.
Significant alteration of the landscape was necessary to build the new road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modification - the act of making something slightly different
  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of changing to suit a new purpose
  • Antonym: unchanged - remaining in the same condition

Etymology

From Latin 'alteratio', from 'alterare' (to change), from 'alter' (other).
