distal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəl / (BrE)
(anatomy) situated away from the center or midline of the body or from the point of attachment.
anatomy: farther from the body's center
medical

Examples

The fingers are distal to the wrist.
In dentistry, the distal surfaces of teeth face away from the center of the dental arch.
The tibia is distal to the femur in the human leg.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proximal - situated nearer to the center or point of attachment

Etymology

From Latin 'distare', meaning 'to stand apart'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.təl / (BrE)
(of a body part) situated farthest from the point of attachment or origin, especially of a limb or bone.
situated away from point of attachment
medical

Examples

The distal end of the femur articulates with the knee joint.
Nerve damage often affects the distal parts of the limbs first, such as the hands and feet.
In anatomy, the thumb is on the distal side of the radius bone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proximal - closest to the point of attachment or origin (anatomy)

Etymology

From Latin 'distare' (to stand away), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'stare' (to stand).
