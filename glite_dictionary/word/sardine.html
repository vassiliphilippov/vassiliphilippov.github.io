sardine

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdin / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːn / (BrE)
A small, oily fish found in the herring family, often canned for food.
small, oily canned fish

Examples

She opened a tin of sardines for lunch.
Fishermen caught dozens of sardines off the coast.
The recipe calls for chopped sardine mixed with lemon juice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - small, salty, oily fish often canned

Etymology

From French 'sardine', from Latin 'sardina', probably from Sardinia, where the fish was once abundant.

3. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdin / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːn / (BrE)
a small, silver-colored fish found in large schools along European coasts, smaller and rounder than herring, often canned for food
a small, edible fish often canned

Examples

The fisherman brought a bucket full of sardines to sell at the market.
She opened a can of sardines for her lunch.
Seagulls swarmed over the water, chasing after a school of sardines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: herring - a larger, elongated fish related to the sardine

Etymology

From French *sardine*, from Latin *sardina*, after the Italian island of Sardinia, where they were once abundant.

Examples
