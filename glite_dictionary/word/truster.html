truster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌstər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstə / (BrE)
a person who believes or accepts something as true; someone who places trust in someone or something
person who trusts or believes

Examples

The company's truster base remained strong even after the controversy.
As a natural truster, she tends to see the good in everyone.
Without at least one truster on the team, skepticism might overwhelm any new idea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Synonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true or real

Etymology

From the verb 'trust' + -er, denoting a person who trusts.
