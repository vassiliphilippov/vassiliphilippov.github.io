certificate

1. NOUN

/ sɚˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (AmE), / səˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (BrE)
an official document that states certain facts to be true, such as achievement, ownership, or qualifications
official document stating facts

Examples

She received a certificate of completion after finishing the training course.
You need to show your birth certificate when applying for a passport.
This certificate confirms that he owns the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diploma - an official document certifying completion of a course of study
  • Synonym: license - an official document giving permission to do something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificatum, from certificare (to certify).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06483016-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ sərˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (AmE), / səˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (BrE)
a formal document, especially in finance, that gives the holder a right to receive interest or dividends
formal finance document giving rights to income

Examples

He invested in a certificate that guaranteed regular interest payments.
The certificate entitled her to annual dividends from the company.
Many people keep their stock certificate in a safety deposit box.

Synset Examples

he held several valuable securities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security - a financial asset such as a stock or bond
  • Synonym: bond - a certificate issued by a government or corporation promising to repay borrowed money with interest

Etymology

From Old French certificat, from Medieval Latin certificatum (something certified), from Latin certificare ('to certify').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13437518-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ sərˈtɪfɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈtɪfɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to formally present someone with a certificate, typically as an official recognition of achievement or completion
formally present a certificate

Examples

The school principal will certificate each graduate during the ceremony.
The organization certificated hundreds of volunteers last year for their training completion.
She was certificated at the end of the course in a small formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to certify - to formally attest or confirm in writing; to attest as being true or meeting a standard

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'certificatus', past participle of 'certificare' (to certify).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02266946-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sərˈtɪfɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈtɪfɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to officially recognize or authorize by issuing a certificate
authorize by issuing a certificate

Examples

The school is authorized to certificate its own teachers after a thorough evaluation process.
After completing all the requirements, the board will certificate you as a licensed practitioner.
Only accredited institutions are permitted to certificate professional translators in this country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to certify - to attest or confirm formally, usually in writing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificat-, past participle of certificare (to certify).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00805664-v
Synset POS: v
