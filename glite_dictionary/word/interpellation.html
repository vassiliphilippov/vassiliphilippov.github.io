interpellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˌpɛˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌpɛˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal parliamentary procedure in which a member demands that a government official explain an act or policy.
formal demand for official explanation in parliament
legal

Examples

During yesterday's session, the opposition filed an interpellation regarding the government's new tax plan.
The minister was required to respond to the interpellation submitted by several members of parliament.
The practice of interpellation serves as a check on executive power in many legislative bodies.

Etymology

From Latin interpellatio (interruption, questioning), from interpellare (to interrupt, question).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of interrupting a speaker with a question, remark, or objection, especially in a formal assembly or discussion.
act of interrupting with a remark or question

Examples

During the heated debate, the senator's interpellation caught everyone off guard.
The chairman grew frustrated by the constant interpellation from the opposition benches.
Her sudden interpellation shifted the direction of the conversation entirely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - the action of stopping something from continuing
  • Synonym: interjection - a sudden remark, often interrupting someone

Etymology

From Latin interpellatio (interruption), from interpellare (to interrupt).
