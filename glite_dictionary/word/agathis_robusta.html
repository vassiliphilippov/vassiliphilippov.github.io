Agathis robusta

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæθɪs roʊˈbʌstə / (AmE), / əˈɡæθɪs rəʊˈbʌstə / (BrE)
an Australian coniferous tree with soft, light wood, resembling the kauri, valued for timber
Australian timber tree, soft wood

Examples

The soft and light wood of the Agathis robusta makes it a popular choice for furniture makers.
In Queensland, forests of Agathis robusta are harvested for their valuable timber.
The Agathis robusta is sometimes confused with the New Zealand kauri due to its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kauri - a large coniferous tree native to New Zealand, known for valuable timber

Etymology

Agathis from Greek agathos (good), robusta from Latin robustus (strong), referring to the tree's characteristics.
