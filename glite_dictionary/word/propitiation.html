propitiation

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌpɪʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of gaining or regaining the favor of a god, spirit, or person by doing something that pleases them, especially as atonement for wrongdoing
act of appeasing or atoning, esp. a deity

Examples

Many ancient rituals were performed as acts of propitiation to appease the gods.
The priest spoke about the significance of propitiation in religious observance.
According to the legend, only a great sacrifice could serve as propitiation for their sins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atonement - reparation for a wrong or sin
  • Synonym: expiation - the act of making amends or reparation for guilt or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin propitiatio (an atonement), from propitiare (to appease).

2. NOUN

/ prəˌpɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌpɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of gaining or regaining the favor of someone, especially by doing something to please or appease them
act of appeasing or regaining favor

Examples

The ancient ritual was performed as a propitiation to the gods during times of trouble.
After the argument, his gift of flowers was meant as a propitiation for his harsh words.
The leader offered a public apology as a propitiation to the offended community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeasement - the act of calming or pacifying someone
  • Synonym: conciliation - the act of stopping someone from being angry
  • Antonym: provocation - action or speech that makes someone angry

Etymology

From Latin 'propitiatio', from 'propitiare' (to appease).
