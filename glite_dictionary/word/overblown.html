overblown

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbləʊn / (BrE)
Having passed the stage of full bloom; faded or decayed, especially used of flowers.
past full bloom; faded

Examples

The garden was filled with overblown roses shedding their petals in the summer heat.
She carefully trimmed the overblown tulips from the bouquet before placing it on the table.
An overblown peony loses much of its vibrant color as it ages.

Synset Examples

overblown roses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilted - (of a plant) limp through heat, loss of water, or age

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'blown' (in bloom, from 'to blow' meaning 'to blossom').

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbləʊn / (BrE)
exaggerated, pompous, or pretentious; inflated beyond what is reasonable or justified, especially in speech or manner
excessively pompous or exaggerated

Examples

The senator gave an overblown speech that failed to address any real issues.
Some critics dismissed the film’s style as overblown and self-indulgent.
Her overblown claims did not impress the skeptical audience.

Synset Examples

a grandiloquent and boastful manner
overblown oratory
a pompous speech
pseudo-scientific gobbledygook and pontifical hooey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pompous - affectedly grand, solemn, or self-important
  • Synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty or extravagant language
  • Antonym: modest - not boastful or exaggerated

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'blown', the past participle of 'blow'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01855888-s
Synset POS: s
