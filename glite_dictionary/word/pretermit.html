to pretermit

1. VERB

/ ˌpriː.tərˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriː.təˈmɪt / (BrE)
to leave out, neglect, or fail to do something
leave out or neglect

Examples

The contract cannot pretermit certain obligations imposed by law.
She chose to pretermit mentioning her earlier achievements during the meeting.
Important details were pretermitted in the summary, leading to confusion.

Synset Examples

How could I miss that typo?
The workers on the conveyor belt miss one out of ten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to omit - leave out or exclude
  • Synonym: neglect - fail to care for or do something

Etymology

From Latin 'praetermittere', from 'praeter' (beyond) + 'mittere' (to send).

2. VERB

/ ˌpriː.tərˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriː.təˈmɪt / (BrE)
to intentionally disregard, omit, or allow something to pass without mention
to disregard or omit intentionally

Examples

The committee decided to pretermit any discussion of the controversial issue.
He chose to pretermit minor details in his report to focus on the main points.
The court may pretermit certain claims if they are deemed irrelevant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out or exclude
  • Synonym: neglect - to fail to care for or pay proper attention to

Etymology

From Latin 'praetermittere', from 'praeter' (beyond) + 'mittere' (to send).

Synset POS: v
