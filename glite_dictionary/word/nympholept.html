nympholept

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪmfəˌlɛpt / (AmE), / ˈnɪmfəlɛpt / (BrE)
A person who is seized by nympholepsy; one who is passionately enamored with nymphs or a spirit of nature, especially to the point of frenzy.
person seized by nympholepsy

Examples

After seeing the mystical waterfall, he seemed a true nympholept, lost in reverie and longing.
The poet was called a nympholept for his obsession with woodland spirits in his works.
Legends tell of the nympholept who wandered the forests, forever seeking the elusive nymphs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nympholepsy - an emotional frenzy inspired by nymphs or nature spirits

Etymology

From Greek *nympholēptos* (caught by nymphs), from *nymphē* (nymph) + *lambanein* (to seize).
