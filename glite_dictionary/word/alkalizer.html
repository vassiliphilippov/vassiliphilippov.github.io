alkalizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a substance that neutralizes or counteracts acidity, especially in the stomach
substance that reduces acidity
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended taking an alkalizer to help relieve the symptoms of heartburn.
Certain foods and drinks can act as a natural alkalizer in the digestive system.
She purchased an alkalizer at the pharmacy to reduce her stomach acidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antacid - a substance that neutralizes stomach acidity

Etymology

From 'alkali' (Arabic *al-qaly* 'ashes of saltwort') + '-izer', meaning an agent that makes something alkaline.
