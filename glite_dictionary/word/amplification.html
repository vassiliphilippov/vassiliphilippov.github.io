amplification

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmpləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌamplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of increasing the strength of an electrical signal, such as voltage, power, or current, often by means of an electronic device
increase of electrical signal strength

Examples

The amplification of the audio signal improved the sound quality during the presentation.
In this circuit, the amplification is achieved using a transistor.
High amplification is necessary for weak radio signals to be clearly detected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attenuation - the reduction in the strength of a signal

Etymology

From Latin 'amplificatio', from 'amplificare' (to enlarge).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæmpləfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌamplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in signal strength, voltage, or current, often measured as the ratio of output to input
increase in signal strength or voltage

Examples

The engineer measured the amplification of the audio signal to ensure the sound quality.
A high level of amplification can cause distortion in electronic circuits.
The amplification of weak electrical currents is essential in radio transmitters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gain - the increase in power or amplitude of a signal (usually in electronics)

Etymology

From Latin amplificatio (an enlarging, exaggeration), from amplificare (to enlarge).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæm.plə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæm.plɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding further detail, illustration, or clarification to something, especially in speech or writing
addition of more detail or clarification

Examples

The author provided further amplification of his argument in the footnotes.
Her amplification of the basic outline helped everyone understand the project goals.
During the discussion, he offered an amplification of his earlier remarks.

Synset Examples

a few remarks added in amplification and defense
an elaboration of the sketch followed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaboration - addition of more detail to what has already been said

Etymology

From Latin ampliatio, from amplificare (to enlarge, amplify).
