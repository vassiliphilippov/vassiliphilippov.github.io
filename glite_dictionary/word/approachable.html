approachable

1. ADJ

/ əˈproʊ.tʃə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈprəʊ.tʃə.bəl / (BrE)
friendly and easy to talk to or deal with; accessible for conversation or business.
friendly and easy to talk to

Examples

The new manager is very approachable, so employees feel comfortable sharing their ideas.
Despite being the CEO, she remained approachable and always had time for her staff.
People found him approachable because he listened carefully and responded kindly to everyone.

Synset Examples

a friendly approachable person

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unapproachable - not friendly or easy to talk to
  • Synonym: accessible - able to be reached or easily approached or understood

Etymology

From 'approach' + '-able', following the standard English adjective formation.

2. ADJ

/ əˈproʊtʃəbəl / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃəbl / (BrE)
friendly or easy to approach or reach; easy to talk to or access.
easy to approach or reach

Examples

The manager was always approachable and willing to listen to staff concerns.
The island is only approachable by boat during high tide.
Many students find the new professor very approachable and easy to talk to.

Synset Examples

a site approachable from a branch of the Niger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unapproachable - not easy to approach or reach
  • Synonym: accessible - easy to approach, reach, or use

Etymology

From Latin 'appropinquare', meaning 'to draw near'.

3. ADJ

/ əˈproʊtʃəbəl / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃəbəl / (BrE)
(of a work, subject, or person) easy to understand, appreciate, or deal with; not difficult or intimidating.
easy to understand or deal with

Examples

Despite its technical subject, the book is surprisingly approachable for beginners.
Her lectures are very approachable and clearly explained, making the material more understandable.
The documentary presents complex issues in an approachable and engaging way.

Synset Examples

readily accessible to the nonprofessional reader
the tales seem more approachable than his more difficult novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessible - easy to approach, reach, or understand
  • Antonym: intimidating - frightening or discouraging, hard to approach

Etymology

From Middle English approchen (to approach) + -able.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
