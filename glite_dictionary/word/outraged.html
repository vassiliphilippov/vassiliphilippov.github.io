outraged

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing strong anger and shock, especially at something perceived as unjust or wrong.
feeling strong anger or shock

Examples

She was outraged by the unfair accusations made against her in the meeting.
Many citizens felt outraged when the government increased the taxes without warning.
His outraged response made it clear that he would not tolerate such behavior.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indignant - feeling or showing anger at unfair treatment
  • Degree Higher: furious - extremely angry
  • Degree Lower: annoyed - slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

From Middle French *outrager* (to insult, commit excess) and *outrage* (excess, insult).
