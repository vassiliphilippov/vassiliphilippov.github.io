Atherinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈrɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈrɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small spiny-finned fishes found in both saltwater and freshwater habitats; commonly known as silversides
family of small spiny-finned fishes (silversides)

Examples

Many species in the Atherinidae family are important for maintaining ecological balance in coastal waters.
Researchers conducted a study on the migration patterns of Atherinidae in estuarine environments.
Aquarists sometimes keep members of the Atherinidae family in tanks due to their silvery appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Atherina + -idae (the zoological family suffix).
