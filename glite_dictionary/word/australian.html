Australian

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈstreɪliən / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Australia.
person from Australia

Examples

As an Australian, she felt proud to represent her country at the Olympics.
We met an Australian who told us all about life in Sydney.
The restaurant is owned by an Australian who moved here ten years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Zealander - a native or inhabitant of New Zealand
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From Australia + -an, denoting belonging or relating to.

2. ADJ

/ əˈstreɪliən / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪlɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Australia, its people, or its languages.
of or relating to Australia

Examples

The Australian outback is famous for its unique wildlife.
Many Australian cities are located near beautiful beaches.
She admires Australian art for its vibrant colors and native influences.

Synset Examples

Australian deserts
Australian aborigines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aussie - informal term for Australian

Etymology

From Australia + -an, forming the demonym and adjective.
