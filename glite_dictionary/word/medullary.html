medullary

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʌləri / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌləri / (BrE)
relating to the medulla oblongata, the lower part of the brainstem
of the medulla oblongata
medical

Examples

The patient showed medullary damage, leading to problems with breathing and heart rate.
Researchers studied the medullary centers responsible for vital autonomic functions.
A tumor in the medullary region can have serious neurological consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medulla oblongata - the lower portion of the brainstem

Etymology

From Latin medulla (marrow) + -ary (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ məˈdʌləri / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌləri / (BrE)
containing, consisting of, or resembling bone marrow; relating to the medulla, especially bone marrow or the inner region of an organ or structure.
containing or resembling bone marrow
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed a medullary pattern characteristic of certain types of tumors.
In anatomy, the medullary cavity refers to the central cavity of bone shafts.
The doctor explained that the disease affected the medullary region of the adrenal gland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cortical - relating to the outer layer (cortex) of an organ or structure, especially the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'medullaris', from 'medulla' (marrow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02771825-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ məˈdʌləri / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌləri / (BrE)
relating to or resembling the medulla, especially the central part of an organ, tissue, or bone
relating to the medulla (central part)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the tumor was located in the medullary region of the kidney.
Certain bones have a medullary cavity where marrow is stored.
Medullary thyroid carcinoma is a rare form of thyroid cancer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cortical - relating to the cortex, the outer layer of an organ or body part

Etymology

From Latin medullaris, from medulla meaning marrow or pith.

Source: WordNet
