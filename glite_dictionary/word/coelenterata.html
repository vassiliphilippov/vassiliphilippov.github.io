Coelenterata

1. NOUN

/ siˌlɛntəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / siːˌlɛntəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
an obsolete taxonomic group of aquatic invertebrate animals that includes hydras, jellyfish, sea anemones, and corals; now divided into Cnidaria and Ctenophora
obsolete group of aquatic invertebrates (jellyfish, corals, etc.)

Examples

In older biology texts, jellyfish are classified in the group Coelenterata.
The study of Coelenterata provides insight into early animal evolution.
Hydras, corals, and sea anemones were originally placed in Coelenterata.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cnidaria - a phylum including jellyfish, sea anemones, corals, and related animals
  • Degree Lower: Ctenophora - a phylum of marine animals known as comb jellies

Etymology

From Greek koilos (hollow) + enteron (intestine), referring to animals with a simple digestive cavity.

2. NOUN

/ siːlɛnˈtɛrətə / (AmE), / sɪlɛnˈtɛrətə / (BrE)
a former phylum of aquatic invertebrate animals including hydras, jellyfish, sea anemones, and corals, now usually divided into the phyla Cnidaria and Ctenophora
group of aquatic invertebrates (jellyfish, corals, etc.)

Examples

Studying the Coelenterata reveals fascinating variations in how simple animals can adapt to marine environments.
Jellyfish and corals are classic members of the once-recognized phylum Coelenterata.
With advances in taxonomy, scientists now separate the Coelenterata into Cnidaria and Ctenophora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cnidaria - current phylum including jellyfish, corals, etc.

Etymology

From Greek *koilos* (hollow) + *enteron* (intestine), referring to the animals' single body cavity.
