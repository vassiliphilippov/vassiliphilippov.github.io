schizoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪt͡sˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈskɪt.sɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting symptoms similar to those of schizophrenia, or showing withdrawal from social relationships and emotional expression.
relating to schizophrenia or social withdrawal
medical

Examples

Some patients with schizoid personality disorder tend to avoid close relationships.
The character was described as having a schizoid attitude toward society.
Her schizoid traits made it difficult for her to express emotions in social situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenic - relating to or affected by schizophrenia

Etymology

From Greek 'schizein' (to split) + '-oid' (like, resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪt.sɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈskɪt.sɔɪd / (BrE)
a person who is characterized by symptoms resembling but less severe than those of schizophrenia, often showing emotional aloofness or detachment from social relationships
person with symptoms less severe than schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The doctor noticed the patient exhibited several schizoid traits, such as emotional detachment.
Despite being a schizoid, he managed to maintain some close relationships.
Some schizoids may appear indifferent to praise or criticism from others.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: schizophrenic - a person with schizophrenia

Etymology

From German Schizoid, from Greek skhizein (to split) + -oid (resembling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14412163-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈskɪt.sɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈskɪt.sɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or personality) marked by withdrawal from social relationships and an inability to form close emotional bonds.
socially withdrawn; unable to form close relationships
medical

Examples

Despite his schizoid tendencies, he tried to engage with his coworkers during meetings.
The psychologist diagnosed her with a schizoid personality disorder after several sessions.
His schizoid behavior made it difficult for him to maintain close friendships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introverted - having a personality that is shy and enjoys spending time alone
  • Antonym: sociable - enjoying spending time with other people

Etymology

From Greek 'skhizein' (to split) + English '-oid' (resembling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01590300-s
Synset POS: s
