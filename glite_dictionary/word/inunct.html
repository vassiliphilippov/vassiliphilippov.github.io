to inunct

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnʌŋkt / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌŋkt / (BrE)
to apply oil or ointment to, especially as part of a religious ceremony of blessing
anoint with oil or ointment, especially religiously

Examples

In some traditions, priests inunct the sick with blessed oil as a sign of healing.
The bishop inuncted the newly ordained with fragrant ointment.
It is customary to inunct the forehead during the sacred ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to smear or rub with oil, often as part of a religious rite

Etymology

From Latin 'inunguere' (to anoint, smear upon), from 'in-' (on) and 'unguere' (to anoint).
