loaf

1. NOUN

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
a quantity of food, not necessarily bread, shaped into a compact mass, typically with rounded or elongated sides
shaped mass of food (not only bread)

Examples

He prepared a delicious meat loaf for dinner last night.
The bakery sold a beautifully shaped sugar loaf at the holiday market.
She molded the cheese into a large loaf before aging it.

Synset Examples

meat loaf
sugar loaf
a loaf of cheese

Etymology

From Old English *hlāf* (bread, loaf).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07665191-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
to spend time aimlessly; to move around a place without any particular purpose or effort
spend time aimlessly, wander lazily

Examples

The group of teenagers liked to loaf around the shopping mall after school.
Instead of working, he would loaf in the park all afternoon.
She was criticized for loafing about when there was still work to do.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around without apparent purpose

Etymology

From Middle English 'lofen' (to idle), probably related to 'loaf' (bread) in reference to idleness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645159-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
A shaped mass of baked bread, typically sliced before eating.
shaped mass of bread

Examples

She bought a fresh loaf of whole wheat bread from the bakery.
Please slice the loaf before serving it at the table.
The aroma of a baking loaf filled the entire kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bun - a small, round piece of bread
  • Synonym: roll - a small bread portion shaped and baked separately

Etymology

From Old English *hlāf* (bread, loaf), related to Old High German *hlaub* and Old Norse *hleifr*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07699531-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
to spend time in a lazy or idle way; to do nothing productive
be lazy; do nothing productive

Examples

He spent the afternoon loafing on the couch, watching television and doing nothing.
Sometimes I wish I could just loaf around the house instead of going to work.
They were loafing by the lake, enjoying the warm weather and chatting.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing; avoid work
  • Antonym: work - to do work; be engaged in productive activity

Etymology

From Old English *hlāf* (loaf, bread); verb sense from the phrase 'loaf about', possibly by association with idling about like a loaf lying around.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645690-v
Synset POS: v
