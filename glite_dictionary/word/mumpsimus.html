mumpsimus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmpsɪməs / (AmE), / ˈmʌmpsɪməs / (BrE)
An outdated or unreasonable custom, belief, or idea that is obstinately adhered to, especially after being shown to be wrong.
an obstinately held mistaken belief or practice
archaic

Examples

Despite all the evidence, he continued to defend his old mumpsimus about grammar rules.
Her adherence to that outdated mumpsimus frustrated her colleagues.
Some traditions persist as mere mumpsimus long after their logic has faded.

Synset Examples

he still holds to the old mumpsimus that a woman's place is in the kitchen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sumpsimus - a correct use or belief, especially after previously being wrong

Etymology

From a misreading of Latin 'sumpsimus' as 'mumpsimus' in a religious context, popularized in 16th-century English.
