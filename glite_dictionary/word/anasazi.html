Anasazi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˈsɑːzi / (AmE), / ˌanəˈsɑːzi / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Native American people who lived in the southwestern United States and built cliff dwellings, especially in the regions now known as Colorado, Utah, Arizona, and New Mexico
ancient Native American people of the Southwest

Examples

The Anasazi are renowned for the impressive cliff dwellings they left behind in Mesa Verde.
Archaeologists have uncovered pottery and artifacts that shed light on Anasazi culture.
Many people visit the ancient homes of the Anasazi to learn about early Native American history.

Etymology

From Navajo 'anaasází' meaning 'ancient enemies' or 'ancient people'.
