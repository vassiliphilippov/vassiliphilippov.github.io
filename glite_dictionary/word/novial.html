Novial

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈviːəl / (AmE), / nəʊˈviːəl / (BrE)
a constructed international auxiliary language designed for universal communication, with vocabulary mainly from Germanic and Romance languages and grammar influenced by English
constructed international auxiliary language

Examples

Linguists sometimes compare Novial with Esperanto and other constructed languages.
She studied Novial to better understand its grammar and vocabulary.
There are still a few enthusiasts who write articles in Novial today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - most widely used constructed international auxiliary language

Etymology

From Latin 'novus' (new) and 'international auxiliary language', coined by linguist Otto Jespersen in 1928.
