weekender

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkˌɛndər / (AmE), / ˈwiːkˌɛndə / (BrE)
a person who spends weekends away from home, typically on short vacations or traveling to a holiday home
person taking weekend trips

Examples

The small coastal town was crowded with weekenders looking for relaxation.
Many city dwellers become weekenders, escaping to the countryside every Friday.
She bought a cottage upstate and now joins the other weekenders every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacationer - a person who is on vacation

Etymology

From 'weekend' + '-er', meaning a person associated with a weekend activity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkˌɛndər / (AmE), / ˈwiːkˌɛndə / (BrE)
a small suitcase or bag suitable for clothing and accessories needed for a weekend trip
small suitcase for a weekend trip

Examples

She packed her essentials into a stylish weekender before leaving for the train station.
For short getaways, I prefer to travel light with just a weekender.
He bought a new leather weekender to carry on the plane for business trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overnighter - a small bag for an overnight trip
  • Degree Higher: trunk - a large suitcase or lockable box for luggage

Etymology

From 'weekend' + '-er', denoting something designed for use on weekends.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04578101-n
Synset POS: n
