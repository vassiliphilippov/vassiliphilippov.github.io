rightfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being morally or legally right; conformity to justice or correctness
the quality of being right or just

Examples

Many historians debate the rightfulness of the king's claim to the throne.
The lawyer argued convincingly for the rightfulness of her client's actions.
They questioned the rightfulness of punishing people for laws enacted after the fact.

Synset Examples

he feels he is in the right
the rightfulness of his claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice - fairness; the quality of being just
  • Synonym: rightness - the state of being correct or morally good
  • Antonym: wrongfulness - the quality of being unjust or incorrect

Etymology

From 'rightful' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being rightful.
