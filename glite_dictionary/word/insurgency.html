insurgency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɜrdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
An organized attempt by a group of people to overthrow an established government, typically by subversion and armed conflict.
armed rebellion against authority

Examples

The military responded quickly to suppress the insurgency in the northern provinces.
Over the last decade, the region has been plagued by a violent insurgency.
Talks between the government and leaders of the insurgency finally began last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - open resistance to authority, often with violence
  • Synonym: insurrection - violent uprising against an authority or government
  • Synonym: revolution - forcible overthrow of a government or order

Etymology

From Latin 'insurgere' (to rise up), via the noun 'insurgent'.
