Helminthostachys

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˌmɪnθoʊˈstæskɪs / (AmE), / hɛlˌmɪnθəˈstækɪs / (BrE)
a genus of terrestrial fern found in southeastern Asia and Australia, containing a single species, Helminthostachys zeylanica.
genus of fern in Asia and Australia

Examples

Helminthostachys is easily recognized by its spore-bearing spike resembling a worm.
Botanists found a rare specimen of Helminthostachys growing in the Australian rainforest.
The genus Helminthostachys comprises only a single species in its family.

Etymology

From Greek *helminthos* (worm) + *stachys* (spike), referring to the worm-like fertile spike of the fern.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlˌmɪnθəˈstækɪs / (AmE), / hɛlˌmɪnθəˈstækɪs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns native to southeastern Asia and Australia, containing a single species.
a genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

The rare fern Helminthostachys is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual appearance.
Helminthostachys is easily recognized by its distinctive spore-bearing spike.
Researchers studied the habitat requirements of Helminthostachys in southeastern Asia.

Etymology

From Greek helminthos (worm) and stachys (ear of grain), referring to the appearance of the fertile spike.
