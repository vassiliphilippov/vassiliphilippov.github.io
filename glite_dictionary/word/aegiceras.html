Aegiceras

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːdʒɪˈsɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌiːdʒɪˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs or small trees, commonly known as river mangrove, belonging to the family Primulaceae.
genus of river mangrove shrubs or trees

Examples

Aegiceras populations are commonly found along the riverbanks in coastal Australia.
The traditional use of Aegiceras was first recorded in local medicinal practices.
Researchers sampled leaves from several Aegiceras trees for their ecological study.

Etymology

From Greek aig-, aigos (goat) and keras (horn), referring to the shape of the fruit.
