polygamous

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪɡəməs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪɡəməs / (BrE)
having more than one mate, spouse, or partner at the same time; especially relating to marriage or relationships
having multiple mates or spouses

Examples

Some animal species are naturally polygamous, forming bonds with multiple partners each season.
He was raised in a polygamous household where his father had three wives.
The debate on whether humans are naturally polygamous or monogamous continues among scientists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monogamous - having only one mate or spouse at a time

Etymology

From Late Latin polygamus (having multiple wives), from Greek polygamos (married to many).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪɡəməs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪɡəməs / (BrE)
(Of a plant) having both male, female, and often bisexual flowers on the same individual.
bearing different types of flowers (male, female, bisexual) on one plant
medical

Examples

Some species of maple are polygamous, producing male, female, and bisexual flowers on the same tree.
Botanists observed that the polygamous plant displayed a unique floral arrangement during the blooming season.
The polygamous nature of this shrub contributes to its adaptability and reproductive success in diverse environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monoecious - having both male and female reproductive organs on the same plant
  • Antonym: dioecious - having male and female reproductive organs on different plants

Etymology

From Greek polygamos (married to many), from polys (many) + gamos (marriage).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
