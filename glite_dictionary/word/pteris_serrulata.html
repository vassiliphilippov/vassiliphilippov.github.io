Pteris serrulata

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɪs səˈruːlətə / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪs sɛˈruːlətə / (BrE)
A species of fern native to North Africa, the Azores, and the Canary Islands.
a fern species of North Africa, Azores, Canary Islands

Examples

Pteris serrulata thrives in humid, shaded forests of the Canary Islands.
Botanists have documented the distribution of Pteris serrulata across several Mediterranean islands.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Pteris serrulata for its delicate fronds and unique appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pteris' meaning 'fern', 'serrulata' referring to finely toothed (serrated) leaf margins.
