fledgling

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a young bird that has just acquired its flight feathers and become capable of flying
young bird that can just fly

Examples

The robin's nest was soon empty as each fledgling took its first flight.
Scientists watched the fledgling struggle before it finally soared into the air.
After weeks of care, the rescued owl was released as a healthy fledgling.

Etymology

From 'fledge' (to acquire feathers necessary for flight) + '-ling' (diminutive suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01506820-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
A person or organization that is new to an activity or field and lacks experience.
someone new to a field or activity

Examples

As a fledgling in marketing, she was eager to learn from her more experienced colleagues.
The tech company, though a fledgling in the industry, quickly gained recognition for its innovation.
He proved that a fledgling could succeed with enough determination and hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From 'fledge' (to grow feathers needed for flying) and the diminutive suffix '-ling'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10375148-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a young bird) having just developed the feathers necessary for flight; newly able to fly.
young bird with flight feathers

Examples

The fledgling sparrows left their nest for the first time today.
A fledgling owl perched awkwardly on the branch, uncertain how to take flight.
We found a fledgling robin hopping on the ground, watched closely by its parents.

Synset Examples

a fledgling robin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fledge - to acquire the feathers necessary for flight (of a young bird)

Etymology

From the verb 'fledge' (to acquire feathers necessary for flight) + -ling (diminutive suffix).
