arrester

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛstər / (AmE), / əˈrɛstə / (BrE)
a device used to slow airplanes as they land, typically on the flight deck of an aircraft carrier
device that slows landing airplanes

Examples

The fighter jet caught the arrester wire and came to a stop on the deck.
Maintenance crews inspect each arrester system after every landing to ensure safety.
Without an arrester in place, landing on an aircraft carrier would be extremely dangerous.

Etymology

From 'arrest' (to stop) + '-er', indicating an agent noun.
