Salix alba sericea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˈælbə səˈraɪsiə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˈalba sɛˈrɪsɪə / (BrE)
A North American variety of the white willow, characterized by greyish silky leaves that typically blacken upon drying.
North American variety of white willow

Examples

The leaves of Salix alba sericea have a distinctive silky texture and pale color.
Many parks in North America include Salix alba sericea for its ornamental value.
The dried leaves of Salix alba sericea usually turn black, which helps in identifying the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically having narrow leaves and slender branches

Etymology

From Latin: Salix (willow), alba (white), sericea (silky).
