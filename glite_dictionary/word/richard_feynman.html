Richard Feynman

1. PROPN

/ ˈɹɪtʃɚd ˈfaɪnmən / (AmE), / ˈɹɪtʃəd ˈfaɪnmən / (BrE)
(1918–1988) A United States physicist known for fundamental contributions to quantum electrodynamics and for popularizing science through writing and teaching.
American physicist, quantum electrodynamics expert

Examples

Many physics students admire Richard Feynman for his engaging lectures and books.
The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Richard Feynman in 1965, along with Julian Schwinger and Sin-Itiro Tomonaga.
Richard Feynman's contributions to quantum theory have had a lasting impact on modern science.
