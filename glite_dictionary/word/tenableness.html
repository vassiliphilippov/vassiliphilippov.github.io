tenableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnəbəlnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəbəl.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be defended, supported, or accepted as reasonable or plausible.
quality of being defensible or reasonable

Examples

The tenableness of her argument convinced the entire committee to support her proposal.
They doubted the tenableness of the theory once new evidence was presented.
The lawyer argued for the tenableness of his client's position throughout the trial.

Synset Examples

he questioned the tenability of my claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenability - the quality or state of being tenable
