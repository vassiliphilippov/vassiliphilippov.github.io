analeptic

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
A drug or medication that stimulates the central nervous system, often used to counteract depression or unconsciousness.
central nervous system stimulant (medication)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an analeptic to help the patient regain consciousness.
Some analeptics are used in emergency medicine to treat respiratory failure.
Research into new analeptic drugs is ongoing in the pharmaceutical industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulant - A substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From French analeptique, from Greek analeptikos (restorative), from analambanein (to restore).

2. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or agent) stimulating the central nervous system.
stimulating the central nervous system
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an analeptic to help counteract the effects of the sedative.
Some analeptic agents are used in emergency medicine to stimulate a patient's breathing.
Caffeine is considered an analeptic because it stimulates the central nervous system.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depressant - an agent that reduces or suppresses central nervous system activity

Etymology

From Greek 'analeptikos', from 'analepsis' (restoration), from 'analepsis' (recovery).
