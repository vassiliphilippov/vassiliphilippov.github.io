acromicria

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəʊˈmɪkriə / (AmE), / ˌækrəʊˈmɪkriə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormally small or underdeveloped fingers and toes.
abnormally small extremities (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with acromicria due to the noticeable underdevelopment of her fingers and toes.
Certain genetic disorders can result in acromicria, affecting both hands and feet.
Researchers are studying potential treatments for acromicria to improve limb function in affected individuals.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (extremity) and 'mikros' (small).
