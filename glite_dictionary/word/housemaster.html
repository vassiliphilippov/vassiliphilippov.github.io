housemaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a male teacher who is in charge of a boarding house in a school
teacher in charge of a school boarding house
british

Examples

The housemaster was responsible for the students' welfare and discipline outside of class.
When problems arose in the dormitory, the housemaster was always there to help.
Mr. Evans served as housemaster for over a decade at the boarding school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: housemistress - female teacher in charge of a boarding house in a school

Etymology

From 'house' (boarding house) + 'master' (person in charge), common in British schools.
