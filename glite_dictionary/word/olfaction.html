olfaction

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ɒlˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the sense or faculty of smelling; the ability to perceive and distinguish odors
sense of smell

Examples

Dogs have a highly developed olfaction that allows them to track scents over long distances.
Damage to the nasal cavity can impair a person’s olfaction.
Scientists are studying how olfaction influences memory and emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell - the faculty or sense by which odors are perceived
  • Degree Lower: taste - the sense that enables perception of flavors
  • Antonym: vision - the faculty of seeing

Etymology

From Latin 'olfactio' (smelling), from 'olfacere' (to smell).
