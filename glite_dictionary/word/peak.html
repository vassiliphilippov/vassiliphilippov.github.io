peak

1. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
the pointed top or highest part of something, especially a mountain or hill
highest point; mountain top

Examples

We finally reached the peak of the mountain after hours of climbing.
From the hotel, you can see the snow-capped peak in the distance.
The flag was placed at the peak to mark the team's achievement.

Synset Examples

the view from the peak was magnificent
they clambered to the tip of Monadnock
the region is a few molecules wide at the summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summit - the highest point of a hill or mountain
  • Antonym: base - the lowest or supporting part of something

Etymology

From Old French *pic* (pointed tip, beak), from Vulgar Latin *piccus*, probably of pre-Latin origin.
