crevice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛv.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛv.ɪs / (BrE)
A narrow opening or crack, especially in a rock or wall.
narrow opening or crack

Examples

The lizard darted into a crevice between the rocks to escape the heat.
Years of erosion had formed a deep crevice in the side of the mountain.
She found a small crevice in the wall where she hid the spare key.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow crack or opening
  • Degree Higher: gap - a break or opening in something

Etymology

From Old French *crevace*, from crever (to burst), from Latin *crepare* (to crack, burst, make a noise).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛv.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛv.ɪs / (BrE)
a narrow opening or crack, especially in a rock or wall
narrow crack or opening

Examples

A lizard hid in the crevice between the rocks to escape the sun.
Tiny plants grew in every crevice of the crumbling stone wall.
Rainwater seeped into the crevice and slowly widened it over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage
  • Degree Higher: gap - an empty space or interval; a break in continuity

Etymology

From Old French crevice, from crever (to crack, burst), ultimately from Latin crepare (to crack, creak).
