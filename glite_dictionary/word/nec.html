NEC

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.iːˈsi / (AmE), / ˌɛn.iːˈsi / (BrE)
an acute inflammatory disease of the intestines, primarily affecting premature infants and potentially leading to tissue death
intestinal disease in premature infants
medical

Examples

The premature baby was diagnosed with NEC after showing severe abdominal symptoms.
Early detection and treatment are crucial in cases of NEC.
Researchers are studying new methods to prevent NEC in neonatal intensive care units.

Etymology

Acronym of necrotizing enterocolitis.
