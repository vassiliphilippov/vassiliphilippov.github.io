pyaemic

1. ADJ

/ paɪˈiːmɪk / (AmE), / paɪˈiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of pyemia, a type of blood poisoning caused by pus-forming bacteria
relating to pyemia (blood infection)
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected a pyaemic condition due to the presence of multiple abscesses.
Pyaemic symptoms often include high fever and severe chills.
She was admitted to the hospital with a pyaemic infection following surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyemia - a type of blood poisoning caused by pus-forming bacteria

Etymology

From 'pyemia', from Greek 'puon' (pus) + '-emia' (blood condition).
