impedimenta

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛdəˈmɛntə / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛdɪˈmɛntə / (BrE)
objects, equipment, or other things that hinder movement or make progress more difficult, especially unnecessary or cumbersome baggage.
things that hinder progress

Examples

After hours of climbing, the hikers stopped to remove any impedimenta that slowed their ascent.
The soldiers discarded their unnecessary impedimenta before entering battle.
Modern offices are often cluttered with impedimenta that distract from actual work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes it difficult
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction

Etymology

From Latin impedīmenta, plural of impedīmentum (hindrance).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛdəˈmɛnə / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛdɪˈmɛntə / (BrE)
objects, equipment, or baggage, especially that which slows one's progress, such as the supplies and gear carried by an army.
baggage or equipment that hinders movement

Examples

The soldiers moved slowly under the weight of their impedimenta.
We left most of our impedimenta at the base camp to travel more lightly.
Tourists often bring more impedimenta than they actually need for their trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baggage - suitcases, bags, and belongings taken when traveling
  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin *impedimenta* (things that hinder), plural of *impedimentum*.
