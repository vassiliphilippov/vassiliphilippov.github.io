greegree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːˌɡriː / (AmE), / ˈɡriːˌɡriː / (BrE)
An amulet or charm, especially from West Africa, believed to protect the wearer from harm or bring good luck.
West African protective amulet

Examples

He wore a greegree around his neck for protection.
Many traders in the market carried a small greegree in their pockets.
The shaman prepared a new greegree to ward off evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amulet - An object worn to protect against danger or disease
  • Synonym: talisman - An object thought to have magical powers and bring good luck

Etymology

From West African languages (e.g. Mandinka 'grigri'), widely adopted in English to refer to amulets or charms.
