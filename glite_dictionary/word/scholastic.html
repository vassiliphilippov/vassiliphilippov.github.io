Scholastic

1. NOUN

/ skəˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / skəˈlastɪk / (BrE)
a philosopher or theologian who followed the methods and teachings of the Scholastic tradition, especially in medieval Europe
follower of medieval Scholastic philosophy

Examples

Thomas Aquinas is perhaps the most influential Scholastic in the history of Western philosophy.
During the Middle Ages, a Scholastic would often debate intricate theological questions at university.
The library contains many works written by renowned Scholastics from the twelfth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schoolman - a medieval scholar or theologian, especially one adhering to Scholasticism

Etymology

From Latin scholasticus (of a scholar), from Greek skolastikos (devoting one's leisure to learning).
