hundredth

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌndrədθ / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrɪdθ / (BrE)
The position in a sequence that comes after ninety-ninth and before hundred and first; 100th.
position number 100 in a series

Examples

He finished in hundredth place out of all the runners.
This is the hundredth anniversary of the founding of the school.
She was the hundredth person to sign the petition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundred - the number 100
  • Degree Higher: hundred and first - the ordinal next after hundredth, 101st
  • Degree Lower: ninety-ninth - the ordinal before hundredth, 99th

Etymology

From 'hundred' + '-th', an Old English ordinal suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌndrədθ / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrədθ / (BrE)
Each of one hundred equal parts into which something may be divided; the ordinal number matching the number 100 in a sequence.
one part in a hundred; 100th

Examples

Only a hundredth of the entire population was affected by the rare disease.
A hundredth of a second can make all the difference in a race.
The company owns just a hundredth share of the massive oil field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percent - one part in a hundred (expressed as a percentage)

Etymology

Formed from "hundred" plus the ordinal suffix "-th."
