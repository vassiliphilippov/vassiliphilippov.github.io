Pedilanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdɪˈlanθəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛdɪˈlanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American succulent shrubs, valued for their distinctive flowers and foliage.
genus of tropical American succulent shrubs

Examples

The Pedilanthus in her garden thrived in the hot, dry climate.
Botanists often study Pedilanthus for its unusual slipper-shaped flowers.
Some varieties of Pedilanthus make excellent houseplants due to their drought tolerance.

Etymology

From Greek 'pedilon' (slipper) and 'anthos' (flower), reflecting the shape of the flowers.
