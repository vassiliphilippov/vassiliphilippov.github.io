vindicated

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪndɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪndɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
shown or proven to be right, reasonable, or free from blame or suspicion
shown to be free from blame or suspicion

Examples

She felt completely vindicated after the truth came out at the trial.
The investigation vindicated the scientist's controversial theories.
After the scandal, the official was finally vindicated by new evidence.

Synset Examples

is absolved from all blame
was now clear of the charge of cowardice
his official honor is vindicated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolved - freed from blame or responsibility
  • Synonym: blameless - not deserving blame; innocent
  • Antonym: guilty - responsible for a specified wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin vindicatus, past participle of vindicare (to claim, to avenge).
