Dalbergia latifolia

1. NOUN

/ dælˈbɜrdʒiə lætɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / dælˈbɜːdʒiə lætɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to India, valued for its hard, dark purple timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
species of tree with prized dark wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Dalbergia latifolia, renowned for its beautiful color and grain.
Harvesting Dalbergia latifolia is tightly regulated due to its high value and endangered status.
Several guitars made from Dalbergia latifolia are prized for their rich tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian rosewood - Common name for Dalbergia latifolia

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: genus name 'Dalbergia' honors Swedish botanist Nils Dalberg; species 'latifolia' means 'broad-leaved' in Latin.
