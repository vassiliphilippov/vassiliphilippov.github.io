riotous

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪ.ə.təs / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ət.əs / (BrE)
marked by noisy disorder, wildness, or unrest; involving violent or uncontrolled behavior
noisy and disorderly; wild

Examples

The city streets became riotous after the championship game ended.
During the revolution, there were riotous scenes outside the parliament building.
The party grew increasingly riotous as midnight approached.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumultuous - full of confusion, commotion, or disorder
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
  • Antonym: orderly - characterized by organization and neatness, not disorderly

Etymology

From Old French *rioteux* (disorderly), from *riote* (dispute, quarrel).

2. ADJ

/ ˈraɪətəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪətəs / (BrE)
Behaving in a wild, unrestrained, or morally uncontrolled way; lacking restraint by social convention or morality.
wild and unrestrained; morally uncontrolled

Examples

The author’s memoir tells of his riotous youth spent in nightclubs across Europe.
The festival was marked by riotous celebrations that went long into the night, defying local curfews.
His riotous lifestyle eventually led to scandal and financial ruin.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissipated - indulging excessively in sensual pleasures; lacking moral restraint
  • Synonym: debauched - indulging in immoral or wild behavior
  • Antonym: temperate - showing moderation or self-restraint

Etymology

From Old French riot (disorder, debauchery) + -ous, from Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01553629-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈraɪətəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪətəs / (BrE)
(of plants or growth) produced or growing in extreme abundance; luxuriant or exuberant.
growing in extreme abundance

Examples

The garden was filled with riotous blossoms of every color imaginable.
Vines grew in riotous profusion along the cottage walls.
After the spring rain, the countryside erupted in riotous greenery.

Synset Examples

their riotous blooming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxuriant - rich and profuse in growth; lush
  • Synonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities; plentiful

Etymology

From 'riot', Middle English from Old French 'riote' (debauchery, dispute), meaning evolved to exuberant or excessive growth by extension.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
