Hudsonia

1. NOUN

/ hʌdˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / hʌdˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen subshrubs native to North America, commonly found in sandy or rocky habitats.
genus of small evergreen North American subshrubs

Examples

The sandy dunes along the coast are home to several species of Hudsonia.
Botanists are studying the resilience of Hudsonia in harsh environments.
Rare patches of Hudsonia brighten the landscape with their yellow blossoms in spring.

Etymology

Named after the English botanist William Hudson (1730–1793).
