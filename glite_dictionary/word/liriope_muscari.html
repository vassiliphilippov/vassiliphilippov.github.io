Liriope muscari

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈraɪ.ə.pi mʊˈskɛəri / (AmE), / lɪˈraɪ.ə.pi mʌsˈkɑːri / (BrE)
An Asiatic perennial herb with tufted, grasslike evergreen leaves and clusters of dark purple, grape-like flowers, commonly used as ground cover.
species of perennial herb used as ground cover

Examples

Liriope muscari is often planted along borders for its attractive evergreen foliage.
Gardeners value Liriope muscari for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
The clusters of purple flowers on Liriope muscari appear in late summer and autumn.

Etymology

From the genus name Liriope and species name muscari, following botanical Latin naming conventions.
