articulatio synovialis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjəˈleɪʃioʊ saɪˌnoʊˈviːəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃɪəʊ sɪˌnəʊˈvɪəlɪs / (BrE)
A freely movable joint in the body, also known as a synovial joint, characterized by the presence of a fluid-filled joint cavity.
freely movable joint; synovial joint
medical

Examples

The knee is the largest articulatio synovialis in the human body, allowing a wide range of motion.
Damage to the articulatio synovialis can lead to pain and limited mobility in the affected joint.
Arthritis often affects the articulatio synovialis, causing inflammation and stiffness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synovial joint - a joint that permits free movement; medical/anatomical term

Etymology

From Latin 'articulatio' (joint) and 'synovialis' (of synovia, joint fluid).
