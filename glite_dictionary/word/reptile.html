reptile

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛp.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛp.taɪl / (BrE)
any cold-blooded vertebrate animal of the class Reptilia, such as snakes, lizards, turtles, or crocodiles, typically having dry scaly skin and laying soft-shelled eggs on land
cold-blooded, scaly vertebrate animal

Examples

The zoo has a special exhibit for reptile species found in the rainforest.
Snakes and lizards are two well-known types of reptile.
She was fascinated by the ancient reptile fossils discovered in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amphibian - a cold-blooded vertebrate of the class Amphibia, such as frogs or salamanders
  • Antonym: mammal - a warm-blooded vertebrate animal of the class Mammalia, distinguished by the possession of hair or fur, females secrete milk

Etymology

From Latin 'reptilis' (creeping, crawling), from 'repere' (to creep).
