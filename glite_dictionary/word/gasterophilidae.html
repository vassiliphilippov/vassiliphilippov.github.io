Gasterophilidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs.təˌrɑfəˈlɪdəˌi / (AmE), / ɡastəˌrɒfɪˈlɪdɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of botflies whose larvae are internal parasites of horses and other animals, commonly known as horse botflies.
family of horse botflies

Examples

The Gasterophilidae family includes several species that infest horses with their larvae.
Veterinarians study Gasterophilidae to understand and manage equine parasitic infections.
Gasterophilidae are well known for their unique life cycle involving attachment to the digestive tract of horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gasterophilus' (a genus of botfly) and the suffix '-idae' used for zoological family names.
