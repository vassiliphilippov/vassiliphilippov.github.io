transmittance

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsmɪtəns / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt(ə)ns / (BrE)
the fraction of the incident radiant energy that passes through a substance, usually expressed as a ratio or percentage
fraction of radiant energy transmitted

Examples

The transmittance of the glass was measured to determine how much sunlight could pass through.
High transmittance is desired in lenses to allow more light to reach the eye.
Ultraviolet transmittance is often reduced by protective coatings on windows.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorbance - the amount of light absorbed by a substance

Etymology

From 'transmit' + '-ance', signifying the quality of being transmitted.
