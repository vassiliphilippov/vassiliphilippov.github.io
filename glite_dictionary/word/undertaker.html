undertaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.dɚˌteɪ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌn.dəˌteɪ.kə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to arrange and manage funerals
person who manages funerals

Examples

The undertaker arrived early to prepare for the service.
My grandfather worked as an undertaker in a small town for many years.
After the death in the family, we contacted a local undertaker to help arrange the funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funeral director - person whose business is arranging funerals

Etymology

From undertake + -er, originally meaning 'one who undertakes (a task)', later narrowed to funerals.
