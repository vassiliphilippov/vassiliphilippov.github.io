to nuzzle

1. VERB

/ ˈnʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌzəl / (BrE)
(of an animal) to dig out or search for something using the nose or snout, often by rooting or pushing aside material.
dig out with the nose or snout

Examples

The pig used its snout to nuzzle the truffle from beneath the soil.
Farmers watched as the boar began to nuzzle through the leaves, searching for food.
Dogs sometimes nuzzle in the dirt to uncover hidden items.

Synset Examples

the pig nuzzled the truffle

2. VERB

/ ˈnʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌzəl / (BrE)
to gently rub or push with the nose or face, often as a sign of affection
rub or push with nose/face

Examples

The puppy likes to nuzzle against my arm when it’s sleepy.
She gently nuzzled her baby's cheek, making him giggle.
He nuzzled his face into her hair, seeking comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable position

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly related to 'nose'.

3. VERB

/ ˈnʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌzəl / (BrE)
to settle or arrange oneself in a comfortable and cozy position, often by pressing or nestling closely against something or someone
settle closely in a cozy position

Examples

The dog likes to nuzzle into the blankets before falling asleep.
She nuzzled against his shoulder as they watched the movie.
On cold mornings, the kitten would nuzzle under the covers to stay warm.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable position
  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close for warmth or affection

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to Low German 'nuscheln' (to mumble), influenced by 'nose'.
