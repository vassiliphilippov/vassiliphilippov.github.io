inwards

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədz / (BrE)
towards or into the inside of something; inward
toward the inside

Examples

The door swung inwards as she pushed it gently.
As the tide comes in, the water flows inwards towards the shore.
He bent inwards to get a closer look at the painting.

Synset Examples

come in
smash in the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outwards - toward the outside

Etymology

From Middle English inwardes, from inward + -s (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈɪnwərdz / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədz / (BrE)
toward the center or interior of something; from the outside toward the inside.
toward the center or interior

Examples

As the storm approached, everyone moved inwards to avoid the heavy rain.
The petals of the flower curl inwards at night.
She felt a strange sensation pulling her focus inwards rather than outwards.

Synset Examples

move the needle further inwards!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inward - toward the inside or center; alternatively used as adverb and adjective
  • Antonym: outwards - toward the outside or exterior

Etymology

From Middle English inewardes, from Old English inweardes (inwardly, towards the inside).
