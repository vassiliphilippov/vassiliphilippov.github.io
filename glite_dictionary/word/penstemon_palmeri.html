Penstemon palmeri

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəˌmɒn pælˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstəmɒn pɑːlˈmɪəri / (BrE)
a species of Penstemon with fragrant, inflated white to reddish-pink flowers clustered on upright stems, native to the southwestern United States
a southwestern US flowering plant species

Examples

Penstemon palmeri thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout the American Southwest.
Gardeners prize Penstemon palmeri for its showy, fragrant blossoms.
We spotted several blooming Penstemon palmeri along the hiking trail in Utah.

Etymology

Named after botanist Edward Palmer; Penstemon from Greek 'pente' (five) and 'stēmōn' (stamen).
