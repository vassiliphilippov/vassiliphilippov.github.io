arginine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdʒɪˌnin / (AmE), / ˈɑːdʒɪniːn / (BrE)
an amino acid important in nutrition, found in proteins and necessary for proper bodily function, especially the production of sperm.
an amino acid essential for nutrition
medical

Examples

Arginine is often included in dietary supplements aimed at improving athletic performance.
A deficiency in arginine can negatively affect fertility in some animals.
The researchers measured blood levels of arginine before and after treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compounds that combine to form proteins

Etymology

From German Arginin, based on Latin 'argentum' (silver), referring to its discovery in silver-rich extracts.
