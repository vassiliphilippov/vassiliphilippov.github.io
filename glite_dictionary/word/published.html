published

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌblɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a work) produced in printed form and made available for distribution or sale.
issued in print for sale or distribution

Examples

Her published novel received critical acclaim shortly after its release.
Many scientists strive to have their research published in reputable academic journals.
The museum displayed several previously published and unpublished manuscripts by the artist.

Synset Examples

the complete published works Dickens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpublished - not issued in print or made publicly available

Etymology

Past participle of 'publish', from Latin 'publicare' (to make public).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00472920-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌblɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃt / (BrE)
(of information, accounts, etc.) made available to the public in printed or digital form, especially formally or officially.
made available to the public; formally released

Examples

The published accounts of the company were reviewed by external auditors.
Her published research has been cited by academics worldwide.
The findings will not be considered official until they are published in a scientific journal.

Synset Examples

published accounts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpublished - not officially released or made public

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'publish', from Old French 'publier', from Latin 'publicare' (make public).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00471990-s
Synset POS: s
