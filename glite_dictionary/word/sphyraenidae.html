Sphyraenidae

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈriːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sfɪˈriːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, active fishes found in tropical and subtropical waters, commonly known as barracudas.
family of fishes; barracudas

Examples

The Sphyraenidae family includes several species of barracuda found around coral reefs.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of Sphyraenidae in tropical oceans.
Many sport fishermen seek members of the Sphyraenidae family for their size and speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracuda - any of various predatory fishes of the family Sphyraenidae

Etymology

From New Latin Sphyraenidae, from Sphyraena (type genus).
