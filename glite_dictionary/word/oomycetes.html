Oomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.oʊ.maɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.əʊ.maɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A group of fungus-like, nonphotosynthetic organisms that resemble algae and reproduce by forming oospores, sometimes classified as protoctists.
fungus-like organisms that form oospores

Examples

Many plant pathogens belong to the Oomycetes, causing diseases such as late blight in potatoes.
The Oomycetes differ from true fungi by having cell walls made of cellulose rather than chitin.
Researchers studied how the Oomycetes infect aquatic plants in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - simple organism such as molds, mildews, and mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek 'oo-' (egg) + 'mycetes' (fungus), referring to egg-shaped spores.

Examples

The Oomycetes differ from true fungi in several key structural ways.
