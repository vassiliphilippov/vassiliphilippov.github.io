fast-paced

1. ADJ

/ fæst ˈpeɪst / (AmE), / fɑːst ˈpeɪst / (BrE)
moving or developing very quickly; characterized by rapid progress or activity, especially in speech or writing.
moving or developing quickly

Examples

The fast-paced dialogue in the movie kept the audience engaged.
She prefers reading fast-paced novels with exciting plot twists.
Their meeting was fast-paced and covered many topics in a short time.

Synset Examples

a fast-paced talker
fast-paced fiction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow-paced - moving or developing at a slow speed
