Ceroxylon

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈrɒksɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / sɪəˈrɒksɪlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of tall South American palm trees, commonly known as wax palms, noted for their smooth, waxy trunks.
a genus of wax palms from South America

Examples

The Ceroxylon palms are the tallest of all palm species, reaching heights over 60 meters.
Colombia’s national tree is a species of Ceroxylon, known for its slender, waxy trunk.
Researchers studied how the Ceroxylon wax palms provide habitat for endangered species.

Etymology

From Greek 'kēros' (wax) and 'xylon' (wood).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈrɑksiˌlɒn / (AmE), / sɛˈrɒksɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of tall South American wax palms known for their waxy trunks and featherlike leaves.
a genus of South American wax palms

Examples

The Ceroxylon genus includes some of the tallest palm trees in the world.
Researchers studied the unique adaptation of wax to cold climates in Ceroxylon palms.
Conservation efforts aim to protect endangered species in the Ceroxylon genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax palm - any palm with a trunk coated in wax, especially Ceroxylon species

Etymology

From Greek 'kēros' (wax) + 'xylon' (wood).
