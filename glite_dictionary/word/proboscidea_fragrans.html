Proboscidea fragrans

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌbɒsɪˈdiːə ˈfreɪɡrənz / (AmE), / prɒˌbɒsɪˈdiːə ˈfreɪɡrənz / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous plant in the genus Proboscidea, known for its fragrant flowers.
herbaceous plant species with fragrant flowers

Examples

The garden’s collection includes a remarkable specimen of Proboscidea fragrans in full bloom.
Botanists are studying the unique aroma produced by Proboscidea fragrans.
Many pollinators are attracted to Proboscidea fragrans because of its fragrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Proboscidea - a genus of herbaceous plants commonly known as devil's claw

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Proboscidea' (referring to trunk-like fruit) and 'fragrans' (fragrant).
