beryllium

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɪliəm / (AmE), / bəˈrɪliəm / (BrE)
a light, strong, brittle, grey, toxic metallic element with the symbol Be and atomic number 4, used chiefly in alloys
a light metallic element; symbol Be

Examples

The researchers tested the strength of beryllium in extreme temperatures.
Because beryllium is toxic, workers must wear protective gear when handling it.
Aerospace engineers value beryllium for its combination of lightness and strength.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: magnesium - a light, silver-white metallic element, symbol Mg, atomic number 12
  • Degree Lower: lithium - a soft, silvery-white alkali metal, symbol Li, atomic number 3

Etymology

From Greek 'bēryllos' (beryl, a mineral from which it was first isolated).
