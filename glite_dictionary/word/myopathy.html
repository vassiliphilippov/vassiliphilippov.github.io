myopathy

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑpəθi / (AmE), / maɪˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
A disease or abnormal condition of the muscles, not caused by nerve problems.
disease of the muscles
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myopathy after experiencing progressive muscle weakness.
Certain medications are known to increase the risk of developing myopathy.
Genetic testing revealed that his myopathy was inherited from his mother.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neuropathy - disease or damage of the nerves

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'pathos' (suffering, disease).
