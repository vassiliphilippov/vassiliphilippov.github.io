ranger

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
an official employed to manage, protect, and oversee a forest, park, or nature reserve
official managing a forest or park

Examples

The ranger explained the rules of the national park to the visitors.
A team of rangers patrols the forest every day to prevent illegal logging.
After the storm, the ranger checked the trails for fallen trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warden - a person responsible for supervising a particular place or area
  • Antonym: poacher - a person who illegally hunts or fishes on protected land

Etymology

From Old French *rangier* (to arrange, set in a row), related to 'range' (a stretch of land).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a member of a special military unit trained for rapid, surprise attacks, often operating behind enemy lines
member of special military unit

Examples

The ranger unit successfully executed a night operation deep behind enemy lines.
He trained for months to qualify as an Army ranger.
During the ceremony, several rangers were recognized for their bravery in combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commando - a soldier specially trained for surprise raids
  • Synonym: paratrooper - a soldier trained to parachute into combat
  • Degree Lower: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot

Etymology

From Middle English 'ranger', meaning a forest guardian; later used for special military units in the 18th century.
