Lycium halimifolium

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiː.əm hæˌlɪ.mɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪ.əm haˌlɪ.mɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub with spiny branches and violet-purple flowers, native to southeastern Europe to China, that produces orange-red berries.
species of deciduous shrub with spiny branches

Examples

The gardens were lined with Lycium halimifolium, adding splashes of color every spring.
Wildlife often feeds on the berries of Lycium halimifolium during the autumn months.
Researchers studied how Lycium halimifolium adapts to various soil conditions across its native range.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lycium' is a genus name originating from Lycia, a region in Anatolia; 'halimifolium' means 'saltbush-leaved' (from Latin 'halimus' and 'folium' for leaf).
