masked

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæst / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskt / (BrE)
Having markings or features that resemble a mask, often referring to animals with distinctively colored areas on their faces.
having mask-like markings

Examples

The masked owl has distinct dark circles around its eyes.
Many species of fish are described as masked due to their unique facial patterns.
The panda's masked appearance makes it instantly recognizable.

Synset Examples

the masked face of a raccoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banded - marked with a band or stripe

2. ADJ

/ mæst / (AmE), / mɑːskt / (BrE)
having its true nature or intention concealed, especially to mislead or deceive
concealed or disguised (intent or nature)

Examples

The CEO issued a masked threat to the employees about consequences for poor performance.
His true feelings were masked by a calm and friendly demeanor.
The offer seemed generous, but there was a masked intention behind it.

Synset Examples

masked threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguised - having concealed or altered appearance or character
  • Antonym: barefaced - not disguised or concealed; open

Etymology

From mask + -ed, indicating 'covered as with a mask' or 'disguised'.
