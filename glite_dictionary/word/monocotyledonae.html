Monocotyledonae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.nəˌkɑː.tɪlɪˈdoʊniː / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˌkɒ.tɪlɪˈdəʊniː / (BrE)
A large class of flowering plants (angiosperms) whose seeds contain a single cotyledon, typically with parallel-veined leaves; includes grasses, lilies, palms, and orchids.
class of plants with one seed leaf

Examples

Many staple crops, such as rice and wheat, belong to the Monocotyledonae.
Palm trees are classic examples of plants classified under the Monocotyledonae.
Unlike dicotyledons, members of the Monocotyledonae typically have parallel leaf veins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicotyledon - group of flowering plants with two seed leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'kotyledon' (cotyledon, seed leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.noʊˌkɑː.tɪlɪˈdoʊ.niː / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˌkɒ.tɪ.lɪˈdəʊ.niː / (BrE)
a major class of flowering plants whose seeds contain a single embryonic leaf, including grasses, lilies, orchids, and palms
class of flowering plants with one seed leaf

Examples

Most grasses and some common garden plants, such as lilies, belong to the Monocotyledonae.
Orchids are one of the largest families within the Monocotyledonae.
The structure of their leaves helps to distinguish Monocotyledonae from other classes of flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicotyledon - flowering plant with two seed leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) + 'cotyledon' (seed leaf).
