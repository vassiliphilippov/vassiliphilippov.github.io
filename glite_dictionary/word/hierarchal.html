hierarchal

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈɛr.ɑːr.kəl / (AmE), / haɪˈɛə.rɑː.kəl / (BrE)
arranged or classified into levels or layers according to rank or authority; relating to a hierarchy.
arranged in levels or hierarchy

Examples

The new manager implemented a hierarchal structure to improve communication within the company.
Religious organizations often maintain a strictly hierarchal order among their officials.
Her approach to teaching is less hierarchal and encourages more collaboration among students.

Synset Examples

it has been said that only a hierarchical society with a leisure class at the top can produce works of art
in her hierarchical set of values honesty comes first

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchical - arranged in order of rank or authority

Etymology

From Medieval Latin hierarchia (sacred order), modeled on Greek hierarkhia.
