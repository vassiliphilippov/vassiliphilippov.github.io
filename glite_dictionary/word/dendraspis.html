Dendraspis

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdræspɪs / (AmE), / dɛnˈdraspɪs / (BrE)
A former genus name for the mambas, a group of highly venomous snakes native to Africa; now usually classified under the genus Dendroaspis.
former genus of African mamba snakes

Examples

The black mamba was once classified in the genus Dendraspis before being placed in Dendroaspis.
Some older scientific texts still refer to mambas as Dendraspis species.
A specialist explained that Dendraspis is now considered a synonym of Dendroaspis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mamba - highly venomous African snake of the genus Dendroaspis

Etymology

From Greek 'dendron' (tree) and 'aspis' (snake, shield), referring to tree-dwelling snakes.

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdræspɪs / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɑːspɪs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous African snakes, commonly known as mambas.
genus of African mambas (snakes)

Examples

The black mamba is a well-known species within the genus Dendraspis.
Scientists have studied the venom composition of several members of Dendraspis.
Due to habitat loss, some Dendraspis species face population declines in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mamba - a highly venomous African snake of the genus Dendraspis

Etymology

From Greek 'dendron' (tree) and 'aspis' (snake), reflecting the arboreal habits of these snakes.
