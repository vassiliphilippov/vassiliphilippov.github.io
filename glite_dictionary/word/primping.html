primping

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪmpɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of grooming oneself with excessive or careful attention to detail, often in preparation for an event.
elaborate or finicky grooming

Examples

Her primping before the mirror delayed our departure by half an hour.
He spent the morning primping for his job interview.
All the primping in the world couldn't calm her nerves before the big date.

Synset Examples

the primping alone took more than an hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preening - to groom oneself with elaborate care

Etymology

From the verb 'primp,' possibly imitative or related to 'prim.'
