unnerved

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnɜrvd / (AmE), / ʌnˈnɜːvd / (BrE)
deprived of courage or confidence; made to feel nervous or fearful.
deprived of courage or confidence

Examples

She felt unnerved by the strange noises coming from the empty hallway.
After receiving the unexpected news, he looked completely unnerved.
The judge's quiet stare left the witness unnerved and hesitant to speak further.

Synset Examples

the steeplejack, exhausted and unnerved, couldn't hold on to his dangerous perch much longer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing confidence

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'nerve', referring to loss of nerve (courage).
