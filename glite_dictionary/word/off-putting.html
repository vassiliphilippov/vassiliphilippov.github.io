off-putting

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌpʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌpʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing dislike, annoyance, or a feeling of repulsion
causing dislike or annoyance

Examples

Her constant criticism was really off-putting to everyone in the group.
The restaurant's strange smell was a bit off-putting, but the food was excellent.
He made an off-putting comment that ended the conversation abruptly.

Synset Examples

an off-putting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or pleasing
  • Antonym: appealing - pleasant or attractive

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'put off', meaning to repel or discourage; formed as an adjective in the 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔf ˌpʊ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌpʊ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
unpleasant or disturbing in a way that makes someone feel uncomfortable or unwilling to continue; discouraging or repellent.
discouraging or repellent; unpleasant

Examples

Some people find the formal atmosphere of the restaurant off-putting.
The strong smell from the kitchen was rather off-putting.
His rude behavior can be off-putting to new acquaintances.

Synset Examples

The trappings of upper-class life are off-putting and sterile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing; likely to attract interest or attention
  • Antonym: appealing - attractive or interesting; pleasant and inviting

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'put off', meaning to repel or discourage, combined as an adjective.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɔf ˌpʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌpʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
making you feel dislike or disapproval; unpleasant or distasteful.
causing dislike or distaste

Examples

The strong smell in the room was quite off-putting to the guests.
I find his arrogance really off-putting in social situations.
Her off-putting attitude made it hard for her to make friends.

Synset Examples

She found his knobbish remarks quite off-putting.
He finds her toffy behavior quite off-putting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or agreeable
  • Antonym: appealing - attractive or interesting

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'put off,' meaning to repel or discourage, plus the participial ending '-ing.'
