pademelon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpædəˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˌpadɪˈmɛlən / (BrE)
A small, reddish-brown marsupial of the genus Thylogale, related to wallabies, found in scrubby areas of Australia and New Guinea.
small marsupial of Australia and New Guinea

Examples

The pademelon darted through the undergrowth as we walked along the forest trail.
Researchers are studying how habitat loss is affecting the pademelon population in Tasmania.
We spotted two pademelons feeding quietly at the edge of the clearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized kangaroo
  • Degree Higher: kangaroo - large hopping marsupial of Australia

Etymology

From Dharuk (an Indigenous Australian language), possibly via earlier English spelling variants.
