stagflationary

1. ADJ

/ stæɡˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / stæɡˈfleɪʃənəri / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of stagflation, a situation in which high inflation and high unemployment occur simultaneously.
relating to economic stagflation

Examples

The economy remained in a stagflationary state despite government intervention.
Analysts are concerned about the potential for another stagflationary period in the global market.
The central bank’s policies failed to resolve the stagflationary pressures affecting growth and employment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagflation - persistent high inflation combined with high unemployment and stagnant demand

Etymology

Formed from 'stagflation' + '-ary', where 'stagflation' blends 'stagnation' and 'inflation'.
