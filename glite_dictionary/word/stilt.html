stilt

1. NOUN

/ stɪlt / (AmE), / stɪlt / (BrE)
a long-legged wading bird with black-and-white plumage, found in inland ponds, marshes, or brackish lagoons
long-legged black-and-white wading bird

Examples

We watched a stilt wading gracefully through the shallow water near the reeds.
A stilt was spotted nesting at the edge of the brackish lagoon.
The long red legs of the stilt help it move through deep marshes with ease.

2. NOUN

/ stɪlt / (AmE), / stɪlt / (BrE)
one of a pair of tall wooden or metal poles with supports for the feet, used to walk high above the ground
pole with foot support for walking high

Examples

The children had fun learning to walk on stilts at the summer fair.
Circus performers often use stilts to tower above the crowd and entertain.
Workers used stilts to paint the high ceilings without scaffolding.

Synset Examples

he was so tall I thought he was on stilts

Etymology

Probably from Middle English stilt (support pole), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ stɪlt / (AmE), / stɪlt / (BrE)
a long-legged wading bird of brackish marshes, especially found in Australia, known for its slender legs and three-toed feet.
long-legged Australian wading bird

Examples

We spotted a stilt feeding gracefully along the muddy edge of the marsh.
The delicate legs of a stilt allow it to wade through shallow water in search of food.
Many birdwatchers travel to see the rare Australian stilt in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocet - another type of long-legged wading bird

4. NOUN

/ stɪlt / (AmE), / stɪlt / (BrE)
a long, upright post or column, especially one used to support a building above the ground or water
supporting post for buildings

Examples

The house was built on stilts to protect it from flooding during the rainy season.
Workers drove several stilts into the sand to support the new pier.
Many coastal homes in the area stand on stilts above the marshy ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile - a long, slender column driven into the ground to support structures

Etymology

From Middle English 'stylt', of uncertain origin; related to Low German 'stelt' (support, leg).
