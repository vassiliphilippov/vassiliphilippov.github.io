textolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstoʊˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstəʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A laminated composite material, often made from layers of fabric or paper bonded with resin, used in electrical engineering for components such as inserts and bearings.
laminated composite material for electrical components

Examples

The casing was made of textolite to provide extra insulation for the device.
Engineers often choose textolite because of its durability and resistance to heat.
The laboratory showcased vintage electronics featuring original textolite panels.

Synset Examples

Textolite is characterized by its heat endurance, elasticity (as compared to metals) and long service period.

Etymology

From Russian 'текстолит' (tekstolit), modelled on 'textile' and '-ite'; originally a trade name.
