assured

1. ADJ

/ əˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / əˈʃʊəd / (BrE)
having confidence or certainty; showing assurance or self-assurance
confident; showing assurance

Examples

Her performance was assured and full of grace from start to finish.
He spoke in an assured voice that put everyone at ease.
The artist moved her brush in assured strokes across the canvas.

Synset Examples

she paints with an assured hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - sure of oneself; displaying certainty
  • Antonym: hesitant - uncertain or lacking confidence

Etymology

From Old French *asseurer* (to assure), based on Latin *securus* (secure).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / əˈʃʊəd / (BrE)
confident and certain to happen or be achieved; guaranteed
guaranteed; certain to happen

Examples

Her position in the company is assured thanks to years of hard work.
We left the meeting with an assured sense of success.
They invested to provide their children with an assured future.

Synset Examples

a tiny but assured income
we can never have completely assured lives

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not definite or sure
  • Synonym: guaranteed - certain to happen; ensured

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurer', based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'securus' (secure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02101483-s
Synset POS: s
