catalufa

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈluːfə / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈluːfə / (BrE)
A brightly colored, carnivorous fish found in the western Atlantic and West Indies waters, belonging to the family Priacanthidae.
a brightly colored Atlantic and Caribbean fish

Examples

Divers were fascinated by the brilliant red color of the catalufa swimming near the coral reef.
The chef prepared a delicious dish using freshly caught catalufa from the local market.
Marine biologists are studying the feeding habits of the catalufa in the Caribbean Sea.

Etymology

From Spanish 'catalufa', likely of Caribbean origin.
