inhospitality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌhɑːspɪˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnhɒspɪˈtælɪti / (BrE)
unfriendly or unwelcoming treatment towards guests or strangers
unfriendliness; lack of hospitality

Examples

The travelers were surprised by the inhospitality they received in the small town.
She could not excuse the inhospitality of her neighbors during the holidays.
Despite his reputation for warmth, a moment of inhospitality cost him many friends.

Synset Examples

he was taken aback by such inhospitality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hospitality - friendly and generous reception and entertainment of guests

Etymology

From Latin hospes (guest, host) with prefix in- (not) and hospitality.
