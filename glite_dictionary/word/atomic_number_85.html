atomic number 85

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ ˈeɪti faɪv / (AmE), / əˌtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə ˈeɪti faɪv / (BrE)
the chemical element astatine; a highly unstable radioactive element and the heaviest of the halogen series, occurring as a decay product of uranium and thorium
element astatine (At), atomic number 85

Examples

The isotope with atomic number 85 is known as astatine, and it is extremely rare in nature.
Scientists have studied the decay of atomic number 85 to better understand radioactive processes.
Because it is highly unstable, atomic number 85 has no significant commercial uses.
