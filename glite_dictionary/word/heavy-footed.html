heavy-footed

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their movements) moving in a way that lacks ease or lightness; clumsy or lumbering in movement.
moving with a lack of ease or lightness

Examples

After a long day at work, he made his way up the stairs in a heavy-footed manner.
Her heavy-footed stride could be heard from across the hallway.
Despite his excitement, his heavy-footed steps betrayed his exhaustion.

Synset Examples

his tired heavy-footed walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or action
  • Antonym: nimble - quick and light in movement or action

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
moving in a way that is slow, clumsy, or lacking lightness, as if weighed down
moving clumsily or heavily

Examples

After running the marathon, he became heavy-footed on his walk home.
The heavy-footed guard made it easy to hear him approaching, even from a distance.
Despite her effort to be quiet, her heavy-footed steps echoed down the hallway.

Synset Examples

The heavy-footed elephant trudged through the jungle.
She walked heavy-footed after a long day.
He was heavy-footed on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - moving awkwardly or without grace
  • Antonym: light-footed - moving quickly and lightly
