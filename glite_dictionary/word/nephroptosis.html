nephroptosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛfroʊpˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrɒpˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a kidney has descended from its normal position (prolapse of the kidney)
prolapse of the kidney
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with nephroptosis after noticing a dropped kidney on the ultrasound.
Some individuals with nephroptosis may experience pain when standing for prolonged periods.
Surgical intervention may be required if nephroptosis causes significant discomfort or urinary symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + ptosis (a falling)
