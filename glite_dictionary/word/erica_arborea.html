Erica arborea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪkə ɑrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪkə ɑːˈbɔːrɪə / (BrE)
An evergreen Mediterranean shrub or small tree with fragrant white flowers and hard woody roots, used especially for making tobacco pipes.
Mediterranean shrub with fragrant flowers and hard roots

Examples

The roots of Erica arborea are highly valued for making quality tobacco pipes.
In the spring, Erica arborea produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.
Botanists found a thriving population of Erica arborea along the Mediterranean hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree heath - A common English name for Erica arborea, the Mediterranean shrub

Etymology

From Latin: 'erica' (heath) + 'arborea' (tree-like).
