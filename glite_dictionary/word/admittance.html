admittance

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪtəns / (AmE), / ədˈmɪtəns / (BrE)
permission to enter a place or the right of entry
right or permission to enter

Examples

Admittance to the concert is only for ticket holders.
She was denied admittance to the members-only club.
The sign on the door read 'No admittance without authorization.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - act of allowing to enter or be accepted
  • Antonym: exclusion - the act or state of not being allowed to enter

Etymology

From Latin 'admittentia', from 'admittere' (to allow to enter).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪtn̩s / (AmE), / ədˈmɪtns / (BrE)
the act of allowing someone to enter a place or join an organization
permission or right to enter

Examples

No admittance will be granted without a valid ticket.
The club reserves the right to refuse admittance to anyone.
Due to security concerns, admittance to the facility is strictly controlled.

Synset Examples

the surgery was performed on his second admission to the clinic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the process or fact of entering a place or institution

Etymology

From Latin *admittĕre* (to allow to enter) via Old French *admittance*.

WordNet ID: oewn-01250738-n
Synset POS: n
