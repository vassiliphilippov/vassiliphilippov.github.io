Euphractus sexcinctus

1. NOUN

/ juˈfræktəs sɛkˈsɪŋktəs / (AmE), / juːˈfræktəs sɛkˈsɪŋktəs / (BrE)
A species of armadillo native to South America, characterized by six movable bands and hairy underparts.
a species of armadillo with six bands

Examples

Researchers observed the Euphractus sexcinctus digging for insects in its natural habitat.
The Euphractus sexcinctus is commonly known as the six-banded armadillo.
Conservationists are studying the population trends of Euphractus sexcinctus across South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six-banded armadillo - another common name for Euphractus sexcinctus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Euphractus' is the genus name, and 'sexcinctus' means 'six-banded'.
