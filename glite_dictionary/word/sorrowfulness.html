sorrowfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːroʊfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of feeling deep sadness or grief
state of deep sadness

Examples

His sorrowfulness was evident as he spoke about his late friend.
The poem expresses a deep sorrowfulness that lingers long after reading.
Despite her sorrowfulness, she tried to smile for her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadness - the state of being sad
  • Antonym: happiness - the state of being happy

Etymology

From 'sorrow' + '-ful' + '-ness'. Related to Old English 'sorg'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːroʊf əlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊf əlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sorrowful; deep sadness or unhappiness
the state of being sorrowful or sad

Examples

Her sorrowfulness was evident after she received the bad news.
There was a deep sorrowfulness in his eyes that could not be hidden.
The play captured the sorrowfulness of loss and longing with great sensitivity.

Synset Examples

she tired of his perpetual sadness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadness - the condition or quality of being sad
  • Antonym: happiness - the state of being happy

Etymology

From 'sorrowful' + suffix '-ness', originally from Old English 'sorg' (sorrow).
