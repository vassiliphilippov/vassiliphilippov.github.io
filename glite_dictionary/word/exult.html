to exult

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzʌlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzʌlt / (BrE)
to show or feel triumphant joy; to rejoice greatly, especially as a result of success or victory
show or feel triumphant joy

Examples

The team exulted after winning the championship for the first time in a decade.
She exulted in her success, unable to hide her excitement from her friends.
Crowds exulted in the streets as the final results were announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • Antonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret, often for something lost

Etymology

From Latin exsultare (to leap up), frequentative of exsilire, from ex- (out of) + salire (to leap).

2. VERB

/ ɪgˈzʌlt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzʌlt / (BrE)
to show or feel great happiness or triumphant joy, especially as the result of a success
show or feel great joy

Examples

The players exulted after their unexpected victory in the finals.
She couldn't help but exult when she received the good news.
Crowds gathered to exult in the nation's historic achievement.

Synset Examples

Who cannot exult in Spring?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • Antonym: to mourn - to feel or express sorrow or grief

Etymology

From Latin exsultare (to leap up), frequentative of exsilire (to spring out).
