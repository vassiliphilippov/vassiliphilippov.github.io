dolce

1. ADV

/ ˈdoʊltʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɒltʃeɪ / (BrE)
(in music) to be played gently and sweetly.
musical direction: gently and sweetly

Examples

The pianist performed the passage dolce, creating a soft and tender atmosphere.
The score instructs the violinist to play this melody dolce for a sweeter tone.
She interpreted the phrase dolce, emphasizing its gentle and lyrical quality.

Etymology

From Italian dolce (sweet), from Latin dulcis (sweet).
