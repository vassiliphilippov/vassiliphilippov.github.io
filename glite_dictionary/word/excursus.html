excursus

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskɝː.səs / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜː.səs / (BrE)
a detailed discussion or digression that departs from the main subject, often included as an appendix or explanatory note in a written work
detailed digression from main subject

Examples

The essay included an excursus on the history of the region, which provided valuable context.
In the middle of his speech, the professor offered an excursus about ancient civilizations.
The book features several lengthy excurses that explore related topics in depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'excursus' (a running out), from 'excurrere' meaning 'to run out' or 'digress'.
