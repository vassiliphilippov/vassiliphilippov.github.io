synergistically

1. ADV

/ sɪnɚˈdʒɪstɪkli / (AmE), / sɪnəˈdʒɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or results from synergy; in a cooperative manner that produces a combined effect greater than the sum of separate effects.
in a synergistic, cooperative way

Examples

The two teams worked synergistically to complete the complex project ahead of schedule.
These vitamins act synergistically to improve overall health and increase energy levels.
When used together, the drugs affect the body synergistically, enhancing their therapeutic benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperatively - in a cooperative manner
  • Antonym: independently - without cooperation with others

Etymology

From synergy, itself from Greek sunergos (working together), plus the adverb-forming suffix -ally.

2. ADV

/ sɪˌnɝːˈdʒɪstɪkli / (AmE), / sɪˌnɜːˈdʒɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or produces synergy; acting together to create an effect greater than the sum of their separate effects.
in a synergistic manner

Examples

The two medications work synergistically to reduce inflammation more effectively than either alone.
These enzymes interact synergistically to accelerate the chemical reaction.
Multiple muscles contract synergistically to produce smooth and coordinated movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synergistic - relating to synergy; acting together for greater effect
  • Antonym: antagonistically - in a way that opposes or counteracts, the opposite of synergistically

Etymology

From synergistic (from Greek 'synergos', working together) + -ally.

Source: WordNet
