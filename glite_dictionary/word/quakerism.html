Quakerism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The religious beliefs and practices of the Society of Friends, emphasizing pacifism, simplicity, inner spiritual experience, and rejecting formal creeds and rituals.
beliefs and practices of the Quakers

Examples

She had a deep respect for Quakerism and its emphasis on peace.
The principles of Quakerism shaped his charitable work and personal integrity.
Many are drawn to Quakerism because of its rejection of formal rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaker - a member of the Society of Friends (Religious Society of Friends)

Etymology

Formed from 'Quaker' (member of the Society of Friends) + '-ism'. 'Quaker' originated as a nickname in the 17th century from 'to quake' with religious fervour.
