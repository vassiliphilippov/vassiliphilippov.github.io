afferent

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
a nerve that carries sensory impulses toward the central nervous system
nerve carrying signals to CNS
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that an injury to an afferent nerve can reduce sensation in that area.
Sensory information from the skin is transmitted to the brain by afferent fibers.
Damage to the afferent pathways may result in loss of feeling or reflexes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efferent - a nerve that carries impulses away from the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin afferentem, present participle of afferre (to bring to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
(of nerves or nerve impulses) carrying sensory information from the sense organs toward the central nervous system.
carrying sensory signals to the CNS
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that afferent nerves transmit sensory signals from the skin to the brain.
A problem with the afferent pathway can result in loss of sensation.
Damage to afferent fibers hinders the body's ability to respond to external stimuli.

Synset Examples

afferent nerves
afferent impulses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efferent - (of nerves or impulses) carrying signals away from the central nervous system to the body

Etymology

From Latin afferens, present participle of afferre (to bring toward).
