vinery

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəri / (AmE), / ˈvaɪnəri / (BrE)
A place, typically a farm or plantation, where grapevines are grown, especially for producing wine grapes.
farm for growing grapevines

Examples

They visited the local vinery to learn about traditional grape growing methods.
The estate features a historic vinery that has been operating for over a century.
She works at a vinery in the Napa Valley, helping harvest the grapes each season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vineyard - a plantation of grapevines, especially for wine production

Etymology

From vine + -ery, patterned after 'winery'.
