Alecto

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɛktoʊ / (AmE), / əˈlɛktəʊ / (BrE)
One of the three Furies in Greek and Roman mythology, responsible for punishing moral crimes, particularly those involving anger.
Fury who punishes anger (mythology)

Examples

According to the myth, Alecto was relentless in pursuing those driven by anger.
In the painting, Alecto appears with snakes in her hair, symbolizing her fury.
The legend says Alecto tormented mortals who committed crimes of rage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fury - a goddess of vengeance in Roman and Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Erinyes - female deities of vengeance in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'Alecto', from Greek 'Alecto' (Ἀληκτώ), one of the Erinyes (Furies).
