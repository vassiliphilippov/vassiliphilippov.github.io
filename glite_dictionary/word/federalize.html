to federalize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to put (something, such as an organization or territory) under the control and authority of a federal government
bring under federal government control

Examples

The government decided to federalize the National Guard during the emergency.
Several countries have debated whether to federalize their education systems to ensure uniform standards.
After the crisis, the decision was made to federalize disaster response efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - to bring under control of the national government
  • Synonym: to centralize - to bring under a single central authority
  • Antonym: to privatize - to transfer from government to private control

Etymology

From federal + -ize, patterned after French fédéraliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00505545-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to unite individual states or groups into a single federation with a common government or purpose
to unite into a federation

Examples

Several states agreed to federalize and share a central government.
After years of negotiation, the regions decided to federalize for mutual security.
Some countries choose not to federalize, preferring to remain independent entities.

Synset Examples

The republics federated to become the Soviet Union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unify - to make or become united, uniform, or whole
  • Antonym: fragment - to break up or separate into smaller parts

Etymology

From French 'fédéraliser', from 'fédéral' (federal), ultimately from Latin 'foedus' (league, treaty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370095-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to unite under a federal system of government; to bring together individual states or entities into a federation
to unite under a federal system

Examples

After decades of local rule, the leaders voted to federalize the country.
The new constitution aims to federalize several regions to promote unity and stability.
Many citizens debated whether it was the right time to federalize the formerly independent states.

Synset Examples

The country was federated after the civil war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unify - to make or become united or whole
  • Antonym: fragment - to break or separate into pieces; to divide

Etymology

From federal + -ize, following the pattern of verbs formed from adjectives or nouns.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
