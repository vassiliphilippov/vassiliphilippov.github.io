Austria-Hungary

1. PROPN

/ ˌɔstriə ˈhʌŋɡəri / (AmE), / ˌɒstriə ˈhʌŋɡəri / (BrE)
a former country in central and eastern Europe, existing from 1867 to 1918, composed mainly of modern-day Austria, Hungary, and several other nations
former country in central/eastern Europe, 1867–1918

Examples

The collapse of Austria-Hungary at the end of World War I led to the creation of several new nations.
Franz Ferdinand was the heir to the throne of Austria-Hungary before his assassination in 1914.
Many people in modern Central Europe can trace their ancestry back to the former empire of Austria-Hungary.

Etymology

From the official name of the dual monarchy formed in 1867 when the Austrian Empire and the Kingdom of Hungary united under a single monarch.
