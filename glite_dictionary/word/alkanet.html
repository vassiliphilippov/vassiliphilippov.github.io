alkanet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.nɪt / (BrE)
a perennial or biennial herb of the borage family, typically with blue flowers, sometimes cultivated for its roots or ornamental value
a herb with blue flowers, borage family

Examples

The gardener planted alkanet along the edge of the flower bed for its lovely blue blooms.
Some traditional dyes are made from the root of alkanet.
Wild alkanet can often be found growing in meadows and roadside verges.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-ḥinnāʼ' (henna); passed into Middle English via Old Spanish and Medieval Latin.
