shoveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvələr / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvələ / (BrE)
a person whose job or task is to move or remove material with a shovel
person who shovels

Examples

The shoveller cleared the walkway of snow each morning.
A skilled shoveller can move tons of coal in a single shift.
During the cleanup, every shoveller was given a sturdy tool and protective gloves.

Synset Examples

a shoveler of coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digger - a person or machine that digs, especially a laborer

Etymology

From 'shovel' + '-er', denoting a person who operates a shovel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvələr / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvələ / (BrE)
a freshwater duck of the Northern Hemisphere, noted for its broad, flat bill used for sweeping food from the water
freshwater duck with a broad, flat bill

Examples

The northern shoveller is easily recognized by its distinctive spatula-shaped bill.
A flock of shovellers was spotted feeding in the wetlands during migration season.
The male shoveller displays striking green and white plumage during the breeding period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

From 'shovel' + '-er', referring to the duck's shovel-shaped bill.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01851617-n
Synset POS: n
