emotionally

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənəli / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows strong feelings or emotions.
in an emotional manner

Examples

She reacted emotionally to the news of his return.
He spoke emotionally about his childhood experiences.
The film moved her so emotionally that she cried in the theater.

Synset Examples

at the funeral he spoke emotionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm or unemotional manner

Etymology

From 'emotional' + '-ly', with 'emotional' from Latin 'emovere' (to move out, stir up).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənəli / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects the emotions.
in a way relating to emotions

Examples

She was emotionally drained after the long and difficult conversation.
The movie left the audience emotionally moved and reflective.
He is emotionally attached to his childhood home.

Synset Examples

emotionally secure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentally - in a way that relates to the mind

Etymology

From 'emotion' + '-ally', where 'emotion' comes from French 'émotion', from Latin 'emovere' (to move out, stir up).

Synset POS: r
