halobacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæloʊbəˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌhaləʊbəˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of microorganisms that thrive in highly saline environments, such as salt flats or the Dead Sea.
salt-loving microorganism genus
medical

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species of halobacterium in the salt flats of Bolivia.
The intense pink color of some salt ponds is caused by colonies of halobacterium.
Genetic studies on halobacterium have provided insight into how organisms adapt to extreme environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'halos' (salt) + New Latin 'bacterium' (rod-shaped microorganism).
