embroidery

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbroʊdəri / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪd(ə)ri / (BrE)
The art or process of decorating fabric or other materials using a needle to apply thread or yarn, often forming a raised pattern or image.
decorative needlework

Examples

Her grandmother taught her embroidery when she was a child.
The museum displayed a collection of traditional Persian embroidery.
Modern artists are finding new ways to reinvent the art of embroidery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlework - the art or process of sewing or embroidery by hand

Etymology

From Old French 'embroderie', from 'embroder' (to embroider), from Medieval Latin 'imbrodare'.

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbrɔɪdəri / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪd(ə)ri / (BrE)
the addition of decorative or sometimes fictitious details when interpreting or recounting something, especially to make it more interesting
the use of decorative or fictitious details

Examples

The historian was criticized for adding too much embroidery to the story of the battle.
His account of the conversation was full of embroidery, making the events seem more dramatic than they were.
The article is entertaining, but you can tell there's some embroidery in the details.

Synset Examples

the mystery has been heightened by many embellishments in subsequent retellings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embellishment - a decorative detail or feature added to something to make it more attractive

Etymology

From Old French 'embroderie', from 'embroder' (to embroider), ultimately from Medieval Latin 'imbroderare'.
