intramolecular

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntrəmoʊˈlɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəməˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
Occurring within a single molecule; referring to reactions or interactions between different parts of the same molecule.
within a single molecule (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The protein structure is stabilized by intramolecular hydrogen bonds.
An intramolecular reaction takes place when two functional groups within the same molecule react with each other.
Chemists studied how intramolecular forces affect the folding of polymers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intermolecular - existing or occurring between molecules

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) and 'molecular' (from Latin 'molecula,' diminutive of 'moles,' meaning 'mass').
