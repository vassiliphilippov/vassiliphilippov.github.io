Lord Britten of Aldeburgh

1. PROPN

/ lɔrd ˈbrɪtən əv ˈɔldəˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈbrɪtən əv ˈɔːldɪbɜːrə / (BrE)
The formal title of Benjamin Britten (1913–1976), a prominent English composer known for his operas and significant influence on 20th-century music.
British composer, formal title

The works of Lord Britten of Aldeburgh continue to inspire musicians around the world.
