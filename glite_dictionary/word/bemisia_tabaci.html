Bemisia tabaci

1. NOUN

/ bɛˈmɪziə təˈbeɪsaɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈmɪsiə təˈbɑːkiː / (BrE)
a species of whitefly that is a major pest of agricultural crops, feeding on many vegetables and ornamental plants
species of whitefly pest

Examples

Bemisia tabaci has caused significant losses to tomato crops in many regions.
Scientists are developing new methods to control the spread of Bemisia tabaci.
Outbreaks of Bemisia tabaci impact both greenhouse and field-grown vegetables.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Bemisia' as the genus and 'tabaci' possibly referring to tobacco, a host plant.
