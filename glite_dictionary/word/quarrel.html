quarrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
a short, heavy arrow with a four-edged head, shot from a crossbow, especially in medieval times
crossbow arrow with four-edged head

Examples

The knight fitted a quarrel into his crossbow before taking aim at the target.
During the siege, the defenders fired quarrels from the battlements to repel the attackers.
Archaeologists discovered several iron quarrels near the ruins of the ancient fortress.

Etymology

From Old French *quarrel* (bolt, dart), from Vulgar Latin *quadrellus*, diminutive of Latin *quadra* (square), referring to the shape of the head.

2. VERB

/ ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
to have an angry argument or disagreement with someone, especially about something unimportant
to argue angrily

Examples

The siblings quarreled over who would get to use the computer first.
They have quarreled so often that they barely speak anymore.
Please don't quarrel with your friends over such trivial matters.

Synset Examples

We quarreled over the question as to who discovered America
These two fellows are always scrapping over something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to exchange reasons or opinions, often in a heated way
  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion, to consent

Etymology

From Old French 'querele', from Latin 'querela' (complaint).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔɹ.əl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒɹ.əl / (BrE)
an angry argument or disagreement, often between people who are usually on good terms
angry argument or dispute

Examples

They had a quarrel over how to spend their summer vacation.
The quarrel between the neighbors lasted for months before they made up.
After a brief quarrel, the brothers quickly forgave each other.

Synset Examples

they had a quarrel
they had words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a heated exchange or disagreement
  • Synonym: fight - a violent confrontation or argument
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord in opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Old French *querele* (complaint, dispute), from Latin *querela* (complaint).
