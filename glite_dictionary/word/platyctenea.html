Platyctenea

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪkˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ˌplætɪkˈtiːniə / (BrE)
an order of marine invertebrates within the phylum Ctenophora, characterized by flattened, often creeping bodies and absence of tentacles in adults
order of marine ctenophores
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the order Platyctenea during the deep-sea expedition.
Many Platyctenea exhibit creeping movement rather than free swimming.
The anatomical traits of Platyctenea set them apart from other ctenophores.

Etymology

From Greek 'platy-' meaning flat and 'ktenos' meaning comb, referring to the animal's body shape and comb-like structures.
