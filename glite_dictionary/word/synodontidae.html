Synodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.nəˈdɑn.tɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.əʊˈdɒn.tɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of soft-finned, bottom-dwelling fishes commonly known as lizardfishes, found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.
family of bottom-dwelling lizardfishes

Examples

The Synodontidae are known for their elongated bodies and sharp teeth.
Marine biologists studied several species within the Synodontidae family along the coral reefs.
The lizardfishes, members of the Synodontidae, are often found resting on sandy sea floors.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'syn' (together) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to their toothed jaws.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌnoʊˌdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌnɒdɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of soft-finned, bottom-dwelling fishes commonly known as lizardfishes, found in marine environments worldwide.
family of soft-finned, bottom-dwelling fishes

Examples

The Synodontidae are known for their elongated bodies and sharp teeth.
Marine biologists often study Synodontidae to understand the biodiversity of tropical reefs.
A large specimen from the Synodontidae family was discovered off the coast last year.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Synodontidae, based on Synodus (a genus in the family) + -idae (a family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
