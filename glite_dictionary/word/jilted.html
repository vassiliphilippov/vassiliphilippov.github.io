jilted

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪltɪd / (BrE)
Suddenly rejected or abandoned by a lover, especially without warning.
abandoned by a lover

Examples

After being jilted by her fiancé, she struggled to trust again.
He wrote a novel about a jilted lover seeking revenge.
The jilted bride left the church in tears.

Synset Examples

jilted at the altar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumped - rejected by a romantic partner
  • Synonym: spurned - rejected, especially with disdain

Etymology

From 'jilt', probably from earlier 'jillet', diminutive of 'Jill' (girl’s name), meaning a wanton woman.
