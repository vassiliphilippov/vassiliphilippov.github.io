pragmatist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræɡmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or adheres to the philosophical doctrine of pragmatism, which judges ideas by their practical consequences
supporter of philosophical pragmatism

Examples

As a leading pragmatist, he argued that beliefs must be measured by their usefulness.
Many American philosophers in the early twentieth century were pragmatists who focused on practical effects.
The debate was shaped by pragmatist thinkers who prioritized real-world outcomes over abstract principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatism - the philosophical doctrine that evaluates theories or beliefs by their practical consequences

Etymology

From Greek *pragma* (deed, act), via French and English, relating to 'pragmatism' as a philosophy developed in the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpræɡmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who deals with problems or situations sensibly and realistically, focusing on practical results rather than abstract theories
person focused on practical results

Examples

As a pragmatist, she looked for effective solutions instead of dwelling on theoretical possibilities.
The company hired a pragmatist to steer it through the financial crisis.
He approached the negotiations as a true pragmatist, focusing on achievable outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idealist - a person guided by ideals rather than practical considerations

Etymology

From Greek *pragmatikos* (practical) via Late Latin *pragmaticus*.
