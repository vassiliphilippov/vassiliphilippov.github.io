bran-new

1. ADJ

/ brænˈnuː / (AmE), / brænˈnjuː / (BrE)
Conspicuously new; unused and in perfect condition.
conspicuously new
archaic

Examples

He wore a bran-new suit to the ceremony, looking quite distinguished.
The kitchen looked bran-new after the renovation was finished.
She proudly showed off her bran-new bicycle to her friends.

Synset Examples

shiny brand-new shoes
a spick-and-span novelty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand-new - entirely new; unused

Etymology

Variant of 'brand-new', from 'brand' (a piece of burning wood), originally meaning 'fresh from the fire.'
