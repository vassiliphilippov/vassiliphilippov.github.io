fir

1. NOUN

/ fɝː / (AmE), / fɜː / (BrE)
A type of evergreen tree of the genus Abies, typically found in upland areas, with needle-like leaves and upright cones.
evergreen tree of genus Abies

Examples

A tall fir tree stood at the edge of the snowy forest.
The furniture was made from fir wood, giving it a pleasant aroma.
Many people decorate a fir as their Christmas tree each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - another type of evergreen conifer tree
  • Synonym: spruce - a different but similar type of evergreen conifer tree

Etymology

From Old English 'furh', related to Old Norse 'fyri', meaning a conifer tree.

2. NOUN

/ fɝː / (AmE), / fɜː / (BrE)
The wood from a fir tree, typically light, nonresinous, and used for construction and carpentry.
wood from a fir tree

Examples

The table was made from solid fir, giving it a light and natural appearance.
Builders often choose fir for structural beams because of its strength and workability.
He stacked planks of fir in the workshop, preparing for the next project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - wood from a pine tree, often resinous

Etymology

From Old English 'fyrh', related to Old Norse 'fyri' (fir tree).

Synset POS: n
