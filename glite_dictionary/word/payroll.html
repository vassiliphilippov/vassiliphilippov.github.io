payroll

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌrəʊl / (BrE)
the department within an organization that is responsible for calculating and paying employees' wages or salaries
department handling employee wages

Examples

The payroll is responsible for ensuring all employees are paid on time each month.
If you have questions about your salary, please contact the payroll department.
Due to a software issue, the payroll could not process last week's payments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human resources - department responsible for managing people in an organization

Etymology

From pay + roll, referring to the list of people to be paid.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.rəʊl / (BrE)
a list of a company's employees and the amount of money they are to be paid
list of employees and their salaries

Examples

There are over 500 employees on the payroll this year.
The finance department processes the payroll every two weeks.
After the merger, the company reduced its payroll significantly.

Synset Examples

the company had a long payroll

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wage bill - the total amount paid to employees
  • Synonym: staff - the group of people employed by an organization

Etymology

From 'pay' + 'roll', originally referring to a roll (list) of those to be paid.

/ ˈpeɪˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌrəʊl / (BrE)
the total amount of money that a company pays to its employees as wages and salaries
total amount paid to employees

Examples

Many small businesses struggle to meet payroll during tough economic times.
After the merger, the combined payroll exceeded two million dollars per month.

Synset Examples

the company had a large payroll

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wages - money paid regularly for work
  • Synonym: salary - fixed regular payment, typically monthly

Etymology

From 'pay' + 'roll'; originally referring to the roll or list of persons to be paid.
