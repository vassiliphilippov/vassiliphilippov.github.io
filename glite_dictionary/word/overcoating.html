overcoating

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈkoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
an additional layer of protective coating, such as paint or varnish, applied over an existing surface
additional protective coating

Examples

The wooden fence needed an overcoating to protect it from the harsh winter weather.
After the primer has dried, apply the overcoating carefully for a smooth finish.
A clear overcoating was used to seal and preserve the mural on the wall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercoat - a coating applied beneath the final coat of paint
  • Synonym: topcoat - the final coat of paint or finish applied to a surface

Etymology

From 'overcoat' (a covering layer), with '-ing' forming the noun.
