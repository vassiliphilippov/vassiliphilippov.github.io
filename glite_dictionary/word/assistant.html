assistant

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / əˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
a person who helps or supports someone in their work or in achieving a goal
person who helps or supports another

Examples

Sarah was promoted to assistant manager after showing great leadership skills.
The scientist worked late into the night with the help of her trusted assistant.
We need an assistant to handle administrative tasks in the office.

Synset Examples

my invaluable assistant
they hired additional help to finish the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helper - a person who assists someone else
  • Synonym: aide - a person who acts as an assistant
  • Antonym: supervisor - a person in charge of overseeing others

Etymology

From Latin 'assistens', present participle of 'assistere' (to stand by, assist).

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / əˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
having a role or function that is subordinate to another, especially in a job title.
subordinate in a job title

Examples

The company hired an assistant manager to support the department head.
She accepted the assistant director position in the new film project.
He was promoted from assistant professor to full professor after several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deputy - a person who is the second in rank to another and can act as a substitute
  • Antonym: chief - the person in highest rank or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'assistens', present participle of 'assistere' (to stand by, help).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00796805-s
Synset POS: s
