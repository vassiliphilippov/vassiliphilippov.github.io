2. ADJ

/ ˈɪtsi ˌbɪtsi / (AmE), / ˈɪtsi ˌbɪtsi / (BrE)
extremely small; tiny
very small; tiny (informal)
informal

Examples

She found an itsy-bitsy spider crawling across the windowsill.
The bakery sells delicious itsy-bitsy cupcakes that are perfect for parties.
Even in the itsy-bitsy apartment, they managed to make it feel cozy.

Synset Examples

He built an itsy-bitsy model of the Eiffel Tower.
The kitten was just an itsy-bitsy ball of fur.
She wore an itsy-bitsy dress to the beach party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - very small in size
  • Synonym: minute - extremely small
  • Antonym: huge - extremely large

Etymology

Reduplicative form, likely from 'itsy' (diminutive of 'it') and 'bitsy' (from 'bit'), popularized in American English in the 20th century.
