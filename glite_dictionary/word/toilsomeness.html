toilsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪl.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪl.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of requiring hard and prolonged effort
state of being laborious or arduous

Examples

The toilsomeness of the journey left the travelers exhausted and sore.
She described the toilsomeness of her daily chores with a weary sigh.
Despite the toilsomeness of the task, he persisted until it was complete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduousness - the quality of being extremely difficult or strenuous
  • Antonym: ease - the absence of difficulty or effort

Etymology

From 'toilsome' + '-ness'; 'toil' from Old French 'toiler' (struggle).
