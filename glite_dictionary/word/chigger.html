chigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪɡə / (BrE)
A small tropical flea; the fertile female burrows under the skin of the host, including humans, causing irritation.
small flea that burrows under the skin

Examples

The bite of a chigger can cause intense itching and red bumps on the skin.
After walking through the tall grass, she discovered several chiggers had burrowed into her ankles.
Doctors recommend applying antiseptic cream to relieve irritation from chigger bites.

Etymology

Probably from Carib *chico*, meaning 'small.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪɡə / (BrE)
A small, reddish larval mite that feeds on the blood of vertebrates, including humans, and causes intense skin irritation.
bloodsucking larval mite causing irritation

Examples

After hiking in the woods, I discovered several chigger bites on my ankles.
The chigger is most active during the warm summer months.
Scratching a chigger bite can lead to further skin irritation or infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - tiny arachnid similar to a tick

Etymology

Probably from Carib *chico* (tiny biting insect) via American Spanish *chigre*.

Synset POS: n
