magnanimous

1. ADJ

/ mæɡˈnænəməs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnænɪməs / (BrE)
generous and noble, especially in forgiving or overlooking an insult or injury
generous and noble in spirit

Examples

She was magnanimous in victory, praising her opponents for their hard work.
His magnanimous response to the criticism impressed everyone at the meeting.
Even though he lost the contest, he was magnanimous in congratulating the winner.

Synset Examples

a greathearted general
a magnanimous conqueror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: mean-spirited - not generous or kind, having a small and ungenerous nature

Etymology

From Latin magnanimus, from magnus (great) + animus (mind, spirit).

2. ADJ

/ mæɡˈnænɪməs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnænɪməs / (BrE)
generous, especially towards someone less powerful or who has wronged you; showing a noble spirit of forgiveness and tolerance.
generous and forgiving; noble-spirited

Examples

Despite losing the game, she was magnanimous in defeat and congratulated her opponent warmly.
He made a magnanimous gesture by forgiving the mistake and offering help instead of blame.
Many admired the leader’s magnanimous response to criticism, choosing to listen rather than retaliate.

Synset Examples

a heart big enough to hold no grudges
that's very big of you to be so forgiving
a large and generous spirit
a large heart
magnanimous toward his enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give and share unselfishly
  • Synonym: forgiving - willing or able to forgive
  • Antonym: petty - having small-minded or unforgiving attitudes
  • Antonym: mean-spirited - showing or having an unkind or ungenerous nature

Etymology

From Latin 'magnanimus', from 'magnus' (great) + 'animus' (soul, spirit).
