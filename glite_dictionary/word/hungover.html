hungover

1. ADJ

/ hʌŋˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / hʌŋˈəʊvə / (BrE)
Feeling unwell as a result of drinking too much alcohol the previous day.
feeling ill after drinking alcohol
informal

Examples

He was still hungover from the party the night before.
She called in sick because she felt hungover after too much wine.
You shouldn't drive if you're hungover the next morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sober - Not affected by alcohol; not hungover or drunk.
  • Degree Lower: drunk - Intoxicated with alcohol.

Etymology

From 'hangover,' noun, with adjectival suffix '-over' added.
