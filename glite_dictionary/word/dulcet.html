dulcet

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌl.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌl.sɪt / (BrE)
(especially of a sound) sweet and soothing; extremely pleasant in a gentle way.
sweet and pleasant, gentle (esp. of sounds)

Examples

The dulcet tones of the harp calmed everyone in the room.
She awoke to the dulcet sounds of birds singing outside her window.
His voice was so dulcet that the audience was completely captivated.

Synset Examples

the most dulcet swimming on the most beautiful and remote beaches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasant or jarring to the senses, especially sound; antonym for 'dulcet'

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *doucet* (sweet), diminutive of *doux*, from Latin *dulcis* (sweet).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌl.sət / (AmE), / ˈdʌl.sɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) pleasing and sweet to hear.
pleasant-sounding; sweet to hear

Examples

The dulcet sound of her voice soothed everyone in the room.
Birdsong filled the morning air with a dulcet melody.
We listened to the dulcet notes of the piano drifting from the open window.

Synset Examples

the dulcet tones of the cello

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - having a pleasant melody
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasant to the senses, especially to the ear

Etymology

From Old French 'doucet', diminutive of 'doux', from Latin 'dulcis' (sweet).
