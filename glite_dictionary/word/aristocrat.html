aristocrat

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪstəˌkræt / (AmE), / əˈrɪstəkræt / (BrE)
A member of the aristocracy; a person who belongs to the highest social class, often by birth or title.
member of the aristocracy

Examples

The lavish party was attended by every major aristocrat in the city.
Though born an aristocrat, she championed the rights of ordinary people.
He acted with the quiet confidence of an aristocrat used to privilege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noble - person of high rank or birth
  • Antonym: commoner - person who is not of noble rank

Etymology

From French 'aristocrate', from Greek 'aristokratēs' (nobleman).
