trainband

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪnˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnˌbænd / (BrE)
a company of militia organized for local defense in England or America from the 16th to the 18th century
historical militia company (16th–18th century)
archaic

Examples

The local trainband assembled on the village green to prepare for inspection.
During the unrest, each trainband was expected to supply men for the defense of the town.
He traced his ancestry back to a captain in a colonial trainband.

Etymology

From 'trained band', meaning a group trained for military service in 16th-century England.
