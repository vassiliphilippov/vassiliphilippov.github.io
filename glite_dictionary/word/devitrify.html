to devitrify

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvɪtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to change from a glassy (vitreous) state into a crystalline state
change from glassy to crystalline

Examples

Over time, the surface of the volcanic glass will devitrify due to exposure to moisture.
Some ceramics devitrify when reheated, affecting their transparency and strength.
The scientist observed that the sample began to devitrify during the cooling process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crystallize - to form or cause to form crystals

Etymology

From French 'dévitrifier', from Latin 'vitrum' (glass) + '-ify'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause glassy materials to become brittle or opaque by changing their structure from vitreous (glassy) to crystalline
turn glassy materials brittle/opaque

Examples

If cooled improperly, the glass may devitrify and lose its clarity.
Some ceramics manufacturers intentionally devitrify glazes to achieve a matte finish.
Failure to control the temperature can cause molten glass to devitrify during the production process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to vitrify - to convert into glass or a glassy substance by heat

Etymology

From French 'dévitrifier', from 'dé-' (de-) + 'vitrifier' (to vitrify), from Latin 'vitrum' (glass).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00506593-v
Synset POS: v
