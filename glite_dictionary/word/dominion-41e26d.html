dominion

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnjən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjən / (BrE)
the power or right to govern and control; authority, especially legal or sovereign power over a territory or people
power or authority to govern

Examples

The British Empire once exercised dominion over many overseas colonies.
After the conquest, the king established his dominion across the entire region.
The constitution limits the dominion the government has over individual rights.

Synset Examples

France held undisputed dominion over vast areas of Africa
the rule of Caesar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - the exercise of authority or control
  • Synonym: control - the power to direct or command
  • Synonym: subjugation - the act of bringing under control

Etymology

From Latin 'dominium' (ownership, property), from 'dominus' (lord, master).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnjən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjən / (BrE)
a self-governing territory, especially one that was part of the British Empire; a region or area under administrative control
self-governing territory or administrative region

Examples

Canada was once a dominion within the British Empire, having its own government but recognizing the British monarch.
The new constitution granted greater autonomy to the dominion and its people.
Each dominion had a governor who was responsible for administration and order in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a territory under the political control of another country

Etymology

From Latin *dominium* (ownership, property, right of ownership), from *dominus* (lord, master).
