prosimian

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsɪmiən / (AmE), / prəˈsɪmiən / (BrE)
a primitive primate with large ears and eyes, typically active at night, such as a lemur or loris
primitive nocturnal primate

Examples

Lemurs and bushbabies are examples of prosimians found in Madagascar and Africa.
Unlike other primates, prosimians have a keen sense of smell and are mainly nocturnal.
Scientists study prosimians to learn more about the early evolution of primates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a type of prosimian native to Madagascar
  • Degree Higher: primate - a member of the group of mammals that includes monkeys, apes, and humans
  • Degree Higher: monkey - a primate distinct from prosimians

Etymology

From Latin 'pro-' (before) and 'Simia' (ape), meaning 'before monkeys/apes'.
