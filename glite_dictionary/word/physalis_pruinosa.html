Physalis pruinosa

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈseɪlɪs pruːˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs pruːˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of annual, hairy plant native to eastern North America, producing sweet, edible yellow fruits enclosed in a papery husk.
plant with sweet yellow fruits

Examples

The farmers are growing Physalis pruinosa for its uniquely sweet yellow fruits.
Botanists recently observed a wild patch of Physalis pruinosa thriving near the woodland edge.
Jam made from Physalis pruinosa is prized for its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - common name for plants of the Physalis genus with small edible fruit
  • Degree Higher: Physalis peruviana - closely related Physalis species known as cape gooseberry

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Physalis' from Greek phusa (bladder), referring to the inflated calyx, and 'pruinosa' from Latin pruinōsus (frosty), in reference to the plant’s appearance.
