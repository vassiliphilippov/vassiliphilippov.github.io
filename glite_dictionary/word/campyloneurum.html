Campyloneurum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pɪ.loʊˈnʊər.əm / (AmE), / ˌkæm.pɪ.ləʊˈnjʊə.rəm / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic ferns native to tropical America, typically growing on other plants but not parasitic.
genus of tropical American epiphytic ferns

Examples

The botanist discovered a rare Campyloneurum species growing high in the rainforest canopy.
Many collectors prize Campyloneurum for its attractive, arching fronds and ability to thrive as an epiphyte.
Some Campyloneurum varieties are cultivated in greenhouses for their ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek 'kampylos' (curved) and 'neuron' (nerve), referring to the curved veins in the fronds.
