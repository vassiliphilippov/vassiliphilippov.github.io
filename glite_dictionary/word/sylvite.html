sylvite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlˌvaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlˌvaɪt / (BrE)
A mineral composed of native potassium chloride, commonly found in sedimentary deposits and used as an important source of potassium.
a mineral; potassium chloride ore

Examples

Large deposits of sylvite are mined for use in fertilizer production.
This sample contains both sylvite and halite crystals.
Geologists identified the layer as sylvite based on its chemical composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halite - a mineral form of sodium chloride (rock salt)

Etymology

From French *sylvite*, after English chemist Sir Humphry Davy (initially 'Sylvine', named for Davy's interest in potassium, with 'syl-' from 'Sylvanite') and the mineral suffix '-ite'.
