arariba

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrəˈriːbə / (AmE), / ˌɑːrəˈriːbə / (BrE)
A Brazilian tree noted for its attractively marked wood, used in carpentry and woodworking.
tree with handsomely marked wood (Brazil)

Examples

The cabinet was made from rare arariba, prized for its striking grain patterns.
Conservation efforts aim to protect the remaining arariba trees in the Brazilian rainforest.
Carpenters value arariba for both its beauty and durability.

Etymology

From Portuguese araribá, likely from Tupi-Guarani.
