shlep

1. NOUN

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
An awkward or stupid person, especially one who is regarded as behaving in a foolish or clumsy way.
an awkward, stupid person
informal

Examples

Despite his best efforts, Joel was always considered a shlep by his more athletic classmates.
She didn't want to be seen dating a shlep who couldn't dance or hold a conversation.
Don't be such a shlep; get up and help us move these boxes!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schlemiel - a clumsy or unlucky person; a habitual bungler

Etymology

From Yiddish *shlep* (to drag, to pull), used figuratively for a person who is inept or awkward.

2. NOUN

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
An arduous, tedious, or difficult journey or trip, often implying inconvenience.
tedious or difficult journey
informal

Examples

It was a real shlep getting across town during rush hour.
After that long shlep up the mountain, we were completely exhausted.
Visiting her means a two-hour shlep from the suburbs into the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trek - a long arduous journey, especially on foot
  • Antonym: jaunt - a short excursion or journey for pleasure

Etymology

From Yiddish 'shlepn' (to drag, pull), from German 'schleppen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311251-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
to carry or drag something heavy or awkward with effort
carry or drag with effort
informal

Examples

It's exhausting to shlep these groceries all the way home.
He had to shlep his suitcase up three flights of stairs.
Why do I always have to shlep everyone's stuff to the car?

Synset Examples

Can you shlep this bag of potatoes upstairs?
She pulled along a large trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lug - to carry or drag something heavy or awkward
  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag with effort or force
  • Synonym: tote - carry, especially something heavy or awkward

Etymology

From Yiddish *shlepn* (to drag, pull), from Middle High German *sleppen*.
