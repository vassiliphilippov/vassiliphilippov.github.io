Oceania

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊsiˈæniə / (AmE), / ˌəʊsiˈɑːniə / (BrE)
a region consisting of many islands in the South Pacific Ocean, including Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia, and sometimes Australasia and the Malay Archipelago
region of islands in the South Pacific

Examples

Many countries in Oceania are known for their stunning beaches and coral reefs.
Australia and New Zealand are sometimes included in the definition of Oceania.
The climate of Oceania varies greatly from island to island.

Etymology

From Latin Oceanus, from Greek Ὠκεανός (Okeanós), meaning 'the ocean.'
