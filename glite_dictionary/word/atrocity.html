atrocity

1. NOUN

/ əˈtrɑːsəti / (AmE), / əˈtrɒsəti / (BrE)
an extremely cruel, violent, or shocking act, especially one involving physical violence or injury
an extremely cruel or violent act

Examples

The world was horrified by the atrocity committed against innocent civilians.
Reports of another atrocity have sparked international outrage and calls for justice.
The survivors spoke about the atrocity they witnessed during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an action that is against the law
  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone

Etymology

From Latin atrocitas, from atrox (fierce, cruel).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtrɑːsəti / (AmE), / əˈtrɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being shockingly cruel, brutal, or inhumane
extreme cruelty or brutality

Examples

The dictator was responsible for an atrocity that shocked the entire world.
Reports of atrocity committed during the conflict horrified the international community.
Many survivors still struggle with the memory of the atrocity they witnessed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, or considerate

Etymology

From Latin atrocitas (fierceness, cruelty), from atrox, atroc- (fierce).
