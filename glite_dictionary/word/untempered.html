untempered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
Not moderated, restrained, or made less extreme; lacking any element or influence that softens or controls.
not moderated or restrained

Examples

Her untempered criticism made the team feel discouraged.
The storm raged with untempered fury throughout the night.
His untempered ambition sometimes led him to make reckless decisions.

Synset Examples

his untempered individualism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrestrained - not restrained or held back; free and uncontrolled
  • Antonym: tempered - moderated or made less extreme by a balancing force

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + tempered, meaning not made moderate or controlled.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01525412-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not brought to the desired consistency or hardness, especially by heat or additives, as in construction or metallurgy.
not properly hardened or adjusted

Examples

The walls began to crumble because the builders used untempered mortar.
A sword made from untempered steel is more likely to bend or break.
Mixing with water alone, the clay remained untempered and fragile.

Synset Examples

untempered mortar
untempered steel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tempered - brought to a desired hardness or consistency, especially by heat-treatment

Etymology

From un- (not) + temper (to bring to a desired consistency or hardness, especially by heating).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01526157-a
Synset POS: a
