kilobit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪl.əˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.ə.bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 bits, used to measure data storage or transmission rates
unit of 1,000 digital bits

Examples

The internet speed was measured in kilobits per second.
A standard MP3 song file is often several thousand kilobits in size.
Data transfer rates are commonly expressed in kilobits rather than bits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kilobyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bytes or 8,000 bits
  • Degree Lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing (binary digit)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'kilo-' (meaning 1,000) + 'bit' (binary digit), modeled after metric units.
