Eunectes murinus

1. NOUN

/ juˈnɛk.tiːz mjuˈraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / juːˈnɛk.tiːz mjʊˈraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the green anaconda, a large, primarily aquatic boa native to tropical South America
green anaconda species; large South American boa

Examples

The Eunectes murinus is considered the heaviest snake in the world.
Researchers tracked the movement of a Eunectes murinus through the flooded forests of the Amazon.
Zoo visitors were fascinated by the size of the Eunectes murinus on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green anaconda - common name for Eunectes murinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Eunectes' meaning 'good swimmer,' 'murinus' meaning 'of mice' (referring to color or pattern).
