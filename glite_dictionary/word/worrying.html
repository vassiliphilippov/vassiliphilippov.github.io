worrying

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʌriɪŋ / (BrE)
causing anxiety, distress, or concern; making someone feel worried.
causing worry or anxiety

Examples

The rising number of thefts is a worrying trend in the neighborhood.
It is worrying that so few people are taking health precautions.
She received some worrying news about her job yesterday.

Synset Examples

distressing (or disturbing) news
lived in heroic if something distressful isolation
a disturbing amount of crime
a revelation that was most perturbing
a new and troubling thought
in a particularly worrisome predicament
a worrying situation
a worrying time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbing - causing anxiety or unease
  • Antonym: comforting - making someone feel less worried or anxious

Etymology

Present participle of 'worry', originally from Old English 'wyrgan' (strangle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːriɪŋ, ˈwʌriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʌrɪɪŋ, ˈwɜːrɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of persistently harassing, bothering, or troubling someone
persistent harassment or bothering

Examples

The victim reported that the worrying by her colleagues made work unbearable.
Years of worrying led to him seeking legal protection.
Persistent worrying of the witness was condemned by the judge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harassment - the act of systematically annoying or attacking someone

Etymology

Present participle noun from the verb 'worry', originally meaning to strangle or harass, from Old English *wyrgan* (to strangle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00426281-n
Synset POS: n
