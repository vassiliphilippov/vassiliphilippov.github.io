blastogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæstoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌblɑːstəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
Asexual reproduction by budding, especially in lower organisms.
asexual reproduction by budding
medical

Examples

Many simple organisms, such as hydras, reproduce through blastogenesis.
Researchers observed blastogenesis in the laboratory cultures of yeast cells.
The study of blastogenesis helps scientists understand the evolutionary strategies of asexual reproduction.

Etymology

From Greek blastos (germ, sprout) + -genesis (origin, creation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌblæstəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌblastəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
a theory in biology that inherited characteristics are transmitted by germ plasm rather than by environmental influences or acquired traits
theory: inheritance via germ plasm

Examples

The concept of blastogenesis was once central to debates about heredity in the 19th century.
Unlike pangenesis, blastogenesis emphasizes inheritance through the germ plasm alone.
Modern genetics has largely superseded the theory of blastogenesis with the discovery of DNA.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pangenesis - a competing theory positing that characteristics are inherited from all parts of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'blasto-' (germ, bud) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
