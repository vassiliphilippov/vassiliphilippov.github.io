reciprocally

1. ADV

/ rɪˈsɪprəkli / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəli / (BrE)
In a manner such that two things have an inverse or opposite relationship, especially in mathematics or logic.
in an inverse or opposite manner

Examples

In mathematics, the two variables change reciprocally as one increases and the other decreases.
The interests of both parties are reciprocally influenced by these decisions.
The laws of supply and demand often affect prices reciprocally in economic models.

Synset Examples

inversely related
wavelength and frequency are, of course, related reciprocally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inversely - in an opposite or reverse manner

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way) + -ally.

2. ADV

/ rɪˈsɪprəkli / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəli / (BrE)
In a way that involves mutual action, influence, or support; in a shared or mutual manner.
in a mutual or shared way

Examples

The two countries agreed to reciprocally lower their trade barriers.
The procedures are reciprocally dependent, each requiring input from the other.
Access to the software is granted reciprocally to all researchers in the consortium.

Synset Examples

the agreement was mutually satisfactory
the goals of the negotiators were not reciprocally exclusive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutually - in a way that is shared by two or more parties

Etymology

From reciprocal (from Latin reciprocus, moving backward and forward) + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00407555-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ rɪˈsɪprəkli / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəli / (BrE)
in a way that is mutual; in exchange or in reciprocation
in a reciprocal or mutual way

Examples

The two countries agreed to reduce tariffs reciprocally to boost trade.
Privileges were given reciprocally as a sign of trust between the companies.
Members of the alliance support each other reciprocally in times of need.

Synset Examples

gave up our seats on the plane and in return received several hundred dollars and seats on the next plane out
we get many benefits in return for our taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutually - in a mutual way; shared by each

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way) + -ally (adverbial suffix).
