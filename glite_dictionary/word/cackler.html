cackler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæk.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæk.lə / (BrE)
a hen that has just laid an egg and makes a loud, shrill noise
hen that squawks after laying an egg

Examples

The farmer could always hear the cackler in the morning after collecting eggs.
A loud cackler startled the children as they walked past the henhouse.
After laying her egg, the cackler strutted around the coop, squawking proudly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - to make a loud, shrill sound like a hen

Etymology

From 'cackle', imitative of a hen's sound, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæk.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæk.lə / (BrE)
any of several insect-eating Old World birds known for their loud, repetitive song, sometimes classified in the family Muscicapidae
a noisy, insectivorous Old World bird

Examples

The cackler is easily recognized by its persistent, chattering call in the forest canopy.
Birdwatchers gathered early to catch a glimpse of the elusive cackler at dawn.
Many cacklers feed on insects, making them important for the local ecosystem.
