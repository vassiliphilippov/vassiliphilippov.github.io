sparkler

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrklɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːklə / (BrE)
(informal) A diamond, especially one that is very noticeable or glittering.
diamond (informal)
informal

Examples

She couldn't stop admiring the massive sparkler on her engagement ring.
He bought his wife a new sparkler for their anniversary.
Everyone stared at the dazzling sparkler she wore to the gala.

Synset Examples

look at the ice on that dame!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diamond - a precious stone made of crystalized carbon

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrklɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːklə / (BrE)
a small firework that burns slowly while emitting bright sparks, often held in the hand
hand-held firework emitting sparks

Examples

Each child waved a sparkler during the evening celebration.
For her birthday party, they put a sparkler on top of the cake instead of a candle.
Please be careful not to touch the hot end of the sparkler after it burns out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firework - an object containing explosive powder that makes a display of light or sound when lit

Etymology

From 'spark' + diminutive suffix '-ler'.
