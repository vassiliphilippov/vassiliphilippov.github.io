inulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnjəˌlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪnjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a carbohydrate (polysaccharide) obtained from plants, used in the manufacture of fructose and in medical tests to assess kidney function
plant carbohydrate used in medicine and food industry
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an inulin clearance test to evaluate her kidney function.
Food manufacturers sometimes add inulin to products to increase dietary fiber.
Chicory root is a natural source of inulin, often used as a food additive.

Etymology

From Latin 'inula' (a kind of plant) + chemical suffix '-in'.
