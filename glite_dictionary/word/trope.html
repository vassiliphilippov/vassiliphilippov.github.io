trope

1. NOUN

/ troʊp / (AmE), / trəʊp / (BrE)
a word or phrase used in a figurative or nonliteral sense; a figure of speech
figurative use of language

Examples

The poet relies on the trope of light to convey hope and renewal.
'Time is a thief' is a commonly used trope in literature.
Writers often employ tropes to add depth and imagery to their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech describing something as if it were something else
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

From Greek tropos (a turn, manner, style) via Latin tropus.
