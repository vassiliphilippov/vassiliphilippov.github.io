Celebrex

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛlɪˌbrɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪˌbrɛks / (BrE)
A trademarked anti-inflammatory drug (celecoxib) used to relieve pain and inflammation, especially in arthritis, without causing significant harm to the digestive tract.
a trademarked anti-inflammatory medication
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Celebrex to help manage my arthritis pain.
Some patients experience fewer stomach issues when taking Celebrex compared to traditional NSAIDs.
The pharmacist explained how Celebrex works to reduce inflammation without harming the stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celecoxib - the generic name for the medication marketed as Celebrex

Etymology

Trademark; brand name for celecoxib, a selective COX-2 inhibitor.
