Melolonthidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈlɒnθɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈlɒnθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family (or sometimes subfamily) of beetles that includes the typical cockchafers or May beetles, often regarded as a separate family in some classifications.
a family of beetles (cockchafers/May beetles)

Examples

Many species in the Melolonthidae family are known to damage crops as larvae.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Melolonthidae in the rainforest.
The cockchafer, an insect from the Melolonthidae, emerges in large numbers in spring.

Etymology

From New Latin Melolontha (type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈlɒnθɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈlɒnθɪdiː / (BrE)
a subfamily (sometimes classified as a family) of beetles commonly known as May beetles or chafers, found worldwide and including many species important to agriculture
a subfamily of beetles (May beetles, chafers)

Examples

The Melolonthidae are notorious for damaging crops as larvae feed on plant roots.
Scientists collected several specimens from the Melolonthidae during their field study.
Many garden pests belong to the beetle group called Melolonthidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarab - a type of beetle in the family Scarabaeidae

Etymology

From Greek 'melolonthē' (beetle) + scientific family suffix '-idae'.
