Enceliopsis

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛliˈɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛlɪˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of xerophytic (drought-resistant) herbaceous plants native to the southwestern United States.
a genus of desert herbs

Examples

Enceliopsis species are known for their bright yellow flowers that thrive in arid regions.
Several types of Enceliopsis can be found in the deserts of Nevada and Utah.
Botanists often study Enceliopsis to understand how plants adapt to dry climates.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the related genus name 'Encelia' and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛliˈɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛlɪˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of drought-tolerant herbs native to the southwestern United States.
genus of xerophytic herbs (Southwestern US)

Examples

Several species in the Enceliopsis genus thrive in arid regions of Nevada and Arizona.
Researchers study the adaptations of Enceliopsis to survive extended droughts.
The large, daisy-like flowers make Enceliopsis a popular plant among desert gardeners.

Etymology

From Greek 'Encelia' (a related genus) + Greek suffix '-opsis' (appearance, view).
