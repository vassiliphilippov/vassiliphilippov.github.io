Moorish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʊrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪʃ / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the Moors, especially their culture, art, or architecture.
of or relating to the Moors

Examples

The palace featured intricate Moorish designs on its walls and ceilings.
She admired the graceful arches of the Moorish style courtyard.
Many Spanish cities have buildings with a distinct Moorish influence.

Synset Examples

Moorish courtyard

Etymology

From 'Moor' (referring to inhabitants of North Africa) + -ish.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʊrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʊərɪʃ / (BrE)
Of or relating to a style of architecture and decorative art characteristic of the Moors in Spain from the 13th to 16th centuries, notable for features such as horseshoe-shaped arches.
style of architecture from Moorish Spain

Examples

The palace is famous for its Moorish arches and intricate tilework.
Tourists admired the Moorish influence in the old city's design.
Many Spanish cathedrals display Moorish architectural elements blended with Christian styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic - relating to Islamic art and architecture

Etymology

From 'Moor', referring to the Muslim inhabitants of North Africa and Spain; ultimately from Latin 'Maurus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05852214-n
Synset POS: n
