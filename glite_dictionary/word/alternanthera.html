Alternanthera

1. NOUN

/ ˌæltərnənˈθɛrə / (AmE), / ˌæltənænˈθɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing herbs native to tropical regions of America and Australia, often grown for their ornamental foliage
a genus of tropical herbs

Examples

The leaves of Alternanthera plants display a striking variety of colors in garden beds.
Gardeners appreciate Alternanthera for its vibrant, low-maintenance ground cover.
Many species in the genus Alternanthera thrive in both wet and dry tropical habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Latin 'alternus' (alternate) and Greek 'anthēra' (anther, part of a flower).

Examples

Several species of Alternanthera are popular choices for aquarium plants due to their colorful leaves.
