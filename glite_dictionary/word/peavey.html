peavey

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːvi / (AmE), / ˈpiːvi / (BrE)
a stout lever with a sharp metal spike at one end, used for handling and rolling logs
lever with spike for handling logs

Examples

The lumberjack used a peavey to roll the heavy log into place.
With his trusty peavey, he could maneuver even the largest timber.
Every logging crew made sure to bring at least one peavey to the work site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cant hook - a similar lever but without a sharp spike, used for handling logs

Etymology

Named after Joseph Peavey (1820–1873), the American inventor who designed the tool.
