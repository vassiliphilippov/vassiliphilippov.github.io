to abbreviate

1. VERB

/ əˈbriː.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈbriː.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to reduce something in scope while keeping its essential elements; to make something shorter
make shorter while keeping essentials

Examples

The editor decided to abbreviate the lengthy report without omitting important details.
You should abbreviate your speech so it fits within the time limit.
The text was abbreviated to highlight only the essential information.

Synset Examples

The manuscript must be shortened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shorten - to make or become shorter
  • Synonym: to abridge - to shorten a text without losing the essence
  • Antonym: to extend - to make longer or more extensive

Etymology

From Latin abbreviatus, past participle of abbreviare (to shorten), from ad- (to) + brevis (short).

2. VERB

/ əˈbri.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈbriː.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to shorten a word, phrase, or text by omitting letters, substituting shorter forms, or using only the main parts
to shorten a word or phrase

Examples

Many organizations abbreviate their names to save space on documents.
Please abbreviate each term in the notes for clarity.
The company’s full name is often abbreviated to three letters.

Synset Examples

Abbreviate ‘New York’ and write ‘NY’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shorten - to make something shorter in length or duration
  • Antonym: to extend - to make something longer in length or duration

Etymology

From Latin abbreviatus, past participle of abbreviare (to shorten), from ad- (to) + breviare (make short), from brevis (short).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
