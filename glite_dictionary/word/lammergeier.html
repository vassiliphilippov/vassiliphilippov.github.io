lammergeier

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmərˌɡaɪər / (AmE), / ˈlaməɡʌɪə / (BrE)
a large Old World vulture (Gypaetus barbatus) found in mountainous regions of Eurasia and Africa, known for its black beard-like feathers hanging around its bill
large bearded vulture of Eurasia and Africa

Examples

The lammergeier soars above the mountain cliffs, searching for food.
We spotted a lammergeier during our trek in the Himalayas.
Unlike other vultures, the lammergeier is known for dropping bones to crack them open for marrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearded vulture - another name for the lammergeier

Etymology

From German 'Lämmergeier', meaning 'lamb vulture'.
