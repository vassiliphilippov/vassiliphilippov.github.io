quenching

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwentʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of extinguishing something, especially by putting out a fire or stopping something from burning
the act of extinguishing or putting out

Examples

The firefighters' rapid quenching of the blaze prevented major damage to the building.
Proper quenching of embers is essential to avoid forest fires.
The quenching of the torch signaled the end of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

the extinction of the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinguishing - the act of causing something (especially a fire or light) to stop burning

Etymology

From the verb 'quench', from Old English *cwencan* (to extinguish).
