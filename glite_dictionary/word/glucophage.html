Glucophage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːkoʊˌfɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡluːkəˌfɑːʒ / (BrE)
A trademarked antidiabetic drug (metformin) prescribed for the treatment of type II diabetes.
trade name for a diabetes drug
medical

Examples

Her doctor prescribed Glucophage to help manage her blood sugar levels.
Many patients with type II diabetes take Glucophage as part of their treatment plan.
The pharmacist explained the possible side effects of Glucophage to the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metformin - an oral antidiabetic drug used to treat type II diabetes

Etymology

From Latin 'gluco-' (relating to glucose) and French 'phage' (eater), trademarked name for metformin.
