to imitate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪteɪt / (BrE)
to copy the actions, appearance, or behavior of someone or something
to copy or mimic

Examples

Young children often imitate their parents' behavior and speech.
The comedian can imitate dozens of different celebrity voices perfectly.
Many artists imitate the styles of famous painters to learn new techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimic - to imitate, especially in order to entertain or ridicule
  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy of
  • Antonym: to originate - to create or initiate rather than copy

Etymology

From Latin imitari (to copy, imitate).

/ ˈɪməˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪteɪt / (BrE)
to copy the behavior, appearance, or actions of someone or something
copy the behavior or looks of

Examples

Many comedians imitate celebrities to entertain their audiences.

Parrots can often imitate human voices with surprising accuracy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
