distinction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
a difference or contrast that makes one thing or person different from another, especially between things or people that are otherwise similar
difference between similar things or people

Examples

It is important to understand the distinction between fact and opinion.
The legal distinction between murder and manslaughter is very important.
Her research draws a clear distinction between rural and urban communities.

Synset Examples

he learned the distinction between gold and lead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference - the state or condition of being different

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctio' (separation, distinction), from 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish).
