timber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
a large piece of wood, especially a beam or post prepared for use in building or construction.
a large piece of wood for building

Examples

The barn was supported by thick timbers that have lasted for centuries.
Workers lifted the heavy timber into place for the new fence.
Rain had rotted the old timber at the corner of the shed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used to support weight
  • Synonym: post - an upright supporting member in a structure, typically made of wood

Etymology

From Old English *timber* (building, structure, wood), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
land that is covered with trees and shrubs, especially with reference to its suitability for use as wood; woodland or forested land.
land covered with trees; woodland

Examples

The company purchased several acres of timber for conservation purposes.
Much of the timber in this region has been logged over the past decade.
They hiked through dense timber before reaching the open meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with woods or trees

Etymology

From Old English 'timber' (building, wood, trees), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
A long, solid piece of wood cut for building, especially as a beam or support in structures.
wooden beam used in construction

Examples

The old barn was supported by large timber beams, some of which were over a hundred years old.
They replaced the rotten timber in the bridge to ensure its safety.
Each ceiling timber was carefully measured and fitted into place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used to support the roof or floor of a building

Etymology

From Old English 'timber' (building, wood), related to German 'Zimmer' (room, timber).

4. NOUN

wood prepared for building

Examples

5. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
the quality of a musical sound or voice that distinguishes different types of sound production, such as voices or musical instruments
sound quality in music or voice

Examples

The unique timber of the violin made the solo especially beautiful.
Each singer in the choir has a slightly different timber, creating a rich blend of voices.
Sound engineers pay close attention to the timber when mixing music tracks.

Synset Examples

the timbre of her soprano was rich and lovely
the muffled tones of the broken bell summoned them to meet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timbre - the quality or color of a musical sound that distinguishes it from others
  • Synonym: tone - the general quality or character of a musical or vocal sound

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'timbre', from French 'timbre' (quality of a musical sound).
