Sambucus ebulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmbjʊkəs ˈɛbjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈsæmbjʊkəs ˈɛbjʊləs / (BrE)
A species of dwarf herbaceous elder native to Europe, with pink flowers and a disagreeable smell.
dwarf European elder with pink flowers

Examples

The roots and berries of Sambucus ebulus have been used in traditional medicine in Europe.
Botanists distinguish Sambucus ebulus from other elders by its upright herbaceous stems.
A field guide noted the unpleasant odor of Sambucus ebulus when the leaves are crushed.

Etymology

From Latin sambucus (elder tree) and ebulus (dwarf elder).
