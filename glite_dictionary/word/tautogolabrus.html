Tautogolabrus

1. NOUN

/ tɔːˌtoʊɡoʊˈleɪbrəs / (AmE), / tɔːˌtɒɡəˈleɪbrəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Labridae, containing the species bergall.
genus of marine fish (wrasses)

Examples

The genus Tautogolabrus is well known among ichthyologists for its single species, the bergall.
Researchers found that Tautogolabrus inhabits coastal waters in the western Atlantic Ocean.
Marine biologists study Tautogolabrus to understand its role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergall - A species of wrasse (Tautogolabrus adspersus) found in North America
  • Degree Higher: Labridae - The family of fish commonly known as wrasses

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'tautoga' (a type of fish) and 'Labrus' (a wrasse genus).
