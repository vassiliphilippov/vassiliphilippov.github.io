biochemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈkɛm.ə.stri / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
The branch of science concerned with the chemical substances and processes that occur within living organisms.
study of chemical processes in living organisms

Examples

She is studying biochemistry at university, focusing on how enzymes work in the body.
Advances in biochemistry have led to new treatments for various diseases.
His research in biochemistry explores the molecular basis of cell communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - the scientific study of the properties and behavior of matter
  • Degree Lower: biology - the science that studies living organisms

Etymology

From Greek bio- (life) + chemistry.
