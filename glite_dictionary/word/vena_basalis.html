vena basalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə bəˈseɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə bəˈseɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a large vein running along the medial surface of the temporal lobe, draining blood into the great cerebral vein
a vein of the brain's temporal lobe
medical

Examples

The vena basalis collects blood from the medial regions of the temporal lobe.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the vena basalis.
An obstruction in the vena basalis can impact cerebral venous drainage.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'basalis' (basal, of the base).
