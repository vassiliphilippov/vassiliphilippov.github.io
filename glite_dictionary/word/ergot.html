ergot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrɡət / (AmE), / ˈɜːɡət / (BrE)
a fungus that infects certain cereal plants, forming black, hardened masses in place of grains and yielding alkaloids used in medicine
a fungus infecting cereals, source of alkaloids

Examples

The rye crop was contaminated with ergot, making it unsafe for consumption.
Scientists extract alkaloids from ergot for use in pharmaceuticals.
Historically, outbreaks of ergot poisoning have caused severe illness in rural communities.

Etymology

From Old French 'ergot' (spur), originally referring to the spur-shaped fungus growth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrɡət / (AmE), / ˈɜːɡət / (BrE)
A disease of plants, especially cereals, caused by a parasitic fungus that affects the grain and can produce toxic compounds.
fungal disease of grains

Examples

Many crops were ruined after an outbreak of ergot devastated the rye fields.
The presence of ergot in bread made from infected grain can cause serious illness in humans.
Farmers must monitor their fields to prevent the spread of ergot among their cereal crops.

Etymology

From Old French argot or ergot (cock’s spur), referring to the spur-like shape of the fungus on the grain.
