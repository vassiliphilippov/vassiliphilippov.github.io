frustration

1. NOUN

/ frʌˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / frʌˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of anger or annoyance caused by being prevented from achieving something or being criticized
annoyance caused by hindrance or criticism

Examples

After waiting in line for an hour, his frustration was obvious to everyone.
The constant road closures led to a growing sense of frustration among commuters.
She tried to hide her frustration when her work was unfairly criticized.

Synset Examples

her constant complaints were the main source of his frustration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - a feeling of being annoyed or irritated
  • Antonym: satisfaction - the feeling of pleasure when a desire or need is fulfilled

Etymology

From Latin frustratio (a deceiving, disappointment), from frustra (in vain).

2. NOUN

/ frʌˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / frʌˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the feeling of being upset or annoyed because you cannot achieve what you want
feeling of being thwarted or annoyed

Examples

She felt a deep frustration after her project was rejected again.
Years of hard work ended only in frustration and bitterness.
He tried to hide his frustration when the computer crashed for the third time.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Degree Lower: disappointment - sadness caused by the non-fulfillment of expectations

Etymology

From Latin frustratio (a deception, disappointment), from frustra (in vain).

3. NOUN

/ frʌˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / frʌˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of preventing someone from achieving their aims or fulfilling their plans.
act of preventing achievement

Examples

The frustration of our efforts led us to abandon the project.
Legal loopholes allowed the frustration of regulations intended to protect the environment.
Continued frustration of his plans eventually caused him to seek other opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or delay; an obstacle
  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or impedes progress
  • Antonym: facilitation - the act of making a process easier or helping progress

Etymology

From Latin frustratio (a deception, disappointment), from frustrare (to deceive, disappoint).
