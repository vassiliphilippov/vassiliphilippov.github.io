Oxonian

1. ADJ

/ ɑkˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ɒkˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the city of Oxford in England, or to its inhabitants
of Oxford or its people

Examples

The Oxonian architecture blends medieval and modern styles.
Many Oxonian traditions date back centuries.
She admired the Oxonian charm of the university town.

Synset Examples

his Oxonian neighbors
Oxonian bookstores

Etymology

From Latin 'Oxoniensis', meaning 'of or from Oxford'.

/ ɑˈksoʊniən / (AmE), / ɒkˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a native or resident of Oxford, England
native or resident of Oxford

Examples

As an Oxonian, she takes great pride in her city's rich academic heritage.
Several Oxonians gathered to celebrate the anniversary of the university.
He spoke about his childhood experiences growing up as an Oxonian.

Related Terms

From Latin Oxoniensis, meaning 'of Oxford'.
