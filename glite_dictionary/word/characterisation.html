characterisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrɪktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkærɪktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of describing or representing the distinctive qualities or features of someone or something
describing distinctive qualities
british

Examples

The journalist's characterisation of the politician sparked controversy among the public.
Her novel is known for its subtle characterisation of complex emotions and personalities.
The film's characterisation of the villain was praised for its depth and realism.

Synset Examples

the media's characterization of Al Gore as a nerd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characterization - the act of describing distinctive characteristics; US spelling

Etymology

From characterise + -ation, influenced by French caractérisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkærɪktərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkærɪktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a graphic or vivid verbal description of a person, place, or thing
vivid verbal description
british

Examples

The novelist's characterisation of the bustling city brought it to life for the reader.
Her characterisation of the landscape was vivid and evocative.
The report's bleak characterisation of conditions shocked officials.

Synset Examples

too often the narrative was interrupted by long word pictures
the author gives a depressing picture of life in Poland
the pamphlet contained brief characterizations of famous Vermonters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: description - a spoken or written account of something
  • Synonym: depiction - the action of describing or illustrating something

Etymology

From character + -isation, following French 'caractérisation'.
