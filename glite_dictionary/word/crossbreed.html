crossbreed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbriːd / (BrE)
an animal or plant that is the offspring of parents of different breeds, varieties, species, or stocks, especially as a result of intentional breeding
offspring from different breeds or varieties

Examples

A mule is a classic example of a crossbreed between a horse and a donkey.
The farmer introduced a new crossbreed of sheep to improve wool quality.
Many gardeners prefer a crossbreed of roses that combines the best features of two different types.

Synset Examples

a mule is a cross between a horse and a donkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - an organism produced by combining different varieties or species
  • Antonym: purebred - an organism from parents of the same breed or variety

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' (interbreed) and 'breed' (lineage or stock).

2. VERB

/ ˈkrɔsˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbriːd / (BrE)
to produce an animal or plant by mating individuals of different breeds, varieties, or species
to breed from different varieties

Examples

Scientists often crossbreed two plant species to create disease-resistant crops.
The farmer decided to crossbreed his sheep with another breed to improve wool quality.
To obtain unique flowers, gardeners frequently crossbreed different varieties of roses.

Synset Examples

cross a horse and a donkey
Mendel tried crossbreeding
these species do not interbreed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hybridize - to produce hybrids by crossbreeding
  • Antonym: inbreed - to breed from closely related individuals

Etymology

From 'cross' (to interbreed) + 'breed' (to produce offspring), likely modeled after earlier noun use.
