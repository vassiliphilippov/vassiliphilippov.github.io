leader

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈliːdə / (BrE)
an article of merchandise offered at a loss in order to attract customers to a store
item sold at a loss to attract customers

Examples

The supermarket used a popular cereal as a leader to bring shoppers into the store.
Many retailers introduce a leader during sales events to increase foot traffic.
Offering a leader can help new businesses build a loyal customer base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loss leader - an item sold at below cost to attract customers

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːdər / (AmE), / ˈliːdə / (BrE)
a person who directs, commands, or influences a group, organization, or country; someone who inspires or guides others
a person who guides or commands others

Examples

The company's new leader inspired employees to work together towards common goals.
As a leader in her community, she organized several successful charity events.
Natural leaders motivate teams even during challenging times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: follower - a person who follows the direction or leadership of another
  • Synonym: chief - a person with the highest authority or rank in a group

Etymology

From Old English 'lǣdere', from 'lǣdan' (to lead).
