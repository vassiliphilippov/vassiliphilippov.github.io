two-step

1. VERB

/ ˈtuˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌstɛp / (BrE)
to dance the two-step, a lively partner dance with quick and slow steps
to dance the two-step

Examples

At the wedding, everyone gathered to two-step around the dance floor.
She taught her friends how to two-step at the country bar.
He loves to two-step whenever he hears country music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - to dance the waltz
  • Synonym: fox-trot - to dance the foxtrot

Etymology

From the name of the dance two-step, popularized in the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌstɛp / (BrE)
a ballroom dance in duple meter, characterized by sliding steps
ballroom dance with sliding steps

Examples

They danced the two-step perfectly at the wedding reception.
The country band played a song that inspired everyone to try the two-step.
She learned the two-step in her first ballroom dance class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtrot - another popular ballroom dance
  • Synonym: waltz - ballroom dance in triple time

3. NOUN

/ ˈtuːˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌstɛp / (BrE)
a dance with a quick-quick-slow rhythm, or a simple dance step involving two movements; especially a partner dance popular in country music
a simple dance step or style

Examples

The band played a lively tune, and the whole crowd started doing the two-step.
I learned the two-step at my first country dance lesson.
She invited him onto the floor for a quick two-step before the music ended.

Synset Examples

We learned a fun two-step dance at the party last night.
She performed a graceful two-step on the dance floor.

Etymology

From the combination of "two" and "step," describing the basic movement pattern of the dance.
