George Bernard Shaw

1. PROPN

/ dʒɔrdʒ bərˈnɑrd ʃɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈbɜː.nəd ʃɔː / (BrE)
(1856–1950) Irish-born British playwright and critic, known for his influential plays and as a founder of the Fabian Society.
Irish-born British playwright and critic

Examples

George Bernard Shaw wrote many plays that are still performed worldwide today.
The wit and humor of George Bernard Shaw have influenced generations of writers.
