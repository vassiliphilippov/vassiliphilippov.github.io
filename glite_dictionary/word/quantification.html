quantification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or result of applying a quantifier to a variable in logic, thereby limiting its possible values (as with 'some', 'all', or 'no').
logical limitation using quantifiers

Examples

In mathematics, quantification allows us to state properties that are true for all or some elements in a set.
The distinction between universal and existential quantification is fundamental in logic.
Proper quantification ensures that the meaning of a statement is clear within a logical system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantifier - a word or phrase expressing quantity in logic or grammar

Etymology

From Latin 'quantificare' (to make quantity) + English '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɑːntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kwɒntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of measuring or stating the quantity of something
the process of measuring quantity

Examples

Accurate quantification of the chemicals in the solution is essential for the experiment.
The quantification of risk factors helps businesses make informed decisions.
Recent advances in technology have improved the quantification of greenhouse gas emissions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - to ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Latin quantificare (to make quantity), formed from quantus (how much) and facere (to make).
