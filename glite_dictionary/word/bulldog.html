bulldog

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.dɒg / (BrE)
to throw a steer by grabbing its horns and twisting its neck, as in a rodeo event
throw a steer by its horns (rodeo)

Examples

During the competition, he expertly bulldogged the steer to the ground in just under six seconds.
She learned how to bulldog after years of watching her father in rodeos.
Spectators cheered as the cowboy bulldogged his steer with remarkable speed.

Etymology

Named after bulldogs, which were bred to seize bulls by the nose; the verb reflects the dog's action.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111226-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌdɒg / (BrE)
to attack or pursue someone or something with determination and ferocity, like a bulldog
attack or pursue with fierce determination

Examples

The press bulldogged the politician with relentless questions all evening.
Despite fierce competition, she bulldogged her way to the top of the industry.
Security guards bulldogged the intruder until he was safely restrained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something

Etymology

From the noun 'bulldog', referring to the breed known for its tenacity.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01121448-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.dɒg / (BrE)
a sturdy, thickset, short-haired dog breed with a large head and strong undershot lower jaw, originally developed in England for bull-baiting
a sturdy breed of dog with a short muzzle

Examples

The bulldog sat patiently by its owner's feet, watching people pass by.
Despite its fierce appearance, the bulldog is known for being gentle and loyal.
She decided to adopt a bulldog because of its friendly temperament and manageable size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxer - a medium-sized, muscular dog breed with a square muzzle and short coat
  • Synonym: terrier - a dog breed originally bred to hunt vermin

Etymology

From 'bull' (referring to bulls) + 'dog', due to its use in bull-baiting.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02111324-n
Synset POS: n
