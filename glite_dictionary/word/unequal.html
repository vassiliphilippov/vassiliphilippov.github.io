unequal

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈiːkwəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːkwəl / (BrE)
not the same in quality, amount, or value; not evenly matched or balanced.
not the same in quantity or value

Examples

The distribution of resources in the region is unequal, leading to significant disparities.
They faced an unequal contest, as one team was much stronger than the other.
An unequal division of the profits caused tension among the partners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equal - having the same quantity, value, or measure

Etymology

From Latin 'inaequalis', from 'in-' (not) + 'aequalis' (equal).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈikwəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːkwəl / (BrE)
not having the necessary qualities, ability, or resources to do something successfully; not up to a task or challenge
not up to a task or challenge

Examples

She felt unequal to the responsibility of leading the team alone.
After hours of hiking, he realized he was unequal to the challenge of climbing the mountain.
The player seemed unequal to the demands of professional competition.

Synset Examples

inadequate training
the staff was inadequate
she was unequal to the task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inadequate - lacking the quality or quantity required; insufficient
  • Antonym: sufficient - having enough resources or qualities for a task; adequate

Etymology

From un- (not) + equal, from Latin aequālis (level, equal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00051936-a
Synset POS: a
