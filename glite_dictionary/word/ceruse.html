ceruse

1. NOUN

/ səˈruːs / (AmE), / sɪˈruːs / (BrE)
A white pigment containing lead, formerly used in cosmetics and painting but now recognized as poisonous.
poisonous white lead pigment

Examples

The artist refused to use ceruse because of its toxic properties.
Renaissance women applied ceruse to their faces to achieve a pale complexion.
Modern conservationists wear gloves when handling paintings that contain ceruse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white lead - a white pigment made by corroding lead; main component of ceruse

Etymology

From French 'céruse', from Latin 'cerussa' (white lead).
