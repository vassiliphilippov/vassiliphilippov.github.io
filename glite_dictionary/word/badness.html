badness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbædnɪs / (AmE), / ˈbadnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being bad, especially with regard to ethics, decency, or standards.
the state or quality of being bad

Examples

She was shocked by the sheer badness of his behavior at the meeting.
Despite his charm, there's a certain badness to his actions that can't be ignored.
You have to accept the badness in life along with the good.

Synset Examples

take the bad with the good

Related Terms

  • Antonym: goodness - the quality or state of being good
  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality or wickedness

Etymology

Formed from 'bad' + '-ness', a common noun suffix indicating state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbædnəs / (AmE), / ˈbadnəs / (BrE)
(especially in children) the quality of being mischievous, naughty, or behaving badly.
mischievous or naughty behavior, esp. in children

Examples

The teacher was frustrated by the constant badness of the children at the back of the class.
His badness often made his parents laugh, even when they tried to scold him.
She blamed her son's badness on the influence of his older cousins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naughtiness - the quality or state of being naughty or disobedient, especially in children
  • Antonym: goodness - the quality of being good; virtue or kindness

Etymology

From 'bad' + suffix '-ness' (forming nouns of quality) in Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbædnəs / (AmE), / ˈbædnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or degree of being bad, unpleasant, or undesirable.
the state or extent of being bad

Examples

The badness of the storm surprised everyone in the small town.
She complained about the badness of her headache to her doctor.
Scientists tried to measure the badness of the air pollution in the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: goodness - the quality or degree of being good
  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality or wickedness

Etymology

From 'bad' + '-ness', a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
