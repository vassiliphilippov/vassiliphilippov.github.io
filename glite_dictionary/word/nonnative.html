nonnative

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) originating from a region outside the area where it is currently found; not indigenous.
not originally from the area (plant or animal)

Examples

Many nonnative plants have been introduced to the region by humans.
The forest is now dominated by nonnative species that outcompete local flora.
Ecologists are concerned about the spread of nonnative animals disrupting the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: native - (of a plant or animal) originating in a particular place

Etymology

From non- (not) + native (from Latin natīvus, 'innate, native').

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
not originating or belonging naturally to a particular place or environment; not aboriginal or indigenous.
not originally from a place

Examples

Many nonnative plants have been introduced to the region over the past century.
The city has a large nonnative population from various countries.
Scientists are studying the impact of nonnative species on local wildlife.

Synset Examples

the nonnative population of South Africa

Related Terms

  • Antonym: native - belonging to a particular place by birth or origin

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'native'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01041087-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ nɑnˈneɪtɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈneɪtɪv / (BrE)
(of a person, plant, or animal) not born, grown, or originating in a particular place; foreign or introduced from elsewhere.
not born or originating in a place

Examples

The city has a significant nonnative community from around the world.
Many nonnative species can disrupt local ecosystems.
As a nonnative speaker, she sometimes struggles with English idioms.

Synset Examples

our large nonnative population

Related Terms

  • Antonym: native - a person or thing born or originating in a specified place

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) + 'native' (born in a particular place).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
