adrift

1. ADV

/ əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
Floating without being anchored or moored; not attached to anything and moving freely, especially on water.
floating freely; not anchored

Examples

The old fishing boat was found adrift miles from shore.
After the rope snapped, the raft floated adrift on the open sea.
Several buoys had come adrift during the storm and needed to be recovered.

Synset Examples

the boat was set adrift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afloat - floating on the surface of water

Etymology

From Old English 'on drifte' (on the drift), influenced by 'drift'.

2. ADV

/ əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
Without direction or purpose; off course or aimlessly wandering.
without direction; off course

Examples

After the storm, the ship was left adrift in the open sea.
Her mind went adrift during the monotonous lecture, barely registering the words.
Many young people feel adrift after finishing university and facing the challenges of adult life.

Synset Examples

there was a search for beauty that had somehow gone adrift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - unable to find one's way; not knowing one's whereabouts
  • Antonym: purposeful - having or showing determination or resolve

Etymology

From 'a-' (on) + 'drift', relating to drifting movement on water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00268988-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
Without direction, purpose, or guidance; moving aimlessly or without being anchored.
moving aimlessly; without purpose

Examples

After losing his job, Tom felt completely adrift and unsure what to do next.
The boat floated adrift in the open sea after the engine failed.
Many graduates feel adrift during their first year out of university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - unable to find one's way; not knowing one's location
  • Antonym: anchored - held in place; not drifting

Etymology

From 'a-' (on) + 'drift' (movement by current); first used in a nautical context.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01917033-s
Synset POS: s

(of a boat or object) floating on water without being anchored or steered; not moored or attached.
floating unmoored on water

Examples
