redstart

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a small flycatching warbler found in eastern North America, the male being marked with bright orange on its sides, wings, and tail.
small North American warbler with orange markings

Examples

We spotted a redstart perched on a low branch in the morning light.
The male redstart is easy to identify by its vibrant orange tail and wings.
Birdwatchers often visit the park in spring to catch a glimpse of the elusive redstart.

Etymology

From Middle English red (red) + start (tail; from Old English steort, tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌstɑːt / (BrE)
A small Old World songbird with a reddish breast and tail, related to the robin.
Old World songbird with red tail and breast

Examples

We spotted a beautiful redstart perched on a low branch in the garden.
The redstart is known for its striking orange-red tail feathers and cheerful song.
During migration, the woods are filled with the lively calls of the redstart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - a small Old World songbird with a red breast

Etymology

From 'red' + 'start' (an old word for tail), referring to its red tail.

Synset POS: n
