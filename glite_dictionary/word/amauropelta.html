Amauropelta

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔːroʊˈpɛl.tə / (AmE), / əˌmɔːrəˈpɛl.tə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns found as epiphytes or on the ground in America, Africa, and Polynesia.
genus of ferns

Examples

The Amauropelta genus is notable for its adaptability to both epiphytic and terrestrial habitats.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Amauropelta genus in the rainforests of South America.
Many Amauropelta species contribute to the biodiversity of tropical and subtropical forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'amauros' (dim, obscure) and 'pelta' (small shield), referring to characteristics of the fern's indusia.

2. NOUN

/ əˌmɔːrəˈpɛltə / (AmE), / əˌmaʊərəˈpɛltə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic or terrestrial ferns native to the Americas, Africa, and Polynesia.
genus of ferns

Examples

The Amauropelta genus consists of many ferns found in subtropical rainforests.
Recent studies focus on the distribution of Amauropelta species in Africa and Polynesia.
Botanists discovered a new Amauropelta growing epiphytically on tree trunks.
