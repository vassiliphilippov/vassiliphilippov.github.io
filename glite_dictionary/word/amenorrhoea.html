amenorrhoea

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.məˈnɔː.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˌæm.ɪ.nəˈriː.ə / (BrE)
the absence or suppression of normal menstrual periods in women of reproductive age
absence of menstrual periods
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with amenorrhoea after she missed several menstrual cycles.
Primary amenorrhoea can occur if a young woman has not started menstruating by age 16.
Stress and excessive exercise are known to cause amenorrhoea in some women.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: menstruation - monthly discharge of blood from the uterus

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'men' (month) + 'rhoia' (flow).
