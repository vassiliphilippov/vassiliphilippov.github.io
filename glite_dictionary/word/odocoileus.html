Odocoileus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˈkɔɪliəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈkɔɪliəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American deer, including species such as the white-tailed deer and mule deer.
genus of North American deer

Examples

The genus Odocoileus includes both white-tailed and mule deer native to North America.
Scientists conducted a study on the population dynamics of Odocoileus in the region.
Because of its distinctive antlers, Odocoileus is easily identified among other North American deer.

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) and 'koilos' (hollow), referring to characteristics of the teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˈkɔɪliəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈkɔɪliəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American deer, including species such as the white-tailed deer and mule deer.
a genus of North American deer

Examples

The Odocoileus genus includes the white-tailed and mule deer found throughout North America.
Biologists are studying how Odocoileus species adapt to changing habitats.
Hunting regulations often differ depending on the specific Odocoileus species in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer - a hoofed grazing or browsing animal of the family Cervidae

Etymology

From Greek 'odous, odont-' (tooth) and 'koilos' (hollow), referring to the hollows in the teeth of these deer.
