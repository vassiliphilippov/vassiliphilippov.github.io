affright

1. NOUN

/ əˈfraɪt / (AmE), / əˈfraɪt / (BrE)
A feeling of intense fear or terror; sudden fright.
an intense feeling of fear
archaic

Examples

The sudden roar of thunder filled him with affright.
She gazed into the darkness, her heart pounding in affright at every sound.
Old tales of haunted castles still cause children to tremble with affright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - an intense and overwhelming fear
  • Synonym: fright - a sudden intense feeling of fear

Etymology

From Middle English 'afright', from Old English 'āfyrhtan' (to frighten).

2. VERB

/ əˈfraɪt / (AmE), / əˈfraɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) To cause fear; to frighten.
(archaic) cause fear, frighten
archaic

Examples

The legend of the haunted castle did little to affright the brave visitors.
It is said that the thunderstorm would affright the villagers each year.
No wild beast in the dark woods could affright her bold heart.

Synset Examples

The stranger who hangs around the building frightens me
Ghosts could never affright her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone afraid

Etymology

From Middle English afrighten, from Old English āfyrhtan (to frighten), related to 'fright'.
