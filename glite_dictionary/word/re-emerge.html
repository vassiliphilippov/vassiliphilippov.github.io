to re-emerge

1. VERB

/ ˌri ɪˈmɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌri ɪˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to appear or become visible again after having disappeared or been in obscurity
appear or become visible again

Examples

After several years of silence, the artist began to re-emerge with new work.
Old traditions often re-emerge during times of cultural revival.
The disease may re-emerge if vaccination rates decline.

Synset Examples

The sores reappeared on her body
Her husband reappeared after having left her years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reappear - to appear again after having disappeared
  • Degree Lower: to emerge - to come into view or become apparent

Etymology

From the prefix 're-' (again) and 'emerge', from Latin 'emergere' (to rise out or up).
