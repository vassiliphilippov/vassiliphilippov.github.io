protohemin

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈhiːmɪn / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈhiːmɪn / (BrE)
a reddish-brown chloride derivative of heme, produced from hemoglobin in laboratory tests to detect the presence of blood
chloride derivative of heme used in blood tests
medical

Examples

The presence of protohemin confirmed the existence of blood in the sample.
In forensic analysis, protohemin is often formed when testing for blood stains.
Researchers isolated protohemin from the broken-down hemoglobin during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemin - a chloride of heme, often used in laboratory tests

Etymology

From 'proto-' (first) and 'hemin', itself from Greek 'haima' (blood).
