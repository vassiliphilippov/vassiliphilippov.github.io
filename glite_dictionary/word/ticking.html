ticking

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
A strong, tightly-woven fabric, typically with a striped pattern, used for covering mattresses and pillows.
strong fabric for mattresses and pillows

Examples

The mattress was covered with sturdy ticking to protect it from wear.
She chose blue-and-white striped ticking for the pillowcases.
Upholsterers often use ticking for its durability and tight weave.

Etymology

From Old English *ticcen* (covering cloth), influenced by Middle Dutch *tike* (tick, mattress covering).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a light, regular tapping or clicking sound, especially that made by a clock or watch
the sound of a clock or watch

Examples

The only noise in the silent room was the persistent ticking of the grandfather clock.
He found the rhythmic ticking of his watch soothing.
The sudden silence was broken by the faint ticking coming from the wall clock.

Synset Examples

he counted the ticks of the clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a light tapping or clicking sound
