semester

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɛstɚ / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛstə / (BrE)
A period of six months, especially as one of the two divisions of an academic year at a school or university.
half-year academic period

Examples

Many students choose to study abroad for one semester during their undergraduate degree.
Our final exams will take place at the end of the spring semester.
The course runs over a single semester and includes both lectures and practical sessions.

Etymology

From Latin 'semestris' (of six months), from 'sex' (six) + 'mensis' (month).

2. NOUN

/ səˈmɛstɚ / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛstə / (BrE)
one of the two main periods into which an academic year is divided, typically lasting around 15 to 18 weeks
half-year academic term

Examples

She plans to study abroad for one semester next year.
The fall semester begins in September and ends in December.
He took five classes during the spring semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: term - a fixed period of time during which something, such as school or a court session, lasts

Etymology

From German 'Semester', from Latin 'semestris' (of six months).
