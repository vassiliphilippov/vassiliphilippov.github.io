valedictory

1. ADJ

/ ˌvæl.əˈdɪk.tɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˌval.ɪˈdɪk.təri / (BrE)
relating to a farewell, especially a speech or address given at a formal occasion of departure or graduation
relating to farewell or departure

Examples

Her valedictory speech at the ceremony moved everyone to tears.
The CEO delivered a valedictory address on his final day at the company.
He was honored with a valedictory dinner before relocating abroad.

Synset Examples

a valedictory address
valedictory praise for his uniformly manly course
a suitable valedictory gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - an act of parting or of marking someone's departure

Etymology

From Latin valedictorius, from vale (farewell) + dicere (to say).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02833168-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌvæl.əˈdɪk.tɔr.i / (AmE), / ˌvæl.ɪˈdɪk.təri / (BrE)
a farewell speech, especially one given at a graduation ceremony by a top student
farewell speech at graduation

Examples

She delivered a heartfelt valedictory at her high school graduation ceremony.
The university president praised the valedictory given by the top student of the class.
He anxiously practiced his valedictory for weeks, determined to leave a lasting impression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye
  • Synonym: oration - a formal speech, especially on a ceremonial occasion

Etymology

From Latin 'valedicere' (to say farewell), from 'vale' (farewell) + 'dicere' (to say).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07258225-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
(of a speech, statement, or event) serving as a farewell or expressing leave-taking, especially at a formal occasion.
expressing a farewell; serving as a farewell

Examples

Her valedictory speech at the graduation ceremony moved many of the students to tears.
As his valedictory remarks concluded, the audience gave him a standing ovation.
The ambassador gave a valedictory dinner to mark the end of his service.

Synset Examples

a valedictory address

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye

Etymology

From Latin valedicere 'to bid farewell,' from vale 'farewell' + dicere 'to say'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01294221-s
Synset POS: s
