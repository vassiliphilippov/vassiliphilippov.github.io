rewa-rewa

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪwəˈreɪwə / (AmE), / ˌreɪwəˈreɪwə / (BrE)
a slender, elegant tree of New Zealand, known for its racemes of red flowers and valuable mottled red timber; also called New Zealand honeysuckle
New Zealand tree with red flowers and valuable timber

Examples

The rewa-rewa blooms in late spring, attracting birds with its striking red flowers.
Many craftspeople prize the unique grain of rewa-rewa wood for carving and furniture.
We walked through a forest of rewa-rewa, their tall trunks rising above us in the sunlight.

Etymology

From Māori rewarewa, the indigenous name for the tree.
