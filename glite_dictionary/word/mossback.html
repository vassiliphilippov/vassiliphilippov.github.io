mossback

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈmɒsˌbæk / (BrE)
a person who holds extremely old-fashioned or conservative views, especially in politics or social matters
person with very old-fashioned views

Examples

Many considered the senator a mossback unwilling to accept any form of social change.
The party was divided between energetic reformers and stubborn mossbacks.
She dismissed his ideas as those of a mossback stuck in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - a person opposing political or social progress or reform
  • Antonym: progressive - a person favoring or advocating progress, change, improvement, or reform

Etymology

Originally from the image of a fish or turtle with moss growing on its back, transferred to people with outdated views in the 19th century.
