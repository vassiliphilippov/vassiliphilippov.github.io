succession

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
The acquisition of property or title by legal descent or according to a will.
inheritance of property or title
legal

Examples

The succession of his estate was contested by several relatives.
Under the terms of her will, succession passed to her eldest daughter.
The complicated laws of succession made dividing the property difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - property received from an ancestor by legal succession or will

Etymology

From Latin successio, from succedere (to follow after).

4. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
(ecology) The gradual, orderly process by which one community of plants and animals is replaced by another in an ecosystem, eventually leading to a stable climax community.
ecological community replacement process
medical

Examples

After the wildfire, ecological succession began with the return of grasses, followed by shrubs and eventually trees.
Primary succession occurs on bare rock after a volcanic eruption, starting with pioneering species like lichens.
The restoration project aims to accelerate natural succession in the abandoned field, allowing forests to reclaim the land.

Etymology

From Latin 'successio', from 'succedere' meaning 'to follow after'.
