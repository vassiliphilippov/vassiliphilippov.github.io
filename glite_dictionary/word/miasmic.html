miasmic

1. ADJ

/ miˈæzmɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈazmɪk / (BrE)
filled with or resembling a miasma; full of vapor, especially with an unpleasant or unhealthy quality
full of vapor or miasma

Examples

The explorers found it hard to breathe in the miasmic air of the swamp.
A miasmic haze lingered over the polluted river at dawn.
She recoiled from the miasmic fumes rising from the decaying trash.

Synset Examples

miasmic jungles
a vaporous bog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasmal - full of miasma; vaporous
  • Antonym: clear - not clouded or vapory

Etymology

From New Latin 'miasma' (pollution, vapor) + English suffix '-ic'.

2. ADJ

/ maɪˈæzmɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈazmɪk / (BrE)
having the quality of a noxious or foul-smelling atmosphere, often caused by pollution or decay.
noxious, foul-smelling (atmosphere)

Examples

The city was shrouded in a miasmic haze that made breathing difficult.
A miasmic odor rose from the polluted river at sunset.
They fled the miasmic atmosphere of the swampland in search of fresh air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasmal - relating to or resembling a miasma
  • Antonym: fresh - clean, not polluted or foul-smelling (air)

Etymology

From 'miasma', from Greek 'míasma' (pollution, defilement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01057903-s
Synset POS: s
