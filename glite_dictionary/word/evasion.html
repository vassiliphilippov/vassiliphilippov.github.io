evasion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the deliberate act of avoiding paying money, such as debts or taxes
deliberate avoidance of payment

Examples

The company was fined for tax evasion after an investigation by authorities.
His frequent evasion of rent payments led to his eviction from the apartment.
The new laws are designed to make financial evasion more difficult for individuals and businesses.

Synset Examples

his evasion of all his creditors
he was indicted for nonpayment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidance - the action of keeping away from something

Etymology

From Latin 'evasio', from 'evadere' meaning 'to escape'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a statement that avoids directly addressing an unpleasant truth, often by being indirect or misleading without being literally false
indirect way of avoiding the truth

Examples

When pressed for details, the politician's evasion frustrated the reporters.
Her answer was an evasion rather than a clear response to the difficult question.
He was caught using an evasion to avoid admitting his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevarication - the act of speaking or acting in an evasive way
  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being clear and straightforward

Etymology

From Latin evasio (a going out, escape), from evadere (to escape, avoid).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of escaping from someone or something, especially by skillful or clever movement
act of escaping, especially skillfully

Examples

The fox's evasion of the hunters was remarkable and left everyone impressed.
The burglar was caught after a brief evasion through the alleyways.
Her quick evasion of his grasp showed her athletic skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - the act of breaking free from confinement or control

Etymology

From Latin 'evasio' (a getting away), from 'evadere' (to escape).

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of avoiding something that you are supposed to do, especially by deceit or trickery
deceitful avoidance of duties or obligations

Examples

His evasion of paying taxes eventually led to a hefty fine.
The manager noticed her frequent evasion of responsibility at work.
There are legal consequences for the evasion of customs duties.

Synset Examples

his evasion of his clear duty was reprehensible
that escape from the consequences is possible but unattractive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidance - the act of keeping away from or not doing something
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of conforming to or fulfilling a requirement or request

Etymology

From Latin 'evasio' (a getting away), from 'evadere' (to escape).
