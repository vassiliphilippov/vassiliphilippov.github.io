bibliophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪbliəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɪbliəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
A person who loves or collects books, especially as examples of fine or rare printing, binding, or the like.
person who loves or collects books

Examples

As a bibliophile, Clara spends her weekends exploring secondhand bookstores.
His bibliophile tendencies are evident from the overflowing bookshelves in his study.
Every bibliophile dreams of discovering a rare first edition hidden in a dusty shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookworm - person who reads a lot or is devoted to books

Etymology

From French *bibliophile*, from Greek *biblion* (book) + *philos* (loving).
