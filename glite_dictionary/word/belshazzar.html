Belshazzar

1. PROPN

/ bɛlˈʃæzər / (AmE), / bɛlˈʃæzə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) A Babylonian prince or king, son of Nebuchadnezzar II, who was warned of his downfall by mysterious handwriting on the wall, as interpreted by the prophet Daniel.
Babylonian prince in the Old Testament

Examples

The biblical story of Belshazzar and the handwriting on the wall is often cited as a warning of impending doom.
In his history class, David learned about Belshazzar and the fall of Babylon.
The painter depicted Belshazzar's feast as a dramatic scene of excess and prophecy.

Etymology

From Hebrew Belsha'tstsar, from Akkadian Bēl-šar-uṣur meaning 'Bel, protect the king'.
