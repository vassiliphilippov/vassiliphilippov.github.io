Ar

1. NOUN

/ ɑr / (AmE), / ɑː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for argon, a colorless, odorless inert gas that makes up about 1% of the earth's atmosphere
chemical symbol for argon gas

Examples

When filling the chamber, they used Ar to create an inert atmosphere.
The periodic table lists Ar with atomic number 18.
Welding with Ar prevents oxidation of metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argon - a colorless, odorless inert gas element, symbol Ar

Etymology

From Latin 'Argon', from Greek 'argōn' (inactive), used as the symbol for the element argon.
