wassail

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːseɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɒseɪl / (BrE)
A hot drink made from sweetened ale or wine mixed with spices and roasted apples, traditionally served at Christmas celebrations.
spiced ale or wine drink, esp. at Christmas

Examples

At the Christmas party, they served traditional wassail with roasted apples and spices.
He ladled hot wassail into mugs as carolers sang outside the house.
A steaming bowl of wassail sat at the center of the festive table.

Etymology

From Middle English wæs hæl (be in good health), a toast used in festive drinking.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɑːˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɒseɪl / (BrE)
to celebrate noisily, often with lots of drinking; to engage in lively and festive revelry
celebrate noisily, often with drinking
archaic

Examples

They decided to wassail until sunrise after the victory.
The townsfolk would wassail together during the winter festival.
Long ago, people would gather to wassail in the village square with songs and cider.

Synset Examples

The members of the wedding party made merry all night
Let's whoop it up — the boss is gone!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revel - to take part in noisy festivities, especially involving drinking
  • Synonym: carouse - to drink and enjoy oneself in a noisy way

Etymology

From Middle English 'wassailen', from Old Norse 'ves heill' meaning 'be well'.

3. VERB

/ ˈwɑːˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɒseɪl / (BrE)
(archaic) To drink alcohol and propose toasts to celebrate an occasion; to drink in honour of someone or something.
to propose a toast; drink in celebration (archaic)
archaic

Examples

Every year, the villagers would wassail to wish each other good fortune for the next harvest.
They wassailed the newlyweds with songs and cups raised high.
Let us wassail to the success of this daring venture!

Synset Examples

Let us toast the birthday girl!
Let's drink to the New Year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toast - to drink in honour of a person or event

Etymology

From Old Norse *ves heill* ('be healthy'); later adopted into Old English as a drinking salutation.
