status

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.təs / (BrE)
the particular condition or state of something at a specific time
condition or state at a particular time

Examples

The company is currently reviewing the status of your application.
Doctors are monitoring the patient’s medical status closely.
The project’s financial status will be discussed at the next meeting.

Synset Examples

a condition (or state) of disrepair
the current status of the arms negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state - the condition or situation of something at a particular time
  • Synonym: condition - the particular state that something or someone is in

Etymology

From Latin 'status' (state, condition), from 'stare' (to stand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtəs, ˈstætəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtəs, ˈstætəs / (BrE)
a person's or thing's social or professional position compared to others; the level of importance or respect that someone or something has in a society, organization, or group
social or professional standing

Examples

His new job gave him a higher status within the company.
Owning a home is often seen as a mark of social status.
The club only admits members of a certain status.

Synset Examples

he had the status of a minor
the novel attained the status of a classic
atheists do not enjoy a favorable position in American life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rank - position in a hierarchy or organization
  • Synonym: standing - reputation or status within a group or community
  • Synonym: position - social or official rank or status

Etymology

From Latin status (state, condition, position).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13968971-n
Synset POS: n
