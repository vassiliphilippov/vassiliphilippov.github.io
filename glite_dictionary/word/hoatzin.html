hoatzin

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈɑːtsɪn / (AmE), / həʊˈætsɪn / (BrE)
a crested South American bird (Opisthocomus hoazin), notable for its strong odor and for the claws on the wings of its young
South American bird with wing-clawed chicks

Examples

The hoatzin is famous for the clawed fingers on the wings of its chicks.
We spotted a hoatzin perched among the dense Amazonian foliage.
Because of its strong odor, the hoatzin is sometimes called the stinkbird.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *huātsīn*, via Spanish *hoatzín*.
