yottabit

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊtəˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɒtəˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to one septillion (10^24) bits, used in computing and data storage.
unit of 10^24 bits (data)

Examples

Storing the world's internet data for a year would require multiple yottabits of capacity.
The new satellite transmits several yottabits of data during its lifetime.
Scientists predict that global data traffic may soon reach the scale of yottabits per year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zettabit - A unit of information equal to 10^21 bits
  • Synonym: yottabyte - A unit of information equal to 10^24 bytes

Etymology

Formed from 'yotta-' (International System of Units prefix for 10^24, from Greek 'októ', meaning 'eight', as it is the eighth power of 1000) and 'bit'.
