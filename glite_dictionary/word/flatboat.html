flatboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈflætˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a long, flat-bottomed boat with square ends, used especially for transporting heavy loads on rivers and canals
flat-bottomed boat for carrying heavy loads

Examples

The settlers loaded their supplies onto a flatboat for the journey down the river.
During the 19th century, flatboats were commonly used to transport goods along the Mississippi.
The museum displays a restored flatboat used by pioneers.

Etymology

Formed from 'flat' + 'boat', referring to its shape.
