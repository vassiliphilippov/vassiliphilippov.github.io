to palatalise

1. VERB

/ ˈpælətəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpalətəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce a consonant by bringing the tongue close to or against the hard palate
pronounce with tongue against the palate
british

Examples

In Russian, you must palatalise the consonant before certain vowels.
Many Slavic languages palatalise consonants before front vowels.
He carefully palatalised the /k/ sound, following the teacher's instruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to palatalize - pronounce a consonant with the tongue against the palate (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'palate' + '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize')
