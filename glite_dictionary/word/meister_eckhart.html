Meister Eckhart

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪstər ˈɛkhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪstə ˈɛkhɑːt / (BrE)
(c. 1260–c. 1328), German Roman Catholic theologian and mystic known for his philosophical and mystical writings.
German theologian and mystic (1260–1328)

Examples

Many scholars study Meister Eckhart for his contributions to Christian mysticism.
The teachings of Meister Eckhart have influenced both Eastern and Western spiritual traditions.
In her dissertation, she analyzed the sermons of Meister Eckhart in their historical context.

Etymology

From German, 'Meister' meaning 'master' and 'Eckhart', his given name.
