Ploceidae

1. NOUN

/ ploʊˈsiːɪˌdi / (AmE), / pləʊˈsiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as weaverbirds, native to Africa and Asia, distinguished by their elaborate nest-building.
family of weaverbirds

Examples

Members of the Ploceidae are famous for their intricately woven nests.
The Ploceidae family includes many colorful species found in sub-Saharan Africa.
Scientists studied the social behavior of Ploceidae in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - any bird of the family Ploceidae, known for weaving elaborate nests

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ploceidae', from the genus 'Ploceus', based on Greek 'plokeus' (weaver).
