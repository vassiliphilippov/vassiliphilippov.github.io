footstep

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈfʊtstɛp / (BrE)
the distance covered in a single step while walking
distance covered by a single step

Examples

The ancient stones were placed a footstep apart to help travelers cross the stream.
He measured the length of the garden using each footstep.
Every footstep brought him closer to the mountain peak.

Synset Examples

he stepped off ten paces from the old tree and began to dig

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pace - a single step taken when walking; also a measure of distance equal to one step

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.stɛp / (BrE)
an act of taking a step while walking
an act of taking a step

Examples

She heard a footstep behind her in the hallway, making her turn quickly.
Each footstep left a faint mark in the freshly fallen snow.
He measured the distance in careful footsteps, counting as he walked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step - the act of moving by lifting the foot and setting it down again

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + 'step'.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈfʊtstɛp / (BrE)
the sound made by a person's step when walking
sound of a step when walking

Examples

She lay awake listening to every footstep in the hallway.
A sudden footstep behind him made Jack turn around quickly.
The faint footsteps grew louder as someone approached the room.

Synset Examples

he heard footsteps on the porch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tread - the sound or mark of someone walking

Etymology

From Middle English 'footstep,' compound of 'foot' + 'step.'
