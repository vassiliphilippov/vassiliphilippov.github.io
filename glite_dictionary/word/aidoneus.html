Aidoneus

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈdoʊniəs / (AmE), / aɪˈdəʊniəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of the underworld; brother of Zeus and husband of Persephone, also known as Hades.
Greek god of the underworld

Examples

According to legend, Aidoneus ruled the realm of the dead with his queen, Persephone.
Some ancient texts use Aidoneus as another name for the deity known as Hades.
The myth describes how Aidoneus abducted Persephone to be his wife in the underworld.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - Greek god of the underworld
  • Synonym: Pluto - Roman name for the god of the underworld

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'Αἰδωνεύς' (Aidōneús), an epithet used for Hades.
