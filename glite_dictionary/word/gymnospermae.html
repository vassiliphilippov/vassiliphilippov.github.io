Gymnospermae

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnɑːˈspɜːrmiː / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
A group of seed-producing plants whose seeds are not enclosed within an ovary, including conifers, cycads, and ginkgo.
group of seed-producing plants with naked seeds

Examples

Pine trees, which belong to the Gymnospermae, reproduce using cones rather than flowers.
Biologists study Gymnospermae to understand how plants with naked seeds evolved.
The division Gymnospermae includes well-known species such as fir, spruce, and ginkgo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angiosperm - plant group with seeds enclosed in an ovary, flowering plants
  • Synonym: conifer - type of gymnosperm, plant with cones

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'sperma' (seed).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑːspərˌmi / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒspəˌmiː / (BrE)
A group of seed-producing plants whose seeds are not enclosed within an ovary, including conifers, cycads, and ginkgo; sometimes treated as a class or division in botanical classification.
group of seed-producing plants with naked seeds

Examples

The Gymnospermae include conifers like pine and spruce, which bear seeds in cones.
Botanists distinguish Gymnospermae from angiosperms by their exposed seeds.
Fossils of Gymnospermae date back to the Paleozoic era, showing their ancient origins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angiosperm - seed plant with seeds enclosed in an ovary, such as flowering plants

Etymology

From Greek gymnos (naked) + sperma (seed), referring to seeds not enclosed in an ovary.
