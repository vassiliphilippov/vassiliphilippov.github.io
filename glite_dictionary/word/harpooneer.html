harpooneer

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˌpuˈnɪr / (AmE), / hɑːˌpuːˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who throws or operates a harpoon, especially in whaling or fishing
person who throws harpoons

Examples

The harpooneer stood ready at the bow as the whale surfaced.
As a skilled harpooneer, he never missed his target on the hunt.
Melville's 'Moby-Dick' features a famous harpooneer named Queequeg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpooner - a person who uses a harpoon

Etymology

From harpoon + -eer, patterned after words like 'pioneer'.
