Antonio Allegri da Correggio

1. PROPN

/ ænˌtoʊniˌoʊ əˈlɛgri dɑ koʊˈreɪdʒioʊ / (AmE), / anˌtɔːnɪˌəʊ əˈlɛgri dɑ kəˈrɛdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 1489–1534) An Italian painter renowned for his innovative use of chiaroscuro and perspective during the High Renaissance.
Italian Renaissance painter

Examples

Many art historians consider Antonio Allegri da Correggio a master of illusionistic ceiling painting.
The use of light and shadow by Antonio Allegri da Correggio deeply influenced later Baroque artists.
Visitors to Parma can admire several masterpieces by Antonio Allegri da Correggio in local churches.
