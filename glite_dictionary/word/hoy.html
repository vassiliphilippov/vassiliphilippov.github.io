hoy

1. NOUN

/ hɔɪ / (AmE), / hɔɪ / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed boat used to carry heavy loads, especially on canals
flat-bottomed cargo boat

Examples

The old merchant used a hoy to transport grain along the canal.
Several crates were loaded onto the hoy before it set off for the next town.
During the 18th century, travelers sometimes crossed short sea routes in a hoy.

Etymology

From Dutch *hoey* or *hoei*, a type of small vessel.
