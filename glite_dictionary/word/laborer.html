laborer

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bərə / (BrE)
a person who does unskilled physical work, especially outdoors or on building sites
person doing manual physical work

Examples

The construction site hired several laborers for the building project.
Many laborers work long hours under challenging conditions to support their families.
He started his career as a laborer before becoming a building supervisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worker - a person who works, especially one employed in a particular field
  • Antonym: artisan - a skilled manual worker, especially one in a trade

Etymology

From Latin 'laborare' (to work), via Middle English and Old French.
