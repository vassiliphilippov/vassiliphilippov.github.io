to motorise

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip with armed and armored motor vehicles, especially for military use
equip with armed vehicles (military)
british

Examples

The army decided to motorise its infantry divisions to improve mobility on the battlefield.
During World War II, many countries rushed to motorise their cavalry units for rapid deployment.
Modern tactics often require militaries to motorise support teams to keep up with frontline units.

Synset Examples

mechanize armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - to equip with machines, especially for military or industrial purposes
  • Antonym: demotorise - to remove motor vehicles or revert to non-motorized equipment, especially in a military context

Etymology

From 'motor' + 'ise', patterned after 'motor', from Latin 'motor' (mover).
