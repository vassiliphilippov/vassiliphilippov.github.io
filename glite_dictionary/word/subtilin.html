subtilin

1. NOUN

/ səbˈtɪlɪn / (AmE), / sʌbˈtɪlɪn / (BrE)
a polypeptide antibiotic, similar to bacitracin, obtained from certain soil bacteria, especially Bacillus subtilis
a polypeptide antibiotic from soil bacteria
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that subtilin is effective against several types of Gram-positive bacteria.
Pharmaceutical companies are interested in the potential applications of subtilin as an antibiotic.
The production of subtilin involves culturing specific strains of Bacillus subtilis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacitracin - a polypeptide antibiotic similar in action to subtilin

Etymology

From modern Latin *Bacillus subtilis*, the bacterium from which it was first obtained, with '-in' suffix.
