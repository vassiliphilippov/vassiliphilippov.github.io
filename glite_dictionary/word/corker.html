corker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrkər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkə / (BrE)
a machine used for inserting corks into bottles, especially in bottling operations
machine for corking bottles

Examples

The winery purchased a new corker to speed up the bottling process.
After filling the bottles with wine, they ran them through the corker.
The old-fashioned corker required manual operation, unlike the automated machines today.

Etymology

From 'cork' (the material) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrkər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkə / (BrE)
(dated, informal) A person or thing that is remarkably good, excellent, or surprising.
remarkable or excellent person or thing
informal slang archaic

Examples

That match was a real corker and kept everyone on the edge of their seats.
Everyone agreed that her latest joke was an absolute corker.
He produced a corker of an idea during the meeting, impressing the whole team.

Synset Examples

that story was a corker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - an outstanding or excellent person or thing
  • Antonym: dud - something that is unsuccessful or worthless
