unfledged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of an arrow) not provided with feathers for flight.
arrow: without feathers

Examples

The archer realized too late that he had nocked an unfledged arrow, which flew wildly off course.
It is dangerous to use an unfledged arrow, as it will not travel accurately.
He experimented with both unfledged and feathered arrows to test their flight characteristics.

Synset Examples

shot an unfledged arrow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fledged - (of an arrow) equipped with feathers for flight

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fledged', from Old English 'flycge' (having feathers for flight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01003090-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a young bird) not yet having developed feathers and therefore not ready to leave the nest.
not yet feathered (of young bird)

Examples

The unfledged chicks huddled together for warmth in the nest.
She found an unfledged robin on the ground beneath the tree.
Rescuers carefully placed the unfledged birds back in their nest.

Synset Examples

a small unfledged sparrow on the window sill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fledged - (of a bird) having developed wing feathers for flight

Etymology

From un- (not) + fledge (to acquire feathers), from Middle English flegen, from Old English flycge (ready to fly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01100164-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) inexperienced or immature; not fully developed or trained.
young and inexperienced

Examples

The unfledged interns needed extra guidance during their first week at the company.
Her unfledged attempt at public speaking revealed a lack of confidence.
As an unfledged artist, he was eager to learn from more established painters.

Synset Examples

a fledgling enterprise
a fledgling skier
an unfledged lawyer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fledgling - a person or organization that is inexperienced or immature
  • Antonym: experienced - having gained knowledge or skill through practice

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'fledged', referring to a bird yet to acquire feathers for flight, later extended figuratively.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00940267-s
Synset POS: s
