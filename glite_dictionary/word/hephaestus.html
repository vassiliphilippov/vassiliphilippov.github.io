Hephaestus

1. PROPN

/ həˈfɛstəs / (AmE), / hɪˈfiːstəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of fire, metalworking, and crafts, often depicted as lame, and identified with the Roman god Vulcan.
Greek god of fire and metalworking

Examples

In Greek mythology, Hephaestus was known as the master blacksmith of the gods.
According to legend, Hephaestus forged magnificent weapons for heroes and gods alike.
The temple dedicated to Hephaestus still stands in ancient Athens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vulcan - the Roman god of fire and smithing, counterpart to Hephaestus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἥφαιστος (Hēphaistos).
