Vinca rosea

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪŋkə roʊˈziə / (AmE), / ˈvɪŋkə rəʊˈziːə / (BrE)
A tropical perennial plant, commonly cultivated for its large pink or red flowers; now widely known as Madagascar periwinkle.
plant with pink or red flowers; Madagascar periwinkle

Examples

Vinca rosea is often used as a bedding plant for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
The gardener recommended planting Vinca rosea in full sunlight for best results.
Extracts from Vinca rosea have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - another common name for the plant Vinca rosea

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Vinca' and the species epithet 'rosea' (Latin for 'rosy').
