contradictory

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
(of statements, beliefs, or actions) containing or involving elements that are in conflict or disagreement.
involving disagreement or conflict

Examples

His explanation was full of contradictory statements, making it difficult to trust his story.
The witness gave contradictory accounts of the events in court.
The scientist noticed several contradictory results in the experiment.

Synset Examples

the figures are at odds with our findings
contradictory attributes of unjust justice and loving vindictiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistent - not containing any contradictions; in agreement or harmony
  • Synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout; containing contradictions

Etymology

From Latin 'contradictorius', from 'contradict-', past participle stem of 'contradīcere' (to speak against).

containing or involving elements that are opposed or inconsistent with each other; mutually opposing or in conflict
mutually opposed or inconsistent

Examples

Their statements are contradictory, so one of them must be mistaken.
He displays contradictory behavior, sometimes friendly and other times hostile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout; containing contradictory elements
  • Antonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

3. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
(of words or propositions) mutually exclusive such that both cannot be true and both cannot be false at the same time.
mutually exclusive in truth; logically opposed

Examples

The terms 'internal' and 'external' are contradictory in philosophical discussions.
A statement and its direct negation are contradictory and cannot both be true.
In logic, 'alive' and 'dead' function as contradictory predicates.

Synset Examples

5. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
(logic) A statement or proposition that is in direct opposition to another, such that both cannot be true or both cannot be false at the same time.
statement that opposes another in logic

Examples

The statements 'It is raining' and 'It is not raining' are contradictories in logic.
Every proposition has a unique contradictory that cannot be true at the same time as itself.
Debaters often illustrate their points using pairs of contradictories for emphasis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a combination of statements, ideas, or features that are opposed to one another
  • Antonym: tautology - a statement that is true in every possible interpretation

Etymology

From Late Latin contradictorius (speaking against), from Latin contrā 'against' + dictus 'said'.
