Docetism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊsəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the doctrine that Jesus Christ only appeared to have a physical body and to suffer and die, but in reality did not possess a true human body, often associated with certain Gnostic teachings and regarded as heretical by orthodox Christianity.
the doctrine that Jesus's human form and suffering were only apparent

Examples

Early Christian writers condemned Docetism as it denied the real humanity of Jesus.
The spread of Docetism forced church leaders to clarify their doctrine of the Incarnation.
Many Gnostic sects embraced Docetism, opposing mainstream Christian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnosticism - a collection of ancient religious ideas and systems, some of which influenced or included Docetism
  • Antonym: orthodox - conforming to established, accepted beliefs, especially in religion; opposite of heretical

Etymology

From Greek *dokein* (to seem, to appear) via Latin *docetismus*.
