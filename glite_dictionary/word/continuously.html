continuously

1. ADV

/ kənˈtɪnjuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjʊəsli / (BrE)
Without interruption; in an unbroken or constant manner.
without interruption; steadily

Examples

The river flowed continuously through the valley for miles.
She spoke continuously during the entire meeting, barely pausing for breath.
The security cameras record continuously to ensure the building is always monitored.

Synset Examples

The function is continuously differentiable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantly - continuously over a period of time without interruption
  • Antonym: intermittently - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuously

Etymology

From continuous + -ly; 'continuous' from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted).

2. ADV

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.jʊ.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that does not stop or is not interrupted; without pausing or ceasing
without stopping or interruption

Examples

The machines in the factory operate continuously throughout the night.
She talked continuously for over an hour without taking a break.
Rain fell continuously for three days, causing widespread flooding.

Synset Examples

dance inspires him ceaselessly to strive higher and higher toward the shining pinnacle of perfection that is the goal of every artiste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantly - happening all the time or very often over a period of time
  • Synonym: ceaselessly - without stopping; in an unending manner
  • Antonym: intermittently - in an irregular or interrupted manner

Etymology

From 'continuous' + '-ly'; 'continuous' from Latin 'continuus' (joining, uninterrupted).
