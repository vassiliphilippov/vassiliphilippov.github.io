electromagnetics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that studies electromagnetic phenomena and the interaction of electric and magnetic fields.
physics of electromagnetic phenomena

Examples

She specializes in electromagnetics and teaches courses on its principles at the university.
Advances in electromagnetics have led to the development of more efficient wireless communication systems.
Modern radar technology relies heavily on concepts from electromagnetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electromagnetism - the physical interaction between electric and magnetic fields
  • Degree Higher: physics - the science of matter, energy, and their interactions

Etymology

From 'electro-' (relating to electricity) and 'magnetics' (study of magnetism).
