unsafe

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪf / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪf / (BrE)
Not protected from or exposed to possible attack or danger.
not protected from attack or danger

Examples

That road is unsafe to use at night because of frequent robberies.
After the attack, residents felt unsafe in their own homes.
The border region is considered unsafe due to ongoing conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - protected from harm or danger

Etymology

From un- (not) + safe, ultimately from Latin salvus (uninjured, healthy).

not safe; likely to cause harm or risk

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪf / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪf / (BrE)
not protected from danger, harm, or risk; not safe
not safe; dangerous or risky

Examples

The old bridge is considered unsafe and has been closed to vehicles.
Many people felt unsafe walking alone in the area at night.
Eating food that has not been cooked properly can be unsafe for your health.

Synset Examples

his fortune was increasingly insecure
an insecure future

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - protected from danger or risk; opposite of unsafe
  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury; similar in meaning to unsafe

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'safe', from Old French 'sauf', from Latin 'salvus' (uninjured, healthy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02102035-a
Synset POS: a
