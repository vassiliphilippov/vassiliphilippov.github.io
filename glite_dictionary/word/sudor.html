sudor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdɔr / (AmE), / ˈsuːdɔː / (BrE)
a salty fluid secreted by sweat glands, especially as a response to heat or stress; sweat
salty fluid secreted as sweat
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that excessive sudor may be a symptom of certain medical conditions.
After exercising in the hot sun, his shirt was soaked with sudor.
Medical textbooks often refer to sweat as sudor.

Synset Examples

sweat poured off his brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat - salty liquid produced by sweat glands

Etymology

From Latin sudor (sweat, perspiration).
