nephrolithiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛfroʊlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
the medical condition of having kidney stones in the kidney
medical condition of kidney stones
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with nephrolithiasis after complaining of severe back pain.
Chronic dehydration can increase your risk of developing nephrolithiasis.
A family history of nephrolithiasis may predispose individuals to kidney stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney stone - a hard mass formed in the kidneys, also known as a renal calculus

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + lithos (stone) + -iasis (medical condition).
