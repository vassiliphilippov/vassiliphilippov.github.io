botch

1. NOUN

/ bɑtʃ / (AmE), / bɒtʃ / (BrE)
an embarrassing or clumsy mistake, especially one resulting from poor work or carelessness
an embarrassing or clumsy mistake

Examples

The repair job turned out to be a botch and had to be redone by a professional.
After the botch in the kitchen, dinner was completely ruined.
The company tried to cover up their botch during the product launch, but customers noticed immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: fiasco - a complete failure, especially in a ludicrous or humiliating way
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

From Middle English bocchen, related to patching or mending poorly.

2. VERB

/ bɑtʃ / (AmE), / bɒtʃ / (BrE)
to carry out a task badly or clumsily; to spoil something through ineptitude
carry out badly; spoil through clumsiness

Examples

I completely botched the presentation by forgetting my main points.
She was worried she might botch the interview if she got too nervous.
They botched the repairs, making the leak even worse.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungle - to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or make a mistake
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or correct something

Etymology

Probably from Middle English bocche (swelling, boil, patch), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
