archer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃə / (BrE)
a person skilled in shooting with a bow and arrow
person skilled with a bow and arrow

Examples

The skilled archer hit the center of the target with every shot.
During medieval battles, an archer could be a valuable asset to any army.
She trained for years to become an accomplished archer and compete in the national championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowman - a person who shoots with a bow

Etymology

From Old French 'archier', from Latin 'arcarius' (from 'arcus', bow).
