ovular

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvjələr / (AmE), / ˈəʊvjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or of the nature of an ovule, especially in plants or fungi
of or relating to an ovule
medical

Examples

The botanist examined the ovular tissue under a microscope.
Certain mutations affect the development of ovular structures in flowering plants.
The ovular stage is critical for fertilization to occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovule - a small structure in seed plants that develops into a seed after fertilization

Etymology

From ovule + -ar, modeled after similar adjectival forms.

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.vjə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒv.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an ovum (egg cell)
relating to an ovum (egg cell)
medical

Examples

Certain ovular structures can be observed during the early stages of embryonic development.
The scientist examined the ovular membrane under the microscope for abnormalities.
Fertilization occurs when a sperm cell merges with an ovular cell.

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' (egg) + '-ular', a suffix forming adjectives.
