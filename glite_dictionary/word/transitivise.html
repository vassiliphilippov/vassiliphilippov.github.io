to transitivise

1. VERB

/ ˈtrænzɪtəˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrænsɪtɪvaɪz / (BrE)
to make a verb transitive; to cause (a verb) to take a direct object
make (a verb) transitive

Examples

Linguists often transitivise verbs by adding appropriate suffixes or particles.
Some English verbs can be transitivised through minor morphological changes.
The teacher explained how to transitivise the verb to fit the sentence structure.

Synset Examples

adding ‘out’ to many verbs transitivizes them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intransitivise - to make a verb intransitive

Etymology

From 'transitive' (typically via the addition of the suffix '-ise'/'-ize').
