bewitchment

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈwɪtʃmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪtʃmənt / (BrE)
a state caused by the influence of magic, or a magical spell; the act of enchanting or casting a spell on someone
the state of being under a magic spell

Examples

The villagers feared the bewitchment that had fallen over their town.
She claimed that his sudden illness was the result of a bewitchment.
Under the bewitchment of the sorceress, he lost control of his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchantment - the state of being under a magical influence
  • Antonym: curse - a spell intended to harm or bring bad luck

Etymology

From 'bewitch' (to cast magic over) + '-ment' (action or process suffix).
