uncompromising

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑmprəˌmaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒmprəˌmaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to change one's opinions, principles, or demands, even if this may cause difficulties or make others unhappy.
not willing to compromise

Examples

The leader remained uncompromising throughout the negotiations, refusing to yield on any issue.
Her uncompromising honesty sometimes made people uncomfortable, but they always trusted her judgment.
The artist was known for his uncompromising commitment to his craft, never altering his style for commercial success.

Synset Examples

took an uncompromising stance in the peace talks
uncompromising honesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflexible - not willing to change or adapt
  • Synonym: rigid - not able or willing to change or adapt
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt easily
  • Antonym: compromising - willing to make concessions

Etymology

From un- (not) + compromising (from Latin compromittere, to make a mutual promise).
