Holbein the Younger

1. PROPN

/ ˈhoʊlbaɪn ðə ˈjʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlbaɪn ðə ˈjʌŋə / (BrE)
(1497–1543) German painter and engraver renowned for his remarkable portraits, especially those of Henry VIII and the English court.
German portrait painter of the English court

Examples

The portrait of Thomas More by Holbein the Younger reveals stunning attention to detail and character.
During his time in England, Holbein the Younger created iconic images of Henry VIII and his court.
Art historians admire the lifelike realism of Holbein the Younger in his portrait works.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Holbein the Elder - German painter, father of Holbein the Younger

Etymology

Distinguished from his father, Hans Holbein the Elder, a German painter.
