patronized

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæt.rəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a place or business) having regular customers or visitors; frequented by patrons.
having regular customers; frequented

Examples

The newly opened café quickly became a patronized spot for locals and tourists alike.
Many independent bookstores are less patronized nowadays due to online retailers.
They prefer restaurants that are highly patronized because they believe the food is fresher.

Synset Examples

street full of flourishing well-patronized shops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpatronized - having no customers or patrons

Etymology

From the verb 'patronize', from French 'patroniser' (to act as a patron), based on 'patron' (supporter).
