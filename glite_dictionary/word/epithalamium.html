epithalamium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛp.ə.θəˈleɪ.mi.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛp.ɪ.θəˈleɪ.mɪ.əm / (BrE)
a poem or ode written in honor of a bride and bridegroom, typically celebrating their marriage
a poem honoring a bride and bridegroom

Examples

The poet was asked to compose an epithalamium for the royal wedding.
Her epithalamium was filled with joyful blessings for the newlyweds.
In ancient Greece, an epithalamium was sung outside the bridal chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding song - a song associated with weddings

Etymology

From Latin 'epithalamium', from Greek 'epithalamion' (bridal song), from 'epi-' (upon) + 'thalamos' (bridal chamber).
