swearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsweərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making a solemn promise or declaration, especially by oath in a court of law, to tell the truth or fulfill a duty
making a formal oath or promise

Examples

He began the trial by swearing to tell the truth before the judge.
The act of swearing is an important part of giving testimony in court.
Witnesses must complete the process of swearing before their statements are accepted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • Antonym: perjury - the offense of willfully telling a lie under oath

Etymology

From Middle English 'sweren', from Old English 'swerian' (to take an oath).

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsweə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of using profane or obscene language, often as an expression of anger or surprise
use of profane or obscene language

Examples

Her constant swearing annoyed many of her colleagues in the office.
He tried to control his anger, but loud swearing could be heard from his room.
The movie was criticized for its frequent use of swearing throughout the dialogue.

Synset Examples

expletives were deleted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cursing - the act of uttering offensive words in anger or frustration
  • Synonym: expletive - an oath or swear word, especially one considered blasphemous or obscene
  • Antonym: polite language - speech that avoids obscenities or strong language

Etymology

From Middle English swere (to swear) + -ing.
