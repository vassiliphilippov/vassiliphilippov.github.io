homeward

1. ADV

/ ˈhoʊmwɚd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmwəd / (BrE)
Toward home; in the direction of one's home.
toward home

Examples

As dusk fell, the hikers turned homeward along the forest path.
After months abroad, she finally set out homeward with a sense of relief.
The dog wagged its tail and trotted homeward after exploring the park.

Synset Examples

fought his way homeward through the deep snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homewards - in the direction of home; alternative form, chiefly British

Etymology

From Middle English 'homeward', from 'home' + '-ward' (directional suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmwɚd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmwəd / (BrE)
toward or relating to one's home or returning home
toward home; returning home

Examples

After a long day at work, she began her homeward journey.
We watched the flock take a homeward path at sunset.
The homeward voyage was much smoother than the outgoing trip.

Synset Examples

in a homeward direction
homeward-bound commuters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outward - moving or directed away from home or a central place

Etymology

From Middle English 'homward', from Old English 'hāmweard' (toward home).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01688009-s
Synset POS: s
