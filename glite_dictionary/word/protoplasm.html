protoplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtəˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəˌplazəm / (BrE)
the living substance within a cell, including the cytoplasm and the nucleus
living substance of a cell

Examples

The microscope revealed the movement of protoplasm within the plant cell.
Scientists study protoplasm to better understand how cells function.
Early biologists believed that protoplasm was the physical basis of all life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cytoplasm - the material within a cell, excluding the nucleus

Etymology

From Greek prōto- (first) + plasma (something formed or molded).
