episcopal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈpɪskəpəl / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪskəpl / (BrE)
relating to a bishop or bishops, or to the system of church government by bishops.
relating to bishops or their authority

Examples

The church follows an episcopal form of governance, with authority held by bishops.
Many historical documents refer to the episcopal duties within the diocese.
Some Christians prefer episcopal traditions over congregational ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, usually in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Late Latin episcopalis, from episcopus meaning 'bishop'.
