weaned

1. ADJ

/ wiːnd / (AmE), / wiːnd / (BrE)
(of a young mammal or child) having been accustomed to food other than its mother's milk; no longer dependent on something.
no longer dependent (esp. on mother's milk)

Examples

The weaned kittens explored the kitchen with curiosity and energy.
After a month, the weaned puppy stopped following its mother everywhere.
The farmer kept the weaned calves in a separate pen to get them used to eating hay.

Synset Examples

the just-weaned calf bawled for its mother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dependent - relying on something or someone for support

Etymology

Past participle of 'wean', from Old English *wenian* (to accustom).
