tufted

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌftɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʌftɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) having a bunch or cluster of small, usually soft, hair-like or feathery parts, especially as an ornament on the head; often used in combination.
having an ornamental bunch or cluster (tuft)

Examples

The tufted duck is easily recognized by the tuft of feathers on its head.
A tufted capuchin monkey has a noticeable patch of hair above its ears.
She admired the tufted loosestrife blooming by the pond.

Synset Examples

golden crested
crested iris
crested oriole
tufted duck
tufted loosestrife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crested - having a crest or tuft, especially on the head of a bird or animal

Etymology

From 'tuft' (a small bunch or cluster), with the adjectival suffix '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʌftɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʌftɪd / (BrE)
(of plants) growing in small, dense clumps or tufts.
growing in dense clumps (of plants)

Examples

The tufted grasses made the hillside look soft and uneven.
Several tufted plants thrive in rocky, dry environments where water is scarce.
Their garden features many tufted species, which add interesting texture to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumped - growing in clusters or masses
  • Antonym: sparse - thinly scattered or distributed

Etymology

From 'tuft' (a small bunch of something) + -ed (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02261573-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈtʌftɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʌftɪd / (BrE)
having tufts or decorated with tufts; bearing or featuring clusters of soft threads, feathers, or similar material attached at one end and left loose at the other.
having or decorated with tufts

Examples

The new living room features a luxurious tufted velvet sofa that adds elegance to the space.
A tufted headboard brings texture and sophistication to any bedroom décor.
The fields were covered with tufted grasses swaying in the gentle breeze.

Synset Examples

a tufted bedspread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufty - having tufts, especially in a natural manner
