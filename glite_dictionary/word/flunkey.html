flunkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈflʌŋki / (BrE)
A male servant, especially one performing menial tasks or acting in a fawning or obsequious manner, such as a footman.
male servant; obsequious attendant

Examples

The millionaire arrived surrounded by his loyal flunkeys carrying his bags.
She refused to be treated like a mere flunkey, following orders without question.
The diplomat's flunkeys stood at attention throughout the entire banquet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lackey - servant or someone who behaves very obsequiously to authority
  • Synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially a household employee

Etymology

From Scottish flunky, perhaps from French flanquer (to flank, to hang at one's side).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈflʌŋki / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an excessively obedient or servile way to someone in authority
servile or subservient person

Examples

He refused to act like a flunkey and questioned the manager's decisions.
The dictator surrounded himself with loyal flunkeys who did as they were told.
She despised being treated as a mere flunkey in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone in order to gain advantage
  • Synonym: lackey - a servile follower or attendant
  • Antonym: boss - a person in authority or control

Etymology

From Scottish 'flunkey', probably from French 'flanqué' (having someone at one's side, attendant).
