Peziza coccinea

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzaɪzə kɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / pɛˈziːzə kɒkˈsɪniːə / (BrE)
a European fungus with a bright scarlet, cup-shaped fruiting body, commonly found on woodland ground in spring
scarlet European cup fungus

Examples

The vivid red cups of the Peziza coccinea brightened the forest floor after the rain.
Several photographers visited the park to capture images of the rare Peziza coccinea in bloom.
Mycology students often study Peziza coccinea as a classic example of a European cup fungus.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Peziza (a type of fungus) + coccinea (scarlet), referring to its color.
