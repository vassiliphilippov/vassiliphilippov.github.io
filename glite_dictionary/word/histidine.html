histidine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstəˌdin / (AmE), / ˈhɪstɪdiːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in many proteins, important for tissue growth and repair
an essential amino acid
medical

Examples

Histidine is crucial for the growth and repair of bodily tissues.
Foods rich in histidine include meat, fish, and dairy products.
A deficiency in histidine can lead to anemia and delayed growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compound that forms the building blocks of proteins

Etymology

From German 'Histidin', from 'histos' (Greek, meaning 'tissue') + '-idine' (chemical suffix).
