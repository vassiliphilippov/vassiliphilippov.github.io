unbroken

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
(of an animal) not yet tamed, subdued, or trained for service or use.
not tamed or trained (of animals)

Examples

The rancher showed us the unbroken mustangs in the field.
Training an unbroken horse requires patience and skill.
The auction featured several young, unbroken colts.

Synset Examples

unbroken colts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tamed - brought under control or made less wild (of animals)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02398287-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
not damaged or divided; whole and intact; in one piece.
not broken; whole and intact

Examples

The windowpane was remarkable for remaining unbroken after the storm.
She treasured the antique vase, which was still unbroken after all these years.
The horse galloped across an unbroken field, free from fences or obstacles.

Synset Examples

fortunately the other lens is unbroken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intact - remaining whole or undamaged
  • Antonym: broken - damaged or no longer in one piece

Etymology

formed by prefixing un- (not) to 'broken'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00290231-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
(of land or farmland) not plowed or cultivated; left in its natural state.
not plowed or cultivated (of land)

Examples

The settlers gazed across the unbroken prairie, planning where they would first begin to farm.
Much of the region remains unbroken, home to native plants and wildlife.
The developers bought a vast expanse of unbroken land at the edge of town.

Synset Examples

unplowed fields
unbroken land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plowed - (of land) land that has been turned over with a plow
Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
Not interrupted; continuous in space, time, or sequence.
not interrupted; continuous

Examples

The hiking path stretched for miles as an unbroken line through the forest.
She enjoyed the unbroken silence of the early morning hours.
The team maintained an unbroken winning streak throughout the season.

Synset Examples

cars in an unbroken procession
the unbroken quiet of the afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous - continuing without interruption
  • Antonym: interrupted - having a break or interruption

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'broken' (past participle of 'break').

5. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
(especially of promises or contracts) not violated, disregarded, or interrupted; maintained continuously as agreed.
not violated; kept (of promises/contracts)

Examples

After years of unbroken trust, their partnership thrived.
The treaty remained unbroken for decades, leading to lasting peace.
He valued her unbroken promise more than any gift.

Synset Examples

unbroken promises
promises kept

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broken - violated or disregarded (as in broken promises)

Etymology

un- (not) + broken, past participle of 'break'.
