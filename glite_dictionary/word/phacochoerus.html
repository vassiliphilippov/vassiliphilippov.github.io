Phacochoerus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfækəˈkwɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌfækəʊˈkɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of African wild pigs, commonly known as warthogs, recognized by their large facial warts and curved tusks.
genus of African warthogs

Examples

The Phacochoerus is easily recognized by its large warts and impressive tusks.
Researchers observed a herd of Phacochoerus grazing near the savanna.
Unlike other wild pigs, Phacochoerus prefers open woodland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warthog - A wild African pig with distinctive tusks and facial warts

Etymology

From Greek phakos (wart) + khoiros (pig), referring to the facial warts and pig-like appearance.
