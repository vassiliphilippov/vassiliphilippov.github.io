Tapirus

1. NOUN

/ təˈpaɪrəs / (AmE), / təˈpaɪrəs / (BrE)
The genus of odd-toed ungulates in the family Tapiridae, comprising the tapirs.
genus of tapirs (mammals)

Examples

The genus Tapirus includes several species of tapirs found in Central and South America.
Scientists recently discovered a fossil belonging to the ancient Tapirus lineage.
Members of the Tapirus genus are recognized by their distinctive snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large, herbivorous mammal of the genus Tapirus

Etymology

From Latin Tapirus, from Tupi (indigenous South American language) 'tapira' (tapir).

2. NOUN

/ təˈpaɪərəs / (AmE), / təˈpaɪərəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tapiridae, comprising tapirs; a genus of large, herbivorous mammals native to Central and South America and Southeast Asia.
the type genus of tapirs (Tapiridae)

Examples

The genus Tapirus is known for its distinctive prehensile snout and large size.
Several extinct species once belonged to the genus Tapirus, as revealed by fossil records.
Researchers studying South American wildlife often focus on members of the genus Tapirus due to their ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large, herbivorous mammal of the Tapiridae family

Etymology

New Latin, from Latin 'tapirus' (tapir).
