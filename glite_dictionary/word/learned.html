learned

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɝːnɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd / (BrE)
having or showing extensive knowledge or education; scholarly
having extensive knowledge; scholarly

Examples

The professor was known as a truly learned scholar in his field.
Her learned opinions were frequently sought by other experts.
The panel included several learned scientists who contributed valuable insights.

Synset Examples

a learned jurist
an erudite professor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudite - having or showing great knowledge or learning
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

Past participle of 'learn', used as an adjective to mean scholarly since the late Middle English period.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɜrnɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd / (BrE)
(of behavior or responses) acquired by conditioning or experience rather than inherited or instinctive.
acquired through learning or experience

Examples

Many phobias are the result of learned behavior in response to traumatic events.
The experiment demonstrated that the rats could develop a learned avoidance of certain flavors.
Unlike innate reflexes, a learned response requires prior experience or training.

Synset Examples

a conditioned response

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instinctive - arising naturally, not acquired through learning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01641174-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɝːnɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing profound knowledge, especially gained from study; highly educated or knowledgeable.
highly educated; knowledgeable

Examples

The professor is a truly learned man, respected for his wisdom and scholarship.
Her learned analysis of the texts impressed everyone at the conference.
The book was reviewed by a panel of learned experts in the field.

Synset Examples

knowing instructors
a knowledgeable critic
a knowledgeable audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledgeable - well-informed; showing knowledge

Etymology

From Middle English 'lerned', past participle of 'lernen' (to learn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00833590-s
Synset POS: s
