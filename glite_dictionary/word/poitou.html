Poitou

1. PROPN

/ pwɑːˈtuː / (AmE), / pwɑːˈtuː / (BrE)
a low-lying region in west central France on the Bay of Biscay, known for its distinct history and geography
region of western France

Examples

He spent a summer exploring the charming villages of Poitou.
Poitou is renowned for its Romanesque churches and green countryside.
After moving to Poitou, she quickly adapted to the region's culinary traditions.

Etymology

From French Poitou, ultimately from Latin Pictavi, name of the ancient Pictones tribe.
