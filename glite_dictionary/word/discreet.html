discreet

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrit / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
careful and prudent in one's speech or actions, especially in order to avoid causing offense or to gain an advantage; showing good judgment in avoiding unnecessary attention or risk
careful and prudent; avoiding unnecessary attention

Examples

She was always discreet when discussing sensitive topics with her colleagues.
The company prefers a discreet approach to handling customer complaints.
He made a discreet inquiry about the job opening, not wanting to attract too much attention.

Synset Examples

circumspect actions
physicians are now more circumspect about recommending its use
a discreet investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumspect - careful to consider all circumstances and possible consequences

Etymology

From Latin discretus (separated, distinct), via Old French discret (discerning, prudent).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrit / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
careful and prudent in speech or action, especially in order to avoid causing offense or to gain an advantage; showing modesty and wise self-restraint.
careful and prudent; self-restrained

Examples

She made a discreet inquiry about the job opening, careful not to attract too much attention.
Please be discreet with this information, as it is confidential.
The jeweler created a discreet but elegant ring, perfect for everyday wear.

Synset Examples

his trusted discreet aide
a discreet, finely wrought gold necklace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - showing care and thought for the future
  • Antonym: indiscreet - not showing careful judgment or prudence; lacking in self-restraint

Etymology

From Old French *discret*, from Latin *discretus* (separated, distinct, discerning).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrit / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
careful and sensitive in speech or action, especially in order to avoid causing offense or to gain an advantage; able to notice or understand things without drawing attention.
careful and tactful; unobtrusively perceptive

Examples

Despite knowing the details, the advisor remained discreet and did not share private information with others.
She gave a discreet nod to show her understanding without interrupting the conversation.
His discreet handling of the conflict helped maintain peace in the office.

Synset Examples

a discerning editor
a discreet silence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactful - showing sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues
  • Antonym: indiscreet - not careful or sensible in speech or actions

Etymology

From Latin discretus (separate, distinct), past participle of discernere (to separate, distinguish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02393240-s
Synset POS: s
