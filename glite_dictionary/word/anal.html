2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to the stage in early childhood development, in Freudian theory, when the child's interest is focused on the anus and bowel control; associated with personality traits if fixation occurs at this stage.
psychosexual development stage (Freudian theory)
medical

Examples

Freud's theory suggests that fixation at the anal stage can lead to obsession with orderliness.
The anal phase occurs around the ages of eighteen months to three years.
Psychologists debate the impact of anal stage experiences on adult personality traits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oral - relating to the first stage of Freudian psychosexual development, focused on the mouth
  • Degree Higher: phallic - relating to the third stage of Freudian psychosexual development, focused on the genitals

Etymology

From Latin 'anus' (ring, anus), via psychological terminology.
