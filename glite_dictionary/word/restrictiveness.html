restrictiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of limiting or qualifying the meaning of another word or phrase, making it more specific
grammatical quality of limiting meaning

Examples

The restrictiveness of the adjective 'red' in 'red hat' narrows down the possible meanings.
In English grammar, restrictiveness helps clarify which noun the speaker is referring to.
Teachers often explain the restrictiveness of clauses to help students avoid ambiguity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - limiting the meaning of another element in grammar

Etymology

From 'restrictive' + '-ness', with roots in Latin 'restringere' (to bind back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of limiting freedom or preventing certain actions or behaviors
quality of being restrictive

Examples

Some parents worry that too much restrictiveness can stifle their children's creativity.
The restrictiveness of the new company policies frustrated many employees.
Teachers debated the restrictiveness of the school dress code in a faculty meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissiveness - the quality of allowing behavior with few restrictions
  • Synonym: strictness - the quality of being very demanding or rigid in rules or requirements

Etymology

From 'restrictive' + '-ness'; 'restrictive' from Latin 'restrictus', past participle of 'restringere' (to bind fast, confine).
