Packera aurea

1. NOUN

/ pəˈkɛɹə ɔˈɹiə / (AmE), / pəˈkɛəɹə ɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the daisy family, native to the eastern United States and Texas, with golden-yellow flowers, sometimes considered invasive.
a North American herb with golden-yellow flowers

Examples

Packera aurea is prized by gardeners for its vibrant yellow blooms in spring.
The conservation team worked to control the spread of Packera aurea in the local wetlands.
Some botanists still refer to Packera aurea by its older name, Senecio aureus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden ragwort - common name for Packera aurea
  • Synonym: Senecio aureus - alternative botanical name for this species

Etymology

From Latin: Packera (genus) and aurea meaning 'golden', describing the flower's color.
