em

1. NOUN

/ ɛm / (AmE), / ɛm / (BrE)
a unit of measurement in printing, equal to the width of the letter 'M' in a given type size, traditionally about 1/6 of an inch in standard printing.
unit of measurement in printing

Examples

In traditional typography, an em is used to specify the size of an indentation.
The designer increased the margin by one em to improve readability.
Page layouts in CSS often use em units for scalable fonts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: en - a printing unit half the width of an em

Etymology

From the name of the letter 'M', as the unit was originally based on the width of the 'M' in a given typeface.

2. NOUN

/ ɛm / (AmE), / ɛm / (BrE)
a unit in typography equal to the width of the letter 'M', originally a square block used as a space or quad in printing
typographic unit or quad

Examples

Each paragraph indent was exactly one em wide, matching the traditional typesetting rule.
The designer adjusted the spacing between words to be two ems for emphasis.
In old printing, a em quad created a clear, square space between text blocks.

Synset Examples

since ‘em quad’ is hard to distinguish from ‘en quad’, printers sometimes called it a ‘mutton quad’

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: en - a unit in typography half the width of an em

Etymology

From the name of the letter M, whose width defined the original unit in typesetting.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
