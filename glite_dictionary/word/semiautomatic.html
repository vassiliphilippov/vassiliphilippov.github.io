semiautomatic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
a pistol that automatically reloads after each shot but requires a separate pull of the trigger for each round fired
a pistol that reloads automatically with each shot

Examples

The officer drew his semiautomatic and ordered the suspect to put his hands up.
He prefers carrying a semiautomatic for its quick reloading feature and reliability.
The new law restricts the sale of semiautomatics to civilians in the state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolver - a handgun with a revolving cylinder containing multiple chambers for bullets
  • Degree Higher: automatic - a firearm that continues firing as long as the trigger is pressed

Etymology

From 'semi-' (half, partially) and 'automatic', indicating partial automatic function.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiːmiˌɔːtəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a machine or device) only partly automatic, performing some tasks automatically but requiring manual input for others, especially describing firearms that automatically reload but require a separate trigger pull for each shot.
partly automatic; partially self-operating

Examples

The officer carried a semiautomatic pistol in his holster at all times.
Many household appliances today are semiautomatic, requiring both user input and automated processes.
She explained how a semiautomatic washing machine needs to be filled and emptied manually.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: automatic - operating without direct human control
  • Degree Lower: manual - operated by hand without automation

Etymology

From semi- (half, partly) + automatic; first used in early 20th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˌɔtəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɔːtəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of a firearm) capable of automatically loading the next round but requiring a separate trigger pull for each shot.
firearm that loads automatically, fires one shot per trigger pull

Examples

The new rifle is a semiautomatic weapon designed for rapid but controlled firing.
Many police forces use semiautomatic pistols because they combine speed and safety.
Hunters often prefer a semiautomatic shotgun for follow-up shots.

Synset Examples

an autoloading rifle

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: automatic - (of firearms) capable of firing continuously while the trigger is pressed
  • Degree Lower: manual - (of firearms) requiring manual action to load and fire each round

Etymology

From semi- (half) and automatic, meaning partially automatic in function.
