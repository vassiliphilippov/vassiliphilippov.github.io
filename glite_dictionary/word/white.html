White

1. NOUN

/ waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
a person of European descent or someone with a light or pale skin complexion, especially in contrast to people of other racial backgrounds
person of European descent; pale-skinned person

Examples

During the 20th century, laws in some countries separated Whites from people of other races.
She wondered what challenges Whites faced as immigrants in a new land.
In some communities, Whites are now a minority compared to other groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Black - a person with dark skin, typically of African ancestry
