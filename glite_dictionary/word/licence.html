licence

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.səns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.səns / (BrE)
an official document that gives someone permission to do or own something
official permission document
british

Examples

You need a licence to drive a car in the UK.
The restaurant lost its alcohol licence after breaking the law.
She applied for a fishing licence before the season started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - official document granting permission (American English spelling)
  • Synonym: permit - document granting legal permission

Etymology

From Latin 'licentia' (freedom, license), via Old French 'licence'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06561652-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
freedom to deliberately depart from established rules or conventions, especially in behavior, speech, or artistic expression.
freedom from usual rules or conventions
british

Examples

The director took considerable artistic licence with the story's historical events.
Certain comedians are given more licence in their jokes than others.
The poet's use of grammar shows a deliberate licence to create a unique style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - freedom to deviate from rules; US spelling variant
  • Antonym: restraint - limitation or control over behavior or expression

Etymology

From Latin licentia (freedom, liberty), via Old French licence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14018291-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈlaɪ.səns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.səns / (BrE)
to give official permission for someone to do or operate something, especially after verifying qualifications or standards
officially permit or authorize
british

Examples

He is licenced to drive heavy vehicles in the UK.
The government must licence all operators before they can provide service.
After passing the exam, she was licenced as a pharmacist.

Synset Examples

I am licensed to practice law in this state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - to officially give permission; American spelling

Etymology

From Latin licentia (freedom, liberty, license), via Old French licence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02449527-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
Freedom that is excessive or lacks proper restraint, often leading to disregard for rules or standards.
excessive or unrestrained freedom

Examples

Some critics argue that media licence can threaten social stability if left unchecked.
The new policy was condemned for granting licence to irresponsible behavior.
He confused artistic freedom with licence to offend traditional values.

Synset Examples

when liberty becomes license, dictatorship is near
the intolerable license with which the newspapers break … the rules of decorum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control; a measure or condition that keeps someone or something under control

Etymology

From Latin licentia (freedom, liberty, leave), via Old French licence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14018863-n
Synset POS: n
