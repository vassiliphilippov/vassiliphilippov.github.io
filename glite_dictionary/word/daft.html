daft

1. ADJ

/ dæft / (AmE), / dɑːft / (BrE)
silly, foolish, or not sensible, often in a way that is mildly amusing
silly or foolish
informal british

Examples

You must be daft to go outside without a coat in this weather.
She laughed at his daft sense of humor and silly jokes.
It sounds daft, but I think this crazy idea just might work.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - showing a lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensible - having or showing good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Middle English, perhaps related to Old English 'gedæfte' (mild, gentle).
