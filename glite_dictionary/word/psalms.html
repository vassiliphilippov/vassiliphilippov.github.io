Psalms

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːmz / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmz / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament consisting of a collection of 150 religious poems or songs, traditionally attributed to King David
book of religious poems in the Bible

Examples

The choir sang passages from the Psalms during the Sunday morning service.
She reads a different Psalms chapter every night before bed.
Many composers have set texts from the Psalms to music throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psalm - a sacred song or hymn, in particular any of those contained in the biblical Book of Psalms

Etymology

From Greek *psalmoi* (songs sung to a harp), from Hebrew *tehillim* (songs of praise).
