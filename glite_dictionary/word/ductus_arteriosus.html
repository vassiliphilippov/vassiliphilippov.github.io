ductus arteriosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌk.təs ɑːrˌtɪr.iˈoʊ.səs / (AmE), / ˈdʌk.təs ɑːˌtɪə.riˈəʊ.səs / (BrE)
A short blood vessel in a fetus that connects the pulmonary artery to the aorta, allowing blood to bypass the lungs before birth; it normally closes soon after birth.
fetal blood vessel connecting pulmonary artery to aorta
medical

Examples

The ductus arteriosus is an essential part of fetal circulation, allowing blood to bypass the lungs.
If the ductus arteriosus does not close after birth, it can result in a condition known as patent ductus arteriosus.
Surgeons sometimes perform procedures to close a persistently open ductus arteriosus in infants.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'arterial duct'.
