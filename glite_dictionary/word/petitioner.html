petitioner

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪʃənər / (AmE), / pɪˈtɪʃənə / (BrE)
a person who formally presents a petition to a court, especially to seek redress of a grievance or to recover a right
person who submits a petition to a court
legal

Examples

The petitioner asked the court to protect her intellectual property rights.
The Supreme Court granted the petitioner a hearing on the matter.
As the petitioner in the case, he was required to provide evidence supporting his claim.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respondent - person who responds to a petition, especially in court
  • Synonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in court

Etymology

From petition + -er, influenced by Latin petere (to seek).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪʃənər / (AmE), / pəˈtɪʃənə / (BrE)
A person who makes a formal or humble request for something, especially in a legal or official context, or a person who prays earnestly for something.
a person who formally requests or prays for something

Examples

The petitioner knelt before the king, hoping for mercy and a pardon.
As a petitioner, she addressed the council with a heartfelt plea for justice.
During the ceremony, each petitioner offered prayers and requests for a prosperous harvest.

Synset Examples

a suppliant for her favors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppliant - a person making a humble plea to someone in power or authority
  • Antonym: respondent - a person who responds to a petition, especially in a legal case (antonym)

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman *peticioner*, from Old French *petition*, from Latin *petitio* (request) + English agent suffix -er.
