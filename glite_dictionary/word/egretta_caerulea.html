Egretta caerulea

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛtə siːəˈruːliə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡrɛtə siːəˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
A small bluish-grey heron native to the Americas, commonly known as the little blue heron.
species of small bluish-grey heron

Examples

The Egretta caerulea can often be seen wading in shallow wetlands across the southern United States.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a rare juvenile Egretta caerulea among the reeds.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of Egretta caerulea to understand their habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little blue heron - common name for Egretta caerulea

Etymology

From Neo-Latin. 'Egretta' is a diminutive of French 'aigrette' (egret), 'caerulea' from Latin for 'blue'.
