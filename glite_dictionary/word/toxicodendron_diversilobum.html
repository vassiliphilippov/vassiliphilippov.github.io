Toxicodendron diversilobum

1. NOUN

/ tɑksɪkoʊˈdɛndrən daɪvɚˈsaɪləbəm / (AmE), / tɒksɪkəʊˈdɛndrən dɪˌvɜːsɪˈləʊbəm / (BrE)
A poisonous shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, which causes an itchy rash when touched.
poisonous shrub causing skin rash
medical

Examples

Many hikers develop a rash after brushing against Toxicodendron diversilobum on California’s trails.
Toxicodendron diversilobum can be difficult to identify when it loses its leaves in winter.
Wearing long sleeves can help prevent contact with Toxicodendron diversilobum during forest work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison oak - common name for Toxicodendron diversilobum

Etymology

From Greek 'toxikos' (poisonous), 'dendron' (tree), and Latin 'diversilobum' (different lobes).
