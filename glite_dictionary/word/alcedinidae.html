Alcedinidae

1. NOUN

/ ælˌsɛdɪˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ælˌsɛdɪˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, small to medium-sized birds known as kingfishers, characterized by large heads, long pointed bills, and short legs.
family of birds known as kingfishers

Examples

The Alcedinidae are found in various regions across the world, often near water bodies.
Researchers observed members of the Alcedinidae family nesting along the riverbank.
Many birdwatchers are drawn to the vivid plumage of Alcedinidae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird with a large head and long bill, known for catching fish

Etymology

From Latin 'Alcedo' (kingfisher) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
