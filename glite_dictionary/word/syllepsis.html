syllepsis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɛpsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which a word is used to govern two or more other words, though agreeing in form only with one, often producing a rhetorical effect.
figure of speech: one word governs multiple others

Examples

The poet used syllepsis in the line, 'He lost his coat and his temper.'
In the sentence 'She broke his car and his heart,' syllepsis creates a clever play on words.
Writers often employ syllepsis to add wit and complexity to their language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeugma - use of a word to modify or govern two or more words, often in different senses

Etymology

From Greek syllepsis (a taking together), from syllambanein (to take together).
