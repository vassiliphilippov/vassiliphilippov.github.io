poeticism

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or expression characteristic of poetry, especially one used for a poetic effect or style
poetic word or expression

Examples

The poet's use of archaic language is a deliberate poeticism to evoke an earlier era.
Some readers find excessive poeticisms distracting in modern poetry.
The word 'thy' is now mostly encountered as a poeticism in literature.

Synset Examples

Just as ‘hath’ is a poeticism in Keats and an unmarked form for Shakespeare, ‘sogebided’ may have been a poeticism to Alfred and an unmarked form for Qedmon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaism - a word or expression that is no longer in common use

Etymology

Formed from 'poetic' + '-ism', indicating a characteristic or practice.
