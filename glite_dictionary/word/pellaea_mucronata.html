Pellaea mucronata

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliːə mjuː.kroʊˈnɑː.tə / (AmE), / pɛˈliːə mjuː.krəˈnɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of fern native to California and Baja California, noted for its purple-brown leafstalks and typically found growing on cliffs.
a cliff brake fern species of California and Baja California

Examples

The steep canyon wall was covered in patches of Pellaea mucronata growing between the rocks.
Botanists surveyed populations of Pellaea mucronata in several protected nature reserves.
The distinctive leafstalks of Pellaea mucronata make it easy to identify among other ferns.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name *Pellaea* from Greek *pelos* (dark), species name *mucronata* meaning 'pointed' in Latin, referring to the shape of the leaf tips.
