rabid

1. ADJ

/ ˈræbɪd / (AmE), / ˈræbɪd / (BrE)
affected by or relating to rabies, a serious viral disease transmitted by the bite of infected animals.
affected by rabies

Examples

A rabid dog was captured and tested by animal control officials.
The veterinarian wore protective gloves when treating the rabid fox.
After being bitten by a rabid raccoon, she immediately sought medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabies - a viral disease causing inflammation of the brain in mammals

Etymology

From Latin 'rabidus' (raving, mad), from 'rabere' (to rave).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræbɪd / (AmE), / ˈræbɪd / (BrE)
having or showing extreme, unreasonable enthusiasm or support for a particular idea, person, or activity
extremely enthusiastic or fanatical

Examples

During the debate, he was a rabid supporter of the new policy.
The team's rabid fans cheered wildly after every goal.
Her rabid enthusiasm for the band surprised everyone.

Synset Examples

rabid isolationist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatical - having very strong beliefs or feelings about something, often extreme
  • Synonym: zealous - showing great energy or enthusiasm for a cause or objective
  • Antonym: moderate - not extreme or excessive; reasonable

Etymology

From Latin rabidus (raging, mad), related to rabere (to rave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01731073-s
Synset POS: s
