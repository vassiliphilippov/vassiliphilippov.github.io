namby-pamby

1. ADJ

/ ˌnæmbiˈpæmbi / (AmE), / ˌnambiˈpambi / (BrE)
lacking courage, strength, or conviction; weak and ineffective.
weak or lacking in courage

Examples

She dismissed his namby-pamby attitude and urged him to take action.
The critics called the film's plot too namby-pamby for their taste.
Don't be so namby-pamby; stand up for yourself when you know you're right.

Synset Examples

he was a yellow gutless worm
a spineless craven fellow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spineless - lacking courage or determination
  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking bravery; easily frightened
  • Antonym: courageous - having or showing bravery

Etymology

Said to be from mockery of poet Ambrose Phillips’ name (early 18th century), who wrote sentimental verse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnæmbi ˌpæmbi / (AmE), / ˈnambi ˌpambi / (BrE)
a person who is weak, feeble, or excessively sentimental
weak or sentimental person

Examples

No one respected him because they thought he was just a namby-pamby who couldn't make tough decisions.
She refused to be a namby-pamby and stood her ground during the debate.
The coach warned the players that there was no room for namby-pamby attitudes on the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakling - a weak or feeble person
  • Synonym: sentimentalist - someone who is excessively sentimental
  • Antonym: tough guy - a person who is strong or unyielding

Etymology

From the nickname 'Namby-Pamby' for English poet Ambrose Philips, known for his sentimental verse (early 18th century).
