coriaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔriˈeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a texture that is tough but flexible, like leather; leathery.
tough and flexible, like leather

Examples

The plant's coriaceous leaves help it conserve water in dry environments.
Some tropical trees produce fruit with a thick, coriaceous rind that protects the seeds.
The old book's pages had become coriaceous with age and handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leathery - having a tough, flexible texture like leather

Etymology

From Latin coriaceus (of leather), from corium (leather).
