Marcus Junius Brutus

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrkəs ˈdʒuːniəs ˈbruːtəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ˈdʒuːnɪəs ˈbruːtəs / (BrE)
(c. 85–42 BC) A Roman statesman and one of Julius Caesar's assassins, noted for his role in the conspiracy with Cassius to kill Caesar.
Roman assassin of Julius Caesar

Examples

Many historians debate the motivations of Marcus Junius Brutus in joining the plot against Caesar.
Shakespeare's play portrays Marcus Junius Brutus as a conflicted and honorable figure.
The actions of Marcus Junius Brutus dramatically changed the course of Roman history.

Etymology

Latin personal name Marcus Junius Brutus.
