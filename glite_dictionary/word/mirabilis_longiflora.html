Mirabilis longiflora

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌlɒŋɡɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌlɒŋɡɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A leafy wildflower native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, having fragrant, slender, white or pale pink trumpet-shaped flowers.
wildflower of southwestern US and northern Mexico

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of blooming Mirabilis longiflora in the evening.
Mirabilis longiflora thrives in arid regions and adds beauty to desert landscapes.
Botanists documented the rare white variety of Mirabilis longiflora found in northern Mexico.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mirabilis' meaning 'wonderful', and 'longiflora' meaning 'long-flowered'.
