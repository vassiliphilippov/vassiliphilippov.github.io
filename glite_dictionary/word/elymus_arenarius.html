Elymus arenarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɪməs ˌɛrəˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪməs ˌaːrəˈnɑːrɪəs / (BrE)
a grass native to sandy coastal areas, often planted to stabilize dunes and prevent soil erosion
a dune grass used to stabilize coastal sand

Examples

Elymus arenarius is commonly planted on beaches to help stabilize shifting sands.
Many coastal restoration projects use Elymus arenarius for dune reinforcement.
The roots of Elymus arenarius are effective at preventing erosion along sea coasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand ryegrass - another name for Elymus arenarius, a dune grass

Etymology

From Latin: Elymus, a genus of grasses; arenarius, meaning 'growing in sand.'
