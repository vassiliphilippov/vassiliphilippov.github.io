Fructidor

1. NOUN

/ fruːk.tɪˌdɔr / (AmE), / fruːk.tɪˌdɔː / (BrE)
The twelfth month of the French Revolutionary calendar, corresponding roughly to August and September and known as the month of fruit.
twelfth month of the French Revolutionary calendar

Examples

The peace treaty was signed on the 18th of Fructidor, year VI.
Harvest celebrations took place during Fructidor, marking the abundance of fruit.
In historical documents, dates are often given as 'the 5th Fructidor' instead of using the Gregorian calendar.

Etymology

From French 'Fructidor', based on Latin 'fructus' (fruit) and the suffix '-dor' (from Greek 'dōron', meaning gift), created during the French Revolution.
