Romneya coulteri

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːm.ni.ə ˈkoʊl.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.nɪ.ə ˈkɒl.tə.ri / (BrE)
A tall branching subshrub native to California and Mexico, known for its silvery-blue foliage and large, fragrant white flowers; also called Matilija poppy.
a Californian/Mexican subshrub; Matilija poppy

Examples

The striking flowers of the Romneya coulteri made it the highlight of the garden tour.
Botanists study Romneya coulteri for its drought resistance and ornamental value.
You can recognize Romneya coulteri by its delicate white petals and unusual foliage.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Thomas Coulter, an Irish botanist, and the genus Romneya, named for Irish astronomer John Thomas Romney Robinson.
