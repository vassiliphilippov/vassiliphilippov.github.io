viscountcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊntsi / (AmE), / ˈvɪˌkaʊntsi / (BrE)
the rank, office, or jurisdiction of a viscount
title or rank of a viscount

Examples

After his father's death, he inherited the viscountcy and became a member of the House of Lords.
The viscountcy of Oxford was created in the 17th century.
He was proud of his family's long association with the viscountcy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscount - a nobleman ranking above a baron and below an earl or count

Etymology

Formed from 'viscount' + '-cy', modeled after titles like 'countcy' and 'duchy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊntsi / (AmE), / ˈvɪsˌkaʊntsi / (BrE)
the rank, title, or dignity held by a viscount or viscountess
rank or title of a viscount

Examples

After decades of service, he was finally elevated to the viscountcy by the monarch.
The viscountcy had been in her family for over two centuries.
Upon his father's death, James inherited the viscountcy and all associated estates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: barony - the rank or dignity of a baron
  • Degree Higher: earldom - the rank or dignity of an earl
  • Synonym: viscount - a British nobleman ranking below an earl and above a baron

Etymology

From viscount + -cy, denoting an office or condition.
