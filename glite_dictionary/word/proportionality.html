proportionality

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpɔr.ʃəˈnæl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / prəˌpɔː.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of achieving a harmonious relationship among the parts of something, so that they fit together well as a whole
harmonious arrangement of parts

Examples

The proportionality of the columns and arches contributed to the elegance of the cathedral.
Artists often strive for proportionality to create visually appealing compositions.
The architect admired the building's proportionality, noting how every part seemed to belong to the whole.

Synset Examples

in all perfectly beautiful objects there is found the opposition of one part to another and a reciprocal balance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetry - the quality of being made up of exactly similar parts facing each other
  • Synonym: balance - a state in which different things occur in equal or proper amounts

Etymology

From Late Latin proportionalitas (symmetry, due relation to parts).

2. NOUN

/ prəˌpɔrʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / prəˌpɔːʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of two quantities having a constant ratio
constant ratio between quantities

Examples

In mathematics, proportionality is fundamental to understanding similar triangles.
The scientist examined the proportionality between the two chemical substances.
Direct proportionality means that as one variable increases, the other does as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportion - the relationship of one part to another or to the whole

Etymology

From Latin 'proportionalis' (in due proportion) + English suffix '-ity'.
