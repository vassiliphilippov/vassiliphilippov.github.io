Acacia cambagei

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə kæmˈbædʒiː / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə kæmˈbædʒiː / (BrE)
A scrubby Australian acacia tree known for its extremely foul-smelling blossoms.
Australian acacia tree with foul-smelling blossoms

Examples

The blossoms of Acacia cambagei are notorious for their strong, unpleasant odor.
Ecologists studied how Acacia cambagei thrives in the arid Australian outback.
Local guides warned hikers about the pungent scent of blooming Acacia cambagei nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acacia - a genus of shrubs and trees in the pea family

Etymology

The genus name 'Acacia' is from Greek 'akakia'; 'cambagei' honors Australian botanist R.H. Cambage.
