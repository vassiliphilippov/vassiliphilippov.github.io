streaming

1. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the continuous movement of cytoplasm within a living cell, distributing nutrients and organelles
movement of cytoplasm in a cell
medical

Examples

Cytoplasmic streaming helps distribute materials efficiently inside plant cells.
The microscope allowed us to observe streaming in the cell's cytoplasm.
Disruption of streaming can interfere with cellular functions.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11464025-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈstriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of audio, video, or data) transmitted or received over the internet or a network in a continuous flow, allowing access as earlier parts are still being sent or processed.
continuous transmission of audio, video, or data

Examples

Many users prefer streaming music rather than downloading files to their devices.
Educational institutions now offer streaming lectures for remote students.
With streaming video technology, viewers can start watching a movie before it has fully downloaded.

Synset Examples

streaming audio
streaming video recording

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to download - transfer (data or programs) from a server to a local device

Etymology

From the verb 'stream' (to flow continuously), with specialized use in computing from the late 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01959729-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈstriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) giving off a fluid, such as tears, sweat, or mucus, in large or continuous amounts.
exuding fluid in large amounts (body)

Examples

She wiped her streaming eyes with a tissue after the sad movie.
After running in the heat, his streaming face was covered with sweat.
The child came in from the cold, his nose streaming with mucus.

Synset Examples

his streaming face
her streaming eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping - shedding tears; crying
  • Antonym: dry - not exuding fluid; lacking moisture

Etymology

Present participle of 'stream', from Old English 'strēam' (a river, current).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03161489-a
Synset POS: a
