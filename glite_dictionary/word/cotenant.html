cotenant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌtɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌtɛnənt / (BrE)
one of two or more people who jointly hold tenancy or title to the same property
person who jointly holds tenancy or title
legal

Examples

Each cotenant has the right to occupy the entire property.
If one cotenant wants to sell their share, they must notify the others.
Disputes sometimes arise when a cotenant disagrees about property expenses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord

Etymology

From co- (together) + tenant, modeled after legal terms for shared ownership.
