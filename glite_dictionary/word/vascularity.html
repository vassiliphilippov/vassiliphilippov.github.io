vascularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæskjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌvæskjʊˈlærɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which a tissue, organ, or part of the body has blood vessels or is supplied with blood vessels
degree of blood vessel presence in tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted the increased vascularity of the tumor during the procedure.
Athletes may train to improve the vascularity of their muscles for better performance.
The pathologist commented on the rich vascularity seen in the tissue sample.

Synset Examples

a prominent vascularity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vascular - relating to blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin vasculum (small vessel) + -ity.
