rosefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A North Atlantic rockfish, typically reddish in color, found in deep waters and valued as food.
a North Atlantic rockfish

Examples

Fishermen in the region often catch rosefish for local markets.
The deep red color of the rosefish makes it easy to identify.
Many restaurants serve rosefish as a delicacy due to its firm, mild-flavored flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean perch - another name for rosefish, especially Sebastes marinus

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, reddish fish found along the northern Atlantic coasts of North America and Europe
large reddish Atlantic fish

Examples

The chef prepared the rosefish with a lemon butter sauce for the evening's special.
Fishermen often catch rosefish in the deep waters off the Norwegian coast.
The rosefish is prized for both its delicate flavor and vibrant coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redfish - another name for rosefish, used regionally
  • Synonym: ocean perch - alternative common name for rosefish
