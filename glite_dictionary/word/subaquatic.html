subaquatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.əˈkwæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.əˈkwæt.ɪk / (BrE)
growing, living, or located under water; existing or occurring beneath the surface of water.
existing or occurring under water

Examples

The diver discovered several subaquatic plants thriving near the lake bed.
Researchers use special cameras to study subaquatic habitats in the ocean.
The pond's subaquatic environment creates a haven for many species of fish.

Synset Examples

viewing subaqueous fauna from a glass-bottomed boat
submerged leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subaqueous - underwater; existing, formed, or taking place under water

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'aquaticus' (relating to water).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.əˈkwæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.əˈkwæt.ɪk / (BrE)
living or growing partly on land and partly in water; partially aquatic.
partially aquatic; living or growing partly on land and in water

Examples

The riverbank supports a diverse subaquatic ecosystem, with plants thriving both in water and on land.
Certain frogs are known for their subaquatic lifestyle, living near ponds and frequently moving between water and shore.
Researchers examined the subaquatic vegetation along the wetland's edge to understand its adaptation to fluctuating water levels.

Synset Examples

a marginal subaquatic flora

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aquatic - living or growing in water

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'aquaticus' (relating to water).

Synset POS: s
