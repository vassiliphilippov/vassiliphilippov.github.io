Phleum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliəm / (AmE), / ˈfliːəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses native to temperate regions, commonly known as timothy grasses.
genus of temperate grasses

Examples

The hay was mostly composed of Phleum and clover.
Farmers often sow Phleum for its high-quality forage.
Many wild meadows feature abundant Phleum among other grasses.

Etymology

From Greek *phleos* (a kind of marsh plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfliːəm / (AmE), / ˈfliːəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses native to temperate regions, including the well-known timothy grass.
a genus of temperate-region grasses

Examples

Several species in the Phleum genus are important for animal fodder.
Researchers examined the growth patterns of Phleum in different climates.
Timothy grass, or Phleum pratense, is widely cultivated in North America.

Etymology

From Latin *phleum*, from Greek *phleos* (a kind of marsh plant).
