Johnny

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑni / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni / (BrE)
A nickname for a Confederate soldier, especially as used by Union troops during the American Civil War.
Confederate soldier (nickname, U.S. Civil War)

Examples

During the Civil War, Union soldiers often referred to their Southern adversaries as Johnny or 'Johnny Reb'.
A Johnny in his greyback uniform marched bravely across the battlefield.
Many historians discuss how the term Johnny reflected the culture and camaraderie of Confederate troops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Yankee - A nickname for Union soldiers, especially as used by Confederates in the American Civil War

Etymology

Diminutive form of the name John; used as a general nickname for Confederate soldiers in the U.S. Civil War.
