idoliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who worships idols or treats someone or something with excessive admiration
a person who worships idols
british

Examples

In ancient times, an idoliser might have created statues to honor deities.
The priest condemned the idolisers for their devotion to false gods.
While some saw him as a revolutionary, his followers became idolisers of his every word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolater - a person who worships idols
  • Synonym: idolizer - American spelling of 'idoliser'

Etymology

From idol + -iser, from Latin idolum (image, idol) and English -ise/-ize suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəlaɪzə / (BrE)
A person who admires or worships someone intensely, often without critical judgment.
someone who worships or adores another

Examples

As a devoted idoliser of classic rock bands, she collected rare albums and memorabilia.
He was accused of being an idoliser, unable to see any flaws in his mentor.
The novelist described himself as an idoliser of ancient philosophers and their ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolator - a person who worships idols or excessively admires someone or something

Etymology

From verb 'idolize' + agent suffix '-er'.
