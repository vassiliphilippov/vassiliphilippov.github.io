Quetzalcoatl

1. PROPN

/ kɛtsɑlˈkoʊɑtəl / (AmE), / kɛtsælˈkəʊətəl / (BrE)
An Aztec deity represented as a plumed serpent, associated with wind, air, and learning.
Aztec deity, plumed serpent

Examples

Ancient murals depict Quetzalcoatl as a feathered serpent flying across the sky.
Historians debate the significance of Quetzalcoatl in Mesoamerican culture.
Some legends claim that Quetzalcoatl brought knowledge and agriculture to the Aztec people.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *Quetzalcōātl*, meaning 'feathered serpent' (*quetzalli* 'feather' + *coatl* 'serpent').
