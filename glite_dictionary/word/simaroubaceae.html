Simaroubaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɪməˌruːbəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / sɪməˌruːbəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs with bitter bark, producing dry, usually one-seeded winged fruits.
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Simaroubaceae family includes some species used for traditional medicines.
Many Simaroubaceae trees have very bitter bark, which deters herbivores.
Characteristic one-seeded winged fruits make Simaroubaceae easily recognizable for botanists.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Simarouba, a type of tree in this family, plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae for families.
