unvaned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈveɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪnd / (BrE)
(of an arrow) not equipped with feathers or vanes.
arrow without feathers/vanes

Examples

The archer accidentally shot an unvaned arrow, which flew off course.
Old practice arrows were often left unvaned to reduce their range.
Because it was unvaned, the arrow lacked stability in flight.

Synset Examples

shot an unfledged arrow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vaned - fitted with vanes or feathers (of an arrow)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'vaned' (having vanes or feathers).
