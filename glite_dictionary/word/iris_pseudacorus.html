Iris pseudacorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs ˌsuːdəˈkɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪs ˌsjuːdəˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of iris with bright yellow flowers, native to Europe and North Africa and widely naturalized elsewhere, often cultivated in gardens and wetlands.
yellow-flowered iris species

Examples

The pond was lined with Iris pseudacorus blooming in vibrant yellow.
Gardeners often plant Iris pseudacorus to add color near water features.
Iris pseudacorus has become invasive in certain wetlands outside its native range.

Etymology

Latin; 'Iris' is the genus; 'pseudacorus' means 'false acorus', named for its resemblance to the plant Acorus calamus.
