Amygdalus communis amara

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɪɡˈdɑːləs kəˈmjuːnɪs əˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / əˌmɪɡˈdɑːləs kəˈmjuːnɪs əˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
A variety of almond tree with white blossoms, producing poisonous nuts that yield an oil used for flavoring and medicinal purposes.
bitter almond tree; produces poisonous nuts

Examples

The oil extracted from Amygdalus communis amara is used in some traditional medicines.
Consuming nuts from Amygdalus communis amara without proper processing can be dangerous.
Gardeners should differentiate Amygdalus communis amara from the sweet almond tree due to its toxic seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter almond - edible nut from Amygdalus communis amara, containing toxic compounds

Etymology

From Latin: Amygdalus (almond), communis (common), amara (bitter).
