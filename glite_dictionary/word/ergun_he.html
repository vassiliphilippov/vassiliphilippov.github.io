Ergun He

1. PROPN

/ ˈɜr.gʊn hɛ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.gʊn heɪ / (BrE)
a river in eastern Asia that rises in China, flows northeast along the border between China and Russia, and joins the Amur River as a tributary.
river on China–Russia border, tributary of Amur

Examples

The Ergun He forms part of the natural boundary between China and Russia.
Wildlife along the Ergun He is diverse, attracting many ecologists and birdwatchers.
The waters of the Ergun He eventually flow into the Amur River.

Etymology

From Chinese 额尔古纳河 (É'ěrgǔnà Hé), with 'He' meaning 'river'.
