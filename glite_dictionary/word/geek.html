geek

1. NOUN

/ ɡik / (AmE), / ɡiːk / (BrE)
(historical) A carnival performer who performs bizarre or repulsive acts, such as biting the heads off live animals.
carnival performer doing shocking acts
archaic

Examples

The old circus had a geek whose only act was biting the heads off chickens.
She was horrified at the spectacle performed by the carnival geek.
In early twentieth-century sideshows, a geek was expected to shock and disgust the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak - person in a carnival act with unusual physical characteristics

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from English dialect 'geck' meaning 'fool' or 'simpleton', or Low German 'gek' (fool, freak).

2. NOUN

/ ɡik / (AmE), / ɡiːk / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as socially awkward or eccentric, often having unusual interests or hobbies
socially awkward or eccentric person
informal

Examples

He's always been a bit of a geek, obsessed with collecting rare insects.
In high school, she was labeled a geek because of her passion for computers.
At the convention, geeks from all over the country gathered to share their interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerd - a person who is very interested in technical subjects and is socially awkward

Etymology

Originally US slang, possibly from Scots keek (‘look, peep’) or from circus slang for a sideshow performer.
