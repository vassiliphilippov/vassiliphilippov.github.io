to redispose

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.dɪsˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪsˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to dispose or arrange again, especially in a different way
arrange or dispose again

Examples

After the renovation, the curator decided to redispose the paintings across different galleries.
We had to redispose the inventory after the shipment was delayed.
The florist will redispose the flowers to improve the arrangement for the evening event.

Synset Examples

The goods were redisposed at a great loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispose - to place or arrange in a particular way

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + dispose (from Latin disponere)
