Bel-Merodach

1. PROPN

/ bɛl məˈroʊˌdæk / (AmE), / bɛl məˈrəʊˌdæk / (BrE)
the chief god of Babylonian mythology, often regarded as the principal deity of Babylon; his consort was Sarpanitu.
chief deity of Babylonian mythology

Examples

Ancient texts frequently refer to Bel-Merodach as the most powerful deity in Babylon.
The temple dedicated to Bel-Merodach was a center of religious activity.
Mythological accounts describe Bel-Merodach as the defender of the city against chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marduk - Babylonian god; the same deity as Bel-Merodach

Etymology

From Akkadian 'Bēl' (lord) and 'Marduk', the principal Babylonian god.
