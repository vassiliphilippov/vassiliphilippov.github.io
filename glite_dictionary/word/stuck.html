stuck

1. ADJ

/ stʌk / (AmE), / stʌk / (BrE)
Unable to understand, solve, or proceed because of confusion or difficulty; baffled.
unable to solve or proceed; baffled

Examples

I'm completely stuck on this math problem and don't know what to do next.
She tried several approaches to fix the bug, but was still stuck.
He sat there stuck, unsure of how to answer the question.

Synset Examples

this problem has me completely stuck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baffled - confused or puzzled
  • Synonym: puzzled - unable to understand or solve something
  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or understand

Etymology

Past participle of 'stick', meaning to be fixed in one place or unable to move.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01771470-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ stʌk / (AmE), / stʌk / (BrE)
unable to move from a particular position or place, typically because something is holding or trapping one there.
unable to move; caught or trapped

Examples

Her car got stuck in the snow and she couldn't move it.
I'm stuck in traffic, so I'll be late to the meeting.
He reached for the jar, but the lid was stuck tight.

Synset Examples

stuck in the mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapped - unable to escape from a place or situation
  • Antonym: free - able to move or go as one wishes; not stuck or restrained

Etymology

Past participle of 'stick,' from Old English 'stician' (to pierce, remain fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00162330-a
Synset POS: a
