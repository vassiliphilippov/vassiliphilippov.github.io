to distend

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛnd / (BrE)
to become wider or expand by stretching, usually as a result of pressure from within
become wider by stretching

Examples

The balloon began to distend as air was pumped into it.
After the meal, her stomach appeared to distend slightly.
Medical conditions can cause veins to distend under the skin.

Synset Examples

His pupils were dilated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expand - to become or make larger or more extensive
  • Antonym: contract - to become smaller or narrower

Etymology

From Latin distendere, from dis- (apart) + tendere (to stretch).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstɛnd / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to swell or expand from or as if from internal pressure
swell or expand due to pressure

Examples

The patient's abdomen began to distend noticeably as the condition worsened.
Hot air can cause a balloon to distend rapidly.
When overfed, the stomach will distend to accommodate excess food.

Synset Examples

The distended bellies of the starving cows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to fill with air or gas and make something swell
  • Synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size, typically due to internal pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'distendere', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'tendere' (to stretch).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈstɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛnd / (BrE)
to cause (something) to swell or expand by stretching from inside, often as a result of internal pressure
to expand or swell from within

Examples

The surgeon noted that the patient's abdomen was distended due to the buildup of fluid.
Overeating can sometimes distend the stomach and cause discomfort.
The balloon distended rapidly as it filled with air.

Synset Examples

The gas distended the animal's body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expand - to become or make larger in size, volume, or quantity
  • Antonym: compress - to press or squeeze something so that it becomes smaller or more compact

Etymology

From Latin distendere, from dis- (apart) + tendere (to stretch).
