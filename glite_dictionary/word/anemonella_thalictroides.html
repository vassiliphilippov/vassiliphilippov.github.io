Anemonella thalictroides

1. NOUN

/ əˌnɛməˈnɛlə θəˌlɪktrɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / əˌnɛmɒˈnɛlə θəˌlɪktrɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A woodland flower native to eastern North America, with delicate, cup-shaped flowers resembling anemones.
woodland flower native to eastern North America

Examples

The delicate white flowers of Anemonella thalictroides appear in early spring in shaded woodlands.
Gardeners often admire Anemonella thalictroides for its dainty blooms and attractive foliage.
We spotted a patch of Anemonella thalictroides growing along the hiking trail last April.

Etymology

From Greek 'anemōnē' (anemone) and 'thaliktroides' (resembling Thalictrum).
