doxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑksi / (AmE), / ˈdɒksi / (BrE)
an archaic term for a woman who lives with or is kept by a man, especially one not his wife
archaic: woman who cohabits with a man
archaic

Examples

The lord was often seen dining with his doxy at the inn.
In many old plays, a doxy is portrayed as a scheming character.
He kept a doxy in secret, away from polite society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistress - a woman who has a continuing sexual relationship with a man who is married to someone else

Etymology

From early modern English, possibly from obsolete Dutch *docke* (doll, slut).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑksi / (AmE), / ˈdɒksi / (BrE)
an archaic term for a woman who is a companion or mistress, often used pejoratively
archaic term for mistress or female companion
archaic

Examples

In the old novels, the villain often had a doxy by his side.
He was seen at the tavern, drinking with his doxy late into the night.
The magistrate disapproved of any association with a known doxy.

Synset Examples

The doxy was often seen accompanying the wealthy man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistress - a woman in an extramarital relationship with a man

Etymology

Probably from obsolete Dutch docke, dok ('doll, little girl').
