canistel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪstɛl / (AmE), / ˈkænɪstɛl / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with ovoid shape, orange-yellow skin and flesh, and a sweet, mealy texture, native to Florida and the West Indies.
tropical orange-yellow sweet fruit

Examples

The canistel is sometimes called 'eggfruit' because of its smooth, yellow flesh.
Baskets of ripe canistels were sold at the farmers’ market in Miami.
She added chopped canistel to her smoothie for a boost of sweetness and color.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkænɪˈstɛl / (AmE), / ˌkanɪˈstɛl / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen tree of Florida and the West Indies (Pouteria campechiana), cultivated for its edible yellow fruit resembling an eggfruit
tropical tree with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The canistel tree produces a fruit often called 'eggfruit' because of its texture.
Locals in the Caribbean enjoy the ripe canistel as a dessert or blended into drinks.
She planted a canistel in her backyard hoping to harvest fruit in two years.
