Virgo

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A person born between August 23 and September 22, when the sun is in the Virgo astrological sign.
person born under Virgo sign

Examples

My sister is a Virgo and believes in astrology.
As a Virgo, she is said to be practical and analytical.
Many Virgos are thought to be detail-oriented and organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pisces - person born under the Pisces sign
  • Synonym: Leo - person born under the Leo sign

Etymology

From Latin 'Virgo', meaning 'virgin'; used as the name of a zodiac sign.

2. PROPN

/ ˈvɝːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a large constellation of the zodiac lying on the celestial equator, situated between Leo and Libra
constellation of the zodiac between Leo and Libra

Examples

The Virgo constellation is easily visible in the Northern Hemisphere during spring months.
Scientists have discovered dozens of galaxies in the Virgo Cluster.
Ancient astronomers named Virgo after the Latin word for 'virgin', symbolizing purity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Leo - constellation of the zodiac, lies to the west of Virgo
  • Degree Higher: Libra - constellation of the zodiac, lies to the east of Virgo

Etymology

From Latin 'Virgo' meaning 'virgin'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːɡəʊ / (BrE)
the sixth sign of the zodiac, covering those born between approximately August 23 and September 22
zodiac sign from August 23 to September 22

Examples

She was born under the sign of Virgo, which means her birthday falls in early September.
Many people believe that Virgo individuals are practical and attentive to detail.
The horoscope for Virgo predicts a successful month ahead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Libra - the seventh sign of the zodiac, following Virgo
  • Degree Lower: Leo - the fifth sign of the zodiac, preceding Virgo

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' meaning 'virgin', the symbolic representation of the sign.
