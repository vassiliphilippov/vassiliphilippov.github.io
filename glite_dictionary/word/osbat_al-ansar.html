Osbat al-Ansar

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈbɑt æl ɑnˈsɑr / (AmE), / ɑsˈbɑːt æl ænˈsɑː / (BrE)
A Palestinian Sunni extremist group, active mainly in Lebanon since the early 1990s, advocating Salafism and involved in violent activities including murders and bombings; allegedly seeks to overthrow the Lebanese government and control Palestinian refugee camps.
Palestinian Sunni extremist group in Lebanon

Examples

The Lebanese authorities increased their surveillance of Osbat al-Ansar after a series of attacks in the region.
Intelligence reports suggested that Osbat al-Ansar maintained ties with international terrorist organizations.
Osbat al-Ansar was accused of attempting to destabilize the government by inciting violence in refugee camps.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Usbat al-Ansar' meaning 'League of Supporters'.
