Loch Linnhe

1. PROPN

/ lɑk ˈlɪni / (AmE), / lɒx ˈlɪni / (BrE)
an inlet of the Atlantic Ocean located on the western coast of Scotland, known for its scenic beauty and maritime significance
inlet of the Atlantic Ocean in western Scotland
british

Examples

Many tourists come to photograph the breathtaking sunsets over Loch Linnhe.
The small village lies on the shores of Loch Linnhe in western Scotland.
Fishermen often set out early in the morning from the harbors along Loch Linnhe.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'loch' meaning 'lake' or 'sea inlet', and 'Linnhe', of uncertain origin.
