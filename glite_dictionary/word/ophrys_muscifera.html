Ophrys muscifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfrɪs ˌmʌsəˈfɛrə / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs ˌmʌsɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A European orchid species whose flowers resemble flies, commonly known as the fly orchid.
a European orchid with fly-like flowers

Examples

The Ophrys muscifera attracts insects because its flowers closely mimic the appearance of flies.
In southern England, you can sometimes spot the rare Ophrys muscifera in limestone meadows.
Scientists study the pollination strategy of Ophrys muscifera to understand plant-insect mimicry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a flowering plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

From Latin; 'Ophrys' is the orchid genus, 'muscifera' means 'fly-bearing.'
