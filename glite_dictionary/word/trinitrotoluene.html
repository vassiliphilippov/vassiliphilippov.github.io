trinitrotoluene

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪˌnaɪtroʊˈtɑljuˌin / (AmE), / ˌtrɪ.nɪ.trəʊˈtɒl.juː.iːn / (BrE)
a yellow, crystalline, flammable compound (C7H5N3O6) used chiefly as an explosive (TNT) and as a reagent in chemical analysis
an explosive chemical compound (TNT)

Examples

The demolition team used trinitrotoluene to bring down the old building safely.
Due to its stability, trinitrotoluene is widely used in military applications.
Chemists handle trinitrotoluene with extreme care because of its toxicity and explosive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TNT - abbreviation for trinitrotoluene, a powerful explosive

Etymology

From Latin roots: tri- (three), nitro- (nitro group), toluene (an aromatic hydrocarbon from tolu, a resin).
