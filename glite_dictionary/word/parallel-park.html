to parallel-park

1. VERB

/ ˌpærəˌlɛlˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ˌpærəlɛlˈpɑːk / (BrE)
to park a vehicle parallel to the curb, typically in a line of other parked cars
to park parallel to the curb

Examples

It took her several tries to parallel-park along the busy street.
Most city driving tests require you to parallel-park between two cars.
He learned how to parallel-park before getting his driver's license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: park - to bring a vehicle to a stop and leave it temporarily

Etymology

Formed from 'parallel' (from Greek 'parallēlos', meaning side by side) + 'park'.
