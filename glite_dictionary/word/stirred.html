stirred

1. ADJ

/ stɝd / (AmE), / stɜːd / (BrE)
(of a person) emotionally moved or provoked to feel or express strong emotion.
moved or emotionally affected

Examples

She was deeply stirred by the music's haunting melody.
His words left everyone in the audience visibly stirred.
I always feel stirred when I read stories of bravery.

Synset Examples

too moved to speak
very touched by the stranger's kindness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moved - having strong feelings stirred, especially emotionally affected
  • Synonym: touched - emotionally affected, especially with sympathy or gratitude
  • Antonym: unmoved - not affected emotionally; not influenced by emotion

Etymology

Past participle of 'stir', from Old English 'styrian' (to move, agitate).

2. ADJ

/ stɝd / (AmE), / stɜːd / (BrE)
(of a person or their feelings) emotionally aroused, especially in response to something moving or exciting.
emotionally aroused; moved

Examples

The audience was deeply stirred by the emotional speech.
She left the theater feeling stirred and inspired.
Everyone was stirred by the news of the rescue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moved - feeling strong emotion, especially sympathy or sadness
  • Antonym: unmoved - not affected emotionally
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00923735-s
Synset POS: s
