tetrametric

1. ADJ

/ tɛˈtræmətrɪk / (AmE), / tɛˈtræmɪtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or composed in lines of verse with four metrical feet (tetrameter)
relating to verse in tetrameter

Examples

The poem is written in tetrametric lines, giving it a distinctive rhythm.
Many English ballads use a tetrametric structure to create a lyrical effect.
The professor explained how tetrametric verse differs from pentametric verse in classical poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetrameter - a line of verse with four metrical feet

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'metron' (measure); via Late Latin 'tetrametrus'.
