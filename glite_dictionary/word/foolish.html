foolish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
lacking good sense or judgment; unwise or showing a lack of intelligence.
lacking good sense; unwise

Examples

It was foolish to leave your keys in the car overnight.
His foolish mistake cost the team the game.
She regrets her foolish decision to trust him.

Synset Examples

ridiculous anserine behavior
a dopey answer
a dopey kid
some fool idea about rewriting authors' books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - showing a lack of common sense or judgment
  • Antonym: wise - showing good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Old French folage, from fol (foolish, mad), from Latin follis (bellows, windbag, i.e. 'empty-headed').

2. ADJ

/ ˈfuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
lacking good sense or judgment; unwise or silly.
lacking good sense or judgment

Examples

It was foolish of him to forget his umbrella on a rainy day.
She realized her foolish mistake only after it was too late.
Don't make a foolish decision just because you're angry.

Synset Examples

foolish remarks
a foolish decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - showing a lack of good sense; absurd or foolish
  • Antonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From Old French fol (fool), from Latin follis (bellows, windbag, fool).
