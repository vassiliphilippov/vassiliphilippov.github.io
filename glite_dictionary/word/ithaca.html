Ithaca

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪθəkə / (AmE), / ˈɪθəkə / (BrE)
A Greek island in the Ionian Sea, west of mainland Greece, known in Homeric legend as the kingdom of Odysseus.
Greek island; kingdom of Odysseus in legend

Examples

Odysseus yearned to return to Ithaca after the Trojan War.
Tourists often visit Ithaca to explore its ancient sites and natural beauty.
In literature, Ithaca symbolizes homecoming and longing.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἰθάκη (*Ithákē*), the island's classical name.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɪθəkə / (AmE), / ˈɪθəkə / (BrE)
A city in central New York State, known for being a college town on Lake Cayuga.
city in central New York, college town

Examples

Ithaca is home to Cornell University and Ithaca College.
Many students choose to spend their summers in Ithaca to enjoy the nearby waterfalls and parks.
The vibrant downtown area is one of Ithaca's most popular attractions.

Etymology

Named after the Greek island of Ithaca, home of Odysseus in Homer’s Odyssey.
