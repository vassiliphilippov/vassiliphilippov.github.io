Cycas

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪ.kas / (BrE)
a genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs in the family Cycadaceae, native to Old World regions, characterized by pinnate leaves and stout, columnar stems covered with old leaf bases.
genus of tropical evergreen trees (family Cycadaceae)

Examples

The gardens feature several rare species of Cycas collected from Southeast Asia.
A mature Cycas can live for centuries and is prized for its unique foliage.
Botanists are studying the reproductive habits of Cycas in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'kykas', possibly a scribal error for 'koikas' (a kind of palm).
