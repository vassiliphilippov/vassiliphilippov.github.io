scramsax

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræmˌsæks / (AmE), / ˈskræmˌsaks / (BrE)
A single-edged knife or sword used by the Anglo-Saxons for fighting and hunting.
Anglo-Saxon single-edged knife or sword

Examples

Archaeologists found a well-preserved scramsax at the Anglo-Saxon burial site.
The warrior drew his scramsax to defend himself in battle.
Collectors value an authentic scramsax for its historical significance and craftsmanship.

Etymology

From Old English *scrāmasax*, from Proto-Germanic *skramasahs* (cutting knife).
