labetalol hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ləˈbeɪtəlɒl ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ləˈbiːtəlɒl ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
an antihypertensive drug that blocks alpha and beta-adrenergic receptors, used to treat high blood pressure
antihypertensive drug for high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed labetalol hydrochloride to manage her high blood pressure during pregnancy.
Some patients experience dizziness as a side effect of labetalol hydrochloride.
Intravenous labetalol hydrochloride was administered in the emergency room to quickly lower the patient's blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Normodyne - another brand name for labetalol hydrochloride
