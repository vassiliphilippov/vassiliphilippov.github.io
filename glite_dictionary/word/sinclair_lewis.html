Sinclair Lewis

1. PROPN

/ sɪnˌklɛr ˈluɪs / (AmE), / sɪŋˌklɛː ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(1885–1951) An American novelist known for his sharp satire of middle-class life, especially in his novel Main Street.
American novelist and satirist (1885–1951)

Examples

Sinclair Lewis was the first American writer to receive the Nobel Prize in Literature.
Many critics praise Sinclair Lewis for his satirical depiction of early twentieth-century America.
Her thesis focused on the impact of Sinclair Lewis on modern American fiction.
