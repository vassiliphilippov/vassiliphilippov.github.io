cagey

1. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdʒi / (BrE)
careful not to give too much information; showing shrewdness and self-interest in dealing with others
shrewd and cautious

Examples

The spokesperson was cagey about the details of the contract.
She gave a cagey response, avoiding a direct answer to the reporter's question.
Politicians are often cagey when asked about their future plans.

Synset Examples

a cagey lawyer
too clever to be sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewd - having or showing sharp powers of judgment
  • Synonym: guarded - cautious, not revealing information freely
  • Antonym: open - willing to share information, not secretive

Etymology

Probably from cage + -y, suggesting someone as wary or elusive as something in a cage.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
Very cautious, especially to avoid giving away information or being trapped; wary or shrewd.
very cautious or wary

Examples

He gave a cagey reply when asked about his future plans.
The witness remained cagey during the entire interview, avoiding any direct answers.
Investors grew cagey after the company’s recent financial scandal.

Synset Examples

a cagey avoidance of a definite answer
chary of the risks involved
a chary investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wary - careful because you think something might be dangerous or harmful
  • Synonym: chary - cautiously or suspiciously reluctant to do something
  • Antonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action

Etymology

Probably from the word 'cage' + -y, suggesting the wariness of a caged animal.
