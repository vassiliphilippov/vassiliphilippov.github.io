Pseudocolus fusiformis

1. NOUN

/ suːd.oʊˈkoʊləs fjuːsɪˈfɔrmɪs / (AmE), / sjuː.dəʊˈkəʊləs fjuːsɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
a species of stinkhorn fungus that initially appears as a small puffball before splitting open to form a stalk with tapering, arching arms that meet at a point
species of stinkhorn fungus

Examples

In the forest, we discovered a Pseudocolus fusiformis growing near a fallen log.
The unique structure of Pseudocolus fusiformis makes it easily recognizable among other fungi.
Researchers have studied Pseudocolus fusiformis to better understand the diversity of stinkhorn mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a type of fungus known for its foul odor

Etymology

From Greek pseudes (false) + Latin colus (distaff) and Latin fusiformis (spindle-shaped).
