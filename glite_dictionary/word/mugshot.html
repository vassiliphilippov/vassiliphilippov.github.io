mugshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡʃɒt / (BrE)
a photograph of a person's face taken for police records, especially after an arrest
police photograph of a person's face

Examples

The police released the mugshot of the suspect to the public.
Her mugshot was taken immediately after the arrest.
Journalists obtained the celebrity’s mugshot from the police records.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: photograph - an image made by a camera
  • Synonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, or photograph of a person

Etymology

From 'mug' (slang for face) and 'shot' (photograph), originating in American English c. 1950s.
