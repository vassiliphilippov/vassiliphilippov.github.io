Giovanni Battista Montini

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊˌvɑːni bɑːˈtiːstə mɒnˈtiːni / (AmE), / dʒəʊˌvæni bæˈtiːstə mɒnˈtiːni / (BrE)
(1897–1978) The birth name of Pope Paul VI, Italian pope from 1963 to 1978, known for modernizing the Catholic Church and easing restrictions on fasting and interfaith marriages.
Italian pope (Pope Paul VI), 1963–1978

Examples

Giovanni Battista Montini became Pope Paul VI and led the Catholic Church through significant changes.
Many historians credit Giovanni Battista Montini for his role in the Second Vatican Council.
Before his papacy, Giovanni Battista Montini served as Archbishop of Milan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope Paul VI - Papal name of Giovanni Battista Montini, pope from 1963 to 1978

Etymology

Giovanni Battista is an Italian given name meaning 'John the Baptist'; Montini is an Italian surname.
