Pasternak

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæs.tərˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tə.næk / (BrE)
(Boris) Russian writer (1890–1960), best known for his novel Doctor Zhivago, which was banned in the Soviet Union but published abroad.
Russian novelist and poet (1890–1960)

Examples

Pasternak won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1958 for his contributions to Russian literature.
Many readers around the world have been moved by Pasternak's novel, Doctor Zhivago.
Despite government censorship, the works of Pasternak reached a global audience.
