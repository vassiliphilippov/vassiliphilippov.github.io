controllership

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊlərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊləʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of a controller, especially in finance or corporate management
position or office of a controller

Examples

She was promoted to the controllership after only three years at the company.
The controllership oversees all accounting operations within the corporation.
He decided to leave the controllership to pursue opportunities in a different industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - person who manages financial accounts in a company

Etymology

From 'controller' plus the noun-forming suffix '-ship', denoting position or office.
