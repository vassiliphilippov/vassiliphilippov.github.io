Camelus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːləs / (AmE), / kəˈmiːləs / (BrE)
a genus of large, long-necked mammals that includes camels, native to Asia and Africa.
the camel genus

Examples

Scientists classify the dromedary and Bactrian camel under the genus Camelus.
Fossil remains of Camelus have been found across prehistoric Eurasia.
The genus Camelus is distinguished by its unique ability to conserve water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camel - a large mammal with a humped back, used as a beast of burden

Etymology

From Latin 'Camelus', from Greek 'kamēlos' meaning camel.
