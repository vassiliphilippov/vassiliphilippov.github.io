Tellus

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛl.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.əs / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the earth, protector of marriage and fertility, identified with the Greek goddess Gaea.
Roman earth goddess; counterpart to Gaea

Examples

In ancient Roman religion, Tellus was venerated as the nurturing mother of the earth.
The festival in honor of Tellus celebrated fertility and agricultural abundance.
Some myths describe Tellus as playing a key role in the creation of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaea - Greek goddess of the earth, equivalent to Tellus

Etymology

From Latin Tellus (earth, land, ground).
