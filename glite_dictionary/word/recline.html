to recline

1. VERB

/ rɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to move into a position where the upper body is leaning or lying backwards or downwards
lean or lie back

Examples

She pressed the button to recline her seat during the long flight.
He likes to recline on the sofa after work and watch television.
The dentist told me to recline in the chair before the examination began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lean back - to incline the upper body backward
  • Antonym: to sit up - to move from a reclining to a more upright position

Etymology

From Latin reclinare, from re- (back) + clinare (to lean).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to cause something, such as a part of the body, to lean or lie back in a resting position
cause to lean or lie back

Examples

She gently reclined her head on the soft pillow.
The dentist reclined the patient’s chair before starting the procedure.
He reclined the seat so his daughter could sleep comfortably during the journey.

Synset Examples

She reclined her head on the pillow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lean back - to move or rest in a backward position

Etymology

From Latin reclinare, from re- (back) + clinare (to lean).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01550423-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to lean or lie back in a comfortable resting position
lean or lie back comfortably

Examples

She likes to recline on her sofa with a good book in the evening.
Please recline your seat for greater comfort during the flight.
He reclined in his chair and closed his eyes, enjoying the sunshine.

Synset Examples

He was reposing on the couch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to be in a horizontal or resting position
  • Antonym: to sit up - to move into or remain in a more upright seated position

Etymology

From Latin reclinare (to bend back), from re- (back) + clinare (to lean).
