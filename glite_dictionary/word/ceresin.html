ceresin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛrəsɪn / (BrE)
a white or yellowish wax obtained from ozokerite, used in making candles, polishes, and cosmetics
a wax extracted from ozokerite

Examples

The laboratory used ceresin to create a stable base for the ointment.
She bought a candle made from natural ceresin at the craft fair.
Certain polishes contain ceresin because of its excellent consistency and shine.

Etymology

From Latin Ceres (the Roman goddess of agriculture), likely referring to its use and appearance.
