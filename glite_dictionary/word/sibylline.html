sibylline

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪbəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪliːn / (BrE)
mysterious or prophet-like; resembling or characteristic of a prophet or prophecy, especially in being enigmatic or oracular
mysterious and prophetic

Examples

The priest delivered a sibylline warning that left everyone puzzled.
Her sibylline remarks hinted at changes to come, but gave no specifics.
The novel's ending was deliberately sibylline, leaving readers to interpret its true meaning.

Synset Examples

the high priest's divinatory pronouncement
mantic powers
a kind of sibylline book with ready and infallible answers to questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - relating to or characteristic of a prophet or prophecy
  • Synonym: oracular - resembling an oracle; mysterious and prophetic

Etymology

From Latin sibyllinus, relating to a sibyl (prophetess).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪbəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪˌliːn / (BrE)
mysterious or difficult to interpret; having a hidden or secret meaning
mysterious or obscure in meaning

Examples

His speech was so sibylline that it left the audience puzzled and intrigued.
She delivered a sibylline warning that seemed to conceal more than it revealed.
The author is known for his sibylline remarks, always open to multiple interpretations.

Synset Examples

cabalistic symbols engraved in stone
cryptic writings
thoroughly sibylline in most of his pronouncements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • Synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious

Etymology

From Latin Sibylla (a prophetess in ancient Greece and Rome), associated with cryptic prophecies.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00903031-s
Synset POS: s
