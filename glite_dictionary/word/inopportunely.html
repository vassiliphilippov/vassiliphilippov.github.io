inopportunely

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnˈɑːpɚˌtunli / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈɒpətjuːnli / (BrE)
At an inconvenient or unsuitable time.
at an inconvenient time

Examples

He called inopportunely when I was just heading out the door.
The storm arrived inopportunely, delaying the outdoor event.
Her message was sent inopportunely in the middle of an important meeting.

Synset Examples

he arrived inopportunely just as we sat down for dinner
she answered malapropos

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opportunely - at a convenient or suitable time

Etymology

From Latin 'inopportunus' (inconvenient, untimely), from 'in-' (not) + 'opportunus' (favorable).
