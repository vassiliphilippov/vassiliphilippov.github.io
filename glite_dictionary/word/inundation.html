inundation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ʌnˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ʌnˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An overwhelming number or amount of things occurring at once.
overwhelming quantity or flood

Examples

The company received an inundation of job applications after posting the new position online.
During the sale, there was an inundation of customers at the store.
Social media saw an inundation of comments following the celebrity's announcement.

Synset Examples

a flood of requests
a torrent of abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - a large amount occurring at once (figurative sense)
  • Synonym: deluge - an overwhelming quantity or number

Etymology

From Latin inundatio (a flooding), from inundare (to flood).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnʌnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnʌnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the flooding of land caused by water overflowing onto areas that are usually dry
flooding of dry land with water

Examples

The sudden inundation of the coastal village caused widespread damage to homes and crops.
Each spring, the river's inundation brings much-needed nutrients to the soil.
Ancient Egypt depended on the annual inundation of the Nile for its agricultural success.

Synset Examples

plains fertilized by annual inundations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits

Etymology

From Latin inundatio, from inundare (to flood).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
