Iresine

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈriːsɪni / (AmE), / aɪˈriːsiːni / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American herbs or subshrubs, some species of which are cultivated for their brightly colored foliage.
genus of tropical American herbs or subshrubs

Examples

The vivid leaves of Iresine make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Several varieties of Iresine are prized for their striking red stems and foliage.
Botanists have discovered new medicinal uses for certain Iresine species.

Etymology

From Greek iris (rainbow), referring to the colorful leaves of some species.
