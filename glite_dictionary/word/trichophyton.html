Trichophyton

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑfəˌtɑn / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒfɪˌtɒn / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Moniliaceae, some species of which cause skin diseases like ringworm and favus in humans and animals.
fungus genus causing ringworm and favus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her skin condition as an infection caused by Trichophyton.
Researchers are studying how Trichophyton species resist antifungal treatments.
Trichophyton is one of the most common genera responsible for athlete's foot.

Etymology

From Greek 'trikhos' (hair) + 'phyton' (plant), reflecting its association with hair infections.

2. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑː.fəˌtɑn / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒ.fɪˌtɒn / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Moniliaceae, several species of which are pathogenic and cause infections such as ringworm and favus in humans and animals.
genus of pathogenic fungi causing ringworm, favus
medical

Examples

Several species within the genus Trichophyton are responsible for athlete's foot and ringworm.
Microscopic examination revealed that the infection was caused by Trichophyton fungi.
Treatment for Trichophyton infections usually involves topical antifungal medications.

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) + 'phyton' (plant), indicating its association with hair infections.
