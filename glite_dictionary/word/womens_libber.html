women's libber

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmɪnz ˈlɪbər / (AmE), / ˈwɪmɪnz ˈlɪbə / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes dated) A person who supports or advocates for the women's liberation movement and women's rights.
advocate for women's liberation
informal

Examples

As a women's libber, she spoke out against workplace discrimination.
The mayor dismissed her critics as mere women's libbers from the protest march.
Some see the label women's libber as outdated, but it once defined a generation of activists.

Synset Examples

As a women's libber, she fought for equal rights.
Her grandmother was a proud women's libber in the 70s.
The documentary featured interviews with famous women's libbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminist - a person who supports feminism and gender equality

Etymology

Formed from 'women's lib' (women's liberation) + 'libber' (one who liberates or supports liberation), emerged in the U.S. in the 1960s-70s.
