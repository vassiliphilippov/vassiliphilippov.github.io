Hernaria glabra

1. NOUN

/ hɜrˈnɛəriə ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / həːˈnɛːrɪə ˈɡlæbrə / (BrE)
A low-growing herb native to Europe and Asia, often used as a ground cover and formerly believed to have medicinal properties for treating ruptures.
prostrate herb used as ground cover

Examples

The gardener planted Hernaria glabra to create a dense, green carpet in the shaded areas of the lawn.
Historically, some believed that Hernaria glabra could help cure ruptures and other ailments.
Many landscaping experts recommend Hernaria glabra as an alternative to traditional grass in challenging soils.
