discoloration

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of changing from the original color to a duller, faded, or less attractive color
change of color to a duller or faded state

Examples

The discoloration on the fabric was caused by sunlight exposure over several years.
She noticed a slight discoloration around the edges of the old photograph.
Water damage led to the discoloration of the ceiling tiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - an area of discoloration caused by a substance
  • Synonym: fading - the process of losing brightness or color

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + coloration, and ultimately from Latin colorare (to color).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an area or mark where the original color has been changed, often becoming less attractive or dirty
an area of changed, often unwanted, color

Examples

There was a noticeable discoloration on the old photograph where the sun had faded it.
She tried to remove the discoloration from her white shirt without success.
The plumber explained that the brown discoloration in the water was caused by rust in the pipes.

Synset Examples

the wine left a dark stain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a mark made by a foreign substance that is difficult to remove

Etymology

From the Late Latin discoloratio (change of color), from discolorare (to discolor).
