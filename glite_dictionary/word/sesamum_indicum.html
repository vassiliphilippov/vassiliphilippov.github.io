Sesamum indicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzəˌmʌm ˈɪndɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːzəməm ˈɪndɪkəm / (BrE)
the plant species commonly known as sesame, an annual herb cultivated for its edible seeds and oil
the sesame plant (species name)

Examples

Sesamum indicum is widely grown in tropical regions for its highly valued seeds.
The oil extracted from Sesamum indicum is used in cooking and cosmetics.
Scientists studied the genetic diversity of Sesamum indicum to improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame - seeds and oil derived from the Sesamum indicum plant

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Sesamum' denotes sesame and 'indicum' refers to India, indicating Indian origin.
