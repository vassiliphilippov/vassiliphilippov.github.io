elegiac

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛlɪˈdʒaɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈdʒaɪ.ək / (BrE)
expressing sorrow or lamentation, especially for something now past; resembling or characteristic of an elegy
expressing sorrow; elegy-like

Examples

The author wrote an elegiac tribute to her childhood hometown.
His paintings have an elegiac quality, evoking a sense of longing and loss.
The music's elegiac tone reflected the somber mood of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

an elegiac poem on a friend's death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - feeling or expressing sadness; sorrowful
  • Antonym: joyful - full of joy; expressing happiness

Etymology

From Latin elegiacus, from Greek elegeiakos, relating to an elegy.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌliːdʒiˈæk / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈdʒaɪ.ək / (BrE)
expressing sorrow or lamentation, especially for something in the past
expressing sorrow for the past

Examples

The poet's latest collection has an elegiac tone, reflecting on lost innocence.
Her speech was deeply elegiac, mourning the passing of a beloved era.
The film's soundtrack is haunting and elegiac, evoking memories of days gone by.

Synset Examples

an elegiac lament for youthful ideals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness, regret, or grief
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Greek 'elegeiakos', relating to an elegy.
