salami

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑmi / (AmE), / səˈlɑːmi / (BrE)
A highly seasoned, cured sausage made from pork and beef, typically sliced and eaten cold.
seasoned cured sausage

Examples

She ordered a sandwich with salami, cheese, and lettuce for lunch.
A traditional Italian antipasto platter often includes slices of salami.
After opening the package, he stored the leftover salami in the refrigerator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sausage - minced meat stuffed in a casing, often seasoned and cooked

Etymology

From Italian 'salame', meaning salted meat.
