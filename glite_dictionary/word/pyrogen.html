pyrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərəʤən / (BrE)
a substance that produces fever by raising body temperature when introduced or released into the body
fever-causing substance
medical

Examples

The laboratory checks all products for pyrogen contamination before release.
A pyrogen can be bacterial toxins or substances produced by the body's own immune system.
Doctors suspect that an unidentified pyrogen caused the severe fever in patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antipyretic - a drug used to prevent or reduce fever

Etymology

From Greek 'pyro-' (fire) + '-gen' (producer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪrədʒɛn / (BrE)
a substance that is highly flammable and easily ignited
highly flammable substance

Examples

Certain chemicals in the laboratory are classified as pyrogens due to their risk of quick ignition.
Always store pyrogens away from heat sources to prevent accidental fires.
The new safety protocol requires labeling all pyrogen containers with hazard warnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flammable - easily set on fire; combustible
  • Synonym: inflammable - easily set on fire; combustible
  • Antonym: nonflammable - not easily set on fire

Etymology

From Greek pyr (fire) + -genēs (born, produced), meaning 'fire-producing'.
