Cyprinus carpio

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpraɪnəs ˈkɑrpioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈprɪnəs ˈkɑːpiəʊ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish, commonly known as the common carp, native to Asia and introduced widely to Europe and North America, often kept for food or sport
species of freshwater fish (common carp)

Examples

The pond was stocked with Cyprinus carpio for recreational fishing.
In certain countries, Cyprinus carpio is considered a delicacy and is often farmed for food.
Scientists study populations of Cyprinus carpio to understand their impact on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a common freshwater fish related to 'Cyprinus carpio'

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Cyprinus' (meaning 'carp') + species 'carpio' (Latin for 'carp').
