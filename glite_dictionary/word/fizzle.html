fizzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪzəl / (BrE)
a complete failure, especially after a promising beginning; a flop
a complete failure; a flop
informal

Examples

The much-anticipated launch turned out to be a fizzle and disappointed investors.
Despite the star-studded cast, the movie was a fizzle at the box office.
Her attempt at a surprise party was unfortunately a bit of a fizzle.

Synset Examples

the play was a dismal flop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flop - a total failure, especially of a performance or product
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'fuss' or related to 'fizz,' originally meaning to hiss or sputter out.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪzəl / (BrE)
(of an event, process, or activity) to end in a weak or disappointing way, often failing to meet expectations.
end weakly or disappointingly

Examples

After a promising start, the negotiations began to fizzle and eventually ended without an agreement.
Their plans for a big celebration eventually fizzled when most people couldn't attend.
The excitement of the new project soon started to fizzle as the challenges became apparent.

Synset Examples

The music just petered out — there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to peter out - to gradually come to an end; to diminish slowly until it stops

Etymology

Imitative origin, likely related to the word 'fiss' (to break wind quietly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02689494-v
Synset POS: v
