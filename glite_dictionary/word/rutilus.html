Rutilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtɪləs / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly known as roaches, found in Eurasia.
genus of freshwater fishes (roaches)

Examples

The freshwater fish of the genus Rutilus are common throughout Europe and parts of Asia.
Researchers discovered a new population of Rutilus in the river ecosystem.
Anglers often seek out Rutilus species for their abundance and sporting qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roach - common name for certain species in the genus Rutilus

Etymology

From Latin rutilus (reddish, golden-red), originally describing coloration.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːtɪləs / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly known as roaches, found throughout Europe and northern Asia.
a genus of freshwater fish (roaches)

Examples

The common roach, a species within the genus Rutilus, is widespread in European rivers and lakes.
Ichthyologists have identified several new species in the Rutilus genus based on genetic data.
The dietary habits of Rutilus species help maintain the ecological balance of freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roach - a common freshwater fish in the genus Rutilus

Etymology

From Latin rutilus, meaning 'reddish' or 'golden-red', referring to the color of some species.
