tapering

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪpɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪpərɪŋ / (BrE)
Becoming gradually narrower, smaller, or less in amount toward one end or over time.
gradually narrowing or diminishing

Examples

The mountain has a tapering peak that touches the sky.
Her dress features a tapering silhouette that flatters her figure.
The company's profits have been tapering off for the past few quarters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrowing - becoming smaller in width
  • Antonym: widening - becoming broader or wider

Etymology

From 'taper' (to become gradually narrower), based on Middle English 'tapere' (candle, taper).

3. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.pər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.pər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of gradually reducing something in size, amount, or intensity, especially in stages
gradual reduction in size or amount

Examples

The doctor recommended a tapering of the medication over several weeks to prevent withdrawal symptoms.
Athletes often include a tapering phase in their training schedules before major competitions.
A gradual tapering of financial support allowed the organization to adjust to lower funding levels.

Synset Examples

the doctor prescribed the tapering of the dose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: increase - the act of becoming larger or greater in amount

Etymology

From the verb 'taper', ultimately from Middle English 'tapere' (candle).
