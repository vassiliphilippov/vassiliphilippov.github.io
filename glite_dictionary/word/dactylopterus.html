Dactylopterus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪˈlɑːp.tər.əs / (AmE), / ˌdaktɪˈlɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Dactylopteridae, commonly known as flying gurnards, noted for their large, wing-like pectoral fins.
genus of flying gurnard fish

Examples

The Dactylopterus spreads its large fins to startle predators and search for food.
One of the most distinctive features of Dactylopterus is its wing-like pectoral fins.
Scientists classify the flying gurnard under the genus Dactylopterus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dactylopteridae - the family of fishes to which Dactylopterus belongs

Etymology

From Greek daktylos (finger) + pteron (wing), referring to the wing-like fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪˈlɑːp.tɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˌdæk.tɪˈlɒp.tər.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Dactylopteridae, known as flying gurnards, recognized for their large, wing-like pectoral fins.
genus of flying gurnard fish

Examples

The Dactylopterus genus is noted for its members' ability to glide short distances above the sea floor.
Marine biologists often study Dactylopterus to better understand fin evolution in fish.
A well-known species in the Dactylopterus genus is Dactylopterus volitans, the flying gurnard.

Etymology

From Greek 'daktylos' (finger) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the fin structure.
