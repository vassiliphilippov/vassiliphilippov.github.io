xeroradiography

1. NOUN

/ ˌzɪroʊˌreɪdiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəʊˌreɪdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a radiographic imaging technique in which X-rays are used with xerographic (electrostatic) processes rather than traditional film methods
X-ray imaging using xerographic techniques
medical

Examples

Xeroradiography is often used in mammography because it produces images with excellent contrast.
Unlike traditional techniques, xeroradiography uses a dry processing method to develop X-ray images.
The hospital invested in new xeroradiography equipment to improve diagnostic accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiography - imaging technique using X-rays or other radiation

Etymology

From Greek 'xeros' (dry) + 'radiography' (imaging with radiation); reflects the dry electrostatic process used.
