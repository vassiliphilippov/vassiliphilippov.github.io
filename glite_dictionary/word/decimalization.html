decimalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛs.ɪ.mə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛs.ɪ.mə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting to a decimal system, especially in currency or measurement
conversion to a decimal system

Examples

The decimalization of British currency occurred in 1971, introducing pounds and new pence.
Many countries have undergone decimalization to simplify their monetary systems.
After decimalization, calculations and transactions became more straightforward.

Synset Examples

the decimalization of British currency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decimalise - to convert (something) to a decimal system

Etymology

From decimal + -ization, after the model of words like 'organization'.
