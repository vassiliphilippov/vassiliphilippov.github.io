abrogator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.rəˌɡeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈæb.rəˌɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
an authority or official who has the power to abolish, annul, or repeal laws or regulations
an official who annuls or repeals laws

Examples

The council appointed an abrogator to oversee the repeal of outdated statutes.
As the chief abrogator, she annulled several old city ordinances.
A new law limited the powers of the abrogator to rescind policies without review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abrogate - to abolish or annul, especially by authority

Etymology

From Latin abrogator, from abrogare (to annul, repeal).
