temperately

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛmpɚətli / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətli / (BrE)
in a moderate or restrained manner; without excess or extravagance
in a moderate way, without excess

Examples

The judge spoke temperately, maintaining a calm and balanced tone throughout the proceedings.
We should approach the problem temperately rather than reacting with anger or impulsiveness.
He indulges in desserts temperately, never eating more than a small piece.

Synset Examples

these preferences are temperately stated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderately - in a moderate manner, avoiding extremes
  • Antonym: excessively - to an extreme degree; more than is necessary

Etymology

From Latin temperatus (moderated, restrained), from temperare (to restrain, moderate) + -ly.

/ ˈtɛmpərətli / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətli / (BrE)
in a moderate or restrained manner; avoiding excess or overindulgence
in a moderate or restrained way

Examples

The judge spoke temperately, choosing his words with care and balance.
She enjoyed her wine temperately, never having more than a single glass with dinner.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderately - in a moderate manner; not excessively or extremely
  • Antonym: excessively - to an excessive degree; beyond what is necessary

Etymology

3. ADV

/ ˈtɛmpɚətli / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətli / (BrE)
in a way that shows restraint or moderation; without excess
with restraint or moderation

Examples

He spoke temperately during the heated debate, careful not to provoke anyone.
Alcohol should be consumed temperately for a healthy lifestyle.
Judges are expected to act temperately and avoid showing bias.

Synset Examples

he used the privileges of his office temperately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderately - to a moderate degree; not excessively
  • Antonym: excessively - to a greater than necessary degree; too much

Etymology

From Latin 'temperatus' (moderate, restrained), from 'temperare' (to moderate, mix properly).
