description

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a particular kind or variety of something; sort or type
type or kind; sort

Examples

The exhibition included artwork of every description, from classic to modern.
People of every description attended the festival.
We stock goods of every description in our store.

Synset Examples

every description of book was there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type - a category or kind of thing or person
  • Synonym: sort - a category into which something is classified

Etymology

From Latin descriptio (a sketch, representation), from describere (to describe).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving a spoken or written account of something, including details that help others to understand what it is like
the act of describing something

Examples

Can you give me a description of the man you saw?
The brochure includes a detailed description of each exhibit.
Her vivid description made me feel as if I were really there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: definition - a statement of the exact meaning of a word or phrase

Etymology

From Latin descriptio (a sketch, description), from describere (to write down, copy, sketch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07216025-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a statement or account that gives details about what someone or something is like
account giving details; depiction in words

Examples

The police asked him for a description of the suspect.
Her vivid description made the scene come alive for the readers.
Please provide a brief description of your qualifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the action of depicting something, especially visually or in words
  • Synonym: representation - the action of describing or portraying something

Etymology

From Latin descriptio (a representation, delineation), from describere (to write down, copy off).
