piffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪf.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɪf.əl / (BrE)
silly or trivial talk or ideas; nonsense.
silly or trivial nonsense

Examples

Don’t waste your time listening to his piffle about conspiracy theories.
The critic dismissed the play as utter piffle.
Whenever the meeting gets off-topic, it turns into complete piffle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing with no meaning or value

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an imitation of meaningless chatter or puff.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06620496-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪf.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɪf.əl / (BrE)
to behave or speak in a trivial, foolish, or ineffectual manner
act in a trivial or foolish way

Examples

Stop piffling and get to the point; we're running out of time.
The committee piffled around for hours without making any decisions.
Instead of working, he just piffles about trivial matters all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish or excited way

Etymology

Imitative in origin, echoing the sound or sense of nonsense talk.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106597-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈpɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪf.əl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and incessantly about trivial or unimportant matters
talk rapidly about trivial matters

Examples

Stop piffling and get to the point already.
He piffled on about nothing for half an hour.
She tends to piffle when she's nervous in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound of idle chatter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v
