thrombus

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːmbəs / (AmE), / ˈθrɒmbəs / (BrE)
A blood clot that forms inside a blood vessel and remains attached to its original site.
blood clot inside a vessel
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered a large thrombus blocking the patient's artery.
If a thrombus forms in a vein, it can lead to serious complications.
Treatment aims to dissolve the thrombus and restore normal blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: embolus - a blood clot or other substance that moves through the bloodstream to another location

Etymology

From Greek *thrombos* (a lump, clot of blood).
