anaclinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈklaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌanəˈklʌɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a valley or river) running in a direction opposite to the dip of the surrounding rock layers.
valley/river running opposite to rock dip

Examples

The river follows an anaclinal course, running contrary to the dip of the strata.
Geologists identified the valley as anaclinal because it flows against the surrounding rock layers.
Studying anaclinal streams helps scientists understand ancient geological processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synclinal - (of a valley or river) running in the same direction as the dip of surrounding rock layers

Etymology

From Greek ana- (against, up) + klino (to lean), referring to direction against the inclination.
