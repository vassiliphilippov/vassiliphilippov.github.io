oilseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌsid / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlsiːd / (BrE)
a seed from which oil is extracted, especially one grown or processed commercially for this purpose
seed that yields oil

Examples

Farmers in the region grow oilseed crops such as canola and sunflower for export.
The production of oilseed has increased due to the demand for vegetable oils.
After harvesting, the oilseed is processed to extract edible oil for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapeseed - a type of oilseed crop known for canola oil
  • Synonym: sunflower seed - the seed of the sunflower, often used for oil extraction
  • Synonym: oilcake - the solid residue after oil has been extracted from oilseed
