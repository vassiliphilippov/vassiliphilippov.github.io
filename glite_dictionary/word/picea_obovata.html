Picea obovata

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌsiːə oʊˈboʊvətə / (AmE), / pɪˌsiːə əʊˈbəʊvətə / (BrE)
A tall spruce tree native to northern Europe and Asia, closely related to the Norway spruce.
spruce tree of northern Europe and Asia

Examples

The dense forests of Siberia are dominated by Picea obovata and other conifers.
Botanists have found Picea obovata adaptable to various cold climates.
The cones of Picea obovata differ slightly from those of the Norway spruce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norway spruce - A related European spruce species (Picea abies)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Picea' meaning spruce, and 'obovata' meaning inversely egg-shaped, referring to the leaf or cone shape.
