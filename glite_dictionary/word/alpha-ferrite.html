alpha-ferrite

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfə ˈfɛraɪt / (AmE), / ˌalfə ˈfɛraɪt / (BrE)
A magnetic form of iron, stable at temperatures below 906°C, commonly found in steel and iron alloys.
magnetic form of iron stable below 906°C

Examples

The microstructure of low-carbon steel is primarily composed of alpha-ferrite.
As the temperature drops below 906°C, iron transforms into alpha-ferrite.
Metallurgists often analyze the distribution of alpha-ferrite to improve steel properties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: austenite - a non-magnetic allotrope of iron, stable at high temperatures
  • Degree Higher: gamma iron - another allotropic form of iron, stable at higher temperatures

Etymology

From 'alpha', denoting the phase, and 'ferrite' from Latin ferrum (iron).
