floodgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɡeɪt / (BrE)
a gate or valve used to control the flow of water, especially through a sluice or a dam
gate or valve controlling water flow

Examples

Heavy rainfall forced workers to open the floodgate to prevent the dam from overflowing.
The old mill was built next to a stream, with a floodgate to control the water supply.
Authorities monitored the floodgate closely during the storm to manage potential flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice gate - a sliding gate for regulating water flow in a sluice

Etymology

From flood + gate, originally signifying a gate to hold back or release floodwaters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɡeɪt / (BrE)
a gate for shutting out, admitting, or releasing a flow of water; figuratively, something that controls or restrains a large outpouring of anything
device or thing that restrains or releases outpourings

Examples

The collapse of the dam's floodgate caused water to pour into the valley.
Lifting the restrictions opened the floodgates to a wave of new applications.
Her resignation acted as a floodgate for further complaints against the management.

Synset Examples

suspension of surveillance opened the floodgates to illegal immigrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - a structure that blocks or controls movement
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting free or letting out

Etymology

From Middle English 'flodgate', from 'flod' (flood) + 'gate' (barrier, opening).
