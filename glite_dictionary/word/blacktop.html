blacktop

1. VERB

/ ˈblækˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈblækˌtɒp / (BrE)
to cover (a surface, especially a road or driveway) with blacktop (asphalt)
cover with asphalt

Examples

The crew will blacktop the entire street by the end of the week.
Homeowners often blacktop their driveways to improve appearance and durability.
After construction, they blacktopped the parking lot for smoother access.

Synset Examples

blacktop the driveway

Etymology

Formed from 'black' and 'top', referring to the dark-colored asphalt covering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈblæktɒp / (BrE)
A black bituminous material used for paving roads or other surfaces, typically laid over crushed stone.
a black paving material for roads
american

Examples

The crew laid fresh blacktop on the main street last week.
The parking lot was resurfaced with blacktop after the harsh winter.
Children played basketball on the smooth blacktop behind the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphalt - a mixture of dark bituminous pitch with sand or gravel, used for surfacing roads
  • Antonym: concrete - a building material made from a mixture of cement, aggregate, and water
