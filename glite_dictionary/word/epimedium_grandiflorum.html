Epimedium grandiflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈmidiəm ˌɡrændɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmiːdiəm ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of slow-growing creeping plant, often with semi-evergreen leaves on erect wiry stems, commonly used as ground cover in gardens.
a creeping plant used as ground cover

Examples

The shady border of her garden was filled with Epimedium grandiflorum to help suppress weeds.
Gardeners often plant Epimedium grandiflorum for its attractive foliage and delicate spring flowers.
Thanks to its creeping habit, Epimedium grandiflorum works well as a ground cover beneath trees.

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Epimedium' is the genus name and 'grandiflorum' means 'large-flowered' from Latin.
