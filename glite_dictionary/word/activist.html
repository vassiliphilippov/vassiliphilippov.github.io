activist

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tɪ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.vɪst / (BrE)
Advocating or engaged in activism; supporting or participating in efforts to bring about political or social change.
supporting activism; promoting change

Examples

The activist members of the group pushed for immediate policy changes.
She takes a strong activist stance on environmental issues.
Their activist approach helped draw public attention to social injustices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activistic - relating to activism
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From 'activism', derived from German 'Aktivismus', from 'aktiv' (active), influenced by Latin 'activus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈæktɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who campaigns vigorously for political or social change, often as part of a group
person advocating social or political change

Examples

The environmental activist organized a protest to raise awareness about climate change.
Many activists have been arrested for demanding equal rights and justice.
She became an activist after witnessing injustices in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaigner - a person who actively supports a cause
  • Synonym: reformer - someone who works for change, especially social or political

Etymology

From Latin 'activus' (active), influenced by 'activity'.
