sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm iˌθɪlmɜːrˌkjʊrɪˌθaɪoʊsəˌlɪsɪˈleɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm iˌθɪlmɜːkjʊˌrɪθaɪəʊsəˌlɪsɪˈleɪt / (BrE)
A light-colored crystalline powder, also known by the trade name Merthiolate, used as a surgical antiseptic.
antiseptic compound (Merthiolate)
medical

Examples

Many hospitals once used sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate as a topical antiseptic for minor wounds.
The label on the bottle listed sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate as its active ingredient.
Concerns about mercury content led to reduced use of sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate in medical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thimerosal - another name for sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate, used as an antiseptic and preservative
  • Degree Higher: antiseptic - substance preventing the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From sodium (element name), ethyl (from Greek 'aithēr' meaning 'upper air'), mercury (from Latin 'hydrargyrum'), thio- (from Greek 'theion' meaning 'sulfur'), and salicylate (from Latin 'salix' meaning 'willow').
