sceptically

1. ADV

/ ˈskɛptɪkli / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows doubt or disbelief; with scepticism.
in a sceptical manner; with doubt
british

Examples

She glanced sceptically at the proposal, unsure if it would succeed.
He listened sceptically to their claims about the miracle cure.
The audience reacted sceptically when the magician announced his next trick.

Synset Examples

he looked at her sceptically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptically - in a skeptical manner; showing or expressing doubt (American spelling)
  • Synonym: doubtfully - in a manner expressing doubt or uncertainty
  • Antonym: trustfully - in a manner showing trust or belief

Etymology

From "sceptical" + "-ly"; "sceptical" from Greek *skeptikos* (thoughtful, inquiring).
