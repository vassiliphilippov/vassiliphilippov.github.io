ineffectiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of lacking the ability to produce a desired effect or result.
lack of effectiveness

Examples

The manager was frustrated by the team's ineffectiveness in meeting the project deadline.
Public outrage grew due to the ineffectiveness of the new policy.
She recognized her own ineffectiveness in resolving the argument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effectiveness - the degree to which something is successful in producing a desired result
  • Synonym: efficiency - the ability to accomplish a job with minimum expenditure of time and effort

Etymology

From in- (not) + effectiveness, based on Latin roots.
