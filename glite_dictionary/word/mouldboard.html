mouldboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈməʊldˌbɔːd / (BrE)
the curved blade of a plough that lifts and turns over soil in a furrow
curved soil-turning blade of a plough

Examples

The mouldboard of the plough turned the heavy clay soil with ease.
After years of use, the mouldboard had worn thin and needed replacement.
Farmers adjusted the angle of the mouldboard for more efficient tilling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploughshare - main cutting blade of a plough

Etymology

From 'mould' (soil, earth) + 'board', referring to the board turning the soil.
