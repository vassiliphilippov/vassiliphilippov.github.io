intellectual

/ ˌɪn.t̬əˈlek.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tjʊ.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving the use of the intellect; appealing to or requiring mental effort or reasoning.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəlˈɛktʃuːəl / (BrE)
a person who uses creative or scholarly thought, especially one with a high degree of mental ability or interest in academic matters
a person engaged in creative or scholarly thought

Examples

Her circle of friends included many renowned intellectuals from diverse academic backgrounds.
He was considered an intellectual for his insightful writings on philosophy and science.
The debate attracted artists, writers, and other intellectuals interested in cultural trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinker - a person who thinks deeply and seriously
  • Synonym: academic - a person who works as a teacher or researcher at a university or other institution of higher education
  • Antonym: philistine - a person who is indifferent to culture and intellectual pursuits

Etymology

From Latin 'intellectualis', from 'intellectus' (understanding).
