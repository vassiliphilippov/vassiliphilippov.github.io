back-number

1. NOUN

/ bæk ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / bæk ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a person who is no longer important, influential, or fashionable
person no longer popular or influential

Examples

His critics dismissed him as a back-number who had lost touch with the times.
Many thought the actor was just a back-number, but he made a surprising comeback.
In today's fast-moving industry, you can quickly become a back-number if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: has-been - a person who is no longer famous, successful, or important

Etymology

From newspaper publishing, where 'back numbers' referred to previous issues no longer current.
