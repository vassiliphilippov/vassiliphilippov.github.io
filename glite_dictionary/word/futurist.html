futurist

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of futurism, especially in art, literature, or design
relating to futurism (art, style, ideas)

Examples

The gallery features a collection of futurist paintings from early twentieth-century Italy.
Her architectural designs display a futurist aesthetic, with bold lines and dynamic forms.
Many critics debated whether the new music could truly be classified as futurist or simply experimental.

Synset Examples

futurist art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futuristic - having a very modern appearance or using the newest ideas and methods

Etymology

From 'futurism,' after Italian 'futurista' (futurist) and 'futuro' (future).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies and predicts what may happen in the future, especially based on current trends
someone who predicts the future

Examples

As a leading futurist, she often speaks about how technology will shape our daily lives.
Many companies hire a futurist to help anticipate market changes and new opportunities.
The conference featured a futurist discussing innovations that could change transportation in the next decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forecaster - a person who predicts future events, especially weather or financial trends
  • Synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God, sometimes predicting the future

Etymology

From Latin 'futurus' (about to be) + English suffix '-ist'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10098309-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃərɪst / (BrE)
a theologian who believes that the prophecies in the Book of Revelation are predictions of future events
theologian interpreting Revelation as future prophecy

Examples

Many futurists believe that the events described in Revelation have yet to occur.
The debate between the preterist and futurist interpretations has shaped much of Christian eschatology.
She studied under a futurist who specialized in apocalyptic prophecy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preterist - a theologian who interprets prophecies in Revelation as having already happened
  • Antonym: historicist - a theologian who interprets prophecies in Revelation as unfolding across history

Etymology

From Latin futurus (about to be) + -ist (denoting a follower or specialist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10136359-n
Synset POS: n
