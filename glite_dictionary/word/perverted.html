perverted

1. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrtɪd / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːtɪd / (BrE)
departing significantly from what is considered morally right or proper; showing behavior or attitudes that are considered abnormal or corrupt
deviating from moral or proper conduct

Examples

Some people have a perverted sense of humor that others may find offensive.
The judge called his crimes the result of a perverted mind.
She condemned the perverted actions of the group as completely unacceptable.

Synset Examples

depraved criminals
a perverted sense of loyalty
the reprobate conduct of a gambling aristocrat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depraved - morally corrupt; wicked
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From the verb 'pervert', originating from Latin 'pervertere' meaning 'to turn about, corrupt'.

2. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrtɪd / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːtɪd / (BrE)
(of something such as a statement, idea, or translation) altered from its original meaning so that it is no longer true or accurate.
altered from true or original meaning

Examples

The politician gave a perverted account of the events to manipulate public opinion.
Historians criticized the perverted translation for changing the author’s original intent.
Rumors spread quickly through a perverted version of the story on social media.

Synset Examples

many of the facts seemed twisted out of any semblance to reality
a perverted translation of the poem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distorted - changed from the original meaning, especially misleadingly
  • Synonym: misrepresented - conveyed in a way that gives a false or misleading impression
  • Antonym: faithful - remaining true to the original meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'pervertere' meaning 'to turn around, corrupt'.

abnormal or deviant sexual behavior

The book explores the psychology behind perverted attractions and taboo behaviors.
