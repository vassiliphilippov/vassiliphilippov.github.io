rheumatism

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɪzəm / (BrE)
A general term for painful conditions affecting the joints, muscles, or connective tissues, often characterized by inflammation and stiffness.
painful joint or muscle disorder
medical

Examples

She suffers from rheumatism in her knees, especially during cold weather.
The doctor prescribed medication to ease his rheumatism.
Years of manual labor left him with chronic rheumatism in his hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints, often used synonymously with 'rheumatism' in lay contexts
  • Synonym: lumbago - pain in the lower back, often classed as a type of rheumatism

Etymology

From Greek rheumatismos (rheumatic complaint), from rheuma (that which flows, discharge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɪzəm / (BrE)
A chronic condition characterized by inflammation and pain in the joints, muscles, or fibrous tissue, often due to an autoimmune response.
chronic joint inflammation and pain
medical

Examples

As he grew older, he suffered from rheumatism in his knees and hands.
Many people believe cold, damp weather can worsen the symptoms of rheumatism.
She was prescribed medication to help manage the pain caused by her rheumatism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints causing pain and stiffness

Etymology

From Late Latin rheumatismus, from Greek rheuma meaning 'that which flows' (referring to humors or fluids).
