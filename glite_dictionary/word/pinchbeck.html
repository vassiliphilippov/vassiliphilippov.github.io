pinchbeck

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪntʃˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈpɪntʃˌbɛk / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and zinc, resembling gold, used especially in cheap jewelry as an imitation of gold
imitation gold alloy used in cheap jewelry

Examples

The antique pendant was made of pinchbeck rather than real gold.
Collectors sometimes mistake pinchbeck jewelry for genuine gold pieces.
Victorian brooches crafted from pinchbeck remain popular among vintage enthusiasts.

Etymology

Named after Christopher Pinchbeck (1670–1732), the English inventor of the alloy.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪntʃˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈpɪntʃˌbɛk / (BrE)
(of an object or quality) made to imitate something valuable or genuine; not authentic or real; sham or counterfeit.
imitation or counterfeit; not genuine

Examples

His diamond ring turned out to be nothing but pinchbeck jewelry.
The politician's speech was full of pinchbeck patriotism that failed to convince anyone.
Critics dismissed the play as a work of pinchbeck genius, lacking real originality.

Synset Examples

pinchbeck heroism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; imitation
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic; real, not imitation

Etymology

From Pinchbeck, a type of imitation gold invented by Christopher Pinchbeck, an 18th-century English watchmaker.
