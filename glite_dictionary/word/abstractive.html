abstractive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstræk.tɪv / (AmE), / əbˈstræk.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the ability to abstract; capable of forming abstractions or dealing with concepts apart from concrete realities.
relating to abstraction or abstracting

Examples

The model uses abstractive methods to summarize long texts efficiently.
His approach to philosophy was highly abstractive, focusing on general concepts rather than specific examples.
Linguists distinguish between extractive and abstractive techniques in text summarization.

Synset Examples

abstractive analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or concrete existence

Etymology

From abstract + -ive, modeled after similar Latinate formations.
