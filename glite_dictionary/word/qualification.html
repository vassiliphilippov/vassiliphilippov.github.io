qualification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a personal attribute, achievement, or requirement that makes someone suitable for a particular job, activity, or privilege
requirement or credential for suitability

Examples

Her impressive qualifications set her apart from the other job applicants.
One common qualification for this program is fluency in at least two languages.
You must meet certain qualifications before you can volunteer with the organization.

Synset Examples

her qualifications for the job are excellent
one of the qualifications for admission is an academic degree
she has the makings of fine musician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credential - a qualification, achievement, quality, or aspect of a person's background, especially when used to indicate their suitability for something
  • Antonym: disqualification - the state of being declared unfit or ineligible for something typically due to not meeting requirements

Etymology

From Medieval Latin qualificatio (the act of making fit or able), from Latin qualis (of what kind).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑː.lɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒ.lɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statement or fact that limits or modifies a previous claim or assertion
limiting or modifying statement

Examples

He gave his support for the plan, but with one important qualification.
Her endorsement of the project came with several qualifications about its feasibility.
No qualification was mentioned in his recommendation letter.

Synset Examples

he recommended her without any reservations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reservation - a doubt or condition attached to an agreement or statement
  • Synonym: exception - a case or example that does not follow a general rule
  • Antonym: unqualified - without any limitations or conditions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin qualificatio (the action of making of a quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑː.lə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒ.lɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an act or statement that modifies or limits the force or generality of something said or proposed
an act of limiting or modifying a statement

Examples

The prime minister added a qualification to his earlier statement to clarify his position.
She agreed to support the proposal, though with a qualification concerning funding details.
Without any qualification, his assertion sounded too sweeping and absolute.

Synset Examples

his new position involves a qualification of his party's platform

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - an act or condition of restricting or limiting
  • Antonym: amplification - an act of increasing or expanding the force or scope of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin qualificatio (marking with a quality), from Latin qualis (of what sort) + facio (make).

Synset POS: n
