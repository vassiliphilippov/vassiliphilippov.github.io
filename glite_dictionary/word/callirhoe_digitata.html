Callirhoe digitata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪroʊ.iː daɪˈdʒeɪtə / (AmE), / kəˌlɪr.əʊ.iː dɪˈdʒeɪ.tə / (BrE)
A species of perennial poppy mallow native to the southern plains of the United States, known for its rose-red or rose-purple flowers.
species of poppy mallow with rose-red flowers

Examples

Callirhoe digitata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it ideal for prairie gardens.
Gardeners often choose Callirhoe digitata for its attractive, deeply lobed leaves and striking magenta blooms.
A patch of Callirhoe digitata can add vibrant color to native wildflower meadows in the summer.

Etymology

From the genus Callirhoe, named after a figure in Greek mythology, and Latin 'digitata' meaning 'fingered,' referring to the shape of the leaves.
