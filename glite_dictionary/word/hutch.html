hutch

1. NOUN

/ hʌtʃ / (AmE), / hʌtʃ / (BrE)
a cage, typically made of wood and wire mesh, for keeping small animals such as rabbits
cage for small animals

Examples

They built a hutch in the backyard to house their new rabbits.
The guinea pigs were safe inside their spacious hutch during the night.
She cleaned the hutch every week to keep the animals healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cage - an enclosure with bars or wire for confining animals

Etymology

Probably from Old French huche (chest, bin).

2. NOUN

/ hʌtʃ / (AmE), / hʌtʃ / (BrE)
a small, crude shelter used as a dwelling, often temporary and makeshift
small crude dwelling shelter

Examples

The refugees built a hutch from scraps of wood and metal.
He spent several nights in a hutch by the river after losing his home.
Children pretended their playhouse was a hutch hidden deep in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple shelter or dwelling

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French huche (chest, storage box).
