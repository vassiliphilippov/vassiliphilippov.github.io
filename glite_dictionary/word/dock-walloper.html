dock-walloper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑk ˌwɑləpər / (AmE), / ˈdɒk ˌwɒləpə / (BrE)
a laborer who loads and unloads ships in a port; a longshoreman.
port laborer; longshoreman
archaic

Examples

The old port was once filled with dock-wallopers unloading cargo by hand.
He worked as a dock-walloper before the age of container ships.
Stories of tough dock-wallopers abound in the city’s maritime history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longshoreman - a person employed to load and unload ships

Etymology

Formed from 'dock' (harbor area) and 'wallop' (to strike or hit), suggesting manual labor. First recorded 19th century.
