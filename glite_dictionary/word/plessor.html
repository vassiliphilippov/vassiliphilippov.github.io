plessor

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛsər / (AmE), / ˈplɛsə / (BrE)
A small hammer with a rubber head used by doctors to perform percussive examinations, such as testing reflexes or examining the chest.
medical hammer for examining reflexes
medical

Examples

The doctor tapped my knee with a plessor to check my reflexes.
A neurologist will often use a plessor during routine examinations.
The clinic purchased several new plessors for their examination rooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion hammer - tool for tapping body parts during a medical examination

Etymology

From Latin 'plessor' (striker), related to 'plesso' (to strike).
