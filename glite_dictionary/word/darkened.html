darkened

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrkənd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkənd / (BrE)
(of a place or object) made dark or having less light than usual; not illuminated.
made dark or less lit

Examples

She sat alone in the darkened room, waiting for the storm to pass.
The darkened hallway made it difficult to see where we were going.
The movie began in a darkened theater as the audience grew quiet.

Synset Examples

a darkened house
the darkened theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark - lacking or having very little light
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00275564-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrkənd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkənd / (BrE)
(of fabrics or paper) having become darker in color over time, especially due to age, exposure, or use.
made darker in color over time

Examples

The old letter had darkened edges after years in the attic.
Several darkened patches were visible where the sun had not touched the paper.
The map's darkened surface revealed its age and constant handling.

Synset Examples

the darkened margins of the paper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faded - (of fabric or color) having lost brightness or intensity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01649594-s
Synset POS: s
