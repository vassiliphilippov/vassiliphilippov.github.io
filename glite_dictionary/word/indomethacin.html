indomethacin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊˈmɛθəsɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈmɛθəsɪn / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug used to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation, often prescribed for arthritis.
anti-inflammatory drug for pain and inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed indomethacin to help reduce the joint inflammation.
Patients taking indomethacin should be monitored for possible gastrointestinal side effects.
Indomethacin is sometimes used to treat severe cases of arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - another common nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
  • Antonym: acetaminophen - a pain reliever not classified as an NSAID
