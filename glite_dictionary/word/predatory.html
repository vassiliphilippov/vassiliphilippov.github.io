predatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈprɛdəˌtəri / (BrE)
seeking to exploit, control, or victimize others for personal gain; characterized by ruthlessly taking advantage of others
ruthlessly exploiting others for gain

Examples

Some companies have been criticized for their predatory lending practices.
He warned his daughter about the dangers of predatory individuals at parties.
The predatory behavior of the scammers left many victims in financial ruin.

Synset Examples

predatory capitalists
a predatory, insensate society in which innocence and decency can prove fatal
a predacious kind of animal — the early geological gangster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploitative - making use of a situation or treating others unfairly in order to gain an advantage or benefit

Etymology

From Latin praedatorius (pertaining to plundering), from praedari (to plunder).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈprɛdəˌtəri / (BrE)
(of people, groups, or actions) seeking to plunder, rob, or exploit others for personal gain, often by force.
seeking to plunder or rob

Examples

The pirates launched a predatory attack on the coastal villages, looting and burning as they went.
For centuries, predatory raids between rival clans devastated the region's economy.
She warned that predatory groups might exploit the chaos after the earthquake.

Synset Examples

bands of marauding Indians
predatory warfare
a raiding party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marauding - roaming about in search of things to steal or people to attack
  • Synonym: plundering - stealing goods, especially in time of war or civil disorder
  • Antonym: lawful - acting within or conforming to the law; not criminal

Etymology

From Latin praedatorius, from praedator (plunderer), from praeda (prey, booty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01633893-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈprɛdətəri / (BrE)
(Of an animal) living by hunting and consuming other animals for food.
hunting and eating other animals

Examples

Many predatory animals have sharp claws and teeth for catching their prey.
Owls are predatory birds that hunt mainly at night.
A predatory fish stalked the smaller fish hiding among the rocks.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnivorous - (Of an animal) feeding on other animals

Etymology

From Latin praedatorius (‘relating to plundering or robbery’), from praedator (‘plunderer’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00084491-s
Synset POS: s
