elaterid

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪtərɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtərɪd / (BrE)
A beetle of the family Elateridae, commonly known as a click beetle, found in various regions worldwide.
beetle of the Elateridae family

Examples

An elaterid can make a distinct clicking sound by flexing its body.
Many elaterid larvae are known as wireworms and damage crops.
The elaterid family includes beetles with an elongated body and strong jump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click beetle - common term for beetles of the family Elateridae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Elateridae', the family name, based on Greek 'elatēr' (driver, springer).
