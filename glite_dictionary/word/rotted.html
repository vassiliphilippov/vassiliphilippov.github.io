rotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɒtɪd / (BrE)
having been damaged or decayed so as to become unsound or useless.
damaged or decayed; unsound

Examples

The wooden floorboards were so rotted that they collapsed under his weight.
We had to replace the rotted beams in the old barn to make it safe again.
The fruit left out on the counter quickly became rotted and inedible.

Synset Examples

rotten floor boards
rotted beams
a decayed foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotten - decayed, decomposed, or in a state of rot
  • Synonym: decayed - damaged or decomposed through decomposition

Etymology

Past participle of the verb "rot," from Old Norse "rotna" (to decay).
