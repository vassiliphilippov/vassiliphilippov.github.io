appositional

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈzɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌapəˈzɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving apposition, especially in grammar
relating to apposition (grammar)

Examples

In the phrase 'my friend Alice,' 'Alice' is an appositional noun.
Linguists often analyze appositional structures when studying sentence composition.
The teacher explained the use of appositional modifiers in English grammar.

Synset Examples

an appositive noun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appositive - a noun or phrase placed next to another noun to rename or describe it

Etymology

From Latin 'appositio' (a placing near), with suffix '-al'.
