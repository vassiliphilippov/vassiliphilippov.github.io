to pronk

1. VERB

/ prɑŋk / (AmE), / prɒŋk / (BrE)
(of an animal) to jump straight up into the air with all four legs stiff, usually as a display or to avoid predators.
jump straight up (animals)

Examples

Springbok often pronk to show off their agility to potential mates and predators.
We watched as the gazelles began to pronk across the grassland, leaping into the air.
During the chase, the young antelope pronking made it more difficult for the cheetah to catch it.

Synset Examples

kangaroos pronk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stot - (of an animal) spring up off the ground with all four feet at once

Etymology

Probably from Afrikaans, related to Dutch 'pronken' (to show off, strut).
