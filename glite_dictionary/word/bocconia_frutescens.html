Bocconia frutescens

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈkoʊniə fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (AmE), / bəˈkəʊniə fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A small tree native to Central America, with loose clusters of green flowers tinged with purple.
species of Central American tree

Examples

The leaves of Bocconia frutescens are often used in traditional medicine.
Conservationists are studying the impact of Bocconia frutescens on native plant species.
Gardeners appreciate Bocconia frutescens for its unusual purple-tinted green flowers.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical naming; 'Bocconia' named for Paolo Boccone, Italian botanist, and 'frutescens' meaning 'becoming shrubby' in Latin.
