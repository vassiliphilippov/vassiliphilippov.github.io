Allium porrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ˈpɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈælɪəm ˈpɔːrəm / (BrE)
The scientific name for the leek, a plant with a large slender white bulb and overlapping dark green leaves, used in cooking.
scientific name for leek

Examples

The gardener planted Allium porrum in early spring for a late summer harvest.
Many traditional recipes call for Allium porrum as a key ingredient.
Researchers are studying Allium porrum for its potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - an edible plant related to the onion, with mild-flavored leaves and bulb

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic) and 'porrum' (leek).
