hyperthermy

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɜrˌθɜrmi / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈθɜːmi / (BrE)
A condition in which the body temperature is abnormally high; sometimes medically induced, especially for cancer treatment.
abnormally high body temperature
medical

Examples

Deliberate hyperthermy can enhance the effectiveness of certain cancer therapies.
After hours in the sun without shade, the patient developed symptoms of hyperthermy.
Doctors closely monitor body temperature when inducing hyperthermy in clinical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperthermia - abnormally high body temperature
  • Antonym: hypothermia - dangerously low body temperature

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over) + thermē (heat).
