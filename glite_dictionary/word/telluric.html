telluric

1. ADJ

/ təˈlʊrɪk / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊərɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing the chemical element tellurium.
relating to tellurium (chemical element)

Examples

The telluric compounds are studied extensively in inorganic chemistry.
Researchers discovered a rare telluric mineral in the sample.
A telluric alloy was used in the experiment to observe its properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tellurium - a chemical element with symbol Te and atomic number 52

Etymology

From Latin 'tellurium', named after the Earth (tellus).

2. ADJ

/ tɛˈlɜrɪk / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊərɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the earth or the land, as opposed to the sea or air
relating to the earth or land

Examples

The volcano released telluric gases into the atmosphere during the eruption.
Geologists often study telluric processes to understand how landforms evolve over time.
The novel contrasted telluric creatures with mythical beings of the air and sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth or land

Etymology

From Latin 'tellus' (earth, land), via French 'tellurique'.
