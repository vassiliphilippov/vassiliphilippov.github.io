Nyctanassa

1. PROPN

/ nɪk.təˈnæs.ə / (AmE), / nɪk.təˈnæs.ə / (BrE)
A genus of American night herons, including the yellow-crowned night heron.
genus of American night herons

Examples

The Nyctanassa genus includes the well-known yellow-crowned night heron.
Researchers studied migration patterns in the Nyctanassa along the Gulf Coast.
Birdwatchers hope to spot a member of the Nyctanassa during spring migration.

Etymology

From Greek nux (night) + anassa (queen), referring to the nocturnal habits of the birds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˈnæsə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈnæsə / (BrE)
A genus of American night herons, comprising species such as the yellow-crowned night heron.
genus of American night herons

Examples

The genus Nyctanassa includes the yellow-crowned night heron, commonly found in coastal regions.
Scientists studied nesting habits of birds belonging to the Nyctanassa genus.
Unlike other herons, Nyctanassa species are often active at dusk and during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - wading bird with long legs, neck, and bill
  • Degree Lower: Butorides - genus of small herons

Etymology

From Greek 'nykta' (night) + 'anassa' (queen).
