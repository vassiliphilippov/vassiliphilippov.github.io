malarkey

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɑːrki / (AmE), / məˈlɑːki / (BrE)
nonsense; empty talk, especially rhetoric that is insincere, exaggerated, or foolish.
nonsense; empty talk
informal

Examples

Don't listen to his malarkey; he just likes to exaggerate.
The politician's speech was full of malarkey and empty promises.
She rolled her eyes at the obvious malarkey in the advertisement.

Synset Examples

that's a lot of wind
don't give me any of that jazz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that are foolish, senseless, or meaningless
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly US slang from the 1920s.

2. NOUN

/ məˈlɑrki / (AmE), / məˈlɑːki / (BrE)
nonsense; empty, insincere, or exaggerated talk or ideas.
nonsense; insincere talk
informal

Examples

The politician's promises were dismissed as pure malarkey by the audience.
Don't give me any of that malarkey—I know what really happened.
Most of what you hear in those advertisements is just malarkey.

Synset Examples

that's a lot of wind
don't give me any of that jazz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: bunk - nonsense; foolish talk
  • Antonym: sense - sensible or reasonable talk or ideas

Etymology

Origin unknown; popularized in the U.S. in the 1920s.
