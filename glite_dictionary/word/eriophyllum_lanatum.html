Eriophyllum lanatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɑːfɪləm ləˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌɛriˈɒfɪləm ləˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A greyish, woolly-leaved perennial plant with branched stems topped by stalks bearing golden-yellow flower heads, native to dry areas of western North America; also known as Oregon sunshine or woolly sunflower.
A woolly-leaved golden-flowered plant of western North America
medical

Examples

The rocky slopes in the region are dotted with Eriophyllum lanatum in late spring.
Gardeners value Eriophyllum lanatum for its drought tolerance and bright yellow blossoms.
During our hike, we admired a hillside covered in blooming Eriophyllum lanatum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oregon sunshine - Common name for Eriophyllum lanatum, a golden-flowered perennial of western North America
  • Synonym: woolly sunflower - Another common name for Eriophyllum lanatum, a perennial with golden-yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'phyllon' (leaf) and Latin 'lanatum' (woolly), referring to its woolly leaves.
