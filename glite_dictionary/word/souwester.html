sou'wester

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊˈwɛstər / (AmE), / ˌsaʊˈwɛstə / (BrE)
a strong wind that blows from the southwest
strong southwest wind

Examples

The sou'wester swept across the bay, making the sea choppy and dangerous for small boats.
Fishermen stayed ashore, wary of the forecasted sou'wester that was expected to hit overnight.
A sudden sou'wester brought heavy rain and strong gusts to the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northwester - a strong wind from the northwest
  • Synonym: southwesterly - wind or direction from the southwest

Etymology

Contraction of 'southwester', from 'southwest'.

2. NOUN

/ saʊˈwɛstər / (AmE), / saʊˈwɛstə / (BrE)
a waterproof hat with a wide, slanting brim, longer at the back than the front, worn especially by sailors in wet weather
waterproof hat with long back brim

Examples

The fisherman pulled his sou'wester tighter as the rain began to pour.
She wore a bright yellow sou'wester to keep her dry during the storm.
Old photographs show children playing on the beach in oilskins and sou'westers.

Etymology

From 'southwester,' originally referring to strong southwesterly winds; later applied to the hat worn for protection against such weather.
