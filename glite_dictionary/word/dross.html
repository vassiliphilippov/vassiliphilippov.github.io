dross

1. NOUN

/ drɑs / (AmE), / drɒs / (BrE)
the scum or waste material that forms on the surface of molten metal during smelting or refining
waste material on molten metal

Examples

After melting the silver, the black dross was carefully skimmed off the surface.
The foundry worker removed the dross to ensure the purity of the ingot.
During the process, dross collects on top and must be separated from the usable metal.

Etymology

From Old English drōs (dregs, refuse), related to Middle Low German dros (dregs, lees).

2. NOUN

/ drɑs / (AmE), / drɒs / (BrE)
worthless, unwanted, or harmful material that is separated from something valuable, especially as a result of a process such as smelting
worthless, unwanted material

Examples

After refining the metal, the workers removed the dross from the surface.
Critics felt that most of the movie was dross with only a few worthwhile moments.
The report was full of technical dross that obscured the main findings.

Synset Examples

there were impurities in the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impurity - unwanted or harmful substance within something pure
  • Synonym: slag - stony waste matter separated from metals during smelting

Etymology

From Old English 'drōs' (impurity, dregs), related to Old Norse 'drōs' (dregs).
