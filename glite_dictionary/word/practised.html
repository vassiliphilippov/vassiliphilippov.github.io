practised

1. ADJ

/ ˈpræk.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪst / (BrE)
Having acquired skill or experience through repeated practice; skillful.
skilled from repeated practice
british

Examples

She is a practised pianist who performs with great confidence.
With a practised eye, he quickly spotted the problem in the design.
He spoke with the practised ease of someone who has given hundreds of lectures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having the ability to do something well
  • Antonym: unpractised - lacking experience or training in something

Etymology

Past participle of 'practise,' from Old French 'practiser', from Medieval Latin 'practicare'.
