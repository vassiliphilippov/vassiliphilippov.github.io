to renounce

1. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to formally declare one's abandonment of a claim, right, belief, or habit
formally give up a right, belief, or habit

Examples

After much reflection, he decided to renounce his citizenship and move abroad.
She publicly renounced her previous beliefs in order to join the new organization.
He plans to renounce all worldly possessions and live a simple life.

Synset Examples

I am foreswearing women forever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relinquish - to give up or abandon something voluntarily
  • Synonym: abandon - to give up completely (a practice, course of action, or way of thinking)

Etymology

From Old French *renoncer*, from Latin *renuntiare* (to announce, report, renounce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02232269-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
To formally declare one's abandonment of (a claim, right, belief, or association); to refuse to continue to recognize or be connected with someone or something.
formally give up or reject

Examples

After much consideration, she decided to renounce her citizenship and move abroad.
He publicly renounced his former beliefs in a televised interview.
By joining the new religion, he chose to renounce all ties with his past.

Synset Examples

She renounced her husband
The parents repudiated their son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repudiate - to refuse to accept or be associated with; synonym of renounce

Etymology

From Old French *renoncer*, from Latin *renuntiare* (to report, renounce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00759589-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to formally give up or voluntarily leave a job, post, or position
voluntarily leave a job or position

Examples

After years of service, she decided to renounce her position as company president.
The ambassador was forced to renounce his post after the scandal became public.
He chose to renounce his job in order to pursue further education.

Synset Examples

She vacated the position when she got pregnant
The chairman resigned when he was found to have misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resign - to voluntarily leave a job or office
  • Synonym: to quit - to leave a job or position voluntarily
  • Antonym: to retain - to continue to keep or hold a position

Etymology

From Old French 'renoncier', from Latin 'renuntiare' (to announce back, report, renounce).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to formally give up or reject a claim, responsibility, right, or position, especially by declaration
formally give up or reject something

Examples

After the scandal, the leader decided to renounce his official duties and step down from office.
She chose to renounce her inheritance, preferring to make her own way in life.
By signing the treaty, the country agreed to renounce its claims to the disputed territory.

Synset Examples

The King abdicated when he married a divorcee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abdicate - to give up a position of power or responsibility, especially as a monarch
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; to give up
  • Antonym: claim - to assert a right or title to something

Etymology

From Old French *renoncier*, from Latin *renuntiare* (to report, renounce).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
