to avenge

1. VERB

/ əˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / əˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
to punish or harm someone in return for a wrong or injury done to oneself or another, especially as an act of justice or retribution
take revenge or payback for a wrong

Examples

He swore he would avenge his family after the attack.
The heroine set out to avenge her father's unjust death.
Some people seek to avenge their friends when they have been wronged.

Synset Examples

He wants to avenge the murder of his brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - to inflict harm or punishment in return for a wrong
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful towards someone for an offense or mistake

Etymology

From Old French *avengier*, from Latin *vindicare* (to claim, avenge, punish).
