sanfoin

1. NOUN

/ sænˈfɔɪn / (AmE), / sænˈfɔɪn / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb cultivated as a forage crop, with pale pink flowers and curved pods, often used for animal feed and honey production.
forage plant with pink flowers, used for animal feed

Examples

The farmer planted sanfoin in the field to improve soil quality and feed his livestock.
Sanfoin yields a rich nectar, making it valuable for local beekeepers.
Cattle grazed on the lush sanfoin that flourished in the calcareous grasslands.

Etymology

From French *sainfoin*, from Old French *sain foin* (wholesome hay).
