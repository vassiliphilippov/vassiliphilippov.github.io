cash

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
Money in the form of coins or notes; prompt payment in currency or by check for goods or services.
money in coins, notes, or immediate payment

Examples

We offer a 5% discount if you pay in cash.
The shop only accepts cash or debit cards, not credit cards.
He counted the cash before placing it in the register.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in general use
  • Antonym: credit - buying goods or services with payment delayed

Etymology

From Middle French 'casse' (money box) and Italian 'cassa' (box), from Latin 'capsa' (case, box).

2. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
money in the form of bills and coins, rather than checks or electronic payments
money in physical form (bills and coins)

Examples

She paid for the groceries with cash instead of her credit card.
The restaurant only accepts cash payments on weekends.
He withdrew some cash from the ATM before traveling.

Synset Examples

there is a desperate shortage of hard cash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in general use in a country

Etymology

From Middle French 'casse' (money box, treasury), from Old Italian 'cassa' (box, chest), from Latin 'capsa' (box).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13407787-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
to exchange (a check, bond, etc.) for money; to obtain cash for something
exchange for money, especially a check

Examples

He went to the bank to cash his paycheck.
I forgot to cash the check before the bank closed.
You can cash your traveler's cheques at most hotels.

Synset Examples

I cashed the check as soon as it arrived in the mail

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deposit - to place money into a bank account

Etymology

From Middle French 'caisse' (money box, cash box), from Italian 'cassa', from Latin 'capsa' (box).
