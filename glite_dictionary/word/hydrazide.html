hydrazide

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌzaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪdrəzaɪd / (BrE)
any of a class of organic compounds containing the functional group –CO–NH–NH2 or derivatives in which at least one substituent is an acyl group bonded to a nitrogen-nitrogen linkage.
organic compound with -CO-NH-NH2 group

Examples

The synthesis of a new hydrazide derivative improved the yield of the final product.
Many pharmaceuticals contain a hydrazide group as part of their molecular structure.
Researchers tested the antioxidant activity of different hydrazide compounds in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

The general structure for a hydrazide is E(=O)-NR-NR2, where the R's are frequently hydrogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrazine - a chemical compound (N2H4) from which hydrazides are derived

Etymology

From hydrazine + -ide, after International Scientific Vocabulary conventions.
