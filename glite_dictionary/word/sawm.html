sawm

1. NOUN

/ sɑm / (AmE), / sɑːm / (BrE)
The Islamic practice of fasting from sunrise to sunset during Ramadan, one of the Five Pillars of Islam.
Islamic fasting during Ramadan

Examples

Many Muslims observe sawm every year during the holy month of Ramadan.
Sawm is considered an act of worship and self-purification in Islam.
Breaking sawm before sunset is not permitted unless there are valid reasons, such as illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ramadan - the Islamic holy month of fasting

Etymology

From Arabic صَوْم‎ (ṣawm, fasting), used in Islamic religious texts.
