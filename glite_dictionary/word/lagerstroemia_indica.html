Lagerstroemia indica

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪɡərˈstriːmiə ɪnˈdɪkə / (AmE), / ˌleɪɡəˈstriːmiə ɪnˈdɪkə / (BrE)
A species of ornamental shrub or small tree from eastern India, widely grown for its attractive flowers, especially in the southern United States; commonly known as crape myrtle.
a flowering ornamental shrub (crape myrtle)

Examples

The vibrant blossoms of Lagerstroemia indica make it a popular choice for gardens in the southern states.
Gardeners prize Lagerstroemia indica for its long blooming season and striking colors.
After planting Lagerstroemia indica along the driveway, they enjoyed beautiful flowers every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crape myrtle - common name for Lagerstroemia indica

Etymology

Named after Magnus von Lagerström, Swedish naturalist, and from Latin 'indica' meaning 'of India'.
