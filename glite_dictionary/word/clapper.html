clapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæpɚ / (AmE), / ˈklæpə / (BrE)
a person who applauds or gives applause, especially in a theater or at a performance
person who applauds

Examples

The clapper in the front row cheered loudly after every act.
A professional clapper was hired to encourage the audience's enthusiasm.
She became known as the most enthusiastic clapper at every school play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applauder - a person who applauds

Etymology

From clap + -er, meaning one who claps.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæpər / (AmE), / ˈklapə / (BrE)
(archaic, metaphorical) The tongue, especially regarded as the organ of speech.
the tongue (metaphorical, archaic)
archaic

Examples

She never stopped wagging her clapper from morning till night.
Poets once likened the clapper to the source of all mischief in society.
He bit his clapper while eating, wincing in pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue - the muscular organ in the mouth for tasting, swallowing, and speech

Etymology

From Middle English claper, from Old English *clappan* (to clap), originally referring to the part of a bell that strikes to make noise, later applied metaphorically to the tongue.

3. NOUN

/ ˈklæpər / (AmE), / ˈklapə / (BrE)
a metal striker that hangs inside a bell and sounds the bell by striking its sides
metal striker inside a bell

Examples

The clapper swung back and forth, ringing the bell loudly across the valley.
When the clapper broke, the church bell could no longer chime the hour.
He examined the old bell and discovered its clapper was missing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell - a hollow instrument that makes a ringing sound when struck

Etymology

From Middle English claper, from clap (to make a sudden noise), from Old English clappan.

Source: WordNet
