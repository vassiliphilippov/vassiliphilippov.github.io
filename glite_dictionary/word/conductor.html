conductor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a person who directs the performance of an orchestra, choir, or other musical group
person directing a musical group

Examples

The conductor raised his baton and the orchestra fell silent, ready to begin.
She dreams of becoming a conductor and leading world-renowned symphonies.
The audience applauded the conductor for his passionate interpretation of the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maestro - a distinguished conductor or music director

Etymology

From Latin conducere (to lead together), via French conducteur.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktər / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a substance that allows electricity or heat to pass through it easily
material allowing electric or heat flow

Examples

Copper is often used as a conductor of electricity in wiring.
Rubber is not a good conductor of heat.
Silver is the best conductor of electricity among all metals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insulator - a material through which electricity or heat does not easily flow

Etymology

From Latin 'conductor' (carrier, conductor), from 'conducere' (bring together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect fares and look after passengers on a public vehicle, such as a bus or train
person collecting fares on public transport

Examples

The conductor walked down the aisle collecting tickets from the passengers.
A conductor asked me where I wanted to get off so he could give me the right fare.
On many city buses, the conductor helps elderly passengers find a seat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticket collector - person who checks or collects tickets on public transport
  • Antonym: driver - person who drives a vehicle such as a bus or train

Etymology

From Latin 'conductus', past participle of 'conducere' (to lead).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a material or device that allows electricity, heat, or sound to pass through it easily
device/material transmitting electricity or heat

Examples

Copper is a very good conductor of electricity, making it ideal for use in wires.
Glass is not a conductor of heat, which is why it is often used as insulation.
The experiment required a conductor to demonstrate the flow of electric current.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insulator - a material that does not easily transmit electricity, heat, or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'conductor' (one who leads), from 'conducere' (to lead or bring together).
