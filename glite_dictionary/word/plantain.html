plantain

1. NOUN

/ ˈplænˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
A starchy banana-like fruit, eaten cooked as a staple food in many tropical regions.
starchy tropical fruit, cooked as staple

Examples

Fried plantain is a common side dish in many Caribbean countries.
He sliced the green plantains before boiling them for dinner.
The market sells both sweet bananas and starchy plantains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - elongated, edible fruit of the banana plant, typically sweet and eaten raw

Etymology

From Spanish 'plátano', which derives from Cariban.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɪn / (BrE)
a small herbaceous plant of the genus Plantago, typically found by roadsides and lawns, with broad leaves and spikes of tiny greenish flowers; some species' seeds are used medicinally
a small herbaceous plant (genus Plantago)

Examples

The plantain is a common weed that grows along pathways and in lawns.
Traditional herbalists use plantain leaves for their soothing properties.
Children sometimes make whistles from the stalks of the plantain flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantago - the genus of small herbaceous plants commonly called plantains

Etymology

From Latin 'plantago', from 'planta' (sole of the foot, plant), referring to the shape of the leaves.

3. NOUN

/ ˈplænˌteɪn, plænˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˈplæntɪn, ˈplænˌteɪn / (BrE)
A tropical or subtropical plant related to the banana, bearing starchy green fruit that is typically cooked before eating.
starchy banana-like tropical fruit

Examples

Fried plantain is a popular dish in many West African countries.
She bought several green plantains to make tostones for dinner.
The tropical climate is ideal for growing plantain trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - edible yellow fruit from a related tropical plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'plátano', from Latin 'platanus' (plane tree), influenced by resemblance of broad leaves.
