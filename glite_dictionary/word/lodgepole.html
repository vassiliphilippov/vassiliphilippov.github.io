lodgepole

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a type of pine tree (Pinus contorta) found in the coastal northwestern United States, characterized by two needles per cluster and bark fissured into small squares; also known as lodgepole pine.
Northwestern US pine tree species

Examples

The forest was dominated by lodgepole and ponderosa pines, stretching as far as the eye could see.
Wildfires play a natural role in the regeneration of lodgepole across the region.
Native Americans traditionally used lodgepole trunks to build their tipi frames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodgepole pine - common name for the tree Pinus contorta

Etymology

From the use of its wood by Native Americans to build the framework for lodges.
