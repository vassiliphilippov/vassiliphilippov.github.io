Hare Krishna

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːreɪ ˈkrɪʃ.nə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːreɪ ˈkrɪʃ.nə / (BrE)
a member of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, who worships Krishna according to the group's teachings
member of Krishna worship group (ISKCON)

Examples

He became a Hare Krishna after traveling to India.
Hare Krishnas are often seen chanting in public squares.
Her uncle joined the Hare Krishna movement in the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Krishna - the Hindu god worshipped as the eighth avatar of Vishnu

Etymology

From Sanskrit, 'Hare' (O Lord) and 'Krishna' (the supreme being), used in the society's primary chant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːrɪ ˈkrɪʃnə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːri ˈkrɪʃnə / (BrE)
a member of a religious movement founded in the United States in 1966, based on Hindu Vedic scriptures, whose followers chant the name of Krishna and practice vegetarianism and celibacy
member of a Hindu-inspired religious movement

Examples

The city streets filled with Hare Krishna devotees singing and dancing in colorful robes.
Curious tourists watched as a group of Hare Krishna members chanted their mantra in the park.
He became a Hare Krishna in college and adopted their vegetarian lifestyle.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Hare' (addressing the divine energy of Krishna) + 'Krishna' (a major Hindu deity); popularized in English after 1966.
