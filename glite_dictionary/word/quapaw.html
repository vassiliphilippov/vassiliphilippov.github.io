Quapaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.pɔː / (AmE), / ˈkwɑː.pɔː / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people of the Siouan family living in the Arkansas river valley in Arkansas.
a member of a Native American people of Arkansas

Examples

The Quapaw were known for their skillful farming along the Arkansas River.
A descendant of the Quapaw people shared stories at the cultural event.
During the 1800s, many Quapaw relocated to present-day Oklahoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

From the Siouan language, self-designation meaning 'downstream people'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌpɔ / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːˌpɔː / (BrE)
the Dhegiha Sioux dialect spoken by the Quapaw people, native to the Arkansas region.
Dhegiha Sioux dialect of the Quapaw people

Examples

The Quapaw language is critically endangered, with few fluent speakers remaining.
Linguists have documented the unique grammar of Quapaw to aid revitalization efforts.
He devoted years to studying Quapaw and recording oral histories in the dialect.

Etymology

From the Quapaw people's name for themselves, from their own Siouan language.
