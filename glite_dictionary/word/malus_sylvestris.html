Malus sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪləs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪləs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
a species of wild crab apple native to Europe, considered a primary ancestor of cultivated apple varieties
wild European crab apple tree

Examples

Botanists have long studied Malus sylvestris as a key ancestor of cultivated apple trees.
The hedgerows of Europe are often dotted with Malus sylvestris in the spring, covered in delicate blossoms.
Wildlife relies on the fruit of Malus sylvestris for food during the autumn months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit of the apple tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'malus' (apple tree) and 'sylvestris' (of the woods, wild).
