catamount

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬əˌmaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkætəmaʊnt / (BrE)
A wild feline, such as a cougar or lynx, typically with a short tail and tufted ears, valued for its fur.
wildcat such as a cougar or lynx

Examples

The catamount is stealthy and rarely seen by hikers in the mountains.
Hunters once pursued the catamount for its valuable fur.
Legends abound about the fearsome catamount prowling the forests at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cougar - a large wild cat native to the Americas
  • Synonym: lynx - a wild cat with tufted ears and a short tail

Etymology

Contraction of 'cat of the mountain.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬əˌmaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈkætəmaʊnt / (BrE)
a large wild feline of North America, especially the cougar or puma
large North American wild feline (cougar, puma)

Examples

Early settlers in the region feared the catamount as much as wolves.
The catamount prowls the forests and mountains in search of prey.
Several legends in American folklore tell of encounters with a fierce catamount.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cougar - a large American wild cat (also called mountain lion or puma)
  • Synonym: mountain lion - another name for cougar or puma
  • Synonym: puma - another name for cougar or mountain lion

Etymology

Shortened from 'catamountain,' from Old French 'cat' + 'mountain.'
