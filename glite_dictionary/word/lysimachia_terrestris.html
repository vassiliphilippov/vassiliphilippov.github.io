Lysimachia terrestris

1. NOUN

/ laɪsɪˈmeɪkiə tərˈrestrɪs / (AmE), / laɪsɪˈmeɪkɪə tɛˈrestrɪs / (BrE)
A North American plant with spikes of yellow flowers, typically found in wet habitats.
North American plant with yellow flower spikes

Examples

Lysimachia terrestris thrives in marshes and along the edges of freshwater ponds.
The distinctive yellow spikes of Lysimachia terrestris make it easy to spot during summer walks.
Gardeners sometimes plant Lysimachia terrestris to attract pollinators to wetland gardens.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Lysimachia' and species epithet 'terrestris' meaning 'of the earth, terrestrial'.
