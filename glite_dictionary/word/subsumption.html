subsumption

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of including something within a broader or more general category.
inclusion under a broader category

Examples

The subsumption of individual facts under a general rule is a key part of scientific reasoning.
In legal contexts, subsumption helps determine how specific cases fit under existing laws.
Their classification system relies on the subsumption of rare languages into larger language families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclusion - the act of including something or someone
  • Antonym: exclusion - the act of leaving something or someone out

Etymology

From Late Latin subsumptio (a taking under), from Latin sub (under) and sumere (to take).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
(Logic) The minor premise in a syllogism that contains the subject of the conclusion.
minor premise in a syllogism

Examples

In traditional logic, the subsumption is the premise that pairs the subject with a general class.
Philosophy students practiced identifying the subsumption in various syllogistic arguments.
The professor explained how the subsumption relates to the other parts of a syllogism.

Etymology

From Late Latin subsumptio (a taking up), from Latin sub (under) + sumere (to take).
