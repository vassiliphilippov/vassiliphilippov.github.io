Ajaia

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒaɪ.ə / (AmE), / əˈdʒaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds in the family Threskiornithidae, commonly known as spoonbills, especially found in the Americas.
a genus of spoonbill birds

Examples

The roseate spoonbill belongs to the genus Ajaia, known for its distinctive pink feathers.
Scientists observed a flock of Ajaia feeding in the wetlands of Florida.
This birdwatching guide contains detailed information about the Ajaia species found in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platalea - a genus of spoonbill birds closely related to Ajaia

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʒaɪ.ə / (AmE), / əˈdʒaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Threskiornithidae, which includes the roseate spoonbill.
genus of wading birds (roseate spoonbill)

Examples

The roseate spoonbill, classified under the genus Ajaia, is known for its striking pink plumage.
Scientists recently updated the taxonomy of birds in the genus Ajaia based on genetic evidence.
Members of the genus Ajaia are found in marshes and coastal regions in the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platalea - a related genus of spoonbills in the same family as Ajaia
