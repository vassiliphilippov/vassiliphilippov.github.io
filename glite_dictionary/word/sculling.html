sculling

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport or activity of rowing a boat with a pair of small oars, typically by a single rower in a racing shell.
rowing a boat with two oars by one person

Examples

She began sculling early in the morning, gliding quickly across the lake's smooth surface.
Olympic athletes often compete in both sculling and sweep rowing events.
Proper technique is essential for speed and efficiency in sculling competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowing - the sport or activity of propelling a boat with oars

Etymology

From 'scull' (a type of oar or to row with oars), possibly from Middle English 'sculle', of uncertain origin.
