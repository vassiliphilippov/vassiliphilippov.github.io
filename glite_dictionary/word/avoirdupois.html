avoirdupois

1. NOUN

/ ˌævɚ.dəˈpɔɪz / (AmE), / ˌæv.ə.djuːˈpwɑːz / (BrE)
a system of weights based on a 16-ounce pound, used chiefly in the United States and Britain for most commodities except precious metals, stones, and medicines
system of weights (16-ounce pound)

Examples

Many groceries in the US are sold by avoirdupois weight rather than by the metric system.
The avoirdupois system is commonly used to measure body weight in the United States.
Gold and silver are typically weighed using the troy system instead of avoirdupois.

Etymology

From Old French avoir de pois (goods of weight), from avoir (to have) + pois (weight).
