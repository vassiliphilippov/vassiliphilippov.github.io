shortage

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔr.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a situation in which there is not enough of something that is needed or expected
a lack or deficiency of something needed

Examples

There is a serious shortage of nurses in the hospital.
The water shortage forced the community to impose strict conservation measures.
A shortage of raw materials delayed the production process.

Synset Examples

new blood vessels bud out from the already dilated vascular bed to make up the nutritional deficit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed
  • Synonym: deficit - the amount by which something is too small

Etymology

From short + -age, modelled after French pénurie and similar words.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrt.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
A situation in which there is not enough of something that is needed.
a lack or insufficiency of something

Examples

There was a severe shortage of food during the drought.
The company had to close several factories due to a labor shortage.
A water shortage led the government to impose strict conservation measures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed
  • Synonym: deficit - the amount by which something is too small

Etymology

From short + -age, first used in late 19th century English.
