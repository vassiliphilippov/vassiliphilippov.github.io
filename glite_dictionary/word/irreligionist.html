irreligionist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrɪˈlɪdʒənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈlɪdʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who is indifferent or hostile to religion
person indifferent or hostile to religion

Examples

The debate included an irreligionist who questioned the role of faith in public life.
Some argue that an irreligionist perspective is necessary for a truly secular government.
She never hid her views as an outspoken irreligionist in a deeply religious community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheist - a person who does not believe in the existence of God or gods
  • Antonym: religionist - a person who is strongly religious or committed to a religion

Etymology

From 'irreligion' (lack of religion) + '-ist' (one who practices or is concerned with something).
