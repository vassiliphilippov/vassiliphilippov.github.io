option

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
something that you can choose from a set of possibilities; a choice or alternative.
a choice or alternative

Examples

We were given the option of working from home or coming into the office.
The only option left was to start over from scratch.
After reading the menu, she realized that the vegetarian option sounded best.

Synset Examples

what option did I have?
there is no other alternative
my only choice is to refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternative - another possibility or choice
  • Synonym: choice - the act of choosing or the option selected
  • Antonym: necessity - something that is unavoidable or required, opposite of optional

Etymology

From Latin 'optio' (the power of choice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ʃən / (BrE)
a legal right to buy or sell property or securities at a specified price within a set period, the right being purchased and forfeited if not exercised by the expiration date
right to buy/sell at set price (finance)

Examples

She purchased an option on 100 shares of the company at $50 each.
The option expires at the end of the month if it is not exercised.
Investors use an option to hedge against potential losses in the stock market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derivative - a financial security whose value depends on an underlying asset or group of assets
  • Degree Higher: future - a legal agreement to buy or sell a particular commodity or financial instrument at a predetermined price at a specified time in the future

Etymology

From Latin optio (free choice or wish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13262498-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
the act of choosing or the power or freedom to choose between alternatives
the act of choosing; a choice

Examples

You always have the option to decline the invitation if you're not interested.
She weighed each option carefully before making a decision.
Taking the train is a convenient option for commuters.

Synset Examples

your choice of colors was unfortunate
you can take your pick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - an act of selecting or making a decision
  • Synonym: selection - the action or fact of carefully choosing someone or something as being the best or most suitable

Etymology

From Latin 'optio', meaning 'free choice or wish'.
