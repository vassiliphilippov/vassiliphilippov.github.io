Xenorhyncus

1. NOUN

/ ˌziːnəˈraɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌzɛnəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large storks native to East India and Australia.
genus of storks (East India, Australia)

Examples

The Xenorhyncus is recognized for its long legs and striking appearance.
Birdwatchers in Australia often hope to spot a rare Xenorhyncus during the migration season.
Researchers have studied the habitat preferences of the Xenorhyncus in tropical wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged wading bird of the family Ciconiidae

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' (strange) + 'rhynchos' (beak).
