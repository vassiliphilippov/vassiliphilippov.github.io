Mullidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as goatfishes or red mullets, characterized by their barbels and found chiefly in tropical and subtropical waters.
a family of marine fish (goatfishes, red mullets)

Examples

Species belonging to the Mullidae family are often sought after for their delicate flavor.
Marine biologists discovered several new Mullidae in the coral reefs of the Pacific.
The Mullidae are easily recognized by the barbels on their chins, used to search for food on the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goatfish - a common name for any fish in the Mullidae family
  • Synonym: red mullet - a common name for several species in the Mullidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mullidae', based on Latin 'mullus' (a type of fish, red mullet).
