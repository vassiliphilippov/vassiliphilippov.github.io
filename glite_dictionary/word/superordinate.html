superordinate

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈɔːrdənət / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
of higher rank, status, or value than others; superior in a hierarchy or order
of higher rank or status

Examples

In the military, a general is superordinate to a colonel.
The superordinate category 'animal' includes mammals, birds, and reptiles.
In the company structure, managers have a superordinate role compared to their teams.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinate - of lower rank or status; under the authority of another
  • Synonym: superior - higher in rank, status, or quality

Etymology

From Latin superordinatus, past participle of superordinare (to arrange above).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈɔːr.dən.ət / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈrɔː.dɪ.nət / (BrE)
a person or thing of higher rank, status, or quality compared to others in a hierarchy or classification
person or thing of higher rank or status

Examples

The manager acted as the superordinate in all major decision-making processes.
In the animal kingdom, a lion may be considered a superordinate in its environment.
Each department has its own superordinate who oversees the team's activities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinate - a person or thing of lower rank or status

Etymology

From Latin superordinatus, past participle of superordinare ('to set above'): super- ('above') + ordinare ('to arrange').

3. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈɔːr.dən.ət / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pərˈɔː.dɪ.nət / (BrE)
a word whose meaning includes that of another, more specific word; a more generic term.
a more generic word; general class term

Examples

In the relationship between 'animal' and 'dog', 'animal' is the superordinate.
The word 'fruit' is a superordinate to 'apple', 'banana', and 'pear'.
Teachers often use a superordinate to help students categorize new vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyponym - a word of more specific meaning than a general or superordinate term

Etymology

From Latin superordinatus, past participle of superordinare (to set over or above).

4. VERB

/ suːˈpɚˌɔrdəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˈɔːdɪneɪt / (BrE)
to place something in a higher order or rank in comparison to others
to rank as superior or higher

Examples

The classification system superordinates mammals over reptiles.
In some traditions, the priestly class is superordinated to the laity.
Linguists often superordinate broad categories over specific subgroups.

Synset Examples

These two notions are superordinated to a third

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinate - to place in a lower or inferior rank or class

Etymology

From Latin 'superordinare' (to arrange above), from 'super-' (above) + 'ordinare' (to order).
