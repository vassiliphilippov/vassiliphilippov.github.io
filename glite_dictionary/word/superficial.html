superficial

1. ADJ

/ ˌsupɚˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
existing or occurring at or on the surface, rather than deep or thorough; relating to the outer layer.
existing on or near the surface

Examples

The knife left only a superficial cut on his arm, which healed quickly.
After repainting, the superficial area of the wall looked much brighter.
The technician took superficial measurements before starting more detailed analysis.

Synset Examples

superficial measurements
the superficial area of the wall
a superficial wound

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deep - extending far down from the top or surface

Etymology

From Latin 'superficialis', from 'superficies' (surface).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02816313-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
Not considering or dealing with anything in depth; only concerned with what is apparent or obvious, and lacking thoroughness, depth, or serious thought.
not thorough; only surface-level

Examples

Her analysis of the novel was disappointingly superficial, missing the underlying themes and deeper meanings.
Although the injury appeared superficial, the doctor insisted on a full examination to ensure there was no internal damage.
He was criticized for offering only superficial comments during the important meeting.

Synset Examples

superficial similarities
a superficial mind
his thinking was superficial and fuzzy
superficial knowledge
the superficial report didn't give the true picture
only superficial differences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deep - involving or showing thoughtful consideration or thorough understanding
  • Synonym: shallow - lacking depth of intellect or knowledge

Etymology

From Latin 'superficialis', from 'superficies' meaning 'surface'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01879374-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
Not studying or dealing with something thoroughly; lacking depth or attention to detail.
not thorough; lacking depth

Examples

Her superficial analysis of the report missed several important issues.
The investigation was superficial and did not uncover the real cause of the accident.
Although he appeared confident, his understanding of the topic was actually quite superficial.

Synset Examples

a casual (or cursory) inspection failed to reveal the house's structural flaws
a passing glance
perfunctory courtesy
In his paper, he showed a very superficial understanding of psychoanalytic theory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cursory - hasty and not thorough
  • Antonym: thorough - done carefully and completely
  • Synonym: perfunctory - done quickly without much care

Etymology

From Latin superficialis, from superficies (surface).

4. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
(of wounds, injuries, or conditions) occurring on or near the surface of the skin; not deep or serious.
on or near the skin's surface

Examples

The patient suffered only superficial burns on his hands and face.
A cut can appear severe but may be just a superficial injury.
She was relieved to learn her son's scrape was superficial and did not require stitches.

Synset Examples

superficial burns
superficial facial injuries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deep - extending far below the surface, especially of wounds or injuries

Etymology

From Latin superfacialis, from super ('above') + facies ('face, surface').

Source: WordNet

5. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
Not having or showing any depth of understanding or importance; minor or insignificant.
minor or insignificant; lacking depth

Examples

Their criticism was dismissed as superficial and not worthy of serious attention.
While the report suggested a few superficial changes, it ignored the main issues.
He gave a superficial explanation that failed to address the core of the problem.

Synset Examples

a few superficial editorial changes
only trivial objections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin superficiālis (‘of or pertaining to the surface’), from superficies (‘surface’).
