to remold

1. VERB

/ riˈmoʊld / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊld / (BrE)
to give new treads to a tire by reshaping or recasting it
fit new treads to a tire

Examples

Many companies now remold old tires to reduce waste and save money.
The factory specializes in remolding truck tires for extended use.
After thousands of miles, the tire was remolded to restore its traction.

Etymology

From re- (again) + mold (to shape or form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163880-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈmoʊld / (AmE), / ˌriːˈməʊld / (BrE)
to shape something again or in a different way
shape again or differently

Examples

She decided to remold the clay figure into a new sculpture.
The company must remold its strategies to survive in a changing market.
Past experiences can remold a person's outlook on life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reshape - to shape again or differently
  • Synonym: mold - to form or shape

Etymology

From re- (again) + mold (to shape, from Old French mouldier, from Latin modulus, measure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01664764-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈmoʊld / (AmE), / ˌriːˈməʊld / (BrE)
to shape or cast something again, especially after it has been damaged or requires alteration
to cast or shape again

Examples

After the statue was damaged, the artists decided to remold it from scratch.
The company will remold the old tires into new products.
If the original part is misshapen, you can always remold the material.

Synset Examples

The bell cracked and had to be recast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recast - to cast again; to form something again in a new shape or arrangement

Etymology

From re- (again) + mold (to shape or form).
