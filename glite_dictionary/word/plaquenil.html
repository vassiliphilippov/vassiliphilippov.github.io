Plaquenil

1. NOUN

/ ˈplækwənɪl / (AmE), / ˈplækwɪnɪl / (BrE)
a trademarked anti-inflammatory drug (hydroxychloroquine) used to treat rheumatoid arthritis, malaria, and lupus erythematosus
a drug for arthritis, malaria, and lupus
medical american

Examples

The doctor prescribed Plaquenil to help manage her lupus symptoms.
Some patients taking Plaquenil need regular eye exams due to possible side effects.
Treatment with Plaquenil can reduce joint pain in rheumatoid arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxychloroquine - the generic name for Plaquenil, an anti-malarial and anti-rheumatic drug
