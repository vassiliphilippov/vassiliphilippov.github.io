Prokayotae

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkæjoʊti / (AmE), / prəʊˈkæɪəʊtiː / (BrE)
A former kingdom in biological taxonomy, comprising prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria and blue-green algae that lack a distinct nucleus.
kingdom of prokaryotic organisms

Examples

The kingdom Prokayotae includes many microscopic organisms such as bacteria.
Scientists debated the classification criteria for Prokayotae throughout the 20th century.
Recent discoveries have led to a better understanding of the diversity within Prokayotae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteria - unicellular microorganisms lacking a nucleus
  • Synonym: prokaryote - an organism whose cells lack a nucleus
  • Antonym: Eukaryotae - organisms with cells containing a nucleus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek pro- (before) + karyon (nut, kernel, nucleus), referring to lack of a nucleus.
