Azolla

1. NOUN

/ əˈzɑːlə / (AmE), / əˈzɒlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, floating aquatic ferns often found in ponds and wetlands, sometimes placed in its own family (Azollaceae).
genus of small aquatic ferns

Examples

Azolla is often used as a biofertilizer in rice paddies due to its ability to fix nitrogen.
Farmers introduced Azolla into their waterlogged fields to improve soil fertility.
The surface of the pond was covered with vibrant green clusters of Azolla.

Etymology

From the New Latin, based on Greek 'azo' (to dry) and 'olla' (to kill), referring to the plant's intolerance of dry conditions.

2. NOUN

/ əˈzɑːlə / (AmE), / əˈzɒlə / (BrE)
a genus of small aquatic ferns in the family Salviniaceae, often floating on the surface of fresh water; sometimes placed in its own family Azollaceae
a genus of aquatic ferns

Examples

The water surface was covered in a thick layer of Azolla that provided shade for the fish below.
Researchers studied the rapid growth of Azolla in nutrient-rich ponds.
Certain species of Azolla are used in rice paddies to fix nitrogen and improve soil fertility.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Azolla, name given by botanists; possibly from Greek a- (not) + zōllē (dry land).
