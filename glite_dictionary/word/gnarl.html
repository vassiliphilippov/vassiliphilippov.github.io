gnarl

1. VERB

/ ɡnɑrl / (AmE), / ɡnɑːl / (BrE)
to twist or contort into a knotted or deformed shape, especially referring to wood or trees
twist into a deformed shape

Examples

Centuries of harsh weather have gnarled the branches of the ancient oak.
Arthritis can gnarl a person’s fingers over time.
The fierce winds gnarled the saplings along the coast, giving them a striking, twisted appearance.

Synset Examples

The wind has gnarled this old tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to form into a bent, curling, or distorted shape
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01283411-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ nɑrl / (AmE), / nɑːl / (BrE)
a twisted, knotted, or swollen part, especially in wood or in the shape of a body part
twisted or knotted part (wood, muscle, etc.)

Examples

The old oak tree was full of gnarls where branches had once grown.
His hands were covered in gnarls from years of hard labor.
A large gnarl protruded from the trunk, giving the tree a unique shape.

Synset Examples

their muscles stood out in knots
the old man's fists were two great gnarls
his stomach was in knots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - a hard, cross-grained mass of wood where a branch was; a lump or knob

Synset POS: n
