Blennius

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛniəs / (AmE), / ˈblɛniəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes known as typical blennies, which are the type genus of the family Blenniidae.
genus of marine fishes (blennies)

Examples

The species within the genus Blennius are commonly found in rocky coastal waters.
Many aquarists are fascinated by the appearance of Blennius in their marine tanks.
The typical Blennius displays a scaleless, elongated body adapted for hiding among rocks.

Etymology

From Latin 'blennius', from Greek 'blennos' (mucus), referring to the fish's slimy coating.
