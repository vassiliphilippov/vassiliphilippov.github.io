Gymnorhina tibicen

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nɔːrˈhaɪ.nə tɪˈbaɪ.sən / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nɔːˈriː.nə tɪˈbɪ.sən / (BrE)
a crow-sized black-and-white Australian bird known for its melodious call and ability to mimic sounds
Australian black-and-white songbird

Examples

The distinctive carol of the Gymnorhina tibicen is a familiar sound in Australian suburbs.
Researchers studied the vocal mimicry abilities of Gymnorhina tibicen in various environments.
Many bird watchers visit Australia hoping to catch a glimpse of the striking Gymnorhina tibicen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - common name for Gymnorhina tibicen and related birds

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Gymnorhina' and species name 'tibicen' meaning 'piper' or 'flute-player' in Latin.
