Centropristis philadelphica

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˌtroʊˈprɪstɪs ˌfɪləˈdɛlfɪkə / (AmE), / sɛnˌtrɒˈprɪstɪs ˌfɪləˈdɛlfɪkə / (BrE)
A species of sea bass found in the western Atlantic Ocean, recognized for its importance in marine ecology and fisheries.
a species of sea bass

Examples

The Centropristis philadelphica is commonly studied by marine biologists in the Atlantic region.
Local fishermen occasionally catch Centropristis philadelphica among other types of sea bass.
Due to overfishing, populations of Centropristis philadelphica may require careful monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - a common name for various marine fish in the family Serranidae

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Centropristis + Latin philadelphica (of Philadelphia, indicating locality).
