profane

1. VERB

/ prəˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
to corrupt morally, especially through indulgence in sensual pleasures or intemperance
corrupt morally by intemperance or sensuality

Examples

He was accused of trying to profane the youth with his radical ideas.
The decadent lifestyle threatened to profane even the most upright citizens.
Some claimed that unchecked luxury could easily profane their society’s moral values.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make morally depraved or wicked
  • Synonym: debauch - to lead into a life of depravity, moral corruption

Etymology

From Latin profanare (to desecrate, to treat unworthily), from profanus (unholy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02585532-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ proʊˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect for sacred things; irreverent or blasphemous toward what is held to be holy
irreverent toward sacred things

Examples

The comedian's profane jokes offended several members of the audience.
Some critics considered the artist's work profane for its use of religious imagery.
Entering the temple with muddy boots was viewed as a profane act by the locals.

Synset Examples

blasphemous rites of a witches' Sabbath
profane utterances against the Church
it is sacrilegious to enter with shoes on

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sacred - regarded with reverence and respect, often due to religious significance

Etymology

From Latin 'profanus' (not sacred, outside the temple).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02019844-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ proʊˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
not related to or concerned with religion; secular rather than sacred
not religious; secular

Examples

The museum features both sacred and profane artworks from the Renaissance.
The festival has a profane atmosphere, with little reference to any religious traditions.
She wrote a book on the differences between profane and religious education.

Synset Examples

sacred and profane music
secular drama
secular architecture
children being brought up in an entirely profane environment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sacred - connected with God or a deity; religious and holy
  • Synonym: secular - not connected with spiritual or religious matters

Etymology

From Latin profanus (not sacred, outside the temple).

4. ADJ

/ prəˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
(of a place, object, or act) not holy because not consecrated, or considered impure or defiled; secular rather than religious.
not holy; unconsecrated or impure

Examples

The temple grounds were considered off-limits to all profane activities.
The priest warned against bringing profane things into the holy sanctuary.
Some believed that the abandoned church had become a profane place.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sacred - regarded with reverence; holy

Etymology

From Latin profanus (not sacred, unholy), from pro- (before) + fanum (temple).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02062330-s
Synset POS: s

5. VERB

/ proʊˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
to treat something sacred with irreverence or disrespect; to violate the sanctity of a place, object, or language
treat sacred things with disrespect

Examples

The vandals attempted to profane the ancient temple with graffiti.
It was considered a grave offense to profane the name of their deity.
Some people believe that careless language can profane cultural traditions.

Synset Examples

desecrate a cemetery
violate the sanctity of the church
profane the name of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
  • Synonym: to blaspheme - to speak irreverently about God or sacred things

Etymology

From Latin profanus (outside the temple, not sacred).

Source: WordNet

6. ADJ

/ prəˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
(of language or behaviour) characterized by profanity, irreverence, or obscene language; not respectful of religious practice or the sacred.
irreverent or obscene language/behavior

Examples

The comedian's profane jokes drew both laughter and criticism from the audience.
She was scolded for using profane language in the classroom.
Some people find certain song lyrics too profane for radio play.

Synset Examples

foul-mouthed and blasphemous
blue language
profane words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasphemous - showing disrespect to God or sacred things
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive to moral principles; indecent
  • Antonym: sacred - connected with God or religion and deserving respect

Etymology

From Latin profanus (unholy, not sacred), from pro- (before) + fanum (temple).
