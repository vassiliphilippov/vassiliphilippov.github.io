Potamogeton gramineus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˈdʒitɒn ɡræˈmiːniəs / (AmE), / pəˌtæməˈdʒiːtɒn ɡræˈmiːniəs / (BrE)
a species of aquatic plant found in Europe (except the Mediterranean area) and the northern United States, commonly known as grass-leaved pondweed.
a species of aquatic plant (grass-leaved pondweed)

Examples

The clear water of the lake was dotted with patches of Potamogeton gramineus.
Researchers studied the distribution of Potamogeton gramineus in northern U.S. wetlands.
Many fish species rely on Potamogeton gramineus as a source of shelter and food.

Etymology

From Latin 'Potamogeton' (from Greek potamos 'river' + geiton 'neighbor') and 'gramineus' meaning 'grassy'.
