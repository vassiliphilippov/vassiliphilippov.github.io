uncommon

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑmən / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒmən / (BrE)
not often found, seen, or experienced; unusual or remarkable in nature or degree
not common; unusual or remarkable

Examples

These flowers are uncommon in most gardens but thrive in tropical climates.
His generosity was truly uncommon among business leaders of his time.
Finding an uncommon coin in your change can be quite exciting.

Synset Examples

uncommon birds
frost and floods are uncommon during these months
doing an uncommon amount of business
an uncommon liking for money
he owed his greatest debt to his mother's uncommon character and ability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rare - not occurring very often; uncommon
  • Antonym: common - occurring frequently; usual

Etymology

From un- (not) + common, from Latin communis (common).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑmən / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒmən / (BrE)
remarkable for being unusual, especially because of being particularly excellent or extreme of its kind
remarkable or exceptional; not usual

Examples

Her kindness to strangers was truly uncommon and left a lasting impression on everyone she met.
He has an uncommon talent for solving complex mathematical problems.
The chef prepared the dish with an uncommon attention to detail, making it stand out from the rest.

Synset Examples

what is so rare as a day in June
a rare skill
an uncommon sense of humor
she was kind to an uncommon degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rare - unusually good or exceptional in quality
  • Antonym: common - occurring frequently or found in large numbers

Etymology

From un- (not) + common, from Latin communis (common, general).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01682771-s
Synset POS: s
