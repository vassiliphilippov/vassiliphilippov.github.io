colonnaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːləˈneɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈneɪdɪd / (BrE)
having a series of columns arranged at regular intervals, especially in architecture
having columns arranged in a row

Examples

The ancient temple featured a colonnaded courtyard that impressed every visitor.
A colonnaded walkway provided shade along the side of the historic building.
We sat beneath the colonnaded porch, admiring the sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcaded - having arches or covered walkways (arcades)
  • Synonym: columned - having columns; supported by columns

Etymology

From 'colonnade' + '-ed', where 'colonnade' comes from French 'colonnade', from 'colonne' (column).
