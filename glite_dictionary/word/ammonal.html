ammonal

1. NOUN

/ ˈæməˌnɑl / (AmE), / ˈamənal / (BrE)
An explosive composed of ammonium nitrate and aluminum powder, used especially in mining and military applications.
type of explosive compound

Examples

The engineers filled the tunnel with ammonal to demolish the bridge.
Miners carefully transported barrels of ammonal for the controlled blasts.
Because of its high energy, ammonal was historically favored for military mining.

Synset Examples

Ammonal used for military mining purposes was generally contained within metal cans or rubberised bags to prevent moisture ingress problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TNT - a common high explosive, trinitrotoluene

Etymology

Blend of 'ammonium' (from ammonium nitrate) and 'aluminum.'
