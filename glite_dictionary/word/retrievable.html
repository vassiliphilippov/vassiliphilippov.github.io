retrievable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtriːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be recovered, regained, or fetched, especially with effort or difficulty.
able to be recovered or regained

Examples

Thanks to the backup, all the lost data is now retrievable.
The documents are retrievable from the archives if you know where to look.
Some memories are easily retrievable, while others fade away over time.

Synset Examples

he believed the information was retrievable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irretrievable - unable to be recovered or regained
  • Synonym: recoverable - able to be recovered or regained

Etymology

From 'retrieve' + suffix '-able', indicating possibility or capability.
