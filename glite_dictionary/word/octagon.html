octagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑktəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈɒktəɡən / (BrE)
A flat shape with eight straight sides and eight angles; an eight-sided polygon.
eight-sided polygon

Examples

The stop sign is shaped like an octagon to help drivers recognize it quickly.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the area of a regular octagon.
The mixed martial arts fights are held in a cage called the octagon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polygon - a flat shape with three or more straight sides
  • Degree Lower: hexagon - a six-sided polygon
  • Degree Higher: decagon - a ten-sided polygon

Etymology

From Greek oktágōnos (eight-angled), from oktá (eight) + gōnia (angle).
