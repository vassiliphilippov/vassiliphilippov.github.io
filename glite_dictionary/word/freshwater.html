freshwater

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is not salty, especially as found in lakes, rivers, and streams, suitable for drinking or agriculture
water that is not salty

Examples

Fish that live in freshwater environments require specific conditions to thrive.
The reservoir provides freshwater for the entire city.
Many species cannot survive if freshwater sources become contaminated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saltwater - water that contains a significant amount of salt, especially sea water

Etymology

Old English 'fersc wæter' (fresh water), 'fersc' meaning not salty.
