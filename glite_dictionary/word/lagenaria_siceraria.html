Lagenaria siceraria

1. NOUN

/ ləˌgɛnɛˈɛriə sɪsəˈrɛriə / (AmE), / ləˌɡɛnərɪə sɪsəˈrɑːrɪə / (BrE)
An Old World climbing plant with hard-shelled, bottle-shaped gourds, often used for utensils or musical instruments; also called the bottle gourd.
bottle gourd plant

Examples

Lagenaria siceraria is widely cultivated for its edible gourds in many parts of Asia and Africa.
The dried fruits of Lagenaria siceraria can be fashioned into water bottles and ladles.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity of Lagenaria siceraria across different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle gourd - another common name for Lagenaria siceraria

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Lagenaria* (from *lagena*, 'flask') and species name *siceraria* (from *sicera*, 'strong drink').
