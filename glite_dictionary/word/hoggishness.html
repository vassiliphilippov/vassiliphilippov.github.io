hoggishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
An excessive or greedy desire for food; gluttonous behavior.
excessive greed for food

Examples

His hoggishness at the buffet embarrassed his friends.
She was criticized for her hoggishness when she took all the desserts for herself.
The hoggishness of his appetite was unmatched at the dinner table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food

Etymology

From 'hoggish' (like a hog) + '-ness', meaning the state or quality of.
