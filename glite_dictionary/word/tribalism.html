tribalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.bə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.bə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The state or condition of living together as organized tribes, or strong loyalty to one's own tribe or social group.
state of living as a tribe

Examples

Some anthropologists study tribalism to understand early human societies.
The rise of tribalism can sometimes lead to tensions between different communities.
Historical tribalism shaped social structures in many ancient regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clannishness - strong association or loyalty to one's own group or clan

Etymology

From tribe + -al + -ism, indicating a system or condition based on tribes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.bəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.bəl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the way of life, organization, or beliefs characteristic of a tribe, especially the strong loyalty to one's own group.
beliefs and ways of a tribe

Examples

The rise of tribalism can be seen in communities that value allegiance to their group above all else.
Anthropologists have studied how tribalism affects decision-making in traditional societies.
Modern politics sometimes displays a resurgence of tribalism in the form of strong in-group loyalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of close-knit and interrelated families

Etymology

From tribe + -alism, formed in English in the 19th century.
