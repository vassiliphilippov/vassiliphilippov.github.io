relative

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
a person connected to you by blood or marriage
a family member by blood or marriage

Examples

The hospital staff tried to contact his relative after the accident.
She spent the holidays visiting every relative she could find.
As her only living relative, he inherited everything.

Synset Examples

police are searching for relatives of the deceased
he has distant relations back in New Jersey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relation - a person connected by blood or marriage

Etymology

From Late Latin *relativus* (having reference or relation to), from Latin *relatus* (past participle of *referre*, to refer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10255246-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
considered in comparison with something else, especially in amount, size, or degree; proportional to something.
considered in comparison to something else

Examples

Her success is impressive when viewed in a relative context.
The importance of the change is relative to its impact on society.
Their profit looks large, but it's quite small relative to their investment.

Synset Examples

the punishment ought to be proportional to the crime
earnings relative to production

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolute - not compared to anything else; not relative
  • Synonym: proportional - having a constant relation in degree or number to something else

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation), from 'relatus', past participle of 'referre' (to refer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00484662-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
an animal or plant related to another by common descent or classification, such as belonging to the same family or genus
related animal or plant

Examples

The domestic dog is a close relative of the gray wolf.
Many vegetables in your garden may be distant relatives of wild species.
The African elephant and the mammoth are evolutionary relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kin - an animal, plant, or person related by blood or descent
  • Antonym: unrelated - not connected or not having a relationship by descent or classification

Etymology

From Latin relativus (having relation), from referre (to refer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01330943-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
considered in relation to something else or in comparison with other things, rather than on its own; not absolute or complete
measured by comparison; not absolute

Examples

His success is only relative compared to others in his field.
Whether the room is big or small is a relative concept.
Our perception of comfort is often relative to what we're used to.

Synset Examples

a relative stranger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolute - not dependent on or compared with anything else; complete and total

Etymology

From Latin *relativus* (having reference or relation), from *referre* (to refer).

Source: WordNet
