Clupea harengus

1. NOUN

/ kluˈpiə həˈrɛŋɡəs / (AmE), / kluːˈpiːə həˈrɛŋɡəs / (BrE)
a commercially important herring of northern Atlantic and Pacific waters, widely used for food
a herring species, important food fish

Examples

The commercial catch of Clupea harengus supports many fishing communities in northern Europe.
Smoked Clupea harengus is a delicacy in many Scandinavian countries.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Clupea harengus to protect its populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish of the family Clupeidae, often used for food

Etymology

From the Latin genus name Clupea and species name harengus (herring).
