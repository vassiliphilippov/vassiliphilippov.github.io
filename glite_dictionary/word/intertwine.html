to intertwine

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind two or more things together so that they are linked or tangled
twist or wind together

Examples

The dancers' arms intertwined gracefully as they moved.
She likes to intertwine colorful threads when weaving her scarves.
Vines intertwined around the old wooden fence, covering it completely.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to form into a winding or spiral shape

Etymology

From inter- (between) + obsolete twine (to twist together), from Old English twinan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520294-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to make lacework or a pattern by knotting or looping threads together
make lacework by knotting or looping

Examples

She spent the afternoon intertwining threads to make delicate lace.
The lace was created by carefully intertwining fine, white fibers.
He learned to intertwine colorful strings into intricate patterns for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weave - to make fabric by interlacing threads

Etymology

From inter- (between, among) + twine (to twist together), from Old English 'twinan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01678439-v
Synset POS: v
