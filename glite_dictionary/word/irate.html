irate

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈreɪt / (AmE), / aɪˈreɪt / (BrE)
feeling or showing extreme anger
extremely angry

Examples

The customer became irate when her order was delayed for the third time.
An irate letter was sent to the company, demanding an explanation for the error.
The irate crowd shouted loudly at the decision made by the referee.

Synset Examples

irate protesters
ireful words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or agitation
  • Degree Higher: furious - extremely angry; full of rage

Etymology

From Latin 'iratus' (angry), past participle of 'irascī' (to be angry).
