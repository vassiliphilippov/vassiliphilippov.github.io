firedrake

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌdreɪk / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌdreɪk / (BrE)
a mythical dragon or fire-breathing creature from Germanic and Teutonic legends, typically depicted as a winged reptile
fire-breathing dragon of Teutonic mythology

Examples

The ancient legend spoke of a firedrake guarding treasure beneath the mountain.
Scholars debated whether the beast slain by the hero was a mere serpent or a true firedrake.
In the poem, a mighty firedrake emerges from its lair to wreak havoc on the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon - a large, serpentine legendary creature that appears in folklore

Etymology

From Old English *fȳrdraca* ('fȳr' meaning 'fire' + 'draca' meaning 'dragon').
