unmindful

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmaɪndfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmaɪndf(ə)l / (BrE)
not conscious of or not noticing something; not aware, especially in a way that shows a lack of attention or consideration (usually followed by 'of' or 'to').
not aware or attentive

Examples

He walked through the busy street, completely unmindful of the commotion around him.
She was unmindful of how her words might affect her friends.
The children played, unmindful to the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

oblivious of the mounting pressures for political reform
oblivious to the risks she ran
not unmindful of the heavy responsibility

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mindful - conscious or aware of something
  • Synonym: oblivious - not aware of or not concerned about what is happening

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'mindful' (attentive, aware).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmaɪn.fəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmaɪn.fəl / (BrE)
not giving attention or thought to something; inattentive or heedless.
not attentive; heedless

Examples

The driver was unmindful of the speed limit, risking a hefty fine.
She walked through the busy streets, unmindful of the noise around her.
He was so lost in thought that he remained unmindful of the rain soaking his clothes.

Synset Examples

while thus unmindful of his steps he stumbled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mindful - paying careful attention; aware
  • Synonym: heedless - showing a reckless lack of care or attention

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + mindful (attentive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01985067-a
Synset POS: a
