cursorily

1. ADV

/ ˈkɜr.sɚ.ɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.sər.ɪ.li / (BrE)
In a quick and superficial way, without paying close attention or taking care.
in a hasty or superficial manner

Examples

She glanced cursorily at the report before the meeting started.
The document was only cursorily reviewed, so many errors went unnoticed.
He cleaned the room cursorily, leaving dust in the corners.

Synset Examples

he looked cursorily through the magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficially - in a way that lacks depth or thoroughness
  • Antonym: thoroughly - in a detailed and careful manner

Etymology

From Latin 'cursorius' (running, hasty), from 'cursor' (runner).
