Tineola

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈnoʊlə / (AmE), / tɪˈniːələ / (BrE)
A genus of small moths, including the common clothes moth, whose larvae feed on wool, fur, and other animal fibers.
genus of clothes moths

Examples

The larvae of Tineola can cause severe damage to stored woolen garments.
Pest control experts often identify Tineola as the culprit when dealing with wardrobe infestations.
Proper storage techniques can help keep Tineola away from valuable textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothes moth - small moth whose larvae eat natural fibers, especially in clothing

Etymology

From modern Latin Tineola, diminutive of Latin tinea (moth, worm).

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈniː.ələ / (AmE), / tɪˈnɪə.lə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths known as webbing clothes moths, whose larvae are notorious for feeding on fabrics.
genus of moths that eat fabric

Examples

The larvae of Tineola are responsible for much of the damage found in old wardrobes.
Scientists studied the behavior of Tineola to develop better methods of moth control.
Infestations of Tineola can go unnoticed until valuable fabrics are already ruined.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tineidae - the family of moths to which Tineola belongs
  • Synonym: clothes moth - a common name for several species of moths that eat fabric, including Tineola

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'tinos' (moth) and Latin diminutive suffix '-ola'.
