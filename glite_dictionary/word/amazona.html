Amazona

1. NOUN

/ əˈmæzənə / (AmE), / əˈmazənə / (BrE)
A genus of parrots comprising the Amazon parrots, native to Central and South America, known for their vibrant plumage and loud calls.
genus of Amazon parrots

Examples

The Amazona genus includes several brightly colored parrot species found in South America.
Many Amazona parrots are popular pets due to their intelligence and ability to mimic sounds.
Ornithologists have identified over thirty species within the Amazona genus.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Spanish 'Amazonas' (the Amazon region), where these parrots are native.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæm.əˈzoʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˌam.əˈzəʊ.nə / (BrE)
a genus of parrots native to the Americas, known as Amazon parrots, characterized by their green plumage and vocal abilities
genus of Amazon parrots

Examples

The Amazona genus includes species such as the yellow-headed parrot and the blue-fronted parrot.
Many Amazona parrots are popular pets because of their intelligence and ability to mimic speech.
Researchers studied several wild Amazona populations in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird of the order Psittaciformes, often colorful and able to mimic sounds

Etymology

Modern Latin, from the Amazon region of South America, their native range.
