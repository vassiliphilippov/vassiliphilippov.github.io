unassisted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈsɪs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈsɪs.tɪd / (BrE)
Not helped or supported by anyone else.
not helped or supported by others

Examples

She managed to climb the stairs unassisted after her operation.
The team scored an unassisted goal in the final minutes of the match.
He completed the entire project unassisted, without any help from his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assisted - given help or support by someone else
  • Synonym: independent - not depending on others for support

Etymology

From un- (not) + assisted (helped).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈsɪs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈsɪs.tɪd / (BrE)
Not receiving or involving help from others; without assistance.
without help or assistance

Examples

She climbed the mountain unassisted, relying solely on her own skills.
The baby’s first unassisted steps were a proud moment for the family.
He completed the project unassisted, without any support from his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assisted - receiving help or support

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'assisted', past participle of 'assist'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02362685-a
Synset POS: a
