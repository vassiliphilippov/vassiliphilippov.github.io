phrasing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the choice and arrangement of words in speech or writing to express something; the way something is expressed.
the way words are chosen and arranged

Examples

He was careful with his phrasing to avoid offending anyone.
The phrasing in her letter made her intentions very clear.
Good phrasing can make even a complex idea easy to understand.

Synset Examples

use concise military verbiage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wording - the words used to express something
  • Synonym: formulation - the way in which something is formulated or expressed

Etymology

From 'phrase' + '-ing', where 'phrase' is from French 'phraser', rooted in Greek 'phrasis' (speech, way of speaking).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the way in which a sequence of musical notes is grouped and articulated to form musical phrases in a melodic line
grouping of musical phrases

Examples

The violinist's phrasing brought out the emotional depth of the melody.
Good phrasing can transform a simple tune into a moving musical performance.
The conductor praised her excellent phrasing during the slow movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulation - the clarity in the production of musical notes and phrases

Etymology

From 'phrase' (sentence, expression) + -ing (action, process).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01014550-n
Synset POS: n
