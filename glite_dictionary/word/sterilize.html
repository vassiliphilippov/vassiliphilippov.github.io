to sterilize

1. VERB

/ ˈstɛr.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something free from bacteria or other living microorganisms, usually by using heat, chemicals, or radiation
make free from bacteria
medical

Examples

Hospitals routinely sterilize their surgical instruments to prevent infection.
You can sterilize baby bottles by boiling them in water for several minutes.
After the experiment, all the equipment was carefully sterilized in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - clean something to remove or kill harmful organisms

Etymology

From Latin 'sterilis' (barren) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈstɛr.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal unable to produce offspring, typically by a medical procedure
make unable to produce offspring
medical

Examples

The doctors had to sterilize the dog to prevent it from breeding.
In the past, some governments wrongly sterilized people without their consent.
After the procedure, she discovered she was sterilized and could not have children.

Synset Examples

in some countries, people with genetically transmissible disabilities are sterilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castrate - to remove the testes or ovaries, making an individual infertile
  • Antonym: to fertilize - to make fertile or able to produce offspring

Etymology

From French stériliser, from Latin sterilis (barren).
