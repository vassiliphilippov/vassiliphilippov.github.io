technique

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈniːk / (AmE), / tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
the skill or ability in performing the basic elements of a particular activity, usually developed through practice and familiarity
skill in the fundamentals of an activity

Examples

Her piano technique has improved markedly thanks to hours of daily practice.
The dancer impressed the judges with his flawless technique during the performance.
You need good technique to master advanced yoga poses safely.

Synset Examples

practice greatly improves proficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficiency - a high degree of skill or competence
  • Antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

From French *technique*, from Greek *technikē* (art, skill).

2. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnik / (AmE), / tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
a practical method, skill, or way of doing something, especially one acquired through training or experience
method or skill for carrying out a task

Examples

The artist used a new technique to create stunning visual effects.
Learning the proper technique is crucial for playing the piano well.
Doctors are always searching for the best surgical technique to minimize patient recovery time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a planned way of doing something
  • Synonym: procedure - an established or official way of doing something
  • Synonym: approach - a way of dealing with something

Etymology

From French 'technique', from Greek 'tekhnē' (art, skill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05672990-n
Synset POS: n
