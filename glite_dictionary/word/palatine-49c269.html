palatine

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tɪn / (BrE)
relating to a count palatine or to the royal privileges and authority held by such a person.
relating to a count palatine's rights or authority

Examples

The duke held palatine powers within his territory, ruling almost like a king.
During the Middle Ages, certain regions enjoyed palatine status, allowing their rulers special privileges.
The palatine authority granted him judicial independence from the monarch.

Etymology

From Latin palatinus (of the palace), originally referring to officials of the imperial palace.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tɪn / (BrE)
either of a pair of irregularly shaped bones forming the back part of the hard palate, part of the nasal cavity, and the floor of the eye sockets
bone at back of hard palate
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified a fracture in the palatine during the operation.
An infection in the palatine can cause serious complications in the nasal cavity.
The shape of the palatine bones affects the structure of the upper jaw.

Etymology

From Latin 'palatum' (palate), referring to its location in the mouth.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈpal.ə.tiːn / (BrE)
an important official in ancient Rome, often serving in administrative or judicial roles close to the emperor
high official in ancient Rome

Examples

During the reign of Augustus, the palatine wielded great influence over legal decisions.
Many palatines were chosen from the emperor’s close advisers and confidants.
The palatine was responsible for overseeing important state rituals and ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrate - civil officer or judge with authority in Roman society

Etymology

From Latin palatinus (‘of the palace’), originally referring to officials connected with the imperial palace on the Palatine Hill.

5. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tiːn / (BrE)
(historical) A nobleman who, in the Middle Ages, was granted royal privileges and governed a palatinate, exercising sovereign powers within his territory.
medieval nobleman governing a palatinate
archaic

Examples

The palatine wielded considerable authority within his lands during the Middle Ages.
In some regions, a palatine had powers equivalent to those of the king.
The castle was the seat of the local palatine, who ruled over the territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duke - a high-ranking nobleman
  • Synonym: count - a European nobleman ranking below a duke

Etymology

From Latin palatinus, meaning 'of the palace.'
