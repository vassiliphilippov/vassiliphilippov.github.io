imperativeness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛrəˈtɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˌpærəˈtɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being urgent, commanding, or impossible to ignore
quality of being imperative or pressing

Examples

The imperativeness of the situation was evident in her tone of voice.
He spoke with a sense of imperativeness that demanded the audience's attention.
In emergencies, the imperativeness of clear communication cannot be overstated.

Synset Examples

he pressed his demand with considerable instancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgency - the quality of being very important and needing immediate attention
  • Degree Lower: importance - the state of being important or significant

Etymology

From 'imperative' + '-ness', formed from Latin 'imperativus' (commanding).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛrəˈtɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛrəˈtɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of demanding immediate notice or attention; urgency or insistence
state of being urgent or insistent

Examples

The imperativeness of the situation left them no time to hesitate.
He spoke with such imperativeness that everyone immediately paid attention.
The imperativeness of her request was impossible to ignore.

Synset Examples

the insistence of their hunger
the press of business matters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgency - the quality of needing to be dealt with immediately
  • Antonym: triviality - the quality of being unimportant

Etymology

From imperative + -ness, based on Latin *imperare* (to command).
