manageable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒəbl / (BrE)
that can be managed, controlled, or dealt with without great difficulty
able to be managed or controlled

Examples

The project is large, but it's still manageable if we work together.
Her hair is thick but surprisingly manageable after using the new conditioner.
We divided the tasks into manageable chunks to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmanageable - difficult or impossible to control or manage
  • Synonym: controllable - able to be controlled or restrained

Etymology

From 'manage' + '-able', from Italian 'maneggiare' (to handle, especially horses) + Latin '-abilis' (able).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒəbəl / (BrE)
that can be managed, controlled, or dealt with easily
that can be easily managed or controlled

Examples

The new software update makes the system much more manageable.
Despite the large number of tasks, she found her workload manageable.
His hair is thick but still quite manageable after using conditioner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmanageable - not able to be managed or controlled
  • Synonym: controllable - able to be controlled; subject to control

Etymology

From manage + -able, after French *maniable* (easily handled).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01477656-a
Synset POS: a
