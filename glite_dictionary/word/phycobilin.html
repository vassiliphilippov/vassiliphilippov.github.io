phycobilin

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪkoʊˈbɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɪkəʊˈbɪlɪn / (BrE)
a water-soluble pigment found in red algae and cyanobacteria, responsible for capturing light energy during photosynthesis
pigment in red algae and cyanobacteria
medical

Examples

Red algae contain phycobilin pigments that enable them to absorb blue and green light for photosynthesis.
The unique color of cyanobacteria is partly due to the presence of phycobilin.
Scientists study phycobilin to better understand the light-gathering mechanisms of certain algae.

Etymology

From Greek phykos (seaweed) + bilin (a pigment group name from bile).
