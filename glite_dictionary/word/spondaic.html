spondaic

1. ADJ

/ spɑnˈdeɪɪk / (AmE), / spɒnˈdeɪɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of spondees (metrical feet with two long or stressed syllables)
relating to or made of spondees

Examples

The poem's rhythm featured a strong spondaic pattern.
Classical poetry often uses spondaic meter to convey solemnity.
The line was analyzed as a spondaic hexameter by the critic.

Synset Examples

spondaic hexameter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spondee - a metrical foot with two long or stressed syllables

Etymology

From Latin *spondaicus*, from Greek *spondaikos*, relating to a spondee.
