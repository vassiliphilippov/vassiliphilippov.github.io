Agathis australis

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæθɪs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / əˈɡæθɪs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to New Zealand, also known as the kauri, valued for its white, straight-grained timber.
New Zealand kauri tree

Examples

The forests of Northland are famous for their ancient Agathis australis specimens towering above the canopy.
Early settlers in New Zealand used Agathis australis timber for building ships and houses.
A huge Agathis australis stands in Waipoua Forest, drawing visitors from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kauri - another name for Agathis australis, a large New Zealand tree

Etymology

From Greek agathis (ball of thread), used as a genus name, and Latin australis (southern).
