Nothofagus procera

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈθoʊfəɡəs proʊˈsɛrə / (AmE), / nɒˈθɒfəɡəs prəʊˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A large timber tree native to Chile, yielding coarse-grained lumber, commonly known as the Chilean oak.
large Chilean timber tree

Examples

The forests of southern Chile are home to the impressive Nothofagus procera.
Lumber from Nothofagus procera is often used in local construction projects.
Researchers studied how the Nothofagus procera adapted to changes in climate.

Etymology

From Greek *nothos* (false) + *phagos* (beech); 'procera' from Latin meaning tall or lofty.
