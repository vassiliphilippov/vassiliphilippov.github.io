sultry

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌl.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.tri / (BrE)
(of a person, voice, look, or atmosphere) sexually attractive or suggestive; expressing or arousing sexual desire.
sexually attractive or alluring

Examples

The actress gave a sultry glance that captivated everyone in the room.
He danced with a sultry energy that left the audience breathless.
A sultry voice on the radio made the late-night show even more intriguing.

Synset Examples

sensual excesses
a sultry look
a sultry dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensual - relating to or arousing gratification of the senses, often sexual
  • Synonym: sexy - sexually attractive or exciting

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌltri / (AmE), / ˈsʌltri / (BrE)
(of weather or air) oppressively hot and humid; uncomfortably warm and sticky.
oppressively hot and humid (weather)

Examples

August afternoons in the city were unbearably sultry and made walking outside exhausting.
A sultry breeze wafted through the room, making everyone sweat in silence.
The sultry weather finally broke with an evening thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

the summer was sultry and oppressive
the stifling atmosphere
the sulfurous atmosphere preceding a thunderstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifling - very hot and uncomfortable, making it difficult to breathe
  • Synonym: muggy - (weather) warm and damp in an unpleasant way

Etymology

Probably from Old French *sultrie* (humid heat), of unknown origin.
