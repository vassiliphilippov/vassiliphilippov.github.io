moa

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.ə / (BrE)
a large, extinct, flightless bird native to New Zealand, belonging to the family Dinornithidae, which became extinct around the late 15th century.
extinct flightless bird of New Zealand

Examples

The moa was hunted to extinction by the ancestors of the Māori people.
Scientists study the bones of the moa to learn about its habitat and diet.
Today, no living moa exists, but their remains can be seen in New Zealand museums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large, flightless bird native to Africa

Etymology

From Maori 'moa', meaning 'fowl' or 'bird'.
