Heritiera trifoliolata

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌrɪtiˈɪrə traɪˌfoʊliˈoʊlətə / (AmE), / hɛˌrɪtiːˈɪərə traɪˌfəʊliˈɒlətə / (BrE)
a large tree species native to Australasia, belonging to the genus Heritiera
Australasian tree species

Examples

The Heritiera trifoliolata is valued for its strong and durable timber in certain coastal regions.
Researchers discovered a mature Heritiera trifoliolata deep within the rainforest during their expedition.
Many conservationists are studying the unique ecosystem supported by Heritiera trifoliolata in Australasia.

Etymology

From the genus name Heritiera, named for French botanist Charles Louis L'Héritier de Brutelle, with Latin species epithet trifoliolata meaning 'three leaflets'.
