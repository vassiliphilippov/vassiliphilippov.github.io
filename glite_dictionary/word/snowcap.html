snowcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌkap / (BrE)
a layer of snow that covers the top of a mountain or hill, especially one that remains throughout the year
layer of snow covering a mountain top

Examples

The mountain's snowcap glistened in the morning sunlight.
As the climate warms, many snowcaps are shrinking around the globe.
From our campsite, we could see the distant snowcap atop the rugged peak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacier - a slowly moving mass of ice and snow
