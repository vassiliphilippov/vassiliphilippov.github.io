catchfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
any of several flowering plants of the genus Silene, whose stems are often sticky and can trap small insects.
flowering plant with sticky stems

Examples

Bright pink catchfly flowers lined the edge of the meadow in early summer.
The stem of a catchfly is covered in sticky hairs that trap small insects.
Gardeners value the catchfly for its colorful blossoms and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campion - any of various plants of the genus Silene or Lychnis with showy flowers

Etymology

Named for the sticky stems that can trap small insects, as if 'catching' flies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkætʃ.flʌɪ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Silene, typically with sticky stems that can trap small insects; found in the north temperate regions.
herbaceous plant with sticky stems

Examples

The catchfly is easily recognized by its sticky, glandular stems.
Gardeners admire the delicate flowers of the catchfly in springtime.
Many insects are trapped on the surface of the catchfly plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campion - A related plant, often with similar appearance, in the genus Silene or Lychnis

Etymology

Named for its tendency to catch small flies on its sticky stems.
