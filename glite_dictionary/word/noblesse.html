noblesse

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈblɛs / (AmE), / nɒˈblɛs / (BrE)
the class of people belonging to the nobility, especially in France; aristocracy.
members of the nobility, especially French

Examples

The French noblesse played a pivotal role in the country’s history before the Revolution.
He married into the noblesse, enhancing his political influence.
The traditions of the noblesse are still evident in some rural parts of France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, often holding hereditary titles

Etymology

From Old French, from Latin nobilis (noble).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈblɛs / (AmE), / nəʊˈblɛs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being of noble birth; the noble class collectively
noble birth or the noble class

Examples

The old manor house was a symbol of the family's noblesse and privilege.
Many members of the noblesse lost their titles during the revolution.
His behavior reflected an upbringing among the noblesse rather than the common people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobility - the group of people belonging to the noble class

Etymology

From Old French 'noblesse' (nobility), from Latin 'nobilis' (notable, noble).
