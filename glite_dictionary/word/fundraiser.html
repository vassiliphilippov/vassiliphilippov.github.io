fundraiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndˌreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfʌndˌreɪzə / (BrE)
a person who seeks to collect money for a cause, organization, or project, typically by organizing events or campaigns
person who solicits donations

Examples

Sarah volunteered as a fundraiser for the local animal shelter this year.
The charity hired an experienced fundraiser to help increase donations.
As a fundraiser, he organizes gala events to support medical research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or help to a person or organization

Etymology

from 'fund' + 'raiser', meaning someone who raises (collects) funds

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10136135-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndˌreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfʌndˌreɪzə / (BrE)
an event or activity organized to collect money for a particular cause, often involving social gathering or entertainment
event to raise money for a cause

Examples

The school is holding a fundraiser next week to support the music program.
Many local businesses donated prizes for the cancer charity fundraiser.
She volunteered to organize the annual fundraiser for the animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity event - an event held to raise money for charitable causes
  • Synonym: benefit - an event held to raise funds, usually for a charitable cause

Etymology

From 'fund' (a sum of money for a specific purpose) + 'raiser' (one that raises or collects).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07470250-n
Synset POS: n
