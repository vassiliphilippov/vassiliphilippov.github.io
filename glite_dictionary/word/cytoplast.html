cytoplast

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtəˌplæst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtəplɑːst / (BrE)
the part of a cell consisting of the cytoplasm and cell membrane but lacking the nucleus
cell without its nucleus
medical

Examples

To create a hybrid cell, scientists fused a donor nucleus with a cytoplast from another cell.
The cytoplast is used in somatic cell nuclear transfer techniques in genetic research.
When the cytoplast is separated from the nucleus, the resulting structure can still support some cellular functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytoplasm - the contents of a cell excluding the nucleus
  • Antonym: nucleus - the central and most important part of a cell

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + 'plastos' (formed, molded).
