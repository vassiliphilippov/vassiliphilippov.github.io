Armenian

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmiːniən / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Armenia
native or citizen of Armenia

Examples

The museum features ancient artifacts collected by Armenians throughout history.
She met an Armenian who shared fascinating stories about his homeland.
Many Armenians celebrate their cultural heritage with traditional music and dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armenia - country in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia

Etymology

From Latin Armenius, from Ancient Greek Armenios, from Armenia.

3. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmiːniən / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən / (BrE)
the writing system of the Armenian language, consisting of an alphabet of 38 letters
alphabet used for writing Armenian

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were carefully transcribed using the Armenian script.
Learning the Armenian alphabet can be challenging due to its unique characters.
He researched how the Armenian writing system developed independently from other alphabets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyrillic - writing system used for Russian and some other languages
  • Synonym: Greek alphabet - alphabet used for the Greek language

Etymology

From Latin Armenius, relating to Armenia or its people.
