unarmoured

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːrmɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːməd / (BrE)
(of animals or objects) lacking a protective covering or armor.
without protective covering; not armored

Examples

The unarmoured fish made an easy target for predators in the open water.
Unlike turtles, most frogs are unarmoured and rely on camouflage for protection.
Some ancient species evolved from being heavily armored to becoming unarmoured for greater speed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armoured - having a protective covering or armor

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'armoured', from Old French 'armure' (armor).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑrmɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːməd / (BrE)
(especially of people or vehicles) not wearing or provided with protective armor.
not wearing or equipped with armor

Examples

The scouts were unarmoured and vulnerable to enemy attack.
He drove an unarmoured vehicle into the dangerous territory.
Many soldiers in early battles fought unarmoured, relying on speed and agility instead of protection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armoured - provided with protective armor

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'armoured' (provided with armor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00145996-a
Synset POS: a
