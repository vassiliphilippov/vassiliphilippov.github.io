dispassionate

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈpæʃənət / (AmE), / dɪsˈpæʃənət / (BrE)
not influenced by strong emotion, and so able to be impartial or fair
not influenced by emotion; impartial

Examples

The judge remained dispassionate throughout the lengthy trial.
It is important for scientists to maintain a dispassionate approach to their research.
Despite the heated debate, she gave a dispassionate assessment of the situation.

Synset Examples

a journalist should be a dispassionate reporter of fact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - not biased; treating all rivals equally
  • Synonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + passionatus (full of passion).
