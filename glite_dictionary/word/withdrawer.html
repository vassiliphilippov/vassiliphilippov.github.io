withdrawer

1. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔɚ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːə / (BrE)
a person, especially a drug addict, who is in the process of discontinuing the use of narcotics; someone undergoing withdrawal
person discontinuing narcotics use
medical

Examples

The clinic has a specialized program to help each withdrawer cope with symptoms safely.
As a withdrawer, he found group therapy sessions to be particularly supportive.
After years of addiction, she became a withdrawer determined to reclaim her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the process of discontinuing drug use, often resulting in symptoms

2. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔɚ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːə / (BrE)
a person, especially a contestant, who withdraws from a competition or event
person who withdraws from a competition

Examples

The tennis tournament announced that a withdrawer would be replaced by a lucky loser from the qualifying rounds.
Officials confirmed that the withdrawer cited an injury as the reason for leaving the competition.
Despite being a top seed, he became a withdrawer just before the finals due to illness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competitor - a person who takes part in a competition

3. NOUN

/ wɪðˈdrɔɚ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːə / (BrE)
a person who withdraws money, especially from a bank account they previously deposited funds into
person who takes out money from an account

Examples

Each withdrawer must present valid identification at the counter.
The bank keeps a record of every withdrawer who takes out large sums of cash.
A frequent withdrawer may have to explain the purpose of their transactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depositor - a person who deposits money in a bank

Etymology

From 'withdraw' + '-er', indicating an agent noun.

a student who leaves an educational institution before completing their studies
student who leaves school or university early

Examples

After facing financial difficulties, the withdrawer decided to return home and work full-time.
The university tracks the number of withdrawers each semester to improve student retention.
Being a withdrawer does not mean you cannot return to education later in life.
