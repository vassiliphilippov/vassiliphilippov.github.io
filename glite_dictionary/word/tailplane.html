tailplane

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌpleɪn / (BrE)
the horizontal stabilizing surface at the tail of an aircraft, to which the elevator is attached
horizontal stabilizer at the tail of an aircraft

Examples

During the inspection, the engineer carefully checked the tailplane for any signs of damage.
The tailplane helps maintain the aircraft's stability during flight.
A malfunction in the tailplane could severely affect the plane's ability to pitch up or down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horizontal stabilizer - the fixed horizontal airfoil in an aircraft tail assembly

Etymology

Formed from 'tail' + 'plane', referring to its position and shape.
