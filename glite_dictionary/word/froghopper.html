froghopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔːɡˌhɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈfrɒɡˌhɒpə / (BrE)
A small jumping insect of the family Cercopidae, known for its leaping ability and for producing spittle-like foam as a larva.
small jumping insect; type of spittlebug

Examples

The froghopper is known for its impressive leaps, which can reach many times its body length.
After the rain, children spotted a froghopper resting on a blade of grass.
Scientists studied the froghopper to understand the mechanics of its jumping ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spittlebug - another name for a froghopper; insect producing foamy secretions as a larva

Etymology

Formed from 'frog' and 'hopper', referring to its jumping ability.
