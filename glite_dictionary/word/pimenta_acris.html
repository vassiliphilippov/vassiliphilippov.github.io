Pimenta acris

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmɛntə ˈækrɪs / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntə ˈækrɪs / (BrE)
A species of tree native to the West Indies, known as the source of bay rum.
West Indian tree; source of bay rum

Examples

The fragrant leaves of the Pimenta acris are used in the production of bay rum.
Botanists were eager to study the Pimenta acris during their trip to the Caribbean.
Pimenta acris trees thrive in the tropical climate of the West Indies.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Pimenta' and species 'acris', meaning sharp or pungent.
