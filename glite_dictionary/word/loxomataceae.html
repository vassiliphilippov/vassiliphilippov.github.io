Loxomataceae

1. NOUN

/ lɒkˌsoʊməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / lɒksəʊməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
A small family of ferns native to New Zealand, comprising the single genus Loxoma.
family of New Zealand ferns

Examples

The Loxomataceae are a rare and ancient lineage of ferns found only in New Zealand forests.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Loxomataceae family.
Conservation efforts have helped protect the unique Loxomataceae from extinction.

Etymology

From the genus name Loxoma + -aceae, a standard family suffix in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ loʊkˌsoʊməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / lɒkˌsəʊməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
A very small family of ferns native to New Zealand, comprising a single genus, Loxoma.
family of ferns from New Zealand

Examples

The Loxomataceae are endemic to New Zealand and are rarely found elsewhere.
Some botanists specialize in the study of Loxomataceae due to their unique characteristics.
Members of Loxomataceae include only one genus, making it a particularly small plant family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant that has feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From the genus name Loxoma, with standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
