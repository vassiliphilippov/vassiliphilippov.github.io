Gymnocalycium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnoʊkəˈlɪsiəm / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒkəˈlɪsiəm / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing, globular cacti native to South America, known for their spiny ribs and distinctive tubercles
genus of South American globular cacti

Examples

The Gymnocalycium in his greenhouse produced beautiful pink flowers this summer.
Many collectors prize rare Gymnocalycium species for their unique patterns and ease of care.
She specialized in growing Gymnocalycium cacti due to their compact shape and vibrant blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'kalyx' (calyx), referring to the naked flower tube.
