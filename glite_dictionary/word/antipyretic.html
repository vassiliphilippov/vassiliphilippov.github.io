antipyretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæntiˌpaɪˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌpaɪˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) preventing or reducing fever.
reducing or preventing fever
medical

Examples

Paracetamol is commonly used as an antipyretic treatment for mild fevers.
An antipyretic drug can help lower a patient's body temperature during an infection.
Some herbal remedies are believed to have antipyretic properties, helping to reduce fever naturally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrifuge - a medicine that reduces fever

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + pyretos (fever).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.paɪˈrɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.paɪˈrɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
a medicine used to reduce or prevent fever by lowering body temperature
medicine for reducing fever
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antipyretic to help manage her high fever.
Paracetamol is a commonly used antipyretic for treating mild to moderate fevers.
Always check the dosage before giving an antipyretic to children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrifuge - a medicine that reduces fever

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + pyretos (fever).
