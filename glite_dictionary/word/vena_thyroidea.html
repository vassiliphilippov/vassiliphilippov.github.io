vena thyroidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə θaɪˌrɔɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə θaɪˌrɔɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
Any of several small veins that drain blood from the thyroid region of the neck.
vein draining the thyroid area
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the vena thyroidea during the procedure.
Ultrasound revealed an enlargement near the vena thyroidea in the patient's neck.
Multiple venae thyroideae may be visible depending on the degree of vascularization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroid vein - a vein that drains blood from the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'thyroidea' (thyroid).
