Dreissena

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈsiːnə / (AmE), / draɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater mussels, including the zebra mussel, known for their invasive impact on aquatic ecosystems.
genus of freshwater mussels

Examples

Scientists are monitoring the spread of Dreissena species in the Great Lakes.
The Dreissena genus includes several invasive freshwater mollusks.
Water treatment facilities often struggle to deal with Dreissena infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra mussel - a small invasive freshwater bivalve mollusk

Etymology

Named after 19th-century German zoologist Simon Dreissena.

2. NOUN

/ draɪˈsiːnə / (AmE), / draɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of small freshwater mussels, including zebra mussels, found in lakes and rivers, some species of which are invasive
genus of freshwater mussels (zebra mussels)

Examples

The spread of Dreissena species has affected many freshwater ecosystems in North America.
Scientists studied the rapid colonization of new areas by Dreissena in the Great Lakes.
The invasive zebra mussel, a member of the Dreissena genus, clogs water intake pipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra mussel - a small invasive freshwater mussel (Dreissena polymorpha)

Etymology

Named after Simon Dreissen, an 18th-century Belgian physician.
