tantalizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Arousing curiosity, desire, or interest, especially when something is difficult or impossible to obtain.
arousing unattainable desire or curiosity

Examples

The aroma of freshly baked bread was tantalizing to the hungry children.
She caught a tantalizing glimpse of the ocean through the trees.
The job offer was tantalizing, but ultimately too good to be true.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing taste of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - attractive or appealing (tempting the senses or desires)
  • Antonym: unattainable - impossible to achieve or obtain

Etymology

From Tantalus, a figure in Greek mythology punished by being kept just out of reach of food and water.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtan.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Exciting one's senses or desires in a way that is pleasantly inviting or tempting.
excitingly attractive or tempting

Examples

The tantalizing smell of freshly baked bread filled the kitchen.
She caught a tantalizing glimpse of the painting through the open door.
The chef's new dish looked tantalizing on the menu, making it hard to resist.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing aroma
a tempting repast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - attractive in a way that makes people want to have or do it
  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or pleasing

Etymology

From the mythological character Tantalus in Greek mythology, who was eternally tempted by food and water just out of reach.
