vandalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvændəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvændəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the intentional and deliberate destruction or damage to property belonging to others, often in a willful or malicious manner
deliberate damage to property

Examples

The city spent thousands repairing the park after the recent vandalism.
Acts of vandalism can range from graffiti to smashing windows.
The museum increased security to prevent vandalism of its exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying something
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state

Etymology

From French *vandalisme*, after the Vandals, a Germanic tribe known for sacking Rome in 455 AD.
