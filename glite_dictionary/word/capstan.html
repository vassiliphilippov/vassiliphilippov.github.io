capstan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.stən / (AmE), / ˈkap.stən / (BrE)
A revolving cylindrical device, mounted upright and turned by hand or by mechanical means, used on ships to move heavy weights such as an anchor or sails.
revolving device on ships for lifting heavy weights

Examples

The crew worked together to turn the capstan and raise the heavy anchor.
Several sailors gripped the bars of the capstan to haul in the mooring line.
Modern ships often use a motorized capstan instead of manual power.

Etymology

From Old Provençal *cabestan*, from Latin *capistrum* (halter), from *capere* (to seize).
