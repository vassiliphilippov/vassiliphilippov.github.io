flavourer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪvərər / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvərə / (BrE)
a substance added to food or drink to give it a particular taste
substance added for flavour
british

Examples

Her grandmother always used fresh herbs as a flavourer in her soups.
Vanilla extract is a common flavourer in many dessert recipes.
The chef experimented with a new flavourer to enhance the sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavoring - a substance added to food or drink to give a particular taste (American spelling)

Etymology

Formed by adding the agent suffix '-er' to 'flavour'; chiefly British spelling.
