ctene

1. NOUN

/ ˈktiːn / (AmE), / ˈktiːn / (BrE)
a locomotor organ found in some marine invertebrates, consisting of a row of fused cilia used for movement
row of fused cilia for movement in some marine animals
medical

Examples

The shimmering rows of ctenes propel the comb jelly gracefully through the water.
Scientists studied how each ctene contributed to the animal's swimming efficiency.
When illuminated, the movement of the ctenes created a rainbow effect in the tank.

Etymology

From Greek *kteis* (comb), referring to the comb-like appearance.
