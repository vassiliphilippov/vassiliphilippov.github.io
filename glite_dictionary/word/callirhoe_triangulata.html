Callirhoe triangulata

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪroʊ.i traɪˌæŋɡjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / kaˈlɪr.əʊ.i traɪˌaŋ.ɡjuːˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the mallow family, native to parts of North America, with densely hairy stems, mostly triangular basal leaves, and rose-purple flowers in branched clusters.
perennial plant with triangular leaves and purple flowers

Examples

The garden's new border features several Callirhoe triangulata with vibrant, rose-purple blooms.
Botanists admire Callirhoe triangulata for its unique triangular leaves and eye-catching flowers.
She planted Callirhoe triangulata in her yard to attract butterflies and add color to the landscape.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek mythology (Callirhoe), species name from Latin 'triangulata' meaning 'triangular'.
