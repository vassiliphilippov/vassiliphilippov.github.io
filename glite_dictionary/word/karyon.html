karyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈkærɪən / (BrE)
The nucleus of a cell, containing DNA and RNA and responsible for the control of growth and reproduction.
nucleus of a cell
medical

Examples

The karyon plays a central role in controlling the cell's genetic functions.
Researchers isolated the karyon to study its DNA content.
Certain diseases can affect the structure of the karyon in human cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleus - central part of a cell containing genetic material

Etymology

From Greek karyon (nut, kernel).
