wakeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪkfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfʊl / (BrE)
Alert and watchful, especially in a careful or cautious way; on the lookout for possible danger.
alert and watchful

Examples

The guards remained wakeful throughout the night, alert for any sign of movement.
A wakeful parent kept a close eye on the playground from the bench.
Her wakeful attitude helped her notice the subtle change in her surroundings.

Synset Examples

a policy of open-eyed awareness
the vigilant eye of the town watch
there was a watchful dignity in the room
a watchful parent with a toddler in tow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Synonym: watchful - being alert and observing closely
  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention or being watchful

Etymology

From Middle English wakeful, from wake (be awake) + -ful (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪkfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfəl / (BrE)
fully conscious and alert; not asleep
alert and not asleep

Examples

The baby was wakeful all night, making it hard for anyone to sleep.
After drinking coffee, I felt much more wakeful and ready to study.
He remained wakeful during the long, boring lecture.

Synset Examples

worked every moment of my waking hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond; fully awake
  • Antonym: asleep - in a state of sleep; not awake

Etymology

Formed from 'wake' (to be awake) + '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈweɪkfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfʊl / (BrE)
(of sleep) easily disturbed or light; not deep, so that one wakes easily.
light or easily disturbed sleep

Examples

He spent a wakeful night, tossing and turning in bed.
Children often have wakeful sleep when they are anxious or excited.
My wakeful hours increased as the thunderstorm raged outside.

Synset Examples

in a light doze
a light sleeper
a restless wakeful night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; constantly moving
  • Antonym: deep - (of sleep) difficult to rouse from; not easily disturbed

Etymology

From Old English 'wācful', from 'wacan' (to wake) + '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00697019-s
Synset POS: s
