Cyclops

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪklɑps / (AmE), / ˈsaɪklɒps / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of a race of giants each having a single eye in the center of the forehead.
one-eyed giant in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the myth, the Cyclops lived on an isolated island in the Mediterranean.
Odysseus blinded the Cyclops Polyphemus to escape from his cave.
The ancient Greeks depicted the Cyclops as a fearsome monster with a single eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength; often appears in mythology
  • Synonym: monster - a mythical creature that is typically large, ugly, and frightening

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κύκλωψ (*Kúklōps*), from κύκλος (*kúklos*, 'circle') and ὤψ (*ōps*, 'eye').

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌklɑps / (AmE), / ˈsɪˌklɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater copepods, commonly known as water fleas, characterized by a single central eye.
a genus of small freshwater copepods

Examples

Many ponds and lakes are inhabited by Cyclops, which play a role in the aquatic food web.
The single eye of the Cyclops is a distinctive feature among freshwater crustaceans.
Some species within the genus Cyclops can serve as intermediate hosts for parasites affecting humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copepod - a group of small aquatic crustaceans
  • Synonym: water flea - small aquatic crustaceans, including Daphnia and related genera

Etymology

From Greek 'Kýklōps', the mythological giant with a single eye, alluding to the genus' single eye.
