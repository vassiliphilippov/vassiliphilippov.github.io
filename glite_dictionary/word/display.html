display

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
an electronic device that shows information visually, such as a computer screen, monitor, or digital panel
electronic device showing information visually

Examples

The laptop has a high-resolution display that makes images look sharp and clear.
She adjusted the brightness on the display to reduce eye strain while working.
Modern smartphones are known for their vibrant displays with deep color and contrast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor - a device or screen used to display images and information from computers or other sources

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspleɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
an act or instance of exhibiting or showing something conspicuously, often to attract attention or communicate a particular impression
an act of showing something to create an impression

Examples

Her confident display of knowledge impressed everyone at the meeting.
They put on a dramatic display of unity for the cameras.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show - an act of putting something on view or demonstrating
  • Synonym: exhibition - a public display of art or items
  • Antonym: concealment - the act of keeping something secret or hidden
