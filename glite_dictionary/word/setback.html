setback

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈsɛtbæk / (BrE)
an event or difficulty that delays progress or makes a situation worse
a difficulty that delays progress

Examples

Despite the unexpected setback, the team continued to work towards their goal.
Her injury was a serious setback to her chances of winning the championship.
They viewed the loss as just a temporary setback rather than a failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or delay
  • Antonym: advantage - something that helps success or progress
