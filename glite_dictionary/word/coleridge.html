Coleridge

1. PROPN

/ ˈkoʊlərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlərɪdʒ / (BrE)
Samuel Taylor Coleridge (1772–1834), an English Romantic poet, critic, and philosopher, noted for works such as 'The Rime of the Ancient Mariner'.
English Romantic poet (1772–1834)

Examples

Many consider Coleridge a founding figure of the English Romantic movement.
I recently reread 'Kubla Khan' by Coleridge and was captivated by its imagery.
Coleridge and Wordsworth collaborated on the famous collection 'Lyrical Ballads'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wordsworth - English Romantic poet, often associated with Coleridge
