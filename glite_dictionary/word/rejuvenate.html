to rejuvenate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈdʒuːvəˌneɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒuːvəneɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively
make look or feel younger or fresher

Examples

A fresh coat of paint can rejuvenate an old house and make it look new again.
Spending time at the spa really helped rejuvenate her spirit and energy.
The city launched several projects to rejuvenate its aging downtown area.

Synset Examples

The contact with his grandchildren rejuvenated him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revitalize - give new energy or life to
  • Antonym: age - to become or make older

Etymology

From Latin 'iuvenis' (young), with 're-' (again) + 'juvenate' (make young).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈdʒuːvənˌeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒuːvɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of land) to develop more youthful topographical features, often as a result of renewed uplift or erosion.
make landforms youthful again; renew land features

Examples

Over millions of years, tectonic uplift can rejuvenate a region's landscape, creating new valleys and steeper slopes.
Geologists study the ways in which rivers rejuvenate their courses after changes in elevation.
Glacial melting helped to rejuvenate the terrain, making old hills appear young and sharp again.

Synset Examples

the land rejuvenated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revitalize - to give new life or vigor to something

Etymology

From Latin juvenis (young), via French rajeunir (make young again).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈdʒuːvəˌneɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒuːvəneɪt / (BrE)
(of a stream or river) To renew or increase the erosive activity, often as a result of uplift in the land.
restore erosive action of a river

Examples

Tectonic uplift can rejuvenate a river, causing it to carve a deeper valley.
Geologists observed that the area was rejuvenated during the last period of uplift.
Glacial retreat can also rejuvenate streams, triggering renewed erosion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to degrade - to reduce the erosive power or elevation of a river

Etymology

From Latin juvenis (young), via French réjuvener (to make young again).

5. VERB

to make or become young or youthful again; to restore to a former state or condition of youth, freshness, or vigor
make or become young again

Examples

Many people seek new experiences to rejuvenate their spirits during retirement.
After several days at the spa, she felt completely rejuvenated.
The company's innovative changes helped to rejuvenate its aging workforce.

  • Antonym: age - to become older or show signs of aging

From Latin juvenis (young) with re- (again), via French rajeunir and English rejuvenate (early 19th century).
