irreparable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛpərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛpərəbəl / (BrE)
Impossible to repair, correct, or make right again.
impossible to repair or fix

Examples

The flood caused irreparable damage to the historic building.
Her reputation suffered irreparable harm after the scandal.
Losing those documents was an irreparable mistake for the company.

Synset Examples

irreparable harm
an irreparable mistake
irreparable damages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repairable - able to be repaired or rectified
  • Synonym: irreversible - impossible to reverse or undo

Etymology

From Latin 'irreparabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'reparabilis' (able to be repaired).
