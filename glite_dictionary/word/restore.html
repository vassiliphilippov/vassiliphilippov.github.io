to restore

1. VERB

/ rɪˈstɔr / (AmE), / rɪˈstɔː / (BrE)
to return something to its original, usable, or functioning condition
return to original or working condition

Examples

After months of hard work, they finally restored the old painting to its former beauty.
The company plans to restore the historic building and open it as a museum.
Efforts are underway to restore the wetlands that once covered this area.

Synset Examples

restore the forest to its original pristine condition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - to fix something that is damaged or broken
  • Antonym: damage - to harm or spoil something

Etymology

From Latin 'restaurare' (to rebuild, renew).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈstɔr / (AmE), / rɪˈstɔː / (BrE)
to give back something that was lost, stolen, or taken to its original owner; to bring back to a former condition or position
to give back or return to original state

Examples

The museum will restore the ancient sculpture to its rightful place.
After years apart, the children were restored to their family.
Please restore all borrowed books to the library by next week.

Synset Examples

Restore the stolen painting to its rightful owner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to give back something to its owner
  • Antonym: remove - to take away from a place or position

Etymology

From Latin 'restaurare' (to repair, rebuild, restore).

/ rɪˈstɔr / (AmE), / rɪˈstɔː / (BrE)

to bring back to health or energy

Examples

A good night's sleep can restore both your body and mind.
Her confidence was restored after she finished the project successfully.
Taking a short walk outside often helps to restore my energy during a busy day.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exhaust - to drain of energy or strength

From Latin restaurare (to renew, repair).
