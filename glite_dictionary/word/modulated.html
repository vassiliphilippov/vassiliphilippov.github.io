modulated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of sound, voice, pitch, etc.) changed or adjusted in strength, tone, or volume.
adjusted in pitch, tone, or volume

Examples

The singer delivered her lines in a beautifully modulated voice.
He spoke in a quietly modulated tone that calmed the audience.
A modulated signal is required for clear communication in radio transmission.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmodulated - not changed or adjusted in pitch, tone, or volume

Etymology

From Latin modulatus, past participle of modulare (to measure, regulate, sing).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of sound or voice) altered in volume, tone, or pitch, especially to make it more pleasant or varied.
altered in volume, tone, or pitch

Examples

The speaker's modulated voice kept the audience engaged throughout the presentation.
Her modulated tone conveyed both authority and warmth during the interview.
He sang in a beautifully modulated manner, adjusting his pitch as the song required.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monotone - a sound or voice that does not vary in pitch or tone

Etymology

From Latin modulatus, past participle of modulare (to measure, regulate, or sing).

Synset POS: s
