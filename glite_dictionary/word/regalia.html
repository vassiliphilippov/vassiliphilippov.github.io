regalia

1. NOUN

/ rəˈɡeɪliə / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪliə / (BrE)
Elaborate and impressive attire, often used for special occasions, ceremonies, or as a symbol of rank or office.
ceremonial or elaborate clothing

Examples

The king arrived at the ceremony wearing his most elaborate regalia.
Graduates marched proudly across the stage in their academic regalia.
The ancient crown remains a key piece of the royal regalia displayed at the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finery - elegant or showy clothes and accessories
  • Synonym: apparel - clothing, especially formal or fine clothing
  • Antonym: plainclothes - ordinary clothes, especially as opposed to uniforms or regalia

Etymology

From Medieval Latin regalia (royal rights, privileges, or insignia), plural of regale, from Latin regalis (royal).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡeɪliə / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪliə / (BrE)
the special clothes, objects, and symbols worn or carried by a king, queen, or other person of high rank at official ceremonies
ceremonial dress and symbols of royalty or office

Examples

The crown and scepter are important pieces of royal regalia used during the coronation ceremony.
Each member of the ancient order wore distinctive regalia at the annual banquet.
The university president marched at graduation in full academic regalia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignia - a distinguishing badge or emblem of office or honor
  • Synonym: paraphernalia - miscellaneous articles, especially the equipment needed for a particular activity

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *regalia* (royal rights, privileges), neuter plural of Latin *regalis* (royal).
