Erin

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛr.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛr.ɪn / (BrE)
An early or poetic name for Ireland, mainly used in poetry.
poetic name for Ireland
archaic

Examples

Many Irish ballads speak of Erin and her green fields.
He longed to return to Erin, the land of his ancestors.
The poet often referred to Ireland as Erin in his verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ireland - a large island to the west of Great Britain, called Éire in Irish

Etymology

From Irish 'Éirinn', the dative form of 'Éire' (Ireland).
