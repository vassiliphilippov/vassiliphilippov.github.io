atheistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪθiˈɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪθɪˈɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Rejecting or characterized by the rejection of any belief in gods; relating to or supporting atheism.
rejecting belief in gods
archaic

Examples

The pamphlet presented an atheistical view of the universe.
He faced criticism from religious leaders for his atheistical writings.
The laws once punished atheistical opinions as dangerous to society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheistic - relating to or characterized by atheism or the rejection of belief in gods
  • Antonym: theistic - relating to or characterized by belief in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

Formed from 'atheist' + '-ical', modeled after 'atheistic'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌeɪθiˈɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪθɪˈɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to, characterized by, or given to atheism; not believing in the existence of God or gods.
relating to atheism; not believing in God

Examples

The teacher faced criticism for his atheistical opinions in a conservative community.
Some writers of the eighteenth century were accused of holding atheistical beliefs.
Her speech was condemned as atheistical by members of the local church.

Synset Examples

atheist leanings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheist - a person who does not believe in the existence of God or gods
  • Antonym: religious - believing in or following the principles of a religion

Etymology

From Greek atheos ('without god') via Late Latin atheisticus; see atheist + -ical.
