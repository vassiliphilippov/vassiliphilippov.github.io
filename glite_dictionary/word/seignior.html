seignior

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnjər / (AmE), / ˈsiːnjə / (BrE)
a man of rank or authority, especially in the feudal system or under the ancien régime
man of rank in feudal or old French society
archaic

Examples

The seignior exercised control over his lands and the peasants who worked them.
In the old manor house, the portrait of the seignior hung above the grand fireplace.
During the tumult of the revolution, many a seignior was forced to flee his estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lord - a man of high rank or authority, especially in feudal Europe
  • Antonym: vassal - a person in a subordinate position under a feudal lord

Etymology

From Old French *seigneur*, from Latin *senior* (elder).
