prim

1. VERB

/ prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
to contract or purse one's lips, often in a prim, affected, or self-conscious manner
contract or purse the lips

Examples

She primmed her lips before giving her answer.
He primmed his mouth in disapproval at the suggestion.
The child primmed her lips, trying not to giggle during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

She primmed her lips after every bite of food

2. ADJ

/ prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
very formal and proper, showing or pretending to show perfect manners and decorum, often in an exaggerated or affected way
excessively formal or proper

Examples

She always sits upright and acts so prim at dinner parties.
His prim response to the joke made everyone else feel self-conscious.
Dressed in a prim suit, she carefully avoided all the muddy puddles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proper - behaving in a socially correct way
  • Antonym: informal - having a relaxed and friendly style or manner

Etymology

Originates from late 18th century, an abbreviation of 'primitive', used in the sense of 'simple, precise'.

3. ADJ

/ prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
very formal and proper, sometimes in a way that seems exaggerated or old-fashioned
very proper, sometimes overly so

Examples

Despite her young age, she always sat in a prim and proper manner at the table.
He gave her a prim nod as he passed, barely acknowledging her presence.
The teacher’s prim attitude made her seem distant and unapproachable.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proper - behaving in a socially correct and formal way
  • Synonym: straitlaced - having very strict morals
  • Antonym: informal - not formal in manner or style

Etymology

From Old French *prim*, from Latin *primus* (first), in the sense of 'first in manners' or 'excellent, virtuous'.

4. VERB

/ prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
to assume a formally proper or demure appearance or expression, especially in an exaggerated or affected way
to behave in a formally proper or affected way

Examples

She couldn't help but prim in front of the strict headmistress.
He likes to prim whenever important guests arrive at dinner.
The children would prim and giggle, imitating their teachers' manners.

Synset Examples

They mince and prim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posture - to assume a particular pose or stance, often to impress others

Etymology

From English 'prim', meaning neat or precise, possibly from French 'prême', variant of 'prime' (first, fine).

5. VERB

/ prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
to dress or behave in a very formal, proper, or precise manner
to behave or dress formally/properly

Examples

She always prims herself before an important meeting, straightening her dress and smoothing her hair.
He primed for the ceremony, fussing over every detail of his outfit.
They spent an hour priming before joining their distinguished guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preen - to tidy and arrange one's appearance in a self-satisfied manner
  • Antonym: to relax - to become less formal or strict in manner or dress

Etymology

From the adjective 'prim', based on French 'prîm' (precise, neat) or Latin 'primus' (first); as a verb meaning to make prim.
