deoxyadenosine

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˈædəˌnoʊsin / (AmE), / diˌɒksiˈædɪnəʊziːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside that is a constituent of DNA, consisting of the base adenine attached to a deoxyribose sugar.
nucleoside component of DNA
medical

Examples

The concentration of deoxyadenosine in the sample was measured using chromatography.
In DNA, deoxyadenosine pairs with thymine to form stable base pairs.
A mutation affected the synthesis of deoxyadenosine in the laboratory experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenosine - a nucleoside composed of adenine and ribose

Etymology

From deoxy- (lacking oxygen) + adenosine, indicating it is adenosine missing an oxygen atom.
