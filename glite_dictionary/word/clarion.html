clarion

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæriən / (AmE), / ˈklæriən / (BrE)
(of a sound) loud and clear; strongly expressed and easy to hear or understand.
loud and clear (of a sound or call)

Examples

His message was a clarion warning about the risks ahead.
The president's clarion call for unity resonated with the nation.
A clarion voice rang out over the restless crowd, demanding justice.

Synset Examples

a clarion call

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret; not ambiguous
  • Synonym: shrill - high-pitched and piercing in sound quality

Etymology

From Latin 'clarionem', a kind of trumpet, from 'clarus' (clear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæriən / (AmE), / ˈklæriən / (BrE)
a medieval brass instrument with a clear, shrill tone, used especially in fanfares and military music
medieval brass instrument with clear, shrill tone

Examples

The herald blew a clarion to announce the arrival of the king.
In the medieval parade, musicians played the clarion alongside drums and lutes.
The sound of the clarion echoed through the castle courtyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpet - a brass wind instrument with a bright, penetrating tone

Etymology

From Old French clarion (trumpet), from Latin clarus (clear, loud).

Synset POS: n
