Benjamin

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛn.dʒə.mən / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the youngest son of Jacob and Rachel, and the ancestor of one of the twelve tribes of Israel.
the youngest son of Jacob in the Bible

Examples

Benjamin was Jacob's youngest son according to the Old Testament.
Among the twelve tribes of Israel, one was descended from Benjamin.
Many biblical genealogies mention Benjamin due to his important lineage.

Etymology

From Hebrew Binyāmīn, meaning 'son of the right (hand)' or 'son of the south'.
