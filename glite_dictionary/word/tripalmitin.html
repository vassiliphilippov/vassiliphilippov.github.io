tripalmitin

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈpæl.mɪ.tɪn / (AmE), / traɪˈpæl.mɪ.tɪn / (BrE)
A triglyceride formed from the esterification of three molecules of palmitic acid with one molecule of glycerol.
a triglyceride of palmitic acid
medical

Examples

Tripalmitin is commonly found in various vegetable fats and oils.
Researchers measured the melting point of purified tripalmitin in the laboratory.
The formation of tripalmitin involves ester bonds between glycerol and three palmitic acid molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmitin - a triglyceride of palmitic acid, usually containing two or three palmitic acids

Etymology

From tri- (three) + palmitin (a glyceride of palmitic acid).
