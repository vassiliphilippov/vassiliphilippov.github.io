Edmund Husserl

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdmənd ˈhʊsərl / (AmE), / ˈɛdmənd ˈhʊzəl / (BrE)
(1859–1938) German philosopher who founded phenomenology, a philosophical movement exploring the structures of consciousness and experience.
German philosopher, founder of phenomenology

Examples

Edmund Husserl is widely regarded as the founder of phenomenological philosophy.
The writings of Edmund Husserl influenced many twentieth-century thinkers, including Heidegger and Sartre.
Many philosophy students study Edmund Husserl's major work, 'Logical Investigations.'
