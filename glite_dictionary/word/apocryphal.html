apocryphal

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɑː.krə.fəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒ.krɪ.fəl / (BrE)
Relating to or belonging to the Apocrypha, a group of ancient writings not included in the standard biblical canon.
relating to the Apocrypha (biblical writings)

Examples

Certain books rejected by the Protestant churches are considered apocryphal in their tradition.
She wrote a thesis on the apocryphal texts of the Old Testament.
The origins of the apocryphal stories in early Christian writings are still debated.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin apocryphus (apocryphal), from Greek apokryphos (hidden, obscure).

2. ADJ

/ əˈpɑkrəfəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒkrɪfəl / (BrE)
(of a story or statement) widely circulated but of doubtful authenticity; not regarded as genuine.
of doubtful authenticity

Examples

Many historians believe the story of George Washington and the cherry tree is apocryphal.
He dismissed the report as apocryphal until more evidence could be found.
Several apocryphal tales surround the origins of this ancient city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authentic - known to be true or genuine

Etymology

From Medieval Latin apocryphus, from Greek apokryphos (hidden).
