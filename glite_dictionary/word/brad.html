brad

1. NOUN

/ bræd / (AmE), / bræd / (BrE)
a small, thin nail with a slight projection on one side of the head, used especially for fastening thin pieces of wood
a small thin nail

Examples

The carpenter used a brad to attach the trim to the cabinet.
She carefully hammered a brad into the wooden frame to hold the pieces together.
A package of brads is essential for delicate craft projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - a slender metal pin used to fasten things together

2. VERB

/ bræd / (AmE), / bræd / (BrE)
to fasten, attach, or secure with brads (small nails or pins)
fasten with small nails (brads)

Examples

After aligning the trim, she carefully bradded it to the frame.
The craftsman brads the wood pieces together for extra security.
They spent the afternoon bradding decorative panels to the cabinet doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - to fasten with a nail or nails

Etymology

From 'brad', a noun referring to a thin nail or tack, Middle English 'brad' (a nail).
