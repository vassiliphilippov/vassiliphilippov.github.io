propellant

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / prəˈpɛl.ənt / (BrE)
Having the ability to propel or cause to move forward; tending to impart motion.
capable of causing motion or propulsion

Examples

The engineers developed a propellant system for the new rocket design.
Hydrogen peroxide can be used as a propellant agent in certain engines.
Her propellant ideas helped advance the entire field of robotics.

Synset Examples

propellant fuel for submarines
the faster a jet plane goes the greater its propulsive efficiency
universities … the seats of propulsive thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsive - tending to drive or push forward

Etymology

From Latin propellere (to drive forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00813359-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ prəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / prəˈpɛlənt / (BrE)
A substance that is used to drive or move something forward, especially one that causes movement in rockets or sprays.
substance that drives or propels

Examples

The rocket uses a solid propellant to achieve lift-off.
Aerosol cans release their contents using a pressurized propellant.
Scientists are developing a new kind of propellant for high-efficiency thrusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsive - tending to propel or drive forward

Etymology

From Latin 'propellere' (to drive forward), via 'propellant' (something that propels).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14715983-n
Synset POS: n
