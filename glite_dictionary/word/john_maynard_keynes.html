John Maynard Keynes

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈmeɪnərd ˈkeɪnz / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈmeɪnəd ˈkeɪnz / (BrE)
(1883–1946) An English economist known for advocating government intervention in the economy to maintain full employment and limit inflation.
English economist, advocate of government intervention

Examples

The economic theories of John Maynard Keynes revolutionized government policy during the Great Depression.
Many modern economists continue to debate the relevance of John Maynard Keynes and his ideas.
John Maynard Keynes argued that active government intervention is essential for economic stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Keynesian - relating to the economic theories of Keynes
