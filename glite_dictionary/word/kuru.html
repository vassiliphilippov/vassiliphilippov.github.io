kuru

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːruː / (AmE), / ˈkuːruː / (BrE)
a fatal, progressive neurological disease found among the Fore people of Papua New Guinea, historically transmitted by cannibalistic consumption of infected human brain tissue
rare fatal neurological disease
medical

Examples

The mysterious outbreak of kuru baffled scientists for decades until its cause was discovered.
Researchers learned that kuru was passed on through ritualistic cannibalism among the Fore people.
After cannibalism ceased, new cases of kuru all but disappeared in the affected region.

Etymology

From Fore (Papua New Guinea) language, meaning 'to shake' or 'trembling'.
