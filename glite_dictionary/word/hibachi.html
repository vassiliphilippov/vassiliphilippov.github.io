hibachi

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈbɑːtʃi / (AmE), / hɪˈbɑːtʃi / (BrE)
a portable grill or small brazier, originally from Japan, used for cooking food over charcoal
Japanese portable charcoal grill

Examples

We cooked vegetables and meat on the hibachi during our camping trip.
The chef prepared shrimp and steak on a sizzling hibachi at our table.
He brought his portable hibachi to the picnic for grilling hot dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a device on which food is cooked over an open fire

Etymology

From Japanese 火鉢 (hibachi), literally meaning 'fire bowl'.

2. VERB

/ hɪˈbɑːtʃi / (AmE), / hɪˈbætʃi / (BrE)
To cook food over a small charcoal grill, commonly known as a hibachi.
cook on a small charcoal grill

Examples

We're going to hibachi some steaks for dinner tonight.
She hibachied vegetables at the campsite, adding a smoky flavor to the meal.
I love hibaching shrimp for a summer cookout with friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - to cook food over direct heat, typically on a grill

Etymology

From Japanese 火鉢 (hibachi), meaning 'fire bowl'.
