Vittaria

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / vɪˈtɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical epiphytic ferns characterized by long, straplike fronds.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Vittaria genus includes ferns that thrive in humid, tropical forests.
Botanists collected several specimens of Vittaria for their study on epiphytic plants.
Some Vittaria species can be found clinging to tree branches in rainforest canopies.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Italian botanist Carlo Vittari's surname.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / vɪˈtɛərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical epiphytic ferns with long, straplike fronds, commonly found in moist regions.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

Several species in the Vittaria genus are known for their slender, straplike fronds.
The botanist carefully studied the unique reproductive structures of Vittaria ferns.
In tropical forests, Vittaria often grows on tree trunks as an epiphyte.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Italian botanist Carlo Vittari's surname.
