sonorously

1. ADV

/ səˈnɔɾɚəsli / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrəsli / (BrE)
In a way that produces a deep, full, or rich sound.
with a deep, resonant sound

Examples

The orator spoke sonorously, captivating everyone's attention in the hall.
Rain drummed sonorously on the metal roof throughout the night.
He always cleared his throat sonorously before beginning his lecture.

Synset Examples

the congregation consisted chiefly of a few young folk, who snored sonorously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonorous - having a deep, full, or rich sound

Etymology

From 'sonorous' + '-ly'; 'sonorous' from Latin 'sonorus' (resounding).
