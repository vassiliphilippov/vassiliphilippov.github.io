to commentate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.mənˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən.teɪt / (BrE)
to provide a spoken description or analysis of an event, especially for radio or television during live broadcasts
provide spoken commentary

Examples

He was invited to commentate on the football match for national television.
She has been asked to commentate during the Olympic Games this year.
It is not easy to commentate live, as you have to think quickly and react to the action on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - to make a remark or observation

Etymology

Formed from 'comment' + '-ate,' modeled after 'commentator.'

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.mənˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən.teɪt / (BrE)
to provide a spoken description and explanation of an event, especially a sports competition, as it happens, typically on radio or television
to act as a commentator

Examples

He was asked to commentate on the football match for national radio.
She loves to commentate on live tennis matches and often shares insightful analysis.
During the championship final, two experts commentated throughout the entire game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commentator - a person who provides a spoken description during an event

Etymology

Back-formation from 'commentator', influenced by the verb 'comment'.

Synset POS: v
