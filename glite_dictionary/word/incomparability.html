incomparability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɑmp(ə)rəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒmp(ə)rəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being so much better than anything else as to be beyond comparison.
state of being beyond comparison

Examples

The incomparability of her talent set her apart from everyone in the field.
Many critics have noted the incomparability of the artist's latest masterpiece.
The company prides itself on the incomparability of its customer service.

Synset Examples

Due to the incomparability of SmartMouse technology in the CAD industry, it has been a hit among users and CAD experts alike by providing a whole new UI experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being unique or one-of-a-kind
  • Synonym: superiority - the state of being superior or better than others

Etymology

From in- (not) + comparability (from Latin comparabilis, comparable).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɑːmpərəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒmpərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being impossible to compare or equate, often because of lack of a common measure or a significant difference in nature.
state of being impossible to compare

Examples

The incomparability of the two artists makes it difficult to judge who is better.
Some works of art have a unique incomparability that challenges critics and historians.
The committee struggled with the incomparability of the competing proposals.

Synset Examples

The aim is to produce a complete result which is satisfactory to the decision maker, through the reduction of the cases of incomparability between alternatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomparableness - the quality of being incomparable; similar meaning

Etymology

From in- (not) + comparability, based on comparable, from Late Latin comparabilis.
