rain

1. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
a rapid or abundant fall or outpouring of things happening in quick succession, often used figuratively.
rapid outpouring or succession (figurative)

Examples

The speech triggered a rain of protests from the audience.
After her promotion, she received a rain of congratulations from colleagues.
The new policy led to a rain of complaints at the customer service desk.

Synset Examples

a rain of bullets
a pelting of insults

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shower - a sudden, short period in which a lot of things fall or are given

Etymology

Old English 'regn', of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
(of water) to fall from the clouds in drops as precipitation.
to fall as precipitation from clouds

Examples

If it rains tomorrow, the picnic will be postponed.
It started to rain just as we stepped outside.
It has been raining heavily all afternoon.

Synset Examples

If it rains much more, we can expect some flooding

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drizzle - to rain lightly in very small drops
  • Degree Higher: to pour - to rain heavily with great intensity

Etymology

Old English regn (noun), rēnian (verb), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02762516-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
Water that falls from clouds in small drops as precipitation.
water drops falling from clouds

Examples

The steady rain forced us to postpone the picnic until the following day.
After a long drought, the farmers were grateful for the rain that finally arrived.
Children love to splash in puddles left by the rain after a summer storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precipitation - water that falls from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface
  • Antonym: snow - frozen precipitation in the form of white flakes
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain with very small drops
  • Degree Higher: downpour - a heavy rain

Etymology

From Old English 'regn' or 'ren', of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15033174-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
Water that falls from the clouds in drops, especially when considered as weather.
water falling in drops from clouds

Examples

The rain fell steadily all night, filling the rivers and streams.
We had to cancel the picnic because of the heavy rain.
After weeks of drought, the rain finally arrived and refreshed the fields.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very small drops
  • Degree Higher: downpour - a heavy, sudden rain
  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being dry; lack of rain

Etymology

Old English 'regn', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'regen' and German 'Regen'.
