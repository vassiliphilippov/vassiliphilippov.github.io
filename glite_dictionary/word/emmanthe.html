Emmanthe

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈmænθi / (AmE), / ɛmˈmænθi / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the borage family, commonly known as yellow bells.
genus of flowering plants (yellow bells)

Examples

The brilliant yellow blooms of the Emmanthe stood out in the arid landscape.
Botanists have studied Emmanthe for its adaptation to dry climates.
Gardeners value Emmanthe for its vibrant flowers and drought resistance.

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈmænθi / (AmE), / ɛmˈmanθi / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mint family, distinguished by having only one known species, commonly known as yellow bells.
genus of mint family plants (yellow bells)

Examples

The bright flowers of Emmanthe are a distinctive feature in Mediterranean gardens.
Botanists recently classified yellow bells under the genus Emmanthe.
The species belonging to Emmanthe flourish in dry, sunny climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phlomis - another genus in the mint family
