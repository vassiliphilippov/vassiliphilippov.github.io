snobby

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈsnɒbi / (BrE)
having or showing an attitude of superiority and dislike for people regarded as socially inferior
showing a sense of superiority; elitist

Examples

Her snobby attitude made her unpopular among her classmates.
He sounded snobby when he refused to eat at any restaurant that wasn't five-star.
Despite his wealth, James avoids snobby behavior and treats everyone with respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snob - a person with an exaggerated respect for high social position or wealth who seeks to associate with social superiors and dislikes people or activities regarded as lower-class
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From 'snob' (a person who believes themselves superior), + '-y' (adjective forming suffix).
