malfunction

1. NOUN

/ ˌmælˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmælˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A failure to operate or function normally or properly.
failure to function properly

Examples

The engine shut down due to a malfunction in the cooling system.
A computer malfunction caused the website to go offline for hours.
Pilots are trained to respond quickly if a technical malfunction occurs during flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success or inability to function
  • Synonym: breakdown - a mechanical or technical failure
  • Antonym: function - operate or work in the correct way

Etymology

From Latin 'male' (badly) + 'function'

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌmælˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmælˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
(of a machine, device, or system) to fail to work correctly or as intended.
fail to work properly (verb)

Examples

If the brakes on your car malfunction, you must get them repaired immediately.
The elevator malfunctioned and stopped between floors, requiring the fire department's assistance.
Technicians discovered that the device was malfunctioning due to a software error.

Synset Examples

the coffee maker malfunctioned

Related Terms

  • Antonym: function - to work or operate in the correct way
  • Synonym: to break down - to stop working because of a fault

Etymology

From Latin mal- (bad) + function (performance of a duty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01528083-v
Synset POS: v
