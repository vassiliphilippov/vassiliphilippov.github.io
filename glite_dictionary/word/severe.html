severe

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈvɪr / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or forceful) very strong or intense; extreme in degree or effect.
very strong or intense

Examples

The storm inflicted a severe blow to the coastal village, destroying several homes.
She suffered a severe injury during the championship match, which forced her to withdraw.
The building's roof collapsed under the severe weight of the snow after days of heavy storms.

Synset Examples

a hard left to the chin
a knockout punch
a severe blow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mild - not severe or extreme in degree

Etymology

From Latin 'severus' (serious, strict).
