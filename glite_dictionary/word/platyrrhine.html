platyrrhine

1. ADJ

/ ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (BrE)
relating to New World monkeys with nostrils that are far apart, or to people with broad, flat noses.
having broad or widely separated nostrils

Examples

The scientist studied platyrrhine monkeys in the rainforests of South America.
Certain indigenous groups are described as having platyrrhine nasal features in anthropological studies.
The platyrrhine nose distinguishes New World monkeys from their Old World counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - relating to Old World monkeys and apes with narrow, downward-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek *platys* (broad) + *rhis*, *-rhinos* (nose).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (BrE)
A monkey of a group native to Central and South America, characterized by a broad, flat nose with widely separated nostrils and usually a prehensile tail.
New World monkey with a flat nose

Examples

The spider monkey is a well-known platyrrhine found in South American rainforests.
Unlike catarrhines, platyrrhines have prehensile tails and broad, flat noses.
Researchers observed the social behaviors of several platyrrhine species in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - Old World monkey or ape with close-set downward-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek platy- (broad) + rhin- (nose).
