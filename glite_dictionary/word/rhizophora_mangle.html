Rhizophora mangle

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzɑːfəˈrɑː ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / raɪˌzɒfəˈrɔː ˈmaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A tropical tree or shrub, commonly known as the red mangrove, with prop roots that form dense thickets in coastal regions and fruit that germinates while still attached to the tree.
species of red mangrove tree

Examples

The dense roots of the Rhizophora mangle are vital in stabilizing coastal shorelines.
Researchers studied the unique germination process of Rhizophora mangle seeds.
A thriving community of fish lives among the prop roots of Rhizophora mangle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red mangrove - common name for Rhizophora mangle

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Rhizophora' meaning 'root-bearer' and 'mangle' from Spanish for mangrove.
