electrolysis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
A method of removing unwanted hair by passing an electric current through the hair root to destroy it.
hair removal using electric current
medical

Examples

She decided to have electrolysis to remove unwanted facial hair.
Many people choose electrolysis for permanent hair removal on small areas.
The clinic offers electrolysis treatments performed by certified technicians.

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + -lysis (from Greek lusis, 'loosening').

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The chemical decomposition of a substance by passing an electric current through a solution containing ions.
decomposition by electric current (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The industrial production of chlorine involves electrolysis of brine.
During the science experiment, students observed electrolysis splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen gas.
Copper can be purified using electrolysis in an electrolytic cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrolyte - a substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water
  • Synonym: electroplating - process of using electrolysis to deposit a metal coating

Etymology

From Greek 'ēlektron' (amber) + 'lysis' (loosening, dissolving).
