succulent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌk.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.jʊ.lənt / (BrE)
a plant with thick, fleshy tissues adapted to store water, typically found in arid climates
plant with water-storing tissues

Examples

Many people keep succulents on their windowsills because they require little water.
Aloe vera is a popular succulent known for its medicinal properties.
The desert landscape was dotted with succulent plants thriving in the dry soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a type of succulent plant adapted to dry environments, often with spines

Etymology

From Latin 'succulentus' (juicy), from 'succus' (juice).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌk.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.jʊ.lənt / (BrE)
tender, juicy, and full of moisture, especially when referring to food or plants
tender and juicy

Examples

The peaches were so succulent that the juice dripped down our fingers as we ate them.
She served a succulent roast chicken with herb stuffing for dinner.
Desert environments often feature succulent plants that store water in their thick leaves.

Synset Examples

lush fruits
succulent roast beef
succulent plants with thick fleshy leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juicy - full of juice; succulent, especially when describing food

Etymology

From Latin succulentus (full of juice), from sucus (juice).
