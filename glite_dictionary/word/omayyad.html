Omayyad

1. PROPN

/ oʊˈmaɪ.jæd / (AmE), / əʊˈmaɪ.jæd / (BrE)
the first dynasty of Arab caliphs, ruling from 661 to 750 CE with its capital in Damascus
first Arab caliph dynasty, capital Damascus

Examples

The Omayyad dynasty established their capital in Damascus after coming to power.
Many architectural achievements in Spain are attributed to the Omayyad period.
The fall of the Omayyad caliphate marked a significant shift in Islamic history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Abbasid - the dynasty that succeeded the Omayyads as caliphs of the Islamic world

Etymology

From Arabic 'Umayyad, referring to descendants of Umayya ibn Abd Shams.
