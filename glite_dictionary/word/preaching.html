preaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of delivering moral advice or criticism in a way that is seen as self-righteous or unwelcome
moralistic lecturing or rebuke

Examples

She was tired of his constant preaching about her lifestyle choices.
I don't need your preaching; I just want your support.
His preaching fell on deaf ears, as no one wanted to listen to his lectures.

Synset Examples

your preaching is wasted on him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - a long or serious speech of warning or reprimand
  • Synonym: sermonizing - speaking in a moralizing manner
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpritʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an address or speech on religious matters, especially one delivered during a church service
religious address or sermon

Examples

The pastor's preaching moved many members of the congregation to tears.
She listened attentively to the preaching during the Sunday morning service.
His preaching always offers comfort and practical advice to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermon - a talk on a religious or moral subject, especially given during a church service

Etymology

From Middle English 'prechyng', from Old French 'preachier', from Latin 'praedicare' (to proclaim, preach).
