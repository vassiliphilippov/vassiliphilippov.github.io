copse

1. NOUN

/ kɑps / (AmE), / kɒps / (BrE)
a small group of trees or bushes growing close together, often forming a thicket
small group of trees or bushes

Examples

A rabbit darted into the copse to escape the fox.
The children played hide-and-seek in a copse behind the house.
Morning sunlight filtered through the leaves of the copse near the river bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees

Etymology

Shortened from 'coppice,' from Old French *copeiz* (cutting), from Late Latin *colpaticium* (to cut).
