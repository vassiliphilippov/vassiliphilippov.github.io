Prosopium williamsonii

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpiəm ˌwɪljəmˈsɒniːaɪ / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpiəm ˌwɪljəmˈsɒniːaɪ / (BrE)
A species of freshwater whitefish native to rivers and lakes in the western United States and Canada, commonly known as the mountain whitefish.
species of North American whitefish

Examples

The Prosopium williamsonii can be found in cold, clear mountain streams and lakes.
Anglers prize the Prosopium williamsonii for its abundance and delicate flavor.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize Prosopium williamsonii populations in several river systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a common name for several species of freshwater fish in the Salmonidae family

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'prosopion' (face), species named for David S. Williamson, naturalist.
