plasticizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪ.zə / (BrE)
a substance added to plastics or other materials to increase their flexibility and workability
substance that makes plastics more flexible

Examples

A plasticizer is often added to PVC to make it softer and more flexible.
Manufacturers choose the type of plasticizer according to the desired properties of the final product.
Concerns have been raised about the safety of certain plasticizers used in food packaging.

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-izer', denoting something that makes plastic more workable.
