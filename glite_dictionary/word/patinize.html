to patinize

1. VERB

/ ˈpæt̬.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to coat or treat (a surface) so that it develops a patina, typically to achieve a desired color or texture
to coat with a patina

Examples

The artist decided to patinize the bronze statue for an antique appearance.
Over time, exposure to the elements can naturally patinize copper roofs.
Special chemicals are often used to patinize jewelry, giving it unique colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patinate - to cover with or acquire a patina

Etymology

From patina (thin layer formed on surfaces) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
