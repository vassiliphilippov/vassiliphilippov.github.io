Washingtonian

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɑːʃɪŋˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌwɒʃɪŋˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of George Washington or his principles.
of or relating to George Washington

Examples

The senator gave a Washingtonian response, emphasizing honesty and integrity.
Many admire the Washingtonian dedication to public service evident in his presidency.
The school adopted a Washingtonian approach to leadership education.

Etymology

From the name George Washington + English suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03049093-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌwɑʃɪŋˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌwɒʃɪŋˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
a native or resident of the city of Washington, especially Washington, D.C.
native or resident of Washington, D.C.

Examples

As a proud Washingtonian, she always celebrates the city's history and traditions.
Many Washingtonians commute to work using the Metro system every day.
Despite moving away years ago, he still considers himself a true Washingtonian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Yorker - a native or resident of New York City

Etymology

Formed from the name of Washington (the city), with the suffix -ian denoting origin or affiliation.
