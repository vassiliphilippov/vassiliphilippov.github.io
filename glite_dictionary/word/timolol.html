timolol

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪməˌlɔl / (AmE), / ˈtɪməlɒl / (BrE)
a beta blocker drug, used especially to treat high blood pressure, prevent heart attacks, and relieve certain kinds of glaucoma
a beta blocker medication
medical

Examples

After experiencing chest pain, his doctor prescribed timolol to help manage his heart condition.
The ophthalmologist recommended timolol eye drops to reduce intraocular pressure in glaucoma patients.
Research has shown that timolol can lower the risk of complications after a heart attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propranolol - another beta blocker medication
  • Synonym: beta blocker - medication that blocks beta-adrenergic receptors
