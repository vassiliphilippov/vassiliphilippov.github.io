concoction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkɑk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkɒk.ʃən / (BrE)
a food or drink prepared by mixing different ingredients together, often in an unusual or inventive way
mixture of ingredients (food or drink)

Examples

Her new soup concoction was surprisingly delicious, despite its odd color.
The bartender served a bright blue concoction with a slice of pineapple on top.
He was always experimenting in the kitchen, and last night’s concoction was a spicy vegetable stew.

Synset Examples

he volunteered to taste her latest concoction
he drank a mixture of beer and lemonade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things

Etymology

From Latin concoquere (to cook together), via Old French and late Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈkɑk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkɒk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of preparing something, especially a drink, medicine, or dish, by mixing different ingredients together
the act of mixing ingredients to create something

Examples

The chef's latest concoction was a soup made from seven different vegetables.
She watched as the pharmacist prepared a strange concoction of herbs and powders.
Mixing fruit juices with spices resulted in a refreshing concoction for the party guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things blended together

Etymology

From Latin concoctio (cooking together), from concoquere (to cook together).
