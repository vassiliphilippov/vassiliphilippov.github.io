forewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is the leader or spokesperson of a jury
female leader of a jury

Examples

The forewoman announced the verdict clearly and confidently in front of the court.
She was appointed forewoman after a unanimous vote by the rest of the jurors.
As forewoman, it was her responsibility to communicate the jury's decision to the judge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreman - a man who is the leader or spokesperson of a jury
  • Synonym: foreperson - the leader or spokesperson of a jury (gender-neutral)

Etymology

Formed from 'fore-' (meaning 'in front') and 'woman', by analogy with 'foreman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is in charge of a group of workers, especially in a factory or on a construction site.
female supervisor of workers

Examples

The forewoman supervised the assembly line, ensuring every product met quality standards.
As forewoman of the jury, she delivered the verdict to the court.
The construction team respected their forewoman for her leadership skills and clear communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreman - a person in charge of a group of workers

Etymology

Formed from 'fore-' (meaning 'before' or 'in front') and 'woman', modeled after 'foreman'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
