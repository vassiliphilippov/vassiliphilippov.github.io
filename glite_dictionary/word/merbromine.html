merbromine

1. NOUN

/ mɚˈbroʊmin / (AmE), / mɛˈbrəʊmiːn / (BrE)
a red mercurial compound used as a topical antiseptic, commonly known as Mercurochrome
a topical antiseptic compound
medical

Examples

The nurse applied merbromine to the child's scraped knee to prevent infection.
Older first aid kits often contained bottles of merbromine for treating minor cuts and abrasions.
Due to mercury content, the use of merbromine has been restricted in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mercurochrome - trademark name for merbromine
  • Synonym: antiseptic - substance that prevents infection

Etymology

From mercury + bromine, indicating its chemical components.
