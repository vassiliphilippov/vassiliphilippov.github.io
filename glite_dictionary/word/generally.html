generally

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛn.ɚ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ər.əli / (BrE)
In most cases; without distinguishing between individual instances or exceptions.
in most cases; without exception

Examples

Generally, people tend to be more relaxed on weekends than during the workweek.
The rule generally applies, but there can be a few rare exceptions.
He doesn’t like spicy food, but he generally enjoys trying new dishes.

Synset Examples

he is interested in snakes in general

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usually - in most cases or situations, frequently
  • Antonym: specifically - in a clear and exact way, for a particular case

Etymology

From Latin 'generalis' (relating to a whole class).

in most cases; usually

Examples

3. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəlli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəli / (BrE)
Usually; in most cases; as a rule.
usually; in most cases

Examples

Generally, the bus arrives on time every morning.
People generally prefer to avoid conflict when possible.
The meetings are generally held on Fridays, but sometimes they're scheduled for other days.

Synset Examples

by and large it doesn't rain much here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usually - in most cases; most often
  • Antonym: rarely - not often; seldom

Etymology

From general + -ly; originally from Latin 'generalis' (of a whole kind or class).
