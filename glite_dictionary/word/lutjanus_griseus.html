Lutjanus griseus

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtʃeɪnəs ˈɡraɪsiəs / (AmE), / lʊˈtʃɑːnəs ˈɡrɪsiəs / (BrE)
A species of snapper fish, commonly known as the gray snapper, found in shallow coastal waters of the western Atlantic, particularly near Florida.
species of snapper fish

Examples

The Lutjanus griseus is highly sought after by recreational fishermen along the Florida coast.
Marine biologists studied a population of Lutjanus griseus in the coral reefs of the Caribbean Sea.
Grilled Lutjanus griseus is a popular dish in many coastal Floridian restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray snapper - common name for Lutjanus griseus, a species of snapper fish

Etymology

From Latin; 'Lutjanus' is the genus of snappers, 'griseus' means 'gray' in Latin.
