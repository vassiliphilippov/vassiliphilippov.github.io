Mandeanism

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdiːəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / manˈdiːəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A Gnostic religion that originated in the 2nd and 3rd centuries, which regards John the Baptist as the Messiah and incorporates Jewish and Christian elements into a dualistic framework.
Gnostic religion from the 2nd-3rd centuries

Examples

Scholars have spent years examining the doctrines of Mandeanism and its historical development.
Some historians argue that Mandeanism preserves ancient Gnostic beliefs with unique interpretations.
The influence of Mandeanism can be seen in certain ritual practices in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnosticism - Religious movements emphasizing esoteric knowledge for salvation

Etymology

From Mandaean, the name of the religious group, plus -ism.
