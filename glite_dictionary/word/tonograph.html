tonograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.nəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɒn.əˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A medical instrument that makes a record of pressure or tension measurements, particularly intraocular pressure.
instrument recording pressure/tension, esp. in medicine
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist used a tonograph to assess the patient's eye pressure.
Advancements in technology have made the modern tonograph more precise and easier to use.
A technician demonstrated how to calibrate the tonograph before the start of the clinic.

Synset Examples

A tonometer is an apparatus that makes a record of tension measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonometer - instrument for measuring tension or pressure, especially intraocular

Etymology

From Greek 'tonos' (tension, tone) + '-graph' (writing, recording).
