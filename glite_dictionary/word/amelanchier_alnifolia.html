Amelanchier alnifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈlænkiər ælˈnɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈlaŋkɪə ælˈnɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to northwestern North America, with fragrant creamy white flowers and small, edible purple-red fruits, commonly known as saskatoon berry.
shrub with edible purple-red berries (saskatoon)

Examples

The Amelanchier alnifolia produces sweet berries enjoyed by both wildlife and people.
Many gardeners in Canada plant Amelanchier alnifolia for its attractive flowers and edible fruit.
Researchers are studying the nutritional value of Amelanchier alnifolia berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saskatoon berry - common name for the fruit of Amelanchier alnifolia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Amelanchier' of uncertain origin, 'alnifolia' from Latin 'alnus' (alder) and 'folium' (leaf), referring to the alder-like leaves.
