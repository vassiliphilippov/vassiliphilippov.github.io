verboseness

1. NOUN

/ vɚˈboʊsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈbəʊsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of using more words than are necessary; wordiness
the use of unnecessary words; wordiness

Examples

The author's verboseness made the report difficult to follow.
Critics complained about the verboseness in the politician's speech.
In academic writing, it's best to avoid unnecessary verboseness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
  • Antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point

Etymology

From Latin 'verbosus' (wordy) + '-ness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
