medalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who has been awarded a medal, especially in a sporting competition
person who has won a medal

Examples

The Olympic gold medalist was greeted by cheering fans at the airport.
She became a three-time medalist at the World Championships.
As a proud medalist, he gave an inspiring speech to young athletes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - person who wins first place in a competition

Etymology

From medal + -ist, indicating a person associated with medals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəˌlɪst / (BrE)
(in golf) the player with the lowest score in a medal (stroke play) tournament.
winner in golf stroke play

Examples

She was the medalist at the amateur golf championship, finishing four strokes ahead of the rest.
After an impressive final round, he was named medalist of the local club tournament.
The medalist receives special recognition for achieving the lowest total score in the event.

Etymology

From 'medal' + '-ist', originally for one who is awarded a medal, later specialized in sporting contexts.
