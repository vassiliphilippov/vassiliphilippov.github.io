urethra

1. NOUN

/ juˈriθrə / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːθrə / (BrE)
The tube through which urine is discharged from the bladder to the outside of the body, and which also serves as the genital duct in males.
tube for urine discharge from bladder
medical

Examples

A blockage in the urethra can make urination difficult or painful.
During the procedure, a catheter was inserted into the patient's urethra.
In males, the urethra also carries semen during ejaculation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ureter - tube that carries urine from the kidney to the bladder
  • Degree Higher: bladder - organ that stores urine

Etymology

From Greek ourēthra, from ouron (urine).
