altostratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.toʊˈstreɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˌal.təʊˈstraː.təs / (BrE)
A cloud of the mid-altitude type, forming a grayish or bluish layer that often covers the whole sky, usually found between 2 and 7 kilometers (about 6,500 to 23,000 feet) above ground.
mid-altitude layered cloud

Examples

The sky was covered by a thick layer of altostratus clouds, blocking the sun.
Pilots often check for altostratus when determining flight visibility.
Ahead of the storm, a broad sheet of altostratus stretched from horizon to horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratus - a low-level, layered cloud type
  • Degree Higher: cirrostratus - a high-level, thin cloud layer
  • Degree Lower: nimbostratus - a thick, rain-bearing cloud layer, generally lower

Etymology

From Latin 'altus' (high) + 'stratus' (spread, layer).
