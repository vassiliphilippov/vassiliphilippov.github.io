Saint Luke

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt luːk / (AmE), / seɪnt luːk / (BrE)
the Apostle of the New Testament closely associated with Saint Paul and traditionally regarded as the author of the third Gospel
Apostle and author of the third Gospel

Examples

Saint Luke is traditionally considered the author of the third book of the New Testament.
Many churches around the world are named after Saint Luke.
According to tradition, Saint Luke was a close companion of the Apostle Paul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Paul - Apostle in the New Testament and companion of Saint Luke
  • Synonym: Gospel of Luke - the third Gospel in the New Testament, traditionally attributed to Saint Luke
