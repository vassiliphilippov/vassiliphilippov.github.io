miasma

1. NOUN

/ miˈæzmə / (AmE), / miˈæzmə / (BrE)
An unpleasant or unhealthy smell or vapor, especially one that was formerly believed to cause disease.
unhealthy or unpleasant vapor

Examples

A thick miasma hung over the swamp, making it hard to breathe.
The miasma from the landfill drifted across the town during the summer.
In the novel, a miasma of smoke filled the dimly lit room.

Synset Examples

the miasma of the marshes
a miasma of cigar smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effluvium - an unpleasant or harmful odor or discharge
  • Antonym: aura - a distinctive atmosphere or quality, often pleasant

Etymology

From Greek *miasma* (pollution), from *miainein* (to pollute).

2. NOUN

/ miˈæzmə / (AmE), / mɪˈæzmə / (BrE)
A highly unpleasant or unhealthy atmosphere or influence, often one that seems to spread negativity or corruption.
unpleasant or unhealthy atmosphere

Examples

A miasma of suspicion surrounded the political scandal.
The abandoned house was filled with a miasma of neglect and decay.
After the accident, a miasma of fear hung over the community.

Synset Examples

the novel spun a miasma of death and decay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place, situation, or work of art

Etymology

From Greek 'miasma' (stain, pollution), from 'miainein' (to pollute).
