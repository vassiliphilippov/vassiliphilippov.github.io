sialolith

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈæləˌlɪθ / (AmE), / saɪˈaləlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone or calculus formed in a salivary gland or its duct.
stone formed in a salivary gland
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed a sialolith causing the patient's swollen salivary gland.
During the procedure, a small sialolith was removed from the duct.
Symptoms such as pain and dry mouth may indicate the presence of a sialolith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculus - a hard mass formed by minerals, especially in the body; a stone

Etymology

From Greek 'sialon' (saliva) + 'lithos' (stone).
