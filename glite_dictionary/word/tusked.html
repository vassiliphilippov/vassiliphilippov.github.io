tusked

1. ADJ

/ tʌskt / (AmE), / tʌskt / (BrE)
(of an animal) having tusks.
having tusks (of animals)

Examples

The tusked elephant is easily recognized by its long ivory tusks.
Many prehistoric mammals were tusked creatures, using them for defense or digging.
The tusked walrus hauled itself onto the ice to rest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tuskless - (of an animal) without tusks

Etymology

From 'tusk' + '-ed' suffix forming adjectives meaning 'having a specified thing'.
