insufficient

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.səˈfɪʃ.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈfɪʃ.ənt / (BrE)
not enough in amount, degree, or quality to meet a need or requirement
not enough; inadequate

Examples

There was insufficient evidence to convict the suspect.
The water supply is insufficient to meet the city's needs during a drought.
He was unable to attend the trip due to insufficient funds in his account.

Synset Examples

insufficient funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficient - not sufficient; not enough
  • Antonym: adequate - sufficient; good enough for a need
  • Antonym: sufficient - as much as needed
  • Synonym: scant - barely sufficient or not enough
  • Degree Higher: abundant - more than enough, plentiful

Etymology

From Latin 'insufficientem', from 'in-' (not) + 'sufficere' (to be sufficient).
