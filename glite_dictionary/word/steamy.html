steamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstiːmi / (AmE), / ˈstiːmi / (BrE)
(of a person or situation) expressing or feeling strong sexual desire; sexually charged.
sexually charged or desirous

Examples

The novel is full of steamy encounters between the main characters.
She gave him a steamy look across the crowded room.
The movie was criticized for its steamy love scenes.

Synset Examples

feeling horny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horny - feeling or showing strong sexual desire
  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings, especially sexual desire

2. ADJ

/ ˈstiːmi / (AmE), / ˈstiːmi / (BrE)
filled with steam or emitting moisture as vapor or mist
full of or giving off steam

Examples

The bathroom mirror was covered in steamy condensation after my shower.
We entered the steamy greenhouse, where tropical plants flourished.
On cold mornings, the street vendors sold steamy bowls of soup to passersby.

Synset Examples

a steaming kettle
steamy towels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - full of or covered with mist
  • Antonym: dry - lacking moisture or steam

Etymology

From 'steam' + '-y' (adjective suffix).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsti.mi / (AmE), / ˈstiː.mi / (BrE)
(of weather or a place) hot or warm and humid, often uncomfortably so.
hot and humid (weather or place)

Examples

It was so steamy outside that my clothes stuck to my skin.
We walked through the steamy jungle, sweating within minutes.
The weather turned steamy after the afternoon rainstorm.

Synset Examples

muggy weather
the steamy tropics
sticky weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muggy - (of weather) unpleasantly warm and humid
  • Synonym: humid - having a high level of moisture in the air
  • Antonym: dry - without moisture or humidity; not wet

Etymology

From 'steam' + '-y', where 'steam' comes from Old English 'stēam' (vapor, fume).
