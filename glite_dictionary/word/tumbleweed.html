tumbleweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmbəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəlˌwiːd / (BrE)
A plant, especially of arid regions, that breaks away from its roots when mature and is driven by the wind as a rolling mass.
wind-blown rolling plant of arid regions

Examples

A single tumbleweed rolled across the barren desert landscape.
Farmers often find tumbleweeds piling up against their fences after a windy day.
The sight of a tumbleweed drifting by added to the sense of emptiness.

Etymology

From tumble + weed, describing the plant’s movement and nature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmbəlˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəlwiːd / (BrE)
A plant of arid regions that breaks away from its roots when mature and is driven by the wind as a light, rolling mass, typically dispersing its seeds as it goes.
rolling arid-region plant

Examples

A single tumbleweed rolled across the empty road, driven by the wind.
During the drought, fields were covered with tumbleweeds instead of crops.
The old western films often show tumbleweed drifting through deserted towns.

Etymology

From 'tumble' (to roll over repeatedly) + 'weed' (wild plant), describing its rolling motion.

A plant of arid regions, especially in the western United States, that breaks away from its roots when mature and is driven by the wind as a light, rolling mass.
plant that rolls in the wind

Examples

During the drought, tumbleweeds piled up against fences and buildings.

Etymology

From 'tumble' (to roll over and over) + 'weed', referring to its rolling motion.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmbəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəlˌwiːd / (BrE)
A bushy plant of arid regions in North America that breaks off at the stem base when mature and is tumbled about by the wind, dispersing its seeds.
bushy plant that rolls with the wind

Examples

The tumbleweed rolled across the deserted street, pushed by the strong wind.
During the drought, fields were empty except for the occasional tumbleweed.
In Western movies, a tumbleweed often drifts by to emphasize the lonely landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian thistle - a common species of tumbleweed

Etymology

From 'tumble' (to roll over and over) and 'weed' (wild plant).
