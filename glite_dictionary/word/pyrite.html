pyrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.raɪt / (BrE)
a common mineral consisting of iron disulfide, typically pale yellow and resembling gold; also known as fool's gold
a yellow iron mineral, fool's gold

Examples

The shiny crystals in the rock turned out to be pyrite rather than real gold.
Geologists often find pyrite veins running through quartz deposits.
Many miners once mistook pyrite for valuable gold due to its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's gold - a common name for pyrite, resembling gold in appearance

Etymology

From Greek *pyrites lithos* (stone of fire), from *pyr* (fire), referring to its ability to produce sparks when struck.
