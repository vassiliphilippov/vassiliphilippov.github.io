ostinato

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstəˈnɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒstɪˈnɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A short phrase or rhythm in music that is repeated persistently throughout a composition or section.
repeated musical phrase or rhythm

Examples

The cello section played a driving ostinato throughout the entire movement.
The composer's use of an ostinato gives the piece a hypnotic quality.
You can hear a repeating ostinato in the bass line of this song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riff - short repeated phrase in popular music
  • Synonym: motif - a short musical idea or pattern

Etymology

From Italian 'ostinato' (stubborn), from Latin 'obstinatus' (obstinate).
