Hydrilla

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɪlə / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic plants in the frog's-bit family, with slender stems and whorls of narrow leaves, including the species Hydrilla verticillata
aquatic plant genus, includes Hydrilla verticillata

Examples

Hydrilla is considered an invasive species in many freshwater lakes and rivers.
Researchers are studying how Hydrilla impacts water ecosystems by crowding out native plants.
The rapid spread of Hydrilla can impede boating and fishing activities.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' (water), referring to its aquatic nature.
