drama

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑː.mə / (BrE)
The quality of being exciting, emotionally intense, or strikingly theatrical.
quality of excitement or intense emotion

Examples

The film's drama kept audiences on the edge of their seats from start to finish.
She brings a sense of drama to every event she attends, making everything feel more intense.
The drama of the rescue operation captured headlines around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being peaceful and untroubled

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *drâma* (action, deed), from *drāō* (to do, act).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
a situation or episode that is full of excitement, tension, or emotion, especially one that attracts attention
exciting or emotional episode

Examples

There was a lot of drama at the family reunion when old arguments resurfaced.
She likes to avoid drama and prefers peaceful surroundings.
Last night's meeting turned into unexpected drama when tempers flared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incident - an event or occurrence, especially one that is unusual or important
  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a period or state of peace and tranquility

Etymology

From Greek 'drāma' (action, play), from 'dran' (to do, to act).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑː.mə / (BrE)
A serious play intended for performance by actors on a stage; a literary work characterized by the representation of action and conflict.
a play intended for stage performance

Examples

The local theatre is staging a modern drama about family relationships.
She has always loved reading and performing in drama since high school.
His first drama was performed on Broadway and received critical acclaim.

Synset Examples

he wrote several plays but only one was produced on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comedy - a humorous play intended to entertain
  • Antonym: tragedy - a play dealing with tragic events and having an unhappy ending
  • Synonym: play - a literary work written to be performed by actors

Etymology

From Greek 'drāma' (action, deed), from 'dran' (to do, act).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑː.mə / (BrE)
A literary genre that consists of works intended to be performed on stage by actors; plays and other theatrical works.
theatrical literary genre

Examples

The school is putting on a modern drama about teenage life.
Shakespeare's most famous drama is often considered to be Hamlet.
She prefers reading drama to novels or poetry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: comedy - a type of drama intended to amuse and entertain
  • Degree Higher: tragedy - a type of drama dealing with serious or sorrowful themes
  • Synonym: play - a written work designed for theatrical performance; synonymous with drama in some contexts
  • Degree Lower: farce - a light, humorous dramatic work with exaggerated situations

Etymology

From Greek *drāma* (action, play), from *drān* (to do, act).
