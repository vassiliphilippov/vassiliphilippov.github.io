thoroughfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːrəˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈθʌrəˌfeə / (BrE)
a main road or public roadway that leads from one place to another and is used by the general public
main public road or street

Examples

The city council plans to widen the main thoroughfare to reduce traffic congestion.
Vendors set up their stalls along the busy thoroughfare during the festival.
The parade will proceed down the town’s oldest thoroughfare on Saturday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street - a public road in a city or town
  • Degree Lower: alley - a narrow passageway or lane between or behind buildings

Etymology

From Middle English 'thurghfare', from 'thurgh' (through) + 'fare' (journey, passage).
