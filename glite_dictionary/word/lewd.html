lewd

1. ADJ

/ luːd / (AmE), / luːd / (BrE)
showing or intended to excite lustful or sexual desires; indecent or obscene in a sexual way.
sexually indecent or obscene

Examples

He was suspended for making lewd comments toward his coworkers.
The movie was banned due to its lewd scenes and explicit content.
She found his lewd gestures deeply offensive and reported him to HR.

Synset Examples

libidinous orgies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lascivious - feeling or revealing an overt sexual interest or desire
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality, often relating to sex
  • Antonym: modest - having or showing a moderate estimation of one's abilities or not showy in sexual matters

Etymology

From Old English *lǣwede* (unlearned, common), later shifting to mean 'lascivious'.

2. ADJ

/ luːd / (AmE), / luːd / (BrE)
referring to behavior, speech, or material that is sexually suggestive or indecent, especially in a way considered offensive or intended to provoke sexual desire
sexually indecent or suggestive

Examples

The comedian was criticized for making lewd jokes during the family event.
Posting lewd images online can lead to disciplinary action at many workplaces.
He was shocked by the lewd comments written on the bathroom wall.

Synset Examples

lewd pictures
obscene telephone calls
salacious limericks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality or decency
  • Antonym: innocent - free from moral wrong; not intended to be sexually suggestive

Etymology

From Old English lǣwede (lay, unlearned, uneducated), later evolving to mean 'lascivious' or 'licentious.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00427259-s
Synset POS: s
