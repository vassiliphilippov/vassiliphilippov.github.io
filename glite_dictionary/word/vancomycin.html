vancomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæŋkəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌvaŋkəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat serious bacterial infections, especially those resistant to other antibiotics
antibiotic for serious bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed vancomycin to treat the patient's severe infection.
Some strains of bacteria have become resistant to vancomycin.
Vancomycin is often used when other antibiotics have failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug that kills or inhibits bacteria

Etymology

From the genus name Streptomyces orientalis (the source organism) and -mycin, a suffix used for antibiotics from Streptomyces.
