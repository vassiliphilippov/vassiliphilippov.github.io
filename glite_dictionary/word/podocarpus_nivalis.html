Podocarpus nivalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdoʊˈkɑːrpəs nɪˈvælɪs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊdəˈkɑːpəs nɪˈvælɪs / (BrE)
a low, wide-spreading coniferous shrub native to the mountains of New Zealand
New Zealand mountain shrub

Examples

The alpine gardens featured Podocarpus nivalis as a hardy groundcover.
Hikers admired the dense patches of Podocarpus nivalis clinging to rocky slopes.
Podocarpus nivalis survives extreme cold in New Zealand’s mountainous regions.

Etymology

From genus name Podocarpus (Greek podos 'foot' + karpos 'fruit') and Latin nivalis 'of snow'.
