diffusibility

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌfjusəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / dɪˌfjuːsɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance, especially a solution or gas, to spread or move from an area of higher concentration to one of lower concentration
ability of a solution or substance to diffuse

Examples

The diffusibility of the solution was tested in the lab to determine its rate of mixing.
Scientists compared the diffusibility of various gases under controlled conditions.
High diffusibility allows certain chemicals to spread more rapidly through membranes.

Synset Examples

The diffusibility of the solution was tested in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the process by which particles spread from high to low concentration

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' (to pour out) + English '-ibility' (ability).
