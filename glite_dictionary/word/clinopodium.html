Clinopodium

1. NOUN

/ klɪnəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / klɪnəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family, including wild basil and other species, often found in temperate regions
a genus of mint-like aromatic herbs

Examples

Clinopodium species, such as wild basil, are often found in temperate grasslands.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Clinopodium in traditional herbal remedies.
Flowers of Clinopodium attract many types of pollinators during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild basil - a common name for several species within Clinopodium

Etymology

From Greek 'klinō' (to lean) + 'pódion' (little foot), referring to the plant's characteristic sepal shape.

2. NOUN

/ klɪnəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / klɪnəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family, including wild basil and related species.
genus of aromatic mint herbs

Examples

The herb Clinopodium is widely used in traditional herbal remedies.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Clinopodium in the Mediterranean region.
Many gardeners cultivate Clinopodium for its aromatic leaves and small flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild basil - a common name for plants of the genus Clinopodium

Etymology

From Greek 'klino' (bed) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the plant's creeping habit.
