os hamatum

1. NOUN

/ ɑs həˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / ɒs həˈmeɪtəm / (BrE)
the hook-shaped bone in the wrist aligned with the fourth and fifth fingers; also known as the hamate bone
hook-shaped wrist bone (hamate)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the os hamatum can lead to persistent pain in the wrist.
The surgeon examined the os hamatum for signs of injury.
Golfers are prone to injuries involving the os hamatum due to their grip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamate bone - the hook-shaped carpal bone of the wrist

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + hamatum, neuter of hamatus (hooked).
