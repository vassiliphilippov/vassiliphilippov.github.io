haploid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a single set of unpaired chromosomes; characteristic of gametes such as eggs and sperm.
having a single set of chromosomes
medical

Examples

Most gametes in animals and plants are haploid and contain only one set of chromosomes.
The haploid number is restored to diploid after fertilization occurs.
Researchers studied the genetic traits of haploid yeast cells in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diploid - (of a cell or organism) having two complete sets of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'haploos' (single, simple) and '-oid' (form, resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a single complete set of chromosomes, as found in gametes.
having a single set of chromosomes
medical

Examples

In humans, gametes such as sperm and egg cells are haploid and contain 23 chromosomes each.
The fungus alternates between haploid and diploid stages during its life cycle.
Researchers studied the effects of radiation on haploid yeast cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diploid - having two complete sets of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek haploeides (single, simple), from haploos (single, simple) + -oid (form, like).

Synset POS: n
