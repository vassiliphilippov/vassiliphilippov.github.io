rapscallion

1. NOUN

/ ræpˈskæ.li.ən / (AmE), / ræpˈskæl.i.ən / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a mischievous or playful way, often causing trouble but not serious harm
mischievous or playful person

Examples

The children, being rapscallions, hid their teacher's keys as a prank.
Emma always had a soft spot for the lovable rapscallion in the story.
He laughed, calling his little brother a rapscallion for sneaking extra cookies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person
  • Synonym: scamp - a person, especially a child, who is mischievous in an amusing way

Etymology

Likely an alteration of 'rascal', possibly influenced by 'scallion' as playful exaggeration.

2. NOUN

/ ræpˈskæljən / (AmE), / ræpˈskæljən / (BrE)
A mischievous or unprincipled person; a rogue or scoundrel.
mischievous or unprincipled person

Examples

The story features a lovable rapscallion who always gets into trouble but means no harm.
Many believed the rapscallion was behind the mysterious disappearance of the pies.
Despite being called a rapscallion, he was admired for his clever tricks and bold adventures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a person who does bad things or is wicked

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'rascal,' influenced by archaic forms like 'rampallion.'
