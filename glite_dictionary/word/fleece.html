fleece

1. NOUN

/ fliːs / (AmE), / fliːs / (BrE)
a soft, warm fabric with a deep pile, used mainly for clothing such as jackets and blankets
soft, bulky fabric for clothing

Examples

She wore a fleece jacket on the chilly morning.
This fleece blanket is incredibly soft and cozy.
Many hikers prefer fleece clothing because it is lightweight and warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - fibers from sheep or other animals, often used in textiles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03367485-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ fliːs / (AmE), / fliːs / (BrE)
to charge someone an excessive amount of money; to swindle or cheat someone, especially by asking an unreasonable price
to swindle or overcharge

Examples

Tourists often get fleeced by street vendors near popular attractions.
The company fleeced its customers with hidden fees and inflated prices.
I can't believe I was fleeced into paying double for that concert ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: to rip off - to cheat someone, especially by charging too much

Etymology

Originally from Old English *flēos* (wool cut from a sheep), later used figuratively to mean 'strip of money.'

3. NOUN

/ flis / (AmE), / fliːs / (BrE)
the tanned skin of a sheep with the wool left on, used especially for making garments and coverings
tanned sheep skin with wool, used for clothing

Examples

Traditional jackets were made from fleece to provide warmth in cold climates.
The artisan crafted a rug from a large fleece sourced locally.
Ancient shepherds used fleece to make durable outerwear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheepskin - the skin of a sheep, especially when tanned with the fleece remaining; used as material

Etymology

Old English *flēos* (fleece, wool), from Proto-Germanic *fleusaz*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14787687-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ flis / (AmE), / fliːs / (BrE)
the woolly covering of a sheep or a similar animal, especially after having been sheared off in one piece
wool of a sheep or similar animal

Examples

The farmer sheared the fleece from the sheep in one smooth motion.
A thick fleece keeps the animal warm during cold winters.
She spun yarn directly from the freshly washed fleece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - the fine, soft curly hair forming the coat of a sheep

Etymology

From Old English flēos (fleece), related to Old High German flōz (fleece) and Latin plectere (to braid).

to remove the wool from a sheep or similar animal by shearing
to shear wool from an animal

Examples

During spring, the farmers fleece the sheep to collect wool for textiles.
It is important to fleece the animals gently to avoid injuring them.
They fleeced all the sheep before the hot weather arrived.
