Dylan ail Don

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɪlən aɪl ˈdɒn / (AmE), / ˈdɪlən aɪl ˈdɒn / (BrE)
In Welsh mythology, a Celtic god of the sea, son of Arianrhod, associated with the waves.
Celtic sea god in Welsh mythology

Examples

According to Welsh legend, Dylan ail Don was cast into the sea at birth and immediately swam like a fish.
Poets have drawn inspiration from the mysterious story of Dylan ail Don and his connection to the waves.
The tale of Dylan ail Don reflects the deep relationship between the Celtic people and the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arianrhod - Welsh mythological mother of Dylan ail Don
  • Synonym: Manannán mac Lir - Another Celtic god associated with the sea

Etymology

Welsh, literally 'Dylan son of Don' (Ail Don means 'son of Don').
