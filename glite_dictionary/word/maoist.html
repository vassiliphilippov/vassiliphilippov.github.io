Maoist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈmaʊ.ɪst / (BrE)
A supporter or advocate of Maoism, the communist doctrines of Mao Zedong.
advocate of Maoism

Examples

He was known as a prominent Maoist during the revolutionary period.
Several Maoists participated in the political protest.
She read extensively about Maoist ideology and its influence in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - an advocate of communism

Etymology

From Mao Zedong (Chinese revolutionary leader) + -ist (denoting adherents).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊɪst / (AmE), / ˈmaʊɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting Maoism, the communist doctrines of Mao Zedong, especially as practiced in China and later influenced political movements elsewhere
relating to Maoism or its supporters

Examples

The Maoist movement gained momentum in several rural provinces during the 1960s.
She wrote her thesis on Maoist ideology and its impact on Asian politics.
The party split after a faction adopted a strongly Maoist agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maoism - the communist doctrines of Mao Zedong

Etymology

From Mao (Zedong), the Chinese leader, combined with the suffix -ist to denote follower or supporter.

WordNet ID: oewn-02770062-a
Synset POS: a
