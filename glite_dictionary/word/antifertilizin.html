antifertilizin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.fərˈtɪl.ɪ.zɪn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.fɜːˈtɪl.ɪ.zɪn / (BrE)
A substance produced by animal sperm that specifically attracts or reacts with the egg before fertilization.
sperm substance involved in egg attraction
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that antifertilizin plays a crucial role in the early stages of fertilization.
The interaction between fertilizin and antifertilizin helps ensure species-specific sperm-egg recognition.
When the production of antifertilizin is disrupted, successful fertilization rates decrease significantly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fertilizin - a substance in eggs aiding sperm attraction and fertilization

Etymology

From anti- (against) + fertilizin (a substance inducing fertilization, from Latin fertilis 'fertile').
