Dialeurodes citri

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˌlʊr.oʊˈdiːz ˈsɪːtraɪ / (AmE), / daɪ.əˌljʊə.rəʊˈdiːz ˈsɪtrɪ / (BrE)
A species of whitefly that infests and damages citrus trees.
a whitefly species affecting citrus

Examples

Dialeurodes citri can cause significant damage to orange and lemon crops if not controlled.
Symptoms of infestation by Dialeurodes citri include a sticky residue and leaf yellowing.
The spread of Dialeurodes citri has made citrus farming more challenging in affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefly - a small, white-winged insect, many species of which are pests
  • Synonym: Aleyrodidae - the family of small plant-feeding insects to which Dialeurodes citri belongs

Etymology

From the genus name Dialeurodes (Greek dia- ‘through’ + aleuron ‘flour’) and species name citri (Latin, ‘of citrus’).
