sanitization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsanɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something clean and free from germs, typically by sterilizing or disinfecting it
the act of making something sanitary

Examples

Regular sanitization of kitchen surfaces helps prevent the spread of bacteria and viruses.
The sanitization of public spaces became a priority during the epidemic.
Proper sanitization of medical equipment is essential for patient safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning something to destroy bacteria
  • Synonym: sterilization - the process of making something free from bacteria or other living microorganisms

Etymology

From 'sanitize', from Latin 'sanitas' (health) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action).
