superload

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌləʊd / (BrE)
an exceptionally heavy or variable load applied to a structure, such as a bridge, especially by moving vehicles or equipment
very heavy load on a structure

Examples

The new bridge was engineered to support a superload of oversized transport vehicles.
Transporting the wind turbine blades required a special permit due to the superload they imposed.
Engineers monitored the superload as the convoy of trucks crossed the structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: load - weight or force placed on a structure
  • Degree Lower: live load - the weight of moving objects on a structure

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'load'.
