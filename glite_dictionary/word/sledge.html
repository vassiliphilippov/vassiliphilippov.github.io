sledge

1. NOUN

/ slɛdʒ / (AmE), / slɛdʒ / (BrE)
a heavy long-handled hammer used for driving stakes or wedges
heavy hammer with a long handle

Examples

He swung the sledge over his shoulder and brought it down on the metal stake.
The workers used a sledge to break apart the old concrete foundation.
Make sure to wear gloves when using a sledge for heavy demolition work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sledgehammer - a large heavy hammer with a long handle

Etymology

From Middle English *slegge*, from Old English *slecg* (‘hammer’), related to ‘slag’ and ‘slog’.

2. NOUN

/ slɛdʒ / (AmE), / slɛdʒ / (BrE)
a vehicle with runners for travelling over snow, typically pulled by animals such as horses or dogs
vehicle for travel over snow

Examples

The team loaded the supplies onto the sledge before setting off across the snow.
She watched as the sledge glided smoothly over the frozen lake, pulled by a pair of strong huskies.
In remote villages, people often travel by sledge when the roads are covered in deep snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - vehicle for travelling on snow, often used synonymously
  • Synonym: toboggan - a simple sled without runners; a flat-bottomed sled

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *sledge*, related to Old English *slæd* (valley, sled).
