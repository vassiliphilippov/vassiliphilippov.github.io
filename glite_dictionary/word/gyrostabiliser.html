gyrostabiliser

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪroʊˈsteɪ.bə.laɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪ.rəʊˈsteɪ.bɪ.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
A device consisting of a heavy gyroscope that spins on a vertical axis, used to reduce side-to-side rolling of ships or aircraft.
device reducing ship or plane rolling

Examples

The ocean liner was fitted with a gyrostabiliser to improve passenger comfort during rough seas.
Engineers recommended installing a gyrostabiliser to minimize the aircraft’s side-to-side oscillations.
Advancements in gyrostabiliser technology have significantly improved the stability of modern ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabilizer - a device for making something steady or stable

Etymology

From gyro- (relating to rotation or gyroscopes) + stabiliser (something that stabilizes).
