to overcrowd

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈkraʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkraʊd / (BrE)
to cause (a space or place) to become filled with too many people or things
to fill with too many people or things

Examples

The trains often overcrowd during the morning rush hour.
If we overcrowd the auditorium, it could become unsafe.
They were warned not to overcrowd the cages with too many animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crowd - to fill a place so that it becomes full or almost full
  • Synonym: to cram - to fill something tightly beyond its capacity
  • Antonym: underpopulate - to fill a place with fewer than the usual or necessary number of people

Etymology

From 'over-' (too much) + 'crowd' (to fill with people), first recorded in the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkraʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkraʊd / (BrE)
to cause (a place) to become too full or have too many people or things in it
make a place excessively crowded

Examples

Authorities warned not to overcrowd the stadium for safety reasons.
When too many books are overcrowded onto a shelf, it can bow and break.
The event organizers tried not to overcrowd the venue to ensure everyone had enough space.

Synset Examples

The students overcrowded the cafeteria

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crowd - fill a place so as to leave little or no room for movement
  • Synonym: pack - to fill tightly or completely

Etymology

Formed from the prefix over- (excessively) + crowd (gather closely); first attested in the mid-19th century.
