to defraud

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfrɔd / (AmE), / dɪˈfrɔːd / (BrE)
to illegally obtain money, property, or advantage from someone by deception or dishonesty
obtain by fraud or deception

Examples

The company director was arrested for attempting to defraud investors of millions of dollars.
They discovered that the contractor had defrauded several clients with fake invoices.
Anyone who tries to defraud the insurance company will face severe legal consequences.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or assets
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: to deceive - to mislead or trick someone
  • Antonym: honor - to act with honesty and integrity

Etymology

From Latin 'defraudare', from 'de-' (from) + 'fraudare' (to cheat).
