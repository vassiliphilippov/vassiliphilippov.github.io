unmoderated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɑdəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɒdəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not made less extreme or moderate; not controlled to reduce intensity or severity.
not moderated; not made less extreme

Examples

She delivered her criticism with unmoderated intensity, leaving no room for compromise.
Unmoderated comments on internet forums can quickly become offensive or extreme.
The debate grew heated as participants spoke with unmoderated candor.

Synset Examples

spoke with unmoderated harshness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderate - make less extreme or intense

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'moderated', from Latin 'moderatus' (to restrain, moderate).
