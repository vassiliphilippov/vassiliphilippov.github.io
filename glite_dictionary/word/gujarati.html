Gujarati

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡuːdʒəˈrɑːti / (AmE), / ˌɡʊdʒəˈrɑːti / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken by people from Gujarat, a state in western India
language of Gujarat, India

Examples

Over fifty million people speak Gujarati as their first language.
She learned to read and write in Gujarati at a young age.
The family uses both Hindi and Gujarati at home.

Etymology

From Hindi गुजराती (Gujarātī), from Gujarat (a state in India) + -i (adjectival suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡuːdʒəˈrɑːti / (AmE), / ˌɡuːdʒəˈrɑːti / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken primarily by people from the state of Gujarat in western India
Indo-Aryan language of Gujarat, India

Examples

Many people in the region speak Gujarati as their first language.
She is fluent in both Gujarati and English.
The book was translated from Gujarati into several other languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - the most widely spoken Indo-Aryan language in India
  • Synonym: Marathi - another major Indo-Aryan language spoken in western India

Etymology

From Gujarati ગુજરાતી (Gujarātī), meaning 'of Gujarat'.

She is a proud Gujarati who often visits her hometown in India.
