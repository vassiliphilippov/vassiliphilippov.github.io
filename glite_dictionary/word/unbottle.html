to unbottle

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɑːtəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
to express or release emotions, thoughts, or creativity that have previously been repressed or held back
release suppressed emotions or ideas

Examples

After months of silence, he finally decided to unbottle his feelings and talk to his friends.
Artists often need a safe space to unbottle their creativity and try new ideas.
Sometimes it's healthy to unbottle your emotions instead of keeping everything inside.

Synset Examples

He needed to unbottle his creativity in the art class.
It was time to unbottle her feelings about the situation.
They encouraged him to unbottle his ideas during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to bottle up - to restrain or suppress emotions, thoughts, etc.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' and 'bottle', by analogy with 'bottle up' meaning to repress emotions.
