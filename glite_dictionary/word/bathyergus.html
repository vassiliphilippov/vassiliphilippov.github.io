Bathyergus

1. PROPN

/ bæˈθaɪər.ɡəs / (AmE), / bæˈθaɪə.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing rodents known as dune mole-rats, native to southern Africa, including the Namaqua dune mole-rat and the Cape dune mole-rat.
genus of southern African dune mole-rats

Examples

The Bathyergus genus consists of burrowing mammals adapted to life in sandy environments.
Scientists recently observed a new burrowing pattern in the Bathyergus population of the region.
Members of Bathyergus are important for maintaining the ecological balance of their native habitats.

Etymology

From Greek bathys (deep) + ergon (work), referring to their burrowing habits.

2. NOUN

/ bæˈθaɪərɡəs / (AmE), / bæˈθaɪəɡəs / (BrE)

genus of African dune mole-rats

Examples

The Bathyergus genus is distinguished by its members' adaptation to sandy environments.

Etymology
