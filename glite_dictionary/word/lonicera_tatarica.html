Lonicera tatarica

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə təˈtærɪkə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə tətəˈrɪkə / (BrE)
A species of honeysuckle shrub native to southern Russia and central Asia, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
species of honeysuckle shrub

Examples

Lonicera tatarica is often planted in gardens for its attractive pink flowers.
Some regions consider Lonicera tatarica an invasive species due to its rapid growth.
The berries of Lonicera tatarica are not edible and can be toxic if consumed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - common name for plants in the genus Lonicera

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lonicera' after German botanist Adam Lonitzer; 'tatarica' refers to the Tatar region of Central Asia.
