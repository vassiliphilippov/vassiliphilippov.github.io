non-buoyant

1. ADJ

/ nɑn ˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / nɒn ˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
not able to float in a liquid or rise in air; likely to sink or fall rather than remain aloft or on the surface
not able to float; tends to sink or fall

Examples

The stone was completely non-buoyant and sank to the bottom instantly.
Because the object is non-buoyant, extra support is needed to keep it afloat.
Helium balloons float, but an air-filled rubber ball is usually non-buoyant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buoyant - able to float in a liquid or rise in air
