defender

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛndər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a person who protects someone or something from harm or danger
person who protects

Examples

She acted as a defender of animal rights, always speaking out against cruelty.
The castle needed a defender to protect it from invaders.
As a defender of the weak, he gained great respect in his community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protector - a person who protects someone or something
  • Antonym: attacker - a person who attacks or tries to harm others

Etymology

From Old French 'defendre', from Latin 'defendere' (to ward off, protect).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a person who fights to protect or resist against attack; someone who guards or stands up for something or someone
someone who resists attack or protects

Examples

The city relied on every defender to hold the walls against the invading army.
She was praised as a brave defender of civil rights during the protest.
The goalkeeper and the last defender worked together to stop the opposing team from scoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protector - a person who protects or shields others
  • Antonym: attacker - a person who attacks or assaults others

Etymology

From Old French 'defendre' and Latin 'defendere' (to defend).
