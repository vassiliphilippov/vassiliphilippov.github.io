raptorial

1. ADJ

/ ræpˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ræpˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of birds of prey, especially in reference to their predatory habits or physical adaptations.
relating to birds of prey

Examples

The owl's raptorial talons are specially adapted for catching small mammals.
Scientists studied the raptorial habits of hawks in the region.
Eagles have strong, raptorial bills for tearing flesh.

Synset Examples

raptorial claws and bill for seizing prey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - relating to or characterized by predation, especially animals that prey on others

Etymology

From Latin 'raptor' (plunderer, thief) + '-ial', relating to.

2. ADJ

/ ræpˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ræpˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a bird) living by hunting and feeding on other animals; predatory.
predatory, hunting other animals

Examples

Many raptorial birds have sharp talons for catching their prey.
The owl is a raptorial species that hunts small mammals at night.
Scientists observed the raptorial behavior of hawks in their natural habitat.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - relating to animals that live by hunting and eating other animals
  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants, not animals

Etymology

From Latin raptorius, from raptor (plunderer, robber), from rapere (to seize).

Synset POS: s
