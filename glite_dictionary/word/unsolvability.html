unsolvability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌsɑːlvəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌsɒlvəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to solve or resolve
impossibility of being solved

Examples

The unsolvability of the puzzle frustrated even the most experienced players.
Some mathematical problems are famous for their unsolvability.
Debates about the unsolvability of certain mysteries continue among philosophers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solvability - the property of being able to be solved

Etymology

From un- (not) + solvable + -ity (forming nouns).
