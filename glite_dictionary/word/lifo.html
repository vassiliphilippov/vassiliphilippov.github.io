LIFO

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪˌfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfəʊ / (BrE)
An inventory valuation method in which the most recently acquired items are assumed to be sold first.
inventory accounting method: last in, first out

Examples

Many companies switched to LIFO to reduce their taxable income during periods of inflation.
The use of the LIFO method can affect reported profits and inventory values.
Our accountant explained the differences between LIFO and FIFO during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: FIFO - inventory accounting method: first in, first out

Etymology

Acronym of 'last in, first out'.
