hematochrome

1. NOUN

/ həˈmætəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / hɪˈmætəkrəʊm / (BrE)
a reddish pigment found in certain algae, responsible for their coloration
red pigment in some algae
medical

Examples

The presence of hematochrome gives certain algae their distinct red hue.
Scientists studied the hematochrome content to understand the adaptation of algae to sunlight.
Under the microscope, the hematochrome pigment was clearly visible in the algal cells.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + khrōma (color).
