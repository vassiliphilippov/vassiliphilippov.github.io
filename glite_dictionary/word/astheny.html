astheny

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsθəni / (AmE), / ˈæsθəni / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal loss of strength or energy.
abnormal loss of strength
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with astheny after complaining of persistent weakness and fatigue.
Severe astheny can be a symptom of underlying neurological diseases.
Following the treatment, his sense of astheny gradually diminished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - lack of strength or firmness

Etymology

From Greek astheneia (weakness), from a- (without) + sthenos (strength).
