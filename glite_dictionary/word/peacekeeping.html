peacekeeping

1. ADJ

/ ˈpisˌkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
connected with preventing or resolving conflict and maintaining peace between hostile groups, typically through the deployment of international military forces.
connected with preserving peace by international forces

Examples

The United Nations sent a peacekeeping force to monitor the ceasefire agreement.
Several countries contributed troops to various peacekeeping missions across the globe.
He specialized in providing logistical support for peacekeeping operations.

Synset Examples

a peacekeeping force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacekeeping force - an armed force deployed to maintain or restore peace in a conflict area

Etymology

Formed from 'peace' + 'keeping' in the mid-20th century, especially in reference to United Nations activities.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpisˌkiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of preventing or stopping conflict between groups or nations, often by deploying military forces to maintain or enforce a ceasefire or truce, especially as an international operation
maintaining peace by military intervention

Examples

The United Nations sent a peacekeeping force to help monitor the ceasefire.
Canadian troops have a long history of peacekeeping operations around the world.
Effective peacekeeping requires the cooperation of all parties involved in the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacemaking - the process of bringing about peace, often through negotiation and diplomacy
  • Antonym: combat - active fighting in a war or conflict

Etymology

Formed in the mid-20th century from 'peace' + 'keeping', in reference to the activities of international organizations such as the United Nations.
