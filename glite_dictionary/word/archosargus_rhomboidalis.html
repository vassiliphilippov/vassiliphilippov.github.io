Archosargus rhomboidalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.koʊˈsɑːr.ɡəs rɒm.bɔɪˈdeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəʊˈsɑː.ɡəs rɒm.bɔɪˈdeɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of sea bream found in warm Atlantic waters.
warm Atlantic sea bream species

Examples

The Archosargus rhomboidalis is commonly caught by anglers along the Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists are studying the population of Archosargus rhomboidalis in the Gulf of Mexico.
Local fishermen value Archosargus rhomboidalis for its firm and tasty meat.

Etymology

From Greek 'archos' (ruler, chief) and Latin 'rhomboidalis' (rhomboidal, diamond-shaped), referring to the shape of the fish.
