laticifer

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtɪsəfər / (AmE), / ləˈtɪsɪfə / (BrE)
a specialized plant cell or duct that contains and transports latex
plant duct containing latex

Examples

Many tropical plants have laticifers that produce latex as a defense against herbivores.
The milky fluid seen when a dandelion stem is cut comes from its laticifer.
Natural rubber is collected from the laticifers of Hevea brasiliensis trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'latex' (liquid) + '-fer' (bearing), meaning 'latex-bearing'.
