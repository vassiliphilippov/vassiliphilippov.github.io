Chelonethida

1. NOUN

/ kəˌloʊnəˈθaɪdə / (AmE), / kɛˌləʊnɪˈθɪdə / (BrE)
An order of small arachnids known as false scorpions, characterized by their pincer-like pedipalps and lack of a stinger.
order of arachnids; false scorpions

Examples

The Chelonethida are often mistaken for true scorpions, but they lack the long tail and stinger.
Biologists discovered a rare species of Chelonethida living under the bark of an old tree.
Members of the Chelonethida play an important role in controlling insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudoscorpion - A small arachnid of the order Chelonethida, resembling a scorpion but lacking a stinger

Etymology

From Greek 'chelonē' (tortoise) and 'thidos' (resembling), indicating their shell-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ kɛləˈnɛθɪdə / (AmE), / kɛləˈniːθɪdə / (BrE)
An order of small arachnids commonly known as false scorpions or pseudoscorpions, resembling scorpions but lacking a stinger.
order of arachnids; pseudoscorpions

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Chelonethida while exploring a tropical rainforest.
Members of the Chelonethida are often mistaken for true scorpions due to their pincers.
The biodiversity survey documented several families within the Chelonethida order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudoscorpion - common name for members of Chelonethida; small arachnids resembling scorpions without a stinger

Etymology

From Greek 'chelōnē' (turtle) and '-thida', referring to the shape of the body.
