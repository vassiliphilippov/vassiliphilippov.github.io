cul-de-sac

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl də ˈsæk / (AmE), / ˌkʌl də ˈsæk / (BrE)
a street or passage that is closed at one end, with no way through
street closed at one end

Examples

The children played safely at the end of the cul-de-sac where there was no through traffic.
We bought a house on a quiet cul-de-sac to avoid busy streets.
Garbage trucks have to reverse out because the cul-de-sac doesn't allow for a loop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a street or passage with only one entry/exit; no through route

Etymology

From French 'cul-de-sac' meaning 'bottom of a sack'.

2. NOUN

/ kʌl də ˈsæk / (AmE), / kʌl də ˈsæk / (BrE)
a street or passage closed at one end; a dead-end street, especially one with a rounded end for turning vehicles
a dead-end street

Examples

Their house is located on a quiet cul-de-sac away from busy roads.
The children love riding their bikes in the cul-de-sac because there is hardly any traffic.
Delivery drivers often miss our cul-de-sac on their first visit.

Synset Examples

They found a lovely house at the end of a cul-de-sac.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a street with no outlet; a dead-end

Etymology

From French 'cul-de-sac', literally 'bottom of a bag'.
