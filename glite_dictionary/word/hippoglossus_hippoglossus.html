Hippoglossus hippoglossus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈɡlɑsəs ˌhɪpəˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
the Atlantic halibut, a large flatfish species found in the North Atlantic, valued as a food fish
large North Atlantic flatfish (Atlantic halibut)

Examples

Commercial fishing of Hippoglossus hippoglossus has declined due to overfishing in the North Atlantic.
The Hippoglossus hippoglossus can grow to impressive sizes, weighing several hundred pounds.
Marine biologists track Hippoglossus hippoglossus populations to monitor ocean health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halibut - a large flatfish of the genus Hippoglossus, especially valued as food

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the fish's shape.
