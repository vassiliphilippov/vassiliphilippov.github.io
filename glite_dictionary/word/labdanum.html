labdanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæb.də.nəm / (AmE), / ˈlæb.də.nəm / (BrE)
a sticky, dark resin obtained from certain species of rockrose, used in perfumes as a scent and fixative
resin from rockrose used in perfumes

Examples

The perfumer added labdanum to give the fragrance a warm, amber undertone.
Ancient civilizations valued labdanum for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
Genuine labdanum is harvested from wild-growing rockrose bushes in the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin labdanum, from Greek ladanón (a resin from the cistus shrub).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæb.də.nəm / (AmE), / ˈlæb.də.nəm / (BrE)
a dark brown or greenish resin with a fragrant odor, obtained from certain rockrose plants and used as a fixative in perfumes
fragrant resin used in perfumes

Examples

The perfumer added labdanum to the blend for its rich, amber scent.
Traditional incense often contains labdanum for its aromatic qualities.
She recognized the distinct fragrance of labdanum in the luxurious perfume.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin labdanum, from Greek 'lábdanon', denoting the resin.
