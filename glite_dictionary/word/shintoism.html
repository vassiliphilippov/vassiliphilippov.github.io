Shintoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪn.toʊˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈʃɪn.təʊˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
the indigenous religion of Japan, centered on the worship of spirits in nature and ancestors, with no formal doctrine.
indigenous religion of Japan

Examples

Shintoism emphasizes the importance of harmony between people and nature.
Many traditional Japanese ceremonies are rooted in Shintoism.
The ancient shrines across Japan are places of worship for followers of Shintoism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shinto - the Japanese religion devoted to kami (spirits); another form of the headword

Etymology

From Japanese Shintō (the way of the gods) + -ism.
