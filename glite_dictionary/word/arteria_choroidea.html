arteria choroidea

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə koʊˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə kɒˈrɔɪdiə / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the choroid plexus of the brain.
artery supplying the choroid plexus
medical

Examples

The arteria choroidea is crucial for delivering nutrients to the choroid plexus in the brain.
Blockage of the arteria choroidea can lead to serious neurological symptoms.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the arteria choroidea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choroid plexus - network of cells in the brain that produce cerebrospinal fluid

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'choroidea' (related to the choroid).
