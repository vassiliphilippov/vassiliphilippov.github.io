metamorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from metamorphosis, especially referring to rocks that have been changed in form by heat, pressure, or other natural forces.
relating to change by metamorphosis, esp. rocks

Examples

Marble is a type of metamorphic rock formed from limestone.
The region contains impressive metamorphic stages in the geological record.
The geologist analyzed the metamorphic changes that had occurred over millions of years.

Synset Examples

metamorphic stage
marble is a metamorphic rock that takes a high polish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: igneous - relating to rocks formed from cooled magma or lava
  • Antonym: sedimentary - relating to rocks formed by sediment deposition

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōsis (transformation), via Latin metamorphosis.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
undergoing or characterized by metamorphosis or a marked change in form, structure, or substance
relating to metamorphosis or transformation

Examples

The caterpillar enters a metamorphic stage as it becomes a butterfly.
The region is known for its metamorphic rocks, which were formed under intense pressure and heat.
Her metamorphic journey inspired others to embrace positive change in their own lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformative - causing a marked change in someone or something
  • Antonym: unchanging - not changing; remaining the same

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōtikos (causing transformation), from metamorphoun (to transform).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01533762-a
Synset POS: a
