Johannes Gutenberg

1. PROPN

/ joʊˈhɑːnəs ˈɡuːtnˌbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / jəʊˈhanəs ˈɡuːtɛnˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
(1400–1468) A German inventor and printer, famous for introducing movable type printing to Europe and developing the printing press.
German inventor of the printing press

Examples

Johannes Gutenberg revolutionized publishing with his invention of movable type in the 15th century.
Many historians credit Johannes Gutenberg with making books more accessible to the general public.
Without Johannes Gutenberg, the spread of knowledge throughout Europe would have been much slower.

Etymology

Johannes is a German form of John; Gutenberg is from German, meaning ‘good mountain’.
