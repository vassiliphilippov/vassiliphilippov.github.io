pine

1. NOUN

/ paɪn / (AmE), / paɪn / (BrE)
the pale-colored, durable, often resinous wood from any of various trees of the genus Pinus, used especially in carpentry and furniture making
wood from a pine tree

Examples

The cabin was built entirely out of pine to give it a warm, rustic atmosphere.
She admired the pine paneling on the interior walls of the house.
Furniture made from pine is popular due to its light color and affordability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oak - hardwood from oak trees
  • Synonym: spruce - wood from a spruce tree

Etymology

From Old English *pīn*, from Latin *pinus* (pine tree).

2. NOUN

/ paɪn / (AmE), / paɪn / (BrE)
an evergreen coniferous tree with long needle-shaped leaves, belonging to the genus Pinus, often valued for its wood and resin.
a type of evergreen coniferous tree

Examples

The forest was filled with tall, ancient pines that reached towards the sky.
The scent of pine needles reminded her of her childhood home in the mountains.
They used pine wood to build the new cabin because it is both strong and lightweight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - another type of coniferous evergreen tree
  • Synonym: spruce - a coniferous evergreen tree similar to pine

Etymology

From Old English 'pīn', from Latin 'pinus' (pine tree).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ paɪn / (AmE), / paɪn / (BrE)
to feel a deep, often lingering longing or yearning, especially for someone or something that is absent
yearn deeply for something or someone absent

Examples

After moving away, she would often pine for the familiar sights and sounds of her hometown.
He spent years pining for the love he had lost.
Though separated by distance, they still pine for each other's company.

Synset Examples

She ached for a cigarette
I am pining for my lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
  • Synonym: long - to have a strong desire for something or someone

Etymology

From Old English 'pīnian' (to torment, cause to suffer), from 'pīn' (pain, punishment).
