reticent

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtɪsənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪsənt / (BrE)
not willing to share thoughts or feelings readily; reserved or inclined to keep silent.
not talkative; reserved

Examples

Although he was usually reticent, he surprised everyone by delivering a heartfelt speech.
She remained reticent about her plans for the future.
The reticent witness answered only after being asked direct questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taciturn - habitually silent or uncommunicative
  • Antonym: talkative - inclined to talk a lot; chatty

Etymology

From Latin 'reticens', present participle of 'reticēre' meaning 'to keep silent'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛt̬.ə.sənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛt.ɪ.sənt / (BrE)
Not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily; reserved or restrained in manner.
reserved; not readily expressive

Examples

Despite the prying questions, he remained reticent about his personal life.
She gave a reticent smile and avoided eye contact during the meeting.
Their new manager is reticent with praise, preferring to give feedback privately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotions or opinions
  • Antonym: forthcoming - willing to talk or share information

Etymology

From Latin reticens, reticent-, present participle of reticēre (to keep silent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00724494-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtɪsənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪsənt / (BrE)
not willing to share one's thoughts or feelings easily; reluctant to draw attention to oneself.
reluctant to share thoughts or draw attention

Examples

Although he was very talented, Jack remained reticent about his achievements.
The manager seemed reticent when asked about the company's future plans.
She is naturally reticent, preferring to listen rather than speak in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - showing self-restraint in words or actions; synonym of 'reticent'
  • Antonym: forthcoming - willing to divulge information or feelings; opposite of 'reticent'

Etymology

From Latin 'reticent-', from 'reticere' meaning 'to keep silent'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00158534-s
Synset POS: s
