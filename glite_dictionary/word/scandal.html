scandal

1. NOUN

/ ˈskændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈskændl / (BrE)
disgraceful gossip or talk about the private lives of other people, often causing public outrage or embarrassment.
disgraceful gossip about others

Examples

The latest celebrity scandal was discussed by everyone at the office.
That newspaper is always printing some new scandal about politicians.
She hates being the subject of scandal and tries to keep her life private.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - informal talk about other people's private affairs
  • Synonym: rumor - a currently circulating story of uncertain or doubtful truth

Etymology

From Old French 'scandale', from Latin 'scandalum' (cause for offense, scandal), from Greek 'skandalon' (snare, stumbling block).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskændl / (AmE), / ˈskændl / (BrE)
an event or action that causes public outrage or shock due to its immoral, illegal, or improper nature
a disgraceful or shocking event

Examples

The politician resigned after a major scandal rocked the government.
The company tried to cover up the scandal to avoid damaging its reputation.
Years later, people still talked about the scandal that nearly destroyed the celebrity's career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controversy - a prolonged public dispute or debate
  • Antonym: honor - high respect or esteem; absence of disgrace

Etymology

From Latin 'scandalum' (cause for offense, stumbling block), via Old French and Late Latin.
