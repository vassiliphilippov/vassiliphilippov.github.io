Prumnopitys amara

1. NOUN

/ prum.noʊˈpɪ.tis əˈmɑː.rə / (AmE), / prum.nəʊˈpɪ.tɪs əˈmɑː.rə / (BrE)
A large, fast-growing tropical evergreen tree with glossy, lance-shaped leaves, native to rainforests from Sumatra and the Philippines to northern Queensland.
a tropical evergreen tree species

Examples

The Prumnopitys amara is prized for its rapid growth and attractive foliage in botanical gardens.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Prumnopitys amara in the rainforest canopy.
The timber of Prumnopitys amara is valued in some regions for construction purposes.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: genus 'Prumnopitys' and species epithet 'amara' meaning 'bitter' in Latin.
