Habenaria hookeri

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæbəˈnɛriə ˈhʊkəri / (AmE), / ˌhabəˈnɛːrɪə ˈhʊkəri / (BrE)
A species of long-spurred orchid with basal leaves and petals that converge under the upper sepal.
a species of long-spurred orchid

Examples

The rare Habenaria hookeri was discovered in a remote forest clearing by botanists.
Gardeners value Habenaria hookeri for its elegant long-spurred flowers.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of Habenaria hookeri in its native range.

Etymology

Named after Sir William Jackson Hooker, an English botanist, and the genus Habenaria (from Latin 'habena', meaning 'rein' or 'strap', referring to the shape of the petals).
