warpath

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌpɑːθ / (BrE)
A course or state of being prepared for or engaged in warfare or conflict, often used metaphorically for being angry and ready to confront or attack someone.
course leading to battle or conflict

Examples

After hearing the news, the general was on the warpath and ready for battle.
She's been on the warpath all morning, furious about the mistake in the report.
When the rival company tried to poach their clients, they went on the warpath to protect their interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive action against a person or place
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or quiet; absence of war

Etymology

From Native American usage, originally referring to the route taken by a war party.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːpɑːθ / (BrE)
A mood or condition of being angry or ready to argue or fight, especially in pursuit of retribution.
hostile or belligerent mood

Examples

He was clearly on the warpath after hearing about the broken window.
If you criticize her work today, you'll find her on the warpath.
The manager is on the warpath over the missed deadline.

Synset Examples

the chief is on the warpath today

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Degree Higher: rage - violent and uncontrolled anger

Etymology

From the practice of Native American groups preparing for war, entering the 'warpath' as a route to battle.
