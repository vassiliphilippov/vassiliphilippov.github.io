Copehan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpəhæn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpəhæn / (BrE)
A group of Penutian languages historically spoken by Indigenous peoples west of the Sacramento River in California.
Penutian language group west of Sacramento River

Examples

Linguists have studied the Copehan languages to better understand indigenous cultures of California.
The Copehan language group includes several dialects once spoken near the Sacramento River.
Efforts are underway to document and revitalize Copehan languages among descendant communities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Penutian - a proposed language family of several indigenous languages of western North America
