Euphorbia medusae

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbiə məˈdjuːsi / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə mɪˈdjuːsiː / (BrE)
A dwarf succulent shrub native to Africa, characterized by numerous slender, drooping branches, belonging to the spurge family.
African dwarf succulent shrub species

Examples

The unique appearance of Euphorbia medusae makes it a popular choice among succulent collectors.
Botanists admire Euphorbia medusae for its numerous, snake-like branches.
Euphorbia medusae thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Euphorbia*, honoring the Greek physician Euphorbus, and species name *medusae*, referencing the snake-haired Medusa of Greek mythology, alluding to the plant's branching shape.
