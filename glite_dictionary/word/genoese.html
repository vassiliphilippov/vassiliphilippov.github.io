Genoese

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.əˈwiz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.əʊˈiːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Genoa, a city in northwestern Italy
native or inhabitant of Genoa, Italy

Examples

The Genoese were known for their maritime trading empire in the Middle Ages.
She met a group of Genoese while traveling through Italy.
Many Genoese emigrated to the Americas in search of new opportunities.

Etymology

From Italian 'Genovese', from 'Genova' (Genoa).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒɛnoʊˈiz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəʊˈiːz / (BrE)
relating to Genoa or its inhabitants; of or characteristic of Genoa, Italy
of or relating to Genoa, Italy or its people

Examples

The Genoese sailor Christopher Columbus changed the course of history.
Many Genoese merchants were influential in Mediterranean trade.
Her Genoese ancestry is a source of family pride.

Synset Examples

the Genoese sailor we call Columbus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Genovese - another form of the adjective for Genoa or its people

Etymology

From Italian *Genovese*, from Latin *Genua* (Genoa).
