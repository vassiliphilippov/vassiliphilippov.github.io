Charadrius

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪdriəs / (AmE), / kəˈreɪdrɪəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Charadriidae, comprising various species of plovers, small wading birds found worldwide
genus of plovers (birds)

Examples

Biologists have discovered a new species within the genus Charadrius along the coastline.
Many shorebirds, such as those in Charadrius, exhibit complex nesting behaviors.
The little ringed plover is classified as Charadrius dubius in taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - a wading bird belonging to the family Charadriidae

Etymology

From Latin Charadrius, from Ancient Greek kharadrios (a bird found in ravines).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪdriəs / (AmE), / kəˈreɪdrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized plovers, wading birds found worldwide, belonging to the family Charadriidae.
a genus of plovers (wading birds)

Examples

The Charadrius genus includes many species of plovers commonly found along shorelines.
Researchers studied migration patterns of Charadrius birds across different continents.
The little ringed plover, a member of Charadrius, nests near freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Latin *Charadrius*, a bird mentioned in the Vulgate Bible, ultimately from Ancient Greek *kharadrios* (a bird found in river valleys).
