esthesis

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈθiːsɪs / (AmE), / ɛsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
the elementary or basic awareness resulting from stimulation of a sense organ; a simple, unelaborated sensation
basic sensory awareness; simple sensation
medical

Examples

He lost all esthesis in his fingertips after the accident.
The patient reported a disturbance of esthesis in her left leg.
Basic esthesis occurs before higher-level processing, such as recognition or interpretation.

Synset Examples

a sensation of touch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensation - an awareness resulting from stimulation of a sense organ

Etymology

From Greek aisthēsis (sensation, perception, feeling).
