trollop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːləp / (AmE), / ˈtrɒləp / (BrE)
a woman regarded as dirty or untidy, or as having loose morals
disparaging term for an untidy or promiscuous woman

Examples

He dismissed her as a trollop because of her unkempt appearance.
The novel's villain referred to the servant girl as a mere trollop.
She was unfairly labeled a trollop by the gossips in her village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slattern - a dirty or untidy woman
  • Synonym: hussy - an impudent or immoral girl or woman

Etymology

Origin unknown; in use since the mid-18th century as a term for a slovenly woman.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑː.ləp / (AmE), / ˈtrɒl.əp / (BrE)
A woman who is considered sexually promiscuous, typically used as a derogatory term.
sexually promiscuous woman (derogatory)
offensive archaic

Examples

The novel depicts her as a trollop who scandalizes the conservative town.
He unfairly called her a trollop for simply enjoying herself at the party.
In Victorian literature, the word trollop was often used to shame independent women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slut - a woman considered to have many casual sexual partners (offensive)
  • Synonym: harlot - a woman who has many sexual partners, especially for money (archaic/derogatory)
  • Antonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity

Etymology

Origin unknown; recorded from the 17th century, possibly influenced by 'troll' (verb, to stroll or saunter) or 'trollop' (slovenly woman).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːləp / (AmE), / ˈtrɒləp / (BrE)
a woman who is considered to have loose sexual morals; a promiscuous woman, often used disparagingly or insultingly.
promiscuous woman (disparaging)
offensive archaic

Examples

She was cruelly called a trollop by the townsfolk after the scandal.
In old literature, the term trollop often describes women rejected by society for their relationships.
The tabloid unfairly portrayed her as a trollop, causing a public outcry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slut - a woman who has many casual sexual partners
  • Synonym: adulterer - someone who commits adultery
  • Antonym: prude - a person who is or claims to be easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity

Etymology

Origin unknown, recorded in English since the 17th century.
