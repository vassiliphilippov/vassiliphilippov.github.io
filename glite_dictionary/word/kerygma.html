kerygma

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪgmə / (AmE), / kɛˈrɪgmə / (BrE)
the proclamation or preaching of the Christian gospel, especially as carried out by the early church
preaching of the Christian gospel

Examples

The early apostles spread the kerygma throughout the ancient world.
Many sermons today still echo elements of the original kerygma.
Scholars debate how the kerygma of the early church differs from later doctrines.

Etymology

From Greek kerygma (proclamation), from kēryssein (to proclaim).
