faded

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
no longer having the original brightness, freshness, or strength of color; having become dull or washed out
having lost color or brightness

Examples

She wore her favorite pair of faded jeans to the party.
The curtains in the living room were faded after years of sunlight.
The old photograph looked faded but held many memories.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - having a lot of light or color; not faded
  • Synonym: washed-out - lacking color or brightness; faded

Etymology

Past participle of 'fade', from Old French 'fader', ultimately from Latin 'fatuus' (foolish, insipid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
having lost brightness, strength, or freshness; reduced in intensity or vividness, especially due to age or exposure.
diminished in brightness or strength

Examples

The old photograph showed faded colors after years of sunlight exposure.
Her memories of childhood have faded over time.
The denim jacket looked stylishly faded around the collar and sleeves.

Synset Examples

the faded tones of an old recording

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - full of light or vivid in color

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fade', from Middle English 'faden', probably from Old French 'fader' (to vanish, diminish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00885408-s
Synset POS: s
