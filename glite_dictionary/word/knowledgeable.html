knowledgeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑlɪdʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnɒlɪdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
Well informed or highly educated about a particular subject through study or experience.
well informed; highly educated

Examples

She is very knowledgeable about ancient history and loves to share interesting facts.
The tour guide was so knowledgeable that every question was answered in great detail.
Employers appreciate candidates who are knowledgeable in their field.

Synset Examples

this girl, so intimate with nature
knowledgeable about the technique of painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informed - having or showing knowledge about a particular subject
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

Formed from 'knowledge' + '-able', first recorded in the 16th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɑlɪdʒəbl / (AmE), / ˈnɒlɪdʒəbl / (BrE)
Having or showing a lot of knowledge, understanding, or education in a particular subject or in general.
well-informed; highly educated

Examples

She is very knowledgeable about art history and often gives lectures at the museum.
Our guide was extremely knowledgeable, answering all of our questions about the ancient ruins.
He proved to be a knowledgeable critic, offering insightful reviews of the performance.

Synset Examples

knowing instructors
a knowledgeable critic
a knowledgeable audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informed - having or showing knowledge about a particular subject or situation
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From 'knowledge' + suffix '-able', meaning capable of having knowledge.
