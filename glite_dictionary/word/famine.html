famine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪn / (BrE)
A severe shortage of food in a region, causing widespread hunger, starvation, and often death.
severe shortage of food, causing starvation

Examples

The region suffered a terrible famine after years of drought and failed harvests.
International aid was crucial during the famine to prevent further loss of life.
Many people migrated to escape the effects of the famine in their homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starvation - Suffering or death caused by lack of food
  • Antonym: plenty - A situation in which food and resources are abundant

Etymology

From Latin 'fames' (hunger).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪn / (BrE)
a severe shortage or extreme scarcity of something essential
an extreme shortage or scarcity

Examples

After the war, the country suffered a famine of medical supplies.
There was a famine of new ideas in the organization for years.
Unexpectedly, the tech industry experienced a famine of skilled engineers last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcity - a state of being in short supply; shortage
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plentifulness

Etymology

From Old French *famine*, from Latin *fames* (hunger).
