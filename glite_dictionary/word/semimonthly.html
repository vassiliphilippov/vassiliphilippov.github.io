semimonthly

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (BrE)
Happening or appearing twice each month.
occurring twice a month

Examples

The magazine is published on a semimonthly schedule.
Employees are paid semimonthly, on the 15th and the last day of each month.
We hold staff meetings semimonthly to keep everyone updated.

Synset Examples

a semimonthly publication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bimonthly - occurring every two months or twice a month

Etymology

From Latin 'semi' (half) + English 'monthly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01977079-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (BrE)
A periodical or publication issued twice a month, usually resulting in 24 issues each year.
publication issued twice a month

Examples

The company newsletter is a semimonthly sent out on the first and fifteenth of every month.
She writes an article for a semimonthly that focuses on financial topics for young adults.
The library subscribes to several semimonthlies covering science and technology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bimonthly - a publication issued every two months
  • Degree Higher: weekly - a publication issued every week

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) + 'monthly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06606580-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmʌnθli / (BrE)
Occurring or done twice a month.
twice a month

Examples

Salaries are paid semimonthly at her company.
The newsletter is published semimonthly, reaching readers twice each month.
They hold semimonthly meetings to discuss department progress.

Synset Examples

salaries are paid semimonthly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bimonthly - occurring once every two months or, less commonly, twice a month

Etymology

From prefix 'semi-' (half, occurring twice) + 'monthly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256661-r
Synset POS: r
