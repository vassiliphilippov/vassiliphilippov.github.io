Gnostic

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːstɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
relating to Gnosticism or its adherents; associated with the beliefs, practices, or writings of the Gnostics
relating to Gnosticism or its adherents

Examples

The scholar devoted years to studying Gnostic texts and traditions.
Many early Christian writings were influenced by Gnostic beliefs.
The museum's exhibit includes several Gnostic manuscripts from the second century.

Synset Examples

Gnostic writings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnostic - one who claims neither faith nor disbelief in God; relating to agnosticism

Etymology

From Greek *gnōstikos* (knowing), from *gnōsis* (knowledge), referring to esoteric spiritual knowledge.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
A person who advocates or adheres to Gnosticism, an ancient religious movement emphasizing esoteric knowledge of spiritual truth.
adherent of Gnosticism

Examples

The early Christian church struggled with the influence of Gnostic teachings.
Some Gnostics believed that salvation came through secret knowledge.
As a Gnostic, he devoted his life to the pursuit of spiritual truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gnosticism - ancient religious belief system focused on spiritual knowledge

Etymology

From Greek *gnōstikos* (knowing), from *gnōsis* (knowledge).

Synset POS: n
