Caryocar nuciferum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.i.oʊˌkɑr nuːˈsɪf.ərəm / (AmE), / ˌkæ.rɪ.əʊˌkɑː njuːˈsɪf.ərəm / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to South America, with trifoliate leaves and edible nutlike seeds used as food and for cooking oil.
South American evergreen tree; edible seeds

Examples

The oil extracted from the Caryocar nuciferum is highly prized in local South American cuisine.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Caryocar nuciferum in different rainforest regions.
The seeds of the Caryocar nuciferum are commonly referred to as 'butter nuts' and are used in cooking.

Etymology

From genus Caryocar and Latin nuciferum, meaning 'nut-bearing'.
