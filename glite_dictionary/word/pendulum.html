pendulum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.dʒə.ləm / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.djʊ.ləm / (BrE)
a device consisting of a weight suspended from a fixed point so that it can swing freely back and forth, typically under the influence of gravity
a swinging weight on a fixed point

Examples

The grandfather clock ticks as its pendulum swings steadily with each second.
Galileo studied the motion of a pendulum to understand principles of physics.
A pendulum can be used in experiments to measure gravitational acceleration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - movement back and forth like a pendulum
  • Synonym: metronome - device using a pendulum to mark time

Etymology

From Latin 'pendulum', neuter of 'pendulus' (hanging), from 'pendere' (to hang).
