BJA

1. NOUN

/ ˌbiː dʒeɪ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌbiː dʒeɪ ˈeɪ / (BrE)
abbreviation for Bureau of Justice Assistance, an agency within the U.S. Department of Justice that helps local agencies reduce or prevent crime, violence, and drug abuse.
Bureau of Justice Assistance (U.S. DOJ agency)
american

Examples

The BJA awarded several grants to support community policing efforts this year.
Local law enforcement agencies often collaborate with the BJA for training and resources.
The BJA plays a key role in developing strategies to prevent drug abuse in cities.
