designate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛzɪɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt / (BrE)
to officially choose someone to do a particular job or to be something
to assign a person to a post or task

Examples

The manager will designate a team leader for the new project.
She was designated as the official spokesperson for the event.
You must designate someone to handle the logistics during your absence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign someone to a job or official position
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a job or position

Etymology

From Latin designatus, past participle of designare (to mark out, appoint).

Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛzɪɡneɪt / (BrE)
Appointed to an office or position but not yet installed or officially in charge.
appointed but not yet in office

Examples

The board has named her the director-designate until she officially takes office next month.
After the election, he was referred to as the president-designate by the media.
The prime minister-designate met with cabinet members to begin preparations for her new role.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - currently in office or holding a position

Etymology

From Latin 'designatus', past participle of 'designare' (to mark out, appoint).
