ileostomy

1. NOUN

/ ɪliˈɑstəmi / (AmE), / ɪliˈɒstəmi / (BrE)
A surgical operation in which an opening is made from the ileum through the abdominal wall, usually to allow waste to leave the body after removal of the colon.
surgical opening from the ileum to the abdomen
medical

Examples

After his surgery, he had to learn how to care for his ileostomy properly.
The doctor explained the reasons for performing an ileostomy to the patient.
Living with an ileostomy requires changes in daily routines and diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colostomy - a surgical opening from the colon through the abdominal wall

Etymology

From ileum (part of the small intestine) + Greek stoma (mouth, opening).
