Hymenoptera

1. NOUN

/ haɪməˈnɑptərə / (AmE), / haɪməˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
a large order of insects that includes bees, wasps, ants, and related groups, characterized by two pairs of membranous wings and an ovipositor or stinger
order of insects including bees, wasps, and ants
medical

Examples

Bees, ants, and wasps all belong to the order Hymenoptera.
The Hymenoptera are known for their complex social behaviors and division of labor.
Entomologists study Hymenoptera to learn more about pollination and insect societies.

Etymology

From Greek 'hymen' (membrane) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to their membranous wings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪməˈnɑptərə / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
A large order of insects that includes bees, wasps, ants, ichneumon wasps, sawflies, and related forms, characterized by two pairs of membranous wings and, in many species, a stinger.
order of insects including bees, wasps, ants

Examples

The Hymenoptera are known for their complex social structures, particularly in ants and bees.
Scientists recently discovered several new species within the Hymenoptera order in South America.
Many parasitic wasps belong to the Hymenoptera, playing a crucial role in controlling pest populations.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek hymen (membrane) + pteron (wing).
