inanimateness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnænɪmətnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmətnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking life or consciousness; not being alive
state of not having life

Examples

The inanimateness of the objects made the room feel cold and empty.
In linguistics, inanimateness can affect how nouns are classified and used in sentences.
He was struck by the profound inanimateness of the abandoned town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inanimacy - the state of being inanimate; absence of life or consciousness
  • Synonym: inanimate - not alive; lacking life or consciousness
  • Antonym: animateness - the quality of being alive or having lifelike qualities

Etymology

From inanimate + -ness.
