unemotional

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈmoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈməʊʃənl̩ / (BrE)
not showing or expressing strong emotions; cool and formal in manner.
not showing emotion; cool in manner

Examples

Throughout the tense meeting, he remained remarkably unemotional, never raising his voice or changing expression.
She gave her statement in a calm, almost unemotional tone that surprised the reporters.
His unemotional response made it difficult to know how he truly felt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong emotions or feelings
  • Synonym: impassive - not feeling or showing emotion

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + emotional.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈmoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈməʊʃənl̩ / (BrE)
not displaying or influenced by strong emotions; not easily affected by feelings.
not showing emotion

Examples

Her response to the news was surprisingly unemotional given the circumstances.
He spoke in a calm and unemotional manner throughout the meeting.
Despite the tragedy, she remained unemotional and focused on finding a solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotionless - showing no emotion; without feeling
  • Antonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or beliefs

Etymology

un- (not) + emotional, from emotion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00860047-a
Synset POS: a
