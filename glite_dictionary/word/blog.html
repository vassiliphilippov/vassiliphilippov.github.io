blog

1. NOUN

/ blɑg / (AmE), / blɒg / (BrE)
a website or web page, typically maintained by an individual or group, where entries are regularly updated and displayed in chronological order, often in the style of a personal journal
an online journal or website with regular posts

Examples

She started a blog to share her travel adventures with friends and family.
Many people read his technology blog for expert advice and reviews.
Their cooking blog features new recipes and tips every week.

Synset Examples

postings on a blog are usually in chronological order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weblog - an original term for a blog; an online journal

Etymology

From 'web log', shortened to 'blog' in the late 1990s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06413845-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ blɑg / (AmE), / blɒg / (BrE)
to write, read, or edit entries in a shared online journal or website, typically reflecting personal opinions, experiences, or information
write or edit an online journal

Examples

She plans to blog about her trip to Japan every day.
Many people blog as a way to express their opinions and connect with others.
He started blogging in college and quickly gained a large following.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weblog - a website on which someone writes regularly about topics that interest them
  • Degree Lower: microblog - to post short updates or content to a microblogging platform (e.g. Twitter)

Etymology

Formed from 'web log', shortened to 'blog' in the late 1990s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00866944-v
Synset POS: v
