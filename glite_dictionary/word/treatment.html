treatment

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrit.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtriːt.mənt / (BrE)
a thorough and detailed discussion or examination of a subject in speech or writing
detailed discussion of a subject

Examples

The professor's treatment of ancient history in his lectures was both comprehensive and engaging.
Her latest article offers a fresh treatment of climate change policy.
The book's detailed treatment of economic theory made it an essential read for students.

Synset Examples

the book contains an excellent discussion of modal logic
his treatment of the race question is badly biased

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - an act or process of talking about something in order to reach a decision or to exchange ideas
  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of the elements or structure of something
  • Degree Lower: overview - a general review or summary of a subject

Etymology

From treat + -ment, from Old French traitement, from traiter (to deal with).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrit.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtriːt.mənt / (BrE)
the process or manner of dealing with or managing a person, group, or thing
manner of managing or dealing with

Examples

The treatment of all employees should be fair and consistent in the workplace.
Proper treatment of confidential information is critical for maintaining trust.
The new recycling policy changed the treatment of household waste in the city.

Synset Examples

the handling of prisoners
the treatment of water sewage
the right to equal treatment in the criminal justice system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handling - the act of managing, controlling, or dealing with something
  • Antonym: mismanagement - the process of handling something badly

Etymology

From treat + -ment, from Old French traiter (to deal with) and Latin tractare.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtriːtmənt / (AmE), / ˈtriːtmənt / (BrE)
medical care or procedures given to cure or relieve an illness or injury; action taken to improve a situation.
medical care or intervention

Examples

After months of physical treatment, her arm finally healed properly.
The hospital offers advanced treatment for cancer patients.
He sought treatment abroad when his local doctors could not help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapy - the treatment of physical or mental problems, especially by talking or practical methods rather than drugs or operations
  • Synonym: cure - a treatment or remedy that gets rid of an illness or problem
  • Antonym: diagnosis - the identification of a disease or other condition

Etymology

From treat + -ment, based on Latin tractare (to handle, manage).
