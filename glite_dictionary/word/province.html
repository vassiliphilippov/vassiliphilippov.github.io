province

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.vɪns / (AmE), / ˈprɒv.ɪns / (BrE)
a principal administrative division of certain countries or empires, forming a territorial unit of government
administrative region in a country

Examples

The country is divided into several provinces, each governed locally.
She was born in a small province far from the capital city.
Many Canadians identify with their home province as much as with the country itself.

Synset Examples

his state is in the deep south

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state - an organized political community forming part of a federal republic
  • Synonym: region - an area, especially part of a country or the world with definable characteristics

Etymology

From Old French 'province', from Latin 'provincia' (territory under Roman rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.vɪns / (AmE), / ˈprɒv.ɪns / (BrE)
an area of responsibility, activity, or knowledge that is considered to belong to a particular person, organization, or group
a person's area of responsibility or expertise

Examples

Organizing the details of the event was her province and she handled everything beautifully.
It is not within my province to approve such expenditures.
Debate about ethics is the province of philosophers and theologians.

Synset Examples

it was his province to take care of himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of knowledge or activity
  • Synonym: sphere - an area of activity or interest
  • Synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something

Etymology

From Latin provincia (territory under Roman rule).

Synset POS: n
