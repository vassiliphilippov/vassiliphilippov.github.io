cyanogenetic

1. ADJ

/ saɪˌænədʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / saɪˌænədʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of producing cyanide, especially when metabolized or decomposed.
producing cyanide
medical

Examples

Some plants contain cyanogenetic compounds that can be toxic if ingested raw.
The seeds of bitter almonds are known for their cyanogenetic properties.
Researchers analyzed several cyanogenetic glycosides present in cassava roots.

Synset Examples

amygdalin is a cyanogenetic glucoside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanogenic - producing cyanide, especially in plants

Etymology

From cyanogen (a compound forming cyanide) + -etic (relating to or characterized by).
