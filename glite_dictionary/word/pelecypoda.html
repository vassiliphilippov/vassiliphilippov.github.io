Pelecypoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˈsɪpədə / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˈsɪpədə / (BrE)
a large class of molluscs, including oysters, clams, scallops, and mussels, whose bodies are enclosed within two hinged shells; also known as bivalves
class of molluscs with two shells
medical

Examples

The class Pelecypoda includes familiar shellfish such as oysters and clams.
Scientists study the anatomy of Pelecypoda to understand mollusc evolution.
A course in marine biology covers the diversity of Pelecypoda found in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bivalvia - scientific synonym for Pelecypoda; class of two-shelled molluscs

Etymology

From Greek 'pelekys' (axe) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the axe-shaped foot of these molluscs.
