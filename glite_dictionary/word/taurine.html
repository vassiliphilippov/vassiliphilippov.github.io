taurine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrˌin / (AmE), / ˈtɔːr.iːn / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline compound found mainly in bile, important in the metabolism of mammals and frequently used as a dietary supplement
amino sulfonic acid found in bile
medical

Examples

Energy drinks often contain taurine as one of their main ingredients.
Scientists discovered that taurine plays a crucial role in heart and brain function.
The liver produces small amounts of taurine naturally in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound that combines to form proteins

Etymology

From Latin 'taurinus' (of or like a bull), from 'taurus' (bull); originally isolated from ox bile.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɔːrin / (AmE), / ˈtɔːriːn / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a bull; of or pertaining to bulls
relating to bulls

Examples

The taurine features of the sculpture gave it a powerful presence in the exhibit.
Many ancient cultures revered taurine deities as symbols of strength and fertility.
His costume included a taurine mask with large, curved horns.

Related Terms

Etymology
