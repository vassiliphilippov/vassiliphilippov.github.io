justly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒʌstli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstli / (BrE)
in a fair, moral, or rightful manner
in a fair or rightful way

Examples

The manager promoted her justly, based solely on merit and hard work.
He was justly rewarded for his dedication to the community.
They criticized the policy justly, pointing out its unintended negative consequences.

Synset Examples

that serves him right
do right by him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unjustly - in an unfair or wrongful manner
  • Synonym: fairly - in a fair or impartial manner

Etymology

From Latin 'justus' (just) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒʌstli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstli / (BrE)
In a fair, honest, or morally right way.
in a fair or honest way

Examples

The judge treated both parties justly throughout the trial.
She was justly proud of her achievements, having worked hard for them.
Critics justly praised the novel for its originality and depth.

Synset Examples

he was rightly considered the greatest singer of his time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairly - in a fair or just manner
  • Synonym: rightly - in a correct or just manner
  • Antonym: unjustly - in an unfair or morally wrong way

Etymology

From just + -ly, ultimately from Latin iustus (just, fair).
