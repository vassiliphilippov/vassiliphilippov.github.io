unusually

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈjuːʒuəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːʒʊəli / (BrE)
to a remarkable or exceptional degree; more than is common or expected
to a remarkable extent

Examples

The weather was unusually warm for early spring this year.
She completed the assignment unusually quickly, surprising her teacher.
The painting features unusually vibrant colors that catch the viewer's eye.

Synset Examples

she was unusually tall
Notably missing from the network's fall line-up are any half-hour scripted comedies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptionally - to an unusual or extraordinary degree
  • Antonym: normally - in a usual or regular manner
