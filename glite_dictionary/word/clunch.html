clunch

1. NOUN

/ klʌntʃ / (AmE), / klʌntʃ / (BrE)
a stiff clay or an impure, compact limestone, often used as a building material in some regions
stiff clay or building limestone

Examples

The old cottage was built from local clunch, giving it a distinctive pale color.
Historically, clunch was a common building material in parts of eastern England.
Excavation revealed thick layers of clunch beneath the surface soil.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'clench', related to 'clinch' and possibly of onomatopoeic origin, imitating the sound of something hard or compacted.
