Pseudotaxus chienii

1. NOUN

/ suː.doʊˈtæks.əs tʃiˈɛ.ni.aɪ / (AmE), / sjuː.dəʊˈtæks.əs tʃiːˈɛ.ni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of yew native to southeastern China, distinguished from other yews by its white berries.
Chinese yew species with white berries

Examples

The endangered Pseudotaxus chienii is valued for its unusual white berries.
Botanists are working to conserve Pseudotaxus chienii in its native habitat.
Unlike other yews, Pseudotaxus chienii produces distinctive white arils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - evergreen trees or shrubs of genus Taxus

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + 'Taxus' (yew), and named after Chinese botanist Woon Young Chien.
