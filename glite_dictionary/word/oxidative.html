oxidative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑksɪˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the process of combining with oxygen or taking place in the presence of oxygen, especially as a chemical reaction
involving combination with oxygen

Examples

Prolonged exercise can increase oxidative stress in muscle tissue.
Certain fats are prone to oxidative rancidity when exposed to air.
The oxidative process is essential for cellular respiration.

Synset Examples

oxidative glycolysis
oxidative rancidity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reductive - relating to or involving a chemical reduction or removal of oxygen

Etymology

From 'oxidation', ultimately from French 'oxygène' (oxygen) and Latin 'oxygenium'.
