musculus adductor hallucis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs ædˈdʌktɔr hæˈluːsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ædˈdʌktɔː həlˈuː.sɪs / (BrE)
A muscle of the foot that draws the great toe toward the other toes.
muscle that moves the great toe inward
medical

Examples

Damage to the musculus adductor hallucis can impair the ability to move the big toe inward.
During the anatomy lecture, we discussed the function of the musculus adductor hallucis.
Surgical procedures on the foot may sometimes involve the musculus adductor hallucis.

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'adductor' (one that draws toward), 'hallucis' (of the big toe).
