Cacajao

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːkəˈhaʊ / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈhaʊ / (BrE)
A genus of South American monkeys known as uakaris, characterized by their short tails and brightly colored faces.
genus of South American monkeys (uakaris)

Examples

The Amazon rainforest is home to the rare Cacajao, known for its crimson face.
Researchers observed a group of Cacajao moving swiftly through the treetops.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of the Cacajao from deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uakari - A type of South American monkey with a reddish face and short tail

Etymology

From indigenous Tupi language, used in scientific Latin classification.
