to deaden

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛdən / (AmE), / ˈdɛdən / (BrE)
to make or become less lively, intense, strong, or active; to dull or reduce the force, effect, or sensation of something
make less intense or lively; dull

Examples

The thick curtains helped to deaden the noise from the street.
Painkillers can deaden the sensation in your mouth during dental treatments.
Repeated disappointment can deaden a person's enthusiasm over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - to make less sharp, intense, or lively
  • Antonym: to intensify - to make more intense or stronger

Etymology

From Middle English deden, from dead + -en.
