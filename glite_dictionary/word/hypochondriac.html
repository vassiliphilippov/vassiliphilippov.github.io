hypochondriac

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈkɑndriˌæk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkɒndrɪak / (BrE)
Suffering from or affected by hypochondria; excessively worried about one's health without medical reason.
Affected by hypochondria

Examples

Her hypochondriac concerns led her to visit the doctor almost every week.
Even as a child, he displayed hypochondriac tendencies, constantly fearing illness.
The novel's main character is portrayed as a hypochondriac patient obsessed with minor symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotic - suffering from excessive anxiety or obsession
  • Antonym: healthy - free from illness or anxiety about one's health

Etymology

From Greek hypokhondriakos, from hypokhondria (upper abdomen).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈkɑn.dri.æk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈkɒn.dri.æk / (BrE)
a person who is excessively worried about having a serious illness, often without medical evidence
person excessively worried about illness

Examples

Ever since his last checkup, Tom has been a hypochondriac who visits the doctor every week.
Her friends dismissed her constant complaints as those of a hypochondriac.
Being a hypochondriac can make it difficult to enjoy life without constant anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypochondria - excessive worry about one's health

Etymology

From Greek hypokhondriakos, relating to the upper abdomen (hypochondrium), believed to be the seat of melancholy.
