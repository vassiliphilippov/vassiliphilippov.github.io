muffled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a sound) made quieter or less distinct by being obstructed or covered.
softened or unclear sound

Examples

We heard the muffled voices of people talking in the next room.
The sound of the alarm was muffled by the thick walls.
Heavy curtains made the street noises seem muffled inside the apartment.

Synset Examples

the dull boom of distant breaking waves
muffled drums
the muffled noises of the street
muted trumpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muted - (of a sound) made quieter or less distinct; similar in meaning
  • Antonym: distinct - (of a sound) clear and easily heard; opposite of muffled

Etymology

From Middle English 'muflen', related to 'muffle' meaning to cover or wrap up.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) wrapped up in coverings, especially for warmth, protection, or secrecy, so as to be difficult to see or recognize.
wrapped up for warmth, protection, or secrecy

Examples

The children were muffled in layers of scarves and coats before going outside.
She sat muffled in her blanket, barely visible in the dim light.
The spy stayed muffled in a heavy coat and hat to avoid recognition.

Synset Examples

children muffled almost to the eyebrows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swaddled - wrapped tightly with cloth, especially a baby
  • Antonym: exposed - not covered or protected

Etymology

From the verb 'muffle', from Middle English 'muflen', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
