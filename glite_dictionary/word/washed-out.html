washed-out

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑʃt aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃt aʊt / (BrE)
(of a color or object) having lost brightness or vividness; faded or pale in appearance.
faded or pale in color

Examples

After many summers in the sun, the curtains looked completely washed-out.
She wore a washed-out blue shirt that had lost its original color.
The photograph had a washed-out quality, making the scene look pale and faded.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faded - having lost color or brightness
  • Antonym: bright - vivid or intense in color
  • Degree Lower: pale - light in color, not vivid

2. ADJ

/ wɑʃtˈaʊt / (AmE), / wɒʃtˈaʊt / (BrE)
Extremely tired or exhausted; lacking energy or effectiveness.
extremely tired; exhausted

Examples

After working three double shifts, he looked completely washed-out and could barely stand.
By the end of the marathon, every runner appeared utterly washed-out.
She felt washed-out after spending the whole night caring for her sick child.

Synset Examples

the day's shopping left her exhausted
he went to bed dog-tired
was fagged and sweaty
the trembling of his played out limbs
felt completely washed-out
only worn-out horses and cattle
you look worn out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - Extremely tired; having no energy left
  • Synonym: worn-out - Extremely tired or damaged from overuse
  • Antonym: rested - Refreshed by rest; not tired
