Sade

1. PROPN

/ sɑːd / (AmE), / sɑːd / (BrE)
the Marquis de Sade (1740–1814), a French soldier and writer known for works that depicted sexual cruelty, giving rise to the term 'sadism'
French writer; source of the term 'sadism'

Examples

Many scholars debate whether Sade's writings were critiques of power or celebrations of excess.
The term 'sadism' is derived from the name of Sade, reflecting his notorious reputation.
Sade remains a controversial figure in both literary and psychological studies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sadism - the tendency to derive pleasure from inflicting pain, suffering, or humiliation

Etymology

From Donatien Alphonse François de Sade, French nobleman and writer.
