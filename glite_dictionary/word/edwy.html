Edwy

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdwi / (AmE), / ˈɛdwi / (BrE)
(c. 941–959) King of England from 955 to 959, who was renounced by Northumbria in favor of his brother Edgar.
King of England (955–959)

Examples

Edwy became king of England in 955 at a very young age.
Many nobles in Northumbria rebelled against Edwy and supported his brother Edgar instead.
Historians debate whether Edwy's reign was troubled by his mistakes or by his opponents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Edgar - King of England and Edwy's brother who succeeded him

Etymology

Old English personal name, possibly from ead 'wealth, fortune' + wig 'war'.
