pycnodysostosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪk.noʊˌdaɪ.səˈstoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɪk.nəʊˌdɪs.ɒsˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A rare genetic disorder characterized by short stature, brittle bones, and dental abnormalities.
rare disorder with brittle bones and short stature
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with pycnodysostosis after observing the combination of short stature and brittle bones.
Recent studies have identified genetic mutations responsible for pycnodysostosis.
People with pycnodysostosis may require regular dental and orthopedic care.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' (dense), 'dys-' (bad), and 'ostosis' (condition of the bone).
