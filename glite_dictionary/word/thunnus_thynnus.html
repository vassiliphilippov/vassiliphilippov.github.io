Thunnus thynnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌn.əs ˈθɪn.əs / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.əs ˈθɪn.əs / (BrE)
the Atlantic bluefin tuna, a very large species of tuna found mainly in temperate seas, known for its migratory habits and valued as a food fish
Atlantic bluefin tuna species

Examples

The Thunnus thynnus is highly prized for sushi and sashimi in Japanese cuisine.
Overfishing has caused a dramatic decline in wild Thunnus thynnus populations.
Scientists have tagged several Thunnus thynnus to track their migration patterns across the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large and fast marine fish of the mackerel family

Etymology

From Latin Thunnus (tuna), from Greek thynnos (tuna); thynnus is the Latin specific name.
