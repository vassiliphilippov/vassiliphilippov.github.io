dissenting

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛntɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛntɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing disagreement, especially with the views or decisions of a majority or authority
expressing disagreement with a majority

Examples

She wrote a dissenting report outlining the flaws she saw in the committee's decision.
The Supreme Court justice delivered a strongly dissenting opinion on the controversial case.
Even within the team, there were dissenting voices that questioned the chosen strategy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreeing - expressing agreement, especially with the majority
  • Synonym: dissent - to disagree, especially with a formal opinion or majority

Etymology

From Latin dissentīre (to disagree), via the verb 'dissent'.
