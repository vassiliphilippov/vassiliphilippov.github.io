casserole

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.əˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæs.ər.əʊl / (BrE)
A dish consisting of meat, vegetables, or other ingredients baked together and served in a deep dish.
baked dish of mixed ingredients

Examples

She prepared a delicious casserole of chicken and rice for dinner.
The restaurant is known for its hearty beef casserole during the winter months.
Leftover vegetables can easily be turned into a tasty casserole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stew - dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid

Etymology

From French 'casserole' (stewpan), from Provençal 'cassa' (pan).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.əˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæs.ərəʊl / (BrE)
A large, deep dish used for cooking and serving food, often in the oven.
large deep cooking and serving dish

Examples

Please place the green casserole on the table next to the salad.
She cooked the lasagna in a glass casserole for the family dinner.
After baking, let the casserole cool before serving the meal.

Etymology

From French *casserole* (saucepan), from Provençal *cassa* (pan).
