talcum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæl.kəm / (AmE), / ˈtæl.kəm / (BrE)
A soft, fine-grained mineral consisting of hydrated magnesium silicate, used in products like talcum powder.
a soft mineral; used in talcum powder

Examples

The laboratory analyzed the sample and confirmed it was talcum.
Many cosmetics include finely ground talcum for its smooth texture.
You should avoid inhaling talcum dust, as it can be harmful to your lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talc - the mineral form used as an ingredient in talcum powder

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'talcum', from Arabic 'ṭalq' (talc).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæl.kəm / (AmE), / ˈtæl.kəm / (BrE)
a fine white powder made from purified talc, used to absorb moisture and often scented for use as a toiletry
toiletry powder made from talc

Examples

She sprinkled some talcum on the baby's skin after the bath.
The bathroom cabinet always had a tin of talcum powder.
Using talcum regularly can help keep your skin dry and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talc - a soft mineral, hydrated magnesium silicate, used to make talcum powder

Etymology

From Latin *talcum* (talc), from Arabic *ṭalq* (talc).
