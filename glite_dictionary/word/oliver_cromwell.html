Oliver Cromwell

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːlɪvər ˈkrɒmwɛl / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪvə ˈkrɒmwəl / (BrE)
(1599–1658) An English general and statesman who led the parliamentary forces during the English Civil War and later ruled as Lord Protector of England.
English general and statesman (1599–1658)

Examples

Oliver Cromwell played a leading role in the overthrow of King Charles I during the English Civil War.
Many historians debate whether Oliver Cromwell's rule was that of a dictator or a hero.
The legacy of Oliver Cromwell remains a subject of intense discussion in British history.
