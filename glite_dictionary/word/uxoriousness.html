uxoriousness

1. NOUN

/ ʌgˈzɔriəsnəs / (AmE), / ʌkˈsɔːriəsnəs / (BrE)
excessive or foolish fondness for or submissiveness to one's wife
excessive devotion to one's wife

Examples

His uxoriousness was apparent to all, as he catered to his wife's every whim without question.
Friends often teased him about his uxoriousness, saying he was incapable of saying no to her.
The novel depicts the protagonist's uxoriousness as both a virtue and a flaw in his character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devotion - strong love or loyalty for someone or something
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern; not caring

Etymology

From Latin 'uxoriosus' (excessively fond of one's wife), from 'uxor' (wife).
