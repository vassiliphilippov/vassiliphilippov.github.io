former

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
having previously held a particular role or position; of the immediate past, especially used of a person who has recently left an office or position.
previous holder of a role or position

Examples

The former manager still visits the office occasionally to offer advice.
She was introduced as the former champion of the tournament.
Many people remember the policies of the former prime minister with nostalgia.

Synset Examples

the former president
our late President is still very active
the previous occupant of the White House

Related Terms

  • Synonym: previous - happening or existing before the present one
  • Antonym: current - belonging to the present time; happening or being used now

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
referring to the first of two people or things that have been mentioned
the first of two mentioned

Examples

Some students chose coffee and others tea; the former preferred their drink hot.
Between hiking and swimming, I enjoy the former more during summer vacations.
She offered me lemon or lime, and I took the former in my water.

Synset Examples

the novel was made into a film in 1943 and again in 1967; I prefer the former version to the latter one

Related Terms

  • Antonym: latter - referring to the second of two people or things mentioned

Etymology

From Latin 'formare' (to form), but this use is from the comparative of 'fore' (before, earlier).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrmɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
the first of two people or things previously mentioned
the first of two previously mentioned

Examples

Of the two proposals, I prefer the former because it is more practical.
He compared the lives of Jane and Mary; the former became an artist, while the latter became a scientist.
Between tea and coffee, the former is my usual choice in the morning.

Synset Examples

Tom and Dick were both heroes but only the former is remembered today

Related Terms

  • Antonym: latter - the second of two people or things previously mentioned

Etymology

From Latin 'formare' (to form), but this sense arises from the comparative of 'fore' (before).

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
belonging to an earlier time; previous or earlier in position, status, or existence.
previous; belonging to an earlier time

Examples

She visited her former school to meet some of her old teachers.
After his injury, he could never return to his former level of performance.
That building was the former headquarters of the company before they moved downtown.

Synset Examples

erstwhile friend
our former glory
the once capital of the state
her quondam lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: previous - existing or occurring before in time or order
  • Antonym: current - belonging to the present time; happening now

Etymology

From Old English 'forma' (first) + comparative suffix '-er'.

of or belonging to an earlier or distant past time; having existed in the past.
of the distant past

Examples

Historians often write about the traditions of former generations.
The castle stands as a relic of former times.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
