nimble

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪmbəl / (BrE)
able to move quickly and easily; quick and light in movement or action.
quick and light in movement

Examples

The dancer's nimble feet seemed to barely touch the floor as she moved.
He solved the puzzle with a nimble mind and steady hands.
Despite his age, the old gardener remained nimble and quick on his feet.

Synset Examples

sleek and agile as a gymnast
as nimble as a deer
nimble fingers
quick of foot
the old dog was so spry it was halfway up the stairs before we could stop it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agile - able to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or action

Etymology

From Middle English, probably from Old English *nǣmel* (quick to take or grasp), related to German *nehmen* (to take).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɪm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪm.bəl / (BrE)
quick and light in movement or action; showing quickness of mind or resourcefulness
quick and resourceful; mentally rapid

Examples

Her nimble mind allowed her to solve the puzzle in seconds.
The nimble student responded to questions faster than anyone else in the class.
With a nimble wit, he always had the perfect comeback ready.

Synset Examples

an agile mind
nimble wits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agile - able to think and understand quickly
  • Antonym: slow-witted - slow to understand or think

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *nemel* (quick to seize), from Old English *nǣmel* (quick to grasp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01337785-s
Synset POS: s
