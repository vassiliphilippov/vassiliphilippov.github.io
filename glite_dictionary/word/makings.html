makings

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkɪŋz / (BrE)
the necessary ingredients or components for making or doing something
ingredients or components needed for something

Examples

She bought all the makings for the salad at the market.
The shed has the makings of a great workshop if you add a few more tools.
They gathered the makings for a campfire before it got dark.

Synset Examples

the recipe listed all the makings for a chocolate cake

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkɪŋz / (BrE)
the qualities or attributes that are likely to enable someone to become or achieve something; potential
qualities indicating potential

Examples

The coach said that Sarah has the makings of an Olympic champion.
He showed all the makings of a successful entrepreneur even as a teenager.
With her leadership skills, she has the makings of a great manager.

Synset Examples

her qualifications for the job are excellent
one of the qualifications for admission is an academic degree
she has the makings of fine musician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential - qualities that may develop into something in the future
  • Synonym: qualification - a quality or accomplishment that makes someone suitable for a particular job or activity

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'make' plus noun-forming suffix '-ing', originally referring to that which is made; later extended to mean innate qualities or potential.
