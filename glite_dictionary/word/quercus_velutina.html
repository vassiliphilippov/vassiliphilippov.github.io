Quercus velutina

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs vəˈluːtɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs vɛˈluːtɪnə / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree native to eastern North America, with dark outer bark and yellow inner bark, known as black oak.
species of black oak tree

Examples

The Quercus velutina is valued for its strong timber and tannin-rich bark.
Many hikers admire the distinctive dark bark of Quercus velutina in the Appalachian forests.
Foresters often identify Quercus velutina by its yellow inner bark and bristle-tipped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black oak - common name for Quercus velutina, a species of oak tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'velutina' meaning 'velvety', referring to velvety texture of young leaves or twigs.
