Pipilo

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈpaɪloʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈpiːləʊ / (BrE)
a genus of North and Central American birds in the family Passerellidae, known as towhees, which includes several species such as the Eastern towhee and the spotted towhee.
genus of towhee birds

Examples

The Pipilo genus includes birds commonly found in scrubby woodlands across North America.
Scientists recently observed unusual mating calls in several members of the Pipilo genus.
Field guides typically highlight the distinctive plumage of the Pipilo species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towhee - common name for several species in the genus Pipilo and related genera

Etymology

From Latin 'pipilo', meaning 'I chirp' or 'I peep', used for birds with a chirping song.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpɪˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɪpɪləʊ / (BrE)
a genus of North American songbirds in the family Passerellidae, commonly known as towhees
genus of North American songbirds (towhees)

Examples

The Pipilo genus includes the well-known Eastern towhee and the spotted towhee.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of birds in the Pipilo genus.
The green-tailed towhee, a member of Pipilo, inhabits shrubby areas across western North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towhee - any of several North American sparrows in the genera Pipilo and Melozone

Etymology

New Latin, from Latin pipilare (to chirp or pipe).
