Dendroctonus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.drəʊkˈtoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrɒkˈtəʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small bark beetles that infest and destroy mature conifer trees, often causing significant damage to forests.
genus of bark beetles damaging conifers

Examples

Dendroctonus species are among the most destructive pests in North American pine forests.
The outbreak of Dendroctonus beetles led to widespread damage in the lodgepole pine stands.
Forestry experts are researching ways to control Dendroctonus populations to protect valuable timber resources.

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + ktonos (killer), referring to the beetle's destructive activity in trees.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˌdrɑkˈtoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌdɛnˌdrɒkˈtəʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small bark beetles known for causing extensive damage to mature conifer trees.
genus of bark beetles harmful to conifers

Examples

Outbreaks of Dendroctonus beetles have devastated large areas of pine forest.
Researchers are developing methods to control Dendroctonus populations in commercial timberlands.
A single Dendroctonus infestation can kill hundreds of mature conifers in a season.

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + ktonos (killer, slayer), referring to their destructive effect on trees.
