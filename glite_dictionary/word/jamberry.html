jamberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒæmbəri / (BrE)
A Mexican plant (Physalis ixocarpa) naturalized in North America, bearing yellow to purple edible fruit resembling small tomatoes.
plant with edible fruit like small tomatoes

Examples

Many gardeners grow jamberries for their unique, tangy fruit.
The salsa recipe called for diced jamberry to add a burst of flavor.
After harvesting the jamberries, we made a delicious homemade jam.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒamˌbɛri / (BrE)
an annual plant native to Mexico and the southern United States, bearing edible, sticky purplish fruit resembling small tomatoes
edible-fruited annual plant

Examples

The jamberry thrives in warm climates and produces fruit in late summer.
Children enjoyed picking fresh jamberries during the harvest season.
The sticky, purple fruit of the jamberry is often used in preserves and desserts.
