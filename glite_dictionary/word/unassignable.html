unassignable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəˈsaɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnəˈsaɪnəbəl / (BrE)
(of a right, property, or interest) not able to be transferred to another person or party.
unable to be transferred

Examples

The contract contained an unassignable clause preventing the transfer of rights to a third party.
Certain benefits are strictly unassignable under the terms of the agreement.
The court ruled that the patent was unassignable and must remain with the original owner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assignable - able to be transferred to another party

Etymology

from un- (not) + assignable, from Latin assignare (to allocate, allot)
