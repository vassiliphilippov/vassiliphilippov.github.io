braggadocio

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræɡ.əˈdoʊ.tʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˌbræɡ.əˈdəʊ.tʃi.əʊ / (BrE)
boastful or arrogant behavior; vain and empty boasting
vain boasting; arrogance

Examples

His braggadocio at the meeting annoyed his colleagues.
The athlete's braggadocio did not match his actual performance on the field.
Despite all his braggadocio, he failed to deliver anything substantial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boasting - talking with excessive pride about oneself
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of not bragging or showing off

Etymology

From Italian 'braggadoccio', a boastful character in Spenser's The Faerie Queene, from 'brag'.
