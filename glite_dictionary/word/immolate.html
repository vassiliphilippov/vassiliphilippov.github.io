to immolate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪməʊleɪt / (BrE)
to kill or destroy, especially by fire, as a sacrifice or offering to a deity or for some purpose
to sacrifice by killing or destruction

Examples

The ancient ritual required priests to immolate a bull on the altar at dawn.
Rebels threatened to immolate priceless artwork to prevent it from being captured by the enemy.
She chose to immolate her ambitions for the sake of her family's happiness.

Synset Examples

The Aztecs immolated human victims
immolate the valuables at the temple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice - to offer or kill as a religious offering
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep safe from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Latin immolatus, past participle of immolare (to sacrifice), from in- (upon) + mola (sacrificial meal).
