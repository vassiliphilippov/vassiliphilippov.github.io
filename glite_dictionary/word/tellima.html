Tellima

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɪmə / (AmE), / tɛˈlɪmə / (BrE)
a genus of hardy perennial plants with palmately lobed leaves and long racemes of small nodding five-petaled flowers, native to western North America
genus of perennial flowering plants

Examples

The Tellima plants thrive in shady woodland gardens in the Pacific Northwest.
Gardeners often choose Tellima for its delicate, nodding flowers and attractive foliage.
Tellima is sometimes called fringecup due to the fringed edges of its blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'tellos' (end) and 'heima' (cloak), referring to the flower's structure.
