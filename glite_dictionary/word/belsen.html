Belsen

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛl sən / (AmE), / ˈbɛl sən / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration camp for Jews located in northwestern Germany during World War II, known for the suffering and deaths of thousands of prisoners.
Nazi concentration camp in WWII Germany

Examples

Many survivors of Belsen have shared their harrowing experiences with the world.
The liberation of Belsen by British troops revealed the horrors of the Holocaust.
Historians continue to study the conditions at Belsen to understand the extent of Nazi atrocities.
