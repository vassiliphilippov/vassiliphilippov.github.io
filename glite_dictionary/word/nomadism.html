nomadism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊˌmædɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌmædɪzəm / (BrE)
a way of life in which people have no permanent home and move from place to place, often seasonally, in search of food, water, or grazing land
way of life involving seasonal migration

Examples

In some regions, nomadism remains a common way of life for pastoral communities.
Anthropologists have studied the social structures that develop in societies practicing nomadism.
The disappearance of traditional nomadism has impacted the culture and livelihoods of many indigenous groups.

Synset Examples

Pastoral nomadism is a livelihood form that is ecologically adjusted at a particular level to the utilization of marginal resources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sedentarism - a lifestyle characterized by living in one place permanently

Etymology

From French 'nomadisme', from Greek 'nomas' (roaming, wandering).
