multinomial

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.təˈnoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈnəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
having the nature of a polynomial with more than one term; consisting of multiple terms in algebraic expressions.
having several terms; in algebra, like a polynomial

Examples

The equation represented a multinomial expression with five separate terms.
In advanced algebra, multinomial equations become increasingly complex to solve.
Each multinomial sequence can be broken down into simpler polynomial factors.

Synset Examples

a polynomial expression

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: binomial - an algebraic expression with two terms
  • Synonym: polynomial - an algebraic expression of multiple terms

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + nomen (name), patterned after 'binomial'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌl.təˈnoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈnəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
a mathematical expression consisting of the sum of several terms, each a product of constants and variables raised to non-negative integer powers
mathematical expression with multiple terms

Examples

The equation contains a multinomial with four terms, each representing a different process.
Factoring a multinomial can be challenging, especially when there are more than three terms.
A multinomial can generalize a binomial by allowing more than two terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polynomial - a mathematical expression of two or more algebraic terms

Etymology

From Latin 'multinomialis' (having many names), from 'multi-' (many) and 'nomen' (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05870439-n
Synset POS: n
