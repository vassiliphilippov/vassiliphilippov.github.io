to traumatise

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɔːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to experience severe emotional or psychological distress as a result of a shocking or upsetting event
inflict psychological or emotional trauma

Examples

The accident traumatised her for many years afterward.
Children can be traumatised by witnessing violence in their homes.
Losing his job unexpectedly traumatised him and affected his confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to traumatize - American spelling: to inflict a trauma upon
  • Antonym: to heal - to make or become healthy after trauma or injury

Etymology

From Greek 'trauma' (wound) via medical Latin 'traumat-', with the verb-forming suffix '-ise'.
