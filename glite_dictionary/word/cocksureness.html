cocksureness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌʃʊr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌʃʊə.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being completely certain, often with excessive or unwarranted confidence
complete or excessive confidence

Examples

Her cocksureness during the meeting impressed some and annoyed others.
There was a cocksureness in his answer that suggested he hadn't really considered other options.
You need confidence, but cocksureness can easily become a liability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - firm belief or trust in oneself or abilities
  • Degree Higher: arrogance - an attitude of superiority and self-importance
  • Antonym: doubt - lack of certainty or confidence

Etymology

From the adjective 'cocksure', itself from 'cock' (rooster) + 'sure', originally meaning as sure as a cock is certain to crow.
