kinfolk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪnˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈkɪn.fəʊk / (BrE)
relatives; people descended from a common ancestor, especially extended family members.
relatives; extended family members

Examples

She invited all her kinfolk to the big reunion at the farm.
Many of his kinfolk settled in the same small town.
He enjoys learning stories about his ancestors and kinfolk.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage
  • Synonym: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'cynn' (family, kin) + 'folk' (people).
