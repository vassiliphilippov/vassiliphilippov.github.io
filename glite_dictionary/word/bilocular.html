bilocular

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈlɑkjələr / (AmE), / baɪˈlɒkjʊlə / (BrE)
having two compartments or chambers, especially in botanical or biological structures.
having two compartments or chambers

Examples

The fruit of this plant develops into a bilocular capsule, each cell containing several seeds.
Microscopic examination revealed a bilocular structure in the specimen's tissue.
In this species, the ovary is distinctly bilocular, separated by a thin septum.

Synset Examples

having a bilocular capsule

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilocular - having a single compartment or chamber
  • Antonym: multilocular - having many compartments or chambers

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'loculus' (compartment, diminutive of 'locus' meaning place).
