damnation

1. NOUN

/ dæmˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dæmˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of condemning someone to eternal punishment or a state of being condemned by God; the state of being damned
condemnation to eternal punishment

Examples

The preacher warned of damnation for those who strayed from the path of righteousness.
He feared eternal damnation for his sins.
The story explores themes of damnation and redemption.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salvation - the act of being saved from sin or its consequences

Etymology

From Latin 'damnatio' (condemnation), from 'damnare' (to condemn).

2. NOUN

/ dæmˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dæmˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being condemned to eternal punishment in Hell, especially according to religious beliefs
condemnation to eternal punishment (Hell)

Examples

Some religions warn of damnation for those who commit mortal sins.
The preacher spoke passionately about salvation and damnation in his sermon.
Literary works often depict characters who fear eternal damnation for their actions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salvation - deliverance from sin and its consequences
  • Synonym: condemnation - the act of condemning or the state of being condemned

Etymology

From Latin damnatio (condemnation), from damnare (to condemn).
