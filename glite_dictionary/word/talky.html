talky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑki / (AmE), / ˈtɔːki / (BrE)
inclined to talk a lot, especially about trivial matters; chatty or gabby
inclined to talk at length about trivial things

Examples

My talky coworker always distracts me with small talk when I'm trying to focus on work.
The movie was a bit too talky for my taste, with endless dialogue and little action.
Their talky children kept up a constant stream of questions and comments during the drive.

Synset Examples

kept from her housework by gabby neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatty - tending to talk a lot in a friendly way
  • Synonym: gabby - excessively or annoyingly talkative
  • Antonym: taciturn - reserved or uncommunicative in speech

Etymology

Formed from 'talk' + '-y', following the pattern of adjectives denoting propensity.
