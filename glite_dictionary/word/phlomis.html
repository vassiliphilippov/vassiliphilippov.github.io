phlomis

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊmɪs / (AmE), / ˈfləʊmɪs / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the mint family, grown mainly for their dense whorls of lipped flowers and attractive foliage
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Several species of phlomis are prized for their drought resistance in Mediterranean gardens.
The garden features a border filled with phlomis and other aromatic perennials.
Bright yellow flowers distinguish the most common phlomis found in British gardens.

Etymology

From Greek *phlōmis*, a type of plant, possibly mullein.
