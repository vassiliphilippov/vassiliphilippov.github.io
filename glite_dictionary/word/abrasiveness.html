abrasiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈbreɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being harsh, rude, or causing offense in manner or tone
harsh or rude quality

Examples

His abrasiveness often alienated colleagues who preferred a more diplomatic approach.
She regretted the abrasiveness of her reply after seeing how upset he was.
The manager’s abrasiveness was evident during the tense meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntness - the quality of being direct and unceremonious
  • Antonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues

Etymology

From 'abrasive', from Latin 'ab-' (away) + 'radere' (to scrape).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪ.sɪv.nəs / (AmE), / əˈbreɪ.sɪv.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being rough or harsh enough to cause wear, scratching, or scraping to other materials.
quality of being rough or causing abrasion

Examples

The abrasiveness of the cleaning pad made it suitable for scrubbing tough stains.
Engineers tested the abrasiveness of the new sandpaper to ensure it met safety standards.
Repeated exposure to materials with high abrasiveness can damage delicate surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of being rough
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth

Etymology

From abrasive + -ness, indicating the state or quality.
