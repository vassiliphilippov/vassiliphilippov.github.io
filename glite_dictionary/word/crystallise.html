to crystallise

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪz / (BrE)
to assume or develop a crystalline form; to become crystallized
to become crystalline in structure
british

Examples

Sugar will crystallise on the sides of the pan if you heat the syrup too much.
Under the right conditions, minerals in the solution begin to crystallise.
If you let the mixture cool slowly, the salt will crystallise into large, clear shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crystallize - to assume crystalline form; become crystallized (US spelling)
  • Antonym: dissolve - to become or cause to become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution

Etymology

From Greek 'krystallos' (ice, crystal), via Medieval Latin and French, with '-ise/-ize' suffix.

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a substance) to form crystals or to assume a crystalline structure
form or cause to form crystals
british

Examples
