lighterage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the fee charged for transporting goods in lighters (flat-bottomed barges) between ship and shore or between ships
fee for shipping goods in lighters

Examples

The shipping company added lighterage to the final invoice for delivering the cargo to the shore.
Due to the use of lighters in the harbor, their lighterage costs increased last year.
Negotiating lower lighterage fees helped the exporters save money on transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - the charge for transporting goods
  • Synonym: demurrage - a charge for delaying cargo beyond allotted time

Etymology

From 'lighter' (barge for loading/unloading ships) + '-age' (denoting a fee or action)

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or cost of transporting goods by means of a lighter (a type of flat-bottomed barge)
transport of goods by lighter (barge)

Examples

The port charged a fee for lighterage when the cargo had to be transferred from the ship to shore.
Due to the shallow harbor, lighterage was necessary to move goods to the main dock.
In the 19th century, many ports relied heavily on lighterage to facilitate trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighter - a flat-bottomed barge used to transfer goods to or from larger ships

Etymology

Derived from 'lighter' (a barge for loading or unloading ships) + '-age' (denoting action or related cost).
