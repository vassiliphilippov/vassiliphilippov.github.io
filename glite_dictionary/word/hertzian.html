Hertzian

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɜrtsiən / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtsiən / (BrE)
Relating to the German physicist Heinrich Hertz or his scientific work, especially in the field of electromagnetism.
relating to Heinrich Hertz or his work

Examples

The discovery of Hertzian waves revolutionized wireless communication.
His research focused on Hertzian contact mechanics in physics.
Early radio signals were first described as Hertzian oscillations.

Synset Examples

Hertzian discoveries

Etymology

From the name of Heinrich Rudolf Hertz (1857–1894), German physicist, with the suffix -ian meaning 'relating to.'
