Acridotheres tristis

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrɪdoʊˈθɪriːz ˈtraɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˌækrɪdəˈθɪəriːz ˈtrɪstɪs / (BrE)
A species of dark brown crested bird native to southeastern Asia, also known as the common myna.
species of bird; common myna

Examples

The Acridotheres tristis thrives in both urban and rural environments across its native range.
Researchers observed the distinctive calls of the Acridotheres tristis in the city park.
The introduction of Acridotheres tristis to new habitats has led to significant ecological changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common myna - common name for Acridotheres tristis, a widespread bird of Asia

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Acridotheres (from Greek *akris* 'locust' + *thēr* 'hunter') and species Latin tristis 'sad'.
