colours

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
the flag or flags showing the nationality of a ship, regiment, or other group, especially in a military context
national flag(s) of a group or ship
british

Examples

The ship proudly displayed her colours as she entered the harbor.
The regiment marched behind their colours during the ceremony.
Sailors are expected to salute the colours as they come aboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colors - flag(s) showing nationality (American spelling)

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
flags, emblems, or distinctive garments serving as the symbol of an organization, group, or institution, especially as a mark of loyalty or membership.
emblem or flag representing a group
british

Examples

The soldiers defended their colours to the last man.
He proudly wore the team's colours to the match.
The old school colours still hang in the main hall.

Synset Examples

his tie proclaimed his school colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colors - American spelling; emblems or flags representing a group

Etymology

From Old French 'colur', from Latin 'color' (color, appearance, outward sign), used in the sense of heraldic or identifying emblems.
