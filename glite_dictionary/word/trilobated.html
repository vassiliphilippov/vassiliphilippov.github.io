trilobated

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈloʊbeɪtɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈləʊbeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf or similar structure) divided into three distinct lobes.
having three lobes (botanical)

Examples

The trilobated leaves help botanists identify this rare species in the wild.
The tree's trilobated foliage made it stand out among the more common oval-leaved plants.
A trilobated pattern is visible on each leaf blade of the specimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trilobed - having three lobes

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lobatus' (having lobes).
