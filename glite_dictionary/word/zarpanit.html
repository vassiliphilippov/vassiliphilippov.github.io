Zarpanit

1. PROPN

/ zɑːrˈpɑːnɪt / (AmE), / zɑːˈpænɪt / (BrE)
a goddess in ancient Babylonian mythology, recognized as the consort of the god Marduk
Babylonian goddess, consort of Marduk

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Zarpanit, the revered consort of Marduk.
Ancient texts often refer to Zarpanit in prayers and hymns.
Scholars have studied the myths surrounding Zarpanit and her role in Babylonian religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marduk - chief god in Babylonian mythology

Etymology

From Akkadian 'Ṣarpanitum', the name of the goddess in Babylonian mythology.
