post-mortem

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊst ˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst ˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
an examination of a dead body to determine the cause of death or the effects of disease; an autopsy
examination of a dead body; autopsy
medical

Examples

The coroner ordered a post-mortem to establish the cause of death.
A post-mortem revealed evidence of heart disease.
After the accident, a detailed post-mortem was carried out by the forensic team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - a post-mortem examination to determine the cause of death

Etymology

From Latin 'post mortem' meaning 'after death'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
A discussion or analysis of an event, especially after it has finished, to understand what happened or what could be improved.
discussion after an event to analyze it

Examples

After the project failed, the team held a post-mortem to identify what went wrong.
The coach scheduled a post-mortem to review the game's critical moments.
Every major incident is followed by a detailed post-mortem to improve future responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debriefing - a meeting to discuss an event after it happens, especially to analyze it
  • Synonym: autopsy - medical examination of a body after death (original sense of post-mortem)

Etymology

From Latin post mortem, meaning 'after death'; originally referring to autopsy, later used more broadly for analysis after an event.
