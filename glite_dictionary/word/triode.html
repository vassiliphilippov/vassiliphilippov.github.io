triode

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəʊd / (BrE)
a type of thermionic vacuum tube with three electrodes, used to amplify electrical signals
three-electrode vacuum tube

Examples

The triode was a revolutionary advancement in early electronic amplifiers.
Vacuum-tube radios often relied on a single triode to boost weak radio signals.
Many classic guitar amplifiers feature at least one triode in their preamp stages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: diode - a vacuum tube with two electrodes
  • Degree Higher: tetrode - a vacuum tube with four electrodes

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'hodos' (way, path), referring to its three electrodes.
