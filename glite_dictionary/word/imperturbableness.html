imperturbableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɜrtɚbəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːtɜːbəbəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of remaining calm and unshaken, even in difficult situations
state of being calm and unruffled

Examples

Despite the chaos all around him, his imperturbableness impressed everyone in the room.
She handled the sudden emergency with remarkable imperturbableness.
The general's imperturbableness during the crisis reassured his troops.

Synset Examples

he performed with all the coolness of a veteran

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperturbability - the quality of being incapable of being upset or agitated
  • Antonym: agitation - the state of being nervous or disturbed
  • Synonym: calmness - the state of being calm

Etymology

From Latin imperturbabilis (not to be disturbed), from in- (not) + perturbare (to disturb).
