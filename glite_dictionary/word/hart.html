hart

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt / (AmE), / hɑːt / (BrE)
A logarithmic unit of information or entropy, based on base 10 logarithms and powers of 10.
unit of information (logarithmic, base 10)

Examples

One hart represents the information content of an event with a probability of exactly one in ten.
The hart is similar to the bit, but uses a decimal rather than a binary base.
Engineers sometimes use harts when calculations involve decimal-based systems.

Synset Examples

The ban pre-dates Shannon's use of bit as a unit of information by at least eight years, and remains in use in the early 21st Century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a unit of information based on the binary logarithm (base 2)
  • Synonym: nat - a unit of information based on the natural logarithm (base e)

Etymology

Named after John Hart, who promoted the use of decimal-based logarithmic units.

2. NOUN

/ hɑrt / (AmE), / hɑːt / (BrE)
an adult male deer, especially a red deer
an adult male deer (esp. red deer)
archaic

Examples

The old tale spoke of a noble hart roaming the ancient forest.
Hunters once sought the majestic hart for its impressive antlers.
In the poem, the wounded hart symbolizes unattainable desire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stag - an adult male deer, especially a red deer
  • Antonym: hind - an adult female red deer

Etymology

From Old English heort (deer), from Proto-Germanic *herut- (deer).
