pussy-paws

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊsiˌpɔz / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌpɔːz / (BrE)
A small perennial plant of western North America, known for its pink, densely clustered flowers that resemble the pads of a cat's feet.
plant with pink flower clusters resembling cat's paws

Examples

The delicate pussy-paws bloomed along the forest trail, adding splashes of pink to the scenery.
In early spring, hikers enjoy spotting pussy-paws growing among the pine needles.
The soft texture of pussy-paws makes them easy to recognize among wildflowers.

Etymology

From 'pussy' (cat) and 'paw', referring to the plant's soft, pad-like flower clusters.
