Rhoeadales

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.iˈæd.eɪlz / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.iˈæd.eɪlz / (BrE)
An order of dicotyledonous flowering plants, which includes families such as Capparidaceae, Cruciferae, Moringaceae, Papaveraceae, Resedaceae, and Tovariaceae.
an order of dicotyledonous plants
medical

Examples

The family Papaveraceae is included in the Rhoeadales order.
Many plants in the Rhoeadales share medicinal properties.
Taxonomists once classified crucifers under the Rhoeadales.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rhoead-', referring to the poppy genus Rhoeades, plus the standard taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
