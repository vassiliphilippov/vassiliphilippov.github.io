Opsanus tau

1. NOUN

/ ɑpˈseɪnəs taʊ / (AmE), / ɒpˈseɪnəs taʊ / (BrE)
a species of bottom-dwelling fish with scaleless, slimy skin, a broad thick head, and a wide mouth, also known as the oyster toadfish
species of bottom-dwelling fish (oyster toadfish)

Examples

The Opsanus tau is commonly found in the brackish waters of the Atlantic coast.
Biologists studied the unique sound-making ability of the Opsanus tau during breeding season.
Fishermen often avoid handling Opsanus tau because of its slimy skin and powerful jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster toadfish - common name for Opsanus tau, a bottom-dwelling fish
