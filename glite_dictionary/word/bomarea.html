Bomarea

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈmɛəriə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmeəriə / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical American climbing plants known for their showy, often spotted, umbrella-shaped flowers.
genus of tropical American flowering vines

Examples

The Bomarea is prized by gardeners for its colorful, exotic blossoms.
Many species within the Bomarea genus are popular in tropical horticulture.
Researchers discovered a new variety of Bomarea in the Peruvian Andes.

Etymology

From New Latin, named for Jacques-Christophe Valmont de Bomare, French naturalist.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈmɑːriə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɑːriə / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical American vines with showy, often spotted, umbrella-like flowers, sometimes classified in the family Liliaceae or Alstroemeriaceae.
genus of tropical American flowering vines

Examples

The gardens featured several species of Bomarea climbing elegantly along the trellis.
Many botanists study Bomarea for its diverse and colorful flowers.
The Bomarea genus is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alstroemeria - a related genus of South American flowering plants, often called Peruvian lily

Etymology

Named after Jacques Bomare, French naturalist (1742–1829).
