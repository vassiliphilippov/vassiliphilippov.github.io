Hohenzollern

1. PROPN

/ hoʊənˈzɔːlərn / (AmE), / həʊənˈzɒlən / (BrE)
a German noble family that ruled Brandenburg and Prussia, and later the German Empire
German ruling noble family

Examples

The Hohenzollern dynasty played a major role in German and European history.
After the fall of the monarchy, the Hohenzollern family lost their ruling status.
The Prussian kings came from the Hohenzollern house.

Etymology

From the name of the family's ancestral seat, Hohenzollern Castle in Germany.
