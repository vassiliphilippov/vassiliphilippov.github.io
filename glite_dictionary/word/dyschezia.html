dyschezia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkiːziə / (AmE), / dɪsˈkiːziə / (BrE)
difficulty in defecating, usually resulting from prolonged voluntary suppression of the urge to defecate
difficulty in defecation
medical

Examples

The patient reported dyschezia accompanied by abdominal discomfort over several weeks.
Chronic dyschezia can result from ignoring the natural urge to have a bowel movement.
Her doctor diagnosed her with dyschezia after reviewing her symptoms and medical history.

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'chezia' (defecation from 'chezein', to defecate).
