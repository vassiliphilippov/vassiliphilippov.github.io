empowered

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpaʊərd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊəd / (BrE)
having been given legal power or official authority to do something.
given legal power or official authority

Examples

The elected committee members are empowered to make decisions on behalf of the community.
She felt empowered by the trust and responsibility given to her in her new role.
Only the board is empowered to approve major policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorized - having official permission or power to do something
  • Antonym: powerless - lacking power or authority

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'empower,' from Old French 'em-' (in) + 'power.'
