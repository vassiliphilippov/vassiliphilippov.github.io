mayapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌæpəl / (BrE)
A North American herb (Podophyllum peltatum) with umbrella-like leaves, a poisonous rootstock, and a yellowish edible fruit.
North American herb with edible fruit

Examples

The mayapple blooms early in the spring, covering the forest floor with its distinctive umbrella-shaped leaves.
Although the mayapple fruit is edible when ripe, other parts of the plant are poisonous.
We spotted several mayapple plants along the woodland trail during our hike.
