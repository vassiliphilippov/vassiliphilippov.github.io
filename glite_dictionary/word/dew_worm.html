dew worm

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈdjuː wɜːm / (BrE)
A large earthworm, especially one that comes to the surface at night or in damp conditions, commonly used as fishing bait.
large earthworm used as bait

Examples

The fisherman dug in the garden to find a dew worm for bait.
After the rain, dew worms could be seen crawling across the sidewalk.
Gardeners appreciate the work dew worms do in aerating the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a terrestrial worm that burrows and helps aerate soil
