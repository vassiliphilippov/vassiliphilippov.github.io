psalterium

1. NOUN

/ sɔlˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / sɒlˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
the third compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, located between the reticulum and the abomasum
third stomach compartment of ruminant
medical

Examples

The psalterium is responsible for further filtering ingested material before it enters the abomasum.
A blockage in the psalterium can severely affect the cow's digestion.
During the dissection, we identified the reticulum, psalterium, and abomasum as distinct chambers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omasum - the third compartment of a ruminant's stomach (alternative term)
  • Degree Higher: abomasum - the fourth compartment of a ruminant's stomach
  • Degree Lower: reticulum - the second compartment of a ruminant's stomach

Etymology

From Late Latin *psalterium* (psaltery), due to the resemblance of the internal folds to the strings of a psaltery (a type of ancient musical instrument).
