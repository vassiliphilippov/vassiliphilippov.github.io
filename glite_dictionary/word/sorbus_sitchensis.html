Sorbus sitchensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbəs sɪtˈʃɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbəs sɪtˈʃɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of mountain ash native to the western coast of North America, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white flowers and orange-red berries.
a mountain ash species native to western North America

Examples

The Sorbus sitchensis produces clusters of bright orange berries in late summer.
Botanists studied how Sorbus sitchensis adapts to the cool, moist climate of the Pacific Northwest.
Many birds feed on the fruit of Sorbus sitchensis during the autumn months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - any of several species of small trees with compound leaves and clusters of berries

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sorbus' is the genus name for mountain ashes, and 'sitchensis' refers to Sitka (Alaska) where it was first identified.
