flimsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzi / (BrE)
A thin, strong, lightweight, translucent paper used especially for making carbon copies.
thin lightweight paper for carbon copies

Examples

He wrote the letter on a flimsy so a carbon copy could be made.
The old files are filled with yellowed flimsies from decades ago.
She needed a flimsy to create an extra copy for her records.

2. ADJ

/ ˈflɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzi / (BrE)
lacking solidity, strength, or substance; easily broken or damaged
lacking strength or solidity

Examples

The box was made of flimsy cardboard that could easily tear.
She wore a flimsy dress on a chilly evening and regretted it.
The evidence against him was flimsy and unconvincing.

Synset Examples

a flimsy table
flimsy construction
vinyl siding has become the standard-bearer for cheap, insubstantial construction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built
  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

3. ADJ

/ ˈflɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzi / (BrE)
Lacking strength, substance, or convincing quality; insubstantial or unconvincing.
lacking strength or significance

Examples

The jury found the prosecution's evidence far too flimsy to convict the defendant.
His flimsy excuses did not convince the teacher, who demanded a better explanation.
The story was entertaining, but the plot was rather flimsy and lacked depth.

Synset Examples

slight evidence
a tenuous argument
a thin plot
a fragile claim to fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - Lacking strength or conviction
  • Antonym: solid - Strong or substantial in argument or structure

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Scandinavian (compare Norwegian 'flims').

4. ADJ

/ ˈflɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzi / (BrE)
(of an excuse, argument, etc.) weak and unconvincing; not persuasive or credible.
weak or unconvincing (argument, excuse)

Examples

The lawyer dismissed the accusation, calling it a flimsy attempt to undermine his client.
Her explanation for being late was so flimsy that no one believed her.
The committee rejected the proposal because it was based on flimsy evidence.

Synset Examples

a flimsy argument
as unconvincing as a forced smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconvincing - not capable of persuading someone that something is true or valid
  • Antonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scandinavian (compare Norwegian 'flimsa' meaning 'to flutter').
