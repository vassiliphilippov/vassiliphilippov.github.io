APC

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.piːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.piːˈsiː / (BrE)
a combination of aspirin, phenacetin, and caffeine, formerly used in some over-the-counter headache remedies
aspirin, phenacetin, and caffeine drug mixture
medical

Examples

The pharmacist explained that APC was once a common remedy for headaches.
Some people experienced side effects from APC due to the phenacetin component.
Doctors no longer recommend APC because phenacetin was found to be harmful.

Etymology

Acronym from the initial letters of aspirin, phenacetin, and caffeine.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.piːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.piːˈsiː / (BrE)
a military armored vehicle used to transport infantry, usually equipped with caterpillar tracks
armored military vehicle for troop transport

Examples

The soldiers quickly boarded the APC before moving towards the front line.
Enemy fire bounced harmlessly off the thick armor of the APC.
Several APCs rolled across the field, carrying troops to their next objective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored personnel carrier - a military vehicle for transporting soldiers

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'armored personnel carrier'.
