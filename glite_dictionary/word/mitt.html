mitt

1. NOUN

/ mɪt / (AmE), / mɪt / (BrE)
A large glove with sections for the thumb and fingers, used by baseball fielders to catch the ball.
baseball glove for fielders

Examples

The outfielder stretched out his mitt to make the catch.
He forgot his mitt at home and had to borrow one for the game.
A worn mitt can make it harder to field ground balls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glove - a covering for the hand with separate sections for each finger

Etymology

Short for 'mitten', from Middle English 'mitain' (mitten, glove), from Old French.

2. NOUN

/ mɪt / (AmE), / mɪt / (BrE)
(informal) a person's hand.
informal term for hand
informal

Examples

He caught the ball with one mitt and waved with the other.
Get your mitts off my sandwich!
The thief slipped the wallet right out of his unsuspecting mitt.

Synset Examples

he had the hands of a surgeon
he extended his mitt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand - the end part of a person's arm beyond the wrist

Etymology

Originally from 'mitten', meaning a type of glove, later used to refer to the hand itself in informal English.
