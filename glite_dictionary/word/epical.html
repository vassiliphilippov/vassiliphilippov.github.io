epical

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an epic, especially in its style, scope, or grandeur.
relating to an epic

Examples

The film was praised for its epical sweep and dramatic intensity.
Her storytelling had an epical quality that captivated audiences for hours.
The epical battles described in the novel evoke the grandeur of ancient legends.

Synset Examples

epic tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - A long narrative poem or something grand and heroic

Etymology

From Latin epicus (epic), patterned after 'epic' + '-al'.
