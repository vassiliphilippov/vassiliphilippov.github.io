tribade

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌbeɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.eɪd / (BrE)
an archaic term for a lesbian; a female homosexual
archaic term for a lesbian
archaic

Examples

In historical texts, the term tribade was often used to describe women who loved other women.
The word tribade is seldom encountered in modern English, except in academic discussions.
Medical writings of the 18th century sometimes referred to female homosexuality by calling the women tribades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesbian - a female homosexual

Etymology

From French 'tribade', from Latin 'tribas', from Greek 'tribas' (woman who engages in sexual activity with another woman), from 'tribein' (to rub).
