tropical

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to the tropics, or either tropic; characteristic of regions near the equator, typically hot and humid.
relating to the tropics; very hot and humid

Examples

The island has a tropical climate with high temperatures and frequent rainfall.
Many exotic fruits, such as mangoes and papayas, grow best in tropical regions.
They dreamed of spending their honeymoon on a tropical beach.

Synset Examples

tropical year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temperate - relating to regions with mild climate

Etymology

From Greek *tropikos* (of or pertaining to a turn or change, especially of the sun at the solstice).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
(Of language or expression) characterized by the use of tropes or figures of speech; figurative rather than literal.
figurative; involving tropes

Examples

The poet often employs tropical language to convey complex ideas.
In rhetorical analysis, tropical expressions are distinguished from literal ones.
Her novel is filled with tropical imagery, turning everyday objects into rich metaphors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figurative - (Of language) representing something by means of a figure or symbol; not literal
  • Antonym: literal - Taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor

Etymology

From Latin tropicus, from Greek tropikos (of a turn, relating to turning), later also applied to 'trope' meaning a figure of speech.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
(of weather or climate) hot and humid, like that typical of the tropics.
hot and humid, like the tropics

Examples

The city experiences tropical weather throughout most of the year.
After the rain, the air felt thick and tropical inside the greenhouse.
They escaped the cold winter by visiting a tropical island.

Synset Examples

tropical weather

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temperate - of climate: moderate, not extreme

Etymology

From Latin tropicus (of a solstice), from Greek tropikos (of turning), referring to the tropics near the equator.

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈtrɑːpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the tropics, the region near the equator, especially in terms of climate, vegetation, or location.
relating to the tropics, near the equator

Examples

The climate here is hot and tropical year-round, with frequent rainstorms.
She filled her garden with tropical plants that thrive in warm, humid weather.
We spent our vacation on a tropical island surrounded by crystal-clear water.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temperate - relating to regions with moderate climate, not extreme

Etymology

From Latin tropicus (of or pertaining to a turn or change, from tropus ‘turn’, from Greek tropos).
