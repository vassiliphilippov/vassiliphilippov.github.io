clew

1. NOUN

/ kluː / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
(archaic) A fact or object that helps to solve a mystery or problem; a clue.
archaic form of 'clue' (evidence to solve a mystery)
archaic

Examples

The detective searched for a clew that would lead him to the culprit.
Ancient texts often refer to a clew in solving riddles or puzzles.
He grasped at every clew, desperate to find the answer hidden in the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a fact or object that helps to solve a mystery or problem

Etymology

Old spelling of 'clue;' originally from Old English 'cliwen' (ball of thread), influenced by Greek mythology.

2. VERB

/ kluː / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
to roll something into a ball or rounded mass, especially a coil of rope or yarn
roll into a ball or mass
archaic

Examples

The sailor began to clew the rope before stowing it in the hold.
It's important to clew the yarn neatly to prevent tangling.
They clewed the cable after finishing the repairs on deck.

Etymology

From Old English 'cliewen' (ball, skein of yarn), related to Dutch 'kluwen', German 'Knäuel'.

3. NOUN

/ kluː / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
A ball of yarn, thread, or cord, especially one that can be unwound.
ball of yarn, cord, or thread

Examples

The adventurer followed a clew of yarn through the winding passages.
She handed him a clew to start his weaving project.
In ancient myths, a clew helped heroes find their way out of mazes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ball - a mass of thread, yarn, or string wound into a spherical shape

Etymology

Old English *clēowen* (ball of thread), related to Dutch *kluwen* and German *Klüwel*.

4. NOUN

/ kluː / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
each of the cords by which a hammock is suspended at either end.
cord used to suspend a hammock

Examples

The clews of the hammock were tightly secured to the trees for stability.
He examined the clews to ensure the hammock could safely hold his weight.
After a long day, she rested comfortably, supported by the sturdy clews of her hammock.

Etymology

From Middle English *clewe*, from Old English *cliewen* (ball of thread, ball), related to winding or tying.
