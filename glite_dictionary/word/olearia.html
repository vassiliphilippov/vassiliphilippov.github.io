Olearia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈlɪəriə / (AmE), / əʊˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian evergreen shrubs or small trees, recognized for their large daisylike flowers
genus of Australian evergreen shrubs

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Olearia along the stone path.
Many species of Olearia are cultivated for their attractive flowers and foliage.
The botanist identified the shrub as an Olearia native to southern Australia.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Adam Olearius (1603–1671).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈlɪəriə / (AmE), / əʊˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian evergreen shrubs or small trees, known for their large, daisylike flowers
Australian genus of shrubs or small trees

Examples

Many species of Olearia are cultivated in gardens for their attractive flowers and foliage.
The coastal cliffs were dotted with Olearia bushes in full bloom.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Olearia genus during their expedition in Australia.

Etymology

Named after the German botanist Johann Gottfried Olearius (1635–1711).
