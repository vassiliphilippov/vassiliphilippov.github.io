to mechanize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənaɪz / (BrE)
to introduce machines or automatic devices instead of manual labor; to make mechanical
make mechanical; use machines instead of people

Examples

The company decided to mechanize its assembly line to increase efficiency.
Efforts to mechanize agriculture dramatically reduced the need for farm labor.
They are planning to mechanize the process to speed up production.

Synset Examples

mechanize the procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - make a process operate automatically using technology

Etymology

From French 'mécaniser', from 'mécanique' (mechanical), from Greek 'mēkhanē' (machine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00480529-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛk.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to equip (an army or military unit) with armed and armored motor vehicles such as tanks and personnel carriers
equip a military force with armored vehicles

Examples

The government decided to mechanize its infantry divisions to improve mobility and firepower.
After World War I, many nations rushed to mechanize their armies for modern warfare.
The general advocated plans to mechanize more units to match their rivals' advanced forces.

Synset Examples

mechanize armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to motorize - to equip with motor vehicles (general, not just armored or military)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481682-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɛk.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiː.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make a process, job, or activity automatic or routine, often resulting in monotony or lack of creativity
make automatic or monotonous

Examples

If you mechanize your daily routine, life can start to feel dull and repetitive.
Factories often mechanized tasks that were once performed by skilled workers.
Over time, she began to mechanize her responses, giving the same answer to every customer.

Synset Examples

If your work becomes too mechanized, change jobs!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - to use machines or computers to do work instead of people

Etymology

From French *mécaniser*, based on Greek *mēkhanē* (machine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481500-v
Synset POS: v
