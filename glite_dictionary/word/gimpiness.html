gimpiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmpi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmpɪnɪs / (BrE)
difficulty or disability in walking, typically caused by a problem with the legs or feet
difficulty in walking due to leg or foot disability
informal

Examples

His gimpiness made it difficult for him to keep up with the rest of the group during the hike.
After the accident, she was self-conscious about her gimpiness but determined to walk unaided.
Doctors recommended physical therapy to reduce the gimpiness in his gait following surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lameness - the condition of being unable to walk normally because of injury or illness

Etymology

From 'gimp' (a slang term for someone with a limp) + '-iness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.
