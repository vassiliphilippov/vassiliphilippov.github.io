Physa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪsə / (AmE), / ˈfɪsə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater, air-breathing snails in the family Physidae.
a genus of freshwater snails

Examples

The Physa genus includes several species of freshwater snails commonly found in ponds and streams.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Physa to better understand their ecological roles.
The shell of a Physa is usually thin and elongated compared to other snail genera.

Etymology

From Greek phusa (bubble, bellows), referring to the snail's shape.
