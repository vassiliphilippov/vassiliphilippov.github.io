en bloc

1. ADV

/ ɑn ˈblɑk / (AmE), / ɒ̃ ˈblɒk / (BrE)
all together; as a single unit or group, without dividing into individual parts.
as a single unit; all together

Examples

The legislation was approved en bloc by the committee without further discussion.
The board decided to reject the proposed amendments en bloc rather than considering them individually.
She purchased the collection en bloc, acquiring every item in a single transaction.

Synset Examples

the students turned out en masse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: en masse - in a mass; all together

Etymology

From French 'en bloc', meaning 'in a block' or 'as a whole'.
