eightpence

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtpɛns / (AmE), / ˈeɪtpəns / (BrE)
A former British coin worth eight pennies.
old British coin worth eight pennies
archaic british

Examples

The collector displayed a rare eightpence among his coin collection.
During the 16th century, an eightpence was a significant amount of money.
He paid for the loaf of bread with an eightpence.

Etymology

From 'eight' + 'pence', meaning eight pennies.
