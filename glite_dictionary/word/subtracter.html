subtracter

1. NOUN

/ səbˈtræktər / (AmE), / səbˈtræktə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism that performs the operation of subtraction, especially in computing or arithmetic machinery.
machine that subtracts numbers

Examples

The digital circuit includes a subtracter to process the incoming data signals.
Engineers used a special subtracter to automate complex calculations in the laboratory.
Each arithmetic unit contains an adder and a subtracter for mathematical operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adder - a machine or circuit that adds numbers

Etymology

From 'subtract' plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.
