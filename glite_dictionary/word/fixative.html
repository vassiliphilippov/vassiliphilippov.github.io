fixative

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪksətɪv / (BrE)
a chemical substance, often a varnish dissolved in alcohol, that is sprayed or applied to drawings, paintings, or photographs to prevent smudging or fading
chemical to prevent smudging of art

Examples

The artist applied a layer of fixative to her charcoal sketch to protect it.
Always use a well-ventilated area when spraying fixative on your artwork.
Without fixative, the pastel drawing would easily smear and lose its details.

Etymology

From French 'fixatif', from Latin 'fixare' (to fix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪk.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.sə.tɪv / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to preserve tissues or cells by stabilizing their structure, especially for microscopic examination
chemical for preserving tissues or cells
medical

Examples

The tissue samples were placed in a fixative to prevent decay before analysis.
Formaldehyde is a common fixative used in biological laboratories.
Always handle the fixative with care, as it can be hazardous to your health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservative - a substance used to keep something from decaying

Etymology

From Latin fixare (to fix) + -ive (forming adjectives/nouns).
