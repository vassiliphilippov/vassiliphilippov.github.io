to beg

1. VERB

/ bɛɡ / (AmE), / bɛɡ / (BrE)
to ask someone earnestly or urgently for something; to entreat or plead
to entreat or plead urgently

Examples

I beg you to reconsider your decision, as it will affect many people.
She begged him for forgiveness after the argument.
The child was begging his parents for a new bicycle all week.

Synset Examples

I beg you to stop!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to implore - to beg earnestly or desperately
  • Synonym: to plead - to make an emotional appeal
  • Antonym: demand - to ask authoritatively or forcefully

Etymology

From Old English *beggan*, of uncertain origin; possibly from Old Norse *begja* (to bend, bow).

2. VERB

/ bɛɡ / (AmE), / bɛɡ / (BrE)
(of a statement or argument) to evade a direct answer or to assume the truth of the point at issue, especially in the phrase 'beg the question'.
evade the issue; assume a point (in argument)

Examples

The politician tried to beg the question instead of providing a clear answer.
She accused her opponent of attempting to beg the point in the debate.
Rather than addressing the issue directly, he chose to beg the question.

Synset Examples

beg the question
beg the point in the discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid giving a direct answer
  • Synonym: to assume - to suppose something without proof
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00811888-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɛg / (AmE), / bɛg / (BrE)
to ask for something in an urgent or earnest way, especially repeatedly or persistently
ask urgently or persistently

Examples

I had to beg for forgiveness after my mistake at work.
The children begged their parents for more time at the park.
She kept begging her friend to reconsider the decision.

Synset Examples

Henry IV solicited the Pope for a divorce
My neighbor keeps soliciting money for different charities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plead - to ask for something earnestly, especially in a legal or emotional context
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully or authoritatively, often with the expectation of compliance

Etymology

From Old English *beggan*, probably of Celtic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00784104-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ bɛɡ / (AmE), / bɛɡ / (BrE)
to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something, especially for money or food, typically as charity or without payment
ask for something as charity

Examples

The homeless man sat on the corner and begged for small change.
She was embarrassed to beg for food when she lost her job.
Children sometimes beg their parents for treats after school.

Synset Examples

beg money and food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panhandle - to beg for money in a public place

Etymology

From Middle English 'beggen', possibly from Old English 'bedecian' (to beg for), or Old French 'begart' (mendicant).

Source: WordNet
