conceptualisation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛp.tʃu.ə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɛp.tjʊ.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The action or process of forming an idea or concept in the mind.
the process of forming an idea

Examples

The conceptualisation of the new project began with a brainstorming session.
Her conceptualisation of social justice has influenced many other thinkers.
Effective conceptualisation is key to solving complex scientific problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceptualization - alternate spelling (American) of 'conceptualisation'

Etymology

From English 'conceptualize' + '-ation'; 'concept' from Latin 'conceptus' (something conceived).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛptʃuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɛptʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming an idea or concept; the creation of a comprehensive notion or mental representation of something
the process of forming a concept
british

Examples

The conceptualisation of the project took months of careful planning and discussion.
His conceptualisation of justice is rooted in fairness and equality.
During the early stages, the team's conceptualisation helped shape the entire research direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceptualization - the act or process of forming a concept (American spelling)
  • Synonym: conception - the forming or devising of a concept or idea
  • Synonym: formulation - the action of devising or creating something

Etymology

From concept + -ual + -isation, patterned after 'conceptualise' + '-ation'.
