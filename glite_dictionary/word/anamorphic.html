anamorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of an optical system or lens) distorting an image, especially so that it appears normal only when viewed by a specific device or from a particular angle.
distorting optical system or lens

Examples

The director chose to shoot the film with anamorphic lenses for a widescreen cinematic effect.
The projection system required an anamorphic adapter to display the correct aspect ratio.
Architects sometimes use anamorphic images to create optical illusions in public spaces.

Synset Examples

an anamorphic lense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distorting - changing the shape or appearance of something

Etymology

From Greek 'ana-' (again) + 'morphē' (form, shape).

2. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈmɔɹfɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the gradual evolution or transformation from one type of organism to another
pertaining to gradual evolutionary change
medical

Examples

Some fossils exhibit anamorphic traits that indicate a slow transition between species.
The study focused on anamorphic evolutionary processes seen in ancient amphibians.
Anamorphic changes in the organism’s structure were observed over multiple generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolutionary - relating to the process of evolution or gradual development

Etymology

From Greek 'anamorphosis' (transformation), from 'ana-' (up, again) + 'morphē' (form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02631409-a
Synset POS: a
