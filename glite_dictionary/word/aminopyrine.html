aminopyrine

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˈpaɪˌrin / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈpaɪriːn / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound formerly used as an analgesic and antipyretic to relieve pain and reduce fever
a medicinal compound used to relieve pain and fever
medical

Examples

The doctor once prescribed aminopyrine for severe headaches, although it is rarely used today.
Researchers studied the effects of aminopyrine on reducing fever in clinical trials.
Due to potential side effects, aminopyrine has largely been replaced by safer alternatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain
  • Synonym: antipyretic - a substance that reduces fever

Etymology

From amino- (referring to the amino group) + pyrazine (the chemical ring structure base).
