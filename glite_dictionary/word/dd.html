DD

1. NOUN

/ diː diː / (AmE), / diː diː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for Doctor of Divinity, a doctoral degree in religion or theology.
Doctor of Divinity (religious degree)
american british

Examples

Reverend Smith holds a DD from Yale University.
She was awarded an honorary DD for her contributions to theology.
Many bishops in the Anglican Church have a formal DD degree.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Divinitatis Doctor' (Doctor of Divinity).
