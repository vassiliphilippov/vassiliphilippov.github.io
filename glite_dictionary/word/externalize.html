to externalize

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something external or objective; to express or represent an internal process, thought, or feeling as something observable or real
make something external, objective, or visible

Examples

Children often externalize their fears through drawings and stories.
She tried to externalize her emotions by writing in her journal daily.
The company’s culture begins to externalize in the way employees interact with customers.

Synset Examples

language externalizes our thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to internalize - to make something internal or subjective; incorporate within oneself

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) + English suffix '-ize'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00533687-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɜrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to regard or treat something, such as a feeling or thought, as existing outside oneself, or as objective rather than subjective
regard as existing outside oneself

Examples

He tends to externalize his frustrations, blaming others for his problems.
By externalizing their anxiety, patients often attribute internal conflicts to external factors.
Some theories suggest that people externalize their fears by projecting them onto the world around them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to internalize - to make something, such as an idea or attitude, part of one's own thinking or being

Etymology

From Latin 'externus' (outside) + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00731627-v
Synset POS: v
