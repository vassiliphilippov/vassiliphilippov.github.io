to sightsing

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪtˌsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtˌsɪŋ / (BrE)
to sing a piece of music from a written score without having seen or practiced it beforehand
sing music from score at first sight

Examples

At her audition, she was asked to sightsing a challenging melody.
He can sightsing complex choral works with remarkable accuracy.
Students are required to sightsing new sheet music every week in class.

Synset Examples

This tenor can sightsing even the most difficult pieces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sight-read - to perform music by reading it for the first time

Etymology

Formed from 'sight' and 'sing', modeled after 'sight-read'.
