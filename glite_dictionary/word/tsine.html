tsine

1. NOUN

/ tsaɪn / (AmE), / tsiːn / (BrE)
a wild ox native to the Malay Archipelago, known for its robust build and found in forested areas
wild ox native to the Malay Archipelago

Examples

The tsine is rarely seen outside remote forests in Borneo and Sumatra.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered tsine.
Researchers tracked several tsines to study their migration patterns.
