hoar

1. NOUN

/ hɔr / (AmE), / hɔː / (BrE)
A white frost consisting of ice crystals that form on surfaces, especially outdoors, in cold conditions.
white frost of ice crystals on surfaces

Examples

In the early morning, the grass sparkled with hoar created by the cold night air.
A delicate layer of hoar covered the branches, turning the forest silver.
The old fence posts were edged with hoar after the sudden drop in temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frost - a deposit of small white ice crystals formed on the ground or other surfaces
  • Synonym: rime - frost formed on cold objects by the rapid freezing of water vapor

Etymology

Old English hār (gray, venerable), originally referring to white or gray hair, later extended to frost.

2. ADJ

/ hɔr / (AmE), / hɔː / (BrE)
(literary, archaic) showing characteristics of age, especially having grey or white hair.
showing grey or white hair with age
archaic

Examples

Time had left his once-black beard quite hoar.
The ancient king greeted them with his hoar hair shining in the sunlight.
Legends spoke of a hoar elder whose wisdom guided the village.

Synset Examples

whose beard with age is hoar
nodded his hoary head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoary - having grey or white hair, aged or venerable

Etymology

From Old English hār (gray, venerable, old), related to German hehr (noble, grand).
