arteria iliaca

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə ɪˈlaɪəkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ɪˈlaɪəkə / (BrE)
either of two large arteries in the pelvis that supply blood to the pelvic organs and legs, formed by the bifurcation of the abdominal aorta
major pelvic artery
medical

Examples

A blockage in the arteria iliaca can cause serious circulation issues in the legs.
Surgeons repaired the damaged arteria iliaca after the traumatic injury.
The arteria iliaca branches off the aorta to supply blood to the pelvic region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iliac artery - large artery in the pelvis branching from the aorta

Etymology

From Latin, 'arteria' (artery) + 'iliaca' (pertaining to the ilium).
