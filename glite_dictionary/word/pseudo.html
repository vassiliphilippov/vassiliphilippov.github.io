pseudo

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊ / (BrE)
Not genuine; appearing to be something but not actually so, often used as a prefix in combination with other words.
not genuine; fake or false

Examples

Many experts dismissed his theory as pseudo science with no real basis in fact.
She wore a pseudo diamond ring that sparkled just like the real thing.
The movie was filled with pseudo philosophical dialogue that sounded deep but meant little.

Synset Examples

a pseudo esthete
pseudoclassic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - truly what it is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine
  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; a forgery or imitation

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false), used as a prefix meaning 'false' or 'sham'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊ / (BrE)
A person who pretends to be something they are not; an impostor or fake.
a person making false pretenses
informal

Examples

Everyone thought he was a real expert, but he turned out to be a pseudo.
The club quickly exposed the pseudo who claimed to be an Olympic athlete.
Beware of the pseudo in the group—he is always pretending to be someone he's not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - a person who pretends to be someone else
  • Synonym: fake - a person who is not what they claim to be
  • Antonym: genuine - being truly what something is said to be

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false). Commonly used informally in English since the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10221154-n
Synset POS: n
