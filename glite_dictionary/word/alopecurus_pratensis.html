Alopecurus pratensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈpiːkjʊrəs prəˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaləˈpiːkjʊrəs praˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A robust perennial grass native to northern regions of Europe and Asia, with silky flower spikes; widely cultivated for pasture and hay and naturalized in North America.
a perennial pasture grass (meadow foxtail)

Examples

Alopecurus pratensis is an important grass species for grazing livestock in temperate climates.
The lush meadows were dominated by Alopecurus pratensis during the spring.
Farmers often plant Alopecurus pratensis to improve hay yields and soil quality.

Etymology

From Latin *Alopecurus* ('foxtail'), from Greek *alopekourus* (fox-tailed), and Latin *pratensis* (of the meadow).
