animism

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈanɪmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that natural objects, phenomena, and the universe itself possess souls or consciousness
belief that natural objects have souls

Examples

Many indigenous religions incorporate animism into their worldviews.
The concept of animism teaches that rivers, trees, and mountains are alive.
Anthropology students often study animism when learning about early human societies.

Synset Examples

animism is common among primitive peoples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritualism - belief that spirits exist or can interact with the living
  • Antonym: materialism - belief that only physical matter exists; opposite of spiritual belief in souls

Etymology

From Latin anima (soul) + -ism (belief or doctrine).
