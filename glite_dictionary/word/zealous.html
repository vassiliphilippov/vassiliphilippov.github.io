zealous

1. ADJ

/ ˈzɛləs / (AmE), / ˈzɛl.əs / (BrE)
showing great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective
showing strong enthusiasm or passion

Examples

She is extremely zealous about protecting the environment.
The zealous supporters cheered loudly for their team.
His zealous approach to work often inspires his colleagues.

Synset Examples

an avid sports fan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'zelosus', from Latin 'zelus' (zeal).
