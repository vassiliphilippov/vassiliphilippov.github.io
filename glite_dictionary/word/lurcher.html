lurcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝː.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɜː.tʃə / (BrE)
a person who waits in hiding, often with the intention of ambushing or taking advantage of someone or something
person hiding in concealment
archaic

Examples

The lurcher crouched behind the bushes, waiting for the travelers to approach.
Stories of the old forest warned of lurchers preying on the unwary.
He moved swiftly, fearing that a lurcher might be hiding in the alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lurker - someone who hides or moves stealthily

Etymology

From 'lurch,' meaning to lurk or lie in wait, possibly from Old French 'lecher' (to ambush).
