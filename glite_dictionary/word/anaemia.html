anaemia

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the number of red blood cells or the amount of hemoglobin is below normal, resulting in fatigue and weakness.
deficiency of red blood cells
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with anaemia after noticing persistent fatigue and paleness.
Iron supplements are often prescribed to treat anaemia.
A diet lacking in iron-rich foods can lead to anaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - US spelling of anaemia, a deficiency of red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'anaimia', from 'an-' (without) + 'haima' (blood).
