confrontation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.frənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.frʌnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which two or more groups oppose each other, often in a hostile or tense manner.
hostile opposition between groups

Examples

The threat of a violent confrontation between rival gangs forced police to intervene.
A confrontation with management over working conditions seemed inevitable.
Diplomats worked late into the night to avoid further confrontation between the nations.

Synset Examples

the government was not ready for a confrontation with the unions
the invaders encountered stiff opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - A serious disagreement or argument; a clash between opposing parties
  • Antonym: accord - A situation of harmonious agreement or peace between groups

Etymology

From Latin confrontatio, from confrontare (to border on, confront).

5. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which there is a hostile or argumentative meeting or conflict between opposing parties, especially face-to-face.
hostile or argumentative encounter

Examples

The confrontation between the two leaders was broadcast live on television.
After years of tension, the debate finally erupted into open confrontation.
Police were called in to prevent a violent confrontation between the protesters and security guards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • Synonym: dispute - a disagreement, argument, or debate
  • Antonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties

Etymology

From French confrontation, from Latin confrontare (to border on, to confront).
