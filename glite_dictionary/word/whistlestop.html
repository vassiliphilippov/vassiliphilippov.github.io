to whistlestop

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪs.təl.stɑp / (AmE), / ˈwɪs.təl.stɒp / (BrE)
To travel from place to place, usually by train, making brief stops to campaign for votes during an election.
make campaign stops to solicit votes

Examples

The candidate plans to whistlestop through five states in just three days.
She whistlestopped across the Midwest, greeting supporters at every station.
With a tight schedule, they whistlestopped their way from town to town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - to work in an organized and active way toward a goal, especially for a political or social cause

Etymology

From 'whistle stop', referring to small train stations where trains stop only on signal; verb use given prominence in US political campaigns.
