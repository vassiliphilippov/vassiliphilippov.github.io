paresthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.əˈsθi.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌpar.ɪsˈθiː.zɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal sensation of the skin, such as tingling, prickling, or numbness, typically caused by nerve damage or irritation
abnormal skin sensation (tingling, numbness, etc.)
medical

Examples

The patient reported a constant paresthesia in her left hand after the injury.
Many people experience paresthesia or 'pins and needles' after sitting with their legs crossed for a long time.
Diabetic neuropathy can lead to chronic paresthesia in the feet and toes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numbness - loss of sensation in a part of the body
  • Synonym: tingling - a slight prickling or stinging sensation of the skin

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, abnormal) and 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
