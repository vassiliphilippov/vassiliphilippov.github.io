Inula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈɪn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs or subshrubs in the daisy family, including elecampane.
genus of herbs including elecampane

Examples

The leaves of Inula are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
Several species within the genus Inula are cultivated for their ornamental flowers.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Inula in ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elecampane - a tall plant of the genus Inula, used in herbal medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'inula', the classical name for elecampane.
