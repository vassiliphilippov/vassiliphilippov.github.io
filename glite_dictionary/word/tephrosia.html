Tephrosia

1. PROPN

/ tɛˈfroʊʒiə / (AmE), / tɛˈfrəʊziə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical herbs or shrubs in the pea family, known as hoary peas, often with hairy or silvery foliage.
genus of tropical/subtropical herbs or shrubs (hoary peas)

Examples

Several species of Tephrosia are valued for their use in traditional medicine and agriculture.
Botanists discovered a rare Tephrosia shrub during their study in the tropical forest.
The leaves of Tephrosia often have a distinctive silvery appearance.

Etymology

From Greek tephros (ash-colored), referring to the grayish color of some species’ leaves.
