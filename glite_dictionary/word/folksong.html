folksong

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
a song that is traditionally passed down among the people of a community, often reflecting their culture, history, or beliefs
traditional song of a people or region

Examples

The musician performed a beautiful folksong that had been handed down through generations.
She enjoys collecting and researching folksongs from different cultures around the world.
His latest album features several traditional folksongs from rural England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk music - traditional music of a community or nation

Etymology

From 'folk' (people) + 'song', first recorded in the 19th century.
