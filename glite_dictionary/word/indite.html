to indite

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪt / (BrE)
to write or compose a literary work, especially in a formal or literary style
write or compose (literary work)
archaic

Examples

The poet would often indite sonnets inspired by nature.
During the ceremony, she began to indite a formal address to the assembly.
Many writers indite reflective memoirs later in their careers.

Synset Examples

She composed a poem
He wrote four novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compose - to create or write a piece of writing or music
  • Synonym: to write - to form letters, words, or symbols on a surface, or to create written works

Etymology

From Latin 'indictare', frequentative of 'indicare', meaning to declare or pronounce.
