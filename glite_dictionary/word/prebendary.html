prebendary

1. NOUN

/ prɛˈbɛndəri / (AmE), / prɪˈbɛndəri / (BrE)
A member of clergy, typically a canon of a cathedral or collegiate church, who receives a stipend known as a prebend for their services.
clergyman receiving a prebend

Examples

The newly appointed prebendary attended services at the cathedral every Sunday.
In the Middle Ages, a prebendary received income from church lands in exchange for his duties.
After years of dedication, she was honoured with the title of prebendary at the historic chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon - a clergy member serving in a cathedral or collegiate church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin prebendarius, from prebenda (prebend).
