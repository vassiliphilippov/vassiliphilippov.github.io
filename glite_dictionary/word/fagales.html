Fagales

1. NOUN

/ fəˈɡeɪliːz / (AmE), / fəˈɡeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of dicotyledonous trees and shrubs that includes beeches, oaks, birches, and related families
an order of trees and shrubs (beeches, oaks, etc.)

Examples

The Fagales include many important timber-producing trees such as oaks and beeches.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the Fagales to better understand global forest diversity.
Several members of the Fagales are valued for their edible nuts, including walnuts and chestnuts.

Etymology

From New Latin Fagales, from Fagus (Latin for 'beech tree') + -ales (Latin suffix for order of plants).
