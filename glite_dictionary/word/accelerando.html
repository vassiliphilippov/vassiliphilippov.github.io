accelerando

1. ADJ

/ əkˌsɛləˈrɑndoʊ / (AmE), / akˌsɛləˈrandəʊ / (BrE)
(music) with a gradual increase in speed or tempo.
music: gradually increasing in tempo

Examples

The piece should be played accelerando in the final bars for dramatic effect.
As the orchestra progressed, the conductor signaled an accelerando to build excitement.
During the dance, the music took an accelerando that energized the entire room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ritardando - (music) gradually decreasing in tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'accelerando', present participle of 'accelerare' (to accelerate).

3. NOUN

/ əksɛləˈrændoʊ / (AmE), / æksɛləˈrændəʊ / (BrE)
an increase in the speed or tempo of music, or a passage played with gradually increasing speed
increase in musical tempo

Examples

The orchestra executed a dramatic accelerando towards the end of the piece.
During the accelerando, the musicians had to closely follow the conductor’s tempo changes.
The composer marked an accelerando in the third measure of the score.
