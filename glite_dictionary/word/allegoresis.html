allegoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪɡəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌalɪɡəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the interpretation of written, oral, or artistic expression as an allegory
interpretation of something as allegory

Examples

Medieval scholars often employed allegoresis to explain Biblical passages.
The professor encouraged the use of allegoresis when analyzing classical literature.
His essay was criticized for relying too heavily on allegoresis rather than direct interpretation.

Synset Examples

The allegoresis of secular texts had been largely neglected since Fulgentius (sixth century), and was only reprised in the diffuse commentary tradition on Martianus that preceded Eriugena's study of that text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegory - a story, poem, or picture that can be interpreted to reveal a hidden meaning

Etymology

From Greek allegoria (allegory) + -esis (process or action).
