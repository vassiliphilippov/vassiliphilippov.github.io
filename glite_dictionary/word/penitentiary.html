penitentiary

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəri / (BrE)
a prison for people convicted of serious crimes
prison for major crimes

Examples

The prisoners spent years in the penitentiary for their crimes.
The old penitentiary was known for its strict discipline and harsh conditions.
After the trial, he was sent to a maximum-security penitentiary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial
  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime, typically for short periods

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'penitentiarius' (place of penitence), from Latin 'paenitentia' (repentance).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəri / (BrE)
relating to or used for the punishment or reform of criminals or wrongdoers, especially in institutions such as prisons
relating to prisons or punishment of criminals

Examples

The city established penitentiary facilities to house those convicted of serious crimes.
She spoke about the penitentiary system's focus on both punishment and rehabilitation.
Many penitentiary policies emphasize reform over retribution.

Synset Examples

penitentiary institutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penal - relating to punishment, especially by law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *penitentiarius* (place of penance).
