Euphrosyne

1. PROPN

/ juːˈfrɑːsɪni / (AmE), / juːˈfrɒsɪni / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the three Graces, personifying joy or mirth.
a Grace in Greek mythology; joy personified

Examples

In the painting, Euphrosyne is depicted dancing alongside her sisters, Aglaia and Thalia.
Celebrations in ancient Greece often invoked Euphrosyne as a symbol of joy.
The poet compared her happiness to the radiant Euphrosyne of myth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aglaia - one of the three Graces; personifies splendor
  • Synonym: Thalia - one of the three Graces; personifies bloom or festivity

Etymology

From Greek Εὐφροσύνη (Euphrosynē), meaning 'mirth' or 'joy'.
