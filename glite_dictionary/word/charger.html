charger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a device used for supplying electric power to recharge a battery, especially in electronic devices
device for recharging batteries

Examples

I forgot my charger at home and now my phone is dead.
She bought a new charger for her laptop after the old one broke.
Many hotels now provide USB chargers in their rooms for guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adapter - a device for connecting incompatible devices or systems
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a strong, swift horse used in battle, especially in the past
a horse ridden into battle
archaic

Examples

The knight rode his charger fearlessly into the thick of battle.
In medieval tournaments, noblemen paraded their finest charger before the crowds.
The charger galloped across the field, its armor glinting in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warhorse - a horse trained for battle; synonym, broader term

Etymology

From Old French 'chargier' (to load, burden), related to the horse's role in bearing riders into battle.
