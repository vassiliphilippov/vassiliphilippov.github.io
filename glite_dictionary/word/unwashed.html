unwashed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɑʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒʃt / (BrE)
not cleaned with or as if with soap and water; not having been washed
not cleaned; not washed

Examples

There was a pile of unwashed laundry in the corner of his room.
His hands were still unwashed after gardening all day.
The kitchen sink was overflowing with unwashed dishes.

Synset Examples

a sink full of unwashed dishes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: washed - cleaned with water or soap and water

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'washed', from Old English 'wascan'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɑʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒʃt / (BrE)
(usually the unwashed) Used humorously or disparagingly to refer to the masses or the common people considered as a group, especially as lacking refinement or privileges.
the masses; ordinary people (often disparaging)

Examples

Some politicians appeal directly to the unwashed in their campaign speeches.
The upscale restaurant was designed to be out of reach for the unwashed.
He spoke of the unwashed masses with barely disguised contempt.

Synset Examples

the common people in those days suffered greatly
behavior that branded him as common
his square plebeian nose
a vulgar and objectionable person
the unwashed masses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common - belonging to or shared by all; ordinary
  • Synonym: plebeian - of or characteristic of the common people
