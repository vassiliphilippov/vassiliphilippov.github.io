footedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The tendency to prefer one foot over the other for activities such as kicking or stepping.
preference for one foot over the other

Examples

Some soccer players develop strong footedness after years of training.
His left footedness is evident whenever he takes a penalty kick.
Scientists study footedness to understand brain lateralization in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handedness - preference for one hand over the other

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + '-ed' + '-ness', modeled on analogy with 'handedness'.
