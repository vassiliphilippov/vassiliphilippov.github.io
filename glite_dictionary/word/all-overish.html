all-overish

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔlˈoʊvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌɔːlˈəʊvərɪʃ / (BrE)
feeling unwell, uneasy, or lacking focus; disorganized or unsettled in mood or behavior
feeling unwell or unfocused

Examples

After working all night, I felt rather all-overish the next morning.
Her all-overish mood made it hard for her to concentrate on anything.
The room had an all-overish atmosphere, as if nobody knew what to do next.

Synset Examples

His attitude was all-overish, making it hard to focus.
She felt all-overish after the long hike in the sun.
The project was all-overish, lacking clear direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwell - feeling ill or not healthy
  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or structure; not well planned or focused

Etymology

Formed from 'all over' plus the suffix '-ish', denoting a vague or general feeling.
