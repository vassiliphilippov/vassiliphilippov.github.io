sprinkler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋklɚ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklə / (BrE)
a device with holes or rotating arms that sprays water over an area, especially used for watering lawns or gardens
device for spraying water, esp. on lawns

Examples

We set up a sprinkler in the backyard to keep the grass green during the summer.
The children ran through the sprinkler laughing and squealing with delight.
Remember to turn off the sprinkler after you water the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprayer - a device used for spraying liquids

Etymology

From sprinkle (to scatter in drops) + -er (agent noun suffix).
