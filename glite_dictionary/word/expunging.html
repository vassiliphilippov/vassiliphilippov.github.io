expunging

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspʌndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of erasing or removing completely, especially from a record or document
the act of erasing or deleting

Examples

The expunging of his criminal record gave him a fresh start.
Some historians oppose the expunging of controversial events from the textbooks.
Her privacy was protected by the expunging of personal data from the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erasing - the act of removing written or recorded material
  • Synonym: deletion - the process of removing or deducting

Etymology

From Latin 'expungere' (to mark for deletion), via the verb 'expunge.'
