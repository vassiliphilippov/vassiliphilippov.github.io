progressive

1. ADJ

/ prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a tax or taxation system) increasing in rate as the taxable amount (such as income) rises.
tax rate that increases with income

Examples

Many argue that a progressive tax system is more equitable than a flat tax.
Under the progressive tax law, those with higher incomes pay a higher percentage in taxes.
Some politicians advocate for making the property tax more progressive to reduce inequality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regressive - (of a tax) decreasing in rate as the amount subject to taxation increases

Etymology

From Latin 'progressivus', from 'progressus' (advance), relating to moving forward or advancing.

4. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports social reform, progress, and the protection of civil liberties, especially in politics
supporter of social and political reform

Examples

She describes herself as a progressive who supports universal healthcare and education reform.
Many progressives advocate for stronger environmental policies and climate change action.
The new mayor is considered a leading progressive in the city’s political landscape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
