nescience

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛsiəns / (AmE), / ˈnɛsiəns / (BrE)
lack of knowledge or awareness; ignorance, especially of orthodox beliefs or established knowledge.
ignorance; lack of knowledge or awareness

Examples

His nescience of quantum mechanics made the lecture difficult to follow.
Religious authorities often criticized those who lived in nescience regarding sacred texts.
Admitting one's nescience is sometimes the first step towards gaining real knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information
  • Antonym: knowledge - awareness or understanding gained through experience or education

Etymology

From Latin nescientia (ignorance), from nesciens (not knowing), from ne- (not) + scire (to know).
