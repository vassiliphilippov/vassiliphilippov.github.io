consort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.sɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɔːt / (BrE)
the husband or wife of a reigning monarch
spouse of a reigning monarch

Examples

She became the queen consort upon her husband's accession to the throne.
The prince was honored as the royal consort during official state events.
As consort to the king, she had many ceremonial duties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: monarch - a king, queen, or emperor; a sovereign ruler

Etymology

From Latin consors (sharing, partner).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːnsɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɔːt / (BrE)
to associate with someone, especially in a way that is disapproved of by others
associate with (often disapproved)

Examples

Politicians should be careful about whom they consort with in public.
Rumors spread after the ambassador was seen consorting with known criminals.
She rarely consorts with people outside her professional circle.

Synset Examples

He associates with strange people
She affiliates with her colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to spend time socially with another person or group
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'consors', literally 'sharing with', from 'con-' (together) + 'sors' (lot, fate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.sɔːrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɔːt / (BrE)
A group of similar musical instruments played together, especially in Renaissance or Baroque music.
group of similar musical instruments

Examples

The viol consort performed a suite of 16th-century dances at the concert.
A recorder consort consists of recorders of different sizes playing together.
Composers often wrote for the broken consort, mixing strings and winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians who perform together

Etymology

From Middle French 'consort', from Latin 'consors' (partner, sharer).
