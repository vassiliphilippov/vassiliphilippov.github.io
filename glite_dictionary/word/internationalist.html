internationalist

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
Supporting or relating to the principle of internationalism; advocating greater cooperation among nations.
supporting internationalism

Examples

The new administration adopted a distinctly internationalist approach to foreign policy.
Many economists hold internationalist views, promoting open borders for trade and cooperation.
She has always been an internationalist, believing in solving global problems through united action.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalist - supporting national interests over international cooperation

Etymology

From international + -ist, modeled after internationalism.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təɹˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
a member of a socialist or communist international organization; someone who supports or participates in actions coordinated across national boundaries within such movements
member of a socialist/communist international organization

Examples

The internationalist attended the congress representing his party's views on global cooperation.
As a committed internationalist, she believed in solidarity among workers worldwide.
Many internationalists played a crucial role in organizing transnational labor strikes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalist - a person who strongly identifies with their own nation and supports its interests

Etymology

From 'international' + '-ist'; 'international' from French 'international', based on 'nation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates international cooperation or internationalism.
advocate of international cooperation

Examples

She is a prominent internationalist who has worked tirelessly for global peace initiatives.
Many internationalists attended the conference to discuss cross-border environmental challenges.
As an internationalist, he believes global cooperation is essential for addressing climate change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalist - a person who advocates for national independence or interests

Etymology

Formed from 'international' + '-ist', indicating a proponent of internationalism.
