cleric

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪk / (BrE)
a member of the clergy; a person ordained for religious duties, especially in a Christian church
member of the clergy

Examples

The cleric delivered a moving sermon during Sunday mass.
As a respected cleric, she played a vital role in the community.
The debate was moderated by a local cleric who advocated for peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - an ordained minister responsible for performing religious ceremonies
  • Antonym: layperson - a non-ordained member of a religious group

Etymology

From Greek 'klērikos' (of the clergy), via Late Latin 'clericus'.
