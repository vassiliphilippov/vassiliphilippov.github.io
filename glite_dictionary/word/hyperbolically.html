hyperbolically

1. ADV

/ haɪ.pɚˈbɑː.lɪ.kli / (AmE), / haɪ.pəˈbɒl.ɪ.kli / (BrE)
in an exaggerated or overstated manner; using hyperbole
in an exaggerated way

Examples

He described the weather hyperbolically, claiming it was the worst storm of the century.
The story was told hyperbolically to emphasize its dramatic elements.
Critics argued that the risks were presented hyperbolically in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggeratedly - in a way that is exaggerated

Etymology

From hyperbolic + -ally, ultimately from Greek 'hyperbolē' (exaggeration).
