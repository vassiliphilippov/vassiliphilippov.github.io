butcherly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊtʃərli / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəli / (BrE)
done in a crude, clumsy, or unskillful manner; lacking refinement or skill.
done in a crude or unskilled way

Examples

The repairs to the car were so butcherly that it barely ran.
Their butcherly handling of the situation made things much worse.
He delivered a butcherly speech, stumbling over every other word.

Synset Examples

a botchy piece of work
it was an unskillful attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botched - done badly or clumsily
  • Synonym: unskillful - lacking skill or dexterity

Etymology

From 'butcher' + '-ly', originally referring to the rough manner of a butcher, later figurative.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʊtʃərli / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəli / (BrE)
involving or characterized by great bloodshed or brutality; savage or cruel
savage; involving bloodshed and brutality

Examples

The general's butcherly tactics left entire villages in ruins.
The film was criticized for its butcherly violence and lack of remorse.
His butcherly actions during the conflict were condemned by the international community.

Synset Examples

this bitter and sanguinary war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanguinary - involving or causing much bloodshed
  • Synonym: bloodthirsty - eager to shed blood; violent or cruel
  • Antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness

Etymology

From 'butcher' + '-ly', meaning like or characteristic of a butcher.
