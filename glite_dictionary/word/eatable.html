eatable

1. ADJ

/ ˈiːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈiːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
safe or suitable to be eaten; fit for consumption as food
suitable to be eaten as food

Examples

The berries were found to be eatable after careful examination.
Is this mushroom eatable or should I avoid it?
Much of the food was barely eatable due to overcooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edible - fit to be eaten (often as a technical or formal term)

Etymology

From the verb 'eat' + suffix '-able', meaning 'able to be eaten'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00832318-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈiːtəbəl / (BrE)
a thing that is fit or suitable to be eaten; an article of food
an item of food; something edible

Examples

She packed a bag full of eatables for the picnic.
There were all kinds of eatables on the table during the festival.
We stocked our shelves with canned eatables for the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edible - something fit to be eaten (adj. and n.)

Etymology

Formed from 'eat' + '-able', modeled after 'edible'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07572202-n
Synset POS: n
