Yahoo

1. PROPN

/ jæˈhu / (AmE), / jəˈhuː / (BrE)
a widely known web portal and search engine that provides information, news, images, email, and other online services
web portal and search engine

Examples

I often use Yahoo to check the latest news and stock prices.
During the late 1990s, Yahoo was one of the most popular web portals.
She created her first email account with Yahoo when she started college.

Etymology

Originally a fanciful word from Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels,' adopted as a company name by founders of Yahoo Inc. in 1994.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjæhu / (AmE), / ˈjɑːhuː / (BrE)
A member of a race of brutish and uncivilized creatures, resembling humans, that are subjugated by the Houyhnhnms in Jonathan Swift's novel 'Gulliver's Travels'; often used to describe a crude or uncouth person.
brutish creature from 'Gulliver's Travels'

Examples

The Yahoos in Swift's novel are portrayed as savage and filthy, contrasting with the wise Houyhnhnms.
He behaved like a complete Yahoo at the dinner party, shocking all the guests.
In literary criticism, the term Yahoo is used to describe individuals who are crude and uncultured.

Etymology

Coined by Jonathan Swift in 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).
