musk

1. NOUN

/ mʌsk / (AmE), / mʌsk / (BrE)
a strong-smelling substance secreted by the male musk deer, used in perfumery, especially as a fixative
aromatic deer secretion used in perfume

Examples

The rare and expensive musk is often used in luxury perfumes.
Harvesting musk from deer is controversial due to animal welfare concerns.
The scent of musk can linger on fabrics for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - a fragrant liquid typically made from essential oils and aroma compounds, used to give a pleasant scent to the body

Etymology

From Late Latin muscus, from Greek moschos, from Persian mušk (musk).

2. NOUN

/ mʌsk / (AmE), / mʌsk / (BrE)
a strong-smelling substance secreted by the male musk deer, used in perfumes and formerly in medicine
scented secretion from the musk deer

Examples

The musk from the deer was highly prized by perfumers in ancient times.
Some perfumes still attempt to recreate the warm scent of natural musk.
In the past, hunters would extract musk from male deer for use in medicine and fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - a fragrant liquid typically made from essential oils and aroma compounds

Etymology

From Late Latin muscus ‘musk’, from Persian ‘mushk’, from Sanskrit ‘mushka’ meaning ‘testicle’, referring to the gland’s shape.
