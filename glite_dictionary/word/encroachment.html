encroachment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkroʊtʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of entering or intruding on someone else's property, rights, or territory without permission
intrusion on another's property or rights

Examples

The neighbors were concerned about the encroachment of the new building onto their land.
Legal action was taken to stop the continual encroachment on the wildlife reserve.
He was fined for the encroachment caused by building his fence over the property line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespass - entry onto someone's land or property without permission

Etymology

From Old French 'encrochier' (seize, fasten upon), from 'croche' (hook).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkroʊt͡ʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊt͡ʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of gradually entering or intruding upon an area, space, or rights, especially without permission
gradual intrusion or entry into an area

Examples

There is growing concern about the encroachment of urban development into rural areas.
Strict laws exist to prevent the encroachment of commercial fishing into protected marine zones.
The villagers protested the encroachment of tourists onto their sacred land.

Synset Examples

an invasion of tourists
an invasion of locusts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - the act of entering a place in large numbers, often forcefully
  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering without permission
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of leaving or moving away from a place

Etymology

From Old French *encrochier* (to seize, fasten upon), from en- (in) + croc (hook).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07444576-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkroʊtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊt.mənt / (BrE)
the act of gradually and often undesirably influencing or affecting something, especially by spreading into its area
gradual, unwanted influence or intrusion

Examples

The encroachment of commercial interests on public lands has sparked protests.
Many worry about the encroachment of digital technology into every aspect of daily life.
He spoke out against the encroachment of Western culture on traditional values.

Synset Examples

they resented the impingement of American values on European culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or affecting without permission
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing or pulling back influence

Etymology

From Old French *encrochier* (to seize, fasten upon), from *en-* (in) + *croche* (hook).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
