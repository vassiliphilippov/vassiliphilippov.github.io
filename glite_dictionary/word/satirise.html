to satirise

1. VERB

/ ˈsætəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsætəraɪz / (BrE)
to use satire to criticize or ridicule someone or something, often by exaggeration or irony
criticize with satire
british

Examples

The novel skillfully satirises the excesses of celebrity culture.
Cartoonists often satirise political figures to highlight their flaws.
The play satirised the government’s response to the crisis with sharp wit.

Synset Examples

The writer satirized the politician's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satirize - to ridicule with satire (American spelling)
  • Synonym: parody - to imitate in a humorous or satirical way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From French *satiriser*, from *satire* (satire); influenced by Latin *satira* (a literary composition).
