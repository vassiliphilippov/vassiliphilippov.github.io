Euproctis phaeorrhoea

1. NOUN

/ juːˈprɑktɪs ˌfiːəˈroʊiə / (AmE), / juːˈprɒktɪs ˌfiːəˈrəʊɪə / (BrE)
A small brown and white European moth, now present in eastern United States, known as a pest of various shade and fruit trees.
species of European moth; pest of shade and fruit trees

Examples

The larvae of Euproctis phaeorrhoea can cause significant damage to orchard trees.
Farmers in the eastern United States are concerned about infestations of Euproctis phaeorrhoea.
Pesticides are often used to control populations of Euproctis phaeorrhoea in fruit plantations.

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'eu-' (good, well), 'proktos' (anus), and species name based on coloration and appearance.
