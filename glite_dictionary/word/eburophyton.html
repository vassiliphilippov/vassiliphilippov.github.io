Eburophyton

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌbjʊrəˈfaɪtən / (AmE), / ɛˌbjʊərəˈfʌɪtən / (BrE)
a genus of monocotyledonous plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae, known for species with distinctive white flowers
genus of orchids

Examples

The rare Eburophyton can be found in certain woodlands of North America.
Botanists were excited to discover a previously undocumented population of Eburophyton.
Eburophyton is prized by collectors for its delicate, ivory-colored blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orchidaceae - the orchid family, a large family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Greek 'eburos' (ivory) and 'phyton' (plant), referring to the ivory-colored flowers.
