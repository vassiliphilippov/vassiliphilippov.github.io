ceric

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪərɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing cerium with a valence of four (+4 oxidation state).
relating to cerium in +4 state

Examples

The bright yellow color of ceric compounds distinguishes them from cerous compounds.
The laboratory synthesized a new ceric salt for use in oxidation reactions.
Certain ceric solutions are powerful oxidizing agents in analytical chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cerous - relating to cerium with valence three (+3 oxidation state)

Etymology

From cerium, named after the asteroid Ceres, with the suffix -ic indicating a higher oxidation state.
