unoriginality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnəˌrɪdʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˌrɪdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking originality; not being new, inventive, or creative
lack of originality

Examples

Critics pointed out the unoriginality of the movie's plot and characters.
Her essay was marked down due to its unoriginality and reliance on clichés.
The unoriginality of the design disappointed the judges in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being original; inventiveness

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'original' + '-ity' (noun suffix).
