demodulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of extracting the original information signal from a modulated carrier wave, especially in radio or telecommunications
signal extraction from a carrier wave

Examples

The radio uses demodulation to recover audio signals from broadcast waves.
Accurate demodulation is essential for clear data transmission in digital communications.
Modern televisions rely on sophisticated demodulation techniques to display high-definition images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modulation - the process of combining a signal with a carrier wave

Etymology

From de- (reverse) + modulation, modeled after modulation.
