dysgenics

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdʒɛnɪks / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɛnɪks / (BrE)
The study of factors that contribute to the degeneration of the quality or genetic characteristics of a population or its offspring.
study of genetic degeneration in populations

Examples

The controversial field of dysgenics raises important ethical questions in scientific communities.
Some argue that certain social trends could unintentionally contribute to dysgenics within human populations.
Her research paper discussed historical theories related to dysgenics and their social implications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eugenics - the study of improving genetic quality of populations

Etymology

From dys- (bad) + -genics (relating to production), modeled after 'eugenics'.
