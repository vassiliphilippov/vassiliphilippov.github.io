ordered

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
arranged or organized according to a particular system, sequence, or rule; systematic.
systematically arranged

Examples

The books on her shelf were arranged in an ordered fashion, making everything easy to find.
In mathematics, an ordered pair consists of two elements arranged in a specific sequence.
He prefers an ordered workspace because it helps him concentrate.

Synset Examples

an ordered sequence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disordered - not arranged in an orderly or systematic way

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
arranged or organized in a neat, logical, or aesthetically consistent way; having an orderly structure or pattern
arranged or organized systematically

Examples

The files are kept in an ordered sequence by date.
She prefers an ordered environment to keep her mind calm and focused.
The architect praised the ordered design of the new city park.

Synset Examples

a coherent argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disordered - not arranged in an orderly way; chaotic
  • Synonym: organized - arranged in a systematic way

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinare' (to arrange, put in order) via Old French 'ordre'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
arranged or disposed according to a particular sequence or structure; organized or systematic.
systematically arranged or organized

Examples

The children sat in ordered rows, waiting for the assembly to begin.
Books were placed in ordered stacks on the library shelves.
The museum's ordered layout made it easy for visitors to navigate.

Synset Examples

the carefully arranged chessmen
haphazardly arranged interlobular septa
comfortable chairs arranged around the fireplace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disordered - not arranged in a regular or systematic way
  • Synonym: organized - arranged in a systematic way

Etymology

From the verb 'order,' which comes from Old French 'ordre,' from Latin 'ordo' (row, series, rank).
