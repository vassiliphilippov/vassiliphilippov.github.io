Clinopodium vulgare

1. NOUN

/ klɪn.əˈpoʊ.di.əm vʌlˈɡɛr.i / (AmE), / klɪn.əˈpəʊ.di.əm vʌlˈɡɪə.ri / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial herb with heads of small pink or whitish flowers, widely distributed in the United States, Europe, and Asia
aromatic herb with pink or whitish flowers

Examples

The leaves of Clinopodium vulgare can be used as a mild herbal tea.
Clinopodium vulgare thrives in meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
Gardeners enjoy planting Clinopodium vulgare for its attractive pink flower clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild basil - another common name for Clinopodium vulgare

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Clinopodium' from Greek 'klino' (bed) and 'pous' (foot), referring to the shape of the calyx; 'vulgare' means 'common' in Latin.
