firing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəɹɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of setting something on fire.
the act of setting something on fire

Examples

The firing of the forest was an act of deliberate destruction.
The villagers protested the firing of their homes by the invading army.
After the firing of the old barn, the smell of smoke lingered for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition - the act of starting to burn or catch fire
  • Antonym: extinguishing - the act of putting out a fire

Etymology

From the verb 'fire' + -ing, meaning to set on fire.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of discharging a gun or other firearm; shooting
the act of shooting a firearm

Examples

The sudden firing startled everyone at the range.
There was heavy firing during the battle last night.
The police responded quickly to reports of firing in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting - the act of firing a gun or other weapon
  • Antonym: ceasefire - an agreement to stop firing weapons

Etymology

From the verb 'fire', of Germanic origin, meaning 'to set on fire' or 'to shoot'.
