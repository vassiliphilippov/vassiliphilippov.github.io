spoiled

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a child) having a character or disposition that has been negatively affected by excessive pampering or indulgence.
pampered or overindulged (esp. child)

Examples

The parents' constant gifts made their son a truly spoiled child.
Many people believe that only children are more likely to be spoiled.
The teacher found it difficult to manage the spoiled students in her new class.

Synset Examples

a spoiled child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulged - (of a person) given too much of what they want, often in an unhealthy way
  • Antonym: disciplined - (of a person) having self-control and good habits from guidance and correction

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'spoil', from Old French 'espoillier', from Latin 'spoliare' (to strip, rob).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
(of food) no longer safe or fit to eat because it has decayed or gone bad.
no longer safe to eat (food)

Examples

He threw out the spoiled milk because it smelled sour.
Any spoiled vegetables should be removed from the refrigerator immediately.
The picnic was ruined when we discovered most of the food had become spoiled.

Synset Examples

bad meat
a refrigerator full of spoilt food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotten - (of food) decayed and no longer fit to eat
  • Antonym: fresh - not spoiled; recently made, harvested, or prepared
