partible

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːrtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪbəl / (BrE)
(of property) capable of being separated or divided into parts.
capable of being divided
legal

Examples

Under certain laws, the partible nature of an estate affects inheritance rights.
Community property in some regions is considered partible among heirs.
The court must decide if the assets are partible or must remain intact.

Synset Examples

a partible estate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indivisible - not able to be divided or separated

Etymology

From Latin 'partibilis', from 'partire' (to divide).
