executable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛksɪˌkjutəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪˌkjuːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be carried out or put into effect, given the available means or circumstances
able to be carried out; achievable

Examples

Under the current budget, the proposed plan is simply not executable.
We must find a solution that is executable within the given time frame.
The team presented an executable strategy for expanding into new markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • Antonym: impracticable - not able to be put into practice

Etymology

From Latin exsequi (to carry out) via execute + -able.
