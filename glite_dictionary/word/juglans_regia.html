Juglans regia

1. PROPN

/ dʒʌɡˈlænz ˈriːdʒiə / (AmE), / dʒʌɡˈlanz ˈriːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian walnut tree valued for its large edible nuts and hard, beautifully grained wood; widely cultivated.
species of walnut tree

Examples

The timber of Juglans regia is prized for making fine furniture and instruments.
Commercial orchards across Europe cultivate Juglans regia for its high-quality nuts.
Her research focused on improving the resistance of Juglans regia to common diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - a common name for the nut produced by Juglans regia

Etymology

From Latin: 'juglans' (walnut) and 'regia' (royal).
