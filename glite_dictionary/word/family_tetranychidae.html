family Tetranychidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈnɪkɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈnɪkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of plant-feeding mites, many of which are pests of crops and ornamental plants.
family of plant-feeding mites
medical

Examples

The family Tetranychidae includes many species commonly known as spider mites.
Agricultural experts have studied how members of the family Tetranychidae damage crops worldwide.
Some mites in the family Tetranychidae can develop resistance to pesticides over time.

Etymology

From modern Latin Tetranychidae, from the genus Tetranychus, based on Greek tetra- (four) and onykhis (claw).
