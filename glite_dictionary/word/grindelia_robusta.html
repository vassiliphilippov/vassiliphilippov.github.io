Grindelia robusta

1. NOUN

/ ɡraɪnˈdiːliə roʊˈbʌstə / (AmE), / ɡrɪnˈdiːliə rəʊˈbʌstə / (BrE)
A perennial species of gumweed native to California and Baja California, known for its sticky, resinous foliage.
a perennial gumweed plant species

Examples

Grindelia robusta is commonly found in coastal areas of California.
Traditional herbal remedies sometimes use extracts from Grindelia robusta.
Botanists studied the resinous properties of Grindelia robusta for potential medicinal applications.

Etymology

Named after David Hieronymus Grindel, a Latvian botanist; 'robusta' from Latin for 'strong' or 'robust'.
