misdeed

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈdid / (AmE), / mɪsˈdiːd / (BrE)
a bad or morally wrong action
bad or immoral action

Examples

The judge forgave the boy's misdeed after he apologized.
She regretted her misdeed and promised never to repeat it.
Even a small misdeed can hurt someone's feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an illegal act or wrongdoing
  • Synonym: crime - an action that is against the law
  • Antonym: good deed - an action that is morally right or beneficial

Etymology

From Old English 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'deed' (action).
