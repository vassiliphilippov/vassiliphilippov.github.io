Charles Gounod

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrl guːˈnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːrl güːˈnəʊ / (BrE)
(1818–1893) a French composer best known for his operas, especially 'Faust' and other classical works.
French composer of operas (1818–1893)

Examples

Charles Gounod composed the opera 'Faust', which remains popular in theaters today.
Music students often study the works of Charles Gounod to understand Romantic-era composition.
The church choir performed a piece by Charles Gounod during the concert.
