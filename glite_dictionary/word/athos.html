Athos

1. PROPN

/ ˈæθoʊs / (AmE), / ˈæθɒs / (BrE)
an autonomous peninsula in northeastern Greece, known for its historic Greek Orthodox monasteries and religious significance
monastic peninsula in Greece

Examples

The monks of Athos have preserved their traditions for centuries.
Pilgrims travel from all over the world to visit Athos and its monasteries.
Athos is considered one of the most important spiritual centers of the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Etymology

From Greek Áthos (Ἄθως), the name of the mountain and region.
