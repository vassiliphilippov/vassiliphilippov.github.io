Phallales

1. NOUN

/ fæˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / fæˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi that includes the stinkhorns and related species, characterized by a slimy, foul-smelling mature fruiting body
an order of fungi, including stinkhorns

Examples

The Phallales are well known for their distinctive, foul odor that attracts insects for spore dispersal.
Some members of the Phallales can be found growing in woodlands after rain.
Mycologists have long debated the classification of various stinkhorns within the Phallales.

Etymology

From Greek 'phallos' (phallus) referring to the shape of some species, and the taxonomic suffix '-ales' (order).
