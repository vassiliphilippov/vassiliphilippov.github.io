Orthilia

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɪliə / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of small, rhizomatous, evergreen perennial herbs that grow in damp coniferous woodlands of northern temperate regions.
a genus of evergreen woodland herbs

Examples

The rare Orthilia was discovered in the shaded areas of the forest.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of Orthilia in northern woodlands.
A patch of Orthilia thrived near the damp coniferous trees.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'orthos' (straight), referring to the plant's upright stalks.

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɪliə / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby perennial evergreen herbs with rhizomatous roots, found in damp coniferous woodlands of northern temperate regions.
a genus of northern perennial evergreen herbs

Examples

Orthilia is a genus of evergreen herbs native to cool, damp woodlands in northern climates.
Botanists recorded several specimens of Orthilia in the shaded undergrowth of the coniferous forest.
The unique rhizomatous roots help Orthilia thrive in moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintergreen - a common name for some species in the genus Orthilia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Orthilia', the genus name, probably derived from Greek 'orthos' (straight) with a botanical suffix.
