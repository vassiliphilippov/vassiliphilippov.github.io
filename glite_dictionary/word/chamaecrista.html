Chamaecrista

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪmiːˈkrɪstə / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈkrɪstə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical herbs or subshrubs with sensitive leaves and seed pods that burst open suddenly, some species formerly classified as Cassia
genus of tropical plants with sensitive leaves

Examples

Several species within the genus Chamaecrista are known for their sensitive, touch-responsive leaves.
Botanists recently reclassified some members of Chamaecrista that were formerly placed in the genus Cassia.
The explosive pods of Chamaecrista help the plant disperse its seeds widely in tropical habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia - a related genus of flowering plants

Etymology

From Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) + 'kristos' (divided, perhaps referring to divided leaves).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæmiˈkrɪstə / (AmE), / ˌkæmiˈkrɪstə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs or subshrubs in the legume family, noted for sensitive leaves and pods that split open suddenly; some species were formerly placed in the genus Cassia.
genus of tropical legumes with sensitive leaves

Examples

The botanist collected samples of Chamaecrista during her fieldwork in the Amazon rainforest.
Many species in the Chamaecrista genus are known for their sensitive, touch-reactive leaves.
Some Chamaecrista plants were previously classified under the genus Cassia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia - a different but closely related genus of legumes

Etymology

From Greek khamai (on the ground) + kristos (divided, separated).
