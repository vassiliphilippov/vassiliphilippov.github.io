tempering

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of strengthening or hardening a material, especially metal or glass, by heating and then cooling it
heat treatment for hardening material

Examples

The tempering of steel gives it the necessary toughness for construction use.
Glass used in car windows undergoes tempering to increase its strength and safety.
Proper tempering is essential to ensure the durability of metal tools.

Etymology

From Latin 'temperare' (to moderate, mix properly), via Old French 'temprer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something less severe or extreme; moderation.
moderation or moderation process

Examples

The tempering of criticism with empathy can foster better communication.
Her tempering influence prevented the meeting from becoming confrontational.
Through careful tempering, the harsh policy was made more acceptable to the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the process of making something less extreme
  • Antonym: intensification - the process of making something stronger or more severe

Etymology

From the verb 'temper', from Latin temperare (to moderate, blend, restrain).
