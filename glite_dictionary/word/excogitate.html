to excogitate

1. VERB

/ ɛkˈskɑdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈskɒdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think out or devise (an idea, plan, explanation, etc.) through careful mental effort
devise or think out through mental effort

Examples

It took the researchers weeks to excogitate a solution to the complex problem.
She managed to excogitate a novel approach to the design.
He spent all night excogitating ways to improve the team's performance.

Synset Examples

excogitate a way to measure the speed of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: to invent - to create or design something that has not existed before

Etymology

From Latin excogitatus, past participle of excogitare (to think out).

2. VERB

/ ɛkˈskɑːdʒɪteɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈskɒdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
To think about or reflect on something very deeply or thoroughly; to consider carefully.
reflect or think deeply

Examples

The committee was urged to excogitate on the long-term consequences before making a decision.
She likes to excogitate on philosophical issues late into the night.
After hearing the proposal, he chose to excogitate privately before responding.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to speculate - to form a theory about something without firm evidence
  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think deeply about something

Etymology

From Latin excogitare (to think out), from ex- (out) + cogitare (to think).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00632042-v
Synset POS: v
