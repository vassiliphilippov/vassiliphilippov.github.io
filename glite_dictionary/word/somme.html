Somme

1. PROPN

/ sɑm / (AmE), / sɒm / (BrE)
a major battle of World War I, fought in 1916 along the Somme River in northern France, notable for its heavy casualties and limited territorial gains
World War I battle in 1916 in France

Examples

The Battle of the Somme is remembered as one of the bloodiest conflicts in military history.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the Somme offensive in 1916.
Historians still debate the strategic impact of the Somme on the outcome of World War I.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Verdun - major battle in World War I in France, 1916
  • Synonym: Ypres - site of several significant World War I battles in Belgium

Etymology

Named after the Somme River in northern France, where the battle took place.
