lepidomelane

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈdɑməˌleɪn / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈdɒmɪleɪn / (BrE)
a black, iron-rich variety of biotite mica mineral found in igneous rocks
an iron-rich mica mineral

Examples

The dark lepidomelane crystals were easily identified under the microscope.
Granites rich in lepidomelane tend to have a darker hue than those containing other micas.
Geologists discovered a vein of lepidomelane in the volcanic rock sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotite - a common dark mica mineral

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'melas' (black), referring to its scaly appearance and dark color.
