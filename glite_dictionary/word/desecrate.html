to desecrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪt / (BrE)
to treat (a sacred place or object) with violent disrespect or to remove its sacred status
to violate the sacredness of something

Examples

The vandals desecrated the ancient temple, leaving graffiti on its walls.
It is a crime to desecrate a cemetery, regardless of the intent.
The invading soldiers desecrated the holy site by looting its relics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - to treat something sacred with irreverence or disrespect
  • Antonym: consecrate - to declare something as sacred or dedicate formally to a divine purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'desecratus', past participle of 'desecrāre' (to make unholy), from 'de-' (away from) and 'sacer' (sacred).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪkreɪt / (BrE)
to damage or show disrespect for something considered sacred or holy, especially a place or object
to violate something sacred

Examples

Vandals attempted to desecrate the ancient temple with graffiti.
It is a crime to desecrate graves in the cemetery.
Some consider it offensive to desecrate sacred texts by treating them carelessly.

Synset Examples

desecrate a cemetery
violate the sanctity of the church
profane the name of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - to treat something sacred with irreverence
  • Antonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (do the opposite of) + 'sacrāre' (to make holy), from 'sacer' (sacred).
