housepaint

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌpeɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌpeɪnt / (BrE)
Paint specially formulated for use on the exterior surfaces of a house, typically to protect and decorate woodwork.
paint for a house's exterior surfaces

Examples

We spent the weekend applying a fresh coat of housepaint to the front porch.
The old housepaint was peeling, so we had to scrape it off before repainting.
This brand of housepaint is weather-resistant and designed to last for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enamel - hard, glossy paint used for surfaces
  • Synonym: varnish - protective finish for wood, often transparent

Etymology

From 'house' + 'paint', referring to paint used on houses.
