kapok

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒk / (BrE)
A silky fiber from the seed pods of the kapok tree, used for stuffing mattresses, pillows, and for insulation.
plant fiber used for stuffing

Examples

The old chair was stuffed with kapok instead of modern foam.
Pillows filled with kapok are known for being light and comfortable.
After harvesting, the kapok fibers are cleaned and sorted for use in bedding.

Etymology

From Malay 'kapuk', meaning the kapok tree or its fiber.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒk / (BrE)
A large tropical tree (Ceiba pentandra) with a thick, deeply ridged trunk, producing pods containing seeds covered in silky fibers used as a stuffing material.
tropical tree yielding silky fiber

Examples

The kapok tree can grow over 200 feet tall in some rainforests.
Traditional life vests were once filled with kapok fibers because of their buoyancy.
Many pillows are stuffed with kapok for a natural and soft filling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk-cotton tree - another name for the kapok tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'capoc', from Malay 'kapuk' (the kapok tree or its fiber).
