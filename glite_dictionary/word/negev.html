Negev

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛɡɛv / (AmE), / ˈneɡɛv / (BrE)
a large desert region in southern Israel, known for its arid climate and sparse population
desert region in southern Israel

Examples

The Negev receives very little rainfall throughout the year.
Many Bedouin communities live in the Negev region of Israel.
Scientists study unique desert plants that thrive in the Negev.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Negev', meaning 'south' or 'dry'.
