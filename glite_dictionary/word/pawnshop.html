pawnshop

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔnˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnʃɒp / (BrE)
A shop where people can obtain loans by leaving personal possessions as security for the money borrowed.
shop lending money for pledged items

Examples

She took her grandmother's necklace to the pawnshop to get a loan.
The pawnshop on Main Street is always busy with customers.
He retrieved his guitar from the pawnshop after paying back the loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawnbroker - a person who lends money at a pawnshop

Etymology

From 'pawn' (to pledge), and 'shop', referring to a place where business is conducted.
