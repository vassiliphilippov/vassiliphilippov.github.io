Jean-Paul Sartre

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn ˌpɔl ˈsɑrt(rə) / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ ˈpɔːl ˈsɑːtr(ə) / (BrE)
(1905–1980) French writer and existentialist philosopher, known for his works on existentialism and his influence on 20th-century philosophy.
French existentialist writer and philosopher

Examples

Jean-Paul Sartre was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1964, but he declined the honor.
Many students study Jean-Paul Sartre's works to understand existential philosophy.
The impact of Jean-Paul Sartre can still be seen in modern literary and philosophical debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existentialism - a philosophical theory emphasizing individual freedom and choice
