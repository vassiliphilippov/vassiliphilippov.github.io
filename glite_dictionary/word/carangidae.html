Carangidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrændʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəˈraŋɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of narrow-bodied marine food fishes with deeply forked tails, chiefly found in warm seas; includes jacks, pompanos, and related species
family of marine food fishes (jacks, pompanos)

Examples

Many species in the Carangidae family are highly valued by commercial and recreational fishermen.
The silver trevally, a member of the Carangidae, is common in tropical waters.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Carangidae family off the coast of Indonesia.

Etymology

From modern Latin Carangidae, based on the type genus Caranx.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrændʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəˈrandʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of narrow-bodied marine food fishes with widely forked tails, chiefly found in warm seas
family of narrow-bodied marine fishes

Examples

The Carangidae family includes well-known species such as jacks, trevallies, and pompanos.
Fishermen in tropical waters often target members of the Carangidae for their firm, tasty flesh.
Many aquariums display species from the Carangidae family because of their distinctive appearance and swimming style.

Etymology

From New Latin Carangidae, based on the type genus Caranx.
