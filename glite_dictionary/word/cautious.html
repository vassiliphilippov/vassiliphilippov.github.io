cautious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəs / (BrE)
Careful to avoid excess or risk; taking actions in a restrained or moderate way.
avoiding excess or risk; restrained

Examples

He gave a cautious estimate of the project's cost to avoid any surprises later.
Her cautious approach made sure the team never overpromised what they could deliver.
The scientist used cautious language when presenting the uncertain results.

Synset Examples

a conservative estimate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favouring moderation, not extreme; especially in estimates or actions

Etymology

From Latin 'cautiosus', from 'cautus' (past participle of 'cavere', to take care).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəs / (BrE)
Showing care and attention to avoid danger or mistakes; careful and deliberate.
careful; avoiding risks

Examples

She was always cautious when crossing the busy street.
Investors remain cautious about putting money into unfamiliar markets.
His cautious approach helped the company avoid costly mistakes.

Synset Examples

reserved and cautious; never making swift decisions
a cautious driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - taking care to avoid mistakes or danger
  • Antonym: reckless - without thinking about danger or consequences

Etymology

From Latin 'cautiosus', from 'cautus' (careful), past participle of 'cavere' (to take care).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00326179-a
Synset POS: a
