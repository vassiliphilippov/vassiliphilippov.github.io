khan

1. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɑːn / (BrE)
a title given to rulers or important people, especially in Central Asia, Afghanistan, and certain other Asian countries
Asian noble or ruler's title

Examples

Genghis khan founded one of the largest empires in history.
In some regions, the local khan still holds considerable influence over the community.
The ancient tomb was believed to be that of a powerful khan.

Etymology

From Turkic and Mongolic *khan* (ruler or lord). Entered English via Persian and Arabic.

2. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɑːn / (BrE)
(in some eastern countries) an inn with a large courtyard, providing accommodation for caravans and travelers.
inn with courtyard for caravans

Examples

The weary travelers decided to rest at a khan along the dusty road.
Merchants and their camels filled the khan every evening during the trading season.
The ancient khan stood at the heart of the bustling bazaar, offering shelter to all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravanserai - an inn with a central courtyard for travellers in Asia or North Africa

Etymology

From Persian 'khān' (house, inn).
