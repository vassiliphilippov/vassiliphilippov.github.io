plumbism

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌm.bɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈplʌm.bɪzəm / (BrE)
A toxic condition caused by the absorption of too much lead into the body.
lead poisoning (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the worker with plumbism after years of exposure to industrial lead.
Chronic plumbism can result in neurological damage and other serious health issues.
Strict regulations were introduced to prevent plumbism among children living in old buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead poisoning - illness caused by exposure to lead

Etymology

From Latin plumbum (lead) + -ism (condition).
