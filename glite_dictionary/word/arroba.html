arroba

1. NOUN

/ əˈroʊbə / (AmE), / əˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
A liquid measure used in some Spanish-speaking countries, with varying values depending on the region.
a liquid measure (Spanish-speaking countries)

Examples

In colonial times, an arroba of wine was a common unit of purchase.
The recipe called for an arroba of olive oil, which is much more than a liter.
Farmers in Spain once measured their grape harvests in arrobas.

Etymology

From Spanish 'arroba', from Arabic 'ar-robʿ' (the quarter).

2. NOUN

/ əˈroʊbə / (AmE), / əˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in Spain and some Spanish-speaking countries, traditionally equal to about 25 pounds (11.5 kilograms) but varying locally.
unit of weight in Spanish-speaking countries

Examples

The shipment weighed over fifty arrobas, making it difficult to transport by hand.
Traditionally, an arroba was used to measure both liquids and dry goods in Spain.
Local farmers still price their olives by the arroba at the regional market.

Etymology

From Spanish 'arroba', from Arabic 'ar-rubʿ' (the quarter).
