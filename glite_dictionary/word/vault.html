vault

1. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
a strong room or compartment, often made of steel, used for storing valuable items securely
secure room for valuables

Examples

The bank keeps gold bars in a high-security vault deep underground.
Access to the company’s vault is restricted to authorized personnel only.
They discovered ancient coins locked away in the old family vault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe - a strong container for storing valuables securely

Etymology

From Latin 'volta' (arched structure), via Old French 'voute'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04531126-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
an act of leaping or jumping over something, especially using the hands or a pole for support
act of jumping over something

Examples

The gymnast's vault over the horse was flawless and impressive.
He cleared the fence with a single vault during the escape.
Her record-breaking vault secured her first place in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - a forceful jump or quick movement
  • Synonym: dive - a jump or plunge headfirst into something

Etymology

From Old French volter, from Latin volvitare, frequentative of volvere 'to roll, turn'.

3. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
an underground room or chamber used for burial, typically constructed to house the remains of several people
underground burial chamber

Examples

The family was interred in a stone vault beneath the old cathedral.
After centuries, the royal vault remains untouched by grave robbers.
They discovered ancient treasures hidden in the burial vault during the excavation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crypt - an underground room or vault beneath a church, used as a chapel or burial place

Etymology

From Latin 'volta' meaning an arched structure.

4. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
an arched structure of brick, stone, or concrete forming a ceiling or roof
arched ceiling or roof

Examples

The cathedral's central vault was decorated with intricate frescoes.
The ancient crypt was protected by a thick stone vault.
Builders carefully restored the collapsed vault of the medieval chapel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dome - a rounded vault forming the roof of a building or structure, typically with a circular base
  • Synonym: arch - a curved symmetrical structure spanning an opening and typically supporting the weight of a bridge, roof, or wall above it

Etymology

From Latin 'voluta' (a scroll), via Old French 'voute', relating to something rolled or arched.

5. VERB

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
to jump across or leap over something, especially by using the hands or a pole for support
jump or leap over an obstacle

Examples

The athlete managed to vault over the high bar with ease.
She vaulted the fence in a single, graceful motion.
He gripped the pole tightly and vaulted across the wide stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump a long way, high, or in a great manner
  • Synonym: jump - to spring off the ground or other base by a muscular effort of the legs and feet

Etymology

From Old French *vauter* (to leap), from Latin *volvitare*, frequentative of *volvere* (to turn, roll).

6. VERB

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
to jump or leap vigorously, especially by using the hands or a pole for support
to leap energetically (often with support)

Examples

He managed to vault over the fence in one swift motion.
The athlete vaulted across the stream with the help of a sturdy pole.
Children vault from rock to rock, laughing as they cross the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump high or a long distance
  • Synonym: jump - to push oneself off a surface and into the air

Etymology

From Old French 'volter' (to turn, leap), from Italian 'voltare' (to turn), from Latin 'volvitare', frequentative of 'volvere' (to roll, turn).
