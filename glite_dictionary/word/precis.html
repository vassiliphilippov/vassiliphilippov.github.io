to precis

1. VERB

/ preɪˈsiː / (AmE), / ˈpreɪsiː / (BrE)
to make a summary of (a text, speech, or information)
make a summary of

Examples

Could you precis this report for the meeting?
Students were asked to precis the article in fifty words or less.
She skillfully precised the main points of the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - give a brief statement of the main points of
  • Synonym: to abridge - shorten (a text) without losing its essence

Etymology

From French 'précis' (precise, condensed), from Latin 'praecisus' (cut off, brief).
