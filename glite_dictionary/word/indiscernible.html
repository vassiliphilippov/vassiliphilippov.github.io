indiscernible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsɜːnəbəl / (BrE)
impossible or very difficult to see, hear, or notice clearly; barely perceptible.
impossible or hard to perceive

Examples

The difference between the two colors was almost indiscernible in the dim light.
His voice was so soft it was indiscernible at the back of the room.
Time seemed to pass with indiscernible changes in the quiet countryside.

Synset Examples

the transition was almost indiscernible
an almost insensible change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperceptible - impossible to perceive
  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear

Etymology

From Latin 'indiscernibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'discernibilis' (able to be distinguished).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈsɝː.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈsɜː.nə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible or very difficult to see, hear, or distinguish clearly
impossible to perceive or distinguish

Examples

The path was covered by an indiscernible layer of dust after the storm.
His voice was so quiet it became almost indiscernible in the noisy room.
There was an indiscernible shift in her attitude during the discussion.

Synset Examples

an indiscernible increase in temperature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperceptible - impossible or very difficult to notice or sense
  • Antonym: discernible - able to be perceived or distinguished

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + discernibilis (able to be distinguished).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00584515-a
Synset POS: a
