antitumor

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈtuː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈtjuː.mə / (BrE)
acting against or preventing the growth of tumors, especially cancerous ones
preventing or fighting tumor growth
medical

Examples

The new drug showed significant antitumor activity in clinical trials.
Researchers are developing therapies with improved antitumor effects for patients.
This medication is known for its strong antitumor properties in laboratory studies.

Synset Examples

anticancer drug
an antineoplastic effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticancer - acting against cancer or cancerous cells
  • Synonym: antineoplastic - inhibiting or preventing the growth and spread of tumors

Etymology

From prefix 'anti-' (against) and 'tumor' (swelling, especially an abnormal mass), ultimately from Latin 'tumor'.
