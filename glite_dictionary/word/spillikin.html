spillikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪlɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɪlɪkɪn / (BrE)
A thin strip of wood or straw used in playing the game of jackstraws.
thin stick used in jackstraws

Examples

The children scattered the spillikins across the table to begin their game.
She carefully picked up a spillikin without disturbing the others.
A set of antique spillikins was found in the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackstraw - a thin stick used in the game of jackstraws (pickup sticks)

Etymology

From dialectal English 'spill', meaning a splinter or thin piece of wood, with the diminutive suffix '-kin'.
