infection

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
(Phonetics) The alteration of a speech sound due to the influence of a neighboring sound.
sound alteration by neighboring sound (phonetics)

Examples

In phonetics, infection can cause a vowel to change its sound depending on surrounding consonants.
The process of infection often leads to subtle variations in spoken language.
Linguists study infection to understand historical shifts in word pronunciation.

Etymology

From Latin 'infectio' meaning 'a dyeing, staining, infection,' from 'inficere' (to dye, stain, corrupt).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
A disease or condition caused by the invasion and multiplication of harmful microorganisms in the body.
illness caused by harmful microorganisms

Examples

The doctor prescribed antibiotics to treat the infection in her lungs.
Hand washing is important to prevent the spread of infection.
After the surgery, he developed a serious infection that delayed his recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagion - the process of disease transmission by direct or indirect contact
  • Antonym: immunity - resistance to a particular infection or toxin

Etymology

From Latin 'infectio', from 'inficere' (to taint, infect).
