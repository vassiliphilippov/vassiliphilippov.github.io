phylogenetically

1. ADV

/ ˌfaɪloʊdʒəˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪlɒdʒɪˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the evolutionary development and history of a species or group of organisms
in terms of evolutionary history

Examples

The trait has evolved phylogenetically independently in several lineages.
Some instincts are best understood when considered phylogenetically.
Crocodiles and birds are phylogenetically related, sharing a common ancestor.

Synset Examples

a phylogenetically primitive part of the brain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylogeny - the evolutionary history or development of a species or group

Etymology

Formed from phylogeny (from Greek 'phylon' meaning 'race, tribe' and 'geneia' meaning 'origin') plus -ically.
