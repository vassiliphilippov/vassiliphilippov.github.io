nor'-west

1. NOUN

/ nɔrˈwɛst / (AmE), / nɔːˈwɛst / (BrE)
the compass point that is halfway between north and west, corresponding to 315 degrees
compass point between north and west (315°)

Examples

The wind was blowing steadily from the nor'-west all afternoon.
He set his course towards the nor'-west to reach the island before dusk.
Sailors watched as dark clouds gathered in the nor'-west, warning of a coming storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northwest - the compass point midway between north and west

Etymology

Contraction of 'north-west', using 'nor'' for 'north'.

2. ADV

/ nɔɹˈwɛst / (AmE), / nɔːˈwɛst / (BrE)
To, toward, or in the northwest direction.
to or toward the northwest

Examples

The wind shifted nor'-west in the early afternoon.
They sailed nor'-west to avoid the storm.
The hikers turned nor'-west following the old compass trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northwest - To, toward, or in the northwest direction

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'north-west', commonly used in nautical and some regional speaking.
