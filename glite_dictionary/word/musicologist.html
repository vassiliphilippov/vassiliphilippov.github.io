musicologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːzɪˈkɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪˈkɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in musicology, the scholarly analysis and research-based study of music
expert in the study of musicology

Examples

As a musicologist, she has published several articles on Baroque composers.
The musicologist gave a fascinating lecture on the evolution of jazz.
Many musicologists spend years researching the history of folk music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musicology - the scholarly study of music

Etymology

From musicology, modeled after terms like biologist; musicology itself from French musicologie, from musique (music) + -logie (-logy).
