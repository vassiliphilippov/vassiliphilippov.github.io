autopsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.tɑp.si / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tɒp.si / (BrE)
An examination of a dead body to determine the cause of death or the extent of disease.
examination of a dead body
medical

Examples

The coroner performed an autopsy to determine the exact cause of death.
Results from the autopsy revealed signs of a rare disease.
After the autopsy, the family was informed of the findings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postmortem - an examination of a body after death

Etymology

From Greek autopsia, meaning 'seeing with one’s own eyes.'

2. VERB

/ ˈɔː.tɑp.si / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tɒp.si / (BrE)
to perform a post-mortem examination on a dead body to determine the cause of death or study effects of disease
to examine a dead body post-mortem

Examples

The medical examiner will autopsy the victim to establish the cause of death.
Do you know who will autopsy the body after the family gives consent?
After the mysterious case, the coroner autopsied three individuals from the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postmortem - to examine a body after death to determine cause

Etymology

From Greek autopsia, meaning 'seeing with one's own eyes'; auto- (self) + opsis (sight).
