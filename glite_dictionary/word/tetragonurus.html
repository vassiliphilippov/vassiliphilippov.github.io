Tetragonurus

1. NOUN

/ tɛtˌræɡəˈnʊrəs / (AmE), / tɛtˌræɡəˈnjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of deep-sea fish in the family Stromateidae, commonly known as squaretails.
genus of deep-sea fish (squaretails)

Examples

The Tetragonurus is known for its distinctive square-shaped tail.
Marine biologists study the distribution of Tetragonurus species in tropical waters.
Specimens of Tetragonurus are sometimes found in deep-sea trawling nets.

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning 'four' and 'gonu' meaning 'knee' (angle, corner), referring to the shape of the tail.
