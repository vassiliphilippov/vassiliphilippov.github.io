apologetically

1. ADV

/ əˌpɑːlədʒɛtɪkli / (AmE), / əˌpɒlədʒɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses an apology or regret.
in an apologetic or regretful manner

Examples

She smiled apologetically after interrupting the meeting.
He explained apologetically that he had forgotten her birthday.
The waiter apologetically informed us that our order would be late.

Synset Examples

he spoke apologetically about his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apologetic - showing or expressing an apology or regret
  • Antonym: defiantly - in a manner that shows open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From 'apologetic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'apologia' (a speech in defense).
