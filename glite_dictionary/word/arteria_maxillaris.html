arteria maxillaris

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə mæksɪˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə mæk.sɪˈleərɪs / (BrE)
Either of two arteries that branch from the external carotid artery and supply structures of the face.
artery of the face branching from external carotid
medical

Examples

The arteria maxillaris plays an important role in providing blood to the teeth and jaw.
During surgery, careful management of the arteria maxillaris is essential to avoid excessive bleeding.
Damage to the arteria maxillaris can result in significant facial hematoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxillary artery - either of two arteries supplying deep structures of the face; an English synonym

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) and 'maxillaris' (of the jaw).
