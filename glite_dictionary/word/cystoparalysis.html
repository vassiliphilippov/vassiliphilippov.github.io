cystoparalysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪstoʊpəˈræləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəʊpəˈralɪsɪs / (BrE)
paralysis of the urinary bladder, resulting in loss of bladder function.
paralysis of the urinary bladder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with cystoparalysis following spinal cord injury.
Chronic cystoparalysis can lead to complications such as urinary retention.
Treatment for cystoparalysis may involve catheterization or surgical intervention.

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) + 'paralysis'.
