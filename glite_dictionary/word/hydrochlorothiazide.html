hydrochlorothiazide

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑːkləˌθaɪəˌzaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒkləʊˌθaɪəˌzaɪd / (BrE)
a diuretic drug used to treat high blood pressure and fluid retention
a diuretic used for hypertension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydrochlorothiazide to help control his blood pressure.
Patients taking hydrochlorothiazide should have their potassium levels monitored regularly.
Hydrochlorothiazide is commonly used in combination with other medications to treat hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diuretic - a substance that promotes urine production
  • Synonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to lower high blood pressure

Etymology

From hydro- + chloro- + thiazide, indicating chemical structure.
