Hypertensin

1. NOUN

/ haɪpərˈtɛnsɪn / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈtɛnsɪn / (BrE)
a trademarked vasoconstrictor drug containing substances such as angiotensin II that cause blood vessels to narrow
vasoconstrictor drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor administered Hypertensin to quickly raise the patient's blood pressure during the procedure.
Hypertensin is no longer as commonly used due to the availability of safer alternatives.
Some patients responded to Hypertensin with a significant increase in blood pressure.

Etymology

Formed from 'hyper-' (over, excessive) and 'tensin' (from 'tension'); used as a trade name for vasoconstrictors.
