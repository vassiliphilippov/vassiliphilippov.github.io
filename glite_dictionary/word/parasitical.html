parasitical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, caused by, or characteristic of parasites.
relating to parasites

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the illness was of parasitical origin.
Tropical regions often face challenges with parasitical diseases like malaria.
The researchers studied the impact of parasitical worms on livestock health.

Synset Examples

parasitic infection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic - relating to, or caused by parasites
  • Antonym: symbiotic - involving interaction between two different organisms living in close physical association

Etymology

From Greek parasitikos (serving as a parasite) via Latin parasiticus.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or plants) having the qualities or habits of a parasite; living at the expense of others without giving anything in return.
having the qualities of a parasite

Examples

The company was criticized for its parasitical dependence on government subsidies.
Some vines display parasitical behavior by extracting nutrients from host trees.
He lived a parasitical lifestyle, relying entirely on the generosity of friends.

Synset Examples

a wealthy class parasitic upon the labor of the masses
parasitic vines that strangle the trees
bloodsucking blackmailer
his indolent leechlike existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic - having the nature of a parasite; drawing sustenance from another
  • Antonym: self-sufficient - able to provide for oneself without needing help

Etymology

From Greek parasitikos (relating to a parasite) via Latin parasiticus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00730167-s
Synset POS: s
