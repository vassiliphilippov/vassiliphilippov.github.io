cloudburst

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdbɝst / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdbɜːst / (BrE)
a sudden, very heavy rainfall, often of short duration
sudden heavy rainfall

Examples

The sudden cloudburst flooded the streets within minutes.
A cloudburst interrupted the outdoor concert, sending everyone running for cover.
Last night, a cloudburst soaked the farmland, saving the crops from drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a heavy and sudden rain

Etymology

From 'cloud' + 'burst', first recorded in 1817.
