entozoan

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛntəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
(of an organism) living within a living animal, usually as a parasite.
living inside a living animal (as a parasite)
medical

Examples

Certain entozoan parasites can cause diseases in their animal hosts.
The presence of entozoan worms was confirmed during the autopsy.
Veterinarians study entozoan infections to prevent outbreaks in livestock.

Synset Examples

entozoic worms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ectozoan - an organism living on the outside of an animal

Etymology

From Greek 'ento-' (within) + 'zōion' (animal).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛntəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌɛntəʊˈzəʊən / (BrE)
relating to entozoa; pertaining to or characteristic of internal animal parasites.
relating to internal animal parasites
medical

Examples

The researchers noted an entozoan infection in several fish populations.
An entozoan parasite may cause disease by damaging the host's internal organs.
Certain flatworms are classified as entozoan, living inside animal bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic - living as a parasite in or on another organism
  • Antonym: ectozoan - relating to organisms living on the surface of animals

Etymology

From Greek ento- (within) + zōon (animal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03075481-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
any of various parasites that live inside the internal organs of animals, especially intestinal worms
internal animal parasite; intestinal worm
medical

Examples

The veterinarian discovered a dangerous entozoan infecting the dog's intestines.
Certain entozoa can cause severe digestive problems in livestock.
Medical students studied the life cycle of an intestinal entozoan under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoparasite - a parasite living inside the body of its host
  • Antonym: ectozoan - a parasite living on the surface of a host

Etymology

From Greek ento- (within) + zōion (animal).
