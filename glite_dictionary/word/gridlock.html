gridlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪdˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdlɒk / (BrE)
A situation in which very severe traffic congestion blocks all movement in every direction, making progress impossible.
complete standstill of traffic

Examples

After the accident on the main road, the city center was in complete gridlock for hours.
Morning commuters were frustrated by gridlock that stretched for several miles.
Emergency vehicles struggled to get through the gridlock caused by the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traffic jam - a situation in which vehicles are unable to move due to congestion

Etymology

Originally U.S., from 'grid' + 'lock', after the pattern of blocked intersections forming a grid in cities.
