constancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.stən.si / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stən.si / (BrE)
Faithfulness and dependability in personal relationships, especially as shown by remaining loyal or true.
faithfulness; dependability in relationships

Examples

Her constancy throughout the long years apart made their reunion so much sweeter.
Despite many challenges, his constancy in their marriage never wavered.
The story celebrates the constancy of lovers who stay true to each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful or loyal
  • Synonym: fidelity - loyalty and faithfulness, especially in relationships
  • Antonym: infidelity - the action or state of being unfaithful to a partner

Etymology

From Latin constantia (steadfastness), from constans, constant- (standing firm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnstənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being enduring and unchanging; stability or steadfastness over time
quality of being unchanging or stable

Examples

Her constancy in the face of adversity inspired everyone around her.
The constancy of the laws of physics allows us to predict natural phenomena with confidence.
Despite many challenges, his constancy to his values never wavered.

Synset Examples

early mariners relied on the constancy of the trade winds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - the state of being stable and not likely to change
  • Antonym: changeability - the quality of being likely to change or vary

Etymology

From Latin 'constantia' (steadfastness), from 'constans' (standing firm).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.stən.si / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stən.si / (BrE)
(psychology) The tendency for perceived objects to appear unchanged despite variations in observation conditions, such as changes in lighting, distance, or viewpoint.
perceptual consistency despite changes
medical

Examples

Perceptual constancy allows us to recognize familiar faces even under different lighting conditions.
Children develop constancy as they learn that objects maintain their properties despite perspective changes.
The study investigated constancy of color perception in visually complex environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptual constancy - the psychological phenomenon of objects maintaining consistent appearance

Etymology

From Latin constantia, from constans (standing firm).
