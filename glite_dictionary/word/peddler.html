peddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛd.lə / (BrE)
a person who sells illegal drugs, especially without a license or in secret
illegal drug dealer

Examples

The police arrested a well-known peddler during last night's raid downtown.
Many neighborhoods struggle with the presence of peddlers who sell drugs near schools.
Authorities launched a new campaign to discourage youth from becoming peddlers of illegal substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dealer - a person who buys and sells goods, especially as a business; in this case, one who sells illegal drugs

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛdlə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place selling goods, typically in the streets or at markets
itinerant street vendor

Examples

The peddler wandered through the village, offering colorful scarves and trinkets to passersby.
Street peddlers often gather at the festival, selling food, toys, and souvenirs.
A peddler knocked on our door yesterday, hoping to sell cleaning supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vendor - a person or company offering something for sale
  • Synonym: hawker - a person who sells goods in the street or door-to-door
  • Antonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services

Etymology

From Middle English *pedlere*, probably from *ped* (a basket), related to carrying goods for sale.
