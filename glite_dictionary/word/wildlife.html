wildlife

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldlaɪf / (BrE)
animals, birds, and other living things that are not domesticated, especially those living in their natural environment.
undomesticated animals and living things

Examples

The reserve is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including rare birds and large mammals.
Protecting wildlife habitats helps preserve the balance of nature.
Tourists can observe wildlife in its natural habitat during the safari tour.

Synset Examples

chemicals could kill all the wildlife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauna - the animals of a particular region, habitat, or geological period

Etymology

Formed from 'wild' + 'life', first used in this sense in early 20th century.
