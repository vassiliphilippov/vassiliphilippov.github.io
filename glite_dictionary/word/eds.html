EDS

1. NOUN

/ i di ˈɛs / (AmE), / iː dɪ ˈɛs / (BrE)
an automated system that detects plastic explosives in luggage using X-ray technology and computers, commonly used in airports for security screening
system for detecting explosives in airport luggage

Examples

The airport recently installed a new EDS to improve passenger safety and security.
Baggage is automatically routed through the EDS before being loaded onto an aircraft.
Technicians regularly maintain the EDS to ensure it operates accurately.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'explosives detection system'.
