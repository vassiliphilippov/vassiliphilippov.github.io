antithyroid

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈθaɪ.rɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈθaɪ.rɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) counteracting or inhibiting the action of the thyroid gland or excessive thyroid activity.
counteracting thyroid gland activity
medical

Examples

Antithyroid medications are commonly prescribed to patients with hyperthyroidism.
The doctor discussed the possible side effects of antithyroid drugs with his patient.
Recent studies have examined the long-term effectiveness of antithyroid therapy in treating Graves' disease.

Synset Examples

antithyroid drugs

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + thyroid (relating to the thyroid gland).
