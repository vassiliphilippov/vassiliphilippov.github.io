vocalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.kəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kə.lɪzəm / (BrE)
the sound produced by the vibration of the vocal cords and modified by the resonance of the vocal tract; vocal sound or utterance, especially as studied in linguistics or phonetics
production of vocal sounds

Examples

The linguist gave a lecture on the different types of vocalism in human languages.
Damage to the vocal cords can severely affect a person’s vocalism.
Researchers are investigating the vocalism of various primates to better understand the evolution of speech.

Synset Examples

a singer takes good care of his voice
the giraffe cannot make any vocalizations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocalization - the act or process of producing vocal sounds

Etymology

From Latin vocalis (vocal) + -ism (denoting a system or practice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the system or arrangement of vowels in a particular language or dialect
system of vowels in a language

Examples

The English vocalism differs greatly from the vocalism of Italian.
Changes in Old English vocalism led to the modern pronunciation of many words.
Comparative linguists often analyze vocalism to trace language evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vowel system - the inventory and arrangement of vowels in a language

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalismus', from 'vocalis' (vocal).
