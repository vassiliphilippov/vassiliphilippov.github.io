inunction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
The act of anointing with oil or ointment, especially as part of a religious ceremony or healing ritual.
act of anointing as a ritual

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest performed the inunction with consecrated oil.
In medieval times, inunction was believed to have both spiritual and healing powers.
The tradition of inunction continues in several religious rites around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anointing - the act of applying oil or ointment as part of a religious rite

Etymology

From Latin inunctio, from inunguere (to anoint).
