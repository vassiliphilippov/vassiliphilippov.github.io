spasmodic

1. ADJ

/ spæzˈmɑdɪk / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒdɪk / (BrE)
occurring irregularly or in sudden brief bursts rather than steadily
occurring in sudden, irregular bursts

Examples

Her spasmodic coughing fits worried the doctor.
Progress has been spasmodic due to frequent interruptions.
Gunfire erupted in spasmodic bursts throughout the night.

Synset Examples

fitful bursts of energy
spasmodic rifle fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitful - occurring intermittently or not regular in occurrence
  • Antonym: steady - regular, continuous, not interrupted

Etymology

From French 'spasmodique', from Greek 'spasmos' (spasm).

2. ADJ

/ spæzˈmɑːdɪk / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒdɪk / (BrE)
Characterized by or affected with sudden, brief, and irregular movements or spasms resembling a muscle spasm.
occurring in spasms; sudden and irregular

Examples

After the accident, he experienced spasmodic movements in his left arm.
The patient's spasmodic breathing alarmed the doctor.
Her progress toward recovery was spasmodic rather than steady.

Synset Examples

convulsive motions
his body made a spasmodic jerk
spastic movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spastic - relating to or affected by muscle spasm
  • Antonym: smooth - not interrupted by irregular movements or spasms
  • Synonym: convulsive - producing or consisting of convulsions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin spasmodicus, from Greek spasmos (spasm).
