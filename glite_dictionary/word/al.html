Al

1. NOUN

/ æl / (AmE), / æl / (BrE)
the chemical element aluminum, a lightweight, silvery, ductile metal used widely in industry
chemical element; symbol for aluminum

Examples

The foil is made entirely of Al, making it both light and strong.
Engine blocks are often constructed from Al alloys to reduce weight.
Researchers measured the conductivity of pure Al at varying temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum - the chemical element with symbol Al, atomic number 13

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'aluminum,' from Latin 'alumen' (alum).
