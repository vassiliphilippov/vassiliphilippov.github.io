Oxydendrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛndrəm / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛndrəm / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees in the heath family, known for their sour-tasting leaves and white flowers; especially the sourwood tree (Oxydendrum arboreum) of eastern North America
a genus of trees including the sourwood

Examples

The Oxydendrum arboreum, commonly known as sourwood, blooms with delicate white flowers in summer.
Botanists identified the tree as part of the Oxydendrum genus.
The leaves of the Oxydendrum have a distinctive sour taste that deters many herbivores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourwood - a deciduous tree (Oxydendrum arboreum) with sour-tasting leaves and white flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'oxys' (sour) and 'dendron' (tree), referring to the sour taste of its leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛndrəm / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛndrəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering trees in the heath family, Ericaceae, commonly known as sourwood.
genus of sourwood trees

Examples

The Oxydendrum genus is represented chiefly by the sourwood tree native to eastern North America.
Oxydendrum trees are valued for their attractive white flowers and brilliant fall foliage.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Oxydendrum to determine its classification within the Ericaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourwood - a common name for trees in the genus Oxydendrum

Etymology

From Greek oxys (sharp, sour) and dendron (tree).
