pyromania

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
A mental disorder characterized by an irresistible urge to start fires deliberately.
impulse to set fires (disorder)
medical

Examples

The suspect was diagnosed with pyromania after repeatedly setting fires in the neighborhood.
Her struggle with pyromania led to several visits to psychiatric clinics.
Researchers are studying the causes and treatment of impulse-control disorders like pyromania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arson - the criminal act of deliberately setting fire to property

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'mania' (madness).
