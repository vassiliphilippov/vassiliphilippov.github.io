Mithridates VI of Pontus

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪθ.rɪˈdeɪ.tiz sɪksθ əv ˈpɒn.təs / (AmE), / ˌmɪθ.rɪˈdeɪ.tiːz sɪksθ ɒv ˈpɒn.təs / (BrE)
(c. 132–63 BC) An ancient king of Pontus who expanded his kingdom in Asia Minor, led several wars against Rome, and was eventually defeated and driven from power by the Roman general Pompey.
ancient king of Pontus, opponent of Rome

Examples

Historians regard Mithridates VI of Pontus as one of Rome's most formidable adversaries.
The decisive campaigns against Mithridates VI of Pontus changed the balance of power in Asia Minor.
Archaeologists have discovered coins bearing the image of Mithridates VI of Pontus near the Black Sea.

Etymology

From the Greek name Mithridatēs; Mithridates VI Eupator was the official Latin designation.
