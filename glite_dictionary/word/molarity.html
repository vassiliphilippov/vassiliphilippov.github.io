molarity

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈlærɪti / (AmE), / məˈlarɪti / (BrE)
the concentration of a solution measured by the number of moles of solute per liter of solution
concentration measure in moles per liter
medical

Examples

The molarity of the acid solution was calculated to ensure accurate mixing for the experiment.
Increasing the molarity will result in a more concentrated solution.
She prepared a 1.0 molarity sodium chloride solution for the biology lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the amount of a substance in a given volume

Etymology

Formed from 'mole' (chemistry) and the suffix '-arity' (denoting quality or state).
