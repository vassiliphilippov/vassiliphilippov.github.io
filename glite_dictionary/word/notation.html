notation

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a brief comment or instruction added to a piece of writing or document
a short written comment or instruction

Examples

She wrote a helpful notation in the margin of the textbook.
A brief notation was left on my desk giving me the details.
He added a notation to the envelope so the package would be delivered correctly.

Synset Examples

his notes were appended at the end of the article
he added a short notation to the address on the envelope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a brief written record or comment

Etymology

From Latin notatio (a marking, noting down), from notare (to mark, note).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of symbols or signs used to represent numbers, quantities, tones, or other special things in writing, especially in mathematics and science
system of written symbols (math, music, etc.)

Examples

Musical notation allows composers to convey melodies to performers accurately.
The scientist introduced a new mathematical notation to simplify complex equations.
Understanding chemical notation is essential for students of chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - a mark or character representing something else

Etymology

From Latin notatio (a marking, notation), from notare (to mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06821351-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the activity or process of representing things using a special system of marks, symbols, or characters, especially in subjects such as mathematics, music, or science.
system of symbolic representation

Examples

Mathematical problems often require a specific kind of notation to express complex ideas succinctly.
Musicians learn to read musical notation from an early age.
Chemical formulas use unique notation to represent different compounds and reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • Synonym: representation - the action of depicting or describing something

Etymology

From Latin notatio (a marking, note) from notare (to mark, note).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00616469-n
Synset POS: n
