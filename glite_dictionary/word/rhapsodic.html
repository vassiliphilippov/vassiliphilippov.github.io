rhapsodic

1. ADJ

/ ræpˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / ræpˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
expressing or characterized by great enthusiasm, joy, or ecstasy
full of great enthusiasm or delight

Examples

The audience broke into rhapsodic applause at the end of the concert.
Her rhapsodic description of the landscape made everyone want to visit the place.
He spoke in a rhapsodic tone about his new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatic - feeling overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement
  • Synonym: rapturous - expressing great pleasure or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: matter-of-fact - unemotional and practical

Etymology

From Greek 'rhapsōidikos', relating to rhapsody.
