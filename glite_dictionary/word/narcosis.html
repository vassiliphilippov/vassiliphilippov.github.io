narcosis

1. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / nɑːˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A state of stupor or unconsciousness produced by narcotics or anesthesia.
unconsciousness from narcotics or anesthesia
medical

Examples

After the accident, the patient fell into narcosis due to the high dose of painkillers.
Certain anesthetic gases can cause narcosis if inhaled for prolonged periods.
The doctor monitored her vital signs closely while she was under narcosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation induced for medical purposes
  • Degree Higher: coma - a prolonged state of deep unconsciousness

Etymology

From Greek narkōsis (numbness, stupor), from narkoun (to make numb).
