Chrysanthemum segetum

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsænθəməm səˈdʒiːtəm / (AmE), / kraɪˌsænθɪməm sɪˈdʒiːtəm / (BrE)
A European herbaceous plant with bright yellow flowers, commonly found as a weed in grain fields.
yellow-flowered European weed

Examples

The farmer was frustrated to find Chrysanthemum segetum spreading rapidly throughout his wheat field.
Bright clusters of Chrysanthemum segetum added unexpected color to the otherwise uniform barley crop.
Researchers studied how Chrysanthemum segetum competes with other plants for nutrients in agricultural soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn marigold - Common name for Chrysanthemum segetum

Etymology

From Latin: 'chrysanthemum' from Greek 'khrusanthemon' (gold flower), 'segetum' relating to cornfields.
