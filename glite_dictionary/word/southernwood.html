southernwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərnwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsʌðənwʊd / (BrE)
A shrubby aromatic plant of the daisy family (Artemisia abrotanum), native to Europe and sometimes used in brewing and as a garden plant.
aromatic shrubby European plant

Examples

A border of southernwood lined the garden path, releasing a pleasant scent when brushed.
She added dried southernwood to the mixture for its distinctive aroma.
In colonial times, southernwood was often used to flavor homemade beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a group of aromatic plants of the genus Artemisia
