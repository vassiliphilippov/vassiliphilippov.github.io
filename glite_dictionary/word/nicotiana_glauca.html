Nicotiana glauca

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə ˈɡlaʊkə / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtiˈɑːnə ˈɡlaʊkə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to South America and naturalized in other regions, known for its yellow flowers and toxic properties to livestock
toxic South American shrub; tree tobacco

Examples

Nicotiana glauca can be toxic to livestock if they consume its leaves or stems.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Nicotiana glauca for a harmless ornamental shrub because of its attractive yellow flowers.
Authorities warned that the spread of Nicotiana glauca may threaten native plant habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree tobacco - another name for Nicotiana glauca, the same plant species
  • Degree Lower: Nicotiana tabacum - cultivated species of tobacco, source of commercial tobacco products

Etymology

From Latin Nicotiana (the tobacco genus, named after Jean Nicot) and glauca (Latin for 'bluish-gray', referring to the waxy leaf color).
