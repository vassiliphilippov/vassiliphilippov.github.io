Fenusa

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnuːsə / (AmE), / fəˈnjuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of small sawflies whose larvae are known as birch leaf miners, feeding within the leaves of birch trees.
genus of leaf-mining sawflies

Examples

The larvae of Fenusa can cause significant damage to birch trees by mining their leaves.
Researchers have studied the life cycle of Fenusa to help manage pest outbreaks in urban areas.
During late spring, infestations of Fenusa are often visible as brown blotches on birch foliage.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used in taxonomy for this genus.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnuːsə / (AmE), / fəˈnjuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of small sawflies in the family Tenthredinidae, some species of which are known as birch leaf miners that feed on the leaves of birch and other trees.
genus of leaf-mining sawflies

Examples

The larvae of Fenusa species can cause significant damage to birch trees by mining the leaves.
Gardeners often monitor for infestations of the birch leaf miner, a common pest from the genus Fenusa.
Several Fenusa sawflies were discovered feeding on the young leaves in early spring.

Etymology

From New Latin Fenusa, scientific genus name in entomology.
