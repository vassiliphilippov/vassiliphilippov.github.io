intransitiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrænzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtranzɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being intransitive; the condition of a verb that does not require or cannot take a direct object
quality of being intransitive (of verbs)

Examples

The intransitiveness of certain verbs makes them unsuitable for use with direct objects.
Understanding intransitiveness is essential when studying how sentences are constructed in English grammar.
Linguists analyze the intransitiveness of verbs to better describe language patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transitivity - the quality of taking a direct object (in grammar)

Etymology

From intransitive + -ness.
