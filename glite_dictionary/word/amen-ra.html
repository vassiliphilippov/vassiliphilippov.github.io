Amen-Ra

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːmənˈrɑː / (AmE), / ˌɑːmɛnˈrɑː / (BrE)
in ancient Egyptian religion, the sun god and supreme deity resulting from the merger of Amen (Amun) and Ra, especially worshipped during Theban supremacy.
Egyptian sun god, supreme deity

Examples

The ancient Egyptians built grand temples in honor of Amen-Ra.
During Theban rule, worship of Amen-Ra became widespread across Egypt.
Priests performed elaborate rituals to please Amen-Ra as the supreme deity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amun - Egyptian god associated with air and sun, merged with Ra
  • Synonym: Ra - Egyptian sun god, aspect merged with Amun to form Amen-Ra

Etymology

Amen from Egyptian 'Imn' (Amun) combined with Ra, the Egyptian sun god.
