to contravene

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈviːn / (BrE)
to act in violation of a law, rule, or agreement; to go against or oppose officially established regulations
to violate a rule or law

Examples

Companies that contravene safety regulations may face heavy fines.
He was found guilty of contravening the international treaty.
The proposal contravenes established ethical guidelines.

Synset Examples

He ran afoul of the law
This behavior conflicts with our rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or law
  • Antonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a rule or law

Etymology

From Latin contravenire (to come against), from contra (against) + venire (to come).

2. VERB

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈviːn / (BrE)
to deny or contradict the truth of a statement or assertion
deny or contradict the truth of

Examples

The defendant sought to contravene the prosecutor’s version of events during the trial.
He attempted to contravene her statement with evidence to the contrary.
Scholars have tried to contravene the widely accepted theory, but with little success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true
  • Antonym: to affirm - to state firmly that something is true

Etymology

From French contravener, from Latin contravenire (to oppose, come against).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00825097-v
Synset POS: v
