larghetto

1. ADJ

/ lɑːrˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / lɑːˈɡɛt.əʊ / (BrE)
(in music) at a tempo that is slightly faster than largo and slower than andante; broadly and rather slowly.
somewhat slow musical tempo

Examples

The symphony's second movement is marked larghetto, giving it a calm and expansive character.
The conductor asked the orchestra to play the passage larghetto for a gentler mood.
Her piano piece opens with a larghetto section before moving into a faster tempo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: largo - very slow and broad tempo in music

Etymology

From Italian 'larghetto', diminutive of 'largo' (broad, slow).

2. NOUN

/ lɑrˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / lɑːˈɡɛtəʊ / (BrE)
(music) a passage or piece played at a slow tempo, slightly faster than largo but slower than adagio.
slow tempo in music, between largo and adagio

Examples

The second movement of the symphony is marked larghetto, giving it a gentle and relaxed feel.
She practiced the larghetto passage repeatedly to capture the subtle changes in tempo.
The conductor asked the orchestra to start the piece at larghetto speed, not too slow but unhurried.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: largo - very slow tempo in music
  • Degree Higher: adagio - slow tempo in music, slightly faster than larghetto

Etymology

Italian, diminutive of largo ('slow, broad').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07060795-n
Synset POS: n
