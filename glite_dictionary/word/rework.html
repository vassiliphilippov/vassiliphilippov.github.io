to rework

1. VERB

/ riˈwɝk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwɜːk / (BrE)
to use or adapt something again in an altered or improved form
use or adapt again in altered form

Examples

She decided to rework her essay after receiving feedback from her teacher.
The director plans to rework the script before filming begins.
Many designers rework old concepts to fit current trends.

Synset Examples

retread an old plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revise - to re-examine and improve the form or content of something
  • Degree Lower: to reuse - to use something again

Etymology

From re- (again) + work (to make or do), indicating doing work again or modifying.
