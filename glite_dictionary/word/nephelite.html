nephelite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛfəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɛfɪˌlʌɪt / (BrE)
a whitish mineral composed of sodium or potassium aluminum silicate, found in crystalline form and used in ceramics and enamels
a mineral used in ceramics and enamels

Examples

The laboratory identified the white crystals as nephelite during their mineral analysis.
Nephelite is commonly found in igneous rocks, especially in nepheline syenites.
Manufacturers use nephelite as a raw material in the production of ceramic tiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nepheline - alternative spelling of nephelite, same mineral

Etymology

From German 'Nephelin', from Greek 'nephelē' (cloud), due to its cloudy appearance.
