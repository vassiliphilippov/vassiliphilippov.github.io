Solanum melongena

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌleɪnəm məˈlɒndʒənə / (AmE), / səˌleɪnəm mɛˈlɒndʒɪnə / (BrE)
A species of plant native to southeastern Asia, widely cultivated for its large, glossy, edible fruit known as eggplant or aubergine.
plant species grown for its edible fruit (eggplant/aubergine)

Examples

The leaves of Solanum melongena are broad and slightly hairy.
Modern varieties of Solanum melongena produce fruit in purple, white, or green.
Farmers in Southeast Asia have cultivated Solanum melongena for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eggplant - common name for Solanum melongena, edible purple fruit
  • Synonym: aubergine - British term for the fruit of Solanum melongena

Etymology

From Latin: 'solanum' (nightshade) and 'melongena' (eggplant).
