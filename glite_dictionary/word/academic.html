academic

1. ADJ

/ ˌækəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
overly concerned with formal rules, theory, or minor details of learning, often lacking practical relevance
focused on learning's minor or formal aspects

Examples

His arguments tended to be rather academic and disconnected from real-world concerns.
The debate soon became academic, focusing on minute historical details rather than practical solutions.
Some professors are criticized for their academic approach that values theory over application.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedantic - excessively concerned with minor details or rules

Etymology

From Latin academicus, from Academia (the school of Plato in Athens).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02091054-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌækəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
a person who teaches or conducts research at a college or university, especially as a member of the faculty
university educator or researcher

Examples

As an academic, she is dedicated to advancing scientific knowledge through her research.
Many academics spend their summers writing papers and attending conferences.
The university is hiring several new academic staff members this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholar - a specialist in a particular branch of study, especially the humanities; a learned person
  • Synonym: professor - a university academic of the highest rank

Etymology

From Greek akadēmaikos, relating to the Academy, the school founded by Plato.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09778400-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌækəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
relating to education, schools, colleges, universities, or scholarly activities
relating to education or scholarly activities

Examples

Her academic achievements earned her a scholarship to a prestigious university.
The university is known for its strong academic programs and research facilities.
During graduation, the students wore traditional academic gowns and caps.

Synset Examples

the academic curriculum
academic gowns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholarly - involving or relating to serious academic study
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas

Etymology

From Latin academicus, from Academia (the school where Plato taught).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02610356-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌækəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
Concerned with ideas, theories, or questions that are not practical or likely to have a real effect; hypothetical or not related to real situations.
theoretical; not of practical relevance

Examples

The answer to that is purely academic since the law is no longer enforced anyway.
Her objections were deemed academic because the project had already started.
Whether they would have succeeded is an academic question at this point.

Synset Examples

an academic discussion
an academic question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with or involving the theory of a subject rather than its practical application
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actually doing or using something rather than theories or ideas

Etymology

From Latin academicus, from Academia, the name of Plato's school.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00865905-s
Synset POS: s
