Chiococca

1. NOUN

/ ki.oʊˈkɑː.kə / (AmE), / ki.əˈkɒk.ə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, known for their white or yellowish berries.
genus of tropical American shrubs

Examples

The Chiococca shrub thrives in warm, subtropical climates across the Americas.
Researchers discovered several new medicinal compounds in the roots of Chiococca.
Gardeners often admire the white berries produced by Chiococca species.

Etymology

From Greek 'kiōn' (pillar) and 'kokkos' (berry).

2. NOUN

/ kaɪ.oʊˈkɑː.kə / (AmE), / kaɪ.əʊˈkɒ.kə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs native to tropical and subtropical regions of the New World, belonging to the family Rubiaceae.
genus of tropical and subtropical shrubs

Examples

The botanical garden recently acquired several species from the Chiococca genus.
Chiococca plants are often noted for their striking white berries.
A researcher studied the medicinal properties of Chiococca shrubs found in the Caribbean.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek roots khion (snow) and kokkos (berry), referring to the white berries of some species.
