Ypres

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːprə / (AmE), / ˈiːprə / (BrE)
A major battle site in World War I, particularly known for the 1915 battle during which Germany first used chlorine gas as a weapon.
WWI battle site in Belgium

Examples

The Second Battle of Ypres marked the first large-scale use of poison gas in World War I.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the fighting around Ypres in 1915.
Historians often discuss the significance of Ypres when examining the use of chemical warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Somme - A major World War I battle in 1916 on the Western Front
  • Synonym: Battle of Verdun - One of the largest battles of World War I, fought between France and Germany in 1916

Etymology

From French Ypres, the name of the Belgian city.

2. PROPN

/ ˈiːprə / (AmE), / ˈiːprə / (BrE)
A major battle of World War I (1917) in Flanders, where an Allied offensive failed partly due to waterlogged ground; notable for the German use of mustard gas.
World War I battle in Flanders (1917)

Examples

The Third Battle of Ypres was marked by heavy casualties and terrible muddy conditions.
Many soldiers suffered from mustard gas attacks during the fighting at Ypres.
My great-grandfather fought at Ypres in 1917 and survived the dreadful conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passchendaele - Another name for the Third Battle of Ypres, major WWI battle

Etymology

Named after Ypres, a town in Flanders (now Ieper, Belgium), where the battles took place.

3. PROPN

/ ˈipɹə, iˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːprə, iˈprɛs / (BrE)
A town in Belgium, notable as the site of major battles during World War I, especially a heavy but indecisive engagement in 1914 between Allied and German forces.
town in Belgium; World War I battle site

Examples

The First Battle of Ypres marked the beginning of trench warfare on the Western Front.
Many soldiers lost their lives at Ypres during World War I.
The devastation at Ypres is still remembered by visitors to the region today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Somme - major World War I battle in France
  • Synonym: Passchendaele - major World War I battle near Ypres

Etymology

From French Ypres, the name of the town in Belgium.
