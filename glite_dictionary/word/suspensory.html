suspensory

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɛn.sə.ri / (AmE), / səˈspɛn.səri / (BrE)
a bandage or device of elastic fabric designed to support and uplift a dependent or sagging part of the body, such as the scrotum or a pendulous breast
supporting medical bandage or device
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended wearing a suspensory to relieve discomfort after surgery.
He was fitted with a suspensory to support the injured area during recovery.
A suspensory can help reduce strain on sensitive tissues following certain medical procedures.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'suspensorium', from Latin 'suspendere' meaning 'to hang up'.
