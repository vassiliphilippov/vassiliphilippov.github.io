Katsuwonus

1. NOUN

/ kætˈsuːwəˌnəs / (AmE), / katˈsuːwəˌnəs / (BrE)
A genus of oceanic bonito fishes in the family Scombridae, including the commonly known skipjack tuna.
genus of oceanic bonitos

Examples

The genus Katsuwonus includes the well-known skipjack tuna, which is widely fished around the world.
Recent studies have reclassified some bonitos into the genus Katsuwonus.
Katsuwonus species are important both ecologically and economically in tropical and subtropical oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipjack tuna - a species within the genus Katsuwonus, known scientifically as Katsuwonus pelamis

Etymology

From Japanese *katsuo* (bonito) and Latinized Greek *onos* (tuna).

2. NOUN

/ kætˈsuːwənəs / (AmE), / katˈsuːwɒnəs / (BrE)
a genus of oceanic bonitos, sometimes classified in its own family Katsuwonidae, including the skipjack tuna.
a genus of oceanic bonitos (fish)

Examples

The Katsuwonus genus is well known among marine biologists for its commercially important species.
Fishermen often target Katsuwonus for their abundant skipjack tuna populations.
Research shows that Katsuwonus species migrate long distances across the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipjack tuna - a species (Katsuwonus pelamis) included in this genus

Etymology

From Japanese 'katsuo' (bonito) + Greek 'onos' (tuna).
