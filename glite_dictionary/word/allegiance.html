allegiance

1. NOUN

/ əˈlidʒəns / (AmE), / əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
loyalty or commitment to a group, cause, or individual
loyalty or commitment

Examples

The soldier swore allegiance to his country before departing for service.
Her allegiance to the team remained strong even after several losses.
The new law required citizens to declare their allegiance openly.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - a strong feeling of support or allegiance
  • Antonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country

Etymology

From Old French 'aligeance' or 'ligeance', from Medieval Latin 'allegantia' (loyalty).

2. NOUN

/ əˈliːdʒəns / (AmE), / əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
loyalty or commitment of a subordinate to a superior or of an individual to a group or cause, especially loyalty that citizens owe to their country or subjects to their sovereign.
loyalty owed to a country or ruler

Examples

The soldiers swore their allegiance to the new government.
He changed his allegiance from one political party to another.
Questions about allegiance often arise during times of war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - faithful adherence to a person, group, or cause
  • Antonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country

Etymology

From Old French *alégeance*, influenced by Latin *ligare* (to bind).
