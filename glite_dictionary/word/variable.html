variable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛriəbəl / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbəl / (BrE)
(of a device) able to have a property, such as intensity or capacity, adjusted or changed within a range.
capable of being adjusted (esp. devices)

Examples

We installed a variable resistor to control the current in the circuit.
The camera lens features a variable aperture for different lighting conditions.
This lamp comes with a variable brightness setting so you can change the mood easily.

Synset Examples

a variable capacitor
variable filters in front of the mercury xenon lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustable - able to be adjusted or changed to fit requirements

Etymology

From Latin variabilis (changeable), from variare (to change).

WordNet ID: oewn-01032411-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈver.i.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈveəri.ə.bəl / (BrE)
an element, feature, or factor that is liable to change or vary; something subject to variation
something subject to change or variation

Examples

In any experiment, temperature is an important variable that must be controlled.

The weather is one variable that can affect the outcome of a sporting event.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəbəl / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbl / (BrE)
a symbol, often a letter, used in mathematics or logic to represent a quantity that can change or take on different values
a symbol representing a changeable quantity

Examples

Scientists often change one variable at a time to observe its effect on the outcome.
The value assigned to a variable can be updated as the program runs.
