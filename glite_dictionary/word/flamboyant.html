flamboyant

1. NOUN

/ flæmˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
A tropical tree (Delonix regia), native to Madagascar, widely grown for its large and vivid scarlet or orange flower clusters.
a tropical tree with scarlet or orange flowers

Examples

The garden was dominated by a magnificent flamboyant in full bloom.
Many streets in the city are lined with flamboyant trees, creating a canopy of red and orange flowers.
A single flamboyant can transform a plain landscape into a vibrant spectacle during flowering season.

Etymology

From French flamboyant (flaming), referring to its dazzling flowers.

2. ADJ

/ flæmˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
elaborately or excessively decorated; showy or ostentatious in appearance or style.
elaborately showy or decorated

Examples

The actress wore a flamboyant gown covered in sequins to the premiere.
His living room was decorated in a flamboyant style, with gold curtains and bright patterns everywhere.
Critics described the architect's new building as flamboyant and out of place among the older, simpler structures.

Synset Examples

flamboyant handwriting
the senator's florid speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately decorated or adorned
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way, not showy

Etymology

From French 'flamboyant' (flaming), from 'flambe' (flame).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01799280-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ flæmˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
(of people or things) excessively showy or elaborate, often in a tasteless or gaudy way.
showy and ostentatious, often tasteless

Examples

Her flamboyant dress drew stares from everyone at the party.
The actor was known for his flamboyant gestures and dramatic speeches.
They found the decorations a bit too flamboyant for such a formal event.

Synset Examples

a cheap showy rhinestone bracelet
a splashy half-page ad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentatious - intended to attract notice and impress others
  • Synonym: showy - making an impressive display
  • Antonym: subdued - not showy or flamboyant, restrained in style

Etymology

From French *flamboyant* (flaming), from *flamboyer* (to flame), ultimately from Latin *flamma* (flame).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
