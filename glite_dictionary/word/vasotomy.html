vasotomy

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsɑtəmi / (AmE), / vəˈsɒtəmi / (BrE)
A surgical incision into or division of the vas deferens.
surgical incision of vas deferens
medical

Examples

The urologist performed a vasotomy as part of the corrective surgery.
A vasotomy is sometimes required to access blocked seminal ducts.
Recovery after a vasotomy usually involves minimal discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasectomy - surgical removal of part of the vas deferens

Etymology

From Latin 'vas' (vessel) and Greek 'tomia' (cutting).
