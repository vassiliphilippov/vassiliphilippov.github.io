ample

1. ADJ

/ ˈæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈæmpəl / (BrE)
more than enough; plentiful in amount, size, or capacity
more than enough; plentiful

Examples

The hotel room offered ample space for all our luggage and belongings.
She had ample time to finish her assignment before the deadline.
We received ample warnings about the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

had ample food for the party
an ample supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentiful - existing in large amounts; more than enough
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; hard to find

Etymology

From Latin 'amplus' (large, spacious).

3. ADJ

/ ˈæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈampl̩ / (BrE)
enough or more than enough; plentiful in quantity, size, or capacity; affording an abundant supply.
plentiful; more than enough

Examples

There was ample room for everyone in the spacious hall.
They stored an ample amount of food for the winter months.
The evidence against the suspect was ample to convict him.

Synset Examples

copious provisions

a plenteous grape harvest
a rich supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentiful - existing in great quantity; more than sufficient
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; in short supply

Etymology

From Latin 'amplus' (large, spacious, ample).
