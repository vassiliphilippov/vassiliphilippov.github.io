cheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃitˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an annual grass native to Eurasia, with softly hairy leaves, often considered a weed in North America
a weedy annual Eurasian grass

Examples

Cheatgrass spreads quickly in disturbed soils, outcompeting native plants.
Large areas of rangeland have been overtaken by cheatgrass in recent decades.
After the wildfire, cheatgrass was the first plant to return to the landscape.
