tedium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈtiːdiəm / (BrE)
The state of being boring or monotonous, especially due to length, repetition, or slowness.
the quality of being boring or monotonous

Examples

The tedium of the long meeting made it difficult to stay awake.
He tried to find ways to escape the tedium of his daily routine.
Many people listen to music at work to break up the tedium of repetitive tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boredom - the state of feeling bored
  • Antonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Latin taedium (weariness, boredom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈtiːdiəm / (BrE)
the state of being bored and weary because something is tedious or monotonous
the state of being bored by tediousness

Examples

After hours of paperwork, the tedium became almost unbearable.
She tried to find ways to break the tedium of her daily routine.
The film was so slow-paced that I couldn't escape the tedium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boredom - the state of feeling bored
  • Antonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Latin 'taedium' (disgust, weariness).
