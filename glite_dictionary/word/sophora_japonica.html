Sophora japonica

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfɔrə dʒæˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / səˈfɔːrə dʒæˈpəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to China and Japan, known for its round crown, dark green compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant creamy-white flowers.
deciduous tree with creamy-white flowers, native to East Asia

Examples

The Sophora japonica in the garden bloomed impressively this summer.
Traditional healers in Asia have used Sophora japonica for centuries for various purposes.
A line of mature Sophora japonica trees provided welcome shade along the avenue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese pagoda tree - Common name for Sophora japonica

Etymology

Latinized from genus name Sophora and specific epithet japonica meaning 'of Japan.'
