hakham

1. NOUN

/ hɑˈkɑm / (AmE), / hɑˈkɑːm / (BrE)
A Hebrew title of respect given to a wise and highly educated man, often a rabbi or community leader.
respected wise man (Hebrew title)

Examples

The community sought advice from the hakham on important religious matters.
As a renowned hakham, he was respected throughout the region for his wisdom.
He studied for many years before becoming a hakham in his synagogue.

Etymology

From Hebrew חָכָם (ḥakhám), meaning 'wise' or 'wise man.'
