ingot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋ.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.ɡət / (BrE)
a block of metal, typically oblong, that has been cast in a convenient shape for storage or further processing
block of cast metal

Examples

The gold ingot was stored securely in the bank vault.
Workers poured molten steel into molds to form each ingot.
She purchased a silver ingot as an investment.

Etymology

From Old French 'ingot' (ingot, mass of metal), of uncertain origin.
