Crotalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.təl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.tə.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of large rattlesnakes native to the Americas, characterized by a segmented rattle at the end of the tail.
genus of rattlesnakes

Examples

The Crotalus genus includes some of the most well-known rattlesnakes in North America.
Herpetologists have studied Crotalus behavior to understand how these snakes adapt to different environments.
A Crotalus can deliver a potent venomous bite, though it rarely attacks unless threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous snake with a rattle at the end of its tail, found chiefly in the Americas

Etymology

From Greek *krotalon* (a rattle or castanet), referring to the animal's rattling tail.
