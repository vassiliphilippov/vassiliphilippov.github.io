tunefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtun.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːn.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a pleasant melody or musical sound
the quality of being melodious

Examples

The tunefulness of the choir's performance delighted everyone at the concert.
Some birds are admired for the tunefulness of their songs.
Critics praised the composer's work for its tunefulness and originality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodiousness - the quality of being melodious or tuneful
  • Antonym: tunelessness - the quality of lacking a melody or being unmusical

Etymology

From 'tuneful' + '-ness', where 'tuneful' comes from 'tune' and '-ful'.
