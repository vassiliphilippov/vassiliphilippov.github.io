Anthonomus grandis

1. NOUN

/ ænˌθɑnəˌmoʊs ˈɡrændəs / (AmE), / ænˌθɒnəməs ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
A species of greyish weevil that lays its eggs in cotton bolls, often damaging cotton crops.
species of weevil damaging cotton

Examples

The infestation of Anthonomus grandis devastated the region's cotton fields for several seasons.
Scientists are researching ways to control Anthonomus grandis using environmentally friendly methods.
The arrival of Anthonomus grandis in the southern United States changed the cotton industry forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boll weevil - common name for Anthonomus grandis, a pest beetle of cotton

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Anthonomus' and species name 'grandis', meaning 'large'.
