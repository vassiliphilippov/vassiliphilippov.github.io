Rodrigo Borgia

1. PROPN

/ roʊˈdriːgoʊ ˈbɔːrʒə / (AmE), / rəʊˈdriːgəʊ ˈbɔːdʒiə / (BrE)
(1431–1503) Pope Alexander VI, a prominent Renaissance pope and father of Cesare and Lucrezia Borgia, known for his controversial and influential papacy.
Renaissance pope, father of Cesare and Lucrezia Borgia

Examples

Rodrigo Borgia became pope in 1492, taking the name Alexander VI.
Historians often debate the legacy of Rodrigo Borgia and his influence on Renaissance Italy.
The children of Rodrigo Borgia, especially Cesare and Lucrezia, became infamous figures in their own right.

Etymology

From Spanish Rodrigo Borgia; Borgia is a family name of Italian origin.
