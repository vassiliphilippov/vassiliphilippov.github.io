mother

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who has given birth to a child; also used as a form of address for one's own female parent
female parent; woman who has given birth

Examples

His mother always encouraged him to follow his dreams.
She became a mother for the first time last year.
I called my mother to wish her a happy birthday.

Synset Examples

the mother of three children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: father - male parent; man who has begotten a child
  • Synonym: parent - a person's father or mother

Etymology

From Old English *modor*, from Proto-Germanic *mōdēr*, from Proto-Indo-European *méh₂tēr* (mother).

/ ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə / (BrE)

He respectfully called the matronly caregiver mother during his stay at the shelter.
In some communities, it is common to address an older woman as mother out of respect.

3. VERB

/ ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
to give birth to or bring into existence (offspring); to be the female parent of.
to give birth to (as a mother)

Examples

The queen mothered five children during her reign.
She mothered twins in her early thirties.
Many animals can mother several litters in their lifetime.

Synset Examples

Abraham begot Isaac
John fathered four daughters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: father - to be the male parent; to sire offspring

Etymology

From Old English *modor*, related to Proto-Germanic *mōdēr* and Latin *mater* (mother).

5. VERB

/ ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
to care for or protect someone as a mother would; to treat someone with great affection and attention, often excessively
to care for like a mother

Examples

Even though her children are grown, she still likes to mother them whenever they visit.
He felt his girlfriend tried to mother him by always reminding him to eat and sleep well.
She tends to mother her friends, making sure everyone is comfortable and cared for.

Synset Examples

She fusses over her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurture - to care for and encourage the growth or development of someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Old English 'mōdor', ultimately from Proto-Germanic '*mōdēr', related to maternal roots in many Indo-European languages.
