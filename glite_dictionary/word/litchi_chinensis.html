Litchi chinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪtʃ.i tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree native to southeastern China, widely cultivated for its edible, oval-shaped fruit known as the lychee.
tropical tree cultivated for lychee fruit

Examples

The Litchi chinensis tree thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
Farmers in India have expanded the cultivation of Litchi chinensis due to high demand for its fruit.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Litchi chinensis to improve fruit yield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - the edible fruit of Litchi chinensis

Etymology

From Chinese 'lìzhī' (荔枝), the fruit's name, plus Latin 'chinensis' (Chinese).
