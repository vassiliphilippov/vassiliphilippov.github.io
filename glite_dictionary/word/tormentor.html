tormentor

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈmɛntər / (AmE), / tɔːˈmɛntə / (BrE)
a piece of stage scenery placed at the side of the stage to conceal the wings from the audience's view
theater curtain or flat hiding the wings

Examples

The stage crew quickly adjusted the tormentor to block the audience's view of the wings.
Each tormentor must be perfectly aligned to maintain the illusion of a complete set.
Damaged tormentors can ruin a production by exposing behind-the-scenes activity.

2. NOUN

/ tɔrˈmɛntər / (AmE), / tɔːˈmentə / (BrE)
a person who causes someone to suffer mental or physical pain, often deliberately and repeatedly
a person who causes suffering

Examples

She could not escape her tormentor, who followed her everywhere with cruel remarks.
The bully became the tormentor of many students in the school.
Years later, he finally confronted his childhood tormentor and found closure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of a crime, accident, or other event or action

Etymology

From Latin 'tormentum' (instrument of torture) via Old French and late Middle English.
