due

1. ADJ

/ du / (AmE), / djuː / (BrE)
(of an event or result) caused by or attributable to something or someone.
caused by; attributable to

Examples

The flight was delayed due to heavy fog.
His illness is due to overwork and stress.
The cancellation was due to unforeseen circumstances.

Synset Examples

punctuation errors ascribable to careless proofreading
the cancellation of the concert was due to the rain
the oversight was not imputable to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attributable - regarded as being caused by
  • Synonym: caused by - produced as a result of

Etymology

From Latin debitus (owed, proper, due) via Old French deu.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00173133-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ duː / (AmE), / djuː / (BrE)
a thing that is deserved or owed to someone, especially as a right or just reward
something deserved or owed

Examples

He believes that everyone should receive their due for hard work.
Give credit where due—she did a fantastic job on the project.
The player was finally given his due after years of dedication.

Synset Examples

give the devil his due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert - something deserved, especially a punishment or reward
  • Synonym: debt - something, typically money, that is owed or due

Etymology

From Old French deu (owed, due), from Latin debitus (owed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05183609-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ duː / (AmE), / djuː / (BrE)
(adverb) Directly or exactly in a particular direction, especially on a compass; straight.
directly or exactly in a direction

Examples

The explorers traveled due east to reach the coast.
We sailed due north for several days without changing course.
Point the telescope due south to observe the star.

Synset Examples

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ duː / (AmE), / djuː / (BrE)
(of a sum of money) owed and payable either immediately or on demand.
owed and payable (money/debt)

Examples

The rent is due on the first day of each month.
Your final payment will be due at the end of the year.
No interest will be charged if the bill is paid as soon as it becomes due.

Synset Examples

payment is due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payable - (of money) required to be paid
  • Antonym: overdue - (of a payment) past the expected or required time of payment

Etymology

From Old French 'deu', past participle of 'devoir' (to owe), from Latin 'debere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137425-a
Synset POS: a
