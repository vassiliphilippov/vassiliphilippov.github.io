pepperwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəwɜːt / (BrE)
any of several small plants, especially of the genus Marsilea, characterized by compound leaves with four leaflets and typically found in wet habitats; also sometimes refers to certain plants of the genus Lepidium.
water fern or small plant with four-part leaves

Examples

The shallow edges of the pond were thick with pepperwort thriving among the reeds.
Botanists studied the pepperwort to better understand its unique leaf structure.
Some species of pepperwort can be found in marshy fields throughout Europe.

Etymology

Named for its peppery taste and wort (Old English for 'plant' or 'herb').

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəwɜːt / (BrE)
An annual herb of the genus Lepidium, used as a salad green and garnish, known for its peppery flavor.
annual herb used as salad green

Examples

She sprinkled fresh pepperwort over the salad to add a spicy kick.
Pepperwort is often used as a garnish in contemporary cuisine.
The chef added chopped pepperwort to the sandwich for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden cress - a related plant also used as a salad green

Etymology

Formed from 'pepper' (referring to its taste) and 'wort', an old word for plant or herb.
