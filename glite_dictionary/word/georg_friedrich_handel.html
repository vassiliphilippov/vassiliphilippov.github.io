Georg Friedrich Handel

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæn.dəl / (AmE), / ˈhændl̩ / (BrE)
(1685–1759) German-born British Baroque composer, best known for his oratorio 'Messiah' and many other significant works.
German-British Baroque composer (1685–1759)

Examples

Georg Friedrich Handel composed the famous oratorio 'Messiah' in 1741.
Many musicologists study the works of Georg Friedrich Handel to understand the evolution of Baroque music.
The city of Halle, Germany, celebrates Georg Friedrich Handel's legacy with an annual music festival.

Etymology

From German, original full name Georg Friedrich Händel; anglicized as George Frideric Handel after naturalization in Britain.
