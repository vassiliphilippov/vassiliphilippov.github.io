Terebellidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈbɛlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈbɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine polychaete worms that burrow or form tubes, usually having long, thick bodies and numerous tentacles
family of marine burrowing polychaete worms

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Terebellidae during their deep-sea expedition.
The Terebellidae construct tubes using sand grains on the ocean floor.
Many Terebellidae are recognized by their numerous, filamentous tentacles.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Terebella, from Latin 'terebella' (little borer, diminutive of 'terebra,' borer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈbɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈbɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine burrowing or tube-forming polychaete worms, typically with long, thick bodies and numerous tentacles
family of marine polychaete worms

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species within the Terebellidae during the deep-sea expedition.
The Terebellidae are known for their long, tentacle-like structures used to collect food particles from the sediment.
Many members of the Terebellidae family build tubes from bits of shells and sand on the ocean floor.

Etymology

From New Latin Terebellidae, based on genus Terebella and suffix -idae (family).
