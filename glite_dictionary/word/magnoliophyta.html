Magnoliophyta

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / maɡˌnəʊlɪəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A large division of plants comprising all flowering plants that produce seeds enclosed within an ovary, also known as angiosperms.
division of flowering plants
medical

Examples

Magnoliophyta includes all modern flowering plants, from grasses to oaks.
Botanists classify roses and lilies within the division Magnoliophyta.
Understanding Magnoliophyta is essential for the study of plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angiospermae - class of flowering plants with seeds enclosed in an ovary
  • Antonym: Gymnospermae - class of seed plants with unenclosed seeds

Etymology

From Modern Latin Magnoliophyta, named after the genus Magnolia + Greek phyton (plant).
