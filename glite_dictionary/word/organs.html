organs

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡənz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənz / (BrE)
the edible internal parts of a butchered animal, such as the liver, heart, or kidneys, often used as food
edible animal internal parts (as food)

Examples

Some traditional dishes include fried organs seasoned with herbs and spices.
The chef explained how to prepare different organs for a rich and hearty stew.
Markets in the region sell fresh organs alongside other cuts of meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offal - the entrails and internal organs of an animal used as food
