darkening

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming darker in color or less bright
becoming darker in color

Examples

The gradual darkening of the sky signaled that a storm was approaching.
Frequent use of the fireplace led to a noticeable darkening of the walls above it.
Exposure to sunlight can cause the darkening of some fabrics over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightening - the process of becoming lighter in color

Etymology

From the verb 'darken' + -ing, originally from Old English 'deorc' (dark).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming dark or darker, especially as light fades or clouds gather
becoming dark or darker

Examples

As evening approached, the darkening clouds threatened to bring rain.
She could barely make out the path in the darkening forest.
We hurried home under the darkening sky.

Synset Examples

the darkening sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to darken - to become or make dark
  • Antonym: lightening - becoming lighter or brighter

Etymology

From the verb 'darken' + -ing, originally from Old English 'deorc' (dark).
