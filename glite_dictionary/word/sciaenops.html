Sciaenops

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænɒps / (AmE), / saɪˈiːənɒps / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, which includes the red drum, also known as the channel bass or redfish.
genus of fish (includes red drum)

Examples

The genus Sciaenops contains only one species, the red drum.
Marine biologists studied Sciaenops populations along the Gulf Coast.
Sciaenops is often referenced in scientific literature about North American fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red drum - a large game fish found in the Atlantic Ocean, member of genus Sciaenops

Etymology

From Greek 'skia', meaning 'shadow', and 'ops', meaning 'face' or 'appearance'.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːnɑps / (AmE), / saɪˈiːnɒps / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Sciaenidae, known especially for the red drum or redfish.
a fish genus (red drum)

Examples

The Sciaenops genus includes species popular among saltwater anglers.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sciaenops in coastal estuaries.
The red drum, a member of the Sciaenops genus, is prized for its size and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red drum - a large, reddish fish native to the Atlantic coast of North America, species within Sciaenops

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Sciaena (a related fish genus) and Greek ōps (appearance).
