Esq

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛs.kaɪə / (BrE)
A title of respect placed after a man's surname, originally for members of the English gentry ranking below a knight, now used chiefly for lawyers in the United States.
title of respect; especially for lawyers or gentry

Examples

John Smith, Esq., will represent the company in all legal matters.
She sent the invitation to Henry Carter, Esq., at his law office.
In formal correspondence, the title Esq. is often used after the recipient’s name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esquire - a title of respect for men of higher social rank, abbreviated as Esq

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'esquire', from Old French 'escuier', from Latin 'scutarius' (shield-bearer).
