Ophiodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊfiəˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈɒfiədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Ophiodontidae, including the lingcod.
genus of marine fish (Ophiodontidae)

Examples

The Ophiodon is well known among Pacific coast anglers for its aggressive behavior.
Scientists studied several species within the genus Ophiodon to learn more about their evolution.
Fishermen often target Ophiodon for its firm, white flesh, which is popular in local cuisine.

Etymology

From Greek ophis (snake) + odous, odontos (tooth), referring to the fish's toothy jaws.

A genus of marine fish in the family Ophiodontidae, including the lingcod.
a genus of marine fish

Examples

The Ophiodon genus includes the well-known lingcod found off the Pacific coast.
Scientists are studying the evolution of Ophiodon species to understand their ecological role.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to its snake-like teeth.
