paracosm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əˌkɑz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpær.əˌkɒz.əm / (BrE)
an imaginary world, often with detailed geography, language, and history, typically invented by a child or group of children over a long period
elaborate imaginary world, often from childhood

Examples

As a child, Sara created an elaborate paracosm complete with its own maps and languages.
His stories are inspired by the paracosm he shared with his siblings throughout their childhood.
Many famous authors began by inventing a secret paracosm before writing their first books.

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + 'kosmos' (world, order).
