scarp

1. NOUN

/ skɑrp / (AmE), / skɑːp / (BrE)
a steep artificial slope or wall in front of a fortification, serving as a defensive barrier
steep slope in front of a fortification

Examples

The defenders took position behind the scarp to repel the assault.
Engineers constructed a new scarp to strengthen the old fortress.
The scarp made direct attacks on the walls nearly impossible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escarpment - a long, steep slope at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights

Etymology

From Italian 'scarpa' (slope, escarpment); also related to French 'escarpe'.

2. NOUN

/ skɑrp / (AmE), / skɑːp / (BrE)
a long, steep slope or cliff at the edge of a plateau or ridge, often formed by erosion
steep slope or cliff at a plateau's edge

Examples

The southern edge of the plateau falls away in a dramatic scarp.
As we hiked, we reached the top of the scarp and looked down at the valley below.
The village sits at the base of a natural scarp created by centuries of erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escarpment - steep slope or long cliff formed by erosion or faulting

Etymology

From Italian 'scarpa' (slope, escarpment), possibly of Germanic origin.
