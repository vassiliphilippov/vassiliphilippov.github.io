Campephilus principalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpɪˈfaɪləs prɪnˈsɪpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌkam.pɛˈfiː.ləs prɪnˈsɪ.pə.lɪs / (BrE)
a large black-and-white woodpecker native to the southern United States and Cuba, known for its ivory-colored bill and thought to be nearly extinct
large, nearly extinct woodpecker species

Examples

For decades, the Campephilus principalis was thought to be extinct in North America.
The Campephilus principalis was once a remarkable sight in southern U.S. forests.
Conservationists continue to search for evidence of living Campephilus principalis in remote swampy regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory-billed woodpecker - common name of Campephilus principalis, a large woodpecker species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin scientific binomial: 'Campephilus' (genus name) and 'principalis' (Latin for 'princely' or 'chief').
