espadrille

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛspəˈdrɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛspəˈdrɪl / (BrE)
A light sandal with a sole made of rope or rubber and an upper part made of cloth.
rope- or rubber-soled sandal with cloth upper

Examples

She wore her favorite espadrilles to the beach party.
The shop window displayed colorful espadrilles for the summer season.
He prefers espadrilles for their comfort and casual style.

Etymology

From French espadrille, from Occitan espardilho, from espardenya (a type of shoe), from espart (a kind of grass used to make rope).
