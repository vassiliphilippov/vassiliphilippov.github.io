Walkman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkmən / (BrE)
(trademark) A portable cassette or compact disc player with lightweight headphones, designed to be carried while walking.
portable personal stereo device

Examples

She listened to her favorite band on her Walkman while jogging every morning.
During the 1980s, owning a Walkman was a symbol of coolness among teenagers.
He found his old Walkman in the attic, along with a box of cassette tapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal stereo - a portable audio player for individual use

Etymology

From Sony's trademark, formed from 'walk' and the suffix '-man', first introduced in 1979.
