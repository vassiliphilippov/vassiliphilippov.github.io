mermaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.meɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.meɪd / (BrE)
A mythical sea creature with the upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish.
mythical half-woman, half-fish sea creature

Examples

The sailor claimed to have seen a mermaid sitting on a rock by the shore.
Stories of mermaids have fascinated people for centuries.
A beautiful mermaid appeared in his dreams, calling him to the sea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: merman - mythical sea creature with the upper body of a man and the tail of a fish

Etymology

From Old English 'mere' (sea) + 'maid' (woman).
