marihuana

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæriˈhwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈwɑːnə / (BrE)
a plant whose dried leaves and flowers are smoked or consumed, often for their psychoactive effects or medicinal benefits
plant smoked for psychoactive effect

Examples

Medical use of marihuana remains controversial in many countries.
They discussed the legalization of marihuana during the debate.
The strong scent of marihuana was detectable in the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - common alternative spelling of 'marihuana'

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'marihuana', possibly of Nahuatl origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrɪˈhwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈhwɑːnə / (BrE)
A variant spelling of 'marijuana': the dried leaves and flowers of the hemp plant, used as a psychoactive drug, especially for recreational or medicinal purposes.
variant spelling of 'marijuana'; psychoactive hemp drug

Examples

The use of marihuana for medicinal purposes is permitted in some countries.
He was arrested for possession of marihuana at the airport.
The debate about legalizing marihuana continues in many states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - the dried leaves and flowers of the hemp plant, used as a drug

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'marihuana', variant of 'marijuana'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02953127-n
Synset POS: n
