Myxobacteriales

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksəˌbæktɪˈɛərliˌiz / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊˌbæktɪˈeəliaɪz / (BrE)
An order of higher bacteria in the class Deltaproteobacteria, known for complex social behavior and production of fruiting bodies.
order of higher bacteria
medical

Examples

Myxobacteriales are studied for their unique gliding motility and ability to form fruiting bodies.
Scientists have discovered new antibiotics produced by members of the Myxobacteriales order.
The complex social behavior of Myxobacteriales distinguishes them from many other bacteria.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'myxo-' (mucus) and 'bacterium' (bacterium), with '-ales' (order).
