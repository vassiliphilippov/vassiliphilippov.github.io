marasca

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːskə / (AmE), / məˈrɑːskə / (BrE)
A variety of sour cherry tree (Prunus cerasus marasca) native to the Dalmatian region, valued for its bitter fruit used in making maraschino liqueur.
Dalmatian sour cherry for maraschino liqueur

Examples

The famous maraschino liqueur is traditionally made from the marasca cherry.
Farmers in coastal Croatia cultivate marasca trees for their prized fruit.
The bitter flavor of the marasca distinguishes it from other cherry varieties.

Etymology

From Italian 'marasca', from 'amarasca' (a bitter cherry), related to 'amaro' meaning 'bitter'.

2. NOUN

/ məˈræskə / (AmE), / məˈræskə / (BrE)
a small, bitter cherry of a southern European variety, used especially to make maraschino liqueur
type of bitter cherry used for liqueur

Examples

The famous maraschino liqueur is traditionally produced from the juice of the marasca cherry.
She tasted a marasca for the first time and found it surprisingly tart.
Maraschino is made using both the fruit and the pits of the marasca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maraschino - a sweet liqueur made from marasca cherries

Etymology

From Italian marasca, from marasco (bitter cherry).

3. NOUN

/ məˈræskə / (AmE), / məˈrɑːskə / (BrE)
A Dalmatian wild cherry tree (Prunus cerasus var. marasca) with bitter fruit used in making maraschino liqueur.
Dalmatian wild cherry tree; source of maraschino liqueur

Examples

The famous maraschino liqueur is made from the bitter fruit of the marasca cherry tree.
Growers in Dalmatia take pride in cultivating the rare marasca for its unique flavor.
The marasca is prized for its small, tart cherries used in confections and spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maraschino - A sweet liqueur made from marasca cherries

Etymology

From Italian 'marasca', from 'amarasca', relating to bitterness ('amaro' meaning 'bitter').
