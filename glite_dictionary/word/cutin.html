cutin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːtɪn / (AmE), / ˈkjuːtɪn / (BrE)
A waxy, transparent substance found in the cuticle of plants, consisting of highly polymerized esters of fatty acids.
waxy plant cuticle substance
medical

Examples

The protective layer on the leaf surface is made of cutin and waxes.
Researchers studied how cutin helps plants retain moisture in arid environments.
A buildup of cutin can affect the permeability of plant tissues.

Etymology

From Latin *cutis* (skin).
