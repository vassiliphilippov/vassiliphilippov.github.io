hodman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːdˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈhɒdˌman / (BrE)
a male laborer who carries bricks, mortar, or other materials to masons or bricklayers on a building site
a laborer who carries building materials

Examples

The hodman carried bricks up the scaffolding all morning.
It takes skill and strength to be a good hodman on a busy construction site.
As a young man, he worked as a hodman before training to be a bricklayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laborer - a person performing unskilled manual work

Etymology

From 'hod' (a V-shaped trough for carrying loads) + 'man'.
