Pleuronectiformes

1. NOUN

/ plʊˌrɒnɛktəˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌplʊərənɛktɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of flatfishes that includes halibut, sole, flounder, plaice, turbot, and tonguefishes
an order of flatfishes

Examples

Many commercially important fish, such as halibut and sole, belong to the Pleuronectiformes.
Scientists study the anatomy of Pleuronectiformes to understand how flatfishes evolved.
The unique eye placement in Pleuronectiformes sets them apart from other fish orders.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) + 'nēktēs' (swimmer) + -formes (having the form of).
