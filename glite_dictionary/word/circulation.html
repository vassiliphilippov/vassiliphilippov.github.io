circulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜɹ.kjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the number of copies of a newspaper or magazine that are sold or distributed regularly
total number of sold/distributed copies (media)

Examples

The magazine doubled its circulation after launching an online edition.
Higher circulation often attracts more advertisers to a newspaper.
Declining circulation forced the business section to reduce its pages.

Synset Examples

by increasing its circulation the newspaper hoped to increase its advertising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: readership - the number or group of people who read a publication

Etymology

From Latin circulatio, from circulare (to move in a circle), referring metaphorically to distribution.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.kjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Library science) The number of books loaned by a library during a particular period.
number of books loaned by a library

Examples

The library's circulation increased significantly after it started offering digital books.
Annual reports often highlight trends in circulation to demonstrate the library's community impact.
A decline in circulation prompted the library to launch new engagement programs.

Etymology

From Latin 'circulatio' (a moving in a circle), adapted in English to mean the movement or distribution of things such as books.

Examples
