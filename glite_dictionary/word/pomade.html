pomade

1. NOUN

/ pəˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒmeɪd / (BrE)
a scented ointment or oil used for styling hair and making it look shiny
scented hair ointment or oil

Examples

He used a little pomade to slick back his hair before the meeting.
The old-fashioned barber offered me some pomade to give my hair more shine.
She prefers pomade over hair gel for a softer, glossier finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair gel - a styling product that holds hair in place

Etymology

From French 'pommade', from Italian 'pomata', from 'pomo' (apple), because apple was once an ingredient.

2. VERB

/ poʊˈmeɪd / (AmE), / pɒˈmɑːd / (BrE)
to apply pomade to the hair, typically to make it smooth or shiny
apply pomade to hair

Examples

He carefully pomaded his hair before the interview to give it a sleek appearance.
She always pomades her hair to maintain that classic, glossy style.
After washing his hair, he pomaded it to keep the curls in place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grease - to apply greasy or oily substance to (hair or machinery)
  • Antonym: wash - to clean or remove substances from (the hair)

Etymology

From the noun 'pomade', ultimately from French 'pommade' (ointment), from Italian 'pomata', from 'pomo' (apple), referring to the original scenting agent.
