ventral

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated on or near the belly or the front of the body, especially in animals and humans.
on or near the belly/front side
medical

Examples

The fish has a lighter color on its ventral side to help it blend with the sunlight above.
In humans, the navel is located on the ventral surface of the body.
In comparative anatomy, scientists use the term ventral to describe features on the underside of animals.

Synset Examples

the ventral aspect of the human body
the liver is somewhat ventral in position
ventral (or pelvic) fins correspond to the hind limbs of a quadruped

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dorsal - relating to the back or upper side

Etymology

From Latin 'venter' meaning 'belly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl / (BrE)
relating to the underside or belly side of an animal or organ; situated toward or on the lower or front part of the body.
of the underside or belly side
medical

Examples

The ventral surface of the fish is usually lighter in color than its dorsal side.
Examination of the ventral side of the leaf revealed tiny hairs.
Certain injuries can damage the ventral roots of spinal nerves, affecting muscle movement.

Synset Examples

the upper side of a leaf is known as the adaxial surface

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dorsal - relating to the back or upper side of an animal or organ

Etymology

From Latin 'venter' (belly).
