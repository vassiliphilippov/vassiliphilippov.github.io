granduncle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænˌʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrandˌʌŋkl / (BrE)
The brother of one's grandparent; an uncle of one's father or mother.
uncle of one's father or mother

Examples

My granduncle told me stories about his childhood in Italy.
We visited my granduncle during our summer vacation last year.
Her granduncle served in the navy during World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great-uncle - the brother of one's grandparent

Etymology

Formed from 'grand' (meaning 'a generation above') and 'uncle'; modeled after 'grandfather'.
