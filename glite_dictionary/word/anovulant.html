anovulant

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.oʊˈvjuː.lənt / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈvjuː.lənt / (BrE)
a contraceptive pill containing estrogen and progestin that inhibits ovulation to prevent pregnancy
contraceptive pill inhibiting ovulation
medical

Examples

Many women rely on the anovulant as their preferred method of birth control.
Your doctor may prescribe an anovulant to regulate your menstrual cycle.
The pharmacist explained how to properly take the anovulant for maximum effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraceptive - a device or drug preventing pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin 'an-' (not) + 'ovulatio' (ovulation), referring to suppression of ovulation.
