blastosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstəˌsfɪə / (BrE)
An early stage of an embryo, consisting of a hollow, fluid-filled sphere of cells formed by cleavage of a fertilized ovum, usually occurring before implantation in the uterus.
early embryonic stage; hollow cell sphere
medical

Examples

At the blastosphere stage, the embryo is composed of a single layer of cells surrounding a cavity.
The formation of the blastosphere represents the first major structural change after fertilization.
Researchers studied the development of the blastosphere in order to better understand early embryogenesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastula - early embryo formed as a hollow ball of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, bud) + 'sphere' (globe, ball).
