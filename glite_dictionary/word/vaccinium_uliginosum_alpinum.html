Vaccinium uliginosum alpinum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm juˌlɪdʒəˈnoʊsəm ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm juˌlɪdʒɪˈnəʊsəm ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A variety of bog bilberry, an evergreen shrub with leathery leaves found in alpine and subarctic regions.
an alpine variety of bog bilberry shrub

Examples

The Vaccinium uliginosum alpinum thrives in the moist, acidic soils of mountain valleys.
Scientists observed that Vaccinium uliginosum alpinum provides food for wildlife in high-altitude regions.
Hikers in the Alps often encounter Vaccinium uliginosum alpinum along rocky trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bog bilberry - a shrub of the species Vaccinium uliginosum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vaccinium' (a genus of shrubs including bilberries and blueberries), 'uliginosum' (growing in wet places), 'alpinum' (of the Alps or high mountains).
