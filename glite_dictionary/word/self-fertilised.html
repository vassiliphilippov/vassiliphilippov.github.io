self-fertilised

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈfɝːtəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈfɜːtɪlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a flower or plant) fertilized by its own pollen rather than pollen from another individual of the same species.
fertilized by its own pollen

Examples

The pea plants in Mendel's experiments were initially self-fertilised for several generations.
Some species of flowers are naturally self-fertilised, which helps them reproduce in the absence of pollinators.
Researchers observed that the self-fertilised plants showed less genetic diversity compared to cross-fertilised ones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-fertilised - fertilized by pollen from another individual; the opposite process

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'fertilised', meaning fertilized by itself.
