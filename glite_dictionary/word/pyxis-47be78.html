pyxis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪksɪs / (BrE)
a small cylindrical box with a lid, used in ancient Greece to hold medicines, cosmetics, or jewelry
small ancient Greek box

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully decorated pyxis from the fifth century BCE.
Archaeologists discovered a pyxis containing ancient medical herbs at the site.
This pyxis is made of ivory and features intricate carvings on its lid.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyxis' (box), from 'pyxos' (box-tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪksɪs / (BrE)
A type of dry fruit capsule, found in some plants, that releases its seeds when the top part falls off.
a fruit capsule that opens by the top falling off

Examples

In some species, the seeds are dispersed when the pyxis opens and its lid drops away.
The pyxis is easily recognized by its characteristic capsule structure resembling a box with a removable lid.
Botanists use the term pyxis to describe fruit in which the top separates to release seeds.

Etymology

From Latin *pyxis* (small box), from Greek *pyxís* (box).
