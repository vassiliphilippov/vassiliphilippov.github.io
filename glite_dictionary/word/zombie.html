2. NOUN

/ ˈzɑːm.bi / (AmE), / ˈzɒm.bi / (BrE)
A person who acts or responds in a mechanical, apathetic, or emotionless way, as if lacking consciousness.
person acting mechanically or apathetically

Examples

After working late, I felt like a zombie during the morning meeting.
She stared at the screen like a zombie, barely reacting to anything around her.
He moved through his chores as if he were a zombie, showing no sign of interest.

Synset Examples

only an automaton wouldn't have noticed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automaton - a person who acts in a robotic or mechanical fashion

Etymology

From Haitian Creole 'zonbi', referring to a reanimated corpse in voodoo folklore; figurative use for apathetic person is 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈzɑːm.bi / (AmE), / ˈzɒm.bi / (BrE)
A deity or spirit in voodoo cults of African origin, especially as worshipped in the West Indies.
a voodoo deity or spirit

Examples

In certain communities, a zombie is invoked during important ceremonies for spiritual guidance.
The myths describe a zombie as a powerful deity that protects the village.
Anthropologists have documented rituals dedicated to the zombie in remote parts of the West Indies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loa - a spirit or deity in Haitian voodoo
  • Degree Lower: zombie - a reanimated corpse in popular culture or folklore

Etymology

From West African Kongo or Kikongo *nzambi* (god, spirit), via Haitian Creole and West Indian usage.

A dead body that has been brought back to life by supernatural means, especially in stories or folklore.
reanimated dead body (in folklore)

Examples

  • Synonym: ghoul - Evil spirit or phantom, especially one supposed to rob graves and feed on corpses
  • Degree Lower: corpse - A dead body, usually of a human but not reanimated

From West African Kongo *zumbi* or Kikongo *nzambi* (god, spirit of a dead person), entering English via Haitian Creole.

5. NOUN

/ ˈzɑːm.bi / (AmE), / ˈzɒm.bi / (BrE)
(in voodoo) a corpse said to be revived by a supernatural force or spirit, especially in Haitian folklore.
reanimated corpse in voodoo belief

Examples

According to voodoo belief, a zombie is a dead person brought back to life by magic.
The villagers feared the arrival of a zombie in their community.
Legends describe how a sorcerer could use a spirit to create a zombie from a corpse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghoul - an evil spirit or phantom associated with graveyards
  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person
  • Synonym: corpse - a dead body, usually of a human

Etymology

From West African Kongo (nzambi: god or spirit), through Haitian Creole and Louisiana Creole French.
