gob

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑb / (AmE), / ɡɒb / (BrE)
(informal) the mouth, especially as used in speaking or eating.
informal term for mouth
informal british

Examples

He told me to shut my gob and listen.
She stuffed the sandwich into her gob before I could say anything.
If you keep running your gob like that, you'll get in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the opening in the face used for eating and speaking

Etymology

Probably of Celtic origin; related to Irish and Scottish Gaelic 'gob' (beak, mouth).

/ ɡɑb / (AmE), / ɡɒb / (BrE)

lump of slimy stuff
informal

Examples

A thick gob of glue oozed out from under the paper.
She wiped a gob of jam off her chin with a napkin.

3. NOUN

/ ɡɑb / (AmE), / ɡɒb / (BrE)
(slang, dated) A man who serves as a sailor, especially in the United States Navy.
sailor (slang, dated)
slang archaic american

Examples

My grandfather was a gob in the navy during World War II.
The salty old gob told sea stories that made us laugh all night.
Several gobs gathered at the dock to await their orders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Scottish 'gob' (mouth) or from the sense 'lump, portion', popularized among U.S. Navy personnel in the early 20th century.
