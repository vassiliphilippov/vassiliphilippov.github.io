forgotten

1. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɑtn / (AmE), / fəˈɡɒtn / (BrE)
not noticed, remembered, or attended to; neglected or overlooked unintentionally
unintentionally neglected or overlooked

Examples

The important message lay forgotten on the kitchen table all day.
Among the numerous uncompleted tasks, a few forgotten ones caused unexpected problems.
She felt like a forgotten guest at the crowded party.

Synset Examples

her aching muscles forgotten she danced all night
he was scolded for his forgotten chores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglected - not given proper attention or care
  • Antonym: remembered - kept in mind or not forgotten

Etymology

Past participle of 'forget', from Old English 'forgietan'.
