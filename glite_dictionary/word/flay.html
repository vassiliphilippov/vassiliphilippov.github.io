to flay

1. VERB

/ fleɪ / (AmE), / fleɪ / (BrE)
to remove the skin from a person or animal, especially as a punishment or when preparing meat
to strip the skin off

Examples

The butcher expertly flayed the animal before cutting it into pieces.
In medieval times, traitors were sometimes flayed as a gruesome punishment.
The chef began flaying the fish in preparation for the evening meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - to remove the skin from an animal or person

Etymology

From Old English *flēan* (to flay), related to Old Norse *flá* (to skin).
