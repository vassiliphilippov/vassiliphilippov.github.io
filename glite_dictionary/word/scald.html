scald

1. NOUN

/ skɔld / (AmE), / skɑːld / (BrE)
A burn caused by hot liquid or steam.
burn from hot liquid or steam

Examples

He suffered a scald on his hand after spilling boiling water.
The nurse treated the child's scald with cooling ointment.
Steam from the kettle can cause a serious scald if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - an injury caused by heat, chemicals, electricity, or radiation

Etymology

From Late Latin *excaldare* (to wash in hot water), from Latin *ex* (out) + *calidus* (hot).

4. VERB

/ skɔld / (AmE), / skɔːld / (BrE)
to treat or immerse (food or other substances) briefly in boiling or very hot water, especially to loosen skin or clean.
treat briefly with boiling water

Examples

To easily remove the skins, scald the tomatoes in boiling water for one minute.
You need to scald the milk before adding it to the recipe.
Some recipes instruct you to scald almonds to help peel them.
