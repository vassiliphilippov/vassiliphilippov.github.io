burden

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdn / (BrE)
The central theme or main idea that is developed in a speech, piece of writing, or discourse.
central theme or main idea in a text

Examples

The burden of his speech was the urgent need for reform.
The novel's burden is the triumph of hope over despair.
Scholars have debated the burden of the poet's message for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - an idea that recurs in or pervades a work of art or literature
  • Synonym: subject - the topic dealt with in a text, discourse, or conversation

Etymology

From Old English *byrthen* (load, responsibility); used figuratively for theme since the 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdən / (BrE)
A heavy load, especially one that is carried physically or endured mentally.
heavy load or weight to be carried

Examples

The donkey struggled with the burden of heavy sacks on its back.
She carried the emotional burden of her past wherever she went.
The bridge is designed to support a great burden of freight traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: load - something that is carried, especially a heavy thing
  • Synonym: weight - a heavy object to be carried or supported
  • Synonym: encumbrance - a thing that impedes or is a burden
  • Antonym: relief - removal or lightening of a burden

Etymology

From Old English *byrthen* (load, weight), related to *beran* (to bear, carry).
