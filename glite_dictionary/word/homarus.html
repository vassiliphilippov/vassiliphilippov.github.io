Homarus

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɛərəs / (AmE), / həʊˈmɑːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of edible lobsters including the common lobster of the North Atlantic.
genus of edible lobsters

Examples

The genus Homarus comprises both the European and American lobsters.
Species within Homarus are highly valued for their culinary use.
Marine biologists study the life cycle of Homarus in coastal ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek hómāros (sea lobster or crayfish).
