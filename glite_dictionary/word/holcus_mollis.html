Holcus mollis

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlkəs ˈmɑlɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɒlkəs ˈmɒlɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial grass with soft, velvety leaves, often found in meadows and pastures.
a soft-leaved European grass species

Examples

Holcus mollis thrives in moist meadows across much of Europe.
Gardeners may consider removing Holcus mollis if it spreads too aggressively.
The soft texture of Holcus mollis makes it easily distinguishable from other grasses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Holcus lanatus - a related species of Holcus, called Yorkshire fog

Etymology

From Latin: Holcus (a genus of grass) + mollis (soft).
