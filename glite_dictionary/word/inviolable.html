inviolable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvaɪ.ə.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈvʌɪ.ələ.bl̩ / (BrE)
Too important or sacred to be broken, violated, or dishonored.
too important to violate; sacred

Examples

The constitution is considered inviolable in many democratic countries.
They treated the ancient forest as an inviolable sanctuary for wildlife.
He regarded the promise as inviolable, refusing to ever break it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrosanct - regarded as too important or valuable to be interfered with
  • Antonym: violable - able to be violated or broken

Etymology

From Latin inviolabilis, from in- (not) + violare (to violate).

4. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɑɪ.ələ.bəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪ.ələ.bəl / (BrE)
that must not be broken, violated, or dishonored; impossible to infringe or trespass upon
not able to be violated or broken

Examples

The constitution guarantees certain inviolable rights to every citizen.
Her trust in him was considered inviolable, no matter what happened.
They treated the ancient rituals as inviolable laws that could never be changed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infrangible - unable to be broken or violated
  • Synonym: sacrosanct - regarded as too important or valuable to be interfered with
  • Antonym: violable - capable of being violated or broken

Etymology

From Latin 'inviolabilis' ('not able to be violated'), from 'in-' (not) + 'violabilis' (that may be injured).
