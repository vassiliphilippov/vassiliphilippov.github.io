auger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːɡər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡə / (BrE)
a hand tool with a helical bit used for boring holes, especially in wood.
tool for boring holes

Examples

He used an auger to drill holes in the wooden beam for the pegs.
Before electric tools, carpenters often relied on an auger to create openings for dowels.
The archaeologist discovered an old auger among the ancient ship's remains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - tool for making holes, often using a rotating motion

Etymology

Old English *næġel* (nail) and later influenced by Latin *augere* (to increase), ultimately from Middle English *nauger* (mistakenly divided as 'an auger').

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡə / (BrE)
a long, flexible tool usually made of steel, used for clearing blockages in curved pipes
tool for clearing pipe blockages

Examples

The plumber used an auger to remove the clog from the kitchen sink drain.
She found the old pipe auger in the garage and used it to fix the bathroom drain.
An auger is essential when dealing with stubborn blockages in curved plumbing pipes.

Etymology

From Old English *augur*, related to Latin *augurium* (divination), but modern tool sense developed independently.
