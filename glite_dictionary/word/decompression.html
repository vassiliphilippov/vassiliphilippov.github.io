decompression

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring compressed digital data to its original or usable form
restoring compressed data to original form

Examples

The software handles the decompression of large files quickly and efficiently.
After the download, decompression is required before you can access the documents.
Advances in decompression algorithms have improved the quality of video streaming.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compression - reducing the size of data by encoding information more efficiently

Etymology

Formed from 'de-' (reverse) and 'compression' (from Latin 'comprimere' meaning 'to press together').

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of relieving or reducing pressure, especially by returning a compressed person or object gradually to normal atmospheric pressure
relieving or reducing pressure (medical/technical)

Examples

Divers must ascend slowly to avoid decompression sickness caused by a sudden reduction in pressure.
The crew underwent decompression after spending hours in the hyperbaric chamber.
A controlled decompression is crucial when treating patients with severe altitude sickness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compression - the act of pressing something into a smaller space or volume

Etymology

From de- (removal) + compression, patterned after French décompression.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00358277-n
Synset POS: n
