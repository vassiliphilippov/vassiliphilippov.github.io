Vaccinium membranaceum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˌmɛm.brəˈneɪ.si.əm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪn.i.əm ˌmɛm.brəˈneɪ.si.əm / (BrE)
a species of blueberry shrub native to the western United States, noted for its solitary flowers and somewhat sour berries
western US blueberry species

Examples

The Vaccinium membranaceum thrives in the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.
Bears love to eat the berries of Vaccinium membranaceum in late summer.
Researchers studied the abundance of Vaccinium membranaceum to understand the local ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - common name for several berries closely related to Vaccinium membranaceum

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinium' (blueberry) and 'membranaceus' (membranous), used in botanical naming.
