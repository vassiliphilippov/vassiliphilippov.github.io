syntax

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn.tæks / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.tæks / (BrE)
A systematic and orderly arrangement, especially of elements or parts within a system.
systematic arrangement

Examples

The syntax of the machine's wiring ensured efficient power distribution.
Urban designers studied the syntax of city blocks to improve traffic flow.
The artist appreciated the syntax of shapes in modern sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement or organization of parts to form an entity

Etymology

From Greek 'syntaxis' (arrangement), from 'syn-' (together) + 'tassein' (to arrange).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnˌtæks / (AmE), / ˈsɪntæks / (BrE)
The set of rules and principles that govern the structure of sentences in a language, specifically the correct arrangement of words and phrases.
rules of sentence structure

Examples

The essay shows a strong command of English syntax and clear sentence structure.
Children learning a new language often struggle with proper syntax at first.
Computer programming languages also follow strict rules of syntax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grammar - the system and structure of a language, including syntax and morphology

Etymology

From Greek suntaxis (arrangement), from sun- (together) + tassein (arrange).
