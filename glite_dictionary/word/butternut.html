butternut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ərˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənʌt / (BrE)
the edible, oily, egg-shaped nut of the North American white walnut tree (Juglans cinerea)
edible nut of the white walnut tree

Examples

The old tree in our yard produces large butternuts every autumn.
She made a delicious pie using crushed butternut kernels.
Wildlife enjoy eating butternut as a natural food source in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white walnut - the North American tree (Juglans cinerea) producing the butternut

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ərˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənʌt / (BrE)
A North American walnut tree (Juglans cinerea) with light-brown wood and edible nuts, also known as white walnut; the nut or wood from this tree.
North American walnut tree; its nut or wood

Examples

The butternut tree produces nuts that are valued for their rich, oily flavor.
Old furniture made from butternut has a distinct pale-brown color.
Early settlers used butternut bark to produce a natural dye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - a nut from a tree in the genus Juglans or the tree itself

Etymology

Named for the buttery texture and nutty taste of its edible seed.
