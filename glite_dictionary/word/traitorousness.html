traitorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪtərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪtərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being disloyal or treacherous to one’s country, friends, or cause.
the quality of being traitorous or disloyal

Examples

The general’s traitorousness shocked the entire nation.
He was executed for his traitorousness during the war.
Her traitorousness was eventually discovered by her closest allies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disloyalty - lack of loyalty; unfaithfulness
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal; faithfulness

Etymology

From 'traitorous' + '-ness'; 'traitorous' from Old French 'traitreus', based on Latin 'traditor' (betrayer).
