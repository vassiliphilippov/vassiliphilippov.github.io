penetratingly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪ.tɪŋ.li / (AmE), / ˈpen.ɪ.treɪ.tɪŋ.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows deep understanding, insight, or the ability to perceive beyond the surface.
with deep insight or perception

Examples

She looked at him penetratingly, as if trying to discern his true intentions.
The critic wrote penetratingly about the novel's underlying themes.
He questioned her penetratingly during the interview, leaving no topic untouched.

Synset Examples

the author treats his subject penetratingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insightfully - in a way that shows deep understanding

Etymology

From 'penetrating' + '-ly'. Ultimately from Latin 'penetratus', past participle of 'penetrare' (to enter, pierce).
