falseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔls.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːls.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being untruthful, deceitful, or hypocritical
quality of being deceitful or untruthful

Examples

She could sense the falseness in his compliments and chose not to trust him.
The falseness of the witness's testimony was soon discovered by the jury.
Despite his friendly smile, there was a certain falseness in his words that made her uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty; tendency to lie or deceive
  • Antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being honest and telling the truth

Etymology

From Old English 'fals' (false) + '-ness'.

Examples

The novel explores themes of loyalty and falseness in human relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithfulness - quality of being loyal and trustworthy

the quality or state of being false, dishonest, or untrue

Examples
