Erolia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊliə / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊliə / (BrE)
a genus of small wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, including some stints and sandpipers
genus of small wading birds

Examples

The bird identified as Erolia minutilla is commonly known as the least sandpiper.
Many taxonomists debate whether Erolia should be merged with the genus Calidris.
Several species in the genus Erolia migrate over long distances each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calidris - genus of sandpipers closely related to Erolia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used in zoological nomenclature.

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈroʊliə / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊliə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, including certain species of stints and sandpipers.
genus of stints and sandpipers

Examples

Many ornithologists debate the classification of birds within the genus Erolia.
The small sandpipers of Erolia are often seen along coastal mudflats.
Recent studies suggest some species previously assigned to Erolia belong in different genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calidris - a related genus of sandpipers
  • Synonym: sandpiper - a wading bird of the family Scolopacidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Erolia*, used in zoological nomenclature.
