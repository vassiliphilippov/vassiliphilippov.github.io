Gregory Nazianzen

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrɛɡəri næziˈænzən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛɡəri næziˈænzən / (BrE)
(c. 329–391) A prominent early Christian theologian and Church Father, known for his influential role in developing the doctrine of the Trinity and opposing heresies.
early Christian theologian and Church Father

Examples

Gregory Nazianzen played a crucial role in shaping Christian theology during the 4th century.
Many of Gregory Nazianzen's sermons are still studied by theologians today.
The influence of Gregory Nazianzen endures in both Eastern and Western Christian traditions.
