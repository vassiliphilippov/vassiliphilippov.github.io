horizontal

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntl̩ / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntl̩ / (BrE)
A horizontal line, surface, or position; something that is parallel to the horizon or level ground.
a horizontal line or position

Examples

Draw a horizontal across the page at the midpoint.
The structure is made of one horizontal and two verticals.
You need to align the text to the horizontal for a balanced layout.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertical - a vertical line or position; oriented up and down

Etymology

From Latin horizontalis, from Greek horizont- (separating, limiting), from horizōn (horizon).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntl̩ / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntl̩ / (BrE)
Parallel to the horizon or to a base line; at right angles to the vertical.
parallel to the horizon

Examples

The table has a perfectly horizontal surface.
Please hang the picture so it is horizontal with the floor.
They drew a thick horizontal line across the whiteboard.

Synset Examples

a horizontal surface

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertical - at right angles to the horizon; upright

Etymology

From Latin horizontalis, from Greek horizont- (limiting), from horizō (to limit, to divide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01235508-a
Synset POS: a
