might-have-been

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪt hæv bɪn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪt həv biːn / (BrE)
an event or outcome that could have happened but did not actually occur
possible event that never happened

Examples

She wondered about the might-have-been of her life if she had chosen a different career.
Sometimes it's hard not to dwell on the might-have-been after a difficult decision.
The novel explores the might-have-been of a love story that was never realized.
