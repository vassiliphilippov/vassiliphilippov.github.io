cytostome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtəˌstoʊm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtəstəʊm / (BrE)
the cellular mouth or specialized opening through which a protozoan takes in food
mouth opening of a protozoan
medical

Examples

The cytostome is essential for the feeding process in many single-celled organisms.
Some protozoa have a well-developed cytostome that is visible under the microscope.
Researchers observed food particles being drawn into the cytostome during ingestion.

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (container, cell) + 'stoma' (mouth).
