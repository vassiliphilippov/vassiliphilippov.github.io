to nonplus

1. VERB

/ nɑnˈplʌs / (AmE), / nɒnˈplʌs / (BrE)
to surprise and confuse someone so much that they are unsure how to react; to bewilder or perplex
bewilder or confuse greatly

Examples

The unexpected question seemed to nonplus the speaker, who paused in confusion.
Her strange behavior did not nonplus him; he acted as if nothing was wrong.
They were nonplussed by the results, unable to explain what had happened.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewilder - to confuse or puzzle completely; perplex
  • Synonym: to perplex - to make someone confused or uncertain
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'non plus' meaning 'no more, no further'.
