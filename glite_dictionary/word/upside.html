upside

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌp.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌp.saɪd / (BrE)
the top or uppermost surface of something.
top or uppermost surface

Examples

The upside of the box had a label stuck on it.
Raindrops collected on the upside of the car roof after the storm.
Dust settled on the upside of the old bookshelf.

Synset Examples

put your books on top of the desk
only the top side of the box was painted

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underside - the lower or bottom side of something

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'side', indicating the side that faces upward.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌp.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌp.saɪd / (BrE)
the positive or beneficial aspect of a situation or decision
positive aspect; benefit

Examples

There is an upside to working late—you might impress your boss.
One upside of city living is the easy access to amenities.
Although the plan has risks, the upside is substantial financial gain.

Synset Examples

The upside to moving is meeting new people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downside - the negative aspect of a situation
  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
