Lesbos

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛzbɑs / (AmE), / ˈlɛzbɒs / (BrE)
a Greek island in the eastern Aegean Sea, historically renowned for its lyric poetry and association with the poet Sappho
Greek island in the eastern Aegean Sea

Examples

Sappho, the famous poet, was born on Lesbos in the seventh century BCE.
Tourists often visit Lesbos for its beautiful beaches and historic sites.
The unique culture of Lesbos has influenced much of Greek literature and music.

Etymology

From ancient Greek Λέσβος (Lésbos), the island's original name.
