Gaylussacia baccata

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˌlʌˈseɪʃə bəˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˌlʌˈseɪsɪə bəˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A low shrub native to eastern North America, bearing shiny black edible berries; commonly known as the black huckleberry.
species of huckleberry shrub

Examples

The Gaylussacia baccata thrives in dry, wooded areas across the eastern United States.
Wildlife rely on the berries of Gaylussacia baccata as a crucial food source in late summer.
Foragers often seek out Gaylussacia baccata for its sweet, edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - a small, round, edible berry and the plant producing it

Etymology

Named after French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac; 'baccata' from Latin for 'berry-bearing'.
