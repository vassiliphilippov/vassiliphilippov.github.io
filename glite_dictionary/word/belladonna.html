belladonna

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛləˈdɑnə / (AmE), / ˌbɛləˈdɒnə / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian herb (Atropa belladonna) with reddish bell-shaped flowers and shiny black berries, whose roots and leaves yield the drug atropine.
poisonous Eurasian plant; source of atropine

Examples

The berries of the belladonna plant are highly poisonous when ingested.
Doctors once used belladonna extracts to dilate patients' pupils for eye examinations.
She carefully identified the wild plant to ensure it was not belladonna before picking the berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly nightshade - another name for belladonna, the poisonous plant

Etymology

From Italian 'bella donna' meaning 'beautiful lady', referring to the plant's historic cosmetic use.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛləˈdɑnə / (AmE), / ˌbɛləˈdɒnə / (BrE)
a poisonous plant (Atropa belladonna) whose extract or tincture, containing alkaloids such as atropine, is used in medicine as a sedative and to relieve muscle spasms
poisonous plant and its medicinal extract
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a belladonna tincture to help alleviate her muscle spasms.
Old herbal remedies sometimes included extracts of belladonna, despite its toxicity.
Researchers are studying how belladonna can be used safely in modern medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly nightshade - another name for the belladonna plant

Etymology

From Italian 'bella donna' (beautiful lady), referring to the historical cosmetic use of the plant.
