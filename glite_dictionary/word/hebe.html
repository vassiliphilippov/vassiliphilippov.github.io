Hebe

1. PROPN

/ ˈhiːbi / (AmE), / ˈhiːbi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of youth and spring, daughter of Zeus and Hera, wife of Hercules, and cupbearer to the Olympian gods.
Greek goddess of youth and spring

Examples

In the story, Hebe serves nectar to the Olympian gods.
Ancient sculptures often depict Hebe as a young woman holding a cup.
The festival commemoration honored Hebe as the bringer of youth and renewal.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἥβη (Hēbē), meaning 'youth' or 'prime of life'.
