idiotically

1. ADV

/ ˌɪdiˈɑtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɪdɪˈɒtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of intelligence or good judgment; foolishly or senselessly.
in a foolish or senseless manner

Examples

The manager responded idiotically to the clear evidence presented by his team.
He idiotically ignored the warning signs and proceeded anyway.
They acted idiotically during the meeting, making everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

what arouses the indignation of the honest satirist is not the fact that people in positions of power or influence behave idiotically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishly - in a way that lacks good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensibly - in a way that shows good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'idiotic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek idiōtikós (unskilled, ignorant).
