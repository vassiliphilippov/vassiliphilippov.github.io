quotation

1. NOUN

/ kwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kwəˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the stated current price at which a security or commodity can be bought or sold on the market
stated current market price

Examples

The trader checked the latest quotation for crude oil before placing an order.
Stock quotations are updated in real time on financial news websites.
The quotation for those bonds increased after the positive earnings report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price - the amount of money expected, required, or given in payment for something

Etymology

From Latin 'quotatio' (a numbering), from 'quotus' (of what number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06768978-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kwəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or practice of quoting words from books, plays, or other sources
the practice of quoting from sources

Examples

The professor encouraged the use of quotation to support our arguments.
He filled his essays with lengthy quotation from famous authors.
Excessive quotation can sometimes make writing seem unoriginal.

Synset Examples

since he lacks originality he must rely on quotation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - the act of quoting or referring to a source

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'quotationem', from 'quotare' (to mark the number of), related to 'quotus' (of what number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01032674-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kwəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a short note giving recognition to the source of information or a quoted passage, especially in scholarly or literary works
note citing a source of information

Examples

In her paper, she included a quotation to properly recognize the original author.
Every academic book should contain a quotation for each referenced fact or idea.
The editor requested more quotation entries to support the main arguments.

Synset Examples

the student's essay failed to list several important citations
the acknowledgments are usually printed at the front of a book
the article includes mention of similar clinical cases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - an act of quoting or referring to a source, especially in scholarly work

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'quotationem', from Latin 'quotare' (to mark the number of), from 'quot' (how many).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06776392-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ kwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kwəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a passage or statement taken from a book, speech, or other source and repeated by someone other than the original author or speaker
a quoted passage or statement

Examples

She included a quotation from Shakespeare at the beginning of her essay.
The book is filled with memorable quotations from famous leaders.
Can you give me a quotation that summarizes the author's main argument?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out a passage or statement from a source

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'quotationem', from Latin 'quotare' (to mark the number of).
