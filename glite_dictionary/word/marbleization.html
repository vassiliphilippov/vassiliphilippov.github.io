marbleization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɑːbələɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the appearance or texture that resembles marble, typically involving veining or mottling.
texture or appearance like marble

Examples

The marbleization of the beef is highly prized by chefs for its tenderness and flavor.
The artist achieved a remarkable marbleization effect on the wooden columns.
The living room's wallpaper mimics the marbleization found in classic Italian palaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marbling - veining or mottling resembling marble, especially in meat or stone

Etymology

From 'marble' + '-ization', denoting the process or result of becoming like marble.
