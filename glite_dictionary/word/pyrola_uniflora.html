Pyrola uniflora

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌroʊlə juːˈnɪflɔːrə / (AmE), / pɪˌrəʊlə juːˈnɪflərə / (BrE)
A delicate evergreen dwarf herb found in northern temperate regions, bearing a single white terminal flower; sometimes classified in the genus Pyrola.
species of dwarf evergreen herb with white flower

Examples

We found several specimens of Pyrola uniflora growing beneath the pine trees.
The white flower of Pyrola uniflora makes it easy to spot in the mossy forest floor.
Botanists documented the distribution of Pyrola uniflora across northern temperate woodlands.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pyrola' (a diminutive of 'pyrus', meaning pear, referring to the leaf shape) and 'uniflora' (uni- 'one' + -flora 'flowered').
