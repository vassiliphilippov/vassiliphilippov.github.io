squib

1. NOUN

/ skwɪb / (AmE), / skwɪb / (BrE)
A small firework consisting of a tube filled with powder that burns with a fizzing noise.
small firework that fizzes

Examples

The children watched as the squib fizzed and popped on the pavement.
After lighting the squib, a trail of sparks shot out into the night.
She was disappointed when the squib failed to explode as expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - a type of small explosive firework

Etymology

Origin uncertain; dates to the 16th century, possibly from earlier forms referring to a short satirical writing or a small explosive.

2. VERB

/ skwɪb / (AmE), / skwɪb / (BrE)
to write a short, often witty or critical, article or commentary, especially in a newspaper or magazine
write a short commentary or article

Examples

Every week, she squibs clever pieces for the university magazine.
The columnist squibbed a satirical article about the mayor's recent proposal.
He enjoys squibbing brief reviews of new restaurants for the food section.

Synset Examples

He wrote a squib for the local newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - to express an opinion or statement about something
  • Synonym: review - to write a critique or evaluation of a work

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'squib', originally meaning a short, sharp piece of writing or satire.
