illusionist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːʒənɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənɪst / (BrE)
a performer who entertains an audience by creating the illusion of magical or supernatural feats, typically using sleight of hand and tricks
a performer of magic tricks

Examples

The famous illusionist amazed the crowd by making a dove appear from an empty hat.
She dreamed of becoming an illusionist and practiced magic tricks in her room for hours.
The illusionist's final act left the audience in stunned silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - a person who performs magic tricks
  • Synonym: conjurer - someone who conjures tricks or illusions
  • Synonym: mentalist - one who performs mental magic or mind-reading tricks

Etymology

From French *illusionniste*, from *illusion* (deceptive appearance).
