neuralgic

1. ADJ

/ nʊˈræl.dʒɪk / (AmE), / njʊəˈræl.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by neuralgia (intense, typically intermittent pain along the course of a nerve)
relating to nerve pain (neuralgia)
medical

Examples

She suffered from neuralgic pain that made daily activities difficult.
Cold weather often triggers neuralgic episodes in patients with facial nerve issues.
Doctors prescribed medication to help manage his neuralgic discomfort.

Synset Examples

neuralgic pains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuralgia - intense pain along a nerve

Etymology

From neuralgia, ultimately from New Latin, combining 'neur-' (nerve) + Greek 'algos' (pain).
