chitin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈkʌɪtɪn / (BrE)
A tough, semitransparent substance that forms the main component of the exoskeletons of arthropods and the cell walls of some fungi.
tough substance in exoskeletons and fungal cell walls

Examples

The shells of crabs and lobsters are primarily composed of chitin.
Certain mushrooms contain chitin in their cell walls, making them more resilient.
Researchers are studying how chitin can be used to produce biodegradable plastics.

Etymology

From French *chitine*, from Greek *chitōn* (covering, tunic).
