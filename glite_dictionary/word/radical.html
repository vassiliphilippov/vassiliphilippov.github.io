radical

1. ADJ

/ ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
(of leaves or plant parts) growing from or situated at the base of a plant or stem, especially arising directly from the root or rootstock.
(botany) at the base of a plant; from the root

Examples

The plant produces a rosette of radical leaves close to the ground.
These herbs are identified by their radical shoots emerging from the rootstock.
The radical flowers appear before the stem elongates in spring.

Synset Examples

basal placentation
radical leaves

Etymology

From Latin radix (root).
