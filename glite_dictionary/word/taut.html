taut

1. ADJ

/ tɔt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
stretched or pulled tightly; not slack
stretched tightly, not slack

Examples

The tent lines were pulled taut to keep the structure stable in the wind.
She stretched the canvas taut over the wooden frame for painting.
His muscles grew taut with anticipation as he prepared to jump.

Synset Examples

taut sails
a tight drumhead
a tight rope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slack - loose or not taut

Etymology

From Middle English "tought" (pulled tight), related to Old Norse "thottr" (tight).

2. ADJ

/ tɔt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
stretched or pulled tight; not slack; subjected to great tension.
stretched tight; under tension

Examples

The rope was pulled so taut that it could almost snap.
Her expression remained taut with anxiety throughout the interview.
He kept the lines taut while sailing to maintain control of the boat.

Synset Examples

the skin of his face looked drawn and tight
her nerves were taut as the strings of a bow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slack - not taut; loose or not tightly held

Etymology

From Old French taut, of uncertain origin.

3. ADJ

/ tɔt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
stretched or pulled tight; showing tension, strain, or unease, especially in muscles or atmosphere.
stretched tight; tense or strained

Examples

The rope was pulled taut between the two posts.
Her voice was taut with emotion during the argument.
After hours of sitting tense and alert, his nerves felt taut.

Synset Examples

The tension in the room was taut and uncomfortable.
His muscles were taut after the intense workout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tight - firmly held or stretched; not slack
  • Antonym: slack - not stretched or held tight; loose

Etymology

From Middle English 'taut' (tight), of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'thōttr' (tight).
