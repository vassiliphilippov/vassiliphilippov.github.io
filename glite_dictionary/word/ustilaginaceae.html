Ustilaginaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.stɪ.læ.dʒɪˈneɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌjuː.stɪ.ladʒ.ɪˈneɪ.siː / (BrE)
A family of fungi known as loose smuts, which contains plant parasitic fungi affecting cereals and grasses.
family of smut fungi affecting plants
medical

Examples

Certain species within the Ustilaginaceae family are responsible for damaging grain crops.
Researchers are studying Ustilaginaceae to find methods of controlling smut diseases in agriculture.
The black, powdery spores produced by Ustilaginaceae can be seen on infected plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ustilago - a genus of smut fungi, type genus of Ustilaginaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Ustilago, from Latin 'ustilare' (to burn, referring to the dark, burnt-like spores).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌstɪˌlædʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌʌstɪˌlædʒɪˈniːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi that includes the loose smuts, parasitic on grasses and cereal crops.
fungus family of loose smuts

Examples

Many crop diseases are caused by fungi belonging to the Ustilaginaceae.
The Ustilaginaceae family is well known among plant pathologists for its economic impact on agriculture.
Several species in the Ustilaginaceae infect wheat and other cereals worldwide.

Etymology

From the genus name Ustilago (type genus), from Latin 'ustilare' meaning 'to burn', referring to the appearance of affected plants.
