blighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈblaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈblaɪtɪd / (BrE)
damaged, destroyed, or spoiled by blight; adversely affected so that growth, development, or prosperity is hindered
damaged or spoiled; affected by blight

Examples

The abandoned building stood in the middle of a blighted neighborhood.
Several crops were lost due to blighted fields after the drought.
The council plans to revitalize the blighted downtown area.

Synset Examples

a blighted rose
blighted urban districts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - destroyed or severely damaged
  • Antonym: thriving - prospering, growing strongly

Etymology

From the past participle of 'blight' (to harm or ruin); 'blight' of uncertain origin.
