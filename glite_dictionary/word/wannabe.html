wannabe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑnəˌbi / (AmE), / ˈwɒnəˌbi / (BrE)
a person who aspires to be someone else or to gain a particular role or status, often without real qualifications or talent
an aspiring or would-be imitator
informal

Examples

The party was crowded with wannabe rock stars hoping to get noticed by a scout.
She dismissed him as just another wannabe with big dreams and little talent.
Many wannabe actors move to Hollywood in search of stardom.

Synset Examples

a lofty aspirant
two executive hopefuls joined the firm
the audience was full of Madonna wannabes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirant - a person who seeks to achieve a particular goal
  • Synonym: hopeful - a person wishing for success in a particular area

Etymology

From 'want to be', popularized in the 1980s to describe those who imitate celebrities or famous figures.
