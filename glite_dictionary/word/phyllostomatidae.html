Phyllostomatidae

1. NOUN

/ fɪlˌoʊstoʊˈmætɪdiː / (AmE), / fɪlˌɒstəʊˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of bats native to the Americas, known as New World leaf-nosed bats, characterized by a fleshy leaf-shaped growth on the nose.
family of New World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

Phyllostomatidae are distinguished by their unique nose leaves and diverse diet.
Many species within the Phyllostomatidae family play key roles in pollination and seed dispersal.
Researchers are studying the echolocation abilities of Phyllostomatidae to better understand bat evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal mammal capable of sustained flight

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the leaf-shaped nose structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪl.oʊˌstoʊ.məˈtɪd.i / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.əʊˌstəʊ.məˈtɪd.iː / (BrE)
A family of New World leaf-nosed bats comprising diverse species found primarily in Central and South America.
family of New World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

Researchers observed several species in the Phyllostomatidae family feeding on fruit and insects.
The Phyllostomatidae have a distinctive leaf-shaped structure on their noses.
Many Phyllostomatidae play important roles in pollination and seed dispersal in tropical forests.

Etymology

From modern Latin *Phyllostomatidae*, based on genus *Phyllostomus* from Greek *phyllon* (leaf) + *stoma* (mouth).
