semirigid

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
Partly but not wholly rigid; having a form maintained by a rigid internal structure as well as by internal gas pressure.
partly rigid; partially kept in shape by internal structure and gas pressure

Examples

The semirigid airship relies on both a metal keel and gas pressure to maintain its shape.
A semirigid structure offers more flexibility than a fully rigid one but greater strength than a flexible design.
Engineers chose a semirigid material for the frame to balance stability and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rigid - not able to be bent; stiff; not flexible

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partly') + rigid (from Latin rigidus, 'stiff').

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛm.iˈrɪdʒ.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛ.mɪˈrɪdʒ.ɪd / (BrE)
partly but not fully rigid; having some stiffness but not completely inflexible.
partly but not fully rigid

Examples

The material was semirigid, allowing it to bend slightly without breaking.
A semirigid splint provides support while still permitting limited movement.
This boat uses a semirigid hull for improved stability and strength.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rigid - completely stiff or inflexible
  • Degree Lower: flexible - easily bent; not stiff

Etymology

From semi- (partly) + rigid (stiff; inflexible).
