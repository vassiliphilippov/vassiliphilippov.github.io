Mikania scandens

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkeɪniə skænˈdɛnz / (AmE), / mɪˈkɑːniə ˈskændɛnz / (BrE)
A climbing herb native to tropical America, known for its vanilla-scented flowers, often growing up trees.
climbing herb with vanilla-scented flowers

Examples

The fence in the botanical garden was covered by the Mikania scandens and its fragrant blossoms.
Gardeners often plant Mikania scandens for its attractive, vanilla-scented flowers.
Entwined with native trees, Mikania scandens created a lush green curtain along the riverbank.

Etymology

From the genus name Mikania, named after the Czech botanist Johann Christian Mikan, and Latin 'scandens' meaning 'climbing'.
