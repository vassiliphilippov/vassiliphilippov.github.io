Micropterus salmoides

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɒptərəs ˌsælˈmɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒptərəs ˌsælˈmɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a species of large black bass, commonly known as the largemouth bass, distinguished by its large mouth extending past the eye
species of largemouth bass

Examples

The Micropterus salmoides thrives in warm, slow-moving waters across North America.
Many anglers consider catching a Micropterus salmoides a true test of skill.
Biologists studied the growth patterns of Micropterus salmoides in various ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: largemouth bass - common name for Micropterus salmoides, a popular freshwater game fish

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'pteron' (fin), and Latin 'salmoides' (resembling a salmon).
