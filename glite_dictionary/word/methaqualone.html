methaqualone

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˈkwɑːloʊn / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəˈkwæləʊn / (BrE)
A sedative-hypnotic drug formerly prescribed as a sleeping pill and now commonly known as a drug of abuse, often referred to by the trade name Quaalude.
a sedative-hypnotic drug; Quaalude
medical

Examples

The abuse of methaqualone became a major social issue in the 1970s.
Doctors no longer prescribe methaqualone due to its high risk of addiction and abuse.
Authorities seized a shipment of illegal methaqualone tablets at the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaalude - the trade name for methaqualone, a sedative-hypnotic drug
  • Degree Higher: barbiturate - a class of sedative-hypnotic drugs similar in use to methaqualone
  • Degree Lower: benzodiazepine - a class of milder sedative-hypnotic drugs

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: 'metha-' (methyl) + 'aqualone', trade name Quaalude.
