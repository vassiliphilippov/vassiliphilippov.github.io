Crotalus cerastes

1. PROPN

/ kroʊˈtæləs səˈræstiz / (AmE), / krəʊˈtæləs səˈræstiːz / (BrE)
A small, pale-colored species of rattlesnake found in the deserts of the southwestern United States, known for moving in a distinctive sidewinding motion.
species of desert rattlesnake

Examples

The Crotalus cerastes is well adapted to the sandy deserts where it lives.
Scientists studied the movement of the Crotalus cerastes to understand its unique sidewinding locomotion.
You can identify a Crotalus cerastes by its pale color and the small horns above its eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewinder - common name for Crotalus cerastes, a desert rattlesnake

Etymology

From Latin; 'Crotalus' from Greek 'krotalon' (castanet/rattle), 'cerastes' from Greek 'kerastēs' (horned).
