unmanageable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanɪdʒəb(ə)l / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to control, direct, or handle.
difficult or impossible to control

Examples

The project became unmanageable as more people got involved.
His hair was so curly that it often seemed unmanageable.
After only a few hours, the crowd had grown unmanageable for the small security team.

Synset Examples

uncontrollable children
an uncorrectable habit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: controllable - able to be controlled
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be managed or controlled
  • Synonym: uncontrollable - impossible to control or restrain

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'manageable' (able to be managed), from manage + -able.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanɪdʒəbəl / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to handle, control, or use, especially because of size, weight, or awkward shape.
hard to handle or control

Examples

The box was so large and unmanageable that it took two people to carry it.
Her hair became wild and unmanageable in the humid weather.
After the snowfall, the car felt heavy and unmanageable on the slippery road.

Synset Examples

we set about towing the unwieldy structure into the shelter
almost dropped the unwieldy parcel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manageable - easy to handle, control, or deal with
  • Synonym: unwieldy - difficult to move or handle because of size or shape

Etymology

From un- (not) + manageable, ultimately from Old French *manegier* (to handle).
