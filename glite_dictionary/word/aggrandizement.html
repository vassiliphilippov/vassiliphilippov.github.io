aggrandizement

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡræn.dɪz.mənt / (AmE), / əˈɡræn.dɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the process of increasing the power, wealth, importance, or reputation of a person or thing, often in an excessive or unjustified way
increase of power, wealth, or importance

Examples

The politician was accused of seeking personal aggrandizement at the expense of the public good.
The aggrandizement of the corporation led to its dominance over smaller competitors.
His memoir focuses on the aggrandizement of his family's name and reputation.

Synset Examples

the aggrandizement of the king
his elevation to cardinal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaltation - the action of elevating someone in rank or power
  • Antonym: diminution - the act of reducing or decreasing the size or importance of something

Etymology

From French 'agrandissement', from 'agrandir' (to make larger).
