chives

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪvz / (AmE), / tʃaɪvz / (BrE)
A perennial herb with slender, hollow leaves, used as a seasoning in cooking.
edible herb used for seasoning

Examples

He sprinkled fresh chives on top of the scrambled eggs.
The soup tasted better after adding a handful of chopped chives.
You can grow chives easily in a kitchen garden window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallion - a type of young onion with a long green stalk
  • Synonym: onion - a bulbous plant used as a vegetable and seasoning

Etymology

From Old French 'cive', from Latin 'cepa' (onion).

2. NOUN

/ tʃaɪvz / (AmE), / tʃaɪvz / (BrE)
the long, thin, green leaves of a small plant of the onion family, used fresh to give a mild onion flavor to food
mild onion-flavored leaves used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh chives over the soup before serving.
Adding chives gives the salad a delicate onion flavor without overpowering it.
You can grow chives in a small pot on your windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallion - a long-necked onion with non-bulbous roots, used as a seasoning
  • Degree Higher: onion - a round vegetable with a strong flavor and smell, related to chives

Etymology

From Old French 'cive', from Latin 'cepa' (onion).
