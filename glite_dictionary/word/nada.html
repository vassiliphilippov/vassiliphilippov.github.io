nada

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑdə / (AmE), / ˈnɑːdə / (BrE)
nothing; not anything; a quantity of no importance.
nothing; zero; none
informal

Examples

After hours of searching, we found nada in the old attic.
Don't worry about the cost—it's nada compared to your peace of mind.
He expected surprises, but there was nada in the envelope.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothing - no thing; not anything
  • Synonym: zilch - nothing; zero (slang)
  • Antonym: something - a thing that is unspecified or unknown

Etymology

From Spanish 'nada' (nothing), ultimately from Latin 'nata' (small thing).
