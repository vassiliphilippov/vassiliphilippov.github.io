die-hard

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪ hɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ hɑːd / (BrE)
resisting change or new ideas very strongly; stubbornly conservative or traditionalist
very resistant to change; strongly traditionalist

Examples

He remained a die-hard supporter of the old regime even after the reforms.
Many die-hard fans camped outside the stadium for days to get tickets.
Despite changing times, she held onto her die-hard beliefs about tradition.

Synset Examples

an inflexible (or die-hard) conservative
rock-ribbed republican

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflexible - not willing to change or compromise
  • Synonym: hardline - strictly adhering to a set of beliefs or policies
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or promoting change or innovation

Etymology

Originally from a 19th-century British military command urging soldiers to fight until death; later adopted figuratively.
