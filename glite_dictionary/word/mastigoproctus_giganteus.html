Mastigoproctus giganteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪgoʊˈproʊktəs dʒɪˈgænˌtiəs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪgəʊˈprɒktəs dʒɪˈgæntiəs / (BrE)
a large species of whip-scorpion native to Mexico and the southern United States, known for emitting a vinegary odor when alarmed
large whip-scorpion species emitting vinegar-like odor

Examples

The Mastigoproctus giganteus can spray a vinegary liquid as a defense mechanism.
Biologists studied the habitat of Mastigoproctus giganteus in the deserts of Texas.
Many people mistake the Mastigoproctus giganteus for a true scorpion, though it is harmless to humans.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mastix' (whip) + 'proctus' (anus) + 'giganteus' (giant), used in taxonomy.
