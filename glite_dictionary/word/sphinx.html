sphinx

1. NOUN

/ sfɪŋks / (AmE), / sfɪŋks / (BrE)
a person who is difficult to read or who keeps their thoughts and intentions secret; an inscrutable person
an inscrutable or enigmatic person

Examples

No one could tell what she was planning—she was a total sphinx in the meeting.
He remained a sphinx, giving nothing away with his expression.
To his friends, Mark was something of a sphinx, always keeping his feelings hidden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enigma - a mysterious or puzzling person
  • Synonym: mystery - a person or thing that is difficult to understand
  • Antonym: open book - a person who is easy to read or understand

Etymology

From Greek Sphinx, a mythical creature famous for asking riddles.

2. NOUN

/ sfɪŋks / (AmE), / sfɪŋks / (BrE)
A large stone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a man, built by the ancient Egyptians as a symbol of strength and wisdom.
Egyptian statue with lion's body and human head

Examples

The Great sphinx at Giza is one of Egypt's most famous monuments.
Tourists from around the world visit the ancient sphinx every year.
Archaeologists are still uncovering the mysteries behind the sphinx and its origins.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphinx', possibly derived from Egyptian 'shesep-ankh' (living image).
