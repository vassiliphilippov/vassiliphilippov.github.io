reasonable

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːzənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəbl̩ / (BrE)
Based on or showing good sense and sound judgment; fair and sensible.
showing good judgment; fair

Examples

Her demands were quite reasonable given the circumstances.
Please try to be reasonable and listen to my explanation.
A reasonable person would have checked the details before signing.

Synset Examples

sane nuclear policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rational - based on reason or logic
  • Synonym: sensible - having or showing good sense
  • Antonym: unreasonable - not guided by reason or fairness

Etymology

From Old French raisonnable, from Latin rationabilis (reasonable, rational), from ratio (reason).

having or showing sound judgment; fair and sensible

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈriːzənəbəl / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəbl / (BrE)
not excessive or extreme; moderate in amount, extent, or degree
moderate; not excessive or extreme

Examples

They offered a reasonable price for the repairs, so we accepted the estimate.
A reasonable amount of time was given to finish the project.
The restaurant serves reasonable portions that are filling but not overwhelming.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - not extreme or excessive in amount or degree
  • Antonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable

Etymology

From Old French raisonnable, from Latin rationabilis (reasonable, rational).
