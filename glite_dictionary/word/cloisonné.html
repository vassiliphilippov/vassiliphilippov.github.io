cloisonné

1. ADJ

/ klɔɪˈsɑːneɪ / (AmE), / klwɑː.zɒˈneɪ / (BrE)
(of metalwork) decorated with colored enamel separated by thin metal strips, which are fired to create compartments of color.
decorated metalwork with enamel compartments

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful cloisonné vase from the Ming dynasty.
She admired the intricate patterns on the cloisonné jewelry.
The artisan explained how cloisonné enamel is created by filling metal compartments with colored glass paste.

Etymology

From French 'cloisonné', from 'cloison' (partition).

2. NOUN

/ ˌklɔɪ.zəˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˌklwɑː.zɒˈneɪ / (BrE)
A decorative art technique in which colored enamel is separated into compartments by thin metal strips fused to a metal surface.
decorative enamelware with metal compartments

Examples

The museum displayed an exquisite cloisonné vase from the Ming dynasty.
She collects cloisonné jewelry because of its vibrant colors and intricate designs.
A craftsman demonstrated how cloisonné is made using thin wires and enamel paste.

Etymology

From French 'cloisonné', past participle of 'cloisonner' (to partition), from 'cloison' (partition), from Latin 'clausio' (closing).
