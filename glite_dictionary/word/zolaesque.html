Zolaesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌzoʊləˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌzəʊləˈɛsk / (BrE)
Characteristic of or resembling the style of Émile Zola, especially his detailed realism and concern with social issues.
in the style of Émile Zola

Examples

The author’s Zolaesque descriptions bring to life the harsh realities of working-class Paris.
Her latest novel adopts a Zolaesque tone, exposing the struggles of the urban poor with unflinching detail.
Critics praised the film’s Zolaesque attention to social injustice and its stark, realistic portrayal of life.

Etymology

From the name of Émile Zola (1840–1902), French novelist, + -esque (in the manner of).
