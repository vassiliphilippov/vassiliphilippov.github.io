to acid-wash

1. VERB

/ ˈæsɪd wɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd wɒʃ / (BrE)
to treat fabric, especially denim, with acid or chemicals to produce a faded or bleached appearance
treat fabric to produce faded look

Examples

Many designers acid-wash their jeans to create a distinct, vintage look.
She prefers to acid-wash her own denim jackets at home.
These jeans were carefully acid-washed to give them a stylish, worn-in appearance.

Synset Examples

acid-wash blue jeans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - to make fabric lighter in color using chemicals
