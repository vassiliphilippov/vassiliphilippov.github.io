ligan

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡən / (BrE)
goods or wreckage lying on the sea bed, especially those attached to a buoy so that they can be recovered
goods on sea bed attached to a buoy

Examples

The salvage crew located the ligan quickly because it was attached to a floating marker.
English maritime law distinguishes between ligan, flotsam, jetsam, and derelict.
Centuries ago, merchants often relied on ligan buoys to mark valuable cargo lost in storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotsam - goods found floating on the sea after a shipwreck
  • Synonym: jetsam - goods that have been thrown overboard from a ship, usually to lighten it
  • Synonym: derelict - property abandoned at sea without hope of recovery

Etymology

From Old French *ligane* (from *lier* 'to tie'), relating to being tied to a buoy.
