Funkaceae

1. NOUN

/ fʌŋˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / fʌŋˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a former family of flowering plants, now largely subsumed under the Liliaceae, that included the genus Hosta; this classification is not widely accepted.
a former family of flowering plants including Hosta

Examples

The genus Hosta was once classified under the family Funkaceae in some older botanical systems.
Although Funkaceae is rarely used today, it appears in certain historical plant taxonomies.
Many botanists now include plants formerly in Funkaceae within the broader Liliaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Hosta, honoring Austrian botanist Nicolaus Thomas Host; 'Funkaceae' is based on the taxonomic convention for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ fʌŋˈkeɪˌsiː / (AmE), / fʌŋˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants once separated from Liliaceae in some botanical classification systems, including the genus Hosta, though not widely recognized today.
former plant family including Hosta

Examples

Some older botanical texts still classify hostas within the Funkaceae family.
Unlike modern systems, this guide places certain species in Funkaceae rather than Liliaceae.
The genus Hosta has at times been assigned to Funkaceae by taxonomists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Liliaceae - the lily family, traditionally including many related plants
  • Synonym: Hosta - a genus of shade-loving perennial plants commonly known as plantain lilies

Etymology

Named after the Swiss botanist Heinrich Christian Funck.
