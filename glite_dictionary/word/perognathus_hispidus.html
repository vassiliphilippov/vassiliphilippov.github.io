Perognathus hispidus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːɡnəθəs ˈhɪspɪdəs / (AmE), / pɛˈrɒɡnəθəs ˈhɪspɪdəs / (BrE)
a species of large, stiff-haired rodent, commonly known as the hispid pocket mouse, found in the shortgrass prairies of the United States
large, stiff-haired rodent of U.S. prairies

Examples

The Perognathus hispidus is well adapted to the dry environment of the shortgrass prairie.
Researchers captured several specimens of Perognathus hispidus for their ecological study.
Unlike other rodents, Perognathus hispidus stores seeds in its cheek pouches for later use.

Etymology

From Greek 'peros' (pouch), 'gnathos' (jaw), and Latin 'hispidus' (bristly, rough).
