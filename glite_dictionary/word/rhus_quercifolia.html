Rhus quercifolia

1. NOUN

/ ruːs kwɜrˌsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ruːs kwɜːˌsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A poisonous shrub of the southeastern United States that causes a rash on contact with its leaves or sap.
poisonous shrub causing skin rash

Examples

Hikers should avoid touching Rhus quercifolia to prevent a painful skin rash.
The botanist collected samples of Rhus quercifolia for research on poisonous plants.
Local folklore often warns children about the dangers of Rhus quercifolia in wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison oak - a common name for toxic shrubs of the genus Toxicodendron found in North America

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Rhus' as the genus name and 'quercifolia' meaning oak-leaved ('quercus' oak + 'folia' leaves).
