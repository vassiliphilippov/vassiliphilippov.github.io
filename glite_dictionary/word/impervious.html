impervious

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɝːviəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːviəs / (BrE)
not allowing liquid, gas, or other substances to pass through; not affected or influenced by something
not allowing things to pass through; unaffected by

Examples

The seemingly solid rock was surprisingly impervious to rainwater.
She remained impervious to criticism throughout the debate.
These new boots are advertised as completely impervious to snow and mud.

Synset Examples

a material impervious to water
someone impervious to argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through it

Etymology

From Latin 'impervius', from 'in-' (not) + 'pervius' (letting through).
