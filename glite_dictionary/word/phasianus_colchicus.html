Phasianus colchicus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈzeɪni.ənəs kɒlˈkʰaɪ.kəs / (AmE), / fəˈzeɪ.ni.əs kɒlˈkiː.kəs / (BrE)
the common pheasant, a bird species characterized by bright plumage and a white neck ring, commonly found in Eurasia and introduced elsewhere
species of pheasant with bright plumage

Examples

The Phasianus colchicus has adapted well to farmland across much of Europe and North America.
Male Phasianus colchicus are known for their striking, colorful feathers and long tails.
Wild populations of Phasianus colchicus can often be seen foraging in open fields at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pheasant - large, long-tailed game bird with showy plumage
  • Synonym: ring-necked pheasant - common name for Phasianus colchicus

Etymology

From Latin Phasianus (pheasant) and Colchicus (of Colchis, a region on the Black Sea).
