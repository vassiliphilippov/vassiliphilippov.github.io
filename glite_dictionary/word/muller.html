muller

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlər / (AmE), / ˈmʌlə / (BrE)
a vessel used for heating and mixing wine with spices, typically when making mulled wine
vessel for making mulled wine

Examples

The chef poured the spiced wine into the muller to gently heat it.
An antique muller can still be found in the kitchens of some historic homes.
As the party began, they prepared the holiday drink in a large muller.

Etymology

From the verb 'mull' (to heat and spice, especially wine) plus -er, a noun-forming suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlər / (AmE), / ˈmʌlə / (BrE)
a heavy, flat-bottomed tool, typically made of stone or iron and fitted with a handle, used for grinding and mixing substances such as pigments, drugs, or grains on a slab
tool for grinding materials on a flat surface

Examples

The artist used a muller to grind pigments for making his own paints.
In old pharmacies, a muller was essential for preparing medicinal powders.
Restorers often use a muller to mix colors directly on a marble slab.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *mullere* (to crush), related to mill.
