Brassica rapa chinensis

1. NOUN

/ bræˈsɪkə ˈreɪpə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈreɪpə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
An Asian plant cultivated for its edible, white-stemmed leaf clusters with dark green leaves, commonly known as bok choy.
Asian vegetable, also called bok choy

Examples

You can add Brassica rapa chinensis to stir-fries for a crunchy texture and mild flavor.
The farmer specialized in growing Brassica rapa chinensis for local Asian markets.
Chefs often recommend steaming Brassica rapa chinensis to preserve its nutrients and fresh taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bok choy - A common English name for Brassica rapa chinensis, an Asian leafy vegetable

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining genus 'Brassica' (cabbage) + species/modifier 'rapa' (turnip) + 'chinensis' (Chinese).
