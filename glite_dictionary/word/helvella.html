Helvella

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈvɛlə / (AmE), / hɛlˈvɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Helvellaceae, known for species with saddle-shaped or irregular caps.
genus of saddle-shaped fungi

Examples

Some species in the genus Helvella are popularly called elfin saddles due to their distinct shape.
Researchers discovered a rare Helvella specimen in the alpine forest.
The Helvella mushrooms should be handled with caution as some can be toxic.

Etymology

From Latin 'Helvella', an old name used for various fungi.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlˈvɛlə / (AmE), / hɛlˈvɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi having ascocarps that are stalked, pleated, or often folded, typically found in woodland areas.
genus of fungi with folded ascocarps

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with the delicate forms of Helvella fungi.
She identified the strange mushroom as belonging to the genus Helvella.
Many Helvella species are prized by mushroom enthusiasts for their unusual shapes.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Helvella', the genus name assigned by Linnaeus.
