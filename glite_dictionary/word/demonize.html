to demonize

1. VERB

/ ˈdiːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdiːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to portray or treat someone or something as evil, wicked, or demonic
portray as evil or demonic

Examples

Some politicians try to demonize their opponents to gain public support.
The media was criticized for attempting to demonize certain groups.
Parents shouldn't demonize video games without understanding their content.

Synset Examples

Power had demonized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vilify - to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
  • Antonym: to deify - to treat or worship someone as a god (opposite direction of meaning)

Etymology

From 'demon' + '-ize'; 'demon' from Greek daimōn (spirit, supernatural being).

2. VERB

/ ˈdiːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdiːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to portray someone or something as wicked, evil, or threatening, often unfairly or inaccurately
portray as evil or harmful

Examples

It's easy to demonize someone you don't understand or agree with.
Some media outlets tend to demonize certain groups to provoke fear or anger.
We should try to understand others instead of quickly demonizing them.

Synset Examples

It's easy to demonize someone you don't understand.
Politicians often demonize their opponents during campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vilify - to speak or write about someone in a disparaging way; insult severely
  • Antonym: to humanize - to portray as more human or with human qualities

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit or demon), via French démoniser.
