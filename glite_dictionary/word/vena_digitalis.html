vena digitalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə dɪˈdʒɪtəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə dɪˈdʒɪtəlɪs / (BrE)
A vein that serves one of the fingers or toes.
vein in a finger or toe
medical

Examples

During surgery, the vena digitalis must be carefully preserved to avoid complications.
The doctor explained that the vena digitalis helps drain blood from the fingertips.
An injury to the vena digitalis can cause significant swelling in the affected finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digital vein - any vein of the fingers or toes

Etymology

From Latin, 'vena' (vein) and 'digitalis' (of a finger or toe).
