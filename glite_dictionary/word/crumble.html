to crumble

1. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmbəl / (BrE)
to break or fall apart into small fragments, especially as part of a process of deterioration or collapse
to break or fall apart

Examples

The ancient walls began to crumble after centuries of neglect.
His resolve seemed to crumble under pressure from his opponents.
The cookie will crumble if you handle it too roughly.

Synset Examples

the building crumbled after the explosion
Negotiations broke down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break up into small parts as a result of decay or loss of cohesion
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumblen', related to 'crumb'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02045722-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmbl / (BrE)
to fall apart gradually, especially as a result of decay or neglect
fall apart through decay or neglect

Examples

The old theatre began to crumble after years of neglect.
Without maintenance, the fortress walls would slowly crumble to dust.
Their once strong partnership started to crumble as trust faded.

Synset Examples

The unoccupied house started to decay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - to rot or decompose over time
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break into small parts or lose cohesion

Etymology

From Old English *crymelan* (to break into crumbs), related to 'crumb'.

3. VERB

/ ˈkrʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to break or fall apart into small fragments, especially as part of a gradual process of decay or disintegration
fall or break into small pieces

Examples

The old wall began to crumble after years of neglect.
She watched the paper crumble to dust in her hands.
If you squeeze the dry cake too hard, it will crumble easily.

Synset Examples

The cookies crumbled
The Sphinx is crumbling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break into small parts or lose cohesion
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • Antonym: to solidify - to become solid or make into a solid form

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumblen', related to 'crumb' and the suffix '-le', denoting repetitive or diminutive action.
