frightful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtfəl / (BrE)
extremely shocking, horrible, or provoking a sense of terror or horror.
provoking horror or shock

Examples

The survivors spoke of the frightful conditions they endured during the storm.
She let out a frightful scream when she saw the shadowy figure in the doorway.
The report described the frightful damage caused by the earthquake.

Synset Examples

an atrocious automobile accident
a frightful crime of decapitation
an alarming, even horrifying, picture
war is beyond all words horrible
an ugly wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - causing horror; very unpleasant
  • Synonym: atrocious - shockingly bad or evil
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From 'fright' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtfʊl / (BrE)
Extremely distressing, shocking, or causing fear; very unpleasant or bad.
extremely distressing or shocking

Examples

The storm did a frightful amount of damage to the coastline.
She made a frightful mess of the dinner party.
The movie contained some frightful scenes that left the audience uneasy.

Synset Examples

fearful slum conditions
a frightful mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearful - causing fear; very frightening
  • Synonym: horrible - very unpleasant or bad; causing horror
  • Antonym: delightful - giving great pleasure or delight

Etymology

From fright + -ful (suffix denoting "full of").

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01130978-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtfʊl / (BrE)
Extremely bad or serious; very great in degree, amount, or impact.
extremely bad or serious; very great in amount

Examples

The team made a frightful number of mistakes during the match.
She was in a frightful hurry to catch her train that morning.
They spent a frightful amount of money on renovations last year.

Synset Examples

in a frightful hurry
spent a frightful amount of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - very bad or serious
  • Synonym: awful - extremely bad or unpleasant

Etymology

From Middle English 'frightful', from 'fright' + '-ful'.
