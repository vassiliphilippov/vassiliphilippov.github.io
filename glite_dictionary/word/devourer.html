devourer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaʊ.ɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊ.ər.ə / (BrE)
a person or creature that eats something eagerly and in large quantities; someone who consumes something voraciously
someone who eats greedily or voraciously

Examples

The wolf was a notorious devourer of livestock in the region.
As a devourer of novels, she finished a new book every two days.
He was known as a devourer of desserts at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glutton - a person who eats excessively
  • Synonym: to devour - to eat eagerly or in large amounts

Etymology

From 'devour' (Middle English 'devouren', from Old French 'devorer', from Latin 'devorare' meaning 'to swallow down').
