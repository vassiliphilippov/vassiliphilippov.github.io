clouded

1. ADJ

/ ˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) filled or covered with clouds; not clear.
covered with clouds

Examples

The clouded sky signaled that rain was likely later in the day.
During the journey, the mountains were hidden behind a clouded horizon.
She watched the sunrise through her clouded window, wishing for a clear morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - (of the sky) free from clouds or mist
  • Synonym: overcast - (of the sky) completely covered with clouds

2. ADJ

/ ˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's face or expression) showing signs of being troubled, worried, or distressed.
showing a troubled or distressed appearance

Examples

Her clouded eyes revealed that she had been crying.
His expression became clouded when he heard the bad news.
A clouded face often betrays inner anxiety or doubt.

Synset Examples

his face was clouded with unhappiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubled - showing distress or worry in expression
  • Antonym: serene - calm and peaceful in appearance or expression
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02466067-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of thoughts, the mind, or judgment) confused, troubled, or lacking in clarity, often due to strong emotions or mental disturbance.
confused or troubled in mind

Examples

After the accident, his mind was clouded and he struggled to remember details.
Grief had clouded her judgment, making decisions difficult.
Fatigue can leave your thoughts clouded and unfocused.

Synset Examples

a mind clouded by sorrow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - free from confusion or doubt
  • Synonym: confused - uncertain, unable to think clearly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00438259-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
not clear in form, understanding, or expression; obscured or made difficult to perceive or comprehend
unclear or obscured; difficult to understand

Examples

His judgment was clouded by anger, making it hard to see the situation clearly.
The true meaning of the poem remained clouded despite several careful readings.
Her explanation was so clouded that nobody could follow what she meant.

Synset Examples

the blurred aims of the group
sometimes one understood clearly and sometimes the meaning was clouded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • Synonym: obscure - not clear or hard to understand

Etymology

From the verb 'cloud', from Old English 'clud' (mass of rock, hill, cloud).
