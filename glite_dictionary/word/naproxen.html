naproxen

1. NOUN

/ nəˈprɑksən / (AmE), / nəˈprɒksən / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug used to reduce inflammation and relieve pain, especially in conditions like arthritis and musculoskeletal disorders.
nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)
medical

Examples

After her surgery, the doctor prescribed naproxen to help manage her pain and reduce inflammation.
Many people use naproxen for relief from chronic conditions like arthritis or back pain.
You should not take naproxen on an empty stomach to avoid irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - another common nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)
  • Antonym: acetaminophen - an analgesic and antipyretic drug, not an NSAID

Etymology

From chemical name, based on naphthalene and propionic acid.
