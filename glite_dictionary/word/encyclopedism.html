encyclopedism

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪzəm / (BrE)
the possession of profound, comprehensive scholarly knowledge; encyclopedic learning
profound scholarly knowledge

Examples

His encyclopedism impressed the entire faculty at the university.
The breadth of her encyclopedism made her a valuable asset to the research team.
In an age of specialists, true encyclopedism has become increasingly rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudition - extensive knowledge acquired chiefly from books; learning

Etymology

From 'encyclopedic', based on Greek 'enkuklios paideia' (general education) + '-ism'.
