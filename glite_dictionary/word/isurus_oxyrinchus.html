Isurus oxyrinchus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsʊɹəs ˌɒksɪˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / aɪˈsjuːrəs ˌɒksɪˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A species of swift, active, bluish shark found worldwide in warm waters, known commonly as the shortfin mako shark and valued as a game fish.
a species of swift, bluish shark (shortfin mako)

Examples

The Isurus oxyrinchus can reach swimming speeds up to 60 kilometers per hour.
Many anglers dream of catching an Isurus oxyrinchus because of its incredible strength and speed.
Marine researchers are studying how the Isurus oxyrinchus migrates across entire oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortfin mako shark - common name for Isurus oxyrinchus

Etymology

From Greek *isurus* (equal tail) and *oxyrhincus* (sharp-snouted).
