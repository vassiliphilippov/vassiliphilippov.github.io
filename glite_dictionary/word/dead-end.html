dead-end

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdˌɛnd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌɛnd / (BrE)
(of a job, situation, etc.) offering no prospects for progress or advancement; with little or no future.
offering no prospects for advancement

Examples

He felt trapped in a dead-end job with no chance for promotion.
Many people worry that working in retail can become a dead-end situation if they're not careful.
She left a dead-end position to seek new opportunities in a growing company.

Synset Examples

stuck in a dead-end job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnant - not advancing or developing
  • Antonym: promising - likely to develop in a positive way
