aroid

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛəˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of plants of the family Araceae.
of or relating to the Araceae plant family

Examples

The botanical garden features several rare aroid species in its tropical greenhouse.
Many aroid plants produce strikingly unusual flowers and foliage shapes.
Some aroid leaves can cause skin irritation due to their calcium oxalate crystals.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Araceae' (the plant family) + '-oid' (resembling, related to).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛə.rɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
Any plant of the family Araceae, typically having small flowers crowded on a spadix and surrounded by a large spathe.
plant of the Araceae family

Examples

The titan arum is the world's largest aroid, famous for its huge and pungent flower.
She grows several species of aroid in her greenhouse, including the peace lily and anthurium.
Many tropical aroids have striking foliage, making them popular ornamental plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Arum (the type genus) with suffix -oid (‘resembling’ or ‘like’).
