gallberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːlˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of eastern North America (Ilex glabra) with oblong, leathery leaves and small black berries
evergreen shrub with black berries

Examples

The dense thickets of gallberry are common in the pine flatwoods of the southeastern United States.
Bees are attracted to the small flowers of the gallberry during the spring.
The gardener planted gallberry along the edge of the property for its hardy foliage.
