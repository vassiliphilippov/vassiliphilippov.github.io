alexandrine

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændriːn / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændriːn / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of six iambic feet, especially common in French poetry but also used in English prosody.
a verse line of six iambic feet

Examples

The poem reached a crescendo with a particularly striking alexandrine.
English poets occasionally employ the alexandrine for dramatic effect.
The final line of the stanza, an alexandrine, provided a sense of closure.

Etymology

From French 'alexandrin', originally used for Old French poems about Alexander the Great.
