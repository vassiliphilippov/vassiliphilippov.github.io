to lose

1. VERB

/ luːz / (AmE), / luːz / (BrE)
to fail to hear, see, or understand something; to miss part or all of what is said or shown
fail to perceive or understand

Examples

I'm sorry, I lost what you just said—could you repeat it?
She often loses the thread of the conversation when people talk too fast.
By the time he finished the explanation, I had already lost his point.

Synset Examples

I missed that remark
She missed his point
We lost part of what he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss - to fail to notice or understand

Etymology

From Old English *losian* (to perish, be lost), from *los* (loss).
