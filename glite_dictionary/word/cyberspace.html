cyberspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪbərˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the notional environment in which communication over computer networks occurs, especially the Internet
virtual environment of computer networks

Examples

The information was stored in cyberspace, accessible from anywhere in the world.
Debates about privacy in cyberspace are growing more urgent as technology advances.
Many new businesses exist solely in cyberspace without any physical storefront.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internet - the global computer network enabling communication worldwide

Etymology

Formed from 'cyber-' (from 'cybernetics') and 'space', first used in science fiction in the 1980s.
