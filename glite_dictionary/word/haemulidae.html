Haemulidae

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmjuːlɪdiː / (AmE), / hiːˈmjuːlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical and subtropical marine fish, commonly known as grunts, found in warm seas worldwide.
family of fish called grunts

Examples

Several species in the Haemulidae family are popular among aquarium enthusiasts.
The Haemulidae are easily identified by the grunting sounds they produce underwater.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of Haemulidae along the coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - common name for fish of the Haemulidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek haimulos (bloody) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
