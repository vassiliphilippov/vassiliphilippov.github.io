stupefaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌstuːpəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌstjuːpɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
A state of being so astonished or shocked that one is unable to think or react.
state of stunned astonishment

Examples

He stared in stupefaction as the magician pulled a rabbit out of the hat.
The news of her sudden promotion was met with stupefaction by her colleagues.
After the explosion, a sense of stupefaction hung over the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazement - A feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: bewilderment - A state of being confused and puzzled

Etymology

From Late Latin stupefactio (amazement, stupefaction), from stupefacere (to stupefy).

2. NOUN

/ stuːpəˈfæʃən / (AmE), / stjuːpɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
The act of making someone unable to think or feel properly, often by shocking, astonishing, or dulling them.
state of being stupefied or made dull

Examples

The audience sat in stupefaction, unable to react to the shocking announcement.
Her face wore an expression of stupefaction as she tried to process what she had heard.
The hypnotist's performance left many participants in a state of stupefaction for several minutes.

Synset Examples

the professor was noted for his stupefaction of the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishment - A feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: alertness - The quality of being quick to notice things; wakefulness

Etymology

From Latin stupefactio, from stupefacere (to make numb or astonish).

3. NOUN

/ ˌstuː.pəˈfæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌstjuː.pɪˈfæk.ʃən / (BrE)
A state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility, often caused by shock, exhaustion, or strong emotion.
state of near-unconsciousness

Examples

He was left in a state of stupefaction after hearing the shocking news.
The boxer stumbled around the ring in stupefaction after the hit.
She stared in stupefaction at the strange sight before her.

Synset Examples

his grogginess was caused as much by exhaustion as by the blows
someone stole his wallet while he was in a drunken stupor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupor - a state of being almost unconscious or insensible
  • Antonym: alertness - state of being fully conscious and attentive

Etymology

From Latin stupēfactiō (amazement), from stupēfacere (to stun).
