pyridoxine 5'-phosphate

1. NOUN

/ pɪrɪˈdɑksiːn ˈfaɪv prɪm ˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / pɪrɪˈdɒksiːn ˈfaɪv praɪm ˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B6 that is essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch in the body
form of vitamin B6 important in metabolism
medical

Examples

Pyridoxine 5'-phosphate is required for many enzymatic reactions in the body.
Doctors may measure blood levels of pyridoxine 5'-phosphate to assess vitamin B6 status.
Deficiency of pyridoxine 5'-phosphate can lead to problems with amino acid metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B6 - an essential nutrient involved in metabolism, also known as pyridoxine

Etymology

From 'pyridoxine' (a B6 vitamin), and 'phosphate' (from Greek 'phosphoros', bearing light).
