lunula

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.njələ / (AmE), / ˈluː.njʊ.lə / (BrE)
the small, pale, crescent-shaped area at the base of a fingernail or toenail
crescent at nail base
medical

Examples

She noticed that the lunula on her thumb was larger than on her other fingers.
Doctors sometimes examine the lunula for signs of health issues.
The pale lunula at the base of each nail is often most visible on the thumbs.

Etymology

From Latin 'lunula' (diminutive of 'luna', meaning 'moon').

2. NOUN

/ ˈluː.njələ / (AmE), / ˈluː.njʊ.lə / (BrE)
a crescent-shaped metal ornament from the Bronze Age, typically worn as a necklace.
Bronze Age crescent-shaped ornament

Examples

The gold lunula was discovered during the archaeological excavation in Ireland.
Many museums display a lunula as an example of ancient craftsmanship.
The distinctive shape of the lunula reflects its symbolic connection to the moon.

Etymology

From Latin 'lunula', diminutive of 'luna' (moon), referring to its crescent shape.
