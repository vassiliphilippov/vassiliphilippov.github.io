Pyracantha

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈkænθə / (AmE), / ˌpɪrəˈkanθə / (BrE)
A genus of thorny shrubs with small white flowers and bright red or orange-red berries, commonly known as firethorn.
thorny shrub with red or orange berries

Examples

The Pyracantha in the front garden is covered with bright orange berries every autumn.
Birds love to nest in the dense branches of a Pyracantha shrub.
She planted a Pyracantha hedge to provide both security and color for her yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firethorn - common name for plants of the genus Pyracantha

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' meaning 'fire' and 'akantha' meaning 'thorn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈkænθə / (AmE), / ˌpɪərəˈkanθə / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian evergreen thorny shrubs in the rose family, bearing clusters of small white flowers followed by bright red or orange-red berries; commonly known as firethorn.
genus of evergreen thorny shrubs (firethorn)

Examples

The garden was bordered with Pyracantha, their red berries brightening the winter landscape.
Many birds are attracted to Pyracantha shrubs because of the plentiful fruit they provide.
She pruned the old Pyracantha carefully to encourage new growth next spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'akantha' (thorn).
