plica vocalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪ.kə voʊˈkeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.kə vəʊˈkɑː.lɪs / (BrE)
either of the two folds of mucous membrane in the larynx, commonly known as the vocal cords, that are essential for voice production
the vocal cords; folds in the larynx
medical

Examples

Damage to the plica vocalis can result in hoarseness and difficulty speaking.
The surgeon examined the patient's plica vocalis during the procedure to assess any abnormalities.
Voice therapy may help improve the function of the plica vocalis after injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocal cord - either of the two folds of mucous membrane in the larynx involved in voice production

Etymology

From Latin plica (fold) and vocalis (vocal), meaning 'vocal fold'.
