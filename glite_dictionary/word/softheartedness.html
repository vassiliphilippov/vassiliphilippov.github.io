softheartedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑftˈhɑrtɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɒftˈhɑːtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, compassionate, and easily moved to pity or concern for others, especially those in need or vulnerable
compassionate and tender concern for others

Examples

Her softheartedness was evident when she adopted the stray kitten from the street.
Many admired his softheartedness toward the elderly residents of the community.
Despite criticism, her softheartedness inspired others to help those less fortunate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - a deep awareness of and sympathy for another's suffering
  • Antonym: cruelty - lack of pity or compassion for others

Etymology

From 'softhearted' (kind and compassionate) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
