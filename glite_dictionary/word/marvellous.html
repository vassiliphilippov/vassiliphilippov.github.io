marvellous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːr.vəl.əs / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.vəl.əs / (BrE)
Causing great wonder; extraordinarily good or miraculous.
extraordinarily good, miraculous

Examples

The show was a marvellous display of acrobatics and skill.
Her marvellous recovery from the illness amazed the doctors.
We spent a marvellous afternoon exploring the galleries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonderful - inspiring delight, pleasure, or admiration; extremely good
  • Synonym: miraculous - occurring through divine or supernatural intervention

Etymology

From Middle French *merveilleux*, from Latin *mirabilia* (wonderful things).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːrvələs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvələs / (BrE)
so extraordinary or improbable as to be difficult to believe; astonishing
extraordinarily hard to believe; astonishing

Examples

The entire tale was so marvellous that no one could believe it was true.
He told a marvellous story about dragons and magic that defied all logic.
The discovery seemed almost too marvellous to accept as fact.

Synset Examples

a tall story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incredible - impossible or very hard to believe
  • Synonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to happen
  • Antonym: believable - able to be believed; credible

Etymology

From Middle French merveilleux, from Latin mīrābilis (wonderful).
