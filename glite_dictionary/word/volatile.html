volatile

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːlətl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
A substance that easily changes from a solid or liquid state into a vapor, often at room temperature.
substance that easily vaporizes

Examples

The laboratory technician measured the amount of volatile released during the experiment.
Paint often contains volatile components that can evaporate into the air.
After heating the sample, they analyzed the remaining volatile residues.

Synset Examples

it was heated to evaporate the volatiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance, usually liquid, that dissolves a solute forming a solution

Etymology

From Latin 'volatilis' (flying, fleeting), from 'volare' (to fly); originally describing things that evaporate or are fleeting.

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɑː.lə.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance) evaporating readily at normal temperatures and pressures.

Examples

Some cleaning agents are made with volatile solvents that evaporate soon after application.
