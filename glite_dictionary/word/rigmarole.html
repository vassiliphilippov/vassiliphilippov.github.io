rigmarole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡməˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡmərəʊl / (BrE)
a long, complicated, and confusing process or set of statements, often regarded as meaningless or pointless
confused or meaningless statements/process

Examples

She made me go through the whole rigmarole just to submit a simple form.
The meeting turned into a rigmarole of unrelated complaints and stories.
I'm tired of all this rigmarole just to get a permit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are foolish or meaningless
  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'ragman roll', a historical term for a long list or roll.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡ.məˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡ.məˌrəʊl / (BrE)
a long, complicated, and confusing procedure, especially when unnecessary or pointless
a lengthy, confusing procedure

Examples

I had to go through a lot of rigmarole just to renew my driver's license.
The committee meeting was a pointless rigmarole that achieved nothing.
She avoided the usual rigmarole by speaking directly to the manager.

Synset Examples

all that academic rigmarole was a waste of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protocol - official procedure or system of rules
  • Synonym: hassle - a situation causing difficulty or trouble
  • Antonym: shortcut - an alternative route that is shorter or quicker

Etymology

From earlier 'ragman roll', a medieval document listing names, later misinterpreted and altered in form.
