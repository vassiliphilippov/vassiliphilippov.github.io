fictive

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪktɪv / (BrE)
not real or genuine; adopted in order to deceive; invented or imagined for a particular purpose
not real; invented or feigned

Examples

She gave a fictive address to preserve her privacy.
His fictive sympathy did little to comfort her.
The suspect provided a fictive name when questioned by police.

Synset Examples

an assumed name
an assumed cheerfulness
a fictitious address
fictive sympathy
a pretended interest
a put-on childish voice
sham modesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fictitious - not real or true; invented or fabricated
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From Latin fictivus (feigned), from fingere (to form, contrive).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or capable of imaginative creation; fictional or invented rather than real.
imaginary; relating to creation or fiction

Examples

The artist's fictive world allows viewers to escape reality for a moment.
She was praised for her fictive imagination and the originality of her stories.
His fictive approach to design sets him apart from traditional architects.

Synset Examples

fictive talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fictional - imaginary; invented for the purposes of fiction
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring in fact

Etymology

From Latin fictivus (invented), from fingere (to shape, form, devise).
