wait

1. NOUN

/ weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a period of remaining in one place while expecting something to happen or someone to arrive
a period of waiting

Examples

After a long wait, the train finally arrived at the station.
The doctor's office had an hour-long wait before I could be seen.
A sudden wait in the conversation made everyone feel awkward.

Synset Examples

the wait was an ordeal for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - a period or state of being delayed
  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop or break

Etymology

From Old Northern French *waiter*, of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a period of time during which something is expected or awaited
period of waiting

Examples

3. VERB

/ weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
to delay taking action until a later time or until something happens
delay before acting

Examples

The committee decided to wait before making any final decisions.
She chose to wait until she had more information before responding.
Let's wait for a few more days before we proceed.

Synset Examples

the scientists held off announcing their results until they repeated the experiment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned
  • Antonym: act - to do something, especially after waiting

Etymology

From Old Northern French *waiter*, variant of Old French *guaitier* (to watch, to guard).
