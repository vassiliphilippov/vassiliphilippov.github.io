Tanacetum douglasii

1. NOUN

/ tænəˌsiːtəm ˌduːɡˈlæsaɪ / (AmE), / tænəˌsiːtəm ˌdʌɡˈleɪsaɪ / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of the aster family, with aromatic, feathery leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers, found in sand dunes along the Pacific coast.
perennial plant of the Pacific coast, North America

Examples

Tanacetum douglasii can be found growing in sandy soils along the Pacific coastline.
Botanists often study Tanacetum douglasii for its adaptation to coastal environments.
The aromatic leaves of Tanacetum douglasii distinguish it from other dune plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas' tansy - Common name for Tanacetum douglasii, a perennial of Pacific coast North America

Etymology

From the genus name 'Tanacetum' (Latin for tansy) and species honorific 'douglasii', named after botanist David Douglas.
