thiotepa

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪoʊˈtɛpə / (AmE), / ˌθaɪəʊˈtɛpə / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug used in chemotherapy to treat certain types of cancer
chemotherapy drug for cancer
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed thiotepa as part of her cancer treatment regimen.
Some side effects of thiotepa can include nausea and low blood cell counts.
Researchers are studying new uses for thiotepa in treating different types of cancer.

Etymology

From thio- (indicating sulfur) + TEPA (triethylenephosphoramide, a related compound).
