jetsam

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛtsəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛtsəm / (BrE)
objects or material that are thrown overboard from a ship, especially to lighten the vessel in an emergency, and are often washed ashore.
goods thrown overboard from a ship

Examples

During the storm, the crew threw jetsam into the sea to keep the ship afloat.
Much of the beach was scattered with jetsam after the hurricane had passed.
The coastguard identified the jetsam as spare parts from a cargo vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotsam - wreckage or cargo found floating on the sea after a shipwreck
  • Synonym: lagan - goods thrown into the sea with a buoy attached, expected to be recovered

Etymology

From 'jettison', which derives from Old French 'getaison', from Latin 'iectātiō' (act of throwing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛt.səm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛt.səm / (BrE)
objects or wreckage that have been thrown from a ship and are floating in the sea or washed ashore, especially as a result of a shipwreck.
floating wreckage from a ship

Examples

After the storm, jetsam from the wrecked boat washed up along the shoreline.
The coastline was littered with jetsam, evidence of the severe shipwreck.
Fishermen discovered pieces of jetsam floating among the seaweed near the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotsam - floating wreckage or debris from a ship accidentally lost

Etymology

From jettison (to throw overboard), influenced by the pattern of similar maritime terms.
