concentrical

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / kənˈsɛntrɪkəl / (BrE)
Having a common center, especially used to describe circles or other shapes that share the same center point.
having a common center

Examples

The ancient temple was built with several concentrical walls to protect its inner sanctum.
A series of concentrical circles appeared on the target after the test was completed.
Many garden layouts use concentrical pathways to create a pleasing visual effect.

Synset Examples

concentric rings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentric - having a common center

Etymology

Variant of 'concentric', from Latin 'concentricus' (with a common center), combining 'con-' (together) and 'centrum' (center).
