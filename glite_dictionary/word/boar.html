boar

1. NOUN

/ bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
an uncastrated male pig, especially one kept for breeding
uncastrated male pig

Examples

The farmer introduced a new boar to the herd for breeding purposes.
Wild boars can be aggressive and are found in many forests around the world.
The young piglets quickly gathered around the large boar during feeding time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sow - an adult female pig, especially a breeding one

Etymology

From Old English *bār* (boar).

2. NOUN

/ bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
A wild pig native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, with a narrow body and prominent tusks; the ancestor of most domestic pigs.
wild pig with tusks; pig ancestor

Examples

The hunters spotted a large boar rooting in the forest.
Wild boars are known for their aggressive behavior and distinctive tusks.
Most domestic pigs are descended from the wild boar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pig - domesticated animal related to the boar
  • Antonym: sow - an adult female pig or boar

Etymology

Old English *bār* (boar), of Germanic origin.
