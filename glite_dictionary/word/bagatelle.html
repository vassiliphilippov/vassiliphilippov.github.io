bagatelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæɡəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌbaɡəˈtɛl / (BrE)
a short, light piece of music, especially for the piano
short, light piano composition

Examples

Beethoven composed several bagatelles that are still performed today.
The pianist ended the concert with a lively bagatelle.
She enjoys practicing short bagatelles to improve her technique.

Etymology

From French 'bagatelle' meaning 'trifle', from Italian 'bagatella'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæɡəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌbaɡəˈtɛl / (BrE)
a thing of little importance or value; a trivial matter
trivial or unimportant thing

Examples

She dismissed the broken vase as a mere bagatelle and didn't worry about it.
Compared to the challenges we faced last year, this setback is a bagatelle.
For him, losing a few dollars was a bagatelle, hardly worth mentioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifle - something of little importance or value
  • Synonym: triviality - something trivial or unimportant

Etymology

From French 'bagatelle' (trifle), from Italian 'bagatella' (trifle, little thing).

3. NOUN

/ ˌbæɡəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌbaɡəˈtɛl / (BrE)
A table game in which players use short cues to strike balls into holes, often guarded by wooden pegs, with penalties for knocking over the pegs.
table game using cues and balls

Examples

We found an old bagatelle table in the attic and played for hours.
The rules of bagatelle require caution, as knocking over the pegs leads to penalties.
He became fascinated with bagatelle after visiting a vintage games exhibition.

Etymology

French, from Italian *bagatella* (trifle, knick-knack).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
