Doris Lessing

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɔːrɪs ˈlesɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒrɪs ˈlesɪŋ / (BrE)
(1919–2013) An English author and Nobel laureate, known for her novels and short stories exploring themes such as psychology, politics, and society; she grew up in Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe).
English novelist and Nobel laureate

Examples

Doris Lessing won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2007 for her outstanding contribution to fiction.
Many readers admire the way Doris Lessing explored social and political issues in her writing.
The Golden Notebook, written by Doris Lessing, is regarded as a landmark novel in feminist literature.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdɔːrɪs ˈlɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒrɪs ˈlɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
(1919–2013) An English novelist and short story writer, born in Persia and raised in Southern Rhodesia, renowned for her explorations of social, political, and psychological themes.
English novelist and short story writer

Examples

Doris Lessing won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2007 for her rich and diverse body of work.
'The Golden Notebook' is one of Doris Lessing's most acclaimed novels.
Many scholars have examined how Doris Lessing's upbringing in Africa shaped her fiction.
