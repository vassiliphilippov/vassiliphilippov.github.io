Heidegger

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪˌdɛɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdɛɡə / (BrE)
Martin Heidegger (1889–1976), a German philosopher known for his influential work on the nature of existence and human experience.
German existential philosopher (1889–1976)

Examples

Many scholars consider Heidegger one of the most important philosophers of the twentieth century.
The concept of 'Being-in-the-world' is central to Heidegger's philosophy.
Some critics argue that Heidegger's political affiliations cast a shadow over his intellectual legacy.
