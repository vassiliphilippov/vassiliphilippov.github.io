Nestorian

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈstɔriən / (AmE), / nɛˈstɔːriən / (BrE)
A member of a Christian sect that follows the teachings of Nestorius, especially relating to the distinctness of Christ’s human and divine natures.
follower of Nestorius' Christian teachings

Examples

The early Nestorian missionaries traveled as far as China spreading their interpretation of Christianity.
Many historians debate the influence of Nestorian doctrine on Eastern Christian churches.
He studied the disputes between Nestorian Christians and other religious groups in the fifth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Monophysite - A member of a Christian sect emphasizing Christ's single divine nature; contrasts with 'Nestorian'.

Etymology

From Nestorius (c. 386–c. 451), a Byzantine theologian and Archbishop of Constantinople, plus the suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ nɛˈstɔriən / (AmE), / nɛsˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
relating to Nestorius (a 5th-century Christian theologian) or to Nestorianism, a doctrine emphasizing the disunion between the human and divine natures of Jesus.
of Nestorius or Nestorianism

Examples

The Nestorian controversy shaped much of early Christian theological debate.
A Nestorian church still exists in parts of the Middle East and Asia.
He wrote a book about the influence of Nestorian teachings on Eastern Christianity.

Etymology

From Nestorius, a 5th-century Patriarch of Constantinople, whose teachings led to the development of Nestorianism.
