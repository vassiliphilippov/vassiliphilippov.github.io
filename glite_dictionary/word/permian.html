Permian

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːmiən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːmɪən / (BrE)
the last period of the Paleozoic era, lasting from about 298 to 252 million years ago, notable for the diversification of reptiles and ending with a mass extinction
period of the Paleozoic era marked by reptiles

Examples

The Permian period ended with the largest mass extinction in Earth's history.
Many coal deposits were formed during the late Permian.
Scientists study fossils from the Permian to understand prehistoric life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Carboniferous - the geological period preceding the Permian
  • Degree Higher: Triassic - the geological period following the Permian

Etymology

Named after Perm, a region in Russia where Permian rocks were first studied.
