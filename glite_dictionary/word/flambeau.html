flambeau

1. NOUN

/ flæmˈboʊ / (AmE), / ˈflæmbəʊ / (BrE)
A torch, typically one that is flaming, used especially in processions at night.
flaming torch used in processions

Examples

The ancient ceremony was lit by flambeaux as the crowd marched through the darkened streets.
At Mardi Gras parades, performers often carry large flambeaux to light the way.
A sudden gust threatened to extinguish the flambeau held aloft by the leader of the procession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torch - a portable light usually consisting of a burning stick or piece of wood

Etymology

From French 'flambeau', diminutive of 'flambe' (flame), from Latin 'flamma'.
