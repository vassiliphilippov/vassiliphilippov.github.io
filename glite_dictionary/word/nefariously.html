nefariously

1. ADV

/ nɪˈfɛriəsli / (AmE), / nɪˈfɛːrɪəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is wicked, criminal, or morally wrong.
in a wicked or criminal manner

Examples

The company acted nefariously to avoid paying taxes.
He nefariously manipulated the results to benefit himself.
They were nefariously involved in the cyber-attack.

Synset Examples

nefariously involved in a conspiracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nefarious - wicked or criminal
  • Synonym: wickedly - in a wicked manner

Etymology

From Latin 'nefas' (wrong, crime) via 'nefarious'.
