to convene

1. VERB

/ kənˈvin / (AmE), / kənˈviːn / (BrE)
to come together for a formal meeting or gathering; to assemble, especially for official or serious purposes
to assemble formally

Examples

The board will convene at 9 a.m. to discuss the budget proposal.
A special committee was convened to investigate the incident.
Experts from around the world will convene for the international climate summit next month.

Synset Examples

The council convened last week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to come or bring together as a group

Etymology

From Latin convenīre (to come together), from con- 'together' + venīre 'to come'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02028354-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈvin / (AmE), / kənˈviːn / (BrE)
to bring people together or to gather for a meeting or assembly
assemble or call together (for a meeting)

Examples

The committee will convene at 10 a.m. to discuss the proposal.
An emergency meeting was convened to address the crisis.
Every year, experts from around the world convene for this international conference.

Synset Examples

The students were convened in the auditorium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to gather together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: to adjourn - to suspend a meeting to a later time or indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin convenire (to come together, assemble), from 'com-' (together) + 'venire' (to come).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00794699-v
Synset POS: v
