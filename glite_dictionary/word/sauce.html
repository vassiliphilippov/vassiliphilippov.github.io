sauce

1. VERB

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
to add sauce or relish to food to enhance its flavor
to add sauce or relish to food

Examples

He carefully sauced the steak with a rich mushroom reduction before serving it.
The chef likes to sauce each plate right at the table for extra flair.
After grilling the chicken, she sauced it generously with barbecue glaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - to add salt, pepper, or spices to food to improve its flavor

2. VERB

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
to speak or behave toward someone in a rude or cheeky way; to show impudence or disrespect.
speak or behave impudently
archaic

Examples

Don’t you sauce your elders, young man!
The student sauced the teacher and was promptly sent out of the room.
She was never one to sauce authority, always responding politely instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheek - impertinent talk or behavior
  • Antonym: respect - showing polite regard or deference

Etymology

From the noun 'sauce', which developed a figurative sense of 'impertinence' in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02524622-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
to add sauce or flavor to (food); to make something more lively or interesting
add sauce or zest to; enliven

Examples

She sauced the pasta with a spicy tomato blend to enhance its flavor.
His jokes really sauced up the otherwise dull presentation.
The chef always sauces the meat before serving to bring out the taste.

Synset Examples

sauce the roast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - to add flavor with spices or condiments
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor or liveliness

Etymology

From Old French 'sauce', from Latin 'salsa' (salted things), feminine of 'salsus' (salted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00536924-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
A liquid or semi-liquid substance served with food to add flavor, moisture, or visual appeal.
flavorful food accompaniment

Examples

He poured tomato sauce over his spaghetti before eating.
The chef created a creamy mushroom sauce to complement the steak.
Could you pass me the hot sauce, please?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy - A sauce made from meat juices, often served with roast meat or potatoes
  • Synonym: condiment - A substance such as salt, mustard, or ketchup added to food for flavor

Etymology

From Old French 'sausse', from Latin 'salsa' (salted).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
