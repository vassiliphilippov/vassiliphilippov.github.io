temporality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛmpəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpəˈrælɪti / (BrE)
the worldly possessions or secular properties belonging to a church or religious institution, as opposed to its spiritual authority
church's secular possessions

Examples

Many medieval conflicts centered on the division of temporality and spiritual power within the church.
The bishop was responsible for both spiritual duties and the management of the diocese's temporality.
After the Reformation, some monarchs seized the temporality of disbanded monasteries.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin temporalitas, from Latin temporalis (temporal, worldly).
