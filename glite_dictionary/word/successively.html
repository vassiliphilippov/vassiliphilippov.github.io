successively

1. ADV

/ səkˈsɛsɪvli / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsɪvli / (BrE)
in the order in which things follow one another; one after another in sequence
one after another, in sequence

Examples

The runners started successively rather than all at once.
Each player took their turn successively during the game.
The awards were presented successively to the winners on stage.

Synset Examples

talked to each child in turn
the stable became in turn a chapel and then a movie theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutively - following one right after another without interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'successivus' (following in order), from 'succedere' (to follow).
