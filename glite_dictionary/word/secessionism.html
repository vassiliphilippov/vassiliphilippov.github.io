secessionism

1. NOUN

/ səˈsɛʃ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / sɪˈsɛʃ.ən.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief, doctrine, or policy supporting the right of a region or group to withdraw from a larger political entity or organization
belief in the right to secede

Examples

The rise of secessionism threatened to fragment the country into several independent states.
Debates over secessionism have shaped regional politics throughout history.
Academic articles on secessionism analyze its causes and implications in various nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secession - the action of withdrawing formally from membership of a federation or body

Etymology

From 'secession' + '-ism', with 'secession' from Latin 'secessio' (a withdrawal).
