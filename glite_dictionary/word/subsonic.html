subsonic

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈsɑːnɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˈsɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a speed) slower than the speed of sound in a specified medium, especially air.
slower than the speed of sound

Examples

The jet remained subsonic during the entire flight to avoid breaking the sound barrier.
Many commercial airplanes operate at subsonic speeds for safety and fuel efficiency.
At subsonic speed, the aircraft is quieter and produces less aerodynamic drag.

Synset Examples

aircraft flying at subsonic speeds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supersonic - faster than the speed of sound

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + sonic (relating to sound).
