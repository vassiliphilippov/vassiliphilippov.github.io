limonene

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪməˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈlɪməniːn / (BrE)
a liquid terpene with a lemon-like odor, found in lemons, oranges, and other citrus oils, used in flavorings and fragrances
lemon-scented terpene in citrus peels
medical

Examples

The citrus scent in cleaning products often comes from limonene extracted from orange peels.
Scientists studied the effects of limonene on cancer cells in laboratory experiments.
Essential oils rich in limonene are commonly used in aromatherapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terpene - any of a large group of volatile unsaturated hydrocarbons found in plants

Etymology

From French *limon* (lemon), with chemical suffix -ene indicating hydrocarbons.
