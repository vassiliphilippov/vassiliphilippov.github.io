equable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛkwəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛkwəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a person) calm and even-tempered; not easily disturbed or angered.
calm and even-tempered

Examples

Despite the chaos in the office, he remained remarkably equable throughout the day.
She has such an equable nature that even stressful news doesn't fluster her.
His equable temperament makes him a great mediator in arguments.

Synset Examples

an equable temper
not everyone shared his placid temperament
remained placid despite the repeated delays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placid - not easily upset or excited; calm
  • Antonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered

Etymology

From Latin 'aequabilis' (equal, uniform), from 'aequare' (to make equal).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛkwəb(ə)l, ˈiːkwəb(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈɛkwəbl̩, ˈiːkwəbl̩ / (BrE)
not changing or varying greatly; steady or uniform in nature, character, or temperature
not varying; steady and uniform

Examples

The region is famous for its equable climate, attracting visitors all year round.
His equable temperament made him well-suited to leadership positions.
The temperature in these coastal waters remains relatively equable throughout the year.

Synset Examples

an equable climate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - liable to change or vary; not constant
  • Synonym: steady - firmly fixed, constant, not changing or fluctuating

Etymology

From Latin 'aequabilis' (equal, even, uniform), from 'aequare' (to make equal).
