Dionaea muscipula

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.oʊˈniː.ə mʌsˈsɪpjʊlə / (AmE), / dʌɪ.əˈnɪə mʌsˈsɪpjʊlə / (BrE)
A carnivorous plant native to the coastal plains of the Carolinas, known for its hinged, bristled leaf blades that close to trap insects; commonly called the Venus flytrap.
carnivorous plant, Venus flytrap

Examples

The Dionaea muscipula is famous for its rapid leaf movement to catch unsuspecting insects.
Many plant enthusiasts cultivate Dionaea muscipula for its unique method of capturing prey.
The natural habitat of Dionaea muscipula is restricted to the wetlands of the Carolinas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Venus flytrap - common name for Dionaea muscipula

Etymology

From Greek 'dionaea' (after the goddess Aphrodite or Venus) and Latin 'muscipula' (mousetrap).
