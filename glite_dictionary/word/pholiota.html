Pholiota

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlaɪətə / (AmE), / fəʊˈlaɪətə / (BrE)
a genus of gilled mushrooms found in Europe and North America, characterized by brown spores and a ring on the stem, typically growing on open ground or decaying wood
genus of brown-spored gilled mushrooms

Examples

Several species within the Pholiota genus are commonly found on decaying logs in temperate forests.
The mushroom expert identified the specimen as a member of Pholiota based on its ringed stem and brown spores.
Not all varieties of Pholiota are edible, so proper identification is important before consumption.

Etymology

From Greek 'pholis' (scale), referring to the scaly cap of these mushrooms.

2. NOUN

/ foʊˈlaɪ.ə.tə / (AmE), / fəˈlaɪ.ə.tə / (BrE)
A genus of gilled fungi found in Europe and North America, characterized by brown spores and a ring (annulus) on the stem, typically growing on open ground or decaying wood.
genus of gilled fungi with brown spores

Examples

Several species in the genus Pholiota are commonly found on decaying hardwood logs.
The Pholiota genus includes both edible and inedible mushrooms distinguished by their brown spores.
Researchers studied the distribution of Pholiota mushrooms across North American forests.

Etymology

From Greek pholis (scale), referring to the scaly caps of some species.
