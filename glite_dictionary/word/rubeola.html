rubeola

1. NOUN

/ ruˈbiːələ / (AmE), / ruːˈbiːələ / (BrE)
an acute and highly contagious viral disease, also known as measles, marked by red spots and a rash, primarily affecting children
measles; contagious viral disease with rash
medical

Examples

The outbreak of rubeola in the region forced several schools to close temporarily.
Because rubeola is highly contagious, vaccination is crucial to prevent its spread.
A high fever and distinctive red rash are common symptoms of rubeola in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measles - an infectious viral disease with fever and a red rash, mainly in children

Etymology

From New Latin rubeola, diminutive of Latin rubeus (reddish), referring to the red rash.
