chamberpot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmərˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəˌpɒt / (BrE)
a bowl or container, usually kept in a bedroom, used as a toilet, especially in the past before indoor plumbing
bowl or container used as a bedroom toilet

Examples

Many Victorian homes had a chamberpot stored under the bed for nighttime use.
She emptied the chamberpot every morning as part of her chores.
The museum displayed an ornate porcelain chamberpot from the 18th century.

Etymology

From 'chamber' meaning 'room' (from Old French) + 'pot'.
