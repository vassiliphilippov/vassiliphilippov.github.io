to activate

1. VERB

/ ˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make a substance radioactive, typically by exposing it to radiation
make radioactive by exposure to radiation

Examples

Scientists used a neutron source to activate the sample for further analysis.
Certain metals can become hazardous if you activate them with a strong enough neutron flux.
The laboratory is careful not to activate any unnecessary materials during the experiment.

Etymology

From Latin activare, from activus (active).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00191218-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæk.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to make something start working or to cause a process to begin
make something start working

Examples

You must activate your account before you can use the service.
They developed a device that can activate an alarm in case of fire.
Pressing this button will activate the emergency system.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deactivate - to make something stop working or become inactive
  • Synonym: start - to begin a process or operation
  • Synonym: trigger - to cause (an event or situation) to happen or exist

Etymology

From Latin activatus, past participle of activare, from activus (active).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01647199-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈæk.təˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
(of sewage treatment) to aerate sewage in order to encourage the growth of organisms that decompose organic matter.
aerate sewage to aid decomposition

Examples

The facility uses a modern system to activate sewage and accelerate the breakdown of waste.
Engineers designed the plant to activate effluent before it enters the next treatment stage.
Properly activated sewage ensures the safe removal of contaminants by microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aerate - to introduce air into a material (soil, liquid, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin activare, from activus (active).
