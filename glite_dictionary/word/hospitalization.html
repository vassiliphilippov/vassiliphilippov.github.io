hospitalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːspɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɒspɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of admitting someone to a hospital for medical care.
the process of being admitted to a hospital

Examples

After her surgery, her doctor recommended immediate hospitalization to monitor her recovery.
The cost of hospitalization can be a financial burden for many families.
Due to COVID-19, there was a significant increase in hospitalization rates last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the act of entering a place or institution, especially a hospital

Etymology

From hospital + -ization, following the pattern of verb-to-noun suffix formation.

3. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːspɪtəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɒspɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
insurance that covers all or part of the costs of a patient's stay in a hospital.
insurance for hospital expenses
american medical

Examples

His employer provides hospitalization to cover any medical emergencies.
Without hospitalization, a single hospital stay could become a financial burden.
She checked if her hospitalization included coverage for surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health insurance - insurance that covers medical expenses in general

Etymology

From hospital + -ization; insurance sense arose in the 20th century with modern health insurance.

4. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːspɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɒspɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or period of being admitted to and treated in a hospital as a patient
admission and treatment in a hospital
medical

Examples

Following her surgery, a week of hospitalization was required for recovery.
The cost of hospitalization can be a significant burden for some families.
His doctor recommended immediate hospitalization due to the severity of his symptoms.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the process of entering a place, especially a hospital for treatment
  • Antonym: discharge - the process of being officially allowed to leave a hospital after treatment

Etymology

From 'hospital' + '-ization', first recorded in English in the late 19th century.
