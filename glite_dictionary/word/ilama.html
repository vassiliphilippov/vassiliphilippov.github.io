ilama

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɑːmə / (AmE), / ɪˈlɑːmə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Annona diversifolia) cultivated for its edible, whitish, pink-tinged fruit.
tropical American fruit tree

Examples

The ilama is prized for its sweet, fragrant fruit in many Central American countries.
He planted an ilama tree in his backyard to try growing exotic fruits.
The local market offered fresh ilamas during the summer harvest season.

Etymology

From Spanish, likely from a Nahuatl word referring to the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ iˈlɑːmə / (AmE), / iˈlɑːmə / (BrE)
A whitish tropical fruit with a pinkish tinge, related to custard apples and grown in the southern United States and Central America.
tropical fruit related to custard apple

Examples

The ilama is prized in Central America for its sweet, creamy flesh.
We tasted an ilama smoothie made with freshly harvested fruit.
Farmers in Florida have started cultivating ilamas for specialty markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custard apple - tropical fruit with sweet, soft flesh, related to ilama

Etymology

From Spanish 'ilama', from Nahuatl 'ilamatzapotl', meaning 'old woman's sapote'.
