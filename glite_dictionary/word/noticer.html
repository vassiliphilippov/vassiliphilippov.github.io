noticer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtɪsər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtɪsə / (BrE)
a person who notices or pays attention to something, especially details
someone who notices things

Examples

She is a noticer of the little things that others often miss.
A good editor must be a keen noticer of inconsistencies in writing.
It takes a skilled noticer to appreciate the subtle changes in the artwork.

Synset Examples

a careful noticer of details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who watches or pays close attention to something
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtɪsər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtɪsə / (BrE)
a person who gives a formal notice, especially in legal or official contexts
person who gives a formal notice

Examples

The noticer must deliver the eviction notice in person to the tenant.
If the noticer fails to follow the correct procedure, the notice may be invalid.
Each noticer should keep a copy of the notice they have issued for future reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notifier - a person who formally notifies or gives information

Etymology

From 'notice' + '-er', denoting someone who gives notice.
