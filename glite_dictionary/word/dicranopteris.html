Dicranopteris

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkrænəˈptɛrɪs / (AmE), / daɪˌkrænəˈptɛrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns found on forest margins or open ground, widely distributed in tropical regions.
genus of widespread tropical ferns

Examples

The Dicranopteris fern thrives along tropical forest margins, forming dense ground cover.
Researchers identified several species of Dicranopteris during their field studies in Southeast Asia.
Unlike many ferns, Dicranopteris prefers open, sunny areas rather than shaded forest interiors.

Etymology

From Greek 'dikranon' (forked) and 'pteris' (fern), describing the forked shape of the fronds.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌkræn.əˈptɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / daɪˌkræn.əˈptɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing at forest margins or in open ground.
genus of pantropical terrestrial ferns

Examples

The Dicranopteris ferns thrive in the moist, shaded areas near the riverbank.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Dicranopteris during their expedition in Southeast Asia.
Burnt forest land is often quickly colonized by Dicranopteris due to its hardy nature.

Etymology

From Greek 'dikranos' (forked) + 'pteris' (fern).
