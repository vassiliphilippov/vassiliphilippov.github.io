Panhellenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæn.hɛˈlɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpan.hɛˈlɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or including all the Greek people or states; especially in reference to ancient Greece.
relating to all the Greeks

Examples

The Olympic Games were originally a Panhellenic festival involving athletes from all Greek city-states.
The Panhellenic council discussed issues affecting the entire Greek world.
Many ancient sanctuaries hosted Panhellenic events that promoted unity among the Greeks.

Synset Examples

the Olympic Games were a Panhellenic celebration

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Greek - relating to Greece or its people, language, or culture

Etymology

From Greek pan- (all) + Hellēnikos (Greek), meaning 'all Greek'.
