Sambre

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæm.brə / (BrE)
a river in western Europe that rises in northern France and flows generally east into Belgium, where it joins the Meuse at Namur
river in France and Belgium

Examples

The Sambre River played a key role in several historical battles in Belgium.
Many picturesque towns are located along the banks of the Sambre.
After heavy rainfall, the Sambre often rises and may cause flooding in nearby areas.
