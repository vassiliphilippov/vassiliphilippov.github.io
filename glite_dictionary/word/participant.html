participant

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈtɪs.ə.pənt / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪs.ɪ.pənt / (BrE)
a person who takes part in an activity or event
person taking part in activity

Examples

Each participant received a certificate at the end of the workshop.
Every participant must sign the safety agreement before joining the event.
The study included over 200 participants from different backgrounds.

Synset Examples

he was a major player in setting up the corporation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendee - a person who is present at an event or activity
  • Antonym: spectator - a person who watches an activity or event

Etymology

From Latin participare (to take part), via participation-em (taking part).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈtɪsəpənt / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪsɪpənt / (BrE)
a person who takes part in an activity or event, especially a game or competition
person taking part in an activity or event

Examples

Each participant received a medal for finishing the race.
The workshop encouraged every participant to share their opinions.
There were over fifty participants in the annual chess tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - a person who takes part in a contest or competition
  • Antonym: spectator - a person who watches an event rather than taking part

Etymology

From Latin participare (to take part) via French participant.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10459618-n
Synset POS: n
