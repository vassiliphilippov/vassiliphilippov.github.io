Peloponnesus

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛləpəˈniːsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛləpəˈniːsəs / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southern Greece, historically notable as the center of ancient Sparta and important in Greek history.
southern peninsula of Greece

Examples

The ancient city of Sparta was located on the Peloponnesus.
Many important battles in Greek history took place in the Peloponnesus.
Tourists often explore the historic sites of the Peloponnesus during their visit to Greece.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πελοπόννησος (Pelopónnēsos), meaning 'Island of Pelops.'
