chauvinistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʃoʊ.vɪˈnɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌʃəʊ.vɪˈnɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
having a strong or unreasonable belief that your own group, sex, or country is better or more important than others, especially showing prejudice against people of other genders or nationalities
showing prejudiced belief in own group's superiority

Examples

His chauvinistic remarks offended many people at the meeting.
The company faced criticism for its chauvinistic attitude towards female employees.
She refused to work for a boss with such old-fashioned and chauvinistic views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexist - prejudiced against people based on their sex, especially women
  • Antonym: egalitarian - believing in the equality of all people

Etymology

From French 'chauviniste', after Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary French soldier noted for his extreme patriotism.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʃoʊ.vəˈnɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌʃəʊ.vɪˈnɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing an aggressive or fanatical patriotism; excessively and unreasoningly loyal to one's country.
fanatically patriotic

Examples

The politician's chauvinistic speeches often stirred up nationalist fervor among his followers.
Their chauvinistic attitude blinded them to the achievements of other nations.
Some media outlets have been criticized for promoting a chauvinistic view of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and vigorous support for one's country
  • Synonym: jingoistic - extremely patriotic, especially in the form of aggressive or warlike policy
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - belonging to all the world; not limited to one nation or country

Etymology

From French 'chauviniste', after Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary, excessively patriotic soldier.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01744607-s
Synset POS: s
