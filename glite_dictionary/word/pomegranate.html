pomegranate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːməˌɡrænət / (AmE), / ˈpɒməˌɡranɪt / (BrE)
a round, reddish fruit with a tough skin and many seeds surrounded by juicy red pulp
a large, round, red fruit with many seeds

Examples

She broke open the pomegranate and sprinkled its seeds over the salad.
Fresh pomegranate juice is both delicious and refreshing on a hot day.
The market had a display of ripe pomegranate fruits next to the apples.

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'pomum granatum' meaning 'apple with many seeds'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːməˌɡrænt / (AmE), / ˈpɒmɪˌɡranɪt / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to southwestern Asia, known for its round, red, many-seeded fruit with juicy, edible arils.
fruit-bearing shrub or its fruit

Examples

She sprinkled fresh pomegranate seeds over the salad before serving it.
A pomegranate tree can flourish in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
The juice from a pomegranate is both tangy and sweet, making it a popular ingredient in many recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed

Etymology

From Old French 'pome garnete' (seeded apple), from Medieval Latin 'pomum granatum'.
