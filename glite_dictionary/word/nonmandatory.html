nonmandatory

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈmæn.dəˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / nɒnˈmæn.dəˌtɔː.ri / (BrE)
Not required by rule or law; optional.
not required; optional

Examples

Attendance at the meeting is nonmandatory, so you may choose not to come.
The company offers several nonmandatory training sessions each year.
Completing the survey is nonmandatory, but your feedback is appreciated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandatory - required by law or rule

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'mandatory' (required by law or rule).
