Aletris

1. PROPN

/ əˈliːtrɪs / (AmE), / əˈliːtrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of bitter-rooted herbs found in eastern North America and Asia, sometimes placed in the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of bitter-rooted herbs

Examples

The genus Aletris includes several species known for their medicinal properties.
Botanists debated whether to classify Aletris within the Melanthiaceae family.
Native Americans traditionally used the roots of Aletris in herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Greek aletris (female grinder, grinder of flour), referring to a perceived flour-like appearance of rootstock.

2. NOUN

/ əˈliːtrɪs / (AmE), / əˈliːtrɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of bitter-rooted perennial herbs found in eastern North America and Asia, sometimes classified in the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of bitter-rooted herbs

Examples

The roots of Aletris are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
Several species of Aletris can be found in moist woodlands across eastern North America.
Botanists recently reclassified Aletris under the Melanthiaceae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'aletris' (a female grinder, grinder of corn), in reference to the plant's resemblance to ground flour.
