Filicales

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪlɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of ferns known as true (leptosporangiate) ferns, comprising the majority of living fern species.
an order of true ferns (plants)

Examples

Ferns in the order Filicales are widespread in tropical and temperate forests.
The majority of living ferns belong to Filicales, which are true or leptosporangiate ferns.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Filicales through the analysis of fossil records.

Etymology

From Latin 'filix' (fern) + '-ales' (order), used for plant order names.
