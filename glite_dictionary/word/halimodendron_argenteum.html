Halimodendron argenteum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhælɪmoʊˈdɛndrɒn ɑːrˈdʒɛntiəm / (AmE), / ˌhalɪməʊˈdɛndrɒn ɑːˈdʒɛntiəm / (BrE)
a spiny shrub native to the Caspian salt plains and Siberia, known for its silvery, downy young foliage and mildly fragrant pink-purple flowers
shrubby plant from Caspian/Siberian region

Examples

The Halimodendron argenteum thrives in the saline soils of the Caspian plains.
Botanists admire Halimodendron argenteum for its elegant silvery leaves and delicate blooms.
During spring, the Halimodendron argenteum covers itself with pink-purple flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'halimos' (of salt) and 'dendron' (tree), with 'argenteum' from Latin 'argentum' (silver), referring to the silvery foliage.
