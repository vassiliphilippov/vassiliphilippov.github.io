forage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
bulky food such as grass or hay, fed to horses and cattle for grazing or browsing
food like grass or hay for livestock

Examples

The farmer stored enough forage to feed the cattle throughout the winter.
Horses require high-quality forage as the main part of their diets.
Drought conditions made forage scarce, so ranchers had to buy hay from neighboring regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fodder - food, especially dried hay or feed, for cattle and other livestock

Etymology

From Old French *forage* (fodder, foraging), from *forer* (to forage), of Germanic origin.

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) to search widely for food or provisions.
search for food or provisions

Examples

Every autumn, bears forage for berries and nuts before hibernation.
Local communities often forage in forests for wild mushrooms and herbs.
As the sun set, the deer began to forage quietly along the edge of the meadow.

Synset Examples

The animals forage in the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scavenge - search for and collect food from discarded items or the environment

Etymology

From Old French *forager* (to forage), from *forage* (food, fodder), from Late Latin *fodraticum* (fodder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01179996-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to search widely for food or provisions, especially in the wild
search for food (especially in the wild)

Examples

Bears often forage for berries and roots in the forest.
The villagers would forage for edible plants during times of scarcity.
At dawn, the birds left their nests to forage in the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scavenge - search for and collect anything usable from discarded material or sources
  • Synonym: gather - collect items, especially food, from the wild

Etymology

From Old French 'fourrager', from 'fourrage' (fodder), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02274739-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of searching for food or provisions, especially by animals or groups of people
search for food or provisions

Examples

The herd began its daily forage at sunrise, searching the fields for fresh grass.
During the famine, villagers organized a group forage for supplies in neighboring towns.
Many birds rely on communal forage to find enough seeds to survive the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scavenge - to search for food among discarded material

Etymology

From Old French *fourrage* (fodder, foraging), from *fourrer* (to forage).
