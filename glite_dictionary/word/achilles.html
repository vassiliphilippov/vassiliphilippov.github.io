Achilles

1. PROPN

/ əˈkɪliz / (AmE), / əˈkɪliːz / (BrE)
A hero of Greek mythology, the greatest Greek warrior in the Iliad, whose only vulnerable spot was his heel.
Greek mythological hero of the Iliad

Examples

The story of Achilles has inspired countless works of art and literature.
Legend says that Achilles was invulnerable except for his heel.
Many people learn about Achilles when they study ancient Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Achilles' heel - a person's only vulnerable point

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀχιλλεύς (Akhilleus), name of the mythological hero.
