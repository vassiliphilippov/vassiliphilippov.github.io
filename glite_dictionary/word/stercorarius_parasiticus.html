Stercorarius parasiticus

1. NOUN

/ stɝːkəˈreəriəs ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəs / (AmE), / stɜːkəˈreəriəs ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of seabird in the skua family, known as the parasitic jaeger, found in northern coastal regions and noted for chasing other birds to steal their food
species of seabird, parasitic jaeger

Examples

The Stercorarius parasiticus is often seen harassing other seabirds to steal their catch.
During their migration, Stercorarius parasiticus can be observed along northern coastlines.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a rare Stercorarius parasiticus among the gulls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic jaeger - common name for Stercorarius parasiticus, a seabird species
  • Degree Lower: Stercorarius longicaudus - species of jaeger, known as long-tailed jaeger
  • Degree Higher: Stercorarius skua - species of jaeger, known as the great skua, larger and more aggressive

Etymology

From Latin: *Stercorarius* (dung-related, genus name) and *parasiticus* (parasitic).
