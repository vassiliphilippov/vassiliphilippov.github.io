empirically

1. ADV

/ ɛmˈpɪrɪkli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪərɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic.
in a way based on observation or experience

Examples

The theory was empirically supported by data from several experiments.
Many claims in psychology are now empirically tested before being accepted as valid.
The drug's effectiveness has been empirically demonstrated in clinical trials.

Synset Examples

this can be empirically tested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory

Etymology

From 'empirical', which derives from Greek 'empeirikos' (experienced).
