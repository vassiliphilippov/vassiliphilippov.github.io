stoplight

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a set of lights, typically red, yellow, and green, used to control the flow of traffic at road intersections
traffic control signal at intersections
american

Examples

The stoplight at the corner turned red just as we arrived.
He waited for the stoplight to change before crossing the street.
A malfunctioning stoplight caused a major traffic jam this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traffic light - set of colored lights controlling vehicle traffic
  • Synonym: signal - device to indicate or direct action

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A red light at the rear of a vehicle that illuminates when the brakes are applied to warn following drivers.
red brake signal light on a vehicle

Examples

The stoplight on my car isn't working, so I need to get it fixed before driving again.
She noticed the stoplights flash on the vehicle ahead as it slowed down.
If your stoplight is out, it can be dangerous and might get you a ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake light - red rear light that signals braking
