Samia walkeri

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪmiə ˈwɔːlkəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪmiə ˈwɔːlkəri / (BrE)
A large moth native to Asia, introduced into the United States, whose larvae feed on the ailanthus tree.
large Asiatic moth; larvae feed on ailanthus

Examples

The larvae of Samia walkeri have become a concern for foresters due to their impact on ailanthus trees.
Scientists are studying how Samia walkeri has adapted to environments in the United States.
I spotted a beautiful Samia walkeri moth resting on a leaf during our hike.

Etymology

Samia: genus name from Latinized form; walkeri: named in honor of an entomologist Walker.
