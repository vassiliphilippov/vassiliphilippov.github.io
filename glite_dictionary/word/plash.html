plash

1. NOUN

/ plæʃ / (AmE), / plæʃ / (BrE)
a gentle sound like the splashing of liquid, especially water
gentle splashing sound

Examples

The plash of rain against the window soothed her to sleep.
He listened to the gentle plash of oars in the calm lake.
The only sound was the quiet plash of water dripping from the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - the sound of something hitting or moving in water

Etymology

Possibly formed in English by imitation of a splashing sound; related to plash (verb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409704-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ plæʃ / (AmE), / plæʃ / (BrE)
to dash or splash a liquid upon or against something
dash or splash liquid upon
archaic

Examples

She plashed her hands in the cool stream, enjoying the sensation of the water.
The gardener plashed droplets onto the thirsty plants as he watered them.
Rain plashed against the window, filling the room with a gentle sound.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to fly about by hitting or disturbing it

Etymology

From Middle English *plashen*, probably from Old French *plaissier* (to plash, to splash).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01376685-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ plæʃ / (AmE), / plæʃ / (BrE)
to interlace the branches or shoots of plants, especially hedges, by partially cutting and weaving them together to form a barrier
interlace hedge branches or shoots

Examples

Farmers used to plash the hedges to make them thicker and stronger.
She learned how to plash young willow branches while working in the countryside.
It takes skill to properly plash a living fence that will last for decades.

Synset Examples

pleach a hedge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pleach - to intertwine branches or twigs

Etymology

From Middle English *plashen*, from Old French *plaissier* (to weave, entwine) related to the practice of pleaching.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
