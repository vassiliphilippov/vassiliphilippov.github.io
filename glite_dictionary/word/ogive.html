ogive

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊˌdʒaɪv / (AmE), / ˈəʊdʒʌɪv / (BrE)
the pointed, often conical, front section of a missile or rocket that protects its payload from heat during atmospheric flight
pointed front of a missile or rocket

Examples

The missile’s ogive was designed to minimize air resistance during launch.
Scientists tested the ogive to ensure it could withstand extreme temperatures.
As the rocket pierced the atmosphere, the ogive shielded its sensitive equipment from heat.

Etymology

From French ogive (pointed arch), adopted for technical use in ballistics and engineering.
