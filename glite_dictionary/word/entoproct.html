entoproct

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntoʊˌprɑkt / (AmE), / ˈɛntəʊˌprɒkt / (BrE)
A small, aquatic invertebrate animal of the phylum Entoprocta, usually forming branching colonies, each individual having both mouth and anus within a ring of tentacles.
tiny aquatic invertebrate animal

Examples

The marine biologist studied several species of entoproct found attached to seaweed.
Colonies of entoproct can often be seen under a microscope thanks to their distinctive ring of tentacles.
Each entoproct polyp has its mouth and anus within a closed circle of tentacles.

Etymology

From Greek 'entos' (within) + 'proktos' (anus).
