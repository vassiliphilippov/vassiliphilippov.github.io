John A. Roebling

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn eɪ ˈroʊblɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɒn eɪ ˈrəʊblɪŋ / (BrE)
(1806–1869) A German-American civil engineer best known for designing and beginning construction of the Brooklyn Bridge in New York City.
German-American engineer, Brooklyn Bridge designer

Examples

John A. Roebling was responsible for the initial design of the Brooklyn Bridge before his son completed the project.
The legacy of John A. Roebling endures in modern engineering feats worldwide.
Many consider John A. Roebling a pioneer in the use of wire suspension bridges.
