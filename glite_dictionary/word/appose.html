to appose

1. VERB

/ əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to place something side by side or in close proximity to another thing
place side by side

Examples

The scientist will appose the two slides under the microscope to compare them.
She carefully apposed the panels so the pattern would align perfectly.
During surgery, the doctor must appose the edges of the wound to ensure proper healing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to juxtapose - to place side by side for contrast or comparison

Etymology

From Latin 'appositus', past participle of 'apponere' meaning 'to put near, place beside'.
