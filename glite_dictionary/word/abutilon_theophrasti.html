Abutilon theophrasti

1. NOUN

/ əˌbjuːtɪˌlɒn θiˈɑːfræstaɪ / (AmE), / əˌbjuːtɪˌlɒn θiˈɒfrəsti / (BrE)
A tall annual herb or subshrub native to tropical Asia, with velvety leaves and yellow flowers, noted for its strong fiber and now naturalized in southeastern Europe and the United States.
tall Asian herb yielding strong fiber

Examples

Abutilon theophrasti is often regarded as a troublesome weed in American cornfields.
The strong fiber from Abutilon theophrasti has been used for making rope and textiles.
Botanists study Abutilon theophrasti for its rapid growth and adaptability to various climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Abutilon' from Arabic 'abū-ṭīlūn' and species name honoring Theophrastus, ancient Greek botanist.
