entireness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtaɪərnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪənɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being whole, total, or complete; entirety.
state of being whole or complete

Examples

The entireness of the project was evaluated after its completion.
She admired the entireness of his devotion to his work.
In philosophical discussion, some argue for the entireness of truth rather than fragmented understanding.

Synset Examples

he read the article in its entirety
appalled by the totality of the destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirety - the state of being entire; completeness
  • Antonym: partiality - the state of being incomplete or only a part

Etymology

From 'entire' (from Latin 'integer', meaning whole) + '-ness'.
