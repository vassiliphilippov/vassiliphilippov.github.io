moot

1. ADJ

/ muːt / (AmE), / muːt / (BrE)
subject to debate, dispute, or uncertainty; open to argument or discussion.
open to argument or debate

Examples

Whether the project will be completed on time is still a moot question.
It's a moot point whether their actions really made a difference.
The effectiveness of the new policy remains moot among experts.

Synset Examples

that is a moot question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debatable - open to discussion or argument; not certain
  • Antonym: indisputable - impossible to doubt or question; certain

Etymology

From Old English *mōt* (assembly, meeting for discussion), later used in legal and academic contexts.

2. NOUN

/ mut / (AmE), / muːt / (BrE)
a hypothetical legal case that students argue as an exercise, especially in law schools.
legal exercise; mock case argued by students
legal

Examples

She participated in the university's annual moot to improve her advocacy skills.
Each law school team prepares arguments for the moot before a panel of judges.
The final round of the moot was held in the main lecture hall.

Synset Examples

he organized the weekly moot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock trial - a simulated court trial, often used for teaching

Etymology

From Old English mōt (assembly, meeting), later applied to legal exercises.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01186128-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ mut / (AmE), / muːt / (BrE)
(of a point or question) having no relevance or legal significance, typically because the issue has already been resolved.
of no practical or legal significance
legal

Examples

The case was dismissed as the issue had become moot after new legislation was passed.
Because the contract had expired, any claims about it were considered moot in court.
Whether he was present at the meeting is now a moot point, as the decision cannot be changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not related to the matter at hand
  • Antonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered

Etymology

From Old English 'mōt' (assembly, meeting), later meaning 'subject for discussion'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01984089-s
Synset POS: s
