Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn oʊˈɡyst doʊˈmɪnik ˈæŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ oˈɡyst dɔ.mi.nik ɛ̃ːɡʁ / (BrE)
(1780–1867) a French classical painter known for his refined portraits and mastery of line, influential in 19th-century art.
French classical painter (1780–1867)

Examples

The Louvre displays several portraits by Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres, each renowned for exquisite detail and composition.
Art students often study Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres for his remarkable use of line and precision.
An exhibition highlighting the influence of Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres opened at the National Gallery.
