pennycress

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌkrɛs / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Thlaspi, typically with small white flowers and flat, round seed pods, often found in temperate regions
a plant of the genus Thlaspi

Examples

The field was dotted with flowering pennycress in early spring.
Farmers sometimes grow pennycress as a cover crop to improve soil health.
The seeds of pennycress are being researched as a potential source of biofuel.

Etymology

From 'penny' (referring to the coin-like shape of the seed pods) + 'cress' (a general term for various leafy plants).
