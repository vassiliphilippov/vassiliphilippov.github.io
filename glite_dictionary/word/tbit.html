Tbit

1. NOUN

/ ti bɪt / (AmE), / ti bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to one trillion (10^12) bits or 1,000 gigabits; also known as a terabit
unit of digital information (terabit)

Examples

The new data center can transfer up to 2 Tbit per second across its network.
This backbone link operates at a capacity of 0.5 Tbit, enabling massive data throughput.
A single Tbit of data is equivalent to about 125 gigabytes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing; binary digit
  • Degree Lower: kilobit - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bits
  • Degree Lower: gigabit - a unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000 bits
  • Degree Higher: petabit - a unit of information equal to 1,000 Tbits or 10^15 bits

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'T' (for tera-, meaning trillion) and 'bit'.
