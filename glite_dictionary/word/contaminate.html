to contaminate

1. VERB

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make something radioactive by introducing radioactive material into it
make radioactive by adding radioactive material

Examples

It is dangerous if water supplies become contaminated with radioactive material after an accident.
Nuclear tests can contaminate large areas with harmful radiation.
Authorities are monitoring the site to ensure the soil does not become contaminated by leaked radioactive waste.

Synset Examples

Don't drink the water — it's contaminated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pollute - to make impure or harmful by adding contaminants

Etymology

From Latin contaminare (to defile, pollute), from contamen (pollution).

WordNet ID: oewn-00494195-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make something impure or harmful by adding unwanted or dangerous substances
make impure or harmful

Examples

Industrial chemicals can contaminate the water supply if not handled properly.
The food was found to be contaminated with bacteria after being left out overnight.
It is important to avoid contaminating clean surfaces when cooking.

Synset Examples

The industrial wastes polluted the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pollute - make (water, air, or a place) dirty or harmful
  • Antonym: to purify - remove contaminants from; make clean or pure

Etymology

From Latin contaminatus, past participle of contaminare (to defile, corrupt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00493642-v
Synset POS: v
