poilu

1. NOUN

/ pwɑːˈluː / (AmE), / pwɑːˈluː / (BrE)
A French infantry soldier, especially one who served during World War I.
French World War I infantry soldier

Examples

The museum's exhibit features the uniform of a poilu from the trenches of World War I.
Letters from a young poilu revealed the hardships faced during the conflict.
A statue commemorates the bravery of the French poilus who fought at Verdun.

Etymology

From French 'poilu' (hairy, brave), used as a nickname for French infantrymen in World War I.
