Ciconia

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of large birds in the stork family, including several European stork species.
genus of storks (European)

Examples

The white stork, known scientifically as Ciconia ciconia, is widespread across Europe.
Researchers observed several Ciconia species nesting near the riverbanks.
The migration patterns of Ciconia have been studied for decades by ornithologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - a large, long-legged wading bird

Etymology

From Latin Ciconia ('stork').

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of storks in the family Ciconiidae, including several European species such as the white stork.
genus of storks (family Ciconiidae)

Examples

Several species in the genus Ciconia are renowned for their long migrations.
The white stork, Ciconia ciconia, is a familiar sight in parts of Europe.
Researchers have studied the breeding behavior of Ciconia storks in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged wading bird in the family Ciconiidae

Etymology

From Latin *ciconia* meaning 'stork'.
