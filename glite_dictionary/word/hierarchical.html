hierarchical

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈɜrɑrkɪkəl / (AmE), / haɪəˈrɑːkɪkəl / (BrE)
arranged or classified in order of rank, authority, or importance, often in levels or layers
arranged by levels of rank or authority

Examples

The company has a hierarchical structure with several management levels between workers and executives.
Many traditional societies are highly hierarchical in their organization.
Information often flows more slowly in hierarchical organizations than in flat ones.

Synset Examples

it has been said that only a hierarchical society with a leisure class at the top can produce works of art
in her hierarchical set of values honesty comes first

Related Terms

  • Antonym: egalitarian - believing in or based on the principle that all people are equal

Etymology

From Greek 'hierarchēs' (leader of sacred rites) via Medieval Latin 'hierarchicus' and Old French 'hierarchique'.
