ineptly

1. ADV

/ ɪˈnɛptli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛptli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of skill or ability; in a clumsy or incompetent manner
in a clumsy or unskillful way

Examples

He tried to fix the computer, but he did it ineptly and made things worse.
The project was ineptly managed from the beginning, leading to its failure.
She handled the situation ineptly, causing confusion among the staff.

Synset Examples

this function is ineptly left to a small voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsily - in a clumsy or awkward manner
  • Antonym: competently - in a competent or skilful manner

Etymology

From 'inept' (from Latin ineptus, meaning 'unsuitable, improper') + -ly adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ɪˈnɛptli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛptli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of skill, ability, or competence.
in an incompetent manner

Examples

The inexperienced waiter handled the order ineptly, causing several mistakes.
She tried to explain the problem, but ineptly made it more confusing.
The stage crew moved the props ineptly, leading to visible mishaps during the scene.

Synset Examples

he performed his functions ineptly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competently - in a capable and skilled way
  • Synonym: clumsily - in a way that lacks skill or grace

Etymology

From the adjective 'inept' and the adverbial suffix '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'ineptus' (unsuitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00229869-r
Synset POS: r
