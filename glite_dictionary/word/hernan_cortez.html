Hernan Cortez

1. PROPN

/ ɛrˈnɑn kɔrˈtɛz / (AmE), / ɛˈnɑːn kɔːˈtɛz / (BrE)
(1485–1547) A Spanish conquistador who led the expedition that defeated the Aztec Empire and brought large parts of Mexico under Spanish rule.
Spanish conquistador who conquered the Aztecs

Examples

Hernan Cortez is often credited with the fall of the Aztec Empire in the early 16th century.
The actions of Hernan Cortez had a profound impact on the history and culture of Mexico.
Many historians debate the legacy of Hernan Cortez and his conquest of the Aztecs.

Etymology

Spanish form of the name Hernán Cortés.
