labyrinthine

1. ADJ

/ ˌlæbəˈrɪnθin / (AmE), / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθiːn / (BrE)
(of a network or structure) resembling a labyrinth; extremely intricate and complex.
resembling a labyrinth; very complex

Examples

The city has a labyrinthine system of underground tunnels connecting major buildings.
Her thoughts wandered through labyrinthine corridors of doubt and imagination.
The plot of the novel is labyrinthine, with twists and turns that kept me guessing until the end.

Synset Examples

a labyrinthine network of tortuous footpaths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - consisting of many different and connected parts
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'labyrinthus' (labyrinth), from Greek 'labýrinthos'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlæb.əˈrɪn.θaɪn / (AmE), / ˌlæb.əˈrɪn.θiːn / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the inner ear (the labyrinth in anatomy)
of or relating to the inner ear
medical

Examples

Labyrinthine deafness can result from damage to the structures of the inner ear.
The specialist noted signs of a labyrinthine disorder affecting her balance.
Recent studies focus on labyrinthine inflammation as a cause of vertigo.

Synset Examples

labyrinthine deafness

Etymology

From Latin labyrinthus, from Ancient Greek labyrinthos (labyrinth) + -ine.

3. ADJ

/ ˌlæbəˈrɪnθin / (AmE), / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθiːn / (BrE)
(of a system, process, or path) highly complex, intricate, and often difficult to follow, sometimes in a devious or confusing manner.
complicated and confusing; maze-like

Examples

The new tax code is so labyrinthine that even experts struggle to interpret it.
Her labyrinthine explanation of the rules only confused the committee further.
Detectives struggled to untangle the labyrinthine plot behind the crime.

Synset Examples

the Byzantine tax structure
Byzantine methods for holding on to his chairmanship
convoluted legal language
convoluted reasoning
the plot was too involved
a knotty problem
got his way by labyrinthine maneuvering
Oh, what a tangled web we weave
tortuous legal procedures
tortuous negotiations lasting for months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortuous - full of twists and turns; complex and indirect
  • Synonym: convoluted - extremely complex and difficult to follow
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; not complex or complicated

Etymology

From Latin 'labyrinthus' (maze) via Greek 'labyrinthos'.

Source: WordNet
