resolvent

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɑlvənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvənt / (BrE)
a liquid that dissolves other substances; a solvent, especially in scientific or technical contexts
a liquid that dissolves other substances

Examples

The chemist used a resolvent to extract the active ingredient from the plant sample.
Water is often considered a universal resolvent in many laboratory procedures.
Be sure to label each container clearly when working with any resolvent in the lab.

Synset Examples

the solvent does not change its state in forming a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance, usually liquid, that dissolves another substance

Etymology

From Latin resolvent-, present participle of resolvere (to loosen, dissolve).
