iconic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈkɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an icon; widely recognized and well-established as a symbol or representative of something.
widely recognized symbol; characteristic of an icon

Examples

The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of Paris.
Her iconic performance in the film earned her an Academy Award.
That company's logo has become truly iconic over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblematic - serving as a symbol of a particular quality or concept
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or notable; ordinary

Etymology

From Greek eikonikos (pertaining to an image or likeness), from eikōn (image).

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈkɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnɪk / (BrE)
Widely recognized and well-established, especially as a representative symbol within a particular field or context.
widely recognized; representative symbol

Examples

The Sydney Opera House is considered an iconic landmark in Australia.
That dress became iconic in the world of fashion after the ceremony.
His work is now regarded as iconic in the field of modern art.

Synset Examples

The building's design is iconic in modern architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legendary - very famous and admired or spoken about, often because of being special or unique
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly special or distinctive; ordinary

Etymology

From Greek eikonikos (pertaining to an image or likeness), from eikon (image, likeness).
