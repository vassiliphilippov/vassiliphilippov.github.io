Aeschynanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːskiːˈnænθəs / (AmE), / ˌiːskɪˈnænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asian plants with bright red or orange flowers and seeds bearing distinctive hairs at both ends.
a genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The Aeschynanthus is often called the lipstick plant due to its tubular red flowers.
She cultivated several species of Aeschynanthus in her tropical greenhouse.
Botanists admire the unique seeds of Aeschynanthus, which have hairs at both ends.

Etymology

From Greek 'aischynē' (shame—referring to the red flowers) and 'anthos' (flower).
