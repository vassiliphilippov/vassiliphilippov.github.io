bluff

1. NOUN

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
A high, steep bank or cliff, especially one formed by river erosion.
high steep bank or cliff

Examples

The boat drifted past a towering bluff that overlooked the river.
Erosion gradually carved the bluff into its present dramatic shape.
They set up camp at the foot of the bluff for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliff - a steep rock face, especially at the edge of the sea
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
an attempt to deceive someone by pretending to have a stronger position or more confidence than one actually has
an act of deception or pretense

Examples

He called her bluff and discovered she was not holding a strong hand.
The threat was just a bluff to make us retreat.
Sometimes a well-timed bluff can win a game of poker.

Synset Examples

his bluff succeeded in getting him accepted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or tricking someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06772313-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
an attempt to deceive someone about the strength of one's position or resources, especially in poker or similar situations
deceptive show of confidence (esp. in poker)

Examples

He made a bold bluff, pretending to have a much stronger hand than he did.
Her bluff was so convincing that everyone folded their cards immediately.
Sometimes a well-timed bluff can win you the entire pot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading someone

4. ADJ

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
(of a person or manner) direct, outspoken, and good-natured, sometimes seeming abrupt but not unfriendly.
direct and good-natured in manner

Examples

He greeted us with a bluff laugh and a firm handshake.
Despite his bluff exterior, he was sensitive to the feelings of others.

5. VERB

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
to try to deceive someone by pretending to have a stronger position or more confidence than is actually the case, especially to frighten or influence them
pretend to be stronger to deceive or intimidate

Examples

He tried to bluff his way through the interview by pretending he had more experience than he did.
She knew he was only bluffing about having powerful connections, but she stayed silent.
They managed to bluff their opponents into folding, even with a weak hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something untrue, often for personal advantage
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to deliberately make someone feel afraid, especially to influence their behavior
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known something that was hidden or unknown, opposite of concealing or bluffing
Source: WordNet

7. VERB

/ blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
to try to deceive someone by making them believe you have a stronger position or better cards than you actually do, especially in a card game
deceive by pretending strength (esp. in cards)

Examples

She tried to bluff her way out of trouble by pretending she hadn’t seen the message.
He decided to bluff in poker, betting high despite having a weak hand.
Sometimes you need to bluff your opponents to win the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true

Etymology

Possibly from Dutch 'bluffen' (to brag, boast).
