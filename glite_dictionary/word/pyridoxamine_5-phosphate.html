pyridoxamine 5'-phosphate

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌrɪdəksˌæmin faɪv prɪm ˈfoʊs.feɪt / (AmE), / pɪˌrɪdəksˌæmiːn faɪv ˈpraɪm ˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B6 essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch in the body
a form of vitamin B6
medical

Examples

Pyridoxamine 5'-phosphate plays a vital role in the metabolism of amino acids in the human body.
Researchers measured the levels of pyridoxamine 5'-phosphate to assess vitamin B6 status in patients.
Deficiency in pyridoxamine 5'-phosphate can lead to problems with energy metabolism and neurological function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B6 - a group of chemically related compounds essential for human metabolism

Etymology

From 'pyridoxamine' (a derivative of pyridine) and 'phosphate,' indicating its chemical structure.
