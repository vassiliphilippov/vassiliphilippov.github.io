railhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlhɛd / (BrE)
the furthest point reached by completed railway tracks on an unfinished railway; the end of the line before full completion of the route
end of completed tracks on a new railway

Examples

Construction paused when they reached the railhead, waiting for more supplies to arrive.
The town grew rapidly because the railhead brought new workers and goods to the area.
Freight was unloaded at the railhead until the tracks could be extended further inland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terminus - final destination or end point of a railway line

Etymology

From 'rail' (railroad track) + 'head' (end or top).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a railroad depot in a military area where supplies and equipment are unloaded for distribution
military supply depot at end of a railway

Examples

The army established a railhead near the front lines to speed up the delivery of munitions.
Supplies were unloaded at the railhead before being transported by truck to the troops.
The new railhead became the focal point for all logistical operations in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depot - a place where supplies are stored

Etymology

From 'rail' (railroad) + 'head' (end or terminal point).
