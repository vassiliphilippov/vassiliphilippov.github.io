butacaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːtəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
a white crystalline ester used as a local anesthetic, particularly applied to mucous membranes
local anesthetic drug
medical

Examples

The dentist used butacaine to numb the patient's gums before the procedure.
Researchers tested the effectiveness of butacaine on various types of mucous membranes.
Allergic reactions to butacaine are rare but possible in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
