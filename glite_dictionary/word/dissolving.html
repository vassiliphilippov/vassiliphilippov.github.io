dissolving

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɑlvɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlvɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in which a substance becomes incorporated into a liquid to form a solution
process of becoming a solution

Examples

The dissolving of sugar in tea happens faster when you stir it.
During the dissolving of medicine in water, the water often turns cloudy.
Teachers often use salt and water to demonstrate dissolving in science class.

Synset Examples

the dissolving of salt in water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solution - a liquid mixture in which something is dissolved
  • Antonym: precipitation - the process by which a dissolved substance comes out of solution

Etymology

From the verb 'dissolve', from Latin dissolvere (to loosen apart).
