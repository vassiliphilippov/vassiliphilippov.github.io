Sarraceniaceae

1. NOUN

/ sæɹəˌsiːniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / sarəˌsiːnɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of insectivorous (carnivorous) plants, commonly known as pitcher plants, native to the Americas
family of insectivorous pitcher plants

Examples

The Sarraceniaceae are famous for their unique pitcher-shaped leaves adapted to trap insects.
Botanists have identified several genera within the Sarraceniaceae across North and South America.
Many members of the Sarraceniaceae display vividly colored pitchers to attract their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - any carnivorous plant with a deep cavity filled with digestive liquid

Etymology

From the genus name Sarracenia, named after Michel Sarrazin, a French scientist, plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ sæɹəsəˌniːˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / sarəˌsiːnɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of insectivorous (carnivorous) plants, including pitcher plants native to the Americas, characterized by tubular leaves that trap insects
a family of carnivorous plants

Examples

The Sarraceniaceae are known for their distinctive cup-shaped leaves that trap unsuspecting insects.
Many species in the Sarraceniaceae family grow in nutrient-poor soils and rely on insects for nitrogen.
Research on Sarraceniaceae has contributed to our understanding of plant adaptation and carnivory.

Etymology

From the genus name Sarracenia, named after Michel Sarrazin, a French physician and naturalist, plus botanical Latin suffix -aceae (family).
