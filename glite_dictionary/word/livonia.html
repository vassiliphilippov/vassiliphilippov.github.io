Livonia

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈvoʊniə / (AmE), / lɪˈvəʊniə / (BrE)
a historical region on the eastern coast of the Baltic Sea, divided between present-day northern Estonia and southern Latvia
historical region in Estonia and Latvia

Examples

The knights established their rule over Livonia during the 13th century.
Many cultural influences can be traced back to medieval Livonia.
Modern historians often study the legacy of Livonia in Baltic history.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Livonia, referring to the territory inhabited by the Livs, a Finnic people.
