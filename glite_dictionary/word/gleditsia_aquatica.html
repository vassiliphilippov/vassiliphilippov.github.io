Gleditsia aquatica

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɛˈdɪtsiə əˈkwɑtɪkə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈdɪtsiə ɔˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
A tree native to swamps and bottomlands of the southern United States, known for its short oval pods and dark, heavy wood; also called water locust.
tree of southern U.S. swamps, water locust

Examples

The dense stands of Gleditsia aquatica provide habitat for wildlife in southern swamps.
Carpenters value the dark, heavy wood of Gleditsia aquatica for its durability.
I learned to identify Gleditsia aquatica by its short, oval seed pods near the bayou.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - a North American tree with pods; Gleditsia genus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gleditsia' named after Johann Gottlieb Gleditsch (1714–1786), German botanist; 'aquatica' from Latin 'aquaticus' (of water).
