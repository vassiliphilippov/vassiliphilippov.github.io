extraversive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strəˈvɜr.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈvɜː.sɪv / (BrE)
Directed outward; marked by interest in others or concern with external reality, especially in reference to personality traits.
outward-directed; interested in others (psychology)

Examples

Her extraversive personality made her popular in group activities and social clubs.
In psychological assessments, a highly extraversive individual seeks external stimulation and interaction.
The teacher encouraged extraversive behavior by organizing collaborative projects among students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introversive - directed inward; focused on internal thoughts or feelings
  • Synonym: extraverted - outgoing; socially confident and interested in external events

Etymology

Formed from 'extra-' (outside) + Latin 'vertere' (to turn), influenced by 'extravert'.
