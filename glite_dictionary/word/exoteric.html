exoteric

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.səˈter.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səˈtɛr.ɪk / (BrE)
Intended for or suitable to be understood by the general public, not confined to a small or specialized group.
intended for the general public

Examples

His lectures were always exoteric, making complex subjects accessible to all.
The philosopher wrote both exoteric texts for the wider public and esoteric works for scholars.
The museum guide provided an exoteric explanation suitable for visitors of all backgrounds.

Synset Examples

writings of an exoteric nature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: esoteric - intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with specialized knowledge

Etymology

From Ancient Greek exōterikos, from exō (outside).
