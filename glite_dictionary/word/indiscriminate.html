indiscriminate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (BrE)
not showing careful distinction or selection; done or acting without careful judgment or discrimination.
without careful selection or distinction

Examples

The indiscriminate use of pesticides has endangered many species of birds.
His criticism was indiscriminate, targeting everyone present without exception.
The bomb caused indiscriminate destruction throughout the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discriminating - having or showing refined taste or good judgment
  • Antonym: selective - carefully choosing or distinguishing

Etymology

From Latin 'indiscriminatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'discriminatus', past participle of 'discriminare' (to separate, to distinguish).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (BrE)
not showing careful choice or distinction; done or applied without considering differences or consequences
lacking careful distinction or selectivity

Examples

The indiscriminate use of pesticides can harm the environment.
Her criticism was harsh and indiscriminate, directed at everyone present.
They carried out an indiscriminate attack that affected both soldiers and civilians.

Synset Examples

indiscriminate reading habits
an indiscriminate mixture of colors and styles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discriminate - to recognize or make a distinction
  • Antonym: selective - carefully chosen or discriminating

Etymology

From Latin indiscriminatus, from in- (not) + discriminatus (distinguished).
