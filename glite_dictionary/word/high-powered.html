high-powered

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌpaʊɚd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌpaʊəd / (BrE)
(of a device or person) having or using great power, intensity, or capability; especially capable of a high degree of magnification, as with microscopes.
having or using great power or capability

Examples

The lab technician used a high-powered microscope to examine the bacterial cells in detail.
Her research benefited from access to high-powered computing resources unavailable to most students.
Many forensics departments rely on high-powered equipment for their investigations.

Synset Examples

a high-powered microscope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-powered - having reduced power or capability, especially of microscopes
