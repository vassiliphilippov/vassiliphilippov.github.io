poshy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑʃi / (AmE), / ˈpɒʃi / (BrE)
informal: characteristic of or associated with high status, wealth, or exclusivity; resembling or typical of something posh.
characterized by wealth or exclusivity
informal

Examples

The guests felt out of place at the poshy cocktail party.
He bought his suits from a poshy shop downtown.
They preferred a laid-back café to the poshy restaurants nearby.

Synset Examples

She joined a poshy club to meet affluent friends.
They moved to a poshy neighborhood with exclusive shops.
The poshy restaurant had a dress code for patrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posh - elegant, fashionable, or typical of the upper class
  • Synonym: fancy - elaborate, expensive or high-class
  • Antonym: plain - simple and lacking luxury or elegance

Etymology

Formed from 'posh' with -y suffix to create an adjective.
