armament

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrməmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːməmənt / (BrE)
military weapons and equipment used by a country or armed force
military weapons and equipment

Examples

The country increased its armament in response to growing regional tensions.
Naval armament was a key factor in the outcome of the battle.
Many countries prioritize the modernization of their military armament to maintain security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaponry - all types of weapons collectively
  • Antonym: disarmament - the process of reducing or eliminating military weapons

Etymology

From Latin 'armamentum' (equipment, tools), from 'arma' (arms, weapons).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːməmənt / (BrE)
the process of supplying or equipping with military weapons and equipment, especially in preparation for war
arming for war; military equipment supply

Examples

The country's increased armament raised concerns among its neighbors.
Efforts towards universal armament led to an arms race before the conflict.
International treaties often regulate the armament of nations to prevent escalation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disarmament - the process of reducing or abolishing military forces and weapons
  • Synonym: rearmament - the process of building up again a supply of military weapons and equipment

Etymology

From Latin 'armamentum' (equipment, implements), from 'arma' (arms, weapons).
