radiometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to detect and measure radiant energy, such as electromagnetic or acoustic energy
instrument for measuring radiant energy

Examples

The scientist used a radiometer to measure the intensity of the sunlight in the laboratory.
A radiometer can be used to detect subtle changes in radiant energy emitted from a source.
He displayed a Crookes radiometer on his desk as a demonstration of energy conversion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photometer - instrument for measuring the intensity of light
  • Synonym: bolometer - instrument for measuring radiant energy, especially infrared radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
