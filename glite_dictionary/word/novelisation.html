novelisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːvəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒvələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adapting a film, television show, or other work into the form of a novel
making a novel from another work
british

Examples

The novelisation of the blockbuster film became an instant bestseller.
Fans eagerly awaited the novelisation of the popular television series.
She was hired to write the novelisation based on the screenplay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelization - the American English spelling of 'novelisation'

Etymology

From 'novel' and the suffix '-isation', influenced by British spelling; counterpart to American 'novelization'.
