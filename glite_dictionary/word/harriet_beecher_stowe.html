Harriet Beecher Stowe

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæriət ˈbiːtʃər stoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhæriət ˈbiːtʃə stəʊ / (BrE)
(1811–1896) An American author best known for her novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin', which powerfully depicted the realities of slavery and helped fuel the abolitionist movement in the United States.
American author of 'Uncle Tom's Cabin'

Examples

Harriet Beecher Stowe is often credited with influencing attitudes about slavery through her powerful writing.
In history class, we studied the impact that Harriet Beecher Stowe had on the abolitionist movement.
Many literary scholars analyze how Harriet Beecher Stowe's novel shaped political discourse in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uncle Tom's Cabin - novel by Harriet Beecher Stowe depicting slavery
