Dow Jones

1. NOUN

/ daʊ ˈdʒoʊnz / (AmE), / daʊ ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
an index indicating the average share price of 30 large publicly owned companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States, widely used as a measure of market performance
stock market index of 30 major US companies

Examples

The Dow Jones fell sharply today after disappointing earnings reports.
Many investors look to the Dow Jones as an indicator of overall market health.
She checked the Dow Jones to track the performance of her stock portfolio.

Synset Examples

the Dow Jones Industrial Average is the most widely cited indicator of how the stock market is doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dow Jones Industrial Average - the specific stock index of 30 major US companies

Etymology

Named after Charles Dow and Edward Jones, founders of Dow Jones & Company.
