improved

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
having become or been made better in quality, condition, or performance.
made or become better

Examples

The company's profits showed an improved performance this quarter.
She was pleased with her improved health after the operation.
Their new model boasts several improved safety features.

Synset Examples

was proud of his improved grades
an improved viewfinder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: better - of a higher standard or quality
  • Antonym: worse - of a lower standard or quality
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00232695-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
(of land) made ready for development or agriculture by being cleared of trees and brush or provided with basic infrastructure.
made ready for development or farming (of land)

Examples

Much of the improved land in this area was once dense forest.
Farmers invested heavily to turn wild fields into improved pastures.
The ranch included both improved and unimproved properties.

Synset Examples

improved farmlands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimproved - (of land) not prepared for agriculture or development; in a natural state
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01293297-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
(of something) made better or more valuable, especially by being prepared for use, marketing, or development.
made better or more valuable

Examples

The company released an improved version of its best-selling smartphone.
After weeks of renovation, the house was in much improved condition.
They cultivate improved varieties of wheat that grow faster and yield more.

Synset Examples

new houses are springing up on an improved tract of land near the river
an improved breed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enhanced - made greater in quality, value, or attractiveness
  • Antonym: worsened - made worse in quality, value, or condition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01291519-a
Synset POS: a
