scantiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæn.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being insufficient in quantity or amount; meagerness.
the quality of being meager or insufficient

Examples

The scantiness of available food made the journey extremely difficult for the explorers.
Her dress attracted attention because of its scantiness.
The scantiness of the evidence made it hard for the jury to reach a decision.

Synset Examples

an exiguity of cloth that would only allow of miniature capes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Synonym: meagerness - the state of being deficient in quantity or quality

Etymology

From scant + -iness, patterned after analogous formations such as 'emptiness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæn.ti.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of a garment being very small, light, or revealing, so that it covers little of the body.
insufficient or revealing clothing

Examples

The scantiness of her dress turned several heads at the formal event.
Some critics argue that the scantiness of runway fashions is growing each year.
Despite the scantiness of his costume, the performer moved confidently across the stage.

Synset Examples

She cared only for brightness of colour, ease of fit, and a brevity and skimpiness of skirt absolutely astounding in these days of crinolines and trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimpiness - the quality of clothing providing little coverage; meagerness or lack of material

Etymology

From 'scant', Middle English, from Old Norse 'skamt' (short, scanty) + -iness (forming nouns of quality).
