sidecar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdkɑː / (BrE)
a small vehicle or platform attached to the side of a motorcycle to carry one passenger or cargo
small vehicle attached to a motorcycle

Examples

She loved riding in the sidecar while her father drove the motorcycle through the countryside.
The old motorcycle with a sidecar drew curious looks from pedestrians.
They put their bags in the sidecar before starting their long trip.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdkɑː / (BrE)
a cocktail made from brandy, orange liqueur, and lemon juice, usually served in a cocktail glass
brandy-based cocktail with orange liqueur and lemon juice

Examples

He ordered a sidecar to start off his evening at the jazz bar.
The sidecar is a classic cocktail that's perfect for citrus and brandy lovers.
She learned to mix a perfect sidecar during her bartending course.

Etymology

Named after the motorcycle sidecar, reportedly after a patron who arrived in one; first recorded in early 20th-century France.
