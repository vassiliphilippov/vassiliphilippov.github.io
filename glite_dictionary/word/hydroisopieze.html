hydroisopieze

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈaɪsəˌpaɪz / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈaɪsəˌpiːz / (BrE)
A line on a map that connects points of equal hydraulic pressure within a water-bearing layer underground.
line of equal water pressure (hydrology)

Examples

The groundwater model displayed several hydroisopieze lines to indicate pressure variations across the aquifer.
When analyzing the well data, the geologist mapped out each hydroisopieze to understand subsurface water flow.
Accurate placement of a hydroisopieze can help identify regions suitable for sustainable water extraction.

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) + Greek isos (equal) + piezein (to press).
