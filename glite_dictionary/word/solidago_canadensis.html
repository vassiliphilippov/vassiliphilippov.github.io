Solidago canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑː.lɪˈdeɪ.goʊ ˌkæn.əˈdɛn.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɒ.lɪˈdeɪ.gəʊ ˌkæn.əˈden.ɪs / (BrE)
A large North American goldenrod with arching branches and showy clusters of yellow flowers; often considered a weed.
North American goldenrod species

Examples

The roadside was lined with tall stands of Solidago canadensis, their yellow flowers brightening the autumn landscape.
Gardeners often struggle to control the spread of Solidago canadensis in backyard flowerbeds.
While Solidago canadensis is attractive to pollinators, it can quickly become invasive if not managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a plant of the genus Solidago with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solidago' meaning 'to make whole' (for its medicinal use), 'canadensis' meaning 'of Canada'.
