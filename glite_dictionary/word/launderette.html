launderette

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔn.dɚˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌlɔːn.dərˈet / (BrE)
a place equipped with coin-operated washing machines for customers to wash and dry their laundry themselves
self-service laundry facility

Examples

She took her clothes to the launderette because her washing machine was broken.
There is a launderette on the corner that stays open until midnight.
Many students use the campus launderette during exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Laundromat - trademarked term for a self-service laundry facility, common in American English

Etymology

From 'launder', from Latin *lavare* (to wash), with the diminutive suffix '-ette', influenced by French.
