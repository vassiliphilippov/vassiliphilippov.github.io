Opuntia tuna

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈpʌn.ti.ə ˈtuː.nə / (AmE), / əʊˈpʌn.tɪə ˈtjuː.nə / (BrE)
A species of prickly pear cactus native to Jamaica and other parts of tropical America, known for its flattened, spiny stems and edible fruit.
a Jamaican prickly pear cactus

Examples

The Opuntia tuna thrives in the dry, sandy soils of coastal Jamaica.
Local farmers harvest the fruit of Opuntia tuna to make jams and beverages.
Researchers study Opuntia tuna for its drought-resistant properties and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - a type of cactus with spiny pads and edible fruit

Etymology

Opuntia from Latin, referencing the town of Opus in Greece; 'tuna' from Spanish name for the prickly pear fruit.
