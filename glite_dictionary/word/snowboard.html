snowboard

1. VERB

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɔːd / (BrE)
to glide down a snow-covered slope while standing and balancing on a snowboard
glide down snow on a snowboard

Examples

Many people travel to the Alps each winter to snowboard on the fresh powder.
He learned to snowboard before he could ski.
They spent the afternoon snowboarding with friends at the mountain resort.

Synset Examples

The children love to snowboard in winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ski - to move over snow on skis

Etymology

Formed from 'snow' and 'board', modeled after 'surfboard'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02109138-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a flat board resembling a large ski, ridden in a standing position to slide down snow-covered slopes, especially as a sport
board used for sliding down snow slopes

Examples

She strapped her boots onto the snowboard before heading down the mountain.
After renting a snowboard, he spent the afternoon practicing on the beginner slope.
He designed his own custom snowboard with a unique graphic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ski - long narrow runner used for gliding over snow

Etymology

Formed from 'snow' and 'board', modeled after 'surfboard'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04258901-n
Synset POS: n
