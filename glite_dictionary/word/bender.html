bender

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.də / (BrE)
an extended period of heavy drinking or lively partying, often involving excessive alcohol consumption
extended drinking spree or party
informal

Examples

After losing his job, he went on a bender that lasted the entire weekend.
She regretted the bender she had with her friends after waking up with a headache.
Their wild bender made for some unbelievable stories the next day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spree - a lively or wild outburst of activity, often drinking or partying

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛndər / (AmE), / ˈbɛndə / (BrE)
a tool used for bending materials such as metal or wire
tool for bending materials

Examples

The mechanic reached for a bender to shape the metal rod.
She used a bender to curve the wire into a loop.
This bender makes it easy to bend pipes without damaging them.

Synset Examples

he used pliers as a bender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pliers - a tool with two handles used for gripping or bending objects

Etymology

From bend + -er, meaning one that bends.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɛndə / (BrE)
(North American, informal) A pitch in baseball that is thrown with spin, causing it to curve as it approaches the batter; a curveball.
curveball pitch in baseball (North American slang)
informal american

Examples

The pitcher threw a nasty bender that completely fooled the hitter.
Danny’s signature pitch is his wicked bender that breaks late over the plate.
She practiced throwing a bender until she could finally get the perfect curve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curveball - a baseball pitch that curves as it approaches the batter
