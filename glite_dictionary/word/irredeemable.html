irredeemable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪrɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbəl / (BrE)
(of paper money) not able to be exchanged for coin or precious metal at the holder's request.
unable to be redeemed for coin (of money)

Examples

Before 1933, U.S. banknotes were backed by gold, but modern currency is often irredeemable.
During periods of financial crisis, governments sometimes declare banknotes irredeemable to stabilize the economy.
The irredeemable notes caused concern among those who preferred assets backed by precious metals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: redeemable - able to be exchanged for coin or precious metal

Etymology

From Latin 'ir-' (not) + 'redimere' (to buy back).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪrɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbl / (BrE)
impossible to reform, improve, or correct; incapable of being saved or made better.
impossible to reform or correct

Examples

After years of criminal behavior, he was dismissed as irredeemable by the authorities.
The damage to the environment was deemed irredeemable after the spill.
Some critics argued that the novel's protagonist was irredeemable, lacking any qualities worth saving.

Synset Examples

vicious irreclaimable boys
irredeemable sinners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorrigible - not able to be corrected or reformed
  • Antonym: redeemable - able to be saved, improved, or corrected

Etymology

From Latin 'ir-' (not) + 'redimere' (to buy back, redeem).
