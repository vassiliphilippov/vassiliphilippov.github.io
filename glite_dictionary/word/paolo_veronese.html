Paolo Veronese

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaː.oʊ vəˈroʊˌneɪzi / (AmE), / ˌpaʊ.ləʊ vəˈrɛə.neɪzi / (BrE)
(1528–1588) An Italian Renaissance painter known for his grand historical and religious paintings, especially in Venice.
Italian Renaissance painter

Examples

The vibrant colors in Paolo Veronese's paintings are admired by art historians worldwide.
The Louvre has several masterpieces by Paolo Veronese on display.
Paolo Veronese was famed for his large, elaborate works such as 'The Wedding at Cana'.

Etymology

Italian, Paolo (given name) + Veronese (“from Verona”), reflecting his birthplace.
