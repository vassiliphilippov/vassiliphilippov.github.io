isolating

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪsəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a language) characterized by words that typically express only one idea and grammatical relationships shown mainly by word order and particles, rather than inflection.
describing a language type with minimal inflection

Examples

Mandarin Chinese is often cited as an isolating language because it uses little inflection.
In linguistics, an isolating language relies on word order rather than word endings to show meaning.
Many Southeast Asian languages are considered isolating, with each word representing a single grammatical function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflectional - (of a language) employing inflections to indicate grammatical relationships
  • Antonym: agglutinative - (of a language) using words formed by stringing together morphemes

Etymology

From the verb 'isolate', from Italian 'isolato', from Latin 'insula' (island).
