Blephilia celiata

1. NOUN

/ blɛˈfɪliə siˈleɪtə / (AmE), / blɛˈfɪliə siˈliːətə / (BrE)
A species of wood mint characterized by pink-purple flowers arranged in tiers resembling a pagoda.
a species of wood mint

Examples

The garden was filled with Blephilia celiata, their pagoda-like blooms attracting many bees.
Researchers studied Blephilia celiata to understand its role in pollinator support.
In spring, the Blephilia celiata displayed vibrant pink-purple flowers along the woodland path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood mint - a common name for plants in the genus Blephilia
