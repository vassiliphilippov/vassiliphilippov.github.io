ex tempore

1. ADV

/ ɛks ˈtɛmpəri / (AmE), / ɛks ˈtɛmpəri / (BrE)
spoken or done without preparation; impromptu.
without preparation; impromptu

Examples

The judge asked the lawyer to answer the question ex tempore during the hearing.
She delivered an ex tempore speech that impressed everyone with its clarity.
He often plays piano ex tempore, composing music as he goes.

Synset Examples

I don't know the figures off-hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed
  • Synonym: offhand - done without previous thought or preparation

Etymology

From Latin 'ex tempore', meaning 'out of the time, at the moment'.
