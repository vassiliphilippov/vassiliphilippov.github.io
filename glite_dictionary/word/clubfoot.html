clubfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkləbˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈklʌbˌfʊt / (BrE)
A congenital deformity of the foot in which it is twisted out of shape or position, typically with the ankle, heel, and toes turned inward or downward.
congenital foot deformity
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that their baby had a clubfoot and would need treatment soon after birth.
Children born with clubfoot can often walk normally after corrective procedures.
Modern medicine offers several options for treating a clubfoot effectively.

Etymology

From 'club' (a rounded mass) + 'foot', referring to the deformed shape.
