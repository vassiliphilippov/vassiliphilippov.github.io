endolymph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.doʊ.lɪm(f) / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dəʊ.lɪmf / (BrE)
the fluid that fills the membranous labyrinth of the inner ear, involved in the sense of hearing and balance
inner ear fluid
medical

Examples

The movement of endolymph in the semicircular canals helps detect head rotation.
An imbalance in endolymph levels can lead to vertigo and hearing problems.
Scientists study endolymph to better understand inner ear disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perilymph - the fluid between the membranous labyrinth and the bony labyrinth of the inner ear

Etymology

From French endolymphe, from endo- (within) + Greek lýmphē (water, lymph).
