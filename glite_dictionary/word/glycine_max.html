Glycine max

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪsiːn ˈmæks / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsɪn ˈmæks / (BrE)
The scientific name for the soybean, an annual plant cultivated for its edible, oil-rich seeds, native to Asia.
scientific name for soybean plant

Examples

Researchers studied how Glycine max adapts to different soil types across the region.
High yields of Glycine max have helped make soybeans a staple crop worldwide.
The seeds of Glycine max are prized for their high protein and oil content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - an annual leguminous plant cultivated for its edible seeds; common name

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Glycine' from Greek 'glykys' (sweet) and species epithet 'max' (great, large).
