Baptists

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪsts / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪsts / (BrE)
Members of various evangelical Protestant Christian groups that believe in the baptism of adult or voluntary believers by full immersion.
Protestant Christian denomination emphasizing believer's baptism

Examples

My grandparents have been devoted Baptists for over fifty years.
Many Baptists believe in the importance of baptism by immersion.
The local Baptists organized a charity event for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protestant - A member or follower of any of the Western Christian churches separate from the Roman Catholic Church
  • Synonym: Methodist - A member of a Protestant denomination originating in the 18th-century revival movement founded by John Wesley

Etymology

From Greek 'baptistēs' (baptizer), via Latin and Old French.
