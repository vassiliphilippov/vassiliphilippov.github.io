bodily

1. ADV

/ ˈbɑːdɪli / (AmE), / ˈbɒdɪli / (BrE)
in physical form; as a whole body rather than in spirit or part
in physical form, as a whole body

Examples

The statue was carried bodily out of the building during the renovations.
The legend says the saint ascended bodily into the sky.
He lifted the small child bodily and placed her on his shoulders.

Synset Examples

he was translated bodily to heaven

Etymology

From Old English 'bodig' (body) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00227970-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːdɪli / (AmE), / ˈbɒdɪli / (BrE)
relating to or having a physical, material body; pertaining to the body rather than the mind or spirit
relating to the physical body

Examples

The philosopher discussed the nature of bodily existence versus spiritual existence.
She complained of bodily discomfort after the long flight.
Regular exercise is important for maintaining bodily health.

Synset Examples

bodily existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical - relating to the body as opposed to the mind; material
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than to physical things

Etymology

From Middle English 'bodili', from 'body' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00633170-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːdɪli / (AmE), / ˈbɒdɪli / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the body as opposed to the mind or spirit
relating to the body (not mind or spirit)

Examples

Regular exercise helps maintain bodily health throughout life.
Her illness caused both mental and bodily suffering.
The doctor assessed his bodily functions during the examination.

Synset Examples

bodily needs
a corporal defect
corporeal suffering
a somatic symptom or somatic illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical - relating to the body rather than the mind
  • Antonym: mental - relating to the mind rather than the body

Etymology

From Old English bodiglic (of the body), from bodig (body) + -lic (-ly, -like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01783480-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːdəli / (AmE), / ˈbɒdəli / (BrE)
relating to or connected with the body, especially the human body, rather than the mind or spirit.
relating to the body

Examples

The patient showed no signs of bodily injury after the accident.
She was concerned about her bodily health and exercised regularly.
Many religions teach that the soul is separate from the bodily form.

Synset Examples

a bodily organ
bodily functions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical - relating to the body rather than to the mind
  • Antonym: mental - relating to the mind

Etymology
