Celosia cristata

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊsiə krɪˈsteɪtə / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊsɪə krɪˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
A garden annual plant with striking, featherlike spikes of red or yellow flowers, commonly known as cockscomb.
ornamental plant with feathery red or yellow flowers

Examples

Celosia cristata adds vibrant color and unusual texture to summer gardens.
Gardeners often choose Celosia cristata for its unique crested flowers.
The brilliant red varieties of Celosia cristata are a favorite in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockscomb - common name for Celosia cristata

Etymology

From Latin; 'Celosia' from Greek 'kelos' (burned), referring to the flame-like appearance; 'cristata' means 'crested' in Latin.
