diphthong

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪf.θɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɪf.θɒŋ / (BrE)
a vowel sound in which the tongue moves from one position to another during pronunciation, combining two distinct vowel qualities within one syllable
vowel sound with gliding articulation

Examples

In the word "coin," the diphthong glides from an 'o' sound to an 'i' sound.
Australian English has several diphthong vowel sounds that differ from those in British English.
When learning French, many students struggle with the unfamiliar diphthong sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monophthong - a vowel sound with a single unchanging quality

Etymology

From Greek 'diphthongos' meaning 'having two sounds', from 'di-' (two) + 'phthongos' (sound, voice).
