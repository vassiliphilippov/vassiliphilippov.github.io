to reorient

1. VERB

/ riˈɔriˌɛnt / (AmE), / riːˈɔː.ri.ent / (BrE)
to change the direction, position, or focus of something; to cause something or someone to face a different direction.
change direction or position

Examples

After getting lost, she stopped to reorient herself with the map.
The company had to reorient its strategy after the market changed.
The sculpture was accidentally rotated, so the workers had to reorient it to face the entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orient - to align or position something relative to a point or direction

Etymology

From prefix 're-' (again) + 'orient' (to align or position), from Latin 'orient-', meaning 'rising' or 'east'.

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈɔriˌɛnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɔːrɪənt / (BrE)
to orient again or to change the direction or focus of something or someone, especially after becoming disoriented
orient again or change direction/focus

Examples

After getting lost in the woods, it took them a few minutes to reorient themselves using the map.
The company decided to reorient its strategy to focus more on digital markets.
She paused to reorient her thoughts before answering the difficult question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orient - to align or position toward a particular direction

Etymology

From re- (again) + orient (to align or position).

3. VERB

/ ˌriˈɔriˌɛnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɔːriːənt / (BrE)
to set or arrange something in a new or different position or direction
set in a new position or direction

Examples

After the renovation, they decided to reorient the front door to face the garden.
You may need to reorient the furniture in your office for better lighting.
The organization had to reorient its strategy to adapt to the new market trends.

Synset Examples

Reorient the house towards the South

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orient - to align or position something relative to a point or direction

Etymology

From re- (again) + orient (to place facing the east or a particular direction), from Latin orient-, 'rising, east'.
