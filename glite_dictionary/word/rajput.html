Rajput

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːdʒˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈrɑːdʒˌpʊt / (BrE)
A member of a Hindu warrior caste traditionally dominant in northern India, known for their martial history and social status.
member of a Hindu warrior caste in India

Examples

The Rajput rulers built impressive forts that still stand today in Rajasthan.
She traced her ancestry back to a proud Rajput clan.
Several Rajput festivals celebrate the martial traditions of the community.

Etymology

From Hindi 'Rājpūt', from Sanskrit 'rājaputra' meaning 'son of a king'.
