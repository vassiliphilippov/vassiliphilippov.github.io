nickel-and-dime

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɪkəl ənd ˈdaɪm / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəl ənd ˈdaɪm / (BrE)
Of little importance or value; involving trivial amounts of money or significance.
of little importance or value
american

Examples

It's just a nickel-and-dime project with no real impact.
They were running a nickel-and-dime business that barely broke even.
Don't waste time on those nickel-and-dime issues when bigger problems need attention.

Synset Examples

a nickel-and-dime operation run out of a single rented room
a small-time actor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Synonym: petty - of secondary or lesser importance, minor
  • Antonym: major - of great importance or significance

Etymology

From U.S. English, referring to the small denominations of a nickel (five cents) and a dime (ten cents).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01284805-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ ˌnɪkəl ən ˈdaɪm / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəl ənd ˈdaɪm / (BrE)
to accumulate something gradually by saving small amounts or through numerous minor additions or reductions, often in a tedious or limiting way
accumulate gradually in small amounts
american

Examples

She managed to nickel-and-dime enough money together to pay the bills each month.
The organization hopes to nickel-and-dime small donations until they reach their fundraising goal.
He had to nickel-and-dime his way through college by working part-time jobs.

Synset Examples

she nickeled-and-dimed together a small house for her family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrimp - to save or economize severely
  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or acquire over time

Etymology

Originally from the American terms for small coins (nickels and dimes), indicating paltry sums; used figuratively from early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01387667-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnɪkəl ən ˌdaɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ənd ˌdaɪm / (BrE)
to handle money very frugally by spending as little as possible, especially in a restrictive or petty way
spend money frugally or restrictively
informal american

Examples

The company nickel-and-dimes its employees on expense reimbursements.
He tends to nickel-and-dime every part of his budget, sometimes to a fault.
We can't nickel-and-dime our way to financial success without making bigger changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrimp - to be thrifty or parsimonious; to economize

Etymology

From U.S. coins 'nickel' (five cents) and 'dime' (ten cents), indicating small amounts of money.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02271629-v
Synset POS: v

4. ADJ

/ ˈnɪkl ənd daɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkl ənd daɪm / (BrE)
(of a job or payment) involving only small amounts of money; low-paying or insignificant.
low-paying or insignificant (esp. job/payment)
american informal

Examples

She was tired of working nickel-and-dime jobs that barely covered her rent.
Most nickel-and-dime gigs offer no benefits or job security.
He decided it was time to leave behind the nickel-and-dime work and pursue a real career.

Synset Examples

a nickel-and-dime job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menial - (of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige
  • Antonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit; highly paid

Etymology

American English, originally referring to small denominations of money (nickel = 5 cents, dime = 10 cents).
