rubbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
a picture or impression made by placing paper over an object and rubbing it with something like charcoal or crayon to reproduce the object's texture or design
impression made by rubbing paper over an object

Examples

He made a rubbing of the ancient gravestone to preserve its inscription.
The museum displayed rubbings of medieval brass memorials.
She taught students how to create a rubbing using charcoal and paper.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
The resistance that occurs when one surface is moved in contact with another.
resistance between contacting surfaces

Examples

The loud rubbing of shoes on the wooden floor distracted the audience.
Engineers try to minimize rubbing between moving parts to improve efficiency.
You can feel the rubbing when you slide your hand along the rough surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving one object over another with pressure, typically to produce heat, clean a surface, or relieve soreness
act of moving with pressure

Examples

She felt a rubbing on her ankle where the new shoes were too tight.
The rubbing of the stones together eventually started a small fire.
After hours of rubbing, the tarnish was finally removed from the silver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another
