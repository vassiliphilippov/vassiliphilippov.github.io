Thomas Malory

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːməs ˈmæləri / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ˈmæləri / (BrE)
(c. 1415–1471), English writer known for compiling and translating romances about King Arthur, particularly in 'Le Morte d'Arthur'.
English author of Arthurian romances

Examples

Thomas Malory is best known for his influential work 'Le Morte d'Arthur', which shaped the Arthurian legend in English literature.
Many scholars debate the true identity of Thomas Malory and his sources for the stories about King Arthur.
Thomas Malory's retelling of Arthurian tales remains a cornerstone of medieval English literature.
