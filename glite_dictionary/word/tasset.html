tasset

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæs.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈtas.ɪt / (BrE)
one of a pair of pieces of armor that hang from the fauld to protect the upper thighs
piece of armor protecting upper thighs

Examples

A knight's tasset was dented during the fierce tournament.
The tassets were fastened securely to the fauld to shield the thighs.
Medieval armorers crafted each tasset to fit the wearer precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor covering the body from neck to waist

Etymology

From Middle French 'tasset', from Old French 'tasse' (pocket, plate), ultimately from Latin 'taxa' (pouch).
