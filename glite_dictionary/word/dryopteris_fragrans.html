Dryopteris fragrans

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˈfreɪɡrəns / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˈfreɪɡrəns / (BrE)
A species of fern native to northern Eurasia and North America, notable for its fragrant fronds.
a woodland fern with fragrant fronds

Examples

The Dryopteris fragrans is prized by botanists for its unusual sweet-smelling leaves.
During the hike, we spotted several Dryopteris fragrans growing on a shaded rocky slope.
Gardeners in cooler regions often cultivate Dryopteris fragrans for its attractive and aromatic fronds.

Etymology

From Greek *drys* (oak) and *pteris* (fern), and Latin *fragrans* (fragrant).
