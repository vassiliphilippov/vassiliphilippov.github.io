nutmeg

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ / (BrE)
The hard, aromatic seed of a tropical tree, used as a spice when grated or ground.
aromatic seed used as spice

Examples

She added a pinch of nutmeg to the custard for extra flavor.
Ground nutmeg is a common spice in holiday baking recipes.
The aroma of freshly grated nutmeg filled the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mace - a spice made from the outer covering of the nutmeg seed

Etymology

From Middle English 'notemugge', from Old Provençal 'noz muscada' (musky nut).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈnʌtmɛɡ / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to the East Indies, cultivated for its aromatic seed used as a spice; also, the seed of this tree, used in cooking and baking.
aromatic tropical tree and its seed (spice)

Examples

Grate a little nutmeg over the top of the custard before serving.
The islands are famous for their production of nutmeg and other exotic spices.
She bought a small nutmeg tree for her tropical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mace - aromatic spice from the reddish aril of the nutmeg seed

Etymology

From Old Provençal 'noz moscada' meaning 'musky nut', via Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
