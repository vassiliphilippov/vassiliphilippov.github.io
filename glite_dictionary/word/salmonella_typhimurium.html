Salmonella typhimurium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælməˈnɛlə taɪˌfɪmjʊˈriəm / (AmE), / ˌsælməˈniːlə taɪˌfɪmjʊˈriːəm / (BrE)
a species of Salmonella bacterium that commonly causes food poisoning in humans, leading to symptoms such as diarrhea and abdominal pain
species of Salmonella causing food poisoning
medical

Examples

The outbreak of food poisoning was traced to Salmonella typhimurium in the undercooked chicken.
Researchers use Salmonella typhimurium as a model organism to study bacterial infections.
Proper handling and cooking of food can prevent illness caused by Salmonella typhimurium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmonella - a genus of bacteria that causes food poisoning

Etymology

From genus name Salmonella (named after American pathologist Daniel Elmer Salmon) + typhimurium, from New Latin, meaning mouse typhoid-causing.
