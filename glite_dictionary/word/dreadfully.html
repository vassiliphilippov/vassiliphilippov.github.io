dreadfully

1. ADV

/ ˈdrɛd.fəli / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that is very bad, unpleasant, or shocking; extremely.
in a very bad or extreme manner

Examples

The film was dreadfully boring, and we left halfway through.
He spoke to her in a dreadfully rude tone.
I'm dreadfully sorry for the mistake I made.

Synset Examples

there was a dreadfully bloody accident on the road this morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or serious way

Etymology

From 'dreadful' + '-ly', meaning in a dreadful manner.

2. ADV

/ ˈdrɛd.fəli / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that causes fear, shock, or suffering; extremely or very badly.
in a dreadful or very bad way

Examples

The weather has been dreadfully cold this winter, making it hard to go outside.
She felt dreadfully sorry for missing her friend's birthday party.
The play was dreadfully boring, and many people left before it ended.

Synset Examples

as he looks at the mess he has left behind he must wonder how the Brits so often managed to succeed in the kind of situation where he has so dismally failed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - in a very bad or unpleasant way
  • Antonym: wonderfully - in a way that causes wonder or pleasure

Etymology

From 'dreadful' + '-ly', using the adjective 'dreadful' (causing dread) and the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00317880-r
Synset POS: r
