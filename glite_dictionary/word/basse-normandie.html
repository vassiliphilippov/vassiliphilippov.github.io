Basse-Normandie

1. PROPN

/ bɑs nɔr.mɑ̃.di / (AmE), / bas nɔːˈmɑ̃.di / (BrE)
a former administrative region in northwestern France, forming the western part of historic Normandy, now part of Normandy region since 2016
former region of northwestern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Basse-Normandie for its beautiful coastline and historic sites.
The cuisine of Basse-Normandie is famous for its cheeses and apple-based dishes.
After the regional reorganization in 2016, Basse-Normandie became part of the new Normandy region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Normandy - region in northern France, of which Basse-Normandie formed the western part
  • Antonym: Haute-Normandie - former region in northern France, now part of Normandy; means 'Upper Normandy'

Etymology

French, meaning 'Lower Normandy'. From 'basse' (lower) + 'Normandie' (Normandy).
