extant

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstænt, ˈɛkstənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstænt, ˈɛkstənt / (BrE)
still in existence; not destroyed, lost, or extinct
still existing; surviving

Examples

Many extant species are in danger of becoming extinct within the next century.
Several extant letters from the author shed light on her life and work.
Only a few extant copies of the ancient manuscript remain in the world.

Synset Examples

extant manuscripts
specimens of graphic art found among extant barbaric folk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extinct - no longer in existence
  • Synonym: surviving - still living or existing

Etymology

From Latin 'extantem', present participle of 'extare' (to stand out, exist).
