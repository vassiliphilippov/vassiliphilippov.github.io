cooking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of preparing food by heating it, typically by boiling, baking, frying, or roasting.
preparing food with heat

Examples

My grandmother's cooking always filled the house with wonderful aromas.
Italian cooking is known for its rich flavors and fresh ingredients.
He developed a love for cooking during his college years.

Synset Examples

cooking can be a great art
people are needed who have experience in cookery
he left the preparation of meals to his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookery - the art or practice of cooking food
  • Degree Lower: baking - the act of cooking food by dry heat in an oven
  • Degree Higher: roasting - cooking food, especially meat, with dry heat in an oven or over a fire

Etymology

From the verb 'cook,' which comes from Old English *cocian*, from Latin *coquere* (to cook).
