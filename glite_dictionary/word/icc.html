ICC

1. NOUN

/ aɪ si si / (AmE), / aɪ siː siː / (BrE)
abbreviation for Interstate Commerce Commission, a former U.S. federal agency that regulated interstate transportation until its termination in 1995.
abbreviation for Interstate Commerce Commission (U.S. federal agency)
american

Examples

The ICC played a significant role in regulating railroad rates during the 20th century.
Following its abolition in 1995, most of the ICC's functions were transferred to other agencies.
Historians often cite the formation of the ICC as a turning point in federal economic regulation.

Synset Examples

the ICC was established in 1887 as the first federal agency

Etymology

Abbreviation of Interstate Commerce Commission.
