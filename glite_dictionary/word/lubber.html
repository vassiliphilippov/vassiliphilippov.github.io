lubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌb.ə / (BrE)
an inexperienced or clumsy sailor, especially one on their first voyage
inexperienced sailor

Examples

The captain could tell at once that the new recruit was a lubber unfamiliar with shipboard life.
During his first week at sea, the lubber made several mistakes with the ropes.
The experienced sailors often teased the lubber for getting seasick in calm waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or sailing

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'lobre' (lazy person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌbər / (AmE), / ˈlʌbə / (BrE)
an awkward or clumsy person, often considered to be inexperienced or lacking social grace
awkward or clumsy person

Examples

Everyone laughed when the big lubber tripped over his own feet at the party.
Despite being called a lubber by his friends, Tom tried hard to improve his dancing.
The pirate captain had no patience for any lubber who could not climb the rigging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking grace or skill in movement or action
  • Synonym: oaf - a stupid, clumsy, or uncultured person

Etymology

From Middle English *lobur* (clumsy person), likely related to *lobb* (a clumsy person).

Synset POS: n
