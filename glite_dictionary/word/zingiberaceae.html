Zingiberaceae

1. NOUN

/ zɪn.dʒə.bəˈreɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / zɪn.dʒɪ.bəˈreɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of tropical plants, including ginger and turmeric, characterized by aromatic rhizomes and often showy flowers
family of tropical plants (ginger family)
medical

Examples

Many species within the Zingiberaceae family are cultivated for their culinary and medicinal uses.
Turmeric and cardamom both belong to the Zingiberaceae plant family.
The exotic flowers of the Zingiberaceae are popular in tropical gardens.

Etymology

From Latin 'Zingiberaceae', from 'Zingiber' (the genus for ginger).
