to emend

1. VERB

/ iˈmɛnd / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to correct or improve a text, especially by removing errors
correct or improve a text

Examples

The editor was asked to emend several passages that were unclear.
Scholars continue to emend the manuscript as new evidence emerges.
She carefully emended the typos in the original document before submission.

Synset Examples

the text was emended in the second edition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting and modifying it
  • Synonym: to amend - to make minor changes to a text or document

Etymology

From Latin emendare (to correct, to free from fault).
