Hypericum crux-andrae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌkrʌks ˈændriː / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌkrʌks ˈændriː / (BrE)
A shrubby plant native to the southeastern United States, bearing yellow to apricot four-petaled flowers arranged in a cross.
shrubby plant with cross-shaped yellow flowers

Examples

The Hypericum crux-andrae blooms every summer in the southeastern United States.
Gardeners admire Hypericum crux-andrae for its distinctive cross-shaped flowers.
Botanists identified the shrub as Hypericum crux-andrae during their field study.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Hypericum' and species 'crux-andrae' meaning 'Andrew's cross'.
