filter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.tə / (BrE)
A device or material that removes impurities or unwanted elements from a liquid, gas, light, or sound that passes through it.
device that removes impurities

Examples

The air filter needs to be changed every three months to ensure the system runs efficiently.
She poured the water through a filter to remove any dirt or debris.
The photographer attached a red filter to his camera lens for special effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purifier - a device that removes contaminants from something
  • Synonym: strainer - a device for separating solid matter from a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'filtrum' (felt used as a filter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03344588-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
to remove impurities or unwanted elements by passing a substance through a filter
remove impurities by filtering

Examples

You should filter the water before drinking it to remove harmful bacteria.
The scientists filtered the air sample to collect particles for analysis.
Coffee is usually made by filtering hot water through ground coffee beans.

Synset Examples

filter out the impurities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - make something pure by removing contaminants
  • Antonym: to contaminate - make something impure by adding harmful substances

Etymology

From Medieval Latin filtrum (felt used as a filter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01461293-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.tər / (BrE)
an electrical device that selectively allows certain frequencies to pass while attenuating others from a signal
electrical device for frequency selection

Examples

The audio engineer installed a filter to remove low-frequency noise from the recording.
A high-pass filter blocks signals below a certain frequency, allowing only higher frequencies through.
Modern radios often use a digital filter to improve sound clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandpass filter - a filter that allows only a specific range of frequencies to pass
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03344954-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, light, etc.) to pass slowly through a material that stops certain things contained in it, allowing only specific parts to pass through.
pass through a material, allowing only certain parts

Examples

Sunlight began to filter through the leaves, casting dappled shadows on the ground.
The rainwater will filter down through the soil before reaching the river.
Rumors about the new policy slowly filtered out to the entire staff.

Synset Examples

Water permeates sand easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread through; to pass through the pores or openings of something
  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material

Etymology

From Old French *filtrer*, from *filtre* (filter), from Medieval Latin *filtrum* (felt used for filtering).

Source: WordNet
