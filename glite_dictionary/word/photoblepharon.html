Photoblepharon

1. PROPN

/ ˌfoʊtəʊˈblɛfərɒn / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈblɛfərən / (BrE)
A genus of flashlight fishes in the family Anomalopidae, found in reef environments of the Western Pacific Ocean, known for their light-producing organs under the eyes.
a genus of flashlight fishes

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Photoblepharon near the coral reefs of Papua New Guinea.
The Photoblepharon uses its bioluminescent organs to navigate and hunt at night.
Marine biologists often study Photoblepharon to understand the evolution of light production in fishes.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-' (light) + 'blepharon' (eyelid).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈblɛfərɒn / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈblɛfərɒn / (BrE)
A genus of flashlight fishes in the family Anomalopidae, native to reefs in the Western Pacific Ocean, known for their bioluminescent organs.
genus of flashlight fish

Examples

The Photoblepharon uses its luminous organs to lure prey and evade predators at night.
Several species within the genus Photoblepharon inhabit coral reefs throughout Indonesia and the Philippines.
Biologists study the Photoblepharon to better understand the evolution of bioluminescence in marine fish.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-' (light) + 'blepharon' (eyelid), referring to light organs near the eyes.
