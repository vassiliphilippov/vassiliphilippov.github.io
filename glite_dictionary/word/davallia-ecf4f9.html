Davallia

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæliə / (AmE), / dəˈvæliə / (BrE)
a genus of Old World tropical ferns, some species of which are placed in the family Polypodiaceae
genus of Old World tropical ferns

Examples

The Davallia genus includes several ferns known for their creeping rhizomes.
Many homes feature Davallia species as ornamental plants, commonly called 'rabbit's foot ferns'.
Studying Davallia helps botanists understand fern evolution in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery fronds and reproducing by spores

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanist Edmond Davall (1763–1798).
