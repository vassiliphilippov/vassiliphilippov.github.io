recognisance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑː.nə.zəns / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒn.ɪ.zəns / (BrE)
(Law) a formal pledge made before a court, in which a person promises to do a specific act (such as appearing in court), with a sum of money forfeited if the obligation is not met.
formal pledge or bond in law
legal british

Examples

The defendant was released on recognisance until the trial date.
She entered into a recognisance to keep the peace for twelve months.
If the accused breaks the conditions of the recognisance, the sum pledged may be forfeited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - an agreement with legal force, especially one involving a sum of money

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman recognisaunce, from Old French reconoissance, based on Latin recognoscere (to recognize).
