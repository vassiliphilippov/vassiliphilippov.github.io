vestige

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small remaining part or sign of something that no longer exists or is disappearing
small remaining trace or sign

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a vestige of the ancient civilization buried beneath the desert sands.
There was not a vestige of doubt in her mind after she saw the evidence.
Modern buildings now stand where only a vestige of the forest remains.

Synset Examples

there wasn't a trace of evidence for the claim
a tincture of condescension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount or sign of something that remains
  • Synonym: remnant - a small part left after the rest has been used, removed, or destroyed
  • Synonym: relic - an object surviving from an earlier time, especially one of historical or sentimental interest

Etymology

From Latin 'vestigium' meaning 'footprint' or 'trace'.
