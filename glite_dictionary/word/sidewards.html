sidewards

1. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdwədz / (BrE)
toward one side; in a direction to the side rather than forward or backward.
toward one side

Examples

He glanced sidewards to check for any approaching cars.
The crab moved sidewards along the sandy shore.
With a gentle push, the box slid sidewards off the shelf.

Synset Examples

turn the figure sideward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - in a direction to the side

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + the adverbial suffix '-wards', modeled on similar directional terms (e.g., 'backwards').
