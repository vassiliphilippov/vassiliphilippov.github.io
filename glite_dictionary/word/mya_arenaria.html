Mya arenaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.ə ɛr.əˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.ə əˈrɛn.ə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
an edible clam with a thin, oval shell, found in coastal regions of the United States and Europe, also known as the soft-shell clam
a species of edible clam (soft-shell clam)

Examples

Fishermen in Maine harvest large quantities of Mya arenaria for local seafood markets.
Mya arenaria is often steamed and served with butter in coastal New England cuisine.
The thin shell of Mya arenaria makes it more fragile than many other edible clams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - common name for Mya arenaria

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Mya' is a genus of clams and 'arenaria' means 'sandy', referring to its habitat.
