skimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmər / (AmE), / ˈskɪmə / (BrE)
a gull-like seabird with an elongated lower mandible, which it immerses in the water while flying to catch food
seabird with a long lower bill used for skimming

Examples

The skimmer glided just above the water, dipping its long bill to catch small fish.
Many photographers travel to coastal areas to observe skimmers in their natural habitat.
A group of skimmer chicks huddled together on the sandy shore.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmər / (AmE), / ˈskɪmə / (BrE)
a kitchen utensil with a flat or perforated surface used for removing fat, foam, or other substances from the surface of liquids
kitchen utensil for removing surface fat or solids

Examples

She used a skimmer to remove the foam from the boiling soup.
After frying the potatoes, he lifted them out of the oil with a skimmer.
A skimmer is essential for making clear broth by taking off the fat and impurities.

Etymology

From the verb 'skim' (to remove from the surface) + agent suffix '-er'.

a stiff hat made of straw with a flat crown and a flat brim, traditionally worn by men in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, especially in summer.
stiff straw hat with flat crown

Vintage skimmers are now collector's items prized by fashion enthusiasts.

4. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmər / (AmE), / ˈskɪmə / (BrE)
a person who reads something quickly and superficially, without paying close attention to details
a rapid, superficial reader

Examples

She is a skimmer who rarely finishes a novel in detail.
The professor warned the skimmers that they might miss important information on the test.
He admitted being a skimmer, preferring to get the gist rather than read every word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reader - a person who reads
  • Antonym: studier - someone who examines or investigates carefully

Etymology

From 'skim' (to move quickly over a surface) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
