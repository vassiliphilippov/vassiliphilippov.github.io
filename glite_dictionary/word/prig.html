prig

1. NOUN

/ prɪɡ / (AmE), / prɪɡ / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a self-righteous or morally superior way that is considered irritating or smug
self-righteous, annoyingly proper person

Examples

No one wanted to work with the office prig, who constantly criticized everyone's manners.
She sounded like a prig when she lectured her friends about every little rule.
Don't be such a prig; relax and enjoy yourself for once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snob - a person who believes themselves superior to others
  • Antonym: boor - a rude or insensitive person

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'prig' (to steal), later influenced by slang meanings of affected or conceited person.
