to misappropriate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.əˈproʊ.pri.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈprəʊ.pri.eɪt / (BrE)
to dishonestly or illegally take something, especially money or property, for one's own use when it has been entrusted to one's care.
dishonestly take entrusted property

Examples

It is illegal to misappropriate company funds for personal use.
The official was charged after he tried to misappropriate public money.
She was fired when it was discovered she had misappropriated confidential documents.

Synset Examples

The accountant embezzled thousands of dollars while working for the wealthy family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal money placed in one's trust or belonging to the organization one works for
  • Degree Lower: appropriate - to take something for one's own use, typically without the owner's permission

Etymology

From Latin mis- (wrongly) + appropriate (from appropriatus, past participle of appropriare, to make one's own).
