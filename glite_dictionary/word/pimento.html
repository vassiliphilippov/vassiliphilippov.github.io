pimento

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmɛntoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
A fully ripened sweet red pepper, typically red in color, often used cooked or stuffed in foods such as olives and cheeses.
sweet red pepper used in cooking

Examples

She stuffed the olives with pimento before serving them at the party.
The cheese spread had a mild pimento flavor that everyone enjoyed.
Sliced pimento is often added to salads for color and sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell pepper - a large, mild, sweet pepper, often green, red, or yellow

Etymology

From Spanish 'pimiento' (pepper), ultimately from Latin 'pigmentum' (pigment, spice).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈmɛntoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
A plant (Capsicum annuum) bearing large, mild, thick-walled, usually bell-shaped red fruits used especially in salads and as stuffing.
mild, bell-shaped red pepper plant

Examples

The chef stuffed each pimento with a delicious cheese blend for the appetizer.
Farmers harvested the ripe red pimentos at the end of summer.
She prefers using pimento in her salads for its sweet, mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell pepper - large, mild variety of pepper, closely related to pimento

Etymology

From Spanish 'pimiento' (pepper), ultimately from Latin 'pigmentum' (spice, pigment).
