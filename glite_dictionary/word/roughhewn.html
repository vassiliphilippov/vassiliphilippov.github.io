roughhewn

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfˌhjuːn / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌhjuːn / (BrE)
(of stone or timber) shaped in a rough, unfinished manner, lacking smoothness or refinement.
shaped roughly without finishing

Examples

The old cabin was built from roughhewn logs stacked by hand.
A roughhewn stone fireplace dominated the room, giving it a rustic feel.
His roughhewn features made him look rugged and outdoorsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfinished - not finished or lacking refinement
  • Antonym: polished - finished to a smooth and refined surface

Etymology

From 'rough' + 'hewn', past participle of 'hew' (to cut or shape with a tool).
