bunt

1. NOUN

/ bʌnt / (AmE), / bʌnt / (BrE)
A disease of wheat in which the grains are replaced by greasy masses of foul-smelling smut spores.
a wheat disease causing smelly smut spores

Examples

The farmer lost much of his crop to bunt this year.
Scientists are developing wheat varieties resistant to bunt.
Early detection of bunt is crucial to prevent widespread contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smut - a fungal disease affecting plants, often producing soot-like spores

2. NOUN

/ bʌnt / (AmE), / bʌnt / (BrE)
a disease of cereal crops, especially wheat, caused by certain fungi such as Tilletia caries, characterized by smutty or fetid grains
fungal disease of wheat and cereals

Examples

Many wheat fields were devastated by bunt last year, affecting the farmers' harvest.
The presence of bunt can significantly reduce the quality of wheat grains.
Researchers are developing resistant wheat varieties to combat bunt in crops.

/ bʌnt / (AmE), / bʌnt / (BrE)

soft hit in baseball or softball

Examples
