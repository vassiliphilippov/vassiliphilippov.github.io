prosodic

1. ADJ

/ prəˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / prəˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to the rhythmic and intonational aspects of speech, such as pitch, stress, and juncture
relating to rhythm and intonation in speech

Examples

The researcher analyzed the prosodic features of the recorded speech to identify patterns of stress and intonation.
Poets often use prosodic elements, such as rhythm and meter, to enhance the musicality of their verses.
Children learn to recognize prosodic cues, like sentence melody and pauses, when acquiring their first language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonational - relating to the rise and fall of the voice in speech

Etymology

From Greek 'prosōidikos', relating to song or accent.
