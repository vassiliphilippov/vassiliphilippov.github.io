minder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndə / (BrE)
A person who looks after babies or young children, especially in their own home while the parents are working.
person who looks after babies or children

Examples

The couple hired a minder to care for their infant while they were at work.
She works as a minder for several families in her neighborhood.
Finding a reliable minder gives parents peace of mind during the workday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babysitter - a person who takes care of children temporarily, often at the children's home

Etymology

From the verb 'mind' (to look after) plus suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndə / (BrE)
a person, especially in certain countries with authoritarian governments, assigned to accompany and monitor foreign visitors
government-assigned watcher of visitors

Examples

During the conference, a government-assigned minder followed the journalists everywhere they went.
Our tour guide was friendly, but the official minder kept a close eye on our group.
Foreign delegations were often accompanied by a minder to control what they saw and whom they spoke to.

Synset Examples

I turned around and there, a few hundred feet away, was our government minder, Li Wong Su, huffing and puffing toward us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaperone - a person who accompanies and looks after another, often to supervise or guide
  • Synonym: escort - a person who goes with someone to give protection or guidance
