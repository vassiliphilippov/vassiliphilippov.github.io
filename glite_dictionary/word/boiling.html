boiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of cooking food by immersing it in a liquid that has been brought to its boiling point
cooking in boiling liquid

Examples

The boiling of eggs takes about ten minutes for a hard yolk.
Add pasta to the boiling and cook until tender but firm.
Proper boiling of vegetables preserves more nutrients than overcooking.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simmering - cooking just below boiling point
  • Degree Lower: poaching - cooking in gently simmering liquid

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'boil', from Old French *boillir*, from Latin *bullīre* (to bubble, to boil).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of heating a liquid until it changes into a gas, especially by reaching its boiling point
application of heat to turn liquid into gas

Examples

The boiling of water is a key step in cooking pasta.
During boiling, bubbles of steam form throughout the liquid.
They observed the boiling of various liquids under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: evaporation - the process of a liquid turning into vapor (not necessarily at boiling point)
  • Synonym: vaporization - the change of a substance from liquid or solid to vapor
  • Antonym: condensation - the process by which a gas turns into a liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'boil', from Old French 'boillir', from Latin 'bullīre' (to bubble, to boil).

used to emphasize the extreme degree of a feeling or state; extremely or intensely
intensely; extremely (emphatic adverb)

Examples
