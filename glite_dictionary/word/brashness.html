brashness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbræʃ.nəs / (BrE)
Behaviour or appearance that is offensively bold or showy, especially in a tasteless way.
offensively bold or showy behaviour

Examples

Her brashness at the gala made her stand out for all the wrong reasons.
The designer's brashness was evident in the bold and flashy colors of the outfit.
Some critics disliked the brashness of the new building, calling it tasteless and overdone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentation - pretentious and vulgar display
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of vanity or showiness

2. NOUN

/ ˈbræʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbræʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being confident in an aggressive, rude, or hasty way; rashness or lack of caution
rash or rude boldness

Examples

His brashness often got him into trouble at work.
I admired her courage but thought her brashness verged on recklessness.
The team leader's brashness offended several members during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rashness - quality of acting without thinking or care; impulsiveness
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From 'brash' (mid-19th century, of uncertain origin) + '-ness'.
