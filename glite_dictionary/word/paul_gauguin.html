Paul Gauguin

1. PROPN

/ pɔl ɡoʊˈɡæ̃n / (AmE), / pɔːl ɡəʊˈɡæ̃ / (BrE)
(1848–1903) A French Post-Impressionist painter known for his bold use of colour and works created in the South Pacific.
French Post-Impressionist painter (1848–1903)

Examples

Paul Gauguin's paintings are celebrated for their vivid colours and striking symbolism.
An exhibition of Paul Gauguin's work drew large crowds at the museum.
Many art historians consider Paul Gauguin a key influence on modern art.
