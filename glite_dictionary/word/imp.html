imp

1. NOUN

/ ɪmp / (AmE), / ɪmp / (BrE)
In folklore, a small, mischievous supernatural being, often considered a minor demon or sprite.
mischievous supernatural being in folklore

Examples

In old fairy tales, a cunning imp would often play tricks on villagers.
She described her little brother as an energetic imp who was always up to mischief.
The garden statue depicted a grinning imp perched on the edge of the fountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprite - small, elusive supernatural being
  • Degree Higher: demon - evil supernatural entity

Etymology

From Old English 'impa' (graft, shoot, offshoot), later used for young shoots or scions, and then transferred to mean a small devil or mischievous child.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmp / (AmE), / ɪmp / (BrE)
A child or person who behaves in a playfully mischievous or naughty way.
playfully mischievous person or child

Examples

Their daughter is a little imp, always playing tricks on her brother.
He smiled like an imp after sneaking a cookie from the jar.
At recess, the imp in the red hat led the others into harmless mischief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person, especially a child
  • Antonym: angel - someone who is very good and well-behaved

Etymology

From Old English 'impa', meaning a young shoot or scion, later used metaphorically for 'child' or 'offspring'.
