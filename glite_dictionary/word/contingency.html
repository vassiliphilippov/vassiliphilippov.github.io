contingency

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
a possible event or condition that might happen in the future, especially one that could cause problems or requires preparation
possible future event or condition

Examples

We always have a backup plan in case of any contingency.
Insurance provides financial protection against unexpected contingencies.
The company created a contingency fund to prepare for possible emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possibility - something that could happen or exist
  • Antonym: certainty - something sure to happen or unquestionable

Etymology

From Latin contingentia (contact, occurrence), from contingere (to happen, befall).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
the state of being dependent on or conditioned by something else; the possibility that something may occur depending on uncertain factors
dependence on something else; possibility

Examples

The contract was voided because it failed to address this crucial contingency.
Their travel plans are subject to weather-related contingency.
Management is required to have a contingency plan in case of unexpected events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possibility - the state or fact of being likely or possible
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being certain or inevitable

Etymology

From Latin contingentia (chance, possibility), from contingere (to touch, befall).
