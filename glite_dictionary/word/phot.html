phot

1. NOUN

/ foʊt / (AmE), / fəʊt / (BrE)
a unit of illumination equal to 1 lumen per square centimeter, chiefly used in photometry
unit of illumination (1 lumen/cm²)

Examples

The older laboratory equipment used the phot as its standard unit of light intensity.
He measured the surface illumination and found it was several phots.
10,000 phots are equivalent to one lux in the SI system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lux - SI unit of illumination equal to 1 lumen per square meter

Etymology

From Greek phōs (light).
