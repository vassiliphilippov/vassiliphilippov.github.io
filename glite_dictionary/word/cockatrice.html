cockatrice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːk.ə.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.ə.trɪs / (BrE)
A mythical creature, typically depicted as a two-legged dragon or serpent hatched by a reptile from a cock's egg, believed to be able to kill with a glance.
mythical creature hatched from a cock's egg

Examples

Legends warned that even the shadow of a cockatrice could be deadly to those who crossed its path.
In the ancient bestiary, the entry on the cockatrice describes it as a fearsome foe with a killing gaze.
The villagers believed a cockatrice lurked in the ruins, and few dared to venture near after sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilisk - legendary reptile said to kill by its glance

Etymology

From Latin 'calcātrix', via Old French, meaning 'tracker', influenced by irregular folk etymology relating to 'cock'.
