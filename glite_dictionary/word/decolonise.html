to decolonise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒl.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
To grant independence to a country or territory that was previously a colony.
grant independence to a colony

Examples

Many African countries were decolonised in the twentieth century after years of struggle.
The process to decolonise territories often involved complex negotiations with colonial powers.
Historians have studied the movement to decolonise India in great depth.

Synset Examples

West Africa was decolonized in the early 1960's

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to colonise - establish a colony in or on a place

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + colonise, after French décoloniser.
