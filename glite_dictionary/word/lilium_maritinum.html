Lilium maritinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm məˈrɪtɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm məˈrɪtɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of lily with orange flowers, native to the Pacific coast of the United States.
orange-flowered lily of the Pacific coast US

Examples

The vibrant Lilium maritinum brightens the coastal meadows each spring.
Gardeners along California's coast often cultivate Lilium maritinum for its striking orange blooms.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the rare Lilium maritinum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a bulbous plant with large trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lilium' meaning 'lily' and 'maritinum' meaning 'of the sea' or 'coastal'.
