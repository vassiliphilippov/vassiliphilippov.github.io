breakdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
the separation of a substance into two or more substances, especially as a result of a chemical reaction
chemical separation into component substances

Examples

The breakdown of water produces hydrogen and oxygen gases.
Exposure to light accelerates the breakdown of certain chemicals in the solution.
Scientists studied the breakdown of proteins during digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decomposition - the process of breaking down into simpler substances, especially by chemical means

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
A sudden failure of health, particularly mental health, often causing someone to be unable to function normally.
sudden failure of health (mental or physical)

Examples

She suffered a breakdown after months of continuous stress at work.
Following his breakdown, he took a leave of absence to recover.
The news of the accident caused a complete breakdown of his mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - a sudden failure of health or strength
  • Antonym: recovery - a return to good health after illness or a breakdown

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'break down,' meaning to separate or collapse.
