peonage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.ə.nɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpiː.ə.nɪdʒ / (BrE)
A system or practice in which a debtor is forced to work for a creditor until a debt is repaid.
system of forced labor for debt repayment

Examples

The sharecroppers were kept in peonage by unfair crop liens and mounting debt.
After the law was passed, peonage was officially declared illegal in the region.
Many historical accounts describe peonage as a form of involuntary servitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt bondage - the practice of forcing someone to work to pay off a debt
  • Degree Higher: slavery - state of being owned and compelled to work for someone else

Etymology

From Spanish 'peonaje', from 'peón' (laborer, foot soldier), ultimately from Latin 'pedon-' (pedestrian).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.ə.nɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpiː.ənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a system or condition in which people are bound in servitude to work off debts or obligations, often through forced or compulsory labor
system of servitude or debt bondage

Examples

After the war, many workers suffered under peonage, forced to pay off never-ending debts to landowners.
The novel explores the cruelty and injustice of peonage in the early 20th-century South.
Laws were eventually passed to abolish peonage and protect the rights of laborers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slavery - the condition of being owned by another person and forced to work without pay
  • Synonym: serfdom - the state of being a serf or laborer bound to a lord's land
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think without hindrance

Etymology

From Spanish 'peonaje', from 'peón' (laborer, foot soldier).
