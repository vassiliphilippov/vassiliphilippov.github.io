unmerchantable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈmɜrtʃəntəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈmɜːtʃəntəbl / (BrE)
Not fit or suitable for sale; lacking the quality required to be sold on the market.
not fit for sale

Examples

The court ruled that the spoiled food was unmerchantable and not suitable for sale.
Damaged packaging can render otherwise good products unmerchantable in many retail stores.
Goods found to be unmerchantable may be returned for a refund under some consumer protection laws.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: merchantable - fit for sale

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + merchantable (fit for sale).
