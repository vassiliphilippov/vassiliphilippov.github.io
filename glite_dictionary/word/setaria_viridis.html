Setaria viridis

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛəriə vɪˈraɪdɪs / (AmE), / sɛˈtɛːrɪə vɪˈrɪdɪs / (BrE)
A species of grass, commonly known as green foxtail, native to Europe and naturalized in North America, often considered a weed.
a species of grass; green foxtail

Examples

Setaria viridis is frequently found in disturbed soils and crop fields across North America.
Farmers often struggle to control Setaria viridis due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
The invasive spread of Setaria viridis can significantly reduce crop yields if not managed effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green foxtail - common name for Setaria viridis, a weedy grass species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Setaria' (bristly), 'viridis' (green).
