Cannaceae

1. NOUN

/ kænəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / kænəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
A family of tropical flowering plants consisting solely of the genus Canna, known for their large leaves and colorful flowers.
family of tropical flowering plants (Cannas)

Examples

The brightly colored flowers in the garden belong to the Cannaceae family.
Botanists have studied the evolution and distribution of Cannaceae across tropical regions.
Many ornamental plants used in landscaping are members of the Cannaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canna - A genus of flowering plants in the family Cannaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus name Canna.
