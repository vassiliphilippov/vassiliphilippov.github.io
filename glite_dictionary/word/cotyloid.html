cotyloid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊ.tɪˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.tɪ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or shaped like a cup-shaped socket, especially the cavity in the hipbone that receives the head of the thigh bone (femur)
of a cup-shaped hip socket
medical

Examples

The cotyloid cavity forms a secure socket for the femoral head.
A fracture involving the cotyloid region requires specialized orthopedic treatment.
Doctors examined the cotyloid structure on the X-ray to assess joint alignment.

Etymology

From Greek kotyle (cup, cavity) + -oid (like, resembling).
