Cyprian

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪpriən / (AmE), / ˈsɪpriən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Cyprus
native or inhabitant of Cyprus

Examples

The ancient city was once populated by Cyprians who traded pottery across the Mediterranean.
As a proud Cyprian, she cherishes the island's traditions and cultural festivals.
Cyprians have a rich history reflected in their art and architecture.

Etymology

From Latin Cyprius (of Cyprus) + -an.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪpriən / (AmE), / ˈsɪpriən / (BrE)
relating to Cyprus or its people, language, or culture
of Cyprus or its people/culture

Examples

The Cyprian landscape is famous for its rocky coastline and olive groves.
Ancient Cyprian art often reflects influences from neighboring cultures.
She admired the intricate patterns of traditional Cyprian embroidery.

Synset Examples

Cypriot expatriates
Cypriote monasteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypriot - a person from Cyprus or relating to Cyprus

Etymology

From Latin Cyprius (of Cyprus) with the suffix -an.
