autoclave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtoʊˌkleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊkleɪv / (BrE)
a strong, pressurized device used to heat substances above their boiling point, especially for chemical manufacturing or sterilizing surgical instruments
pressurized device for sterilizing or heating
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician placed the glassware in the autoclave for sterilization.
After every surgery, the instruments are cleaned in an autoclave to eliminate bacteria.
Modern hospitals require at least one autoclave in the sterilization room.

Etymology

From French 'autoclave', from Greek 'auto-' (self) + Latin 'clavis' (key, referring to closure).

2. VERB

/ ˈɔtoʊˌkleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊkleɪv / (BrE)
to subject something to the action of an autoclave, especially for sterilization by steam under pressure
to sterilize using an autoclave
medical

Examples

Laboratory technicians routinely autoclave surgical instruments to eliminate all microorganisms.
After cleaning the glassware, she autoclaved it to ensure it was sterile.
The hospital staff were trained to autoclave waste materials before disposal.

Etymology

From French autoclave, from Greek auto- (self) + Latin clavis (key, meaning 'self-locking vessel').
