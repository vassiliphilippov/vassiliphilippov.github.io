Cercopithecus aethiops pygerythrus

1. NOUN

/ sɜrkoʊˈpɪθəkəs ˌiːθiˈɒps paɪɡəˈrɪθrəs / (AmE), / sɜːkəˈpɪθɪkəs ˌiːθɪˈɒps paɪɡəˈrɪθrəs / (BrE)
a subspecies of vervet monkey native to southern Africa, characterized by a black face and hands
South African subspecies of vervet monkey

Examples

The Cercopithecus aethiops pygerythrus is commonly seen near rivers in southern Africa.
Researchers observed a group of Cercopithecus aethiops pygerythrus foraging in the savannah.
The distinctive black face of the Cercopithecus aethiops pygerythrus makes it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vervet monkey - a common name for members of species and subspecies of Cercopithecus aethiops

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus name 'Cercopithecus' (Greek kérkos, tail + píthēkos, monkey); 'aethiops' meaning 'Ethiopian'; 'pygerythrus' from Greek pyge, rump + erythros, red, referring to reddish coloration.
