mononuclear

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈnuːkliər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈnjuːkliə / (BrE)
(of a cell) having only one nucleus.
having one nucleus (biology)
medical

Examples

White blood cells such as lymphocytes are described as mononuclear.
The mononuclear structure distinguishes these cells from others with multiple nuclei.
Researchers analyzed mononuclear phagocytes collected from patient samples.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multinuclear - (of a cell) having more than one nucleus

Etymology

From mono- (one) + nuclear (relating to a nucleus).
