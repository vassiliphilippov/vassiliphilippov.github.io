abstractionist

1. NOUN

/ æbˈstrækʃənɪst / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃənɪst / (BrE)
an artist, especially a painter, who creates abstract works rather than representing real objects or scenes
artist who creates abstract art

Examples

The gallery is featuring works from a renowned abstractionist who revolutionized modern art.
As an abstractionist, she explored shapes and colors rather than depicting realistic scenes.
Many critics admired the bold techniques of the young abstractionist in his debut exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract artist - artist who creates abstract art

Etymology

From 'abstraction' + '-ist', indicating a practitioner of abstraction in art.

2. ADJ

/ əbˈstrækʃənɪst / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃənɪst / (BrE)
(of art) not representing or imitating external reality or the objects of nature; relating to abstract art.
relating to abstract (non-representational) art

Examples

The gallery featured a collection of abstractionist works that challenged traditional perspectives.
Her abstractionist style set her apart from other painters in the exhibit.
Many critics praised the abstractionist approach for its emotional intensity.

Synset Examples

a large abstract painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - (of art) not representing realistic forms; existing in thought or as an idea

Etymology

From 'abstraction' + '-ist', originally related to practitioners or supporters of abstract art.

Synset POS: s
