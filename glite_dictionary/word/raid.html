raid

1. NOUN

/ reɪd / (AmE), / reɪd / (BrE)
an attempt by speculators to manipulate stock prices or defraud investors, often by sudden or aggressive actions in the market
attempt by speculators to defraud investors

Examples

The aggressive raid on the company's shares caused panic among small investors.
After the raid, the stock price dropped significantly as confidence eroded.
The government is investigating the raid for signs of market manipulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manipulation - the act of controlling or influencing a situation or person cleverly or unscrupulously, especially in finance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00778821-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ reɪd / (AmE), / reɪd / (BrE)
(of police or soldiers) to make a sudden, unexpected attack on a place, often to search it or arrest people.
make a sudden surprise attack or search

Examples

The police raided the warehouse in the middle of the night.
Federal agents raided several offices looking for evidence of fraud.
The military raided the enemy camp at dawn, catching them completely off guard.

Synset Examples

The police raided the crack house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against a place or person

Etymology

From Middle English *raide* (a riding, a raid), from Old French *raide* (a ride, journey), from *rider* (to ride).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02500499-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ reɪd / (AmE), / reɪd / (BrE)
(of an individual or organization) to take over a company by acquiring a controlling interest in its stock, often suddenly or unexpectedly.
take over a company by buying its stock

Examples

Several investors attempted to raid the struggling technology company last year.
The entrepreneur planned to raid his competitor by quietly buying up shares.
After hearing the rumors, the management feared that someone might raid their firm.

Synset Examples

T. Boone Pickens raided many large companies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take over - to gain control of a company by buying its shares
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ reɪd / (AmE), / reɪd / (BrE)
a sudden and surprise attack, especially by a small group in military or police action
sudden surprise attack

Examples

Police carried out a raid on the warehouse early this morning.
The enemy launched a sudden raid across the border at dawn.
The bank was closed for the investigation after the raid by robbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Synonym: assault - a violent attack
  • Antonym: defense - the action of protecting from attack

Etymology

From Scottish or Northern English; related to Old English 'rādan' (to ride).

Vikings used to raid coastal settlements throughout Europe in search of treasure.
