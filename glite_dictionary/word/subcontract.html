subcontract

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.kənˌtrækt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.kənˌtrækt / (BrE)
a legal agreement in which a party assigns some of its contractual obligations to another party
contract assigning obligations to a third party
legal

Examples

The company signed a subcontract with a local firm to complete the electrical work.
Before starting the project, we had to review every subcontract for compliance.
She specializes in negotiating complex subcontract agreements for large-scale developments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between parties

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (meaning 'under') and 'contract', referring to a contract made under another contract.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06536758-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (BrE)
to hire another person or company to carry out part or all of a contract that one is responsible for, especially in business or construction.
hire another to do contracted work

Examples

The company decided to subcontract the electrical work to a local specialist firm.
Due to time constraints, we will subcontract some aspects of the project.
They often subcontract labor to smaller agencies when demand increases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier

Etymology

From sub- (meaning 'under') and contract; modelled after 'contract'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02466306-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.kənˈtrækt / (BrE)
to arrange for contracted work or services to be carried out by another company or individual, rather than doing it oneself
arrange for work to be done by others

Examples

The company decided to subcontract the electrical work to a local specialist.
Many IT firms subcontract their support services to companies overseas.
Due to a lack of resources, the firm subcontracted part of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
  • Synonym: delegate - to assign responsibility or tasks to another

Etymology

From sub- (under) + contract, indicating a contract made under or subordinate to another.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02466486-v
Synset POS: v
