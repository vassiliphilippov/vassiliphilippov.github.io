garnierite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrniˌɪraɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪəraɪt / (BrE)
a green mineral consisting of hydrated nickel magnesium silicate, occurring as a source of nickel ore
a green nickel ore mineral

Examples

Geologists discovered significant deposits of garnierite while surveying the new mining site.
The green coloration of garnierite makes it easy to distinguish from other minerals in the field.
Because garnierite is rich in nickel, it is a valuable ore for the metal industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel - a silvery-white metallic element, source of metal in garnierite

Etymology

Named after Jules Garnier (1839–1904), French engineer and geologist who discovered it.
