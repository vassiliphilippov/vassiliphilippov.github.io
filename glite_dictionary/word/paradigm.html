paradigm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.əˌdaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.daɪm / (BrE)
A set of all the inflected forms of a word, systematically arranged, particularly in grammar or linguistics.
set of all inflected forms of a word

Examples

In Latin, the noun paradigm for 'puella' displays all its case endings.
Students had to memorize the paradigm of irregular verbs for their exam.
The grammar book includes a paradigm illustrating verb endings for each tense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declension - systematic arrangement of noun/adjective inflections
  • Synonym: conjugation - systematic arrangement of verb inflections

Etymology

From Greek paradeigma (pattern, example), via Latin paradigma.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əˌdaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.daɪm / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A set of linguistic items that can be substituted for each other in a grammatical slot within sentences.
set of grammatically substitutable forms

Examples

In this sentence, the noun paradigm includes words like 'cat', 'dog', and 'teacher'.
Verb forms such as 'run', 'runs', 'ran', and 'running' belong to the same paradigm.
Linguists analyze how different words fit into a paradigm within sentence structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a group with shared features or attributes

Etymology

From Greek paradeigma (pattern, example), via Latin paradigma.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəˌdaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpærədaɪm / (BrE)
a generally accepted model or pattern in a particular field or discipline, especially one that shapes methods and attitudes
accepted model or framework in a field

Examples

His research challenged the established paradigm in cognitive science.
The rise of quantum theory sparked a major shift in the scientific paradigm.
Sociologists often debate which paradigm best explains changes in society.

Synset Examples

he framed the problem within the psychoanalytic paradigm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a representation or example serving as a pattern
  • Synonym: framework - a basic structure underlying a system or concept

Etymology

From Greek *paradeigma* (pattern, example), via Latin *paradigma*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌdaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpærədaɪm / (BrE)
a typical example or pattern of something; a model that serves as a standard or guide
a typical example or model

Examples

His approach to education became a paradigm for teachers throughout the country.
Einstein's theories represent a paradigm shift in the world of physics.
The company was seen as a paradigm of good business practices.

Synset Examples

he is the prototype of good breeding
he provided America with an image of the good father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prototype - the first or preliminary model of something from which others are copied
  • Synonym: model - a system or thing used as an example to follow or imitate
  • Antonym: exception - something that does not follow the rule or pattern

Etymology

From Late Latin paradigma, from Greek paradeigma (pattern, example), from paradeiknunai (to show side by side).
