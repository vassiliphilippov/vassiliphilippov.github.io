to spend

1. VERB

/ spɛnd / (AmE), / spɛnd / (BrE)
to use a period of time doing a particular activity
use time in a particular way

Examples

We decided to spend the afternoon exploring the city.
He likes to spend his weekends hiking in the mountains.
She plans to spend her vacation learning a new language.

Synset Examples

how are you spending your summer vacation?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waste - to use time in a careless or unnecessary way
  • Synonym: pass - to make time go by in a particular way

Etymology

From Old English *spendan*, from Latin *expendere* (to pay out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02714280-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spɛnd / (AmE), / spɛnd / (BrE)
to pay out money, especially for goods or services
pay out money

Examples

She tries to spend as little money as possible when shopping.
We spent a fortune on our vacation last year.
He regrets spending so much on unnecessary gadgets.

Synset Examples

spend money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: save - to keep money rather than spend it
  • Synonym: to expend - to use up or spend resources

Etymology

From Old English *spendan*, from Latin *expendere* (to pay out) via Old French *despendre*, meaning to disburse or pay out.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02271905-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spɛnd / (AmE), / spɛnd / (BrE)
to use up or pay out money, time, or resources; to exhaust or consume completely
use up money or resources

Examples

He managed to spend all his savings on a new car within a month.
If you spend too much time on your phone, you'll have little time left for homework.
She spent her last dollar on a cup of coffee before payday.

Synset Examples

I spend my pocket money in two days

Related Terms

  • Antonym: save - to keep money or resources instead of spending them
  • Synonym: to expend - to use or spend resources

Etymology

From Old English 'spendan', from Latin 'expendere' (to pay out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02272374-v
Synset POS: v
