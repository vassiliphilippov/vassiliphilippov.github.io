Thrinax

1. NOUN

/ ˈθraɪ.næks / (AmE), / ˈθrɪ.naks / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized fan palms native to the Caribbean and tropical Americas.
a genus of fan palms

Examples

The Thrinax palm is often used in landscaping in tropical areas.
Studies have shown that Thrinax species are drought-resistant and adaptable.
We spotted several Thrinax trees growing along the sandy beach.

Etymology

From Greek *thrinax* (a kind of palm or trident-shaped object).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθraɪ.næks / (AmE), / ˈθrɪ.naks / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized fan palms native to tropical regions of the Americas.
genus of fan palms

Examples

The Thrinax palms are often found along Caribbean coastlines.
Botanists have documented several unique features within the genus Thrinax.
A mature Thrinax can grow up to ten meters tall in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Ancient Greek θρῖναξ (thrínax), meaning 'trident' or 'three-pronged fork'.
