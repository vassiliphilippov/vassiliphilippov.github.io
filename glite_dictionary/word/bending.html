bending

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of causing something to curve or change shape from a straight form
the act of making something curve

Examples

The bending of the metal pipe required special tools.
She suffered a back injury after repeated bending at work.
Engineers tested the bending strength of the new bridge materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightening - the act of making something straight or less curved

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of shaping or forcing something into a curve or angle
the act of making something bend

Examples

Repeated bending of the metal can cause it to break.
Yoga often involves the bending of the spine in different directions.
The bending of the branch under the weight of the fruit was noticeable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightening - the act of making something straight after it has been bent

Etymology

From the verb 'bend', of Old English origin (bendan, meaning to bend or curve).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the property or process of being bent or deflected, especially under force; the action or effect of making something curve
the quality or act of being bent

Examples

The bending of the metal beam was visible after the heavy weight was applied.
Proper testing can measure the bending strength of construction materials.
He studied the bending properties of different types of plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexure - the act of bending or the condition of being bent
  • Antonym: straightening - the process of making or becoming straight
