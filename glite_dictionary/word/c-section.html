C-section

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / siː ˈsekʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to deliver a baby by making incisions through the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical delivery of a baby
medical

Examples

The doctors decided that a C-section was the safest option for both mother and child.
After a long labor, she ultimately gave birth by C-section.
Rates of C-section deliveries have increased in many countries over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caesarean section - surgical delivery of a baby through the abdomen

Etymology

Short for 'Caesarean section', from Latin 'Caesarea' (relating to Caesar, from the belief about Julius Caesar's birth).
