preserver

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɜrvər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvə / (BrE)
a buoyant belt or jacket designed to keep a person afloat and prevent drowning
buoyant rescue device

Examples

Every passenger was given a preserver before the boat left the dock.
He wore a brightly colored preserver while learning to swim.
The safety instructions explained how to put on the preserver in an emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifejacket - a sleeveless buoyant jacket for keeping a person afloat
  • Synonym: life belt - a belt or ring made of buoyant material for saving people from drowning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03668857-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɝːvɚ / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvə / (BrE)
a person who protects someone or something from harm or danger
person who protects from harm or danger

Examples

He was hailed as a preserver of local traditions and history.
Thanks to the quick actions of the preserver, the animal shelter was saved from fire.
Many view her as the preserver of peace in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protector - a person who protects someone or something
  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from danger or harm
  • Antonym: endangerer - one who puts at risk of harm or danger

Etymology

From preserve + -er, originally from Latin praeservare (to keep beforehand).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɜrvər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvə / (BrE)
a person who preserves fruits, meat, or other foods, especially by canning or other storage methods
person who preserves foods

Examples

My grandmother was an expert preserver and filled our pantry with homemade jams.
The village preserver prepared jars of pickled vegetables every autumn.
He became a skilled preserver after learning traditional meat-curing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canner - a person who preserves food by canning

Etymology

From preserve + -er, meaning one who preserves.

4. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɜrvər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvə / (BrE)
a skilled worker employed to restore or refinish buildings or antique furniture
worker who restores or refinishes objects

Examples

The preserver worked meticulously to restore the historic mansion's wooden paneling.
An experienced preserver can bring antique furniture back to its original glory.
Museums often employ a preserver to care for valuable artifacts and furnishings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restorer - a person who restores works of art, furniture, or buildings

Etymology

From preserve + -er, denoting a person who preserves.
