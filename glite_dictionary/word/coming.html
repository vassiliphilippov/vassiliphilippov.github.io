coming

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
The time when something is about to happen or is drawing nearer; arrival or approach.
approaching or impending arrival

Examples

The coming of spring brings new hope each year.
Everyone was eagerly awaiting the coming of the new product.
The coming of winter forced the animals to seek shelter.

Synset Examples

the approach of winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrival - the act of reaching a place or event
  • Antonym: departure - the act of leaving, opposite of arrival

Etymology

From Old English 'cuman' (to come) plus '-ing'.

WordNet ID: oewn-05053622-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of events, times, etc.) about to happen or expected to happen soon.
happening soon; upcoming

Examples

We have an important meeting scheduled for this coming Monday.
The director announced big changes in the coming months.
In the coming weeks, the weather is expected to improve.

Synset Examples

the approaching election
this coming Thursday
the forthcoming holidays
the upcoming spring fashions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upcoming - about to happen soon
  • Synonym: forthcoming - going to happen soon
  • Antonym: past - already happened
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
