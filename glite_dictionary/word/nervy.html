nervy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɝːvi / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvi / (BrE)
showing courage or boldness, especially in situations involving danger or risk
showing courage or boldness

Examples

The nervy climber scaled the sheer rock face without hesitation.
It was a nervy move to challenge the established authority in front of everyone.
Her nervy performance earned her the admiration of even her harshest critics.

Synset Examples

the nervy feats of mountaineers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearless - lacking fear; very brave
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvi / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvi / (BrE)
feeling or showing nervousness or tension; in a tense and uneasy state
feeling nervous or tense

Examples

He became nervy as the deadline for the project approached.
During the interview, she seemed a bit nervy and kept tapping her foot.
The nervy anticipation was obvious in the locker room before the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; anxious or tense

Etymology

From 'nerve' + -y, denoting being full of nerves or nervousness.

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvi / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvi / (BrE)
Showing a boldness that can be seen as rude or disrespectful; brash or impudent.
offensively bold; brash

Examples

It was a nervy move to question the director’s decision in front of everyone.
She made a nervy comment that left the audience stunned.
Despite being a nervy critic, he rarely offered useful solutions.

Synset Examples

a brash newcomer disputed the age-old rules for admission to the club
a nervy thing to say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brash - self-assertive in a rude or overbearing way
  • Synonym: audacious - showing a willingness to take surprisingly bold risks
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00206371-s
Synset POS: s
