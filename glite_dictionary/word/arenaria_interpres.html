Arenaria interpres

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɛːnəriə ɪnˈtɜrprəs / (AmE), / əˌrɛnəriə ɪnˈtɜːprɪs / (BrE)
A species of migratory wading bird known as the ruddy turnstone, which breeds in the Arctic and winters in South America and Australia.
a migratory wading bird (ruddy turnstone)

Examples

Every spring, flocks of Arenaria interpres can be seen migrating along the coastline.
The Arenaria interpres is easily recognized by its striking plumage and habit of flipping over stones for food.
Researchers tracked the Arenaria interpres as it traveled thousands of miles between breeding and wintering grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruddy turnstone - the common name for Arenaria interpres

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arenaria' meaning 'of sand' and 'interpres' meaning 'messenger' or 'explainer.'
