dispirited

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing a lack of enthusiasm or hope; having lost spirit or morale.
lacking enthusiasm or hope

Examples

After the team's third consecutive loss, the players looked dispirited as they left the field.
She returned from the meeting feeling dispirited by the lack of progress.
His dispirited attitude made it difficult to motivate the rest of the group.

Synset Examples

a dispirited and divided Party
reacted to the crisis with listless resignation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downcast - depressed or dejected in mood
  • Antonym: elated - very happy or proud

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritus' (breath, spirit) via 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'spirit', meaning spiritless or lacking spirit.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Feeling a loss of enthusiasm or hope; lacking in spirit, energy, or courage.
lacking spirit or enthusiasm

Examples

After hearing the bad news, he looked dispirited and withdrawn.
The team grew dispirited after losing three games in a row.
She tried to cheer her dispirited friend with a kind message.

Synset Examples

downcast after his defeat
feeling discouraged and downhearted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downcast - feeling sad or discouraged
  • Synonym: discouraged - lacking confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'spirited', from Latin 'spiritus' (breath, spirit).
