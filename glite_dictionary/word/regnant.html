regnant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛɡ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡ.nənt / (BrE)
reigning; having power or authority, especially as a ruler or dominant force.
reigning; exercising authority

Examples

The regnant queen ruled her kingdom with wisdom and justice.
During the regnant dynasty, the country experienced great cultural growth.
A regnant idea in politics today is the need for greater transparency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reigning - exercising sovereign power; currently ruling
  • Antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or authority, not having control

Etymology

From Latin regnant-, present participle of regnare (to reign).
