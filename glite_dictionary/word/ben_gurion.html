Ben Gurion

1. PROPN

/ bɛn ɡʊˈriːən / (AmE), / bɛn ɡʊəˈriːən / (BrE)
David Ben Gurion (1886–1973), Israeli statesman and Zionist leader who became the first prime minister of Israel and played a key role in the founding of the state.
first prime minister of Israel

Examples

Ben Gurion was instrumental in declaring the independence of Israel in 1948.
Many international flights to Israel arrive at Ben Gurion Airport, named in his honor.
As prime minister, Ben Gurion led the country through its formative years.

Etymology

From Hebrew בן-גוריון (Ben-Gurion), meaning 'son of Gurion', his chosen Hebrew name.
