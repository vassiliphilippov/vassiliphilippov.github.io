to demobilize

1. VERB

/ diˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to release someone from military service, especially after a war, or to disband military forces
release from military service

Examples

After the war ended, the government began to demobilize thousands of soldiers.
The army plans to demobilize several units as part of its restructuring efforts.
Many veterans struggled to adjust to civilian life after being demobilized.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mobilize - to prepare and organize troops for active service

Etymology

From French démobiliser, from dé- (reverse) + mobiliser (to mobilize).

2. VERB

/ diˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈməʊbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to release someone or something, especially troops, from military service or readiness for war
to release from military service

Examples

After the war ended, the government began to demobilize the army.
Many soldiers were eager to return home once they were demobilized.
The country took months to demobilize its forces after the peace treaty was signed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mobilize - to prepare and organize troops for active service

Etymology

From French 'démobiliser', from 'mobiliser' (to mobilize).
