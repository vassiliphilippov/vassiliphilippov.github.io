scuppernong

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌpərˌnɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈskʌpənɒŋ / (BrE)
a variety of amber-green muscadine grape native to the southeastern United States, often used for making wine and preserves
amber-green muscadine grape of southeastern US

Examples

We tasted a refreshing juice made from scuppernong grapes at the local farmers' market.
Scuppernong vines thrive in the warm climate of North Carolina.
She made jelly using ripe scuppernong picked from her backyard orchard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscadine - a type of grape native to the southeastern United States

Etymology

Named after the Scuppernong River in North Carolina, USA, where the grape was first noted.
