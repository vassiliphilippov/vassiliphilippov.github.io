Anu

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.nuː / (AmE), / ˈɑː.njuː / (BrE)
In Babylonian mythology, the god of the sky and one of the supreme triad along with Bel and Ea.
Babylonian sky god

Examples

Anu was regarded as the supreme authority among the Babylonian gods.
Priests built temples dedicated to Anu in ancient Mesopotamia.
Alongside Bel and Ea, Anu formed a powerful divine triad.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɑːnu / (AmE), / ˈɑːnu / (BrE)
A mother goddess in ancient Irish mythology, sometimes identified with Danu as the mother of the gods.
mother goddess in Irish mythology

Examples

Many ancient sites in Ireland are believed to be sacred to Anu.
In Irish mythology, Anu is revered as the mother of the gods.
The two hilltops known as the Paps of Anu are named after this goddess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Danu - mother goddess in Irish mythology, sometimes considered identical with Anu

Etymology

Of Celtic origin, from Old Irish 'Anu', the name of a mother goddess.
