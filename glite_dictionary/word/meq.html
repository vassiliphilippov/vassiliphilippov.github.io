meq

1. NOUN

/ ɛm iː kjuː / (AmE), / ɛm iː kjuː / (BrE)
A unit equal to one-thousandth of an equivalent (milliequivalent), used to express concentrations in chemistry and medicine.
unit: one-thousandth of an equivalent
medical

Examples

The patient's potassium level was measured in meq per liter.
Most intravenous fluids indicate sodium content in meq.
Lab results often include electrolyte concentrations reported as meq/L.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milliequivalent - a unit of measure equal to one-thousandth of an equivalent
  • Degree Higher: equivalent - a unit of chemical combining power

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'milliequivalent', from Latin 'mille' (thousand) + 'equivalent'.
