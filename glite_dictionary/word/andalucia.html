Andalucia

1. PROPN

/ ˌændəˈluʃə / (AmE), / ˌændəˈluːθiə / (BrE)
a region in southern Spain, known for its rich Moorish history and cultural heritage, bordering both the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea.
region in southern Spain

Examples

This summer we traveled through Andalucia to visit Granada, Seville, and Córdoba.
The cuisine of Andalucia is famous for its use of olive oil, seafood, and vibrant spices.
Many architectural masterpieces in Andalucia reflect its Moorish past.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Andalucía', or Arabic 'al-Andalus' (historic name for Muslim-ruled Iberia).
