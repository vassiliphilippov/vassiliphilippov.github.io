forger

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒə / (BrE)
a person who operates a forge, typically working with metal by heating and shaping it; a blacksmith.
person who operates a forge

Examples

The forger skillfully shaped the red-hot iron into a new horseshoe.
As a young apprentice, he learned from the village forger how to craft tools.
Before the advent of factories, every town relied on a skilled forger for its metalwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blacksmith - a person who makes and repairs things in iron by hand

Etymology

From Old French 'forgier', from Latin 'fabrica' (workshop).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒə / (BrE)
a person who makes fake copies of documents, artwork, money, or similar items, usually for illegal purposes
person who makes illegal copies

Examples

The police arrested the forger after discovering several counterfeit passports in his apartment.
Art experts were able to identify the painting as the work of a skilled forger.
The forger was sentenced to prison for producing fake documents used in the scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeiter - a person who makes exact imitations of something valuable with intent to deceive or defraud

Etymology

From Old French *forgier* (to forge), from Latin *fabricare* (to fabricate, make).

Synset POS: n
