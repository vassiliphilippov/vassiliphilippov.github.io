Tutelo

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈtɛloʊ / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɛləʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Siouan-speaking Indigenous people from Virginia and North Carolina.
member of a Siouan people of Virginia

Examples

The Tutelo had a rich oral tradition, passing down stories through generations.
Several descendants of the Tutelo still preserve elements of their ancestral language.
Historians have documented the movements of the Tutelo during colonial expansion.

2. NOUN

/ tuːˈtiːloʊ / (AmE), / tuːˈtiːləʊ / (BrE)
the extinct Siouan language formerly spoken by the Tutelo people of the eastern United States
an extinct Siouan language

Examples

The Tutelo language is no longer spoken today but has been studied by linguists.
She wrote her thesis on the grammar of Tutelo.
Some vocabulary from Tutelo has been preserved in historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

From the name of the Tutelo people, recorded by English settlers.
