botched

1. ADJ

/ bɑtʃt / (AmE), / bɒtʃt / (BrE)
(of a task, action, or project) spoiled or ruined as a result of incompetence or clumsiness.
spoiled due to incompetence or clumsiness

Examples

The repairs to the roof were botched, leading to even more leaks.
She was disappointed after seeing the botched paint job on her car.
A botched rescue attempt left the hikers stranded for another day.

Synset Examples

a bungled job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungled - carried out incompetently or clumsily
  • Antonym: flawless - without faults or errors

Etymology

From Middle English bocchen (to mend or patch badly).
