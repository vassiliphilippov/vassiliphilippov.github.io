acuate

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈak.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
ending in a sharp or pointed tip; tapering to a fine point
ending in a sharp point

Examples

The leaf is acuate, ending in a distinct, sharp tip.
Under the microscope, the insect’s wing appeared acuate, tapering precisely to a point.
Certain shells possess an acuate apex that distinguishes them from similar species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute - having a sharp end or point; sharply angled

Etymology

From Latin 'acuatus', past participle of 'acuare' (to sharpen), from 'acus' (needle).
