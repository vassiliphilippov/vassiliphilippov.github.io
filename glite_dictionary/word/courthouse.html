courthouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a building where a court of law is held, often housing offices for county government as well
building for court and county offices

Examples

The old courthouse at the center of town is now a museum.
Jury duty requires her to report to the courthouse every morning this week.
The mayor’s office is located in the county courthouse along with other government departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal presided over by a judge, judges, or a magistrate in civil and criminal cases

Etymology

From 'court' (law court) + 'house'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A building where judicial courts are held and legal cases are heard.
building where courts are held

Examples

The city plans to renovate the historic courthouse in the town square.
Jury duty requires you to report to the courthouse on Monday morning.
Lawyers gathered outside the courthouse after the verdict was announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal presided over by a judge, judges, or a magistrate in civil and criminal cases

Etymology

From 'court' (as in law court) + 'house'.
