baronage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ə.nɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbær.ənɪdʒ / (BrE)
all the barons or peers of a kingdom considered collectively; the nobility of a realm.
the collective nobility or peers of a kingdom

Examples

The medieval baronage played a crucial role in shaping the English legal system.
Several members of the Scottish baronage attended the king's coronation.
During the 13th century, the influence of the baronage often challenged royal authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peerage - the nobility as a class; peers collectively

Etymology

From Old French *baronage* (rank of baron, nobility), from *baron*.
