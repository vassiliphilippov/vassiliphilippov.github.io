altruism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæltruˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæltruːɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of selfless concern for the well-being of others
selfless concern for others

Examples

Many people admire altruism in those who dedicate their lives to helping others.
Her acts of altruism often inspired her friends to be more generous.
Some question whether true altruism exists, or if all actions are ultimately motivated by self-interest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selfishness - concern with one's own interests above others

Etymology

From French 'altruisme', based on Italian 'altrui' (somebody else), from Latin 'alter' (other).
