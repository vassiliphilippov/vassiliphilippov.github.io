to oppugn

1. VERB

/ əˈpjun / (AmE), / əˈpjuːn / (BrE)
to challenge or call into question the truth, validity, or propriety of something.
to dispute or question the validity of
archaic

Examples

Several critics have oppugned the findings of the official report.
The defense attorney sought to oppugn the witness's credibility during cross-examination.
Some historians oppugn the traditional accounts of the event, offering alternative explanations.

Synset Examples

We must question your judgment in this matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to dispute the truth or validity of something
  • Synonym: question - to express doubt about something's truth or correctness
  • Antonym: to defend - to support or uphold the validity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'oppugnare' (to attack), from 'ob-' (against) + 'pugnare' (to fight).
