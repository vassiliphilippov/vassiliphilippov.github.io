irascible

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈræsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈrasɪbəl / (BrE)
Easily provoked to anger or having a tendency to become angry quickly.
quick to anger

Examples

His irascible demeanor made him difficult to work with during stressful times.
The irascible old man yelled at anyone who walked across his lawn.
Despite his irascible reputation, he was kind to children and animals.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded commander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotheaded - prone to acting rashly or becoming angry quickly
  • Antonym: calm - not easily angered or upset

Etymology

From Latin 'irascibilis', from 'irasci' (to become angry).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈræsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈræsɪbəl / (BrE)
easily provoked to anger; having a tendency to become angry quickly.
easily angered; quick-tempered

Examples

The irascible manager lost his temper over the slightest mistake.
Her irascible remarks made it clear she was not in the mood for jokes.
He became so irascible that nobody dared to approach him.

Synset Examples

a choleric outburst
an irascible response

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choleric - bad-tempered or irritable
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger

Etymology

From Latin irascibilis (easily angered), from irasci (to become angry).
