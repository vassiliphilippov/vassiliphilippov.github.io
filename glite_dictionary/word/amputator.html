amputator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmpjəˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæmpjʊˌteɪtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a surgeon, who performs amputations by removing part or all of a limb
a person who performs amputations
medical

Examples

The amputator carefully assessed the damage before removing the patient's lower leg.
As a renowned amputator, he specialized in complex trauma surgery.
During the war, the field hospital relied on the skills of its chief amputator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgeon - a doctor qualified to perform surgery

Etymology

From Latin 'amputare' (to cut off), with '-or' denoting one who performs an action.
