brassbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræsˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of an object) having trim or fittings made of brass, especially as decoration or reinforcement.
having brass fittings or trim

Examples

The captain stored valuable documents in a brassbound chest for extra security.
We admired the brassbound trunk that belonged to her grandfather.
The old mansion had a huge, brassbound door at its entrance.

Synset Examples

a brassbound campaign chest
the heavy brassbound door

Etymology

Formed from 'brass' + 'bound', indicating something bound or decorated with brass.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbræsˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of attitudes, rules, or traditions) rigidly fixed and unlikely to change; inflexibly entrenched.
rigidly fixed or inflexible (especially of traditions)

Examples

The company followed a brassbound set of procedures that left no room for innovation.
Many critics argue that brassbound regulations prevent meaningful reform in the education system.
He found it difficult to fit in with the brassbound traditions of his new workplace.

Synset Examples

brassbound traditions
brassbound party loyalists
an ironclad rule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironclad - too strong or firm to be altered; rigid
  • Antonym: flexible - able to change or adapt easily

Etymology

Originally a nautical term describing uniforms with brass trim, later used metaphorically for rigidity.
