immiscible

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɪsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪsɪbl̩ / (BrE)
(of liquids) incapable of mixing or forming a homogeneous mixture.
incapable of mixing (of liquids)

Examples

Oil and water are immiscible and form separate layers when combined.
The two chemicals remained immiscible despite vigorous stirring.
Most organic solvents are immiscible with water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miscible - (of liquids) capable of being mixed to form a homogeneous solution

Etymology

From Latin 'immiscibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'miscibilis' (able to be mixed).
