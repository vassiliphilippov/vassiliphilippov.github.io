fallibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌfælɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the tendency or likelihood to make mistakes or be wrong
likelihood of making mistakes

Examples

Scientists recognize the fallibility of their theories and always seek further evidence.
The judge addressed the jury about the fallibility of eyewitness testimony.
Her willingness to admit her own fallibility earned her colleagues’ respect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infallibility - the inability to be wrong or make mistakes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fallibilis (liable to err) + -ity.
