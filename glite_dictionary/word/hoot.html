hoot

1. NOUN

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
a loud, harsh cry or call, especially the characteristic sound made by an owl
loud cry, esp. of an owl

Examples

The barn owl let out a soft hoot in the darkness.
We heard a loud hoot echoing through the woods at night.
Suddenly, the silence was broken by the hoot of an owl perched on the branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - a loud, harsh, piercing cry
  • Degree Lower: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects

Etymology

Imitative origin, from Middle English 'houten', resembling the sound made.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
a loud cry or sound made by people to show their displeasure or contempt, especially during performances or public events
loud cry of displeasure or contempt

Examples

The comedian's terrible joke was met with a loud hoot from the audience.
As the player was sent off, a hoot of anger rose from the stands.
The proposal was greeted with a chorus of hoots and jeers from the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boo - a loud cry of disapproval, often from an audience

Etymology

Imitative or from Middle English *houten* (to shout, call out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07138578-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
(of an owl) to make its characteristic low, resonant call.
make the call of an owl

Examples

We could hear an owl hoot in the distance as night fell.
Every night, the barn owl hoots from its perch in the old tree.
The forest was silent except for the occasional hooting of an owl.

Etymology

Imitative in origin, from the sound made by owls.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01055093-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
A thing of little or no value (used especially in the negative, as in 'not worth a hoot').
something of little value (used in negatives)
informal

Examples

He didn't care a hoot about what people thought of his decision.
The old car isn't worth a hoot anymore, so we might as well sell it for scrap.
She doesn't give a hoot about fashion trends, and wears whatever she likes.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - something of little value (in phrases like 'not worth a damn')
  • Synonym: red cent - something of very small value (in 'not worth a red cent')
  • Synonym: shucks - something considered to have little worth
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05148040-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
to make a loud, clamorous shout or cry, often to express disapproval or excitement
make a loud shout or cry

Examples

The angry crowd began to hoot at the politician as she took the stage.
Fans would hoot loudly whenever their team scored a goal.
He hooted in disbelief when he heard the surprising news.

Synset Examples

the toughs and blades of the city hoot and bang their drums, drink arak, play dice, and dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to shout mocking or insulting remarks
  • Antonym: cheer - to shout in encouragement or approval

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound itself.
