hydrographical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the science of hydrography, which studies the physical features of bodies of water and their surrounding terrain
relating to hydrography (study of water bodies)

Examples

The hydrographical survey revealed important changes in the riverbed over the past decade.
Maps with detailed hydrographical data are essential for safe navigation in the area.
Researchers analyzed the hydrographical characteristics of the bay to understand its ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrographic - alternative form meaning relating to hydrography

Etymology

From hydrography + -ical, based on Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'graphō' (write).
