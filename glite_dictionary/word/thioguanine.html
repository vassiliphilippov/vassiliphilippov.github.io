thioguanine

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪ.oʊˈɡwɑːnin / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.əʊˈɡwɑːniːn / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug used in the treatment of acute leukemias
a drug for treating acute leukemia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed thioguanine as part of her leukemia treatment regimen.
Researchers are studying the long-term effects of thioguanine on childhood leukemia survivors.
Because of its side effects, liver function must be monitored during thioguanine therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercaptopurine - an antineoplastic drug structurally related to thioguanine

Etymology

From 'thio-' (containing sulfur) and 'guanine' (a nucleic acid base), indicating its chemical structure.
