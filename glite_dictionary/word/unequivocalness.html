unequivocalness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪˈkwɪvəkəlnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈkwɪvəkəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clear and not open to more than one interpretation; unambiguity
the quality of being unambiguous or clear

Examples

Her unequivocalness in stating the facts left no room for confusion among the listeners.
The unequivocalness of the contract terms protected both parties from misunderstanding.
I appreciate the unequivocalness of your response; it makes the decision straightforward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguity - the quality of being open to more than one interpretation
  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From 'unequivocal' + '-ness', where 'unequivocal' is from Latin 'aequivocus' (ambiguous) with prefix 'un-' (not).
