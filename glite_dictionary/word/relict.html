relict

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪkt / (BrE)
(Geology) A physical feature that remains from a previous geological formation after other parts have been removed or eroded away.
remnant of an earlier geological formation

Examples

The isolated hill is a relict from an ancient mountain range that has mostly eroded away.
This valley contains several relicts that provide evidence of past geological processes.
Scientists studied the rock formation, a relict of volcanic activity from millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity or fragment of something

Etymology

From Latin relictus (left behind, abandoned), past participle of relinquere (to leave behind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪkt / (BrE)
an organism or species that survives as a remnant of an otherwise extinct group in a changed environment
organism surviving as remnant of extinct group

Examples

The tuatara is considered a relict of a once-diverse group of reptiles.
Some ancient pine species survive as relicts in isolated mountain regions.
The discovery of a living relict species excited biologists around the world.

Etymology

From Latin relictus (left behind, abandoned), past participle of relinquere (to leave behind).
