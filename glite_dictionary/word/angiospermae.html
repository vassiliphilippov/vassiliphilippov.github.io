Angiospermae

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒioʊˈspɜːrmiː / (AmE), / ˌændʒiəʊˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
A large group of plants that produce flowers and whose seeds are enclosed within an ovary (fruit); also known as flowering plants. In some classification systems, considered a class or a division.
flowering plants group (seeds in ovary)

Examples

Angiospermae include most of the familiar plants such as roses, orchids, and grasses.
In evolutionary terms, Angiospermae are considered more advanced than gymnosperms.
Scientific studies have shown that Angiospermae dominate many terrestrial ecosystems worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gymnosperm - a group of seed-producing plants whose seeds are not enclosed in an ovary

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek angeion (vessel) + sperma (seed), referring to seeds contained within a vessel (ovary).

2. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiəˈspɜːrmiː / (AmE), / ˌændʒɪəʊˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
A large group of plants comprising all flowering plants that produce seeds enclosed within an ovary.
group of flowering plants

Examples

Angiospermae are distinguished from gymnosperms by having seeds contained within an ovary.
Many of the world's crops, such as wheat and rice, belong to the Angiospermae.
Scientists classify Angiospermae based on their floral structures and seed types.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gymnosperm - plant that has seeds unprotected by an ovary or fruit

Etymology

From Greek 'angeion' (vessel) + 'sperma' (seed).
