bison

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪsən / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪsən / (BrE)
A large wild animal of the cattle family with a humped back, shaggy mane, and short horns, native to North America and Europe.
large wild humped animal

Examples

The American bison once roamed the Great Plains in vast herds.
At the wildlife sanctuary, we saw a group of European bison grazing.
The bison is an important symbol in many Native American cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo - large wild bovine animals closely related to bison

Etymology

From Latin 'bison', from Greek 'bison', likely of Germanic origin.
