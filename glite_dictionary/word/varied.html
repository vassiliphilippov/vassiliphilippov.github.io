varied

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛɹid / (AmE), / ˈveərid / (BrE)
showing differences; consisting of or characterized by diverse elements or forms; not uniform or identical.
showing differences; diverse

Examples

The landscape was varied, ranging from rolling hills to deep forests.
She enjoys a varied diet that includes dishes from many countries.
Our experiences on the trip were varied, from relaxing beaches to challenging mountain hikes.

Synset Examples

her quickly varied answers indicated uncertainty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniform - not changing in form or character; remaining the same in all cases
  • Synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different

Etymology

From Latin 'variatus', past participle of 'variare' (to change, to vary).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛr.iɪd / (AmE), / ˈveə.rɪd / (BrE)
incorporating or showing a wide range of different types, kinds, or forms; diverse.
consisting of different types or forms

Examples

The museum features a varied collection of artwork from around the world.
Her interests are varied, ranging from classical music to mountain climbing.
The restaurant offers a varied menu to please guests with different tastes.

Synset Examples

varied motives prompt people to join a political party
varied ethnic traditions of the immigrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different
  • Antonym: similar - resembling without being identical

Etymology

Past participle of 'vary', from Latin variāre (to change, make different).

characterized by diversity or containing many different types or elements
showing diversity or variety

Examples

Related Terms
