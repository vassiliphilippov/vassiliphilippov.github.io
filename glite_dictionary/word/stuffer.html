stuffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfər / (AmE), / ˈstʌfə / (BrE)
an advertising leaflet or circular inserted with other mail, typically for promotional purposes
advertisement enclosed in mailings

Examples

The company included a stuffer in each envelope to promote their new product line.
Every catalog mailing came with a colorful stuffer advertising seasonal discounts.
She tossed the stuffer into the recycling bin without reading it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - an advertisement placed inside a newspaper or mailing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07265395-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfər / (AmE), / ˈstʌfə / (BrE)
a person who stuffs and mounts the skins of animals for display, especially as part of taxidermy
person who stuffs and mounts animals

Examples

The stuffer carefully mounted the bird for the museum display.
She apprenticed with a skilled stuffer to learn the basics of taxidermy.
The old shop was run by a well-known stuffer in the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxidermist - a person skilled in the art of stuffing and mounting animal skins
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10712955-n
Synset POS: n
