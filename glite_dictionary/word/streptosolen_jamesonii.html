Streptosolen jamesonii

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛptəˌsoʊlən dʒæˈmɛsəniˌaɪ / (AmE), / stɹɛptəˌsəʊlən dʒæˈmɛsəniˌaɪ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to South America, known for its showy, trumpet-shaped orange flowers and often cultivated as an ornamental or houseplant
evergreen shrub with orange trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Streptosolen jamesonii make it a favorite among gardeners.
We placed a Streptosolen jamesonii on the patio to add a splash of color.
Streptosolen jamesonii thrives in warm, sunny locations with well-drained soil.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'solen' (tube), referring to the flower's shape; named after botanist William Jameson.
