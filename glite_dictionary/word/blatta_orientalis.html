Blatta orientalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːtə ɔːriˈɛn.təlɪs / (AmE), / ˈblætə ɒˌriːɛnˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of large, dark brown cockroach originally from the Orient, now found worldwide; also called the Oriental cockroach.
species of dark brown cockroach
medical

Examples

The pest inspector identified the infestation as Blatta orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental cockroach.
Blatta orientalis thrives in damp, dark environments and is difficult to eliminate once established.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Blatta orientalis in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oriental cockroach - common name for Blatta orientalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'blatta' meaning cockroach and 'orientalis' meaning eastern/oriental.
