to consider

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɪd.ɚ / (AmE), / kənˈsɪd.ə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something attentively; to observe or examine closely
look at attentively

Examples

She paused to consider the painting in silence before moving on.
He sat on the porch, considering the landscape as the sun set.
The judge considered the defendant's face for a long moment before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to observe - to watch carefully

Etymology

From Latin 'considerare' (to look at closely, contemplate).
