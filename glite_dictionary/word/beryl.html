beryl

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛrəl / (BrE)
a transparent or translucent mineral, typically green, blue, or yellow, that is the chief source of beryllium and occurs in gemstone varieties such as emerald and aquamarine
a mineral and gemstone; source of beryllium

Examples

The museum displays a rare beryl crystal that is nearly flawless.
Gem cutters value beryl for its clarity and rich colors such as green and blue.
Emerald is a green variety of beryl prized for its beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emerald - a green gemstone variety of beryl
  • Synonym: aquamarine - a blue-green gemstone variety of beryl

Etymology

From Greek bēryllos (beryl, a precious blue-green color of sea water), via Latin beryllus.
