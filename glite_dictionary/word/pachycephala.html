Pachycephala

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A genus of arboreal insectivorous birds, commonly known as whistlers, native to Australasia and Southeast Asia.
genus of whistling birds

Examples

The Pachycephala are often recognized by their melodious calls in the rainforests of New Guinea.
A Pachycephala was spotted flitting through the trees, searching for insects among the leaves.
Researchers study the behavior and habitat range of the Pachycephala throughout Australasia.

Etymology

From Greek *pachys* (thick) + *kephalē* (head), referring to their robust skulls.

2. NOUN

a genus of arboreal insectivorous birds native to Australasia and Southeast Asia, known as whistlers
genus of whistler birds

Examples

The Pachycephala are well-known for their melodious whistling calls in the forests.
Researchers observed a Pachycephala hunting for insects in the tree canopy.
A brightly colored male Pachycephala perched high above the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistler - any bird of the genus Pachycephala, known for their whistling calls

Etymology

From Greek *pakhys* (thick) + *kephalē* (head), referring to the birds' distinctive thick heads.
