Titan

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.tən / (BrE)
the largest moon of Saturn, known for its thick, hazy nitrogen atmosphere
largest moon of Saturn

Examples

The Cassini spacecraft sent back detailed images of Titan during its mission.
Scientists believe Titan's atmosphere may hold clues to the origins of life.
A thick layer of clouds covers the surface of Titan, making it mysterious and intriguing.

Etymology

Named after the Titans of Greek mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.tən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, any of the primordial giant gods who ruled the world before being overthrown by Zeus; the Titans were the children of Uranus (Sky) and Gaea (Earth).
giant gods of Greek mythology

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed that Cronus was the most powerful Titan.
Prometheus, a Titan, was said to have given fire to humanity.
Zeus led the gods in a great war against the Titans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength in mythology or folklore
  • Antonym: Olympian - one of the twelve principal gods who resided on Mount Olympus in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek *Titanes* (Τιτᾶνες), the name of the primordial gods.
