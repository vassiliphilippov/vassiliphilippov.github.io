Persea

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈsiːə / (AmE), / pəˈsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs in the laurel family, including the avocado.
genus of trees including the avocado

Examples

The Persea genus includes several species of trees native to Central and South America.
Scientists are studying the growth patterns of Persea trees across different climates.
The most well-known member of the Persea genus is the avocado tree, Persea americana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocado - a pear-shaped fruit with a rough leathery skin, smooth oily edible flesh, and a large stone; or the tree producing it

Etymology

From Latin 'Persea', from Greek 'persea', an ancient name for a tree of Egypt.

2. NOUN

/ pɚˈsiːə / (AmE), / pəˈsiːə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs of the laurel family, including the avocado tree, native to warm regions of the Americas and Asia.
genus of trees including the avocado

Examples

The Persea genus includes several species of trees valued for their fruit and timber.
Avocado, scientifically classified as Persea americana, is cultivated worldwide for its nutritious fruit.
Botanists studied the distribution and ecology of flora within the Persea genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocado - a fruit-bearing tree, Persea americana, of the genus Persea

Etymology

From Latin Persea, from Greek persea (a tree in Egypt identified with the laurel tree).
