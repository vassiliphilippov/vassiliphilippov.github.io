Psychotria

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑːtriə / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical, chiefly South American shrubs and trees, some of which have medicinal or ornamental uses
genus of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

Many species of Psychotria are found in the rainforests of South America.
The Psychotria genus includes both shrubs and small trees with glossy leaves.
Researchers discovered new medicinal compounds in a type of Psychotria.

Etymology

From Greek psukhē (life, soul) and -tria (related to caring), used in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɒtriə / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs and small trees, chiefly found in South America, some species of which are used in traditional medicines.
tropical genus of shrubs and trees

Examples

The Psychotria genus includes over 1,800 species of tropical plants.
Researchers discovered a new species of Psychotria in the Amazon rainforest.
Some Psychotria plants are known for their strikingly colorful flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and '-trion' (a kind of plant).
