sea

1. NOUN

/ siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
A large body of salt water, smaller than an ocean, that is partly or wholly enclosed by land.
large body of salt water partly enclosed by land

Examples

The Adriatic sea is known for its clear waters and beautiful coastline.
Fishermen set out early each morning to work on the open sea.
Many islands are connected by ferries that travel across the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean - the largest bodies of salt water covering most of the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Old English sæ, related to Dutch zee and German See.

2. NOUN

/ siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
a large quantity or vast amount of something, especially when it seems endless or limitless
a vast or limitless amount

Examples

There was a sea of faces in the stadium during the concert.
After the announcement, she was flooded with a sea of supportive messages.
He found himself lost in a sea of paperwork after his vacation.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
a state of the ocean or another large body of water characterized by large, turbulent waves and swells, especially as experienced by ships at sea
turbulent water with large waves

Examples

The ship struggled through the sea as giant waves crashed against its hull.
Fishermen often face rough seas during storms.
After the storm, the sea finally began to calm, making travel safer.

Synset Examples

heavy seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - state of water with little or no movement or waves
  • Degree Lower: surf - waves breaking on the shore, usually with turbulence

Etymology

From Old English sæ, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch zee, German See, and Gothic saiws.
