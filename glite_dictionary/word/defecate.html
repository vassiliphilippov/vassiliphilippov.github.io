to defecate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛfəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to discharge solid waste from the bowels through the anus; to have a bowel movement
to pass solid waste (bowel movement)

Examples

The doctor asked if he could defecate without discomfort.
Children are taught where it is appropriate to defecate.
Many animals will not defecate close to their living areas.

Synset Examples

The dog had made in the flower beds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evacuate - to discharge waste from the body
  • Antonym: to urinate - to discharge urine from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'defaecare', meaning 'to purify' or 'cleanse'.
