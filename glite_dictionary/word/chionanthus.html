Chionanthus

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɑːnænθəs / (AmE), / kaɪˈɒnænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs, commonly known as fringe trees, native to parts of North America and Asia.
genus of deciduous trees/shrubs (fringe trees)

Examples

The Chionanthus in our garden produces delicate white blossoms every spring.
Botanists have discovered a new species within the Chionanthus genus in Southeast Asia.
Many landscapers recommend planting Chionanthus for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Etymology

From Greek 'chion' (snow) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the tree's white, fringe-like blossoms.
