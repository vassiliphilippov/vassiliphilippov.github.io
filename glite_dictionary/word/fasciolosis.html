fasciolosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæʃioʊˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfæsiəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with the liver fluke Fasciola hepatica, often resulting in liver damage.
disease from liver fluke infestation
medical

Examples

Cattle affected by fasciolosis may show signs of liver damage and reduced productivity.
Human cases of fasciolosis are rare but can occur after eating contaminated water plants.
Veterinarians recommend preventive treatment for fasciolosis in areas where liver fluke is common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascioliasis - infestation with flukes of the genus Fasciola

Etymology

From New Latin Fasciola (a genus of flukes) + -osis (disease suffix).
