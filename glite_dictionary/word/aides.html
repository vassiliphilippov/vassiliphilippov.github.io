Aides

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈaɪdiːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the underworld; brother of Zeus and husband of Persephone.
Greek god of the underworld

Examples

In ancient myths, Aides ruled the realm of the dead with strict justice.
Artists often depicted Aides with a scepter, emphasizing his power over the underworld.
Unlike Zeus, Aides rarely left his shadowy kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - Greek god of the underworld (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ᾍδης (Haidēs), meaning 'the unseen one'.
