Alstonia scholaris

1. NOUN

/ ælˈstoʊni.ə skəˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ælˈstəʊni.ə skəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to eastern Asia and the Philippines, with large leathery leaves and small green-white flowers; its bark has been used medicinally.
evergreen tree of Asia and Philippines

Examples

The bark of Alstonia scholaris has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
Botanists identified the specimen as Alstonia scholaris during their survey in the forest.
Alstonia scholaris is often planted as an ornamental tree in tropical regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Alstonia, honoring botanist Charles Alston (1685–1760), and species name scholaris, referring to its use for school slates.
