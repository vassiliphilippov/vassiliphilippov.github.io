catalpa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtælpə / (AmE), / kəˈtælpə / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Catalpa, native to North America and East Asia, with large heart-shaped leaves, white flowers, and long, slender seed pods.
tree with large leaves and long pods

Examples

The catalpa in our yard produces long, slender pods every summer.
A row of catalpa trees lines the main street, their heart-shaped leaves providing dense shade.
In late spring, the catalpa is covered with clusters of white flowers.

Etymology

From Muscogee (Creek) *kutuhlpa* (head with wings), the Indigenous name for the tree.
