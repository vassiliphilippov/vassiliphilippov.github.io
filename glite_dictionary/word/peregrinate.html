to peregrinate

1. VERB

/ ˈper.ə.ɡrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.ɡrɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to travel or journey around, through, or over a place, especially on foot
travel or wander, especially on foot

Examples

The group decided to peregrinate across the countryside to explore its hidden beauty.
She loves to peregrinate through old city streets, discovering new corners and stories.
They had peregrinated much of the region before settling on their favorite village.

Synset Examples

peregrinate the bridge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to make a journey, typically over a distance
  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without a fixed course, aim, or goal

Etymology

From Latin peregrinari (to travel abroad), from peregrinus (foreigner, traveler).
