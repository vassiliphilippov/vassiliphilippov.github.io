toxicological

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɑːksɪkəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the scientific study of poisons and their effects on living organisms (toxicology)
relating to the science of poisons
medical

Examples

The scientists conducted toxicological tests to determine the effects of the chemical on fish.
A toxicological analysis revealed dangerous levels of contaminants in the sample.
She published a paper on the toxicological risks of exposure to heavy metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxicology - the scientific study of poisons and their effects
  • Degree Lower: toxic - containing or caused by a poisonous substance

Etymology

From 'toxicology' + '-ical'; 'toxicology' comes from French 'toxicologie', from Greek 'toxikon' (poison for arrows) + '-logia' (study).
