monogynic

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑdʒɪnɪk / (AmE), / məˈnɒdʒɪnɪk / (BrE)
having one principal wife or one female in a position of chief status (such as among animals or humans), while others such as concubines or subordinate females may be present.
having one principal wife or chief female

Examples

In this ant species, the colony is monogynic, with only one reproductive queen per nest.
The society was primarily monogynic, meaning a man had only one chief wife, though he might take concubines.
Certain traditional monarchies were monogynic, granting official status to just one queen among multiple women.

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (one) and 'gynē' (woman, wife).
