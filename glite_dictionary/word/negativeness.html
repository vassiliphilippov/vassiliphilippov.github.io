negativeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being habitually skeptical, opposed, or resistant to suggestions or commands; a tendency towards negativity
habitual skepticism or resistance

Examples

Her constant negativeness made it difficult to agree on any plans with the team.
He tried to overcome his negativeness by focusing on the positive aspects of life.
The manager addressed the growing negativeness in the workplace with an encouraging speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negativity - the state of being negative or pessimistic
  • Antonym: positiveness - the state or quality of being positive or optimistic

Etymology

From 'negative' + '-ness', based on Latin 'negativus' (expressing denial).
