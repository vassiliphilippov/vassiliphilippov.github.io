megawatt

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌwɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəwɒt / (BrE)
a unit of power equal to one million watts, commonly used to express the output of power plants or the amount of electricity required by large consumers.
unit of power (one million watts)

Examples

The new solar farm will have a capacity of 500 megawatts.
A typical wind turbine can generate several megawatts of electricity.
The city consumes over one megawatt of power during peak hours.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilowatt - a unit of power equal to one thousand watts
  • Degree Higher: gigawatt - a unit of power equal to one billion watts

Etymology

From 'mega-' meaning 'million' and 'watt', named after James Watt, Scottish engineer.
