gunnery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnəri / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnəri / (BrE)
guns collectively, especially as used in military operations or on a ship.
guns collectively (esp. military)

Examples

The gunnery on the battleship was upgraded to increase its combat effectiveness.
During the demonstration, the sound of gunnery echoed across the valley.
Naval officers inspected the gunnery to ensure all weapons were ready for action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare

Etymology

From 'gunner' + '-y', dating from the mid-17th century.
