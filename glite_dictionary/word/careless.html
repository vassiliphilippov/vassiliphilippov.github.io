careless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.ləs / (BrE)
not giving enough attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors; not careful
not paying attention; not careful

Examples

His careless driving caused a minor accident on the way to work.
She made a careless mistake on the test, costing her valuable points.
You should not be careless with other people's feelings.

Synset Examples

careless about her clothes
forgotten by some careless person
a careless housekeeper
careless proofreading
it was a careless mistake
hurt by a careless remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: careful - giving close attention to avoiding harm or errors
  • Degree Higher: reckless - showing a lack of concern for danger or consequences
  • Synonym: negligent - failing to take proper care in doing something

Etymology

From Old English 'carleas' (free from care), from 'caru' (care) + 'leas' (less).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.ləs / (BrE)
(of a person or action, usually followed by 'of') not showing sufficient thought or concern; unconcerned about possible consequences.
not showing concern for consequences

Examples

It was terribly careless of him to forget the safety instructions.
She drove with a careless disregard for the icy roads.
He was careless of the risks, acting as if nothing could go wrong.

Synset Examples

careless of the consequences
crushing the blooms with regardless tread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - not showing consideration for others or consequences
  • Synonym: heedless - showing a reckless lack of care or attention
  • Antonym: careful - making sure to avoid potential problems or dangers; attentive

Etymology

From care + -less, dating from Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01197642-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɛrləs / (AmE), / ˈkeələs / (BrE)
Showing a natural ease or grace that appears effortless and unstudied.
effortless; unstudied in manner or style

Examples

Her careless laughter brought a relaxed charm to the dinner party.
He struck a careless pose, exuding effortless sophistication.
The painting captures the careless elegance of a dancer lost in motion.

Synset Examples

an impression of careless elegance
danced with careless grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effortless - achieved with little or no effort
  • Antonym: studied - deliberate or planned rather than spontaneous

Etymology

From Middle English 'carles', from 'care' + '-less'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
