bromoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.mɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.mɔɪl / (BrE)
a photographic print produced by the bromoil process, characterized by soft, paint-like qualities
photographic print with paint-like effect

Examples

The exhibition featured a rare bromoil by a renowned early twentieth-century photographer.
She admired the bromoil for its dreamy, painterly look unlike ordinary photographs.
Collectors often seek bromoil prints because of their unique artistic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromoil process - a photographic printing process using oil-based ink on bromide paper

Etymology

From 'bromide' (a type of photographic paper) and 'oil' (referring to the oil-based ink used in the process).
