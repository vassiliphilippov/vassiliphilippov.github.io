President Polk

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt poʊk / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt pəʊk / (BrE)
the 11th President of the United States (1795–1849), known for expanding U.S. territory through the Mexican-American War and annexing large parts of the Southwest
11th U.S. President, 1795–1849, expansionist

Examples

President Polk was instrumental in achieving the territorial expansion of the United States in the mid-19th century.
During his administration, President Polk oversaw the annexation of Texas and the acquisition of California.
Many historians debate the long-term effects of the policies enacted by President Polk.
