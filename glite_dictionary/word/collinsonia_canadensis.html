Collinsonia canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑlɪnˈsoʊniə kəˌnædɛnˈsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɪnˈsəʊnɪə kəˌnædɛnˈsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb of the mint family, native to eastern North America, with serrated pointed leaves and loose clusters of yellowish flowers.
an herbaceous plant native to eastern North America

Examples

Collinsonia canadensis is often found in moist woodlands throughout the eastern United States.
Herbalists value Collinsonia canadensis for its traditional medicinal uses.
The yellowish flowers of Collinsonia canadensis bloom in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoneroot - common name for Collinsonia canadensis

Etymology

Named after English botanist Peter Collinson and the Latin 'canadensis', meaning 'from Canada'.
