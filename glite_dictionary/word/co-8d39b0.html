Co

1. NOUN

/ koʊ / (AmE), / kəʊ / (BrE)
a hard, silver-white metallic element that is ferromagnetic and used in alloys and as a trace element in biology; chemical symbol: Co.
the metallic element cobalt

Examples

The magnetic properties of Co make it valuable in manufacturing strong alloys.
Vitamin B12 contains Co as an essential component for animal nutrition.
Scientists discovered a new application for Co in rechargeable batteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobalt - the chemical element with symbol Co

Etymology

From German 'Kobalt', from Middle High German 'kobolt' (goblin), originally a mythical being believed to cause trouble in mines.
