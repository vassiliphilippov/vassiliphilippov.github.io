alleviant

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːviənt / (AmE), / əˈliːviənt / (BrE)
A remedy that relieves pain or symptoms without providing a cure.
remedy that eases symptoms without curing
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an alleviant to manage her arthritis pain.
This ointment acts as an alleviant but will not cure the underlying infection.
Many over-the-counter drugs are used as alleviants for minor aches and pains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palliative - Relieving pain without dealing with the cause of the condition

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviare' (to lighten, relieve).
