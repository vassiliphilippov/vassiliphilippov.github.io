revitalizing

1. ADJ

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈvaɪtəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
making something strong, active, or healthy again; imparting new energy or life to something
making energetic or lively again

Examples

A revitalizing walk in the forest helped him clear his mind.
The new policy had a revitalizing effect on the local economy.
This herbal tea has a revitalizing aroma that lifts your mood.

Synset Examples

the renewing warmth of the sunshine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being
  • Synonym: invigorating - making one feel strong, healthy, and full of energy

Etymology

From re- (again) + vitalize (to give life), modeled after 'revitalize.'
