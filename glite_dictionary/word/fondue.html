fondue

1. NOUN

/ fɑnˈdu / (AmE), / fɒnˈdjuː / (BrE)
A dish in which small pieces of meat or seafood are cooked in hot oil at the table, then dipped into a variety of sauces.
meat or seafood dish cooked in hot oil

Examples

We enjoyed a traditional beef fondue with a selection of savory dipping sauces.
At the restaurant, each person cooked their own shrimp in the hot oil fondue.
The chef recommended pairing the seafood fondue with a crisp white wine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cheese fondue - dish of melted cheese served in a communal pot
  • Degree Lower: chocolate fondue - dish of melted chocolate for dipping fruit or cake

Etymology

From French 'fondue', feminine past participle of 'fondre' (to melt).

2. NOUN

/ fɑnˈdu / (AmE), / fɒnˈdjuː / (BrE)
A Swiss dish of hot melted cheese or chocolate served in a communal pot, into which pieces of bread or fruit are dipped.
Swiss dish of melted cheese or chocolate for dipping

Examples

After dinner, we shared a chocolate fondue with strawberries and bananas.
The restaurant specializes in traditional cheese fondue served with crusty bread.
Guests gathered around the table to enjoy a savory fondue on a cold winter night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raclette - Swiss dish of melted cheese, often served over potatoes or vegetables

Etymology

From French 'fondue', feminine past participle of 'fondre' (to melt).
