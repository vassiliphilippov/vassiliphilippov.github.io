blandly

1. ADV

/ ˈblændli / (AmE), / ˈblændli / (BrE)
In a manner that is unemotional, uninteresting, or lacking in strong characteristics.
unemotionally or uninterestingly

Examples

She responded blandly to the criticism, showing no emotion.
He blandly informed us that the meeting was canceled.
The news was delivered blandly, without any hint of excitement or concern.

Synset Examples

his blandly incompetent attempts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; mild

Etymology

From 'bland' + '-ly', after Latin 'blandus' (charming, mild).
