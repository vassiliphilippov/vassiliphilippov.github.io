Cyperus alternifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪˈpɪrəs ˌɔːlˌtɜrnəˈfoʊli.əs / (AmE), / ˌsɪpərəs ˌɔːltəˈnɪfəʊliˌʌs / (BrE)
an African sedge widely grown as an ornamental water plant, notable for its umbrellalike cluster of slender, grasslike leaves at the stem tips
African ornamental water plant with umbrellalike leaves

Examples

The Cyperus alternifolius thrives best in shallow water near garden ponds.
Many gardeners value Cyperus alternifolius for its unique umbrellalike foliage.
I added a Cyperus alternifolius to my conservatory for a touch of tropical greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella sedge - common name for plants of the Cyperus genus, especially Cyperus alternifolius

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Cyperus' and specific epithet 'alternifolius', meaning 'having alternate leaves'.
