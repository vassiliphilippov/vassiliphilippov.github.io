porkfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A species of fish (Anisotremus virginicus) in the grunt family, characterized by black and gold coloring, found from Bermuda to Brazil.
a black and gold grunt fish

Examples

The porkfish is often seen swimming in shallow reefs in the Caribbean.
Snorkelers admired the vibrant stripes of a nearby porkfish.
Marine biologists studied the behavior of the porkfish in its natural habitat.
