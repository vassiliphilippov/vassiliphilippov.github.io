Gentianopsis crinita

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈnɑp.sɪs krɪˈnaɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tɪ.əˈnɒp.sɪs krɪˈnaɪ.tə / (BrE)
A tall, widely distributed fringed gentian native to eastern North America, known for its violet-blue or white fringed flowers.
fringed gentian species of eastern North America

Examples

The Gentianopsis crinita flourished along the banks of the river, displaying its brilliant blue fringed blossoms.
In the wildflower guide, Gentianopsis crinita is listed as a native species of eastern North America.
Many nature enthusiasts look forward to seeing the rare Gentianopsis crinita in bloom each autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a common name for Gentianopsis crinita, a North American plant with fringed blue flowers

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Gentianopsis, from 'gentiana' (Gentian) + Greek 'opsis' (appearance); 'crinita' from Latin 'crinitus' (hairy, fringed), referring to the fringed flower petals.
