plenum

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈpliːnəm / (BrE)
an enclosed space or chamber in which the air pressure is higher than that outside; commonly used in ventilation and building systems.
enclosed air space with higher pressure

Examples

The air in the plenum is filtered before being distributed through the building.
Engineers inspected the plenum to ensure adequate air pressure was maintained.
A false ceiling can create a plenum for heating and cooling systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vacuum - a space devoid of matter or where pressure is lower than surrounding

Etymology

From Latin plēnum (neuter of plēnus, meaning 'full'), used in scientific English since the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈpliːnəm / (BrE)
A meeting of a legislative or other deliberative body in which all members are present.
meeting with all members present

Examples

All party members are expected to attend the plenum scheduled for Thursday afternoon.
Decisions made at the annual plenum are binding for the entire committee.
The bill was discussed in several subcommittees before reaching the plenum for a final vote.

Synset Examples

the plenum will vote on all tax increases

Etymology

From Latin 'plēnum' (full), neuter of 'plēnus' (full).
