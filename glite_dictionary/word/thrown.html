thrown

1. ADJ

/ θroʊn / (AmE), / θrəʊn / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) knocked or caused to fall to the ground, especially by being pushed, tackled, or forced down.
knocked to the ground

Examples

The thrown rider quickly got back on his horse after the accident.
Several thrown wrestlers lay on the mat after the intense match.
A thrown football player struggled to regain his footing on the field.

Synset Examples

the thrown rider got back on his horse
a thrown wrestler
a ball player thrown for a loss

Etymology

Past participle of 'throw', from Old English *thrawan* (to turn, twist).

2. ADJ

/ θroʊn / (AmE), / θrəʊn / (BrE)
(of filaments) twisted together, especially spun into thread or yarn.
twisted, spun filaments (textiles)

Examples

The finest scarves are made from thrown silk, giving them unmatched smoothness.
In traditional weaving, only thrown yarn is used for the warp threads.
He inspected the thrown filaments before weaving them into the fabric.

Synset Examples

thrown silk is raw silk that has been twisted and doubled into yarn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spun - made by twisting fibers together to form thread or yarn

Etymology

From the traditional textile process sense of 'throw', meaning to twist or spin (Middle English, from Old English 'thrawan', to twist).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
