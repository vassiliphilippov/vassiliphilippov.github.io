imprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prɪnt / (BrE)
A mark or impression made by pressure, especially one made by printing or pressing onto a surface.
mark made by pressure or printing

Examples

He found the imprint of a leaf in the soft clay.
The book's title page bore the imprint of the publisher.
A muddy imprint of a shoe was left on the carpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impression - a mark produced by pressing

Etymology

From Latin imprimere, meaning 'to press into or upon'.
