necessary

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛsəˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəsəri / (BrE)
Determined by prior circumstances and therefore unavoidable.
unavoidably determined by prior circumstances

Examples

The court ruled that these were the necessary results of the contract violations.
His choices led to the necessary conclusion that he would have to resign.
The chain of events produced a necessary outcome that could not be avoided.

Synset Examples

the necessary consequences of one's actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable
  • Antonym: contingent - dependent on something else that might or might not happen

Etymology

From Latin necessarius (unavoidable, indispensable), from necessitas (necessity).

WordNet ID: oewn-00344451-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɛsəˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəsəri / (BrE)
absolutely essential; required to be done, achieved, or present.
absolutely essential

Examples

It is necessary to have a passport if you want to travel abroad.
Clean water is necessary for good health and hygiene.
If it becomes necessary, I can stay late to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Antonym: optional - left to one's choice; not required or mandatory

Etymology

From Latin necessarius (unavoidable, indispensable), from necesse (necessary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01584458-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛsəˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəsəri / (BrE)
a thing that is indispensable or essential
something essential or indispensable

Examples

Please bring only the necessary for your trip; we have limited space.
Shelter and food are considered basic necessary for survival in harsh climates.
When packing for the expedition, she included all the necessary and nothing more.

Synset Examples

food and shelter are necessities of life
the essentials of the good life
allow farmers to buy their requirements under favorable conditions
a place where the requisites of water fuel and fodder can be obtained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - the fact of being necessary, or a necessary thing
  • Antonym: luxury - something non-essential or desirable but not necessary

Etymology

From Latin necessarius (unavoidable, indispensable), from necesse (necessary).
