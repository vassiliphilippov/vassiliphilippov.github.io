dusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌsti / (AmE), / ˈdʌsti / (BrE)
(of ideas, practices, etc.) lacking originality, freshness, or spontaneity; considered old-fashioned or stale.
old-fashioned; lacking freshness or originality

Examples

Many critics found the director's latest play dusty and uninspired.
His dusty approach to teaching bored the students every year.
The conference was filled with dusty debates that nobody wanted to revisit.

Synset Examples

moth-eaten theories about race
stale news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stale - no longer fresh or new; unoriginal
  • Antonym: fresh - new, original, or lively

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌsti / (AmE), / ˈdʌsti / (BrE)
covered with or full of dust; not clean or clear due to the presence of dust
covered with dust

Examples

He found a dusty old bicycle in the garage.
The furniture in the abandoned house was thickly dusty.
She sneezed as she opened the dusty attic trunk.

Synset Examples

a dusty pile of books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with an unclean substance
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains
