Hieracium venosum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɛəriəkiəm viːˈnoʊsəm / (AmE), / hɪəˈræsiəm vɪˈnəʊsəm / (BrE)
A species of hawkweed native to North America, characterized by a rosette of purple-veined basal leaves.
North American hawkweed with purple-veined leaves

Examples

The purple veins on the leaves make Hieracium venosum easy to identify in the wild.
Botanists discovered several patches of Hieracium venosum growing in the forest understory.
Gardeners in Canada sometimes encounter Hieracium venosum along woodland trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkweed - a common name for plants of the genus Hieracium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hieracium' (the hawkweed genus) and 'venosum' meaning 'veined'.
