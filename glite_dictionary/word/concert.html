concert

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnsɚt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsət / (BrE)
a performance of music by musicians or singers, usually given in public and not involving theatrical staging
musical performance by musicians or singers

Examples

We're going to a concert at the park this weekend.
The concert began with a lively orchestral piece that thrilled the audience.
Tickets for the band's concert sold out within minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recital - a musical performance given by one performer or a small group

Etymology

From Italian 'concerto', from Latin 'concertare' (to work together, agree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06905814-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kənˈsɝːt / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to arrange or plan (something) by mutual agreement with others; to act together in a coordinated way
to plan or arrange together

Examples

The two companies concerted a strategy to enter the new market together.
Several nations concerted their efforts in response to the crisis.
He concerted with his allies to issue a joint statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate - to arrange or organize something so that its parts work together effectively

Etymology

From Italian *concertare* (to bring into agreement), from Latin *concertare* (to contend, debate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01642545-v
Synset POS: v
