octagonal

1. ADJ

/ ɑkˈtæɡənl̩ / (AmE), / ɒkˈtæɡənl̩ / (BrE)
having eight sides and eight angles; shaped like an octagon.
having eight sides; octagon-shaped

Examples

The museum was built in an octagonal shape that stood out among the rectangular buildings.
They installed an octagonal window at the top of the staircase for added elegance.
An octagonal stop sign is a common sight on roadways around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hexagonal - having six sides and six angles
  • Degree Higher: decagonal - having ten sides and ten angles

Etymology

From Latin 'octagonum' ('octagon') plus '-al' (forming adjectives).
