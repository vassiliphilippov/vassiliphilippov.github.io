farmland

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːrˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmlænd / (BrE)
Land used for farming, especially for growing crops or raising animals.
land used for farming

Examples

After years of drought, much of the farmland lay barren and unused.
Suburban expansion is quickly replacing local farmland with new housing developments.
This region is known for its rich farmland and abundant crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arable land - land suitable for growing crops
  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass used for grazing animals

Etymology

From 'farm' + 'land', denoting land designated for farming purposes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmlænd / (BrE)
Land used for growing crops and raising agricultural produce.
land used for agriculture

Examples

The rolling hills are covered in rich farmland that supports the entire region.
Much of the local farmland has been converted into suburban housing developments.
Drought devastated the farmland, reducing crop yields for the second year in a row.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arable land - land suitable for growing crops

Etymology

From 'farm' + 'land', indicating land associated with farming.
