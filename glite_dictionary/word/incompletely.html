incompletely

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnkəmˈplitli / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpliːtli / (BrE)
To an extent that is not whole or finished; not fully or entirely.
not fully or entirely

Examples

The task was incompletely finished due to lack of time.
She answered the question incompletely, so the teacher asked for more details.
The form was submitted incompletely, missing several required sections.

Synset Examples

words incompletely understood
a form filled out incompletely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: completely - entirely; fully; to the greatest extent
  • Synonym: partially - in part; not wholly

Etymology

From the prefix 'in-' (not) + 'completely'.
