squarish

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskweərɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat square in shape or appearance; resembling a square but not exactly square
somewhat square in shape

Examples

The old table was squarish rather than perfectly square.
She wore a squarish hat that made her stand out in the crowd.
The building has a squarish footprint with rounded corners.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: square - having the shape of a square
  • Synonym: rectangular - having the shape of a rectangle

Etymology

Formed from 'square' + suffix '-ish' (meaning 'somewhat' or 'like').
