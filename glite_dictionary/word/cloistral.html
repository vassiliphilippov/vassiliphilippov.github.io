cloistral

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɔɪstrəl / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstrəl / (BrE)
relating to or suggestive of life in a cloister; secluded or shut away from the outside world, especially as part of a religious order
relating to life in a cloister; secluded

Examples

The nuns lived a cloistral existence, rarely leaving the convent grounds.
His cloistral habits kept him away from the distractions of city life.
The writer enjoyed the cloistral peace of his countryside retreat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastic - relating to monks, monasteries, or religious seclusion
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *cloistralis*, from *cloister* (enclosed religious place).
