brusque

1. ADJ

/ brʌsk / (AmE), / bruːsk / (BrE)
appearing or sounding rude and abrupt due to a very brief or blunt manner
abrupt and rude in manner or speech

Examples

Her brusque reply made it clear that she did not want to discuss the matter.
The manager’s brusque attitude upset several employees.
He answered the questions in a brusque tone, making the interview feel very uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

try to cultivate a less brusque manner
a curt reply
the salesgirl was very short with him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curt - rudely brief in speech or manner
  • Antonym: polite - showing good manners or respect in speech or behavior

Etymology

From French "brusque", from Italian "brusco" (sharp, sour).
