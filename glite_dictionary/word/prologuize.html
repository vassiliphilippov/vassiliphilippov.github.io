to prologuize

1. VERB

/ ˈproʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (BrE)
to write or speak a prologue; to introduce something with a prologue
write or speak a prologue

Examples

The playwright chose to prologuize before the first act, setting the scene for the audience.
She prologuized at length, explaining the historical context before her main argument.
Rather than letting the work speak for itself, the author insisted on prologuizing every chapter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epilogize - to write or deliver an epilogue

Etymology

Formed from 'prologue' + '-ize', after the pattern of similar verbs.
