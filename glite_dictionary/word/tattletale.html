tattletale

1. NOUN

/ ˈtætlˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈtætlteɪl / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who reveals secrets or reports others' wrongdoings to authority, often to cause trouble
someone who reports others' secrets or misdeeds
informal american

Examples

No one likes a tattletale who reports every little misstep to the teacher.
As a child, he was labeled a tattletale for telling on his classmates.
The siblings agreed not to be tattletales and kept each other's secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - a person who informs on someone, especially to the authorities
  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes secretive information or activity within an organization
  • Antonym: confidant - a person with whom one shares a secret or private matter, trusting them not to repeat it to others

Etymology

From 'tattle' (to gossip or speak indiscreetly) + 'tale' (story), American English origin, mid-19th century.
