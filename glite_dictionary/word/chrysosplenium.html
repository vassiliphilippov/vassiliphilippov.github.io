Chrysosplenium

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊˈspliːniəm / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsɒˈsplɛniəm / (BrE)
a genus of widely distributed semiaquatic herbs bearing small greenish-yellow, petal-less flowers
genus of semiaquatic herbs

Examples

Chrysosplenium species are often found near streams and moist woodland areas.
The delicate stems of Chrysosplenium make it a unique ground cover in shaded gardens.
Botanists studied the distribution of Chrysosplenium across different continents.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'splenion' (spleen), referring to the golden color and leaf shape.

2. NOUN

/ krɪˌsoʊˈsplɛniəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsɒˈsplɛniəm / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed semiaquatic herbs with small greenish-yellow, apetalous flowers, commonly known as golden-saxifrages.
genus of semiaquatic herbs; golden-saxifrages

Examples

Several species of Chrysosplenium thrive in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
The delicate, greenish-yellow flowers of Chrysosplenium can be seen throughout spring.
Botanists studied how Chrysosplenium adapts to flooded environments.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek ‘chrysos’ (gold) + ‘splēnion’ (spleenwort, a kind of fern), referring to the plant's color and leaf shape.
