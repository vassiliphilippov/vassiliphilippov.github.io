dolophine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊləˈfiːn haɪˌdrɑːkləˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌdəʊləˈfiːn hʌɪˌdrɒkləˌraɪd / (BrE)
A synthetic narcotic drug used as a substitute for morphine and heroin in the treatment of addiction, with a lower potential for causing dependence.
synthetic narcotic drug for addiction treatment
medical

Examples

The clinic prescribed dolophine hydrochloride as part of his recovery from opioid addiction.
Proper administration of dolophine hydrochloride can help reduce withdrawal symptoms in patients dependent on heroin.
Doctors carefully monitor patients' dosage when using dolophine hydrochloride during detoxification therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methadone - a synthetic opioid used for treatment of pain and opioid dependence

Etymology

Trade name Dolophine; formed from pain (Greek 'dolos') and -phine as in 'morphine'.
