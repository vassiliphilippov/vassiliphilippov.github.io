Citrus limon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs ˈliːmɒn / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs ˈliːmɒn / (BrE)
the small evergreen tree native to Asia, widely grown for its bright yellow, acidic fruit known as the lemon
lemon tree (botanical name)

Examples

Farmers in this region cultivate Citrus limon for its valuable fruit and fragrant blossoms.
The scientific name Citrus limon is often used in horticultural texts and plant catalogs.
Botanists distinguish Citrus limon from related species by its elliptical leaves and thorny branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon - the yellow acidic fruit produced by Citrus limon

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'citrus' (citron tree) and the botanical species designation 'limon' (lemon).
