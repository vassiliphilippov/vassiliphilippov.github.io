petrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtrəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrəl / (BrE)
A small, long-winged seabird with tube-shaped nostrils, known for flying far from land over the open ocean.
small oceanic seabird

Examples

The stormy petrel is often seen gliding effortlessly above the waves of the southern oceans.
Several species of petrel nest on remote islands far from any continent.
A single petrel can spend months at sea without coming to land.

Etymology

Probably from French 'petrel', diminutive of 'Pierre' (Peter), referring to St Peter walking on the water, as the birds appear to run along the water's surface.
