enigmatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnɪɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious.
mysterious; hard to understand

Examples

The artist is known for his enigmatic smile in the portrait.
She gave me an enigmatic answer that left me more confused than before.
The origins of the ancient ruins remain enigmatic to historians.

Synset Examples

I didn't grasp the meaning of that enigmatic comment until much later
prophetic texts so enigmatic that their meaning has been disputed for centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear

Etymology

From Greek ainigma (riddle) via Latin aenigmatus.
