sissiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪsɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being effeminate or lacking traditional masculine qualities, typically used in a derogatory sense
effeminacy; lack of traditional masculinity

Examples

He was often bullied at school because of his perceived sissiness.
The novel explores society's discomfort with any sign of sissiness in men.
Some people wrongly equate sensitivity with sissiness, which discourages emotional openness.

Synset Examples

the students associated science with masculinity and arts with effeminacy
Spartans accused Athenians of effeminateness
he was shocked by the softness of the atmosphere surrounding the young prince, arising from the superfluity of the femininity that guided him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effeminacy - the quality of being feminine in a way considered unmanly
  • Antonym: masculinity - possession of qualities traditionally associated with men

Etymology

From 'sissy' (a derogatory term for an effeminate boy or man) + '-ness'.
