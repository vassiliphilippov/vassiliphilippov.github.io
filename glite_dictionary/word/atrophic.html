atrophic

1. ADJ

/ əˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / əˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by atrophy (the wasting away or decrease in size of an organ, tissue, or body part).
characterized by atrophy; wasting away
medical

Examples

The patient developed atrophic muscles due to prolonged immobility.
A biopsy confirmed the presence of atrophic gastritis in the stomach lining.
Doctors are researching treatments for atrophic age-related macular degeneration.

Synset Examples

atrophic arthritis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertrophic - relating to or characterized by hypertrophy (excessive growth or enlargement of an organ or tissue)

Etymology

From Greek atrophia (lack of nourishment), from a- (without) + trophē (nourishment).
