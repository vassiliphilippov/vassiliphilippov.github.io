sclerosis

1. NOUN

/ skləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / sklɪəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A pathological hardening or thickening of tissues, often caused by inflammation or disease.
abnormal tissue hardening
medical

Examples

Multiple sclerosis is a chronic disease that affects the central nervous system.
Arteriosclerosis refers to the sclerosis of arterial walls, making them less flexible.
The physician noted signs of sclerosis in the patient's lung tissue during the examination.

Etymology

From Greek 'sklērōsis' (hardening), from 'sklērós' (hard).
