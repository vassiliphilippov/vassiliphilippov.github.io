digitalin

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪn / (BrE)
a powerful cardiac stimulant extracted from the foxglove plant, used especially to treat certain heart conditions
cardiac stimulant from foxglove
medical

Examples

The doctor administered digitalin to improve the patient's irregular heartbeat.
Laboratory tests confirmed the presence of digitalin in the blood sample.
Digitalin is derived from the dried leaves of the foxglove plant and was once widely used in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digoxin - a related cardiac drug obtained from foxglove
  • Synonym: digitalis - the foxglove plant or the drug extracted from it for heart treatment

Etymology

From Latin digitālis (foxglove), with the chemical suffix -in.
