whoredom

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrˌdəm / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.dəm / (BrE)
the practice or state of engaging in sexual activity for payment; prostitution
practice of prostitution
archaic

Examples

The preacher spoke out against the city's descent into whoredom and vice.
Medieval laws harshly punished those accused of whoredom.
In some ancient texts, whoredom is mentioned as a grave sin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitution - the practice of engaging in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

From Old English 'hordom' (adultery, prostitution), from 'hore' (whore) + '-dom' (domain, condition).
