discontent

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
a feeling of dissatisfaction with the present situation; a longing for something better
dissatisfaction; longing for improvement

Examples

There was growing discontent among the workers over the new company policies.
She voiced her discontent with the service at the restaurant.
Political discontent can lead to widespread protests and calls for change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissatisfaction - lack of satisfaction with something
  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

From Latin discontentus, from dis- (expressing reversal) + contentus (content).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
feeling or showing dissatisfaction with one's situation; not content
feeling dissatisfied or unhappy

Examples

Many employees felt discontent after the new policies were introduced.
Her discontent glance revealed how she felt about the decision.
He grew increasingly discontent with his tedious daily routine.

Synset Examples

saw many discontent faces in the room
was discontented with his position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: content - feeling satisfied or pleased; opposite of discontent
  • Synonym: dissatisfied - feeling an absence of satisfaction, similar to discontent

Etymology

From Middle French 'descontent', from Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'contentus' (satisfied).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00592317-a
Synset POS: a

3. VERB

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
to make someone dissatisfied or unhappy with their current situation
make dissatisfied

Examples

The unfair decision discontented many fans of the team.
Management worried that new policies would discontent experienced employees.
The changes in the schedule have discontented several students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissatisfy - to make someone not satisfied or pleased
  • Antonym: content - to make someone satisfied or pleased

Etymology

From Old French 'descontenter' (to displease), from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'contentus' (satisfied).

Synset POS: v
