holdfast

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌfɑːst / (BrE)
A device, structure, or part that firmly attaches or secures something in place.
something that secures or attaches

Examples

The seaweed uses a holdfast to cling to rocks in the tidal zone.
Engineers designed a special holdfast to secure the heavy machinery.
Each holdfast ensures the cables remain firmly in place during movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - device used to attach, secure, or close two or more objects

Etymology

From Middle English 'holde' (to hold) + 'fast' (secure).
