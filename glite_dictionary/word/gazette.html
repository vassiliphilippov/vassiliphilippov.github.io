gazette

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzɛt / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛt / (BrE)
an official newspaper or journal that publishes government, legal, or public notices and news
official newspaper or journal

Examples

The government publishes all new laws in the official gazette.
She read about the announcement in the weekly gazette delivered to her door.
Several historical documents were first printed in a London gazette in the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newspaper - a printed publication with news, articles, and advertisements
  • Synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations

Etymology

From French 'gazette', originally Italian 'gazzetta' (a small coin, also the name of a Venetian newspaper).

2. VERB

/ ɡəˈzɛt / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛt / (BrE)
to publish (an official notice or announcement) in a gazette or official journal.
publish in an official gazette

Examples

The new regulations will be gazetted before coming into effect.
His promotion was gazetted in the official government record.
The council gazettes all new bye-laws for public information.

Etymology

From French 'gazette', ultimately from Italian 'gazzetta' (a Venetian coin, also a news sheet).
