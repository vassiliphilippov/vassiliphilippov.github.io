repellent

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to keep away insects or other animals
chemical that repels animals or insects

Examples

This mosquito repellent is very effective during the summer months.
She sprayed repellent on her clothes before hiking in the forest.
The store sells natural repellent for pets to keep away fleas and ticks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insecticide - a substance used to kill insects

Etymology

From Latin repellens, present participle of repellere (to repel).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛl.ənt / (BrE)
A substance used to treat materials, especially fabrics, so that they resist water or other liquids.
water- or liquid-resistant substance for fabrics

Examples

The manufacturer applied a repellent to the jacket to keep it dry during rain.
She searched for a repellent to protect her couch from accidental spills.
Most modern tents are coated with a special repellent to prevent water leakage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproofing - the act or process of making something waterproof

Etymology

From Latin repellēns, present participle of repellere (to drive back).
