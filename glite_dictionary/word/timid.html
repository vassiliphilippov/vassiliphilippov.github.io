timid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmɪd / (BrE)
lacking self-confidence or courage; easily frightened or hesitant in social situations or when taking risks.
lacking self-confidence; shy

Examples

The timid child hid behind his mother when meeting new people.
She gave a timid smile when complimented on her work.
Despite his timid nature, he agreed to speak at the event.

Synset Examples

stood in the doorway diffident and abashed
problems that call for bold not timid responses
a very unsure young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or uncomfortable in the presence of other people
  • Antonym: confident - having strong belief or assurance in oneself

Etymology

From Latin timidus (fearful), from timere (to fear).

/ ˈtɪmɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmɪd / (BrE)
