whoosh

1. VERB

/ wuʃ / (AmE), / wuːʃ / (BrE)
to move very quickly making a rushing or whistling sound
move quickly with a rushing sound

Examples

The bullet whooshed past his ear, leaving him momentarily stunned.
A flock of birds whooshed overhead as we walked through the park.
She whooshed down the slide, laughing all the way to the bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Synonym: zoom - to move very quickly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02072942-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ wuʃ / (AmE), / wuːʃ / (BrE)
a rushing sound made by a sudden movement of air, liquid, or another fluid
rushing sound of air or liquid

Examples

The loud whoosh of the passing train startled the children on the platform.
I heard a whoosh as the rocket shot into the sky.
There was a sudden whoosh when the air conditioning turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swish - a soft rushing or swirling sound
  • Degree Lower: hiss - a sharp sibilant sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07411130-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ wuːʃ / (AmE), / wuːʃ / (BrE)
to move, gush, or squirt out suddenly and rapidly, often making a rushing sound
move or gush with a rushing sound

Examples

The water whooshed out of the broken pipe, flooding the kitchen floor.
When the subway arrived, a blast of air whooshed past the waiting passengers.
He accidentally loosened the valve, and steam whooshed out with a loud hiss.

Synset Examples

Oil whooshed up when the drill hit the well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move quickly with force or speed
  • Synonym: squirt - to eject liquid in a thin, fast stream
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ wuːʃ / (AmE), / wuːʃ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move very quickly with a rushing, hissing, or sibilant sound
move rapidly with a rushing sound

Examples

The train whooshed past the platform, startling the passengers.
She whooshed the curtains open to let in the morning sunlight.
A gust of wind whooshed through the open window, scattering papers everywhere.

Synset Examples

He whooshed the doors open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move quickly with speed or urgency
  • Synonym: zoom - to move very quickly
