scissortail

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsərˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɪsəteɪl / (BrE)
a slender gray flycatcher native to the southwestern United States, Mexico, and Central America, distinguished by its long, deeply forked tail and white breast with salmon and scarlet markings
a long-tailed gray flycatcher bird

Examples

The scissortail is easily recognized by its extremely long, forked tail feathers.
Bird watchers travel to Texas every spring to catch a glimpse of the scissortail.
A beautiful scissortail perched on the fence, its salmon markings glowing in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scissor-tailed flycatcher - another name for the scissortail, especially Tyrannus forficatus

Etymology

From 'scissor' + 'tail', referring to the bird's forked tail resembling the blades of a scissor.
