lustrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstrəm / (AmE), / ˈlʌstrəm / (BrE)
A ceremonial purification of the Roman population held every five years after the census.
Roman five-year purification ceremony

Examples

Every lustrum, Roman officials would conduct a purification ceremony after completing the census.
The lustrum involved sacrifices and prayers to cleanse the city's people.
Historians study the significance of the lustrum in Roman religious life.

Etymology

From Latin lustrum (purification)

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstrəm / (AmE), / ˈlʌstrəm / (BrE)
A period of five years, especially in reference to Roman times or formal settings.
a period of five years

Examples

The empire celebrated its second lustrum since the reign of the new emperor.
Academic reviews were conducted at the end of each lustrum.
Several major reforms were introduced over the past lustrum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinquennium - a period of five years

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrum' (a period of five years, originally a purificatory sacrifice held by the Romans every five years).
