cannular

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.jʊ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
shaped like a tube or consisting of hollow tubes, especially for the passage of fluids
tube-shaped; hollow like a tube

Examples

The device features a cannular structure to facilitate efficient fluid transfer.
Researchers observed that the plant's cannular leaves helped store water.
The artery was replaced with a cannular graft during the surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tubular - shaped like a tube or having the form of a tube

Etymology

From Latin 'cannula' (diminutive of 'canna', meaning 'reed' or 'tube').
