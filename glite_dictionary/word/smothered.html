smothered

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌðɚd / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðəd / (BrE)
completely covered with a substance or objects, often so as to be hidden or overwhelmed
completely covered, often by another substance

Examples

The pancakes were smothered in maple syrup and fresh berries.
The cottage was smothered with climbing roses in the springtime.
Her kindness left him feeling smothered rather than comforted.

Synset Examples

bonnets smothered with flowers
smothered chicken is chicken cooked in a seasoned gravy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covered - having something on all surfaces or sides

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌðɚd / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðəd / (BrE)
(of a sound, action, or feeling) restrained, suppressed, or prevented from being fully expressed or released.
restrained or suppressed

Examples

He let out a smothered laugh, trying not to disturb the others in the room.
She covered her mouth to hide a smothered yawn during the long meeting.
A smothered sob escaped her as she struggled to remain calm.

Synset Examples

a smothered cough
a stifled yawn
a strangled scream
suppressed laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifled - restrained, held back, or suppressed
  • Synonym: suppressed - prevented from being expressed or revealed
  • Antonym: unrestrained - not restrained or held back; free and uncontrolled

Synset POS: s
