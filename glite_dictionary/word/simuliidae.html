Simuliidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌmjuːliːˈɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌmjuːliːˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small biting flies including blackflies and sand flies, many of which are known for transmitting diseases to humans and animals
family of biting flies (blackflies, sand flies)

Examples

Outbreaks of Simuliidae can significantly impact livestock by causing stress and transmitting diseases.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Simuliidae species in the river ecosystem.
Bites from Simuliidae are not only painful but can also lead to allergic reactions in some individuals.

Etymology

From New Latin *Simuliidae*, based on the genus *Simulium*, from Latin *simulo* (to imitate).
