to peptize

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛpˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.taɪz / (BrE)
to disperse (a substance) in a liquid so as to form a colloidal solution or suspension
turn into a colloidal solution

Examples

The chemist attempted to peptize the sample using a dilute acid.
Certain salts can help peptize clay particles in water, creating a stable suspension.
To improve the paint's consistency, the technician needed to peptize the pigment thoroughly.

Etymology

From peptide + -ize, modeled on the creation of colloidal states in chemistry.
