lanky

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋki / (AmE), / ˈlæŋki / (BrE)
Tall and thin, with long, slender limbs; often suggesting awkwardness or a loose-limbed appearance.
tall and thin with long limbs

Examples

The lanky basketball player moved quickly across the court despite his height.
Her son grew into a lanky teenager almost overnight.
A lanky man with long arms reached for the top shelf with ease.

Synset Examples

a gangling teenager
a lanky kid transformed almost overnight into a handsome young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangly - tall and thin, with long awkward limbs
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built

Etymology

Probably from dialect lank (thin, lean), related to Old English *hlanc* (slender, thin).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋki / (AmE), / ˈlaŋki / (BrE)
(of a person) tall and thin, often with long limbs and an awkward appearance.
tall and thin (of a person)

Examples

The lanky teenager towered over his classmates in every group photo.
She recognized him immediately by his lanky frame and long stride across the court.
Despite his lanky build, he moved with surprising grace on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangly - tall and thin, with long arms and legs
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built

Etymology

Probably from lank + -y; 'lank' from Middle English, related to Old English hlanc (slender).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
