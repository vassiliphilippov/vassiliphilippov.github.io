bola

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.lə / (BrE)
a cord fastened around the neck with an ornamental clasp, worn as a necktie, especially in Western clothing.
type of necktie with a cord and clasp

Examples

He wore a bola with a silver clasp to the formal dinner.
A bola is a popular accessory in Southwestern cowboy attire.
The museum displayed several antique bolas from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolo tie - a necktie consisting of a cord with an ornamental clasp

Etymology

From Spanish 'bola' (ball), referring to the ornamental clasp often in the shape of a ball.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlə / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
A rope with weights attached to the ends, designed to be thrown to entangle the legs of animals, especially as used by South American gauchos.
hunting tool: weighted rope for entangling animals

Examples

The hunter skillfully threw the bola to capture the running ostrich.
In the museum, you can see a traditional bola used by Argentine gauchos.
The bola wrapped tightly around the horse’s legs, bringing it to a sudden stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolas - plural form of bola; sometimes used as a variant

Etymology

From Spanish 'bola' (ball), referring to the weighted ends.
