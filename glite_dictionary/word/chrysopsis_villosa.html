Chrysopsis villosa

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɑpsɪs vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / krɪˈsɒpsɪs vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant in the aster family, with hairy stems and yellow flower heads, commonly found in dry areas of North America.
a North American perennial plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Many hikers admire the bright yellow flowers of Chrysopsis villosa in the arid grasslands.
Chrysopsis villosa thrives in sandy soils throughout the western United States.
Botanists sometimes classify Chrysopsis villosa in a different genus based on its unique features.

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *opsis* (appearance), referring to the golden appearance of the flowers; *villosa* is Latin for 'hairy'.
