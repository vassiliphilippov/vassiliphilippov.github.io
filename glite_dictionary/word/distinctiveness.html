distinctiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋktɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clearly different from others; utter dissimilarity.
quality of being different

Examples

The artist's distinctiveness set her work apart from her contemporaries.
Brand distinctiveness is crucial in a crowded marketplace.
The distinctiveness of each region's cuisine makes traveling more interesting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being one of a kind

Etymology

From 'distinct' + '-iveness'. 'Distinct' from Latin 'distinctus', past participle of 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋktɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being clearly different from other things; a distinguishing characteristic or trait.
the quality of being different or unique

Examples

The distinctiveness of her voice made her instantly recognizable on the radio.
Each region tries to preserve the distinctiveness of its local culture.
Brand distinctiveness is crucial in a competitive market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being the only one of its kind
  • Antonym: ordinariness - the quality of being ordinary or not distinctive

Etymology

Formed from 'distinct' (from Latin distinctus, meaning 'separated, distinguished') + '-iveness'.
