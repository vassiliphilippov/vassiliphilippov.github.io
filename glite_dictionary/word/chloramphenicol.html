chloramphenicol

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːr æmˈfɛnɪˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˌklɔːr æmˈfiːnɪkɒl / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat serious bacterial infections, especially typhoid fever
antibiotic for serious infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed chloramphenicol to treat the patient's typhoid fever.
Due to potential side effects, chloramphenicol is only used when other antibiotics are unsuitable.
Researchers are studying how bacteria develop resistance to chloramphenicol.

Etymology

From 'chlor-' (chlorine), 'amphen' (from aminophenol), and '-icol' (suffix based on its chemical structure).
