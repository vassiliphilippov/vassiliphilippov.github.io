Falconidae

1. NOUN

/ fælˈkoʊnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / falˈkəʊnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Falconiformes that includes the falcons and caracaras, characterized by keen eyesight and rapid flight.
family of birds (falcons and caracaras)

Examples

The Falconidae family includes both the peregrine falcon and the caracara.
Scientists study the Falconidae to better understand predatory bird behavior.
Unlike hawks, members of the Falconidae often kill their prey with their beaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey from the family Falconidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Falconidae, from 'Falco' (genus name) + '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ fælˈkoʊnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / falˈkəʊnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds of prey that includes falcons and caracaras, classified under the order Falconiformes.
family of birds including falcons and caracaras

Examples

The Falconidae family is known for its impressive hunting abilities and keen eyesight.
Many members of the Falconidae are capable of extremely fast flight, especially during a dive.
Scientists study Falconidae to learn more about the evolution of raptors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey in the genus Falco, within Falconidae

Etymology

From New Latin Falconidae, based on the genus name Falco and the suffix -idae used for animal families.
