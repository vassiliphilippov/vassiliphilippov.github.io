phylum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A large group of languages that are historically related to each other.
a group of historically related languages

Examples

The Niger-Congo phylum includes hundreds of languages spoken across Africa.
Linguists debated the classification of languages within the Austronesian phylum.
Some propose that several language families in Papua New Guinea belong to the same phylum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: family - a smaller group of related languages within a phylum

Etymology

From New Latin, used for biological classification; from Greek 'phylon' (race, tribe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm / (BrE)
A principal taxonomic category in biology that ranks above class and below kingdom, grouping together related classes of organisms.
major biological taxonomic group

Examples

The animal kingdom is divided into many phyla based on differences in body structure.
Mollusks and arthropods belong to distinct phyla within the animal kingdom.
Biologists debated whether the new species belonged to an existing phylum or represented a new one.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: class - taxonomic rank below phylum and above order
  • Degree Higher: kingdom - taxonomic rank above phylum

Etymology

From Greek phylon (tribe, race, class), used in biological taxonomy since the 19th century.

Synset POS: n
