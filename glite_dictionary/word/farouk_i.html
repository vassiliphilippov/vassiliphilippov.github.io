Farouk I

1. PROPN

/ fəˈruːk / (AmE), / fəˈruːk / (BrE)
(1920–1965) The King of Egypt from 1936 until he was overthrown in a military coup in 1952.
King of Egypt (1936–1952)

Examples

King Farouk I reigned over Egypt during a time of great political change.
After the coup in 1952, Farouk I was exiled to Italy.
Historians often discuss Farouk I's role in the decline of the Egyptian monarchy.

Etymology

From Arabic فاروق (Fārūq), meaning 'one who distinguishes right from wrong'.
