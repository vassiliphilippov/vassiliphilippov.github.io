neurasthenic

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊrəsˈθɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəzˈθɛnɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from neurasthenia, characterized by physical and mental exhaustion due to emotional disturbance or stress
person with nervous exhaustion
medical archaic

Examples

After months of constant stress, he began to resemble a true neurasthenic, unable to concentrate or rest.
The protagonist in the novel is portrayed as a neurasthenic worn thin by urban life.
Doctors once described patients with chronic fatigue as neurasthenics in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurasthenia - a condition involving fatigue and nervous exhaustion

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'asthenēs' (weak).

relating to or suffering from neurasthenia, a condition marked by physical and mental exhaustion
relating to neurasthenia or nervous exhaustion
medical

Examples

After her illness, she exhibited neurasthenic symptoms such as fatigue and irritability.
The protagonist in the novel is portrayed as a neurasthenic intellectual, constantly plagued by anxiety.
Early 20th-century doctors often diagnosed patients with neurasthenic complaints when their symptoms could not be otherwise explained.
