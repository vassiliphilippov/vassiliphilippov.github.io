Karlfeldt

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑrl.fɛlt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːl.fɛlt / (BrE)
Erik Axel Karlfeldt (1864–1931), a Swedish poet known for works that incorporate Swedish customs and folklore.
Swedish poet (1864–1931)

Examples

Karlfeldt was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1931, shortly after his death.
Many of Karlfeldt's poems vividly reflect the landscapes and traditions of rural Sweden.
Scholars continue to study Karlfeldt for his influence on 20th-century Swedish literature.
