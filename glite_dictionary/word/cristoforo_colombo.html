Cristoforo Colombo

1. PROPN

/ krisˌtoʊfəroʊ koʊˈlʌmboʊ / (AmE), / krɪˌstɒfəroʊ kəˈlʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
The Italian name for Christopher Columbus (1451–1506), the Genoese navigator and explorer who completed four voyages across the Atlantic Ocean under the Spanish crown, opening the way for the widespread European exploration of the Americas.
Italian name for Christopher Columbus, explorer

Examples

The statue of Cristoforo Colombo stands proudly in the center of the piazza.
In Italy, Cristoforo Colombo is celebrated as a national hero.
The airport in Genoa is named after Cristoforo Colombo, honoring the famous explorer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christopher Columbus - Genoese navigator and explorer who discovered the Americas

Etymology

Italian form of the Latin 'Christophorus Columbus'; 'Cristoforo' from Greek 'Christophoros' (Christ-bearer); 'Colombo' meaning 'dove' in Italian.
