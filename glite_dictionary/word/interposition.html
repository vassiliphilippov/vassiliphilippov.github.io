interposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚ.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tə.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of placing something or someone between others, or the state of being so placed
the act of placing between

Examples

The interposition of a translator helped them communicate more effectively.
She objected to the interposition of unnecessary steps between the proposal and its approval.
He resented the constant interposition of outside parties in their private affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpose - to place or insert between one thing and another

Etymology

From Latin 'interpositio' (a placing between), from 'interponere' (to put between).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of inserting something, such as a remark or action, between others, especially so as to interrupt or intervene
the act of inserting or intervening

Examples

Her swift interposition prevented the argument from escalating into a heated quarrel.
The meeting was flowing smoothly until his interposition interrupted the discussion unexpectedly.
We welcomed the manager's interposition when the debate became too one-sided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interjection - an abrupt remark, especially as an interruption
  • Synonym: to intervene - to come between so as to prevent or alter a result or course of events

Etymology

From Latin 'interpositio' (a placing between), from 'interponere' (to put between).
