Cananga

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnæŋɡə / (AmE), / kəˈnaŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees native to Southeast Asia, including the ylang-ylang tree, valued for its fragrant flowers.
genus of tropical trees (includes ylang-ylang)

Examples

The essential oil of Cananga is prized in the perfume industry.
Several species within the Cananga genus are cultivated for their fragrant flowers.
Cananga trees thrive in the humid climates of Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ylang-ylang - the best-known species of Cananga, with fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Javanese 'kananga', referring to the ylang-ylang tree.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnæŋɡə / (AmE), / kəˈnaŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees native to Southeast Asia, best known for Cananga odorata (ylang-ylang), valued for its fragrant flowers.
genus of tropical trees (ylang-ylang family)

Examples

The essential oil is distilled from the flowers of Cananga trees.
Several species within the genus Cananga are important in horticulture.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Cananga in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Malay/Indonesian, the native name for the plant.
