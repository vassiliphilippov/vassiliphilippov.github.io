w

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl.juː / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
the twenty-third letter of the English alphabet
the letter 'w'

Examples

The word 'wonder' begins with the letter w.
He wrote a perfect cursive w on the board.
Please write your name, making sure your w is clear.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: v - the twenty-second letter of the English alphabet
  • Degree Higher: x - the twenty-fourth letter of the English alphabet

Etymology

Derived from Old English 'double u', reflecting its written form as a double 'u' or 'v'.
