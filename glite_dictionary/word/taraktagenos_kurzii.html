Taraktagenos kurzii

1. NOUN

/ təˌræktəˈdʒiːnəs ˈkɜːrziː / (AmE), / təˌræktəˈdʒiːnɒs ˈkɜːziː / (BrE)
An East Indian tree whose oily seeds are the source of chaulmoogra oil, historically used for treating leprosy.
tree yielding chaulmoogra oil

Examples

The seeds of Taraktagenos kurzii are pressed to obtain chaulmoogra oil used in medicinal preparations.
Researchers once relied on Taraktagenos kurzii as a primary source of treatment for leprosy.
Local farmers cultivate Taraktagenos kurzii for its valuable oil-producing seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaulmoogra oil - oil derived from the seeds of certain trees, used in leprosy treatment
