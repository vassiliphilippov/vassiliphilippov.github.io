flamefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪmˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A species of cardinalfish found in the tropical coastal waters of the Atlantic Ocean.
a cardinalfish of the tropical Atlantic

Examples

The bright colors of the flamefish make it a popular choice for saltwater aquariums.
Marine biologists recently observed several flamefish near the coral reefs of the Bahamas.
The flamefish is known for its vivid red and orange markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinalfish - a family of small, brightly colored marine fish
