Anhingidae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈhɪndʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / ænˈhɪndɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large aquatic birds known as snakebirds, native to warm regions and distinguished by their long necks and pointed bills.
family of aquatic birds (snakebirds)

Examples

The Anhingidae are often seen swimming with only their necks above water.
Birdwatchers spotted several members of the Anhingidae family in the wetlands.
The unique hunting technique of the Anhingidae makes them fascinating to observe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakebird - Common name for birds of the family Anhingidae
  • Synonym: anhinga - A member of the Anhingidae family; a type of snakebird

Etymology

From genus name Anhinga + Latin -idae, a suffix for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈhɪŋɡɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ænˈhɪŋɡɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large aquatic birds, known as snakebirds or darters, characterized by their long necks and found in warm regions.
family of aquatic birds (snakebirds)

Examples

The Anhingidae are often seen drying their wings on branches overhanging water.
Unlike cormorants, birds in the Anhingidae family have straight, pointed bills for spearing fish.
Ornithologists have observed all four species of Anhingidae across different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakebird - common name for birds of the family Anhingidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Anhinga, itself from Brazilian Portuguese for the anhinga bird.
