Apollo

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑloʊ / (AmE), / əˈpɒləʊ / (BrE)
an asteroid whose orbit around the Sun crosses the orbit of the Earth
class of Earth-crossing asteroid

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new Apollo that could pass close to Earth in the next decade.
The orbits of Apollo asteroids make them important objects to monitor for potential collisions.
An Apollo is classified by its semi-major axis being greater than Earth's and its path crossing Earth's orbit.

Etymology

Named after Apollo, a figure of Greek mythology, following astronomical naming conventions.

2. PROPN

/ əˈpɑːloʊ / (AmE), / əˈpɒləʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of light, prophecy, poetry, music, and healing; the son of Zeus and Leto, and twin brother of Artemis.
Greek god of light, arts, and prophecy

Examples

In ancient myths, Apollo was revered as the god of music and poetry.
The temple of Apollo at Delphi was famous for its oracle.
Many Renaissance artists depicted Apollo holding a lyre and surrounded by muses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Artemis - Greek goddess, twin sister of Apollo
  • Synonym: Zeus - the chief god in Greek mythology, father of Apollo
  • Synonym: Phoebus - another name for Apollo, often used in poetry

Etymology

From Greek Apollōn (Ἀπόλλων), name of the god in classical mythology.
