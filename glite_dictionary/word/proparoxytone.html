proparoxytone

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊˌpæɹ.əkˈsoʊn / (AmE), / ˌprəʊˌpær.əkˈsəʊn / (BrE)
A word in which the stress or acute accent falls on the antepenultimate (third-last) syllable.
word stressed on third-last syllable

Examples

In Spanish, the word pájaro is a classic example of a proparoxytone.
Many learners find it challenging to pronounce proparoxytone words accurately.
Linguists often analyze the frequency of proparoxytone forms in different languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paroxytone - word with stress on the penultimate syllable
  • Antonym: oxytone - word with stress on the final syllable

Etymology

From Greek proparoxýtonos, from pro- (before) + paroxýtonos (having the acute accent on the penultimate syllable).
