chlorophyll

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəfɪl / (BrE)
any of a group of green pigments found in plants, algae, and cyanobacteria, essential for photosynthesis by absorbing light energy
green pigment found in plants for photosynthesis

Examples

The leaves appeared bright green due to the presence of chlorophyll.
Photosynthesis relies on chlorophyll to capture sunlight and convert it into energy.
Without chlorophyll, plants would not be able to produce their own food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotenoid - yellow or orange pigments in plants; another type of photosynthetic pigment

Etymology

From Greek 'khlōros' (green) + 'phyllon' (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈklɔː.rə.fɪl / (BrE)
Any of a group of green pigments found in photosynthetic organisms, essential for the absorption of light to provide energy for photosynthesis.
green pigment in plants
medical

Examples

The leaves appear green because they contain chlorophyll.
During autumn, chlorophyll breaks down, revealing other pigments in the leaves.
Scientists studied how chlorophyll absorbs sunlight for photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotenoid - any of a class of yellow, orange, or red plant pigments
  • Antonym: hemoglobin - iron-containing protein in red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'khlōros' (green) + 'phullon' (leaf).
