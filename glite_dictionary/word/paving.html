paving

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of laying down paving materials, such as stone or asphalt, to make a surface suitable for walking or driving.
the act of laying paving materials

Examples

The city started paving the streets in early spring.
Extensive paving is required to create new parking lots.
After the paving was finished, the road was much smoother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavement - a hard surface laid down to form a road or path

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the hard covering laid on the surface of a road, path, or area
hard surface covering (road, path, etc.)

Examples

The paving on the main road was recently replaced with smooth asphalt.
Loose stones in the paving made it difficult to walk in high heels.
The city council allocated funds to repair the damaged paving downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavement - a hard surface layer, especially of a road or sidewalk

Etymology

From Late Middle English, from the verb 'pave', from Old French 'paver', from Latin 'pavire' (to beat, ram, tread down).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Material, such as stones, concrete, or asphalt, used to cover and make a hard, flat surface, especially for roads or paths.
material for covering roads or paths

Examples

They decided to replace the old gravel with new paving in the backyard.
The construction crew unloaded stacks of paving beside the road.
High-quality paving can significantly enhance the appearance of a public plaza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavement - a hard surface covering a road or path

Etymology

From the verb 'pave', from Old French 'paver', from Latin 'pavire' (to beat, ram, pave).
