to circumcise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝːkəmˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmsaɪz / (BrE)
to remove the prepuce or other tissue covering the clitoris, often as part of a traditional practice or for medical reasons
to cut away the clitoral prepuce or tissue
medical

Examples

Some cultures still circumcise girls as part of a coming-of-age ritual, despite international condemnation.
Doctors strongly advise against procedures that circumcise the clitoris due to health risks and ethical issues.
International campaigns aim to end the practice of circumcising girls worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: female genital mutilation - the partial or total removal of the external female genitalia for non-medical reasons
  • Antonym: uncircumcised - not having had the foreskin or prepuce removed

Etymology

From Latin circumcidere, from circum- (‘around’) + caedere (‘to cut’).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmsaɪz / (BrE)
to remove the foreskin from the penis, especially for religious or medical reasons
remove the foreskin (surgically)

Examples

In some cultures, it is traditional to circumcise newborn boys.
Doctors may circumcise a patient for medical or hygienic reasons.
They decided not to circumcise their son, preferring to leave the decision to him.

Synset Examples

During the bris, the baby boy is circumcised

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncircumcised - not having the foreskin removed

Etymology

From Latin circumcidere, from circum- (around) + caedere (to cut).
