sketch

1. NOUN

/ skɛtʃ / (AmE), / skɛtʃ / (BrE)
a short written account or description that gives the main features of a subject without detailed elaboration
short written account or description

Examples

The journalist wrote a compelling sketch of the famous artist's life.
Her memoir includes a moving sketch of her childhood in the countryside.
Each chapter begins with a brief sketch outlining the main historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - brief summary or general account of something

Etymology

From Dutch *schets* or Italian *schizzo*, meaning 'sketch, draft', from Latin *schedium* (improvised piece).

/ skɛtʃ / (AmE), / skɛtʃ / (BrE)

/ skɛtʃ / (AmE), / skɛtʃ / (BrE)

Examples

Her biography included a brief sketch of each important period in her life.

  • Synonym: outline - a general description or plan giving the essential features of something
