CD4

1. NOUN

/ si di fɔr / (AmE), / siː diː fɔː / (BrE)
a glycoprotein found mainly on the surface of helper T cells, acting as a receptor for HIV and involved in immune response regulation
glycoprotein on helper T cells
medical

Examples

The patient's CD4 count was monitored to assess immune function.
HIV targets cells that express CD4 on their surface.
A drop in CD4 levels is a sign of disease progression in AIDS patients.

Synset Examples

CD4 is a receptor for HIV in humans

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Cluster of Differentiation 4'; the numbering stems from immunology classification.
