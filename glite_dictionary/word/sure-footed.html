sure-footed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʃɝˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃʊəˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Confident and reliable in judgment, decision-making, or action; not likely to make mistakes.
confident and reliable in judgment or action

Examples

She gave a sure-footed response to the unexpected question during the interview.
As a leader, he remained sure-footed even in times of crisis.
The company made a sure-footed move that ensured its long-term success.

Synset Examples

most surefooted of the statesmen who dealt with the depression
demonstrates a surefooted storytelling talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • Antonym: uncertain - not able to rely on judgment, not sure or confident

Etymology

From 'sure' (certain) + 'footed' (having feet of a specified kind), originally referring to animals; extended figuratively.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʃʊrˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃʊəˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
able to walk or move steadily and confidently, especially on difficult ground; not likely to stumble or fall.
not likely to stumble; steady on one's feet

Examples

The goat was sure-footed as it climbed the rocky mountainside.
Thanks to her sure-footed steps, she crossed the icy path without slipping.
The horse is famous for being particularly sure-footed on rough trails.

Synset Examples

on surefooted donkeys

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or lacking coordination
  • Synonym: nimble - able to move quickly and easily

Etymology

Formed from 'sure' (certain, secure) + 'footed' (having feet), dating from the late 16th century.

Source: WordNet
