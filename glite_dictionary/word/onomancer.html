onomancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnəˌmænsər / (AmE), / ˈɒnəˌmansə / (BrE)
a person who practices onomancy, the art of divination based on the interpretation of names.
practitioner of name-based divination

Examples

As an experienced onomancer, she believed that names held the key to understanding one's destiny.
The village onomancer was often consulted before children received their names.
Historians have noted the influence of onomancers in ancient naming rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomancy - divination by interpreting names

Etymology

From Greek 'onoma' (name) + '-mancer' (diviner or prophet, from Greek 'manteia' meaning prophecy).
