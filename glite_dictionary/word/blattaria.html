Blattaria

1. NOUN

/ bləˈtɛriə / (AmE), / bləˈteəriə / (BrE)
An order of insects comprising the cockroaches; in some classifications, considered to include all cockroach species.
order of insects including cockroaches
medical

Examples

Modern classification places all cockroaches in the order Blattaria.
The Blattaria are studied extensively due to their adaptability and persistence.
Some taxonomists debate whether termites belong in Blattaria or should remain separate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common insect of the order Blattaria

Etymology

From Latin *blatta* (cockroach) + New Latin order ending -aria.

2. NOUN

/ blæˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / blæˈtɛəriə / (BrE)
A suborder of insects that includes cockroaches; in some classifications considered an order in itself.
suborder of insects: cockroaches

Examples

Scientists classify cockroaches in the suborder Blattaria.
Fossil evidence suggests that Blattaria have existed for over 300 million years.
Some taxonomists treat Blattaria as a separate order rather than a suborder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common insect of the order Blattodea

Etymology

From Latin 'blatta' (a kind of insect, cockroach).
