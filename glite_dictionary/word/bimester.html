bimester

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈmɛstər / (AmE), / baɪˈmɛstə / (BrE)
A period of two months.
a period of two months

Examples

Students receive grades at the end of each bimester.
The contract renews automatically every bimester unless canceled by either party.
The course is divided into three bimesters throughout the academic year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trimester - a period of three months
  • Degree Higher: semester - a period of six months

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'mensis' (month). Modeled after 'trimester' and 'semester'.
