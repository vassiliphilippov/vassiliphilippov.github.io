nondisposable

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.dɪsˈpoʊ.zə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪsˈpəʊ.zə.bəl / (BrE)
Not designed to be thrown away after use; intended to be reused.
intended for reuse, not to be thrown away

Examples

Many restaurants now offer nondisposable cutlery to reduce plastic waste.
She prefers to use nondisposable water bottles for environmental reasons.
The hospital encourages the use of nondisposable gowns whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disposable - designed to be used once and then thrown away

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'disposable' (designed to be thrown away).

WordNet ID: oewn-00780381-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ nɑn.dɪˈspoʊ.zə.bəl / (AmE), / nɒn.dɪˈspəʊ.zə.bəl / (BrE)
(of assets or resources) not available to be used, spent, or disposed of.
not available for use or spending

Examples

The project required the use of nondisposable assets, which could not be sold or liquidated.
Some funds remain nondisposable due to legal restrictions placed upon them.
You should separate nondisposable income from the money you can actually spend.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disposable - available to be used or spent

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'non-' (not) to 'disposable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00781720-a
Synset POS: a
