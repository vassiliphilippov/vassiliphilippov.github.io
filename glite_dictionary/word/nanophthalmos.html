nanophthalmos

1. NOUN

/ ˌnænoʊˈθælmoʊs / (AmE), / ˌnanɒfˈθalmɒs / (BrE)
A condition in which both eyes are abnormally small but otherwise structurally normal.
abnormally small but normal eyes (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with nanophthalmos after observing the unusually small size of both eyes.
Researchers are studying the genetic causes of nanophthalmos in rare eye disorder cases.
People with nanophthalmos can have normal vision but may be at risk for other eye complications.

Etymology

From Greek 'nano-' meaning 'dwarf' and 'ophthalmos' meaning 'eye'.
