verificatory

1. ADJ

/ vɛˈrɪfəˌkɛːtɔri / (AmE), / vəˈrɪfɪkətəri / (BrE)
serving to verify, support, or corroborate something as true or accurate
serving to verify or support

Examples

The scientist presented verificatory data to support her hypothesis.
Independent studies play a verificatory role in confirming research findings.
Before approving the new process, the engineer requested additional verificatory evidence.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verifying - making sure something is true or accurate

Etymology

From Latin 'verificare' (to make true) + English '-ory' (forming adjectives).
