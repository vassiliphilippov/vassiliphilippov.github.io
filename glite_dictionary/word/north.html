North

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ / (AmE), / nɔːθ / (BrE)
The northern states of the United States, especially those that remained in the Union during the American Civil War.
the northern U.S. states, esp. during the Civil War
american

Examples

During the Civil War, the North had a significant industrial advantage over the South.
She studied the history of the North and its role in abolishing slavery.
Many soldiers from the North fought to preserve the Union.

2. PROPN

/ nɔrθ / (AmE), / nɔːθ / (BrE)
the northern region of the United States, especially the states lying north of the Mason-Dixon line, often in contrast to the South
northern region of the United States

Examples

During the Civil War, the North and South had very different economies and cultures.
Many industrial cities developed in the North due to the presence of natural resources.
She moved from the North to the South for a change of climate and lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South - the southern region of the United States, especially in historical or cultural contrast to the North
