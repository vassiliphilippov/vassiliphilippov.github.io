north

1. ADV

/ nɔrθ / (AmE), / nɔːθ / (BrE)
toward or in the direction of the north
in a northern direction

Examples

We drove north for several hours until we reached the border.
During winter, birds often migrate north after spending time in warmer regions.
They decided to travel north to explore the mountains.

Synset Examples

they earn more up north
Let's go north!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: south - toward or in the direction of the south

2. NOUN

/ nɔrθ / (AmE), / nɔːθ / (BrE)
the northern part of a country, region, or city
northern part of an area

Examples

He grew up in the north of England, near the Scottish border.
Many industries are concentrated in the north of the city.
The north experiences harsher winters than the south.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: south - the southern part of a country, region, or city
  • Antonym: east - the eastern part of a country, region, or city
  • Antonym: west - the western part of a country, region, or city

Etymology

From Old English *north*, from Proto-Germanic *nurtha*, related to Old Norse *norðr*.

Examples

The hikers walked due north until they reached the river.
The compass always points to north.
They live in the north of the country, where winters are colder.

Related Terms
