adjuvant

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒəvənt / (AmE), / ˈadʒʊvənt / (BrE)
A substance added to a drug or vaccine to increase its effectiveness.
substance that boosts medical treatment
medical

Examples

The vaccine contained an adjuvant to stimulate a stronger immune response.
Doctors may use an adjuvant to enhance the effects of chemotherapy.
Researchers tested a new adjuvant in combination with standard treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additive - a substance added to something to improve or preserve it

Etymology

From Latin adjuvans, adjuvant-, present participle of adjuvare (to help).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædʒ.u.vənt / (AmE), / ˈadʒ.ʊ.vənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or process) enhancing the effect of a medical treatment, especially a drug or therapy.
enhancing a medical treatment
medical

Examples

Chemotherapy may be given as an adjuvant treatment to surgery for cancer.
Vaccines sometimes include adjuvant ingredients to improve immune response.
The doctor discussed the use of adjuvant therapies to increase treatment effectiveness.

Synset Examples

the adjuvant action of certain bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supportive - providing additional help or reinforcement, especially in medical contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'adjuvans', present participle of 'adjuvare' (to help).

giving added support or assistance

Examples
