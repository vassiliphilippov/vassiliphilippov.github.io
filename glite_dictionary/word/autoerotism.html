autoerotism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtoʊˈɛrəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈɛrətɪzəm / (BrE)
sexual arousal or gratification obtained by stimulating one's own body, rather than through a partner
sexual self-stimulation or self-arousal
medical

Examples

Some psychologists have studied autoerotism as a normal part of human sexuality.
The book discusses autoerotism in the context of adolescent development.
He read several articles about autoerotism and its role in sexual health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masturbation - the act of stimulating one's own genitals for sexual pleasure

Etymology

From auto- (self) and Greek erōtismos (sexual love), via French autoérotisme.
