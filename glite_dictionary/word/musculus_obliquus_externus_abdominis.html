musculus obliquus externus abdominis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjʊləs oʊˈbliːkwəs ɛkˈstɜrnəs æbˈdɒmɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs əʊˈbliːkwəs ɛkˈstɜːnəs æbˈdɒmɪnɪs / (BrE)
the outer diagonally arranged muscle of the abdomen on either side of the torso, involved in compression of the abdominal wall and rotation of the trunk
outer diagonal abdominal muscle
medical

Examples

The musculus obliquus externus abdominis plays an important role in twisting the torso during movement.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the musculus obliquus externus abdominis in great detail.
Strengthening the musculus obliquus externus abdominis can improve core stability and posture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: external oblique - outer abdominal muscle running diagonally on each side of the abs

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'obliquus externus' (external oblique), 'abdominis' (of the abdomen).
