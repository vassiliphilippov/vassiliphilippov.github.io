Linnaea

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈniːə / (AmE), / lɪˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants containing the twinflower (Linnaea borealis), known for its delicate, fragrant, paired pink flowers.
genus of flowering plant (twinflower)

Examples

The delicate blooms of the Linnaea are often found in cool forested areas.
Botanists value Linnaea for its historical significance and unique floral structure.
She photographed the rare Linnaea during her hike in the northern woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinflower - a small, creeping plant with paired pinkish flowers, Linnaea borealis

Etymology

Named after the Swedish botanist Carl Linnaeus (1707–1778).
