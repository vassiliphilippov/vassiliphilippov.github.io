unworthily

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈwɝːðəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːðɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks worth, merit, or dignity; in a way that is not deserving.
in an unworthy manner

Examples

He behaved unworthily of the trust that had been placed in him.
She felt that she had unworthily dismissed her friend's concerns without proper thought.
The knight was accused of acting unworthily in the face of danger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worthily - in a worthy manner; with merit or dignity

Etymology

From 'unworthy' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
