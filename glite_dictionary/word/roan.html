roan

1. ADJ

/ roʊn / (AmE), / rəʊn / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a horse) having a coat of a main color thickly sprinkled with white or gray hairs.
having a coat mixed with white or gray hairs

Examples

The farm owned a beautiful roan mare with a strikingly dappled coat.
Judges admired the roan horse for its unique coloring at the show.
He preferred roan cattle because of their distinctive appearance.

Synset Examples

a roan horse

Etymology

From Old French 'roan', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ roʊn / (AmE), / rəʊn / (BrE)
a soft, sheepskin leather colored and treated to look like morocco, used especially in bookbinding
soft sheepskin leather for bookbinding

Examples

The rare book was rebound in fine roan to preserve its original look.
Collectors often seek out volumes with original roan bindings.
The texture of roan makes it a preferred choice for high-quality book covers.

Etymology

Probably from Spanish 'raón' meaning 'rough, coarse' (leather); distinct from the color term 'roan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15039412-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ roʊn / (AmE), / rəʊn / (BrE)
a horse with a brownish coat thickly interspersed with white or gray hairs, giving a speckled appearance
horse with brownish, white-mixed coat

Examples

The rancher owned a beautiful roan that stood out from the rest of the herd.
She rode her roan into the ring, its speckled coat shimmering under the sunlight.
Judges admired the roan for its distinctive and attractive coloring.

Etymology

From Spanish *ruano*, based on French *rouan*, perhaps of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02377954-n
Synset POS: n
