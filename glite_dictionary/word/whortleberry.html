whortleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrtəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɜːtəlˌbəri / (BrE)
A small blue-black edible berry of various European heathland plants of the genus Vaccinium, resembling the blueberry but usually smaller and tarter.
blue-black edible berry (European)

Examples

We picked a basket of whortleberries while walking through the heath.
The pie was filled with freshly gathered whortleberries from the hillside.
Her grandmother used to make delicious whortleberry jam every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - North American shrub with small, sweet blue berries

Etymology

From Middle English 'whortelbery', from 'whortel' (wortle, a plant) + 'berry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhwɜr.təlˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈwɜː.tl̩ˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A small, erect European shrub of the heath family with solitary flowers and blue-black edible berries, similar to a blueberry.
European blueberry-like shrub and its berries

Examples

The woods were filled with ripe whortleberries in late summer.
She picked a handful of whortleberries to add to her pie.
Many birds feed on whortleberries during the harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilberry - a similar European shrub producing edible blue-black berries
  • Synonym: blueberry - a North American shrub with sweet blue berries

Etymology

From Middle English *whortelberie*, a combination of *whortle* (related to heath or myrtle) and *berry*.
