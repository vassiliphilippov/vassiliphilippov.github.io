Capital

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæpɪtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
a major work by Karl Marx published in 1867, in which he sets out his economic theories and analysis of capitalism.
Karl Marx's major economic work (1867)

Examples

Many scholars have studied Capital to understand Marx's critique of capitalism.
He borrowed a copy of Capital from the university library for his economics assignment.
Discussions about socialism often reference Marx's Capital and its influence on political thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Das Kapital - the original German title of Karl Marx's book
