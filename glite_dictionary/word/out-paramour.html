to out-paramour

1. VERB

/ aʊt ˈpæməˌrʊr / (AmE), / aʊt ˈpæməˌrʊə / (BrE)
to romantically outdo or surpass someone; to impress someone more than another in romantic matters
to surpass someone in romantic appeal

Examples

Determined to win her heart, he did his best to out-paramour every rival at the party.
She wondered if anyone could truly out-paramour the charismatic newcomer.
In countless romantic comedies, the protagonist tries to out-paramour the competition.

Synset Examples

They laughed as he tried to out-paramour the Turk effortlessly.
He managed to out-paramour the Turk with his charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outshine - to be more impressive or skillful than someone else
  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'to surpass') to 'paramour' (a lover).
