tipped

1. ADJ

/ tɪpt / (AmE), / tɪpt / (BrE)
sloping or leaning out of the true vertical or horizontal position; not upright or level
not upright or level; sloping

Examples

The old bookshelf stood tipped against the wall after the earthquake.
Several tipped gravestones dotted the ancient cemetery.
A tipped glass on the table caused the juice to spill everywhere.

Synset Examples

the leaning tower of Pisa
the headstones were tilted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilted - leaning or sloping, not level or upright
  • Antonym: straight - not leaning; true vertical or horizontal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01237400-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ tɪpt / (AmE), / tɪpt / (BrE)
having a tip, or having a tip as specified, often used in combination (e.g., 'rubber-tipped')
having a tip; e.g., 'rubber-tipped'

Examples

She prefers a tipped brush for precision when painting fine details.
The old man walked steadily with his rubber-tipped cane.
Many darts are tipped with metal to help them stick to the board.

Synset Examples

a rubber-tipped cane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untipped - not having a tip or specified tip
