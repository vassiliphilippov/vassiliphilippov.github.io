thorough

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈθʌrə / (BrE)
(informal, often pejorative) Used to emphasize the extent or intensity of a quality, especially something regarded as bad or undesirable.
used informally to intensify a negative quality
informal

Examples

He's a thorough nuisance who causes problems wherever he goes.
The report described the project as a thorough failure from start to finish.
I left the meeting feeling like a thorough fool for forgetting my notes.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈθʌrə / (BrE)
done carefully and completely, with attention to every detail; not superficial or partial
complete and detailed; not superficial

Examples

She completed a thorough investigation before making her decision.
After a thorough cleaning, the kitchen sparkled like new.
The doctor gave him a thorough examination to check for any hidden problems.

Synset Examples

an exhaustive study
made a thorough search
thoroughgoing research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaustive - including everything possible; thorough
  • Antonym: superficial - not thorough; concerned only with the obvious or apparent

Etymology

From Middle English 'thurgh', from Old English 'thurh' meaning 'through'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈθɜːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈθʌrə / (BrE)
careful and complete in every detail; painstakingly accurate and attentive
careful and complete; attentive to detail

Examples

She conducted a thorough review of the report before submitting it.
The cleaning crew did a thorough job, leaving every corner spotless.
He is known for being especially thorough in his research and preparation.

Synset Examples

our accountant is thorough
thorough research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors

Etymology

From Middle English 'thorow,' from Old English 'thurh' (through).
