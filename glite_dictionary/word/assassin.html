assassin

1. NOUN

/ əˈsæs.ɪn / (AmE), / əˈsæs.ɪn / (BrE)
a person who murders someone, especially a prominent figure, typically for political or religious reasons or for payment, and usually by a sudden or secret attack
a hired or secret murderer, often of famous people

Examples

The president was protected from a potential assassin by tight security.
The notorious assassin carried out the attack in broad daylight.
Authorities believe the assassin was hired to eliminate the rival leader.

Synset Examples

his assassins were hunted down like animals
assassinators of kings and emperors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who kills another person intentionally
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or killed as a result of a crime

Etymology

From Arabic ḥashshāshīn (hashish-eaters), used for a secret order of killers in the Middle Ages.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsæs.ɪn / (AmE), / əˈsas.ɪn / (BrE)
(historical) A member of a secret order of Muslims, founded in the 12th century, who terrorized and killed Christian Crusaders.
member of a secret medieval order of Muslim killers

Examples

The legend of the assassin order has fascinated historians for centuries.
During the Crusades, the feared assassins would strike suddenly and disappear without a trace.
Marco Polo wrote accounts of the secretive assassin sect in his travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin - a person who murders an important figure for political or religious reasons
  • Synonym: murderer - someone who kills another person intentionally

Etymology

From Arabic ḥashshāshīn (plural: ḥashshāshūn), referring to Nizari Isma'ili sect noted for political assassinations during the Crusades.
