lacerated

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæsəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlasəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
torn or cut in an irregular, jagged way, as if slashed or ripped
torn or jagged; irregularly cut

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's lacerated arm after the accident.
The hurricane left behind lacerated trees and twisted debris across the landscape.
She wore gloves to protect her hands from the lacerated edges of the broken glass.

Synset Examples

lacerate leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torn - ripped or pulled apart by force
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired; complete

Etymology

From Latin laceratus, past participle of lacerare (to tear, mangle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæsəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlasəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a wound or tissue) having edges that are torn or jagged, typically as a result of injury.
torn or jagged (of wounds or tissue)

Examples

The doctor cleaned the lacerated tissue before stitching the wound.
He suffered a lacerated arm after falling onto the broken glass.
The animal's lacerated ear slowly healed over several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torn - damaged by being pulled apart or ripped
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface or edge

Etymology

From Latin 'laceratus', past participle of 'lacerare' (to tear).
