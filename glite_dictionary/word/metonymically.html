metonymically

1. ADV

/ məˌtɑnɪˈmɪkli / (AmE), / mɛtəˌnɪmɪˈkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or relates to metonymy; by means of a word or phrase used in place of another with which it is closely associated.
in a manner relating to metonymy

Examples

The novelist often refers to the city metonymically, using its streets to represent its people.
The phrase 'the crown' is used metonymically to refer to the monarchy.
The author wrote metonymically, substituting concrete objects for abstract concepts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: metaphorically - in a manner relating to metaphor

Etymology

From 'metonymic' + '-ally'; ultimately from Greek 'metōnymía' (change of name).
