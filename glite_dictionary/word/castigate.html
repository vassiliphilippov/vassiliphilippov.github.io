to castigate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæstɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize or reprimand someone severely, especially for wrongdoing or mistakes
criticize or reprimand severely

Examples

The editor did not hesitate to castigate the officials responsible for the error in the report.
She was publicly castigated for her reckless comments on social media.
Instead of offering support, his boss chose to castigate him for missing the deadline.

Synset Examples

She chastised him for his insensitive remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chastise - to punish or criticize severely
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin castigatus, past participle of castigare (to chastise, correct).

2. VERB

/ ˈkæstəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæstɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize or punish someone severely
criticize or punish severely

Examples

The principal did not hesitate to castigate the student for his repeated misbehavior.
The editor publicly castigated the journalist for making factual errors in the article.
Some commentators castigate politicians for failing to address the climate crisis effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin castigatus, past participle of castigare (to chastise, correct).
