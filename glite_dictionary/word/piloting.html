piloting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlətɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of guiding or navigating ships or airplanes from one place to another
guidance or navigation of ships or airplanes

Examples

Proper piloting is crucial for ensuring the safety of an aircraft during takeoff and landing.
He spent years studying the art of piloting before becoming a harbor master.
Advances in technology have changed many aspects of piloting ships across busy waterways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the process or activity of accurately ascertaining one's position and planning and following a route

Etymology

From 'pilot' + '-ing', where 'pilot' comes from Middle French 'pilot' and Italian 'pilota', meaning helmsman.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlətɪŋ / (BrE)
the occupation or activity of operating and navigating an aircraft or ship as a pilot
the work of operating an aircraft or ship

Examples

He found piloting to be a challenging but rewarding career.
The art of piloting requires calm nerves and quick reflexes.
After years of piloting, she decided to become a flight instructor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - a person who operates the controls of an aircraft or ship

Etymology

From 'pilot' (person who operates aircraft or ships) + '-ing' (forming a noun indicating action or process).
