adrenergic

1. NOUN

/ ˌædrəˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌadrəˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
A drug that produces effects similar to those of epinephrine by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system.
drug mimicking effects of epinephrine
medical

Examples

The doctor administered an adrenergic to increase the patient's heart rate during surgery.
Some adrenergics are used to treat asthma by relaxing airway muscles.
Excess use of an adrenergic can lead to increased blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathomimetic - a drug that mimics the effects of the sympathetic nervous system

Etymology

From 'adrenaline' + '-ergic', meaning 'activated by adrenaline'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌædrəˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌadrəˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting nerve cells or receptors in which epinephrine (adrenaline) or similar substances act as a neurotransmitter or hormone.
relating to adrenaline (epinephrine) or its effects
medical

Examples

The sympathetic nervous system activates adrenergic receptors to prepare the body for action.
Certain medications block adrenergic responses to lower blood pressure.
Researchers studied how adrenergic stimulation affects heart function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cholinergic - relating to acetylcholine as a neurotransmitter or receptor

Etymology

From adrenal + -ergic (from Greek ergon 'work', meaning substance producing a physiological effect).
