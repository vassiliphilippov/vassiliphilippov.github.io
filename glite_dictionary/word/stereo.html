stereo

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛr.iˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstɪə.ri.əʊ / (BrE)
a sound system with two or more speakers that creates the effect of three-dimensional or spatial sound
sound system with spatial audio effect

Examples

She listened to her favorite song on the stereo in the living room.
The new stereo system offers excellent sound quality and rich bass.
He installed surround speakers to upgrade his stereo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mono - sound system with a single audio channel

Etymology

Short for stereophonic, from Greek 'stereos' (solid) and 'phōnē' (sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04323086-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛriˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəʊ / (BrE)
two photographs taken from slightly different angles that create a three-dimensional effect when viewed together
pair of photographs for 3D viewing

Examples

The museum displayed a fascinating stereo collection from the Victorian era.
If you look at the stereo through special glasses, the image appears three-dimensional.
He inherited a box of old stereos from his grandfather's photography collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereograph - pair of images producing a stereoscopic effect

Etymology

Short for 'stereograph' or 'stereoscopic photograph', from Greek 'stereos' (solid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04323413-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈstɛrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəʊ / (BrE)
(of sound or audio equipment) using two or more channels to reproduce depth or directionality of sound.
using multiple channels for sound

Examples

The concert was broadcast in stereo for a more immersive listening experience.
He bought a new set of stereo speakers for his home theater.
Some older recordings are only available in mono, not stereo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mono - using only one audio channel

Etymology

Short for stereophonic, from Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'phonic' (sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00242247-s
Synset POS: s
