forehanded

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɔrˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a racket stroke) made with the palm of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke, as in tennis or other racket sports.
racket stroke with palm facing forward

Examples

She practiced her forehanded swings for hours before the tournament.
A strong forehanded shot can put opponents on the defensive.
His forehanded return sailed over the net and landed inside the baseline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: backhanded - (of a racket stroke) made with the back of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke

Etymology

Formed from 'forehand' (the front of the hand) + '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ fɔrˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
having made provision for the future; prepared or prudent, especially in matters of finance or planning
prepared for the future

Examples

Her forehanded approach to saving ensured a comfortable retirement.
Farmers who are forehanded plant extra crops in case of a poor harvest.
He was praised for being forehanded and never leaving things to chance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with care and thought for the future
  • Antonym: improvident - not providing for the future; lacking foresight

Etymology

Formed from 'fore-' (before) and 'handed,' suggesting having matters in hand beforehand.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01901697-s
Synset POS: s
