macer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsə / (BrE)
an official who carries a ceremonial mace, especially in courts of law or during formal academic ceremonies
official who carries a ceremonial mace

Examples

During the graduation ceremony, the macer led the academic procession into the hall.
The judge entered the courtroom as the macer stood at attention with the mace.
In some Scottish courts, a macer signals the start of proceedings by striking the floor with the mace.

Etymology

From Old French *mace* (mace) + -er (agent suffix).
