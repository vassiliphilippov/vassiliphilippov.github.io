dismissal

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
official notice that someone has been removed from their job
official notice of being fired

Examples

After the dismissal, he struggled to find another job in the same field.
Her unfair dismissal led to a lengthy legal dispute with the company.
The company announced several dismissals due to restructuring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termination - ending of employment
  • Antonym: resignation - voluntary act of leaving a job

Etymology

From dismiss + -al, based on Latin dimittere (send away).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
the act of removing someone from their job or position; termination of employment
the act of ending someone's employment

Examples

The company announced the dismissal of several employees due to budget cuts.
She challenged her unfair dismissal in court and won compensation.
After his sudden dismissal, he quickly started searching for a new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termination - the act of ending something, especially employment
  • Antonym: hiring - the act of giving someone a job

Etymology

From the verb 'dismiss', from Latin 'dimittere' (to send away).

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
(Law) A judgment or ruling that disposes of a case without a trial.
legal judgment ending a case without trial
legal

Examples

The judge granted a dismissal because the evidence was insufficient.
Her lawyer filed a motion for immediate dismissal of the lawsuit.
A dismissal was issued before the case could go to trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From dismiss + -al, modeled on similar legal derivatives.

4. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
the act of giving someone permission to leave or sending someone away, especially from a place or gathering
permission to leave or sending away

Examples

The school bell rang, signaling dismissal for the students at the end of the day.
After the meeting, the chairman announced the dismissal of all attendees from the conference room.
The judge ordered the dismissal of the courtroom after the case was adjourned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - the act of letting someone go or allowing them to leave
  • Antonym: detention - the act of keeping someone in a place, not allowing them to leave

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dismissio, from Latin dimittere (to send away).
