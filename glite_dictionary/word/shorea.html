Shorea

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔːˈriːə / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of Indonesian and Malaysian trees belonging to the dipterocarp family, valued for their timber and resin
genus of resin-rich timber trees

Examples

Many species of Shorea provide valuable hardwood widely used in construction.
The rainforest is home to several impressive Shorea trees reaching up to 80 meters in height.
Researchers are studying the resin produced by Shorea for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

Named after Sir John Shore, British colonial administrator in India.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔriə / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Indonesian and Malaysian timber trees valued for their resin and wood.
genus of tropical timber trees

Examples

The forests of Malaysia are home to many species of Shorea.
Some varieties of Shorea produce valuable resin used in varnish.
Illegal logging of Shorea threatens biodiversity in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after Sir John Shore, a British colonial administrator in India.
