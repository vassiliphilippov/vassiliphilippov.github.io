fabulist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæbjəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfæbjəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who invents or narrates fables or stories, especially ones that are imaginative or untrue
person who tells or invents fables

Examples

The fabulist captivated the children with his magical stories about talking animals.
Many historians regard him as a fabulist rather than a true chronicler of events.
Although charming, the fabulist was not always trusted to tell the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storyteller - a person who tells stories
  • Degree Higher: liar - a person who tells lies

Etymology

From Latin 'fabulista' (storyteller), from 'fabula' (story, fable).
