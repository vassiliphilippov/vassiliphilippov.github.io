encephalogram

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɛfələˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɛfələɡram / (BrE)
a graphical record of the electrical activity of the brain, produced by an electroencephalograph
record of brain's electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor reviewed the encephalogram to assess the patient's brain function after the seizure.
An encephalogram can help in diagnosing epilepsy and other neurological disorders.
Researchers analyzed the encephalogram data collected during the sleep study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroencephalogram - a recording of electrical activity of the brain made by an electroencephalograph

Etymology

From Greek enkephalos (brain) + -gram (something written or recorded).

2. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɛfələˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɛfələɡræm / (BrE)
an X-ray image of the brain made by replacing spinal fluid with a gas to enhance contrast
X-ray of the brain using contrast gas
medical

Examples

The neurologist studied the patient's encephalogram to detect signs of abnormal brain activity.
An encephalogram was performed after the patient experienced repeated seizures.
Advancements in brain imaging have largely replaced the traditional encephalogram technique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroencephalogram - a record of brain activity produced by electroencephalography

Etymology

From Greek enkephalos (brain) + -gram (something written or recorded).
