Henry Cavendish

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈkævəndɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈkævəndɪʃ / (BrE)
(1731–1810) British chemist and physicist known for discovering that water is a compound of hydrogen and oxygen and for calculating the density of the Earth.
British chemist and physicist (1731–1810)

Examples

The scientist Henry Cavendish is famous for measuring the density of the Earth with great accuracy.
Many students learn about Henry Cavendish as the discoverer of hydrogen in their chemistry classes.
The achievements of Henry Cavendish have influenced generations of physicists and chemists.
