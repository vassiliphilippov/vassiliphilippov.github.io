thaumaturgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔməˌtɝdʒi / (AmE), / ˈθɔːməˌtɜːdʒi / (BrE)
the working of wonders or miracles, especially by supernatural or magical powers; the performance of seemingly magical feats
performance of miracles or magic

Examples

The ancient text described the thaumaturgy performed by enigmatic priests in dazzling detail.
His skillful illusions verged on thaumaturgy to the amazed spectators at the carnival.
People in olden times attributed thaumaturgy to those who could heal the sick with a touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magic - the use of supernatural powers to influence events
  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary event attributed to a supernatural cause

Etymology

From Greek thaumatourgia (miracle-working), from thauma (wonder) + ergon (work).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɔməˌtɝdʒi / (AmE), / ˈθɔːməˌtɜːdʒi / (BrE)
the performance of wonders or miracles, especially by supernatural means; the use of magic or miraculous powers
working of wonders or miracles by magic

Examples

The ancient texts describe the thaumaturgy performed by legendary wizards.
Many cultures have stories in which thaumaturgy is used to heal the sick or perform feats of wonder.
His interest in alchemy led him to study the secrets of thaumaturgy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magic - the use of supernatural powers to influence events
  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary and welcome event attributed to a supernatural cause
  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic, especially evil magic

Etymology

From Greek thaumaturgia, from thauma (wonder, miracle) + ergon (work).
