thwartwise

1. ADV

/ ˈθwɔrtˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈθwɔːtˌwaɪz / (BrE)
Extending or lying across; in a crosswise direction; at right angles to the long axis.
in a crosswise direction; at right angles
archaic

Examples

The beams were placed thwartwise to support the structure.
The artist stretched the canvas thwartwise along the wooden frame.
The road ran thwartwise to the valley, making travel more difficult.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosswise - in a direction across something
  • Antonym: lengthwise - along the length rather than across

Etymology

From 'thwart' (across, crosswise) + 'wise' (in the manner of).
