Clement VII

1. PROPN

/ ˈklɛm.ənt ˌsɛv.ənθ / (AmE), / ˈklɛm.ənt ˌsɛv.ənθ / (BrE)
the pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1523 to 1534, known for his conflict with Henry VIII over the king's divorce and subsequent marriage to Anne Boleyn.
pope from 1523 to 1534; opposed Henry VIII's divorce

Examples

During his reign, Clement VII faced the sack of Rome by Charles V's troops.
The refusal of Clement VII to annul Henry VIII's marriage led to England's split from Rome.
Historians often debate the political strategies of Clement VII during the turbulent Reformation period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Henry VIII - King of England who sought a divorce from Catherine of Aragon, leading to break with the Pope
