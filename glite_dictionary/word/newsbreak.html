newsbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuzˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌbreɪk / (BrE)
a brief interruption in regular programming or publication to deliver a short report on a current news story
brief news report or interruption

Examples

The newsbreak interrupted the regular programming to announce the election results.
During the movie, there was a sudden newsbreak reporting the severe weather alert.
I heard the tragic news from a newsbreak on the radio this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulletin - a short official statement or broadcast summary of news
  • Synonym: newsflash - a brief important news announcement, especially broadcast suddenly

Etymology

Formed from 'news' + 'break', originally from broadcasting terminology.
