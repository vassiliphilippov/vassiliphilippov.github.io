Kohleria

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlɪriə / (AmE), / kəʊˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs in the family Gesneriaceae, known for their colorful, tubular flowers.
genus of tropical American flowering shrubs

Examples

The Kohleria genus produces vibrant flowers that are prized in horticultural collections.
Several species of Kohleria thrive in shaded, humid environments in Central and South America.
Enthusiasts often hybridize Kohleria to create unique ornamental varieties.

Etymology

Named after Michael Kohler, a 19th-century Swiss botanist.
