to tighten

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to make something tight or tighter
make something tighter

Examples

She used a wrench to tighten the bolt.
You should tighten your seatbelt before the ride starts.
The coach told the team to tighten their defense in the second half.

Synset Examples

Tighten the wire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - to make something less tight

Etymology

From 'tight' + the verb-forming suffix '-en'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00421085-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to become tight or tighter; to make something more secure or less loose
to become tight or less loose

Examples

He pulled on the string and felt it tighten around the package.
As the weather grew colder, the skin on her hands began to tighten.
The rules will tighten if too many people break them.

Synset Examples

The rope tightened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - to make or become less tight; opposite of tighten

Etymology

From tight + -en (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00420903-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make something more strict or to narrow a range or limit; to increase restrictions or control
make stricter or more limited

Examples

The government plans to tighten immigration controls to reduce the number of arrivals.
After the scandal, the company decided to tighten its privacy policy.
New safety regulations will tighten procedures in the construction industry.

Synset Examples

reduce the influx of foreigners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less strict or less firmly controlled
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00233575-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make rules or controls more strict or severe; to limit the scope or extent of something more narrowly
make more strict or severe

Examples

The government plans to tighten regulations on air pollution.
After the incident, the school decided to tighten its security procedures.
The bank may tighten lending standards during an economic downturn.

Synset Examples

tighten the rules
stiffen the regulations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less strict or more relaxed (rules or controls)
  • Antonym: to relax - to make a rule or restriction less severe or strict

Etymology

From Old English 'tyhtan', related to 'tight', meaning to make tight or firm.
