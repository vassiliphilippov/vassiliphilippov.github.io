heedful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhid.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhiːd.fəl / (BrE)
paying careful attention; taking notice of something
paying careful attention

Examples

Parents should be heedful of their children's online activities.
She listened to his advice in a heedful manner.
Being heedful can prevent many unnecessary mistakes.

Synset Examples

careful of her feelings
heedful of his father's advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - cautious and attentive to possible dangers or errors
  • Antonym: heedless - showing a reckless lack of care or attention

Etymology

From Middle English 'hedeful', from 'heed' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhiːdfəl / (AmE), / ˈhiːdfʊl / (BrE)
paying careful attention to something; attentive or mindful
paying careful attention

Examples

He was heedful of the risks involved when making his decision.
Always be heedful of traffic when crossing the street.
The manager praised her for being heedful of the customers' concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attentive - paying close attention; alert
  • Antonym: heedless - not paying attention; careless

Etymology

From heed + -ful; 'heed' from Old English 'hēdan' (to observe, look after).

3. ADJ

/ ˈhid.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhiːd.fəl / (BrE)
paying careful attention to something; attentive and aware, especially of advice, warnings, or details.
attentive, paying careful attention

Examples

She remained heedful of the teacher's instructions throughout the experiment.
Travelers should be heedful of local customs to avoid offending residents.
He was heedful of every sign that something was amiss.

Synset Examples

heedful of the warnings
so heedful a writer
heedful of what they were doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attentive - paying close attention to something
  • Synonym: careful - taking pains to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Antonym: heedless - showing a reckless lack of care or attention

Etymology

From 'heed' (Old English 'hēdan', to observe, pay attention) + '-ful'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01198026-a
Synset POS: a
