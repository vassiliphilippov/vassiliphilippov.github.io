myrmecophagous

1. ADJ

/ mɜrˌmikˈɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / mɜːˌmiːˈkɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding on ants as its primary food source.
feeding on ants (zoology)

Examples

The myrmecophagous anteater has a long tongue adapted for consuming ants and termites.
Several lizard species exhibit myrmecophagous behavior in arid environments.
Scientists studied the myrmecophagous habits of the aardvark in the African savanna.

Synset Examples

myrmecophagous squirrel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insectivorous - feeding on insects

Etymology

From Greek myrmēx (ant) + -phagous (eating).
