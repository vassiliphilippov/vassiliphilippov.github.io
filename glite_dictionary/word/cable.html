cable

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a thick, insulated wire or group of wires for transmitting electrical power or signals
insulated wire for power or signals

Examples

The company installed a new cable to supply power to the building.
Please check whether the cable connecting the monitor is plugged in properly.
Fiber optic cable allows for high-speed internet connections.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wire - thin, flexible thread of metal used to conduct electricity

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a message sent by telegraph, especially one sent overseas; a telegram.
telegram sent abroad

Examples

She received a cable from her family while traveling in Europe.
In the early 20th century, international news was often transmitted by cable.
He sent a cable to London to inform them of the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegram - a message sent by telegraph

Etymology

From Latin 'capulum' (a cord), via French 'câble'; the meaning extended to telegraphic communications transmitted by undersea cables.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a thick, strong rope or wire made by twisting together strands of hemp, steel, or other material, used for supporting structures, towing, or transmitting mechanical force
a strong thick rope or wire

Examples

The old bridge was held together with thick cables running from tower to tower.
A steel cable was used to lift the heavy machinery into place.
The climbers secured the ropes to a metal cable anchored in the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rope - a length of strong cord made by twisting strands together
  • Degree Lower: wire - a thin, flexible thread of metal

Etymology

From Old French 'cable', from Latin 'capulum' (halter), originally from Greek 'kábos' (large rope).

6. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A television system in which programs are transmitted by signals sent through a wire directly to the subscriber's television receiver, rather than by broadcasting through the air.
television service transmitted via cable

Examples

We decided to cancel our cable service to save money.
Many sports channels are only available on cable rather than regular TV.
She spends her evenings watching old movies on cable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satellite TV - television service transmitted via satellite signal
  • Antonym: broadcast - television transmitted over the air via electromagnetic waves

7. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A unit of length used at sea, equal to one tenth of a nautical mile (about 185.2 meters or 202 yards).
nautical unit of length

Examples

The ship anchored half a cable from the shore to avoid hitting the rocks.
According to the charts, the harbor is two cables deep in that area.
He reported the vessel was drifting about one cable offshore after the storm.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'cable', from Latin 'capulum' (a rope, halter).
