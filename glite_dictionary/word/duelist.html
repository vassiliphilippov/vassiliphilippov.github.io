duelist

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːəlɪst, ˈdjuːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who engages in a formal fight or contest, typically using weapons, according to agreed rules, usually to settle a dispute or uphold honor
person who fights in a duel

Examples

The notorious duelist challenged anyone who questioned his honor.
In the nineteenth century, being a duelist could ruin a gentleman's reputation.
The skilled duelist never lost a sword fight in his lifetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duel - a formal fight between two people, usually with weapons

Etymology

From French 'duelliste', from 'duel' (a formal fight).
