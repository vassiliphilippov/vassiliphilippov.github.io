penetrable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.bəl / (BrE)
allowing penetration or passage through; able to be pierced or entered.
able to be penetrated or passed through

Examples

The fortress proved less penetrable than the attackers had hoped.
These materials are highly penetrable to water and require protective coatings.
She found his icy demeanor surprisingly penetrable after several earnest conversations.

Synset Examples

a penetrable wall
penetrable defenses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impregnable - unable to be penetrated or entered
  • Synonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through

Etymology

From Latin penetrabilis, from penetrare (to penetrate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.bəl / (BrE)
That can be entered, passed through, or pierced; able to be penetrated.
capable of being penetrated

Examples

The fortress was easily captured, as its penetrable walls offered little resistance.
Fog made the mountain pass barely penetrable to even the most determined hikers.
Some fabrics are designed to be penetrable to air for better breathability.

Synset Examples

penetrable defenses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impermeable - not allowing fluid or substances to pass through
  • Antonym: impenetrable - impossible to be penetrated or entered

Etymology

From Latin penetrare (to penetrate) + -able (capable of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02534722-s
Synset POS: s
