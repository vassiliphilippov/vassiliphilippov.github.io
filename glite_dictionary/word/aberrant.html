aberrant

1. ADJ

/ əˈbɛr.ənt / (AmE), / əˈbɛr.ənt / (BrE)
deviating from what is normal, usual, or acceptable, typically in an unwelcome way
deviating from normal or accepted standards

Examples

His aberrant behavior at the meeting surprised his colleagues.
The scientist was concerned about the aberrant results from the experiment.
Her aberrant choices set her apart from the rest of her family.

Synset Examples

aberrant behavior
deviant ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviant - departing from usual or accepted standards, especially in social or sexual behavior
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin aberrantem (wandering away), present participle of aberrāre, from ab- (away) + errāre (to wander)

2. NOUN

/ əˈbɛr.ənt / (AmE), / əˈbɛr.ənt / (BrE)
a person whose behavior significantly departs from what is considered normal or acceptable within a group
person deviating from group norms

Examples

The study focused on an aberrant who consistently challenged the school’s rules and traditions.
Some labeled him an aberrant for refusing to follow the community’s strict religious customs.
Fear of being seen as an aberrant kept many from expressing their true opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviant - a person who differs markedly from a group norm
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices

Etymology

From Latin aberrantem (wandering away, deviating).
