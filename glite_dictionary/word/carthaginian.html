Carthaginian

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrθəˈdʒɪniən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːθəˈdʒɪniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Carthage, a powerful city-state in North Africa
native or inhabitant of ancient Carthage

Examples

The Carthaginian army, led by Hannibal, famously crossed the Alps to invade Italy.
Archaeologists uncovered artifacts that belonged to an ancient Carthaginian.
The rivalry between the Romans and the Carthaginians shaped Mediterranean history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Punic - related to the people or language of Carthage

Etymology

From Carthage, the ancient city, plus English suffix -ian.
