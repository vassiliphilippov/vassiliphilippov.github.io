rectoplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.təˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.tə.plæsti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to reconstruct or repair the rectum or anus
reconstructive surgery of the rectum or anus
medical

Examples

The patient required a rectoplasty following severe trauma to the lower bowel.
Advances in rectoplasty have improved outcomes for children born with congenital rectal malformations.
After the rectoplasty, the surgeon monitored the patient closely for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anoplasty - surgical repair of the anus
  • Synonym: rectorrhaphy - suturing of the rectum

Etymology

From Latin 'rectum' (rectum) and Greek 'plastos' (molded, formed).
