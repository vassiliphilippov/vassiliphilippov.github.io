splashboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A panel or cover designed to protect people or surfaces from splashing water, mud, or similar substances.
protective panel against splashes

Examples

The old carriage still had its original splashboard to keep mud off the passengers.
She installed a splashboard behind the sink to protect the wall from water.
The truck's splashboard was dented after driving through muddy roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splashback - A panel to protect walls from water or food splashes, especially behind a sink or stove.

Etymology

From 'splash' + 'board,' meaning a board that prevents splashes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a protective plank or barrier along the side of a boat, designed to prevent water from splashing over the gunwale.
plank on boat to stop splashing

Examples

The fisherman repaired the damaged splashboard before setting out to sea again.
Waves hit the side of the boat, but the splashboard kept most of the water out.
The old sailboat’s splashboard was worn, letting spray onto the deck in rough weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunwale - the upper edge of the side of a boat

Etymology

Formed from 'splash' + 'board', referring to a board preventing splashes.
