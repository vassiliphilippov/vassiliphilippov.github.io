Microtus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrətəs / (BrE)
A genus of voles found throughout the Northern Hemisphere, including many common field voles and meadow voles.
a genus of voles in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The Microtus genus includes several species commonly known as field voles.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Microtus in alpine environments.
Many predators depend on Microtus as a primary food source in temperate ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + ous, otos (ear), referring to the animal's size and ear shape.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪkrəˌtəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪkrətəs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Cricetidae, comprising various species of voles found in the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of voles in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The field vole, a member of the genus Microtus, is common in European grasslands.
Scientists studied several Microtus species to understand differences in their habitats.
North American prairies are home to many rodents of the genus Microtus.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Microtus' (from Greek 'mikros' meaning 'small' and 'ous, otos' meaning 'ear').
