nightshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Solanum, many of which are poisonous, though some bear edible fruit such as tomatoes and potatoes
a plant of the Solanum genus, often poisonous

Examples

Some species of nightshade are highly toxic and should never be ingested.
The tomato is a common edible member of the nightshade family.
Gardeners often remove wild nightshade plants to protect pets and children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly nightshade - a poisonous plant also known as belladonna, Atropa belladonna
  • Synonym: solanum - the botanical genus including nightshade plants

Etymology

From Old English 'nihtscada', literally 'shade of night'.
