tokamak

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.kəˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈtɒ.kəˌmæk / (BrE)
A doughnut-shaped chamber used in nuclear fusion research, where plasma is heated and confined by magnetic fields.
fusion reactor chamber

Examples

Scientists are working on improving the efficiency of the tokamak to achieve sustainable nuclear fusion.
The plasma inside a tokamak must reach temperatures hotter than the Sun's core.
A new tokamak facility was inaugurated to advance fusion energy research.

Etymology

From Russian токамак (tokamak), an acronym for 'toroidalnaya kamera s magnitnymi katushkami' (toroidal chamber with magnetic coils).
