trews

1. NOUN

/ truːz / (AmE), / truːz / (BrE)
(plural noun) Tight-fitting tartan trousers, especially as part of Scottish Highland dress.
tight-fitting tartan trousers

Examples

He wore traditional Scottish trews at the wedding instead of a kilt.
The soldiers' trews were made from clan tartan cloth.
His father still has a pair of woolen trews from his days in the pipe band.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic *triubhas*, meaning trousers.
