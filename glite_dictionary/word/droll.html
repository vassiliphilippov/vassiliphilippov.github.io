droll

1. ADJ

/ droʊl / (AmE), / drəʊl / (BrE)
Amusing in an odd, whimsical, or unusual way.
amusingly odd or whimsical

Examples

The comedian's droll observations kept the audience laughing throughout the evening.
She told a droll story about her misadventures on her first day at work.
His droll sense of humor set him apart from the other teachers.

Synset Examples

a droll little man with a quiet tongue-in-cheek kind of humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - causing laughter or amusement
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or humorous

Etymology

From French *drolle* (imp, goblin), probably from Middle Dutch *drol* (goblin, jester).
