to write

1. VERB

/ raɪt / (AmE), / raɪt / (BrE)
to produce a literary work, such as a poem, story, or novel, typically by composing text
produce a literary work

Examples

She spent years trying to write her first novel.
Many poets write about love and nature in their works.
He plans to write a collection of short stories next year.

Synset Examples

She composed a poem
He wrote four novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compose - to create or write a literary or musical work

Etymology

Old English 'wrītan' (to score, write) of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01702148-v
Synset POS: v

8. VERB

/ raɪt / (AmE), / raɪt / (BrE)
To communicate or express thoughts, ideas, or information by putting words or symbols on paper or another surface using a pen, pencil, or keyboard.
communicate or express in writing

Examples

She loves to write letters to her friends all over the world.
He learned to write his name before starting school.
Many authors write about their personal experiences in their books.

Synset Examples

He wrote about his great love for his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type - to write using a typewriter or keyboard
  • Synonym: scribble - to write carelessly or hastily
  • Antonym: to erase - to remove writing

Etymology

Old English *wrītan* (to score, outline, draw the figure of), related to Old High German *rīzan* (to write), originally meaning 'to scratch, cut'.
