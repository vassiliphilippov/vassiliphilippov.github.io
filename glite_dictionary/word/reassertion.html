reassertion

1. NOUN

/ ˌri.əˈsɝː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈsɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stating or affirming something again, especially to reinforce or restore its importance or truth
the act of stating again

Examples

The leader’s reassertion of his commitment to reform was met with applause.
After the debate, there was a reassertion of the original policy.
Her reassertion of her position made her intentions perfectly clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief

Etymology

From re- (again) + assertion, based on assert (from Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere', to claim).
