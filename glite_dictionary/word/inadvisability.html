inadvisability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnədˌvaɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˌvaɪ.zəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being inadvisable; the fact that something is not recommended or is unwise
quality of being not advisable or unwise

Examples

The inadvisability of investing all your savings in one stock cannot be overstated.
He warned her about the inadvisability of traveling alone at night in that area.
There was much debate over the inadvisability of proceeding with the proposed plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inadvisable - not recommended; unwise
  • Antonym: advisability - the quality of being advisable or recommended

Etymology

From inadvisable + -ity, modeled on Latin-based noun-forming patterns.
