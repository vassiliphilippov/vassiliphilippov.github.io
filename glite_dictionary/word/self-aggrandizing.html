self-aggrandizing

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈɡrænˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.daɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in a way that makes oneself seem more important, powerful, or impressive than is actually the case
making oneself seem more important

Examples

Her self-aggrandizing remarks quickly annoyed everyone at the meeting.
The politician's self-aggrandizing speeches were dismissed as arrogant by his opponents.
He adopted a self-aggrandizing attitude, exaggerating his achievements during the interview.

Synset Examples

big talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boastful - showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'aggrandize' (to make greater), from French 'agrandiser' and Latin 'grandis' (great).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈɡrændəˌzaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌself əˈɡræn.dɪˌzaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Promoting oneself as being more important, powerful, or successful than is actually the case.
promoting oneself as important or powerful

Examples

His self-aggrandizing remarks made him unpopular among colleagues.
The politician's self-aggrandizing attitude was evident in every speech.
She avoided self-aggrandizing behavior, preferring to let her work speak for itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boastful - Showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction in one's achievements; synonym for self-aggrandizing
  • Antonym: humble - Having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance; antonym for self-aggrandizing

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'self-' and 'aggrandize', from French 'agrandir' (to make larger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02805676-a
Synset POS: a
