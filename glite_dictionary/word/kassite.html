Kassite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkasʌɪt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people who ruled Babylonia between about 1600 and 1200 BC
member of an ancient Babylonian dynasty

Examples

The Kassite dynasty brought stability to Babylonia after centuries of turmoil.
Archaeologists uncovered artifacts from the Kassite period in southern Iraq.
Many scholars have studied how the Kassite rulers influenced the region's culture and language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæsaɪt / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language formerly spoken by the Kassite people of Mesopotamia
ancient language of the Kassites

Examples

The Kassite language remains only partially deciphered by modern linguists.
Few written records exist in Kassite, making it difficult to reconstruct its grammar.
Scholars believe that Kassite was unrelated to other nearby Mesopotamian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elamite - an ancient language spoken in Elam (southwestern Iran)
  • Synonym: Akkadian - an ancient Semitic language of Mesopotamia

Etymology

From Kassite, the name of the ancient people who spoke the language.
