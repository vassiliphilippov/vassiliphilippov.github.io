Clichy-la-Garenne

1. PROPN

/ kliːˈʃi lɑ ɡəˈrɛn / (AmE), / kliːˈʃi lɑ ɡəˈrɛn / (BrE)
A northwestern suburb of Paris, historically notable as the residence of the Merovingian royalty in the 7th century.
northwestern suburb of Paris

Examples

The royal court moved to Clichy-la-Garenne during the Merovingian era.
He lives in Clichy-la-Garenne, just outside the bustle of Paris.
Many significant historical events took place in Clichy-la-Garenne in the 7th century.

Etymology

From French Clichy-la-Garenne, named after the original village and 'garenne' meaning a warren or preserve.
