bifacial

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈfeɪʃəl / (AmE), / baɪˈfeɪʃəl / (BrE)
having two faces or fronts; having two distinct surfaces or sides.
having two faces or sides

Examples

The ancient tool was bifacial, worked on both surfaces for a sharp edge.
Modern solar panels are sometimes bifacial, generating power from both sides.
In botany, some leaves are described as bifacial due to their two distinctly different surfaces.

Synset Examples

the Roman Janus is bifacial

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unifacial - having only one face or surface

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'facies' (face, surface).
