phacelia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːliə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːliə / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Phacelia, typically herbaceous and often bearing clusters of blue or purple flowers.
plant of the genus Phacelia

Examples

The gardener planted phacelia to attract bees and improve soil health.
Wild phacelia covered the slopes with vibrant purple flowers in spring.
Many farmers use phacelia as a cover crop during the off-season.

Etymology

From New Latin Phacelia, the genus name, from Greek phakelos (bundle), referring to the flower clusters.
