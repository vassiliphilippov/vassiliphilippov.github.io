parochialism

1. NOUN

/ pəˈroʊkiəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a narrow or limited outlook, especially focused on local or small concerns rather than general or global ones
limited or narrow outlook

Examples

The report criticizes the parochialism of the committee, which ignored the needs of the broader community.
Academic institutions strive to overcome parochialism by encouraging international collaboration.
Her parochialism prevented her from appreciating customs different from her own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provincialism - narrowness of outlook, especially associated with regions or provinces
  • Antonym: cosmopolitanism - being open to or familiar with different cultures; having a global outlook

Etymology

From 'parochial' (relating to a parish), from Late Latin 'parochialis'.
