rallentando

1. ADV

/ ˌrɑːlənˈtɑːndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌral.ɛnˈtan.dəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) with a gradual slowing of tempo; becoming slower.
gradually slowing in music

Examples

The pianist played the final bars rallentando, drawing out the emotional impact of the piece.
In the score, the composer marked a rallentando just before the ending.
The orchestra responded perfectly to the conductor's signal for rallentando in the closing measures.

Synset Examples

this passage should be played rallentando

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritardando - gradually slowing down in music

Etymology

From Italian 'rallentando' (slowing down), present participle of 'rallentare' (to slow down).

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɑːlənˈtɑːndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌral.ənˈtan.dəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) gradually decreasing in tempo.
gradually slowing down (music)

Examples

The conductor signaled the orchestra to play rallentando as they approached the final bars.
The piece ends with a rallentando, creating a gentle fade-out effect.
During rehearsal, the maestro emphasized the rallentando section to achieve the right mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritardando - gradually becoming slower (music)
  • Antonym: accelerando - gradually increasing in speed (music)

Etymology

From Italian 'rallentando', present participle of 'rallentare' (to slow down).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02549225-s
Synset POS: s
