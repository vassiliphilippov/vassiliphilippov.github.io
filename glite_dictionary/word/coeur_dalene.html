Coeur d'Alene

1. PROPN

/ kɔːr də ˈleɪn / (AmE), / kɜː dæl ˈeɪn / (BrE)
A town in the northern panhandle of Idaho, known as a popular resort area.
town in northern Idaho; resort area

Examples

They spent their summer vacation in Coeur d'Alene, enjoying the lakes and outdoor activities.
Coeur d'Alene attracts tourists year-round with its beautiful scenery and vibrant local culture.
Many artists are inspired by the natural beauty surrounding Coeur d'Alene.

Etymology

From French, meaning 'heart of an awl', named by French traders after the local indigenous people.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɜːr dəˈleɪn / (AmE), / kɜː dəˈleɪn / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people living primarily in northern Idaho near Coeur d'Alene Lake
a Native American people of northern Idaho

Examples

Members of the Coeur d'Alene have lived in Idaho for centuries.
The Coeur d'Alene are known for their strong cultural traditions.
A Coeur d'Alene elder spoke about their people's history with the land.

Etymology

From French 'coeur d' Alène' meaning 'heart of an awl', a name given by French traders.
