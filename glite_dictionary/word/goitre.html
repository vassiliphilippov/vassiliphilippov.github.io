goitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔɪtə / (BrE)
An abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland, often visible as a swelling at the front of the neck, typically resulting from hormonal imbalance or iodine deficiency.
enlarged thyroid gland
medical british

Examples

She was diagnosed with a goitre after noticing a swelling in her neck.
A deficiency of iodine in the diet is a common cause of goitre.
The surgeon recommended removing the goitre to ease her breathing difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goiter - abnormally enlarged thyroid gland; chiefly US spelling

Etymology

From French 'goitre', from Latin 'guttur' (throat).
