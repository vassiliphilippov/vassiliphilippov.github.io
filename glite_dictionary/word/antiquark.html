antiquark

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tiˌkwɑrk / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪˌkwɑːk / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that is the antiparticle counterpart of a quark, possessing the same mass but opposite electric charge and other properties.
antiparticle of a quark

Examples

In the experiment, scientists successfully detected an antiquark among the particles produced.
A proton consists of two up quarks and one down quark, but if you add an antiquark, you can form other particles.
The collision created a pair of particles: a quark and its corresponding antiquark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quark - a fundamental constituent of matter in particle physics

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against, opposite) + 'quark', a term coined by physicist Murray Gell-Mann.
