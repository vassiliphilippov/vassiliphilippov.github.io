tattoo

1. NOUN

/ təˈtu / (AmE), / tæˈtuː / (BrE)
The practice or process of marking the skin with a design by pricking it and inserting ink, dye, or pigments.
skin marking by inserting ink

Examples

The popularity of getting a tattoo has increased dramatically in recent years.
She went to a professional studio to learn the art of tattoo.
Cultural attitudes toward the practice of tattoo vary widely around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattoo - a permanent mark or design made on skin with ink

Etymology

From Samoan 'tatau', meaning 'to mark'.

2. NOUN

/ təˈtu / (AmE), / təˈtuː / (BrE)
A drumbeat or bugle call in the military that signals personnel to return to their quarters.
military signal for returning to quarters

Examples

The evening tattoo echoed across the base, signaling the day's end.
As the soldiers heard the tattoo, they gathered their things and returned to their barracks.
The traditional tattoo is still played at military ceremonies today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reveille - a bugle call that signals soldiers to wake up and assemble

Etymology

From Dutch 'taptoe', meaning 'close the (beer) tap', used as a military signal.

3. NOUN

/ təˈtu / (AmE), / tatˈuː / (BrE)
a permanent design or image made on the skin by inserting ink into it, typically as a form of body art
a permanent inked design on the skin

Examples

He got a tattoo of a dragon on his arm.
She admired the detailed tattoo on his shoulder.
Many people choose their first tattoo to mark a significant event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattooing - the process of making a tattoo

Etymology

From Samoan *tatau*, meaning 'to mark' or 'strike'.

/ təˈtuː / (AmE), / təˈtuː / (BrE)
