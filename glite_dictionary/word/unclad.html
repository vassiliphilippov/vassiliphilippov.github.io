unclad

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklæd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklad / (BrE)
not wearing any clothes; naked or bare.
not wearing clothes; naked

Examples

The statue was depicted as unclad, showing every detail of the human form.
After a quick swim in the lake, the children ran unclad across the grass.
The artist preferred to sketch his models unclad to capture the body's natural lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - not wearing any clothes
  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'clad' (dressed, covered), with 'clad' from Old English 'clædan' (to clothe).
