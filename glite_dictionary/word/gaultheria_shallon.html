Gaultheria shallon

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔlˈθɪəriə ʃəˈlɒn / (AmE), / ɡɔːlˈθɪəriə ʃəˈlɒn / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with edible dark purple berries resembling grapes
Pacific coast evergreen shrub with edible berries

Examples

Hikers often spot Gaultheria shallon growing along forest trails in the Pacific Northwest.
The dark berries of Gaultheria shallon are edible and often used in jams and jellies.
Native Americans have traditionally harvested Gaultheria shallon for its nutritious fruit.

Etymology

From genus name Gaultheria, honoring French physician Jean François Gaultier, and specific epithet 'shallon', of unclear origin.
