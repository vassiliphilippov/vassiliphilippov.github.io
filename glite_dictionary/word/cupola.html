cupola

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpələ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpələ / (BrE)
a vertical cylindrical furnace used for melting iron in foundries, typically for casting
vertical furnace for melting iron

Examples

The old foundry still uses a cupola to melt scrap iron for casting.
Melting iron in a cupola allows for continuous operation during production.
The factory replaced its aging cupola with a more efficient electric furnace.

Etymology

From Italian 'cupola' (dome), from Latin 'cupula,' diminutive of 'cupa' (cask, barrel); the furnace's shape resembles a dome or cask.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpələ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpələ / (BrE)
A small roof or ceiling structure in the shape of a dome, often placed on top of a larger roof or dome as an architectural feature.
dome-shaped roof structure

Examples

The old town hall is famous for its green copper cupola that rises above the skyline.
Sunlight streamed through the glass cupola at the top of the rotunda.
The architect designed a small cupola to crown the central hall of the mansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dome - a rounded vault forming the roof of a building or structure

Etymology

From Italian 'cupola', diminutive of 'cupa' (cup), from Latin 'cupa'.
