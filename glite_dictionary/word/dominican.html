Dominican

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnɪkən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of the Dominican Republic
native or inhabitant of the Dominican Republic

Examples

The Dominican greeted us with a warm smile as we arrived in Santo Domingo.
Many Dominicans celebrate their independence each year with colorful festivals.
As a Dominican, she is proud of her country's rich culture and history.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnɪkən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkən / (BrE)
A member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded by Saint Dominic, typically a friar wearing the order's distinctive black mantle.
member of a Roman Catholic religious order

Examples

The Dominican preached to the townspeople from the stone pulpit every Sunday.
During the Middle Ages, Dominican friars were known for their dedication to scholarship and teaching.
He decided to join the Dominican order and devote his life to service and prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franciscan - member of the Roman Catholic religious order founded by St. Francis of Assisi
  • Synonym: Jesuit - member of the Society of Jesus, a Roman Catholic religious order

Etymology

From Latin Dominicus, name of Saint Dominic (founder of the order).
