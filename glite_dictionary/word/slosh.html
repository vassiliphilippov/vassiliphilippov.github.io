to slosh

1. VERB

/ slɑʃ / (AmE), / slɒʃ / (BrE)
to move with a splashing sound, especially when liquid is being disturbed or spilled
make a splashing sound

Examples

The bucket began to slosh water as she carried it across the yard.
Rainwater sloshed noisily in the bottom of the boat during the storm.
He could hear his boots sloshing through the puddles after the rain.

Synset Examples

water was splashing on the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter and make a sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02191314-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ slɑʃ / (AmE), / slɒʃ / (BrE)
to walk heavily and noisily through wet mud or water
walk heavily through mud or water

Examples

We had to slosh across the flooded field to reach the road.
She sloshed through the mud in her rain boots after the storm.
The children enjoyed sloshing in the puddles during their walk in the park.

Synset Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically due to exhaustion or unfavorable conditions
  • Synonym: to slog - to walk with effort through something, especially mud or wet ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01925584-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ slɑʃ / (AmE), / slɒʃ / (BrE)
to spill, splash, or move a liquid in a way that sends it about clumsily or with force, often making a mess
to splash or spill liquid clumsily

Examples

Be careful not to slosh the coffee as you carry it to the table.
He accidentally sloshed water on his shirt while watering the plants.
The bucket sloshed as she walked, spilling soapy water onto the floor.

Synset Examples

slosh paint all over the walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter or fly in drops
  • Synonym: spill - to cause liquid to flow over the edge of its container
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01377252-v
Synset POS: v
