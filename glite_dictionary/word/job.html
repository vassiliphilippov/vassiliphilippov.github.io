Job

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊb / (AmE), / dʒəʊb / (BrE)
a figure in the Old Testament who remained faithful to God despite severe suffering and trials, often cited as a symbol of patience and endurance.
biblical figure of patience and suffering

Examples

The story of Job is often referenced as a lesson in patience and faith.
Many people admire Job for his unwavering belief during difficult times.
Her suffering made her feel like Job, enduring one hardship after another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, problems, or suffering

Etymology

From Hebrew *Iyov* (patient one), via Latin *Iobus* and Greek *Iōb*.

2. NOUN

/ dʒoʊb / (AmE), / dʒəʊb / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the story of Job, his sufferings, his pleas to God, and God's response
book of the Old Testament about Job's sufferings

Examples

The story in Job explores profound questions about suffering and faith.
Many people find comfort and inspiration in reading the Book of Job.
Scholars often debate the interpretation of God's speeches in Job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Job - a man in the Old Testament known for his patience and faith amid suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'Iob', from Hebrew 'Iyov', the name of the biblical character.

3. NOUN

/ dʒoʊb / (AmE), / dʒəʊb / (BrE)
a person who patiently bears great suffering or misfortune without complaining, referring to the biblical figure noted for his endurance
person showing patient endurance in suffering

Examples

Despite his hardships, he remained a Job, never complaining about his misfortunes.
She is a real Job, enduring so much pain with quiet strength.
Sometimes you need to be a Job in tough times, showing patience and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martyr - a person who suffers greatly, often for a cause or belief

Etymology

From the biblical figure Job, regarded as a model of patience in suffering.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10242619-n
Synset POS: n
