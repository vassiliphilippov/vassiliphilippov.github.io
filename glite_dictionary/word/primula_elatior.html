Primula elatior

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪmjʊlə ɪˈleɪtiər / (AmE), / ˈprɪmjʊlə ɪˈleɪtɪə / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian primrose with pale yellow flowers clustered in a one-sided umbel, commonly known as oxlip.
a Eurasian primrose species (oxlip)

Examples

The woodland clearing was dotted with Primula elatior in full bloom.
Botanists identified the pale yellow flowers as Primula elatior during the field survey.
Gardeners often plant Primula elatior to add color to shaded borders in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a plant of the genus Primula, with bright flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'primula' (primrose), 'elatior' meaning 'taller'.
