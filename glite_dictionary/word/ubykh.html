Ubykh

1. NOUN

/ ˈuːbɪk / (AmE), / ˈjuːbɪk / (BrE)
an extinct Northwest Caucasian language formerly spoken exclusively in Turkey by the Ubykh people
extinct Caucasian language of Turkey

Examples

Linguists have studied Ubykh extensively because of its large consonant inventory.
The last native speaker of Ubykh died in 1992, marking the language's extinction.
Efforts to document Ubykh included audio recordings and comprehensive grammar books.

Etymology

From the Ubykh people's own name for themselves, "Tʷaχɨbza".
