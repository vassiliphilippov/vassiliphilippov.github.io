moneran

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈnɪr.ən / (AmE), / məˈnɪə.rən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Monera, a former biological kingdom comprising prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria and cyanobacteria
relating to Monera (prokaryotic organisms)
medical

Examples

Some scientists consider archaea to be separate from other moneran organisms.
The cell structure of a moneran is simpler than that of eukaryotes.
Research on moneran classification has evolved over the past century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prokaryotic - relating to cells without a distinct nucleus

Etymology

From modern Latin Monera (a biological kingdom), derived from Greek monērēs (single, solitary).

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɪr.ən / (AmE), / məˈnɪə.rən / (BrE)
an organism formerly classified in the kingdom Monera, typically reproducing asexually and obtaining nutrients by absorption, photosynthesis, or chemosynthesis
single-celled organism from the former Monera kingdom
medical

Examples

The moneran can reproduce rapidly through simple cell division.
Scientists once classified both bacteria and blue-green algae as monerans.
With advances in genetics, the classification of moneran became obsolete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - a microscopic single-celled organism, especially from kingdom Monera

Etymology

From modern Latin Monera (kingdom name), from Greek monērēs (single, solitary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01344910-n
Synset POS: n
