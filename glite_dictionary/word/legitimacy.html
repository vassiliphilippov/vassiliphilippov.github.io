legitimacy

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (BrE)
The quality of being accepted as valid, reasonable, or having undisputed credibility.
undisputed credibility or validity

Examples

The new government's legitimacy was quickly recognized by other countries.
Questions about the legitimacy of the election results led to widespread protests.
His argument lacked legitimacy in the eyes of his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authenticity - the quality of being authentic or genuine
  • Antonym: illegitimacy - the state or quality of not being legitimate or lawful

Etymology

From Latin legitimitas, from legitimus (lawful).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (BrE)
The quality of being lawful, authorized, or in accordance with established rules or laws.
lawfulness or legality

Examples

The judge questioned the legitimacy of the contract before making a final ruling.
Many citizens doubted the legitimacy of the election results after the irregularities were reported.
She argued that the company's actions lacked legitimacy because they violated several regulations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegitimacy - the state of not being lawful or authorized
  • Synonym: lawfulness - the quality of conforming to law

Etymology

From Latin 'legitimitas' (lawfulness, genuineness), from 'legitimus' (lawful).

Synset POS: n
