civility

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
polite or courteous behavior, especially when it is formal or only superficial
formal politeness or courtesy

Examples

Despite their disagreement, both candidates treated each other with civility during the debate.
She greeted him with the bare minimum of civility necessary to be polite.
Good civility is important in maintaining a positive atmosphere at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior that shows respect for other people
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of manners or politeness; impolite behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'civilitas' (courtesy), from 'civilis' (civil, courteous).

2. NOUN

/ səˈvɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
polite behavior or speech that shows respect for other people
politeness and respect in behavior or speech

Examples

She always treats her colleagues with civility, even in stressful situations.
A little civility goes a long way in customer service.
Despite their differences, the debate was marked by civility and mutual respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior that shows respect for others
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of courtesy or manners; impoliteness

Etymology

From Old French *civilté*, from Latin *civilitas* (courtesy, politeness), from *civilis* (civil, courteous).
