Polybotrya

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈbɑːtriə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American ferns, typically terrestrial when young but often climbing in maturity.
a genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

The lush rainforest floor was covered with young Polybotrya plants.
As it matured, the Polybotrya began to climb the surrounding trees.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Polybotrya in its native habitat.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'botrys' (cluster), referring to the inflorescence.
