haemorrhoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.əˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhiː.mə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
A swollen and inflamed vein in the region of the anus, which can be internal or external to the anal sphincter, often causing pain or bleeding.
swollen vein around the anus
medical british

Examples

The patient complained of severe pain caused by a haemorrhoid.
Many people are reluctant to seek treatment for a haemorrhoid due to embarrassment.
After the surgery, she finally found relief from her chronic haemorrhoid problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhoid - swollen vein around the anus (common American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek *haimorrhoides* (liable to discharge blood), from *haima* (blood) + *rhein* (to flow).
