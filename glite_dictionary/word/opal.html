opal

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pəl / (BrE)
a translucent mineral composed of hydrated silica, often displaying varied colors and used as a gemstone
translucent mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The crown was decorated with a large opal surrounded by diamonds.
She inherited a beautiful opal ring from her grandmother.
The surface of the opal reflected flashes of green and blue.

Etymology

From Latin 'opalus', via Greek 'opallios', meaning 'precious stone'.
