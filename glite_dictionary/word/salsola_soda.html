Salsola soda

1. NOUN

/ sælˈsoʊlə ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / sælˈsəʊlə ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A bushy plant native to Old World salt marshes and sea beaches, known for its prickly leaves and formerly burned to produce crude soda ash.
plant yielding soda ash

Examples

In coastal gardens, Salsola soda is grown for its edible shoots and tolerance of salty soil.
The ashes of Salsola soda were once an important source of soda ash for glassmaking.
During the 18th century, Salsola soda was widely cultivated in Europe for its industrial use.

Etymology

From botanical Latin, genus name *Salsola* meaning 'salty' (referring to saline habitats), and Latin *soda* denoting soda ash.
