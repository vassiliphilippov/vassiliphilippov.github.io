Tectona grandis

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtoʊnə ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / tɛkˈtəʊnə ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
A tall tropical tree native to South and Southeast Asia, cultivated for its hard, durable wood known as teak.
species of tree yielding teak wood

Examples

Much of the best outdoor furniture is made from Tectona grandis because of its strength and resistance to weather.
Many plantations of Tectona grandis have been established in tropical Africa to supply the timber industry.
The distinctive grain of Tectona grandis makes it highly valued for luxury boat decking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teak - hard, durable wood from Tectona grandis

Etymology

From Modern Latin: genus name 'Tectona' from Greek 'tektōn' (carpenter), species name 'grandis' meaning 'large' in Latin.
