sponge

1. NOUN

/ spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
a person who is capable of learning new things quickly and absorbing information or skills readily
person who absorbs knowledge quickly

Examples

As a new employee, she proved to be a sponge, quickly mastering every aspect of her job.
Children are often called sponges because they absorb new ideas and experiences rapidly.
He is a sponge when it comes to technology, always learning the latest advancements without effort.

Synset Examples

she soaks up foreign languages like a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quick learner - a person who learns things rapidly

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the sponge as an object that soaks up liquid, to a person who soaks up information.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10521038-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
A primitive multicellular aquatic animal with a porous body supported by a fibrous skeleton, typically living in sessile colonies in the sea.
aquatic animal with porous body

Examples

Divers found a large sponge attached to the coral reef.
The sponge filters water to obtain its food from plankton and other particles.
Scientists study the structure of the sponge to understand early animal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poriferan - animal phylum including sponges

Etymology

From Latin *spongia*, from Greek *spongia* (a sponge).

Source: WordNet
