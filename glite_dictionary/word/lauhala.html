lauhala

1. NOUN

/ laʊˈhɑːlə / (AmE), / laʊˈhɑːlə / (BrE)
A tropical tree (Pandanus tectorius), native to Polynesia, whose leaves are used for weaving mats, hats, and other items.
Polynesian screw pine used for weaving

Examples

She learned to weave traditional mats using lauhala leaves from her grandmother.
The villagers gathered lauhala for making baskets and hats.
An exhibition displayed beautiful crafts made from lauhala in the cultural center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pandanus - A genus of tropical trees including the screw pine

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'lauhala', from 'lau' (leaf) + 'hala' (Pandanus tree).
