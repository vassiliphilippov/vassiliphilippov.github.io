resume

1. NOUN

/ rɛˈzuːmeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛzjʊmeɪ / (BrE)
A brief written account of a person's education, qualifications, and previous employment, typically sent with a job application.
a summary of qualifications and work history

Examples

She attached her resume to the job application for the company's review.
His resume lists several years of experience in project management.
Make sure your resume highlights your relevant skills and achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CV - a written overview of someone's career, qualifications, and education, mainly used in the UK and academia

Etymology

From French 'résumé' (summary, abstract), from past participle of 'résumer' (to summarize).

/ rɪˈzuːm / (AmE), / rɪˈzjuːm / (BrE)
to start doing something again after stopping for a period of time
begin again after a pause

Examples

After a short break, the meeting will resume at three o'clock.
The orchestra resumed playing after the conductor signaled.
She plans to resume her studies next semester after taking a year off.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to keep happening or existing without stopping
  • Antonym: pause - to stop doing something for a short time before continuing

Etymology

From Old French *resumer* (to take up again), from Latin *resumere*, from *re-* (again) + *sumere* (to take).

A short account or summary of events, facts, or a subject, often used to provide an overview.

Examples

Related Terms
