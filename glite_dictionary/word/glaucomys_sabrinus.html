Glaucomys sabrinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlaʊˈkoʊmɪs səˈbraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌɡlaʊˈkəʊmɪs səˈbraɪnəs / (BrE)
a large species of flying squirrel, native mainly to forests in Canada and parts of the northern United States
large North American flying squirrel species

Examples

The northern forests are home to the elusive Glaucomys sabrinus, a nocturnal flying squirrel.
Researchers observed a Glaucomys sabrinus gliding silently from tree to tree at dusk.
Climate change is affecting the habitat of the Glaucomys sabrinus across its Canadian range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying squirrel - a squirrel with a membrane allowing it to glide between trees

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus 'Glaucomys' and species 'sabrinus', used in zoological classification.
