Argyreia

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒɪəriə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of woody climbing plants native to tropical Asia and Australia, belonging to the morning glory family
genus of woody climbers (plants)

Examples

Several species of Argyreia are known for their large, showy flowers and vigorous growth.
The leaves of Argyreia have a silvery underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
Botanists discovered a new species of Argyreia in the forests of Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ipomoea - genus of flowering plants related to morning glories

Etymology

From Greek *argyros* (silver), referring to the silvery appearance of some species.
