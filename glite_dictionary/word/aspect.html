aspect

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈaspɛkt / (BrE)
the appearance or expression of someone's face, especially as it shows emotion
facial expression showing emotion

Examples

Her solemn aspect made everyone in the room fall silent.
He entered the room with a cheerful aspect that immediately lifted our spirits.
The child's frightened aspect revealed how nervous she was.

Synset Examples

a sad expression
a look of triumph
an angry face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the look on someone's face that shows what they are thinking or feeling
  • Synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing

Etymology

From Latin 'aspectus' (appearance, look), from 'aspicere' (to look at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈæspɛkt / (BrE)
(Grammar) A grammatical category expressing the beginning, duration, completion, or repetition of an action as indicated by a verb.
grammatical category of verb action

Examples

The progressive aspect is used to indicate an ongoing action in the present.
Perfect aspect expresses a completed action relevant to another point in time.
Some languages have many more aspect distinctions than English does.

Etymology

From Latin aspectus (appearance, look), from aspicere (to look at).

Examples
