Electrophorus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑːfərəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large South American freshwater fish, commonly known as electric eels, capable of generating strong electric shocks.
genus of electric eels

Examples

The Electrophorus is renowned for its ability to deliver a powerful electric shock to both prey and predators.
Scientists studied the Electrophorus to better understand how electric organs evolved in fish.
A mature Electrophorus can generate a voltage strong enough to stun a human.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric eel - a species of Electrophorus known for its ability to generate electricity

Etymology

From Greek 'ēlektron' (amber, electricity) + 'phoros' (bearing, carrying), referring to its electric-producing capability.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈfoʊrəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈfɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of electric eels, the type genus of the family Electrophoridae, known for producing electric shocks.
genus of electric eels

Examples

The Electrophorus is capable of delivering a strong electric shock to stun its prey.
Scientists study the Electrophorus to understand bioelectricity.
Several rivers in South America are home to the Electrophorus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric eel - a species of freshwater fish known for generating electricity

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'ēlektron' (amber, electricity) and 'phoros' (bearing).
