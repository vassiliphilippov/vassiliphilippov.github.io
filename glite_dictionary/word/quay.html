quay

1. NOUN

/ ki / (AmE), / kiː / (BrE)
A wharf or reinforced platform, typically built parallel to the shoreline, for loading and unloading ships.
platform for loading/unloading ships

Examples

The ship was moored alongside the quay awaiting its cargo.
Tourists enjoyed strolling along the bustling quay lined with cafés and shops.
Fish were unloaded onto the quay by the harbor workers each morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wharf - a structure built along the shore for ships to load and unload
  • Synonym: pier - a platform extending from the shore into the water, often used for docking boats

Etymology

From Old French 'kai' (sand bank, wharf), likely of Celtic origin.
