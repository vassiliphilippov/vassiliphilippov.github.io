Echidnophaga gallinacea

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə ˌɡælɪˈneɪsiə / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə ˌɡælɪˈneɪsiə / (BrE)
a species of flea that parasitizes poultry, especially chickens, often attaching to their heads and causing irritation
a flea species parasitic on chickens
medical

Examples

The poultry farm struggled with an infestation of Echidnophaga gallinacea among its chickens.
Veterinarians recommend regular checks for Echidnophaga gallinacea to protect flock health.
Treatment was necessary after several birds were found carrying Echidnophaga gallinacea on their heads.

Etymology

From Greek *echidna* (viper) and *phaga* (eater); *gallinacea* from Latin *gallina* (hen, chicken).
