mutilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of damaging or disfiguring the appearance or surface of something, often making it imperfect or unsightly
the act of damaging or disfiguring

Examples

The ancient statue showed signs of mutilation, likely caused by vandals centuries ago.
Environmentalists criticized the mutilation of the forest for commercial development.
Covering graffiti with thick paint resulted in the mutilation of the building's historic façade.

Synset Examples

the defacement of an Italian mosaic during the Turkish invasion
he objected to the dam's massive disfigurement of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defacement - the act of spoiling the appearance of something
  • Synonym: disfigurement - the action of spoiling the appearance or shape of something

Etymology

From Latin mutilatio, from mutilare (to cut off, maim).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of severely injuring or damaging a body, especially by removing a limb or other important part, often causing disfigurement.
severe injury causing disfigurement or loss of a body part

Examples

The victim suffered extensive mutilation during the attack.
Legislation was passed to prevent the ritual mutilation of children.
He was left with permanent mutilation as a result of the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiming - the act of wounding or injuring seriously, especially by depriving of the use of a limb
  • Synonym: amputation - the removal of a limb, typically by surgery

Etymology

From Latin mutilatio (act of maiming or cutting off).
