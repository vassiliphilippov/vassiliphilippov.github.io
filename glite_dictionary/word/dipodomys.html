Dipodomys

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑdəˌmɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒdəˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as kangaroo rats, native to North America and recognized for their long hind legs and bipedal hopping.
genus of kangaroo rats

Examples

The genus Dipodomys includes several species of kangaroo rats inhabiting arid regions of North America.
Recent studies have focused on how Dipodomys adapts to its desert environment.
Scientists observed the unique locomotion of Dipodomys during field research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - a small rodent with long hind legs found in western North America

Etymology

From Greek 'dipoús' (two-footed) and 'mys' (mouse).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈpɒdəˌmɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒdəmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents commonly known as kangaroo rats, native to North America.
genus of kangaroo rats (rodents)

Examples

The genus Dipodomys includes several species adapted to arid environments.
Scientists have studied the behavior of Dipodomys to understand desert survival strategies.
Many Dipodomys species store seeds in their burrows to survive long dry periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - a burrowing rodent native to North America, with long hind legs for jumping

Etymology

From Greek 'dipoús' (two-footed) + 'mys' (mouse), referring to their bipedal hopping movement.
