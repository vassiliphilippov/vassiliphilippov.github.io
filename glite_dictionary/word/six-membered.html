six-membered

1. ADJ

/ sɪks ˈmɛmbɚd / (AmE), / sɪks ˈmɛmbəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having a ring made up of six atoms or groups.
having a ring of six atoms/groups (chemistry)

Examples

Benzene is a classic example of a six-membered aromatic ring.
The stability of six-membered rings often makes them common in organic compounds.
This compound contains a six-membered ring with alternate double bonds.

Synset Examples

having three carbon and three nitrogen atoms in a six-membered ring

  • Degree Higher: seven-membered - (of a chemical compound) having a ring made up of seven atoms or groups
