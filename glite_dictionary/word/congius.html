congius

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑndʒiəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒiəs / (BrE)
a British imperial unit of capacity (liquid or dry) equal to 4 quarts or 4.545 liters, used chiefly in historical contexts
old British unit of volume, 4 quarts

Examples

The ancient Roman vessel held exactly one congius of olive oil.
Records mention a congius of wine being distributed per soldier.
A congius was equivalent to four imperial quarts in the British measurement system.

Etymology

From Latin congius (a liquid measure, about 3.48 liters).
