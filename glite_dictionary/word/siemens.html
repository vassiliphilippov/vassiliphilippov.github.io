siemens

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːmənz / (AmE), / ˈsiːmənz / (BrE)
the SI unit of electrical conductance, equal to one reciprocal ohm
SI unit of electrical conductance

Examples

The electrical conductance of the wire measured 2 siemens under laboratory conditions.
The new sensor can detect changes as small as 0.01 siemens in conductance.
One siemens is the reciprocal of one ohm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ohm - the SI unit of electrical resistance

Etymology

Named after German engineer and inventor Werner von Siemens (1816–1892).
