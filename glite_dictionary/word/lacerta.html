Lacerta

1. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜrtə / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtə / (BrE)
A genus of lizards in the family Lacertidae, commonly called the true lizards, found mainly in Eurasia.
genus of Eurasian lizards

Examples

The common wall lizard belongs to the genus Lacerta.
Many species of Lacerta are native to Europe and parts of Asia.
Some members of Lacerta are popular in herpetological studies.

Etymology

From Latin 'lacerta' meaning 'lizard'.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜrtə / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtə / (BrE)
a genus of lizards in the family Lacertidae, commonly known as true lizards, found throughout Eurasia.
a genus of Eurasian lizards

Examples

The common wall lizard belongs to the genus Lacerta.
Several species within Lacerta are found in Southern Europe.
Scientists studied behavioral differences among Lacerta populations across various habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail and movable eyelids

Etymology

From Latin *lacerta* (lizard).
