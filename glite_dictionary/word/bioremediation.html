bioremediation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪoʊrɪˌmiːdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊrɪˌmiːdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of living organisms, especially microorganisms, to remove or neutralize pollutants from a contaminated site
removal of pollutants using organisms

Examples

Bioremediation is often used to clean up oil spills in marine environments.
Scientists are developing new methods of bioremediation to address soil contamination.
The city invested in bioremediation as part of its sustainable waste management strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biodegradation - breakdown of substances by biological means

Etymology

From 'bio-' (life) and 'remediation' (process of remedying).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.rɪˌmiː.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.rɪˌmiː.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The use of microorganisms to break down or remove pollutants and contaminants from soil, water, or other environments.
removal of pollutants using microorganisms
medical

Examples

Scientists are exploring bioremediation as a solution for cleaning up oil spills in marine environments.
The company specializes in bioremediation of contaminated industrial sites.
Graduate students studied the effects of bioremediation on heavy metal removal from soil samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detoxification - the process of removing toxins or harmful substances

Etymology

From bio- (life) + remediation (the act of remedying).
