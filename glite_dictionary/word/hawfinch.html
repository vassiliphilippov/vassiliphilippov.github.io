hawfinch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːˌfɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːfɪntʃ / (BrE)
a large Eurasian finch with a powerful bill, known for its ability to crack hard seeds.
a large Eurasian finch

Examples

We spotted a brightly colored hawfinch perched in the old apple tree.
The hawfinch is famous for its massive beak, designed to crack open hard seeds.
In spring, you may see a hawfinch feeding on cherry stones in woodland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small passerine songbird of the finch family

Etymology

From 'haw', referring to the hawthorn plant, and 'finch', from Old English 'finc'.
