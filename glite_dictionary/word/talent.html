talent

1. NOUN

/ ˈtælənt / (AmE), / ˈtælənt / (BrE)
a natural ability to do something well; an innate skill or aptitude
natural ability or aptitude

Examples

She has a real talent for painting that was obvious from a young age.
His talent as a musician impressed everyone at the audition.
Many companies seek employees with talent and creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - a natural ability or talent
  • Synonym: ability - the power or skill to do something
  • Antonym: inability - lack of ability

Etymology

From Old French 'talent', from Latin 'talentum' (a sum of money, later figuratively a gift or ability), from Ancient Greek 'talanton' (balance, weight, talent as a unit of weight or value).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtælənt / (AmE), / ˈtæl.ənt / (BrE)
a person who possesses a natural aptitude or remarkable ability in a particular field or activity
naturally gifted person

Examples

The orchestra is always searching for new talent to join its ranks.
Several young talent competed in the piano competition.
She was considered the brightest talent in the design industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - a person of exceptional intellectual or creative power
  • Synonym: prodigy - a person, especially a young one, endowed with exceptional abilities
  • Antonym: amateur - a person engaging or competent in a pursuit but not as a profession or at a high level

Etymology

From Latin 'talenta', the plural of 'talentum', referring initially to a unit of weight or money, later metaphorically to intellectual ability.
