triangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.æŋ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
A flat shape with three straight sides and three angles.
three-sided polygon

Examples

The teacher drew a triangle on the board to explain the Pythagorean theorem.
A yield sign is often shaped like an upside-down triangle.
You can cut the sandwich into a triangle for easier eating.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadrilateral - a four-sided polygon

Etymology

From Latin 'triangulum', meaning 'three-cornered' or 'triangle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.æŋ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
a flat, typically plastic or metal, triangular tool used in technical drawing to draw straight lines at specific angles, usually 30°, 45°, or 60°.
triangular drawing instrument

Examples

The architect used a triangle to draw precise parallel lines on the blueprint.
Please hand me the 45-degree triangle from the drafting table.
In geometry class, we learned how to use a triangle for technical sketches.

Etymology

From Latin triangulum, meaning 'three-cornered' or 'triangle'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
a percussion instrument made from a metal bar bent into an open triangle, which is struck with a metal beater to produce a clear, ringing tone
metal percussion instrument shaped like a triangle

Examples

The percussionist tapped the triangle at the start of the piece, adding a bright sound to the music.
When cued, she struck the triangle lightly and filled the hall with a shimmering note.
Children often enjoy playing simple tunes on the triangle during music class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tambourine - a small percussion instrument with a frame and jingles, often played by shaking or hitting

4. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.æŋ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.æŋ.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
an object, area, or shape that is approximately or roughly in the form of a triangle, typically with three sides and three angles
object approximating a triangle shape

Examples

The three mountain peaks formed a natural triangle on the horizon.
We arranged the tables in a triangle to fit more guests in the room.
The layout of the garden paths creates a rough triangle in the center.

Synset Examples

the coastline of Chile and Argentina and Brazil forms two legs of a triangle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular - having three sides or relating to a shape with three sides

Etymology

From Latin 'triangulum', from 'tri-' (three) + 'angulus' (angle).
