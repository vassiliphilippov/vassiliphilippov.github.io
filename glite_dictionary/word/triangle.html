Triangle

1. PROPN

/ ˈtraɪˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
a small northern constellation located between Andromeda and Aries, near Perseus
small northern constellation

Examples

The Triangle constellation is best seen in the northern hemisphere during autumn.
Amateur astronomers often search for Triangle when locating nearby constellations such as Andromeda.
Triangle contains several galaxies that are popular targets for stargazers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Triangulum', meaning 'triangle', from its three bright stars forming a triangle.
