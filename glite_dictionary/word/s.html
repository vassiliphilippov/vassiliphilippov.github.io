/ ɛs / (AmE), / ɛs / (BrE)

Examples

The periodic table lists S as the symbol for sulfur.

Chemical symbol from the Latin word 'sulphur', adopted as 'S' in the international chemical notation.

4. NOUN

/ ɛs / (AmE), / ɛs / (BrE)
the symbol for entropy, a thermodynamic quantity representing the amount of energy in a system that is unavailable for doing work
symbol for entropy (thermodynamics)

Examples

The change in S of the system was calculated after the reaction occurred.
In this formula, S denotes the entropy of the gas under standard conditions.
As temperature increases, S generally increases as well for most substances.

Synset Examples

entropy increases as matter and energy in the universe degrade to an ultimate state of inert uniformity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entropy - thermodynamic quantity representing disorder or unavailable energy

Etymology

Adopted in the 19th century for the German word 'S' (from 'S' for 'Satz' meaning 'statement' or 'sentence', used by Clausius, the inventor of the entropy concept).

5. NOUN

/ ɛs / (AmE), / ɛs / (BrE)
the direction toward the south, or the cardinal compass point at 180 degrees
cardinal compass point, south

Examples

The hikers walked due S for most of the morning.
On the map, the village is located just to the S of the river.
The wind shifted and began blowing from the S.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: south - the direction toward the South Pole
  • Antonym: N - the cardinal compass point at 0 or 360 degrees (north)
  • Degree Lower: E - the cardinal compass point at 90 degrees (east)
  • Degree Higher: W - the cardinal compass point at 270 degrees (west)

Etymology

From the initial letter of 'south'.
