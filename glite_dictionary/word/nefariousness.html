nefariousness

1. NOUN

/ nəˈfɛriəsnəs / (AmE), / nɪˈfeəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being extremely wicked or morally wrong
wickedness; extreme immorality

Examples

The villain's nefariousness shocked the entire town.
History is filled with tales of nefariousness committed by corrupt leaders.
Her nefariousness was eventually exposed, leading to her arrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being morally wrong or evil
  • Antonym: virtue - moral excellence

Etymology

From nefarious + -ness; 'nefarious' from Latin nefas (wrong, crime).
