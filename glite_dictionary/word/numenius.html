Numenius

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmiːni.əs / (AmE), / njuˈmiːni.əs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, commonly known as curlews, characterized by long, down-curved bills.
genus of curlews

Examples

Several species within the genus Numenius are noted for their long migratory journeys.
The Eurasian curlew, part of Numenius, can be identified by its distinctive call.
Research has shown that Numenius populations are declining due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - a large wading bird with a long, down-curved bill

Etymology

From Greek 'noumēnios', perhaps meaning 'of the new moon', referring to the bill's crescent shape.

2. NOUN

/ nuˈmiːniəs / (AmE), / njuːˈmiːniəs / (BrE)
a genus of large wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, commonly known as curlews, characterized by long, down-curved bills and mottled brown plumage.
a genus of wading birds (curlews)

Examples

Several species in the genus Numenius are known for their impressive migratory journeys.
The slender, curved bill is a distinctive feature of all members of Numenius.
Conservationists are concerned about declining populations of Numenius worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - a large wading bird with a long, curved bill

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Ancient Greek noumenios (a type of bird, possibly the curlew).
