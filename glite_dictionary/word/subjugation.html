subjugation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.dʒəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dʒʊˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control, especially by conquest
the act of conquering or controlling

Examples

The country's long history of subjugation left deep scars on its people.
The subjugation of indigenous populations has been widely condemned by historians.
Efforts to end the subjugation of minorities continue around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domination - the exercise of power or control over others
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from oppression

Etymology

From Latin subjugatio (act of subduing), from subjugare (to subdue, bring under the yoke).

3. NOUN

/ sʌb.dʒəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / sʌb.dʒʊˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of bringing a person, group, or country under domination or control, especially by conquest.
the act of conquering or controlling

Examples

The subjugation of indigenous peoples is a major topic in world history.
A century of subjugation finally ended when the colony achieved independence.
He wrote a book about the subjugation of the region by foreign powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conquest - the act of conquering or taking control
  • Antonym: emancipation - the act of being freed from restraint, control, or power

Etymology

From Latin subjugatio, from subjugare meaning 'to subjugate'.
