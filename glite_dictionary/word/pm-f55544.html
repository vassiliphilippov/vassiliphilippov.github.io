p.m.

1. NOUN

/ piː ˈɛm / (AmE), / piː ˈɛm / (BrE)
the period of time from noon until midnight, used to specify times after 12:00 noon.
the time between noon and midnight

Examples

The meeting is scheduled for 3:00 p.m. tomorrow.
She doesn't usually eat lunch until 2 p.m..
Our flight departs at 6:45 p.m. sharp.

Synset Examples

AM to PM

Related Terms

  • Antonym: a.m. - the period between midnight and noon

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'post meridiem' (after midday).

2. ADV

/ ˌpiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌpiː ˈem / (BrE)
used to indicate the time between noon and midnight in the 12-hour clock system.
between noon and midnight

Examples

The store closes at 10 p.m. every night.
Our flight departs at 5:30 p.m. sharp.
She usually starts her evening shift at 6 p.m..

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: a.m. - used to indicate the time between midnight and noon

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'post meridiem' meaning 'after midday'.
