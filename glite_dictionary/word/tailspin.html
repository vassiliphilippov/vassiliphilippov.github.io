tailspin

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌspɪn / (BrE)
A rapid downward spiral movement of an aircraft, especially with loss of control.
spiraling descent of an aircraft

Examples

The plane went into a tailspin after losing power to one of its engines.
During training, pilots learn how to recover from a tailspin safely.
A sudden gust of wind sent the small aircraft into a terrifying tailspin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - an uncontrolled rapid descent of an aircraft with a steep spiral path

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌspɪn / (BrE)
A state of rapidly deteriorating emotional control, often resulting in confusion or collapse.
emotional collapse or confusion

Examples

After losing his job, Tom went into a tailspin and struggled to cope for months.
The news sent her emotions into a tailspin, leaving her unable to focus at work.
Their sudden breakup threw him into a deep tailspin of anxiety and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakdown - a sudden collapse in mental health or emotional functioning
  • Synonym: panic - a sudden overwhelming fear, often producing irrational behavior
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not easily disturbed emotionally

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'tailspin' from aviation, where an aircraft falls rapidly nose-down in a spiral; first recorded in early 20th century.
