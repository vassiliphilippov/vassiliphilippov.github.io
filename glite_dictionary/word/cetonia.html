Cetonia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Cetoniidae, commonly known as rose chafers.
genus of beetles (rose chafers)

Examples

Many gardeners are familiar with the metallic green beetles of the genus Cetonia.
The larvae of Cetonia species develop in decaying wood and compost.
Cetonia beetles are sometimes called rose chafers due to their fondness for flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Cetonia*, used in taxonomic nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ siˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored scarab beetles in the family Cetoniidae, commonly known as rose chafers.
genus of rose chafer beetles

Examples

The Cetonia genus includes beetles often seen feeding on flower pollen.
One of the best-known members of Cetonia is the rose chafer, common in European gardens.
Larvae of Cetonia species develop in decaying wood or compost.

Etymology

From Latin *Cetonia*, a genus name assigned in scientific classification.
