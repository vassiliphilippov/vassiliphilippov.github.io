Captain James Cook

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæp.tən ˈdʒeɪmz kʊk / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪn ˈdʒeɪmz kʊk / (BrE)
(1728–1779) English navigator and explorer who charted the eastern coast of Australia, claimed it for Britain, and discovered several Pacific islands.
English navigator and explorer (1728–1779)

Examples

Many history books describe how Captain James Cook mapped the coast of Australia.
Captain James Cook made three major voyages to the Pacific, expanding European knowledge of the region.
The museum features artifacts from Captain James Cook's famous expeditions.
