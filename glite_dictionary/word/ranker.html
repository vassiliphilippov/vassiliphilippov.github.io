ranker

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋkər / (AmE), / ˈræŋkə / (BrE)
a commissioned officer in the armed forces who has been promoted from the enlisted ranks
commissioned officer promoted from enlisted status

Examples

The new captain is a ranker, having begun his career as a private.
Many rankers bring invaluable experience from their time as enlisted personnel.
Despite being a ranker, she gained the respect of officers and troops alike.

Etymology

From 'rank' (military grade) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræŋkər / (AmE), / ˈræŋkə / (BrE)
an enlisted soldier serving in the regular ranks of the armed forces, rather than an officer
enlisted soldier in the military

Examples

He joined the army as a ranker and rose through the ranks to become an officer.
Unlike many officers, she began her career as a ranker on the front lines.
The awards ceremony recognized several rankers for their outstanding service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officer - a member of the armed forces holding a position of authority, usually commissioned

Etymology

From 'rank' (row, line of soldiers) + '-er' (agent suffix).
