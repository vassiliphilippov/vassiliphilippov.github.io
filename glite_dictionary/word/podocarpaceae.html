Podocarpaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˌkɑrˈpeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˌkɑːˈpeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of gymnospermous trees and shrubs with persistent needlelike or scalelike leaves, native mainly to the Southern Hemisphere
family of coniferous trees and shrubs

Examples

The Podocarpaceae are widespread in southern temperate forests, especially in New Zealand and South America.
Members of the Podocarpaceae family have needlelike or scalelike leaves adapted to a range of habitats.
Botanists are studying the evolutionary history of Podocarpaceae to understand conifer diversification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Podocarpus - a genus of coniferous trees and shrubs in Podocarpaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Podocarpus plus the botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdoʊˌkɑːrˈpeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəʊˌkɑːˈpeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of gymnospermous trees and shrubs having simple, persistent needle-like or scale-like leaves, mainly found in the Southern Hemisphere
a family of gymnosperm trees and shrubs

Examples

The Podocarpaceae are widespread throughout the Southern Hemisphere, especially in Australasia and South America.
Some species in the Podocarpaceae family are valued for their timber and ornamental use.
The leaves of Podocarpaceae are typically needle-like or scale-like and remain green year-round.

Etymology

From New Latin; from Greek 'pod' (foot) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the stalked fruit of the type genus Podocarpus.
