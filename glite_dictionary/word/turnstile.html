turnstile

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝnˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnstaɪl / (BrE)
A mechanical gate with revolving horizontal arms, installed in passageways to control or count the flow of people entering or exiting an area.
revolving gate for controlling entry

Examples

I inserted my ticket and pushed through the turnstile into the subway.
The stadium uses electronic turnstiles to track attendance for each event.
The security guard watched as hundreds of people filed past the turnstile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - structure blocking or controlling access

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'stile', the latter from Old French 'estile' (a movable barrier).
