disinfectant

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛktənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛktənt / (BrE)
a substance or agent that destroys microorganisms to prevent infection or disease, especially on surfaces.
substance that kills microorganisms

Examples

You should always clean the kitchen counters with a disinfectant to reduce the risk of illness.
The hospital uses a powerful disinfectant to keep patient rooms germ-free.
Exposure to sunlight can act as a natural disinfectant for water in outdoor tanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiseptic - substance that prevents the growth of microorganisms, especially on living tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'infectare' (to stain, pollute).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛktənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛktənt / (BrE)
used to prevent infection by inhibiting the growth or action of microorganisms; having disinfecting properties.
preventing infection; having disinfecting properties

Examples

The nurse wiped the wound with a disinfectant solution before applying the bandage.
These wipes have disinfectant properties and are effective against most bacteria.
Doctors recommend washing your hands with disinfectant soap during flu season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiseptic - preventing the growth of disease-causing microorganisms (adj.)

Etymology

From disinfect + -ant, after French désinfectant (adj. & n.), from désinfecter (to disinfect).
