Colossian

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɒʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient city of Colossae in Phrygia
native of ancient Colossae

Examples

The ancient Colossian community was known for its textiles and dyes.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts that belonged to a wealthy Colossian.
The letter was addressed to the early Christian Colossian congregation.

Etymology

From Colossae, an ancient city in Phrygia, with English suffix -ian meaning 'person from'.
