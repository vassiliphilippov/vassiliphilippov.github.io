coronation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔɹəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒɹəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the ceremony of crowning and formally investing a monarch with regal power
the crowning of a new monarch

Examples

The coronation of the new king was watched by millions around the world.
Historians studied the rituals and symbols used in the coronation ceremony.
People lined the streets to celebrate the queen's coronation in the historic cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthronement - the ceremony of formally placing a monarch on the throne

Etymology

From Latin 'coronatio' (crowning), from 'corona' (crown).
