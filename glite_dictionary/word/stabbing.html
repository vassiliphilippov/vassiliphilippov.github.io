stabbing

1. ADJ

/ ˈstæbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstæbɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a sudden sharp pain or emotional hurt, either physical or psychological.
causing sharp pain or hurt

Examples

He felt a stabbing pain in his chest that took his breath away.
Her stabbing comment left him speechless and deeply hurt.
The patient described the headache as a stabbing sensation behind the eyes.

Synset Examples

a stabbing remark
wounding and false charges of disloyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing pain or discomfort
  • Synonym: hurtful - causing emotional pain
  • Antonym: comforting - making someone feel better, lessening pain

2. ADJ

/ ˈstæbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstabɪŋ / (BrE)
(of pain or sensation) intense and sharp, as if caused by a pointed object.
sharp, piercing (of pain)

Examples

She felt a stabbing pain in her side that made her stop walking.
A stabbing sensation shot through his hand when he accidentally touched the ice.
The patient complained of a sudden, stabbing headache after the accident.

Synset Examples

a cutting wind
keen winds
knifelike cold
piercing knifelike pains
piercing cold
piercing criticism
a stabbing pain
lancinating pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piercing - sharp and intense (of pain or sensation)
  • Antonym: dull - not sharp or intense (of pain or sensation)

Etymology

From the verb 'stab' + -ing, referring to the sensation of being stabbed.
