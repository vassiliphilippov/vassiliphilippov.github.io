tornillo

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈniːjoʊ / (AmE), / tɔːˈniːjəʊ / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, noted for its spirally twisted seed pods.
shrub/tree with twisted pods (SW US, NW Mexico)

Examples

The tornillo is often recognized by its distinctive twisted seed pods.
In desert landscaping, the tornillo is valued for its drought resistance.
We found a cluster of tornillos growing near the canyon trail.

Etymology

From Spanish 'tornillo' (meaning 'little screw'), referring to the twisted shape of the seed pods.
