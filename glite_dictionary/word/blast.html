blast

1. NOUN

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
an explosion, especially one caused by dynamite or another powerful force
explosion or powerful burst

Examples

The miners set off a blast to clear the tunnel entrance.
A sudden blast shook the windows and startled everyone nearby.
The demolition crew used a controlled blast to bring down the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent burst or release of energy

Etymology

From Old English 'blǣst' (a blowing, blast).
