Lupinus arboreus

1. NOUN

/ luˈpaɪnəs ɑːrˈbɔːri.əs / (AmE), / luːˈpaɪnəs ɑːˈbɔːrɪ.əs / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, with showy yellow or blue flowers, also naturalized in Australia
a flowering evergreen shrub from the U.S. Pacific coast

Examples

The Lupinus arboreus is often planted to stabilize sandy soils along the Californian coast.
In spring, Lupinus arboreus produces dense clusters of brightly colored flowers.
Gardeners in Australia appreciate Lupinus arboreus for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Latin 'lupinus' (lupine, a type of plant) and 'arboreus' (tree-like).
