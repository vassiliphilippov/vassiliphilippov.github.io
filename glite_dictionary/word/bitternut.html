bitternut

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənʌt / (BrE)
A hickory tree of eastern North America with compound leaves of 7 or 9 leaflets and bitter, thin-shelled nuts; also, the nut of this tree.
a type of hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The bitternut is easily recognized by its distinctive yellow buds.
While hiking, we came across a grove of bitternut hickories along the riverbank.
Squirrels often avoid the bitternut because of its intensely bitter seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of tree of the genus Carya, known for hard wood and edible nuts

Etymology

Formed from 'bitter' + 'nut', referring to the taste of the tree's nuts.
