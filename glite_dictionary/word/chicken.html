chicken

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is easily frightened or lacks courage
cowardly or timid person
informal

Examples

Don’t be a chicken—just go up and talk to her.
He called me a chicken for not trying the roller coaster.
Whenever it's time to speak in public, she turns into a total chicken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

Derived from the figurative use of 'chicken' as a timid animal, first recorded in the 14th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
a game or contest in which the participants engage in a dangerous or risky activity, continuing until one gives in or pulls out, thereby losing; typically done to test courage.
a dangerous contest of nerve

Examples

The two drivers played chicken, speeding toward each other until one swerved at the last second.
Teenagers sometimes play chicken by standing on railway tracks and waiting until the last possible moment to jump away.
The politician accused his rival of playing chicken with public safety policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dare - an act or challenge to prove courage

Etymology

From the tendency to associate 'chicken' with cowardice, as in 'being chicken' (cowardly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07472727-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪkˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
(informal) Easily frightened; lacking in courage; cowardly.
easily frightened; cowardly (informal)
informal

Examples

Don't be such a chicken—just go talk to her!
He admitted he was too much of a chicken to try bungee jumping.
They called me a chicken for refusing to ride the roller coaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage, easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage, not afraid

Etymology

Metaphorical use comparing timidity to the bird 'chicken', known for being easily startled.

5. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
A domesticated bird kept for its eggs and meat, believed to originate from the red jungle fowl.
domestic fowl for eggs or meat

Examples

They raise chickens on their farm for both eggs and meat.
The children fed the chicken some corn in the backyard.
Grilled chicken is one of my favorite dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen - female chicken, especially an adult
  • Synonym: rooster - male chicken, also called cock

Etymology

Old English 'cicen', diminutive of 'cock' or 'hen'.
