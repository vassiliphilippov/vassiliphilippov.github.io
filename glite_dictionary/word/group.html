group

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɡruːp / (BrE)
a number of people or things that are considered or classed together as a single unit
set of people or things considered as a unit

Examples

The group met every Thursday to discuss the book of the month.
We spotted a group of deer grazing near the river.
She joined a study group to prepare for her exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things or people gathered together
  • Antonym: individual - a single person or thing, as opposed to a group

Etymology

From French groupe, from Italian gruppo (knot, cluster, group).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00031563-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɡruːp / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A set of elements with an operation that is closed, associative, has an identity element, and in which every element has an inverse.
mathematical set with certain properties

Examples

The set of integers under addition forms a group in mathematics.
If a group has only two elements, it is called a cyclic group of order two.
Every element in a group must have an inverse within the group.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06026202-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ɡruːp / (BrE)
(Chemistry) Two or more atoms bonded together, functioning as a single unit and forming part of a molecule.
collection of atoms acting as a molecular unit
medical

Examples

The hydroxyl group in alcohols is responsible for their reactivity.
A methyl group consists of one carbon atom bonded to three hydrogen atoms.
Adding a phosphate group can change the molecule's function significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional group - a specific group of atoms within a molecule responsible for characteristic chemical reactions
