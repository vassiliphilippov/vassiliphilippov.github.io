toe

/ toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
to place or move the toes so that they are in a particular position or direction, especially while walking or standing

Examples

Make sure you toe straight ahead during the race.

4. NOUN

/ toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
one of the five small parts at the end of each foot in humans and many animals; a digit of the foot.
one of the digits of the foot

Examples

She stubbed her toe on the edge of the table and winced in pain.
Each toe helps you keep your balance while walking or running.
The child counted the toes on her foot to make sure there were five.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finger - one of the digits of the hand
  • Antonym: heel - the back part of the human foot below the ankle

Etymology

Old English tā, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch teen and German Zehe.
