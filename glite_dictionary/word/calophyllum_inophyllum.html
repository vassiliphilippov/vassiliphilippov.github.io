Calophyllum inophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈfɪliəm ɪˈnɒfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈfɪliəm ɪˈnɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree native to coastal areas of southern India to Malaysia, with fragrant white flowers, also known as Alexandrian laurel.
tropical tree; fragrant flowers; southern Asia

Examples

The wood of the Calophyllum inophyllum is valued for its durability in boat building.
In southern India, Calophyllum inophyllum is often planted as an ornamental tree for its glossy leaves and fragrant flowers.
Traditional medicine sometimes uses oil extracted from the nuts of Calophyllum inophyllum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alexandrian laurel - another common name for Calophyllum inophyllum

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'phyllon' (leaf) for the genus; species name from Greek 'ino-' (fiber) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
