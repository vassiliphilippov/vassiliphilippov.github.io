retromandibular vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːtroʊˌmændɪˈbjulər veɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːtrəʊˌmændɪˈbjuːlə veɪn / (BrE)
A vein formed by the joining of the superficial temporal and maxillary veins, located in front of the ear and serving as a posterior branch of the facial vein.
vein behind the lower jaw
medical

Examples

During the procedure, the surgeon identified the retromandibular vein to avoid accidental injury.
The retromandibular vein can be seen running down from in front of the ear toward the angle of the mandible.
Radiologists often evaluate the retromandibular vein in head and neck imaging studies.

Etymology

From Latin 'retro-' (behind) + 'mandibula' (jaw), referring to its position behind the mandible.
