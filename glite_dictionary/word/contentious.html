contentious

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛnʃəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃəs / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause disagreement or controversy; argumentative
causing controversy or argument

Examples

The proposal quickly became a contentious issue among community members.
Her contentious remarks started a heated debate at the meeting.
The law remains contentious despite several attempts at reform.

Synset Examples

a central and contentious element of the book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controversial - likely to give rise to public disagreement
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or controversy

Etymology

From Latin 'contentiosus' (quarrelsome), from 'contentio' (strife, contest).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛn(t)ʃəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛn.ʃəs / (BrE)
often causing, or likely to cause, argument or disagreement; showing an inclination to dispute or argue.
causing or likely to cause arguments

Examples

The new policy became a contentious topic at the staff meeting, dividing colleagues into opposing camps.
She has a contentious manner that often leads to heated debates.
The contract negotiations proved to be contentious, with both sides refusing to compromise.

Synset Examples

a style described as abrasive and contentious
a disputatious lawyer
a litigious and acrimonious spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argumentative - given to expressing or expressing a tendency to argue
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or argument

Etymology

From Latin 'contentiosus' (quarrelsome), from 'contentio' (strife, dispute).
