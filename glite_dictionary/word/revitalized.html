revitalized

1. ADJ

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / riˈvaɪtəlaɪzd / (BrE)
restored to new life, strength, or energy; given renewed vitality
restored to new life or energy

Examples

The revitalized downtown area now attracts thousands of visitors each weekend.
New policies led to a revitalized agricultural sector, boosting productivity and morale.
After months of neglect, the park was revitalized by community efforts.

Synset Examples

a revitalized economy
a revitalized inner-city neighborhood
Berlin has been reborn after probably the most intense period of construction since the post-war period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revived - restored to life, consciousness, or activity
  • Synonym: restored - brought back to a former or original state

Etymology

From re- (again) + vitalize, ultimately from Latin 'vita' (life).
