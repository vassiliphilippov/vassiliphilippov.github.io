Defoe

1. PROPN

/ dɪˈfoʊ / (AmE), / dɪˈfəʊ / (BrE)
Daniel Defoe (1659–1731), an English writer known especially for his novel 'Robinson Crusoe'.
English novelist, author of 'Robinson Crusoe'

Examples

Defoe is often credited with shaping the development of the English novel.
Many students read Defoe's 'Robinson Crusoe' as part of their literature curriculum.
The influence of Defoe can be seen in the works of later English writers.
