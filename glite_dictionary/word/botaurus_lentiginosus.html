Botaurus lentiginosus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtɔːrəs lɛnˌtɪdʒɪˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈtɔːrəs lɛnˌtɪdʒɪˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A North American species of bittern, a wading bird in the heron family, noted for its camouflaged plumage and booming call.
North American species of bittern (wading bird)

Examples

The wetland reserve is home to the elusive Botaurus lentiginosus.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a Botaurus lentiginosus among the reeds.
The camouflaged Botaurus lentiginosus is difficult to see when standing motionless in marsh grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American bittern - the common English name for Botaurus lentiginosus

Etymology

From Latin: genus Botaurus (bittern), species lentiginosus (freckled or speckled).
