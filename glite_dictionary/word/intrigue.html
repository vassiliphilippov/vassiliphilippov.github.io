intrigue

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
to arouse the curiosity or interest of someone; fascinate
arouse curiosity or interest

Examples

The mysterious painting on the wall intrigued her for hours.
His unusual ideas always intrigue his colleagues.
What really intrigues me is how they solved the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fascinate - to attract strong interest or attention
  • Antonym: bore - to cause to feel weary and uninterested

Etymology

From French *intriguer*, from Italian *intricare* (to entangle), from Latin *intricare*.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrig / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːg / (BrE)
a secret or illicit love affair, especially one that is kept hidden
secret romantic or sexual affair

Examples

Their intrigue lasted for years before anyone discovered their secret relationship.
The novel tells the story of a royal intrigue that nearly brought down the monarchy.
After the scandal broke, everyone gossiped about the politician's intrigue with his assistant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affair - a romantic or sexual relationship, especially a secret one

Etymology

From French 'intrigue', from Italian 'intrigo', from 'intrigare' (to entangle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13954389-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
a secret and complicated plot, often with sinister or dishonest aims
secret and complex plot

Examples

The palace was a place of constant political intrigue and betrayal.
Their intrigue was uncovered before they could carry out their plan.
Tales of intrigue and mystery fascinated the young readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan, especially to achieve something illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or underhand plan

Etymology

From French 'intrigue', from Italian 'intrigo' (entanglement), from 'intricare' (to entangle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05917114-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
to make secret plans, often for dishonest or underhand purposes; to scheme or plot
make secret plans; scheme

Examples

The rival nobles intrigued against the king, creating unrest at court.
She spent years intriguing her way into powerful circles of government.
It was whispered that some employees had intrigued for his dismissal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to make secret plans to achieve something, especially something illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: to conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act

Etymology

From French *intriguer*, from Italian *intricare* (to entangle).
