Yucca gloriosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə ɡloʊˈriːoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ɡlɒˈriːəsə / (BrE)
A species of yucca native to the southeastern United States, similar to the Spanish bayonets but with a shorter trunk and smoother leaves.
a yucca species of the southeastern US

Examples

Yucca gloriosa is often planted in gardens for its striking appearance and hardiness.
The leaves of Yucca gloriosa are smoother than those of the Spanish bayonet.
Botanists identified the unknown plant as Yucca gloriosa after examining its trunk and foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish bayonet - a related species of yucca distinguished by sharp, pointed leaves

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Yucca' and specific epithet 'gloriosa' meaning 'glorious'.
