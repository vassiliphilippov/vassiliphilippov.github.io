incident

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.sɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sɪ.dənt / (BrE)
(of a beam of light, radiation, or particles) falling on or striking a surface.
(physics) falling onto a surface (light, radiation)

Examples

The scientist measured the angle of the incident beam on the surface.
When incident radiation strikes the mirror, part of it is reflected.
In optics, understanding how incident light interacts with materials is crucial for designing lenses.

Synset Examples

incident light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflected - (of light or radiation) thrown back from a surface
  • Antonym: transmitted - (of light) passing through a substance or object

Etymology

From Latin incident- (falling upon), from incidere (to fall into, fall upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02999091-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɪdənt / (BrE)
an event or occurrence, especially one that is unusual, important, or violent
unusual or notable event

Examples

There was a minor incident at the airport that delayed the flight.
The company tried to downplay the security incident in their official report.
She still remembers the incident from her childhood that changed her perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - something that happens, especially a significant occurrence

Etymology

From Latin 'incidentem', present participle of 'incidere' (to fall into, happen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07322095-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪnsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɪdənt / (BrE)
an event, especially one that causes a public disturbance or draws attention, often requiring a response from authorities
public disturbance or notable event

Examples

The police responded quickly to the incident outside the stadium.
There was an incident at the school that disrupted classes for an hour.
Following the incident, security measures were increased throughout the building.

Synset Examples

the police investigated an incident at the bus station

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - a thing that happens, especially one of importance
  • Synonym: disturbance - an interruption of peace or order

Etymology

From Latin 'incidentem', present participle of 'incidere' (to fall into or upon).
