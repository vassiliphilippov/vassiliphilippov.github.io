ragbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌbaɡ / (BrE)
a bag used for collecting and storing rags or scraps of cloth
bag for storing rags or cloth scraps

Examples

She kept an old ragbag in the kitchen for cleaning cloths and towels.
The ragbag was full by the end of the week and needed to be emptied.
His grandmother always carried a small ragbag whenever she was doing chores around the house.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04053184-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌbæɡ / (BrE)
a collection of various unrelated or miscellaneous things; a motley assortment
a miscellaneous collection of things

Examples

His essay was a ragbag of random ideas and unrelated arguments.
The charity shop accepted all sorts of items, resulting in a real ragbag of goods.
She keeps her old sewing materials in a ragbag by the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hodgepodge - a confusing mixture or jumble
  • Synonym: miscellany - a collection of various items

Etymology

From 'rag' + 'bag', originally referring to a bag for storing rags; later used figuratively for any assorted mix.
