Sydney

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪdni / (AmE), / ˈsɪdni / (BrE)
The largest city in Australia, located in southeastern Australia on the Tasman Sea; the capital of New South Wales and Australia's chief port.
largest city in Australia

Examples

She has always wanted to visit Sydney because of its famous Opera House and beautiful harbor.
After moving to Sydney, he found a job in the city center within a week.
The 2000 Summer Olympics were held in Sydney, attracting visitors from around the world.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Townshend, Lord Sydney (1733–1800), British Home Secretary at the time of its founding.
