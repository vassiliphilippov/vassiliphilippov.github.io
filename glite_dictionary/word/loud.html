loud

1. ADJ

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(in music) Indicating that a passage should be played with a high volume or strong intensity.
high volume in music

Examples

The score instructs the orchestra to play this section loud for dramatic effect.
During the crescendo, the choir sang loud to fill the entire hall with sound.
The composer marked the passage loud to emphasize the power of the melody.

Synset Examples

the forte passages in the composition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - loud in music (as a direction to play loudly)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01461579-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
In a manner that is noisy or with a high volume; noisily.
noisily; with high volume (adverb)
informal american

Examples

She spoke so loud that everyone in the next room could hear her.
Please don't play your music so loud at night.
He called loud for help when he saw the fire.

Synset Examples

the band played loudly
she spoke loudly and angrily
he spoke loud enough for those at the back of the room to hear him
cried aloud for help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudly - in a loud manner; with high volume

Etymology

From Old English *hlūd*, related to Old Norse *hljóðr* (noisy).

3. ADJ

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(of colors, clothes, etc.) having a very bright or showy appearance in a way that is considered unpleasant or tasteless.
showy or gaudy in appearance

Examples

His loud shirt made him stand out at the formal event.
The living room was decorated with loud colors that clashed painfully.
She preferred simple jewelry to anything too loud or flashy.

Synset Examples

a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garish - unpleasantly bright or showy
  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy in appearance
Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(of a sound) producing or capable of producing much noise; strong in volume or intensity.
producing much noise; intense sound

Examples

The music at the concert was so loud that I could feel the floor vibrating.
Please keep your voices down; don't be loud in the library.
The thunder was so loud last night that it woke everyone in the house.

Synset Examples

a group of loud children
loud thunder
her voice was too loud
loud trombones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisy - making or given to making a lot of noise
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • Degree Lower: soft - (of a sound) not loud or harsh

Etymology

From Old English hlūd (noisy), related to Old High German hlūt.
