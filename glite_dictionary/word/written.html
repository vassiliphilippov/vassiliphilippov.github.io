written

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪtən / (AmE), / ˈrɪt.ən / (BrE)
recorded or set down in writing, especially in a systematic or official way
recorded in writing, official

Examples

The country's written laws are available for public review.
He prefers written agreements to avoid any misunderstandings.
A written record of the meeting was kept for future reference.

Synset Examples

written laws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oral - spoken rather than written

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'write', from Old English *wrītan* (to score, write).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪtən / (AmE), / ˈrɪtən / (BrE)
expressed or recorded in writing rather than spoken or oral
expressed in writing

Examples

The contract must be written to be legally binding.
She submitted a written complaint to the management.

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɪtən / (AmE), / ˈrɪtən / (BrE)
(of a speech, music, film, play, etc.) composed or created in written form rather than improvised; prepared as a script or text.
composed or scripted for performance or publication

Examples

The script was written by a famous playwright.
She prefers performing written music to improvising onstage.
This movie was written and directed by the same person.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improvised - created without preparation, on the spot

Etymology

Past participle of 'write', from Old English 'wrītan' (to cut, score, write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02220939-a
Synset POS: a
