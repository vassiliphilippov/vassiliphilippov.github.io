coho

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌhəʊ / (BrE)
The pinkish, fatty flesh of a small salmon found in the Pacific Ocean and Great Lakes, often used as food.
pinkish flesh of a type of salmon

Examples

The chef prepared grilled coho with a lemon-dill sauce for dinner.
I prefer the taste of coho to other types of salmon.
Fresh coho is available at the fish market during the summer season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - the flesh of a large edible fish found in northern waters

Etymology

From the Halkomelem (Salishan language) word *k̓ʷəxʷəxʷ*, referring to the coho salmon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊhəʊ / (BrE)
a small salmon native to the northern Pacific coasts and introduced to the Great Lakes, valued as a game and food fish
a small Pacific salmon species

Examples

The fisherman caught a large coho during the early morning run.
Coho are often sought after by anglers for their fighting spirit.
Fresh coho fillets are highly prized in restaurants for their delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye - a species of salmon with red flesh and greenish head
  • Synonym: chinook - the largest species of Pacific salmon

Etymology

From Halkomelem (Salishan language) 'k̓ʷə́xʷə', referring to the fish.

Synset POS: n
