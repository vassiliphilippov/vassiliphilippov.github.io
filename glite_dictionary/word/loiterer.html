loiterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪtərər / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪtərə / (BrE)
a person who stands or moves about without apparent purpose, often in a public place
person who lingers aimlessly in a place

Examples

The loiterer leaned against the wall, watching people pass by without any intention of moving on.
Police questioned a loiterer outside the closed shop late at night.
The park was empty except for a single loiterer near the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaf - to spend time aimlessly or without purpose
  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place

Etymology

From the verb 'loiter', from Middle English 'loiteren', possibly from Middle Dutch 'loteren' (to wag about).
