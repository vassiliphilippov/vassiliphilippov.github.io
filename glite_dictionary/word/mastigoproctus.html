Mastigoproctus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstəɡoʊˈprɑktəs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪɡəʊˈprɒktəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, non-venomous whip scorpions found in the Americas, known for their whip-like tails.
genus of giant whip scorpions

Examples

The Mastigoproctus is noted for its impressive size and distinctive whip-like tail.
Despite its fearsome appearance, the Mastigoproctus is harmless to humans.
Researchers discovered a new species of Mastigoproctus in the tropical forests of Central America.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastix' (whip) + 'proktos' (anus), referring to the shape of the tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪɡoʊˈproʊktəs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪɡəʊˈprɒktəs / (BrE)
A genus of large whip scorpions found in the Americas, known for their elongated tails and harmless nature.
genus of giant whip scorpions

Examples

The Mastigoproctus genus includes some of the largest whip scorpions in the world.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Mastigoproctus group in South America.
Unlike true scorpions, members of Mastigoproctus are harmless to humans.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastigo', meaning 'whip', and 'proktos', meaning 'anus', referring to the tail structure.
