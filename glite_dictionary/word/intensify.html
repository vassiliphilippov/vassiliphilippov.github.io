to intensify

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛn.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛn.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to become more intense, or to make something more intense
become or make more intense

Examples

As the storm approached, the winds began to intensify and the sky turned dark.
Their rivalry continued to intensify throughout the season, leading to a dramatic final match.
Her symptoms seemed to intensify overnight, prompting a visit to the doctor.

Synset Examples

The debate intensified
His dislike for raw fish only deepened in Japan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to weaken - to become less intense or strong

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, strained) + -fy (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226992-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(in photography) to make an area of a negative denser or more opaque to produce greater contrast between light and dark.
increase darkness/contrast in a photographic negative

Examples

Special chemicals are used to intensify the negative and produce sharper prints.
The photographer decided to intensify the image to bring out more detail in the shadows.
Some traditional methods can intensify black areas in film to increase contrast.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00575812-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to become or make something become stronger, more extreme, or more intense
become or make more intense

Examples

The debate began to intensify as more people joined the discussion.
Strong winds will intensify throughout the night, causing hazardous conditions.
Efforts to reduce pollution must intensify if we are to meet our climate goals.

Synset Examples

The Allies escalated the bombing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to escalate - to become or make something become greater or more serious
  • Antonym: to lessen - to become smaller in amount, degree, or intensity

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, intense) + -fy (to make).

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more intense, strong, or marked
make or become more intense

Examples

The storm continued to intensify as it approached the coast.
She tried to intensify her efforts to meet the deadline.
Tensions in the region may intensify if the conflict escalates.

Synset Examples

The efforts were intensified
Her rudeness intensified his dislike for her
Pot smokers claim it heightens their awareness
This event only deepened my convictions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker; opposite of intensify
  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger; synonym for intensify in some contexts

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, tight) + -fy (to make).
