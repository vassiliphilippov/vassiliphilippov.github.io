3. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪlə / (BrE)
a thing or condition that causes death, especially a disease or event resulting in many deaths
something that causes death

Examples

Heart disease is the leading killer in many developed countries.
Malaria remains a major killer in parts of Africa and Asia.
The flu was once a deadly killer before the development of vaccines.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause of death - a reason or condition leading directly to death

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪlə / (BrE)
a person or animal that causes the death of another person or animal, especially deliberately
person or animal that kills

Examples

The police arrested the killer shortly after the crime was committed.
Sharks are often wrongly portrayed as ruthless killers in popular media.
The disease turned out to be a silent killer, claiming thousands of lives each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who unlawfully kills another
  • Synonym: slayer - a person who kills, especially violently
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or killed by another

Etymology

From kill + -er (agent noun suffix).
