Garonne

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈrɒn / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒn / (BrE)
a river in southwest France that rises in the Pyrenees and flows northwest to the Bay of Biscay
river in southwest France

Examples

The Garonne flows through several major cities, including Toulouse and Bordeaux.
During the summer, tourists enjoy boating on the Garonne.
Flooding of the Garonne has historically affected the surrounding communities.

Etymology

From French 'Garonne', from Latin 'Garumna', a river name of ancient origin.
