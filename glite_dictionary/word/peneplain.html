peneplain

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.nəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nɪˌpleɪn / (BrE)
a broad, nearly level land surface formed by prolonged erosion, marking an advanced stage of landscape evolution and little affected by recent earth movements
level land surface shaped by erosion

Examples

Geologists believe that the region was once a peneplain before it was uplifted by tectonic activity.
The broad peneplain stretched for miles, offering few changes in elevation.
Over millions of years, rivers and streams transformed the area into a gently rolling peneplain.

Etymology

From Latin 'paene' (almost) + English 'plain'.
