righteous

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtʃəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtʃəs / (BrE)
Morally justified or right; acting in accordance with what is considered morally good or proper.
morally justified or right

Examples

He felt a righteous anger at the unfair treatment of the workers.
She believed her actions were righteous and for a greater good.
They fought for a righteous cause, seeking justice for all.

Synset Examples

righteous indignation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: just - morally right and fair
  • Antonym: unjust - not morally right or fair

Etymology

From Old English *rihtwīs* (just, righteous), from *riht* (right) + *wīs* (wise, way, manner).

2. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtʃəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtʃəs / (BrE)
(of a person or conduct) morally right or justifiable; following accepted standards of morality or justice.
morally right; justifiable

Examples

Many admired the righteous actions of the leader during the crisis.
She believed her decision was both just and righteous.
The community honored him as a righteous man who stood up for justice.

Synset Examples

the … prayer of a righteous man availeth much

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: wicked - evil or morally wrong

Etymology

From Old English 'rihtwīs' (just, upright), from 'riht' (right) + 'wīs' (manner, wise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02043985-a
Synset POS: a
