psychonomics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies psychological processes using experimental methods
study of psychological processes by experimentation

Examples

He specialized in psychonomics to better understand how humans learn and make decisions.
Advances in psychonomics have deepened our knowledge of memory and perception.
Psychonomics relies on rigorous experimental methods to study cognitive functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experimental psychology - the branch of psychology concerned with the scientific investigation of basic psychological processes

Etymology

Formed from 'psycho-' (mind) and '-nomics' (from Greek nomos, law).
