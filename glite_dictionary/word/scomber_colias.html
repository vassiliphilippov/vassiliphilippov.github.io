Scomber colias

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːmbər koʊˈlaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈskɒmbə kəʊˈlaɪ.əs / (BrE)
A medium-sized mackerel found in temperate regions of the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico, also known as the Atlantic chub mackerel.
a species of mackerel (Atlantic chub mackerel)

Examples

Fishermen in Portugal often catch Scomber colias during the summer season.
The Scomber colias prefers temperate waters along the coastlines of the Atlantic Ocean.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Scomber colias to better understand its population dynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a common name for several species of pelagic fish in the family Scombridae

Etymology

From Latin: 'Scomber' meaning 'mackerel', and 'colias' referring to a group within the mackerel family.
