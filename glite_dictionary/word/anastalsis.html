anastalsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈstæl.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈstal.sɪs / (BrE)
reverse muscular movement in the alimentary tract, opposite to peristalsis
reverse intestinal muscle movement
medical

Examples

In rare disorders, anastalsis may cause the contents of the intestine to move backward.
The doctor explained how anastalsis differs from the more common process of peristalsis.
Researchers observed anastalsis in animal models when studying reflux conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peristalsis - wave-like muscle contractions moving contents through the digestive tract

Etymology

From Greek ana- (up, again) + stalsis (contraction).
