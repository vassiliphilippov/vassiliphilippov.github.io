radiocommunication

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊkəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the transmission and reception of messages using radio waves as a medium
communication by radio waves

Examples

Modern ships rely on radiocommunication for sending distress signals across long distances.
The development of radiocommunication transformed the way people connect globally.
International agreements govern the use of radiocommunication frequencies to prevent interference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio - system for transmitting sound or signals via electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From radio- (referring to radio waves) + communication.
