Piciformes

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsɪˌfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪˌfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds including woodpeckers, toucans, barbets, honeyguides, jacamars, and puffbirds
order of birds (woodpeckers, toucans, etc.)

Examples

The order Piciformes includes a diverse range of birds such as woodpeckers and toucans.
Toucans, known for their large colorful bills, are members of the Piciformes.
Scientists study the evolutionary relationships within Piciformes to better understand bird diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird known for pecking wood
  • Synonym: tucan - a brightly colored bird of tropical America with a large bill

Etymology

From Latin 'picus' (woodpecker) + '-formes' (having the form of), used in scientific taxonomy.
