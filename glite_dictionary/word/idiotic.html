idiotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdiˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdiˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
extremely foolish or showing a complete lack of good judgment; insanely irresponsible.
extremely foolish; showing a lack of good judgment

Examples

It was an idiotic mistake to leave the door unlocked all night.
He made an idiotic comment during the important meeting, embarrassing everyone.
Running into traffic without looking is completely idiotic.

Synset Examples

an idiotic idea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensible - showing good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Greek idiōtikos (unskilled, ignorant), via French idiote and Latin idioticus.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪdiˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdiˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
extremely foolish, unreasonable, or lacking in common sense; deserving ridicule or derision.
extremely foolish or unreasonable

Examples

It was idiotic to try to cross the busy road without looking both ways.
She dismissed his proposal as completely idiotic and unworkable.
Making such an idiotic mistake in the final match cost the team their championship.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate
  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting mockery or derision; absurd
  • Antonym: sensible - chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence; likely to be of benefit

Etymology

From Greek idiōtikós (private, unskilled), via French idiotique and late Latin idioticus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02581199-s
Synset POS: s
