umlaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈuːm.laʊt / (AmE), / ˈʊm.laʊt / (BrE)
A diacritical mark (two dots) placed over a vowel, especially in Germanic languages, to indicate a change in sound.
diacritical mark (two dots) over a vowel

Examples

In German, the letter 'a' with an umlaut is pronounced differently than a regular 'a'.
The word 'naïve' in English sometimes uses a diacritical similar to an umlaut, but it is actually a diaeresis.
Some Scandinavian languages also use the umlaut in their alphabets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaeresis - two dots placed over a vowel, usually to mark separate pronunciation

Etymology

From German Umlaut (um- 'around' + Laut 'sound').
