Magnolia macrophylla

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliə ˌmæk.rəˈfɪl.i.ə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə ˌmæk.rəˈfɪl.i.ə / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its huge leaves arranged in dense whorls and large creamy flowers with purple at the base.
large tree with huge leaves and creamy flowers

Examples

The Magnolia macrophylla in our garden produces spectacular blooms every spring.
Botanists are fascinated by the enormous leaves of the Magnolia macrophylla.
We spotted a rare Magnolia macrophylla while hiking through the southern forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnolia - any tree or shrub of the magnolia genus, known for large fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'macrophylla' meaning 'large-leaved', from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
