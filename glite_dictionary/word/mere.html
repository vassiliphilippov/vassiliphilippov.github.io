mere

1. NOUN

/ mɪr / (AmE), / mɪə / (BrE)
a small lake or pond, especially one that is shallow or stagnant
small pond or lake
british

Examples

They spotted several ducks swimming in the nearby mere.
The old abbey stood by a quiet mere shrouded in morning mist.
Legend says a sword was cast into the mysterious mere centuries ago.

Etymology

Old English *mere* (lake, pool), related to Dutch *meer* and German *Meer* (sea).

2. ADJ

/ mɪr / (AmE), / mɪə / (BrE)
used to emphasize how small or insignificant something is, or that nothing is added or involved apart from what is mentioned
nothing more than; only

Examples

Her success was due to the mere fact that she worked harder than anyone else.
He trembled at the mere thought of speaking in public.
The mere mention of his name makes her smile.

Synset Examples

only the bare facts
shocked by the mere idea
the simple passage of time was enough
the simple truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheer - used to emphasize the size, degree, or amount of something
  • Synonym: bare - used to emphasize that only a particular thing or person is involved

Etymology

From Latin 'merus' (undiluted, pure).

3. ADJ

/ mɪr / (AmE), / mɪə / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is small, unimportant, or is nothing more than what is stated
nothing more than specified

Examples

He was a mere child when he started playing the piano.
A mere handful of people turned up for the meeting.
It was a mere formality, but it still had to be completed.

Synset Examples

a mere child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: only - used to indicate that no more than what is mentioned is involved
  • Synonym: sheer - used to emphasize the large amount, degree, or extent of something

Etymology

From Latin 'merus' (pure, unmixed), through Old French 'mer(e)'.
