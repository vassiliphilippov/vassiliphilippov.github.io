gao lak

1. NOUN

/ ɡaʊ ˈlɑk / (AmE), / ɡaʊ ˈlak / (BrE)
A type of food, typically chestnuts, often sold roasted at street markets, especially in Vietnam.
roasted chestnut snack (Vietnamese street food)

Examples

Every winter, vendors sell hot gao lak from their street carts.
Tourists love sampling gao lak while exploring the night markets.
She bought a bag of gao lak to snack on as she walked through the bustling market.

Synset Examples

He was excited to try the gao lak at the street market.

Etymology

From Vietnamese 'gạo lắc', meaning 'shaken rice', but in street food often refers to roasted chestnuts.
