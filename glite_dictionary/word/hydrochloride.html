hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a compound formed when an organic base combines with hydrochloric acid, typically used to make certain drugs more water-soluble
compound of an organic base and hydrochloric acid
medical

Examples

The medication is available in its hydrochloride form to improve absorption.
Lidocaine hydrochloride is commonly used as a local anesthetic.
Many pharmaceutical drugs are sold as their hydrochloride salts.

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water or hydrogen) and chloride (a compound of chlorine).
