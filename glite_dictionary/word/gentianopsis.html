Gentianopsis

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnʃiˈænɑpsɪs / (AmE), / dʒɛnʃɪˈanəpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known as fringed gentians, closely related to the genus Gentiana.
genus of fringed gentians (plants)

Examples

Several species within Gentianopsis are prized for their striking blue flowers.
The botanist specialized in the identification of Gentianopsis in North America.
Unlike some relatives, Gentianopsis typically has fringed petals at the edges of the flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gentiana - a related genus of gentians

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'gentiana' (gentian) + 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃi.əˈnɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃi.əˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the gentian family, known as fringed gentians, distinguished by their fringed petals.
a genus of fringed gentians (plants)

Examples

The Gentianopsis blooms add vibrant colors to alpine meadows in late summer.
Botanists studied the pollination mechanisms of several Gentianopsis species.
Many wildflower enthusiasts seek out Gentianopsis for its distinctive fringed petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gentiana - a related genus of plants in the gentian family

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Gentiana' (a plant genus) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).
