Argiopidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌdʒaɪˈɑpɪdi / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒaɪˈɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiders that spin orb-shaped webs and are found worldwide.
family of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

Members of the Argiopidae build intricate orb webs to catch their prey.
The Argiopidae are studied in zoology for their fascinating web designs.
With a cosmopolitan distribution, Argiopidae can be found on every continent except Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araneidae - family of orb-weaving spiders, modern classification

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name 'Argiope' + '-idae', a common suffix for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˌdʒaɪˈɒpɪdi / (AmE), / ˌɑːdʒɪˈɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of spiders, known as orb-weavers, that spin wheel-shaped webs and are found worldwide
family of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

The Argiopidae are known for constructing highly organized orb webs in gardens and forests.
Many species in the Argiopidae family can be recognized by their bright colors and unique patterns.
Research on Argiopidae has contributed to our understanding of spider web architecture worldwide.

Etymology

From the genus name Argiope, from Greek argios (shining) + ops (eye or face).
