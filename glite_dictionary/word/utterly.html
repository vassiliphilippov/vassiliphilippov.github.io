utterly

1. ADV

/ ˈʌtɚli / (AmE), / ˈʌtəli / (BrE)
completely; to the fullest extent possible; used to emphasize the extreme degree of something
completely; to the fullest extent

Examples

The room was utterly silent after the announcement.
She was utterly baffled by the complex instructions.
His explanation proved to be utterly false.

Synset Examples

an absolutely magnificent painting
a perfectly idiotic idea
you're perfectly right
utterly miserable
you can be dead sure of my innocence
was dead tired
dead right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - entirely; to the full extent
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not completely
  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally; used for emphasis

Etymology

From Middle English uttren (to utter) + -ly (adverbial suffix), related to 'utter' meaning 'complete, absolute'.
