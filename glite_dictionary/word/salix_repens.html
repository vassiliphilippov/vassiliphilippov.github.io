Salix repens

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks ˈriːpənz / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks ˈriːpɛnz / (BrE)
A small, low-growing shrub native to Europe and Asia, with straggling branches and silky green leaves, commonly known as creeping willow.
small European and Asian shrub; creeping willow

Examples

The Salix repens is often planted in gardens for ground cover due to its trailing habit.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Salix repens to understand its adaptation to sandy soils.
Several varieties of Salix repens are cultivated for their ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping willow - common name for Salix repens

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning 'willow' and 'repens' meaning 'creeping'.
