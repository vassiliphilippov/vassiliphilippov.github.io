censure

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.ʃə / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval of someone or something, especially in a formal statement
formally express strong disapproval

Examples

The committee decided to censure the senator for his unethical behavior.
The Board publicly censured the CEO over the financial irregularities.
She was censured by her peers for failing to follow the agreed procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to criticize or rebuke someone, especially officially
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Latin censura (judgment, censorship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɛnʃə / (BrE)
the formal expression of severe disapproval or criticism, especially in an official context
harsh criticism or official disapproval

Examples

The senator faced strong censure from his colleagues after the scandal.
Public censure can damage a person's reputation for years.
After the mistake, the board issued a formal censure against the manager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemnation - the act of declaring something as wrong or evil; strong disapproval
  • Antonym: approval - the act of accepting or agreeing with something; positive judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'censura' meaning 'judgment, assessment', from 'censor'.

Synset POS: n
