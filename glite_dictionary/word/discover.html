to discover

1. VERB

/ dɪˈskʌvɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvə / (BrE)
to become aware of something, often unexpectedly or by accident
become aware, often by accident

Examples

I was surprised to discover that my neighbor speaks five languages.
She discovered her mistake only after submitting the assignment.
He discovers something new about his family history every year.

Synset Examples

I learned that she has two grown-up children
I see that you have been promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find out - to learn something new, especially something hidden or unknown
  • Degree Higher: to discover - to find something for the first time

Etymology

From Old French 'descovrir' (to uncover), from Latin 'dis-' (opposite of) + 'cooperire' (to cover).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00600349-v
Synset POS: v

/ dɪˈskʌvɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvə / (BrE)
to identify or recognize (a species, element, etc.) for the first time, especially in scientific fields such as biology or botany

Examples

Scientists were thrilled to discover a new species of orchid in the rainforest.

  • Synonym: find - to discover or discern something previously unknown
