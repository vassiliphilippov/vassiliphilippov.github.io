weasel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwizəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːzl̩ / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as treacherous or sneaky, especially one who is deceitful or evasive
a deceitful or sneaky person

Examples

Everyone knew Tom was a weasel after he lied to cover up his mistakes.
She warned me not to trust him—he's a real weasel when it comes to business deals.
Don't be a weasel and try to get out of your responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak - a person who acts secretly or treacherously
  • Synonym: rat - a person who betrays others, especially by informing
  • Antonym: straight-shooter - a frank and honest person

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'wesle', related to the animal known for its slyness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːzəl / (BrE)
a small carnivorous mammal with a slender, elongated body, short legs, and typically brown or white fur, belonging to the genus Mustela
small elongated carnivorous mammal

Examples

A weasel darted across the path and disappeared into the undergrowth.
Farmers sometimes have trouble keeping weasels away from their chicken coops.
The weasel is known for its agility and sharp hunting skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoat - a related carnivorous mammal, also called the ermine

Etymology

From Old English 'weosule', related to Old High German 'wisula' (weasel).
