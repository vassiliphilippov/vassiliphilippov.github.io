nonelected

1. ADJ

/ nʌnɪˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / nʌnɪˈlɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a position or office) filled by appointment rather than by election.
not filled by election

Examples

The council appointed a nonelected official to fill the vacant position.
Many government agencies are run by nonelected administrators.
He believed that nonelected positions should have less authority than those chosen by the public.

Synset Examples

a nonelective office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective - chosen by election

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) + 'elected'.
