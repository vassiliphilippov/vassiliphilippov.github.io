cautiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careful to avoid potential danger or problems
the quality of being cautious

Examples

His cautiousness helped him avoid making costly mistakes in his investments.
The doctor's cautiousness reassured her patients during the risky procedure.
Her cautiousness was sometimes mistaken for indecision by her colleagues.

Synset Examples

a man of caution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - careful forethought to avoid danger or risk
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of regard for danger or consequences

Etymology

From cautious + -ness, forming a noun of state from the adjective.
