hypophysectomy

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɑːfəˈsɛktəmi / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒfɪˈsɛktəmi / (BrE)
the surgical removal of the pituitary gland, usually to treat certain diseases or tumors
surgical removal of the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a hypophysectomy to treat the patient's pituitary tumor.
Following his hypophysectomy, the patient required lifelong hormone replacement therapy.
A hypophysectomy is sometimes performed when medications fail to control pituitary disorders.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypophysis' (pituitary gland) + '-ectomy' (surgical removal).
