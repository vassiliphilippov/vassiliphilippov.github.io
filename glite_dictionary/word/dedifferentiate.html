to dedifferentiate

1. VERB

/ diˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / diˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) to lose its specialized characteristics and revert to a more primitive or unspecialized state.
lose specialized form or function (biology)
medical

Examples

Certain cancer cells can dedifferentiate and begin dividing uncontrollably.
Some plants have the ability to dedifferentiate their cells for regeneration after injury.
Under stress, the muscle tissue may dedifferentiate and lose its contractile properties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to differentiate - to develop specialized features or functions (biology)

Etymology

From de- (reversal) + differentiate, modeled after scientific terminology.
