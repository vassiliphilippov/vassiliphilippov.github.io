tidiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪd.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being neat, orderly, and well-organized
the quality of being neat and orderly

Examples

Her tidiness impressed everyone who visited her home.
He was praised for his tidiness at work, always keeping his desk organized.
Maintaining tidiness in common spaces is important in shared living arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being neat and free from clutter
  • Antonym: messiness - the quality of being untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From tidy + -ness, with 'tidy' from Old English 'tidig' (timely, neat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdi nəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdi nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or habit of being tidy; the state of being neat and orderly
state of being tidy or neat

Examples

Her tidiness was apparent in every corner of the immaculately arranged room.
The teacher praised the student's tidiness in keeping his desk organized.
Some people value tidiness more than others and find clutter distracting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orderliness - the quality of being orderly and well arranged
  • Antonym: messiness - the state of being untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From tidy + -ness, where 'tidy' derives from Middle English 'tidy' (timely, in order).
