aplomb

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɑm / (AmE), / əˈplɒm / (BrE)
self-confidence and assurance, especially when in a demanding situation; composure under pressure
self-confidence under pressure

Examples

She accepted the challenging role with remarkable aplomb and grace.
Despite the chaos, the surgeon performed the operation with aplomb.
He answered every awkward question from the reporters with complete aplomb.

Synset Examples

keep your cool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-control, especially under strain
  • Antonym: nervousness - state of being anxious or lacking confidence

Etymology

From French à plomb (literally 'according to the plummet') referring to being upright, steady.
