Alsophila

1. NOUN

/ ælˈsɑːfɪlə / (AmE), / ælˈsɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of geometrid moths characterized by pronounced sexual dimorphism, with females having greatly reduced wings.
genus of moths with dimorphic females

Examples

The larvae of Alsophila feed on a variety of plants during the spring.
In Alsophila, females have much smaller wings compared to the males, making them flightless.
Entomologists studied the sexual dimorphism found in Alsophila species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌælˈsɒfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌalˈsɒfɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of geometrid moths noted for pronounced sexual dimorphism, with females exhibiting significantly reduced wings
genus of moths with wing-reduced females

Examples

The larvae of Alsophila feed primarily on deciduous trees in temperate forests.
Researchers observed that female Alsophila moths are rarely seen flying due to their reduced wings.
The distribution of Alsophila extends across several continents, particularly in wooded regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek alsos (grove) and phila (loving).
