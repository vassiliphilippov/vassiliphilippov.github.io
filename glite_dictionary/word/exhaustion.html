exhaustion

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɔstʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːs.tʃən / (BrE)
a state of extreme physical or mental tiredness; complete fatigue
extreme tiredness or fatigue

Examples

After running the marathon, she collapsed from sheer exhaustion.
The team's exhaustion was evident in their slow movements and drooping shoulders.
Working late every night eventually led to complete exhaustion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue - weariness or tiredness
  • Antonym: refreshment - the feeling of being restored or energized

Etymology

From Latin 'exhaustio', from 'exhaurire' meaning 'to draw out'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɑstʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːs.tʃən / (BrE)
A state of extreme physical or mental tiredness resulting from intense activity or prolonged effort.
extreme tiredness or depletion

Examples

After working a twelve-hour shift, she collapsed from sheer exhaustion.
The team reached the summit in a state of complete exhaustion.
Continuous stress can lead to both mental and physical exhaustion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue - extreme tiredness, typically from mental or physical exertion
  • Antonym: energy - capacity to do work or vigorous activity

Etymology

From Latin exhaustio (a draining off), from exhaurire (to draw off, drain out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07345810-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɔs.tʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːs.tʃən / (BrE)
the act of using something completely so that nothing remains
state of being used up

Examples

The exhaustion of natural resources is a growing global concern.
After hours of intense debate, there was a sense of exhaustion in the room.
Continuous use can lead to the exhaustion of the device's battery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depletion - reduction in the number or quantity of something
  • Antonym: repletion - the act of being filled or replenished

Etymology

From Latin exhaurire (to empty out), via French exhaustion.
