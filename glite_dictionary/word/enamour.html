to enamour

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnæmɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈnæmə / (BrE)
to cause someone to be filled with love or admiration (often used as 'be enamoured of/with')
fill with love or admiration

Examples

The charming storyteller enamoured the entire audience with his tales.
She was quickly enamoured of the bustling city life.
The actor's performance enamoured critics and fans alike.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of someone
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or force back; to be distasteful

Etymology

From Old French 'enamourer', from 'en-' (in) + 'amour' (love).
