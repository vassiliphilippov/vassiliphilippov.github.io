Jacquinia armillaris

1. NOUN

/ dʒækˈwɪni.ə ɑrˈmɪlərɪs / (AmE), / dʒækˈwɪni.ə ɑːˈmɪlərɪs / (BrE)
A small shrub or tree native to the West Indies, with hard, glossy, yellow-and-brown patterned seeds that are used to make bracelets.
West Indian shrub/tree with decorative seeds

Examples

The seeds of the Jacquinia armillaris are often used to make beautiful bracelets.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Jacquinia armillaris in its native habitat.
The glossy seeds of the Jacquinia armillaris caught the attention of local artisans.
