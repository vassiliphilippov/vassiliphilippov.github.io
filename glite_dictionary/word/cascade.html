cascade

1. VERB

/ kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kæˈskeɪd / (BrE)
(of liquid, objects, or information) to flow or fall quickly and in large amounts, like a waterfall.
to flow or fall in large quantities

Examples

The water began to cascade over the rocks after the heavy rain.
Her long hair cascaded down her back in waves.
As the fire alarm sounded, people cascaded down the stairwell to reach safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to flow rapidly in a steady stream
  • Degree Lower: trickle - to flow in a small stream or in drops

Etymology

From French 'cascade', from Italian 'cascata' (waterfall), from 'cascare' (to fall).

2. NOUN

/ kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kasˈkeɪd / (BrE)
A sudden, plentiful flow of something, such as tears or sparks, resembling the rush of a waterfall.
sudden outpouring of something

Examples

A cascade of sparks erupted from the fireworks display, illuminating the night sky.
She broke down, and a cascade of tears streamed down her face.
There was a cascade of laughter when the comedian delivered his punchline.

Synset Examples

a little shower of rose petals
a sudden cascade of sparks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torrent - a strong, fast flow of liquid or emotion

Etymology

From French *cascade*, from Italian *cascata* (waterfall), from *cascare* (to fall).

3. NOUN

/ kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kasˈkeɪd / (BrE)
A small waterfall, or a series of small waterfalls, typically one of several that fall in stages down a steep rocky slope.
small waterfall or waterfalls

Examples

The hikers were delighted to discover a beautiful cascade hidden in the forest.
A gentle cascade flowed over the moss-covered rocks, creating a soothing sound.
Small fish darted beneath the cool water of the cascade as it tumbled into the pool below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfall - A steep fall or flow of water in a river or stream

Etymology

From French 'cascade', from Italian 'cascata', from 'cascare' (to fall).

5. VERB

/ kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kæsˈkeɪd / (BrE)
to arrange open windows on a computer desktop so that they overlap in a stepped fashion, leaving the title bars visible
arrange windows to overlap with visible title bars

Examples

To organize her workspace, she decided to cascade all the open documents.
Click 'Window' and select 'Cascade' to cascade the application windows for easier access.
Some users prefer to cascade their browser tabs for a clearer overview.

Etymology

From waterfall sense, metaphorically referring to the stepped appearance.
