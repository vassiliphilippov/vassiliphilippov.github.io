canalis cervicis uteri

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪlɪs ˌsɜːrˈviːs juːˈtɛraɪ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪlɪs ˌsɜːvɪs juːˈtɛraɪ / (BrE)
A spindle-shaped canal in the cervix that extends from the uterus to the vagina.
cervical canal in the female reproductive system
medical

Examples

The canalis cervicis uteri is lined with mucus-secreting cells to protect the uterus from infections.
During certain medical procedures, access to the canalis cervicis uteri is required.
Blockage of the canalis cervicis uteri can lead to complications in reproductive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cervical canal - the passageway running through the cervix from the uterus to the vagina

Etymology

From Latin: 'canalis' (canal), 'cervicis' (of the neck/cervix), and 'uteri' (of the uterus).
