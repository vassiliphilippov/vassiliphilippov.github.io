adducting

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʌktɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈdʌktɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially of muscles) bringing together or drawing toward the midline of the body or toward an adjacent part.
drawing toward the body's midline (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The adducting motion of the thigh is controlled by specific muscles in the inner leg.
Damage to the nerve may impair a person's ability to make adducting movements with their fingers.
During certain exercises, adducting the arms helps strengthen the chest muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abducting - moving away from the midline of the body (of muscles)

Etymology

From Latin adducere, meaning 'to lead toward'.
