Luxembourg

1. PROPN

/ ˈlʌksəmˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / ˈlʌksəmbɜːɡ / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of the country of Luxembourg, located in western Europe, known for its fortified medieval old town.
capital city of Luxembourg (country)

Examples

She visited Luxembourg to explore its historic city center.
The European Court of Justice is based in Luxembourg.
Many banks have their headquarters in Luxembourg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luxembourg (country) - a small landlocked country in western Europe

Etymology

From Luxembourgish 'Lëtzebuerg', from Old High German 'Lucilinburhuc' (little castle).

2. PROPN

/ ˈlʌksəmˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈlʌksəmbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A small country in northwestern Europe, officially a grand duchy, bordered by France, Belgium, and Germany, known as an international financial center.
country in northwestern Europe

Examples

My friend recently traveled to Luxembourg to attend a banking conference.
The picturesque countryside of Luxembourg attracts tourists from all over the world.
Luxembourg is known for its medieval old town, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Etymology

From French Luxembourg, from Luxembourgish Lëtzebuerg, related to Latin Luciliburgum.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08980760-n
Synset POS: n
