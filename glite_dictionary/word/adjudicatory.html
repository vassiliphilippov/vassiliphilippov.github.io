adjudicatory

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒuː.dɪ.kəˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / əˈdʒuː.dɪ.kəˌtɔː.ri / (BrE)
relating to the process of adjudicating or making a formal judgment or decision about a problem or disputed matter, especially in a legal context
relating to formal judgment or adjudication
legal

Examples

The committee held an adjudicatory hearing to resolve the dispute.
An adjudicatory body determines whether a law has been violated.
She specializes in adjudicatory procedures within regulatory agencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judicial - relating to courts or the administration of justice

Etymology

From Latin 'adjudicare' (to award or assign judicially) + English suffix '-ory'.
