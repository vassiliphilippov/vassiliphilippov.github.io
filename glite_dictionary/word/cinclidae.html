Cinclidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋklɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋklɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small aquatic birds commonly known as dippers or water ouzels, found near fast-flowing streams.
family of aquatic birds (dippers, water ouzels)

Examples

The family Cinclidae is unique among passerine birds for its aquatic habits.
Members of the Cinclidae are often seen diving into cold mountain streams in search of insects.
Ornithologists have extensively studied the breeding behavior of the Cinclidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dipper - a small aquatic songbird found near fast-moving streams
  • Synonym: water ouzel - another name for dipper, a small aquatic bird

Etymology

From scientific Latin Cinclidae, based on Greek kinklos (a kind of bird).
