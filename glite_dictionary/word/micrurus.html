Micrurus

1. PROPN

/ maɪˈkruːrəs / (AmE), / mɪˈkrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous coral snakes found in the Americas, known for their brightly colored bands of red, yellow, and black.
genus of venomous coral snakes

Examples

The Micrurus genus includes many species of brightly colored coral snakes.
Researchers studied the venom composition of several Micrurus species.
Unlike some other snakes, Micrurus are typically reclusive and non-aggressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a highly venomous snake with distinct color bands

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + oura (tail), referring to the small tail of these snakes.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈkruːrəs / (AmE), / mɪˈkrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored, highly venomous coral snakes found in the Americas.
genus of coral snakes

Examples

The Micrurus genus includes some of the most venomous snakes in the Americas.
A member of the Micrurus genus was found in the Brazilian rainforest.
Herpetologists study the coloration patterns of Micrurus to understand warning signals in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a common name for venomous snakes of the Micrurus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the snake's slender build.
