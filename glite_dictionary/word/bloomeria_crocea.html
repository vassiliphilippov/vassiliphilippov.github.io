Bloomeria crocea

1. NOUN

/ bluːˈmɪəriə ˈkroʊsiə / (AmE), / bluːˈmɪəriːə ˈkrəʊsiə / (BrE)
A Californian plant with grasslike leaves and showy orange flowers, belonging to the lily family.
Californian plant with orange flowers

Examples

Many hikers admire the vibrant orange blossoms of the Bloomeria crocea each spring.
The Bloomeria crocea thrives in dry, open grasslands throughout central and southern California.
Botanists often refer to Bloomeria crocea as the 'golden star' because of its star-shaped flowers.

Etymology

Genus name 'Bloomeria' honors Hiram Green Bloomer, an American botanist; 'crocea' from Latin for 'saffron-colored'.
