Arethusa bulbosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈθjuːzə bʌlˈboʊsə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈθjuːzə bʌlˈbəʊsə / (BrE)
A North American bog orchid with a single fragrant magenta-pink flower featuring a broad, open corolla.
a North American bog orchid species

Examples

The rare Arethusa bulbosa can be found blooming in peat bogs during late spring.
Botanists admire the striking magenta color of Arethusa bulbosa flowers.
We spotted a solitary Arethusa bulbosa during our hike in the wetlands.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek mythology, Arethusa a nymph, and Latin bulbosa meaning 'bulbous'.
