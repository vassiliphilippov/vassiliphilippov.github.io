asleep

1. ADJ

/ əˈslip / (AmE), / əˈsliːp / (BrE)
In a state of sleep; not awake.
in a state of sleep

Examples

The children were already asleep when their parents returned home.
He fell asleep during the long movie.
By midnight, I was completely asleep after a busy day.

Synset Examples

were all asleep when the phone rang
fell asleep at the wheel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: awake - not in a state of sleep

Etymology

Old English on slæpe (in sleep), from 'on' (in) + 'slæpe' (sleep).

3. ADJ

/ əˈslip / (AmE), / əˈsliːp / (BrE)
(of a limb or body part) numb or lacking sensation, usually as a result of temporary interference with blood flow or nerve signals.
numb; lacking sensation in a limb

Examples

After sitting awkwardly, my hand fell asleep and I couldn't feel my fingers.
She shook her leg because it had gone asleep during the long meeting.
If your foot is asleep, wait a few moments for the tingling to go away.

Synset Examples

my foot is asleep
numb with cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - deprived of the power to feel or move normally
