Mata Hari

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑtə ˈhɑri / (AmE), / ˌmɑːtə ˈhɑːri / (BrE)
a Dutch dancer and courtesan, executed by the French as a German spy during World War I (1876–1917); her name is often used to refer to a seductive female spy
notorious Dutch dancer and spy in World War I

Examples

The film portrayed her as a modern Mata Hari, skilled in the art of deception.
Historians still debate whether Mata Hari was truly guilty of espionage or unjustly convicted.
The mysterious life of Mata Hari continues to fascinate readers and filmmakers alike.

Etymology

Stage name of Margaretha Geertruida Zelle; from Malay, meaning ‘eye of the day’ (i.e., the sun).
