Thevetia neriifolia

1. NOUN

/ θəˈviːtiə nɛriəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / θəˈviːtiə nɛriˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree with glossy dark green leaves and fragrant yellow to orange or peach-colored flowers; all parts are highly poisonous.
poisonous shrub or small tree, Thevetia neriifolia

Examples

All parts of Thevetia neriifolia are highly toxic if ingested.
Gardeners are advised to handle Thevetia neriifolia with care due to its poisonous sap.
The bright flowers of Thevetia neriifolia make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical regions.

Etymology

From the genus Thevetia, named after French botanist André Thévet, and Latin neriifolia meaning 'with leaves like Nerium' (oleander).
