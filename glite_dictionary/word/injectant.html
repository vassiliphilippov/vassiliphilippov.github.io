injectant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛktənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛktənt / (BrE)
a substance or solution that is injected, especially into the body or skin
substance for injection
medical

Examples

The doctor labeled the injectant before giving the patient an injection.
All syringes must be filled with the sterile injectant supplied by the pharmacy.
A yellowish tint in the injectant indicated contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injection - the act of injecting or something that is injected

Etymology

From Latin 'injectus' (thrown in), with English noun-forming suffix '-ant'.
