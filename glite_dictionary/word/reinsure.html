to reinsure

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to insure again, especially by transferring all or part of an insurance risk to another insurance company
to insure an insurance risk again

Examples

The company decided to reinsure part of its portfolio to reduce exposure to large claims.
After a major loss, insurers often reinsure with companies that specialize in catastrophic risks.
Smaller firms typically reinsure liabilities they cannot handle alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insure - to provide or obtain insurance against loss or harm

Etymology

From re- (again) + insure, after the pattern of related legal/financial terms.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to insure again by assuming all or part of the liability from another insurance company that has already covered a risk
to insure again from another insurer

Examples

The company decided to reinsure the risks it had taken on for extra security.
After the major hurricane, several firms opted to reinsure their clients' policies.
To mitigate potential losses, the insurer chose to reinsure a portion of its portfolio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insure - to provide insurance coverage against loss or damage

Etymology

From re- (again) + insure, meaning to insure again or additionally.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00893062-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to arrange for insurance to be insured again by another company, in order to reduce risk
arrange additional insurance by another insurer

Examples

After issuing a large policy, the company decided to reinsure part of it with another firm.
Many insurers reinsure high-risk policies to protect themselves from significant losses.
The company has reinsured most of its earthquake coverage to minimize exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insure - to provide insurance for; to cover against risk
  • Synonym: to underwrite - to take on financial risk, such as for an insurance policy

Etymology

From re- (again) + insure, patterned on the formation of 'reinsurance.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02256317-v
Synset POS: v
