evasive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
tending to avoid giving direct or clear answers, often so as to hide the truth or avoid commitment
deliberately vague or indirect

Examples

When asked about the missing funds, the politician gave an evasive response that failed to answer the main question.
Her evasive remarks left everyone unsure about her true intentions.
Rather than admitting his mistake, he became increasingly evasive during the meeting.

Synset Examples

his answers were brief, constrained and evasive
an evasive statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - saying things in a clear and straightforward way
  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'evadere' (to escape), via Old French 'evasif'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
tending to avoid or escape from difficulty or danger, particularly by quick or clever action
avoiding danger or difficulty

Examples

The pilot made an evasive maneuver to avoid the incoming missile.
She gave an evasive answer when asked about her involvement in the incident.
Firefighters had to take evasive action as the building started to collapse.

Synset Examples

pilots are taught to take evasive action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elusive - difficult to find, catch, or achieve

Etymology

From Latin evasivus (tending to escape), from evadere (to escape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01894383-s
Synset POS: s
