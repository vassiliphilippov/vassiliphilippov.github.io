pharmacist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːrməsɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməsɪst / (BrE)
a person trained and licensed to prepare and dispense medicines and to provide information about their proper use
person who dispenses medicines

Examples

The pharmacist answered my questions about the medication's side effects.
You should ask the pharmacist if these pills can be taken with food.
Every pharmacist must keep accurate records of all controlled substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemist - a person qualified to prepare and dispense medicinal drugs, especially in the UK

Etymology

From French *pharmacien* and medieval Latin *pharmacista*, from Greek *pharmakon* (drug, medicine).
