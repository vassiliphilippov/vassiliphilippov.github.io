undress

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to remove some or all of the clothes from (oneself or another person)
remove clothing

Examples

The doctor asked the child to undress for the examination.
She helped her grandmother undress before getting into bed.
After a long day, he was eager to undress and relax.

Synset Examples

The nurse quickly undressed the accident victim
She divested herself of her outdoor clothes
He disinvested himself of his garments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dress - to put clothes on oneself or another
  • Synonym: to disrobe - to remove one's clothes

Etymology

From un- (not, opposite of) + dress, originally meaning to divest of clothing.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdrɛs / (BrE)
the state of wearing no or only partial clothing; being partly or completely naked
state of partial or complete nakedness

Examples

The children were caught in a state of undress when the fire alarm sounded.
He quickly covered himself, embarrassed to be seen in such undress.
Guests are not permitted in the pool area while in undress.

Synset Examples

a state of undress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nakedness - the state of being without clothes
  • Antonym: dress - the state of wearing clothes; opposite of undress

Etymology

From un- (not) + dress (to clothe); noun use dates from 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14480200-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to take off one's clothes; to remove clothing from oneself or another
remove clothing; take off clothes

Examples

Please don't undress in front of everybody!
He quickly undressed and put on his pajamas before bed.
She began undressing for her shower while talking on the phone.

Synset Examples

please don't undress in front of everybody!
She strips in front of strangers every night for a living

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dress - to put clothes on oneself or someone else
  • Synonym: to disrobe - to remove one's clothes, especially formally or as a stage act
  • Synonym: strip - to remove all or most of one's clothes

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + dress (to put clothes on).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00049617-v
Synset POS: v
