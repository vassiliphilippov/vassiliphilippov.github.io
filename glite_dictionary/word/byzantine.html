Byzantine

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈzænˌtiːn / (AmE), / bɪˈzæntiːn / (BrE)
relating to the Eastern Orthodox Church, its traditions, or the rites and liturgies practiced within it
of the Eastern Orthodox Church or its rites

Examples

The Byzantine liturgy is celebrated on special feast days in the church.
She specialized in the study of Byzantine religious art and icons.
The monastery still follows many Byzantine traditions in its daily rituals.

Synset Examples

Byzantine monks
Byzantine rites

Etymology

From Latin Byzantinus, from Byzantium, the ancient name of Constantinople (now Istanbul).

(of a system, process, or situation) highly complex, intricate, and often involving secret or devious methods.
extremely complex or deviously intricate

Examples

The company's Byzantine regulations made acquiring a permit nearly impossible.
He managed to navigate the Byzantine internal politics of the organization.
The proposal was rejected due to its Byzantine structure and lack of clarity.
