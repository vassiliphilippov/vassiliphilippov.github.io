moneyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌnid / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪd / (BrE)
Having a lot of money or wealth; affluent or rich.
having much money; wealthy

Examples

The moneyed elite often have disproportionate influence over political decisions.
She came from a moneyed family that owned several businesses across the city.
The proposal was backed by several moneyed interests seeking to protect their investments.

Synset Examples

moneyed interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of wealth
  • Antonym: poor - having little or no money

Etymology

From the noun 'money' + -ed, meaning 'having money'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌnid / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪd / (BrE)
having a lot of money; wealthy, especially in reference to people or social groups
having a lot of money; wealthy

Examples

Only the moneyed elite could afford to purchase property in that exclusive area.
The reforms were designed to limit the influence of the moneyed classes in politics.
Despite his humble beginnings, he soon found himself among the moneyed aristocrats.

Synset Examples

an affluent banker
a speculator flush with cash
not merely rich but loaded
moneyed aristocrats
wealthy corporations
a substantial family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Antonym: impoverished - lacking money or material possessions

Etymology

From 'money' + the suffix '-ed', meaning 'having (something)'.
