busload

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌsˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈbʌs.ləʊd / (BrE)
the number of people or amount of cargo that a bus can carry at one time
quantity a bus can carry

Examples

A busload of tourists arrived at the museum just after opening time.
The school sent a busload of students to the science fair.
There were several busloads of fans heading to the football match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truckload - the quantity a truck can carry
  • Synonym: carload - the amount of cargo a car can carry

Etymology

Formed from 'bus' and 'load'.
