Namibia

1. PROPN

/ nəˈmɪbiə / (AmE), / nəˈmɪbɪə / (BrE)
A country in southwestern Africa, bordering the South Atlantic Ocean, which gained independence from South Africa in 1990.
country in southwestern Africa

Examples

Tourists are attracted to Namibia for its stunning desert landscapes and unique wildlife.
After independence in 1990, Namibia established a stable multi-party democracy.
The Namib Desert, spreading across much of Namibia, is considered one of the oldest in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Namibian - relating to Namibia or its people

Etymology

From the Namib Desert, itself from a local Nama word meaning 'vast place', with the suffix -ia denoting 'land of'.
