Teucrium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːkriəm / (AmE), / ˈtjuːkrɪəm / (BrE)
a large genus of perennial herbs, shrubs, or subshrubs in the mint family, native to regions from the Mediterranean to western Asia
genus of herbs and shrubs (botany)

Examples

Many species of Teucrium are cultivated for their attractive foliage and flowers.
The Teucrium plants thrived in the Mediterranean climate of the garden.
Botanists discovered a new variety within the Teucrium genus in western Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'Teucrium', the ancient name for germander.
