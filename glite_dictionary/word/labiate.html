labiate

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.bi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a part or structure) having lips or parts that resemble lips, especially in plants or anatomy.
having parts resembling lips

Examples

The flower is described as labiate because its petals form a distinct lip-like shape.
Some plants in the mint family have labiate corollas that help attract pollinators.
You can identify this species by its labiate mouthparts, which resemble small lips.

Etymology

From Latin 'labiatus' (having lips), from 'labium' (lip).
