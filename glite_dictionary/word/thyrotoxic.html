thyrotoxic

1. ADJ

/ ˌθaɪroʊˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəʊˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by hyperthyroidism, a condition caused by excessive thyroid hormone in the body.
relating to hyperthyroidism
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as thyrotoxic and required immediate treatment.
Certain symptoms such as rapid heartbeat are commonly seen in thyrotoxic individuals.
A thyrotoxic crisis can be life-threatening without prompt intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperthyroid - having or relating to an overactive thyroid gland
  • Antonym: hypothyroid - having or relating to an underactive thyroid gland

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreos' (shield, referring to the thyroid gland) and 'toxic' (poisoned).
