anisette

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈsɛt / (BrE)
a sweet, usually colorless liqueur with a liquorice flavor, made from aniseed
sweet anise-flavored liqueur

Examples

She offered her guests a glass of anisette after dinner.
The recipe calls for a splash of anisette to enhance the dessert's flavor.
Many Mediterranean countries produce their own varieties of anisette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sambuca - an Italian anise-flavored liqueur
  • Synonym: ouzo - a Greek anise-flavored spirit

Etymology

From French 'anisette', diminutive of 'anis' (anise).
