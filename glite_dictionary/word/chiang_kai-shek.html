Chiang Kai-shek

1. PROPN

/ tʃjɑŋ kaɪˈʃɛk / (AmE), / tʃɪˈæŋ kaɪˈʃɛk / (BrE)
(1897–1975) A Chinese military and political leader who led the Nationalist government in China, was defeated by the communists in the Chinese Civil War, and served as president of Nationalist China in Taiwan until his death.
Chinese military and political leader (1897–1975)

Examples

Chiang Kai-shek led the Nationalist forces during the Chinese Civil War.
History classes often discuss the legacy of Chiang Kai-shek and his leadership in Taiwan.
In 1949, Chiang Kai-shek retreated to Taiwan after losing to the communists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mao Zedong - Leader of the Chinese Communist Party during the Chinese Civil War

Etymology

From Mandarin Jiǎng Jièshí (蔣介石), Chinese name for Chiang Kai-shek.
