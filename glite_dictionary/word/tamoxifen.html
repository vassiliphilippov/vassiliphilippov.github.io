tamoxifen

1. NOUN

/ təˈmɑːksəˌfɛn / (AmE), / təˈmɒksɪˌfɛn / (BrE)
A drug that acts as an estrogen antagonist and is used in the treatment and prevention of breast cancer.
drug for treating breast cancer
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tamoxifen to help reduce the risk of breast cancer recurrence.
Many patients take tamoxifen for several years as part of their treatment plan.
Research shows that tamoxifen can be effective in preventing some types of breast cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - a hormone important for female sexual development and reproduction

Etymology

From the trademark Tamoxifen, based on elements of its chemical structure.
