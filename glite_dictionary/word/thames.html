Thames

1. PROPN

/ θɛmz / (AmE), / tɛmz / (BrE)
the longest river in England, flowing east through London to the North Sea
the main river running through London, England

Examples

The Thames is famous for the annual Oxford and Cambridge boat race.
A walk along the Thames provides beautiful views of London landmarks.
Many bridges cross over the Thames as it winds through the city.

Etymology

From Middle English 'Temese', from Latin 'Tamesis', of Celtic origin.
