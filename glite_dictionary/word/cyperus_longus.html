Cyperus longus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpɪr.əs ˈlɒŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / saɪˈpiː.rəs ˈlɒŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A European sedge with rough-edged leaves, reddish flower spikelets, and aromatic roots.
a European sedge plant with aromatic roots

Examples

The banks of the river were lined with Cyperus longus, adding a spicy fragrance to the air.
Botanists studied the aromatic roots of Cyperus longus for their medicinal properties.
Gardeners sometimes plant Cyperus longus to help control erosion in wetland areas.

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus name 'Cyperus' and 'longus' meaning 'long'.
