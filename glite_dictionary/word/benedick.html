benedick

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnɪˌdɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪdɪk / (BrE)
A man who has recently married, especially after a long period of bachelorhood.
newly married man, ex-bachelor

Examples

After years of insisting he would never wed, Charles finally became a benedick at forty.
The once notorious playboy is now a contented benedick enjoying his honeymoon.
At the wedding reception, his friends joked about him joining the ranks of the benedicks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bachelor - an unmarried man

Etymology

From Benedick, the name of a confirmed bachelor who marries in Shakespeare's 'Much Ado About Nothing'.
