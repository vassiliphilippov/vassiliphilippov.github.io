yob

1. NOUN

/ jɑb / (AmE), / jɒb / (BrE)
A young man who is rude, noisy, and sometimes violent, especially in public places.
rude or violent young man (British)
slang british

Examples

The police were called after a group of yobs started causing trouble outside the pub.
She was frightened by the yob who shouted insults as he passed by.
The yobs were arrested for vandalizing the bus stop last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lout - an uncouth or aggressive man or boy
  • Synonym: hooligan - a violent young troublemaker, typically one of a gang
  • Antonym: gentleman - a chivalrous, courteous, or honorable man

Etymology

Backslang for 'boy', used in British slang since the 19th century.
