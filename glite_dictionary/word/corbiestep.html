corbiestep

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.biˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.bi.stɛp / (BrE)
(architecture) A step-like projection forming the top edge of a gable wall, characteristic of some traditional buildings.
step-shaped feature atop a gable wall

Examples

Many old Scottish houses feature distinctive corbiesteps along their gable ends.
The mason carefully restored the damaged corbiesteps during the building's renovation.
The silhouette of corbiesteps was visible against the evening sky.

Etymology

From Scots 'corbie' (crow) and 'step', referring to the crow-step shape of the gable.
