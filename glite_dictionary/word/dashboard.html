dashboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
the panel in a vehicle or aircraft containing instruments and controls for operation
vehicle instrument/control panel

Examples

Sarah checked the fuel gauge on the dashboard before starting her trip.
A warning light flashed suddenly on the car's dashboard.
The airplane's dashboard is filled with complex instruments and displays.

Etymology

Originally referred to a board on a horse-drawn carriage to stop mud from being splashed up ('dashed') by the horses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɑːʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
a panel or screen on a vehicle positioned in front of the driver or occupants to protect against splashing water, mud, or debris, originally used in horse-drawn carriages
panel protecting from splashes in vehicles

Examples

The old carriage featured a wooden dashboard to shield passengers from flying mud.
Early automobiles borrowed the dashboard design from horse-drawn coaches.
Farmers often crafted a simple dashboard for their carts to keep out splashes during rainy travel.

Etymology

From dash + board, originally referring to a board on a carriage that 'dashed' off mud or water from the horses' hooves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04288513-n
Synset POS: n
