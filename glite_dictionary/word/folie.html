folie

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈli / (AmE), / fɒˈli / (BrE)
A psychological disorder of thought or emotion; a technical term for a mental illness, especially used in psychiatry.
psychological disorder (psychiatry term)
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist diagnosed the patient with a rare form of folie characterized by shared delusions.
Some nineteenth-century texts describe various types of folie in great clinical detail.
His research focuses on the cultural perceptions of folie throughout European history.

Etymology

From French 'folie' (madness, folly), from Latin 'folia' (folly, madness).
