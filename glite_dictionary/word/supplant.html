to supplant

1. VERB

/ səˈplænt / (AmE), / səˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to take the place of someone or something, especially by force or as something more suitable
replace or take the place of

Examples

The smartphone has supplanted traditional cameras for many casual photographers.
New technologies often supplant older methods of communication.
He hoped to supplant his rival as chairperson of the committee.

Synset Examples

Smith replaced Miller as CEO after Miller left
the computer has supplanted the slide rule
Mary replaced Susan as the team's captain and the highest-ranked player in the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replace - to take the place of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'supplantare' (to trip up, overthrow), from 'sub-' (under) + 'planta' (sole of the foot).
