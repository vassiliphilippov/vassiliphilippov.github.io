counterpoison

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌpɔɪzən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪzən / (BrE)
A substance or remedy used to neutralize or counteract the effects of a poison.
remedy that counteracts a poison

Examples

The herbalist recommended a counterpoison to treat the bite.
Quick administration of a counterpoison can save lives after a venomous snakebite.
Researchers are developing a new counterpoison for rare toxins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidote - a medicine taken to counteract a particular poison

Etymology

From counter- (against) + poison, dating from Late Middle English.
