insight

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪt / (BrE)
the ability to understand the true nature of something, especially a complex situation, clearly and often suddenly
clear understanding of a complex situation

Examples

Her insight into the company's problems helped guide the new strategy.
The psychologist provided valuable insight into how children develop social skills.
Sometimes a sudden insight can completely change your perspective on an issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'insīht' (internal sight, understanding), from 'in-' + 'sīht' (sight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪt / (BrE)
the ability to gain an accurate and deep understanding of someone or something, especially by grasping the true nature of a situation intuitively
deep intuitive understanding

Examples

Her insight into the team's dynamics helped resolve their conflicts quickly.
The novel offers remarkable insight into cultural differences and human motivations.
After years of research, he finally gained an insight that changed his approach entirely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively, without conscious reasoning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old English 'in-sight' (sight with the eyes of the mind), modeled after earlier Germanic forms.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪt / (BrE)
an understanding of the true nature of something; the ability to see clearly and intuitively into a situation or problem
clear understanding; deep perception

Examples

Her years of research gave her unique insight into the causes of the disease.
After listening to both sides, he suddenly had an insight that changed his opinion.
The documentary offered a fascinating insight into life in a remote village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - immediate understanding without reasoning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'insihte' (sight, view, knowledge, wisdom); related to in- (in) + sight.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪt / (BrE)
the ability to gain a clear, deep, and accurate understanding of a situation or problem
deep understanding or perception

Examples

Her insight into human behavior makes her an excellent psychologist.
That book gave me a new insight into the causes of the conflict.
He offered some valuable insights during the team meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively, without the need for conscious reasoning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Middle English, probably from Old English 'insīht' (sight, vision) or a calque of Dutch 'inzicht' or German 'Einsicht'.
