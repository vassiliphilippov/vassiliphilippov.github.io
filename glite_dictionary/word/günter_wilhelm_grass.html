Günter Wilhelm Grass

1. PROPN

/ ˈgʊntər ˈvɪlhɛlm ɡrɑːs / (AmE), / ˈɡʏntə ˈvɪlhɛlm ɡras / (BrE)
(1927–2015) A German writer, poet, and playwright, best known for his novel 'The Tin Drum' and a key figure in postwar German literature.
German novelist, poet, and playwright

Examples

The Nobel Prize in Literature was awarded to Günter Wilhelm Grass in 1999 for his rich literary work.
Many critics consider Günter Wilhelm Grass one of the most important German authors of the 20th century.
Günter Wilhelm Grass's novel 'The Tin Drum' is regarded as a masterpiece of modern literature.
