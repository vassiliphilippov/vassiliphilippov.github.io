tassel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæsəl / (AmE), / ˈtæsəl / (BrE)
A bunch of threads, cords, or other strands fastened at one end and hanging loose at the other, used as a decorative ornament.
decorative bunch of threads or cords

Examples

The curtains were tied back with a gold tassel at each window.
She added a bright red tassel to her graduation cap for decoration.
A silk tassel dangled from the bookmark he gave her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe - decorative border of threads hanging loosely

Etymology

From Middle English 'tassel', from Old French 'tassel', diminutive of 'tasse' (a clasp or buckle).
