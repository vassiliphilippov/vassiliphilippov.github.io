genocide

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.əˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ə.saɪd / (BrE)
The deliberate and systematic killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular ethnic, national, or religious group.
systematic mass killing of a group

Examples

The international community condemned the genocide committed during the conflict.
He was charged with crimes against humanity and genocide.
Efforts to prevent genocide require early intervention and cooperation between nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - the indiscriminate and brutal killing of many people

Etymology

From Greek 'genos' (race, people) + '-cide' (killing, from Latin 'caedere')
