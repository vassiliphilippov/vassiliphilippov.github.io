Ramesses the Great

1. PROPN

/ ræˈmɛsiːz ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ræˈmɛsiːz ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
the third pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty of Egypt (reigned c. 1279–1213 BC), renowned for his military leadership and monumental building projects
Egyptian pharaoh, 13th century BC

Examples

Many statues of Ramesses the Great stand tall in Egyptian temples today.
Historians often refer to Ramesses the Great as Egypt's most powerful pharaoh.
The reign of Ramesses the Great was marked by impressive architectural achievements.

Etymology

From Ramesses, from Ancient Egyptian 'Ra is the one who gave birth to him'; 'the Great' denotes his distinction among pharaohs.
