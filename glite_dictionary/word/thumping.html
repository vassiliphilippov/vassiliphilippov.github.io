thumping

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
a heavy, dull sound produced by the impact of heavy objects
heavy, dull impact sound

Examples

The constant thumping from the construction site kept me awake all night.
She heard a loud thumping on the door just after midnight.
The steady thumping of the drum echoed through the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thud - a dull sound, as of a heavy object striking a surface
  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, as of an explosion or impact

2. ADJ

/ ˈθʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
Used informally to emphasize the large size or great degree of something; very large or impressive.
very large; impressive (informal)
informal

Examples

The team suffered a thumping defeat in the final match.
That was a thumping good dinner—thank you for cooking!
They made a thumping profit on the sale of their house.

Synset Examples

a thumping loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size or amount
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount
