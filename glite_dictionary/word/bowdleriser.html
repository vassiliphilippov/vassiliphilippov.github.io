bowdleriser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊd.ləˌraɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊd.ləˌraɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who edits a text by removing or altering parts considered obscene or offensive
person who expurgates texts

Examples

The publisher hired a bowdleriser to ensure the novel was appropriate for younger readers.
Many literary critics argue that a bowdleriser dilutes the author's original intent.
She refused to let a bowdleriser remove controversial passages from her play.

Synset Examples

Thomas Bowdler was a famous expurgator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expurgator - a person who removes objectionable content from books or texts

Etymology

Named after Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), who published expurgated versions of Shakespeare's works.
