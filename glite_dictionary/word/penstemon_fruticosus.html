Penstemon fruticosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnstəˌmɑn fruːˈtɪkəsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛnstɪˌmɒn fruːˈtɪkəsəs / (BrE)
A low, bushy plant native to western North America, with large, showy pale lavender or blue-violet flowers in narrow clusters at the ends of stems.
species of bushy flowering plant

Examples

The garden's Penstemon fruticosus attracted several species of bees and butterflies.
Along the hiking trail, clusters of Penstemon fruticosus added vibrant color to the rocky hillside.
Horticulturists recommend Penstemon fruticosus for its drought tolerance and striking blooms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Penstemon' (genus name from Greek 'pente' meaning five + 'stēmōn' meaning stamen), 'fruticosus' meaning bushy or shrubby.
