grounds

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊndz / (AmE), / ɡraʊndz / (BrE)
an area of land that is used or set aside for a specific purpose, such as recreation, a building site, or burial.
land set aside for a special purpose

Examples

The university grounds are beautifully landscaped with gardens and old trees.
Visitors strolled through the museum grounds enjoying the outdoor sculptures.
The family gathered at the burial grounds for the memorial service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate - a large area of land, especially with a grand house
  • Synonym: cemetery - a place where the dead are buried

3. NOUN

/ ɡraʊndz / (AmE), / ɡraʊndz / (BrE)
reasons or justification for believing, doing, or saying something, especially as a basis for action or decision.
justification or reason for something

Examples

There are insufficient grounds for reopening the investigation.
She resigned on medical grounds after years of service.
His appeal was rejected because it lacked solid legal grounds.

Synset Examples

they had good reason to rejoice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reason - a cause or justification for something
  • Synonym: cause - a reason for an action, event, or condition

Etymology

Plural use of 'ground', from Old English 'grund' (bottom, foundation).

5. NOUN

/ ɡraʊndz / (AmE), / ɡraʊndz / (BrE)
A reason, justification, or basis for a belief, action, or feeling.
reason or basis for belief or action

Examples

There are strong grounds for believing the new treatment is effective.
She was dismissed on the grounds of misconduct.
The judge found insufficient grounds to issue a warrant.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reason - a cause, explanation, or justification for an action or event
  • Synonym: justification - a good reason or explanation for something
  • Antonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason

Etymology

From the noun 'ground' in the sense of 'foundation' or 'basis,' dating to Old English 'grund' (bottom, foundation).
