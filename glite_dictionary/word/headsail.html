headsail

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛdseɪl / (BrE)
A sail set forward of the foremast of a vessel, typically used to help steer and balance the boat.
sail set forward of the foremast

Examples

The crew quickly hoisted the headsail to catch the morning breeze.
A smaller headsail can make steering easier in strong winds.
With the headsail fully unfurled, the yacht surged forward through the waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foresail - a sail set on the foremast of a ship
  • Antonym: mainsail - the principal sail on a ship's mainmast

Etymology

From 'head' (front of the vessel) + 'sail'.
