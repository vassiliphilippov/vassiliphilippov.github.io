judgeship

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or tenure of a judge
the position of a judge

Examples

After years of hard work, she was finally appointed to a judgeship.
He lost his judgeship after a lengthy investigation into his conduct.
There are several judgeships opening up on the federal court this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judiciary - the body of judges or judicial authorities
  • Synonym: judgeship - the office or position of being a judge

Etymology

Formed from 'judge' + '-ship' (denoting state or condition).
