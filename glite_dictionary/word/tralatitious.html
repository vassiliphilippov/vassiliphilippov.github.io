tralatitious

1. ADJ

/ træləˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / træləˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a belief, story, or custom) passed down from one person or generation to another; handed on or traditional.
passed down through generations
archaic

Examples

The scholar criticized the tralatitious interpretation of the text as lacking originality.
Many rituals in the village are tralatitious, preserved from ancient times.
Some legal principles remain tralatitious, inherited from earlier generations of jurists.

Synset Examples

among Biblical critics a tralatitious interpretation is one received by expositor from expositor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditional - existing as part of a tradition; handed down

Etymology

From Latin 'tralatiticius', from 'tralatus' (past participle of 'transferre', to transfer).
