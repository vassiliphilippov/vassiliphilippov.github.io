threshold

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃˌhəʊld / (BrE)
the smallest level of a stimulus that can be detected by the senses
smallest detectable sensation

Examples

The pain threshold varies greatly from person to person.
He has a low threshold for detecting sweet flavors in food.
Scientists measure the threshold at which a sound becomes audible to the human ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limen - a technical term for the threshold of sensation

Etymology

From Old English 'þrescwald', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃˌhəʊld / (BrE)
the sill of a doorway; a horizontal strip of wood, stone, or metal forming the bottom of a doorframe that you step over when entering or leaving a room or building
bottom strip of a doorway

Examples

She wiped her feet before stepping over the threshold into the house.
The old wooden threshold creaked as the door swung open.
They painted the threshold to match the rest of the entryway.

Etymology

Old English þrescwald (also threscold, threshold), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03228389-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃ.həʊld / (BrE)
the point at which something begins or changes, especially marking the start of a new state, experience, or event
starting point or beginning of a new state

Examples

The country stood at the threshold of a new era after the election.
She was on the threshold of her career as a scientist.
Advances in technology have brought us to the threshold of a medical breakthrough.

Synset Examples

on the threshold of manhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • Antonym: end - the final point of something; when something stops or finishes

Etymology

From Old English 'þrescwald', of Germanic origin; the exact development of the second element is uncertain.

4. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
the point or level at which something begins or changes, especially marking a boundary or limit
point or boundary where something begins or changes

Examples

The patient had a low pain threshold, making small injuries feel intense.
Crossing the threshold of adulthood brings new responsibilities.
This year, the company finally passed the profitability threshold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line that marks the limits of an area; a dividing line
  • Synonym: limit - the point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass

Etymology

From Old English 'þrescwald', related to 'thresh' (to tread) and 'wald' (wood, board).
