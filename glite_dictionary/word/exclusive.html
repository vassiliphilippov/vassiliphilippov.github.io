exclusive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
Limited to only one person or group and not shared with others.
not shared; limited to one

Examples

The company granted them exclusive rights to market the new product.
We have exclusive use of the conference room this morning.
Membership in this club is exclusive to employees only.

Synset Examples

they have exclusive use of the machine
sole rights of publication

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shared - used or possessed by more than one person or group
  • Synonym: sole - the only one; single; exclusive

Etymology

From Latin exclusivus, from excludere (to shut out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02160400-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
a news report or story published or broadcast by only one organization before it becomes available to others
news report first released by one media outlet

Examples

The newspaper printed an exclusive about the prime minister’s resignation.
Their reporter managed to land an exclusive interview with the famous actor.
We were the first to break the story with our exclusive on the financial scandal.

Synset Examples

he got a scoop on the bribery of city officials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - a news report published or broadcast before others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06695928-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
(of attention, devotion, etc.) directed solely to one object, person, or purpose; not shared or divided among several.
not shared; focused on one thing

Examples

She gave her exclusive attention to her daughter's needs during the illness.
The club rewarded him with their exclusive loyalty for years.
His exclusive devotion to science meant he rarely pursued other interests.

Synset Examples

judging a contest with a single eye
a single devotion to duty
undivided affection
gained their exclusive attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undivided - not divided; giving all attention to something
  • Antonym: shared - divided among several people or things

Etymology

From Latin 'excludere' (to shut out), via French 'exclusif'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00541609-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
(of a place, organization, or group) only allowing certain people to join or participate; restricted to a particular group.
restricted to a particular group

Examples

The country club is very exclusive and only accepts members by invitation.
She shops at exclusive boutiques that cater to a wealthy clientele.
The gala was so exclusive that only celebrities and dignitaries were invited.

Synset Examples

exclusive clubs
an exclusive restaurants and shops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusive - allowing everyone to participate; not excluding anyone

Etymology

From Latin exclusivus, from excludere (to shut out).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
