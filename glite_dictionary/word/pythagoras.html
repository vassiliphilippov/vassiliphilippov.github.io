Pythagoras

1. PROPN

/ paɪˈθæɡərəs / (AmE), / pɪˈθæɡərəs / (BrE)
(c. 580–500 BC) A Greek philosopher and mathematician known for establishing the Pythagorean theorem and making significant contributions to mathematics and philosophy.
ancient Greek philosopher and mathematician

Examples

Pythagoras is best known for the theorem in geometry that bears his name.
Many ancient legends surround the life of Pythagoras and his mathematical discoveries.
The influence of Pythagoras extended beyond mathematics to philosophy and mysticism.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πυθαγόρας (Pythagóras).
