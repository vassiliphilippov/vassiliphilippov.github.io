to aver

1. VERB

/ əˈvɝː / (AmE), / əˈvɜː / (BrE)
to state or assert to be the case, especially in a firm or confident way
state or assert confidently

Examples

The witness averred that he was nowhere near the scene of the crime.
She avers her innocence despite the evidence against her.
In his statement, he averred that the company had always followed ethical practices.

Synset Examples

He alleged that he was the victim of a crime
He said it was too late to intervene in the war
The registrar says that I owe the school money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something declared or believed is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'adverare' (to make true), from 'ad-' (to) + 'verus' (true).

2. VERB

/ əˈvɝ / (AmE), / əˈvɜː / (BrE)
to state or assert that something is true, especially with confidence or force
to assert or declare as true

Examples

In court, the witness averred that she saw the defendant at the scene of the crime.
He averred his innocence despite the mounting evidence against him.
During the meeting, she averred that all safety protocols had been followed.

Synset Examples

Before God I swear I am innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin *adverare* (to prove true), from *ad-* (to) + *verus* (true).

Synset POS: v
