rightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally good, just, or appropriate; correctness in conduct or judgment
moral correctness; appropriateness

Examples

She always tries to act with complete rightness in her decisions.
Many admired the rightness of his actions during the crisis.
Doubts were raised about the rightness of their motives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrongness - the quality of being morally wrong or incorrect

Etymology

From Old English 'rihtness', from 'riht' (right) + '-ness'.
