triskele

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈskɛl.i / (AmE), / trɪˈskɛl.i / (BrE)
An ancient symbol made up of three stylized arms or legs (or curved lines) radiating from a central point, often used in art and heraldry.
ancient symbol of three arms or lines

Examples

The triskele was a prominent motif in Celtic art, representing motion and spiritual concepts.
She wore a pendant shaped like a triskele, believed to symbolize balance and unity.
Ancient coins from Sicily often feature a triskele design with three human legs.

Etymology

From Greek *triskelēs* (three-legged), from *tri-* (three) + *skelos* (leg).
