auricular

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjələ / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the ear, especially in anatomy or medicine.
relating to the ear
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed an auricular infection in her right ear.
He wore an auricular device to assist his hearing during conversations.
Some ancient jewelry was designed specifically for auricular adornment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otic - relating to the ear; auricular

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (little ear), diminutive of 'auris' (ear).

2. ADJ

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to an auricle of the heart; pertaining to the atrium in cardiac anatomy
relating to a heart auricle (atrium)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with auricular fibrillation, a common type of irregular heartbeat.
Some older medical texts use the term auricular to describe atrial contractions.
The doctor explained the difference between ventricular and auricular rhythms in the heart.

Synset Examples

auricular fibrillation

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (little ear), referring to the ear-shaped structure of the heart atrium.

relating to the ear or hearing

Examples

His auricular confession was heard privately by the priest.
The plant's leaves had a distinctive auricular shape, resembling small ears.
