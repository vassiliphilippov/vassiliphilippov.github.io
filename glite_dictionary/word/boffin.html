boffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑf.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒf.ɪn / (BrE)
(British, informal) A scientist or technical expert, especially one involved in scientific or technical research, often in a military or government context.
scientist or technical expert (UK, informal)
informal british

Examples

The laboratory was filled with boffins working on secret projects.
During the war, a talented boffin designed this new piece of radar equipment.
Although I'm no boffin, I can see that this technology is groundbreaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scientist - a person who is studying or has expert knowledge in one or more of the natural or physical sciences
  • Synonym: technician - a person employed to look after technical equipment or do practical work in a laboratory

Etymology

Originally British slang, first attested during World War II; origin uncertain, perhaps humorous or invented word.
