saltbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltˌbɒks / (BrE)
a traditional wooden house style found in New England, characterized by a long, pitched roof that slopes down to the back, giving it two stories in front and one at the rear.
traditional New England house with sloping roof

Examples

The old saltbox at the edge of town is a fine example of colonial architecture.
Many early settlers in New England built saltbox houses for their practicality and charm.
The distinct roofline of the saltbox makes it easy to recognize among other historic homes.

Etymology

So called due to its resemblance to the wooden boxes used for storing salt in colonial times.
