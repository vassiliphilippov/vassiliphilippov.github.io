herrerasaurus

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌreɪrəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / hɛˌreərəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early theropod dinosaur that lived in what is now Argentina during the Late Triassic period
an early theropod dinosaur from Argentina

Examples

Scientists believe that herrerasaurus was one of the earliest large predatory dinosaurs.
The fossils of herrerasaurus were discovered in northwestern Argentina in the 1950s.
Visitors to the museum were fascinated by the reconstructed skeleton of the herrerasaurus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles, dominant during the Mesozoic era
  • Synonym: theropod - a suborder of bipedal, mostly carnivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From Spanish, named after Victorino Herrera, who discovered the first fossils, + Ancient Greek 'sauros' (lizard).
