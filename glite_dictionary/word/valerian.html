valerian

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɛriən / (AmE), / vəˈlɪəriən / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Valeriana, especially Valeriana officinalis, known for its fragrant white or pink flowers and use in herbal medicine
herbaceous plant of the genus Valeriana

Examples

The roots of valerian are often used to make herbal remedies.
Gardeners admire valerian for its clusters of delicate pink flowers.
Some people grow valerian as an ornamental plant in their backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valerian officinalis - the common medicinal species of valerian

Etymology

From Latin valeriana (the plant name), possibly from Valeria (an ancient place name).
