Libra

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.brə / (AmE), / ˈliː.brə / (BrE)
A person born under the astrological sign of Libra, typically between September 23 and October 22.
person born under Libra sign

Examples

My sister is a Libra and loves harmony in her relationships.
Many Libras are said to have a keen sense of justice and fairness.
As a Libra, he believes in keeping life balanced and peaceful.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Scorpio - person born under the astrological sign Scorpio
  • Degree Lower: Virgo - person born under the astrological sign Virgo

Etymology

From Latin 'lībra' meaning 'scales', the zodiac sign symbolized by a balance scale.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːbrə / (AmE), / ˈliːbrə / (BrE)
A small, faint zodiacal constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, situated between Virgo and Scorpius.
constellation between Virgo and Scorpius

Examples

The stars of Libra are visible in the night sky during spring in the southern hemisphere.
Astronomers have long debated the origin of the name Libra for this constellation.
Between Virgo and Scorpius, you can find Libra in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

Latin for 'scales', reflecting the shape resembling a balance scale.

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːbrə / (AmE), / ˈliːbrə / (BrE)
the seventh sign of the zodiac, represented as a pair of scales, with the sun in this sign from approximately September 23 to October 22
zodiac sign (September–October)

Examples

My sister was born in October, so her sign is Libra.
People born under Libra are said to value balance and harmony.
The horoscope predicted big changes for those with Libra as their zodiac sign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zodiac - a band of the sky divided into twelve parts, each named after a constellation

Etymology

From Latin 'libra' meaning 'scales'.
