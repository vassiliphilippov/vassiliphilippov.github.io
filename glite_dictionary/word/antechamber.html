antechamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.t̬iˌtʃeɪm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈæn.tiˌtʃeɪm.bə / (BrE)
a small room leading to a main one, used as an entrance or waiting area
room preceding a main one

Examples

The butler led us into the antechamber to wait for the ambassador.
Paintings adorned the walls of the antechamber, just before the grand hall.
Guests assembled in the antechamber before entering the banquet room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteroom - a room leading to a larger, more important room, often used for waiting

Etymology

From French 'antichambre', from Latin 'ante' (before) + 'camera' (chamber).
