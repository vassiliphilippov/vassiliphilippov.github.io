fermata

1. NOUN

/ fərˈmɑtə / (AmE), / fəˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
A musical symbol indicating that a note, chord, or rest should be held or prolonged for an unspecified length of time at the performer's discretion.
musical symbol for holding a note

Examples

Please hold the last note as indicated by the fermata in the score.
The conductor paused dramatically at the fermata before continuing the piece.
Each musician interpreted the fermata slightly differently, making the performance unique.

Etymology

From Italian 'fermata' (stop), from 'fermare' (to stop).

2. NOUN

/ fɚˈmɑːtə / (AmE), / fəˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
a symbol in musical notation placed over a note, chord, or rest to indicate that it should be prolonged beyond its usual value
musical symbol indicating prolongation

Examples

The conductor signaled the orchestra to pause on the fermata above the final note.
Studying the score, she noticed a fermata over the long-held chord.
A fermata at the end of the piece gave it a dramatic, lingering quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold - a pause or sustain in music, indicated by a fermata
  • Degree Lower: rest - a symbol indicating a period of silence in music

Etymology

From Italian 'fermata' (a stop), from 'fermare' (to stop).

Source: WordNet
