to sorcerize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɔːrsəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to transform or change something by means of sorcery or magical power
transform by sorcery

Examples

The wizard threatened to sorcerize anyone who dared to enter his castle.
In the story, the potion could sorcerize ordinary objects into strange magical creatures.
She claimed she could sorcerize his fate with a single spell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to put under a spell; to bewitch
  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form or appearance

Etymology

Formed from 'sorcer(y)' + '-ize', patterning after 'baptize' and similar verbs.
