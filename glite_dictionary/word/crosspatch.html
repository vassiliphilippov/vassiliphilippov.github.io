crosspatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌpætʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌpætʃ / (BrE)
a person who is habitually irritable or bad-tempered
bad-tempered person

Examples

My grandfather can be quite a crosspatch when things don't go his way.
Nobody wanted to disturb the crosspatch in the corner, so we left him alone.
She apologized for being such a crosspatch earlier in the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grump - a person who is often in a bad mood
  • Antonym: cheerful person - a person who is habitually happy and good-tempered

Etymology

From 'cross' (angry) + 'patch' (possibly a term for a person, of uncertain origin), attested from the 18th century.
