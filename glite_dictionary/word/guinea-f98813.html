guinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
a former British gold coin, worth 21 shillings, used from the 17th to the early 19th century
former British gold coin (21 shillings)
british archaic

Examples

The painting was sold for five guineas at auction.
Doctors once often charged their fees in guineas rather than pounds.
In 1816, the guinea was replaced by the pound as the standard British currency.

Etymology

Named after the African region Guinea, where much of the gold used to mint the coin originated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
a West African bird with dark plumage mottled with white, native to Africa but raised for food in many parts of the world; also called guinea fowl.
West African bird raised for food

Examples

We keep several guineas on our farm to help control insects.
The distinctive spotted feathers of the guinea make it easy to identify.
Guinea is often roasted or stewed and served as a delicacy in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - an African bird with spotted feathers, often raised for food

Etymology

Shortened from 'guinea fowl', named after the Guinea region of West Africa where the bird originates.
