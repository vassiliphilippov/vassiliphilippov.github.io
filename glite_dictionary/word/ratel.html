ratel

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑːtəl / (BrE)
a nocturnal, badger-like carnivore found in wooded regions of Africa and southern Asia, also known as the honey badger
badger-like carnivorous mammal of Africa and Asia

Examples

The ratel, more commonly known as the honey badger, is famous for its fearless nature.
Researchers observed a ratel raiding a beehive in the late evening hours.
Despite its small size, the ratel can defend itself against much larger predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey badger - another common name for the ratel, a fierce mammal of Africa and Asia
  • Degree Lower: badger - a burrowing carnivorous mammal with short legs and a heavy body

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'ratel', meaning 'rattle', probably from its grunting sounds.
