pillock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlək / (AmE), / ˈpɪlək / (BrE)
an idiot or a foolish person; someone who is not very bright
a foolish or stupid person
informal british

Examples

Don't be such a pillock, just follow the instructions!
He looked like an absolute pillock standing there in the rain without an umbrella.
I can't believe I forgot my keys again—I feel like a real pillock.

Synset Examples

The economy, stupid!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Synonym: fool - a person who behaves in a silly or unwise way

Etymology

British slang; possibly from Norwegian dialect 'pillock' meaning 'idiot', related to 'pillicock' (penis) as an insult.

/ ˈpɪlək / (AmE), / ˈpɪlək / (BrE)

foolish or stupid person
informal british

Examples

He forgot his keys again, the pillock.
She called him a pillock for dropping his phone in the toilet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a stupid or foolish person

Originally British slang; possibly from Norwegian dialect *pillicock* (penis), later generalized as an insult.
