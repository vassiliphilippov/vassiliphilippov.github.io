Lama peruana

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːmə peɪˈruːənə / (AmE), / ˈlɑːmə peˈruːənə / (BrE)
A domesticated South American animal, used in the Andes as a pack animal and for its wool; a breed of llama.
Andean domesticated pack animal, source of wool

Examples

The Lama peruana has been an essential pack animal in the Andes for centuries.
Farmers in Peru rely on the wool produced by their Lama peruana.
Tourists often take photographs with a Lama peruana while visiting the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: llama - A domesticated South American camelid used as a pack animal and for wool

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'Peruvian llama', with 'llama' from Quechua 'lama'.
