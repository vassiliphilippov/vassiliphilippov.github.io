hull

1. NOUN

/ hʌl / (AmE), / hʌl / (BrE)
the main body of a ship or boat, including the frame and exterior, but not the masts, engines, or superstructure
main body of a ship or boat

Examples

The storm caused damage to the hull of the cargo ship.
Divers inspected the hull for signs of leakage after the collision.
A fresh coat of paint protected the hull from rust and corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superstructure - the parts of a ship above the main deck

Etymology

From Old English hulu (husk, pod, outer covering).

2. NOUN

/ hʌl / (AmE), / hʌl / (BrE)
the dry outer covering of a fruit, seed, or nut
outer covering of fruit or seed

Examples

Please remove the hull from the sunflower seeds before eating them.
The peas were taken out of their hull and cooked for dinner.
Rice grains need to have their hull removed during processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of some fruits, nuts, or seeds
  • Synonym: husk - the dry outer covering of some fruits or seeds, especially grains

Etymology

From Middle English 'hulle', from Old English 'hulu' (husk, pod).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13160695-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hʌl / (AmE), / hʌl / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering or husk from seeds, fruit, or vegetables
remove outer covering from seeds or fruit

Examples

Before making jam, you need to hull the strawberries.
She carefully hulled the peas and placed them in a bowl.
Many recipes recommend hulling berries to improve their texture and taste.

Synset Examples

hull the berries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - to remove the shell from something, especially nuts or peas
  • Synonym: peel - to remove the skin from fruit or vegetables

Etymology

From Middle English hull (outer covering, husk).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ hʌl / (AmE), / hʌl / (BrE)
the persistent enlarged calyx at the base of certain fruits, such as strawberries or raspberries
persistent calyx at fruit base

Examples

When preparing strawberries, be sure to remove the hull before slicing.
The hull of the raspberry can sometimes stick to the fruit after picking.
It's easier to eat the berries once their hulls have been taken off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the sepals of a flower, collectively forming a protective layer around a flower bud
