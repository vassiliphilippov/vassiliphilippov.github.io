Gobiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊ.bi.aɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈgəʊ.bɪ.aɪ.diː / (BrE)
A large family of small, often bottom-dwelling fishes (gobies) found in marine, brackish, and freshwater habitats worldwide.
family of small fishes (gobies)

Examples

The Gobiidae family consists of over 2,000 species of gobies worldwide.
Many Gobiidae are adapted to life on the ocean floor and have fused pelvic fins forming a disc.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Gobiidae family in the coastal waters of Indonesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goby - any small fish of the Gobiidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gobiidae', from 'Gobius', the type genus, and '-idae', a standard zoological family suffix.
