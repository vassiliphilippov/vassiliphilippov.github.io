overkill

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌkɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌkɪl / (BrE)
the use of much more of something than is needed, often to a point where it becomes excessive or unnecessary
use of excessive force or effort

Examples

Bringing three laptops to the meeting seemed like overkill.
The security at the event was impressive, but some people felt it was overkill.
Adding twelve flavors of syrup to your coffee might be overkill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - an amount that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control; the avoidance of excess

Etymology

Originally a military term referring to nuclear capability exceeding any realistic target requirements; 'over' + 'kill'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌkɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌkɪl / (BrE)
The use of more force, weapons, or resources than necessary to achieve a goal, especially in a military context.
excessive use of force or resources

Examples

The military's response was considered overkill for such a minor threat.
Deploying ten missiles to destroy a single target is unnecessary overkill.
Some critics argue that new security measures are simply overkill and not really needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdose - an excessive and dangerous dose of a drug
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control, especially in the use of force

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) + 'kill', originating in military slang in the 1950s nuclear era.
