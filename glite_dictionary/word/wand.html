wand

1. NOUN

/ wɑnd / (AmE), / wɒnd / (BrE)
a slender rod, often used as a symbol of authority or in ceremonial settings
ceremonial staff or rod

Examples

The magician lifted his wand to begin the ritual.
During the ceremony, the priest carried a golden wand as a symbol of office.
The judge tapped the podium with her ceremonial wand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scepter - an ornamental staff carried by rulers on ceremonial occasions
  • Synonym: rod - a straight, thin stick or bar

Etymology

From Old Norse vöndr (rod, switch).

2. NOUN

/ wɑnd / (AmE), / wɒnd / (BrE)
a slender stick or rod, especially one used by a magician or a water diviner to produce magical effects or locate water
slender rod used by a magician or diviner

Examples

The magician waved his wand and made the rabbit disappear.
She bought a crystal wand for her collection of mystical objects.
The water diviner used a special wand to search for underground springs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - a long stick or pole used as a support or symbol of authority
  • Degree Higher: scepter - an ornamented staff carried by rulers as a symbol of sovereignty

Etymology

From Old Norse *vöndr* (rod, staff).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04556915-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ wɑnd / (AmE), / wɒnd / (BrE)
a thin tapered rod used by a conductor to direct an orchestra or choir
conductor's directing rod

Examples

The conductor raised his wand and the orchestra immediately grew silent.
She bought a new wand before her first performance as choir leader.
With a flick of his wand, the maestro signaled the strings to begin.

Etymology

From Old Norse *vöndr* (rod, stick).

4. NOUN

/ wɑnd / (AmE), / wɒnd / (BrE)
a thin, flexible twig or rod, often used for decoration, magic, or as a part of a plant
thin, flexible twig or rod

Examples

The magician waved his wand and the rabbit disappeared.
She picked a slender wand of willow from the riverbank.
The plant sends up tall wands of purple flowers in summer.

Synset Examples

stems bearing slender wands of flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rod - a straight, slender stick or bar, typically made of wood or metal
  • Synonym: stick - a thin piece of wood that has fallen or been cut from a tree

Etymology

From Old Norse 'vöndr' (rod, staff).
