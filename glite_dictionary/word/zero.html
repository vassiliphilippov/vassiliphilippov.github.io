2. NOUN

/ ˈziroʊ / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The number 0, which, when added to any number, leaves the number unchanged.
the number 0; additive identity

Examples

Adding zero to any number leaves it unchanged.
The sum of five and zero is still five.
In mathematics, zero is known as the additive identity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one - the number 1, the multiplicative identity in mathematics
