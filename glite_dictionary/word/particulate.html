particulate

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪkjʊlət / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
a tiny discrete mass of solid or liquid matter, especially one dispersed in air or liquid, often regarded as a pollutant
tiny solid or liquid particle, often a pollutant

Examples

The air quality test revealed high levels of particulate matter near the industrial site.
Fine particulate pollution can penetrate deep into the lungs, causing serious health problems.
Regulations limit how much particulate material factories can emit into the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle - a very small piece or part

Etymology

From Latin particula (‘small part’) via French particule.

2. ADJ

/ pɑrˈtɪkjələt / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
consisting of or composed of minute, separate particles, especially as distinct from a continuous substance
composed of separate fine particles

Examples

The scientists analyzed the particulate matter in the air samples to assess pollution levels.
A filter is required to remove particulate contaminants from drinking water.
Regulations limit the amount of particulate emissions that factories can release into the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granular - consisting of small grains or granules
  • Antonym: continuous - forming an unbroken whole; without interruption

Etymology

From Medieval Latin particulatus, from Latin particula (small part, particle).

Synset POS: a
