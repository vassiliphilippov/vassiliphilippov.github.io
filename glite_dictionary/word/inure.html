to inure

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnʊr / (AmE), / ɪˈnjʊə / (BrE)
to make someone or something accustomed to something unpleasant or difficult so that they are no longer affected by it
become accustomed to something unpleasant

Examples

Years of harsh criticism had inured her to public opinion.
After living in the mountains, he became inured to the cold weather.
Soldiers are often inured to hardship during their training.

Synset Examples

He was inured to the cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to habituate - to become used to something, especially something unpleasant

Etymology

From Old French enurer, from en- (in) + oeuvre (work), ultimately from Latin operari (to work).
