Brickelia

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈkɛliə / (AmE), / brɪˈkiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs native to southwestern America, characterized by creamy florets and one-seeded fruits enclosed in a prominent bristly sheath.
genus of southwestern American herbs

Examples

The desert landscape featured several species of Brickelia in full bloom.
Botanists studied the distribution of Brickelia across the arid regions of the Southwest.
Brickelia plants are often recognized by their bristly fruit sheaths.

Etymology

Named after John Brickell, an American naturalist.
