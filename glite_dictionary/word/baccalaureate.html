baccalaureate

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈlɔːriət / (AmE), / ˌbakəˈlɔːrɪət / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded to someone who has successfully completed undergraduate studies at a college or university
undergraduate academic degree

Examples

She received her baccalaureate from a prestigious university in France.
Completing a baccalaureate is a prerequisite for entering graduate school.
The ceremony celebrated students who had earned their baccalaureate degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bachelor's degree - an undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin baccalaureatus, from baccalaureus (student with a first degree) and laurea (laurel, a symbol of academic success).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈlɔriət / (AmE), / ˌbakəˈlɔːrɪət / (BrE)
a farewell sermon or address given to a graduating class at their commencement ceremonies
farewell address at graduation

Examples

The college invited a distinguished alumnus to deliver the baccalaureate at this year’s graduation.
Attendance at the baccalaureate is optional but many students find it a meaningful tradition.
The inspiring words spoken during the baccalaureate resonated with the entire graduating class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement address - a speech given at a graduation ceremony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'baccalaureatus', referring originally to a bachelor's degree.
