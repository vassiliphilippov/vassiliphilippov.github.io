Alnus rhombifolia

1. PROPN

/ ˈælnəs roʊmˌbɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈalnəs rɒmˌbɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of alder tree native to the western United States, known for its rhombic-shaped leaves and use in riparian habitats.
species of alder tree native to western U.S.

Examples

The Alnus rhombifolia thrives along stream banks in California and Oregon.
Botanists studied the leaf patterns of Alnus rhombifolia to understand its adaptation to riparian zones.
The restoration project included planting several Alnus rhombifolia saplings to stabilize the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - A tree or shrub of the birch family, genus Alnus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Alnus' (alder) and species epithet 'rhombifolia' from 'rhombus' (diamond) and 'folium' (leaf).
