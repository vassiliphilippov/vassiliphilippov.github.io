linear

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə / (BrE)
of, in, or relating to a straight line; involving or having only one dimension.
relating to a straight line or one dimension

Examples

The artist drew a linear pattern across the canvas.
A ruler is typically used to make linear measurements.
The concept of linear movement is fundamental in physics and engineering.

Synset Examples

a linear measurement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonlinear - not arranged in a straight line; not relating to a single dimension

Etymology

From Latin 'linearis', from 'linea' meaning 'line'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00661426-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniər / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə / (BrE)
(Mathematics) Designating or relating to an equation whose terms are of the first degree, such as y = mx + b.
of equations: first degree

Examples

A linear equation graphs as a straight line on the coordinate plane.
The relationship between force and acceleration is linear according to Newton's second law.
In algebra, students learn to solve linear equations before tackling more complex ones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonlinear - describing an equation that is not of the first degree

Etymology

From Latin linearis, from linea (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01420302-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪə / (BrE)
(of a leaf) long and narrow with nearly parallel sides, like a straight line.
long and narrow leaf shape

Examples

The grass blades are linear, giving the field a sleek, uniform appearance.
This plant is identified by its linear leaves, which can be easily distinguished from broader types.
Botanists described the specimen as having small, linear foliage along its stem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lanceolate - (of a leaf) shaped like a lance tip; longer than wide and tapering at the end

Etymology

From Latin linearis (of or belonging to a line), from linea (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02176256-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə / (BrE)
(of a circuit or device) having an output directly proportional to the input; operating without distortion or nonlinearity.
output proportional to input (in electronics)

Examples

A linear amplifier ensures the output signal accurately follows the input.
The linear response of the circuit makes it ideal for audio processing.
Engineers prefer linear devices in applications where signal distortion must be minimized.

Synset Examples

analogue device
linear amplifier

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonlinear - (of a circuit or device) having an output not proportional to the input

Etymology

From Latin 'linearis' (of or belonging to a line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00111308-a
Synset POS: a

5. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə / (BrE)
measured or extending in a straight line, especially in reference to lengthwise measurement rather than area or volume.
measured in a straight line, lengthwise

Examples

The cost of the carpet is calculated per linear meter.
They measured the fence based on its linear length, not the area it enclosed.
A linear measurement is needed when buying wood for the project.

Synset Examples

cost of lumber per running foot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length

Etymology

From Latin linearis, from linea (line).
