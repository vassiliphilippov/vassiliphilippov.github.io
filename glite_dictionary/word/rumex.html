Rumex

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.mɛks / (AmE), / ˈruː.mɛks / (BrE)
a large genus of annual or perennial herbs, commonly known as docks or sorrels, found mainly in the north temperate regions
genus of plants including docks and sorrels

Examples

Many species in the Rumex genus are considered weeds in North American agriculture.
The gardener identified the broad leaves as belonging to Rumex plants.
Sorrel, a type of Rumex, is often used in traditional recipes for its tart flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - common name for several species within the Rumex genus
  • Synonym: sorrel - common name for several edible species of the Rumex genus

Etymology

From Latin *rumex*, the name for sorrel or dock.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruː.mɛks / (AmE), / ˈruː.mɛks / (BrE)
A genus of coarse herbs and shrubs known as docks, mostly native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of dock plants

Examples

Several species of Rumex are commonly found growing along riverbanks and ditches.
The leaves of Rumex are known to be edible in some cultures, often added to salads.
A botanist collected samples of Rumex to study their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin *rumex* (a kind of dock or sorrel).
