lisp

1. NOUN

/ lɪsp / (AmE), / lɪsp / (BrE)
a speech defect in which sibilant sounds, such as 's' and 'z', are pronounced like 'th' sounds
speech defect mispronouncing sibilants as 'th'

Examples

Because of her lisp, she sometimes struggled to pronounce certain words clearly.
He attended speech therapy to help correct his lisp before the school play.
Many people with a lisp can improve their pronunciation with regular practice.

Etymology

From Old English 'wlisp' (lisping, lisping speech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14426167-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ lɪsp / (AmE), / lɪsp / (BrE)
to speak with a speech defect in which 's' and 'z' sounds are pronounced like 'th'
to speak with a lisp

Examples

She used to lisp when she was a child, pronouncing 'sun' as 'thun'.
The boy lisped nervously during his first public speech.
He still lisps slightly despite years of speech therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lisp - speech defect causing 's' and 'z' to sound like 'th'

Etymology

From Old English 'wlyspian' (to lisp), related to 'wlisp' (lisping).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
