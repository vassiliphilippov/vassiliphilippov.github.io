touch-me-not

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃ mi nɑt / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ mi nɒt / (BrE)
A North American annual plant, typically with yellow or orange flowers, that grows mainly on wet, acidic soil.
a plant with yellow/orange flowers, grows in wet soil

Examples

The forest clearing was filled with blooming touch-me-not plants after the rainy season.
Children are fascinated by the way the seed pods of touch-me-not burst when touched.
Many gardeners appreciate touch-me-not for its bright orange flowers and unique seed dispersal mechanism.

Etymology

Refers to the plant's explosive seed pods that burst when touched.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃ mi nɑt / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ mi nɒt / (BrE)
A tropical plant with sensitive leaflets that fold or droop when touched, belonging to the genus Mimosa.
plant with sensitive folding leaves

Examples

The touch-me-not's leaves instantly folded inward when the child brushed against them.
Botanists observed the touch-me-not in its native tropical habitat to study its unique leaf movement.
She planted a touch-me-not in her garden to show visitors its sensitive leaves.

Etymology

Named for its characteristic leaves, which fold when touched, from the phrase ‘touch me not’.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃ mi ˌnɑt / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ mi ˌnɒt / (BrE)
A plant whose ripe seed pods or fruit burst open and release seeds when touched, especially species in the genus Impatiens or Ecballium.
plant with explosive seed pods

Examples

The touch-me-not plant flung its seeds into the air as soon as I brushed against it.
Gardeners appreciate the touch-me-not for its unique way of dispersing seeds.
When ripe, the fruit of the touch-me-not bursts open at the slightest touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Impatiens - a genus of plants also commonly called touch-me-nots

Etymology

Named for its sensitive seed pods that burst when touched.
