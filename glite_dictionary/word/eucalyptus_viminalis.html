Eucalyptus viminalis

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkliːptəs vɪˈmɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / juːˌklɪptəs vɪˈmɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a tall species of eucalyptus tree, native to Australia, that produces a substance known as false manna
a tall Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The smooth, white bark of the Eucalyptus viminalis is a common sight in southeastern Australia.
Koalas often feed on the leaves of the Eucalyptus viminalis tree.
The Eucalyptus viminalis produces a sweet substance called false manna, attractive to birds and insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucalyptus - a genus of trees and shrubs native to Australia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Eucalyptus' (from Greek 'eu' meaning 'well' and 'kalyptos' meaning 'covered') and species name 'viminalis' (from Latin 'vimen', 'osier, twig').
