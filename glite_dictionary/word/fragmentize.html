to fragmentize

1. VERB

/ ˈfræɡ.mənˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.mən.taɪz / (BrE)
to break or cause to break into fragments or pieces
break into fragments

Examples

Over time, the ancient wall began to fragmentize due to harsh weather conditions.
Some artists fragmentize images deliberately to create abstract pieces.
If you drop that vase, it will fragmentize on the floor.

Synset Examples

The plate fragmented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - to break into smaller pieces; to shatter

Etymology

From Latin 'fragmentum' (a fragment) + English '-ize' (to make or become).
