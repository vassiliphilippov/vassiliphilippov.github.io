outgoing

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
friendly and socially confident; at ease in talking to others
sociable, friendly, and confident

Examples

Her outgoing personality makes her popular at every event.
As an outgoing person, he finds it easy to make new friends.
Although his brother is shy, Mark is very outgoing and talkative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extroverted - outgoing and socially confident
  • Antonym: shy - nervous or timid about social interaction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02266005-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, vehicle, or thing) leaving a place, or relinquishing a position or office.
leaving a place or position

Examples

The outgoing flights were delayed due to heavy fog.
The outgoing president gave a farewell speech to the staff.
All outgoing mail must be dropped off by 5 p.m. each day.

Synset Examples

an outgoing steamship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incoming - arriving at a place or assuming a new position

Etymology

From out + going, dating from Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01298564-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or official) leaving or about to leave a position or office.
leaving a position or office

Examples

The outgoing president delivered a farewell speech to the nation.
An outgoing CEO often helps their successor transition smoothly into the role.
The firm held a party to honor the outgoing chairperson's years of service.

Synset Examples

the outgoing president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incoming - about to take up a position or office
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01297308-a
