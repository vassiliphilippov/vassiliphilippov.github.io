closed-chain

1. ADJ

/ kloʊzd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / kləʊzd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(of a molecule) having atoms linked in such a way that they form a closed ring or cyclic structure, as opposed to an open chain.
having atoms linked in a closed ring

Examples

In organic chemistry, glucose can exist in both an open-chain and a closed-chain form.
Benzene is a well-known example of a closed-chain hydrocarbon.
Researchers synthesized a new closed-chain compound with unique properties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-chain - having atoms arranged in a linear or branched (non-cyclic) structure

Etymology

From 'closed', past participle of 'close', and 'chain'; used in chemistry to describe ring-shaped molecules.
