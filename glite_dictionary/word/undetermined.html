undetermined

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈtɜrmənd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
not decided or settled; not brought to a conclusion or established as definite.
not decided or settled

Examples

The winner of the match is still undetermined due to a disputed point.
Her future plans remain undetermined at this stage of the project.
The cause of the outage was undetermined after the initial investigation.

Synset Examples

an open question
our position on this bill is still undecided
our lawsuit is still undetermined

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undecided - not having reached a decision or conclusion
  • Antonym: resolved - decided or settled, especially after consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'indeterminatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'determinatus' (limited, defined).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndəˈtɜrmɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
not yet decided, established, or defined; not ascertained or settled
not yet decided or defined

Examples

The cause of the fire is still undetermined after a week of investigation.
The animal is of undetermined species, making classification difficult.
Her future plans remain undetermined at this stage.

Synset Examples

of undetermined species

Related Terms

  • Antonym: determined - decided or settled

Etymology

From Latin 'indeterminatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'determinare' (to limit, determine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01615331-a

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌndəˈtɜrmɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
not exactly decided, specified, or established; not known or fixed in advance
not decided or established

Examples

The cause of the fire remains undetermined pending further investigation.
They postponed the meeting to an undetermined date.
Her future plans are still undetermined at this time.

Synset Examples

of indeterminate age
a zillion is a large indeterminate number
an indeterminate point of law
the influence of environment is indeterminate
an indeterminate future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeterminate - not exactly known, established, or defined
  • Antonym: decided - clearly determined or resolved
  • Antonym: determined - established exactly or decided upon

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'determined', from Latin 'determinare' (to limit, fix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00743919-a
Synset POS: a
