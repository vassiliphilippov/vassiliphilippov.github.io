acclimatization

1. NOUN

/ əˌklaɪmətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌklaɪmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming accustomed to a new climate, environment, or altitude, allowing for better functioning in those conditions
adaptation to new climate or environment

Examples

Proper acclimatization is essential before climbing to high altitudes.
The research studied the acclimatization of plants to urban environments.
After a week of acclimatization, the team began their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of changing to suit new conditions

Etymology

From French 'acclimatization', based on 'climat' (climate).
