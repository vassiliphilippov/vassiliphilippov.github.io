caranda

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrændə / (AmE), / kəˈrændə / (BrE)
A South American palm whose leaves yield a wax similar to carnauba wax.
South American palm producing wax

Examples

The caranda is valued for the wax produced from its leaves.
Researchers studied the potential uses of caranda wax in cosmetics and polishes.
In rural Brazil, locals harvest caranda palms every season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba - a Brazilian palm tree whose leaves produce carnauba wax

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carandá', likely of indigenous South American origin.
