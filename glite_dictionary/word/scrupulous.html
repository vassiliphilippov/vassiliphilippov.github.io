scrupulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈskruːpjələs / (AmE), / ˈskruːpjʊləs / (BrE)
Very careful to do what is morally right and honest; acting with strict regard for what is considered proper or ethical.
having strong moral principles; careful and honest

Examples

She is known for her scrupulous attention to detail in every project she undertakes.
A scrupulous accountant would never approve such questionable expense reports.
Despite pressure to bend the rules, he remained scrupulous in his business dealings.

Synset Examples

less scrupulous producers sent bundles that were deceptive in appearance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unscrupulous - having or showing no moral principles; not honest or fair

Etymology

From Latin scrupulosus, from scrupulus (scruple, doubt).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskruːpjələs / (AmE), / ˈskruːpjʊləs / (BrE)
Extremely careful to do things properly and with great attention to detail.
extremely careful and thorough

Examples

She was scrupulous about checking every fact in the report before submitting it.
The surgeon performed the operation with scrupulous attention to cleanliness.
It is important to be scrupulous when handling confidential information.

Synset Examples

conscientious application to the work at hand
painstaking research
scrupulous attention to details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: careless - not giving attention or care; not thorough

Etymology

From Latin scrupulosus (anxious, careful), from scrupulus (small sharp stone, anxiety).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00310936-s
Synset POS: s
