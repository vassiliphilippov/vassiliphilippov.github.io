macrocytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊ.saɪˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊ.sɪˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
the presence of abnormally large red blood cells (macrocytes) in the blood, typically indicating certain medical conditions
presence of large red blood cells in blood
medical

Examples

The blood test revealed macrocytosis consistent with vitamin B12 deficiency.
Patients with macrocytosis may require further examination to determine the underlying cause.
The doctor explained that macrocytosis can be a sign of certain types of anemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microcytosis - presence of abnormally small red blood cells in blood

Etymology

From macro- (large) + Greek kytos (cell) + -osis (condition).
