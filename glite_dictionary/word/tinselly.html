tinselly

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪnsəli / (AmE), / ˈtɪnsəli / (BrE)
Shiny or glittering, especially in a way that is superficial or showy, like tinsel.
glittering or showy, like tinsel

Examples

The decorations looked tinselly but cheap.
She wore a tinselly dress that sparkled under the lights.
The parade was full of tinselly floats and shimmering costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glittery - shining with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless

Etymology

Formed from 'tinsel' + '-ly' (adjective suffix). 'Tinsel' from Old French 'estincele' (sparkle, spark).
