gentleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being kind, mild, and gentle in manner or character.
the quality of being gentle and mild

Examples

Her gentleness helped to calm the frightened child.
He spoke with such gentleness that everyone listened intently.
The gentleness of her touch made the procedure less intimidating.

Synset Examples

his fingers have learned gentleness
suddenly her gigantic power melted into softness for the baby
even in the pulpit there are moments when mildness of manner is not enough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly and considerate
  • Antonym: harshness - the quality of being rough or severe

Etymology

From Old French gentelesse, from Latin gentilis (of a clan, gentle, noble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being gradual or not steep, especially when referring to a slope or incline.
quality of being gradual or not steep

Examples

The gentleness of the hillside made it easy for children to climb.
Hikers appreciated the gentleness of the trail as it wound through the valley.
Because of its gentleness, the ramp was accessible to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steepness - the quality of being steep or rising sharply

Etymology

From Middle English gentilesse, gentlenesse, from Old French gentilesse, ultimately from Latin gentilis (of the same clan).
