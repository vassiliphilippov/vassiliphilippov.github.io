litany

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtəni / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəni / (BrE)
A form of prayer consisting of a series of petitions recited by a leader with fixed responses by the congregation.
series of prayer invocations with responses

Examples

The priest led the congregation in a solemn litany during the evening service.
They recited a litany of prayers for the sick and the suffering.
The litany was sung in a traditional call-and-response manner.

Etymology

From Late Latin litania, from Greek litaneia (supplication, entreaty).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtni / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəni / (BrE)
a long, repetitive, and often tedious list or account, especially of complaints or problems
tedious list or recital

Examples

The report outlined a litany of problems facing the company.
Tired of listening to his endless litany of grievances, she eventually walked away.
At the meeting, the manager presented a litany of customer complaints gathered over the month.

Synset Examples

the patient recited a litany of complaints
a litany of failures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalogue - a list of items, especially one that is tedious or exhaustive
  • Synonym: enumeration - the act of mentioning things one by one, especially in a tedious way
  • Antonym: summary - a brief statement of main points, opposite in length and tedium

Etymology

From Old French 'letanie', from Late Latin 'litania', from Greek 'litaneia' (supplication, entreaty).
