Xylomelum

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɒmələm / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɒmɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of small Australian trees or shrubs grown for their distinctive woody fruits and flowers.
genus of Australian trees or shrubs

Examples

The Xylomelum is easily recognized by its large, woody fruit and unique flower clusters.
Several species of Xylomelum are valued in Australian gardens for their ornamental appeal.
Researchers studied the seed dispersal mechanisms of the rare Xylomelum in Western Australia.

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) + 'melon' (apple), referring to the woody fruit.
