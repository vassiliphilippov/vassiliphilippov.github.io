giro

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A cheque paid by the British government to a person who is unemployed or receiving benefits, which can be cashed at a bank or post office.
British government benefit cheque
british

Examples

He received his giro in the mail every Thursday.
People queued outside the post office to cash their giro payments.
After losing his job, he relied on the giro to pay his rent.

Etymology

From Italian 'giro', meaning ‘circulation’, adopted by the British banking system.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A system of transferring money electronically from one bank or post office account to another, especially used in Britain.
British money transfer system
british

Examples

She arranged a giro to pay her monthly rent automatically.
In the UK, many benefits are paid directly via giro into recipients' bank accounts.
The giro system makes regular payments easier to manage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank transfer - an electronic movement of money between bank accounts

Etymology

From German 'Giro' (circulation of money), from Italian 'giro' (turn, circulation), from Latin 'gyrus'.
