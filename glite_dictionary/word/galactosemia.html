galactosemia

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˌlæk.təˈsi.mi.ə / (AmE), / ɡəˌlæk.təˈsiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder in which the body is unable to properly metabolize galactose due to a deficiency or absence of a specific enzyme
genetic disorder preventing galactose metabolism
medical

Examples

Newborns with galactosemia must avoid milk and dairy products to prevent serious complications.
Doctors routinely screen for galactosemia in the first days after a baby is born.
Early diagnosis and dietary management can help children with galactosemia lead healthier lives.

Etymology

From galactose (a type of sugar) + -emia (from Greek haima, blood), indicating presence in the blood.
