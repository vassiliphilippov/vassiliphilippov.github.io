Albizzia lebbeck

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪziə lɛˈbɛk / (AmE), / ælˈbɪziə lɛˈbɛk / (BrE)
a large, spreading tree of the Old World with big leaves, globular clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and long seed pods that rattle in the wind
large Old World tree with rattling seed pods

Examples

The Albizzia lebbeck is often planted as a shade tree in parks and gardens.
On windy days, the seed pods of the Albizzia lebbeck make a distinctive rattling sound.
Botanists admire the delicate flowers and foliage of the Albizzia lebbeck during the blooming season.

Etymology

From the genus name Albizzia, named after Italian nobleman Filippo degli Albizzi; species name from Latin.
