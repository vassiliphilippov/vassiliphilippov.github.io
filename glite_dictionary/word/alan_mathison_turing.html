Alan Mathison Turing

1. PROPN

/ ˈælən ˈmæθɪsən ˈtɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈælən ˈmæθɪsən ˈtjʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
(1912–1954) English mathematician and computer scientist, known for inventing the concept of the Turing machine and for breaking German codes during World War II.
English mathematician, codebreaker, and pioneer of computer science

Examples

Alan Mathison Turing is widely regarded as the father of computer science.
Historians credit Alan Mathison Turing with playing a crucial role in breaking the Enigma code.
The legacy of Alan Mathison Turing continues to influence technology and artificial intelligence.
