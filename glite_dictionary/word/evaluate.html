to evaluate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvæljuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈval.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
to form a critical opinion or make a judgment about the value, quality, or significance of something
form a critical opinion or judgment

Examples

The committee will evaluate each application before making a decision.
It's important to evaluate your sources when conducting research.
She carefully evaluated the results of the experiment before drawing conclusions.

Synset Examples

I cannot judge some works of modern art
How do you evaluate this grant proposal?
We shouldn't pass judgment on other people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assess - to judge or estimate the value or quality of something
  • Synonym: judge - to form an opinion or conclusion about
  • Synonym: to appraise - to evaluate the worth or quality of

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth) via 'valutare' (to assess).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvæl.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvæl.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
to judge or determine the significance, worth, or quality of something; to assess carefully
to assess or judge the value or quality of

Examples

The board will evaluate each proposal before making a decision.
Teachers often evaluate students' progress using a variety of methods.
She evaluated the quality of the painting before making an offer.

Synset Examples

I will have the family jewels appraised by a professional
access all the factors when taking a risk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assess - to evaluate or estimate the nature, quality, or ability of
  • Synonym: to appraise - to evaluate the worth, significance, or status of
  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of

Etymology

From Latin 'evaluatus', past participle of 'evaluare', from 'e-' (out) + 'valere' (to be strong, worthy).
