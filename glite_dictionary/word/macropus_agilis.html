Macropus agilis

1. NOUN

/ məˈkroʊpəs əˈdʒɪlɪs / (AmE), / məˈkrəʊpəs əˈdʒɪlɪs / (BrE)
a small species of wallaby native to northern Australia and New Guinea, characterized by its agility and slender build
a small agile wallaby species

Examples

The Macropus agilis can often be spotted feeding in grassy woodlands at dawn and dusk.
Researchers observed a group of Macropus agilis migrating across the floodplains.
Many zoos now feature the Macropus agilis in their Australian animal exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallaby - small- to medium-sized kangaroo-like marsupial

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus Macropus (meaning 'large foot') and species name agilis (Latin for 'nimble, agile').
