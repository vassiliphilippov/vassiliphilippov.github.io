thinning

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making a liquid or substance less concentrated by adding a thinner or diluent
making a substance less concentrated

Examples

The thinning of the paint with water made it easier to apply evenly.
Before using the ink, a thinning with a special solvent is sometimes needed.
The recipe requires a thinning of the sauce to achieve the desired consistency.

Synset Examples

the cutting of whiskey with water
the thinning of paint with turpentine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilution - the process of making a liquid less concentrated

Etymology

From the verb 'thin' + -ing, referring to the process of making something thinner or less dense.
