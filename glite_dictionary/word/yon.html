yon

1. ADJ

/ jɑn / (AmE), / jɒn / (BrE)
(dialectal or archaic) Distant but within sight; yonder.
distant but within sight
archaic

Examples

Do you see yon mountain in the distance?
He pointed toward yon cottage by the lake, barely visible through the mist.
In yon field, the cattle are grazing peacefully.

Synset Examples

yonder valley
the hills yonder
what is yon place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yonder - distant but within sight

Etymology

From Middle English 'yon', variant of 'yonder', from Old English geond (beyond, over).

2. ADV

/ jɑn / (AmE), / jɒn / (BrE)
At or in that (usually distant) place; over there. Now chiefly archaic or dialectal.
at or in a distant place (archaic)
archaic

Examples

We could see the mountain rising high yon beyond the fields.
The travelers wandered here and yon in search of shelter.
Look yon, where the river bends out of sight.

Synset Examples

the house yonder
scattered here and yon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yonder - at or in that indicated place, over there

Etymology

From Middle English *yon*, variant of *yonder*, related to Old English *geond* (beyond).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
