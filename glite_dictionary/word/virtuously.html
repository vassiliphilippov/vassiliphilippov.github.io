virtuously

1. ADV

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows high moral standards or virtue
in a morally good manner

Examples

She always behaved virtuously and was admired by her peers.
He lived virtuously, never straying from his ethical beliefs.
The monk virtuously dedicated his life to helping others.

Synset Examples

she lived chastely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wickedly - in an evil or morally wrong manner

Etymology

From 'virtuous' + '-ly'; 'virtuous' from Middle English, from Old French vertuous, from Latin virtuosus (full of virtue).

2. ADV

/ ˈvɝtʃuəsli / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʃuəsli / (BrE)
In a moral, righteous, or virtuous manner.
in a morally upright way

Examples

She always tries to act virtuously, even when no one is watching.
He donated much of his earnings virtuously to charity each year.
The knight fought virtuously for justice and the common good.

Synset Examples

he acted morally under the circumstances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morally - in a moral manner
  • Antonym: wickedly - in an evil or morally wrong manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'vertuously', from Old French 'vertueux', from Latin 'virtus' (virtue) + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
