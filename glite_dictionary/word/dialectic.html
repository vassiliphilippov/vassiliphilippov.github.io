dialectic

1. NOUN

/ daɪəˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / daɪəˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
(in philosophy) the process or interaction in which opposing ideas or forces confront each other, resulting in a resolution or new understanding.
contradiction or interplay of opposing ideas

Examples

The debate between the two philosophers was driven by a dialectic of reason and emotion.
In Hegelian philosophy, progress is achieved through the dialectic of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis.
The historian analyzed the dialectic between individual freedom and collective responsibility in society.

Synset Examples

this situation created the inner dialectic of American history

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dialogue - conversation or discussion to resolve a conflict or reach understanding

Etymology

From Greek dialektikē (art of conversation or debate), from dialegesthai (to converse).

2. NOUN

/ daɪəˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / daɪəˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
a method of reasoning or argument that involves the exchange of logical arguments to arrive at the truth, especially as a formal system in philosophy
method of reasoning by logical exchange

Examples

The philosopher explained the concept of dialectic as a way to uncover deeper truths through discussion.
Hegel's dialectic shaped much of modern philosophy with its focus on thesis, antithesis, and synthesis.
In academic debates, dialectic helps participants refine their arguments and approach the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - the science of reasoning, proof, and validity

Etymology

From Greek dialektikē (art of debate, discussion), from dialegesthai (to converse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06173264-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
relating to dialectic; using or characteristic of logical argumentation or the dialectical method, especially in philosophy.
relating to dialectic or logical argumentation

Examples

The philosopher explained the dialectic approach to debating abstract concepts.
Her analysis was based on a dialectic framework that encouraged critical thinking.
Many ancient Greek thinkers valued dialectic reasoning as a path to truth.

Synset Examples

the dialectical method

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialectical - relating to dialectic; logical argumentation

Etymology

From Greek dialektikos (of conversation or debate), from dialegesthai (to converse).
