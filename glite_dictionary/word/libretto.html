libretto

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈbrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈbrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
The text or words of an opera or other long musical work.
text of an opera or musical

Examples

She carefully translated the libretto before the performance.
The composer teamed up with a poet to write the libretto for the new opera.
Many music lovers buy the libretto so they can follow along with the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - the written text of a play, film, or broadcast

Etymology

From Italian 'libretto', diminutive of 'libro' (book), from Latin 'liber'.
