suitable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsuːtəbəl / (BrE)
right or appropriate for a particular purpose, person, or situation; fitting or proper for the circumstances
appropriate; fitting for a purpose

Examples

This room is suitable for small meetings.
She found a suitable gift for her friend’s birthday.
Please wear suitable clothing for outdoor activities.

Synset Examples

a tractor suitable (or fit) for heavy duty
not an appropriate (or fit) time for flippancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: unsuitable - not right or appropriate for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Late Latin 'suitabilis', from Latin 'suitare', frequentative of 'sequi' (to follow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsuːtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsuːtəbəl / (BrE)
being appropriate or worthy of being chosen for a particular role or relationship, especially as a spouse
worthy of being chosen, esp. as a spouse

Examples

The family was searching for a suitable match for their eldest daughter.
They considered him a suitable candidate for marriage.
Her parents wondered if her boyfriend was truly suitable for her.

Synset Examples

the parents found the girl suitable for their son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriate - right or suitable for a particular situation
  • Antonym: unsuitable - not right or appropriate for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Late Latin 'suitabilis', from 'suitare' (to suit).
