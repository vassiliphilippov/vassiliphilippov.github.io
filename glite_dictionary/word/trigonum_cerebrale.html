trigonum cerebrale

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈɡoʊ.nəm səˈrɛ.brəˌli / (AmE), / trɪˈɡəʊ.nəm səˈrɛ.brəˌli / (BrE)
a bundle of white nerve fibers at the base of the brain connecting the hippocampi of both hemispheres and projecting to the thalamus and mamillary bodies
white fiber bundle at brain base
medical

Examples

The trigonum cerebrale plays a key role in connecting the hippocampi across the hemispheres.
Certain neurological studies focus on the functions of the trigonum cerebrale in memory processing.
Damage to the trigonum cerebrale can affect the communication between different parts of the brain.

Etymology

From Latin 'trigonum' (triangle) and 'cerebrale' (of the brain).
