achromia

1. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊ.mi.ə / (BrE)
An absence of normal pigmentation, especially in the skin or in red blood cells.
absence of normal pigmentation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with achromia after noticing extensive patches of white skin.
A complete lack of pigmentation, known as achromia, can be seen in certain genetic disorders.
Blood tests revealed achromia in the red blood cells indicating a rare condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albinism - Congenital absence of pigmentation in the skin, hair, and eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'chroma' (color).
