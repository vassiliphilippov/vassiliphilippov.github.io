delineation

1. NOUN

/ dəˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing something by drawing, painting, or outlining
representation by drawing or painting

Examples

The architect's delineation of the building revealed a modern and innovative design.
Her careful delineation of the landscape brought every tree and shadow to life.
The exhibit featured an impressive delineation of historical events through detailed paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the act of depicting, especially in art
  • Antonym: abstraction - representation that does not attempt to show exact likeness

Etymology

From Latin delineatio (outline, sketch), from delineare (to sketch, to outline).

3. NOUN

/ dəˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪnɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a drawing that shows the outlines or contours of shapes or objects; the act or product of representing forms with lines
drawing showing outlines or forms

Examples

The architect's delineation of the building included every decorative detail along the façade.
A precise delineation helps artists capture the true shape of an object.
The museum exhibited a nineteenth-century delineation of the city streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a line or set of lines that shows the shape or outer edge of something

Etymology

From Latin delineatio, from delineare (to mark out in lines, to sketch).
