witness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who sees an event, typically a crime or accident, and reports what happened
person who observes and reports an event

Examples

The police interviewed every witness who saw the accident.
As a witness to the robbery, she gave a detailed statement to the authorities.
The court called the witness to testify about what he observed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who watches or notices something
  • Synonym: bystander - a person who is present at an event but does not take part
  • Antonym: suspect - a person thought to be guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Old English 'wītnes' (attestation, testimony), related to 'witan' (to know).

7. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
A person who, in a legal context, attests to the authenticity of a document or signature by adding their own signature.
person who signs to verify a document
legal

Examples

The contract was valid because a witness had signed next to each party's signature.
You must have an independent witness sign your will for it to be legally binding.
The attorney asked the witness to add his signature to the document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attestor - a person who attests or bears witness to something, especially the signing of a document

Etymology

From Old English witnes (testimony, witness), from wit (knowledge) + -ness.
