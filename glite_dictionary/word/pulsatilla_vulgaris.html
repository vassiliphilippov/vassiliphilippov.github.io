Pulsatilla vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlsəˈtɪlə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌpʊlsəˈtɪlə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a European perennial plant, commonly known as the pasque flower, having usually violet or white flowers that bloom in spring
European perennial flowering plant

Examples

Pulsatilla vulgaris is often grown in gardens for its striking spring blooms.
The delicate violet petals of the Pulsatilla vulgaris emerge just as winter ends.
Many wildflower enthusiasts seek out Pulsatilla vulgaris during early spring walks in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasque flower - another common name for Pulsatilla vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pulsatilla' (diminutive of 'pulsare', to beat) and 'vulgaris' meaning common.
