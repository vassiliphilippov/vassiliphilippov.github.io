cochineal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.tʃɪ.niːl / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.tʃɪ.niːl / (BrE)
A Mexican scale insect that feeds on cacti and is used as the source of a natural red dye.
red dye-producing Mexican insect

Examples

The bright red color in the fabric was achieved using cochineal dye.
Some food products are colored with a natural extract made from cochineal.
Insects called cochineal were once harvested by hand from cactus plants in Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carmine - a vivid red pigment made from cochineal insects

Etymology

From Spanish *cochinilla*, from Classical Nahuatl *cōchīnlī* (insect).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.tʃəˌnil / (AmE), / ˈkɒtʃ.ɪ.niːl / (BrE)
a natural red dye made from the dried bodies of female cochineal insects, formerly used in food, cosmetics, and fabrics
red dye from cochineal insects

Examples

The artist used cochineal to create a brilliant red in her painting.
Historically, cochineal was a prized export from the Americas for European textile producers.
The dress was dyed with natural cochineal, giving it a rich crimson color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carmine - a vivid red dye derived from cochineal insects

Etymology

From Spanish 'cochinilla', from Latin 'coccinus' (scarlet), from Greek 'kokkos' (berry, kermes insect).
