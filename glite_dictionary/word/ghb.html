GHB

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː eɪtʃ ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː eɪtʃ ˈbiː / (BrE)
a depressant drug, often used recreationally or for date rape, that is available in liquid or powder form and is sometimes combined with alcohol
a recreational depressant drug, sometimes used illicitly

Examples

The clubgoers were warned about the dangers of GHB being slipped into drinks.
Because GHB is colorless and odorless, it is difficult to detect in beverages.
Some countries have classified GHB as a controlled substance due to its misuse.

Etymology

Abbreviation of gamma-hydroxybutyrate.
