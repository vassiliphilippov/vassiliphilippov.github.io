stowage

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪdʒ / (BrE)
a room or space used for storing things, especially on a ship or vehicle
room or space for storing things

Examples

All supplies were kept in the stowage below deck.
The yacht has limited stowage for large items.
Crew members checked the stowage to make sure everything was secure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage - the action or method of storing something for future use

Etymology

From Old French *estouage*, from *estouer* (to store up, stow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of packing or storing something away, especially in a space designed for holding cargo or goods
the act of packing or storing away

Examples

Careful stowage of goods ensures they arrive undamaged at their destination.
The ship's stowage plan must be completed before loading begins.
Overhead stowage compartments are provided for passengers' luggage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage - the action or method of storing something for future use

Etymology

From stow + -age; 'stow' from Old English 'stōw' (place), '-age' denoting action or result.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00813582-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee or charge for placing goods in storage, especially for loading goods on a ship
fee for placing goods in storage or on a ship

Examples

The shipping company included the stowage in their final invoice.
We had to pay extra stowage for our oversized crates.
All stowage charges must be settled before unloading the cargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage - the act of storing something or space for storing things

Etymology

From the verb 'stow' + '-age', modelled after similar nouns for fees (like postage).
