meandering

1. ADJ

/ miˈændɚɪŋ / (AmE), / miˈændərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a path, river, or road) following a winding or indirect course, often with many turns or curves.
winding; having many curves or turns

Examples

We walked along a meandering path through the tranquil forest.
The meandering river shaped the landscape over thousands of years.
Her thoughts took a meandering route as she daydreamed during the lecture.

Synset Examples

meandering streams
rambling forest paths
the river followed its wandering course
a winding country road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winding - having many bends and curves
  • Antonym: straight - extending continuously in the same direction without curves

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'meander', from Greek Maiandros, the name of a winding river in Phrygia.
