myxomatosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksəˌməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a usually fatal viral disease of rabbits, characterized by swelling and lesions, caused by the myxoma virus
viral disease of rabbits
medical

Examples

The outbreak of myxomatosis devastated the local rabbit population.
Farmers were concerned when cases of myxomatosis were reported nearby.
Researchers are studying how myxomatosis spreads among wild rabbits.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus) + -osis (disease), via New Latin.
