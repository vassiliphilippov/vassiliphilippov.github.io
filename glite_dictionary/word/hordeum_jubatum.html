Hordeum jubatum

1. NOUN

/ hɔrˈdiəm dʒuˈbeɪtəm / (AmE), / hɔːˈdiːəm dʒuːˈbeɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of barley native to North America and northeastern Asia, often grown for its highly ornamental flower heads with long, silky awns
ornamental barley species

Examples

Gardeners often plant Hordeum jubatum for its striking, silky flower heads that wave in the breeze.
The delicate awns of Hordeum jubatum shimmer beautifully in the summer sunlight.
In some regions, Hordeum jubatum grows wild along roadsides and open meadows.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hordeum' meaning 'barley' and 'jubatum' meaning 'mane-like' (referring to the plant's long awns).
