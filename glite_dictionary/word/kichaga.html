Kichaga

1. NOUN

/ kiˈtʃɑːɡə / (AmE), / kiˈtʃɑːɡə / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken by the Chaga people in northern Tanzania
Bantu language of northern Tanzania

Examples

The Kichaga language is widely spoken in the foothills of Mount Kilimanjaro.
Researchers are documenting traditional stories in Kichaga to preserve the region's oral history.
He learned basic Kichaga phrases before traveling to northern Tanzania.

Etymology

From Swahili 'Kichaga', where 'Ki-' denotes language and 'Chaga' is the ethnic group.
