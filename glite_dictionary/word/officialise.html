to officialise

1. VERB

/ əˈfɪʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃ.əl.aɪz / (BrE)
to make something official; to give formal approval or recognition to
make something official
british

Examples

The government will officialise the new policy next week.
They decided to officialise their partnership with a signed contract.
After years of discussion, the council finally officialised the language as part of the curriculum.

Synset Examples

We officialized our relationship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to officialize - make something official (American spelling)

Etymology

Formed from "official" + "-ise", after the pattern of other -ise/-ize verbs.
