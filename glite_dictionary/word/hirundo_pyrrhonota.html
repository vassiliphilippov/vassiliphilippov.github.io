Hirundo pyrrhonota

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈrʌndoʊ pɪˌroʊˈnoʊtə / (AmE), / hɪˈrʌndəʊ pɪˌrɒˈnəʊtə / (BrE)
A North American swallow that nests in colonies and builds bottle-shaped mud nests on cliffs and walls.
species of North American swallow

Examples

Every spring, large flocks of Hirundo pyrrhonota return to the same nesting sites along the canyon walls.
The bottle-shaped mud nests of Hirundo pyrrhonota are a remarkable sight on the undersides of highway bridges.
Researchers studied the colony behavior of Hirundo pyrrhonota to understand how they avoid parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliff swallow - North American swallow species known for nesting on cliffs; common name for Hirundo pyrrhonota

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hirundo' meaning 'swallow' and 'pyrrhonota' referring to 'flame-colored back.'
