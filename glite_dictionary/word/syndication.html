syndication

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming or managing an organization or group, especially one created for a specific business or financial purpose
formation or management of a syndicate

Examples

The syndication of the loan allowed several banks to share the risk.
The company specializes in the syndication of television programs.
Following its syndication, the group expanded its investments into several new sectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syndicate - a group formed to carry out business or financial activities jointly

Etymology

From syndicate, ultimately from French syndicat (council, association).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of selling written articles, cartoons, or similar content for publication in multiple newspapers or magazines at the same time
selling content to multiple publications

Examples

She makes a steady income from the syndication of her comic strip.
The syndication of opinion columns allows writers to reach national audiences.
Newspapers benefit from syndication by accessing quality articles they could not otherwise afford.

Synset Examples

he received a comfortable income from the syndication of his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients

Etymology

From 'syndicate', based on French 'syndicat' (council, association).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01117669-n
Synset POS: n
