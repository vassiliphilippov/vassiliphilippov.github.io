canonised

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkanəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
treated as sacrosanct or given authoritative status, often officially recognized as sacred or exemplary.
accorded authoritative or sacred status

Examples

The early writings of the philosopher were later canonised by academic institutions.
Some traditions have canonised certain texts, granting them unrivaled authority.
Once canonised, her theories became essential reading in the field of psychology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctified - made or declared holy
  • Synonym: deified - treated as a god or given god-like status
  • Antonym: condemned - expressed complete disapproval of

Etymology

From Latin 'canonizare', meaning 'to enact as a rule', via Old French and late Middle English.
