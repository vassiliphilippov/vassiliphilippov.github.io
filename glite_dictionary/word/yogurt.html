yogurt

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊɡɚt / (AmE), / ˈjəʊɡət / (BrE)
A semi-solid food made by fermenting milk with bacteria, often eaten plain or flavored.
fermented milk food product

Examples

She enjoys a bowl of fresh yogurt with berries in the morning.
Greek yogurt has a thicker texture than regular varieties.
Adding yogurt to your diet can be beneficial for digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curd - the coagulated part of milk used to make cheese
  • Synonym: kefir - a fermented milk drink similar to yogurt

Etymology

From Turkish 'yoğurt', from Old Turkic 'yoğurt' (to be curdled or coagulated, to thicken).
