countermand

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌmænd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmænd / (BrE)
an order that cancels or reverses a previous command
order cancelling a previous command

Examples

When the general issued a countermand, all previous instructions were immediately revoked.
A sudden countermand from headquarters halted the deployment of troops.
The manager sent a countermand to reverse the earlier directive about overtime work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: command - an authoritative order

Etymology

From Old French 'contremander', from Latin 'contra' (against) + 'mandare' (to command).

2. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərˈmænd / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈmænd / (BrE)
to officially cancel or reverse a previous command or order
officially cancel a command or order

Examples

The general decided to countermand the previous orders given to the troops.
Due to new information, the CEO chose to countermand the company's announcement.
The manager had to countermand the directive after realizing it contained errors.

3. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərˈmænd / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
to revoke or cancel (an order or command) previously given
revoke or cancel a previous order

Examples

Due to the unexpected risk, the general decided to countermand the attack order.
The CEO can countermand any company policy she deems unwise.
After realizing the mistake, he immediately countermanded the shipment instructions.

Synset Examples

To ensure safety, he had to countermand all instructions.
The manager had to countermand the previous order.
She decided to countermand the travel plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revoke - to take back or withdraw (an order or agreement)
  • Synonym: to rescind - to officially cancel (an order, law, or decision)
  • Antonym: to confirm - to make an order or statement valid or official

Etymology

From French 'contre-' (against) and 'mander' (to command), from Latin 'mandare' (to order).
