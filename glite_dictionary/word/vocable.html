vocable

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəbl / (BrE)
A word or sequence of sounds that is uttered, especially one regarded as a distinct linguistic unit; a spoken word.
spoken word or uttered sound

Examples

The linguist analyzed each vocable in the singer's chant.
Children often use nonsensical vocables while learning to speak.
The song contains many repeated vocables that don't have literal meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: word - a single distinct meaningful element of speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'vocabulum' (name, word), from 'vocare' (to call).
