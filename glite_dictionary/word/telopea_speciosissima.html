Telopea speciosissima

1. NOUN

/ təˈloʊpiə spɪˌsiːəˈsɪmə / (AmE), / tɪˈləʊpɪə spɛsɪəʊˈsɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A shrub native to southeastern Australia, notable for its narrow leaves and dense, bright red flower clusters; also known as the New South Wales waratah.
Australian shrub with conspicuous red flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the Telopea speciosissima attract many birds during spring.
Gardeners prize Telopea speciosissima for its striking appearance and hardiness in Australian gardens.
The emblem of New South Wales is the Telopea speciosissima, reflecting its cultural significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waratah - Common name for Telopea speciosissima, an Australian native shrub with red flowers

Etymology

From Greek *telopos* (seen from afar) and Latin *speciosissima* (most beautiful).
