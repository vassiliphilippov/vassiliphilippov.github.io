Cheiranthus cheiri

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaɪərˈænθəs ˈkɪəraɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɪəˈrænθəs ˈkɪəri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of southern Europe, often called the wallflower, with fragrant yellow or orange flowers, often found growing on old walls or cliffs; sometimes classified in the genus Erysimum.
a fragrant European wallflower plant

Examples

The old stone walls were covered in Cheiranthus cheiri in early spring, creating a splash of yellow and orange.
Gardeners often plant Cheiranthus cheiri for its long-lasting fragrance and vivid blossoms.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Cheiranthus cheiri should instead be placed in the genus Erysimum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - common name for Cheiranthus cheiri, an ornamental plant with fragrant flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Cheiranthus, from Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'anthos' (flower), possibly alluding to its use in handheld bouquets; 'cheiri' is a species epithet.
