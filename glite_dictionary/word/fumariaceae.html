Fumariaceae

1. NOUN

/ fuːˌmɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / fjʊˌmɛərɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants comprising erect or climbing herbs, including bleeding heart, Dutchman's breeches, and fumitory, native to the Northern Hemisphere and southern Africa
a family of flowering plants (bleeding heart, fumitory, etc.)

Examples

The Fumariaceae includes several ornamental plants such as bleeding heart and Dutchman's breeches.
Many species in the Fumariaceae are cultivated for their delicate, lacy foliage and colorful flowers.
Recent genetic studies have led to the reclassification of Fumariaceae within the poppy family, Papaveraceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Fumaria, from Latin 'fumus' (smoke).
