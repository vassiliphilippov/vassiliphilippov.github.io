Algonquin

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkwɪn / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪn / (BrE)
a family of North American Indigenous languages spoken from eastern Canada to the Great Plains
family of North American Indigenous languages

Examples

The Algonquin languages were once spoken across much of the northeastern United States and Canada.
Researchers are documenting the vocabulary of the Algonquin language family.
Many names of rivers and places in North America come from Algonquin languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - the broader language family including Algonquin

Etymology

From Maliseet-Passamaquoddy *elakómkwik* (they are our relatives), via French *Algonquin*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06919747-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑːŋkwɪn / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪn / (BrE)
A member of a North American Indigenous people traditionally living in the subarctic regions of eastern Canada and speaking an Algonquian language.
member of an Indigenous people of Canada

Examples

The Algonquin were adept at surviving harsh subarctic winters using traditional knowledge and skills.
Many Algonquin traditions have been preserved and are celebrated today in eastern Canada.
He is studying the history and culture of the Algonquin people for his anthropology course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - family of related Indigenous languages of North America

Etymology

From French 'Algonquin', possibly derived from a Maliseet word meaning 'they are our relatives' or 'at the place of spearing fish and eels'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09666446-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ælˈgɑŋkwɪn / (AmE), / ælˈgɒŋkwɪn / (BrE)
of or relating to the Algonquin people, their tribe, or their language.
relating to the Algonquin people or language

Examples

The Algonquin language is still spoken by some communities in Canada.
Many Algonquin traditions have been preserved through oral storytelling.
She studied Algonquin history as part of her graduate research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - pertaining to the large group of related tribes and languages of which the Algonquin are a part

Etymology

From the name of the Algonquin people, possibly from Maliseet or Mi'kmaq Algonkin (they are our relatives/they are our allies).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02621859-a
Synset POS: a
