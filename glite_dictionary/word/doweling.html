doweling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊ.əlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.əlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of fastening objects together using dowels, typically in woodworking or construction
fastening with dowels

Examples

The carpenter used doweling to join the two boards securely.
Proper doweling ensures the furniture will remain stable for years.
He specializes in doweling techniques for antique furniture restoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowel - a pin, usually made of wood, used for holding two parts together

Etymology

From 'dowel' (small peg used in construction) + '-ing' (forming a noun indicating action).
