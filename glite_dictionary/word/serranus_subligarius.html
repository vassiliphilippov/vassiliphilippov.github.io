Serranus subligarius

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈrænəs ˌsʌblɪˈɡɛəriəs / (AmE), / sɛˈrænəs ˌsʌblɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A species of small sea bass found in warm shallow waters of the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of sea bass in the western Atlantic

Examples

The Serranus subligarius is commonly found near coral reefs in the western Atlantic.
Marine biologists observed the behavior of Serranus subligarius during the spawning season.
Anglers often catch Serranus subligarius while fishing in shallow coastal waters.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Serranus' (a genus of sea basses), 'subligarius' (from subligare, to bind underneath, possibly referring to markings).
