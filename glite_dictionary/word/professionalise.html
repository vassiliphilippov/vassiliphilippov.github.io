to professionalise

1. VERB

/ prəˈfɛʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more professional in character, standards, or methods
make more professional
british

Examples

Many companies aim to professionalise their staff by offering ongoing training and certification.
The association was set up to professionalise the standards of journalism in the region.
Efforts to professionalise sports coaching have led to higher qualifications and salaries.

Synset Examples

Philosophy has not always been professionalized and used to be a subject pursued only by amateurs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to professionalize - make professional or give a professional character to (American spelling)
  • Antonym: amateurize - make something more amateur in character

Etymology

From 'professional' + '-ise', following British spelling conventions; compare American 'professionalize'.

2. VERB

/ prəˈfəʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become professional, especially by adopting standards, methods, or practices typical of a profession or by engaging in an activity as a paid occupation
make or become professional

Examples

The club decided to professionalise its management structure to attract top talent.
Several amateur teams plan to professionalise in order to compete at a higher level.
Education reforms aim to professionalise teaching and raise standards in schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to professionalize - American English spelling of 'professionalise'

Etymology

From 'professional' + '-ise', following the pattern of verbs formed from nouns or adjectives.

Synset POS: v
