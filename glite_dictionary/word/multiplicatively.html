multiplicatively

1. ADV

/ ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that involves multiplication or operates by multiplication, rather than by addition.
in a multiplicative manner

Examples

The population grew multiplicatively over several decades, resulting in exponential growth.
When combining risks, the effects are often calculated multiplicatively rather than additively.
The errors compounded multiplicatively, causing the final result to be much larger than expected.

Synset Examples

the errors increased multiplicatively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: additively - in an additive manner; by addition

Etymology

Formed from 'multiplicative' + '-ly', from Latin 'multiplicare' (to multiply).
