thermel

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːrməl / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl / (BrE)
A thermometer that uses a thermoelectric current to measure temperature.
thermometer using thermoelectric current
medical

Examples

The scientist used a thermel to accurately gauge the temperature of the reaction.
Modern medical laboratories may employ the thermel for precise thermal measurements.
Because it relies on thermoelectric current, the thermel offers quick and reliable temperature readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermocouple - a device for measuring temperature using a junction of two metals
  • Synonym: thermometer - an instrument for measuring temperature

Etymology

From 'thermo-', relating to heat, and possibly modeled after 'thermocouple' or 'thermal'.
