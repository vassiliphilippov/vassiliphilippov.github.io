Informel

1. NOUN

/ ɛ̃.fɔrˈmɛl / (AmE), / ɛ̃.fɔːˈmɛl / (BrE)
A French movement in abstract art, especially in the 1950s, characterized by irregular application of colors and forms, similar to abstract expressionism.
French abstract art movement of the 1950s

Examples

Many art historians consider Informel to be a critical stage in postwar European painting.
Her latest exhibition reflects the influence of both Informel and American abstract expressionism.
The gallery features a collection of works by leading figures of Informel.

Synset Examples

Around 1945 a number of European and American painters established a crucial phase in modern art known broadly as Art Informel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract expressionism - American art movement known for gestural abstraction and emotional intensity

Etymology

From French 'informel' (formless), used to describe art lacking formal structure.
