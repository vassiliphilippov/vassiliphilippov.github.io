Lithophragma

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌθɑːˈfræɡmə / (AmE), / lɪˌθɒˈfræɡmə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to western North America, commonly known as woodland stars, belonging to the saxifrage family.
genus of western North American perennial herbs

Examples

Several species of Lithophragma are prized by gardeners for their delicate, star-shaped flowers.
We saw Lithophragma blooming along the rocky slopes during our hike in California.
The botanist collected a specimen of Lithophragma for further study in the lab.

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + phragma (fence), referring to the plant's habitat.
