incentive

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛntɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛntɪv / (BrE)
an additional payment or benefit offered to employees to encourage increased productivity or motivation at work
additional payment or reward to motivate employees

Examples

The company introduced a new incentive to reward employees who exceed their sales targets.
Year-end incentives helped to boost team performance across all departments.
A financial incentive can be an effective way to encourage productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonus - an extra payment to employees in addition to their normal wages

Etymology

From Latin incentivum (something that sets the tune), from incinere (to strike up).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛntɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛntɪv / (BrE)
something that encourages a person to do something or to work harder; a motivating influence.
something that encourages action or effort

Examples

The company offered a bonus as an incentive for employees to meet their sales targets.
Tax reductions can serve as an effective incentive for investment in new technologies.
Without the right incentive, people may not be motivated to change their behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way
  • Antonym: disincentive - something that discourages or restrains action or effort

Etymology

From Late Latin incentivum (something that sets the tune, incentive), from Latin incentus (set the tune, spur on).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
