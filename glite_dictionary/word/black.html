Black

1. NOUN

/ blæk / (AmE), / blæk / (BrE)
A person belonging to a racial group with dark skin, especially of African ancestry; often capitalized and used as an identity term.
person of African ancestry

Examples

Many Blacks in the United States celebrate their heritage during Black History Month.
The university increased scholarships for Blacks and other underrepresented groups.
Some Blacks face unique challenges related to their identity and history in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American - an American of African descent
  • Antonym: white - a person belonging to the racial group with light skin
