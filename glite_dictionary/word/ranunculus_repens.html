Ranunculus repens

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ˈrɛpənz / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ˈrɛpɛnz / (BrE)
a perennial European herb with long creeping runners, commonly known as creeping buttercup, often found in moist grasslands and gardens
a creeping buttercup plant species

Examples

Ranunculus repens often invades lawns and moist meadows, outcompeting native grasses.
Gardeners frequently struggle to control Ranunculus repens due to its rapid spread by runners.
The bright yellow flowers of Ranunculus repens bloom from late spring to early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping buttercup - common name for Ranunculus repens, a creeping perennial plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'ranunculus' meaning 'little frog' (alluding to wet habitats), and 'repens' meaning 'creeping'.
