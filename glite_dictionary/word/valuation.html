valuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌval.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an estimate of the worth or value of something, especially a property or asset
estimate of value or worth

Examples

After the expert's valuation, the house was listed for a much higher price.
We need an official valuation of the artwork before the auction.
The insurance company requested a valuation of my jewelry collection.

Synset Examples

the valuation of this property is much too high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal - an act of assessing something or someone
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of making a judgment about something

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth), via French 'évaluation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaljuːˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of judging or estimating the worth or value of something
estimation of worth or value

Examples

The bank requested a formal valuation of the property before approving the loan.
She placed a high valuation on honesty and integrity in her employees.
Experts provided an accurate valuation for the collection of rare coins.

Synset Examples

he set a high valuation on friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal - an act of assessing the value or quality of something
  • Synonym: assessment - the evaluation or estimation of the nature, quality, or ability of someone or something
  • Synonym: valuation - in law, a determination of value for taxation or compensation

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth) via Late Latin 'valuatio' (a valuing).
