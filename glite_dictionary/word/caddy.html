caddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædi / (AmE), / ˈkædi / (BrE)
a small container, often with a lid, used for storing tea
container for storing tea

Examples

She kept her favorite green tea in a decorative caddy on the shelf.
The antique silver caddy was passed down through generations of her family.
He opened the caddy and scooped out a spoonful of loose-leaf tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canister - a container, usually cylindrical, for storing dry goods

Etymology

From Malay 'kati', a measure of weight for tea, adopted into English in the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈkæd.i / (AmE), / ˈkæd.i / (BrE)
to act as a caddie and carry golf clubs for a player
carry golf clubs for a player

Examples

He offered to caddy for his friend during the tournament.
After school, she would sometimes caddy at the local golf club.
Many teenagers learn responsibility by caddying in the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddie - a person who carries golf clubs for a player

Etymology

From 'caddie', originally Scottish, from French 'cadet' (meaning younger son, junior).
