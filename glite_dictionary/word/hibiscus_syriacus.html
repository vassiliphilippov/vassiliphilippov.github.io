Hibiscus syriacus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs sɪˈriːəkəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs sɪˈrɪəkəs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia, widely grown for its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers in various colors.
a flowering shrub native to Asia

Examples

The Hibiscus syriacus in our garden blooms with vibrant purple flowers every summer.
Gardeners prize Hibiscus syriacus for its long flowering season and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
She planted a row of Hibiscus syriacus along the fence to add color and privacy to her backyard.

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus name Hibiscus and the species name syriacus, meaning 'of Syria'.
