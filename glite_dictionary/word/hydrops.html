hydrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.drɑps / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.drɒps / (BrE)
An abnormal swelling of a part of the body caused by excessive accumulation of watery fluid in cells, tissues, or cavities.
swelling from excess body fluid
medical

Examples

The patient developed hydrops due to severe heart failure.
Fetal hydrops is a concerning condition often detected during prenatal scans.
Doctors monitored the hydrops to determine the best course of treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edema - swelling caused by fluid accumulation in the body

Etymology

From Greek 'hydōr' (water) + '-ops' (appearance).
