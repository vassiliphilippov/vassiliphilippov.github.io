wispy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪspi / (AmE), / ˈwɪspi / (BrE)
Thin, delicate, and weak in appearance or form.
thin and weak in appearance

Examples

The kitten had a wispy body and barely squeaked when it meowed.
His wispy frame made him seem even younger than he was.
She wore a wispy dress that seemed to float in the summer breeze.

Synset Examples

a wispy little fellow with small hands and feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frail - physically weak or delicate
  • Antonym: sturdy - strong and solid in form or constitution

Etymology

From 'wisp' (a small bundle or flock), with the suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪspi / (AmE), / ˈwɪspi / (BrE)
thin, faint, or lacking clarity or distinctness; not clearly defined or strong
thin or faint; lacking clarity

Examples

Through the fog, we could see only a wispy outline of the old house.
As time passed, her memories of the event became increasingly wispy.
The artist captured only a wispy suggestion of the landscape in soft charcoal.

Synset Examples

a dim figure in the distance
only a faint recollection
shadowy figures in the gloom
saw a vague outline of a building through the fog
a few wispy memories of childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined
  • Synonym: dim - not bright, clear, or distinct
  • Synonym: shadowy - full of shadows, indistinct or faint

Etymology

From "wisp" (a small bundle or streak) + "-y".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00785775-s
Synset POS: s
