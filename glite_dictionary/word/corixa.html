Corixa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪksə / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪksə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic insects in the family Corixidae, commonly known as water boatmen.
genus of aquatic insects (water boatmen)

Examples

The Corixa species are often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.
Scientists identified several Corixa in the water sample during the survey.
Members of the genus Corixa are commonly known as water boatmen due to their swimming style.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Corixidae - family of aquatic insects including water boatmen

Etymology

From Greek kōrix (bug).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪksə / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪksə / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic insects (family Corixidae), commonly known as water boatmen, which are characterized by their oar-like hind legs used for swimming.
genus of water boatmen insects

Examples

Several species within the genus Corixa can be found in freshwater ponds across Europe.
Scientists are studying the feeding behaviors of Corixa in controlled aquatic environments.
Unlike some other water bugs, members of the genus Corixa are not known to bite humans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Corixidae - family of aquatic insects known as water boatmen

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'koris' (bug).
