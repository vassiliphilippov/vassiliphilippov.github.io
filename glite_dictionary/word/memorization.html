memorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛmərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛmərʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the action or process of learning something so that it can be recalled exactly from memory
the act of committing to memory

Examples

Her daily memorization of vocabulary helped her excel in her language class.
The memorization of multiplication tables is often part of early math education.
Actors spend hours on memorization to prepare for their performances.

Synset Examples

the actor's memorization of his lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recitation - the act of repeating something aloud from memory

Etymology

From Latin memoria (memory) + -ization.
