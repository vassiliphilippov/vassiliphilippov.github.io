sheepman

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːpmən / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpmən / (BrE)
a man whose occupation is herding or tending sheep, especially on open range
male sheep herder

Examples

The sheepman rode out early every morning to check on his flock.
Local ranchers and sheepmen gathered to discuss grazing rights on the open range.
As a sheepman, he spent most of his days in the hills with his dogs and sheep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who tends and rears sheep

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːpmæn / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpmən / (BrE)
a man whose job is to raise or tend sheep
man who raises/tends sheep

Examples

The sheepman checked his flock early every morning before sunrise.
As a sheepman, he had a deep knowledge of the land and the animals he cared for.
Competition between cattlemen and the local sheepman led to many disputes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who tends and rears sheep

Etymology

From sheep + man, combining the animal 'sheep' with 'man' to specify occupation.
