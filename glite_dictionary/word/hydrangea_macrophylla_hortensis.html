Hydrangea macrophylla hortensis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒiə məˈkrɒfɪlə hɔːrˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈdreɪndʒɪə mæˈkrɒfɪlə hɔːˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub with round-headed flower clusters that open green and mature to pink or blue, commonly grown as a garden plant
garden hydrangea shrub with colored flower clusters

Examples

The Hydrangea macrophylla hortensis in her backyard blooms brilliant blue in summer.
Gardeners often choose Hydrangea macrophylla hortensis for its striking, long-lasting flower clusters.
Soil acidity can affect the color of Hydrangea macrophylla hortensis flowers, turning them pink or blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - shrub with showy flower clusters, genus Hydrangea

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' (water) + 'angeion' (vessel), Latin 'macrophylla' (large-leaved), and Latin 'hortensis' (of a garden).
