Sabinea

1. NOUN

/ səˈbaɪniə / (AmE), / səˈbiːniə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees or shrubs from the West Indies, commonly known as carib wood.
genus of West Indian trees or shrubs

Examples

The botanist identified the rare Sabinea among the dense foliage of the Caribbean forest.
Some species of Sabinea are prized for their attractive flowers and hardwood.
Researchers are studying how Sabinea adapts to changes in the island's climate.

Etymology

Named in honor of Joseph Sabine, a British naturalist (1770–1837).

2. NOUN

/ səˈbiːniə / (AmE), / səˈbiːniə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees or shrubs native to the West Indies, known as carib wood.
genus of West Indian trees and shrubs

Examples

The Sabinea is prized for its durable wood and vibrant flowers.
Botanists discovered several new specimens of Sabinea during their expedition in the Caribbean.
The leaves of the Sabinea species are characteristically compound and deciduous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legume - plant in the pea family (Fabaceae)

Etymology

Named after Joseph Sabine, an English naturalist and secretary of the Royal Horticultural Society.
