quadrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drət / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.reɪt / (BrE)
a square or nearly square-shaped object; an object shaped like a square.
square or square-shaped object

Examples

The specimen was notable for its unusually large quadrate at the center.
In geometry class, we studied the properties of a quadrate.
Architects sometimes use a quadrate in designing floor plans for symmetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square - a plane figure with four equal straight sides and four right angles

Etymology

From Latin quadratus (squared), from quattuor (four).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drət / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.reɪt / (BrE)
a cubelike object, especially one having equal dimensions or nearly so on all sides.
cubelike object

Examples

The ancient architect placed a quadrate at each corner of the structure for balance.
Each quadrate was meticulously carved to ensure uniformity and precision.
The collection included a rare gemstone cut into a perfect quadrate.

Etymology

From Latin 'quadratus' (made square), past participle of 'quadrare' (to make square).

3. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑː.drət / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.reɪt / (BrE)
having four sides and four angles; square or rectangular in shape
having four sides and four angles

Examples

The ancient building was constructed on a quadrate foundation.
A quadrate plot of land is ideal for efficient farming.
He admired the quadrate symmetry of the classical garden design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square - having all four sides of equal length and four right angles
  • Synonym: rectangular - having four sides and four right angles, opposite sides equal

Etymology

From Latin quadratus, past participle of quadrare 'to make square', from quattuor 'four'.
