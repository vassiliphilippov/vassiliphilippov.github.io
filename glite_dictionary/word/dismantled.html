dismantled

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈmæntəld / (AmE), / dɪsˈmæntəld / (BrE)
Having been taken apart, torn down, or broken up so that it no longer functions or exists as a whole.
taken apart; torn down

Examples

The old factory stood empty and dismantled for years before it was finally demolished.
After the festival ended, the once vibrant stage was completely dismantled and cleared away.
The government promised to restore schools in dismantled neighborhoods affected by the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyed - completely ruined or put out of existence
  • Antonym: assembled - brought together into a single unit; put together

Etymology

From 'dismantle', based on Old French desmanteler (to strip of covering).
