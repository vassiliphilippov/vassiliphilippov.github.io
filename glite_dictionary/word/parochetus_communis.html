Parochetus communis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæroʊˈkiːtəs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈkiːtəs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
A trailing herb found in Asia and Africa, characterized by trifoliate leaves and cobalt blue flowers.
a trailing herb with blue flowers

Examples

Parochetus communis is often grown in botanical gardens for its striking cobalt blue flowers.
The hillside was covered in Parochetus communis, making the landscape vibrant and colorful.
Botanists have studied the medicinal properties of Parochetus communis in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Parochetus' as a genus name given by botanists, 'communis' meaning 'common'.
