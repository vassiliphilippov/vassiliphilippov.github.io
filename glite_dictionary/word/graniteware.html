graniteware

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænəˌtwɛr / (AmE), / ˈɡran.ɪt.wɛə / (BrE)
Enamel-coated metal kitchenware or cookware with a mottled, stone-grey appearance resembling granite.
enamel kitchenware with granite-like look

Examples

My grandmother still bakes bread in her old graniteware roasting pan.
The flea market had a selection of vintage graniteware coffee pots in various colors.
Collectors value certain types of graniteware because of their unique patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enamelware - metal cookware coated with enamel

Etymology

Formed from 'granite' (the rock) + 'ware', referring to the stone-like appearance.
