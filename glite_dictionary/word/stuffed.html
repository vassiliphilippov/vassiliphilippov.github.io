stuffed

1. ADJ

/ stʌft / (AmE), / stʌft / (BrE)
Feeling very full after having eaten a large amount of food.
very full after eating

Examples

After three slices of cake, I was absolutely stuffed and couldn't eat another bite.
He leaned back in his chair, feeling stuffed after the holiday feast.
Whenever we go to that restaurant, I always leave stuffed.

Synset Examples

a full stomach
I feel stuffed

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: full - having eaten enough so as not to feel hungry

2. ADJ

/ stʌft / (AmE), / stʌft / (BrE)
filled with a substance or material, especially as preparation or for decorative or culinary purposes.
filled with something

Examples

We served a delicious stuffed chicken for the holiday dinner.
The children hugged their favorite stuffed animal tightly at bedtime.
The baker made stuffed pastries filled with cheese and herbs.

Synset Examples

a stuffed turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filled - containing as much as possible; full
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied

Etymology

From the verb "stuff," from Old French "estoffer" (to equip, furnish), from Germanic origin.
