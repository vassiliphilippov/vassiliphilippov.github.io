reclamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making land that was previously unusable suitable for habitation or cultivation
making land usable again

Examples

The government invested heavily in reclamation projects to turn marshes into farmland.
Coastal reclamation has provided new space for city development.
Efforts at reclamation have dramatically increased the region's arable land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former condition

Etymology

From Latin 'reclamatio' (a cry of protest), later used in English for recovery of land.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of recovering useful substances from waste products
recovery of useful substances from waste

Examples

The new plant focuses on reclamation of metals from electronic waste.
Efficient reclamation of plastic helps reduce environmental pollution.
Strict regulations now require reclamation of solvents in many industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recycling - the process of converting waste materials into reusable materials

Etymology

From Latin 'reclamatio' meaning 'a calling back'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of rescuing someone from moral error or wrongdoing and restoring them to the right path
rescuing and restoring to the right course

Examples

The charity was dedicated to the reclamation of troubled youth through guidance and education.
A major goal of the program is the reclamation of individuals who have strayed from the law.
The reclamation of former criminals often requires patience and community support.

Synset Examples

the reclamation of delinquent children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitation - the process of restoring someone to health or normal life
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior; deviation from what is right

Etymology

From Latin reclamatio (a protest, exclamation), from reclamo (to cry out against).
