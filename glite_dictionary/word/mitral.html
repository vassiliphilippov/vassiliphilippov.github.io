mitral

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪtrəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪtr(ə)l / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a miter, especially the ceremonial hat worn by certain clerics.
resembling a cleric's miter

Examples

The mitral valve gets its name because its shape is similar to a bishop's miter.
Several ceremonial robes feature mitral headdresses as part of their design.
The architecture of the tower included a mitral dome reminiscent of church headgear.

Etymology

From Latin 'mitra' (headband, turban), from Greek 'mitra' (headband).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪtrəl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtrəl / (BrE)
Relating to or located in or near the mitral valve of the heart.
relating to the mitral valve
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with mitral valve prolapse during the cardiac examination.
Surgical repair of the mitral valve can significantly improve heart function.
A murmur caused by mitral insufficiency was detected by the doctor.

Synset Examples

mitral insufficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitral valve - valve in the heart between left atrium and left ventricle

Etymology

From Latin 'mitra' (a headband or bishop's mitre), named for the valve's resemblance to a bishop's mitre.
