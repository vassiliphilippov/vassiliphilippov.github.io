cataract

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬.əˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkæt.ər.ækt / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the lens of the eye becomes cloudy, causing impaired vision
clouding of the lens of the eye
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended removing the cataract to restore the patient's vision.
Many older adults develop a cataract in one or both eyes over time.
Her blurry vision was eventually traced to a cataract forming on the lens.

Etymology

From Latin 'cataracta' (waterfall, portcullis), from Greek 'kataraktēs' (down-rushing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkatərakt / (BrE)
A large waterfall, especially one with a steep drop and intense flow of water.
large waterfall; violent rush of water

Examples

The mighty cataract thundered down the cliffs, sending mist into the air.
Tourists gathered to admire the breathtaking view of the cataract at sunset.
During the rainy season, the river transforms into a powerful cataract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfall - A flow of water falling from a height

Etymology

From Latin 'cataracta', meaning 'waterfall' or 'portcullis', from Greek 'kataraktēs' (something that strikes down).
