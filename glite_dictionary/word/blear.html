blear

1. ADJ

/ blɪɹ / (AmE), / blɪə / (BrE)
tired or dim, especially in the eyes; appearing weary or exhausted.
dim or weary, especially in the eyes

Examples

After working all night, his blear eyes struggled to stay open.
She stared with a blear expression after staying up for three days straight.
Early morning found him looking blear and disoriented from lack of sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleary - dim, blurred, or tired, especially in the eyes

Etymology

From Middle English *bleren* (to dim), probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. VERB

/ blɪr / (AmE), / blɪə / (BrE)
to make dim or indistinct; to blur or obscure (something, especially vision)
make dim or indistinct

Examples

The long night had bleared his tired eyes.
Cigarette smoke began to blear the outlines of the figures in the room.
Our memories of that event are bleared by the passage of time.

Synset Examples

The fog blurs my vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blur - make or become unclear or less distinct
  • Antonym: to sharpen - make or become clear or more distinct

Etymology

From Middle English 'bleren', possibly of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'bleren' (to bleat, blare).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313791-v
Synset POS: v
