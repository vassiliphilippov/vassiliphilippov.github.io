Tolypeutes

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊlɪˈpjuːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌtɒlɪˈpjuːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of armadillos in the family Dasypodidae, known for the two species of three-banded armadillo.
genus of three-banded armadillos

Examples

The Tolypeutes genus includes the well-known three-banded armadillo of South America.
Researchers observed how the Tolypeutes armadillos roll into a ball for protection.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize some populations within the Tolypeutes genus.

Etymology

From Greek tolypē (ball or bundle of yarn) and -utes (having the nature of).
