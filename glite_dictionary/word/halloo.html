halloo

1. VERB

/ həˈluː / (AmE), / həˈluː / (BrE)
to urge on, especially dogs during a hunt, by shouting or calling out
urge on with shouts, esp. in hunting

Examples

The hunters halloo the hounds as they chase the fox through the woods.
He hallooed loudly to urge the search party forward.
As the pack disappeared into the trees, she continued hallooing to keep them on the trail.

Synset Examples

halloo the dogs in a hunt

Etymology

Imitative origin, possibly from the Middle English hallow, related to the cry of 'halloo' used in hunting.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01233704-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ həˈlu / (AmE), / həˈluː / (BrE)
to shout 'halloo' in greeting or to attract someone's attention, especially in a loud or boisterous way
to shout a greeting or seek attention
archaic

Examples

He hallooed at his friend across the field to catch his attention.
The rider hallooed loudly as the fox appeared from the undergrowth.
She hallooed into the thick fog, hoping someone would answer her call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hail - to call out to; to greet loudly
  • Synonym: hello - to greet with a call or shout

Etymology

Imitative in origin, related to the call used in hunting or to attract attention.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00916014-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ həˈlu / (AmE), / həˈluː / (BrE)
a loud shout or cry used to attract attention, especially in hunting or calling out to someone
shout to attract attention
archaic

Examples

He gave a great halloo across the fields, hoping his friends would hear him.
A distant halloo echoed through the woods, startling the birds nearby.
With a cheerful halloo, the farmer called his dog back to the farmhouse.

Synset Examples

he gave a great halloo but no one heard him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hello - an exclamation used for greeting or attracting attention
  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry, especially to attract attention

Etymology

From Old English 'halō' or variant of 'hallo' (an exclamation), related to 'hello'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
