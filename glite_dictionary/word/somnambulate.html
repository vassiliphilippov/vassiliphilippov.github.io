to somnambulate

1. VERB

/ sɑmˈnæmbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / sɒmˈnæmbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
To walk while asleep; to engage in sleepwalking.
walk in one's sleep

Examples

Some people somnambulate several times a week without ever remembering their night-time journeys.
The doctor explained that children are more likely to somnambulate during periods of stress.
Last night, she was found to somnambulate out of her bedroom and into the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sleepwalk - to walk while asleep
  • Antonym: to awaken - to stop sleeping, to wake up

Etymology

From Latin somnus (sleep) + ambulare (to walk).
