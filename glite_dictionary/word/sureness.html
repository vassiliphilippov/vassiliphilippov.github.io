sureness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being confident and free from doubt; certainty or assurance, especially in oneself or one's abilities
certainty; confidence in oneself

Examples

She answered each question with a sureness that impressed the interviewers.
His movements on stage were marked by a sureness gained from years of practice.
The sureness in her tone left no room for argument.

Synset Examples

his assurance in his superiority did not make him popular
after that failure he lost his confidence
she spoke with authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - the feeling or belief that one can rely on something; firm trust
  • Synonym: assurance - certainty in oneself or one's abilities
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From sure + -ness, after Old French *seür* (sure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊrnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃʊənɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being steady, confident, and unfailing in action or belief
state of being steady and unfailing

Examples

The sureness of his movements showed years of experience.
She spoke with a sureness that impressed everyone in the room.
The sureness of her grip prevented the cup from slipping out of her hand.

Synset Examples

sureness of hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or confident
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From sure + -ness, meaning the state or quality of being sure.
