handiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændinɪs / (AmE), / ˈhændinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being conveniently available or accessible when needed
quality of being conveniently available

Examples

The handiness of the toolkit made quick repairs much easier.
She appreciated the handiness of having a grocery store nearby.
The handiness of the app saves me time every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convenience - the state of being able to proceed with something with little effort or difficulty

Etymology

From 'handy' (convenient, readily accessible) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændinɪs / (AmE), / ˈhændinɪs / (BrE)
skill and ease in using the hands; manual dexterity
manual skill or dexterity

Examples

Her handiness with tools impressed everyone in the workshop.
You need a certain level of handiness to fix small appliances at home.
Developing handiness is essential for artists and craftsmen.

Synset Examples

he can do any sort of work requiring handiness and dexterity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dexterity - skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of skill or grace, especially with the hands

Etymology

From 'handy' + '-ness', denoting the quality of being handy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05648769-n
Synset POS: n
