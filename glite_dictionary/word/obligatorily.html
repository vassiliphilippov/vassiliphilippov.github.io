obligatorily

1. ADV

/ əˈblɪɡəˌtɔrəli / (AmE), / əˈblɪɡətərɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is required by law, rule, or necessity; in an obligatory manner.
in an obligatory manner

Examples

All employees must obligatorily complete the safety training before starting work.
Masks were obligatorily worn in all public spaces during the outbreak.
Attendance at the meeting is obligatorily required for all department heads.

Synset Examples

this rule applies obligatorily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optionally - in a manner that is not obligatory; as an option

Etymology

Formed from 'obligatory' + '-ly'; 'obligatory' from Latin obligare (to bind).

2. ADV

/ əˈblɪɡəˌtɔrəli / (AmE), / əˈblɪɡətərɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is required by a rule, law, or necessity; in a manner that cannot be avoided
in a required or unavoidable way

Examples

Vaccinations for schoolchildren are obligatorily administered in some countries.
Employees were obligatorily enrolled in the new pension scheme by law.
The safety training must be obligatorily completed before starting work.

Synset Examples

the ministry considers that contributions to such a fund should be met from voluntary donations rather than from rates compulsorily levied.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntarily - in a way that is done by choice, not forced

Etymology

From obligatory + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'obligare' (to bind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00289970-r
Synset POS: r
