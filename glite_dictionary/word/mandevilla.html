Mandevilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌmændəˈvɪlə / (AmE), / ˌmændɪˈvɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical South American woody vines with large, showy racemose flowers, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of tropical flowering vines

Examples

The gardener planted a Mandevilla near the trellis to add vibrant color to the patio.
During the summer, the Mandevilla blooms with large trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red.
Many people choose a Mandevilla as a centerpiece for their container gardens due to its ornamental appeal.

Etymology

Named after Henry Mandeville (1773–1861), British diplomat and gardener.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmændəˈvɪlə / (AmE), / ˌmandɪˈvɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical South American perennial woody vines with large, showy flowers and milky sap.
genus of South American climbing vines

Examples

The Mandevilla is prized for its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the summer.
Gardeners often train Mandevilla vines to climb trellises or arbors for a dramatic effect.
While caring for a Mandevilla, be cautious of its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.

Etymology

Named after Henry John Mandeville (1773–1861), British diplomat and horticulturist.
