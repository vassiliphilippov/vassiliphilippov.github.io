stride

1. NOUN

/ straɪd / (AmE), / straɪd / (BrE)
The distance covered by a single step when walking or running.
distance of a single step

Examples

His long stride allowed him to cross the room in just three steps.
She measured the field by counting each stride as she walked.
The runner increased his stride to gain speed during the race.

Synset Examples

he stepped off ten paces from the old tree and began to dig

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step - the distance covered by a step when walking

Etymology

From Old English 'strīdan' (to stride, march), related to 'straddle'.

4. VERB

/ straɪd / (AmE), / straɪd / (BrE)
to walk with long, decisive steps in a particular direction, often with confidence or purpose
to walk with long, purposeful steps

Examples

He strode confidently into the meeting, making an immediate impression.
She strides along the beach every morning, enjoying the fresh air.
The general strode across the battlefield, issuing commands to his troops.

Synset Examples

He strode confidently across the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - to walk quickly with regular steps, especially in a group or with determination
  • Degree Lower: stroll - to walk in a leisurely way

Etymology

Old English *strīdan* (to stride), of Germanic origin.
