conductive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
having the property or ability to conduct heat, electricity, or sound
able to conduct heat, electricity, or sound

Examples

Copper is highly conductive, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
The researchers measured how conductive the new material was under various conditions.
You should not touch that wire unless you know it is not conductive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insulating - preventing the passage of heat, electricity, or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'conductivus', from 'conducere' (to lead, bring together).
