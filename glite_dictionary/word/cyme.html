cyme

1. NOUN

/ saɪm / (AmE), / saɪm / (BrE)
a flat-topped or convex cluster of flowers in which the central or terminal flower blooms first
type of flower cluster (botany)

Examples

The jasmine plant displays a cyme with its central flower blooming before the others.
In a typical cyme, the oldest flower is found at the top or the center.
Their garden includes several plants that have a distinctive cyme arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: umbel - a flower cluster with stalks of nearly equal length from a common center

Etymology

From Latin cymā (young shoot, sprout), from Greek kuma (anything swollen, wave, sprout).
