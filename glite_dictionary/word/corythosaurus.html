Corythosaurus

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌrɪθəˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪθəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of duck-billed, herbivorous dinosaurs from the late Cretaceous period, characterized by a distinctive crest on its head.
genus of crested duck-billed dinosaurs

Examples

The fossilized skull of a Corythosaurus reveals its distinctive crest.
Scientists believe Corythosaurus used its head crest for vocalization and display.
Several well-preserved skeletons of Corythosaurus have been found in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - member of family of duck-billed dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek 'korythos' (helmet) + 'sauros' (lizard).
