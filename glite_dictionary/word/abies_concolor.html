Abies concolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz kɒnˈkʌl.ər / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz kɒnˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
A species of fir tree native to central and western United States, known for its narrow upright crown and soft wood; commonly called white fir.
a species of white fir tree

Examples

The Abies concolor is often planted as an ornamental tree in parks and gardens.
Foresters study Abies concolor for its ecological role in western American forests.
The needles of Abies concolor have a distinct bluish-green tint.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Abies' meaning fir, and 'concolor' meaning uniformly colored.
