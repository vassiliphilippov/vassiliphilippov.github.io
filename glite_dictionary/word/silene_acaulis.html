Silene acaulis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈliːni əˈkɔːlɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni əˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A low, mat-forming perennial plant of Arctic and alpine regions in Europe and North America, known for its pink flowers.
mat-forming Arctic/alpine plant

Examples

Silene acaulis often forms dense cushions on rocky alpine slopes.
Botanists observed Silene acaulis blooming early in the short Arctic summer.
The survival strategies of Silene acaulis are studied for adaptation to harsh climates.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Silene + specific epithet acaulis (stemless).
