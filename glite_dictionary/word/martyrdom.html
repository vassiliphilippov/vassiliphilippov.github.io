martyrdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtɚˌdəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.tə.dəm / (BrE)
the suffering or death of a person because of their adherence to a religious faith or cause
death or suffering for one's beliefs

Examples

Her unwavering faith led her to martyrdom in the face of persecution.
The story of his martyrdom inspired others to continue the struggle for justice.
Many religious traditions honor the martyrdom of their early followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice - the act of giving up something valuable for a cause
  • Degree Lower: persecution - hostility or ill-treatment based on beliefs

Etymology

From Old English martyrdom (martyr's death), from martyr + -dom (state, condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtɚdəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtədəm / (BrE)
A state or experience of intense suffering, often endured for a cause or belief.
state of intense suffering

Examples

After months of isolation, he described his ordeal as a form of martyrdom.
Her martyrdom in silence made her friends more concerned for her wellbeing.
The martyrdom she endured during recovery was evident to everyone around her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - Extreme physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: suffering - The state of undergoing pain, distress, or hardship
  • Antonym: relief - A feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress

Etymology

From Old English martyrdom, from martyr (witness, especially one who suffers for a cause) + -dom (state or condition).
