to baby-sit

1. VERB

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪt / (BrE)
to take care of someone or something temporarily, especially a child, while the parent or owner is away
look after someone or something temporarily

Examples

Could you baby-sit my dog while I'm on vacation next week?
She agreed to baby-sit her niece every Friday evening.
Last summer, he used to baby-sit for several families around the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

I baby-sit the neighbor's plants when she is out of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to babysit - to look after children temporarily
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'baby' and 'sit', first attested in US English in the 1930s, originally referring to watching over babies.

2. VERB

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪt / (BrE)
to look after a child or children temporarily while their parents are away
look after children temporarily

Examples

I promised to baby-sit for my neighbors every Friday evening.
She often baby-sits her younger cousins when their parents go out.
Have you ever baby-sat for anyone outside your family?

Synset Examples

I cannot baby-sit tonight: I have too much homework to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babysitter - a person who looks after children while parents are away

Etymology

Formed from "baby" + "sit", first recorded in 1940s American English.
