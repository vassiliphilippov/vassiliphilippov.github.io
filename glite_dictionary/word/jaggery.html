jaggery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæɡəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɡəri / (BrE)
A type of unrefined brown sugar made from the sap of palm trees or sugarcane, commonly used in South Asian and African cooking.
unrefined brown sugar from palm sap

Examples

Traditional sweets in India are often flavored with jaggery instead of white sugar.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of jaggery to add a deep, caramel flavor.
After harvesting the sap, workers boil it down to produce jaggery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raw sugar - sugar that is only partially refined

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jágara', from Malayalam 'chaakarā' or Kannada 'bella', meaning coarse sugar.
