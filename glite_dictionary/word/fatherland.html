fatherland

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːðɚˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðəlænd / (BrE)
the country where one was born or feels a strong allegiance, often used patriotically
one's native country

Examples

He fought bravely to defend his fatherland during the war.
After many years abroad, she felt a deep longing for her fatherland.
Poets often write about their devotion to their fatherland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherland - a person's native country; often used interchangeably with 'fatherland'
  • Synonym: homeland - the country where someone was born or has roots

Etymology

From German 'Vaterland' (father's land).
