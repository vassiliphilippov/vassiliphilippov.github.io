arc

1. NOUN

/ ɑrk / (AmE), / ɑːk / (BrE)
a curve or a part of the circumference of a circle or other curved shape
a curved shape or section

Examples

A rainbow forms a bright arc across the sky.
The story follows the arc of the hero's journey from childhood to adulthood.
She drew an elegant arc on the paper with her pencil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - a continuously bending line without angles
  • Antonym: line - something long and straight, the opposite of a curve

Etymology

From Latin arcus (bow, arch, curve).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrk / (AmE), / ɑːk / (BrE)
A continuous electrical discharge through a gas, producing light and heat, caused by an applied electric field.
continuous electrical discharge through a gas

Examples

A lightning bolt is a natural form of an electric arc.
The welder created a bright arc to fuse the metal pieces together.
An arc lamp produces illumination by sustaining an electric discharge between two electrodes.

Etymology

From Latin 'arcus' (bow), referring to the shape of the discharge or spark.

3. NOUN

/ ɑrk / (AmE), / ɑːk / (BrE)
a continuous part of the circumference of a circle or other curve
part of a circle's curve

Examples

The rainbow formed a perfect arc across the sky.
To draw a circle, start by marking an arc with a compass.
The bridge has an elegant arc stretching over the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segment - the area between a chord and an arc of a circle
  • Antonym: chord - a straight line joining two points on a curve

Etymology

From Latin arcus (bow, arch).

4. VERB

/ ɑrk / (AmE), / ɑːk / (BrE)
to move or form in a curved shape or path; to make or become arched
form a curve or arch

Examples

The bird's wings arc gracefully as it glides through the air.
She watched the basketball arc toward the hoop and drop in.
Lightning sometimes arcs across the sky during fierce storms.

Synset Examples

her hips curve nicely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - to form or cause to form an arch, to curve
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight, not curved

Etymology

From Latin arcus (bow, arch).
