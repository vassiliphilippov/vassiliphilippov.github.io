topi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊpi / (BrE)
A lightweight hat, typically of cork or pith, worn in tropical countries as protection from the sun.
lightweight sun hat, often worn in tropics

Examples

He wore a topi to shield his face from the harsh afternoon sun.
Many colonial officers in India used to don a topi while traveling.
The lightweight topi kept her cool during the safari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pith helmet - a lightweight helmet made of pith material, worn for sun protection in the tropics

Etymology

From Hindi 'ṭopī' (hat), ultimately from Sanskrit 'ṭopī.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.pi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.pi / (BrE)
a large African antelope (Damaliscus lunatus) with long legs and a reddish-brown coat, known for its speed
large African antelope noted for speed

Examples

The topi is widely regarded as the fastest of all hoofed mammals in Africa.
During the migration, herds of topis can be seen crossing the plains.
A lone topi stood watchfully on a termite mound, scanning for predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running hoofed mammal found in Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Swahili 'topi', the local name for the animal.
