maritime

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːrɪˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmærɪtaɪm / (BrE)
connected with the sea, ships, navigation, or seafaring; relating to shipping or naval affairs.
relating to the sea or shipping

Examples

The country has a long maritime tradition due to its extensive coastline.
New regulations were introduced to improve maritime safety standards.
He studied maritime law at university, focusing on shipping contracts.

Synset Examples

nautical charts
maritime law
marine insurance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautical - relating to sailors, ships, or navigation
  • Antonym: terrestrial - relating to the land rather than the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'maritimus' (of the sea), from 'mare' (sea).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛrəˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmærɪtaɪm / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the sea, or characteristic of people living by the sea
relating to the sea or seacoasts

Examples

The region has a strong maritime tradition due to its long coastline.
Many maritime provinces depend on fishing and shipping for their economies.
The climate in maritime areas tends to be milder than inland regions.

Synset Examples

a maritime province
maritime farmers
maritime cultures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - situated away from the coast

Etymology

From Latin 'maritimus' (of the sea), from 'mare' (sea).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465432-s
Synset POS: s
