Helicteres isora

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪkˈtɪriz ɪˈsɔrə / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪkˈtɪəriːz ɪˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
An East Indian shrub often cultivated for its hairy leaves and orange-red flowers.
East Indian shrub with hairy leaves and orange-red flowers

Examples

The bark of Helicteres isora has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.
Gardeners appreciate Helicteres isora for its attractive orange-red flowers and hairy foliage.
Several herbal remedies are derived from the fruit of Helicteres isora.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name 'Helicteres' (from Greek heliktēr, “twisted, spiral”) and the species epithet 'isora.'
