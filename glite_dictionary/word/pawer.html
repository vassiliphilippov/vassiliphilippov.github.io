pawer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːə / (BrE)
a person who handles or caresses someone in a clumsy or overly familiar manner, often in a way that is unwelcome
person who handles others clumsily or familiarly

Examples

After the party, several people complained about the office pawer making unwelcome advances.
She quickly distanced herself from the persistent pawer at the club.
No one wanted to dance with the notorious pawer once his reputation spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groper - a person who touches someone else sexually without consent, typically in a public place

Etymology

Formed from 'paw' (verb, to touch clumsily) + '-er' (agent suffix).
