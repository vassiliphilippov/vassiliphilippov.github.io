kunzite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌnˌzaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.zaɪt / (BrE)
A pink to lilac variety of the mineral spodumene, used as a gemstone.
pink-lilac gemstone, variety of spodumene

Examples

The pale violet kunzite in her necklace glimmered under the bright restaurant lights.
Collectors prize kunzite for its delicate coloring and impressive clarity.
A jeweler showed me a ring set with a rare pink kunzite stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spodumene - a pyroxene mineral that is a source of lithium and often used as a gemstone

Etymology

Named after George F. Kunz, American mineralogist (1856–1932).
