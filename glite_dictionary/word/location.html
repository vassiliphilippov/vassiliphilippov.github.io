location

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a place outside a film or television studio where some or all scenes are filmed
filming site outside a studio

Examples

The director decided to shoot the entire movie on location in Hawaii for authenticity.
Shooting on location adds realism but increases production costs.
Finding the right location was crucial to capturing the mood of the script.

Synset Examples

they shot the film on location in Nevada

Related Terms

  • Antonym: studio - the main place or building where films or television programs are recorded

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular place or position in space; the manner in which something is situated
place or position

Examples

The location of the conference has changed to a larger venue downtown.
We need to choose a suitable location for our new store.
Her GPS quickly determined her exact location on the map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: place - a particular position or point in space
  • Synonym: position - the manner in which someone or something is placed or arranged

Etymology

From Latin 'locatio' (a placing), from 'locare' (to place).

3. NOUN

/ loʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of placing something in a particular position or place.
the act of placing something

Examples

The precise location of the wires in the wall was crucial to the installation.
Accurate location of sensors improves the safety of the system.
The location of the microphone was adjusted for better sound quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the act of placing something
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away

Etymology

From Latin *locare* (to place), from *locus* (place).
