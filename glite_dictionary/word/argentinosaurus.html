Argentinosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌhɛn.ti.nəˈsɔɹ.əs / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒɛn.tɪ.nəˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
a genus of enormous herbivorous dinosaurs from the middle Cretaceous period, discovered in Argentina, thought to be among the largest land animals ever to have lived
giant Cretaceous dinosaur from Argentina

Examples

The Argentinosaurus is believed to have reached lengths of over 30 meters.
Paleontologists discovered the first fossils of Argentinosaurus in Patagonia during the 1990s.
Due to its immense size, Argentinosaurus likely had few predators in its ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles from the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Latinized form of 'Argentina' (the country) + Greek 'sauros' (lizard).
