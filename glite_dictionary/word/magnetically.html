magnetically

1. ADV

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to or resembles magnetism, especially as though having an irresistible attraction.
in a way suggestive of magnetism or strong attraction

Examples

The two artists were magnetically drawn to each other's creative energy.
She smiled magnetically, captivating everyone in the room.
Iron filings lined up magnetically along the pattern of the field.

Synset Examples

he was magnetically attracted to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the force of attraction or repulsion produced by magnets

Etymology

From 'magnetic' + adverbial suffix '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'magnetis' (magnet stone).

2. ADV

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
By means of magnetism; using or relating to magnetic forces or properties.
by use of magnetism

Examples

The trains are magnetically levitated above the tracks for a smoother ride.
This substance can be magnetically separated from the non-magnetic materials.
The data is stored magnetically on hard drives in the computer lab.

Synset Examples

magnetically contaminated material

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - The physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces.

Etymology

From 'magnetic' + '-ally', with 'magnetic' from Greek 'magnetikos'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00508629-r
Synset POS: r
