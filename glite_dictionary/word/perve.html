perve

1. NOUN

/ pɝv / (AmE), / pɜːv / (BrE)
(informal) A person whose behavior is considered sexually inappropriate or deviant.
sexually inappropriate person (informal)
informal

Examples

The kids at school used to call him a perve because of his creepy stares.
I felt uncomfortable when that perve started following me around the park.
She warned her friends to stay away from the local perve who lurked near the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pervert - a person whose sexual behavior is considered unacceptable

Etymology

Shortened form of 'pervert'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ pɜrv / (AmE), / pɜːv / (BrE)
(slang) To look at someone with amorous or lecherous intentions, often in a way that is inappropriate or makes them uncomfortable.
look at someone lecherously (slang)
slang british

Examples

He was caught perving on his co-worker at the office party.
Stop perving on people at the gym—it's really creepy.
She felt uncomfortable when she realized someone was perving on her from across the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ogle - to stare at in a lecherous manner
  • Synonym: leer - to look at someone in an unpleasant, sexually interested way

Etymology

From 'pervert' (noun), used as a back-formation in slang.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02168498-v
Synset POS: v
