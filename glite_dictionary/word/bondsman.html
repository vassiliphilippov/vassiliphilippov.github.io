bondsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzmən / (BrE)
a man who is legally or forcibly bound to serve another person without pay; a slave or serf
man bound to serve without pay
archaic

Examples

The plantation owner treated the bondsman harshly, denying him any wages for his labor.
In medieval times, a bondsman was not free to leave the land he worked on.
The bondsman hoped one day to purchase his freedom and escape servitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is legal property of another and forced to obey them

Etymology

From 'bond' (meaning tie or obligation) + 'man', Middle English and Old Norse origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzmən / (BrE)
a man who promises to pay money if someone does not fulfill an obligation, especially one who acts as surety for a defendant's bail
man acting as surety; bail guarantor

Examples

The bondsman appeared in court to guarantee the defendant’s release on bail.
Without a bondsman, the accused would have remained in custody until trial.
She hired a bondsman to fulfill the financial requirements of her bail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surety - a person who takes responsibility for another's performance of an undertaking, e.g. appearing in court

Etymology

From bond + man, originally referring to someone bound by a legal obligation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzmən / (BrE)
A male slave or serf, especially in a historical context; a man who is owned by another or owes servitude to another person.
male slave or serf (historical)
archaic

Examples

Through the centuries, the bondsman toiled on the land for his master.
The plantation's bondsman was promised freedom after many years of loyal service.
Literature often depicts the harsh realities of life as a bondsman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person who is not a slave or serf

Etymology

From Middle English 'bondsman' (a man in bondage), from 'bond' (serf, slave) + 'man'.
