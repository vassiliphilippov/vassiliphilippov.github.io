hold-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a restriction or control that limits something, especially the amount or rate of increase
limitation or restriction

Examples

Many citizens demanded a hold-down on rent increases in the city.
The company implemented a strict hold-down on travel expenses to control costs.
Without a hold-down on emissions, pollution levels continued to rise.

Synset Examples

taxpayers want a hold-down on government spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction - something that limits or controls what someone can do
  • Synonym: constraint - a limitation or restriction

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'hold down', meaning to keep under control.
