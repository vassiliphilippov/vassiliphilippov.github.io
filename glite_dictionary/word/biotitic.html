biotitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪəˈtɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈtɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or containing biotite, especially as a constituent of rocks or minerals.
containing or relating to biotite (mineral)

Examples

The granite in this region is distinctly biotitic, containing large amounts of dark mica.
A biotitic layer within the rock sample indicated the presence of biotite during formation.
The geologist described the outcrop as a biotitic gneiss due to its mineral content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotite - a common phyllosilicate mineral within the mica group

Etymology

From biotite (a mineral) + -ic (adjectival suffix).
