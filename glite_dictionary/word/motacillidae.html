Motacillidae

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌtæsɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / məʊˌtæsɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, slender birds including pipits and wagtails, found in many parts of the world.
family of birds: pipits and wagtails

Examples

The family Motacillidae includes both the pipits and wagtails found across Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed several new species within Motacillidae in the mountainous regions.
Members of Motacillidae are known for their characteristic bobbing tail movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - a small songbird from the family Motacillidae
  • Synonym: wagtail - a small bird known for wagging its tail, in the family Motacillidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Motacilla (wagtail) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ moʊˌtæsɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / məʊˌtæsɪˈlɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized passerine birds including pipits and wagtails, found chiefly in Eurasia, Africa, and Australia.
family of birds: pipits and wagtails

Examples

The Motacillidae are known for their slender bodies and long tails, which are often wagged up and down.
Many ornithologists study the migration patterns of birds in the Motacillidae family.
Both pipits and wagtails belong to the Motacillidae, which can be found throughout much of the Old World.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - type of small songbird in the family Motacillidae
  • Synonym: wagtail - small insectivorous songbird, genus Motacilla, in the family Motacillidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Motacilla', a genus of wagtails, plus the taxonomic suffix '-idae' (denoting a family).
