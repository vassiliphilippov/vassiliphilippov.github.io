thyrotrophin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəʊˈtrɒfɪn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce thyroid hormones.
pituitary hormone stimulating thyroid function
medical

Examples

A deficiency of thyrotrophin can lead to an underactive thyroid gland.
Doctors measured his thyrotrophin levels to assess his thyroid function.
The release of thyrotrophin is regulated by signals from the hypothalamus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroid-stimulating hormone - another common name for thyrotrophin

Etymology

From Greek 'thureos' (shield, referring to the thyroid) + 'trephein' (to nourish), by analogy with 'gonadotrophin'.
