to trivialise

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪviəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɪviəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem less important, serious, or significant than it really is
make something seem unimportant

Examples

It's wrong to trivialise the challenges faced by people with mental illness.
Please don't trivialise my concerns; they are important to me.
Some media reports tend to trivialise serious environmental problems.

Synset Examples

Don't trivialize the seriousness of the issue!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to minimise - reduce something to its smallest possible amount or degree
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - make something seem more important or significant than it is

Etymology

From trivial + -ise, patterned after similar verbs.
