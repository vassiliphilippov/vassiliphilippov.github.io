Cnossos

1. PROPN

/ nɑːˈsɒs / (AmE), / nɒˈsɒs / (BrE)
an ancient city on the island of Crete, noted as the principal center of Minoan civilization from about 2000 to 1400 BC.
ancient city on Crete, center of Minoan civilization

Examples

Archaeologists discovered elaborate frescoes during the excavation of Cnossos.
The palace of Cnossos is famous for its labyrinthine architecture.
Tourists flock to the ruins of Cnossos to learn about Minoan history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Knossos - alternative spelling of Cnossos, ancient city on Crete

Etymology

From Greek Κνωσός (Knōsós), the ancient name of the city.
