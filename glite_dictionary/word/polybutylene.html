polybutylene

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈbjutɪlin / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbjuːtɪliːn / (BrE)
a polymer made from butylene, used in lubricants, synthetic rubber, and piping systems
a polymer of butylene used in industry

Examples

The water pipes in the house were made of polybutylene, which was common in the 1980s.
Manufacturers often use polybutylene in making flexible hoses for plumbing systems.
Some lubricants contain polybutylene to improve their performance under pressure.

Etymology

From poly- (many) + butylene (a hydrocarbon based on butene), denoting a polymer formed from butylene units.
