chaja

1. NOUN

/ tʃaˈhɑ / (AmE), / tʃaˈhɑ / (BrE)
A large crested bird native to southern Brazil and Argentina, also known as the southern screamer.
large crested South American bird

Examples

The loud cry of the chaja can be heard from miles away across the wetlands.
Researchers observed a flock of chajas feeding near the marshy lake.
The chaja is known for its distinctive crest and noisy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screamer - any of three South American birds in the family Anhimidae

Etymology

From Spanish 'chajá', from Guaraní describing the bird's call.
