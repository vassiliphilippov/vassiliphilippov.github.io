nave

1. NOUN

/ neɪv / (AmE), / neɪv / (BrE)
the central part of a church building, intended to accommodate the congregation, usually flanked by aisles
central part of a church

Examples

The choir sang beautifully from the nave during the Easter service.
Tall columns lined the nave, leading the eye toward the altar.
Tourists admired the stained glass windows that illuminated the nave.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'navis' (ship), alluding to the shape of the building.
