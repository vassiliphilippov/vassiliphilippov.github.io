Haemanthus coccineus

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmænθəs ˌkɒkˈsɪniːəs / (AmE), / hiːˈmænθəs ˌkɒkˈsɪniːəs / (BrE)
A striking South African plant with large, prostrate leaves marked with reddish-purple and showy coral-red flower heads surrounded by fleshy bracts.
species of striking South African flowering plant

Examples

The Haemanthus coccineus produced its bright red flower heads after the first rains of autumn.
Gardeners value Haemanthus coccineus for its unusual foliage and vibrant blooms.
In its native habitat, Haemanthus coccineus thrives in well-drained, sandy soil.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name from Greek *haima* (blood) and *anthos* (flower), species name *coccineus* meaning 'scarlet' in Latin.
