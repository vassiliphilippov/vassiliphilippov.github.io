honorific

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːnəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnərˈɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a title, word, or act) showing or conferring honor or respect.
showing or conferring honor or respect

Examples

Membership in that society is an honorific distinction granted only to a select few.
She received an honorific title in recognition of her years of community service.
The university awarded him an honorific degree for his contributions to science.

Synset Examples

honorific social status commonly attaches to membership in a recognized profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectful - showing deference or respect
  • Antonym: derogatory - showing a critical or disrespectful attitude

Etymology

From Latin honorificus ('honoring'), from honor ('honor') and -ficus ('making').

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
a title, word, or form of address expressing respect or high status
expression of respect or title of honor

Examples

In some cultures, using an honorific is essential when speaking to elders.
The professor was often addressed with the honorific 'Doctor' by her students.
Japanese language includes a range of honorifics to convey respect.

Synset Examples

the Japanese use many honorifics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - a name describing a person's position or job

Etymology

From Latin honorificus (honoring), from honor (honor) + -ficus (making).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07084923-n
Synset POS: n
