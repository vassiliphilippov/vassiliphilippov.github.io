conjugally

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑndʒəɡəli / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to marriage or the relationship between married partners.
in a marital manner

Examples

The couple were bound conjugally for over fifty years.
Benefits were distributed conjugally under the law to both partners.
They lived conjugally as husband and wife despite legal complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugal - relating to marriage or the relationship of spouses

Etymology

From Latin 'coniugalis' (of marriage), from 'coniugium' (marriage).
