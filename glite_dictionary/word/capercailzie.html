capercailzie

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpərˈkeɪzi / (AmE), / ˌkapəˈkeɪlzi / (BrE)
A large, black woodland grouse native to Europe and Asia, notable for the male's elaborate courtship display.
large woodland grouse of Europe and Asia

Examples

The sight of a male capercailzie displaying its plumage is a highlight for birdwatchers.
Forests in Scotland are home to the rare capercailzie.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered capercailzie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - a bird of the family Tetraonidae, often hunted as game

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'capall-coille' meaning 'horse of the woods.'
