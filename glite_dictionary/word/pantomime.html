pantomime

1. VERB

/ ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (BrE)
to express or represent something by exaggerated gestures and bodily movements without using speech
to act out using gestures without speech

Examples

During rehearsal, the actors pantomimed the entire scene because no props were available.
She pantomimed washing her hands to show what the character was supposed to do.
At the party, everyone had to pantomime a famous movie scene and let the others guess.

Synset Examples

The acting students mimed eating an apple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - to use only movement and expression to act out something without words

Etymology

From Latin pantomimus, from Greek pantómimos (imitator of all).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (BrE)
A theatrical performance or act in which the story is told through body movements, facial expressions, and gestures, without spoken words.
wordless performance using gestures

Examples

The children enjoyed watching the pantomime at the local theater during the holidays.
He communicated his frustration through pantomime when words failed him.
During the silent pantomime, the actors conveyed the entire story without uttering a single word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - A performance or performer imitating actions without words

Etymology

From Latin 'pantomimus' (mime actor), from Greek 'pantomimos' ('panto-' meaning 'all' + 'mimos' meaning 'imitator, actor').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00551053-n
Synset POS: n
