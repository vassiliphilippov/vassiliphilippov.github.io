Marat

1. PROPN

/ məˈrɑː / (AmE), / ˈmærɑː / (BrE)
Jean-Paul Marat (1743–1793), a French revolutionary leader known for his radical journalism and involvement in the overthrow of the Girondists; assassinated in his bath by Charlotte Corday.
French revolutionary leader (1743–1793)

Examples

Many historians regard Marat as one of the most radical voices of the French Revolution.
A famous painting by Jacques-Louis David depicts the assassination of Marat.
The newspaper published by Marat was influential among revolutionaries in Paris.

Etymology

From the surname of Jean-Paul Marat (1743–1793), of Sardinian and Swiss origin.
