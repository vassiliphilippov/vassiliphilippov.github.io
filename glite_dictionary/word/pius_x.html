Pius X

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaɪəs ˈtɛnθ / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəs ˈtɛnθ / (BrE)
(1835–1914) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1903 to 1914, known for condemning religious modernism and being canonized as a saint in 1954.
Pope and Roman Catholic saint (1835–1914)

Examples

Many Catholics regard Pius X as a model of humility and devotion to the poor.
The reforms of Pius X had a lasting impact on the Catholic Church.
Pius X was canonized for his deep personal piety and care for the needy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope - the Bishop of Rome and head of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Degree Lower: Pius IX - pope who preceded Pius X
  • Degree Higher: Pius XI - pope who succeeded Pius X
