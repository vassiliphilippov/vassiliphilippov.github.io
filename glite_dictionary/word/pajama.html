pajama

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdʒɑmə / (AmE), / pəˈdʒɑːmə / (BrE)
A loose-fitting jacket and trousers worn for sleeping or lounging, usually made of soft fabric and worn as nightclothes.
loose-fitting nightwear

Examples

She changed into her favorite pajamas before going to bed.
Every Christmas, the children receive new pajamas from their grandparents.
After dinner, he put on his pajamas and settled in to watch a movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyjamas - British spelling of 'pajama', loose-fitting nightclothes

Etymology

From Hindi 'pājāma', from Persian 'pāy' (leg) + 'jāma' (garment).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈdʒɑːmə / (AmE), / pəˈdʒɑːmə / (BrE)
A pair of loose-fitting trousers, usually tied at the waist, traditionally worn by men and women in some Asian countries.
loose trousers worn in Asia

Examples

In some regions, men wear pajama as traditional attire for festivals and ceremonies.
The cotton pajama is comfortable in the hot, humid climate of South Asia.
She bought a pair of silk pajama during her travels in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyjama - Alternative British spelling of 'pajama'

Etymology

From Hindi *pājāma*, via Persian *pāy-jāmeh* (leg garment).
