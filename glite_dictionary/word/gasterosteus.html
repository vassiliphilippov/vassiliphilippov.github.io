Gasterosteus

1. NOUN

/ gæs.təˈroʊ.sti.əs / (AmE), / ˌɡastəˈrɒstɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of small fish known as sticklebacks, belonging to the family Gasterosteidae.
genus of sticklebacks (fish)

Examples

The three-spined Gasterosteus is commonly found in European rivers and streams.
Researchers study Gasterosteus for insights into evolutionary biology and adaptation.
Several species of Gasterosteus display remarkable variations in body armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickleback - any small fish of the family Gasterosteidae

Etymology

From Greek 'gaster' (belly) and 'osteon' (bone).
