cosmotron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑz.məˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.məˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a large proton synchrotron that uses frequency modulation of an electric field to accelerate protons, historically used in particle physics research
large proton synchrotron (particle accelerator)

Examples

The cosmotron was one of the early particle accelerators used to study subatomic particles.
Researchers used the cosmotron to investigate the properties of high-energy protons.
The invention of the cosmotron marked a significant advancement in the field of nuclear physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrotron - a type of particle accelerator that accelerates charged particles in a circular path

Etymology

Formed from Greek 'kosmos' (universe) and '-tron' (instrument suffix).
