Pecten irradians

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛktən ɪˈreɪdi.ənz / (AmE), / ˌpɛktɛn ɪˈreɪdɪ.ənz / (BrE)
A small species of scallop native to shallow waters and mud flats along the Atlantic coast of North America.
a small Atlantic coast scallop species

Examples

The edible Pecten irradians is harvested commercially along the eastern seaboard.
Children searched the tidal pools for Pecten irradians shells on the mud flats.
Overfishing caused a noticeable decline in the population of Pecten irradians in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a marine bivalve mollusk with a fan-shaped shell

Etymology

From Latin: 'pecten' meaning 'comb' and 'irradians' meaning 'radiating'.
