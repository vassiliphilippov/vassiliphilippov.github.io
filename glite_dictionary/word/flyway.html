flyway

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪ.weɪ / (BrE)
a route regularly used by migrating birds.
bird migration route

Examples

Millions of waterfowl travel along the Mississippi flyway every spring and autumn.
Scientists monitor bird populations by tracking their movements along established flyways.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting key habitats along the Pacific flyway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migration route - route taken by animals during migration

Etymology

From 'fly' (to move through the air) + 'way' (path, route).
