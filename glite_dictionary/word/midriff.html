midriff

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌrɪf / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.rɪf / (BrE)
the middle part of the human torso, especially the front area between the chest and waist
middle part of the torso (front)

Examples

She wore a crop top that revealed her midriff at the party.
High-waisted pants can help cover the midriff area for a modest look.
During yoga class, his shirt lifted and exposed his midriff.

Synset Examples

young American women believe that a bare midriff is fashionable

Etymology

Old English 'midhrif' (diaphragm, middle of the body); 'mid' (middle) + 'hrif' (belly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌrɪf / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.rɪf / (BrE)
the region of the body between the chest and the waist, or the muscular partition (diaphragm) separating the abdominal and thoracic cavities
region between chest and waist; diaphragm

Examples

The tight shirt revealed her midriff, drawing attention to her toned abs.
Doctors explained that the midriff plays a key role in respiration.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully examined the patient's midriff for any signs of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphragm - the muscular membrane that separates the chest from the abdomen and plays a major role in breathing

Etymology

Old English midhrif, from mid ('middle') + hrif ('belly, womb').
