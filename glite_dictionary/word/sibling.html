sibling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
a brother or sister; one of two or more children having one or both parents in common
a brother or sister

Examples

She is the youngest sibling in her family.
Having a sibling can help children learn to share and cooperate.
My siblings and I grew up in the same town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brother - a male sibling
  • Synonym: sister - a female sibling

Etymology

From Latin 'sobrinus' (cousin), adapted in modern English in the early 20th century to mean 'brother or sister'.
