vena gastrica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə ɡæˈstrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌveɪnə ɡæˈstrɪkə / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the walls of the stomach
vein draining the stomach walls
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the vena gastrica before proceeding with the next step of the operation.
Damage to the vena gastrica can cause significant bleeding during stomach surgery.
An enlarged vena gastrica may be a sign of increased pressure in the portal vein.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'gastrica' (of the stomach).
