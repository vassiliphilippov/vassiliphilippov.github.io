sparge

1. VERB

/ spɑrdʒ / (AmE), / spɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to scatter or sprinkle (a surface) with liquid; to wet lightly, especially by spraying or sprinkling
scatter or sprinkle with liquid

Examples

The brewer will sparge the grains to extract more sugars during the brewing process.
Farmers sometimes sparge the fields with water to keep the soil moist.
He carefully sparged the plants to avoid overwatering their roots.

Synset Examples

Sprinkle the lawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinkle - to scatter liquid or small particles over a surface
  • Antonym: soak - to make something very wet by immersing in liquid

Etymology

From Latin spargere (to scatter, strew).

2. NOUN

/ spɑrdʒ / (AmE), / spɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an act of sprinkling or spraying liquid, especially water, over something; commonly used in brewing to refer to rinsing grain with water to extract sugars
act of sprinkling or spraying liquid

Examples

A gentle sparge ensured all the grains were thoroughly rinsed during the brewing process.
He performed a quick sparge over the garden to keep the plants hydrated in the heat.
The technician instructed us to use a warm water sparge to extract maximum flavor from the malt.

Synset Examples

baptized with a sprinkling of holy water
a sparge of warm water over the malt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinkle - to scatter small drops or particles over a surface
  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in very small drops

Etymology

From Middle French *espargir* (to sprinkle), from Latin *spargere* (to scatter, sprinkle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ spɑrdʒ / (AmE), / spɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to agitate or aerate a liquid by introducing air or a gas, especially in industrial, scientific, or brewing processes
introduce air or gas to agitate a liquid

Examples

The technician began to sparge the solution with nitrogen to remove impurities.
During the brewing process, they sparge the mash to extract more sugars.
To improve oxygen levels in the fish tank, simply sparge the water with air.

Synset Examples

sparge the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aerate - to introduce air into a liquid or substance

Etymology

From Latin 'spargere' (to scatter, sprinkle), via obsolete sense 'to sprinkle'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
