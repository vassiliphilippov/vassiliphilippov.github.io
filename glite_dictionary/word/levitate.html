to levitate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛvɪteɪt / (BrE)
to rise or cause to rise and float in the air, apparently defying gravity, usually by supernatural or magical means
rise or float in the air

Examples

The magician appeared to levitate above the stage, stunning the audience.
Some people believe that intense meditation can enable a person to levitate.
In the film, the young girl seemed to levitate out of her bed during the haunting.

Synset Examples

The guru claimed that he could levitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid or in the air

Etymology

From Latin 'levis' (light) and medieval Latin 'levitat-' (made light), from 'levitas' (lightness).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛv.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to cause (something or someone) to rise and float in the air, seemingly defying gravity, especially by means of supernatural powers or tricks
cause to float in the air

Examples

The magician managed to levitate a table in front of the amazed audience.
Some people claim to have the power to levitate objects with their minds.
In the performance, the assistant appeared to levitate above the stage.

Synset Examples

The magician levitated the woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid or in the air

Etymology

From Latin 'levis' (light) via modern Latin 'levitat-' (raised).
