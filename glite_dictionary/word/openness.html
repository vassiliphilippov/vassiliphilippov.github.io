openness

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənnəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənnəs / (BrE)
a willingness to consider new ideas, experiences, or opinions without prejudice or bias
willingness to consider new ideas or experiences

Examples

She admired his openness to new cultures and perspectives.
The company's openness to innovation has led to many successful products.
Effective leaders foster an atmosphere of openness and trust in their teams.

Synset Examples

he was testing the government's receptiveness to reform
this receptiveness is the key feature in oestral behavior, enabling natural mating to occur
their receptivity to the proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptivity - the quality of being ready or willing to receive something
  • Antonym: closed-mindedness - unwillingness to consider different ideas or opinions

Etymology

From open + -ness, modelled after Old English openness (state of being open).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənnəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being honest and transparent, especially about one's actions or intentions; the absence of secrecy or concealment
willingness to be open and transparent

Examples

The manager's openness about the company's finances built trust with employees.
She valued openness in her friendships and always encouraged honest conversation.
Government openness is essential for a functioning democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • Antonym: secrecy - the state of keeping something secret or hidden

Etymology

From open + -ness, dating to Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04661423-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənnəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being open and unobstructed, especially allowing free passage or a clear view
absence of obstructions; clear space or view

Examples

The openness of the countryside made the sunset visible for miles.
We admired the openness of the mountain valley as we hiked.
The openness of the office layout encourages collaboration and easy movement.

Synset Examples

the openness of the prairies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exposure - the state of being without cover or protection; openness to view or the elements

Etymology

From open + -ness, forming a noun from the adjective 'open.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05091112-n
Synset POS: n
