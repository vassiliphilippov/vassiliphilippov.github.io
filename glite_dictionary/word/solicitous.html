solicitous

1. ADJ

/ səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs / (AmE), / səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
showing concern, care, or anxiety for someone or something; attentive and anxious to help or protect.
showing concern or anxious care

Examples

Her solicitous questions made it clear she cared about his well-being.
He was always solicitous of his elderly neighbor's comfort and safety.
Their teacher was especially solicitous when any student seemed upset.

Synset Examples

solicitous parents
solicitous about the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concerned - worried or showing care about something
  • Antonym: indifferent - lacking concern or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'sollicitus' (anxious, worried).

2. ADJ

/ səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs / (AmE), / səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
showing anxious concern or care for someone's well-being; attentive and eager to help or protect
anxiously concerned; attentive

Examples

His solicitous manner comforted the worried patients in the waiting room.
She was always solicitous about her grandmother's needs, checking on her every day.
The teacher gave a solicitous glance at her struggling student, ready to offer help if needed.

Synset Examples

solicitous about her health
made solicitous inquiries about our family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attentive - paying close attention to something
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin sollicitus (anxious, concerned).
