to disturb

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɝb / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːb / (BrE)
to change the arrangement or position of something, often causing disorder or interruption
change arrangement or position

Examples

Please do not disturb the papers on my desk; I have arranged them carefully.
The wind disturbed the sand, leaving patterns across the beach.
She disturbed the neatly set table by moving the plates around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disarrange - to put out of order or neatness
  • Synonym: to disrupt - to interrupt the normal arrangement of something

Etymology

From Latin 'disturbare', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'turbare' (to throw into disorder).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstɝb / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːb / (BrE)
to affect someone deeply and unpleasantly, usually by causing worry, anxiety, or emotional discomfort
emotionally affect; upset

Examples

The tragic news disturbed her deeply for several days.
Some of the images in the film may disturb sensitive viewers.
His words disturbed me more than I expected.

Synset Examples

This book upset me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upset - to make someone feel worried or unhappy
  • Synonym: to unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or uneasy
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less worried

Etymology

From Latin 'disturbare', from 'dis-' (completely) + 'turbare' (to throw into disorder).
