carborundum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrbəˈrʌndəm / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəˈrʌndəm / (BrE)
A hard, abrasive material consisting of silicon carbide crystals, used in grinding and cutting tools.
abrasive silicon carbide material

Examples

The grinding wheel is coated with carborundum for maximum efficiency.
She used a carborundum stone to sharpen her garden tools.
Industrial sandpapers often contain carborundum to enhance their abrasive properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicon carbide - a very hard substance made from silicon and carbon, also known as carborundum
  • Degree Higher: diamond - the hardest known natural material, used as an abrasive
  • Degree Lower: emery - an abrasive powder or grain, less hard than carborundum

Etymology

Trademark, from carbon and corundum (a form of aluminum oxide).
