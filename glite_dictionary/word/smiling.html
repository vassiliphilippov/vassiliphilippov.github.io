smiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
A facial expression in which the corners of the mouth are turned upward, typically indicating pleasure, amusement, or friendliness.
act or expression of smiling

Examples

Her smiling made everyone in the room feel more comfortable.
The photograph captured his genuine smiling as he greeted old friends.
There was constant smiling and laughter during the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - A facial expression of displeasure or concern in which the corners of the mouth are turned downward
  • Synonym: grin - A broad smile, often showing the teeth

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
Having a smile or expressing happiness, pleasure, or optimism through one's facial expression.
showing a smile; cheerful-looking

Examples

The smiling children greeted their teacher at the door every morning.
She handed him a smiling invitation to join her at the party.
A smiling waiter welcomed us to the restaurant with a friendly nod.

Synset Examples

Come to my arms, my beamish boy!
a room of smiling faces
a round red twinkly Santa Claus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: frowning - showing a serious or saddened expression

Etymology

Present participle of 'smile', from Middle English 'smilen'.
