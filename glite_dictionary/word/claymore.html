claymore

1. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.mɔː / (BrE)
a type of antipersonnel land mine that projects its blast in a specific direction towards approaching enemies
directional antipersonnel land mine

Examples

The soldiers carefully placed a claymore along the expected path of enemy troops.
He was trained in the use of the claymore and other defensive devices during basic training.
Because the claymore is directional, it must be oriented correctly to maximize its effectiveness.

Etymology

Named after the Scottish "claymore" sword, due to its shape and name engraved on the device.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.mɔː / (BrE)
a large double-edged broadsword used formerly by Scottish Highlanders
large Scottish broadsword

Examples

The museum displayed a genuine claymore from the 16th century.
During the reenactment, he wielded a heavy claymore as the Highlanders did.
Legends spoke of a warrior who never parted with his trusty claymore.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'claidheamh mór' meaning 'great sword'.
