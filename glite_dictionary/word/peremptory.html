peremptory

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛmptəri / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmptəri / (BrE)
insisting on immediate obedience or action, leaving no opportunity for denial or refusal
insisting on immediate obedience; not allowing refusal

Examples

The manager gave a peremptory order, expecting everyone to follow without question.
She closed the discussion with a peremptory gesture, leaving no room for further debate.
His peremptory tone silenced the entire room.

Synset Examples

spoke in peremptory tones
peremptory commands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperious - arrogantly domineering or overbearing
  • Synonym: authoritative - commanding and self-confident; likely to be respected and obeyed

Etymology

From Latin peremptorius (decisive, final), from perimere (to take entirely, destroy).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛmp.tɔːr.i / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmp.təri / (BrE)
(of an order, decision, or manner) insisting on immediate attention or obedience, leaving no opportunity for denial or refusal.
insisting on immediate obedience

Examples

The judge issued a peremptory order requiring them to vacate the premises immediately.
His peremptory tone left no room for argument or discussion.
A peremptory decision was made to end the negotiations without delay.

Synset Examples

a peremptory decree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decisive - having the power to decide; conclusive
  • Synonym: authoritative - able to be trusted as being accurate or true; reliable

Etymology

From Latin peremptorius (destructive, decisive), from perimere (to take entirely, destroy).

3. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛmp.tə.ri / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmp.təri / (BrE)
insisting on immediate attention or obedience in an arrogant or domineering way; offensively self-assured or dictatorial
arrogantly demanding or dictatorial

Examples

Her peremptory tone made it clear she expected everyone to obey immediately.
The manager issued a peremptory order, leaving no room for discussion.
His peremptory gesture silenced the room at once.

Synset Examples

an autocratic person
autocratic behavior
a bossy way of ordering others around
a rather aggressive and dominating character
managed the employees in an aloof magisterial way
a swaggering peremptory manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritative - confident, commanding
  • Synonym: bossy - fond of giving people orders; domineering
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others

Etymology

From Latin peremptorius (decisive, final), from perimere (to take away entirely).
