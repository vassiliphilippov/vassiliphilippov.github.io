macintosh

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɪn.tɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪn.tɒʃ / (BrE)
a lightweight waterproof fabric, typically rubberized, used especially for making raincoats.
waterproof fabric used for raincoats

Examples

The raincoat was made of a durable macintosh that kept me dry during the storm.
Early macintosh fabric was known for its distinctive rubbery smell.
His jacket, crafted from macintosh, resisted water even in a heavy downpour.

Etymology

Named after Charles Macintosh (1766–1843), Scottish chemist who invented the material.
