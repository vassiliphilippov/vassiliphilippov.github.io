inexpedient

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
not suitable or advisable for achieving a particular purpose; unwise or impractical in a situation.
not suitable or advisable

Examples

It would be inexpedient to proceed without further consultation.
The plan was deemed inexpedient by the board due to high risks.
Delaying the meeting any longer seemed inexpedient given the urgent circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expedient - suitable or convenient for achieving a particular end

Etymology

From Latin 'inexpediens', from 'in-' (not) + 'expedient' (advantageous).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌɛkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɛkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
not suitable or advisable in a particular situation; unwise or impractical.
not suitable; unwise or impractical

Examples

The board considered it inexpedient to invest in the risky venture at this time.
It would be inexpedient to reveal our strategy to competitors.
She argued that such an action was politically inexpedient and might lead to unintended consequences.

Synset Examples

an inexpedient tactic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expedient - suitable or advisable; convenient and practical

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + expedient (from Latin expedientem, 'beneficial')

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00935636-a
Synset POS: a
