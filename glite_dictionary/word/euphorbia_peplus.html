Euphorbia peplus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbi.ə ˈpiːpləs / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˈpiːpləs / (BrE)
A species of spurge native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia, often found as a weed in many regions including the eastern United States.
a species of spurge plant

Examples

Euphorbia peplus can quickly colonize disturbed ground in gardens and fields.
Gardeners are often troubled by Euphorbia peplus invading their flower beds.
Despite its weedy habit, Euphorbia peplus has been researched for potential medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin 'Euphorbia' (the genus name), named for Euphorbus, a Greek physician, and Latin species epithet 'peplus'.
