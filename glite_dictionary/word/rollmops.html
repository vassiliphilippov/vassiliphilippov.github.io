rollmops

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlˌmɑps / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌmɒps / (BrE)
A dish consisting of pickled herring fillets rolled around a savory filling, typically a pickle or onion.
pickled herring fillets rolled around a filling

Examples

He ordered rollmops as a traditional appetizer at the German restaurant.
You can find jars of rollmops in many European supermarkets.
My grandmother taught me how to make homemade rollmops for festive occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish often pickled or smoked for food

Etymology

From German Rollmops (roll 'roll' + Mops 'fat fish').
