Judea

1. PROPN

/ dʒuˈdiːə / (AmE), / dʒuːˈdiːə / (BrE)
the southern region of ancient Palestine, which succeeded the kingdom of Judah and became a Roman province at the time of Christ
ancient region in southern Palestine

Examples

Many events in the New Testament take place in Judea, especially in Jerusalem.
After the Romans took control, Judea became an important province in their empire.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient artifacts from Judea dating back to biblical times.

Etymology

From Latin 'Judaea', from Greek 'Ioudaia', from Hebrew 'Yəhūḏāh' (Judah).
