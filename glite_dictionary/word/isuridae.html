Isuridae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsjʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / aɪˈsjʊə.rɪ.diː / (BrE)
A former or alternative scientific name for the family Lamnidae, which includes mackerel sharks such as the mako and great white shark.
former name for shark family Lamnidae

Examples

Modern taxonomists generally treat Isuridae as a synonym of Lamnidae.
The family Isuridae was once used to classify certain mackerel sharks separately.
Some older scientific literature refers to the great white shark as belonging to the Isuridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamnidae - the family of mackerel sharks including the great white and mako

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Isurus (a shark genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈsjʊrəˌdiː / (AmE), / aɪˈsjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of sharks sometimes considered synonymous with the family Lamnidae
a family of sharks in taxonomy

Examples

The Isuridae are often found in temperate and tropical seas around the world.
Some scientists classify the great white shark under the family Isuridae rather than Lamnidae.
Research on Isuridae has contributed to our understanding of shark evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamnidae - family of mackerel sharks, including the great white

Etymology

From New Latin Isuridae, after Isurus, a genus of sharks, combined with the taxonomic suffix -idae.
