unchanging

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
remaining the same or not undergoing any change over time; steady or consistent.
not changing; remaining the same

Examples

Despite the passage of years, her dedication to her work remained unchanging.
The mountain stood unchanging through all seasons and storms.
He appreciated the unchanging traditions of his hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same over time; unchanging
  • Antonym: variable - liable to change; not consistent

Etymology

From un- (not) + changing, present participle of 'change'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
remaining the same over time; showing little or no change
not changing; constant

Examples

The ancient traditions of the village have remained unchanging for generations.
His unchanging routine brought him comfort and stability.
Despite the chaos around her, her expression was calm and unchanging.

Synset Examples

a static population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same over time
  • Antonym: variable - likely to change; not constant

Etymology

From un- (not) + changing (present participle of 'change').
