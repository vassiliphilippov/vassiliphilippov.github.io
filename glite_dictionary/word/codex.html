codex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.dɛks / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.dɛks / (BrE)
an ancient manuscript in book form, especially one that is handwritten and unbound, as opposed to a scroll
ancient manuscript in book form

Examples

The museum displayed an illuminated codex dating back to the 5th century.
Scholars studied the codex to learn about ancient philosophical traditions.
Unlike a scroll, a codex allowed for easier navigation between pages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scroll - a roll of parchment or paper for writing on, especially one from ancient times

Etymology

From Latin codex (book, manuscript).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.dɛks / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.dɛks / (BrE)
an official list or collection of chemicals, medicines, or similar items, especially used as a standard reference
official list of chemicals or medicines

Examples

The laboratory followed the standards set out in the codex for pharmaceutical substances.
Manufacturers must comply with the codex governing food additives to ensure safety.
The new edition of the codex included several recently approved chemicals.

Etymology

From Latin codex (book, manuscript).
