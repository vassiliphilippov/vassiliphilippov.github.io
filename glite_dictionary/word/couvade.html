couvade

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈveɪd / (AmE), / kuːˈvɑːd / (BrE)
a custom observed in some cultures in which the husband of a pregnant woman simulates pregnancy or childbirth, often by taking to bed during or after the child's birth
custom where husband imitates childbirth

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the ritual of couvade in several indigenous societies.
During the couvade, the father may stay in bed while the mother recovers from childbirth.
The tradition of couvade reflects beliefs about paternal involvement and childbirth.

Etymology

From French 'couvade', from 'couver' (to brood, to sit on eggs), from Latin 'cubare' (to lie down).
