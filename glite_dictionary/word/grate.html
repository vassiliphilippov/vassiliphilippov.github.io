grate

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
a framework of parallel or crossed bars, typically covering an opening and allowing air or light to pass through while preventing access
framework of bars covering an opening

Examples

She placed the package behind the grate covering the fireplace.
The old building had a rusty grate guarding the basement window.
Rainwater flowed into the drain through a metal grate on the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a framework of metal bars used for cooking food over a fire

Etymology

From Old French *grate* (grille), from Latin *cratis* (wickerwork, lattice).

/ ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
