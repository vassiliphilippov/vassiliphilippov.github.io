to percolate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.kə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kə.leɪt / (BrE)
to prepare (a beverage, especially coffee) in a percolator by allowing water to pass through the grounds repeatedly
prepare coffee or similar in a percolator

Examples

He likes to percolate his coffee every morning for a richer flavor.
The aroma fills the kitchen as the coffee begins to percolate.
Some people still prefer to percolate their tea instead of using a kettle.

Synset Examples

percolate coffee

Etymology

From Latin 'percolare' (to strain through).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01461093-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.kəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kə.leɪt / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to pass slowly through a porous substance, especially to extract a soluble component
cause a liquid to filter through a substance

Examples

The chemist allowed the solution to percolate through the charcoal to purify it.
Hot water is percolated through ground coffee to make an aromatic drink.
To extract the active ingredient, they percolate alcohol through the dried roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to pass a liquid or gas through a device to remove unwanted substances

Etymology

From Latin percolare, from per- ('through') + colare ('to strain, filter').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01460857-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈpɝːkəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkəleɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) to move gradually through a porous material or substance.
move through a porous material

Examples

After the rainstorm, water began to percolate through the soil into the underground reservoirs.
It can take hours for the coffee to percolate through the filter, creating a rich and strong brew.
Chemicals from the landfill may percolate into the groundwater, causing environmental concerns.

Synset Examples

Water permeates sand easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to pass a liquid, gas, light, or sound through a device to remove unwanted substances
  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material or small holes

Etymology

From Latin percolare, from per- (through) + colare (to strain, filter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01460583-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.kə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kə.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a feeling, idea, or quality) to spread slowly or gradually through something; to become known or felt little by little.
spread gradually through

Examples

Rumors about the policy change began to percolate through the office on Monday.
A sense of excitement percolated among the students as graduation day approached.
The light from the hallway slowly percolated into the dark room.

Synset Examples

Light percolated into our house in the morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to pass or leak slowly through something (literal sense)
  • Synonym: diffuse - to spread over a wide area or among many people

Etymology

From Latin *percolare* (to strain through), from *per-* (through) + *colare* (to strain, filter).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ˈpɜrkəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkəleɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, idea, or feeling) to spread gradually through a substance or area; to penetrate or become known little by little.
to spread or penetrate gradually

Examples

Rainwater can percolate slowly through layers of soil and rock.
It took a while for the news to percolate through the company.
The aroma of freshly baked bread percolated throughout the house.

Synset Examples

the fertilizer leached into the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
  • Synonym: to penetrate - to go into or through something
  • Degree Higher: to saturate - to cause something to become thoroughly soaked

Etymology

From Latin percolare (to strain through), from per- (through) + colare (to strain, filter).
