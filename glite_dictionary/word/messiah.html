Messiah

1. PROPN

/ məˈsaɪ.ə / (AmE), / mɪˈsaɪ.ə / (BrE)
the awaited king and deliverer of the Jewish people, prophesied in the Hebrew Scriptures
the prophesied Jewish savior and king

Examples

Many Jews still await the coming of the Messiah to restore peace and justice.
The concept of the Messiah is central to Jewish religious tradition.
Throughout history, several individuals have claimed to be the Messiah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christ - the title for Jesus as the Messiah in Christianity

Etymology

From Hebrew 'māšīaḥ' meaning 'anointed', via Latin and Greek.

2. NOUN

/ məˈsaɪ.ə / (AmE), / mɪˈsaɪ.ə / (BrE)
an oratorio composed by George Frideric Handel in 1742, based on texts from the Bible and widely performed, especially at Christmas and Easter.
Handel's 1742 oratorio

Examples

Each December, our choir performs Handel's Messiah to celebrate the holiday season.
The Messiah is renowned for its stirring 'Hallelujah' chorus.
Tickets for last night's Messiah concert sold out within hours.

3. NOUN

/ məˈsaɪ.ə / (AmE), / mɪˈsaɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in Christianity) Jesus Christ, regarded by Christians as the promised savior and deliverer of humankind.
Jesus Christ as the promised deliverer in Christianity

Examples

Many Christians believe that Jesus is the Messiah prophesied in the Old Testament.
Handel's oratorio 'Messiah' celebrates the birth, death, and resurrection of the Messiah.
In Christian theology, the Messiah is seen as the redeemer of humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christ - Another name for Jesus, especially as the Messiah

Etymology

From Hebrew *māšīaḥ* (anointed one), via Latin and Greek.
