consonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.sə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nəns / (BrE)
A combination of sounds or notes that are harmonious when heard together; the quality of being agreeable or compatible in sound.
harmonious combination of sounds

Examples

The choir's consonance created a soothing and peaceful atmosphere in the church.
In music, consonance is achieved when notes blend smoothly without harshness.
Poets often use consonance to give their verses a pleasing rhythm and sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dissonance - a lack of harmony among musical notes

Etymology

From Latin 'consonantia', from 'consonare' (to sound together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnsənəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənəns / (BrE)
the repetition of consonant sounds, especially at the ends of words, as a stylistic device in poetry or prose
repetition of consonant sounds in words

Examples

The poet used consonance to create a musical effect in her verses.
Notice how the consonance in the phrase 'blank and think' draws attention to the ending sounds.
Writers often use consonance alongside rhyme to enrich the sound of their poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliteration - the repetition of the same sound at the beginning of words

Etymology

From Latin 'consonantia', from 'consonare' (to sound together).
