pyramid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪr.əˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.rə.mɪd / (BrE)
a massive stone monument with a square base and four triangular sides meeting at a point, built especially in ancient Egypt as a royal tomb
ancient Egyptian tomb monument

Examples

The Great pyramid of Giza is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
They marveled at the size and precision of the ancient pyramid.
Archaeologists studied the interior chambers of the pyramid to learn about Egyptian burial practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mausoleum - a large stately tomb or a building housing such a tomb

Etymology

From Greek 'pyramis', probably of Egyptian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04035601-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪr.əˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.rə.mɪd / (BrE)
A series of financial transactions in which a trader increases their holdings by using the rising market value of their assets as a margin for additional purchases.
stock transactions based on increasing margins

Examples

She decided to pyramid her profits by using them as margin for further stock purchases.
Aggressive investors sometimes employ a pyramid strategy to maximize their returns during a bull market.
The trader lost money when his pyramid of leveraged positions collapsed after the market turned.

Etymology

From Latin 'pyramis', via Greek 'puramis', probably in reference to the structure's shape; in finance, refers to the ascending accumulation of positions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13364299-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpɪr.əˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.rə.mɪd / (BrE)
to arrange or build up in the shape or structure of a pyramid, typically increasing in levels or layers.
arrange or build in the shape of a pyramid

Examples

At the event, volunteers pyramided the canned goods into a massive display for charity.
The costs tend to pyramid as more features are added to the original plan.
They began to pyramid the chairs for storage, stacking each layer smaller than the one below.

Etymology

From Latin *pyramis*, from Greek *pūramis* (pyramid).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɪr.ə.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪr.ə.mɪd / (BrE)
a solid geometric figure with a polygonal base and triangular faces that meet at a common point (the apex)
solid shape with a polygonal base and triangular faces

Examples

The ancient Egyptians built the Great pyramid of Giza using massive stone blocks.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a pyramid.
Each face of a pyramid, except the base, is a triangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prism - a solid with two parallel, congruent bases connected by parallelogram faces

Etymology

From Greek 'pyramis', of unknown origin, possibly related to the shape of wheat cakes.

5. VERB

/ ˈpɪrəmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪərəmɪd / (BrE)
To use paper profits from previous trades as margin to buy more of the same asset, especially during a continued rise in the market.
enlarge holdings in financial markets using profits as margin

Examples

Many traders choose to pyramid their positions during a strong bull market.
She wisely decided not to pyramid her investments, avoiding excessive risk.
By pyramiding, he quickly increased his stake, but also his exposure to losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to average up - increase average cost per share by buying more at higher prices
  • Antonym: average down - reduce average cost per share by buying more at lower prices

Etymology

From the noun 'pyramid', referring to the expanding base of holdings built atop earlier trades.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ ˈpɪr.əˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.rə.mɪd / (BrE)
to use or deal in something, especially stocks or investments, in a way that resembles a pyramid scheme, by using proceeds or borrowed money to increase one's position in successive transactions.
deal using a pyramid scheme or structure

Examples

The trader attempted to pyramid his profits by reinvesting them into larger positions.
Some investors pyramid aggressively, hoping to amplify returns through leveraging.
He lost everything when he tried to pyramid his small initial investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speculate - to engage in risky financial transactions

Etymology

From the noun 'pyramid', by analogy to the layered structure of pyramid schemes.
