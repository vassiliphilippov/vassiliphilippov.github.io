Patrick Henry

1. PROPN

/ ˈpætrɪk ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪk ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
(1736–1799) An American statesman, orator, and leader in the American Revolution, famous for his declarations against British rule.
American orator and revolutionary leader

Examples

Patrick Henry is best remembered for his bold declaration, “Give me liberty, or give me death!”
Many students study Patrick Henry’s speeches to understand the rhetoric of the American Revolution.
As governor of Virginia, Patrick Henry played a key role in shaping the new nation.
