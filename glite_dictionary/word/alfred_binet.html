Alfred Binet

1. PROPN

/ ælˌfred bɪˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˈælfred ˈbɪneɪ / (BrE)
(1857–1911) French psychologist known for pioneering work on intelligence testing and the development of the first practical IQ test.
French psychologist; IQ test pioneer

Examples

Many consider Alfred Binet the father of modern intelligence testing.
The research of Alfred Binet greatly influenced educational psychology.
Alfred Binet's methods remain foundational in contemporary psychological assessment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence test - test designed to measure intelligence; connected to Binet's work
