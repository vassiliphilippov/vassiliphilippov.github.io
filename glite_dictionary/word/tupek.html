tupek

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.pɛk / (AmE), / ˈtuː.pɛk / (BrE)
a tent made of animal skins or fabric, traditionally used by some Eskimo peoples as a summer dwelling
an Eskimo summer tent dwelling

Examples

The family lived in a tupek during the short Arctic summer.
When the ice melted, the villagers replaced their igloos with a tupek.
A traditional tupek is made by stretching skins over a wooden frame.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: igloo - a dome-shaped Eskimo house, usually built from blocks of snow

Etymology

From Inuit languages; relates to traditional indigenous shelters of the Arctic.
