maudlin

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔd.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔːd.lɪn / (BrE)
showing or expressing too much emotion, especially in a way that is insincere or overly sentimental
overly sentimental or tearfully emotional

Examples

After drinking too much wine, he became maudlin and started reminiscing about his childhood.
The movie's maudlin ending made some viewers roll their eyes rather than cry.
She grew maudlin whenever she talked about her time abroad, repeating the same old stories.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or affected by uncontrolled emotions rather than reason
  • Antonym: stoic - not showing or feeling emotion

Etymology

From Mary Magdalene, depicted as weeping in medieval art and plays.
