trilby

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪl.bi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪl.bi / (BrE)
a soft felt hat with a narrow brim and a deeply indented crown
a type of soft felt hat

Examples

He tipped his trilby as he entered the smoky jazz club.
The detective wore a grey trilby and a long overcoat.
Many vintage fashion enthusiasts collect classic trilbies from the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fedora - a soft felt hat with a wider brim than a trilby

Etymology

Named after the heroine Trilby O'Ferrall in George du Maurier's 1894 novel 'Trilby', in which such hats were worn.
