nonviolent

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvaɪələnt / (AmE), / nɒnˈvaɪələnt / (BrE)
Not involving physical force or bloodshed; achieved without violence.
without violence or bloodshed

Examples

The country experienced a nonviolent revolution that changed its government peacefully.
His commitment to nonviolent protest inspired many followers around the world.
A nonviolent transfer of power occurred after the negotiations.

Synset Examples

an unbloody transfer of power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: violent - involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'violent' (involving physical force).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvaɪ.ə.lənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈvaɪ.ə.lənt / (BrE)
Avoiding the use of physical force or violence, especially as a matter of principle.
not using physical force

Examples

The protesters remained nonviolent despite provocation from the police.
He is a strong advocate of nonviolent resistance to injustice.
Her approach to conflict resolution is always nonviolent and constructive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: violent - using or involving physical force
  • Synonym: peaceful - not involving or provoking violence; tranquil

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'violent', first recorded in early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02522980-a
Synset POS: a
