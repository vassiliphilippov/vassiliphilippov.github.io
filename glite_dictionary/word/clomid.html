Clomid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊmɪd / (AmE), / ˈkləʊmɪd / (BrE)
A trademarked fertility drug used to stimulate ovulation, often prescribed to treat infertility in women.
fertility drug that stimulates ovulation
medical

Examples

After months of trying to conceive, her doctor prescribed Clomid to help induce ovulation.
Some couples choose Clomid as a first-line treatment for unexplained infertility.
The side effects of Clomid can include hot flashes and mood swings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clomiphene - the generic name for Clomid, a fertility drug
