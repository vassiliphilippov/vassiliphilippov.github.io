watchful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑtʃ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.fəl / (BrE)
paying close attention to possible danger or problems; being alert and observant
alert and observant

Examples

The security guard remained watchful throughout his entire shift.
Parents are often watchful when their children play near the street.
She cast a watchful eye over the proceedings, ready to step in if needed.

Synset Examples

caught by a couple of alert cops
alert enough to spot the opportunity when it came
constantly alert and vigilant, like a sentinel on duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention

Etymology

From Middle English 'waccheful', from 'watch' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑtʃ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.fəl / (BrE)
(literary) Experiencing or accompanied by sleeplessness; characterized by wakefulness or inability to sleep.
unable to sleep; sleepless (literary)

Examples

He spent another watchful night, unable to drift into sleep despite his exhaustion.
The watchful hours before dawn seemed endless as she lay awake.
Their journey was marked by many watchful nights, when rest would not come.

Synset Examples

insomniac old people
insomniac nights
lay sleepless all night
twenty watchful, weary, tedious nights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepless - unable to sleep; without sleep
  • Synonym: wakeful - not sleeping; unable to sleep

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɑtʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃfəl / (BrE)
paying close attention to possible danger or problems; alert and observant
alert and observant

Examples

The mother kept a watchful eye on her children as they played in the park.
Security guards remained watchful throughout the event to prevent any trouble.
Our teacher is always watchful during exams to catch any signs of cheating.

Synset Examples

a policy of open-eyed awareness
the vigilant eye of the town watch
there was a watchful dignity in the room
a watchful parent with a toddler in tow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to potential danger
  • Antonym: negligent - failing to take proper care or attention

Etymology

Formed from 'watch' + '-ful', meaning full of watching or observation.
