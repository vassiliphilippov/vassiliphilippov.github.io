syzygy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪz.ɪ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈsɪz.ɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
an alignment of three celestial bodies (such as the Sun, Earth, and Moon) in a straight line within a gravitational system, especially during an eclipse or new/full moon
alignment of three celestial bodies

Examples

A lunar eclipse occurs during a syzygy when the Earth is positioned between the Sun and the Moon.
Astronomers eagerly awaited the rare syzygy that would produce a total solar eclipse.
The gravitational effects of syzygy can cause exceptionally high tides on Earth.

Etymology

From Greek *syzygia* (union, conjunction), from *syzygos* (yoked together).
