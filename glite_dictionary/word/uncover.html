to uncover

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to make information known to the public that was previously secret or hidden
make secret information public

Examples

The investigative journalist helped uncover evidence of corruption in the company.
It took years to uncover the truth about what really happened.
The report aims to uncover hidden connections between the two organizations.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something known that was secret or hidden
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make information public that was previously secret
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From un- (not) + cover (from Old French 'covrir', from Latin 'cooperire', to cover).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to reveal something by removing a cover or making it visible
reveal by removing a cover

Examples

She gently uncovered the old painting hidden beneath a sheet.
The investigation aims to uncover the truth behind the incident.
As the snow melted, it uncovered the fresh green grass below.

Synset Examples

The curtain rose to disclose a stunning set

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cover - to place something over or upon, to hide or protect
  • Synonym: to reveal - to make visible or known; to disclose

Etymology

From un- (not) + cover; modeled after the French 'découvrir'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02142175-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to remove clothing in order to reveal part or all of one's body
remove clothing to show body

Examples

The doctor asked her to uncover her arm for the injection.
Please uncover your back so I can apply the medicine.
During the examination, he was told to uncover his chest.

Synset Examples

uncover your belly
The man exposed himself in the subway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to reveal or make something visible, especially something usually hidden

Etymology

From un- (not) + cover, ultimately from Old French covrir, from Latin cooperire (to cover completely).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01342187-v
Synset POS: v
