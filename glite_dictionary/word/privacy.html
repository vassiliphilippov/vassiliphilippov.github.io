privacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvəsi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvəsi / (BrE)
The state of being free from public attention or unsanctioned intrusion; the condition of being concealed or hidden from others.
state of being concealed or free from intrusion

Examples

She valued her privacy and rarely invited guests to her home.
New curtains gave the family more privacy in their living room.
Many people use hedges around their yards for added privacy from neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or the state of being kept secret
  • Antonym: publicity - the condition of being public or open to everyone

Etymology

From Latin 'privatus' meaning 'withdrawn from public life, private'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvəsi / (AmE), / ˈprɪvəsi / (BrE)
the state of being free from the observation or intrusion of others
state of being free from observation or intrusion

Examples

Most people value their privacy when they are at home.
She closed the door to have some privacy while working on the project.
Laws are in place to protect your privacy online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
  • Antonym: publicity - the state of being open to the view of others or public attention

Etymology

From Old French *privauté*, from Latin *privatus* (apart from the state, individual).
