flatware

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈflætˌweə / (BrE)
implements such as knives, forks, and spoons used for eating; eating utensils collectively.
eating utensils such as knives, forks, and spoons

Examples

We need to buy new flatware before the dinner party.
The restaurant owner chose stainless steel flatware for durability.
All the flatware should be arranged neatly beside each plate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutlery - knives, forks, and spoons collectively
  • Synonym: silverware - eating utensils made of or plated with silver

Etymology

From 'flat' + 'ware', referring to the typically flat shape of such utensils.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈflætˌweə / (BrE)
table utensils such as knives, forks, and spoons, typically made as a single piece and relatively flat in shape
table utensils like knives, forks, and spoons

Examples

The restaurant provided polished flatware for every guest at the table.
Please remember to set out the flatware before dinner is served.
She bought a new set of stainless steel flatware for her kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverware - utensils made of silver or similar metal used for eating
  • Synonym: cutlery - knives, forks, and spoons used for eating or serving food

Etymology

From 'flat' + 'ware', patterned after 'tableware'.
