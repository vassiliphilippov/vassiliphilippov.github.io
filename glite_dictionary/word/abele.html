abele

1. NOUN

/ əˈbiːl / (AmE), / əˈbiːl / (BrE)
A type of poplar tree, widely cultivated in the United States, with white bark and leaves that are white on the underside.
a poplar tree with white bark and whitish leaf undersides

Examples

The abele is known for its striking white bark, making it popular in landscaping.
In the fall, the leaves of the abele turn yellow before shedding for the winter.
Several abeles were planted along the riverbank to provide shade and prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white poplar - a species of poplar tree with white bark and leaves
  • Degree Higher: poplar - a deciduous tree of the genus Populus

Etymology

From Dutch 'abeel', from Latin 'populus alba' meaning 'white poplar'.
