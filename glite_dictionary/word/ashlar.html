ashlar

1. NOUN

/ ˈæʃ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈæʃ.lə / (BrE)
a rectangular block of finely cut stone used in building, typically with straight edges and square corners
rectangular building stone block

Examples

The castle walls were constructed from massive ashlar blocks that fit seamlessly together.
Skilled masons laid each ashlar carefully to create a smooth, even façade.
Restoration work revealed the original ashlar beneath layers of later renovations.

Etymology

From Old French *aiselier* (stone worked square), from Latin *axilla* (little board).
