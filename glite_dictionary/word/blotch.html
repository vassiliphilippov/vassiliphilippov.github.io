blotch

1. NOUN

/ blɑtʃ / (AmE), / blɒtʃ / (BrE)
a large, irregularly shaped spot or area, especially on a surface or the skin, that is typically discolored from its surroundings
irregularly shaped spot

Examples

A large red blotch appeared on his cheek after he ate the strawberries.
There were blue paint blotches all over the kitchen floor.
The old map was covered in brown blotches from water damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round area that has a different color or texture from the surrounding surface
  • Synonym: stain - a discolored or dirty mark on something that is difficult to remove
  • Degree Lower: speckle - a small spot or mark

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps related to 'blot' meaning a spot or stain.

2. VERB

/ blɑtʃ / (AmE), / blɒtʃ / (BrE)
to mark with spots or irregular patches of different colors or shades, as if stained
mark with spots or patches of color

Examples

The paint began to blotch the wall after the rain leaked in.
The artist blotched the canvas with dark blue and red to create a dramatic effect.
If you use this marker on wet paper, it may blotch the surface unpredictably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - mark with a small round or roundish stain, dot, or speck
