alliterator

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪtəˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / əˈlɪtəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who uses alliteration in speech or writing
person who uses alliteration

Examples

The poet was a skilled alliterator, weaving words with matching sounds throughout his verses.
Children's book authors often act as alliterators to create fun, memorable phrases.
As an accomplished alliterator, she delighted audiences with her playful tongue-twisters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliteration - the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent words

Etymology

From alliterate (to use alliteration) + -or (agent noun suffix).
