residency

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌdɛnsi / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənsi / (BrE)
the act of living or staying in a particular place
the act of living in a place

Examples

After five years of residency, she applied for permanent citizenship.
Proof of residency in the state is required to register to vote.
His residency at the address was confirmed by utility bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence - the place where someone lives

Etymology

From Latin 'residentia' (remaining, dwelling, residence).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛz.ə.dən.si / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ɪ.dən.si / (BrE)
the period during which a physician receives specialized training in a hospital after completing medical school, typically following an internship
physician's specialized hospital training period
medical

Examples

After medical school, she began her residency at a prestigious teaching hospital.
Completing a residency is a requirement before becoming a fully qualified doctor.
The residency program in surgery lasts for five years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: internship - period of supervised training for physicians before residency

Etymology

From Latin 'residentia' (act of remaining or residing).
