ommastrephes

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˌmæstrəˈfiːz / (AmE), / ɒˌmæstrəˈfiːz / (BrE)
A genus of extremely active cylindrical squids with short, strong arms and large rhombic terminal fins.
genus of active cylindrical squids

Examples

Researchers observed a large school of ommastrephes swimming rapidly near the ocean surface.
The ommastrephes is known for its agility and distinctive rhombic fins.
Fishermen in the Pacific sometimes catch ommastrephes as part of their squid harvest.

Etymology

From Greek 'omma' (eye) and 'strephes' (to turn).
