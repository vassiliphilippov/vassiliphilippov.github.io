hackles

1. NOUN

/ ˈhækəlz / (AmE), / ˈhækəlz / (BrE)
a feeling of anger or animosity, especially as expressed by becoming defensive or aggressive
feeling of anger or animosity

Examples

Whenever he felt criticized, his hackles would rise instantly.
The comment put her hackles up, and she responded sharply.
His hackles were clearly raised during the tense meeting.

Synset Examples

having one's hackles or dander up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Middle English hakel, originally referred to the erectile hairs along an animal's back; figurative sense relates to the hairs rising when angry.
