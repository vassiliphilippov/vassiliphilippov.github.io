elongation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɔŋˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / iːˌlɒŋˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality or state of being made longer or extended in length
the state of being lengthened

Examples

The elongation of the bridge was necessary to span the wider river.
Bamboo's rapid elongation allows it to grow several feet in a single day.
Astronomers study the elongation of planetary orbits to understand celestial movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extension - the action of becoming longer or extending
  • Antonym: shortening - the process or result of making something shorter

Etymology

From Latin elongatio (lengthening), from elongare (to lengthen).
