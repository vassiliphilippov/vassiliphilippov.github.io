hobbyhorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbiˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈhɒbiˌhɔːs / (BrE)
a child's toy consisting of a model of a horse mounted on rockers or a stick, which the child rides by straddling and pretending to ride a real horse
child's toy horse for riding

Examples

As a child, she loved riding her hobbyhorse around the living room for hours.
The old-fashioned hobbyhorse was passed down through three generations in their family.
He received a new hobbyhorse for his birthday and couldn't wait to show his friends.

Etymology

From 'hobby', a small horse (obsolete), and 'horse'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbiˌhɔːrs / (AmE), / ˈhɒbihɔːs / (BrE)
a subject, idea, or topic that a person talks about repeatedly or is particularly preoccupied with
a favored or recurring topic

Examples

Climate change is his hobbyhorse, and he brings it up in every meeting.
Try not to mention taxes around her, or she'll start riding her hobbyhorse again.
He's always talking about recycling—it's become his hobbyhorse lately.

Synset Examples

don't get him started on his hobbyhorse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsession - something that preoccupies a person excessively
  • Synonym: pet topic - a favorite topic or subject someone repeatedly discusses

Etymology

Originally referred to a child's toy horse; later, figurative use for an obsession or recurring topic.
