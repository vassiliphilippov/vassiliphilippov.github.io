Turkmen

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrkˌmɛn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkmɛn / (BrE)
relating to Turkmenistan, its people, or their culture
of Turkmenistan or its people/culture

Examples

The Turkmen language is one of the official languages of Turkmenistan.
Some Turkmen customs are unique to Central Asia.
He enjoyed learning about Turkmen history and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmenistani - relating to Turkmenistan or its people/culture

Etymology

From Turkish Türkmen, referring to the people of Turkmenistan.

2. NOUN

/ tɜrˈkmɛn / (AmE), / tɜːkˈmen / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic-speaking ethnic group native to Turkmenistan and neighboring regions.
member of Turkic ethnic group

Examples

The Turkmen have preserved many of their traditional customs and clothing.
Many Turkmen migrated to Iran during the 20th century.
He studied the culture and history of the Turkmen in Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmenistan - a country in Central Asia, homeland of the Turkmen people

Etymology

From Persian ترکمن‎ (Torkaman), of Turkic origin.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkˌmɛn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkmɛn / (BrE)
A former Soviet republic in Central Asia, located east of the Caspian Sea, now known as Turkmenistan.
former Soviet republic; now Turkmenistan

Examples

The Turkmen SSR was established in 1925 as part of the Soviet Union.
After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the Turkmen republic gained independence as Turkmenistan.
During the 1980s, economic policies in the Turkmen region focused on cotton production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmenistan - a Central Asian country, formerly the Turkmen SSR

Etymology

From Persian ترکمن (Turkmen), the name of the Central Asian ethnic group and region.
