Behmenism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪmɪnˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbeɪmənɪzəm / (BrE)
the mystical theological doctrines of Jakob Boehme, particularly his views on the nature of God and creation, which influenced later religious movements such as the Quakers
mystical doctrines of Jakob Boehme

Examples

The influence of Behmenism can be traced in many early Quaker writings.
Some theologians criticized Behmenism for its mystical and non-traditional interpretation of Christian doctrines.
She studied Behmenism to better understand the roots of modern Christian mysticism.

Etymology

From the name of German mystic Jakob Boehme (1575–1624) plus English -ism.
