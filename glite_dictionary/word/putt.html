putt

1. VERB

/ pʌt / (AmE), / pʌt / (BrE)
to hit a golf ball gently along the ground, especially on the green, towards the hole
hit a golf ball gently on the green

Examples

She putted confidently, sinking the ball with a single stroke.
He putted poorly on the 18th green and lost the match.
After lining up the shot carefully, she putted straight into the hole.

Synset Examples

he lost because he putted so poorly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drive - to hit a golf ball forcefully, usually with a driver

Etymology

Probably imitative or from Scots put (to push, shove).

2. NOUN

/ pʌt / (AmE), / pʌt / (BrE)
a stroke in golf made by gently hitting the ball along the green towards the hole, usually using a putter
a gentle golf stroke on the green

Examples

She made a perfect putt to win the tournament.
He missed the short putt by just an inch.
The golfer studied the green carefully before attempting his next putt.

Synset Examples

his putting let him down today
he didn't sink a single putt over three feet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drive - a long, powerful shot in golf, usually from the tee
  • Antonym: chip - a short shot in golf lofted onto the green from close by
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ pʌt / (AmE), / pʌt / (BrE)
to strike a golf ball gently with a putter, usually to make it roll towards or into the hole
strike a golf ball gently (golf)

Examples

She putted the ball slowly towards the hole and waited anxiously for the result.
After missing the green, he carefully putted from the edge and nearly made it in.
Golfers often practice for hours to perfect their ability to putt accurately.

Synset Examples

he putted the ball several feet past the hole

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drive - to hit a golf ball a long distance with a full swing
  • Degree Higher: chip - to hit a short, lofted golf shot close to the green
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ pʌt / (AmE), / pʌt / (BrE)
to hit a golf ball gently so that it rolls along the ground, especially towards or into the hole
gently hit in golf towards the hole

Examples

She calmly putted the ball into the hole to win the match.
It took him three tries to putt from just a few feet away.
After a long approach shot, he lined up his putt carefully.

Synset Examples

After a poor drive, she made a great putt from the rough.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drive - in golf, to hit the ball a long distance, usually from the tee

Etymology

From Scottish, perhaps imitative of the action.
