presbyopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɛzbiˈɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɛzbɪˈɒpɪk / (BrE)
Able to see distant objects clearly but having difficulty focusing on near objects, typically as a result of aging.
able to see distant objects clearly (age-related)
medical

Examples

As people age, they often become presbyopic and require reading glasses for close work.
Her presbyopic condition made it easy to read distant street signs, but hard to see the text on her phone.
During the eye examination, the doctor confirmed that he was presbyopic and prescribed bifocals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsighted - able to see distant objects clearly (hyperopic or presbyopic)
  • Antonym: nearsighted - able to see near objects clearly, not distant ones (myopic)

Etymology

From Greek presbys (old man) + -opia (vision condition).
