suggestive

1. ADJ

/ səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / səɡˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
clearly showing or indicating something, often used with 'of' to specify what is implied or revealed.
clearly indicating or revealing something

Examples

The faint aroma in the air was suggestive of freshly baked bread.
His manner was suggestive of someone hiding a secret.
The dark clouds were suggestive of an approaching storm.

Synset Examples

actions indicative of fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicative - serving as a sign or indication of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin suggestivus, from Latin suggestus (pp. of suggerere 'to bring up, suggest').

2. ADJ

/ səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
making you think of something improper or indecent without stating it directly.
implying something improper or indecent

Examples

The comedian's jokes were too suggestive for a family audience.
She gave him a suggestive wink across the crowded room.
The film was criticized for its suggestive scenes.

Synset Examples

a suggestive nod
suggestive poses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for doubt or implication, often about sexual content

Etymology

From Latin suggestivus (prompting, suggestive), from suggerere (to suggest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00687611-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
Making one think of something; tending to suggest or imply something without stating it directly.
tending to suggest or imply

Examples

The old ruins were suggestive of a vibrant ancient civilization.
Her tone was deliberately suggestive without saying anything outright.
The symbol is highly suggestive of religious meaning.

Synset Examples

artifacts suggestive of an ancient society
an implicative statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicative - serving as a sign or indication of something
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for doubt or implication

Etymology

From suggest + -ive, from Latin suggestivus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00726986-s
Synset POS: s
