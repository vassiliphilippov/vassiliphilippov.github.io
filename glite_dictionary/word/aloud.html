aloud

1. ADV

/ əˈlaʊd / (AmE), / əˈlaʊd / (BrE)
audibly; in a way that can be heard by others, not silently or in the mind
in an audible voice, not silently

Examples

Please read the instructions aloud so everyone can hear.
She gasped aloud when she saw the surprise.
The teacher asked the students to say their answers aloud.

Synset Examples

please read the passage aloud
he laughed out loud

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silently - without making any sound; in silence
  • Synonym: out loud - in a voice that can be heard; audibly

Etymology

From Middle English 'aloud', from 'a-' (on) + 'loud'.

2. ADV

/ əˈlaʊd / (AmE), / əˈlaʊd / (BrE)
in a voice that can be heard; at a relatively high volume, not silently or in one's head
in a voice that can be heard

Examples

The teacher asked the students to read the poem aloud to the class.
She laughed aloud at his joke, surprising everyone in the room.
When you think aloud, it helps others understand your reasoning.

Synset Examples

the band played loudly
she spoke loudly and angrily
he spoke loud enough for those at the back of the room to hear him
cried aloud for help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudly - in a loud manner; with high volume
  • Antonym: quietly - in a quiet manner; at low volume

Etymology

From Middle English 'aloud', from Old English 'on hlūde' (aloud, loudly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00070301-r
Synset POS: r
