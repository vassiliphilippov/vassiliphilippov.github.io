Phaius

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.ɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids native to Asia and Australia, known for their large, showy flowers.
genus of Asiatic and Australian orchids

Examples

The Phaius genus includes the popular Nun's Orchid, admired for its striking flowers.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Phaius genus in tropical Australia.
Growing Phaius orchids requires well-draining soil and a humid environment.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phaius, the genus name given by botanists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of orchids known for their large, folded leaves and tall clusters of showy flowers.
a genus of showy-flowered orchids

Examples

The Phaius orchid is prized for its striking flowers and attractive foliage.
In her greenhouse, she cultivated several rare Phaius species.
Gardeners admire Phaius for its resilience and ornamental value.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek phaios meaning 'dusky' or 'gray.'
