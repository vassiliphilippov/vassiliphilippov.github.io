self-victimization

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the tendency to perceive oneself as a victim, often resulting in feelings of powerlessness and hindering personal growth or problem-solving
perceiving oneself as a victim

Examples

Her pattern of self-victimization made it difficult for her to take responsibility for her actions.
Therapists often work with clients to help them overcome self-victimization and develop resilience.
Constant self-victimization can damage relationships and prevent personal progress.

Synset Examples

He realized his self-victimization was holding him back.
Self-victimization can be a barrier to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim mentality - an attitude of consistently perceiving oneself as a victim
