Saint-John's-bread

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈdʒɑnz ˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈdʒɒnz ˌbrɛd / (BrE)
powder made from the ground seeds and pods of the carob tree, used as a substitute for chocolate
carob powder used as chocolate substitute

Examples

Health food stores often sell Saint-John's-bread as a natural alternative to cocoa powder.
Some people with chocolate allergies use Saint-John's-bread in their baking recipes.
The bakery offers a cake made with Saint-John's-bread instead of traditional chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - a powder or sweet substance from the pods of the carob tree

Etymology

So named because carob pods were traditionally associated with John the Baptist; 'bread' refers to the edible pods.
