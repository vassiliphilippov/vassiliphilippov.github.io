to melanize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːləˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to convert into or infiltrate with melanin, especially in biological tissues
convert or infiltrate with melanin
medical

Examples

Under certain conditions, some fungi melanize to protect themselves from ultraviolet radiation.
The tissues of the affected area began to melanize during the disease process.
Scientists observed that the insect larvae would melanize in response to infection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depigment - to remove pigment from, especially melanin

Etymology

From melanin + -ize, meaning to make into or treat with melanin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00281547-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːləˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become black or dark, especially by the formation or deposition of melanin
make or become black by melanin
medical

Examples

Certain fungi can melanize their cell walls in response to environmental stress.
The skin may melanize after prolonged exposure to sunlight.
As the wound healed, the surrounding tissue began to melanize.

Synset Examples

The smoke blackened the ceiling
The ceiling blackened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blacken - to make or become black

Etymology

From Greek melas (black) via melanin + -ize.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
