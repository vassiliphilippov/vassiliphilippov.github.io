crampon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræm.pɑn / (AmE), / ˈkræm.pɒn / (BrE)
an iron or steel framework with spikes, attached to boots to prevent slipping on ice or snow, especially during climbing
spiked device for boots to grip ice or snow

Examples

The mountaineer adjusted his crampon before tackling the icy slope.
Modern crampons are lighter and more durable than earlier models.
Without a crampon, walking on the glacier would have been nearly impossible.

Etymology

From French *crampon* (hook, clamp), from Old French *crampoun*; related to the Germanic root seen in 'cramp'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræm.pɑn / (AmE), / ˈkræm.pɒn / (BrE)
a hinged metal device with curved bars used for raising or lifting heavy objects
device for lifting heavy objects

Examples

The workers secured the beam using a crampon to lift it into position.
He attached the crampon carefully before attempting to hoist the crate.
A sturdy pair of crampons is essential for moving such heavy machinery.

Etymology

From French crampon (iron hook or clamp).
