uninterested

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɪntrəstɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɪntrɪstɪd / (BrE)
not showing any interest or concern; having no desire to know or be involved.
not interested; indifferent

Examples

He seemed completely uninterested in the discussion about politics.
The children were uninterested in playing the same game again.
Despite the exciting news, she remained uninterested throughout the meeting.

Synset Examples

she appeared totally uninterested

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interested - showing curiosity or concern about something
  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

un- (not) + interested.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɪn.t̬ɚˌɛs.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɪn.tər.ɪst.ɪd / (BrE)
Not having or showing any interest or concern in something.
not interested; not showing concern

Examples

She seemed completely uninterested in what we were saying.
An uninterested audience can make any presenter's job difficult.
He gave an uninterested shrug and walked away.

Synset Examples

an uninterested spectator

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interested - showing curiosity or concern about something

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'interested'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01345607-a
Synset POS: a
