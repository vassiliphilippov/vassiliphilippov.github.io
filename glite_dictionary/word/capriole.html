capriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæpriəʊl / (BrE)
In dressage, a leap performed by a horse in which it jumps upward and flicks its hind legs outward at the height of the jump.
a horse's upward leap with a hind-leg kick (dressage)

Examples

The disciplined Lusitano performed a capriole perfectly during the classical dressage demonstration.
To master the capriole, the rider and horse must train together for years.
Spectators were amazed when the Andalusian executed a stunning capriole in the arena.

Etymology

From French *capriole*, from Italian *capriola* (somersault), from *capriolo* (roe deer), referring to jumping.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.ri.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈkap.rɪ.əʊl / (BrE)
a playful leap or jump, especially one performed by a horse or a dancer
playful leap or jump

Examples

The young horse performed a capriole in the paddock, much to the delight of its owner.
With a sudden capriole, the dancer leaped across the stage, enchanting the audience.
Her playful caprioles brought laughter to the group of children watching nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - an act of jumping from one place to another
  • Synonym: jump - an act of springing off the ground

Etymology

From French 'capriole', from Italian 'capriola' (a somersault, leap), from 'capriolo' (roebuck, leaper).

3. VERB

/ ˈkæpriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæprɪəʊl / (BrE)
(of a horse) to leap into the air, kicking out with the hind legs, especially as a movement in classical dressage.
leap in the air (of a horse) in dressage

Examples

The stallion began to capriole with grace and precision in the arena.
Many riders admire how the Lipizzaner horses capriole as part of their classical dressage routine.
It takes years of training before a horse can successfully capriole on command.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump or spring a long way

Etymology

From French *capriole*, from Italian *capriola* (a leap), from *capra* (goat).

4. VERB

/ ˈkæpriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæprɪəʊl / (BrE)
To perform a capriole, a leap in which the dancer jumps into the air and beats the legs together, used in ballet and other forms of dance.
to leap with legs beaten together (ballet)

Examples

The lead dancer will capriole during the finale to showcase her agility.
As he caprioled across the stage, the audience was captivated by the display of skill.
She has practiced caprioling every day to perfect her technique for the upcoming performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump - to leap into the air, usually from a standing position

Etymology

From French 'capriole', from Italian 'capriola' (somersault), from 'capra' (goat).
