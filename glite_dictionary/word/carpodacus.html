Carpodacus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrpoʊˈdeɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpəˈdeɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of finches comprising species such as the house finch and purple finch, native to Eurasia and North America.
genus of finches (e.g. house finch, purple finch)

Examples

The Carpodacus genus includes both the house finch and the purple finch.
Many birdwatchers enjoy observing different species within the Carpodacus group.
The coloration of males in the Carpodacus genus is often bright and striking.

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + dakno (to bite), referencing the birds' diet and bill shape.

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈpɑːdəkəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɒdəkəs / (BrE)
A genus of finches including the house finch and purple finch, found mainly in North America and Eurasia.
genus of finches including house and purple finches

Examples

The Carpodacus genus includes several colorful species of finches.
Ornithologists observed a rare member of the Carpodacus in the mountain region.
House finches, classified under Carpodacus, are common in many suburban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small seed-eating songbird

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + daknos (to bite), referring to the feeding habits of these finches.
