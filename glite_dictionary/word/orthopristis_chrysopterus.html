Orthopristis chrysopterus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrθoʊˈprɪstɪs ˌkraɪsˈɑːptərəs / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊˈprɪstɪs ˌkrɪsˈɒptərəs / (BrE)
A species of marine fish in the grunt family, commonly known as the pigfish, found in the western Atlantic Ocean from Long Island to the Gulf of Mexico.
species of marine fish (pigfish)

Examples

Fishermen off the coast of Florida often catch Orthopristis chrysopterus in their nets.
Marine biologists study the feeding habits of Orthopristis chrysopterus to better understand its ecosystem.
The distinctive grunting noise made by Orthopristis chrysopterus gives the pigfish its common name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigfish - Common name for Orthopristis chrysopterus, a marine fish of the western Atlantic

Etymology

From Greek 'ortho-' meaning straight and 'pristis' meaning saw (referring to the fish's shape); 'chrysopterus' from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'pteron' (fin/wing), referring to its golden fins.
