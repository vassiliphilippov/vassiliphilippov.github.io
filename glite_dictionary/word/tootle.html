tootle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈtuːtəl / (BrE)
a light, casual sound produced by playing a musical instrument, especially a wind instrument, in an informal or cheerful manner
light casual musical sound

Examples

The tootle of a clarinet drifted softly from the next room as the sun set.
We heard the merry tootle of trumpets as the parade came down the street.
A faint tootle echoed from the bandstand while the musicians tuned their instruments.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed hearing the tootles of their horns as the musicians warmed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toot - a short, sharp sound, especially from a horn or whistle

Etymology

Imitative; related to the verb 'to tootle', which imitates the sound produced, and 'toot'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈtuːtəl / (BrE)
to play a musical instrument, especially a wind instrument, in a light, casual, or aimless manner
play music casually (wind instrument)

Examples

In the quiet afternoon, he would tootle on his clarinet by the open window.
She likes to tootle a few cheerful notes on her flute before dinner.
The band tootled happily as the parade passed through town.

Synset Examples

the saxophone player was tootling a sad melody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toot - to make a short, sharp sound with a horn or similar instrument

Etymology

Imitative; related to 'toot' (make a sound with a horn or similar instrument).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
