cheapjack

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃiːpˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːpˌdʒæk / (BrE)
Of inferior quality, made with poor workmanship and cheap materials; shoddy or cheaply made.
of poor quality; shoddily made

Examples

The market was full of cheapjack souvenirs that broke after a few uses.
Many critics dismissed the film as cheapjack entertainment lacking in substance.
He warned them against buying cheapjack furniture that wouldn't last a year.

Synset Examples

cheapjack moviemaking … that feeds on the low taste of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoddy - badly made or done; of poor quality
  • Antonym: superior - of high quality or excellence

Etymology

Originally denoting a street hawker selling cheap goods; from 'cheap' + 'Jack' (a generic name for a man).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃipˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːpdʒak / (BrE)
a person who sells shoddy or inferior goods at low prices, often in a showy or aggressive manner
peddler of inferior goods

Examples

The cheapjack at the fair tried to sell me a watch that broke after one day.
She dismissed him as a cheapjack with nothing of value to offer.
Many buyers learned to be wary of cheapjacks and their questionable merchandise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawker - a person who travels selling goods in the street
  • Synonym: pedlar - a person who sells goods from place to place
  • Degree Higher: merchant - someone who buys and sells goods, especially in large quantities

Etymology

Originated in early 19th century England, from 'cheap' (inexpensive) and the name 'Jack' used generically for a man.
