Stanley Baldwin

1. PROPN

/ ˈstænli ˈbɔl.dwɪn / (AmE), / ˈstænli ˈbɔːl.wɪn / (BrE)
(1867–1947) English statesman and member of the Conservative Party who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom three times.
British Prime Minister and statesman

Examples

Stanley Baldwin led the country through several turbulent periods between the World Wars.
The policies of Stanley Baldwin significantly influenced British politics in the early twentieth century.
Many historians debate the effectiveness of Stanley Baldwin's leadership before World War II.
