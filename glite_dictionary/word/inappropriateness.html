inappropriateness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.əˌproʊ.priˈeɪt.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.əˌprəʊ.priˈeɪt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unsuitable or improper for a particular situation or context
state of being inappropriate

Examples

The inappropriateness of his jokes made everyone uncomfortable during the meeting.
She apologized for the inappropriateness of her comments at the dinner table.
The review highlighted the inappropriateness of using informal language in the official report.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appropriateness - the quality or state of being suitable; opposite of inappropriateness
  • Synonym: impropriety - failure to observe standards or show due honesty or modesty; synonym for inappropriateness

Etymology

From 'inappropriate' + '-ness'; 'inappropriate' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'appropriate'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪn.əˌproʊ.pri.ətnəs / (AmE), / ɪn.əˈprəʊ.pri.ətnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being suitable or proper in a particular situation
the state of being inappropriate

Examples

The inappropriateness of his remarks became clear only after the meeting ended.
She was embarrassed by the inappropriateness of her outfit for the formal event.
The judge pointed out the inappropriateness of making personal comments in a legal argument.

Synset Examples

he retracted nothing that he had said about the inappropriateness of either a corporeal God or a God who is a person
his praise released from her loud protestations of her unworthiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriacy - the state of being inappropriate; synonym for inappropriateness
  • Antonym: appropriateness - the quality of being suitable or proper

Etymology

From 'inappropriate' + '-ness', modelled on Latin roots in- (not) and appropriatus (made suitable).
