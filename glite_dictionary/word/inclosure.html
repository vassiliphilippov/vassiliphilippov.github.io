inclosure

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
a document or item that is included inside an envelope with a covering letter, usually as supporting material
item enclosed with a letter

Examples

Please find the inclosure with the details of your appointment.
The letter had an inclosure that explained the new policy in depth.
He forgot to include the inclosure in the envelope before mailing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclosure - a document or item included in an envelope with a letter

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'enclosure', from Late Middle English, based on Old French 'enclos' (enclosed).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of enclosing something within something else; the process of surrounding or confining.
the act of enclosing something
archaic british

Examples

The inclosure of the common fields changed traditional farming practices in the region.
She supported the inclosure of the land for use as a private garden.
Debates about the inclosure of open spaces were frequent in Parliament during that period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclosure - the act of enclosing something inside something else; modern spelling

Etymology

From Middle English, variant spelling of 'enclosure', ultimately from Latin 'includere' (to shut in).
