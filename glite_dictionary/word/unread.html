unread

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛd / (BrE)
not informed or knowledgeable about a subject because of not having read about it
not knowledgeable due to not reading

Examples

The professor was surprised that so many students were unread in classic literature.
Because he was largely unread in philosophy, the arguments seemed unfamiliar to him.
A person unread in legal matters might find the contract confusing.

Synset Examples

he seems to have been wholly unread in political theory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-read - highly informed or knowledgeable as a result of extensive reading

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'read' (to have read).
