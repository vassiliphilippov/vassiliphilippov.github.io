Melilotus officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈloʊtəs ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌmelɪˈlɒtəs ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A biennial yellow-flowered Eurasian plant with aromatic leaves, used as a carminative or flavoring agent and widely grown as green manure or a cover crop.
yellow-flowered Eurasian herb; green manure crop

Examples

Farmers often plant Melilotus officinalis to improve soil fertility through its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
Historically, Melilotus officinalis was used for its mild, sweet scent in traditional herbal remedies.
The bright yellow flowers of Melilotus officinalis attract many pollinators in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - any cloverlike plant of the Melilotus genus, including Melilotus officinalis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'melilotos' (meaning 'sweet clover') and Latin 'officinalis' (of or belonging to an officina, a monastery storeroom or pharmacy, indicating recognized medicinal value).
