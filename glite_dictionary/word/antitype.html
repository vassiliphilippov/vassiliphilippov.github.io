antitype

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tiˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.taɪp / (BrE)
a person or thing that is represented or foreshadowed by an earlier symbol or event, especially a figure or event in the New Testament having a counterpart in the Old Testament
person or thing foreshadowed by an earlier type

Examples

In Christian theology, Christ is often regarded as the antitype to Old Testament figures like Adam or Moses.
The Passover lamb is seen as a type, and Jesus as its antitype in the New Testament narrative.
Scholars debate which Old Testament events serve as types and which New Testament figures are their antitypes.

Synset Examples

Aaron and Jesus Christ are the type and antitype for the high priest who made atonement for the sins of the people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: type - a person or thing that foreshadows or prefigures another; original model in theological usage

Etymology

From Greek antitupos, meaning 'corresponding to a type'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ti.taɪp / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.taɪp / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the opposite or direct contrast of another type
opposite or contrasting type

Examples

Some literary characters are written as the antitype of the protagonist, serving as their foil.
The architect designed the new building as the antitype of the historic one across the street.
Her cheerful demeanor was the antitype of his constant gloom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: type - a kind, class, or category of things or people sharing common characteristics
  • Synonym: counterpart - a person or thing that corresponds to or has the same function as another in a different place or situation

Etymology

From Greek antitupos (striking back, corresponding as an opposite), from anti- (against) + tupos (type).
