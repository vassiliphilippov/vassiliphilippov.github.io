pachycheilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈkaɪliə / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈkiːlɪə / (BrE)
An abnormal condition characterized by excessive thickness of the lips.
abnormally thick lips (medical)
medical

Examples

The rare congenital disorder included pachycheilia as one of its primary symptoms.
A surgeon was consulted to evaluate the patient's severe pachycheilia.
In medical literature, pachycheilia is noted as a feature of several genetic syndromes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrocheilia - unusually large lips, especially due to swelling or abnormal growth

Etymology

From Greek 'pakhys' (thick) + 'kheilos' (lip).
