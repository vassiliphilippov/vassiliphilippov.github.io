dusk

1. VERB

/ dʌsk / (AmE), / dʌsk / (BrE)
(of the day or sky) to become dusk; to grow dark as evening approaches.
grow dark at evening

Examples

As the sun set, the sky began to dusk over the quiet town.
The landscape slowly dusked, hiding the hills in shadow.
It was around eight o'clock when the lake started to dusk and the first stars appeared.

2. NOUN

/ dʌsk / (AmE), / dʌsk / (BrE)
The period of time just after the sun has set and before it becomes completely dark.
time just after sunset

Examples

We took a long walk at dusk and watched the sky turn deep blue.
Birds often become more active at dusk as they prepare to roost for the night.
The streetlights flickered on as dusk settled over the city.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the time between sunset and darkness
  • Antonym: dawn - the time of day when light first appears, before sunrise

Etymology

Probably from Middle English dusken (to grow dark), of uncertain origin.
