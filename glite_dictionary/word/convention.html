convention

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛn(t)ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɛn(t)ʃən / (BrE)
an international agreement between countries, especially one less formal than a treaty
international agreement

Examples

The Geneva Convention outlines protections for prisoners of war.
Several countries signed the convention to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
Under the new international convention, trade restrictions were lifted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between countries
  • Synonym: protocol - an official procedure or system of rules, sometimes meaning a formal international agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'conventio' (a meeting, agreement), from 'convenire' (to come together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
an accepted way of behaving or doing something that most people in a society or group follow
an accepted social or cultural practice

Examples

In many cultures, it is a convention to shake hands when meeting someone for the first time.
The artist challenged every convention of classical painting in her new work.
Using a fork and knife to eat is merely a social convention, not a necessity.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something
  • Synonym: tradition - a belief or behavior passed down within a group or society with symbolic meaning
  • Antonym: innovation - a new method, idea, or product, especially one that changes established practices

Etymology

From Latin 'conventio' (agreement, meeting), from 'convenire' (to come together).
