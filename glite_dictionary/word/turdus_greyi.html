Turdus greyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːdəs ˈɡreɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs ˈɡreɪ.i / (BrE)
A species of robin found in Mexico and Central America.
robin species of Mexico and Central America

Examples

The Turdus greyi is commonly spotted in the forests of southern Mexico.
Research on Turdus greyi has contributed to our understanding of robin migration patterns.
Birdwatchers travel to Central America hoping to photograph the elusive Turdus greyi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - A small passerine bird with a red or orange breast

Etymology

From Latin: 'Turdus' meaning 'thrush'; specific epithet honors an individual named Grey.
