post-menopause

1. NOUN

/ poʊstˈmɛnəˌpɔz / (AmE), / pəʊstˈmɛnəˌpɔːz / (BrE)
the period in a woman's life after the cessation of menstrual cycles and ovulation, typically following menopause
the state after a woman has stopped ovulating
medical

Examples

Many women experience different symptoms during post-menopause, such as changes in bone density.
Regular health checkups are important after entering post-menopause.
Some women feel a renewed sense of energy during post-menopause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menopause - the time when a woman permanently stops menstruating

Etymology

Formed from 'post-' (after) and 'menopause' (the cessation of menstruation).
