untruth

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtruθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruːθ / (BrE)
a statement that is not true; something false or a lie
false statement; lie

Examples

He was accused of telling an untruth during the meeting.
Spreading an untruth can damage someone's reputation.
She apologized after realizing her statement was an untruth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + truth, first recorded in Middle English.
