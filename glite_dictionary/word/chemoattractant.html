chemoattractant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkimoʊ.əˈtræktənt / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊ.əˈtræktənt / (BrE)
a substance that attracts and directs the movement of motile cells, especially by inducing positive chemotaxis
substance attracting cell movement
medical

Examples

White blood cells use chemoattractant signals to locate sites of infection.
Researchers identified a new chemoattractant involved in wound healing.
The presence of the chemoattractant caused the bacteria to move in a directed manner.

Synset Examples

The major role of chemokines is to act as a chemoattractant to guide the migration of cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemokine - a type of chemoattractant cytokine that directs immune cell movement

Etymology

From chemo- (relating to chemicals) and attractant (something that attracts), indicating a substance that attracts via chemical means.
