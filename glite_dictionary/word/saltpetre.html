saltpetre

1. NOUN

/ sɔltˈpiːtər / (AmE), / sɔːltˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a naturally occurring mineral, potassium nitrate (KNO₃), used especially as a fertilizer and in explosives such as gunpowder
potassium nitrate used in fertilizer and explosives

Examples

The ancient Chinese were among the first to use saltpetre in the manufacture of gunpowder.
Farmers commonly add saltpetre to soil to improve crop yields.
The storage facility contained barrels of saltpetre for industrial purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium nitrate - the chemical compound consisting of potassium, nitrogen, and oxygen (KNO₃)

Etymology

From French 'salpêtre', from Medieval Latin 'sal petrae' (salt of rock).
