cappuccino

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpəˈtʃinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkapʊˈtʃiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A type of coffee made with equal parts espresso and steamed milk, often topped with foamed milk and sometimes a sprinkle of cinnamon, nutmeg, or whipped cream.
coffee drink with espresso and steamed milk

Examples

She ordered a cappuccino at the café before heading to work.
The barista decorated the cappuccino with a beautiful heart-shaped pattern in the foam.
Would you like a sprinkle of cinnamon on your cappuccino?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latte - coffee made with espresso and lots of steamed milk
  • Synonym: espresso - strong coffee brewed by forcing steam through ground coffee beans

Etymology

From Italian 'cappuccino', diminutive of 'cappuccio' meaning 'hood', referring to the Capuchin friars' hoods whose color resembles the drink.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæpəˈtʃinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkæpəˈtʃiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A coffee drink made with equal parts of espresso and steamed milk, often topped with foamed milk and sometimes sprinkled with cocoa or cinnamon.
espresso coffee with steamed milk foam

Examples

She ordered a cappuccino and a croissant for breakfast at the cafe.
I enjoy sprinkling a little cinnamon on top of my cappuccino.
The barista skillfully poured the steamed milk to create the perfect cappuccino.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: latte - coffee drink with espresso and more steamed milk, less foam
  • Degree Lower: espresso - strong black coffee made by forcing steam through ground coffee beans

Etymology

From Italian cappuccino, referring to the brown color of Capuchin friars’ robes.
