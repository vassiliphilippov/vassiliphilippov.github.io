pulasan

1. NOUN

/ puːˈlɑːsɑn / (AmE), / pʊˈlɑːsən / (BrE)
A tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia, similar in appearance to the rambutan but with sweeter flesh.
sweet tropical fruit similar to rambutan

Examples

The market had a fresh batch of pulasan alongside mangosteens and rambutans.
Locals enjoy eating pulasan fresh or adding it to desserts for its sweet flavor.
The skin of the pulasan fruit is easily twisted open to reveal the juicy flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - a tropical fruit with hairy rind, related to the lychee

Etymology

From Malay 'pulas' meaning 'to twist', referring to how the fruit is opened.

2. NOUN

/ puːˈlɑːsɑːn / (AmE), / puːˈlɑːsan / (BrE)
A tropical tree of Southeast Asia (Nephelium mutabile), closely related to the rambutan and bearing edible, sweet, reddish fruits with a thick rind.
tropical fruit tree like rambutan

Examples

The pulasan is prized for its sweet, juicy flesh, making it a favorite in Southeast Asian markets.
Unlike the rambutan, the skin of the pulasan is easier to twist open by hand.
Farmers harvest pulasans in the late summer, when the fruits turn a deep red color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - tropical fruit similar to the pulasan, but less sweet

Etymology

From Malay 'pulas', meaning to twist, referring to the way the fruit is opened.
