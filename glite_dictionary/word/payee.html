payee

1. NOUN

/ peɪˈi / (AmE), / peɪˈiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom money is paid, especially as the recipient named in a bill, cheque, or other payment.
person to whom money is paid

Examples

The cheque should be endorsed by the payee before it can be cashed.
In most transactions, the payee must provide identification to receive payment.
Both the payer and the payee must keep records for tax purposes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: payer - a person who pays; one who makes a payment

Etymology

From 'pay' + '-ee', the suffix indicating the recipient of an action, patterned after similar legal and financial terms from French.
