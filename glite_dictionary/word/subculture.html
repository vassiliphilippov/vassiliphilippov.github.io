subculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a group within a larger society whose members have distinct values, behaviors, or interests that set them apart from the mainstream culture
distinct social group within a culture

Examples

The punk subculture emerged in the 1970s with its own music and fashion.
Members of the gaming subculture often gather at large conventions.
She studied a youth subculture that rejects traditional values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterculture - a culture with values and norms opposed to those of the mainstream
  • Antonym: mainstream - the dominant trend in opinion, fashion, or the arts

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'culture' from Latin 'cultura' (cultivation, culture).
