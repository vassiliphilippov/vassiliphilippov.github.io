vicereine

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪsəˈreɪn / (AmE), / ˌvaɪsɪˈreɪn / (BrE)
the wife of a viceroy or a woman who acts as a viceroy
wife of a viceroy or female viceroy

Examples

During the colonial era, the vicereine played a prominent role in social and charitable activities.
The new vicereine was welcomed by the local nobility at an elaborate reception.
Historical records often describe the influence that the vicereine had on court politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - governor ruling as representative of a sovereign

Etymology

From French 'vicereine', feminine of 'vice-roi' (viceroy), from Latin 'vice' (in place of) + 'regina' (queen).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪsˈreɪn / (AmE), / ˌvʌɪsˈreɪn / (BrE)
A female governor of a country or province who rules as the representative of a king, queen, or sovereign.
female governor acting as a sovereign's representative

Examples

The vicereine presided over the ceremony with grace and authority.
During the colonial era, the vicereine played a vital role in the administration of the province.
Many portraits depict the vicereine alongside her husband, the viceroy, at official functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - governor ruling as a sovereign's representative

Etymology

From Italian 'viceré' (viceroy) with feminine suffix '-ine'.
