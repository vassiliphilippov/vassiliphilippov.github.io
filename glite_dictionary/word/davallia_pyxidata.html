Davallia pyxidata

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæliə pɪkˈsaɪdətə / (AmE), / dəˈvalɪə pɪkˈsɪdətə / (BrE)
A species of hare's-foot fern native to eastern Australia, known for its creeping rhizomes covered in soft, brown hairs.
a species of hare's-foot fern

Examples

In her greenhouse, she grows several unusual plants, including a Davallia pyxidata near the window.
The creeping rhizomes of Davallia pyxidata are covered with soft hair, giving it the common name hare's-foot fern.
Gardeners often favor the Davallia pyxidata for its decorative foliage and easy maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare's-foot fern - a common name for ferns of the genus Davallia, with hairy creeping rhizomes

Etymology

Genus name Davallia honors 18th-century Swiss botanist Edmond Davall; species name from Greek 'pyxis' (box) and Latin '-atus' (provided with).
