Akaba

1. PROPN

/ ɑːˈkɑːbə / (AmE), / əˈkɑːbə / (BrE)
a port city in southwestern Jordan on the Gulf of Aqaba, known for its strategic location and tourism industry
port city in southwestern Jordan

Examples

Many tourists visit Akaba for its beautiful beaches and coral reefs.
Trade through Akaba has increased significantly in recent years.
The city of Akaba lies at the northern tip of the Red Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aqaba - variant spelling of Akaba, Jordanian port city

Etymology

From Arabic 'Aqaba' (العقبة), meaning 'the pass' or 'the steep path'.
