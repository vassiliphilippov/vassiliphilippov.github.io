Marasmius oreades

1. NOUN

/ məˈræz.mi.əs ɔˈri.eɪdɛz / (AmE), / məˈraz.mɪ.əs ɔːˈriːədiːz / (BrE)
a small edible mushroom that commonly grows in grassy areas in fairy rings, also known as the fairy ring mushroom
a mushroom forming fairy rings in grass

Examples

The Marasmius oreades is often found growing in circles on lawns and meadows.
Many foragers prize Marasmius oreades for its delicate, nutty flavor.
Be sure to correctly identify Marasmius oreades before harvesting it for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy ring - a naturally occurring ring or arc of mushrooms in grass

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Marasmius' from Greek 'marasmus' (wasting), and 'oreades' from Oreads, nymphs of Greek mythology living in mountains.
