portcullis

1. NOUN

/ pɔrtˈkʌlɪs / (AmE), / pɔːtˈkʌlɪs / (BrE)
a strong heavy grating made of iron or wood that could be lowered vertically to block the gateway of a castle or fortified town
a heavy castle gateway grating

Examples

The castle's portcullis was lowered at dusk to protect against intruders.
Tourists admired the massive iron portcullis at the entrance of the medieval fortress.
With a loud clang, the portcullis fell, sealing off the passageway.

Etymology

From Old French 'porte coleice', from 'porte' (gate) and 'coliz' (sliding), from Latin 'porta' (gate).
