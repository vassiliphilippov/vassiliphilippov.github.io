/ ˈwɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈwɪmzi / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈwɪmzi / (BrE)
the quality of being fanciful, unpredictable, or subject to sudden changes of mind; whimsicality
quality of being whimsical or unpredictable

Examples

Her decision to redecorate the entire room was driven by a sense of whimsey.
The artist's whimsey shines through in his playful sculptures.
He acts with a certain whimsey that makes predicting his choices nearly impossible.

Synset Examples

I despair at the flightiness and whimsicality of my memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whimsy - the quality of being fanciful or capricious; alternate spelling
  • Synonym: caprice - a sudden and unaccountable change of mood or behavior
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being firm, stable, or predictable

Etymology

From English 'whim', of uncertain origin, with variant spelling.
