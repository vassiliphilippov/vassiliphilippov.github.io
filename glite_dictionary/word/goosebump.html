goosebump

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsbʌmp / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsbʌmp / (BrE)
A small raised area on the skin that appears when a person is cold, afraid, or excited, caused by the contraction of tiny muscles at the base of hair follicles.
small raised skin area from cold or emotion

Examples

The scary movie gave me goosebumps during the most intense scenes.
When she heard the beautiful song, goosebumps appeared on her arms.
Walking outside on a chilly morning, I instantly got goosebumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose pimples - Another term for goosebumps, used especially in British English.
  • Synonym: chill - Sensation of cold, a common cause of goosebumps.

Etymology

From 'goose' and 'bump', referring to the surface of goose skin resembling the appearance of human skin when experiencing this effect.
