vintage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪntɪdʒ / (BrE)
The wine produced during a particular season and from a specific vineyard; also, the year or season in which the grapes were harvested.
year's wine yield from a vineyard

Examples

The 2015 vintage was considered exceptional by wine experts.
This bottle is from a rare vintage that collectors highly prize.
Each vintage reflects the unique weather and soil conditions of that year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - the process or period of gathering crops

Etymology

From Old French *vendange* (grape harvest), from Latin *vindemia* (grape gathering).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
The age or oldness of a wine, especially as a factor affecting its quality.
the oldness of wine

Examples

The vintage of this bottle gives it a uniquely smooth flavor.
Collectors often seek wines of a particular vintage for their rarity and taste.
Experts can estimate the vintage of a wine by assessing its color and aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: age - the length of time that something has existed

Etymology

From Old French *vendage* (wine harvest), from Latin *vindemia* (grape-gathering).
