Punicaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːnɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌpjuːnɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a former botanical family consisting of the pomegranate and its relatives
former plant family of pomegranates

Examples

The Punicaceae family is best known for its sole surviving member, the pomegranate.
Botanists used to classify pomegranates in the Punicaceae before merging it with the Lythraceae family.
References to Punicaceae are still found in older botanical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Punica - genus of flowering plants including the pomegranate

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Punica, from Latin Punicus (Carthaginian).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːnɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌpjuːnɪˈkeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a small family of flowering plants consisting solely of the pomegranate, formerly recognized in some classifications
pomegranate plant family

Examples

The Punicaceae family was once used to classify only the pomegranate tree.
Botanists now often include Punicaceae within the broader Lythraceae family.
Fruit from the Punicaceae family has been cultivated since ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lythraceae - the loosestrife family, which now commonly includes pomegranates in modern classifications

Etymology

From Latin Punica (genus of pomegranate) + -aceae (family suffix in botanical Latin).
