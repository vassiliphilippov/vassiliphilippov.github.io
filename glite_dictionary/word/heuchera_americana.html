Heuchera americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌhjuːkərə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˌhjuːkərə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant with mottled basal leaves and loose clusters of small flowers on erect stems, native to North America; commonly called American alumroot.
a North American perennial plant (American alumroot)

Examples

The shaded garden featured several clumps of Heuchera americana alongside ferns and hostas.
Botanists study the leaf patterns of Heuchera americana to distinguish it from related species.
Native Americans once used the roots of Heuchera americana for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumroot - another common name for Heuchera americana

Etymology

From the genus Heuchera, named after German physician Johann Heinrich von Heucher, and Latin 'americana' meaning 'of America'.
