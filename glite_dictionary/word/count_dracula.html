Count Dracula

1. PROPN

/ kaʊnt ˈdrækjələ / (AmE), / kaʊnt ˈdrækjʊlə / (BrE)
the fictional vampire count and antagonist in Bram Stoker's 1897 gothic novel 'Dracula', often representing the archetypal vampire in literature and popular culture
the famous fictional vampire from Bram Stoker's novel

Examples

Many films and books have been inspired by Count Dracula and his sinister legend.
The character of Count Dracula has become synonymous with vampires in modern culture.
In Bram Stoker's novel, Count Dracula travels from Transylvania to England to spread his curse.

Etymology

From the character name in Bram Stoker's novel, inspired by Vlad Dracula (Vlad the Impaler), a 15th-century ruler of Wallachia.
