unlettered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɛtɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛtəd / (BrE)
lacking the ability to read or write; having little or no education.
illiterate; lacking in education

Examples

Many of the unlettered adults in the village struggle with basic paperwork.
Education programs aim to help unlettered children learn to read and write.
The historian wrote about how unlettered people contributed to oral traditions.

Synset Examples

special tutorials to assist the unlettered sector of society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

From un- (not) + lettered (educated, literate).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɛt̬ɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛtəd / (BrE)
not able to read or write; lacking education or refinement.
not able to read or write; uneducated

Examples

She grew up in a rural town among mostly unlettered neighbors.
The novel satirizes the arrogance some feel toward the unlettered masses.
Despite being unlettered, he possessed a keen natural intelligence.

Synset Examples

an ignorant man
nescient of contemporary literature
an unlearned group incapable of understanding complex issues
exhibiting contempt for his unlettered companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write
  • Antonym: educated - having been taught, especially at school or university

Etymology

From un- (not) + lettered (literate, educated), referring to letters of the alphabet.
