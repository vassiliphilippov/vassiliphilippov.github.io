gaggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæɡəl / (BrE)
a group of geese when they are on the ground or in water; sometimes used informally for any noisy group of people
a group of geese (on ground or water)

Examples

We saw a gaggle of geese near the lake early this morning.
A gaggle of geese honked loudly as they crossed the road.
Children watched as a gaggle of geese waddled through the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds, especially when flying or feeding together

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, imitative of the sounds made by geese.
