devil-ridden

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəl ˌrɪdən / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl ˌrɪdən / (BrE)
Influenced, controlled, or troubled by evil or demonic forces.
plagued by evil or demonic forces

Examples

The old house was rumored to be devil-ridden, keeping locals away for decades.
He claimed that his nightmares were the result of a devil-ridden mind.
The story featured a devil-ridden priest who struggled with his inner demons.

Synset Examples

The novel described a devil-ridden town plagued by misfortune.
They avoided the devil-ridden area after dark.
Her devil-ridden dreams left her feeling restless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunted - frequently visited or troubled by ghosts or spirits
  • Antonym: blessed - endowed with divine favor or protection

Etymology

Formed from 'devil' + '-ridden', meaning oppressed or dominated by (cf. 'guilt-ridden').
