Chipewyan

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈpwiːən / (AmE), / tʃɪˈpwiːən / (BrE)
a member of the Athapaskan Indigenous people living in western Canada, chiefly between Great Slave Lake and Hudson Bay
member of an Indigenous group in western Canada

Examples

The Chipewyan have lived in the region for thousands of years, adapting to the harsh northern climate.
She is studying the culture and traditions of the Chipewyan people for her anthropology thesis.
Many Chipewyan communities maintain their traditional ways of hunting and fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athapaskan - group of related Indigenous peoples and languages of North America

Etymology

From Cree Cree speakers’ pronunciation of the group's self-designation; adopted in English via early explorers and traders.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɪpəˈwaɪən / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪpɪˈwaɪən / (BrE)
the Athabaskan language spoken by the Chipewyan people of northern Canada.
Athabaskan language of the Chipewyan people

Examples

Many elders in the community still speak Chipewyan as their first language.
She is taking classes to learn Chipewyan and preserve her heritage.
The story was translated from English into Chipewyan for the local radio station.

Etymology

From Chipewyan, an anglicized form of the Cree word *Cīpwayān* (“pointed skins,” referring to the style of parka).
