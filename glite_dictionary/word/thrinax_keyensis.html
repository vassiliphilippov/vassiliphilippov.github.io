Thrinax keyensis

1. NOUN

/ θraɪˌnæks kiˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / θrɪˌnaks kiˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a small, stocky fan palm native to southern Florida and Cuba, known for its slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves
a species of fan palm from Florida and Cuba

Examples

The Thrinax keyensis can be seen thriving in the coastal hammocks of south Florida.
Gardeners value Thrinax keyensis for its resilience to salty conditions and strong winds.
The small white fruits of the Thrinax keyensis attract a variety of birds.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'thrinax' (trident, referring to divided leaves); species name refers to the Florida Keys.
