epiplexis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈplɛksɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈplɛksɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which the speaker reproaches or questions the audience in order to persuade or incite them.
rhetorical reproach for persuasion

Examples

The politician employed epiplexis to shame the audience into supporting the new policy.
Her speech was filled with epiplexis, as she berated listeners for their apathy.
Teachers sometimes use epiplexis to encourage students to reflect on their actions.

Etymology

From Greek epiplexis (rebuke), from epiplexis, from epiplekein (to rebuke, upbraid).
