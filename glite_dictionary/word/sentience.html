sentience

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.ʃəns / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪ.əns / (BrE)
the capacity to experience feelings or sensations; the state of being conscious or aware
capacity for feeling or consciousness

Examples

Debates about animal rights often focus on the sentience of non-human creatures.
Meditation can increase one's awareness of their own sentience.
Some philosophers argue that sentience is what gives beings moral value.

Synset Examples

the crash intruded on his awareness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consciousness - the state of being aware of and able to think; awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'sentientia' (feeling, perception), from 'sentire' (to feel).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.ti.əns / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪəns / (BrE)
the capacity to feel, perceive, or experience sensations and consciousness
capacity for feeling or consciousness

Examples

Some philosophers argue that animals possess sentience and therefore deserve moral consideration.
The debate about artificial intelligence often centers on the possibility of machine sentience.
Her novel explored themes of sentience in alien life forms and their capacity for suffering.

Synset Examples

gave sentience to slugs and newts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consciousness - the state of being aware of and able to think and perceive
  • Antonym: insentience - the lack of capacity to feel or perceive sensations

Etymology

From Latin sentientia (feeling, perception), from sentire (to feel).
