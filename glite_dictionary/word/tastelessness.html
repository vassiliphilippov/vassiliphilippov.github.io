tastelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈteɪstləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no flavor
the state of having no flavor

Examples

The tastelessness of the dish disappointed everyone at the dinner party.
He complained about the tastelessness of hospital food after his surgery.
You could tell something was missing from the soup because of its tastelessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - lack of flavor or zest
  • Antonym: flavorfulness - the quality of having a strong or interesting flavor

Etymology

From 'tasteless' + '-ness', after 'taste' (flavor) + '-less' (without).

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈteɪstlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking good taste or aesthetic judgment; inelegance or crudity.
lack of good taste or refinement

Examples

The tastelessness of the décor put off many of the guests at the party.
His tastelessness in jokes sometimes made people uncomfortable.
Critics pointed out the tastelessness of the film's script and costumes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tastefulness - the quality of having good taste or refinement
  • Synonym: vulgarity - the quality of being crude or lacking refinement

Etymology

From 'tasteless' (without taste) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).
