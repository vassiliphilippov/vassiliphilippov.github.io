salpinx

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.pɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈsæl.pɪŋks / (BrE)
a tubular anatomical structure, especially the Fallopian tube in the uterus or the Eustachian tube in the ear
anatomical tube in uterus or ear
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the patient's right salpinx as the site of the blockage.
Inflammation of the salpinx can lead to complications such as infertility.
The Eustachian tube is also known as the auditory salpinx.

Etymology

From Greek salpinx (σαλπίγξ), meaning 'trumpet', referring to trumpet-shaped tubes.
