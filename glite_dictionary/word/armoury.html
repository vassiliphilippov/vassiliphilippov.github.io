armoury

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
all the weapons and military equipment possessed by a country or organization.
collection of weapons and equipment

Examples

The country expanded its armoury to prepare for potential threats.
Diplomatic relations helped reduce the size of their nuclear armoury.
Modern armies rely on an extensive armoury of advanced technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a collection or supply of weapons and military equipment

Etymology

From Old French armarie, from Latin armārium (chest for weapons), from arma (arms, weapons).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
a place where weapons and military equipment are manufactured
place where weapons are manufactured

Examples

The old armoury was converted into a museum of military history.
Highly trained workers operated machinery in the royal armoury.
Strict security measures are enforced at the armoury to protect the production process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a place where weapons and military equipment are stored or made

Etymology

From Old French *armorie*, from *arme* (weapon), ultimately from Latin *arma* (arms, weapons).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
A building or place where weapons, ammunition, and military equipment are stored and where training in the use of arms is provided.
building for storing weapons and military training

Examples

The soldiers gathered at the armoury before heading out on patrol.
The city's old armoury has been converted into a museum.
Access to the armoury is restricted and closely monitored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a place where weapons and military equipment are stored or made

Etymology

From Old French 'armorie', from Latin 'armarium' (closet for tools, chest, or armory), from 'arma' (arms, weapons).

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
A collection of resources, skills, or strategies available for use, especially as metaphorical tools to achieve something.
collection of resources or strategies

Examples

She considered patience one of the greatest tools in her armoury as a teacher.
The company's vast armoury of patents gives it an advantage over competitors.
He drew upon his armoury of persuasive techniques to win the debate.

Synset Examples

he dipped into his intellectual armory to find an answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a collection of weapons or resources for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French armoirie, from armoire (chest), ultimately from Latin armārium (closet, chest for arms).
