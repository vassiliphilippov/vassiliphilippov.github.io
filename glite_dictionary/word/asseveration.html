asseveration

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɛvəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsɛvəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an emphatic or forceful declaration or assertion, often made as if it requires no supporting evidence
emphatic declaration or assertion

Examples

His asseveration that he had never lied was met with skepticism.
Despite her repeated asseverations of innocence, the jury remained unconvinced.
The witness's asseveration was delivered with unwavering conviction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'asseveratio', from 'asseverare' (to assert seriously).
