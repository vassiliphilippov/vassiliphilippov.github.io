devotion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃən / (BrE)
strong commitment or dedication to a cause, activity, or purpose
strong commitment or dedication

Examples

His devotion to the project inspired his colleagues to work harder.
She showed great devotion to her family throughout her life.
The devotion of countless volunteers made the event possible.

Synset Examples

the devotion of his time and wealth to science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - the quality of being committed to a task or purpose
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'devotio' (a vow, dedication).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃən / (BrE)
deep love and commitment shown towards someone or something
deep love and commitment

Examples

Her devotion to her family was clear in everything she did.
He spoke of his lifelong devotion to his wife with obvious pride.
Their devotion for one another helped them through difficult times.

Synset Examples

their devotion to each other was beautiful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - the quality of being committed to a task or purpose
  • Degree Lower: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Degree Higher: adoration - deep love and respect

Etymology

From Latin 'devotionem' (dedication, consecration), from 'devotus', past participle of 'devovēre' (to vow or dedicate).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃən / (BrE)
deep religious commitment or enthusiasm, often expressed by a willingness to serve or worship God.
deep religious commitment or zeal

Examples

Her lifelong devotion to the church inspired many people in her community.
The monk's daily routines reflected his deep devotion to God.
During the ceremony, everyone admired the devotion with which she offered her prayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piety - devoutness or reverence towards God
  • Antonym: irreverence - lack of respect or devotion toward religious practices

Etymology

From Latin 'devotio' (act of consecrating), from 'devotus', past participle of 'devovēre' (to vow).

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃən / (BrE)
(usually plural) an act of religious observance or prayer, especially one performed privately or silently.
act of religious prayer or observance

Examples

He returned to his devotions after dinner, spending time in prayer and reflection.
Many people begin their day with morning devotions in the quiet of their own rooms.
During Lent, she found comfort in the daily devotions at her local church.

Synset Examples

he returned to his devotions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - a solemn request or expression of thanks addressed to God or an object of worship

Etymology

From Latin 'devotio' (a solemn vow), from 'devovere' (to vow).
