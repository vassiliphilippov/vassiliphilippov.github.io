decigram

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪɡram / (BrE)
A metric unit of mass equal to one tenth of a gram.
one tenth of a gram (mass unit)

Examples

The mixture should contain exactly five decigrams of the compound.
A decigram is commonly used for precise measurement in chemistry labs.
One decigram is equal to one hundred milligrams.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gram - the base metric unit of mass
  • Degree Lower: milligram - a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a gram

Etymology

From French 'décigramme', based on 'déci-' (ten) and 'gramme' (gram), from Greek 'gramma' (a small weight).
