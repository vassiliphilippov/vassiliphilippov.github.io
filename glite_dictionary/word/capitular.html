capitular

1. ADJ

/ kəˈpɪtʃələr / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to an ecclesiastical chapter, especially the governing body of a cathedral or collegiate church
of an ecclesiastical chapter

Examples

The cathedral's capitular statutes were revised in the eighteenth century.
He was entrusted with the management of the capitular estates.
The capitular archives contain records dating back to the Middle Ages.

Synset Examples

capitular estates

Etymology

From Latin capitulum (chapter, little head), referring to a chapter of a church.
