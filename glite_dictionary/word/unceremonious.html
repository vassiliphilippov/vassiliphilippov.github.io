unceremonious

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnsɛrəˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnsərəˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
done without ceremony or formality; abrupt or lacking courtesy.
without ceremony or formality

Examples

His unceremonious departure surprised everyone at the party.
She gave an unceremonious wave and walked out of the meeting.
The announcement was made in a rather unceremonious manner, with no special event or speech.

Synset Examples

an unceremonious speech

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ceremonious - marked by formality or ceremony

Etymology

From un- (not) + ceremonious, after ceremony.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnsəˌrɛməˈnoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəˌrɛməˈnəʊniəs / (BrE)
(of an action or manner) done abruptly and without the usual formality or ceremony.
done without formalities or ceremony

Examples

His unceremonious departure surprised many of his colleagues.
The manager gave an unceremonious response to the proposal, ending the discussion abruptly.
Guests were given an unceremonious welcome, with little attention to custom or tradition.

Synset Examples

unceremonious dismissal from office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ceremonious - marked by formality or ceremony

Etymology

From un- (not) + ceremonious (relating to ceremony).
