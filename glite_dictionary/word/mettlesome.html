mettlesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛt.əl.səm / (AmE), / ˈmɛt.əl.səm / (BrE)
full of spirit and courage; lively and unyielding in character
spirited; courageous and lively

Examples

Her mettlesome attitude inspired everyone on the team to give their best.
The mettlesome horse was difficult for inexperienced riders to handle.
Despite the challenges, he remained mettlesome and refused to give up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirited - full of energy, enthusiasm, or determination
  • Antonym: timid - lacking in courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

Formed from 'mettle' (variant of 'metal', meaning spirit or courage) + '-some'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛt.əl.səm / (AmE), / ˈmɛt.əl.səm / (BrE)
full of spirit and courage; willing to face danger or difficulty
spirited and courageous

Examples

Despite the risks involved, the mettlesome firefighter dashed into the burning building.
Her mettlesome spirit inspired others to volunteer for the dangerous expedition.
The young colt was so mettlesome that it required a firm, experienced rider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'mettle' (variant of 'metal' in the sense of spirit or temperament) + suffix '-some'.
