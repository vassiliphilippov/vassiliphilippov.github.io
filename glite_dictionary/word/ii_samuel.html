II Samuel

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈsæmjuəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈsæmjʊəl / (BrE)
the second of two books in the Old Testament, describing the reigns of Saul and David as kings of Israel
second book of Samuel in Old Testament

Examples

Many scholars study II Samuel to understand the history of Israel's monarchy.
The story of King David and Bathsheba is found in II Samuel.
Every Sunday, the pastor reads a passage from II Samuel during the service.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: I Samuel - the first book of Samuel in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Secundus Liber Samuelis', named after the prophet Samuel.
