babysitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbiˌsɪtər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiˌsɪtə / (BrE)
a person who takes care of children while their parents are away, usually for a short period of time
person caring for children temporarily

Examples

We hired a babysitter so we could go out for dinner.
The babysitter read stories to the children before bed.
It's hard to find a reliable babysitter on short notice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanny - a person employed to care for children in their own home, often full-time
  • Antonym: parent - the mother or father of a child

Etymology

From 'baby' + 'sitter', literally one who sits with babies; popularized in the early 20th century.
