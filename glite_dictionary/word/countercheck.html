countercheck

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
An additional check made to confirm or verify the correctness of a previous check.
secondary verification; confirmation check

Examples

The accountant performed a final countercheck to ensure there were no discrepancies in the financial report.
Always run a countercheck after your initial inspection to catch any missed errors.
The software included an automatic countercheck for data validation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: check - an examination or inspection to ensure accuracy
  • Degree Higher: to double-check - to check again for added certainty

Etymology

Counter- (against) + check, indicating a check that goes against or verifies a previous one.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
an action, measure, or check that restrains or neutralizes another check or action; often used to describe a move in chess that responds to a check with another check.
a check that restrains another check

Examples

In response to the opponent's move, the player delivered a brilliant countercheck that forced a draw.
They introduced a financial oversight committee as a countercheck to prevent fraud.
The government placed a countercheck on the new policy to ensure it would not be misused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - an act or instance of stopping or restraining; in chess, a threat to the king

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'check', referring to an opposing action or restraint.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14038737-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
to oppose or check something by taking a counteraction
oppose or check by counteraction

Examples

The committee was formed to countercheck any potential misuse of funds.
Government agencies often countercheck each other's findings to ensure accuracy.
Strict regulations are needed to countercheck the growing influence of monopolies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to counteract - to act against something to reduce its force or effect

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'check' (to stop or restrain).

4. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
to check or verify something a second time, often to confirm accuracy or correctness
to check again for accuracy

Examples

Please countercheck the figures before submitting the report to ensure there are no errors.
After the engineer inspected the machine, the supervisor went on to countercheck the results.
It is good practice to countercheck your answers for any possible mistakes before turning in your test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to double-check - to check something twice to be sure it is correct or safe

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against, in opposition) + 'check' (to examine).
