to randomise

1. VERB

/ ˈræn.dəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræn.də.maɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or order something in a random manner, especially for experimental or statistical purposes
arrange in a random order
british

Examples

The researchers decided to randomise the sample groups to avoid bias.
It is important to randomise the questions on the test to prevent cheating.
You should randomise the order of the playlist for a new listening experience.

Synset Examples

Randomize the order of the numbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to randomize - arrange in random order (American spelling)

Etymology

From random + -ise, patterned after 'randomize' (American spelling).
