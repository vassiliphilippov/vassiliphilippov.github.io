Chaldean

1. NOUN

/ kælˈdiːən / (AmE), / kælˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person skilled in occult learning, especially in ancient astrology and magic, often regarded as a wise man in antiquity
ancient wise man or occult scholar
archaic

Examples

The king summoned the Chaldean to interpret his troubling dream.
In ancient texts, a Chaldean is often depicted as a master of celestial wisdom.
Some believed the Chaldean possessed secret knowledge far beyond ordinary men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magus - a wise man or magician of ancient times
  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magic powers

Etymology

From Latin 'Chaldaeus', from Greek 'Khaldaios', originally referring to a Babylonian people renowned for astrology and magic.

2. ADJ

/ kælˈdiːən / (AmE), / kalˈdiːən / (BrE)
relating to ancient Chaldea, its people, language, or culture
of ancient Chaldea or its people

Examples

The Chaldean civilization made significant contributions to astronomy and mathematics.
Scholars have studied ancient Chaldean texts to learn about early Mesopotamian culture.
The museum features relics from the Chaldean period in southern Mesopotamia.

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Khaldaios, from Akkadian Kaldu (Chaldea).

3. NOUN

/ kælˈdiː.ən / (AmE), / kælˈdiː.ən / (BrE)
an inhabitant of ancient Chaldea, a region in southern Mesopotamia
person from ancient Chaldea

Examples

The ancient Chaldean civilization made significant advances in astronomy.
Artifacts from a Chaldean tomb were displayed in the museum.
She studied the language of the Chaldean scribes.

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Chaldaios, from Akkadian Kaldu.
