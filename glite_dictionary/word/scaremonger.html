scaremonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈskerˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈskeəmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who spreads rumors or information intended to cause public fear or alarm
person spreading fear through rumors

Examples

Critics accused the politician of being a scaremonger during the election campaign.
The newspaper dismissed rumors of economic collapse as the work of a scaremonger.
Health officials warned the public not to listen to scaremongers spreading false information online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarmist - a person who exaggerates dangers or predicts disaster
  • Synonym: propagandist - a person who deliberately spreads ideas or rumors

Etymology

Formed from 'scare' (frighten) + 'monger' (dealer or trader), originally from Old English 'mangere'.
