dicarboxylic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪkɑːrˌbɑkˈsɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪkɑːˌbɒkˈsɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of a molecule) containing two carboxyl groups per molecule.
having two carboxyl groups

Examples

Oxalic acid is a simple dicarboxylic acid found in many plants.
The dicarboxylic nature of the compound makes it useful for polymer production.
Scientists studied the reactions of several dicarboxylic acids in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monocarboxylic - containing one carboxyl group per molecule
  • Degree Higher: tricarboxylic - containing three carboxyl groups per molecule

Etymology

From di- (two) and carboxylic, referring to carboxyl groups.
