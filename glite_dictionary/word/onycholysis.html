onycholysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnɪˈkɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒnɪˈkɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The separation of a fingernail or toenail from its normal attachment to the nail bed, usually starting at the tip or sides.
separation of nail from nail bed
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with onycholysis after noticing the lifting of her fingernails.
Prolonged exposure to chemicals can sometimes cause onycholysis in workers.
Treatment of onycholysis involves keeping the affected nails dry and avoiding irritants.

Etymology

From Greek onyx (nail) + lysis (loosening, dissolving).
