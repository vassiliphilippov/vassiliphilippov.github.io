Physeter

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈsiːtər / (AmE), / fɪˈsiːtə / (BrE)
The genus of large toothed whales comprising the sperm whale.
genus of large toothed whales

Examples

The genus Physeter includes the well-known sperm whale.
Scientists have studied the characteristics of Physeter for centuries.
Fossil records indicate that Physeter once had a wider distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm whale - large marine mammal of genus Physeter

Etymology

From Greek phusētēr (blowpipe, blower).

2. NOUN

/ faɪˈsiːtər / (AmE), / fɪˈsiːtə / (BrE)
a genus of large toothed whales, including the sperm whale, and the type genus of the family Physeteridae
genus including the sperm whale

Examples

Scientists classify the sperm whale under the genus Physeter.
Fossils belonging to the extinct members of Physeter have been discovered in several locations worldwide.
The defining characteristics of the genus Physeter set it apart from other large whales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm whale - the largest toothed whale, belonging to the genus Physeter

Etymology

From Greek physētēr (blower), referring to the whale's blowhole.
