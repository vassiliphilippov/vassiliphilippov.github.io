underneath

1. ADV

/ ˌʌndɚˈniθ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈniːθ / (BrE)
in, to, or at a lower place or position, directly below something else
directly below something

Examples

The cat hid underneath the table during the thunderstorm.
The old wallpaper was peeling off, revealing a layer of paint underneath.
When the snow melted, the grass underneath was still green.

Synset Examples

we could see the original painting underneath
a house with a good foundation underneath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneath - in or to a lower position than; below
  • Antonym: above - at a higher place or position

Etymology

From Middle English 'underneþen', from Old English 'underneothan' (under beneath), from 'under' + 'neothan' (below).

2. ADV

/ ˌʌndɚˈniθ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈniːθ / (BrE)
on the lower side or surface of something; on the underside
on the underside; below

Examples

The label was stuck underneath the table and went unnoticed for weeks.
He found his keys underneath the pile of papers on his desk.
The cat hid underneath the sofa whenever there were loud noises.

Synset Examples

a chest of drawers all scratched underneath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneath - in or to a lower position; below
  • Antonym: above - in or to a higher position; over

Etymology

From Middle English, from under + neath (an obsolete word related to beneath).
