Muammar Gaddafi

1. PROPN

/ muˈɑːmɑr ɡəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / muˈɑːmɑː ɡəˈdɑːfi / (BrE)
(1942–2011) The leader of Libya who came to power through a coup in 1969 and ruled the country for over four decades, establishing a regime marked by socialism and Islamic principles.
Libyan leader, ruled 1969–2011

Examples

Muammar Gaddafi ruled Libya for more than four decades after taking power in a 1969 coup.
The regime of Muammar Gaddafi was known for its authoritarian governance and controversial foreign policies.
In 2011, a popular uprising led to the fall and death of Muammar Gaddafi.
