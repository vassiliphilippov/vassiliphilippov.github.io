frostweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑstˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with clusters of white flowers, commonly found in fields and open woodlands; also known as white crownbeard.
a tall perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

The woods behind our house are filled with frostweed blooming in early autumn.
In winter, ice formations around the base of frostweed create a beautiful, crystalline landscape.
Gardeners appreciate frostweed for attracting butterflies and providing late-season nectar.

Etymology

Named for the plant's tendency to exude sap that freezes into frosty formations in cold weather.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑstˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌwiːd / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to eastern North America, known for its early yellow flowers and the formation of ice crystals on its stems during the first frosts
a North American plant that forms ice crystals at first frost

Examples

The frostweed bloomed with bright yellow flowers at the edge of the forest.
In late autumn, you can see ice curling from the stems of frostweed on chilly mornings.
Botanists are fascinated by how frostweed produces delicate ribbons of ice during the season's first frost.

Etymology

From 'frost' + 'weed', referring to the plant's tendency to form ice crystals in cold weather.
