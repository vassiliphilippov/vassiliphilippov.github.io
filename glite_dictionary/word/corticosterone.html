corticosterone

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrdɪkoʊˈstɜrˌoʊn / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈstɜːrəʊn / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex that plays a role in regulating water and electrolyte balance in the body
a hormone regulating water and salt balance
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of corticosterone are often observed in animals experiencing chronic stress.
The researchers measured blood corticosterone to assess the hormonal response to dehydration.
Production of corticosterone in the adrenal glands is regulated by signals from the pituitary.

Etymology

From cortex (Latin for 'bark', referring to the adrenal cortex) and 'sterone' (from steroid and ketone).
