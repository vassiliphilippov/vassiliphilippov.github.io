Mars

1. PROPN

/ mɑrz / (AmE), / mɑːz / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of war and agriculture, regarded as the father of Romulus and Remus and the counterpart to the Greek god Ares.
Roman god of war and agriculture

Examples

In ancient texts, Mars is often depicted as a powerful and fearless deity leading armies to victory.
The festival of Mars was celebrated by Roman soldiers seeking his favor before battle.
According to legend, Mars was the father of Rome's founders, Romulus and Remus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ares - Greek god of war, counterpart to Mars

Etymology

From Latin Mars, the name of the Roman god of war.
