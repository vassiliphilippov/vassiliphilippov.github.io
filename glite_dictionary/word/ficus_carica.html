Ficus carica

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪkəs kəˈriːkə / (AmE), / ˈfiːkəs ˈkærɪkə / (BrE)
A species of small Mediterranean tree, widely cultivated for its edible fruit, the fig.
Mediterranean fig tree species

Examples

The Ficus carica is known for producing sweet, nutritious figs enjoyed around the world.
Farmers in southern Europe grow Ficus carica in both orchards and private gardens.
Botanists often study the Ficus carica for its unique pollination process and fruit development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - The sweet edible fruit of Ficus carica

Etymology

From Latin, where 'ficus' means 'fig' and 'carica' means 'from Caria' (an ancient region in Turkey).
