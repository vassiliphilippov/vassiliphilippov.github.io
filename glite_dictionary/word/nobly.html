nobly

1. ADV

/ ˈnoʊbli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows fine personal qualities or high moral principles.
in a noble or honorable manner

Examples

She handled the difficult situation nobly, refusing to blame others.
Despite the risk, he acted nobly to defend those in need.
The soldiers were remembered for fighting nobly in the face of danger.

Synset Examples

she has behaved nobly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignobly - in a dishonorable or base manner
  • Synonym: honorably - in an honorable manner

Etymology

From Old French noblement (nobly), from Latin nobilis (noble).
