saxicolous

1. ADJ

/ sæksˈɪkələs / (AmE), / sækˈsɪkələs / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or organism) growing on or living among rocks.
living on or among rocks

Examples

The saxicolous lichens formed colorful patches across the exposed granite.
Several saxicolous plants thrive in the rocky outcrops of the region.
Biologists discovered a saxicolous species of snail during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terricolous - living on or in the soil
  • Antonym: epiphytic - growing on another plant

Etymology

From Latin saxum (rock) + -colous (dwelling in, inhabiting), from Latin colere (to inhabit).
