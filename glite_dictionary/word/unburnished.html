unburnished

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɜrnɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɜːnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of metals or surfaces) not made shiny and smooth by rubbing or polishing.
not polished or made shiny

Examples

The artist preferred the look of unburnished copper for his sculptures.
The sword's unburnished blade gave it a rugged, ancient appearance.
Over time, the unburnished surfaces developed a natural patina.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: burnished - made shiny and smooth by rubbing

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'burnished' (from Old French 'burniss-', of Germanic origin).
