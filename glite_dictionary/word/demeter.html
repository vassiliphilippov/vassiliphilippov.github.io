Demeter

1. PROPN

/ dəˈmiːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈmiːtə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of agriculture, fertility, and marriage; the counterpart of the Roman goddess Ceres.
Greek goddess of agriculture and fertility

Examples

In ancient Greek religion, Demeter was worshipped as the bringer of fertility and the harvest.
The legend tells how Demeter's grief caused the earth to become barren.
Many ancient festivals were held in honor of Demeter to celebrate the changing seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ceres - Roman goddess of agriculture and counterpart of Demeter

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Δημήτηρ (Dēmḗtēr), possibly meaning 'earth mother'.
