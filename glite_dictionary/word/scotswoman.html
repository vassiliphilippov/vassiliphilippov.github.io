Scotswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑtsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈskɒtsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is a native or inhabitant of Scotland
female native of Scotland

Examples

As a proud Scotswoman, she celebrated St. Andrew's Day with her family every year.
The first Scotswoman to climb Mount Everest inspired many young girls in her country.
Having lived abroad for years, she still identified most strongly as a Scotswoman.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Scotsman - a man who is a native or inhabitant of Scotland

Etymology

Formed from 'Scot' (referring to a person from Scotland) + 'woman'.
