letterpress

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtərˌprɛs / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəprɛs / (BrE)
a method of printing in which raised letters or images are inked and pressed onto paper
printing method using raised type

Examples

Many small art studios still use letterpress to create unique invitations and business cards.
The texture left by letterpress gives a tactile quality that digital printing cannot match.
He bought an old letterpress machine to experiment with traditional printing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offset printing - printing method that transfers ink from a plate to a rubber blanket then to paper

Etymology

From 'letter' + 'press', referring to the pressing of inked raised letters onto paper.
