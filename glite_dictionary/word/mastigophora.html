Mastigophora

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈɡɑːfərə / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈɡɒfərə / (BrE)
a group of protozoa characterized by the possession of one or more flagella for locomotion
protozoa with flagella
medical

Examples

Some Mastigophora use their flagella to move rapidly through water.
The study of Mastigophora helps scientists understand protozoan locomotion.
Modern taxonomy has reclassified many Mastigophora into different groups.

Etymology

From Greek mastix (whip) + -phoros (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈɡɑːfərə / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈɡɒfərə / (BrE)
a former class of protozoa characterized by possessing one or more flagella for locomotion.
former class of flagellated protozoa

Examples

Some species formerly classified in Mastigophora are now grouped under other categories in modern taxonomy.
The movement of Mastigophora is primarily enabled by their long, whip-like flagella.
Researchers studied the behavior of Mastigophora to understand how flagellated protozoa adapt to changing environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Flagellate - a protozoan with one or more flagella

Etymology

From Greek 'mastix' (whip) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to whip-like flagella.
