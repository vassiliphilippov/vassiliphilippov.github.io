to dowse

1. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to make something or someone thoroughly wet; drench
to wet or drench thoroughly

Examples

A sudden wave dowsed the deck, leaving everything soaked.
She dowsed the plants with water during the hot afternoon.
The boy was accidentally dowsed when the garden hose slipped from his hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drench - to make completely wet
  • Synonym: soak - to make or become thoroughly wet
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02655322-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to search for underground water, minerals, or other substances using a divining rod
search with a divining rod

Examples

Local farmers still dowse for water in this part of the country.
He hired someone to dowse a suitable spot for drilling a well.
She claims she can dowse metals buried underground with her special rod.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to douse - to drench with liquid; sometimes a spelling variant of 'dowse'

Etymology

Perhaps a dialectal variant of 'douse.' May also relate to Cornish or Old English words for 'strike' or 'pull.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01317800-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to pour liquid over someone or something; to drench or soak with liquid
pour liquid over; drench

Examples

She accidentally dowsed her dress with coffee during breakfast.
The children dowsed each other with water on the hot summer day.
Firefighters quickly dowsed the flames with buckets of water.

Synset Examples

souse water on his hot face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to douse - to pour liquid over; to extinguish with liquid

Etymology

Probably a variant of "douse," originally of uncertain origin.
