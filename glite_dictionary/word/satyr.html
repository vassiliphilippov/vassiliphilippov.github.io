satyr

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈseɪtə / (BrE)
a man who is regarded as having strong or uncontrollable sexual desires, often in a way that is considered lecherous or lascivious
man with strong sexual desires

Examples

He was often described as a satyr because of his constant pursuit of women.
The old man’s behavior at the party was that of a satyr, making many guests uncomfortable.
In literature, a satyr is sometimes portrayed as a symbol of uncontrollable desire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecher - a man given to excessive sexual indulgence
  • Antonym: prude - a person excessively proper in conduct, especially regarding sexual matters (opposite)

Etymology

From Greek *satyros* (woodland god noted for riotousness and lasciviousness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.tə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a member of a class of lustful, drunken woodland gods, often depicted as having a man's upper body and the legs, ears, and tail of a goat, and serving as an attendant to Bacchus (Dionysus).
mythical woodland deity (Greek/Roman)

Examples

The painting depicted a mischievous satyr chasing nymphs through the forest.
Ancient myths often describe satyrs as companions to Dionysus, indulging in wine and revelry.
She dressed as a satyr for the masquerade ball, complete with horns and a furry tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faun - Roman mythological woodland deity similar to a satyr

Etymology

From Latin satyrus, from Greek satyros (woodland deity).
