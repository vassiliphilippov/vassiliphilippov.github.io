element

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
the natural or most suitable environment for a plant or animal
most suitable environment for a living thing

Examples

The dolphin thrives in the open sea, which is its element.
For many birds, the air is their natural element.
The forest serves as the perfect element for many rare plants.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means and is made up of atoms all with the same atomic number; one of the more than 100 basic substances that compose all matter.
basic chemical substance

Examples

Gold is an element that has been prized for thousands of years.
Oxygen is a vital element required by most living organisms.
Scientists discovered a new element and added it to the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound - a substance formed from two or more elements chemically combined

Etymology

From Latin elementum (basic principle, rudiment).

7. NOUN

/ ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
(in ancient and medieval science) any of the four substances—earth, air, fire, and water—believed to constitute the physical universe.
ancient substance, e.g. earth, air, fire, water

Examples

In medieval philosophy, each element was associated with different qualities and temperaments.
Some poets refer to the four elements to symbolize balance in nature.
The ancient Greeks classified everything in the universe as a combination of the four elements.

Synset Examples

the alchemists believed that there were four elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earth - the solid material forming the surface of the planet, considered as one of the elements
  • Synonym: air - the invisible gaseous substance surrounding the earth, considered as one of the elements
  • Synonym: fire - the phenomenon of combustion regarded as one of the elements
  • Synonym: water - the liquid element essential for life, considered as one of the elements

Etymology

From Latin elementum (rudiment, first principle).
