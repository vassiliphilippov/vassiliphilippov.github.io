annulet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jə.lət / (AmE), / ˈanjʊlɪt / (BrE)
A small, ring-shaped molding, often found at the top of a column in classical architecture.
ring-shaped molding on a column

Examples

The fluted shaft is decorated with an annulet near its capital.
Restorers carefully repaired each annulet to preserve the column's classical style.
A simple annulet encircles the top of the pillar, just below the capital.

Etymology

From Latin 'anellus', diminutive of 'anulus' (ring).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jə.lət / (AmE), / ˈan.jʊ.lɪt / (BrE)
(Heraldry) A charge or emblem in the shape of a small ring, used as a symbol on a coat of arms.
heraldic symbol shaped like a small ring

Examples

The family's coat of arms displays an annulet in the center shield.
An annulet is often used to distinguish cadet branches in heraldic traditions.
The crest was adorned with a golden annulet symbolizing fidelity.

Etymology

From Latin 'annulus' (ring).
