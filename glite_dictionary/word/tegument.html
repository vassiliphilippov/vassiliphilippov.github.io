tegument

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
The natural outer covering of an organism, especially skin or a similar protective layer.
natural protective body covering

Examples

The reptile's tegument helps prevent dehydration in harsh environments.
Damage to the tegument can lead to increased vulnerability to infection.
The scientist examined the tegument of the worm under a microscope.

Synset Examples

your skin is the largest organ of your body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the thin layer of tissue forming the natural outer covering of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'tegumentum' (a covering), from 'tegere' (to cover).
