barbarisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːrbərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑːbəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or act of making a person, group, or society primitive, uncivilized, or barbaric.
making people or societies uncivilized

Examples

The barbarisation of society can result from prolonged warfare and the breakdown of law and order.
Some historians argue that the barbarisation of the empire began during this turbulent century.
Cultural leaders warn against the barbarisation of the arts due to increasing censorship and repression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilization - the process of becoming more civilized or advanced
  • Synonym: barbarity - extreme brutality or lack of civilization

Etymology

From 'barbarise' + '-ation', ultimately from Greek 'barbaros' (foreign, uncivilized).
