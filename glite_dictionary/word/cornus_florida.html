Cornus florida

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs ˈflɔrɪdə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs ˈflɒrɪdə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its showy white or pink bracts, attractive autumn foliage, and red berries; commonly called the flowering dogwood.
a North American tree, the flowering dogwood

Examples

The Cornus florida in our yard bursts into white blossoms every spring.
Gardeners prize Cornus florida for its brilliant red berries and autumn foliage.
Many parks in the eastern United States feature Cornus florida as a landscape highlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering dogwood - common name for Cornus florida

Etymology

From Latin: 'cornus' meaning 'dogwood' and 'florida' meaning 'flowery'.
