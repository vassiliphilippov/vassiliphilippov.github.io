to overspread

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈsprɛd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsprɛd / (BrE)
to spread or extend across the surface of something; to cover completely or partially
cover or extend across

Examples

A gentle mist began to overspread the valley as the sun set.
Anxiety seemed to overspread her face when she heard the news.
The painter watched the color overspread the entire canvas.

Synset Examples

A big oil spot spread across the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put something over or on top of something else
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a cover from; to reveal

Etymology

From over- (across) + spread (to extend over an area); first recorded in the late 16th century.
