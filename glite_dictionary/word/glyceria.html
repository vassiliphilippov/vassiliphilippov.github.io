Glyceria

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈsɪriə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses commonly known as manna grass, found in wet habitats and recognized for their slender, spreading stems.
a genus of grasses (manna grass)

Examples

Many wetlands are dominated by Glyceria, which provides important habitat for wildlife.
The botanist identified several species of Glyceria along the riverbank.
Restoration efforts included replanting native Glyceria to stabilize the soil.

Etymology

From Greek glycys (sweet), referring to the sweet-tasting exudations of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈsɪriə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses known as manna grass, comprising species commonly found in wetlands and damp environments.
a genus of wetland grasses (manna grass)

Examples

Several species in the Glyceria genus thrive in marshy areas and along riverbanks.
The Glyceria plants are important food sources for waterfowl in their native habitats.
Botanists collected samples of Glyceria to study their adaptation to wet environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manna grass - common name for grasses of the genus Glyceria

Etymology

From Greek glykeros (sweet), referring to the sweet taste of the seeds or sap.
