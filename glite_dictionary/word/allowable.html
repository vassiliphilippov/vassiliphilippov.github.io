allowable

1. ADJ

/ əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Permitted or acceptable according to rules, standards, or circumstances.
permitted or acceptable

Examples

There is a limit to the allowable noise level in residential areas.
Only allowable expenses will be reimbursed by the company.
The court must decide whether the evidence is allowable under these circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permissible - allowed, especially by law or rule
  • Antonym: forbidden - not allowed; prohibited

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'alouer' (to allow) and the suffix '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of an item or expense) permitted to be deducted according to tax laws or official regulations.
deductible expense under law

Examples

The company claimed allowable expenses on its tax return.
Only allowable deductions should be included when calculating your taxable income.
Some entertainment costs are not allowable under current tax regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deductible - able to be subtracted from taxable income

Etymology

From verb 'allow' + suffix '-able', meaning 'able to be allowed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00693155-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
permitted by law, rules, or standards; acceptable within set limits
permitted by rules or law

Examples

The company only reimburses you for allowable expenses.
There is an allowable amount of waste in each batch produced.
Certain deductions are allowable under the current tax laws.

Synset Examples

permissible behavior in school
a permissible tax deduction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permissible - permitted by law or rules
  • Antonym: forbidden - not allowed; prohibited by rules or law

Etymology

From 'allow' + '-able', meaning capable of being allowed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01765456-a
Synset POS: a
