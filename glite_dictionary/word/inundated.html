inundated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.ʌnˌdeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or area) covered with water, especially as a result of flooding.
covered with water by flooding

Examples

After days of torrential rain, the roads were completely inundated and impassable.
The village fields were inundated, destroying much of the season's crops.
Rescue workers helped evacuate families from inundated homes near the river.

Synset Examples

the main deck was afloat (or awash)
the monsoon left the whole place awash
a flooded bathroom
inundated farmlands
an overflowing tub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flooded - covered or overflowing with water

Etymology

From Latin 'inundatus', past participle of 'inundare' (to flood).
