orthodonture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrˌθoʊˈdɑːn.tʃər / (AmE), / ˌɔːˌθəʊˈdɒn.tʃə / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry concerned with the prevention and correction of irregularities of the teeth and jaws
branch of dentistry correcting teeth alignment
medical

Examples

After studying orthodonture, she began her career as a specialist in dental correction.
Advances in orthodonture have made braces less noticeable and more comfortable for patients.
He sought a consultation in orthodonture to address his overcrowded teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodontics - branch of dentistry dealing with the straightening of teeth

Etymology

From French 'orthodonture', related to 'orthodontia' (from Greek 'orthos' meaning straight and 'odous' meaning tooth).
