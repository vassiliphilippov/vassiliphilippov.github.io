alfalfa

1. NOUN

/ ælˈfælfə / (AmE), / ælˈfælfə / (BrE)
A perennial leguminous plant with trifoliate leaves and blue-violet flowers, commonly grown as pasture and for hay.
forage plant grown for hay or pasture

Examples

Farmers often grow alfalfa to provide nutritious hay for their livestock.
The bright blue-violet flowers of alfalfa are a favorite for bees during the summer.
After harvesting the alfalfa, the field was left to rest for a season.

Etymology

From Spanish 'alfalfa', from Arabic 'al-faṣfaṣa' (the fresh fodder).

2. NOUN

/ ælˈfælfə / (AmE), / ælˈfælfə / (BrE)
A perennial leguminous plant widely grown for hay, pasture, and forage, especially valued as feed for livestock.
leguminous plant grown for hay or forage

Examples

The farmer planted alfalfa to provide nutritious feed for his dairy cows.
Fields of alfalfa stretched for miles across the countryside.
She learned that alfalfa enriches the soil by adding nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucerne - another name for alfalfa, especially in British English

Etymology

From Spanish 'alfalfa', itself from Arabic 'al-faṣfaṣa' (the fresh fodder).
