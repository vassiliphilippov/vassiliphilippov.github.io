constructive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
having a useful or positive effect; intended to help improve or develop something
helpful for improvement or development

Examples

Her manager always gives constructive feedback that helps her grow professionally.
They engaged in a constructive discussion about how to improve the team's workflow.
It is important to give constructive criticism rather than simply pointing out faults.

Synset Examples

constructive criticism
a constructive attitude
a constructive philosophy
constructive permission

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destructive - causing damage or harm; not constructive

Etymology

From Latin 'constructus', past participle of 'construere' (to build up).

WordNet ID: oewn-00587481-a

2. ADJ

/ kənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
intended to be helpful or to improve something, especially by offering useful criticism or advice; promoting improvement or progress.
intended to be helpful or improve

Examples

She provided constructive feedback to help her colleague improve his presentation skills.
It's important that our meetings focus on constructive solutions, not just complaints.
The teacher admires students who respond to constructive criticism with a willingness to learn.

Synset Examples

constructive criticism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destructive - causing harm or damage rather than improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'constructus' (built), past participle of 'construere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01823581-s
Synset POS: s
