shearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪrər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut the wool off sheep or other animals
person who shears sheep

Examples

The shearer moved quickly and efficiently through the flock, expertly removing the wool.
Each spring, a skilled shearer is hired to help with the sheep shearing on the farm.
After the competition, the shearer was praised for both speed and precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who tends and rears sheep

Etymology

From Middle English 'sherer', from Old English 'scēara', related to 'shear'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪrər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut leather, metal, or textiles using shears
person who cuts with shears

Examples

The shearer skillfully trimmed the rough edges from the leather hide.
Each shearer in the workshop is responsible for a specific type of textile.
Experienced shearers are in high demand during periods of peak production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutter - a person or device that cuts materials

Etymology

From Middle English sheren (to shear) + -er (agent noun suffix).
