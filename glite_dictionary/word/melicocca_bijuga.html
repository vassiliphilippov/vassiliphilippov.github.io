Melicocca bijuga

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈkɑkə baɪˈjuːɡə / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈkɒkə bɪˈjuːɡə / (BrE)
a tropical American tree bearing small edible fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp; also known as the Spanish lime or mamoncillo
tropical tree with edible fruit (Spanish lime)

Examples

The Melicocca bijuga thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its delicious fruit.
Children enjoyed picking the sweet, juicy fruit of the Melicocca bijuga tree after school.
The market had a selection of ripe Melicocca bijuga alongside other tropical fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish lime - another common name for Melicocca bijuga
  • Synonym: mamoncillo - another common name for Melicocca bijuga

Etymology

From Latinized form of a South American indigenous name; 'Melicocca' from Greek 'meli' (honey) and 'kokkos' (berry), referring to the sweet fruit.
