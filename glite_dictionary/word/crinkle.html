crinkle

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to form small wrinkles or creases on a surface, or to cause something to become wrinkled or creased
form small wrinkles or creases

Examples

She likes to crinkle the edges of her drawings for effect.
He watched the old man’s eyes crinkle as he smiled.
Don’t crinkle the paper; I need it smooth for the presentation.

Synset Examples

The dress got wrinkled
crease the paper like this to make a crane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make or become creased or folded
  • Antonym: smooth - to make even or remove wrinkles

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'crenkelen', frequentative of 'crenken' (to twist, bend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01280991-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a small fold, wrinkle, or crease, especially in a surface such as skin or fabric.
slight fold or wrinkle in a surface

Examples

The old photograph showed a deep crinkle in the corner where it had been folded.
She laughed, creating a crinkle at the edge of her eyes.
After sitting in his pocket, the letter had developed a noticeable crinkle.

Synset Examples

his face has many lines
ironing gets rid of most wrinkles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold in something, especially skin or fabric
  • Synonym: crease - a line made by folding or pressing something

Etymology

From Middle English crenkelen, frequentative of crinken (to bend, turn), related to 'crank'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13928798-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to become wrinkled, crumpled, or creased, especially in a way that forms small lines or folds
to become wrinkled or creased

Examples

If you wash this shirt in hot water, it may crinkle.
Her eyes crinkled at the corners when she smiled.
The paper crinkled noisily as he folded it.

Synset Examples

This fabric won't wrinkle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crumple - to become crushed so as to form creases or wrinkles
  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make or become creased or folded

Etymology

From Middle English crinkelen, diminutive of crinken (to bend), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00565934-v
Synset POS: v
