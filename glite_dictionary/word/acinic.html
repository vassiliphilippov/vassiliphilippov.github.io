acinic

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪnɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɪnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling an acinus, one of the small saclike cavities of a compound gland.
relating to a small glandular sac (acinus)
medical

Examples

The tumor was identified as an acinic cell carcinoma due to its structure.
Under the microscope, the gland showed typical acinic arrangements.
Certain salivary glands consist mainly of acinic cells that secrete digestive enzymes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acinous - pertaining to or resembling acini (saclike cavities in glands)

Etymology

From Latin 'acinus' (berry, grape; later a small sac in a gland) + -ic (forming adjectives).
