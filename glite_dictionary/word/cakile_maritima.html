Cakile maritima

1. NOUN

/ kəˌkaɪli məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / kəˌkaɪli məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
A salt-tolerant annual plant found on seashores, grown for its fragrant rose or violet flowers and fleshy grey-green foliage.
a coastal flowering plant species

Examples

Cakile maritima often grows on sandy beaches along the Atlantic coastline.
Botanists study Cakile maritima for its remarkable salt tolerance.
The fragrant flowers of Cakile maritima attract pollinators in coastal habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searocket - a common name for Cakile maritima, a coastal plant

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Cakile' and species 'maritima' meaning 'of the sea'.
