chorioallantois

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔri.oʊ.əˈlæn.toʊ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.ri.əʊ.əˈlæn.təʊ.ɪs / (BrE)
the highly vascular fetal membrane formed by the fusion of the chorion and the adjacent wall of the allantois, important in embryonic development of birds and some reptiles
vascular fetal membrane composed of chorion and allantois
medical

Examples

The chorioallantois plays a crucial role in gas exchange for a developing chick embryo.
Scientists used the chorioallantois to study blood vessel growth in fertilized eggs.
Damage to the chorioallantois can significantly affect embryonic development in birds.

Etymology

From Greek 'chorion' (membrane enclosing the fetus) and 'allantois' (sausage-shaped, from 'allant-', sausage).
