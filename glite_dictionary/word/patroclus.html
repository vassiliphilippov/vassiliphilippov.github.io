Patroclus

1. PROPN

/ pəˈtroʊkləs / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊkləs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a close friend and companion of Achilles, whose death in the Trojan War prompted Achilles to rejoin the battle.
friend of Achilles in Greek mythology

Examples

The death of Patroclus moved Achilles to return to the battlefield.
Homer describes Patroclus as a loyal and courageous friend to Achilles.
In many adaptations of the Iliad, the relationship between Achilles and Patroclus is a central theme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Achilles - a legendary Greek hero, central character in the Iliad

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πάτροκλος (Pátroklos).
