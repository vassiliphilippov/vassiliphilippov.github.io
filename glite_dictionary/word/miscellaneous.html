miscellaneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsəˈleɪniəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈleɪnɪəs / (BrE)
consisting of a mixture of various kinds; made up of diverse or different items, often without order
mixed or various kinds

Examples

The box was filled with miscellaneous tools and spare parts.
She sorted her miscellaneous papers into neat folders.
The shop sells a wide variety of miscellaneous goods.

Synset Examples

an arrangement of assorted spring flowers
assorted sizes
miscellaneous accessories
a mixed program of baroque and contemporary music
a motley crew
sundry sciences commonly known as social

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assorted - of various types or kinds, mixed
  • Synonym: motley - exhibiting great diversity; mixed

Etymology

From Latin 'miscellāneus' (mixed), from 'miscēre' (to mix).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsəˈleɪniəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈleɪnɪəs / (BrE)
consisting of a mixture of different kinds, often with various qualities or aspects; of various types or from different sources.
of various kinds; mixed assortment

Examples

The charity received a miscellaneous collection of donations, including books, toys, and kitchenware.
Her desk drawer was filled with miscellaneous items, from old keys to broken pens and spare change.
They discussed a few miscellaneous topics before moving on to the main agenda.

Synset Examples

a many-sided subject
a multifaceted undertaking
multifarious interests
the multifarious noise of a great city
a miscellaneous crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varied - having different qualities or forms; not uniform
  • Synonym: assorted - of various different sorts
  • Antonym: homogeneous - of the same kind; alike

Etymology

From Latin miscellāneus (mixed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02516967-s
Synset POS: s
