Gaylussacia

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪ.lʌˈseɪ.ʃə / (AmE), / ɡeɪ.lʌˈseɪ.sɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs native to North America, known as the black huckleberries.
genus of shrubs (black huckleberries)

Examples

The Gaylussacia shrubs produce clusters of small, dark berries in late summer.
Several species of Gaylussacia are found in the sandy soils of eastern North America.
Botanists studied Gaylussacia for its ecological role and its edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - Edible berry from American shrubs of the genera Gaylussacia and Vaccinium

Etymology

Named after the French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac (1778–1850).
