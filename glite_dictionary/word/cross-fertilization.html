cross-fertilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɔsˌfɜrt̬əlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɒsˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process in which an egg from one individual is fertilized by sperm from a different individual of the same species.
fertilization by gametes from different individuals
medical

Examples

Cross-fertilization in plants increases genetic diversity, leading to stronger and more adaptable offspring.
Some animals depend on cross-fertilization to maintain healthy populations and avoid inbreeding.
The botanist explained how cross-fertilization happens naturally when insects carry pollen between flowers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-fertilization - fertilization by union of gametes from the same individual

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' + 'fertilization', referring to mixing between individuals.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɔsˌfɜrt̬əlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɒsˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the exchange of ideas, influences, or methods between different subjects, groups, or cultures that results in productive and beneficial outcomes
productive exchange between different areas or cultures

Examples

The conference encouraged cross-fertilization between scientists and artists from around the world.
Many of the company's breakthroughs resulted from cross-fertilization between different departments and teams.
The university values cross-fertilization of ideas among students from diverse backgrounds.

Synset Examples

the cross-fertilization of science and the creative arts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce or create something
  • Antonym: isolation - the state of being separate from others, with little influence or interaction

Etymology

From the biological term, based on 'cross-' + 'fertilization', originally meaning the fertilization of one plant by pollen from another.
