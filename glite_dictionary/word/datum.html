datum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.təm / (BrE)
an individual piece of factual information obtained through observation, measurement, or research
single fact or data point

Examples

Each datum was carefully recorded in the scientist's notebook.
This datum supports the hypothesis that temperature affects plant growth.
One datum alone is insufficient to draw a reliable conclusion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: data - facts or information, especially when examined and used to find out things or to make decisions

Etymology

From Latin 'datum' (something given), neuter past participle of 'dare' (to give).
