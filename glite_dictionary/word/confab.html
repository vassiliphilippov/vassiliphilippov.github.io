confab

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌfæb / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fæb / (BrE)
an informal conversation or discussion, often private or casual in nature
informal conversation

Examples

They had a brief confab after the meeting to discuss their next steps.
Sarah called Mark over for a quick confab by the coffee machine.
The two managers had a private confab before making their announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - an informal talk or conversation
  • Synonym: conversation - a talk between two or more people

Etymology

Shortened from 'confabulation,' from Latin 'confabulari' (to talk together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07149558-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.fæb / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fæb / (BrE)
to talk informally or have a casual conversation, especially about unimportant matters
talk informally; have a casual conversation
informal

Examples

The two colleagues would often confab by the water cooler about the latest office news.
After the meeting, we stayed to confab about our weekend plans.
While waiting for the bus, they started to confab about nothing in particular.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: converse - to engage in conversation

Etymology

Shortened from 'confabulate', from Latin 'confabulari' (to talk together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01040538-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈfæb / (AmE), / kənˈfab / (BrE)
to meet and discuss something in order to exchange ideas or make a decision
to discuss or confer together

Examples

The project leaders decided to confab before making any final decisions.
Let's confab over lunch about the next steps for the proposal.
We confabbed in the hallway to make sure everyone agreed on the plan.

Synset Examples

We conferred about a plan of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to discuss something with others before making a decision
  • Synonym: to consult - to seek advice or information from someone

Etymology

Shortened from ‘confabulate’, from Latin 'con-' (together) + 'fabulari' (to talk, chatter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00878196-v
Synset POS: v
