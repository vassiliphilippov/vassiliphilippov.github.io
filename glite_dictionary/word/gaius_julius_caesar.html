Gaius Julius Caesar

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈdʒuː.li.əs ˈsiː.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈdʒuː.li.əs ˈsiː.zə / (BrE)
(100–44 BC) Roman general, statesman, and dictator who played a critical role in the transformation of the Roman Republic into the Roman Empire.
Roman general and dictator (100–44 BC)

Examples

Many historians consider Gaius Julius Caesar one of the most influential leaders in world history.
The assassination of Gaius Julius Caesar marked a turning point in the history of Rome.
Shakespeare's famous play dramatizes the last days of Gaius Julius Caesar.
