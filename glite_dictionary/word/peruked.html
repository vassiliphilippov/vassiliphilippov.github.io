peruked

1. ADJ

/ pəˈruːkt / (AmE), / pəˈruːkt / (BrE)
Wearing a wig, especially a peruke popular among men in the 17th and 18th centuries.
wearing a historical wig
archaic

Examples

The judge, peruked and robed, entered the courtroom in silence.
In the portrait, the nobleman is peruked as was the fashion of his time.
A group of peruked gentlemen gathered to discuss politics in the candlelit hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wigged - wearing a wig

Etymology

From 'peruke,' itself from French 'perruque' (wig).
