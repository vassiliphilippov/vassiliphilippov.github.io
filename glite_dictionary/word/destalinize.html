to destalinize

1. VERB

/ diˈstɑlɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈstælɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to reverse, counteract, or eliminate the effects, policies, or practices associated with Stalinism, especially in government or political systems
reverse Stalinist policies

Examples

After the dictator's death, the new government began to destalinize its institutions and leadership.
It took decades for the country to fully destalinize its legal and educational systems.
Many former Soviet states attempted to destalinize their public spaces by removing monuments and renaming streets.

Synset Examples

Russia was slowly destalinized in the late 1950's

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to stalinize - to bring under the influence or policies of Stalinism

Etymology

Formed from de- (removal, reversal) + Stalin (surname of Joseph Stalin) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
