lend-lease

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛndˌlis / (AmE), / ˈlɛndˌliːs / (BrE)
the provision of goods and services by one country to an ally, especially during wartime, to assist in a shared cause, typically without immediate payment
wartime transfer of goods and services to allies

Examples

The United States implemented lend-lease to support its allies during World War II.
Under the lend-lease program, military equipment was sent overseas to nations fighting against the Axis powers.
Historians credit lend-lease as a turning point in the Allied war effort.

Synset Examples

lend-lease during World War II was extremely generous

Etymology

Coined in the United States during World War II from the verbs 'lend' and 'lease', describing the U.S. policy of supplying allies.
