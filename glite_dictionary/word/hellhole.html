hellhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈhɛlˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a place that is extremely unpleasant, dirty, or dangerous
extremely unpleasant or horrible place

Examples

The abandoned warehouse was such a hellhole that even rats wouldn't stay there.
She described her old apartment as a hellhole with leaking pipes and constant noise.
After spending a week in that hellhole of a prison, he was desperate for freedom.

Synset Examples

the hell of battle
the inferno of the engine room
when you're alone Christmas is the pits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump - an unpleasant or messy place
  • Antonym: paradise - a place or state of great happiness or delight

Etymology

From 'hell' + 'hole', suggesting a place as dreadful as hell.
