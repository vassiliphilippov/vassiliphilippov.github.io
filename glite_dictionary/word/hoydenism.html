hoydenism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔɪdənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhɔɪdənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
boisterous or tomboyish behavior in girls or young women, characterized by roughness or lack of traditional femininity
boisterousness in girls or young women

Examples

Her hoydenism often got her into trouble at the strict finishing school.
The spirited hoydenism of the heroine contrasted with the reserved manners of her peers.
Victorian novels sometimes criticized young women's hoydenism as a threat to proper decorum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomboyishness - behavior of a girl who acts in a boisterous or boyish way

Etymology

From 'hoyden' (a boisterous girl), probably of Dutch origin, plus '-ism'.
