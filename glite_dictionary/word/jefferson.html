Jefferson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛf.ər.sən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛf.əs.ən / (BrE)
Thomas Jefferson (1743–1826), the 3rd President of the United States, principal author of the Declaration of Independence, responsible for the Louisiana Purchase, and initiator of the Lewis and Clark Expedition.
3rd President of the United States

Examples

Jefferson drafted the Declaration of Independence in 1776, setting a precedent for American democracy.
The Louisiana Purchase was negotiated under President Jefferson's administration, doubling the size of the United States.
Many historians consider Jefferson to be one of the most influential founding fathers.
