Arabis Canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɹ.ə.bɪs ˌkæn.əˈdɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæɹ.ə.bɪs ˌkan.əˈdɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of North American rock cress known for its very long, curved seed pods.
plant species; North American rock cress

Examples

The wildflower guide identifies Arabis Canadensis by its distinctive curved seed pods.
Botanists found a cluster of Arabis Canadensis growing along the riverbank in early spring.
Gardeners often confuse Arabis Canadensis with other types of rock cress due to its similar appearance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Arabis' is the genus name, 'canadensis' means 'of Canada' in Latin.
