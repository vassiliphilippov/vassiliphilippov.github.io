PTSD

1. NOUN

/ ˌpi ti ɛs ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌpiː tiː ɛs ˈdiː / (BrE)
An anxiety disorder caused by exposure to traumatic events, resulting in symptoms such as reliving the trauma, emotional numbness, and survivor guilt.
anxiety disorder after trauma
medical

Examples

Many veterans suffer from PTSD years after returning from combat.
She began therapy to help manage her PTSD symptoms.
Awareness about PTSD has increased in recent decades, helping more people seek treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post-traumatic stress disorder - Anxiety disorder following exposure to traumatic events

Etymology

Acronym for 'post-traumatic stress disorder.'
