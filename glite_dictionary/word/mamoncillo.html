mamoncillo

1. NOUN

/ məˌmɔnˈsiːjoʊ / (AmE), / mamɒnˈsiːjəʊ / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Melicoccus bijugatus) bearing small edible fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp.
tropical American fruit tree

Examples

The mamoncillo is popular at Caribbean markets during the summer months.
Children enjoyed snacking on the sweet flesh of the mamoncillo fruit after school.
You can find mamoncillo trees growing along many rural roadsides in Puerto Rico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quenepa - another name for mamoncillo, used in the Caribbean

Etymology

From Spanish 'mamoncillo', diminutive of 'mamón', likely referring to the way the fruit is eaten by sucking the pulp.
