Penstemon serrulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəmɒn səˈrʌlətəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstəmən sɛˈruːleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest, with whorls of deep blue to dark purple flowers growing at the tips of erect leafy stems, typically found in moist locations.
a Pacific Northwest flowering plant species

Examples

Penstemon serrulatus is commonly found in moist areas along the Pacific Northwest coast.
Gardeners value Penstemon serrulatus for its vibrant blue flowers and hardy nature.
Hiking trails near Seattle are dotted with blooms of Penstemon serrulatus each spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Penstemon', a genus name possibly from Greek 'pente' (five) + 'stemon' (stamen); 'serrulatus' from Latin 'serrula' meaning 'little saw', referring to the finely toothed leaf edges.
