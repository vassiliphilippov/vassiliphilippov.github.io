Hydrocharis morsus-ranae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈkærɪs ˌmɔrsəsˈræni / (AmE), / ˌhɪdrəʊˈkarɪs ˌmɔːsəsˈrɑːniː / (BrE)
A small European floating aquatic plant with rounded, heart-shaped leaves and small white flowers, commonly known as frogbit.
European floating aquatic plant (frogbit)

Examples

Many ponds in Europe are covered by Hydrocharis morsus-ranae during the summer months.
The floating leaves of Hydrocharis morsus-ranae provide habitat for small insects and fish.
Gardeners sometimes introduce Hydrocharis morsus-ranae to water gardens for its attractive white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogbit - Common English name for Hydrocharis morsus-ranae, a floating aquatic plant

Etymology

From Greek 'hydrocharis' (water joy) and Latin 'morsus ranae' (frog's bite), referring to the plant's habitat and appearance.
