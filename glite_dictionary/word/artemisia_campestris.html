Artemisia campestris

1. NOUN

/ ɑrtɪˈmɪziə kæmˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈmɪzɪə kæmˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of wormwood native to Europe and Asia, similar to common wormwood, with aromatic leaves and sometimes used in herbal medicine.
a species of European wormwood plant

Examples

Artemisia campestris thrives in dry, sandy soils across much of Europe and Asia.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of Artemisia campestris for its potential use in herbal remedies.
The leaves of Artemisia campestris are aromatic and have a slightly bitter taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - an aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia, with a bitter taste

Etymology

From Latin: 'Artemisia' (the genus, named after the Greek goddess Artemis) and 'campestris' (meaning 'of the fields').
