slowdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsləʊˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a period or process in which activity or progress becomes slower than usual
a decrease in speed or activity

Examples

Recent months have seen a significant slowdown in economic growth.
The company blamed the production slowdown on supply chain issues.
After a busy morning, there was a noticeable slowdown in customer orders during the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recession - a period of economic decline
  • Antonym: acceleration - the act of increasing speed or rate
