Gerris lacustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛr.ɪs ləˈkʌs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛr.ɪs ləˈkʌs.trɪs / (BrE)
a species of water strider commonly found on freshwater surfaces in Europe and Asia
species of water strider

Examples

Gerris lacustris can often be seen skating across the surfaces of ponds in early summer.
Scientists studied the movement of Gerris lacustris to understand how insects adapt to aquatic environments.
The pond was teeming with Gerris lacustris during the warm months, moving quickly over the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - an insect that moves across water surfaces

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gerris' (genus of water striders) + 'lacustris' (of lakes).
