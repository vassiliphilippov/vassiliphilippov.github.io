coulisse

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈliːs / (AmE), / kuːˈliːs / (BrE)
A flat piece of scenery set in the wings of a theater stage, often used to mask backstage areas or create the illusion of depth.
theater scenery flat in stage wings

Examples

The crew carefully positioned the coulisse to hide the performers waiting in the wings.
A painted coulisse created the illusion of a distant forest along the stage edge.
Backstage, the coulisses were arranged to ensure smooth scene changes throughout the play.

Etymology

From French 'coulisse' (groove, track; side scenery), from 'couler' (to flow, slide).

2. NOUN

/ kuˈlis / (AmE), / kuˈliːs / (BrE)
A groove or timber fitted with a channel to allow a panel or partition to slide.
groove for sliding panel

Examples

The carpenter fitted a coulisse to allow the door to slide smoothly.
Panels were inserted into the coulisse as part of the stage construction.
The antique cabinet features a hidden coulisse for a sliding drawer.

Etymology

From French 'coulisse', meaning 'sliding groove' or 'slot'.
