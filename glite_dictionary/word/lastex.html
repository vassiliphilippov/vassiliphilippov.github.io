Lastex

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæsˌtɛks / (AmE), / ˈlas.tɛks / (BrE)
a type of yarn with an elastic core wound with threads of cotton, silk, nylon, or rayon, used especially in stretchable fabrics
elastic yarn with a wrapped thread covering

Examples

The swimsuit is made of Lastex to provide extra stretch and comfort.
Manufacturers began using Lastex in hosiery for a better fit.
She chose a dress with Lastex panels for flexibility.

Etymology

Trade name coined in the 1930s from 'elastic' and 'tex(tile)'.
