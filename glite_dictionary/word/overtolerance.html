overtolerance

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈtɑːlərəns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɒlərəns / (BrE)
excessive tolerance, especially when it results in too much permissiveness or indulgence
excessive tolerance or permissiveness

Examples

The school's overtolerance of disruptive behavior led to a decline in academic standards.
Some argue that overtolerance in public policy can undermine social cohesion.
Critics accused the committee of overtolerance toward unethical practices in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tolerance - willingness to accept or permit something, especially differences or behaviors
  • Antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'tolerance' (from Latin tolerantia).
