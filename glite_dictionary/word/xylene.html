xylene

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪliːn / (AmE), / ˈzaɪliːn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable, volatile liquid hydrocarbon used as a solvent, especially in the chemical and printing industries
volatile flammable solvent hydrocarbon

Examples

Laboratory technicians often use xylene to clean microscope slides.
Spills of xylene should be handled with care due to its toxicity and flammability.
The paint thinner contains a mixture of xylene and other solvents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toluene - a similar mono-methyl aromatic hydrocarbon solvent

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood), as it was first discovered in wood tar.
