Hypericum maculatum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɪrɪkəm ˌmakjəˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A European perennial herb, commonly known as St John's wort, found from Ireland and France to western Siberia.
European perennial St John's wort plant

Examples

Hypericum maculatum is often used in traditional remedies across Europe.
Botanists documented the spread of Hypericum maculatum from Ireland to Siberia.
The meadow was dotted with the bright yellow flowers of Hypericum maculatum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - common name for various plants in the genus Hypericum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Hypericum' and species 'maculatum', meaning 'spotted'.
