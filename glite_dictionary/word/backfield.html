backfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbakˌfiːld / (BrE)
the group of offensive football players who line up behind the linemen, typically including the quarterback and running backs
offensive football players behind linemen

Examples

The team's backfield worked well together, leading to a successful running game.
Injuries in the backfield forced the coach to change his strategy.
The quarterback handed the ball off to a member of the backfield for a touchdown.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: back - the area or position toward the rear
  • Degree Higher: line - a position at the front in football, especially linemen

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'field', referring to the area behind the line of scrimmage in football.
