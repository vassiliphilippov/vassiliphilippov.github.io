perversity

1. NOUN

/ pərˈvɝːsəti / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
the quality of deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable or morally wrong
deliberate unreasonable or immoral behavior

Examples

Her perversity shocked everyone at the meeting.
He seemed driven by a kind of perversity that delighted in breaking the rules.
Despite knowing the right thing to do, her perversity led her to act otherwise.

Synset Examples

there will always be a few people who, through macho perversity, gain satisfaction from bullying and terrorism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perversion - an act or instance of perverting; corruption or distortion

Etymology

From Old French *perversité*, from Latin *perversitas* (turning the wrong way).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈvɜrsəti / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
deliberate and stubborn resistance to guidance, discipline, or accepted standards of behavior.
stubborn resistance to guidance or discipline

Examples

Her perversity made every attempt to help her feel like an uphill battle.
Sometimes, children show perversity simply to test boundaries set by adults.
His perversity in ignoring good advice eventually led to his downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrariness - opposite or stubborn opposition
  • Synonym: obstinacy - stubbornness or refusal to change behavior
  • Antonym: compliance - willingness to follow guidance or rules

Etymology

From Old French *perversité*, from Latin *perversitas* (turned the wrong way).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04916195-n
Synset POS: n
