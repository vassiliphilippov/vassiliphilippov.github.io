Tulipa

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈliːpə / (AmE), / tjuːˈliːpə / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian perennial bulbous flowering plants known as tulips, widely cultivated for their bright, showy flowers.
genus of flowering plants: tulips

Examples

The gardens were filled with beautiful Tulipa species in full bloom.
Several varieties of Tulipa originate from Central Asia and Eastern Europe.
Botanists study the classification and genetics of Tulipa to improve cultivated tulips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip - a flower of the genus Tulipa

Etymology

From Latin Tulipa, from Turkish tülbent (turban), referring to the flower's shape.

2. NOUN

/ tuˈliːpə / (AmE), / tjuːˈliːpə / (BrE)
a genus of Eurasian perennial bulbous herbs widely known for their showy, brightly colored flowers; includes species commonly called tulips
a genus of flowering plants (tulips)

Examples

The genus Tulipa comprises about 75 species native to Eurasia and North Africa.
Many garden varieties have been developed from Tulipa species for their vibrant flowers.
Botanists classify all true tulips in the plant genus Tulipa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip - a spring-flowering plant of the genus Tulipa, with brightly colored, cup-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'tulipa', which is derived from Turkish 'tülbend' (turban), referring to the flower's shape.
