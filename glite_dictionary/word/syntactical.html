syntactical

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈtæktɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪnˈtaktɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or conforming to the rules of syntax in a language.
relating to syntax

Examples

The professor explained the syntactical structures found in ancient Greek.
Errors in syntactical agreement can make sentences difficult to understand.
Her analysis focused on the syntactical differences between spoken and written language.

Synset Examples

the syntactic rules of a language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syntactic - relating to syntax; alternative form

Etymology

From Greek suntaktikos (putting together, arranging), from suntaxis (arrangement).
