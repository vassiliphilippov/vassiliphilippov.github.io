demerit

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
A fault or disadvantage; the quality of being inadequate or falling short of perfection.
a fault or disadvantage; weakness

Examples

In her evaluation, she listed both the merits and demerits of the proposal.
Every plan has its own set of demerits, no matter how carefully it is crafted.
He was aware of the demerit in his argument but hoped it would be overlooked.

Synset Examples

they discussed the merits and demerits of her novel
he knew his own faults much better than she did

Related Terms

  • Antonym: merit - a good quality or feature that deserves praise; excellence
  • Synonym: fault - a mistake, flaw, or disadvantage

Etymology

From Latin demeritum (fault, offense), from de- (away, down) + merere (to deserve).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
a mark recorded against a person, especially in a school or the armed forces, for a fault or misconduct
mark for misconduct or failure

Examples

If a student receives three demerits in one week, she must attend detention on Friday.
The soldier's repeated demerits eventually led to his suspension from duty.
He was careful to follow the rules to avoid getting another demerit on his record.

Synset Examples

ten demerits and he loses his privileges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: merit - a mark given for achievement or a good action

Etymology

From Latin demeritum (fault, offense), from demereri (to merit fully, do (good or ill) to someone).
