immobility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmoʊˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪməʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of not moving or being unable to move
state of not moving

Examples

The patient's immobility led to muscle weakness over time.
Immobility during long flights can increase the risk of blood clots.
The statue's sense of immobility gave it a quiet, solemn presence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobility - the ability to move or be moved easily

Etymology

From Latin 'immobilitas', from 'immobilis' (not movable).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of not moving or being unable to move; lack of motion or movement.
lack of movement; inability to move

Examples

The doctor was concerned about the patient's prolonged immobility after surgery.
Snow and ice caused the complete immobility of vehicles on the mountain roads.
His immobility during meditation helped him concentrate deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionlessness - the state of being without motion
  • Synonym: immobility - inability to move
  • Antonym: motion - the act of moving

Etymology

From French immobilité or Latin immobilitas, from immobilis (not movable).
