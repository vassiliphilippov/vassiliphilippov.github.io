Bettongia

1. NOUN

/ bɛˈtɒndʒiə / (AmE), / bɛˈtɒndʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal marsupials, known as bettongs, native to Australia and related to kangaroos.
genus of small Australian marsupials

Examples

The genus Bettongia includes several endangered marsupial species native to Australia.
Members of Bettongia are sometimes called rat-kangaroos due to their size and appearance.
Conservation programs have been established to protect various Bettongia populations from extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettong - a small, nocturnal Australian marsupial

Etymology

From Latinized form of 'bettong,' an Australian Aboriginal term, plus taxonomic '-ia.'

2. NOUN

/ bɛˈtɒndʒiə / (AmE), / bɛˈtɒndʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupial mammals known as bettongs or rat-kangaroos, native to Australia.
genus of small Australian marsupials

Examples

The genus Bettongia includes several endangered species native to Australia.
Researchers are working to reintroduce Bettongia species into their natural habitats.
Members of the genus Bettongia are sometimes called rat-kangaroos due to their appearance.

Etymology

Named from Latinized form based on the Australian Aboriginal word 'bettong'.
