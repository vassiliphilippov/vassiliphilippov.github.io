clotheshorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊðzˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðzˌhɔːs / (BrE)
a frame on which wet clothes are hung to dry indoors
frame for drying clothes indoors

Examples

She placed the wet towels on the clotheshorse in the laundry room.
During winter, they dry their clothes on a clotheshorse rather than outside.
The clotheshorse in the corner was filled with freshly washed shirts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drying rack - a rack used for drying clothes, especially indoors

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊðzˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðzˌhɔːs / (BrE)
(informal) A man who pays a great deal of attention to his clothes and appearance.
man obsessed with clothes and appearance
informal

Examples

James is a real clotheshorse; he spends hours picking out his outfit every morning.
The party was full of clotheshorses, each trying to outdo the other with designer labels.
Being a clotheshorse can be expensive, but some people love keeping up with the latest trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man unduly devoted to style, neatness, and fashion in dress and appearance
  • Synonym: fop - a man excessively concerned with his clothes and appearance
