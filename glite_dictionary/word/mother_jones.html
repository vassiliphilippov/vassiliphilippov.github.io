Mother Jones

1. PROPN

/ ˌmʌðər ˈdʒoʊnz / (AmE), / ˌmʌðə ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
(1830–1930) An Irish-born American labor leader who co-founded the Industrial Workers of the World and advocated for workers' rights.
Irish-born American labor leader (1830–1930)

Examples

Mother Jones played a central role in the American labor movement at the turn of the 20th century.
The speeches of Mother Jones inspired thousands of workers to demand better conditions.
Long after her death, Mother Jones remains a symbol of resilience in the fight for social justice.

Etymology

Nickname of Mary Harris Jones, known as 'Mother Jones' for her maternal role in labor organizing.
