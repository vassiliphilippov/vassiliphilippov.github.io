archway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃˌweɪ / (BrE)
a passageway with a curved, arch-like structure above it, typically made of masonry or stone
passage under an arch

Examples

The old castle's entrance was marked by a stone archway covered in ivy.
Sunlight streamed through the archway into the quiet courtyard beyond.
Visitors walked under the ornate archway to enter the city's historic district.

Synset Examples

they built a triumphal arch to memorialize their victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure supporting the weight above it

Etymology

From 'arch' + 'way'; 'arch' from Latin 'arcus' (bow).
