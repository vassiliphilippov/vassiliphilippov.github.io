eukaryotic

1. ADJ

/ juˌker.iˈɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / juːˌkæː.riˈɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having cells with membrane-bound nuclei and other organelles.
having cells with membrane-bound nuclei
medical

Examples

Fungi, plants, and animals are all considered eukaryotic organisms.
Unlike bacteria, eukaryotic cells contain a distinct nucleus surrounded by a membrane.
The complex structure of eukaryotic cells allows them to perform specialized functions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prokaryotic - lacking a membrane-bound nucleus (of cells/organisms)

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, well) + 'karyon' (nut, kernel, nucleus) + '-otic' (relating to).
