opera

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.pɚ.ə / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ə / (BrE)
A theater or building where operatic performances are given.
theater for opera performances

Examples

The city's new opera attracts visitors from all over the world.
Plans are underway to renovate the historic opera downtown.
We bought tickets for the opening night at the opera.

Etymology

From Italian 'opera' (work, composition), from Latin 'opera' (work, effort).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.pə.rə / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ə / (BrE)
a dramatic work in which the actors sing most or all of their parts, usually with orchestral accompaniment
dramatic music performance with singing and orchestra

Examples

She attended an opera at the famous La Scala theater in Milan.
The company is performing a new opera by a contemporary composer next season.
He enjoys listening to opera because of its powerful emotional expression and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musical - a play or film whose story is told with songs and often dancing

Etymology

From Italian 'opera', meaning 'work', from Latin 'opera'.
