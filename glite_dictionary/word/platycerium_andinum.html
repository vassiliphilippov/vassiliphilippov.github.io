Platycerium andinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm ænˈdiːnəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm ænˈdiːnəm / (BrE)
A species of staghorn fern native to Peru and Bolivia, known for its distinctive, antler-like fronds.
species of staghorn fern of Peru and Bolivia

Examples

The botanical garden recently acquired a Platycerium andinum to add to its collection of exotic ferns.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Platycerium andinum in its native habitats in Peru and Bolivia.
Collectors prize Platycerium andinum for its unique frond shape and tropical origins.

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) + 'keros' (horn), referring to the wide, horn-like fronds; species name from Latinized 'Andinum', meaning 'of the Andes'.
