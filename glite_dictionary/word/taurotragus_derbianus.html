Taurotragus derbianus

1. NOUN

/ tɔroʊˈtræɡəs dɜrˈbaɪ.ən.əs / (AmE), / tɔːrəˈtræɡəs dɜːˈbɪən.əs / (BrE)
A large, dark-striped species of eland native to western equatorial Africa.
a large African antelope species

Examples

The Taurotragus derbianus is renowned for its impressive spiral horns and striking stripes.
Conservationists are working to protect the dwindling population of Taurotragus derbianus in its natural habitat.
Unlike other antelopes, the Taurotragus derbianus prefers densely forested areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eland - a large African antelope

Etymology

From Greek 'tauros' (bull) + 'tragos' (goat), and the specific epithet after the 13th Earl of Derby, who supported African exploration.
