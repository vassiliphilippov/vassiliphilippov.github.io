km

1. NOUN

/ kiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / keɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to 1,000 meters (or approximately 0.62 miles)
metric unit of length; 1,000 meters

Examples

The nearest town is five km away from here.
She ran 10 km in the charity race last weekend.
The mountain peak stands at an elevation of 2,000 km above sea level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilometer - metric unit of length equal to 1,000 meters
  • Antonym: mile - Imperial unit of length equal to 1,609.34 meters

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilometer', from French 'kilomètre', from Greek 'khilioi' (thousand) + 'metron' (measure).
