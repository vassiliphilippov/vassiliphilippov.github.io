Lithophragma parviflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθəˈfræɡmə ˌpɑrvəˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəˈfræɡmə ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant with mainly basal leaves and slender, open racemes of white or pale pink flowers, native to prairies and open forests of northwestern United States, British Columbia, and Alberta.
a flowering plant native to northwest North America

Examples

Lithophragma parviflorum is often found blooming in the spring across open prairies in Washington.
The delicate white flowers of Lithophragma parviflorum stood out against the green undergrowth.
Botanists in British Columbia monitor populations of Lithophragma parviflorum due to its ecological significance.

Etymology

From Greek 'lithos' (stone) + 'phragma' (fence) and Latin 'parviflorum' (small-flowered).
