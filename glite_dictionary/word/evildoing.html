evildoing

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːvəlˌduːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvəlˌduːɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of doing evil, especially a violation of moral or legal principles.
the act of doing evil

Examples

The community condemned the evildoing that had taken place in their midst.
His evildoing eventually caught up with him and brought shame to his family.
She refused to be a part of any evildoing despite the pressure from her peers.

Synset Examples

the boy was punished for the transgressions of his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the state or quality of being wicked
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

Formed from 'evil' + 'doing'.
