Walloon

1. NOUN

/ wəˈlun / (AmE), / wəˈluːn / (BrE)
a member of a French-speaking ethnic group living mainly in southern and eastern Belgium and neighboring regions of France
a French-speaking Belgian ethnic group

Examples

The Walloons have a rich cultural heritage distinct from other regions of Belgium.
Many Walloons live in the Wallonia region and speak a dialect of French.
The history of the Walloons is closely linked to the development of the Belgian state.

Etymology

From French Wallon, from Old French *walon* (of Celtic origin, meaning 'foreigner').

2. NOUN

/ wəˈlun / (AmE), / wəˈluːn / (BrE)
a dialect of French spoken mainly in southern Belgium and in parts of northern France
Romance dialect of Belgium and France

Examples

Many people in the region still speak Walloon at home with their families.
Some traditional songs are sung in Walloon rather than standard French.
Researchers are documenting the grammar and vocabulary of Walloon before it disappears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken in France and many other countries

Etymology

From French Wallon, from Middle Dutch *Wals* (foreigner, Romanic-speaking person).

3. NOUN

/ wəˈluːn / (AmE), / wəˈluːn / (BrE)
a member of the French-speaking ethnic group primarily living in southern Belgium
French-speaking Belgian ethnic group member

Examples

Many Walloons live in the southern region of Belgium known as Wallonia.
The cultural traditions of the Walloon people are distinct from those of the Flemings.
During the festival, both Walloons and Flemings celebrated together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Fleming - a member of the Dutch-speaking people of northern Belgium

Etymology

From French 'Wallon', from Middle Dutch 'Walsch' (foreign, Romance-speaking).
