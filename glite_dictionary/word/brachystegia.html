Brachystegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækɪˈstiːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈstiːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African timber trees with pale golden heartwood uniformly striped with dark brown or black.
genus of tropical African timber trees

Examples

The dense wood of Brachystegia is prized for its durability in construction.
Forests dominated by Brachystegia species are vital habitats for many African animals.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Brachystegia trees to understand the region's ecosystem.

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) + 'stegē' (covering, roof), referring to parts of the plant structure.
