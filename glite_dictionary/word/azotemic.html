azotemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæz.oʊˈtiː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌaz.əʊˈtiː.mɪk / (BrE)
relating to or having an excess of nitrogenous waste products in the blood, usually as a result of kidney dysfunction
having excess nitrogenous waste in the blood
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as azotemic following a series of blood tests.
Chronic kidney disease commonly leads to an azotemic state if untreated.
Doctors monitored his azotemic condition closely to adjust his treatment.

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen), from Greek 'azotos' (lifeless) + '-emic' (from Greek 'haima', blood).
