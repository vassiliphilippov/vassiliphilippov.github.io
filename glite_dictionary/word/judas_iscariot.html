Judas Iscariot

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒudəs ɪsˈkæriət / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs ɪsˈkæriət / (BrE)
the Apostle who betrayed Jesus Christ to his enemies for thirty pieces of silver, according to the New Testament
the Apostle who betrayed Jesus

Examples

Some consider the act of Judas Iscariot to be the ultimate betrayal in Christian tradition.
In many paintings, Judas Iscariot is shown handing Jesus over to the authorities.
The name Judas Iscariot has become synonymous with treachery and betrayal.

Etymology

From Latin 'Iscariotes', from Greek 'Iskariōth', meaning man of Kerioth (a place in Judaea).
