monazite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnəˌzaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌzaɪt / (BrE)
a reddish-brown mineral containing rare earth elements, and an important source of thorium and cerium
rare earth mineral; source of thorium, cerium

Examples

Geologists found a rich deposit of monazite in the ancient river sands.
Monazite is processed to extract valuable rare earth metals used in electronics.
The reddish-brown color of monazite makes it relatively easy for miners to identify.

Etymology

From German Monazit, from Greek monazein (to be solitary), referring to its occurrence in isolated crystals.
