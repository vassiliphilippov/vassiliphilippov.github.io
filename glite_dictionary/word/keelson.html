keelson

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛl.sən / (AmE), / ˈkiːl.sən / (BrE)
A longitudinal beam fixed above the keel of a ship or boat to reinforce and stiffen the framework.
longitudinal reinforcing beam above the keel

Examples

The keelson was installed to provide additional strength to the vessel's frame.
During restoration, the shipbuilders discovered the original keelson was still intact.
A damaged keelson can compromise the integrity of the entire boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keel - the main structural element running along the bottom of a ship or boat

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'kielson', diminutive of 'kil' (keel).
