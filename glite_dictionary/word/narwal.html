narwal

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑr.wɑl / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.wəl / (BrE)
a small Arctic whale, the male of which has a long, spiral ivory tusk extending from its upper jaw
Arctic whale with a long, spiral tusk

Examples

The narwal is often called the unicorn of the sea because of its long, spiral tusk.
Researchers tracked a pod of narwals through the icy Arctic waters.
Many legends about the narwal circulated among Inuit peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narwhal - Another spelling of 'narwal', an Arctic whale with a spiral tusk

Etymology

From Danish 'narhval', originally from Old Norse 'nár' (corpse) + 'hvalr' (whale), referring to the animal's pale color.
