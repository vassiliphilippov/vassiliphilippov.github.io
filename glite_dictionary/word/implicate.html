to implicate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to involve or imply as a necessary part, accompaniment, or consequence
to involve or imply as necessary

Examples

Any changes to the contract implicate new legal responsibilities for the company.
Increasing production will implicate the need for more resources and staff.
The policy shift could implicate a rise in transportation costs for all clients.

Synset Examples

What does this move entail?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entail - to involve something as a necessary or inevitable part or consequence
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out or not include as a necessary part or result

Etymology

From Latin implicare (to entangle, involve), from in- (in) + plicare (to fold).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪm.plɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.plɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to show or suggest that someone is involved in a crime or wrongful act
to show involvement in a crime

Examples

Several senior officials were implicated in the financial scandal that rocked the company.
The evidence clearly implicates him in the robbery last year.
She refused to answer questions that might implicate her friends.

Synset Examples

He is implicated in the scheme to defraud the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to involve - to include someone or something in a situation or activity

Etymology

From Latin implicatus, past participle of implicare (to entangle, involve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02683142-v
Synset POS: v
