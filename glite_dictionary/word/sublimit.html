sublimit

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
(Insurance) A specific limit on coverage for a particular type of loss within an insurance policy, separate from the overall policy limit.
limit within an insurance policy

Examples

The policy has a sublimit of $5,000 for jewelry losses.
Many insurance contracts contain a sublimit for flood damage.
Always check the sublimit clauses when reviewing your coverage options.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix "sub-" (meaning "under" or "below") and "limit".
