hematopoiesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiː.mə.toʊ.pɔɪˈiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiː.mə.təʊ.pɔɪˈiː.sɪs / (BrE)
the process of formation and development of blood cells in the body, especially in the bone marrow
formation of blood cells in the body
medical

Examples

During childhood, hematopoiesis occurs mainly in the long bones.
Disorders that affect hematopoiesis can lead to anemia or leukemia.
Researchers study hematopoiesis to understand how blood diseases develop.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: erythropoiesis - formation of red blood cells
  • Degree Lower: leukopoiesis - formation of white blood cells

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + poiesis (making, formation).
