Flanders

1. PROPN

/ ˈflændərz / (AmE), / ˈflɑːndəz / (BrE)
a medieval region in northern Europe, covering areas now part of northern France, Belgium, and the southwestern Netherlands
medieval region in northern Europe

Examples

During the Middle Ages, Flanders was renowned for its thriving textile industry.
The armies fought several major battles in Flanders during the war.
Much of modern-day Belgium was once part of medieval Flanders.

Etymology

From Old French *Flandres*, from Dutch *Vlaanderen*.
