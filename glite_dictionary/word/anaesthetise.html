to anaesthetise

1. VERB

/ əˈnɛsθətaɪz / (AmE), / əˈniːsθətaɪz / (BrE)
to administer an anesthetic drug to a person or animal, causing loss of sensation or consciousness, especially before a medical procedure
administer an anesthetic drug
medical british

Examples

The dentist will anaesthetise the area before starting the procedure.
It is important to anaesthetise patients properly to ensure they feel no pain during surgery.
Veterinarians often anaesthetise animals before performing operations.

Synset Examples

The patient must be anesthetized before the operation
anesthetize the gum before extracting the teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anesthetize - to administer an anesthetic drug to (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility), via French and modern Latin.
