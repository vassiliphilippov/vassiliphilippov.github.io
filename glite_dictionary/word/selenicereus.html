Selenicereus

1. NOUN

/ səˌliːnɪˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / sɛˌliːnɪˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic, climbing cacti that bloom at night, native to tropical America.
genus of night-blooming climbing cacti

Examples

The spectacular flowers of the Selenicereus cactus open only for a single night each year.
Gardeners often cultivate Selenicereus species for their impressive, fragrant blooms.
Many Selenicereus varieties are grown as houseplants due to their unique climbing habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epiphyllum - A related genus of epiphytic cacti with flattened stems and night-blooming flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'selene' (moon) and 'cereus' (candle), referring to nocturnal blooming and candle-like stems.

2. NOUN

/ səˌliːnɪˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / sɪˌliːnɪˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic climbing cacti, known for blooming at night.
genus of night-blooming climbing cacti

Examples

The Selenicereus cactus is famous for producing large, fragrant flowers that open only at night.
Gardeners often grow Selenicereus species for their spectacular nocturnal blooms.
A mature Selenicereus can climb trees and walls with its long, trailing stems.

Etymology

From Greek selēnē (moon) + cereus (Latin for waxy or torch, a related cactus genus), referring to its moonlit flowering.
