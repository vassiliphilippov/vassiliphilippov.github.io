Polyangiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˌændʒiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌændʒɪˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of bacteria that are found mostly in soils and on dung
a family of soil- and dung-dwelling bacteria

Examples

The Polyangiaceae family includes bacteria that play an important role in decomposing organic material in soil.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Polyangiaceae in samples taken from forest floor litter.
Members of Polyangiaceae are commonly found on dung, aiding its breakdown and recycling nutrients.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Polyangium' (type genus) and '-aceae' (a standard suffix for family names in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌlæŋdʒiˈeɪsii / (AmE), / pɒˌlæŋdʒiˈeɪsii / (BrE)
A family of bacteria primarily found in soils and on dung, belonging to the order Myxococcales.
family of soil and dung bacteria

Examples

The Polyangiaceae are commonly studied for their complex social behavior among bacteria.
Soil samples revealed a high abundance of Polyangiaceae, suggesting their ecological importance.
Researchers found members of the Polyangiaceae thriving on decomposing organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Polyangium with the standard bacterial family suffix -aceae.
