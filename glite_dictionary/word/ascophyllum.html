Ascophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæs.kəˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / ˌæs.kəˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of brown algae with distinctive compressed or inflated branchlets, commonly found in the North Atlantic and important in marine ecosystems.
genus of brown algae

Examples

Ascophyllum is often harvested for use in fertilizers due to its rich nutrient content.
Scientists are studying Ascophyllum to understand its role in carbon sequestration in coastal ecosystems.
The intertidal zone was covered with thick mats of Ascophyllum and other brown algae.

Etymology

From Greek 'askos' (bag) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the inflated, leaf-like structures.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of brown algae distinguished by compressed or inflated branchlets along the axis, commonly found in northern Atlantic shorelines
genus of brown algae

Examples

Ascophyllum nodosum is often used as a source of seaweed fertilizers for crop production.
Researchers studied the impact of tidal changes on Ascophyllum populations along the rocky coast.
Many birds rely on the shelter provided by dense beds of Ascophyllum during low tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fucus - another genus of brown algae

Etymology

From Greek 'askos' (bag) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the shape of its air bladders.
