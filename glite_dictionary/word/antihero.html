antihero

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tiˌhɪr.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈæn.tiˌhɪə.rəʊ / (BrE)
A main character in a story who lacks the traditional qualities of a hero, such as courage, idealism, or morality.
a protagonist lacking traditional heroic qualities

Examples

The film's antihero is a flawed but compelling figure who never quite earns the audience's trust.
Unlike a classic hero, an antihero may act out of self-interest or moral ambiguity.
Many modern novels center around an antihero rather than a traditional heroic character.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hero - the main character who displays admirable qualities or courage

Etymology

From Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'hero' (from Greek 'hērōs').
