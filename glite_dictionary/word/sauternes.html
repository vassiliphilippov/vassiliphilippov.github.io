Sauternes

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈtɜrnz / (AmE), / soʊˈtɜːnz / (BrE)
A semisweet golden-colored dessert wine from the Sauternes region near Bordeaux, France, or similar wines made elsewhere, especially in California.
semisweet golden dessert wine from France

Examples

The restaurant offers a fine selection of Sauternes to pair with your dessert.
She brought a bottle of Sauternes to complement the fruit tart.
Many collectors prize vintage Sauternes for their rich, honeyed flavor.

Etymology

From French Sauternes, the name of the region near Bordeaux where the wine is produced.
