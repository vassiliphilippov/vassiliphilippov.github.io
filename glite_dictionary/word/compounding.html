compounding

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of combining two or more things to form a new whole
combining things to make a whole

Examples

The compounding of the chemicals created a stable new substance.
In linguistics, compounding occurs when two words are joined to form a new word.
The financial advisor explained the compounding of interest over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combination - the act of combining two or more things

Etymology

From 'compound', from Latin 'componere' (to put together), plus the suffix '-ing'.
