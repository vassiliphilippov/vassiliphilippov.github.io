apomixis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈmɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˌapəˈmɪksɪs / (BrE)
A type of reproduction in which offspring are produced without fertilization, common in some plants and lower organisms.
reproduction without fertilization
medical

Examples

Some dandelions reproduce through apomixis, meaning they do not require pollination to form seeds.
The study focused on apomixis as a strategy for producing crops with uniform traits.
Scientists hope that understanding apomixis could help increase food production by bypassing the need for fertilization.

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (away from) and 'mixis' (mixing).
