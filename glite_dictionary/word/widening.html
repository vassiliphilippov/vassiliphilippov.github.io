widening

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something wider.
act of making wider

Examples

The widening of the road helped ease traffic congestion.
She noticed a widening gap between the two buildings.
Critics opposed the widening of income inequality in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadening - the act or process of making something broader
  • Antonym: narrowing - the act or process of making something narrower

Etymology

From the verb 'widen' + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdnɪŋ / (BrE)
a part of a road that has been made wider to allow vehicles to pass or park
section of a road made wider

Examples

The car pulled into the widening on the side of the road to let the bus pass.
A small widening was constructed near the intersection for extra parking.
You can safely stop at a widening if your vehicle breaks down on this highway.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
an increase in width; the process or result of becoming wider
an increase in width

Examples

The engineer suggested a widening of the road to reduce traffic congestion.
There has been a significant widening of the income gap in recent years.
The river's widening near the delta creates an ideal habitat for birds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowing - a decrease in width; the process of becoming narrower
