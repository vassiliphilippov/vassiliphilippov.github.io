to conglutinate

1. VERB

/ kənˈgluːtɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / kɒnˈgluːtɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to cause (two or more things) to stick together; to join by adhesion
to cause to stick together

Examples

The surgeon skillfully caused the tissues to conglutinate after the operation.
When dried, the sap helps to conglutinate the bark pieces together.
Certain proteins can conglutinate cells, forming larger clumps.

Synset Examples

The wounds were coapted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick fast or cleave; to attach firmly to something else

Etymology

From Latin 'conglutinare' (to glue together), from 'con-' (together) + 'glutinare' (to glue).

2. VERB

/ kənˈɡluːtɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / kɒnˈɡluːtɪneɪt / (BrE)
to stick or be stuck together; to cause to adhere, especially by a viscous substance
to stick together

Examples

After the surgery, the doctor expected the wound edges to conglutinate over time.
Certain substances make the small particles conglutinate into larger clusters.
When exposed to heat, the plastic sheets began to conglutinate and form a single layer.

Synset Examples

the edges of the wound conglutinated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick firmly to a surface or substance
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being joined together

Etymology

From Latin 'conglutinare' (to glue together), from 'con-' (together) + 'gluten' (glue).
