musculus rhomboideus major

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kjəl.əs ˌroʊm.bɔɪˈdiː.əs ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌrɒm.bɔɪˈdiː.əs ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
A rhomboid muscle located in the upper back that draws the scapula toward the spinal column.
upper back muscle moving scapula inward
medical

Examples

An injury to the musculus rhomboideus major can cause pain and restrict shoulder movement.
The physical therapist recommended exercises to strengthen the musculus rhomboideus major.
During anatomy class, we learned how the musculus rhomboideus major helps stabilize the scapula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhomboid major - the large rhomboid-shaped muscle of the upper back
  • Degree Lower: musculus rhomboideus minor - the smaller rhomboid muscle next to the major

Etymology

From Latin musculus (muscle), rhomboideus (rhomboid-shaped), major (greater/larger).
