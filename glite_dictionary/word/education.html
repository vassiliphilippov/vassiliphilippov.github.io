education

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdʒəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the knowledge and skills acquired through learning and instruction
knowledge gained by learning

Examples

He always valued a good education above material wealth.
Many people travel to other countries to pursue higher education.
Her education included both formal schooling and practical experience.

Synset Examples

it was clear that he had a very broad education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning - the process of acquiring knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge, understanding, or education

Etymology

From Latin 'educatio' (a breeding, a bringing up), from 'educare' (to bring up, rear, educate).

profession of teaching

Examples

Both of his parents work in education at the local university.
A shortage of qualified staff is affecting education across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaching - the act or profession of instructing others
  • Antonym: learning - process of acquiring knowledge or skills

Etymology

From Latin 'educatio' (a bringing up, rearing), from 'educare' (to bring up, rear, educate).
