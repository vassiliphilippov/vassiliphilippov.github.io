eosinophile

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːoʊˈsɪnəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˌiːəʊˈsɪnəˌfiːl / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell (leukocyte) that is easily stained by the dye eosin, often involved in allergic reactions and defense against parasites.
white blood cell stained by eosin
medical

Examples

Blood tests revealed an elevated count of eosinophiles in the patient.
The presence of eosinophiles is often associated with allergic reactions.
Researchers studied the role of eosinophiles in combating parasitic infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eosinophil - a type of white blood cell easily stained by eosin; alternate form/spelling

Etymology

From 'eosin' (a rose-colored dye) and Greek 'philos' (loving).
