Calliphoridae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪfəˈrɪdi / (AmE), / kælɪfəˈrɪːdɪ / (BrE)
a family of flies commonly known as blowflies, often metallic in appearance, whose larvae feed on decaying animal matter
family of blowflies (insects)

Examples

The Calliphoridae are often the first insects to arrive at a carcass, making them important in forensic investigations.
Members of the Calliphoridae family are known for their metallic blue or green bodies.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of Calliphoridae to better understand decomposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - common name for flies in the family Calliphoridae

Etymology

From New Latin Calliphoridae, based on Greek kalliphoros (bearing beauty), referring to the flies’ metallic coloring.
