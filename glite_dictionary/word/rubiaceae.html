Rubiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈbaɪ.əˌsiː.iː / (AmE), / ruːˌbɪˈeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of mainly tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs, including economically important plants such as coffee, gardenia, and cinchona
family of tropical plants (includes coffee, gardenia)
medical

Examples

The Rubiaceae family includes the plant from which coffee beans are harvested.
Gardenias are ornamental shrubs that belong to the Rubiaceae family.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of several Rubiaceae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee - tropical plant of the genus Coffea yielding coffee beans
  • Synonym: Cinchona - tropical South American tree whose bark yields quinine

Etymology

From Latin *rubiaceus*, referring to the genus Rubia (madder), plus the botanical suffix *-aceae* for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ ˌruːbiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌruːbɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a large family of mostly tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs, including the coffee, gardenia, and madder plants
plant family including coffee and gardenia

Examples

The coffee plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which also includes gardenias and madder.
Botanists have discovered new medicinal compounds in members of the Rubiaceae.
Many tropical forests are dominated by shrubs from the Rubiaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Rubia (the type genus, madder) + -aceae (family suffix).
