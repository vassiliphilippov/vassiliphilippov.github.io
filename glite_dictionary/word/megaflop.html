megaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌflɒp / (BrE)
a unit of computing speed equal to one million floating point operations per second
computing speed unit (1 million operations/sec)

Examples

The supercomputer achieved 500 megaflop performance during the benchmark tests.
Early personal computers could not even reach a single megaflop of speed.
Modern graphics cards are capable of processing thousands of megaflops per second.

Etymology

Blend of 'mega-' (meaning million) and 'flop' (floating point operations per second).
