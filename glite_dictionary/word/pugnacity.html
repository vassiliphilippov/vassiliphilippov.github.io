pugnacity

1. NOUN

/ pʌɡˈnæsəti / (AmE), / pʌɡˈnæsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being eager or quick to argue, quarrel, or fight; a combative nature
a tendency to be combative or quarrelsome

Examples

Despite her small stature, her pugnacity made her a formidable opponent in debates.
The politician's pugnacity often led to heated arguments with his colleagues.
His pugnacity was evident whenever a disagreement arose, no matter how trivial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerence - an aggressive or warlike attitude
  • Synonym: aggressiveness - quality of being aggressive or prone to attack
  • Antonym: placidity - the quality of being calm and peaceful

Etymology

From Latin pugnacitas, from pugnax (combative), from pugnare (to fight).
