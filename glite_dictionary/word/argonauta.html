Argonauta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrɡəˈnɔːtə / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡəˈnɔːtə / (BrE)
A genus of pelagic octopuses known as paper nautiluses, characterized by females that produce a thin, papery shell.
genus of octopuses (paper nautilus)

Examples

The Argonauta produces a delicate shell that is often found floating on the ocean surface.
Unlike true nautiluses, Argonauta species have soft bodies and construct temporary shells.
Scientists have long studied Argonauta for their unique reproductive strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautilus - a genus of marine mollusks with coiled shells

Etymology

From Greek 'Argonautēs', referring to the Argonauts of Greek mythology; named for the shell's resemblance to a ship.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑrɡəˈnɔtə / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡəˈnɔːtə / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Argonautidae, comprising cephalopod mollusks known as paper nautiluses
type genus of paper nautiluses (cephalopods)

Examples

The beautiful shell of the Argonauta has fascinated marine biologists for centuries.
Female Argonauta produce a delicate, paper-thin shell to protect their eggs.
While related to octopuses, the Argonauta is best known for its floating eggcase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper nautilus - a pelagic octopus with a delicate shell, belonging to the genus Argonauta

Etymology

From Greek 'Argōnautēs', referring to the Argonauts of Greek mythology, based on the shell's resemblance to a boat.
