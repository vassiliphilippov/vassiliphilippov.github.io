Leir of Britain

1. PROPN

/ lɪr əv ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / lɪə əv ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
a legendary king of Britain, famously depicted as the protagonist of Shakespeare's tragedy, known for being betrayed by his daughters
legendary British king; subject of Shakespeare's tragedy

Examples

The legend of Leir of Britain inspired Shakespeare's famous tragedy.
Early chronicles describe Leir of Britain as a wise ruler undone by his own daughters.
Literary scholars often compare the story of Leir of Britain to other tales of familial betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King Lear - the tragic protagonist of Shakespeare's play based on Leir of Britain

Etymology

From Geoffrey of Monmouth's Latin 'Leir,' a legendary king in British folklore, adapted by Shakespeare as King Lear.
