78

/ ˈsɛv.ən.ti eɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ən.ti eɪt / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛv.ən.ti ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a shellac-based phonograph record designed to play at 78 revolutions per minute, commonly used before the advent of vinyl records.
old shellac phonograph record

Examples

My grandfather still has a collection of old 78 records in his attic.
The antique shop sells restored 78 discs from the early twentieth century.
She bought a vintage gramophone to play her favorite 78s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vinyl - phonograph record made from vinyl, usually played at 33 or 45 rpm

Etymology

Refers to its playback speed: 78 revolutions per minute (rpm).
