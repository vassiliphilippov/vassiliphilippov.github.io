to insulate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.sə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjə.leɪt / (BrE)
to protect something by surrounding it with material that prevents the passage of heat, cold, or sound
protect from heat, cold, or noise

Examples

We decided to insulate the attic to reduce our heating bills.
Double-glazed windows help to insulate your home from outside noise.
Pipes should be insulated to prevent freezing during the winter.

Synset Examples

We had his bedroom insulated before winter came

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate something from others so it is alone
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave something unprotected or open to harm or danger

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' (island), via Italian 'insulare', meaning 'to make into an island'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɪnsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to protect someone or something by placing them apart from others or from negative influences; to keep separate
keep separate or protect from influence

Examples

The wealthy family tried to insulate their children from the harsh realities of the outside world.
Strict policies were introduced to insulate the company from market fluctuations.
He felt the need to insulate himself from negative criticism while working on his project.

Synset Examples

They isolated the political prisoners from the other inmates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to set apart from others
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave unprotected or subject to outside influences

Etymology

From Latin insulatus (made into an island), from insula (island).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00495205-v
Synset POS: v
