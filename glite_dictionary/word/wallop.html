wallop

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləp / (AmE), / ˈwɒləp / (BrE)
a powerful impact or strong effect, especially in a way that is forceful or impressive
powerful impact; strong effect

Examples

The new advertising campaign really packs a wallop and is grabbing everyone's attention.
Her speech delivered an emotional wallop that left the audience in tears.
That scene in the movie has a surprising wallop that stays with you long after.

/ ˈwɑː.ləp / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.əp / (BrE)
to defeat someone completely and decisively, especially in a contest or competition

informal

Examples

If we keep playing like this, we’ll wallop the other team for sure.
Their basketball squad walloped us by more than thirty points.
