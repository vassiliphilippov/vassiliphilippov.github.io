Pope

1. PROPN

/ poʊp / (AmE), / pəʊp / (BrE)
The bishop of Rome and the head of the Roman Catholic Church; regarded as the spiritual leader of Catholics worldwide.
head of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The Pope visited several countries during his papacy, spreading messages of peace and unity.
Many Catholics travel to Rome for a chance to see the Pope address the crowd at St. Peter's Square.
After the election of a new Pope, there was widespread celebration among the faithful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pontiff - Another term for the Pope or high priest in some Christian traditions

Etymology

From Latin 'papa' meaning 'father', via Greek 'pappas'.
