synchronising

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or adjustment of making two or more things happen at the same time or in unison
adjusting events to occur in unison

Examples

The synchronising of the traffic lights allowed vehicles to pass smoothly through the city.
Careful synchronising of the dancers' movements made the performance look flawless.
Accurate synchronising between the sound and visuals is essential for successful film editing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - the process of making things happen simultaneously
  • Antonym: desynchronisation - the process of causing things not to occur at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), via French and Latin.
