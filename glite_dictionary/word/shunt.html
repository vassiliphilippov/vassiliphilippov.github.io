shunt

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
A passage or device used to divert a bodily fluid, especially blood, from one channel to another.
a passage for diverting bodily fluid
medical

Examples

The surgeon inserted a shunt to redirect the flow of cerebrospinal fluid.
A ventricular shunt can help relieve pressure caused by hydrocephalus.
Doctors used a shunt to bypass the blocked artery.

Synset Examples

an arteriovenus shunt

Etymology

From Latin 'sumptus', meaning 'taken up', via French 'shunter' (to turn aside).

2. NOUN

/ ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a conductor of low resistance connected in parallel with another device to divert part of the electric current
low-resistance conductor to divert electric current

Examples

The engineer installed a shunt to measure the current flowing through the circuit.
A precise shunt is essential to protect sensitive components from too much current.
You can use a shunt for current measurement in high-power circuits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass - an alternative path for current or flow

Etymology

From Latin 'sumptus', past participle of 'sumere' (to take), via the obsolete English 'shunt' (to turn aside, to evade).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a medical device in the form of a plastic or rubber tube implanted in the body to drain fluids from one area to another
tube for draining bodily fluids (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient required a shunt to relieve pressure caused by excess cerebrospinal fluid.
Doctors inserted a shunt to help drain fluid from the brain.
A malfunctioning shunt can lead to serious complications if not detected early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catheter - a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or inject fluids

Etymology

From Latin *surgere* (to rise) via early technical use in engineering, later extended to medicine.

4. VERB

/ ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
to provide with or divert current by means of an electrical shunt, especially to protect a circuit or direct electricity along a particular path
to divert with an electrical shunt

Examples

The technician decided to shunt the current to prevent damage to the circuit.
In certain medical devices, surgeons shunt excess fluid using electrical means.
If the voltage rises too high, the system will automatically shunt the flow away from sensitive components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass - to pass or go around; to avoid by diversion

Etymology

From Latin 'scundere' (to jump), via late 19th-century railway slang 'shunt' (to turn aside or switch).

5. VERB

/ ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
to move a train or railway vehicle from one track to another, especially to allow others to pass or to organize railway traffic.
move a train to a different track

Examples

The train was shunted onto a side track to allow the express to pass.
Workers quickly shunt the empty wagons to the storage yard every morning.
If there is congestion ahead, the control center will shunt incoming trains to alternative tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - to change tracks, especially for trains

Etymology

Of unclear origin; first attested in the late 18th century, likely related to dialectal words meaning 'to move aside.'
