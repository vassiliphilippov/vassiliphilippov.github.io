Zinfandel

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪn.fənˌdɛl / (AmE), / ˈzɪn.fənˌdɛl / (BrE)
A type of small, dark-skinned grape grown chiefly in California, or the robust red wine made from this grape.
type of grape or wine

Examples

We ordered a bottle of Zinfandel to go with our steak dinner.
Many California vineyards are famous for their old-vine Zinfandel.
This region produces both red and white wines, but Zinfandel is their specialty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wine - alcoholic drink made from fermented grapes

Etymology

From American English; of uncertain European origin, possibly from a Hungarian or Austrian grape name.
