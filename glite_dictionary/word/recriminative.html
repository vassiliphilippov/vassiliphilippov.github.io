recriminative

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈkrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
involving or characterized by recrimination; making accusations in response to accusations from others
responding with counter-accusations

Examples

The debate quickly became recriminative as both sides started accusing each other of wrongdoing.
His recriminative comments only served to escalate the conflict rather than resolve it.
A recriminative exchange broke out during the meeting, disrupting any chance of compromise.

Synset Examples

recriminatory arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recriminatory - making or characterized by a counter-accusation

Etymology

From Latin rec rīminātus, past participle of recrīminārī (to accuse in return).
