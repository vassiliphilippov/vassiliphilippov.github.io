unsuccessful

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səkˈsɛs.fəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səkˈsɛs.fəl / (BrE)
not achieving the intended result or objective; failing to accomplish what was attempted
not achieving the intended result

Examples

Despite her hard work, the project was ultimately unsuccessful.
The company launched an unsuccessful attempt to break into the European market.
His application to the university was unsuccessful this year.

Synset Examples

an abortive revolt
a stillborn plot to assassinate the President

Related Terms

  • Antonym: successful - achieving the intended result or objective

Etymology

From un- (not) + successful; 'successful' from success + -ful.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səkˈsɛs.fəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səkˈsɛs.fəl / (BrE)
Not achieving the intended goal or result; having failed or produced an unfavorable outcome.
not achieving success; having failed

Examples

Despite months of preparation, the project was ultimately unsuccessful.
Her unsuccessful attempts to reach customer service left her feeling frustrated.
The negotiations were unsuccessful, and no agreement was reached.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: successful - achieving a desired result or goal
  • Synonym: failed - did not succeed or achieve the goal

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'successful', itself from 'success'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02341445-a
Synset POS: a
