Anomiidae

1. NOUN

/ ænˌoʊˈmaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / anˌəʊˈmɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of bivalve mollusks commonly known as saddle oysters, characterized by thin, irregular shells and found attached to substrates in marine environments.
family of saddle oysters (bivalve mollusks)
medical

Examples

The shells of Anomiidae are often thin and translucent, clinging to rocks and shells in shallow seas.
Scientists collected several specimens of Anomiidae for their study of coastal ecosystems.
Members of the Anomiidae family are sometimes called 'jingle shells' due to the sound they make when shaken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle oyster - Common name for bivalves of the Anomiidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Anomia (the type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊ.miˌaɪ.dɪ / (AmE), / əˌnəʊ.mɪˈaɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks, commonly known as saddle oysters, characterized by thin, irregularly shaped shells.
family of marine bivalve mollusks (saddle oysters)

Examples

The Anomiidae are known for their thin, translucent shells that attach to rocks by a byssus.
Several species in the Anomiidae family are found in shallow coastal waters worldwide.
Research on Anomiidae provides insight into the evolution of bivalve attachment mechanisms.

Etymology

From New Latin Anomiidae, based on the genus Anomia, from Greek anōmos (lawless, irregular).
