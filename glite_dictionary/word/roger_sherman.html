Roger Sherman

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑdʒər ˈʃɜrmən / (AmE), / ˈrɒdʒə ˈʃɜːmən / (BrE)
(1721–1793) An American Revolutionary leader and statesman who was a signer of the Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and the United States Constitution.
American Revolutionary leader and Founding Father

Examples

Roger Sherman played a pivotal role in drafting the United States Constitution.
Many historians study the contributions of Roger Sherman to America's founding documents.
Roger Sherman was the only person to sign all four major American founding papers.
