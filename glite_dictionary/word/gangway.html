gangway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋweɪ / (BrE)
a temporary bridge or platform used for boarding or leaving a ship at dockside
temporary bridge for boarding a ship

Examples

The passengers crossed the gangway to board the ship.
As soon as the ship docked, the crew lowered the gangway.
For safety, children should be accompanied when walking on the gangway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangplank - a movable plank used as a ramp to board or leave a ship

Etymology

From gang (walk, go) + way; originally nautical, first recorded in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (BrE)
A temporary passageway made of planks, typically used over mud or uneven ground on building sites or to board ships.
temporary plank passageway

Examples

The workers laid a gangway over the mud to move equipment safely across the site.
Passengers used the gangway to board the ship from the dock.
A temporary gangway connected the building to the road during construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkway - a path for walking, especially one constructed for use by pedestrians

Etymology

From 'gang' (to go) + 'way', originally nautical, referring to a passage on ships.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋweɪ / (BrE)
A passage between rows of seats in an auditorium, a passenger vehicle, or between shelves in a store, providing access and movement.
passage between rows of seats or shelves

Examples

The usher directed us down the gangway to our seats near the stage.
Emergency exits were clearly indicated at each end of the gangway.
He quickly wheeled the trolley through the narrow gangway between the store shelves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aisle - a passage between rows of seats or shelves

Etymology

From gang (to go, walk) + way; 'gang' from Old English 'gangan'.

Source: WordNet
