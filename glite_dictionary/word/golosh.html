golosh

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɑʃ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɒʃ / (BrE)
A waterproof overshoe worn over ordinary shoes to protect them from water or snow.
waterproof overshoe

Examples

He put on his goloshes before leaving the house to walk through the snow.
After removing her goloshes, Sarah placed them by the door to dry.
The children wore bright yellow goloshes on the rainy field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galosh - alternative spelling for a waterproof overshoe

Etymology

From French 'galoche', from Medieval Latin 'galopia', a type of shoe.
