latent

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈleɪt(ə)nt / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) present but not currently active or manifest; existing but not yet developed or visible.
present but not active
medical

Examples

The virus can remain latent in the body for years without causing symptoms.
Doctors monitored the patient for signs of a latent infection emerging.
Latent tuberculosis may not be detected without specialized tests.

Synset Examples

latent infection
latent diabetes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - (of a disease or condition) currently manifest or symptomatic
  • Synonym: dormant - temporarily inactive but capable of becoming active later

Etymology

From Latin 'latens', present participle of 'latere' (to lie hidden).

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈleɪt(ə)nt / (BrE)
Existing but not yet developed or manifest; hidden or concealed.
not visible or active; hidden

Examples

The disease may remain latent in the body for years without causing symptoms.
He discovered a latent ability for music during his teenage years.
The software contained a latent error that only surfaced under specific conditions.

Synset Examples

a latent fingerprint
latent talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormant - temporarily inactive but capable of becoming active
  • Antonym: apparent - clearly visible or understood; obvious

Etymology

From Latin latens, latentis (lying hidden), present participle of latere (to lie hidden).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00044828-s
Synset POS: s
