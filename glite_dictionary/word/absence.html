absence

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səns / (AmE), / ˈæb.səns / (BrE)
The state of being away from a place or not present; lack of presence.
state of being absent; lack of presence

Examples

His repeated absence from meetings raised concern among his colleagues.
During her absence, someone broke into the apartment.
The teacher noticed the absence of enthusiasm in the students' responses.

Synset Examples

he was surprised by the absence of any explanation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: presence - the state of being present or at a place

Etymology

From Latin absentia, from absens (present participle of abesse, to be away).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səns / (AmE), / ˈæb.səns / (BrE)
a brief episode of unconsciousness, typically with a blank stare and sometimes slight movements, often occurring in certain types of epilepsy

medical

Examples

During the classroom lesson, the boy experienced an absence and stared blankly for several seconds.
Many children with epilepsy are diagnosed after repeated absences at school go unnoticed.
Her doctor explained that these absences might look like daydreaming but are actually brief seizures.

4. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səns / (AmE), / ˈæb.səns / (BrE)
The state of being not present in a place or situation; the fact of someone or something being away or lacking.
state of being not present

Examples

James's absence from the meeting was noted by everyone.
Due to her unexpected absence, the project was delayed.
An absence of evidence makes the case difficult to solve.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: presence - the state of being present; being in a place

Etymology

From Latin absentia (state of being away), from absens (absent).
