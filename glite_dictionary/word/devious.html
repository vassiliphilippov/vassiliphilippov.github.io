devious

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːviəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːvɪəs / (BrE)
using dishonest or indirect methods to achieve something; not sincere or straightforward.
not sincere; using dishonest methods

Examples

Her devious actions eventually led to the loss of trust among her colleagues.
The politician's devious tactics were exposed during the investigation.
He gave a devious reply, carefully avoiding the main question.

Synset Examples

a devious character
shifty eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to lie or cheat
  • Antonym: straightforward - honest and direct; not trying to hide anything

Etymology

From Latin 'deviare' meaning 'to turn aside'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdiːviəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːvɪəs / (BrE)
departing from the shortest or most direct way; not straight or straightforward in course or direction
not straight; indirect path or route

Examples

The hikers followed a devious trail through the forest, which made the journey longer than expected.
To avoid the traffic jam, we took a devious route around the city.
The river's devious path through the valley added miles to our canoe trip.

Synset Examples

a scenic but devious route
a long and circuitous journey by train and boat
a roundabout route avoided rush-hour traffic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuitous - longer than the most direct way; roundabout
  • Antonym: direct - going straight without deviation; not roundabout

Etymology

From Latin 'deviare' (to turn aside) + -ous.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdiːviəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːvɪəs / (BrE)
Using indirect, dishonest, or tricky methods to achieve something; not straightforward or sincere.
dishonest or indirect in action

Examples

He took a devious route to avoid being followed.
Her devious methods eventually led to her downfall.
The politician was accused of using devious tactics during the campaign.

Synset Examples

used devious means to achieve success
gave oblique answers to direct questions
oblique political maneuvers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not truthful or trustworthy
  • Antonym: straightforward - honest and direct; not devious

Etymology

From Latin 'devius', meaning 'out of the way, remote', from 'de-' (away from) + 'via' (way).

Synset POS: s
