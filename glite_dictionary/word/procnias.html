Procnias

1. NOUN

/ proʊkˈnaɪ.əs / (AmE), / prɒkˈnaɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American birds in the family Cotingidae, known as bellbirds, recognized for the loud, bell-like calls of the males.
a genus of tropical American bellbirds

Examples

The Procnias genus includes several species found in South American forests.
Male Procnias are famous for their resonant, bell-like calls during the breeding season.
Researchers have studied the vocalizations of Procnias to understand their unique communication methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellbird - any of several birds of the genus Procnias, noted for their bell-like calls

Etymology

From New Latin Procnias, the genus name in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ proʊkˈnaɪəs / (AmE), / prɒkˈnaɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical birds, known as bellbirds, found in Central and South America, recognized for their bell-like calls.
genus of bellbirds

Examples

The genus Procnias includes several species of bellbirds notable for their unique calls.
Researchers are studying the vocalizations of Procnias in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of Procnias inhabit tropical forests in Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellbird - a bird known for its resonant call; often species in the genus Procnias

Etymology

From New Latin *Procnias*, the genus name used in zoological taxonomy.
