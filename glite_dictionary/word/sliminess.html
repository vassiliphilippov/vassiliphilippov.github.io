sliminess

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪminɪs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪmɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpleasantly slippery or disgusting, often causing a feeling of revulsion or distrust
quality of being unpleasantly slippery or disgusting

Examples

She disliked the sliminess of the touch as she picked up the seaweed.
There is a certain sliminess to his charm that makes you distrust him.
The sliminess of the politician's tactics disgusted the public.

Synset Examples

the vileness of his language surprised us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vileness - the quality of being extremely unpleasant or evil
  • Synonym: nastiness - the quality of being very unpleasant

Etymology

From slime + -iness, formed from the adjective 'slimy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪminəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪminəs / (BrE)
the quality of being slimy, often referring to something slippery, sticky, or unpleasant to the touch
the quality of being slimy

Examples

The sliminess of the frog's skin made it difficult to hold.
She recoiled at the sliminess of the seaweed brushing against her leg.
After opening the old container, a distinct sliminess and unpleasant odor filled the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slimy - covered with or resembling slime
  • Synonym: stickiness - the quality of being sticky
  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being dry; lacking moisture or slime

Etymology

From "slimy" + "-ness" forming a noun indicating state or quality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04942582-n
Synset POS: n
