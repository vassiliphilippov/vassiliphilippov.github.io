Mentha longifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnθə lɔŋˈɪfəliə / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə lɒŋˈɪfɪlɪə / (BrE)
A coarse, wild species of mint native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, with long leaves and spikelike clusters of flowers; also known as wild mint or horse mint, now naturalized in parts of North America.
species of wild mint with spikelike flowers

Examples

Mentha longifolia is often found growing along riverbanks and wet meadows in its native range.
The leaves of Mentha longifolia are sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
Gardeners appreciate Mentha longifolia for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mint - common name for Mentha longifolia
  • Degree Lower: Mentha spicata - another species of mint, known as spearmint

Etymology

From Latin: Mentha (the genus for mints) + longifolia (meaning 'long-leaved').
