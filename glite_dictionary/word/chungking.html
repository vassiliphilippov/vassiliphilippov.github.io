Chungking

1. PROPN

/ tʃʌŋˈkɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃʌŋˈkɪŋ / (BrE)
a former spelling of Chongqing, a major city in south-central China on the Yangtze River, known as a commercial center for western China.
former spelling of Chongqing, major Chinese city

Examples

During World War II, Chungking served as the provisional capital of China.
The Yangtze River passes through Chungking, making it a vital transportation hub.
He traveled from Shanghai to Chungking by boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chongqing - modern spelling for the city previously known as Chungking, major city in southwestern China

Etymology

From Chinese 重庆 (Chóngqìng), earlier romanized as Chungking.
