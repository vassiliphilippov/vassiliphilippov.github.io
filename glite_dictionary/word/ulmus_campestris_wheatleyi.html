Ulmus campestris wheatleyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs kæmˈpɛstrɪs ˌwiːθˈliːaɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs kəmˈpɛstrɪs ˌwiːtˈliːaɪ / (BrE)
A variety of English elm characterized by upright branches and broader leaves.
variety of English elm tree

Examples

The garden features several mature Ulmus campestris wheatleyi trees along the main path.
Horticulturists recommend Ulmus campestris wheatleyi for urban planting due to its upright growth habit.
The leaves of Ulmus campestris wheatleyi are noticeably broader than those of other elms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree with serrated leaves and distinct vase-shaped growth
