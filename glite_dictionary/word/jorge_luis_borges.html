Jorge Luis Borges

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɔrheɪ lwiˈis ˈbɔrˌhɛs / (AmE), / ˈhɔːheɪ lwiˈiːs ˈbɔːheɪs / (BrE)
(1899–1986) An Argentinian writer known for his influential short stories, essays, and poems, often exploring themes of identity, reality, and infinity.
Argentinian writer and short story author

Examples

Many literary critics consider Jorge Luis Borges one of the most important writers of the twentieth century.
Jorge Luis Borges's stories often blur the boundaries between reality and fiction.
