Aphyllanthaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪlænˈθeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / əˌfɪlænˈθeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in some botanical classification systems, sometimes separated from the Liliaceae and comprising the genus Aphyllanthes.
a plant family in some classifications

Examples

The genus Aphyllanthes is the sole member of the Aphyllanthaceae family in some botanical systems.
Some botanists recognize Aphyllanthaceae as distinct from the Liliaceae family.
Recent studies question the classification of Aphyllanthaceae as a separate plant family.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without), 'phyllon' (leaf), and 'Anthos' (flower), with the botanical suffix '-aceae' for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ əˌfɪlænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / əˌfɪlænˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes recognized in botanical classification, that includes the genus Aphyllanthes; it has been variably included in or separated from the Liliaceae family
a plant family including genus Aphyllanthes

Examples

The Aphyllanthaceae are sometimes treated as a separate family rather than included within the Liliaceae.
Botanists debate whether Aphyllanthaceae should be recognized as distinct or merged with larger families.
The best-known member of the Aphyllanthaceae is the Mediterranean plant Aphyllanthes monspeliensis.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the type genus Aphyllanthes (from Greek 'a-' without + 'phyllon' leaf + 'anthos' flower) + '-aceae', a standard family suffix in botanical names.
