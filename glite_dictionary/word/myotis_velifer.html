Myotis velifer

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈoʊtɪs ˈvɛlɪfər / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊtɪs ˈvɛlɪfə / (BrE)
a small bat of the southwestern United States, typically found living in caves and similar habitats; commonly known as the cave myotis
species of bat native to US caves

Examples

The Myotis velifer is often observed roosting in large colonies within limestone caves.
Researchers recorded the flight patterns of Myotis velifer to better understand its nocturnal behavior.
Unlike many other bats, Myotis velifer prefers habitats with abundant water sources nearby.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus Myotis (from Greek mys 'mouse' + ous, otos 'ear') and Latin 'velifer' ('bearing a sail').
