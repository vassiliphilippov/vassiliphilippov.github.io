tampion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmpiən / (AmE), / ˈtampɪən / (BrE)
a plug for the muzzle of a gun or cannon, used to keep out dust and moisture
plug for gun or cannon muzzle

Examples

The artillery officer checked that each cannon had a tampion securely in place before transport.
Removing the tampion from the muzzle, the gun crew prepared the cannon for firing.
During long sea voyages, sailors would use a tampion to protect the barrel from saltwater damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug - an object that blocks a hole or opening
  • Synonym: cap - a cover or protective lid for an opening

Etymology

From French 'tampion', from Middle Dutch 'tampe' (plug, stopper).
