sanctum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋktəm / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktəm / (BrE)
A sacred or private place, especially one regarded as holy or used for worship or pilgrimage; a sanctuary.
sacred or private place; sanctuary

Examples

The ancient temple stood as a sanctum for thousands of pilgrims each year.
She entered the inner sanctum, a place rarely seen by outsiders.
Many devotees traveled for miles to reach the holy sanctum of the shrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety, often a holy place

Etymology

From Latin sanctum (holy place), from sanctus (holy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋktəm / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktəm / (BrE)
a private place where you are not to be disturbed; a place of complete privacy or retreat
private place or retreat

Examples

After a long day at work, she escaped to her sanctum to read in peace.
His office, cluttered but cozy, served as his personal sanctum away from the family chaos.
Writers often dream of having a quiet sanctum where inspiration comes easily.

Synset Examples

he withdrew to his sanctum sanctorum, where the children could never go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - a place of seclusion or privacy
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or protection

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctum' meaning 'holy place'.
