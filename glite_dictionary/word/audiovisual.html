audiovisual

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔdioʊˈvɪʒuəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəʊˈvɪʒjuəl / (BrE)
a material or device that uses both sight and sound to present information, especially for teaching or communication purposes
material using sight and sound

Examples

The teacher supplemented her lecture with several audiovisual materials to engage the students.
Modern museums often rely on audiovisual displays to provide interactive experiences for visitors.
We need to check the audiovisual before the conference begins to ensure everything works.

Synset Examples

language tapes and videocassettes and other audiovisuals

Etymology

From Latin 'audio' (I hear) + 'visualis' (visual), coined in the early 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔdiəˈvɪʒuəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəˈvɪʒuəl / (BrE)
involving both hearing and sight, especially in reference to teaching materials or media that use sound and images together
involving both hearing and sight

Examples

The teacher used several audiovisual materials to make the lesson more engaging.
Modern presentations often include audiovisual equipment for sound and video.
The conference room is equipped with state-of-the-art audiovisual technology.

Synset Examples

the school's audiovisual department

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multimedia - using more than one medium of expression or communication

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'audire' (to hear) and 'visualis' (of seeing).
