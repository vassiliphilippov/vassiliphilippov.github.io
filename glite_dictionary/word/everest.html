Everest

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛvərɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛvərɪst / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the world, located in the central Himalayas on the border of Tibet (China) and Nepal, rising to 8,848 meters (29,028 feet) above sea level
highest mountain in the world

Examples

Many climbers dream of reaching the summit of Everest despite the risks involved.
The weather on Everest can change dramatically within hours, making expeditions challenging.
Scientists have studied the effects of altitude on Everest to learn more about the human body.

Etymology

Named after Sir George Everest (1790–1866), British surveyor general of India.
