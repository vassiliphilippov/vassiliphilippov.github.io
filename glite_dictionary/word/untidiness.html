untidiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtaɪdinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌɪdinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being untidy or messy
the state of being untidy

Examples

His room was filled with untidiness after the party.
The untidiness of his desk made it hard to find important papers.
She apologized for the untidiness of her house when guests arrived unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tidiness - the quality of being tidy and neat
  • Synonym: messiness - the state of being messy

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix '-ness' to 'untidy', denoting a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtaɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌɪdɪnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being untidy; lack of order or neatness.
lack of order or neatness

Examples

The untidiness of his desk made it hard to find important papers.
Her mother scolded her for the untidiness of her bedroom.
Despite the untidiness, the room had a certain charm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tidiness - the state of being tidy; neatness
  • Synonym: disorder - a lack of order or organization; messiness
  • Synonym: messiness - the quality of being messy

Etymology

From un- (not) + tidiness, based on tidy.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
