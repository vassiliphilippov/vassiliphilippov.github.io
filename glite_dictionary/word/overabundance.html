overabundance

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərəˈbʌndəns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈbʌndəns / (BrE)
A quantity that is more than what is needed, appropriate, or desirable; an excessive amount.
an excessive or overly large amount

Examples

The region faces an overabundance of rainfall during the monsoon season.
There was an overabundance of food at the party, so much went uneaten.
Some critics argue that television today has an overabundance of reality shows.

Synset Examples

four-year-olds have an overabundance of energy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'abundance' (great quantity), ultimately from Latin 'abundantia' (fullness, plenty).

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚəˈbʌndəns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈbʌndəns / (BrE)
A quantity that is more than needed; an excessive amount of something.
an excessive amount; more than is needed

Examples

The garden produced an overabundance of tomatoes this year, far more than we could eat.
An overabundance of information can make it difficult to make decisions.
There was such an overabundance of volunteers that some had to be turned away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - an amount that is more than necessary
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From Latin 'abundantia' (abundance), with the prefix 'over-' indicating excess.
