corporeal

1. ADJ

/ kɔrˈpɔriəl / (AmE), / kɔːˈpɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a person's body, especially as opposed to their spirit or mind
relating to the physical body

Examples

The pain she felt was entirely corporeal rather than emotional.
Many religions distinguish the corporeal body from the immortal soul.
Yoga seeks to balance corporeal health with mental well-being.

Synset Examples

bodily needs
a corporal defect
corporeal suffering
a somatic symptom or somatic illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bodily - relating to the body
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than the body

Etymology

From Latin corporeus (of the body), from corpus (body).

2. ADJ

/ kɔrˈpɔriəl / (AmE), / kɔːˈpɔːriəl / (BrE)
having a physical, material body or form; not spiritual or intangible.
having physical or material form

Examples

The ghost appeared to have a corporeal presence, surprising everyone in the room.
Philosophers often debate the nature of corporeal versus spiritual existence.
Her corporeal needs, such as food and shelter, could not be ignored during the retreat.

Synset Examples

that which is created is of necessity corporeal and visible and tangible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorporeal - lacking physical form or substance; immaterial

Etymology

From Late Latin corporealis, from Latin corpus (body).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00632838-a
Synset POS: a
