nervus opticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɝː.vəs ɑpˈtiː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.vəs ɒpˈtiː.kəs / (BrE)
the second cranial nerve, responsible for transmitting visual information from the retina to the brain; the optic nerve
the optic nerve (cranial nerve II)
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervus opticus can lead to partial or complete loss of vision.
The surgeon carefully traced the nervus opticus during the procedure to avoid injury.
Diseases that affect the nervus opticus are studied by neuro-ophthalmologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the cranial nerve that transmits visual information from the retina to the brain

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'optic nerve'; 'nervus' means 'nerve' and 'opticus' means 'optical' or 'related to sight'.
