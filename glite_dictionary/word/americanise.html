to Americanise

1. VERB

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmer.ɪ.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make or become American in character, style, or influence
make or become American in character
british

Examples

Many European cities have become increasingly Americanised in recent decades.
The restaurant chain plans to Americanise its menu to appeal to tourists.
Living in New York for five years really Americanised her tastes and habits.

Synset Examples

The year in the US has completely Americanized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Americanize - US spelling of 'Americanise', same meaning

Etymology

From 'America' + '-ise', modelled after similar formations (Americanize in US spelling).

2. VERB

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmer.ɪ.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make or become American in character, form, or style
make or become American in character or style
british

Examples

After living in London for five years, she began to Americanise her accent.
Some traditional foods have been Americanised to appeal to tourists visiting the city.
As the company expanded abroad, its practices started to Americanise in order to compete in the U.S. market.

Synset Examples

After a year in Iowa, he has totally Americanized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Americanize - to make or become American in character (American English spelling)

Etymology

Formed from 'American' + '-ise', a verb-forming suffix.
