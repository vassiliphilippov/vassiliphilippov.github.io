feedback

1. NOUN

/ ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
Comments or responses given to provide information about someone’s work, performance, or the outcome of an action or process.
response or comments about an action or inquiry

Examples

The teacher gave me valuable feedback on my essay, which helped me improve my writing.
After the experiment, the scientists collected feedback from all participants to analyze the results.
We appreciate your feedback and will use it to make our service better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or judgement about something
  • Synonym: input - advice or opinions given to help improve something

Etymology

Formed from 'feed' + 'back'; first used in the early 20th century in engineering before general use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
the process in which part of the output of a system is returned to its input to regulate or modify further output
output looped back to input (system control)

Examples

The climate system is complex and features several feedback mechanisms that regulate global temperature.
In audio systems, a feedback loop can cause unwanted screeching noises if not properly managed.
Engineers designed the controller to use feedback from the sensors to maintain stability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: input - data or power supplied to a system
  • Antonym: output - the result produced by a system

Etymology

From 'feed' + 'back', coined in early 20th century in engineering contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13500583-n
Synset POS: n
