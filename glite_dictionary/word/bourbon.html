Bourbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːbən / (AmE), / ˈbʊəbɒn / (BrE)
a member of a European royal family that ruled France, Spain, and other countries at various times since the 16th century
member of the Bourbon royal family

Examples

King Louis XVI was a Bourbon who ruled France until the French Revolution.
The Spanish royal family is descended from a branch of the Bourbon dynasty.
Many palaces in Europe were built by the Bourbon monarchs.

Etymology

From French Bourbon, named after the Bourbon region in France.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːbən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbən / (BrE)
(historical, US) A reactionary politician, especially from the South, who opposed progressive reforms after the Civil War.
reactionary Southern US politician (historical)

Examples

The Southern Bourbon politicians resisted most attempts at social change during the Reconstruction era.
Some historians refer to post-Civil War leaders in Louisiana as Bourbon Democrats because of their conservative stance.
The influence of the Bourbon faction declined as more progressive reformers gained power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - opposing political or social reform; conservative

Etymology

From the Bourbon dynasty of France, noted for their reactionary views after being restored to the throne.

3. PROPN

/ ˈbɜrbən / (AmE), / ˈbʊəbɒn / (BrE)
a European royal dynasty that ruled France (1589–1793), Spain, and parts of Italy
European royal dynasty

Examples

The Bourbon dynasty was overthrown during the French Revolution.
Many Spanish monarchs have belonged to the Bourbon family.
The palace was decorated with portraits of Bourbon kings and queens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Habsburg - European royal dynasty known for ruling Austria and Spain
  • Synonym: Stuart - Royal house that ruled Scotland and England

Etymology

From French Bourbon, the name of the family's ancestral seat in central France.
