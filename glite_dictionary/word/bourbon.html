bourbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrbən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbən / (BrE)
a type of American whiskey made mainly from corn and aged in charred oak barrels
American whiskey made from corn

Examples

He ordered a glass of bourbon on the rocks after dinner.
The recipe calls for a splash of bourbon to add depth to the sauce.
Many visitors take tours of Kentucky distilleries to learn how bourbon is made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiskey - a spirit distilled from grain mash

Etymology

Named after Bourbon County, Kentucky, where the whiskey was first made.
