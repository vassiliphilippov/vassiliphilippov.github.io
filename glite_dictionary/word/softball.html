softball

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔftˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈsɒftˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A large, soft, white or yellow ball with red stitching, used in the sport of softball.
ball used in the sport of softball

Examples

She tossed the softball to her teammate during practice.
This softball is slightly larger than a standard baseball.
The coach bought a new softball for the upcoming tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softball - team game played with bat and ball similar to baseball

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔftˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈsɒftˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a game similar to baseball, played on a smaller field with a larger ball
a team sport like baseball played with a larger ball

Examples

She has played softball since she was a child and loves pitching for her local team.
The school organized a softball tournament for students of all ages.
He prefers softball over baseball because the games are usually more relaxed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - a bat-and-ball game played between two teams on a diamond-shaped field

Etymology

From 'soft' + 'ball'; so called because early versions used a softer ball than baseball.
