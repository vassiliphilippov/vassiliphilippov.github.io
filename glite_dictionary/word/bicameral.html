bicameral

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkæmɚəl / (AmE), / baɪˈkæmərəl / (BrE)
(of a system, especially a legislative body or an organ) consisting of two chambers or divisions.
having two chambers or divisions

Examples

Many parliaments, such as the US Congress, are bicameral in structure.
The research compared animals with bicameral hearts to those with single-chambered hearts.
Australia has a bicameral legislature, which includes the House of Representatives and the Senate.

Synset Examples

the bicameral heart of a fish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unicameral - having a single chamber or division

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + camera (chamber).

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈkæm.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈkæm.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a legislative body) having two separate chambers or houses.
having two legislative chambers

Examples

The United States Congress is a bicameral legislature, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives.
Some countries, like Sweden, have abandoned their bicameral systems in favor of a single legislative chamber.
Debates in bicameral parliaments often involve complex negotiations between the two houses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unicameral - having a single legislative chamber

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'camera' (chamber).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
