Solanum melanocerasum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm ˌmɛlənoʊˈsɛrəsəm / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˌmɛlənəʊˈsɛrəsəm / (BrE)
an improved garden variety of black nightshade, cultivated for its small edible orange or black berries
garden variety of black nightshade with edible berries

Examples

Solanum melanocerasum is often grown for its attractive berries, which are safe to eat when fully ripe.
Gardeners appreciate Solanum melanocerasum because it produces fruit that adds color to the garden.
You can use Solanum melanocerasum berries to make jams after confirming correct identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black nightshade - a plant species (Solanum nigrum) related to Solanum melanocerasum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' (the nightshade genus) and 'melanocerasum' (black cherry/fruit, from Greek 'melas' meaning black and Latin 'cerasum' meaning cherry).
