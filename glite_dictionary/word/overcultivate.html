to overcultivate

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈkʌl.tə.veɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈkʌl.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
To make land less productive by cultivating it too intensively or frequently, exhausting its resources.
deplete land by excessive cultivation

Examples

If farmers overcultivate their fields, the soil quickly loses its fertility.
The land was overcultivated for years, resulting in poor crop yields.
Efforts are underway to restore areas damaged by overcultivating the soil.

Synset Examples

the farmers overcropped the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcrop - To exhaust land by growing crops on it too frequently or intensively
  • Degree Lower: to cultivate - To prepare and use land for crops or gardening

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) and 'cultivate' (to prepare and use land for crops).
