actomyosin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.t̬oʊˈmaɪ.ə.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌæk.təʊˈmaɪ.ə.sɪn / (BrE)
a complex of actin and myosin proteins found in muscle fibers, responsible for muscle contraction when activated
protein complex causing muscle contraction

Examples

The interaction of actomyosin filaments is essential for muscle contraction.
Researchers studied the properties of actomyosin to better understand muscle diseases.
During strenuous exercise, the breakdown of actomyosin leads to muscle fatigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myosin - a protein involved in muscle contraction
  • Synonym: actin - a protein that forms part of the actomyosin complex

Etymology

From actin + myosin, the two proteins forming the complex.
