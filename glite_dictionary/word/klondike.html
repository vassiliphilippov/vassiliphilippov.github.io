Klondike

1. PROPN

/ ˈklɑn.daɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɒn.daɪk / (BrE)
A region in northwestern Canada, famous for the discovery of gold in 1896 and the ensuing gold rush.
region in NW Canada, site of Gold Rush

Examples

Thousands of prospectors flocked to the Klondike after the discovery of gold in 1896.
The harsh winters of the Klondike tested the endurance of all who traveled there.
Stories of the Klondike are still told as part of Canada's rich history.

Etymology

From the Hän language, tr'ondëk, meaning 'hammerstone water' (referring to the river).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɑnˌdaɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɒn.daɪk / (BrE)
a popular form of solitaire in which players build descending sequences of alternating colors with the goal of sorting all cards into suited piles from ace to king
a type of solitaire card game

Examples

He spent the afternoon playing Klondike on his computer to relax.
Many people learn the rules of Klondike before trying more complex solitaire games.
The goal in Klondike is to move all the cards into foundation piles in order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitaire - a card game played by one person

Etymology

Named after the Klondike region in Canada; possibly so called because the game's challenge was likened to the gold rush there.
