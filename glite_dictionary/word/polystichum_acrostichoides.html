Polystichum acrostichoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈstɪkəm ˌækrəˌstɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm ˌækrəˌstɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A North American evergreen fern with pinnate leaves and dense clusters of lance-shaped fronds, commonly known as the Christmas fern.
species of evergreen North American fern

Examples

The Polystichum acrostichoides provides excellent ground cover in shaded woodland gardens.
Many gardeners choose Polystichum acrostichoides for its resilience through winter months.
While hiking in Pennsylvania, she identified several clumps of Polystichum acrostichoides along the trail.

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (many) + 'stichos' (row), and 'acrostichoides' meaning 'like Acrostichum', another fern genus.
