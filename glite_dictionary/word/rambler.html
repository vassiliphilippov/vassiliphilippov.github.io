rambler

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈræmblə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys taking long walks in the countryside, often as a leisure activity
person who takes country walks

Examples

As an enthusiastic rambler, she spends most weekends exploring the hills around her village.
The club welcomes any ramblers interested in guided country walks.
He describes himself as a keen rambler who loves discovering new paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiker - person who walks long distances, often over trails

Etymology

From the verb 'ramble', which ultimately comes from Middle Dutch *rammelen* (to wander about).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈræmblə / (BrE)
a person whose speech or writing lacks clear structure and wanders aimlessly
person who speaks or writes in a disorganized way

Examples

The professor was a notorious rambler, and his lectures often lost the students' attention.
Her essay was marked down because she wrote like a rambler rather than staying focused on the main point.
As a rambler, he found it difficult to deliver concise and clear presentations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: digresser - someone who strays from the main topic
  • Antonym: orator - a skilled public speaker

Etymology

From English verb 'ramble' meaning 'to wander', plus '-er' agent suffix.
