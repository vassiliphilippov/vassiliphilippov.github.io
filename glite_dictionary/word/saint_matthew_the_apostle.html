Saint Matthew the Apostle

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmæθjuː ði əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmæθjuː ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
one of the twelve disciples of Jesus, traditionally believed to be the author of the first Gospel in the New Testament
disciple of Jesus; author of the first Gospel

Examples

Many churches are named after Saint Matthew the Apostle in recognition of his contributions to Christianity.
The Gospel according to Saint Matthew the Apostle is often cited for its unique perspective on Jesus' teachings.
In religious art, Saint Matthew the Apostle is frequently depicted with a book or scroll, symbolizing his role as an evangelist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matthew - the apostle and traditional author of the first Gospel
