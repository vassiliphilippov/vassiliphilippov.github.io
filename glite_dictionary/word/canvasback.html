canvasback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.vəsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkan.vəs.bæk / (BrE)
A North American wild duck (Aythya valisineria) known for its reddish head and prized for hunting and food.
a type of North American wild duck

Examples

While birdwatching, we spotted a majestic canvasback gliding across the lake.
The chef prepared a delicious meal using locally hunted canvasback.
Many hunters prize the canvasback for its speed and challenging flight patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

Named for the pale, canvas-like color of its back.
