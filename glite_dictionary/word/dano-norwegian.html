Dano-Norwegian

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪnoʊ nɔrˈwiːdʒən / (AmE), / ˌdeɪnəʊ nɔːˈwiːdʒiən / (BrE)
the form of the Norwegian language influenced by Danish, formerly used as a standard written language and one of two official languages of Norway
historical Norwegian language form influenced by Danish

Examples

Dano-Norwegian was used as the standard written language in Norway for many years.
Many classic Norwegian works were originally published in Dano-Norwegian.
The influence of Danish on Dano-Norwegian is evident in its vocabulary and grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bokmål - the standard Norwegian written language derived from Dano-Norwegian

Etymology

From Danish and Norwegian, combining 'Dano-' (Danish) and 'Norwegian.'
