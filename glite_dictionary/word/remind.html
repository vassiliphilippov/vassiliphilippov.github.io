to remind

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmaɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈmaɪnd / (BrE)
to assist someone during speech or performance by prompting them with forgotten or missed words
prompt by suggesting forgotten words

Examples

The stage manager was ready to remind the actor if he forgot his lines.
If you can't recall the next verse, I'll remind you.
She kept her script nearby to remind her in case she stumbled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - to supply forgotten words to a speaker or actor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879379-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɹɪˈmaɪnd / (AmE), / ɹɪˈmaɪnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to remember something; to make someone think of something they must do or have experienced
cause someone to remember

Examples

Could you remind me to buy milk on the way home?
This photo reminds me of our last holiday together.
She set an alarm to remind herself of the meeting.

Synset Examples

Remind me to call Mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to bring back a memory into someone's mind
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From re- (again) + mind; modelled after remindare in Late Latin.
