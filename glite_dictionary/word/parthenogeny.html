parthenogeny

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrθəˈnɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθəˈnɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the process of reproduction in which an egg develops into an individual without being fertilized, especially as it occurs in certain plants and animals; human conception without fertilization by a man
reproduction without fertilization
medical

Examples

Some scientists are exploring the possibility of parthenogeny in mammalian cells.
The lizard population exhibited parthenogeny, with all offspring being female.
If parthenogeny could occur in humans, it would revolutionize reproductive technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parthenogenesis - reproduction from an ovum without fertilization

Etymology

From Greek parthenos (virgin) + -genesis (origin, creation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrθəˈnɑdʒəni / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθəˈnɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the process by which an unfertilized egg develops into a new individual, especially common among insects and some other arthropods
development from an unfertilized egg
medical

Examples

Some species of ants reproduce by parthenogeny, allowing colonies to rapidly expand.
The study of parthenogeny has provided new insights into genetic inheritance in insects.
In laboratory settings, scientists have induced parthenogeny in certain amphibian species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parthenogenesis - reproduction from an unfertilized egg; a synonym for parthenogeny

Etymology

From Greek 'parthenos' (virgin) + 'genea' (generation).
