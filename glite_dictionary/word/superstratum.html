superstratum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈstreɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstreɪ.təm / (BrE)
A stratum or layer that is superimposed upon another, especially in geological or linguistic contexts.
a layer superimposed on another

Examples

A thin superstratum of volcanic ash covered the older sedimentary rock.
The superstratum of loanwords influenced the evolution of the local language.
Researchers analyzed each superstratum to determine changes in soil composition over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substratum - a stratum or layer beneath another

Etymology

From Latin superstratum, from super- (above) + stratum (layer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈstreɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstreɪ.təm / (BrE)
A language imposed by a later conquering or invading people on an indigenous population, which influences the native language by contributing features to it.
dominant invading language influencing natives

Examples

Latin was the superstratum in Gaul, shaping the local language that became French.
In English, Norman French acted as a superstratum after the Norman Conquest.
The influence of a superstratum can dramatically alter the vocabulary and grammar of the indigenous language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substratum - a native or previously existing language that influences an incoming dominant language

Etymology

From Latin superstratum, from super- (over) + stratum (layer).
