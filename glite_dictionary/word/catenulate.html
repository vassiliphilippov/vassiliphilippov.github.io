catenulate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈtɛn.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / kəˈtiː.njʊ.lət / (BrE)
having a form arranged in a chain or resembling a chain, especially of cells or structures
having a chainlike form

Examples

Under the microscope, the catenulate structure of these algae is clearly visible.
The bacteria formed distinctive catenulate colonies in the culture medium.
Some fungi exhibit a catenulate arrangement during certain stages of growth.

Synset Examples

catenulate bacterial cell colonies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catenary - a curve formed by a hanging chain or cable
  • Synonym: chainlike - having the characteristics or appearance of a chain

Etymology

From Latin 'catena' (chain) with suffix '-ulate', meaning furnished with.

2. VERB

/ kəˈtɛnjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈtiːnjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of biological structures) to arrange in a linked series or chain, especially of spores or cells.
arrange in a linked chain, esp. in biology

Examples

Under certain conditions, bacteria catenulate to form long chains.
Some fungal spores catenulate during reproduction, creating characteristic linked patterns.
The cells catenulated as they divided, making their arrangement easy to observe under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain - to connect or arrange in series, as with links in a chain

Etymology

From Latin catēnula (a little chain), diminutive of catēna (chain).
