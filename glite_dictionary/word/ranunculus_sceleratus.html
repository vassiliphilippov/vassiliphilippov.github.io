Ranunculus sceleratus

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs skəˈlɛrətəs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs skəˈlɛːrətəs / (BrE)
A species of annual buttercup that grows in marshy areas, known for its toxic properties.
a marsh-dwelling buttercup species

Examples

Ranunculus sceleratus is commonly found around pond edges and in marshy environments.
The leaves of Ranunculus sceleratus can cause skin irritation if handled without protection.
Botanists identified the unusual plant as Ranunculus sceleratus during their wetland survey.

Etymology

From Latin: 'ranunculus' meaning 'little frog' (buttercup genus), and 'sceleratus' meaning 'wicked' or 'poisonous'.
