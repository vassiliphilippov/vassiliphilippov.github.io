to alter

1. VERB

/ ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
to insert words into a text, often with the intention of falsifying it
insert words into a text, often falsifying

Examples

The editor tried to alter the manuscript by adding several unauthorized sentences.
If you alter official records, you may be accused of fraud.
Historians discovered that someone had altered the original document long ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to produce a copy of something with the intent to deceive

Etymology

From Latin 'alterare' (to change), from 'alter' (other).

/ ˈɔl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə / (BrE)
to become different in some way, but without losing one's fundamental nature or characteristics
to change partly but not entirely

Examples

His voice alters slightly when he's nervous.
