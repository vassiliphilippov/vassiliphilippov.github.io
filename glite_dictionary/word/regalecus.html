Regalecus

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡeɪlɪkəs / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪlɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, elongated deep-sea fish (oarfish) that is the type genus of the family Regalecidae.
genus of large deep-sea oarfish

Examples

The best-known species in the genus Regalecus is the giant oarfish, which can exceed 8 meters in length.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new specimen belonging to the Regalecus genus.
The elongated body and ribbon-like appearance are characteristic features of Regalecus.

Etymology

From Latin 'Regalecus', the genus name given by Cuvier in 1816.

2. NOUN

/ riˈɡælɪkəs / (AmE), / rɪˈɡælɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, elongated deep-sea fish in the family Regalecidae, known for including the oarfish.
genus of large deep-sea fish (oarfish)

Examples

The rare Regalecus was spotted by divers off the coast, sparking scientific interest.
Members of the genus Regalecus can reach impressive lengths of over 10 meters.
The oarfish, classified under Regalecus, is often mistaken for a sea serpent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - a very long, ribbon-like deep-sea fish of the genus Regalecus

Etymology

From Latin 'regalis' (royal), referencing the fish's majestic appearance.
