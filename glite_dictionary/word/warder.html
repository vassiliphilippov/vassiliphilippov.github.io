warder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdə / (BrE)
a person employed to guard prisoners in a prison; a prison officer, especially in British English
prison guard (especially British use)
british

Examples

The warder unlocked the cell door at dawn.
New recruits watched the senior warder on his daily rounds.
She thanked the warder for delivering the package to the prisoner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch, especially for protection or control
  • Synonym: prison officer - an officer in charge of prisoners
  • Antonym: prisoner - a person who is detained in a prison

Etymology

From Middle English *wardere* (one who guards), from Old Northern French *warder*, from Germanic origin.
