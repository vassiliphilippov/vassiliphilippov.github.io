socialist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates for socialism, a political and economic system in which the means of production are owned or regulated by the community as a whole
advocate of socialism

Examples

Her father was a devoted socialist who believed in workers' rights and public ownership.
The candidate introduced himself as a proud socialist during the debate.
Many socialists campaigned for better healthcare and education systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - a person advocating communism
  • Antonym: capitalist - a person who supports capitalism

Etymology

From French socialiste, from social (meaning 'social') + -iste (suffix forming agent nouns)

2. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪst / (BrE)
Advocating or relating to socialism or socialist principles.
advocating or relating to socialism

Examples

The new administration was described as socialist due to its policies on public ownership.
Critics claimed that the reforms were too socialist for a free market economy.
A socialist agenda emphasizes income equality and social welfare programs.

Synset Examples

socialistic government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capitalist - supporting or practising the principles of capitalism

Etymology

From French socialiste, from social (social) + -iste (-ist).
