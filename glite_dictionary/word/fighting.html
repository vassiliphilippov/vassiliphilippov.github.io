fighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of taking part in a physical struggle, conflict, or contest.
the act of physical struggle or contest

Examples

The ongoing fighting in the region has forced many families to flee their homes.
There was intense fighting between the two teams during the final match.
Fighting erupted when the rival groups met in the city square.

Synset Examples

a fight broke out at the hockey game
there was fighting in the streets
the unhappy couple got into a terrible scrap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combat - fighting, especially in war
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from fighting or war

Etymology

From fight + -ing, originally Old English 'feohtan'.

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of troops or forces) engaged in or ready for military or naval operations; active in combat.
ready for combat or military operations

Examples

The fighting troops were stationed along the border, prepared for any conflict.
This is a list of all the fighting units deployed overseas.
The country's fighting forces have been placed on high alert.
