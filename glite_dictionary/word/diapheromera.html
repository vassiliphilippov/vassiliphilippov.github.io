diapheromera

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæfəˈrɒmərə / (AmE), / daɪˌæfəˈrɒmərə / (BrE)
A genus of stick insects native to North America, known for their elongated, twig-like appearance used for camouflage.
a genus of stick insects

Examples

The diapheromera blends seamlessly among the twigs and leaves to evade predators.
Researchers discovered a new species within the diapheromera genus in a North American forest.
A diapheromera's slender body makes it almost invisible in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick insect - insect with an elongated body resembling a stick

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'diapherō' (to differ) and 'meros' (part).
