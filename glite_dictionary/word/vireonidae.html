Vireonidae

1. NOUN

/ vɪəriˈɒnɪdiː / (AmE), / vɪəriˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insect-eating American songbirds, including vireos.
family of small American songbirds

Examples

The Vireonidae are commonly found in forests and woodlands throughout the Americas.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of several species within the Vireonidae family.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot members of the Vireonidae during spring migration.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Vireo and the standard zoological suffix -idae for animal families.
