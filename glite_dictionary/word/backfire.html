backfire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an escape of gases and unburned gunpowder backward from a gun after it is fired
gases and gunpowder escaping backward from a gun

Examples

The old rifle occasionally produced a backfire when it was fired at the range.
A loud backfire startled everyone standing behind the shooter.
Improper cleaning of the barrel can increase the risk of a backfire.
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A loud noise caused by the explosion of fuel in the exhaust or intake of an internal combustion engine.
engine noise from fuel explosion

Examples

A loud backfire startled everyone in the parking lot.
The old car often made a backfire when starting up on cold mornings.
Mechanics say a backfire can indicate problems with the engine timing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a sudden violent burst, especially one causing a loud noise
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07423221-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A fire that is deliberately set to stop an approaching wildfire by burning off fuel in its path.
deliberately set fire to stop a wildfire

Examples

The firefighters set a backfire to prevent the main blaze from spreading to nearby homes.
A carefully controlled backfire helped contain the rapidly advancing grassfire.
Using a backfire is a risky but sometimes necessary tactic in wildfire management.
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
(of an engine or vehicle) to make a loud explosive noise, typically caused by fuel igniting outside the combustion chamber.
make a loud noise due to engine malfunction

Examples

My old car backfires every time I start it on a cold morning.
As the bus approached, its engine suddenly backfired, startling the people nearby.
You could hear the motorcycle backfiring as it sped down the street.

Synset Examples

My old car backfires all the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfire - fail to fire or ignite as intended (engines, firearms)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.faɪə / (BrE)
(of a plan or action) to have the opposite effect to what was intended, with a negative or unwanted result for the person responsible.
have an unintended negative result

Examples

His attempt to surprise her with a party could easily backfire if she finds out in advance.
The company's aggressive advertising campaign backfired and led to a loss of customers.
If you are not careful, your strategy might backfire and hurt your chances of winning.

Synset Examples

Your comments may backfire and cause you a lot of trouble
the political movie backlashed on the Democrats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfire - to fail to achieve the intended result

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'backfire,' describing a fire set intentionally to stop a larger fire or an engine misfiring; figurative use from early 20th century.

set a fire to stop another fire

7. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an action or plan that produces an unintended, harmful, or opposite effect to what was expected; a miscalculation that rebounds on its originator
an action with an unintended negative effect

Examples

His attempt to discredit his opponent turned out to be a backfire that damaged his own reputation.
The new policy was a backfire, making the situation worse instead of better.
Their marketing campaign was a huge backfire and lost them many customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomerang - an action that rebounds adversely on its originator
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result

Etymology

Originally from the automotive sense (engine explosion), extended figuratively to failed actions in the early 20th century.
