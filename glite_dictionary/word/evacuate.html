to evacuate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvækjueɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvækjueɪt / (BrE)
to move people from a dangerous or unsafe place to a safer location
move people from danger to safety

Examples

The government ordered all residents to evacuate the area before the storm hit.
Firefighters helped evacuate patients from the hospital during the emergency.
They had to evacuate quickly when the alarm sounded.

Synset Examples

After the earthquake, residents were evacuated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evacuate - to leave or withdraw from a place, especially for safety

Etymology

From Latin evacuare (to empty out), from ex- (out) + vacuus (empty).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈvækjueɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvækjueɪt / (BrE)
to move people from a place, especially their homes or country, for safety or protection, often because of danger
move people from a place for safety

Examples

Residents were ordered to evacuate the town after the river began to flood.
The government decided to evacuate thousands of people from the war zone.
They had to evacuate their homes due to the approaching wildfire.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to remain - to stay in a place, not move away

Etymology

4. VERB

/ ɪˈvækjueɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvakjueɪt / (BrE)
to remove air or gas from a container, such as a bulb or flask, in order to create a vacuum
remove air or gas to create a vacuum

Examples

The technician needed to evacuate the reaction vessel before adding the reactive gases.
Before coating the mirror, they evacuated the flask to ensure a contaminant-free environment.
Properly evacuating the bulb is essential for the experiment to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to degas - to remove gases from a vessel or material
