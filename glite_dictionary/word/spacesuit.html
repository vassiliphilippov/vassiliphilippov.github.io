spacesuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsˌsut / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsˌsuːt / (BrE)
A suit designed to protect astronauts from the harsh environment of outer space by providing necessary pressure, oxygen, and shielding.
protective suit worn by astronauts in space

Examples

The astronaut put on his spacesuit before exiting the spacecraft.
Each spacesuit is custom-made to fit its wearer perfectly.
A puncture in a spacesuit can be life-threatening in space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure suit - a suit designed to protect the wearer from low-pressure environments

Etymology

Formed from 'space' + 'suit', first used in the mid-20th century with the advent of space exploration.
