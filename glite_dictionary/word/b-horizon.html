B-horizon

1. NOUN

/ bi həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / bi həˈraɪzən / (BrE)
the layer of soil just below the A-horizon, characterized by the accumulation of minerals and organic matter leached from layers above
soil layer beneath the A-horizon

Examples

The B-horizon often contains more clay and minerals than the layers above.
Roots from deep plants may reach into the B-horizon for water and nutrients.
In well-developed soils, the B-horizon can be several feet thick.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A-horizon - the uppermost soil layer, rich in organic material
  • Degree Higher: C-horizon - the soil layer beneath the B-horizon, consisting mainly of parent material

Etymology

From scientific soil horizon classification; 'B' indicates its position as the second major soil layer.
