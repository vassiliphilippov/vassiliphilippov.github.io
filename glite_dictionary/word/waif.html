waif

1. NOUN

/ weɪf / (AmE), / weɪf / (BrE)
a homeless, neglected, or abandoned child, especially one who has been orphaned or forsaken
homeless or abandoned child

Examples

The little waif wandered the streets in search of shelter and food.
She took pity on the waif she found sleeping in the doorway.
During hard times, many waifs could be seen begging in the city squares.

Synset Examples

street children beg or steal in order to survive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents have died
  • Synonym: foundling - an infant who has been abandoned and found by others

Etymology

From Middle English *waif*, from Old French *guaif* meaning 'stray, lost property'.

2. NOUN

/ weɪf / (AmE), / weɪf / (BrE)
a homeless, neglected, or abandoned child, especially one who appears thin and fragile
homeless or neglected child

Examples

The waif wandered the streets, searching for food.
The artist often painted images of waifs dressed in tattered clothing.
A shy waif stood alone by the doorway, too timid to speak.

Synset Examples

The waif wandered the streets, searching for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stray - a person or animal that has no home

Etymology

From Old French 'waif' meaning 'lost, stray', of Germanic origin.
