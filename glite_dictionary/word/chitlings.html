chitlings

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtlɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtlɪŋz / (BrE)
the small intestines of hogs prepared and cooked as food, typically in Southern cuisine
hog intestines used as food

Examples

Many people enjoy serving chitlings with hot sauce and cornbread.
She learned to clean and cook chitlings from her grandmother.
The aroma of simmering chitlings filled the kitchen on New Year's Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chitterlings - small intestines of hogs prepared as food

Etymology

Variant of 'chitterlings,' from Middle English 'cheterling'.
