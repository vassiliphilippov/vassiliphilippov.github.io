ablepharia

1. NOUN

/ əˌblɛfəˈriːə / (AmE), / əˌblɛfɑːˈrɪə / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by the partial or complete absence of eyelids.
absence of eyelids (medical condition)
medical

Examples

The infant was diagnosed with ablepharia shortly after birth.
Surgical intervention may be required to treat severe cases of ablepharia.
Researchers are studying the genetic mutations that lead to ablepharia.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'blepharon' (eyelid) + '-ia' (condition).
