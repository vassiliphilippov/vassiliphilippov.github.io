Cistothorus

1. NOUN

/ sɪsˈtɒθərəs / (AmE), / sɪsˈtɒθərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small songbirds in the wren family, commonly known as marsh wrens.
genus of marsh wrens

Examples

The genus Cistothorus includes several species found across North and South America.
Birdwatchers observed a Cistothorus singing in the reeds near the lake.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the Cistothorus in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh wren - a small North American songbird of marshy habitats

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, constructed from Greek elements cis- (box) and thōros (chested), relating to chest shape.

2. NOUN

/ sɪs.təˈθɔːr.əs / (AmE), / sɪstəˈθɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small North and South American wrens, commonly known as marsh wrens, found in wetlands and grassy habitats.
genus of marsh wrens

Examples

The Cistothorus species are well adapted to life in wetland environments.
Scientists studied the behavior of Cistothorus populations in the marshes.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare Cistothorus singing among the reeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh wren - a small North American songbird found in marshes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kistos' (shrub) and 'thōros' (leaping/jumping).
