blastocoelic

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæstəˈsiːlɪk / (AmE), / ˌblæstəˈsiːlɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the blastocoel, the fluid-filled cavity of a developing embryo formed during early cleavage stages
relating to the blastocoel cavity
medical

Examples

The blastocoelic fluid plays an essential role in early embryonic development.
Researchers studied the blastocoelic pressure changes during gastrulation.
Abnormalities in the blastocoelic cavity can affect embryo viability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastocoel - the cavity inside a blastula during embryonic development

Etymology

From 'blastocoel' (from Greek 'blastos' meaning 'germ, sprout' + 'koilos' meaning 'hollow') + '-ic'.
