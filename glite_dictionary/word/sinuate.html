sinuate

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪn.juˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
having a margin or edge that is strongly wavy, with alternating inward (concave) and outward (convex) curves; especially used to describe leaves or shells
having a strongly wavy, alternately curved edge

Examples

The sinuate edges of the oak leaf distinguish it from other species in the forest.
Some sea shells have a sinuate margin that gives them a wavy appearance.
The biologist carefully drew the sinuate outline of the specimen's wing.

Etymology

From Latin 'sinuatus', past participle of 'sinuare' (to bend, curve), from 'sinus' (curve, fold).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnjuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuːeɪt / (BrE)
Having a wavy, curving form or margin, especially with alternate inward and outward curves.
wavy or curving in and out

Examples

The artist drew sinuate lines across the page to evoke a sense of movement.
Many leaves have sinuate edges instead of smooth ones, giving them a distinctive appearance.
The path took a sinuate course through the forest, winding gently between the trees.

Synset Examples

wiggly lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wavy - having a form or edge that smoothly curves up and down
  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends

Etymology

From Latin 'sinuatus', past participle of 'sinuare' (to bend, wind), from 'sinus' (curve, fold).
