Daoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊ-ɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ-ɪzəm / (BrE)
A Chinese philosophical and religious system founded by Lao-tzu and developed by Chuang-tzu, emphasizing living in harmony with the Dao (the fundamental principle underlying the universe), simplicity, and noninterference with natural events.
Chinese philosophy teaching harmony with the natural order

Examples

Many people are drawn to Daoism for its teachings on living a balanced and natural life.
He spent years studying the principles of Daoism and meditating in the mountains.
Some modern wellness practices are inspired by ancient Daoism and its emphasis on harmony with nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taoism - Alternative spelling of the same tradition, philosophy and religion

Etymology

From Chinese Dàojiā (道家) meaning 'school of the Dao (Way)', where Dao denotes 'the way' or 'path'.
