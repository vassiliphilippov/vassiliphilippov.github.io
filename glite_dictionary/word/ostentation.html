ostentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.stənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒs.tenˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of displaying wealth, knowledge, or possessions in a showy or pretentious way to attract attention or admiration
showy or pretentious display

Examples

His lavish party was an obvious example of ostentation and extravagance.
She disliked the ostentation of expensive clothing and preferred something more simple.
The mansion's gold-plated gates were pure ostentation rather than a necessity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showiness - the quality of being showy or flashy
  • Antonym: modesty - behavior that is humble and does not seek attention

Etymology

From Latin ostentātiōn- (showing, displaying), from ostentāre (to display).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.stɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒs.tɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of displaying wealth, knowledge, or achievements in a way that is meant to attract attention, admiration, or envy; showiness or pretentiousness
showy or pretentious display

Examples

Her constant display of expensive jewelry was sheer ostentation.
The party's lavishness bordered on ostentation, impressing and irritating the guests in equal measure.
He preferred simplicity and despised ostentation in both clothing and speech.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being humble and not showing off
  • Synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of trying to appear more important or clever than you are

Etymology

From Latin ostentatio (display, show), from ostentare, frequentative of ostendere (to show).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.stənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒs.tenˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of displaying wealth, knowledge, or achievements in a way that is meant to attract attention or admiration, often considered excessive or pretentious
pretentious display of wealth or showiness

Examples

Her ostentation at the charity gala was obvious, as she wore a diamond necklace worth a fortune.
Some critics accused the hotel of ostentation, citing its gold-plated lobby and elaborate chandeliers.
He avoided ostentation in his personal style, preferring simple clothes over designer labels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showiness - the state of being flashy or attracting attention
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being unassuming or moderate

Etymology

From Latin ostentatio (display, showing), from ostentare (to display).
