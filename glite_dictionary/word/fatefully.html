fatefully

1. ADV

/ ˈfeɪt.fəli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪt.fəli / (BrE)
in a way that suggests something important or inevitable will happen, often associated with fate or destiny
in a prophetically fateful manner

Examples

She looked at him fatefully, knowing their decision would change everything.
The lawyer paused fatefully before announcing the jury's verdict.
Clouds gathered fatefully on the horizon as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

the nurse whispered fatefully to call the priest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominously - in a way that suggests something bad is going to happen

Etymology

From 'fateful' + '-ly', where 'fateful' derives from 'fate'.
