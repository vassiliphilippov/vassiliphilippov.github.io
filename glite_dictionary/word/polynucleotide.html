polynucleotide

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈnuːkliəˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈnjuːkliətaɪd / (BrE)
a linear polymer consisting of many nucleotide units, forming part of a nucleic acid molecule
polymer of nucleotides in nucleic acids
medical

Examples

DNA and RNA are types of polynucleotide chains found in living organisms.
The scientist analyzed the sequence of the polynucleotide to understand genetic mutations.
Each polynucleotide strand is made up of a specific order of nucleotide bases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleotide - the basic unit of nucleic acids, consisting of a sugar, phosphate group, and base

Etymology

From poly- (many) + nucleotide (from nucleic acid and -ide).
