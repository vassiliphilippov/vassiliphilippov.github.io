catwalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkæt.wɔːk / (BrE)
a narrow, usually raised platform that extends into an auditorium, used by models at fashion shows or by performers during presentations.
platform models walk on at fashion shows

Examples

The models strutted confidently along the catwalk during the fashion week finale.
She dreamed of one day walking the catwalk in Paris.
The new designer's collection was the talk of the catwalk this season.

Synset Examples

models displayed clothes on a catwalk at the fashion show

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runway - a platform on which models walk to display clothes

Etymology

From 'cat' + 'walk', referring to the narrowness and the way models walk gracefully, like a cat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkæt.wɔːk / (BrE)
a narrow, often elevated walkway, especially one high above the ground in a theater, between parts of a building, or along a bridge
narrow elevated walkway

Examples

The stage crew moved the lights from the catwalk above the stage.
Workers must wear a safety harness when crossing the narrow catwalk over the factory floor.
During maintenance, the engineer accessed the pipes via a high catwalk above the machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkway - a passage or path for walking along, especially a raised one

Etymology

Formed from 'cat' (referring to agility) + 'walk'; suggests a place requiring careful, nimble movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02990060-n
Synset POS: n
