Turdidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːr.dɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.dɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of birds commonly known as thrushes, which in some scientific classifications is considered a subfamily (Turdinae) within the family Muscicapidae.
family of birds: thrushes

Examples

The family Turdidae includes robins, bluebirds, and nightingales.
Recent research suggests that some species formerly classified in Turdidae should be moved to a different family.
Many members of the Turdidae are known for their melodious songs and spotted plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - common name for birds in the family Turdidae

Etymology

From Latin *Turdus* (thrush) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
