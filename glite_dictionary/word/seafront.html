seafront

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈsiː.frʌnt / (BrE)
the part of a town or city that is next to the sea, especially where people go for walks or where there are hotels and entertainment
area of a town by the sea
british

Examples

The hotel has a stunning view overlooking the seafront.
We enjoyed a leisurely walk along the seafront before dinner.
Shops and cafes line the busy seafront during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfront - area of land along the edge of a sea, river, or lake

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'front', first recorded mid-19th century in British English.
