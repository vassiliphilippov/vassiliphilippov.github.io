tourney

1. VERB

/ ˈtɜrni / (AmE), / ˈtɜːni / (BrE)
(archaic) To take part in a tournament or contest, especially in jousting.
participate in a tournament (archaic)
archaic

Examples

Many knights would tourney for honor and glory in the king’s court.
He bravely tourneys against the greatest fighters in the land.
Though wounded, she tourneyed tirelessly to defend her lord's name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to strive against others to win something
  • Antonym: to retire - to withdraw from a contest or competition

Etymology

From Old French 'torneiier' (to joust, fight in a tournament), from 'tournoi' (tournament).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːni / (AmE), / ˈtʊəni / (BrE)
a sporting competition in which players or teams compete in a series of games to determine a winner; a tournament
sporting competition; tournament

Examples

The school chess club will host an annual tourney next spring.
After winning last year's tourney, the team is eager to defend their title.
Dozens of golfers participated in the charity tourney over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tournament - a sporting competition with a series of games

Etymology

From Old French 'tornei' (a tournament), from 'torneier' (to joust, to turn).
