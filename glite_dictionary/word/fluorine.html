fluorine

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊrˌin / (AmE), / ˈflʊəriːn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol F), a pale yellow, highly reactive, and poisonous gas of the halogen group
a nonmetallic chemical element (F), a toxic yellow gas

Examples

Fluorine is the most electronegative of all the elements, making it extremely reactive.
Scientists must handle fluorine gas with care due to its toxic and corrosive nature.
The mineral fluorite is a common source of fluorine in industrial chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a chemical element, another halogen, used as a disinfectant
  • Synonym: iodine - a nonmetallic element of the halogen group

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow, stream) + -ine. Named for its occurrence in fluorspar.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʊrˌin / (AmE), / ˈflʊəˌriːn / (BrE)
a poisonous pale yellow gas, the chemical element of atomic number 9, that is a highly reactive nonmetal and belongs to the halogen group
a chemical element, a reactive yellow halogen gas

Examples

Fluorine is the most electronegative element and reacts violently with most other substances.
Scientists handle fluorine gas with extreme care because of its toxicity.
To produce Teflon, manufacturers use compounds containing fluorine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a greenish-yellow halogen element, chemical element of atomic number 17
  • Synonym: bromine - a dark red liquid halogen element, chemical element of atomic number 35
  • Synonym: iodine - a dark violet-black solid halogen element, chemical element of atomic number 53

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow), from minerals in which it was found.
