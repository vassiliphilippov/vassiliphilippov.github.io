thioridazine

1. NOUN

/ θaɪəˈrɪdəˌziːn / (AmE), / θaɪəˈrɪdəziːn / (BrE)
a drug used as a tranquilizer to treat schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders
antipsychotic medication
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist decided to prescribe thioridazine to help manage the patient's symptoms.
Prolonged use of thioridazine may cause serious side effects, so careful monitoring is necessary.
After switching from another medication, she noticed that thioridazine helped reduce her hallucinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorpromazine - an antipsychotic drug used to treat schizophrenia; related class
  • Synonym: haloperidol - another antipsychotic medication used for similar disorders

Etymology

From thio- (containing sulfur) and phenothiazine (a family of antipsychotic drugs).
