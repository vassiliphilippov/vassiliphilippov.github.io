Saxegothea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈɡoʊθiə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈɡəʊθɪə / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the family Podocarpaceae, consisting of one species, Prince Albert's yew, native to South America.
genus of South American conifer (Prince Albert's yew)

Examples

Saxegothea is native to the temperate forests of southern Chile and Argentina.
The Saxegothea genus contains only a single living species, Prince Albert's yew.
Botanists are studying the conservation status of Saxegothea due to its limited natural range.

Etymology

Named in honor of Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha.
