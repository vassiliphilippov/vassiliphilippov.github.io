ironmongery

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrnˌmʌŋɡəri / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌmʌŋɡəri / (BrE)
articles and hardware made of metal, especially those sold in an ironmonger's shop, such as tools, locks, and fittings
hardware goods, especially metal items
british

Examples

The old shop specialized in ironmongery and household tools.
He browsed the shelves of ironmongery looking for a new door handle.
Victorian homes often feature decorative ironmongery on their doors and gates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardware - tools and metal goods sold in hardware stores

Etymology

From ironmonger (dealer in iron goods) + -y (denoting types of goods or business).
