Zigadenus venenosus gramineus

1. NOUN

/ zɪˌɡædənəs vəˌniːnəsəs ɡrəˈmiːniəs / (AmE), / zɪˌɡædənəs vɪˌniːnəsəs ɡræˈmiːnɪəs / (BrE)
A subspecies of death camas, a poisonous plant found in western North America to Mexico, especially dangerous to grazing animals.
poisonous North American plant subspecies

Examples

Zigadenus venenosus gramineus grows in open grasslands and is often mistaken for edible plants.
Cattle can become seriously ill after ingesting Zigadenus venenosus gramineus while grazing.
Botanists warn about the dangers of Zigadenus venenosus gramineus to livestock in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death camas - a common name for several related poisonous plants in North America

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Zigadenus, species venenosus ('poisonous'), subspecies gramineus ('grass-like').
