Ajuga pyramidalis

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːɡə ˌpɪrəˈmɪdəlɪs / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːɡə ˌpɪrəˈmɪdælɪs / (BrE)
A European evergreen perennial plant with a low, spreading growth, commonly used as ground cover in gardens.
a European evergreen ground-cover plant

Examples

The Ajuga pyramidalis thrives in shady, moist woodland gardens where it easily spreads as a ground cover.
Gardeners often choose Ajuga pyramidalis for its attractive blue flower spikes in early summer.
In addition to its ornamental value, Ajuga pyramidalis helps prevent soil erosion on slopes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ajuga - a genus of low-growing flowering plants in the mint family

Etymology

From Latin 'ajuga' (the genus name) and 'pyramidalis' (pyramidal), referring to the shape of its flower spikes.
