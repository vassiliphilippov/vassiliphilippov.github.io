profanation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊfəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒfəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating something sacred with disrespect or irreverence; the act of making something profane
act of desecrating something sacred

Examples

The profanation of the ancient temple outraged the local community.
Many people were shocked by the profanation of the sacred texts during the conflict.
The ceremony was held to atone for the profanation that had previously taken place at the shrine.

Synset Examples

desecration of the Holy Sabbath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desecration - the act of violating the sacredness of something
  • Antonym: sanctification - the act of making something holy or sacred

Etymology

From Latin profanatio (desecration), from profanare (to desecrate)

2. NOUN

/ proʊfəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prəʊfəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating something sacred or worthy of respect with irreverence or disrespect; the act of degrading or cheapening something valuable
degradation of something sacred or respected

Examples

The profanation of the ancient temple outraged the local community.
He spoke against the profanation of cherished traditions for commercial gain.
Some saw the graffiti as a profanation of the city's historic monuments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desecration - the act of treating a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
  • Antonym: veneration - great respect or reverence, especially towards the sacred

Etymology

From Latin profanatio (the act of desecrating), from profanare (to desecrate).
