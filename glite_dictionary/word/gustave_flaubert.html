Gustave Flaubert

1. PROPN

/ ɡuːˌstɑːv floʊˈbɛr / (AmE), / ɡyːˌstav floʊˈbɛː / (BrE)
(1821–1880) French novelist known for his influential works such as 'Madame Bovary'.
(1821–1880) French novelist

Examples

The novels of Gustave Flaubert had a major impact on modern literature.
Many critics consider Gustave Flaubert's 'Madame Bovary' a masterpiece of realism.
