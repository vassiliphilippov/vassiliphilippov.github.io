Selkup

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.kʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.kʊp / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Selkup people of Siberia
a Uralic language of Siberia

Examples

Only a few thousand people speak Selkup in remote regions of Siberia.
The Selkup language is endangered, with efforts being made to preserve it.
She is conducting research on the grammar of Selkup.

Etymology

From Russian Сельку́п (Selkúp), from Selkup самолӄуп (səmolkup), meaning 'forest person'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlˌkuːp / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.kʊp / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of mixed Ostyak and Samoyed origin living in Siberia
member of an indigenous Siberian people

Examples

The Selkup have preserved many elements of their traditional culture despite external influences.
Researchers have been studying the language of the Selkup for decades.
A group of Selkups celebrated their annual festival in the region.

Etymology

From Russian Селькупы (Sel'kupy), the self-designation of the people.
