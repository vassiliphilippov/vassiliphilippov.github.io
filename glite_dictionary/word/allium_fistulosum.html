Allium fistulosum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm fɪˌstjuːloʊsəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm fɪˌstjuːləsəm / (BrE)
A species of onion originating in Asia, with slender bulbs and hollow leaves, commonly used as green onions.
species of onion with slender bulbs

Examples

Many Asian dishes call for fresh Allium fistulosum as a flavorful garnish.
The gardener grew rows of Allium fistulosum for use as green onions in salads.
Allium fistulosum can be harvested young and eaten raw or lightly cooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welsh onion - another name for Allium fistulosum
  • Synonym: green onion - a general term for young onions used for greens

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Allium' (onion, garlic), and species 'fistulosum' (hollow, tubular).
