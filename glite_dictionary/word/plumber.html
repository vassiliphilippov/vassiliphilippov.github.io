plumber

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to install and repair pipes, fittings, and fixtures for water supply and drainage in buildings.
person who installs and repairs pipes

Examples

We had to call a plumber to fix the leaking kitchen sink.
After the bathroom flooded, an experienced plumber arrived to replace the broken pipe.
Many plumbers work as independent contractors or for construction companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrician - person who installs and repairs electrical systems

Etymology

From Old French 'plombier', from Latin 'plumbarius' (worker in lead), from 'plumbum' (lead), originally referring to workers who used lead pipes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmər / (AmE), / ˈplʌmə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to install or repair water pipes, fittings, and related apparatus in buildings.
person who installs or repairs plumbing

Examples

When the bathroom flooded, we had to call a plumber immediately.
The plumber fixed the leaking kitchen sink within an hour.
Good plumbers are always in high demand during harsh winters.

Synset Examples

In emergencies, plumbers and firemen are our heroes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefitter - a worker who installs or repairs pipes, especially for industrial systems
  • Synonym: electrician - a person who installs or repairs electrical systems
  • Synonym: carpenter - a person whose job is making and repairing wooden objects and structures

Etymology

From Old French *plombier* (lead worker), from Latin *plumbum* (lead), as pipes were traditionally made of lead.
