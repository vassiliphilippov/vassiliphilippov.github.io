urethane

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈreɪθiːn / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪθiːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound that is an ester of carbamic acid, used in the manufacture of plastics, resins, and pharmaceuticals
a chemical ester of carbamic acid

Examples

The manufacturer uses urethane to produce flexible foam for furniture and bedding.
Scientists investigated the effects of urethane exposure in laboratory animals.
The lining of the tank was coated with urethane to prevent corrosion.

Etymology

From French 'uréthane', coined from 'ur-' (urine) and 'éthane' (ethane).
