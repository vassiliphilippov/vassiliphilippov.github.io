put-put

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊtˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈpʊtˌpʊt / (BrE)
a small internal combustion engine, especially one on a motorboat, characterized by its popping or chugging sound
small gasoline engine, often on a motorboat

Examples

The old fishing boat still runs on a reliable put-put despite its age.
We could hear the put-put of their little engine as they came into the harbor.
He repaired the put-put before taking his skiff out on the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outboard motor - engine mounted externally on a small boat

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by the engine.
