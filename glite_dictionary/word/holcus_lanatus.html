Holcus lanatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑlkəs ləˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒlkəs ləˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A tall perennial grass native to Europe, with a velvety stem, often naturalized in North America and used as forage.
a species of tall, velvety grass

Examples

Holcus lanatus can quickly take over meadows due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
Farmers sometimes use Holcus lanatus as forage for cattle, especially in wetter climates.
The soft, velvety texture of Holcus lanatus stems makes it easy to distinguish from other grasses.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Holcus, species lanatus (meaning 'woolly'), describing its downy stems.
