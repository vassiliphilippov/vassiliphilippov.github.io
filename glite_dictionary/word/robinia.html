Robinia

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɪniə / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous flowering trees and shrubs, including species such as the black locust, native to North America and commonly planted elsewhere as ornamentals or shade trees
genus of flowering trees and shrubs

Examples

The Robinia in our garden is covered in clusters of fragrant white flowers every spring.
Many city parks feature Robinia trees because they provide ample shade and are resistant to pollution.
Botanists studied the rapid growth of Robinia species in new environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust tree - a common name for trees in the genus Robinia

Etymology

Named after Jean Robin (1550–1629), a French botanist.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɪniə / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous flowering trees and shrubs in the pea family, native to North America, including the black locust.
genus of deciduous flowering trees and shrubs

Examples

The garden features several beautiful Robinia trees that bloom in late spring.
Many species within the genus Robinia are valued for their hard, durable wood.
The leaves of Robinia are compound and often have attractive, fragrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust tree - common name for trees of the genus Robinia

Etymology

Named after Jean Robin, a French botanist (1550–1629) who first cultivated the plant in Europe.
