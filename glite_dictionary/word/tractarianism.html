Tractarianism

1. NOUN

/ trækˈtɛri.əˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / trækˈtɛəri.ənɪzəm / (BrE)
the religious principles and doctrines associated with the Oxford Movement, especially as set forth in the 'Tracts for the Times' published in the early 19th century
principles of the Oxford Movement

Examples

Many clergymen in the nineteenth century were influenced by Tractarianism and sought to revive traditional rituals.
The debates over Tractarianism had a profound impact on the Anglican Church.
He dedicated his thesis to the study of Tractarianism and its role in shaping English religious thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oxford Movement - a 19th-century movement within the Church of England to revive Catholic doctrines and practices

Etymology

From 'Tractarian' (a writer of the 'Tracts for the Times') + '-ism'.
