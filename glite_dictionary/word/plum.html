plum

1. NOUN

/ plʌm / (AmE), / plʌm / (BrE)
a small, round or oval fruit with smooth skin, sweet flesh, and a single hard stone, produced by various trees of the genus Prunus.
a small sweet fruit with a pit

Examples

She picked a fresh plum from the tree and bit into its juicy flesh.
The market was offering ripe plums at a very reasonable price this week.
For dessert, he served a delicious tart filled with plums and cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apricot - a soft, round, orange-yellow fruit with a stone
  • Synonym: peach - a round, juicy fruit with fuzzy skin and a stone

Etymology

From Old English 'plūme', from Latin 'prunum' (plum).

5. NOUN

/ plʌm / (AmE), / plʌm / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Prunus that produces edible, oval fruit with smooth skin and a single hard stone.
tree producing edible fruit (plums)

Examples

The plum in our backyard bears fruit every summer.
Farmers graft different varieties of plum onto strong rootstocks to improve resilience.
A mature plum may reach a height of fifteen feet if not pruned regularly.

Related Terms
