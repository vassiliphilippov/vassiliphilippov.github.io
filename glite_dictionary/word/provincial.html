provincial

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɪnʃəl / (AmE), / prəˈvɪnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to a province or administrative region of a country, especially in matters of government or administration.
relating to a province or its affairs

Examples

The new policy will be implemented at the provincial level across the country.
She works for the provincial government in Alberta.
Many provincial capitals are cultural and economic centers for their regions.

Synset Examples

provincial government

Etymology

From Latin provincialis (of a province), from provincia (province).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02796680-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ prəˈvɪnʃl̩ / (AmE), / prəˈvɪn.ʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of people from the provinces, especially suggesting a narrow or limited outlook; unsophisticated or lacking in urban polish.
narrow-minded or unsophisticated (from the provinces)

Examples

His views were criticized as provincial and lacking awareness of global issues.
Many city dwellers considered her tastes too provincial for the metropolitan art scene.
He found the town charming but a bit provincial compared to the capital.

Synset Examples

deeply provincial and conformist
in that well-educated company I felt uncomfortably provincial
narrow provincial attitudes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - familiar with and at ease in many cultures; worldly
  • Synonym: parochial - having a limited or narrow outlook or scope

Etymology

From Latin provincia (province).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00639387-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪnʃəl / (AmE), / prəˈvɪnʃəl / (BrE)
a person from a province or rural area, regarded as lacking urban sophistication or narrow-minded
person from the provinces, often unsophisticated

Examples

He regarded the newcomers as mere provincials with little knowledge of the world.
The city was filled with provincials seeking work and adventure.
They accused her of being a provincial who did not understand urban life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urbanite - a person who lives in a city, especially one with a modern and cosmopolitan attitude
  • Synonym: countryman - a person from the countryside

Etymology

From Latin 'provincialis', meaning 'of a province'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10430413-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪnʃəl / (AmE), / prəˈvɪnʃəl / (BrE)
an official in a Roman Catholic religious order who is responsible for an ecclesiastical province, acting under the superior general
official in a Catholic religious order

Examples

The provincial called a meeting of all the superiors in the province.
After ten years as provincial, she was appointed to a higher office within the order.
Each provincial submits regular reports to the superior general in Rome.

Synset Examples

the general of the Jesuits receives monthly reports from the provincials

Etymology

From Latin provincialis, meaning 'pertaining to a province'.
