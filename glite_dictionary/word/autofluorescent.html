autofluorescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəʊfluˈrɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊflʊˈrɛsənt / (BrE)
emitting light by natural or self-induced fluorescence, without the addition of external dyes or stains
emitting natural fluorescence

Examples

Many algae species are autofluorescent under ultraviolet light, making them easy to observe in the laboratory.
The cells' autofluorescent properties interfered with the imaging experiment, requiring additional controls.
Some minerals are naturally autofluorescent and can be identified without staining.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fluorescent - emitting light due to fluorescence

Etymology

From auto- (self) + fluorescent, indicating fluorescence occurring naturally or spontaneously.
