leftover

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛftˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈlɛftˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a small portion or item that remains after the main part has been used or consumed; especially, food remaining after a meal
something remaining after the rest is used

Examples

After dinner, we put the leftovers in the fridge for lunch tomorrow.
She used the leftovers from the party to make a new dish.
There weren't many leftovers because everyone was very hungry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something
  • Synonym: residue - a small amount of something that remains after the main part has gone
  • Antonym: main - the principal part or larger portion

Etymology

From 'left' (past participle of 'leave') + 'over'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛfˌtoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛftˌəʊvə / (BrE)
(of food or other items) remaining after the rest has been used or consumed.
remaining after use or consumption

Examples

We ate the leftover pasta for lunch the next day.
He packed the leftover paint to use for touch-ups later.
After the party, there was a lot of leftover cake to share with neighbors.
