anthologist

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ænˈθɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who compiles or edits selections for an anthology
compiler or editor of anthologies

Examples

The poetry collection was curated by a renowned anthologist.
As an anthologist, she has introduced many emerging writers to the public.
Every anthologist brings a unique perspective to the works they select.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who prepares written material for publication

Etymology

From Greek anthologia (collection of flowers) + -ist (denoting a person).
