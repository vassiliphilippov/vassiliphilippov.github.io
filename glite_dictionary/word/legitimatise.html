to legitimatise

1. VERB

/ ləˈdʒɪdʒ.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪt.ɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make something legal; to give official or legal status to something
make legal; give legal status

Examples

The government decided to legitimatise the use of certain herbal medicines.
Some argue that relaxing the laws will only legitimatise dangerous behavior.
The act was passed to legitimatise foreign investments in the country.

Synset Examples

Marijuana should be legalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to legalize - to make something legal, especially by official approval
  • Antonym: to criminalize - to make something illegal

Etymology

From Latin 'legitimare' (to make lawful) via Late Latin 'legitimat-', combined with the English verb-forming suffix '-ise'.
