bona fide

1. ADJ

/ ˌboʊnə ˈfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌbɒnə ˈfaɪdi / (BrE)
genuine; real; not counterfeit or copied.
genuine; authentic

Examples

The offer appeared to be a bona fide opportunity for investment.
Only bona fide documents will be accepted as proof of identity.
The painting was confirmed to be a bona fide work by the artist.

Synset Examples

an authentic signature
a bona fide manuscript
an unquestionable antique
photographs taken in a veritable bull ring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentic - genuine or real, not false or copied
  • Antonym: counterfeit - made in imitation so as to be passed off fraudulently as genuine

Etymology

From Latin bona fide, meaning 'in good faith'.
