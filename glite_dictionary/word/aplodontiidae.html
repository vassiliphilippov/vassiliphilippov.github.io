Aplodontiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpləˌdɒnˈtiːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌæpləʊˌdɒnˈtɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of primitive rodents comprising the mountain beavers, native to western North America
family of rodents: mountain beavers
medical

Examples

The Aplodontiidae are considered the most primitive living rodents, represented today by the mountain beaver.
Among mammals, the Aplodontiidae family is known for having a single extant species.
Researchers studied fossil records of Aplodontiidae to understand rodent evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aplodontia - genus of mountain beavers

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Aplodontia plus the suffix -idae (denoting animal family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæp.loʊˌdɒnˈtaɪ.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌæp.ləʊˌdɒnˈtɪɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of rodents that includes the mountain beaver, native to western North America.
family of rodents (mountain beaver)

Examples

The Aplodontiidae family consists mainly of the mountain beaver found in the Pacific Northwest.
Scientists study Aplodontiidae to learn more about early rodent evolution.
Unlike most rodents, Aplodontiidae species have primitive anatomical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain beaver - a burrowing rodent of North America, the only extant species in the family Aplodontiidae

Etymology

From New Latin Aplodontia (genus name) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
