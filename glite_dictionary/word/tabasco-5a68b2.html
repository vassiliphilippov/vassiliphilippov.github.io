tabasco

1. NOUN

/ təˈbæskoʊ / (AmE), / təˈbæs.kəʊ / (BrE)
A type of very hot, red chili pepper that is usually long and thin, often used in spicy foods and sauces.
a very hot chili pepper

Examples

The chef added chopped tabasco to the salsa for an extra kick.
I accidentally touched my eyes after slicing a tabasco and it burned.
You can grow tabascos easily in a sunny garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - any hot pepper from the Capsicum family
  • Degree Lower: jalapeño - a medium-hot green chili pepper

Etymology

From Tabasco, a state in southern Mexico, where the peppers were originally cultivated.
