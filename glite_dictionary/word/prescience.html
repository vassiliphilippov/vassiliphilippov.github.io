prescience

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.si.əns / (AmE), / ˈprɛs.i.əns / (BrE)
the ability to know or predict what will happen in the future; foresight
the ability to foresee the future

Examples

The scientist's prescience in predicting the pandemic's impact impressed her colleagues.
Many admired his financial prescience during the turbulent markets of the 2000s.
With remarkable prescience, the author described technologies that would not exist for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foresight - the ability to predict or plan for the future

Etymology

From Latin praescientia ‘foreknowledge’, from prae- ‘before’ + scientia ‘knowledge’.
