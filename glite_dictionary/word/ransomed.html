ransomed

1. ADJ

/ ˈræn.səmd / (AmE), / ˈræn.səmd / (BrE)
having been freed or reclaimed by payment of a ransom
freed by payment of a ransom

Examples

After months in captivity, the ransomed hostages finally returned home.
The ransomed paintings were carefully restored to their rightful owners.
A ransomed prisoner usually faces a struggling reintegration into society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unransomable - not able to be freed by payment of a ransom

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'ransom', ultimately from Old French 'rançon'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈræn.səmd / (AmE), / ˈræn.səmˌd / (BrE)
(in religious contexts) Saved or liberated from sin or spiritual captivity, especially through the intervention or sacrifice of Christ.
saved from sin (religious)

Examples

In Christian belief, the faithful are ransomed by the sacrifice of Jesus.
She felt ransomed from her old life after her spiritual awakening.
Hymns often celebrate the joy of being ransomed and forgiven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redeemed - delivered from sin or its consequences

Etymology

From verb 'ransom', from Old French *raçon*, from Latin *redemptio* (a buying back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01454803-s
Synset POS: s
