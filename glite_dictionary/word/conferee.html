conferee

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfəˈriː / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a conference or formal discussion
member of a conference

Examples

The conferee raised an important question during the first session of the summit.
Each conferee was given a packet with the program schedule and a name badge.
As a conferee, she was expected to contribute to the final report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - a person sent to represent others, especially at a conference

Etymology

From confer + -ee, indicating a person receiving or involved in an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.fəˈri / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fəˈriː / (BrE)
a person on whom something, such as an honor, degree, or award, is conferred
person who receives an honor or award

Examples

Each conferee was introduced to the audience before receiving the honorary degree.
The university honored its distinguished conferees during the graduation ceremony.
As a conferee, she felt deeply grateful for the recognition of her work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipient - a person who receives something

Etymology

From confer + -ee, modeled after similar agent nouns (e.g., nominee).
