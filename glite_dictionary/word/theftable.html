theftable

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɛftəbəl / (AmE), / ˈθɛftəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of an object) especially vulnerable or susceptible to theft; easy to steal.
easy to steal; vulnerable to theft
informal

Examples

The unlocked bike was theftable and quickly disappeared from the rack.
Some electronics are much more theftable than others due to their size and value.
Leaving your bag unattended makes it particularly theftable in a busy airport.

Synset Examples

The car's features made it particularly theftable at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stealable - able to be stolen; susceptible to theft

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix -able to 'theft'; modelled after similar English formations.
