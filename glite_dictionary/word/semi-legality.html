semi-legality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmi lɪˈgæləti / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi lɪˈɡæləti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being only partially legal or of uncertain legality.
partial or questionable legality

Examples

The practice operated in a zone of semi-legality due to ambiguous regulations.
Many informal workers exist in a state of semi-legality, lacking the full protection of labor laws.
The export of rare plants was conducted under semi-legality, with authorities often turning a blind eye.

Synset Examples

Farmers who are allowed by the provincial administration to live in semi-legality in the forest reserves are even more vulnerable to the arbitrary exercise of government power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegality - the state of being illegal
  • Antonym: legality - the state of being legal or in accordance with the law

Etymology

From 'semi-' (Latin, meaning 'half' or 'partly') + 'legality'.
