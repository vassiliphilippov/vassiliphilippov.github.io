Ameiuridae

1. NOUN

/ əˌmaɪjʊˈrɪdəˌi / (AmE), / əˌmiːjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of North American freshwater catfishes, including bullheads and related species.
family of North American catfishes

Examples

The Ameiuridae are commonly found in slow-moving rivers and lakes across North America.
Several members of the Ameiuridae family are known as bullhead catfish.
Scientists studied the distribution of Ameiuridae species in the Mississippi River basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ictaluridae - family of North American catfishes sometimes regarded as including Ameiuridae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Ameiurus.
