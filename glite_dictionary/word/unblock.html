to unblock

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈblɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɒk / (BrE)
to make something, especially assets or resources, available for use after they have been blocked or restricted
make assets or resources available again

Examples

The government decided to unblock the frozen assets to help fund humanitarian aid.
After months of negotiations, the bank finally agreed to unblock his account.
Sanctions were lifted, allowing the company to unblock its overseas funds.

Synset Examples

release the holdings in the dictator's bank account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to make something free or available that was previously held or restricted
  • Antonym: freeze - to make assets or accounts unavailable for use, usually for legal reasons
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00270568-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈblɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɒk / (BrE)
to remove an obstruction from something so that it can function or move freely
remove an obstruction

Examples

The plumber arrived promptly to unblock the kitchen sink.
Doctors were able to unblock his arteries after the heart attack.
She used a special tool to unblock the printer when the paper jammed.

Synset Examples

the procedure unblocked his arteries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: block - to prevent movement or flow by obstructing

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + block.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01619364-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈblɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɒk / (BrE)
(in cards) to play so as to remove lower cards from a suit, enabling a partner to win with higher cards in a combined holding.
play cards to allow a partner to win tricks

Examples

You should unblock your suit now to avoid trapping your partner's high cards.
In the last round, she expertly unblocked the diamond suit to help her partner win.
Before leading the ace, remember to unblock any lower cards first.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: block - to prevent the play of high cards in a suit by holding onto lower ones
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01078005-v
Synset POS: v
