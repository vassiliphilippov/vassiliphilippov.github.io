protogeometric

1. ADJ

/ ˌproʊtoʊdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the earliest phase of geometric art, especially in ancient Greece (c. 1050–900 BC)
early phase of Greek geometric art

Examples

The pottery from the protogeometric period displays simple patterns and limited decoration.
Archaeologists uncovered a protogeometric vessel dating back to the 10th century BC.
The museum's collection includes several protogeometric artifacts from early Greek civilization.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: geometric - relating to geometry or geometric art, especially in reference to ancient Greece

Etymology

From proto- (first, earliest) and geometric, referring to the period before the mature Geometric style in Greek art.
