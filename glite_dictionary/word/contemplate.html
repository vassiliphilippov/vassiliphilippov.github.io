to contemplate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.təmˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.təmˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to look at something thoughtfully, especially while deep in thought
look at thoughtfully; observe in deep thought

Examples

She sat on the porch to contemplate the sunset in silence.
He would often contemplate her portrait for hours, lost in memories.
As the years passed, I began to contemplate my future more seriously.

Synset Examples

contemplate one's navel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consider - to think carefully about something

Etymology

From Latin contemplari, meaning 'to observe, consider'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02169256-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.təmˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛm.pleɪt / (BrE)
to think about something carefully, especially as a possible course of action
consider carefully; think about a possibility

Examples

She began to contemplate a career change after ten years in the same job.
He paused to contemplate whether moving to a new city was the right choice.
Before making a final decision, they all contemplated the possible outcomes.

Synset Examples

I contemplated leaving school and taking a full-time job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consider - to think about carefully before making a decision
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to

Etymology

From Latin 'contemplari' (to observe, consider) from 'con-' (thoroughly) + 'templum' (a place for observation, temple).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00705641-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.təm.ˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.təm.ˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to think about something deeply and for a long time, especially for spiritual or philosophical purposes
think deeply; reflect, often spiritually

Examples

She likes to contemplate the meaning of life during quiet moments.
After the ceremony, he sat alone to contemplate his next steps.
Some monks spend hours each day contemplating in silence.

Synset Examples

He is meditating in his study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meditate - to deeply focus one's mind for reflection or spiritual purposes

Etymology

From Latin contemplari (to gaze attentively, observe) from templum (a place for observation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00706154-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.təm.pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.təm.pleɪt / (BrE)
to think about something deeply and at length, especially in order to understand or consider it carefully
think deeply about something

Examples

She likes to contemplate the meaning of life while walking in the park.
Before making a decision, he sat quietly to contemplate all his options.
The artist would often contemplate his work for hours before picking up his brush again.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to consider - to think about carefully, especially in making decisions
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin contemplari (to observe, consider).
