scolder

1. NOUN

/ ˈskoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈskəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who habitually scolds or criticizes others, often in an annoying or nagging way
person who habitually scolds

Examples

The children dreaded being left alone with their scolder of a nanny.
Everyone in the office tried to avoid the scolder who was always criticizing others.
He was known as a constant scolder, rarely offering praise but quick to point out mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - a person, especially a woman, who scolds habitually
  • Synonym: complainer - someone who often complains

Etymology

From 'scold' (to rebuke or criticize angrily), with agentive suffix '-er'.
