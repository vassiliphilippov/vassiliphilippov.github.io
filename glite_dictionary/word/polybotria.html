Polybotria

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒliˈbɑːtriə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American ferns, usually terrestrial when young but growing as climbers (scandent) later in life.
a genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

The tropical forest was abundant with Polybotria and other unique ferns.
When young, Polybotria grows on the forest floor, but it eventually becomes a climbing plant.
Botanists discovered a new species of Polybotria during their expedition in South America.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'botrys' (cluster), referring to many clustered sori.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈbɒtriə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American ferns, usually terrestrial when young but climbing as they mature.
genus of tropical American climbing ferns

Examples

Several species within the Polybotria genus can be found in the rainforests of South America.
Young plants of Polybotria are terrestrial, but often become climbing as they mature.
Botanists have discovered a new variety of Polybotria in the Amazon region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'poly' (many) + 'botrys' (cluster, bunch), referring to the clustered sori.
