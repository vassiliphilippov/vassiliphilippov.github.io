realizable

1. ADJ

/ ˌriəˈlaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlaɪzəbl / (BrE)
able to be achieved, accomplished, or made real; possible to do or bring about
able to be achieved or made real

Examples

Achieving national unity seemed difficult but ultimately realizable with enough effort.
The team presented a realizable plan for reducing energy usage at the factory.
Their design is innovative, but is it actually realizable with current technology?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievable - able to be brought about or accomplished
  • Synonym: possible - able to be done or happen
  • Antonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done

Etymology

From real + -izable, based on Latin realis (actual) and -abilis (able to be).

2. ADJ

/ ˌriːəˈlaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being achieved, made real, or put into effect
capable of being achieved or made real

Examples

The project has several realizable goals that we can accomplish this year.
These improvements are only realizable if the budget is approved.
The committee discussed whether the proposed benefits were truly realizable in practice.

Synset Examples

realizable benefits of the plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievable - able to be brought about or accomplished
  • Antonym: unattainable - impossible to achieve or reach

Etymology

From 'realize' + '-able'; 'realize' from Old French 'realiser', from Late Latin 'realis' (actual).
