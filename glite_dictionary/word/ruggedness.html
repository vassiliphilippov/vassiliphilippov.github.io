ruggedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong and sturdy in form or appearance
quality of being strong and sturdy

Examples

The ruggedness of the old mountain climber impressed everyone at the base camp.
Engineers designed the vehicle with extra ruggedness to withstand harsh terrain.
Despite his age, his physical ruggedness allowed him to work long hours outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy
  • Antonym: frailty - the state of being weak or delicate

Etymology

From 'rugged' + '-ness', where 'rugged' comes from Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult, challenging, or demanding to do or accomplish
difficulty or demanding nature

Examples

The ruggedness of the mountain trail made the hike a real challenge for beginners.
She admired the ruggedness of the final exam, believing it tested true understanding.
Despite the ruggedness of the project, the team completed it ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

he assigned a series of problems of increasing hardness
the ruggedness of his exams caused half the class to fail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - the state or condition of being hard to do, understand, or deal with
  • Antonym: ease - the state of being easy or not difficult

Etymology

From rugged + -ness; 'rugged' from Middle English, perhaps from Old Norse *rugga* 'to rock'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being uneven or rough, especially in relation to land or natural features.
quality of being uneven or rough

Examples

The ruggedness of the terrain made it challenging for hikers to reach the summit.
You can appreciate the natural ruggedness of the coastline from this viewpoint.
Despite the ruggedness of these mountains, several rare plants thrive here.

Synset Examples

the ruggedness of the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - The state or quality of having an uneven or irregular surface
  • Antonym: smoothness - The quality of being even and without roughness

Etymology

From "rugged" + "-ness," with "rugged" from Middle English, possibly from Old Norse "roggva" (to be rough).
