Henri Matisse

1. PROPN

/ ɑnˌri mæˈtis / (AmE), / ɒ̃ˈʁi maˈtis / (BrE)
(1869–1954) A French painter and sculptor, leading figure of the fauvism art movement, renowned for his use of color and fluid draftsmanship.
French painter, leader of fauvism

Examples

The exhibition featured several original works by Henri Matisse.
Henri Matisse is celebrated for his use of vibrant colors and expressive forms.
Art students often study Henri Matisse and his influence on modern painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauvism - an early 20th-century art movement known for bold colors and brushwork

2. PROPN

/ ɑnˈri mæˈtis / (AmE), / ɒ̃ˈʁi maˈtis / (BrE)
(1869–1954) A French painter and sculptor, a leading figure of Fauvism known for his use of color and innovative approach to art.
French painter and leader of Fauvism

Examples

The vivid colors in Henri Matisse's paintings revolutionized modern art.
Many consider Henri Matisse the father of Fauvism.
The museum hosted an exhibition dedicated to Henri Matisse's later works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pablo Picasso - Spanish painter and sculptor, leading 20th-century artist, co-founder of Cubism
  • Synonym: Fauvism - Art movement characterized by vivid colors, of which Matisse was a leader

Etymology

From the French given name Henri and surname Matisse.
