to asphyxiate

1. VERB

/ æsˈfɪksiˌeɪt / (AmE), / æsˈfɪksieɪt / (BrE)
to deprive someone or something of oxygen, preventing normal breathing and potentially causing unconsciousness or death
cause inability to breathe; suffocate

Examples

The leaking gas threatened to asphyxiate everyone in the room before anyone could escape.
He tried to asphyxiate the fire by covering it with a blanket.
Some chemicals can asphyxiate fish if released into rivers.

Synset Examples

Othello smothered Desdemona with a pillow
The child suffocated herself with a plastic bag that the parents had left on the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air; synonym for asphyxiate

Etymology

From Modern Latin asphyxiatus, past participle of asphyxiare, from Greek a- (not) + sphyzein (to throb).

2. VERB

/ æsˈfɪksiˌeɪt / (AmE), / æsˈfɪksieɪt / (BrE)
to die or cause someone to die by preventing them from breathing oxygen; to suffocate
to suffocate from lack of oxygen

Examples

Without proper ventilation, chemicals can asphyxiate workers in confined spaces.
A plastic bag over his head nearly caused him to asphyxiate before help arrived.
Medical staff acted quickly so the patient did not asphyxiate during the operation.

Synset Examples

The child suffocated under the pillow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air

Etymology

From Greek 'asphyxia' (stopping of the pulse) via Modern Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360165-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ æsˈfɪksiˌeɪt / (AmE), / æsˈfɪksieɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to be unable to breathe normally, especially by obstructing the airways, leading to unconsciousness or death
cause inability to breathe

Examples

The victim began to asphyxiate after being trapped in the smoke-filled room.
Failure to remove the obstruction could asphyxiate the patient within minutes.
Some poisonous gases can quickly asphyxiate anyone who inhales them.

Synset Examples

The foul air was slowly suffocating the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air
  • Synonym: choke - to prevent someone from breathing by blocking the throat

Etymology

From Greek asphyxia (stopping of the pulse), from a- (not) + sphuxis (pulse).
