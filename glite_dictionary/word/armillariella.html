Armillariella

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌmɪləˈriːələ / (AmE), / ɑːˌmɪləˈriːlə / (BrE)
A genus of honey-colored fungi that grow in clusters, some species of which are edible when cooked but are often considered pests.
genus of honey-colored fungi

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with clusters of Armillariella after the autumn rain.
Some species of Armillariella are safe to eat when properly cooked.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Armillariella from infected trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armillaria - genus of fungi related to honey fungus

Etymology

Diminutive form of New Latin 'Armillaria', from Latin 'armilla' (bracelet), referring to the ring on the mushroom stalk.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːr.mɪˌlɛr.iˈɛl.ə / (AmE), / ɑː.mɪˌlær.iˈel.ə / (BrE)
A genus of honey-colored fungi, typically growing in clusters; edible when cooked but often regarded as a troublesome fungus due to its destructive tendencies.
genus of honey-colored fungi

Examples

The forest was infested with Armillariella, causing concern among local ecologists.
Many people collect Armillariella in autumn, but they must be cooked before eating.
Efforts to control the spread of Armillariella have been met with mixed success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Armillaria - a related genus of fungi, including the honey fungus, often considered synonymous with Armillariella

Etymology

From New Latin, diminutive of Latin armilla (bracelet), referring to the ring on the mushroom's stem.
