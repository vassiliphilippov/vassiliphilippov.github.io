incurability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪŋkjʊrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪŋkjʊərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to cure or heal.
state of being impossible to cure

Examples

The doctor discussed the incurability of the genetic disorder with the patient's family.
Medical researchers are constantly searching for solutions to diseases known for their incurability.
She refused to give up hope despite the suggested incurability of her illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incurable - not able to be cured or healed

Etymology

From Latin 'incurabilis' (not able to be cured) plus the suffix '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkjʊrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkjʊərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to change, especially in character, disposition, or habits.
impossibility of being changed (esp. habits or character)

Examples

Despite his many failures, the incurability of his optimism inspired those around him.
The incurability of her stubbornness made negotiations difficult for everyone involved.
Some admire the incurability of his cheerful outlook, even in challenging times.

Synset Examples

the incurability of his optimism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - The quality of being unchangeable
  • Antonym: changeability - The quality of being changeable

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + curabilis (capable of being cured).
