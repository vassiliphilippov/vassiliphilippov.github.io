spirometer

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrɑːmətər / (AmE), / spaɪˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the vital capacity and other volumes of the lungs
instrument measuring lung capacity
medical

Examples

The doctor used a spirometer to assess the patient's lung function after surgery.
Patients with asthma often take a breathing test using a spirometer.
A spirometer records how much air you inhale, how much you exhale, and how quickly you exhale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmometer - instrument for measuring the capacity of the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'spirare' (to breathe) + Greek '-meter' (measurer).
