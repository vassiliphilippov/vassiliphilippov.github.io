pyrites

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈraɪtiz / (AmE), / paɪˈraɪtiːz / (BrE)
any of several metallic-looking sulfide minerals, especially pyrite, often with a brass-yellow appearance
metallic-looking sulfide minerals

Examples

Most pyrites are found in igneous and metamorphic rocks.
The miners extracted several tons of pyrites during their exploration.
Pyrites can create acid mine drainage when exposed to water and air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - a common mineral, iron disulfide, also known as fool’s gold

Etymology

From Latin 'pyrites' (flint, striking fire), from Greek 'pyritēs' (of fire).
