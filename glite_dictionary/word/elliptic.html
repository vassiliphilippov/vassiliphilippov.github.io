elliptic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪptɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪptɪk / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other object) having the shape of an ellipse; oval.
shaped like an ellipse; oval

Examples

The elliptic leaves of the plant made it easy to identify.
This species has elliptic petals arranged around a central disk.
Most of the trees in the garden have elliptic foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - having a rounded, typically elongated, shape, like an egg

Etymology

From Greek elleiptikos (pertaining to falling short, elliptical), from elleipein (to fall short).

She admired the elliptic curve of the ancient amphora.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Greek elleiptikos, from elleipein (to fall short, leave out), relating to ellipse.

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪptɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪptɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) characterized by the use of few words, often so as to be difficult to understand because of omission of elements or explicit details.
using few words; deliberately obscure

Examples

The critic described the novel's style as elliptic, leaving much to the reader's imagination.
His elliptic comments often puzzled his colleagues, who wished he would be more straightforward.
The author's elliptic prose appealed to readers who enjoyed piecing together subtle hints.

Synset Examples

the dialogue is elliptic and full of dark hints
the explanation was concise, even elliptical to the verge of obscurity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concise - using few words; brief and to the point
  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • Antonym: explicit - stating something clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion

Etymology

From Greek elleiptikos (deficient), from elleipein (to fall short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00550249-s
Synset POS: s
