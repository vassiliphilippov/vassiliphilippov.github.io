pushback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃˌbæk / (BrE)
an act of forcing an enemy or opposing group to retreat or withdraw, especially by counterattack.
an act of forcing a retreat

Examples

The army's sudden pushback forced the enemy to retreat to higher ground.
Recent months have seen a strong pushback against rebel advances in the north.
The general ordered a coordinated pushback to regain control of the territory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing or pulling back forces in battle
  • Synonym: counterattack - an attack made in response to an enemy's attack

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'push back', formed by combining 'push' and 'back'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃˌbæk / (BrE)
resistance or negative reaction to a proposal, change, or action, especially from a group or audience
resistance or negative reaction

Examples

The mayor encountered significant pushback when she announced the new housing policy.
Despite initial pushback from staff, the company proceeded with the restructuring.
There was little pushback to the proposed schedule changes at the meeting.

Synset Examples

We received pushback from customers regarding the price increase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - opposition to something
  • Synonym: opposition - active resistance or disagreement

Etymology

From the phrase 'push back', originally meaning to resist or oppose, later developed into a noun in American English (20th century).
