carefully

1. ADV

/ ˈkɛr.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great attention, caution, or prudence.
with caution or attention

Examples

Please drive carefully when the roads are wet.
He placed the vase carefully on the shelf to avoid breaking it.
The scientist carefully recorded the results of each experiment.

Synset Examples

she ventured cautiously downstairs
they handled the incident with kid gloves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a careful or wary manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - in a careless or heedless manner

2. ADV

/ ˈkɛr.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows care or attention to detail.
in a careful, attentive way

Examples

She checked the contract very carefully before signing it.
He carefully placed the vase on the shelf to avoid breaking it.
The children listened carefully to the teacher's instructions.

Synset Examples

they watched carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a cautious manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - without thinking or caring about the consequences

Etymology

From 'careful' + '-ly', where 'careful' derives from 'care' (concern, caution) + '-ful'.
