cosmetic

1. NOUN

/ kɑzˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
A substance or preparation applied to the body, especially the face, to improve or change its appearance.
beauty product for the body

Examples

She always keeps her cosmetic bag with her for quick touch-ups.
The new law requires all cosmetic products to be tested for safety.
Many people use at least one cosmetic daily, such as lotion or lipstick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: makeup - cosmetics applied to the face to enhance appearance
  • Synonym: toiletry - an article used in washing and taking care of one's body
  • Antonym: medicine - a substance used for treating illness, antonym in the context of function (health vs. appearance)

Etymology

From Greek kosmētikos (skilled in arranging or adorning), from kosmein (to arrange) and kosmos (order).

2. ADJ

/ kɑzˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
intended to improve appearance rather than serve a practical or functional purpose
improving appearance only; not functional

Examples

The changes to the software were mostly cosmetic and didn't affect how it worked.
The company spent a fortune on cosmetic improvements to the building's facade.
He argued that the new policy was just a cosmetic measure and wouldn't solve the real problem.

Synset Examples

cosmetic fenders on cars
the buildings were utilitarian rather than decorative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorative - serving to make something look more attractive rather than being functional
  • Antonym: functional - designed to be practical or useful rather than attractive

Etymology

From Greek kosmetikos (skilled in adornment), from kosmein (arrange, adorn).

3. ADJ

/ kɑzˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
intended to improve or restore appearance, especially of the body, without affecting function; serving an aesthetic rather than a functional purpose
relating to beautifying appearance

Examples

He decided on a cosmetic procedure to reduce the appearance of scars.
The changes to the software were mainly cosmetic and did not impact its performance.
Many people use cosmetic products to enhance their appearance.

Synset Examples

cosmetic surgery
enhansive makeup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorative - serving only for appearance and not function
  • Antonym: functional - designed for or serving a practical use

Etymology

From Greek kosmētikos (skilled in adornment), from kosmein (to arrange, adorn)

Source: WordNet
