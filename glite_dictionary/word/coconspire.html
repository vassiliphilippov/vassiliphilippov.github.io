to coconspire

1. VERB

/ ˌkoʊ.kənˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.kənˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to act together with others in a secret plan, especially for an unlawful or harmful purpose
conspire with others

Examples

The two executives coconspired to fix the bidding process.
They allegedly coconspired with others to commit fraud.
The prosecutor argued that the defendants had knowingly coconspired to evade taxes.

Synset Examples

The two men coconspired to cover up the Federal investigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful act

Etymology

Formed from co- (together) + conspire, from Latin conspirare (to plot together).
