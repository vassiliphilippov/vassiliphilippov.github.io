procrastination

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌkræstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌkræs.tɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of delaying or postponing something, especially a necessary task
the act of delaying tasks or decisions

Examples

Procrastination often leads to last-minute stress and rushed decisions.
His habit of procrastination made completing projects on time very difficult.
She recognized that procrastination was holding her back from reaching her goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the act of making something happen later than planned or expected
  • Antonym: expedition - speedy action; promptness in doing something

Etymology

From Latin procrastinatio, from procrastinare (to postpone), from pro- (forward) and crastinus (of tomorrow).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌkræstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌkræstəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of delaying or postponing something unnecessarily, especially tasks that need to be done
the act of delaying tasks

Examples

Her procrastination led to a last-minute rush to finish the project.
Chronic procrastination can negatively affect both work and personal life.
Instead of tackling his chores right away, he gave in to procrastination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the act of making something happen later than planned
  • Synonym: prevarication - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer or action, especially to delay
  • Antonym: promptness - the quality of doing something without delay

Etymology

From Latin 'procrastinatio' (a putting off), from 'procrastinare' (to put off until tomorrow).
