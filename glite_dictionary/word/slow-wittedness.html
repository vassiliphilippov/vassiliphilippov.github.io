slow-wittedness

1. NOUN

/ sloʊˈwɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / sləʊˈwɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being slow to understand or mentally limited in intelligence.
lack of quick mental understanding

Examples

His slow-wittedness made it difficult for him to keep up with the rapid conversation.
The film gently mocks the slow-wittedness of one of its main characters.
Despite her slow-wittedness, she always tried her best to understand complex ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or excitement; also slow to understand
  • Synonym: stupidity - the quality of being unintelligent or lacking sense
  • Antonym: quick-wittedness - the quality of having a quick and resourceful mind

Etymology

Formed from 'slow-witted' + '-ness'.
