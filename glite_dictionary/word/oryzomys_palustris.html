Oryzomys palustris

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈraɪzoʊˌmɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪzəmɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
a hardy and agile rat native to grassy marshes in Mexico and the southeastern United States, commonly known as the marsh rice rat
species of marsh-dwelling rat

Examples

The Oryzomys palustris is commonly found in wetlands throughout the southeastern United States.
Researchers studied the diet of Oryzomys palustris to understand its role in marsh ecosystems.
Predators such as owls and snakes often prey on Oryzomys palustris in their natural habitat.

Etymology

From Greek 'oryza' (rice) + 'mys' (mouse), and Latin 'palustris' (of the marsh).
