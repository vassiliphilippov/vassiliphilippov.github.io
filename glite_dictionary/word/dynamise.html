to dynamise

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more dynamic, energetic, or effective
make more dynamic or energetic
british

Examples

The new manager hopes to dynamise the team by introducing innovative strategies.
Her passion for teaching dynamised the classroom environment, inspiring students to participate more actively.
They are planning to dynamise the company's image with a bold rebranding campaign.

Synset Examples

She was dynamized by her desire to go to grad school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - give energy to; invigorate
  • Synonym: to invigorate - give strength or energy to

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' (power), via 'dynamic' + '-ise' (verbal suffix).
