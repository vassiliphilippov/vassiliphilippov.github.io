extrication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of freeing someone or something from a difficult, tangled, or trapped situation
the act of freeing from entanglement

Examples

The firefighters' swift extrication of the trapped driver saved his life.
After hours of careful planning, the team completed the extrication of the hikers from the cave.
During surgery, the doctor focused on the safe extrication of the foreign object.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - saving someone from a dangerous or distressing situation
  • Antonym: entanglement - the state of being tangled or involved in difficulties

Etymology

From Latin 'extricatio', from 'extricare' (to disentangle).
