legislative

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪslətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the making of laws or the process of legislating.
relating to lawmaking or legislation

Examples

The legislative body voted to pass the new education reform bill.
Several legislative changes were implemented after the committee's recommendations.
The governor consulted experts before drafting the legislative proposal.

Synset Examples

legislative proposal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: executive - having the power to put plans or laws into effect (contrasting branch)
  • Antonym: judicial - relating to courts of law or the administration of justice (contrasting branch)

Etymology

From Latin legis lātīvus, from lex (law) and lātus (carried, borne).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪslətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the making of laws or to a legislature; composed of or pertaining to lawmakers or a lawmaking body.
relating to lawmaking or a legislature

Examples

The legislative process can be slow and complex in many democracies.
She works as an advisor to the legislative committee responsible for drafting the new bill.
A legislative body, such as a parliament, debates new laws before they are passed.

Synset Examples

legislative council

Related Terms

  • Antonym: executive - relating to the branch of government responsible for implementing laws

Etymology

From Latin legis lativus, literally 'law bearing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02840450-a
Synset POS: a
