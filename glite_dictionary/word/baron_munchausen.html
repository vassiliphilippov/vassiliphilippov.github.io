Baron Munchausen

1. PROPN

/ bɑːɹən ˈmʌnˌʃaʊzən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɹən ˈmʊnˌʃaʊzən / (BrE)
A German nobleman famous for telling wildly exaggerated and fantastical stories; his name is often used to refer to someone who tells outrageous lies or tall tales.
exaggerator; teller of tall tales

Examples

His tales were so unbelievable that everyone called him a modern Baron Munchausen.
The politician was accused of being a Baron Munchausen for making such wild claims.
He spun a story worthy of Baron Munchausen about fighting off pirates with his bare hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • Antonym: truth-teller - someone who habitually tells the truth

Etymology

From the name of Baron Hieronymus Karl Friedrich von Münchhausen (1720–1797), German officer and storyteller.
