propyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.pɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.pɪl / (BrE)
a monovalent organic group with the formula C3H7-, derived from propane, found in various chemical compounds
monovalent organic group from propane
medical

Examples

The compound contains a propyl group attached to the benzene ring.
Researchers synthesized a new drug by substituting an ethyl group with a propyl group.
Addition of a propyl side chain can alter the chemical properties of the molecule.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ethyl - a monovalent organic group with the formula C2H5-, derived from ethane
  • Degree Higher: butyl - a monovalent organic group with the formula C4H9-, derived from butane

Etymology

From propane + -yl, following naming conventions for alkyl groups in chemistry.
