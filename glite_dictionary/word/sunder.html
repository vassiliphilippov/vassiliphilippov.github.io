to sunder

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌndə / (BrE)
to break or split apart, especially by force or violence
break apart violently

Examples

Centuries-old traditions were sundered by the sudden changes in society.
The powerful storm sundered the ancient oak in two.
Their friendship was sundered by a bitter dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart
  • Antonym: to unite - to come or bring together

Etymology

From Old English *syndrian* (to separate, divide), related to German *sondern* (to separate).
