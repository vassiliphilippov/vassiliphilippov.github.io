halophil

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæləˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈhæləˌfɪl / (BrE)
a microorganism, especially an archaebacterium, that needs a salty environment to grow and survive
salt-loving microorganism
medical

Examples

The Dead Sea is home to several species of halophil that flourish in its salty waters.
Scientists isolated a novel halophil from the salt flats in California.
Understanding how a halophil survives extreme salinity can offer insights into microbial adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halophile - an organism that thrives in high-salt environments

Etymology

From Greek halos (salt) + philos (loving).
