addlehead

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.əlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈæd.əlˌhɛd / (BrE)
A foolish or muddle-headed person; someone incapable of serious thought.
a foolish or muddle-headed person
archaic

Examples

He dismissed the plan, calling its creator an addlehead who couldn't think straight.
No one trusted the addlehead to handle important matters in the company.
In the old novel, the village addlehead causes endless trouble for his neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: dunce - a person who is slow to learn
  • Antonym: sage - a wise person

Etymology

From 'addle' (confused, muddled) + 'head', referring to a muddled mind.
