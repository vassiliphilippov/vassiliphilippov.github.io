antipathy

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪpəθi / (AmE), / ænˈtɪpəθi / (BrE)
something or someone that is the object of intense dislike or aversion; something to be avoided.
object of intense dislike or aversion

Examples

Despite his best efforts, cats remained his greatest antipathy.
For some, public speaking is an antipathy that never fades.
Liver was always an antipathy for her, no matter how it was cooked.

Synset Examples

cats were his greatest antipathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Antonym: fondness - a strong liking or affection

Etymology

From Greek antipatheia, from anti- (against) + pathos (feeling).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪpəθi / (AmE), / ænˈtɪpəθi / (BrE)
A strong feeling of dislike or aversion towards someone or something.
strong feeling of dislike

Examples

She had a clear antipathy toward her new coworker, making collaboration difficult.
There was a long-standing antipathy between the two rival teams.
Despite their antipathy for each other, they managed to work together professionally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Antonym: liking - a feeling of attraction or fondness

Etymology

From Greek 'antipatheia', from 'anti-' (against) + 'pathos' (feeling).
