schedule

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒ.uːl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛd.juːl / (BrE)
a plan that arranges times for events or tasks to be done
a plan of events or tasks

Examples

My schedule is packed with meetings all afternoon.
She checked the train schedule to see when the next departure was.
We need to finalize the project schedule before next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timetable - a detailed plan showing events or times for activities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'schedula' (slip of paper, schedule), diminutive of 'scheda' (sheet of paper) from Greek 'skhedē' (tablet, leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒ.uːl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛd.juːl / (BrE)
a plan that lists the times at which particular events or activities are intended to happen
a timetable of planned events or activities

Examples

According to the new schedule, the train will depart at 7:30 every morning.
The conference schedule lists all of the sessions and their starting times.
She checked her schedule before agreeing to attend the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timetable - a table showing the times of events

Etymology

From Medieval Latin schedula (a strip of paper, a schedule), diminutive of scheda (slip of paper or tablet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06507319-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈskɛdʒ.uːl / (AmE), / ˈʃed.juːl / (BrE)
to arrange for something to take place at a particular time
arrange a time for something

Examples

We have scheduled a meeting for Friday afternoon.
The maintenance is scheduled to begin at 8 a.m. tomorrow.
She schedules all her appointments weeks in advance.

Synset Examples

I've scheduled a concert next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to organize or make plans for something to happen
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not happen

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *schedula* (a small slip of paper, label), diminutive of Latin *scheda* (sheet of paper), from Greek *skhedē* (a papyrus leaf).

4. VERB

/ ˈskɛdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdjuːl / (BrE)
to arrange for something to take place at a particular time; to plan the time and place for events
arrange a time for an event

Examples

We need to schedule a meeting for next week to review the project.
The surgery was scheduled early in the morning to allow enough recovery time.
She is scheduling interviews with all the candidates this afternoon.

Synset Examples

I scheduled an exam for this afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide in advance what you are going to do
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not take place
  • Synonym: to reschedule - to change the time of a planned event

Etymology

From Old French *cedule*, from Latin *schedula* (a small slip of paper).
