impairer

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeərə / (BrE)
a person or thing that impairs or makes something worse or less effective
one who or that which impairs

Examples

Excessive use of fertilizers can act as an impairer of long-term soil health.
Stress is often seen as a major impairer of cognitive performance during tests.
Noise pollution is a serious impairer of urban residents' quality of life.

Synset Examples

crops can be great impairers of the soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improver - a person or thing that improves

Etymology

From the verb 'impair' + agentive suffix '-er'.
