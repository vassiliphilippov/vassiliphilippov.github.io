snapline

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnæpˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsnæpˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A length of string coated with chalk used in construction to mark straight lines on surfaces by snapping the taut string against them.
chalked string used to mark straight lines

Examples

The carpenter used a snapline to ensure the tiles were laid in a straight row.
Before hanging drywall, she marked a guide with her blue snapline.
A quick flick of the snapline left a crisp, straight mark on the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalk line - a line made with a chalked string; synonym for snapline
