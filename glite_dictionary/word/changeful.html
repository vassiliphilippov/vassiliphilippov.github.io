changeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn(d)ʒ.fəl / (BrE)
Likely to change; having a tendency to undergo frequent or marked alteration.
prone to frequent change

Examples

The politician's changeful opinions made it difficult for voters to trust him.
Spring weather in the region is known for being especially changeful.
Her changeful moods kept her friends guessing about how she would react.

Synset Examples

changeable behavior
changeable moods
changeable prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: changeable - able to change or be changed; adaptable
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change; steady

Etymology

Formed from 'change' + suffix '-ful'; attested in English since the 16th century.
