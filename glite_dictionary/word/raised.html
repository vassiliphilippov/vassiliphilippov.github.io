raised

1. ADJ

/ reɪzd / (AmE), / reɪzd / (BrE)
positioned or lifted above the surrounding area or normal level; elevated.
elevated above surroundings

Examples

The raised lettering made the sign easy to read by touch.
She placed the cake on a raised platform for display.
His raised eyebrows indicated surprise at the news.

Synset Examples

a raised design
raised eyebrows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elevated - lifted up; raised to a higher level or position
  • Antonym: sunken - situated or placed below the surface or the normal position
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪzd / (AmE), / ˈreɪzd / (BrE)
(of an amount, number, or degree) increased beyond the usual or normal level.
increased; above normal level

Examples

The doctor said my blood pressure was raised after the test.
A raised heart rate can be a sign of stress or illness.
Residents were warned about raised pollution levels due to poor air quality.

Synset Examples

raised temperature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowered - reduced in amount or degree
  • Synonym: elevated - increased to a higher level

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'raise', from Middle English 'reisen', from Old Norse 'reisa' (to raise).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈreɪzd / (AmE), / ˈreɪzd / (BrE)
(of a pattern, design, or surface) decorated with an elevated or projecting design, often created by pressure, embroidery, or similar techniques.
decorated with a projecting pattern

Examples

The invitation was printed on heavy paper with a raised floral pattern along the edges.
She admired the antique mirror with its beautifully raised silver frame.
The artisan created a raised design on the leather cover using traditional embossing techniques.

Synset Examples

embossed leather
raised needlework
raised metalwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embossed - decorated with a raised pattern, typically on leather or metal surfaces
  • Synonym: brocaded - richly decorated, especially with raised patterns in textile
