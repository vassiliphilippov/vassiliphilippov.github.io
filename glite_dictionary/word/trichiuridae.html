Trichiuridae

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkiːjʊˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / trɪˌkɪjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as cutlassfishes, characterized by their elongated, ribbon-like bodies.
a family of marine cutlassfishes

Examples

The family Trichiuridae includes many species of cutlassfish found in tropical and temperate oceans.
Members of Trichiuridae are recognizable by their elongated, ribbon-like bodies and sharp teeth.
Researchers have studied the migration patterns of Trichiuridae to understand their role in marine food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutlassfish - a marine fish with a long, slender, silver body

Etymology

From the genus name Trichiurus, derived from Greek 'thríx' (hair) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the fishes' long, slender bodies and tails.
