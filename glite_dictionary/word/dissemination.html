dissemination

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of spreading something, especially information, widely so that it reaches many people or places
the act of spreading widely

Examples

The dissemination of accurate information is vital during a public health crisis.
Recent advances have accelerated the dissemination of scientific research across the globe.
He played a key role in the dissemination of new agricultural techniques among farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the process of widely spreading or dispersing

Etymology

From Latin disseminatio (‘a scattering’), from disseminare (‘to sow, scatter seeds’).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of spreading information, ideas, or something else widely so that it reaches many people; the process of making a subject widely discussed and debated
the act of spreading or publicizing widely

Examples

The dissemination of accurate information is crucial during a public health crisis.
There was widespread dissemination of the new findings in scientific journals and the media.
Public dissemination of debate topics encouraged many more people to participate in the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulation - the act of passing something around widely, especially information or materials
  • Antonym: suppression - the act of preventing something from being known or circulated

Etymology

From Latin 'disseminatio', from 'disseminare' meaning 'to scatter, to sow'
