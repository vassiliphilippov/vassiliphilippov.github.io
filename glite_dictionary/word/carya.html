Carya

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːriə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large deciduous, nut-bearing trees, native to North America and East Asia, including hickories and pecans.
genus of nut-bearing trees (hickories)

Examples

The genus Carya includes all types of hickory trees found in North America.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Carya genus in eastern China.
The Carya trees are valued both for their wood and their edible nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a common tree of the genus Carya; produces edible nuts

Etymology

From Greek kárya (walnut tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈker.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large deciduous nut-bearing trees, commonly known as hickories, native to the United States and China.
genus of nut-bearing trees (hickories)

Examples

The wood of trees in the Carya genus is prized for its strength and hardness.
Several species within Carya produce edible nuts enjoyed by wildlife and humans.
The distribution of Carya includes both North America and parts of East Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - tree of the genus Carya; nut-bearing hardwood tree

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *karya* (nut tree, walnut).
