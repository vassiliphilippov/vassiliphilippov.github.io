ennui

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈwi / (AmE), / ɒnˈwiː / (BrE)
a feeling of listlessness and dissatisfaction arising from a lack of occupation or excitement; boredom
boredom; feeling of listlessness

Examples

After a week of rainy weather and no internet, a sense of ennui settled over the group.
The novel explores the ennui experienced by young people in a small, sleepy town.
She tried painting to combat the ennui of her daily routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boredom - the state of being bored
  • Antonym: interest - a feeling of wanting to know or learn about something
  • Synonym: tedium - the state of being tedious or monotonous

Etymology

From French 'ennui' (boredom, annoyance), from Old French 'enui', from 'ennuyer' (to annoy).
