pod

1. NOUN

/ pɑd / (AmE), / pɒd / (BrE)
A detachable container on an aircraft, used especially to carry fuel externally.
detachable fuel container on an aircraft

Examples

During the long flight, the aircraft relied on an external pod for additional fuel.
The ground crew inspected the fuel pod for any signs of leakage before takeoff.
Engineers designed a new type of pod to increase the jet's range.

2. NOUN

/ pɑd / (AmE), / pɒd / (BrE)
a long, narrow outer covering of certain plants, such as peas or beans, that contains the seeds
seed vessel of a plant

Examples

The pea pod split open to reveal a row of green peas inside.
After the pod dried on the plant, the seeds could be collected and stored.
She carefully examined each bean pod for signs of ripeness.

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'podd', meaning 'bag' or 'pouch'.
