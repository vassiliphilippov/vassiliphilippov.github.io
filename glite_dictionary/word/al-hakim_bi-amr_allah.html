Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah

1. PROPN

/ æl hɑːˈkiːm biː ˌɑːmr ɑːˈlɑː / (AmE), / æl hæˈkiːm biː ˌɑːmə ˈɑːlɑː / (BrE)
(985–1021) An Ismaili caliph of Egypt who declared himself an incarnation of God and was a central figure in the founding of the Druze religious sect.
Ismaili caliph of Egypt, Druze founder

Examples

The rule of Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah marked a period of radical changes in Egyptian society.
Many stories surround the mysterious disappearance of Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah in 1021.
The Druze faith recognizes Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah as a key religious figure.

Etymology

From Arabic ‘al-Ḥākim bi-Amr Allāh’ meaning 'Ruler by God's Command'.
