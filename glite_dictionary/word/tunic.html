tunic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.nɪk / (BrE)
a membrane or layer of tissue that covers or envelops an organ, part, or structure in the body
a covering membrane or tissue layer
medical

Examples

The cornea consists of several tunics with different functions.
The vascular tunic of the eye contains many blood vessels.
Each artery is composed of three tunics that protect and support it.

Etymology

From Latin tunica (a covering, garment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.nɪk / (BrE)
a loose-fitting garment, typically sleeveless and reaching to the hips or knees, worn as an outer garment in ancient times or as part of modern uniforms and fashion
loose outer garment reaching hips or knees

Examples

The Roman soldier wore a red tunic beneath his armor.
She chose a blue linen tunic to wear over her jeans for a casual look.
In the ceremony, all the children wore a traditional white tunic with a colored sash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment

Etymology

From Latin tunica (a garment worn by the Romans).
