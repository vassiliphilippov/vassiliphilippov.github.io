comforter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətə / (BrE)
a thick bedding quilt made of two layers of cloth with insulating filling stitched between them, used as a bed covering
quilted bed covering with filling

Examples

He pulled the comforter up to his chin and quickly fell asleep.
The floral comforter added a splash of color to the bedroom.
She replaced her old blanket with a new down-filled comforter for extra warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duvet - a soft quilt filled with down or synthetic fiber, used instead of an upper sheet and blankets
  • Degree Lower: blanket - a large piece of soft cloth used to cover oneself for warmth

Etymology

From Old French 'conforter' (to comfort), from Late Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərtɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətə / (BrE)
a device, usually made of rubber or plastic, given to an infant to suck or bite on for comfort; a pacifier.
an infant's device to suck or bite on
british

Examples

The baby calmed down as soon as she was given her comforter.
Please make sure the comforter is clean before handing it to the infant.
Some parents prefer not to use a comforter to avoid dependency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifier - an artificial nipple for babies

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətə / (BrE)
a person who provides comfort or consolation to someone who is distressed or anxious
person who gives comfort or support

Examples

Her mother was always a gentle comforter during difficult times.
He became a trusted comforter to his grieving friends.
As a comforter, she had a special way of calming everyone's fears.

Synset Examples

a reliever of anxiety
an allayer of fears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consoler - a person who consoles; gives comfort or sympathy
  • Synonym: reassurer - a person who reassures, making someone feel less worried

Etymology

From Middle English comfortour, from Old French conforteur, from conforteur (‘one who comforts’).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfərtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətə / (BrE)
a person who offers sympathy and support to someone who is distressed or has suffered misfortune
person who gives comfort or sympathy

Examples

After hearing the tragic news, Susan became a trusted comforter to her grieving friend.
He found a comforter in his neighbor, who listened patiently to his troubles.
During difficult times, a sincere comforter can make all the difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consoler - a person who consoles someone
  • Synonym: sympathizer - a person who expresses sympathy
  • Antonym: tormentor - a person who inflicts pain, often opposed in meaning to one who comforts

Etymology

From Middle English *comforten* (to comfort) and suffix *-er* forming agent nouns.
