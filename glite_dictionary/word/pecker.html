pecker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛkər / (AmE), / ˈpɛkə / (BrE)
the beak or bill of a bird; a bird's projecting horny mouthpart
bird's beak or bill

Examples

The woodpecker used its pecker to drill holes in the tree bark.
The robin wiped its pecker clean after eating a worm.
Some birds have a long, slender pecker adapted for reaching nectar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beak - the hard, pointed mouthpart of a bird

Etymology

From 'peck' (to strike with the beak) + -er.
