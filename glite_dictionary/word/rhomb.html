rhomb

1. NOUN

/ rɑm / (AmE), / rɒm / (BrE)
a parallelogram with four equal sides and oblique angles; an equilateral parallelogram that is not a square
oblique-angled parallelogram with equal sides

Examples

The floor was decorated with a pattern of interlocking rhombs in different colors.
A rhomb differs from a square in that its angles are not right angles.
In geometry class, we learned that a rhomb is also called a diamond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhombus - a parallelogram with four equal sides; may refer to the same shape as rhomb
  • Degree Higher: square - a parallelogram with four equal sides and right angles

Etymology

From Greek *rhombos* (spinning top, rhombus).
