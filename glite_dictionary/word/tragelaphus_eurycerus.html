Tragelaphus eurycerus

1. NOUN

/ træˈɡɛləfəs ˌjʊriˈsɪrəs / (AmE), / trəˈɡiːləfəs ˌjʊərɪˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
a large forest antelope from central Africa, known for its reddish-brown coat with white stripes and long spiral horns
central African striped forest antelope

Examples

The Tragelaphus eurycerus is often called the bongo and is notable for its striking appearance.
Conservationists are working to protect Tragelaphus eurycerus due to habitat loss in Central Africa.
A Tragelaphus eurycerus can be recognized by its white vertical stripes and spiraled horns.

Etymology

From New Latin genus Tragelaphus + species eurycerus, from Greek 'eury-' (broad) and 'keras' (horn).
