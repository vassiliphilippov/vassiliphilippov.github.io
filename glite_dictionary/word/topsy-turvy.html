topsy-turvy

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɑpsi ˈtɝːvi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvi / (BrE)
in a state of utter disorder or confusion; upside down or in complete disarray
in complete disorder or confusion

Examples

After the move, the entire living room was topsy-turvy for days.
The child's sudden tantrum turned the peaceful afternoon topsy-turvy.
She opened the closet to find her clothes in a topsy-turvy heap.

Synset Examples

a disorderly pile of clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorderly - lacking organization or order
  • Synonym: chaotic - completely disorganized or confused
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized neatly

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from dialectal English or playful rhyming reduplication during the 16th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˌtɑpsi ˈtɝvi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvi / (BrE)
in a state of confusion, disorder, or disarray; upside down or chaotic
in a state of confusion or disorder

Examples

After the move, our new house was completely topsy-turvy with boxes everywhere.
Her thoughts felt topsy-turvy after hearing the unexpected news.
The sudden changes in policy left the company operations topsy-turvy.

Synset Examples

Everything felt topsy-turvy after the sudden storm.
His room was always topsy-turvy, with clothes everywhere.
The election results left the city feeling topsy-turvy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - completely disordered or confused
  • Antonym: orderly - well-arranged or organized; in order

Etymology

Originated in the mid-16th century, probably from the phrase 'top over (the) turvy' (an old dialect word for 'upside-down').
