therapist

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɛɹəpɪst / (AmE), / ˈθɛːrəpɪst / (BrE)
a person trained and qualified to practise a particular type of therapy, especially in mental or physical health
person trained to provide therapy

Examples

She spoke with her therapist once a week to manage her anxiety.
After his injury, the athlete worked closely with a physical therapist to recover.
A good therapist listens attentively and offers helpful guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counselor - a professional who gives advice and support, especially concerning personal or psychological problems
  • Antonym: patient - a person receiving medical care or treatment

Etymology

From Greek therapeutes (an attendant or one who treats), via French thérapeute.
