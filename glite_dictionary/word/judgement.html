judgement

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a legal document that states the reasons for a court's decision in a case
court document explaining a legal decision
legal

Examples

The court issued its judgement after several days of testimony and deliberation.
A copy of the final judgement was sent to all parties involved in the case.
In his written judgement, the judge explained the reasons behind the verdict.

Synset Examples

opinions are usually written by a single judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a formal statement by a judge, especially the explanation for a legal decision
  • Synonym: decree - an official order issued by a legal authority

Etymology

From Old French *jugement*, from Latin *judicium* (judgement).
