cedarwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.dɚ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈsiː.də.wʊd / (BrE)
the durable, aromatic wood of trees of the cedar genus, often used for chests, closets, and furniture because of its fragrance and resistance to decay
aromatic, durable wood from cedar trees

Examples

The closet was lined with cedarwood to keep moths away from clothing.
She bought a beautiful cedarwood chest as a wedding present.
The pleasant aroma of cedarwood filled the workshop as he sanded the planks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees yielding fragrant wood

Etymology

Compound of 'cedar' (from Old French cedre, from Latin cedrus, from Greek kedros) and 'wood'.
