Brahma

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmə / (BrE)
a large breed of heat- and tick-resistant humped cattle, originally developed in the U.S. by crossbreeding Indian cattle, used mainly for crossbreeding and beef production
a breed of heat-resistant cattle

Examples

The rancher decided to breed Brahma cattle because of their resistance to heat and ticks.
At the livestock show, the prize-winning bull was a massive Brahma.
Many farmers cross Brahma with other cattle breeds to improve hardiness and meat quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brahman - a breed of zebu cattle originating from India, used in the development of Brahma cattle

Etymology

Named after Brahman cattle, from the Sanskrit 'Brahman' (a member of the priestly caste) due to their Indian origin.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmə / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the Creator god, one of the three major deities in the Hindu pantheon, alongside Vishnu and Shiva.
the Creator god in Hinduism

Examples

Many devotees worship Brahma as the creator of the universe.
Brahma is often depicted with four faces, each reciting one of the Vedas.
Unlike Vishnu and Shiva, there are very few temples dedicated solely to Brahma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vishnu - the Preserver god, another principal deity in Hinduism
  • Synonym: Shiva - the Destroyer god, a principal deity in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit *Brahmā*, name of the Creator god.
