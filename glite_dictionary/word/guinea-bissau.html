Guinea-Bissau

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡɪni bɪˈsaʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡɪni bɪˈsaʊ / (BrE)
A country on the northwestern coast of Africa, recognized as independent from Portugal in 1974.
country in West Africa

Examples

She traveled to Guinea-Bissau to study its unique wildlife.
After gaining independence in 1974, Guinea-Bissau became a member of the United Nations.
The official language of Guinea-Bissau is Portuguese.

Etymology

From the name of the region 'Guinea' and its capital 'Bissau' to distinguish it from neighboring Guinea.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɡɪni bɪˈsaʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡɪni bɪˈsaʊ / (BrE)
A country in West Africa, on the Atlantic coast, recognized as independent from Portugal in 1974.
country in West Africa

Examples

She traveled to Guinea-Bissau to conduct research for her thesis on African politics.
The official language of Guinea-Bissau is Portuguese, although various African languages are widely spoken.
After gaining independence, Guinea-Bissau became a member of the United Nations.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Guiné-Bissau', with 'Bissau' added to distinguish it from neighboring Guinea.
