roughage

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌf.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
Coarse, indigestible plant material in food that aids digestion by adding bulk to the diet.
indigestible plant material aiding digestion

Examples

A diet rich in roughage can help prevent constipation.
Doctors recommend increasing roughage intake for better digestion.
Vegetables, fruits, and whole grains are good sources of roughage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - dietary material from plants resistant to digestion

Etymology

Formed from 'rough' + '-age', referring to the coarse quality of such plant material.
