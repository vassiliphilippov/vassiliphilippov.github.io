Ornithogalum umbellatum

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˌnɪθoʊˈɡeɪləm ʌmˌbɛləˈtɑːm / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəʊˈɡɑːləm ʌmˌbɛləˈtɑːm / (BrE)
A small perennial plant with narrow, grasslike leaves and clusters of star-shaped white flowers with green stripes, native to Europe and Asia and also found in the eastern United States.
small plant with star-shaped white flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Ornithogalum umbellatum in early spring.
Some people consider Ornithogalum umbellatum an invasive species in local habitats.
Botanists identified the wildflowers as Ornithogalum umbellatum due to their distinct star-shaped blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'ornis' (bird) + 'gala' (milk), referring to its white flowers; Linnaean botanical naming.
