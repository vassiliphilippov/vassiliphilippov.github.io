mistily

1. ADV

/ ˈmɪstɪli / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that is vague, indistinct, or lacking clear details.
in a vague or indistinct way

Examples

The landscape appeared mistily through the early morning fog.
She recalled the events of her childhood mistily, unable to remember the exact details.
His explanation was so mistily worded that no one understood what he meant.

Synset Examples

he looked vaguely familiar
he explained it somewhat mistily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or definite

Etymology

From 'misty' + '-ly', with 'misty' from Old English 'mistig' (full of mist).

2. ADV

/ ˈmɪstɪli / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by mist or by being indistinct or hazy.
in a misty or hazy manner

Examples

Moonlight shone mistily through the foggy window.
She remembered her childhood mistily, as if it were a distant dream.
The city lights appeared mistily across the damp fields at dawn.

Synset Examples

the summits of the mountains were mistily purple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - full of or resembling mist
  • Antonym: clearly - in a clear manner

Etymology

Formed from 'misty' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00404962-r
Synset POS: r
