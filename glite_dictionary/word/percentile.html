percentile

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɛn.taɪl / (AmE), / pəˈsɛn.taɪl / (BrE)
(Statistics) Any of the 99 points dividing an ordered set of values into 100 equal parts, each containing 1% of the data.
statistical division into hundredths
medical

Examples

She scored in the 90th percentile on the standardized test, meaning she outperformed 90% of test-takers.
Babies whose weight falls below the 5th percentile may require special medical attention.
Admission is limited to students in the top ten percentile of their class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartile - One of three points that divide data into four equal groups
  • Synonym: median - The value separating the higher half from the lower half of a data set

Etymology

From Latin 'per centum' (by the hundred) + '-ile', a suffix forming nouns.
