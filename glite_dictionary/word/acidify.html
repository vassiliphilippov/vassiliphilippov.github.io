to acidify

1. VERB

/ əˈsɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əˈsɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become acidic; to increase the acidity of a substance
make or become acidic

Examples

Industrial pollution can acidify lakes, harming aquatic life.
Adding lemon juice will acidify the mixture and enhance the flavor.
Over time, acid rain can acidify the soil, affecting plant growth.

Synset Examples

the solution acetified

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to neutralize - to make chemically neutral or less acidic

Etymology

From acid + -ify, patterned after Latin roots meaning 'to make acidic'.

2. VERB

/ əˈsɪd.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əˈsɪd.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make or become sour or more acidic
make or become more acidic

Examples

Adding lemon juice will acidify the sauce and enhance its flavor.
Excess fertilizer can acidify the soil, harming plant growth.
Fermentation tends to acidify the mixture, resulting in a tangy taste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to neutralize - to make a substance chemically neutral; remove acidity or alkalinity

Etymology

From French 'acidifier', from 'acide' (acid) + '-fier' (to make), based on Latin 'acidus' (sour).
