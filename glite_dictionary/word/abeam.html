abeam

1. ADV

/ əˈbiːm / (AmE), / əˈbiːm / (BrE)
at a right angle to the length of a ship or aircraft; directly to the side
at a right angle to a ship or aircraft

Examples

The lighthouse was positioned abeam of our vessel as we passed.
They spotted the fishing boat abeam during their evening watch.
The aircraft reported an object abeam the left wing at 10,000 feet.

Etymology

From 'a-' (on) + 'beam,' referring to the crosswise timbers of a ship.
