Atlantides

1. PROPN

/ æˈtlæn.tɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / atˈlan.tɪ.diːz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a group of nymphs, usually said to be the daughters of Atlas, who were tasked with guarding the golden apples given by Gaea to Hera as a wedding gift.
mythological nymphs who guarded golden apples

Examples

In ancient stories, the Atlantides guarded Hera's prized golden apples in a hidden garden.
Artists often depicted the Atlantides as beautiful nymphs in lush, mysterious landscapes.
Mythologists debated whether the Atlantides were three, seven, or even more sisters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hesperides - Nymphs who tended a blissful garden with golden apples in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'Atlantides', plural of 'Atlantis', meaning daughters of Atlas in Greek mythology.
