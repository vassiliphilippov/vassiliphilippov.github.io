spectroscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌspɛktrəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɛktrəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving spectroscopy, especially in scientific analysis of substances using their interaction with electromagnetic radiation
relating to spectroscopy

Examples

The researchers used spectroscopic techniques to identify the chemical composition of the sample.
Advances in spectroscopic technology have greatly improved our understanding of molecular structures.
A spectroscopic analysis revealed the presence of rare elements in the mineral.

Synset Examples

spectroscopic analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscopy - the scientific study of spectra produced when matter interacts with electromagnetic radiation

Etymology

From spectroscopy + -ic, based on the structure of science adjectives.
