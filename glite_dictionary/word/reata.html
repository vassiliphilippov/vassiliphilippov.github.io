reata

1. NOUN

/ riˈɑtə / (AmE), / riˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a long, noosed rope, especially one used to catch animals, chiefly in the western United States and Mexico
long noosed rope for catching animals

Examples

The cowboy expertly threw his reata over the running calf.
With a quick flick of his reata, he snared the wild horse.
She learned how to coil and handle a reata during the summer on the ranch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lasso - a long rope with a noose at one end used for catching livestock

Etymology

From Spanish 'reata', meaning 'a lasso or rope'.
