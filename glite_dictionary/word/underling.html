underling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person with lower status or rank who is subordinate to someone else, especially in a job or organization
subordinate or lower-ranking person

Examples

As a senior manager, she delegated most routine tasks to her underling.
The CEO barely acknowledged his underlings during the meeting.
He disliked the way his boss treated him like a mere underling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subordinate - a person of lower rank or position in an organization
  • Antonym: superior - a person of higher rank or authority

Etymology

From Middle English 'underland,' influenced by 'under,' meaning beneath or subordinate, and the suffix '-ling' denoting a smaller or lesser person.
