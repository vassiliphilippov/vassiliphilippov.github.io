idolisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪdələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of admiring or revering someone or something excessively.
excessive admiration

Examples

The idolisation of celebrities can lead to unrealistic expectations among fans.
Her idolisation of her mentor inspired her to work harder.
He was uncomfortable with the idolisation he received after his success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoration - deep love and respect
  • Synonym: worship - the act of showing reverence and adoration for a deity or person
  • Antonym: disdain - the feeling that someone or something is unworthy of one's consideration or respect

Etymology

From idol + -isation, modeled on idolize; ultimately from Latin idolum (image, idol).

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪdələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of admiring or worshiping someone or something to an excessive or blind degree
excessive or blind admiration or worship

Examples

The idolisation of celebrities is a common aspect of modern culture.
His idolisation of his mentor sometimes prevented him from seeing her flaws.
Critics worry that the idolisation of wealth sends the wrong message to young people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolization - the American English spelling of 'idolisation'
  • Degree Lower: admiration - a feeling of respect and approval
  • Degree Higher: worship - the act of revering someone or something as divine or extremely important

Etymology

From idol + -isation, patterned after French 'idolisation' or directly from 'idolise.'
