doublespeak

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəlˌspiːk / (AmE), / ˈdʌblˌspiːk / (BrE)
deliberate use of ambiguous or evasive language, especially to mislead or obscure the truth.
ambiguous or evasive language

Examples

The politician's doublespeak made it hard to understand her true position on the issue.
Corporate doublespeak often disguises job cuts as 'downsizing' or 'rightsizing'.
His speech was full of doublespeak, leaving the audience confused about what he really meant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered harsh
  • Antonym: truthfulness - state of being truthful or honest

Etymology

Formed on the model of 'doublethink', from George Orwell's '1984', combining 'double' and 'speak'.
