peripeteia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəpɪˈtaɪə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpɪˈtɪːə / (BrE)
a sudden or unexpected reversal of fortune or circumstances, especially in a literary work
reversal of fortune in literature

Examples

The play reaches its peripeteia when the protagonist's fortunes quickly change for the worse.
A tragic peripeteia at the novel's midpoint forces the hero to rethink his actions.
In classical Greek drama, the peripeteia often marks the transition from hope to despair.

Synset Examples

a peripeteia swiftly turns a routine sequence of events into a story worth telling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a decisive moment of change in a story or situation
  • Synonym: anagnorisis - the critical moment of recognition or discovery in a plot

Etymology

From Greek *peripeteia* (sudden change), from *peri-* (around) + *piptein* (to fall).
