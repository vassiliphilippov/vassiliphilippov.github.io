sandblaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌblæstər / (AmE), / ˈsændˌblɑːstə / (BrE)
a machine or tool that sprays sand at high pressure to clean or smooth hard surfaces
machine for cleaning with high-pressure sand

Examples

The technician used a sandblaster to remove rust from the old metal gate.
Before repainting the car, a sandblaster was employed to strip away the chipped paint.
She wore protective gear while operating the sandblaster in the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandblasting - the process of cleaning or smoothing surfaces using a sandblaster

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'blaster', referring to blasting (propelling) sand.
