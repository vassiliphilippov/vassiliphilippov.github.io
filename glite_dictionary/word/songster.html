songster

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔŋstər / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋstə / (BrE)
a composer or writer of words or music for popular songs
composer or writer of popular songs

Examples

The renowned songster has written dozens of hits that topped the charts across decades.
As an aspiring songster, she spends hours perfecting lyrics and melodies.
Some songsters prefer to remain anonymous, letting the performers take the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songwriter - a person who writes the words or music for songs

Etymology

From English 'song' + '-ster', a suffix denoting a person associated with an activity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔŋstər / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋstə / (BrE)
a person who sings with notable skill, especially a singer or vocalist
a skilled singer

Examples

The acclaimed songster captivated the audience with her powerful voice.
As a young man, he dreamed of becoming a famous songster on the stage.
Many local songsters performed at the talent show last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singer - a person who sings, especially professionally

Etymology

From 'song' + '-ster', an agent noun suffix; '-ster' originally denoted a person engaged in an activity.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔŋstər / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋstə / (BrE)
a bird known for producing musical or melodious calls or songs
a bird with a musical call

Examples

The woods were alive with the sounds of the songster perched high above.
Each spring, the melodious songster fills the garden with music.
Many birdwatchers come to hear the first songster of the season at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songbird - a bird typically known for singing melodious songs

Etymology

From 'song' + '-ster', an agent suffix of Old English origin.
