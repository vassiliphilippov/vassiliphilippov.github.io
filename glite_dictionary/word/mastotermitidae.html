Mastotermitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstəˌtɜrmɪˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌmastəʊˌtɜːmɪˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of primitive termites, considered among the most ancient lineages of termites, distinguished by several primitive traits
family of primitive termites
medical

Examples

Fossil records show that Mastotermitidae have existed since the Cretaceous period.
The Mastotermitidae family contains only one living species today, Mastotermes darwiniensis.
Scientists study Mastotermitidae to understand the evolution of termites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social, wood-eating insect

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek mastos (breast) and tērēs (worm), forming the type genus Mastotermes + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæstoʊˌtɜr.mɪˈtaɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌmæstəʊˌtɜː.mɪˈtaɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of primitive termites, representing an ancient group of insects within the order Blattodea, known for retaining ancestral characteristics.
family of primitive termites

Examples

The Mastotermitidae are considered the most primitive family of termites.
Researchers discovered a fossil belonging to the Mastotermitidae in northern Australia.
Unlike other termites, the Mastotermitidae exhibit features similar to cockroaches.

Etymology

From genus name Mastotermes, from Greek 'mastos' (breast) and 'termes' (woodworm), with '-idae' denoting a zoological family.
