embezzlement

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɛzlmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛzlmənt / (BrE)
the act of dishonestly taking money or property that has been entrusted to one's care, especially for personal gain
fraudulent taking of entrusted funds or property
legal

Examples

The accountant was charged with embezzlement after unauthorized withdrawals were discovered.
Strict auditing procedures can help prevent embezzlement in nonprofit organizations.
He lost his job due to allegations of embezzlement of company funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the action or crime of stealing
  • Synonym: misappropriation - the act of taking something dishonestly for one's own use
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

From Old French *embesillement*, from *embesiller* (to embezzle).
