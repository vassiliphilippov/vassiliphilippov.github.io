Votyak

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtjæk / (BrE)
the Udmurt language, a Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Votyak (Udmurt) people in Russia
the Udmurt language

Examples

The Votyak language is primarily spoken in the Udmurt Republic of Russia.
She is studying Votyak as part of her research on Finno-Ugric languages.
Few young people today are fluent in Votyak, raising concerns about the language's survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Udmurt - the Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Udmurt people

Etymology

From Russian 'вотяк' (votyak), referring to the Udmurt people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈvɒtɪak / (BrE)
a member of a Finno-Ugric-speaking people native to eastern European Russia, now more commonly known as the Udmurt.
member of the Udmurt people of Russia

Examples

The Votyak were historically known for their distinctive music and folklore.
Anthropologists have studied the customs and beliefs of the Votyak.
In the 20th century, many Votyak began to refer to themselves as Udmurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Udmurt - a member of the Udmurt people, the name now commonly used for this group

Etymology

From Russian Вотяк (Votyak), an exonym for the Udmurt people.
