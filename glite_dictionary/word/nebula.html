nebula

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛbjələ / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
In pathology, a faint, cloudy spot on the cornea of the eye.
faint corneal spot (medical)
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist identified a nebula that could affect her vision if left untreated.
After the injury, a small nebula appeared on the patient's cornea.
Long-standing infections sometimes result in a nebula that causes blurred sight.

Etymology

From Latin *nebula* (mist, cloud).

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛbjələ / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
an immense cloud of gas and dust in outer space, often visible as a bright or dark patch
cloud of gas and dust in space

Examples

Through the telescope, we could clearly see the colorful nebula in the night sky.
The Orion nebula is one of the brightest and most studied regions in astronomy.
Dust and gas from a collapsing nebula can eventually form new stars and planets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: galaxy - a massive system of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'nebula' meaning 'mist' or 'cloud'.
