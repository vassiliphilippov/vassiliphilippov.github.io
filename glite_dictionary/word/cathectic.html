cathectic

1. ADJ

/ kəˈθɛktɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɛktɪk / (BrE)
relating to the investment of emotional or mental energy in a person, object, or idea (cathexis)
relating to emotional or mental energy investment
medical

Examples

The therapist noted his cathectic attachment to childhood memories.
Dreams often emerge from cathectic processes directed toward unconscious desires.
Researchers studied the cathectic dynamics present in early parental relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathexis - investment of psychic or emotional energy in an object or idea

Etymology

From 'cathexis', which derives from Greek 'kathexis' (holding, occupying), via German 'Kathexis'.
