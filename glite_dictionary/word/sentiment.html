sentiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪ.mənt / (BrE)
a tender, romantic, or nostalgic feeling or emotion
tender or nostalgic emotion

Examples

The old love letters were full of sentiment and longing.
He spoke with genuine sentiment about his childhood home.
The film was criticized for its excessive sentiment, but many viewers found it touching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotion - a strong feeling deriving from one's mood or relationships
  • Synonym: feeling - an emotional state or reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'sentimentum' (feeling), from 'sentire' (to feel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪmənt / (BrE)
an opinion, belief, or attitude that is not based on definite proof or certainty
personal opinion or belief

Examples

Her sentiment about the new policy was shared by many of her colleagues.
Public sentiment on the issue has shifted in recent years.
He expressed a sentiment of optimism regarding the company's future.

Synset Examples

my opinion differs from yours
I am not of your persuasion
what are your thoughts on Haiti?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a view or judgment formed about something; belief
  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • Antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true

Etymology

From Latin 'sentimentum' (feeling, thought), from 'sentire' (to feel).
