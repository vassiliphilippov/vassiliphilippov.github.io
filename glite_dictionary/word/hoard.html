hoard

1. NOUN

/ hɔrd / (AmE), / hɔːd / (BrE)
a secret stock or store of valuable objects or money, often accumulated for future use
secret store of valuables or money

Examples

The archaeologists discovered a hoard of ancient coins buried beneath the floor.
She kept a hoard of chocolates hidden in her desk drawer for special occasions.
A secret hoard of gold was found concealed in the old wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cache - a hidden store of things, especially weapons or valuables

Etymology

From Old English hord (treasure, valuable stockpile), related to German 'Hort'.

2. VERB

/ hɔrd / (AmE), / hɔːd / (BrE)
to collect and store large quantities of something, often secretly, for future use
collect and store for future use

Examples

During the crisis, many people began to hoard food and essential supplies.
He has a tendency to hoard money rather than spend it.
The squirrels hoard nuts in preparation for winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stash - to store something away safely or secretly
  • Synonym: to accumulate - to collect or gather over time
  • Antonym: to squander - to spend or use wastefully

Etymology

From Old English hord (treasure, store), related to Old Norse hodd (treasure).

3. VERB

/ hɔrd / (AmE), / hɔːd / (BrE)
to collect and store large amounts of something, often secretly and for future use
collect and store something, often secretly

Examples

During times of crisis, people often hoard essential supplies like food and water.
She began to hoard newspapers in her attic, fearing she might need them one day.
Some animals hoard nuts and seeds to survive the winter months.

Synset Examples

I am accumulating evidence for the man's unfaithfulness to his wife
She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis
She rolled up a small fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect, especially little by little
  • Synonym: to amass - to gather together or collect a large quantity
  • Antonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner

Etymology

From Old English *hord* (treasure, valuable stock or store).
