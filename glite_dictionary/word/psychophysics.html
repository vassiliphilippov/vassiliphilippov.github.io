psychophysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
the branch of psychology concerned with measuring the relationship between physical stimuli and the sensations and perceptions they produce
study of relationships between stimuli and sensation
medical

Examples

She specialized in psychophysics, examining how people perceive changes in sound intensity.
Modern psychophysics often uses controlled experiments to map human sensory responses.
His research on psychophysics revealed new insights about the human visual system.

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and 'physics' (nature, science of matter).
