auspiciously

1. ADV

/ ɔːˈspɪʃəsli / (AmE), / ɔːˈspɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that suggests a positive or successful outcome; favorably or with good omens.
in a favorable or promising way

Examples

He began his new role auspiciously, impressing everyone on the very first day.
The event auspiciously coincided with the city’s annual festival.
Their marriage started auspiciously, blessed by clear skies and joyous celebrations.

Synset Examples

he started his new job auspiciously on his birthday

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inauspiciously - in an unfavorable or unpromising manner
  • Synonym: favorably - in a way that increases the chance of success; positively

Etymology

From "auspicious" + "-ly"; "auspicious" from Latin auspiciōsus (of good omen).
