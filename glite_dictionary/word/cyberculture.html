cyberculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪbɚˌkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəkʌltʃə / (BrE)
The culture and social practices that develop from the use and integration of computers, the internet, and digital technologies for communication, entertainment, and business.
culture arising from digital technology use

Examples

Many scholars study cyberculture to understand how digital communities form and interact.
The rise of cyberculture has transformed how people communicate around the world.
Her research focuses on the impact of cyberculture on identity and social interaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digital culture - culture shaped by digital media and technology

Etymology

From 'cyber-' (relating to computers or cyberspace) and 'culture'.
