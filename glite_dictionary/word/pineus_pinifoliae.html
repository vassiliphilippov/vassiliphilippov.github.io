Pineus pinifoliae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪni.əs ˌpɪnɪˈfoʊli.i / (AmE), / ˌpaɪni.əs ˌpɪnɪˈfəʊli.i / (BrE)
A species of adelgid insect that feeds on various species of spruce and pine trees, such as red, black, Engelmann, blue, and white spruce, as well as white and western white pines.
a species of adelgid insect affecting spruce and pine

Examples

Foresters monitor Pineus pinifoliae populations to prevent damage to valuable spruce and pine stands.
Outbreaks of Pineus pinifoliae can lead to significant needle loss in affected trees.
Biologists studied the impact of Pineus pinifoliae infestations on the health of local pine forests.

Etymology

From Pineus (genus name, derived from Latin 'pinus' meaning 'pine tree') + pinifoliae (referring to pine needles).
