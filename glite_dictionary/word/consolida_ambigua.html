Consolida ambigua

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsoʊlɪdə æmˈbɪɡjuə / (AmE), / kənˌsɒlɪdə æmˈbɪɡjuə / (BrE)
A species of larkspur native to southern Europe, commonly grown for its tall, unbranched spikes of blue, purple, or pinkish flowers; sometimes classified under the genus Delphinium.
a species of larkspur with spikelike flowers

Examples

Consolida ambigua is often chosen for cottage gardens due to its vivid blue blossoms.
Gardeners prize Consolida ambigua for its ability to thrive in sunny borders and poor soils.
The delicate spikes of Consolida ambigua add height and color to floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larkspur - a plant with tall, showy spikes of blue flowers, especially of the genus Delphinium or Consolida

Etymology

From Latin; 'consolida' meaning to strengthen or consolidate and 'ambigua' meaning doubtful or ambiguous.
