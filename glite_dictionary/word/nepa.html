Nepa

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːpə / (AmE), / ˈniːpə / (BrE)
A genus of elongate-oval aquatic insects in the family Nepidae, commonly known as water scorpions.
genus of aquatic insects (water scorpions)

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Nepa in South America.
The body of Nepa species is well adapted to their aquatic habitats.
Many students observe Nepa under the microscope during entomology classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nepidae - family of aquatic insects known as water scorpions

Etymology

From Latin *nepa* (scorpion).

genus of aquatic insects (water scorpions)

Examples

The Nepa genus is known for its distinctive raptorial forelegs adapted for hunting underwater.
Scientists collected several Nepa specimens from the pond for analysis.
Members of the Nepa genus are often called water scorpions due to their appearance and predatory behavior.
