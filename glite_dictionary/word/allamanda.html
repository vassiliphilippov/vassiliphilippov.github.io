Allamanda

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈmændə / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈmandə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American woody vines with brightly colored, trumpet-shaped flowers, commonly grown as ornamentals.
genus of tropical American vines

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the Allamanda vine add vibrant color to tropical gardens.
Botanists studied several species within the Allamanda genus to understand their growth patterns.
In warm climates, gardeners often choose Allamanda for its impressive display of trumpet-shaped blooms.

Etymology

Named after Frédéric-Louis Allamand, an 18th-century Swiss botanist.
