wrathfully

1. ADV

/ ˈræθ.fəli / (AmE), / ˈrɒθ.fəli / (BrE)
In a manner showing intense anger or rage.
in an intensely angry manner

Examples

He glared wrathfully at the person who had just insulted him.
She wrathfully slammed the door behind her, making the whole house shake.
The manager spoke wrathfully after discovering the serious mistake in the report.

Synset Examples

he looked at her, not wrathfully now, but quizzically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - In a manner showing anger

Etymology

From 'wrathful' + '-ly'; 'wrathful' from Old English 'wræththu' (anger, wrath).
