rigor mortis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪɡər ˈmɔrtɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɪɡə ˈmɔːtɪs / (BrE)
the temporary stiffness and rigidity of the muscles and joints that occurs shortly after death
post-mortem muscle stiffness
medical

Examples

The investigator noted that rigor mortis had already set in, indicating the man had died several hours earlier.
Rigor mortis typically begins within a few hours after death and lasts for up to 72 hours.
Forensic scientists used the stage of rigor mortis to estimate the time of death.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'stiffness of death' ('rigor' = stiffness, 'mortis' = of death).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɪɡər ˈmɔrtɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɪɡə ˈmɔːtɪs / (BrE)
the stiffening of the muscles that occurs several hours after death and lasts for up to four days
post-mortem muscular stiffening
medical

Examples

The presence of rigor mortis helped the pathologist estimate the time of death.
After death, rigor mortis typically sets in within a few hours.
Investigators noted that rigor mortis had already progressed throughout the body.

Etymology

From Latin meaning 'stiffness of death': 'rigor' (stiffness) + 'mortis' (of death).
