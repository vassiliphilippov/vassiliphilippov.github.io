achromatous

1. ADJ

/ əˈkroʊmətəs / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətəs / (BrE)
having little or no color; lacking in coloration or pigment
lacking color or pigment

Examples

The scientist described the bacteria as achromatous under the microscope.
Albino animals are often almost achromatous due to the absence of pigment.
The achromatous leaves made the plant appear sickly and pale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorless - lacking color; without hue or pigment

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + chrōma (color) + -ous (adjective suffix).
