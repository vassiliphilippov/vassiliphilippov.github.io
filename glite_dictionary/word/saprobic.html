saprobic

1. ADJ

/ səˈproʊbɪk / (AmE), / səˈprəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or environment) living in or characteristic of settings rich in decomposing organic matter and usually lacking oxygen.
living in rich organic, oxygen-poor environments
medical

Examples

Certain bacteria are described as saprobic because they thrive in flooded, oxygen-poor soils.
A saprobic habitat supports organisms that depend on decaying plant or animal matter for nutrition.
Some fungi exhibit a saprobic lifestyle, feeding exclusively on organic detritus in their surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saprophytic - deriving nourishment from decaying organic matter, especially plants

Etymology

From Greek sapros (decayed) + bios (life), via modern scientific Latin.
