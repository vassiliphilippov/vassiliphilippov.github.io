ladybird

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small round beetle, often brightly colored and spotted, that feeds on aphids and other insect pests
small, spotted, brightly colored beetle
british

Examples

A ladybird landed softly on her finger as she walked through the garden.
The gardener released dozens of ladybirds to help control the aphid population.
As a child, I loved searching for red and black ladybirds in the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybug - North American term for the same small, spotted beetle

Etymology

From 'lady' referring to the Virgin Mary and 'bird' (an old term for insect), used in British English. In American English, the insect is called 'ladybug'.
