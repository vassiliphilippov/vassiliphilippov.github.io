felafel

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːfəl / (AmE), / fəˈlæfəl / (BrE)
a small, deep-fried ball or patty made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, often seasoned and served in pita bread
deep-fried chickpea or fava bean ball

Examples

We ordered a plate of felafel with hummus at the new café.
His favorite vegetarian meal is a felafel wrap with lots of vegetables.
She learned how to make homemade felafel during her trip to Israel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falafel - another spelling for felafel, deep-fried chickpea/fava bean ball

Etymology

From Arabic 'falāfil' (plural of 'filfil', meaning 'pepper').
