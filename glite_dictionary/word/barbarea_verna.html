Barbarea verna

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑrbəˈɛəriə ˈvɜrnə / (AmE), / ˌbɑːbəˈɪəriə ˈvɜːnə / (BrE)
A species of leafy plant in the mustard family, native to southwestern Europe and cultivated for its edible leaves, also known as upland cress.
species of edible leafy plant (upland cress)

Examples

Young Barbarea verna leaves are often added to salads for their peppery flavor.
Farmers in Florida have started cultivating Barbarea verna as an alternative leafy green.
The botanical garden features a collection of rare plants, including Barbarea verna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upland cress - another name for Barbarea verna, an edible leafy plant

Etymology

From the genus name Barbarea (after Saint Barbara) and Latin 'verna' meaning 'of the spring'.
