Ophiodon elongatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːfioʊˈdɑːn ɪˌloʊŋˈɡeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌɒfiəˈdəʊn ɪˌlɒŋˈɡeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of food fish of the northern Pacific Ocean, related to greenlings and commonly known as lingcod.
a northern Pacific food fish, lingcod

Examples

The Ophiodon elongatus is prized by anglers for its firm, white flesh.
Conservation efforts in the Pacific Northwest have helped restore Ophiodon elongatus populations.
Seafood markets in Alaska often feature freshly caught Ophiodon elongatus during the summer season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingcod - common name for Ophiodon elongatus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Ophiodon from Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'odous' (tooth); 'elongatus' is Latin for 'elongated.'
