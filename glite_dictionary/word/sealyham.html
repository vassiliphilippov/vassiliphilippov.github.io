Sealyham

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːliˌhæm / (AmE), / ˈsiːliˌham / (BrE)
a breed of small, wire-haired terrier with short legs, originally bred in Sealyham, Wales
breed of small wire-haired terrier

Examples

The Sealyham is known for its friendly and fearless nature.
She adopted a Sealyham from the local animal shelter last year.
Grooming a Sealyham requires regular brushing to manage its wiry coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a breed of dog originally bred to hunt vermin

Etymology

Named after Sealyham, an estate in Pembrokeshire, Wales, where the breed was first developed.
