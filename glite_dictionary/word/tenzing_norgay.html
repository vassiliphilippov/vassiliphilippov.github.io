Tenzing Norgay

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛn.zɪŋ ˈnɔrˌɡaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.zɪŋ ˈnɔː.ɡeɪ / (BrE)
(1914–1986) Nepalese Sherpa mountaineer who, with Sir Edmund Hillary, was one of the first two people known to reach the summit of Mount Everest in 1953.
Nepalese Sherpa mountaineer; first Everest climber

Examples

Tenzing Norgay is celebrated around the world for his historic ascent of Mount Everest.
Many books have been written about the partnership between Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay.
In 1953, Tenzing Norgay reached the summit of the world's highest mountain.
