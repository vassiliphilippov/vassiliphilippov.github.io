Tilletia caries

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈlɛtiə ˈkɛəriːz / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛtiə ˈkɛəriːz / (BrE)
a fungus that infects wheat, destroying the kernels by replacing them with greasy masses of foul-smelling spores, known as bunt or stinking smut
fungus causing stinking smut in wheat

Examples

Farmers monitor their crops for signs of Tilletia caries, which can destroy entire wheat harvests.
The presence of Tilletia caries results in kernels turning into black, smelly masses.
Modern seed treatments have greatly reduced the incidence of Tilletia caries in wheat fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunt - a fungal disease of wheat caused by Tilletia species
  • Synonym: smut - fungal diseases of plants producing unpleasant spores

Etymology

From the genus name 'Tilletia', named after French botanist Mathieu Tillet, and Latin 'caries' meaning decay or rot.
