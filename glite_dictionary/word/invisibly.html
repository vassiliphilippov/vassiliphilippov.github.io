invisibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈvɪzəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be seen; without being visible.
without being seen

Examples

The magician moved invisibly behind the curtain during the trick.
Viruses can spread invisibly before symptoms become apparent.
She slipped out of the party almost invisibly, without anyone noticing.

Synset Examples

these organisms enter the body invisibly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visibly - in a way that can be seen; obviously

Etymology

From 'invisible' + '-ly'. 'Invisible' from Latin invisibilis (not able to be seen).
