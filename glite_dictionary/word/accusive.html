accusive

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjuːsɪv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːsɪv / (BrE)
containing or expressing an accusation or blame
expressing accusation or blame

Examples

His accusive tone made everyone uncomfortable in the meeting.
She shot him an accusive glance as he entered the room late.
The accusive letter detailed every perceived wrongdoing over the past year.

Synset Examples

an accusative forefinger
black accusatory looks
accusive shoes and telltale trousers
his accusing glare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusatory - expressing or implying accusation
  • Antonym: defensive - intended to defend or protect

Etymology

From Latin accusare (to accuse), via French accuser.
