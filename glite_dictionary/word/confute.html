to confute

1. VERB

/ kənˈfjuːt / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːt / (BrE)
to prove a person or an argument to be wrong or false, especially by argument or evidence
prove false, disprove by argument

Examples

The professor skillfully confuted the theory with clear evidence from his own research.
She tried to confute her opponent's argument during the debate, citing several reliable sources.
The lawyer's objective was to confute the witness's false testimony.

Synset Examples

The physicist disproved his colleagues' theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refute - prove a statement or person to be wrong or false
  • Synonym: to disprove - prove that something is not true
  • Antonym: to prove - demonstrate the truth or existence of something

Etymology

From Latin 'confutare', meaning to restrain, silence, or refute.
