to demonstrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛm.ənˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛm.ən.streɪt / (BrE)
to show clearly that something is true, exists, or is the case, especially by providing evidence or examples
show or prove clearly

Examples

The study was designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the new vaccine.
Her commitment to the project demonstrates her dedication and reliability.
The experiment clearly demonstrated how the chemical reaction takes place.

Synset Examples

His high fever attested to his illness
The buildings in Rome manifest a high level of architectural sophistication
This decision demonstrates his sense of fairness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prove - to establish the truth or validity of something by evidence or argument
  • Antonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true or is false

Etymology

From Latin demonstrare, meaning 'to point out, show, demonstrate'.

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛmənˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛmənstreɪt / (BrE)
to take part in a public protest or march to express strong views or opposition
take part in a protest or march

Examples

Thousands of people gathered to demonstrate against the government’s new policy.
Students plan to demonstrate peacefully in front of the parliament tomorrow.
Groups from across the city will demonstrate in solidarity with the striking workers.

Synset Examples

Thousands demonstrated against globalization during the meeting of the most powerful economic nations in Seattle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - express strong objection or disapproval, especially publicly

Etymology

From Latin demonstratus, past participle of demonstrare (to point out, show).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true

From Latin demonstratus, past participle of demonstrare (to point out, show).

4. VERB

/ ˈdɛm.ənˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛm.ən.streɪt / (BrE)
to show or explain how something works or is done, especially to an audience
show or explain how something works

Examples

The teacher will demonstrate the experiment to the class tomorrow.
At the conference, the company plans to demonstrate their latest product live on stage.
He carefully demonstrated how to operate the new coffee machine.

Synset Examples

She shows her dogs frequently
We will demo the new software in Washington

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show - to cause or allow something to be seen
  • Synonym: display - to exhibit or put something in view
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something from being seen

Etymology

From Latin 'demonstratus', past participle of 'demonstrare' (to point out, show).
