to hydrolyse

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to undergo or cause to undergo a chemical reaction with water, resulting in decomposition into two or more products.
undergo chemical decomposition by water
british

Examples

Enzymes can hydrolyse proteins into their amino acid components.
In acidic solutions, sucrose will hydrolyse to produce glucose and fructose.
The compound does not hydrolyse easily at room temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrolyze - to undergo or cause hydrolysis (American spelling)

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and Greek lysis (loosening, dissolving). Variant spelling of "hydrolyze."
