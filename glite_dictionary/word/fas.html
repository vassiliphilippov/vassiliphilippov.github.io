FAS

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.eɪˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛf.eɪˈɛs / (BrE)
a congenital condition caused by prenatal exposure to alcohol, leading to physical deformities, facial abnormalities, and cognitive impairment
fetal alcohol syndrome; birth defects from alcohol during pregnancy
medical

Examples

Children born with FAS can struggle with lifelong learning difficulties.
The clinic offers support for families affected by FAS.
Doctors emphasize that FAS is entirely preventable if alcohol is avoided during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetal alcohol syndrome - congenital disorder caused by alcohol exposure during pregnancy

Etymology

Acronym for 'fetal alcohol syndrome'.
