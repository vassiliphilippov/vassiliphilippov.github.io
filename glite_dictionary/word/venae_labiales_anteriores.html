venae labiales anteriores

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪniː læˈbaɪəliːz ænˈtɪriˌɔːriz / (AmE), / ˈviːniː læˈbeɪliːz ænˈtɪəriˌɔːriːz / (BrE)
the veins that drain blood from the labia majora to the external pudendal vein
veins from labia majora to external pudendal vein
medical

Examples

The venae labiales anteriores play a key role in draining blood from the labia majora.
Damage to the venae labiales anteriores can result in localized swelling and discomfort.
During the examination, the doctor identified inflammation near the venae labiales anteriores.

Etymology

From Latin: venae (veins), labiales (of the lips), anteriores (anterior/front).
