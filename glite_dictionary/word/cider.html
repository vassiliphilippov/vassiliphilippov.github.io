cider

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdə / (BrE)
an alcoholic or non-alcoholic drink made from the juice of pressed apples
drink made from apple juice

Examples

She poured a glass of cider to enjoy with her lunch.
At the festival, they offered both sweet and hard cider.
Nothing says autumn like a mug of warm cider on a chilly night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple juice - juice made by pressing apples, typically non-alcoholic

Etymology

From Old French *cidre*, from Medieval Latin *cicera* (fermented drink).
