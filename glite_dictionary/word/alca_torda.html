Alca torda

1. NOUN

/ ˈælkə ˈtɔrdə / (AmE), / ˈælkə ˈtɔːdə / (BrE)
A species of auk found in the northern Atlantic, recognized by its black-and-white plumage and sharply compressed bill; also known as the razorbill.
a species of northern Atlantic auk (razorbill)

Examples

The Alca torda is easily distinguished by its striking black-and-white plumage.
Every spring, large colonies of Alca torda gather on rocky cliffs to breed.
Marine biologists monitor populations of Alca torda to assess the health of coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razorbill - Northern Atlantic auk with sharp-edged bill (common name for Alca torda)

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Alca' means auk and 'torda' is the specific species name.
