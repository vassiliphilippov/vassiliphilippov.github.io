Tunguska

1. PROPN

/ tʊŋˈɡʊskə / (AmE), / tʊŋˈɡʊskə / (BrE)
A river in southeastern Siberia that flows northwest from Lake Baikal to join the Yenisei River.
river in southeastern Siberia

Examples

The Tunguska River is known for the mysterious explosion that occurred nearby in 1908.
A team of scientists conducted research along the banks of the Tunguska.
Several native communities live near the Tunguska, relying on it for fishing.

2. PROPN

/ tʊŋˈɡʊskə / (AmE), / tʊŋˈɡʊskə / (BrE)
a river in Siberia that flows northwest to become a tributary of the Yenisei River
river in Siberia, tributary of the Yenisei

Examples

The Tunguska River is famous for the site of a mysterious explosion in 1908.
Researchers traveled up the Tunguska to study its unique ecosystem.
The Tunguska flows into the Yenisei, one of the largest river systems in Siberia.

Etymology

From Russian Тунгуска (Tunguska), possibly from the name of the Tungus people.

3. PROPN

/ tuŋˈɡuːskə / (AmE), / tʊŋˈɡʊskə / (BrE)
A river in Siberia, Russia, that flows north and west from north of Lake Baikal to the Yenisei River.
river in Siberia, Russia

Examples

The Tunguska River is famous for the mysterious explosion that occurred nearby in 1908.
Many scientific expeditions have traveled along the Tunguska to study its wildlife and geology.
The Tunguska flows through vast, sparsely populated regions of Siberia.

Etymology

From Russian Тунгуска (Tunguska), referring to the river of that name.
