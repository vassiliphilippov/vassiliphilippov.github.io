relaxer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlæksər / (AmE), / rɪˈlæksə / (BrE)
a thing or substance that helps someone to feel relaxed or causes relaxation
thing that induces relaxation

Examples

After a long day at work, a cup of herbal tea is my favorite relaxer.
Listening to soft music can be a powerful relaxer when you're stressed.
The hotel spa offers a variety of natural relaxers for tired travelers.

Synset Examples

music is a good relaxer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmant - a thing that calms or soothes
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises alertness or activity

Etymology

Formed from 'relax' + '-er', denoting an agent or thing that causes relaxation.
