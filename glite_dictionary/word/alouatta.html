Alouatta

1. NOUN

/ ˌæluˈætə / (AmE), / ˌæluˈætə / (BrE)
A genus of large New World monkeys, commonly known as howler monkeys, native to Central and South America.
genus of howler monkeys

Examples

The genus Alouatta includes some of the loudest land animals in the world.
Researchers observed a group of Alouatta feeding high in the rainforest canopy.
Alouatta populations are threatened by habitat loss and deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howler monkey - a large New World monkey known for its loud calls

Etymology

From French *alouate* (howler monkey), itself from a South American language.
