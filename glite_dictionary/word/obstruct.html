to obstruct

1. VERB

/ əbˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to block or get in the way of something so as to prevent it from being seen or accessed
block from sight or passage

Examples

A large billboard obstructs the view from my window.
During the parade, crowds obstructed the entrance to the building.
Make sure nothing obstructs the emergency exit at any time.

Synset Examples

The thick curtain blocked the action on the stage
The trees obstruct my view of the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct, prevent movement or access
  • Antonym: clear - to remove obstruction or blockage

Etymology

From Latin 'obstruere' (to build against, block up), from 'ob-' (against) + 'struere' (to build).

2. VERB

/ əbˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to hinder or prevent the progress, movement, or accomplishment of something or someone
to hinder or block progress

Examples

The fallen tree obstructed the road, forcing drivers to find another route.
He was accused of trying to obstruct the investigation by destroying evidence.
Construction work can sometimes obstruct pedestrian access to public buildings.

Synset Examples

His brother blocked him at every turn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone or something to do something or for something to happen
  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement through or past by obstructing; to hinder
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier

Etymology

From Latin 'obstruere' (to build against, block up), from 'ob-' (against) + 'struere' (to build).

3. VERB

/ əbˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to block a road, passage, or opening, or to prevent something from moving through it
block or hinder passage

Examples

A fallen tree obstructed the main road, causing a long traffic jam.
The protesters tried to obstruct the entrance to the building.
Please do not leave bags in the aisle, as they may obstruct the exit.

Synset Examples

obstruct the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement or progress by means of a physical obstacle
  • Antonym: to unblock - to remove an obstruction or block from somewhere

Etymology

From Latin 'obstruere', from ob- 'against' + struere 'to build'.
