Ostrya virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə vɜrˌdʒɪniˈeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə vɜːdʒɪnɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to eastern North America, also known as the American hophornbeam, with hard wood and hop-like fruit clusters.
North American hop hornbeam tree species

Examples

The wood of the Ostrya virginiana is prized for its strength and durability.
During our hike in the Appalachian foothills, we spotted several mature Ostrya virginiana trees.
Leaves of the Ostrya virginiana turn a soft yellow in the autumn months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hophornbeam - A common name for trees of the genus Ostrya

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus name Ostrya and species epithet virginiana, meaning 'from Virginia'.
