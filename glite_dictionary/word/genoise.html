genoise

1. NOUN

/ ʒəˈnwɑz / (AmE), / ʒɛˈnwɑːz / (BrE)
A light, rich Italian sponge cake made with eggs, sugar, and flour, typically without added fat except for melted butter.
Italian sponge cake

Examples

The chef demonstrated how to bake a classic genoise from scratch.
For the wedding cake, they chose layers of chocolate genoise filled with raspberry jam.
A light genoise pairs perfectly with fresh strawberries and whipped cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge cake - A light, airy cake made with eggs, sugar, and flour

Etymology

From French génoise, named after the city of Genoa, Italy.
