collegiate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlidʒ.i.ət / (AmE), / kəˈliː.dʒi.ət / (BrE)
relating to a college or college students; typical of a college or its students.
of or relating to college or college students

Examples

The university is known for its collegiate atmosphere and strong student community.
He enjoyed the collegiate traditions that made the campus unique.
Her wardrobe reflected a casual, collegiate style.

Synset Examples

collegiate living
collegiate attitudes
collegiate clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - relating to education or scholarship

Etymology

From Latin collegiatus (joined together, associate).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlidʒiət / (AmE), / kəˈlɪdʒ.i.ət / (BrE)
relating to a college or its students; typical of a college environment or culture.
relating to a college or its students

Examples

She preferred the friendly, collegiate atmosphere of the small university.
The students wore collegiate sweaters with the school's emblem.
His collegiate years were some of the most memorable of his life.

Synset Examples

collegiate living
collegiate attitudes
collegiate clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - relating to education or scholarship
  • Synonym: scholastic - relating to schools or education

Etymology

From Medieval Latin collegiatus (member of a college), from Latin collegium (association, college).
