bulbous

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌlbəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌlbəs / (BrE)
curving outward in a round, swollen, or bulging shape, like a bulb.
rounded and swollen in shape

Examples

The frog's bulbous eyes gave it a comical appearance.
She admired the bulbous nose of the statue, which made it unique.
The plant had a bulbous stem at its base, storing water for dry seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulging - swelling outwards in a round shape
  • Antonym: concave - curving inward instead of outward

Etymology

From Latin 'bulbosus', from 'bulbus' meaning 'bulb'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌlbəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌlbəs / (BrE)
having a rounded or swollen shape like a bulb, especially at the base
shaped like a bulb; rounded or swollen

Examples

The frog's bulbous eyes stuck out from its head.
He had a bulbous nose that dominated his face.
The plant stores nutrients in its bulbous roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spherical - having the shape of a sphere; round
  • Antonym: pointed - having a sharp or tapered end; not rounded

Etymology

From Latin bulbosus (bulbous), from bulbus (bulb).
