terrified

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛrəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪfaɪd / (BrE)
extremely frightened; filled with intense fear.
extremely frightened

Examples

She was terrified of flying in airplanes after the accident.
The children were terrified by the sound of thunder during the storm.
He looked terrified when he heard the unexpected footsteps behind him.

Synset Examples

became panicky as the snow deepened
felt panicked before each exam
trying to keep back the panic-stricken crowd
the terrified horse bolted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afraid - feeling fear or apprehension
  • Synonym: petrified - so frightened that one is unable to move; terrified
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or fear

Etymology

From Latin *terrificare* (to frighten), via French *terrifier*.
