Louis X

1. PROPN

/ luˈi tɛn / (AmE), / ˈluːi tɛn / (BrE)
(1289–1316) King of France from 1314 to 1316, known as Louis the Quarrelsome.
King of France, 1314–1316

Examples

Louis X was succeeded by his brother Philip V after his short reign.
Historians often refer to Louis X as Louis the Quarrelsome due to his turbulent rule.
The policies of Louis X had lasting effects on the French monarchy.
