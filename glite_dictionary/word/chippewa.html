Chippewa

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɪpəˌwɑ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpəwə / (BrE)
A member of an Algonquian Indigenous people living mainly in areas stretching from eastern Canada to the northern United States, especially around Lake Superior; also called Ojibwe.
member of an Algonquian Indigenous people (Ojibwe)

Examples

Many Chippewa still live on reservations near Lake Superior in the United States and Canada.
The Chippewa have a rich tradition of storytelling and craftwork.
He traced his ancestry to the Chippewa people of northern Minnesota.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwe - another name for the Chippewa people

Etymology

From Ojibwe 'Ojibwe' (also rendered as 'Chippewa' in English).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪpəˌwɑ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpəwə / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Ojibwa people of North America
Algonquian language of the Ojibwa

Examples

She is studying Chippewa to better understand her family's heritage.
The Chippewa language has many dialects spoken across the Great Lakes region.
Several schools now offer courses in Chippewa to preserve indigenous languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwa - another name for the Ojibwa language and people

Etymology

From Ojibwa 'Ojibwe', altered through English and French contact.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06924142-n
Synset POS: n
