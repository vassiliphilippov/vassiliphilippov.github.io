carbuncled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrˌbʌŋkəld / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌbʌŋk(ə)ld / (BrE)
afflicted with or covered by carbuncles; resembling a carbuncle in appearance
afflicted with carbuncles

Examples

The old man's carbuncled nose glowed red in the candlelight.
The villain in the novel had a carbuncled complexion that made him appear even more sinister.
Her hands were carbuncled and swollen from years of difficult labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbuncular - related to or afflicted by carbuncles

Etymology

From 'carbuncle' (a painful cluster of boils), with the suffix '-ed' forming the adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrˌbʌŋkəld / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌbʌŋkəld / (BrE)
covered with or adorned by carbuncles (either the precious stone or, less commonly, red pustules); set with carbuncles.
set with carbuncles or covered in carbuncles

Examples

The carbuncled crowns sparkled on the royal statues in the ancient hall.
His carbuncled nose glowed red under the dim tavern light.
Legends spoke of a carbuncled chalice hidden deep in the caverns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeweled - adorned with jewels
  • Antonym: unadorned - not decorated or ornamented

Etymology

From 'carbuncle' + '-ed'. 'Carbuncle' from Latin 'carbunculus' (small coal, precious stone).
