Xanthophyceae

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθɑːfəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / zanˌθɒfɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a class of yellow-green algae chiefly found in fresh water, distinguished by their pigment composition and cell structure
a class of yellow-green algae
medical

Examples

Members of the Xanthophyceae are characterized by the yellow-green color of their chloroplasts.
Scientists often study Xanthophyceae to understand pigment diversity in aquatic environments.
Unlike other algae, Xanthophyceae have distinctive cell wall compositions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: algae - simple non-flowering plants including seaweeds and freshwater forms

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + phykos (seaweed, algae), with scientific Latin suffix -eae.

2. NOUN

/ zænˌθɑfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / zanˌθɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of yellow-green algae that are mainly found in freshwater and contain distinctive pigments giving them their characteristic color
a class of yellow-green algae

Examples

Members of the Xanthophyceae are often significant in the plankton communities of freshwater habitats.
Some researchers study the unique pigments produced by Xanthophyceae to understand their adaptation to light conditions.
The cell walls of most Xanthophyceae are composed primarily of cellulose and occasionally silica.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) + 'phyke' (seaweed) + Latin '-ae' (plural suffix for classes).
