Rallidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈrælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈralɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of medium-sized birds including rails, crakes, gallinules, and coots, often found in wetlands.
family of birds: rails, crakes, gallinules, coots

Examples

The Rallidae are known for their elusive behavior and habitat in dense wetlands.
Many conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Rallidae species worldwide.
Among the Rallidae, the coot is perhaps the most commonly observed by birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - any bird of the family Rallidae, typically found in marshes

Etymology

From modern Latin Rallidae, from Rallus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
