zeal

1. NOUN

/ ziːl / (AmE), / ziːl / (BrE)
great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective.
great enthusiasm or eagerness

Examples

She approached her new job with remarkable zeal and dedication.
The volunteers worked with zeal to clean up the neighborhood park.
His zeal for environmental protection inspired many others to get involved.

Synset Examples

they were imbued with a revolutionary ardor
he felt a kind of religious zeal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardor - intense enthusiasm or passion
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Greek zēlos (ardor, rivalry) via Latin zelus.

2. NOUN

/ zil / (AmE), / ziːl / (BrE)
Great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective, sometimes to an excessive degree.
great or excessive enthusiasm

Examples

Her zeal for learning impressed all her teachers.
He approached each new challenge with remarkable zeal.
Sometimes, too much zeal can become overwhelming for others.

Synset Examples

he had an absolute zeal for litigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Late Latin 'zelus' (ardor, emulation, jealousy), from Greek 'zēlos' (zeal, ardour, jealousy).

3. NOUN

/ zil / (AmE), / ziːl / (BrE)
great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective; eager willingness to do something.
great enthusiasm or eagerness

Examples

Her zeal for volunteer work inspired others to get involved in community service.
The teacher's zeal for education was evident in every lesson she taught.
He attacked the new project with remarkable zeal, completing his work ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

readiness to continue discussions
they showed no eagerness to spread the gospel
they disliked his zeal in demonstrating his superiority
he tried to explain his forwardness in battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek zēlos (ardour, emulation) via Latin zelus.
