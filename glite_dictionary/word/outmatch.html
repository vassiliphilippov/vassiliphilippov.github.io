to outmatch

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈmætʃ / (AmE), / aʊtˈmatʃ / (BrE)
to surpass or do better than someone or something; to exceed in performance or ability
surpass; do better than

Examples

Despite his experience, he could not outmatch the champion in the final round.
Their innovative design outmatched all the competitors at the convention.
If you want to outmatch your rival, you'll need to work even harder.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be better than; to go beyond in achievement or quality
  • Synonym: to outperform - to perform better than someone or something
  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than; to exceed in performance
  • Antonym: to underperform - to perform less well than expected or less well than others

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (beyond) + 'match' (to equal or be comparable to).
