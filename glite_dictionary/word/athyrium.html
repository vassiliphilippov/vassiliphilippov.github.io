Athyrium

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / əˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly temperate and tropical lady ferns, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae or genus Asplenium.
genus of lady ferns

Examples

Athyrium is commonly known as lady fern and is popular in shade gardens.
Researchers found a new species within the Athyrium genus in tropical Asia.
The delicate fronds of Athyrium make it a favorite among horticulturists.

Etymology

From Greek athyros (doorless), referring to the lack of indusium in some species.

2. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / əˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of lady ferns found in temperate and tropical regions, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae or the genus Asplenium.
genus of lady ferns

Examples

The Athyrium genus includes several species prized for their delicate, lacy foliage.
Gardeners often plant Athyrium to add texture to shady borders and woodlands.
Some botanists debate whether Athyrium should be classified in its own family or within Polypodiaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady fern - a common name for ferns in the Athyrium genus

Etymology

From Greek 'athýrios', meaning ‘doorless’, referring to the appearance of the sporangia.
