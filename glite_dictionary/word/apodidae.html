Apodidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑdəˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds known as swifts, characterized by slender bodies and long, narrow wings, formerly sometimes included in the order Coraciiformes
family of birds known as swifts

Examples

The family Apodidae includes over a hundred species of swifts found worldwide.
Members of Apodidae are known for their aerial agility and speed.
Ornithologists debate whether Apodidae should be placed in its own order or remain within Coraciiformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift - a small, fast-flying bird of the family Apodidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Apodidae', from 'Apus' (genus name for swifts), itself from Greek 'apous' meaning 'footless'.
