Myrmecophaga

1. NOUN

/ mɜrməˈkɑfəɡə / (AmE), / mɜːmɪˈkəʊfəɡə / (BrE)
A genus of large, insect-eating mammals native to Central and South America, including the giant anteater.
genus of giant anteaters

Examples

The Myrmecophaga genus includes the fascinating giant anteater found in South America.
Researchers studied the diet of Myrmecophaga to better understand its ecological impact.
The Myrmecophaga typically uses its long tongue to extract ants and termites from nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - a mammal that feeds mostly on ants and termites

Etymology

From Greek myrmēx (ant) + phagein (to eat), referring to ant-eating habits.
