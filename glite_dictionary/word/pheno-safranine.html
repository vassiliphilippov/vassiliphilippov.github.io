pheno-safranine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnoʊˈsæfrəniːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəʊˈsæfrəniːn / (BrE)
a purplish red, water-soluble dye used in microscopy for staining specimens
a purplish red dye used in microscopy
medical

Examples

The tissue sample was stained with pheno-safranine to highlight cellular structures.
Researchers commonly use pheno-safranine in histological studies for its intense color contrast.
After treating the slide with pheno-safranine, the bacteria became visible under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safranine - a synthetic dye used in staining microscopy specimens

Etymology

Derived from 'phen-' (relating to phenol chemistry) and 'safranine', a type of synthetic dye.
