unsoundness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not valid, true, or logically sound; the presence of a mistaken or fallacious basis
state of being not valid or logically sound

Examples

The unsoundness of his argument was quickly exposed by the experts.
Many were convinced by the proposal, despite its obvious unsoundness.
She criticized the unsoundness in their reasoning during the debate.

Synset Examples

the unsoundness of his conclusion was obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or incorrect belief
  • Antonym: validity - the quality of being logically or factually sound

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'sound' (in the sense of solid, reliable) + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
The state or condition of being damaged, decayed, or not structurally sound.
the condition of being damaged or decayed

Examples

The unsoundness of the bridge was revealed during the safety inspection.
Doctors found unsoundness in the horse's leg, making it unfit for racing.
Prolonged moisture can contribute to the unsoundness of wooden structures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soundness - the state of being in good condition or undamaged
  • Synonym: decay - gradual destruction or decomposition

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'sound' (free from injury, stable) + suffix '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsaʊndnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊndnɪs / (BrE)
the state of not being mentally or physically healthy; a condition of instability or lack of soundness in mind or body
state of not being healthy or stable

Examples

The psychiatrist questioned the unsoundness of his patient's judgment during the interviews.
Some legal contracts can be invalidated due to the mental unsoundness of one of the participants.
She was dismissed from the training program because of a suspected unsoundness of health.

Synset Examples

no one can be a poet without a certain unsoundness of mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - lack of firmness or stability
  • Antonym: soundness - the state of being whole, healthy, or stable

Etymology

From 'unsound' (not healthy, not stable) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).
