anticlimax

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
A shift from an exciting or serious point to one that is disappointing or less important, especially in speech or writing.
disappointing shift from excitement

Examples

The ending of the movie was an anticlimax after so much built-up tension.
When the surprise guest turned out to be the janitor, it was a real anticlimax.
The party promised excitement, but the rain made it a disappointing anticlimax.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climax - the most intense or exciting point in something

Etymology

From Latin 'anticlimax', patterned after 'climax' (ladder, ascent).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
A disappointing or unsatisfying end or decline following an exciting or impressive build-up.
a disappointing decline after a peak

Examples

The end of the novel was an unexpected anticlimax after so much suspense.
After hours of buildup, the final scene felt like a bit of an anticlimax.
His retirement was considered an anticlimax after his illustrious career.

Synset Examples

the anticlimax of a brilliant career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letdown - a disappointing or frustrating experience
  • Antonym: climax - the most exciting or important point, usually at the end of something

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + climax (ladder, staircase), meaning a reversal from a climax.
