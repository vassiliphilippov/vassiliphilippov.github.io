Diospyros kaki

1. NOUN

/ diˈɑːspɪˌrɒs ˈkɑːki / (AmE), / daɪˈɒspɪˌrɒs ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia, cultivated for its large, red or orange edible but astringent fruit, commonly known as the persimmon.
oriental persimmon tree species

Examples

The Diospyros kaki is widely grown in East Asia for its delicious fruit.
Leaves from the Diospyros kaki turn a beautiful orange in autumn.
Our orchard includes several Diospyros kaki trees, which produce fruit each October.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persimmon - the edible fruit produced by Diospyros kaki

Etymology

From Greek 'diospyros', meaning 'divine fruit', and Japanese 'kaki' for the fruit.
