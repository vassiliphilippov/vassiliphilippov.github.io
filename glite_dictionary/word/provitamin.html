provitamin

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊˈvaɪtəmɪn / (AmE), / ˌprəʊˈvɪtəmɪn / (BrE)
a substance that can be converted within the body into a vitamin
substance converted into a vitamin
medical

Examples

Beta-carotene is a well-known provitamin that the body can convert into vitamin A.
Some foods are valued for their high provitamin content, especially in vegetarian diets.
Research focuses on how efficiently the body transforms each provitamin into its corresponding vitamin.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vitamin - an organic compound required in small quantities for metabolism

Etymology

From prefix 'pro-' meaning 'before' and 'vitamin', indicating a precursor to a vitamin.
