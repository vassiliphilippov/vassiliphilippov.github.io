pedant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛdənt / (BrE)
A person who is excessively concerned with minor details, rules, or displaying academic learning.
person overly concerned with rules or learning

Examples

He can be such a pedant about grammar that it's hard to enjoy a conversation.
As a self-confessed pedant, she insisted on correcting every minor error on the report.
The meeting was delayed by a pedant who argued every tiny point of procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickler - person who insists on a certain quality or type of behavior
  • Degree Higher: scholar - person highly educated or with profound knowledge
  • Degree Lower: novice - person new to or inexperienced in a field

Etymology

From French pédant, from Italian pedante (teacher, schoolmaster).
