to underact

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈækt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrækt / (BrE)
to perform (a role) with too much restraint or subtlety, often resulting in a lack of emotional expression
act a role too restrainedly

Examples

He tends to underact in emotional scenes, making his character seem flat.
Directors often warn actors not to underact or the audience may not connect with the story.
In this film, she deliberately chose to underact in contrast to her co-star's intense style.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overact - to act in a role with exaggerated emotion or energy

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' meaning 'insufficiently' and 'act'.
