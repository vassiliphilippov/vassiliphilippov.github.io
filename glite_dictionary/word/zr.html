Zr

1. NOUN

/ zi ɑr / (AmE), / ziː ɑː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for zirconium, a lustrous grey, strong metallic element used in nuclear reactors and obtained chiefly from zircon
chemical symbol for zirconium

Examples

The reactor core is constructed using Zr alloys for better neutron absorption.
Scientists analyzed the impurities present in the Zr metal samples.
When writing the formula, always use Zr to represent zirconium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zirconium - a lustrous grey strong metallic element, symbol Zr

Etymology

From 'zirconium', based on the mineral 'zircon'. Chemical symbols use Latin or source name abbreviations.
