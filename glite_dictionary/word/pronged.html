pronged

1. ADJ

/ prɔŋd / (AmE), / prɒŋd / (BrE)
having one or more thin pointed parts that stick out from a central point, often in the manner of a fork or branch.
having projecting pointed parts, like a fork

Examples

The electrician used a two-pronged adapter to plug in the device.
A fork has several pronged tines for picking up food.
The lightning struck the tree with a pronged flash that split into two branches.

Synset Examples

the biramous appendages of an arthropod
long branched hairs on its legs on which pollen collects
a forked river
a forked tail
forked lightning
horseradish grown in poor soil may develop prongy roots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forked - divided into two or more branches or prongs

Etymology

From 'prong' + '-ed'; 'prong' from Middle English 'pronge' (pointed instrument).

2. ADJ

/ prɔŋd / (AmE), / prɒŋd / (BrE)
having prongs or tines; typically used in combination to indicate the number of prongs.
having prongs or tines

Examples

The gardener used a pronged tool to dig up the weeds.
This three-pronged approach should ensure success.
He was startled by the sharp, pronged shadow on the wall.

Synset Examples

a three-tined fork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tined - having tines; furnished with pointed projections

Etymology

From 'prong' (a pointed projection), with the adjective-forming suffix '-ed.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02492187-s
Synset POS: s
