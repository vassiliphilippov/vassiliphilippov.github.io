Volga

1. PROPN

/ ˈvoʊlɡə / (AmE), / ˈvɒlɡə / (BrE)
the longest river in Europe, located in Russia, flowing into the Caspian Sea
the longest river in Europe, in Russia

Examples

The Volga River is a crucial waterway for transportation and trade in Russia.
Many towns and cities were established along the Volga due to its historic significance.
During the summer, tourists often take cruises on the Volga to enjoy scenic views.

Etymology

From Russian Волга (Volga).
