intimidating

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɪməˌdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing fear or nervousness, often making someone feel less confident or hesitant
causing fear or nervousness

Examples

The interview felt especially intimidating because the panel asked so many tough questions.
Standing before such a large audience can be intimidating for new speakers.
Her intimidating manner made it hard for coworkers to approach her with questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frightening - making someone afraid or anxious
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or more confident

Etymology

From Latin intimidare, from in- (in) + timidus (fearful).
