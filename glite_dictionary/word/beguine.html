beguine

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡin / (AmE), / bəˈɡiːn / (BrE)
A slow, rhythmic ballroom dance that originated in the French West Indies and is similar to the rumba.
Caribbean ballroom dance, like rumba

Examples

The orchestra played a lively beguine, filling the dance floor with swaying couples.
She learned to dance the beguine while visiting Martinique.
The rhythmic steps of the beguine are said to evoke the spirit of the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a ballroom dance of Cuban origin with a similar rhythm

Etymology

From French 'beguine', probably from the name of the dance's origin in the French West Indies.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈɡin / (AmE), / bəˈɡiːn / (BrE)
a dance and music style from the French Caribbean, characterized by a bolero rhythm, popularized in ballroom and Latin music
Caribbean dance and music style

Examples

The orchestra played a lively beguine that got everyone on the dance floor.
She learned to dance the beguine while visiting Martinique.
The composer blended elements of jazz and the beguine in his new piece.

Etymology

From French 'beguine', name of the dance; possibly from the Caribbean island of Martinique.
