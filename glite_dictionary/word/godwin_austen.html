Godwin Austen

1. PROPN

/ ˈgɑd.wɪn ˈɔː.stɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.wɪn ˈɔː.stɪn / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Karakoram Range in northern Kashmir, also known as K2; the second highest mountain in the world, standing 28,251 feet (8,611 meters) high.
second highest mountain (K2) in the Karakoram Range

Examples

The climbers prepared for their ascent of Godwin Austen, braving treacherous conditions.
Due to its extreme difficulty, Godwin Austen has earned a reputation among mountaineers worldwide.
Photographs of Godwin Austen reveal its imposing, pyramid-shaped peak rising above the Karakoram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: K2 - the world's second highest mountain, also called Godwin Austen

Etymology

Named after English geologist Henry Haversham Godwin-Austen, who surveyed the region in the 19th century.
