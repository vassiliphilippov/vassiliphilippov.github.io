Arundo conspicua

1. NOUN

/ əˈrʌndoʊ kənˈspɪkjuə / (AmE), / əˈrʌndəʊ kənˈspɪkjʊə / (BrE)
a tall New Zealand grass with feathery flower heads, traditionally used by Māori people for thatching roofs
a tall New Zealand grass used for thatching

Examples

The roof of the hut was made from layers of Arundo conspicua, providing excellent shelter from the rain.
In traditional Māori construction, Arundo conspicua was prized for its durability and insulating properties.
Fields of Arundo conspicua waved in the breeze near the village.

Etymology

From Latin: ‘arundo’ meaning ‘reed’ and ‘conspicua’ meaning ‘conspicuous’ or ‘notable’.
