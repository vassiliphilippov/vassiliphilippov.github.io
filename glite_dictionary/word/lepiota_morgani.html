Lepiota morgani

1. NOUN

/ ˌlepiˈoʊtə ˈmɔrɡəni / (AmE), / ˌlepiˈəʊtə ˈmɔːɡəni / (BrE)
A large, poisonous species of mushroom found in North America, also known as the false parasol.
a poisonous mushroom species

Examples

The Lepiota morgani is considered one of the most dangerous wild mushrooms in North America.
Foragers are warned never to consume a Lepiota morgani due to its toxic properties.
When identifying wild fungi, it's crucial to distinguish Lepiota morgani from edible mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorophyllum molybdites - another name for Lepiota morgani or a closely related mushroom species

Etymology

Named after Italian mycologist Giovanni Battista Morgagni; Lepiota from Greek 'lepis' (scale) and '-otes' (quality).
