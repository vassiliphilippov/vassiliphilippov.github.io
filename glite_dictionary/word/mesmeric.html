mesmeric

1. ADJ

/ mɛzˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / mɛzˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
Having the power to attract and hold attention as if by a spell; fascinating or hypnotic.
fascinating; holding attention like a spell

Examples

The dancer's mesmeric performance held the audience in total silence.
He spoke with a mesmeric calm that made everyone relax.
She found his eyes oddly mesmeric, unable to look away.

Synset Examples

read the bedtime story in a hypnotic voice
she had a warm mesmeric charm
the sheer force of his presence was mesmerizing
a spellbinding description of life in ancient Rome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnotic - having a sleep-inducing or spellbinding effect
  • Synonym: spellbinding - holding attention as if by magic
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From French 'mésmérique', from 'Mesmer', the name of Franz Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who developed mesmerism (hypnotism).
