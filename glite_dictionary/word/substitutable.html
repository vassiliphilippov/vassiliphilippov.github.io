substitutable

1. ADJ

/ səbˈstɪtʃuˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈstɪtjʊtəbl / (BrE)
able to be replaced or exchanged for something else of the same kind or value.
able to be replaced or exchanged

Examples

Plastic parts are often substitutable with metal ones in car manufacturing.
The ingredients in this recipe are fully substitutable if you have allergies.
These economic goods are perfectly substitutable in the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replaceable - able to be replaced
  • Antonym: irreplaceable - impossible to replace

Etymology

From 'substitute' + '-able', suffix meaning capable of.

2. ADJ

/ səbˈstɪtʃuˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / səbˈstɪtjʊtəbəl / (BrE)
(of words, things, etc.) able to be replaced or exchanged with something else, especially without changing meaning or function.
able to be replaced or exchanged

Examples

These two terms are not always substitutable in technical writing.
Pepsi and Coke are often considered substitutable products in market analysis.
In the sentence, 'shop' and 'store' are substitutable without changing the meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged with each other without any difference
  • Synonym: replaceable - able to be replaced with something else

Etymology

From 'substitute' + '-able', modelled on other adjectives with this form.

Source: WordNet
