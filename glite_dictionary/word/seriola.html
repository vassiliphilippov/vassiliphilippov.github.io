Seriola

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈraɪ.ələ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪ.ələ / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, including the amberjacks.
a genus of amberjack fish

Examples

The genus Seriola includes several species of large game fish known as amberjacks.
Marine biologists study the distribution of Seriola in tropical and subtropical oceans.
The Seriola species are popular targets for sport fishing due to their size and strength.

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive form of 'seria', meaning a kind of fish.

A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as amberjacks.
genus of fishes (amberjacks)

Examples

Fishermen caught a large Seriola off the coast of Florida.
The Seriola species are popular targets for sport fishing due to their strength and speed.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of the Seriola genus.

Related Terms
