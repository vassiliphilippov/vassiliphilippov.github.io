rhetorically

1. ADV

/ rəˈθɔːrɪkli / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses language effectively or persuasively, often to make a point rather than to get an answer.
in a rhetorical manner

Examples

She asked the question rhetorically, knowing no answer was expected.
The politician used exaggeration rhetorically to emphasize his point.
He smiled and shrugged rhetorically during the debate.

Synset Examples

‘What can be done?’ he asked rhetorically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetorical - relating to effective or persuasive speaking or writing

Etymology

From 'rhetorical', which comes from Latin 'rhetoricus', from Greek 'rhētorikos' (oratorical).
