to scrutinize

1. VERB

/ ˈskruːtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskruːtənaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something very carefully, especially in order to discover information or verify accuracy
examine closely and carefully

Examples

The teacher will scrutinize every essay for plagiarism before grading.
After the accident, investigators scrutinized the train's safety record for possible failures.
She scrutinized the document, searching for any discrepancies in the details.

Synset Examples

audit accounts and tax returns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something closely to assess its condition or quality
  • Synonym: scan - to look at all parts of something carefully in order to detect some feature

Etymology

From Latin scrutinium (inquiry), from scrutari (to search or examine).

2. VERB

/ ˈskruːtɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskruːtənaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something very carefully and thoroughly, often in order to find information or detect flaws
examine closely and thoroughly

Examples

The committee will scrutinize the new proposal before making a decision.
She scrutinized the fine print of the contract for hidden fees.
Every detail was scrutinized during the inspection to ensure quality standards were met.

Synset Examples

he scrutinized his likeness in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it, find problems, etc.
  • Synonym: to examine - to look at something closely and carefully

Etymology

From Late Latin 'scrutinium' (inquiry), from Latin 'scrutari' (to examine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02157817-v
Synset POS: v
