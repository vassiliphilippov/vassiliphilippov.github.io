howler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlə / (BrE)
A joke or remark that is extremely funny and causes loud laughter, often used informally.
extremely funny joke or remark
informal

Examples

Last night's comedy show was full of howlers that had everyone in stitches.
He told a howler at the party that made even the serious guests laugh out loud.
That was such a howler—I couldn't stop laughing for minutes afterward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side-splitter - an extremely funny joke or story

Etymology

Originally from 'howl' (to emit a loud cry), referring to causing people to 'howl' with laughter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlə / (BrE)
a glaring or ridiculous mistake, especially one that is embarrassing or amusing
a glaring or amusing mistake
informal british

Examples

The newspaper published a howler in yesterday’s headline, spelling the prime minister’s name incorrectly.
His answer in the exam was a real howler, confusing Austria with Australia.
She burst out laughing when she spotted the howler in the company’s annual report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional and embarrassing error
  • Antonym: triumph - a great victory or achievement, opposite to a blunder

Etymology

From 'howl' + '-er', originally meaning something that makes people howl (with laughter or disbelief) at its absurdity.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlə / (BrE)
A large monkey found in the forests of Central and South America, known for its loud, howling calls.
monkey known for howling cries

Examples

We spotted a group of howlers swinging high up in the trees.
The loud calls of the howler could be heard for several miles through the jungle.
Researchers observed the sleeping habits of a young male howler in the rainforest.
