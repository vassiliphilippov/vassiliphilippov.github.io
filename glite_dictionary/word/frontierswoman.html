frontierswoman

1. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪrzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / frʌnˈtɪəzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who lives or settles on the frontier, especially during periods of expansion or colonization.
woman living on the frontier

Examples

The life of a frontierswoman in the 1800s demanded resilience and courage.
As a frontierswoman, she learned to hunt, farm, and defend her home.
Stories about the American West often highlight the adventures of the frontierswoman as well as the frontiersman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frontiersman - a man who lives on the frontier

Etymology

Formed in English from 'frontier' + 'woman', modeled on 'frontiersman'.
