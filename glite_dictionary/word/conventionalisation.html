conventionalisation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɛnʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌvɛnʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something conform to a conventional or established style or practice
process of adopting a conventional style
british

Examples

The conventionalisation of new art movements often leads to a loss of their original radical spirit.
Over time, the conventionalisation of language helps communities understand one another more easily.
There was criticism about the conventionalisation of once experimental theatre techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventionalization - the process of making something conventional (American spelling)
  • Antonym: innovation - the process of introducing new ideas or methods

Etymology

From conventional + -isation (British spelling of -ization).
