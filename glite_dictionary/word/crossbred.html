crossbred

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɔsˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsbrɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) bred from parents of different varieties or species.
bred from different varieties or species

Examples

The farmer introduced several crossbred cattle to improve milk production on his farm.
Many crossbred dogs are valued for their unique combination of traits.
The research focused on the adaptability of crossbred plants in harsh climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - an animal or plant produced by combining different varieties or species

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' (meaning to mix breeds) + 'bred' (past participle of 'breed').
