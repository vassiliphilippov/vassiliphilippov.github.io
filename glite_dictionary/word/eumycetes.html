Eumycetes

1. NOUN

/ juːmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / juːmʌɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A group or division in some classifications comprising the true fungi, distinct from slime molds and other fungus-like organisms.
group of true fungi

Examples

Scientists studied the characteristics of Eumycetes to better understand fungal evolution.
Unlike slime molds, Eumycetes possess cell walls containing chitin.
Many plant diseases are caused by pathogenic species of Eumycetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - any member of the kingdom Fungi, including yeasts, molds, and mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek eu- (true, good) + mykēs (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ juːmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / juːmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a major group or class of true fungi, used in some biological classifications, equivalent to the division Eumycota
group or class of true fungi

Examples

The Eumycetes are distinguished from other fungi by their cell wall composition.
Many plant diseases are caused by species within the Eumycetes.
Taxonomists debate the boundaries of the Eumycetes in modern classification systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eumycota - division of true fungi

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, true) + 'mykēs' (fungus).
