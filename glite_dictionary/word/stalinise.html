to stalinise

1. VERB

/ ˈstɑːləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstælɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to transform (a country, institution, etc.) in accordance with the policies and methods of Joseph Stalin, especially involving centralized control and repression
transform according to Stalin's policies

Examples

After the revolution, the new government attempted to stalinise the entire political system.
Many intellectuals fled when the universities began to stalinise their curricula.
The party was quickly stalinised, discouraging any form of dissent.

Synset Examples

Russia was slowly stalinized after Lenin's death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stalinize - Alternate spelling, mainly US English, of 'stalinise'

Etymology

From Stalin (surname of Joseph Stalin, Soviet leader) + -ise (verb-forming suffix).
