Dama dama

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪmə ˈdeɪmə / (AmE), / ˌdɑːmə ˈdɑːmə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the fallow deer, a small Eurasian species with spotted coat and broad, palmate antlers.
scientific name for fallow deer

Examples

The natural habitat of Dama dama extends across much of Europe and western Asia.
Researchers observed a herd of Dama dama grazing peacefully in the meadow.
The palmate antlers of Dama dama distinguish it from other deer species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallow deer - a small Eurasian deer with spotted coat and broad antlers

Etymology

From Latin dama (deer, fallow deer).
