pigweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌwiːd / (BrE)
a common weedy plant, especially of the genus Amaranthus, often used as a potherb and widespread in Europe and North America.
a common weedy plant, often used as a potherb

Examples

The farmer cleared the pigweed from his field to protect his crops.
Some people cook pigweed leaves as a nutritious vegetable dish.
Pigweed often grows rapidly in disturbed soils and gardens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡ.wiːd / (BrE)
any of several annual herbaceous plants of the genus Amaranthus, some of which are used as potherbs or for their seeds as cereal.
a type of Amaranthus plant, often used as food

Examples

The farmer cleared pigweed from his fields to protect his crops.
Some communities cook pigweed leaves as a nutritious vegetable.
During the summer, pigweed often overruns neglected gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus, often cultivated for edible leaves or seeds

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several types of edible or weedy plants of the genus Amaranthus, whose leaves are sometimes collected from the wild and eaten as food.
edible wild plant of the Amaranthus genus

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control pigweed in their fields because it grows rapidly.
In some cultures, the leaves of pigweed are cooked and eaten as a nutritious vegetable.
After the rains, we gathered fresh pigweed from the countryside to add to our soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - plant genus that includes pigweeds
