headsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdz.mən / (AmE), / ˈhɛdz.mən / (BrE)
a man whose job was to execute people by beheading them, especially in the past
a male executioner who beheads

Examples

The dreaded headsman stood ready with his axe beside the scaffold.
In medieval times, the headsman was both feared and reviled by the townspeople.
The king called upon the royal headsman to perform the execution at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: executioner - a person who carries out a death sentence

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + '-sman', with 'head' referring to beheading.
