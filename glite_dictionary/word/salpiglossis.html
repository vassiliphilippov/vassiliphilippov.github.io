salpiglossis

1. NOUN

/ sæl.pɪˈɡlɑː.sɪs / (AmE), / sæl.pɪˈɡlɒ.sɪs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Salpiglossis, native to South America, known for its vividly colored, trumpet-shaped flowers.
a genus of colorful South American flowering plants

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a rare salpiglossis with velvety purple blooms.
Many horticulturists value salpiglossis for its striking and intricate flowers.
She planted several salpiglossis seedlings along the fence in early spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'salpinx' (trumpet) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the flower's shape.
