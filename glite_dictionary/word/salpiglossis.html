Salpiglossis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælpɪˈɡlɒsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsælpɪˈɡlɒsɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering herbs native to the southern Andes, known for their large, showy flowers.
genus of Andean flowering herbs

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Salpiglossis make it a popular choice in ornamental gardens.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Salpiglossis genus high in the Andes mountains.
Many gardeners appreciate Salpiglossis for its striking, velvety blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek *salpinx* (trumpet) and *glōssa* (tongue), referring to the flower shape.
