Calocarpum zapota

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɑːrpəm zəˈpoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɑːpəm zəˈpəʊtə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree with wood similar to mahogany and sweet, edible, egg-shaped fruit; sometimes classified in the genus Calocarpum.
tropical American fruit tree

Examples

The fruit of the Calocarpum zapota is prized for its sweet flavor in Central America.
Timber from the Calocarpum zapota is sometimes used as a substitute for mahogany in furniture.
Scientists studied the medicinal properties of Calocarpum zapota in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapodilla - Another name for the same tropical fruit tree species
