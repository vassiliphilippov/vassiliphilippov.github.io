Parthia

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑːrθiə / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθɪə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in southwestern Asia, southeast of the Caspian Sea, which dominated the region from about 250 BC to AD 226.
ancient kingdom in southwestern Asia

Examples

The armies of Parthia were renowned for their cavalry tactics.
Trade routes passed through Parthia during its height as an empire.
Scholars study the influence of Parthia on the history of Central Asia.

Etymology

From Latin Parthia, from Ancient Greek Parthua, from Old Persian Parthava.
