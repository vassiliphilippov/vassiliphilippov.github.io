Lanthanotus

1. NOUN

/ lænˈθæn.ə.təs / (AmE), / lanˈθæn.ə.təs / (BrE)
A genus of lizard containing the species Lanthanotus borneensis, commonly known as the earless monitor lizard, native to Borneo.
genus of earless monitor lizard

Examples

Scientists recently discovered several new specimens of Lanthanotus in the wild.
The unique scale pattern of Lanthanotus distinguishes it from other lizards native to Southeast Asia.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of Lanthanotus to assist in conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lanthanotus borneensis - species of earless monitor lizard in Borneo

Etymology

From Greek 'lanthanō' (to lie hidden) and 'ōtus' (ear), referring to the lizard's hidden or absent external ears.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlænθəˈnoʊtəs / (AmE), / ˌlanθəˈnəʊtəs / (BrE)
a genus of reptiles containing only the earless monitor lizard (Lanthanotus borneensis), native to Borneo
genus with the earless monitor lizard

Examples

Scientists discovered a new specimen of Lanthanotus in the rainforests of Borneo.
The genus Lanthanotus is notable for its lack of external ear openings.
Conservationists are working to protect Lanthanotus from habitat loss in its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lanthanotus borneensis - the only species in the Lanthanotus genus, the earless monitor lizard

Etymology

From Greek lanthanein (to lie hidden) and ous, ōtos (ear), referring to its lack of external ears.
