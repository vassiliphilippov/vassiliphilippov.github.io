Jacobinical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒæ.kəˈbɪn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒæk.əˈbɪn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the Jacobins of the French Revolution, especially for their radical or revolutionary political views or actions.
relating to the Jacobins (French Revolution)

Examples

The government feared further Jacobinical uprisings would destabilize the region.
Her Jacobinical rhetoric alarmed more moderate members of the assembly.
Historians debated whether the reforms were truly Jacobinical in spirit.

Synset Examples

Jacobinic terrorism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacobin - A member of a radical revolutionary club during the French Revolution

Etymology

From Jacobin, a member of the radical revolutionary group during the French Revolution, plus the suffix -ical.
