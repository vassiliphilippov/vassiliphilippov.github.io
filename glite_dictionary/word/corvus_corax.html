Corvus corax

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrvəs ˈkɔˌræks / (AmE), / ˈkɔːvəs ˈkɔːræks / (BrE)
the scientific name for the common raven, a large black bird with a straight bill and a long wedge-shaped tail
the common raven (scientific name)

Examples

The Corvus corax is renowned for its intelligence and adaptability.
Researchers tracked the movement patterns of several Corvus corax in the wild.
Many cultures have myths and legends featuring the Corvus corax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raven - a large black bird of the crow family (Corvus corax)

Etymology

From Latin: corvus meaning 'raven,' corax from Greek 'korax' meaning 'raven.'
