to encipher

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪfər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌɪfə / (BrE)
to convert (a message, text, or information) into a coded form so that it cannot be easily understood without the key
convert text into code

Examples

The software will encipher your personal data before storing it on the server.
During the war, soldiers had to encipher sensitive messages to keep them secret.
She used a complex algorithm to encipher the document, making it unreadable to unauthorized users.

Synset Examples

We should encode the message for security reasons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decipher - convert coded text back into ordinary language
  • Synonym: to encode - convert information into a particular form, often for secrecy
  • Synonym: to encrypt - secure data by converting it to a coded format

Etymology

From French 'chiffre' (cipher), influenced by 'en-' (to make or put into).
