Copernicia australis

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌpɜːrˈnɪʃiə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / kəʊˌpɜːnˈɪsɪə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of South American palm that produces a wax similar to carnauba wax
South American palm yielding wax

Examples

The leaves of the Copernicia australis are harvested for their valuable wax.
Many industries rely on wax extracted from Copernicia australis as an alternative to carnauba wax.
Botanists study Copernicia australis for its ecological and economic importance in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Copernicia prunifera - another species of palm known for producing carnauba wax
  • Synonym: carnauba wax - a yellowish wax obtained from the leaves of the carnauba palm

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus 'Copernicia' named after Nicolaus Copernicus; 'australis' from Latin meaning 'southern'.
