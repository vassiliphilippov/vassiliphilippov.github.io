St. Patrick

1. PROPN

/ sənt ˈpætrɪk / (AmE), / sənt ˈpætrɪk / (BrE)
The Apostle and patron saint of Ireland, a 5th-century English missionary who played a key role in converting Ireland to Christianity.
patron saint of Ireland

Examples

Every year, people around the world celebrate St. Patrick's Day with parades and festivities.
According to legend, St. Patrick used a shamrock to explain the Holy Trinity.
The story of St. Patrick is central to Irish heritage and culture.
