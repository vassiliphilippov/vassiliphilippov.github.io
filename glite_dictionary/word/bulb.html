bulb

1. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
An electric lamp with a transparent or translucent glass enclosure containing a wire filament that produces light when heated by electricity.
electric glass lamp with filament

Examples

Please replace the bulb in the lamp; it has burned out.
She bought an energy-saving bulb for the kitchen ceiling light.
When the bulb flickered, the room was plunged into darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light bulb - an electric light with a glass globe and metallic filament

Etymology

From Latin bulbus (bulb, onion), adopted into English in Middle English; sense evolved by analogy with the bulbous shape of the lamp.

3. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
(archaic) The lower or hindmost part of the brain, continuous with the spinal cord; an old term for the medulla oblongata.
(archaic) old term for medulla oblongata
archaic medical

Examples

In outdated medical texts, the term bulb is sometimes used instead of medulla oblongata.
Some early anatomists referred to the medulla as the bulb of the brain due to its shape.
The vital functions regulated by the bulb include breathing and heart rate.

Synset Examples

the medulla oblongata is the most vital part of the brain because it contains centers controlling breathing and heart functioning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medulla oblongata - the lower part of the brain stem, controlling vital body functions

Etymology

From Latin bulbus, meaning 'bulb, onion; any rounded swelling.'

6. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
a rounded underground storage organ in some plants, consisting of a short stem surrounded by fleshy leaf bases, which serves as a reproductive structure
underground plant storage organ

Examples

The tulip bulb should be planted in the autumn for spring flowers.
Onions are grown from a bulb that stores nutrients underground.
She carefully dug up the daffodil bulb to divide and replant it in another spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corm - a similar underground stem in plants, but solid rather than layered
  • Synonym: tuber - an enlarged storage organ in plants, such as a potato

Etymology

From Latin bulbus and Greek bolbos (plant with a rounded underground stem).
