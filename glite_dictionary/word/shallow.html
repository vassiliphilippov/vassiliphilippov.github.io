shallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊ / (BrE)
a place in a body of water where the depth is relatively low compared to surrounding areas
a stretch of shallow water

Examples

The boat ran aground on a shallow near the riverbank.
Fishermen often gather at a shallow where the water is warm.
Swimmers should be cautious when venturing beyond the shallows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoal - a naturally submerged ridge or bank of sand or gravel in a body of water

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊ / (BrE)
(of a person, idea, or argument) lacking depth of intellect, insight, or knowledge; superficial or concerned only with what is obvious.
lacking intellectual or emotional depth

Examples

Many critics dismissed the movie as shallow and unoriginal.
I found his response rather shallow, lacking any real understanding of the issue.
Despite the complicated words, the report was ultimately shallow in its analysis.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - concerned only with the surface or obvious; lacking depth
  • Antonym: deep - showing great knowledge or insight

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃæləʊ / (BrE)
Having little physical depth; not extending far down from the surface or outward from a center.
lacking physical depth

Examples

The river became very shallow near the banks, making it easy to wade across.
She placed the soup in a shallow dish so it would cool quickly.
Only shallow cuts were visible on his hand after the accident.

Related Terms
