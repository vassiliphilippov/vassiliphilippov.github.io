clamour

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmər / (AmE), / ˈklæmə / (BrE)
a loud and persistent outcry, especially from a large group of people, expressing public demand or protest
loud public outcry
british

Examples

A clamour of disapproval rose from the audience as the decision was announced.
The children made a clamour outside the school gates, waiting for the doors to open.
Despite the public clamour, the policy remained unchanged.

Synset Examples

he ignored the clamor of the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Antonym: silence - absence of noise or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'clamor' (shout, outcry) via Old French 'clamour'.

WordNet ID: oewn-07136826-n

2. VERB

/ ˈklæmər / (AmE), / ˈklæmə / (BrE)
to shout, demand, or proclaim something loudly and persistently
shout or proclaim insistently
british

Examples

Protesters began to clamour for the resignation of the prime minister.
Fans clamoured for an encore after the band's final song.
Journalists are clamouring for more information about the incident.

Synset Examples

The delegates clamored their disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - ask for something forcefully or insistently
  • Antonym: murmur - speak or make sound softly or indistinctly

Etymology

From Latin 'clamor' (shout, outcry), via Old French 'clamour'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00916994-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈklæmər / (AmE), / ˈklæmə / (BrE)
to demand something loudly or forcefully; to make a public outcry for something
demand something loudly

Examples

The crowd began to clamour for the speaker to answer more questions.
Children clamoured for ice cream as soon as the truck arrived.
Activists clamoured for stricter environmental regulations in front of the parliament.

Synset Examples

he clamored for justice and tolerance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - ask for something forcefully or authoritatively
  • Synonym: protest - express strong objection or disapproval publicly

Etymology

From Latin 'clamare' (to shout), via Old French 'clamer' and Middle English 'clamour'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00776686-v
Synset POS: v
