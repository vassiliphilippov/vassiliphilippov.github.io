euphorbium

1. NOUN

/ juˈfɔrbiəm / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪəm / (BrE)
an acrid, brownish gum resin obtained from certain species of spurge, formerly used in medicine and now mainly in veterinary practice
acrid gum resin used in veterinary medicine

Examples

The veterinarian decided to use euphorbium in a topical treatment for the injured horse.
Historically, euphorbium was considered a powerful purgative despite its acrid properties.
Collectors valued euphorbium for its rarity and distinctive resinous appearance.

Etymology

From Latin euphorbium, from Greek euphórbion, after Euphorbus, physician to King Juba II of Mauretania.
