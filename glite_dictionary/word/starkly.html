starkly

1. ADV

/ ˈstɑrk.li / (AmE), / ˈstɑːk.li / (BrE)
in a way that is clearly distinct, sharply outlined, or in strong contrast.
in a sharply distinct or contrasting way

Examples

The mountains stood starkly against the bright blue sky.
Economic inequalities are starkly visible in the city center.
Her opinion was starkly different from everyone else's in the meeting.

Synset Examples

the black walls rose starkly from the snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vividly - in a way that produces powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
  • Antonym: faintly - in a way that is weak, unclear, or not pronounced

Etymology

From the adjective 'stark' + the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈstɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈstɑːkli / (BrE)
In a way that is sharply clear or harsh; in a blunt or unadorned manner.
in a blunt or sharply clear manner

Examples

The documentary presented the facts starkly, without any attempt to soften the impact.
He spoke starkly about the company's financial problems to the shareholders.
The mountain stood out starkly against the clear morning sky.

Synset Examples

in starkly realistic terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntly - in a direct and unceremonious manner
  • Antonym: softly - in a gentle, mild, or less harsh manner

Etymology

From 'stark' (Middle English, from Old English 'stearc') + '-ly'.

3. ADV

/ ˈstɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈstɑːkli / (BrE)
In a way that is very plain, obvious, or sharply clear, often lacking any decoration or disguise.
in a plain or sharply clear manner

Examples

The economic disparities were starkly visible in the contrasting neighborhoods.
Her explanation was starkly honest, leaving no room for doubt.
The mountains stood out starkly against the bright morning sky.

Synset Examples

He was starkly unable to achieve coherence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plainly - in a clear and obvious manner
  • Degree Higher: vividly - in a way that is very clear, powerful, and detailed
  • Antonym: subtly - in a way that is not obvious or not easily noticed

Etymology

From 'stark' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Stark' from Old English 'stearc' (stiff, strong).
