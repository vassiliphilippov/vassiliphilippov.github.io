gainful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnfʊl / (BrE)
(of work, activity, etc.) producing money or other financial benefit; profitable.
producing profit or financial benefit

Examples

Many immigrants seek gainful employment as soon as they arrive in a new country.
He decided to leave the family business in search of more gainful work.
The charity helps the homeless find gainful occupations to support themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitable - yielding financial gain
  • Antonym: unprofitable - not yielding financial gain

Etymology

From Middle English *gain* (profit) + *-ful* (full of).
