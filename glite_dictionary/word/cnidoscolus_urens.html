Cnidoscolus urens

1. NOUN

/ niːdəˈskɑːləs ˈjʊrɛnz / (AmE), / nɪdɒˈskɒləs ˈjʊərɛnz / (BrE)
A species of stinging herb native to tropical America, known for its nettle-like hairs that can cause irritation upon contact.
American stinging herb species

Examples

People who touch the Cnidoscolus urens plant often experience a painful stinging sensation on their skin.
The dense tropical undergrowth was dotted with patches of Cnidoscolus urens.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of the hairs on Cnidoscolus urens to understand its defensive mechanisms.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'knidos' (nettle) and 'skolos' (thorn), with 'urens' meaning 'stinging' in Latin.
