Ichthyosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθioʊˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiəˈsɔːriə / (BrE)
an extinct order of marine reptiles, known as ichthyosaurs, that lived during the Mesozoic era and resembled modern dolphins.
order of extinct marine reptiles (ichthyosaurs)

Examples

Fossils from the Ichthyosauria provide valuable clues about marine life in the Mesozoic era.
Large members of the Ichthyosauria once dominated prehistoric seas alongside plesiosaurs and other marine reptiles.
The distinctive body shape of Ichthyosauria resembled that of present-day dolphins, despite their reptilian heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - any individual animal of the genus Ichthyosaurus, a type of marine reptile

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) + 'sauros' (lizard), referring to their fish-like appearance.
