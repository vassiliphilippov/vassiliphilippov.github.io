sunken

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌŋkən / (AmE), / ˈsʌŋ.kən / (BrE)
(of a surface or area) lower than the surrounding level; hollowed or depressed.
lowered or hollowed area

Examples

Her cheeks appeared sunken after several days of illness.
The garden featured a beautiful sunken area with a fountain.
Years of erosion had left a sunken path through the forest.

Synset Examples

hunger gave their faces a sunken look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed - of a surface lower than the surrounding area
  • Antonym: raised - of a surface higher than the surrounding area

Etymology

Past participle of 'sink', from Old English 'sincan'.
