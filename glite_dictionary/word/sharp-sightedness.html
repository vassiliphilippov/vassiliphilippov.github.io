sharp-sightedness

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp ˈsaɪtɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ʃɑːp ˈsaɪtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having sharp vision; the ability to see fine detail clearly
the quality of having sharp vision

Examples

Her sharp-sightedness allowed her to spot even the smallest details in the painting.
Owls are known for their remarkable sharp-sightedness, especially in low light conditions.
Regular eye examinations help to monitor changes in one's sharp-sightedness over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual acuity - sharpness of vision; the ability to see fine detail

2. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp ˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʃɑːp ˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being keenly observant and attentive; acute perceptiveness.
keen observant attentiveness

Examples

Her sharp-sightedness allowed her to notice the smallest details others missed.
The detective’s sharp-sightedness proved crucial in solving the complex case.
You need sharp-sightedness to succeed in such a fast-paced work environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alertness - state of being alert and attentive
  • Synonym: vigilance - careful watchfulness, especially for danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattentiveness - lack of attention

Etymology

Formed from 'sharp-sighted' + '-ness', following English morphological patterns.
