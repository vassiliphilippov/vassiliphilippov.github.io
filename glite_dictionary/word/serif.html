serif

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛr.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.ɪf / (BrE)
A small line or stroke regularly attached to the end of a larger stroke in a letter or symbol within a particular font.
small line at end of letter stroke

Examples

Many traditional newspapers use fonts with serifs for easier reading.
The designer chose a modern, serif typeface for the headings.
He prefers books printed in serif fonts over sans-serif ones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sans serif - typeface style without serifs

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'schreef' (line, written mark).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛr.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.ɪf / (BrE)
A short line or stroke regularly attached to the end of a larger stroke in a letter or symbol within a particular font or typeface.
short line or stroke at a letter's end

Examples

The designer chose a classic font with elegant serifs for the headline.
I prefer reading books in typefaces that have serifs, as they feel more traditional.
Modern digital screens often display serif fonts less clearly than sans-serif ones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sans-serif - a typeface without serifs

Etymology

From Dutch 'schreef' (line, stroke), influenced by French 'empattement' or German 'Serife'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06841111-n
Synset POS: n
