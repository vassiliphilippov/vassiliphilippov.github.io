Druse

1. NOUN

/ druːz / (AmE), / druːz / (BrE)
a member of an esoteric monotheistic religious sect, mainly living in Syria and Lebanon, whose followers believe Al-hakim was an incarnation of God
member of a Middle Eastern religious sect

Examples

Many Druse reside in the mountainous regions of Lebanon and Syria.
The Druse community has preserved its unique religious beliefs for centuries.
He wrote a book about the history and traditions of the Druse people.

Synset Examples

a Druze is permitted to conform outwardly to the faith of the unbelievers among whom he lives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Druze - member of a Middle Eastern religious sect

Etymology

From Arabic *Druze* (دروز‎), from the name of early leader al-Darazī.
