Ptilocrinus

1. NOUN

/ taɪˌlɒkrɪnəs / (AmE), / tɪˌlɒkrɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea lilies, marine animals belonging to the family Ptilocrinidae within the class Crinoidea.
genus of sea lilies (marine animals)

Examples

The Ptilocrinus fossils provide valuable insight into ancient marine ecosystems.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Ptilocrinus genus in deep-sea habitats.
Members of the genus Ptilocrinus are characterized by their feathery arms and stalked bodies.

Etymology

From Greek 'ptilon' (feather) and 'krinon' (lily), referring to the feather-like arms of sea lilies.
