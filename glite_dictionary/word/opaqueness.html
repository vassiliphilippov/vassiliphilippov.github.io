opaqueness

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈpeɪknəs / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being opaque and not allowing light to pass through
degree of being opaque

Examples

The opaqueness of the frosted glass prevented anyone from seeing inside the room.
She adjusted the curtains to increase their opaqueness and block the morning sunlight.
Scientists measured the opaqueness of the material to assess its suitability for laboratory use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque, also used in technical contexts
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of being transparent or clear

Etymology

From opaque + -ness; 'opaque' is from French 'opaque', from Latin 'opacus' (shaded, dark).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈpeɪknəs / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to understand due to obscurity of meaning
obscurity or incomprehensibility of meaning

Examples

The opaqueness of the legal document left many readers confused about its true implications.
Critics complained about the opaqueness of the poet's language, making the poems hard to interpret.
He was frustrated by the opaqueness of her explanation, which only added to his confusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being easy to understand

Etymology

From opaque + -ness, based on French opaque, from Latin opacus (shaded, dark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04830489-n
Synset POS: n
