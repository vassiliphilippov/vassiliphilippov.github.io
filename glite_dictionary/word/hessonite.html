hessonite

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛsəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛsənaɪt / (BrE)
a variety of garnet that ranges in color from yellow to brown, often used as a gemstone
a yellow to brown garnet gemstone

Examples

The jeweler showed us a rare hessonite set in a gold ring.
Collectors value hessonite for its warm, honey-like color.
He identified the orange-brown stone as a hessonite garnet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

From Greek hēssōn (inferior) + -ite, referring to its lower hardness compared to other garnets.
