deductible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʌktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪbəl / (BrE)
Able to be subtracted from an amount, especially from taxable income or tax liability.
eligible to be deducted (esp. for tax)

Examples

Only business-related expenses are considered deductible on your tax return.
Charitable donations up to a certain limit are deductible from your taxable income.
Some medical expenses may not be deductible unless they exceed a certain percentage of your income.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nondeductible - not able to be subtracted from taxable income

Etymology

From Latin 'deductibilis', from 'deducere' meaning 'to lead away, subtract'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʌktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktəbl̩ / (BrE)
a specified amount of money that the insured must pay before an insurance company will pay a claim
initial expense paid before insurance covers remaining costs

Examples

Our car insurance has a $500 deductible, so we pay that amount before coverage starts.
You can lower your monthly premium by choosing a higher deductible.
After she met her health plan’s deductible, the insurance began to cover most of her costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - amount payable by an insured person before insurance benefits apply (British usage)

Etymology

From Latin 'deductibilis', from 'deducere' (to deduct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06405067-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʌktəbl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktəbl / (BrE)
an amount of money that can be subtracted from your total income or tax when calculating the amount of tax you owe, especially for income tax purposes
amount subtracted from taxable income
legal

Examples

Your medical expenses may be listed as a deductible on your income tax form.
The accountant explained which business expenses were considered deductible for tax purposes.
She asked if her charitable donations would count as a deductible this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemption - an amount allowed by law that reduces taxable income
  • Synonym: allowance - a portion of income on which no tax is paid
  • Antonym: taxable - subject to being taxed; not deductible

Etymology

From Latin 'deducere' (to deduct), via 'deduct' + '-ible'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13294573-n
Synset POS: n
