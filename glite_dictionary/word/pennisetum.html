Pennisetum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses native to the Old World, known for their feathery flower heads, some species of which are grown as ornamental plants or for fodder.
genus of Old World grasses

Examples

Gardeners often plant Pennisetum for its attractive, feathery plumes.
Several species within the Pennisetum genus are valuable as animal fodder.
The landscaping design includes Pennisetum to add texture and movement to the garden.

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek 'penni-' (feather) and 'seta' (bristle), referring to the feathery seed heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
a genus of Old World grasses that includes several species grown as ornamental or forage plants
a genus of Old World grasses

Examples

Many ornamental grasses in garden landscapes belong to the genus Pennisetum.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of several Pennisetum species.
Pearl millet, an important crop, is classified within the Pennisetum genus.

Etymology

From Latin pennisetum, from 'penna' (feather) and 'seta' (bristle), referring to the feathery bristles on the plant.
