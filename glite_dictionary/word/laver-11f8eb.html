laver

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
an edible red seaweed, often used as food, especially in Wales and parts of Asia.
edible red seaweed

Examples

Dried laver is commonly used to make sushi rolls in Japan.
She bought fresh laver from the market to cook a traditional Welsh dish.
The restaurant serves a delicious laver bread made from seaweed.

Etymology

From Welsh 'llyfr' (seaweed), possibly via Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈleɪvə / (BrE)
A type of edible seaweed with translucent, crinkly green or purple fronds, used especially in Welsh and Asian cuisine.
edible seaweed with crinkly fronds

Examples

In Wales, laver is often used to make a traditional dish called laverbread.
She harvested laver from the rocky shoreline at low tide.
The sushi roll was wrapped in a thin sheet of laver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae

Etymology

From Latin 'laver', possibly from Celtic origins, referring to the seaweed.

3. NOUN

/ ˈleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈleɪvə / (BrE)
A large basin used by priests in an ancient Jewish temple for ritual washings or ablutions, especially as described in the Old Testament.
basin for ritual washings (Old Testament)

Examples

The priest approached the laver to wash his hands before entering the sanctuary.
According to scripture, the laver was made of bronze and stood in the Temple courtyard.
The ancient Israelites used the laver for ceremonial purification rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basin - a wide, round open container, especially one used for holding liquid

Etymology

From Latin *lavacrum* (bath, basin), from *lavare* (to wash).
