Kuangchou

1. PROPN

/ kwɑŋˈtʃoʊ / (AmE), / kwæŋˈtʃəʊ / (BrE)
A former transliteration of Guangzhou, a city on the Zhu Jiang (Pearl River) delta in southern China, capital of Guangdong province and a major deep-water port.
former spelling of Guangzhou, Chinese port city

Examples

During the early 20th century, Kuangchou was one of China's most important trading centers.
Many historical documents refer to Kuangchou before the current romanization became standard.
She visited Kuangchou to study its rapid urban development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guangzhou - modern romanization of the same Chinese city

Etymology

Former romanization of Chinese 广州 (Guǎngzhōu), now more commonly romanized as 'Guangzhou'.
