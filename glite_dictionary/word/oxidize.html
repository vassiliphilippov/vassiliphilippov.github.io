to oxidize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪz / (BrE)
to combine with oxygen or cause to combine with oxygen, resulting in the formation of an oxide
combine with oxygen; form an oxide

Examples

The iron fence began to oxidize after years of exposure to rain.
If you leave apple slices out in the air, they quickly oxidize and turn brown.
Some metals oxidize more rapidly than others, affecting their durability.

Synset Examples

This metal oxidizes easily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to reduce - to cause a chemical substance to gain electrons, the opposite of oxidize

Etymology

From French *oxyder* or Medieval Latin *oxidare*, from *oxide* + *-ize*.

2. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to combine or treat with oxygen, or to undergo a chemical reaction in which a substance combines with oxygen
combine with oxygen

Examples

Iron will oxidize over time, leading to the formation of rust.
The apple slices began to oxidize and turn brown after being left out.
Certain chemicals can oxidize pollutants in water, making them less harmful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to reduce - to remove oxygen from or add hydrogen to a substance (chemical context)

Etymology

From French oxyder, based on oxygen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00239753-v
Synset POS: v
