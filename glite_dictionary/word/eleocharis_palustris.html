Eleocharis palustris

1. NOUN

/ iˌliːəˈkɛrɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ɛlɪˌɒkərɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
a perennial sedge with cylindrical stems, commonly found in wetlands and marshes in temperate regions
a sedge found in wetlands, marsh spike-rush

Examples

The wetland was full of Eleocharis palustris, swaying gently in the breeze.
Conservationists studied Eleocharis palustris as an indicator of healthy marshland ecosystems.
We found a dense patch of Eleocharis palustris near the edge of the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh spike-rush - another common name for this species of sedge

Etymology

From Latin: Eleocharis (from Greek 'helos' meaning 'marsh' and 'charis' meaning 'grace'); palustris means 'of marshes'.
