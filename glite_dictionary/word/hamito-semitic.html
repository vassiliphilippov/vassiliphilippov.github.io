Hamito-Semitic

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæmɪtoʊ sɪˈmɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪtəʊ sɪˈmɪtɪk / (BrE)
a major family of related languages formerly classified as spoken in parts of northern Africa and southwestern Asia, including the Semitic and Berber languages
language family of Africa and Asia

Examples

Many linguists now prefer the term Afroasiatic to Hamito-Semitic family.
The Hamito-Semitic languages include Arabic, Hebrew, and Berber.
Her research focuses on the phonological features of Hamito-Semitic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afroasiatic - the modern linguistic term for the language family formerly called Hamito-Semitic

Etymology

Formed from 'Hamite' (descendants of Ham in the Bible) and 'Semitic' (descendants of Shem), reflecting an older classification of language groups.
