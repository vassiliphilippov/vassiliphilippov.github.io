mange

1. NOUN

/ meɪndʒ / (AmE), / meɪndʒ / (BrE)
a contagious skin disease of mammals caused by parasitic mites, characterized by inflammation, itching, and loss of hair
contagious skin disease in mammals
medical

Examples

The stray dog was suffering from mange and had patches of missing fur.
Farmers often treat their sheep to prevent outbreaks of mange.
If left untreated, mange can cause severe discomfort in pets.

Etymology

From Old French 'mange', from Latin 'manducare' (to chew), referring to the itching sensation.
