muskat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌskat / (BrE)
any of several cultivated grapevines that produce sweet white grapes, used especially for making wines and raisins
a type of grapevine with sweet white grapes

Examples

The vineyard is famous for its delicious muskat grapes used in dessert wines.
She added fresh muskat to the fruit salad for a burst of sweetness.
Many winemakers choose muskat for its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscat - a grape producing aromatic sweet white wines, and the wine made from it

Etymology

From German 'Muskat', itself from French 'muscat', referring to the muscatel grape.
