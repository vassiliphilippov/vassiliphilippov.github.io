trespass

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛs.pæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛs.pæs / (BrE)
The act of entering someone's land or property without permission or legal right.
unlawful entry onto property

Examples

The farmer accused the hikers of trespass after finding them on his land.
A sign was posted to warn people against trespass.
The company filed a lawsuit for trespass following repeated unauthorized entry onto its property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of wrongfully entering upon, seizing, or taking possession of the property of another

Etymology

From Old French 'trespasser' (to pass beyond), based on Latin 'trans' (across) + 'passus' (a step).

5. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛspəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs / (BrE)
A wrongful interference with the possession of property, especially real estate, or a legal action to recover damages for such interference.
wrongful interference with property
legal

Examples

The company filed a lawsuit for trespass after repeated unauthorized entry onto their land.
If you damage someone's car while committing a trespass, you could be held liable.
The legal definition of trespass includes both interference with land and with personal property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encroachment - an intrusion on another's rights or territory
  • Synonym: invasion - an unwelcome entry into someone else's domain or property
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the action of ceasing to occupy or interfere with property
