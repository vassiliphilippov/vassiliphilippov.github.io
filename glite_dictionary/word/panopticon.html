panopticon

1. NOUN

/ pænˈɑptɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / pænˈɒptɪkən / (BrE)
a circular building designed so that all parts can be observed from a central point, or any place or system where everything is visible to an observer
building or system for total observation

Examples

The prison was designed as a panopticon, allowing guards to monitor all inmates without being seen themselves.
Many modern surveillance systems are inspired by the idea of a panopticon.
Social media sometimes feels like a digital panopticon, with everyone's actions always visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance - close observation, especially of people or activities

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) and 'optikon' (seeing), coined by Jeremy Bentham in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ pænˈɑptɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / pænˈɒptɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
A circular prison building with cells arranged around a central observation point, allowing all inmates to be observed by a single guard; originally proposed by Jeremy Bentham.
circular prison with central observation tower

Examples

In the panopticon, prisoners are constantly visible from the central tower.
Many modern surveillance systems are compared to Bentham's panopticon.
The museum features a scale model of a panopticon designed in the 18th century.

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) + 'optikon' (seeing), coined by Jeremy Bentham in the late 18th century.
