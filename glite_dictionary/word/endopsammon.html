endopsammon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈsæm.ɒn / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈsæm.ɒn / (BrE)
The microscopic organisms living in the sand and mud of aquatic environments.
microscopic aquatic sand/mud organisms
medical

Examples

Scientists study endopsammon to understand the ecological health of sea floors.
The diversity of endopsammon varies between coastal and freshwater habitats.
These small organisms, known as endopsammon, play a vital role in nutrient recycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psammon - organisms that inhabit sandy environments, especially in water

Etymology

From Greek 'endo-' (within) + 'psammos' (sand), meaning organisms living within sand.
