Rudolph Laban

1. PROPN

/ ˈruːdɒlf ləˈbɑːn / (AmE), / ˈruːdɒlf ləˈbɑːn / (BrE)
(1879–1958) A Hungarian choreographer and dance theorist who developed Labanotation, a system for analyzing and recording human movement.
Hungarian choreographer and dance theorist

Examples

Rudolph Laban's work revolutionized the way dancers and choreographers think about movement.
Many universities teach Rudolph Laban's theories in their dance programs.
Researchers often reference Rudolph Laban when discussing the evolution of modern choreography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Labanotation - a system for analyzing and recording movement in dance developed by Rudolf Laban
