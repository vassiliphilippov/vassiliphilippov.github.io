St. Leo I

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈliː.oʊ ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈliː.əʊ ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 400–461) Italian pope who extended the authority of the papacy in the West and persuaded Attila the Hun not to attack Rome; also known as Leo the Great.
pope who expanded papal authority (400–461)

Examples

Historians regard St. Leo I as one of the most influential popes of the early Church.
St. Leo I is remembered for negotiating with Attila the Hun to save Rome.
The leadership of St. Leo I strengthened the position of the papacy in Western Europe.
