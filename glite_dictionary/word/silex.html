silex

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɛks / (BrE)
A pure form of finely ground silica, often used in filters, abrasives, and laboratory applications.
finely ground pure silica

Examples

The laboratory ordered more silex for their filtration apparatus.
He used silex to polish the glass surfaces until they shone.
Fine silex is often required for sensitive chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silica - the chemical compound silicon dioxide, often found in sand

Etymology

From Latin silex (hard stone, flint).
