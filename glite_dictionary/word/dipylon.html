Dipylon

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɪpɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɪpɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
the name of a gateway to the west of ancient Athens, near which a distinctive style of ancient Greek pottery has been found
gateway in ancient Athens; pottery style origin

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered a new collection of Dipylon vases near the ancient gateway.
The Dipylon served as the main entrance for processions during Athenian festivals.
Scholars have long studied the Dipylon inscription for insights into early Greek writing.

Etymology

From Greek *Dipylon* meaning 'two gates', referring to its structure.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪp.ɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɪp.ɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
of or relating to the Dipylon gateway, a large double gateway on the west side of ancient Athens.
relating to the Dipylon gateway in ancient Athens

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered several Dipylon vases near the ancient gateway.
The Dipylon cemetery reveals much about burial practices in early Athens.
She delivered a lecture on the architecture of the Dipylon gate.

Etymology

From Greek 'Δίπυλον' (Dipylon), meaning 'double gate'.
