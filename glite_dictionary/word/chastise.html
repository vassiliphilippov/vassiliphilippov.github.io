to chastise

1. VERB

/ tʃæˈstaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃæstaɪz / (BrE)
to criticize or reprimand severely; to rebuke strongly for wrongdoing or error
criticize or rebuke severely

Examples

The teacher chastised the students for talking during the exam.
His manager chastised him in front of the whole team for missing the deadline.
She regrets chastising her friend so harshly over a minor mistake.

Synset Examples

She chastised him for his insensitive remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English 'chastisen', from Old French 'chastier', from Latin 'castigare' (to correct).
