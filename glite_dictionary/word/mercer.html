mercer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsə / (BrE)
a dealer in textiles, especially silks and fine fabrics
dealer in textiles or silks

Examples

During the Renaissance, a wealthy mercer would supply the nobility with luxurious silks and brocades.
The mercer's shop was renowned for its fine selection of imported fabrics.
She apprenticed with a mercer to learn the art of textile trading.

Etymology

From Old French *mercier* (merchant), from Latin *merx* (goods, merchandise).
