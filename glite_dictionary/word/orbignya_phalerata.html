Orbignya phalerata

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈbɪɡniə fəˈlɛrətə / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪɡnɪə fəˈlɛrətə / (BrE)
A tall feather palm native to northern Brazil, whose hard-shelled nuts produce valuable oil and a vegetable ivory.
a Brazilian palm tree yielding oil and vegetable ivory

Examples

The Orbignya phalerata is cultivated for its nuts, which are pressed to extract oil.
Much of the vegetable ivory used in crafts comes from the Orbignya phalerata.
In northern Brazil, products derived from Orbignya phalerata support many local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babassu - alternative common name for Orbignya phalerata

Etymology

From the genus name 'Orbignya' (after French paleontologist Alcide d'Orbigny) and Latin 'phalerata' (adorned).
