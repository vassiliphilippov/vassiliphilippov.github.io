lieu

1. NOUN

/ lu / (AmE), / luÀê / (BrE)
the place or role taken by someone or something else; used especially in the phrase 'in lieu of' to mean 'instead of'
the position or function of another; place

Examples

Many employees receive extra vacation days in lieu of overtime pay.
She donated money to charity in lieu of giving gifts this year.
Please bring your passport in lieu of a driver's license for identification.

Synset Examples

can you go in my stead?
took his place
in lieu of

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stead - the place or function taken by someone or something else

Etymology

From Old French 'lieu' (place), from Latin 'locum' (place, position).
