physically

1. ADV

/ ˈfɪzɪkli / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəli / (BrE)
in a way that conforms to the laws of physics or material reality; as dictated by physical laws
according to the laws of physics

Examples

It is physically impossible for a person to lift such a heavy object unaided.
The bridge must be physically stable to withstand strong winds and earthquakes.
Teleportation of matter as seen in science fiction is not physically possible with current technology.

Synset Examples

it is physically impossible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done
  • Degree Lower: possible - able to occur or be done

Etymology

From physical + -ly; 'physical' from Medieval Latin physicalis, from Greek physikos (natural, relating to nature).
