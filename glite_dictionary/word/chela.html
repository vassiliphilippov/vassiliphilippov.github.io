chela

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪlə / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪlə / (BrE)
A disciple or pupil, especially of a Hindu religious teacher (swami).
Hindu disciple or pupil

Examples

The swami was accompanied by his devoted chela throughout his pilgrimage.
As a chela, she spent years studying the scriptures under her guru's guidance.
Each chela must follow a strict discipline to attain spiritual growth.

Etymology

From Hindi 'chelā' (disciple, follower), from Sanskrit 'ceṭa' (servant, attendant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːlə / (AmE), / ˈkiːlə / (BrE)
a pincer-like grasping structure found at the end of a limb in certain crustaceans and arthropods, such as crabs and lobsters
pincer-like structure on an arthropod's limb

Examples

The crab used its chela to grasp and break open the shell.
A lobster’s large chela is used both for defense and for hunting prey.
Some scorpions have developed specialized chelae for capturing their food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claw - a sharp, curved nail on the foot of an animal; also refers to similar structures

Etymology

From Greek khēlē (claw, pincer).
