Indigofera anil

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈɡoʊfərə əˈnɪl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈɡəʊfərə əˈnɪl / (BrE)
A shrub native to the West Indies and South America, cultivated as a source of indigo dye.
shrub, source of indigo dye

Examples

The leaves of Indigofera anil are harvested to produce natural indigo dye.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Indigofera anil in its native environment.
Traditional textile makers value Indigofera anil for its rich color extract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - deep blue dye obtained from plants of the genus Indigofera

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Indigofera' meaning 'bearing indigo' and Spanish 'añil' meaning 'indigo'.
