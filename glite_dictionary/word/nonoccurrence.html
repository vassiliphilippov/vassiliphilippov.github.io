nonoccurrence

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːn.əˈkɝː.rəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈkʌr.əns / (BrE)
the absence or failure of something to happen or take place
absence of happening

Examples

The nonoccurrence of rain this month has affected the farmers' crops.
Scientists were puzzled by the nonoccurrence of expected side effects during the trial.
Her disappointment stemmed from the nonoccurrence of the long-awaited event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occurrence - an event or the fact of something happening

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'occurrence', modeled on Latin-forming patterns.
