Panini

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑːniˌni / (AmE), / ˈpɑːniːni / (BrE)
an ancient Indian grammarian whose rules for Sanskrit are regarded as the earliest known work on descriptive linguistics (circa 400 BC)
ancient Indian grammarian (c. 400 BC)

Examples

Many scholars consider Panini the father of linguistics for his work on Sanskrit grammar.
In his research, he referenced Panini's influential text, the Ashtadhyayi.
The precision of Panini's rules has fascinated linguists for centuries.

Etymology

From Sanskrit पाणिनि (Pāṇini), the grammarian's own name.
