to schematise

1. VERB

/ ˈskiːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskiːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or arrange something in a systematic or conventional form, often using a simplified or structured outline
to arrange in a systematic form
british

Examples

The artist chose to schematise the landscape using only bold geometric shapes.
In his lectures, the professor tended to schematise complex theories for easier understanding.
Many ancient cultures would schematise animals and plants in their decorative art.

Synset Examples

some art forms schematise designs into geometrical patterns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to schematize - the American English spelling of 'schematise'
  • Synonym: to systematize - to arrange or organize according to a system
  • Synonym: to formalize - to give something a definite or official form

Etymology

From Greek *schēmatizein* (to form, to shape), via Medieval Latin *schematizare*.
