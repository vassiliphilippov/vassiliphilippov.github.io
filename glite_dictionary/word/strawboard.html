strawboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A coarse, yellowish cardboard made from straw pulp, used in packaging and bookbinding.
cardboard made from straw pulp

Examples

The boxes were reinforced with strawboard for extra durability.
He used a sheet of strawboard to make the book cover.
Many packaging materials once relied on strawboard before plastics became common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardboard - a stiff, thick paper-based material used for boxes and packaging

Etymology

Formed from 'straw' (the dried stalks of grain plants) and 'board'.
