Proteles

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːliːz / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of African mammals in the family Hyaenidae, consisting of the aardwolf, known for feeding primarily on termites.
genus of African mammals (aardwolf)

Examples

The Proteles is unique among the Hyaenidae in its insectivorous diet.
Researchers studied the behavior of Proteles in its native savannah habitat.
Unlike other hyenas, Proteles primarily eats termites rather than larger prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardwolf - a nocturnal African mammal (Proteles cristatus) of the hyena family, feeding on termites

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'pro-' (before) + 'telos' (end, completion).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːliːz / (AmE), / prəˈtiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of insectivorous hyena-like mammals, of which the aardwolf is the only living species.
genus of mammals; includes aardwolf

Examples

The Proteles genus is represented today only by the aardwolf.
Scientists studied fossils from the extinct relatives of Proteles to understand its evolutionary history.
Unlike other hyenas, members of Proteles feed mainly on termites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardwolf - a nocturnal mammal (Proteles cristata) native to Africa

Etymology

From Greek 'protos' (first) + 'telos' (end, purpose), used in taxonomy.
