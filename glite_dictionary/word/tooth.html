tooth

1. NOUN

/ tuθ / (AmE), / tuːθ / (BrE)
one of the regular projections on a gear or similar part, designed to fit into the corresponding part of another mechanism
projection on a gear or similar part

Examples

The tooth of the gear must be properly shaped to ensure smooth motion.
Over time, the teeth on the sprocket can become worn and affect performance.
A broken tooth in a clockwork mechanism can stop the entire system from working.

2. NOUN

/ tuθ / (AmE), / tuːθ / (BrE)
A hard, pointed structure found in the mouth or alimentary canal of some invertebrates, or on the shell of certain animals, resembling a tooth in vertebrates.
toothlike structure in invertebrates

Examples

The snail's radula is covered with rows of tiny teeth that help it scrape food.
Some mollusks have a shell edge lined with sharp teeth for protection.
Researchers studied the arrangement of teeth in the mouthparts of marine worms.

Etymology

From Old English *tōþ*, from Proto-Germanic *tanþs*, related to Dutch *tand* and German *Zahn*.

3. NOUN

/ tuːθ / (AmE), / tuːθ / (BrE)
Each of the hard, white structures in the mouth of people and animals, used for biting and chewing food.
hard structure in the jaw for biting and chewing

Examples

The dentist examined each tooth for signs of decay.
A shark can lose a tooth and grow a new one in its place.
She lost her first baby tooth when she was six years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teeth - plural form of tooth

Etymology
