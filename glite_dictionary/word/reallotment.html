reallotment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.əˈlɑt.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈlɒt.mənt / (BrE)
the act of redistributing or reassigning something, especially congressional seats in the United States based on census results
redistribution, especially of legislative seats

Examples

The reallotment of congressional seats followed the release of new census data.
There was a heated debate over the fairness of the reallotment process.
Population shifts often necessitate the reallotment of political representation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allotment - the action of allotting or distributing

Etymology

From re- (again) + allotment.
