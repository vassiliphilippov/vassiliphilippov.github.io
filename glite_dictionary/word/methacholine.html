methacholine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθ.əˈkoʊ.lin / (AmE), / ˌmɛθ.əˈkəʊ.liːn / (BrE)
a parasympathomimetic drug used to stimulate secretions and smooth muscle activity, often in diagnostic testing
a drug that stimulates secretions and smooth muscle
medical

Examples

The doctor administered methacholine to assess the patient's airway responsiveness.
Inhalation of methacholine can help diagnose asthma in individuals with breathing difficulties.
Methacholine challenge tests are commonly used in pulmonary function laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetylcholine - a neurotransmitter that methacholine is structurally related to

Etymology

From 'methyl' and 'acetylcholine', indicating its chemical structure as a methyl derivative of acetylcholine.
