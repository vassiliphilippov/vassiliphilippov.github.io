magpie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.paɪ / (BrE)
a large, long-tailed bird of the crow family with black-and-white plumage and a chattering call, known for its attraction to shiny objects
large, black-and-white bird with a chattering call

Examples

A magpie perched on the fence, chattering loudly at the cat below.
Several magpies swooped down to collect shiny coins from the grass.
The distinctive black-and-white feathers make it easy to spot a magpie in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - a large, black bird of the corvid family

Etymology

From Mag (a nickname for Margaret, used as a generic name for women) + pie (Old French 'pie' meaning magpie).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.pʌɪ / (BrE)
a person who talks excessively and foolishly, often in an annoying or trivial way
person who talks a lot foolishly

Examples

He’s a real magpie—you can never get a word in edgewise when he’s around.
The meeting was delayed by a magpie who wouldn’t stop telling irrelevant stories.
She tried to focus, but the magpie in the next cubicle kept chattering away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters
  • Synonym: blabbermouth - a person who talks excessively or indiscreetly
  • Antonym: taciturn - a person who is reserved or says very little

Etymology

From the name of the magpie bird, known for its noisy chatter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09931028-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.paɪ / (BrE)
a person who habitually collects things, especially things that have been discarded by others
person who collects discarded items

Examples

My aunt is a true magpie, always picking up old furniture from the curb.
He has a magpie's eye for finding treasures in other people's trash.
As a child, Sam was a little magpie, collecting marbles, bottle caps, and any shiny object he found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collector - a person who collects things as a hobby or occupation

Etymology

From the bird name 'magpie', known for collecting small objects.

Source: WordNet
