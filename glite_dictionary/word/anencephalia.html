anencephalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ɛn.sɪˈfeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌan.ɛn.sɪˈfeɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
A congenital defect in which major parts of the brain, especially the cerebral hemispheres, are absent or underdeveloped.
congenital absence of brain hemispheres
medical

Examples

The prenatal scan revealed that the fetus was affected by anencephalia, a serious neural tube defect.
Doctors explained that anencephalia typically results in the absence of the cerebral hemispheres at birth.
Research into the causes of anencephalia has led to important insights in prenatal health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anencephaly - condition of being born without a major portion of the brain

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + enkephalos (brain).
