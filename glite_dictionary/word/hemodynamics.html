hemodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physiology concerned with the movement of blood and the forces involved in its circulation
study of blood flow and forces
medical

Examples

Recent studies in hemodynamics have improved our understanding of heart disease.
The doctor specializes in hemodynamics, focusing on how blood pressure affects organs.
Measuring hemodynamics is essential during major surgery to monitor patient stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circulation - movement of blood through the body

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and English 'dynamics'.
