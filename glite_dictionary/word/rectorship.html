rectorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, position, or term of a rector, especially in a church or university
the office of a rector

Examples

His rectorship at the university was marked by significant expansion of the campus.
During her rectorship, several major reforms were introduced to the curriculum.
The rectorship of the parish changed hands after the retirement of Father James.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rector - a person in charge of a church or educational institution

Etymology

From rector + -ship, an Old English suffix denoting a state or office.
