Acoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌakəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A small family of flowering plants, sometimes used to classify the genus Acorus, which is usually placed in the Araceae family.
plant family including Acorus

Examples

The genus Acorus is sometimes placed in the Acoraceae family by some botanists.
Recent molecular studies support recognizing Acoraceae as a separate family from Araceae.
There is ongoing debate among taxonomists regarding the classification of Acoraceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araceae - the arum family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Modern Latin, formed from Acorus (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard botanical suffix for plant family names).

2. NOUN

/ əˌkɔːrəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / əˌkɔːrəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a plant subfamily that includes the genus Acorus, sometimes classified in its own family or as part of Araceae
plant subfamily for genus Acorus

Examples

The Acoraceae are sometimes treated as a separate family from the Araceae in botanical texts.
Recent genetic studies have clarified the relationship of Acoraceae to other monocot families.
Botanists debate whether Acoraceae should remain distinct or be merged with Araceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Araceae - the plant family often including Acorus

Etymology

From the genus name Acorus plus the Latin suffix -aceae, denoting plant families.
