Asplenium scolopendrium

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm ˌskoʊləˈpɛndrɪəm / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm ˌskɒləˈpɛndrɪəm / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian fern with simple, lance-shaped fronds, commonly known as hart's-tongue fern.
a Eurasian fern; hart's-tongue fern

Examples

The shady woodland was covered in Asplenium scolopendrium, giving the ground a lush green appearance.
Botanists noted the presence of Asplenium scolopendrium along the riverbank during their survey.
Gardeners value Asplenium scolopendrium for its unique, undivided fronds and ability to thrive in moist, shaded areas.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Asplenium* and species name *scolopendrium*, from Greek *skolopendra* (centipede), referring to the frond shape.
