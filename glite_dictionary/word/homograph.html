homograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːməˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɒməˌɡræf / (BrE)
A word that is spelled the same as another word but differs in meaning, and sometimes in pronunciation.
word spelled same as another with different meaning

Examples

The word 'lead' is a homograph because it can refer to a metal or to the act of guiding.
Many English homographs have different pronunciations, like 'tear' the noun and 'tear' the verb.
Understanding homographs can help learners avoid confusion when reading English texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homonym - a word spelled and pronounced like another but different in meaning
  • Antonym: homophone - a word pronounced the same as another but differing in meaning, and often in spelling

Etymology

From Greek homo- (same) + -graphos (written).
