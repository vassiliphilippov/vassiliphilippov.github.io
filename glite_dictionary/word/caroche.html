caroche

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊʃ / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊʃ / (BrE)
a large, luxurious carriage used by European nobility in the 16th and 17th centuries
luxurious 16th–17th century carriage

Examples

The royal procession included a gilded caroche drawn by six white horses.
She arrived at the ball in a splendid caroche befitting her status.
Historians marveled at the elegance of the 17th-century caroche displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a vehicle for people, usually horse-drawn, used before cars were invented

Etymology

From French 'carrosse', meaning 'carriage'.
