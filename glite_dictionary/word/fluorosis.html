fluorosis

1. NOUN

/ flʊˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / flʊəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by excessive intake of fluorine, usually resulting in changes to teeth or bones.
condition from excess fluorine intake
medical

Examples

Dental fluorosis can cause white spots or streaks on children's teeth.
Chronic fluorosis may lead to joint pain and bone deformities in severe cases.
In some regions, high levels of fluoride in groundwater have resulted in widespread fluorosis.

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow, stream) + '-osis' (condition, process).
