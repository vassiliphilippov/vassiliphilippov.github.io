Monsignor

1. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsiːnjər / (AmE), / mɒnˈsiːnjɔː / (BrE)
A title of honor in the Roman Catholic Church given to some priests.
Catholic title for certain priests

Examples

The bishop addressed Monsignor Rossi during the ceremony.
Many parishioners attended the farewell dinner for Monsignor.
The new church was blessed by Monsignor O'Leary last Sunday.

Etymology

From Italian 'monsignore', literally 'my lord', used as a title for certain priests.
