cooper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːpər / (AmE), / ˈkuːpə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make or repair wooden barrels, casks, or tubs
barrel or tub maker

Examples

The old village still has a cooper who crafts barrels by hand.
He apprenticed as a cooper before opening his own workshop.
A skilled cooper can make a watertight cask without using glue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel maker - a person who makes barrels

Etymology

From Middle English *couper*, from Middle Dutch *cūper* (maker of casks), related to *cūpe* (tub, vat).

2. VERB

/ ˈkuː.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈkuː.pə / (BrE)
to make or repair barrels and casks as a cooper does
make or repair barrels and casks

Examples

He learned to cooper barrels in his grandfather's workshop.
The villagers hired someone to cooper new casks for the wine harvest.
Before machinery, people had to cooper each container by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - a cylindrical container, typically made of wooden staves and metal hoops
  • Synonym: cask - a large container, similar to a barrel, used for storing liquids

Etymology

From Middle English couper, from Middle Dutch kuper (barrel maker), related to 'coop' meaning barrel.
