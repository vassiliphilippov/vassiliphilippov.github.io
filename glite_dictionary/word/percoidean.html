percoidean

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈkɔɪdiən / (AmE), / pɜːˈkɔɪdiən / (BrE)
any of various spiny-finned fishes of the order Perciformes, which includes many familiar freshwater and marine species.
spiny-finned fish of order Perciformes
medical

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep percoidean species due to their vibrant colors and active behavior.
Researchers discovered a new percoidean fish in the remote lake.
The diet of most percoideans includes smaller fish and aquatic insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perciform - relating to or resembling fishes of the order Perciformes

Etymology

From New Latin Percoidea (type suborder of Perciformes), from 'perca' (perch) + '-oid' (resembling) + '-ean'.
