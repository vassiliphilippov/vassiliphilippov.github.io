predilection

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛdəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
A strong liking or preference for something.
a strong liking or preference

Examples

She has always had a predilection for classical music over modern pop.
Despite his predilection for spicy food, he enjoyed the mild dish.
The professor's predilection for lengthy lectures was well known among the students.

Synset Examples

my own preference is for good literature
the Irish have a penchant for blarney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over others
  • Synonym: penchant - a strong or habitual liking for something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin 'praediligere', meaning to prefer, by way of French 'prédilection'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛdɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a strong liking for something; a preference or special fondness
preference; strong liking

Examples

She has a predilection for classical music, especially Mozart.
His predilection for spicy food is well known among his friends.
Despite his predilection for solitude, he attended the crowded party.

Synset Examples

a predilection for expensive cars
his sexual preferences
showed a Marxist orientation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + diligere (to choose).
