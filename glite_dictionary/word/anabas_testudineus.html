Anabas testudineus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ə.bæs tɛsˌtjuː.dɪˈniː.əs / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.bæs tɛsˌtjuː.dɪˈniː.əs / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of India and Southeast Asia, able to breathe air and move over land using its spiny pectoral fins
an air-breathing freshwater fish species

Examples

The Anabas testudineus can survive out of water for several hours due to its specialized gills.
Farmers often find Anabas testudineus crossing damp fields after heavy rains.
In many rural markets, Anabas testudineus is sold as a source of fresh protein.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus 'Anabas' and species name 'testudineus'.
