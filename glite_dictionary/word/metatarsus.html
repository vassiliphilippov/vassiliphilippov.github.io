metatarsus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈtɑrsəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈtɑːsəs / (BrE)
the group of bones in the human foot located between the toes (phalanges) and the ankle bones (tarsus); also, the equivalent part in the feet of birds and the hind feet of quadrupeds.
middle part of the foot's skeleton
medical

Examples

An X-ray revealed a fracture in his left metatarsus after the accident.
The anatomy lecture focused on the structure and function of the metatarsus in mammals.
In birds, the elongated metatarsus supports strong running or perching abilities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tarsus - the group of small bones in the ankle area, connecting the metatarsus to the leg bones
  • Degree Higher: phalanges - toe bones, next in the sequence after the metatarsus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'meta-' (after, beyond) + 'tarsos' (ankle, flat of the foot).
