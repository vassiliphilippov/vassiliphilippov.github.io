literature

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
the academic study of written works, especially as an area of scholarship or discipline
study of written works; academic discipline

Examples

He decided to major in literature at university, focusing on 19th-century novels.
The department of literature offers courses on modern and classical works.
Her passion for literature led her to become an English professor.

Synset Examples

he took a course in Russian lit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literary studies - academic study of literature

Etymology

From Latin 'litteratura', meaning writing formed with letters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ərətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
all the published writings on a particular subject or in a specific area, especially technical or academic works
published writings on a subject

Examples

She reviewed all the recent literature on climate change before writing her report.
The safety literature for this medication lists several possible side effects.
New studies have added important insights to the medical literature on the disease.

Synset Examples

the technical literature
one aspect of Waterloo has not yet been treated in the literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publishing - the occupation, business, or activity of preparing and issuing books, journals, and other material for sale

Etymology

From Latin 'litteratura' (writing, grammar), from 'littera' (letter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06376547-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərətʃə / (BrE)
written works, especially those considered to have artistic or intellectual value, such as novels, poems, and plays
creative writing of artistic value

Examples

He is an expert in European literature of the nineteenth century.
Many students find American literature both challenging and fascinating.
Poetry is just one form of literature that explores deep human emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiction - prose writing about imaginary events and people
  • Synonym: poetry - literary work in verse form, expressing ideas and feelings imaginatively
  • Antonym: nonfiction - prose writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people

Etymology

From Latin litteratura (writing, grammar), from littera (letter, writing).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

the profession or art of writing

Many students choose to pursue a career in literature after university.

Related Terms
