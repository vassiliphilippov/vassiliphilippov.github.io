pederasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌræsti / (AmE), / ˈpɛdərɑːsti / (BrE)
sexual activity involving an adult man and a boy; especially an older man with a pubescent or adolescent boy, historically in some cultures a socially acknowledged relationship.
sexual relationship between an adult man and a boy

Examples

Historical accounts often describe pederasty as a common practice in ancient Greece.
Laws in many countries now strictly prohibit pederasty and similar acts.
The term pederasty frequently appears in discussions about ethics and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedophilia - sexual attraction to prepubescent children

Etymology

From Greek paiderastia, from pais (boy) and erastēs (lover).
