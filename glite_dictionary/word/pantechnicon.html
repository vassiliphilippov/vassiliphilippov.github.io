pantechnicon

1. NOUN

/ pænˈtɛknɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / panˈtɛknɪkən / (BrE)
a large van or wagon used for transporting furniture, especially during house moves
large furniture-moving van
british

Examples

The old pantechnicon was parked outside the house, ready to be loaded with boxes and furniture.
During the 19th century, a pantechnicon was a common sight on London streets during house removals.
They hired a pantechnicon to move their belongings to their new flat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moving van - a vehicle for transporting household belongings during a move

Etymology

From Greek pan- (all) + tekhnikon (of art or skill); originally the name of a building in London used as a furniture warehouse, then applied to furniture vans.
