Scomberomorus maculatus

1. NOUN

/ skɒm.bəˈroʊ.mə.rəs ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / skɒm.bəˈrɒ.mə.rəs ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A large, commercially important species of mackerel found in the Atlantic coastal waters of North America, also known as the Spanish mackerel.
Atlantic Spanish mackerel species

Examples

Commercial fisheries along the eastern coast rely heavily on the Scomberomorus maculatus for their catch.
The Scomberomorus maculatus is distinguished by its spotted sides and forked tail.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the Scomberomorus maculatus to help manage its population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish mackerel - Common name for Scomberomorus maculatus

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Scomberomorus' and species name 'maculatus' meaning 'spotted'.
