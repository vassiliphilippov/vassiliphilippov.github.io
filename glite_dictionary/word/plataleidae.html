Plataleidae

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtæliˌidaɪ / (AmE), / pləˈtæliːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large wading birds known as spoonbills, characterized by their distinctive long, flat, spoon-shaped bills.
family of birds (spoonbills)

Examples

All members of the Plataleidae family share a unique spatula-shaped bill used for feeding in shallow waters.
Biologists studied nesting patterns among different species within the Plataleidae.
The Plataleidae can be found in both temperate and tropical regions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonbill - wading bird with a long, flat, spoon-shaped bill

Etymology

From modern Latin Plataleidae, from Platalea (type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
