Mimosa pudica

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈmoʊsə pjuːˈdɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə pjuːˈdɪkə / (BrE)
A tropical plant with sensitive leaves that fold and droop when touched or at night, native to the Americas and Australia, often known as the 'sensitive plant.'
tropical plant with touch-sensitive leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Mimosa pudica close rapidly when touched, fascinating botanists and children alike.
In her indoor garden, she keeps a Mimosa pudica because she enjoys watching its sensitive movements.
Farmers in some regions consider Mimosa pudica a troublesome weed due to its fast spread and sharp thorns.

Etymology

From Latin: *mimosa* (from Greek *mimos*, 'mimic') and *pudica* ('shy, bashful').
