verbalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrbəˌlɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈvəːbəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the act or process of expressing ideas, beliefs, or opinions in words, especially spoken or written words
expression or communication in words

Examples

The author's verbalism allowed complex ideas to be conveyed clearly and effectively.
His tendency toward excessive verbalism sometimes made his lectures hard to follow.
Relying solely on verbalism is not enough; actions must support the promises made.

Synset Examples

expressions of good will
he helped me find verbal expression for my ideas
the idea was immediate but the verbalism took hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The use of an excessive amount of words without adding substance or meaning; overabundance of words in speech or writing.
excessive use of words; verbosity

Examples

The politician's speech was criticized for its verbalism and lack of clear proposals.
Academic writing should avoid unnecessary verbalism to communicate ideas effectively.
Her letter was filled with verbalism, making it difficult to understand her main point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
  • Antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word) + '-ism'.
