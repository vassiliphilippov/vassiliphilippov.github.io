synchrony

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkrəni / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrəni / (BrE)
the state or quality of events occurring at the same time or in precise coordination
simultaneous occurrence

Examples

The flashing lights pulsed in perfect synchrony, creating a mesmerizing pattern.
Researchers observed synchrony among the dancers' movements during the performance.
High synchrony in brain activity is often a sign of effective neural communication.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asynchrony - the state of not occurring at the same time
  • Synonym: simultaneity - the quality of two or more things happening at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (happening at the same time), from 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
