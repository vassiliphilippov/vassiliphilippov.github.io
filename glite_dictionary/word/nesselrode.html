Nesselrode

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛsəlˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈnɛslˌrəʊd / (BrE)
A rich, frozen dessert made with chopped chestnuts, maraschino cherries, candied fruits, and liqueur or rum, traditionally served as a pudding or ice cream.
rich dessert with chestnuts and fruit

Examples

The chef prepared a classic Nesselrode for dessert, featuring chestnuts and maraschino cherries.
She tasted the Nesselrode and was delighted by its creamy texture and rich flavors.
At the holiday party, guests enjoyed a traditional Nesselrode served with whipped cream.

Etymology

Named after Count Karl Nesselrode (1780–1862), a Russian diplomat, after whom the dish was created.
