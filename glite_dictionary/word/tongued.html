tongued

1. ADJ

/ tʌŋd / (AmE), / tʌŋd / (BrE)
(used in combination) having a specified manner of speaking, often as part of a compound adjective.
used in compounds – manner of speaking

Examples

He is often described as silver-tongued because of his persuasive speeches.
Her sharp-tongued remarks offended many people at the meeting.
The diplomat, known for his smooth-tongued approach, was able to negotiate a difficult peace deal.

Synset Examples

golden-tongued
sharp-tongued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-tongued - speaking in a harsh or critical manner
  • Synonym: golden-tongued - speaking in an eloquent or persuasive manner

2. ADJ

/ tʌŋd / (AmE), / tʌŋd / (BrE)
having a tongue or a tongue-like part; equipped with or resembling a tongue, often used in compounds.
having or resembling a tongue

Examples

The snake had a distinctly tongued appearance with its forked tip.
Several species of flowers are pollinated by long-tongued bees.
They installed tongued boards to create a strong, interlocking floor.

Synset Examples

tongued shoes
tongued boards
tongued lightning
long-tongued

Etymology

From 'tongue' + '-ed', an adjectival suffix forming from nouns in English.
