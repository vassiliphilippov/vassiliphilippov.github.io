to despond

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɑnd / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to lose confidence or hope; become dejected or discouraged
become dejected or lose hope

Examples

Many investors began to despond after the stock market crash.
Do not despond if the results are not as expected; persistence pays off.
She refused to despond, even in the face of repeated setbacks.

Synset Examples

The supporters of the Presidential candidate desponded when they learned the early results of the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - lose all hope or confidence
  • Antonym: hope - feel expectation or desire for a certain thing to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'despondere' (to give up, lose hope), via Old French 'despondre'.
