Thomas Edward Lawrence

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ˈɛdwərd ˈlɔrəns / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ˈɛdwəd ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
(1888–1935) A Welsh soldier, scholar, and writer known for organizing the Arab revolt against Ottoman rule during World War I and for his memoir, 'Seven Pillars of Wisdom'.
Welsh soldier and author, led Arab revolt (WWI)

Examples

Thomas Edward Lawrence played a key role in the Middle Eastern theater during the First World War.
The exploits of Thomas Edward Lawrence inspired the film 'Lawrence of Arabia.'
Many historians continue to study the strategies developed by Thomas Edward Lawrence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lawrence of Arabia - The popular name for Thomas Edward Lawrence, famous for his role in the Arab revolt
