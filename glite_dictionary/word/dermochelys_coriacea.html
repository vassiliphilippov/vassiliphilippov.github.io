Dermochelys coriacea

1. NOUN

/ dɜːr.moʊˈkiːlɪs kɔːr.iˈeɪ.ʃə / (AmE), / dɜː.məʊˈkiːlɪs kɒr.iˈeɪ.siə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the leatherback sea turtle, a large marine turtle with a flexible, leathery shell and the largest living turtle species
leatherback sea turtle (scientific name)

Examples

The Dermochelys coriacea migrates thousands of kilometers across the world's oceans each year.
Marine biologists are working to protect the endangered Dermochelys coriacea populations.
A fully grown Dermochelys coriacea can weigh up to 700 kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherback turtle - the common name for Dermochelys coriacea, the world's largest turtle

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'chelys' (turtle); 'coriacea' from Latin 'coriaceus' (leathery).
