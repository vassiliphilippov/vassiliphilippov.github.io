pomatum

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / pəʊˈmeɪtəm / (BrE)
A scented oily substance or ointment used to style and dress the hair.
perfumed hair oil or ointment
archaic

Examples

In the eighteenth century, gentlemen would use pomatum to give their wigs a glossy sheen.
She found a jar of lavender pomatum among her grandmother’s old toiletries.
Before the dance, he carefully applied pomatum to tame his unruly curls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomade - a scented ointment or wax for hairdressing

Etymology

From Latin 'pomatum', from 'pomum' (apple, fruit) due to original use of apple scent.
