allegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæl.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a formal statement by someone in authority that someone has done something wrong or illegal, often without proof, especially in a legal context
formal accusation, especially in law
legal

Examples

The company issued a statement denying the allegation of financial misconduct.
The politician faced several allegations of corruption during the election campaign.
The court will consider the allegation when reviewing the evidence.

Synset Examples

an allegation of malpractice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'allegatio' (bringing forward as evidence), from Latin 'allegare' (to assert).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæl.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statement or claim that someone has done something wrong or illegal, made without proof at the time it is stated
an unproven claim or accusation

Examples

The company denied the allegation that it had been involved in fraud.
She made a serious allegation against her former employer in court.
Until there is evidence to support the allegation, we should not jump to conclusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a statement that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • Antonym: denial - a statement asserting that something is not true

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'allegationem', from Latin 'allegare' (to allege).
