Heshvan

1. NOUN

/ hɛʃˈvɑn / (AmE), / hɛʃˈvɑn / (BrE)
the second month of the civil year and the eighth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in October and November
Jewish calendar month (October–November)

Examples

The Jewish holiday in Heshvan does not have any special religious observances.
This year, Heshvan begins in late October according to the Gregorian calendar.
Many bar mitzvah ceremonies are scheduled in Heshvan due to the absence of major festivals.

Etymology

From Hebrew חֶשְׁוָן (Ḥeshván).
