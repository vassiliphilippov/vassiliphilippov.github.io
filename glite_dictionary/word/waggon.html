waggon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A car with a long body and a rear door, providing space behind the rear seat for cargo or passengers; also known as a station wagon.
long car with rear cargo space

Examples

They packed their bicycles into the back of the waggon for the weekend trip.
The family's old waggon easily fit all the groceries and luggage behind the seats.
She prefers driving a waggon because of its extra cargo space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station wagon - automobile with an extended rear cargo area and rear door
  • Antonym: sedan - car with a separate compartment for luggage; no rear cargo space

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'wagon', from Middle English 'wagon', from Dutch 'wagen'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
a heavy vehicle with four wheels, usually pulled by animals or a tractor, used for transporting goods
vehicle for carrying goods, drawn by animals or tractor
british

Examples

The farmers loaded the hay onto the waggon and drove to the market.
A horse-drawn waggon is still used in some rural communities.
The museum displayed an old wooden waggon from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagon - four-wheeled vehicle for transporting goods (US spelling)

Etymology

From early English 'waggon', borrowing from Dutch 'wagen' or German 'Wagen' (cart, vehicle).
