foreskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskɪn / (BrE)
a fold of skin covering the tip of the penis or clitoris
fold of skin covering penis or clitoris

Examples

During the medical examination, the doctor noted that the foreskin was completely normal.
Some mammals, including humans, have a foreskin that protects the sensitive tip of the organ.
The foreskin can sometimes become irritated if not kept clean.

Etymology

From fore- (front) + skin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskɪn / (BrE)
the fold of skin that covers the tip of the penis, often removed during circumcision
fold of skin covering tip of the penis

Examples

The doctor explained the function of the foreskin to the patient.
Some cultures practice circumcision, which involves removing the foreskin.
Infections can sometimes occur if the foreskin is not cleaned properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepuce - another term for foreskin

Etymology

From fore- (before) + skin, referring to its position at the front of the penis.
