to readmit

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈæd.mɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈæd.mɪt / (BrE)
to allow someone to enter or join something again after being removed or absent
to admit again

Examples

After her surgery, the hospital readmitted her due to complications.
The student was readmitted to the university after meeting all the requirements.
When his visa was approved, he was finally readmitted to the country.

Synset Examples

The refugee was readmitted into his home country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to allow entry or access
  • Antonym: to expel - to force someone to leave

Etymology

From re- (again) + admit, following the pattern of Latin re-admittere.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːədˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːədˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to enter or join a place or group again after being absent or excluded
allow to enter again

Examples

After paying the fine, the player was readmitted to the tournament.
The patient was readmitted to the hospital just two days after being discharged.
Students who leave for personal reasons can apply to be readmitted to the program.

Synset Examples

After paying a penalty, the player was readmitted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to allow to enter; to let in
  • Antonym: to exclude - to prevent someone from entering; to keep out

Etymology

From re- (again) + admit, from Latin admittere (to allow to enter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02395928-v
Synset POS: v
