heliopause

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːli.oʊˌpɔz / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪəˌpɔːz / (BrE)
the boundary marking the edge of the sun's influence, where the solar wind is stopped by the interstellar medium
boundary at edge of sun's influence

Examples

The Voyager 1 spacecraft crossed the heliopause and entered interstellar space in 2012.
Scientists study the heliopause to understand how the solar wind interacts with forces from beyond our solar system.
The location of the heliopause can change due to fluctuations in solar activity.

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + English pause (to stop)
