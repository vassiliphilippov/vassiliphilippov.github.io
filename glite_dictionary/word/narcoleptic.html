narcoleptic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑr.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɑː.kəˈlep.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by narcolepsy, a disorder characterized by excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden sleep attacks.
relating to narcolepsy
medical

Examples

People with a narcoleptic condition often struggle to stay awake during the day.
The patient's narcoleptic symptoms began in adolescence.
Some medications can help manage narcoleptic episodes and improve quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcolepsy - A disorder characterized by uncontrollable episodes of deep sleep.

Etymology

From French 'narcoleptique', from Greek 'narkē' (numbness, stupor) + '-leptic' (seizing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑr.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɑː.kəˈlep.tɪk / (BrE)
a person who has narcolepsy, a condition marked by frequent and uncontrollable episodes of falling asleep during the day
person with narcolepsy
medical

Examples

The support group welcomed a new narcoleptic who wanted to learn strategies for managing the disorder.
As a narcoleptic, he sometimes struggles to stay awake during meetings at work.
The doctor has seen many narcoleptic patients over the years, each with unique sleep challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcolepsy - chronic sleep disorder causing uncontrollable sleep attacks

Etymology

From narcolepsy, itself from French 'narcolepsie', based on Greek 'narkē' (numbness) and 'lepsis' (seizure).

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːr.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɑː.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (BrE)
A drug that induces an uncontrollable desire to sleep; a soporific agent.
sleep-inducing drug
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a narcoleptic to help the patient finally get some rest.
Certain medications may act as a narcoleptic if taken in large doses.
She was warned that the effects of the narcoleptic would last for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soporific - a drug or substance that induces sleep

Etymology

From Greek 'narkē' (numbness, stupor) and 'lepsis' (seizure).
