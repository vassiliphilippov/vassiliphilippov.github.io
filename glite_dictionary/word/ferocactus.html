Ferocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛr.oʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌfɛr.əʊˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A genus of nearly spherical barrel cacti native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.
genus of barrel cacti

Examples

The Ferocactus is known for its large, robust spines and barrel-shaped body.
Several species of Ferocactus are cultivated as ornamental plants in arid gardens.
During our trip to Arizona, we saw many impressive Ferocactus along the desert trails.

Etymology

From Latin, combining ferus (fierce) and cactus, referring to the plant's spiny appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛrəʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊˈkaktəs / (BrE)
A genus of nearly globular, spiny cacti native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, known as barrel cacti.
genus of barrel cacti

Examples

The Ferocactus can grow up to a meter in diameter in the wild.
Many desert gardeners value the Ferocactus for its unique shape and colorful flowers.
Researchers studied the water retention abilities of Ferocactus during drought conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel cactus - a common name for cacti of the genus Ferocactus

Etymology

From Latin 'ferus' (fierce) and 'cactus', referring to the plant's long spines.
