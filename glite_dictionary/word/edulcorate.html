to edulcorate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈdʌl.kəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈdʌl.kəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to make (something) sweeter in taste; to sweeten
make sweeter in taste

Examples

The pharmacist will edulcorate the medicine to make it more palatable for children.
They attempted to edulcorate the bitter solution by adding a small amount of sugar.
The chef used honey to edulcorate the tartness of the sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweeten - make or become sweet or sweeter

Etymology

From Latin 'edulcorare', from 'e-' (out) + 'dulcor' (sweetness).
