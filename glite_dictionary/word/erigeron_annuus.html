Erigeron annuus

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪdʒərɒn ˈænjuː.əs / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdʒərɒn ˈænjuː.əs / (BrE)
A species of North American herb with small white daisylike flowers, commonly found and widely naturalized.
white-flowered North American herb

Examples

Erigeron annuus is commonly seen in meadows and along roadsides throughout North America.
Botanists often study Erigeron annuus for its adaptability in various environments.
The delicate white petals of Erigeron annuus make it a charming addition to wildflower gardens.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Erigeron' (from Greek ἠριγέρων, meaning 'early old man', perhaps referring to the appearance of the seed heads) and species name 'annuus' meaning 'annual'.
