unguiculate

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɡwɪkjələt / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡwɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
A mammal that has nails or claws.
mammal with nails or claws

Examples

The primate is an unguiculate, possessing flat nails rather than hooves.
Most rodents are classified as unguiculates due to their sharp claws.
Unlike hoofed animals, unguiculates have claws or nails on their digits.

Etymology

From Latin 'unguiculatus', from 'unguiculus' (a small nail or claw).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡwɪkjələt / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡwɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
having or resembling claws or nails; especially used to describe animals, appendages, or plant parts with claw-like features
having claws or claw-like features

Examples

Some mammals, like cats, are considered unguiculate due to their sharp retractable claws.
The botanist observed the unguiculate petals at the base of the flower.
Birds of prey are classic examples of unguiculate animals.

Synset Examples

unguiculate animals
an unguiculate flower petal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unguiculated - having small claws or nails; alternative form

Etymology

From Latin unguiculatus (with small claws or nails), from unguiculus, diminutive of unguis (nail, claw).
