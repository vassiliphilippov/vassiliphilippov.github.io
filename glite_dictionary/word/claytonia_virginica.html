Claytonia virginica

1. NOUN

/ kleɪˈtoʊniə vɜrˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / kleɪˈtəʊniə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A small perennial plant of eastern North America, known for its low rosette of succulent leaves and racemes of pink-tinged white flowers; commonly called spring beauty.
North American spring beauty (plant species)

Examples

Claytonia virginica is one of the first wildflowers to bloom in eastern forests each spring.
Gardeners appreciate Claytonia virginica for its delicate, star-shaped blossoms and shade tolerance.
The presence of Claytonia virginica can indicate rich, moist woodland soils.

Etymology

Genus 'Claytonia' named after John Clayton, an 18th-century Virginia botanist; species name refers to Virginia.
