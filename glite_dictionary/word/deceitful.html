deceitful

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsitfəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːtfʊl / (BrE)
behaving in a dishonest way by pretending to have feelings or intentions that are not genuine; intended to mislead or deceive
dishonest or intended to deceive

Examples

She realized too late that her closest friend had been deceitful all along.
The company's deceitful advertising led many customers to file complaints.
His deceitful actions eventually caught up with him, ruining his reputation.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not truthful or trustworthy
  • Antonym: truthful - telling the truth; honest

Etymology

From Old French *deceit* (deception) + *-ful* (full of).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsitfəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːtfʊl / (BrE)
behaving in a dishonest way, or intending to make someone believe something that is not true
dishonest; intending to deceive

Examples

Many consumers were misled by the company's deceitful advertising.
His deceitful actions hurt the trust between friends.
She accused the salesman of being deceitful about the car's true condition.

Synset Examples

deceitful advertising
fallacious testimony
smooth, shining, and deceitful as thin ice
a fraudulent scheme to escape paying taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to lie or cheat
  • Antonym: truthful - tending to tell the truth; honest

Etymology

From Old French *deceit*, from Latin *decipere* (to deceive), with the suffix *-ful* meaning 'full of'.
