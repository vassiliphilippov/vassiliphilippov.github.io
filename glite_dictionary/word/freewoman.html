freewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is not a serf or a slave; a woman with the legal status of freedom
a woman who is not a slave or serf

Examples

In medieval England, a freewoman could own property and conduct business in her own right.
The document certified her status as a freewoman after years of bondage.
Having been born a freewoman, she valued her independence above all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeman - a man who is not a serf or a slave
  • Antonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another

Etymology

Formed from 'free', meaning not in bondage, and 'woman'. Patterned after 'freeman' (from Old English 'frēoman').
