congenial

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒiːniəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
(of a person, place, or environment) pleasant because it suits one's character or tastes.
pleasant or suitable to one's character or tastes

Examples

She found the quiet library to be a congenial place for studying late at night.
His colleagues created a congenial atmosphere that made work enjoyable.
They clicked immediately, discovering they had a congenial sense of humor.

Synset Examples

a congenial atmosphere to work in
two congenial spirits united … by mutual confidence and reciprocal virtues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeable - pleasant, enjoyable, or suitable
  • Antonym: uncongenial - not pleasant or not suitable to one's character or tastes

Etymology

From Latin congenialis (of the same kind, agreeable), from com- (together) + genialis (of birth).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒiniəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
(of plants) capable of being cross-fertilized or grafted together due to compatibility.
compatible for grafting or fertilization (plants)

Examples

These two rose species are congenial, making them ideal for grafting.
Choosing congenial rootstocks is vital for successful fruit tree propagation.
The plants proved surprisingly congenial, allowing for efficient cross-breeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'congenialis' (of the same kind, congenial), from 'com-' (together) + 'genus' (kind, birth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00509701-s
Synset POS: s
