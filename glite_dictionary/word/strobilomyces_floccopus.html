Strobilomyces floccopus

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˌbɪloʊˈmaɪsiːz fləˈkɑːpəs / (AmE), / strəʊˌbɪlɵˈmaɪsiːz flɒˈkɒpəs / (BrE)
an edible mushroom of eastern North America, with a mild taste, found in coniferous woodlands
an edible woodland mushroom species

Examples

We found a cluster of Strobilomyces floccopus while hiking through the pine forest.
The cap of Strobilomyces floccopus has a characteristic shaggy surface, making it easy to identify.
Some mushroom hunters prize Strobilomyces floccopus for its mild, earthy flavor in wild mushroom dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old man of the woods - common name for Strobilomyces floccopus

Etymology

From Greek 'strobilos' (pine cone) and 'mykes' (fungus); species epithet 'floccopus' from Latin 'floccus' (tuft of wool) and 'pous' (foot), describing the mushroom's appearance.
