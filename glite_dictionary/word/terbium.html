terbium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.bi.əm / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bi.əm / (BrE)
a silvery-white metallic element of the lanthanide series, used in lasers and as a dopant in phosphors; chemical symbol Tb
a soft, silvery metal element, Tb

Examples

The scientist used terbium to create a new type of laser material.
Phosphors containing terbium are often used in green television screens.
Deposits of terbium can be found in minerals such as monazite and xenotime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yttrium - a metallic element also named after Ytterby, related in discovery

Etymology

From Latin 'Ytterby', the Swedish town where its ore was first discovered, with the name patterned after other rare earth elements.
