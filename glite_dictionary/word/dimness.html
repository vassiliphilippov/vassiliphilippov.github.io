dimness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪmnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dim; lack of brightness, clarity, or distinctness
lack of brightness or clarity

Examples

The dimness of the room made it hard to read the text on the page.
She stumbled along the hallway, struggling to see through the evening's dimness.
As your eyes adjust to the dimness, you begin to make out the shapes in the shadows.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being bright
  • Synonym: faintness - the quality of being faint or lacking brightness

Etymology

From 'dim' (Middle English, of Germanic origin) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not bright or not intense, especially in light or sound
lack of brightness or intensity

Examples

The dimness of the room made it difficult to read the labels on the jars.
His voice had a dimness to it, barely rising above a whisper in the dark hallway.
She adjusted the lamp to reduce its dimness and fill the space with brighter light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightness - the quality or state of being bright
  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of being dull or lacking vividness or intensity

Etymology

From 'dim' + '-ness', with 'dim' from Old English 'dymm' (dark, obscure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04963084-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having little or insufficient light; poor illumination
low or poor illumination

Examples

The dimness of the room made it difficult to read the book.
She walked carefully through the dimness of the early morning.
As the sun set, a gentle dimness spread across the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightness - the quality or state of giving out or reflecting a lot of light
  • Degree Higher: darkness - the partial or total absence of light

Etymology

From 'dim' (obscure, not bright) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
