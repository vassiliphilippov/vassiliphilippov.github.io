Toxicodendron vernix

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑksɪkoʊˈdɛndrən ˈvɜrnɪks / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪkəʊˈdɛndrən ˈvɜːnɪks / (BrE)
A smooth North American swamp shrub with pinnate leaves and greenish flowers, producing greenish white berries and an oily substance that can cause skin irritation; known as poison sumac.
swamp shrub known as poison sumac

Examples

Many people develop a rash after coming into contact with Toxicodendron vernix in wetland areas.
Toxicodendron vernix has distinctive smooth branches and clusters of pale berries.
Botanists studied Toxicodendron vernix to better understand the chemical composition of its irritating oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison sumac - Common name for Toxicodendron vernix, a poisonous shrub of North American wetlands

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'toxicon' (poison) + 'dendron' (tree); species from Latin 'vernix' (varnish, referring to sap).
