St. Francis of Assisi

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs əv əˈsiːsi / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs əv əˈsiːsi / (BrE)
an Italian monk and preacher (1181–1226), founder of the Franciscan order, revered for his devotion to poverty and nature
Italian monk; founder of the Franciscan order

Examples

St. Francis of Assisi is often depicted with birds and animals, symbolizing his love for all creatures.
Many churches are named in honor of St. Francis of Assisi throughout the world.
The teachings of St. Francis of Assisi inspired generations to embrace humility and compassion.
