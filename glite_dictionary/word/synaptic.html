synaptic

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈnæptɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈnæptɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving synapses, the junctions between nerve cells where signals are transmitted
relating to synapses (nerve cell junctions)
medical

Examples

The synaptic connections in the brain are essential for learning and memory.
Scientists studied synaptic transmission to understand how neurons communicate.
Certain drugs can interfere with synaptic activity, affecting brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synapse - the junction between two nerve cells

Etymology

From Greek 'synapsis' (connection, junction) and -ic.
