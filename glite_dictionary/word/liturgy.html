liturgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɪtədʒi / (BrE)
a Christian rite or sacrament commemorating the Last Supper by consecrating bread and wine
Christian rite with consecrated bread and wine

Examples

The Sunday liturgy is attended by hundreds of parishioners.
During the Orthodox liturgy, the priest consecrates bread and wine.
The liturgy includes prayers, hymns, and the celebration of the Eucharist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - the Christian sacrament of Holy Communion, commemorating the Last Supper
  • Synonym: Mass - the main eucharistic service in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Greek leitourgia (public service, worship), from leitourgos (public servant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
a set form of public worship, especially the prescribed rites and rituals of a religious service
set form of public worship

Examples

The church introduced a new liturgy to make services more accessible.
Many Christians are familiar with the structure of the Sunday liturgy.
The priest led the liturgy during the festive celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rite - a ceremonial act or series of acts in religious observance

Etymology

From Greek leitourgia (public service, worship), via Late Latin liturgia.
