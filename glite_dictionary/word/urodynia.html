urodynia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈdɪniə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈdɪnɪə / (BrE)
painful or difficult urination; pain experienced during urination
pain during urination
medical

Examples

The patient complained of urodynia following the medical procedure.
Chronic urodynia can be a symptom of urinary tract infection.
Doctors often inquire about urodynia when evaluating urinary symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysuria - painful or difficult urination (medical term)

Etymology

From Greek 'ouron' (urine) + 'odynē' (pain).
