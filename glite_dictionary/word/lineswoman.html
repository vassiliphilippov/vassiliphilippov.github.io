lineswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnzwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnzwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to install or repair electrical or telephone lines
female installer or repairer of electrical or telephone lines

Examples

After years of training, she became the first lineswoman in her city.
The lineswoman quickly repaired the downed power cable after the storm.
Many young women aspire to become a lineswoman in the electrical industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linesman - a person who installs or repairs electrical or telephone lines, regardless of gender

Etymology

Formed from 'line' (a cable or wire) and 'woman', modeled on 'linesman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnzwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnzwʊmən / (BrE)
a female official in sports such as tennis, soccer, or football, who assists the referee by making decisions about the boundaries of the playing area or offside.
female sports official assisting referee

Examples

The lineswoman raised her flag to signal that the ball was out of play.
During the match, the lineswoman spotted an offside, helping the referee make the correct call.
The tennis player thanked the lineswoman for her sharp observation after a close call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linesman - an official in sports who assists the referee by judging boundary decisions or offside, regardless of gender

Etymology

Formed from 'line' and 'woman', patterned after 'linesman'.
