contest

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɛst / (BrE)
a struggle for superiority or victory between rivals or opponents
struggle for superiority or victory

Examples

The presidential election was a fierce contest between the two major parties.
A close contest developed as both athletes pushed themselves to the limit.
The teams faced off in a contest that kept the audience on the edge of their seats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the activity or condition of striving to gain or win something by defeating others
  • Antonym: cooperation - the process of working together to the same end

Etymology

From Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness), from 'con-' (together) + 'testari' (to bear witness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɛst / (BrE)
an event in which people compete, and a winner is chosen from among the participants
competitive event with a winner

Examples

She won first prize in the annual baking contest.
The school held a spelling contest for all students.
Thousands entered the photo contest hoping to win the grand prize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - an event or activity in which people compete against each other
  • Synonym: match - a sports or other contest between two or more people or teams

Etymology

From Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness).

3. VERB

/ kənˈtɛst / (AmE), / kənˈtɛst / (BrE)
to formally dispute or challenge the validity or outcome of something, especially in a competition or legal context
to formally dispute or challenge

Examples

The lawyers decided to contest the validity of the contract in court.
Several athletes contested the results, claiming there was a timing error.
She plans to contest the will because she believes it was forged.

Synset Examples

They contested the outcome of the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to dispute the truth or validity of something
  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to or receive without protest

Etymology

From Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness), from 'con-' (together) + 'testari' (to witness).
