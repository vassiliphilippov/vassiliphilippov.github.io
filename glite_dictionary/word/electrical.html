electrical

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with electricity or the use of electricity.
relating to electricity

Examples

The new building requires several electrical upgrades to meet safety standards.
He is studying to become an electrical engineer at university.
The electrical system in the house was completely rewired last year.

Synset Examples

an electrical engineer
electrical and mechanical engineering industries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric - using, producing, or operated by electricity

Etymology

From Latin 'electricus' (produced from amber by friction), via New Latin and English 'electric'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, using, producing, or operated by electricity.
relating to electricity or its use

Examples

The house needed a complete electrical rewiring to meet modern standards.
They hired an engineer to solve the electrical problems in the building.
Lightning struck during an electrical storm, causing a temporary power outage.

Synset Examples

electric current
electric wiring
electrical appliances
an electrical storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric - operated by, producing, or charged with electricity
  • Antonym: mechanical - relating to machines or machinery, especially as opposed to electricity

Etymology

From electric + -al, modeled on scientific terminology in the 18th century.
