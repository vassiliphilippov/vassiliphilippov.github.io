muscat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌskət / (BrE)
A sweet, aromatic variety of grape used to make raisins and wine.
sweet aromatic grape variety

Examples

The vineyard specializes in muscat, producing both sweet wines and raisins.
A bowl of fresh muscat grapes sat on the kitchen table, giving off a fragrant aroma.
She prefers muscat grapes for baking because of their distinct sweetness.

Etymology

From French 'muscat', from Italian 'moscato', ultimately from Latin 'muscus' (musk), referring to its scent.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kæt / (BrE)
a variety of grapevine that produces sweet white grapes, often used for wine or table consumption
sweet white grape variety

Examples

The muscat grape is prized for its fragrant, sweet flavor in dessert wines.
Farmers harvested a record crop of muscats this season, perfect for fresh consumption and winemaking.
Many Mediterranean desserts feature the distinctive taste of muscat grapes.

Etymology

From French 'muscat', from Italian 'moscato', from Latin 'muscatus' (musky), referring to the aroma.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kæt / (BrE)
A sweet wine made from muscat grapes, typically characterized by a strong, fragrant aroma.
sweet wine from muscat grapes

Examples

We served a chilled glass of muscat with dessert to complement the fruit tart.
After dinner, he poured himself a small amount of muscat to enjoy the floral aroma.
Australian muscat is famous for its rich, sweet flavor and amber color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moscato - Italian sweet wine made from muscat grapes

Etymology

From French 'muscat,' ultimately from Italian 'moscato,' from Latin 'muscatum' (meaning musky, due to the aroma).
