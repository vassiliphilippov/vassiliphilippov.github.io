Trachurus symmetricus

1. NOUN

/ trəˈkjʊrəs sɪˈmɛtrɪkəs / (AmE), / trəˈkjʊərəs sɪˈmɛtrɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of jack mackerel native to the Pacific coast of North America, commonly found off California and used as food fish
Pacific jack mackerel species (food fish)

Examples

Fishermen in California often catch Trachurus symmetricus for local seafood markets.
Scientists are studying the population decline of Trachurus symmetricus along the Pacific coast.
The restaurant serves grilled Trachurus symmetricus with a side of seasonal vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack mackerel - a common name for edible fish species of the Carangidae family

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Trachurus' as the genus name for horse mackerels, and 'symmetricus' meaning 'symmetrical'.
