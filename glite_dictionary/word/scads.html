scads

1. NOUN

/ skædz / (AmE), / skædz / (BrE)
a large number or amount of something; a lot (usually used in plural form, informal)
a large number or amount (informal)
informal

Examples

She has scads of experience in project management.
After winning the lottery, he spent scads of money on new cars and trips.
There were scads of people at the festival this weekend.

Synset Examples

made lots of new friends
she amassed stacks of newspapers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loads - a large number or amount (informal)
  • Synonym: tons - a great deal; a large amount (informal)
  • Antonym: few - not many; a small number

Etymology

Probably from 'scad' (great quantity), American English slang from the late 19th century.
