powerbroker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊərˌbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌbrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who wields significant influence or control over people or votes, often in politics or business, by virtue of their connections and ability to mobilize support
person with significant influence or control

Examples

He became a powerbroker in city politics, shaping decisions from behind the scenes.
Major powerbrokers gathered at the summit to discuss the country's future.
The media described her as a powerbroker with the ability to sway important elections.

Synset Examples

a power broker who does you a favor will expect you to return it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingmaker - a person who has great influence in the selection of a candidate for public office

Etymology

Formed from "power" + "broker," originally used in the context of politics in the mid-20th century.
