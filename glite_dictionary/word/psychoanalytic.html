psychoanalytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌænəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌanəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or based on psychoanalysis, a system of psychological theory and therapy developed by Sigmund Freud
related to psychoanalysis (Freud's theory/therapy)

Examples

The psychoanalytic approach emphasizes the importance of unconscious motives in behavior.
She began psychoanalytic therapy to explore her early childhood experiences.
Many modern theories of personality expand upon psychoanalytic concepts developed by Freud.

Synset Examples

Freud's psychoanalytical theories
psychoanalytic treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalytical - alternate form meaning the same as 'psychoanalytic'

Etymology

From psycho- (mind, psychology) + analytic, after psychoanalysis, the therapy developed by Freud.
