to symbolise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or express something by means of a symbol or symbols
represent by a symbol
british

Examples

The white dove symbolises peace in many cultures.
In the painting, the color red symbolises passion and energy.
They chose an olive branch to symbolise hope and reconciliation.

Synset Examples

The poet symbolizes love in this poem
These painters believed that artists should symbolize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symbolize - To represent by means of a symbol (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek sumbolon (symbol) via French symboliser and late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or express an idea, quality, or concept indirectly by using a symbol, image, or figure.
represent by a symbol or image

Examples

The olive branch symbolises peace across many cultures.
To her, the mountain symbolises an insurmountable challenge she must face.
In this mural, the rising sun symbolises hope and new beginnings.

Synset Examples

What does the Statue of Liberty symbolize?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - to stand for or be a sign or symbol of something
  • Synonym: to signify - to be a sign of; to indicate
  • Synonym: to denote - to be a sign or indication of

Etymology

From Greek symbolon (mark, token, sign), via Old French and Late Latin symbolizare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837915-v
Synset POS: v
