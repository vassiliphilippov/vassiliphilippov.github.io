grooming

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of styling or caring for the hair to improve its appearance
hair styling or care

Examples

Daily grooming is essential to keep long hair healthy and shiny.
She specializes in men's grooming at the upscale salon.
Proper grooming techniques can make your hairstyle last longer.

Synset Examples

I wanted to get these out to you guys because you guys have been asking for styling products since the beginning, so I'm so glad to finally have actual styling products that I can, um, you know, present to you guys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: styling - the act of arranging or designing hair

Etymology

From 'groom' (to tend or make neat), originally from Middle English 'grome' (boy, servant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of preparing yourself for appearance, especially by washing, dressing, and making yourself neat
personal preparation and dressing

Examples

She spent an hour on her grooming before leaving for the party.
Good personal grooming is important for making a good impression at work.
His morning routine includes exercise, breakfast, and grooming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing - the activity of putting on clothes
  • Antonym: undressing - the act of removing clothes

Etymology

From 'groom' (to tend to) + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00829898-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːmɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of preparing or training someone for a particular activity, role, or skill.
training or preparation for a skill or role

Examples

The academy is dedicated to the grooming of future athletes.
Her early grooming as a musician set the foundation for her success.
The mentorship program focuses on the grooming of young leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: training - the process of teaching or learning skills

Etymology

From 'groom' (train, prepare) + -ing (action, process).
