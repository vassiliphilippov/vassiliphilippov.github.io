maidenhair

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdnˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdnˌheə / (BrE)
A type of fern of the genus Adiantum, known for its delicate, finely divided fronds resembling hair.
a delicate fern of the genus Adiantum

Examples

The maidenhair added a delicate touch to the shaded corner of the garden.
She recognized the fine, lacy appearance of maidenhair growing beside the stream.
Botanists admire the unique structure of maidenhair fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - another term for maidenhair, especially Adiantum capillus-veneris

Etymology

From 'maiden', referring to delicacy and grace, and 'hair', for the appearance of its fronds.
