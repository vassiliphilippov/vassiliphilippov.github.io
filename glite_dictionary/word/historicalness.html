historicalness

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔr.ɪ.kəl.nəs / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒr.ɪ.kəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being significant because of its history.
quality of historical significance

Examples

The historicalness of the site attracts scholars from around the world.
Many buildings were preserved because of their historicalness rather than their architectural value.
She argued for the historicalness of the artifact as a reason to keep it on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: historicity - the historical authenticity or actuality of something

Etymology

From 'historical' + '-ness', where 'historical' derives from Greek 'historikos' (of history).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having actually existed or occurred in the past
quality of being historical

Examples

Some scholars debate the historicalness of ancient legends.
The historicalness of the document gives it significant value to researchers.
Questions remained about the historicalness of the artifacts discovered at the site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: historicity - the quality of being part of history; having actually happened or existed

Etymology

From 'historical' + '-ness', denoting quality or state.
