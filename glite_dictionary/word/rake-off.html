rake-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪk ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈreɪk ɒf / (BrE)
a share or commission taken from profits, often illicitly or as a result of corrupt practice
illicit share or commission from profits

Examples

The boss demanded a rake-off from every deal made in his territory.
Some corrupt officials expect a rake-off before granting construction permits.
The casino always takes a small rake-off from the poker pot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kickback - a payment made secretly for favorable treatment
  • Synonym: bribe - money or favor given to influence someone illegally
  • Synonym: commission - a fee paid to an agent for services

Etymology

From the phrase 'rake off,' originally referring to literal raking of profits or proceeds aside.
