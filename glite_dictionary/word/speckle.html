speckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛk.əl / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.əl / (BrE)
a small spot or mark of contrasting color, especially one of many on a surface
small contrasting spot or mark

Examples

The robin's eggs were pale blue with tiny speckles on them.
Dark speckles scattered across the surface of the rock added to its beauty.
Her dress was white with delicate red speckles along the hem.

Synset Examples

a bald spot
a leopard's spots
a patch of clouds
patches of thin ice
a fleck of red

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round mark of a different color
  • Synonym: fleck - a small patch of color or light
  • Synonym: blotch - an irregular patch of color

Etymology

From Middle English 'spekkel', probably related to 'speck'.

2. VERB

/ ˈspɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈspɛkəl / (BrE)
to mark or cover with small spots or patches of color; to produce a mottled effect
mark with small spots

Examples

The artist speckled the canvas with bright yellow paint to create a lively effect.
Morning dew speckled the grass, sparkling in the early sunlight.
Small flecks of chocolate speckle the top of the dessert, enhancing its visual appeal.

Synset Examples

The sunlight stippled the trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - to scatter small drops or spots of a substance
  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small round dots
  • Synonym: to stipple - to mark with numerous small dots or specks

Etymology

From Middle English, diminutive of 'speck' (a small spot).

3. VERB

/ ˈspɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈspɛkəl / (BrE)
to cover or mark with many small spots or specks of a different color or texture
to mark with small spots

Examples

Tiny flecks of paint speckled her apron after she finished the mural.
Golden light speckles the forest floor through gaps in the leafy canopy.
Raindrops speckled the window, distorting the view outside.

Synset Examples

speckle the wall with tiny yellow spots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small dots or patches
  • Synonym: stain - to mark or discolor with patches of different color
  • Synonym: daub - to coat or smear carelessly with a thick or sticky substance

Etymology

From Middle English speckel, related to 'speck' (a small spot), probably of Low German or Scandinavian origin.
