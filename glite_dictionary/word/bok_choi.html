bok choi

1. NOUN

/ bɑk tʃɔɪ / (AmE), / bɒk tʃɔɪ / (BrE)
A type of Chinese cabbage with dark green leaves and thick white stalks, often used in Asian cooking.
type of Chinese cabbage

Examples

She added fresh bok choi to the stir-fry for extra crunch and flavor.
At the market, we bought a bundle of organic bok choi for tonight’s dinner.
Steaming bok choi helps retain its nutrients and bright color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pak choi - another name for bok choi

Etymology

From Cantonese 白菜 (baak choi), literally ‘white vegetable’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːk ˈtʃɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌbɒk ˈtʃɔɪ / (BrE)
A type of Chinese cabbage with white stalks and dark green leaves, eaten as a vegetable.
Chinese cabbage with white stalks and green leaves

Examples

Stir-frying bok choi with garlic makes a simple and healthy dish.
He added chopped bok choi to the soup for extra flavor and texture.
The market sells fresh bok choi all year round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pak choi - Another name for bok choi, common variant spelling
  • Synonym: Chinese cabbage - General term for several leafy green vegetables of Chinese origin, including bok choi

Etymology

From Cantonese 白菜 (baak choi), meaning ‘white vegetable’.
