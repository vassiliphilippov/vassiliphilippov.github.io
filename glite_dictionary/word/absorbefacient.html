absorbefacient

1. ADJ

/ əbˌzɔrbəˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / əbˌsɔːbəˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
Inducing or promoting absorption, especially the absorption of fluids or substances by tissues.
promoting absorption (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an absorbefacient cream to help reduce the swelling.
This medication acts as an absorbefacient in treating skin inflammation.
Researchers are studying absorbefacient agents for more effective drug delivery.

Etymology

From Latin 'absorbere' (to absorb) + 'facient' (making, causing).
