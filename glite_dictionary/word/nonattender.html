nonattender

1. NOUN

/ nɑn.əˈtɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / nɒn.əˈtɛn.də / (BrE)
a person who does not attend an event, meeting, or duty, especially when expected to be present
someone who does not attend when expected

Examples

The school contacted the parents of every nonattender after the field trip.
As a frequent nonattender at meetings, he missed important updates.
The club president made an effort to reach out to known nonattenders to increase participation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attender - a person who is present at an event or duty

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'attender' (one who attends).
