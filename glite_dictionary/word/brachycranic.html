brachycranic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbrækiˈkreɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈkrænɪk / (BrE)
having a short, broad head, typically with a cephalic index over 80
short, broad-headed (cephalic index > 80)
medical

Examples

The ancient skull was identified as brachycranic due to its noticeably broad and short shape.
Researchers classified the population as predominantly brachycranic based on cranial measurements.
A brachycranic head shape is associated with certain genetic lineages in anthropology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dolichocranic - having a long, narrow head (cephalic index below 75)

Etymology

From Greek 'brakhys' (short) and 'kranion' (skull).
