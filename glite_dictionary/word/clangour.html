clangour

1. VERB

/ ˈklæŋər / (AmE), / ˈklæŋə / (BrE)
to make a loud, resonant, metallic noise.
make a loud resonant noise

Examples

The bells clangoured in the old church tower at midnight.
Sirens clangoured through the city at the break of dawn, warning residents of the approaching storm.
Metal gates clangour loudly whenever the wind picks up.

Synset Examples

the alarm clangored throughout the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - to make a loud, resonant metallic noise
  • Synonym: ring - to make a clear resonant sound

Etymology

From Old French *clangor*, from Latin *clangor* (a ringing sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02178905-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæŋər / (AmE), / ˈklæŋə / (BrE)
a loud, resonant, and usually repeated metallic noise or ringing sound
loud ringing metallic noise

Examples

The clangour of the church bells echoed through the valley on Sunday morning.
She woke suddenly to the clangour of pots and pans in the kitchen below.
The clangour from the construction site made concentration nearly impossible.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clangor - alternative spelling meaning the same loud metallic noise

Etymology

From Latin 'clangor' (a ringing sound), via Old French 'clangor'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07394744-n
Synset POS: n
