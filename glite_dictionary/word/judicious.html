judicious

1. ADJ

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəs / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having or showing good judgment or sense in making decisions, especially in practical matters.
having or showing good judgment

Examples

The company made a judicious decision to invest in new technology.
She always gives judicious advice when her friends face difficult choices.
A judicious use of resources helped the team complete the project on time.

Synset Examples

judicious use of one's money
a wise decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment; unwise

Etymology

From Latin iūdicium (judgment) + -ous.
