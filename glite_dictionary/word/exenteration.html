exenteration

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɛn.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌsɛn.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
surgical removal of the organs from a body cavity, especially those of the pelvis
surgical removal of internal organs
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended an exenteration to treat the advanced pelvic tumor.
After undergoing exenteration, the patient required significant postoperative care and support.
Orbital exenteration may be necessary in cases of aggressive eye cancer.

Etymology

From Latin exenteratio, from Greek exenteran (remove the entrails), from ex- (out of) + enteron (intestine).
