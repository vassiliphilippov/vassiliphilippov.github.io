dill

1. NOUN

/ dɪl / (AmE), / dɪl / (BrE)
An aromatic herb of the parsley family, with fine, feathery leaves and seeds used to flavor food.
aromatic herb used as seasoning

Examples

She added fresh dill to the salad for extra flavor.
The pickles were seasoned with garlic and dill seeds.
In Scandinavian cooking, dill is a popular herb for fish dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic herb with feathery leaves, similar to dill

Etymology

From Old English 'dile', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ dɪl / (AmE), / dɪl / (BrE)
the aromatic, threadlike leaves of the dill plant, used as a seasoning in cooking
aromatic herb used as seasoning

Examples

She sprinkled fresh dill over the potatoes before serving.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of chopped dill.
I grow dill in my garden to use in salads and pickles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic yellow-flowered plant related to dill, also used as a spice and herb

Etymology

From Old English 'dyle', related to Old Norse 'dilla', meaning to lull (referring to the herb's soothing qualities).
