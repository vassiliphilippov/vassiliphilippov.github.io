plod

1. NOUN

/ plɑd / (AmE), / plɒd / (BrE)
a slow, heavy, and laborious walk
slow, heavy walk

Examples

The unmistakable plod of boots echoed down the hallway.
She watched his lonely plod across the muddy field.
After hours of hiking, their plod became even slower.

Synset Examples

I could recognize his plod anywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically from exhaustion or harsh conditions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292809-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ plɑd / (AmE), / plɒd / (BrE)
to walk with slow, heavy steps, often because of tiredness or difficulty
walk with slow, heavy steps

Examples

We had to plod through thick mud to reach the campsite.
After a long day of work, she plodded home in silence.
The old horse continued to plod along the winding country road.

Synset Examples

Mules plodded in a circle around a grindstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically due to exhaustion or harsh conditions
