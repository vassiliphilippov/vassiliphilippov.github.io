sedimentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛdəˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌsɛdɪˈmɛntri / (BrE)
consisting of, containing, or formed by the accumulation of sediment, especially as related to rocks and geological formations
relating to or formed by sediment

Examples

The region is famous for its sedimentary rock formations, which reveal millions of years of Earth's history.
Scientists studied the sedimentary layers to understand past climates and environmental changes.
Most oil reservoirs are found within sedimentary basins beneath the surface.

Synset Examples

sedimentary deposits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: igneous - relating to rocks formed by solidification from molten magma
  • Antonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks that have been transformed by heat and pressure

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sedimentarius, from Latin sedimentum (settling, sediment).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛdəˈmɛn.t̬ə.ɹi / (AmE), / ˌsɛd.ɪˈmen.tə.ri / (BrE)
(of rock or material) formed by the deposition and solidification of mineral or organic particles, often by the action of water, wind, or ice.
formed from deposited material (often by water)

Examples

The scientist collected samples of sedimentary rock from the riverbank for analysis.
Many fossils are found in sedimentary layers, which were formed by ancient seas.
The sedimentary deposits indicated that the area was once underwater.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: igneous - rock formed by solidification of magma or lava
  • Antonym: metamorphic - rock formed by transformation of existing rock under heat and pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'sedimentum' (settling, sediment) + '-ary'.
