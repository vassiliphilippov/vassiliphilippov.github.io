honourableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnərəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of deserving honor or respect; the state of being honorable.
quality of being honorable or respectable

Examples

His honourableness was never questioned by colleagues or friends.
The judge was impressed by the honourableness with which she admitted her mistakes.
We admired the honourableness that guided every decision he made.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonourableness - The quality of being dishonorable; opposite of honourableness
  • Synonym: honorableness - American English spelling of 'honourableness'

Etymology

From Latin 'honorabilis' (worthy of honor), via Old French 'honorable', with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun.
