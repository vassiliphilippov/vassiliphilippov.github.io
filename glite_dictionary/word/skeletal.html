skeletal

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɛlətl̩ / (AmE), / ˈskelɪtəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or forming a skeleton; connected to or part of the skeleton.
relating to the skeleton

Examples

The skeletal system provides support and structure to the human body.
Doctors studied the skeletal remains to learn more about the ancient civilization.
Skeletal muscles are responsible for voluntary movements in animals.

Synset Examples

the skeletal system
skeletal bones
skeletal muscles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: muscular - relating to muscles
  • Degree Higher: bone - hard, dense connective tissue forming the skeleton

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'skeletalis', from Greek 'skeletos' (dried up, mummy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɛlətl / (AmE), / ˈskɛlətl / (BrE)
Extremely thin, as if reduced to the bones, especially due to illness, hunger, or cold.
extremely thin, like a skeleton

Examples

After months on the run, the stray dog looked skeletal and weak.
Many famine victims appeared skeletal because of prolonged hunger.
His face had become so skeletal that his cheeks seemed hollow.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - extremely thin and bony, especially from hunger or illness
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape, opposite of very thin

Etymology

From Greek *skeletos* (dried up), via Latin *skeletus* (skeleton).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00992194-s
Synset POS: s
