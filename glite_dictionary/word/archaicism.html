archaicism

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈkeɪ.ɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪ.ɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the use or conscious adoption of an archaic word, expression, or style, especially in writing or speech
use of an archaic word or expression

Examples

The poet's frequent archaicism gives the poem a sense of antiquity.
His use of archaicism made the legal document difficult to understand.
Intentionally adding an archaicism can sometimes enhance the tone of historical fiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaism - an archaic word or expression; also, the use of archaic words or expressions

Etymology

From ancient Greek ‘arkhaïkós’ (ancient) via French ‘archaïsme’.
