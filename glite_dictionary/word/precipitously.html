precipitously

1. ADV

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtəsli / (BrE)
very steeply or suddenly; in a manner that is abrupt or extremely steep
in a very steep or abrupt manner

Examples

The cliffs rise precipitously above the rocky coastline, making the area difficult to access.
After the announcement, stock prices dropped precipitously within hours.
The road climbs precipitously through the mountain pass, challenging even experienced drivers.

Synset Examples

the mountains rose precipitously from the shore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - suddenly or unexpectedly, without warning
  • Antonym: gradually - slowly and steadily, not suddenly or abruptly

Etymology

From 'precipitous' (steep, abrupt), ultimately from Latin ‘praeceps’ (headlong, steep).

2. ADV

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtʃuəsli / (BrE)
Very suddenly and to a great extent; in a way that happens rapidly and steeply.
suddenly and steeply

Examples

Share prices fell precipitously after the surprising announcement.
The mountain path dropped precipitously toward the valley below.
The river's water level rose precipitously following days of heavy rain.

Synset Examples

conditions that precipitously increase the birthrate
prices rose sharply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - very suddenly or unexpectedly
  • Antonym: gradually - slowly over time, not suddenly

Etymology

From Latin 'praecipitōsus' (steep), via 'precipitous' + '-ly'.

Synset POS: r
