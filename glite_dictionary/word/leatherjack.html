leatherjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌdʒæk / (BrE)
Any of several tropical fish species of the New World, noted for their very small embedded scales and leather-like skin.
tropical fish with tiny embedded scales

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a large leatherjack caught off the coast of Florida.
Many anglers are intrigued by the unusual scales of the leatherjack.
In tropical waters, the leatherjack is known for its tough, leather-like skin.
