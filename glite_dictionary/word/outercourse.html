outercourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtɚˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtəˌkɔːs / (BrE)
sexual activity involving stimulation without vaginal penetration, often used as a method to avoid pregnancy or sexually transmitted infections.
sexual stimulation without penetration

Examples

Some couples prefer outercourse as a way to enjoy intimacy and reduce the risk of pregnancy.
Practicing outercourse can help partners avoid sexually transmitted infections from penetrative sex.
The workshop provided information on safe outercourse techniques for sexual health education.

Synset Examples

since pregnancy cannot happen if sperm are kept out of the vagina, outercourse is one method of birth control

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intercourse - sexual activity involving penetration

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'intercourse', combining 'out-' (meaning 'outside') and 'course'.
