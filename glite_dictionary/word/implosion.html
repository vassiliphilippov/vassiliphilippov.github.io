implosion

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈploʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊʒən / (BrE)
The initial phase during the articulation of a stop consonant, when the airflow is briefly blocked in the vocal tract.
initial phase in stop consonant articulation

Examples

A linguist may analyze the timing of the implosion when studying speech sounds.
The implosion of a stop consonant is followed by a sudden release of air.
Accurate recording equipment is needed to clearly capture the subtle implosion in some languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explosion - the release phase of a stop consonant articulation

Etymology

From Latin implosio, from implodere (to burst inward).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈploʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊʒən / (BrE)
a sudden inward collapse, especially as a result of external pressure
sudden inward collapse

Examples

The submarine suffered an implosion when it descended too deep below the surface.
Experts investigated the cause of the building’s sudden implosion.
The implosion of the vacuum tube was both loud and dangerous.

Synset Examples

the implosion of a light bulb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explosion - a violent outward burst or blowing apart

Etymology

From Latin im- (in) + plosio (a striking), modeled after explosion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07380124-n
Synset POS: n
