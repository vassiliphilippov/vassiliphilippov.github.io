neglected

1. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (BrE)
Not cared for or attended to; left without necessary attention or support.
not cared for or attended to

Examples

The old house looked neglected, with overgrown weeds and peeling paint.
The shelter takes in neglected animals who have nowhere else to go.
After months of being neglected, the garden was full of dead plants.

Synset Examples

a neglected child
many casualties were lying unattended

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandoned - left without care or support

Etymology

From past participle of 'neglect', from Latin 'neglectus', past participle of 'neglegere' (to disregard, neglect).

2. ADJ

/ nɪɡˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (BrE)
not given proper attention or consideration; disregarded or ignored.
not given proper attention; disregarded

Examples

The warning signs were neglected until it was too late to act.
Despite her talent, her contributions were often neglected by her colleagues.
The garden had become wild and overgrown from years of being neglected.

Synset Examples

his cries were unheeded
Shaw's neglected one-act comedy, ‘A Village Wooing’
her ignored advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignored - not given attention; deliberately disregarded
  • Antonym: heeded - paid careful attention to; considered

Etymology

From past participle of 'neglect', from Latin 'neglegere' (to disregard).
