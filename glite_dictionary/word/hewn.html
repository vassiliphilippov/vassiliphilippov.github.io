hewn

1. ADJ

/ hjuːn / (AmE), / hjuːn / (BrE)
cut or shaped with hard blows from a heavy cutting instrument such as an axe or chisel
cut or shaped with heavy blows

Examples

The ancient temple was built with massive hewn stones.
They made furniture from hewn logs, giving it a rustic appearance.
A hewn path led through the dense forest to the village.

Synset Examples

a house built of hewn logs
rough-hewn stone
a path hewn through the underbrush

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rough-hewn - cut or shaped but left with a rough surface

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'hew', from Old English *heawan* (to cut, strike).
