Endamoeba

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndəˈmiːbə / (AmE), / ˌɛndəˈmiːbə / (BrE)
A genus of single-celled amoeboid protozoa, forming the type genus of the family Endamoebidae.
genus of amoeboid protozoa
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the life cycle of Endamoeba under laboratory conditions.
The genus Endamoeba includes several species found in the intestines of various animals.
Identification of Endamoeba is important in parasitology.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'en-' (within) and 'amoeba' (a type of protozoan).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛndəˈmiːbə / (AmE), / ˌɛndəˈmiːbə / (BrE)
A genus of free-living or parasitic amoeboid protozoa; the type genus of the family Endamoebidae.
genus of amoeboid protozoa

Examples

Scientists have studied the life cycle of Endamoeba to better understand its adaptations.
Some species in the genus Endamoeba are found living in the intestines of animals.
The classification of Endamoeba has changed as molecular data has emerged.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'endēmos' (native) + 'amoeba'.
