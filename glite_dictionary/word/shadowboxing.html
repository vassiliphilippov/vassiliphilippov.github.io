shadowboxing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊˌbɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊˌbɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of moving as if fighting an imaginary opponent, often as a form of exercise or training in boxing or martial arts.
boxing exercise with an imaginary opponent

Examples

Professional boxers spend several minutes shadowboxing before each workout to warm up their bodies.
She improved her footwork by shadowboxing in front of the mirror every morning.
The coach demonstrated effective shadowboxing routines for the beginners to practice at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparring - practicing fighting techniques with a partner

Etymology

From 'shadow' + 'boxing', referring to fighting with no real opponent.
