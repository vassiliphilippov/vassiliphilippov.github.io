Richard Strauss

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ˈʃtraʊs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ˈʃtraʊs / (BrE)
(1864–1949) German composer known for his orchestral works and operas, including collaborations with librettist Hugo von Hofmannsthal.
German composer and opera writer

Examples

Richard Strauss composed several acclaimed operas that are still performed worldwide.
The music of Richard Strauss played a central role in the development of late Romanticism.
Many critics consider Richard Strauss's "Der Rosenkavalier" as one of his masterpieces.
