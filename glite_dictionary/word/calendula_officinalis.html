Calendula officinalis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɛn.dʒə.lə əˌfɪs.ɪˈnæl.ɪs / (AmE), / kəˌlɛndjʊlə ˌɒfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant known as the common marigold, widely grown for its yellow or orange blooms and used in gardens and herbal remedies
the common marigold plant

Examples

Many gardeners plant Calendula officinalis for its cheerful yellow flowers and natural pest-repellent qualities.
The petals of Calendula officinalis are often used in herbal salves and teas for their soothing properties.
Calendula officinalis thrives best in sunny spots and well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a bright yellow or orange garden flower

Etymology

From Latin: 'calendula' meaning marigold, and 'officinalis' meaning used in medicine.
