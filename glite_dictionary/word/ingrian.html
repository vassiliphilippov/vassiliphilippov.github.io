Ingrian

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡriən / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡriən / (BrE)
a Finnic language traditionally spoken by the Ingrian people of northwestern Russia
Finnic language of the Ingrian people

Examples

The Ingrian language has a rich oral tradition dating back centuries.
Few people today are fluent in Ingrian due to historical and cultural changes.
Linguists are working to preserve Ingrian by documenting its grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - a Uralic language spoken chiefly in Finland
  • Synonym: Estonian - a Finnic language spoken in Estonia

Etymology

From Ingrian, pertaining to Ingria, a region around St. Petersburg in Russia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡriən / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡriən / (BrE)
a member of a western Finnish people formerly inhabiting the Baltic region where Saint Petersburg now stands
member of a western Finnish ethnic group

Examples

Historians have documented the traditions of the Ingrian people in the Baltic area.
An Ingrian community settled near the coast in the 17th century.
Today, very few Ingrian descendants remain in the region around Saint Petersburg.

Etymology

From the region name Ingria, itself likely from Russian Ingermanland or Finnish Inkeri.
