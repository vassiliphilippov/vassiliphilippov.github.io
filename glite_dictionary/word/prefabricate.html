to prefabricate

1. VERB

/ ˌpriˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfæbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to manufacture parts or sections, especially of a building, in advance, usually in a factory, so that they can be easily transported and quickly assembled on site.
manufacture building sections in advance

Examples

The company will prefabricate the wall panels before transporting them to the construction site.
Modern builders often prefabricate major components to save time and reduce costs.
They chose to prefabricate the frames in a factory environment for greater precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - put together parts or components to form a complete unit

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'fabricate' (to make, from Latin 'fabricari').

2. VERB

/ priˈfræbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfræbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to produce or construct in advance, especially in a standardized, artificial, or unoriginal way
produce or construct in advance, often artificially

Examples

Some journalists tend to prefabricate stories instead of relying on genuine research.
The company began to prefabricate its products to increase efficiency and consistency.
Critics argue that the movie prefabricates emotions, leading to a predictable and unoriginal plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to make or construct, especially with skill

Etymology

From pre- (before) + fabricate, based on Latin 'fabricatus' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01625482-v
Synset POS: v
