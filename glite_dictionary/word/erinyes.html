Erinyes

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪniˌiz / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪniːiːz / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) the hideous snake-haired monsters, usually three in number, who pursued and punished unpunished criminals; also known as the Furies.
mythological avenging goddesses

Examples

The Erinyes appeared in Greek tragedies to torment those who escaped justice.
Legends say that the Erinyes haunted Orestes after he killed his mother.
Ancient poets often described the Erinyes as relentless and terrifying pursuers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Furies - Roman name for the Erinyes, avenging goddesses

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἐρινύες (Erinyes), the name for these avenging spirits.
