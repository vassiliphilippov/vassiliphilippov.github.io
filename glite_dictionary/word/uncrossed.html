uncrossed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkrɔst / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒst / (BrE)
(of limbs or objects) not crossed; in a straight or separate position.
not crossed; straight or apart

Examples

She sat with her legs uncrossed, trying to appear relaxed.
The teacher asked everyone to keep their arms uncrossed during the activity.
He placed the wires uncrossed on the table to avoid confusion.

Synset Examples

sitting primly with uncrossed legs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crossed - with parts placed over each other or intersected

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to crossed (from cross).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkrɔst / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒst / (BrE)
(of a check) not marked with two parallel lines, so it can be paid in cash over the counter rather than being deposited in a bank account.
not marked (of a check); payable in cash

Examples

Please ensure the payment is made only by uncrossed checks, as requested by the payee.
The thief attempted to cash an uncrossed check at the local branch.
An uncrossed check can be easily exchanged for cash at the bank counter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crossed - (of a check) marked with two parallel lines to restrict its payment to a bank account
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00656457-a
Synset POS: a
