arteriole

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriəʊl / (BrE)
a small branch of an artery that leads into capillaries
small artery leading to capillaries
medical

Examples

Each arteriole branches out to supply blood to a different part of the tissue.
The doctor explained how high blood pressure could damage the arterioles in the kidneys.
After leaving the artery, the blood enters an arteriole before reaching the capillaries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • Degree Lower: capillary - the smallest blood vessel connecting arterioles and venules

Etymology

From French 'artériole', from Latin 'arteriola', diminutive of 'arteria' (artery).
