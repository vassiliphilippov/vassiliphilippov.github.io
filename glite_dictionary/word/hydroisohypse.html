hydroisohypse

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈaɪsoʊˌhɪps / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈaɪsəʊˌhɪps / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points of equal elevation on a piezometric surface, used in hydrology to represent groundwater levels.
contour line for equal groundwater level
medical

Examples

The groundwater flow direction can be interpreted by analyzing the hydroisohypse lines on the map.
Contaminant migration studies often depend on accurately mapping hydroisohypse patterns.
A sudden bend in a hydroisohypse may indicate an underground barrier or change in aquifer properties.

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) and Greek isohypse (equal height).
