pleasantly

1. ADV

/ ˈplɛzəntli / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntli / (BrE)
in a friendly, good-humoured, or polite manner that gives pleasure or enjoyment
in a friendly or good-humoured manner

Examples

She greeted her guests pleasantly and made everyone feel at home.
He pleasantly surprised his coworkers with coffee and pastries on Monday morning.
Despite the criticism, she responded pleasantly and remained composed.

Synset Examples

‘I'll do the dishes,’ he said pleasantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfully - in a way that shows happiness or optimism
  • Antonym: rudely - in an impolite or disrespectful manner

Etymology

From 'pleasant' + '-ly'. 'Pleasant' from Old French plaisant (pleasing).

2. ADV

/ ˈplɛzəntli / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntli / (BrE)
in a way that gives pleasure or enjoyment; agreeably or charmingly
in an enjoyable or agreeable way

Examples

She smiled pleasantly at the waiter as he brought their food.
The weather was pleasantly warm for an afternoon walk.
He was pleasantly surprised by the thoughtful gift.

Synset Examples

we spent a pleasantly lazy afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeably - in a pleasing or enjoyable manner
  • Antonym: disagreeably - in an unpleasant or offensive manner
