to secern

1. VERB

/ sɪˈsɝːn / (AmE), / sɪˈsɜːn / (BrE)
to distinguish or recognize as different; to separate by discernment
distinguish or recognize as different

Examples

It is important to secern genuine artifacts from clever imitations.
The botanist could easily secern the two species based on their leaf structures.
Children quickly secern right from wrong as they mature.

Synset Examples

We distinguish several kinds of maple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distinguish - to recognize or point out a difference
  • Synonym: to differentiate - to recognize or state the difference between things

Etymology

From Latin 'secernere' meaning 'to separate', from 'se-' (apart) + 'cernere' (to sift, distinguish).
