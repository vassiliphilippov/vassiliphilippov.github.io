Pali

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːli / (AmE), / ˈpɑːli / (BrE)
an ancient Middle Indo-Aryan language, derived from Sanskrit, used as the liturgical and scriptural language of Theravada Buddhism
ancient Buddhist liturgical language

Examples

Many Buddhist scriptures were originally written in Pali.
Scholars often study Pali to better understand early Buddhist teachings.
He can recite several Buddhist suttas in Pali.

Etymology

From Sanskrit pāli (row, line, series), referring to a line of canonical texts.
