re

1. NOUN

/ reɪ / (AmE), / reɪ / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the second note of a major scale in solfège (solmization) systems.
second note in major scale (solfège)

Examples

Many choral exercises begin with singing re after do to help establish pitch.
The children sang the scale: do, re, mi, fa, so, la, ti, do.
In tonal music, re typically serves as the supertonic of the major scale.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: do - the first note (tonic) of a major scale in solfège
  • Degree Higher: mi - the third note (mediant) of a major scale in solfège

Etymology

From Italian solfeggio system, using syllables from a medieval hymn.
