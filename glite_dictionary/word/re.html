Re

1. PROPN

/ reɪ / (AmE), / reɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian sun god, often depicted with the head of a hawk, regarded as a universal creator and central deity who merged with Amen to form Amen-Ra.
ancient Egyptian sun god and creator

Examples

Many ancient temples were dedicated to Re as the supreme deity of the Egyptian pantheon.
Priests would greet the rising sun as an act of worship to Re.
The myth of Re includes stories of his daily journey across the sky in a solar barque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ra - alternative spelling of Re, the Egyptian sun god
  • Degree Higher: Amen-Ra - combined deity of Amen and Re in Egyptian mythology

Etymology

From Egyptian *Rꜥ* (Re), the name of the sun god in ancient Egyptian religion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːniəm / (AmE), / ˈriːniəm / (BrE)
a rare, heavy, metallic chemical element (symbol Re), resembling manganese, used in high-temperature alloys and obtained as a by-product when refining molybdenum.
rare metallic chemical element

Examples

The alloy contains a small amount of Re to increase its resistance to heat.
Scientists discovered traces of Re while refining molybdenum ores.
Due to its rarity, Re is one of the most expensive precious metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhenium - the chemical element with symbol Re and atomic number 75

Etymology

From Latin 'Rhenus', the Rhine river, where it was first discovered.
