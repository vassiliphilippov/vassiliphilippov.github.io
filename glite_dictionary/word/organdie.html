organdie

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡəndi / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡəndi / (BrE)
a fine, sheer, stiff cotton fabric, often used for dresses and curtains
sheer, stiff cotton fabric

Examples

The bride chose a delicate organdie gown for her wedding ceremony.
Elegant curtains made of white organdie hung in the living room windows.
For the summer party, she wore a light pink dress of organdie that fluttered in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muslin - a plain-woven cotton fabric

Etymology

From French 'organdi', possibly named after the city of Urganch (Khiva, Uzbekistan), a center of cotton production.
