stateswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is a distinguished and respected leader in national or international affairs, especially one with significant influence or wisdom.
distinguished female leader in government

Examples

Margaret Thatcher was regarded by many as a true stateswoman of her era.
The conference welcomed a renowned stateswoman to discuss global policy challenges.
She hopes to become a stateswoman who inspires future generations of leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statesman - a distinguished male (or generic) leader in national or international affairs

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'statesman', from 'state' + 'woman'.
