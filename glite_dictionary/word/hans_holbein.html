Hans Holbein

1. PROPN

/ hɑnz ˈhoʊl.baɪn / (AmE), / hans ˈhɒl.baɪn / (BrE)
(1497–1543) A German painter and engraver renowned for his detailed portraits, especially those commissioned by Henry VIII of England.
German portrait painter (1497–1543)

Examples

Hans Holbein painted a famous portrait of Henry VIII that still hangs in the National Gallery.
Many art historians regard Hans Holbein as one of the greatest portraitists of the Renaissance.
The intricate details in Hans Holbein's work reveal his extraordinary skill as an artist.
