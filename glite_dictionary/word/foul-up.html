foul-up

1. NOUN

/ faʊl ʌp / (AmE), / faʊl ʌp / (BrE)
an embarrassing or serious mistake, especially due to confusion or incompetence
embarrassing or serious mistake
informal

Examples

There was a major foul-up in the scheduling, which caused the meeting to be canceled.
His foul-up at work led to an awkward conversation with his boss.
Despite the foul-up, the project was completed on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or misguided
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose
