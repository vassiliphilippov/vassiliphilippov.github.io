J. J. Thomson

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪ ˈdʒeɪ ˈtɑmsən / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪ ˈdʒeɪ ˈtɒmsən / (BrE)
(1856–1940) An English physicist who discovered the electron and determined its charge and mass, greatly advancing the understanding of atomic structure.
English physicist, discovered the electron

Examples

J. J. Thomson revolutionized physics with his discovery of the electron in 1897.
The charge-to-mass ratio of the electron was first measured by J. J. Thomson.
Many students learn about J. J. Thomson's experiments in their introductory chemistry classes.
