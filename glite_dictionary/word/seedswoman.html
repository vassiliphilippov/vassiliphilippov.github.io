seedswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsidzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈsiːdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who sells or deals in seeds, especially as a profession
female dealer in seeds

Examples

The well-known seedswoman introduced many rare plants to the market.
She became a successful seedswoman after inheriting her family's business.
Historically, a seedswoman played a crucial role in local agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seedsman - a person (especially a man) who deals in seeds

Etymology

Formed from 'seed' + 'woman', paralleling 'seedsman'.
