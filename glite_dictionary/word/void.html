void

1. VERB

/ vɔɪd / (AmE), / vɔɪd / (BrE)
(medical or formal) To excrete or discharge bodily waste, especially urine or feces, from the body.
discharge bodily waste
medical

Examples

The patient was asked to void before the procedure began.
Certain medications can make it difficult to void urine normally.
Infants are unable to void consciously until they develop bladder control.

Etymology

From Latin 'vocidus', past participle of 'vocare' (to call away; to empty, nullify), via Old French and Middle English.
