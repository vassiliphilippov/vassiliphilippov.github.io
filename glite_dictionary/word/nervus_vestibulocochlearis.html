nervus vestibulocochlearis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɜrvəs vɛˌstɪbjuloʊkɑːˈkliərɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɜːvəs vɛˌstɪbjʊˌləʊkɒkˈliːərɪs / (BrE)
the eighth cranial nerve; a composite sensory nerve that supplies the hair cells of the vestibular organ (for balance) and the cochlea (for hearing)
the eighth cranial nerve for hearing and balance
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervus vestibulocochlearis can result in both hearing loss and problems with balance.
During anatomy class, we learned that the nervus vestibulocochlearis carries signals from the ear to the brain.
An MRI was ordered to check for tumors affecting the nervus vestibulocochlearis in patients with sudden hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestibulocochlear nerve - the eighth cranial nerve, responsible for hearing and balance

Etymology

From Latin: 'nervus' (nerve), 'vestibulum' (entrance hall, referring to vestibular system), 'cochlea' (snail shell, the inner ear structure).
