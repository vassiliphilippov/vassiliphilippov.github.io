to regularize

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to rules, standards, or laws; to bring order or uniformity to something, often by imposing regulations
to bring into conformity with rules

Examples

The government introduced new policies to regularize the recruitment process.
Several banks were required to regularize their accounting procedures after an audit.
Efforts to regularize informal settlements have sparked debates about urban planning.

Synset Examples

We cannot regulate the way people dress
This town likes to regulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to regulate - to control or maintain something by rules or regulations

Etymology

From Medieval Latin regularizare, from Latin regula (rule).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make regular or bring into conformity with a rule, standard, or pattern
make regular or orderly

Examples

The company decided to regularize its hiring process to ensure fairness.
Doctors often use medication to regularize an abnormal heartbeat.
The government is working to regularize the status of undocumented migrants.

Synset Examples

regularize the heart beat with a pace maker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - make things conform to a standard
  • Synonym: to systematize - arrange according to a system

Etymology

From Medieval Latin regularizare, from Latin regularis (regular).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00276406-v
Synset POS: v
