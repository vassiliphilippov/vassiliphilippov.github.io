Tulu

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuːluː / (AmE), / ˈtuːluː / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken mainly in the southwestern part of Karnataka and northern Kerala, India
a Dravidian language of southwestern India

Examples

She is fluent in both Kannada and Tulu.
The Tulu language has a rich tradition of oral literature.
He learned Tulu to communicate more easily with the locals in Mangalore.

Etymology

From local endonym in southern India; related to the people (Tuluva) and region (Tulu Nadu).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːluː / (AmE), / ˈtuːluː / (BrE)
a member of a Dravidian people native to the southwestern coast of India, primarily in Karnataka and Kerala
member of a Dravidian ethnic group of India

Examples

Many Tulu have preserved their language and culture for centuries.
The coastal towns are home to a significant Tulu population.
She met a Tulu artist during her travels in Karnataka.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dravidian - a member of a group of peoples in southern India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Kannada ತುಳು (Tulu), referring to the Tulu people and their language.
