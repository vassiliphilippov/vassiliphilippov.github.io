Mattole

1. NOUN

/ məˈtoʊl / (AmE), / məˈtəʊl / (BrE)
the Athabaskan language once spoken by the Mattole people of northern California.
Athabaskan language of the Mattole people

Examples

Linguists have preserved recordings of the Mattole language for future generations.
Few people today are fluent in Mattole, as the language is considered endangered.
The Mattole language has unique features that distinguish it from other Athabaskan tongues.

Etymology

From the name of the Mattole people of northern California.

2. NOUN

/ məˈtoʊl / (AmE), / məˈtəʊl / (BrE)
A member of an Athapaskan-speaking Indigenous people living in northwestern California.
Indigenous people of northwestern California

Examples

The Mattole have a rich tradition of storytelling and basket weaving.
Some descendants of the Mattole still live in their ancestral homelands today.
Researchers have worked to preserve the language and culture of the Mattole.
