sesquipedality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛskwəpɪˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˌsɛskwiːpɪˈdalɪti / (BrE)
the use of long words or the tendency to use long words in speech or writing
the use of long words

Examples

His sesquipedality in the essay made it difficult for most readers to understand his point.
Some critics accuse legal documents of unnecessary sesquipedality.
The professor’s sesquipedality amused the students but confused visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesquipedalian - a person who uses long words; characterized by the use of long words

Etymology

From Latin 'sesquipedalis' (a foot and a half long), also seen in 'sesquipedalian'.
