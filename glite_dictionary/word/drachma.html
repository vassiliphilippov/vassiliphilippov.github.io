drachma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrækmə / (AmE), / ˈdrakmə / (BrE)
a unit of apothecary weight equal to one eighth of an ounce or to 60 grains
apothecary weight (1/8 ounce or 60 grains)
archaic

Examples

In old medical recipes, a drachma of powder would often be prescribed for daily use.
The pharmacist carefully measured out a drachma of the compound.
Each drachma in the apothecary system corresponds to about 3.89 grams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dram - another name for the same unit of apothecary weight

Etymology

From Latin 'drachma', from Ancient Greek 'drakhmē' (a handful, a coin, a weight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrækmə / (AmE), / ˈdrækmə / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Greece before the adoption of the euro, used in ancient and modern times
former Greek unit of currency

Examples

Before joining the eurozone, Greece used the drachma as its official currency.
A rare collection of ancient Greek drachmas was sold at the auction.
Many people remember prices in drachmas even years after the currency was replaced.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: euro - the official currency of most European Union countries, including Greece after 2002

Etymology

From Ancient Greek δραχμή (drakhmē), meaning 'a handful' or 'a drachma coin'.
