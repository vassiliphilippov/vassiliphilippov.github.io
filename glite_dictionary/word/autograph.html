autograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.təˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a signature written by a person, especially an admired person, as a keepsake or memento
a person's own signature

Examples

The fan waited patiently for the singer's autograph after the concert.
He proudly displayed the baseball with the player's autograph on his shelf.
Could you please give me your autograph before you leave?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written in their own handwriting

Etymology

From Greek 'autographos', from 'auto-' (self) + 'graphos' (written).

2. VERB

/ ˈɔtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to write one's signature on something, especially as a memento for someone
sign one's name, often as a keepsake

Examples

The famous athlete autographed a jersey for the young fan.
She waited in line for an hour to have her book autographed by the author.
Would you mind autographing this photo for my collection?

Synset Examples

The author autographed his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document or item as identification or approval

Etymology

From Greek 'autographos', from 'autos' (self) + 'graphein' (to write).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
something written by a person, especially a signature, in their own handwriting; often used to mean a person's signature given to a fan or admirer.
a person's handwritten signature or note

Examples

She was thrilled when her favorite author gave her his autograph after the reading.
The baseball player patiently signed autographs for his fans outside the stadium.
I found an old book with Charles Dickens' autograph on the first page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written in their own handwriting

Etymology

From Greek 'autographos', from 'auto-' (self) + 'graphein' (to write).
