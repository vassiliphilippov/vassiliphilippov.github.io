Lilium auratum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm ɔːˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm ɔːˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to Japan, characterized by large white flowers with golden bands or spots; also known as the Japanese lily.
species of Japanese lily with golden bands

Examples

The garden was filled with the fragrance of Lilium auratum in full bloom.
She specializes in cultivating rare lilies, especially Lilium auratum.
We admired the stunning golden stripes on the petals of the Lilium auratum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium with large, prominent flowers

Etymology

From Latin: lilium (lily) + auratum (gilded, golden).
