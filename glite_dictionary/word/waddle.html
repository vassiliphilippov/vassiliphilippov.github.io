waddle

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒdəl / (BrE)
to walk with short steps and a clumsy, swaying motion, like that of a duck
walk with short, swaying steps

Examples

The ducklings waddle after their mother along the riverbank.
After spraining his ankle, he could only waddle across the room.
She waddled around the house in her oversized boots.

Synset Examples

small children toddle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to toddle - to walk with short, unsteady steps, especially as a small child does
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the movement associated with waddling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01922796-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑd.əl / (AmE), / ˈwɒd.əl / (BrE)
a way of walking with short steps and a clumsy swaying motion from side to side, like that of a duck
a clumsy, swaying walk

Examples

The penguin's distinctive waddle charmed all the visitors at the zoo.
He crossed the yard with a slow waddle, resembling a duck on land.
Her playful waddle down the hallway made the children giggle.

Synset Examples

ducks walk with a waddle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall

Etymology

From Middle English 'waden' (to go, wade); perhaps imitative of the movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00291250-n
Synset POS: n
