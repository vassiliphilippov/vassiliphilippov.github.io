bouncer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnsər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsə / (BrE)
a person employed to remove troublemakers or control crowds at a bar, nightclub, or public event
person who removes troublemakers from venues

Examples

The bouncer asked the group to leave after they started a fight near the bar.
She works as a bouncer at one of the busiest nightclubs in town.
When trouble broke out, the bouncer quickly intervened to restore order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorman - a person who controls entry at the door of a building, especially a nightclub
  • Synonym: security guard - a person employed to protect a building or event and keep order
