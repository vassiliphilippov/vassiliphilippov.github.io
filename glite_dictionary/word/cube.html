cube

1. NOUN

/ kjuːb / (AmE), / kjuːb / (BrE)
a solid figure with six equal square faces, all angles right angles
solid figure with six square faces

Examples

The mathematician drew a cube on the board to demonstrate volume.
Each side of the cube measures five centimeters.
They stacked one wooden cube on top of another to build a tower.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cuboid - a solid shape with six rectangular faces
  • Degree Lower: square - a plane figure with four equal straight sides and four right angles

Etymology

From Latin 'cubus', from Ancient Greek 'kybos' (a six-sided die, a cube).

4. VERB

/ kjuːb / (AmE), / kjuːb / (BrE)
to raise a number or quantity to the third power by multiplying it by itself twice
raise to the third power (math)

Examples

To find the volume of a cube, first cube the length of one side.
He asked the students to cube the number four as part of the exercise.
If you cube ten, the answer is one thousand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: square - raise to the second power (math)

Etymology

From Latin 'cubus', via Old French 'cube', originally meaning a six-faced solid, later applied to mathematics.

7. NOUN

/ kjub / (AmE), / kjuːb / (BrE)
A solid object with six equal square faces, all angles right angles; often used for a block in this shape.
solid with six equal square faces

Examples

They stacked one cube on top of another to build a tower.
The ice melted quickly as each cube was dropped into the glass.
A cube has six identical square faces and twelve edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - a solid piece of hard material with flat surfaces on each side
  • Antonym: sphere - a perfectly round three-dimensional object, different in shape from a cube

Etymology

From Latin 'cubus', from Ancient Greek 'kybos' (cube, die).
