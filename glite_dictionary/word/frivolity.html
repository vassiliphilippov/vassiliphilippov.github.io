frivolity

1. NOUN

/ frɪˈvɑːlɪti / (AmE), / frɪˈvɒlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not serious, sensible, or having little value; silliness or lightheartedness
lack of seriousness; lightheartedness

Examples

There was no room for frivolity during the important meeting.
Her sense of frivolity made her popular at parties.
Sometimes a little frivolity can brighten up a stressful day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality or state of being serious

Etymology

From Latin 'frivolus' (silly, worthless) via French 'frivolité'.

2. NOUN

/ frɪˈvɑlɪti / (AmE), / frɪˈvɒlɪti / (BrE)
something that is not serious or has little value or importance; silliness or trivial behavior or things
trivial or silly quality or act

Examples

She was criticized for her frivolity during such a serious meeting.
The party was full of laughter and frivolity, a welcome escape from stress.
He regretted spending so much time on frivolity instead of meaningful work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or important
  • Synonym: triviality - something that is of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'frivolus' (worthless, trifling) via French 'frivolité'.

3. NOUN

/ frɪˈvɑl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / frɪˈvɒl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
silly or light-hearted behavior lacking seriousness; the quality of being frivolous.
silly or light-hearted behavior

Examples

His constant frivolity during meetings made it hard for the team to focus on important issues.
The party was full of laughter and frivolity, much to everyone's delight.
Despite the frivolity of her jokes, she was deeply thoughtful at heart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality or state of being serious, not frivolous
  • Synonym: levity - lightness of mind or behavior; lack of seriousness

Etymology

From Latin 'frivolus' (worthless, trivial) via Late Latin 'frivolitas'.
