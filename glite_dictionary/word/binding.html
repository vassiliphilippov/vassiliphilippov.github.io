binding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
a strip of material sewn over or along the edge of something, such as fabric or paper, for reinforcement or decoration
strip sewn on for reinforcement or decoration

Examples

She stitched a colorful binding around the edge of the quilt.
The binding on the book's spine was starting to fray.
This dress features a contrasting binding along the hem for extra detail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of wrapping a bandage around a wound or injury
the act of bandaging a wound
medical

Examples

The nurse performed a careful binding of the patient's wounded leg.
Proper binding can help stop the bleeding until medical help arrives.
Before applying the binding, she cleaned the area to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandaging - the application of a bandage to a wound or injury

Etymology

From bind + -ing; 'bind' from Old English 'bindan' (to tie, fasten).
