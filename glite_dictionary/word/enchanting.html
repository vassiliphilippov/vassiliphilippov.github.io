enchanting

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtʃæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntɪŋ / (BrE)
captivating or charming in a way that seems magical
captivating or magical

Examples

The enchanting melody lingered in my mind long after the concert ended.
She wore an enchanting dress that caught everyone's attention at the party.
The garden had an enchanting quality, especially in the morning light.

Synset Examples

bewitching smile
Roosevelt was a captivating speaker
enchanting music
an enthralling book
antique papers of entrancing design
a fascinating woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewitching - captivating or enchanting as if by magic
  • Synonym: captivating - attracting and holding interest; charming
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Old French *enchanter*, from Latin *incantare* (to chant a magic spell).
