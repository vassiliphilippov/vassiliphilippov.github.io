to prance

1. VERB

/ præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
to walk or move with a lively, proud, or exaggeratedly confident manner, often to attract attention or impress others
walk or move with lively, showy steps

Examples

The model began to prance down the runway, captivating the audience with her confident steps.
He couldn't help but prance around the office after getting his promotion.
Children pranced happily through the puddles after the rain stopped.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait

Etymology

From Middle English prauncen, of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative or related to 15th-century French prancer (to strut).

2. VERB

/ præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
(of a horse) to move with high, springy steps, often lifting the forelegs as if showing off; or (of a person) to walk or move with a lively, proud, or showy manner.
move with springy, high steps (horse or person)

Examples

The young colt loves to prance around the paddock every morning.
She pranced onto the stage, full of energy and confidence.
The children pranced in their costumes during the parade.

Synset Examples

The young horse was prancing in the meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *prauncen*, of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01963040-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
to cause (a horse) to bound or move forward in a lively or springy manner, often with a proud or spirited gait
cause a horse to move in a lively way

Examples

The trainer skillfully made the horse prance across the parade ground.
He taught his steed to prance on command, delighting the audience.
With a gentle tug on the reins, she urged the horse to prance forward.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English prancen, related to Middle Dutch prantsen (to strut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01962881-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
(of a horse or its rider) to move forward with high, springy steps in a lively or spirited manner.
move forward with springy steps (esp. of horses)

Examples

The stallion began to prance as the parade passed by, drawing everyone's attention.
As she pranced her horse around the ring, the crowd cheered her on.
Young colts often prance excitedly when let out into the field.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: amble - to move at a relaxed, easy pace (of a horse)
  • Degree Higher: gallop - to move very fast, especially of a horse running

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *prauncen* (to strut, boast).

Source: WordNet
