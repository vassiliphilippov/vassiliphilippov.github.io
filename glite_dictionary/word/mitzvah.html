mitzvah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪts.və / (AmE), / ˈmɪts.və / (BrE)
A commandment or precept of Jewish law, often regarded as a good deed performed as a religious duty.
Jewish religious commandment or good deed

Examples

Studying the Torah is considered a fundamental mitzvah in Judaism.
Helping the poor is seen as a noble mitzvah by many members of the community.
At her bat mitzvah, she promised to perform a mitzvah every day.

Etymology

From Hebrew מִצְוָה (mitzvah), meaning 'commandment'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪts.və / (AmE), / ˈmɪts.və / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a good deed performed as a religious duty.
a good deed done from religious duty

Examples

Helping the elderly neighbor carry her groceries was considered a mitzvah in their community.
He did a mitzvah by volunteering at the synagogue's food drive last weekend.
Every time you perform a mitzvah, you help make the world a better place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good deed - an act done with good intentions and helpful results
  • Antonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression

Etymology

From Hebrew מִצְוָה (mitzvāh, commandment).
