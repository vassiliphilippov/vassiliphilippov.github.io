lustreless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌstərləs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstələs / (BrE)
Not having shine, brightness, or vitality; lacking brilliance or liveliness.
dull; lacking brightness or vitality

Examples

The once vibrant city became lustreless after years of neglect.
Her hair appeared lustreless and dry after the long winter.
He gave a lustreless performance that failed to impress the audience.

Synset Examples

a dull lackluster life
a lusterless performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness or liveliness
  • Synonym: lackluster - lacking in brilliance or vitality
  • Antonym: shiny - having a bright or glossy surface

Etymology

From 'lustre' (British spelling of 'luster') + '-less', meaning 'without shine'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌstərləs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstələs / (BrE)
not having shine, brightness, or a reflective quality; dull or lacking luster.
dull, lacking shine or brightness

Examples

She felt her once-beautiful hair looked lustreless after the long illness.
The old coins appeared lustreless in the dim light of the attic.
His lustreless eyes stared blankly across the room, betraying his exhaustion.

Synset Examples

staring with lackluster eyes
lusterless hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness or vividness
  • Antonym: shiny - having a smooth, bright, reflective surface

Etymology

From luster (American 'luster', British 'lustre'), from Latin 'lustrare' (to illuminate), with the suffix '-less' (without).
