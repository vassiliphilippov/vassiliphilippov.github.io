aloft

1. ADV

/ əˈlɔft / (AmE), / əˈlɒft / (BrE)
at or on or toward the masthead or upper rigging of a ship; in a position high above the deck.
in the upper parts of a ship

Examples

The sailor climbed aloft to fix the rigging before the storm arrived.
While working aloft, she secured her harness tightly to the mast.
From his vantage point aloft, he could see the entire deck below.

Synset Examples

climbed aloft to unfurl the sail

Etymology

From Middle English, probably from Old Norse 'á lopt' (up in the air).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501282-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ əˈlɔft / (AmE), / əˈlɒft / (BrE)
in or into the air or the sky, especially at a considerable height above the ground; in the higher atmosphere above the earth
at a considerable height in the air

Examples

The pilot reported turbulence aloft during the descent.
Strong winds aloft can impact the flight path of commercial jets.
Meteorologists monitor conditions aloft to predict severe weather.

Synset Examples

weather conditions aloft are fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhead - above one's head; in the sky

Etymology

From Middle English 'alofte', from Old Norse 'á lopt' (up in the air).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500697-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ əˈlɔft / (AmE), / əˈlɒft / (BrE)
upwards; in or into the air or a high place
upward; in the air

Examples

The bird soared aloft on the rising wind.
She held her trophy aloft for everyone to see.
Flames shot aloft as the fireworks exploded.

Synset Examples

the good news sent her spirits aloft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upward - towards a higher place or position
  • Antonym: downward - towards a lower place or position

Etymology

From Old Norse 'á lopt' (in the air).

4. ADV

/ əˈlɔft / (AmE), / əˈlɒft / (BrE)
high up in or into the air; at or to a great height above the ground
high up in the air

Examples

The colorful balloons floated aloft against the blue sky.
A flag was held aloft by the cheering crowd.
The helicopter hovered aloft for several minutes before landing.

Synset Examples

eagles were soaring aloft
dust is whirled aloft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhead - above one's head; in the air above
  • Antonym: below - at a lower level or position

Etymology

From Middle English 'a-loft', from Old Norse 'loft' (air, sky).
