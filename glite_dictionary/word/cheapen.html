to cheapen

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃiː.pən / (AmE), / ˈtʃiː.pən / (BrE)
to make something seem less valuable, important, or respected; to lower the price or quality of something
make seem less valuable or important

Examples

Criticizing their efforts only serves to cheapen their achievements.
The new advertising campaign risks cheapening the brand's image.
Mass production often cheapens the quality of handmade goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devalue - to reduce or underestimate the worth or importance of something
  • Synonym: to depreciate - to diminish in value over a period of time
  • Antonym: to enhance - to increase or improve in value, quality, or attractiveness

Etymology

From 'cheap' (Middle English 'chep', dealing, bargain) plus '-en'.
