foul

1. NOUN

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
an action in a sport that breaks the rules, usually resulting in a penalty
rule violation in sports

Examples

The referee called a foul after the defender tripped the attacker in the box.
He committed his fifth foul and was disqualified from the basketball game.
Fans argued whether the player's handball was an intentional foul or just an accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty - punishment for breaking a rule in sports
