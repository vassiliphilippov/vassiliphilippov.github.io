to piece-dye

1. VERB

/ pis-daɪ / (AmE), / piːs-daɪ / (BrE)
to dye fabric after it has been woven, rather than dyeing the fibers or yarns before weaving
to dye woven fabric after weaving

Examples

The manufacturer decided to piece-dye the cotton fabric to match the season’s color trends.
It's often cheaper to piece-dye garments than to dye the yarns individually.
Many fashion companies prefer to piece-dye so they can respond quickly to changing color demands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to yarn-dye - to dye the yarn before it is woven into fabric

Etymology

Formed from 'piece' and 'dye', referring to dyeing cloth by the piece rather than before weaving.
