Z

1. NOUN

/ zi / (AmE), / zɛd / (BrE)
the last or final part of something; the ending in a series or sequence
the ending of a series or sequence

Examples

In the phrase 'from Z to A,' the speaker meant going backward from end to beginning.
The project is finally at its Z, after months of hard work.
He described himself as the Z of the organization, always last in line for tasks.

Synset Examples

the Alpha and the Omega, the first and the last, the beginning and the end

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Alpha - the beginning in a series; the first

Etymology

From the letter Z, the last letter of the Latin and English alphabets, used symbolically to mean the end.

2. NOUN

/ zi / (AmE), / zɛd / (BrE)
the 26th and last letter of the English alphabet, pronounced 'zee' in American English and 'zed' in British English
the 26th letter of the alphabet

Examples

Children learn to recite the alphabet from A to Z.
In American English, the letter Z is pronounced 'zee'.
My last name starts with a Z.

Synset Examples

the British call Z zed and the Scots call it ezed but Americans call it zee
he doesn't know A from izzard

Etymology

From Greek zēta, from Phoenician zayin.
