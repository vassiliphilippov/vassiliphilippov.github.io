indecency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdisnsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsnsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of being indecent; behavior or speech that is morally or socially offensive or improper.
behavior or quality of being indecent

Examples

There are laws against public indecency in many countries.
The actor was fined for indecency after his controversial performance.
Many people were shocked by the indecency shown on television that night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decency - the quality of being decent
  • Synonym: impropriety - behavior that is not proper or appropriate

Etymology

From Latin indecentia (unbecoming, unseemliness), from indecens (unbecoming).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdiːsənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsənsi / (BrE)
an act or behavior that is considered offensive or improper by accepted standards of morality or decency
an improper or offensive act

Examples

The comedian was fined for an act of indecency during his performance.
Public indecency is considered a misdemeanor in many countries.
She was shocked by the indecency of their comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impropriety - a failure to observe standards of honesty or modesty; improper conduct
  • Antonym: decency - behavior that conforms to accepted standards of morality or respectability

Etymology

From Latin indecentia, from indecens (unbecoming, improper).
