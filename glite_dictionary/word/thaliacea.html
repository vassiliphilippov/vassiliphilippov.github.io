Thaliacea

1. NOUN

/ θæliˈeɪsiə / (AmE), / θalɪˈiːsɪə / (BrE)
A small class of free-swimming, gelatinous tunicates found in marine environments, sometimes treated as an order.
class of free-swimming marine tunicates

Examples

Thaliacea are often found drifting in open ocean currents.
Scientists study Thaliacea to better understand planktonic ecosystems.
Unlike many other tunicates, Thaliacea move freely through the water.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek thalassa (sea).

class of free-swimming marine tunicates
medical

Examples

Scientists collected several Thaliacea specimens during the ocean expedition.
The Thaliacea are important for studying the flow of marine food webs.
Unlike other tunicates, Thaliacea are adapted for free-swimming in open water.

Etymology

From New Latin *Thaliacea*, based on Greek *thalía* (abundance, festivity), alluding to their plentiful appearance in the sea.
