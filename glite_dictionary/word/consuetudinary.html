consuetudinary

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsjʊˈtuːdnˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsjuːˈtjuːdɪnəri / (BrE)
a book or manual that sets out the customary rules or ceremonial practices of a particular group, especially of a monastic order or religious community
manual of customary practices

Examples

The monks referred to the consuetudinary to ensure they followed the correct rituals.
A new consuetudinary was written to reflect changes in the community's daily practices.
The library contained an ancient consuetudinary detailing the order's unique traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customary - based on or established by custom

Etymology

From Medieval Latin consuetudinarius, from Latin consuetudo (custom).
