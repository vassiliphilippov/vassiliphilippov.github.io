vapors

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpɚz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəz / (BrE)
(archaic) A state of depression or low spirits, especially as a temporary condition.
(archaic) state of depression
archaic

Examples

In old novels, ladies would sometimes fall into vapors after receiving distressing news.
The doctor dismissed her complaints as mere vapors, not realizing she was deeply unhappy.
He was in the vapors for days after losing his job, barely speaking to anyone.

Synset Examples

he had a bad case of the blues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blues - a feeling of sadness or depression
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness
  • Antonym: euphoria - a feeling of intense happiness or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'vaporum' (steam), generalized in earlier English to various ailments attributed to bodily vapors.
