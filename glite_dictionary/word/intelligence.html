intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the ability to learn, understand, and apply knowledge; the capacity for reasoning and understanding
mental ability to learn and understand

Examples

Her quick intelligence allowed her to solve the puzzle in minutes.
The new test measures different aspects of intelligence, such as problem-solving and memory.
Many animals demonstrate remarkable intelligence in finding food and adapting to new environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to think quickly and cleverly
  • Antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense

Etymology

From Latin intelligentia (understanding, discernment), from intelligere (to understand).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
secret information, especially about a foreign country or an enemy, gathered by a government or other organization
secret information about an enemy or rival

Examples

The army received critical intelligence about enemy troop movements before launching the operation.
Satellite images provided valuable intelligence for national security agencies.
She worked in intelligence and analyzed intercepted communications for possible threats.

Synset Examples

we sent out planes to gather intelligence on their radar coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the activity of spying or using spies to obtain secret information
  • Antonym: counterintelligence - activities designed to prevent or thwart spying or the gathering of secret information by an enemy

Etymology

From Latin intelligentia (understanding, knowledge), but this sense developed in English from the idea of 'messages' or 'news' conveyed secretly.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the activity of collecting secret information about the plans or activities of other countries or organizations, especially for military or governmental purposes
secret information-gathering activity

Examples

The agency specializes in gathering intelligence on foreign threats.
She worked in military intelligence during her time in the army.
Accurate intelligence is crucial for national security operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying or using spies to obtain secret information
  • Antonym: counterintelligence - activities intended to prevent or thwart spying by an enemy

Etymology

From Latin intelligentia (understanding, knowledge), from intellegere (to understand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00983916-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛl.ə.dʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛl.ɪ.dʒəns / (BrE)
an organization or unit responsible for collecting and analyzing information, especially regarding enemy activities and strategies.
information-gathering unit or agency

Examples

The army's intelligence provided critical information about the enemy's plans.
She works in military intelligence and specializes in data analysis.
After a security breach, the intelligence unit reviewed all classified reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence agency - an organization responsible for spying and gathering confidential information, often for national security
  • Antonym: counterintelligence - activities or departments aiming to prevent spying by enemies

Etymology

From Latin intelligentia (understanding, knowledge), from intelligere (to understand).
