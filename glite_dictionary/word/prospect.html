prospect

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.spɛkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pɛkt / (BrE)
the likelihood or chance that something, especially success, will happen in the future
chance of future success

Examples

The prospect of promotion motivated her to work even harder.
With declining sales, the company's prospects look uncertain.
Graduates are concerned about their job prospects after finishing university.

Synset Examples

his prospects as a writer are excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance - possibility that something may happen
  • Synonym: possibility - something that may occur or be true
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectus' (view, outlook), from 'prospicere' (to look forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14506552-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈprɑːˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pɛkt / (BrE)
to search an area for valuable minerals, oil, or other natural substances
search for minerals or valuable resources

Examples

The company plans to prospect the area for gold deposits next year.
During the 19th century, many people moved west to prospect for silver and other minerals.
They spent months prospecting in remote valleys, hoping to strike it rich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explore - to travel through a place in order to learn about it or search for resources

Etymology

From Latin prospectare (to look out), from pro- (forward) + spectare (to look).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00649724-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN
