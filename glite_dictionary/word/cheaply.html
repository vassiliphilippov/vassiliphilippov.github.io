cheaply

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃiːpli / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːpli / (BrE)
In a way that costs little money; at a low price or with little expense.
inexpensively; at low cost

Examples

Many people try to travel as cheaply as possible during their holidays.
The house was built cheaply, so repairs are now needed.
You can eat cheaply if you avoid fancy restaurants.

Synset Examples

a cheaply dressed woman approached him in the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexpensively - at little cost
  • Antonym: expensively - at great cost
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00335934-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈtʃipli / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːpli / (BrE)
In a way that involves little expenditure of money; at a low price.
at a low price

Examples

We managed to travel across Europe very cheaply by using budget airlines and hostels.
She bought most of her furniture cheaply at yard sales last summer.
The repairs were done cheaply, but the quality was still excellent.

Synset Examples

I bought this car very cheaply

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expensively - in a way that costs a lot of money

Etymology

From 'cheap' + suffix '-ly'.
