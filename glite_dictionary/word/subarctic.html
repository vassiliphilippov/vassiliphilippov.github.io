subarctic

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈɑːrktɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the regions just south of the Arctic Circle, typically marked by cold climates and short summers.
relating to areas just south of the Arctic Circle

Examples

The forest is located in a subarctic region with long, harsh winters.
Animals living in subarctic climates must adapt to extreme cold and limited vegetation.
Scientists study climate change by observing trends in subarctic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: arctic - relating to and around the North Pole and the Arctic Circle

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, near) + 'Arcticus' (of the north, Arctic).
