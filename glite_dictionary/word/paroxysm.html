paroxysm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əkˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpær.ək.sɪz.əm / (BrE)
a sudden and uncontrollable attack or outburst of a particular emotion or activity
sudden uncontrollable attack or outburst

Examples

She was seized by a paroxysm of coughing during the meeting.
News of the defeat triggered a paroxysm of anger among the fans.
He burst into a paroxysm of laughter after hearing the joke.

Synset Examples

a paroxysm of giggling
a fit of coughing
convulsions of laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - a sudden attack or burst of activity, emotion, or illness
  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary contraction or outburst

Etymology

From Greek paroxysmos (irritation, exasperation), from para- (beyond) + oxys (sharp, keen).
