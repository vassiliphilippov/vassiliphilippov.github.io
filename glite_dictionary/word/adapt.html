to adapt

1. VERB

/ əˈdæpt / (AmE), / əˈdæpt / (BrE)
to change something so that it is suitable for a new use or situation
make suitable for a new use or purpose

Examples

It can be challenging to adapt ancient traditions for modern audiences.
The movie was adapted from a bestselling novel.
Farmers must adapt their techniques to the changing climate.

Synset Examples

Adapt our native cuisine to the available food resources of the new country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modify - to make partial changes to something
  • Synonym: to adjust - to alter or move something slightly to achieve the desired fit
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state

Etymology

From Latin 'adaptare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'aptare' (fit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00300361-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈdæpt / (AmE), / əˈdæpt / (BrE)
to change your behavior or ideas in order to deal with new or different circumstances
change to suit new conditions

Examples

It can take some time to adapt to living in a new country.
Businesses must adapt quickly to changing market demands.
She found it hard to adapt when her job responsibilities changed.

Synset Examples

We must adjust to the bad economic situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to change something slightly to make it fit or work better
  • Synonym: to conform - to behave according to socially acceptable conventions or standards
  • Antonym: to resist - to oppose or fight against change

Etymology

From Latin adaptāre, from ad- (to) + aptāre (to fit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00150107-v
Synset POS: v
