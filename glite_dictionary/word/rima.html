rima

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːmə / (AmE), / ˈriːmə / (BrE)
A narrow elongated opening or fissure, especially between two symmetrical parts of an organ or structure.
narrow elongated fissure
medical

Examples

The rima glottidis is the opening between the vocal cords in the larynx.
A small foreign body can sometimes become lodged in the rima of an organ.
The surgeon carefully examined the rima to assess any obstruction.

Etymology

From Latin rima (fissure, crack).
