sinewy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnju.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː.i / (BrE)
consisting of or resembling sinews; having a tough, lean, and muscular quality.
tough and muscular; like sinews

Examples

The sinewy arms of the athlete rippled as he lifted the weights.
She admired the sinewy grace with which the dancer moved across the stage.
Thick and sinewy roots pushed up through the hard-packed earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Antonym: flabby - soft and lacking firmness or muscle

Etymology

From Old English *sinu* (sinew), influenced by the suffix -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnju.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ju.i / (BrE)
(of meat) containing many sinews; tough and difficult to chew.
full of sinews; tough meat

Examples

The sinewy steak was difficult to cut and even harder to chew.
She pushed aside the sinewy pieces of meat on her plate, preferring the tender parts.
After hours of simmering, the once sinewy beef became soft and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - difficult to cut or chew (especially of meat)

Etymology

From sinew + -y, dating back to Middle English, with 'sinew' from Old English 'sinu' (tendon, ligament).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02455307-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnju.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː.i / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) having strong muscles and little fat; lean and muscular.
lean and muscular

Examples

She admired the sinewy arms of the rock climber as he reached for the next hold.
The sinewy runner moved swiftly across the rugged terrain, barely breaking a sweat.
His sinewy frame revealed years of dedicated physical labor on the farm.

Synset Examples

a hefty athlete
a muscular boxer
powerful arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles; strong
  • Antonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony

Etymology

From Old English 'sinu' (sinew) + '-y', meaning 'full of sinews'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
