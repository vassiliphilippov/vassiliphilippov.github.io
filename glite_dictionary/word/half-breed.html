half-breed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæfˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbriːd / (BrE)
(of animals) having only one purebred parent; not of pure blood on both sides.
animal with one purebred parent

Examples

The half-breed puppy inherited traits from both its Labrador father and its mixed-breed mother.
At the show, only purebreds were allowed to compete, so the half-breed dogs could not enter.
Farmers sometimes prefer half-breed animals for certain tasks due to their hybrid vigor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbreed - animal or plant resulting from crossing different breeds or varieties
  • Antonym: purebred - animal with parents of the same recognized breed

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'breed'. First recorded in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbriːd / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person whose parents are of different racial groups, especially one of Caucasian and American Indian ancestry.
offensive term for a person of mixed racial ancestry
offensive

Examples

Historically, the term half-breed was often used in a derogatory way in North America.
Many Native Americans found the label half-breed deeply insulting and dehumanizing.
The use of half-breed is now widely recognized as offensive and inappropriate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mulatto - offensive term for a person of mixed white and Black ancestry
  • Synonym: mestizo - person of mixed European and Indigenous American descent
