echelon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛʃ.əˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛʃ.əl.ɒn / (BrE)
a diffraction grating made of plates of equal thickness stacked stepwise, each offset by a constant distance, used to analyze the spectrum of light
stepwise diffraction grating in optics

Examples

The scientist used an echelon to disperse the incoming light into its component wavelengths.
An echelon allows for high-resolution spectroscopic measurements in advanced optical experiments.
The laboratory recently acquired a new echelon grating for their spectrograph.

Etymology

From French 'échelon' (rung of a ladder), originally from 'échelle' (ladder), applied to stepped arrangements.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛʃəˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛʃəlɒn / (BrE)
a formation of troops, ships, or aircraft arranged in parallel lines with each unit stepped to the side of the one in front, typically used in military contexts
troop or ship line formation

Examples

The soldiers moved forward in an echelon to cover more ground across the field.
During the exercise, each tank advanced in echelon for coordinated firing.
The aircraft flew in echelon to maintain visual contact and formation discipline.

Etymology

From French 'échelon' (rung of a ladder, level), from Old French 'eschelon', diminutive of 'eschelle' (ladder), from Latin 'scala'.
