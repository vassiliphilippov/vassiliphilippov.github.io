spandril

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæn.drəl / (AmE), / ˈspæn.drɪl / (BrE)
an approximately triangular area between the curve of two adjoining arches and the straight line above them
triangular area between arches and above

Examples

The decorative tiles filled the spandril above the cathedral's arches.
Each spandril was ornately painted with scenes from mythology.
Restorers carefully cleaned the dust from the carved stone spandril.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spandrel - alternative spelling for 'spandril', same meaning in architecture

Etymology

From Old French *spandrel*, related to 'expand'.
