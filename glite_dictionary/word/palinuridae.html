Palinuridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪ.ləˈnʊr.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌpal.ɪˈnjuː.rɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of marine crustaceans known as spiny lobsters, characterized by a hard shell and long, spiny antennae
family of spiny lobsters

Examples

The Palinuridae family includes several important species for commercial fisheries.
Researchers study the migration patterns of Palinuridae along the Mediterranean coast.
Many members of the Palinuridae exhibit striking colors and long, spiny antennae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell and long antennae

Etymology

From New Latin Palinurus (a genus of spiny lobsters) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpælɪˈnʊrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌpalɪˈnjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine crustaceans commonly known as spiny lobsters, characterized by long antennae and the absence of large claws.
family of spiny lobsters

Examples

The Palinuridae family includes the well-known Caribbean spiny lobster.
Researchers studied several species within Palinuridae to learn more about their migratory behavior.
Commercial fisheries often target members of Palinuridae for their culinary value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a lobster-like crustacean with long antennae and no large claws

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Palinurus.
