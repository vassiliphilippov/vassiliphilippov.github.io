to superscribe

1. VERB

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write or inscribe on the outside or upper part of something, such as an envelope or document
write on the outside or top of something

Examples

Please superscribe your name and address on the envelope before mailing it.
The ancient tablet was superscribed with the king’s decree.
Applicants are instructed to superscribe the post applied for on the top of the application envelope.

Synset Examples

superscribe an envelope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inscribe - to write or engrave words or symbols onto something

Etymology

From Latin superscribere (to write above; super- above + scribere to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695822-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ suː.pɚˈskraɪb / (AmE), / suː.pəˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write or inscribe (words, a name, or an address) on the top or outside of something, especially an envelope or package
write on the top or outside

Examples

Please superscribe your full address in the space provided at the top of the form.
He superscribed the envelope with the recipient's name and address clearly.
All files must be superscribed with the project code before submission.

Synset Examples

superscribe one's name and address

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inscribe - to write or carve words onto something

Etymology

From Latin superscribere, from super- (above) + scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695991-v
Synset POS: v
