crystallization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which crystals are formed from a solution, melt, or gas
process of forming crystals

Examples

The crystallization of salt from seawater is a common example of this process.
During the cooling of magma, crystallization produces various minerals.
The scientist observed the crystallization occurring slowly under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precipitation - the process by which a substance separates from a solution as a solid

Etymology

From French 'cristallisation', based on 'cristal' (crystal), ultimately from Greek 'krustallos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an idea or plan becomes clear, fixed, and definite, as if taking a solid form
making something definite or clear in the mind

Examples

After weeks of discussion, there was finally a crystallization of the project's main objectives.
Her thoughts went through a gradual crystallization until she knew exactly what she wanted to say.
The novel shows the crystallization of his political ideas during those turbulent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formulation - the action of devising or creating something in a definite form
  • Synonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand

Etymology

From French 'cristallisation,' from 'cristalliser' (to crystallize), ultimately from Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal).
