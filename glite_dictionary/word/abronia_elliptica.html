Abronia elliptica

1. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə ɪˈlɪptɪkə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə ɪˈlɪptɪkə / (BrE)
A plant species with heads of fragrant white, trumpet-shaped flowers, native to sandy arid regions.
species of plant with white trumpet flowers

Examples

Abronia elliptica is often found blooming in the deserts of the southwestern United States.
The fragrant white flowers of Abronia elliptica attract many night-flying moths.
Botanists study Abronia elliptica for its adaptation to sandy, arid soils.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Abronia, and Latin elliptica (elliptical), referring to the shape of its leaves.
