indistinguishable

1. ADJ

/ ɪn.dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪ.ʃə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪn.dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪ.ʃə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to tell apart; so similar that no difference can be seen or detected.
impossible to tell apart

Examples

The two paintings were so well forged that they were indistinguishable to the untrained eye.
The twins were dressed alike and looked almost indistinguishable in the group photo.
After mixing the dyes, the colors became indistinguishable from one another.

Synset Examples

rows of identical houses
cars identical except for their license plates
they wore indistinguishable hats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - exactly the same
  • Antonym: distinct - recognizably different

Etymology

From in- (not) + distinguish + -able, meaning 'not able to be distinguished'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be recognized as different or distinct from something else; impossible to tell apart.
impossible to tell apart

Examples

From a distance, the fake painting was indistinguishable from the original masterpiece.
The two flavors were so similar that they were almost indistinguishable to most people.
In the fog, the outlines of the houses became indistinguishable from one another.

Synset Examples

the two specimens are actually different from each other but the differences are almost indistinguishable
the twins were indistinguishable
a colorless person quite indistinguishable from the colorless mass of humanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - exactly the same in every detail
  • Antonym: distinct - clearly different or separate

Etymology

From Latin 'indistinguibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'distinguibilis' (that can be distinguished).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00585227-a
Synset POS: a
