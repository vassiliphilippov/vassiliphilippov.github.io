Mahabharatum

1. PROPN

/ məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtəm / (AmE), / məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtəm / (BrE)
(in Hinduism) the ancient Sanskrit epic poem of India that narrates the struggle between two rival families, the Pandavas and the Kauravas.
ancient Sanskrit epic poem of India

Examples

The Mahabharatum is one of the longest epic poems in the world.
Many philosophical ideas found in the Mahabharatum continue to influence modern Indian society.
He spent years studying Sanskrit to read the Mahabharatum in its original language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahabharata - alternate transliteration of the same epic poem

Etymology

From Sanskrit महाभारतम् (Mahābhāratam), meaning 'the great tale of the Bharata dynasty'.
