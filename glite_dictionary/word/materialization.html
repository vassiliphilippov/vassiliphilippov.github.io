materialization

1. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process by which something, especially a spirit, appears in bodily or tangible form
appearance of something in bodily form

Examples

There are many stories of the materialization of ghosts during séances.
Witnesses claimed that the materialization of the spirit startled everyone in the room.
The materialization of the apparition was accompanied by a sudden drop in temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifestation - an appearance or demonstration of something, often a spirit or supernatural entity

Etymology

From materialize +‎ -ation; based on Latin materia (matter) and Late Latin materialis (of matter).

2. NOUN

/ məˌtɪəriələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming real or actual; the act of something coming into existence.
the process of becoming real or actual

Examples

The sudden materialization of funds made the project possible at last.
We witnessed the materialization of years of hard work in the new library building.
Her persistent efforts led to the eventual materialization of her lifelong dream.

Synset Examples

the materialization of her dream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realization - the act of becoming aware of or making something real

Etymology

From 'materialize' + '-ation'; ultimately from Latin 'materialis' (relating to matter).

3. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that comes into existence as a result of an action, idea, or process; the concrete form of something formerly abstract.
the result or embodiment of something

Examples

The new bridge is the materialization of years of planning and hard work.
His ambition found materialization in the form of a successful company.
The project is a clear materialization of their innovative ideas.

Synset Examples

industrialism prepared the way for acceptance of the French Revolution's various socialistic offspring
this skyscraper is the solid materialization of his efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifestation - an event, action, or object that embodies something abstract
  • Synonym: realization - the act of making something real or tangible

Etymology

From materialize + -ation, modeled after French matérialisation.
