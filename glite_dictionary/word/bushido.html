Bushido

1. NOUN

/ buːˈʃiːdoʊ / (AmE), / buːˈʃiːdəʊ / (BrE)
the traditional code of the Japanese samurai, emphasizing courage, loyalty, self-discipline, and simple living
the samurai code of conduct

Examples

He admired the principles of Bushido and tried to live by them every day.
The film explores the influence of Bushido on modern Japanese culture.
Many martial arts schools teach the values found in Bushido.

Etymology

From Japanese 'bushidō' (way of the warrior), from 'bushi' (warrior) + 'dō' (way, path).
