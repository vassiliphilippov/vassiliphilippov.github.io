oxygenase

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪdʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒəˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the incorporation of molecular oxygen into other molecules during biochemical reactions
enzyme incorporating oxygen in reactions
medical

Examples

Cytochrome P450 is a well-studied oxygenase involved in drug metabolism.
The presence of an oxygenase allows bacteria to break down aromatic compounds aerobically.
Scientists discovered a new oxygenase that is essential for plant growth.

Etymology

From oxygen + -ase (enzyme suffix).
