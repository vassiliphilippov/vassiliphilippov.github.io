Alliaria officinalis

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪˈɛəriə ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / alɪˌaːrɪə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A European herb, commonly known as garlic mustard, with leaves that smell like garlic.
European herb with garlic-like smell

Examples

Alliaria officinalis is commonly found along forest edges and shady pathways across Europe.
Many chefs avoid using Alliaria officinalis because of its strong, garlic-like aroma.
Researchers are studying the invasive potential of Alliaria officinalis in North American woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic mustard - Common name for Alliaria officinalis, a European herb with a garlic smell

Etymology

From Latin 'Alliaria' (referring to garlic-like characteristics) and 'officinalis' (used in medicine).
