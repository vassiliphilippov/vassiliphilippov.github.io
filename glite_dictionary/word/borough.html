borough

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌrə / (BrE)
A town in England that is represented by a member of parliament, or an administrative division with certain self-government rights.
English town represented in parliament
british

Examples

She grew up in a small borough just outside of London.
Each borough elects its own member of parliament.
The historic borough still retains many medieval buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituency - an area whose voters elect a representative to a legislative body

Etymology

From Old English *burh* (fortified town), related to German *Burg* and Dutch *burg*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌr.ə / (BrE)
an administrative division of a large city, often having its own local government
administrative division of a city

Examples

Brooklyn is one of the five boroughs of New York City.
Each borough in London has its own local council.
The city expanded, incorporating several surrounding towns as new boroughs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a town or city, especially for administrative purposes

Etymology

From Old English *burh* (fortress, town), related to German *Burg*.
