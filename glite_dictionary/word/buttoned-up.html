buttoned-up

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌt.ənd ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ənd ˈʌp / (BrE)
very formal or reserved, especially in manner or appearance; conservative, particularly in professional or social situations.
very formal or reserved in manner

Examples

He always dresses in a buttoned-up suit, never a hair out of place.
Her buttoned-up attitude made it difficult for colleagues to get to know her.
The company culture is quite buttoned-up, valuing traditional business etiquette.

Synset Examples

employers are looking for buttoned-up types

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; cautious or restrained in style
  • Antonym: relaxed - not strict, formal, or tense; easygoing

Etymology

From 'button up', referring to the action of fastening all the buttons on clothing, symbolic of formality and reserve.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌt.ənd ʌp / (AmE), / ˌbʌt.ənd ˈʌp / (BrE)
(British, informal) Reserved and not inclined to conversation; emotionally restrained or uncommunicative.
reserved; not inclined to conversation
informal british

Examples

Despite his friendly smile, Mark is quite buttoned-up and seldom shares personal stories.
At the party, Jane was more buttoned-up than usual and hardly spoke to anyone.
Her buttoned-up demeanor makes it difficult for colleagues to know her well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotions or opinions
  • Antonym: outgoing - sociable and willing to talk to people
