doppelganger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːpəlˌɡæŋər / (AmE), / ˈdɒpəlˌɡæŋə / (BrE)
A ghostly double or exact look-alike of a living person, often regarded as an omen or haunting presence.
ghostly double of a living person

Examples

Some legends say seeing your doppelganger is a sign of bad luck.
In the story, the protagonist was haunted by the appearance of his doppelganger.
She believed she saw her doppelganger standing at the end of the hall.

Etymology

From German 'Doppelgänger', literally meaning 'double-goer'.
