fleck

1. NOUN

/ flɛk / (AmE), / flɛk / (BrE)
a very small spot, mark, or fragment, especially one that is detached from a larger whole
a small spot or fragment

Examples

A fleck of dust landed on the white tablecloth.
He rubbed at the fleck of paint on his shirt sleeve.
Tiny flecks of glass were scattered across the floor.

Synset Examples

a bit of rock caught him in the eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a tiny spot or small particle
  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture
  • Synonym: streak - a long, thin mark, typically one made by a different substance or color

Etymology

From Middle English flekk, from Old Norse flekkr (spot, stain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09244958-n

2. VERB

/ flɛk / (AmE), / flɛk / (BrE)
to mark or cover with small spots or patches of color or light
mark with small spots or patches

Examples

Morning sunlight flecked the forest floor with golden patches.
Her hair was flecked with bits of blue paint after the art class.
Dark clouds flecked the horizon as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

The wine spotted the tablecloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckle - to mark with a large number of small spots or marks
  • Synonym: spot - to mark with a spot or spots

Etymology

From Middle English *flekk*, from Old Norse *flekkr* (spot, blot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01534786-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ flɛk / (AmE), / flɛk / (BrE)
a very small patch, spot, or mark that contrasts with the surrounding area, often of a different color or material
small contrasting spot or mark

Examples

She noticed a fleck of blue paint on her shirt after finishing the mural.
Each lily pad was adorned with a single fleck of dew in the morning sunlight.
The old photograph had a fleck of dust that blurred one corner of the image.

Synset Examples

a bald spot
a leopard's spots
a patch of clouds
patches of thin ice
a fleck of red

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a tiny spot or small particle
  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture
  • Degree Higher: streak - a long, thin line or mark of a different substance or color

Etymology

From Middle English *flecke* (spot, blemish), related to Old Norse *flekkr* (spot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04689819-n
Synset POS: n
