Lipo-Hepin

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪpoʊˈhɛpɪn / (AmE), / ˌlaɪpəʊˈhɛpɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for a form of heparin, an anticoagulant drug used to prevent blood clots during surgery and in the treatment of thrombosis.
anticoagulant drug (trade name for heparin)
medical

Examples

The patient received an injection of Lipo-Hepin before undergoing heart surgery.
Doctors often prescribe Lipo-Hepin to prevent blood clots in high-risk patients.
As a form of heparin, Lipo-Hepin is widely used in hospitals for anticoagulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heparin - an anticoagulant drug used to prevent blood clotting
