Neotoma fuscipes

1. NOUN

/ niˈɑːtəˌmə ˈfʌsɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / niˈɒtəˌmə ˈfʌsɪˌpiːz / (BrE)
A species of woodrat native to western North America, commonly known as the dusky-footed woodrat.
species of woodrat native to western North America

Examples

Researchers studied the nesting habits of the Neotoma fuscipes in mixed oak woodlands.
The Neotoma fuscipes builds large, complex dens from sticks and leaves.
In northern California, the Neotoma fuscipes serves as a host to the Lyme disease tick.

Etymology

From modern Latin Neotoma (genus name, from Greek ‘neo-’ new + ‘toma’ cut, referencing tooth morphology) + fuscipes (Latin for 'dark-footed').
