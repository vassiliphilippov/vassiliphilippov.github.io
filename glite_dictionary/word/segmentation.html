segmentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.mɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(embryology) the repeated division of a fertilized ovum into smaller cells during early development.
division of fertilized ovum in embryology
medical

Examples

During early embryonic development, segmentation leads to the formation of multiple identical cells.
The study focused on how errors in segmentation can result in developmental abnormalities.
In many animals, segmentation begins shortly after fertilization and is essential for proper growth.

Etymology

From Latin segmentum (a piece cut off), from secare (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.mɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into separate parts or sections
division into parts or sections

Examples

The segmentation of the market allows companies to better target their customers.
Cell segmentation is a crucial step in analyzing microscope images.
Geographic segmentation enables businesses to tailor their services to different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the action of separating something into parts
  • Antonym: integration - the action of combining or unifying parts into a whole

Etymology

From Latin segmentum (a piece cut off), from secare (to cut).
