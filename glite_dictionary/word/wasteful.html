wasteful

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪstfəl / (BrE)
using something in a careless way so that it is wasted; tending to squander resources or opportunities.
using resources carelessly

Examples

Leaving the lights on all day is extremely wasteful.
The company's wasteful spending led to financial problems.
Try to avoid wasteful use of paper in the office.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thrifty - using resources carefully and avoiding waste
  • Synonym: extravagant - spending or using more than is necessary

Etymology

From the noun 'waste' + suffix '-ful', first recorded in Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪst.fəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪst.fəl / (BrE)
Using more of something, such as time, effort, or materials, than is necessary; not efficient or economical.
inefficient; using resources excessively

Examples

The company's wasteful spending led to a significant financial loss.
It is wasteful to leave the lights on when no one is in the room.
She criticized the project as both expensive and wasteful.

Synset Examples

a clumsy and wasteful process
wasteful duplication of effort
uneconomical ebb and flow of power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efficient - using resources well and without waste
  • Antonym: thrifty - careful not to waste resources
  • Synonym: extravagant - spending or using resources more than necessary

Etymology

From 'waste' + '-ful', Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844698-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪstfʊl / (BrE)
(archaic) Causing widespread destruction or devastation; laying waste.
causing destruction; laying waste (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The wasteful armies left nothing but ruins in their path.
Years of wasteful raids reduced the ancient city to rubble.
Poetry often speaks of wasteful storms destroying harvests and homes.

Synset Examples

when wasteful war shall statues overturn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable damage

Etymology

From Middle English wasteful, from waste + -ful.
