to diabolise

1. VERB

/ daɪˈɑːbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / daɪˈɒbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to turn into a devil or make devilish in character; to portray as evil or demon-like
make devilish or evil

Examples

The novel's villain is thoroughly diabolised by the author's vivid descriptions.
She accused the media of diabolising her actions during the trial.
War can diabolise even the most compassionate individuals over time.

Synset Examples

Man devilized by war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demonize - to portray as wicked or threatening

Etymology

From French 'diaboliser', from Late Latin 'diabolus' (devil).
