frolicsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑlɪksəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪksəmnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being lively, playful, and full of high-spirited fun.
lively, high-spirited playfulness

Examples

The frolicsomeness of the children at recess was infectious to watch.
She brought a sense of frolicsomeness to every party she attended.
Even the old dog displayed surprising frolicsomeness on the first day of spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playfulness - the quality of being light-hearted and full of fun
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being serious, solemn, or not playful

Etymology

From 'frolicsome' (playful, lively) plus the suffix '-ness', forming a noun.
