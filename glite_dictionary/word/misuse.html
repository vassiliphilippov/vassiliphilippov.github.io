misuse

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈjus / (AmE), / mɪsˈjuːz / (BrE)
the act of using something in an improper or excessive way
improper or excessive use

Examples

His misuse of company resources led to a formal warning from his supervisor.
The misuse of prescription drugs can have serious health consequences.
Concerns were raised about the misuse of taxpayer funds in the project.

Synset Examples

alcohol abuse
the abuse of public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - improper or wrongful use, especially in a way that is harmful
  • Antonym: proper use - using something in the correct or intended way

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly, badly) + use, patterned on Old French mesuser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00949535-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈjus / (AmE), / mɪsˈjuːz / (BrE)
to use something in the wrong way or for a purpose for which it was not intended
use something wrongly or improperly

Examples

Employees who misuse company property may face disciplinary action.
She accused him of having misused his authority in the decision.
Be careful not to misuse this medicine, or it could cause harmful side effects.

Synset Examples

Don't abuse the system
The director of the factory misused the funds intended for the health care of his workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use something for a bad purpose or in a harmful way

Etymology

From Old French 'mesuser', from 'mes-' (badly) + 'user' (to use).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00203575-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɪsˈjus / (AmE), / mɪsˈjuːz / (BrE)
to use something in a way that is wrong, or for the wrong purpose; to apply or treat wrongly or incorrectly
use wrongly or incorrectly

Examples

He was accused of misusing company funds for personal expenses.
If you misuse this medication, it could have serious side effects.
Teachers worry that some students misuse digital resources by plagiarizing material.

Synset Examples

The words are misapplied in this context
You are misapplying the name of this religious group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - use (something) to bad effect or for a bad purpose; misuse
  • Antonym: use - to put into service; employ

Etymology

From Middle English, from mis- (wrongly) + use (from Old French user, from Latin uti).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01163606-v
Synset POS: v
