Tremella reticulata

1. NOUN

/ trəˈmɛl.ə rɛˌtɪk.jəˈleɪ.t̬ə / (AmE), / trɛˈmɛl.ə rɛˌtɪk.jʊˈlɑː.tə / (BrE)
a species of jelly fungus with an upright whitish fruiting body and variable shape, sometimes resembling coral fungi
a whitish jelly fungus species

Examples

The delicate structure of Tremella reticulata makes it a fascinating subject for mycologists.
After the rain, we spotted Tremella reticulata growing on decaying logs in the forest.
Photographs of Tremella reticulata often highlight its coral-like appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: Tremella (genus name meaning 'trembling') and reticulata (networked, from 'reticulum' meaning 'net').
