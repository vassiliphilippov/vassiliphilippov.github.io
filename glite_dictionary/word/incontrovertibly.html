incontrovertibly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnˌkɑntrəˈvɝtəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɒntrəˈvɜːtəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be denied or disputed; undeniably or unquestionably.
undeniably; unquestionably

Examples

The evidence presented was incontrovertibly in support of the defendant's innocence.
The climate is incontrovertibly changing, according to scientific research.
Her findings have been incontrovertibly proven through multiple independent studies.

Synset Examples

his documentary sources are demonstrably wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputably - in a way that cannot be challenged or denied
  • Antonym: controvertibly - in a way that can be denied or disputed

Etymology

From 'incontrovertible' (not able to be denied) + '-ly'; from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'controversus' (disputed).
