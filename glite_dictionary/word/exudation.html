exudation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡzjʊˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksjʊˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A substance that oozes out from plant pores or surfaces, often as a result of injury or natural secretion.
substance oozed from a plant

Examples

The pine tree's bark was covered in a sticky exudation after it was damaged.
Some plants produce a sweet exudation that attracts insects for pollination.
Under the microscope, you can see the exudation forming on the leaf surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretion - a substance produced and released by a cell, gland, or organism

Etymology

From Latin exsudare (to sweat out or ooze out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡ.zəˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛks.juˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of slowly discharging liquid, especially from blood vessels, pores, or breaks in cell membranes
the slow escape of fluid (scientific/medical)
medical

Examples

The exudation of fluid from the wound indicated inflammation.
Careful observation revealed a slow exudation of sap from the damaged tree branch.
In cases of infection, excessive exudation may require medical intervention to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretion - the process of discharging substances from cells or glands
  • Antonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up a substance

Etymology

From Latin exsudatio (a sweating out), from exsudare 'to sweat out'.
