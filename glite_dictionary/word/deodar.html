deodar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.oʊˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈdiː.əˌdɑː / (BrE)
A tall cedar tree native to the Himalayas, known for its spreading branches with drooping tips and highly valued for both its appearance and durable timber.
tall Himalayan cedar tree

Examples

The park is known for its ancient deodar trees lining the mountain paths.
Craftsmen prefer deodar wood for building because of its durability and fragrance.
A single majestic deodar stands in the center of the temple courtyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - a type of evergreen coniferous tree with fragrant wood

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'devadāru' meaning 'timber of the gods'.
