zinnwaldite

1. NOUN

/ zɪnˈwɑlˌdaɪt / (AmE), / zɪnˈwɔːlˌdaɪt / (BrE)
a brown or dark mica mineral containing iron and lithium, found in granite and similar rocks
a mica mineral with iron and lithium

Examples

The geologist identified the shiny brown mineral as zinnwaldite during the field survey.
Samples of granite from that region often contain zinnwaldite alongside quartz and feldspar.
Lithium extracted from zinnwaldite is important for various industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of silicate minerals that split into thin, flexible sheets

Etymology

From Zinnwald (now Cínovec), a mining district in the Czech Republic, where it was first identified, + -ite.
