tabor

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.bə / (BrE)
a small drum with one head, traditionally played with a stick, often used in folk or medieval music
small, single-headed drum

Examples

The musician played a tabor and pipe as people danced around the maypole.
In medieval times, the tabor was a common instrument for announcing the arrival of important guests.
The rhythm of the tabor echoed through the crowded village square.

Etymology

Middle English from Old French *tabour*, probably from Arabic *ṭubūr* (pl. of *ṭabl*, drum).
