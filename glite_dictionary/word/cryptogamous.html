cryptogamous

1. ADJ

/ krɪpˈtɑɡəməs / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒɡəməs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of cryptogams (plants that reproduce by spores without flowers or seeds, such as ferns, mosses, and algae)
relating to cryptogams (spore-producing plants)
medical

Examples

Some ferns and mosses are classified as cryptogamous plants because they do not produce flowers or seeds.
The forest floor was covered with various cryptogamous species, including liverworts and lichens.
The botanist specialized in the study of cryptogamous organisms found in humid environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phanerogamous - relating to phanerogams (seed-producing plants)

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + gamos (marriage), via New Latin cryptogamus.
