Sarcodes sanguinea

1. NOUN

/ sɑːrˈkoʊdiːz sænˈɡwɪniə / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊdiːz sæŋˈɡwɪniə / (BrE)
A fleshy, bright red, saprophytic plant native to the mountains of western North America, typically appearing in early spring while snow is still on the ground.
bright red saprophytic mountain plant of western North America

Examples

The bright red stems of Sarcodes sanguinea are often visible poking through the snow in early spring.
Hikers in the Sierra Nevada may spot Sarcodes sanguinea growing beneath the pines.
Unlike most plants, Sarcodes sanguinea does not rely on photosynthesis, as it is saprophytic.

Etymology

From Greek sarx (flesh) + odes (like); Latin sanguinea (bloody, blood-red), referring to its color.
