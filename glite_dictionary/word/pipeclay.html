pipeclay

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpˌkleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪp.kleɪ / (BrE)
a fine white clay used in making tobacco pipes, pottery, and for whitening leather
fine white clay for pipes, pottery, and leather

Examples

The craftsman shaped the pipeclay into delicate tobacco pipes.
She used pipeclay to whiten her leather boots before the parade.
Archaeologists discovered fragments of pipeclay pipes at the ancient site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kaolin - a fine, pure white clay used in ceramics and as a filler
