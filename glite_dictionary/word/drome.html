drome

1. NOUN

/ droʊm / (AmE), / drəʊm / (BrE)
an airfield with a control tower, hangars, and facilities for passengers and cargo
airfield with control tower and hangars

Examples

The abandoned drome was once a hub of local air travel.
During the war, supplies were delivered daily to the front-line drome.
Pilots rested in modest accommodations at the small coastal drome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerodrome - an airfield or airport, especially outside North America

Etymology

Shortened from 'aerodrome', from Greek *dromos* (racecourse, running).
