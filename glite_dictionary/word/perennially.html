perennially

1. ADV

/ pəˈrɛniəli / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəli / (BrE)
In a way that continues or repeats regularly; enduringly or constantly.
continually; enduringly

Examples

The town is perennially popular with tourists because of its charming streets.
She is perennially optimistic despite the challenges she faces.
Mountains in this region are perennially covered with snow.

Synset Examples

We want to know what is perennially new about the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continually - repeatedly over time
  • Antonym: occasionally - from time to time; irregularly

Etymology

From 'perennial' (from Latin perennis ‘lasting through the year’), with adverbial suffix -ly.
