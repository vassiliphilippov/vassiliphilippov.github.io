Costanoan

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑstəˈnoʊən / (AmE), / ˌkɒstəˈnəʊən / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people historically inhabiting the coastal region of California between Monterey and San Francisco Bay
Native American people of coastal California

Examples

The Costanoan people developed complex societies along the central California coast.
Artifacts belonging to the Costanoan have been discovered in archaeological sites near Monterey.
Many descendants of the Costanoan continue to preserve their language and cultural traditions today.

Etymology

From Spanish 'costeño' (coastal dweller), from 'costa' (coast), with English suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑstəˈnoʊən / (AmE), / ˌkɒstəˈnəʊən / (BrE)
a member of a group of related Penutian languages formerly spoken by the Costanoan people of coastal central California.
Native American language of central California

Examples

The last fluent speaker of Costanoan passed away in the twentieth century.
Linguists are working to document the grammar and vocabulary of Costanoan.
Several words in English are derived from Costanoan place-names in California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penutian - a proposed family of Native American languages of western North America

Etymology

From Spanish costeño (coastal) plus -an, referencing the native people along the coast.
