opaline

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.pəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
having an iridescent or milky play of lustrous rainbow colors, like those seen in opal
having iridescent rainbow-like colors

Examples

The vase's opaline surface shimmered with subtle hues of pink and green under the light.
She admired the opaline sheen of the morning clouds, which reminded her of delicate seashells.
The artist used an opaline glaze to give the pottery a magical, iridescent finish.

Synset Examples

an iridescent oil slick
nacreous (or pearlescent) clouds looking like mother-of-pearl
a milky opalescent (or opaline) luster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles
  • Synonym: pearly - resembling pearl in luster or color
  • Antonym: matte - having a dull or non-glossy surface (opposite in luster)

Etymology

From French 'opalin', from 'opale' meaning 'opal'.
