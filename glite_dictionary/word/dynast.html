dynast

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌnæst / (AmE), / ˈdɪnæst / (BrE)
a person who rules or holds power within a hereditary line, especially within a dynasty
hereditary ruler

Examples

The young dynast inherited the throne after the passing of her father.
Throughout history, each dynast of the royal house expanded the kingdom's territory.
As a dynast, he was expected to continue his family's legacy of governance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch - a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor
  • Antonym: usurper - a person who takes a position of power or importance illegally or by force

Etymology

From Greek 'dunastēs' (ruler, lord), from 'dunasthai' (to be able, to have power).
