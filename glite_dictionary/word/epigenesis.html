epigenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
a geological change in the mineral content of rock occurring after the rock has formed
post-formation change in rock minerals

Examples

The epigenesis of certain ore deposits can significantly alter their economic value.
Geologists studied the epigenesis of the limestone to understand its unusual mineral composition.
Regional epigenesis has resulted in the replacement of some minerals with new chemical forms.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + genesis (origin, formation), originally applied in biology.
