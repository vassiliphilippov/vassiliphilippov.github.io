to carnify

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑrnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue or an organism) to become muscular or fleshy.
become muscular or fleshy

Examples

Over time, the tissue may carnify as part of the healing process.
With intense training, his once thin arms began to carnify.
Certain diseases can cause organs to carnify unnaturally.

Etymology

From Latin 'caro, carnis' (flesh) + '-ify' (to make or become).
