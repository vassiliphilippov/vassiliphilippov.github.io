please

1. ADV

/ pliːz / (AmE), / pliːz / (BrE)
Used to add politeness to a request, question, or instruction.
used in polite requests

Examples

Please take a seat while you wait for your appointment.
Could you please close the door behind you?
Please remember to turn off the lights when you leave.

Synset Examples

please pay attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindly - used to make a polite request

Etymology

From Middle English 'plese', from Old French 'plaisir', from Latin 'placēre' (to please, satisfy).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. VERB

/ pliz / (AmE), / pliːz / (BrE)
to make someone feel happy or satisfied; to give pleasure to someone
to make someone happy or satisfied

Examples

The new design will please most of our customers.
It pleases me to see you so happy.
We hope these changes please everyone involved.

Synset Examples

These colors please the senses
a pleasing sensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - to give great pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: to displease - to cause someone to feel unhappy or annoyed

Etymology

From Old French 'plaire', from Latin 'placere' (to please, satisfy).

/ pliːz / (AmE), / pliːz / (BrE)

to wish or choose (to do something)

Examples
