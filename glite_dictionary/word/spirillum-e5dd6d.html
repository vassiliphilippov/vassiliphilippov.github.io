spirillum

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrɪləm / (AmE), / spaɪˈrɪləm / (BrE)
a bacterium with a spiral or helical shape, typically found in stagnant water
spiral-shaped bacterium
medical

Examples

Some species of spirillum can be observed in pond water under a microscope.
A spirillum has a distinctive helical shape that differentiates it from other bacteria.
The laboratory cultured samples of spirillum to study their movement.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of Latin 'spira' (coil, spiral).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrɪləm / (AmE), / spɪˈrɪləm / (BrE)
a type of flagellated, aerobic bacteria with a spiral or helical rod-like shape
spiral-shaped, flagellated bacteria
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the scientist observed a distinctive spirillum moving rapidly through the water sample.
Some diseases in humans are caused by the presence of spirillum in contaminated water.
The unique shape of a spirillum helps it swim efficiently using its flagella.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bacillus - rod-shaped bacteria
  • Antonym: coccus - spherical bacteria

Etymology

From Latin 'spirillum', diminutive of 'spira', meaning coil or spiral.
