Menurae

1. NOUN

/ məˈnʊr.iː / (AmE), / mɛˈnjʊə.riː / (BrE)
A taxonomic group comprising lyrebirds and scrubbirds, known for their elaborate tails and vocal abilities.
group of lyrebirds and scrubbirds

Examples

The Menurae are remarkable for their extraordinary mimicry and elaborate plumage.
Researchers studied the natural habitats of the Menurae in the forests of Australia.
Both lyrebirds and scrubbirds belong to the avian group known as Menurae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'menoura' (lyrebird genus name), from 'mēnē' (moon) + 'oura' (tail).
