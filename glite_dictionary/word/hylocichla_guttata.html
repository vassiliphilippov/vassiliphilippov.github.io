Hylocichla guttata

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.ləˈkɪk.lə ɡʌˈteɪ.tə / (AmE), / hɪ.ləˈkɪk.lə ɡʌˈtɑː.tə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the wood thrush, a North American bird noted for its complex and melodious song.
scientific name for wood thrush

Examples

The haunting song of the Hylocichla guttata is a highlight of spring mornings in eastern forests.
Researchers track Hylocichla guttata populations to monitor environmental changes in woodlands.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Hylocichla guttata during the annual migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood thrush - a North American songbird with a distinctive, melodious song

Etymology

From Greek hylē (forest) + kichlē (thrush); guttata from Latin (spotted).
