psychopathology

1. NOUN

/ saɪkoʊpəˈθɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / saɪkəʊpəˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of mental disorders, including their diagnosis, causes, and treatment.
study of mental disorders
medical

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in psychopathology after witnessing the effects of mental illness firsthand.
The journal publishes cutting-edge research in the field of psychopathology.
A deep understanding of psychopathology is essential for effective psychiatric treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatry - the medical specialty dealing with diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders
  • Degree Lower: psychology - the scientific study of mind and behavior

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind) + 'pathos' (suffering) + '-logy' (study).
