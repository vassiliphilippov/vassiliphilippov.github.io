fakery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈfeɪkəri / (BrE)
the act of faking something or the result produced by faking; fraud or deception
deception; the act or result of faking

Examples

The art expert easily detected the fakery in the painting.
Political fakery undermines public trust in institutions.
Half the products on display were blatant fakery and not genuine antiques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being authentic; genuineness

Etymology

From fake + -ery, denoting action or result.
