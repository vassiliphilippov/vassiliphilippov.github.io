ozaena

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈziːnə / (AmE), / əʊˈziːnə / (BrE)
A chronic disease of the nose marked by a foul-smelling nasal discharge and atrophy of the nasal tissues.
chronic nasal disease with foul discharge
medical

Examples

Chronic ozaena can lead to significant discomfort and social embarrassment due to the persistent odor.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with ozaena after observing the characteristic symptoms.
Medical texts describe ozaena as a condition involving both atrophy and offensive nasal discharge.

Etymology

From Greek ozaina, from ozein (to smell).
