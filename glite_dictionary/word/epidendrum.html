Epidendrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌepɪˈdɛndrəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm / (BrE)
A large and diverse genus of orchids, native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, including some species found in the United States.
a genus of American orchids

Examples

Several Epidendrum species thrive in the cloud forests of Central America.
The botanical garden recently acquired a rare Epidendrum orchid for its collection.
Horticulturists often hybridize Epidendrum plants to produce new varieties.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + dendron (tree), referring to the epiphytic habit of many species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm / (BrE)
A large and diverse genus of orchids, including terrestrial, epiphytic, and lithophytic species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
a genus of orchids native to the Americas

Examples

Many species in the Epidendrum genus are found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.
The gardener specializes in cultivating various Epidendrum orchids for botanical exhibitions.
Some native Epidendrum species can be found in the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for its complex and colorful flowers

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + dendron (tree), referring to the plant's typical growth on trees.
