cranium

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪniəm / (AmE), / ˈkreɪniəm / (BrE)
the part of the skull that encloses and protects the brain
part of the skull enclosing the brain

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the patient's cranium before beginning the procedure.
A fracture to the cranium can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
The skull consists of the cranium and the facial bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skull - the bone structure forming the head,
  • Synonym: braincase - the portion of the skull that encloses the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'cranium', from Greek 'kranion' (skull).
