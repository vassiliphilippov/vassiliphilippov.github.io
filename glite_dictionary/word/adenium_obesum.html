Adenium obesum

1. NOUN

/ əˈdiːniəm oʊˈbiːsəm / (AmE), / əˈdiːniəm əʊˈbiːsəm / (BrE)
a species of South African shrub with a swollen succulent stem and showy pink and white flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant in tropical regions
ornamental succulent shrub with pink and white flowers

Examples

The Adenium obesum in her garden attracts attention when it blooms with vibrant pink flowers.
Bonsai enthusiasts often cultivate Adenium obesum for its striking, swollen trunk and beautiful blossoms.
After the rainy season, the Adenium obesum produces abundant flowers even though its branches are bare of leaves.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Adenium' (possibly from 'Aden', Yemen) and the species epithet 'obesum', Latin for 'fat' or 'swollen', referring to the plant's thick stem.
