to extemporize

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛmpəraɪz / (BrE)
to manage in a makeshift way, using whatever is available at the moment, especially without preparation
make do with what is available; improvise

Examples

When their tent collapsed, they had to extemporize sleeping arrangements using tarps and blankets.
During the blackout, the chef managed to extemporize a simple meal with whatever was left in the pantry.
The hikers extemporized a bridge from fallen branches to cross the stream.

Synset Examples

after the hurricane destroyed our house, we had to improvise for weeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously or without preparation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin extemporaneus (on the spur of the moment) + -ize.

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛmpərəʌɪz / (BrE)
to perform, speak, or produce something without prior preparation; to improvise
perform or speak without preparation

Examples

She had to extemporize her speech when the notes went missing.
During the concert, the pianist chose to extemporize rather than follow the sheet music.
The actor skillfully extemporized a funny monologue for the audience.

Synset Examples

he extemporized a speech at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to perform or make up something without preparation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin extemporaneus (‘on the spur of the moment’), from Latin ex tempore (‘out of the time’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01732719-v
Synset POS: v
