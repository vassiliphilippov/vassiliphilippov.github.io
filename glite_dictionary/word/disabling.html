disabling

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
depriving someone of a legal right or making them legally disqualified to do something
making legally disqualified
legal

Examples

The new policy introduced several disabling clauses that prevent certain groups from applying.
His conviction created a disabling condition, barring him from public office.
The law imposed disabling restrictions on foreign investors.

Synset Examples

certain disabling restrictions disqualified him for citizenship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enabling - giving someone legal power or right; opposite of disabling in legal context
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01198884-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to be unable to do certain things; making someone less able or incapable, often due to injury or illness.
causing incapacity or inability

Examples

Her disabling back injury prevented her from returning to work.
The accident left him with a disabling condition affecting his mobility.
Some diseases can have a disabling impact on patients' daily lives.

Synset Examples

a crippling injury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incapacitating - making someone unable to function normally
  • Antonym: enabling - making someone able to do something or more capable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01171710-s
Synset POS: s
