Dendroica fusca

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˈdroʊɪkə ˈfʌskə / (AmE), / ˌdɛnˈdrɔɪkə ˈfʌskə / (BrE)
A small North American wood warbler with black-and-white plumage and an orange-and-black head and throat, commonly known as the Blackburnian warbler.
species of North American wood warbler

Examples

The Dendroica fusca can be identified by its striking orange throat and bold black markings.
During migration, birdwatchers eagerly seek out the Dendroica fusca in eastern forests.
The vibrant colors of the Dendroica fusca make it a favorite among North American warbler species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blackburnian warbler - common name for Dendroica fusca

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Dendroica' (tree-dweller) and species 'fusca' (dusky or dark).
