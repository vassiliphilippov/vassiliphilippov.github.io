effleurage

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛ.fləˈrɑʒ / (AmE), / ˌɛ.flɜːˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
a light, rhythmic stroking movement used in massage and physical therapy to relax muscles or stimulate circulation
light, rhythmic stroking used in massage
medical

Examples

The therapist used effleurage to begin the relaxing back massage.
During labor, gentle effleurage can help ease discomfort and anxiety.
A session of effleurage is often recommended for patients recovering from muscle injuries.

Synset Examples

effleurage of the abdomen is used in the Lamaze method of childbirth

Etymology

From French effleurer (to skim or touch lightly).
