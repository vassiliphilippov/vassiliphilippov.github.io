Yugoslavian

1. ADJ

/ juːˌgoʊˈslɑːviən / (AmE), / juːˌɡɒˈslɑːviən / (BrE)
Relating to the former country of Yugoslavia, its people, or its languages.
of or relating to Yugoslavia

Examples

This old bottle contains a rare Yugoslavian wine from the 1970s.
The museum features several Yugoslavian artifacts from before the country dissolved.
She speaks fluent Yugoslavian languages, including Serbian and Croatian.

Synset Examples

Yugoslavian wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yugoslav - relating to Yugoslavia or its people

Etymology

From Yugoslavia (name of former country) + -an (adjectival suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviən / (AmE), / ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the former country of Yugoslavia
person from Yugoslavia (historical)

Examples

My grandfather was a proud Yugoslavian who cherished his homeland's diverse cultures.
Many Yugoslavians emigrated to other countries during the 1990s.
The author interviewed several former Yugoslavian citizens for her book on Balkan history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yugoslav - a native or inhabitant of Yugoslavia

Etymology

From Yugoslavia (country name) + -an (denoting nationality or origin).

Synset POS: n
