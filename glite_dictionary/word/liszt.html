Liszt

1. PROPN

/ lɪst / (AmE), / lɪst / (BrE)
(1811–1886) A Hungarian composer and piano virtuoso, renowned for his technical brilliance and influential works during the Romantic period.
Hungarian composer and pianist (1811–1886)

Examples

Many consider Liszt to be one of the greatest piano composers of all time.
The orchestra performed a spectacular rendition of a Liszt symphonic poem.
Her interpretation of Liszt's Hungarian Rhapsodies captivated the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chopin - Polish composer and pianist of the Romantic era
  • Synonym: Beethoven - German composer and pianist of the Romantic and Classical eras

Etymology

From Hungarian Liszt, the surname of Franz Liszt.
