freightage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪ.t̬ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the charge or payment for transporting goods by a carrier
charge for transporting goods

Examples

The company was responsible for paying the freightage on all imported materials.
Due to increased freightage costs, our shipping expenses doubled this year.
The invoice lists the total freightage charged for transport by rail.

Synset Examples

we pay the freight
the freight rate is usually cheaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft

Etymology

From French 'fret' (freight), with suffix '-age' denoting action or result.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪtɪdʒ / (BrE)
the transportation of goods commercially, especially in bulk and usually at rates lower than express rates
transportation of goods commercially

Examples

The company specializes in freightage across international waters for bulk commodities.
Rising freightage costs have affected the prices of imported goods.
He arranged the freightage with a reputable shipping agency to ensure timely delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft

Etymology

From freight + -age (suffix forming nouns), ultimately from Old French 'frete' (cargo).
