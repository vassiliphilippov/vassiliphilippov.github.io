andrena

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɛnə / (AmE), / ænˈdriːnə / (BrE)
a bee belonging to the genus Andrena, known as mining bees due to their habit of nesting in the ground
a bee of the genus Andrena

Examples

Scientists discovered several andrena species nesting near the riverbank last spring.
The andrena plays an important role as a pollinator in many ecosystems.
Unlike honeybees, an andrena digs individual burrows in the soil for its nest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mining bee - a common name for bees in the genus Andrena, which nest in the ground

Etymology

From New Latin Andrena, the genus name, possibly from Latin 'andrēna', a kind of bee.
