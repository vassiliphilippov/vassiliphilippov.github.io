hemiparasitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛmiˌpæɹəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) partially parasitic, typically capable of photosynthesis but also obtaining some nutrients from other plants.
(of plants) partially parasitic
medical

Examples

Many mistletoe species are hemiparasitic, drawing nutrients from their host trees while still performing photosynthesis.
The hemiparasitic nature of these plants allows them to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
Researchers studied the impact of hemiparasitic grasses on native ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: parasitic - living as a parasite or pertaining to parasitism

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + parasitic, indicating partial parasitism.
