DM

1. NOUN

/ diː ɛm / (AmE), / diː ɛm / (BrE)
An abbreviation for diabetes mellitus, a disease caused by insufficient insulin and characterized by excessive urination.
abbreviation for diabetes mellitus
medical

Examples

The nurse noted that the patient had a long history of DM managed by insulin therapy.
Many studies focus on the complications associated with DM in adults.
Effective management of DM requires regular monitoring of blood glucose levels.

Synset Examples

when doctors say ‘diabetes’ they usually mean ‘diabetes mellitus’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabetes mellitus - chronic disease of high blood sugar due to insulin problems

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'diabetes mellitus', from Latin 'diabetes' (siphon) + 'mellitus' (honeyed).
