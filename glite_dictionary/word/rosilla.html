rosilla

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / rəʊˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
a type of sneezeweed native to the southwestern United States, especially southern California
sneezeweed of southwestern U.S.

Examples

The rosilla blooms in large yellow clusters during the spring in southern California.
Many gardeners seek out rosilla for its showy appearance and tolerance of dry climates.
Researchers documented the medicinal uses of rosilla among indigenous communities in the Southwest.

Etymology

From Spanish 'rosilla', diminutive of 'rosa' (rose), likely referring to the appearance of the plant.
