hyperlipidaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərlɪpɪˈdimɪə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəlɪpɪˈdiːmɪə / (BrE)
the condition of having abnormally high levels of lipids in the blood
high blood lipid levels (medical)
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with hyperlipidaemia after reviewing his blood test results.
Lifestyle changes are often recommended to manage hyperlipidaemia.
Untreated hyperlipidaemia can increase the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - the American spelling of 'hyperlipidaemia'

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over) + lipos (fat) + -aemia (condition of the blood).
