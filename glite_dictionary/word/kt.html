kt

1. NOUN

/ keɪ ti / (AmE), / keɪ tɪ / (BrE)
An abbreviation for 'karat,' a unit of measurement for the proportion of gold in an alloy, with 24-karat gold being pure gold.
abbreviation for 'karat' (gold purity measure)

Examples

The necklace is made of 18kt gold, giving it a rich yellow hue.
Jewelry marked as 24kt is considered pure gold without any other metals.
She prefers 14kt gold for its durability and affordability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: karat - unit of gold purity

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'karat,' from Arabic 'qīrāṭ' via Italian 'carato.'
