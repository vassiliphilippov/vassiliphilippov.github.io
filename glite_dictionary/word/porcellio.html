Porcellio

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈsɛlioʊ / (AmE), / pɔːˈsɛlɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of isopod crustaceans commonly found in the Old World, especially known for its terrestrial species such as woodlice.
genus of isopod crustaceans

Examples

The genus Porcellio includes several species commonly known as woodlice.
Biologists often study Porcellio for its role in soil ecosystems.
A specimen of Porcellio was collected under the rock in the garden.

Etymology

From Latin *porcellio* (a small pig), used as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ pɔrˈsɛliˌoʊ / (AmE), / pɔːˈsɛlɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of isopod crustaceans found in the Old World, including several species of woodlice.
genus of isopod crustaceans (woodlice)

Examples

The most common woodlouse in Europe belongs to the genus Porcellio.
Biologists collected several specimens of Porcellio from under the rocks.
She specializes in the study of Porcellio in Mediterranean habitats.

Etymology

From Latin *porcellio* (a kind of small pig), diminutive of *porcus* (pig), used for its resemblance to pigs in the way woodlice move.
