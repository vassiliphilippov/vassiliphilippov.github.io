Pterocnemia pennata

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛroʊkˈnɛmiə pɛˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɒkˈniːmɪə pɛˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A species of large, three-toed, flightless bird native to South America, also known as Darwin's rhea.
a South American flightless bird (Darwin's rhea)

Examples

The Pterocnemia pennata is found in grasslands from Peru to the Strait of Magellan.
Unlike the common rhea, the Pterocnemia pennata is slightly smaller and adapted to colder climates.
Naturalists observed a group of Pterocnemia pennata running across the Patagonian steppe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhea - flightless South American bird related to the ostrich

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + knēmē (shin) and Latin pennata (feathered).
