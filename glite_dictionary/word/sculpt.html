to sculpt

1. VERB

/ skʌlpt / (AmE), / skʌlpt / (BrE)
to create a work of art by carving or shaping materials such as stone, wood, or clay
carve or shape a material into art

Examples

The artist will sculpt a new statue for the city square.
She learned how to sculpt delicate figures from marble during her studies in Italy.
He decided to sculpt a swan out of a block of ice for the party.

Synset Examples

sculpt a swan out of a block of ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to cut (a hard material) into a shape

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptus', past participle of 'sculpere' (to carve).

2. VERB

/ skʌlpt / (AmE), / skʌlpt / (BrE)
to shape or carve a material such as stone, wood, or clay into a specific form, often as a work of art
shape or carve material (art)

Examples

She patiently sculpted an intricate statue from a single piece of marble.
The artist loves to sculpt animals from driftwood found on the beach.
He spends his weekends sculpting clay figures in his garage studio.

Synset Examples

She is sculpting the block of marble into an image of her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to cut (a hard material) in order to produce an object, design, or inscription

Etymology

From Latin 'sculpere' (to carve), via French 'sculpter'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
