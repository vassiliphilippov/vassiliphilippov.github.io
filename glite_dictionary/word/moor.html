Moor

1. NOUN

/ mʊr / (AmE), / mɔː / (BrE)
a member of a Muslim people of North African origin, of mixed Arab and Berber descent, who conquered parts of Spain in the 8th century
member of a North African Muslim people

Examples

The Alhambra was built during the rule of the Moors in Spain.
Historians have studied the influence of Moors on European art and architecture.
Her ancestry traces back to the Moors who settled in Andalusia.

Etymology

From late Latin 'Maurus', from ancient Greek 'Mauros' (an inhabitant of Mauretania, region of North Africa).
