ninefold

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈnaɪnfəʊld / (BrE)
Having nine units, parts, or elements; consisting of nine parts.
having nine parts or elements

Examples

The country experienced a ninefold increase in internet usage over the decade.
A ninefold symmetry can be found in certain natural patterns.
He received a ninefold return on his initial investment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenfold - having ten units or components
  • Degree Lower: eightfold - having eight units or components

Etymology

From 'nine' + 'fold', following the pattern of similar English multipliers (e.g., 'twofold', 'threefold').

2. ADV

/ ˈnaɪnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈnaɪnfəʊld / (BrE)
By nine times as much; increased or multiplied by a factor of nine.
by a factor of nine

Examples

In the past decade, the company's profits have increased ninefold.
Her workload grew ninefold after the merger was completed.
CO2 emissions were reduced ninefold as a result of the new energy policies.

Synset Examples

my investment has increased ninefold

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenfold - by a factor of ten
  • Degree Lower: eightfold - by a factor of eight

Etymology

From "nine" (the number) + "-fold" (from Old English 'feald', meaning 'multiplied by').
