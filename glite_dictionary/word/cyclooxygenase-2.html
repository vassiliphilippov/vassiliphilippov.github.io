cyclooxygenase-2

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkloʊˌɑksɪˌdʒiːneɪs tuː / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkləʊˌɒksɪˌdʒiːneɪs tuː / (BrE)
an enzyme involved in the production of prostaglandins, contributing to inflammation, pain, and fever
enzyme linked to inflammation and pain
medical

Examples

Some painkillers work by blocking cyclooxygenase-2 and reducing inflammation.
Researchers are studying drugs that selectively inhibit cyclooxygenase-2 to manage chronic pain.
Excessive activity of cyclooxygenase-2 can lead to increased swelling and fever.

Synset Examples

the beneficial effects of NSAIDs result from their ability to block Cox-2

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclooxygenase-1 - a related enzyme that also makes prostaglandins, but is active in many normal bodily functions

Etymology

Coined from 'cyclooxygenase' (an enzyme group name) and '2', indicating the second isoform; 'cyclo-' from Greek 'kyklos' (circle), 'oxygen' and 'ase' (enzyme suffix).
