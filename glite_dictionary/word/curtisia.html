Curtisia

1. NOUN

/ kərˈtɪziə / (AmE), / kɜːˈtɪziə / (BrE)
a genus of large evergreen tree native to South Africa, especially Curtisia dentata, known for its dense wood and leathery leaves
genus of evergreen South African tree

Examples

The Curtisia tree is highly valued for its durable timber.
Many birds find shelter in the thick foliage of the Curtisia.
Curtisia is sometimes planted in parks for its attractive, evergreen appearance.

Etymology

Named after William Curtis (1746–1799), English botanist.

2. NOUN

/ kɜrˈtɪziə / (AmE), / kɜːˈtɪziə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees found in southern Africa, especially known for the species Curtisia dentata
genus of evergreen South African trees

Examples

The forest was dominated by several ancient Curtisia trees, which provided dense shade.
Botanists discovered a rare Curtisia sapling growing in the reserve.
The bark of the Curtisia tree has traditional medical uses in South Africa.

Etymology

Named after British botanist William Curtis (1746–1799).
