Fissurella

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / fɪsjʊˈrɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of marine gastropod mollusks known as keyhole limpets, characterized by a conical shell with a hole at the top.
genus of keyhole limpets (marine mollusks)

Examples

Several species within the genus Fissurella can be found along rocky intertidal shores.
The shell of a Fissurella is easily recognized by the keyhole-shaped opening at its apex.
Marine biologists collected data on various Fissurella populations during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - a type of marine mollusk with a conical shell, related group

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of 'fissura' (cleft, opening), referring to the shell's hole.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪʃəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / ˌfɪʃjʊˈrɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of marine gastropod mollusks known as keyhole limpets, which have a shell with a hole at the top.
genus of keyhole limpets (sea snails)

Examples

The shells of Fissurella species are easily recognized by the small hole at the top.
Researchers collected several specimens of Fissurella from the rocky shore.
Many marine biologists study Fissurella to understand gastropod evolution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fissurellidae - The family of marine gastropods known as keyhole limpets

Etymology

From Latin 'fissura' (a cleft or crack), referring to the hole in the shell.
