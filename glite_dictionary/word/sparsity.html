sparsity

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrsɪti / (AmE), / ˈspɑːsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being sparse; the condition of being thinly scattered or insufficient in quantity or number
the quality of being sparse or scanty

Examples

The sparsity of rainfall in the region has led to a severe drought.
Recent advances in data science exploit the sparsity of information in large datasets.
The sparsity of the furniture made the room feel empty and cold.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: density - the degree of compactness; opposite of sparsity

Etymology

From Latin 'sparsus', past participle of 'spargere' (to scatter), via Middle English and Old French.
