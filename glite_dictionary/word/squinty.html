squinty

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɪn.ti / (AmE), / ˈskwɪn.ti / (BrE)
(of a glance or look) directed to one side, often with doubt, suspicion, or envy.
looking sideways with suspicion or envy

Examples

He gave me a squinty glance when I mentioned the missing money.
Her squinty eyes made it clear she didn't quite believe my story.
The cat watched the newcomer with a squinty suspicion from the corner of the room.

Synset Examples

her eyes with their misted askance look
sidelong glances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askance - with an attitude or look of suspicion or disapproval
  • Synonym: sidelong - looking or directed to one side

Etymology

From 'squint' (to look with partly closed eyes) + '-y' (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00766264-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈskwɪnti / (AmE), / ˈskwɪnti / (BrE)
having eyes that squint or appear to squint; characterized by squinting or partially closed eyes.
characterized by squinting (eyes or expression)

Examples

She gave him a squinty look when she didn't understand his question.
The bright sunlight made his eyes appear squinty in every photo.
His squinty eyes suggested he was trying to read the small print on the sign.

Synset Examples

he looked with squinty eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squint - to look with partly closed eyes

Etymology

From squint + -y, forming an adjective meaning 'having the quality of squinting.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02810700-a
Synset POS: a
