Odyssey

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːdəsi / (AmE), / ˈɒdɪsi / (BrE)
An ancient Greek epic poem attributed to Homer, recounting the adventures of Odysseus as he journeys home after the fall of Troy.
Greek epic poem by Homer

Examples

Many scholars have studied the themes and structure of the Odyssey.
The Odyssey remains one of the most influential works in Western literature.
Her favorite story was the Odyssey, especially the part about Odysseus outwitting the Cyclops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iliad - Another Greek epic poem attributed to Homer

Etymology

From Greek 'Odysseia', the title of the poem.
