locus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəs / (BrE)
(mathematics) The set of all points or lines that satisfy a given condition or a set of conditions.
set of points defined by conditions (math)

Examples

In geometry, a circle is the locus of all points equidistant from a center.
The locus of possible solutions forms a straight line on the graph.
Mathematicians often ask students to describe the locus defined by specific constraints.

Synset Examples

the locus of points equidistant from a given point is a circle

Etymology

From Latin locus (place).

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəs / (BrE)
the specific position or site of a particular gene on a chromosome
site of a gene on a chromosome

Examples

A mutation at this locus can cause the disease in humans.
Different forms of a gene at the same locus are called alleles.
Geneticists identified the exact locus responsible for the trait.
