Quercus wislizenii

1. NOUN

/ kwɜrkəs wɪsliˈzeni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs wɪsliˈzeni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of evergreen oak tree native to the foothills of mountain ranges in western North America, also known as the interior live oak.
a Western North American evergreen oak tree

Examples

The Quercus wislizenii thrives in the foothills of the Sierra Nevada, where it forms dense groves.
Many animals depend on the acorns produced by the Quercus wislizenii for food.
The rugged bark and evergreen leaves help Quercus wislizenii survive in dry, rocky soils.

Etymology

Neo-Latin: from Latin 'quercus' (oak) + named after Frederick A. Wislizenus, German-American botanist.
