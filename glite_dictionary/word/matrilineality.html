matrilineality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrəˌlɪniˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪlɪniˈælɪti / (BrE)
the system or principle of tracing descent and inheritance through the mother's line
descent traced through mother's line

Examples

Many indigenous societies practice matrilineality, inheriting family names and property through the mother's side.
Studies of matrilineality show how kinship structures affect social dynamics and inheritance patterns.
She researched the impact of matrilineality on women's status within various cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrilineality - descent traced through the father's line

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) + 'linea' (line), patterned after 'patrilineality'.
