baroqueness

1. NOUN

/ bəˈroʊk.nəs / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being elaborate, ornate, and highly decorated, especially in reference to the Baroque style of art and architecture popular in 17th-century Europe.
elaborate, ornate quality (Baroque style)

Examples

The baroqueness of the palace interior dazzled every visitor who entered its golden halls.
Some critics feel that the baroqueness of the painting detracts from its emotional impact.
She admires the baroqueness found in European churches from the 1600s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornateness - the quality of being elaborately decorated
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being plain or uncomplicated

Etymology

From 'baroque', from French 'baroque', of uncertain origin, possibly from Portuguese 'barroco' (irregular pearl).
