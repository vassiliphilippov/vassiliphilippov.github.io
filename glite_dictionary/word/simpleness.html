simpleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.pəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.pl̩.nəs / (BrE)
a lack of complexity, subtlety, or penetration; the quality of being simple or straightforward
lack of subtlety or complexity

Examples

Her simpleness made her an easy target for deception.
The simpleness of the answer surprised the committee members.
He admired the simpleness with which she approached professional challenges.

Synset Examples

they took advantage of her simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple; absence of complexity or embellishment

Etymology

From Middle English simplenesse, from Latin simplex (simple).

/ ˈsɪmpəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being simple or uncomplicated.
state of being simple

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple, plain, or easy to understand
