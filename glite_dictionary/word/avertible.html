avertible

1. ADJ

/ əˈvɜrtəbəl / (AmE), / əˈvɜːtɪbəl / (BrE)
capable of being avoided or prevented from occurring
able to be avoided or warded off

Examples

Many diseases are avertible through proper hygiene and vaccination.
The accident was avertible if safety protocols had been followed.
Environmental damage is often avertible with timely intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidable - able to be prevented or avoided

Etymology

From Latin 'avertibilis', from 'avertere' meaning to turn away.
