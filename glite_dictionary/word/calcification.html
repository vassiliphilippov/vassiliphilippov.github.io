calcification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkælsɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process by which tissue becomes hardened due to the deposition of calcium salts, or an area of tissue that has become hardened in this way.
hardening of tissue by calcium salts
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a calcification near the patient's shoulder joint.
Progressive calcification of the arteries can lead to serious health problems.
A small calcification was detected in the lung tissue during the scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossification - the process of bone formation or turning into bone

Etymology

From Latin 'calx' (lime) + '-fication' (forming or becoming).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkælsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkalsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of becoming rigid, inflexible, or unchanging, often used metaphorically to describe ideas, institutions, or situations
inflexibility or rigidity (figurative)

Examples

The committee's refusal to consider new ideas led to a complete calcification of policy.
Over time, the organization suffered from bureaucratic calcification that made change impossible.
Negotiations broke down due to the growing calcification of each side's demands.

Synset Examples

the calcification of negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and unyielding
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily

Etymology

From Latin 'calx, calcis' (lime) + '-fication' (making).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.sə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæl.sɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which calcium or calcium salts are deposited within tissue or other substances
deposition of calcium in substances or tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that calcification in the arteries can increase the risk of heart disease.
A bone spur is often the result of excessive calcification over time.
Geologists study the calcification of coral reefs to track changes in the ocean environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossification - the natural process of bone formation
  • Antonym: decalcification - the loss or removal of calcium or calcium salts in tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'calx' (lime) + 'fication' (making or becoming).
