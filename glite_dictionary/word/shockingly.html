shockingly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃɑːkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
to a shocking or surprising degree; extremely or outrageously
to a shocking or extreme degree

Examples

The cost of the repairs was shockingly high, much more than I expected.
She was shockingly rude to the staff at the restaurant.
The city streets were shockingly empty during the storm.

Synset Examples

teachers were shockingly underpaid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surprisingly - to a surprising degree; unexpectedly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00454436-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈʃɑkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that is very bad, unexpected, or causes shock or surprise
in a very bad or surprising way

Examples

The staff behaved shockingly during the inspection and received several complaints.
Her apartment was shockingly dirty after the party, with trash everywhere.
Many found his comments shockingly insensitive given the situation.

Synset Examples

they behaved shockingly at the funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appallingly - in an extremely bad or horrifying way
  • Antonym: wonderfully - in a very good or admirable way

Etymology

formed from 'shocking' + '-ly', where 'shocking' derives from 'shock', ultimately from French 'choc' (a violent blow).

3. ADV

/ ˈʃɑkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes shock, surprise, or strong disapproval.
in a shocking way

Examples

The company paid its workers shockingly low wages, drawing criticism from the press.
Her behavior at the meeting was shockingly rude, upsetting everyone present.
He was shockingly honest about his mistakes, which surprised his colleagues.

Synset Examples

One day, she lost her temper, completely, suddenly and, even to herself, shockingly
Suddenly, shockingly, the clergyman's son was a desperado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surprisingly - in a way that causes surprise
  • Synonym: outrageously - in a way that greatly offends or shocks

Etymology

From 'shocking' + '-ly', formed from 'shock' (to cause distress or surprise) + adjectival and adverbial suffixes.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
