New London

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈlʌn.dən / (AmE), / njuː ˈlʌn.dən / (BrE)
A town in southeastern Connecticut, USA, near Long Island Sound, historically known as a major whaling center in the 19th century.
town in southeastern Connecticut, USA

Examples

The submarine museum is one of the main attractions in New London.
In the 19th century, New London was famous for its bustling whaling industry.
They drove down the coast to spend a weekend in New London.

Etymology

Named after London, England, reflecting the English heritage of early settlers.
