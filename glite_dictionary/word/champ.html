champ

1. VERB

/ tʃæmp / (AmE), / tʃamp / (BrE)
(of a horse) to chew noisily or bite repeatedly on the bit out of impatience or excitement; figuratively, to show impatience or eagerness to start.
chew or bite the bit impatiently (of a horse)

Examples

The horse began to champ at the bit as the race was about to start.
She was champing impatiently, eager for the meeting to end.
After a long wait at the gates, the crowd started to champ for the performance to begin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chomp - to chew or bite on something loudly

Etymology

Probably from Old French *champer* (to chew), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ tʃæmp / (AmE), / tʃamp / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who has won first place in a competition; a champion
informal: champion, winner
informal

Examples

Congratulations, champ! You really earned that trophy tonight.
The coach smiled and said, 'Way to go, champ. You made us proud.'
Every champ remembers the hard work it takes to reach the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - a person who has defeated or surpassed all rivals in a competition
  • Antonym: loser - a person who is defeated in a competition

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'champion'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09926157-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ tʃæmp / (AmE), / tʃamp / (BrE)
to chew food or something else noisily and forcefully
chew noisily or vigorously

Examples

The horses champed their bits impatiently as they waited to race.
She champed her gum so loudly that it distracted everyone in the quiet room.
During the hike, he champed on a granola bar to keep up his energy.

Synset Examples

The boy chomped his sandwich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chomp - to chew or bite vigorously and noisily

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of chewing; related to Middle English 'cham' (to chew).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
