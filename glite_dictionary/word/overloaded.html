overloaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
having a load that exceeds the safe or intended capacity; burdened with too much weight, work, or responsibility.
loaded past safe or intended limit

Examples

The truck was overloaded and could not make it up the hill.
My schedule feels completely overloaded with meetings this week.
The electrical circuit became overloaded during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underloaded - not loaded to full capacity
  • Degree Lower: loaded - carrying a load or burden

Etymology

From 'over-' meaning 'excessively' + 'loaded', past participle of 'load'.
