Mustela rixosa

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛlə rɪkˈsoʊsə / (AmE), / mʌˈstiːlə rɪkˈsəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of small weasel native to Canada and the northeastern United States, commonly known as the least weasel.
species of small North American weasel

Examples

The Mustela rixosa is the smallest member of the weasel family found in North America.
Biologists observed Mustela rixosa hunting voles in the grasslands of Manitoba.
Unlike larger weasels, Mustela rixosa can squeeze into very narrow burrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: least weasel - the common name for Mustela rixosa, a small North American weasel

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Mustela' (weasel); specific epithet from Neo-Latin.
