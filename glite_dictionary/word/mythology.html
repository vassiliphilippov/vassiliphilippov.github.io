mythology

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study and interpretation of myths, especially those belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition
the study of myths

Examples

She majored in mythology and focused her research on Greek legends.
The university offers a fascinating course in Norse mythology.
His deep interest in mythology led him to travel to ancient sites around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folklore - traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community

Etymology

From Greek mythología (story-telling, the study of myths), from mythos (myth) + -logia (study).

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A collection of myths, especially those belonging to a particular cultural, religious, or social tradition.
the collection of myths of a culture

Examples

Ancient Greek mythology tells the stories of gods and heroes who shaped the world.
Every culture has its own unique mythology that explains natural phenomena and traditions.
The company's origin has become part of its corporate mythology over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated
  • Synonym: folklore - the traditional beliefs and stories of a community
  • Antonym: history - the study of past events, especially human affairs

Etymology

From Greek 'mythologia' (myth + narration, account).
