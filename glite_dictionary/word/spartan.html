Spartan

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrtən / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtən / (BrE)
A person who comes from or lives in the ancient Greek city-state of Sparta.
resident of ancient Sparta

Examples

Leonidas was a famous Spartan king known for his leadership at the Battle of Thermopylae.
The Spartans were renowned for their military discipline and bravery.
Many Spartan customs have become legendary in Western history.

Etymology

From the name of the ancient Greek city-state Sparta.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrtᵊn / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtən / (BrE)
of or relating to Sparta or its people
of Sparta or its people

Examples

The Spartan army was renowned for its discipline and strength.
She studied Spartan culture as part of her ancient history course.
Artifacts from the Spartan period were displayed in the museum.

Etymology

From Latin Spartanus, from Greek Spartanos, from Sparta.
