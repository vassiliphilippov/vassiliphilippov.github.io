spartan

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑːrtən / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtən / (BrE)
showing or characterized by great self-discipline and avoidance of luxury or comfort; austere
showing great self-discipline or austerity

Examples

After the fire, the family lived a spartan existence while they rebuilt their home.
He led a spartan lifestyle, avoiding all unnecessary pleasures and comforts.
The athlete’s spartan training routine required early mornings and strict discipline.

Synset Examples

Be systematically ascetic … do … something for no other reason than that you would rather not do it
a desert nomad's austere life
a spartan diet
a spartan existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austere - strict, simple, and lacking luxury or comfort
  • Antonym: luxurious - very comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense

Etymology

From the reputation of ancient Sparta in Greece, known for strict discipline and simplicity.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɑːr.tn̩ / (AmE), / ˈspɑː.tən / (BrE)
severe and showing little comfort or luxury; strict and uncompromising in discipline or judgment
austere, strict, uncompromising

Examples

He led a spartan lifestyle, refusing any unnecessary comforts.
The school's spartan rules demanded absolute punctuality and hard work from every student.
Her apartment was spartan, with only the bare essentials for living.

Synset Examples

a parent severe to the pitch of hostility
a hefty six-footer with a rather severe mien
a strict disciplinarian
a Spartan upbringing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance
  • Antonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, involving great expense

Etymology

From Sparta, an ancient Greek city-state noted for its austerity and strict discipline.

3. ADJ

/ ˈspɑːrtən / (AmE), / ˈspɑːtən / (BrE)
showing courage and self-discipline in the face of pain, hardship, or adversity, with little display of emotion or complaint
showing courage and self-discipline in adversity

Examples

She faced her illness with spartan determination, refusing to let it overwhelm her.
Their home was spartan but comfortable, and they made do with what they had.
Despite the freezing temperatures, the mountaineers maintained a spartan resolve to reach the summit.

Synset Examples

spartan courage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoic - showing endurance of pain or hardship without complaint
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence

Etymology

From the name of Sparta, an ancient Greek city-state noted for its strict discipline and courage.
