Weimaraner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪ məˌrɑː nər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ məˌrɑː nə / (BrE)
a large breed of dog with a short, smooth, greyish coat, originally developed in Germany for hunting.
large grey German hunting dog breed

Examples

The Weimaraner is known for its intelligence and athleticism, making it a popular choice among hunters.
She adopted a Weimaraner puppy from the local animal shelter last spring.
Their Weimaraner loves to run in open fields and needs plenty of exercise every day.

Etymology

From German, named after Weimar, a city in Germany, where the breed originated.
