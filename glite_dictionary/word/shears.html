shears

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪrz / (AmE), / ʃɪəz / (BrE)
large scissors with strong blades, typically used for cutting thick material such as hedges or fabric
large, strong scissors

Examples

The gardener sharpened his shears before trimming the hedges.
She used a pair of heavy-duty shears to cut the thick fabric.
Farmers often use shears to clip the wool from sheep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scissors - a tool used for cutting paper, cloth, etc., with two blades

Etymology

From Old English 'sceara' (a cutting instrument), related to 'shear'.
