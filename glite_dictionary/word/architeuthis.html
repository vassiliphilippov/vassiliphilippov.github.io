Architeuthis

1. PROPN

/ ɑːr.kɪˈtuː.θɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kɪˈtjuː.θɪs / (BrE)
A genus of very large, deep-ocean squid known as the giant squid, reaching lengths up to 18 meters (60 feet).
giant squid genus

Examples

Fascination surrounds the elusive Architeuthis, which has inspired countless sea monster legends.
Until recently, scientists had never observed a live Architeuthis in its natural habitat.
The tentacles of Architeuthis can reach impressive lengths, making it a formidable deep-sea predator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant squid - the common name for Architeuthis, a very large squid species

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' (chief) + 'teuthis' (squid).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtuːθɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtjuːθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of very large, deep-ocean squid known as giant squids, among the largest invertebrates in the world.
giant squid genus

Examples

A team of scientists discovered a living Architeuthis off the coast of Japan.
The Architeuthis can grow up to 13 meters in length, making it a true giant of the sea.
Until recently, sightings of Architeuthis were rare and surrounded by myth and speculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant squid - very large, deep-sea squid species

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' meaning 'chief' and 'teuthis' meaning 'squid'.
