inviolate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / ɪnˈvʌɪ.ələt / (BrE)
not harmed or changed; kept pure or untouched, especially in the sense of remaining sacred or unbroken.
not violated; kept pure or sacred

Examples

Her privacy was considered inviolate by everyone in the family.
The ancient treaty remained inviolate for centuries, respected by all parties involved.
A sense of tradition kept the historic site inviolate despite pressures for modernization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrosanct - regarded as too important to be interfered with
  • Antonym: violated - having been harmed or treated disrespectfully

Etymology

From Latin 'inviolatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'violatus' (violated).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (BrE)
(of a woman, dated or medical) Having the hymen unbroken; remaining a virgin.
having the hymen unbroken (dated)
archaic medical

Examples

In certain historical societies, a woman's reputation depended on remaining inviolate until marriage.
Legal documents once used the term inviolate to refer to a woman whose hymen was unbroken.
She was described as inviolate and pure in the old text.

Synset Examples

she was intact, virginal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virginal - remaining a virgin; not having experienced sexual intercourse
  • Antonym: deflowered - having lost virginity

Etymology

From Latin inviolatus (unviolated), from in- (not) + violatus (violated).
