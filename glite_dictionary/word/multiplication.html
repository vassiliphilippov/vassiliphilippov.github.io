multiplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of producing offspring; reproduction by generating new individuals
reproduction by creating offspring

Examples

Rapid multiplication of bacteria can quickly lead to infection.
The multiplication of insects in spring ensures a stable food chain for birds.
Selective breeding encourages the multiplication of desirable traits in domesticated animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproduction - the process of producing offspring; biological reproduction

Etymology

From Latin multiplicatio (a multiplying) from multiplicare (to multiply).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltəplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in the number or quantity of something, especially by repeated or rapid reproduction or duplication
an increase in number or quantity

Examples

The unchecked multiplication of weeds quickly overtook the garden.
A multiplication of responsibilities made his job overwhelming.
Rapid multiplication of bacteria can cause food to spoil in just a few hours.

Synset Examples

repeated copying leads to a multiplication of errors
this multiplication of cells is a natural correlate of growth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - the process of becoming larger or greater in amount
  • Synonym: proliferation - a rapid increase in numbers

Etymology

From Latin multiplicatio (a multiplying), from multiplicare (to multiply).

3. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltəpləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of finding the product of two or more numbers or quantities; an arithmetic operation that is the inverse of division
arithmetic operation to find a product

Examples

The teacher explained multiplication using simple visual aids.
Learning multiplication tables is an important step in elementary school math.
The multiplication of large numbers can be done quickly with a calculator.

Synset Examples

the multiplication of four by three gives twelve
four times three equals twelve

Related Terms

  • Antonym: division - arithmetic operation that is the inverse of multiplication

Etymology

From Latin multiplicatio, from multiplicare (to multiply).
