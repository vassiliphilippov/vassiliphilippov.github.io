Cromwellian

1. ADJ

/ krɑmˈwɛliən / (AmE), / krɒmˈwɛliən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Oliver Cromwell or his time, policies, or followers, especially in strictness or austerity
relating to Oliver Cromwell or his era

Examples

The government adopted Cromwellian reforms aimed at increasing discipline and austerity.
The old house still retains some Cromwellian architectural features from the seventeenth century.
His Cromwellian attitude toward entertainment made him unpopular among the more liberal members of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puritanical - practicing or advocating strict religious or moral behavior

Etymology

From the name Oliver Cromwell (1599–1658), English military and political leader, with the suffix -ian.
