Astilbe japonica

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪlbi dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / əˈstɪlbi dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to Japan, cultivated for its dense clusters of small, colorful flowers and often grown as an ornamental shrub.
species of ornamental Japanese flowering shrub

Examples

Gardeners often choose Astilbe japonica for its vibrant spring blooms and attractive foliage.
The florist arranged a bouquet featuring white Astilbe japonica for the Easter celebration.
Astilbe japonica thrives in shaded areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Astilbe - genus of shade-loving perennial flowering plants

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Astilbe' from Greek a- (not) + stilbos (shining); species name 'japonica' meaning 'of Japan'.
