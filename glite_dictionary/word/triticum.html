Triticum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪtɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪtɪkəm / (BrE)
a genus of annual cereal grasses, including wheat, native to the Mediterranean and widely cultivated in temperate regions
genus of grasses including wheat

Examples

Triticum is studied extensively by botanists for its importance as a food crop.
Many varieties of wheat belong to the genus Triticum.
The hybridization of Triticum species has contributed to advances in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant of the genus Triticum, widely cultivated

Etymology

Latin *triticum* (wheat, from *tritum*, meaning 'threshed')

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪtɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪtɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of annual cereal grasses native to the Mediterranean region, including the common wheat and its relatives, widely cultivated in temperate areas.
genus of wheat grasses

Examples

Common wheat, known scientifically as Triticum aestivum, is a staple crop worldwide.
The Triticum genus includes several important cultivated species.
Botanists classify all common wheats under the Triticum genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain, used to make flour

Etymology

From Latin *triticum* (wheat).
