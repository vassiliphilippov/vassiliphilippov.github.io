mint

1. NOUN

/ mɪnt / (AmE), / mɪnt / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Mentha, typically with aromatic leaves and small mauve or white flowers, commonly used in cooking and for flavoring
aromatic plant of the genus Mentha

Examples

She added fresh mint leaves to the salad for extra flavor.
The scent of mint filled the garden in early summer.
Many herbal teas contain dried mint for a refreshing taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peppermint - an aromatic hybrid mint with a strong flavor
  • Synonym: spearmint - a common species of mint with a mild flavor

Etymology

From Old English *minte*, from Latin *mentha*, from Greek *minthē* (a type of mint plant).
