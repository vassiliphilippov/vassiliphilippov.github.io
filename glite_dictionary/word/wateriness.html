wateriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtɚinəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being watery, especially in having a thin or diluted consistency like water
quality of being watery or thin

Examples

The wateriness of the soup disappointed everyone at the dinner table.
She complained about the wateriness in the texture of the sauce.
Too much broth can add wateriness to the stew, making it less flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diluteness - the quality of being diluted or less concentrated
  • Antonym: thickness - the quality of being thick or viscous

Etymology

From 'watery' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚi nɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of containing too much water; the condition of being watery, often implying dilution, thinness, or lack of substance (in both literal and figurative uses)
quality of being watery or diluted

Examples

The soup's wateriness disappointed everyone at the table.
Critics complained about the wateriness of the novel's arguments.
His eyes reflected a certain wateriness after hours of crying.

Synset Examples

the haziness and wateriness of his disquisitions
the wateriness of his blood
no one enjoys the burning of his soup or the wateriness of his potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watery - containing or resembling water; dilute or thin
  • Antonym: richness - the quality of having depth, substance, or abundance (often as antonym to thinness or wateriness)

Etymology

From 'watery' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning 'the state of being watery'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being watery, especially the wetness of ground that is covered or soaked with water
the state of being watery or wet

Examples

The wateriness of the field made it impossible to play soccer after the storm.
Excessive wateriness in the soil can harm the roots of many plants.
They complained about the wateriness of the newly paved road, which remained muddy for days.

Synset Examples

the baseball game was canceled because of the wateriness of the outfield
the water's muddiness made it undrinkable
the sloppiness of a rainy November day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wetness - the state of being wet or moist
  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being dry

Etymology

Formed from 'watery' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being watery.
