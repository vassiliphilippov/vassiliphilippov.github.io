whiplashy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪpˌlæʃi / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.læʃ.i / (BrE)
Having the quality of sudden, sharp, or jerking movement, or resembling the effects of whiplash.
resembling a sudden or jerking movement
informal

Examples

After the bumpy flight, I felt a bit whiplashy getting off the plane.
His storytelling style can be whiplashy, jumping rapidly between topics.
The roller coaster's sharp turns made everyone feel whiplashy by the end.

Synset Examples

The car's sudden stop left me feeling whiplashy.
The roller coaster ride was so thrilling, it felt like whiplash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiplash - an injury or sensation caused by sudden jerking of the head or body

Etymology

From 'whiplash' + '-y' suffix, denoting quality or likeness.
