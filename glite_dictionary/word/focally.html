focally

1. ADV

/ ˈfoʊkəli / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is limited to a specific focus or localized area, especially in medical or scientific contexts.
in a focal or localized manner
medical

Examples

The infection was found to be focally present in the tissue sample.
This type of tumor grows focally rather than spreading diffusely throughout the organ.
In radiology, abnormalities may appear focally rather than being widespread.

Synset Examples

the submucosa was focally infiltrated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focal - related to a focus or focal point

Etymology

From 'focal' + '-ly', with 'focal' from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace).
