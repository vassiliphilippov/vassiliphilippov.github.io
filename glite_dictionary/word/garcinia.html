Garcinia

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈsɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen tropical trees and shrubs, including the mangosteen, valued for their edible fruit and medicinal properties
a genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The fruit of the Garcinia tree is prized for its sweet, tangy flesh.
Botanists have discovered several new species within the Garcinia genus in Southeast Asia.
Some supplements use extracts from Garcinia due to its supposed health benefits.

Etymology

From the Latinized name of Laurent Garcin, a French botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs native to tropical regions, including species such as the mangosteen.
genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The Garcinia genus includes several species known for their medicinal properties.
The famous fruit mangosteen comes from the Garcinia mangostana tree.
Researchers studied various types of Garcinia for their potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangosteen - a tropical fruit produced by Garcinia mangostana

Etymology

Named after Laurent Garcin, a French botanist (1683–1751).
