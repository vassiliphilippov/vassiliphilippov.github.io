Chrysopsis mariana

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɒpsɪs məˈriːænə / (AmE), / kraɪˈsɒpsɪs məˈriːənə / (BrE)
A perennial species of golden aster native to the southeastern United States.
perennial golden aster of southeastern US

Examples

The sandy fields were dotted with blooms of Chrysopsis mariana each autumn.
Botanists conducted a survey of Chrysopsis mariana to study its response to habitat changes.
Gardeners in the southeast often plant Chrysopsis mariana to attract pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'opsis' (appearance); species name from Latinized form of 'Mariana.'
