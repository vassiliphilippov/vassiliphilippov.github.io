to jactitate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒæk.tɪ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.tɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
(rare) To move, toss, or shake about violently.
to toss or move violently

Examples

The child began to jactitate in panic during the thunderstorm.
The patient would often jactitate restlessly in his hospital bed at night.
During the nightmare, she seemed to jactitate uncontrollably, disturbing the sheets.

Synset Examples

The feverish patient thrashed around in his bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to move or stir about violently
  • Synonym: to writhe - to twist or move violently, often in pain

Etymology

From Latin jactitatus, past participle of jactitare (to toss about, to boast).
