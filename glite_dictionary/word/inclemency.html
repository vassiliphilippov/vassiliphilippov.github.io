inclemency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (BrE)
excessive sternness or severity, especially in behavior, character, or judgment.
excessive sternness or severity

Examples

The judge's inclemency surprised everyone, as many had expected a lighter sentence.
His inclemency made him feared by his subordinates in the company.
Many residents criticized the mayor's inclemency in dealing with minor infractions.

Synset Examples

severity of character
the harshness of his punishment was inhuman
the rigors of boot camp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being severe or harsh
  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected

Etymology

From Latin 'inclementia', from 'in-' (not) + 'clementia' (mildness, mercy).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛmənsi / (BrE)
(formal) the quality of being severe, rough, or unpleasant, especially referring to weather or climate.
severe or unpleasant weather

Examples

Flights were canceled due to the inclemency of the weather.
The game's organizers feared inclemency would keep crowds away.
Because of the inclemency of the day, the picnic was postponed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clemency - mildness, especially of weather

Etymology

From Latin 'inclementia', from in- (not) + 'clementia' (mildness).
