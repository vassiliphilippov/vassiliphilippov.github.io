artificially

1. ADV

/ ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not natural; accomplished by human or non-natural means.
by non-natural means

Examples

The flowers were artificially colored to enhance their appeal.
Doctors kept the patient alive artificially using a ventilator.
The lake was artificially created to provide water for nearby farms.

Synset Examples

artificially induced conditions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naturally - in a natural manner or by natural means

Etymology

From 'artificial' + '-ly'; 'artificial' from Latin 'artificialis', from 'ars' (art) + 'facere' (to make).
