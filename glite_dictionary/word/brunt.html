brunt

1. NOUN

/ brʌnt / (AmE), / brʌnt / (BrE)
the main force or impact of something unpleasant or harmful
main force or impact of something

Examples

After the scandal, the CEO took the brunt of the criticism from the media.
Coastal towns will bear the brunt of the approaching storm.
Parents often feel the brunt of financial pressures during the holiday season.

Synset Examples

bore the brunt of the attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'brunt' (a blow, attack), of uncertain origin, possibly Scandinavian.
