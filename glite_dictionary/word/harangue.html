harangue

1. NOUN

/ həˈræŋ / (AmE), / həˈræŋ / (BrE)
a lengthy and aggressive speech, often delivered in a loud and forceful manner
forceful, lengthy speech

Examples

The politician delivered a passionate harangue against the new tax law.
During the meeting, her unexpected harangue left everyone in stunned silence.
He launched into a furious harangue about the failures of the administration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
  • Synonym: diatribe - a forceful and bitter verbal attack
  • Antonym: eulogy - a speech in praise of someone, typically after their death

Etymology

French *harangue*, from Italian *aringa* (public address, speech).

2. VERB

/ həˈræŋ / (AmE), / həˈraŋ / (BrE)
to address someone in a forceful, critical, or lengthy manner, often with strong emotion
lecture someone forcefully

Examples

The politician harangued the crowd for nearly an hour, barely pausing to take a breath.
She felt uncomfortable as her manager harangued her about the missed deadline in front of her colleagues.
Instead of listening, he harangued his friend about the dangers of unhealthy eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - give a long or serious speech to someone
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of someone

Etymology

From Middle French 'harangue', from Italian 'aringa' (public speech), likely from Latin 'harenga' (speech to an assembly).

Synset POS: v
