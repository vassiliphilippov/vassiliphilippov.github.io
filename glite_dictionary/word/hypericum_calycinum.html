Hypericum calycinum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm kəˈlɪsɪnəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm kəˈlɪsɪnəm / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen shrub with bright yellow, star-shaped flowers in summer, often grown as ground cover.
creeping shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

Hypericum calycinum is often planted on slopes to help prevent soil erosion.
Gardeners appreciate Hypericum calycinum for its long-lasting yellow flowers and dense foliage.
We chose Hypericum calycinum as a low-maintenance ground cover for the backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose of Sharon - another common name for Hypericum calycinum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Hypericum' is a plant genus, and 'calycinum' refers to prominent calyx features.
