to ingest

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
To take food, drink, or another substance into the body, typically by swallowing or eating.
take in food or substance

Examples

Doctors recommend that you ingest plenty of fluids when you are sick.
The patient had accidentally ingested a small object while eating.
Some plants can be toxic if ingested by pets.

Synset Examples

Have another bowl of chicken soup!
I don't take sugar in my coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consume - to eat or drink something
  • Degree Higher: digest - to break down food in the body after eating

Etymology

From Latin ingestus, past participle of ingerere (to carry in).
