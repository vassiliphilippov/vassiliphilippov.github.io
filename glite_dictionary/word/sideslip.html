sideslip

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdslɪp / (BrE)
an unexpected sideways slide, especially of a vehicle or aircraft, often caused by loss of traction or control
an unexpected sideways slide

Examples

The car went into a sideslip on the icy road, but the driver regained control quickly.
During the storm, the airplane experienced a brief sideslip before landing safely.
A sudden gust of wind caused the cyclist to have a dangerous sideslip on the wet pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skid - a sudden uncontrolled slide, especially of a vehicle

Etymology

From 'side' + 'slip', indicating a slipping movement to the side.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌslɪp / (BrE)
An aircraft maneuver in which the plane moves sideways in the air while maintaining its forward direction.
aircraft sideways flight maneuver

Examples

The pilot performed a sideslip to lose altitude quickly before landing.
A sideslip can help align the aircraft with a short runway.
During the approach, he used a sideslip to counteract a strong crosswind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skid - an uncontrolled slide, often sideways, in an aircraft or vehicle

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'slip,' describing the act of slipping sideways.
