Anas discors

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəs ˈdɪskɔrs / (AmE), / ˈænəs ˈdɪskɔːz / (BrE)
A small North American duck, also known as the blue-winged teal, recognized by its distinctive blue wing patches.
North American duck species (blue-winged teal)

Examples

The Anas discors migrates south in the winter, often gathering in large flocks.
Researchers banded several Anas discors to track their migration patterns.
The distinctive blue on the wings makes the Anas discors easy to identify among other ducks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-winged teal - Common name for Anas discors, a small duck species of North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anas' meaning 'duck' and 'discors' meaning 'different' or 'incongruous.'
