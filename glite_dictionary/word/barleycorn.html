barleycorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑr.liˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.li.kɔːn / (BrE)
A grain or seed of barley.
a grain of barley

Examples

The farmer showed me a perfect barleycorn from this year's harvest.
An inch was once defined as the length of three dried barleycorns placed end to end.
She found a barleycorn stuck in her boot after walking through the field.

Etymology

Old English 'bærlicorn' (barley seed), from 'barley' + 'corn'.

Examples

Etymology

Formed from 'barley' + 'corn', where 'corn' in Old English meant 'grain'.
