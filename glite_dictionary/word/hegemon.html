hegemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒɪˌmɑn, hɪˈdʒimɑn / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒɪˌmɒn, hɪˈdʒiːmɒn / (BrE)
a state, organization, or person that has dominant influence or authority over others, especially in political or international affairs
dominant power or leader

Examples

Many historians consider the United States to be the global hegemon in the post-Cold War era.
Following its victory, the empire emerged as the regional hegemon, shaping neighboring countries' policies.
The rise of a new hegemon in the region led to a balance-of-power shift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superpower - a very powerful and influential nation
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person or group of lower rank or power

Etymology

From Greek hēgemōn (leader, ruler), from hēgeisthai (to lead).
