Leymus condensatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.məs ˌkɑn.dənˈseɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.məs ˌkɒn.dɛnˈseɪ.təs / (BrE)
A stout perennial grass native to western North America, commonly found in dry habitats.
perennial grass of western North America

Examples

Leymus condensatus is commonly used in habitat restoration projects across California.
Native Americans traditionally used Leymus condensatus for basket weaving and building shelter.
Leymus condensatus can grow over two meters tall in optimal conditions.

Etymology

From the genus name Leymus, after Swedish botanist Fredrik Magnus Leitner (anglicized), and Latin condensatus meaning 'condensed' or 'thick'.
