hyperemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpərˈiːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈriːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by an excess of blood in the vessels of an organ or tissue (hyperemia).
of or relating to hyperemia (excess blood in vessels)
medical

Examples

The patient's skin appeared hyperemic after the allergic reaction.
A hyperemic conjunctiva is often seen in cases of bacterial eye infections.
The surgeon noted hyperemic tissue surrounding the inflamed area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperemia - an excess of blood in the vessels of an organ or tissue

Etymology

From 'hyperemia' + '-ic', from Greek 'hyper' (over) + 'haima' (blood).
