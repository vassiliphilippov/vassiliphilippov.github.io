Syngnathus

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈnæθəs / (AmE), / sɪŋˈnæθəs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Syngnathidae, commonly known as pipefishes, characterized by long, slender bodies and tubular snouts.
genus of pipefishes

Examples

The genus Syngnathus includes several species of pipefishes found in coastal waters.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Syngnathus in the Mediterranean Sea.
Several members of the Syngnathus genus exhibit remarkable camouflage abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - a small fish with a long, tubular snout, closely related to seahorses
  • Synonym: Syngnathidae - the biological family of pipefishes and seahorses

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + gnathos (jaw), referring to the fused jaws of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈnaθəs / (AmE), / sɪŋˈnaθəs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Syngnathidae, which includes pipefishes and related species.
a genus of pipefishes

Examples

The Syngnathus genus is well known for its elongated body shape and tubular snout.
Several species within Syngnathus can be found in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
Marine biologists identified the specimen as belonging to the Syngnathus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - any long, slender fish of the family Syngnathidae

Etymology

From Greek 'syn' (together) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the fused jaws characteristic of the genus.
