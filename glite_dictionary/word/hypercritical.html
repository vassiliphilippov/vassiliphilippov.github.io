hypercritical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
Excessively and unreasonably critical; inclined to judge too harshly.
excessively critical; overly severe in judgment

Examples

The editor was hypercritical of every small error in the manuscript.
Sometimes, parents can become hypercritical of their children's choices.
Her hypercritical attitude made it difficult for anyone to please her.

Synset Examples

hypercritical of colloquial speech
the overcritical teacher can discourage originality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcritical - excessively critical; too harsh in judgment
  • Degree Lower: critical - inclined to find fault or judge severely
  • Antonym: forgiving - inclined to pardon or overlook faults

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, above) + critical, from Greek kritikos (able to discern).
