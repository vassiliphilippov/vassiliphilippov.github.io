rheological

1. ADJ

/ ˌriːəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the science of rheology, which deals with the flow and deformation of matter
relating to flow and deformation of matter

Examples

The rheological properties of the new polymer make it ideal for high-stress applications.
Engineers measured the rheological behavior of the mixture to optimize the production process.
Understanding rheological characteristics is essential for designing effective lubricants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rheology - the branch of science dealing with the flow and deformation of matter

Etymology

From 'rheology' + -ical; 'rheology' is from Greek 'rheos' (stream, flow).
