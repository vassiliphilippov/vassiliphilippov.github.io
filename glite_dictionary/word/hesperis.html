Hesperis

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛspərɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɛspərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of biennial or perennial erect herbs with fragrant flowers that release scent at night, commonly known as dame's violet or night-scented stock.
genus of fragrant-flowered herbs

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet evening scent of Hesperis blossoms.
Botanists admire Hesperis for its delicate purple or white flowers and nocturnal fragrance.
She planted Hesperis near the patio to enjoy its perfume at dusk.

Etymology

From Greek *hespera* (evening), referring to the evening scent of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛspərɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɛspərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of biennial or perennial erect herbs, native to Eurasia, known for their nocturnally fragrant flowers; commonly called dame's violet or rocket.
a genus of fragrant Eurasian herbs

Examples

The Hesperis blooms are especially fragrant in the evening.
Gardeners often plant Hesperis for its attractive, night-scented flowers.
Several species of Hesperis are popular in cottage gardens.

Etymology

From Greek *hesperis*, 'of the evening', referencing its flowers' night fragrance.
