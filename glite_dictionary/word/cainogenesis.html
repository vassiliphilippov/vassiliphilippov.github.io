cainogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪnoʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkeɪnəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the introduction during embryonic development of features or structures not present in the earlier evolutionary history of a strain or species
development of new features in embryos
medical

Examples

The emergence of the placenta in mammals is an example of cainogenesis.
Some researchers study cainogenesis to understand evolutionary innovation in species.
Cainogenesis contrasts with the repetition of ancestral traits during embryonic development.

Etymology

From Greek kainos (new) + genesis (origin, creation).
