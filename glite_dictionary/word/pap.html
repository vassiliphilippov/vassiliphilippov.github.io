pap

1. NOUN

/ pæp / (AmE), / pæp / (BrE)
a small projection of a mammary gland, such as a nipple or teat, especially in animals or archaic use in humans
small projection of a mammary gland
archaic

Examples

The sow fed her piglets from each pap in turn.
Old texts often refer to the human nipple as the pap.
In some mammals, the pap is barely visible except when nursing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nipple - the small projection on the breast through which milk is drawn
  • Synonym: teat - the projection on a mammary gland from which the milk is sucked

Etymology

From Middle English 'pap', from Old French, of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ pæp / (AmE), / pæp / (BrE)
soft, bland food that requires little or no chewing, often given to people who are ill or have difficulty swallowing
soft food requiring little chewing

Examples

After his surgery, he was put on a pap diet until he healed.
The elderly residents were served pap each morning for breakfast.
Infants often start with pap before moving on to solid foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruel - a thin liquid food of oatmeal or other meal boiled in milk or water
  • Synonym: porridge - a soft food made by boiling grains in water or milk

Etymology

From Middle English 'pap', from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German, imitative of the sound made by a baby eating.

3. NOUN

/ pæp / (AmE), / pap / (BrE)
ideas, writing, or information that is considered worthless, simplistic, or lacking in substance
worthless or oversimplified ideas
informal

Examples

The speech was full of pap and lacked any real policy proposals.
Most television talk shows nowadays serve up nothing but intellectual pap.
She wanted analysis, not the usual pap presented by the morning news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drivel - silly or meaningless talk; nonsense
  • Antonym: substance - meaningful or significant content

Etymology

Originally referred to soft food or mush; figurative sense of 'bland or insipid content' arose in the 20th century.
