clearness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pure, transparent, or easy to see through, especially in reference to liquids like water
the quality of being pure or transparent (of water)

Examples

The clearness of the lake allowed us to see fish swimming near the bottom.
Scientists measured the clearness of the water to monitor pollution levels.
After filtration, the clearness of the sample improved dramatically.

Synset Examples

when she awoke the clarity was back in her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear; transparency; often used for liquids

Etymology

From Middle English cleernesse, from Old French cler, from Latin clarus (clear, bright).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈklɪənəs / (BrE)
The quality of being easy to perceive, understand, or interpret; the state of being clear.
the quality of being clear or easy to understand

Examples

The clearness of her explanation made the concept much easier to grasp.
He admired the clearness of the mountain air during his morning hike.
The report was praised for its clearness and concise language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular

Etymology

From Middle English 'clernesse', from Old French 'clarté', from Latin 'claritas'.
