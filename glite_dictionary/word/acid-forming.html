acid-forming

1. ADJ

/ ˈæsɪd ˌfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance) yielding an acid when dissolved in water.
producing acid in solution

Examples

Certain fertilizers are considered acid-forming because they increase soil acidity.
Rainwater is often acid-forming due to air pollution.
A diet high in animal proteins tends to be more acid-forming than one rich in fruits and vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkaline-forming - producing an alkaline effect in solution
