argemone

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒɛməni / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɛməni / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants with prickly leaves and stems, and large white or yellow flowers, found chiefly in tropical America.
a genus of prickly-flowered plants

Examples

The garden featured a variety of unusual plants, including the striking argemone.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several argemone species native to Mexico.
The argemone is recognizable by its prickly leaves and bright yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'argemone', from Greek 'argemōnē' (a poppy-like plant).
