Crotalus tigris

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.təl.əs ˈtaɪ.ɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.tæl.əs ˈtɪ.ɡrɪs / (BrE)
A species of rattlesnake with irregular cross-bands on its back, native to arid foothills and canyons of southern Arizona and Mexico.
species of tiger rattlesnake

Examples

The Crotalus tigris is known for its small, rattle-tipped tail and tiger-like crossbands.
Wildlife biologists carefully monitored the population of Crotalus tigris in the Arizona desert.
You can identify Crotalus tigris by its distinctive pattern and relatively small size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger rattlesnake - common name for Crotalus tigris

Etymology

From Latin: Crotalus (genus name meaning 'rattle') and tigris ('tiger').
