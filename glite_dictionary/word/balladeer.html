balladeer

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæləˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbæl.əˈdɪə / (BrE)
a singer or performer of ballads, especially popular or narrative songs
singer of ballads

Examples

The famous balladeer captivated the audience with his heartfelt songs.
She became known as a balladeer in folk music circles during the 1970s.
As a talented balladeer, he brings traditional stories to life through song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubadour - a composer and performer of Old Occitan lyric poetry and song

Etymology

From 'ballad' + '-eer', modeled after words like 'buccaneer'.
