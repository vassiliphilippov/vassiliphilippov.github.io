timekeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌkiː.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌkiː.pə / (BrE)
a device or instrument used to measure or record time accurately
device for measuring or recording time

Examples

The referee used a timekeeper to ensure the game lasted exactly ninety minutes.
My grandfather's antique timekeeper still chimes every hour on the mantelpiece.
A reliable timekeeper is essential for conducting scientific experiments accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clock - device for measuring and indicating time

Etymology

From 'time' + 'keeper', meaning one who or that which keeps time.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌkiːpə / (BrE)
an official in sports responsible for keeping track of the time elapsed during a game or event
person who tracks time in sports

Examples

The timekeeper signaled the end of the match with a loud buzzer.
As the official timekeeper, she monitored each period carefully to ensure fairness.
The coach argued with the timekeeper about the remaining seconds in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referee - an official who enforces the rules in sports

Etymology

From 'time' + 'keeper'.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a person, usually a clerk, responsible for recording the hours worked by employees or workers
person who records employees' work hours

Examples

The timekeeper ensured that all employees' hours were accurately recorded each week.
As a timekeeper, she checked the daily log sheets and calculated overtime.
The factory hired a new timekeeper to improve payroll accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paymaster - person responsible for paying wages

Etymology

From 'time' + 'keeper', meaning one who keeps or records time.
