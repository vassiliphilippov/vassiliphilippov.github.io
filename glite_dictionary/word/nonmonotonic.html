nonmonotonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˌmɑːnəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌmɒnəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a process, function, or relationship) not consistently increasing or decreasing; not monotonic.
not consistently increasing or decreasing

Examples

The algorithm fails when it encounters a nonmonotonic sequence of inputs.
Climate change models must account for nonmonotonic relationships between variables.
Her research focuses on nonmonotonic reasoning in artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monotonic - consistently increasing or decreasing; without change in direction

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'monotonic', which is from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'tonos' (tone).
