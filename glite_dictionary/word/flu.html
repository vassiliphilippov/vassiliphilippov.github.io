flu

1. NOUN

/ fluː / (AmE), / fluː / (BrE)
a common, highly contagious viral infection causing fever, aches, and respiratory symptoms
common viral infection (influenza)

Examples

She stayed home from work because she had the flu.
Doctors recommend getting vaccinated every year to protect against the flu.
His body ached and he had a high fever, classic signs of the flu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza - technical or full name for flu, viral illness
  • Degree Lower: cold - milder viral infection with similar symptoms

Etymology

Shortened form of 'influenza' (Italian, from Latin, meaning 'influence').

2. NOUN

/ flu / (AmE), / flu / (BrE)
a common infectious illness caused by the influenza virus, characterized by fever, aches, and respiratory symptoms
common viral infection (influenza)

Examples

She had to stay home from work because she caught the flu.
Every winter, hospitals see an increase in flu cases.
The flu vaccine can help prevent serious illness.

Synset Examples

She became sicker as the flu spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza - the medical term for flu, a contagious viral infection
  • Degree Lower: cold - a common viral infection with milder symptoms than flu

Etymology

Shortened form of 'influenza', from Italian 'influenza' (influence).
