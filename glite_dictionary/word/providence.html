providence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.vɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
the protective care of God or of nature as a spiritual power; a manifestation of foresightful divine care
God's protective care; divine foresight

Examples

Many people saw their survival during the famine as an act of providence.
She credited providence for guiding her through difficult times.
The settlers believed it was providence that had led them to the fertile valley.

Etymology

From Latin providentia (foresight, prudence), from providere (to foresee, provide for).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.və.dəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
the careful and sensible management of resources; prudence in providing for the future
prudent resource management

Examples

Through providence and planning, she was able to save enough money for her children's education.
The community's survival was ensured by the providence of its leaders during difficult times.
His providence allowed the organization to weather the financial crisis with minimal losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise in practical affairs

Etymology

From Latin providentia (foresight, prudence) via Old French providence.

/ ˈprɑːvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒvɪdəns / (BrE)

divine guidance or care

Examples

She trusted in providence to see her through in difficult times.
