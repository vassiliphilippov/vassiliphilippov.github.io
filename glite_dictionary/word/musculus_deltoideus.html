musculus deltoideus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs dɛlˈtɔɪ.di.əs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs dɛlˈtɔɪ.di.əs / (BrE)
the large, triangular muscle covering the shoulder joint, responsible for raising, flexing, extending, and rotating the arm
the deltoid muscle of the shoulder
medical

Examples

The musculus deltoideus is essential for lifting your arm away from your body.
Injury to the musculus deltoideus can make shoulder movement painful and difficult.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the musculus deltoideus during shoulder operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deltoid - the thick, triangular shoulder muscle

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'deltoideus' (shaped like the Greek letter delta, Δ).
