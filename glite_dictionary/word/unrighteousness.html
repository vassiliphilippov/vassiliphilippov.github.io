unrighteousness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈraɪtʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈraɪtʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not behaving according to moral principles; wickedness or injustice.
lack of moral principles; wickedness

Examples

The judge condemned the unrighteousness that had plagued the city's administration.
She spoke out against unrighteousness in all its forms, insisting on justice for everyone.
They sought forgiveness for their unrighteousness and vowed to lead better lives.

Synset Examples

forgave us our sins and cleansed us of all unrighteousness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: righteousness - the quality of being morally right or justifiable
  • Synonym: wickedness - the state of being morally wrong or evil

Etymology

From un- (not) + righteous (from Old English rihtwīs) + -ness (forming nouns of quality or state).
