to defalcate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfælk.eɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfæl.keɪt / (BrE)
to embezzle or misappropriate funds or property entrusted to one's care
to embezzle funds entrusted to one
legal

Examples

The manager was charged when he tried to defalcate funds from the company account.
Anyone who attempts to defalcate public money commits a serious crime.
Auditors discovered she had defalcated a significant sum over several years.

Synset Examples

The accountant embezzled thousands of dollars while working for the wealthy family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - steal or misappropriate (money placed in one's trust)
  • Synonym: to misappropriate - use something for a wrong purpose, especially dishonestly or illegally

Etymology

From Latin defalcare (to deduct), from de- (down) + falx (sickle).
