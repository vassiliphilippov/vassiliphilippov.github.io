Carex arenaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛɹɛks ɛˈnɛɹiə / (AmE), / ˈkɛəɹɛks əˈnɛːriə / (BrE)
A European maritime sedge, also known as sand sedge, naturalized along the Atlantic coast of the United States; its rootstock is noted for properties similar to sarsaparilla.
a species of sand sedge plant

Examples

Carex arenaria grows naturally in sandy soils, especially near coastal dunes.
Botanists have studied Carex arenaria for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.
You can identify Carex arenaria by its creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Etymology

From Latin: 'carex' meaning sedge, and 'arenaria' meaning growing on sand.
