sympathiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who shares the feelings, opinions, or aims of another and hopes for their success
one who shares another's feelings or aims
british

Examples

Many civil rights sympathisers gathered outside the courthouse to show their support.
She considered herself a sympathiser of the environmental movement.
The government cracked down on sympathisers of the opposition party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who approves of and encourages someone or something
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or resists an idea or cause

Etymology

From sympathize + -er; 'sympathize' from Greek sympathēs (having a fellow feeling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who expresses sympathy or shares in the suffering of someone who has experienced misfortune
someone who expresses sympathy

Examples

After her father's passing, many sympathisers visited to offer their condolences.
The victims of the disaster received support and thanks from sympathisers around the world.
She appreciated the thoughtful messages from sympathisers during her difficult time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a cause or individual
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who opposes something or someone

Etymology

From verb sympathise + -er (person who does an action), from Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling).
