athleticism

1. NOUN

/ əˈθlɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈθlɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
vigorous physical strength, agility, or energetic vitality, often as shown in sports or in the enthusiastic character of someone or something
physical strength, agility, or energetic vitality

Examples

Her athleticism was evident in every aspect of her busy daily routine.
The athleticism of the dancers brought the performance to life.
Critics praised the athleticism in his compositions, noting the high energy and drive in his music.

Synset Examples

his music is characterized by a happy athleticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
  • Antonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From 'athlete' (via French 'athlète') + '-ism', ultimately from Greek 'athlētēs' (contestant in the games).
