Dehong Dai

1. NOUN

/ dəˈhɔŋ daɪ / (AmE), / dəˈhɒŋ daɪ / (BrE)
a branch of the Tai languages spoken in Southwest Yunnan Province, especially in the Dehong Dai and Jingpo autonomous prefectures of China
Tai language of Southwest Yunnan, China

Examples

The Dehong Dai language has a rich tradition of folk tales and poetry.
Linguists study Dehong Dai to understand the evolution of the Tai language family.
Many ethnic Dai people in Yunnan speak Dehong Dai as their first language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai language - a family of languages spoken in Southeast Asia, including Thai and Lao

Etymology

From Dehong (a region in Yunnan, China) and Dai (the Tai-speaking ethnic group).
