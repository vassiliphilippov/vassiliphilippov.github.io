southernism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌðənɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or pronunciation characteristic of the southern United States
southern U.S. word or expression
american

Examples

The use of the word 'y'all' is a classic southernism heard throughout the region.
Her accent is marked by several distinct southernisms that locals immediately recognize.
When he moved north, his southernism of saying 'fixin’ to' often puzzled his coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northernism - a locution or pronunciation peculiar to the northern United States

Etymology

Formed from 'Southern' + '-ism', denoting a distinctive practice or characteristic.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərnˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌðənɪzəm / (BrE)
an attitude, custom, or expression characteristic of people from the southern United States
attitude or trait of US Southerners

Examples

His gentle manners were a classic southernism that set him apart from his northern colleagues.
The writer's novel is full of southernism, both in dialogue and in attitudes toward family and tradition.
He explained the southernism of always greeting neighbors, no matter the time of day.

Etymology

Formed from 'Southern' + '-ism' (suffix indicating a characteristic or ideology).
