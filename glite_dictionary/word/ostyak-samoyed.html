Ostyak-Samoyed

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiˌæk səˈmɔɪɛd / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˌæk səˈmɔɪɛd / (BrE)
a member of a people of mixed Ostyak and Samoyed origin living in Siberia
person of mixed Ostyak and Samoyed descent

Examples

The Ostyak-Samoyed people have preserved many of their traditional customs and language.
A researcher traveled to Siberia to study the folklore of the Ostyak-Samoyed community.
Descendants of Ostyak-Samoyed families can still be found in remote parts of Russia.

Etymology

Ostyak from Russian Остю́к (Ostyuk), Samoyed from Russian Самоед (Samoyed); ethnonym formed by combining the two group names.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑstiˌæk səˈmɔɪɛd / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˌæk səˈmɔɪɛd / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Selkup people of Siberia, also known as Selkup
Uralic language of the Selkup people

Examples

Scholars have produced several grammars of the Ostyak-Samoyed language to preserve its unique features.
Very few people today still speak Ostyak-Samoyed as their mother tongue.
Linguists consider Ostyak-Samoyed to be an important branch of the Uralic language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Selkup - Uralic language spoken by the Selkup people of Siberia

Etymology

From Russian Остяко-самоедский (Ostyako-samoyedsky), referring to the historical names of the Ostyak and Samoyed peoples.
