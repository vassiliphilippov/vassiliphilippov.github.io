extractant

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstræktənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktənt / (BrE)
a substance, especially a liquid, used to remove a specific component from a mixture or another substance, often by dissolving or separating it
substance used for extracting another

Examples

The laboratory used an organic extractant to separate rare earth metals from the solution.
A new, more efficient extractant has been developed for the purification of uranium.
It is important to choose the right extractant to maximize the yield in the extraction process.

Synset Examples

Copper can be extracted using hydroxyoximes as extractants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a liquid that dissolves another substance to form a solution

Etymology

From 'extract' + '-ant', denoting an agent or thing that performs an action.
