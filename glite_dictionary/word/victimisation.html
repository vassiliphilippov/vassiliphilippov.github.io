victimisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪktəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating someone unfairly or cruelly, making them a victim, especially through exploitation or discrimination
the act of treating someone unfairly

Examples

The new policy led to the victimisation of minority workers within the company.
She spoke out against the victimisation she had experienced because of her beliefs.
Many people joined the protest to raise awareness about the victimisation of refugees.

Synset Examples

capitalistic exploitation of the working class
paying Blacks less and charging them more is a form of victimization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploitation - the action or fact of treating someone unfairly to benefit from their work
  • Synonym: persecution - hostile and unfair treatment, especially because of race or beliefs
  • Antonym: fairness - impartial and just treatment or behavior without favoritism or discrimination

Etymology

From victim + -isation; influenced by the verb 'victimise'.
