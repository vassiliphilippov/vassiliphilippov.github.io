iguanid

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnɪd / (BrE)
a member of a family of lizards found in the Americas, Madagascar, and some Pacific islands, typically with long tails and often a bright throat patch in males
a type of lizard from the Americas and nearby regions

Examples

The green iguanid basked on a sunlit branch above the river.
Scientists observed several species of iguanids during their expedition to the Galápagos Islands.
Male iguanids often display vibrant throat patches to attract mates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a type of reptile with four legs and a long tail

Etymology

From modern Latin Iguanidae, based on Spanish iguana.
