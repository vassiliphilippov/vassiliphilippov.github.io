sculpin

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlpɪn / (AmE), / ˈskʌlpɪn / (BrE)
a spiny, large-headed fish with a broad mouth, typically lacking scales, found in various marine and freshwater environments
a spiny, large-headed fish

Examples

The river was home to several rare species, including the elusive sculpin.
Fishermen often catch sculpin along the rocky coastal shores.
The sculpin is known for its spiny fins and unusually large head.

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in English in the early 19th century.
