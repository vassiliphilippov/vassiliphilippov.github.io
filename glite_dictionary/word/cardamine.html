Cardamine

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdæmɪni / (AmE), / kɑːˈdæmɪni / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), commonly known as bittercress, with small white or pink flowers.
genus of plants called bittercress

Examples

Several species of Cardamine can be found growing wild along the riverbanks.
The delicate flowers of Cardamine burst open in early spring.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control Cardamine as it quickly spreads in moist soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittercress - any plant of the genus Cardamine

Etymology

From Latin 'cardamine', from Greek 'kardamínē', a kind of cress.
