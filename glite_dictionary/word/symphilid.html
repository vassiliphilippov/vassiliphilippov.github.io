symphilid

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈfɪlɪd / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɪlɪd / (BrE)
A minute, soil-dwelling arthropod of the order Symphyla, often found infesting the underground parts of crops and greenhouse plants.
small arthropod pest in soil

Examples

The gardener discovered that symphilids were damaging the roots of his vegetable plants.
Greenhouse crops are particularly vulnerable to infestations of symphilids in the soil.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of symphilids to develop better pest management strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symphyla - A class of small, centipede-like soil arthropods to which symphilids belong

Etymology

From modern Latin Symphyla (the order name) + -id (suffix forming names of organisms, especially in zoology).
