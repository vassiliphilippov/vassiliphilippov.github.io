Cupressus abramsiana

1. NOUN

/ kuˈprɛsəs ˌæbrəmˈziːənə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ˌæbrəmˈziːənə / (BrE)
A rare species of cypress tree native to California, taller than but closely related to the Gowen cypress and sometimes considered the same species.
rare California species of cypress tree

Examples

The Cupressus abramsiana is endemic to a few locations in the Santa Cruz Mountains.
Botanists debated whether Cupressus abramsiana should be distinct from the Gowen cypress.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining stands of Cupressus abramsiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cupressus goveniana - Gowen cypress, a related California cypress species

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Cupressus' (cypress) and species honoring American botanist LeRoy Abrams.
