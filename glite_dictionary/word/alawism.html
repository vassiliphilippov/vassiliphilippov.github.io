Alawism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌwɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈal.əˌwɪz.əm / (BrE)
A branch of Shia Islam, originating in the 9th century, practiced mainly in northwestern Syria and neighboring areas of Turkey and Lebanon, known for its distinct religious beliefs and practices.
branch of Shia Islam, mainly in Syria

Examples

Many members of the ruling elite in Syria have roots in Alawism.
The history and theology of Alawism have long fascinated religious scholars.
Growing up in a region where Alawism is practiced influenced his spiritual outlook.

Synset Examples

Additionally, there has been a recent movement to unite Alawism and the other branches of Twelver Islam through educational exchange programs in Syria and Qom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shiism - The branch of Islam comprising Shia Muslims who regard Ali as the legitimate successor to Muhammad

Etymology

From Arabic ‘Alawī, meaning 'follower of Ali' (Ali ibn Abi Talib), with the English suffix -ism.
