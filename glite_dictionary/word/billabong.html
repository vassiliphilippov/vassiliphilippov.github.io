billabong

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.əˌbɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.əbɒŋ / (BrE)
A stagnant pool or watercourse, typically formed when a river changes course and leaves behind a cutoff meander, found especially in Australia.
stagnant pool formed by a river (Australia)

Examples

During the dry season, the only water left in the outback was a billabong shaded by eucalyptus trees.
Birds gathered at the billabong to drink and escape the midday heat.
The children swam in the cool waters of the billabong on a hot afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxbow lake - a crescent-shaped lake formed beside a winding river

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Aboriginal Australian language) *bilabang* (a creek or watercourse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.əˌbɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.ə.bɒŋ / (BrE)
A branch of a river forming a blind channel, filled with water only during high river flows, commonly found in Australia.
branch of a river filled during floods
british

Examples

During the rainy season, water fills the billabong and the whole area comes alive with birds.
The children played near the edge of the billabong, watching for frogs and turtles.
Many Australian poems and songs mention the tranquil beauty of a billabong at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxbow lake - U-shaped lake formed when a wide meander from the main stem of a river is cut off

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) 'bilabaŋ' meaning a watercourse that runs only after rain.
