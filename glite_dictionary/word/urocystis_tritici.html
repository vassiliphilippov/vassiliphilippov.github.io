Urocystis tritici

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈsɪstɪs traɪˈtaɪsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈsɪstɪs trɪˈtɪsaɪ / (BrE)
a species of fungus that infects the leaves and stems of wheat, causing disease
fungus infecting wheat

Examples

Urocystis tritici is responsible for causing flag smut in wheat crops worldwide.
Farmers monitor fields regularly to detect early signs of Urocystis tritici infection.
Research on Urocystis tritici has led to improved disease resistance in certain wheat varieties.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Urocystis' (from Greek 'oura' tail + 'kystis' bladder), species 'tritici' (from Latin 'triticum' wheat).
