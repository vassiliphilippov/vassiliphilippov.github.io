visored

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈvɪzəd / (BrE)
having or equipped with a visor, or having a visor of a specified type
having a visor

Examples

The knight entered the arena wearing a visored helmet for protection.
The soldiers stood at attention in their dark-green, visored caps.
She chose a visored sunhat to shade her eyes from the bright sun.

Synset Examples

their spic, red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visorless - lacking a visor

Etymology

From 'visor' (projecting front or shield) + '-ed' (forming adjectives).
