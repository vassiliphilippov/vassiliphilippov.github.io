Bastet

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæstɛt / (AmE), / ˈbæstɛt / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian goddess depicted with the head of a cat or lioness, representing the life-giving power of the sun and associated with home, fertility, and protection.
Egyptian cat-headed goddess

Examples

Many ancient statues depict Bastet with the head of a cat, symbolizing her protective nature.
Worshipers offered music and dance to honor Bastet at her temple in Bubastis.
The story of Bastet reveals the importance of cats in ancient Egyptian society.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sekhmet - lioness-headed Egyptian goddess of war

Etymology

From Egyptian 'Bꜣstt', a goddess worshipped in the Nile Delta region.
