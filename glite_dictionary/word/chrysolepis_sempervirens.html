Chrysolepis sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪsoʊˈlɛpɪs ˌsɛmpərˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈliːpɪs ˌsɛmpəˈvʌɪrɛnz / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to the mountains of California, resembling the golden chinkapin.
evergreen Californian mountain shrub

Examples

The slopes were dotted with Chrysolepis sempervirens thriving in the rocky soil.
Botanists identified the shrub as Chrysolepis sempervirens during their field survey.
Chrysolepis sempervirens is prized for its glossy leaves and resilience to drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden chinkapin - an evergreen tree or shrub of North America, related species

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *lepis* (scale), and Latin *sempervirens* (evergreen).
