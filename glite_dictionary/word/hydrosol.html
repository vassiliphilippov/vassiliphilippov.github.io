hydrosol

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌsɒl / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrəˌsɒl / (BrE)
A colloidal solution in which water is the continuous (dispersing) phase.
colloid with water as continuous phase

Examples

The scientist prepared a hydrosol by dispersing fine clay particles in water.
Silver hydrosol is commonly marketed for its supposed health benefits.
In the experiment, the properties of the hydrosol were carefully measured and recorded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol - colloid in which the continuous phase is gas

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) + sol (solution).
