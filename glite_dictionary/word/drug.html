drug

1. VERB

/ drʌɡ / (AmE), / drʌɡ / (BrE)
to take or use recreational drugs, especially illegally or habitually
use recreational drugs

Examples

He began to drug heavily during his college years and struggled to stop.
Some teenagers feel pressure to drug at parties to fit in with their peers.
Many young people are unaware of the dangers when they drug without understanding the risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use something, especially a drug, in a way that is harmful or illegal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01203078-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ drʌɡ / (AmE), / drʌɡ / (BrE)
a substance used as a medicine or for its narcotic effects
substance used as medicine or narcotic

Examples

Doctors prescribed a new drug to help control her symptoms.
Many teenagers are warned about the dangers of illegal drugs.
This type of drug is commonly used to relieve pain after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - a substance used to treat illness or injury
  • Synonym: narcotic - a drug affecting mood or behavior, often used illegally

Etymology

From Old French 'drogue', of uncertain origin; possibly from Middle Dutch 'droge vate' (dry barrels, i.e. dry goods).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03252323-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ drʌɡ / (AmE), / drʌɡ / (BrE)
to administer a drug, especially to someone, often to induce unconsciousness or alter behavior
administer a drug to someone

Examples

The criminals drugged their victims before robbing them.
The veterinarian drugged the lion so it could be safely examined.
Authorities suspect someone drugged the drink at the party.

Synset Examples

They drugged the kidnapped tourist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to medicate - to treat with medicine
  • Antonym: poison - to administer a harmful substance

Etymology

From Middle English 'drogge', from Old French 'drogue' (drug).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00084491-v
Synset POS: v
