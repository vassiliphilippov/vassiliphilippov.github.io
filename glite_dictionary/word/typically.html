typically

1. ADV

/ ˈtɪpɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is usual for a particular person, thing, or group; in a characteristic or representative manner.
in a usual or characteristic way

Examples

She typically arrives at work before anyone else in the office.
The weather in April is typically unpredictable in this region.
He typically forgets to lock the door when he leaves.

Synset Examples

Tom was typically hostile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usually - in most cases or on most occasions
  • Antonym: atypically - in a way that is not typical

Etymology

From 'typical' + '-ly'; 'typical' from Greek 'typikos' (of a type).
