imminence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.ə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈɪm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the state or fact of being about to happen very soon
state of being about to happen

Examples

The imminence of the storm forced the residents to evacuate the area quickly.
Scientists warned about the imminence of an earthquake along the major fault line.
People sensed the imminence of change as the election results came in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminency - state of being imminent; alternative form
  • Antonym: remoteness - state of being far away in time; opposite of imminence

Etymology

From Latin imminentia ‘overhanging’ (from imminere ‘to project, impend’).
