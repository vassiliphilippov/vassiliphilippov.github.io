deed

1. NOUN

/ did / (AmE), / diːd / (BrE)
a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially relating to the ownership or transfer of property
legal property document
legal

Examples

She received the deed to the house after the sale was completed.
Before you can sell the land, you must locate the deed.
The lawyer examined the deed to verify the rightful owner.

Synset Examples

he signed the deed
he kept the title to his car in the glove compartment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title deed - a legal document proving ownership of property
  • Antonym: act - something that is done or performed (general sense)

Etymology

From Old English dǣd (action, deed), influenced in legal sense by Norman French 'deed' (legal document).

2. NOUN

/ did / (AmE), / diːd / (BrE)
an action, especially one that is significant, intentional, or notable
an intentional or notable action

Examples

She was praised for her brave deed during the rescue mission.
His good deeds did not go unnoticed by the community.
The story celebrates the heroic deeds of its main character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - a thing done; an action
  • Synonym: action - something done or performed
  • Antonym: word - a promise or statement as opposed to actions

Etymology

From Old English 'dēd' (action, deed), related to Old High German 'tat' and Old Norse 'dáð' (deed).
