Doliolum

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈliːələm / (AmE), / dəʊˈlɪələm / (BrE)
A genus of small, barrel-shaped marine tunicates in the family Doliolidae.
genus of barrel-shaped marine tunicates

Examples

The Doliolum can be found drifting in warm ocean waters around the world.
Marine biologists study the life cycle of Doliolum to understand their unique reproductive strategies.
Unlike many tunicates, Doliolum moves by jet propulsion, allowing it to navigate through the sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Doliolidae - family of tunicates to which Doliolum belongs
  • Degree Higher: tunicate - marine invertebrate animal with a sac-like body structure

Etymology

From Latin *doliolum* ('small barrel'), diminutive of *dolium* ('barrel').
