stalk

1. NOUN

/ stɑk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
The material made up of seed coverings and small pieces of stem or leaves that have been separated from the seeds, especially after threshing or processing.
loose plant material separated from seeds

Examples

Farmers often burn the stalk after harvesting the crops to clear the fields.
The floor was covered with stalk and other plant debris from the grain processing.
She used the leftover stalk as bedding for the animals.

2. NOUN

/ stɔk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
A hunt for game in which the hunter follows the animal stealthily or lies in wait for it in ambush.
stealthy hunt for game

Examples

The lion's stalk through the tall grass was both silent and deadly.
He set out at dawn for a stalk that lasted several hours.
During the stalk, the hunter moved carefully to avoid startling the deer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - a surprise attack from a concealed position

/ stɑk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)

5. NOUN

/ stɔk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
the slender or elongated main stem or supporting structure of a plant or a part of a plant, such as a leaf or flower
supporting stem of a plant or plant part

Examples

The tomato plant's stalk grew tall and supported many leaves.
She carefully cut the stalk of celery before adding it to the salad.
A single flower bloomed at the top of the long green stalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stem - the main structural axis of a plant
  • Synonym: petiole - the stalk that attaches a leaf to the plant stem

Etymology

From Old English 'stalc', related to Middle Dutch 'stalke' (stalk, stem).
