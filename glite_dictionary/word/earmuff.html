earmuff

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌmʌf / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌmʌf / (BrE)
Either of a pair of padded coverings for the ears, usually connected by a band, worn to protect the ears from cold weather.
coverings for the ears to keep them warm

Examples

She wore a pair of earmuffs to keep her ears warm during the snowstorm.
The children chose brightly colored earmuffs before heading outside to play.
His earmuffs matched his scarf and gloves perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earflap - a flap that covers the ears (on a hat, cap, or helmet)

Etymology

Formed from 'ear' + 'muff' (a protective covering); first recorded in the 19th century.
