Phalaris tuberosa

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛərɪs tuːbəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / fəˈlærɪs tjuːbəˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to Australia and South Africa, grown as forage and introduced to North America.
forage grass species; perennial

Examples

Phalaris tuberosa is commonly planted in pastures to provide high-quality forage for livestock.
Some researchers study the ecological effects of Phalaris tuberosa after its introduction to new regions.
Farmers in southern Australia use Phalaris tuberosa to improve soil conservation on their properties.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Phalaris and the Latin 'tuberosa' (tuberous).
