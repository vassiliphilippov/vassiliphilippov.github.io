phototropism

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.t̬oʊˈtroʊ.pɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təˈtrəʊ.pɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the growth or movement of a plant or other organism in response to light, usually towards the source of light
growth or movement in response to light
medical

Examples

Most plants exhibit phototropism, bending their stems toward a window to reach more sunlight.
The lack of light caused the seedlings to display abnormal phototropism in the laboratory experiment.
The study measured the degree of phototropism in various flower species under controlled conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phototaxis - movement of an organism toward or away from a light source

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'tropos' (turn).
