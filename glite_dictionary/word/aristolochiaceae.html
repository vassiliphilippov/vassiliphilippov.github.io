Aristolochiaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪstəˌlɑkiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌrɪstəˌlɒkɪˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants known as birthworts, which includes wild ginger and other related species
family of flowering plants – birthworts

Examples

The Aristolochiaceae family includes several plants used in traditional medicine.
Many species in the Aristolochiaceae are native to tropical and temperate regions around the world.
Botanists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Aristolochiaceae in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aristolochia - a genus of plants in the Aristolochiaceae family (birthworts)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Aristolochia.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstəˌloʊkiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˌlɒkiˈeɪsiˌi / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants known as the birthwort family, which includes wild ginger and other species with aromatic herbs and vines
family of flowering plants; birthwort family

Examples

The Aristolochiaceae include a variety of climbing and herbaceous plants.
Members of the Aristolochiaceae are often found in tropical and temperate regions.
Wild ginger is a well-known member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Aristolochia (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
