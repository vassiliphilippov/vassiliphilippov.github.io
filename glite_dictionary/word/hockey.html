hockey

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːki / (AmE), / ˈhɒki / (BrE)
A team sport played on a field where two teams use curved sticks to drive a ball into the opponents' goal.
field sport using sticks and ball

Examples

She has played hockey for her school's team since she was ten years old.
The local league hosts a hockey tournament every summer.
Field hockey is especially popular in countries like India and the Netherlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice hockey - a similar sport played on ice with skates and a puck

Etymology

Originally from Middle French *hoquet* (shepherd’s crook), referring to the stick's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːki / (AmE), / ˈhɒki / (BrE)
a fast-paced game played on an ice rink between two teams of six players, who use sticks to direct a puck into the opponent's goal
ice-based team sport with puck and sticks

Examples

He learned to play hockey on frozen ponds as a child.
The local team won the hockey championship last night.
She enjoys watching hockey games every Saturday with her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field hockey - a similar game played on grass with a ball, not a puck

Etymology

From French 'hoquet' (shepherd's crook), referring to the shape of the stick.
