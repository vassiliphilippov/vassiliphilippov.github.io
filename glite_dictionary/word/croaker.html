croaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
any of several fish that produce a croaking noise, especially members of the family Sciaenidae
a fish that makes a croaking noise

Examples

Fishermen often catch croaker in coastal waters during the summer months.
The croaker is named for the distinct grunting noises it produces.
Scientists studied how the croaker generates its characteristic sound.

Etymology

From 'croak' (imitative of a low, harsh sound) + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
the lean flesh of a saltwater fish, especially any of several species of the drum family found along the Atlantic coast of the southern United States, used as food
lean flesh of a saltwater fish (food)

Examples

The chef prepared the croaker fillets with a spicy southern seasoning.
Fishermen along the coast often sell fresh croaker at the market each morning.
Grilled croaker is a favorite dish in some southern seafood restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - fish of the family Sciaenidae, often called croaker
