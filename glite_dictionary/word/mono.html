mono

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəʊ / (BrE)
(of sound) transmitted, recorded, or reproduced using a single audio channel, as opposed to stereo.
single-channel audio

Examples

This old radio only plays in mono, not stereo.
The album was originally released in mono before the stereo version came out.
Some audiophiles prefer mono recordings for their clarity and focus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stereo - (of sound) using two or more channels to create an impression of directionality and depth

Etymology

Shortened from 'monophonic', from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'phōnē' (sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00242685-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəʊ / (BrE)
An infectious disease, especially among young people, that causes fever, sore throat, fatigue, and swollen glands; an informal term for mononucleosis.
informal term for mononucleosis (disease)
informal medical american

Examples

My doctor said I have mono, so I need to rest for a few weeks.
After catching mono during her first semester, she missed several classes.
He was surprised how tired he felt when he had mono as a teenager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mononucleosis - An infectious disease causing fever and swollen lymph nodes

Etymology

Shortened from 'mononucleosis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14158759-n
Synset POS: n
