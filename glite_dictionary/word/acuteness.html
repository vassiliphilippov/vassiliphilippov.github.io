acuteness

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjutnəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being highly sensitive or finely developed, especially in a faculty such as sight, hearing, or smell
keen sensitivity or sharpness

Examples

The acuteness of her hearing allowed her to notice even the faintest sounds.
Many animals rely on the acuteness of their senses to survive in the wild.
He was praised for the acuteness of his sense of smell during the experiment.

Synset Examples

dogs have a remarkable acuteness of smell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpness - the quality of being sharp or acute (of the senses)
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of sensitivity or intensity, opposite of acuteness

Etymology

From Latin acūtus (sharp, pointed) + English -ness.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a sharp edge or point; keenness or sharpness in form
sharpness of an edge or point

Examples

The acuteness of the blade made it perfect for cutting fine pieces of paper.
Scientists measured the acuteness of the needle under a microscope.
The acuteness of the thorn pricked my finger as I picked the rose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpness - the quality of being sharp

Etymology

From Latin 'acūtus' (sharp, pointed) plus the suffix '-ness'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a quick and penetrating intelligence or sharpness of mind
sharpness of mind or intelligence

Examples

Her acuteness allowed her to solve the intricate problem faster than anyone else.
The acuteness of his observations impressed the entire panel of judges.
We need the acuteness of a detective to see through these complex clues.

Synset Examples

he argued with great acuteness
I admired the keenness of his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keenness - the quality of being eager or having sharp quickness of mind
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of intelligence or quick perception

Etymology

Formed from 'acute' (from Latin acutus, sharp) with the suffix '-ness'.
