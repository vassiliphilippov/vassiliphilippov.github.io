megahertz

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌhɝts / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəhɜːts / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one million cycles per second
unit equal to one million cycles per second

Examples

The computer's processor runs at 800 megahertz, making it much faster than older models.
Radio stations are often assigned frequencies measured in megahertz.
Modern WiFi networks commonly operate around 2400 megahertz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilohertz - unit of frequency equal to one thousand cycles per second
  • Degree Higher: gigahertz - unit of frequency equal to one billion cycles per second

Etymology

From mega- (million) and hertz (unit of frequency named after Heinrich Hertz).
