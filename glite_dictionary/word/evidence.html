evidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛvədəns / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
Information or material presented in a court of law to prove or disprove alleged facts in a case.
information used to prove facts in court
legal

Examples

The judge ruled that the evidence was admissible in court.
There was insufficient evidence to convict the accused.
Her lawyer presented new evidence to support the appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement given in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'evidentia' (clearness, obviousness), from 'evidens' (obvious, clear).

WordNet ID: oewn-06746688-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
The facts, signs, or objects that make you believe that something is true.
something that shows something is true

Examples

The muddy footprints were clear evidence that someone had been in the house.
Her cheerful smile was evidence of her excitement about the trip.
There is no evidence linking the two crimes together.

Synset Examples

his trembling was evidence of his fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence strong enough to establish something as true
  • Synonym: indication - a sign that suggests the existence or truth of something
  • Antonym: disproof - evidence that shows something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'evidentia' (clearness, obviousness), from 'evidens' (clear, obvious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06656171-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
to provide or serve as evidence or proof of something

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin evidens, evidentis (obvious, apparent), via Old French evidence (appearance from which inferences may be drawn).

6. NOUN

/ ˈɛv.ə.dəns / (AmE), / ˈɛv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
facts, information, or signs that help to show if something is true or valid; the basis for a belief or opinion
facts or information supporting belief

Examples

There was no evidence to support his claim of innocence.
Scientific evidence suggests that climate change is accelerating.
The lawyer examined all the evidence before preparing her argument.

Synset Examples

the evidence that smoking causes lung cancer is very compelling

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: proof - conclusive evidence that establishes truth
  • Degree Lower: indication - something that suggests or points to a fact, but less strong than evidence
  • Synonym: testimony - a statement or declaration serving as evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'evidentia' (clearness), from 'evidens' (clear, apparent).
