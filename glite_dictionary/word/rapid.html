rapid

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd / (BrE)
a section of a river where the water moves very fast and often becomes rough
fast-flowing section of a river

Examples

The kayak overturned in the dangerous rapid.
They scouted the rapids before starting their white-water rafting trip.
Fish often struggle to swim upstream through a powerful rapid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: waterfall - a steep fall or flow of water in a river

Etymology

From Latin rapidus (seizing quickly, swift).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈræpɪd / (BrE)
happening or done very quickly; taking only a short period of time.
very quick; happening in a short time

Examples

Her recovery after surgery was remarkably rapid, astonishing the doctors.
The company has experienced rapid growth in the last two years.
A rapid response to the emergency saved many lives.

Synset Examples

a rapid rise through the ranks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - moving or happening at high speed
  • Antonym: slow - not quick; taking a long time
  • Synonym: swift - moving very quickly

Etymology

From Latin rapidus (seizing, hasty), from rapere (to seize, snatch).

3. ADJ

/ ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd / (BrE)

moving or happening very quickly

Examples

She made a rapid recovery from her illness, surprising her doctors.
The pace of change in technology is increasingly rapid.
Reacting with rapid movements, he caught the falling glass before it shattered.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - moving or happening quickly; at great speed
  • Antonym: slow - moving or happening at a low speed
  • Synonym: swift - moving or capable of moving at high speed
  • Synonym: speedy - done or occurring quickly
  • Degree Higher: instantaneous - occurring or done instantly and immediately
  • Synonym: quick - done or happening with speed

Etymology

From Latin rapidus (seizing, swift), from rapere (to seize, snatch).
