Psychopsis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑpsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of epiphytic orchids native to Central and South America, formerly classified within the genus Oncidium.
genus of epiphytic orchids

Examples

The rare Psychopsis orchid is prized by collectors for its striking butterfly-shaped flowers.
Several species within Psychopsis thrive in the humid rainforests of Central America.
Botanists recently reclassified certain orchids from Oncidium to the genus Psychopsis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oncidium - a large genus of flowering orchids, previously including Psychopsis

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (butterfly) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the butterfly-like flowers.
