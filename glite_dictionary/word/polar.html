polar

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.lə / (BrE)
(of a molecule or compound) having a pair of equal and opposite electric charges, resulting in a dipole moment.
having equal and opposite charges

Examples

Water is a polar molecule, which enables it to dissolve many substances.
Because hydrochloric acid is polar, it can readily conduct electricity in solution.
The polar nature of ammonia makes it highly soluble in water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonpolar - (of a molecule) not having regions of positive and negative charge

Etymology

From Latin polaris, from polus (pole), relating to poles or having a dipole.
