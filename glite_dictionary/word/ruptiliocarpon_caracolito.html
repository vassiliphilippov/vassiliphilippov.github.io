Ruptiliocarpon caracolito

1. NOUN

/ ruːpˌtɪli.oʊˈkɑːrpɒn ˌkɑːrəkoʊˈliːtoʊ / (AmE), / rʌpˌtɪlɪəʊˈkɑːpɒn ˌkærəkəʊˈliːtəʊ / (BrE)
A large tree native to Costa Rica with light-colored wood used in cabinetry, notable for its similarity in wood and fruit structure to African lepidobotrys.
Costa Rican tree with pale wood for cabinetry

Examples

The forests of Costa Rica are home to the impressive Ruptiliocarpon caracolito, valued for its fine wood.
Local artisans prefer to use Ruptiliocarpon caracolito in cabinetry due to its durability and color.
Because of its unique structure, the Ruptiliocarpon caracolito is often compared to certain African trees.
