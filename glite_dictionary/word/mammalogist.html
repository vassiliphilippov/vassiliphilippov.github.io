mammalogist

1. NOUN

/ məˈmæləˌdʒɪst / (AmE), / məˈmæləˌdʒɪst / (BrE)
A scientist who specializes in the study of mammals.
scientist who studies mammals

Examples

The mammalogist spent years in the field observing wild wolves in their natural habitat.
During the conference, a renowned mammalogist presented new findings on primate behavior.
She aspired to become a mammalogist after developing a fascination with bats as a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoologist - a scientist who studies animals
  • Degree Lower: ornithologist - a scientist who studies birds

Etymology

From Latin 'mammalis' (of the breast) + Greek '-logistēs' (one who studies).
