tractability

1. NOUN

/ træk.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / træk.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily managed or persuaded
quality of being easy to persuade or manage

Examples

The tractability of the staff made implementing new policies much easier.
A child's tractability can be a blessing for parents and teachers alike.
His tractability in negotiations helped the company reach a quick agreement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intractability - the quality of being hard to manage or persuade
  • Synonym: compliance - willingness to conform or yield

Etymology

From Latin tractabilis (that may be touched or handled), from tractare (to handle).
