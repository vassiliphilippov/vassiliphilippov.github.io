Allium

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial and biennial bulbous plants including garlic, onion, leek, and chive, known for their pungent flavor.
genus of pungent bulbous plants (garlic, onion, etc.)

Examples

Many species in the genus Allium are cultivated for their strong flavor and culinary use.
Botanists classify both onions and garlic under the Allium genus.
The garden features several ornamental Allium varieties that bloom in late spring.

Etymology

From Latin allium (garlic), of uncertain origin.
