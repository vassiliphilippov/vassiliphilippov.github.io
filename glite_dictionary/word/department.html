department

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
an administrative and territorial division in some countries, such as France or Colombia
administrative division (France, Colombia, etc.)

Examples

The French region of Brittany is divided into four departments.
Each department in Colombia is headed by a governor.
The reforms aimed to increase the autonomy of local departments.

Etymology

From Old French departement, from departir ‘to divide’

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑɹtmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
a specialized area of knowledge, activity, or study within a broader subject or organization
specialized area of knowledge or activity

Examples

Baking is not my department, but I can help decorate the cake.
She is highly respected in the department of molecular biology.
The university recently established a department dedicated to environmental studies.

Synset Examples

baking is not my department

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field - an area of study or activity
  • Synonym: discipline - a branch of knowledge or learning

Etymology

From Old French *departement* (division), from *départir* (to divide).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
A specialized division within a large organization such as a business, university, or government, responsible for a particular area of work.
division of an organization

Examples

She works in the department of Biology at the university.
The customer service department handles all the complaints and inquiries.
Each department in the company submits a monthly report.

Synset Examples

you'll find it in the hardware department
she got a job in the historical section of the Treasury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: section - a distinct part of an organization or document

Etymology

From Old French 'departement', from 'departir' (to divide), from Latin 'departire'.
