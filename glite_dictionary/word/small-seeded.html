small-seeded

1. ADJ

/ smɔl ˈsidɪd / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant or crop) having relatively small seeds compared to others of its kind.
having relatively small seeds

Examples

The small-seeded variety tends to mature faster than the large-seeded one.
Researchers are developing small-seeded crops that are more resilient to drought.
Farmers prefer small-seeded grains because they are easier to process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: large-seeded - having relatively large seeds
  • Antonym: seedless - having no seeds
