Dioscoreaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əsˌkɔːriˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˌdɪ.əˌskɔː.riˈeɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants commonly known as the yam family, which includes the true yams and related species.
the yam plant family (Dioscoreaceae)

Examples

The Dioscoreaceae family includes many species that are cultivated for their edible tubers.
Scientists study the Dioscoreaceae to better understand the evolution of monocot plants.
Members of the Dioscoreaceae can be found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - A starchy tuber from plants of the genus Dioscorea, part of this family

Etymology

From genus name Dioscorea, after the Greek physician Dioscorides, plus botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌɑskoʊriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / daɪˌɒskɔːriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants typically known as the yams, including many climbing or twining species producing edible tubers.
family of yam plants

Examples

The Dioscoreaceae family includes several species cultivated for their edible tubers.
Botanists recently discovered a new genus within the Dioscoreaceae.
Most commercial yams sold worldwide belong to the Dioscoreaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - edible starchy tuber produced by plants in the Dioscoreaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Dioscorea, named after the ancient Greek physician Dioscorides.
