Emberiza

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɛrɪzə / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɛrɪzə / (BrE)
a genus of small passerine birds known as Old World buntings, found across Europe, Asia, and Africa
genus of Old World buntings

Examples

Many species within the genus Emberiza are characterized by their distinctive head patterns and melodious songs.
The yellowhammer, scientifically known as Emberiza citrinella, is common throughout Europe.
Field guides often include identification keys for various Emberiza buntings observed in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small seed-eating songbird of the family Emberizidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the German 'Emberitze' meaning 'bunting'.

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɛrɪzə / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɛrɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the bunting family found in the Old World, commonly known as Old World buntings.
genus of Old World buntings

Examples

The Emberiza genus includes many attractive and melodious species.
Researchers observed a rare Emberiza in the protected wetland.
Several members of the Emberiza genus migrate across Europe every year.

Etymology

From New Latin Emberiza, from Old High German amari (bunting).
