oratory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrətɔːri / (AmE), / ˈɒrətəri / (BrE)
the art or practice of formal public speaking, especially in a grand or rhetorical style
formal or rhetorical public speaking

Examples

The politician's oratory moved the crowd to give a standing ovation.
He studied oratory in order to improve his public speaking skills.
Some leaders are remembered as much for their oratory as their actions.

Synset Examples

he loved the sound of his own oratory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetoric - the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing
  • Synonym: speech - a formal address or discourse delivered to an audience

Etymology

From Latin oratoria (the art of speaking), from orator (speaker).
