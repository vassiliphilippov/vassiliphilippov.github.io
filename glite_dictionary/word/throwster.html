throwster

1. NOUN

/ ˈθroʊstər / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊstə / (BrE)
a person who twists silk or rayon filaments into thread or yarn
worker who twists filaments into yarn

Examples

The silk factory hired a skilled throwster to produce fine threads for luxury fabrics.
In the early 20th century, becoming a throwster was a common job in textile towns.
A throwster expertly combines delicate filaments to create strong, uniform yarn.

Etymology

From 'throw', an old term for twisting silk, plus the agent suffix '-ster'.
