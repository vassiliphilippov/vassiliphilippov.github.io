reactive

1. ADJ

/ riˈæktɪv / (AmE), / riˈæktɪv / (BrE)
undergoing or participating readily in chemical or other types of reactions
readily undergoing reactions

Examples

Sodium is a highly reactive metal and must be stored carefully.
Some gases become more reactive at higher temperatures.
Chlorine is much more reactive than nitrogen under normal conditions.

Synset Examples

sodium is a reactive metal
free radicals are very reactive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inert - not tending to react chemically

Etymology

From 'react' + '-ive', patterned after 'active'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01935007-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ riˈæktɪv / (AmE), / riˈæktɪv / (BrE)
responding readily to a stimulus or influence; showing a response or reaction when stimulated or affected by something.
responding to a stimulus or influence

Examples

Some people have highly reactive immune systems that trigger allergies easily.
Chemically reactive substances must be handled with great caution in the laboratory.
Her skin became more reactive to sunlight after the treatment.

Synset Examples

the skin of old persons is less reactive than that of younger persons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsive - reacting quickly and positively; synonym for responsive to stimuli
  • Antonym: passive - not reacting visibly to something that might be expected to produce manifestations of an emotion or feeling; antonym for reactive

Etymology

From Latin 'reactivus', from 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02112456-s
Synset POS: s
