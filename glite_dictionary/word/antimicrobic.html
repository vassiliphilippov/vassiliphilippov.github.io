antimicrobic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkroʊ.bɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkrəʊ.bɪk / (BrE)
An agent, such as a chemical, heat, or radiation, that destroys or inhibits the growth of microorganisms.
agent that destroys microorganisms
medical

Examples

The hospital uses a powerful antimicrobic to sterilize surgical instruments.
Exposure to ultraviolet light acts as an effective antimicrobic in water purification systems.
The new toothpaste contains an antimicrobic to help prevent gum disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimicrobial - agent that kills or inhibits microorganisms
  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections
  • Synonym: disinfectant - a chemical used to destroy microorganisms on surfaces

Etymology

From anti- (against) and microbe (from French microbe, from Greek mikros 'small' + bios 'life').

2. ADJ

/ ˌænti.maɪˈkroʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌænti.maɪˈkrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
Capable of destroying or inhibiting the growth of disease-causing microorganisms.
destroying or inhibiting microorganisms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antimicrobic ointment to treat the infection.
This laboratory tests the antimicrobic activity of new pharmaceutical compounds.
Some essential oils have notable antimicrobic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimicrobial - destroying or inhibiting microorganisms

Etymology

From anti- (against) + microbe (microorganism) + -ic (forming adjectives).
