chandelier

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃæn.dəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌʃæn.dəˈlɪə / (BrE)
a large, often ornate, light fixture with branches for several lights, designed to hang from a ceiling
ornate ceiling light fixture

Examples

A sparkling chandelier hung above the grand dining table, casting light across the room.
They replaced the old light with a modern chandelier to brighten the entryway.
The crystals on the chandelier reflected colorful patterns onto the walls.

Etymology

From French 'chandelier' (candlestick, chandelier), from Latin 'candelabrum' (candlestick).
