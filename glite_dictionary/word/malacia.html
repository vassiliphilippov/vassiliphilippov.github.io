malacia

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪʃə / (AmE), / məˈleɪsɪə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by abnormal softening of bodily tissue, often used in medical terminology.
abnormal softening of tissue (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with malacia after tests revealed softening of the cartilage.
Several diseases can cause malacia in different tissues of the body.
Laryngomalacia is a specific type of malacia affecting the larynx in infants.

Etymology

From Greek malakia (softness), from malakos (soft).
