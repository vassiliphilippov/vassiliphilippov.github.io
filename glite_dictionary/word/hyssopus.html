Hyssopus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈsɑːpəs / (AmE), / hɪˈsɒpəs / (BrE)
a genus of Eurasian perennial herbs or subshrubs in the mint family, including the common herb hyssop
genus of Eurasian perennial herbs

Examples

Hyssopus species are widely cultivated for their aromatic leaves and traditional medicinal uses.
The garden showcased several herbs, including Hyssopus growing near the lavender and sage.
Researchers are studying the essential oils extracted from Hyssopus for potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyssop - an aromatic herb (Hyssopus officinalis) used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'hyssopus', from Greek 'hyssōpos', referring to the hyssop herb.
