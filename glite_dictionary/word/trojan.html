Trojan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient city of Troy, especially as mentioned in classical literature
native of ancient Troy

Examples

Homer's Iliad tells the story of the war between the Greeks and the Trojans.
According to legend, the Trojans were famed for their courage and endurance.
Many artifacts have been found in the area believed to be the ancient city of the Trojans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greek - a native or inhabitant of ancient Greece, often opposed to Trojans in classical literature

Etymology

From Latin 'Troianus', from 'Troia' (Troy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtroʊdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən / (BrE)
of or relating to the ancient city of Troy or its inhabitants
relating to Troy or its people

Examples

The archaeologist unearthed Trojan artifacts that shed new light on ancient history.
She wrote her thesis on Trojan society and its role in classical literature.
The Trojan War is a central event in Greek mythology.

Synset Examples

Trojan cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trojan horse - a strategy or device of subterfuge, referencing the wooden horse used by the Greeks during the Trojan War

Etymology

From Latin Troianus, from Troia (Troy), the ancient city.
