Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac

1. PROPN

/ ˌʒoʊzəf luːˈi ɡeɪ lʊˈsæk / (AmE), / ˌʒəʊzɛf luːˈiː ɡeɪ lʊˈsæk / (BrE)
(1778–1850) A French chemist and physicist known for isolating boron and for formulating Gay-Lussac's law on the behavior of gases at constant pressure.
French chemist and physicist (1778–1850)

Examples

Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac made significant contributions to the understanding of gases in the early 19th century.
The isolation of boron was one of Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac's notable scientific achievements.
Many chemistry students learn about Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac and his experiments with gases in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gay-Lussac's law - law relating the pressure and temperature of a gas
