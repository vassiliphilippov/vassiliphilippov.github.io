Cosmocampus profundus

1. NOUN

/ kɑz.moʊˈkæm.pəs prəˈfʌn.dəs / (AmE), / kɒz.məʊˈkæm.pəs prəˈfʌn.dəs / (BrE)
A species of pipefish about 8 inches long, found in waters from eastern Florida to the western Caribbean.
a pipefish species from the Caribbean region

Examples

The Cosmocampus profundus thrives in the warm Caribbean waters.
Marine biologists recently observed the elusive Cosmocampus profundus off the coast of Florida.
Unlike its relatives, the Cosmocampus profundus prefers deeper reef habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmos' (order, universe) and Latin 'campus' (field); 'profundus' meaning 'deep' in Latin, referring to its habitat.
