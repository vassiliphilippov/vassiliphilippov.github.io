inhibitory

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhɪbətɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪtəri / (BrE)
tending to restrain, restrict, or slow down an action, process, or response.
restraining or restricting action

Examples

The medication has an inhibitory effect on the growth of bacteria.
His inhibitory attitude prevented the team from trying new strategies.
Strict rules can have an inhibitory impact on creative thinking.

Synset Examples

a repressive regime
an overly strict and inhibitory discipline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - serving or tending to restrict
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior

Etymology

From Latin inhibere, meaning 'to hold in, restrain,' plus the suffix -ory indicating 'having the function of.'
