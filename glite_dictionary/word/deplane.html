to deplane

1. VERB

/ diˈpleɪn / (AmE), / diˈpleɪn / (BrE)
to leave or disembark from an aircraft after a flight
disembark from an aircraft

Examples

Passengers are asked to deplane in an orderly fashion.
We had to wait nearly twenty minutes before we could deplane at the gate.
Once the aircraft landed, the crew instructed everyone to deplane quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to embark - to board a vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship
  • Synonym: to disembark - to leave a vehicle, ship, or aircraft

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (indicating removal or reversal) and 'plane' (airplane).
