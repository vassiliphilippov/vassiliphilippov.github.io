brutish

1. ADJ

/ ˈbruː.tɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbruː.tɪʃ / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a brute; showing insensitivity or lack of human feeling
showing lack of human sensibility

Examples

The prisoners endured brutish treatment during their captivity.
His brutish behavior at the meeting shocked everyone.
She recoiled from the brutish noise coming from the alley.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bestial - of or like an animal or beast; lacking reason
  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

From brute + -ish; 'brute' from Latin brutus (dull, insensible).
