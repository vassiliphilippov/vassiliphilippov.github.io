plantation

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(historical) A newly established colony or settlement, especially in the context of colonization of North America or Ireland.
historical colony or settlement

Examples

In the 17th century, many English settlers sailed to America to join the plantation in Virginia.
Convicts were sometimes sent to serve on distant plantations as part of their punishment.
Historical records describe the establishment of the Ulster plantation in Ireland.

Synset Examples

the practice of sending convicted criminals to serve on the Plantations was common in the 17th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial political control of another country

Etymology

From Latin 'plantatio' (a planting), through Old French and Middle English; sense of 'colony' developed in reference to planted settlements.

2. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A garden or area where trees have been planted in a deliberate arrangement, typically forming a small wood without undergrowth.
area of planted trees without undergrowth

Examples

The old estate featured a plantation of oak trees with grass between the trunks.
On weekends, they walked through the plantation admiring the neat rows of birches.
A small plantation was established near the school to provide shade and beauty.

Etymology

From Latin 'plantatio' (a planting), from 'plantare' (to plant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03468764-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A large estate on which crops such as coffee, sugar, or rubber are grown, especially in tropical or subtropical regions, often for commercial purposes.
large estate growing cash crops

Examples

The old cotton plantation stretched for miles across the countryside.
Many workers were employed to harvest timber on the tea plantation.
The plantation economy shaped much of the region's history and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farm - area of land for growing crops and raising animals

Etymology

From Latin 'plantatio' (a planting), via Old French 'plantation'.
