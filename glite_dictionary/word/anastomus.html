Anastomus

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæs.tə.məs / (AmE), / əˈnastəməs / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds in the stork family, commonly known as openbills, found in Asia and Africa.
genus of storks called openbills

Examples

The African Anastomus is often seen near lakes and marshes.
Scientists observed the breeding habits of Anastomus in Southeast Asia.
Unlike other storks, Anastomus feeds primarily on aquatic snails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: openbill - common name for birds of the genus Anastomus

Etymology

From Greek anastomoun, meaning 'to furnish with a mouth or opening', referring to the bird’s gapped bill.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnæstəˌmoʊs / (AmE), / əˈnæstəˌməs / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds known as openbills, found in tropical Asia and Africa, with distinctive bills that do not close completely.
genus of birds called openbills

Examples

The genus Anastomus includes birds commonly known as openbills due to their uniquely shaped beaks.
Researchers observed that Anastomus species primarily feed on snails and other mollusks.
The African Anastomus can be distinguished by the gap in its bill when closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: openbill - A type of stork with a distinctive gap between the mandibles

Etymology

From Greek anastomos (having a mouth or opening).
