splotch

1. NOUN

/ splɑtʃ / (AmE), / splɒtʃ / (BrE)
a large, irregularly shaped mark or spot, especially one of color or dirt
irregularly shaped spot or mark

Examples

There was a bright red splotch on his shirt where the tomato sauce had landed.
A dark splotch appeared on the ceiling after the heavy rainfall.
She tried to wash out the paint splotch from her jeans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture
  • Synonym: blotch - a large, irregular patch or stain, typically of color or ink
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04702157-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ splɑtʃ / (AmE), / splɒtʃ / (BrE)
to mark or cover with irregular spots or blotches, typically of color or liquid
to mark with blotches or spots

Examples

She accidentally splotched paint on her new jeans while working on the mural.
The children splotched mud all over the clean kitchen floor after playing outside.
He splotched blue ink across the paper, leaving messy marks everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blotch - to cover or mark with blotches
