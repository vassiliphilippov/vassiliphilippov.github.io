acerose

1. ADJ

/ ˈæsɚˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈæsərəʊs / (BrE)
narrow, long, and pointed like a needle; especially referring to the leaves of certain plants such as pines.
needle-shaped (botanical, esp. pine leaves)

Examples

The pine tree's acerose leaves help it retain water in dry climates.
Many conifers have acerose foliage adapted to cold environments.
The acerose needles of fir trees make them easy to distinguish from broad-leaved species.

Etymology

From Latin 'acerosus', from 'acus' (needle).
