chlorofucin

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˈfjuːsɪn / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˈfjuːsɪn / (BrE)
a type of chlorophyll found in brown algae, diatoms, and flagellates
chlorophyll in brown algae and some protists
medical

Examples

Brown algae contain chlorofucin, which allows them to absorb light at different wavelengths than land plants.
Researchers discovered that chlorofucin plays a crucial role in the photosynthesis of diatoms.
Unlike the chlorophyll found in green plants, chlorofucin is unique to some algae and flagellates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorophyll - the green pigment found in plants and algae

Etymology

From 'chloro-' meaning green and 'fucin', after the genus Fucus (a type of brown algae).
