mendacious

1. ADJ

/ mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
habitually lying; not telling the truth; dishonest
habitually dishonest or untruthful

Examples

The politician's mendacious statements damaged her credibility.
His reputation as a mendacious employee made it hard for him to find a new job.
She grew tired of her friend's mendacious excuses for being late.

Synset Examples

a mendacious child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truthful - habitually telling the truth
  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; deceiving or fraudulent

Etymology

From Latin mendax, mendacis (lying, false).

2. ADJ

/ mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
not telling the truth; deliberately lying.
deliberately untruthful

Examples

The politician was known for his mendacious claims during the campaign.
Her mendacious account of the events was quickly uncovered by reporters.
It is dangerous to trust someone with a mendacious reputation.

Synset Examples

a mendacious statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truthful - telling the truth; honest
  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; intending to mislead or cheat

Etymology

From Latin mendax, mendac- (lying, false).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02471317-s
Synset POS: s
