teacher

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃə / (BrE)
something (such as an experience, thing, or abstraction) that imparts knowledge or instructs, especially in a figurative or personified sense.
a non-human thing that instructs; personified

Examples

For many, failure has been the greatest teacher of all.
Travel can be a valuable teacher, opening your eyes to new perspectives.
Time is often a patient teacher that reveals important truths about ourselves.

Synset Examples

books were his teachers
experience is a demanding teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructor - a person or thing that teaches
  • Synonym: mentor - a trusted guide or teacher, especially in a figurative sense
  • Antonym: student - someone who is taught or learns

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to teach, especially in a school or college.
person whose job is teaching

Examples

Her favorite teacher inspired her to become a scientist.
The teacher explained the lesson clearly to the class.
He worked as a math teacher for over twenty years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructor - a person who teaches, especially in a college or university
  • Antonym: student - a person who is studying, especially formally at a school or university

Etymology

From Old English 'tæcan' (to teach) + '-er' (agent suffix).
