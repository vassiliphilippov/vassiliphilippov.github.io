churchyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃˌjɑrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃˌjɑːd / (BrE)
an area of ground adjoining a church, often used as a burial ground
ground surrounding a church, often for burials

Examples

The old churchyard is filled with moss-covered headstones dating back centuries.
Visitors strolled quietly through the churchyard, paying their respects to loved ones.
At dusk, the churchyard took on an eerie, peaceful atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graveyard - a place where people are buried, especially near a church

Etymology

From Middle English 'chirche-yerd', combining 'church' + 'yard'.
