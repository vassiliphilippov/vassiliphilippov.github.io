jeopardy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛpɚdi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛpədi / (BrE)
danger of harm, loss, or injury; a situation in which there is a risk of loss or danger.
danger of harm or loss

Examples

Her careless actions put the entire operation in jeopardy.
The species is in jeopardy due to habitat loss.
Failing the test could place your scholarship in jeopardy.

Synset Examples

drinking alcohol is a health hazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or risk

Etymology

From Old French 'jeu parti' (a divided game or contest), later interpreted in English as 'jeopardy.'
