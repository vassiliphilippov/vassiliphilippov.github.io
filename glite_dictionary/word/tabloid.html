tabloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a newspaper with pages that are about half the size of those of a standard newspaper, often focusing on sensational news and entertainment
small-format sensational newspaper

Examples

The celebrity scandal was splashed across the front page of the tabloid.
Many people read a tabloid for its gossip and entertainment news.
The tabloids were full of sensational headlines during the election.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broadsheet - a newspaper with large pages considered more serious or authoritative

Etymology

From 'tablet' via the name of a medicine in compressed tablet form, later transferred to the compressed (smaller) format of newspapers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtæb.lɔɪd / (BrE)
A newspaper or magazine focusing on sensational stories, gossip, or entertainment, often with little serious news.
sensationalist newspaper or magazine

Examples

Many people read the tabloid for its celebrity gossip and eye-catching headlines.
She doesn't trust news reported in a tabloid because of its sensational style.
The front page of the tabloid featured another shocking story about a famous actor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broadsheet - a newspaper with more serious content, printed on large sheets

Etymology

From tablet, in reference to the small format of such newspapers; coined in late 19th century.
