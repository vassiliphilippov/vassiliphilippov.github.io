paddlewheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædəlˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈpædəlˌwiːl / (BrE)
a large wheel with paddles attached around its edge, turned by an engine to propel a boat through water
engine-driven wheel with paddles for boat propulsion

Examples

The steamboat's paddlewheel churned the water as it traveled down the river.
Tourists flocked to see the historic vessel with its iconic paddlewheel.
When the paddlewheel broke, the boat drifted helplessly until repairs could be made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller - device with blades used for propulsion in water or air

Etymology

From 'paddle' (oar with a broad blade) + 'wheel'.
