Anaspida

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæspɪdə / (AmE), / əˈnaspɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct order of jawless prehistoric fishes known for their streamlined bodies and absence of bony shields, primarily from the Silurian and Devonian periods.
extinct order of jawless vertebrates

Examples

The discovery of Anaspida fossils provided valuable insights into the evolution of early vertebrates.
In paleontology, Anaspida are recognized for their distinctive lack of armored plates.
Many specimens of Anaspida have been unearthed in Silurian and Devonian rock formations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostracoderm - extinct armored jawless fish

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + aspis (shield), referring to their lack of bony armor.
