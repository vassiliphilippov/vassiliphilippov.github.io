brownstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a reddish-brown sandstone commonly used for building, especially in the construction of row houses
a reddish-brown building stone

Examples

Many historic homes in Brooklyn are built with brownstone.
The facade was restored using original brownstone materials.
Architects prized brownstone for its durability and warm color in urban landscapes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A townhouse or row house built of reddish-brown sandstone, especially common in northeastern US cities.
a house built of reddish-brown sandstone

Examples

Many families dream of owning a brownstone in Brooklyn.
The architect restored the old brownstone to its original charm.
Rows of brownstone houses line the city streets.

Etymology

From 'brown' + 'stone', referring to the reddish-brown sandstone used in construction.
