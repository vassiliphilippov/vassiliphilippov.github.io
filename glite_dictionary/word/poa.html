Poa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly perennial grasses found in cool temperate regions, including species such as bluegrass.
a genus of grasses, including bluegrass

Examples

The Poa genus includes many species common in lawns and pastures.
Botanists identified the grass as a member of Poa.
Kentucky bluegrass, a type of Poa, is valued for its lush appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *poa* (grass, herbage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly perennial grasses found in cool temperate regions, including common bluegrasses.
a genus of perennial grasses

Examples

Several species within the Poa genus are popular in lawns and sports fields.
Kentucky bluegrass is probably the most well-known Poa in North America.
The botanist examined samples of Poa found in the high-altitude pasture.

Etymology

From Latin 'poa', from Greek 'poa' meaning 'grass'.
