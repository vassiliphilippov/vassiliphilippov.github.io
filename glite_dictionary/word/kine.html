kine

1. NOUN

/ kaɪn / (AmE), / kaɪn / (BrE)
(Archaic) Cows or cattle collectively; domesticated bovine animals as a group, regardless of sex or age.
archaic plural of 'cow'; cattle
archaic

Examples

The field was filled with grazing kine under the summer sun.
In the ancient story, seven fat kine were followed by seven thin one.
The farmer counted his kine before bringing them into the barn for the night.

Synset Examples

so many head of cattle
wait till the cows come home
seven thin and ill-favored kine
a team of oxen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle - large domesticated bovine animals (group)
  • Degree Lower: cow - adult female domesticated bovine

Etymology

Old English cȳna, plural of cū (cow).
