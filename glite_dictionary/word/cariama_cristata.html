Cariama cristata

1. NOUN

/ kɑːriˈɑːmə krɪˈsteɪtə / (AmE), / kəˈriːəmə krɪˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
A large, long-legged South American bird, commonly known as the Red-legged Seriema, which is the only extant species in the genus Cariama.
South American bird (Red-legged Seriema)

Examples

The Cariama cristata is known for its loud, distinctive calls that can be heard across the grasslands.
Ornithologists often study the Cariama cristata to understand its unique hunting and nesting behaviors.
I saw a Cariama cristata while traveling through the Brazilian savanna last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Red-legged Seriema - common English name for Cariama cristata

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus and species name; 'Cariama' from a native Brazilian word, 'cristata' from Latin meaning 'crested'.
