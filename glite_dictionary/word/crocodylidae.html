Crocodylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑː.kəˈdɪl.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɒ.kəˈdɪl.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, carnivorous reptiles commonly known as the true crocodiles, native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia.
family of true crocodiles

Examples

The family Crocodylidae includes several species of true crocodiles, such as the Nile crocodile and the saltwater crocodile.
Unlike alligators, members of Crocodylidae generally have narrower, V-shaped snouts.
Paleontologists study fossil remains of Crocodylidae to learn about their evolution and adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws
  • Antonym: Alligatoridae - family containing true alligators and caimans

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Crocodylus' (crocodile) + '-idae', a suffix denoting animal families.
