referent

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛfərənt / (BrE)
the thing or entity that a word, phrase, or symbol refers to in a specific context
thing or entity referred to by a term

Examples

In the sentence 'The cat sat on the mat,' the referent of 'the cat' is a specific animal.
Linguists study the relationship between words and their referents in the real world.
The precise referent of the pronoun 'it' was unclear in that paragraph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - the act of referring to something
  • Synonym: antecedent - a word, phrase, or clause to which another word refers

Etymology

From Latin *referentem*, present participle of *referre* (to refer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛfərənt / (BrE)
the person or thing to which a word or phrase refers, especially in logic or linguistics, where it is the first term in a proposition to which others are related
thing or person a word refers to

Examples

In the sentence 'John runs', the referent of 'John' is the person performing the action.
The word 'tree' can have any number of physical trees as its referent.
The philosopher explained how each term in a proposition must have a specific referent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - the act of mentioning or alluding to something; also in linguistics, the relationship between language and what it refers to

Etymology

From Latin referent-, present participle of referre (to bring back, refer).

3. NOUN

/ rəˈfɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛfərənt / (BrE)
the thing or person to which a word, phrase, or symbol refers
thing/person a word refers to

Examples

In the sentence 'The cat sat on the mat,' the referent of 'cat' is the actual animal.
Sometimes the referent of a pronoun can be unclear without more context.
The word 'Paris' has the city in France as its primary referent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - the act of mentioning or alluding to something

From Latin referent-, present participle of referre (to bring back).
