tabernacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæbərˌnækəl / (AmE), / ˈtabənakəl / (BrE)
(in Judaism) The portable sanctuary used by the Israelites during their exodus to house the Ark of the Covenant.
portable sanctuary for the Ark (Judaism)

Examples

During their journey through the wilderness, the Israelites constructed a tabernacle to house the Ark of the Covenant.
The Bible describes the intricate details of the tabernacle built under Moses's leadership.
Archaeologists study ancient texts to better understand how the tabernacle was assembled and used by the Hebrews.

Etymology

From Latin 'tabernaculum' (tent, hut, booth), diminutive of 'taberna' (hut, tavern).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæbɚˌnækəl / (AmE), / ˈtæbənækəl / (BrE)
a large building used for worship and other religious gatherings by members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, especially the Salt Lake Tabernacle
a Mormon place of worship

Examples

The choir performed in the historic tabernacle in Salt Lake City.
Many visitors tour the famous Mormon tabernacle for its unique architecture and acoustics.
The annual conference is held at the LDS tabernacle downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temple - a building dedicated to religious worship

Etymology

From Latin *tabernaculum* (tent, hut), originally referring to a tent sanctuary in the Hebrew Bible.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæbərˌnækəl / (AmE), / ˈtabəˌnakl / (BrE)
A place of worship for a Jewish congregation; also, a movable dwelling place for the divine presence, especially as described in the Hebrew Bible.
Jewish place of worship; biblical sanctuary

Examples

The ancient Israelites carried the tabernacle with them during their journey through the desert.
Many communities gathered in the tabernacle to worship and celebrate religious festivals.
The museum displays a model of the biblical tabernacle as described in the Old Testament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synagogue - A Jewish house of worship, often having facilities for religious instruction

Etymology

From Latin tabernaculum (tent, hut, booth), from taberna (hut, tavern).
