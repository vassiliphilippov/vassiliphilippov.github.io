phlegmatizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛɡməˌtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈflɛɡməˌtaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance added to an explosive to reduce its sensitivity to detonation, making it safer to handle and transport
material used to stabilize explosives

Examples

The addition of a phlegmatizer can significantly improve the safety of transporting industrial explosives.
Scientists tested several chemicals to find the most effective phlegmatizer for the new propellant system.
Without a phlegmatizer, the explosive compound would be too unstable for commercial use.

Etymology

From Greek phlegma (inflammation, heat) and the suffix -izer, indicating one who or that which causes a specified thing.
