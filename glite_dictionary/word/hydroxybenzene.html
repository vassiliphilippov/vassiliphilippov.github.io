hydroxybenzene

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksiˈbɛnzin / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
a toxic white crystalline compound derived from benzene, used in manufacturing, disinfectants, and antiseptics; also known as phenol
toxic compound used in industry and disinfectants
medical

Examples

Exposure to hydroxybenzene can cause skin irritation and severe health effects if inhaled or ingested.
Many industrial processes use hydroxybenzene as a precursor for producing synthetic resins and plastics.
The laboratory stored hydroxybenzene in tightly sealed containers due to its toxic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - alternative name for hydroxybenzene

Etymology

Formed from 'hydroxy-' (containing hydroxyl) and 'benzene'.
