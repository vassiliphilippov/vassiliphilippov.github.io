scorpaenid

1. NOUN

/ skɔˈrpiːənɪd / (AmE), / skɔːˈrpiːənɪd / (BrE)
Any of various carnivorous, usually bottom-dwelling marine fishes of the family Scorpaenidae, found mainly in warm waters, especially in the Pacific Ocean.
warm-water bottom-dwelling marine fish (Scorpaenidae)

Examples

The venomous spines of the scorpaenid serve as an effective defense against predators.
Several species of scorpaenid are popular among aquarium enthusiasts for their striking appearance.
The marine biologist collected a scorpaenid from the coral reef to study its unique adaptations.

Etymology

From modern Latin Scorpaenidae, from Greek skorpaínē (a kind of fish).
