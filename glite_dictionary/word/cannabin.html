cannabin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.ə.bɪn / (AmE), / ˈkan.ə.bɪn / (BrE)
a resin obtained from the hemp plant, believed to be the active narcotic component of marijuana
resin from hemp, narcotic agent
medical

Examples

Scientists isolated cannabin from the hemp plant to study its properties.
The presence of cannabin is a key factor in the psychoactive effects of marijuana.
Regulations regarding cannabin vary in different countries due to its narcotic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabis - the hemp plant from which cannabin is derived
  • Synonym: marijuana - the drug containing the active narcotic agent from hemp

Etymology

From Latin 'cannabis' (hemp), with scientific formation.
