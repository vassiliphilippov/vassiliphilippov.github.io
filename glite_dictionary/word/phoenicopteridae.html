Phoenicopteridae

1. NOUN

/ finɪˌkɑːmptəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / fɪˌnɪkɒmptəˈrɪdɪiː / (BrE)
a family of large wading birds known as flamingos, characterized by their long legs, necks, and pink or reddish plumage
the flamingo family of birds

Examples

Members of the Phoenicopteridae family are easily recognized by their distinctive pink plumage and curved bills.
The wetlands are an important habitat for several species within the Phoenicopteridae.
Scientists observed mating rituals among the Phoenicopteridae during the spring migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamingo - a tall wading bird with pink or reddish feathers

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phoenicopterus' (type genus) + '-idae', used for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ finɪˌkɑptəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / fiːnɪˌkɒptəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wading birds known as flamingos, characterized by their long legs, necks, and distinctive pink coloring.
family of birds; flamingos

Examples

The Phoenicopteridae are easily recognized by their striking pink plumage and long legs.
There are six living species within the family Phoenicopteridae.
Scientists study Phoenicopteridae to learn more about the unique adaptations of flamingos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamingo - large pink or red wading bird in the family Phoenicopteridae

Etymology

From Latin scientific name Phoenicopteridae, from Greek phoinikopteros (flamingo) + -idae (family suffix in taxonomy).
