retroversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtrəˈvɝʒən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of translating a text back into its original language
translation back into the original language

Examples

For their assignment, students were asked to perform a retroversion of the English passage back into Latin.
The accuracy of the retroversion was crucial for understanding the original intent of the author.
Linguists sometimes use retroversion to reconstruct lost parts of ancient texts.

Synset Examples

the teacher translated Latin texts into English which he gave to his students for retroversion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: translation - the act of rendering text from one language into another

Etymology

From Latin retroversio (a turning back), from retro (back) + verto (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a return to a previous or earlier state, condition, or style
return to a former state

Examples

After several reforms, the country's policies seemed to undergo a retroversion to earlier, more restrictive practices.
The sudden retroversion of the software erased all the improvements from the latest update.
Critics feared the new leadership would prompt a retroversion of social attitudes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversion - a return to a previous state or condition
  • Antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving gradually towards a more advanced state

Etymology

From Latin 'retroversio' (turning back), from 'retro-' (back) + 'vertere' (to turn).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the backward turning or tilting of an organ or body part, especially the uterus
backward tilting of a body part
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed a retroversion of the uterus that required careful monitoring.
Doctors explained that retroversion can sometimes cause discomfort but is often harmless.
In some cases, retroversion of the pelvic organs may go unnoticed for years.

Synset Examples

retroversion of the uterus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anteversion - forward tilting of an organ or body part

Etymology

From Latin retroversio(n-), from retro- (backward) + vertere (to turn).
