Euphorbia corollata

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔr.bi.ə kəˌrɑːˈleɪ.t̬ə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔː.bɪ.ə kɒˌrɒl.əˈtɑː / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of the spurge family, with showy white bracts resembling petals.
a North American perennial spurge with white bracts

Examples

The Euphorbia corollata bloomed profusely in the meadow, displaying its distinctive white bracts.
Gardeners often plant Euphorbia corollata for its resilience and attractive summer flowers.
You can identify Euphorbia corollata by its clusters of tiny flowers surrounded by white, petallike bracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - a plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Euphorbia' and species 'corollata', meaning 'having a corolla' (flower whorl).
