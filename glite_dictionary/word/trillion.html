trillion

1. NOUN

/ trɪl.jən / (AmE), / trɪl.jən / (BrE)
The number one million million; written as 1,000,000,000,000 (1 followed by twelve zeros).
one million million (1,000,000,000,000)

Examples

The company is valued at over one trillion dollars.
A trillion stars fill our galaxy according to astronomers.
By 2050, the global population could reach almost ten trillion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: billion - one thousand million (1,000,000,000)
  • Degree Higher: quadrillion - one thousand trillion (1,000,000,000,000,000)

Etymology

From French 'trillion', based on 'million'.

6. NUM

/ ˈtrɪljən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪljən / (BrE)
The number one million million, written as 1,000,000,000,000 (10^12) in the United States.
one million million (10^12) in US usage

Examples

The national debt reached over fifteen trillion dollars last year.
Only a trillionth of a second passed before the reaction occurred.
Some scientists estimate there are more than a trillion stars in our galaxy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: billion - a thousand million in US English; a million million in British English (historically)
  • Degree Higher: quadrillion - one thousand trillion (10^15) in US English; one million billion in British English (historically)

Etymology

From French *trillion*, from Italian *trilione* (triple million), formed from *tri-* (three) + *-illion* (as in million, billion).

7. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪljən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪljən / (BrE)
(British, dated) The number represented as 1 followed by 18 zeros (1,000,000,000,000,000,000); a million million million.
one followed by 18 zeros (British, dated)
british archaic

Examples

In historic British texts, a trillion referred to a million million million, not simply a thousand billion.
The national debt would have been estimated in trillions under the old British system.
Scientific papers from the 19th century sometimes mention quantities as large as a trillion in this sense.

Synset Examples

in England they call a quintillion a ‘trillion’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 12 zeros (American, now also standard British)
  • Degree Lower: billion - the number represented as 1 followed by 12 zeros (in modern usage)
  • Degree Higher: quadrillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 24 zeros (British, dated)

Etymology

From French *trillion*, patterning on *million* and *billion*.
