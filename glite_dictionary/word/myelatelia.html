myelatelia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪələˈtɛliə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələˈtiːliə / (BrE)
a developmental defect of the spinal cord
spinal cord developmental defect
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with myelatelia, explaining the range of neurological symptoms.
Doctors are conducting research to better understand the causes of myelatelia in infants.
Early intervention is important in managing complications arising from myelatelia.

Etymology

From Greek 'myelos' (marrow, spinal cord) and 'atelos' (imperfect, incomplete).
