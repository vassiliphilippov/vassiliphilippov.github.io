dissimilative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪmɪlətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪlətɪv / (BrE)
Concealing or disguising the true nature of something, often with the intention to deceive.
concealing with intent to deceive

Examples

The spy relied on his dissimilative skills to remain undetected for years.
Her dissimilative response made everyone doubt her true intentions.
Politicians are often accused of using dissimilative tactics to mislead the public.

Synset Examples

dissimulative arts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissimulating - intentionally concealing or disguising the truth
  • Antonym: honest - telling the truth, not hiding or disguising feelings or facts

Etymology

From Latin dissimulatus, past participle of dissimulare (to disguise, conceal).
