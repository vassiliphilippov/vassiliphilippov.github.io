to shorten

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtn̩ / (BrE)
to become short or shorter in length, duration, or extent
become shorter in length or duration

Examples

As autumn approaches, the days shorten and the nights grow longer.
The skirt will shorten if you wash it in hot water.
With each passing year, the meetings seem to shorten in duration.

Synset Examples

In winter, the days shorten

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lengthen - to become longer

Etymology

From Middle English shortnen, from short + -en (verb suffix), ultimately from Old English sceort.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00317295-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtən / (BrE)
to make something shorter in duration, length, or scope while keeping its essential parts
make shorter in length or scope

Examples

The editor asked him to shorten the article for publication.
They will shorten the route to save time on the marathon day.
The speech was too long and had to be shortened to fit the schedule.

Synset Examples

The manuscript must be shortened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abridge - to shorten a text by removing parts but keeping meaning
  • Antonym: to lengthen - to make something longer in duration or extent

Etymology

From Old English scyrtan, related to 'short'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00244786-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈʃɔr.tn̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.tən / (BrE)
to make something shorter in length, duration, or extent
make shorter in length or time

Examples

The tailor will shorten your trousers if they're too long.
Exercising regularly can shorten the duration of a cold.
She decided to shorten her speech to fit the time limit.

Synset Examples

shorten the skirt
shorten the rope by a few inches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lengthen - to make or become longer
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

From Middle English 'shortnen', from 'short', based on the Old English 'sceort' (short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00561970-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtən / (BrE)
to make something shorter in length, amount, or duration; to reduce the size or extent of something
make shorter in length or duration

Examples

We may need to shorten the meeting if the schedule is tight.
Due to bad weather, the pilot decided to shorten the flight route.
You can shorten the pants by hemming them above the ankle.

Synset Examples

He shortened his trip due to illness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lengthen - to make longer in length or duration
  • Synonym: short - having little length, distance, or duration

Etymology

Formed from the word 'short' + the suffix '-en', dating from Middle English.
