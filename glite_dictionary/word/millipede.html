millipede

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌpid / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪpiːd / (BrE)
A small, elongated arthropod with a segmented body, most segments bearing two pairs of legs, feeding mainly on decaying vegetation.
arthropod with many legs

Examples

A millipede curls up tightly when threatened by predators.
We found a large millipede slowly making its way across the garden path.
Unlike centipedes, a millipede is not venomous and poses no danger to humans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centipede - arthropod with a similar body shape, each segment bears one pair of legs

Etymology

From Latin 'mille' (thousand) + 'pes, pedis' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌpid / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌpiːd / (BrE)
A small arthropod with a long, cylindrical body divided into many segments, most of which have two pairs of legs; millipedes are herbivorous and nonpoisonous.
herbivorous arthropod with many legs

Examples

A millipede curled up on the forest floor as it sensed danger nearby.
Unlike centipedes, the millipede moves slowly and feeds mainly on decaying plants.
We found a giant millipede in the garden after last night's rain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centipede - arthropod with a flatter body and one pair of legs per segment

Etymology

From Latin 'mille' (thousand) + 'pes, pedis' (foot).
