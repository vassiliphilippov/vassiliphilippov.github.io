Fungia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋ.gi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of free-living mushroom corals found chiefly in Indo-Pacific reefs, characterized by a flat, disk-like shape.
genus of mushroom corals

Examples

The reef was home to a diverse range of corals, including several Fungia species.
Marine biologists study Fungia to understand coral regeneration.
Individual polyps of Fungia can survive after separating from the main disc.

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom), referring to the coral's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of solitary mushroom corals found in tropical Indo-Pacific oceans, characterized by a flat, disc-like shape.
a genus of mushroom corals

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Fungia during their expedition to the coral reef.
The coral Fungia is known for its ability to move slowly across sandy substrates.
Unlike many other coral species, Fungia lives as a solitary polyp rather than in colonies.

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom), referring to the mushroom-like shape of the corals.
