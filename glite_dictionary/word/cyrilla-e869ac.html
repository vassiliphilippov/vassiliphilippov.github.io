Cyrilla

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪlə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering trees and shrubs, comprising a single species, known for having acute or twisted petals and wingless fruit, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Cyrilla genus comprises only a single recognized species in modern taxonomy.
Botanists discovered a rare specimen of Cyrilla in the subtropical forest.
Flowers of Cyrilla are distinctive for their twisted petals and fruit without wings.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, possibly named after Cyrillus, a historical personal name.
