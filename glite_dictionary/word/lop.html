to lop

1. VERB

/ lɑp / (AmE), / lɒp / (BrE)
to cut off branches, twigs, or parts of a plant or tree, especially to encourage growth or tidy its appearance
cut off branches or parts of a plant

Examples

It is important to lop the dead branches off the apple tree in early spring.
She had to lop several limbs to keep the hedge tidy and healthy.
After the storm, workers lopped off all the broken branches from the trees.

Synset Examples

dress the plants in the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prune - to trim a tree, shrub, or bush by cutting away dead or overgrown branches

Etymology

Probably from Middle English lopen, possibly of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian 'loppa' (to cut).

2. VERB

/ lɑp / (AmE), / lɒp / (BrE)
to cut off a part (such as a branch or limb) from something, especially with a single quick stroke
cut off part of something, especially a branch or limb

Examples

He used an axe to lop off the dead branches from the tree.
The gardener lopped the hedge to keep it neat and tidy.
She carefully lopped away the damaged leaves from her plant.

Synset Examples

His head was severed from his body
The soul discerped from the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prune - to trim by cutting away unwanted parts, especially branches

Etymology

Probably from Middle English lappen (to hang loosely, droop, or flap).
