to short-sheet

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɔrt ˌʃit / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt ˌʃiːt / (BrE)
to make a bed by folding the sheets in such a way that it appears normal but prevents someone from stretching out fully, typically as a prank.
prepare a bed as a prank by folding the sheets

Examples

We decided to short-sheet our friend's bed before he arrived for the weekend.
As a camp prank, the kids love to short-sheet each other's beds.
She laughed when she discovered that someone had short-sheeted her bed.

Synset Examples

The prankster short-sheeted the guest's bed last night.
I always short-sheet my brother's bed for fun.
They had a good laugh after I short-sheeted their beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a mischievous trick played on someone
