Dactylorhiza maculata fuchsii

1. NOUN

/ dæk.tɪ.lɔːˈraɪ.zə məˈkjuː.lə.tə ˈfʊk.si.aɪ / (AmE), / dæk.tɪ.lɔːˈraɪ.zə məˈkjuː.lə.tə ˈfʊk.si.aɪ / (BrE)
A subspecies of the heath spotted-orchid, native to Europe, with lance-shaped leaves spotted with purple and pink to white or mauve flowers marked with deep red or purple.
a European spotted orchid subspecies

Examples

The Dactylorhiza maculata fuchsii is often found thriving in moist European meadows during the summer.
Botanists admire the distinctive spotted leaves of Dactylorhiza maculata fuchsii when surveying wild orchids.
Hikers came across several clumps of Dactylorhiza maculata fuchsii blooming in the nature reserve.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Dactylorhiza and the species maculata; 'fuchsii' honors German botanist Leonhart Fuchs.
