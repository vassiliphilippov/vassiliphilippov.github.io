Calamagrostis

1. NOUN

/ kæləˌmæˈɡrɑstɪs / (AmE), / kæləˌmæˈɡrəʊstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses commonly known as reed grass, found in temperate regions and often growing in wetlands and meadows.
a genus of reed grasses

Examples

Several species of Calamagrostis are native to North America and are used in ornamental landscaping.
The wetland restoration project included planting Calamagrostis to help stabilize the soil.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Calamagrostis in different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed grass - any grass resembling a reed, especially of wet habitats

Etymology

From Greek *kalamos* (reed) + *agrostis* (a type of grass).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæləməˈɡrɑstɪs / (AmE), / ˌkaləməˈɡrɒstɪs / (BrE)
a genus of reed grasses found in temperate regions, especially in wetlands and meadows
a genus of reed grasses

Examples

The wetland was dominated by Calamagrostis, providing shelter for dozens of bird species.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Calamagrostis to monitor changes in the ecosystem.
A variety of Calamagrostis is often used for ornamental landscaping due to its tall, feathery plumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed grass - type of grass found near water

Etymology

From Greek kalamos (reed) and agrestis (of the field).
