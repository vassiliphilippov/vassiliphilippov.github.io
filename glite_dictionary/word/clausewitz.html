Clausewitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈklaʊzəˌvɪts / (AmE), / ˈklaʊzəvɪts / (BrE)
(1780–1831) Prussian general and influential military theorist best known for his doctrine that war is a continuation of politics by other means.
Prussian military theorist (1780–1831)

Examples

Many military academies require students to read Clausewitz as part of their curriculum.
The ideas of Clausewitz continue to influence modern strategic thinking.
She referenced Clausewitz when discussing the relationship between war and politics.

Etymology

From the surname of Carl von Clausewitz, German general and theorist.
