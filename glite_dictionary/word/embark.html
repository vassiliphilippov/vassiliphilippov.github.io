to embark

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to begin a course of action, especially one that is new, important, or involves risk
start something new or risky

Examples

The company decided to embark on a major expansion into new markets.
She was nervous but excited to embark on her first solo journey abroad.
Despite warnings, they chose to embark on a risky adventure through the mountains.

Synset Examples

We ventured into the world of high-tech and bought a supercomputer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venture - to undertake a risky or daring journey or course of action

Etymology

From Old French embarquer, from em- (in) + barque (boat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02378427-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to start or begin a significant project, activity, or course of action, often something new or challenging
begin something important or new

Examples

After careful consideration, she decided to embark on a new business venture.
Many students embark upon graduate studies immediately after college.
He is preparing to embark on a journey around the world.

Synset Examples

she embarked upon a new career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commence - to begin; to start (something)
  • Synonym: to undertake - to take on or begin a responsibility or task
  • Antonym: finish - to bring something to an end or completion

Etymology

From Old French 'embarquer', from 'em-' (in) + 'barque' (boat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00348906-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to go on board a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle; to begin a journey or enterprise
to go on board a vehicle or begin a journey

Examples

Passengers were asked to embark the cruise ship at 4:00 PM.
The group will embark on their journey to Antarctica tomorrow.
Before you embark, make sure you have all necessary documents with you.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle

Etymology

From French 'embarquer', from 'em-' (in) + 'barque' (boat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01983476-v
Synset POS: v
