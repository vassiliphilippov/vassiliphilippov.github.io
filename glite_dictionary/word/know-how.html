know-how

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌhaʊ / (BrE)
practical knowledge and skill to do something effectively, especially of a technical nature
practical knowledge and skill

Examples

The company's success relies on the technical know-how of its engineers.
She didn't have the know-how to fix the computer problem herself.
Building a website requires both creativity and some know-how in coding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - specialized skill or knowledge in a particular area
  • Antonym: inexperience - lack of experience or practical knowledge

Etymology

From the phrase 'know how', denoting the possession of necessary knowledge or expertise; popularized in American English in the early 20th century.
