avuncular

1. ADJ

/ əˈvʌŋkjələr / (AmE), / əˈvʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
having qualities or characteristics of an uncle, especially in being kind, helpful, or friendly towards younger people
like an uncle; kindly and friendly

Examples

He had an avuncular manner that put everyone at ease.
Many students valued their professor's avuncular advice during difficult times.
With his avuncular smile, he quickly became a favorite among the neighborhood children.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uncle - the brother of one's father or mother or the husband of one's aunt
  • Antonym: paternal - relating to a father

Etymology

From Latin avunculus (maternal uncle).

2. ADJ

/ əˈvʌŋkjələr / (AmE), / əˈvʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
kind and friendly, like an uncle; showing kindness and indulgence characteristic of an uncle.
kind, uncle-like (especially in manner)

Examples

Her avuncular manner made the children feel at ease during the interview.
The professor took an avuncular interest in his students' lives outside the classroom.
He offered advice in an avuncular tone that was both gentle and supportive.

Etymology

From Latin avunculus (maternal uncle) + -ar.
