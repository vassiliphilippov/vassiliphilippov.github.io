agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who acts on behalf of another in business, especially someone who buys or sells goods or services for a commission
business intermediary; buys or sells for others

Examples

She hired an agent to negotiate the contract with the publishers.
The real estate agent showed them several houses in the area.
As an agent, he earns a commission on every sale he arranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broker - a person who arranges transactions between buyers and sellers for a commission

Etymology

From Latin agens, agentis (doer, actor), from agere (to do).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09796453-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The semantic role of the animate entity that instigates or causes the action expressed by a verb in a clause.
instigator of action in a sentence (linguistics)

Examples

In the sentence 'The cat chased the mouse,' 'the cat' functions as the agent.
Linguists identify the agent as the performer of the action in a verb phrase.
Sometimes, the agent is omitted in passive constructions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patient - the entity that undergoes an action in linguistics

Etymology

From Latin agent-, agens, present participle of agere (to do, to act).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person or thing that produces a particular effect or is responsible for a specific result, especially in a process or change
person or thing causing an effect

Examples

The disinfectant acts as an agent in killing harmful bacteria.
Enzymes function as catalysts and agents in chemical reactions within the body.
He investigated the unknown agent that was causing the mysterious illness.

Synset Examples

their research uncovered new disease agents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause - something that produces a result or effect
  • Antonym: effect - a result or outcome produced by a cause

Etymology

From Latin agent-, agens 'doing', from agere 'to do'.

/ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)

This chemical agent is found in many household disinfectants.
