autonomous

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɑːnəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəməs / (BrE)
acting independently or having the freedom to do so; existing or functioning as a separate and independent entity.
acting independently; self-governing

Examples

After gaining sovereignty, the region became fully autonomous with its own government and legislature.
Each division of the company operates as an autonomous unit within the larger organization.
The university's colleges are autonomous and manage their own administrative affairs.

Synset Examples

the partitioning of India created two separate and autonomous jute economies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others; self-sufficient

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomos', from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law).

2. ADJ

/ əˈtɑːnəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəməs / (BrE)
(of a person) free to govern oneself and make independent decisions, not controlled by external authority.
independent; self-governing (person)

Examples

Teenagers often seek to become more autonomous as they grow older and crave independence from their parents.
The committee valued having autonomous members who could make impartial judgments.
She prided herself on being an autonomous thinker, forming opinions without outside influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not controlled or influenced by others; self-reliant
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on or controlled by someone else

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomos', from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00731669-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈtɑːnəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəməs / (BrE)
(of a country or region) having the freedom to govern itself or control its own affairs; not controlled by outside forces.
self-governing; independent

Examples

After years under colonial rule, the region finally became autonomous in its decision-making.
The university is proud to be an autonomous institution, free from government interference.
Each province in the country has an autonomous assembly responsible for local legislation.

Synset Examples

an autonomous judiciary
a sovereign state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - free from outside control; not depending on another's authority
  • Antonym: dependent - controlled or determined by someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek *autonomos*, from *auto-* (self) + *nomos* (law).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
