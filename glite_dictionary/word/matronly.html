matronly

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪ.trən.li / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trən.li / (BrE)
having the characteristics or appearance considered typical of a mature woman, especially in a dignified or motherly way.
having the qualities of a mature woman

Examples

She wore a matronly dress that lent her an air of quiet authority.
With her matronly warmth, she made everyone feel at home.
Her matronly figure and gentle demeanor reminded me of my aunt.

Synset Examples

her matronly figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherly - characteristic of a mother; maternal

Etymology

From 'matron' + '-ly'; 'matron' from Latin 'matrona' (married woman).
