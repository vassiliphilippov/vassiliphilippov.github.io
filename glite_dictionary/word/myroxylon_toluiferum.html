Myroxylon toluiferum

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌrɒksɪˌloʊn toʊˌluːɪˈfɛrəm / (AmE), / mɪˌrɒksɪˌləʊn təˌluːɪˈfɪərəm / (BrE)
A medium-sized tropical American tree that yields tolu balsam and fragrant, hard wood used in high-quality furniture and cabinetwork.
tropical American tree yielding tolu balsam and durable wood

Examples

The fragrant wood of Myroxylon toluiferum is highly valued in furniture making.
Tolu balsam, derived from Myroxylon toluiferum, is used in perfumes and flavors.
Botanists studied the habitat of Myroxylon toluiferum in South American forests.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining Greek 'myron' (perfume) and 'xylon' (wood), and 'toluiferum', meaning 'producing tolu' (resin from Tolu, Colombia).
