plumage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpluːmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpluːmɪdʒ / (BrE)
the feathers covering a bird's body, especially when considered collectively or for their color and pattern
the feathers of a bird

Examples

The peacock is famous for its brightly colored plumage.
During mating season, many birds display their plumage to attract a mate.
The bird's plumage changed as it grew older, becoming more vibrant each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathers - individual structures covering a bird's body

Etymology

From Middle French plumage, from plume (feather).
