sworn

1. ADJ

/ swɔrn / (AmE), / swɔːn / (BrE)
(of a relationship or role) having made a solemn promise or vow, especially formally bound by an oath.
formally bound by oath or promise

Examples

He regarded her as his sworn enemy after the betrayal.
They became sworn brothers after taking an oath of loyalty.
She is a sworn witness in the trial.

Synset Examples

according to an early tradition became his sworn brother
sworn enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness

Etymology

Past participle of 'swear', from Old English 'swerian'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00520474-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ swɔrn / (AmE), / swɔːn / (BrE)
having made a solemn promise or oath; formally affirmed or bound by oath
bound by oath or solemn promise

Examples

He considered her his sworn enemy ever since the betrayal.
As a sworn witness, she gave her testimony in court.
They became sworn friends after completing the challenging journey together.

Synset Examples

now my sworn friend and then mine enemy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsworn - not having taken an oath

Etymology

Past participle of 'swear,' from Old English 'swerian' (to swear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02379911-a
Synset POS: a
