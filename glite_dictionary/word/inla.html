INLA

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ ˌɛn ˌɛl ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌɛn ˌɛl ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a radical Irish republican paramilitary group dedicated to ending British rule in Northern Ireland and achieving Irish unification
Irish republican paramilitary group

Examples

The INLA claimed responsibility for the attack in the early 1980s.
Security forces increased patrols in response to activities by the INLA.
Books on Irish history often mention the violent campaigns conducted by the INLA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IRA - Irish Republican Army, another republican paramilitary group

Etymology

Acronym for Irish National Liberation Army.
