.38 calibre

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɜːrtiˈeɪt ˈkælɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˌθɜːtiˈeɪt ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
relating to a gun or ammunition with a bore diameter of thirty-eight hundredths of an inch (0.38 inch)
of a gun with a .38 inch bore

Examples

The suspect was found carrying a .38 calibre revolver.
This ammunition is compatible only with .38 calibre firearms.
Police recovered several .38 calibre bullets from the scene.

Synset Examples

a .38 caliber shell

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: .22 calibre - relating to guns or ammunition with a 0.22 inch bore
  • Degree Higher: .45 calibre - relating to guns or ammunition with a 0.45 inch bore

Etymology

Calibre from French *calibre*, referring to a gun's bore; '.38' denotes the measurement in hundredths of an inch.
