barbarism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəˌrɪzəm / (BrE)
a brutal or savage act; extreme cruelty or inhuman behavior
a brutal or savage act

Examples

The massacre was viewed as an act of barbarism that shocked the world.
Many historians have documented the barbarism committed during the conflict.
They condemned the destruction of cultural monuments as sheer barbarism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocity - an extremely wicked or cruel act
  • Antonym: civilization - an advanced state of human society

Etymology

From Latin *barbarismus*, from Greek *barbarismos* (foreign speech, barbarous behavior).
