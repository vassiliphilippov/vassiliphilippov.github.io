LASEK

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.sɛk / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.sɛk / (BrE)
A surgical procedure to correct vision by reshaping the cornea, involving the creation of a flap in the outer layer of the eye's surface.
type of eye surgery to correct vision
medical

Examples

After undergoing LASEK, she found her vision significantly improved within a few weeks.
The surgeon explained that LASEK might be more suitable for people with thin corneas than LASIK.
Many patients choose LASEK because it involves less removal of corneal tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LASIK - A similar eye surgery that creates a flap deeper in the corneal tissue to correct vision

Etymology

Acronym from Laser-Assisted Sub-Epithelial Keratectomy.
