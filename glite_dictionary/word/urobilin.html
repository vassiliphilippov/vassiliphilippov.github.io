urobilin

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈbaɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈbʌɪlɪn / (BrE)
a brownish pigment formed from urobilinogen and present in feces and, in small amounts, in urine
brown pigment in feces and urine
medical

Examples

High levels of urobilin in the urine may indicate certain liver conditions.
The characteristic color of feces is partly due to the presence of urobilin.
Laboratory tests revealed reduced urobilin in the patient's urine sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urobilinogen - a colorless compound formed in the intestines from bilirubin
  • Degree Higher: bilirubin - a yellow compound formed by the breakdown of red blood cells

Etymology

From 'uro-' (relating to urine) and 'bilin' (bile pigment), based on scientific Latin roots.
