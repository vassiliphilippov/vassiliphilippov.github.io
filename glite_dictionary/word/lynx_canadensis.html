Lynx canadensis

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / lɪŋks kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Canada lynx, a wild cat native to northern North America.
species of wild cat (Canada lynx)

Examples

The Lynx canadensis thrives in the boreal forests of Canada and Alaska.
Researchers observed several Lynx canadensis during their winter field study.
Habitat loss poses a threat to the survival of Lynx canadensis in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada lynx - A wild cat native to northern North America, known for its thick fur and tufted ears

Etymology

From Latin words 'lynx' (lynx) and 'canadensis' (of Canada).
