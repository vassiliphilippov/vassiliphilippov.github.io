queasy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwiːzi / (AmE), / ˈkwiːzi / (BrE)
feeling sick, especially as if you might vomit; feeling uneasy or uncomfortable in the stomach.
feeling sick or nauseous

Examples

The smell of the spoiled food made me feel queasy instantly.
After the rollercoaster ride, she looked pale and a bit queasy.
He grew queasy just thinking about getting a shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseous - feeling affected with nausea
  • Synonym: sick - feeling ill, especially in the stomach
  • Antonym: comfortable - not feeling sick or uneasy

Etymology

From Middle English quasy, coisy, of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwizi / (AmE), / ˈkwiːzi / (BrE)
feeling or causing worry, uneasiness, or slight nervousness.
feeling uneasy or slightly nervous

Examples

He gave a queasy glance at the clock, worried he might be late for the meeting.
The atmosphere before the announcement felt tense and queasy, with everyone fearing bad news.
Her queasy feeling grew as she realized something was wrong with the deal.

  • Antonym: calm - not feeling angry, nervous, or upset

3. ADJ

/ ˈkwiːzi / (AmE), / ˈkwiːzi / (BrE)
Causing or likely to cause nausea or a feeling of sickness.
causing nausea or sickness

Examples

The queasy odor from the garbage made everyone leave the room.
She felt a queasy sensation in her stomach after smelling the spoiled milk.
A queasy look crossed his face as he watched the gruesome scene on television.

Synset Examples

a nauseating smell
nauseous offal
a sickening stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseating - causing disgust or nausea
  • Synonym: sickening - making one feel sick or nauseous

Etymology

From Middle English queisy (causing nausea), of uncertain origin.
