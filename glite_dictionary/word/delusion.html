delusion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈluʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈluːʒən / (BrE)
a false belief or opinion that is maintained despite being contradicted by reality or rational argument
a false or mistaken belief

Examples

He lives under the delusion that he will win the lottery someday.
Her belief that everyone was against her was just a delusion.
It would be a delusion to think that this problem will solve itself.

Synset Examples

he has delusions of competence
his dreams of vast wealth are a hallucination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief, often involving perception
  • Synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin deludere (to deceive).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈluʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of deceiving oneself or others by believing or making others believe things that are not true, especially as a persistent false belief.
false belief or deception

Examples

The idea that he could finish the project in one day was a delusion.
After months of delusion, she finally accepted the truth.
He lived under the delusion that everyone liked him.

Synset Examples

She has a delusion that she is being spied on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief, often involving perception
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin deludere (to deceive).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈluʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈljuːʒən / (BrE)
A false belief or impression that is strongly held despite clear evidence to the contrary, often as a symptom of mental disorder.
firm false belief despite contrary evidence
medical

Examples

He continues to believe, under a delusion, that he will win the lottery despite all evidence.
The patient's delusion that she could fly posed a serious risk to her safety.
Living in a delusion can prevent someone from recognizing and addressing real problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false appearance or deceptive impression of reality
  • Synonym: hallucination - a sensory experience of something that does not exist outside the mind
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin deludere (to deceive, mock), via Old French delusion.
