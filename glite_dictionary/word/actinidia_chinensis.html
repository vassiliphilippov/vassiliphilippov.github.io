Actinidia chinensis

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪˈnɪdiə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ækˌtɪˈnɪdɪə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a climbing vine native to China, cultivated for its edible fruit known as kiwifruit
species of vine producing kiwifruit

Examples

The Actinidia chinensis vine thrives best in temperate climates with good rainfall.
Many commercial growers in New Zealand cultivate Actinidia chinensis for its high market value.
Scientists are studying Actinidia chinensis to improve disease resistance in kiwifruit crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwifruit - the edible fruit produced by Actinidia chinensis

Etymology

From the genus name 'Actinidia' (Greek aktis, aktin- 'ray', after the radiating styles of the flower) and 'chinensis' (Latin for 'Chinese').
