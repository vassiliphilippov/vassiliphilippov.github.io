wildness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld.nəs / (BrE)
a state or quality of intense, uncontrolled, or overwhelming emotion
extreme emotional intensity

Examples

She was swept away by the wildness of her excitement when the news arrived.
There was a certain wildness in his laughter that startled everyone in the room.
The wildness of her grief made her impossible to comfort.

Synset Examples

the wildness of his anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frenzy - a state of uncontrolled excitement or emotion

Etymology

From wild + -ness, forming a noun meaning the quality or state of being wild.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld.nɪs / (BrE)
an uncultivated or barbarous state; the quality or condition of being wild and untamed
quality of being wild or untamed

Examples

The wildness of the jungle made travel nearly impossible for the explorers.
She admired the wildness of the mountains, untouched by human hands.
There was a certain wildness in his spirit that refused to be tamed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savagery - the quality of being fierce or uncivilized
  • Antonym: civilization - the stage of human social and cultural development that is considered advanced

Etymology

From Old English 'wilde' (wild) + '-ness' (state or quality).

Examples
