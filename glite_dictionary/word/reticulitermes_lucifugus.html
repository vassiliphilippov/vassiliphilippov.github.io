Reticulitermes lucifugus

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌtɪkjəlˈtaɪmiːz ˌluːsɪˈfjuːɡəs / (AmE), / rɪˌtɪkjʊˈlaɪtiːmiːz ˌluːsɪˈfjuːɡəs / (BrE)
A species of destructive European termite found in wood structures and tree roots.
species of European termite

Examples

Reticulitermes lucifugus can cause serious damage to old wooden buildings in southern Europe.
Pest control experts were called in when an infestation of Reticulitermes lucifugus was discovered beneath the floorboards.
Proper wood treatment is essential to guard against Reticulitermes lucifugus in vulnerable areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - an insect that feeds on wood

Etymology

From Latin: 'reticulitermes' meaning 'net-like termite' and 'lucifugus' meaning 'avoiding light'.
