toot

1. NOUN

/ tuːt / (AmE), / tuːt / (BrE)
an occasion of lively drinking and celebration; a festive drinking party
lively drinking party or celebration
informal

Examples

They went out for a toot after the big win.
Last night’s toot left everyone in high spirits.
We're organizing a toot at Mike's place this Saturday.

2. NOUN

/ tuːt / (AmE), / tuːt / (BrE)
a short, sharp sound or blast, especially made by a horn or whistle
a brief blast of a horn

Examples

I heard a loud toot from the car behind me as the traffic light turned green.
She gave a cheerful toot on her whistle to signal the end of the game.
The train announced its arrival with a long toot from its horn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honk - a loud, sharp sound made by a car horn
  • Synonym: beep - a short, high-pitched sound, often made by a horn or electronic device
