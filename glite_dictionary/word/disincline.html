to disincline

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪn / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to make someone unwilling or reluctant to do something
make someone unwilling or reluctant

Examples

The bad weather disinclined us from going on a picnic.
His recent experiences have disinclined him to trust new acquaintances.
Strict rules may disincline students from participating in extracurricular activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: to encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (not) + 'inclīnāre' (to lean).
