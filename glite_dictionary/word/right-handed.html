right-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhændɪd / (BrE)
using or intended for use with the right hand, or having a preference for using the right hand or side
using or intended for the right hand

Examples

He is a right-handed pitcher known for his fastball.
These right-handed scissors are designed for greater comfort and control.
She found it difficult to use a right-handed mouse with her left hand.

Synset Examples

a right-handed batter
right-handed scissors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-handed - using or intended for the left hand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02035990-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌraɪtˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌraɪtˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a rotation, helix, or screw) turning or rotating to the right, particularly in a clockwise direction when viewed from a specific angle.
rotating to the right (technical sense)

Examples

The screw has a right-handed thread that tightens when turned clockwise.
In physics, a right-handed helix follows the direction of the fingers when the right hand is curled.
The scientist explained the difference between right-handed and left-handed rotation to the class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-handed - rotating to the left or counterclockwise
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00443944-s
Synset POS: s
