Myosotis scorpiodes

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌoʊˈsoʊtɪs skɔrˈpiːoʊdiːz / (AmE), / mɪˌɒsəˈtɪs ˌskɔːpɪˈəʊdiːz / (BrE)
a small perennial herb, commonly known as a type of forget-me-not, noted for its bright blue or white flowers and found in wet habitats
a small perennial herb with blue or white flowers

Examples

The delicate Myosotis scorpiodes thrives along the edges of ponds and marshes.
Gardeners appreciate Myosotis scorpiodes for its charming, long-lasting blue flowers.
We spotted several clusters of Myosotis scorpiodes blooming beside the quiet stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forget-me-not - a common name for species within the genus Myosotis, including Myosotis scorpiodes

Etymology

From Greek 'myosotis' meaning 'mouse's ear' (referring to the shape of the leaves), combined with 'scorpiodes' meaning 'like a scorpion,' describing the coiled flower clusters.
