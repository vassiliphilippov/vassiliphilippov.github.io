Pangea

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / panˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
the supercontinent that included all of Earth's landmasses before they separated during the late Paleozoic and early Mesozoic eras
prehistoric supercontinent

Examples

The theory of Pangea explains how continents drifted apart over millions of years.
Scientists believe that Pangea began breaking up about 175 million years ago.
Many fossil distributions make sense only if Pangea once existed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Gondwanaland - the southern supercontinent formed after the split of Pangea
  • Degree Lower: Laurasia - the northern supercontinent formed after the split of Pangea

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'gaia' (earth, land).
