lancers

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.sɚz / (AmE), / ˈlɑːn.səz / (BrE)
A type of quadrille dance for eight or sixteen couples, popular in the 19th century.
a quadrille dance for 8 or 16 couples

Examples

At the Victorian ball, the guests performed the lancers with remarkable precision.
The lancers was one of the most anticipated dances of the evening.
Historical societies often include the lancers in their re-enactment programs.

Etymology

From French 'lanciers', referring to the cavalry lancers, as the dance's figures were named after military movements.
