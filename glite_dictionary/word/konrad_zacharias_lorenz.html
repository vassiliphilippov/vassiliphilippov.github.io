Konrad Zacharias Lorenz

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑn.rɑd ˌzɑː.kəˈraɪ.i.əs ləˈrɛnts / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.rad ˌzæ.kəˈraɪ.i.əs lɒˈrɛnts / (BrE)
Austrian zoologist (1903–1989) known for pioneering studies of animal behavior, particularly imprinting in birds, and his emphasis on innate behavioral patterns.
Austrian zoologist, pioneer of animal behavior research

Examples

Konrad Zacharias Lorenz received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his groundbreaking work on imprinting in geese.
Many students learn about the fixed action patterns described by Konrad Zacharias Lorenz in their first biology course.
The studies conducted by Konrad Zacharias Lorenz continue to influence research in animal behavior today.
