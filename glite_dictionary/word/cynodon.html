Cynodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.nəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of creeping perennial grasses native to tropical and southern Africa, including species such as Bermuda grass
genus of creeping perennial grasses

Examples

The invasive nature of Cynodon makes it difficult to control in some environments.
Cynodon species are widely used for pasture due to their resilience and rapid growth.
Botanists are studying Cynodon to improve the quality of turfgrass in sports fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - a common species of Cynodon, used for lawns and pastures

Etymology

From Greek kynos (of a dog) + odous (tooth), referring to the shape of some species' leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.nəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.nəˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of creeping perennial grasses native to tropical and southern Africa, including species such as Bermuda grass
genus of creeping tropical grasses

Examples

Cynodon is often used as a lawn grass because of its resilience to drought.
Researchers studied several species within the Cynodon genus for their adaptability to different climates.
The turf on the golf course was primarily composed of Cynodon species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - a common species of grass in the genus Cynodon, widely used for lawns and pastures

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the shape of the grass's spikes.
