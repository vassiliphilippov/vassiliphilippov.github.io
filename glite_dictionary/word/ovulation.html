ovulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which an ovary releases an egg, typically occurring midway through the menstrual cycle in females
release of an egg from an ovary
medical

Examples

Tracking your ovulation can help you determine the most fertile days in your cycle.
Hormonal changes trigger ovulation, allowing an egg to be released for possible fertilization.
Irregular ovulation can make it difficult for some women to become pregnant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menstruation - monthly discharge of blood from the uterus
  • Degree Higher: fertilization - process by which an egg and sperm join

Etymology

From Latin 'ovulum' (diminutive of 'ovum', meaning egg) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action or process).
