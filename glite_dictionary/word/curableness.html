curableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊrəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being curable or capable of being healed
the quality of being curable

Examples

Doctors were optimistic about the curableness of the disease after reviewing new treatment data.
The curableness of many cancers has improved thanks to advances in medical science.
She asked the specialist about the curableness of her condition before agreeing to start therapy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incurableness - the quality of being incurable
  • Synonym: curable - able to be cured or healed

Etymology

From 'curable' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting quality or state).
