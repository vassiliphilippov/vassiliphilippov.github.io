cleat

1. NOUN

/ klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
a projecting piece of metal or leather attached to the sole of a shoe to prevent slipping, especially in sports footwear
projection on shoe sole to prevent slipping

Examples

The football player's cleat left a distinctive mark on the muddy field.
She replaced the worn-out cleats on her running shoes for better grip.
A broken cleat can increase the risk of slipping on wet grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stud - projection on the sole of a shoe for traction

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *clete* (wedge, lump), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
A strip of wood or metal fastened to a surface to provide added strength, support, or to serve as a fastening point.
strip of wood or metal for support

Examples

The shelves were secured to the wall using wooden cleats for added stability.
He fastened a metal cleat to the deck to tie down the cargo securely.
Carpenters often use a cleat when building sturdy frames for heavy equipment.

Etymology

From Middle English *clete*, possibly from Old English *clēt* (wedge, lump).

4. NOUN

/ klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
a metal or wooden fitting with projecting arms to which a rope can be fastened, especially on a boat, dock, or wharf
fitting for fastening rope

Examples

The sailor quickly tied the rope to the cleat on the dock.
Make sure you wrap the line securely around the cleat to prevent the boat from drifting away.
The metal cleat was bolted firmly to the side of the pier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - device used to attach or secure objects together

Etymology

From Old French ‘clou’ (nail, peg), related to Latin ‘clavus’ (nail).
