bollock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlək / (AmE), / ˈbɒlək / (BrE)
a pulley-block located at the head of a topmast, used in sailing for handling ropes and rigging
nautical pulley-block at top of mast

Examples

The sailor inspected the bollock to ensure the rigging was secure before they raised the sails.
Repairing a damaged bollock required specialized tools and knowledge of the ship’s rigging system.
A new bollock was fitted to the topmast after the old one had worn out from years of use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlək / (AmE), / ˈbɒlək / (BrE)
(slang, chiefly British) a testicle; one of the two male reproductive glands that produce sperm.
slang term for a testicle
slang british

Examples

He doubled over in pain after being hit in the bollocks during the rugby match.
The comedian made a joke about slipping and landing on his bollocks.
It's a sensitive subject—try not to mention his bollock injury.

Synset Examples

she kicked him in the balls and got away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testicle - one of the two male reproductive glands in males
  • Antonym: ovary - the female reproductive gland that produces eggs

Etymology

From Middle English *bollok*, from Old English *bealluc*, diminutive of *beallucas* (testicles).
