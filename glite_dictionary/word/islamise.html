to Islamise

1. VERB

/ ˈɪsləˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪsləmaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (a person, group, or place) to the religion, customs, or culture of Islam
convert to Islam
british

Examples

During the 16th century, the empire sought to Islamise vast regions of its new territory.
Some rulers attempted to Islamise their populations through law and education reforms.
The policy was gradually put in place to Islamise public life and institutions.

Synset Examples

The Mughals Islamized much of Northern India in the 16th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Islamize - convert to Islam (US spelling)

Etymology

From Islamic + -ise, after late Latin and French formation patterns.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00386356-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪsləˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪsləmaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something, such as laws or customs, to conform to Islamic law or principles
make conform to Islamic law

Examples

The government attempted to Islamise the education system by revising school curricula.
Certain groups hope to Islamise social practices in accordance with religious teachings.
There was debate over plans to Islamise local dietary regulations.

Synset Examples

Islamize the dietary laws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to secularise - make something less influenced by religion; remove religious influence
  • Synonym: to Christianise - cause to conform to Christian principles or beliefs

Etymology

From Islam + -ise (from French -iser or Latin -izare, forming verbs meaning 'to make or become').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00386923-v
Synset POS: v
