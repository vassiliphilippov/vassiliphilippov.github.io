Haemodorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈdɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈdɔːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the family Haemodoraceae, native to Australia and known for their red roots.
genus of Australian herbs (Haemodoraceae)

Examples

Several species of Haemodorum are valued for their striking red flower spikes.
The roots of Haemodorum contain pigments once used by Indigenous Australians for dye.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of Haemodorum to sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) and doron (gift), referring to the red roots.
