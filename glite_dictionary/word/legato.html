legato

1. ADV

/ ləˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / lɛˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) in a manner that is smooth and connected, so that each note flows into the next without any perceptible break.
smoothly and connectedly (music)

Examples

The violinist performed the passage legato, creating a seamless, flowing sound.
Please play the next section legato rather than staccato.
The composer indicated that this phrase should be sung legato for emotional effect.

Synset Examples

play this legato, please

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staccato - (in music) short and detached notes

Etymology

From Italian "legato," past participle of "legare" (to tie, bind).

2. ADJ

/ lɪˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(music) played or sung smoothly and connectedly, with no noticeable breaks between notes.
smoothly connected (music)

Examples

The violinist played the entire melody legato, creating a warm, flowing sound.
In piano music, phrases marked legato should be performed with smoothly connected notes.
Her legato singing style gave the song an elegant and graceful quality.

Synset Examples

a legato passage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staccato - (music) performed with each note sharply separated from the others

Etymology

From Italian 'legato', past participle of 'legare' (to tie, bind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02301995-a
Synset POS: a
