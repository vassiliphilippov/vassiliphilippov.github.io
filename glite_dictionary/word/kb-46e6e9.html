kb

1. NOUN

/ keɪ biː / (AmE), / keɪ biː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 bits, used in computing to measure data size
a unit of 1,000 bits (computing)

Examples

The file size is just one kb, so it downloads instantly.
Internet speeds are often measured in kb per second.
A simple text message uses only a few kb of data.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mb - a unit of information equal to 1,000,000 bits
  • Degree Lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing
  • Synonym: kilobyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilobit', from 'kilo-' (thousand) and 'bit' (binary digit).
