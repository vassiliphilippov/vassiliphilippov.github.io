Eugene Gladstone O'Neill

1. PROPN

/ juːˈdʒin ˈɡlædstoʊn oʊˈniːl / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːn ˈɡlædstəʊn əʊˈniːl / (BrE)
(1888–1953) An influential United States playwright known for his powerful dramas, including 'Long Day's Journey Into Night' and 'The Iceman Cometh'.
American playwright (1888–1953)

Examples

Many critics regard Eugene Gladstone O'Neill as the father of American drama.
The plays of Eugene Gladstone O'Neill often explore complex family relationships and existential themes.
Eugene Gladstone O'Neill won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1936.
