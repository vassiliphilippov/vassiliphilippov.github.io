Cereus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of treelike or shrubby cacti with pronounced ribs, needlelike spines, and usually white nocturnal flowers.
a genus of ribbed, spiny cacti

Examples

The night-blooming Cereus filled the greenhouse with a sweet fragrance after dusk.
He specializes in cultivating rare Cereus species for botanical gardens.
A large Cereus cactus can live for decades and grow several metres tall.

Etymology

From Latin 'cereus' meaning 'wax candle', referring to the candle-like shape of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, branching cacti with pronounced ribs, rounded needlelike spines, and usually white, night-blooming flowers.
genus of columnar or shrubby cacti

Examples

The Cereus cactus blooms spectacularly at night, revealing large white flowers.
Many species in the Cereus genus thrive in arid environments across the Americas.
Gardeners prize the nocturnal blossoms of Cereus for their beauty and fragrance.

Etymology

From Latin 'cereus' meaning 'wax candle', referring to the candle-like shape of some species.
