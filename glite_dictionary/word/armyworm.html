armyworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmiˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɑːmiˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a noctuid moth larva that migrates in large groups and destroys crops, especially grasses and grains
caterpillar pest of crops

Examples

The corn fields were devastated by an infestation of armyworms last summer.
Farmers use pesticides to protect their crops from armyworm invasions.
An outbreak of armyworms can ruin entire fields of grain in just a few days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutworm - larva of several moth species attacking plants at ground level

Etymology

From the resemblance of their mass movements to armies on the march.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmiˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɑːmiˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A moth larva that travels in large groups and can cause extensive damage to crops.
destructive moth larva harming crops

Examples

The cornfields were devastated by an infestation of armyworms last summer.
Farmers struggle to control armyworm outbreaks during the rainy season.
When left unchecked, armyworms can destroy entire crops in a matter of days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutworm - larva of various moths that cuts and destroys plants

Etymology

From 'army' (referencing the way the larvae move in large groups) + 'worm'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmiˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɑːmiˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A caterpillar, especially the larva of a fungus gnat or certain moths, that feeds on cereals and other grains and is known for moving in large groups when food is scarce.
agricultural pest caterpillar

Examples

Farmers took action when a swarm of armyworms suddenly attacked their wheat fields.
The damage left by armyworms can devastate entire crops within days.
After the food was gone, the armyworms moved on in a steady, marching line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutworm - larva of certain moths that damages crops
  • Synonym: caterpillar - larval stage of butterflies and moths

Etymology

Named for their habit of moving in large, army-like groups.
