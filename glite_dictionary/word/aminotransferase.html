aminotransferase

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˈtrænsfərˌeɪs / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈtrænsfəreɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of an amino group from one molecule to another, especially from an amino acid to a keto acid
enzyme transferring amino groups
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered blood tests to check for elevated aminotransferase levels.
Liver diseases are often diagnosed by measuring the activity of aminotransferase enzymes.
Aspartate aminotransferase is commonly evaluated as part of a liver function panel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transaminase - another term for aminotransferase, an enzyme catalyzing transamination

Etymology

From amino (relating to an amino group) + transferase (an enzyme that catalyzes transfer reactions).
