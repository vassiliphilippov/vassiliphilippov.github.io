to blue-pencil

1. VERB

/ bluː ˈpɛnsəl / (AmE), / bluː ˈpɛnsəl / (BrE)
to censor, cut, or eliminate text or content, especially as part of editing or review
to censor or cut text during editing

Examples

The publisher decided to blue-pencil several controversial paragraphs from the article.
She had to blue-pencil parts of the script before it went on air.
Due to strict regulations, the committee chose to blue-pencil sensitive information from the report.

Synset Examples

she edited the juiciest scenes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - to suppress or remove content considered objectionable
  • Synonym: to redact - to edit text for publication, sometimes by removing sensitive parts

Etymology

From the traditional use of a blue pencil by editors to indicate changes or deletions in manuscripts.
