lowly

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊli / (AmE), / ˈləʊli / (BrE)
of low social status or humble origin; having a low rank or position in society.
of low social status or humble origin

Examples

Although her family was lowly, she achieved great success through hard work.
He was born into a lowly station but rose to prominence.
The prince disguised himself as a lowly servant to learn about the people.

Synset Examples

baseborn wretches with dirty faces
of humble (or lowly) birth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; of low social rank
  • Synonym: baseborn - born to parents of low social status

Etymology

Formed from 'low' + '-ly', modeled after similar English adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊli / (AmE), / ˈləʊli / (BrE)
of low rank, status, or importance; humble or unpretentious.
of low rank or status; humble

Examples

She began her career in a lowly position at the company but rose to the top.
He came from a lowly background but achieved great success through hard work.
The lowly farmhouse stood at the edge of the grand estate.

Synset Examples

a lowly parish priest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble - having or showing a low estimate of one's importance; not proud
  • Synonym: modest - not excessively proud or confident; moderate in estimation of abilities
  • Antonym: high-ranking - having a high rank or position

Etymology

From Old English 'lāw', meaning low in position, with suffix '-ly' forming adjectives.

4. ADJ

/ ˈloʊli / (AmE), / ˈləʊli / (BrE)
(of work or position) not requiring much skill or education; regarded as inferior or humble.
humble or unskilled (of work)

Examples

She started her career doing lowly tasks in a hotel kitchen.
Many lowly jobs are essential for the smooth running of an organization.
He rose from a lowly position to become the company's director.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menial - (of work) not requiring skill and lacking prestige
  • Synonym: humble - of low social, administrative, or professional rank
  • Antonym: skilled - requiring special abilities or training, opposite of unskilled

Etymology

From Middle English 'lōweli', formed from 'low' + '-ly'.
