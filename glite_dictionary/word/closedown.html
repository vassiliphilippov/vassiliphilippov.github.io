closedown

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊzˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzdaʊn / (BrE)
the act of stopping business, production, or other operations permanently or for an extended period
termination of operations

Examples

After the closedown of the factory, many workers were left unemployed.
The government announced the closedown of several outdated power plants.
Financial difficulties led to the abrupt closedown of the local newspaper.

Synset Examples

they regretted the closure of the day care center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closure - the fact or process of closing something, especially an institution, thoroughfare, or frontier
  • Synonym: shutdown - a closure of a factory or system, typically temporary

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'close' and the particle 'down,' modeled after phrasal verb constructions.
