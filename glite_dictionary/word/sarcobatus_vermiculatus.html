Sarcobatus vermiculatus

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˌkoʊˈbeɪtəs vɜrˌmɪkjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / sɑːˌkəʊˈbeɪtəs vɜːˌmɪkjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A low, hardy, much-branched spiny shrub common in alkaline soils of western North America, also known as greasewood.
spiny shrub of western North America

Examples

Sarcobatus vermiculatus is often found in salt flats and desert valleys across Nevada and Utah.
The alkaline soil was dominated by Sarcobatus vermiculatus and sagebrush.
Many birds and small mammals use Sarcobatus vermiculatus as shelter in the harsh desert climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greasewood - common name for Sarcobatus vermiculatus, a spiny shrub of alkali soils

Etymology

From Greek 'sarkos' (flesh) and 'batos' (bramble); 'vermiculatus' from Latin meaning 'full of little worms' (referring to the root appearance).
