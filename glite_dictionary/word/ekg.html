EKG

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːˌkeɪˈdʒiː / (AmE), / ˌiːˌkeɪˈdʒiː / (BrE)
A graphical recording of the heart's electrical activity produced by an electrocardiograph; electrocardiogram.
graph recording of heart's electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an EKG to check the patient's heart rhythm.
His EKG results showed some irregularities that required further testing.
Nurses are trained to read an EKG and identify potential cardiac problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ECG - alternative acronym for electrocardiogram
  • Synonym: electrocardiogram - medical term for the recording of heart's electrical activity

Etymology

Acronym from German 'Elektrokardiogramm'.
