Cantharellus cinnabarinus

1. NOUN

/ kænˌθæˈrɛləs sɪn.ə.bəˈraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / kanˌθaːˈrɛləs sɪn.ə.bəˈraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A species of wild mushroom with a distinctive pink to red or vermillion fruiting body, commonly known as the cinnabar chanterelle.
a pink to red wild mushroom species

Examples

We found a cluster of Cantharellus cinnabarinus while hiking in the forest.
The bright color of Cantharellus cinnabarinus makes it easy to spot among the leaf litter.
Many foragers value Cantharellus cinnabarinus for its attractive appearance and unique flavor.

Etymology

From Latin: Cantharellus (diminutive of cantharus, a drinking vessel), and cinnabarinus (cinnabar-red).
