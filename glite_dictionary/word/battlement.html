battlement

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəlmənt / (AmE), / ˈbætl̩mənt / (BrE)
a parapet at the top of a castle or fort, usually with regular gaps for shooting through, used as a defensive architectural feature
defensive parapet with gaps on a castle

Examples

The ancient castle's battlement was silhouetted against the evening sky.
Soldiers kept watch from behind the battlement during the siege.
Tourists admired the views from the battlement of the restored fortress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony

Etymology

From Old French 'bataillement', from 'batailler' (to fortify, to fight).
