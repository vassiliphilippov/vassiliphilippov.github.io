Isatis

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈseɪtɪs / (AmE), / aɪˈseɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), including species such as woad, native to parts of Europe and Asia.
genus of plants including woad

Examples

Isatis tinctoria was historically cultivated for its blue dye extraction.
Botanists have identified several species within the Isatis genus.
The leaves of Isatis are lance-shaped and often used in traditional dyeing processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woad - A plant (Isatis tinctoria) historically used for blue dye

Etymology

From Latin 'Isatis', from Greek 'isatis', a plant used to produce blue dye.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.sə.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sətɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family, native to the Old World, including the woad.
genus of plants including woad

Examples

Isatis is best known as the genus that includes the woad plant, historically used for blue dye.
Several species in the Isatis genus are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
Botanists have studied Isatis to understand the evolution of dye-producing compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woad - a plant (Isatis tinctoria) valued for its blue dye

Etymology

From Latin *Isatis*, from Greek *isatis*, a plant used as a dye.
