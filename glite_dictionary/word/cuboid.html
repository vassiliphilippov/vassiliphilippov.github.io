cuboid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjuː.bɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɔɪd / (BrE)
Having the shape of a cube or a rectangular box.
cube- or box-shaped

Examples

The building was designed with a cuboid structure to maximize interior space.
The package arrived in a cuboid box that fit perfectly on the shelf.
Many minerals crystallize into cuboid shapes, making them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubical - having the shape of a cube

Etymology

From Latin 'cubus' (cube) and Greek '-oeidēs' (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.bɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɔɪd / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure with six rectangular faces, forming a rectangular box shape; a rectangular parallelepiped.
rectangular box-shaped solid

Examples

The box had the shape of a cuboid and fit perfectly on the shelf.
A textbook is often modeled as a cuboid in math problems to simplify calculations.
Architects sometimes use a cuboid design for modern buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cube - A solid figure with six equal square faces

Etymology

From Latin "cubus" (cube) + "-oid" (like, resembling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13907587-n
Synset POS: n
