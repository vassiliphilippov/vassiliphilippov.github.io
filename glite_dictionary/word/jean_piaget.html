Jean Piaget

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn piˈɑʒeɪ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ pjaˈʒɛ / (BrE)
(1896–1980) Swiss psychologist known for pioneering research on cognitive development in children.
Swiss psychologist; cognitive development specialist

Examples

Jean Piaget greatly influenced our understanding of how children acquire knowledge.
Many university courses on child psychology reference the theories of Jean Piaget.
The work of Jean Piaget is essential reading for students interested in developmental psychology.
