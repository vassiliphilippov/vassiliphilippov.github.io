stringency

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being strict or precise in following rules, standards, or requirements
strict observance of rules

Examples

The stringency of the safety regulations ensured that all procedures were carefully followed.
Many criticized the stringency of the new immigration policies.
Due to its stringency, the dress code left little room for personal expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strictness - the quality of being strict
  • Antonym: laxity - the quality of being lax or less strict

Etymology

From Latin 'stringentia' (astringency, strictness), from 'stringere' (to bind tight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
a situation in which money and credit are difficult to obtain, often leading to economic hardship or constraint
a shortage of money and credit

Examples

Periods of economic stringency often lead to reduced consumer spending and investment.
The government responded to the financial stringency by introducing emergency credit measures.
Banks became extremely cautious about lending during the prolonged period of stringency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tightness - a state of being hard to obtain or restricted, especially in finance

Etymology

From Latin 'stringere' (to draw tight), via stringent + -cy.

Synset POS: n
