saddlery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.lɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsæd.ləri / (BrE)
A workshop where a saddler makes, repairs, or sells saddles and related equipment.
workshop for making or repairing saddles

Examples

The old village saddlery still has tools from the nineteenth century on display.
He apprenticed in a saddlery before starting his own leatherworking business.
A visit to the saddlery revealed how much skill goes into crafting a saddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddler - a person who makes, repairs, or sells saddles and harnesses

Etymology

From saddle + -ery, denoting a place of trade or manufacture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.lɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsæd.ləri / (BrE)
equipment, such as saddles and bridles, used for horses
equipment for horses such as saddles and bridles

Examples

The old barn was filled with saddlery and other horse equipment.
Good saddlery can make a big difference in a rider's comfort and safety.
She inherited her passion for making saddlery from her grandfather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tack - equipment for horses, especially saddles, bridles, etc.

Etymology

From saddle +‎ -ery, modeled after similar words for shops or products.
