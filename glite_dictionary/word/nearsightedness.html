nearsightedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪrˌsaɪtɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɪəˌsaɪtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
an eyesight condition in which distant objects appear blurred because the eye focuses images in front of the retina; short-sightedness
visual defect where distant objects appear blurred
medical

Examples

Sarah was diagnosed with nearsightedness and now wears glasses to see the board clearly.
Modern corrective surgeries can help people overcome nearsightedness and regain clear distance vision.
Her nearsightedness made it difficult for her to read signs while driving at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myopia - the medical term for nearsightedness
  • Antonym: farsightedness - a condition in which nearby objects appear blurred
