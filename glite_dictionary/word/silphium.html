Silphium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.fi.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.fi.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tall North American perennial herbs in the daisy family, known for their large yellow flower heads and resinous stems.
a genus of tall North American perennial herbs

Examples

The prairie was dotted with vibrant Silphium swaying in the warm breeze.
Many gardeners cultivate Silphium for its showy yellow blossoms and sturdy stems.
Botanists studied the resin produced by Silphium to learn more about its protective properties.

Etymology

From Latin 'silphium', a resin-yielding plant; adopted in botanical Latin for this genus.
