adolescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈlɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌadəˈlɛsəns / (BrE)
the period in a person's life between childhood and adulthood, typically marked by the onset of puberty and significant physical, emotional, and social changes
period between childhood and adulthood

Examples

Many young people find the transition through adolescence both exciting and challenging.
During adolescence, teenagers often seek greater independence from their parents.
She struggled with her identity throughout much of her adolescence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: puberty - the stage of development when a child becomes physically capable of reproduction
  • Degree Higher: adulthood - the state of being fully grown or mature

Etymology

From Latin adolescentia (youth), from adolescere (to grow up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈlɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌadəˈlɛsəns / (BrE)
the period in human development between the onset of puberty and the attainment of adulthood, usually marked by physical, emotional, and psychological changes
period between puberty and adulthood

Examples

During adolescence, teenagers often experience rapid changes in mood and behavior.
Many lifelong habits and interests are formed during adolescence.
The transition from childhood to adulthood can be challenging throughout adolescence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: childhood - the period of being a child, before adolescence
  • Degree Higher: adulthood - the state of being fully grown or mature, after adolescence

Etymology

From Latin adolescentia, meaning 'youth' or 'growing up'.
