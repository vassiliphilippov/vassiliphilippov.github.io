arhant

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈhɑnt / (AmE), / ɑːˈhʌnt / (BrE)
(in Buddhism) a person who has attained nirvana by following the path of enlightenment; a perfected being.
a Buddhist who has attained nirvana

Examples

The monk was revered as an arhant after years of meditation and discipline.
Many Buddhists aspire to the state of an arhant, striving for spiritual liberation.
According to tradition, an arhant is free from the cycle of rebirth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arhat - a Buddhist who has attained nirvana; alternate spelling

Etymology

From Sanskrit *arhat*, meaning 'worthy' or 'deserving'.
