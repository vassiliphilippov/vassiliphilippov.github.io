casually

1. ADV

/ ˈkæʒuəli / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjʊəli / (BrE)
In a relaxed or unconcerned manner; without serious intent or commitment.
in a relaxed or unconcerned way

Examples

She glanced casually at the menu before placing her order.
They spoke casually about their plans for the weekend, not making any firm decisions.
He dressed casually for the meeting, wearing jeans and a t-shirt.

Synset Examples

glanced casually at the headlines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without giving enough attention or thought
  • Antonym: deliberately - in a careful or intentional manner

Etymology

From 'casual' + '-ly'. 'Casual' from Late Latin casualis (by chance).

2. ADV

/ ˈkæʒuəli / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjuəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not methodical, careful, or according to a plan; showing a lack of seriousness or attention to detail.
without method or plan

Examples

He finished his assignments casually and made several careless mistakes.
She glanced casually at the clock, unconcerned about running late.
The report was prepared casually with little attention to detail.

Synset Examples

he dealt with his course work casually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without care or attention
  • Antonym: methodically - in an orderly and systematic manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00265324-r
Synset POS: r
