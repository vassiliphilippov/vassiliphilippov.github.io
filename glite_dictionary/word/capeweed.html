capeweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpˌwiːd / (BrE)
A European plant (Arctotheca calendula) widely naturalized in North America, recognized for its yellow flower heads and leaves resembling a cat's ears.
European weed with yellow flowers

Examples

The field was covered in capeweed, its yellow blossoms stretching to the horizon.
Gardeners often struggle to remove capeweed because it spreads quickly in disturbed soils.
After the heavy rains, patches of capeweed appeared along the roadside.

Etymology

From 'Cape' (reference to the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa) + 'weed'.
