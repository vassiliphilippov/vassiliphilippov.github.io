to restructure

1. VERB

/ ɹiˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
to organize or form something in a new or different way, often in order to improve it or adapt to new circumstances
organize or form anew; change structure

Examples

After the merger, the company decided to restructure its management team.
She had to restructure her daily routine to include physical therapy after the surgery.
Many organizations restructure their departments to increase efficiency and reduce costs.

Synset Examples

After his accident, he had to restructure his life
The governing board was reconstituted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reorganize - to organize something again or differently
  • Antonym: to dismantle - to take something apart so that it no longer works as a whole

Etymology

From re- (again) + structure, from Latin structura (fitting together, building).
