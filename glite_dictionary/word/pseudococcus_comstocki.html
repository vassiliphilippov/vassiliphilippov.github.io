Pseudococcus comstocki

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.doʊˈkɑːkəs kəmˈstɑːki / (AmE), / ˌsjuː.dəʊˈkɒk.əs kəmˈstɒki / (BrE)
An Asiatic insect species, accidentally introduced into the United States, that is a pest affecting citrus and apple trees.
insect pest on citrus and apple trees

Examples

Fruit growers are worried about the threat Pseudococcus comstocki poses to their apple orchards.
The agricultural inspector identified the pest as Pseudococcus comstocki during his routine survey.
Control measures have been implemented to prevent the spread of Pseudococcus comstocki in citrus groves.

Etymology

From the New Latin genus name 'Pseudococcus', meaning 'false berry', and the species epithet 'comstocki' honoring entomologist John Henry Comstock.
