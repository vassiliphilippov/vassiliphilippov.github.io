Jew's-ears

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːz ˌɪrz / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːz ˌɪəz / (BrE)
a widely distributed edible fungus (Auricularia auricula-judae) shaped like a human ear, commonly found growing on decaying wood
edible ear-shaped fungus on wood

Examples

The soup was flavored with slices of Jew's-ears for a unique texture.
During our walk in the woods, we spotted some Jew's-ears growing on a fallen log.
Many Asian dishes use dried Jew's-ears as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood ear - another name for the edible fungus Auricularia auricula-judae

Etymology

Name derives from the earlier term 'Judas's ear,' referring to Judas Iscariot and the folklore that he hanged himself from an elder tree, on which the fungus often grows.
