to overfatigue

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərfəˈtiɡ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəfəˈtiːɡ / (BrE)
to make excessively tired or fatigued; to exhaust beyond normal limits
to tire excessively

Examples

Prolonged study without breaks can overfatigue the mind and reduce concentration.
The intense training program began to overfatigue the young athletes.
Doctors warned that working late nights could easily overfatigue her until she became ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to drain of strength or energy, wear out, or fatigue greatly
  • Antonym: to refresh - to give new strength or energy to; revive

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'fatigue' (tiredness, exhaustion), from French 'fatigue', from Latin 'fatigare' (to tire out).
