tympani

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpəni / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpəni / (BrE)
A large, bowl-shaped percussion instrument made of brass or copper with a tunable drumhead, played with mallets.
large tunable drum; kettledrum

Examples

The orchestra's tympani thundered during the dramatic climax of the symphony.
He carefully tuned the tympani before the performance started.
Students learned how to play the tympani in their music class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kettledrum - a large drum with a rounded shell, tunable by tension

Etymology

From Italian 'timpani', plural of 'timpano', from Latin 'tympanum' (drum).
