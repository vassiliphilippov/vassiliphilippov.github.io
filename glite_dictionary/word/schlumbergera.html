Schlumbergera

1. NOUN

/ ʃlʌmˈbɜːrɡərə / (AmE), / ʃlʌmˈbɜːɡərə / (BrE)
A genus of South American cacti, often grown as ornamental plants, known for their flattened stems and colorful, tubular flowers, including the popular Christmas cactus.
a genus of South American cacti

Examples

The popular Christmas cactus is actually a species of Schlumbergera.
Many gardeners prize Schlumbergera for its beautiful winter blooms.
I brought home a new Schlumbergera to add to my collection of houseplants.

Etymology

Named after French cactus collector Frédéric Schlumberger (1823–1893).

2. NOUN

/ ʃlʌmˈbɜrɡərə / (AmE), / ʃlʌmˈbɜːɡərə / (BrE)
A genus of South American cacti, often grown as houseplants, known for their segmented stems and brightly colored flowers.
genus of South American cacti

Examples

The Schlumbergera blooms indoors every winter, adding a splash of color to the living room.
Many plant enthusiasts enjoy collecting rare Schlumbergera species and hybrids.
Because Schlumbergera are epiphytic, they thrive in well-draining, organic-rich soil.

Etymology

Named after Frédéric Schlumberger (1823–1893), a French cactus collector.
