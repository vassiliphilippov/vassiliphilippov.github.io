Kansas

1. PROPN

/ ˈkænzəs / (AmE), / ˈkænzəs / (BrE)
A state in the midwestern United States, with Topeka as its capital.
U.S. midwestern state

Examples

Many people travel through Kansas on their way across the Midwest.
The tornado season in Kansas can be particularly severe.
Dorothy lived in Kansas before her adventures in Oz.

Etymology

Named after the Kansas River, which was named after the Kansa Native American tribe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænzəs / (AmE), / ˈkænzəs / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Siouan language family, historically living in the Kansas River valley in present-day Kansas.
a Native American people of Kansas

Examples

The Kansas were known for their skill in hunting and farming along the river banks.
A group of Kansas elders shared stories about their tribe's history with local students.
Anthropologists have studied the culture and traditions of the Kansas people extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kansa - another word for the Kansas people

Etymology

From the indigenous tribal name, itself derived from a Siouan language word for 'south wind.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.zəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.zəs / (BrE)
the Dhegiha dialect of the Siouan language family spoken by the Kansa people, also known as the Kansa language
Native American language of the Kansa people

Examples

The Kansas language is now considered endangered and has few native speakers left.
Linguists have documented traditional stories told in Kansas to help with language preservation.
She studied Kansas as part of her research into Dhegiha dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kansa - the Kansa people; Native American group after whom the state of Kansas is named

Etymology

From the name of the Kansa people, sometimes spelled Kanza; their autonym is 'Káⁿza.'
