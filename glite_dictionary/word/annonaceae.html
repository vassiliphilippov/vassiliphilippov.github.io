Annonaceae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈoʊnəˌsiː / (AmE), / əˌnɒnəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs, including the custard apple and soursop, known for aromatic fruits and flowers
family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Annonaceae family includes many species valued for their edible fruits.
Researchers studied the distribution of Annonaceae in Amazonian rainforests.
Many perfume ingredients are derived from the aromatic flowers of Annonaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Annona + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌanəˈneɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs, including the custard apple and cherimoya, known for aromatic flowers and edible fruits.
family of tropical trees/shrubs (custard apple family)

Examples

The Annonaceae family includes many species valued for their edible fruits.
Several members of the Annonaceae are cultivated in tropical regions worldwide.
Botanists studied the aromatic flowers typical of plants in the Annonaceae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Annonaceae, from Annona, the type genus, plus -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
