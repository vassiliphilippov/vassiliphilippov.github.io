Pitt the Elder

1. PROPN

/ pɪt ði ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / pɪt ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
(1708–1778) An English statesman and prime minister, recognized for his leadership during the Seven Years' War and significant influence on British politics.
English statesman and prime minister (1708–1778)

Examples

Pitt the Elder played a crucial role in guiding Britain to victory during the Seven Years' War.
Many historians credit Pitt the Elder with reforming the British military and colonial administration.
The legacy of Pitt the Elder continues to influence British political thought today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Pitt the Younger - British statesman and son of Pitt the Elder, also a prime minister

Etymology

Nickname to distinguish William Pitt, 1st Earl of Chatham, from his son William Pitt the Younger.
