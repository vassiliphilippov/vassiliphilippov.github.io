optimistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒptɪˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
expecting the best possible outcome; hopeful and confident about the future
expecting good outcomes; hopeful

Examples

Despite the setbacks, she remained optimistic about the outcome of the project.
The weather forecast is optimistic, predicting sunshine for the entire weekend.
He gave an optimistic speech to inspire the team after their loss.

Synset Examples

in an optimistic mood
optimistic plans
took an optimistic view

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst possible outcome; having a negative outlook
  • Synonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism about a future event

Etymology

From Latin 'optimus' (best) via French 'optimiste'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːp.təˈmɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒp.tɪˈmɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
expecting that good things will happen or that a situation will have a positive outcome
expecting the best outcome

Examples

Despite the setbacks, she remains optimistic about the future of the project.
Economists are optimistic that the market will recover by the end of the year.
His optimistic attitude helps motivate the whole team during difficult times.

Synset Examples

an affirmative outlook

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst to happen

Etymology

From French *optimiste*, from Latin *optimum* (the best).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01823412-s
Synset POS: s
