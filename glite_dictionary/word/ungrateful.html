ungrateful

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪtfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪtfəl / (BrE)
(of a task or situation) unpleasant, disagreeable, or unrewarding.
disagreeable or thankless (of tasks, etc.)

Examples

The manager assigned me the ungrateful duty of delivering the bad news to the team.
Sorting through years of neglected paperwork proved to be an ungrateful task for anyone involved.
He bravely undertook the ungrateful job of mediating between the two hostile departments.

Synset Examples

I will not perform the ungrateful task of comparing cases of failure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thankless - (of a task) difficult or unpleasant and unlikely to be appreciated

Etymology

From un- (not) + grateful; influenced by Latin ingratus (unpleasant, thankless).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪt.fəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪt.fəl / (BrE)
not showing or feeling thanks for something done or given; lacking gratitude.
not showing gratitude

Examples

After everything we did for him, he was still ungrateful and rude.
She felt ungrateful for complaining when so many people had helped her.
It hurt to see her children act so ungrateful on her birthday.

Synset Examples

ungrateful heirs
How sharper than a serpent's tooth it is / To have a thankless child!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thankless - showing or feeling no gratitude
  • Antonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks

Etymology

From Latin 'ingratus' (unpleasing, not thankful), via Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01150547-a
Synset POS: a
