Ulmaceae

1. NOUN

/ ʌlˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / ʌlˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous trees and shrubs including the elms and related genera.
family of trees and shrubs (elms, etc.)

Examples

The Ulmaceae are well known for their hardwood trees, such as the common elm.
Many species within the Ulmaceae family provide important habitats for wildlife.
Research into the Ulmaceae has focused on disease resistance among elm trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin 'Ulmus' (elm) with the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ ʌlˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / ʌlˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including the elms, hackberries, and related trees and shrubs, classified in the order Urticales.
family of trees and shrubs including elms
medical

Examples

The Ulmaceae family is best known for its elm trees, which are common in temperate regions.
Many members of the Ulmaceae have serrated leaves and are valued for timber and shade.
Scientists are studying the disease resistance of several Ulmaceae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of tree in the genus Ulmus, part of Ulmaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Ulmus (elm) and the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
