phony

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊni / (AmE), / ˈfəʊni / (BrE)
Not genuine; intended to deceive or mislead by appearance.
fraudulent or misleading in appearance

Examples

That document looks phony to me; I'd check if it's real.
The company made phony claims about the benefits of its product.
She gave a phony smile, trying to hide her real feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; a forgery or counterfeit
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic; truly what something is said to be

Etymology

American English origin, early 20th century, probably from 'fawney' (a gilt brass ring used by swindlers).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊni / (AmE), / ˈfəʊni / (BrE)
a person who pretends to have beliefs, feelings, or qualities that they do not really have in order to deceive others
deceptive or insincere person

Examples

Everyone could tell he was a phony pretending to care about the community.
The town was full of phonies trying to impress the tourists.
She called him a phony because he never meant what he said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - a person who is not what they pretend to be
  • Synonym: hypocrite - a person who claims to have moral standards or beliefs they do not actually possess
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic

Etymology

Probably alteration of 'fawney', a slang term for a gilt brass ring used in swindles, of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10215212-n
Synset POS: n
