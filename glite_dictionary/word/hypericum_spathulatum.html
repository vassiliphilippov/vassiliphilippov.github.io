Hypericum spathulatum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm spæθjʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɪərɪkəm spæθjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A stiff shrub with oblong, entire leaves and dense clusters (cymes) of yellow flowers, belonging to the Hypericum genus.
species of shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

Hypericum spathulatum is often found growing in rocky, mountainous areas across the Mediterranean region.
The bright yellow flowers of Hypericum spathulatum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Hypericum spathulatum due to its traditional herbal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. John's wort - common name for plants in the genus Hypericum

Etymology

From New Latin, 'Hypericum' (the genus name) and 'spathulatum,' from Latin 'spathulatus' meaning 'spoon-shaped,' referring to the leaves.
