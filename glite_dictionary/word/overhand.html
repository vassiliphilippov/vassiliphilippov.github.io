overhand

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvərˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhænd / (BrE)
(of sewing) sewn together with overhand stitches, which are close vertical stitches that pass over and draw two edges together.
sewn with overhand stitches

Examples

The fabric edges were overhand for extra durability.
She carefully finished the quilt with overhand seams along each border.
Each patch on the blanket was overhand, making the pieces tightly joined.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00677289-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvərˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhænd / (BrE)
(of a stroke, throw, etc.) made with the hand brought forward and down from above shoulder level.
performed with hand moving down from above shoulder

Examples

The pitcher delivered an overhand throw, surprising the batter.
She preferred using an overhand stroke in her tennis serve.
Swimmers often use the overhand technique in freestyle events.

Synset Examples

an overhand pitch
an overhand stroke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underhand - performed with the hand moving upward below shoulder level

Etymology

From over- + hand, describing hand movement from above.
