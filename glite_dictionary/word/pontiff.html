pontiff

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːntɪf / (AmE), / ˈpɒntɪf / (BrE)
the Pope; the head of the Roman Catholic Church
the Pope; leader of Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The new pontiff was greeted by crowds at St. Peter's Square.
During his tenure, the pontiff emphasized dialogue between faiths.
Many Catholics look to the pontiff for spiritual leadership and guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pope - the Bishop of Rome and head of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin pontifex, meaning 'bridge-builder', a title of high priests in ancient Rome, later used for bishops and the Pope.
