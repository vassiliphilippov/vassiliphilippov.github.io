turban

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrbən / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbən / (BrE)
A headdress made by winding a long piece of cloth around the head, especially worn in South Asia, the Middle East, and by some Muslim men.
headdress of cloth wound around the head

Examples

He wore a bright red turban at the wedding ceremony.
Sikhs are known for wearing a turban as a symbol of faith.
The patterned turban protected him from the desert sun.

Etymology

From Persian 'dulband', through Turkish 'tülbent' and French 'turban'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.bən / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bən / (BrE)
a small, close-fitting, round woman's hat, often twisted or draped in a style resembling a turban
woman's round, turban-like hat

Examples

She wore a stylish turban to the dinner party, matching her elegant dress.
The actress was photographed wearing a velvet turban from the new collection.
During the 1940s, the turban became a popular accessory in women's fashion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillbox hat - a small, round, brimless woman's hat with straight, upright sides

Etymology

From Persian 'dulband', referring to a wrap for the head; the fashion hat style imitates the original form.
