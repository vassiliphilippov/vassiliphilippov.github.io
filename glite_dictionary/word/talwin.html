Talwin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæl.wɪn / (AmE), / ˈtæl.wɪn / (BrE)
A trademark for an analgesic drug (pentazocine) that is less addictive than morphine, used for the relief of moderate to severe pain.
analgesic drug (pentazocine) trade name
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Talwin to manage her postoperative pain.
Some patients may tolerate Talwin better than other opioids.
Because Talwin is less addictive than morphine, it is sometimes preferred for pain relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentazocine - the generic name of the drug marketed as Talwin
