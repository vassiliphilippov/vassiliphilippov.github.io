vocally

1. ADV

/ ˈvoʊkəli / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəli / (BrE)
In a way that uses the voice; in a vocal manner, by speaking or expressing oneself aloud.
using the voice; aloud

Examples

She protested vocally during the meeting, making sure her concerns were heard.
The coach vocally encouraged his team from the sidelines throughout the game.
The issue was vocally supported by several community leaders at the forum.

Synset Examples

she defended herself vocally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silently - without making any sound; not vocally

Etymology

From Latin vocālis (vocal), from vox (voice).
