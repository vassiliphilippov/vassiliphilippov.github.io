to revolve

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to move in a circle around a central point or axis
move in a circle around a center or axis

Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun once every year.
The dancers gracefully revolved around the stage during the show.
As the wheel revolves, it powers the mechanism inside the clock.

Synset Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun
The lamb roast rotates on a spit over the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - to turn around a central point or axis

Etymology

From Latin 'revolvere' (to roll back, turn over), from 're-' (back) + 'volvere' (to roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02048992-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to move in a circular course or orbit around a central point or object
move in a circular orbit

Examples

The planets revolve around the sun in precise orbits.
Satellites revolve around the Earth to provide global communication coverage.
As electrons revolve around the nucleus, they create magnetic fields.

Synset Examples

The moon orbits around the Earth
The planets are orbiting the sun
electrons orbit the nucleus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - turn or spin around an axis
  • Synonym: circle - move all the way around something in a circular path

Etymology

From Latin revolvere (to roll back, revolve), from re- (again) + volvere (to roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02047875-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to cause something to move in a circular path or to rotate around a central point or axis
cause to move in a circle or rotate

Examples

The teacher used a model to revolve the planets around the sun.
She could revolve the sculpture with a gentle push.
Engineers revolve the wheel to check for balance.

Synset Examples

She rolled the ball
They rolled their eyes at his words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - to move or cause to move in a circle around an axis
  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly on a central point

Etymology

From Latin revolvere (to roll back, to revolve), from re- (again) + volvere (to roll).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
