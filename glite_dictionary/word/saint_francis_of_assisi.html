Saint Francis of Assisi

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs əv əˈsiːsi / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfrænsɪs ɒv əˈsiːsi / (BrE)
(1181–1226) An Italian Catholic friar, preacher, and founder of the Franciscan Order, known for his devotion to poverty and nature.
Italian Catholic friar and saint

Examples

Many animal lovers consider Saint Francis of Assisi to be the patron saint of animals.
The teachings of Saint Francis of Assisi emphasize humility and compassion for all creatures.
Each October, some churches hold services to bless pets in honor of Saint Francis of Assisi.

Etymology

From Italian 'San Francesco d'Assisi', meaning 'Saint Francis from Assisi'.
