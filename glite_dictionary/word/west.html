West

1. PROPN

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
the region of the United States located to the west of the Mississippi River, often referring to states such as California, Nevada, and Colorado.
region of western United States

Examples

Many pioneers traveled to the West seeking new opportunities during the 19th century.
The West is known for its national parks and stunning mountain ranges.
She moved from New York to the West after graduating from college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midwest - region of north-central United States between the Appalachian Mountains and the Rockies
  • Synonym: South - southern region of the United States

2. PROPN

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
the countries of Europe and, later, North America and South America, considered as a group with shared political and cultural values, especially in contrast to other parts of the world
Europe and the Americas as a group

Examples

The economic influence of the West has shaped global trade policies.
During the Cold War, the West and the Soviet bloc were often in opposition.
Some countries aspire to adopt the values commonly associated with the West.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: East - Asian and sometimes Middle Eastern countries, contrasted with 'the West' culturally and politically
