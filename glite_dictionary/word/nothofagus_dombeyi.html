Nothofagus dombeyi

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌθoʊˈfeɪɡəs dɒmˈbeɪ.i / (AmE), / nəʊˌθɒˈfæɡəs dɒmˈbeɪ.i / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to Chile and Argentina, whose leafy boughs are often used for thatching; also known as Dombey's beech.
Chilean evergreen tree used for thatching

Examples

The roofs of many rural homes are still thatched with branches from the Nothofagus dombeyi.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Nothofagus dombeyi in the Patagonian forests.
Nothofagus dombeyi is prized for its durability and attractive evergreen leaves.

Etymology

From genus name Nothofagus (Greek 'nothos' meaning 'false' + 'Fagus', beech genus) and species named for French botanist Joseph Dombey.
