splintery

1. ADJ

/ ˈsplɪntɚi / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntəri / (BrE)
consisting of, resembling, or full of sharp, slender fragments called splinters, especially in reference to wood
having or resembling sharp wood fragments

Examples

The old fence was so splintery that I got a splinter in my hand.
We should sand the splintery edges before painting the bench.
He avoided walking barefoot on the splintery wooden deck.

Synset Examples

a rough splintery floor of old pine boards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splintered - broken into sharp, slender fragments

Etymology

From 'splinter' (a small sharp fragment of wood or similar material) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsplɪntəri / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntəri / (BrE)
(of a material or object) liable to break into sharp, slender pieces; characterized by or resembling splinters.
liable to break into sharp, slender pieces

Examples

The old wooden fence had become splintery and unsafe for children to climb.
Be careful with that splintery board; it could hurt your hands.
After years of exposure, the bench's surface turned rough and splintery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittle - easily broken; not flexible
  • Synonym: splinter - a small, thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, or similar material

Etymology

From 'splinter' + -y, meaning 'characterized by splinters' or 'prone to splinter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00713070-s
Synset POS: s
