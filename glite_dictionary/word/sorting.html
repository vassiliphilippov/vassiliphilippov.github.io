sorting

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of arranging things into groups or categories according to shared characteristics or order
arranging into groups or categories

Examples

The sorting of the mail was completed by noon every day.
Proper sorting of waste helps with recycling and environmental protection.
After the data was collected, the next step was the sorting of results into meaningful categories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of organizing things into categories

Etymology

From the verb 'sort', from Middle French 'sortir' (to arrange, assign a lot or fate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of arranging or classifying items into groups based on a specific criterion
arranging items into groups

Examples

Efficient sorting of data speeds up computer processing.
After the delivery, staff began the sorting of parcels by destination.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouping - the act of putting things together in groups

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of arranging or grouping things according to class, kind, or size
arranging by class, kind, or size

Examples

The sorting of mail is done at the central office before delivery.
Children enjoyed the activity of sorting blocks by color and size.
After the harvest, the sorting of apples is completed by hand to separate the best fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classifying - assigning things to categories
  • Synonym: organizing - arranging things systematically

Etymology

From the verb 'sort,' ultimately from Old French *sortir* (to arrange, allot).
