spirt

1. VERB

/ spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to gush forth or shoot out suddenly in a stream or jet.
to gush or jet forth suddenly (liquid)

Examples

When she squeezed the bottle, water spirted onto the floor.
Oil spirted out of the engine when he removed the cap.
Blood spirted from the wound, alarming the onlookers.

Synset Examples

water gushed forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - to gush forth suddenly in a stream

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'spurt', influenced by Middle English 'spirtle' (to squirt)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02072341-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
A sudden brief burst or jet of liquid or other substance.
sudden jet or discharge of liquid

Examples

A sudden spirt of water shot out of the broken pipe.
He squeezed the bottle, and a spirt of ketchup landed on his plate.
The whale surfaced with a spirt of mist from its blowhole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - A sudden jet or burst of liquid or activity

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'spurt' of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07450700-n
Synset POS: n
