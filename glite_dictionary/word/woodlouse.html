woodlouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌlaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.laʊs / (BrE)
A small terrestrial crustacean with a flat, segmented, oval body, usually found in damp habitats.
small terrestrial crustacean

Examples

I found a woodlouse under the flowerpot in the garden.
Children often collect woodlice as part of science projects at school.
The woodlouse curled up tightly when disturbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillbug - terrestrial isopod that can roll into a ball

Etymology

From wood + louse; 'louse' used for small creeping animals.
