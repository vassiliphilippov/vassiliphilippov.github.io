Typha

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪfə / (AmE), / ˈtɪfə / (BrE)
A genus of tall marsh plants with cylindrical flower spikes, commonly known as cattails or reed maces, found in wetlands.
genus of marsh plants (cattails)

Examples

Wetlands across North America are often dominated by Typha species, providing critical habitat for wildlife.
The distinctive brown spikes of Typha make them easy to identify along riverbanks.
Researchers studied the growth of Typha in response to changes in water levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - marsh plant with a brown, cigar-shaped flower spike
  • Synonym: reed mace - British term for cattail

Etymology

From Latin *typha*, from Greek *typhē* (a type of marsh plant).
