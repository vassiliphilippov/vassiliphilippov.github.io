black-and-white

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
considering things as entirely one way or another, without recognizing intermediate possibilities; lacking nuance or shades of meaning
lacking nuance; sharply divided categories

Examples

She disliked his black-and-white approach to complex problems.
The issue isn't as black-and-white as you make it out to be.
His views are very black-and-white, leaving no room for compromise.

Synset Examples

he rejected a black-and-white world
there are no black-and-white certainties
there were no grey areas, you were either for him or against him, he was all black-and-white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binary - involving only two options or states
  • Antonym: nuanced - characterized by subtle shades of meaning or gradation

Etymology

From the contrasting colors black and white, used metaphorically to represent mutually exclusive options.
