tillable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪləbəl / (BrE)
(of land) suitable for being ploughed and used for growing crops.
able to be cultivated (land)

Examples

Only the tillable land along the river was used for planting corn.
Much of the region is mountainous, with little tillable soil available for agriculture.
Farmers seek out tillable acres that can be efficiently worked with modern machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arable - (of land) suitable for growing crops; able to be ploughed
  • Antonym: barren - (of land) unable to support the growth of crops

Etymology

From 'till' (to cultivate land) + '-able' (able to be).
