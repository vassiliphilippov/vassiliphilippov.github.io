Paxil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæk.sɪl / (AmE), / ˈpæk.sɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked name for paroxetine, a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) used as an antidepressant.
antidepressant medication (trade name)

Examples

The doctor prescribed Paxil to treat her depression.
Many patients have reported side effects while taking Paxil.
He has been on Paxil for several months and noticed significant improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paroxetine - the generic name for the drug marketed as Paxil, an SSRI antidepressant
