Buddha

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdə / (AmE), / ˈbʊdə / (BrE)
a person who has achieved a state of perfect enlightenment, especially Siddhartha Gautama, the founder of Buddhism
enlightened person in Buddhism; the historical Siddhartha Gautama

Examples

Many people meditate in hopes of reaching the understanding attained by the Buddha.
The teachings of the Buddha have inspired millions around the world.
In some traditions, anyone who achieves enlightenment can be called a Buddha.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bodhisattva - a being destined to become a Buddha but who postpones enlightenment to help others

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Buddha' meaning 'enlightened, awakened'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbuːdə, ˈbʊdə / (AmE), / ˈbʊdə, ˈbuːdə / (BrE)
the title given to Siddhartha Gautama (c. 563–c. 483 BC), the founder of Buddhism, who attained enlightenment and shared his teachings to help others reach spiritual awakening
founder of Buddhism; enlightened teacher

Examples

Many people visit temples to honor the Buddha and meditate on his teachings.
The life story of the Buddha has inspired millions around the world.
Statues of the Buddha are commonly found in homes and gardens as symbols of peace and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gautama Buddha - another name for Siddhartha Gautama, the historical Buddha

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'buddha' meaning 'enlightened, awakened'.
