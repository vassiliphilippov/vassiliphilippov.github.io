Uub

1. NOUN

/ juː ˌjuː ˈbiː / (AmE), / juː juː ˈbiː / (BrE)
the former temporary systematic name for the chemical element with atomic number 112, now known as copernicium
former temporary name of element 112 (copernicium)

Examples

Scientists referred to element 112 as Uub prior to its official naming as copernicium.
The discovery of Uub was confirmed by an international team of researchers in 1996.
In earlier research papers, Uub was often listed in the table of elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copernicium - the chemical element with atomic number 112

Etymology

From Latin roots 'un' (one) and 'bi' (two), denoting atomic number 112, following IUPAC element naming conventions.
