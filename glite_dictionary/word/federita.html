federita

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈriːtə / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈriːtə / (BrE)
A variety of Sudanese sorghum with exceptionally large, soft, white grains.
Sudanese sorghum with large white grains

Examples

The farmer decided to plant federita because of its large, nutritious grains.
Many Sudanese families rely on federita as a staple crop for making porridge and bread.
Scientists are studying federita to improve grain size and yield in other varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of cultivated grasses known for their edible grains
