instilling

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gradually teaching or impressing an idea, attitude, or habit on someone, especially by persistent repetition.
the act of gradually teaching or impressing

Examples

His parents believed in the instilling of strong moral values from a young age.
The instilling of confidence in students is vital for their success.
Through constant practice and encouragement, coaches focus on the instilling of discipline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inculcation - the act of teaching by persistent instruction
  • Synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically

Etymology

From the verb 'instill,' which comes from Latin 'instillare' (to drip in).
