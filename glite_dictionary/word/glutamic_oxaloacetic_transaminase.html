glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtæmɪk ɒkˌsæloʊəˈsɛtɪk trænzˈæmɪneɪs / (AmE), / ɡluˈtæmɪk ɒkˌsæləʊəˈsɛtɪk trænzˈæmɪneɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of an amino group from glutamic acid to oxaloacetic acid, important in amino acid metabolism and used as a marker for liver function
an enzyme used as a liver function marker
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase in the blood may indicate liver damage.
Doctors often check glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase as part of a routine liver function test.
The results showed a significant increase in glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase after the patient began the new medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspartate transaminase - another name for glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase
  • Degree Lower: alanine transaminase - an enzyme used as a marker for liver function (ALT)

Etymology

From 'glutamic acid', 'oxaloacetic acid', and 'transaminase' (an enzyme that transfers amino groups).
