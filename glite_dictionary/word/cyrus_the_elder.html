Cyrus the Elder

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪrəs ði ˈɛldər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪrəs ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
King of Persia and founder of the Persian Empire, who reigned circa 600–529 BC.
King of Persia, founder of the Persian Empire

Examples

Cyrus the Elder unified the Medes and Persians to create one of the largest empires of the ancient world.
Many historians regard Cyrus the Elder as a model ruler for his policy of tolerance toward conquered peoples.
The tomb of Cyrus the Elder is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Pasargadae, Iran.
