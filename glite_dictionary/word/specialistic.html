specialistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌspɛʃəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or showing a narrow, highly focused field of knowledge, training, or expertise
showing specialized training or focus

Examples

The course provides a specialistic approach to environmental science, focusing on conservation techniques.
Her specialistic knowledge in rare diseases made her a valuable asset to the research team.
Many specialistic journals only accept articles on highly specific subjects.

Synset Examples

specialist training

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialized - relating to or designed for a particular purpose or area of knowledge

Etymology

From specialist + -ic, modeled after similar English adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˌspɛʃəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of specialists or a specialized field.
relating to specialists

Examples

The research paper adopted a specialistic approach, focusing on a narrow medical subject.
Her specialistic skills made her the best candidate for the advanced engineering team.
Many academic journals have become increasingly specialistic in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialized - relating to a specific area or subject; highly focused or developed

Etymology

Formed from 'specialist' plus the suffix '-ic.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02810811-a
Synset POS: a
