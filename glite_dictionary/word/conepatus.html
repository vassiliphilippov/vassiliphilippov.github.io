Conepatus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈnɛpətəs / (AmE), / kəˈnɛpətəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the weasel family (Mustelidae), known as hog-nosed skunks, native to the Americas.
a genus of hog-nosed skunks

Examples

The Conepatus genus includes several species of hog-nosed skunks found throughout Central and South America.
Unlike the more familiar striped skunk, a Conepatus has a distinctive long snout for digging.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Conepatus genus in the forests of Argentina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small, black-and-white mammal known for its ability to spray a foul-smelling liquid

Etymology

From Greek 'kōnē' (dust) and 'pous, podos' (foot), possibly referencing the animal's digging habit.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈnɛpətəs / (AmE), / kəˈnɛpətəs / (BrE)
A genus of skunks known as hog-nosed skunks, native to the Americas, recognized for their distinctively elongated snouts.
genus of hog-nosed skunks

Examples

The Conepatus genus includes several species of hog-nosed skunks found across Central and South America.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of the Conepatus genus in the Andes mountains.
Unlike other skunks, those in the Conepatus genus have long, flexible snouts adapted for digging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small mammal known for its ability to spray a foul-smelling liquid

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used for scientific classification of these skunks.
