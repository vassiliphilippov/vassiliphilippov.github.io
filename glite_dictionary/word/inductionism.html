inductionism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʌkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the philosophical approach in science that emphasizes deriving general laws from observation and evidence, rather than through deduction.
deriving laws from observation in science

Examples

Many early natural philosophers subscribed to inductionism as the only valid approach to discovering scientific laws.
Critics of inductionism argue that observations alone are insufficient for developing comprehensive scientific theories.
Despite its challenges, inductionism remains influential in debates about scientific methodology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deductionism - the doctrine emphasizing deduction over induction in science
  • Synonym: empiricism - philosophy prioritizing knowledge from sensory experience

Etymology

From 'induction' (from Latin inductio) + '-ism'.
