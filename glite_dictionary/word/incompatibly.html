incompatibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæmpətəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is not compatible; such that things cannot exist or work together without conflict.
in an incompatible manner

Examples

The two software programs functioned incompatibly, causing frequent system crashes.
Their personalities clashed so incompatibly that living together became impossible.
These chemicals react incompatibly when mixed, creating a hazardous situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incompatible - unable to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

Formed from 'incompatible' + '-ly', based on Latin roots: 'in-' (not) + 'compatibilis' (capable of existing together).
