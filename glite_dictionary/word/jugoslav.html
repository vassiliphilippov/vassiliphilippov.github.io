Jugoslav

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːgəˌslɑːv / (AmE), / ˈjuːgəˌslɑːv / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Yugoslavia, especially as used historically
person from Yugoslavia (historical term)
archaic

Examples

Her grandfather was a proud Jugoslav who moved to England after the war.
A delegation of Jugoslavs attended the international conference in 1952.
In the 1970s, many Jugoslav artists gained recognition across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yugoslav - a native or inhabitant of Yugoslavia (modern spelling)

Etymology

Variant of 'Yugoslav', from Serbo-Croatian *Jugoslav* (jug 'south' + slav 'Slav').
