Europa

1. PROPN

/ juˈroʊpə / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpə / (BrE)
the fourth largest moon of Jupiter, covered with a smooth shell of frozen water
moon of Jupiter (icy surface)

Examples

Scientists believe that Europa may contain a subsurface ocean beneath its icy crust.
The Galileo spacecraft captured detailed images of Europa during its mission to Jupiter.
Some astrobiologists consider Europa as a promising place to search for extraterrestrial life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ganymede - the largest moon of Jupiter
  • Degree Lower: Io - a large volcanic moon of Jupiter

Etymology

From Latin Europa, from Ancient Greek Εὐρώπη (Eurṓpē), a name from Greek mythology.
