quiet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪət / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət / (BrE)
a period during which the weather is calm or peaceful, especially after or between storms
period of calm weather

Examples

After hours of heavy rain, there was a welcome quiet before the next wave of the storm.
Sailors waited for the quiet to end before raising anchor again.
The sudden quiet after the howling winds made the air seem eerily peaceful.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lull - a temporary interval of quiet or lack of activity

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwaɪət / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət / (BrE)
(of the sun) characterized by a low level of surface activity, such as sunspots.
of the sun: having little surface activity

Examples

During a quiet phase, the sun displays few or no sunspots visible from Earth.
Scientists monitor changes in solar radiation during both quiet and active periods of the sun.
The last solar minimum was marked by an unusually quiet sun with prolonged inactivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - (of the sun) showing a high level of surface phenomena such as sunspots
