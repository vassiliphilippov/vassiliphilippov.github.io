lycaenid

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnɪd / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːnɪd / (BrE)
any butterfly belonging to the family Lycaenidae, typically small and brightly colored
butterfly of the Lycaenidae family

Examples

The vibrant blue lycaenid fluttered among the wildflowers in the meadow.
Several species of lycaenid are known for their intricate wing patterns and mutual relationships with ants.
Researchers observed a decline in lycaenid populations due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From modern Latin Lycaenidae, the family name, based on Lycaena (a genus of butterflies).
