cross-index

1. VERB

/ krɔs ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / krɒs ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
to create or include references in an index that direct the reader from one entry to others related to it
create references between index entries

Examples

To make the catalog more useful, the librarian decided to cross-index all the new subjects.
These medical records are cross-indexed for quick retrieval based on multiple criteria.
The database allows users to cross-index entries for comprehensive searches.

Synset Examples

These references are cross-indexed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: index - to list or catalog systematically
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02478381-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈɪnˌdɛks / (AmE), / krɒs ˈɪnˌdɛks / (BrE)
a reference within a book or document that directs the reader to related information in another part of the same work
reference to another place in the same work

Examples

Please use the cross-index to find related chapters throughout the textbook.
The glossary includes a helpful cross-index for technical terms.
Editors often cross-index subjects to improve the usability of manuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-reference - a reference in one part of a book or document to related information elsewhere
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06777578-n
Synset POS: n
