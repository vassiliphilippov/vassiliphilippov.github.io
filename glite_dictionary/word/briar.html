briar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
A wild rose with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, and bright pink flowers, common in Eurasia and bearing scarlet hips.
prickly Eurasian wild rose

Examples

The briar bushes in the hedgerow were covered with bright pink blossoms in spring.
She carefully picked her way through the briar, avoiding the sharp thorns on the stems.
The scent of crushed briar leaves filled the air as they walked through the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose - a woody perennial plant of the genus Rosa, known for its fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Old English brēr (prickly bush or briar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
A tobacco pipe made from the hard root of the tree heath (Erica arborea), valued for its heat resistance and durability.
a tobacco pipe made from tree heath root

Examples

He collected antique briars because of their unique grain patterns and craftsmanship.
After dinner, he relaxed with his favorite briar in hand, enjoying a quiet smoke.
The artisan carved the briar pipe from a single piece of tree heath root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube with a bowl for smoking tobacco

Etymology

From French *bruyère* (heath, heather), referring to the root of the tree heath used to make pipes.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean shrub (Erica arborea) with fragrant white flowers and hard woody roots used to make tobacco pipes
Mediterranean shrub with woody roots for pipes

Examples

The best tobacco pipes are often crafted from briar due to its durability and heat resistance.
She admired the delicate white blooms on the briar growing in the Mediterranean garden.
Traditional pipe-makers prize the hard woody roots of the briar for their unique properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a shrubland habitat dominated by low-growing plants, especially Erica species

Etymology

From French 'bruyère' (heath), referring to the plant Erica arborea.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
A prickly woody vine, especially one found in the eastern United States, with tangled growth, tough round stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries.
prickly woody vine of eastern U.S.

Examples

The path was overgrown with briar, making it difficult to walk through without getting scratched.
After the storm, thick briar blocked the entrance to the old garden.
Birds sometimes build nests among the briar for protection from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - a prickly, scrambling vine or shrub, particularly of the genus Rubus

Etymology

Probably from Old English brēr (prickly bush).
