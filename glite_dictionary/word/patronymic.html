patronymic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæt.rəˈnɪm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpat.rəˈnɪm.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a name) derived from the name of a father or ancestor, especially by the addition of a prefix or suffix.
derived from a father's or ancestor's name

Examples

In many cultures, a patronymic surname is inherited from the father.
Icelandic last names are often patronymic, reflecting the father's first name.
The study focused on the use of patronymic naming systems in different regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'patrōnymikos', from 'patēr' (father) and 'onyma' (name).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpætɹəˈnɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpætrəˈnɪmɪk / (BrE)
a family name derived from the name of one's father or a paternal ancestor, often with a prefix or suffix added
surname from father's or ancestor's name

Examples

In Iceland, each person's last name is a patronymic rather than a family surname.
'MacDonald' is a classic Scottish patronymic, meaning 'son of Donald'.
The use of patronymic surnames was once common in many parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matronymic - a name derived from one's mother's or female ancestor's name

Etymology

From Greek 'patrōnymikos', from 'patēr' (father) + 'onyma' (name).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
