to fabricate

1. VERB

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to construct or manufacture something, typically from prepared components
manufacture or construct from components

Examples

The company fabricates metal frames for industrial use.
Modern factories fabricate thousands of components every day.
He fabricated the device using materials from the hardware store.

Synset Examples

the company fabricates plastic chairs
They manufacture small toys
He manufactured a popular cereal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufacture - to make something on a large scale using machinery

Etymology

From Latin fabricatus, past participle of fabricare (to fashion, build), from fabrica (workshop).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01656984-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to invent false information in order to deceive someone
to invent or falsify

Examples

He was accused of trying to fabricate evidence to avoid jail.
The journalist lost her job after it was discovered she had fabricated interviews.
If you fabricate stories, people will stop trusting you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to make false by altering evidence or information
  • Synonym: to invent - to create something that is not true; to make up
  • Antonym: to tell the truth - to say what is true, not lie

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricatus', past participle of 'fabricari' (to make, fashion), from 'fabrica' (a workshop).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01637966-v
Synset POS: v
