jeweller

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːələ / (BrE)
A person or business that makes, sells, or repairs jewelry and sometimes watches.
person who sells or makes jewelry

Examples

The jeweller showed us a selection of diamond rings.
After inheriting her grandmother's brooch, she took it to the jeweller to have it cleaned.
Local jewellers often offer custom design services for engagement rings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewelry - objects such as rings, necklaces, or bracelets that are made of valuable metals and stones

Etymology

From Old French 'juelier', from 'jouel' (jewel), of Latin origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːələr / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːələ / (BrE)
a person who makes, repairs, or sells jewelry
person who makes or sells jewelry

Examples

The jeweller carefully crafted a beautiful diamond necklace for her client.
My grandmother took her ring to the jeweller to have it repaired.
Every piece in the store was handmade by a local jeweller.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldsmith - a person who makes or repairs objects from gold

Etymology

From Old French 'juelier', from 'jouel' (jewel).
