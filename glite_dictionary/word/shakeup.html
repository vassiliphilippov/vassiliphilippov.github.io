shakeup

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
a major reorganization, typically involving significant changes in personnel or structure within an organization
major organizational reorganization

Examples

The company underwent a massive shakeup after the new CEO took over.
There are rumors of an upcoming shakeup in the coaching staff.
Following the scandal, a government shakeup was inevitable.

Synset Examples

a committee was appointed to oversee the reorganization of the curriculum
top officials were forced out in the cabinet shakeup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reorganization - the act of changing the way in which something is organized
