midwifery

1. NOUN

/ mɪdˈwɪfəri / (AmE), / mɪdˈwɪfəri / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with childbirth and the care of women giving birth
branch of medicine for childbirth

Examples

She decided to study midwifery because she wanted to support women during childbirth.
Advances in midwifery have greatly improved maternal and infant health.
The hospital offers an excellent midwifery program for expecting mothers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstetrics - the branch of medicine and surgery concerned with childbirth

Etymology

From Old English mid (with) + wif (woman); originally meaning 'with-woman', referring to a woman assisting childbirth.

2. NOUN

/ mɪdˈwɪfəri / (AmE), / mɪdˈwɪfəri / (BrE)
the profession or practice of assisting women during childbirth
profession of assisting in childbirth

Examples

She chose to study midwifery because she wanted to help women during childbirth.
The practice of midwifery has a long and respected tradition in many cultures.
Many hospitals now offer midwifery services as an alternative to obstetric care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstetrics - the branch of medicine and surgery concerned with childbirth and the care of women giving birth

Etymology

From 'midwife' + '-ery'; 'midwife' from Middle English 'midwif', meaning 'with-woman'.
