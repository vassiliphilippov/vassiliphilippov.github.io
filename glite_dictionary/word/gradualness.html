gradualness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒjʊəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of happening or developing slowly over time in small stages
quality of being gradual

Examples

The gradualness of the economic recovery surprised the experts, who expected rapid improvement.
She appreciated the gradualness with which the changes were introduced into their routine.
Some plants depend on the gradualness of seasonal changes to trigger blooming.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suddenness - the quality of occurring unexpectedly or very quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'gradualis' (by steps) with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒjuəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being gradual or proceeding by slow degrees, especially of a slope or change
the quality of being gradual or slow

Examples

The gradualness of the hill made the climb much easier for the hikers.
The architect emphasized the gradualness of the staircase to ensure accessibility for everyone.
You can appreciate the gradualness of change in the landscape as you travel further south.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steepness - the quality of being steep, opposite of gradualness in a slope

Etymology

From 'gradual' + '-ness', with 'gradual' from Late Latin 'gradualis' (by steps or degrees).
