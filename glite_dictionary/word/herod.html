Herod

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrəd / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəd / (BrE)
(c.74–4 BC) King of Judea, known in the New Testament for ordering the massacre of young children in Bethlehem in an attempt to kill the infant Jesus.
king of Judea noted for the Massacre of the Innocents

Examples

According to the Gospel of Matthew, Herod ordered the killing of all boys under two years old in Bethlehem.
Many historians debate the extent of Herod's cruelty and his role in the biblical narrative.
The reign of Herod marked significant architectural development in Judea, including the expansion of the Second Temple.

Etymology

From Greek Hērōdēs, from Hebrew Hordos.
