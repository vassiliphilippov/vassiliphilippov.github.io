uniformity

1. NOUN

/ ˌjunəˈfɔrmɪti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈfɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being the same or consistent throughout, without diversity or variation
lack of variation; sameness

Examples

The uniformity of the houses made the neighborhood seem dull and lifeless.
Critics argued that too much uniformity in education stifles creativity and innovation.
She grew tired of the uniformity in her daily routine and longed for change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diversity - the state of being diverse; variety
  • Synonym: monotony - lack of variety and interest; tedious repetition and routine

Etymology

From Latin uniformitas, from uniformis (having one form).

2. NOUN

/ ˌjunɪˈfɔrmɪti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈfɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being the same in all parts or among all members; lack of variation or diversity
the state of being uniform

Examples

The uniformity of the test results impressed the scientists.
Strict uniformity in dress code is required at the academy.
There was a striking uniformity in the houses along the street.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diversity - the state of being diverse; variety
  • Synonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way

Etymology

From Latin 'uniformitas', from 'uniformis' (of one form).
