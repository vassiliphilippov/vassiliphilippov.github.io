Nucifraga columbiana

1. NOUN

/ nuˌsɪˈfreɪɡə koʊˌlʌmˈbiːənə / (AmE), / njuːˌsɪˈfreɪɡə kəˌlʌmˈbiːənə / (BrE)
The scientific name for Clark's nutcracker, a bird native to the mountainous regions of western North America.
scientific name for Clark's nutcracker (bird)

Examples

The Nucifraga columbiana is known for its remarkable memory in storing pine seeds.
Researchers observed a flock of Nucifraga columbiana in the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike many other birds, Nucifraga columbiana plays a crucial role in forest regeneration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clark's nutcracker - a North American bird known for storing pine seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'Nucifraga' meaning 'nut-breaker', 'columbiana' referring to the Columbia region.
