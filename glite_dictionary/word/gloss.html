gloss

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs / (BrE)
an explanation or translation of a word or phrase, especially in the margin or between the lines of a text
an explanatory note for a word in a text

Examples

The medieval manuscript included a helpful gloss in the margin to explain the rare term.
Scholars added a modern English gloss to the Latin text for clarity.
I struggled with the passage until I found a gloss that clarified its meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotation - a note added to explain or comment on a text

Etymology

From Greek 'glossa' meaning 'tongue, language, word', through Latin 'glossa' (a difficult or foreign word needing explanation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06756695-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡlɑs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs / (BrE)
to make a surface shiny or glossy, typically by rubbing or polishing
make shiny or glossy by rubbing

Examples

She carefully glossed the wooden table until it reflected the light.
To achieve a perfect manicure, they gloss each nail after applying the polish.
He glossed his shoes with a soft cloth before the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • Antonym: dull - to make or become less shiny or bright

Etymology

From Old French 'glose', from Late Latin 'glossa' (gloss, tongue, lustre).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01686477-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡlɑs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs / (BrE)
to give a deceptively favorable or misleading explanation or excuse for something, often to cover up faults or mistakes
deceptively explain or excuse (verb)

Examples

The spokesperson tried to gloss over the company's mistakes during the press conference.
He attempted to gloss his absence by blaming a non-existent traffic jam.
Politicians often gloss uncomfortable facts to protect their public image.

Synset Examples

color a lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitewash - to cover up wrongdoing with a misleading explanation
  • Degree Lower: to explain - to make something clear by describing it in more detail
  • Degree Higher: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Greek glōssa (tongue, language, explanation), via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00838812-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ɡlɑs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs / (BrE)
a superficially attractive appearance or explanation that is meant to conceal something unpleasant or untrue
deceptively attractive appearance

Examples

He tried to give his actions a gloss of respectability, but his motives were quickly questioned.
The company added a gloss to its report to distract from declining profits.
Beneath the gloss of her cheerful demeanor, she was deeply troubled.

  • Synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
