Crataegus mollis

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs ˈmɑlɪs / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˈmɒlɪs / (BrE)
A species of American hawthorn with red fruit, having stems and leaves densely covered with short woolly hairs.
a North American red-fruited hawthorn

Examples

The Crataegus mollis is admired for its dense, woolly foliage and bright red berries in autumn.
Gardeners in the Midwest often choose Crataegus mollis for its ornamental qualities and resilience.
During our hike, we identified a Crataegus mollis growing near the path by its unique hairy leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a genus of shrubs and trees in the rose family, bearing fruit and thorns

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crataegus' (hawthorn genus) + 'mollis' (soft), referring to the woolly hairs.
