onychophoran

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnɪˈkɑːfəˌrən / (AmE), / ˌɒnɪˈkɒfərən / (BrE)
any of various worm-like, velvet-skinned carnivorous animals found in tropical forests, having features of both arthropods and annelid worms; also called 'velvet worms'
a velvet-skinned worm-like animal (velvet worm)

Examples

The onychophoran hunts small insects in the damp undergrowth of the rainforest.
Scientists were excited to discover a new species of onychophoran in South America.
Unlike insects, the onychophoran moves using stumpy, clawed legs.

Etymology

From Greek 'onycho-' (claw) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their claw-like legs.
