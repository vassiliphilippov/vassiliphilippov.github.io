dryly

1. ADV

/ ˈdraɪli / (AmE), / ˈdraɪli / (BrE)
in a manner that is deliberately unemotional, matter-of-fact, or subtly humorous, often in an understated or ironic way
in a laconic, unemotional, or subtly witty manner

Examples

He commented dryly that it would probably rain, despite the clear skies.
Julia responded dryly to the criticism, raising an eyebrow but saying nothing more.
"That went well," Mark said dryly after the disastrous presentation.

Synset Examples

‘I know that’, he said dryly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironically - in a way that expresses irony
  • Synonym: sarcastically - in a sarcastic manner
  • Antonym: emotionally - in an emotionally expressive manner
