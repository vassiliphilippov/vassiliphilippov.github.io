Rubus caesius

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ˈsiːsi.əs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ˈsiːsi.əs / (BrE)
A creeping European bramble species that bears bluish-black dewberries.
European bramble species; dewberry

Examples

The Rubus caesius grows wild along the riverbanks in many parts of Europe.
Botanists noted the unique bluish bloom on the fruit of Rubus caesius.
Many birds feed on the dewberries produced by Rubus caesius during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - A trailing bramble bearing soft, edible berries

Etymology

From Latin: Rubus (“bramble” or “blackberry”) and caesius (“bluish gray”).
