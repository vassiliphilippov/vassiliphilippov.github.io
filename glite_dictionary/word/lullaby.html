lullaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlʌləˌbʌɪ / (BrE)
a soft, gentle song sung to help a child fall asleep
gentle song to help a child sleep

Examples

She sang a sweet lullaby to her baby every night before bed.
The soft melody of the lullaby helped the child drift off to sleep.
Many cultures have their own traditional lullabies sung to children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cradle song - another term for a song sung to send a child to sleep
  • Antonym: wake-up song - a song intended to rouse someone from sleep

Etymology

From English 'lull' (to soothe) + 'by', imitative of sounds made to comfort babies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlʌl.ə.baɪ / (BrE)
a gentle song sung to send a child to sleep
gentle song to soothe a child to sleep

Examples

She sang a soft lullaby to her baby every night.
The gentle lullaby quickly put the infant to sleep.
He remembered the old lullabies his mother used to sing when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cradle song - a song sung to lull a baby to sleep

Etymology

From late Latin *lullare* (to lull) and *by* (as in 'bye-bye', soothing refrain).
