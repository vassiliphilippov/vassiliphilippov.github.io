to sovietise

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ə.taɪz / (BrE)
to make (a country or its institutions) adopt the social, political, and economic systems characteristic of the former Soviet Union
make adopt Soviet systems

Examples

After the revolution, the new leadership tried to sovietise the educational system.
Several Eastern European countries were rapidly sovietised in the late 1940s.
Policies designed to sovietise agriculture led to widespread resistance.

Synset Examples

Castro sovietized Cuba

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to westernise - to make more Western in character

Etymology

From Soviet, referring to the Soviet Union, plus the verb-forming suffix '-ise'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ə.taɪz / (BrE)
to bring (a country, institution, or system) under the control or influence of the former Soviet Union; to organize according to Soviet principles or models
bring under Soviet control
british

Examples

After World War II, the USSR attempted to sovietise many countries in Eastern Europe.
The new regime began to sovietise education, industry, and the media.
It took decades to reverse the policies used to sovietise the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sovietize - to bring under Soviet control; American spelling of 'sovietise'

Etymology

From Soviet + -ise (British spelling; also spelled Sovietize in American English).
