lymphadenitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪm.fə.dəˈnaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəd.iˈnaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of one or more lymph nodes, usually as a result of infection.
inflammation of lymph nodes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the swelling in her neck as lymphadenitis.
Antibiotics are often prescribed to treat bacterial lymphadenitis.
Chronic lymphadenitis can indicate an underlying infection or immune condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphadenopathy - disease of the lymph nodes, often referring to their abnormal size or consistency

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'lymph' and Greek 'aden' (gland) + '-itis' (inflammation).
