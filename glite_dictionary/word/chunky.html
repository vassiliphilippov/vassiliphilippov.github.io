chunky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌŋki / (BrE)
having a thick texture with small lumps, or containing small solid pieces.
having small lumps or solid pieces

Examples

She prefers chunky peanut butter over the smooth variety.
The tomato soup was thick and chunky, filled with pieces of vegetables.
This paint is too chunky—it won't go on smoothly.

Synset Examples

the dumplings were chunky pieces of uncooked dough

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface, without lumps

Etymology

From 'chunk' (block or thick piece) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

WordNet ID: oewn-02148436-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌŋki / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) short and thick in form or build; having a stocky appearance.
short and thick; stocky

Examples

The puppy had a chunky body and short legs.
His chunky build made him stand out among the runners.
She bought a chunky sweater to keep warm in winter.

Synset Examples

some people seem born to be square and chunky
a dumpy little dumpling of a woman
dachshunds are long lowset dogs with drooping ears
a little church with a squat tower
a squatty red smokestack
a stumpy ungainly figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built
  • Antonym: slender - thin in an attractive or graceful way

Etymology

From 'chunk' (a thick lump), with the suffix '-y' forming adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02395530-s
Synset POS: s
