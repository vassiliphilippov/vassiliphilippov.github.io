to diabolize

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.bə.laɪz / (BrE)
to turn into a devil or make devilish in character
make devilish; turn into a devil

Examples

The villain's actions began to diabolize his reputation among the townspeople.
Centuries of conflict seemed to diabolize their leaders in the history books.
Some critics argue that media coverage can diabolize certain groups unjustly.

Synset Examples

Man devilized by war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demonize - to portray as evil or demonic

Etymology

From Medieval Latin diabolizare, from Latin diabolus (devil).
