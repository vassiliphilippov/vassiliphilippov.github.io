cushat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊʃæt / (AmE), / ˈkʊʃat / (BrE)
A Eurasian pigeon, specifically the wood pigeon (Columba palumbus), known for its distinctive white patches on the wings and neck.
Eurasian wood pigeon with white markings

Examples

The soft cooing of the cushat could be heard throughout the quiet woodland.
In the poem, the cushat is mentioned as a symbol of rural tranquility.
Several cushats flew up from the field as we approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood pigeon - a large Eurasian pigeon (Columba palumbus) with a white neck patch

Etymology

From Middle English cushat, from Old English cūsceat, a compound of 'cū' (cow) and 'sceat' (pigeon), referring to the wood pigeon.
