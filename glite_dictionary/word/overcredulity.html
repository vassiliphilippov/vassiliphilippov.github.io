overcredulity

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərkrəˈduːləti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkrɪˈdjuːlɪti / (BrE)
Excessive readiness or willingness to believe things; too much credulity.
excessive willingness to believe

Examples

Her overcredulity made her an easy target for scams and manipulative people.
The journalist warned against overcredulity when reading sensational news reports online.
He regretted his overcredulity after believing so many unfounded rumors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: credulity - willingness to believe or trust too readily
  • Antonym: skepticism - doubt or disbelief toward things that others accept as true

Etymology

From 'over-' (meaning excessively) and 'credulity' (from Latin 'credulitas', from 'credulus', believing), meaning excessive credulity.
