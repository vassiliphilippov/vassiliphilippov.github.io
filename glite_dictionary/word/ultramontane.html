Examples

4. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈmɑːnteɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈmɒnteɪn / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic who supports the authority of the Pope over local churches, especially in matters of faith and discipline.
supporter of papal supremacy

Examples

During the 19th century, many French Catholics identified as ultramontanes, supporting papal authority over local bishops.
The ultramontane argued passionately for the Pope's infallibility at the council.
He was labeled an ultramontane by critics for defending Roman centralization of church power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultramontanism - belief in the supreme authority of the Pope over the Church
  • Antonym: gallican - a Roman Catholic who advocates the independence of the national church from papal authority

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ultramontanus, meaning 'beyond the mountains' (referring to Rome, situated beyond the Alps from the perspective of northern Europe).
