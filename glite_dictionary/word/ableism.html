ableism

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bəlˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bəl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
discrimination or prejudice in favor of able-bodied people, especially against people with disabilities.
discrimination against disabled people

Examples

The organization fights against ableism in schools and workplaces.
Many activists work to raise awareness about ableism in society.
She called out the ableism embedded in popular media and advertising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discrimination - unfair treatment of people based on group membership
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience

Etymology

From 'able' (having the power to do something) + '-ism' (a distinctive practice, system, or philosophy), modeled after similar terms like 'racism'.
