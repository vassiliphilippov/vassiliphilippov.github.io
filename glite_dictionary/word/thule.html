Thule

1. PROPN

/ ˈθuːli / (AmE), / ˈθjuːli / (BrE)
A town in northwestern Greenland, notable for its United States naval base established during World War II.
town in northwestern Greenland

Examples

The U.S. maintained a military presence at Thule throughout the Cold War.
Scientists launched several Arctic expeditions from Thule in Greenland.
The remote location of Thule made it ideal for monitoring missile activity during the 1950s.

Etymology

From Latin Thule, referring to distant northern places in classical literature; later applied to this Greenland location.

2. PROPN

/ ˈθuːli / (AmE), / ˈθjuːli / (BrE)
an ancient name for the farthest north region of the known world, used by Greek and Roman writers to refer to a distant northern land, often identified with Scandinavia or Iceland.
ancient name for a distant northern land

Examples

Ancient writers described Thule as the northernmost edge of the known world.
The location of Thule has been debated by historians for centuries.
Some believe that Thule refers to Iceland, while others suggest it was Norway or Greenland.

Etymology

From Latin Thule, from Greek Thoulē, an ancient place name; possibly of pre-Greek or Scandinavian origin.
