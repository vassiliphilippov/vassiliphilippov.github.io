redfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a North Atlantic rockfish, especially one of several species with reddish coloring such as the rosefish or ocean perch
a North Atlantic rockfish

Examples

Fishermen in the region often catch redfish during the summer months.
The redfish population has declined due to overfishing in the Atlantic.
She grilled a fresh redfish for dinner, seasoned with lemon and herbs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A male salmon that has recently spawned, typically exhibiting a reddish coloration.
male salmon after spawning

Examples

The biologist identified the brightly colored redfish swimming upstream.
After spawning, the male salmon turns into a redfish and can be easily spotted in the river.
Fishermen say it's rare to catch a redfish at this time of year.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large edible fish found off the coast of the United States from Massachusetts to Mexico, typically with reddish coloring
a large edible reddish fish of US Atlantic coast

Examples

Fishermen along the Gulf Coast often seek out redfish for both sport and food.
The chef prepared a delicious grilled redfish with lemon butter sauce.
Catching a giant redfish was the highlight of their boat trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose fish - another name for the redfish, especially in its northern range
  • Synonym: red drum - an alternate species commonly called 'redfish' in the southern United States

Etymology

From 'red' (referring to color) + 'fish'.
