Philomachus

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈloʊməkəs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒməkəs / (BrE)
A former genus of wading birds in the sandpiper family, including the ruff (Philomachus pugnax), now usually placed in Calidris.
former bird genus including the ruff

Examples

The ruff was formerly classified under the genus Philomachus before taxonomic revisions.
Many older ornithology books still refer to Philomachus in their classification systems.
Specimens labeled as Philomachus can be found in several European natural history museums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruff - a species of wading bird formerly in genus Philomachus
  • Synonym: Calidris - the current genus including the ruff and similar sandpipers

Etymology

From Greek philos (loving) + machē (battle), applied in scientific naming.
