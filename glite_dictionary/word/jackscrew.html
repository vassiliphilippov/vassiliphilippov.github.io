jackscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌskru / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌskruː / (BrE)
A mechanical device that uses a screw mechanism to lift, lower, or move a heavy load.
mechanical screw-operated lifting device

Examples

The engineer used a jackscrew to raise the heavy platform during installation.
You should inspect the jackscrew regularly to ensure it operates safely and smoothly.
Aircraft elevators are commonly moved by a jackscrew mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screw jack - a type of jack operated by a screw mechanism
