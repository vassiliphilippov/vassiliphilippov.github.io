mesothelioma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsoʊˌθiliˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊˌθɪliˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A rare and aggressive type of cancer that develops in the mesothelium, the protective lining covering the lungs, abdomen, or heart, often linked to asbestos exposure.
type of cancer affecting the mesothelium
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with mesothelioma after years of working in the construction industry.
Symptoms of mesothelioma may not appear until decades after initial exposure to asbestos.
Treatment options for mesothelioma often include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinoma - a type of cancer that starts in epithelial cells

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle), 'thēlē' (nipple or teat), and '-oma' (tumor), referring to its origin in the mesothelium.
