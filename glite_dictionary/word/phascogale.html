phascogale

1. NOUN

/ fæˈskoʊɡeɪl / (AmE), / fæˈskəʊɡeɪl / (BrE)
a small, carnivorous marsupial native to Australia, belonging to the genus Phascogale, also known as pouched mice.
small carnivorous Australian marsupial

Examples

The phascogale is a rare marsupial found in parts of Australia.
Researchers are studying the habitat of the phascogale to understand its declining numbers.
A phascogale hunts insects and small vertebrates at night.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phascogale, from Greek 'phaskōlos' (pouch) + 'gale' (weasel).
