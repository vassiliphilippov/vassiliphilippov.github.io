Polygonum aubertii

1. NOUN

/ pɑːˌlɪɡəˌnʌm oʊˈbɜːrti.aɪ / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡənəm ɔːˈbɜːti.aɪ / (BrE)
A perennial twining vine with racemes of fragrant greenish flowers, native to western China and Russia.
species of flowering vine

Examples

The Polygonum aubertii is often grown on fences for its rapid growth and attractive appearance.
Gardeners admire the fragrant greenish flowers that cover Polygonum aubertii in late summer.
Because Polygonum aubertii is drought-tolerant, it thrives in a variety of climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver lace vine - common name for Polygonum aubertii

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'polygonon' (many-kneed), species named after the French missionary Jean Pierre Aubert.
