paganism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A term for religious beliefs and practices that are not part of the world's main monotheistic religions, especially those other than Christianity, Judaism, or Islam, often referring to ancient or folk traditions.
non-Abrahamic religions or beliefs

Examples

The village preserved many traditions rooted in paganism long after the country converted to Christianity.
Some scholars study the rituals and beliefs associated with early European paganism.
He became interested in modern forms of paganism after attending a summer solstice festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polytheism - belief in or worship of multiple gods
  • Antonym: monotheism - belief in a single deity

Etymology

From Latin 'pāgānismus', meaning 'country-dweller' or 'civilian'; later used to mean non-Christian religion.
