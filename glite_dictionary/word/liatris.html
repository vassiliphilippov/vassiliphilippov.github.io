Liatris

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈætrɪs / (AmE), / laɪˈætrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial North American herbs, often with aromatic, cormous roots, and spikes of purple or white flowers, commonly called blazing star or gayfeather
a genus of North American flowering herbs

Examples

Many gardeners plant Liatris for its tall, vibrant flower spikes in summer borders.
The roots of Liatris are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
Several species of Liatris attract butterflies and bees to the garden.

Etymology

From New Latin, of uncertain origin, used as the genus name in botanical classification.

2. NOUN

/ liˈætrɪs / (AmE), / liˈætrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial North American herbs in the daisy family, with aromatic, usually cormous roots and spikes of purple flowers.
genus of perennial North American herbs

Examples

Many gardeners grow Liatris for its tall spikes of purple flowers and easy maintenance.
The roots of Liatris are often aromatic and can be cormous in nature.
Several pollinators, including butterflies, are attracted to the flowers of Liatris.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on an American vernacular name.
