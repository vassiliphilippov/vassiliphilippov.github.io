supported

1. ADJ

/ səˈpɔrtɪd / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtɪd / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) held up or having its weight sustained, especially from below.
held up; weight borne from below

Examples

The old bridge is still supported by its original stone piers.
Each shelf is supported by sturdy metal brackets underneath.
The statue remained supported by the pedestal even during the earthquake.

Synset Examples

supported joints in a railroad track have ties directly under the rail ends

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsupported - not held up or having its weight borne
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02359268-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ səˈpɔrtɪd / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtɪd / (BrE)
sustained or maintained by aid, help, or backing rather than by direct physical means.
maintained by aid or assistance

Examples

The local shelter is supported by generous donations from the community.
Her research project was supported through government grants.
This news outlet is supported solely by its subscribers.

Synset Examples

a club entirely supported by membership dues
well-supported allegations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsupported - not sustained or maintained by aid; lacking support
  • Synonym: backed - having support, especially financial or moral
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02361206-a
Synset POS: a
