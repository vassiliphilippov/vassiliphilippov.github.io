frog's-bit

1. NOUN

/ frɔɡz bɪt / (AmE), / frɒɡz bɪt / (BrE)
A European aquatic plant with floating, rounded, heart-shaped leaves and small white flowers, often found in still or slow-moving water.
European aquatic plant with round leaves and white flowers

Examples

The pond was covered with frog's-bit and other aquatic plants.
We spotted white flowers of frog's-bit floating among the lily pads.
Gardeners recommend frog's-bit for natural water features because it thrives in calm waters.

Etymology

Named for its supposed resemblance to a frog's bite or the habitat often shared with frogs.
