Aeolis

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːəlɪs / (BrE)
an ancient region along the northwest coast of Asia Minor, including Lesbos, settled by the Aeolians around 1100 BC.
ancient region in northwestern Asia Minor

Examples

Many ancient Greek poets were said to have lived in Aeolis during the early centuries.
Archaeological discoveries in Aeolis have provided valuable insights into Aeolian culture.
The island of Lesbos was an important part of Aeolis in antiquity.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰολίς (Aiolís), meaning 'land of the Aeolians'.
