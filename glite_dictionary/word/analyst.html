analyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈan.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially a licensed practitioner, who specializes in psychoanalysis
practitioner of psychoanalysis

Examples

After years of training, she became a licensed analyst and opened her own practice.
The patient discussed his dreams with his analyst every week.
Choosing the right analyst can be crucial for effective psychoanalytic therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalyst - a person trained to practice psychoanalysis

Etymology

From analysis + -ist, modeled after specialist terms in the late 1800s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈan.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies financial data and trends in order to provide advice or recommendations for businesses or investors
expert in financial data and trends

Examples

The financial analyst recommended investing in technology stocks this quarter.
Many companies rely on a market analyst to provide insight into sales trends.
As an analyst, she closely examines data before making any investment suggestions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economist - a person who studies and analyzes economic data and trends
  • Synonym: advisor - a person who gives advice in a particular field, especially in finance

Etymology

From analysis + -ist, indicating a person who conducts analysis.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈænəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈænəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who examines information or situations in detail in order to understand them or make decisions
a person who examines information

Examples

The financial analyst predicted a rise in stock prices this quarter.
After reviewing the data, the analyst offered several recommendations for improvement.
She works as a market analyst for a leading technology firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examiner - a person who inspects or investigates something
  • Degree Higher: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill

Etymology

From analysis + -ist, meaning a person who practices or is concerned with something.
