manifold

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
A pipe or chamber with several lateral outlets or inlets, used to collect or distribute fluids, especially in engines or plumbing systems.
pipe with multiple outlets/inlets

Examples

The exhaust manifold collects gases from multiple cylinders into one pipe.
A plumber installed a new manifold to better control water flow to different rooms.
The engine's intake manifold was leaking, causing performance issues.

Etymology

From Old English *manigfeald* (many + fold), originally meaning 'many in one'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A topological space that locally resembles Euclidean space near each point, often forming surfaces in two or more dimensions.
mathematical space like a surface

Examples

The concept of a manifold is fundamental to modern geometry and topology.
A sphere and a torus are both examples of two-dimensional manifolds.
Physicists often use the idea of a four-dimensional manifold to describe spacetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside layer of something, especially an area that is flat or smooth

Etymology

From Old English manigfeald, meaning 'many-fold' or 'multiple forms.'
