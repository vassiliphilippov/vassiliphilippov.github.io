Stenotomus aculeatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˈtoʊməs ˌækjʊˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌstiːnəˈtəʊməs ˌækjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of porgy found in the southern Atlantic coastal waters of North America.
species of porgy fish

Examples

Many anglers hope to catch Stenotomus aculeatus during the summer fishing season.
The Stenotomus aculeatus is a common sight along the southern Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists are studying the habitat of Stenotomus aculeatus in North American waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porgy - A fish of the family Sparidae, commonly found in coastal waters

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'tomos' (section), combined with Latin 'aculeatus' (spiny or prickly), referring to the fish's features.
