haemostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmoʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈhɪəməʊˌstæt / (BrE)
A surgical instrument used to stop bleeding by clamping a blood vessel.
surgical instrument that stops bleeding
medical british

Examples

The surgeon quickly reached for a haemostat to control the bleeding vessel.
Before suturing the wound, she applied a haemostat to ensure there was no blood flow.
A haemostat is essential during operations to grasp and clamp blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemostat - American English spelling for haemostat

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + '-stat' from 'stasthai' (to stand, stop).
