blastoporic

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæstəˈpɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌblæstəˈpɔːrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or associated with a blastopore, the opening that forms during the early embryonic development of some animals
relating to a blastopore in embryos
medical

Examples

During gastrulation, the blastoporic region plays a critical role in forming the digestive tract.
Researchers examined the blastoporic changes in various amphibian embryos under a microscope.
The arrangement of cells around the blastoporic opening influences later tissue development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastopore - the opening created during early embryonic development

Etymology

From 'blastopore' + '-ic', with 'blastopore' from Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout) and 'poros' (passage).
